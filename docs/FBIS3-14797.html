Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS corespondent Olga Solodova] [Text] Moscow February 25 TASS -- Former Russian Security Minister Viktor Barannikov, who was arrested as an organiser of the armed opposition revolt last October in Moscow, welcomed the amnesty to anti-Yeltsin rebels proclaimed by the State Duma, but does not believe himself to be guilty of anything. "I welcome the amnesty if it leads to stabilisation of the political situation in the country", Barannikov was quoted as saying by his lawyer Dmitriy Matveyenko on Friday. Matveyenko, however, told TASS that the former minister understands that an amnesty covers only guilty people and "does not consider himself to be such a man". Barannikov is currently staying at home. He was transferred from prison to hospital because of heart problems and later released on condition that he stays in moscow. The lawyer said his client had been already discharged from hospital and his state of health is satisfactory.
