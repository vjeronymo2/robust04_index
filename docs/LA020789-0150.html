February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Late Final Edition

WORLD;

15 KILLED IN MEXICO BUS PLUNGE

Fifteen people were killed and 26 injured when a city bus overturned on a winding stretch of road and plunged down a 60-foot cliff, authorities said today.

The accident occurred Monday about four miles from downtown on a curved, steep portion of road near the Valle Verde neighborhood on the outskirts of this Baja California resort, Fire Department officials said. A spokesman for the department said bus driver Enrique Hernandez de Jesus apparently lost control of the vehicle while trying to negotiate the curve. One surviving passenger told investigators that the bus driver said, "Hang on tight, I have no brakes," moments before the bus slid over the cliff.
