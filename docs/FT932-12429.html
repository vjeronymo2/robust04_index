930428 FT 28 APR 93 / International Company News: Sears Canada sees profits after first-term improvement SEARS Canada, one of the country's biggest retailers, predicts a return to profitability this year after posting its worst loss for 40 years in 1992. The first-quarter loss was CDollars 21.8m (USDollars 17.4m) or 23 cents a share, a 40 per cent improvement on the loss of CDollars 35.6m, or 42 cents, a year earlier. Sales slipped slightly to CDollars 772m. Last year, Sears Canada, in which Sears, Roebuck of the US is the largest shareholder, restructured and laid off several thousand workers. It operates 109 stores across Canada, and its national catalogue sales business. Biochem Pharma, the main Canadian associate of Glaxo Holdings, the UK pharmaceuticals group, posted a loss of CDollars 13.1m, or 32 cents, for the year ended January 31 after special items, against profits of CDollars 12.6m, or 37 cents, a year earlier.
