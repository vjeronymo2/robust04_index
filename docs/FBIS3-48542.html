Language: Indonesian Article Type:BFN [Text] Indonesia is ready to continue defense and security cooperation, particularly military training with Papua New Guinea [PNG]. Vice President Try Sutrisno expressed Indonesia's readiness to do this during a courtesy call by PNG Defense Minister Paul Tohian at his residence on Diponegoro Street in Jakarta today. The visit is aimed at enhancing bilateral relations, particularly in defense and security. According to Defense Minister Paul Tohian, to enable the establishment of a national language, a number of ethnic groups in the country, which has a population of four million and 700 languages, need to be given a new formula. The PNG defense minister urged Indonesia to receive a PNG delegation for an APEC [Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation] meeting to be held in Jakarta this year. The PNG guest, whose visit will end on 30 January, will visit the Army Industry, the Nusantara Aircraft Industry, the National Shipyard, the Ammunition Plant, and tourist spots in Jakarta and Yogyakarta. He will also observe military rural assistance programs undertaken by military personnel in Indonesia. He will fly from Surabaya to Irian Jaya, where he will visit border villages.
