Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The northern regional security committee has assured residents of Tamale that the military and the police are in control of the general security in the area and they can therefore go about their normal duties without fear. In a statement issued at Tamale, the committee requested the public to cooperate and warned that any person found involved in criminal activities would be severely dealt with. The assurance came in the wake of fears generated by the general state of insecurity as a result of mob attacks on certain sections of the community since last week. The military and police have been deployed at strategic areas in the municipality to curb further acts of violence. Some reinforcements have also been dispatched to the East Gonja and Yendi Districts where fighting is reported to be still going on despite the call for cease-fire by the northern sector brigade commander of the Ghana Armed Forces. According to people fleeing the fighting from Salaga, the casualty rate is high. In another development, Dagbon leaders in Tamale have formed a task force to help stop the burning and looting of property by irate mobs. Meanwhile, a GHANA NEWS AGENCY report said that the state of insecurity had resulted in many people, mainly in the public service, leaving the municipality. Already all first and second cycle schools in the municipality have closed down following threats of mob attack. The National Mobilization Program [NMP] and the Red Cross Society in Tamale have, meanwhile, registered over 2,000 refugees who have fled the ethnic fighting in parts of the region. The refugees are mainly students, national service personnel, and displaced people, including women and children. The report said that about 600 of the refugees are camped at the Nyohene Vocational Center and are fed by the local community because the NMP and the Red Cross have nothing in stock. It said the rest of the refugees, who feel their lives are endangered in Tamale, are being kept in a military camp.
