BFN [Text] The Ministry of Awqaf and Religious Affairs has completed the printing of 60,000 copies of the Holy Koran. Dr. 'Abd-al-Mun'im Ahmad Salih, minister of awqaf and religious affairs, has explained that the new edition is similar to the one which was printed in Germany in 1980 in three sizes. The minister added that the Holy Book will be of medium size and will be offered to citizens as part of the faith campaign led by the struggler leader Saddam Husayn. He said the ministry will print another 15,000 copies in the next few weeks in the form of sections of the Holy Koran to be used in the mosque religious courses held throughout the country during the summer holidays.
