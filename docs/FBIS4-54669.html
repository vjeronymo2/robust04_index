BFN [Excerpts] The Croatian dinar's days are numbered. Croatia will be introducing its own currency on Statehood Day, 30 May. As you already know, this will be the kuna. The dinar will remain legal tender until 7 June, but converting it into the new currency will be possible until the end of the year. This was revealed to reporters this afternoon by National Bank Governor Pero Jurkovic. [passage omitted] The replacement of the Croatian dinar by the new currency of the Republic of Croatia will be realized by knocking off three zeros, according to National Bank Deputy Governor Zdravko Rogic.
