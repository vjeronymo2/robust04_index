BFN [Unattributed station "special dispatch"; from the "Morning News" program] [Text] Chinese Vice Premier Li Lanqing said on 26 April that the Chinese Government will draw a lesson from the recent Qiandao Hu incident, and will intensify crackdown on crime and strengthen the management of social order. Li Lanqing made the above remark when meeting and discussing tourism industry with An Tong-son, chairman of the Committees on Commerce, Industry, and Resources of the Republic of Korea National Assembly. He said: The Qiandao Hu case was a chance occurrence. This criminal case has been solved. He expressed regrets and pains over the occurrence of the incident. However, he believed that some people in Taiwan are exploiting it to stir up a hue and cry and to pursue ulterior motives. He said the incident will have little effect on China's tourism industry, adding that overall the safety rate of sightseeing in China is relatively high as compared with other countries in the world.
