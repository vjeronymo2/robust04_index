BFN [Text] A joint communique has been issued at the conclusion of the visit of Mr. Vo Van Kiet, prime minister of the Socialist Republic of Vietnam [SRV], to the Union of Myanmar [Burma]. The full text of the communique reads: At the invitation of Senior General Than Shwe, prime minister of the Union of Myanmar, Mr. Vo Van Kiet, prime minister of the SRV, paid an official visit to the Union of Myanmar from 11 May to 14 May 1994. Prime Minister of the SRV and Madame Vo Van Kiet were accompanied by Minister of Foreign Affairs and Madame Nguyen Manh Cam; Mr. Tran Hoan, minister of culture and information; Mr. Nguyen Quang Ha, minister of forestry; Lieutenant General Nguyen Thoi Bung, vice minister of national defense; Mr. Doan Manh Giao, vice chairman of the Government Office; Mr. Tran Van Tuyen, vice minister of commerce; Mr. Ngo Thi Doan, vice minister of agriculture and food industry; Mr. Nguyen Thuong Bang, vice minister of heavy industry; Mr. Do Quang Trung, chairman of the Vietnam Tourism Administration; Mr. Luu Van An, chairman of the People's Committee of Hue City; Mr. Tran Viet Tan, Vietnamese ambassador to Myanmar; Mr. Doan Thao, deputy director general of the Physical and Sports General Department; and high-ranking officials of the Government of the SRV. Prime Minister and Madame Vo Van Kiet and their entourage were accorded a warm welcome by the government and the people of Myanmar, reflecting the traditionally friendly relations between the two countries. The prime minister expressed great pleasure in having the opportunity to visit the Union of Myanmar and conveyed to the people of Myanmar the cordial greetings and wishes of the Vietnamese people. During their stay in Myanmar, Prime Minister and Madame Vo Van Kiet and their entourage visited places of cultural and historical interests in Yangon [Rangoon] and Mandalay. They also had the opportunity to acquaint themselves with the progress and transformation taking place in Myanmar in the areas of agriculture and urban development. The prime minister of the SRV paid a call on and had talks with Prime Minister Sr. Gen. Than Shwe. The two prime ministers had a friendly exchange of views on matters of mutual interests and current regional and international issues. The talks took place in an atmosphere of friendship and understanding. Present at the meeting with Prime Minister Sr. Gen. Than Shwe and Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet were: General Maung Aye, vice chairman of the State Law and Order Restoration Council [SLORC]; Lt. Gen. Tin Tun, deputy prime minister; Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, secretary-1 of the SLORC; Lt. Gen. Tin U, secretary-2 of the SLORC; U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs; U Nyunt Swe, deputy minister of foreign affairs; U Aye, Myanmar ambassador to the SRV; Thura U Aung Htet, director general of the Protocol Department of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs; Mr. Nguyen Manh Cam, minister of foreign affairs; Mr. Tran Hoan, minister of culture and information; Mr. Nguyen Quang Ha, minister of forestry; Lt. Gen. Nguyen Thoi Bung, vice minister of national defense; Mr. Doan Manh Giao, vice chairman of the Government Office; Mr. Tran Van Tuyen, vice minister of commerce; Mr. Ngo Thi Doan, vice minister of agriculture and food industry; Mr. Nguyen Thuong Bang, vice minister of heavy industry; Mr. Do Quang Trung, chairman of the Vietnam Tourism Administration; Mr. Luu Van An, chairman of the People's Committee of Hue City; Mr. Tran Viet Tan, Vietnamese ambassador to Myanmar; and Mr. Doan Thao, deputy director general of the Physical and Sports General Department. Lt. Gen. Tin Tun, deputy prime minister, and Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, secretary-1 of the SLORC, paid separate calls on Mr. Vo Van Kiet, prime minister of the SRV. The Myanmar side acquainted the visiting prime minister with the emerging constitutional, political, economic, and social changes taking place in the country and with the efforts to secure national reconciliation. The Myanmar side reaffirmed its independent and active foreign policy based on the five principles of peaceful coexistence and its active participation in the nonaligned movement. The Vietnamese side informed the Myanmar side of the comprehensive reform policy and the achievements recorded as well as the measures aimed at bringing the country into the new stage of development by gradually realizing industrialization and modernization. The Vietnamese side also reported on its independent sovereign, and opened and diversified foreign policy. The Myanmar side highly appreciated the reform policy and the achievements recorded by the Vietnamese people and wished the Vietnamese people many new successes in their noble cause. The two sides reaffirmed that the people of each country has the right to freely choose their own political, economic, and social system according to their needs, tradition, and culture and agreed to continue their cooperation in upholding these principles. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet highly valued the efforts of the government and the people of Myanmar to achieve economic development and social progress and expressed his wishes for the success of these endeavors. During the talks between the two foreign ministers, current political and economic development in the region were reviewed with emphasis on bilateral and regional cooperation. Separate talks at ministerial level of industrial, agricultural, forestry, commercial, cultural, physical and sports, and tourism affairs were also held. They agreed to promote cooperation in the respective fields between the two countries. The following agreements were signed after the talks: 1. agreement on the establishment of a joint commission for bilateral cooperation, 2. trade agreement and 3. agreement on tourism cooperation. Lt. Gen. Nguyen Thoi Bung, vice minister of national defense, also called on Lt. Gen. Tin U, chief of the No.1 Special Operation Bureau and chief of staff of the Army. The two sides reaffirmed their desire to promote cooperation in the economic, environmental, cultural, tourism, agricultural, forestry, educational, and scientific field as well as in the fight against narcotic drugs. In the field of transport both sides agreed on specific steps to be taken to establish direct airline service between Myanmar and Vietnam at the earliest possible date. The two prime ministers expressed their satisfaction on the successful visit of Mr. Vo Van Kiet, prime minister of the SRV, and his delegation to the Union of Myanmar, which significantly contributed to the strengthening of friendship and cooperation between the two countries. They welcomed the opportunity provided by the visit to renew personal contacts. Prime Minister of the SRV and Madame Vo Van Kiet conveyed their sincere thanks to Prime Minister and Madame Than Shwe for the warm and friendly hospitality provided to them and the entourage during the visit. The prime minister of the SRV and Madame Vo Van Kiet also extended an invitation to Prime Minister of the Union of Myanmar and Madame Than Shwe to visit the SRV at a mutual convenient time, and the invitation was accepted with pleasure.
