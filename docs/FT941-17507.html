940104 FT 04 JAN 94 / Arts: Today's Television Ballet on television is problematical. Point cameras at the stage and the dancers often look tiny. Get choreographers to create for the medium and the results are frequently embarrassingly pretentious. This week BBC2 makes four attempts in Dance For The Camera starting today with Shobana Jeyasingh's 'Duets With Automobiles' which brings together Indian classical dance and London office blocks (7.30). BBC1 devotes time to the 30th anniversary of Top Of The Pops with a retrospective at 8.00 and, at 10.50, in Pan's People: Digging The Dancing Queens, a fond glance back to the days when physically well endowed women were allowed to strut their stuff just as readily as the mentally well endowed. Kind Of Blue (9.00 C4) is a 60-minute documentary about melancholia/depression which suggests the condition need not be regarded as all bad. 40 Minutes, which the BBC has announced it is scrapping, looks at a bizarre habit among the richer natives of Britain: they send their eight-year-old sons away to institutions called boarding schools (9.50 BBC2).
