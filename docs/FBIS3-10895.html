Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 13 Mar (NA) -- Arriving on an official four-day visit today, UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali emphasized Argentina's significant role in the UN peace-keeping projects. He arrived this morning at the Ezeiza international airport. He will ask Argentine President Carlos Menem to send more troops to former Yugoslavia during a meeting tomorrow. In remarks to a Buenos Aires newspaper, Butrus-Ghali noted that the United Nations wants the Argentine Government to send an additional battalion of blue helmets to join those already in Croatia. Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella welcomed the Egyptian diplomat, emphasized the "esteemed value" of the visit, and confirmed Argentina's desire to continue participating in the UN peace-keeping missions. Issues like the UN debt following the various deployments of Argentine blue helmets will also be discussed during the visit. The formation of an international force to struggle against hunger -- the White Helmets -- will also be reviewed. During the meeting with Menem tomorrow, an agreement to assist the Mothers, Infants, and Nutrition Program [Programa Materno Infantil y Nutricion] (PROMIN), financed by the World Bank, will also be signed.
