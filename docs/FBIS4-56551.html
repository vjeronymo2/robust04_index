BFN [From the "Morning Information Review"] [Text] U.S. President William Clinton gave a detailed explanation of the priorities of U.S. foreign policy during a journalists forum organized with CNN in Atlanta, Georgia. In this regard, Clinton talked about current areas of tension such as Bosnia, Rwanda, and Haiti. He also talked about the elections held in South Africa, among other topics. As for Haiti, Clinton said that Washington does not exclude the possibility of military intervention based on the facts that this Caribbean country is a separate case and that there is a need to stop the killings and restore democracy. Clinton spoke in favor of not lifting sanctions on Serbia until the negotiations for a peaceful solution in Bosnia begin. As for Bosnia, Clinton announced new interventions by NATO planes if the Bosnian-Serb forces attack UN-protected territory. The head of the White House saluted the agreement signed on Wednesday between the PLO and Israel and praised the results of the elections in South Africa saying it opens up the possibility of contributing to this country's development, which could be the key to the African continent because of its capability, according to Clinton. As for Cuba, William Clinton reiterated his support for the trade and economic blockade of the island. Regarding the ties with the PRC in the last two decades, he said that Washington will work hard to iron out the differences between the two nations.
