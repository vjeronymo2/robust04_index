Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by unidentified correspondents in Colonia, Uruguay] [Text] Paraguayan President Juan Carlos Wasmosy has obtained assurances from his Brazilian counterpart Itamar Franco that customs restrictions to bilateral trade enforced by Brazilian officials that amount to a virtual intimidation campaign -- through the confiscation of commodities purchased by Brazilian citizens at the Paraguayan city of Ciudad del Este -- "will be immediately revised and that the appropriate corrective measures will be adopted to end this situation that runs contrary to the integration process between Paraguay and Brazil." Early yesterday morning, before the opening of the Mercosur [Common Market of the South] summit, Wasmosy conveyed this problem to Franco based on a protest note submitted to him by Ciudad del Este Mayor Guillermo Campuzano and by the Foz do Yguazu-based Parana-Paraguay Chamber of Commerce. The note charges Brazilian customs officials with a harassment campaign "that has caused a major reduction in the flow of tourists to Foz do Yguazu and Ciudad del Este, with the subsequent reduction in sales for stores, hotels, and restaurants." The drop in business has been estimated at nearly 40 percent.
