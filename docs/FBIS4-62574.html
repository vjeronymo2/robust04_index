BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Konstantin Zhukovskiy] [Text] Moscow May 16 TASS -- Russia and Argentina initialed the agreement on the principles of relations between the two countries in Moscow on Monday. The agreement was initialed by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and his Argentinian counterpart Guido di Tella. They also signed a protocol on consultations and an agreement allowing the diplomats of the two countries to travel to and from Russia and Argentina without visas. The signed documents are the basis and at the same time only the tip of the iceberg of cooperation between Russia and Argentina which are connected by long-standing historic ties, Kozyrev said. Argentina is a U.N. Security Council member and its cooperation with Russia is not just of bilateral, but global importance, he added. Additionally, Argentina is one of the key countries in Latin America and Russia is launching active work in this direction to expand and develop contacts with regional political and economic organisations, primarily the Organisation of American States. "Guido di Tella's visit to Moscow is a big event, a landmark not only in bilateral, but also Russian-Latin American relations," Kozyrev said. The talks focused on a wide range of issues and were held in a business-like and friendly atmosphere. The Argentinian minister said that the talks in Moscow have brought relations between the two countries to a new level and revealed the proximity in the positions of the two countries. This is a sign that Russia and Argentina are moving together to a new world order based on peace, cooperation and mutual respect, he said.
