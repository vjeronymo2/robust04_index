BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vadim Byrkin] [Text] Moscow, 29 Apr -- An agreement on cooperation in solving questions of the social protection of military servicemen and the moral, spiritual, patriotic and aesthetic education of servicemen and the members of their families was signed on Thursday by the Russian Ministry of Defense and the Independent Union of Military Servicemen. An ITAR-TASS correspondent was told at the union's central committee today that this took place during the signing of the Social Accord Treaty in the St. George's Hall of the Kremlin, during the course of which the sides were able to work out optimal approaches to resolving vital problems of the armed forces. The sides believe that one of the basic tasks facing the state and society is the reshaping of the army and the navy, and they pledge to carry out joint work in shaping the Russian armed forces on a professional basis and in educating servicemen and their families, and also to revive and publicize the ancient spiritual and moral traditions of the Russian army and navy. The Ministry of Defense and the independent servicemen's union agreed to carry out joint work on the social adaptation of citizens discharged from military service, on the construction of housing, the opening of new enterprises based on converted military facilities, the creation of new jobs and the retraining of servicemen in civilian professions. The agreement points out that the sides pledge "to prevent the army and navy being dragged into political games, a reduction in the level of defense capability, and a weakening in the social protection of servicemen and citizens discharged to the reserve or into retirement, and their families". Joint preparations for the celebrations to mark the 50th anniversary of the victory in the Great Patriotic War will also be carried out. The agreement was signed by Russian Defense Minister Army General Pavel Grachev, and Mikhail Kolchev, chairman of the Central Committee of the Independent Union of Military Servicemen of the Russian Federation.
