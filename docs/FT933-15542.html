930708 FT 08 JUL 93 / Parliament and Politics: Lawson urges independent Bank HANDING responsibility for monetary policy to the Bank of England would lead to lower interest rates and strengthen the accountability of bank officials, Lord Lawson, the former chancellor, said yesterday. Lord Lawson told the Treasury and Civil Service committee that the present system under which which politicians controlled interest rates led to international investors demanding a 'risk premium' when buying sterling and threatened the UK's anti-inflation credentials. However, Lord Lawson's views were opposed by Lord Healey, a former Labour chancellor, who told the committee he was 'an agnostic veering towards scepticism' on the need for an independent bank. Mr Kenneth Clarke is to make his first appearance as chancellor before the Treasury and Civil Service committee on July 21.
