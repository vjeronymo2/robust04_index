October 11, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

TEACHING EVOLUTION

Regarding the issue of evolution being the only "scientific" view presented in California science textbooks ("Textbook Panel Bolsters Stand on Teaching Evolution," Part I, Sept. 29), most of the heat in the argument is generated by the secularists refusal to acknowledge that science is neither conducted nor taught in a philosophical/ideological vacuum.

Honest creationists and evolutionists both recognize that their basic philosophical/ideological views will color their conclusions. For instance, all of man's experience to date has shown that design demands a designer. To the creationist, this is perhaps the most profound evidence for the existence of God and his supernatural work in the origin of all things. Because the secular evolutionist presupposes that natural things can have only natural causes, he concludes that design in the universe must have come about by chance.

Those conclusions are at the heart of this whole controversy. There are thousands of "facts" or observations that appear to support both positions.

All that the honest, Bible-believing Christian has ever wanted is fairness in the teaching of evolution. Since evolution is the only position secular humanism can take regarding origins, it is obvious that evolution will be taught. I believe that it is only just that all the facts be presented, including those that create serious doubt about many of the arguments for evolution. To deny that right to teachers only proves that the state is taking an unscientific and dogmatic position.

DEAN A. OHLMAN

President, Christian Nature

Federation, Fullerton
