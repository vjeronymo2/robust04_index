BFN [Text] Moscow April 13 TASS -- President of Kazakhstan Nursultan Nazarbayev will not attend the CIS summit opening in Moscow on Friday, TASS learned from reliable sources on Wednesday, The Kazakh delegation will be headed by Vice-President Yerik Asanbayev. Although official reasons are not announced, sources in Alma Ata [Almaty] said the decision of Nazarbayev was caused by his dissappointment with the CIS. During his recent visit to Moscow Nazarbayev voiced an idea of creating an Euro-Asian Union instead of the CIS. Later, during a visit to Japan, he said the Commonwealth of Independent States has played its role and again advocated the idea of the new union. Nazarbayev believes that the CIS has ensured a more or less stable transformation of former Soviet republics into independent states. Now the states which are ready for close ties should think about the Euro-Asian Union, according to the president of Kazakhstan. [Moscow Mayak Radio Network in Russian at 0700 GMT on 13 April carries the following report: "Kazakhstani President Nursultan Nazarbayev's initiative to set up the Eurasian Union will be one of the main subjects for discussion at the Moscow meeting of CIS heads of state scheduled for 15 April. Among the tasks of the Eurasian Union is the drawing up of agreed economic reform programs."
