Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku (March 12) TURAN -- On March 10 Geydar Aliyev, on his way back to Baku, made a stop-over in Almaty were he met with the President of Kazakhstan, Nursultan Nazarbayev. According to official sources, the two leaders discussed bilateral relations. It was agreed that Kazakhstan would deliver grain to Azerbaijan. In addition, was discussed the possibility of the delivering to Baku raw oil from Kazakhstan. In particular, the oil-pipeline from Kazakhstan to Azerbaijan and Europe was discussed. Towards the end of talks, President Nazarbayev told journalists that "both countries are oil states and have tremendous possibilities for cooperation in different spheres of industry and I believe that our links will develop successfully." Speaking about the Azeri-Armenian conflict, President Nazarbayev said that "the CIS countries must find a way out of the situation...which can be implemented through the resolutions of the UN Security Council." President Aliyev said that Azerbaijan attaches great importance to cooperation with Kazakhstan. He said, "President Nazarbayev will visit Azerbaijan in the near future, and approximately ten documents will be signed." Speaking about the Azeri-Armenian conflict, Geydar Aliyev also pointed to Azerbaijan's readiness to apply the UN Security Council resolution as a model for solving the conflict peacefully. Furthermore, he said that Azerbaijan's territorial integrity must be provided. He also said that the CIS has many opportunities.
