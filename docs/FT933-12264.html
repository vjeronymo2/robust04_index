930724 FT 24 JUL 93 / Books: Lippi The missing name in your library of great Italian masters of the quattrocento may well be Fra Filippo Lippi, writes Patricia Morison. Few books have been devoted to this marvellously distinctive and beautiful Florentine painter, and none in English. Jeffrey Ruda's Fra Filippo Lippi: Life and Work with a Complete Catalogue (Phaidon Pounds 95, 560 pages) fills the gap admirably, if expensively, with clear and unpretentious text and many beautiful illustrations such as that pictured above: 'The Madonna and child with the Birth of the Virgin and the Meeting of Joachim and Anna.'
