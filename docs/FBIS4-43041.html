BFN [Text] Montreal, 10 Jun (NOTIMEX) -- The Canadian Government will suspend commercial flights to Haiti beginning on 25 June, Andre Ouellet, the Canadian minister of foreign affairs, has announced. He added that the decision to suspend commercial flights to Haiti was made to comply with a resolution that the OAS adopted last week calling for increased pressure on the coupist Haitian Government. Ouellet said that he plans to discuss with the cabinet the adoption of "additional measures," which according to official sources, "will affect the financial transactions" between the two countries. The United States today announced the suspension of flights to Haiti, beginning on 25 June. The Canadian Foreign Affairs Ministry also recommended to its citizens in that country to "leave the island while possible through regular flights."
