BFN [Text] Turkish State Minister Mehmet Ali Yilmaz concluded his contacts in the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus [TRNC] and left the island today. Speaking at a news conference before his departure at Ercan State Airport, Yilmaz said that during his stay he visited the investment projects in the TRNC and was briefed on them. He noted that the investments that continued in 1994 will be concluded in accordance with a time table. Pointing out that the greatest problem of the TRNC is the budget deficit, Yilmaz added that the Turkish government will try and solve this problem. He concluded by expressing his satisfaction over this first visit to the TRNC. Prime Minister Hakki Atun said that Yilmaz's visit gave them the opportunity to brief the Turkish minister on the investments being made, adding that Turkish delegation was informed on the problems of the coalition government. He noted that the talks between Turkey and the TRNC were beneficial and that the Turkish side had a sincere approach to the problems of the Turkish cypriots. Atun concluded by thanking the delegation for their approach. Atun, Deputy Prime Minister Ozker Ozgur, Turkish Ambassador to Lefkosa [Nicosia] Cahit Bayar, commander of the security forces, president of the supreme court, various ministers and other officials were present at the airport ceremony.
