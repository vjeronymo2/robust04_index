June 17, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

THE CHURCH'S VIEWS

Can it be that the Catholic Church has its left foot in its mouth and the right foot doesn't know? View on June 6 makes one wonder. The reporting of two rites of passage -- the Latino quinceanera and high society's coming out -- reflects opposing church attitudes.

We read that the Archdiocese of Los Angeles has issued new guidelines for quinceaneras to simplify a tradition that has ". . . become too secular, too social and too costly" ("Is the Party Over?" Page 1).

On another page of the same issue, we read that in a ballroom of the Beverly Hilton Hotel there was "no shortage of pomp" as this year's designer-clad (Social Services Auxiliary) debutantes were presented to the Archbishop of Los Angeles ("On View").

If there is an award for ambivalence, I hereby nominate the Catholic Church.

LEA PETRICK
