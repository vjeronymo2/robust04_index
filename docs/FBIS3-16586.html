Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] President Yeltsin's representative at talks in Chisinau on the settlement of the Transdniestrian conflict Vladlen Vasev put forward a Russian plan for Transdniestria's possible legal status, he said in an exclusive interview with INTERFAX on Thursday. Vasev will not comment on the plan until a meeting with Moldovan President Mircea Snegur and Transdniestrian leader Igor Smirnov scheduled for 17 March. He said that it gives Transdniestria greater autonomy. "It is important for Moldova and the Transdniestrian region that this status should be within the framework of a single and integral Moldovan state," Vasev pointed out.
