920125 FT 25 JAN 92 / Finance & The Family: CGT and assets THE TABLE above shows capital gains tax allowances for assets sold in December. Multiply the original cost of the assets by the figure shown for the month in which you sold them. If you subtract the result from the proceeds of your sale, the difference will be your capital gain for tax purposes. Suppose, that you bought shares for Pounds 5,000 in July 1985 and sold them in December 1991 for Pounds 12,000. Multiplying the original cost by the July 1985 figure of 1.425 gives a total of Pounds 7,125. Subtracting that from the proceeds of Pounds 12,000 gives a gain for tax purposes of Pounds 4,875 - below the annual Pounds 5,500 CGT allowance. If you realised no other gains during the year, the profit should be tax-free.
