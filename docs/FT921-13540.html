920121 FT 21 JAN 92 / Appointments: Fraser leaves Whitbread for Shepherd Neame Angus Fraser, recently chairman of Whitbread's Thresher off-licence chain and the group's hotels, is to join Shepherd Neame, the independent Kent brewer, as a non-executive director and board consultant. He will advise the company, which has been expanding rapidly and now has 315 pubs in the south-east, on the development of its retail activities and staff training. Fraser, 52, a former chairman of the Brewers' Society retail committee, is leaving Whitbread in March after eight years with the brewing and retailing group. He said yesterday that he would be pursuing a range of other activities. He joined Whitbread in 1984 as managing director of Whitbread Inns after 15 years with Watneys where he became deputy managing director of the Chef & Brewer. Before entering brewing, Fraser was in the Queen's Royal Irish Hussars for nine years; he is on the board of the Bedfordshire Tec.
