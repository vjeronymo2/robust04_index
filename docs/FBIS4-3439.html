BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, April 20 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese Communist Party delegation headed by Hu Jintao, member of the Standing Committee of the party's Politburo, arrived here Wednesday [20 April] on a four-day official visit to Argentina. The Chinese delegation is touring the South American country at the invitation of Argentina's ruling Justicialist Party. Hu said in a written statement at the airport that the main purpose of his visit to Argentina is to "enhance friendship, strengthen cooperation, and promote peace and development." Hu expects to exchange points of view on international and domestic issues of common concern and bilateral relations with argentine leaders. The Chinese delegation was greeted by secretary general of the Presidency of Argentina, Eduardo Bauza, leaders of the Justicialist Party, and Argentine diplomats. The Chinese delegation ended its two-day visit to Uruguay earlier Wednesday and flew to Argentina, the second leg of their three-nation tour of Uruguay, Argentina and Brazil.
