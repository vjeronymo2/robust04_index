BFN [Text] Zhanjiang, May 11 (XINHUA) -- A group of Bolivian visitors headed by H. Alfonso Ferrufino Valderrama, vice-president of Bolivia's Chamber of Deputies, said that they will take Zhanjiang as a base to enhance economic and trade cooperation between Bolivia and China. Situated on the Leizhou Peninsula in Guangdong Province, Zhanjiang city is one of eight major sea ports in China. During their business tour in Zhanjiang city, the Bolivian guests visited Nansan Island near the city. The island occupies 123.4 square kilometers with 34 percent of its land covered with forests. The Bolivian guests consider the island an ideal place for establishing a trade base between Bolivia and China. Eduardo la Fuente Roca, general manager of the Industrial and Commercial Chamber of Cochabamba of Bolivia, said that the base will provide an opportunity for businessmen in Latin American countries to engage in free trade and entrepot trade. He said that his chamber has sent letters to related Chinese departments to apply to establish of a business center in Zhanjiang city. The Bolivian businessmen also expressed their intention to invest in flower raising and refined processing of livestock products.
