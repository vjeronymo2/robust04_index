940730 FT 30 JUL 94 / Eurotunnel set for passenger licence The go-ahead for passenger train services to run through the Channel tunnel is expected very shortly, Eurotunnel said yesterday. The company said that it understood that the Anglo-French Intergovernmental Commission, which must approve passenger services, had met on Thursday to make its decision. Eurotunnel said it had completed its presentations to the commission's safety authority, which must satisfy itself on the safety aspects of train operations in the tunnel, earlier in the week. Letters setting out the commission's decision were expected very shortly. The company, which already runs freight services, wants to operate two types of passenger service: Eurostar high-speed passenger services between London, Paris and Brussels. Limited Le Shuttle passenger services between tunnel terminals at Cheriton in the UK and Sangatte in France.
