BFN [Text] The Defense Ministry has signed an agreement to buy 22 infantry fighting vehicles from Daewoo Corporation of South Korea. The signatories were the ministry's secretary general, Datuk Kamaruddin Sharif, and Daewoo President Ki Bum-yu. A ministry statement said the infantry vehicles were expected to be delivered next month or in August. The vehicles worth 32 million ringgit will be sent to the Malaysian Battalion in Bosnia-Herzegovina, bringing to 64 the number of such vehicles there.
