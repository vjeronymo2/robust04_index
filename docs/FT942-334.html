940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / International Capital Markets: Optimism on HK paper Prospects for Hong Kong's bond market are improving, but its current structure cannot fully accommodate funding needs, AP-DJ reports from Hong Kong. In its monthly economic report, Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation said Hong Kong alone will earmark HKDollars 350bn over the next 10 years on infrastructure projects, while China will spend USDollars 25bn this year and its needs are growing by 20 per cent a year. Such demand requires funding on a massive scale and there are doubts that the current structure of Asia's financial markets will be able to accommodate all these needs, the report said. The lack of long-term government paper as a benchmark has 'dogged the development of a local bond market,' Hongkong Bank said.
