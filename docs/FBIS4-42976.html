BFN [Excerpt] Douglas Hurd, British secretary of state for foreign and Commonwealth affairs, has declared that the confidence-building measures [CBM] package submitted by the United Nations on the Cyprus issue is not dead. U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher also declared: I hope that the CBM's are not really dead. Hurd and Christopher expressed their views on the subject in Istanbul, where they are currently attending NATO meetings, in response to reporters' questions regarding Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias' statement that the CBM's are dead as far as Greece and the Greek Cypriot side are concerned. Hurd said that he does not share the views of Papoulias, who described the package as dead, adding: Efforts are continuing in order to reach an agreement. Comprehensive progress has been achieved.
