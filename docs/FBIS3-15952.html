Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 10 Mar -- Iberia news agency has learned that at a meeting of the Presidium of the Georgian Cabinet of Ministers Prime Minister Otar Patstatsia said that the 20-billion [currency not specified] credit received from Russia will be fully spent on the electric power supply and the import of fuel to Georgia. According to Vice Premier Zurab Kervalishvili, 8 billion of the 20 billion will be required for the maintenance of the Tbilisi regional power station. Zurab Kervalishvili has been instructed to create a mechanism of having the population pay fees for electricity otherwise, it should be declared that the population is supplied with electricity, gas, and the like free of charge.
