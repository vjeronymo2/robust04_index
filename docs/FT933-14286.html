930715 FT 15 JUL 93 / Parliament and Politics: Government heading for climbdown over adoption THE GOVERNMENT was yesterday heading for a climbdown over its plans to charge couples for adopting children, after both Downing Street and Mrs Virginia Bottomley, the health secretary, poured cold water on the idea. The controversial proposals - that were to have been set out in a white paper on adoption law from the Department of Health - sparked a public outcry last week. Mr John Bowis, the health minister who outlined the plans, was charged with creating a 'cash-register society' after he disclosed that councils would be given the power to impose fees of up to Pounds 1,500 for social workers' reports to assess the suitability of prospective parents. Yesterday Mrs Bottomley appeared to overrule Mr Bowis when she said she very much doubted whether there was 'any prospect of any type of charging for domestic adoptions'. There was 'no question' of financial matters standing in the way of adoption, she said. It was clear that adoption was a 'very sensitive' subject. Her position was supported by Downing Street, which said it did not believe local authorities should be charging people in a manner that would exclude good parents. It was 'unlikely' there would be much charging, Downing Street said, confirming it was 'looking at' the white paper in conjunction with the health department. Labour last night welcomed the government U-turn, describing charging for adoption as 'one of the Tories' crazier notions'. Mr David Blunkett, shadow health secretary, said: 'We should be encouraging, not discouraging, good parents to come forward.'
