930202 FT 02 FEB 93 / Job market for graduates declines DEMAND for graduates fell in every area of business activity except part of the financial sector during the six months to October 1992 compared with the same period in 1991. But graduate salaries are still rising faster than inflation, according to the Statistical Quarterly bulletin of the Central Services Unit for Graduate Employment. Demand for graduates in finance excluding banking, insurance and actuarial jobs rose 24 per cent. The report also shows that overall demand, measured by the number of advertised posts in the unit's Current Vacancies magazine, fell by 4.8 per cent compared with the same period a year earlier. Vacancies in systems analysis, software design, security and protection and hotel and catering management fell by more than 90 per cent, it found. Statistical Quarterly, January 1993.
