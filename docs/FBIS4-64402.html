BFN [Unattributed article: "Three Parties Forced Into Single Parliamentary Caucus"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, April 28 -- Three smaller parties that do not have enough seats in the Lower House to form separate groups will be forced to join a single so-called mixed group because the speaker's office on Thursday [28 April] turned down their applications to form separate ones. The Socialist and Democratic Alliance parties each have 15 Lower House deputies while the Pact for Italy has 13, all below the 20-member floor limit for the formation of a separate group under the current rules. The three parties ran separately in the March elections (in some constituencies with the progressives) and are not keen to be forced into a single caucus and risk being seen by public opinion as interchangeable.
