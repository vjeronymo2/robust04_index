Language: Japanese Article Type:CSO [Text] The Power Reactor and Nuclear Fuel Development Corporation (PNC) has completed the loading of internal core fuel assemblies (108 units) in the prototype fast breeder reactor (FBR) ``Monju'' (rated output 280,000 kilowatts), which started on 13 October, and it began evaluation tests on flow distribution in the core on 4 November. In these tests, instruments to measure flow will be installed in the reactor and the flow of natrium circulating among the fuel assemblies will be measured and evaluated. To cool the ``Monju's'' core efficiently, the coolant natrium is circulated in response to the amount of heat generated by core fuel assemblies and blanket fuel assemblies, important components of the core. Evaluation testing will continue until around the beginning of February 1994.
