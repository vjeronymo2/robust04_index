940303 FT 03 MAR 94 / International Company News: Profits climb at Korean Air Korean Air, South Korea's biggest airline, reported net profits of Won11.8bn (Dollars 14.6m) for 1993, up from from Won1.8bn in 1992, as sales rose by 15 per cent to Won2,701bn, writes John Burton in Seoul. It predicted that earnings would increase in 1994 because of declining fuel costs and lower interest rates. It also expected increased air travel to China following a new aviation agreement, and the resumption of flights to Taiwan.
