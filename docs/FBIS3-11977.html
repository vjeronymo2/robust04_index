Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Panamanian elections scheduled for 8 May 1994. Ernesto Perez Balladares, Democratic Revolutionary Party (PRD) presidential candidate, toured Arraijan District on 17 March where the various communities greeted him. When asked about the foreign debt issue, he said "the debt was used for great works during President Torrijos' time, but the current government has handled it irresponsibly." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 17 March 94) While touring the corregimiento of Calidonia on 23 March, Perez Balladares said the other presidential candidates who are trying to unite the political force to undermine his presidential candidacy in the 8 May elections "will fail." Perez Balladares said his opponents were once united against Noriega, but now nothing is holding them together. (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 24 March 94 p A1) Ruben Dario Carles, Change 94 presidential candidate, has visited a mining company which is seeking to exploit gold deposits in Canazas, Veraguas Province. Carles then told the community he requested the company pay just salaries and conduct reforestation programs. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 17 Mar 94) Carles has vowed to defend the continuity of democracy and reduce energy rates. At a rally in Tole, Chiriqui Province, Carles promised he will revise energy rates. Carles said: "We must reduce energy rates; energy service is too expensive and inefficient." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 21 March 94) Carles is reportedly recovering from an accident he had on 22 March and therefore is limiting his political activities. Carles and a group of supporters fell from a balcony, and he reportedly twisted his ankle. Although his health was not endangered, Carles was advised to rest until his swollen ankle improves. Regarding this mishap, Carles concluded: "We have talked with other candidates to adopt appropriate measures to prevent people from continuing to live in old, rundown homes." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 23 March 94) Carles has requested the national government "to immediately suspend the renegotiation of the foreign debt with international banks, as it is unadvisable and extemporaneous," adding the government "should concentrate all of its efforts until September on attaining free elections which will pave the way for the new president and his team." (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 24 March 94 p 2A) Should she win the 8 May elections, Mireya Moscoso de Gruber, Arnulfista Party (PA) presidential candidate, has promised she would revise the minimum wage. De Gruber announced her intention after visiting several communities throughout the country where she found there is economic stagnation due to an increase in basic market products. De Gruber said: "Our government program establishes, after assuming the Presidency on 1 September, that we will conduct an in-depth and prompt study on the minimum wage. We must increase the minimum wage." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 17 March 94) In statements to the newscast "Telemetro Reports," de Gruber has admitted that political forces have become polarized between the PRD and the PA. Given this situation, de Gruber reiterated her call to all political parties to form one single political coalition behind the PA so as to confront the PRD. De Gruber asked other candidates to decline their candidacies in her favor. Regarding electoral violence, de Gruber has said she hopes violence could be averted. (Panama City Telemetro Television Network in Spanish 1730 GMT 21 March 94) The Solidarity Party will not heed other parties' call to form a civic alliance, Solidarity Party presidential candidate Samuel Lewis Galindo has warned. Members of the National Consensus Alliance, comprised by the Solidarity and National Unity Mission, have visited the Central Avenue area. Lewis Galindo said he does not favor a civic alliance. Galindo said: "The people are beginning to realize that in the fights between the candidates, we are the only ones who can attain the requisite national unity to fight poverty." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 23 March 94) Carlos Anel Cordero, Political Capsule columnist, reports Eduardo Vallarino, presidential candidate for the Christian Democratic Party, PDC, has addressed the National Federation of Business and Professional Women where he said the PDC government will insist on a more active role by women in administrative functions. Cordero adds all presidential candidates, except for the PA's Mireya Moscoso de Gruber, recently attended a meeting with the civic clubs during which they committed themselves to promote, respect, and strengthen civic values and morals. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 19 March 94 p 3A) A 500-word article by Marco Julio de Obaldia states the PRD has one-third of the people's vote, "and this will be evident on 8 May." De Obaldia writes "these votes were cultivated throughout 21 years," and most of the voters feel "they cannot go wrong by voting for the PRD." In conclusion, the writer urges the people to forget voting for the party of their choice, rather, to vote for the civic candidate who has "the greatest chance of winning," the only way to defeat the PRD candidate. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 19 March 94 p 8A)
