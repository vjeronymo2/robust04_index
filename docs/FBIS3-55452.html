Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Union of Russian Citizens held a meeting in Narva yesterday, calling for action against the "genocide policy" of the Estonian Government. Spokesman of the Union Vladimir Yefimov invited the persons attending to use all means against the Estonian authorities, including civic disobedience. A letter will be sent to the UN and the CE [Council of Europe], he announced, adding that a major meeting in central Narva is intended for January. The unemployed held a meeting in Jaanilinn (Ivangorod) yesterday, demanding that all inhabitants of Narva be dismissed from the enterprises in Jaanilinn and the jobs provided for the locals. Approximately 15 hundred of those present had been dismissed from the firms in Narva as a number of major enterprises cut jobs late last year. Mayor of Jaanilinn Viktor Dmitriyev announced that the Russian laws would not allow such a move.
