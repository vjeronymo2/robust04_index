Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Mexican elections scheduled for 21 August 1994. Cuauhtemoc Cardenas, presidential candidate of the Democratic Revolution Party (PRD), has received important support from the National Democratic Alliance today. The new electoral alliance is comprised by the center-left PRD and by more than 100 small political parties and social and people's organizations. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 2105 GMT 5 Feb 94) Cardenas, the PRD candidate, referred to a democracy and justice agreement signed by the eight political parties and their candidates. "What I want to tell you comrades about what we saw in these agreements is a possibility of free elections being held, a possibility for our democracy to progress; that is the only guarantee we have for peace in the nation," Cardenas said. He attended an ecologist forum organized by the PRD this morning. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 5 Feb 94) PRD candidate Cardenas has called on all the nation's political forces and the government during a political rally at the Revolutionary monument last weekend "to go from promises and words to concrete actions to guarantee honest elections on 21 August." (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 7 Feb 94) Cardenas led a march entitled 100 Hours for Democracy in the Mexican capital today. During a news conference, the PRD candidate talked about the goal of the this march: "To summon officially a special congressional session to implement the necessary legislative reforms to guarantee the electoral authorities' impartiality," Cardenas stated. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 10 Feb 94) Luis Donaldo Colosio, the PRI's (Institutional Revolutionary Party) official presidential candidate, proposed six basic points to guarantee impartial elections on 21 August today. He urged PRI members not to resort to methods that may damage the nation, thereby risking Mexicans' security. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 4 Feb 94) PRI candidate Colosio, on the campaign trail in Mexico City during the commemoration of the Constitution's 70th anniversary, said the Constitution is a guarantee for civil coexistence. "I want to restate my conviction and loyalty to its ideals, aspirations. I firmly believe in individual rights consecrated in the Constitution. I believe deeply in its social orientation and valuable democratic contents," he commented. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 5 Feb 94) Colosio has made a commitment to work toward gaining convinced and reasoned votes in the coming elections. On the campaign trail in Mexico City, Colosio has invited the people to compare the PRI proposal with those of the other parties. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 7 Feb 94) PRI candidate Colosio has met with leaders of national teachers associations, saying public education is our sovereignty's source of vitality. Colosio has said: "I defend and will defend the lay and free characteristics of the education offered by the state." Subsequently, Colosio met with Broad Socialist-Leftist Party members led by Manuel Terrazas, who offered him their support. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 8 Feb 94) Colosio has acknowledged that an ostentatious campaign is an insult to the Mexican people; therefore, his will be austere. "I will continue with a simple, sober, broadly represented campaign in each and every community as we have been doing in those we have visited," he has said. (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in spanish 0030 GMT 9 Feb 94) On the campaign trail in Jalisco State, Colosio has made a commitment to lead a government of actions, not of words. "I have come to make a commitment before you, without demagogy, without simulations, or myths, because what you appreciate the most is the truth. You appreciate straightforward talk; that is the way you speak, and that is the way I want us to speak to each other," he has said. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 10 Feb 94) Diego Fernandez de Cevallos, National Action Party (PAN) presidential candidate, has rejected the state as lord and master of the economy. Fernandez has said if he were elected president he would continue with the Solidarity Program, but he said he would strengthen municipal structures. He has spoken out for "a peaceful not a violent change in Mexico." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 5 Feb 94) Jorge Gonzalez Torres, presidential candidate for the Mexican Green Ecologist Party, has announced as of today his political campaign will be against NAFTA, as well as against the constitutional reforms, which will allow sons of foreigners to aspire to the Presidency after the year 2000. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1803 GMT 7 Feb 94) Marcela Lombarda Otero, Popular Socialist Party presidential candidate, has said during her tour in Zacatecas that to hold the coming elections peacefully, "Mexicans should be on guard, given the prevailing threats and pressures." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 10 Feb 94) Antonio Lozano Garcia, National Action Party secretary general, has said perfecting the electoral legal framework is fundamental to changing the nation's political reality. Lozano has said the PRI and government practices must be eliminated, given that until a clear division is made between the official party and the government, the democratic process cannot be achieved. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 4 Feb 94) Pablo Emilio Madero, National Opposition Union (UNO) candidate, has said more important than reviewing electoral law is "the need to count on impartial authorities." (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 4 Feb 94) UNO candidate Madero has told students of the Spanish American University more reforms are necessary to the electoral rulings to guarantee honest and democratic elections. Madero has said: "The conditions are set to allow for clean elections. There has been an undeniable opening in the media, something I have said before." (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 8 Feb 94) During the last day of his campaign trail in Morelos, UNO candidate Madero held a meeting with party members at municipal headquarters. After traveling to Jocultal, he said election conditions had changed radically. He said the next step for Mexico "is to achieve democracy, as this is for the good of the nation. Let no one hesitate," he said, "because the good of the nation should be above any party interests." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 10 Feb 94) Cecilia Soto, presidential candidate for the Labor Party, has said if elected she will propose procedural changes to achieve justice in the nation, and she would reopen the Guadalajara gas explosion case, "as someone must be made responsible." "I have no possibilities of being elected if the nation and the elections develop without any significant," Soto has said. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 10 Feb 94)
