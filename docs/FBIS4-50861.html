BFN [By Sofia Wu] [Text] Taipei, May 21 (CNA) -- Taiwan and Russia may cooperate in nuclear waste research and disposal, a Taiwan power company (Taipower) spokesman said Saturday [21 May]. Taipower may commission Russia to dispose of high-level radioactive waste, the spokesman said. Taipower Chairman Chang Chung-chien visited Moscow earlier this week at the invitation of the Russian Academy of Science to inspect its nuclear energy research facilities. Chang has reached consensus with Russian academy officials on cooperation in high-level nuclear waste research and treatment, the spokesman said. "The two sides may sign a letter of intent in the near future confirming their willingness to cooperate in nuclear waste disposal," the spokesman said. In addition to waste treatment, the two sides may [words indistinct] operation to the study of radioactive substances and technology for high-level nuclear waste disposal. The Russian academy is expected to draft a definitive framework for bilateral cooperation in these fields in the near future, the spokesman noted. To test Russia's treatment technology and determine transport routes, Taipower may first send a batch of medium-level nuclear waste to Russia for disposal, the spokesman said. "We'll make sure that the cooperative project is carried out without violating any international regulations or causing unnecessary environmental disputes," he added. Chang left Moscow Friday for Norway where he will attend an international energy resources development seminar.
