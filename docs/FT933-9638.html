930809 FT 09 AUG 93 / Gould demands fight for south LABOUR'S leadership was warned yesterday that to regard the south of England as a no-go area for the party could cost it a majority government at the next election. Mr Bryan Gould, who resigned from the shadow cabinet last year over Europe, said the party should offer the south 'something different and distinctive'. He added: 'We must not allow ourselves to be browbeaten into accepting that the south is somehow a no-go area for Labour or that we can break the Tory hegemony only by trailing along on the Liberal Democrats' coat-tails.'
