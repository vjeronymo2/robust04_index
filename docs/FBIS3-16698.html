Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] President Kravchuk is resorting to the powers granted to him by the Ukrainian Supreme Council in regard to the enforcement of Ukrainian laws in the Crimea, stated Mykola Mykhalchenko, chief of the Presidential Service for Issues of Domestic Policy, commenting on the president's decree concerning Yuriy Meshkov's decision on holding a poll among the Crimean people. He emphasized that separatist circles in the Crimea will accuse President Kravchuk of not giving the Crimea the possibility to conduct reforms. Nevertheless, in the opinion of Mykola Mykhalchenko, in the long term, the Ukrainian president's decree will stabilize the political situation in both Crimea and Ukraine, and it serves as a serious argument in the battle with separatism, preventing the creation of a legal basis for Crimea's secession from Ukraine.
