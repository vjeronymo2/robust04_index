Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Georgian head of state has issued an edict on securing order at oil-prospecting areas on the territory of the Republic of Georgia. As from the beginning of 1994, oil prospecting, which will contribute to bringing the country out of the energy crisis, is planned to be carried out with the participation of foreign firms in the territory of the Republic of Georgia. In order to secure order at geological survey sites at the oil fields: 1. Full responsibility for the security of people engaged in work at the oil fields and the establishment of order in the corresponding areas will be given to the Georgian Ministry of Internal Affairs. The Ministry of Internal Affairs and the state Georgian Petroleum will sign an appropriate agreement and create proper conditions for police sub-units to carry out the assignment. 2. The Ministry of Internal Affairs will prevent any attempts by armed formations to obstruct establishment of proper order at oil- prospecting sites and refining installations. 3. The Georgian Ministry of Justice and the Procurator's Office will examine the state of the management of security at the installations and submit propositions about appropriate amendments to be introduced in the legislation to the Georgian parliament and the Georgian head of state. [Signed] Eduard Shevardnadze
