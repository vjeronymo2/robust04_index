Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Belgrade, 11 Feb (TANJUG) -- The Serbian Assembly this evening elected the following three candidates to the posts of vice president of the republic's parliament in the first round of voting: Vojislav Mihailovic (DEPOS [Democratic Movement of Serbia] and Serbian Renewal Movement), Vojislav Andric (DEPOS and New Democracy) and Djordje Basic (Democratic Party). Deputies Dragan Todorovic of the Serb Radical Party [Srpska Radikalna Stranka] and Vladan Batic of the Democratic Party of Serbia [Demokratska Stranka Srbije] failed to gain the necessary number of votes and subsequently withdrew their candidature. Simultaneously with withdrawing their candidate for vice president the Radicals also withdrew their members from 23 asembly committees and ther candidates for the Chamber of the Republics of the Federal Assembly. [passage omitted] After a brief break Serbian Assembly president Dragan Tomic said that the heads of the groups of deputies failed to nominate a candidate for the fourth vice president's position, so this election will take place at one of the next sessions. The Serbian Assembly elected 16 of the 20 deputies to the Federal Assembly's Chamber of Republics -- nine from the Socialist Party of Serbia, four from DEPOS, two from the Democratic Party [DS], and one from the Democratic Community of Vojvodina Hungarians [DZVM]. This group consists of Socialists Zoran Andjelkovic, Slobodan Babic, Radmilo Bogdanovic, Vojislav Zivkovic, Branka Jesic, Milivoje Miletic, Miroljub Mladenovic, Radovan Pankov and Zoran Sokolovic. The DEPOS federal deputies are Milan Bozic, Vuk Draskovic, Dusan Mihajlovic and Gvozden Rosic. The DS members of this group are Zoran Djindjic and Borivoje Radic. The DZVM deputy is Mihalj Secei. [passage omitted] This evening the assembly re-elected Ljiljana Petkovic as general secretary of the parliament. [passage omitted]
