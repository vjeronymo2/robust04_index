Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 20 (XINHUA) -- A senior TV delegation from the United States, headed by Managing Director of the United States-China Chamber of Commerce Bruno Z. Wu, and representatives from China's 10 provincial TV stations met here today to discuss how to promote exchanges between them. Representatives from another 20 provincial TV stations will start discussions with the U.S. delegation Tuesday [22 February]. At the meeting this morning, B. Z. Wu, also president of the China International Partnership Advertising Agency (CIPAA), said that China's economic development has attracted world attention. Jonathan Jephcott, director in charge of business development in Asia and the Pacific region of the A.C. Nielsen [firm], said that his company is ready to carry out cooperation with its Chinese counterparts, to help upgrade China's advertising business in line with international practices. Representatives from the Metro Goldwyn Mayer (MGM) and the Jones International Ltd. expressed views on the exchange of TV programs and TV education.
