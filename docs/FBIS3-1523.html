Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Security in Burundi: The Interior Minister Leonard Nyangoma, currently in Brussels, has sounded the alarm. He has asked the international community to send a 5,000-man buffer force to his country to put an end to inter-ethnic clashes. Leonard Nyangoma was interviewed by Anne-Marie Mouradian: [Begin Nyangoma recording] The mission of this force would be to guarantee the security of institutions and the population. It could also contribute to the reorganization of the national army, because our army is no longer capable of guaranteeing the security of institutions and the population today. I do not think the OAU can undertake such an operation on its own. The West has come to the aid of Uganda, Somalia, and Sarajevo, why shouldn't it do the same for Burundi? [end recording] [passage omitted]
