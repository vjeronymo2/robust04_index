November 8, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

RESTAURANTS RAISE $11,435 DONATION FOR RED CROSS

Orange County restaurants donated $11,435 to the Red Cross Disaster Fund this week, responding to restaurateur Rex Chandler's challenge to donate 10% of their take from Saturday's receipts.

Chandler, owner of Rex Restaurant in Newport Beach and the Newport Oyster Bar & Grill, also is a board member of the county's American Red Cross chapter. Last week he asked fellow restaurateurs to designate Saturday as "Nov. 4 for the Red Cross."

In addition to Chandler's own restaurants, those participating included the Bouzy Rouge Cafe, the Cannery, Marrakesh, Malarkey's Irish Pub and Cano's, all in Newport Beach.

Others were Ruby's Diners in Fullerton, Balboa, Costa Mesa, Mission Viejo and Seal Beach; Back Bay Rowing & Running Club, Costa Mesa; Bangkok 4, Costa Mesa; Studio Cafe, Corona del Mar, and Salud! in Huntington Beach.

Chandler said there has been "a tremendous outpouring of concern" from Orange County for victims of the Oct. 17 Bay Area earthquake. He said 200 people from Orange County have worked for the Red Cross in the Bay Area.

In addition, Bill Medley's Oct. 29 earthquake relief benefit show at the Righteous Brothers' Hop in Fountain Valley raised about $5,500, according to Cherlynn Hurdle, office manager at the Hop. The money, earmarked for San Francisco disaster aid, was donated through the Orange County chapter of the American Red Cross. Medley's show featured a guest appearance by Jennifer Warnes for "(I've Had) The Time of My Life," the duo's hit theme from "Dirty Dancing."
