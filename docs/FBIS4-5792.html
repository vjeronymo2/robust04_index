BFN [Text] Fukuoka, April 14 KYODO -- Japanese and South Korean business executives opened a two-day meeting Thursday [14 April] on Japan's westernmost main island of Kyushu to discuss economic cooperation between the two countries. About 300 executives are attending the 26th annual joint meeting of the private Japan-Korea Economic Committee and its South Korean counterpart at a Fukuoka hotel. Nobuya Hagura, chairman of the Japan-Korea Economic Association and adviser to Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank, and Pak Yong-hak, chairman of its South Korean counterpart, made opening speeches at the meeting, the first to be held in Kyushu. The meeting will close Friday, when it issues a joint statement after holding subcommittee sessions on trade, industrial cooperation and various exchanges between the two countries.
