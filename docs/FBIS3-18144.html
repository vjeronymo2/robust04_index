Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] [Passage omitted] [Correspondent Giulio Colavolpe] Trieste is a city that symbolizes Italian unity, and it is where a great, new, scientific machine -- the sincrotrone [as heard] -- has been unveiled. Carlo Azeglio Ciampi chose its inauguration to make an upbeat statement. Just as the chaos surrounding the forthcoming elections seems to have reached its zenith, the prime minister has said that he has no doubts that the machinery has been set in motion and rather than giving up, Italy is on the path to recovery. According to Ciampi the first signs of the recovery can already be seen: [Begin Ciampi recording] What is certain is that the worst of the recession is behind us. There can be no doubts about this in Italy. Nor should the widespread uncertainty about the elections be mistaken for chaos -- it is an essential part of all true democracies.
