April 2, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

ERMA BOMBECK: ANOTHER BIG SPENDER LEAVES THE FOLD

Your son opens the envelope and his first credit card falls out. Is it his first taste of maturity? Or his last meal before execution?

Suddenly, small moments from the past invade the present like a pesky gnat that won't go away. You remember the first time he emptied out his piggy bank and tried to buy a $30 wagon with $1.57. But he was a naive child then. Not to worry.

Later, for no reason, you recall when he bought a $60 sweater for a girl he met in a line to see "Kiss of the Spider Woman," whom he dated for two weeks. If he didn't eat lunch or put gas in the car for three months, he could afford it. But he was only an impulsive teen-ager then. Not to worry.

Then you remember how he borrowed $40 for a concert ticket from you in 1984 and still hasn't paid you back. It's time to worry!

It's all your fault. You piffled away all those years telling him not to talk with food in his mouth and never to sit on a public toilet seat, but you didn't teach him about money. He thinks all you have to do is insert a bank card in a slot in a brick building and it drools 10s and 20s into a little tray.

Hey, he'll be all right. He's older now. Once he sits down and reads all the small print about interest rates, and checks the charges against the slips he's going to file in his desk each month, and keeps tabs on how much he's spending by keeping a little notebook in his billfold, he'll . . . end up in jail. That's what he's going to do.

Remember his first checkbook? He thought it was a scratch pad for phone messages and shopping lists. When he wasn't ripping out one to wrap his gum in, he was scribbling out a check for something minor. He once was clocked at writing 23 checks in one week under $2.

What are you worried about? The credit card will be different. He has only one little piece of plastic to keep track of. . . . Wait a minute. What if he gets more than one credit card!? No one has just one credit card. His name and address are already in a worldwide computer system to alert everyone on this planet that another "big spender" is in tow.

A picture begins to form. He'll charge, charge, charge until all he is paying is interest. He'll get a second job to keep up with his time payments. He won't be able to afford his car. Without a car, he won't get dates. Without a date, he won't get married. Without marriage, you'll never be a grandmother. Why is he ruining your life!?

As he prepares to leave the house, sanity returns. There is no reason in the world why he can't change into a responsible human being who is committed to being accountable for his own actions. Maybe he isn't the flake you thought he was.

As the motor of his car turns over, you see the credit card on top of the toaster. Should you keep the card and sleep at night? Or should you run out and give it to him?

What do you mean, give you a minute to think!?
