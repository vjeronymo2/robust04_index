Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Greece is to send an estimated one billion drachmas worth of military airplanes, helicopters, army trucks, and uniforms to help the Palestinians organize a security force in accordance with the Israeli-PLO accord signed at the White House last year. The offer has been accepted by the Palestinians except for six CH-13 helicopters and two U-17AB reconnaissance planes, considered to be outdated equipment. Defense Minister Yerasimos Arsenis and Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias toured Greek army camps inspecting the material which will be given free as part of the Greek contribution towards peace in the Middle East. PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat made a request for such supplies during a meeting with Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou on his recent visit to Greece.
