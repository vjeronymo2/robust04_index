September 28, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

A HUMORLESS AND ARROGANT OVERREACTION

Getting ticked off because an unreformable old buck uses a double-standard phrase that was, at worst, the equivalent of a burp, showed Molly Yard caricaturing the humorless and hypersensitive side of feminism.

Yard came to the hearings on a mission of wild zealotry, consistent with an abortion-rights fanaticism that led her to say that confirming David Souter would mean "ending freedom for women in this country."

This style of apocalyptic trembling, fit for an embattled speech at an abortion rally, puts Yard over the line that separates authentic social revolutionaries from cause-mongering pop-offs who are so sure of the correctness of their stance that anything slightly askew -- especially the near-harmless remark of an 87-year-old codger -- is not allowed to stand.

In arguing against Souter, Molly Yard had much to say about women's rights and reproductive rights but nothing on fetal rights. She defines women's freedom in a narrowness that she would find intolerable if expressed by the other side. For Yard, women's freedom means full empowerment to kill what is alive and growing, which is a bizarre definition of freedom.

Many credible feminists -- female and male -- reject the violence of abortion because they oppose all forms of violence. Molly Yard doesn't speak for them.
