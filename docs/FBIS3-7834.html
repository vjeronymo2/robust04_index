Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Slovene Foreign Minister Lojze Peterle is paying a visit to Romania today. He was received by his Romanian counterpart Teodor Melescanu. After the talks the sides signed an agreement between the governments of the two countries on cooperation in education, culture, science, and sports and a protocol on cooperation between the two Ministries of Foreign Affairs. According to the statement made by the Romanian minister of foreign affairs, the discussion with his Slovene counterpart has emphasized the continuous improvement of the bilateral relations, particularly regarding trade exchanges. The sides also tackled the need to harmonize the positions of the two countries on the issue of Euro-Atlantic integration and the cooperation among the countries in our region. Mr. Lojze Peterle expressed satisfaction with the documents signed, which he assessed as an important step forward in the cooperation between Romania and Slovenia. He added that he talked with the Romanian foreign minister about the need to speed up the efforts to find new avenues for economic and political cooperation, our correspondent reports. Slovene Foreign Minister Lojze Peterle also had talks at the Ministry of Trade on the current stage and prospects of the trade and cooperation relations between the two countries. He pointed out that, although the transition to the market economy and the embargo imposed on Yugoslavia have affected the bilateral trade exchanges, they nevertheless doubled last year, compared to 1992. The Romanian and Slovene delegations expressed the hope that the two states will shortly sign an accord on economic cooperation, which has already been agreed upon by the experts of the two countries, as well as other agreements and understandings to facilitate trade exchanges and bilateral cooperation. The Slovene foreign minister will also meet with the Romanian president, the prime minister, and the presidents of the two chambers of parliament this evening.
