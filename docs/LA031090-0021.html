March 10, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

IT MAKES MORE SENSE TO MAKE MORE DOLLARS

I couldn't believe what I was reading. Brian Weston (Viewpoint, March 3) wants to get rid of Curtis Crayon, who has picked winners paying $253 more than Bob Mieszerski, and replace him with "someone who knows what he is doing."

Then he calls Mieszerski's picks "rock-solid and professional." A person goes to the track to make as much money as he can, not to see how many winners he can pick.

WALTER HARRIS, Woodland Hills
