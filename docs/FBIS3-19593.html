Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Deric Henderson, PRESS ASSOCIATION] [Text] The Government is blocking the path to peace in Northern Ireland, Sinn Fein President Gerry Adams claimed today. Downing Street's attitude was the major obstacle to ending the current impasse, he said. The process required flexibility and imagination, but the comments of John Major and other Ministers suggested those qualities were lacking in Government circles. Mr. Adams said: "Rather than seeking to remove the roadblocks on the road to peace, Mr. Major and his collegues appear determined to erect more." He welcomed President Clinton's "positive and encouraging" attitude in his St. Patrick's Day address and the continued interest by the U.S. Government in helping the peace process. "This administration has clearly shown its willingness to take a fresh approach to this conflict and the means by which it can be ended," added Mr. Adams. But the Sinn Fein president attacked remarks in the House of Commons by Ulster Secretary Sir Patrick Mayhew who claimed that the Irish Republic's constitutional claim to Northern Ireland was an "anachronism." Mr. Adams asked: "What of Britain's claim of sovereignty? Is the Government of Ireland Act not an issue? Are we to accept that there is no dispute over sovereignty? Has Mr. Mayhew not simply dismissed the national aspirations of Irish nationalists? How can there be equality if our aspirations are anachronistic?"
