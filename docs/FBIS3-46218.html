Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shenzhen, January 21 (XINHUA) -- Land ports in Shenzhen, one of the earliest opened special economic zones in south China's Guangdong Province, will simplify examination procedures to raise efficiency in the near future, a top Chinese official said here today. Yu Xiaosong, vice-minister of the State Economic and Foreign Trade Commission, made the remarks at a conference held at the prompting of the Preparatory Committee for the Hongkong Special Administrative Region under the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress. The conference put forward many constructive proposals on how to improve the management of Shenzhen port and promote the prosperity and stability of Hong Kong. In the spirit of the conference, the land ports in Shenzhen will simplify the procedures for examination of incoming and outgoing vehicles and collect a package fee so as to improve efficiency. Concerned documents will be clarified for public supervision and work hours of ports will be extended. Upon the completion of the Guangzhou-Shenzhen expressway, the Huanggang port will experiment with around-the-clock service. Lu Ping, director of the Hong Kong and Macao Affairs Office under the State Council and some members of the Preparatory Commission for the Hongkong Special Administrative Region attended the conference.
