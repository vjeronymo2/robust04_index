910430 FT 30 APR 91 / Letter: An anomaly in the constitution Sir, What welcome relief toread your leader 'Changing the constitution' (April 26). Electoral reform andespecially fixed term parliaments are vital. There was however one majoromission - the removal of the Monarch's constitutional powers. There continuesto be an anomaly in that the fair society we strive to create is one based on advancement through merit and yet at the same time the head of state is a hereditary position. Which political party will grasp this thorny issue? Julian Rowse, 259 West 10th St, New York
