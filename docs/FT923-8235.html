920812 FT 12 AUG 92 / Californian compromise budget fails THE California legislature, agonising over the largest US state's desperate finances, has failed to pass a compromise budget, Reuter reports from Sacramento. California has been without a budget since June 30, when Governor Pete Wilson and members of the legislature could not agree on a solution to bridge a Dollars 10.7bn (Pounds 5.6bn) spending gap. The legislature held a rare Sunday evening session to debate a Dollars 57.6bn compromise budget, seeking deep cuts in state spending and education. But the measure was not backed by Republicans on Monday. 'I think the situation may be irreconcilable,' Mr John Vasconcellos, a Democratic member of the assembly, said.
