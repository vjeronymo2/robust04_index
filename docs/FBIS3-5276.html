Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Video report by Yasar Kefeli in Australia] [Text] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin's visits to New Zealand and Australia were based on the aim of further improving the existing good relations between Turkey and those countries. Cetin stressed that the concrete results of these visits will be observed in the near future. At the end of his official visits to New Zealand and Australia, Cetin made a statement to Turkish Radio and Television. He said that the political will indeed exists to develop the relations between these countries. He pointed out that his visits were beneficial, and that they showed the importance given to Turkey. Cetin also said that additional agreements might be signed with those countries. [Begin Cetin recording] Turkey, Australia, and New Zealand are members of the western group of the United Nations. New Zealand is also a Security Council member. Australia plans to be a Security Council member in the future. During my trip, we assessed the tasks before the United Nations in the coming period and the viewpoint of the UN Security Council regarding the developments taking place around Turkey. The issues of Bosnia-Herzegovina, Nagorno-Karabakh, the developments in the Middle East, and the embargo on Iraq are issues that closely concern Turkey and that are on the agenda of the United Nations. We held a very close exchange of views on these topics. [end recording]
