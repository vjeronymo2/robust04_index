931021 FT 21 OCT 93 / Anti-graft chief to step down The head of President Boris Yeltsin's anti-corruption commission yesterday said he was quitting and warned Russia's leaders not to miss a rare chance to tackle corruption, writes Leyla Boulton. Mr Andrei Makarov, a prominent lawyer who plans to run for parliament, said he wanted to devote his time to politics. But friends say the real reason is that he is pessimistic about the prospects for fighting corruption, and believes there are too many high-placed officials having a vested interest in seeing his commission disbanded.
