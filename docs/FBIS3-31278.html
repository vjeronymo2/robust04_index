Language: Bulgarian Article Type:BFN [Text] On 15 February in Skopje Macedonian President Kiro Gligorov received Angel Dimitrov, the newly appointed Bulgarian ambassador to Macedonia, who presented his credentials. The meeting was attended by Foreign Minister Stevo Crvenkovski, our reporter Kostadin Filipov reports. [Begin Filipov recording] In his speech Ambassador Angel Dimitrov stressed that Bulgaria had been the first state to recognize the Republic of Macedonia, and the first direct neighbor of Macedonia that had established full diplomatic relations with that state. This is an expression of the Republic of Bulgaria's prudence, farsightedness, and determination to develop relations with the Republic of Macedonia on the basis of mutual respect and sincere friendship -- the only possibility of asserting peace and stability on the Balkan peninsula, Ambassador Dimitrov pointed out. Macedonian President Kiro Gligorov stressed the unique personal contribution of Bulgarian President Zhelyu Zhelev, with whom he said he has relations of sincere friendship and understanding. He also pointed out Macedonia's great interest in maintaining stable and goodneighborly relations with Bulgaria. Mr. Gligorov also expressed Macedonia's interest in developing and strengthening cooperation in all areas of mutual interest and benefit. Now a broad field opens for cultural, economic, scientific, and other cooperation, connecting the two countries with the necessary railroad, modern highways, and telecommunications, Kiro Gligorov stressed. [end recording]
