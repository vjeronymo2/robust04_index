Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Singapore and Pakistan have agreed to further liberalize their air services agreement, the Civil Aviation Authority of Singapore (CAAS) said on 27 January. Effective immediately, the revised agreement allows each country's designated airline to operate four-weekly services using any aircraft type between the two countries. Now Singapore Airlines, SIA, operates one weekly Boeing B747 and two weekly Airbus A310 services to Karachi, with a total weekly seat capacity of 767 seats. For its part, Pakistan International Airlines operates three weekly A310 services to Singapore, providing a total weekly capacity of 204 seats. Both airlines plan to increase their services this year, CAAS said.
