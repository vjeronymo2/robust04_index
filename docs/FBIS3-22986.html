Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuwaiti Parliamentary sources have said that Saddam Husayn's regime is now setting impossible conditions for the release of the Kuwaiti captives in Iraqi prisons. One of these, they said, is the release of the 11 Iraqis now on trial in Kuwait in connection with a plan to assassinate former U.S. President George Bush. The sources said the regime also wants Kuwait not to refer to its prisoners as hostages or detainees, but as missing. The sources said Kuwait responded through the Arab League, saying that the Iraqis who are now standing trial in Kuwait entered their country carrying weapons and bombs (?and tried to carry out) assassinations, bombings, and terrorist acts. They have confessed to this and are now in the hands of the Kuwaiti judiciary. The Kuwaiti captives, on the other hand, are prisoners and [words indistinct] because the tyrant's forces kidnapped them by force in front of their families and friends during the occupation.
