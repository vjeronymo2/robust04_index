BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 20 (KCNA) -- A statement of the DPRK Foreign Ministry on establishing a new peace arrangement was fully supported by organizations and public figures of different countries. The Finland-Korea Association and the Finish Solidarity Committee for Korea's reunification in a letter to the United States administration adopted at a joint meeting, called on it to accept the proposal to establish a new peace arrangement on the Korean peninsula at an early date and show in action its efforts to ensure peace. The Secretariat of the Communist Party of Finland (Unity) in a statement said that the present situation requires the replacement of the Armistice Agreement with a peace agreement immediately and establishment of a new peace arrangement on the Korean peninsula, and expressed the hope that the Korean problem would be solved in a peaceful way in any case. Negotiations to replace the Armistice Agreement with a peace agreement and establish a new peace arrangement for creditably guaranteeing a durable peace and security of the Korean peninsula will open a vista to remove the hostile relations between the DPRK and the United States and resolve the nuclear issue and other complicated problems in this area, the Popular Party for Democracy and Development of Ghana said in a statement. Madhav Kumar Nepal, general secretary of the Central Committee of the Nepal Communist Party (United Marxism-Leninism), pointed out in a statement that they support the proposal for a peace agreement, and expressed the belief that it would help solve the dispute and achieve peace and stability on the Korean peninsula. We call upon the peace-loving people of the world to actively in denouncing the U.S. hostile policy toward Korea join said Dilip Barua, general secretary of the Communist Party of Bangladesh (Marxist-Leninist). He positively supported the just proposal of the DPRK to establish a new peace arrangement which will replace the Armistice Agreement.
