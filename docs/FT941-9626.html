940214 FT 14 FEB 94 / Business Travel (Travel Update): Hotel prices Hotel rooms in Osaka are the most expensive in the world, costing an average Pounds 166 a night, a Hogg Robinson Business Travel survey reports. Tokyo was the second most expensive city, at Pounds 162 a night. Third place was held by New York (Pounds 122), followed by Hong Kong (Pounds 119), Zurich (Pounds 118), Paris (Pounds 114) Frankfurt (Pounds 111) and Brussels (Pounds 109). London emerges as the surprise bargain: average room rates of Pounds 83 put it in 38th place.
