Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Chile monitored through 24 March. The Swedish Government on 10 March donated $5 million to Bolivia. This money will be used to pay a debt to the World Bank. The agreement was signed by Bolivian Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar and Swedish Ambassador to Bolivia Lars Schonander at the Foreign Ministry. (La Paz PRESENCIA in Spanish 11 Mar 94 p 1) The World Bank will grant $94 million to Bolivia in June. Of this, $50 million will be designed for the general budget and to help the government face transition costs. The remainder will be earmarked for the hydrocarbons industry, the Bolivian Government Oil Deposits privatization project, and for technical assistance to the National Electricity Enterprise. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 11 Mar 94 National Page p 1) Pension fund administration boards' profits were $2.4616 billion in 1993, a drop of 46 percent compared with 1992. Last year, six of the 13 boards had losses, compared with two in 1992. Santiago Inc. registered the highest deficit with $218.6 million. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 10 Mar 94 p 16) The Central Bank of Chile termed 1993's economic results satisfactory. Economic growth was 6 percent last year. Construction, commerce, and transportation were the most dynamic sectors. Investment hit a record 27 percent. Production in 1993 totaled $43.6 billion. (Santiago Television Nacional de Chile Imagen Internacional in Spanish 0100 GMT 24 Mar 94)
