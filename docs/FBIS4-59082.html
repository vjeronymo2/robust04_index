BFN [Excerpt] Riyadh, 10 May, KUNA -- Shaykh 'Abdallah al-Ahmar, the speaker of the Yemeni Parliament has said that he is visiting Riyadh to clarify what is happening in Yemen "within the framework of the solid and traditional relations between the two countries." Shaykh al-Ahmar, who arrived in Riyadh tonight, told KUNA: "We have not come to look for mediation." In reply to a question whether he was hoping for a Saudi role to solve the Yemeni crisis, Shaykh al-Ahmar said: "The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia strives to do good deeds always and everywhere." Shaykh al-Ahmar, who is accompanied by the planning minister, 'Abd-al-Karim al-Iryani, is carrying a message for King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, custodian of the two holy mosques, from the Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, on "the developments of the situation in Yemen and issues of mutual interest." Shaykh al-Ahmar has denied the existence of Arab mediation to resolve the conflict, indicating that "since he left Sanaa this afternoon he has not heard of any mediation." He added that "the conflict has been decided militarily after the northern troops reached Shaykh 'Uthman area, situated north of Aden." He further said that the southern air base of al-'Anad had been captured and "it was brought under control this afternoon." [passage omitted]
