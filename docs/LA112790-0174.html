November 27, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: 'RECLUSIVE' REDFORD WILL GET 'REAL' IN TV SPECIAL DEC. 9

ROBERT REDFORD, so often described as "elusive, "reclusive" and "intensely private," will finally lift that veil of secrecy -- a bit -- and reveal a side of him never caught on camera before.

Producers Mark Cowen and Paul Hall are putting the final touches on a TV special for NBC, set to air Dec. 9 at 8 p.m. This will focus on the "real" Robert Redford and also his working relationship with Sydney Pollack. The seventh Redford/Pollack movie collaboration, "Havana," opens, not so coincidentally, on Dec. 10.

Redford has apparently decided to let his vast public finally "meet" him. And to that end he sat for several candid, wide-ranging interviews with Cowen and Hall. The two producers obtained rare film and early TV clips of Redford, including some blooper-type material that shows Redford's seldom seen funny side.

Executives at Universal, who have screened portions of the upcoming special are happy and convinced that it will add not only to Redford's already vast legion of fans, but also to the box-office of "Havana," too.

Oh, yes, Redford will escort his leading lady, Lena Olin, to the premiere of "Havana" in New York. "Please don't read anything into that," my source said. "They are just both in town at the same time and they are starring in the movie together. It's just convenient. OK?" OK.
