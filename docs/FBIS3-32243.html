Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Costa Rican economic developments monitored through 2 February. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. The Central Bank has reported preliminary figures on the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) up through June 1993. The "financial establishments, insurance, and other services" sector grew more -- 12.7 percent -- than all the other sectors that make up the GDP. The GDP grew 6.3 percent; from 13.4336 billion colones in 1992 to 14.3068 billion colones in 1993. The agriculture, hunting, and fishing sector grew 2.5 percent; the industry sector grew 7 percent; electricity and water grew 8 percent; the construction sector grew 6.3 percent; trade grew 8.5 percent; transportation and communications grew 10.6 percent; the real estate sector grew 3.1 percent; the general government sector, 2.5 percent; and the other personal services sector grew 6.1 percent. The Central Bank added that the final yearly figures will be available at the end of 1994. (San Jose LA NACION in Spanish 19 Jan 94, p 28A) Central Bank President Jorge Corrales issued a communique based on data from the IMF saying Costa Rica attained one of the lowest inflation rates in Latin America and the Caribbean in 1993. The communique points out that Costa Rica had a single-digit, 9.05 percent, inflation rate in 1993. (San Jose LA NACION in Spanish 20 Jan 94, p 8A) Central Bank Director Alberto Franco Mejia has stated that, in accordance with the preliminary figures for 1993, the physical capital investment totaled 3.5966 billion colones, which is 15 percent more than the amount registered in the previous period and represents the highest amount in the history of the country. (San Jose LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 27 Jan 94, p 12A) On 2 February, Deputy Economy Minister Gustavo Gutierrez reported the inflation rate for the month of January 1994 was 0.87 percent. He also said the consumer price index accumulated over 1993 was 9.58 percent, 2.42 percent less than the official projections and the lowest of the last 15 years. The official added that the goal for the current year will be 10 percent. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2030 GMT 30 Jan 94)
