BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Kuzmin] [Text] Tehran, 14 May -- The main points on the agenda of the talks that will be held by Kazakhstani Prime Minister Sergey Tereshchenko, who arrived in the Iranian capital this morning on an official three-day visit, will be questions of commercial and economic cooperation between Kazakhstan and Iran. He is accompanied by a delegation that includes the ministers of power engineering and fuel resources, transport, the chairman of the Chamber of Commerce, the chairman of the central bank, and parliamentarians. It is intended that the Kazakhstani prime minister will be received by Iranian President 'Ali Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani. Speaking on his arrival at Tehran's Mehrabad airport, S.Tereshchenko stressed that "very close and friendly relations" exist between Kazakhstan and Iran. He noted that a number of basic documents on bilateral cooperation were signed during the visits by the presidents of Kazakhstan and Iran to Tehran and Almaty. Therefore, the main aim of the present visit is to step up the work to implement the agreements that have been reached already and to remove all obstacles in the way of stepping up commercial and economic cooperation between the two countries. Iranian First Vice President Hasan Habibi, who met Tereshchenko, confirmed that the talks would focus on stepping up the implementation of agreements signed earlier by the two presidents, as well as on other accords.
