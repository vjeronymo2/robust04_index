Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Iranian President `Ali Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani has said Iran fully supports Pakistan's stand on the Kashmir issue. Speaking to the Pakistani prime minister's special emissary, Professor N.D. Khan, in Tehran today, the Iranian president said Pakistan can trust Iran for its support on the Kashmir issue not only at the UN Human Rights Commission in Geneva but in other organizations as well. He said Iran is fully aware of the plight of the Muslims in occupied Kashmir. Earlier, the special emissary handed over to the Iranian president a letter from Ms. Benazir Bhutto, appealing for Iran's support for Pakistan's resolution to be moved at the Human Rights Commission to express concern over the human rights violations in occupied Kashmir. Prof. N.D. Khan also apprised the Iranian leader of the Indian atrocities in occupied Kashmir and told him that India refuses to take steps to halt these atrocities. He also thanked Iran for its traditional and solid support on the Kashmir stand.
