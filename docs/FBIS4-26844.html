BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 25 (XINHUA) -- Kim Yong-sun was named today as the leader of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea's (DPRK) delegation to the forthcoming pre-Korean summit talks at the truce village of Panmunjom. Kim, chairman of the Reunification Policy Committee of the Supreme People's Assembly, will be accompanied at the June 28 meeting by An Pyong-su, vice-chairman of the committee for the Peaceful Reunification of the Fatherland, and Paek Nam-chun, chief councillor of the DPRK Administration Council (cabinet). Details of the delegation membership were given to South Korean Premier Yi Yong-tok by the DPRK Prime Minister Kang Song-san today. The meeting was proposed by Kang on June 20 as a means of organizing detailed arrangements for a North-South summit. The DPRK accepted the proposal two days later. Meanwhile, a DPRK Foreign Ministry spokesman said the outstanding questions between the DPRK and the U.S., including the nuclear issue of the Korean peninsula and other issues, could be resolved in a package deal at the third-round DPRK-U.S. talks. Officials from the two countries will meet in Geneva in the first week of July for talks over a wide range of political and economic issues. However, the spokesman also criticized Japan and South Korea following their public outcry calling for a "special inspection" of the DPRK's military facilities.
