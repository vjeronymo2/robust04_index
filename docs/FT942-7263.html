940526 FT 26 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television For reasons best known to themselves the people who run BBC2 have changed the name of 'Business Matters' to The Business. The new series begins by considering 'the rise and rise of Bill Gates', inventor of the DOS computer system and head of Microsoft. At 38 he is said to possess a personal fortune of seven billion dollars but, as David Lomax reports, is accused of unfair business practices (7.20). That is followed by Wooldridge On Whisky, in which the journalist tells the story of the famous drink. Did you know that Humphrey Bogart on his death bed said 'I should never have switched from Scotch to dry Martinis'? (8.00). The fashion for stretching successful dramas to create comedy series, as with 'Outside Edge', continues in Moving Story. Jack Rosenthal's original work was called 'The Chain' and told the portmanteau tale of a sequence of house moves. In today's episode a new boy joins the moving men (8.30 ITV). Subject of BBC2's series True Brits is the 'shoe string' style of the British embassies in the 15 states which used to form the Soviet Union (9.30).
