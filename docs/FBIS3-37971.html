Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Feb 24, BNS -- Flying to Latvia Wednesday [23 February], Polish President Lech Walesa transmitted a greeting to Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas when the plane was above Lithuanian territory. The Polish president expressed satisfaction that "our foreign ministers have completed preparatory work for the signing of the agreement on friendly relations and neighborly cooperation between the republics of Lithuania and Poland." The accord had been under negotiation for one and a half years. Lithuania and Poland completed the talks Tuesday in Warsaw. So far there have been no reports as to the exact formulas chosen to mention the 1920-39 occupation of Vilnius and its region by Poland. Vaclovas Domarkas, Lithuanian deputy minister of foreign affairs, said after the fifth and final round of talks that the treaty includes "historic moments which trouble us, but no specific names or dates." According to unofficial reports, the accord says that Vilnius has always been the historic capital of the Lithuanian state.
