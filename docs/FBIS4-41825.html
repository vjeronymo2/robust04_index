BFN [Text] Chisinau BASAPRESS, 24/6/1994 -- The chief of the Federal Intelligence Service of Russia, Sergey Stepashin [name as received], arrived today in Chisinau. Sources from the Moldovan Ministry of National Security stated to BASAPRESS that the Russian dignitary is in Chisinau to sign an agreement of cooperation between the security services of both states. The draft agreement stipulates the coordination of joint activities in combating terrorism, smuggling, drug business, and illegal armament trade. The parties also intend to come to an agreement concerning joining forces in combating the illegal immigration of citizens of underdeveloped countries to Western Europe, by using the territories of Russia and Moldova as transit stations. The same sources announced to BASAPRESS that Sergey Stepashin will stay in Moldova two days and will meet President Mircea Snegur and Premier Andrei Sangheli. Stepashin also intends to pay a visit to Tiraspol. No terrorist acts were committed this year in Moldova. In 1993, the Moldovan frontier guards prevented the attempts of dozens of illegal immigrants to traverse Moldova.
