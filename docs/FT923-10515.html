920730 FT 30 JUL 92 / UK Company News: PT Inco blames reduced metal prices for 56% drop LOWER metal prices and reduced deliveries led to a 56 per cent fall in second-quarter net earnings, at PT International Nickel Indonesia (PT Inco). Net earnings were USDollars 8.6m, or 4 cents a share, compared with Dollars 19.4m, or 8 cents, in the second quarter last year. Net earnings for the first half of 1992 were down by 43 per cent from Dollars 29m, or 12 cents, to Dollars 16.4m, or 7 cents. PT Inco, which was floated on the Jakarta stock exchange in April 1990 but is still 58 per cent owned by Inco of Canada, said second-quarter nickel production fell to 18.5m lb from 19.7m lb because of a planned rebuilding and upgrading of one of its three electric furnaces. Nickel deliveries fell to 16.8m lb from 21.3m lb. The company's net realised price for nickel in matte - an intermediate product - in the second quarter was Dollars 2.61 a lb compared with Dollars 2.67 in the first quarter and Dollars 3.07 in the second quarter of 1991. Nickel stocks, which were 1.4m lb at the end of March, had risen to 3.1m lb at the end of the second quarter. Net sales in the quarter fell from Dollars 66m to Dollars 44.6m and in the half year from Dollars 111.2m to Dollars 86m.
