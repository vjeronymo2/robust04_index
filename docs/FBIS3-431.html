Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Focus on Africa" program] [Text] The veteran president of Malawi, Hastings Banda, has made a rare public appearance, attending a church service in the city of Blantyre. Dr. Banda went to a Roman Catholic church to commemorate those killed during the campaign for independence from Britain. Dr. Banda, who is officially in his late 80s, but widely believed to be much older, underwent brain surgery in South Africa last year. He returned home in October and was last seen in public on 1 January. Dr. Banda has ruled Malawi for some 30 years and he is a candidate in the country's first multiparty elections due to be held in May.
