November 26, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

PICTURING CALIFORNIA

The Ansel Adams photograph, "Winter Sunrise, The Sierra Nevada, from Lone Pine, Calif.," shown as part of "Picturing California" (Sept. 3), reminded me of the absolutely magnificent views of that mountain range that can be seen from Highway 395 and the incredibly talented eye possessed by Adams. However, if the camera is looking west toward the north-south Sierra Nevada range from Lone Pine, the rays of the morning sun should be coming from behind. Can you shed any light on this quirk of nature?

SHEL ERLICH

Van Nuys

The correct title of the photo is "Mount Williamson, The Sierra Nevada, From Manzanar, California, 1945."

-- The Editors
