BFN [Text] Cairo, 27 Apr (MENA)--AL-AHRAM newspaper says that developments in the Bosnia-Herzegovina issue indicate the failure of the U.S. plan to increase pressure on the Serbs by normalizing relations between the Muslims and Croats and pushing them into a confederation which the Serbs would later be compelled to join. In today's editorial, the paper notes that since an agreement was signed between the Muslims and Croats in Washington in March, fighting between the Muslims and Serbs has intensified, and it seems obvious that NATO's ability to intervene militarily against the Bosnian Serbs has limitations. The paper says the Russian Federation foiled the first ultimatum by deploying its forces on the hills above Sarajevo, while the Serbs foiled the second ultimatum by signing an accord with the United Nations on a withdrawal from Gorazde. Before they withdrew, however, they burned most of the city. The paper concludes that it is necessary for the international community, represented by the United Nations and the European Union, to agree on a formula acceptable to the three parties by which they will return to the negotiating table in order to produce a cease-fire and then unconditionally discuss solutions. The newspaper expresses its belief that the arena must not be left for the United States to use as a guinea pig to test its relations with the Russian Federation.
