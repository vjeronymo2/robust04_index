920904 FT 04 SEP 92 / Japan takes soft line on Beijing Mr Kiichi Miyazawa, the Japanese prime minister, last night went further than any western leader in accepting Beijing's intransigent attitude to criticism of its human rights record, Our Foreign Staff writes. 'According to the Chinese, the most fundamental human right is to be able to live, not to die of hunger, to have a house to live in . . . I think it is half true,' he said. After much agonising, Japan last month accepted an invitation for Emperor Akihito to visit China in October. The Emperor is expected to acknowledge Japanese guilt for wartime brutality, while stopping short of an outright apology.
