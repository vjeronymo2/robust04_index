931020 FT 20 OCT 93 / World News in Brief: IRA offers concessions Gerry Adams (left), leader of Sinn Fein, the political wing of the IRA, said the Republican movement was ready to make concessions to bring an end to 24 years of violence in Ulster and that he wanted to see the IRA 'in permanent retirement'. Prime minister John Major will hear from the Irish government next week details of the recent peace initiative created by Mr Adams and John Hume of the Social Democratic and Labour party.
