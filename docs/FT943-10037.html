940808 FT 08 AUG 94 / Union demands pensions payouts Thirty-six pensions companies will today receive letters from the GMB general union demanding compensation for alleged bad pensions advice given to some of its members, Chris Tighe writes. Ninety cases relating to the companies have been selected from hundreds of calls to the union's pensions helpline, set up in May to assist members who believe they may have been wrongly advised on opting out of company schemes and into personal pensions. Mr John Edmonds, the union's general secretary, has also asked the Serious Fraud Office to investigate the cases. The SFO said it was considering the matter. The union says it wants each case to be thoroughly examined by the pensions companies, and full compensation - likely, it estimates, to exceed Pounds 1m in total - to be provided. The union said the 'worst culprit' among its 90 cases was the Prudential with 17. The Prudential said it had yet to receive any evidence from the union. The transfer of active members of company schemes to new schemes had never been its policy, it said, adding: 'We believe we have behaved properly and well within the regulators' rules and regulations.'
