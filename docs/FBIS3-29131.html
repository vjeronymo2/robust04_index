Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 15 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa downplayed concern Tuesday [15 February] over threatened retaliation by the United States against Japan over what is viewed as its closed mobile phone market. Hosokawa said, "the basic relationship between Japan and the U.S. will not be impaired if trade ties deteriorate a bit." In the wake of the breakdown of the bilateral "framework" trade talks, U.S. President Bill Clinton said Monday he is weighing up the possibility of introducing sanctions against Japan, because of its restricted foreign entry in the cellular phone market.
