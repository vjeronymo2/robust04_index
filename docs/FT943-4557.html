940908 FT 08 SEP 94 / Observer: Sinned against One can begin to understand why Democratic Unionist Ian Paisley has become a trifle jumpy recently when the International Herald Tribune styles Sinn Fein boss Gerry Adams as 'the radical republican leader of Northern Ireland'. In the European elections in June, Paisley polled 29 per cent and Sinn Fein 10 per cent of the vote in the province. But then who's counting anyway?
