Language: Urdu Article Type:CSO [News Report: "Attempt To Smuggle 63 Kilograms of Heroin Foiled"] [Text] Rawalpindi, 17 December (PPA)--The antinarcotics task force has foiled a smuggling attempt and has recovered a huge quantity of heroin valued at millions of rupees in the foreign market. According to a press release issued by the task force, this heroin was being smuggled from the tribal areas of Sarhad. The task force personnel recovered 63 kilograms of high grade heroin hidden in a jeep with fake license plates. This raid was led by Brigadier Ali Akbar, regional head in Peshawar. It should be remembered that the real owner of the jeep, who was riding in another car 30 kilometers away, was also arrested on identification by the driver. Investigations are going on and a "big fish" is believed to be behind this crime. They expect to unmask and arrest that person.
