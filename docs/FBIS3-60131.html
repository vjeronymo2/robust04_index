Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] The Cali Cartel is as powerful in Peru as it is in Colombia. According to intelligence reports, the organization operates by bribing the authorities who try to fight them. The very few who have dared to report on them are journalists. Myriam Ortiz brings us a report, done with the cooperation of Peru's Channel 9: [Begin Ortiz recording] According to authorities, the so-called Cali Cartel also has a well-structured organization in Peru known as Los Calenos. Waldo Vargas, alias El Ministro, is considered the main leader. He purchases drugs worth $144 million every year. Vargas, born in Colombia, is the lord and master of the Bajo Huallaga area. He has gone as far as using Peruvian Air Force helicopters to transport drugs, according to information given to the general command. Demetrio Limoniel Chavez, also known as El Vaticano, is the other leader of the organization. It is estimated that he buys $216 million worth of drugs every year. El Vaticano works with his brother Humberto, also known as Calavera, who manages the landing strips, but his main storage center is in Palma Pampa, Ayacucho, from where the flights leave for Colombia. Berta Chavez, also known as La Calena, is El Vaticano's sister, and she markets 5 tons of coca paste each month. She was arrested in September 1993, but a judge released her recently.
