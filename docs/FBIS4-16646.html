BFN [Text] Russian Vice Premier Sergey Shakhray said there was no alternative to the idea of national accord proposed by the Russian president. He stressed in an interview with an Interfax correspondent in Novosibirsk that this provided for real accord aimed at restoring the political and economic stability in Russia and not just for a one-time peace agreement between different political parties and movements. Shakhray is currently in Novosibirsk where he is taking part in a scientific conference on regional economic issues on the invitation of the local administration. Shakhray pointed out that many political parties and movements, their factions in the Federal Assembly and representatives of the regional authorities were struggling for spheres of influence in Russia's energy, military-industrial and agrarian complexes. Shakhray believes that the president and the government must act as mediators in order not to let any political forces worsen the situation in the Russian economy. Shakhray emphasized that only this agreement could bring economic stability to Russia.
