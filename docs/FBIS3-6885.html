Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, March 22 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The central newspaper "ARDIYN ERH" informs that the State Great Hural spring session finished consideration of the draft law on currency. The draft law was adopted by the majority of votes and it was decided to call it as the law on regulation of currency. The law will come into force from 1 May. At the same time adopted was the resolution of the State Great Hural on measures on realization of this law. At the yesterday's sitting of the State Great Hural, consideration of the issue about making amendments and additions in the law on elections began. This issue has been submitted to the parliament by the President of Mongolia P. Ochirbat. The newspaper informs that the Mongolian delegation headed by the vice-chairman of the State Great Hural, deputy chairman of the Mongolian parliamentary group J. Gombojab has left Ulaanbaatar for Paris to attend the regular conference of the Interparliamentary Union. The newspaper informs also that the Vice-Premier of Mongolia L. Enebish left on a visit to Hungary, Czechia and Belgium. L. Enebish is planning to get acquainted with the experience of the above-mentioned countries in the sphere of state governing and market relations. The newspaper carries an interview of its correspondent with the deputy chairman of the standing emergency commission Major-General G. Damdinsuren about the situation in animal-husbandry. G. Damdinsuren points out to difficult weather conditions in Tub Aymag, where there was a heavy snow-fall. As G. Damdinsuren notes there will be great difficulties in breeding cattle this spring. The newspaper publishes an article by the State Great Hural member T. Turmandah apropos of the statements made by the representatives of the opposition parties in the parliament, concerning involvement of some members of the state leadership in corruption. T. Turmandah considers these accusations as groundless. The newspaper "HUMUUN BICHIG," which is issued by the MONTSAME agency in the old Mongolian script informs about the sitting of the state commission on preparation for introduction of the traditional Mongolian script in official clerical work. In the information it is noted that the Mongolian script will be used in clerical work and official correspondence from 1995. The newspaper acquaints the readers with reacclimatization of wild horses-tahi in the primordial places of their inhabitance. Wild horse-tahi is at present only in zoological gardens of foreign countries. These countries have sent to Mongolia about 20 wild horses, which are being acclimatized in the Gobi regions of the country. The newspaper continues publishing the exercises for those learning the old Mongolian script, texts for reading.
