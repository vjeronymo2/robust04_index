930226 FT 26 FEB 93 / Parliament and Politics: Move on service charges rejected THE government yesterday rejected recommendations that would have prevented restaurants adding additional service and cover charges to bills. A committee set up by the government to monitor the pricing provisions of the Consumer Protection Act recommended last year that compulsory cover and service charges should be incorporated into the prices of individual items on the menu. This would give customers a clear idea of what the final bill would be. Baroness Denton, consumer affairs minister, said yesterday that further regulation was not the best way forward. She said she would prefer to rely on a voluntary approach and customers' common sense. She added: 'I know that payment for service charges can sometimes cause difficulties and confusion but I am not convinced that further legislation is the answer. We are looking to reduce rather than increase regulation. I would prefer to rely on the responsibility and good sense of those in the industry.'
