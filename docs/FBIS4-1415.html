BFN [Text] Durban April 28 SAPA -- A helicopter from 15 Squadron in Durban left Newcastle on Thursday to distribute ballot papers throughout the rural areas of northern Natal, SABC [South African Broadcasting Corporation] radio news reports. An estimated 600,000 ballot papers will be distributed, following a massive shortage in the area. Helicopters have also been sent to southern Natal where a shortage of papers caused major problems on Wednesday. Meanwhile angry voters were staging a sit-in at Nqutu in northern Natal in protest against the disorganisation at polling stations. Very little voting has taken place in the area. At Charlestown on the Natal/Transvaal border, voting only started at 5PM on Wednesday while voting only got underway at Ingogo near Newcastle at 3PM.
