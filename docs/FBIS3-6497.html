Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington--U.S. CIA Director James Woolsey on 17 March denounced North Korea as one of the states involved directly and indirectly in exporting mass lethal weapons and terrorism. Appearing on the NBC television network that day, Director Woolsey pointed out North Korea, which recently refused a full-scale inspection by the International Atomic Energy Agency, as being such a state, along with Iran, Iraq, and Libya. He stressed that these two points, including the export of terrorism, will possibly impose a serious threat to us, and therefore, these points should be dealt with at top priority. Director Woolsey again revealed "the fact that North Korea has extracted plutonium capable of manufacturing at least more than one nuclear weapon is the view of the majority of U.S. intelligence authorities." He said that "there is a possibility that North Korea will threaten peace in Northeast Asia and that North Korea is a state which proliferates weapons, thus posing a serious problem."
