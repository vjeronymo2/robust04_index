Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, January 14 (XINHUA) -- Russia, the United States and Ukraine signed here today a tripartite agreement on eliminating nuclear weapons on Ukrainian territory. Well-informed sources told reporters that the accord was signed by Russian President Boris Yeltsin, U.S. President Bill Clinton and Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk in the Kremlin Palace this morning. Meanwhile, the three leaders reaffirmed "the non-nuclear status of Ukraine and provided mechanisms of the withdrawal of nuclear warheads from Ukraine to Russia, as well as compensation to Ukraine for the warheads turned over to Russia," the sources said. There are 176 nuclear missiles and about 1,500 warheads in the former second-largest Soviet Republic. Yeltsin and Clinton told Kravchuk that "Russia and the United States will guarantee the security of Ukraine as a non-nuclear state," the sources added. The three leaders began their tripartite summit talks at St. Catherine's Hall of the Grand Kremlin Palace at 09:00 a.m., and signed the accord in accordance with the results of the talks.
