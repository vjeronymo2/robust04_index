BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Akimov] [Text] Almaty 7 June TASS -- An overwhelming majority of experts do not believe in the reforming ability of the Kazakh Parliament and many do not rule it out that it will be dissolved soon, according to an opinion poll taken by the Almaty Centre for Humanitarian Studies among Kazakh political scientists, leaders of public movements and political parties, ranking civil servants and prominent journalists. The findings of the poll, published on Tuesday [7 June], indicate that 61.8 percent of respondents believe that the Kazakhstan parliament in its present composition will not be able to act as a catalyst of reforms in society, 44.1 percent of experts maintain that the deputies corps has a low level of political culture, and 41.2 percent explain their attitude by the parliamentarians' estrangement from the electorate due to various kinds of irregularities during the parliamentary elections and the imperfection of the electoral system. The opinion of 61.8 percent of experts is that the election campaign was of an utterly undemocratic nature, and 44.1 percent of respondents do not rule out the probability that the Kazakh Parliament will be dissolved soon.
