Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Durban March 1 SAPA -- Inkatha Freedom Party [IFP] leader Mangosuthu Buthelezi arrived at a Durban hotel at about 9.45am Tuesday [1 March] for a meeting with African National Congress [ANC] President Nelson Mandela, indicating that much of their talks would centre on violence. Mr Buthelezi told the media as he arrived: "The matter concerns us very deeply ... we haven't come here solely to discuss the elections." The IFP president refused to answer questions about his party's threat to boycott the elections, saying his standpoint on the issue was well known. In anticipation of talks with the ANC president, Mr Buthelezi said: "I don't expect concessions from Mr Mandela and he shouldn't expect concessions from me." Later he said the meeting was important "by virtue of the fact of our constituencies". "Both of us love our country and both of us have struggled for it in different ways." Mr Buthelezi said their supporters should benefit by the culmination of their struggle.
