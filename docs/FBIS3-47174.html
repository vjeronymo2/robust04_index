Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hakodate, Hokkaido, Jan. 10 KYODO -- Grains of rice which may have been discolored by mold have been found in an emergency shipment of rice from Thailand, Hokkaido Prefectural Government officials said Monday [10 January]. The discolored rice was discovered in a 5,775-ton shipment of long-grain rice for food processing that arrived from Bangkok at Hakodate Port on January 1. Inspectors found the discolored rice in some of the 50-kilogram bags that are being unloaded from a Panamanian-registered freighter. A total of 1,400 tons of the rice had been unloaded through Sunday. The Hakodate Bureau of the Hokkaido Food Office was not certain if the discolored rice was like that when it left Thailand or if it became discolored during the trip to Japan. The officials said that the rice was not discolored by the kind of mold caused by condensation that was found in imported rice from Thailand at the port of Maizuru, Kyoto Prefecture last month.
