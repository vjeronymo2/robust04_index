February 9, 1989, Thursday, Late Final Edition

SPORTS;

SLOOP CLOSES IN ON 135-YEAR RECORD

Thursday's Child, a sloop on pace to smash the 135-year New York-San Francisco sailing record, entered Southern California waters today with not far to go.

The modern three-man, 60-foot racing yacht was seeking to beat the record of 89 days and eight hours for the 14,500-mile journey around treacherous Cape Horn at the tip of South America. Set in 1854 by the legendary three-masted clipper ship Flying Cloud, it is the oldest existing speed record.

Thursday's Child reached the latitude of San Diego early today and was on target to enter San Francisco's Golden Gate on Friday or early Saturday. The vessel left New York Nov. 23 and has only to reach San Francisco by 2 a.m. on Feb. 21 to break the record.
