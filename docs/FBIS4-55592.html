BFN [Text] Brcko, May 17 (TANJUG) -- Bosnian Moslem and Croat forces on Tuesday attacked Bosnian Serb positions in an attempt to cut off the Serb land corridor in the Posavina region near Brcko in northern Bosnia. Around 15:00 hours local time on Tuesday, Moslem forces fired 15 82-mm shells on Serb positions in Begovaca in the Brcko area, Bosnian Serb army sources said in a statement. The Bosnian Serb army did not return fire, it said. Croat units in Orasje, Posavina, opened fire from anti-aircraft machine guns and anti-tank weapons on Serb settlements of Lesnici and Jenjici, the statement said, adding that the Serb side again did not return fire. Croatian and especially Moslem forces have massed heavily armed units around Brcko, a town which is situated in the narrowest part of the Serb land corridor linking the eastern and western parts of the Bosnian Serb republic. Moslem and Croat military commanders have announced an offensive on this part of the Serb corridor. Earlier this month, Moslem forces launched artillery attacks on central Brcko, killing four civilians and wounding 15, which was confirmed by monitors of the United Nations Protection Force (UNPROFOR).
