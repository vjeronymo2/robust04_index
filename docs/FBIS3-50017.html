Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 17/1/1994 -- Romania's Government will direct its efforts simultaneously toward the consolidation of its 1993 achievements, new short-term objectives and the creation of the conditions required by the medium and long-term objectives of the Romanian diplomacy, Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu told ROMPRES. The priorities of the Romanian foreign policy, defined through the correlation between the national interests and the developments in Europe and the world over, include: 1. Continuation of efforts for Romania's integration in the political, strategic and economic Western-European and Euro-Atlantic structures, aiming to consolidate Romania's position as factor of stability in the region, in the context of the geo-political evolution in the zone. 2. Development of bilateral relations with a particular stress on the relations with states with long-standing democratic traditions and powerful economies, able to support the democratization process and reform in Romania. The promotion of good neighborly relations, as well as of Romania's traditional ties with countries outside Europe. Special stress will be set on the development of bilateral relations in the economic field. 3. Continuation of the special relations with the Republic of Moldova aimed at deepening economic integration and the consolidation of the cultural and spiritual common space as essential premises for accomplishing the natural aspirations of all Romanians. 4. Further more active participation within the European and universal structures, giving special attention to the observance of human rights, including the rights of individuals belonging to ethnic minorities, rejecting any xenophobe, anti-semite and racist approaches. 5. Promotion of a policy of close relations with international financial bodies, especially with the International Monetary Fund, the World Bank and the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development. 6. Assertion of the Romanian spirituality in the international arena through the elaboration and implementation of a widely- covering program to present the national values. 7. Strengthening of relations with Romanians living abroad, in concordance with constitutional provisions, luring the energies of the Romanian diaspora in supporting the reform and the democratization of the Romanian society. 8. Active participation in international efforts aiming to define new concepts and adopt new standards, especially in human rights, including the persons belonging to ethnic minorities.
