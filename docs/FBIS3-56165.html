Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] A significant number of the inhabitants of Kutaisi [Georgia's second city] have remained without electric power and water. Naturally, inhabitants of rayons in western Georgia are unable to watch television. However, the situation has become even more complex during the last four-five months. Last night, for almost six hours, Kutaisi and its environs were transformed, as they say, into a "dead" zone. Electricity was cut, all work came to a halt, and telephone communications were severed. In short, the city and its 300,000 inhabitants are experiencing the most difficult conditions. There is, in general, no natural gas; it is not possible to supply the minimum amount of electric power; water is being supplied barely once a week. [passage omitted] In spite of the situation yesterday, all municipal police employees are honorably fulfilling their duties. They are displaying particular vigilance in such extreme conditions. One can state that Kutaisi's No. 1 and No. 2 bakeries have diesel generators on permanent standby and, as a result, the city is being supplied with bread. Work has not stopped at the bread mill. In short, Kutaisi is in an extremely difficult situation. [passage omitted] Kutaisi has submitted a request to the Cabinet of Ministers that it nevertheless be supplied with electric power according to the schedule and that the city receive natural gas once a week. It is impossible for such a big city to remain paralyzed throughout the entire winter.
