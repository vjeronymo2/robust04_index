921007 FT 07 OCT 92 / Cassell acquires Victor Gollancz CASSELL, the publishing company best known for reference books, has acquired Victor Gollancz from Gollancz Holdings, a subsidiary of US publishers Houghton Mifflin, Raymond Snoddy writes. The deal will extend Cassell's publishing range into areas such as general fiction, science fiction and children's fiction. No price was disclosed for the deal. Last year Cassell had sales of Pounds 14.7m. The present company was formed in 1986 when a consortium bought the Cassell name and a variety of publishing interests from CBS.
