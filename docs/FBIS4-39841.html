BFN [Report by KAZTAG-TASS correspondent Ivan Zakharchenko] [Text] Almaty 10 June TASS -- President of Kazakhstan Nursultan Nazarbayev held talks on Friday [10 Jun] with the Estonian delegation headed by President Lennart Meri who came on a working visit to Almaty. When discussing various aspects of a foreign policy both sides expressed identity or closeness of positions and views in all issues. Specifically, the Estonian president said that he liked Nursultan Nazarbayev's idea of establishing a Eurasian alliance as it is based on democratic principles inherent in the European Union which has been functioning successfully for a long time. The talks ended by the signing of a treaty on mutual understanding and cooperation between Kazakhstan and Estonia, an agreement on cooperation between Ministries of Foreign Affairs of both countries. On 12 June the Estonian delegation is due to leave Almaty for the People's Republic of China.
