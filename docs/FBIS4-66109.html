BFN [Text] Berlin -- Dieter Schulze, 54, has been nominated as the official candidate to succeed Heinz-Werner Meyer, the leader of the German Trade Union Federation [DGB] who died two weeks ago. The chairmen of the individual trade unions decided at a special session today to present Schulte at the DGB Congress scheduled to take place in Berlin between 13 and 17 June as candidate for the chairmanship. DPA learned this from participants in the special session. The 54-year-old official is a member of the Executive Committee of the Metal Workers Trade Union and responsible for the steel industry and for codetermination. The trade union chairmen rejected a crucial vote at the DGB congress between Schulte and DGB Deputy Chairwoman Ursula Engelen-Kefer.
