930514 FT 14 MAY 93 / Parliament and Politics: Plan for 'viable' post offices TORY backbenchers joined with opposition MPs last night in urging the government not to undermine the Post Office network, particularly in rural areas, by channelling the payment of pensions and other benefits through banks and building societies. Mr Neil Hamilton, corporate affairs minister, said a study by the Department of Social Security of different methods of payment had yet to be evaluated. Earlier, the prime minister said the government aimed to 'encourage' automated credit transfers while maintaining a 'viable' network of Post Office outlets.
