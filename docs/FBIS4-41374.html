BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Irina Shchegoleva] [Text] Sochi, 23 Jun -- The Georgian and Abkhazian sides will ask the peacekeeping forces to speed up the mine clearing operations in the area where the pylons of the Inguri GES [hydroelectric power station] power transmission lines are situated. The pylons have been damaged as a result of recent subversive operations. Feliks Kovalev, Russian special envoy, told an ITAR-TASS correspondent today that an accord to this effect was reached in the course of the second session of the Georgian-Abkhazian coordinating commission in charge of economic issues, held with the participation of the Russian Federation and UN representatives in Sochi. "We also asked the Abkhazian delegation to lift the restrictions on power supplies for Georgia, introduced by the Abkhazian side as part of a package of sanctions," Feliks Kovalev further said. The session also examined issues of restoring the power supply lines that cross the territory of Abkhazia. According to the Russian diplomat, an accord was reached to set up a group of experts that will estimate the extent of damage done to the power supply lines. According to Feliks Kovalev, "certain difficulties emerged" during the discussion of the restoration of through road and rail services.
