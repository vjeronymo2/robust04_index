Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] In Port-au-Prince, a movement led by Attorney Mireille Durocher Bertin for the integration of Haitian Women, at a ceremony at the El Rancho Hotel, set as its objectives establishing a long chain of solidarity among Haitian women of all social classes. The organization aims for real integration of women into society, promotion of Haitian culture, returning self-confidence to women through specific programs to provide them access to health, education, and productivity. The inauguration of the Movement for Integration of the Haitian Woman [Mouvement pour L'Integration de la Femme Haitienne] was marked by a reception at the El Rancho Hotel. On this occasion, presentations were made on the situation of women in Haiti, their role in the economy and in the family. Researcher Gilbert Valme calls for a new social contract between the patriarchal and matriarchal systems, a way, according to Mr. Valme, of assuring a better future for Haitian children. Other speakers, engineer Ginette Mathurin and Attorney Mireille Durocher Bertin, spoke of the role of women in the economy, in the peasant environment, and of their legal status. [passage omitted]
