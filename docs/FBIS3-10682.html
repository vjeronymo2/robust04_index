Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] A meeting on anti-aircraft defense was held at unit no. 4790 of the Western Army, the best of its kind in Cuba. The purpose of the meeting was to analyze the new types of preparation for command and control elements under conditions of the special period. Furthermore, they analyzed the action to be taken by anti-aircraft defense regiments to repel air strikes while under a military blockade, and to resist systematic demoralization [desgaste sistematico] efforts. The [words indistinct] localization and pursuit of enemy targets, and the preparation of the reserves and Territorial Troops Militia were other issues discussed in this important meeting. Lieutenant Colonel Enrique Garces Montesino highlights the reasons this unit was selected as the site for the meeting. [Begin Garces recording] During 1991, 1992, and 1993, this regiment achieved the distinction of being the best anti-aircraft defense regiment because of the results obtained in combat training and political preparation, which was demonstrated during an inspection carried out by the Ministry of the Revolutionary Armed Forces in the western territory in 1993. It was rated good, and superior. It was also selected because of its achievements in the food program, conservation and protection of equipment and armaments, and in improvement of its personnel's living conditions. [end recording]
