Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Michal Strak, the head of the Council of Ministers office, commenting on Lech Walesa's strong reaction to the Sejm's rejection of the presential draft laws regarding the so-called citizens' initiative on the Constitution, said that the president's officials had prepared this initiative badly and no one from the Belweder had discussed the draft with the Polish Peasant Party's parliamentary floor group. Strak was speaking at a news conference in Gdansk. Earlier, Minister Strak held a meeting with students--members of the Political Ideas Club [Klub Mysli Politycznej]. During this meeting, he said, among other things, that his favorite hero was General Kutuzov, who, he said, slept during political and wartime debates but always woke up at the right moment.
