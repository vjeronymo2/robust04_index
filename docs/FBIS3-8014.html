Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu, heading a Romanian government delegation, has begun a one-day official visit in Bratislava at the invitation of his Slovak counterpart Vladimir Meciar. At this hour the two delegations, headed by the Slovak and the Romanian prime ministers, are holding a first round of talks, our correspondent, Pavel Mocanu, reports. Today at 1030, at the headquarters of Slovakia's Council of Ministers, the Romanian guests were received by Prime Minister Meciar, Jozef Prokes, deputy chairman of the Council of Ministers, and other members of the government. The agenda of the visit also includes meetings with Slovak President Michal Kovac and Parliament Speaker Ivan Gasparovic. Following the conclusion of their talks, the two prime ministers are to sign a memorandum on the liberalization of commercial ties between Romania and Slovakia and accords on the avoidance of double taxation and the mutual promotion and protection of investments. Flying over the territory of Hungary, Vacaroiu sent a telegram to his Hungarian counterpart, Boross, cordially greeting him and wishing prosperity for the Hungarian people.
