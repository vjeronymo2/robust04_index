Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Mikhail Pogorelyy report: "There Are Problems, But They Can Be Resolved"] [Text] The practical aspects of cooperation between Russia and the United States in questions concerning the conversion of the defense industry and nuclear disarmament were the main theme of the talks between Russian Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin and U.S. Defense Secretary William Perry 18 March in Moscow. In particular, projects to supply American technologies for the construction of residential accommodation in Russia, including accommodation for servicemen, were discussed. After all, the head of the Russian Government, along with American Vice President Albert Gore, is co-chairman of the commission for economic and scientific-technical cooperation between our countries, and also for implementing the conversion of military production. A meeting involving Andrey Kokoshin, Russian Federation first deputy defense minister, Colonel General Mikhail Kolesnikov, chief of the Russian Federation Armed Forces General Staff, and U.S. Defense Secretary William Perry took place 18 March. At the meeting, the discussion of the issues examined at the meeting on the previous day between Russian Federation Army General Pavel Grachev and William Perry was continued. In particular, talk centered on the reinforcement of the ABM Treaty, the eradication of chemical weapons, the halting of nuclear explosions by all countries possessing nuclear weapons, and the prevention of nuclear weapons proliferation. On the previous day there had been meetings with Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and later, in the State Duma, with representatives of the parliamentary committees for defense and security and for international affairs. Attention focused on questions linked with Russia's expected accession to the NATO "Partnership for Peace" program. In the afternoon the U.S. defense secretary left Russia.
