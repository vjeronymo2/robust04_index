BFN [By Flor Wang] [Text] Taipei, May 27 (CNA) -- Nikolai I. Ryzkov, former prime minister of the now defunct Soviet Union, arrived in Taipei Friday [27 May] for a week-long visit. Ryzkov, accompanied by his wife and a six-member party, is here at the invitation of the Republic of China [ROC] & Russia International Association for Humanitarian Dialogue. Cheng Po-chiu, West Asian affairs director of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, officially greeted the Ryzkov delegation at Chiang Kai-shek International Airport. Ryzkov, 65, who was first elected as secretary-general of the Soviet Communist Party by Mikhail Gorbachev in 1985 before being elevated to the post of prime minister, resigned in 1990 after objecting to the 500-day economic reform program launched by the Soviet Government. During his stay here, Ryzkov will call on President Li Teng-hui, Premier Lien Chan, and Foreign Affairs Minister Chien Fu. He will also deliver a speech at the Legislative Yuan and hold seminars with local business leaders.
