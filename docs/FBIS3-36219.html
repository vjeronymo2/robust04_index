Language: Estonian Article Type:BFN [Text] Estonia today joined NATO's Partnership for Peace program, and as with many other eastern European countries, stated its wish to eventually become a full member of the North Atlantic bloc. Estonian Foreign Minister Juri Luik signed the partnership agreement at the NATO headquarters in Brussels. Estonia sees its possible future NATO member status as part of a wider European integration framework, Luik said at the official signing ceremony. The foreign minister noted that the Baltic countries are at the forefront in Europe in the fight for democracy and stability, and confirmed that reports coming from Moscow [as heard] cause anxiety in Estonia. Luik also stated that Estonia wants to have good relations with a democratic Russia.
