Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By correspondents Wang Yadong (3769 0068 2639) and Li Houzhi (2621 0624 1807)] [Text] Khartoum, 11 Jan (XINHUA) -- In an interview with XINHUA reporters today, Sudanese Foreign Minister Husayn Abu-Salih spoke highly of the friendly relations between Sudan and China. Salih stated: Despite a series of changes which have taken place in the international situation, Sudanese-Chinese relations are still enjoying healthy development. This is a result of upholding the five principles of peaceful coexistence in their foreign policies. Salih added: The forthcoming visit to Sudan by Chinese State Council Vice Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen will more effectively promote the development of friendly relations between the two countries. The Sudanese people are proud of their friendship with China. He pointed out: Sudanese-Chinese friendly relations have a long history. Sudan was among one of the earliest countries to support China's participation in the United Nations. The two countries established diplomatic relations as early as 1959. He added: The Sudanese people will never forget the Chinese people's assistance. In the past, China provided Sudan with eight loans which played an important role in Sudan's economic development. Salih also stressed that China has consistently supported the stand of the Third World in international affairs and has won the respect of the world's public opinion as well as that of the Third World.
