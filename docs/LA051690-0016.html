May 16, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

STANFORD'S REVISED CURRICULUM

There can be no place but Stanford where the text of "Uncle Tom's Cabin" is pored over so seriously.

It was a trash novel in its day, written by a housewife who never set foot in the South. It is a device for the study of Western culture just as "Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles" is a device for the study of animal biology.

If the privileged children of Stanford must immerse themselves in the doings of Little Eva, Topsy, and company, let it be as an artifact of the Civil War era. Don't put it on the shelf next to Plato and the Bible.

RICHARD HEALEY

Huntington Beach
