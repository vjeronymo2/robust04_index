Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 24th -- According to the IBERIA news agency, speaking at a Georgian Supreme Council session, Zurab Murvanidze said that he had received a document proving that Georgia was purchasing wheat for 120 U.S. dollars per tonne while it could pay 60 U.S. dollars per tonne. In reply, the head of state said that it was really possible to purchase a tonne of wheat for 60 dollars but credits granted to Georgia by the United States and the EU [European Union] obliged Georgia to purchase wheat at double the prices only in these countries.
