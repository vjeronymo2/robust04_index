BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 21 (KCNA) -- Leaders and parties of different countries expressed full support to the Korean people's cause of national reunification and their efforts for a peaceful solution of the nuclear issue. Meles Zenawi, president of the transitional Government of Ethiopia, when he met a DPRK Government delegation on April 15, expressed solidarity for the struggle of the Korean people to achieve an independent and peaceful reunification of their country, and supported the DPRK's stand to solve the nuclear issue of the Korean peninsula peacefully through DPRK-USA talks. Prime Minister of Uganda George Kosmas Adyebo when he met a delegation of the DPRK Foreign Ministry on April 14, expressed support and solidarity for the just cause of the Korean Government and people for the reunification of the country by means of confederacy. He said he firmly supports Korea's stand to peacefully solve the nuclear issue of the Korean peninsula through the DPRK-USA talks. Antoine Malonga, chairman of the Congolese Movement for the Construction of New Society, when he received the DPRK ambassador to his country on April 9, denounced the anti- socialist, anti-DPRK moves of the United States and its satellite forces and expressed full support to the self- defensive measures of the DPRK. The world will see a great victory of the Korean-style socialism, he stressed. Valeri Sklatov, chairman of the Liberal Patriotic Party for the "Rebirth" of Russia, when he met with the DPRK ambassador on April 7, expressed full support to the DPRK's stand on the nuclear issue and denounced intrigues of the United States. The secretariat of the Central Committee of the Socialist Party of Australia in a statement April 11 urged the United States to stop its threats and pressure on the DPRK at once and implement the policy based on non-interference in the internal affairs of sovereign states.
