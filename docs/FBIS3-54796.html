Language: Armenian Article Type:BFN [Text] In order to expand commodity circulation in the consumer market and reduce production (?losses), the Armenian Government has decided to establish new retail prices for bread, flour, and basic services as of 4 January. The retail price of bread has been fixed at 1.50 dram, or 300 rubles, per kilo; flour at 1.21 dram, or 250 rubles, per kilo. The rate for electricity has been fixed at .20 dram, or 40 rubles per kilowatt-hour. Inter-city passenger transportation on buses, trolleys, and trams has been fixed at .50 dram, or 100 rubles, per person. The cost of basic services--such as rent, water, garbage collection, and telephone service--have also been increased by an average of two-and-a-half times more than their previous cost. Detailed information will be published in the press.
