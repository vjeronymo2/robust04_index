November 25, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

JULIAN SURPASSES AIR BATTLE AS LA QUINTA WINS

La Quinta and Newport Harbor high school's quarterbacks combined for more than 400 yards passing in their second-round Division VI playoff game Friday in front of 2,200 at Bolsa Grande.

But it was Trent Julian's 18-yard breakaway run for a touchdown with about a minute to play that decided the outcome, a 23-16 victory for La Quinta. The Aztecs (8-4) will play La Mirada, a 14-0 winner over Western, in the semifinals next week.

La Quinta went into the fourth quarter leading, 16-9, and had mounted a drive from its 37 to the Newport Harbor 26, but Newport Harbor's Ryan Abby intercepted a pass by Richard Weaver and returned it 40 yards. That set up Stephen Scheck's six-yard touchdown pass to Matt White. Josh Klein's point-after tied the score, 16-16, with just under 3 1/2 minutes to play.

La Quinta then took possession on its 31 and took eight plays to get into the end zone. One key to the drive was a Newport Harbor's penalty.

La Quinta had a third and two at the Newport Harbor 44, but a five-yard penalty against Newport Harbor gave La Quinta the first down. On the next play, Weaver hit Wes Kollar for a 26-yard reception. And two plays later, Julian cut up the middle for the 18-yard touchdown and the game.

Newport Harbor had one last chance with possession at its 32 and less than a minute to play, but Scheck threw two incomplete passes, and was sacked on third down. His completion to Todd Farley on fourth and 15 was short of the first down as time ran out.

Julian, who has been playing on a knee that has damaged cartilage and will require surgery after the season, rushed for 93 yards and two touchdowns in 19 carries. He also caught five passes for 47 yards. Weaver completed 17 of 30 passes for 219 yards and an interception; Newport Harbor's Scheck completed 19 of 31 passes for 187 yards and one touchdown.

Newport Harbor took the lead early. Ken Osako blocked Justin Sagoin's punt at the Sailor 35 and six plays later Todd Farley (16 carries, 48 yards) scored on a one-yard run. La Quinta tied the score, 6-6, on a six-play, 73-yard drive capped by Weaver's six-yard keeper for a touchdown. Weaver also completed a 44-yard pass to Jason Cherms (5 receptions, 91 yards) in the drive.

La Quinta scored in the second quarter on Julian's two-yard run to cap an 80-yard drive, and Matt Logan added a 23-yard field goal to start the second half. Newport Harbor's Klein kicked a 27-yard field goal before the Sailors tied the score on White's touchdown reception.
