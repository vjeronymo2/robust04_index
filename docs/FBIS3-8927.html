Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Article by Miodrag Perovic: "Herzeg-Montenegro"] [Text] "Take me east of Suez where there is no difference between the best and the worst," Rudyard Kipling wished once. This week it was not necessary to travel that far in order to reach a world in which fantasy and reality meet. The second DPS [Democratic Party of Socialists] congress was the place in which the desired and the twisted world in which we live, led by the DPS and its leaders, were equalized. DPS members said in the congress that they were devoted to democracy, but at the same time they use wiretaps and physical threats and misuse the media, which are more brutal than the communist television during the last 50 years. They claim to be establishing a constitutional state and torture people in prisons. They make statements in which they point out that they pursue the politics of peace and friendly relations with their neighbors, but they have obviously forgotten that they led a war outside Montenegro and sowed 250 graves over the country for Montenegrin youngsters who died in that war. They pretend to be developing a civilized state, while supporting ethnic cleansing not only in Bosnia, but also in Boka Kotorska and in northern Montenegro. They support political pluralism and parliamentary democracy but they arm their political followers. They boast about the safety Montenegro citizens allegedly have, but they "forget" to mention the act of kidnapping in Strpci, in which they were involved. They are trying to convince us that their foreign policy is constructive and assessed positively by the whole world, but their plane can only fly to Greece in order to buy furniture for their new apartments there. They are open to all democratic ideas, but "their attitudes are completely identical" with those of the fascist Zhirinovsky. They conducted a "secret" poll about public matters and invited the manager of an "independent" institute of an "autonomous" university to the party congress in order to read what they wrote for him. Their bells [the state media] were ringing two days long telling us that according to that objective poll more than 70 percent of the 1,000 respondents support Yugoslavia, but they did not say that less than 5 percent supported an alliance of Serbian states, called Yugoslavia, into which they want to push Montenegro, too. Momir Bulatovic and Milo Djukanovic revived the idea that Montenegro and Herzegovina should merge. They did not state the reasons, so we cannot think of their opposite in order to know what their real motives are, but let us mention two possible reasons in order to make things clearer. One of the reasons is to offer the greedy and disoriented Montenegrins the chance to spread to Herzegovina. In this way they would be silenced -- they would not dare oppose the process of making Montenegro a Serbian province. They would perhaps not become a national minority, but a weak majority in their own state. The second reason reveals that the constructors of a greater-Serbia in Bosnia have difficulties. If the Muslim-Croatian federation is accomplished, there are good chances that the territories along the Drina River in Eastern Bosnia will be annexed to it, regardless of the fact that they are ethnically clean now. If Serbs oppose the tripartite federation and if Bosnia enters two confederations, with Croatia and Serbia, there is the possibility that Serbian territories in Bosnia be divided into two separate parts. In this case, Herzegovina would be connected with Serbia through Montenegro as a member state of the alliance of Serbian states. The DPS, led by Bulatovic and Djukanovic, keeps on denying Montenegro as a state. In the alliance of Serbian states, although its name might be Yugoslavia, there is no place for Montenegrin individuality, there are no conditions to cherish the national, cultural, religious, and other authentic Montenegrin traditions. The Serbian Institute for the Protection of Monuments was given the task of renovating the Romanesque church of Sveti Luka in Kotor. After the renovation the original dome without a square pedestal was turned into the Raska School type dome with a high square pedestal. This example illustrates how dull the (post)communist Montenegro is. It also shows the Serbian attitude toward Montenegrin cultural heritage and its future in any union of Serbian states. The DPS members who gave standing ovations to the university assistant from Podgorica, should know that they are participants in the crime being committed against Montenegro on their behalf and with their own hands. The fact that this is interpreted as the people's wish does not change the essence.
