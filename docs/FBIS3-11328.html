Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Maturin -- Education authorities in Monagas State decided on Thursday to suspend classes until further notice "given the students' continuous protests in the city. The protests were provoked by an increase in public transportation fares. [no closing quotation mark as published] Monagas State Education Director Lidee de Ascanio made the announcement, saying that "the suspension of education activities applies to the kindergarten, elementary, and junior and senior high school levels in all public schools in the state." Ascanio also urged representatives and members of the education community to honor the decision until the state's public transportation situation returns to normal. A tense atmosphere prevailed in the city yesterday following the recent protests.
