930515 FT 15 MAY 93 / Emergency moves to stem Caracas riots A STATE of emergency was declared in the Venezuelan capital yesterday to stem protests that have broken out ahead of next week's court ruling on whether the president will have to stand trial on corruption charges, Reuter reports from Caracas. Students and public servants have clashed with police in recent days in a series of protests for higher pay and to call on President Carlos Andres Perez to resign. Perez has denied charges of embezzlement and misuse of funds in the purchase of Dollars 17 (Pounds 11m) at a preferential rate.
