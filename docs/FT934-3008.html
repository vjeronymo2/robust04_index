931210 FT 10 DEC 93 / UK Company News: Stirling shares drop on setback Losses in the import division and a Pounds 600,000 provision against property values left Stirling Group, the Marks and Spencer clothing supplier, with pre-tax profits of Pounds 223,000 for the six months to September 30, against Pounds 1.37m. The company said that the import division had been hit by the acquisition of Boftex, the loss of a large shirt importing contract and larger than expected reorganisation costs. On turnover of Pounds 8.2m (Pounds 7.9m) there was an operating loss of Pounds 708,000 (Pounds 414,000 profit). The shares fell 10p to 59p. Group turnover was Pounds 46.7m (Pounds 45.6m). Losses per share were 0.06p (1.07p earnings). The interim dividend, however, is raised to 0.55p (0.5p).
