Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Taba, 14 Feb (MENA)--General Amnon Shahaq, Israeli deputy chief of staff and head of the Israeli delegation to the Taba talks with the Palestinians, has said that the recently initialed Cairo agreement between the two sides has made things easier for the Palestinian and Israeli negotiators. The agreement established a broad base for us to build on, he said. Shahaq was speaking after the morning session of talks in Taba. He said that a number of security issues were discussed, including the formation, responsibilities, and mission of the Palestinian police force. He said this subject will be discussed further during the negotiations, which will continue for a week. Shahaq said the Palestinian-Israeli Civilian Affairs Committee will begin meeting tomorrow, Tuesday, until Thursday. He said the committee will discuss Palestinian detainees in Israeli prisons. This subject, he added, was discussed in the early rounds of the Taba talks, and will be completed in the current round. Shahaq said the negotiations agenda was changed because of the month of Ramadan. MENA has learned that tomorrow's session will be held from 0800 to 1400, and will resume from 2000 to 2200 Cairo time. General Shahaq and Palestinian chief negotiator Nabil Sha'th first held a private meeting. The two delegations then held an expanded meeting. Absent from the talks today were lawyer Jamil al-Tarifi, head of the Palestinian delegation to the Civilian Affairs Committee, and Gadi Zohar, head of the Israeli delegation to the same committee. Al-Tarifi will arrive in Taba this evening. The Palestinian and Israeli delegations held a second session of talks in Taba this afternoon; it is expected to continue until just before iftar [breaking of the fast], when the Israeli delegation will leave Taba for Israel.
