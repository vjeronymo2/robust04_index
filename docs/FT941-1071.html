940326 FT 26 MAR 94 / Finance and the Family: Your CGT The table shows CGT indexation allowances for assets sold in February. Multiply the original cost of the asset by the figure for the month you bought it. Subtract the result from the proceeds of your sale; the balance is your taxable gain or loss. Say you bought shares for Pounds 6,000 in September 1985 and sold them in February 1994 for Pounds 13,000. Multiplying the original cost by the September 1985 figure of 1.481 gives a total of Pounds 8,886. Subtracting that from Pounds 13,000 gives a capital gain of Pounds 4,066, which is within the 1993-94 CGT allowance of Pounds 5,800. If selling shares bought before April 6 1982, use the March 1982 figure. The RPI in February was 142.1.
