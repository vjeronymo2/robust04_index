940826 FT 26 AUG 94 / International Company News: Toronto-Dominion Bank up 59% Toronto-Dominion Bank, Canada's fifth-largest bank, achieved a 59 per cent gain in third-quarter earnings. The bank attributed the rise to a strengthening economy and improvement in its business loan portfolio. Net non-performing loans dropped below CDollars 1bn (USDollars 727m) for the first time since 1990 and the fiscal 1994 loan-loss estimate has been reduced by CDollars 50m to CDollars 400m. Commercial property remains the main area of concern. Net profit for the three months ended July 31 was CDollars 162m, or 50 cents a share, against CDollars 102m, or 32 cents, a year earlier. Return on assets rose to 0.66 per cent from 0.49 per cent. Nine-months' profit was CDollars 488m, or CDollars 1.52 a share, against CDollars 272m, or 57 cents. National Bank of Canada reported a 20 per cent gain in third-quarter earnings with strong business and personal loan demand. Net profit was CDollars 57.4m, or 29 cents, up from CDollars 47.3m, or 27 cents, a year earlier. Nine-months' profit was CDollars 159.9m, or 83 cents a share, up 21 per cent from CDollars 132.4m, or 78 cents. Return on assets rose to 0.53 per cent from 0.48 per cent in the third quarter and to 0.50 per cent from 0.46 per cent in the nine months. Total assets at July 31 were CDollars 44bn, up 11 per cent from a year earlier.
