Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Shanayev] [Text] Vladikavkaz January 16 TASS -- North Ossetia is holding its first ever presidential elections on Sunday. At 7:00 A.M., 300 polling stations located in cities, regional centres and remote mountainous villages opened to receive first electors. The voting will continue until 20:00 P.M. Two candidates have been put on ballot papers, including chairman of the North Ossetian parliament Akhsarbek Galazov and head of the North Ossetian government, Sergey Khetagurov. For the time being, Galazov has the highest rating, according to the results of a sociological poll, ITAR-TASS learned from the Central Electoral Commission. The turn-out as reported by 10:00 A.M. was average. The people are casting their ballots in a calm atmosphere and no violations of the franchise have been reported. Galazov has already voted at polling station No. 189 in Vladikavkaz. Asked by a journalist which candidate he had crossed out from his ballot paper, Galazov said "one of the two". Talking to representatives of the mass media, Galazov said that "the elections of the first North Ossetian president should consolidate the multinational population of the republic and give a positive impetus to reforms in economy and a political life." It was earlier reported that in connection with the elections of the North Ossetian president a state of emergency in North Ossetia and Ingushetia had been suspended for twenty four hours in accordance with the Russian president's decree. Russian peace- keeping forces have been instructed to maintain law and order in public places during this period of time and take measures to ensure citizens' safety.
