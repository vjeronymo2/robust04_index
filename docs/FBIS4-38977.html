BFN [Edict of the Russian Federation President "On the Introduction of a State of Emergency in Part of the Territories of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic" -- Moscow, Kremlin, 30 May, 1994, No. 1112] [Text] In connection with the persistence of the tense situation in a number of regions of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic, the need to bring about the return of refugees and forced resettlers to their places of permanent residence, and the need to implement a package of measures to eradicate the consequences of the armed conflict for the purposes of ensuring state and public safety, as well as in the light of Article 88 of the Russian Federation Constitution, the RSFSR Law "On the State of Emergency," and the Russian Federation Law "On Safety," I decree: 1. That the state of emergency in part of the territories of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic, introduced via the 4 April 1994 and 27 April 1994 edicts of the Russian Federation president No. 657 and No. 836, be abrogated as of 1400 hours 31 May 1994. 2. That, guided by the RSFSR Law "On the State of Emergency" (Article 4.9), a state of emergency be introduced on the territories here listed as of 1400 hours 31 May 1994 through 1400 hours 31 July 1994: The Republic of North Ossetia: Mozdokskiy Rayon (with the exception of the inhabited localities of Telman, Yelbayevo, and Sadovyy), Pravoberezhnyy Rayon (the populated areas of Staryy, Batakoyurt, Zilga, Olginskoye, and Vladikavkaz Airport), Prigorodnyy Rayon (the settlements of Mayskiy, Chermen, Komgaron, Tarskoye, Kurtat, Oktyabrskoye, Kambilevskoye, Dachnoye, Sunzha, Dongaron), and the city of Vladikavkaz (populated areas of Yuzhnyy, Chernorechenskoye, Terk, Balta, Chmi, Nizhniy Lars, Verkhniy Lars, Ezmi, Kartsa, and the "Sputnik" cantonment); The Ingush Republic: Malgobekskiy Rayon (with the exception of the inhabited localities of Novyy Redant, Chkalovo), Nazranovskiy Rayon, Sunzhenskiy Rayon (inhabited localities of Galashki, Muzhichi, Nizhniy Alkun, Verkhniy Alkun), Dzheyrakhskiy Rayon (inhabited localities of Dzheyrakh, Lyazhgi, Beyni, Furtog). 3. That the Provisional Administration on the territories of Mozdokskiy Rayon, Prigorodnyy Rayon, and areas of the Republic of North Ossetia adjacent to it, and on the territories of Malgobekskiy and Nazranovskiy Rayons in the Ingush Republic, which was introduced in the capacity of a special form of government by Edict of the President of the Russian Federation No. 657 of 4 April 1994 "On the Introduction of a State of Emergency in Part of the Territories of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic," be transformed into the Provisional Administration in part of the territories of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic (henceforth referred to as the Provisional Administration). The Provisional Administration is the legal successor to the Provisional Administration on the territories of Mozdokskiy Rayon, Prigorodnyy Rayon, and areas of the Republic of North Ossetia adjacent to it, and Malgobekskiy and Nazranovskiy Rayons in the Ingush Republic. 4. That, for the period of the state of emergency: a special exit and entry system and a procedure for movement within the state of emergency zone be established, incorporating a document check, and, upon receipt of information regarding the possession of weapons by citizens, a body search and a search of belongings, lodgings, and transportation; the protection of public order and facilities guaranteeing the day-to-day existence of the population be stepped up; the staging of meetings, rallies, street processions, and demonstrations, as well as other mass events in the state of emergency zone be prohibited; the staging of strikes be banned; the sale of weapons be prohibited, and that firearms and cold steel, ammunition, noxious and explosive substances be confiscated from citizens; persons who are not inhabitants of a given locality who breach public order be ousted at their own expense to their place of permanent residence, or beyond the borders of the territory on which the state of emergency has been introduced; the activity of political parties, social organizations, and mass movements impeding the normalization of the situation be suspended after preliminary warnings; censorship on information relaying events in the state of emergency zone be introduced, with the right of the provisional seizure of printed output; with due account of the situation, a curfew be introduced, that is, a ban on citizens' being on the streets and in other public places without specially issued permits and documentation identifying that person, at the specified time of day. 5. It is for the Russian Federation Ministry of Internal Affairs, the Russian Federation Federal Counterintelligence Service, and forces from the Russian Federation Ministry of Defense attached to the Provisional Administration to take measures to prevent armed clashes between the belligerent sides, to defend citizens' safety, and to ensure unswerving compliance with the state of emergency ruling. 6. That the joint forces of the Russian Federation Ministry of Defense, the Russian Federation Ministry of Internal Affairs, the Russian Federation Federal Counterintelligence Service, the Federal Agency of Government Communications and Information under the Russian Federation President, and the Russian Federation State Customs Committee be subordinate in operational terms to the head of the Provisional Administration. 7. That it be incumbent on the Russian Federation Ministry of Internal Affairs (V.F. Yerin), the Russian Federation Ministry of Defense (P.S. Grachev), and the Russian Federation General Prosecutor's Office (A.N. Ilyushenko) to ensure the fulfillment of Item 4 of the working plan of specific actions, ratified by the Russian Federation President 30 December 1993. 8. That it be recommended that the Russian Federation General Prosecutor's Office, for the purposes of overseeing the legality and coordination of the work of investigative organs, set up a Prosecutor's Office with the concomitant powers in the state of emergency zone. 9. That the Russian Federation Government: guarantee the implementation of measures connected with the prolongation of the state of emergency ruling, including financing them; finance in good time and in full measure undertakings concerning an all-around solution to the problem of refugees and forced resettlers; guarantee the further organization of talks on settling the conflict; proffer through the Provisional Administration financial and economic aid to the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic in line with federal programs, maintaining the offer of such aid depending on the situation which arises in the state of emergency zone; hear reports in June 1994 from the Government of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Government of the Ingush Republic on work done in fulfillment of the edict of the Russian Federation President No. 2131 of 13 December 1993 and the decree of the Russian Federation Government No. 185 of 9 March 1994; hear a report in June or July 1994 from Provisional Administration Head V.D. Lozovoy on the Provisional Administration's work in eradicating the effects of the armed conflict; guarantee, with the direct participation of the Provisional Administration, the government of the Republic of North Ossetia, and the government of the Ingush Republic, the fulfillment through 31 July 1994 of the edict of the Russian Federation president No. 2131 [number as published] of 13 December 1993 and the creation of the conditions for the return of all refugees and forced resettlers to the places of their former residence on the territories of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic by 1 December 1994; submit proposals for increasing the efficiency of the state of emergency ruling in June 1994. 10. Proceeding from the Treaty on Public Accord, it is for Republic of North Ossetia President A.Kh. Galazov and Ingush Republic President R.S. Aushev by 15 June 1994 to submit an agreed procedure for returning and settling refugees and forced resettlers in the places formerly densely populated by them, in the first stage in the inhabited localities of Chermen, Dongaron, Dachnoye, Kurtat of Prigorodnyy Rayon in the Republic of North Ossetia, and in the next stage also in other inhabited localities of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Ingush Republic. 11. That this edict enter into force from the moment of its signing. 12. That this edict be sent to the Federal Assembly Federation Council for ratification in line with Article 102 of the Russian Federation Constitution. [signed] Russian Federation President B. Yeltsin, [dated] Moscow, the Kremlin, 30 May 1994, No. 1112.
