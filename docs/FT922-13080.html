920414 FT 14 APR 92 / Spanish inflation rises in March Spain's 0.4 per cent rise in the consumer price index (CPI) for March was better than expected and could pave the way for easier interest rates but the government will be hard-pressed to meet its 5 per cent target for the year, economists said, Reuters reports from Madrid. The March rise gave a year-on-year rise of 6.9 per cent, up on February's 6.8 per cent. Many analysts had expected this rate to accelerate to 7 per cent with a month's rise of 0.5 to 0.7 per cent. The figure was additionally positive because it included the annual adjustment for government-controlled rents.
