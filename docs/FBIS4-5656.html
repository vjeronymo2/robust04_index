BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 13 KYODO -- The Export-Import Bank of Japan will extend two untied loans worth 22.5 billion yen to Peru, bank officials said Wednesday [13 April]. A signing ceremony for the loans took place Tuesday at the annual meeting of the Inter-American Development Bank in Guadalajara, Mexico. The loans will go toward improving the country's infrastructure. It is the first time for the bank to extend an untied loan to the country.
