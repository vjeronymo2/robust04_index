Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Feb 15, BNS -- The U.S. Army assistance chief, Owen Moon, left Latvia Saturday [12 February] after having headed the group for 10 months. His successor, Colonel Wayne Koppa, is scheduled to arrive this week. During Moon's office, 33 U.S. defense delegations visited Latvia and consulted Latvian specialists in medicine, law, civil control over military structures and other specific issues. Fifteen groups of Latvian defense officials joined various U.S. government-sponsored workshops and courses in America, Latvian Defense Minister Valdis Pavlovskis told reporters. The U.S. Army assistance group consists of five specialists. It aims at helping Latvia create democratic armed forces after the Western sample. Moon spoke highly of the skills of Latvian defense foces which have been achieved by minimal budget resources. But he criticized the centralized Soviet-style military administration as blocking Latvia from efficient integration into the European security structures.
