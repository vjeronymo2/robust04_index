BFN [Text] Berlin (DDP/ADN) -- German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel has called upon North Korea to open its nuclear installations immediately to inspections of the International Atomic Energy Agency. He was very concerned about North Korea's continuing refusal to meet its obligations under the nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty, Kinkel told the daily newspaper DIE WELT (Tuesday [5 April] edition). Any state's violation of the treaty was a serious danger to world peace. As a state which had renounced the production of ABC [atomic, biological, chemical] weapons under international law, Germany supported the nonproliferation of all weapons of mass destruction. In this connection, Kinkel referred to his suggestion of a worldwide cooperative policy of nonproliferation of 15 December 1993. Kinkel said that he strongly supported the unlimited extension of the nuclear nonproliferation treaty, which was due for renewal in 1995.
