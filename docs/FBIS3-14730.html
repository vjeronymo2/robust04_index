Language: Armenian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Armenian Prime Minister Grant Bagratyan today received the Israeli Ambassador to Armenia Baruch Birenbaun, who also represents his country's interests in Georgia. Trade and economic cooperation between the two countries were discussed during the meeting. Particular attention was given to the establishment of mutually advantageous ties in banking, agricultural, and energy, the basis for which has already been laid during meetings between Armenian and Israeli experts. The ambassador handed a letter to Prime Minister Grant Bagratyan from the Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin. The latter affirmed the Israeli government's readiness and interest in developing Armenian-Israeli ties. Baruch Birenbaun reiterated that he will exert every effort to speed up the establishment of economic, cultural, and scientific ties between the two countries. Arman Kirakosyan, Armenian first deputy foreign minister; Vasili Gazaryan, head of the foreign relations department of the Council of Ministers; and other officials were present at the meeting.
