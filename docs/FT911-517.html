910511 FT 11 MAY 91 / Markets (At a glance): Political pointers from the 'misery index' Political pundits in the City, trying to guess the date of the next general election, are examining the 'misery index' which adds the current rate of inflation to the rate of unemployment. The idea is that the lower the level of 'misery', the higher the government's chances of winning reelection will be. It peaked just before Margaret Thatcher's resignation, and is showing slight signs of going down, but analysts are hedging their bets on the election date. JA
