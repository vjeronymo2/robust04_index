Language: Tagalog Article Type:BFN [Text] President Ramos today signed two proclamations granting amnesty to all rebels who have committed offenses or crimes against public order or in pursuit of their political objectives, and to the Armed Forces of the Philippines and Philippine National Police personnel who have violated the revised Penal Code. Ramos said the two proclamations he signed are based on the recommendations of Justice Secretary Franklin Drilon, Defense Secretary Renato De Villa, and Rafael Alunan III, secretary of the Department of the Interior and Local Government. [Begin Press Secretary Jesus Sison recording, in progress, in English] ...as an anchor of political, economic, and social stability and development has steadily moved forward with the overwhelming acceptance and support of the Filipino people. We shall enhance and hasten this process. We're addressing, among other things, the legal status of the main rebels and insurgents who have already returned or have expressed their desire and readiness to return to the legal fold and join the mainstream of Philippine society, the president said. He added: We also recognize that our men in uniform have, or may have, committed punishable offenses, punishable offenses in relation to their carrying out of counterinsurgency and law enforcement operations. An amnesty is extended to them as well, so as to promote an atmosphere more conducive to the attainment of a just, comprehensive, and lasting peace. [end recording]
