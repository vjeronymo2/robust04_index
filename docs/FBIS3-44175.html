Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The institutional vacuum created by the resignation of Mr. Lanzeni Coulibaly was filled this morning. The head of state, His Excellency Henri Konan Bedie, this morning appointed a new Supreme Court president. He is Michel Koui Mamadou. Soon after his appointment, the new Supreme Court president resigned from his post of deputy and announced that he would leave the Democratic Party of Cote d'Ivoire-African Democratic Rally [PDCI-RDA] because of the new responsibilities assigned to him. Here is the new president's statement soon after his appointment: [Begin Mamadou recording] His Excellency President Henri Konan Bedie has just honored me by appointing me to the high office of the president of the Supreme Court of Cote d'Ivoire. In this solemn moment, I would like to sincerely thank him for fully trusting me--trust that I will strive to further serve. [passage omitted] I would like to stress that while accepting the functions of president of the high institution that President Henri Konan Bedie has assigned to me, I will automatically give up the post of deputy that I have been occupying. I cannot be a judge and parliamentarian at the same. My resignation is effective immediately. Similary, as of today I give up all my functions within the PDCI-RDA steering organs because of the neutrality demanded by my new responsibilities. [end recording]
