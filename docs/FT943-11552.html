940729 FT 29 JUL 94 / UK Company News: Provision lifts Pex losses to Pounds 2.17m Pex, the hosiery manufacturer and distributor, announced a pre-tax loss of Pounds 2.17m for the year to January 31 after a provision of Pounds 900,000 for stock and ongoing costs of former retail properties. In the previous year there was a profit of Pounds 393,000 after a surplus on restructuring of Pounds 1.3m. The company also announced the appointment of Mr John Carr as deputy chairman and chief executive. Turnover was down 14 per cent to Pounds 12.4m (Pounds 14.5m) following the company's repositioning at the upper end of the market and loss of sales at the lower end. Mr Peter Bailey, chairman, said that after many years of struggle 'we can see a way back to profitability'. Losses per share for the year were 11.35p, compared with earnings of 3.13p.
