920424 FT 24 APR 92 / UK Company News: Acquisition prompts jump to Pounds 3m at Starmin REFLECTING acquisition and subsequent reorganisation, Starmin produced pre-tax profits of Pounds 3m for 1991, compared with Pounds 764,000. Mr Owen Rout, chairman, said this was the second year of substantial progress in the aim to develop as a medium sized quarry products group. The proposed sale of the engineering division would allow management to concentrate totally on developing the quarry business, he stated. Extraordinary charges of Pounds 1.06m have been made against that. The year saw substantial capital expenditure particularly in relation to re-equipping acquisitions. Spending in the current year should be significantly lower. The pre-tax profit was split as to quarry products England Pounds 943,000 (Pounds 383,000) and Scotland Pounds 1.57m (Pounds 291,000), landfill Pounds 1.07m (nil), less discontinued businesses loss Pounds 64,000 (Pounds 10,000) and interest paid Pounds 512,000 (received Pounds 100,000). Turnover expanded from Pounds 8.8m to Pounds 18.2m. Earnings per share came to 1p (0.4p) and the final dividend is the forecast 0.2p on capital increased by the rights issue, to make 0.3p (0.1p). Borrowings were Pounds 2.9m representing gearing of just over 9 per cent.
