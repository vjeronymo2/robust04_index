Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, 20 Feb -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin telephoned U.S. President Bill Clinton. Vyacheslav Kostikov, the Russian president's press secretary, said that they discussed problems pertaining to the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. As a result of the conversation, an accord was reached on postponing bombing, taking into account the recent Russian diplomacy. The U.S. President highly assessed Boris Yeltsin's political initiative, which made it possible to ease significantly the crisis around Sarajevo. In the course of the telephone conversation, it was confirmed that decisions stemming from the development of the situation around Sarajevo would be adopted through UN mediation. It was agreed to continue consultations on the settlement of the situation in Bosnia in the upcoming days.
