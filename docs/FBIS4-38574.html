BFN [Text] The Azerbaijani leadership is discussing the possible introduction of presidential rule in the republic and dismissal of the parliament, a high-ranking official close to the office of Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev told INTERFAX. He asked to remain anonymous. The source did not mention details. Acting Chief of the president's Press Service Salahaddin Guliyev said in an interview with INTERFAX that the parliament would not be dismissed in the near future. Neither would presidential rule or other emergency measures be introduced. Observers in Baku stressed that the Azerbaijani media had recently repeatedly mentioned appeals on the part of refugees demanding the dismissal of the parliament and to declaration of presidential rule.
