December 27, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

ROMANIA-BOUND: The Panama intervention hasn't boosted local military recruiting, said an Army spokesman in Santa Ana, but some Orange County youths said they'd go overseas to fight if needed. . . . At least eight young Romanian-Americans put their names on a Southern California list of those willing to fight in Bucharest. . . . Local residents phoned relatives in Romania, said Pastor Daniel Branzai of the Romanian Baptist Church in Anaheim, who "told them about the tremendous need that they had for guns and manpower. That's why they were ready to go." Dana Parsons and Mark I. Pinsky
