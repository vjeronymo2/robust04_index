921016 FT 16 OCT 92 / Fear over extent of urban spread THE AMOUNT of countryside lost every year to urban sprawl may be twice as great as government figures suggest, according to a report by the Council for the Protection of Rural England, Bronwen Maddox writes. In the first survey of land-use of its kind since the second world war, the CPRE claims that 'an area the size of Exeter, Norwich and Oxford' could be urbanised each year.
