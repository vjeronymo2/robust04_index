940528 FT 28 MAY 94 / Six convicted of Pounds 1.5m VAT fraud Six Birmingham-based businessmen were yesterday found guilty of operating a Pounds 1.5m fraud by representing adult clothes as children's garments to avoid value added tax. They are to be sentenced at Birmingham Crown Court on June 17. Customs and Excise said the men had also 'systematically supressed a high percentage of sales between themselves and to other customers'. The men operated a number of businesses involved in clothes manufacturing, the biggest of which had annual sales of Pounds 3m. The fraud took place over five years from 1985. Customs said: 'All the businesses received regular visits fom Customs and Excise but the books and records had been carefully constructed and completed in a fraudulent manner.'
