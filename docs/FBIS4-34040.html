BFN [Text] Port-au-Prince, Jun 14 (AFP) -- This Tuesday, Jean-Claude Martineau, spokesman of exiled President Jean-Bertrand Aristide, condemned the 12 June state of emergency proclamation by provisional President Emile Jonassaint (the international does not recognize him), describing it as a "Duvalierist diatribe and a threat veiled in demagoguery." In a communique forwarded to the AFP office in Port-au-Prince, Martineau says "the country is, in fact, living amid a state of emergency, and all the people's rights have been suspended since 30 September 1991" (date of the coup d'etat.) "If you want to find those who threaten the country's independence, you will not find them abroad because they are in our country," he added.
