Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 26 (XINHUA) -- The organizing committee of the Thirteenth International Archives Conference (IAC) called its second meeting Thursday [24 March] in Beijing. Luo Gan, state councilor and secretary-general of the State Council spoke at the meeting. Wang Gang, director of the State Archives Bureau and vice- chairman of the organizing committee, gave a report on the preparatory work for the coming conference, which is scheduled to be held in Beijing in 1996. Luo, chairman of the organizing committee, stressed the importance of good preparatory work for the IAC conference, saying that the membership will be expanded to the municipal government level to ensure the conference a success. He said that the development of archives work in China has gained speed over that of the past decade and a good archives system has now taken shape. He said, it is necessary to study and use for reference the experience of other countries in archives management and use. The IAC holds an annual meeting every three years, and this will be the first to take place in a developing country.
