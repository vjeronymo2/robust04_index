930429 FT 29 APR 93 / World Trade News: Romania's big phone bill DEVELOPING Romania's telecommunications will need Dollars 7bn-Dollars 8bn, despite the fact that it is only aiming for 25 per cent coverage by the year 2005, Reuter reports from Bucharest. The estimate was given by Mr Dan Stefanescu, a senior Communications Ministry official, speaking at a conference on developing telecommunications in the former eastern bloc. Almost Dollars 190m (Pounds 123.3m) in credits had been granted to Rom Telecom by the World Bank and the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development, said Mr Stefanescu. German and French suppliers had also offered credits exceeding Dollars 85m for switching equipment made in Romania. He said the main goals for the next three years included installation of 500,000 telephone lines and 5,000km of fibre-optic cable, setting up a private television channel and obtaining 5,000-10,000 mobile phone subscribers.
