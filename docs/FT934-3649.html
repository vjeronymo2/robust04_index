931208 FT 08 DEC 93 / Heseltine boosts business-aid cash Mr Michael Heseltine, the trade and industry secretary, yesterday announced a number of initiatives to help industry and small businesses. He used his speech in the final day of the Budget debate in the Commons to announce an increase from Pounds 14m to Pounds 17m in start-up funding for Business Link outlets. The outlets are designed to bring together under one roof most of the services provided by training and enterprise councils, chambers of commerce, local authorities, enterprise agencies, and other private-sector organisations. The first of a planned national network have opened in Birmingham, Leicester, and Congleton in Cheshire. Mr Heseltine said Tecs - which deliver government training schemes in England and Wales - 'have a key strategic role in organising the provision of business support services'. Tecs' budgets for services to small businesses - most of which will be taken into Business Link outlets - is to increase slightly to Pounds 33m. Mr Heseltine also announced an environmental technology best-practice programme, worth Pounds 16m, and a new Link programme on surface engineering. Mr Robin Cook, shadow trade and industry secretary, said spending by Mr Heseltine's department would drop by some 12 per cent in real terms in the next three years, and this reflected the falling investment in Britain's manufacturing industry. 'Out of 18 spending departments, the DTI is 17th,' said Mr Cook. 'Britain provides less support to industry than any other European country.'
