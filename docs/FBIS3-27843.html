Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 28 KYODO -- Japan agreed with Vietnam on Friday [28 January] to extend a loan of 52.3 billion yen and a grant-in-aid of 853 million yen, Foreign Ministry officials said. The Japanese Government pledged the loan at an international conference on aid to Vietnam held in Paris last November. Japanese Ambassador to Vietnam Hiroyuki Yushita and Vietnamese Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam exchanged documents on the loan and the grant in Hanoi, the officials said. The loan is for seven projects to improve infrastructure related to power supply and transportation, and to pay for equipment to repair roads and waterworks, they said. The aid is to support Vietnam's efforts at economic reform and to shift to a market-oriented economy under its policy of "doi moi" or renovation, they said. Japan will also grant 788 million yen for Can Tho University in southern Vietnam to improve its agriculture department. The money will be used to build facilities and to pay for equipment. Another grant of 65 million yen will be used for a project to improve waterworks in northeastern Hanoi.
