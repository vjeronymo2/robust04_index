Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 10 March. Despite the trade surplus in the last month of 1993, the 1993 trade balance deficit reached a record $679.1 million. This was the result of exports that totalled $1.6453 billion, a drop of 3.4 percent, and imports of $2.3244 billion, an increase of 13.7 percent. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 21 Feb 94 Economic Page p 10) The Central Bank reported that the value of fishing exports was $69.6 million in December 1993, a drop of 25.8 percent compared with the $93.8 million in the same month in 1992. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 22 Feb 94 Maritime News Section p 1) The export of nontraditional products increased 20.5 percent between 1989 and 1993. The items that registered a significant increase are: dairy products, 42.5 percent; lumber and furnitures, 166 percent; rubber products, 16 percent; machines and electrical appliances, 53.4 percent; and transportation material, 136 percent. The export of traditional products dropped 27 percent in the past four years. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 24 Feb 94 p 8) The National Ports Administration will invest a $2 million loan from the World Bank to finance projects in the port of Montevideo. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 22 Feb 94 Maritime News Section p 1)
