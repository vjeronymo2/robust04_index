Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Belsky] [Text] Moscow February 8 TASS -- Leader of the Russia's Choice State Duma faction Yegor Gaydar urged today Russian democratic and reform organizations to pull their efforts in the upcoming local elections. Otherwise the spring elections may result in the revival of camouflaged Soviets, says Gaydar's statement. In the opinion of Gaydar, it is necessary to elaborate a joint strategy for the election campaign in the next two or three weeks. It would be good to agree on the following principle: one candidate of all democratic and reform forces to one mandate, he said. Gaydar also suggested an agreement on quotas for each regional reform organization as pre-election tactics. The quotas can be based on the electors' support to democratic electoral coalitions in regions. The principle can be made all-Russian and then specified at joint consultations. Gaydar is ready to insist on quotas in favor of new progressive trade unions, business and workers' organizations and independent professional candidates.
