Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Progress has been made in the talks between Israel and the PLO in Cairo. Negotiations on the issue of protection for the Palestinians in Hebron may be completed by the end of the week. This was stated by Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres stated yesterday at the end of a meeting with U.S. peace talks coordinator Dennis Ross. Peres stressed that before it proceeds to promote the Syrian track, Israel must reach an agreement with the Palestinians about the continuation of the peace process. Our political correspondent Udi Segal reports: [Begin recording] [Segal] By the end of the week the Israeli and PLO delegations meeting in Cairo will conclude the elements of the Hebron package which will enable the resumption of the negotiations on the implementation of the Gaza-Jericho agreement, Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres assessed last night. According to reports received in Jerusalem from the delegation headed by Major General Amnon Shahaq and Foreign Ministry Director General Uri Savir, progress has been made in the two main issues -- the urban police force in Hebron and the nature of the international presence -- but no agreement has yet been reached. At this point, it seems that Peres will not go to Cairo to confer with PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat. However, one person who will be going to Cairo is U.S. peace talks coordinator Dennis Ross. After meeting him last night, Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres commented on Israel's priorities in the peace process. [Peres] If we reach an agreement with the Palestinians, the Syrian track will open. First of all, we must attain an agreement with the Palestinians and overcome all the unsolved problems. I think all of us will make a supreme effort to overcome them. We will see what happens in the next few days. [Segal] In his meeting with Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, Ross said he had found in Tunis a totally different 'Arafat than a week ago. 'Arafat is sure of himself, feels encouraged, and is prepared to make decisions, Ross told the Israelis. The Syrian track did not come up in the meeting of the Israeli and U.S. teams, but only in the tete-a-tete meeting between Ross and Rabin. The impression is that Ross did not bring any dramatic tidings on the Syrian track.
