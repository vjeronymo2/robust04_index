Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] Managua LA PRENSA in Spanish of 9 January 1994 on page 12 reports that the National Development Bank (Banades) will make available 1.296 billion cordobas in loans to small and medium producers for the 1994-95 crop year. According to the article, the bank will attempt to eliminate the credit policy which concentrates the bulk of the available resources in the hands of just a few growers. The bank estimates that the new policy will reach an additional 9,700 customers for a total of 42,700; interest rates will range between 16 and 18 percent. Silvio Baltodano, assistant credit manager at Banades, says that the bank will target nontraditional products, and that loans will be made to those in a position to pay off their loans. According to the article, for short-term financing Banades will devote 454.3 million cordobas for agriculture, 248.5 million for cattle, and 189 million for trade and industry. Long-term financing will total 403.9 million cordobas.
