BFN [Text] Kiev, [no date as received] -- Ukraine's Foreign Ministry told a UNIAN correspondent that Ukrainian Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko will pay an official visit to the Socialist Republic of Vietnam on 21 April. The head of Ukraine's foreign policy department will meet Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet, National Assembly Chairman Nong Duc Manh, and Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam. A joint statement or foreign ministers' declaration is expected to be signed which will set out the basic principles of relations between Ukraine and Vietnam and outline prospects for their development. They will also discuss preparations for the signing of a treaty between Ukraine and Vietnam on the principles of relations and cooperation, and a number of economic agreements, in particular on avoiding double taxation, on protecting and assisting investments in power engineering, and on legal assistance and employment. Anatoliy Zlenko will discuss with Vietnamese government representatives the forthcoming visit to Ukraine by Vietnam Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. According to additional information from Ukraine's Foreign Ministry, the Socialist Republic of Vietnam recognized Ukraine's independence on 27 December 1991. Diplomatic relations were established between the two states in January 1992. Agreements have been signed between Ukraine and Vietnam on air links, terms of mutual visa free travel by citizens, commercial navigation, cooperation in [word indistinct], and electrical communications, and a protocol on consultations between the foreign ministries of the two countries.
