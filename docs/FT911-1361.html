910507 FT 07 MAY 91 / Construction Contracts: Oman housing ministry offices YAHYA COSTAIN, a 60 per cent owned subsidiary of Costain group, has won an Pounds 8.35m order to build a three-storey office block and headquarters for the Ministry of Housing at Al Kuwair in Oman. The octagonal building has a ground floor plan of 77,000 sq metres and its central core rises five storeys - 29 metres to the top of the parapet. In the core is a central atrium starting at first floor level, with an aluminium-framed geodesic dome and stained glass infill roof. The top of the roof will be 37 metres above ground floor level. The three-storey offices form a ring round the central core. The main entrance rises to the same height as the core and has a double-storey height hall with two floors of offices above. On each corner of the octagon, crenellated towers rise, giving the appearance of a fortified structure. The building has a reinforced concrete frame on pad raft foundations with concrete blockwork infill and partition walls, aluminium windows, and glass-reinforced concrete decorative screens. Internally, all circulation and executive areas will have marble cladding and flooring. Mechanical and electrical services, security, landscaping, parking areas and an 850 metre longboundary wall are included in the contract, which is due for completion in October next year.
