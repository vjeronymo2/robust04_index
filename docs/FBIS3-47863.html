Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN [Text] Report on the crushing of the offensive launched by the Vietnamese enemy and its puppets in Kampot Province: 1. From 1 January, the enemy mobilized 2,000 troops from Kampot, Takeo, Kompong Speu, and Phnom Penh to launch an offensive against Democratic Kampuchea's liberated zone in Kampot Province. In this offensive, the enemy divided its forces into four prongs -- the first came from Route 4 heading toward (Ta Vek); the second moved from Vat Chak toward (Ta Kanh); the third moved from (?Chhuk) toward Leak and Kaoh Sla; while the fourth came along Route 16. 2. During the first stage of the battle which lasted from 1 to 5 January, the National Army of Democratic Kampuchea [NADK], guerrillas, and people's armed forces in Kampot Province used guerrilla and people's warfare tactics against the enemy especially all sorts of mines, punji stakes, booby traps, cross-bows, [words indistinct] and various types of guns. The enemy's offensive forces could neither advance nor retreat. 3. From 5 January, the NADK, guerrillas, and people in Kampot Province started their assault. They destroyed 12 large military trucks, two jeeps, 20 military motocycles, over 10 walkie-talkies, two 105-mm mortars, and 12 assorted cannons; seized 53 assorted pistols, 19 B-40's, seven machineguns, 15 crates of AK ammunition, 60 crates of B-40 and B-41 ammunition, and over 100 cannon shells.
