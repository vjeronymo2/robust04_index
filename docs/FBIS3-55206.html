Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin's meeting with representatives of the association of publishers and chief editors of the leading newspapers will be held either tonight or on Thursday. The agreement to hold the meeting was reached at the conference today of First Vice Prime Minister Vladimir Shumeyko, supervising the Press Ministry, with chief editors of the leading newspapers. The meeting was attended by the chief editors of such newspapers as "TRUD" (Potapov), "ROSSIYSKAYA GAZETA" (Natalya Polezhayeva), "KOMSOMOLSKAYA PRAVDA" (Vladislav Fronin), "IZVESTIYA" (first deputy chief editor Nikolay Bodnaruk) as well as the chairman of the state committee for the mass media, Boris Mironov, and the first secretary of the Moscow Journalists' Union, Ludmila Scherbina. Prior to this, the association of publishers and chief editors, unions of journalists of Moscow and Russia, Fund for Protection of Glasnost and other journalist organizations sent a letter to the prime minister listing their demands for saving the Russian press from a crisis. In particular, they demanded revising the government decision to recalculate the basic funds of printing, communications, pulp and paper and other cooperating industries, granting journalists the right to participate in the privatization of printing enterprises and other facilities, introducing subsidies for timely issuing of pre-paid editions, and lower taxes imposed on the mass media. In their letter, journalists proposed the prime minister "immediately receive representatives from the press and journalist organizations."
