Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn -- Brazil will support Germany's application for a nonpermanent two-year seat on the UN Security Council in 1995/1996. Brazil Foreign Minister Celso Amorim declared Brazilian support during a lengthy exchange of views with Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel (Free Democratic Party of Germany) today. In the long run, Germany aspires to a permanent seat on the Security Council. The talks that Amorim began at the Bundestag this morning focused on economic, technological, and environmental cooperation, the Foreign Ministry announced. Both ministers stressed the "excellent state of German-Brazilian relations." Kinkel thanked Amorim for his support concerning the final control regulation for the Brazilian nuclear power plants. He encouraged Brazil to "push ahead vigorously" with the ratification of the Tlatelolco Treaty, which provides for nuclear-free zones in Latin America and the Caribbean. It will "facilitate and widen" technical cooperation in the future, Kinkel stressed.
