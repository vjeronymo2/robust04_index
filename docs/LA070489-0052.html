July 4, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

SILICONE INJECTIONS INCREASE BREAST DISEASE RATE IN RATS

Question: I had a silicone implant operation to increase the size of my breasts. Now I've heard that these implants have been found to cause cancer. Is this true?

Answer: Silicone gel injected into the breast tissue of rats did result in an increased rate of breast cancer, but what is true for animals does not necessarily apply to humans. There is no evidence that these implants are a cause of cancer in women.

Q: It's bad enough to have diabetes, but a nephew of mine has diabetes and a heart condition. To top it off, although he'd like to keep physically active because he knows it would be good for the diabetes, he's afraid of doing anything too strenuous because of the heart condition. I assume that other people must have had this problem, and I'd like to know if anyone has come up with an answer to it.

A: Most people with heart conditions can engage in some form of exercise, but I can't even begin to suggest what might be appropriate for your nephew. He should check with his physician, who may be able to make specific recommendations for an exercise program, or who may refer him to a specialized center.

Q: Why should someone who appears to be in good health become constipated? If constipation does become a problem, is there any away to treat it without drugs?

A: Simple constipation -- that is, irregularity not due to some underlying disorder -- usually results from one of three conditions: a lack of bulk or liquid in the diet, insufficient exercise or failing to respond to body signals.

The frequency with which constipation is being reported these days has been attributed to changes in the diet -- away from high-fiber foods, such as fruits, vegetables and whole-grain breads, and toward fats and refined sugars. Irregular and inappropriate eating habits are important causes of constipation.

The hectic pace of modern life has also been implicated as a cause of irregularity. When a person tends to delay using the toilet, body signals may subside; if this pattern is repeated over a period of time, the result may be chronic constipation.
