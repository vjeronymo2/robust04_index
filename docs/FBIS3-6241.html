Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 14 KYODO -- The Fair Trade Commission (FTC) ordered 25 water supply meter equipment manufacturers Monday [14 March] to pay a 180 million yen penalty for their involvement in a bid-rigging affair, FTC officials said. The 25 companies were found to have violated the antimonopoly law by rigging bids on orders from the Tokyo Metropolitan Government, 15 municipal and town authorities in Osaka Prefecture and 16 municipal, town and village administrations in Fukuoka Prefecture, the officials said. Among the 25 firms were the Tokyo-based Kimmon Mfg. Co., the Nagoya-based Aichi Tokei Denki Co., the Kyoto-based Nitto Seiko Co., and OKK corp. and Toko Co., both based in Osaka. Over the years, the 25 firms held meetings to conspire in determining who would be awarded orders from local administrations and what prices would be set on equipment.
