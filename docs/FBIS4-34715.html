BFN [Excerpts] President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada arrived this morning at 0615 at the El Alto international airport. During his 11-day trip the Bolivian president visited Cartagena, Colombia, where he attended the Ibero-American summit; and the United States, where he attended the inauguration of the World Cup of soccer. During a news conference after his arrival, the president discussed the Bolivian-German soccer match. [passage omitted] The president also referred to the sea outlet issue, which was discussed both with Chilean President Eduardo Frei and at the foreign ministerial level. [Begin Sanchez recording] We want to advance at all levels, at the cultural, political, and economic levels, so that we can succeed in our great objective of achieving an outlet to the sea, both through the waterway and to the Pacific Ocean. I believe we had a good meeting, and that there is the willingness to continue negotiating. I also explained that we want to take specific steps to improve Bolivia's situation. [end recording] Regarding capitalization, journalists asked him whether he had been able during this trip to obtain more investments. He said: [Begin Sanchez recording] We witnessed real interest in this regard in the United States. For example, the Chicago Stock Exchange will be sending a delegation to Bolivia. They are interested in helping us establish a modern and fully computerized stock exchange in Bolivia. I believe things are working out, step by step, in a positive manner, and that there is good acceptance. Obviously this entails a lot of work, but I am optimistic. I believe we are obtaining positive results. [end recording] President Sanchez de Lozada said that his administration will consist of several stages. He said the first stage is that of sowing, which is the current stage. [Begin Sanchez recording] This stage consists of preparing the soil, of sowing. It is a very austere stage, and people do not always see results. Nevertheless it is a necessary stage, as there is no harvest without a season of sowing. We are very confident that during the second year of this administration we will be able to implement the capitalization program. In other words, we estimate that by 6 August 1995 we will have completed the capitalization process, and that the pension funds will be operating by the end of next year. [end recording] The president said that the harvest will be taken in during the third year. He said that the last year of his administration would be unquestionably a political year. Regarding the latest poll conducted by the Catholic University of Bolivia, which indicates that Bolivian public opinion believes a president should not also hold the post of MNR [Nationalist Revolutionary Movement] leader, the president said: [Begin Sanchez recording] We have a government system that is similar to that of a parliamentary system of government. Therefore, if a candidate lacks two-thirds, he has to go to Congress and establish a coalition. This is why I am the leader of a political coalition. This situation is not at all strange for Spain's Felipe Gonzalez, or for German Chancellor Kohl, because if they intend to implement their government programs they must be their party's leaders and establish coalitions in order to be able to govern. [passage omitted] [end recording] [passage omitted]
