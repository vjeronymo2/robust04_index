The cactus ferruginous pygmy-owl (Order Strigiformes; Family Strigidae) is one of three subspecies of the ferruginous pygmy-owl. It occurs from lowland central Arizona south through western Mexico, to the States of Colima and Michoacan, and from southern Texas south through the Mexican States of Tamaulipas and Nuevo Leon (Figure 1.). South of these regions and through Central America, G. b. ridgwayi replaces G. b. cactorum. Throughout South America, G. b. brasilianum is the resident subspecies (Fisher 1893, van Rossem 1937, Friedmann et al. 1950, Schaldach 1963, Phillips et al. 1964, de Schauensee 1966, Karalus and Eckert 1974, Oberholser 1974, Johnsgard 1988).
