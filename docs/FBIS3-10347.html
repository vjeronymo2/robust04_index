Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Lourdes Esquivel; from the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] Foreign Secretary Manuel Tello closed the 10th Mexican-Canadian Ministerial Meeting in Mexico City. The Canadian ministers and the Mexican secretaries and deputy secretaries comprised four groups that analyzed the possibility of bilateral cooperation in the transportation, agriculture, environment, and commerce sectors. Mexican Communications and Transportation Secretary Emilio Gamboa and Canadian Transportation Minister Douglas Young signed three transportation agreements that will allow reciprocal entry to drivers who carry products; the bilateral recognition of commercial drivers' licenses; and technical cooperation in training programs. Canadian Foreign Minister Andre Ouellet and Secretary Manuel Tello discussed regional and global politics. The Canadian foreign minister termed as surprising the efforts exerted to make the process as democratic as possible and said Canada believes in the political and social future of Mexico. Minister Ouellet also referred to the Chiapas conflict. He said Canada supports the actions carried out by the Mexican Government and encourages President Salinas de Gortari to continue along the same path. The Canadian foreign minister and Secretary Tello supported Colombian President Cesar Gaviria's candidacy for OAS secretary general Secretariat and they reasserted their support for the lifting of the blockade in Cuba. The next ministerial meeting will be held in Canada in 1995.
