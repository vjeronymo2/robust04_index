920429 FT 29 APR 92 / Arts: Today's Television The younger comedians in the wake of the generation which seems to be rapidly passing away (Eric Morecambe, Tommy Cooper, Frankie Howerd, Benny Hill) appear to be obsessed by boredom. Paul Morley begins a new run of his half-hour series The Thing Is .. with 'Boredom' (8.30 C4). He talks about boredom to Claire Rayner (there is a programme somewhere this week without Claire Rayner in it), Peter Cook, and Thora Hird. Then we are told that in Sean's Show Sean Hughes' best friend writes a book 'exposing Sean's incredibly boring life' (10.30 C4). Could it be that these young men suspect that, compared to their predecessors, they really are boring? Could they be right? BBC2's series The Buried Mirror about 'Spain and the new world', written and presented by Carlos Fuentes, began with a competent, uninspiring history of Spain. Today's episode compares Aztec customs and religion with the Christian variety of the Conquistadores. The BBC describes Mr Wakefield's Crusade as 'darkly comic'. Others might call it obscurely weird. Peter Capaldi plays the rich amateur sleuth (9.40 BBC2).
