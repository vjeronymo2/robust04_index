930705 FT 05 JUL 93 / Professor Gerald Curtis offers opinions on Japanese political funding reform PROFESSOR Gerald Curtis, probably the foremost western commentator on Japanese politics, offers this judgment about how Japanese politicians responded to the 1975 reform of political funding: 'Japanese politicians rose to the challenge posed by these reforms by searching out every conceivable loophole in the law that might allow them to avoid having to change their time-tried ways of doing things. 'The reforms offered eloquent testimony to the difficulty of engineering political change in a society that has resilient political institutions and traditions. 'They demonstrated not only that the consequences of reform cannot be entirely anticipated, but that they often exacerbate the problems they are intended to correct and create others that are worse.'
