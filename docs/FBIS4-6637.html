BFN [20 April PASASON Editorial: "Steps of Development of Lao-Malaysian Relations and Cooperation"] [Text] At the invitation of His Excellency [H.E.] Khamtai Siphandon, prime minister of the Lao People's Democratic Republic [LPDR], H.E. Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir bin Mohamed, prime minister of Malaysia, led a delegation of the Malaysian Government to pay an official, friendly visit to the LPDR from 16 to 18 April. The Malaysian Government delegation was honorably welcomed at an official ceremony. It was also accorded a warm welcome by H.E. Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon and the multiethnic Lao people everywhere it visited. Noteworthy is that during the visit, H.E. Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir bin Mohamed and his delegation paid a courtesy call on H.E. Nouhak Phoumsavan, president of the LPDR, which proceeded in an atmosphere of close friendship. The delegation also visited various places of economic, historic, and cultural significance in Vientiane capital and Luang Prabang Province. Official talks between a high-level LPDR delegation led by H.E. Khamtai Siphandon and a high-level Malaysian delegation led by H.E. Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir bin Mohamed were held at the prime minister's office in Vientiane. The talks proceeded in an atmosphere of profound friendship and mutual understanding. During the talks, the two sides informed each other about domestic situations, lines, and policies in their respective countries which they considered a firm basis for further, ever more vigorous development of Lao-Malaysian relations and cooperation in various fields, both in the state and private sectors, to bring about concrete benefits to the two peoples. The two sides discussed and exchanged views on various issues, including promoting investment between Laos and Malaysia, an issue in which the Malaysian side is interested. Following the talks, the two sides agreed to step up delegation exchanges at all levels. During the visit, a seminar on the foreign investment atmosphere in the LPDR was also organized. The Malaysian side also assured the Lao side of its willingness to render assistance in training Lao cadres. Part of the assistance will be directly provided by the Malaysian Government and the rest will be in the form of funds from a third country. Noteworthy is that the two sides have reached an agreement on repayment between the Bank of the LPDR and Bank Negara of Malaysia -- the central banks of the two countries. This agreement, together with the agreement on promoting and protecting investment which was signed in 1992, serves as a firm basis and marks a historic milestone for the further, ever more fruitful promotion and expansion of the existing good and friendly relations and cooperation between the two countries and the two peoples of Laos and Malaysia. Another significant point is that the prime ministers of the two countries believe that Laos and Malaysia have maintained sufficient conditions to promote and expand relations and cooperation in all spheres in the immediate future as well as in the long-term period. The multiethnic Lao people wholeheartedly hail the glorious success of the Malaysian prime minister and his delegation on this visit to the LPDR. The visit is considered a new step of development in relations and cooperation between the two countries. The success of the visit also serves as a basis for the two countries to increasingly promote, expand, and strengthen friendly relations and cooperation in various fields, which will thus contribute to establishing and promoting peace, stability, friendship, and cooperation in this region.
