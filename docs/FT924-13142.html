921016 FT 16 OCT 92 / International Company News: Mitsukoshi loss shocks retail sector JAPAN'S oldest department store, Mitsukoshi, yesterday sent shock waves through the retailing sector by announcing it had made an operating loss of Y379m (Dollars 3.16m) in the first half of this fiscal year. Mitsukoshi was one of a clutch of leading retailers, from supermarkets to department stores, which yesterday reported steep falls in profits under the impact of the current severe downturn in Japanese consumer markets. Mitsukoshi's pre-tax profits fell by 87 per cent to Y1,227m in the six months to the end of August on a 2.8 per cent drop in sales. The company, which invested heavily in expansion and refurbishment in the past five years expects its full-year pre-tax profits to fall by about 83 per cent to Y1.7bn. Mitsukoshi is cutting capital expenditure for the year from a planned Y25bn to Y10bn and directors are taking a 10 per cent pay cut. Seven-Eleven Japan, the leading convenience store reported a 5.1 per cent increase in pre-tax profits to Y45bn for the first half of the year, on a 10.6 per cent increase in sales to Y91.7bn. The company forecasts pre-tax profit growth for the year of about 9.4 per cent to Y85bn. However, Seven-Eleven's supermarket group parent, Ito-Yokado, reported only a 0.6 per cent increase in pre-tax profits to Y48bn in the first half, on a 4.6 per cent sales increase to Y749bn. Ito-Yokado's consolidated results, which include all its subsidiaries such as Seven-Eleven and Denny's Japan, the hamburger chain, were even more depressed, with pre-tax profit down in the first half by 12.1 per cent to Y97bn on sales growth of 11.3 per cent to Y1,523bn. Among other retailers reporting, Marui announced a 54 per cent drop in first half pre-tax profits to Y321m, Daimaru department store pre-tax profits fell by 47.4 per cent to Y1.6bn, Matsuzakaya reported a fall of 70 per cent to Y1.5bn while Nichii, the supermarket chain, announced its was reviewing its business forecasts after a 1.1 per cent rise in pre-tax profits to Y12.9bn.
