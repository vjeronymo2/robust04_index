BFN [Text] Ukraine's former Prime-Minister Leonid Kuchma stands the best chance to win the presidential race. According to the sociological service Sotcis-Gullap, 18 percent of the voters favored his candidacy in March. In December 16.6 percent of the voters did so. The service polled a total of 1,200 people older than 15 throughout Ukraine to find out that Viacheslav Chernovil (10 percent in March and 9.1 percent in December) and President Leonid Kravchuk (11 percent in March and 16.5 in December) emerged as two other frontrunners. The People's Rukh movement decided to refrain from running its own presidential nominee.
