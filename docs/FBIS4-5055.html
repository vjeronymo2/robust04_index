BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 7 KYODO -- The Transport Ministry on Thursday [7 April] announced bills to relax car inspection regulations as part of government deregulation efforts. The deregulation involves the elimination of rules requiring a car inspection once every six months as well as a reduction in items required for annual and biennial car inspections. The ministry plans to implement the latest measures next year, ministry officials said. The ministry expects the scrapping of the six-month inspection requirements to save car owners an average 15,000 yen and the reduction in items for annual and biennial inspections to save them 4,000 yen and 8,000 yen, respectively, they said.
