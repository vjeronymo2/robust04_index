Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Germany has assured India that it will continue to adopt a neutral stance on Kashmir and avoid making any value judgments in the matter. Kashmir figured prominently during the talks the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, had with the German leaders in Bonn. The prime minister explained to them the problems faced by India due to Pakistan's abetment of terrorism in Jammu and Kashmir and Punjab. Mr. Narasimha Rao also apprised the German leaders of the serious threat posed to the country's unity and integrity from cross-border terrorism. The prime minister said Pakistan is deliberately creating conditions for not having a meaningful dialogue. He also informed them about the confidence-building measures his government had taken in the past toward good neighborly relations with Pakistan. The issue also figured prominently during the foreign secretary-level talks in Bonn. The Indian side told the Germans about the setting up of the commission to look into the allegations of human rights abuses and permission given to the Amnesty International to visit the country. Attention was also drawn to the threat by Pakistan to move an anti-India resolution at the Geneva session on human rights. New Delhi sought Germany's help to thwart the attempt. Bonn assured that the anti-Indian motion on Kashmir will not serve any useful purpose. Both countries also called for wider representation in the UN Security Council to ensure the political effectiveness of the collective will of international community. Both India and Germany are aspirants of a permanent seat in the council.
