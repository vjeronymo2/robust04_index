March 29, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

NEW ROUTE, LONGER HOURS APPROVED FOR SHUTTLE

The Glendale City Council voted Tuesday to begin operating the popular Beeline shuttles two hours earlier and to expand service into the southern part of the city.

The expansion must be approved by the Los Angeles County Transportation Commission, but city officials predicted the new southern route could be in operation by June.

Under the council's proposal, the free shuttle service would begin at 7 a.m., instead of 9 a.m. Expanding the hours is expected to cost an additional $8,800 per month. The goal is to serve RTD commuters and motorists who drop their cars off for servicing before work, city officials said.

The new southern route would begin at the Amtrak station on Cerritos and Gardena avenues and run north along Central Avenue to Broadway. The shuttle would travel east on Broadway, south on Brand Boulevard, west on Laurel Street, south on Central back to the train station. Two shuttles would travel the 20-minute route, separated by 10-minute intervals. The new southern route is expected to cost $165,000 to start up and operate for the first year.

The southern route is aimed at patrons who visit auto dealers, medical offices, restaurants and other businesses in that part of the city.
