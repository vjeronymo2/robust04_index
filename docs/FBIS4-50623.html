BFN [By Sofia Wu] [Text] Taipei, May 19 (CNA) -- President Li Teng-hui Thursday [19 May] urged mainland Chinese authorities to face the reality of China's division with new concepts and new strategies. President Li made the call in his national affairs address to the National Assembly which is meeting on Yangmingshan in suburban Taipei to discuss sweeping constitutional reforms. Li said Beijing's resistance to recognizing China's division into two separate parts has hindered the establishment of mutual trust between the two sides of the Taiwan strait, adding he hopes the mainland leadership will abandon its aggressive attitude toward Taiwan and take the initiative to promote peaceful competition between the two sides for the well-being of the Chinese people. He continued that defending the security of Taiwan, Penghu, Kinmen and Matsu is now the top goal of the Republic of China [ROC]'s national defense policy. "We are seeking to beef up our defense capabilities by purchasing advanced arms and enhancing combat preparedness while trimming redundant military personnel," he explained. The ROC is also seeking to join as many international organizations as possible, the president noted. With the end of the Cold War, Li said, a new world order is taking shape. "Representation in major world organizations would enable US to participate in discussions and formulation of new international regulations," he noted. The ROC will continue to promote trade with its major trading partners, such as the United States, Japan and European countries, Li said. In addition, the ROC will strengthen economic cooperation with Southeast Asian nations as part of its efforts to help boost Asia-Pacific regional economic integration. Li pointed out that the Republic of China has created in the Taiwan area a multi-party democratic system which has never existed in Chinese history. "We should not be complacent with our current achievements, but should strive even harder for continued national development," he noted. Li said the government is determined to promote further economic and political reforms, foster a cultural renaissance and improve welfare services. The president said all National Assembly members are welcome to present their opinions on constitutional reforms and national affairs. He promised to attend the assembly sessions for the next five days to hear their opinions and reply to their questions. This is the first time for an ROC president to participate in the National Assembly national affairs discussions in their entirety. In the past, the president was present only on the first day of such sessions, with the secretary-general of the presidential office then attending the remaining sessions on the president's behalf. During its current special three-month session, the National Assembly is expected to pass a package of amendments to the ROC constitution to lay a framework for further democratic reforms, including direct popular election of the country's president and vice president.
