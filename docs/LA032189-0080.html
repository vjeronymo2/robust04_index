March 21, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: TV & VIDEO

KSLD-TV Channel 62, the first new TV station in the Los Angeles market in years, went on the air Monday night with programming supplied by Home Shopping Network, instead of Spanish-language programming as originally announced. Station president Jack Hodin said he made the change after he was unable to secure enough Spanish programs. "The group I had a prospective contract with was simply not prepared to get started," Hodin said. KSLD will broadcast from 9 p.m.-6 a.m. until April 3, when it will increase to 24 hours daily. Hodin said his station's Home Shopping programming is "differently packaged and presented" than that carried on Channel 46. ALEENE MacMINN
