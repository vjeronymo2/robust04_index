930323 FT 23 MAR 93 / Chilean economic growth at 10.4% Chile's gross domestic product grew a provisional 10.4 per cent last year, the highest growth rate of the past 27 years, the Central Bank said in a report, Reuter reports from Santiago. In 1991 Chile achieved GDP growth of 6 per cent. The 1992 figure exceeded forecasts by the Central Bank which put growth at just below 10 per cent. The bank has forecast 1993 GDP growth of between 5 per cent and 6 per cent. All sectors of the economy grew in 1992, but commerce grew fastest, showing growth of 14.3 per cent, mainly because of higher imports, followed by transport and communications, with 14.2 per cent. The Central Bank said imports grew 22.2 per cent in 1992, while exports grew 12.3 per cent.
