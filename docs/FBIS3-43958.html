Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The starting date for the training of Mozambique's infantry battalion involving Renamo [Mozambique National Resistance] and government troops is now scheduled for the first week in February. The announcement was made by UN Operation Deputy Force Commander Brigadier Anisur Rahman following yesterday's meeting in Maputo of the Joint Commission charged with setting up the new army. This date represents a new delay under the time table signed by the government, Renamo, and the UN last October. The training should have started this month. The 540 military instructors and [word indistinct] trained in Zimbabwe last year have returned to Mozambique. Brig. Rahman said the government and Renamo will name the members who are going to be part of the country's military high command in 8 days' time from today. Both sides are reported to have already selected representatives for the high command. The two sides also agreed on the insignia for the new army, on the criteria for the selection of troops who will be trained as special forces, and on norms for moving troops from assembly points where they are currently gathering, to the training centers.
