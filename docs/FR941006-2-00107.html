Relating to the Listing Criteria for Certain Hybrid Securities. September 30, 1994. I. Introduction On February 25, 1994, the Chicago Board Options Exchange, Inc. (``CBOE'' or ``Exchange''), pursuant to section 19(b)(1) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (``Act'') 1 and Rule 19b&hyph;4 thereunder, 2 filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (``SEC'' or ``Commission'') a proposed rule change to establish specific listing criteria for certain hybrid securities. Notice of the proposal appeared in the Federal Register on April 7, 1994. 3 No comment letters were received on the proposed rule change. II. Description of the Proposal Pursuant to CBOE Rule 31.5.F, the Exchange may approve for listing securities which can not be readily categorized under the listing criteria for common and preferred stocks, bonds, debentures, warrants, or unit investment trusts. The CBOE is now proposing to: (1) Amend Rule 31.5.F to decrease the minimum aggregate market value of such securities to $4 million; 6 (2) add Rule 31.5.H to provide listing standards for contingent value rights (``CVRs''); (3) add Rule 31.5.I to provide listing standards for equity-linked term notes (``ELNs''); and (4) add Rule 31.5.J to provide listing standards for paired securities. 6 See Amendment No. 1, supra note 4.
