920206 FT 06 FEB 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Dow loses ground after late trading pressure Wall Street SHARE PRICES idled in a slim range yesterday before losing ground in late trading with erosion in Eastman Kodak and Merck putting pressure on the Dow Jones Industrial Average, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow fell 15.21 to 3,257.60 on fairly heavy volume of 262m shares. But the decline did not represent overall market sentiment. On the big board, leading issues outpaced declining by 953 to 747, while other market indices showed gains on the day. The Standard & Poor's 500 closed 0.05 higher at 413.90 and the New York Stock Exchange Composite gained 0.08 to 228.87. On Tuesday, the Dow set a new record of 3,272.81, up 38.69 on the day. Bank and auto stocks, which helped spur Tuesday's rally, added to their previous day's gains. In the banking sector, Citicorp rose Dollars 5/8 to Dollars 16 in heavy trading and Chemical Bank was Dollars 5/8 higher at Dollars 29 5/8 . Chrysler led the auto stocks, gaining Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 16 1/2 . Ford added Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 33 1/2 but General Motors lost Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 34 7/8 after firming in the morning. Merck lost Dollars 1 1/8 in late trading after rising Dollars 4 1/2 on Tuesday, bringing the issue to Dollars 157 3/4 . Late Tuesday the company's Proscar drug received a favourable review from an advisory panel of the Food & Drug Administration. Eastman Kodak was the most active big board stock yesterday, tumbling Dollars 4 1/4 to Dollars 46 1/2 a day after the company unveiled a fourth quarter loss of Dollars 400m, including an after-tax charge of Dollars 597m. Polaroid slid Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 28 3/8 . Federated Department Stores, which emerged from Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection yesterday, was quoted at Dollars 17 3/4 in late trading. A number of high-tech issues performed particularly well early yesterday including National Semiconductor, up Dollars 3/4 to a 52-week high of Dollars 9 1/4 . Among other active high-tech stocks, Advanced Micro Devices moved Dollars 7/8 higher to Dollars 21 3/8 and Unisys rose Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 8. A positive analysts meeting at Browning Ferris, the second biggest US waste management company, prompted a Dollars 1/4 rise in the stock to Dollars 23. Trading was less active in Waste Management, the biggest US waste management group, which was unchanged on the day at Dollars 45 7/8 after the company filed a shelf registration of up to Dollars 1bn in unsecured debt. Chemical Waste Management, which handles hazardous waste, lost Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 20 1/2 after posting fourth quarter earnings of 6 cents a share. Over-the-counter stocks hit new highs in the morning, with the Nasdaq composite climbing 5.97 to 636.97 in late trading. Sun Microsystems and Intel were among the most active issues, adding Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 33 1/2 and Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 62 3/4 respectively. Better-than-expected results from Mentor Graphics, of 2 cents a share against an estimated 1 cent a share, helped the stock advance Dollars 1 1/4 to a new 52-week high of Dollars 20 1/4 . Canada TORONTO share prices staged a slight retreat in continued heavy trading. Based on preliminary data, the composite index dropped 12.15 points, or 0.34 per cent, to end at 3,605.92, with declining issues ahead of gainers 305 to 283. Volume was a solid 32.1m shares worth CDollars 383.8m, compared with the previous 30.8m shares worth CDollars 348.3m. CAE Industries reported higher third-quarter and nine-month earnings, and said its 1992 earnings per share will exceed 25 cents. The stock gained 1/8 to 6 5/8.
