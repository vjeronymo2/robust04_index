921016 FT 16 OCT 92 / UK Company News: Sanctions halt plan by Air London The troubles in Yugoslavia have blocked the efforts of an air charter broker to ease the effects of the recession. Air London International had contracted three aircraft from JAT, the Yugoslav national airline, for use on holiday flights. Mr Tony Mack, chairman, said that when the sanctions were imposed in May the activity came to a halt. Mr Mack made his comments as the USM-quoted company reported pre-tax profits down from Pounds 1.2m to Pounds 1.09m in the year to July 31. Turnover fell to Pounds 17.8m (Pounds 19.3m). Earnings per share were 8.1p (8.9p). The final dividend of 1.9p for an increased total of 3.5p (3.2p).
