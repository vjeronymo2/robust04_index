Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Manaus (Brazil), 14 Mar (EFE) -- The Manaus Federal Police Superintendency reported today that the armed forces of Colombia, Venezuela, Bolivia, and Peru have been invited by Brazil to join a large operation to block the drug trafficking mafia operating in the Amazon River basin. Superintendent Mauro Sposito said the plan was presented to those countries during the Sixth Military Exchange Meeting of the Armed Forces held in Sao Gabriel da Cachoeira last week. Sposito said Venezuela already has confirmed its participation in the campaign that will be named "Operation Blockade" [Operacion Bloqueo] and Colombia hinted that it also will support it. The Manaus (capital of Amazonas State) police superintendent explained that the plan includes the establishment of an information network among the armed forces of the countries sharing the Amazon basin. Sposito added that in Brazil the operation will cover all the federal organizations in Amazonas State and the national and multinational fuel distribution companies. Sposito said 200 Federal Police officers will work exclusively on the operation. Sposito also indicated that "Operation Blockade" has the basic purpose of fighting drug trafficking at the Brazilian border with the other four countries. Brazil also intends to be excluded from the list of countries used as transit routes by international drug traffickers. Sposito pointed out that Brazil and Venezuela have become the first countries on the cocaine route that starts in Colombia and ends in the United States and Europe. Therefore, said Sposito, the Colombian Armed Forces' participation will be essential for the operation's success. "Operation Blockade" seeks to intercept the flow of cocaine on the Amazon River by integrating the Brazilian river police patrols in Tabatinga (at the Colombian border) with the Manaus police. The operation also includes a follow-up by Brazil on actions against drug traffickers in Colombia. Strict air traffic control in the Amazon region that includes the destruction of fuel supply networks and clandestine airfields, as well as the neutralization of the air space in the large Amazon basin, are the main aspects of "Operation Blockade." The operation also will include two radio centers, which began operating last November, in Tabatinga and Tefe (Amazonas State) to intercept the drug trafficking routes.
