Fight Drugs, Other Crimes TA1504192694 Ankara TRT Television Network in Turkish 1700 GMT 15 Apr 94 TA1504192694 Ankara TRT Television Network Turkish BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Official talks were held between Turkey and Ukraine in Ankara today to develop security cooperation. The two countries signed an agreement on cooperation against crime. The agreement was signed by Interior Minister Nahit Mentese and Ukrainian Internal Affairs Minister Andriy Vasylyshyn. In a statement at the signing ceremony, Mentese said the agreement envisages effective and comprehensive cooperation between the two countries in fighting international narcotics smuggling, international terrorism, and other organized crimes. Mentese reported that it was decided to establish a joint committee with representatives from the two sides to supervise the cooperation and eliminate shortcomings. In his statement, Vasylyshyn declared that the two countries will undertake broad cooperation in the security field, including the struggle against the terrorist PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] organization. The Ukrainian minister concluded his contacts in Ankara and left for Istanbul. He was seen off at Ankara's Esenboga Airport by Mentese. Vasylyshyn will leave Turkey on 17 April.
