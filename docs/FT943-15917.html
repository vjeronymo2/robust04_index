940707 FT 07 JUL 94 / World Trade News: Yen's rise lifts imports of vehicles to Japan The yen's ability to defy gravity has been a boon to vehicle imports to Japan, which rose by 40.3 per cent to a record 137,966 units in the first half of this year, writes William Dawkins in Tokyo. Sales of imported vehicles climbed by 42.9 per cent last month from a year earlier, as Japanese consumers took advantage of the falling yen price of foreign cars. Overseas plants of Japanese producers have also benefited. Imports of foreign-made Japanese vehicles nearly doubled last month to just under 9,000 (30 per cent of all imports). Reuter adds from Washington: The US and Japan broke off trade talks yesterday and officials said the negotiators had failed to strike any deals for the Group of Seven summit in Italy this weekend. The two sides plan to resume talks after the summit, once seen as a deadline for a deal. But, said a US official, 'we had no expectations (for a G7 deal) and we have met them.'
