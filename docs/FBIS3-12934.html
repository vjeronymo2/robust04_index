Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Damascus, 7 Mar (MENA)--Muhammad Basyuni, Egypt's ambassador to Israel, has affirmed that Syria is the key to comprehensive peace, without it, there can be no comprehensive peace in the region. This came in an interview with the Egyptian ambassador published in one of the Israeli Arab newspapers of which some issues were distributed in Damascus by the Arab Israeli delegation currently visiting Damascus. Ambassador Basyuni clarified that despite the difficulty in the Syrian-Israeli peace track, it has clear characteristics. He noted that a comprehensive peace between Israel and Syria cannot be reached without full Israeli withdrawal from the Golan Heights. He affirmed that Syria cannot accept anything less than full withdrawal in return for comprehensive peace. He pointed out that peace is the essential demand of all people in the region without exception. He expects that 1994 will be the year of peace in which agreements on the Declaration of Principles will be signed on all peace tracks. As regards reports on terrorism in Egypt, the Egyptian ambassador affirmed in his interview that what is being reported about this topic is exaggerated. Violence and terrorism are an international phenomenon from which many progressive and developed countries are suffering. It is not restricted to Egypt alone.
