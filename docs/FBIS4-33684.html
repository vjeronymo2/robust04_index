BFN [Text] Food staples, medicine, school supplies, and medical equipment donated to Cuba by various Galician friendship organizations arrived on 9 June at the Port of Havana from Vigo, Spain. The 37-ton shipment was brought to Cuba by the Rio Jibacoa, a fishing vessel 106 meters in overall length, and with 3,200-ton displacement. Jose Ramon Fernandez, vice president of the Council of Ministers, was in attendance to receive the donation sent by the associations' Francisco Villamil, Carballino, Bartolome de las Casas, Gomez de Millan, the Galician autonomous government, and the Red Cross. Tomas Gonzalez Ramirez, captain of the Rio Jibacoa, explained that besides the Galician donation, the vessel also carried another donation consisting of 23 packages, mainly medicine, from the Spanish port of Alicante.
