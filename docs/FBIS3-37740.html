Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, February 22 (SNARK) -- Today the details of the yesterday's visit of Otar Patsatsia, the Georgian prime-minister, to Armenia became known. He has met with Levon Ter-Petrosyan, the Armenian president, and Grant Bagratyan, the prime-minister of Armenia. Today Vahan Papazyan, the foreign minister, told reporters about the negotiation. According to him Armenia in contrast to Georgia and other CIS's countries hasn't got debts for gas supplied by Turkmenistan. The minister said that Armenia is ready to give half of the gas purchasing in Turkmenistan to Georgia, however, in return for it, Georgia must provide unimpeded passing of loads for Armenia through its territory, including the loads from the ports of Poti and Batumi. According to Vahan Papazyan, Otar Patsatsia wasn't ready to accept the proposals of Armenian party. But Vahan Papazyan expressed the hope that after appropriate consultations the political authorities of Georgia will accept the proposal suggested by Armenia.
