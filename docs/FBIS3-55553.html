Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow January 10 ITAR-TASS -- Today, on the eve of the first sitting of the Federation Council, the upper chamber of the new Russian parliament, registration began of its deputies arriving in Moscow. Delegates to the chamber from all component parts of the Federation were elected in two-mandate constituencies formed in all republics, except Chechnya, and every territory, region, and autonomy. The list of the chamber's members comprise 171 deputies. According to the Russian Constitution, there total number must be 178, but no representatives were elected in Tatarstan and the Chelyabinsk region, where a repeat election is now scheduled. Only one deputy was elected in the Yamalo-Nenets Autonomous District. The overwhelming majority of the chamber's members say they are office workers (152). A considerable part of them are officials of regional legislative and executive bodies of power. Eight deputies are entrepreneurs, three are peasants, one is a serviceman, and seven are unemployed, a spokesman for the information department told ITAR-TASS. All of the deputies, except two, have graduated from institutions of higher learning. They include ten professors, and 19 doctors of sciences (twelve in economics, five in technical sciences and two in philosophy). Only two deputies are under 30 years of age, and seven are older than 65.
