BFN [Report by Shim'on Schiffer] [Excerpts] PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat has asked Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin to grant financial aid to the Palestinian autonomy in the Gaza Strip and Jericho, which is in dire economic straits. This request is included in one of 'Arafat's three letters to Rabin. Officials in the prime minister's bureau have said that in consultations held on the formulation of a reply to 'Arafat's missives, Rabin ruled that Israel cannot help the PLO, either directly or indirectly. A senior political source said yesterday that at a time when Israel lacks the necessary funds to help the Histadrut labor federation's sick fund, it cannot show generosity toward the PLO. At the same time, Rabin intends to formulate his reply in a positive spirit. [passage omitted] In the letters 'Arafat sent to Rabin, the PLO chairman proposes, among other things, to begin discussions as soon as possible on the early empowerment of the autonomy institutions throughout the rest of the West Bank. Rabin intends to accede to this request, and contacts will soon be held between PLO officials and Israeli political figures in a bid to organize talks on this issue. As reported yesterday in YEDI'OT AHARONOT, 'Arafat proposed that he and Rabin speak directly to one another and stressed the importance of establishing a "hot line" between them. 'Arafat also demanded that Rabin reaffirm Israel's commitment to preserve the status of Palestinian institutions in East Jerusalem. [passage omitted]
