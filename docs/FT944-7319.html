941125 FT 25 NOV 94 / EU funds for rural areas approved Formal approval by the European Union of Pounds 82m in structural funding for the uplands of northern England and Pounds 37m for Dumfries and Galloway over the next six years was given yesterday. Spending plans for eight other rural areas of Britain, involving a total of about Pounds 460m, are to be considered soon by the EU. The Pounds 46m spending plan for East Anglia has already been approved. The structural funds involved are for rural areas that need help to diversify their economic activity and create jobs. They provide grants towards the cost of infrastructure, vocational training, agricultural and fisheries restructuring and other measures.
