BFN [Text] Cairo, March 31 (AFP)--Israel and PLO put the final touches to a security agreement for the West Bank town of Hebron on Thursday before a scheduled signing ceremony, PLO spokesman Usamah Sharab said. Israeli deputy chief of staff, General Amnon Shahaq, and PLO chief negotiator Nabil Sha'th were to sign the agreement. "The two delegations are giving the text a final going over," he said. Both sides had asked for slight changes to be made, a source close to the negotiators said. The accord calls for the deployment of 160 international observers in Hebron, where a Jewish settler killed 30 Palestinian worshippers on February 25.
