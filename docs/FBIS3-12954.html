Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] An official round of talks was held this evening at the Foreign Ministry between Syria and the European Union [EU]. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' led the Syrian team and Mr. Karolos Papoulias, the Greek foreign minister, whose country holds the current presidency of the EU, led the European side. The talks were also joined by EU Commissioner General Hans Van den Broek; Yusuf Shakkur, Syria's assistant foreign minister; and the directors of the Economic Department, Western Europe Department, and the Information Department. For the EU side, the meeting was also attended by Mr. (Khristos Zakharakis), director general of the Greek Foreign Ministry; the director general of bilateral relations at the ministry; and Mr. (Sham Kunwa), an ambassador at the EU; and Mr. Yeorios Yennimatas, the Greek ambassador in Damascus. Discussions reviewed Middle East developments in light of the Ibrahimi Mosque massacre and its serious implications for the situation in the occupied Arab territories, the region, and the peace process. Mr. al-Shar' pointed to the unending obstacles Israel places in the path of peace, and its lack of seriousness in the search for a fair and comprehensive peace in the region. Europe's potential role in the peace process and Syrian-EU cooperation was also discussed. [passage omitted]
