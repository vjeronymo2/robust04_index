Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santiago, 30 Dec (EFE) -- Foreign Minister Enrique Silva Cimma today confirmed that the Chilean Government is looking into the possibility of resuming diplomatic relations with Cuba. He did not specify, however, whether this would take place before the change of government on 11 March. Silva Cimma told the media that the subject is being studied "carefully" and that "every aspect of what the relationship between Chile and Cuba represents" is being taken into account. Diplomatic relations between the two countries were broken following the 11 September 1973 coup to oust Chilean President Salvador Allende. When Patricio Aylwin assumed the Chilean presidency in March 1990, relations were restored at the consular level, despite the interest of the socialist sector of the governing Coalition of Parties for Democracy in fully resuming relations with an exchange of ambassadors. Since then, however, there has been a strong rapprochement in the commercial field between the two countries and several Chilean businessmen have invested in the tourism and agroindustrial fields in Cuba. From the political viewpoint, Carlos Lage, vice president of the Cuban State Council and executive secretary of the Council of Ministers, visited Chile in response to an invitation from the Chilean Government. Lage said that the invitation was a demonstration of the mutual interest in moving toward a gradual integration process Last November, Chile voted at the UN General Assembly in favor of lifting the U.S. commercial embargo on Cuba. According to political sources mentioned by the Santiago daily EL MERCURIO, the Chilean Government wants to resume relations before 11 March in view of Cuban President Fidel Castro's interest in attending the presidential inauguration. EL MERCURIO goes on to say that within the Chilean Government there are also some who are against this idea. They consider it inadvisable that Castro be at the ceremony because the display he makes during his trips abroad might overshadow Chilean President-elect Eduardo Frei. According to EL MERCURIO, these sectors also believe that Castro might create problems with other countries with which Chile wishes to maintain good relations. The daily thus recalled the public disputes between Castro and Argentine President Carlos Menem a few months ago in La Paz during the inauguration of Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Losada.
