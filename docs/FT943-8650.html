940816 FT 16 AUG 94 / UK Company News: BOC buys Dutch group for Pounds 10.8m BOC, the industrial gases and healthcare company, has bought a Dutch equipment supplier for Fl 29.4m (Pounds 10.8m). The Calumatic group, which supplies filling, sterilising and capping lines to the pharmaceutical industry, will be integrated with Edwards Freeze Drying, part of BOC's vacuum technology division and a supplier of freeze driers to the pharmaceutical industry. The acquisition will be funded by cash, a proportion of which is deferred and contingent on future performance. Mr Danny Rosenkranz, managing director, said the acquisition 'allows us to develop our freeze-drying business'. The new business, BOC Edwards Calumatic, will be able to provide complete packaging solutions for a variety of injectable formulations. BOC is implementing a three-year restructuring, and made an Pounds 85m provision in its latest accounts, Pounds 60m of which was for the healthcare business. It reported a fall in pre-tax profits to Pounds 169.3m, compared with Pounds 261.6m, in the nine months to June 30. BOC shares rose 7p to 732p.
