Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Jan. 6 -- Maciej Ilowiecki, chairman of the Council for Mass Media attached to the presidential office, said on Thursday that a practicing journalist or a theoretician should be appointed presidential adviser for mass media rather than Janusz Zaorski who did not meet these requirements. Walesa appointed Zaorski, former head of the State Radio and television, to the post on Wednesday. Ilowiecki, who is deputy chairman of the Radio and TV Council (KRRIT), was asked to comment on the matter during a press conference of the council. The president's press office announced Wednesday that Zaorski would fulfill his function gratuitously.
