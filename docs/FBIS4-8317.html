BFN [Text] Prague, April 12 (CTK) -- Czech President Vaclav Havel firmly believes that the April 10 NATO bombing raid against the Bosnian Serb positions around Gorazde will be the last real warning that would bring the Bosnian Serbs to the negotiating table, Havel told CTK and the daily RUDE PRAVO today. "I have long thought is was necessary that the international community use stronger means to let it be known that (the Serbs) cannot do whatever pleases them," Havel said. Two U.S. Air Force F-16 jets under NATO command attacked Bosnian Serb units which had entered the southern suburbs of Gorazde. It was the first NATO air strike against ground targets in Bosnia.
