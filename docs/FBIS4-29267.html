BFN [By YONHAP from Moscow] [Text] Regarding our country's media reports that China will provide military assistance to North Korea in case a military collision breaks out on the Korean peninsula, a spokesman for the Chinese Foreign Ministry denied such reports, reported ITAR-TASS in a Beijing-dispatched story. The Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman, in a telephone interview with ITAR-TASS, strongly denied such reports by saying that the reports are a absolutely groundless fiction [chonjoguro ssulttae omnun hogurago]. The news agency reported that the spokesman, however, kept silent on reports that a close woman relative of North Korean President Kim Il-song visited Beijing at the end of May and agreed with the Chinese side to receive food and energy if economic sanctions are imposed on North Korea.
