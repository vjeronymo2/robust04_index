BFN [Text] Silistra, March 16 (BTA) -- The tenth session of the Mixed Bulgarian Romanian Commission on Environmental Problems in the Silistra-Calarasi Region, held in Calarasi, decided to propose to the environment ministries of the two countries, to introduce Western European standards in environment monitoring in Bulgaria and Romania, Dr. Marin Peychev, director of the Hygiene and Epidemiology Institute in Silistra, told BTA. In the almost ten-month break in the work of the commission, the Romanians managed to replace over 80 percent of the purification installations of the metallurgical combined works in Calarasi which reduced phenol, hydrogen sulphide and dust emissions. The Bulgarian Institute of Hygiene, Medical Environment and Nutrition carried out joint research with a similar Romanian institute. The Romanian side showed interest in environment related diseases incidence. The Bulgarian side presented the required information and research methods. The measurements taken in January and February in Bulgaria have shown lower concentrations of pollutants against the same period last year.
