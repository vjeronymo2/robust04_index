Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The Second Session of the Eighth Hebei Provincial People's Congress ceremoniously opened at the assembly hall of the Shijiazhuang No. 1 Workers Cultural Palace on 25 February. Lu Chuanzan, Li Yongjin, Zhang Zhenhuan, Liu Zongyao, Dong Naifang, Hao Yongtang, Ning Quanfu, Wang Honglian, and Zhou Xin, executive members of the presidium of the session, were seated in the front line of the rostrum. Seated on the rostrum were Cheng Weigao, Ye Liansong, Li Bingliang, Li Wenshan, Ren Peiyu, Chen Yujie, Han Licheng, Chen Liyou, Xu Yongyue, Zhao Jinduo, Li Zhanshu, Li Haifeng, Guo Hongqi, Gu Erxiong, Wang Youhui, Liu Zuotian, Song Shuhua, Wang Zuwu, Zhang Runshen, Wang Shusen, Huang Lan, Du Benjie, Zhao Huichen, Ma Xinyun, Yu Zhenzhong, Wang Manqiu, Chen Hui, Zhao Yan, Han Shiqian, and (Yuan Ruxian). Ping Yijie, president of the provincial court; Liu Zongxin, chief procurator of the provincial procuratorate; and all members of the presidium were also seated on the rostrum. Also seated on the rostrum were Liu Bingyan, Zhang Shuguang, Xie Feng, Yin Zhe, Yang Zejiang, Guo Zhi, Yue Zongtai, Liu Ying, Hong Yi, Bai Shi, Pan Chengxiao, and Xu Chunxing. At 0900, Lu Chuanzan, executive member of the session, declared the opening of the second session of the eighth provincial people's congress. [passage omitted] [Begin recording] [Lu Chuanzan] Now, we are going to ask Governor Ye Liansong to deliver the work report of the Hebei Provincial People's Government. [Ye Liansong] Fellow deputies: On behalf of the Hebei Provincial People's Government, I am going to deliver the government work report to the session for examinations and discussions and also ask all members of the provincial committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [CPPCC] and all other personnel participating in the session as nonvoting delegates to give opinions on the report. [end recording] Governor Ye Liansong's government work report consists of seven parts. First, review the 1993 work. Second, the guiding ideologies of the 1994 work and the 1994 fighting targets. Third, persistently combine overall progress with key breakthroughs and accelerate the reform work of the province as a whole. Fourth, strive to promote sustained, fast, and sound development of the national economy. Fifth, promote the development of science and technology, education, and culture. Sixth, realistically strengthen the spiritual civilization and the setup of democracy and the legal system. Seventh, strive to improve and strengthen government work. [passage omitted] The participants in the second session of the seventh provincial CPPCC committee attended the session as nonvoting delegates.
