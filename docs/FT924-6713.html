921120 FT 20 NOV 92 / International Company News: O&Y agrees collateral deal with creditors OLYMPIA & York, once the world's biggest real estate developer, is likely to shrink to a modest-sized property manager during the next few months under an agreement hammered out with its creditors. In return for an eight-week postponement of votes on a contentious debt-restructuring plan, the Reichmann family, O&Y's owners, have agreed to allow creditors to seize collateral after January 15 and not to obstruct their efforts in the US courts. Among the assets which are almost certain to be seized are First Canadian Place, O&Y's 72-storey flagship in the heart of Toronto's financial district, and Scotia Plaza, a modern 68-storey building nearby. A spokesman for one group of creditors said yesterday that 'by the middle of January, we should have our building unless someone wants to buy our debt.' O&Y has already lost control of Canary Wharf in London's Docklands and some of its properties in New York, including 55 Water Street, which is the world's biggest office building. The Reichmanns' latest humiliation came in the wake of fierce opposition to a debt-restructuring proposal presented by O&Y last month. The opposition was led by Canadian banks and bondholders with claims secured by specific buildings, who were confident they would be better off seizing their collateral than agreeing to the concessions proposed by O&Y. Under this week's agreement, expected to be approved by an Ontario court on Monday, O&Y must file a new restructuring plan for its 34 creditor groups by December 15, with votes to be held no later than January 15. Under the old plan, the votes were to be held between November 25 and 30. A lawyer for one group of creditors said yesterday 'the revised plan will in effect be a liquidation.'
