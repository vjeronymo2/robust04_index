BFN ["U.S. Moves Against DPRK Condemned Abroad" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, April 7 (KCNA) -- Public organizations of different countries denounced the anti-DPRK acts of the United States which reversed the agreed conclusions reached at the DPRK-U.S. contact in New York and is aggravating the situation on the Korean peninsula. The Committee for Supporting Korea's Reunification, the Peace Action Committee and 14 other organizations of Nepal in a joint statement criticized the United States for reversing its commitment and massing its Armed Forces around the Korean peninsula in a hasty move to resume the "Team Spirit" joint military exercises and deploy Patriot missiles in South Korea, in disregard of the expectations and hope of the world public. The statement strongly urged the United States to desist from complicating the situation of the Korean peninsula any longer by sincerely implementing its commitment to the DPRK on the principles of the DPRK-U.S. joint statement, not going against the trend of the times. The Jordanian Committee for Solidarity with the Korean People in a statement voiced opposition to the U.S. interference in the internal affairs of Korea, urging an immediate halt to the frantic anti-DPRK campaign. The Central Committee of the Organization of Workers of Mozambique in a statement denounced as unreasonable and unfair the act of the United States and the International Atomic Energy Agency in transferring the "nuclear issue" to the UN Security Council. The National Liberation War Veterans Committee of Zimbabwe demanded in a letter to the U.S. chief executive that the United States to [halt] at once it preparations for a war of aggression and embark on a negotiated settlement of the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula.
