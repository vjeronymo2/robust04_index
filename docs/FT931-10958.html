930205 FT 05 FEB 93 / Weapons workforce contract awarded A JOINT VENTURE controlled by the Hunting group has won the contract to take over operation of the government's Atomic Weapons Establishment. Under the seven-year contract, which takes effect from April, Hunting will take responsibility for the workforce, now part of the Civil Service. Its size is classified information but is reckoned to be about 7,000. The facilities, where Britain's nuclear warheads are designed and made, will stay in government hands. The deal covers all four facilities at Aldermaston and Burghfield in Berkshire, and at Foulness, Essex, and Cardiff.
