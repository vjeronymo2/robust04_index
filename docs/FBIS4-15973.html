BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow April 13 TASS -- 100 angry Ingushis blocked a military township with Russian peace-keepers in Nazran on Wednesday in protest against the seizure by North Ossetians of an Ingushi hostage, TASS learned from the temporary administration in the zone of conflict. On Tuesday North Ossetians stopped a convoy of Ingushi refugees returning to the North Ossetian village of Kartsa. They seized six Ingushis who they planned to exchange for three Ossetians captured on March 30. Russian peace-keepers are to safeguard the return of hostages. Head of the Temporary Administration in the State of Emergency Zone Vladimir Lozovoy succeeded to talk the Ossetians into freeing the Ingushis, however one hostage remained in captivity and his fate is unknown. According to the source, the tensions may tell on the second round of negotiations between the North Ossetian and Ingush presidents scheduled to take place in Nalchik.
