January 8, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

'TRADITION' AND THE WORKING WOMAN

For the last two decades, women have been striving to earn their due recognition as individuals -- equal partners with men in the home and workplace. While this change has created anxiety in many women and threatened many men, the net result has given all of us greater options in how we choose to live our lives.

I am discouraged by the recent trend in politics and advertising that professes a return to the "traditional values." As a single parent, I value the unique strength a family can provide, regardless of the form it takes.

Each family must be created by individuals willing to give equally in a manner that best suits its circumstance. The "life style" professed by the Good Housekeeping ad campaign dictates a stereotype that can only create unnecessary anxiety and frustration for women, and men, too.

Parents and children must create their own roles in the changing family and not give in to the simplistic wishes of the advertising industry. Change can be positive when viewed as an opportunity rather than a threat.

RICHARD ALAN HALES

Laguna Hills
