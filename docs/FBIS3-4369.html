Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [By correspondent Yuji Kanemoto] [Text] Beijing, 20 Mar -- During talks with Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa on 20 March, Chinese Premier Li Peng touched on the reform of the United Nations which Japan has proposed. He said: "I support the idea of reforming the UN Security Council at an appropriate time." However, he added: "Because it will affect interests of various countries, careful consideration is necessary." It is said that by these remarks, Li indicated China's cautious stand about giving support to Japan for its bid to become a permanent member of the UN Security Council through its reform.
