April 6, 1989, Thursday, Orange County Edition

WHAT THE POLICE ARE PACKING

The attempts to ban so-called assault rifles is just the latest in a series of controversies involving firearms. Police officers have complained, for example, that they are being outgunned. And while no one can be sure what the bad guys are packing, Orange County police officers have several alternatives. The following list shows the official sidearm issued for individual officers, what is carried in patrol cars and the hardware used by special units such as SWAT teams:

ANAHEIM

Individual officer issue: Smith & Wesson .38- or .45-caliber revolver, 9-millimeter semiautomatic or .45-caliber semiautomatic pistol. City issues the weapon.

Patrol car: Remington 870 12-gauge shotgun.

Special unit: Automatic assault rifles; high-powered sniper rifles; Heckler & Koch MP-5 rifles.

BREA

Individual officer issue: Smith & Wesson .38 or .45 revolver or .45 semiautomatic pistol. City issues the weapon.

Patrol car: Remington 870 shotgun.

Special unit: Remington 870 laser-aimed shotguns and Ruger mini-14 carbines.

BUENA PARK

Individual officer: Smith & Wesson .38 or .45 revolver, .357 magnum, or 9-mm. semiautomatic pistol. Officers provide their own weapons.

Patrol car: Remington 870 shotgun.

Special unit: Sawed-off shotguns; Heckler & Koch MP-5 rifles; AR-15 assault rifles; Benelli Super-90 M-1 12-gauge shotguns.

COSTA MESA

Individual officer: Smith & Wesson semiautomatic .45 pistol. City issues the weapon.

Patrol car: Remington 870 shotgun.

Special unit: AR-15 assault rifles; Uzi submachine guns; .308 sniper rifles; Remington 1100 12-gauge shotguns.

CYPRESS

Individual officer: Smith & Wesson .38 revolver or 9-mm. semiautomatic pistol. City issues the weapon.

Patrol car: Remington 870 shotgun.

Special unit: Use Sheriff's Department.
