921125 FT 25 NOV 92 / International Company News: Court extends O & Y protection AN Ontario bankruptcy court last night approved a motion by Olympia & York Developments to extend the period the developer is protected from creditors in order to allow time for a debt restructuring plan to be negotiated, Reuter reports. It ordered the stay of protection to be extended to February 1 from December 30, and the dates creditors will vote on O&Y's proposed plan to between January 11 and 15 from November 25 to 30. O&Y and its creditors are still working on developing a plan to restructure about CDollars 8.6m (USDollars 6.82m) in debts on its Canadian assets. A new plan is now scheduled to be presented to the court by December 14. DELL Computer has received an 'informal request' from the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) for information and documents relating to trading patterns in the company's shares, writes Louise Kehoe in San Francisco. The personal computer manufacturer said that the SEC's request was focused on trading surrounding the company's third-quarter earnings announced last Thursday and upon 'subsequent events prompted by remarks made by a Kidder Peabody analyst on the following day'. AMERICAN Re, the Princeton-based reinsurance company that was bought out earlier this year from Aetna Life and Casualty by its management and Kolhberg, Kravis Roberts, the leveraged buy-out specialists, is coming back to the stock market, writes Nikki Tait. The insurance company announced yesterday that it planned to make an initial public offering - or flotation - of ordinary shares, to be completed by early 1993.
