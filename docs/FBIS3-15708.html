Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Boris Yeltsin reaffirmed today that Ostankino and the Russian channel television [RTV] should be state-owned. He said this to journalists at the end of his meeting today with the heads of both television companies, which he described as difficult but interesting. The president had a brief meeting with journalists following the ceremony of laying wreaths at Yuriy Gagarin's grave. Replying to an ITAR-TASS correspondent's question, Yeltsin reaffirmed his position set out in the president's annual message to the Federal Assembly to the effect that the first and second programs should be state-owned. Otherwise, Yeltsin believes, we may lose it, it may be broken up by various parties and currents. The Russian president said that he had today received from Aleksandr Yakovlev a milder draft of the edict and an aide-memoire on this question. Oleg Poptsov also presented him with an aide-memoire. In Yeltsin's opinion, the head of Russian Television is of the opinion that the Russian channel should be state-owned. Yeltsin noted that it was not just a question of the problems of television, but of the development of political processes in the country.
