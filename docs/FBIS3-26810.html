Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [From the "News" program] [Excerpts] On the afternoon of 4 February, the regional committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [CPPCC] hosted a tea party at its club to mark the Tibetan New Year -- the Year of the Wooden Dog -- and the Spring Festival. Leaders of party, government, and military departments in Tibet, including Gyaincain Norbu, Basang, Denzim, Lieque, Buqiong, Hu Yongzhu, Puqung, Sengqen Lozong Gyaincain, Pudoje, Namgyai, Samding Doje Pamo, Baima Juoji, Cering Sangzhub, Yang Song, Cering Zuoga, Losang Toinzhub, Luosang Danzeng, Jangzhong Zhaxi Doje, Lhamin Soinam Lhunzhub, Jimzhong Gyaincain Puncog, Lhalu Cewang Doje, Tangmai Gongjue Baimu, Gonbasar Tubdanjigzha, Cedain Zhoima, and Gama Cedain; Zhang Zhu, political commissar of the Tibet Armed Police Corps; Bai Zhao, president of the regional higher people's court; Yang Youcai, chief procurator of the regional people's procuratorate; as well as members of the CPPCC National Committee in Lhasa, and responsible persons of regional departments, bureaus, committees, and offices and relevant departments in Lhasa attended the tea party. [passage omitted] On behalf of the regional party committee and the regional government, Danzim, deputy secretary of the regional party committee, spoke at the tea party. Deputy Secretary Danzim said: Guided by Comrade Deng Xiaoping's theory on building socialism with Chinese characteristics and the guidelines laid down by the 14th national party congress and the fourth enlarged plenary session of the Fourth Tibet Regional Party Committee, Tibet achieved noticeable successes in 1993 in deepening reform and opening up wider to the outside world, as well as in building a socialist market economic system and speeding up the national economic development. In 1993, Tibet's GNP reached 3.65 billion yuan, topping that of the preceding year by 8.1 percent; the economy in agricultural and pastoral areas continued to develop steadily; and the gross agricultural output topped that of 1992 by 5.3 percent, reaching 2.28 billion yuan. In 1993 the region had a bumper agricultural harvest for the sixth year in a row with total grain output reaching 620,000 tonnes, which was a 3.3 percent increase over that a year ago. In animal husbandry, Tibet was able to maintain the main targets set for 1992. Township and town enterprises and other economic undertakings developed quite fast. The per capita income of peasants and herdsmen was about 515 yuan, or an increase of 5.1 percent over that of 1992. Industrial and communications enterprises had noticeably higher returns. Foreign trade and tourism maintained the good developmental momentum. New progress was achieved in bringing in outside resources and establishing ties with other parts of the country. The urban and rural markets became even more prosperous. All social services developed quite rapidly. New victories were won in the struggle against splittism. In 1993, Tibet's economy continued to develop, its political situation was more stable, people of various nationalities are more united, and new progress was achieved in all fields. The situation was good as a whole. [passage omitted] At the tea party, Lhamin Soinam Lhunzhub, vice chairman of the regional CPPCC committee, spoke on bahelf of the regional CPPCC committee.
