Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow February 4 TASS -- A regular plenary meeting of the State Duma, the lower chamber of the Russian parliament, opened here today. According to the preliminary agenda, handed out to MPs before the meeting, the Duma is to hear an information [as received] on decisions adopted by the chamber's council, a report of the Mandate Commission on changes in the composition of the State Duma, and also to discuss a bill "on the publication and entry into force of federal constitutional laws, federal laws, and acts of the two chambers of the Federal Assembly", and a draft resolution to "ensure the work of the State Duma". The Duma is also expected to endorse the composition of its delegation to a meeting of the Council of the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly, scheduled to meet in St. Petersburg from February 8-9. The agenda also includes the so-called "government hour", during which the Duma is expected to hear a report by Central Bank Chairman Viktor Gerashchenko and newly-appointed Finance Minister Sergey Dubinin on ways to settle the crisis of non-payments. It is not ruled out that the MPs will also hear several political statements by parliamentary factions. This item also figures in the Duma's parliamentary agenda. Aleksandr Yakovlev, the Russian president's plenipotentiary representative to the Federal Assembly (parliament), will be presented to the MPs today.
