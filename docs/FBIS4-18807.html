BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] A high-level joint defense group meeting between Turkey and the United States ended in Ankara today. A memorandum of understanding on the meeting was signed by General Ahmet Corekci, deputy chief of the General Staff, and Charles Freeman, U.S. under secretary of defense. In a statement at the signing ceremony, Corekci said that the Turkish Armed Forces' power structure for the 21st century and their long-term needs for military equipment were determined. Freeman, in turn, noted that his country regards Turkey as the third largest country in Europe. Answering reporters' questions, Freeman declared that his country views the PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] as a terrorist organization and does not support it. He remarked that he had the opportunity to observe more closely Turkey's just struggle against terrorism and its respect for human rights in waging this struggle.
