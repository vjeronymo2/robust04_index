September 14, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

HARVARD AT BEVERLY HILLS

In its opener, Beverly Hills showed little defense against Lompoc and none against tailback Napoleon Kaufman, who led the Braves to a 45-24 rout of the Normans. Kaufman scored four touchdowns, including one on a 95-yard kickoff return and another on a 73-yard interception. He rushed for more than 140 yards as well.

Harvard lost in its debut, to Blair, 10-8, but the Saracens received a standout performance from junior defensive back-wide receiver Iheanyi Uwaezuoke , who was born in Nigeria. Uwaezuoke, who presents difficulties not only to opponents but also to game announcers, was particularly effective on defense, making 10 solo tackles, forcing a fumble and knocking down a pass.

The Normans will play host to the Saracens at 3 p.m. Friday.
