BFN [Text] President Glavkos Kliridhis said that he is in continuous contact with Greek Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou, who is on an official visit to the United States. The last briefing, he added, took place last night during a telephone conversation in which they discussed the latest developments in the Cyprus issue. President Kliridhis made the above remarks this morning at Larnaca Airport before departing for Bucharest, Romania. In Bucharest, the president will address a seminar on the political and economic developments in the Black Sea, Mediterranean and surrounding countries. President Kliridhis also noted that Joe Clark, UN secretary general's special representative in Cyprus, will go to Ankara on Sunday [24 April] in an attempt to mobilize Turkey regarding its influence on Rauf Denktas. He also said that Clark will come to Cyprus next Wednesday. In Bucharest, President Kliridhis will also have talks with Romanian President Ion Iliescu, during which they will examine issues of telecommunications and economic cooperation between Cyprus and Romania. President Kliridhis will return to Cyprus tomorrow.
