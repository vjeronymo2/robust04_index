BFN [Text] Santo Domingo, 21 Jun (EFE) -- The military that toppled Haitian President Jean-Bertrand Aristide must receive "some guarantees" for themselves and their relatives within the pact to resolve the crisis in Haiti, Dominican Deputy Foreign Minister Fabio Herrera Cabral said today. Herrera Cabral said that sending the Haitian military to diplomatic posts "so they could be quiet there" could be a solution. He said a solution to the Haitian crisis, however, "depends on Haitians themselves resolving their problems, on the patriotism of Haitians, on the flexibility of Aristide since he is part of the crisis." The Dominican official added: "Each one has to do his share, yield a little." To achieve a political resolution, one has to yield part of his sovereignty, what he believes to be his personal interests. Herrera Cabral justified the U.S Government position of negotiating the stepping down of the Haitian military without need of a military intervention. Regarding the list of food and medicines that Haiti will be allowed to import through the border with the Dominican Republic, he explained there is no "list" since UN Resolution 917 allows humanitarian assistance. Yesterday, Monday, Dante Caputo, special UN and OAS envoy for the Haitian crisis, handed Dominican President Joaquin Balaguer a UN Security Council grantee, allowing food and medicine to enter Haiti from this country.
