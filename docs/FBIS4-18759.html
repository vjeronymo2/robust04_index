BFN [Text] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- Early today, the German Government set in motion the evacuation of German nationals affected by the civil war in Rwanda. The Press Office of the Foreign Ministry stated that, on instructions from Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel, Harald Ganns, the German Government's commissioner for African policy, has been flown by Bundeswehr aircraft to Bujumbura to organize the return of Germans arriving there from Rwanda. The Defense Ministry has provided a 200-seater aircraft. The Foreign Ministry reported that approximately 180 Germans -- chiefly development aid workers and their families -- are in Bujumbura. They reached safety in Burundi yesterday from war-torn Rwanda. It was stated that Kinkel has issued instructions that other foreign nationals who had to flee Burundi would be flown out on the Bundeswehr aircraft if there are any free spaces.
