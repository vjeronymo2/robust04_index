Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, March 24 (KCNA) -- Comrade Kim Chong-il, supreme commander of the Korean People's Army, sent a sixtieth birthday table to Yi Tu-ho, a branch curator of the Fatherland Liberation War Museum. Born into the family of a day labourer before the liberation of the country, Yi could enjoy a true human life and graduate a university in the liberated homeland. He was honored with a commendation of the great leader President Kim Il-song, order of national flag first class and many other state decorations for his devoted services in dealing with revolutionary historical relics. Comrade Kim Chong-il sent a centenary birthday table to An Sang-o, an ordinary woman residing in the neighbourhood unit No.9 of Sungri-tong No.3, Naknang District, Pyongyang. Born into a poor tenant farmer's family on the turu islet in Pyongyang, she devoted her all to defending the system which gave her happiness during the fatherland liberation war and honestly worked for the country in the period of the socialist construction, too. Her family of 49, including sons, daughters, grandsons and great grandsons, is leading a happy life under the loving care of the socialist system.
