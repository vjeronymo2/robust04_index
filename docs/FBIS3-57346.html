Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In a speech to the closed meeting of the parliamentary majority in Tbilisi late on Monday [24 January], Georgian leader Eduard Shevarnadze called to tighten discipline and law enforcement in all tiers of management including parliament, well-informed sources told Interfax. Shevardnadze demanded that parliamentary chief of staff Gamlet Chipashvili be sacked. He blamed the official for failing to include the main point about the country's territorial integrity in a memorandum published in Tbilisi on the results of Georgian-Abkhazian talks in Geneva.
