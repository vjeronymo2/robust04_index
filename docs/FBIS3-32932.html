Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] President Ramiro de Leon Carpio and U.S. Ambassador Marilyn McAfee inaugurated the Fuertes Caminos 94 campaign at the municipal stadium in Salama, Baja Verapaz yesterday. The purpose of this project is to provice humanitarian aid and real opportunities to the communities in the region and to establish ties of cooperation. The campaign will also provide training for some 300 U.S. National Guard Reserve troops. The U.S. soldiers will work with personnel from the Communications, Public Health, and Education Ministries for six months. The work will include constructing bridges, buildings, and rural roads, digging wells, and carrying out medical and educational campaigns. Also present for the inaugural ceremonies were the ministers involved in the project, Congress President Vinicio Villar Anteu, and several deputies. De Leon Carpio thanked the United States for their assistance and asked for support in his fight against poverty.
