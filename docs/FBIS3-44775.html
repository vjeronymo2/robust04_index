Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 6 (CNS)--China's metallurgical industry received U.S.$900 million of foreign investment last year with direct investment by foreign businessmen of U.S.$150 million, according to the Ministry of Metallurgical Industry. Foreign government loans were given to expansion projects of the Baoshan iron and Steel Company, the Wuhan Iron and Steel Company and the Daye Steel Works expansion project in Hubei province and the seamless steel project of the Anshan Iron and Steel Company. U.S.$500 million was raised from the issuance of H stocks in Hong Kong by the Maanshan Iron and Steel Shareholding Company. China has also made progress in its cooperation in the exploitation of mines with Brazil, Mongolia and South Africa with more than 30 million tonnes of ore being imported to China. China has also developed and cultivated its internal market for iron and steel products with such markets in Tianjin and Guangdong now operating. The state has further eased price controls over iron and steel products with such products whose prices were set by the state being reduced to three million tonnes and those under the control of the state mandatory plan dropping from 20.8 million tonnes to 15 million tonnes.
