BFN [Text] In the Far East, 20 miles to the south of Vladivostok, the Russian-U.S. naval exercises Cooperation at Sea '94 are taking place. According to the Russian Navy press center, the exercises include work on joint actions to aid people caught in calamitous circumstances. The United States is being represented by the landing craft Dubuque with 250 marines aboard. The marines are headed by Rear Admiral John Seagler, commander of amphibious forces for the Western Group of the U.S. 7th Fleet. The Russians taking part consist of a large landing craft of the Pacific Fleet and 180 marines. Rear Admiral Nikolay Kocherginin, commander of the Association of Pacific Fleet Landing Craft, is responsible for the general supervision of the exercises, which will continue until 23 June.
