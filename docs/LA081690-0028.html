August 16, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: VICHYSSOISE: HOW TO CUT CALORIES, BOTHER

Vichyssoise is a rich, creamy potato soup that is always served cold. Like many famous French dishes, it actually started out as a way to use up leftovers -- in this case, leftover leek-and-potato soup.

The soup was combined with heavy cream and served cold so that it became totally unrecognizable as a leftover.

My lighter version of this classic is much easier to make than the original French technique. It also has a fraction of the calories and cholesterol per serving.

For formal dinner parties I like to serve Vichyssoise in icers garnished with a dollop of caviar. For less formal buffet gatherings I sometimes serve the soup in mugs before seating my guests for dinner.

VICHYSSOISE WITH CAVIAR

2 medium baking potatoes (1 pound), peeled and diced

2 cups chopped white onions

1 1/2 cups defatted chicken stock

1/2 cup nonfat milk

1/2 cup light sour cream

1/2 teaspoon salt (omit if using salted stock)

2 teaspoons freshly squeezed lemon juice

1/2 cup minced onion

1/4 cup caviar

Combine potatoes and onions in large saucepan. Add chicken stock and bring to boil. Reduce heat and simmer, covered, until potatos are tender, about 20 minutes.

Spoon cooked potato mixture into blender container or food processor. Add nonfat milk, sour cream, salt and lemon juice. Cover and chill well.

To serve, pour 1/2 cup cold soup into each of 8 chilled bowls. Top each serving with 1 tablespoon minced onion and 1/2 tablespoon caviar. Makes 8 (1/2-cup) servings.

Each serving contains approximately 80 calories; 54 mg cholesterol; 4 g fat; 300 mg sodium.
