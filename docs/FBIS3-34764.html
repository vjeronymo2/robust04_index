Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 12 Feb (MENA)--The Arab Labor Organization [ALO] has prepared a draft Palestinian labor law that has been revised by a joint committee made up of experts from the ALO and the PLO, which requested the law. Announcing this, ALO Director General Bakr Muhammad Rasul said the draft law was based on the main principles stipulated in Arab and international labor standards, taking into consideration the Palestinian people's general circumstances and the establishment of a Palestinian state. He explained that the Palestinian draft law has 11 sections. Each section contains several chapters and articles about idioms, definitions, and general rules on employment, individual work contracts, working and rest hours, leaves, wages, juvenile's and women's labor, settling collective labor disputes, collective negotiations, collective work contracts, and inspection.
