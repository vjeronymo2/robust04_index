BFN [Text] Hefei, June 25 (XINHUA) -- Hefei, capital of east China's Anhui Province, had approved the establishment of 727 new foreign-funded enterprises by the end of May this year. According to local officials, these enterprises have promised to invest a total of 520 million U.S. dollars, accounting for one third of the province's total foreign investment. The officials said the distribution of foreign investment in the city has changed from solely industry oriented and become relatively harmonized, with its proportion in agriculture standing for two percent, in industry 68 percent and in the service trades 30 percent. They added that the city is making experimental efforts to establish its own investment funds overseas.
