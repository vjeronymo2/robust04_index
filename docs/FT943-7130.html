940825 FT 25 AUG 94 / Labour attacks NHS 'lottery' Some National Health Service trusts made annual surpluses of almost Pounds 4m in the first two years of operation, while others recorded losses of up to Pounds 3.8m, Labour said yesterday. Mr Alan Milburn, MP for Darlington, said an analysis of government figures showed that the NHS had become 'a national lottery - producing losers as well as winners.' The health department said surpluses were used for patient care.
