930811 FT 11 AUG 93 / People: BBC Enterprises John Thomas, the head of BBC Publications, publishers of the Radio Times, is to be the new managing director of BBC Enterprises, the commercial arm of the BBC. He replaces James Arnold-Baker who has been appointed chief executive of the Oxford University Press. Thomas, who is 49, takes over immediately and will join the board of management. He joined the corporation as director of magazines in 1986 after spending 17 years with Reed Business Publishing, the last two as managing director of its medical publishing division. Since 1988, Thomas, who has a chemistry degree from Leeds University and a PhD from Keele University, has also been chairman of Redwood Publishing, a BBC subsidiary. Including Redwood, the BBC says it is the third largest consumer magazine publisher in the UK, with titles ranging from Fast Forward to BBC Wildlife. Thomas will have the task of trying to raise more money to plough back into BBC programmes. In the 1992/93 financial year BBC Enterprises had a turnover of Pounds 233m including co-production deals.
