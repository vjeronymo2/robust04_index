Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Jan 12 (CTK) -- The hope that good joint use will be made of the meetings on European security being held in Prague was expressed by Polish President Lech Walesa during talks with Czech President Vaclav Havel today. Havel received Walesa in the Brozik Room of Prague Castle, thus opening a series of bilateral talks at the highest level between the leaders of the Visegrad countries (the Czech Republic, Poland, Slovakia, and Hungary). At the same time a series of bilateral meetings is taking place between U.S. President Bill Clinton and the presidents of Hungary, Poland, and Slovakia (Clinton talked to Havel yesterday.) After meeting Havel, Walesa told journalists that he wanted to consider, together with the other presidents of Central European countries, how best they could serve their nations. Poland is one of the most enthusiastic advocates of the fastest possible integration of Central European countries into NATO. In recent days the Polish press has also expressed dissatisfaction with the way in which the Czechs organized the Prague meetings. President Walesa also told the independent Czech daily LIDOVE NOVINY that he was disappointed at the Czech Government's having scheduled Clinton to meet each of the Visegrad heads separately instead of there being a joint Visegrad meeting. The Czech side had also insisted that a joint statement would not be made by the Visegrad Four, he said. If the talks were not successful, Walesa said, Poland would be "forced to say that it was the fault of the organizers." President Havel has made no statement on today's meeting with Walesa.
