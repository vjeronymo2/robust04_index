Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jan 18, BNS -- The Polish Foreign Ministry released a statement in response to a letter signed by 72 Polish lawmakers, who expressed concern about the status of Poles in Lithuania and the course of talks between the two countries. The letter, addressed to the Polish president, the prime minister, the foreign minister and the Polish parliament's speaker, appeared in the Lithuanian press last week. The 72 demand that Poland observe 5 principles when signing an agreement on friendship and cooperation with Lithuania. The agreement has been under negotiation since last summer. In its statement, the Polish ministry assures lawmakers that it is no less concerned about the interests of Poles in Lithuania than the Polish parliament. According to the statement, the Polish Foreign Ministry holds that Lithuanian Poles must be guaranteed equal rights in Lithuania. As previously reported, 72 Polish lawmakers, mainly members of the parliamentary majority, demanded to create a cultural autonomy of the predominantly Polish-populated Vilnius and Salcininkai regions, to return nationalized property to Polish social organizations, to exclude history from the main agreement with Lithuania, etc.
