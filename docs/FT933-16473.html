930703 FT 03 JUL 93 / Botnar accuses jailed executive MR OCTAV BOTNAR, chair-man of Nissan UK, yesterday issued a writ against Mr Frank Shannon, the company's former financial director who was sent to prison on Wednesday, for his part in a tax swindle. Speaking from his Swiss chalet Mr Botnar, who is wanted for questioning by the Inland Revenue, accused Mr Shannon of concealing the fraud. He also criticised the way the prosecution case was presented, saying: 'There is no doubt that I have been tried in my absence - contrary to natural justice.' Mr Shannon was imprisoned for three years after admitting one charge of cheating the Inland Revenue. Mr Michael Hunt, managing director of Nissan UK, was jailed for eight years for his bigger role in the fraud. Mr Botnar criticised the prosecution in the trial of the two men. 'I deny absolutely that Nissan UK was involved in a fraud, and in particular the allegation that I was its 'prime-mover',' he said.
