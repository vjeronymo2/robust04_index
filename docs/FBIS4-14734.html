BFN [Text] The new representative of the Ukrainian president in the Crimea Valeriy Gorbatov believes that "the mission of good will" and the work aimed "at prevention of any hearths of tension in our difficult time" are the main goals of his future activity. In his interview with the agency Interfax-Ukraine on Saturday he expressed hope that he would be able to help to the peoples of the Crimea and Ukraine "to become closer." Valeriy Gorbatov, aged 39, was appointed to the post of the Ukrainian president's envoy in the Crimea on March 31. On March 27 he was elected deputy of Ukraine's Supreme Soviet from the Crimea. Until that time he was head of the agricultural firm named after N. Krupskaya in the Crimea's Nizhegorsk region. In Gorbatov's words, he intends to build his relations with the Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov "on friendly terms because we do the same thing." "If he needs my experience and knowledge to implement the Crimea's economic policy I would not refuse from my participation," declared Gorbatov. At the same time he underlined that there are "enough clever and intelligent people" who are working in the Crimean government now.
