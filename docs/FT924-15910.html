921001 FT 01 OCT 92 / Commodities and Agriculture: Output boost for Danish gas field A DKr6m (Pounds 620,000) programme to increase natural gas production from the Tyra field in the Danish sector of the North Sea was announced yesterday by Danish Underground Consortium. DUC consists of the Danish company AP Moller, Shell, the Anglo-Dutch group, and Texaco of the US, with APM's Maersk Oil & Gas as operator. The plan will increase total production from the field by 30bn cubic metres, said DUC. It includes two new production platforms and nine horizontal wells. Denmark's natural gas production last year was 5.76bn cu m, of which 3.67bn from the Tyra field. Total estimated recoverable gas reserves in the Danish fields are put by the energy ministry at 210bn cu m.
