BFN [Text] Government representatives of Bosnia-Herzegovina and the Croat republic of Herceg-Bosna met for the first time in Mostar today. Jadranko Prlic, prime minister of Herceg-Bosna, and Edi Bukvic, deputy prime minister of Bosnia-Herzegovina, and others spoke mostly about the transformation of the present governments and administrations into the government and other federal bodies. The concrete result of the talks was the setting up of joint expert groups which will be in charge of several fields and development projects. An expert group was also founded which will coordinate the present financial and customs systems of the two governments. Also discussed were the reconstruction of the Sarajevo-Ploce railroad, posts and telecommunications, and infrastructure in the Mostar area. The participants also discussed the media, especially the responsibility of the media on both sides in the consolidation of peace and the restoration of trust between Croat and Muslim people.
