BFN [Text] Doha, 18 May (WAKH)--Yemeni Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah has set four conditions for beginning a cease-fire, namely, recognizing legitimacy and the Constitution, resorting to the Constitution, the merger of the Armed Forces' units, and the surrender of the southern leaders to the legitimate leadership. At a news conference he gave in Doha today at the end of his visit to the State of Qatar, the Yemeni minister said that the southern leaders' failure to meet these conditions will mean their inevitable end. He asserted that secession is impossible, pointing out that Yemen will never again return to partition. He added that what is happening is that the Yemeni leadership is defending legitimacy and the Constitution, adding that the legitimate leadership is eager to end the bloodshed, but that fighting has been imposed on it.
