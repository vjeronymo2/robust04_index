Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Talking to newsmen at the State Bank building cafeteria, Syed Iqbal Haider said the government's efforts to create an atmosphere of understanding with the opposition did not produce any result. The law minister said Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto invited the leader of the opposition in the National Assembly, Mr. Mohammad Nawaz Sharif, for talks on several occasions to evolve consensus on common points contained in their respective manifestos on vital national issues, including matters relating to national security, so that harm is not caused to national interests. But Mr. Mohammad Nawaz Sharif refused. The president, he added, also invited PML-N [Pakistan Muslim League-Nawaz faction] leader in Islamabad and then in Lahore for creating understanding in the political field, but Mr. Nawaz Sharif again refused. The law minister renewed the government's offer of a dialogue on major national issues to Mr. Mohammad Nawaz Sharif.
