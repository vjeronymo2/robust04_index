Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] The PMDB [Brazilian Democratic Movement Party] and the PSDB [Brazilian Social Democracy Party] will vote together on the constitutional reform, but will sponsor separate presidential candidates. In Brasilia today, the executive boards of the two parties held separate meetings. The PMDB will adopt a single position for the vote on the constitutional reform. The decision was made by the party executive board. Party positions will be studied on a weekly basis. As for the presidential succession, the majority believes there should be an internal survey to choose a candidate. The new party program will be submitted to the national party convention in March. Deputy Luiz Henrique, the PMDB president, will lead the drive for unity within the party and for future alliances. He began by talking with members of the PSDB, whose executive board also met in Brasilia today. The two parties consider it possible to form an alliance as early as in the first electoral round, will continue to negotiate, but will not give up the designation of their own candidates for president. [Begin PSDB President Tasso Jereissati recording] During a national party congress, the PSDB decided to run its own presidential candidate. [end recording] [Begin PMDB President Luiz Henrique recording] Initially, the PMDB will strive to sponsor its own candidate. [end recording]
