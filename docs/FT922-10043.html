920502 FT 02 MAY 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Dow pushed lower by sell programmes Wall Street A ROUND of sell programmes pushed US stocks lower yesterday, but trading was quiet as traders headed home early amid fears that violence would spill on to the streets of New York in the aftermath of the Los Angeles riots, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average lost 23.03 to close at 3,336.09, with only 177.4m shares changing hands on the big board. Declining issues led advancing by 908 to 775. Among other market indices, the broadly-based Standard & Poor's 500 was off 2.43 at 412.52 while the Nasdaq composite slid 0.54 to 578.14. On Thursday, the Dow advanced 25.94 to 3,359.12. There was some uneasiness following an unexpectedly weak purchasing managers' index report for April, which fell to 51.3 per cent from 54.1 per cent in March. The market had expected the April index to advance to about 55 per cent. Shares in Chrysler continued to lose ground in the wake of the company's unsatisfactory first quarter results. The stock closed at Dollars 18 1/2 , down Dollars 7/8 , after falling Dollars 3/8 a day earlier. Ford lost Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 44 1/8 , after sliding Dollars 3/8 on Thursday and General Motors eased Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 40 3/4 , on top of its previous Dollars 7/8 decline. In the pharmaceutical sector, Glaxo Holdings' American depositary receipts fell Dollars 1 1/8 to Dollars 25 1/2 . The stock had advanced Dollars 7/8 on Thursday on news that the oral form of the company's migraine drug had received UK regulatory approval. Among other drug company issues, Merck slid Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 143 3/4 , American Home Products added Dollars 1 to Dollars 78 1/4 and Pfizer lost Dollars 5/8 to Dollars 70 7/8 . The sell-off in insurance issues continued, with General Re, the biggest US reinsurance group, sliding Dollars 2 3/8 to Dollars 78 5/8 , Cigna losing Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 51, Travelers off Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 20 3/8 and Aetna down Dollars 1 to Dollars 41 5/8 . Only American International Group bucked the trend, adding Dollars 1/2 to Dollars 83 1/2 . AIG's strong first quarter profits were overshadowed by General Re's warning that its underwriting results would probably be disappointing for the remainder of the year. Among entertainment stocks, Walt Disney firmed Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 146 after PaineWebber upgraded its rating. Paramount Communications slipped Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 45 1/4 in thin trading. In the secondary market, Western Capital Investment soared Dollars 3 5/8 to Dollars 13 1/4 on news that the Colorado-based Savings & Loan had agreed to be acquired by First Bank System for Dollars 150m. On the big board, First Bank fell Dollars 1 3/4 to Dollars 25 3/4 . Trading was also active in Synergen, which climbed Dollars 1 1/2 to Dollars 39 after the company posted a first quarter loss of 2 cents a share against 14 cents a year earlier. Canada TORONTO stocks ended mixed. The TSE-300 composite index added 1.5 to close at 3357.06 in volume of 23.3m shares. Among active issues, Morgan Financial Corp was flat at CDollars 0.055, Rogers class B gained CDollars 1/8 to CDollars 13 5/8 , Encor eased CDollars 0.01 to CDollars 0.33 and Corona firmed CDollars 0.20 to CDollars 4.45.
