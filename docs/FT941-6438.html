940302 FT 02 MAR 94 / World Trade News: Jamaican garments plea Jamaica has asked the textile surveillance arm of the Multi-Fibre Arrangement to resolve a trade dispute with Canada over garments shipped from the island to the US which then enter Canada, Canute James writes from Kingston. Canada has limited the quantity of Jamaican assembled women's underwear which can enter that country after being shipped from the island under a trade treaty with the US, according to Jamaican officials.
