April 22, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

CRITICS' CHOICE: THE FULL CATASTROPHE BY DAVID CARKEET (THE LINDEN PRESS: $18.95; 317 PP.;

" . . . eccentric, hilarious, wildly inventive and eerily accurate. It's the sort of zany social satire that hasn't been encountered much since the '60s . . . Carkeet harks back to classic humorists like Stephen Leacock and Robert Benchley." Tom Nolan
