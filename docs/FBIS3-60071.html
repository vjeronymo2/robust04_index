Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 16 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- The Mexican Attorney General's Office (PGR) confiscated on 16 January a shipment of 52,356 kg [amount as received] of pure heroin--equivalent to 2 million doses--in the northern State of Baja California. In a communique, the PGR reported that during the operation--one of the most important carried out in Mexico--authorities confiscated heroin that could have been sold on the U.S. black market for $20 million. The communique added that five foreigners and three Mexicans, including a Postal Service employee who was used as a liaison to send the drug from Thailand to California, were arrested. The report added that the drug was camouflaged in bottles of shampoo and soap and transported from Asia by Thai citizens Seachao Vern Chieng, Phan Saeng Vern, Vang Yao Fow, Seaphan Sou On, and Seaphan Aecho, all of whom have been arrested. Mexican citizens Maricruz Barrera and Armida Aldaco, who were hiding suitcases containing 42 kg of pure heroin inside a closet, were also arrested. The PGR also arrested Alfonso Romero Verdugo, head of the Mexican Postal Service in Ensenada, who was accused of receiving money from the presumed drug traffickers to use that office for drug shipments. The source said that the eight detainees are confessed criminals, and they will be sent to the corresponding authorities to face charges for various crimes against health.
