BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 9 June. The inflation rate calculated by the Economic Research Institute Foundation, Fipe, indicates a 1.12 percent drop compared to the April rate. The most accentuated drop was in the price of foodstuffs, which increased 44.4 percent in April and only 37.59 percent in May. The price of beef increased 22 percent, well below the level of inflation, while the price of beans decreased 8 percent. The May Average General Price Index, IGP-M, measured by the Getulio Vargas Foundation, totalled 42.58 percent and was lower than expected. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 7 Jun 94 p 1) Science and Technology Minister Israel Vargas has reported that President Itamar Franco authorized an amendment to the budget allowing the allocation of $120 million of privatization resources to science and technology projects. He said that in addition to 30 percent of all Bank of Brazil Foundation resources, the $300 million in loans granted by the Inter-American Development Bank will also be assigned to the sector -- $200 million to finance scientific and technological development projects at Brazilian private companies, and $100 million to the official research organizations. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 2 Jun 94 p 8)
