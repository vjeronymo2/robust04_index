Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] Lord Finsberg, rapporteur for the Cyprus problem to the Political Affairs Committee of the Council of Europe Parliamentary Assembly, has stated that he observed a sincere goodwill on the part of the Turkish Cypriots with regard to assisting the UN secretary general in his efforts to assemble the confidence-building measures package. Lord Finsberg left the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus [TRNC] after concluding his contacts. In a statement he issued to TAK [Turkish Cypriot News Agency], Lord Finsberg stressed that he had observed considerable goodwill on the Turkish side. Stating that constructive and useful exchange of views were conducted while he was in the TRNC, Finsberg said he observed that the Turkish side assisted the UN secretary general in assembling the confidence-building measures. Stating that north Cyprus would benefit from successful negotiations, Finsberg stressed that he encountered the same goodwill in north Cyprus as he did in south Cyprus. Reiterating his decision to advise the COE Parliamentary Assembly members in favor of hearing the Turkish Cypriots' voice, Lord Finsberg added that work will be undertaken to enable the TRNC political party leaders' participation in the parliamentary assembly. Reiterating that it is impossible to fill the chair representing the Turkish side for the time being, Lord Finsberg added that he believes that the TRNC political party leaders will be invited to the parliamentary assembly once progress is made on the confidence-building measures and on a solution to the Cyprus issue.
