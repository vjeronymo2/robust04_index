920923 FT 23 SEP 92 / People: Sir Eric Pountain steps back from full-time role Sir Eric Pountain is to become non-executive chairman of TARMAC at the end of this year. Until last year Sir Eric combined the role of chief executive and chairman, but relinquished day-to-day running of the company when Neville Simms was appointed chief executive. This followed criticism of Sir Eric's stewardship of Britain's biggest housebuilder during the late 1980s when the expansion of its UK housebuilding operations left it exposed to the collapse of house prices and sales. Yesterday Sir Eric said that the appointment of Neville Simms had worked out very well for both of them, but 'I have found lately that there is not enough for me to do on a full-time basis. I also intend to accept other non-executive directorships.' Sir Eric's career moves mirror those of Sir Clifford Chetwood, the former chief executive and chairman of WIMPEY who, in November 1990, stood down as chief executive in favour of Joe Dwyer who, like Simms, is battling to reduce group debt through a programme of disposals and tighter control over spending. The question is whether these are the final acts of both men. Sir Eric is 60 next year - the normal retirement age for Tarmac executives.
