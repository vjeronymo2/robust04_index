Language: English Article Type:BFN [by ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Aleksandrova] [Text] Moscow February 23 TASS -- Russian nationalist leader Vladimir Zhirinovskiy was brief in assessing the Duma decision to pardon anti-Yeltsin rebels on Wednesday. "This is a historic moment. February 23, 1994 means an end to civil war in Russia", he told reporters. However, a leader of another major Duma faction is of a completely opposite opinion. Sergey Yushchenkov of the Russia's Choice said the amnesty to rebels means "the beginning of a civil war in Russia". Yegor Gaydar, the leader of the faction, said the decision was a move towards destabilisation and warned that the freed rebels would return to their activities which would result in a new bloodshed. "I am completely convinced that they would work so that new blood is shed", he said. According to Gaydar, the president cannot veto the Duma decision. Other Duma factions were somewhere in between the radical points of view of Russia's Choice and Zhirinovskiy's party. Sergey Stankevich from the Party of Russian Unity and Accord said that Russia has got a chance for reconciliation, but warned that "it is hard to predict how the chance would be used". Leader of Russian Communists Gennadiy Zyuganov told TASS that he has a "feeling of certain satisfaction" as agreement has been reached among most Duma factions. The Russia's Choice remained alone "and began to immediately intimidate by provocations", he said. Vladimir Lysenko from the "Yabloko" bloc abstained from the vote. He told TASS that it was very tempting to vote for universal pardoning, however "if a considerable part of extremists is freed this can destabilise the situation".
