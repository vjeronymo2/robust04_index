BFN [Studio talk between anchorman 'Adi Talmor in Tel Aviv and correspondent Sefi Hendler in Cairo -- live] [Text] [Talmor] The PLO and Israel signed an agreement on security arrangements for the Arab residents of Hebron a short while ago. This paves the way for the resumption of negotiations on Gaza-Jericho First. Our correspondent in Cairo Sefi Hendler attended the brief signing ceremony. Here is his report: [Hendler] Good afternoon. After having become accustomed to the DOP -- the Declaration of Principles between Israel and the Palestinians -- we will now have to learn a new acronym, TIPH: Temporary International Presence in Hebron [acronym and preceding five words in English]. This is the name of the international force that will be composed of 160 soldiers from Norway, Denmark, and Italy. The agreement stipulates that these soldiers, who will have the status of civilian observers, will be permitted to carry personal handguns for self-defense purposes only. This force will deploy in Hebron for three months. If at the conclusion of this period, the Palestinians and Israelis are jointly interested in extending the observers' mandate, then this will be possible. The goals of the TIPH -- the international force to be deployed in Hebron -- are specified in the agreement, copies of which were handed out to correspondents a short time ago in Cairo. The goals are as follows: One, to give the Palestinians a sense of security. Two, to maintain stability in Hebron in order to guarantee the safety and wellbeing of local residents. Three, to supervise the efforts to restore the Palestinians' sense of security and to allow life to return to normal in Hebron. Four, to report on developments in Hebron to several bodies: first and foremost to a special Hebron committee which will be comprised of two representatives from each side, including the mayor of Hebron and the head of the Civil Administration in Hebron. The Hebron committee will report to a subcommittee which will be established within the framework of negotiations on the implementation of the Declaration of Principles. The United States and Russia, the cosponsors of the peace process, will also establish a committee that will be briefed by the multinational force deployed in Hebron. The second part of today's agreement deals with the implementation of Gaza-Jericho First. It stipulates that the discussions on the implemention of Gaza-Jericho First will resume today. We understood that this constitutes a temporary, and not very significant piece of news which was only noted in order to prove that the negotiations on the implementation of Gaza-Jericho First would start today while the really practical discussions would begin next week, apparently in Cairo. The agreement also stipulates that the Israeli withdrawal from the Gaza Strip and Jericho will be accelerated and posits the early stationing of Palestinian policemen in the Gaza Strip by next week in order to give the Palestinians there a greater feeling of security and to further speed up the Israeli withdrawal. These are the details. The negotiations on Gaza-Jericho First will resume later today following the signing of the agreement on the security package in Hebron known as TIPH. This is the name of the international presence that will number 160 personnel from Italy, Norway, and Denmark. [Talmor] When will the negotiations on Gaza-Jericho First resume in a wider framework? [Hendler] This is not mentioned in the agreement but we understand that this will take place next week, probably on Sunday or Monday [3 or 4 April] in Cairo.
