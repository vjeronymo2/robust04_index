August 24, 1989, Thursday, Valley Edition

ENCINO WINS, GETS REMATCH WITH SEOUL

Chris Ferebee struck out nine and Steve Chavez hit a two-run home run Wednesday as Encino defeated Orland Park, Ill., 7-3, to advance in the Pony League World Series.

Encino must defeat Seoul tonight and again Friday to win the championship. Seoul defeated Encino, 4-0, Tuesday.

Chavez's third-inning home run scored Glen Carson and gave Encino a 3-2 lead. Orland Park, which committed seven errors, made three in the third, allowing two more runs to score.

With his team trailing, 2-0, in the first, Encino's Justin Paperny singled to drive in Cesar Martinez, who had doubled.

Orland Park scored a run in the fourth and Encino countered with two insurance runs in the sixth on Kevin Deitch's double and an RBI single by Jesus Perez.

Although there is no official significance attached to the win, Encino Coach P.C. Shaw considers it tantamount to the national championship.

"We don't get outclassed by anybody," Shaw said. "Check the record -- Encino just won the United States."
