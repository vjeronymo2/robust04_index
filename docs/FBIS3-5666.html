Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 9 KYODO -- Cambodian co-Premiers Prince Norodom Ranariddh and Hun Sen arrived at Narita Airport Wednesday for a four-day stay in Japan to attend a conference on reconstruction aid for Cambodia. The premiers will also meet Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa during their first visit to Japan since their administration was launched in September. They are accompanied by Prince Norodom Sirivut, deputy prime minister and foreign minister, Sam Rangsi, economy and finance minister, Chea Chanto, planning minister, and Keat Chhon, senior minister in charge of rehabilitation and development. Delegations from 32 countries, the European Union and 12 international organizations will attend the second meeting of the International Committee on Reconstruction of Cambodia (ICORC), scheduled for Thursday and Friday in Tokyo. Participants will assess Cambodia's mid- to long-term aid requirements and discuss how to improve its capacity to absorb and utilize aid.
