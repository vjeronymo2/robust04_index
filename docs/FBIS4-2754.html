BFN [By Gabor Kurti] [Text] Budapest, April 13 (CNA) -- Wu Jianmin, spokesman for Mainland China's Foreign Ministry, Tuesday [12 April] attempted to refute accusations that the Beijing government mishandled the March 31 Qiandao boat tragedy in which 24 Taiwan tourists died. "We handled the affair in a very proper way. The accusations are not true," Wu told CNA Budapest. Wu also said that criticism over the Mainland's human rights standards is unwarranted. "It is not true that human rights are not respected," Wu said, noting China's success in feeding its people. "There is no more hunger in China." Wu, visiting Budapest at the invitation of Hungarian Foreign Ministry Spokesman Janos Herman, added that Mainland China is focusing on developing basic shelter and care facilities for its people. "Our priority is development. China's net population increase is 16 million people yearly, we have to care about them, create jobs and housing for them," he said.
