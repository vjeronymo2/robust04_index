BFN [Text] Yesterday President Nazarbayev received Abbas Dzhumagulov, prime minister of Kyrgyzstan, who arrived in Almaty on a working visit. Talks between the Kazakh and Kyrgyz Government delegations led by Prime Ministers Tereshchenko and Dzhumagulov, followed. Attention was focused on the progress of implementation of the trade and cooperation agreement and on discussion of Kazakh debt for electric power equal to $55 million. An intergovernmental protocol was signed on implementation of the trade and economic cooperation as well as an agreement that Kazakhstan will begin this year and continue until 2000 to export raw materials containing uranium to Kyrgyzstan. The prime ministers of both countries told journalists that the working visit of the Kyrgyz delegation began a new spiral in the trade and economic cooperation between the two countries.
