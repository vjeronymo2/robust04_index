Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] [Announcer] In Spain, the foreign minister has justified the NATO attack. Javier Solana said that the planes shot down violated the UN resolutions. Concern is now focused on the Spanish troops serving with the United Nations in the conflict area. The soldiers and the conscientious objectors are well, but on alert awaiting events. The Defense Ministry is in permanent contact with the forces in Bosnia and measures have been taken to avoid danger. Ana Medina reports: [Medina] [passage omitted] The Spanish forces have continued with the tasks of escorting convoys because the Spanish positions are in areas controlled by Croats and Muslims, but convoys to Serb enclaves are suspended. There is also calm among the Spanish conscientious objectors, who are working far from the Serb firing line. The Principe de Asturias aircraft carrier is on alert at Rota base ready to depart if it is considered necessary. The Defense Ministry is in permanent contact with the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Force] command and the Spanish military command in Bosnia. The government supported the NATO action in the voice of Foreign Minister Javier Solana, who said that although on previous occasions air space had been violated, this time there was a special element because the intruding aircraft had bombed Bosnian territory: [Begin Solana recording] Yes, this violation had special significance and it is a violation, which we consider to be important, of a UN resolution. Therefore, there was an emphatic action, as you were able to witness this morning. [end recording] [Medina] The United Left, however, has criticized this operation which it considers to be an extreme and inopportune interpretation of the UN mandate that will not help the process of detente that has been achieved. [Announcer] The prime minister has also just spoken about the latest events in the area. He spoke after visiting the president of Argentina, Carlos Menem, in the Pardo Palace [Madrid]. Pedro Fernandez Cespedes reports: [Cespedes] Following the line of the allied heads of government, Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez says that the NATO air attack is, he believes, the strict fulfillment of the UN Security Council resolutions: [Begin Gonzalez recording] And I hope there will be no more violations so there are no incidents that create added tension at a time when there was an opening, and there still is, of a certain hope for peace in Bosnia after a long time. [end recording] [Cespedes] Repercussions following the shooting down of the four Serb planes cannot be predicted. To speculate on this would be dangerous, although Felipe Gonzalez emphasized that so far there had been no negative reaction.
