BFN [Text] President Eduardo Frei today confirmed that within four weeks' time he will travel to Washington at the invitation of U.S. President Bill Clinton. It had been rumored in Washington that Clinton was interested in inviting his Chilean counterpart to a meeting at the White House. Today in Santiago, President Frei confirmed the invitation to visit the United States at the end of the month. [Begin Frei recording] Well, we are awaiting the official announcement by the White House and by the Chilean Foreign Ministry of a visit to Washington on 28 June, a visit that will include a working day with President Clinton at the White House. [end recording] In Washington, Patricio Silva, Chilean ambassador to the United States, reported that the agenda of the meeting will include political issues, but that Chile's overriding interest will be the possibility of joining NAFTA. [Begin Silva recording] It is a clear indication of the Clinton administration's interest in Latin America--which is expressed in this invitation made to the president--and especially in Chile. As you very well know, Chile's presence in U.S. news, public opinion, and political opinion is quite considerable. [end recording]
