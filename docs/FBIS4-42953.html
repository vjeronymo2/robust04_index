BFN [Text] Ankara, June 9 (A.A.) -- Turkey's current transactions deficit for the first quarter of this year increased by 22.7 percent compared to the same period last year, it was reported on Thursday [9 June]. According to a statment by the Central Bank, this amounted to 1.14 billion U.S. dollars, compared to last year's 925 million dollars. In the balance of payments sheet, foreign trade deficit decreased by 17.8 percent when compared to the same period last year. The foreign trade deficit last year for the same period was 2.2 billion dollars compared to this year's 1.8 billion dollars. In this decrease while imports stayed at the same level, the export increased by 4 percent.
