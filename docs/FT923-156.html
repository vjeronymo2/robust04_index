920930 FT 30 SEP 92 / Sterling's decline helps oil industry THE FALL in the value of sterling after the UK's withdrawal from the European exchange rate mechanism might add Pounds 2.5m a day to the output of the UK oil industry, according to the Royal Bank of Scotland's monthly oil index. The recent rise in the dollar - the currency in which oil is traded - has produced an 'almost complete about-turn' in the fortunes of the North Sea oil industry, the bank's economists said. In August, output rose by 2.6 per cent to just under 1.9m barrels a day, but that was more than offset by a 3 per cent fall in oil prices to just under Dollars 20 a barrel and a 1.2 per cent weakening of the dollar-sterling exchange rate. However, August's production at today's prices would have been worth Pounds 2.5m a day more - at Pounds 21.7m - leading the bank to have a very positive view of likely output value in September and October. The possible strengthening of international oil prices this winter might increase output value even more. Many analysts are forecasting a tight oil market, with an increase in demand. The Organisation of Petroleum Exporting Countries agreed earlier this month to freeze its output at its August level in the fourth quarter. Also, some forecasters are predicting an unusually cold winter, which would also push up oil prices. The Royal Bank of Scotland said: 'The result will be a welcome fillip to UK oil revenues - and particularly to those oil companies which have been suffering earlier this year.'
