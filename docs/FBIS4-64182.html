BFN [Text] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- The death of an employee at the Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution and his wife during a holiday in Libya has still not been cleared up. The case was discussed by the Bundestag Parliamentary Control Commission (PKK) today, its chairman Johannes Gerster said afterwards. The German Government has briefed the Commission about the "brutal" circumstances surrounding the death of the two Germans. The Control Commission strongly criticized the fact that the Libyan Government has still not complied with a German Government demand that the incident be completely cleared up with a comprehensive report. According to press reports, the employee was a section leader in Department VI of the Cologne-based Office for the Protection of the Constitution, a department which deals with extremist acts by foreigners and international terrorism. He is said to have died in a military hospital in Tripoli in mid-April. According to the reports, the "Middle East and North African expert" had a holiday trip to Egypt authorized by his employers. There was a ban on travel to Libya at the Office for the Protection of the Constitution.
