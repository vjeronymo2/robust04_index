Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 25 (YONHAP) -- The ruling Democratic Liberal Party [DLP] and the main opposition Democratic Party [DP] agreed Tuesday to hold the next parliamentary elections on April 14, 1996, and the next presidential election on Dec. 18, 1997. The agreement was reached at a meeting of six representatives from the two parties on legislation of an omnibus election law. Under the agreement, the presidential election will be held on the first Thursday from 70 days before the expiration of the term for the incumbent president. The general elections and elections for local autonomous legislature and for chiefs of local governing bodies will be held on the first Thursday from 50 days before the expiration of the incumbents. According to current laws, elections for local autonomous legislature and chiefs of local autonomous administrations should be carried out between Feb. 27, 1995 and May 21, 1995. But if political parties agree to hold all four elections on the same day, the date for local elections would be specifically mentioned in an additional clause of the election law. With regard to election campaign periods, the two parties could not work out agreement. The DLP wanted 21 days for presidential campaign and 15 days for other elections, while the DP called for 25 days for president and 17 days for parliamentary and local elections. Because of the disagreement, the dates for by-elections and reelections were not fixed. The two parties will begin to discuss laws on political funds Wednesday and Thursday.
