BFN [Excerpt] Cartagena de Indias, Colombia, 11 Jun (EFE) -- Uruguayan President Luis Lacalle said today that the goal of consolidating the Ibero-American community of nations continues firm, "even though many presidents are about to complete their terms." By this statement, Lacalle was alluding to his own case and that of Brazilian President Itamar Franco, Colombian President Cesar Gaviria, Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari, and Panamanian President Guillermo Endara. The Uruguayan president was the first of the 22 chiefs of state and government to arrive in Cartagena de Indias to participate in the fourth Ibero-American summit that begins on Tuesday. "It is a pleasure to step on Cartagena soil and to confirm that the objective we established for ourselves continues forward," Lacalle commented. He also praised "the great work of the government" of Cesar Gaviria who will end his term in August. [passage omitted]
