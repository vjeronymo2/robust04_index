BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 3 KYODO -- Japan will send former Ambassador to the United States Nobuo Matsunaga to Paris as a special government envoy to attend a forthcoming ministerial meeting of the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD), a trade ministry official said Friday [3 June]. Matsunaga will attend the two-day conference, which begins next Tuesday, in place of the three cabinet ministers who have been forced to skip the OECD meeting due to a tight parliamentary schedule, the official at the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said. It has been customary for Japan to send the chiefs of MITI, the Foreign Ministry and the Economic Planning Agency to the annual OECD meeting. All of the Group of Seven major industrial countries except Japan will dispatch cabinet ministers to this year's gathering, which will focus on three key topics of growth and employment, reinforcing multilateral trade systems and a new role of the OECD amid the rapid change in the international situation.
