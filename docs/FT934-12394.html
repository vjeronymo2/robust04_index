931027 FT 27 OCT 93 / Smaller businesses 'maintain upturn' THE ECONOMIC recovery among smaller businesses remains robust, says a survey published today. The survey by the Association of British Factors and Discounters, whose members provide cash for companies against unpaid invoices, found that export sales grew by a third in the latest quarter in spite of mounting concern about the effects of recession in continental markets. 'The recovery of Britain's smaller businesses is still intact,' said Mr Ben Allen, association chairman. Sales by clients of the association's members - 9,500 mainly small and medium-sized companies - rose 22 per cent in the third quarter of the year compared with the corresponding period last year. Although the annual rate was slightly down on the 25 per cent achieved in the previous quarter, it is the second-best figure recorded by the association in the past six years. Mr Allen added: 'In contrast to other economic indicators in recent weeks, our figures suggest that smaller firms paused for breath but did not drop out of the race over the late summer.'
