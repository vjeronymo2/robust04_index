BFN [Text] The Speaker of the Islamic Consultative Assembly of Iran, Hojjat ol-Eslam 'Ali Akbar Nateq-Nuri, has described his talks with the Pakistani leaders as very useful and constructive with more emphasis on strengthening the role of the ECO [Economic Cooperation Organization]. Speaking at a news conference in Karachi today before his departure, the Iranian speaker expressed complete satisfaction on the talks he held with the Pakistani leaders on matters of bilateral interest including Kashmir, Bosnia, and the Palestine. He said the two sides also discussed the project of constructing a gas pipeline from Iran to Pakistan and setting up of an oil refinery in Karachi. Hojjat ol-Eslam 'Ali Akbar Nateq-Nuri said the two countries will shortly exchange delegations for carrying out the feasibility for the proposed project. Replying to a question, he said the pace of cooperation among the ECO member countries would accelerate with the passage of time.
