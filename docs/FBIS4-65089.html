BFN [Text] Madrid, 11 May (EFE) -- Spanish Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez reiterated today, Wednesday, his decision not to resign and to remain in charge of the executive branch to achieve "an atmosphere of confidence, which is necessary for strengthening the economy." In an appearance before the Parliament, Gonzalez recognized the "social alarm" and the "political uncertainty experienced in the last few days" in Spain, but he pointed out that "a greater atmosphere of instability would be created" if I were to resign. Gonzalez said he was willing to ask for the parliament's vote of confidence but only "at the necessary moment and that moment is not here yet."
