Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 16 Mar (MTI) -- On March 11, Hungarian police seized 15 kgs of amphetamine ready to be compressed into tablets, and another 30 kgs of uncleansed material at a secret laboratory set up in the cellar of a country house in Biatorbagy near Budapest. (Amphetamine is a chemically produced synthetic material). This has been the greatest catch in Hungarian drug history, the Budapest daily NEPSZAVA reported today. The search followed a warning from the Swedish police of good-quality amphetamine being distributed near Malmo, presumably transported to Scandinavia from or via Hungary. The drug quantity produced and distributed in Europe is still unknown. A Swedish-resident Hungarian man and a Greek citizen, who rented the house in Biatorbagy, were taken into custody.
