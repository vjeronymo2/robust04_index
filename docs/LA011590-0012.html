January 15, 1990, Monday, Valley Edition

BIG-RIG DRIVER ARRESTED AFTER 60-MILE CHASE

The driver of a tractor-trailer truck was arrested Sunday on suspicion of assault with a deadly weapon after leading officers on a 60-mile chase that involved several minor accidents and the truck's crash on the Ventura Freeway in Camarillo, authorities said.

The driver, whose identity was withheld, was arrested after slamming into a light pole on the westbound freeway near Camarillo Springs Road, said California Highway Patrol Officer Robert Bruce.

It was not immediately known if the driver of the truck was injured in the crash, Bruce said.

The incident began about 7:40 p.m. when the Western Mayflower moving van hit a parked car in Glendora, Bruce said. When the truck failed to stop, the car's driver followed the truck onto the westbound Foothill Freeway, he said.

Soon after, the California Highway Patrol became involved in the pursuit, he said.

The chase continued to the westbound Ventura Freeway at speeds to 70 m.p.h., Bruce said. Several vehicles were reported hit by the truck, but no details were available about damage or injuries, he said.

The chase ended when the driver of the truck lost control of the vehicle and smashed into the light pole about 8:30 p.m., Bruce said. The driver was arrested on suspicion of assault with a deadly weapon and various other charges, Bruce said.
