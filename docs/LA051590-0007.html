May 15, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

MAYOR BRADLEY'S PROPOSAL FOR MANDATORY WATER RATIONING

Mayor Bradley's water rationing proposal victimizes the poor. The poor who live in old homes or tenements with deteriorating plumbing have less control over their water usage than anyone else.

The poor will in effect end up paying for water usage by households better able to control water usage, households with swimming pools, with expansive grounds, with funds available to repair plumbing. In other words, the mayor is asking the poor to subsidize water usage by the rich. The mayor's rationing exemption for households with new swimming pools is particularly outrageous.

While the rationing proposal might work in a city composed mainly of middle-class and upper-class households, the vast majority of households here now have annual incomes less than $25,000. Someone might hint to Bradley that declining city services and increases in crime and pollution have caused middle-class families to flee.

DAVID D. MILLS

Los Angeles
