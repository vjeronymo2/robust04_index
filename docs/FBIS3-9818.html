Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Today Mustafa Efendija Ceric, reis ul ulema of the Islamic community of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina, received Lieutenant General Ante Roso, commander of the Croatian Defense Council [HVO] Headquarters. The reception was also attended by Army General Rasim Delic, supreme commander of the Chief of Staff of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Armed Forces, and Naib Ul Reus Ismet Efendija Spahic. During the long conversation, Reis Ul Ulema Mustafa Efendija Ceric supported all the efforts aimed at a military and political agreement between the Bosniak and Croatian peoples, and stressed that these two peoples are not two sides as they have only one homeland, Bosnia-Herzegovina. Their common fate is to fight a war for peace and to fight in peace for the defense of their own dignity. He stressed that the 1,000-year fate of Bosnia was its multicultural character, and that this feature was the only basis for the world and its existence. The reis ul ulema confirmed that the Islamic community would do all it could to help heal the wounds inflicted by the aggression upon Bosnia-Herzegovina, which has caused grievous losses to the Bosniak people. Expressing his personal conviction as a believer that the Islamic community in Bosnia-Herzegovina and its leaders would offer help in the healing of the wounds caused by the war, Lieutenant General Ante Roso particularly stressed that he had come to Sarajevo because he was convinced that everything should be done to achieve this end. Saying that the establishment of a firm and lasting peace is a difficult task that is beyond the powers of any one man, Roso stressed his conviction that this could be done through joint efforts.
