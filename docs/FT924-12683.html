921020 FT 20 OCT 92 / World Trade News: Malaysian power stake goes to UK NATIONAL Power of the UK is to invest in and operate Malaysia's first two privately-owned power stations, due to have a total capacity of 1,000MW by 1995, Alexander Nicoll, Asia Editor, writes. YTL, a Malaysian company, said yesterday it had accepted a counter-proposal from the Malaysian government on its plan to build the gas-fired stations at Paka, Terengganu and Pasir Gudang, Johor. Private finance will be raised without Malaysian government guarantee. Gas will be supplied by Petronas, the state oil and gas company; the electricity will be sold to Tenaga Nasional, the state electricity company.
