BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 24 May 94. According to a stock market report, net profits for the manufacturing sector rose 13.3 percent compared to the first quarter of 1993. The country's economic situation has not adversely affected Colombiana enterprises, which grew stronger in the first quarter, registering increases in sales and assets and decreasing indebtedness. The strengthening of the Colombian enterprises became evident in the growth of current assets and net worth, which rose 51.8 and 70.6 percent, respectively. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 20 May 94 pp 1B, 2B) Colombian exports to Venezuela dropped 6.5 percent as a result of the bolivar devaluation. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 24 May 94)
