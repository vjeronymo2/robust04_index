BFN [Text] Wuhan, April 22 (XINHUA) -- Construction of Wuhan Economic and Technological Development Zone in central China's Hubei Province, listed as one of China's projected car production centers, is going ahead at full steam. The development zone, approved by the State Council, is where a major Sino-French joint venture to produce 300,000 sedan cars will soon be launched. The zone is to focus on the development of businesses linked with the production of cars and automobile components. According to local officials, Wuhan city, the provincial capital of Hubei, has approved the establishment of 107 projects in the development zone, involving a total investment of nine billion yuan. On eight sq km of the 10 sq km development zone, ground leveling has been completed, said the officials. The Shenlong Automobile Corporation, which was jointly set up by Dongfeng Automobile Company, headquartered in Shiyan city of northwestern Hubei, and the Citroen Automobile Co.,Ltd. of France, has decided to make an investment of 4.6 billion yuan to start businesses in the development zone. Five big plants, with a total floor space of 260,000 sq m, for welding, general assembly and maintenance, for the Sino-French joint venture began to be constructed last year and are expected to be completed in 1996. In addition, the Dongfeng Automobile Company has also joined hands with a Taiwan-based firm to jointly invest 50 million U.S. dollars and build a plant in the development zone for turning out 100,000 light-duty trucks a year. Construction of basic facilities in the development zone has been speeded up, said the officials, adding that 23,000 sq m of standard workshops in the development zone have been completed and put on sale. Moreover, construction of 11 buildings for foreign experts, and a school for their children, have been completed. Local officials are also making concerted efforts to improve facilities of management and for providing services.
