940917 FT 17 SEP 94 / Labour to hold economy debate Labour is soon to hold a one-day conference on the future of the world economy, at which it will debate policies and ideas at the heart of its agenda. The one-day conference has been organised by Mr Gordon Brown, the shadow chancellor, and will be attended by Mr Robert Reich, the US labour secretary. The conference, to be opened by Mr Tony Blair, the Labour leader, will take place on September 27 at the National Film Theatre in London. The Labour party said yesterday that its membership had risen to 280,381 in August - the highest since a scheme of national enrolment was introduced in 1989. The party reported that it had recruited 41,000 members this year.
