BFN [Text] Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk arrived in Luxembourg today on a one-day working visit, following meetings there on Monday [13 June] and Tuesday of the European Union's Council of Foreign Ministers. Also today, he signed an agreement on partnership and cooperation between Ukraine and the European Union. The document confirms that the EEC member-states will support Ukraine as a sovereign and independent state within its existing borders. The agreement lays down the principles of, and a mechanism for, a permanent political dialogue between Ukraine and the EEC and systems of periodic consultations at different levels on the entire range of political, economic, cultural, and other issues. The agreement also provides a qualitatively new basis for economic relations between Ukraine and the European Union on market economy principles. The sides will grant each other most favored nation status in trade, and will lift import restrictions with regard to trade agreements in coal, metal, and nuclear materials. Ukraine will also be able to sell its textile products on the European market. The sides urge employers to lift discriminatory restrictions on employment of the partner country's citizens and ensure that the citizens' pension rights are preserved. National terms are granted to European Union companies functioning on Ukrainian territory and with regard to access of trade ships to each side's ports. The Ukraine and Luxembourg foreign ministers signed an air transport agreement. The Chernobyl aid package to ensure complete shutdown of this nuclear plant and the safety of others will be finally agreed before the G-7 Summit in Naples. Leonid Kravchuk confirmed Kiev's consent to shut down the Chernobyl reactors if the international community assists Ukraine in this matter.
