931005 FT 05 OCT 93 / The Conservative Party Conference: Bookies gloom for Major MR JOHN MAJOR is odds-on to be replaced as prime minister before the next general election, with Chancellor Kenneth Clarke 5/4 favourite to take his place. Bookmakers William Hill quote Mr Major at 8/13 to be replaced as leader of the Conservative party and 6/5 that he will lead them into the election. But Mr Chris Patten, Hong Kong governor and former Tory chairman, last night warned the party not to ditch Mr Major. In an interview for the BBC's Panorama, he said: 'I do not think the electorate would flock to a party which changed its leaders every time things seemed to be tough.' He added: 'The right thing to do is to support the prime minister, not to criticise him, not to bellyache about him, particularly when nobody has a better agenda for the future.'
