October 29, 1989, Sunday, Orange County Edition

COLLEGE ROUNDUP;

TOP-RANKED CAL EDGES NO. 2 IRVINE IN WATER POLO

No. 1-ranked California (16-2) led all the way, defeating UC Irvine, 11-10, in the championship match of the Cal State Long Beach water polo invitational at Belmont Plaza Pool in Long Beach.

Irvine, ranked second in the nation, has a record of 18-4. Irvine's Tom Warde made the game close by scoring three of his four goals in the final 4 1/2 minutes of play.

In the UC San Diego cross-country invitational:

Christ College Irvine freshman Genevieve Graff remained unbeaten by winning the women's division in 18 minutes 33 seconds on the 3.1-mile course.

In nonconference women's soccer:

Cal Poly Pomona 2, UC Irvine 1 -- Pomona (6-10-2) scored twice in the second half in defeating host Irvine (5-11-3).
