November 28, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: COUNTYWIDE;

OPPOSITION SLOWS FREEWAY WIDENING

Plans for widening the Santa Ana Freeway north of the Riverside Freeway are moving slowly because of opposition to the project by officials in Los Angeles County, according to a new report.

Seven miles of the nine-mile project are in Los Angeles County, and neither the cities along the route there nor the Los Angeles County Transportation Commission favor the widening, according to a project status report released by the Orange County Transportation Commission on Monday.

As a result, commission officials said, Caltrans is examining other options for improving the freeway. No details, however, were available.

Current plans call for doubling the freeway's width from six lanes to 12, at least as far as the San Gabriel River Freeway. But Caltrans officials have warned of serious bottlenecks if the freeway were to suddenly narrow from 12 lanes to six at the Los Angeles County line.

Meanwhile, reconstruction of the Newport Boulevard bridge between Pacific Coast Highway and 32nd Street in Newport Beach has fallen several months behind schedule, according to the commission's report. So, too, has the widening of Pacific Coast Highway between Golden West Street and Beach Boulevard in Huntington Beach.

The bridge project has slipped by five months, until April, because of delays in getting Coast Guard approval, the report stated. The PCH project is slowed by a design consultant's delays.

The report showed that five other projects have gone out to bid. They involve extending the Costa Mesa Freeway from 19th Street to Wilson Street, widening the Santa Ana Freeway between Jeffrey Road and Jamboree Road and between Tustin Ranch Road and Newport Avenue, and reconstruction of the Santa Ana-Costa Mesa freeways interchange as well as construction of new Santa Ana Freeway ramps at Beach Boulevard.
