Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, February 15 (BTA) -- The Ministry of Transport is going to adopt stern measures against embargo busting, Transport Minister Kiril Ermenkov told a news conference today. There have been embargo busting attempts involving railway employees and border guard members, Minister Ermenkov said, adding that the cases were reported by anonymous informers. "It is our ambition to guarantee the prevention of any attempts to violate the embargo," the minister said. The measures which the Transport Ministry is planning to implement are specified in a special report which will be presented to the Sanctions Committee. An organization is set up by special order to exercise control at the border checkpoints, it comprises representatives of the Ministry of Transport, the Ministry of Finance and the Ministry of the Interior. Besides, it is planned to handle cargoes only in daytime, the cargo handling will be monitored by operative groups. Bulgaria is ready for transit shipments across the territory of ex-Yugoslavia, Minister Ermenkov said. However, having in mind the complicated situation in Bosnia, such shipments will be effected in the last resort.
