921125 FT 25 NOV 92 / Observer: Hobson's choice The theatrical world gathered in strength yesterday at the actors' church, St Paul's, Covent Garden, for the thanksgiving service for Sir Harold Hobson, long the drama critic of the Sunday Times, and the man who helped build the reputations of such writers as Samuel Beckett and Harold Pinter. It is not only for his enthusiasm that Hobson is remembered; he also knew about seat prices. 'If you have only 17 shillings and sixpence left in the world,' he once wrote, 'go and see Waiting for Godot. If you have 35 shillings, see it twice.' Beckett's play was thus saved from being prematurely withdrawn from the London stage.
