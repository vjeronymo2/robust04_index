BFN [Text] Russian Air Force Commander General Petr Deinekin announced that in 1994, the number of war planes is to be cut by one third. He told Interfax on Wednesday that cuts would made by disbanding Russian aviation units located in the former Soviet republics. He also stated that despite the cuts, the Russian Air Forces' combat efficiency would be retained by equipping them with modern planes of the fourth generation. He also noted that the Defense Ministry would no longer use obsolete planes, whose service life has expired.
