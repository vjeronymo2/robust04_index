Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Havana, 20 Feb (PL) -- President Fidel Castro has met with two Chilean legislators of the ruling Christian Democratic Party who are currently on a working visit to Cuba, the weekly Sunday newspaper JUVENTUD REBELDE reported today. The report said that the Chilean legislators who met with Castro were Senator Carmen Frei and Deputy Eugenio Ortega. The report added that aspects concerning bilateral relations were reviewed during the meeting, confirming advancements made in common interest areas. Cuba and Chile broke diplomatic relations on 11 September 1973 when constitutional president Salvador Allende was overthrown in a bloody coup d'etat headed by General Augusto Pinochet. Relations between the two nations are currently at a consular level.
