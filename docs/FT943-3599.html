940913 FT 13 SEP 94 / UK Company News: Jupiter Tyndall climbs 63% at interim stage Jupiter Tyndall, the UK-based fund mangement group, reported a 63 per cent rise in pre-tax profits to Pounds 5.89m for the six months to June 30, up from Pounds 3.61m. Earnings per share rose from 8.2p to 13.2p. Mr John Duffield, chairman, said the rise reflected fee income on new accounts which did not come to Jupiter Tyndall until the second half of 1993. During the six month period, net new cash under management has totalled a further Pounds 350m, largely from mandates to manage UK specialist equity investments for US and UK pension funds. With the acquisition of Queen Anne's Gate Asset Management, assets under management totalled Pounds 4.15bn at the end of the period. The company had earlier announced the sale of its banking assets, which, for the interim period, earned profits of about Pounds 1.3m. Jupiter Tyndall announced an increased interim cash dividend of 6p (4p).
