920715 FT 15 JUL 92 / The Lex Column: British Airways BA's shares deferentially dipped a penny yesterday as Lord King's much-trailed dying swan routine was duly performed at the annual meeting. His contribution to the airline both before and after privatisation has been immense, but the effect of his departure is essentially symbolic. The real work has for some years been in the hands of his designated successor, Sir Colin Marshall.
