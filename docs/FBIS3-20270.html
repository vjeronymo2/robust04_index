Language: French Article Type:BFN [Baudouin Bollaert report: "The Twelve Save Face"] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] The European Commission itself did not make its opinion known [on the Ioannina voting rights compromise]. It will do so today when the permanent representatives of the Twelve meet in Brussels to "enact" the compromise. Apparently, it will respond positively, although Jacques Delors is concerned about "negative jurisprudence" in the perspective of 1996 [EU institutional reforms]. Just two Commissioners yesterday expressed real reservations. One of them, Christiane Scrivener of France, spoke of "a dangerous and worrying step backward" compared with the progress made in the Single European Act of 1987. Mrs. Scrivener knows full well the problems of finding a consensus, since her brief -- taxation -- is one of the last to require a unanimous vote. For her, the Ioannina compromise "is an attack on the qualified majority" while, on the contrary, an enlarged Europe needs a clearer "decisionmaking process." Of course there is still the major review to come in 1996 but, she says, "work will continue until at least 1998" and in the mean time Europe runs the risk of getting bogged down....
