BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Boris Krivoshey and Vladimir Taranov] [Text] Moscow April 7 TASS -- Vitaliy Churkin, Russian president's special representative and deputy foreign minister, held consultations on Wednesday with Momcilo Krajisnik, chairman of the Skupstina (Federal Assembly) of the Serbian Republic in Bosnia, on problems of the Bosnian settlement. This was reported by Grigoriy Karasin, Russian Foreign Ministry spokesman, at a briefing here on Thursday. According to Grigoriy Karasin, on April 10 Vitaliy Churkin is due to fly to Belgrade where he is expected to meet with the Serbian leadership. On Monday, April 11, the special envoy of the Russian president is due to meet in Zagreb with the Croatian leadership, and on the following day Vitaliy Churkin will take part in the beginning of talks on economic aspects of the settlement which will be held at the Russian embassy. It is possible that in the course of the forthcoming trip President Boris Yeltsin's special envoy will also visit Sarajevo, Grigoriy Karasin pointed out.
