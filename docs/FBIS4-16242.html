BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 15 Apr -- A session of the Council of Heads of CIS States has just opened in the Kremlin. Taking part are the leaders of Azerbaijan, Armenia, Belarussia [Belarus], Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kirghizia [Kyrgyzstan], Moldavia [Moldova], Russia, Tajikistan, Turkmenia [Turkmenistan], Uzbekistan, and Ukraine. Russian President Boris Yeltsin is chairing the meeting. Opening the session, he expressed the hope that it "will be a new step to strengthen the Commonwealth." The Russian president proposed "in connection with Moldova's wish to become a full member of the Commonwealth, to start to discuss the matter immediately." The heads of CIS states are to discuss a declaration and observation of sovereignty, the territorial integrity and inviolability of the borders of CIS member states; a statute on the CIS defense ministers council; and Ukraine's joining the Economic Union as an associate member. It is planned to examine the question of the duration of the stay, the composition, and the tasks of the Collective Peacekeeping Forces in the Republic of Tajikistan, a convention for those belonging to national, ethnic, linguistic, or religious minorities, and other topics.
