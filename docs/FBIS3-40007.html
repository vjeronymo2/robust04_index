Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Turkish Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin has stated that other countries should not be concerned by Turkey's interest in sending troops to Bosnia-Herzegovina. Cetin, at The Hague as the official guest of the Dutch Foreign Minister Peter Kooijmans, concluded todays' contacts. TRT correspondent Ihsan Gunal reports from The Hague: [Begin recording] Following the official meetings, Cetin answered reporters' questions and said that Turkey wants to send troops to Bosnia-Herzegovina in order to have peace established there as soon as possible. Indicating that there are rumors as to Turkish troops should not be sent because one of the parties in Bosnia-Herzegovina might object, Cetin pointed out that in spite of the Bosnians' objections, certain countries' troops are still being sent to the region, noting that this constitutes a double standard. Stressing that Holland also shares Turkey's views on this issue, Cetin indicated that the Dutch believe that fresh military forces are needed in Bosnia-Herzegovina and that they should be reinforced. Cetin also explained that during the official meetings he briefed the Dutch delegation on the terrorism and human rights issue in Turkey. Stating that the Netherlands -- in keeping with its legislation -- cannot ban terrorist organizations, Cetin added that nevertheless it will continue to prohibit terrorist activities adding that the Netherlands finds Turkey's fight against terrorism justified. In answer to a reporter's question, Cetin explained that Greece's closing the Thessaloniki port to Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] exacerbates the crisis in the Balkans. Stressing that this move should not have been perpetrated, Cetin said that, if necessary, Turkey will extend all kinds of help to the FYROM via Varna. [end recording]
