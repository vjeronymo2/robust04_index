Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, February 1 (SNARK)--Armenia falls into the medium degree of classification with the status of a less developed country. There is nothing wrong with this international status we got not so long ago, for over the last three years the gross national product has abruptly fallen. This information was told to SNARK today by the Armenian Minister for Economics Armen Yegiazaryan. He states that the real gross national product in 1990 was on the level of 4 700 [figure as received] dollars per head. Last year it became 700-800 dollars per head. In Yegiazaryan's opinion, it would be incorrect to increase the incomes artificially, and at this stage there is no alternative to the policy of "tightening the belt". The minister thinks the three indexations of wages since January 1 speak in favor of this assertion, as in conformity with the expectations there was no essential rise of the prices. "We have a situation in Armenia when the insolvency of the mainstream led to the drastic consumers' market contraction and, therefore, supply decrease", concluded the minister for economics.
