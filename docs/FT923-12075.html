920721 FT 21 JUL 92 / Part-time workers are 'more efficient' STAFF working part-time, with flexible hours or in job shares perform better than their full-time counterparts, according to a study by Sussex University's Institute of Manpower Studies. The research into 'family-friendly' policies found that staff working non-standard hours were considered by their employers to be 'more efficient, enthusiastic and committed' than full-timers. The report concludes that 'family-friendly' working could more aptly be called 'employer-friendly'. High-street banks, building societies and other large employers including IBM, Shell and the Post Office commissioned the research. Family Friendly Working.
