Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Telepress" newscast] [Text] The government and the National Transportation Coordinating Board reached an agreement early today, thus fuel supplies are expected to return to normal in the next few hours and suburban transportation to resume. President Ramiro De Leon Carpio confirmed that the transportation workers accepted the conditions proposed by the government. [Begin De Leon recording] Yesterday they accepted the government economic policy proposed by the president. The only [words indistinct] is that today we will issue the respective agreements, but they accepted the economic policy. The CNT accepted the proposals and tariffs we presented, so we are satisfied because the problem has been resolved. There will be no strike. At this time today they must inform their members and transportation workers that the problem has been resolved. This is one less problem for the country. We are extremely pleased to see that the government's firm position was accepted, as the government's position must be and will be accepted when we are facing problems that require state decisions. [end recording]
