BFN [Text] Johannesburg March 31 SAPA -- The African National Congress on Thursday [31 March] said it welcomed the announcement of a state of emergency in kwaZulu as long as it allowed free and fair political activity in the area, ANC spokesman Carl Niehaus told SAPA soon after the announcement by President F.W. de Klerk. He said the ANC, as a member of the Transitional Executive Council [TEC], called on Wednesday for a state of emergency "because of the very serious situation that prevails in Natal". "The ANC therefore welcomes the announcement by Mr de Klerk that a state of emergency is introduced in Natal/kwaZulu. "We fully support the state of emergency as long as it allows full freedom of political activity and full freedom to hold political meetings, voter education and that preparations for the elections can be fully guaranteed," Mr Niehaus said. He added that a leadership summit to address the serious situation in Natal was now even more urgent than before and the ANC hoped it would be held as soon as possible.
