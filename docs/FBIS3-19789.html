Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "EP Energy Committee Calls For Clauses on Nuclear Non-Proliferation To Be Included in Cooperation Agreements"; as released by Brussels TEXTLINE database] [Text] Brussels, 17 Mar 94 (AGENCE EUROPE) -- With the adoption of the report by Carlos Robles Piquer on the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty which must be extended in 1995, the EP [European Parliament] Committee on Energy, Research and Technology has called on the European Union to define "as uniform a position as possible" on this issue in order to reinforce the guarantees against the risk of proliferation. According to MEPs, the European Union should tend, moreover, towards establishing an international and universal control system of the "complete cycle" of uranium, plutonium and possibly thorium, for civilian purposes. In addition, MEPs consider that, in the cooperation agreements with third countries, the Union should include clauses on the non-proliferation of nuclear weapons. Finally, MEPs took an explicit stand in favour of establishing a denuclearised zone in the Near East.
