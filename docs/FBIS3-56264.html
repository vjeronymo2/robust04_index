Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Mikhail Poltoranin, a Duma member from the "Russia's Choice" bloc, believes that U.S. President Bill Clinton should not meet with Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, the leader of the Liberal Democratic Party. He told journalists on Thursday that instead of distinguishing between the notion of "Zhirinovskiy himself" and the "phenomenon of Zhirinovskiy," they had been giving too much publicity to him. The phenomenon of Zhirinovskiy means discontent with official policy in Russia today. Zhirinovskiy is disgusting to many people, Poltoranin said, but they seem to have chosen the lesser of the two evils. And Clinton has been right to say that by voting for Zhirinovskiy citizens protested against the policy of the authorities. "The balloting showed that people had gotten tired of the helpless government, rampant bureaucracy, crime, etc. Official policy must be more effective," Poltoranin said.
