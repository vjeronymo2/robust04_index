BFN [From the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev today arrived in Rostov-on-Don to begin his three-day visit to North Caucasus. [Correspondent A.Murtazin -- identified by caption] With the changes in geopolitical realities, southern Russian has become a border region, hence the need for suitable combat-ready troops. [Begin Grachev recording] Along with Transcaucasia, North Caucasus is a priority region for us at the moment. We need to create a strong district manned with strong mobile and land-based forces which are properly armed. [end recording] [Murtazin] From the airport the minister went on to the military cantonment where construction of apartments for the army officers withdrawn from Germany has been under way for four months now. In accordance with the agreement on troop withdrawals, the construction is financed by the German side. Bulgarian and Russian workers are employed. By the end of this year 1,548 apartments will be built. Also today the minister visited the 22d Special Purpose Troops Brigade transferred here from Baku two years ago. During their first winter here these servicemen had to live in tents in the open country. Now their barracks are ready. Soon many of them will be moving into new apartments.
