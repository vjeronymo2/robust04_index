Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, February 2, RIA -- The Council of the Federation which is discussing the articles in the chamber's regulations dealing with the general procedure of the work and the rules in considering the laws adopted by the State Duma has declined the amendments of the president of the Russian Federation concerning the adoption of decisions by the Council of the Federation. In his letter to both chambers of the Federal Assembly Boris Yeltsin proposed that in adopting decisions they should proceed from the total number of the deputies provided for by the Constitution of the Russian Federation -- 178 for the Council of the Federation and 450 for the State Duma, not from the number of the actually elected members of the two chambers which is somewhat smaller than that named in the fundamental law. By declining the presidential amendments, the Council of the Federation has created a precedent for the State Duma whose chairman Ivan Rybkin was elected by a majority of one vote from the number of actually elected deputies to the lower chamber (223 from 444), not from the number of the deputies which should be elected under the Constitution.
