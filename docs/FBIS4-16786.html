BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Palariya] [Text] Moscow, 20 Apr -- Sergey Stepashin, director of the Russian Federal Counterintelligence Service (FSK), and Aleksandr Korzhakov, chief of the Russian Federation president's Security Service, exchanged views at a meeting today on cooperation between and coordinating the activity of the two special services to ensure the safety of the Russia president and state security as a whole. The ITAR-TASS correspondent learned from the FSK that today's meeting was not caused by any extraordinary events. It was held within the framework of establishing collaboration between the counterintelligence service and other Russian special services in order to eradicate the duplication of work. Sergey Stepashin and Aleksandr Korzhakov signed an agreement which lays down the legal and contractual basis for cooperation between the two special services in operational investigation work, and which envisages the regular exchange of information. Earlier, a similar agreement was signed between the FSK and the Foreign Intelligence Service.
