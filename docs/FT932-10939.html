930506 FT 06 MAY 93 / UK Company News: Rationalisation helps Serif cut loss to Pounds 943,000 Losses at Serif, the USM-quoted specialist printer and packaging group, were cut from Pounds 2.21m to Pounds 943,000 pre-tax for the year to the end of December. The period was one of rationalisation and reorganisation. Sales of continuing activities amounted to Pounds 21.4m (Pounds 26.5m). Operating costs were reduced to Pounds 21.8m (Pounds 38.5m) and exceptional provisions were Pounds 649,000 (Pounds 1.04m). Tax took Pounds 80,000 (Pounds 294,000) and after extraordinary charges of Pounds 795,000 (Pounds 5m) the retained loss emerged at Pounds 1.82m (Pounds 7.52m). Losses per share were lower at 4.1p, compared with 10.1p. The company is proposing a capital reduction in order to resume ordinary dividend payments. Mr Alan Brooker is to retire as chairman. He will be succeeded by Mr John Pryke, the chief executive.
