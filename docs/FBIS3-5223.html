Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] While inter-Korean trade is picking up this year, inter-Korean processing-on-commission trade [witak kagong muyok] has amounted to $2.78 million in the first two months of this year, recording 63 percent of the 1993 yearly total of this mode of trade. According to the "Trend of Inter-Korean Trade in February" released by the National Unification Board on 5 March, the processing-on-commission trade during January-February period this year consisted of 160,707 men's jackets, 172,996 pairs of men's trousers, and 200,000 suit-covers on an approval basis, accounting for 63 percent of the total processing-on-commission trade amount of $4.38 million last year. However, the approval amount of inter-Korean trade during the same period was $24 million, registering only a two percent growth over last year. The businesses engaged in processing-on-commission trade are 12 companies including Daewoo, Lucky-Gold Star, Samsung, and Kolon.
