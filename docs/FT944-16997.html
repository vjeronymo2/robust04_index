941011 FT 11 OCT 94 / Equal opportunity policies attacked Equal opportunities policies which single out minority groups from the rest of the workforce are doomed to fail, according to a study to be unveiled later this month at the conference of Britain's main personnel organisation. Ms Johanna Fullerton, who co-wrote the study for the Institute of Personnel Development conference, told the magazine Personnel Today that policies which appear to offer universal benefits for all are more successful. Policies such as setting targets for the composition of the workforce and positive action in recruitment advertising were the least successful, according to the study of 285 companies. 'People seen to be given preferential treatment doubt why they got the job, the promotion or the training - and so do their colleagues,' said Ms Fullerton. However, Ms Marie Cunningham, an equal opportunities adviser with British Rail, told Personnel Today that the problem was with those implementing the policies.
