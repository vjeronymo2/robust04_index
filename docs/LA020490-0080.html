February 4, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

ARCHBISHOP ROGER MAHONY

The highly charged emotional issues of abortion and cigarette smoking in public places have this in common: They restrict population growth, one by forestalling the birth of unwanted children and the other by escalating the death rate; and they polarize perfectly nice, reasonable people instantly into bitter enemies, ready to do bodily harm at the least provocation! Take good Archbishop Mahony, for instance. A pleasant regular guy -- jogs, flies his own helicopter, says "geez," talks just like you, me and the other fellas down at the health club.

But when the subject of abortion comes up, watch out! This mild-mannered Dr. Jekyll metamorphoses -- in a trice -- into fire-breathing Mr. Hyde! Never mind that overpopulation causes most of the world's problems, and that most Latino families live in squalor. "There is no such thing as a pro-choice Catholic public official." To perpetuate such archaic, arbitrary, dilatory dogma, the archbishop (and the Vatican) must be running scared. And with good reason!

MARVIN H. LEAF, Rancho Mirage
