Language: Arabic Article Type:CSO [Report by `Abd-al-'Aziz al-Sadiqi] [Text] Abu Dhabi--In spite of the appointment of Ahmad Sa'id al-Badi, the UAE [United Arab Emirates] health minister, as acting oil minister, succeeding resigning Minister Yusuf Bin-'Umayr Bin-Yusuf, the councils and diwans in the UAE have not stopped whispering about who will occupy the vacant ministerial portfolio. In fact, the speculations have extended to a wide-ranging cabinet shuffle or comprehensive change in the composition of Vice President and Prime Minister Shaykh Maktum Bin-Rashid al-Maktum's government, which has lasted nearly four years. With regard to the fact that some consider the appointment of al-Badi as acting oil minister to be an indication of the UAE leadership's tendency to reduce speculations about the identity of the next oil minister, some believe this appointment to be a temporary transition formula, while awaiting "the cooking up of the new ministerial meal," which will not stop at the doors of the Ministry of Oil, but will continue to include a comprehensive reshuffle in most of the current Council of Ministers. According to what some circles are repeating, the change may extend to the Presidency itself, and these circles say that it is likely that the current president, Shaykh Maktum Bin-Rashid al-Maktum, will vacate his post as vice president and ruler of the Emirate of Dubai. If these rumors are true, the most likely candidate to succeed Shaykh Maktum in the post of president is Shaykh Hamdan Bin-Rashid al-Maktum, the current finance and industry minister and the second oldest brother after Shaykh Maktum Bin-Rashid al-Maktum. This formula has it that Shaykh Sultan Bin-Zayid Al Nuhayyan, the current deputy prime minister of the Council of Ministers, will retain his position in the new government, which tallies with a tradition of separating the two posts so that the one who leads the government is an official from the Emirate of Dubai when his deputy is from the Emirate of Abu Dhabi. Informed sources have ruled out the possibility that some of the major portfolios will change, among them the defense portfolio held by Shaykh Muhammad Bin-Rashid al-Maktum and the higher education portfolio held by Shaykh Nuhayyan Bin-Mubarak Al Nuhayyan, as well as some others held by shaykhs like the Ministry of State for the Supreme Council's Affairs headed by Shaykh Muhammad Bin-Saqr al-Qasimi, the Planning Ministry headed by Shaykh Humayyid Bin-Rashid al-Mu'alla, and the Ministry of Youth and Sports headed by Shaykh Faysal al-Qasimi. Expected changes are in the service ministries, where the sources expect Ahmad Sa'id al-Badi from the Health Ministry to transfer to the Oil Ministry, at which he is acting. It is expected that Ahmad Humayyid al-Tayir, minister of state for financial and industrial affairs, will become minister of finance and industry, succeeding Shaykh Hamdan Bin-Rashid al-Maktum if the latter is appointed president. Among the ministries around which the rumors are circulating is the Foreign Ministry--occupied now by Rashid Bin-'Abdallah al-Nu'aymi--where some expect Shaykh Hamdan Bin-Zayid Al Nuhayyan to become minister in the place of his current post of minister of state for foreign affairs, a post which he has held for about four years.
