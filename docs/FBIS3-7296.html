Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 28 KYODO -- Bangladesh's Prime Minister Begum Khaleda Zia arrived in Tokyo on Monday [28 March] for an official visit which will include talks with Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa. Zia will hold talks with Hosokawa on Tuesday and attend Bangladesh investment seminars in Tokyo and Osaka before leaving Japan on Friday. She plans to explain her government's foreign investment policy and seek to dispel the image of Bangladesh as a country always suffering from poverty and floods. Japan is Bangladesh's top aid donor, providing 300 million dollars to 400 million dollars a year in financial and other forms of aid.
