Language: Bulgarian Article Type:BFN [Report by Mariya Tsankova] [Text] Today's plenary session of the National Assembly was extended to allow the deputies to debate the motion for a no confidence vote in Lyuben Berov's cabinet proposed by the parliamentary group of the SDS [Union of Democratic Forces]. The proposers of the motion argued that the government was to blame for failing to bring the crime rate under control and to act against the growth of organized crime. The Socialist deputies did not [word indistinct] their criticisms of the Berov cabinet, but called for the drafting of a national program to combat crime. The National Assembly deputies of the New Alliance for Democracy gave the government and the Interior Ministry leadership a one-month deadline to conduct a purge at all levels within the Interior Ministry to remove corrupt officials. The actual vote on the no-confidence motion will be held by secret ballot next Wednesday [9 February]. On Tuesday [8 February] the National Assembly will hold an extraordinary plenary session, at which the deputies are likely to debate the state budget bill, the excise bill, the draft law on aliens' residence in Bulgaria, and the draft law on the National Security Service.
