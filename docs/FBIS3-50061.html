Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Report by "sm" in the "Rumor" column: "Problem With Blood Pressure"] [Text] The president of the Slovak Republic [Michal Kovac] did not attend Thursday's [13 January] news conference with representatives of the Slovak Republic who participated in the Prague summit of the Visegrad Four countries with U.S. President Bill Clinton. He was undergoing an examination at that time and, after receiving an infusion, had to lie down for a while. The president also canceled the recording of the "Mr. President, Allow Me To..." [television] program, which was to have been broadcast on Monday, 17 January]. We have learned from circles close to the head of state that the president has blood pressure problems and that he is very tired from a heavy work burden. Supposedly, it is nothing serious.
