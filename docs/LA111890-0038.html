November 18, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

AND NOW, A ROUND OF APPLAUSE FOR THE NEW NC-17 MOVIE RATING

In response to Pete and Maria Francis' remarks about NC-17 and the number of times suggestive words appear in "Henry & June" ads, I say that if they counted the times that words in movie ads -- and in the movies themselves -- pertain to violence, murder, mutilation and drugs, they would be appalled.

Self-righteous people should lighten up and concentrate on the things that are destroying the world instead of berating movies or ads that contain (sexual) words and feature some nudity.

I do not care if someone gets a little aroused from a movie that depicts sex, but I do care if someone tries to emulate Rambo or some crazed killer in a motion picture.

JOHN BYRNES

Santa Monica
