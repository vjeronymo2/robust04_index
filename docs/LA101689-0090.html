October 16, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

NOTES

Hong Kong is looking to the private sector to fund up to 60% of an ambitious $16.2-billion plan for a replacement airport and a new container port complex, according to senior government officials.

The planners are hoping for the massive private participation even though the projects will not be completed until well after 1997, when the British colony reverts to Chinese sovereignty.

"We estimate that the private sector would find it attractive to participate in about 40% to 60% of the total," Treasury Secretary Hamish MacLeod said.

Past Hong Kong infrastructure projects have seen extensive private sector participation. But many banks have been reassessing the risks of investing in long-term projects in Hong Kong after the bloody military crackdown on pro-democracy protesters in Beijing June 3-4, which cast a shadow over Hong Kong's future.

The replacement airport, to be built on a site created by flattening the small island of Chek Lap Kok to the west of Hong Kong island, will cost around $4.5 billion.
