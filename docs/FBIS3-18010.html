Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 30, BNS -- The Saeima (parliament) Homeland and Freedom faction and several Latvian National Independence Movement deputies handed Wednesday [30 March] a declaration to Latvian Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs. In the declaration the deputies demand to be informed about the control over the course of Russian army withdrawal, and measures which will be taken to prevent the growth of number of demobilized officers in Latvia, the Homeland and Freedom faction head, Maris Grinblats told reporters. The deputies also demand that the Latvian delegation did not initial the agreement between Russian and Latvian governments on Russian military pensioners, living in Latvia, and their families' social guarantees. Grinblats says that, in accordance with the Defense Ministry's given information, the leadership of the Russian Northwest Army Group tries to increase the number of retired militaries in Latvia. There can be observed a large scale demobilization of Russian officers in Latvia, and permissions for a stay in Latvia are asked also for the military personnel which has already moved to Russia. The deputies consider that the Cabinet has not taken the necessary measures to prevent the demobilization of Russian militaries in Latvia and transmigration of Russian army in the territory of Latvia. They claim that the Cabinet has to prevent by way of legislation Russian militaries' involving in the activities of business structures, and it has to tightened control over procedure of issuing the stay permissions.
