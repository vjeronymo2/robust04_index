941231 FT 31 DEC 94 / Weekend Money: Finance year January Euro Disney share price tumbles as Michael Eisner, chairman of Walt Disney, threatens to pull the plug on the theme park. Creditor banks meet to discuss rescue package. Paramount, focus of two rival bids, rejects the offer from Viacom and recommends a Dollars 9.9bn bid from QVC, the home-shopping network. Viacom raises bid to more than Dollars 10bn. National Westminster and Barclays Banks, the big UK clearing banks, say they will cut 43,000 jobs over the next few years. Marks and Spencer to offer customers life assurance and pensions from 1995. Metallgesellschaft, the German metals, mining and industrial group, on the brink of collapse after running up massive losses in oil futures. British Aerospace sells Rover Group to BMW of Germany for Pounds 800m. February GKN launches hostile Pounds 496m bid for Westland, the helicopter maker. Brussels fines 16 steel companies, including British Steel, Pounds 79m for operating a cartel. Viacom beats QVC in battle for Paramount with bid of more than Dollars 10bn. Markets in Europe and Asia tumble after US Federal Reserve raises interest rates. March Mirror Group wins fight with Tony O'Reilly for control of Independent newspaper, with Pounds 75m bid. Granada takes control of London Weekend Television for Pounds 760m. Legal & General fined Pounds 180,000 after admitting direct sales agents not controlled properly. Heron International warns creditors of problems meeting obligations. April GKN wins Westland with Pounds 577m bid. Virgin Atlantic unveils transatlantic alliance with Delta Venerable US investment bank Kidder Peabody claims one of its bond traders faked Dollars 350m in profits. Lloyds Bank unveils plan to take over the Cheltenham & Gloucester Building Society for Pounds 1.8bn. BAT Industries in Dollars 1bn deal to buy American Tobacco. Enterprise Oil launches Pounds 1.45bn hostile bid for rival explorer Lasmo. May J. Sainsbury agrees to change packaging of its own-brand Classic cola after threats of legal action from Coca-Cola. Lloyd's of London declares Pounds 2.05bn loss for 1991. Eurotunnel launches Pounds 816m rights issue. June Saudi prince Walid ibn Talal rides to rescue of Euro Disney with cash injection through rights issue. Court rules Lloyds Bank takeover of Cheltenham & Gloucester building society is unlawful. Enterprise Oil raises its hostile bid for oil company Lasmo to Pounds 1.6bn. Stock and bond markets across the world fall heavily because of inflationary concerns. 3i floats with value of Pounds 1.58bn July Enterprise Oil fails to win Lasmo. Tesco increases its offer for Scottish supermarket group William Low to beat counter-bid from Sainsbury. August Cheltenham & Gloucester announces revised terms for merger with Lloyds Bank. Body Shop shares fall as company hit by bad publicity over trading policies. September SmithKline Beecham to buy Eastman Kodak's over-the-counter drug business for Dollars 2.9bn. Deal creates world's biggest over-the-counter drug maker. Unilever admits that its revolutionary detergent launched in the spring under the names Persil Power and Omo Power was 'defective' - but adds that the formula was revised in June. London Stock Exchange proposes Alternative Investment Market for smaller companies. Exxon ordered by a US jury to pay Dollars 5bn to Alaskans who suffered from the 1989 Exxon Valdez oil spill. October Reckitt & Colman quits mustard and buys the household products business L&F Household for Dollars 1.55bn. Kidder Peabody sold by parent General Electric to rival securities house PaineWebber. High Court awards compensation to more than 3,000 loss-making investors in the Lloyd's of London insurance market. Richard Branson takes to drink with agreements to market cola and vodka under the Virgin name. British Aerospace announces agreed all-share bid for VSEL, maker of submarines and warships, of about Pounds 479m. GEC launches rival bid of Pounds 532m. George Walker, founder of Brent Walker, cleared of fraud. November US Federal Reserve increases short-term interest rates by 3/4 point - the sixth rise in interest rates in the year. Cedric Brown, chief executive of British Gas, receives a controversial 75 per cent pay rise, taking his basic annual salary to Pounds 475,000. Tiny Rowland retires from Lonrho board. Renault, the French state-owned motor group, is partially privatised. December BSkyB goes public with value of Pounds 4.4bn. Both VSEL bids referred to Monopolies Commission. Merger talks between banks Morgan Stanley and SG Warburg collapse. Trafalgar House makes a hostile bid for Northern Electric, a regional utility. The bid is rejected. Intel bows to public pressure to replace its flawed Pentium microprocessor chips, on request. Maurice Saatchi is forced to stand down as chairman of advertising company Saatchi & Saatchi. RJB buys English operations of British Coal for Pounds 815m. Mexican government devalues, then loses control of, the peso which falls by nearly 40 per cent.
