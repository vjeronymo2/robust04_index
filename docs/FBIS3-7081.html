Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vientiane, March 23 (KPL) -- Mr. Khamphoui Keoboualapha, deputy prime minister, president of the Committee for Planning and Cooperation, yesterday, received the visiting delegation of the Association of Journalists of Thailand led by its president, Mr. Pramot Fai-upala. The Lao deputy prime minister, as he welcomed the Thai journalists, said that he was pleased to see that journalists of the two neighbours had established links for mutual benefits which would enable the sides to exchange professional experiences. Better understanding among journalists could also be instrumental for the consolidation of the relations of friendship and cooperation between the two nations. The discussion between Mr. Khamphoui Keoboualapha and his Thai guests passed in an atmosphere of friendship. Mr. Bouaban Volakhoun, acting president of the Journalists' Association of Laos, also deputy minister of information and culture, accompanied the Thai journalists to see the deputy prime minister. During the visit from March 21-27, apart from Vientiane, the Thai journalists are scheduled to visit northern Luang Prabang Province. The Journalists' Association of Thailand presented the Journalists' Association of Laos 50,000 bahts [Thai currency].
