August 13, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OUTTAKES: HORSE TRADING

Writer-director Christopher Coppola -- nephew of Francis Ford -- hopes to get his "Black Stallion III" under way this fall in Northern Nevada, he told us. He's just returned from North Dakota, where he found a new black Arabian to star in the latest film installment based on the series of Walter Farley young adult books.

The original Black -- seen in the first two films (1979 and 1983) -- "was too old to be in the movie -- and he'd lost an eye in an accident," Christopher explained.

Kelly Reno, now 22, who starred in the two previous films, is also over the hill. His character -- a young boy who tames a stallion -- is in his mid-teens in "III," coping with the Black becoming wild again, against a '50s American west backdrop.

Mickey Rooney is expected back as the trainer, but casting is under way for the plum role of the boy.

Uncle Francis is exec producer. Stacy Jenel Smith
