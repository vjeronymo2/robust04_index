BFN [Text] Abha, Saudi Arabia, June 5 (AFP) -- Five Gulf Arab states on Sunday [5 June] implicitly recognized the breakaway southern state declared after the outbreak of Yemen's civil war. Foreign ministers of the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) termed as "a fait accompli the declaration by one of the parties of the Yemen Democratic Republic and the return to the situation" before the country was unified in 1990. Only Qatar voiced reservations on the statement issued in the Saudi town of Abha by its partners in the GCC -- Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman and the United Arab Emirates.
