920529 FT 29 MAY 92 / Arts: Today's Television There is a growing trend towards getting the public to provide its own entertainment on television. After those ghastly video clip programmes, full of embarrassing incidents at weddings, and accident prone toddlers, BBC1 is offering Joker In The Pack (8.30). 'Ordinary people' tell their favourite jokes and then, in many cases, collapse in hysterical appreciation of their own wit. Samples: What's orange and sounds like a parrot? A carrot. A ship carrying red paint collided with a ship carrying blue paint; both crews were marooned. You have been warned. However, Rory Bremner (9.00 BBC2) is the real thing. Some impressionists are either limited in the people they can 'do' or mean with their material. Bremner is profligate. Last week in rapid succession he did John Major, Barry Norman, Bruce Forsyth, Ted Lowe, John Smith, Jeremy Paxman, George Bush, Nick Ross, Melvyn Bragg, Donald Sinden, Douglas Hurd, Peter Snow, Enoch Powell. . .and more. And his jokes are good. Alien is a space movie about a rocket ship with a hostile life form on board, directed by Ridley Scott who started off with all those lyrical Hovis commercials (10.40 ITV).
