Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Maynayev] [Text] Bishkek January 31 TASS -- A referendum of confidence in Kyrgyz President Askar Akayev, held on January 30, ended in a landslide victory for his policy. An many as 96.2 per cent of citizens who participated in the poll supported the Kyrgyz leader. A total of 95.9 per cent of Kyrgyz residents having voting rights participated in the referendum. These preliminary results were made public by Presidential Press Secretary Imankadyr Rysaliyev at a briefing on Monday [31 January]. "The turnout at the referendum and Akayev's success showed that the Kyrgyz people have rallied behind the acknowledged leader -- Askar Akayev," the press secretary stressed. "People supported the policy of reforms and the policy of ethnic peace and quietude at our homeland." "The success at the referendum necessitates sharp radicalisation of reforms, and they will follow," Rysaliyev continued. "It is necessary to consolidate all the arms of government, to establish law and order, to improve labour discipline as well as to step up struggle against corruption." Replying to journalists, the press secretary spurned down accusations of forging voting results at the referendum. He stressed that the poll was monitored by representatives from all the Kyrgyz political parties and movements, foreign diplomats accredited to Bishkek and journalists from foreign countries. "Not a single representative said anything about some pressure or violations of generally recognised rules of referendum," the press secretary stressed.
