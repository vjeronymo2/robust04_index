June 20, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: MAN SENTENCED IN 1982 DRUNK-DRIVING DEATHS

A man who eluded police for eight years after causing a 1982 crash in Reseda that killed two women pleaded no contest Tuesday to manslaughter, drunk driving and other charges and agreed to a prison sentence of three years and eight months.

Ruben Perez Lemon, 28, of Woodland Hills, entered the plea after prosecutors said they would not seek the maximum sentence for the charges -- four years and four months in prison.

Sentencing is scheduled for July 12 in Van Nuys.

Ruth Hanzlik of Granada Hills, the mother of one of the women killed, fought successfully after her daughter's death for tougher sentences for convicted drunk drivers. But the new sentences do not apply to Lemon because the crash occurred before they went into effect.
