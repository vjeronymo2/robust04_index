BFN [Aurora Intxausti report: "Arzalluz Warns of People's Party's Scheme To Overthrow Gonzalez and Rough Up His Successor"] [Text] San Sebastian -- Yesterday Basque Nationalist Party [PNV] chairman Xabier Arzalluz stated that he was not prepared to cooperate with the People's Party in the strategy to overthrow Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez. "The People's Party is aiming to overthrow Gonzalez. It does not want elections now, it wants Gonzalez to resign and another Socialist to replace him, so that they can rough him up nicely for a year, then hold general elections and obtain an absolute majority." Arzalluz believes that this strategy by the People's Party is no good for either the Basque or the Catalan nationalists. "It is no good for the country, nor for us nationalists," he said. Arzalluz, who met Gonzalez last Wednesday at the Moncloa palace, stated that the purpose of the meeting had been to analyze the political situation, and that no agreements or common plans had been reached. The PNV's leader denied, in this connection, that Gonzalez had suggested forming a coalition government including the PSOE [Spanish Socialist Workers' Party], the PNV, and CiU [Convergence and Union]. The "lehendakari" Jos Antonio Ardanza explained this aspect further, stating that no changes had occurred in the last eight months to warrant the PNV's joining the government.

The Confidence Issue
Contrary to what a number of opposition parties are requesting, Xabier Arzalluz believes that Felipe Gonzalez need not submit to a vote of confidence. "I believe Gonzalez does enjoy people's confidence, because in fact the people have ratified their trust in him. Technically speaking, it makes no sense for him to propose a confidence vote; rather, it is the opposition who must table a no- confidence motion," he stated. The PNV chairman said he was convinced that the government was doing everything in its power to arrest the Guardia Civil former director Luis Roldan, and he believed that the corruption issue was being used as an easy argument for demagogic purposes. Despite these supportive statements, the "lehendakari" confirmed that his party's relations with the PSOE, the central government, and the socialists had not improved over the last few months. "Eight months ago -- for the reasons we explained at the time -- we were not able to reach an understanding, but the truth is that things have not improved." "Eight months ago," he added, "we stated that seven years had gone by without any new responsibilities being transferred. Today we can say that very soon eight years will have gone by and still nothing has changed." Ardanza then said curtly: "The Socialist government's situation has not improved either, and these are the objective data." "Yesterday (Wednesday -- EL PAIS editor's note), seeing how events were snowballing, I pointed to the urgent need for the captain of this ship named the Spanish Government to face up to the situation that has been created," he concluded.
