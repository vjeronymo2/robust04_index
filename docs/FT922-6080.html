920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / World Trade News: Li Peng urges ties with US Chinese Premier Li Peng yesterday urged the chairman of the US-based Boeing aircraft manufacturer to work for an end to trade disputes between the US and China, AP-DJ reports from Beijing. The comments were made in the run-up to the June deadline for the US to renew Beijing's most favoured nation (MFN) trade status, which allows China low tariffs on goods exported to the US. The Chinese premier said he expected Boeing 'to make fresh efforts toward the removal of trade frictions and the further restoration and expansion of relations between China and the US,' the Xinhua News Agency quoted Li as saying. Boeing and China signed three contracts last year for China to buy 29 jetliners valued at more than Dollars 1.4bn (Pounds 772m).
