BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Rudolf Gladkikh] [Text] Moscow April 28 TASS -- Russia's Federation Council sent an appeal to President Boris Yeltsin on Thursday, approving his address to the Federal Assembly on strengthening the Russian state and basic guidelines of the domestic and foreign policy. The appeal said that the council has thoroughly studied the address and "welcomes the president's will" to "continue the course of reform." It said "We consider the memorandum of civic peace and accord as an important step towards implementing the president's address." The Federation Council supported Yeltsin's proposals to set up a joint constitutional commission and a joint working group to coordinate and increase the effectiveness of law-making in Russia. The upper house also supported other initiatives and proposals outlined by the president in his address. "Stressing our strong agreement with the spirit and the letter of the address, the Federation Council appeals to the president and the Government of the Russian Federation with an urgent request to ensure strict observance of the provisions and norms of the popularly-adopted Constitution of the Russian Federation, the federation treaty and Russian laws, and create favourable conditions for unswerving implementation of these provisions and norms," the appeal said.
