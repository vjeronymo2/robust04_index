BFN [Unattributed report: "Police Patrol Seized"] [Text] On 30 March 1994, around 1100, in the vicinity of the spot called Mecka near Kumanovo, three members of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia's army abducted a three-member police patrol at the police station in Zeglane, reports the Ministry of Internal Affairs. The police patrol was on its regular duty when in the vicinity of Mecka they were surrounded by three members of the FRY army. The latter pointed their guns at the policemen and told them to leave the vehicle and to surrender. Their weapons were then taken and the patrol, together with their vehicle, were brought to the Prohor Pcinski border army station on Yugoslav territory. Somewhere around 2230 on the same day, the patrol were set free and, together with the complete equipment, their arms and the vehicle, were delivered to the Macedonian border authorities. The incident took place on the territory of the Republic of Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] 350-400 meters off the border line. The Foreign Ministry of the Republic of Macedonia delivered a protest note to the Foreign Ministry of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, expressing deep concern considering the fact that such incidents do not contribute to the establishment of regular friendly relations between the two neighboring countries.
