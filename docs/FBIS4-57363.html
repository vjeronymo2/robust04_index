BFN [Text] Mexico City, 10 May (NOTIMEX) -- Agrarian Reform Under Secretary Gustavo Gordillo de Anda exchanged information and experiences on agrarian topics with a Chinese delegation led by Agriculture Vice Minister Liu Chengguo. In the meeting, the goal of which was to strengthen bilateral relations, they analyzed cooperation for various programs and projects to help rural producers. Gordillo de Anda stressed that President Carlos Salinas de Gortari has paid special attention to the rural sector through the reforms of Constitutional Article 27 and the new agrarian law. Chengguo said his country is taking several actions to overcome the crisis in the countryside, including the elimination of the commune system and the organization of cooperatives that will permit the development of the Chinese countryside. He said his country views exchange and cooperation with Mexico as important because its experience in investment, support, and autonomy for peasants is valuable and will be useful for China. The members of the Chinese delegation met with the heads of the Agrarian Prosecutor's Office and the National Agrarian Registry [Registro Agrario Nacional -- RAN], with whom they exchanged viewpoints on the Procede [expansion unknown] and RAN programs. The meeting was also attended by Senior Officer Raul Pineda Pineda, Agrarian Organization Director Ricardo Chavez Perez, and Rosalia Enciso of the Foreign Secretariat.
