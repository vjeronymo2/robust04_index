930408 FT 08 APR 93 / UK Company News: Ingham makes cash call and Pounds 4.5m acquisition INGHAM, the worsted spinner, yesterday said it was proceeding with the acquisition of Moss Europe and raising Pounds 3.64m through a 1-for-4 rights issue of up to 4.04m new shares at 100p. Moss, which supplies parts for British classic sports cars, is being bought for Pounds 4.5m, satisfied by the issue of 3.4m shares and payment of Pounds 1.5m cash. It made Pounds 544,000 pre-tax on Pounds 13.6m turnover in 1992. Net assets were Pounds 2.66m. In addition, Ingham has entered into certain arrangements and contracts with the vendors, Milard - the principal shareholder - Howard Goldman and Glen Adams, which include the sale of the enlarged group's US business to Milard in exchange for Milard convertible loan stock. Ingham is paying a second interim dividend of 4.5p in respect of the 15 months to end-March and declared its intention to pay total dividends of not less than 4.7p for the year to March 31 1994.
