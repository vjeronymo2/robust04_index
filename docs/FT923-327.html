920929 FT 29 SEP 92 / Arts: Today's Television So who are the racists in Canada, the anglophone majority or the francophones of Quebec? Both, according to the native Canadians - formerly known as Indians. Oh Canada] Oh Quebec, opening programme in the new series of BBC 2's Assignment (7.45) addresses the fascinating issue of North-American separatism. The reporter is Mordecai Richler, author of a recent book attacking Quebec separatists and their racially discriminatory language laws. Burgo San Sepulchro, Montepulciano, Urbino, Arezzo . . . The Piero Trail, tonight's Omnibus on BBC 1 (10.25) analyses the ineffably solemn and mysterious art of Piero della Francesca. For centuries after his death, Piero was famed principally as a mathematician. Interesting to see whether virtual reality and computer graphics really can succeed in explaining Piero's researches into perspective. More about the mathematical input into the Italian Renaissance from The Works on BBC 2 (10.10), the series designed to boost the ego of engineers. Tonight's concluding programme reminds us that Leonardo da Vinci was also an engineer, and maps what remains of his 'Last Supper' in Milan with a computer.
