Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature: No. 30 (520); by Sergey Logachev and Andrey Petrovsky; edited by Vladimir Shishlin] [Text] President Leonid Kravchuk's visit to the U.S. scheduled for early March should take relations between the two countries to a new level, head of the foreign political service of the Ukrainian president Anton Buteyko told "Interfax-Ukraine" news agency. In his words, the visit will become "a real realization of the words of the American president that if 1993 was the year of Russia, 1994 will become the year of Ukraine". Buteyko pointed out that during the visit "the Ukrainian president hopes to confirm the development of cooperation", paying special attention to "the transformation of political agreements into economic ones". He said that the Charter on principles of relations between Ukraine and the U.S. will be adopted in Washington. Besides, Buteyko said that documents on the development of economic ties and trade, cooperation in the space field and aid for conversion of the Ukrainian military complex, as well as on humanitarian and financial aid, had been prepared for the signing. Treaties on mutual protection of investment and prevention of dual taxation have also been prepared for the signing. In Buteyko's opinion, "the American administration is finishing the re-evaluation of its attitude to Ukraine".
