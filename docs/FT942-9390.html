940516 FT 16 MAY 94 / Emerging Markets: News Round-up TAIWAN Taiwan has abolished rules restricting foreign insurers from establishing subsidiaries on the island, agencies report. The finance ministry said that it had scrapped a rule restricting a foreign insurer from taking a stake of no more than 30 per cent in a local insurance company. A ceiling of 49 per cent on total investment by foreigners in a local insurer was also abolished, the ministry said. PORTUGAL The country's bond market is to be reformed next month with a comprehensive package of measures designed to increase liquidity, improve efficiency and bring trading practices into line with other European capital markets. The government has said that the measures will create a wide range of new instruments. VENEZUELA The new tax on private sector debit transactions is not to be applied in full force to the stock exchange. Instead, the 0.75 per cent tax, taking effect later this month, will be applied just to the buyer's cheques. However, Mr Tony Ewell of BZW in London noted that foreign investors would be taxed another 0.75 per cent when they seek to repatriate their money. MEXICO The government has sold the final stake in Telefonos de Mexico, the telephone utility, in a convertible offering worth up to Dollars 550m. This concludes the privatisation of Telmex, the largest public company sold by a developing country. The privatisation began at the end of 1990 when the government sold a controlling 20.4 per cent stake for Dollars 1.76bn. CHINA China plans an increase in B share listings which are denominated in dollars for foreign investors in an attempt to encourage its stock markets. Mr Liu Hongru, chairman of the China Securities Regulatory Commission, said the B-share market was too limited. Emerging markets coverage appears daily on the World Stock Markets page.
