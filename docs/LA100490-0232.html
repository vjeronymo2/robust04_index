October 4, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: WHOOPI GOLDBERG EYES 'LETTICE AND LOVAGE,' VICE VERSA

HOW ABOUT this for creative casting? Whoopi Goldberg is said to be meeting with the distinguished British playwright Peter Shaffer (he wrote "Amadeus," for instance). They will talk about Whoopi's playing what is called "the Margaret Tysack role" in the movie version of the Shaffer comedy "Lettice and Lovage." Whoopi, who was dead in the water for a minute there, is now speed-boating along on the raging success of her psychic self in the hit film "Ghost." It has already been noted here that an Academy Award nomination is in order for the Whoop.

Of course, on Broadway -- just as on the London stage -- Margaret Tysack has had a total personal triumph acting as the exacting bureaucrat who chastises the eccentric and delightful fantasist Maggie Smith in this drama. But when it comes to the movie of same -- someone like Whoopi may whoop up the box office.

ONE OF the nicest guys in show biz -- Barry Manilow -- has signed a long-term publishing agreement with BMG Music, the worldwide company of the Bertlesmann Music Group. Manilow produced and directed his own live stage production, "Barry Manilow Presents Copacabana," which opened recently at Caesars Atlantic City. His Christmas album, "Because It's Christmas," on Arista, will be released this fall. The title track is said to be an "instant classic."

BMG also acquired Barry's catalogue of songs.

OH, HOW the naughty have fallen department. This month, you'll only find me once in all of Spy magazine -- in one of their "Separated at Birth" bits where Sam Kinison and Liz are twins. How flattering. I do love the way Sam does "our" hair.

Anyway, as Spy observed only last month, this columnist has now "lost it," so I guess this is why I am no longer featured on every sixth or seventh page in those wonderful unflattering photos they love to buy from the paparazzi. I am crushed.
