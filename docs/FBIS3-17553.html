Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ukraine is prepared to play a limited role in the NATO Partnership for Peace program, as long as this does not call for too much resources, assistant Ukrainian Defense Minister Vadym Grechanynov told a news conference in the Ukrainian Foreign Ministry. He said that Ukraine is short of money for fully-fledged cooperation with NATO. However, the participation of one or two representatives of the Ukrainian general staff is possible in planning training operations. Grechanynov said that Ukraine's involvement in peace-keeping operations in Bosnia-Herzegovina effectively means participation in the Partnership for Peace program. A NATO delegation stayed in Ukraine for several days. It studied possible practical steps for cooperation with NATO. The delegation met the chief of Ukrainian general staff Anatoliy Lopata and Deputy Ukrainian Foreign Minister Borys Tarasyuk.
