December 10, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

POP EYE

BLACK MAGIC: Christmas is a time of tradition, even in heavy-metal circles. So we were cheered to see that headbanger-faves Anthrax is reviving a tradition of their own -- returning to their Brooklyn club roots this weekend to play under the assumed name of Satan's Lounge Band. (Tipper Gore, please relax -- it's a reference to an old Bill Murray "Saturday Night Live" routine, not a satanic endorsement.) The shows, scheduled for Friday and Saturday, will take place at Brooklyn's L'Amour club. . . . Meanwhile, Aerosmith just opened its U.S. tour in Charleston, W.Va., with L.A. dates due sometime early in 1990. (When the band played London's Hammersmith Odeon recently, it was joined by Whitesnake's David Coverdale, who hopped on stage and crooned "I'm Down" with the group.) . . . Blues fans take note: Roomful of Blues will be in town tonight at the Palomino Club. . . . And if you're staying home, you can hear blues concerts every Tuesday on "Bluesstage," which airs from 11 p.m. to midnight on KPCC-FM (89.3). This week the show features performances by pianists Charles Brown and Dr. John, while the Dec. 19 show offers Guitar Slim and Son Seals.
