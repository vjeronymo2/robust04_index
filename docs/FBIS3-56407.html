Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Pacific Ocean" Program] [Text] Four Russian submarines, currently in the Pacific Fleet, will leave Russian territorial waters in the near future to join the naval forces of North Korea. TOKYO SHIMBUN newspaper reported in its evening issue [date not given] that a contract on export of the submarines has already been signed by representatives of Moscow and Pyongyang. Quoting a high-ranking source in the Pacific Fleet command, the newspaper claims that talks with North Korea on delivery of four submarines have been conducted by representatives of the Russian naval forces in complete secrecy. The sum of contract is not known. Currently, the submarines schedule to leave for DPRK are being used for training purposes and are practically unsuitable for combat missions. They were all built in the sixties. A source in the Pacific Fleet command assessed that after necessary repairs, the submarines would join the North Korean combat subunits. However, it is not out of the question that they would be dismantled, and the spare parts used to repair other North Korean submarines.
