Language: French Article Type:BFN [Report by Remy Hourdan from Sarajevo--recorded] [Excerpt] [Remy Hourdan] [passage omitted] Standing in front of a pool of blood this morning at the scene of the attack, General Jean Cot had tears in his eyes: [Cot] You can say what you want, but technically speaking, it is very very difficult. I am not trying to escape from this tragedy, but I am telling you that it is technically difficult [to say where the shell came from]. The day before this attack the shell that landed on Dobrinja came from Serbian positions. Everyone knows that. It killed 10 people. It is a difference of degree, but we know that that shell was Serbian. [Hourdan] And we do not know about this one? [Cot] Technically no. I cannot tell you any more. [passage omitted]
