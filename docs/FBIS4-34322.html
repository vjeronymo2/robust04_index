BFN [Text] Havana, 19 Jun (NOTIMEX) -- The Cuban Foreign Ministry has announced that the government "categorically" confirmed today that it will deny all persons who have forcefully entered a diplomatic mission permission to leave the island. A Foreign Ministry note published in the weekly JUVENTUD REBELDE reports that the refusal will be maintained "even if they leave the embassy. No exception will be made." The note reported that on 28 May more than 100 people entered the residence of the Belgian ambassador, on 13 June, 20 people broke into the German Embassy compound in a vehicle, and on 15 June, nine people entered the Chilean Consulate.
