Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, Jan. 29 (YONHAP) -- China and Russia shared the opinion that it is vital for both South and North Korea not to have nuclear weapons in order to maintain peace and stability in the Korean peninsula, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev says. Kozyrev, who is on a visit to China from Wednesday, held a press conference on Friday in Beijing before leaving for Shenzhen and said that the North Korean nuclear problem was discussed during his talks with Chinese counterpart Qian Qichen. He and Qian confirmed again their countries' opposition to the possession of nuclear weapons both by North and South Korea, he said. Kozyrev said they are watching the situation on the Korean peninsula and that the principle of nuclear nonproliferation should be maintained for world peace and stability. He said Russia and China should help solve the North Korean nuclear crisis. There is the least possibility right now of war breaking out in the Asia-Pacific region since the Second World War, he said, but there still are chances of disputes among nations in the area. He added that the positions of Russia and China on North Korea are very similar. Kozyrev also said that he discussed with Qian ways to strengthen friendly and economic cooperation and to promote bilateral relations. He hinted that he has discussed with Qian on ways of military cooperation between their two nations, but did not elaborate.
