940402 FT 02 APR 94 / International Company News: Bank Hapoalim net climbs sharply Bank Hapoalim, Israel's largest bank, has announced a 36.3 per cent increase in net profits to Shk374.3m (Dollars 126.4m) for 1993. Its net return on equity rose to 8.8 per cent, from 7 per cent in 1992. Net profits in the fourth quarter of 1993 reached Shk162m, almost double their level in the corresponding period of 1992. Mr Amiram Sivan, chief executive officer, attributed the improved performance to increased operating income from capital market activities, greater profitability at various bank subsidiaries and affiliates, and a reduction of 22.8 per cent in provision for doubtful debts. Operating income, given in dollar terms by the bank, rose 16.4 per cent to Dollars 588.6m due mainly to the expansion of diversified banking services in such areas as brokerage, underwriting and mutual and provident fund management. Total consolidated assets increased by 2.3 per cent to Dollars 35.9bn.
