940120 FT 20 JAN 94 / International Company News: PepsiCo expands in east Europe Pepsi-Cola International, the soft drinks arm of PepsiCo, plans to invest Dollars 115m over five years in Slovakia and the Czech Republic, the company announced yesterday in Bratislava. This brings PepsiCo's planned investments in the fast food and drinks sectors in central and eastern Europe to almost Dollars 1bn, Mr Richard Norton, senior vice-president for business development in the Middle East, Africa and eastern Europe, said. The company recently announced plans to invest Dollars 500m in Poland and Dollars 115m in Hungary in local production facilities. It is involved in a Dollars 150m joint venture resine plant in Russia (resine is used to make plastic bottles). The investment will be split roughly according to the size of the two countries, with Dollars 85m going to the Czech Republic and Dollars 30m to Slovakia. It includes a 20,000 sq metre packaging plant under construction on the outskirts of Prague, and the establishment of sales and distribution networks in both countries. PepsiCo will increase its Czech workforce to about 600 employees and provide jobs for about 200 workers in Slovakia. Last month, Kentucky Fried Chicken which along with Pizza Hut and Taco Bell is owned by PepsiCo, announced plans to open up to 40 restaurants in the Czech Republic over four years.
