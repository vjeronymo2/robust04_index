940121 FT 21 JAN 94 / Labour health motion defeated The Labour party last night lost a motion in the Commons attacking bureaucratic growth in the National Health Service 'at the expense of patient care'. The vote was lost by 311 votes to 265, a government majority of 46. A government amendment welcoming 'the improvement in the efficiency of the NHS which has resulted in a substantial increase in the number of patients treated', was carried by 309 votes to 264, a government majority of 45.
