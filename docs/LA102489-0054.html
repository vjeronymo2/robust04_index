October 24, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: ANAHEIM;

CLERK FATALLY INJURED AFTER PURSUING TEEN

An Anaheim market clerk was fatally beaten in a confrontation with a man he chased after what was believed to have been a robbery or theft attempt, police said Monday.

Yeon Kon Chang, 49, died at UCI Medical Center shortly after being found about 8:20 p.m. Sunday with a severe head wound in the parking lot of the J. R. Market, 2612 W. Lincoln Ave. in Anaheim.

Chang, who had been working alone, apparently ran after a teen-age suspect, who may have tried to hold up the store or shoplift.
