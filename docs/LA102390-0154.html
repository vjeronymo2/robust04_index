October 23, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

FOR THE RECORD

Menendez ruling -- An Oct. 11 digest item in The Times regarding the murder case of brothers Lyle and Erik Menendez may have been unclear.

Superior Court Judge James Albracht said that, in ruling that audiotapes made by psychologist Jerome Oziel are not confidential, he determined that Oziel had divulged information about his sessions with the brothers to a third party to avert threatened danger. From the bench, Albracht did not name the third party. However, he said in an interview that Judalon Smyth was the only witness besides Oziel who testified at the hearings on the tapes. Smyth and Oziel have acknowledged that they had an affair.
