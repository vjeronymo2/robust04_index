940706 FT 06 JUL 94 / Arts: Today's Television Remember the experts who swore that smoking was good for asthma? The experts who reckoned we were heading straight for a new ice age? And the other experts who simultaneously warned of the greenhouse effect? Now we are told that 'Experts believe that inactivity is as dangerous as smoking 20 cigarettes a day' and if today's 'couch potato kids' do not mend their ways they will cause a national health crisis. This latest danger is addressed in Open Space (BBC2 8.00). One day someone will have to make a programme reconciling all the life-threatening phenomena identified by experts with the fact that life expectancy keeps going up by leaps and bounds. The first of a new series of Grand Tour (7.20 Radio 4) features Tangiers as seen by novelist Paul Bowles who has lived in the city since the 1940s. Countryfile is one of those Barbour-and-wellie programmes which appear to be intended more for the townie viewer who idealises the countryside than for country dwellers themselves. Today, presenter John Craven investigates Britain's largest agricultural show, at Stoneleigh (7.30 BBC1).
