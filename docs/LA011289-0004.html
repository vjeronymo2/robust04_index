January 12, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

'TEACHERS TAKE EXTRA DUTIES'

This morning, on one of my so-called vacation days, I went to my school to fill out a stolen property report. My room had been broken into, again, and $1,100 worth of my own personal property was taken in addition to $2,200 worth of school property.

In the 15 years that I have taught at Kennedy High School my room has been broken into and/or vandalized five times, twice in the last two months. Last year, while I stayed after school to help some students, my car was stolen by a student. In past years I have suffered a fractured rib after having been knocked down by an automobile in the student parking lot, and in a separate incident I was bitten by a student. Although I am quite aware that there is a large segment of our society that receives a lower annual salary than that of a teacher, for some reason, at this particular moment, my present teacher's salary just doesn't look all that great.

How many of your readers, other than policemen, can document job experiences such as my own? My college education has done little to prepare me for the realities of teaching (drug and alcohol abuse, pregnancy, broken homes, child abuse, parents who don't care) that I have to deal with on a regular basis.

I would hope that all of this would not lead you to believe that all teachers have the same job experiences as myself. Most teachers are able to teach their whole careers without any outside problems, yet, I know for a fact that many teachers have had to endure hardships far greater than my own.

CHUCK HALL

Canoga Park
