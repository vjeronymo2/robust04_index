BFN [Text] Jeddah, June 27, SPA -- The second deputy premier, minister of defense, aviation and inspector general, Prince Sultan Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz presided over the weekly regular session of the Council of Ministers at al-Salam palace in Jeddah this afternoon. In a statement to Saudi Press Agency, Information Minister 'Ali al-Sha'ir said Prince Sultan, at the outset of the meeting, had briefed the cabinet on the latest Arab and international events and reports related to the Yemeni arena. Prince Sultan highlighted also the great role that had been undertaken by Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz to end the fighting and save the blood of the innocent Yemeni brothers. "King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, may the Almighty God preserve him, is making continuous efforts on Arab and international levels for a decisive and firm ceasefire in Yemen," Prince Sultan informed the cabinet. Al-Sha'ir said Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal, the foreign minister presented a detailed report on the outcome of his recent tour to a number of Arab and western countries during the last few days and contents of messages he had sent to a number of Arab foreign ministers and the outcome of his talks with them. Prince Sa'ud also briefed the cabinet on his impressions of the tour. Prince Sa'ud said the wise directives of custodian of the two holy mosques King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz at the current critical circumstances facing the region, necessitate continuous efforts, meetings and consultations to be directed with the aim of dedication of peace and stability in the region. Al-Sha'ir said the cabinet also reviewed the latest reports carried by the media on the deteriorating situation in Somalia, Afghanistan and Bosnia. Referring to Somalia, the cabinet expressed regret over the latest and sharp escalations in fighting in Mogadishu between the conflicting factions for the third day running that led to a big number of casualties and the loss of lives. It also expressed concern on the sudden escalations in air bombardment operations in the western Afghan city of Herat. The cabinet also followed the latest developments in the Bosnian arena and the efforts being made by commander of the Bosnian Army to defend the lives, territories and honour of the Bosnian people on all fronts vis-a-vis the unannounced Serbian attacks. In this context, al-Sha'ir said, the Saudi Council of Ministers appealed to the conflicting parties throughout Arab and Islamic worlds to put weapons aside, seek rational and amicable solutions for disputes bearing in mind that conflicts between brothers would serve no one except their enemies and will never be in the best interests of the Arab and Islamic nations. "The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia is always looking forward for establishment of peace and justice all over the world," al-Sha'ir noted. Meanwhile, the cabinet reviewed a number of domestic items on its agenda and Prince Sultan listened to views and suggestions made by the concerned ministers on them. It also opted for the necessary procedures on them. The cabinet agreed on the renewal of the commercial and economic agreement between the Government of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and the Lebanese Republic which was concluded in 1391 AH [27 February 1971] for one more year effective from 26-1-1415 [5 July 1994]. It endorsed the closing account of the department of retirement and pensions for the fiscal year 1411/1412 AH [24 July 1990 to 13 July 1991] and approved some appointments at Grade 14th.
