921202 FT 02 DEC 92 / International Company News: Canadians have 17% stake in paper venture THE merger of KNP, VRG-Groep and Buhrmann-Tetterode into Europe's second-biggest paper and packaging group will leave Canada's MacMillan Bloedel with an important shareholding, writes Robert Gibbens in Montreal. MacMillan, 49 per cent held by Noranda Forest, Canada's biggest forest products company, owns 30.5 per cent of KNP, worth around CDollars 300m (USDollars 233m) at present prices. The share exchange merger as proposed would leave MacMillan with about 17 per cent of the new company.
