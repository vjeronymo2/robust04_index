BFN [Excerpt] Ankara, June 7 (A.A.) -- Foreign Ministry Deputy Undersecretary Tugay Ulucevik said on Tuesday [7 June] that Turkey called the United Nations Security Council members to have a positive approach toward an agreement on Cyprus that will not pave the way to destroy a possible agreement. A Foreign Ministry statement said Ulucevik met Pakistani, British, Chinese, Russian, U.S., Oman, New Zealand, Spanish, Argentinian, and Brazilian ambassadors on June 6 and 7 separately and conveyed to them that Turkey supported Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus' constructive attitude toward confidence-building measures during a negotiation process. The statement said the UN secretary general's report failed to help find a lasting solution to the Cyprus problem. The statement said Ulucevik wanted the ambassadors to take the May 30 improvements into consideration adding that Turkey expected to find a realistic and balanced solution that is based on equality in Cyprus problem.
