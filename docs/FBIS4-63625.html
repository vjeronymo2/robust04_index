BFN [Text] Moscow, 23 May -- The following is the text of an edict by the Russian president "on the lifting of quotas and licenses on goods and services for export": "To regulate the export of goods and services as well as to create equal economic conditions for all parties supplying goods beyond Russia's borders, I decree: 1. That it be established that from 1 July 1994 quotas and licenses for goods and services exported should be applied only with regard to those goods and services exported in accordance with international obligations of the Russian Federation. 2. That the Russian Government should from 1 July 1994 revoke all privileges with regard to the levying of customs duty on exported goods, including budget allocations for the payment of customs duty. This applies as well as to the export of goods and services for federal and state needs (except for goods exported to the countries of the near abroad in accordance with inter- governmental agreements). This excludes privileges granted in accordance with the Russian law "on customs tariffs," the Russian law "on excise duty," and the Russian customs code. 3. This decree comes into force from the moment of signing. [Signed] Russian President B. Yeltsin, Moscow, the Kremlin [Dated] 23 May 1994
