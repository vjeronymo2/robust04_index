930324 FT 24 MAR 93 / International Capital Markets: Egypt investment fund planned KIDDER Peabody, the Investment & Securities Group of Egypt, and the International Finance Corporation are to create a Dollars 50m investment fund - the first direct investment fund in Egypt to be managed in the country, writes Mark Nicholson in Cairo. The fund, which its creators hope will be up and running by June this year, will invest directly in Egyptian securities and private equity, offer mezzanine capital to Egyptian companies, and possibly invest in privatisation issues under the government's IMF and World Bank guided reform programme. The investment vehicle will be open to individuals and institutions for a minimum subscription of Dollars 500,000.
