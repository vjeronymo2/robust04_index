BFN [Text] Swedish Prime Minister Carl Bildt arrived in Kuala Lumpur today for a three-day official visit to Malaysia. This first visit to Malaysia by a Swedish head of government was welcomed by Foreign Minister Datuk Abdullah Ahmad Badawi at the Kuala Lumpur International Airport. The Swedish prime minister, who is scheduled to hold talks with Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir and Deputy Prime Minister Datuk Sri Anwar Ibrahim, is visiting Malaysia at the invitation of the government. He is also scheduled to attend a briefing at the Economic Planning Unit of the Prime Minister's Department, meet with Malaysian businessmen based in Sweden [as heard], and visit the Proton car plant in Shah Alam. Malaysia has signed several bilateral agreements with Sweden, namely a technical and economic cooperation agreement, a double taxation avoidance agreement, an investment guarantee agreement, and an air services agreement.
