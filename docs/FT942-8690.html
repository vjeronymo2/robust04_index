940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / Observer: Facts found Is the video age catching up with Westminster at last? Having put up with and/or taken starring roles in front of the television cameras in the House for some years now, MPs may be less than thrilled by the emergence of the video-conference. Yesterday, the trade and industry select committee was able to consult two witnesses in Washington DC. Heaven forbid, if this habit catches on, that it could herald the end of all those overseas jollies - sorry, overseas fact-finding visits - when MPs head for the sun at the expense of the British taxpayer.
