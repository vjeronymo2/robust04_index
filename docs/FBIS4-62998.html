BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Denis Perkin] [Text] Moscow, 19 May -- Finnish President Martti Ahtisaari has just left Moscow. On the last day of his official visit to the Russian capital he met with Vladimir Shumeyko and Ivan Rybkin, chairmen of the two chambers of the Russian Federal Assembly. During yesterday's talks, Martti Ahtisaari and Russian President Boris Yeltsin discussed about 20 questions concerning bilateral relations and international affairs. "There are no political problems between Russia and Finland;" this is how Boris Yeltsin described the outcome of the talks. Both countries' points of view on international problems, including the Yugoslav crisis, coincide, he noted. On Thursday, M. Ahtisaari had a meeting with Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin. During the conversation they discussed bilateral commercial and economic cooperation and the Russian regions' more active involvement in partnership with Finland.
