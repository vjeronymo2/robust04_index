November 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

HINTS FROM HELOISE: THANKSGIVING IS ALSO A TIME FOR EXCHANGING PRESENTS

DEAR HELOISE: I would like to share an idea I used last year for a family get-together over the Thanksgiving holiday.

Since all the family would be together during Thanksgiving but not Christmas, I thought of having a "turkey tree" with a nice gift for everyone underneath.

I made it from a medium-sized tree branch. I placed the bare branch upright in a coffee can, then weighted it down with rocks and sand and put the coffee can in a beautiful wicker basket.

Decorating the turkey tree was the fun part, just as it is with a Christmas tree. I applied ribbon bows, cross-stitch turkey and Thanksgiving-motif ornaments and maple leaves throughout the branches. At the base I placed a large table-top turkey decoration and set out all the presents underneath. The tree and presents fit nicely on a buffet table top.

After a delicious Thanksgiving dinner we shared our gifts, and it was a most enjoyable day. Remember, you can't always gather on Christmas so make a special day when your family is lucky enough to be with loved ones.

-- MIMI, San Antonio

DEAR HELOISE: Last year you had a recipe for a peachy substitute for cranberry sauce for Thanksgiving dinner. My family does not like cranberries and I would like to make this peach sauce that I think will go well with the turkey dinner.

-- MS. M., Staten Island, N.Y.

DEAR MS. M: The sauce you are referring to is called jelled peach sauce, and it is a great alternative to cranberry sauce with the Thanksgiving dinner.

You will need: one large can of peaches, one-half cup of sugar, one-half teaspoon of nutmeg and one box of lemon gelatin.

Drain the canned peaches, saving the syrup. Puree the peaches in the blender. To the drained peach syrup, add enough water to make one cup of liquid. Pour into a sauce pan and add the sugar and nutmeg. Bring mixture to a boil and let it boil for about one minute.

Next empty the box of gelatin into a bowl and stir in the hot liquid mixture until the gelatin is dissolved. Add pureed peaches and pour into a mold. Chill until set.

For those who are watching their weight or must limit their sugar intake, substitute diet gelatin and peaches in light syrup or omit the sugar.

-- HELOISE

DEAR HELOISE: For children who like to snap the wishbone of a chicken or turkey carcass, a few seconds in the microwave will dry it out immediately.

This is a lot quicker than placing the wishbone on the windowsill and waiting three days for it to dry.

-- Katherine, Ventura, Calif.

DEAR HELOISE: When a stick of butter or margarine is removed from the wrapper, measuring a tablespoon can become guesswork.

A hint I found helpful is to score the stick at each marking before removing the wrapper. Afterwards, measuring is still accurate.

-- L. N., Winston-Salem, N.C.

DEAR HELOISE: In order to take advantage of special offers that demand proof of purchase (UPC symbol, the bar code), I have started cutting out the bar code of most empty food packages before throwing them out. I then write the name of the product on the back and store them all in an envelope until needed.

This saves me time when a special offer is announced and all I have to do is look up the correct one.

-- CHARLOTTE, Staten Island, N.Y.
