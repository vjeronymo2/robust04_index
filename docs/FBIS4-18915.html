BFN [Text] Bonn, April 11 (AFP) -- Germany has decided to freeze 83 million marks (49 million dollars) in planned development aid for Rwanda in the light of developments in that strife-torn country, the Economic Cooperation Ministry said here on Monday [11 April]. Cooperation Minister Carl-Dieter Spranger said cooperation was currently out of the question because of fighting. The aid, promised at the end of 1993, relates primarily to agriculture, infrastructure work, health, and education.
