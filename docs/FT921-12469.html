920127 FT 27 JAN 92 / Phones venture in Poland SIEMENS, the German electronics company, is to supply Poland with switching equipment for 12 exchanges financed under a Dollars 120m (Pounds 66.2m) World Bank loan designed to modernise the country's telephone system, writes Christopher Bobinski in Warsaw. Siemens will work with Poland's ZWUT telecommunications equipment plant in a joint venture called Cewis. The contract is worth DM58m (Pounds 20.2m). Siemens is already installing switching equipment for an international link as well as local exchanges in Silesia under a DM108m deal.
