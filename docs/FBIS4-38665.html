BFN [Text] Tallinn, 31 May, BNS -- The Estonian Cabinet at its meeting Tuesday [31 May] ruled to postpone its formal decision on setting up the Estonian company under the joint Baltic peacekeeping battalion plan and refer the draft decision for further review to the Defense Ministry. Under the joint Baltic plan, Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania would each set up a company as part of the Baltic battalion to be formed for participation in U.N. peacekeeping operations. All three countries also count on Western support for the project. "International assistance programs are expected to be launched as soon as we have a legal basis for the formation of the peacekeeping company," Enn Tupp, the head of the defense policy department in the Estonian Defense Ministry said in an interview earlier. He said the three countries hope to staff the battalion by August.
