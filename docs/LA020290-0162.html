February 2, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

PREP ROUNDUP;

MISSION VIEJO, EL TORO GIRLS PLAY TO 1-1 TIE IN SOCCER

After El Toro and Mission Viejo high schools battled to a 1-1 tie Thursday, El Toro Coach Bob Chavez realized that poetry and girls' soccer don't mix.

"It's funny, the kids told me to read this to them before a big game," Chavez said, holding a crumpled up piece of paper with the words "JUST DO IT."

"I guess they thought I was just reading poetry."

Mission Viejo (14-4-2, 4-2-2 in league play) dominated South Coast League rival El Toro (15-3-2, 5-1-2) early, but didn't score until Nikki Charette's goal with 10 minutes remaining in the half. The goal came after goalie Allegra Garcia twice had denied Mission Viejo with diving saves.

"We were lucky it wasn't 3-0 before halftime," Chavez said.

During the break, Chavez discarded the "poetry" and screamed at his players with his own inspirational message.

"In the the second half we were ready to play," Chavez said, "We started moving the ball better, but we're in a scoring slump right now."

Chavez credited Mission Viejo's defense for his team's lack of offense.

Shawn Viloria's goal gave El Toro its second tie with Mission Viejo this year.

"I like games like this," Mission Viejo Coach Jim Dutton said, "Both teams never quit, and any time we play El Toro, it's a war."
