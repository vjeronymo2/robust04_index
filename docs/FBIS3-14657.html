Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 30 Mar (MENA)--The Palestinian-Israeli negotiations will resume at 1800 [1600 GMT] today. The negotiators will discuss the security arrangements both in the Gaza Strip and Hebron in view of the grave and explosive situation in these two occupied areas. Citing sources close to the negotiations, MENA says that no agreement has been reached concerning the security arrangements or the international protection in Hebron. The Palestinian leadership has not responded to the proposals presented to it after the round of negotiations that continued until early today, MENA reports. MENA also learned that the Palestinian side has proposed that 100 international observers and 130 Palestinian policemen be deployed in Hebron. But the Israeli side turned down the proposal, MENA added.
