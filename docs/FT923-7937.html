920814 FT 14 AUG 92 / International Company News: Downturn at Laurentian LAURENTIAN Group, the eastern Canada financial services group, minority-held by France's La Victoire, reported lower second-quarter earnings because of the weak economies in Canada and Britain. Earnings fell to CDollars 6.3m (USDollars 5.3m) or 10 cents a share, from CDollars 7.7m, or 13 cents, a year earlier. The British life insurance unit had lower sales. Loan loss provisions were higher in Canada but the property and casualty insurance unit recorded better results. First-half net profits were down to CDollars 14.3m or 23 cents from CDollars 15.3m, or 26 cents, a year earlier on revenues up 3 per cent at CDollars 1.4bn. Donohue, a newsprint producer controlled by Quebecor, reported asecond-quarter loss of CDollars 3m or 10 cents, against a loss of CDollars 3.8m or 12 cents a year earlier, on sales of CDollars 140m, compared with CDollars 125m.
