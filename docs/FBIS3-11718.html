Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The National Newscast"] [Text] Prosecutor General Gustavo de Greiff Restrepo has made his mark on his post. In spite of the fact he has turned out to be quite controversial, the presidential candidates do not dare touch that office. [Begin recording] [Liberal Party Candidate Ernesto Samper Pizano] I would reform the administrative aspect, but under no circumstances would I lessen the authority of the prosecutor as the top official for investigating crimes. [Conservative Party Candidate Andres Pastrana] The important thing is to give the Prosecutor General's Office resources so we can have true justice in Colombia. I would not modify it. If we have not been able to completely organize that office, the important thing is to provide it with the resources so we can have an real Prosecutor General's Office. We must not try to modify something that has not yet been implemented. [General Miguel Maza, National Civic Consensus] The Prosecutor General's Office needs regulations. Why not increase its coverage? It has become deadlocked in the narcotics problem and in the voluntary surrender policy. [Antonio Navarro Wolff, 19 April Movement Democratic Alliance] The prosecutor general deserves the powers he has. He is using them, albeit controversially. Nevertheless, he is undoubtedly making use of his powers, and that is good. He is a person who firmly believes in his convictions. [end recording]
