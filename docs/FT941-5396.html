940307 FT 07 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television You could easily miss the start of what may prove to be a fascinating exercise. At 7.58, at the end of the piano masterclass given by Jean-Bernard Pommier in Young Musician Of The Year (7.20), BBC2 shows a two-minute vignette in somebody's life. Inspired by the remarkable success of its 'Video Diaries' exercises, the BBC's Community Programme Unit is attempting a 'Mass Observation type project'. This week there will be two-minute programmes at 7.58 or 8.28 each day, and plans are being discussed for weekly and monthly programmes with an annual review. One of the most engrossing programmes on television, Horizon, describes the amazing mission to repair the Hubble telescope (8.00 BBC2). C4 continues Phil Agland's documentary account of life in a Chinese city, Beyond The Clouds (9.00). Panorama offers one of those horrifying accounts of entire populations suffering from atomic bomb testing, this time in Utah (9.30 BBC1). ITV begins another batch of its outstanding Memoirs Of Sherlock Holmes with Jeremy Brett as the detective (9.00).
