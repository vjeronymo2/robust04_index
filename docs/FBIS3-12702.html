Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Report by 'Abd-al-Rahman al-Dardiri and Iqbal 'Ali 'Abdallah: "Socialist Party Fears `Somalization' of Yemen"] [Excerpts] Sanaa, Aden -- The Yemeni situation yesterday became a race between political solutions and military deterioration in the north and the south of the country -- which really threatens a "Somalization," as military sources belonging to the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP] put it. The new development at the political level was the announcement that the Council of Ministers will convene in Aden today for five days under the chairmanship of Prime Minister Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas. Meanwhile the military situation continued to deteriorate in Abyan Governorate and in the 'Imran area, north of Sanaa, after 24 hours of cautious calm. The Council of Ministers meetings in Aden will be devoted to examining measures concerning the implementation of the Pledge and Accord Document signed by party leaders in Amman 20 February. However, a prominent minister belonging to the General People's Congress [GPC], which is led by Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council, pointed to the possibility that the GPC ministers will boycott the Council of Ministers meetings in Aden. Al-'Attas said that the "call for the ministerial meeting in Aden came from the dialogue committee in its meeting on 24 February in Sanaa, specifically from one of its members, namely 'Abd-al-Wahhab al-Anasi, secretary general of the Yemeni Reform Grouping and deputy prime minister, who headed the committee meeting at the which the call for convening the Council of Ministers in Aden was announced." The Yemeni prime minister told AL-HAYAH that "the deteriorating conditions require that a schedule be drawn up to prepare the atmosphere and normalize political conditions, ensuring that this program coincides with the implementation of the Pledge and Accord Document and makes it possible to activate the various organizations." Al-'Attas declined to "talk about the serious military deterioration some governorates are experiencing." He merely pointed out that "it is regrettable, and there is a military committee and a committee for dialogue among the political forces. Its task is to halt military operations, which pose a serious threat to the homeland's unity." Despite the military quarters' repeated denial that there is any new fighting in Abyan or north of Sanaa, where the 5th Brigade belonging to former Yemeni President 'Ali Nasir Muhammad is stationed, the Defense Ministry in Yemen insisted on confirming the military deterioration. Aden sources said that the military situation in Abyan Governorate in the south and the area of Harf Sufyan, north of the capital, Sanaa, witnessed a serious development yesterday. And that military officials in the south stress that what is happening is "the start of Somalization, unless a quick solution is found." The Defense Ministry statement in Aden yesterday pointed out that the "northern al-'Amaliqah Brigade, which is stationed in Abyan, has continued to deploy its personnel and various types of weapons, including rocket launchers and heavy artillery, toward the areas of al-Saylah, al-Bayda', Modiyah, Wadi Dufas, al-Dirjaj, and occupied Jabal Sarar, in addition to its large-scale deployment in Zanjibar, capital of the governorate." The statement pointed to the existence of "movements and deployments north of Sanaa, as northern troops from Sanaa headed toward the 5th Brigade to help the forces besieging the brigade belonging to 'Ali Nasir Muhammad." The statement confirmed that "the 5th Brigade's commander, Colonel 'Abdallah al-Shalil, managed to withdraw to al-Jawf area with the help of the Bakil tribes." [passage omitted] In a statement to AL-HAYAH Col. al-Shalil, who was contacted from Aden yesterday, said that "the ferocious attacks he experienced were a war of annihilation against the 5th Brigade, which was subjected to a ferocious offensive using various types of weapons from Saturday evening until its withdrawal the day before yesterday."
