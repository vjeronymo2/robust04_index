April 23, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CINEFILE

Priscilla Presley plays "Girl Friday" to detective Andrew Dice Clay in Fox's "Ford Fairlane," filming locally next month. Look for romantic sparks along with dictation in the off-beat murder mystery from director Renny Harlin and producer Joel Silver. . . .

Julia Roberts is an L.A. street prostitute set up in a plush suite by a visiting business acquisitor in Touchstone's "3000," which films in June. Steven Reuther and Arnon Milchan produce the dark "Pygmalion-ish" tale written by John Lawton and Stephen Metcalfe. Gary Marshall directs. . . .

Matthew Modine and Daphne Zuniga have signed up for med school next month in Disney's "Gross Anatomy," a comedy in the "Paper Chase" mode. Thom Eberhardt directs the screenplay by Mark Spragge and Ron Nyswaner. Howard Rosenman and Debra Hill produce. . . .

Raul Julia is Dr. Frankenstein in "Frankenstein Unbound," which marks the directorial return (a record 18-year hiatus) of Roger Corman. In Corman, Brian Aldis and F.X. Feeney's script, a 21st-Century scientist goes back two centuries to discover the literary creation was real. The Thom Mount Co. production gets underway in Bergamo, Italy in June. . . . In addition to writing-directing "My Father's Eyes," Lou Diamond Phillips now stars in the production next September. Phillips plays a young man -- seen in flashbacks -- who dies in a freak accident shortly after the birth of his son, who's now a teen-ager. Karen Allen co-stars in the Daniel Eisenberg production. . . .

Rip Torn plays Walt Whitman in Cinepix's "Beautiful Dreamers," a romantic idyll filming next month in Toronto. Written and directed by John Kent Harris, the story centers on the writer's affair with a woman doctor in Canada during the 1880s. Colm Feore and Wendel Meldrun co-star in the Steven Roth production. . . .

Richard Ford's novel "Bright Angel" goes to screen this summer with Dermot Mulroney portraying a young man attempting to reconcile with his father. Sam Shepard plays the enigmatic dad in Ford's adaptation for director Michael Fields. Sue Baden-Powell, Page Simpson and Rob MacLean produce for Hemdale. . . .

Guy Madison, TV's Wild Bill Hickock, and Rory Calhoun play old cowhands in Wouk/Ware's "Bad Jim." The shaggy dog western centers on a good guy who goes wrong after finding Billy the Kid's horse. John Clark Gable stars for writer-director Clyde Ware with filming set outside Phoenix next month. . . .

Duet's Alison La Placa joins Orion's "Madhouse" as Kirstie Alley's very jealous older sister. Also added to the cast are John Diehl, Bradley Gregg and Robert Ginty, a befuddled neighbor who sees John Larroquette's peaceful home life crumble as friends and cousins invade. Leslie Dixon produces and Tom Ropelewski writes and directs next month in Tucson. . . .

Donald Pleasence returns as Dr. Loomis in Galaxy's "Halloween 5: The Revenge of Michael Myers," a May start in Salt Lake City. In the new edition actress Danielle Harris has empathic responses with the demonic Michael Myers, who's in pursuit of Wendy Kaplan. Dominique Othenin-Gerard directs and co-wrote with Shem Bitterman and Michael Jacobs for producer Moustapha Akkad. . . . LEONARD KLADY
