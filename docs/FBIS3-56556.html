Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By BELINFORM-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Lyushkevich] [Text] Minsk, 14 Jan -- Within the framework of U.S. President Bill Clinton's upcoming visit to the republic, a number of Belarusian-U.S. agreements was signed at the Belarusian Foreign Ministry today. The agreements were signed by Foreign Minister Petr Kravchenko, and David Swartz, the U.S. ambassador to Belarus. The signed documents include an agreement between the United States and Belarusian Governments on scientific and technical cooperation, exchange letters to the treaty between the Republic of Belarus and the United States on encouraging and mutually protecting investments, a general agreement on promoting projects between the Finance Ministry and the National Bank of the Republic of Belarus and the U.S. Export-Import Bank, as well as a memorandum on mutual understanding between the U.S. Government and the government of the Republic of Belarus regarding the intent to sign an agreement on setting up a joint commission for agro-business and rural development. Also, the Belarusian Foreign Ministry and the U.S. Embassy exchanged notes on the Republic of Belarus' application of the Soviet-U.S. agreement on cooperation between customs.
