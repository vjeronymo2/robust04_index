BFN [Text] Pakistan and Iran have agreed on a proposal to set up an Islamic financial common market [as heard]. An accord to this effect was reached in Islamabad yesterday during the meeting between Yusuf Raza Gilani, National Assembly speaker, and 'Ali Akbar Nateq, the head of the Iranian parliamentary delegation and the speaker of the Iranian Consultative Assembly. The accords provide for the establishment of a separate organization comprising the Muslim member nations of the Interparliamentary Union and Economic Cooperation Organization and a conference for female parliament members from Muslim countries. At the meeting, the Iranian parliament speaker condemned the hegemonic trends appearing in international relations. Nateq also called on President Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari.
