940701 FT 01 JUL 94 / Arts: Today's Television Having been scraped off the highway (again) only last week by paramedics from the ambulance service, I will not have a word said against these splendid people. But do we really need another ambulance-chasing series? I would have been less than keen to have a film crew record my every groan as we charged into casualty, yet that is precisely what Paramedics sets out to do (8.30 BBC1). Broadcasting bosses would sooner drink Sauternes with their salmon than countenance a series called 'Women Behaving Badly', but in this age of rampant feminism it is OK to have a series poking fun at men and called Men Behaving Badly. To be fair, men do have the toughness and emotional stability to let such stuff wash over them whereas women, poor frail things, need the broadcasters' protection (9.30 BBC1). Does ITV's new chat show, Tarbuck Late, hosted by comedian Jimmy Tarbuck, have any chance of taking viewers from C4's new series of the improvisation contest Whose Line Is It Anyway? (both 10.30). In tribute to the late Rudolph Cartier BBC2 shows his Nineteen Eighty Four (11.15).
