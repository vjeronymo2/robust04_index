BFN [Relayed by Warsaw TV Polonia Network in Polish] [Text] [Announcer Jaroslaw Gugala] A private meeting between Polish and Russian Foreign Ministers Andrzej Olechowski and Andrey Kozyrev has taken place in the Polish Embassy in Moscow without the presence of journalists. Minister Olechowski believes that the eastern policy is one of the most important tasks of Polish diplomacy. [Correspondent Witold Laskowski, Television Information Agency (TAI), Moscow, identified by caption] The meeting was a of a purely private character, but both politicians had time to discuss Polish-Russian relations. It is known, among other things, that, in June, Poland will be visited by Prime Minister Chernomyrdin, and we can expect the visit of Boris Yeltsin himself to attend ceremonies commemorating the 50th anniversary of the Warsaw Rising on 1 August. Minister Olechowski confirmed Andrey Kozyrev's international reputation of, as he put it, a splendid interlocutor. In the opinion of the head of our diplomacy, there is a good climate for pushing matters ahead in Polish-Russian relations. We agree with the Russians regarding the objective of Europe without frontiers, the only difference being that we want to achieve this by joining NATO, and the Russians see everything differently.
