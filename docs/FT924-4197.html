921203 FT 03 DEC 92 / UK Company News: Rationalisation costs leave Caffyns lower Caffyns, the south-east motor dealer, improved its operating profit in the half year ended September 30 1992, but saw its pre-tax profit fall substantially. It dropped from Pounds 297,000 to Pounds 102,000 after charging Pounds 94,000 for branch rationalisation, whereas credits of Pounds 295,000 were taken in last time. Turnover was lower at Pounds 61.5m (Pounds 68.8m). Losses per share were 0.4p (earnings 5.5p). The interim dividend is again 5p.
