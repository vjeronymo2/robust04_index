Fish and Wildlife Service Receipt of Application for Permit The following applicant has applied for a permit to conduct Interstate Commerce activities with endangered species. This notice is provided pursuant to Section 10(c) of the Endangered Species Act of 1973, as amended (16 U.S.C. 1531, et seq.): PRT&hyph;793595 Applicant: Miles' To Go, owner Miles Anderson, Tucson, AZ. PRT&hyph;793595. The applicant request a permit to sell in Interstate Commerce within the United States the following captively propagated cacti: Wright fishhook cactus (Sclerocactus wrightiae [=Pediocactus w.]), Mesa Verde cactus (Sclerocactus mesae-verdae), Unita Basin hookless cactus (Sclerocactus glaucas [=Echinocactus g., E. subglaucus, E. whipplei var. g., Pediocactus g., S. franklinii, S. whipplei var.g.]), and San Rafael cactus (Pediocactus despainii). Written data or comments should be submitted to the Assistant Regional Director, Ecological Services, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, P.O. Box 1306, Albuquerque, New Mexico 87103 and must be received within 30 days of the date of this publication. Documents and other information submitted with these applications are available for review, subject to the requirements of the Privacy Act and Freedom of Information Act, by any party who submits a written request for a copy of such documents to the following office within 30 days of the date of publication of this notice: Division of Endangered Species/Permits, Ecological Services, U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, P.O. Box 1306, Albuquerque, New Mexico 87103. James A. Young, Acting Regional Director, Region 2, Albuquerque, New Mexico.
