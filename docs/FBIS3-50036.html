Language: Albanian Article Type:BFN [Text] The meeting held this afternoon between Foreign Minister Alfred Serreqi and Ioannis Papandoniou, Greek alternate national economy minister, assessed Papandoniou's visit to Tirana, following that of Papoulias some time ago, as a concrete step toward implementation of economic cooperation between both countries, in the framework of an atmosphere of good cooperation that is in the interest not only of Albania and Greece but also of our turbulent region. The existence of the Greek minority in Albania and of Albanian refugees in Greece are circumstances that favor this cooperation and ease bilateral understanding. Mr. Serreqi stressed that, viewed from a judicial aspect and considering the resources it possesses, Albania, which has made concrete steps toward a market economy, sees as a favorable circumstance the fact that Greece, as the only Balkan country near the EC and being the EC president for the current six-month period, can possibly influence Albania's concrete engagement in EC economic projects.
