930308 FT 08 MAR 93 / Arts: Today's Television According to World In Action (8.30 ITV) food poisoning in Britain is out of control. Four years after the salmonella row, instances of food poisoning have risen by a third. Yet this report claims that plans for compulsory hygiene training for food handlers have been quietly dropped, various products providing ideal breeding conditions for bugs have been exempted from temperature controls, and ministers now want to dismantle some consumer protection laws altogether. In the first of three programmes called Roots Schmoots (9.00 C4), the wise-cracking novelist Howard Jacobson sets off for Israel to find out what it means to have Jewish roots. We are told that the three-part psychological thriller A Statement of Affairs (9.00 and 10.40 ITV) is about six reasonably contented 30-something people who eat, drink, play badminton and go for long rambles along the seashore 'never suspecting that their cosy world is about to fall apart'. In Panorama (9.30 BBC1), Jane Corbin reports on Iran's efforts to acquire weapons of mass destruction: biological, chemical and even nuclear.
