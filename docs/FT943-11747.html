940728 FT 28 JUL 94 / International Company News: Tobacco side boosts income at Imasco Imasco, the diversified Canadian group controlled by the UK's BAT Industries, boosted second-quarter earnings by 35 per cent to CDollars 128m (USDollars 93.4m), or CDollars 1.05 a share, from CDollars 95m, or 74 cents, a year earlier. Net revenues rose by 4 per cent to CDollars 2.1bn. Strong results from its tobacco and financial services businesses accounted for the gains. Imperial Tobacco lifted operating profit to CDollars 148m, up 49 per cent, while Canada Trust, the country's biggest trust company, contributed CDollars 88m, a jump of 83 per cent. Hardee's, the US fast food unit, improved 13 per cent to CDollars 47m while drugstore retailing was unchanged at CDollars 22m. The property unit improved significantly but general retailing losses were heavier. First-half net profit was CDollars 203m, or CDollars 1.65 a share, up 26 per cent from CDollars 161m, or CDollars 1.24. Net revenues rose 1 per cent to CDollars 3.9bn. Imperial Tobacco's share of the domestic cigarette market reached 64.5 per cent at June 30. But profit growth will be slower in the second half. Rio Algom's first-half net profit was CDollars 24m, or 48 cents a share, up from CDollars 11.1m, or 23 cents, a year earlier on revenues of CDollars 536m, against CDollars 474m. Second-quarter profit equalled 33 cents a share, compared with 8 cents. The Cerro Colarado copper mine in Chile began to contribute.
