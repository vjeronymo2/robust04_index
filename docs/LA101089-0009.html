October 10, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

LAWYER FEES

The front-page story, "Top Lawyers Get $400 an Hour as Fees, Salaries Rise" by Ted Rohrlich (Sept. 18) may leave some readers with the impression that all lawyers are commanding fees in the range presented by the story's headline.

While the Los Angeles County Bar Assn. does not object to any of the facts cited in Rohrlich's report, the association believes that it is just as important to let the public know that most attorneys do not earn these kinds of salaries.

The association believes it is important to note, while reporting on the salaries of the highest paid lawyers, that the number of lawyers who earn in excess of $100,000 a year constitute a very small minority of those in practice. The great majority of lawyers work in offices of 10 or fewer attorneys, and a sizable portion work as sole practitioners representing individuals with relatively small, but nonetheless real problems.

The association hopes to dissuade readers of The Times story from painting all lawyers with the same broad $400-an-hour brush. Those individuals have reached a pinnacle of success enjoyed by few, and certainly do not represent the majority of lawyers in our country.

HARRY L. HATHAWAY

President

L.A. County Bar Assn.
