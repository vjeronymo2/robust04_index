Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Brasilia -- On 3 March the Brazilian Senate approved the appointment of Ruy Coutinho do Nascimento as chairman of the Administrative Council for Economic Defense (CADE), and of Alkimar Moura as head of monetary policy of the Central Bank. Coutinho obtained 46 votes, with three votes against him and two abstentions, while Alkimar obtained 40 votes, seven against and two abstentions. CADE is one of Finance Minister Henrique Cardoso's main instruments to control oligopolies and cartels. It will resort to the antitrust law to prosecute, on behalf of the government, company directors who set excessive prices for their products. If found guilty, these directors may go to prison.
