BFN [Text] Beijing, April 6 (XINHUA) -- A contract was signed here today by the Chinese National Technical Import and Export Corporation (CNTIEC) and the Japanese Tomen Co. to bring loading and unloading equipment from Japan to the construction of the northern China port of Qinhuangdao. Prior to the signing ceremony this afternoon, Chinese Vice- Premier Li Lanqing met with Kentaroo Aikawa, general manager of the Japanese Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Ltd., and Yasuo Matsukawa, general manager of the Tomen Co., who have been here to attend the ceremony. The equipment worth 95 million U.S. dollars is to be provided for the fourth phase of the Qinhuangdao Coal Terminal Project, which is one of the key construction projects of the eighth five-year plan (1991-1995). According to the contract, China will import a whole coal terminal loading and unloading system, affiliated equipment and computer control system. The project is due to be finished by the end of 1996. The coal transport capability of Qinhuangdao will then increase to 90 million tons from 60 million tons. The system was bought, after international bidding, by the CNTIEC with loans from the Japan Overseas Economic Cooperation Fund. The Tomen Co. won the bidding.
