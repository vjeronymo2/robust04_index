Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] On the evening of 9 February, Defense Agency Director General Kazuo Aichi exchanged views by telephone with new U.S. Defense Secretary Perry on the issue of nuclear inspections in the DPRK (North Korea). During the telephone conversation, they agreed on the need for Japan and the United States to communicate and cooperate closely with each other in dealing with the issue. Mr. Aichi telephoned Mr. Perry to congratulate him on his assumption of office as secretary of defense. According to Mr. Aichi, they affirmed that the rough sailing at the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks should not impair Japan-U.S. security relations.
