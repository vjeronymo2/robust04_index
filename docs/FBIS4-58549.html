BFN [Text] The UN special envoy for Afghan affairs, Mr. Mahmoud Mestiri, met Foreign Affairs Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati, this evening. Mestiri conveyed a report on the outcome of his negotiations with the officials of Saudi Arabia, Pakistan, and Afghanistan and said: The United Nations is seeking to find a way to end the current crisis in Afghanistan. He also stressed the use of the Islamic Republic of Iran's experiences in solving the Afghan crisis. Mr. Velayati referred to the Islamic Republic of Iran's efforts to return peace to Afghanistan and said: All of that country's neighbors stress the establishment of peace and stability in Kabul because internal situation in Afghanistan also affects its neighbors. The special UN envoy for Afghan affair also spoke to our correspondent describing the existing unrest in Kabul as the main obstacle on the way to achieve a fundamental solution and the return of Afghan refugees to their country. Mr. Mestiri agreed that the help provided by the United Nations to the Afghan refugees who live in Iran is very little and expressed the hope that the UNHCR would pay attention to that matter. On the basis of the same report, the Afghan High Coordination Council envoys also met and conferred with Mr Velayati this evening. Recent developments in Afghanistan were reviewed during that meeting.
