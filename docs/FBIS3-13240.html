Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 11 Mar (MENA)--An Israeli Arab delegation led by 'Abd-al-Wahhab Darawshah, leader of the Arab Democratic Party and member of the Israeli Knesset, arrived in Cairo from Damascus this morning. Arriving, Darawshah said the delegation met Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad, who stated that Syria wants a just and comprehensive peace in accordance with UN resolutions and that it is ready to apply the principle of "full peace in return for full withdrawal." Darawshah said al-Asad stressed that Syria wants to strengthen and unify Arab coordination to mark real achievements for the Arab parties. Al-Asad explained that Syria has a free Arab will and that no solution can be imposed on it through pressure or coercion. Darawshah said he also met Syrian Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar', who stressed Syria's readiness to receive Israeli Arab delegations any time. Darawshah said the Syrian Government did not ask him to convey a specific message to the Israeli Government. But what he heard is considered a message of peace, which he will sincerely convey to the Israeli Knesset and Government. Concluding, Darawshah said: We trust the Syrian stance, which seeks to achieve peace. The ball is now in Israel's court. Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin must frankly declare that he is ready for a full withdrawal. Darawshah said Syria is against settlement activity in the occupied territories and demands international protection for the Palestinians.
