930810 FT 10 AUG 93 / World News in Brief: Nato warns of air strikes if Serbs fail to lift siege Manfred Worner, Nato secretary-general, yesterday issued a warning to Bosnian Serbs that the alliance was prepared to take military action, including air strikes, if the siege of Sarajevo was not lifted. However, a four-hour meeting of the Nato Council in Brussels stopped short of ordering attacks. The organisation insisted further UN consultations and Nato meetings were needed before action could be taken. Meanwhile, five-year-old Irma Hadzimuratovic, critically injured in a mortar attack, was airlifted from Sarajevo to Great Ormond Street Hospital in London. Nato pulls punches over air strikes, Page 2
