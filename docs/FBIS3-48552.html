Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Naha, Jan. 26 KYODO -- A warning shot was fired Tuesday [25 January] night at a Panamanian-registered ship in the East China Sea as 15 Chinese-speaking men with weapons boarded and inspected the vessel, the Maritime Safety Agency [MSA] said Wednesday. The MSA said the incident took place about 60 kilometers northwest of Okinawa Prefecture's Iheya-Jima Island. The 926-ton Nanshin Maru, a freezer cargo vessel, was boarded by the 15 men from another ship, MSA officials said. One of the men said in Chinese that they were checking the ship as part of measures to keep tabs on unlawful trade between China and Taiwan, the officials said. The man also told a Japanese crew member and others of the Nanshin Maru that he was sorry for the trouble the surprise check had caused, they said. Japanese maritime authorities said that between March 1992 and December 1993 they logged 75 cases of warning shots, quarantine inspections and tailings in the East China Sea. China has acknowledged that its patrol ships were involved in three cases. Japanese officials said Japan will check the latest episode with the Chinese Government.
