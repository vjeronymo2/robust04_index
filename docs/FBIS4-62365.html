BFN [Text] Armenian Defense Minister Serzh Sarkisyan met Aide of the U.S. State Secretary for Coordination on Affairs of the CIS new independent states, James Collins, in Yerevan on 14 May. They discussed guarantees for security of the Nagorno-Karabakh population and the stages for establishment of peace in the zone of the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict after the cease-fire. This was announced to INTERFAX by the Armenian Defense Ministry press center. During the meeting Sarkisyan considered it necessary to separate the conflicting parties after the armistice with utilization of special forces. He suggested that this would guarantee security for the Nagorno-Karabakh population. James Collins proposed that the U.S. and Armenian defense structures continue to cooperate. The NATO Partnership for Peace program was also discussed at the meeting. Contrary to Georgia and Azerbaijan, Armenia has not as yet joined it.
