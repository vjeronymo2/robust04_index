940510 FT 10 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television Attitudes among the chattering classes towards Islamic fundamentalism suggest that a liberal is someone who is incapable of taking his own side in an argument. The appalling nature of these fanatics is as clear from the newsreels showing children being taught to hit their own heads until they bleed as it is from the fatwa against Salman Rushdie. Yet it is rare in this country to hear Hizbollah condemned as mad, bad and deeply uncivilised. It will be interesting to see what image emerges from Assignment's report on 'Allah's Army' (7.45 BBC2). Last week's opening episode of Once Upon A Time In The North received some remarkably good reviews. To me it looked like just another desperately contrived sitcom, with the usual sit (widely diverse family group, Mum a a heroine with common sense, Dad a hypocritical, ineffectual prat) and far too little com (8.30 BBC1). Whatever you may think of what is said on Without Walls you cannot deny that they keep on coming up with interesting topics. Today Blake Morrison considers attitudes towards childhood (9.00 C4).
