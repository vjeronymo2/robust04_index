Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] The Cease-Fire Commission [CCF] has (?issued) a statement concerning the start of the process of destroying ammunition and explosive devices in government and Mozambique National Resistance [Renamo] troop assembly areas. At a meeting in Maputo yesterday, the CCF found that the pace of government and Renamo troop confinement has dropped slightly. The United Nations has called on the two sides to replace troops as they are demobilized. At a meeting to analyze the evolution of the troop confinement and demobilization process, Renamo presented complaints that men were allegedly being trained in Goba, in Maputo Province, and that Mozambique Armed Forces, FAM, soldiers were allegedly selling weapons to civilians in Tete Province. The CCF promised to investigate on the ground the allegations that have been presented by the movement led by Afonso Dhlakama.
