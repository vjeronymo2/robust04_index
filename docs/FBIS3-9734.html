Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, March 28 -- President Lech Walesa on Monday met with Hungarian President Arpad Goncz at the start of his three-day official visit to Poland. "I would like to expand our cooperation, it is possible and even necessary," the Polish president said at the start of the talks. Both presidents agreed that regional cooperation is a "supreme goal" of Central European countries. President Goncz stressed that despite internal problems, the Visegrad Group has stable prospects for development in the future. Polish-Hungarian plenary talks focused on economic issues. Representatives of both countries stressed the necessity to rebuild Polish-Hungarian trade contacts. Turnover between the two countries dropped from 450 million dollars in 1991 to 200 million 1993.
