Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Sitnikov] [Text] United Nations February 10 TASS -- Russia warns of serious consequences of air strikes in Bosnia-Herzegovina and notes that all issues connected with the use of air force should be settled under the aegis of the U.N. Security Council, says a letter by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev addressed to U.N. Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali circulated as a Security Council document here on Wednesday. The Russian minister said that he read with great attention the letter of January 28 from the U.N. secretary-general to the Security Council president where Butrus-Ghali spelled out versions of the use of air force to ensure the rotation of troops in Srebrenica and to open the airport in Tuzla. "We regard this letter as an important step aimed at fulfilling provisions of appropriate Security Council resolutions, which rule that all issues pertaining to the use of air force in security areas of Bosnia and Herzegovina and around them to help U.N. peace keepers in implementing their mandate, are to be settled under the leadership of the Security Council, provided there is close cooperation with the U.N. secretary-general," Kozyrev noted. He wrote: "Frankly speaking, the very possibility of a force cover for opening the airport in Tuzla fills us with misgivings. I'd like to explain: We voted for ensuring security for U.N. peace keepers and do not preclude a direct air cover for U.N. peace keepers in Bosnia and Herzegovina if they are attacked. "However, one should clearly understand that any strikes, even if limited within the scope of an air cover, will bring about serious consequences. Situation at all the fronts in Bosnia and Herzegovina will sharply deteriorate, which will more likely result in an end to humanitarian aid and will put into jeopardy the entire U.N. operation. "Taking into account far-reaching consequences of possible decisions on involving air force in Bosnia and Herzegovina, we believe as before that their adoption should be an exclusive prerogative of the U.N. secretary-general in consultation with the U.N. Security Council. You and I have reached a clear understanding, as it seems to me, on this point," the Russian minister stressed. Kozyrev expressed confidence in the effectiveness of only a political solution of the situation concerning the opening of the airport in Tuzla and called for "the earliest political settlement in Bosnia and Herzegovina".
