Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Jan. 16, IRNA -- Damascus seeks for a just and comprehensive peace for the whole Middle East region, Syrian Ambassador to Tehran Ahmad al-Hasan told Majles Vice-Speaker Hojjat ol-Eslam Hasan Rowhani here today. Referring to the talks between the Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad and the U.S. President Bill Clinton in Geneva, al-Hasan said that Syria in no way intended to settle only its own disputes with Israel. Hojjat ol-Eslam Rowhani in reply referred to the massive arrogance attacks against both Iran and Syria and said Iran is opposed to the Madrid conference. "The Islamic Republic of Iran will express its views in an appropriate opportunity," he added. The two sides also exchanged veiws on the latest developments in the region, and underscored the need for boosting Tehran-Damascus relations in various areas.
