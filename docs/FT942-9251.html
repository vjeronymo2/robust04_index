940517 FT 17 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television There seems to be no let up in the passion for elephant-on-the-floor documentaries: those programmes where an entire television crew with camera and sound gear creeps around like an elephant in gym shoes while the local inhabitants go about their business, studiously pretending to be unaware of the disruption. 20 Miles From Piccadilly Circus is the first in a six part series about life in Eynsford, Kent. This week the effort is on to win the 'Britain In Bloom' competition (7.30 ITV). In Assignment Brian Barron reports on Australia's new law of 'Native Title' which enables aborigines to reclaim ancestral lands. It seems to have raised the same sort of problem as the idea of returning museum pieces to the original owners: where do you stop? (7.45 BBC2). Frederic Raphael pursues Jeremy Bentham's argument in Without Walls, that 'All talk of rights is nonsense on stilts'. He says that today's pursuit of special rights for women, homosexuals and racial minorities encourages people to see themselves as victims and everyone else as enemies (9.00 C4).
