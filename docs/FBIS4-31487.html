BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 9/6/1994 -- A regional center for air traffic control was inaugurated in Arad, western Romania, as the first in a set of five such up-to-date centers designed to ensure coverage for the entire national territory. The inauguration ceremony was attended along with the Romanian authorities by representatives of the Eurocontrol, the body concerned in air traffic control in Europe which assisted Romania in building the center. The Arad center equipment employs the latest technologies. The center is endowed with an over-fly radar and other automatic equipments enabling the pilot to locate any time the position of the center and to know the flight-angle of his plane. The center is furnished also with a computer system securing a swift information coordination. Beside the Arad center, other four similar centers for air traffic control are being built in Bucharest, Cluj, Bacau and Constanta. The Arad center has been recently visited by a NATO delegation that showed interest in the cooperation relations between the civilian and the military flights monitoring. The NATO delegation said that the development and upgrading of the traffic control of the civilian and military flights would speed-up Romania's integration in the European and Euro-Atlantic structures.
