BFN [Text] Chinese Prime Minister Li Peng has said he would like to visit Bangladesh in the later half of the year. He said this during his meeting in Beijing yesterday with the visiting Bangladeshi Foreign Minister A.S.M. Mustafizur Rahman. The Chinese premier was responding to a letter of invitation from Prime Minister Begum Khaleda Zia that was handed over to him by the Bangladeshi foreign minister. The date for the visit will be worked out through normal diplomatic channels. Mr. Li Peng said Bangladesh and China enjoy traditional relations which are excellent and that the two countries have no problems whatsoever. Mr. Mustafizur Rahman conveyed to Premier Li Peng greetings of Prime Minister Begum Khaleda Zia. He said China is one of the first countries Begum Zia visited in 1991 to manifest the confidence that Bangladesh has in its friendship with China. The Bangladeshi foreign minister said his country is looking forward to China's role in helping Asian countries achieve economic development. Mr. Mustafiz also briefed the Chinese premier about the efforts of Bangladesh as chairperson of SAARC [South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation] to develop regional cooperation. He raised Bangladesh's problem with India relating to sharing of water of the Ganges and Tista and the situation relating to Myanmar refugees where China's [words indistinct] support is helpful.
