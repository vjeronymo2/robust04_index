BFN [Excerpts] The delegation of the National Council of the Slovak Republic ended its official visit to Moscow, during which it extended an invitation to Ivan Rybkin, chairman of the lower chamber of the Russian State Duma, to visit Slovakia. Before leaving the Russian capital Jan Smihula, our resident correspondent, interviewed Ivan Laluha, chairman of the Duma's foreign committee. [passage omitted] [Begin recording] [Smihula] There is a great deal of talk about Russia's role in the European security system and specifically about her special status within the Partnership for Peace program. [Laluha] This was also the subject of our discussions. There is some confusion among Russian deputies because they have not been informed in a greater detail about the substance of the latest specific proposals put forward by representatives of the Russian Government. During our talks with the deputy foreign minister at the Foreign Ministry I received assurance that there do not exist any conditions from Russia either to NATO or to the Partnership for Peace program that could provoke fears about the security situation in our geopolitical area. [end recording]
