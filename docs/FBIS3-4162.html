Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, March 26 (XINHUA) -- A special wholesale market for products of foreign-funded enterprises is to be established soon in Shanghai. The wholesale market, to be located in the Nanfang Commercial Center in the western part of the city, will cover a floor space of 14,000 square meters, the size of the famous Shanghai number one department store. It is the first market of its kind in this China's largest city. Officials say that all foreign-funded enterprises from Shanghai and other parts of the country can open a window at the market. It is expected to create an environment for fair competition between Chinese enterprises and foreign-funded ventures. A model of the market shows that automobiles, garments, foodstuffs and household electronic appliances will be on sale in one hall while samples and products made by famous international ventures will be displayed in another hall for order and wholesale. So far, businessmen from Shanghai city, Guangdong, Jiangsu and Zhejiang Provinces as well as from Hong Kong, Taiwan and Japan have consulted with the market's organizers, asking for reserving places, according to Yang Guoqiang, deputy manager of the commercial center. The Nanfang Commercial Center, which is expected to be completed by 1995, will have a total floor space of 26,000 square meters. It is near major highways, the subway system, an expressway and the Hongqiao International Airport.
