December 8, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

MEDFLY SPRAYING CONTROVERSY

Please allay the worries of the people under the spray for the Medfly. Malathion was chosen as the ideal insecticide for control of mosquitoes in the city of New Orleans in 1965. Assuming the formula hasn't been altered, it is quite harmless.

I flew the stuff for seven years over the suburbs of New Orleans on an almost daily basis and have heard nothing of harm to humans or pets. Please halt this unseemly panic by the citizens of Los Angeles County. It is not worth thinking about.

We used the chemical in the strength of 4% on a routine basis. The application was constantly monitored by the health department and we, in the crews who handled this stuff, were subjected to monthly blood tests which were found to be negative in all those years.

Stamp out the Medfly with confidence and impunity.

TERRY HAYES

San Diego
