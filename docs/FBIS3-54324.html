Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] A two-day meeting of the Economic Cooperation Organization [ECO] foreign ministers began in Tehran yesterday. Addressing the meeting on behalf of Foreign Minister Najmuddin A. Shaikh, the Pakistani ambassador to Iran drew the ministerial council's attention to the atrocities being carried out by Indian forces in occupied Kashmir and the serious human rights violations there. He said that the world cannot remain a silent spectator when innocent Kashmiris are being massacred and their houses set on fire. He said that Pakistan wants a peaceful settlement of the Kashmir dispute. It will not, however, deceive the international community by holding meaningless talks. The acting leader of the Pakistani delegation also referred to the killing of innocent citizens in Afghanistan and expressed Pakistan's grave concern in this regard. He discussed the steps Pakistan has taken to achieve the ECO's objectives, saying that the Karakoram Highway will be reinforced to withstand the heavy traffic moving from Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan to China. Earlier, inaugurating the meeting, Iranian President Hashemi-Rafsanjani highlighted the need for regional cooperation to achieve collective self-reliance and economic development.
