BFN [Text] Cairo, April 27 (XINHUA) -- U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher arrived here this evening from Riyadh on a 24- hour visit to Egypt as part of his current Middle East tour. Immediately after arrival, Christopher delivered the first batch of police cars donated by the U.S. Government for the Palestinian police force to Nabil Shaath [spelling of name as received], PLO chief negotiator. Christopher said an effective police force was essential to making a success of Palestinian autonomy when Israel pulls out its troops from the the Gaza Strip and Jericho. He said the shipment of the vehicles was a tangible sign that the process of implementation of the peace accord will be soon be under way. Shaath said in taking the delivery of the U.S. gift that he was happy to have received the the first shipment of the automobiles, which "will make it possible for the Palestinian police to start deploying in Gaza and Jericho." The U.S. official is scheduled to meet Thursday with Egyptian President Husni Mubarak and will hold talks with his Egyptian counterpart 'Amr Mussa on the Middle East peace process and other international issues of common concern. Christopher is expected to be present at a tripartite meeting with Palestinian leader Yasir 'Arafat, who is scheduled to arrive here after midnight, and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, who will arrive in the Egyptian capital early Thursday. The meeting is to remove hurdles blocking the signature of a final Palestinian-Israeli agreement on implementing Palestinian self-rule in Gaza and Jericho. Political observers say the mission of Christophers present Middle East tour is to assist in resolving the delicate problems facing Palestinian and Israeli negotiators currently meeting in Cairo to reach a successful conclusion so 'Arafat and Israeli Premier Yitzhaq Rabin will be able to sign a final agreement early next month. His second task, according to the observers, is to try to convince Israel and the Arab parties concerned to return to the negotiating table and resume the bilateral talks in Washington, suspended in the wake of the Hebron massacre of February 25 by an Israeli settler, killing some 40 of Palestinians. Christopher is to discuss with Egypt how to make efforts to push forward the peace process and he may call on Egypt, the only Arab state that has diplomatic ties with Israel, to play a more active role in resumption of the bilateral Arab-Israeli peace talks, particularly the talks between Syria and Israel. Before leaving Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, Christopher said the Middle East has suffered too long from bloody conflict and repressive rule. Now the region "has a chance to turn a new page, to put war aside, to choose peace, to end the boycott of Israel and lay the foundation for regional development and cooperation," he noted.
