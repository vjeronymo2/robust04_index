BFN [Text] President Askar Akayev believes that in the past two years firm foundations for developing friendly relations between Kyrgyzstan and India have been built. He said so in Bishkek on Monday [6 June], accepting credentials from India's Ambassador to Bishkek, Ramswaruh Mukherjee. Akayev said he hopes that the visit to Bishkek by India's Foreign Minister, Salman Khurshid, will help to resolve many problems connected with bilateral agreements. It will be a three-day visit. On Tuesday, the head of the Indian diplomatic service will join a session of the bilateral commission on economic cooperation. On Wednesday, he will confer with President Akayev and other Kyrgyz leaders. On Thursday, Salman Hurshid is expected to give a news conference. Friday morning he will leave for Dushanbe.
