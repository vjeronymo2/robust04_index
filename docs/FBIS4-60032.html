BFN [Text] Cairo, 20 May (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa said today that Egypt and the EU countries have agreed to hold exploratory preliminary talks to enter a partnership agreement. He added that the talks will involve studying a framework for new relations, especially as such frameworks have been defined between the EU and Israel and between the EU and Morocco and Tunisia. He noted that the political form of these frameworks is the same but their details differ according to each country and its interests and circumstances. Musa said the framework will consider developments in Europe and the new concept of relations between the Mediterranean countries and Europe, between the Middle Eastern countries and Europe, and between Egypt and Europe.
