BFN [Text] After holding his first meeting with representatives of the autonomy in Jericho, Environment Minister Yosi Sarid said that considering the prevailing conditions, he found the Palestinian Authority organized. Sarid asserted that Israel had no complaints with regard to Palestinian control over the area. Sarid was speaking after a meeting with several Palestinian officials, including Faysal al-Husayni, Jibril al-Rujub, and a representative from the Gaza Strip. The meeting was held at the al-Aqsa base, formerly known as the Hanan army camp. After the meeting, Yosi Sarid said it had been held with the approval and encouragement of Prime Minister Rabin. Sarid pointed out that issues related to Jerusalem were not raised at the meeting in Jericho, nor did the participants discuss the order prohibiting Jibril al-Rujub from leaving the Jericho area. In reply to a question, Minister Sarid said that negotiations for continuing the peace process would resume soon. Faysal al-Husayni said that the atmosphere at the meeting in Jericho had been positive, noting that problems pertaining to the daily life of the Palestinian populace had been discussed.
