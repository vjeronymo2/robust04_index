940526 FT 26 MAY 94 / International Company News: Carter Holt ahead Carter Holt Harvey, the forestry group which has interests in New Zealand and Chile, yesterday reported a 34 per cent rise in tax-paid profits to NZDollars 325m (USDollars 191.16m), reflecting cost-cutting and efficiency measures over the past 12 months, writes Terry Hall in Wellington. The chief executive, Mr David Oskin, said that during the coming year the company intended to extend its operations into offshore markets in Australia, Asia and South America and to take aim to become a leading force within the Pacific Rim area. Operating earnings before interest and tax rose 55 per cent to NZDollars 368m. Turnover was NZDollars 2,476m, against NZDollars 2,461m a year ago, and tax was NZDollars 90m, up from NZDollars 19m. International Paper of New York manages Carter Holt Harvey and is the biggest shareholder with 24 per cent. In March, Brierley Investments effectively wound down the joint venture which controlled the company by selling International Paper an 8 per cent stake. BIL retains 7 per cent.
