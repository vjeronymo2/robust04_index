Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladlas Burbulis] [Text] Vilnius January 30 TASS -- The Lithuanian Government has adopted a resolution to declassify documents and instructions issued in the Soviet period by state institutions of power, prosecutors' office, courts, all structures of the Lithuanian Communist Party and secret instructions received from former Soviet departments, Lithuanian radio reported on Sunday. Documents which shall be declassified date back to the period from 1940 to 1941 and from 1944 until March 11, 1990 when the Lithuanian national statehood was restored. The resolution says that documents containing Lithuanian state secrets and which were listed separately, will not be declassified for some time. Such documents concern former KGB agents, KGB intelligence activities and some data pertaining to communications, geodesy, civil defence and some other spheres. The majority of documents, numbering around 520,000 and which are no longer secret, are kept in the archives of the former Lithuanian Communist Party. Lithuanian ministries and departments have been instructed to declassify documents in their possession until may 1, 1994 and submit lists of these documents to the board of the Lithuanian central archive. Declassified documents will be available to anyone who receives a corresponding letter of permission from the organisation to which one belongs.
