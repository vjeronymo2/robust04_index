930407 FT 07 APR 93 / UK Company News: Midland Bank pay-offs MIDLAND BANK paid Pounds 1.46m in severance to two directors who left last year, it was disclosed yesterday. Mr Gene Lockhart, head of group operations, received Pounds 735,658, and Mr George Loudon, chief executive of Midland Montagu, Pounds 720,000. The severance payment of Mr Lockhart, who was the highest paid British banker before he left Midland last July following its acquisition by HSBC Holdings, included Pounds 265,873 as a contribution to a bank pension scheme. Mr Lockhart was paid Pounds 398,128 in 1991, while Mr Loudon received between Pounds 420,000 and Pounds 425,000. Mr Lockhart returned to the US to become president of the First Manhattan Consulting Group after leaving Midland. The bank, which made a pre-tax profit of Pounds 178m (Pounds 36m) for 1992, paid Pounds 2.2m to directors during the year against Pounds 2.5m in 1991 following the departures of Mr Lockhart and Mr Loudon. Mr Brian Pearse, Midland's chief executive who joined the bank in the middle of 1991 from Barclays, was Midland's highest paid director, earning Pounds 342,393. Sir Peter Walters, Midland's chairman, was paid Pounds 200,000. The earnings of Mr Andrew Longhurst, chief executive of Cheltenham & Gloucester Building Society, fell to Pounds 280,936 last year against Pounds 306,041 the previous year. The society did not pay a performance bonus to its directors. Pre-tax profits at C&G, the sixth biggest society, fell to Pounds 130.6m (Pounds 183.8m) in 1992.
