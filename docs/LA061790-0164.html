June 17, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

PLATFORM;

WE CAN'T HAVE THIS

There are very few things left in the country that are considered sacred. Some things just need to be preserved. You're brought up to believe in God and country, and I think the best symbol of our nation is the American flag.

There can't be any other way to go except a constitutional amendment. Leaving it up to the state legislatures, you're going to have the ACLU behind it, or the real liberal legislators -- people like Ted Kennedy, who are saying, "I don't personally believe in flag burning, but it's freedom of expression."

I don't see what flag burning proves. It's just something that really grips you. We had to endure that when we had hostages in Iran. But to see it in our own back yard, and to have Americans burning the flag, it really turns my stomach. That's one thing that I would have thought that our country could stand behind and stand up and say, "No we can't have this, something's got to be done about it, we need a constitutional amendment."
