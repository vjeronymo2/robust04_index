940110 FT 10 JAN 94 / Union warns on Barclays job cuts Union officials warned yesterday that morale among the 70,000 staff at Barclays Bank would be hit sharply by news that a further 3,000 jobs may go over the next two years. 'Morale has already been knocked sideways,' said Mr Rob MacGregor, assistant secretary of the banking union Bifu. 'This news will take it through the floor.' Barclays is already well into a programme of job reductions aimed at bringing the total down by 18,000 over five years from the start of 1991; this was increased from an initial target of 15,000. Some 13,500 jobs have gone, mostly through voluntary redundancy and natural wastage. Mr MacGregor expected other banks and financial groups to announce job cuts shortly. He said: 'Whichever way you look at it, more compulsory redundancies are certainly on the cards.'
