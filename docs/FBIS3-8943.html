Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, March 12 (CTK) -- The debility of fourty years of communist rule is deeper rooted among some Slovak high-ranking political officials and the public than one could imagine, the independent daily SME writes today commenting on the events of the last two days in the Slovak Parliament. "Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar made a three-day sensation of such an ordinary phenomenom as the recall of the government," the paper says, adding that Meciar "paid the price for identifying himself with Slovakia." "A state cannot exist or fall with the fall of one man especially a person who uses deputies merely to push voting buttons in parliament," the paper says. It the Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS) of which Meciar is chairman, refused to form a new government nothing will be left for President Michal Kovac than to charge the Democratic Left Party (SDL), the second strongest party in parliament, with forming a new government, the paper writes, contemplating possible future developments on the Slovak political scene. A change in the distribution of forces in parliament after a new government is established could lead also to the replacement of the parliament chairman. "It is unimaginable for the parliament chairman to be a member of the opposition and the HZDS wants to become opposition, the daily writes.
