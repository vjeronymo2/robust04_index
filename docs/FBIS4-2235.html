BFN [Text] Beijing, April 8 (YONHAP) -- China opposes any military exercises or missile deployment in South Korea which could be detrimental to the easing of tensions in the region, Chinese Foreign Ministry Spokesman Wu Jianmin said Thursday. Responding to reporters' questions about the deployment of Patriot missiles in South Korea and the U.S.-South Korean joint military exercise `Team Spirit,' Wu said China opposed any military activity that might exacerbate strains on the Korean peninsula. Wu emphasized that stability and the easing of tensions in the region are also in the interests of neighboring countries, reiterating China's position that the North Korean nuclear problem should be solved through dialogue. Asked whether Beijing would send a delegation to North Korea for President Kim Il-song's 82nd birthday on April 15, the spokesman replied, "China has never sent any delegation to Pyongyang in connection with Kim's birthday."
