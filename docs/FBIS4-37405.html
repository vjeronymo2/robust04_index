BFN [Text] Rescheduling Algeria's foreign debts will relieve the burden on the oil and gas sectors, particularly as Algeria has a plan under implementation. This is what Amar Mekhloufi, minister of industry and energy said in an interview with the London-based AL-HAYAT newspaper. The minister announced that this plan would be carried out in the next five years and was estimated to cost about $17 billion, 50 percent of which would be in foreign currency. The minister added that the plan would have good production and export results and would enable Algeria to repay its debts in the future.
