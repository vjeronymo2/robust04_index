940510 FT 10 MAY 94 / Economic Watch: Greek inflation edges to 10.4% Greece's annual inflation rate rose to 10.4 per cent in April from 10.2 per cent the previous month, according to figures released yesterday by the national statistical service. Monthly consumer prices were up by 1.5 per cent, the result of a new tax on heating fuel and higher food prices. Rises in household rents and petrol prices also contributed to the rise. Last month's rise reverses a year-long trend that has seen inflation fall steadily from a peak of 16.4 per cent in March 1993. However, Greece's inflation rate is still more than three times the EU average. The government, hoping to meet the target of single-digit inflation by the third quarter of 1994, has avoided price rises for electricity and transport, despite pressure from public utilities. The total included 1,283 insolvent companies, up 32.0 per cent. The number of insolvencies in Austrian companies rose sharply in 1993 to 5,082 from 3,658 in 1992, the Austrian credit-watch agency Kreditschutzverband von 1870 (KSV) said.
