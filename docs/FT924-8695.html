921110 FT 10 NOV 92 / The CBI at Harrogate: Rejoining the European exchange rate mechanism BRITAIN must ultimately rejoin the European exchange rate mechanism, Sir Leon Brittan, European Commission vice-president, told the conference yesterday. However, it might be necessary to devise an interim stage before returning to full membership, Tony Jackson writes. 'Past experience shows that a monetary lodestone is needed,' he said. 'In the 1980s sterling M3 didn't work. Total government discretion, which is what having a vast range of monetary indicators really means, is insufficient.' The Maastricht treaty had an important part to play in helping Britain out of recession, Sir Leon said. 'Without Maastricht there would be an atmosphere of uncertainty. The final outcome would be that a smaller group of European countries would go it alone, leaving Britain in the second division.' Sir Anthony Tennant, chairman of Guinness and chairman of the CBI's Europe committee, attacked British politicians opposed to Maastricht. 'They should now acknowledge the damage they will do to business if their anti-European feelings get the better of them,' he said. Sir Anthony said he saw no real danger to British business within the treaty. 'I believe it contains an awful lot that we need,' he said. 'It also contains much we do not need but is harmless to business. So let those in this country who oppose the treaty propose another practical way of getting what we need or shut up.'
