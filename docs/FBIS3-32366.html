Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] San Jose, 6 Feb (AFP) -- According to the first report issued by the Supreme Electoral Tribunal (TSE), social democratic candidate Jose Maria Figueres leads the presidential election held today with 51.38 percent of the votes compared to 46.11 percent for his ruling party adversary Miguel Angel Rodriguez. In the first official count of 191,727 votes from 1,111 polling tables out of a total of 8,363 tables throughout the country, Figueres, of the National Liberation Party (PLN) received 98,506 votes, while Rodriguez, of the Social Christian Unity (PUSC), got 88,406 votes. Approximately 1.9 million Costa Ricans were eligible today to vote for a new president, two vice presidents, 87 congressional deputies, and municipal authorities for the country's cantons.
