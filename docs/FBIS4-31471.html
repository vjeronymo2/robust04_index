BFN [Text] Prague June 8 (CTK) -- The Czech Government today approved the debt repayment agreement with Russia closed on May 30 by the Czech deputy finance minister, Premier Vaclav Klaus told journalists after a cabinet meeting. The agreement concerns the repayment of $3.5 billion of Russian debts to the Czech Republic by the year 2003. "The Government considers this agreement to be a remarkable success and is content with this one bird in the hand, for the two fat ones weren't so much in the bush as up in the clouds," said Klaus. He mentioned the fact that the Czech negotiators had succeeded in fixing the amount of the debt, interest rate conditions, an exact term of specific repayments, and a one-to-one exchange rate of the rouble to the dollar. Klaus specified that the agreement means the Czech Republic will receive $52 million this year, $101 million the next and $391 million in 1996. He said that it had been clear from his April meeting with Russian Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin in Moscow that the debt would not be repaid with oil or gas.
