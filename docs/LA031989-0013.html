March 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

ATWOOD'S HUMOR

I hope readers won't be put off by the emphasis on pain and poetry in your recent review of Margaret Atwood's "Cat's Eye" (Book Review, Feb. 19). Perhaps it takes a woman to see that Atwood's is a very funny novel, as well as a painful one. I for one am tired of reading about how grim her work is; if you like intellectual black humor, no one catches girls growing up and women growing older better than she. But then male wrinkles and dewlaps can still be termed "distinguished" in this culture. Female readers know otherwise.

MITZI MYERS

FULLERTON
