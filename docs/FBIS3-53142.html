Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Taba, 11 Jan (MENA)--Dr. Nabil Sha'th, head of the Palestinian delegation to the Gaza-Jericho self-rule talks, has stated that there is no agreement yet on the Jericho area, noting that the Palestinians did not make any concessions regarding this issue in today's talks. Speaking to journalists this evening, Sha'th pointed out that an agreement had been reached about the (?self-rule measures) in the fields of telephones, electricity, roads, civil register, economic issues, and some other issues as part of the civilian subcommittee work. He added that the questions of water and archaeology have not yet been discussed, while there is a dispute over the planning of urban areas. He pointed out that so far 20 out of 38 items on the agenda of the civilian subcommittee have been finalized. Dr. Sha'th noted that the drafting subcommittee will begin its work tomorrow, Wednesday, to formulate issues related to the final agreement, the Palestinian authority, and legal matters. Sha'th stressed the need to discuss the points of difference during the current talks and not wait for a later stage. He noted that the security subcommittee, headed on the Palestinian side by Hasan 'Asfur, concluded its meeting today and will meet again tomorrow, Wednesday, in view of the important security issues on its agenda. Expressing optimism, Sha'th added that the peace process will continue so long as there is goodwill. He stressed that the three-week period is enough time to reach a draft agreement. Commenting on Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres' remarks today that there will be no Israeli withdrawal by 13 April as scheduled, Sha'th said: I know that the talks may take more than three weeks, but this depends on the two sides' commitment to reach peace.
