Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] President Jean-Bertrand Aristide will today be in the American state of New Hampshire. The president-in-exile is answering an invitation from Dartmouth College. The Haitian president is due to speak this Thursday before the students. The future of democracy in Haiti will be the central topic of his speech. Jean-Bertrand Aristide will then travel on Saturday 26 February to Paris in the framework of a four-day private visit. The president is due to meet with his French counterpart Francois Mitterrand. He will also talk with French Foreign Affairs Minister Alain Juppe during this trip. The president will also participate in Paris in an international colloquium on the struggle of Abbot Pierre, the founder of the charity association Emaus, organized by UNESCO. On 2 March, Jean-Bertrand Aristide is due to appear before the UN Human Rights Commission in Geneva.
