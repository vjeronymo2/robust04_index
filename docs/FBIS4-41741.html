BFN [Text] Russia's agriculture should be reformed on the basis of freedom to choose form of property, Russian Presidential Advisor Georgy Satarov told Interfax Monday commenting on the results of Boris Yeltsin's meeting with the leaders of the Agrarian Party of Russia and its parliamentary faction this afternoon. He cited Yeltsin as saying that no imposition could be exercised in this issue. The presidential press office said that Yeltsin had pointed out that he was aware of the difficulties faced by Russia's agriculture. He pointed to the importance of developing the agroindustrial complex taking into account the general strategy of the country's economic reforms. The meeting was attended by the leader of the Agrarian Party and its parliamentary faction, Mikhail Lapshin, and a group of agrarian deputies. Satarov said the participants of the meeting came to the conclusion that such meetings should be held regularly as they promoted the process of "joint shaping up a balanced policy for agrarian reforms in Russia."
