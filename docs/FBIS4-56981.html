BFN [Text] In its latest survey, the Peruvian Public Opinion Poll (POP) shows that 64 percent of Peruvian voters believe Fujimori should seek reelection, while 63 percent of them approve of his performance as president. If the 1995 presidential elections were held tomorrow, the incumbent president would obtain 49 percent of the votes, while Javier Perez de Cuellar would obtain 40 percent. The survey also shows that only 28 percent of those polled knew what Perez de Cuellar did for a living. The POP survey focuses on the ideas voters have about Fujimori and Perez de Cuellar as presidential candidates for 1995. They regard the incumbent chief executive as "a strong person who is better acquainted with Peruvian reality and who is better identified with the people." They believe the former UN secretary general has "greater international prestige."
