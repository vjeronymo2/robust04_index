May 22, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON;

SMOKE AND ASTHMA

Question: My brother-in-law smokes even though his children (my niece and nephew) have asthma.

He says that once a child has asthma, it doesn't make any difference if anyone in the house smokes. I told him he's a jerk, and I'd like your opinion.

Answer: I don't know whether your brother-in-law is a jerk, but I can tell you that passive cigarette smoke has an adverse effect on asthmatic symptoms. There are many reasons for people not to smoke. Having a child or children with asthma should be at the top of the list.
