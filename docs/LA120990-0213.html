December 9, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

CHARACTER TRADEMARKS BY JOHN MENDENHALL (CHRONICLE BOOKS: $14.95).

This delightful anthology of characters used as logos by American businesses includes both the famous (Speedy Alka-Seltzer, Poppin' Fresh, The Jolly Green Giant) and the infamous, (the bizarre Chicken Boy who watches over downtown L.A.). The styles of the various characters suggest the changes in advertising graphics -- and taste -- that occurred over the decades: The original Aunt Jemima was an ugly black stereotype, who bears only scant resemblance to the pretty, lighter-skinned woman on modern packages. A treasure trove for illustrators and graphic designers. Charles Solomon
