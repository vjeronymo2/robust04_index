Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] On Friday evening Azerbaijani Defense Ministry reconfirmed to Interfax that an Azerbaijani military aircraft was brought down in the zone of the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict on February 17. At the same time Baku asserts that it was MIG-21 but not SU-19 aircraft. According to Azerbaijani Defense Ministry, the aircraft was shot down not above Armenia's Vardeniz region but above the village of Yanshag in the north of Azerbaijan's Kelbadjar region. Baku has no information about the fate of the pilot. Earlier Armenian sources reported that Armenian air defense forces shot down a SU-19 fighter and captured the pilot. They asserted that the aircraft had violated Armenia's air space. The Defense Ministry also reported that Marat Ishkenov, a 45-year old resident of Kyrgyzstan, a retired officer, and a Tatar by nationality was the pilot of this aircraft. He allegedly reported that every month he received salary in the Azerbaijani army totalling $1000 and $300 per every combat flight.
