Example ABC Fish Co. receives shucked oysters from other processors. The firm examines the labels of the containers in each lot to learn the name, address, and certification number of the last processor. This information is compared to the current ICSSL to confirm that the product is from a certified processor. Containers from uncertified processors and inadequately labeled containers are rejected. The firm contacts the State Department of Health to confirm certification for unlisted processors. Frequency 1. For finding the certification number: each lot or batch. 2. For finding out if the processor is certified: each lot or batch. 3. For rejecting uncertified molluscan shellfish: each batch that does not meet the critical limit. Critical Limits Zero tolerance for molluscan shellfish (i.e., accept no molluscan shellfish) from an uncertified processor. Zero tolerance for molluscan shellfish (i.e., accept no molluscan shellfish) packed in containers that do not list the name, address, and certification number of the last processor. Records Record for each lot or batch that shows the date of receipt, type and quantity of shellfish, and name and certification number of the last processor. Corrective Actions Destroy or recall product that does not meet the critical limit. Any critical limit deviation should cause a timely assessment by management to: Learn whether the process or HACCP plan needs changing to reduce the risk of recurrence of the deviation, and take appropriate followup action.
