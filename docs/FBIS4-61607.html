BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 7 MAY (SNARK) -- Eduard Shevardnadze intends to intensify the activity of three-lateral joint-control commission in South Ossetia, told in his exclusive interview to SNARK today the personal and plenipotentiary envoy of the Georgian state head, Georgian state representative in South Ossetia and parliament deputy, Vazha Khachapooridze [spelling as received]. By his words, the activity of commission bears now a formal character, the result of which is the prolonged settlement of Ossetian problem. At the same time, told Khachapooridze, it is early yet to speak today of the political status of South Ossetia. A group of political experts has to be set up, he said, representing Russian, Georgian, and Ossetian sides.
