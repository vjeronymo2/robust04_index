CSO [Text] Bandar 'Abbas, 22 Farvardin [11 April] -- IRNA: The province of Hormozegan exported 1.368 million tons of goods through customs offices in the year 1372 [21 March 1993-20 March 1994], and this figure represents 45-percent growth compared to the same period last year. Mohammad Hoseyn Bagh-Enayat, supervisor of the customs offices of the province of Hormozegan, Yazd and Kerman, announced the above. He said: These goods include handicrafts, pistachios, various kinds of fruit, summer vegetables, dates, machine-made carpets, clothing, leather shoes and garments, plant seeds, and mineral substances, which were exported to the European nations, Scandinavia, and the Persian Gulf Emirates. He added: The value of export goods is estimated at 40,266,354 rials [R], and this figure represents 52.2-percent growth compared to the year 1371 [21 March 1992-20 March 1993]. Bagh-Enayat added: In the year 1372 more than 6.896 million tons of imported goods valued at 358 billion tumans was released through customs in the province of Hormozegan, representing a decrease of 48 percent by weight compared to the same period in the year 1371. He added: The imported goods include sugar, rice, edible oil, chemical fertilizer, herbicides, raw materials for industrial and producing units, and various types of vehicles. He added: Last year 28,145 various vehicles, cranes, tankers, forklifts, and heavy road-building equipment were cleared through Bandar 'Abbas customs. Bagh-Enayat announced that the revenues at the units affiliated with customs in the year 1372 through the provinces of Yazd, Kerman, and Hormozegan totaled R298.128 billion. He added: Last year 98,155 tons of goods went through Bandar 'Abbas customs to Afghanistan and the Central Asian nations.
