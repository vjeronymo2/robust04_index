BFN [Text] A high-ranking Royal Government of Cambodia [RGC] delegation led by Prince Krompreah Norodom Ranariddh and Samdech Hun Sen, the first and second prime ministers of the RGC, left Phnom Penh at 0730 this morning on a three-day official visit to Indonesia -- 20-22 June -- at the invitation of His Excellency [H.E.] Indonesian President Suharto. Accompanying the delegation was Prince Kromkhun Norodom Sirivut, deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs and international cooperation, and a number of other officials. Seeing the high-ranking delegation off at Pochentong Airport were H.E. Sar Kheng, deputy prime minister and co-minister of the interior, and their excellencies the state ministers, officials, and diplomatic corps accredited to the Kingdom of Cambodia. The visit is aimed at strengthening and expanding cooperation between the two countries of Cambodia and Indonesia. During the visit, it is expected that the two prime ministers will sign three documents, including a joint communique.
