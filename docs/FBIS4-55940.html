BFN [Text] Bratislava, May 23 (CTK) -- Minority representatives from Central and East European countries "must unconditionally" take part in the stability pact talks aimed at settling questions concerning postcommunist countries, Hungarian minority representative Miklos Duray told CTK today. Duray is chairman of Egyutteles, a Hungarian minority party which is part of the Slovak Government coalition. Egyutteles would not seek participation at the starting conference to be held next week in Paris but would like to enter into discussions as part of the process already under way, he said, adding that Egyutteles had been lobbying for the idea in Slovakia and Hungary as well as in other European countries.
