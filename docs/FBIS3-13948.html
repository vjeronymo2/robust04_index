Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Libyan Constitutional Union"] [Excerpt] London, AL-HAYAH -- AL-HAYAH has received a statement from Shaykh Muhammad Bin-Ghalbun, leader of the Libyan Constitutional Union, commenting on recent developments in the Libyan opposition, dwelling specifically on the breaking away of a group from the National Front for the Salvation of Libya (the main opposition faction) and the subsequent establishment of the Libyan Movement for Change and Reform. Bin-Ghalbun said: "Since that group broke away there have been voices calling for prudence and trying to dissuade this group from its plan under the pretext of unity, claiming that splits weaken the unity of ranks and serve the interests of the regime only. But that is untrue, because the split is prompted by the care for national interests and aimed at correcting and strengthening the moderate national line." He expressed his hope that "the new organization will become one of the active and strong Libyan organizations. I know some of the men responsible for its establishment, management, and guidance. They are capable of shouldering the responsibility and are experienced and knowledgeable and their national commitment and sincerity are above all suspicion." [passage omitted]
