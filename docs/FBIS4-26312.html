BFN [By Benjamin Yeh] [Text] Taipei, June 21 (CNA) -- Taiwan has decided to provide NT [new Taiwan] $10 million (US$370,370) worth of food and medicine to Mainland China to help flood victims there. "The purchase of relief materials is expected to be completed in a week and forwarded to the mainland," said Chang Sung-mao, secretary-general of the Red Cross Society of the Republic of China [ROC]. Chang said he had contacted Sun Bochiu and Qu Zhe, vice presidents of the Red Cross Society's mainland chapter, upon learning of the recent floods. "They told me that the damage brought by the floods is serious and expressed hope that Taiwan can lend help," Chang said. According to foreign wire-service reports, 438 people have been killed and 6,470 others injured in six provinces in south China over the past 12 days, with Guangdong and Hunan Provinces being hardest hit. In addition, 480,000 houses have been destroyed and 3 million hectares of rice paddies washed away, with total property losses estimated at US$2 billion.
