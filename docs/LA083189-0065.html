August 31, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: SEAFOOD IN REDUCED-CALORIE SAUCE

DEAR JEANNE: I'm interested in cutting the cholesterol in this seafood recipe without losing its wonderful flavor.

-- RUTH, San Antonio

SEAFOOD IN CREAM SAUCE

2 pounds scallops

1/2 cup dry vermouth

1 cube chicken bouillon

1 cup whipping cream

1/4 cup butter

2 tablespoons flour

1/2 pound mushrooms, sauteed, optional

Bring scallops, vermouth and chicken bouillon to boil. Cover and simmer 2 minutes. Remove scallops.

Cook liquid until reduced by 1/2. Add whipping cream and boil until thick.

Add butter and flour and cook until thick. Pour sauce over scallops and heat at 450 degrees 10 minutes. Add sauteed mushrooms. Makes 4 servings.

DEAR RUTH: I have made some adjustments that cut the cholesterol by more than half and greatly reduce the amount of fat and calories.

Although I have included nutritional information for four servings, I think the recipe can easily serve six, especially with the added mushrooms, which flavor the sauce nicely. Try serving my version of seafood in cream sauce over your favorite pasta.

-- JEANNE

LIGHTER SEAFOOD IN CREAM SAUCE

2 pounds scallops

1/2 cup chicken stock

1/2 cup dry vermouth

1/2 pound mushrooms, sliced

1 cup evaporated skim milk

1 tablespoon corn-oil margarine

2 tablespoons unbleached flour

Bring scallops, chicken stock and vermouth to boil. Cover and simmer 2 minutes. Remove scallops and add mushrooms to liquid. Boil over high heat 10 minutes. Add evaporated skim milk and boil 5 more minutes, until thick.

Reduce heat to low. Add margarine and flour and stir with wire whisk several minutes until thickened. Pour sauce over scallops and heat at 450 degrees 10 minutes. Makes 4 (1-cup) servings.

Each serving contains approximately:
