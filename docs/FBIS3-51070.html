Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Text] President-elect Rafael Caldera has insisted he will do everything possible to fulfill his electoral promise of suspending the controversial value-added tax [VAT]. Caldera made this statement during a ceremony in his honor given by members of the Venezuelan Academy of Language. The ceremony was also attended by Venezuelan President Ramon J. Velasquez and other representatives from the Executive and Judicial Branches. Rafael Caldera pointed out that as soon he starts his administration, he will present several proposals to the new parliament in an effort to make the VAT more just. [Begin Caldera recording] I hope Congress will accept this. I have heard that some groups seem to be interested in learning more about my proposal and in accepting it in principle. I hope this will be the case. [end recording] When consulted about the possibility of dismissing charges against the individuals involved in the massacre at El Amparo, the president-elect emphasized that the current government should be very careful in this respect.
