940301 FT 01 MAR 94 / International Company News: Aetna hit by claims from California earthquake Aetna, the US composite insurer, warned yesterday the southern Californian earthquake and winter storms would cost it Dollars 120m or Dollars 1.07 a share in the current quarter. The company put its losses from the earthquake at Dollars 80m, making it one of the largest losses yet announced by a US insurer. In all, the cost to the insurance industry of the earthquake is expected to reach Dollars 2.5bn. The expected earthquake losses, which are after reinsurance and tax, resulted from 2,500 claims in the affected area, the company said. It added that it expected to receive 31,000 claims from the winter storms of January and early February, with net losses put at Dollars 40m. As a result of these first-quarter items, Aetna said it would drop its earnings forecast for the year to Dollars 5.65 a share, from Dollars 6.50 a share.
