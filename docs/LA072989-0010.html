July 29, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

USC DID RESPOND TO THE SHRINE GAME

Those of us connected with the USC football program are disappointed with the comments of Bob Belyeu, general manager of the Shrine All-Star game, which appeared in The Times July 22.

Belyeu said he was upset with the way USC handled the situation, claiming that "USC just ignored us, like the game means nothing. They did not even show the courtesy to contact us; no phone call or anything."

In fact, Trojan Coach Larry Smith has always encouraged recruits to play in all-star games. However, Smith was not contacted about the Shrine game date or practice schedule until about a month ago, when Belyeu called to alert him of the conflict. Although Smith was on vacation, he called Belyeu back that day and offered to put him in touch with the bridge program's administrator to see if something could be worked out. Belyeu declined this offer.

TIM TESSALONE

USC Sports Information Officer
