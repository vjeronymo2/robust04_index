Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 24 (YONHAP) -- A South Korean nuclear scientist was recently appointed to the Standing Advisory Group on Safeguards and Implementation (SAGSI) of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), the Foreign Ministry said Monday. Hans Blix, director-general of the IAEA, announced the appointment of Dr. Kim Pyong-ku, 50, vice president of the Korea Atomic Energy Research Institute [KAERI], Foreign Ministry officials said. The SAGSI, consisting of 14 regular members, is an influential advisory group to the IAEA director for the formation of policy and execution of policy and projects aimed at preventing proliferation of nuclear arms and promotion of the peaceful use of nuclear energy. Kim, the first Korean to be appointed a SAGSI member, is a graduate of Seoul National University and California Institute of Technology. He worked as a regular researcher at the jet propulsion laboratory in the United States between 1972 and 1975 before returning home to work for the KAERI in May 1975.
