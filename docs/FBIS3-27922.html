Kim Chong-in, former senior presidential secretary for economic affairs, was freed from prison yesterday after the Seoul Appellate Court reduced his sentence to two-and-a-half years in prison with a four-year suspension of execution. He was fined 210 million won. The Seoul District Court sentenced Kim to a five-year prison term. The appellate court also released An Yong-mo, former president of the Tonghwa Bank, by giving him the same period prison term with a four-year suspension of execution. It fined An 80 million won.
