BFN [Text] Presidents Husni Mubarak and Hafiz al-Asad resumed their talks at a closed meeting in the Presidential Office this evening. Information Minister Safwat al-Sharif has stated that the two leaders resumed their talks on developments in the peace process and ongoing contacts among the Arab parties, Israel, and the parties concerned with the Middle East issue. He added that the talks dealt with the Arab stance and regional and international issues, including the situation in Somalia and recent developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina. The information minister added that the summit talks reflected the identical views of both leaders on regional and world issues and were marked by mutual understanding and a desire to serve the greater Arab interests. The closed meeting continued for two hours and 15 minutes, after which the members of the Syrian and Egyptian delegations joined the two leaders in an expanded meeting. The two leaders later resumed their talks over a dinner banquet hosted by President Mubarak for Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad and the accompanying delegation. Senior state officials attended the dinner. The Egyptian and Syrian delegations held talks cochaired by Syrian Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam and Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi. The talks dealt with convening the Egyptian-Syrian Higher Committee and the issues that will be discussed within the framework of strengthening bilateral cooperation. The two sides also assessed the progress made recently in several areas.
