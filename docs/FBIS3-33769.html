Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 Jan (MENA)--Interior Minister Major General Hasan al-Alfi has declared that terrorism does not target Egypt alone but all Arab and Islamic countries. In an interview with the newspaper AKHBAR AL-YAWM published in Saturday's issue, al-Alfi said that terrorist acts in Egypt are similar in the way the perpetrators are armed, the way bombs are manufactured, and the way acts of sabotage and assassinations are planned. [sentence as received] He added that he saw things abroad that prove this. He explained that this shows that terrorist schemes are not limited to Egypt but are designed to harm all Arab countries. The interior minister said that Egypt knows the identities of the countries that finance the terrorists but does not want to reveal them so that the terrorists who are there now will not escape. Maj. Gen. al-Alfi stressed that the police are taking the initiative rather than reacting to operations and are aborting them before they can occur. He said the police have succeeded fully in this task and that a new attempt to assassinate certain public figures was uncovered and the criminal culprits were arrested and referred to the Prosecutor's Office. He explained that no public announcements will be made on this issue until the trial begins. The police recently thwarted plans to sabotage vital installations and assassinate public figures during the Christmas holidays and the Police Day celebrations. The individuals involved were arrested. The interior minister said the police have been equipped with modern equipment and that they depend on scientific planning, sophisticated technology, and modern weapons.
