December 3, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

Little Dorrit: Nobody's Fault (TMC Sunday at 2 p.m.): The first part of Christine Edzard's glorious, landmark adaptation of the Dickens novel concentrates on the fate of an ineffectual businessman (Derek Jacobi); the second, which screens Wednesday at 8 p.m., takes the point of view of the intrepid daughter (Sarah Pickering) of an aristocratic debtor (Alec Guinness, superb). (3:00)

Body and Soul (Channel 11 Monday at 3 a.m.): The 1947 boxing classic, written by Abraham Polonsky, directed by Robert Rossen and starring John Garfield and Lilli Palmer. (2:00)

Love Me Tonight (TMC Monday at 4 p.m.): Rouben Mamoulian's timeless 1932 musical, considered the first to integrate songs and dramatic action, is an enchanting Ruritanian romance involving a princess (Jeanette MacDonald) and a tailor (Maurice Chevalier). (1:30)
