November 2, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

WHO'S HOT

Ivan Wilson of South Gate High rushed for 172 yards and threw a 40-yard touchdown pass as the Rams beat Locke, 27-22, for their 21st straight victory.

Gahr High's Randy Donisthorpe completed 10 of 15 passes for 198 yards and three TDs in a 44-3 rout of Warren.

Mayfair won its first Suburban League game in two years, 21-15, over previously unbeaten Glenn.

St. Paul's Matt Borunda passed for three touchdowns and ran for another as the Swordsmen crushed Servite, 38-6.

Mark Kortenhoeven of Valley Christian rushed for 115 yards and two TDs as the Crusaders beat Miraleste, 28-14, to stay undefeated.

Who's Not

The Compton High Tarbabes were held to 28 yards rushing in their 8-0 loss to Jordan in a Moore League game.

Artesia had only 79 yards total offense and two first downs in its 35-2 loss to Norwalk.

The Lancers of Lakewood turned the ball over five times as they lost a Moore League game to Wilson, 10-0.

Cerritos was shut out for the second time in three games as the Dons dropped their third consecutive game, 35-0, to Paramount.

Downey was defeated, 34-6, by Dominguez, the fourth consecutive loss for the Vikings. They have been outscored 109-22 in those San Gabriel Valley League games.
