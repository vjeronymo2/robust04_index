920326 FT 26 MAR 92 / Election 1992: Welsh chutzpah - Ballot Box Chutzpah may not be the Welsh word for it. But there was a certain style in Neil and Glenys Kinnock's guest list for their twenty-fifth wedding anniversary party in London's Inner Temple last night. It was not just that they had successfully mixed the cultured pink glitterati - John Mortimer, Stephen Fry and Cleo Laine - with the Parrys and Howells of their Welsh youth. The couple also authorised invitations for the Labour leader's whole campaign media entourage - not just the Mirror and The Guardian, but Tory tabloids the Express, The Sun, the Mail et al were wined and dined. That shows confidence. But Mr Kinnock was braver still. Within sight of the television cameras he allowed Tom Sawyer, deputy general secretary of Nupe, the public services union, to give the toast. Mr Sawyer, Labour's understated chairman, conceded that his role was low-key. 'I am the ghost of elections past,' he said in a confession that showed Labour is a broad church open to all minority groups. 'I . . . am a trade union official.'
