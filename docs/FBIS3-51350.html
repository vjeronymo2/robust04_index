Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Ecuadoran economic developments monitored by Panama Bureau through 17 January; source follows in parentheses after the item. A Central Bank communique has affirmed that an international monetary reserve of $1.2 billion and an inflation rate of .6 percent in December reflect the success of the government's economic policy. It added that the inflation rate for 1993 was 30.96 percent, falling within the goals established by the government of Sixto Duran-Ballen. (Paris AFP in Spanish 0315 GMT 14 Jan 94)
