Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Speaking at a New Year exchange of greetings gathering which was held on 31 January at the headquarters of the opposition Liberal Democratic Party [LDP] with the House of Councilors members affiliated with the LDP attending, LDP President Yohei Kono touched on how the Diet should be run in the future and said, "It has become apparent that the Hosokawa government cannot do any major work without the LDP's cooperation." In this way, the LDP president warned the government and ruling coalition parties against taking one-sided actions. The LDP president made the statement taking into account the history of enacting a package of political reform bills in the Diet. At the gathering, Mr. Kono also stressed: "A shift in policy and a decision on policy hinges on whether the LDP agrees or not." In this way, he predicted that the LDP's cooperation will be indispensable for the Hosokawa government to accomplish its major policy tasks. Recalling the past battle between the ruling and opposition parties over the political reform, Mr. Kono said: "The Hosokawa-style political reform and the LDP-style political reform vied with each other, but our party's political reform triumphed."
