BFN [Unattributed report: "Shalikashvili-Gligorov Meeting"] [Text] Macedonian [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] President Kiro Gligorov received yesterday U.S. Chief of Joint Staff General John Shalikashvili. The meeting was also attended by Foreign Minister Crvenkovski and Defense Minister Popovski. They expressed satisfaction with the role of the U.S. contingent serving as members of UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] in Macedonia thus far, and discussed replacing the Nordic contingent with another American unit. They also exchanged ideas on the situation in the region and President Gligorov stressed the importance of resolving the Greek-Macedonian dispute, saying it could destabilize the south of the Balkans. Gligorov again actualized Macedonia's request to be included in the project "Partnership for Peace." General Shalikashvili pointed out his country's interest in a stable Macedonia as an exceptionally significant factor of stability in the Balkans. He also announced appointment of U.S. Army representative to Macedonia in the near future. General Shalikashvili also visited U.S. soldiers in Macedonia and patrol units on the Macedonian-Serbian border.
