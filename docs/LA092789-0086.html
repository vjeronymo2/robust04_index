September 27, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

REINER ON GANGS

More double-talk and equivocation by Dist. Atty. Ira Reiner (Metro, Sept. 20).

He starts out talking firm and strong, " . . . Prosecutors will stop routinely plea-bargaining with gang members." But the article ends with a wimp, " . . . R. Dan Murphy, Reiner's director of special operations, conceded that there still will be cases where there is no option but to plea-bargain."

Typical Reiner nonsense.

Furthermore, it is misleading for him to use the term "plea-bargain." A limit or "lid" on the length of the county jail or state prison sentence is the practical result of all these negotiated sentences. Thus, a more accurate term would be "sentence-bargaining."

RICHARD P. WINSTON

Santa Monica
