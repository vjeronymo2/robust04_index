BFN [Text] A further 227 Russian peacekeepers complete with satellite communication equipment has been airlifted aboard two airforce transport planes from a military airport in Ryazan to ex-Yugoslavia, military officials told Interfax in Moscow. The operation is part of the rotation of the Russian contingent of the United Nations Protection Force there. As many as 600 servicemen of the 1,500-strong Russian peacekeeping force deployed in the former Yugoslavia are to be replaced in April. It is now known that many of the peacekeepers who served a term of six months and earned $6,000 have bought cars and will be travelling home by road.
