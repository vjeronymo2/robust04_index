Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Yesterday, Foreign Minister Efrain Goldenberg Schreiber, president of the Council of Ministers, said that Peru's geographical diversity plus recent changes in investment and commerce laws are important advantages that it can offer investors over other Pacific basin nations. Goldenberg today participated in the first day of sessions of the 10th General Conference of the Pacific Economic Cooperation Council (PECC). Goldenberg was the first speaker at the conference's opening meeting in Kuala Lumpur, the capital of Malaysia. The conference will continue today with discussions on capital and financial markets in Latin America. Goldenberg will be the main speaker. He stressed that Peru has Amazon and Andean regions and a long Pacific Ocean coastline. He said this is an interesting option for investors. He met yesterday with government officials present at the conference. He talked with Malaysian Prime Minister Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir bin Mohamed and with the U.S. Security Council Asian affairs senior adviser with whom he reviewed prospects for regional cooperation, promotion, and investment. Goldenberg also met with the Indonesian representative. He delivered a Peruvian Government invitation to President Suharto to visit Peru. During today's session, besides the discussion on capital and financial markets in Latin America, there will be a forum on international commerce that will be presided over by Eduardo Ferrero Costa, a member of the Peruvian delegation and head of the Peruvian National Committee for the Pacific Basin.
