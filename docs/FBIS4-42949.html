BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin has declared that if the new statute concerning passage from the Istanbul Strait is not enforced, a city of 10 million will be in jeopardy, and Turkey will in no way allow this. Cetin met with Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, who is currently in Turkey for the North Atlantic Cooperation Council meeting. In a statement, Cetin reported that the straits statute was discussed during the meeting. Cetin said that he explained to the Russian foreign minister that the new statute regulating passage from the Bosporus does not violate the Montreux Convention. Cetin declared: [Begin Cetin recording] If necessary, we can clarify some of the issues again and explain that they do not violate the Montreux Convention. It is possible that some technical issues are not entirely clear, but Turkey cannot allow a city of 10 million people to be threatened. A new arrangement is absolutely necessary, and the Russians also concede this point. [end recording] Cetin added that the Azerbaijani-Armenian conflict and the Karkuk-Yumurtalik oil pipeline issues were also discussed with Kozyrev.
