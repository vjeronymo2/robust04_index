BFN [Text] Hong Kong, May 20 (XINHUA) - Asia Satellite Telecommunications Co. Ltd. and a leading German broadcaster have signed a lease agreement for a C-band transponder on Asiasat 2 to be launched by China early next year. The agreement was signed by Peter Jackson, chief executive officer of Asia Satellite Telecommunications Co. Ltd., and Dieter Weirich, director general of Deutsche Welle Radio and TV International of Germany, according to a news release available here today. Asiasat 2, due to be launched by China Long March 2E rocket in early 1995, will carry 33 transponders of different types. Its footprint will stretch from Tokyo to Turkey and south to Jakarta and Australia, covering the sub-continent, Middle East and Eastern Europe. Deutsche Welle will be Asiasat's first customer to lease a C-band transponder on Asiasat 2, and the first European broadcaster using Asiasat for global broadcasting, according to the news release.
