Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan and Oman have affirmed the necessity of finding ways for meaningful negotiations to solve the Kashmir dispute in accordance with the wishes of the Kashmiri people. In a joint statement issued at the end of the three-day official visit of the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, to Oman, the two sides also stressed the need for creating the right environment and condition for meaningful talks to resolve the problem. The prime minister and Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id held in-depth discussions on strengthening bilateral relations and shared views on regional and international issues of common interest and concern, including Kashmir, Palestine, and Bosnia. The two sides expressed their continued support for the Middle East peace negotiations, condemned the al-Khalil incident, and hoped that the peace process in the Middle East would not be hampered by such incidents. They called for full protection of Palestinians in the occupied Arab territories. About Bosnia, Pakistan and Oman called for urgent measures to protect the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Bosnia-Herzegovina as well as humanitarian and relief measures to alleviate the sufferings of the people of that fraternal country. The statement says the ministers of the two countries exchanged views and examined ways for advancing bilateral cooperation for mutual interest and benefit of their people. The two countries agreed to work together for promoting regional cooperation in the interests of peace and security. They agreed to sign agreements in several fields for cooperation. One such agreement for cooperation between Oman Chamber of Commerce and Industry and Federation of Pakistan Chambers of Commerce and Industry was already signed. The prime minister conveyed the invitation of the president of Pakistan to Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id to pay a state visit which was accepted by the sultan.
