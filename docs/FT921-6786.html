920226 FT 26 FEB 92 / Appointments: Utilising expertise This week's appointment of William Alexander as managing director of Thames Water Utilities will enable Mike Hoffman, group chief executive of Thames Water, to concentrate more on developing the non-utility businesses of the group. Hoffman previously combined both roles. Expansion away from the core businesses of water supply and sewage disposal, which are heavily regulated, has been slow but steady at Thames, the biggest of the 10 former regional water authorities privatised over two years ago. It now accounts for annual turnover of over Pounds 250m. Alexander, 45, was formerly technical director of the utilities. He joined Thames in 1989 from British Coal where he was head of engineering. The technical division is responsible for Thames Water's investment programme of Pounds 400m a year as well as research and development and laboratories. Two group appointments have also been announced at Thames. Bill Harper, deputy chairman of the utilities company, has been made group director, corporate activities. He will have special responsibility for the environment and relations at corporate level.
