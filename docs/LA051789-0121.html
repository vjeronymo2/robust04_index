May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

SALES OF STEALTH FOR QUARTER: $657 MILLION

Northrop disclosed its quarterly revenue on the B-2 stealth bomber program for the first time Tuesday, reporting in a letter to shareholders that the bomber had sales of $657 million for the three months ending March 31.

Oddly, the company failed to disclose sales figures for the comparative period last year, saying they remain classified by the Air Force. The letter to shareholders is a more detailed account of the firm's quarterly earnings report made public earlier.

The B-2 sales are almost exactly what securities analysts and procurement experts have been estimating for years when they said that the bomber accounts for about half of Northrop's sales. In the first quarter, the B-2 sales figure amounted to 51% of Northrop's $1.28 billion sales. Quarterly sales the year before were $1.37 billion. Its first-quarter profit was $9.7 million, down sharply from $139.6 million a year earlier.

Ever since Northrop began the B-2 program in 1981, the company has been prevented from disclosing sales and earnings on the program, though it was the most important source of growth for the firm. An abundance of financial detail on other programs, however, made the cloak over the B-2 somewhat transparent.

Wolfgang Demisch, an analyst at UBS Securities, said B-2 sales appear to be declining, based on the $657-million figure. "It was generally believed that the B-2 had maxed out at $3 billion per year and that would give you $750 million a quarter," Demisch said.

In a separate disclosure, the company said it has increased its line of short-term credit, which may indicate that the company is preparing to take on additional debt. Northrop Senior Vice President Les Daly said, "We don't know if we will exercise it."

The aerospace firm experienced a negative cash flow and took on $132 million of additional short-term debt in the quarter, the letter indicates.

The company said it is increasing its credit line to "150% of shareholders' equity," which gives the company short-term borrowing capacity of roughly $1.5 billion, up from about $1.25 billion.
