930529 FT 29 MAY 93 / Tory warns on immigration MR WINSTON Churchill, Conservative MP for Davyhulme, yesterday called for a halt to the 'relentless flow' of immigrants to the UK. He told a meeting in Bolton, Lancashire: 'With this government continuing to bring in immigrants each year at a scale, in Mrs Thatcher's immortal phrase of 15 years ago, 'equivalent to a town the size of Grantham' a halt must be called - and urgently - if the British way of life is to be preserved.' He said the population of many northern English cities was 'well over 50 per cent immigrant'. Muslims claimed that they numbered more than 2m in Britain. Mr Niranjan Deva, the black Tory MP for Brentford and Isleworth, said: 'I am shocked and disappointed by Mr Churchill's comments.' He added: 'For a start, Mr Churchill has got his facts wrong. There is no primary immigration from the Indian sub-continent. The only immigrants from there who are allowed to settle are the dependants of relatives already here.' Mr Keith Vaz, Labour MP for Leicester East, said: 'He (Mr Churchill) is mouthing the empty rhetoric of Powellism, whose prophecies of doom have been proved to be hopelessly wrong.'
