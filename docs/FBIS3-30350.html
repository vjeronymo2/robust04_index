Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Geneva, Feb 1 (AFP) -- A Red Cross aid convoy carrying 186 tonnes of badly needed food and supplies arrived in the besieged Bosnian town of Zenica Tuesday [1 February], the International Committee of the Red Cross [ICRC] said here. The convoy, the first since December 20, crossed through territory held by the besieging Bosnian Serbs under an agreement signed on January 27 which gave the ICRC the right to supply Zenica regularly. The 10 trucks brought to the town, held by the mainly-Moslem Bosnian army, 4,200 litres of fuel oil, 600 litres of kerosene, 60 tonnes of individual parcels, 96 tonnes of flour, 9,600 blankets and medical supplies. A second convoy of similar size was expected in Zenica Thursday [3 February], the ICRC said.
