BFN [Text] A spokesman for the Somali National Alliance, SNA, has said that these days many troublemakers were emerging who were engaged in fighting in some parts of Mogadishu. The spokesman said personalities who signed the Nairobi peace accord on 24 March 1994 and other accords and who called themselves political leaders were fanning new hostilities and war in a bid to divide the Somali people. The spokesman appealed to the Somali people to be on their guard against those causing friction aimed at bringing problems similar to those of the past. The spokesman said the alliance was opposed to the war which was being waged people against peace. He said the SNA was not ready to wage war against anybody, and appealed to its supporters to desist from taking Somali people's lives and committing robbery. The SNA spokesman concluded by saying that peace-loving Somalis should unite and counter those opposed to peace and coexistence.
