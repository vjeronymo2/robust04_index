BFN [Article signed TP: "Security Policy -- European Cooperation: 200 Scientists Meet in Zurich"] [Text] A total of 200 European scientists and experts are meeting in Zurich until Thursday [28 April] to promote scientific cooperation in matters of security policy. An information exchange association is expected to be set up. The organizers of the meeting are the Federal Military Department and the Federal Department for Foreign Affairs. The objective of this international conference is to promote the creation of research institutes with democratic objectives in Central and Eastern Europe. The research institute on security policy matters and on analyzing conflicts of Zurich's Federal Polytechnic School and the UN Research Institute on Disarmament are participating in this conference as are approximately 100 research institutes on security policy issues. Despite tight budgets, international cooperation is essential, Federal Counselor Kaspar Villiger said at the opening of the conference. International criminal organizations, uncontrolled migration flows, rising numbers of mass destruction weapons, and increased nationalist feelings are all serious problems for security policy, he added.
