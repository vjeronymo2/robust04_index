Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Riyadh, 31 Jan -- King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz Al Sa'ud, custodian of the two holy mosques, this afternoon presided over the weekly meeting of the Council of Ministers at al-Yamamah Palace in Riyadh. In a statement to SPA after the session, Information Minister 'Ali Hasan al-Sha'ir said: The custodian of the two holy mosques opened the session with a comprehensive analysis of some issues concerning current events at the Arab and international level. Through a critical review of the current situation in the Arab arena, and in light of new developments in the course of the Palestinian issue and on the basis of the persistent historical role played by the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia devotedly and sincerely in support of the Palestinian people since the painful tragedy of their suffering started until now, the king made clear to the council the nature of the meeting held recently between him and Yasir 'Arafat, president of the State of Palestine and chairman of the PLO's Executive Committee. He said that he had wished success for the positive initiative 'Arafat had undertaken and for the attempts that were still being made and persisting on the peace path to achieve autonomy for the Palestinian people in the territory of their homeland in the form that would satisfy them and which they would view as appropriate to their aspirations. [passage omitted] The information minister went on to say in his statement: Following that, the beloved king talked about the economic condition of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and praised the right steps taken to reduce expenditure in order, God willing, to reach the expected balance over the coming years, pointing to the movement for economic revival through which the Kingdom is living, coupled with the continued growth of the trade movement and the noticeable banking activity, to which the reports of financial and economic experts referred. He said that one of the distinguished Saudi economic position is the excellent financial results which companies participating in vital sectors, such as baking and industry, started to announce.
