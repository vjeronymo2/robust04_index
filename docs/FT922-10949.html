920428 FT 28 APR 92 / International Company News: Walt Disney turns in 29% climb SHARPLY higher film and consumer products earnings helped boost second-quarter net profits by 29 per cent to Dollars 164m at Walt Disney, the movie-to-theme parks group. The company's revenues in the fiscal quarter to end-March were 15 per cent higher at Dollars 1.66bn, while earnings per share stood at 31 cents, against 24 cents a year before. Mr Michael Eisner, chairman, said earnings were helped by the strength of animated film and television products and related character merchandise. The record US box office proceeds of Beauty and the Beast, the animated feature, were especially helpful in the three-month period. Operating income for the film division jumped by 85 per cent to Dollars 85.4m in the quarter, on revenues up by 5 per cent to Dollars 641m. The consumer products division achieved a 33 per cent rise in operating income, to Dollars 73.1m. Revenues were 55 per cent higher at Dollars 240m. The theme parks and resorts division improved from its depressed level of a year ago, at the height of the Gulf war. Operating profits were Dollars 139.1m, up by 6 per cent. Revenues increased by 15 per cent to Dollars 774.1m.
