920706 FT 06 JUL 92 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System The lira dropped on Friday to 760.25 per D-Mark, which took it to within five lire of its floor against the German currency on the European Monetary System grid. It closed on Friday at 757.5 per D-Mark. Sterling was dragged down by the D-Mark's gain against the high-yielding currencies, to close at DM2.8950. Currencies, Page 27 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the EMS's narrow 2.25 per cent fluctuation band. In practice, currencies in the EMS narrow band cannot rise more than 2.25 per cent from the weakest currency in that part of the system. Sterling and the Spanish peseta operate with 6 per cent fluctuation bands.
