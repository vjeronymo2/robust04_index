Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Edict No. 19 of the president of the Russian Federation, signed by Russian Federation President B. Yeltsin and dated Moscow, The Kremlin, 5 January 1994: "On Ratifying the Statute of the Federal Counterintelligence Service of the Russian Federation"] [Text] In connection with the formation of the Federal Counterintelligence Service [FCS] of the Russian Federation, I decree: 1. To ratify the appended Statute of the FCS of the Russian Federation. 2. To permit the director of the FCS of the Russian Federation to have five deputy directors of the FCS of the Russian Federation, including one first deputy director and one deputy director -- chief of the FCS of the Russian Federation Directorate for Moscow City and Moscow Oblast. A collegium comprising 11 persons is to be formed as part of the FCS of the Russian Federation. The numerical strength of officials in the management apparatus of the FCS of the Russian Federation is to be set at 1,500. 3. To set the numerical strength of military and civilian personnel of Russian Federation counterintelligence organs at 75,000 (not including the numerical strength of workers at scientific research and military medical subdivisions and institutions or personnel employed on the utilization, protection, and servicing of service buildings and premises). 5. To permit the director of the FCS of the Russian Federation to introduce amendments to the structure of the FCS of the Russian Federation taking into account edicts and directives of the president of the Russian Federation within the limits of the set numerical strength of military and civilian personnel and the budget appropriations assigned to it. 5. The FCS of the Russian Federation is to bring departmental normative acts in line with the Statute ratified by this edict. 6. Edict No. 42 of the president of the Russian Federation of 24 January 1992 "On Forming the Russian Federation Ministry of Security" and subsequent amendments thereto are hereby declared no longer in force. 7. This edict comes into force from the moment of its signing. [Signed] Russian Federation President B. Yeltsin [Dated] Moscow, The Kremlin, 5 January 1994
