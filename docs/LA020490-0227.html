February 4, 1990, Sunday, Orange County Edition

COLLEGE ROUNDUP;

CHAPMAN FALLS VICTIM TO COLD SPELLS

Cal State Dominguez Hills pulled away in the second half to defeat Chapman, 56-47, in a California Collegiate Athletic Assn. men's basketball game Saturday night in Hutton Center.

The loss was the fifth in six games for Chapman, which dropped to 10-11 and 2-6 in the CCAA.

Dominguez Hills is 8-13, 4-4.

Frantz Reyes made a jump shot to give Chapman a 22-21 lead one minute 10 seconds into the second half, but the Panthers failed to score a point over the next 7:40. Chris Martin made a three-pointer to bring Chapman within five points at 30-25, but the Panthers then went another five minutes without scoring.

Reyes made two free throws with 5:52 remaining, but Chapman was behind, 43-28. The Panthers made a mini-run in the game's final minutes, with Martin and Kevin Stafford making some three-pointers. Martin, who led Chapman with 16 points, had four three-pointers in the second half.

Vincent Washington scored 16 for Dominguez Hills, and Brian Johnson held Chapman's leading scorer, Roger Middleton, to 11 points -- only one of which came in the second half. MARTIN BECK
