Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and Poland have called for further strengthening economic cooperation in the wake of changes in both the countries. Speaking at a banquet hosted in honor of the visiting Polish president, Mr. Lech Walesa, in New Delhi last night, President Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma, said a sense of identity and affinity marks the multi-dimensional growth of ties between the two countries. Describing Mr. Walesa's personal contribution to Polish revolution as significant, Dr. Sharma said the dramatic changes in Poland over the last decade acted as an important catalyst in ending the Cold War and fundamentally transforming international relations. Reciprocating the sentiments, Mr. Walesa lauded India's efforts toward ending the arms race and eliminating the threat of nuclear warfare. Turning to the economic front, Mr Walesa said there is great scope for increasing trade between the two countries and the potential in both the countries should be fully exploited. The Polish president also met the vice president, Mr. K.R. Narayanan, and stressed the need to project Mahatma Gandhi's ideas and values for shaping the new world order.
