941214 FT 14 DEC 94 / Fall in corporate liquidity to 142% The liquidity of large UK companies worsened in the third quarter of 1994, the first deterioration in almost two years. Central Statistical Office figures show that the liquidity of all large companies - measured by the ratio of total current assets to total current liabilities - fell from 147 per cent in the second quarter to 142 per cent in the third. Mr David Owen, UK economist at Kleinwort Benson, said: 'It is the first indication that companies are happier with their balance sheets and will now attempt to take on debt to finance expansion.'
