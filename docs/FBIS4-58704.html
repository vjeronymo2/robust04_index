BFN [Telephone interview with Ghassan al-Khatib, member of the Palestinian delegation to the Middle East peace talks and professor at Bi'r Zayt University, in Jerusalem, by Fayiz al-Miliji in Cairo; date not given; from the "Dialogue From Afar" program -- recorded] [Text] [Al-Miliji] Good morning Mr. Ghassan al-Khatib. We welcome you to our talk show. [Al-Khatib] Good morning and thank you. [Al-Miliji] To start with, could you fill us in on the reactions to the signing of the agreement on starting to implement autonomy in Gaza and Jericho? [Al-Khatib] The reaction is the opposite of what was expected. The Palestinian people in the occupied territories received the signing of this agreement and the celebrations that went with it lukewarmly and cautiously, quite opposite to their reception of the Oslo agreement and the celebrations following its signing in Washington. The reason is that the repeated Israeli stalling in implementing the agreements has created indifference among the Palestinian people. Because of the Israeli stalling the Palestinians have lost faith that such agreements will actually be implemented. The Palestinian man in the street is saying that he will believe these things only after they are implemented, because he has seen many things signed and heard about many agreements. None of these ever changed the course of his practical life. Therefore, the reaction to, or judgement of this agreement by the Palestinians here has been put on hold until after the Palestinian people see the actual implementation or effects of this agreement on their lives. Then, the whole situation will be different. [Al-Miliji] The agreement that was signed the day before yesterday still has some pending matters that have not been agreed upon. Do you think the Palestinian people are worried about these points? [Al-Khatib] Frankly speaking, the Palestinian people are worried about a lot of things. First, they are worried because there are some matters that have been put off. Our people have gone through some difficult times and were promised certain changes that never happened. [Al-Miliji] Although you have always shown concern for the Palestinian people, do you not see any reasons for optimism now, especially since the Palestinian and Israeli sides have adopted the slogan "Let there be peace"? [Al-Khatib] Yes. There are certain things that should make the Palestinians optimistic. But this optimism is hedged with caution and apprehension. For example, the possibility of allowing the Palestinian leadership, police, and the army--that is the Palestinian Liberation Army--to enter the Gaza Strip and Jericho are great sources of hope and optimism for the Palestinian masses. In addition, the possibility of releasing or the majority of the Palestinian detainees, if not all of them, is also a source of hope and optimism for the Palestinian people. However, so far the Palestinian people have not seen any practical results despite the large number of agreements and hopes, so they have mixed feelings of optimism, expectation, and anticipation to see what the practical changes will be. After that, the government can be established. [Al-Miliji] Mr. Ghassan al-Khatib, today is the third day since the agreement was signed. In other words, the it has not been very long. Now, everyone is waiting for the Israeli forces to withdraw from Gaza and Jericho and the Palestinian police to enter. Does this not provide grounds for optimism rather than concern and tension? [Al-Khatib] Yes, but you have to remember that there have been agreements in the past and Israel stalled on implementing them. It also stalled on the negotiations over implementation. It created obstacles everywhere, thus hindering the practical implementation of these agreements. Various dates were agreed upon that were not respected by Israel, who always claimed that deadlines were not sacred. Thus, our people are afraid to be overly optimistic in order to avoid disappointment like after the Madrid conference and the signing of the Oslo agreement, etc. However, there is anticipation and hope. After the Palestinian leadership and police enter the territory, after some changes start taking place, and as soon as the Israeli withdrawal starts, I expect the mood of the Palestinian people to shift. [Al-Miliji] Mr. al-Khatib, have the Israeli forces started withdrawing from Gaza and Jericho? [Al-Khatib] The Israeli forces have vacated a few locations in Gaza City, or the Gaza Strip. This has left a positive impression with the Palestinian people. The people understand clearly that things take time and that complications may crop up. Therefore, they are waiting to see how the agreement will be implemented. They will take their stands based to that. [Al-Miliji] In your capacity as a leading figure of the Palestinian delegation to the peace talks, could you tell us when the Palestinian police will enter? [Al-Khatib] A group of 17 high-ranking Palestinian officers have already entered the Gaza Strip at Rafah crossing. They have begun a series of meetings with ranking Israeli army and police officers to put the final touches on the process of handing over the buildings and security responsibilities to the Palestinian police. After these meetings, this group will submit recommendations on the necessary time period for handing over responsibilities. Based on their recommendations, the date for the Palestinian police's entry will be set. [Al-Miliji] Our last question is: How are the Palestinian people preparing themselves to receive Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat when he enters the occupied territories for the first time since Palestinian autonomy began? [Al-Khatib] Actually, President 'Arafat is greatly respected and supported in the occupied territories. I notice that the Palestinian people are anxiously awaiting the return of the Palestinian leadership headed by brother Yasir 'Arafat, especially since the Palestinian people are expecting his return to spread a sense of security and stability after the difficult conditions we have gone through. Of course, when brother Abu-'Ammar [Yasir 'Arafat] returns he will be accompanied by a large number of Palestinian people, be they leaders or otherwise, who are living abroad. The people here are awaiting this very anxiously. Therefore, I believe that the return of the Palestinian leadership will soon have a positive effect on the mood of the Palestinian people in the occupied territories. [Al-Miliji] At the end of this dialogue, we thank our guest Mr. Ghassan al-Khatib, member of the Palestinian delegation to the peace talks and professor at Bi'r Zayt University, who spoke to us from Jerusalem. Thank you Mr. Ghassan. [Al-Khatib] Thank you.
