Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 26 Feb (MENA)--Zuhdi al-Qidrah, Fatah representative in Cairo, has told MENA that the number of Palestinians martyred by the Israeli massacre in the Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron and the subsequent incidents in the rest of occupied territories has risen to 91 with more than 500 others wounded. Al-Qidrah explained that 79 martyrs and 256 wounded fell in the Ibrahimi Mosque and in the city of Hebron alone as a result of bullets fired by the Israeli occupation forces against the Palestinian masses who came out to rescue their kinsfolk from the massacre. He added that six martyrs fell in Gaza and 70 were wounded, 65 were injured in the al-Aqsa Mosque area in Jerusalem, one martyr fell in Nabulus and several were wounded, one martyr fell in Tulkarm and several were wounded, and several people were wounded in Ramallah and Jericho. The Palestinian official added that one person was martyred in Bethlehem and 12 were wounded, most of them natives of Bayt Jala, a town with a Christian majority. In the Qalandiya township three martyrs fell and several people were wounded. Al-Qidrah announced that he conveyed messages today from Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat to al-Azhar Imam Shaykh Jad-al- Haqq 'Ali Jad-al-Haqq, Awqaf Minister Muhammad 'Ali Mahjub, and His Holiness Coptic Pope Shanudah III explaining to them the details of yesterday morning's massacre at the Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron, which later spread to the rest of the occupied territories. MENA has learned that His Eminence Al-Azhar Imam Shaykh Jad-al- Haqq 'Ali Jad-al-Haqq informed al-Qidrah that prayers for the martyrs' souls will be said in Egypt's mosques. Pope Shanudah II told the Palestinian envoy that masses will be celebrated for their souls in Egypt's churches tomorrow, Sunday.
