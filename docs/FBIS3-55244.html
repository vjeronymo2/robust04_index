Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kazakhstan relies on sweeping aid by international financial agencies to be able to carry out its program of economic reforms, Premier Sergey Tereschenko told Western ambassadors in Almaty on Tuesday. He hopes that European governments will help speed up financial aid to Kazakhstan which carries out economic reforms and a policy of tight crediting and finances relying on the IMF's assistance in luring western investment there. The Prime Minister put the number of joint ventures operating in Kazakhstan at more than 1,100. He also said the IMF was to provide $17 Mn [million] as part of its systematic credits covering the period from July 1992 to June 1994.
