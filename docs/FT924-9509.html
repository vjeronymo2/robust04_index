921105 FT 05 NOV 92 / Arts: Today's Television One of the good things about Waiting for God is that, like the sixties series 'Till Death Us Do Part' and 'Steptoe And Son', it rarely takes the easy option. It manages to generate laughter while, and even by, dealing with big and difficult topics. Recently Diana and Tom, two of the more headstrong inmates of Bayview ('home for the nearly dead') have started sleeping together, causing deep distaste and outrage among younger people. Today Diana falls ill, decides she is dying, and asks Tom to get sleeping pills to help her take matters into her own hands. Is euthanasia funny? If any programme can make it so this one will (8.30 BBC1). Rumpole Of The Bailey (9.00 ITV) returned last week with a timely story about over-keen social workers creating child sex abuse cases by putting leading questions to children. This week the character who finds plot-lines overlapping at home and at work is Mr Justice Featherstone (Peter Bowles). The Channel 4 current affairs series Critical Eye reports on the case of three ex-members of the Ulster Defence Regiment whose convictions for murder were quashed on appeal earlier this year (9.00).
