March 12, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

UCLA STRETCHES ITS WINNING STREAK TO 40 DUAL MEETS

UCLA extended its winning streak to 40 consecutive track and field dual meets by defeating Azusa Pacific, 97-53, and Cal State Northridge, 107-47, Saturday at Drake Stadium.

CS Northridge beat Azusa Pacific, 88-61, while the Bruins recorded 98 points in triangular scoring, to 50 points for Azusa Pacific and 43 for CS Northridge.

UCLA's women's team defeated CS Northridge, 96-40; California, 101-32, and Azusa Pacific, 120-7. In triangular scoring, the Bruins had 96 points, with Cal finishing second with 38; CS Northridge was next with 34, and Azusa Pacific scored 2 points.

Kevin Williams of UCLA won the men's 100 meters in 10.54 seconds and anchored the Bruins' 400-meter relay team, which won in 40.29.
