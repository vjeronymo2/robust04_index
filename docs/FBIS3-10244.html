Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 1 Mar (EFE) -- Mines and Energy Minister Guido Nule Amin said today Colombia does not expect "reprisals" from Cuban exiles or the United States for the announced sale of oil to Cuba. The official, who announced last week the sale of between 15,000 and 20,000 barrels of oil to Cuba per day, said the oil exports will begin in mid-1994. "Cuba's main partners are Mexico and Canada, and it has been no cause for problems with the United States," Nule Amin added. The energy and mines minister said he does not see how Colombia "could have problems" for selling crude to the island. He explained Colombia will sell that quantity of oil, "barely one third of the (Cuban) demand for oil." He said the method of payment of these "direct" sales will be "at international prices, and the Cubans will pay with foreign exchange." Meanwhile, it was reported in Santa Fe de Bogota Cuban exiles in Miami would allegedly boycott Colombian products.
