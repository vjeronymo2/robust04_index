Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd arrived in Athens at 1900 tonight on a two-day working visit. In an arrival statement, Hurd described the six-month term of the Greek European Union [EU] presidency as significant and added that he will discuss some issues with Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou, Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias, and Defense Minister Yerasimos Arsenis. Answering a question on Skopje [The former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM], Hurd said a way must be found for the dialogue to resume. Asked about the Greek sanctions against Skopje and the possibility that they might be lifted, the British foreign secretary described Skopje as pregnable, adding that it must not be put in danger. Hurd will also meet the opposition leader and other personalities.
