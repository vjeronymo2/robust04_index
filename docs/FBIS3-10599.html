Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] In a meeting chaired by Candido Palmero Hernandez, first secretary of the Cuban Communist Party in Havana, and Agriculture Minister Alfredo Jordan Morales, special control and protection measures were adopted in Havana Province for the 1994 Agro-94 [1994 agricultural harvest] campaign. Approximately 1.5 million quintals of tubers and vegetables will be picked this month in Havana Province, including a significant quantity of potatoes, while in April some 2 million quintals of agricultural products are expected to be harvested. These measures were adopted to prevent these agricultural products from reaching other destinations. These measures include the control of the fleet of trucks that will participate in the Agro-94 campaign, preventing them from taking other than assigned routes. These measures will not be limited to the outskirts of Havana, but will be extended to all of the capital's distribution and collection areas up to (La Placita). During the meeting, Palmero Hernandez said that black market proliferation must be stopped. He added that certain elements are dedicated to profiting at the expense of the people.
