BFN [Text] Cairo, April 7 (AFP)--The PLO expressed its condolences Thursday to the families of the seven Israelis killed when a teenage Palestinian militant rammed his explosive-packed car into a bus in the northern Israeli town of Afula. An official PLO spokesman said in a statement to AFP: "The PLO presents its condolences to the families of the victims (of the attack)," which also left 45 wounded. "The PLO hopes that all concerned parties will assume their responsibility to safeguard the peace process and achieve its aims, to ensure a just and equal solution between the Palestinian and Israeli peoples," the statement said. The United States called on PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat Wednesday to condemn the Afula attack, carried out by a 19-year-old member of the Islamic Resistance Movement HAMAS, which is opposed to the peace process.
