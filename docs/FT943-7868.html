940820 FT 20 AUG 94 / UK Company News: Benson lower after exceptionals Exceptional charges left Benson Group, the engineering group, with lower pre-tax profits of Pounds 233,000 for the year to May 31, against Pounds 1.92m. However, before allowing for exceptionals of Pounds 2m profits showed a 16 per cent rise. The exceptional charges related to the cost of acquisition and integration of two companies and the disposal of Wolverhampton Pressings. Turnover increased by 10 per cent from Pounds 25.8m to Pounds 28.3m, of which acquisitions contributed Pounds 1.27m. Operating profits rose 13 per cent to Pounds 2.76m (Pounds 2.45m) with operating margins improving from 9.5 per cent to 9.8 per cent. There was a cash inflow during the year of Pounds 2.34m (Pounds 655,000). With Pounds 1m being spent on capital investment and Pounds 1.6m on acquisitions total debt at the year end was higher at Pounds 5.57m against Pounds 4.51m, however it was lower than at the half year. Mr Richard Phillips, chairman, said that as the economic background improved, growth could be expected in the group's markets. The products division reported a strong improvement with the environmental side showing significant growth in turnover and profits. Higher automotive volumes assisted the results for the components division where turnover, adjusted for the effect of the Wolverhampton Pressings disposal, rose by 12 per cent. Earnings per share fell from 1.45p to 0.18p and the dividend is 0.33p (0.3p) with a recommended final of 0.23p. The shares rose 1 1/2p to 16p.
