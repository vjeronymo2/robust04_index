930726 FT 26 JUL 93 / Observer: Looking up Eyes blearied by peering for signs of recovery in standard macro-economic data may sparkle anew at a micro-indicator of a spectacular up-turn soon to come. The indicator is the micro-miniskirt displayed by Karl Lagerfeld, for Chanel, at the Paris couture shows for autumn/winter 1993. What's more Lagerfeld is by no means alone in discarding the recessionary low hemline. Gianni Versace, for example, has gone for mid-thigh baby doll nighties. Indeed, not since the optimistic 1960s have shorter skirts appeared.
