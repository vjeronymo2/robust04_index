BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 20 (KCNA) -- The South Korean National Committee of Struggle for the Reinstatement of the Detained, wanted and dismissed workers (Chonhaetu), an organization embracing workers of some 40 businesses, called for rising general turnout in the struggle for the reinstatement of dismissed workers at a rally held with the attendance of some 1,000 workers, students and citizens, according to a South Korean newspaper report. It criticized the Kim Yong-sam regime for turning a deaf ear to voices of the workers across the country calling for reinstatement and the guarantee of their right to live. "The reinstatement of the dismissed workers is possible only when a mass struggle is waged," it said, and stressed: "Let all of us devote our efforts, lives and energy to the struggle for their reinstatement." It declared that the dismissed workers in all parts of South Korea would enter into a tent sit-in and a hunger strike for an indefinite period and wage a death-defying battle till their demands have been met.
