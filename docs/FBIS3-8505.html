Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Belgrade, 10 Mar (TANJUG)--At its session today presided over by Yugoslav Prime Minister Radoje Kontic the federal government examined a report on spring sowing and adopted a decision on protected prices of basic agricultural products, the Federal Ministry of Information announced in a statement. [passage omitted] The government also examined and endorsed a letter which the federal prime minister is to send to UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali regarding the extension of the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] mandate in the protected zones on the territory of the former Yugoslavia. It has been concluded that the presence of the UN peace forces in the protected zones is an important factor for the creation of the necessary conditions for reaching a political solution to the problem. The government was also informed about actions taken by the Federal Ministry of Foreign Affairs in connection with a decision of the German authorities to expel FRY [Federal Republic of Yugoslavia] citizens whose status in Germany has not been legalized. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs has been tasked with continuing and intensifying contacts with the relevant German authorities so that the full protection of the rights and interests of all FRY citizens in Germany may be secured, with particular insistence on the full application of the current international conventions and practice, the statement says. [passage omitted] Milenko Perovic, hitherto the Montenegrin president's secretary general, has been appointed chairman of the Federal Council of Magistrates. New Deputy Prime Minister Uros Klikovac has been appointed chairman of the Federal Commission for the Political and Legal System, the Federal Ministry of Information statement concludes.
