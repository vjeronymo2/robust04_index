910510 FT 10 MAY 91 / World Stock Markets (America): Futures-related demand lifts Dow Wall Street SEVERAL rounds of futures-related programme buying fuelled a late-afternoon rally on Wall Street yesterday as US equities shrugged off paltry demand at the 30-year bond auction and moved broadly higher, writes Karen Zagor in New York. At the finish the Dow Jones Industrial Average was 40.25 stronger at 2,971.15, its highest level since standing at 2,999.26 on April 18. The Standard & Poor's 500 climbed 4.74 to 383.25. Rises outpaced falls by 1,113 to 495 after a moderate volume of 180.2m shares, but an increase from the previous day's 157m. The Dow, which has been extremely sensitive to the US Treasury's quarterly refunding auctions this week, overcame surprisingly weak demand at the afternoon sale of Dollars 11.75bn in 30-year notes. Investors were encouraged by the firmness of the bond market, where the treasury's benchmark 30-year ent in late trading. Technology issues contributed to the stock market gains, with IBM rising Dollars 2 to Dollars 105 3/4 . Mobil Oil, which said it will boost capital spending to Dollars 5bn from Dollars 4.4bn, gained Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 69 5/8 . Exxon added Dollars 1 3/8 at Dollars 59 5/8 . Occidental Petroleum, one of the most active New York SE issues for a second consecutive day following an agreement to sell its North Sea oil interests for Dollars 1.35bn, climbed Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 21 1/8 . Ford Motor firmed Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 32 7/8 . At the company's annual meeting the chairman said he could not rule out further lay-offs in the US. Chrysler shed Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 13 but General Motors advanced Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 38 3/8 . Wal-Mart moved ahead Dollars 2 1/8 to Dollars 42 after unveiling a 21 per cent increase in first-quarter net income to Dollars 307m on sales up 37 per cent to Dollars 9.28bn. The secondary market also took part in yesterday's rally, with the Nasdaq composite climbing 5.30 to 497.81. Intel was one of the most actively traded over-the-counter issues, adding Dollars 3 at Dollars 54 1/2 on expectations that it will make a line of personal computers to be sold by Digital Equipment. The latter rose Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 66 1/8 on the NYSE. Bizmart forged ahead Dollars 1 3/4 to Dollars 15 in active trading after the company said it is involved in talks about a possible takeover bid. Immunex, which rose Dollars 1 7/8 to Dollars 42 3/4 , expects to break even in 1991 with the help of sales of Leukine, its GM-CSF white blood cell stimulant product. Canada THE SHARP Wall Street rise spurred Toronto forward in further moderate trading. The composite index closed 22.5 ahead at 3,504.5. Advances led declines by 314 to 221 after volume of 20.8m shares. Memotec Data put on CDollars 3/8 to CDollars 9 in spite of turmoil surrounding the election of its board at next week's annual meeting. Gordon Capital Corp has issued a proxy circular in an attempt to replace three nominees to Memotec's board, including the chairman and the president and chief executive, with three Gordon Capital nominees. Quadra Logic said clinical trials suggest that its Photofrin anti-cancer drug can delay the occurrence of tumours in bladder cancer cases. The shares added CDollars 3/8 at CDollars 10 7/8 after rising CDollars 1 5/8 on Wednesday.
