Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih stated, following his meeting today with Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa, that an agreement had been reached with him on the need to make greater efforts to correct the course of relations between Sudan and Egypt, so as to promote the destiny of the peoples of the Nile Valley. Concerning attempts to separate southern Sudan from the north, the Sudanese foreign minister said that those attempts would be fruitless. He added that Sudan would absolutely not allow any attempt to dismantle its unity. He said that Egypt had declared, strongly and frankly, its support for Sudan, as it was aware of the dangers of disintegration and fragmentation, not only with regard to Sudan itself, but in the entire region as well. Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih expressed his appreciation at Egypt's strategic stand [word indistinct], out of its concern to preserve the unity of Sudan, as the security of Sudan and Egypt were closely linked. For his part Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa said that he had spoken with Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih concerning issues of interest to the countries and the future of relations between them, besides the subjects discussed by the Arab League Council. Mr. 'Amr Musa stressed Egypt's stand on southern Sudan: That it would not support any attempt for the separation of southern Sudan.
