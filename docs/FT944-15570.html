941018 FT 18 OCT 94 / Grants vital in tipping the investment balance Samsung's new electronics complex underlines the fierce competition between areas of Europe for investments, and the substantial incentives offered to tip the balance. In the summer the rift between UK government departments over the DTI aid available for inward investment was exposed in a leaked letter in which Mr Michael Portillo, then chief secretary to the Treasury, criticised the use of grants to deal with regional employment imbalances. But Mr Tim Eggar, trade minister, said on BBC Radio 4 yesterday: 'We have got to live in the real world, and the real world is that major investment decisions of this kind inevitably involve a level of government assistance.' Samsung could, however, have been given more money had it gone to Spain, the other shortlisted location for the project. The Northern Development Company was told informally that more was on offer in Spain than in the UK. Samsung's choice of the north-east will have been influenced by its happy experience in the region, where it has expanded an investment at Billingham, Cleveland. But nobody in the economic development field doubts the important of cash incentives. At Wynyard, a development area, Samsung has been offered Pounds 58m in regional selective assistance, on the basis of a Pounds 450m project. If, as hoped locally, the investment goes up to Pounds 600m, applications for further assistance could be made. In addition, government-backed developer English Partnerships is putting in Pounds 9m and Cleveland County Council and Teesside Training and Enterprise Council Pounds 5m each. Other council aid and European funds have produced an additional Pounds 1.85m. As late as last Friday the Northern Development Company did not know if it had secured the project.
