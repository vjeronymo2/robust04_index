March 16, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

CHOOSING TO LIVE MORE SIMPLY

The dinosaurs were the dominant species on this planet for over 160 million years. They vanished virtually without a trace. Compared to that vast span of time, Homo sapiens' sojourn on Earth is less than the blink of an eye.

And yet in the infinitesimal millisecond of our pitiful existence, we have brought our world to the brink of destruction.

Our technological evolution has surpassed our social evolution. We cannot destroy nature. But what we are doing is destroying ourselves. The planet will ultimately prevail. Only another species will be in the driver's seat. From a purely selfish viewpoint then, it's time to think of ourselves as "one planet -- one species." Then again, considering how we've botched things up, maybe it's time to give the job to someone else.

SAM MINSKY

Santa Monica
