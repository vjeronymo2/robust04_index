BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Paraguay monitored through 9 June. Paraguayan Central Bank, BCP, sources have reported that the domestic public debt, including capital and interests, totaled 1,364,923,000,000 guaranies by 31 December 1993. According to BCP records the domestic public debt by 31 December 1993, involving loans in guaranies and dollars, is broken down as follows: central government (basically the Finance Ministry): 960.282 billion guaranies; decentralized organizations: 1.757 billion guaranies; municipalities: 1.169 billion guaranies; and public enterprises, 401.715 billion guaranies. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 3 Jun 94 p 20) According to the Technical Planning Secretariat, a $264.7 million trade balance deficit was reported during the first four months. During this period exports totaled $265.3 million, while imports totaled $530 million. Cotton exports declined by 41.4 percent, and meat exports declined by 12.2 percent. Soybean exports grew 35 percent, coffee exports grew 77 percent, and timber exports grew 6.7 percent. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 8 Jun 94 p 14)
