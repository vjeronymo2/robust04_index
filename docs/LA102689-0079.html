October 26, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

SAN GABRIEL VALLEY DIGEST: PASADENA;

ASSURING BLACK CATS' SAFETY

Two black kittens and two black cats now in the Pasadena Humane Society will have to wait until after Nov. 1 to find a home because of a moratorium placed on the adoption of black cats until after Halloween.

The temporary ban on adoptions of black cats -- in effect this year from Oct. 16 to Nov. 1 -- began eight years ago out of a desire to protect the animals from Halloween party-goers or cult members.

The Humane Society advises owners to keep all pets indoors during the holiday. Owners are also advised to refrain from giving animals candy and sweets.

A similar moratorium on the adoption of all animals is also put into effect in mid-December to avoid mistreatment of animals bought as Christmas gifts.
