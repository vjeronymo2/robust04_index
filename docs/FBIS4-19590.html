Na": "Paris Is Willing To Help Train the Palestinian Police"] [Text] If the Palestinians are to have the 9,000 policemen who are to guarantee law and order in Gaza and Jericho when Palestinian autonomy comes into force, they must have the technical and financial means of doing so, and this is not yet the case. So, to speed things up, Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh, one of Yasir 'Arafat's advisers, came to Paris on Thursday, 14 April, to have talks with the foreign minister. Alain Juppe told him that France is willing to contribute to the training, by the national gendarmerie, of police officers and junior officers, the bulk of whose men are already being trained in Jordan, Egypt, and Britain. Paris, according to a Quai d'Orsay [Foreign Ministry] spokesman, will also "study" the request for communications equipment submitted by the PLO. But the Palestinians also need money to pay wages. On Monday, 18 April, Paris will ask its European Union partners to use some of the ECU 50 million in community aid to the Palestinians for the benefit of the police. After Paris, Mr. 'Abd-al-Rabbuh, who is being accompanied by a Norwegian special envoy (Oslo chairs the committee for international assistance to the Palestinian police), is due to visit Brussels, Bonn, London, Tokyo, Washington,
