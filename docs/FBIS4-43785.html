BFN [Excerpt] Brussels, June 22 (AFP) -- Russian Prime Minister Andrey Kozyrev arrived here Wednesday [22 June] to sign Russia up to NATO's Partnership for Peace programme of military cooperation, thereby greatly expanding its scope. Earlier, Kozyrev met U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher in the Belgian capital for talks reportedly concerning a peace plan for Bosnia-Herzegovina and the nuclear inspection crisis involving North Korea.
