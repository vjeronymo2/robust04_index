BFN [Text] Belgrade, 14 Jun (TANJUG) -- The situation is under control and there is no danger for the population, the information service of the Yugoslav Army's General Staff reported after a fire broke out which caused an explosion in the Lisicji Potok army ammunition depot Monday evening. The statement sent to TANJUG early this morning says: ``During a storm over Belgrade, at around 2330 last night, lightning hit a depot containing explosives for army drills. ``Fire spread to the depot building, setting off the explosives. No casualties have been registered so far. ``The situation is under control and there is no danger of the explosions spreading. Residential and other buildings and the population are not threatened. "Top-ranking officers of the Yugoslav Army's General Staff are on the spot, headed by [Chief of the General Staff] General Perisic. During the night the scene of the incident was inspected by the head of the Belgrade local authorities, Nebojsa Covic," the statement issued by the information service of the Yugoslav Army's General Staff says.
