BFN [Text] Noumea, June 18 (AFP) -- Fijian President Ratu Sir Kamisese Mara arrived Saturday in New Caledonia for a five-day official visit at the invitation of the French Government. The Fijian leader, who received military honors after arriving from the French territory of Wallis and Futuna, called for closer relations with New Caledonia. He stressed that his country had no objections to French policy in the South Pacific, particularly since the start of the moratorium on nuclear testing in French Polynesia. The president was to meet Saturday with members of the Fijian community in New Caledonia. On Monday he will visit the headquarters of the Commission of the South Pacific and the Agency for the Development of Kanak Culture, before meeting with representatives of New Caledonian political parties and institutions. He is scheduled to leave the territory on Wednesday.
