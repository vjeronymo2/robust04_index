940804 FT 04 AUG 94 / World Trade News: Canadians to expand Budapest airport Huang & Danczkay Properties, a Toronto-based developer, has won a contract worth about CDollars 200m (Dollars 145m) to modernise the main airport at Budapest, the Hungarian capital, writes Bernard Simon in Toronto. One of the airport's two terminals will be rebuilt, doubling capacity to about 6m passengers a year. Following construction, which is due to start this autumn, a Huang & Danczkay subsidiary will operate the two terminals for 12 years and then turn them over to the Hungarian government.
