Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Iraq has protested again over the continued violations of its airspace by U.S. aircraft for purposes of surveillance, provocation, and dropping of flares. In a letter to UN Secretary General Butrus Ghali, Iraq noted that from 10-20 January U.S. aircraft continued to violate Iraqi airspace. They carried out 738 air sorties covering large areas of northern and southern Iraq. On 11 January, a U.S. TR-1 reconnaissance plane violated Iraqi airspace. Afterward, it flew in the direction of Saudi airspace. On 3 January [as heard], the same plane violated Iraqi airspace. It mainly overflew the al-Basrah, al-Nasiriyah, al-'Amarah, southern al-Diwaniyah, and southern al-Najaf areas. Afterward, it flew in the direction of Saudi airspace. The letter indicates that on 18 January, two U.S. aircraft dropped two flares 10 km south of Alqosh in Ninawa governorate. An enemy air formation dropped a flare south of Alqosh hamlet in Ninawa governorate. Another formation dropped a flare 10 km south of the Alqosh area in Ninawa governorate.
