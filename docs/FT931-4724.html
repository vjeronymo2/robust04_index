930310 FT 10 MAR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Aitken promises dockyard decision A DECISION on which of the two competing British dockyards, Rosyth or Devonport, is to win the vital contract to refit Britain's nuclear submarine fleet will be made 'as soon as humanly possible' after the Easter recess, the government said yesterday. Mr Jonathan Aitken, defence procurement minister, also told MPs he was confident there would be 'no significant variation' in the cost to Britain of obtaining Trident strategic missiles for the submarines as a result of US defence cuts. His comment followed a report by the British American Security Information Council, an independent lobby group, that the extra cost of Trident missiles remaining to be paid for by the UK could be more than Pounds 200m.
