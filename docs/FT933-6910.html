930825 FT 25 AUG 93 / People: Young quits ITN for BBC Mark Young, for two years head of business affairs at ITN, is to follow his former boss Bob Phillis, now BBC deputy director, to the BBC. He starts next Tuesday as chief assistant to the deputy dg - who was poached by the BBC from his job as chief executive of ITN in January. Young will provide back-up across the whole range of Phillis's responsibilities, which include the managing directorship of the BBC World Service and co-ordinating the BBC's international commercial and resource activities. Young spent his time at ITN looking at new business opportunities and drawing up business plans. He was also part of the team which put together a London radio franchise application for a consortium in which ITN has 20 per cent. Before joining ITN he was a consultant in the media group at Coopers & Lybrand Deloitte.
