921111 FT 11 NOV 92 / Observer: Whitewash The CBI showed a curious logic when choosing the speakers for its annual conference yesterday. In the debate on corporate governance, the chief proponent of the CBI's case was Martin Taylor, vice-chairman of Hanson. Hanson's contribution to corporate governance is uncertain; it is run by two elderly peers, one of whom - Lord White - is not on the board at all. There then followed a debate on excellence in manufacturing, entitled 'competing with the world's best'. One of the main speakers was Fisons' boss, Cedric Scroggs. Fisons, it may be recalled, has had its profits slaughtered this year because it cannot manufacture some of its drugs to the standards required by the US health authorities.
