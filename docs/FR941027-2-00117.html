[Public Notice 2102] Shipping Coordinating Committee; Maritime Safety Committee and International Conference; Meeting The Shipping Coordinating Committee (SHC) will conduct an open meeting at 9:30 A.M, on Wednesday, November 30, 1994, in room 2415, at U.S. Coast Guard Headquarters, 2100 Second Street, SW., Washington, DC. The purpose of the meeting is to finalize preparations for the 64th Session of the Maritime Safety Committee (MSC&hyph;64), and associated bodies of the International Maritime Organization (IMO), which is scheduled for December 5&hyph;9, 1994 at the IMO Headquarters in London. At the meeting, papers received and the draft U.S. positions will be discussed. Among other things, the items of particular interest are: a. Technical assistance subprogram in maritime safety. b. Role of the human element in maritime casualties. c. Prevention and abatement of marine pollution incidents. d. Survey and Certification e. Existing ships' safety standards f. Reports of various subcommittees (Stability, Load Lines and Fishing Vessel Safety; Life-Saving, Search and Rescue; Containers and Cargoes; Fire Protection; Training and Watchkeeping; Safety of Navigation; and Bulk Chemicals.) Members of the public may attend this meeting up to the seating capacity of the room. Interested persons may seek information by writing to Mr. Gene F. Hammel, U.S. Coast Guard (G&hyph;CI), Room 2114, 2100 Second Street SW, Washington, DC 20593&hyph;0001 or by calling (202) 267&hyph;2280. Dated: October 18, 1994. Charles A.
