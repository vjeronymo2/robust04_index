BFN [Text] Implementing the party Central Committee's third plenum resolution on renovating and revamping the party, the nation has made encouraging achievements over the past two years in its efforts to train and develop newly-recruited party members. Almost 50,000 new party members were admitted during 1993, an increase of 38 percent over the previous year. Among these, 43 percent were admitted by grassroots party organizations, the highest percentage in several years. The An Giang provincial party organization has concentrated its efforts on developing party chapters in hamlets and housing clusters. In 1993, about 50 percent of the province's newly-recruited party members came from rural areas. Many came from 21 of the 26 hamlets that had never before admitted party members. Party organizations in Thai Binh and Hai Hung Provinces also admitted more members as a result of their efforts in holding refresher courses for cadres responsible for recruiting new party members. Party organizations in the Army have paid special attention to recruiting officers, non-commissioned officers, and national defense and professional personnel. In 1993, the Army admitted more than 8,000 new party members, 95 percent of them under the age of 30.
