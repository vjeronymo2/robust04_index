930225 FT 25 FEB 93 / Observer: In absentia The problem of how to get rid of a director when you can't find his body is one of those corporate governance technicalities few company secretaries have to face. No such mercy, however, for Sean Ryan, who keeps the minutes for Irish Life. He was refreshingly honest when Observer contacted him about the departure from his board of former finance director Brendan Hayes, whom nobody has apparently seen since last September. Because no death certificate has ever been issued, Irish Life has had to invoke a little-known rule by which, if board members fail to show up for six months at a stretch, they automatically vacate their directorship. 'Tragic though it is, Brendan had to be removed somehow,' explained Ryan.
