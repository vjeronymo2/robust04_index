920229 FT 29 FEB 92 / UK Company News: Chartered WestLB up Pounds 5m to Pounds 21.2m CHARTERED WestLB, the London-based international merchant bank jointly owned by Standard Chartered and WestLB, made profits of Pounds 21.2m in 1991, up from Pounds 16m. Mr Patrick Macdougall, chief executive, said that almost all activities had been profitable and the return on average capital employed had risen from 28 to 35.7 per cent. Assets advanced to Pounds 1.67bn (Pounds 1.24bn). Developing country finance remained the largest contributor to profits. Turnover in Latin American debt rose by 70 per cent to Dollars 16bn (Pounds 9.1bn). Chartered WestLB carries out a wide range of mergers and acquisitions advisory work in London, Germany, and eastern Europe. It is also preparing to launch a new securitisation product with SG Warburg, based on receivables including credit card debt. A forfaiting operation launched in 1991 was in profit and had concluded over 200 individual purchases in the year, totalling more than Dollars 500m and involving exports to 26 countries.
