BFN [Text] Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs, today received Veljko Knezevic, head of the Bureau of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia in Zagreb, the Croatian Ministry of Foreign Affairs has announced. Their talks focused on the current situation in the UN protected areas in Croatia as well as on the implementation of the Zagreb cease-fire agreement signed on 29 March. The Croatian foreign minister and his guest concluded that negotiations between the Croatian Government and the Serbs from the UN protected areas should continue as soon as possible. They also discussed the possibilities and conditions for the speedy normalization of relations between the Republic of Croatia and the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia. In their talks Minister Granic and envoy Knezevic discussed developments in former Yugoslavia. In this context it was said that negotiations between the Bosnia-Hercegovina federation and the Bosnian Serbs should start soon. The Croatian foreign minister emphasized that these negotiations and a cease-fire would enable talks on a definite territorial and constitutional arrangement for Bosnia-Herzegovina to be resumed as soon as possible. Minister Granic also pointed out that searching for a solution to the problem of UN protected areas in Croatia had to be undertaken simultaneously with the negotiations on Bosnia-Herzegovina.
