Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Berlin -- According to the Federation of German Trade Unions [DGB], the job deficit in the new laender amounted to 2.9 million at the end of last year. This "real job deficit" is 1.7 million higher than the number of unemployed given by the official statistics from Nuremberg, which put the number of unemployed people in the east at some 1.2 million at the end of 1993. The real statistics include those citizens who participate in job-promotion schemes offered by the labor offices or who commute to western Germany, the DGB noted in Berlin on Wednesday [2 February]. In Saxony, approximately 790,000 are "underemployed," in Saxony Anhalt 540,000, in Thuringia 510,000, in Brandenburg 455,000, in Mecklenburg Western Pomerania 365,000, and in the eastern part of Berlin some 275,000. The situation is most favorable in Dresden, where the job deficit was 23.6 percent at the end of 1993. Second came Leipzig (28.4 percent deficit), while, with an unemployment deficit of 41.3 percent, the Suhl region in Thuringia came at the bottom of the list.
