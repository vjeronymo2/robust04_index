Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- Tibor Sagat, physician by profession, was appointed new Slovak health minister today, at the proposal of the Center Bloc. Sagat was born on December 17, 1942, in Cierny Balog, central Slovakia. In 1966 he graduated from the Faculty of Pediatry at Prague's Charles University and since 1985 has been a pediatrics assistant professor. In 1967-1969 he worked as a district pediatrician in Polomec, west Slovakia. Until 1971 he was an assistant physician at Derer Hospital in Bratislava. In 1971-1987 Sagat was an expert assistant at the Institute for Advanced Education for Physicians and Pharmacists. In October 1988 he was named director of Derer Hospital in Bratislava and dismissed by former Health Minister Sobona on June 1, 1993. He continued to work in the hospital as chief physician of the pediatrics clinic.
