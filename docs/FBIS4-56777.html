BFN [Text] Havana, 5 May (PL) -- The positive signs following implementation of the Cuban budget during the first months of the year will make possible a reduction of the current deficit, which amounts to 1 billion pesos (the official exchange rate is one peso per dollar), should these indicators remain the same. This statement was included in an analysis Finance and Prices Minister Jose Luis Rodriguez presented to the recent meeting of the presidents of the Provincial Administration Councils [Consejos de Administracion Provinciales], the local authorities' administrative institutions. Rodriguez told the Chamber of Representatives, which opened its session in this city, efforts at the provincial levels are being made to contribute to the reduction of the national deficit, which was estimated at 4.2 billion pesos in late 1993. The minister said this encouraging sign is based on the implementation of the budget during the first part of the year. He added the budget includes more revenues, less expenditures, and a 6.1-percent reduction in the estimated budget deficit. Rodriguez also praised the aggressive efforts by the provincial and national cities regarding collections and payments because these efforts allowed for a reduction of 440 million pesos in accounts payable of the first quarter of 1994. The finance and prices minister said [words indistinct] to prevent past financial irregularities, for instance, the noncollection of debts, from happening again in the country.
