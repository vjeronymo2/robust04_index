931018 FT 18 OCT 93 / Delors fears EC is drifting towards break-up EUROPEAN Commission President Jacques Delors said yesterday he feared the European Community was drifting towards becoming a soulless free-trade area and ultimately a slow break-up, Reuter reports from Paris. Despite the ratification of the Maastricht treaty on European union, calling for a common foreign policy and a single currency by 1999, a gloomy Mr Delors saw no sign of a revival of the 12-nation Community. 'What I see is European construction drifting towards a free trade zone, that is to say an English-style Europe, which I reject,' Mr Delors, a French Socialist, said on Radio Luxembourg. 'If we do nothing, this drift will lead in 15 years to a break-up (of the EC).'
