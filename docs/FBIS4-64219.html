BFN [Text] Stuttgart (DPA) -- Daimler-Benz Chief Edzard Reuter and PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat have agreed on more intensive cooperation, above all in border-crossing infrastructure projects. This was announced by Reuter, who had invited 'Arafat for a one-day visit to Stuttgart, in a talk with journalists on Monday [2 May]. "We at Daimler-Beinz are convinced that the peace process in the Middle East can be consolidated through the contribution of European companies," Reuter said. The meeting with 'Arafat is a good starting point for the accelerated expansion of economic cooperation. Reuter promised his guest that a development study would be drawn up to solve the infrastructure and traffic problems in Palestine as a gift. The study will compiled by the corporation's subsidiary Donier Management Consulting. 'Arafat, who said that economic recovery is one of the most important bases for the pace process in the Middle East, accepted the gift with thanks. 'Arafat came to Stuttgart two days before the planned signing of the Gaza-Jericho autonomy agreement.
