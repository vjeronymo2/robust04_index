May 18, 1989, Thursday, Valley P.M. Final

LOCAL;

DEPUTIES KILL DRIVER IN LANCASTER WHO CHARGED THEM WITH A RAKE

Los Angeles County sheriff's deputies fatally shot an unidentified, apparently suicidal man who ignored repeated orders to stop charging at one of them with a rake early today in Lancaster, authorities said.

The man had cut his wrists with the three-pronged garden tool before he charged a deputy with it and was shot several times in the head and chest about 1:30 a.m. in the parking lot of Antelope Valley Medical Center, Sheriff's Lt. Dan Burt said.

The shooting occurred after a two-hour chase, Burt said. The man had sped away from a deputy who was trying to tell him to turn off the high beam on his headlights. During the chase, which ended in the hospital parking lot, the man ran at least 40 red lights and tried to ram a patrol car at one point, authorities said.

The names of the three deputies involved in the shooting were not immediately disclosed.
