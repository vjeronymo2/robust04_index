920929 FT 29 SEP 92 / Anti-British feeling causes job cuts A FORD union official yesterday claimed anti-British feeling had provoked Ford of Europe to announce 1,500 job losses at UK car plants, Lisa Wood writes. Mr Andy Richards, convener at Bridgend, south Wales, one of the Ford plants affected by the cuts, alleged that the management board of Ford of Europe was 'dominated by Germans using their position in a nationalistic way.' He said that job losses had only been announced for UK plants, not those in France, Germany and Spain. Ford said yesterday that only two members of the board were German, the rest were mainly Americans.
