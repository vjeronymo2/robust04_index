BFN [Text] On 12 April, Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa discussed with Muneaki Samejima, deputy chief secretary of the Japan New Party, the issue of expanding parliamentary group "Kaikaku [Reform]" to include other political forces. During the discussions at the prime minister's official residence, he said: "It is important for the ruling coalition to go back to the spirit in which they started out and maintain the current framework. On the other hand, it is also necessary to invite other political forces to the ruling coalition." In this way he indicated that, based on the current formation of seven political parties and one parliamentary group, the ruling coalition should establish a new framework by inviting some Liberal Democratic Party members.
