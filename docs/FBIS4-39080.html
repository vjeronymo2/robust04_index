BFN [Text] Tiraspol BASAPRESS, 3/6/1994 -- The Dniester Republic's delegation will not disregard the referendum which expresses the independence of the Dniester Republic's people and its right to individual statehood, stated today to Tiraspol Radio Ana Volkova, former member of the Dniester delegation attending the negotiations on granting a special juridical status to the eastern districts. Nicolae Andronic, Moldovan deputy speaker of parliament, stated to Tiraspol Radio on 31 May that the Tiraspol leaders are to be blamed for jeopardizing the settlement of the Dniester conflict. Concerning these statements, Volkova mentioned that the Moldovan Parliament's statements contain "inaccuracies or even distortions of events." Volkova said that Andronic's statements are unfounded. She mentioned that it was the Tiraspol representatives who proposed a plan for a confederation in Moldova. Volkova said that if Chisinau had accepted that plan, the Dniester conflict would have been solved long ago. Volkova added that it was the Moldovan delegation who left the negotiations "arguing that the commission's members were on vacation or that the parliament was dissolved."
