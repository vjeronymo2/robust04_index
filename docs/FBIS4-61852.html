BFN [By BUSINESS-TASS correspondent Olga Kostromina] [Text] Moscow May 10 TASS -- An average monthly salary at Russian state enterprises amounted to 146,000 rubles (80 U.S. dollars) in the first quarter of 1994. It was 145,000 rubles in the material production sphere, and 148.3 thousand rubles in the non- production sphere. The rates of salary increase amounted to 132, 124 and 132 per cent correspondingly as compared to the fourth quarter of 1993, sources at the Russian Ministry of Economy told BUSINESS-TASS here today. An average monthly salary in the gas industry was 666,000 rubles (361 U.S. dollars) in March 1994 against the general background of 169,000 rubles (92 U.S. dollars), 416,000 rubles (226 U.S. dollars) in the oil-extracting industry, 367,000 rubles (199 U.S. dollars) in the mining industry, 122,000 rubles (66 U.S. dollars) in the machine-building, and some 100,000 (54 U.S. dollars) in the light industry. An average monthly salary in health care lagged behind than in the production sphere by 20 per cent in January-March 1994, an average monthly salary in education lagged behind by 24 per cent, that in culture and arts -- by 29 per cent, science and scientific services -- by 22 per cent. Staffers of crediting and insurance organizations had the largest salary in the non-production sphere in March 1994 -- over 380,000 rubles (206 U.S. dollars), the smallest salary was in the culture and arts sphere -- 110,000 rubles (59 U.S. dollars). The increase of consumer prices was compensated by an income increase by approximately 89 per cent in the first quarter of 1994. The number of persons with an income less than the subsistence minimum is decreasing. The index is now 25.2 million people or 17 per cent of the Russian population as compared to 51.7 million or 34.7 per cent in the first quarter of 1993.
