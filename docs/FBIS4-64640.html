BFN [Text] Anti-LTTE [Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam] activist, Sabalingam was bumped off in Paris on Sunday [May 1] to prevent him from publishing a book on the atrocities committed by LTTE and the movement's other inside activities. According to reports, he had fled from Colombo to Paris after the annihilation of their fellow leader and his organization on the orders of Velupillai Prabhakaran. He at first been in the LTTE youth movement and was also a close associate of fellow leader Sri Sabaratnam. Later, he became an active member of the LTTE. For some reason or other, he later (fell out) with him and was due to publish a book about LTTE atrocities and their other activities. He was reported to have been collecting details of LTTE victims and carrying on anti-Tiger campaign in France. According to reports reaching Colombo, Sabalingam was head of the Mahattaya faction in the Paris office of the LTTE. He was shot dead allegedly by Prabhakaran loyalists. The Foreign Ministry has telexed the Sri Lanka Embassy in Paris for details of the reported shooting of Sabalingam in Paris. Reports said Sabalingam's funeral is being held by Tamil organizations in France.
