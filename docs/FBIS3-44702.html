Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Nanning, January 6 (XINHUA) -- The first Sino-Vietnamese trade fair, to take place on April 25, will be held simultaneously in the Tunghsing Development Zone in south China's Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region and Mong Cai County in northeast Vietnam. An executive of the Tunghsing Development Zone said the fair is sponsored by the management commission of the zone, the Guangxi Academy of Social Sciences and Vietnam's Mong Cai County. During the fair, 20 to 30 experts, scholars and professional executives from both sides will exchange economic reform experiences and discuss the prospects for bilateral economic and technical cooperation as well as the prospects for regional economic development in the border areas of the two countries. Business communities of the two countries will hold talks on economic and technical cooperation. More than 280 Vietnamese firms have expressed their readiness to cooperate with Chinese counterparts. Some 500 Chinese companies, mainly from China's relatively developed areas, will attend the fair. Business people from around the world are expected to conduct talks on investment projects in the region. The fair will also organize commodity exhibitions. Both China and Vietnam will organize groups to exchange visits. According to latest official statistics, the border trade volume in the Tunghsing Development Zone exceeded 200 million yuan (about 23 million U.S. dollars) in 1993.
