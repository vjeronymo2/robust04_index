Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 21 Feb (NOTIMEX) -- The German Government supports the U.S. position of getting Japan to open its market "not only in words but with actions," German Economy Minister Gunter Rexrodt said here today. The official emphasized, however, that Germany's support for Washington would be limited if the United States simply wishes to establish export or market quotas, because such measures could damage all international trade. Last week, the United States announced it will retaliate against Japan because the Far Eastern nation has not allowed several U.S.-made products to enter its market. The German minister, who has been on an official visit here since last Thursday [17 February], asserted that placing limits on exports will only lead to bilateralism, which runs against multinational agreements. He explained that such a decision "would violate GATT and would conflict with the interests of the world's large exporting nations." Rexrodt said his country's investments in Mexico will increase if Mexico signs an agreement for reciprocal promotion and protection of investments. In 1993, 31 percent of Mexico's exports to Europe went to Germany and, in turn, Germany became Mexico's foremost commercial partner in the region. After meeting with Mexican Foreign Secretary Manuel Tello Macias, Rexrodt explained that Germany has given Mexico priority status in its relations with Latin America. An example of this, he said, is the "Technogerma Mexico 1994" industrial fair, which was inaugurated by Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari on Monday.
