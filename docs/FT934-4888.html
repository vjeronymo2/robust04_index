931202 FT 02 DEC 93 / EU warns it may use force in Bosnia The Belgian presidency of the European Union yesterday served notice that Europe was ready to use force against local warlords blocking humanitarian aid in Bosnia. The warning followed talks in Brussels between Mr Jacques Delors, president of the European Commission, and Mr Warren Christopher, US secretary of state, which paved the way for next month's visit by President Bill Clinton to Europe and a Nato summit. The talks were a clear effort to patch disagreements earlier this year, principally over US support for arming the Bosnian Moslems against the Serbs. In Geneva, Bosnia's three warring factions reported progress after deciding to extend negotiations on the republic's ethnic division for another day. Mr Radovan Karadzic, the Bosnian Serb leader, said progress had been made on a Serb insistence of partition of Sarajevo, the capital.
