941003 FT 03 OCT 94 / UK Company News: Betacom falls 61% Betacom, the consumer telecommunications company 66.17 per cent owned by Amstrad, reported pre-tax profits of Pounds 203,000 for the year to June 30. The 61 per cent fall, from Pounds 516,000, came on the back of turnover marginally ahead at Pounds 13.5m (Pounds 12.1m). Earnings per share were cut to 0.3p (0.83p). Operating profits for the period improved to Pounds 138,000, compared with Pounds 57,000; however, this year's pre-tax figure included a Pounds 55,000 provision for loss on sale of fixed assets, while last year's included a Pounds 213,000 profit on the sale of fixed asset investments.
