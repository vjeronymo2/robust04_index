Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, Jan 18, INA--The headquarters of Iraq's Defence Ministry was reopened on Monday after Iraqi reconstruction teams have restored the historical magnificence of the first group of the Ministry's buildings which came under successive air and missiles attacks during the 1991 U.S.-led war. A celebration was held at the site of the reconstructed premises attended by Defence Minister General 'Ali Hasan al-Majid, the Army chief of staff and senior Army offices. The reconstruction work included the removal of 225,000 tones of debris of 17 buildings. Eight new buildings were reconstructed in addition to over-all repair work for 11 other buildings which were partly damaged. On January 17, 1991, the Defence Ministry headquarters came under air and missile attacks. It was attacked again on January 19 and 23, complete parts of the Ministry were demolished. The old buildings were replaced with new modern ones that have exactly the same distinct architectural features of the destroyed ones.
