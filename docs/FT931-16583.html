930106 FT 06 JAN 93 / Orders rise in west Midlands MANUFACTURING companies in the west Midlands have received a stronger flow of orders over the past three months, the west Midlands chambers of commerce say in a joint survey published today. It covers comments from 643 companies in the last quarter of 1992. The proportion of manufacturing companies with larger domestic order books increased to 20 per cent in December from 13 per cent in September. The proportion of such companies receiving more export orders rose to 23 per cent from 19 per cent. The position was less encouraging for service companies. The increase in domestic orders was less marked and the proportion of companies with larger export order books declined by December to 16 per cent from 23 per cent in September. By the end of last year, 48 per cent of manufacturing companies and 39 per cent of service companies were working at less than 80 per cent of capacity.
