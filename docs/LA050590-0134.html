May 5, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

BUSH'S SON TO TESTIFY ABOUT THRIFT FAILURE;

S&LS: LOANS TO NEIL BUSH'S BUSINESS PARTNERS WHILE HE WAS ON THE BOARD OF A COLORADO THRIFT WERE ALLEGEDLY A CONFLICT OF INTEREST.

Neil Bush, the president's son, said Friday that he has agreed to testify before the House Banking Committee about the failure of a Colorado thrift on whose board he served.

The panel, chaired by Rep. Henry B. Gonzalez (D-Tex.), is looking into the December, 1988, collapse of Silverado Banking, Savings & Loan Assn. of Denver.

The connections among Silverado and other failed S&Ls are being examined, including Charles H. Keating Jr.'s Lincoln Savings & Loan Assn. and the junk bond operation of former Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. financier Michael Milken.

The committee has scheduled two hearings. Thrift regulators are expected to testify May 22; Bush and other Silverado board members are to appear May 23.

"I have agreed to participate and will continue to cooperate with Congress, the Office of Thrift Supervision and any other agencies involved," Bush said in a statement released through his attorney's office.

The thrift office has alleged that loans by Silverado to Bush's business partners, developers Kenneth Good and Bill Walters, were a conflict of interest for Bush.

Neil Bush was on the S&L's board from October, 1985, until August, 1988.

In January, regulators began disciplinary procedures against Bush that could result in a lifetime ban from the thrift industry. Bush has denied any wrongdoing.
