BFN [Text] A senior political source has told our political correspondent Shlomo Raz that Israel sees no point in discussing the issues that remain under contention with the PLO because it has no intention of acceding to the PLO's demands. The disagreements revolve around the stationing of a Palestinian policeman on the Allenby Bridge, a Palestinian director at the Rafah border checkpoint, and the size of the Jericho enclave. U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher has been informed of Israel's position. On 10 May, the working groups on refugee affairs will resume discussions in Cairo. The parties will stress the improvement of the refugees's living conditions and refrain from dealing with other aspects of the problem.
