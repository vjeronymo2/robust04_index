Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum, 3 Jan (SUNA) -- Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih has said that Sudan has been playing host to refugees in accordance with the rules of asylum and they are not allowed to undertake any armed activity or any other political action against their own countries. In a statement he made to the newspaper AL-INQADH AL-WATANI published today concerning the infiltration of some armed elements across the border [into Eritrea] under full security cover, he said that no reports had been received about what could be considered [words indistinct] and to disturb the peace in the refugee camps. His excellency added that all the refugee camps were under the full administrative control of the UNHCR. They are far away from the border and constitute no threat of any kind against neighboring countries. He stressed that the UNHCR's priority [words indistinct] monitors all activities by the refugees and does not allow any violation of the international conventions governing asylum. It is worth mentioning that the number of Eritrean refugees in Sudan exceeds 500,000 living in 30 different places. Eritrean President Isayas Afewerki announced that his forces had killed more than 20 persons who tried to infiltrate into Eritrea from Sudan on 16 December. He described Sudan as the source of the problem. He accused the infiltrators as having come to liberate the area.
