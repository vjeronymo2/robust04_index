January 12, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: CHICKEN RECIPE CUTS FAT WITHOUT LOSING FLAVOR

DEAR JEANNE: I've enclosed one of my favorite recipes for revision. It's delicious, but I think a few of the calories could be cut.

-- SHARON, Olympia, Wash.

CHICKEN PARISIENNE

2 whole chicken breasts, halved

Salt, pepper

3 tablespoons butter

1 small onion, chopped

1 cup sliced mushrooms

1 (10 3/4-ounce) can cream of chicken soup

1 cup sour cream

1/2 cup Sherry

Season breasts with salt and pepper. Melt butter in skillet. Brown chicken on both sides. Arrange chicken, onion and mushrooms in baking dish. Combine soup, sour cream and Sherry in separate bowl. Pour over chicken. Bake at 400 degrees 40 to 50 minutes. Makes 4 servings.

Note: Serve with rice.

DEAR SHARON: My revision of your recipe will require just a little more work on your part, but the end result will be just as tasty and much better for you.

I was able to achieve a substantial reduction in the calories and cut the cholesterol and fat by about half simply by making the recipe with homemade cream soup instead of canned. This also results in a huge reduction in sodium and eliminates the additives many canned soups contain.

By substituting margarine for the butter and by reducing the amount used, fat and cholesterol are further reduced. Yogurt works beautifully in place of the sour cream; and you can lower the calories, cholesterol and sodium in my revision even further by simply removing the skin of the chicken before you cook it. In fact, per serving you can save about 80 calories, 10 milligrams cholesterol and another 10 grams of fat if you cook the chicken with the skin removed.

You will notice a big difference in my revised casserole when it cools down -- no more thick layer of congealed fat covering the top.

-- JEANNE

LIGHT CHICKEN PARISIENNE

4 teaspoons corn-oil margarine

1/4 cup unbleached flour

1 cup defatted chicken stock

3/4 cup nonfat milk

1/4 teaspoon salt

Dash garlic powder

Freshly ground black pepper

1/2 cup nonfat plain yogurt

1/2 cup Sherry

2 whole chicken breasts, halved

1 small onion, chopped

1 cup sliced mushrooms

Melt margarine in skillet. Add flour and stir over medium heat 1 minute. Do not brown. Add chicken stock and nonfat milk. Using wire whisk, stir over medium heat until mixture comes to boil.

Add salt, garlic powder and dash of freshly ground black pepper. Continue to cook 1 minute more. Remove from heat and combine with yogurt and Sherry. Set aside.

Season chicken breasts with freshly ground black pepper. Brown chicken on both sides in non-stick skillet. Arrange chicken, onion and mushrooms in baking dish.

Pour soup mixture over chicken and vegetables. Bake at 400 degrees 40 to 50 minutes. Makes 4 (approximately 1 3/4 cup) servings.

Note: Serve with rice.

Each serving contains approximately:

Original Recipe:

Calories: 545

Cholesterol: 131 mg

Fat: 38 gm

Sodium: 1,072 mg

Revised Recipe:

Calories: 376

Cholesterol: 77 mg

Fat: 17 gm

Sodium: 332 mg
