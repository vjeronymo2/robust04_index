BFN [Text] A group of Black Sea Fleet vessels staying in the eastern part of the Black Sea began increased interaction with Russian troops in Western Georgia Friday. The operation is directed by the chief of the Black Sea Fleet command point, Rear Admiral Yuriy Nichik, the fleet's press center told Interfax. The goal of the peace-keeping action is to stabilize the situation in the region. At present, preparation is being carried out for the deployment of Russian troops in the confrontation zone along the Inguri River to ensure security in the region. In the meantime, another group of Black Sea Fleet vessels headed by the anti-submarine vessel Kerch left Sevastopol for the Greek island Corfu. The group's objective is to ensure the security of Russian President Boris Yeltsin as he attends the European Union summit on June 24-25. The group is under command of the First Deputy Chief of the Black Sea Fleet staff, Rear Admiral Anatoliy Manchenko.
