BFN [Vladimir Abarinov report in the "Scandal" column: "Yeltsin's Order on Military Bases in Latvia Does Not Exist. Andrey Kozyrev: `We Are Being Tested for Firmness'"] [Excerpt] [Passage omitted] A highly placed Russian diplomat told SEGODNYA that the Foreign Affairs Ministry did not learn about the existence of the document on the Russian bases until the Russian ambassador in Riga was summoned to the Latvian Foreign Ministry for explanation. The copy of the paper in possession of the Russian delegation has been received from the Latvian Embassy in Moscow and has all the requisites except for Yeltsin's signature. The text says: "Agree with the proposal by the Russian Defense Ministry on creating on the territories of the CIS member states and the Latvian Republic of military bases of the Russian Federation Armed Forces to ensure the security of the Russian Federation and said states and also to test new arms and military equipment. The Russian Foreign Ministry, jointly with the Russian Defense Ministry and other Russian Federation departments concerned, are to hold talks on this question with the CIS member states and the Latvian Republic and to formalize the accords reached by corresponding documents." The paper, which, in the expression of a Russian diplomat, was "slipped in for signing" to the president, bears number 175-RP and is dated 5 April. [passage omitted]
