Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Jan 25 (CTK) -- The process of transformation which the Polish Army is currently undergoing is very similar to that of the Czech Army, Czech President Vaclav Havel said at a meeting with Polish Defense Minister Piotr Kolodziejczyk today. The meeting was also attended by Czech Defense Minister Antonin Baudys, President's Office Head Lubos Dobrovsky told journalists. Havel and Kolodziejczyk agreed that the approach of the Czech Republic and Poland towards the U.S. Partnership for Peace plan was very close and in many points fully identical, he said. Havel spoke in favor of continuing consultations and cooperation in those fields where they could speed up the modernization of armaments systems in the cheapest way. Baudys described Kolodziejczyk's visit as the beginning of a broad cooperation between the two countries' armies and defense ministries and as the starting point of realizing the necessity of a dynamic approach towards the Partnership for Peace project. Kolodziejczyk said NATO standards should be introduced in both armies as soon as possible and that they should jointly proceed towards a secure Europe. He invited Baudys to visit Poland. Kolodziejczyk, who is leaving for home today, was in the Czech Republic at Baudys's invitation.
