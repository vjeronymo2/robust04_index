Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [By correspondent Cha Man-sun from Vienna] [Text] The International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] held the first official contact with North Korea in Vienna, Austria, this morning and discussed the issue of resuming the inspections of the seven nuclear facilities North Korea had reported to the IAEA but a conclusion has not been reached. The three North Korean representatives, including Yun Ho-chin, counselor of the North Korean Embassy in Vienna, visited the IAEA at 0000 this morning, Korean time, and delivered North Korea's official message containing its will [uisa] to accept overall inspections [chonmyon sachal]. Then, the North Korean representatives held the first official working-level contact with (Belcos), director of the IAEA Inspection Department, and negotiated working-level problems regarding the resumption of inspections of the reported seven nuclear facilities for two hours. After the working-level negotiations, North Korean representatives revealed in an interview with KBS that even though the two sides have failed to reach an agreement, they held very productive negotiations. Counselor Yun Ho-chin, North Korea's representative, said that during the official contact the two sides discussed their opinions regarding the resumption of inspections and shared opinions to settle the points at issue through negotiations so that inspections may be smoothly conducted. He said they agreed to meet once again early next week.
