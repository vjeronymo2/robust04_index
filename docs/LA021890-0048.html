February 18, 1990, Sunday, Bulldog Edition

SWEETNESS GETS A BIT GOOEY IN LOVELAND

This town, known as Colorado's Sweetheart City, had a little more sweetness than it could handle Friday.

A molasses storage tank sprang a leak before dawn, spilling its gooey contents onto city streets and an industrial park.

The molasses, stiffened by near-zero temperatures, flowed slowly and more than a foot deep about one-half mile down a city street. City officials estimated that about 100,000 gallons spilled from the tank, which was at an old sugar factory.
