Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Munich (DDP/ADN) -- Following the failure of pay talks in Cologne for the engineering industry, IG Metall has called around 20,000 workers in over 30 factories in Bavaria out on token strike. According to the Bavarian region of the union, those affected include the car manufacturer Audi in Ingolstadt and Bosch in Bamberg, as well as Siemens and Siemens-Nixdorf plants in Upper Bavaria. Rallies and demonstrations are planned outside the workplace in Ingolstadt, Waldkraiburg, and Bamberg. Among the factories affected by the token strikes is the Zeuna-Staerker firm of Hubert Staerker, the president of the Bavarian Engineering Association, in Augsburg.
