October 1, 1990, Monday, Southland Edition

BRIEFLY

Products for the Up-Tight Commuter: Commuter Products Corp. of Emeryville is making things for commuters who are tense from being stuck in stop-and-go traffic.

Mabel Yee's firm offers a massaging car seat, stress-reduction tapes, stereo headrests and an electronic message board that tells tailgaters to back off. Commuter Products also offers gadgets for before or after a commute. Among them: an under-the-pillow alarm that shakes a sleeper without disturbing anyone else, a multi-pocket folder to organize glove box rubble and a Car Finder that turns on the lights and honks the horn of your car from 200 feet way.
