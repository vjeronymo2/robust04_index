920208 FT 08 FEB 92 / Finance & The Family (Briefcase): Modern banking I HAVE been persuaded by Midland bank to change my old-fashioned current account to a Meridian account which both credits me and charges me with interest on a monthly basis, depending on whether I am in credit or overdrawn. Interest payable is of course not an allowable charged for taxation purposes, but what of interest credited? Can I net these interest items for tax purposes or should I just ignore both credits and debits. The interest debited is, as you say, ignored in calculating your tax bill. The interest credited will be net after deduction of basic-rate tax, so you will only have more tax to pay on it if you are a higher-rate taxpayer. The tax withheld would be repayable to you if your personal allowance etc. had not been absorbed elsewhere, of course.
