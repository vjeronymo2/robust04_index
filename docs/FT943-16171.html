940706 FT 06 JUL 94 / Accidents decline in farming The number of people killed in accidents at work in farming and forestry last year was the lowest recorded, the Health and Safety Executive announced yesterday. Forty-one people died in such accidents, 11 fewer than in 1992. The average for the previous seven years was 60. According to the HSE's economic advisers unit about 40 per cent of accidents at work in farming, forestry and fisheries could be prevented if more care was taken. The costs of these accidents are at least Pounds 325m a year, it added.
