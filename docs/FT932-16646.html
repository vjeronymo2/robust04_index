930405 FT 05 APR 93 / Enough official green shoots to make a salad? INDUSTRY Industrial Production - January manufacturing output up 0.8 per cent on month, 1.7 per cent on year CBI Industrial Trends - balance of 10 per cent of companies polled in March say they will boost output Purchasing managers index - up to 53.9 in March Business failures - Dun & Bradstreet says 1st quarter liquidators down to 5,297 from 5,603 the year before HOUSING Nationwide Anglia building society says UK house prices rose 1 per cent in March Housing starts up seasonally adjusted 14 per cent in three months to February compared with previous three months Building Society mortgage commitments - up 40 per cent during February CONSUMER DEMAND Retail sales volume - up 0.2 per cent in February, 2.4 per cent on year Narrow money (M0) - up 4.5 per cent year to February New car registrations - up 15 per cent in February against February last year TRADE Export volume to non-EC nations - up 4.5 per cent in three months to February (excluding oil and erratics) Import volume from non-EC nations - up to 6 per cent in three months to February (excluding oil and erratics) CREDIT Consumer credit - up net Pounds 151m in January Bank and building society lending - up Pounds 700m in February Broad money (M4) - up 3.4 per cent in year to February Bank notes in circulation - up 5.3 per cent in the week to March 31 over the equivalent week a year ago LABOUR MARKET Unemployment - down seasonally adjusted 22,000 in February Job Centre vacancies - up adjusted 17,600 in February CONFIDENCE FT-SE 100 index down 52.2 points to 2869.9 on Friday April 2 (from 2,922.1 four weeks before) Consumer confidence - at an all time low, according to a post-Budget Daily Telegraph-Gallup poll, but MORI for The Times says the public is now less pessimistic
