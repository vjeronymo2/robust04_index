Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 16 Jan (MENA) -- French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe left Cairo today, following a two-day official visit to Egypt, during which he was received by President Husni Mubarak. The French minister paid a three-day private visit to Egypt. While here, Juppe held talks with Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi and Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa on the Middle East situation, developments in the peace process, and the upcoming meeting in Geneva between the Syrian and U.S. presidents. Juppe held talks also with Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid on bolstering Arab-European relations. He met with the members of the Egyptian-French Businessmen's Association at a seminar on Egyptian-French economic, trade, and investment cooperation.
