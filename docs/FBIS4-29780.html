BFN [Text] A senior official at the Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI] revealed on 17 June that Japan and the United States have agreed to hold subcabinet-level negotiations between Deputy MITI Vice Minister Sozaburo Okamatsu and Undersecretary of Commerce Jeffrey Garten in late June to discuss the auto and auto parts issue under the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks. A place for the talks has not been decided. Based on the results of working-level talks to be held in Washington on 23 and 24 June, they will hold final consultations in an effort to reach an agreement before the Naples summit (meeting of leaders of the seven industrialized nations) beginning 8 July. Commenting on negotiations on the government procurement sector, for which the deadline for sanctions is set for the end of June, the official also disclosed that coordination is now in progress on the schedule for talks between Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa and U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor following the vice-ministerial talks to be held on 22 and 23 June.
