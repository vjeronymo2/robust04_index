Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, March 18 (SNARK)--The Armenia's Office of Prosecutor has decided to arrest Vahan Avakyan, the first deputy of the Chief Adviser of the Armenian president on questions of national security. David Shahnazaryan, the acting chief of the Armenia's State Department of National Security, said that on March 15 in the Yerevan airport "Zvartnots" Zaruyi Yavuryan, the wife of Avakyan had been arrested when she tried to take out from the republic 6 floppy diskettes and documents with the secret information, which was, according to the official resolution, state secret. Vahan Avakyan, who was in Moscow in that time, refused to return to Armenia, when the president apparat called him back. Some time later he connected with the president apparat by telephone and said that he was out of the CIS and was under protection of a foreign country. According to Shahnazaryan, they instituted proceeding on these facts. Ashot Manucharyan, the former Chief Adviser of the Armenian president on National security, acts as a witness in this case but he refused to give any testimony. On Shahnazaryan's words, taking into account the publications in the Bulgarian press, Vahan Avakyan is in Bulgaria now. "Armenian officially asked Bulgaria's authorities about that but hasn't received any explanation yet." Shahnazaryan said.
