931216 FT 16 DEC 93 / London Stock Exchange: Seminar lifts BP shares Dealers adopted a strongly bullish view of BP shares after the UK oil group gave what a number of oil sector specialists described as an impressive, competent and confident presentation to analysts and institutional fund managers in London yesterday. The company is scheduled to deliver a similar message to US analysts this morning in New York. The London market registered its pleasure by marking BP shares higher, closing a net 6 1/2 firmer at 333p after keen turnover of 11m. Talking after the presentation, specialists said there was every chance that BP would resume the strong upward path that saw the shares rally from the 180p mark in August 1992 to the 360p reached in November this year. The stock has fallen back sharply from those highs amid stories that US holders have turned sellers of the shares. BP reached a peak 377p in July 1987 and approached that level again in August 1990. Mr David Simon, BP's chief executive, told the assembled group that BP is on track to meet its profits target of Dollars 2bn by 1995 and had increased this to Dollars 3bn for the following year, emphasising that these targets hinged on an oil price of between Dollars 16 to Dollars 18 a barrel. However, the market's arch-bear of BP, Mr John Toalster at Strauss Turnbull, remained unimpressed. 'It was a slick performance,' he said, 'delivered with more style than substance; we are still sceptical and still sellers of BP.'
