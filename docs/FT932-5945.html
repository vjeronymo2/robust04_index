930601 FT 01 JUN 93 / International Company News: Statistical reports circulate under great secrecy Every summer for the past 21 years, bundles of documents stacked a foot high have circulated under conditions of great secrecy around City financial institutions. More than 200 banks, brokerages and consultants in London pay upwards of Pounds 30,000 for these detailed statistical reports and consulting advice to back them up. While the anuual Extel ratings of analysts - the industry's own assessment of its peers - circulate freely around the City, the companies are far more coy about revealing these findings to their competitors or the Press. The money spent does not give them carte blanche to use the findings. They sign a confidentiality agreement and agree that they are renting rather than buying the reports. The company responsible is Greenwich Associates, a US consulting and market research business based in Greenwich, Connecticut. Each year it comes to London and other big financial centres around the world to conduct interviews with institutions and canvass their views on how they are serviced. 'This is the bible,' says one senior figure in a large City securities house, tapping one of the reports, which is firmly closed. 'It is very hard to run a firm without it. It is invaluable if you know how to understand the figures. Luckily not many of our competitors do.'
