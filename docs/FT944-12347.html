941102 FT 02 NOV 94 / Nursery education taskforce set up Mrs Gillian Shephard, the education secretary, told the Commons yesterday that 'a range of options' would be considered by the government in drawing up plans to expand nursery education. She announced that she had set up a special taskforce in her department to implement the promise to increase nursery provision made by Mr John Major, the prime minister, at last month's Cons-ervative party conference. Mrs Shephard said the commitment was to provide, over time, good quality pre-school places for all four-year-olds whose parents wished to take them up. The taskforce would work closely with other departments, and would be canvassing the views of outside organisations. Responding to Labour taunts over her failure to estimate the extra spending likely to be involved, she stressed: 'This is a new provision, and there will be new money.'
