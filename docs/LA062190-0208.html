June 21, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: DIET AND CHOLESTEROL

Question: I have what to me is a fairly simple question, but I've never seen it answered anywhere.

If a person has a high cholesterol level, and his doctor says that he should get it down, why not put him on a diet with absolutely no fat in it?

Answer: When I prescribe a diet for a patient who has an elevated serum cholesterol level, I have a twofold purpose. The first is to reduce the total cholesterol and fat levels. The second is to make certain that the patient continues to have a nutritionally adequate diet. Both goals can generally be achieved even if the diet includes some fat.
