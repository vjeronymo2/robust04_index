931222 FT 22 DEC 93 / Cameroon curbs foreign firms Cameroon, which has 11.5 per cent of Africa's rainforest with 300 varieties of trees, adopted a tropical timber trade law yesterday aimed at reducing foreign domination of the industry, Reuter reports from Yaounde. The country's timber trade is 80 per cent controlled by expatriates, notably the French, and the new law aims to increase state revenue. But the French director of a forestry company said the law would deter reinvestment.
