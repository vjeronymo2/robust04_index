BFN [Text] Kinshasa, 8 Apr (AFP)--The installations of a private cellular telephone company, which has for many years replaced Kinshasa's public communications system, was the target of "sabotage" by an eight-man commando unit, according to reliable sources in the Zairian capital. The commando unit attacked TELECEL's antennae field, located in central Kinshasa, and cut communication cables, rendering stations inoperative. In a press release, the company said that it would not be capable of providing service until further notice. The "sabotage" came 24 hours after a meeting to discuss frequency allocation between the Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications and enterprises hoping to use the telecommunications network.
