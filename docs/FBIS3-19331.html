Language: German Article Type:BFN [DPA report: "Wages in Eastern Germany Reach 80 Percent of Western Level"] [Excerpt] Bonn -- The negotiated wages and salaries increased by an average of 3.2 percent in western Germany and by 12.5 percent in eastern Germany in 1993. Thus, the wage level in the new laender rose from 73 to 80 percent of the western wages and salaries. This has been revealed by the annual assessment of the wage settlements by the Labor Ministry. Yet there are considerable differences between eastern and western Germany concerning fringe benefits. The same applies to the weekly working hours: In the west, the average was 37.76 hours, and in the east over two hours more. [passage omitted]
