921210 FT 10 DEC 92 / Intel urges action Intel, the leading US semiconductor manufacturer, will be seeking 'very aggressive' action by the Clinton administration to enforce terms of the US-Japan chip trade agreement in which Tokyo vowed to open its market to foreign suppliers, writes Louise Kehoe in San Francisco. 'We have made some progress toward the 20 per cent market share target which was stated in the agreement,' said Mr Craig Barrett, Intel executive vice-president, 'but we do not want to see any backtracking by the Japanese during the presidential transition period'.
