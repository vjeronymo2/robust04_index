920929 FT 29 SEP 92 / International Company News: First-half fall at Publicis THE slowdown in the European advertising industry has taken a toll on Publicis, one of France's largest marketing services groups, which yesterday announced a fall in first-half profits. Publicis, which owns the French advertising agency of the same name and has been expanding its interests across Europe, saw net profits fall by 13 per cent from FFr73m (Dollars 14.6m) in the first six months of 1991 to FFr65m in the same period this year. Sales rose by 5 per cent from FFr10bn to FFr10.5bn. Publicis, in common with other French advertising agencies, has been affected by the slowdown in media expenditure reflecting the sluggish state of France's economy. The French advertising scene has also been clouded by controversy over the government's review of the media buying system. Mr Michel Sapin, finance minister, faced criticism from within the industry for his plans to regulate media buying and later unveiled a revised package.
