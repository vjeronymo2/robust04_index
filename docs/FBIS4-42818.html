BFN [Text] Paris, June 9 KYODO -- U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher and Japanese Government representative Nobuo Matsunaga on Wednesday [8 June] agreed that the two nations should continue close dialogue on the North Korean nuclear issue, Japanese officials said. The agreement came as they held talks at the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) headquarters in Paris. They were attending the two-day OECD ministerial meeting. Christopher expressed strong concern over the situation in North Korea. Matsunaga, former ambassador to the United States, said the Japanese Government will in principle take concerted action if the United States imposes economic sanctions on North Korea, the officials said. However, Matsunaga stressed the necessity to maintain further efforts for a peaceful settlement of the issue, they added.
