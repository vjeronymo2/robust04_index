BFN [Text] Chisinau BASAPRESS, 4/6/1994 -- Of some 16,202 offences which have been registered in the five months of 1994, 20 percent were serious crimes, stated today on the Moldovan radio Victor Catana, prime vice minister of interior. The number of crimes against individuals decreased; murders and attempted murder fell by 3.2 percent in comparison with 1993. According to him, the police has solved 41.1 percent of the total number of crimes; 64.1 percent of all offences inflicting serious injuries and 65.9 percent of rapes and attempted rape cases were solved. Catana specified that 27 percent of the offences were committed by workers, 5 percent by peasants and 68 percent by other social categories. He expressed concern about the amount of crimes committed by jobless people and youngsters who do not study. The number of crimes committed by drunk people increased by 40.8 percent, people with penal records by 39.7 percent, groups by 19.2 percent, minors by 36.3 percent and people who do not work or study by 27.7 percent, said Victor Catana.
