920305 FT 05 MAR 92 / Brady makes strong plea on IMF quotas THE Bush administration yesterday renewed its efforts to win approval from Congress for its Dollars 12bn share of the International Monetary Fund's capital increase. Mr Nicholas Brady, treasury secretary, made a strong appeal for Congress to pass 'this all-important legislation'. He warned that failure to do so would block the entire Dollars 60bn quota increase and 'seriously impede the west's entire response to the new states of the former Soviet Union'. Mr Brady said the IMF aid programme for the Russian republic alone would cost roughly Dollars 4bn. 'We simply shouldn't take a chance at this particular point in time with abandoning the democratic changes that are taking place in the republics which formerly constituted the Soviet Union,' he said.
