Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Viktor Chernomyrdin returned to his duties as prime minister today after a week-long absence due to illness, Valentin Grishin [name as heard], first deputy head of the Russian Government's press service, has announced. Viktor Chernomyrdin is today preparing for tomorrow's government sitting, which is to examine the draft budget of the Russian Federation for this year, and also for an expanded government sitting scheduled for 4 March. A traditional weekly working meeting with Boris Yeltsin is scheduled for 1400, after which talks will be held with Uzbekistan's President Islam Karimov, followed by the signing of a package of Russian-Uzbek documents.
