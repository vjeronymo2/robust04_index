Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Chilpancingo, Mexico, 22 Feb (AFP) -- About 700 members of the 500 Years of Indian Resistance Council of Guerrero [Consejo Guerrerense 500 Anos de Resistencia Indigena] began a march to Mexico City to demand a solution to their claims and in support of the Zapatist National Liberation Army (EZLN). The Indians, which make up the four ethnic groups that live in the 34 municipalities in Guerrero State, left Monday night with signs that read "Indians, never again second class citizens," "For a court that judges violations to Indian human rights," and "Solidarity with the noble and fair cause of our Chiapas brothers." Included in their requests, the demonstrators demand compliance with the EZLN demands for democracy, justice, and liberty; autonomy for the Indian peoples; the creation of a state fund for the development of their peoples; and that the state satisfy the needs of employment and services. The Guerrero Indians and other Indians who have arrived from different parts of the country, on 3 March will participate in a Mexico City rally and they have requested an audience with Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari. Yesterday the EZLN and the government began their first peace talks in San Cristobal de las Casas, in the southern State of Chiapas.
