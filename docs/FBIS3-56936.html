Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Aleksandr Pilat report in the "Crimea" column: "The Situation Around the Black Sea Fleet Is Heating Up. Yet Commanders May Come to Agreement"] [Excerpts] Recently Black Sea Fleet servicemen have often been showing their concern over the failure to resolve the Black Sea Fleet issue. [passage omitted] In light of this the Black Sea Fleet Press Center has been authorized to make it known to personnel that documents have been submitted to the command indicating that the Russian Navy leadership shares the Black Sea Fleet servicemen's concern over the dragging out of the talks on the fleet's fate. The Russian Federation Navy Command has sent a telegram to the Ukrainian Defense Ministry pointing to the impermissibility of unilateral actions with respect to the Black Sea Fleet. "The Russian Federation Navy Command is doing everything possible to ensure the implementation of the Massandra agreements whereby the Black Sea Fleet with its infrastructure is to pass under Russia's jurisdiction," the press center's release stresses. Attention in being drawn to the fact that the Russian president has sent a letter to the Ukrainian president suggesting that a detailed investigation be held into the circumstances surrounding the death of Captain 1st Rank Andrey Lazebnikov, head of the Black Sea Fleet press center, and expressing concern over the deterioration of the crime situation in Sevastopol. On 17 January a telephone conversation took place between Black Sea Fleet Acting Commander Vice Admiral Vitaliy Larionov (Black Sea Fleet commander Admiral Eduard Baltin is currently on vacation) and Vladimir Beskaravaynyy, commander of the Ukrainian Navy. The sides discussed the current state of relations between the two fleets, recognizing the impermissibility of unilateral actions prior to top- level decisions on the Black Sea Fleet being made.
