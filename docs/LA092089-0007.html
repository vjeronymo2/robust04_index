September 20, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

UNITING DIVERSE LATINO GROUPS

I dislike the term "Hispanic." Not only does it connote a non-existent unity, but more importantly it goes against the principle of self-determination. It is a government-concocted word that undermines the hard work of Chicano activists of the 1960s and 1970s. Let us not forget that at the very heart of the word Chicano was the belief that Mexican Americans should define for themselves who they were and what they should pursue.

The term "Chicano" came about as a reaction against being considered hyphenated Americans (as in Mexican-American) which in reality meant second-class citizens. Yes, the hyphen has disappeared but in its place has come a new word that reinforces and perpetuates the fact that Chicanos/Latinos/Mexicanos still have no say over their own destiny.

ERNESTO CHAVEZ

Los Angeles
