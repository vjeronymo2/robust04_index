BFN [Report by Xuan Bach] [Text] The provincial party committee, People's Council, and People's Committee of Lai Chau Province held a solemn meeting in Dien Bien Phu [DBP] this morning to mark the 40th anniversary of the historic Dien Bien Phu victory. Present were Doan Khue, Political Bureau member and defense minister, representing the party Central Committee; Deputy Prime Minister Nguyen Khanh, representing the government; National Assembly Vice Chairman Phung Van Tuu; representatives of various departments and branches at the central level; representatives of Lao Cai, Son La, Yen Bai, and Hoa Binh Provinces; representatives of Luang Prabang and Phong Saly Provinces in the Lao People's Democratic Republic; and 20,000 people from various ethnic groups in Lai Chau. Delivering a speech at the meeting, Comrade Nguyen Niem, member of the party Central Committee and secretary of the Lai Chau Province party committee, pointed out the significance of the Dien Bien Phu victory and the lessons of experience to be developed in the cause of socioeconomic construction in Lai Chau in accordance with the renovative spirit initiated by our party. In his speech, Senior General Doan Khue reviewed the development of the war of resistance against the French that climaxed with the Dien Bien Phu campaign. As for the role and significance of the decisive and historic Battle of Dien Bien Phu, the comrade pointed out that the Dien Bien Phu victory was regarded as the common victory of the three peoples on the Indochina peninsula during their war of resistance against the French. The Dien Bien Phu tradition has been developed to the fullest, it contributed to bringing about the great victory in the spring of 1975, and it has helped us continue to overcome difficulties in building socialism successfully in Vietnam. On behalf of the younger generation, Comrade Mu A Cua, secretary of the Lai Chau Province Youth Union, resolved to achieve all socioeconomic targets set by the party and state. The ceremony was followed by a show-of-strength parade and a fitness demonstration program under the theme: Let the Mountains and Jungles Be Green, Fruit Orchards and Rice Fields Be Laden With Fruit and Panicles of Rice, and Smiles Bloom on Young Faces. It was designed to commemorate the 40th anniversary of the Dien Bien Phu victory.
