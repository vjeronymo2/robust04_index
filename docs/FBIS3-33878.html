Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The prime minister held an urgent meeting with the home minister in New Delhi yesterday. Mr. S.B. Chavan, who was in Chandigarh, rushed to Delhi for the talks. Agency reports say that the law and order situation in the Kashmir Valley figured prominently in their talks. The minister of state for home affairs, Mr. Rajesh Pilot, and several senior officials of the Home Ministry were present at the meeting. The prime minister is understood to have given some new directions to deal with the situation. Later, Mr. Chavan left for Jammu where he has held detailed discussions with the state governor, General K.V. Krishna Rao, on the situation in Jammu and Kashmir. The governor apprised him of the steps initiated to achieve the objective of bringing normalcy and an elected government in the state. Mr. Chavan also had a meeting with the four advisers to the governor and the state chief secretary. The home minister will address senior officials of the state government today and also take stock of the implementation of development schemes. He is to visit some of the migrants' camps in Jammu. Mr. Chavan will visit Doda and Kishtwar tomorrow for an on-the-spot study of the situation.
