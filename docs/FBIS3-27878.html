Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [By Yi Il-hwa from Tokyo] [Text] According to a TOKYO SHIMBUN report dispatched from Vienna on 1 February, David Kyd, International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] spokesman, said that North Korea may announce it will suspend negotiations on the nuclear inspection issue in the next talks scheduled this week on North Korea permitting nuclear inspections. Spokesman Kyd said: The IAEA has already explained to North Korea the details of procedures for nuclear inspections. North Korea is expected to respond before the end of this week. The IAEA cannot make any concessions in its original plan for inspections [tangcho-e chesihan sachal naeyongul chogumdo yangbohalsu opta]. Spokesman Kyd also said: If North Korea's response is negative [pujongjok] or clearly shows its aim to stall for time, the IAEA will stop holding negotiations with North Korea and will inform the United States of this. Information Director Kyd said that the IAEA will discuss the North Korean nuclear issue again in its Board of Governors regular meeting to be held beginning 21 February.
