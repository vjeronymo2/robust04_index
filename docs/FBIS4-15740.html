BFN [by ITAR-TASS special correspondent] [Text] Madrid April 11 TASS -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and his Spanish counterpart Javier Solana held talks in Madrid today. At the end of the talks, the heads of foreign ministries of the two countries signed a package of Russian-Spanish documents, which included a treaty on social welfare, an agreement on cooperation in the sphere of culture and education, an agreement on cooperation in the social and labour sphere, an agreement on environmental protection. Under one of the agreements, Russia and Spain will establish a hot line between the Kremlin and the Moncloa Palace, the residence of the Spanish prime minister. Javier Solana expressed his satisfaction with the meeting with Andrey Kozyrev. We signed five agreements, the minister said. Russian-Spanish meetings will be viewed as a positive factor in bringing bilateral relations to new heights when a basic Russian-Spanish treaty and the sixth agreement are signed on Tuesday. The two sides exchanged views on the situation in former Yugoslavia, relations between Russia and NATO, the situation in the Middle East and the role of the United Nations. The Spanish foreign minister noted the importance of the talks which revealed the common position on many problems. The Russian foreign minister agreed with this assessment made by his Spanish counterpart.
