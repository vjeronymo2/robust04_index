BFN [Text] Beijing, June 17 (XINHUA) -- The latest news from the State Flood Control and Drought Relief Headquarters indicates that the Xijiang River in southern China today rose to its highest level in four decades. The No. 2 flood bulletin of the year, issued by China's highest authority for fighting floods, said that on June 16 the third highest water level appeared near Liuzhou city along the river, one of the three major tributaries of the Zhujiang (Pearl) River. After the water level receded some time later, the river saw an even bigger flood peak at one o'clock this afternoon. The level reached 89.25 m, 9.25 m above warning stage, and the water flow reached 27,500 cubic meters per second. The water level along the reaches of Wuzhou city was 23.48 m at two o'clock this afternoon, 8.48 m above the warning stage, and the waters have continued to rise. In the above-mentioned areas, 146 industrial enterprises have been flooded and 46 reservoirs damaged, according to the bulletin. People threatened by the flood have been transferred to other, safer places. The bulletin also said that on June 16, most of the heavy rains in China poured down on the watersheds of the Xijiang, Beijiang, Ganjiang, Xiangjiang and Minjiang rivers. Average precipitation was about 50 mm and surpassed 100 mm in some places. Because of the rain, water levels in these rivers are 1-8 meters above the warning stage.
