Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Quito, 28 Mar (NOTIMEX) -- Ecuadoran Foreign Minister Diego Paredes expressed satisfaction today over the election of Colombian President Cesar Gaviria as OAS secretary general. In a message issued at the OAS office in Washington, Paredes said he is "proud to have supported the Colombian president's candidacy, given the exceptional relationship between our two countries. We are proud because our support has been a demonstration and a reflection of the sentiments of affection the Ecuadoran people have toward Colombia and its president, Cesar Gaviria." In February the government of Ecuadoran President Sixto Duran-Ballen announced its commitment to support the Costa Rican candidate, Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus. Ecuador maintained its support for Costa Rica until a few days before the election. Last Friday [25 March] it changed its position in favor of the Colombian candidate, who was elected Sunday [27 March] with 20 votes. Niehaus received 14 votes. In his message, Paredes also congratulated the Costa Rican candidate "for his lofty and dignified campaign." He said it was a pleasure for the Ecuadoran delegation to adhere to the expressions of affection and recognition of the work carried out by outgoing OAS Secretary General Joao Baena Soares, who has headed the organization for the past 10 years.
