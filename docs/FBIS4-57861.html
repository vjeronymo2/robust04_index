BFN [From the "O ESTADO Column" by Cristiana Lobo] [Text] On 24 May, Strategic Affairs Secretary Mario Cesar Flores informed President Itamar Franco of the result of an international bid for the implementation of Sivam, the Amazon Region Surveillance System. This has been the largest bid ever held in Brazil, with companies from 10 countries participating. The project costs amount to $1.1 billion and will be implemented over eight years. The winners of the tender are a U.S. consortium led by Raytheon and a French consortium led by the state-owned Thomson and Alcatel companies. A German consortium led by Dasa was selected as "an alternate." The loser was a consortium formed by Unisys and IBM, the two largest U.S. computer companies.
