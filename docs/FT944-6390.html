941130 FT 30 NOV 94 / Anti-war activist Rubin dies Former 1960s activist Jerry Rubin died from a heart attack aged 56 in Los Angeles, two weeks after being struck by a car and critically injured. Mr Rubin was a founder of the Youth International Party, known as the Yippies, whose members played a big part in the bloody anti-Vietnam War protests outside the Democratic National Convention in Chicago in August 1968. He was charged, along with seven others including the late Abbie Hoffman and black activist leader Bobby Seale, with conspiracy to incite violence at the Chicago convention.
