BFN [Text] A regular Security Council session, chaired by President Nazarbayev, was held yesterday. At issue were questions related to the state of equipment of the Armed Forces of the Republic of Kazakhstan with technology, armaments, material, and technical resources. The concept of formation and implementation of long-term defense programs and development of the defense complex of the Republic of Kazakhstan were also examined. General Sagadat Nurmagambetov, the defense minister, and Economy Minister Mars Urkumbayev presented reports. An exchange of opinions was held, summed up by Nursultan Nazarbayev: We must precisely decide what an army needs -- how many men we need -- he said. We should urgently tackle all questions connected with equipping the Armed Forces, we should, to a maximum degree, reduce import of military products from outside, and to the best of our possibilities manufacture such products in Kazakhstan. The head of state set an objective--in one week's time--to set up a commission on the military industry. Its chairman will be directly responsible for the situation in this important sphere, which should provide reliable defense capability for our sovereign state. By their decision, the Security Council approved of the concept of formation and implementation of long-term defense programs and development of the defense complex. The council also endorsed several changes in its membership. In particular, Bulat Baekenov was appointed secretary of the council. Speaker Abish Kekilbayev, Akizhan Kazhegeldin, first deputy prime minister, Deputy Prime ministers Galym Abilsiitov and Asygat Zhabagin, and heads of a number of ministries and departments, as well as responsible officials from the presidential administration and that of the Cabinet of Ministers participated in the session.
