BFN [Text] The situation in the Balkans and the Yugocrisis were among the topics that were discussed between President Zhelyu Zhelev and Willy Claes, Belgium's deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs. Bulgaria's position as a stabilizing factor in the Balkans is highly valued by the Belgian Government, the guest pointed out. Kamen Velichkov, adviser to the president on foreign policy issues, has more information about the meeting: [Begin Velichkov recording] Issues related to Bulgaria's integration in the European community had a central place in the talk. Dr. Zhelev expressed our country's gratitude for Belgium's position and the personal efforts of Mr. Claes in resolving some of the issues that emerged after the provisional trade agreement and related issues came into force at the end of 1993. Hope was expressed that Belgium will support our country's efforts to obtain an enhanced status in the West European Union, and to be integrated within NATO. On all those issues a common opinion was expressed that Bulgaria has good prospects regarding gradually joining the European structures. [end recording]
