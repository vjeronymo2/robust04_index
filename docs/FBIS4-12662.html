BFN [Text] The prime minister said acquisition of F-16 fighter aircraft by Pakistan will disturb the security environment and escalate tension in the region. Addressing the Air Force commanders conference in New Delhi today, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao expressed concern over Pakistan's relentless efforts to modernize its armed forces and air power far beyond its legitimate requirements. He said that while the cold war has ended at the super power level, the security scenario in different regions continues to be remain dogged by conflicts and disputes. Calling upon the Indian Air Force [IAF] to maintain constant vigil, the prime minister emphasized that the IAF has to maintain the highest level of operational preparedness. Speaking on the occasion, the minister of state for defense, Mr. Mallikarjun, said that India never seeks confrontation and her quest for peace should not be misunderstood as weakness. Earlier, the chief of air staff, Air Chief Marshal S.K. Kaul, reviewed the air power prevailing in the region and its impact on the security environment.
