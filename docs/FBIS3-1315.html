Language: Somali Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] [passage omitted] The agreement contains two sections: general principles and the new administration. The general principles section deals with the inviolable respect for and sovereignty of the Somali people; opposing the use of force to resolve anything; implementation of the cease-fire and voluntary disarmament; respect for and preservation of human and democratic rights; creation of suitable conditions to enable the Somali people to coexist fraternally in line with their traditions, culture and politics; and rehabilitation and reconstruction of the country to enable it to rise out of the destruction of the civil war. The second section of the agreement provides for the formation of an administration: The restoration of peace to Somalia, giving priorities to trouble spots; the holding in Mogadishu of a conference to be attended by the signatories to the Addis Ababa agreement and the Somali National Movement on 15 April this year; the drafting of election laws and procedures; and methods for participating in the national reconciliation conference. The conference will deliberate on modalities for forming a national council charged with drafting laws, to be formed after a national government has been formed in order to restore our country's dignity and nationhood.
