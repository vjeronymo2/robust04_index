Language: Kazakh Article Type:BFN [Text] Presidents Nazarbayev of Kazakhstan, Niyazov of Turkmenistan, and Karimov of Uzbekistan held a news conference in the framework of the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland. President Nazarbayev said: In the field of foreign politics we adhere to the course of integration with the CIS countries and we support initiatives aimed at strengthening peace, disarmament, and [words indistinct]. When we speak about Russia's policy toward the so-called near abroad, we have to admit that Russia has not yet elaborated its state policy as far as this issue is concerned. There are speeches by certain politicians who do not like the fact that the former Soviet republics became independent states. These politicians would like to continue to rule on all the former USSR territories. But Russia cannot do it now because it is experiencing great difficulties itself. [Words indistinct] all current problems can be settled by way of talks, by political methods -- of which there are not many, in principle. Both Central Asia and the West should be interested in strengthening democracy in Russia and in real reforms being implemented there. Answering a question concerning assessment of the situation in Russia recently [words indistinct] Uzbekistan's President Karimov described it as: Not very well thought out. He said Russia is a guarantor of stability in Central Asia. According to him, all [words indistinct] perceived as chauvinistic attitudes. It is not desirable for them to dominate because they pose certain threat for Uzbekistan. President Niyazov noted that Turkmenistan and Russia do not have unsettled questions between them. Their bilateral relations are good. [Words indistinct] He said: I believe Russia has its strategic and political interests in Central Asia, and we respect them, but we also have our strategic interests in Russia. We seek good neighborly relations with Russia.
