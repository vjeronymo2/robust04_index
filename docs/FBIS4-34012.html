BFN [Text] Cartagena de Indias (Colombia), 14 Jun (EFE) -- The Central American governments have agreed not to receive Haitian refugees, as was requested by U.S. President Bill Clinton, official Central American sources have told EFE. The matter of the refugees was discussed by the chiefs of state of the region who met yesterday in Costa Rica and later in the Colombian city of Cartagena, where the Fourth Ibero-American Summit is being held today and tomorrow. Although they have not made an official announcement en bloc, they decided not to accept the refugees, not even temporarily, sources from a Central American country who chose to remain anonymous have told EFE. Clinton asked the governments of the Central American countries last month to accept the refugees fleeing the Haitian crisis, while it was decided whether their cases could be considered cases of economic or political emigration. If the cases were political, they would be admitted into the United States. The request regarding the Haitian refugees was made directly to the six Central American presidents last month when they met in Tegucigalpa with U.S. Vice President Al Gore. Several countries turned down the request because of economic or emigration problems in their respective countries. Others, such as Costa Rica and El Salvador, said unenthusiastically that they would consider the matter. Honduras, which a couple of years ago gave asylum to a group of Haitians and later had problems assimilating them, had said beforehand that it could not receive any more refugees.
