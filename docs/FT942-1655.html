940623 FT 23 JUN 94 / Arts: Today's Television Prokofiev, it seems, believed that his best opera was The Fiery Angel which he wrote in the early 1920s, though he had to wait 30 years to see it produced. Performances are still infrequent, and those in the original Russian very rare. However, Radio 3 provides one of those rare occasions today with a broadcast of a performance from La Scala, Milan (7.30). It is a five-act work, about black magic and murder in 16th century Germany. A predominantly eastern European cast is led by soprano Galina Gorchakova. On Radio 4 the often excellent Analysis asks whether public scares over matters such as global warming and toxic waste are rational and why environmental concerns take precedence over more obvious and immediate hazards (8.00). The second episode of BBC1's drama Roughnecks, about life on a North Sea oil rig will show whether this is just another forgettable middlebrow, middleweight series or whether there is a bit more to it (9.30). In its True Stories slot C4 shows an over long American home video documentary about living and dying with Aids (9.30).
