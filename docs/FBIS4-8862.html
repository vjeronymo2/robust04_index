BFN [Text] Banja Luka, April 18 (TANJUG) -- The army of the Bosnian Serb republic on Monday repulsed a strong combined Moslem attack on Brcko in the north of the former Yugoslav republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina, Bosnian Serb army sources said in a statement. More than 100 artillery shells were fired on Bosnian Serb positions in the area. The Serb side repulsed a Moslem infantry attack, in which it suffered no casualties, and the Moslems then continued to open artillery and machine-gun fire on Serb positions, the statement said. The attack proves correct the increasingly frequent reports about concentration of Bosnian Moslem and Croat forces towards Serb positions in the valley of the river Sava, where they are planning to cut the only land corridor between the Serb Krajina regions in the west and the federal republic of Yugoslavia in the east.
