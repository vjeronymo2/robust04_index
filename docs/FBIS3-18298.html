Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara -- The Turkish Parliament has postponed a 10-day recess, which would have ended March 10 as Turkey was engulfed this week by rumors of a possible military takeover. Parliamentary sources said that despite Thursday's [24 February] recess decision, members of the True Path Party (DYP) and main opposition Motherland Party (ANAP) groups had decided such a recess was wrong "in a period in which important developments are taking place." Although there was no reference to coup rumors in the postponement decision, sources said both the ongoing crisis with regard to the Kurdish based Democracy Party [DEP] and the debate on a possible military takeover had called for the decision. The DEP started is party assembly meeting on Wednesday to decide on whether it would participate in the upcoming March 27 local elections.
