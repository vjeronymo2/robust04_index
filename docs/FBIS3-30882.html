Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, February 6 (ATA) -- Serbian financial police has recently undertaken an extensive control which is mostly aimed at the Albanian traders, who are not only fined with large sums of money but they are also forced to close down their shops. Serbian civil policemen provoked Albanian traders and under the pretext of non-observation of new monetary measures to help dinar which is going bankrupt, order the closure of their shops. As a result of these measures, 35 shops have been closed until now in Mitrovice, five in Serbice. During the past 15 days alone, the daily Serbian newspaper JEDINSTVO, published in Prishtina, writes 70 Albanian private shops have been closed in the regions which are called "Circle of Kosova."
