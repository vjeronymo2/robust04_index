BFN [Text] Bonn, 24 Apr (EFE) -- Bolivian Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar Quiroga arrived in Bonn on 24 April. He will spend three days in Germany during which he will sign a financial cooperation agreement worth 650 million marks ($380 million). Aranibar Quiroga's official agenda will begin on 25 April: He will meet with his German counterpart Klaus Kinkel; Hans-Peter Repnik, state secretary in the Ministry for Economic Cooperation; and many businessmen and politicians. On 26 April he will hold several political meetings, attend a luncheon hosted by the chairman of the Federal Parliament Foreign Relations Committee, hold a news conference, and depart for Berlin. The Bolivian Foreign Minister will conclude his visit on 27 April in Hamburg where he will meet with Mayor Henning Voscherau. Before departing for Madrid, he will visit the South American German Bank and the Ibero-American Association.
