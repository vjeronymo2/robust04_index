BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis met with British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd, with whom he discussed the latest developments in the Cyprus issue following UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali's report. The meeting took place in Oxford, Hurd's electoral district, where the British foreign secretary was handling other business. Speaking after the meeting, Mikhailidhis said that the two ministers exchanged views on the issue of the promotion of the confidence-building measures and on new developments following the latest Butrus-Ghali report. They also exchanged more general views on what the next steps should be. The foreign minister said he stressed that Cyprus will not participate in a dialogue to amend the 12 March document. Mikhailidhis also said that he briefed the British foreign secretary on the Cyprus Government's belief that there must be a new approach to the Cyprus issue. Foreign Minister Mikhailidhis also said that during the meeting they discussed Cyprus' membership in the European Union [EU], as it will be considered during the upcoming EU Summit in Corfu. Mikhailidhis described his meeting with Hurd as very useful. The foreign minister will return to Cyprus this evening via Athens.
