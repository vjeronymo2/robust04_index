BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 2 (KCNA) -- Chairwoman of the C.C. [Central Committee], the Chondoist Chongu Party Yu Mi-yong in a statement Wednesday urged the political parties and religious organizations of South Korea to accede to a contact at an earlier date to discuss the convocation of a national conference. She said the choice of the entire fellow countrymen for warding off the danger of a nuclear war hanging over the nation, defending peace of the country and opening a signal phase for the independent and peaceful reunification of the country is the great unity of the whole nation and the convocation of a national conference that may help pool the patriotic will and efforts of the nation. "We Chondoist believers cannot give up our hope for a national conference although a tiny handful of those in South Korea opposed to dialogue and reunification are laying hurdles in our way," she said, and added: "We urge that our Chondoist Chongu Party and political parties of South Korea should have bilateral or multilateral contacts at an early date to discuss the convocation of a national conference." She expressed the hope that the South Korean authorities, though belatedly, would show a positive response to the proposal for ont vocation of a national conference and take due measures to select promptly South side's delegates to the preparatory committee for a national conference so that the committee might tackle down to its task.
