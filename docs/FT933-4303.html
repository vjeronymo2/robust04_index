930909 FT 09 SEP 93 / UK Company News: OGC sale lifts Fairhaven to Dollars 37m The sale of 40 per cent of OGC International enabled Fairhaven International, the Bermuda-based specialist oil contractor, to achieve a jump in pre-tax profits from Dollars 9.27m to Dollars 37.4m (Pounds 24.7m) in the half year to June 30. Turnover fell from Dollars 246m to Dollars 207m and operating profits were lower at Dollars 7.25m (Dollars 9.27m). The net gain on the OGC sale amounted to Dollars 30.2m. Earnings per share worked through at 13.9 cents (2.5 cents) and the interim dividend is lifted to 0.5 cents (0.2 cents). OGC International, which was floated in May, also announced its results yesterday with a rise in pre-tax profits from Pounds 4.77m to Pounds 5.3m for the first half of 1993. That was achieved despite a fall in turnover of continuing operations to Pounds 97.1m (Pounds 110m). Earnings improved to 5.69p (4.97p) per share and a dividend of 1.75p is declared.
