Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] The association of black U.S. congressmen held a dinner on Friday, 18 March, in honor of the participants of the third congress of the Black Caucus. Haitian President Jean-Bertrand Aristide was invited to the dinner, during which he made a speech in which he thanked the Black Caucus for its support for the cause of democracy. Let us listen to the details from Jean-Claude Martineau, spokesman for President Aristide. [Begin recording, in Creole] On the evening of 18 March, the association of black U.S. congressmen, the Black Caucus, held a big dinner for everyone participating in the conference they are holding in Miami. When President Aristide entered the room, everybody stood. The president spoke to thank the Black Caucus for the support they have given the Haitian people, who are struggling for life and for democracy. There were thousands of people in the room. The president said there is not much difference between the situation prevailing in Haiti and the situation in South Africa. After the dinner, the president went to a funeral home where a wake was under way for Daniel Buron, a democratic militant and member of the organization known as Keep-an-Eye-on-Them. Before the end of the day, President Aristide participated in two meetings: one with our compatriots working at the consulate; the other with a group working on safety. [end recording]
