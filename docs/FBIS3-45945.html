Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bangkok, January 18 (XINHUA) -- Thai Prime Minister Chuan Likphai said here today that he hopes new progress and achievements would be scored in Thailand-China bilateral cooperation in the fields of science and technology, economy and trade. The prime minister made the remarks when meeting with Deng Nan, vice minister of the Chinese State Science and Technology Commission this morning. Deng said the ties between China and Thailand are at their best time, which has constituted a good foundation for closer bilateral cooperation in science and technology. During meeting with Deng Monday [17 January], Thai Foreign Minister Prasong Sunsiri said that both Thailand and other ASEAN countries are willing to conduct further technological exchanges and cooperation with China. Prasong expressed his hope that China, under the guidance of Deng Xiaoping's blueprint, will achieve even greater success in national construction. Deng Nan is on a five-day good-will visit to Thailand at the invitation of the Department of Technical and Economic Cooperation under the Office of the Prime Minister. She is to leave for home on Wednesday.
