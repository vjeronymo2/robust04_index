Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 31 KYODO -- Orders won in 1993 by Japan's 50 major construction companies totaled 19.73 trillion yen, an 18.2 percent fall from the previous year and the second consecutive yearly decline, the Construction Ministry announced Monday [31 January]. The rate of decline was the greatest since the ministry started the annual surveys in 1961. Ministry officials attributed the plunge chiefly to a 24.1 percent fall in construction orders from the private sector to 12.11 trillion yen -- the third straight yearly drop. Orders from the manufacturing industries, centered on the automotive and food businesses, posted a 37.1 percent decrease, and those from the nonmanufacturing industries a 21.3 percent fall. Orders from the public sector totaled 6.38 trillion yen, down 7.1 percent for the first fall in eight years. Authorities placed more orders with medium-sized and small construction companies and fewer orders with major firms following the uncovering last year of a series of bribery scandals involving general contractors and local government chiefs.
