920514 FT 14 MAY 92 / Legal aid checks are criticised CRIMINAL legal aid is being granted without proper checks on applicants' eligibility, according to a report by the Commons public accounts committee. The matter was investigated after the qualification by Sir John Bourn, then comptroller and auditor-general, of the 1990-91 appropriation account of the Lord Chancellor's Department. Sir John cited 'uncertainty as to the propriety and regularity' of spending on criminal legal aid. Magistrates' courts - which account for more than half the Pounds 333m spent yearly on criminal legal aid - attracted particular criticism. Expenditure on Criminal Legal Aid. HMSO. Pounds 9.75.
