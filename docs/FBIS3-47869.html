Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] A top official of the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said on 18 January that Japan-U.S. accord on the economic framework talks may be postponed if the United States sticks to its demand for setting numerical targets. The official said: "If the United States adheres to the numerical targets, the two nations are unlikely to reach an accord on the objective criteria during the Hosokawa-Clinton talks in February. In that case, the settlement of the issue should be postponed until the next [G-7] summit in summer."
