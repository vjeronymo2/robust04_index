Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuwait, 6 Jan (KUNA) -- AL-WATAN newspaper today reported that Iraqi President Saddam Husayn has committed a new massacre against leaders from the Ba'th party who broke away from him years ago and sought refuge in Damascus. The paper pointed out that Iraqi intelligence prepared a plan to lure a number of Iraqi opposition leaders living in Syria to the Kurdish area and from there to Mosul, where they were left to contact active elements of the [Iraqi] opposition; then they were arrested. The paper added that the plan succeeded and that death sentences were carried out on at least 10, including Muhamad al-Tai, who was described as a member of the dissident Iraqi regional command. Sources of the dissident Iraqi regional command leadership in Damascus remained silent, but AL-WATAN paper reported these sources as saying that al-Tai acted individually and without orders from the leadership. The paper said it was announced in Mosul that the bodies of five victims have been received, and that the rest are believed to have been killed during interrogation.
