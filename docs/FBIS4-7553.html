BFN [Text] Prague, March 31 (CTK) -- Most Czechs support the middle ground in politics or are right of center; only two percent support the extreme left wing and five percent the extreme right, according to the results of a recent poll conducted by the Institute for Public Opinion Research (IVVM). Respondents were asked to define their position on the political spectrum with numbers from 1, signifying extreme left wing, to seven, the extreme right. Twenty-nine percent described themselves as centrist, 38 percent right of center and 17 percent left of center. Nine percent had no opinion. A higher number of those identifying themselves as right of center are in the 30-44 age group or are entrepreneurs, while the position finds less support among people over 60 and workers. IVVM polled 1,559 people over the age of fifteen.
