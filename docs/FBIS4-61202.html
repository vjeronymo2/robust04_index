BFN [Text] Vilnius, May 04, BNS -- Estonian President Lennart Meri and Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis are positive about the friendship and cooperation treaty that was signed by their Lithuanian and Polish counterparts in Vilnius last week, the Lithuanian president's spokesman, Nerijus Maliukevicius, said. The presidents of the three Baltic states met in Latvia Tuesday [3 May] for a discussion of troop withdrawal and Baltic cooperation issues. Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas returned to Vilnius Wednesday. The summit was strictly confidential. In an interview with Lithuanian TV, Brazauskas said Lithuanian and Latvian views on relations with Russia are quite close, while Estonia's position is different, but he declined to disclose its essence.
