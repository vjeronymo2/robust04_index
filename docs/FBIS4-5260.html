BFN [Text] Liu Shuqing, president of the Chinese People's Institute of Foreign Affairs, who is visiting the ROK, said today that China will oppose to resolve the North Korean nuclear issue by applying sanctions, stressing that to take a countermove of sanctions against North Korea will only complicate the issue. President Liu came to Seoul to hold discussions with the ROK on the detail of forming the ROK-PRC Forum, which will be inaugurated in June. His remarks attract the public eye because he is a core person in charge of China's diplomacy who was vice foreign minister and occupied other important posts. President Liu asked back if any problem had ever been resolved through international sanctions and said that, although the North Korean nuclear issue had occasionally faced obstacles, progress was truly made in resolving the issue through dialogue between North Korea and the United States and between North Korea and the International Atomic Energy Agency. He continued to say that on the ground of this progress, China believes firmly [hwaksin] that the North Korean nuclear issue will be resolved through dialogue. President Liu also said that he had met North Korean President Kim Il-song many times, but he would not play the role of a mediator between South and North Korea for resolving the nuclear issue because there are dialogue channels between the two.
