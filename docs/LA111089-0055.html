November 10, 1989, Friday, Valley Edition

CITY SECTION ROUNDUP;

BORDWELL PASS LIFTS EL CAMINO REAL

Jeff Duva, a former El Camino Real High All-City quarterback, returned to watch his first Conquistadore football game in five years Thursday night and had to be impressed with what he saw.

Duva, who led El Camino Real to the City final in 1972, saw Tony Bordwell complete his first touchdown pass of the year -- a 29-yarder to Ronnu Griffin with 46 seconds to play -- to lift El Camino Real to a 13-7 West Valley League victory over Canoga Park (1-7, 1-5) at Pierce College.

Bordwell, who didn't complete a pass in last week's win over Cleveland and missed on his first six attempts against Canoga Park, finished five for 13 for 72 yards.

Fullback Jamal Anderson registered his second consecutive strong performance in two weeks. Anderson, who rushed for 172 yards and two touchdowns last week, gained 182 yards in 15 carries and scored a touchdown on a 54-yard run in the first period.

Anderson also had a 26-yard touchdown run called back on a holding penalty in the second quarter. El Camino Real (4-4, 3-3 in league play) is 4-0 this season in games in which Anderson has rushed for more than 100 yards. JIM COLEMAN
