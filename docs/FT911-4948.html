910417 FT 17 APR 91 / Letter: A lesson learnt in A-levels Sir, In his article on the shortcomings of the American education system (April 15), Michael Prowse observes that the US has 'nothing comparable with the French baccalaureat, the British A-level or the German Abitur'. Is this the same 'British A-level' for whose abolition Mr Prowse used to campaign so relentlessly in your columns? F. Spooner, 'Meadowside', Corfe Castle, Dorset
