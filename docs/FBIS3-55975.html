Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vadim Byrkin] [Text] Moscow, 12 Jan -- After a long break, Azeri Air Force assault aircraft today again appeared in the skies above Stepanakert. The ITAR-TASS correspondent was told on the phone by the press service of the headquarters of the Nagorno-Karabakh Defense Army that in the afternoon, two assault aircraft tried to bomb Stepanakert, but after response actions by Karabakh antiaircraft forces, they were forced to leave the airspace of Nagorno-Karabakh. But today the Azeri forces' offensive on most sections of the front continued. According to a report by the press service of the military department, on Tuesday evening [11 January], subunits of the Karabakh Defense Army repulsed all attacks and maneuvered successfully, managing to surround a group of the Azeri Army in the area of the village of Akop Kamari in the north of Mardakertskiy [Mardakert] Rayon. Forty-six Azeri soldiers were killed and control was reestablished over a number of heights previously seized by the enemy. In the course of repulsing the attacks on other sections of the front, three infantry companies (up to 270 men and officers) of the Azeri National Army were destroyed, mainly by well-aimed artillery fire. The Karabakh side's losses amounted to six killed and 15 wounded.
