930922 FT 22 SEP 93 / London Stock Exchange: Tesco traded heavily VOLUME in food retailer Tesco jumped to a hefty 13m shares, making it the day's most actively traded stock, as analysts downgraded full-year profits estimates after the company reported interim fig-ures just short of market expectations. Profits came in at Pounds 261.5m, against Pounds 252.8m previously, and the dividend was increased by 9 per cent. However, market forecasts for profits ranged from Pounds 265m to Pounds 270m. Having initially fallen 7 to 208p, the shares steadied to end 4 off at 211p. Reseachers were particularly disappointed by the decline in margins. There was also concern that the company's refocused pricing proposition may lead to stiffer competition from rivals; and there were suggestions that Tesco may have overvalued its property portfolio. Researchers reduced full year profits expectations, with UBS said to have cut its estimate by Pounds 30m to Pounds 600m. Mr David Shriver at NatWest Securities issued a more severe profits downgrade, reducing his year-end estimate by Pounds 42m to Pounds 612m. He said: 'Tesco is now in a position to articulate the way forward; the strategy is clearer but in the short term the impact of investment in margins will not be beneficial to the bottom line.'
