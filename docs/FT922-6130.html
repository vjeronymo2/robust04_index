920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / International Company News: Sumitomo Electric in 6.7 percent decline SUMITOMO Electric Industries, Japan's leading producer of electic wires and cables, reported its first annual profit fall in nine years with pre-tax earnings declining by 6.7 per cent to '35.8bn (Dollars 277.5m), writes John Burton. Net profit in fiscal 1991 dropped by 5.5 per cent to Y19bn. Despite the fall and a prediction that earnings will level off in fiscal 1992, Sumitomo will raise its dividend by Y1 to Y10. Sales grew by 2.4 per cent to Y800.4bn. Revenues from electric wires and cables fell by 3 per cent to Y398.1 because of lower copper prices.
