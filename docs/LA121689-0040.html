December 16, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

NATION IN BRIEF;

GEORGIA;

'NORMAL' FLU SEASON MAY PEAK IN JANUARY

This year's flu season may peak in January, earlier than it did last year, but there is no evidence that it will be abnormally bad, an influenza specialist at the national Centers for Disease Control in Atlanta said. "From the evidence we have, it's a normal flu. What many people don't realize is that normal flu kills," said Dr. Lawrence Schonberger. He said the Type A, or Shanghai, flu had been expected, and it has been showing up across the country. Schonberger said flu shots should have been received in November but that they still can be effective now. It takes about two weeks after vaccination for the treatment to reach its fullest effect.
