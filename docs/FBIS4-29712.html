BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 21 (KCNA) -- The great leader President Kim Il-song gave on-the-spot guidance to the Kumdang Cooperative Farm in Onchon County, South Pyongan Province, on June 19. He was accompanied by So Yun-sok, chief secretary of the South Pyongan Provincial Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea [WPK] and chairman of the South Pyongan Provincial People's Committee, So Kwan-hi, secretary of the WPK Central Committee, Kim Won-chin, chairman of the State Agricultural Commission, Kim Nak-hui, chairman of the South Pyongan Provincial Rural Economy Committee, and other officials concerned. The cooperative farmers of Kumdang reaped a high yield of grain last year by strictly applying the chuche farming method of the WPK, and they have become able to look ahead to a rich harvest this year through vigorous efforts to increase the grain output. President Kim Il-song got first-hand knowledge of crop conditions, going round the fields of the farm and noted with deep satisfaction that the agricultural working people of the Kumdang Cooperative Farm had created good crop conditions by concentrating every effort on farming in hearty response to the party's agriculture-first policy. To do a successful farming is most important in resolving the problems of food, clothing and housing, he said, adding that the agricultural working people must do all farm work on a scientific and technical basis as required by the chuche method of farming, deeply conscious that they are responsible for the rice granaries of the nation. He said various kinds of fertilizers must be applied to crops in a rational ratio in order to increase the grain output. He praised the Kumdang Cooperative Farm for effectively irrigating nonpaddy fields with the introduction of various economic and rational methods of irrigation. All the cooperative farms must mobilize all the irrigation facilities to supply enough water to maize and other crops, he added. He said that in order to increase agricultural production, the leading officials of agriculture must enhance their role and the industrial sector must supply larger quantities of farming materials to the countryside.
