April 28, 1990, Saturday, Valley Edition

COLLEGE ROUNDUP;

CLU RELIEVERS WASTE CLARK'S STRONG EFFORT

Cal Lutheran blew a 4-0 lead en route to a 7-4 loss to Biola in a nonconference NAIA District 3 baseball game Friday at Biola.

Leading, 4-0, after seven innings, Cal Lutheran (23-11, 10-7 in district play) pulled starting pitcher Mike Clark because of an 85-pitch limit imposed by the coaches.

Clark entered the game with 22 consecutive shutout innings and a 3-0 record against Biola.

He allowed only four hits and did not walk a batter.

However, three relief pitchers could not protect the lead.

Cal Lutheran's Peter Washington was three for five with a solo home run in the seventh inning. Bob Keller had two doubles and three runs batted in to lead Biola (13-27, 4-16).

In order to earn a playoff berth, the Kingsmen need to win one of their next three District 3 games.

SOFTBALL

Moorpark 10, Santa Monica 0 -- After losing a Western State Conference game at home for the first time in seven years on Wednesday, Moorpark bounced back against visiting Santa Monica in a game shortened to five innings because of the mercy rule.

The win gave Moorpark (25-9, 12-1) a share of its fourth consecutive conference title and its sixth in the past seven years.

Freshman right-hander Robin Thomas (23-6) limited Santa Monica (5-7 in conference play) to four hits and struck out four.

Catcher Missy Cress hit her third home run, a three-run shot, in Moorpark's five-run fourth inning.

Canyons 5, Pierce 1 -- For the second time this week, Canyons held a WSC opponent to one hit.

Michelle Ruffner threw a one-hitter Wednesday, and freshman right-hander Rebecca Schuster held visiting Pierce to a double in the fourth inning.

Schuster (3-1) struck out three and walked two.

Jill Greenwood, Debbie Rendall, Kelly Kepplinger, Debbie Barnard and Roxana Marta all had two hits for Canyons (20-19, 5-8).

SWIMMING

Western State Conference meet -- The Ventura women are in the lead and the Ventura men are in second place after the second day of the three-day competition at Valley College.

Ventura won the men's 200 medley relay and the women's 400 freestyle relay.

The Valley women are in third and the men in fourth after combining for three individual victories on Friday.

Valley's Ashley Watier won the 50-yard backstroke and teammate Krickett Jones won the three-meter and one-meter diving events.

Bill Fox of Valley was victorious on the one-meter board.

Stuart Blair won the 100 butterfly for Canyons, but the Cougars' men's and women's teams are both in fifth. The Pierce men and women hold down last place.
