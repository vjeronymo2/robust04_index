BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow, 27 May -- When the State Duma looks at the second reading of the draft 1994 budget on 8 June, the Budget, Taxes, Banks, and Finance Committee will recommend that the deputies cut spending on a range of articles in the document being debated. Committee Deputy Chairman Gennadiy Kulik informed correspondents about this today at a news conference. Among other things, he said the committee is in favor of cutting outlay on foreign economic activities by 2 trillion rubles and cutting spending on the state management apparatus by 550-560 billion rubles. Gennadiy Kulik stressed that the committee will recommend that Duma deputies increase defense spending by 3.5-4 trillion rubles, and essential scientific research by 700 billion rubles. Kulik also said that in the draft 1994 budget it is planned to allocate in all 15.3 trillion rubles to the agro-industrial complex. Out of this sum, 5.6 trillion rubles is to be spent on purchasing food at federal and regional level. Gennadiy Kulik also stressed that the committee will advise the Duma to allocate 1 trillion rubles toward the creation of a special leasing fund to sell machinery to agricultural producers in installments.
