920323 FT 23 MAR 92 / Bradford businesses gloomy ORDERS and deliveries among members of Bradford's Chamber of Commerce have fallen in the first quarter of this year, Ian Hamilton Fazey writes. The West Yorkshire town's chamber says its domestic and export markets have been hit. Balance figures - the difference between the percentage of companies reporting rises and those reporting declines - showed sharp changes of trend. The balance for home deliveries fell to 5 percentage points from 13 points in the last quarter of last year, while the balance for home orders was minus 2 points, compared with plus 9 points. Export deliveries were just positive at 1 percentage point, but this compared with 19 points in the last survey. Export orders were affected even more, the balance falling to zero from 19. The survey covered 202 member compaies of the Bradford chamber, spread mainly across textiles, engineering, chemicals, printing, packaging, construction and freight forwarding. They employ more than 16,000 people between them.
