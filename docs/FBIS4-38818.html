BFN [Text] The statistical department attached to the Lithuanian Government has announced some information about the situation in all three Baltic countries. It stated that the lowest inflation in April was in Lithuania -- 1.6 percent -- and the highest in Estonia -- 3.1 percent; inflation in Latvia was 2.7 percent. But throughout the whole of last year, from April 1993 to April 1994, the lowest inflation was in Latvia -- 37.4 percent -- and the highest in Lithuania -- 78.5 percent. Fewer industrial products were sold in April than in March in all three states: in Lithuania, 17 percent less, in Latvia, 15.9 percent less, and in Estonia -- 14.3 percent less. Between April 1993 and April 1994, industrial output declined in Lithuania by 43.6 percent, and in Latvia by 18.8 percent; there is no information about this from Estonia, ELTA [Lithuanian News Agency] reports. The highest unemployment in April was in Latvia -- 6.6 percent; unemployment in Lithuania was 3.6 percent, and in Estonia 2.3 percent.
