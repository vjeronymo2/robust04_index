921102 FT 02 NOV 92 / World News In Brief: Panic faces no-confidence vote Milan Panic, the Yugoslav prime minister, may lose a no-confidence vote in the federal parliament today, after a campaign to discredit him organised by Slobodan Milosevic, the Serbian president. Page 3. Meanwhile, intense machine-gun and mortar fire in the Bosnian capital Sarajevo accompanied the start of the UN-brokered 'Week of Tranquillity' for the former Yugoslavia.
