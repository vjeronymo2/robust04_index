BFN [Text] Beijing, April 26 (XINHUA) -- Acting Premier Zhu Rongji met with visiting Canadian Governor-General Ramon John Hnatyshyn at the Diaoyutai State Guesthouse here this afternoon. Zhu welcomed Hnatyshyn and gave him greetings from Premier Li Peng -- away on a Central Asian official tour. Hnatyshyn said that he was glad to meet Zhu again, whom he met with last may in Canada. Zhu said that Hnatyshyn had very good talks with President Jiang Zemin yesterday, and that he believed that his current visit would further promote the friendly cooperative relations between China and Canada. Touching on bilateral trade relations, Zhu said that China attaches great importance to its trade cooperation with canada, especially in the field of agriculture. Zhu said that China was willing to further its trade relations with Canada, adding that the economies of the two countries are mutually complementary, and the potential for future cooperation was great. The Chinese acting premier said that the economic recovery in canadian regions along the Pacific coast was better than that of the region along the atlantic coast. The reason was that the economic cooperation between canada's Pacific region and Asia had developed faster than that of the other regions in canada. Hnatyshyn agreed with Zhu's views. He stressed that the most favored nations trading status between the two countries was unconditional, adding that developing cooperative relations between Canada and China was in the interests of both countries. Sun Yongfu, chairman of the Chinese Government Reception Committee and vice-minister of railways, and Vice-Foreign Minister Jiang Enzhu, as well as Canadian senior officials accompanying the governor-general, took part in the meeting.
