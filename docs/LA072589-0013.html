July 25, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

MEL BLANC

Laughter is a return to childhood. We forget those who take us there.

Today I learned of Mel Blanc's death and remembered how for four years I was chauffeuring him from his home in Pacific Palisades to the Hanna Barbera Studios for some dubbing.

As we drove down Sunset, I told Mel, weakened by emphysema and forced to breathe with the aid of a portable oxygen machine, how much I had loved Bugs Bunny and Daffy Duck as a boy, but how I had always got Elmer Fudd confused with Porky Pig.

We stopped for a traffic light. Mel removed the nostril clip from his oxygen tube and turning, began reciting the litany of voices we love him for -- Bugs, Daffy, Tweety, Sylvester, Yosemite Sam, Road Runner, the Flintstones and many others.

Inside an idling Rolls-Royce in the next lane, two elegant ladies gaped at the elderly man going through his vocal imitations and the appreciative driver laughing as he listened.

That's why it saddened me to learn, "Th-th-at-'s all, folks."

STEWART LINDH

Los Angeles
