Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN in Japanese 21 January Morning Edition] [Text] At a 20 January news conference Hideaki Kumano, administrative vice-minister of international trade and industry, discussed U.S. intentions to conclude the Japan-U.S. comprehensive economic consultations through ministerial negotiations. Kumano said: "It is not realistic for ministers to allocate time for this issue because, from a common-sense point of view, the political schedule is very tight until 10 February (when Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa visits the United States)."
