BFN [Text] Kiev -- As a UNIAN correspondent learned from confidential sources on 7 June, a special budgetary commission has been created in Ukraine's Cabinet of Ministers, which includes ministers and deputy ministers of the economy, statistics, and finance, and also the chairman of the board of the National Bank of Ukraine. Acting Prime Minister Yukhym Zvyahilskyy will confirm a draft of the commission's work. The commission is to examine the deficit of the new budget until the end of the year (this budget is now being elaborated by the Ministry of Finance), discuss the possibility of reducing the expenditure part of the budget, and to submit proposals on reducing the budget deficit in accordance with IMF demands, which are also being adjusted. As Viktor Yushchenko, the chairman of the National Bank of Ukraine said, as of today, the Ministry of Finance's debt to the state is 50 trillion karbovantsi and if the Ministry of Finance's debts are not confirmed in the new budget there could be an inflationary explosion in Ukraine.
