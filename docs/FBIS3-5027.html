Language: Vietnamese Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet held talks with Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong on the afternoon of 2 March. Attending the talks on the Vietnamese side were Nguyen Manh Cam, minister of foreign affairs; Dau Ngoc Xuan, chairman of the State Committee for Cooperation and Investment; Le Van Triet, minister of commerce; Ho Te, minister of finance; Vu Quoc Chan, assistant to the prime minister; Doan Manh Giao, deputy chairman of the Government Office; Vo Hong Phuc, deputy chairman of the State Planning Committee; and leading officials of the Ministries of Justice, Transportation and Communications, Education and Training, and Building and the Tourist and Post and Telegraph General Departments. Vietnamese Ambassador to Singapore Nguyen Manh Hung also attended the talks. On the Singaporean side were Shunmugan Jayakumar, minister of foreign affairs; Chen Tong Yeo, minister of trade and industry; Teo Chee Hean, minister in charge of finance and communications; Toh Hok Ghim, Singaporean ambassador to Vietnam; and many other high-ranking Singaporean officials. During the talks, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet warmly welcomed Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong, his wife, and other distinguished Singaporean guests on their first official friendship visit to our country, regarding this visit not only as an event of very great important significance for the good frienship and comprehensive cooperation between the two countries but also as an important contribution to the trend of cooperation in the region. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet informed the Singaporean prime minister of the multi-faceted achievements of the Vietnamese renovation process. He reiterated the foreign policy of independence, sovereignty, and openness in the direction of diversification and multilateralization and pointed out that the nation's primary interest is to focus on its relations with countries in the region. The Singaporean prime minister expressed his pleasure at visiting Vietnam for the first time and his profound gratitude to Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet, his wife, and the Vietnamese people for having accorded his delegation a very solemn and friendly welcome. The two sides expressed satisfaction at the fast-breaking and fine development of friendship and cooperation between the countries since the visit to Singapore by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet in October 1991. They unanimously agreed with the need to broaden scientific, technological, cultural, and tourist cooperation because there still remains very great potential for bilateral cooperation. The Vietnamese side highly valued Singapore's policy of attaching due importance to efforts to strengthen its ties with Vietnam, particularly on the economic, trade, and investment fronts. It welcomed Singapore's willingness to share its development experiences with Vietnam and its continued efforts to participate in infrastructure building in Vietnam. The Singaporean side expressed its readiness to cooperate with and assist Vietnam in tourist development, encourage Singaporean business people to invest in local industrial development in Vietnam, exchange international bidding experiences, and help with personnel training. The two sides exchanged views on measures to further promote multi-faceted cooperation between the two countries for the sake of bilateral development and prosperity and for the entire region. They also exchanged views on international and regional issues of common concern. The Singaporean side welcomed Vietnamese participation in ASEAN, regarding this as a positive contribution to cooperation and prosperity in the region. It maintained that through its participation in the ASEAN special cooperation committees, Vietnam will discover appropriate domains for promoting Vietnam-ASEAN cooperation, thereby making preparations for ASEAN membership. The talks proceeded in an atmosphere of friendship, straightfordwardness, and mutual understanding.
