Comment: Western is accelerating repayments to periods far shorter than the average or expected service life of the facilities involved. Capital investments are being amortized over unduly short periods. Response: The PRS program is designed to solve at the lowest rate possible that is consistent with sound business principles. The PRS program is designed to calculate a rate over a 50-year period. However, the program will repay investment in a shorter period of time to minimize interest expense, providing revenue is available to accomplish this. If capital investment repayment was deferred, then interest expense would increase, which could result in a higher rate. Comment: P&hyph;DP has an additional 30 MW of firm capacity because Hoover is providing the P&hyph;DP spinning reserves. However, Western should not transfer revenue to the Hoover project with regard to spinning reserves. Response: Western has researched this matter thoroughly and can find no evidence that Hoover is providing spinning reserves to the P&hyph;DP. Although the Consolidated Marketing Plan anticipated that an additional 30 MW of P&hyph;DP capacity would be available for sale as a result of consolidated operations within the Boulder City Area (now the Phoenix Area), spinning reserve requirements have not changed. The PAO operations department, in conjunction with a consultant on loan from MWD, is continuing to investigate this issue. Any identified benefits to the P&hyph;DP will be reflected in future PRSs. Response: Western does not believe that this comment pertains to or impacts the P&hyph;DP provisional rates. Comment: The customers are concerned that they may be paying twice for the same service since Mead is already part of the P&hyph;DP. Western is already charging Edison $0.624/kW/year for use of the substation under their existing agreement. Response: Western has reviewed the provisions concerning the Mead facilities charges in the P&hyph;DP transmission agreements and has determined that there is no double accounting to the customers for the same capital facilities. In determining Mead facilities charges to Parker-Davis transmission customers, the costs of the Mead facilities, replacements, and O&M expenses are first allocated to the P&hyph;DP based upon the number of functions used. This allocation is further allocated based upon the transmission capacity as stated in the contracts.
