Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Report on interview with Ahmad Quray', head of the PLO Economic Department, by Qol Yisra'el correspondent Sa'd Mu'addi, on 13 March; place not given; from the "With the Events" program] [Text] Commenting on the government's decision to outlaw Kakh, Kahana Hay, PLO Economic Department head Ahmad Quray', also known as Abu-al-'Ala' had the following to say: [Begin Quray' recording] Certainly, it is a good step in the right direction. But it is not enough. It does not come anywhere near equaling the crime that has been committed. A more substantial solution should be sought so an appropriate atmosphere is created for the resumption of negotiations. Measures must be taken around the Ibrahimi Mosque and the locations of settlers. That is what we had expected. [end recording]
