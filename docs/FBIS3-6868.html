Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, March 22 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- Dr. Mahathir Mohamed hopes North Korea will allow United Nations officials to inspect its nuclear sites, currently a subject of tension between the two Koreas. The prime minister, however, suggested that an appropriate approach should be adopted to facilitate the inspection. The approach must be one where they (North Koreans) must not feel they are being ordered to do this and that, he [words indistinct] comment on the current stand-off between North and South Korea on the nuclear issue. South Korea Monday said it would resume preparations for war games with the United States in the face of a renewed threat from the North's to withdraw from a global pact limiting the spread of nuclear arms. Speaking to reporters at the 10th International General Meeting of the Pacific Economic Cooperation Council here, Dr. Mahathir said he did not think North Korea wanted to have a nuclear war. I don't think they want to have a war at all, he said.
