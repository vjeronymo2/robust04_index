Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 18 March Evening Edition] [Text] At a news conference following a cabinet meeting held on the morning of 18 March, Hiroshi Kumagai, international trade and industry minister, commented on U.S. Trade Representative (USTR) Mickey Kantor's request to the Japanese Government to guarantee the realization of Japanese automakers' voluntary plans to import foreign-made auto parts and said: "The government is not in the position of being able to guarantee anything concerning private companies' business activities. We cannot accept it." He then pointed out: "Independent efforts by the private companies are necessary. Even if we decide not to accept it, the United States will accept our decision in its own way." As for the fact that Yutaka Kume, chairman of the Japan Automobile Manufacturers Association, criticized the stand of the Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI], which demands that private industry draw up independent plans, the minister of international trade and industry said, "The government is not in the position of giving compulsory guidance to private industry."
