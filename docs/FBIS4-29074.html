BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 14 KYODO -- A three-member delegation of the Social Democratic Party (SDP) [Social Democratic Party of Japan (SDPJ)] to North Korea returned to Japan Tuesday [14 June] night from a four-day visit to Pyongyang. During its stay in Pyongyang, the delegation, led by House of Representatives member Kozo Watanabe of the second largest opposition party, exchanged views over the suspected nuclear arms development program with officials of the North Korea's workers' party. North Korea told the delegation that the country is not trying to develop nuclear weapons, according to SDP officials. The SDP delegation told the North Korean side that the SDP wants a peaceful settlement of the nuclear issue through talks.
