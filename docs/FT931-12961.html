930126 FT 26 JAN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Tories urge caution on Bosnia THE renewed fighting in Bosnia yesterday triggered fresh warnings from Tory MPs that Britain should be prepared to withdraw its troops at short notice, Philip Stephens writes. With concern growing on the Conservative back benches about the risk to British troops, Mr George Walden, a former minister, caught the general mood with the comment: 'At any moment we have to be ready to pull out.' Mr Peter Viggers, Tory MP for Gosport, warned that British soldiers who had been sent to Bosnia on a humanitarian mission could soon find themselves drawn into a 'peace-keeping' role. He said 'very many' colleagues had told him they agreed the government should keep open the option of early withdrawal.
