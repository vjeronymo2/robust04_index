BFN [Text] The inter-Tajik talks to stabilize the situation in Tajikistan are continuing in Moscow. This was told to a correspondent of the Khovar news agency by Rahim Hasanov, a spokesman of the Tajik Embassy in Russia, yesterday. On the second and third days of the talks, the sides continued the discussion of the agenda with the mediation of Ramiro Piriz-Ballon, the UN secretary general's special envoy. The issues related to achievement of a cease-fire and stopping the acts of provocation on the Tajik-Afghan border were entered on the agenda by proposals of the Tajik Government delegation. Shukurjon Zuhurov, the head of the Tajik Government delegation, proposed that a joint declaration to put an end to hostilities, especially on the Tajik-Afghan border, be adopted in the course of talks as a top priority task. Issues related to repatriation of refugees, disarming illegal bands, and involving all citizens of Tajikistan -- in particular those citizens living outside the territories of the Republic -- in discussion of a draft of a new constitution, are among the issues raised by the sides. The sides are going to thoroughly study each point of the agenda. Today, the sides are going to consult with experts. The representatives of the United Nations, the Foreign Ministries of Russia, Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan, and the representatives of the Embassies of Iran, Afghanistan, and Pakistan are attending the talks as observers.
