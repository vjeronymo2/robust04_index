930614 FT 14 JUN 93 / BSI chief resigns after rift on policy MR MICHAEL SANDERSON, first chief executive of the British Standards Institute, has resigned unexpectedly after 18 months, Charles Batchelor writes. The BSI co-ordinates the writing of standards for industry. Mr Sanderson's resignation, which took effect on June 1 but has only now become known, followed what the BSI called 'a deep disagreement with the board on important matters of policy and management'. He joined BSI from AWD Bedford Trucks, where he had been managing director. 'I am very sad,' he said. 'It was a wonderful job. I think we were making significant progress.' Mr Sanderson, who is now 49, was appointed in November 1991 to spearhead the modernisation of the organisation. The BSI said at the time that his task would be to 'lead a major programme of cultural change'. His departure leaves the organisation without a chief executive at a time of growing interest in the issue of standards. BS5750, a quality systems standard, has become the focus of growing controversy with criticism from small businesses that it is unduly bureaucratic. Neither the BSI nor Mr Sanderson would comment further on the reasons for his departure.
