Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Dialogue between Song Yong-tae, chief South Korean delegate to the working-level contact for the exchange of special envoys and Pak Yong-su, his North Korean counterpart, from closed-circuit television coverage of the 19 March eighth working-level contact in Panmunjon--recorded] [Text] During the North-South working-level contact for the special envoy exchange held today, the North Korean side did not hesitate to make black-mail-type remarks to the effect that it would not decline a war. The following is related footage: [Begin recording] [Pak Yong-su] There is an old-saying that fire should be controlled by fire. We do not want war but will never shun your side's insisting on a war. We are also ready to react [taehang]. However, Mr. Song, your side has to deeply consider the dear price of the war. Seoul is not far from here. If a war breaks out, it will be a sea of fire. Mr. Song, it will probably be difficult for you to survive. [Song Yong-tae] What are you talking about? [Pak] Wait, therefore... [Song] Then, do you think we will just sit back? [Pak] Regarding this, you have to deeply consider things. [Song] Are you declaring a war? [Pak] I am telling you that your side declared a war. Are you listening? Did you take a nap? [Song] What? Respond to war with war? [Pak] That's right. Clearly. [Song] Why do you talk about war at the place of talks? [Pak] Because your side declared a war. [end recording]
