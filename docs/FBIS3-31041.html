Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Zagreb, Feb 12 (AFP) -- Croatia is prepared to withdraw "elements" of its army currently fighting in Bosnia if the UN pledges to guarantee the security of Croat enclaves in central Bosnia, Foreign Minister Mate Granic said Saturday [12 February]. Granic told journalists he made the proposal in a letter Friday to United Nations Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali. As well as proposing the withdrawal of "elements" of its army deployed south of the southern Bosnia-Herzegovina city of Mostar, Zagreb also called on the UN to set up frontier controls on the border between Bosnia and Croatia. "At the same time, Croatia is asking for guarantees from the UN concerning the security of the Croat enclaves in central Bosnia," Granic added. The troops in question were deployed on both sides of the Neretva River along a 27-kilometre (16-mile) corridor between the border and the town of Buna, south of Mostar, Granic added. Granic said the troops stationed east of the river were Croatian Army regulars, and those on the western bank were "former members of the Croatian Army" fighting as "volunteers" alongside Bosnian Croat forces. On February 3, the UN Security Council called on Croatia to immediately withdraw all its regular forces from Bosnia or face international sanctions. The UN has estimated between 3,000 and 5,000 Croatian regulars are fighting in Bosnia. Zagreb has denied the allegation, but admitted it has deployed troops south of Mostar and in the hinterland of the Croatian Adriatic port of Dubrovnik, and that around 2,000 Croatian military "volunteers" are active inside Bosnia.
