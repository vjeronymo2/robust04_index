The window period for filing applications for Channel 282C2 at Omak, Washington, will open on Dec. 5, 1994, and close on Jan. 5, 1995. 47 CFR Part 73 [MM Docket No. 93&hyph;319; RM&hyph;8404] Radio Broadcasting Services; Omak, WA AGENCY: Federal Communications Commission. ACTION: Final rule.

SUMMARY: The Commission, at the request of Omak Community Broadcasters, allots Channel 282C2 at Omak, Washington, as that community's second local FM transmission service. See 59 FR 2344, January 14, 1994. Channel 282C2 can be allotted to Omak in compliance with the Commission's minimum distance separation requirements with a site restriction of 16.5 kilometers (10.2 miles) south to avoid short-spacings to vacant Channel 281B, Trail, British Columbia and Station KAFE, Channel 282C2, Bellingham, Washington. The coordinates for channel 282C2 at Omak are north Latitude 48&hyph;15&hyph;44 and West Longitude 119&hyph;31&hyph;58. Since Omak is located within 320 kilometers (200 miles) of the U.S.-Canadian border, concurrence of the Canadian government has been obtained. With this action, this proceeding is terminated. DATES: Effective Dec. 5, 1994. The window period for filing applications for Channel 282C2 at Omak, Washington, will open on Dec. 5, 1994, and close on Jan. 5, 1995.
