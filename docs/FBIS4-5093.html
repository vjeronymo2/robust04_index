BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 5 (KCNA) -- It is 40 years since the revolutionary martyrs' cemetery was built. The great leader President Kim Il-song saw to it that the cemetery was built on April 5, 1954. The cemetery is located on top of the Jujak peak of Mt. Taesong in Pyongyang. Lying buried there are anti-Japanese revolutionary martyrs including Comrade Kim Chong-suk, an indomitable communist revolutionary fighter, who dedicated their precious lives to the anti-Japanese revolutionary struggle and the struggle for the victory of the socialist cause under the wise leadership of President Kim Il-song. In the cemetery there are a monument bearing the autograph of President Kim Il-song, bronze busts of martyrs, and a wreath-laying stand on which the medal of hero of the DPRK is embossed. The cemetery was reconstructed on an expansion basis into a grand monumental edifice on the occasion of the 40th anniversary of the founding of the Workers' Party of Korea (October 10, 1985) under the energetic and meticulous guidance of the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il. The cemetery is a symbol of the deep trust and sense of obligation of President Kim Il-song and Comrade Kim Chong-il for the anti-Japanese revolutionary martyrs, and high appreciation of the exploits of the martyrs who remained loyal to the party and the leader, the country and the people till their last moments. A meeting was held to mark the 40th anniversary of the establishment of the cemetery. At the meeting, Pak Song-chol, Politburo member of the C.C. [Central Committee], the Workers' Party of Korea and vice-president, conveyed a message of thanks of the WPK Central Committee to the officials and employees of the management office of the revolutionary martyrs' cemetery. The message highly praises the officials and employees of the management office for their responsible management of the cemetery and education of people through the cemetery over the last 40 years.
