931023 FT 23 OCT 93 / Abramsky to head new BBC service MS JENNY ABRAMSKY has been appointed controller of the new BBC radio news and sport network, due to be launched on March 28 next year, Raymond Snoddy writes. Ms Abramsky has been editor of news and current affairs for BBC radio since 1987. Mr Mike Lewis, head of sports and outside broadcasts for BBC radio, is to be deputy controller. The appointments coincide with the introduction of a new management structure in BBC news and current affairs combining radio and television resources. Mr Peter Bell, now editor of television news programmes, will become responsible for all news output. BBC Enterprises said yesterday that deals worth Pounds 10m were negotiated during the media market at Cannes, including the sale of Thatcher: The Downing Street Years.
