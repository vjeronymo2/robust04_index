Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Le Van Triet, Vietnamese minister of commerce and tourism, reiterated his country's promise to send 100,000 tons of rice during 1994 as part of a cooperation agreement and mercantile exchange between the two countries. During a meeting with Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina, the Vietnamese minister was optimistic as to the renewal process of the Cuban economy and underscored that his country understands the importance of maintaining the trade agreement. Robaina also met with Nguyen Cong Tan, minister of agriculture and food industry. Both sides expressed their satisfaction with the growing atmosphere of mutually beneficial cooperation between the two countries. Nguyen Cong Tan came out in favor of boosting cooperation not only in agriculture but also in, among other spheres, construction, health, education, science, biotechnology, and maritime transport. Robaina also met with (Nguyen May), vice chairman of the State Commission for Cooperation and Investment, who came out in favor of joint enterprises in construction and other areas before exploring other fields. Roberto Robaina met with Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. He also held talks with his counterpart, Nguyen Manh Cam. Robaina's agenda includes a meeting with the general secretary of the Vietnamese Communist Party and a news conference to wind up his tour of Asia. Foreign Minister Robaina announced his next tour, in April, through Argentina, Uruguay, Peru, Ecuador, Chile, and Paraguay and the possibility of visiting Arab countries
