BFN [Text] Seoul, April 4 (YONHAP) -- The Korea-Russia Far East Siberia Association met Monday morning at the Korea Chamber of Commerce and Industry Building to discuss boosting cooperation. The association is led by Maritime Province Governor Yevgeniy Nazdratenko of Russia and Chang Chi-hyok, chairman of South Korea's Kohap Group. At the meeting, the association agreed to construct an industrial complex for Korean-Russian cooperation and an exclusive quay for South Korean ships at Vostochny port in the Nakhodka special economic zone. The association will also recommend that the Governments of South Korea and Russia designate small special economic zones elsewhere in the Russian Far East and Siberia. The association plans to obtain permission to establish a regular direct line between Seoul and Vladivostok and study ways to open another direct line between South Korea and Irkutsk, Novosibirsk and Kamchatka Provinces. Meanwhile, the association decided to hold regular exhibitions of Korean goods in several Siberian and Far Eastern cities including Khabarovsk and Yuzhno Sakhalinsk as well as seminars and working-level talks between South Korean and Russian businesses.
