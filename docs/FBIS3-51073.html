Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Navy gave a leave of absence for almost 10 days to the majority of its personnel in order to make savings and to alleviate a difficult economic situation "caused by the current circumstances," Navy sources have told BUSQUEDA. The managerial decision, which is known as "general leave," was ordered by the Navy command for the period from 24 December to 2 January. Essential Navy services were not discontinued. A government source said that the Navy was facing "serious" economic problems, noting that "it has debts" and "had asked the government for an advance, which was not granted."
