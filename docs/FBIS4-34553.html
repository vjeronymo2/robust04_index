BFN [Text] Cartagena de Indias, 13 Jun (EFE) -- On 13 June, Chilean President Eduardo Frei said he had come to the Fourth Ibero-American Summit to "reassert Chile's Latin American spirit," a basic issue in its foreign policy. Frei recalled that during his presidential electoral campaign, he stated that Latin America would be the main issue in his foreign policy with prospects for political and economic integration. He added that he has continued to uphold and put this into practice since he came to power on 11 March. Frei told EFE: "It is true that Chile is a country open to the world, that it is going to join the South Pacific Economic Cooperation Conference (APEC), and that the EU is our main commercial partner, but, regardless of this, Chile has a Latin American spirit and we have come to reassert this." Frei added that sound evidence of this are the recent proposals for a partnership with the Common Market of the South (Mercosur) and the proposals Chile made during the recent OAS General Assembly in Brazil. Upon joining the Fourth Ibero-American Summit, which will last two days in Cartagena de Indias, he said Chile will participate with the hope -- "and will make every effort possible in this direction" -- that it will mark the starting point, "in practice rather than in theory," of a real integration of the hemisphere. The summit "must be the forum that will ensure Latin America's transformation into a true community of nations in which support for democracy, human rights, the struggle to overcome poverty, and economic integration prevail." In Frei's opinion, the main thing to attaining these goals is commercial opening through negotiated agreements "with a spirit of open regionalism" that will cast aside protectionism and grant the continent access to the major markets, "free of exclusions and closed blocs." The Chilean chief of state stressed: "This is what we have come to propose at this Fourth Ibero-American Summit, and we will do so with determination and an American spirit." Upon his arrival in Cartagena de Indias, Frei offered Chile's cooperation in rebuilding the towns of Caucas and Huilas, which were devastated last week by the landslide caused by a tremor. Frei reconfirmed this offer to EFE. Chile has already shipped 200 metric tons of aid for the tragedy's victims, but Frei said: "We have talked to the Colombian authorities about cooperating in the most difficult aspects, that is, to rebuild the towns." Frei added: "Unfortunately, Chile has great experience in natural disasters and will place this experience at the service of our Colombian brothers." Shortly after his arrival, the Chilean president held a bilateral meeting with King Juan Carlos of Spain, whom he invited to visit Chile again, specifically the Antarctic, which is one of the king's "great desires." The respective foreign ministries will immediately start working on the initiative to set a date, according to Frei, who said he had asked for an audience with the king "for the affection and support he always has granted Chile." Eduardo Frei is scheduled to hold two more bilateral meetings in Cartagena de Indias: one with Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada on 15 June and one with Cuban President Fidel Castro on 16 June. Frei said the two meetings will have an open agenda to assess the state of bilateral relations and cooperation prospects, but dismissed the possibilities that an agreement to resume diplomatic relations will be reached during either of the two meetings.
