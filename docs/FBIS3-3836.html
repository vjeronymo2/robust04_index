Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 24 (XINHUA) -- South Korean President Kim Yong-sam left here this morning for official visits to Japan and China aimed at improving cooperation with the two countries. In a speech at the Seoul airport, Kim said that he will discuss with Japanese and Chinese leaders cooperation among the three countries as well as peace and stability in eastern Asia, including the nuclear issue on the Korean Peninsula. He said that Japan and China were geographically and historically South Korea's close neighbors, and that the three will become partners in the forthcoming Asia-Pacific era. He pointed out that interdependence among nations is increasingly important, especially since the Uruguay Round of multilateral trade talks of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade reached an agreement which will bring about fierce competition in world trade. This situation highlights the significance of close cooperation among South Korea, Japan and China in seeking joint stability and prosperity, he stated. The three countries can benefit from complimentary cooperation to improve their own competitiveness as they have different business structures and are in different stages of development, President Kim stressed. He added that Japan is South Korea's second largest trading partner, while China is the third largest and has attracted most of South Korea's overseas investment. Kim is expected to meet with Japanese Emperor Akihito and Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, address the Japanese parliament, and visit Waseda University. He is scheduled to leave Japan on March 26 for Shanghai, China's largest city, and will remain in China for five days. During his stay he will hold talks with President Jiang Zemin, Premier Li Peng and other top Chinese leaders. President Kim will also visit the economic development zones in Shanghai and Tianjin and deliver a speech at Beijing University.
