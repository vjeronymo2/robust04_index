Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Here is a special news report following a terrorist attack in the Binyamin area. There was a shooting attack near the settlement of 'Ateret. According to initial reports, two Egged bus passengers were seriously injured. Our correspondent in the territories Golan Yokhpaz reports from the studio: There was a shooting attack near the settlement of 'Ateret, which is situated north of Jerusalem. Apparently two Israelis were seriously injured and three others were also wounded, although their condition remains unclear. The Israelis were hit by gunfire directed at an Egged No. 468 bus which drove by the settlement of 'Ateret in the Binyamin area on the Bi'r Zayt bypass. The bus, was travelling from Jerusalem to Tel Aviv and picking up passengers from settlements in the Binyamin area. Seven Israeli ambulances are currently racing to the scene in order to evacuate the wounded, apparently to Hadassah-'En Kerem Hospital in Jerusalem. Large Army forces are also making their way to the scene of the attack in order to treat the wounded and close off the site. We have just received an update on the condition of the wounded: five are currently receiving first aid at the scene and initial reports indicate that two of the wounded are in serious condition and will soon be evacuated to hospital.
