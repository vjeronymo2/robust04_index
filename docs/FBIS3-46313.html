Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [By correspondent Hidetoshi Kaneko] [Text] Beijing, 25 Dec -- Commenting on the issue of North Korea's refusal to permit international inspections of its nuclear sites, a high-ranking Chinese Foreign Ministry official said on 25 December, "desirable signs have already appeared." The Chinese official's remarks strongly hinted that North Korea may be ready to accept international inspections. The official also disclosed that China is poised to cooperate with the new economic policy that North Korea has adopted. China has publicly taken a neutral position on the North Korean nuclear issue. In fact, however, China is said to have urged North Korea to accept international inspections. The Chinese official was confident of progress in U.S.-North Korea dialogue, saying, "Compared with the situation six months ago, (U.S.-North Korea) dialogue is making steady headway." He also said: "The Workers' Party of Korea recently held a plenary session of its Central Committee to adopt a very important policy of rebuilding North Korea and its economy. This policy is noteworthy." The official spoke highly of the "three-first policy North Korea has adopted to develop trade, agriculture and light industry." The high-ranking Chinese official said: "I believe that China will continue to develop its normal, cooperative relations with North Korea based on the principle of equality and reciprocity." His comments made it clear for the first time that China will cooperate with North Korea in rebuilding Pyongyang's economy.
