BFN [Text] Knin, 13 Apr (TANJUG) -- Milan Martic, the president of the republic of Serb Krajina [RSK], sent a letter today to UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] Commander General Bertrand de Lapresle expressing his surprise after being informed of UNPROFOR's intentions to deploy UN troops along the state border between the RSK and the Autonomous Province of Western Bosnia [APWB]. Such a deployment, Martic says in his letter, is absolutely unacceptable and out of the question for us. The same goes for other RSK borders except those along demarcation lines with the Republic of Croatia, in accordance with the Zagreb agreement. Reminding UNPROFOR Commander de Lapresle that he was one of the signatories of the Zagreb cease-fire agreement, in his letter Martic also points out: The governments of the RSK and the APWB have signed an agreement on friendship and cooperation; there have been no incidents in the border area and there is no need for your presence in this area, Martic says in conclusion.
