Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Ecuadoran economic developments monitored through 11 January. The source is cited in parentheses following each item. The National Secretariat for Administrative Development, SENDA, reported that during 1993, 25,198 public employees stopped working for the government. According to SENDA, the state thus reduced its size by reducing appropriations, eliminating vacant positions, and not renewing contracts. This is part of the effort to modernize the administration. SENDA reported that in August 1992 the number of public servants was 366,227, and by 31 December 1993 the figure dropped to 341,773. (Paris AFP in Spanish 2132 GMT 4 Jan 94) The European Union, EU, approved a donation for approximately $2.8 million to finance social welfare and ecological protection programs. An EU spokesman said that the aid will permit the implementation of a cholera prevention program to be carried out by French experts and Health Ministry officials. He added that an ecological evaluation will also be conducted within the program on the effects of oil exploitation in the jungle. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 4 Jan 94) The Ecuadoran State Petroleum Enterprise, Petroecuador, announced that a barrel of crude was sold at an average daily price of $10.27 in December, thus making the average yearly price $14.45 in 1993. In addition, the Energy Ministry reported that the 1993 Ecuadoran oil production figure was approximately 124.8 million barrels, 6 percent more than the preceding year. According to provisional figures, average daily production was 341,700 barrels in 1993 in comparison to 334,400 barrels in 1992. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 4 Jan 94) Petroecuador reported that a new oil well, which initially produced 500 barrels per day, was discovered in the Amazon region. The well is located near Auca well, eastern Ecuador, which accounts for 80 percent of the country's oil production. Petroecuador President Federico Veintimilla said that an intense exploration program will continue in 1994. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1635 GMT 6 Jan 94) Energy and Mines Minister Francisco Acosta stated that Texaco has filed suit against the Ecuadoran Republic for more than $500 million. He also said that the government has asked the firm for compensation based on technical, tax, and ecological reasons. Acosta noted that an agreement could be reached relating to Ecuadoran interests and recognition of compensation for Ecuador. Rodrigo Perez, the Texaco representative in Ecuador, said that an agreement has been reached in principle on the technical and tax aspects. With regard to the ecological issue, he said that the results of a survey are being reviewed. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 6 Jan 94) Ecuadoran trade showed a positive balance between January and October 1993 of $693 million, compared to $745 million for the same period in 1992. The Central Bank disclosed that exports amounted to $2.4 billion and imports were $1.7 billion in 1993 compared to $2.5 billion in exports and $1.790 billion in imports during the first 10 months of 1992. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 6 Jan 94) President Sixto Duran-Ballen authorized the issuance of government bonds for more than $909 million to cover the balance of the foreign debt which must be serviced by the country's Central Bank. A presidential decree published in the Official Registry stated that the bonds will be issued in several currencies and will be paid in sucres. This bond issue will be used to service the foreign debt with commercial banks, the World Bank, and the Export, Import Bank of Japan, the government newspaper noted. The Ecuadoran foreign debt amounts to more than $12.6 billion. According to the Official Registry, the bond issue will allow the government to cover pending balances for several credits. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 11 Jan 94) President Sixto Duran-Ballen authorized the issuance of three government bonds in sucres for an equivalent of $272.6 million to capitalize the Ecuadoran Central Bank, an executive decree stated. According to the decree, a bond will be issued for 17.8 billion sucres, for two years, including a grace period of one year, at the basic interest rate that is in effect at the beginning of each quarter. The amortization will be made in two equal and consecutive biannual quotas that will expire on 30 June and 31 December 1995. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 11 Jan 94)
