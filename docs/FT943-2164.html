940921 FT 21 SEP 94 / Bank to regulate the Irish SE The Central Bank of Ireland is to take over regulation of the country's stock market after the splitting of the Irish stock exchange from the London stock exchange, scheduled for next April, the government announced yesterday. Under the Stock Exchange Act, due to become law by the end of this year, the Irish stock exchange and its 10 member firms will have to apply to the central bank for permission to trade. However, during the transition period they will be considered as approved while their applications are being dealt with. In order to be approved the Irish stock exchange will have to become a limited company and its council replaced by a new board. The Irish stock exchange has indicated that it intends to adhere to the rules of the London stock exchange following the split.
