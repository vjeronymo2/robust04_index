February 10, 1990, Saturday, Southland Edition

ROMANIA INTERIM COUNCIL VOTES TO EXPAND

The Council of National Unity, formed to give all registered political parties a say in governing Romania until the May elections, held its first meeting Friday and decided to expand itself by a third to 241 members.

Interim President Ion Iliescu, who led organizational talks in the former National Assembly building, apparently will lead the council, but no formal vote was taken.

The Assembly building is being used for the first time since the uprising against Communist dictator Nicolae Ceausescu, who was overthrown Dec. 22 and executed with his wife, Elena, on Christmas Day. It also is the first time since the revolution that opposition groups have had a role in leadership.

After heated debate on whether new organizations representing ethnic minorities should be admitted, the council voted to expand its size from 180 to 241. A statement issued later said some delegates wanted the new groups limited to observer status.

As envisaged in the original 180-seat council, political parties and the non-party bloc each were to get 90 seats.

In the expanded 241-seat Council agreed upon Friday, 105 seats will go to 35 parties. Nine organizations representing ethnic minorities will get 27 seats, three will go to former political prisoners and 106 are assigned to prominent Romanians who were part of the National Salvation Front.
