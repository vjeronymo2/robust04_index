Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum, January 15 (XINHUA) -- 'Umar Hassan Ahmad al-Bashir, president of Republic of Sudan, met with the visiting Chinese Vice Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen here this afternoon. Al-Bashir said during the meeting that Sudan and China have strong, traditional and friendly ties. He hoped to promote the bilateral political and economic relations and increase the visits of officials and non-government personnel between the two countries. The Sudanese president also focused the strengthening of economic and technological cooperation between the two countries in the new world situation. Al-Bashir said Sudan is a big market. He welcomed the investment from Chinese enterprisers and the establishment of companies in various fields. Qian Qichen, on his leg of six-nation African tour, said his visit aimed at further promoting the Sino-Sudanese relations. He expressed satisfaction at political, economic and cultural developments between the two countries. He said Sudan is among the first group of countries that established diplomatic relations with China. The relations between the two countries have been healthy and have not been affected by the changes in the international communities. Qian conveyed Chinese President Jiang Zemin's greetings to the Sudanese president during the meeting. The Chinese foreign minister also held a talk with his Sudanese counterpart Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih later today. Qian's six-nation tour has already taken him to Ethiopia and Eritrea and will continue on to Kenya, Madagascar and Mauritius. The Chinese vice premier and foreign minister left Khartoum this evening for Kenya.
