930318 FT 18 MAR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Labour on attack over BCCI affair LABOUR LAST night launched a fresh attack on the handling of the liquidation of the collapsed Bank of Credit and Commerce International, urging the government to take the process 'by the scruff of its neck' and ensure value for money for interested parties. Mr Stuart Bell, Labour's City spokesman, called on the government to use its influence on the liquidators to reduce their costs - which he said worked out at Pounds 640,000 a week since their appointment - to acceptable levels and to speed up the pace at which the liquidation was proceeding.
