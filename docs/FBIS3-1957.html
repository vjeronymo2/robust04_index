Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Nanjing, March 2 (XINHUA) -- Output of freshwater fish in east China's Jiangsu Province set the highest record in history last year, enabling the province to become the country's second with freshwater-fish production exceeding one million tons. As a famous time-honored land of rice and fish, Jiangsu has rich aquatic resources with 1.7 million ha [hectares]. In total acreage of fresh water. During the past decade, the provincial government has boosted the development of freshwater fish-farming as part of the efforts to readjust agricultural structure and increase farmers' income. To meet the demand of markets, importance has been given to raising of rare and special species, and artificial cultivation of crabs, pearls, eels and soft-shelled turtles, which have become new pillars of local freshwater farming. Based on the burgeoning of aquatic breeding, new economic entities combining cultivation, processing and export of aquatic products have come into being and effectively promoted the expansion of export-oriented fish-farming. By now, annual exports of aquatic products from Jiangsu have reached 20,000 tons with total profits worth 100 million U.S. dollars. Jiangsu has also stressed spread of technology in order to gain high quality, high efficiency and high output in aquatic breeding.
