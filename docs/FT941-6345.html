940302 FT 02 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television Teenagers born in the 1980s (yes, there really are such things) will presumably feel as nostalgic about The Rock'n'Roll Years - The 80s as we used to about the early series which harked back to the 50s and 60s. The new series begins with the year when Charles and Di were married and Ronald Reagan became American president:1981 (7.00 BBC1). That is followed by the first edition of Here And Now, a BBC1 current affairs programme offering three film reports in 30 minutes: a format which has been used astonishingly little in Britain. Today's reports include an update on 'Little Irma', the Bosnian girl whose much-publicised flight to London had nothing to do with improving certain people's political images .(7.30). Dispatches reports that coal mines are being closed to make the industry an attractive privatisation prospect (9.00 C4). BBC2 shows the drama/movie Genghis Cohn in its 'Screen Two' slot: a story of a Nazi possessed by the ghost of a Jewish comedian, starring Anthony Sher and Robert Lindsay (9.00).
