920919 FT 19 SEP 92 / Sterling and the UK Economy: Balloon expenses inflated A DELAY in the start of a transatlantic balloon race increased its cost by Dollars 10,000 because of the sterling crisis, Mr Alan Noble, the race director, said. Five teams took off from Maine in the US on Wednesday after he used his credit card to pay a Dollars 50,000 bill for facilities during a one-month wait for improved weather. As the balloonists floated over the Atlantic, sterling fell and Mr Noble expects his bill to be higher. A race official in Rotterdam said the British were fourth behind the Dutch, Belgians and Americans. The Germans trailed.
