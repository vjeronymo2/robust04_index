August 6, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OFFSHORE DRILLING AND OIL TANKERS

Your editorial ("More Preventable Spills," June 27) on oil spills rightly emphasized prevention as the first line of defense against spills. However, you incorrectly suggested that oil companies are neglecting preventive measures in favor of spill containment.

To the contrary, we view prevention as critical in reducing pollution from tankers. The industry's new $250 million oil spill program places a heavy emphasis on avoiding tanker accidents through recommended changes in our own tanker operations and in government activities regarding tankers.

Our proposals include a preventive step that you recommend: mandatory participation in the Coast Guard advisory vessel traffic systems and the possible expansion of these systems into ports and harbors that need them. We also are calling for study of alternative tanker designs such as double bottoms, double sides and smaller tanks for vessels by an independent body such as the National Academy of Sciences. We are proposing other preventive measures including requiring maneuvering assistance from tugboats where extraordinary navigation hazards exist, stricter pilot standards, better training for tanker personnel and drug and alcohol testing.

The industry also is creating a new spill response organization for U.S. waters -- designed particularly for major spills but on call for any spill -- and it will fund $30 million to $35 million worth of research in prevention and cleanup over the next five years.

V. KENNETH LEONARD

Transportation Director

American Petroleum Institute

Washington, D.C.
