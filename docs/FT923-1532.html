920923 FT 23 SEP 92 / World Trade News: Taiwan carrier confirms Dollars 345m Boeing contract EVA Airways, Taiwan's first private international carrier, yesterday confirmed a Dollars 345m (Pounds 194.9m) order to Boeing for two 747-400 passenger jets. This follows previous firm orders for six of the widebody jets, and two extra options. The airline, a subsidiary of Evergreen Group, which owns the world's biggest container shipping company, began operations in July 1991 with a fleet of Boeing 767-300ERs, flying to eight destinations in the Asia-Pacific region, and to Vienna. EVA will take delivery of the first two 747-400s in November, and intends to use the aircraft on its first trans-Pacific service to Los Angeles in December. The Dollars 345m includes spares and training. The jets will be powered by General Electric engines. Taiwan and Russia have agreed to establish air links to boost trade and tourism, foreign ministry officials said. Reuter reports from Taipei. Taiwan's Vice-Foreign Minister John Chang and Mr Oleg Lobov, visiting chairman of the Russian President's Export Council, had signed a memorandum on setting up air links, the officials added.
