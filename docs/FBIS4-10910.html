BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on El Salvadoran economic developments monitored through 19 April. In 1993, the Inter-American Development Bank, IDB, approved $109 million in loans to El Salvador. According to an official spokesperson, this money was used for social projects and infrastructure plans coordinated by various government offices. (San Salvador Radio Cadena YSU in Spanish 1800 GMT 14 Apr 94) The Central Bank has reported that in the first three months of 1994 Salvadoran families received $223.2 million from family members living abroad. According to the bank, the Salvadoran economy was kept afloat throughout the civil war years by money Salvadorans living abroad sent their family members. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1903 GMT 18 Apr 94) The local press has reported that Japan has approved a $99 million loan to El Salvador to rebuild bridges destroyed during the 12 years of civil war. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2020 GMT 18 Apr 94)
