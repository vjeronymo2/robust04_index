BFN [Text] Baghdad, June 16 (XINHUA) -- Iraqi President Saddam Husayn received here today Chinese Assistant Foreign Minister Wang Changyi and held talks with him on friendly Sino-Iraqi relations and regional and international issues of common interest. The Chinese official, who is currently on a visit to Iraq, conveyed Chinese leaders' regards to Saddam Husayn and said that the Chinese Government attaches importance to consolidating and developing relations with Iraq. Saddam, who is also Iraqi prime minister, asked the Chinese assistant foreign minister to convey his regards to Chinese leaders. He voiced his hope that the friendly relations and cooperation between Iraq and China will develop and continue. He gave an account of Iraq's position on the U.N. Security Council resolutions concerning the Gulf issues. The Chinese official reiterated that China stands for the implementation of the U.N. Security Council resolutions, including gradually lifting the embargo according to that implementation. Wang arrived here on June 14 on a working visit to Iraq. During this visit, Wang met with Iraqi Deputy Prime Minister Tariq 'Aziz, Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf and the Minister of Industry and Minerals Husayn Kamil Hasan. He also held talks with First Undersecretary of the Iraqi Foreign Ministry Abd-el-Gabbar al-Dawri [name as received].
