BFN [Excerpt] Baghdad, 21 Jun (INA)--Iraqi and Sudanese officials began talks on transportation here this morning. The Iraqi side was headed by Transport and Communications Minister Ahmad Murtada Khalil and the Sudanese side was headed by Transport Minister 'Uthman 'Abd-al-Qadir 'Abd-al-Latif. Dr. Ahmad Murtada delivered a speech in which he asserted that Iraq was eager to promote cooperation between the two fraternal countries in the areas of railways, ports, airports, and land and sea transportation. For his part, 'Uthman 'Abd-al-Qadir 'Abd-al-Latif voiced the thanks and gratitude of the Sudanese leadership and people to Iraq for extending support in the areas of transportation, communications, and training and qualifying Sudanese cadres. He also referred to ties of friendship and cooperation between the two friendly countries and to the pan-Arab positions both countries adopt to foil plots concocted by the imperialists and reactionaries to harm our Arab nation. The talks centered on cooperation between the two sides in the areas of railway networks, ports, roads, land and sea transportation, telecommunications system, the exchange of expertise, and training employees.
