BFN [Text] Budapest, 9 May (MTI) -- The National Election Committee (NEC), as obliged by law, on Monday afternoon announced official preliminary results for the first round of parliamentary elections in Hungary. Accordingly, 5,480,056 of the 7,952,445 eligible voters cast their ballots on Sunday, which is a voter turnout of 68.91 percent. Two individual candidates won seats in Parliament in the first round: Mrs Rado, nee Ildiko Gyarfas (Hungarian Socialist Party) from the No. 7 constituency of Borsod-Abauj-Zemplen county, with 50.91 percent of the votes cast, and Gyorgy Keleti (Hungarian Socialist Party) from the No. 3 constituency in Kisber, Komarom-Esztergom county, with 51.41 percent of the votes.
