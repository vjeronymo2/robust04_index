BFN [Text] In a statement to Channel 3 of Algiers radio, Prime Minister Mokdad Sifi answered a number of questions on various topics such as the national economic and social council, which he described as a step forward. The prime minister stated that the National Transitional Council will be installed soon and that sufficient time has been given to all parties to consider the issue of participating in the council. Regarding the priorities of his government's agenda, Mr. Sifi stressed three points: economic stimulation, the strengthening of the state's institutions, and national solidarity. In his reply to a question on the possibility of calling early elections before the end of the transitional period, the prime minister said that the matter depends mainly on the existence of stable conditions. On the agreement with the IMF, which he preferred to call the Algerian restructuring program, which was accepted by the IMF, the prime minister said that it has positive and negative effects and that we should wait for the talks with the Paris Club and the duration of the debt rescheduling period. He added that there is a plan to help low-income social classes. Regarding Algeria's educational system, the prime minister expressed hope that it would be changed from within the regime itself with the participation of the administration, staff, and parents. In conclusion, the prime minister said that he prefers a policy of consultation with the administration and all parties.
