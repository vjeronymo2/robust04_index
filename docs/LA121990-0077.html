December 19, 1990, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

MAIL CALL: If your post office looks busier than usual this holiday season, blame Operation Desert Shield. In a 24-hour period Friday, the central mail facility in Santa Ana processed 91,700 packages -- a 20% increase over last year, with most boxes heading for the Persian Gulf, spokeswoman Christine Dugas said. Still, Dugas vowed that first-class mail sent through Thursday will arrive before Christmas. . . . After that, she recommends overnight Express Mail. Lanie Jones and John O'Dell
