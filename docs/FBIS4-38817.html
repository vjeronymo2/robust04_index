BFN [Text] The presidents of the three republics in Central Asia, Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan will hold a [words indistinct] During the summit, questions on economic cooperation will be examined. Bolat Ahmadgaliyev, from the Kazakh president's office, spoke about this to journalists. It is known that after the breakdown of the former [Soviet] Union the leaders of the Central Asian republics took a number of steps to begin cooperation in the economic field and in many other fields. The difficulties of transferring to a market economy, leaving the problems of monetary unity in the republics untackled, the breakdown of economic ties, and other reasons hindered the process of economic cooperation between the Central Asian republics from fully forging ahead. The situation has now changed. The problems in the process of development are now clear. If Uzbekistan is to introduce its own national currency in July then another possibility will be found. The cooperation now taking shape between the republics of Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan must be expanded. The meetings between the leaders of the three republics have started to show results, particularly in the economic field. At their scheduled summit meeting the presidents of the three countries will examine the possibilities of expanding cooperation in the agro-industrial field and providing for capital and labor forces.
