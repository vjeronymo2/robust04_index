940706 FT 06 JUL 94 / International Company News: Optus confirms flotation plans for next year Optus, the Australian telecoms group which was set up as a competitor to the government-owned Telecom, has confirmed that it plans to float on the Australian Stock Exchange next year. The company has appointed County NatWest Corporate Finance to act as its financial adviser on the flotation, and the two joint lead managers are County NatWest Securities and Potter Warburg/ABN Amro Hoare Govett. Optus, licensed as the second telecoms carrier in 1992, is owned by a consortium of international investors, including BellSouth of the US and Cable & Wireless of the UK. Australia's Mayne Nickless and several Australian institutions also have stakes.
