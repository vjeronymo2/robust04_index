930709 FT 09 JUL 93 / Observer: Speaks for itself The image of English as the language of civilised countries took a further knock yesterday with the Japanese government's issue of a short phrase-book for citizens visiting the west. The 40 or so key phrases, whose meanings tourists are urged to memorise well enough to respond to instantly, are all of the same type. They include 'Hands up', 'Freeze' and 'Get down on your knees'.
