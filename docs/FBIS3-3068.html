Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, March 13 (XINHUA) -- Waigaoqiao bonded zone in Shanghai, the earliest and largest in China, has been operating well since the beginning of this year, a local official said here recently. Last month 30 projects won approval in the zone, involving an investment of more than 16.9 million U.S. dollars, according to Fan Shuming, spokesman for the zone's management committee. Fourteen of the 30 projects are foreign-funded ones. During January and February, the zone imported and exported more than 54.3 million U.S. dollars worth of commodities and transited 799,000 U.S. dollars worth of goods, Fan said. In addition, more than 117 million U.S. dollars worth of products were manufactured and 4.7 million U.S. dollars worth of goods were stored in the zone, he added. So far, the bonded zone, which was approved by the Chinese Government in June 1990 and went into operation last April, has approved 854 projects, involving an investment of more than 2.1 billion U.S. dollars, handled over 300 million U.S. dollars worth of import and export commodities, 160 million U.S. dollars worth of transit goods, 12 million U.S. dollars worth of manufactured goods and over 200 million U.S. dollars worth of stored goods. The bonded zone is planned to develop in an area of 10 sq km. The first-phase project covered four sq km and was completed a year ahead of time. The second-phase project of six sq km has also started ahead of time, Fan said.
