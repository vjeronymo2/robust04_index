BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Vadim Byrkin and Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Stepanakert 18 May TASS -- The situation in the area of the Karabakh conflict remains calm. The warring sides have refrained from active combat operations over the past three days. Last night and on Wednesday [18 May] morning the Karabakh Army was shelled from three directions. Three servicemen were wounded. No fire was opened in retaliation, ITAR-TASS learned from the press service of the Karabakh Defence Army. The Russian initiative to deploy disengagement troops and international observers in the zone of the conflict has been received with understanding in Stepanakert which is pinning great hopes on this initiative to attain peace. In an interview with ITAR-TASS acting chairman of the Nagorno-Karabakh parliament Karen Baburyan voiced regret over the breakdown of the Moscow talks. He believes that "Azerbaijan showed again that it is not a supporter of peaceful settlement of the problem since when it comes down to signing any documents or agreements the Azeri sides torpedoes the peace-keeping process." No official statements were made by Karabakh officials yet. At the same time, an ITAR-TASS correspondent reported from Yerevan that on Tuesday night the Azeri side violated a ceasefire agreement on Armenia's southern border. According to the Armenian Defence Ministry, Azeri troops opened mortar and submachine gunfire at Armenian positions from the territory of the Nakhichevan Autonomous Region near the border settlement of Yeraskhvan of the Armenian Ararat region, which continued from 2030 until 0100 Wednesday. Casualties were reported. According to a representatives of the Defence Ministry, the Armenian side had to open fire in retaliation. Official sources said that in other areas of the Armenian and Azeri border the situation was calm. No incidents were reported.
