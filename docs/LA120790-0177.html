December 7, 1990, Friday, P.M. Final

SHORT TAKES;

ELV1S IS WORTH $132,000 IN UK

A British license plate spelling ELVIS fetched $132,000 at auction today.

Businessman Anson Lane, 47, bought the plate ELV 1S for the price of a small London apartment even though he said he did not own a car to go with it.

"I have a leased company car so now I will have to go out and buy a car of my own. A pink Cadillac would seem appropriate," he said.

The status-conscious rich in Britain can pay the price of an average saloon car for the privilege of a personalized license plate, and the government has been cashing in by selling unique numbers that were previously withheld.

License plates are made up of officially issued letters and figures.
