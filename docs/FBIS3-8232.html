Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Report by ET-1 correspondent Dhimitrios Dheliolanis] [Text] The 10 members of the Central European Initiative, which includes Italy, Austria and eight Eastern European countries, expressed here in Trieste, Italy, their deep concern about Greece's measures against Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM]. At the same time, they called on both sides to continue negotiations in the United Nations. Following the end of the two-day ministerial meeting, Skopje Foreign Minister Stevo Crvenkovski expressed his satisfaction with the communique and repeated his desire for dialogue with Greece. Asked if a goodwill gesture can be expected from Skopje, Crvenkovski replied: [Begin recording, in English] [Crvenkovski] I think that our position is quite clear. We are absolutely for them and for continuing negotiations. We favor developing good relations between the two countries and we are strongly interested in obtaining good relations with.... [Dheliolanis, interrupting] There will be some goodwill gesture on your part? [Crvenkovski] There have been many goodwill gestures in the past few years and I think that now is the time to sit together at the table and continue negotiations in New York. I think the only way to solve any problems between the two countries is to sit together and talk. [end recording] The Skopje minister, however, avoided answering a question about the thinking that led his country to adopt the Sun of Veryina as a symbol. Finally, in an interview with a local newspaper today, Italian Foreign Minister Beniamino Andreatta said that he does not worry about Greece's anger with the planned transportation axis north of Greece.
