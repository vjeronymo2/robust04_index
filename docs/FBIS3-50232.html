Language: Bulgarian Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, 18 January -- In cooperation with the Rights and Freedoms Movement [DPS], Bulgarians want to eliminate the Turkish presence in Bulgaria, Adem Kenan, chairman of the Turkish Democratic Party [TDP], and his first deputy Emin Hamdi claim in an extensive interview given to the Turkish ORTADOGU daily, BTA reports. In addition to the interview, the daily also published an "Open Letter to the State Leadership of the Republic of Bulgaria from the Leadership of the TDP in Bulgaria." Kenan and Hamdi level criticisms against certain DPS leaders, accusing them of cooperation with the KGB and the Bulgarian special services. At the same time, the leaders of the unregistered TDP show concern for the fate of the Khazar Turks and Muslims living in Georgia. According to them, there is a high likelihood and risk of the Georgians applying "the Bulgarian model of assimilation."
