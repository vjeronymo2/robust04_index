BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Pavel Vanichkin and Andrey Loshchilin] [Text] Washington, 20 Apr -- U.S. President Bill Clinton has just spoken on the telephone with Russian President Boris Yeltsin. The leaders of the two states discussed the current situation in Bosnia and measures that the international community may take in order to urgently normalize the situation around Gorazde. According to reports from informed sources, the prospects for widening the use of the NATO air forces against the Bosnian Serbs, a toughening of economic sanctions in relation to Belgrade, and the holding of an international conference on a Bosnian settlement, of which Russia was the initiator, were touched upon during the course of the telephone conversation. In connection with his conversation with B. Yeltsin, the U.S. President postponed a news conference in the White House, earlier scheduled for 2300 Moscow time, by one hour.
