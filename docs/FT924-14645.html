921008 FT 08 OCT 92 / The Conservative Party at Brighton: Scots vote 'shows backing for Union' SCOTLAND'S distinctive identity and contribution to the United Kingdom must be recognised, Mr Ian Lang, the Scottish secretary, told a conference fringe meeting of the Monday Club yesterday. Mr Lang said that the general election, when the Conservatives gained two seats in Scotland rather than losing several as predicted, had been a 'vote of confidence' in the Union, but warned: 'Dissatisfaction with the Union is likely to emerge once again if there is insufficient recognition of Scotland's status as a nation.' 'Far too many people believe that the United Kingdom and England are synonymous,' he said. 'Significant measures' to strengthen the union and Scotland's place in it, together with a new approach to Scotland's government, would emerge from the stocktaking of the Union promised during the election campaign, Mr Lang said. He ruled out a national assembly, but said reform of local government, with the introduction of powerful single-tier authorities, was central to the stocktaking process. The Scottish Office should play a more visible part in Scottish life and become more accessible. The conclusions of the stocktaking are expected in the next few months. Mr Lang admitted mistakes had been made during the Thatcher era in the government's relationship with Scotland. 'Perhaps we have not been sufficiently active in addressing Scotland's different priorities,' he said. 'There is no point in importing policy for the sake of it. More policy should be home grown in Scotland and policy must be right for Scotland,' he added. Mr Lang said he was awaiting findings of a feasibility study on Scottish water privatisation before making a decision. But in his speech he hinted he would not be put off by opinion polls showing that most Scots oppose water privatisation. 'Policymaking by opinion poll doesn't work,' he said, and added: 'I am not afraid of being unpopular.'
