BFN [Excerpt] Budapest (DDP/ADN) -- German Chancellor Helmut Kohl has promised Hungary support in its efforts for full membership of the European Union. Germany would accompany Hungary on this path "with advice, but above all with actions," Kohl said at a news conference in Budapest today at the end of his official visit to Hungary. Without Hungary, Europe is only a torso and such a Europe is undesirable. He will use Germany's EU presidency, which begins on 1 July, to win support in the EU for Hungary's admission, as he has done with Spain and Portugal, among others, the chancellor said. He will moreover place on the table the "proposal, as yet not popular in Europe" that Hungarian deputies should enter the European parliament in an advisory capacity. This is naturally above all help for self-help, said Kohl. The key to EU membership lies with Hungary itself, which is today a "notable example of stability in a difficult period of transformation." The Budapest government is the only one in Eastern Europe to last a full legislative period and can point to considerable successes in spite of all its problems. Germany supports the French proposal of reaching a joint stance on the Bosnian issue, as well as President Yeltsin's warning to the Serbs. The Russian president must also be included in future developments. Kohl spoke against the idea, voiced in Budapest recently by Bavarian Prime Minister Edmund Stoiber, of introducing partial EU membership. The principle still holds that the chancellor is the one who determines the main direction of policy, Kohl said in response to a query. The best preparation for EU membership is closer links between the community and those states that wish to join. To this end "the terminology has to be sorted out," Kohl stressed. For example, Poland is not in eastern Europe, as is so often said, but in central Europe. Prime Minister Peter Boross indicated that Hungary will shortly be approaching the UN in connection with possible compensation for the losses it has incurred from the blockade against Serbia. The close cooperation with Germany is an important factor in involving his country in Europe's economic system. He expressed the hope that with German help Hungary will become a full EU member by the year 2000 or perhaps even earlier. [passage omitted]
