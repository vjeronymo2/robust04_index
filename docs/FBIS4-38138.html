BFN [Text] Kuwait, 25 Jun -- In a press statement published here today, Sabah al-Ahmad al-Jabir Al Sabah, first deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs, stressed the rejection of any threats or accusations made against the Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] because of support for the secessionist Yemeni state, or that it supported one side at the expense of the other. In a statement to the newspaper AL-ANBA, Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad expressed regret over the Yemeni president's attack on GCC states because of the GCC's statement in which it supported the UN Security Council resolution, saying that this attack was a hard to forgive mistake. Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad affirmed that all Damascus Declaration states were awaiting the current international move aimed at resolving the Yemeni crisis and the outcome of the efforts of the UN envoy regarding this crisis. He said that these states were also awaiting the results the envoy had achieved, which he would present to the UN secretary general and the Security Council. In light of these results, Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad added that developments and their required resolutions could be discussed during the meeting of the Damascus Declaration's foreign ministers to be held in Kuwait on 27 June.
