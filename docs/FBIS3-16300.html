Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Belinform correspondent for TASS] [Text] Minsk March 15 TASS -- The Belarusian Supreme Soviet legislature passed on Tuesday a new constitution. Under the new constitution, Belarus is to become a presidential republic. It is declared "a unitary democratic social law-governed state", with man regarded as the highest value of society and the state. Belarus sets the goal of becoming a nuclear-free and neutral power. The republic's president will also be the head of the executive branch. The parliament is to be reduced to 260 deputies from the present 360 lawmakers. It will remain semi-professional, that is part of deputies will work full-time in the parliament's standing commissions, while the rest will combine their deputy's posts with some other jobs. The parliament ruled that presidential elections should be called before this July, while elections to a new parliament are to be held not later than this year. The new fundamental law of the Republic of Belarus is to come into force from the date of its publication.
