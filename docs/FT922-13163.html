920414 FT 14 APR 92 / Severn Bridge tolls to rise TOLLS on the Severn Bridge, which connects England and Wales along the M4 motorway, are to rise on April 26, but will be levied only on traffic entering Wales. The new rates are Pounds 2.80 for a car, Pounds 5.60 for small goods vehicles and Pounds 8.40 for heavy goods vehicles and buses - up from Pounds 1 for a car and Pounds 2 for all other vehicles. The tolls will rise on January 1 each year by 6 per cent plus the annual rise in the retail price index. With inflation at the present level, the cost for a car will be Pounds 4.60 in five years and Pounds 6.10 by the turn of the century. On April 26, responsibility for the bridge will pass from the Department of Transport to a company, Severn River Crossing, which is owned by John Laing, GTM-Entrepose of France, Bank of America and Barclays de Zoete Wedd. SRC is to build and operate the Pounds 300m second crossing of the Severn, three miles downstream from the present bridge, due to open in 1996.
