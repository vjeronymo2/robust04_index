BFN [Report by UKRINFORM correspondent for TASS] [Text] Kiev 13 April, TASS -- We are greatly concerned over the fact that NATO had to launch air strikes against Bosnian Serb positions and view them as the last resort. However, we understand that NATO was forced to resort to this drastic measure, Ukrainian Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko told a briefing on Wednesday. "We are above all concerned over the failed attempts to solve the conflict in this region by political means," he noted. "We believe there is a possibility of going ahead with political dialogue, which successfully got under way in Bosnia-Herzegovina," the foreign minister stressed. "In my opinion, a positive solution to it is quite possible. We see to it that the situation remain under control, since a lot of U.N. peacekeeping forces are deployed in this region, including our Ukrainian battalion. I think we are doing our utmost to prevent any unpredictable developments there, the more so as the situation is under control of U.N. troops and NATO forces. I believe the conflict will be avoided," Zlenko emphasised.
