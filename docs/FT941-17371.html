940105 FT 05 JAN 94 / World Trade News: Global tourism up 3.8% The number of international tourist arrivals worldwide last year was about 500m, a 3.8 per cent increase over 1992, according to the World Tourism Organisation, writes Michael Skapinker, Leisure Industries Correspondent. International tourism receipts rose by 9 per cent to Dollars 324bn (Pounds 219bn). The East Asian and Pacific region recorded the highest level of growth last year, with arrivals up 11.8 per cent to 68.5m. Tourist receipts in the region rose 15.2 per cent to Dollars 52.6bn. The Americas recorded the second highest growth, with arrivals up 5.6 per cent to 106.5m and receipts up 14.3 per cent to Dollars 95.5bn. Europe remained the world's largest tourist destination, with 296.5m arrivals last year and receipts of Dollars 162.6bn. This represented growth over 1992 of 2.1 per cent and 5.7 per cent respectively.
