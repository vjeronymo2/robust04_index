BFN [Text] Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina today began a two-day unofficial visit to Paraguay. The first working day began with a meeting with acting Foreign Minister Oscar Cabello, with whom he exchanged ideas on the future of the Common Market of the South and on the reality of and prospects for Cuban development. Robaina also met with the Public Health Committee of the Chamber of Deputies of Paraguay, whose members recently visited Cuba and demonstrated interest in our country's biopharmaceutical development. Roberto Robaina also met with the main leaders of the various parties in Paraguay. He met with Dr. Manuel Benitez Florentin, of the Authentic Radical Liberal Party; Eugenio Sanabria, president of the Colorado Party; Guillermo Caballero Vargas, president of National Encounter [as heard]; and Senator Victor Sanchez, president of the Febrerista Revolutionary Party. Another important meeting was that between Roberto Robaina and Carlos Romero Pereira, acting president of the Senate, who was accompanied by several members of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee. Later in the evening, Foreign Minister Robaina met with the Asuncion Municipal Council and mayor at the Paraguayan-Japanese Center. Tonight [16 April], Robaina is scheduled to be interviewed by Bruno Massi of Paraguayan television's Channel 9.
