BFN [By reporter Zeng Hu (2582 5706)] [Text] Singapore, 19 May (XINHUA) -- Singapore Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong and Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew today met separately with Chinese Vice Premier Li Lanqing. The two sides briefed each other and exchanged views on the Suzhou Industrial Park and other projects of mutual concern. In the meeting with Li Lanqing, Goh Chok Tong related his recent trip to Germany and Britain, during which he briefed German and British businessmen about the Suzhou Industrial Park project, a Chinese-Singapore joint venture, and invited them to invest in China. Li Lanqing expressed appreciation to the Singapore leader for making a trip to Europe to personally canvass investments for the Suzhou project. Li Lanqing briefed Goh about progress in carrying out the project. In their meeting, Lee Kuan Yew and Li Lanqing held in-depth discussions on the project's personnel training and development plans. The two sides expressed satisfaction with the progress achieved in the project in little more than two months since the two governments signed the joint venture agreement. Hu Ping, director of the State Council's Office on Special Economic Zones; Gan Ziyu, vice minister in charge of the State Planning Commission; and Chinese Ambassador to Singapore Yang Wenchang were present at the meetings.
