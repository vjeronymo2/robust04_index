Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 3 Feb (ITAR-TASS) -- At the end of Boris Yeltsin's official visit to Tbilisi, Georgian head of state Eduard Shevardnadze hosted a formal dinner in honor of the Russian Federation president. In his speech at the dinner, Eduard Shevardnadze described the first official visit of the Russian president to independent Georgia as an historic event, with which hopes are being linked for reviving the traditions of spiritual proximity between the peoples of the two states. "Or, speaking in political terms, hopes for building fundamentally new Georgian-Russian relations in the spirit of equality, mutual understanding, good-neighborliness, and friendship," Eduard Shevardnadze said. Shevardnadze added that this event raises hopes for a "restoration of justice, which to us means restoring Georgia's territorial integrity." In his speech, Boris Yeltsin also noted the importance of the summit in Tbilisi. "We have made mutually beneficial and perspective decisions in the political, economic, and other spheres of mutual cooperation," he underscored. The Russian president said that he is determined to continue patiently seeking mutually acceptable solutions to the conflicts in Abkhazia and South Ossetia in order to transform the whole Caucasus into a zone of stability and stable peace. In conclusion, Boris Yeltsin expressed his gratitude for the warm and hearty welcome "which was rendered to the Russian delegation in friendly Georgia," and proposed a toast toward friendship between the people of Russia and Georgia, and for their further cooperation. This evening the Russian delegation left for Moscow. At Tbilisi Airport Boris Yeltsin and members of the Russian delegation were seen off by Eduard Shevardnadze, Prime Minister Otar Patsatsia, and other officials.
