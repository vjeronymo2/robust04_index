BFN [Text] Cairo May 17 (XINHUA) -- Egyptian Prime Minister 'Atif Sidqi met today with the delegation of the Communist Party of China led by Wei Jianxing, member of the CPC Political Bureau and of its Secretariat. Sidqi had friendly talks with Wei and his delegation and both sides voiced satisfaction at the friendly relations between the two countries and at the all-round development of such relations in the political, economic and cultural fields resulting from the visit to China in April by Egyptian President Husni Mubarak. Wei conveyed greetings of Chinese Premier Li Peng to Sidqi. Sidqi reviewed the achievements of the Egyptian economic reforms over the past years and praised China for its tremendous achievements in carrying out the policies of reforms and opening to the outside world. He noted that the establishment of the socialist market economy according to China's concrete circumstances is a unique experiment, believing that the Chinese people will succeed in achieving their objectives. Wei briefed the Egyptian prime minister on the situation in China and praised the progress made by the Egyptian people in safeguarding independence, developing economy and raising their living standards under the leadership of President Mubarak. He also appreciated the important and distinguished role Egypt has played in the international arena particularly in the Middle East peace process which led to the signing of the agreement between the PLO and Israel on implementing Palestinian self-rule in Gaza and Jericho. General Secretary of the National Democratic Party Yusuf Wali met with Wei Jianxing Monday [16 May] evening and both sides agreed to foster the friendly relations between the two parties. A protocol on cooperation between the two parties was signed this evening. The Chinese delegation arrived Sunday at the invitation of the Egyptian National Democratic Party.
