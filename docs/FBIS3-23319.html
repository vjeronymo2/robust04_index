Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by correspondent Ana Camacho] [Excerpts] With every passing month, Spaniards are becoming more pessimistic about the future of Spain in general, but they are moderately optimistic about their personal situation. So says a study by the Bilbao Vizcaya Bank [BBV] Foundation titled "Tendencias sociales en Espana (1960-1990)" that was released yesterday in Madrid. [passage omitted] Terrorism, which in the early 1980's was described by half of all Spaniards as one of the country's three biggest problems, is now of urgent concern to only one out of four Spaniards surveyed. The fading concern about terrorism is counterbalanced by increasing alarm about crime. [passage omitted]
