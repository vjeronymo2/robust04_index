Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, February 20 (XINHUA) -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin and U.S. President Bill Clinton reached agreement Sunday [20 February] to avoid NATO air strikes on Bosnian Serb positions around Sarajevo, a spokesman for the Russian president said. Yeltsin's Spokesman Vyacheslav Kostikov told ITAR-TASS NEWS AGENCY that the two leaders reached the agreement during a telephone conversation Sunday. "The result of the conversation was agreement in principle to prevent the bombardments, taking into account recent Russian diplomatic efforts," he said. "It was agreed to continue consultations on resolving the situation in Bosnia in the coming days," he said. Earlier, Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev said that there would be no immediate nato air strike. "After my conversation with Mr. Perry, I understood... There will be no strike tonight, unequivocally," the minister told the Russian television program Itogi after talking with U.S. Defense Secretary William Perry Sunday. NATO has given Bosnian Serbs until Sunday 24:00 GMT to withdraw all heavy weapons from around Sarajevo or hand them over to U.N. control, saying that they would otherwise face air strikes.
