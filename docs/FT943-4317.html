940909 FT 09 SEP 94 / Observer: The winner by a nose Forget Future Large Aircraft and Apache helicopters. The real interest of this week's Farnborough Air Show existed in an especially vigorous exchange of words between Boeing, the world's biggest civil aircraft maker, and its European rival, Airbus. Ron Woodard, boss of Boeing's commercial aircraft operations, was patently riled by Airbus claims that its aircraft order book for the first half of 1994 was actually fatter than that of the Seattle giant. So Woodard laid into Adam Brown, Airbus's strategic planning director, himself a virtuoso speaker who has long had a good line in digs at Boeing's expense. Whenever Brown spoke, Woodard attested, you could see his nose growing longer and longer. Brown himself was in two minds as to the merits of being esteemed the Pinocchio of the industry. Was Boeing finally conceding that the European consortium had a better nose for business? Or did it just mean that Woodard still didn't believe a word that Airbus's nasal attache said?
