Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 15 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa called on his ministers Tuesday to come up with a list of new items for possible deregulation by the end of the month, government officials said. Hosokawa made the call in a morning cabinet meeting in response to pressure from the United States and elsewhere for Japan to harmonize its economy with that of the rest of the world. In the Japan-U.S. trade framework negotiations, Washington has proposed dozens of new targets for deregulation. Hosokawa called deregulation a vital component of the government's economic reforms and said it must be actively tackled to promote harmonious economic relations with other countries.
