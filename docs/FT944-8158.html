941121 FT 21 NOV 94 / Hume dismisses reports that the IRA will split Mr John Hume, leader of Northern Ireland's mainly Roman Catholic Social Democratic and Labour party, has dismissed reports that the IRA will split and one faction will resume violence. His comments followed reports that an IRA faction based in South Armagh would take up arms again by March if Britain did not make significant constitutional concessions to the republican movement. He said at the weekend: 'I have been at the centre of the (peace) process from the very beginning. I can tell you there are no deadlines for the IRA, no secret deals.' Earlier Mr Hume said he firmly believed the ceasefires by the IRA and the loyalist paramilitaries would prove durable. He said at his party's annual conference in Cookstown, County Tyrone: 'Can I express the belief that the decisions taken in recent times by the leaderships of the Provisional IRA and the loyalist paramilitaries will prove to be truly historic decisions in that they will prove to be decisions that have taken the gun and the killing of human beings out of our politics for ever.' Mr Hume started bilateral talks with Mr Gerry Adams, president of Sinn Fe'in, the IRA's political wing, in 1988. At the weekend he paid tribute to the part played in the peace process by Mr Albert Reynolds, former prime minister of the Irish Republic, but added: 'We in this party take it for granted that all major parties in the Dail (parliament) are totally committed to the peace process and we will work closely with whatever government emerges to continue the process.' He said: 'Peace and justice in the north transcend party politics in the south.'
