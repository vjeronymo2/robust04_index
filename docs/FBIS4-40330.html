BFN [Text] Kazakh First Deputy Defense Minister Alibek Kasymov told a news conference in Almaty Wednesday that the conditions put forward by Russia for joining the Partnership for Peace program were not acceptable. In his opinion, "absolutely all states within the framework of this program will enjoy equal rights. No one must seek a special status." The chief of the staff of the NATO forces in Europe, Peter Karstens who is on a visit to Kazakhstan said that "there will be no special status for anyone in the Partnership for Peace program and no exceptions are envisioned." At present, Kazakhstan is developing a presentation document specifying aspects of the republic's membership in the program. The documents provides for consultations on a reduction of the Kazakh armed forces, their formation and development, including shaping up the defense budget and help in training personnel. Kasymov admitted that the Kazakh Army suffered from a lack of officers, and the republic's leadership intend to create conditions for citizens of other states to serve in its armed forces.
