BFN [Article by "ao": "Majority Against Federation's Breakup"] [Text] The FOCUS--Center for Social and Marketing Analysis agency conducted a public opinion survey from 9 to 23 May 1994, in which the organizers involved a representative sample of 2,018 respondents. One of the questions asked was: "Had a referendum been held before the division of the CSFR, how would you have voted?" Partition would have been preferred by 27 percent of those questioned (in October 1993, 23 percent gave this reply to the same FOCUS question), 57 percent would have voted against partition (60 percent), 11 percent would not have participated in the referendum (14 percent), and 5 percent did not know (3 percent). Asked what kind of an arrangement of relations between the Czech Republic and the Slovak Republic they would personally wish in the future, the respondents replied as follows: Two independent states would be preferred by 35 percent (32 percent in October 1993), a confederation or union by 30 percent (27), a federal state by 26 percent (35), and another arrangement by 3 percent (2). Six percent (4) could not decide.
