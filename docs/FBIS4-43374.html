BFN [Excerpt] Erfurt, (DDP/ADN) -- The provisional final results of the Thuringian district and town council elections reveal that the CDU [Christian Democratic Union] has gained 37.1 percent of the votes -- 4.8 percent less than in the 1990 local elections. The regional returning officer said today in Erfurt that the SPD [Social Democratic Party] has gained 26.1 percent, 6.5 percent more than four years ago. The third largest vote went to the PDS [Party of Democratic Socialism], which improved its result from 5.2 percent to 15.7 percent. The Greens sustained slight losses with 6.4 percent (1990: 6.6 percent). The FDP [Free Democratic Party], which achieved 7.7 percent in 1990, gained 6.2 percent. The DSU [German Social Union] dropped from 3.3 to 1.4 percent. None of the other parties made the 1 percent mark. [passage omitted]
