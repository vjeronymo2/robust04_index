Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 8 Feb (MENA)--Palestinian and Israeli negotiators resumed their talks this afternoon at al-Andalus Palace. The Palestinian side is chaired by Ahmad Quray', alias Abu-'Ala', head of the PLO's Economic Department. Nabil Sha'th, Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat's political adviser; and Akram Haniyah and Faysal al-Husayni--officials from the occupied territories--are members of the Palestinian delegation. The Israeli side is chaired by Foreign Ministry Director General Uri Savir, Foreign Ministry Legal Adviser Joel Zinger, and Chief of Military Planning Uzi Dayan. Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat and Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa left the Meridien Hotel after a working luncheon given by 'Amr Musa for the Palestinian president, Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, and Israeli Environment Minister Yosi Sarid. The luncheon was also attended by the Palestinian and Israeli delegations to the talks. Nabil Sha'th, Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat's political adviser, stated to journalists before he left for the venue of the talks that work has been going on since the very beginning. We continued our discussions throughout last night until 0300 today, he said. Today, negotiations started once again at 1000. We are determined to reach an agreement. Sha'th said we will be meeting immediately, but we cannot confirm or discuss specific results. However, we are doing our utmost. The Palestinian president's adviser clarified that 'Arafat and Peres will leave Cairo, tomorrow, Wednesday. Therefore, we hope to make some tangible progress before then, he added. Answering a question on whether the agreement will be signed tomorrow, Sha'th said that he does not know yet for sure whether President 'Arafat and Shim'on Peres will meet this evening with President Husni Mubarak. On his part, Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres said that the two sides agreed not to make any statements before things are clarified because they are now at a crucial stage of the negotiations. Peres clarified that the talks are proceeding well. He confirmed that the Palestinian and Israeli sides are trying to find a formula for overcoming the obstacles. The Israeli minister expressed his happiness over his meeting this evening with President Husni Mubarak, which, according to him, took place upon the invitation of his excellency. He said that the negotiations are going on continuously. If the need should arise on both sides for us, "myself and Chairman [al-ra'is] 'Arafat," to meet, "we will meet with each other to overcome the obstacles." Shim'on Peres praised the great cooperation the Palestinians and the Israelis are getting from the Egyptian side. PLO Executive Committee member Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh and head of the PLO's Information Department, clarified on his part that this evening, it will become clear whether there is a possibility to initial a paper over which both sides, the Palestinian and the Israeli, agree. He noted that so far, this has not been confirmed. 'Abd-Rabbuh said that the issues still under discussion are the same that were discussed before in Cairo, Taba, and Davos. These issues revolve round the crossings, the axis roads [al-turuq al-'ardiyah] in the Gaza Strip, and the rest of the issues pertaining to Jericho.
