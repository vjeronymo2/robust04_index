Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Aden, 28 Feb (MENA)-- The military situation and developments in Yemen were discussed in Aden yesterday evening by a joint military committee in the presence of Defense Minister Staff Brigadier General Haytham Qasim Tahir. During the meeting, the committee expressed the hope that efforts will succeed in putting all the remaining tense areas under control, returning all military units to pre-crisis positions, and showing these units' commitment to the committee's instructions in accordance with the authority entrusted to it by Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid. The committee's official spokesman announced that the committee-- composed of the Yemeni-Omani-Jordanian commission--has met in Sanaa and Aden in the presence of the U.S. and French military attaches. It agreed to return all the units that had left their posts during the crisis to their original stations, stop the clashes that have occurred in certain areas, prevent the setting up of new posts on public roads, and remove those newly set up posts, and prohibit any military movement unless the Joint Military Committee approves it. The spokesman stated that the committee will undertake observation duties and supervise the implementation of its decision as of today, Monday.
