940118 FT 18 JAN 94 / Business and the Law: Monopoly threat - Legal Briefs UK solicitors' monopoly on the conduct of litigation is under threat from a new professional body aiming to corner a share of the construction litigation market. The Institute of Commercial Litigators was formed by five international firms of construction consultants that already handle arbitration work - James R. Knowles, Cyril Sweett, Bucknall Austin, Beard Dove and Tweeds. The institute has applied to the Lord Chancellor's Advisory Committee for the right of its members to conduct litigation. It plans to offer clients a faster, cheaper service undercutting solicitors' fees by up to 50 per cent. Institute director Patrick Lineen said: 'There is little difference between complex arbitration and complex litigation.'
