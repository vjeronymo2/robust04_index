BFN [Announcer-read report] [Text] It has been announced that exports increased by 2.4 percent and imports decreased by 13.4 percent in the first four months of this year compared with the same period last year. According to a statement issued by the State Statistics Institute, exports totaled $5,049,626,000 and imports reached $7,612,978,000 during January-April 1994. A 69.1-percent decrease was registered in the foreign trade deficit in April 1994 compared with the previous year. The rate by which revenues from exports met the costs of imports also increased from 43.7 percent to 70.9 percent in the same period. The foreign trade deficit for the first four months of the year diminished by 33.5 percent compared with the same period last year. The rate by which exports met imports in the same period also increased from 56.1 percent to 66.3 percent. A comparison of the import-export figures for April 1993 and 1994 shows that exports increased by 2.7 percent, while imports decreased by 40.1 percent.
