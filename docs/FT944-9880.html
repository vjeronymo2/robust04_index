941114 FT 14 NOV 94 / Concern at loss of building recruits The construction industry is losing most of the recruits it would once have had because of the expansion of full-time education, says Sir Clifford Chetwood, chairman of the Construction Industry Training Board. As the CITB, one of two remaining statutory training boards, prepared to unveil a new training strategy today, Sir Clifford said: 'Eighty per cent of the young people that we normally would have had access to for our apprenticeships are now following a full-time education until 17 or 18.' Sir Clifford, who began his career as a trainee building manager, said it was important for the board to adjust its training schemes 'so that we do not lose access to the non-academic element of the 80 per cent'. He added: 'A lot of them will probably find out after a year that they are not suited academically and will seek to resort to a trade involving physical skills.' When asked if the industry would simply lose many of those who continue their education to other careers, he said: 'You would have to have the IQ. There are a vast number of people out there who are only semi-literate. We will always be dealing with these people. And when they are non-academic they are extremely well co-ordinated.'
