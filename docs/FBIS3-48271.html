Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Phnom Penh 22 Jan (AKP) -- The delegation of the Royal Government of Cambodia led by Prince Krompreah Norodom Ranariddh and Hun Sen, first and second prime ministers, returned to Phnom Penh on Friday [21 January] after an official five-day visit to China at the invitation of the Chinese premier. The delegation met with a PRC Government delegation led by Premier Li Peng during the visit. The meeting was followed by the signing of an agreement by the Cambodian deputy prime minister, Prince Norodom Sirivut, and the Chinese State Council vice chairman. The agreement concerns a 50-million-yuan interest-free loan from the Chinese Government to the Kingdom of Cambodia. The delegation visited the center of industrial development in Hainan and a textile factory in Guangdong. Cambodia's royal delegation also visited His Majesty Norodom Sihanouk, king of Cambodia, who is taking medical treatment in the Chinese capital. The delegation was received on its return at Pochentong airport by Sar Kheng, deputy prime minister and minister of interior and public security; Chem Snguon, state minister and justice minister; Sam Rangsi, state minister and minister of finance and economy; and other high-ranking officials. The representative of the diplomatic corps of the PRC to Cambodia was also present.
