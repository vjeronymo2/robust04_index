940315 FT 15 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television Losing a war to the Vietnamese was bad enough for a nation as huge and rich as the US, yet now they find themselves obliged to pull out of Somalia, unable even to organise a little peace-keeping mission. Somali warlord General Aidid talks to Margaret Gilmour in Assignment (7.45 BBC2). Judging by last week's episode, Channel 4's new series about the law, The Brief, is ready to adopt its own editorial stance. For instance, having sought out an interviewee the reporter may ask a question and then shout down the answer, which is a novel technique (8.30 C4). 40 Minutes investigates the destruction of Freddie Laker's Skytrain scheme in 1982: was he brought down by his own ambition? 'No, I was shafted' he says, and of course BA and other airlines did have to settle with him (9.45 BBC2). Hedd Wyn is a 130-minute historical drama in Welsh with subtitles, telling the story of a young Welsh poet who dreams of winning the prize at the National Eisteddfod (10.35 C4). Omnibus screens a revised version of Leslie Megahey's 1972 portrait of Goya (11.00 BBC1).
