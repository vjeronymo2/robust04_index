BFN [Excerpts] Stuttgart (DPA) -- The plutonium seized in Baden-Wuerttemberg in early May is weapons-grade material. As DPA learned from a well-informed source on Friday [27 May], the material, which was not recognized by the authorities for weeks, is 56 grams of plutonium-239 in powder form. According to the information, the highly poisonous material was found during the search of an apartment in Tengen near Konstanz in the course of investigations involving counterfeiting. [passage omitted] So far, no information has been provided about the origin of the material. The Environment Ministry announced on Thursday that the material probably came from East Europe. The Land Office of Criminal Investigations (LKA) in Stuttgart, which is responsible for the investigations, has announced that it will issue a statement on Friday. The Konstanz Public Prosecutor's Office has not yet commented. [passage omitted]
