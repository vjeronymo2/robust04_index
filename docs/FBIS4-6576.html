BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 22 (KCNA) -- Foreign public circles denounce the U.S. hostile policy toward the DPRK and express support and solidarity for the Korean people's struggle of justice. The chairman of the Central Committee of the National Union of Democratic Youth of Nepal in his speech at a mass rally in Katmandu on April 8 condemned the U.S. hostile policy toward the DPRK. The ralliers adopted a letter of protest to U.S. President Bill Clinton and marched toward the U.S. Embassy shouting slogans with the placards "U.S. Imperialists, Promptly Stop Campaign Against North Korea Over Nuclear Issue" and "U.S. Imperialists, Quit Korean Peninsula." They handed the letter signed by 100 political and public figures to the U.S. Embassy. The letter demanded that the United States pull all its troops and nuclear weapons out of South Korea and give up the projected "sanctions" against the DPRK. At a solidarity meeting held in Cuba earlier, the U.S. campaign against the DPRK was denounced and support and solidarity were expressed for the Korean people's just struggle. A letter to the Korean people was adopted at the meeting. The French Institute for the Development of International Relations in a communique April 6 expressed full support and solidarity for the Korean people's just stand toward the situation prevailing on the Korean peninsula. Pierre Welo Ahondju Ewale, chairman of the Zaire-Korea Friendship Association, in a statement April 5 stressed that the United States must sincerely approach the unshakable attitude of the DPRK Government to find a peaceful, negotiated solution to the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula.
