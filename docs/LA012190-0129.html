January 21, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

OTHER COMMENTARY / EXCERPTS;

A SHOCK TREATMENT THAT MAKES NO SENSE

The Moynihan proposal is the headiest political intoxicant Democrats have sniffed in years. Their delight in it may yet overwhelm all sorts of reservations.

The Reagan tax cuts, combined with indexing and the defense buildup, approximately tripled the national debt, and no end of deficit spending is yet in sight. The Republicans, despite the worries among their wiser heads, have treated this fiscal insanity as a political opportunity.

Now insult has been added to injury, as the smarting Democrats see it, by the 1983 Social Security bailout. That bipartisan accord was designed to provide for the eventual retirement, in massive numbers, of the baby-boom generation. But pending that 21st-Century need, the scheduled payroll tax increases are generating huge surpluses in the Social Security trust accounts. Those surpluses provide fiscal padding for the GOP fiscal scam. They offer a deceptive mask for the still-deepening operating deficit.

The idea, as budget-literate Democrats know, is risky in all sorts of ways, beginning with the near certainty that in a congressional election year, cuts in Social Security taxes will not be balanced by other, more progressive tax increases.

Nonetheless, Moynihan's maneuver is a brilliant political stroke, the first really shrewd Democratic response in eight long, frustrating years of being beaten to death by Republican tax-cut sloganeering.
