Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Algiers, Feb 28 (MAP) -- President and vice-president of the outlawed Islamic Salvation Front (FIS) Abassi Madani and Ali Belhadj, serving a 12-year jail term each since 1992, were reportedly released, Algerian daily `LIBERTE' said Saturday. Certain sources said the two FIS leaders were put under house arrest in Douera (suburb south-east of Algiers) while others said they were in Zeralda (31 km west of Algiers). According to the daily, other party leaders could have been freed last week. The releases, added the daily, are part of a general amnesty which is likely to take place before the end of the holy month of Ramadan.
