940329 FT 29 MAR 94 / International Company News: Foreign holders to pull out of Nigerian bank The main foreign shareholders in the United Bank for Africa (UBA), one of Nigeria's three biggest commercial banks, have said they aim to pull out of the bank, Reuter reports from Lagos. Mr Luke Okafor, UBA's managing director, said Banque Nationale de Paris Internationale and BNP's UK unit Banque Nationale de Paris which own 30.7 per cent of UBA, have indicated they intended to disinvest but not when. 'It would not have a negative impact on UBA.' UBA is 60 per cent owned by Nigerians, with the remaining 9.3 per cent shares divided between Bankers International of the US and Banca Nazionale del Lavoro and Monte dei Pashi di Siena, both of Italy.
