Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Papua New Guinea's [PNG] prime minister, Paias Wingti, has announced more money for military operations against secessionist rebels on the island of Bougainville. The announcement comes just days after the United Nations Human Rights Commission adopted, by consensus, a resolution relating to the conflict on Bougainville. The resolution called on the PNG Government to invite UN investigators to visit Bougainville to look into human rights abuses. Sean Dorney reports: [Begin recording] [Dorney] Reviewing a trooping of the colors ceremony at (Torano) Bay, just outside Port Moresby, at the weekend, Prime Minister Paias Wingti praised the 1st Battalion of the Pacific island's regiment, claiming that its ability to win the hearts and minds of the people have led to abiding their operation gaining public confidence and respect. And Mr. Wingti announced there'd be more money for the army. [Wingti] Yesterday, the cabinet also made a very important decision to allocate 5 million kina, new money for the operations on Bougainville for the defense force. [Dorney] The 1st Battalion has had 21 soldiers killed in the Bougainville conflict. [end recording]
