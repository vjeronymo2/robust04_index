Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Police Commissioner General Rafi Peled, said today that the police will take practical measures tomorrow, Friday to maintain calm and order during the Friday prayers at the al-Aqsa Mosque, such as limiting the number of worshipers, imposing restrictions on those entering al-Aqsa, particularly those below 40 years of age, in addition to not allowing the Jewish worshipers to visit the wailing Wall during these hours. Gen. Peled's statements were made to our correspondent for police affairs, Kayid Zahir, at the annual meeting of the Border Guard held in the Jerusalem theater, and attended by Police Minister Moshe Shahal, and Border Guard officers and commanders. During this meeting, award certificates and police medals were bestowed on several Druze and Jewish members of the Border Guard for outstanding activities while performing their duties in the special units.
