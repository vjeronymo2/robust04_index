930824 FT 24 AUG 93 / Arrests in Covent Garden increase as a result of initiative with Mercury Paging POLICE in London's Covent Garden have made 100 arrests this year as a result of an initiative with Mercury Paging. Using radiopaging technology, shopkeepers in the area are able to inform other retailers and the police of crimes in progress or people acting suspiciously. With PC Alan Marcroft (left), who made the 100th arrest - a suspected shoplifter at the Royal Opera House shop in James Street - are Ms Gina Bruce, the deputy manageress of the shop, and Mr Chris Neary, managing director of Mercury Paging. Mr Neary said yesterday: 'Covent Garden was one of the first areas to adopt the scheme which has now spread to other UK shopping centres and groups of pubs which suffer from vandalism, drug abuse and violence.'
