BFN [Text] Cape Town June 28 SAPA -- The South African National Defence Force [SANDF] had made contingency plans for armed intervention in Lesotho and was waiting to be given instructions, minister of defence Mr Joe Modise said on Tuesday [28 June]. He told a press conference that the SANDF had contingency plans for all southern African flashpoints in case it was required to move in. There had been increasing concern among the Frontline States over events in Lesotho, where dissident police and army factions have been responsible for continuing armed disruption and killings. Earlier this month President Nelson Mandela expressed fears that the unrest could have en effect on South Africa.
