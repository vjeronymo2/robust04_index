BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow April 21 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin has begun a meeting with the heads of the republics which are component parts of the Russian Federation. Opening the meeting, he said, "The system of meetings with the republic heads is being renewed". According to the president, such meetings with republic leaders should be held approximately "five to seven times a year". He noted that Thursday's meeting is the first since the adoption of a new constitution and the elections to the Federal Assembly. "Much has changed in the country in the past few months, political stabilisation is gaining strength, the disastrous state of dual-power has been overcome", Boris Yeltsin pointed out. He also informed the local heads about his work with the chairmen of the Federation Council and State Duma, saying that there "have been no disagreements so far". On the topic of the recent local elections in Russia, the president noted that "the elections were valid in 61 federal regions. In only 17 was voter turnout too low". He expressed the hope that all the local elections would be held by the end of the current year.
