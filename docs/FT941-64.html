940331 FT 31 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Bridport-Gundry Bridport-Gundry reported halved pre-tax losses of Pounds 220,000 for the six months to December 31, against Pounds 439,000. Mr David Sebire, chairman, said that although there was no discernable improvement in its markets, there should be satisfactory profits in the second half, as a result of the lack of one-off costs and progress on improving the company's performance. Turnover for the company, which has interests in the manufacture of medical aviation and defence, sports and industrial products, was Pounds 13.7m (Pounds 12.9m) of which discontinued activities contributed Pounds 856,000 (Pounds 966,000). Operating profits were Pounds 86,000 (Pounds 120,000 losses) after accounting for one-off reorganisation costs of Pounds 245,000. There was a further loss of Pounds 100,000 relating to the sale of the Irish fishing business. Losses per share came out at 1.22p (4.67p) and the interim dividend is 1.25p (1.5p).
