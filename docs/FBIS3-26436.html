Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 7 (XINHUA) -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin met here today with more than 30 veteran foreign experts, including Israel Epstein and Yokogawa Tatsuko, who have been working in China for several decades. The experts come from the United States, France, Britain, Canada, Germany, Austria, Japan, Thailand and Malaysia. During the meeting, Jiang, who is also general secretary of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China (CPC), expressed his delight at meeting these experts as the traditional Chinese New Year is approaching. On behalf of the CPC Central Committee, the State Council and in his own name, Jiang extended thanks to them for their hard work in the past year, wishing them good health, smooth work and family happiness in the new year. At the meeting, the experts put forward proposals and suggestions on China's economic construction, culture, education and press. Jiang expressed his thanks to them for their proposals and concerns for China's development. The Chinese president briefed the experts on China's economic development and the major events of 1993. He said that China will continue to follow the road of building socialism with Chinese characteristics and that it will grasp opportunities, deepen the reforms and opening up, promote development and maintain stability, so as to make the national economy keep a sustained, high-speed and healthy development and thus realize the all-round progress of the whole of society. After the meeting, Jiang hosted a banquet for the foreign experts. Taking part in both were Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen , State Councillor Li Guixian, State Councillor and Secretary-General of the State Council Luo Gan, Director of the State Bureau of Foreign Experts Affairs Ma Junru, and other leading officials of relevant departments. According to sources, China's recruitment of skills and talents from abroad has been developing greatly and a total of over 350,000 foreign experts have been invited to the country since the start of reforms and opening to the outside world. An average of 40,000 foreign experts have been invited and come to work in China annually in the past few years.
