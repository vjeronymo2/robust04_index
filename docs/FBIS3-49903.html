Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Geneva, 18 Jan (TANJUG) -- This year's first round of peace negotiations began in Geneva's Palace of Nations shortly before 1300 today. Lord Owen and [Thorvald] Stoltenberg invited the presidents of Serbia, Montenegro, and Croatia as well as the leaders of the three warring parties in Bosnia-Herzegovina to find ways of communicating and eventually to sign a peace agreement. Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic said on arrival at Geneva Airport that "the Serbian side has fulfilled its share of obligations" and that "now it is the Muslim side and the international community which should fulfill their share of obligations regarding the adoption of a peace plan and an end to sanctions." Republic of Srpska president Radovan Karadzic expressed the following hopes: "The EC should make its move and fulfill what it should fulfill -- this means lifting the sanctions." Montenegrin President Momir Bulatovic stressed: "In all our work so far and all our results, as well as in the commitments expressed by the Assembly of the Serbian republic yesterday, we have repeatedly displayed our commitment to finalizing the peace process. This requires agreement on the part of the Muslims and the Croatian side. I hope that the Muslim side will stop obstructing and I believe that the international community will bring pressure to bear on the Muslim side, if not today then in the near future. This is the sole matter to be addressed to have a peace agreement signed." According to Bulatovic, head of the Montenegrin state, the lifting of sanctions has been included in the mainframe of a general agreement. He also said that the situation in the country precludes it from staking everything on the international community's words and trustworthiness. [passage omitted]
