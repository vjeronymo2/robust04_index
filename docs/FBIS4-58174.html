BFN [Text] Kuwait, 1 May 1994, KUNA -- The Kuwaiti Council of Ministers praised today the firm principled stances expressed by the foreign ministers of the fraternal GCC [Gulf Cooperation Council] member states towards support for the Kuwaiti issue. In a statement after the meeting of the Council, under the chairmanship of the Heir Apparent and Prime Minister Shaykh Sa'd al- 'Abdallah, the minister of state for cabinet affairs,minister of planning and acting foreign minister, 'Abd al-'Aziz al-Dakhil, said that the Council expressed "its consideration for and satisfaction with the confirmation by the U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher" lately "of his country's commitment to defend the security and stability of the Gulf region and its understanding of the Iraqi hostile regime and the permanent threat it constitutes for the security and stability in the region. He also expressed the eagerness of the United States for the necessity to continue pressures on this regime so as it implements the resolutions of the UN Security Council which are related to its aggression against Kuwait".
