Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The three-round Lebanese-Syrian summit, which lasted for seven hours, dealt with the tense situation in the south, developments in the peace process, and the internal Lebanese situation, particularly the regime's actions in the political and reconstruction fields. President al-Hirawi returned to Beirut last night at the end of the Damascus summit. Our correspondent in Damascus has reported that the first round of talks lasted three and half hours and was attended by Syrian Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam. The two leaders discussed the progress made so far on the Lebanese and Syrian tracks in Washington and Israel's attempts to circumvent Resolution 425. The talks also dealt with the U.S. message that warns about the explosive situation in the south. The two presidents' views were identical on the need to continue coordination between the two countries to confront internal and external developments. During the second round of talks, Presidents al-Hirawi and al- Asad discussed the internal situation and bilateral relations. [passage omitted] The two leaders concluded their summit with a third round of talks in which Khaddam, Prime Minister al-Zu'bi, and Foreign Minister al-Shar' attended. The talks dealt with the developments in the region after the Geneva summit and the Arab situation in light of the visits to Damascus by a number of Arab leaders. The two sides also touched on issues of mutual interest. The NATIONAL NEWS AGENCY cited presidential sources as saying that President al-Hirawi raised all issues pertaining to Israel's aggressive designs in the south and western al-Biqa'. President al-Hirawi also briefed the Syrian officials on the course of the Lebanese-Syrian negotiations. He was briefed on the progress made in the Syrian-Israeli negotiations. According to knowledgeable sources, the local Lebanese situation was also discussed during the summit, particularly amid increasing parliamentary opposition. It has been learned that the two presidents stressed the need for the government to promote its efforts by patching up the differences of a number of ministries and the dispute between the Labor Ministry and trade unions. Citing a Syrian official, REUTER reported that Israel is responsible for the failure to achieve any progress in the peace negotiations. Israel still refuses to abide by the peace process and implement the UN resolutions, according to REUTER. The Syrian official emphasized that Israel's failure to withdraw from the occupied Arab territories means that the negotiations have not achieved the desired objective.
