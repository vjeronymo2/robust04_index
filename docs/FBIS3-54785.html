Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Oreshin] [Text] Beijing, 4 Jan (ITAR-TASS) -- Representatives of Amur Oblast and the Chinese border town of Heihe have signed an agreement on setting up a zone of international trade and economic cooperation. The 20 square km zone will be situated on both banks of the Amur River. Within the zone it is intended to open up trade and banking institutions and industrial enterprises using advanced technology and to set up a developed infrastructure. In particular, it is planned to build a road bridge. The economic zone will become a base for processing products targeted at the markets not only of Russia and China but also of other countries. It will have free circulation of goods and free currency exchange and it will be managed by a joint committee with the participation of representatives of the Russian Federation and the PRC.
