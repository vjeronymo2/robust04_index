BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 25 (KCNA) -- A stone plate bearing words about the birth of the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il written 50 years ago appeared recently in Taegu, the Seoul-based radio Voice of National Salvation reported. A scroll bearing the words "The bright star of Paektu is for independence of Korea. Let us uphold the bright star for the liberation of Korea and for the world generation after generation. 1944" was already discovered in Chorwon County, South Korean Kangwon Province, on February 10, 1994, the New Year's day of the lunar calendar. An old man surnamed Ri, a retired professor of Kyongbuk National University in Taegu, on June 12 showed a round stone plate 15 centimetres in diametre he had kept carefully. Written on the stone with a writing brush are the words "O Korea, boast worldwide of the birth of the bright star of Paektu, successor to General Kim Il-song. 1944." He said he found the stone, announcing the auspicious event of the nation, lying on the wooden floor under the eave of his house when he opened the door one morning in August 1944. At that time, a rumour was afloat that a big stone bearing the words "Long live the independence of Korea. Kim Il-song is coming" was found in front of the gate of the house of the chief judge of the Taegu district court. "This stone plate is a most precious national treasure because it tells about the intense joy of our fellow countrymen at the news of the birth of the dear leader Kim Chong-il, and the historical fact that they highly praised him as the bright star of Paektu and the sun of the future," the old man stressed.
