BFN [By reporter Wang Jinhe (3769 6855 0735)] [Text] Washington, 3 May (XINHUA) -- U.S. Acting Secretary of State Talbott met with visiting Chinese Vice Premier Zou Jiahua at the State Department on the morning of 3 May. At the meeting, Talbott extended a welcome and greetings from U.S. Secretary of State Christopher to Zou Jiahua. The secretary of state has been on a tour of the Middle East. The Chinese vice premier in turn asked Talbott to convey his gratitude and regards to Christopher. Talbott said: President Clinton's meeting with the vice premier yesterday has a positive significance for promoting the improvement and development of U.S.-Chinese relations. The U.S. Government has steadfastly observed the principles laid down in the three U.S.-Chinese joint communiques and has implemented the "One China" policy. The two sides have reached a common understanding on the importance of U.S.-Chinese relations. He also emphatically pointed out that in its policy toward China, the United States has adhered to the principles of respecting sovereignty and of mutually beneficial cooperation, adding that Washington is pleased to see the determination shown by both sides to eliminate existing differences between the two countries to facilitate the steady development of U.S.-Chinese relations. After the meeting, Zou Jiahua said: China has all along attached great importance to maintaining and developing relations with the United States. We are sincere in our attitude toward solving problems between the two countries, and have exerted and will continue to exert great efforts to this end. We hope that the United States will have the same attitude and take positive action to facilitate the normalization of relations between the two countries at an early date.
