BFN [Text] The Presidents of Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan and Uzbekistan, Nursultan Nazarbayev, Askar Akayev and Islam Karimov, had a meeting in Cholpon-Ata, Kyrgyzstan. During the meeting, held at the proposal of Kyrgyzstan President Askar Akayev on Friday [29 April] and Saturday, the three leaders spoke about the social and political situation in their countries, discussed the current state of affairs in Central Asia and the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) in general, and dealt with some international problems. The presidents said there was a need for practical mechanisms to carry out the trilateral treaty to create a single economic area, an accord which, they said, is in line with the CIS economic union treaty and is an instrument for the implementation of the latter document within Central Asia. Nazarbayev, Akayev and Karimov also brought up some regional political problems, mainly that of migration and consequent coexistence between different ethnic groups in Central Asian countries, and discussed the damage done to Kyrgyzstan's economy by recent mudslides. The presidents issued a joint communique, in which they urged stronger friendship and cooperation between their countries in all fields.
