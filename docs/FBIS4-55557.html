BFN [HINA report: "Interparty Agreement Possible"] [Text] Zagreb -- At the invitation of Croatian President Franjo Tudjman, a meeting of the presidents of the parliamentary parties and the presidents of the independent clubs of deputies was held on Monday to discuss a proposal of the Croatian Democratic Community [HDZ] regarding the need for the election of new chairmen of the Sabor chambers, bearing in mind that Stipe Mesic and Josip Manolic had left the majority party (HDZ) and formed their own party of the Croatian Independent Democrats (HND). They discussed the need for changes in the parliamentary commissions in light of the newly emerged party breakdown of the Sabor, the bureau of the president of the Republic has reported. The only person not to turn up at the meeting was HND President Stipe Mesic. The representatives of the parties agreed on the need to resolve the highlighted problems in a democratic manner, in observation of the rules of the statute and the rules of procedure, and if possible, with an interparty agreement. It was, thus, agreed to continue the consultations, is stated at the end of a statement by the bureau of the president.
