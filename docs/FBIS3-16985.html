Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, MARCH 21 (SNARK)--There may be no black box in the Iranian airplane crashed in Karabakh last week. The Armenia's Vice-President Gagik Arutyunyan told about that today at the press-briefing for reporters. He said that airplanes S-130 [as received] don't always have black boxes. Mr. Arutyunyan pointed out that the Iranian special commission together with the CIS and Armenia representatives flew off to the accident's place in Karabakh. According to clarified data, 34 people died as a result of this accident, including women and children. Their remains were delivered in Yerevan, and on March 21 they will be conveyed to Iran. Mr. Arutyunyan also said that according to data received from the Iran Embassy to Yerevan, the airplane tried to make an emergency landing. It lost height and got from 7500 to 3500 meters. The Armenian dispatching service tried to get him through the Russian airplane flying from Mineralnye Vody [name as received] to the Arabian Emirates. However this attempt wasn't a success.
