BFN [Text] Cairo, April 29 (AFP)--Israeli and PLO negotiators have reached agreement in principle on the release of all Palestinian prisoners held by Israel who commit themselves to renouncing violence, a senior PLO official disclosed Thursday. The two sides agreed that in addition to 5,000 Palestinians whose release had already been approved, all remaining prisoners would be eligible for a pardon if they pledged "in writing" to abandon violence and to adhere to the policies of the PLO, Nabil Sha'th said. The offer would apply to all Palestinian prisoners regardless of their political affiliation, according to Sha'th, who is the Palestine Liberation Organization's chief negotiator in talks here with Israel. The negotiations are aimed at implementing limited Palestinian self-rule on the Gaza Strip and in the West Bank town of Jericho in accordance with a Declaration of Principles signed by the PLO and Israel in Washington last September. After months of difficult and delicate talks, PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin are to sign an accord here on May 4 to launch Palestinian autonomy, Egyptian President Husni Mubarak announced Thursday. Sha'th said Israeli-PLO contacts would continue after next Wednesday's signing ceremony to determine a timetable for the prisoner releases. He said Palestinians in the autonomous areas would be issued a passport by Palestinian authorities that would be valid for three years and would entitle them to travel to any country accepting the document. On the question of jurisdiction in Gaza and Jericho, which had plagued the Cairo talks earlier this month, Sha'th reported that the two sides had agreed that Palestinian officials would exert authority "in all domains, with the exception of Israeli citizens, Jewish settlements and external security." He said the principal outstanding issue was the scope of the security zones to be established around the settlements on the Gaza Strip. The Palestinians want to reduce the dimensions of the zones to a minimum, according to Sha'th, who added that their proposed boundaries were detailed in maps appended to an agreement here on February 9. The maps have not been made public. Other sources close to the talks said the PLO and Israel still had to agree on the size of the autonomous Jericho area and the presence of a Palestinian guard on the Allenby Bridge between the West Bank and Jordan. In Paris Thursday Israeli and Palestinian officials also reached an accord on future economic relations between Israel and the autonomous areas. In a statement released after five months of arduous talks, the Israeli delegation said "all the obstacles have been overcome and differences settled." "The heads of the delegations think it is possible to reach a general accord on Friday which will form the basis of economic relations between Israeli and Palestinian authorities," the statement said. Details of a final economic agreement were to be worked on overnight ahead of a 9:30 a.m. (0730 GMT) signing ceremony Friday at the French foreign ministry.
