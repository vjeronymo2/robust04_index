BFN [Text] The Russian Foreign Ministry has welcomed the agreement on ceasefire in Nagorno-Karabakh and insisted that it be strictly implemented by all parties. This was announced by an official representative of the Russian Foreign Ministry, Mikhail Demurin, at a briefing in Moscow today. He said it was necessary to achieve a breakthrough in development of the situation, to turn to peace and to confirm the armistice with a legally binding agreement which would put an end to the armed conflict once and for all. Demurin announced that the agreement on ceasefire had been reached on May 12 late at night by the Armenian and Azerbaijani defense ministers and the commander of the Nagorno-Karabakh army with a significant contribution made by Russia and the chairman of the CSCE Minsk Group on Nagorno-Karabakh. "Contrary to the ceasefires unilaterally declared by Nagorno-Karabakh and Azerbaijan this agreement provides for mutual obligations of the parties and a compromise reached with the participation of the mediators," Demurin stressed.
