BFN [Text] Salih Abu-Bakr Bin-Husaynun, the minister of oil and minerals and military commander of the interior axis, praised the state of Kuwait's principled stand. He praised Kuwait's press for its covering of the events. Mr. Bin-Husaynun said in a contact with the AL-QABAS newspaper, published today, that the northern forces had attacked yesterday morning the southern forces at al-'Akbar/Askar axis, and that after violent fighting several northern tanks were destroyed and 11 soldiers were taken prisoner. Bin- Husaynun said that the battle on this axis had begun on 2 June, when the Maghawir forces tried to infiltrate into Khash'in al-Ayn, and were repelled by our forces after fierce fighting. Our Armed Forces destroyed a tank squadron and took 15 soldiers prisoner. The enemy ran away, leaving back a number of artillery, and killed and injured soldiers. Last Tuesday [7 June] the northern forces launched an attack, despite the cease-fire declaration. The enemy suffered the destruction of seven tanks, and the death of soldiers and imprisoning of others, and the shooting down of a MiG-21 warplane.
