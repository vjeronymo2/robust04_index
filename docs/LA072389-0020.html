July 23, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

NEIL AND BRUCE

It is a laughable assertion that Bruce Springsteen would never have written "Born in the U.S.A.," about a bitter and disenfranchised Vietnam veteran, without the precedent of Neil Diamond's "America," an anthem so jingoistic it could be inscribed on the Statue of Liberty.

Perhaps the myopic Mr. Paul derived his understanding of Springsteen's song from Ronald Reagan, who had obviously had heard only the title when he let the song be used in his '84 campaign.

WILLIAM BROUGH

Downey
