BFN [Announcer-read report] [Text] Nusrettin Demiral, chief prosecutor of the Ankara State Security Court, has given the order to place under surveillance the Democracy Party [DEP] deputies whose deputyships were terminated in the wake of a decision made by the Constitutional Court. Six of the DEP deputies whose deputyships were terminated are abroad and two are in Ankara. Another five deputies whose immunities were lifted by the Assembly in March are being detained in the Ankara Central Penitentiary. Answering Turkish Radio and Television correspondent's questions, Demiral said that he gave the order to place under surveillance two of the deputies in Ankara as well as six deputies, currently abroad, in case they return to Turkey. Demiral announced that he could not give additional information on the duration of the surveillance. The DEP deputies who are currently abroad are as follows: Ali Yigit, Mahmut Kilinc, Zubeyir Aydar, Remzi Kartal, Naif Gunes, and Nizamettin Toguc. Selim Sadak and Sedat Yurtdas are at this moment in the Turkish Grand National Assembly.
