BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Aleksandr Kopnov] [Text] Moscow June 2 TASS -- South Korean President Kim Yong-sam said his country can render economic aid to North Korea if the latter allows nuclear inspections on its territory. Addressing Russian lawmakers on Thursday he said that, proceeding from the principle of national co-flourishment, South Korea will develop the policy of active cooperation with Pyongyang. Seoul is also ready to promote better relations between North Korea, the United States and Japan, according to the president. Kim Yong-sam said that he had discussed with the Russian president bilateral relations in full volume, including the issue of making "North Korea refrain from developing nuclear weapons". State Duma Chairman Ivan Rybkin said that Russia understands the wish of the Korean people "to reunite into a single and flourishing state in the Far East". Russia is ready to promote the reunification, he added.
