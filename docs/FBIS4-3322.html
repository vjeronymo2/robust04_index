BFN [Text] Beijing, April 19 (XINHUA) -- Forty Chinese observers, as members of the United Nations Observers Mission (UNOM), left here for South Africa today. They will observe the first non-racial general election in South Africa, which is scheduled to take place from April 26 to 28. On January 14, 1994, the UN Security Council passed resolution 894, which decided to send UNOM to South Africa for the country's election.
