Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Jan 25, BNS -- The permanent representatives of Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania in the United Nations today handed over to the U.N. Secretary-General Butrus-Ghali a joint Baltic statement on the remarks made on Jan. 18 by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev. The Russian minister, speaking to his country's ambassadors in Moscow, declared Russia's "vital interest" in the territory of the former Soviet Union and said Russia must not withdraw its troops from the former republics. A letter sent with today's statement by the three Baltic countries requests that the statement be circulated as a U.N. official document under the "Complete Withdrawal of Foreign Troops from the Territories of the Baltic States" topic of the General Assembly's agenda, as well as in the U.N. Security Council, a representative at the Estonian Foreign Ministry said.
