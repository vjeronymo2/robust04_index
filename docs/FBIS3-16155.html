Language: Russian Article Type:BFN ["Own information" report: "Aleksey Kazannik's Surprise Move"] [Text] Aleksey Kazannik, who is now in Omsk, has confirmed in a telephone conversation with the IZVESTIYA editorial office that he intends to ask the Federation Council, which is responsible under the Constitution for appointing the general prosecutor to his post and releasing him from it, not to accept his resignation which he tendered to the president 26 February in connection with well known events. Kazannik believes that he has not broken the law or thereby provided grounds for being released from his post. He voiced confidence that Russia could have in him a really independent general prosecutor. To date, Kazannik has not received from the Federation Council an invitation to participate in the session at which his question will be discussed, and he does not intend to "angle" for this, although he reckons that the question of his resignation will not, all the same, be examined in the absence of the claimant. This statement appears strange, to say the least, if you recall that Aleksey Kazannik tendered his resignation to Boris Yeltsin of his own will and even against the will of Yeltsin, and it was widely rumored by the General Prosecutor's Office.
