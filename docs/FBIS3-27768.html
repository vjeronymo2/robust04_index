Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 31 (YONHAP) -- President Kim Yong-sam plans to hold summit talks with Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa in Tokyo on March 24 and with Chinese President Jiang Zemin in Beijing on March 28, South Korean Embassy and senior officials at the Japanese Government said Monday. An embassy official said, "the South Korean Government is discussing ways to have summit talks with Japan and China on March 24 and 28, respectively. Since both countries are showing positive reactions to the South Korean proposal, the schedules are expected to be fixed soon." For the proposed three day visit to Japan, President Kim is expected to have two meetings with Hosokawa and attend a banquet to be hosted by Emperor Akihito. Kim also plans to deliver a speech on future-oriented relations between South Korea and Japan to the Diet, according to Japanese officials. After visiting Japan, Kim plans to leave for China on March 26 to have summit talks with Jiang and meet with other senior officials including Premier Li Peng. South Korea is studying plans for President Kim to visit Shanghai and the Yanji autonomous region. During his summit meetings in Japan and China, President Kim is expected to discuss the situation on the Korean peninsula and Northeast Asia including the North Korean nuclear problem and ways to promote cooperation in the fields of trade and economy with the two countries.
