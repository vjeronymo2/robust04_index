930823 FT 23 AUG 93 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System The D-Mark's depreciation towards the end of last week has given a more relaxed air to the exchange rate mechanism's grid. Only 7 percentage points divided the strongest currency, the Dutch guilder, from the weakest, the Danish krone, when trading closed on Friday night. That was down from 8.5 percentage points five days earlier. Nevertheless, all the currencies, except the D-Mark and guilder, are still well outside the fluctuation bands that prevailed before the recent ERM crisis.
