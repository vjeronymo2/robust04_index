BFN [Text] Sanaa, June 7 (AFP) -- Southern Yemeni warplanes flying from Aden airport attacked northern forces all along the front line around the southern port city from daybreak Tuesday [7 June], Yemeni Planning Minister 'Abd-al-Karim al-Iryani said. The planes were attacking northern positions from Little Aden, just west of the main city, to Lahij to the north, "continuously bombarding the whole front," he said. "This is a violation of the ceasefire," announced by the Sanaa authorities late Monday, al-Iryani said. The ceasefire took effect midnight Monday (2100 GMT) but collapsed six hours later. Al-Iryani said the Sanaa government would inform UN Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali about the southern air raids "immediately." Asked whether the northern forces were firing back, al-Iryani said "I presume so." A correspondent in Aden said northern artillery was pounding the airport and surrounding area for the third straight day in an attempt to prevent it being used as a base for the warplanes.
