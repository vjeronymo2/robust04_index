August 12, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

BALZER ON WINE: PRIZE GUENOC WINES

WE OFTEN GET LETTERS and telephone calls from wine lovers trying to find wines that have been glowingly described in this column. They say they can't find them. But rest assured, these wines are out there. Here's some advice, from the late wine expert, Andre L. Simon, on how to make your hunt easier:

"Choose a good wine merchant, the best you can find in your neighborhood. Share with him your likes and dislikes. As you become friends, he'll reward you with good finds to your taste. Reward him with your loyal patronage. Stay with him, until he disappoints you; then find another."

Good wine merchants are rare these days, but even supermarkets have managers who appreciate loyal customers. The wine merchant or supermarket manager can order all the discoveries that I've written about. His store is your store.

And now here's some news about wines from Guenoc, another small winery in California, once owned by Edwardian beauty Lillie Langtry, who produced claret on the 37-square-mile estate. After Prohibition, the Guenoc Valley property was used for cattle grazing -- until Orville Magoon, from Hawaii, came along in the early '70s and planted his first vines. From those plantings, gold medal wines are being produced. Trust engineer Magoon to have a vineyard where each vine is monitored by computer to register the soil's moisture and condition. Production has reached 83,000 cases annually.

I'm particularly fond of the Guenoc Petit Sirah, and the 1987 edition ($9) of this wine is already garnering its share of recognition; the 1986 won three gold medals, plus a "Best of Class." The Guenoc 1986 Napa/ Lake Merlot ($12) and the 1985 Cabernet Sauvignon Premiere Cuvee ($17) took gold medals at the InterVin competition in Canada. The Tasters Guild in Fort Lauderdale, Fla., recently gave its top gold to the 1988 Guenoc Valley Genevieve Magoon Vineyard Chardonnay Reserve ($20).

When you find that Guenoc Petit Sirah, bake a batch of fudge brownies, and in the cool of the evening, nibble on one or two between sips of Petit Sirah. Lillie Langtry never dreamed wine from her estate could be so wonderful.
