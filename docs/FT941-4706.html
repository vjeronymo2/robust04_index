940310 FT 10 MAR 94 / Pay group raps failure to create jobs Average labour costs in manufacturing are lower in the UK than southern Italy, one of the poorest regions of the European Union, a report published by the Low Pay Unit yesterday shows. But it says the UK's success in creating jobs has been much less than countries with a higher level of employment and social protection. The research group said: 'Italy created proportionately six times as many jobs as the UK between 1970 and 1992. Germany created almost four times as many jobs and France created twice as many.' De-regulation, it said, had not allowed the UK to create employment and it had not delivered prosperity. 'It has helped to create a situation in which 40 per cent or more of the UK workforce are employed on a part-time, temporary or casual basis, or are classed as freelance or self-employed.' The unit said the UK was uncompetitive because productivity was low, pushing unit labour costs to a level very much higher than the EU average. In part the UK's low productivity was a result of long working hours and poor conditions of employment. Staff turnover and absenteeism were high by European standards and training was poor.
