BFN [From the "Early Morning" newscast] [Text] In an incident described by the PGR [Attorney General's Office] as one of the harshest blows dealt to the drug trafficking mafia, on 15 June, authorities arrested Moises Juarez Ledezma, alias El Moyo and El Dienton, a key figure in the Sonora Cartel headed by Amado Carrillo Fuentes. El Moyo has been charged with crimes against public health, which include charges of funding, planting, cultivating, storing, and transporting drugs. In the areas of Las Minitas and Las Trincheras in Sonora, which cover some 5,800 hectares, authorities confirmed the discovery of caps and jackets with bogus PGR and PJF [Federal Judicial Police] insignias, which were used by cartel members. Meanwhile, the PGR also announced the 12 June arrest of Eduardo Salazar Carrillo, assistant PJF inspector in Sinaloa; Commander 2d Class Cesar Gomez Castaneda; and policemen Gilberto Barrios Barrios and Eric Escamilla Segovia. The four men are facing charges in connection with the possession of $306,921 in cash, several weapons used exclusively by the Army, and radio equipment.
