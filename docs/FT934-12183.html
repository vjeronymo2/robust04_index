931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / Germany given two months to abolish tax aid for national airlines The European Commission has given Germany two months to abolish tax aid to its national airlines on the grounds that it distorts competition in the EC aviation market, Reuter reports from Brussels. Brussels said Bonn had two months to say whether it would abolish by next January the scheme which allows its airlines to pay less tax by applying special depreciation rules to their investments. The scheme is due to finish at the end of next year.
