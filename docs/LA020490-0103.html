February 4, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

THE MOVIE CHART: FILMS NOW GOING INTO PRODUCTION:

Adios (tentative title) (Electric). Shooting in New Orleans. Nicolas Cage, Judge Reinhold and ex-model Erika Anderson star in this dramatic and erotic love triangle set in New Orleans. Cage plays a struggling artist and Reinhold a boyhood friend who both get tangled up in the passions of the beautiful Anderson. Executive producers Nicole Seguin and Staffan Ahrenberg. Producers William Blaylock and Eyal Rimmon. Director Sam Pillsbury. Screenwriter Mari Kornhauser. Also stars Joe Pantoliano, Viveca Lindfors and Aaron Neville. Fall release.

Battle Gear (Concorde). Shooting in the Philippines. David Carradine and "Streets" villain Eb Lottimer star in this tale of a battalion and their special relationship with company leader Carradine as they go into the jungles of Vietnam. Producer Roger Corman. Director Cirio Santiago. Screenwriter David A. Thomas. Also stars Scott Utley, Don Barnes and David Anthony Smith.

Eminent Domain (SVS/Arama). Shooting in Warsaw. A romantic thriller based on actual events, starring Donald Sutherland and Anne Archer as a high level communist official and his wife. Sutherland's a loyal member of the Polish Politburo whose security clearance is suddenly and inexplicably revoked so he and the wife use their love for each other to fend off the tyrannical government and avoid imprisonment. Producer Shimon Arama. Director John Irvin. Screenwriter Andrej Krakowski. Distributor SVS.

The King of the Kickboxers (Seasonal). Shooting in Bangkok and Toronto. Lots of martial arts action as an undercover cop/kickboxing enthusiast goes to Thailand to break up a ring of illegal movie makers. Executive producer Ng See Yuen. Producer/screenwriter Keith W. Strandberg. Stars Loren Avedon. Summer release.

Scissors (D.D.M.). Shooting in L.A. Sharon Stone and "The Stunt Man's" Steven Railsback star in this psychological thriller of a woman whose deep, dark past threatens to jeopardize her very existence. Producers Hal Polaire, Mel Pearl and Dan Levine. Director Frank De Felitta. Screenwriters De Felitta and the late Joyce Selznick. Also stars Vicki Frederick. David Pecchia
