BFN [Excerpt] Government Spokesman Evangelos Venizelos today reiterated Athens' firm decision to insist that the human rights of the Greek minority in Albania be respected in order to improve relations with Tirana. Venizelos reminded the Albanian Government about the recent demarches that Greece made to international organizations about the issue. The spokesman made this statement after [Albanian President Sali] Berisha's regime accused six individuals from North Ipirotes of serious charges following the border incident in Episkopi. Venizelos stressed that the Greek Government has always linked the improvement of Greek-Albanian relations, an improvement that it wants and pursues, to full respect for the Greek Albanian's human rights. He added that Greek-Albanian relations as well as Albania's relations with the European Union [EU] depend on this prerequisite. Venizelos concluded: Greece will lodge new diplomatic demarches with all the international organizations and third countries. It has already lodged demarches with the United Nations, CSCE, the EU, and the U.S. Government. [passage omitted]
