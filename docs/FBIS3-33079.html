Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] This humble shack became the home of former Chiapas governor General Absalon Castellanos, whom the Zapatist guerrillas kidnapped at the start of the conflict. Just a few hours before his release, Univision was able to obtain this exclusive interview somewhere in the jungle. [Begin Castellanos recording] They told me to accompany them, and I went with them. By then they had blindfolded me and tied my hands. I remained that way in different places. I cannot tell exactly where, I couldn't tell until I got here. He [not further identified] held me responsible for these things, in addition to the death of leader Angulo [not further identified] when I served as governor. I could not answer those accusation. He said I knew.... [end recording] Looking aged and with a humble voice softened by his days in captivity, the former governor now says he understands why violence broke out in Chiapas. [Begin Castellanos recording] I do agree that social injustice really exists. Obviously they are greatly removed from civilization and there was this outburst. The outburst could have taken another form since the government has been willing to resolve all these problems. I hope the problem is resolved soon and that we think of ourselves as brothers, Chiapas residents, all of whom bear responsibility for this situation. [end recording]
