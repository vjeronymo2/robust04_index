BFN [Text] The sitting of the Russian Security Council scheduled for June 8 has been postponed for an indefinite time, the presidential press service reported to Interfax. The employee of the press service did not mention the reason of the postponement of this sitting which was to discuss Russian border issues. Meanwhile, according to Interfax, several key members of the Security Council are not at present in Moscow. Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin, a permanent member of the Security Council, is on vacation till June 15. On Tuesday Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev flew to Paris and Istanbul. Defense Minister Pavel Grachev is due to go on a working trip to the Transcaucasian republics this Wednesday. The Federation Council speaker Vladimir Shumeyko is to chair the sitting of CIS Inter-Parliamentary Assembly in St. Petersburg on Wednesday. The State Duma speaker Ivan Rybkin, though he is in Moscow, will participate at the plenary sitting of the parliament's lower chamber on Wednesday which is to discuss the most important issue -- the draft of the federal budget for 1994 in its second reading. Vice Premier Sergey Shakhray, leader of the Federal Counterintelligence Service Sergey Stepashin, Interior Minister Viktor Yerin, Emergency Situations Minister Sergey Shoigu, leader of the Intelligence Service Yevgeniy Primakov, and the Security Council Secretary Oleg Lobov are also members of the Security Council.
