November 13, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: POP / ROCK

Janet Jackson Good for Kids: Janet Jackson's latest album "Rhythm Nation 1814" is among the children's books and entertainment products commended by the quarterly magazine Parents' Choice. The magazine issues annual awards for books, toys, videos, rock albums, computer programs, television shows and magazines considered of high value to kids. "The aim is to help lead mothers and fathers to holiday purchases that have glamour and good sense," editor-in-chief Diana Huss Green said. "Trends this year indicate toy, video, book manufacturers are reflecting society's and parents' concerns about children." The list ranges from the classics of children's entertainment -- like the Disney Channel presentation of "Great Expectations" -- to newcomers such as Jackson. ABC's "The Wonder Years" and the Home Box Office presentation of "Babar" were among the television programs cited. Shauna Snow
