May 26, 1990, Saturday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: THOUSAND OAKS;

5TH ANNUAL FREE RECYCLING DAY SET

Thousands Oaks officials are hoping to collect more than 181 tons of trash at the fifth annual Community Clean-up and Recycling Day today.

The effort, jointly sponsored by the city and the Ventura Regional Sanitation District, includes free recycling operations at Thousand Oaks, Newbury Park and Westlake high schools from 8 a.m. to 4 p.m.

Grahame Watts, the city's recycling coordinator, said workers and volunteers will collect glass, plastic, metal, newspapers, tree trimmings and most household refuse.

Commercial users are not invited to participate, and no hazardous materials are allowed.

Watts said motor oil can be disposed of from 11 a.m. to 7 p.m. the first Thursday of each month at the municipal services facility at 1851 De Havilland Drive.

Watts said last year that the city collected 74 bins -- 181 tons of trash. He said he had no estimate on how much refuse was recycled and how much went to landfills, but that an increasingly large portion has been recycled each year. CHRISTOPHER REYNOLDS
