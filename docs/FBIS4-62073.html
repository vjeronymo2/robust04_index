BFN [Yuliya Kantor report under the "In Brief" rubric: "Borovoy Wants To Be President"] [Text] "My name will emerge in the lists of candidates for the presidency in the upcoming elections because a refusal to contest them would be immoral," Konstantin Borovoy, leader of the Economic Freedom Party [EFP], who arrived in St. Petersburg yesterday, said in a meeting with journalists. The centerpiece of the visit will be talks about the implementation of the EFP's project to lift out of the crisis the country's air space industry, whose main centers are located, in particular, in St. Petersburg and in northwestern Russia. Regarding the situation in the country, Konstantin Borovoy described the just signed agreement on public accord as "the most cynical action conceivable." The EFP leader, who thinks that currently the Russian Government is pursuing an anti-market policy, said in that respect that he would "not join the Chernomyrdin government" under any circumstances.
