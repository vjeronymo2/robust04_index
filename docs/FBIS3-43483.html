Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Eight Portuguese officers and noncommissioned officers are in Luanda to help train the People's Armed Forces for the Liberation of Angola [FAPLA] marines. The presence of those Portuguese soldiers in Luanda is part of military cooperation between the government led by Portuguese Prime Minister Anibal Cavaco Silva and the Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola [MPLA] government. The aim of such cooperation is to strengthen the MPLA's military capacity to destroy the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola (?militarily). This is something Luanda has long sought.
