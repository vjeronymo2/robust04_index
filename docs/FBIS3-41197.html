Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Rostock -- According to the Regional Finance Office in Rostock, the port of Rostock has developed into the biggest German drug trafficking center for cocaine. Since July 1992, a total of 240 kg of cocaine has been impounded in six operations, a spokesman of the authority announced today when presenting the annual 1993 customs report for Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania. Thus, the situation in Rostock is worse than in Hamburg, Bremen, and even Antwerp. The drugs have a street value of approximately 80 million German marks. In the most recent operation this year, in November, police seized 12 kg of cocaine aboard the freighter Bille Trop from Columbia.
