BFN [Text] Warsaw, April 6 -- Seventy per cent of respondents to the "Demoskop" opinion polling centre survey sized up negatively the work of President Lech Walesa over the past few weeks. Eighteen per cent sized up Walesa's work in this period positively and only very positively. The remaining respondents did not express their opinion on this topic. Forty-four per cent of the respondents sized up Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak's performance positively, 36 per cent -- negatively and two per cent respondents assessed it very positively and 18 per cent did not have an opinion on this topic. Forty-three per cent of respondents gave negative opinion of the government performance over the past few weeks. Twenty-nine per cent offered positive opinion and 7 per cent of respondents had a very low opinion of the government work. Every fifth respondent did not express opinion on this topic. According to 55 per cent of respondents to a poll by the Public Opinion Research Centre (CBOS), people ruling before 1989 are now returning to government and ministerial posts. In the opinion of 41 per cent of respondents, such persons return to provincial authorities. According to 34 per cent, these people return to posts in the management of state enterprises. Thirty-three per cent think that people who ruled before 1989 are now returning to boards of banks.
