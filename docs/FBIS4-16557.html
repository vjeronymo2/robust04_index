BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow, 15 Apr--"Accords on the division of the Russian and Ukrainian Navies based on the shores of the Black Sea coast will be reached within 10 days." Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk said this after the conclusion of talks held today between the Russian and Ukrainian delegations at the President Hotel in Moscow. According to Kravchuk, the two sides "have instructed the Foreign Ministries to begin preparing a full-scale treaty between Russia and Ukraine." He said that "a protocol stating the intention of the two countries to cooperate in the military-technological sphere was signed by them for the first time". Kravchuk also said that the question of the resignation of Black Sea Fleet Commander [Eduard] Baltin had not been discussed but that "the Ukrainian side did not insist, as a matter of principle, on his resignation at the talks that took place." Kravchuk said that accords had been reached with regard to the location of the two countries' naval forces and that their parameters had been established in percentage terms. Summing up today's talks, he added: "There are no issues between Ukraine and Russia that we are unable to resolve. We have fully resolved all problems that existed in our bilateral relations."
