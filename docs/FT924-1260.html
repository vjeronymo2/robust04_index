921218 FT 18 DEC 92 / Author focuses on credit 'crunch' FINANCIAL deregulation enabled domestic financial markets to catch up with international ones but also resulted in an accumulation of personal and corporate debt, which is causing a credit 'crunch', according to a book published yesterday. Mr Christopher Johnson writes in New Players, New Rules, Financing the 1990s, that households and companies are saving more and borrowing less. New Players, New Rules. Lafferty Publications, The Tower, IDA Centre, Pearse Street, Dublin 2, Ireland. Pounds 545.
