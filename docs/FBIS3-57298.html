Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Yuriy Likhopek and Serafim Bykhun] [Text] Orel January 24 TASS--Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin visited several agricultural enterprises in the Orel region, central Russia, on Monday [24 January]. Chernomyrdin is in Orel to attend an agricultural conference opening here on Tuesday. The conference will discuss measures to ensure fuller use of the agroindustrial complex in central Russia and better food supplies to the country as a whole. The conference, which will be attended by the leaders of the federal bodies of power and administration heads of 18 central Russian regions and their deputies in charge of agriculture, is expected to hear the prime minister's first statement on agricultural policy after recent reshuffles in the government. Informed sources told ITAR-TASS that the conference is expected to result in a government resolution on working out a programme for the development of the agroindustrial complexes of the central Russian regions for the period up to the year 2000. Today conference participants visited some agricultural enterprises in the Orel region and food-processing factories. They also had conversations with private farmers. Cherrnomyrdin was accompanied in his trip by Vice-Prime Minister Aleksandr Zaveryukha, Central Bank chairman Viktor Gerashchenko and Orel regional administration head Yegor Stroyev.
