Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, March 19 (TANJUG) -- A U.N. officer has resolutely denied allegations by Moslem authorities in Sarajevo that the explosion that left three persons wounded in the Alipasino Polje suburb on Saturday was caused by a Serb mortar shell. Lieutenant-Colonel [name indistinct] Pernod of the French battalion within the U.N. Protection Force in war-torn Bosnia-Herzegovina said that the explosion was the result of a rifle-launched grenade, the AFP reports. The maximum range of such a grenade is 600 metres and the nearest Serb positions are 1,500 metres away from the place where the explosion occurred, Pernod specified.
