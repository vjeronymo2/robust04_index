Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Mexican elections scheduled for 21 August 1994. On 3 March, Luis Donaldo Colosio, presidential candidate for the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI), urged opposition candidates not to use their campaigns to divide Mexicans. During a visit to Nayarit, Colosio stated: "I want to be president of Mexico to lead the change and transformation of social conditions in our motherland." (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 3 March 94) Colosio officially has registered his presidential candidacy with the Federal Electoral Institute (IFE). Commenting on rumors Peace Commissioner Camacho Solis might run as the PVEM candidate, he has said: "I have great respect for Manuel Camacho. We are members of a party committed to democracy. I have great respect for him, and I support his actions in the Chiapas peace talks. I believe the pluralistic nature of our modern political parties is a product of Mexican society." (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 5 March 94) Members of the Permanent Indian Council, a group that comprises almost half of the 56 ethnic groups in Mexico and has no affiliation with the PRI, visited Luis Colosio on 7 March. The Indians said their urgent needs and extreme poverty drove them to seek support from the PRI candidate. (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 8 Mar 94) Colosio promised on 7 March if elected he will "establish a new pact between the state and 56 ethnic communities to guarantee respect for human dignity, autonomous rights, legal and social justice, and democracy for 10 million Indians, who are part of the poorest of the poor." Colosio also said: "It is time to do justice to our Indians, to grow and share the fruits of that growth more equitably in our motherland, because progress makes no sense if it does not imply more income for the peasants, workers, and the Indian families of all and each one of our communities." (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 8 Mar 94 pp 48, 52) Colosio has announced he will recommend to his party women's participation be doubled in the PRI proposal of congressional candidates. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 9 Mar 94) Rafael Aguilar Talamantes, presidential candidate for the Cardenist Front for National Reconstruction Party, has officially registered his presidential candidacy. Aguilar has said he would be willing to decline his candidacy in favor of Zapatista National Liberation Army (EZLN) Subcommander Marcos. (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 4 March 94) Commenting on the peace talks in San Cristobal de las Casas, National Action Party (PAN) presidential candidate Diego Fernandez has said: "I hope that efforts by the guerrillas, the government, the mediators, and society in general truly lead to a national de jure state and thus to a Mexico free of lies." (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 4 March 94) In Campeche, Fernandez has said: "We will never favor violence. We understand the Chiapas guerrilla movement has justifiable causes, but violence will never give us the peace, prosperity, and justice Mexico wants and deserves." He has also said: "The big challenge this country is facing, the big project to which all Mexicans should commit is so simple and deep that it can be expressed in a few words: The challenge modern-day Mexico faces is to comply with the law." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 7 Mar 94) In Quintana Roo, Fernandez has praised the results of the peace talks in Chiapas, saying: "Other countries have not achieved over many years what Mexico has now accomplished, thanks to the government's prudence." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 5 Mar 94) In Hidalgo, Fernandez has said: "If the people, if millions of Mexicans participate cleanly in these elections, we will leave behind on 21 August a history of corruption, fraud, deception, lies, and we can welcome a true democracy." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 9 Mar 94) During the weekend of 5 March, the PRD registered officially before the IFE Cuauhtemoc Cardenas as its presidential candidate. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 7 March 94) Cardenas has said he will propose an end to the import of farm products, which have harmed Mexican peasants, if he is elected president. Cardenas has added what farmers need are credits, participation, and opportunities to produce, as well as protection against unfair competition from imported low-priced farm products. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 8 March 94 pp 48, 52) Cardenas has reiterated his demand of an independent electoral authority, saying the current electoral law can still be reformed. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 9 March 94) During a visit to Guadalajara, Alvaro Perez Trevino, presidential candidate for the Authentic Party of the Mexican Revolution, has denied he will decline his candidacy in favor of Peace Commissioner Manuel Camacho Solis. Perez Trevino has said: "We have not talked about this with Mr. Camacho Solis; we did not even know he was in charge of the peace talks in Chiapas. It is all a terrible rumor." He added he will register officially his candidacy on 11 March. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 8 March 94) Labor Party presidential candidate Cecilia Soto has rejected the presence of international observers during the 21 August elections. Soto has said Mexicans are able to solve their internal problems without observers. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 4 March 94) Soto has said: "We cannot be frugal with democracy. Social peace in Mexico will be at stake if these elections are not satisfactory; therefore, we should do all we can for these elections to run as smoothly as possible." (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 9 March 94) Jorge Gonzalez Torres, presidential candidate for the Mexican Green Ecologist Party (PVEM), has met with EZLN peasants and urged them to work together to solve Chiapas' agricultural problems. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 4 March 94) Gonzalez has said there should be bilingual teachers so residents of areas where Indian dialects are spoken can learn in their native language. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 8 March 94) In Sinaloa, National Opposition Union presidential candidate Pablo Madero has expressed his satisfaction with the opening of the congressional special sessions and indicated this is the only way to achieve truly democratic electoral reform. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 4 March 94) Madero has reiterated the need to reform electoral laws. In Ciudad Obregon, he has said: "It is urgent that electoral bandits be punished without any right to bail." He has said he would fight impunity and corruption in the government if elected president. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 8 March 94) Marcela Lombardo, presidential candidate for the Popular Socialist Party (PSP), has expressed her discontent with the decision by the PAN candidate to hold televised debates with only the PRI and the Democratic Revolutionary Party (PRD). She has said conditions should have been established to make debates all-inclusive. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 4 March 94) Five of the nine presidential candidates have registered officially their candidacies thus far. Lombardo registered her candidacy on 9 March. Lombardo said strengthening our institutions and democracy is the only way to resolve the crisis. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 9 March 94) On 6 March, the PRD's fifth plenum voted against holding internal elections to choose its candidates for deputies, senators, and assembly people, considering no political conditions exist "to preclude friction and erosion of members' unity." The plenum decided to focus on the presidential campaign. The party's national council decided during a closed-door meeting the positions in question would first be decided by state councils and executive committees. If disputes arise, the national council will have the final word. (Mexico City LA JORNADA in Spanish 7 March 94 p 11)
