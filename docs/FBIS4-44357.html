BFN [Unattributed article: "Martino Going to Bosnia, Berlusconi Pleased With Events"] [Excerpt] (ANSA) Corfu, June 25 -- [passage omitted] In another development in Corfu today, where the EC summit is drawing to a close, Italian Premier Silvio Berlusconi said, "We are working very well to build relations with everyone," with reference to his and [Foreign Minister] Martino's first outing with the other European leaders. He said he was satisfied with the way the new Italian government has joined the complex dialogue at the summit level. He reported that he and his foreign minister are fielding many questions on Italian positions on various issues and added, "The weight of the Italian delegation is expressed in the substance of the interventions." In this connection, he said Martino's remarks were on joblessness were "enlightening." Talking to newspersons last night, Berlusconi managed to make a joke out of the misgiving expressed in the recent past by the Greek foreign minister, Theodhoros Pangalos, who cast doubt on the "democratic legitimacy" of the Italian government because of the presence in the cabinet of National Alliance ministers. "He's always close, he doesn't leave me for a minute," said the Italian premier who also affirmed that he found Pangalos "very likable." "And then, he speaks excellent Italian," he added.
