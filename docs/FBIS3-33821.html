Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, vice chairman of the Presidential Council, received in Aden today brother Badr Hammam, Egypt's assistant foreign minister. During the meeting, the two sides discussed brotherly bilateral relations and ways to reinforce and develop these relations. They also exchanged views on issues and developments of interest to the two fraternal countries. Brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, vice chairman of the Presidential Council, reaffirmed our country's interest in reinforcing fraternal relations and strengthening deep-rooted bilateral ties to serve the interests of both the Yemeni and Egyptian people and the issues of interest to our Arab and Islamic nation. During his meeting with the Egyptian assistant foreign minister, brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid spoke about developments in the national arena, noting the efforts exerted to overcome the obstacles and challenges facing the march toward unity and democracy and a modern Yemeni state in light of the results achieved by the Political Forces Dialogue Committee and the Document of Pledge and Accord initialed in Aden. For his part, brother Badr Hammam conveyed His Excellency Egyptian President Muhammad Husni Mubarak's greetings to his brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, Presidential Council vice chairman, and reaffirmed his country's interest in supporting the march for unity and democracy, noting that it is an important national and pan-Arab accomplishment. The brother vice chairman of the Presidential Council asked his brother the Egyptian assistant foreign minister to convey his greetings to His Excellency Egyptian President Husni Mubarak, wishing him health and happiness and progress and prosperity for the Egyptian people. The meeting was attended by brother Muhammad Salih al-(Qubaysi), acting undersecretary of the Aden Foreign Ministry branch, and brother Mahmud 'Atta Harun, the Egyptian ambassador to Yemen.
