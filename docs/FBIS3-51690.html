Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 24 Jan (NA) -- Upon arriving here on an official visit, Chilean Defense Minister Patricio Rojas said this morning that his country "is not worried" about the purchase of airplanes or any other type of materiel by the Argentine Armed Forces. Rojas was met by Argentine Defense Minister Oscar Camilion; Lieutenant General Mario Candido Diaz, the joint staff chief; and other Defense Ministry officials and military leaders. The Chilean minister will remain here just a few hours, as he is scheduled to return to Santiago this afternoon after meeting with Camilion and lunching at the Alvear Palace Hotel. In remarks to the journalists, the two defense ministers ruled out the possibility of discussing the Laguna del Desierto border problem. For his part, Camilion said the purchase of the A-4 Skyhawk planes will be decided during the meetings that Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella will hold with U.S. officials in the United States. Camilion also confirmed a donation by the United States of three Hercules C-130 planes that will possibly arrive in Argentina before the end of the month.
