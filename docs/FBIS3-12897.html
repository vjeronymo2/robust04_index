Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 4 Mar (MENA)--Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat will arrive in Cairo within the next 48 hours for a visit to Egypt during the visit, he will hold urgent consultations with President Husni Mubarak on the latest developments after the al-Ibrahimi Mosque massacre in Hebron. 'Arafat will meet with Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa and Dr. Usamah al-Baz, director of President Mubarak's Office for Political Affairs. He and the Egyptian officials will discuss ways to achieve the Palestinian demands that the PLO has set as conditions for the resumption of peace talks with Israel. The PLO suspended its participation in these talks because of the al-Ibrahimi Mosque massacre. Palestinian Ambassador in Cairo Sa'id Kamal said Egypt--President Mubarak, the government, and the great people--has been and will remain the main supporter of the Arab and Palestinian people. In a statement to MENA, he added that the growing support of the Egyptian and Arab nations for the condition of the Palestinian people in the occupied territories following the al-Ibrahimi Mosque massacre confirms Egypt's continuing, pioneering role in maintaining pan-Arab security.
