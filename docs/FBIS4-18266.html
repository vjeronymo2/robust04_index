BFN [Text] The official talks aimed at developing Turkish-Libyan relations were held in Ankara today. 'Umar al-Muntasir, Libyan secretary general of the People's Bureau for External Liaison and International Cooperation who arrived in Ankara on an official visit yesterday, called on Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin this morning. Later the Turkish and Libyan delegations headed by the two ministers met. In a statement issued prior to the meeting, Cetin said that bilateral relations were raised to their highest level through the support extended by Libya during the Cyprus Peace Operation and continued as a result of Turkey's contribution to Libya's development and advancement. He noted that the monies owed to Turkish contracting firms in Libya are being paid according to a system that uses oil as payment. Cetin added that all debts are expected to be paid within this year. Al-Muntasir stressed that Libya attaches great importance to its relations with Turkey and that it wants to cooperate in all fields. He will continue his contacts this afternoon as well and will leave the country on 2 April.
