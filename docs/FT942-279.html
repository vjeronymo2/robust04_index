940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / Li Peng warns UK on trade China's premier yesterday warned Britain that cool political relations were bound to affect economic ties and praised Germany for its long-standing co-operation, Reuter reports from Beijing. Mr Li Peng told reporters that differences between China and Britain over democratic reforms for Hong Kong had affected overall relations. 'We hope to see the economic and trade relations between Britain and China develop but it is not possible to free the overall relations from the adverse effects of the cool political relations,' he said. Mr Li's comments came before leaving on a visit to Austria, Germany and Romania, accompanied by economic officials and more than 30 business leaders. 'We have long-standing co-operation with Germany, which is is our biggest partner in western Europe,' Mr Li said. Germany is the biggest European investor in China. Chinese customs data for January to May show trade with Germany at Dollars 4.19bn, up 32.7 per cent over the same period in 1993.
