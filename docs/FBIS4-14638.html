BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Maloveryan] [Text] Tallinn, April 2 (TASS) -- Estonian President Lennart Meri expressed condolences to the family of the border guard killed at a check point near the town of Pechory in the Pskov region, in a telephone conversation with the Russian Ambassador to Estonia Aleksandr Trofimov on Friday. According to earlier reports, two unidentified persons approached the check point from the territory of Estonia in the morning on Friday. One of them fired a pistol at Russian border guards, killing one and wounding two. The two attackers made off to Estonia. At Russia's request, Estonian law-enforcement agencies are undertaking to detain the wrongdoers. Tarmo Kyuts, Estonian top border guard commander, expressed regret over the incident and was quoted by the local media as saying "there is no evidence that the criminals approached the check point from the Estonian territory".
