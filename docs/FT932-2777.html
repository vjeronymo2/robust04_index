930617 FT 17 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television Why has the spirit of the times changed so much that it is now widely regarded as admirable to increase company profits by sacking thousands of people? Could it be that after the millions of family tragedies in a major war each country experiences a strong sense of brotherhood but that today we are a long way away from any such war? The thought is prompted by Business Matters which reports on 'The House That Jack Built': Jack Welch's 'down-sizing' of the General Electric company in the US which has involved 'laying off' 100,000 workers (7.20 BBC2). Practically all Italians seem to agree their country is undergoing a dramatic, albeit virtually bloodless, revolution. They differ only in their predictions of the outcome: some believe it will lead to the first stable Italian state, others that the old power centres (Mafia, political parties, church) will ensure even greater conspiracy than before. As part of its 'Italian Week' The Late Show holds a studio discussion (11.15 BBC2). True Stories offers yet another documentary about American PoWs supposedly still missing after the Vietnam war (9.35 C4).
