BFN [Report by Jose Quintero de Leon] [Text] After six years of negotiations that began with the military government of Panama, the Republic of Panama yesterday formally established diplomatic relations with the Russian Federation in a ceremony attended by Panamanian Foreign Minister Jose Raul Mulino and his Russian counterpart, Andrey Kozyrev, in Moscow. The Panamanian Foreign Ministry disclosed that the two ministers signed an agreement called the "Declaration of Principles of Relations and Cooperation Between the Russian Federation and the Republic of Panama." The essence of the agreement is the expansion of the respective countries' consular missions to the level of embassies. Consular relations began in 1991 when President Guillermo Endara and President Boris Yeltsin decided to establish consulates to handle trade relations and cooperation between the two countries. A Foreign Ministry bulletin reports Foreign Minister Kozyrev said that the Russian Federation considers Panama a future economic and financial power, and that he wants to strengthen trade relations. In addition, Minister Mulino offered Panama's cooperation on banking issues, the Free Trade Zone, the reinsurance system, and on "maintaining constant consultation on Latin American geopolitical issues."
