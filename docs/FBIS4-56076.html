BFN [Text] Last night, General Michael Rose, commander of the UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR] in Bosnia-Herzegovina, held talks with President of the Presidency Alija Izetbegovic on the implementation of the cease-fire and the deployment of peace forces on the east bank of the Drina River near Gorazde. This was announced today at the UNPROFOR command in Sarajevo. General Rose proposed that Bosnia-Herzegovina Army units should withdraw from the east bank of the Drina River and that UNPROFOR units should be deployed in their place to protect Gorazde and maintain the cease-fire. On the basis of UN Security Council Resolution 913 and the NATO ultimatum, Bosnia-Herzegovina Army units are not obliged to withdraw from either this part of town or the 3-km exclusion zone around Gorazde. UNPROFOR representative Major Annink said that the NATO ultimatum was still in force. We [words indistinct] the situation realistically and we are trying to solve the problem in Gorazde through negotiations, said Major Annink today. He added that General Rose yesterday attempted to talk to the aggressor's leader Radovan Karadzic, but that he had not succeeded in this.
