March 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OUTTAKES: TRUTH -- OR TRASH?

Stephen J. Cannell and co-producers are getting ready to shoot a "docu-re-creation" of the personal story of director Peter Bogdanovich and his wife, Louise Stratten -- over their objections. It will be one of five segments of an ABC reality-TV special, "Scandals," in the works for May sweeps. The couple has let it be known "they consider it an invasion of privacy," said Bogdanovich's attorney, Bill Peiffer.

Hugh Hefner and the late Dorothy Stratten will be also be depicted in scenes re-creating moments from their lives; casting for look-alikes is under way, with production to begin this week.

Bogdanovich, 49, who was romantically involved with and wrote a book about murdered Playboy centerfold Dorothy Stratten, recently married the late actress' younger sister, Louise, 20.

Peiffer said he has informed the "Scandals" producers -- Loreen Arbus and Meredith MacRae in association with Cannell -- that "neither Mr. Bogdanovich nor his wife will be cooperating or participating in this project." He added that no legal action will be considered until his clients have viewed the special.

Arbus told us: "Everything we're doing is a matter of public record."

She said that the segment relies on interviews with persons close to the couple, including Louise's closest friend. "Every statement made, every scene re-enacted is validated by a first-hand or credible published source."

Arbus further insisted: "By no stretch of the imagination can this be associated with tabloid or -- God forbid -- trash TV. Stephen Cannell would never be associated with anything that was not legitimate and neither would I. For one thing, my parents would disown me."

Arbus' dad is the legendary Leonard H. Goldenson, founding father of ABC. From Stacy Jenel Smith
