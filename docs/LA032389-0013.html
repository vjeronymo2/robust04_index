March 23, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

WOMEN'S DAY

In your description of a speech by Raisa Gorbachev, you refer to International Women's Day as a Soviet holiday (Part I, March 8). Given that the whole month of March is designated National Women's History Month across the U.S., I think your readers deserve a clarification about the origin of International Women's Day, particularly since the holiday originated in this country.

The holiday, which is observed all over the world, commemorates a March 8, 1908, march of many thousands of young women and their supporters. Their demands were twofold -- women's right to vote, and an end to child labor in the sweatshops of New York City's garment district. The march took place in New York City.

MARK EPSTEIN

Los Angeles
