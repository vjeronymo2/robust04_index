November 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

Yonder, bouncing back from his loss in the Young America last month, won the $242,800 Remsen for 2-year-olds by three-quarters of a length over Roanoke at Aqueduct in New York.

In the $182,400 co-featured Demoiselle Stakes for 2-year-old fillies, Rootentootenwooten won in a driving finish, upsetting the favored Bookkeeper.

Roanoke, ridden by Jose Santos and carrying 122 pounds, had a clear lead entering the stretch but could not hold off the charge of Yonder, ridden by Eddie Maple.

Yonder carried 115 pounds over the 1 1/8 miles in 1:51 1/5, earning $145,680 for Claiborne Farm with his third win in seven career starts. The son of Seattle Slew returned $12.80, $3.80 and $3.40. Roanoke paid $2.60 and $2.40.

Alexander G. Campbell Jr.'s Rootentootenwooten, ridden by Jerry Bailey and carrying 112 pounds, finished a head in front of Bookkeeper, ridden by Santos and carrying 113 pounds. Why Go On Dreaming was another neck back.

Rootentootenwooten went 1 1/8 miles on the fast track in 1:51 3/5, earning $109,440 with her second win in six career starts. She returned $24.60, $8.20 and $3.60.
