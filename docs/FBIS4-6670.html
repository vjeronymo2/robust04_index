BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 21 April Morning Edition] [Text] In relation to the policy coordination being undertaken by the ruling coalition parties in line with their selection of a successor to Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, a government source said on the evening of 20 April that as far as measures to be taken by the government in the event of economic sanctions being imposed against the DPRK are concerned, "although they are talking about taking action within the framework of the Constitution, depending on future developments, legal amendments may of course become necessary." He thus indicated that the possibility remains that emergency legislation may become necessary. With regard to the increase in the consumption tax rate, this source criticized the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ], saying: "The SDPJ is terming administrative reform the source of revenue to cover tax cuts. This is nonsense if you do not have any concrete proposals."
