941126 FT 26 NOV 94 / Weekend Money (Investing for Income): Gilt issues - best value v tax status ----------------------------------------------------------------------- All gilts with a fixed redemption date have two yields. The current yield (also known as the interest yield) relates the coupon to the current share price and tells you what your current income is. The redemption yield also takes account of the capital gain - or loss - you can expect to make if you hold the stock until redemption in order to calculate your total return. Our table shows that total return net of income tax for different types of taxpayer. Your capital gain on a gilt -a UK government bond - is tax free. However, you pay tax on the interest.
