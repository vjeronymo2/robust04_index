BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 23 (KCNA) -- The second anniversary of the publication of the Pyongyang declaration "Let Us Defend and Advance the Cause of Socialism" was observed in Peru and Russia. The declaration was adopted in April 1992 by leaders and representatives of political parties advocating socialism who came to Pyongyang to celebrate the 80th birthday of the great leader President Kim Il-song. Genaro Ledesma Izquieta, chairman of the People's Front of Workers, Peasants and Students of Peru, made a speech at a meeting held at the front on April 13. He said it is only two years since the adoption of the Pyongyang declaration, but more than 200 parties have signed it and its vitality is growing day by day. "The Pyongyang declaration, which clarified the superiority of socialism to capitalism, the inevitability of the victory of socialism and the tasks and ways of defending and advancing socialism, has served as a weapon for the socialists in their struggle," he said, adding that his front which also signed the declaration would resolutely fight to defend and advance the cause of socialism. In his speech at a meeting with representatives of Russian political parties held in Moscow April 20, V.I. Anpilov, secretary of the Central Committee of the Russian Communist Workers' Party, said "the Pyongyang declaration is the hope of our cause and the beacon of socialism for the communists." "In the struggle for socialism," he continued, "the Workers' Party of Korea and its leaders Comrade Kim Il-song and Comrade Kim Chong-il show an example." A.A. Rapin, secretary of the Central Committee of the All-Union Bolshevik Communist Party, in his speech said the Pyongyang declaration opened a new high stage in the development of the international communist movement and the working-class movement. "This declaration will be recorded in history along with the `communist manifesto'," he said.
