Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Kuwaiti Oil Minister 'Ali Ahmad al-Baghli has expressed satisfaction at OPEC's decision tonight to maintain its current output ceiling until the end of this year. The oil minister told KUNA that the agreement is the best that could be reached under the current circumstances. He said he believes that demand for oil and prices will improve during the third and fourth quarters of this year. Concerning OPEC's stance on non-OPEC countries, the oil minister stated that the organization is waiting to get a firm commitment from those countries to freeze their actual output, although it has received a verbal pledge on cooperation with OPEC members.
