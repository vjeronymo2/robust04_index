BFN [Text] The government disclosed at the House of Councillors' Budget Committee today that it is studying concrete measures to be taken if economic sanctions are imposed against North Korea, including a halt on remittances, and restrictions on exports and imports. According to Justice Minister Hiroshi Nakai, Finance Minister Hirohisa Fujii, Minister of International Trade and Industry Eijiro Hata, and Transport Minister Nobuaki Futami, specific measures being considered include: A ban on North Korean diplomats' entry into Japan, a halt to remittances under the Foreign Exchange Control Act, restrictions on exports and imports and the services trade under the Export and Import Trade Control Order, surveillance of ships by the Maritime Safety Agency following amendment of the Trade Control Order. The ministers disclosed that the concerned government ministries and agencies were studying concrete steps to restrict personnel traffic and exports and imports.
