BFN [Excerpt] The French soldiers yesterday pushed their patrols forward as far as 20 km from battlefields in Gikongoro Prefecture. According to Captain Emmanuel Ndahiro of the Rwandan Patriotic Army [RPA], French soldiers found to be standing in RPA's way will be asked to account for it. Although Paris describes the present mission as a humanitarian one, nobody is fooled by the real intentions of its intervention which is to protect criminals. Moreover, these criminals have started (?rejoicing). Prime Minister Kambanda, the so-called prime minister, for example, announced clearly the day before the French troops landed in Goma and Bukavu in eastern Zaire, that his men had received new consignments of arms which would enable them to celebrate the 32nd independence anniversary in Kigali on 1 July.
