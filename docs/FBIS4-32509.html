BFN [Text] A delegation of the Turkish Defense Ministry, led by Colonel General Teoman Ekran, this morning arrived on a two-day visit to the Croatian Defense Ministry. They were welcomed at Zagreb airport by their host Brigadier General Antun Tus, military adviser to the Croatian president. On the first day of their visit to Croatia the Turkish delegation visited the naval institute in Zagreb. [Begin recording] [Tus] The aim of the visit is come to know each other better, to see at what technical and technological level we are both at, what production lines we have, what expertise and scientific potential we have and to achieve some cooperation in future related to the development and production of certain systems included in general economic programs and, certainly also, some programs which can be significant for the defense of the country. [end recording] Questions related to the security in this part of Europe were also discussed. Croatia's integration in Partnership for Peace as soon as possible was on the agenda. The talks are just the beginning of the cooperation between the two countries, Col-Gen Teoman Ekran said. [Begin recording] [Ekran, speaking in Turkish, with superimposed Croatian translation] As you know, the Republic of Turkey was one of the first countries to recognize the Republic of Croatia. This is a model start for the future cooperation between the two countries. Turkey supports Croatian peace efforts for the resolution of the crisis in Bosnia and lends its full support to the Bosnia-Herzegovina federation. After the visit to the naval institute the Turkish delegation visited Koncar complex. Visits to Karlovac and Turanj are scheduled for tomorrow as well as talks with Croatian Defense Minister Gojko Susak. [end recording]
