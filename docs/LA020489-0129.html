February 4, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

ALMANAC / SURVEY OF CLERICAL SALARIES: NORTH COUNTY WORKERS HAVE EDGE ON PAYDAY

Office workers in north Orange County generally are paid higher salaries than their counterparts in the south county, according to an annual survey by Irvine-based Thomas Temporaries.

Among 15 state urban areas surveyed, north Orange County ranked second or third in beginning monthly salaries for three of nine job categories, and no lower than eighth in the other categories.

South Orange County clerical salaries were in the middle range of state areas surveyed but also ranked no lower than eighth in any of the nine occupations.

South county will see more clerical job growth than north county in 1989, the survey indicated. And 46% of the 96 south county businesses surveyed plan to hire additional office staff in the first 6 months of this year, the survey said, with word-processing typists, general clerks and accounting clerks in greatest demand.

In north county, 32% of the 99 businesses surveyed plan staff increases, with the greatest demand for general clerks and word-processing typists.
