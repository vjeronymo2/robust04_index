Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] The first aircraft landed at Tuzla airport at 0935 this morning, carrying 22 metric tons of food, medicine, and seeds, and marking the airport's official opening for the reception of humanitarian aid. On board the aircraft with its UN markings were Yasushi Akashi, special envoy of the UN secretary general, and General de Lapresle, commander of the UN peace troops for former Yugoslavia. At Dubrave [Tuzla] Airport, these esteemed guests were greeted by Stjepan Kljuic, member of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Presidency, General Rasim Delic, commander of the General Staff of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Armed Forces, Salih Kulenovic, coordinating minister for the Tuzla- Drina region, and representatives of the Tuzla district and commune.
