Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Acting Prime Minister Dr. Ma'n Abu-Nuwar announces to the Jordanian family, with great sorrow and sadness, the martyrdom of Jordanian diplomat Na'ib 'Umran al-Ma'ayitah, first secretary at the Jordanian Embassy in Beirut, who was the target of a treacherous attack in Beirut today. [passage omitted] In another development, the Foreign Ministry issued a statement on this sinful action. Following is the text of the statement: We received with great sorrow and pain the news about the martyrdom of one of Jordan's sons, Na'ib 'Umran al-Ma'ayitah, the counselor at the Jordanian Embassy in Beirut, this morning following a sinful attack while he was on his way from his residence to his office at the embassy. While condemning this brutal attack, the Jordanian Government will embark on the necessary measures to learn about the circumstances surrounding the attack, and efforts to pursue the terrorists and criminals who are responsible for this criminal action, regardless of the party to which they belong. The Jordanian Government also condemns all forms of terrorism that target innocent peoples. Lebanese foreign and interior ministers, accompanied by commander of public security and other Lebanese officials, called on the Jordanian Embassy in Beirut, and expressed their denunciation of this criminal action and their full readiness to pursue and identify the perpetrators. An honorary mission left for Beirut in a special airplane to escort the body of the deceased back home.
