BFN [Text] Hong Kong, May 27 (XINHUA) -- An agreement aimed at promoting and protecting mutual investment between Hong Kong and Sweden was signed here today. Speaking at the signing ceremony, T.H. Chau, secretary for trade and industry, said that the agreement would further strengthen the excellent economic ties between the two areas. According to Chau, the agreement provides investors from both sides with guarantees of fair treatment, the unrestricted right to transfer investments and returns, and proper compensation in the event of losses owing to expropriation. The agreement, the fourth of its kind Hong Kong has signed, will enter into force 30 days after signature and remain effective for an initial period of 15 years.
