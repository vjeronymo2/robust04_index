September 24, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

RISKY BUSINESS

I read with interest the Looks column "Risky Business," by Paddy Calistro (Aug. 13), about in-office surgery. As an anesthesia provider in Beverly Hills and West Los Angeles for a number of plastic surgeons, I am in full agreement that some less-than-optimal office surgi-centers exist.

Although many procedures can be performed using only local anesthetics, longer and more involved procedures require heavy sedation or general anesthesia. Only a trained anesthesia provider can do this safely. Your article ignored the fact that a number of these procedures are now performed, using potent sedatives and/or anesthetic drugs, by untrained hands.

DAVID J. FOURNIER, Los Angeles
