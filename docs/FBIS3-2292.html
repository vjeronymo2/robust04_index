Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Danielle Yang] [Text] Taipei, March 3 (CNA) -- Approved Taiwan investment in Mainland China reached US$3.59 billion during the 1991-1993 period, according to statistics released by the Investment Commission Wednesday [2 March]. About 9,830 investment projects were approved during the period, with 9,329 of those investments made in 1993, the commission said. Investment value in 1993 alone also hit US$3.168 billion, the statistics showed. In 1990 and 1991, the government approved a total of 237 and 264 investment projects, worth US$174 million and US$247 million respectively. Most of the approved investments went to Guangdong, Jiangsu, Hebei, Zhejiang, and Shandong Provinces in that order, the commission said. Approved Taiwan investors focused their attention on manufacturing industries in the mainland, including the production of electronic and electric, precision machinery, plastic, food and beverage, basic metal, and chemical products. Meanwhile, unofficial tallies put Taiwan's total investment in the mainland at more than US$11 billion during the three-year period, with large amounts invested in Fujian Province. The great difference reveals the large amount of unofficial Cross-Taiwan Strait investment and indicates that the majority of Taiwan investors in the mainland fail to register their investments with government authorities.
