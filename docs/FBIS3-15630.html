Language: English Article Type:BFN [From "News Bulletin" No. 450] [Text] Vilnius, March 4, ELTA -- French embassy informed ELTA it denied a wrong information published in the daily "LIETUVOS RYTAS" (March 2, 1994) about Lithuanian-French military co-operation. The embassy said that in January, 1993, French authorities presented Lithuanian Army a great amount of military technique [as received] and paid all its shipping expenses. The gifts included one complete field hospital similar to those now used by the French Army in former Yugoslavia as well as communications equipment, not the most modern but still used in some French gendarmerie units and possible to use in a case of a possible mobilization. The embassy also said 30 jeeps used for four years and technically in order with spare parts had been presented. Last November, a delegation of French technicians had visited Lithuania seeking to arrange one more package of spare parts.
