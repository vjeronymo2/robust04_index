BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Roman Zadunayskiy] [Text] Moscow, 8 May -- It has been decided to evacuate the Russian citizens remaining in Yemen on 9 May, using two TU-154 aircraft, which will fly out to Sanaa on Monday. There are plans to send another plane to Djibouti. Marina Ryklina, head of the press center of the Russian Ministry for Emergencies and Natural Disasters, told an ITAR-TASS correspondent that it is planned to bring out 152 Russians, including embassy employees, during the second stage of the evacuation of Russian citizens from Yemen, where a bloody civil war is underway. There are also 78 Russians and Americans and eight French citizens in the port of Al Hudaydah. U.S. and French spokesmen have officially asked the Russian Ministry of Emergencies & Natural Disasters to help evacuate their people to Djibouti and thence to Europe by Russian plane. The Russian cargo vessel "Khudozhnik Kustodiyev" is at the Djibouti roadstead and is ready to take people aboard. It has already been reported that a plane took 113 Russian citizens and two Slovaks from Djibouti to Moscow this morning.
