930730 FT 30 JUL 93 / International Company News: Strong growth for US insurers AFTER-TAX income of US insurance groups AIG and Transamerica for the second quarter exceeded analysts' expectations as both reported earnings growth in most areas of their businesses. AIG's net income for the period rose to Dollars 481.6m, or Dollars 2.27 a share, up from Dollars 419.6, or Dollars 1.98, a year ago. Revenues climbed by nearly 11 per cent, to Dollars 5bn. Pre-tax profits in the general insurance business rose by 14 per cent to Dollars 332.4m, before capital gains, while pre-tax earnings in life insurance climbed 17 per cent to Dollars 180m. Transamerica's second-quarter net income was up at Dollars 123.9m, or Dollars 1.49 a share, from Dollars 97.5m before losses from discontinued operations, or Dollars 1.17 a share, the year before on revenue up from Dollars 1.2m to Dollars 1.3bn. Most of the earnings growth was generated in the group's life assurance side, where net income jumped from Dollars 47.4m to Dollars 75.7m. Growth in assets, cost control and higher spreads accounted for profits growth of more than 10 per cent in life assurance, structured settlements, living benefits, group pensions and reinsurance, Transamerica said. Net income for the first half was Dollars 215.7m, or Dollars 2.57 a share, compared with Dollars 168.6m, or Dollars 2.05, before losses from discontinued operations in the first six months of 1992. Transamerica's shares ended the day at Dollars 56 1/8 , up Dollars 1 1/2 , while AIG's shares rose Dollars 3 1/4 to Dollars 137 7/8.
