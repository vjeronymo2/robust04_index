July 26, 1989, Wednesday, Southland Edition

NEWSWIRE: REDUS LEAVES HOSPITAL AFTER BEING HIT BY PITCH

First baseman Gary Redus of the Pittsburgh Pirates was treated at a Pittsburgh hospital Tuesday morning after having been struck in the face by a fastball thrown by Dodger reliever Tim Crews.

Redus was struck slightly below the left eye by a pitch clocked at 90 m.p.h. during the seventh inning of the Pirates' 7-4 victory Monday night.

Although Redus apparently did not suffer any broken bones and was released after treatment, his status was uncertain as the Pirates began a seven-game trip in New York.
