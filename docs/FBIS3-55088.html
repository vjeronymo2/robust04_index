Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report from roundup of RIA-NOVOSTI and INTERFAX reports] [Text] The first plant in Azerbaijan for the production of gold ingots with a fineness of 999.9 will be constructed in Baku in cooperation with the Moscow "Geotep" institute. The Muscovites have developed the technological part of the project for the future enterprise and handed over the blueprints to representatives of the client -- the "Azerkyzyl" ("Azerzoloto") state company. This was announced by "Azerkyzyl" Vice President Valis Ismaylov. "Geotep," he noted, has assumed the purchase of specialized equipment and its delivery to the construction site. Predicted gold reserves in Azerbaijan are assessed at 1,500 tonnes. At the same time other precious metals, silver in particular, will be mined with it. The planned capacity of the first gold extracting plant is five tonnes of ingots a year. It will be possible to reach this level three to four years after the launch of the enterprise.
