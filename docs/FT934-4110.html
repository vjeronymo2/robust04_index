931206 FT 06 DEC 93 / GCHQ staff plan overtime protest About 50 staff at GCHQ, the government intelligence centre, are protesting against new overtime rosters, Richard Donkin writes. The unco-ordinated action is low key because of the ban on union membership and industrial action at the Cheltenham eavesdropping centre. The disaffected staff are more than half the personnel who have had new overtime rosters imposed on them. Some have made approaches to the GCHQ unions' group set up by those sacked when they refused to give up their union membership after the government introduced the ban in 1984. GCHQ said: 'We have heard that some individuals have banded together to see if there are ways to register dissatisfaction.'
