Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres and PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat last night signed an agreement in Cairo concluding the arrangements at the border crossings and the security arrangements in the Gaza Strip and Jericho. The size of the Jericho area will be decided upon in a meeting between Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin and PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat. Israel and the Palestinians also agreed on several security issues, on the powers of the Palestinian authority under autonomy, and on a number of other disputed issues. The agreement and the accompanying maps were signed by Foreign Ministry Director General Uri Savir and PLO representative Abu-al-'Ala' [Ahmad Quray']. The foreign minister and the PLO chairman added their signatures. Our correspondent in Cairo Moti Amir reports that the signed agreement will be valid for one year after which a committee established by both parties will be able to reexamine it. As for the Gaza Strip, the parties last night agreed that it would have three settlement areas which would also contain the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] bases responsible for the Jewish settlers' security. Major General 'Uzi Dayan clarified that Israel would retain exclusive responsibility for the east-west roads which the settlers would use to drive from the Gaza Strip into Israel. The areas on the side of the road would be under Israeli responsibility in coordination with the Palestinians. Israeli security forces would have the right to act against any elements who either seek to harm or actually attack Israelis moving on the agreed upon roads. At a certain stage, the Palestinians will assume responsibility for the pursuit of the perpetrators. Israel will retain full control of the border area in the Gaza Strip, as well as full responsibility for the security arrangements at the Rafah border crossing. On the Allenby Bridge, Israel will have the inviolable right to check any person crossing the bridge from Jordan and will be authorized to prevent the entry of any undesirable individual. Those barred entry will not be permitted to appeal the decision. An Israeli security guard will be stationed on the bridge. There will be two inspection rooms at the border terminal, one room for Israel and the other for the Palestinians. The agreement also stipulates arrangements for the Jericho area but the size of this area will be decided upon during the meeting between Yitzhaq Rabin and Yasir 'Arafat. Egyptian President Husni Mubarak welcomed the agreement. He telephoned Prime Minister Rabin and invited him to visit Egypt. Mr. Rabin thanked the Egyptians for their contribution to the peace process and said he would gladly visit Egypt after the implementation of the agreement with the Palestinians had begun. Our political correspondent Shlomo Raz reported that the prime minister approved all the details of the agreement. The chief of staff, the deputy chief of staff, the head of the Intelligence Branch, and the director of the Shin Bet arrived at the prime minister's bureau, examined every detail of the agreement, and conveyed their approval to Cairo. Our political correspondent reports that the military, civilian, and economic talks will resume next week in Taba and Paris. The two parties will also discuss the issue of the Gaza Strip roads in detail at the Taba talks. Our army affairs correspondent Karmela Menashe reports that the IDF will be capable of withdrawing from the Gaza Strip and the Jericho area within one month if it is required to do so. Army sources nevertheless asserted that an additional 300 million shekels, on top of the already authorized sum of 250 million shekels, would be required to finance the redeployment.
