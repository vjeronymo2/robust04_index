BFN [Text] Budapest (DDP/ADN)--Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel met his Hungarian counterpart Geza Jeszenszky in Budapest today. The main focus of the meeting was Hungary's integration into the European Union (EU), the Western European Union (WEU), and NATO. Kinkel will address a joint forum this evening of the Friedrich Naumann Foundation and the opposition Federation of Young Democrats (Fidesz) and the Alliance of Free Democrats (SZDSZ) on the subject of "The Visegrad Countries and the EU". [Budapest Kossuth Radio Network in Hungarian at 1700 GMT on 15 April reports that Foreign Minister Jeszenszky spoke to journalists after his discussions with Kinkel and denied that Hungary and Ukraine support the Greek-Romanian-Bulgarian proposal for lifting the embargo against rump Yugoslavia.]
