Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jan 28, BNS -- The visits to Brussels and the Netherlands were "very resultful," Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas said on his return to Vilnius Friday [28 January]. The president told reporters at the airport that "we were received well and had interesting discussions at the NATO headquarters." Brazauskas confirmed reports that NATO experts are scheduled to come to Vilnius next week, to help work out the program of Lithuania's participation in the Partnership for Peace plan. Lithuania signed up for NATO's partnership plan Thursday. Commenting on his visit to the Netherlands, the president said it was "very necessary" because "very little is known about Lithuania" in that country. Brazauskas said the Dutch leadership expressed readiness to develop cooperation with Lithuania when they found out about the present state of Lithuania's relations with Denmark, Sweden and Finland.
