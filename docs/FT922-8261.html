920514 FT 14 MAY 92 / Parliament and Politics: MPs rebuffed over Maxwell MPs campaigning on behalf of the Maxwell pensioners yesterday criticised the rejection by a junior minister of their suggestion that the government should seek ways of keeping pension funds open until investigations into the state of the Maxwell funds were complete. Mr Richard Page, Tory MP for Hertfordshire South-West, and Mr Frank Field, Labour chairman of the social security committee in the last parliament, are to meet Mr Peter Lilley, social security secretary, on Tuesday to press the case for a 'lifebelt' for the pension funds. The talks follow a cross-party meeting on Tuesday, attended by more than 60 MPs, which called on the government for an immediate statement of its position, and asked the government to consider how the funds might be kept open as 'a humanitarian as well as a practical gesture'. But Miss Ann Widdecombe, a junior social security minister, said it would be premature for the government to commit itself to any form of action, and questioned the usefulness of any move to 'drip-feed' the funds. Mr Page expressed regret that she had taken this line, especially before the MPs' meeting with Mr Lilley. 'I don't think it's wise for ministers to close off avenues of discussion before they have heard all the arguments,' he said. The MPs believe that keeping the pension funds alive is critically important in increasing the prospects that the assets can be tracked down and retrieved by the trustees. The prime minister has told pensioners of Maxwell-owned companies that compensation for stolen funds could be paid if there had been 'maladministration, either by the Department of Trade and Industry or Imro'. In a letter to a member of the AGB pension fund, Sir Robert McCrindle, replying for Mr Major, said that if a complaint was made to the parliamentary ombudsman and an investigation proved maladministration had occurred, 'we may be in a similar situtation to that which prevailed in the case of the Barlow Clowes investors.'
