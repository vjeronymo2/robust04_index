BFN [Excerpt] Paris, May 3 (AFP) -- The French National Assembly on Tuesday [3 May] began a debate on a controversial bill laying down fines of up to 20,000 francs ($3,448) for people using English words, when a French equivalent exists. The bill, ridiculed abroad and contested by sections of the ruling right-wing coalition, was nevertheless expected to be voted in its essentials in second reading by the lower house of parliament. The upper house, the Senate, has already voted the bill with communist and right-wing support while socialist senators abstained, calling it "repressive" and "antiyouth." [passage omitted]
