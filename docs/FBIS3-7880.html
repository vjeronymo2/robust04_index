Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava Feb 24 (CTK) -- Yevgeniy Primakov, chief of the Russian Intelligence Service, today ended his visit to Slovakia and left for Vienna. In Bratislava, he held talks with the Slovak Information Service and met with President Michal Kovac, Parliament Deputy Chairman Augustin Marian Huska and Deputy Premier Jozef Prokes. According to official sources, cooperation between the two countries' information services will concentrate on fighting international crime, terrorism, trade in drugs and weapons as well as on exchange of information on sites of war in Europe. The two services have also agreed to exchange their liaison officers in Moscow and Bratislava.
