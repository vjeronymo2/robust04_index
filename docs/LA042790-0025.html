April 27, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

ART CENSORSHIP IN CINCINNATI

In response to your editorial "Censorship in Cincinnati" (April 10), please spare me your superior attitude. Since when is pedophilia or sadomasochism something to be celebrated? Or tolerated? There seems to be a general campaign by civil libertarians to silence those who oppose this point of view by referring to them as unsophisticated yahoos.

It is an effort to desensitize people which I pray to God fails. I am not sure how this type of display can be prevented without endangering the very real cultural benefits of the National Endowment for the Arts. We might start by reminding those in charge that "what we tolerate, we learn to accept, and eventually embrace." It is a thought I can hardly bear.

MRS. HAZEL R. ELLIOTT

Long Beach
