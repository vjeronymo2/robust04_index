BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 4 KYODO -- The Tokyo Stock Exchange said Monday [4 April] it will impose a fine of 21 million yen on Kankaku Securities Co. for its involvement in dubious stock trading deals in violation of securities rules. The Japan Securities Dealers Association also announced a decision to order the second-tier brokerage house to pay a fine of 36 million yen for violating securities rules. It is the third time for the exchange and the association to punish Kankaku Securities for improper dealings. In the latest case, Kankaku Securities was fined for its involvement in a questionable practice known as "tobashi" between 1990 and 1991 and pledging to compensate clients for investment losses between 1985 and 1991. The brokerage is also accused of improperly compensating clients over investment losses. "Tobashi" is a term for transactions involving the temporary transfer of stock that has lost value from one client to another in an attempt to cover up the first client's losses. In the latest case, the Finance Ministry has already ordered Kankaku Securities to suspend operations in its corporate division for 10 days.
