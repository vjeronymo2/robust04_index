Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev -- A UNIAN correspondent has learned from informal sources on 1 March that President of Ukraine Leonid Kravchuk received a letter from U.S. President Bill Clinton recommending that Minister of Economy Roman Shpek be appointed Ukraine's new prime minister. Observers from government circles connect the American President's inclination toward Roman Shpek with the success of the Ukrainian Government delegation's negotiations in Washington at the end of January 1994. UNIAN was unable to obtain official confirmation of this report by the press service of the president and the Cabinet of Ministers.
