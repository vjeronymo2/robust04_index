Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Text] According to Hungarian Prime Minister Peter Boross, a democratic state that rejects both totalitarianism and excessive liberalism has to be established in Hungary. In his closing address to the National Convention of the Hungarian Democratic Forum [MDF] Boross said that legislation should continue to be given a dominant role in the future. However, the executive power should also be given adequate scope. According to Boross, there is a need for a foreign policy which keeps in view the integration into the West as well as the life of Hungarians beyond the borders. The prime minister also spoke about the responsibility of the media, saying that the country needed objective, nonparty political, but pronation [nemzeti] public service media.
