January 14, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: A BACKWARD PLACE BY RUTH PRAWER JHABVALA (FIRESIDE: $7.95)

In this delightful comedy of manners, Ruth Jhabvala uses the story of three English expatriates in Delhi to examine the changing role of women in a society divided between liberal, imported British values and traditional, paternalistic Indian mores.

Clarissa's patronizing efforts to "go native" in grubby peasant costumes embarrass her friends; brittle, blond Etta places so much emphasis on her European sophistication that she becomes a self-caricature. Inspired by the love of her impractical Indian husband, sensible, level-headed Judy learns to reconcile seemingly inimical traditions into an agreeable way of life. Rising above her compatriots, she demonstrates the superiority of spiritual resources to mere creature comforts.
