930706 FT 06 JUL 93 / Patten makes switch in technology education GOVERNMENT funding will be made available to schools which specialise in technology and can find sponsors from local businesses, Mr John Patten, the education secretary, announced yesterday. The move marks a shift from City Technology Colleges, which had previously been at the centre of the government's attempts to encourage technology teaching. CTCs were intended to be newly built schools, funded by the private sector. Employers' organisations, including the Confederation of British Industry, objected that the government was attempting to raise funds from business which could have come from the public sector. Only 14 CTCs have been built, with another opening in September. The new plan provides more government funding and is cheaper because it utilises existing schools. Mr Patten proposes that schools with local businesses represented on their governing bodies should be backed with additional support from the government, enabling the best of them to become technology colleges. He said: 'Technology Colleges will be maintained schools specialising in teaching technology, mathematics and science while still delivering the full breadth of the national curriculum. They will adopt the best practices being developed by the City Technology Colleges.' The move received a frosty reception from teachers. Mr Peter Smith, general secretary of the Association of Teachers and Lecturers, said: 'Eventually, even this government will realise that what we needed all along was better technology facilities in all schools, not just their favourites.'
