Language: Russian Article Type:CSO [Report "New Appointments"] By edict of the president of the Russian Federation, the following people are appointed: KENYAYKIN, Valeriy Fedorovich, Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador of the Russian Federation to the Republic of Italy and the Republic of San Marino, a combined position. V. F. Kenyaykin, born 1946, a Russian. In 1970 graduated from the Institute of Oriental Languages at MGU [Moscow State University] imeni M. V. Lomonosov and in 1984, from the Diplomatic Academy. Knows the Italian, French, English, and Bambara languages. In the MID [Ministry of Foreign Affairs] system since 1970. Worked in diplomatic posts in the central apparat and in USSR embassies in Mali, Belgium, and Italy. In 1989-1990 was counselor-envoy of the USSR Embassy in Italy and at the same time, general consul to the Republic of San Marino; in 1990-1991 was the deputy chief of the Ministry's First European Administration; and in 1991-1993 was the acting chief and then chief of the Cadre Service and member of the Collegium of the Russian Federation MID. Has the diplomatic rank of Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador. KUNADZE, Georgiy Fridrikhovich, Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador of the Russian Federation to the Republic of Korea. G. F. Kunadze, born 1948, a Russian. In 1971 graduated from the Institute of Oriental Languages at Moscow State University imeni M. V. Lomonosov. Candidate of historical sciences. Knows the English and Japanese languages. In 1971-1983 was in scientific work at the USSR Academy of Sciences Institute of Oriental Studies, and in 1987-1991 was a sector chief and department chief of the USSR Academy of Sciences Institute of World Economics and International Relations. In 1983-1987 was science attache at the USSR Embassy in Japan. In 1991-1993 was the Russian Federation deputy minister of foreign affairs. Has the diplomatic rank of Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador. PANOV, Aleksandr Nikolayevich, was released from the duties of Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador of the Russian Federation to the Republic of Korea in connection with his transfer to other work. VOROBYEV, Vitaliy Yakovlevich, Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador of the Russian Federation to the State of Brunei-Darussalam, holding two jobs at the same time. V. Ya. Vorobyev, born in 1944, a Russian. In 1967 graduated from the USSR MID Moscow State Institute of International Relations. Has been in the MID system since 1967. Worked in various diplomatic posts in the USSR Embassy in the PRC [People's Republic of China] (1968-1971 and 1975-1980) as well as in the Ministry's central apparat. In 1980-1986 was counselor and sector chief of the USSR MID First Far Eastern Department; and in 1986-1990 was sector deputy chief and sector chief of the Socialist Countries of Asia Administration. From 1990 through 1993 was counselor-envoy at the Russian Embassy in the PRC. Since March 1993 has been Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador of the Russian Federation to Malaysia. Has the diplomatic rank of Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador.
