Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From MAINICHI SHIMBUN in Japanese 17 January Morning Edition] [Text] Shinseito [Japan Renewal Party] held a ceremony to mark the formation of its Nagaoka Chapter in Nagaoka City, Niigate Prefecture, on 16 January. Speaking at the ceremony, Hiroshi Kumagai, Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI] minister, touched on the "Basic Plan for Public Investment" that has been taken up for discussion at the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks and said: "Japan has promised the world that it will spend 430 trillion yen for public works projects by the year 2000, and the Japanese Government has moved up their implementation. The government is now studying the possibility of spending an additional 100 trillion yen for the public works projects." In this way, Mr. Kumagai made it clear that the government plans to drastically increase the budget for public works projects. Referring to the Japan's economic growth rate, Mr. Kumagai said: "About 3 percent is compatible with the potentiality of the Japanese economy. The government is studying the possibility of adopting a policy to attain a 3-percent growth rate next year."
