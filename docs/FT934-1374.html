931220 FT 20 DEC 93 / World News in Brief: Commuters face disruption after alert shuts stations British Rail was last night working to restore local services into London after six of the capital's stations were closed by a bomb alert, but warned of possible disruption this morning and told commuters to check before attempting to travel. A spokesman for Network SouthEast said the signal box at London Bridge, which controls south east London suburban routes, was evacuated after a coded warning was received. Victoria, Charing Cross, London Bridge, Cannon Street, Blackfriars and Waterloo stations were closed.
