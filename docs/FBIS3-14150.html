Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] President Hafiz al-Asad received Dennis Ross, U.S. Middle East peace process coordinator, and Martin Indyk, U.S. presidential assistant for Middle East affairs, at 0830 today. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' attended the meeting. Mr. Ross conveyed to the president a message from U.S. President Bill Clinton dealing with the peace process and the meetings and contacts he recently held to reinvigorate the process. Mr. Ross briefed the president on the results of his current tour of the region and the subsequent steps for the next stage. He noted the importance of the talks the president held with President Clinton in Geneva and emphasized the United States' commitment to the peace process, its appreciation of the importance of Syria's vital and important role, and its determination to play a more active role in order to push the peace process forward. It was agreed to continue contacts and meetings within the framework of cooperation for the sake of pushing the peace process forward toward its target.
