940606 FT 06 JUN 94 / Call for tax curb on forex Foreign exchange speculators should be taxed to curb their influence on international markets, says Professor James Tobin, a Nobel prize-winning economist at Yale University. Writing in New Economy, the journal of the centre-left Institute for Public Policy Research, he describes speculation on currencies as a 'threat to rational policies' and proposes putting 'sand in the wheels' of currency markets. Prof Tobin says that the tax would 'direct traders' attention to long-run fundamentals and away from contagious but transient market sentiment'. His views were in part contradicted in speech by Mr Eddie George, governor of the Bank of England, at the weekend. He told the Forex 94 conference in London that 'there is a popular misperception of the foreign exchange markets as a huge, single-minded pack of wolves, acting in unison to hunt down and destroy one largely defenceless currency after another in an insatiable lust for short-term profit'. Conceding that free capital movements, and short-term speculators, could complicate the life of central bankers, Mr George went on: 'But there are legions of others, who look . . . like speculators, often managing other people's funds, who are seeking to protect the value of the assets they control against losses, by diversifying risks or covering their currency exposures.'
