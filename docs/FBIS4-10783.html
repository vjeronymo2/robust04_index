BFN [Text] Colombia and Cuba confirmed their bilateral relations in the sports field with the signing of a two-year sports agreement which includes the exchange of coaches, and information on sciences applied to sports. Luis Alfonso Munoz, general director of the Colombian Institute for Youth and Sports; and Conrado Martinez Corona, president of the National Institute for Sports, Physical Education, and Recreation, signed the agreement in the presence of Jose Ramon Fernandez, vice president of the Council of Ministers. In a news conference with the accredited press, Munoz stated: It is the most important agreement that Colombian sports has ever made with a country with as much tradition and experience as Cuba. Munoz went on to say: We are talking about the first country of America in sports and the fifth in the Olympic Games of Barcelona. I believe the sports movement of my country can benefit from Cuba's important achievements in the field of sports.
