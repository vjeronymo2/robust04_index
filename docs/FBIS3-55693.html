Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jan 10, BNS -- During its spring session, the Lithuanian parliament is scheduled to discuss laws dealing with reforms in local administration and the country's administrative-territorial division. The whole package has been tagged "the little Constitution" of Lithuania. The Lithuanian president, lawmakers government members, municipal officials and regional government commissioners met in the parliament Monday [10 January] to discuss the principles of local administration and territorial division reforms. The meeting's participants discussed several issues, including a new administrative-territorial division of Lithuania, municipal property problems and a bill on municipal elections. Municipal elections in Lithuania are scheduled for next fall. The draft provides for a single-mandate system of elections, but several members of the ruling Lithuanian Democratic Labor Party faction proposed to introduce proportional representation. Opposition MP [member of parliament] Andrius Kubilius said Monday the opposition will only back a single-mandate system.
