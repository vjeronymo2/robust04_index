BFN [Excerpts] On at least five separate issues Israel and the PLO have now reached agreements at their talks in Cairo. [passage omitted] The chief Israeli negotiator, Amnon Shahaq, spent the morning in Jerusalem, completing a round of consultations before returning to Cairo. Under the agreement on water, the Gaza Strip and settlements will get their supplies from the Israeli Meqorot Company. The Palestinian autonomous authority which is to pay for the water it gets from Israel has also undertaken to maintain quality standards. Details were given by the chief Palestinian negotiator, Nabil Sha'th. Norway and the PLO are trying to persuade the World Bank to finance projects in Gaza and Jericho regardless of the progress of the peace talks. Palestinian negotiator Sa'ib 'Urayqat and Norway's UN ambassador are concentrating their efforts to raise funds in the United States for sewage, medical, and other projects for the autonomy period. Israel is insisting that industrial concerns in the autonomous areas comply with ecology standards. The request, originated by Israeli industrialists, was put to the Palestinians at the multilateral committee meetings on the environment. One of the Israeli concerns is that pollution will be caused by the unsupervised flow of sewage into a nearby aquifer or a natural underground water reservoir. We have just received a report from Cairo. It is in fact Jamil al-Tarifi, the head of the PLO's committee for civilian matters. He is speaking to Moti Amir in Cairo about agreements the two sides have reached so far: [Begin recording] [Al-Tarifi] We finished the whole negotiations at civilian committee, and we already agreed about all the papers, and we transfer the whole authorities to the Palestinian national authority. [Amir] How many issues? [Al-Tarifi] It's 38 issues, and we already finished it yesterday, at 0400 morning. [Amir] Including the water? [Al-Tarifi] Including the water, including everything. [Amir] And you are satisfied about the water? It was a long discussion yesterday. [Al-Tarifi] Yes. It's not only the water. Many issues took long discussions. The water -- we have the full authority on Gaza Strip and Jericho area, and we have the right to development our resources and to drilling our wells. In the meantime, the wells inside the settlements is under the control of Meqorot Company, but we have to know exactly how many wells they have. And, we have to know, in monthly basis, how many cubic meters they will pump from these wells. [end recording]
