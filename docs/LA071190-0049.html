July 11, 1990, Wednesday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: SANTA PAULA;

REDEVELOPMENT PROJECT GETS TENTATIVE OK

The Santa Paula City Council has given preliminary approval to its first redevelopment project -- a 1,100-acre area that virtually encompasses the southern third of the city.

City Administrator Bob Livingston said a $250-million plan to improve public walkways, flood control channels and underground utility lines could begin in three to five years, provided that there are no legal complications. It will take that long for property values to increase enough to provide the agency with the tax revenue to begin the redevelopment project.

But county planner Bruce Smith, who last week called the redevelopment plan inadequate, said Tuesday that parts of it are internally inconsistent. For instance, Smith said the Land Use Element of the plan does not include areas subject to flooding.

Smith said that the county "can litigate or we can accept a multimillion-dollar loss over the next 40 years." Another option is for the city to offer the county a pass-through agreement.

Under such a pact, which would reduce the likelihood of friction between the city and county, the county would receive an agreed-upon percentage of any revenue realized by an increase in property taxes. Under redevelopment, the city's property tax base is frozen, so that taxing agencies such as the county no longer automatically receive such revenue when property values increase.

The agency has reached accord with the schools, the library district and the water district. It is still talking with representatives of the community college district.

"We have met with county officials, but they don't seem to want to negotiate," said Councilman John Melton, adding that city officials believe the plan is complete.

Santa Paula Councilman Carl Barringer said he believed the county's actions were financially motivated. "It's a delaying tactic to stop the implementation of the Redevelopment Agency. It's a rear guard action on their part."

The next step in the adoption of the project is final approval by the City Council, which is expected at its meeting next Monday, Melton said. If all goes as planned, the adoption will be filed the next day and will go into effect 30 days thereafter. AMELIA CHAFFEE
