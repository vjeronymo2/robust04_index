Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Global TV News" newscast] [Text] Canada will resume bilateral relations with Peru, which were suspended on 5 April 1992. The announcement was made by Christine Stewart, the Canadian minister of state for Latin America and Africa. At a news conference the ranking Canadian official said that her trip to Peru has allowed her to confirm the current situation in the country. She said she is pleased with the progress the country has made in the area of economics and especially in the area of human rights. She pointed out that the Canadian Government is willing to resume bilateral relations with Peru, which were interrupted following the self-inflicted coup on 5 April 1992, and to resume providing social aid for extremely poor sectors of the population. [Begin Stewart recording] Our presence in Peru, which has always remained at the same levels in the past, focuses on programs to provide aid especially for the poor of this country. Overall human development and sustained growth are priorities for my government. [end recording] Christine Stewart said that Canadian businessmen are quite interested in investing in Peru which, evidently, would contribute to the country's development. [Begin Stewart recording] Private Canadian businessmen can also invest here for the improvement of Peru and also to maintain advantages for Canada. [end recording]
