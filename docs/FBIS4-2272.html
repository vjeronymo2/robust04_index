BFN [Text] Chongqing, April 7 (XINHUA) -- Chongqing, a major industrial city in southwest Sichuan Province, has reported rapid expansion of its telecommunications network in recent years. Officials from the telecommunications authority said the city's telephone lines had increased from 20,000 in 1988 to 200,000 by the end of last year. In 1993 alone, near 40,000 lines were installed at an investment of 510 million yuan. The figure equals the total number installed in the previous 40 years, according to the sources. With the new opening of fiber optic lines to Wuhan and Chengdu and a satellite ground station, telecommunications between the city and the outside have become much easier, the sources said. Nearly 95,000 direct call lines now connect the city with over 700 domestic cities and 185 countries across the world. Meanwhile, the city's telecommunications network has become more diversified. It now handles more than 6,000 mobile phones and some 100,000 wireless pagers. The sources said that, as the telephone network expands, applications for household telephones are piling up. The sources disclosed that the city will invest a record one billion yuan this year to add another 210,000 lines to its telephone network.
