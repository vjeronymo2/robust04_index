920403 FT 03 APR 92 / Bifu urges action over armed raids IMPROVED security and professional counselling were demanded yesterday by Bifu, the finance union, to counter a growing number of armed raids on bank and building society branches and the resulting strains on staff, David Barchard writes. Last year the number of armed robberies on such branches rose to 1,633, seven for each working day. This was twice the level of 1990. Mr Ray Shuttleworth, secretary of Bifu's council, said: 'Victims are often young female cashiers who are devastated and traumatised by these events.'
