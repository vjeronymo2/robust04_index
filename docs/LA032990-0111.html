March 29, 1990, Thursday, Valley Edition

MCGUIRE'S GEM SEEMS TO HAVE MADE A MARK

El Camino Real High left-hander Ryan McGuire's 20-strikeout performance in a 7-2 defeat of Cleveland on Tuesday appears to be a seven-inning City Section record.

According to Cal-Hi Sports magazine, which tracks state records in a variety of high school sports, McGuire is the first City player to have achieved the feat.

Or so it would seem.

Most of Cal-Hi's records are based on marks reported to the magazine.

"It's a safe assumption that he set the record," Cal-Hi's Mark Tennis said. "But the key word is 'reported.' "

Tennis said that Cal-Hi has a list of several players who have recorded 20 or more strikeouts in a seven-inning game and that no City pitcher is on it.

The City Section keeps no statistical baseball records, Commissioner Hal Harkness said. STEVE ELLING
