920914 FT 14 SEP 92 / Status of independent schools 'must be scrutinised' AN END TO the charitable status of independent schools that do not fulfil a genuinely charitable function was approved by the Liberal Democrats yesterday, against the proposal in a party discussion document that it should be maintained for all of them, Alison Smith writes. The policy paper, Excellence For All, said that independent schools should still be able to benefit from charitable status, but that they should meet a minimum curriculum entitlement and should play a more active part in the local community. After the debate, Mr Don Foster, Liberal Democrat education spokesman, brushed aside the adoption of the amendment from the Young Liberal Democrats as a minor issue: he did not vote on the issue. Instead, his speech underlined the party's opposition to schools' opting out of local education authority control, with a call for such moves to be vigorously resisted throughout the community, in governing bodies and on local councils as well as in parliament. The party's policy is to replace LEAs with democratically accountable education departments to take strategic decisions, while giving schools themselves greater autonomy. The pledge to raise income tax to spend more on education was a distinctive feature of the Liberal Democrats' general election campaign. Mr Foster accused the government of being 'out of control' in its attitude towards education. 'They are behaving like a demented Basil Fawlty, furiously beating with a large branch a car that doesn't work - in a manic attempt to get it going.'
