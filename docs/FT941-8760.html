940218 FT 18 FEB 94 / World Trade News: Hosokawa orders rapid measures Japan's prime minister, Mr Morihiro Hosokawa, told ministries yesterday to draft emergency market-opening measures, possibly before the end of next week, to contain the growing trade friction with the US, Reuter reports from Tokyo. A special cabinet meeting was also expected today to speed up drafting of the 'voluntary' measures he promised after last week's US-Japan summit broke down over Washington's demands for numerical market access targets. Mr Masayoshi Takemura, chief cabinet secretary, said: 'We must draw up more concrete and visible measures as soon as possible. . . Hosokawa and the top (trade) negotiators agreed that the ball is in Japan's court.' As for specific timing for the new measures, he said: 'As soon as possible, maybe before the next G7 gathering.' Finance ministers and central bank governors from the Group of Seven top industrialised nations meet in Frankfurt, Germany on February 26. Mr Takemura said the two nations should calm down and halt the trans-Pacific war of words. 'In the last stage of the Japan-US framework talks, the two sides agreed to a cooling-off period.'
