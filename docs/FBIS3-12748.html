Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] In the presence of His Highness Crown Prince Hasan, His Majesty King Husayn received Karolos Papoulias, Greek foreign minister and the current chairman of the EC Council of Ministers at the Royal Hashemite Court today. Papoulias conveyed to his majesty an oral message from Greek President Konstandinos Karamanlis, a letter from the EC, and a letter from Greek Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou. The guest minister also conveyed to His Majesty King Husayn the Greek president's greetings and appreciation for his majesty's great role in maintaining security and stability in this region that has suffered from tension and conflict for decades. The EC's letter contained the EC's great appreciation for the continued sincere efforts that his majesty made to achieve Yemeni reconciliation and support the unanimous signing of the document of accord by key Yemeni figures, bodies, and parties. The letter emphasized that the EC looks forward to continued efforts by his majesty to implement and turn into tangible reality the articles of the Yemeni accord document in order to safeguard Yemen's unity and consolidate its democratic march in an atmosphere of security and stability. The Greek foreign minister also handed his majesty a letter from the Greek prime minister. The letter contained Papandreou's admiration of the distinguished and responsible role that His Majesty King Husayn has played for many years in terms of maintaining security and stability in the Middle East. The letter also contained the appreciation by Papandreou and his government for his majesty's continued efforts to achieve a comprehensive, just, and lasting peace in the region based on international legitimacy and the Security Council resolutions. In his letter to King Husayn, Papandreou stressed his appreciation and admiration for the great success that his majesty achieved by reconciling the disputing Yemeni parties, holding the signing the document of accord, and beginning the implementation of its articles out of his care for Yemen's unity and achievements and in order to protect its security and stability. The letter also stressed the deep Jordanian-Greek relations and the need to consolidate and promote them to serve the interests of both friendly peoples. Papandreou's letter also pointed to the distinguished efforts his majesty is making to achieve a comprehensive and just peace in the region. The letter emphasized that these efforts are appreciated by the Greek Government. The meeting was attended by His Excellency al-Sharif Zayd Bin-Shakir, chief of the Royal Hashemite Court; Marwan al-Qasim, his majesty the king's political adviser; Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs; and Amjad al-Majali, the Jordanian ambassador to Greece. It was also attended by the Greek Foreign Ministry secretary general, the Greek ambassador in Amman, and the EC ambassador in Amman.
