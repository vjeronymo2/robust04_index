BFN [Text] Palestine Ambassador to Jordan al-Tayyib 'Abd-al-Rahim, who is minister of presidential affairs in the Palestinian National Authority, has welcomed the progress achieved so far on the Jordanian track in the peace process. He said what was achieved represents a Jordanian accomplishment and commitment to what had been agreed upon between Arab parties involved in the peace process. Mr. 'Abd-al-Rahim denied that negotiation on each track would lead to isolating one Arab party and leave Israel capable of imposing its conditions on it. He said such reasoning is not correct because there are constants and each Arab side seeks just and comprehensive peace. He added: No final peace treaties before each track solves issues related to it. He said what has been achieved on the Jordanian track supports the Palestinian track, stressing accumulative achievements on each track constitute support for the other tracks. Mr. 'Abd-al-Rahim explained that achieving progress on the Jordanian track would generate further breakthroughs on the Palestinian track and would push the Syrian and Lebanese track forward. The Palestinian official said the progress on the Jordanian track spurred underway international efforts for scoring progress on the Syrian and Lebanese tracks. He paid tribute to Jordan's support of the Palestinian side in the field of deepening the Palestinian track and crystallizing the Palestinian identity.
