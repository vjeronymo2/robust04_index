930814 FT 14 AUG 93 / Property industry's debt falls by 10.8% THE AMOUNT of bank debt in the property industry has fallen by 10.8 per cent to Pounds 36.2bn from its peak of Pounds 40.6bn in May 1991, according to the Bank of England, Vanessa Houlder writes. Bank loans to the industry fell by Pounds 550m to Pounds 36.2bn in the quarter to June 1993, the eighth consecutive quarter in which debt has fallen. The sharpest decline was registered by the US banks, which have cut their exposure to UK property by 24 per cent over the past year to Pounds 1.26bn. The UK banks, which have a 58 per cent share of the market, have reduced their outstanding loans by 5 per cent over the past year. Japanese banks have cut their exposure by 10 per cent over the year to Pounds 4.09bn.
