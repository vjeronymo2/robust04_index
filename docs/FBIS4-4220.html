BFN [Text] Brasilia, April 26 (XINHUA) -- Brazilian President Itamar Franco today met with visiting senior Chinese Communist Party official Hu Jintao. Both sides expressed the desire to further expand friendship and cooperation between the two countries. Recalling Chinese President Jiang Zemin's visit to Brazil last year, Franco said that the visit set a framework for pushing their bilateral ties to a new stage. The president said that he will soon visit China to discuss with the Chinese leaders the concrete steps for furthering their cooperation. He expressed the hope that Brazil and China, the two large countries in Latin America and Asia, will enhance their cooperation in the international affairs and strive for the establishment of a new international order. Hu Jintao, member of the Standing Committee of the Chinese Communist Party Politburo, told the president that China is looking forward to his visit, which he said will be a great success. Hu stressed that economy has played an increasing role in the international relations and there are great potentials for the two countries to expand economic cooperation and trade. Earlier today, Hu and his entourage met with President of the Senate Humberto Lucena and President of the Chamber of Deputies Inocencio de Oliveira respectively.
