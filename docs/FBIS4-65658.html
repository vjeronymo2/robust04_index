BFN [Unattributed article from Brussels EUROPE: "Results of Ecofin Council Session"] [Text] Meeting on Monday [16 May] at a session chaired by Giorgos Romeos, Greek Deputy Minister for the National Economy, the Ecofin [economic and financial issues] Council concluded its work shortly after 1800. Its results were summarized as follows: - Economic situation. The Council took note of the Commission's new projections, which were nevertheless sharply criticized by Chancellor of the Exchequer Kenneth Clarke as regards the United Kingdom. Belgian Minister Philippe Maystadt said the Commission had made low-range projections for certain Member States. - Progress of work on the white paper. Based on a Commission note, the Twelve continued preparation of the Corfu Summit, examining in particular a German memorandum which was warmly endorsed by the British delegation. - Financial assistance to Central and East European countries. The expected decisions were taken in part. - Combatting fraud. The ministers held the expected exchange of views. - Banks. The Council adopted the directive on the guarantee system. - Securities exchanges. The Council completed and simplified the directive on prospectuses for admission of shares to the exchange.
