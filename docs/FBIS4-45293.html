Traffickers PY2705221994 Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 15 May 94 p A-2 PY2705221994 Lima EL PERUANO Spanish BFN [Text] The Shining Path (SL) terrorist group--once described as the most lethal and bloody terrorist group in the world--is in the final throes of its alliance with drug traffickers. The harsh and devastating blows it has received at the hands of the forces of order over the last couple of months have considerably undermined its criminal activities in the Peruvian jungle. In the Huallaga Front, where most clashes are taking place, the Armed Forces and the Peruvian National Police [PNP] have separated terrorism from narcotrafficking by attacking it to such an extent that any possibility of a future alliance is almost nonexistent. The Aries offensive is no coincidence. The military command has information on subversive movements in the Cuchara region. This military offensive compelled the SL people's guerrilla army--the MRTA [Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Army] is almost totally annihilated--to desperately take refuge south of the Upper Huallaga region while drug traffickers headed north of Ayacucho and more recently to Madre de Dios where the Army and the PNP are dealing with them harshly. In the Cuchara and Primavera regions--where the Aries operation is being conducted--SL commanders are being severely questioned by their subordinates who, in view of the intensification of military actions, are taking advantage of the Repentance Law. These desertions are prompting SL commanders to indiscriminately kill any SL member who disobeys orders. Carlos, a 13-year-old boy who escaped with his father from the theater of opeations, said: "SL members are killing each other because they do not know what to do in the jungle any more." This can only be interpreted as a desperate move by the SL commanders, who interpret these "rebellions" as the end of their Maoist ideology. The Ashasninkas indigenous group are taking advantage of this situation and escaping the long and ruthless captivity they have endured at the hands of SL. Eight SL terrorists, among them little Carlos, who endured the most deplorable living conditions imposed by the SL commanders, surrendered five weeks ago at the Quiteni antisubversive base in Satipo, in the Frente Mantaro jurisdiction. The living conditions in the Army bases in the jungle are very different. The Army has installed 15 countersubversive bases with military personnel to welcome repentant SL members and to offer them the best conditions and treatment. A Tingo Maria military officer says: "We are not saying this. This is being said by the SL members who surrendered at our bases. They do not want to leave. In our installations they find food and work and they receive the best treatment."
