January 10, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

NURSING CRISIS

In response to the editorial "Curing the Nurse Shortage" (Dec. 29), I disagree with the "experts who prepared the constructive report for the U.S. Department of Health Services."

The commission's finding that the "money matters most" is wrong. The fact is that money cannot replace the decrease in a caring attitude toward patients. However, in addition to the change in attitude, it is important that nurses have "professional recognition, representation on policy-making, regulatory and accreditation boards, and the use of a more collaborative approach between nurses and other health professionals and the management."

The hot-line effort to inform men and women about nursing career opportunities should include one very important question: Does the person, who is inquiring, really understand the meaning of the four-letter word care?

ESTHER KAHAN

Rolling Hills Estates
