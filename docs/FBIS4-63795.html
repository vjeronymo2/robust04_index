BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan, 24 May -- On the whole, Armenia's attitude toward the Partnership for Peace document, which has been put forward by NATO is a positive one, an official spokesman of Armenia's Foreign Ministry said today. It is recognized in Yerevan that this document is aimed at developing cooperation and creating conditions for strengthening confidence measures on the continent of Europe, Aram Safaryan, press secretary of Armenia's Foreign Ministry said at a briefing here. At the same time he stressed that the NATO document needed further consultations within the framework of the North Atlantic Cooperation Council. These were made necessary by a number of circumstances, including the situation in the Transcaucasus. The spokesman of Armenia's Foreign Ministry stressed the Republic's constructive and many-sided involvement in the activity of the North Atlantic Cooperation Council. He recalled that Armenia's Foreign Minister Vagan Papazyan is going to take part in the meeting of heads of the foreign policy departments of the member countries of the North Atlantic Cooperation Council, which is due to take place in Istanbul at the beginning of June.
