BFN [By Li Jianxing (2621 1696 5281): "China's State-Owned Assets Exceed 3,000 Billion Yuan by End of 1992"] [Text] Beijing, 17 May (RENMIN RIBAO)--The gross quantity of state assets in our country has increased rapidly in recent years, and they have also yielded better economic returns. According to statistics from the State Bureau for Management of State-Owned Assets, by the end of 1992, the gross book value of state assets in our country reached 3,069.7 billion yuan (not including resource-like assets and state assets held by the Armed Forces and the armed police force), and increased they had by 14.3 percent or 385.1 billion yuan over the previous year's figure of 2,684.6 billion yuan. State assets managed by central institutions were worth 1,458.4 billion yuan and accounted for 47.5 percent of the total, with state assets managed by localities worth 1,611.3 billion yuan, accounting for 52.5 percent. In terms of use, state assets used in economic operations were worth 2,210.1 billion yuan and accounted for 72 percent, while state assets not involved in economic operations were worth 859.6 billion yuan and accounted for 28 percent. State assets involved in economic operations increased by 13.1 percent, or 256.5 billion yuan, over the previous year. Regarding the distribution of state assets in various trades, at the end of 1992, state assets in state-owned industrial enterprises throughout the country were worth 1,649.8 billion yuan. State-owned enterprises accounted for over 90 percent of all assets in such important industries as energy, transportation, mining, high technology, banking, and foreign trade industries. The gross quantity of state assets in the manufacturing industry, the construction and engineering industry, and agriculture, forestry, and water resources industries increased at a rate 10 percentage points higher than the national average growth rate. Assets in the real estate industry and enterprises adopting the joint-stock system increased at the highest rate. At the end of 1992, state assets in the real estate industry were worth 90.5 billion yuan, an increase of 69.8 percent over the previous year, and the gross quantity of state assets in enterprises that have adopted the joint-stock system reached 28.4 billion yuan, or an increase of 175.7 percent over the previous year. The state-owned economy as a whole grew continuously and became the predominant force in the national economy [sentence as published]. Through in-depth reform and expanded opening, state-owned enterprises continuously increased their dynamism and improved their economic results. By the end of 1992, tax and profit contributions by state-owned enterprises throughout the country amounted to 329.1 billion yuan, increasing by 45.3 billion yuan or 16 percent over the previous year. They earned 117.6 billion yuan of profits, an increase of 11.3 percent or 11.9 billion yuan over the previous year. Enterprises incurring losses accounted for 24.3 percent of the total number of state-owned enterprises, down 7.3 percentage points from the previous year's level of 31.6 percent. The proportion of business losses in economic results declined to 26.7 percent from 32.1 percent in the previous year. Tax and profit contributions made by state-owned enterprises accounted for about two-thirds of the state's fiscal revenues.
