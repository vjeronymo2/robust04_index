BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow April 18 TASS -- A group of 200 Russian paratroopers will leave Ryazan on a peacemaking mission to the Balkans on Tuesday. They will replace their colleagues who will return home after the end of the term of service in the U.N. peace-keeping contingent in the former Yugoslavia. They are the first group of a contingent of 600 soldiers who will be flown to the region, Igor Kashin, spokesman for the Russian airborne troops, said on Monday. He said 400 of them will be deployed in Serb Krajina and the rest in Sarajevo. All of them are volunteers drawn on a contract basis for six months.
