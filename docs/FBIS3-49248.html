Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 6 Jan (TANJUG) -- In connection with a statement issued by the Albanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, which was broadcast by several foreign news agencies from Tirana regarding frequent incidents that have taken place along the Yugoslav-Albanian border, the information service of the General Staff of the Yugoslav Army has issued the following explanation: "Border units of the Yugoslav Army have been strictly observing the rules of border service, and all incidents in which people who were illegally crossing the border were either caught or killed have taken place on the territory of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia. "This has always been established by interstate commissions following investigations. "Members of the Yugoslav Army have been facing an increasing number of attempts by Albanian citizens to cross the border illegally. Such people are armed, and on several occasions armed incidents occurred, resulting sometimes in the wounding or death of Yugoslav border guards. "The latest incident, in which Albanian citizen Mustafa Ramadan was killed, took place on 3 January this year in the region of the border watchtower at Krstac on the territory of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia. The mentioned person lost his life after he tried to escape, despite warnings given by the border guards of the Yugoslav Army," the explanation by the information service of the General Staff of the Yugoslav Army says in conclusion.
