931022 FT 22 OCT 93 / Banks welcome a clear decision BANKS welcomed the House of Lords decision for bringing certainty to the area of what happens to people who act as surety for their partners' borrowings, Alison Smith writes. The customers' group which has taken up the cause of Mrs Bridget O'Brien, the Bank Action Group, saw the judgment as clearing the way to pursue similar cases. Both sides of the argument, however, agreed that the decision reinforced the code of practice which was adopted voluntarily by the banks in March last year. Barclays said that the bank did not expect the decision to affect 'the vast majority' of existing arrangements. 'There is a small number of cases already in dispute, and as the judgment makes clear, each will turn on its own facts,' it added. The British Bankers' Assocation similarly played down the impact of the decision. It emphasised that the judgment underlined existing practice, and said that it would not affect the readiness of banks to lend to small businesses. The association said it expected there to be only a small number of cases where not only had independent legal advice had not been suggested but also where there had been misrepresentation or undue influence and the security for the loan had had to be called upon. But Ms Pat Griffiths, the national co-ordinator of the Bank Action Group, estimated that there were thousands of cases which would be affected by the judgment. She called for an independent statutory regulator for the banks. She added: 'We challenge the government to take action now to regularise British banking practice, as the banks have proved they are incapable of self-regulation.'
