February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County P.M. Final

IN BRIEF;

SUNS FILE PROTEST OVER DOUBLE FOUL

The Phoenix Suns have filed a protest with the NBA, claiming that the referees' misinterpretation of a rule cost their club in last Saturday night's overtime loss here to Atlanta.

With Phoenix trailing 118-116, Suns guard Kevin Johnson collided with Hawks guard Doc Rivers on a drive to the basket with 10 seconds remaining in overtime.

Referee Ed Rush called a charging foul on Johnson while referee Jim Capers called a blocking foul on Rivers. A double-foul was assessed and Phoenix retained possession of the ball. Instead of calling a jump ball at center court, however, referees instructed Phoenix to inbound the ball, and Johnson's desperation jumper at the buzzer bounced off the front of the rim on the final play.

The Suns, in their protest Monday, said that the double-foul rule was misinterpreted by the referees and that there should have been a jump ball.

Phoenix officials said NBA Commissioner David Stern should make a ruling on the protest within five days of receiving the letter.
