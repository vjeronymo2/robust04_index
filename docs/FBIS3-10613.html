Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "This Morning" newscast] [Text] A group of former Government Ministry soldiers occupied the UN offices in Nicaragua for several hours yesterday afternoon and evening. Hours after the seizure of the UN offices, during which 10 officials were held captive, the former soldiers went to the Government Ministry to begin negotiating their demands with Deputy Ministers Joaquin Lovo and Frank Cesar Reyes. The former soldiers, who arrived with their faces covered with red handkerchiefs, were led by Marvin Miranda. They demanded prompt compensation for their years of service during the former Sandinist regime.
