Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Cairo, 7 Feb (MENA)--The Supreme Military Court, which is considering the case of the attempted assassination of Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi, held a session today to continue hearing the testimonies of the prosecution witnesses in the case, in which 15 people are being tried, six of them in absentia. The court saw two videotapes on which the supreme state security prosecution recorded a staged version of the incident and how it was carried out. The fifth and sixth defendants related how the crime was committed and the role of each one of them, as well as the role of the seventh defendant who participated in the crime, how they prepared the explosive charge used in the incident, and how they monitored the prime minister's movements. The court also saw a videotape about an examination of the house of the fifth defendant Salah al-Din [name indistinct] in al-Minufiyah, in which the police found electrical equipment, weapons, and explosives being prepared for use in some terrorist operations. [passage omitted] The Supreme Military Court decided to adjourn its sessions until tomorrow to hear the remaining witnesses who assisted the police or took part in capturing the defendants. The court decided to refer the fifth defendant, Salah al-Din, to a physician to determine the truth about his claim that he was tortured. The court also decided to summon the commander and some members of the prime minister's guard to hear their testimony. [passage omitted]
