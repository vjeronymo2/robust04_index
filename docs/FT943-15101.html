940712 FT 12 JUL 94 / World News in Brief: Reprisals feared after killing of loyalist by IRA Fears of a fresh upsurge of Ulster violence rose last night after the IRA shot dead Protestant militant Ray Smallwoods on the eve of the Protestant marching season. Smallwoods had served seven years for the attempted murder of Irish nationalist politician Bernadette Devlin McAliskey in 1981. Earlier, Mid-Ulster Democratic Unionist MP the Rev William McCrea and his family escaped injury when terrorists fired at their home in Co Londonderry.
