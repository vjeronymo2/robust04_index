Language: English Article Type:BFN [From "News Bulletin" No. 442] [Text] Warsaw, Feb 22, ELTA -- On Tuesday the fifth and, hopefully, the last Lithuanian-Polish negotiation round for the bilateral agreement was held at the Polish Foreign Ministry. The negotiations were headed by the Foreign Vice-Ministers Vladislavas Domarkas and Iwo Byczevski. On the eve of the negotiations Byczevski said that he hoped to reach the final solution of all disputable questions. He added that there would not be a separate declaration on the Vilnius region annexation in 1920. Byczevski also noted that Poland would point out requirements of the Polish minority in Lithuania in the agreement. Members of Lithuanian state delegation also told ELTA that there would not be a separate declaration, but historical aspects important for the both countries would be mentioned in the preamble of the agreement. On Tuesday [22 February] Polish Catholic daily THE SLOWO published an open letter from the federation of the Polish who live in the border territories in Poland (the Kreis organizations) to Polish prime minister. They required to consider the part of the agreement concerning the rights of the Polish minority in Lithuania in the Polish-Lithuanian negotiations.
