Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- After three years of rising government grants and subsidies from western to eastern Germany, 1994 will for the first time show a reduction in comparison with the previous year. In response to a query from DDP/ADN, the Finance Ministry revealed in Bonn on Thursday [13 January] that a total of 138 billion German marks [DM] was transferred to eastern Germany last year. In 1994, this sum will fall by DM5 billion to DM133 billion net (DM175 billion gross). [passage omitted] In 1991, DM107 billion net was made available for eastern Germany. In 1992, this sum rose to DM115 billion and was increased again last year by DM23 billion, according to official figures. That was the highest level of payments since German unification.
