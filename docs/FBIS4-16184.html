BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Nikolay Geronin and Aleksandr Krylovich] [Text] Moscow, 13 Apr -- "We are very grateful to Russia for its interest in the problem of the Korean Peninsula and its concern in this matter", Han Sung-chu, the foreign minister of the Korean Republic, told an ITAR-TASS correspondent at Sheremetyevo-2 airport today. Replying to a question about Seoul's attitude to Moscow's proposal to hold an international conference on questions of ensuring the security and nuclear-free status of the Korean Peninsula, he said that the Republic of Korea will continue consultations on the conference with Russia and other interested sides. Han Sung-chu arrived in Moscow today on a visit, during the course of which on Thursday [14 April] he will be holding talks with Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, and meet the leadership of the Russian Federal Assembly. At the talks with the head of Russia's foreign political department, Han Sung-chu noted, questions of the preparations for the visit of Kim Yong-sam, the president of the Republic of Korea, to Russia in June will be discussed. Various subjects, including how to solve the nuclear problem of the DPRK, will be examined. "We will also examine questions of cooperation between our two countries in the economic and diplomatic spheres," the South Korean minister said.
