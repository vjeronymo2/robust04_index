February 1, 1990, Thursday, San Diego County Edition

HIGH SCHOOL BASKETBALL ROUNDUP;

TORREY PINES DROPS RBV BACK INTO PACK

Torrey Pines held a one-point advantage going into the final quarter, then proceeded to outscore Rancho Buena Vista, 21-16, for a crucial, 64-58 Palomar League victory.

RBV (9-9, 5-4), which had been hanging surprisingly close in the standings, fell three games behind league-leading Mt. Carmel and two behind Torrey Pines (20-2, 8-1).

Torrey Pines' Kyle Armstrong led all scorers with 18. Carter Brown helped put RBV away late in the game with some crucial one-and-ones. He was five of seven from the line and finished with 13 points.

RBV's Bond Schoefel entered the game in the second quarter and scored 10 of his 16 points before halftime. Brad Grubaugh also scored 16 for the Longhorns.

Daryl Parker, who entered the game with a 23.7-point average, was held to just 12.

Mt. Carmel 59, Fallbrook 54 -- Mt. Carmel (16-3, 9-0) maintained an eight-point lead through much of the game, but things tightened up with two minutes left.

Fallbrook's Darnell Lee made a field goal, and Mt. Carmel's Shane Knight was called for a technical foul. Chris Miller made both free throws, then got the ball back on the inbounds and hit a three-pointer to cut Fallbrook's deficit to 55-54.

But Mt. Carmel's Ryan Wootton made a quick layup, Fallbrook (14-7, 5-4) missed its next shot, and the Sundevils then scored again to put it away.

Poway 98, Orange Glen 46 -- Poway (13-5, 4-5) had four in double figures: Greg Sorrell (19), Frank Garrett (15), Kirk Hipple (14) and Zeke Awbrey (13).

Avocado League

El Camino 90, Ramona 51 -- Ramona (12-5, 4-4) never recovered from a two-point first quarter. Jeff Reeves led El Camino (14-5, 7-0) with 21 points.

Oceanside 49, Escondido 30 -- Oceanside (10-10, 4-4) got off to a bad start, scoring only eight points in the first quarter, but Escondido (5-10, 3-5) had the bad finish: just 14 points in the second half.

San Pasqual 87, San Marcos 48 -- Erik Meek led San Pasqual (13-5, 6-1) with 33 points in only three quarters.
