Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Naryshkin] [Text] Zagreb, 21 Feb -- Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin, the Russian president's special representative, had discussions in Sarajevo on Sunday [20 February] with representatives of the Bosnian Serb political and military leadership on issues connected with implementing the pledge they undertook at Russia's request to withdraw heavy weapons from the city or to surrender them to UN control. Churkin stated this in a telephone interview with an ITAR-TASS correspondent. At meetings between the Russian diplomat and Yasushi Akashi, the UN Security Council's representative in the former Yugoslavia, and General Michael Rose, commander of UN forces in Bosnia-Herzegovina emphasis was put on the possibility of maintaining the calm in Sarajevo that has been achieved in recent days. In a conversation with Bosnia-Herzegovina President Alija Izetbegovic, Churkin stressed that the Russian battalion that arrived in Sarajevo on 20 February would carry out its duties honorably and objectively within the UN mandate. He urged the Bosnian leader to do everything necessary to rule out any unfriendly acts against the Russian troops. The situation in Sarajevo is calm and there is no cause for aggravating the situation, the Russian president's special representative concluded.
