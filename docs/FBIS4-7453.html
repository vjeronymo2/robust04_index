BFN [Text] Skopje, 29 Mar (TANJUG) -- Matthew Nimetz, special envoy of the U.S. President responsible for the conflict between Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Repulic of Macedonia -- FYROM] and Greece, arrived from Athens in Skopje today for talks with Macedonian President Kiro Gligorov and Minister of Foreign Affairs Stevo Crvenkovski. In a brief press statement following an hour and a half of talks, Nimetz was confident that the United States would play a useful role in the efforts to resolve the conflict between Skopje and Athens. After his visit to Macedonia, Nimetz is expected to return to Athens. Following his talks with Nimetz, Minister Crvenkovski said that the U.S. envoy was told of Macedonia's satisfaction at President Clinton's decision to become involved in the conflict by appointing an envoy. Crvenkovski said Macedonia was willing to resume negotiations to resolve the conflict within the United Nations and with Cyrus Vance as mediator. He went on to draw attention to Macedonia's view that there were no prospects for resuming the dialogue while the pressure of embargo was in place.
