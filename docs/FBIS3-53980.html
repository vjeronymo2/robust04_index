Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] At 1145 today, President Hafiz al-Asad received Lebanese President Ilyas al-Hirawi, Chamber of Deputies Speaker Nabih Birri, Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri, Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz, and Chief Lebanese Negotiator Suhayl Shammas. Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam, Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar', and Nasir Qaddur, minister of state for foreign affairs, also attended the meeting. People's Assembly Speaker 'Abd-al-Qadir Qaddurah and Prime Minister Mahmud al-Zu'bi joined the meeting at a later time. The talks dealt with the situation in the region, the peace process, the Geneva summit between Presidents al-Asad and Bill Clinton, and the prospects this summit has provided for the efforts to establish a just and comprehensive peace in the region. The president briefed the Lebanese side on his talks with President Clinton on the peace process and the need to advance it on all tracks, including the Lebanese track. Al-Hirawi, Birri, and al-Hariri expressed great appreciation for the efforts President al-Asad exerted at the Geneva summit for the sake of a just and comprehensive peace, and to explain the principled Arab position that is in harmony with the international legitimacy. They also expressed Lebanon's thanks--from its president, government, and people--to President al-Asad for stressing Lebanon's right to liberate its occupied territories, and his call for implementing UN Security Council Resolution No. 425, since it is an indispensable basis for peace on the Lebanese track. During the meeting, the conferees stressed the importance of the continuous cooperation, coordination, and consultation between the two fraternal countries during future stages of the peace process. The Syrian and Lebanese sides expressed their satisfaction with the development of bilateral relations. The meeting was interrupted by a working lunch. President al-Hirawi and the delegation accompanying him returned to Beirut today.
