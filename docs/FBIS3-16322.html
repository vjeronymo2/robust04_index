Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Gridneva] [Text] Dushanbe March 15 TASS -- A meeting of foreign ministers from Central Asia, Kazakhstan and the Russian Federation ended here today after adopting a final communique. The communique supported an appeal of Tajik leader Emomali Rakhmonov to the president of the United Nations Security Council for giving the peace-making forces stationed in Tajikistan the status of a U.N. operation. The document expressed profound alarm with the on-going tension on The Tajik-Afghan border, which destabilizes the Tajik situation and hampers settlement of the inter-Tajik conflict. The meeting supported efforts taken by the Tajik leadership to achieve a political settlement and expressed the hope the negotiations will stop armed actions on the Tajik-Afghan border. The communique praised Russian and U.N. attention given to the Tajik problem with the aim of the soonest inter-Tajik settlement through a political dialogue. It expressed the hope the parties to the conflict will find a political way to achieve national accord and civic peace and confirmed the readiness of Central Asian republics and Russia to help progress in development of inter-state relations. After a brief trip to the Tajik-Afghan border, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev had a tete-a-tete meeting with Tajik leader Emomali Rakhmonov to discuss the border situation and the need for border reinforcement, including that with personnel. The Tajik leader assured the diplomat the Tajik Government will soon send 5,000 draftees to the border in the near future. The sides also discussed the Tajik situation and the need for accelerated political dialogue with the opposition. The sides agreed that negotiations with the opposition to start in the immediate future will help a lot.
