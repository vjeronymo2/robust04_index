BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 30 (KCNA) -- The dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il sent a birthday table to a Japanese woman Choe Sun residing in the central district, Pyongyang Municipality, on her 70th birthday. Choe Sun (Japanese name: Toshiko Motohasi) was born in May 1924 in Suminami Ward, Tokyo metropolis, as the eldest daughter of a railway worker Eisuke Motohasi, her father, and Haru Motohasi, her mother. After graduating from a girls high school, she worked at a farm machinery factory before she came to the Democratic People's Republic of Korea in January 1960 with her Korean husband, Choe Sang-chol, who was chairman of the Suminami branch of the General Association of Korean Residents in Japan (Chongnyon). After finishing the course of a university, she has taught at the Pyongyang University of foreign languages, writing many books for teaching and training hundreds of foreign language experts. Comrade Kim Chong-il made her an assistant professor and gave her the noble political integrity as a member of the Workers' Party of Korea, highly appreciating her services. Receiving the birthday table imbued with loving care amid the blessings of all peoples on May 27, she said she owed it entirely to the politics of grace and the politics of love and trust in this land that she is enjoying such worth and happiness of life today, she who once lived in a capitalist society where the jungle law prevails.
