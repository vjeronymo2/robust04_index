Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 12 Jan -- This morning, President Lech Walesa left Warsaw for Prague where he is to meet the leaders of the Visegrad Group and President Bill Clinton. Asked by journalists at Warsaw military airport whether his "propaganda offensive" has changed the position of western states, the president replied: "I think that no more can be done after the NATO summit in Brussels." The president added that world public opinion "should exert greater pressure on politicians not to repeat the same mistakes." "There are opportunities which have to be taken, agreements which have to be kept," the president said, adding that he hopes that in Prague "we do more for a more secure and more human future."
