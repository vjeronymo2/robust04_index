BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 14 (KCNA) -- The Kum Song Youth Publishing House has brought out many books conducive to the education of the youth and children. Published some time ago was "The Budding Season", the 12th volume of a collection of literary works of young people containing more than 20 works among those sent in for the 13th "April 15 Mangyongdae Loyalty Prize Contest" which was held in celebration of the birthday of the great leader President Kim Il-song. Included in the collection are poems, essays, short stories and fables among them the short story "He Was an Ordinary Young Man" depicting the strong will and selfless spirit of a young demobilized soldier who works with sincerity at the construction site of the Okchon waterway for the prosperity and development of the country and the true story "Red Shoulder Ensign" which deals with a veteran of the fatherland liberation war who instills the soul of the former generation into the mind of his son of new generation who is leaving for military service, handing over to him the ensign. The newly published books include the medium-length story "Pomegranates", an interesting story about a woman village doctor who is implementing the party's line of preventive medicine, the science fiction "The Star Will Return" and Vols. 18, 19 and 20 of a collection of Korean folktales. The collection of folktales include stories about Kye Wol-hyang and other women who fought valiantly against Japanese invaders, and Yi Ok-pong, Sok Kae and other famous woman poets and musicians. There are also stories about the life of righteous-minded, upright and talented people. A collection of short stories "Victory" is based on the fatherland liberation war. Writers of the publishing house are energetically working on full- and medium-length novels of various themes. The full-length novel "The Hill of Life" by Kim Yong-hwan depicting an official who devotes his whole life silently to the development of stock breeding and the medium-length novels "A New Morning on the Planet" and "A Daughter of the Coal Field" which exhale strong odor of the motherland and the hometown will see the light before long. Kim Chin-song is writing the full-length novel "We Ask History the Question" and Chang Song-hun the full-length novel "Flowing Stars" on the theme of national reunification.
