BFN [Report by Vicente Briceno; from the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli has strongly criticized the Argentine Senate's statement on the legal proceedings of the trial of former Venezuelan President Carlos Andres Perez. Burelli believes the Argentine parliament has no right to interfere in Venezuelan affairs. Vicente Briceno reports: [Briceno] As the foreign minister left the special Council of Ministers meeting at Miraflores Palace, he said the branches of the Venezuelan Government have properly handled the case of former President Perez. Despite the Argentine Senate's statement, he said Venezuela will not break relations with the Argentine Government, because the Senate issued that statement, not the executive branch. He also said Venezuela will not send a protest note to Argentina, because the Venezuelan diplomatic delegation in that country handles these types of statements by expressing regret over them. [Begin Burelli recording] The government branches have seriously and flawlessly handled Perez's case. Congress, in its own right, and the judicial branch have intervened in the case, thus allowing the former president to enjoy the rights to which he is entitled. The process has been so impeccable that Perez's own party had expelled him as the judicial branch made a decision. The Venezuelan Senate and state would never dare give opinions on decisions that foreign governments have made. [end recording] [Briceno] Burelli again broached the controversial case of the Haitians who will reportedly be granted asylum in Venezuela, a case which has triggered opposing opinions in the executive cabinet. He has said that only those Haitians whom the government has approved will be granted asylum and that our embassy will select them. The exact number of these Haitians has yet to be determined, however.
