BFN [Letter from ROSSIYSKAYA GAZETA editor N. Polezhayeva to Moscow Prosecutor G.S. Ponomarev: "Witness' Testimony"] [Text] I, Natalya Ivanovna Polezhayeva, chief editor of ROSSIYSKAYA GAZETA, have received a summons from the Moscow prosecutor's office demanding that I appear before investigator Leontyeva for questioning as a witness. I know very well I have not witnessed any crime and so I naturally asked Mikhail Ivanovich Slinko, chief of the department for the investigation of banditry, in what connection and on what basis I was being summoned. It turned out it was in connection with the 12 April publication in ROSSIYSKAYA GAZETA of the article "Between the Criminals and the Bureaucrats.... Who Needed Otari Kvantrishvili's Death?" They wanted to find out from me the name of the author and the source of the information. I do not think that interrogation is needed for these questions. An official request was enough, which had been suggested. But M.I. Slinko, citing some article in the Criminal Code, promised to draw up a compulsory court attendance order. In order not to waste my own working time nor that of the prosecutor's office workers, I have taken the liberty of printing my testimony in the newspaper. In accordance with article 41 of the Russian Federation law "On the Mass Media," "the editorial office is obliged to keep secret its source of information and does not have the right to name a person supplying information on condition he is not named, except for a case when relevant demand has come from a court in connection with a case with which it is dealing." In this case the author made anonymity a condition for the purposes of his own safety. In this connection I cannot give the author's name or the source of the information. But I assure you it was obtained from officials of the Ministry of Internal Affairs and the editorial office has no doubts about it. [signed] Witness N. Polezhayeva
