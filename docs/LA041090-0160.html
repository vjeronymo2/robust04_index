April 10, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: OF HURT'S BABY, 'BATMAN II' AND ELEPHANT SAVIOR

THE TRAUMA of William Hurt's "palimony" trial last year and the possibility of a second go-round in court hasn't hurt the actor's marriage to Heidi Henderson. Bill and Heidi, who live in the East, are expecting their second child. This time they hope for a girl to go with their Sam, age 7 months. But neither parent believes in finding out a baby's sex in advance, so they just have to wait and see what happens in August. And so do we.

AS IF THERE isn't enough turbulence in Great Britain these days, now the movie industry of Blighty is up in arms over news that the "Batman" sequel will be shot in Mexico instead of in England. The fantastic, $5-million "Gotham City" set has been kept intact at Pinewood Studios for more than a year, at a cost of $100,000-a-month storage. And that, of course, is in anticipation of Michael Keaton's "doing it again." But perhaps Warner Bros. doesn't feel obligated to honor commitments made by their ex-Batmoguls, Jon Peters and Peter Guber, who now do their stuff for Columbia Pictures.

MARLENE SANDERS was the first woman to cover the Vietnam War close up, so she is no stranger to the vicissitudes of foreign travel. And it's a good thing, too, because her recent trip to Nepal, for the Discovery Channel's "Profiles in Progress" series, had Marlene traveling both by Land Rover and by elephant.

The elephants were just wonderful, Marlene says, but the Rover got stuck in the middle of a raging river, and the plucky correspondent had to be rescued by canoe. The Land Rover was then brought out by elephant.

ON MONDAY Sotheby's in New York will be the site for the Manhattan Center for Living's gala benefit "Fantasy Auction." (The MCL helps people with life-threatening diseases.) Mike Nichols and Steve Sondheim of the Broadway theater are co-chairing this event, which offers about 40 fantasies donated by stars of entertainment, music, fashion and sport.

You'll be able to bid for tickets to see the great Maggie Smith in "Lettuce and Lovage," then meet and sip Champagne with her backstage, and go on to dine with "L&L" playwright Peter Shaffer at Orso's. (I have dined myself with this writing giant who created "Amadeus," and whoever wins him will be in for a treat.)

The sports-minded might land the right to go with Susan Sarandon, her "Bull Durham" cutie, Tim Robbins, and TV sports commentator Tim McCarver to lunch at the Regency, take in a New York Mets game, and meet the team. (We are not talking cheesy little fantasies here!)

Sotheby's expects bidding to go through the roof for one particular fantasy Breakfast at Tiffany's with Audrey Hepburn. The highest bidder, plus a guest, will enjoy a private fast-breaking with the star in the executive offices of the jewelry emporium. So two lucky people get to discover whether food ever passes those elegant Hepburn lips.
