BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 5 May. Jose Roberto Orellana Milla, president of the Salvadoran Central Reserve Bank, has described as positive the results of the privatization program of the country's banks and financial institutions. Regarding the sale of shares of the Atlacatl financial institution, he has said 25 percent of the shares were made available to small investors, 25 percent to Atlacatl employees, and the remaining 50 percent to investors who would like to obtain more than 100,000 colons. Orellana has announced that thus far five banks and four financial institutions have been privatized. (San Salvador Radio Cadena YSU in Spanish 1800 GMT 26 Apr 94) Central Reserve Bank stabilization certificates totaling 93,715,226.72 colons were negotiated during the early morning stock market sessions. Stabilization bonds of the Bank of Guatemala and Emergency Coffee fund were also negotiated. Yesterday's total negotiations amounting to 109,613,305 colones showed an upward trend, compared with last month's operations, which did not go over the 8-million colons mark. (San Salvador LA PRENSA GRAFICA in Spanish 26 Apr p 14) The Salvadoran Central Reserve Bank has confirmed that in 1993 imports totaled $1.9 billion, an 11.9-percent increase over 1992. Bank sources have said the increase was a result of the stabilized rate of exchange, the commercial opening, and facilities offered to industries. Exports showed a 21-percent increase compared to 1992. (San Salvador Radio Cadena YSU in Spanish 1800 GMT 28 April 94)
