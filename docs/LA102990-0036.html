October 29, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

Goaltender Ron Hextall, who missed all but eight games for the Philadelphia Flyers last season, will be sidelined for two to three weeks after suffering a mild sprain of a knee ligament in Saturday's 5-2 loss to the New York Islanders.

Hextall was playing his first game since Oct. 11, when he suffered a pulled groin muscle in a game against the New Jersey Devils. Last season, Hextall missed 51 games because of a suspension, three groin muscle injuries and a pulled hamstring.
