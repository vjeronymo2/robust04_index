Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Nanjing, March 2 (XINHUA) -- Nanjing, capital of east China's Jiangsu Province, has developed into a major economic center second only to Shanghai in the middle and lower reaches of the Chang Jiang River. The city has formed an economic and co-operative network involving the fields of transportation, trade, banking and information with Shanghai, Wuhan (capital of Hubei Province), Chongqing in Sichuan Province, Hefei (capital of Anhui Province) and Nanchang (capital of Jiangxi Province). Meanwhile, the city's markets for capital goods and international futures have become regional transaction centers. With convenient transportation facilities, Nanjing is regarded by major world consortiums and research institutions as a city with a promising future. Wang Wulong, acting mayor of the city, said Nanjing is improving its urban functions. It has opened 430 professional markets and trading centers, and markets of finance, technology and labor service. Their annual transaction volume tops 10 billion yuan. One of the major cities in the Chang Jiang River Valley, Nanjing has opened four container shipping routes to Japan, Hong Kong, Singapore and the Middle East, respectively. Its annual handling capacity reaches more than 50 million tons at present, and is expected to top 100 million tons by the end of this century. At present, Nanjing's 1,000 financial organizations are doing business with some 200 banking organizations in over 40 countries and regions. In addition, the city is developing a number of credit and trust, securities, financial and international leasing companies to pave the way for the setting up of banking organizations in the middle and lower reaches of the Chang Jiang River Valley. A survey shows that Nanjing has approved the establishment of 2,600 foreign-funded enterprises from more than 40 countries and regions so far.
