June 1, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

PATHE ARRANGES LOANS FOR BULK OF MGM/UA BUYOUT

The $1.2-billion acquisition of MGM/UA Communications Corp. by Pathe Communications Co. remained on course Thursday, despite rumors to the contrary, as new terms were spelled out in lengthy documents filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission.

MGM/UA revealed that Pathe will finance part of the buyout with an unsecured $450-million loan from one if its subsidiaries, Comfinance S.A. of Luxembourg. It also disclosed that Time Warner Inc. will directly loan Pathe $650 million toward the purchase, with the United Artists film library as collateral. Time Warner previously agreed only to guarantee a loan.

MGM/UA also left open the possibility that majority owner Kirk Kerkorian will retain some interest in the company. The deal is supposed to close by next Thursday, though MGM/UA reserved the right to extend the deadline.

Concerns about the purchase surfaced earlier this week when a story spread that a Time Warner board member was stirring opposition to the deal. According to published reports, the board member -- whose name was not revealed -- objected to Time Warner's involvement with Pathe co-President Giancarlo Parretti, who is appealing a "fraudulent bankruptcy" conviction handed down by a Naples court.

A Pathe spokesman denied the reports, and Thursday's announcement of the amended purchase plan appeared at least partially designed to offset concerns that the deal was in trouble.

"It sounds like they're probably going to get it done now," said Jeffrey Logsdon, an entertainment industry analyst with the brokerage Crowell, Weedon & Co. in Los Angeles.

The MGM/UA filing indicates that Pathe's financing for the $20-a-share deal remains somewhat mysterious, however. Comfinance, a holding company, will issue Pathe a one-year, $450-million loan that is funded through "working capital and available financing," but the documents do not explain where Comfinance will acquire the money.

Pathe has the option of paying off the loan at any time within the year and may do so with shares of common stock. It may also repay the loan through the sale of unnamed assets.

Time Warner spokeswoman Marilyn Harris said executives were not available for comment on the revised plan.

Time Warner initially agreed to arrange for the loan on Pathe's behalf in return for distribution rights to the United Artists film library and future MGM/UA and Pathe releases. Under the new plan, the Time Warner loan will be made directly to Pathe, and secured by a note for the loan's amount and certain assets, such as the Pathe and Cannon Group film libraries.

Time Warner is also expected to retain the same film distribution rights.

Finally, MGM/UA's amended filing revealed the sources of money used by Pathe in its non-refundable $150-million down payment. According to MGM/UA, $71 million came from Pathe's "available cash" and $79 million was "advanced by the (company's) European interests."
