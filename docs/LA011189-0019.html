January 11, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

REWARD

After reading Sherry Potter's letter ("Reward", Jan.5) concerning lost wallets and rewards, I'd like to describe my own recent experience. While on jury duty in Glendale in December, I visited the main Pasadena City Library and found a wallet (filled with driver's license and other valuable cards) in the parking lot.

I drove over to the owner's address and left a note with my name and telephone under his door. When I returned to my parked car, I discovered a $20 parking ticket on my windshield. I drove over to Pasadena City Hall and paid the ticket. Two days later, the owner of the wallet telephoned and I mailed the lost wallet back to his Pasadena address. Lo and behold, the owner( Michael Ackley) sent a letter of thanks and enclosed a $25 check to cover the $20 parking ticket and my expenses.

KENNETH LLOYD LARSON

Los Angeles
