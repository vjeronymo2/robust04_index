BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Anna Melnikova and Yuriy Sizov] [Text] Moscow, 30 Apr -- The Russian Armed Forces are to be withdrawn from the territory of the Republic of Latvia by 31 August 1994. ITAR-TASS correspondents learned this during the course of talks between Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis. Russia and Latvia will sign a treaty on conditions, deadlines, and the procedure for the complete withdrawal of the Russian Armed Forces from the territory of the Latvian Republic and their legal situation for the period of the withdrawal. The complete withdrawal of the Armed Forces includes all members of the Russian Armed Forces, members of their families and personal estate. An integral part of the present treaty will also be an agreement on the status of the Skrunda radar station for the period of its functioning and dismantlement. Agreements will be signed between the two countries on matters of social protection for military servicemen and members of their families temporarily resident on the territory of the Latvian Republic until their complete withdrawal, as well as matters of social protection for military pensioners and their families living on the territory of the Latvian Republic.
