940628 FT 28 JUN 94 / International Company News: Axa plans to invest Dollars 200m in Japan Axa, the French insurance group, said yesterday that it plans to invest about Dollars 200m within the next five to seven years to expand its presence in the Japanese market, following provisional approval to set up a life assurance subsidiary in the country. Axa said that it hoped to receive definitive approval to set up the subsidiary at the beginning of next year and plans to start marketing its products from next April. It said it hopes to set up 12 regional offices after its initial entry into the Japanese market. The company, which claims to be the first French group to have received such authorisation to set up a life insurance subsidiary in Japan, said that it aims to achieve FFr1bn of premiums within five years and more than FFr4bn within a decade.
