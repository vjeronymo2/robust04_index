Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 28 (YONHAP) -- The South Korean Government on Monday called the recent killing of Palestinians in the West Bank "an unforgivable act of violence" and urged involved parties to exercise restraint. Foreign Ministry Spokesman Chang Ki-ho issued a statement deploring the killing, saying the Seoul government "believes that such an unforgivable act of violence is in no way of help to the on-going Middle East peace process." The Korean Government calls upon all parties to prevent the recurrence of such a tragic and terrible incident, the statement said. Seoul hopes that "they overcome this tragedy and continue to exert their efforts for early realization of the Palestinian self-rule and for settlement of permanent peace in the region through dialogue and reconciliation."
