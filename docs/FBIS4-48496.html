BFN [From the "Network Africa" program] [Text] The man most likely to succeed Dr. Banda, as the current pattern of results continues, is the UDF [United Democratic Front] candidate Bakili Muluzi. On the line to Blantyre last night, I asked Mr. Muluzi whether he now expected to be Malawi's next president. [Begin recording] [Muluzi] Well, I think so, from the look of the things. From the results that have already been counted, definitely yes. For your information, I had already a call from the vice president of the Malawi Congress Party [MCP] conveying congratulations from Dr. Banda and conceding defeat. [Unidentified reporter] And what about in the parliamentary results. Do you also expect to hold a majority in parliament? [Muluzi] Well, yes, we might just make it. You know that we actually have got most of the seats in the southern region and some seats in the central region; and definitely we might just make it for majority in parliament. [Reporter] With things still not quite over yet, there is still a way to go. If it does emerge that you don't quite have enough of a majority in parliament, is it possible you would consider a coalition, and if so, who do you think that is likely to be with? [Muluzi] Well, we haven't given a thought to that one, but if that comes to that kind of thing, we wouldn't mind talking to the other opposition parties. In this case, we are talking about Aford [Alliance for Democracy]. I have not had any word with our good friend, Mr. Chakufwa Chihana, but if it comws to that, we will definitely be talking to them. After all, we fought this campaign, particularly the referendum, together. So, we definitely, would not mind talking to them at all. [Reporter] So, in other words, would you rule out altogether any kind of a deal with MCP? [Muluzi] Well, yes. Mind you, although it wasn't a fight as such, but I think we are fighting a system and that system was the Malawi Congress Party system. We definitely have not even thought about talking to Malawi Congress Party at all. The opposition that is leading at the moment, apart from the UDF, is Aford, and perhaps one would be more comfortable talking to Aford than MCP. [Reporter] So, Mr. Muluzi, has your success in this election come as something of a surprise to you or not? [Muluzi] I wouldn't say as a surprise. I think our party has been very popular. From the beginning, it was one of those parties which was well organized, and we had support in a quite a number of areas in our country. So, yes, a surprise, but not a lot of surprise. I think we definitely expected some kind of change. Let me just mention again that, I think, the mood in Malawi has been for change. I think people have been looking for a change for a long time. I think it is just time that people wanted change. [Reporter] So, finally at this time, do you have any message for the man who is still, after all, the incumbent president in Malawi, President Banda? [Muluzi] Well, let me just mention one thing to you -- that we definitely recognize the role that Dr. Banda played, you know, during the time he has been president. I mean, he has been president of Malawi for the last 30 years. Definitely, one perhaps would say, look, let him retire in peace. I personally had nothing personal to Dr. Banda [sentence as heard]. I think we are fighting his system of government. So, what I would like to say is that, you know, let him have a peaceful retirement. I would definitely want to thank him for whatever contribution he made to Malawi. I think it is just human to say that. [end recording]
