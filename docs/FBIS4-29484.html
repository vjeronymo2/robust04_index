BFN [Text] France is not ready to grant a Cambodian government request for arms to fight the Khmer Rouge. Deputy Foreign Minister Surin Phitsuwan said yesterday after a recent meeting with the French foreign affairs permanent secretary. Surin added that Australia, which had also been approached by the Cambodian government, had made no decision on the matter and the United States was only interested in supporting military training in Cambodia. Surin, who returned from an overseas tour, stopped over in France after he attended a meeting of ambassadors and counsellors in Morocco. He arrived in Bangkok from Kuala Lumpur yesterday. Apart from narcotics suppression and the Cambodian conflict, Surin said the talks with French officials included discussion on SEA 10, a Southeast Asian grouping which includes the six in the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), the three Indochinese nations and Burma.
