BFN [Unattributed article: "European Commission Concerned Over Kantor Report"] [Text] (ANSA) Brussels, April 5 -- The European Commission on Tuesday [5 April] expressed its concern over what it saw as threats of sanctions against the European Union [EU] implied in the report drawn up by United States trade representative Mickey Kantor on alleged trade obstacles and said it would reply point-by-point to the charges made against the European Union in its own report. Speaking to the press here, an official spokesman for the European Union executive added how this year the report from the U.S. trade representative was much better than previous years in the sense that there was less criticism of the European Union and even recognition that many problems between the United States and the European Union had been resolved over the past months. This is also due to the end of the Uruguay Round negotiations to renew the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) which governs world trade. At the same time, the spokesman stressed how the European Commission remain concerned over the threats implied in the Kantor report, in particular those of sanctions in the field of public contracts and the safeguarding of intellectual property.
