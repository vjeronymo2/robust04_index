Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Dec 2 KYODO -- Japan will loan up to 8.15 billion yen to Argentina to support the nation's efforts to improve the environment, Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa said Thursday [2 December]. Hosokawa made the pledge in a meeting with Argentine President Carlos Menem, according to Foreign Ministry officials. The loan will be provided for Argentina's program to improve sanitary conditions along the Reconquista River, which runs north of the capital of Buenos Aires, the officials said. The loan is part of Japan's plan to expand its Official Development Assistance (ODA) to 900 billion to 1 trillion yen over a five-year period from 1992. Tokyo expressed its intention to expand its ODA at the U.N. Conference on Environment and Development (UNCED) in June last year, they said. The loan will be extended in cooperation with the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB), they said.
