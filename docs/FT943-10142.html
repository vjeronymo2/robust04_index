940806 FT 06 AUG 94 / Finance and the Family: Directors' transactions The week started very quietly with few transactions being reported. Where there has been dealing, the bias remains towards buying, with two companies having open offers. Directors at both Computer People and EFT Group took the opportunity afforded by the new stock to increase their existing holdings. Largest sale of the week was at Camellia Group, the agriculture and financial service provision company. David Bacon, managing director, sold 5,000 shares at Pounds 25.50p. The sale accounted for over 5 per cent of his holding, leaving him with 89,000 shares. Shortly before the closed period at the end of March another director, Donald Green, a non-executive, also sold stock. Asprey, the jewellers, featured recently when John Asprey, the chairman, sold 375,000 shares at 306p. His total stake accounts for 50 per cent of the company so relatively the sale was not significant. This time Timothy Cooper, who manages the Bond Street branch of operations, exercised 35,740 options and then sold them all. The company is anticipated to show pre-tax profits growth of 18 per cent over the next year. Relatively, the share price is underperforming the market. Filofax, the personal organiser people, has also featured on this list before.
