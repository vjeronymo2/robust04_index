BFN [Text] The CIS peace-keeping forces, mostly composed of the Russian military contingent, are to be introduced into the zone of the Abkhazian conflict before June 10. The latest date for their deployment is June 15. The operation will be supervised by Deputy Commander of the Russian infantry troops, Lieutenant-General Aleksandr Sokolov. Interfax learned this from sources in one of the delegations currently negotiating in Moscow on deployment of the peace-keeping forces. The negotiations are expected to end today. The Abkhazian delegation is headed by Abkhazian Defense Minister Sultan Sosnaliyev. Georgian Defense Minister Varden Nadibaidze leads the Georgian delegation. Russia acts as a mediator and is represented by high-ranking military officers. In accordance with the agreements reached on May 14, the peace- keeping forces will be deployed in the security zone which will lay on the Inguri River after the Georgian and Abkhazian troops are withdrawn twelve kilometers from it. The size of the peace forces is expected to be about 2,500-3,000 servicemen. Interfax learned that a group of Russian officers may leave for Abkhazia on Thursday in order to define specific regions for deployment of the peace-keeping forces.
