930628 FT 28 JUN 93 / International Company News: United Industries setback EXCEPTIONAL costs hit United Industries in the year ended April 3 leaving pre-tax losses ahead from Pounds 1.54m to Pounds 3.63m. The materials handling, springs and process machinery group saw turnover fall from Pounds 32.3m to Pounds 29.6m, with the discontinued cutting tool operations accounting for Pounds 2.35m (Pounds 3.94m). The operating deficit was Pounds 556,000 (Pounds 79,000) shared by continuing businesses Pounds 295,000 (profit Pounds 191,000) and discontinued Pounds 261,000 (Pounds 270,000). In the exceptional charges were Pounds 474,000 for reorganising the Dutch spring company, Pounds 447,000 for redundancy and reorganisation, and Pounds 1.35m loss on sale of cutting tool div-ision. The economies in which the group operated worsened during the second half. In particular the Dutch operations which benefited from the strong German market and had been relatively little affected by recession, suffered a sudden and substantial downturn in orders.
