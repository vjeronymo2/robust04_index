Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 3 (YONHAP) -- National Assembly Speaker Yi Man-sop left Seoul Thursday for a seven-day visit to Beijing at the invitation of his Chinese counterpart, Qiao Shi, chairman of the standing committee of the national people's congress. Yi will meet Qiao later in the day and discuss ways to boost Sino-Korean legislative exchanges and bilateral cooperation. He is scheduled to meet with President Jiang Zemin and Prime Minister Li Peng to exchange ideas on resolving the North Korean nuclear issue and boosting bilateral economic partnership. Yi, the first South Korean speaker to visit the communist state, will tour sites in Shanghai where Korean independence fighters were based on his way home. Accompanying the speaker are Reps. Pak Hun-ki and Yi Hwan-ui of the Democratic Liberal Party, Reps. Kim Tae-sik and Choe Chae-sung of the Democratic Party, independent Rep. Pak Ku-il, and his chief secretary, Kang Song-chae.
