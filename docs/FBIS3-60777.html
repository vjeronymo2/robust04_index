Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on regional guerrilla activities and clashes with government military units monitored through 22 December. Source is cited in parentheses following each item.

Colombia
The Army Command in Cali announced on 21 December that Alvaro Martinez, commander of the leftist Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia, FARC, 6th Front died in a clash with Army forces in the rural region of Tulua, in southwestern Valle del Cauca Department. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1346 GMT 21 Dec 93) Five policemen died and five others were wounded and four are missing as a result of a guerrilla ambush carried out in La Chorrera, Bolivar Municipality, in south Cauca. Cartagena also has been the target of terrorism: Three urban bus stops were dynamited on the morning of 22 December by National Liberation Army units. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1730 GMT 22 Dec 93)
Ecuador
Ecuadoran police Colonels Milton Molina and Jorge Poveda have reported that elite police units captured three alleged FARC guerrillas in the Amazon Jungle region. The arrests took place after the ambush by a terrorist FARC column against an Ecuadoran police-military force sailing on the Putumayo River. (Paris AFP in Spanish 0141 GMT 21 Dec 93)
Nicaragua
Three peasants were murdered and a minor injured as a result of terrorist acts carried out by recontra groups of the 3-80 Northern Front in Matagalpa and Jinotega Departments. One of the peasants was killed in El Jobo, near Wiwili; and the other two in Bocay, Jinotega Department. (Managua EL NUEVO DIARIO in Spanish 22 Dec 93 p 8) Army General Humberto Ortega reported today that 487 persons, including irregular forces, military, and civilians, have died so far this year as a result of the violence in northern Nicaragua where up to November there had been 247 clashes. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2124 GMT 22 Dec 93)
