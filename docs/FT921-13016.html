920123 FT 23 JAN 92 / Observer: Retuned Tass, the former Soviet news agency which kow-towed to the August plotters, need not look far for a company song now its name has been changed under Boris Yeltsin's latest bit of organisational retuning. 'Tass' stood for (in translation) Telegraph Agency of the Soviet Union. In full, the new name also has four words whose initial letters are the same in Russian and English. It is Russian Information Telegraph Agency - all together now: 'Lovely Rita media maid . . .'
