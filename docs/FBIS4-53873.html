CSO [Text] (Nagoya) Aichi Steel Works signed a consulting contract concerning the construction of a factory with U.S. forged steel products maker Louisville Forge and Gear Works of Kentucky. Aichi Steel Works will advise Louisville Forge on the layout, equipment specifications, and final manufacturing processes for a new forging plant which the latter plans to build. President J. Payton of Louisville Forge visited Japan on 5 April to sign the contract. Louisville Forge supplies the three major automakers and Japanese automakers with North American plants with forged components. Louisville Forge has maintained a technical assistance agreement with Aichi Steel Works since July 1990 and in August of last year it had reached a technical assistance agreement on the manufacture of crankshafts using "nontempered steel" which does not involve heat processing. Louisville Forge is formulating plans for relocation and construction of a new plant, but the timing and location are still undetermined.
