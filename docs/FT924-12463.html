921021 FT 21 OCT 92 / International Company News: LSI Logic reverses into loss LSI LOGIC, the US semiconductor manufacturer, suffered third-quarter losses after Dollars 102m restructuring charges for the closure of its German production plant and a refocusing of its product lines. Net losses for the quarter were Dollars 111m, or Dollars 2.51 per share, compared with a net profit of Dollars 804,000, or 2 cents a share, in the third quarter last year. Excluding the results of the charge, the operating loss in the quarter was Dollars 3m, compared with an operating profit of Dollars 4m in the third quarter a year ago. Including the restructuring charge, the operating loss in the third quarter was Dollars 105m. Revenues in the third quarter were Dollars 154m, down nearly 11 per cent from Dollars 172m a year ago. Last month, LSI Logic announced it would accelerate the phasing out of older manufacturing capacity in the US and Germany, and emphasise higher value-added products. including application-specific integrated circuits (Asic). The growth in third quarter revenues over the prior second quarter reversed a string of four consecutive quarterly declines. The net loss for the nine months was Dollars 117m, or Dollars 2.63 per share, compared with a net profit of Dollars 7m, or 17 cents a share, in the same period last year. Revenues were Dollars 456m, down nearly 14.5 per cent from Dollars 534m a year ago.
