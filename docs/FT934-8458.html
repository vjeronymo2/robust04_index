931116 FT 16 NOV 93 / Brussels telecoms go-ahead THE European Commission has cleared a joint venture between 12 of the world's largest telecommunications groups, aimed at providing specialised communication services between financial centres, writes Andrew Hill. But the Financial Network Association had to give special undertakings that it would not discriminate against private service suppliers. The FNA also undertook not to use revenue from 'reserved' monopoly services to subsidise services such as data transmission. Companies involved are: France Telecom, Belgacom, the Belgian state telecoms company, Italcable of Italy, Mercury Communications of the UK, Telfonica of Spain, Deutsche Bundespost Telekom of Germany, Australia's Telstra, Hong Kong Telecom, KDD of Japan, MCI of the US, Singapore Telecom and Stentor of Canada.
