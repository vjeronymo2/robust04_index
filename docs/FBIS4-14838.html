BFN [Text] Yerevan, April 4 (SNARK)--Levon Zurabyan, acting press-secretary of the Armenian president declared today Armenian-Iranian relations are still normal. Iranian Government hasn't apply any sanctions on Armenia, despite the recent declaration of Iranian Foreign Ministry, blaming Armenia for the crash of the plain C-130 in Karabakh, said Mr. Zurabyan at a briefing in Yerevan. He mentioned the incident hasn't influence on the whole the relations between Armenia and Iran. At the same time, he reported that the Iranian commission of experts and a similar commission from CIS, having a mandate from UN haven't made any official conclusion on the reasons of the air-crash. According to the available data the plane's flight through the territory of Azerbaijan and Karabakh lasted 15 minutes more than it should be by the schedule. All the communication with the plane was interrupted after it had appeared in the air-space of the Azerbaijani airport Ganja. Its flight was hence controlled by the Azerbaijani Air-Defence forces. All this data is given to the Iranian side, said Mr. Zurabyan.
