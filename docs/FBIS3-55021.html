Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Nerijus Maliukevicius, the Lithuanian President's spokesman for the press reported that the first sitting of the Council for Coordination of Foreign Policy was held today at the president's office. The council is headed by Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas. Prime Minister Adolfas Slezevicius, Lithuanian Seimas Chairman Ceslovas Jursenas, Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys, National Defense Minister Linas Linkevicius, and Justas Paleckis, the president's adviser on foreign policy, took part in today's sitting. The Council for Coordination of Foreign Policy was set up on 9 December of last year by decree of the president. The objective of the council is to coordinate actions in foreign policy among the government institutions and to aim for an effective implementation of these actions, as well as to discuss proposals concerning the execution of foreign policy. Two items on today's agenda were discussed: The first item was Lithuania's cooperation with the European Union and the expansion of NATO, and the second issue was on future visits to foreign countries by Lithuanian top government officials and future visits to Lithuania by officials and leaders of foreign countries. Brazauskas is to make a statement on today's Lithuanian TV Panorama program on one of the issues discussed.
