BFN [Text] The secretariat of the central committee of workers of the agro-industrial complex, whose representatives, alongside the Federation of the Independent Trade Union and other trade unions, are to sign the treaty on civic accord, decided Wednesday not to sign the document on May 5, Interfax was told by deputy chairman of the trade union's central committee, Anatoliy Biryukov. He said that the trade union will not sign the treaty until "we see for ourselves its text with the farmers' amendment and the protocol of an agreement between the trade union and the government." The farmers trade union uniting at present 13 million people joined the demands advanced by the Agrarian Party and the Agrarian Union to supplement the text of the draft treaty with government commitments on the creation of acceptable economic conditions for the trade union functioning, as well as to conclude an agreement with the government on the main guidelines for the agrarian policies for the next two years. The conditions were accepted. However, no corresponding documents were received by the central committee except for media reports, said Biryukov. The secretariat will consider without delay the documents as soon as they come and if they fully correspond to the conditions advanced by farmers, will give powers for the trade union leader to sign the document.
