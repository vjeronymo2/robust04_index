Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Major General Amnon Shahaq, head of the Israeli delegation to the Taba talks, said a short while ago that the first week of talks ended without a breakthrough on security issues. At the same time, he stressed that understandings have been achieved on several issues regarding the transfer of civilian authority. On the phone from Taba is our correspondent Netan'el Semrik: A short time ago, the first week of the Taba talks ended. Let us hear a statement made here a few minutes ago by the head of the Israeli delegation, Maj. Gen. Amnon Shahaq: [Begin Shahaq recording] Today we are concluding the talks for this week, to my regret, because of last night's tragedy. Most of the delegates are leaving to attend the funeral of the Maj. Gen. Nehemya Tamari tomorrow. We had planned to be here until tomorrow night, and we will return here next Monday [17 January] to continue the talks. Over the past week, we continued the meetings of both the Security and the Civilian Committees. We have not reached full conclusion on any of the issues. We have, however, definitely reached understandings on more than one of the civilian issues. We will continue the negotiations in both fields next week. [end recording]
