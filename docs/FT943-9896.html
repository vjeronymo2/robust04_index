940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / World Trade News: Hyundai Indian deal Hyundai, the South Korean industrial group, is setting up its first joint venture in India to make marine freight containers in collaboration with DCM Shriram group of Delhi, writes Shiraz Sidhva in New Delhi. The new company, DCM Hyundai, will build a wholly export-oriented unit at Polivakkam, near Madras in Tamil Nadu, at the cost of Rs700m (Dollars 220m), with an installed capacity of 18,000 TEUs (20ft equivalent unit). The Hyundai group will have a 15 per cent equity stake, and the DCM Shriram group, 26 per cent. DCM Shriram Industries, which has interests in sugar, chemicals, textiles, and leasing, has just begun a Rs1bn modernisation and expansion programme. Mr Bansi Dhar, DCM Hyundai chairman, said the company would enter the capital markets in the next two months to raise Rs150m for the project. The plant is expected to be operational by December.
