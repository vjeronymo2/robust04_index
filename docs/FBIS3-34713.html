Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by Army affairs correspondent Eytan Rabin] [Text] The IDF [Israel Defense Forces] General Staff is satisfied with the Cairo agreement signed between the PLO and Israel on 9 February. A military source yesterday said that "under the present circumstances, it is the best agreement." The source asserted that both sides had conceded on points they would not have compromised on in the past, and the General Staff feels this is the best possible agreement. Senior IDF sources said that the test would be the implementation of the agreement. According to a military source, the IDF has reacted positively to the agreement, but many details have yet to be worked out and many difficulties are to be expected before the agreement can be implemented. IDF sources stressed that the Cairo agreement is being perused in detail by top military officials and every detail is being examined from a professional standpoint. A senior IDF source said: "in general, it is a good agreement. Most of our interests have been protected, especially the security interest." According to the senior source, several issues still remain unresolved even after the agreement, such as the matter of the Palestinian police and the way security measures will be handled at the border crossings. IDF sources state, for example, that the procedures for crossing the Palestinian checkpoint remain unclear. According to the agreement, passengers crossing through the Palestinian checkpoint will walk through a magnetic gate. An Israeli and Palestinian guard will stand on each side of the gate, and in any suspicious case will be permitted to carry out a body search at the gate and to question the traveler. The IDF anticipates the possibility of problems and confrontations at the terminal, with each side demanding to check travelers. The IDF is still studying the Cairo agreement and will establish staff teams over the next few days to analyze the agreements and implement them on the ground. Officers at the Central and Southern Commands are prepared to withdraw from the Gaza Strip and Jericho as soon as the political echelon issues the order. As reported by HA'ARETZ, the General Staff has formulated a plan that will enable the troops to withdraw from the Gaza Strip and Jericho within one month.
