Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Berlin (DDP/ADN) -- Slovene Foreign Minister Lojze Peterle, who is also the former prime minister, arrived in the German capital today for a two-day visit. He will meet Eberhard Diepgen, the governing mayor of Berlin, this afternoon. Peterle has come to the capital at the invitation of the Berlin Aspen Institute. He will take part in roundtable talks on Europe, Slovenia, and the Balkans on 2 February. Talks with business representatives are also planned. Peterle was elected the first prime minister of the first democratically elected Slovene Government in May 1990. He held the post for two years. The 45-year-old became deputy prime minister and foreign minister in 1993.
