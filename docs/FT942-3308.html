940615 FT 15 JUN 94 / UK Company News: Courtaulds chief gets 25% increase Mr Sipko Huismans, chief executive of Courtaulds, received a 25 per cent pay increase from Pounds 284,150 to Pounds 355,353 in 1994, according to the chemical company's annual report. Pre-tax profits last year fell by 35 per cent from Pounds 186m to Pounds 122m. The figure was affected by exceptional items and a higher interest bill although even at the operating level profits slipped by 16 per cent to Pounds 176.4m. Mr Huismans' pay included a basic Pounds 320,000 (Pounds 270,000) and a performance-related bonus of Pounds 12,800 this time. Benefits in kind increased 59 per cent to Pounds 22,553 (Pounds 14,150). Under the company's executive share option scheme, Mr Huismans was granted options on 30,000 more shares taking his total to 195,000. He already owns 20,500 shares. Sir Christopher Hogg, chairman, saw his pay rise by 3.7 per cent, from Pounds 110,058 to Pounds 114,131. He has no executive share options but already owns 121,000 shares. Two other Courtaulds directors received packages worth between Pounds 215,001 and Pounds 220,000. In 1993 the second and third highest paid directors each received between Pounds 185,001 and Pounds 190,000.
