Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Republic of Singapore Air Force [RSAF] can now train in Bangladesh. The two countries today signed a memorandum of understanding [MOU] and the implementation of long-term training. Under this, the RSAF is allowed to deploy a number of aircraft at Bangladesh Air Force bases for several months a year. Mindef [Ministry of Defense] says Singapore welcomes the move by Dhaka. It said the increased interaction between the two air forces will contribute to stronger defense ties. The MOU was signed in Singapore by the Chief of Air Force Brigadier General Bey Soo Khiang and Air Vice Marshal Altaf Hossain Chowdhury, Chief of Air Staff of the Bangladesh Air Force.
