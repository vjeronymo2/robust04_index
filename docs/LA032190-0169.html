March 21, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

BUSS WANTS TO BUILD ARENA IN SAN DIEGO FOR 2 TEAMS

The owner of the Lakers says he is considering building a new San Diego sports arena in which he hopes to eventually house two new professional sports franchises.

Jerry Buss said he has been scouting North County sites to build a home for a National Basketball Assn. team and a National Hockey League expansion franchise.

"If I can get a grant on some land and the franchises, the rest of it is a slam dunk," said Buss, the owner of the Forum in Inglewood, where the NBA Lakers and NHL Kings play.

Buss said he has put together an unidentified group interested in relocating an NBA team or procuring an expansion team for his new building. He said the Lakers would not be involved in the San Diego arena project.

Buss also said he has applied to the NHL for an expansion franchise.

Buss' disclosure places him in direct competition with Harry Cooper and Richard Esquinas, the operators of the existing San Diego Sports Arena, who are in the process of applying for city permits to build a new arena downtown.
