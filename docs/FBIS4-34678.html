BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Panamanian economic developments monitored through 21 June. Trade activities between Panama and Taiwan reached approximately $350 million in 1993, making Panama the second leading market for Taiwanese exports. Trade between the two nations is expected to increase to approximately $370 million this year. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 17 June 94 p 17 A) Based on the behavior of the leading economic indicators during the first four months of the year, no significant changes are expected in the export levels of traditional products compared to figures obtained in 1993. Nontraditional exports, which already total $190 million, have experienced an increase in amount, value, and variety. The export of services for 1994 will experience a 5-percent increase if current figures prevail. Duty-free zone activities will grow, but not as much as in 1993, and the volume of oil carried by pipeline will decrease as anticipated. A growth in tourism will remain moderate. Concerning imports, the collection of tariffs and sales taxes indicate a 15-percent increase. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 17 June 94 p 8 A)
