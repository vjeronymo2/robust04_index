Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, March, 6, IRNA -- Iran and Russia here Saturday agreed to cooperate for peaceful settlement of current disputes in the Republic of Tajikistan. The agreement was reached by Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Mahmud Va'ezi and his Russian counterpart Anatoliy Adamishin, currently here to discuss regional and bilateral issues with Iranian officials. Adamishin, who arrived here last Friday, said that there was proper grounds for Tehran-Moscow cooperation to resolve Tajikistan disputes. Referring to the endeavors made by the Islamic Republic to bring an end to the disputes, Va'ezi reiterated that Iran, like before, would continue efforts to establish a just peace in Tajikistan. Any effort should be based on existing realities inside Tajikistan and be aimed at resolving people's problems, Va'ezi added, stressing that the best way to this end was formation of a government able to materialize national aspirations of the Tajik people. Adamishin is also expected to hold official talks with Tajik government's opposition leaders.
