Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Tunis, 20 Jan (MENA)--PLO Executive Committee member Samir Ghawshah has announced that the committee has decided to take part in the next round of the bilateral peace talks, due to begin in Washington on Monday [24 January]. In a statement to the MENA correspondent in Tunis, he said the Palestinian leadership is holding contacts with Dr. Haydar 'Abd-al-Shafi to lead the Palestinian delegation to these talks. If he refuses, Ghawshah added, Dr. Sa'ib 'Urayqat, the deputy head of the delegation, will lead the delegation in the next round. Ghawshah emphasized the importance of coordination and cooperation among all Arab parties taking part in the peace talks, as was the case before the signing of the Declaration of Principles between the PLO and Israel on 13 September. He said the positive results of the summit held between U.S. President Bill Clinton and Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad will have a positive effect on all tracks and will advance the peace process. He added that the summit results will also have a positive effect on the Palestinians.
