921111 FT 11 NOV 92 / UK Company News: Dissidents fail at Hoskins An attempt by dissident shareholders to take over the management of Hoskins Brewery, the Leicester-based brewer, collapsed yesterday. Mr Richard Cattermole, chief executive of Ryan Elizabeth, an East Anglian pubs and hotels company, and his associate, Mr Jeremy Lea, abandoned moves to oust Messrs Barrie Hoar, chairman, and his brother Robert Hoar, from the Hoskins board. A statement said: 'Mr Cattermole believes the recent attention drawn to Hoskins' financial performance will focus the minds of the Hoar brothers on what has to be done to restore confidence.' Mr Barrie Hoar welcomed the withdrawal, but said he was awaiting formal confirmation.
