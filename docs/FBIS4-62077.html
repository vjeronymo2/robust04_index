BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Larisa Kislinskaya] [Text] Moscow May 11 TASS -- Criminal charges were brought against Police Major-General Igor Shilov, first deputy head of the main criminal department of the Russian Interior Ministry. He is accused of illegal possession of arms, reliable sources told TASS on Wednesday. Shilov was traced by the Moscow Department for Organised Crime when it arrested a certain Korneyev, a swindler with a big criminal record. According to the sources, Shilov and his son maintained "corrupted links with Korneyev". Investigators reportedly possess information that Shilov received bribes from the swindler, however head of the Moscow Department for Organised Crime Colonel Vladimir Rushaylo refused to provide any comment. Shilov's son, who is an advanced student of the Police Academy, also faces criminal charges for illegal possession of weapons. The case is being investigated by the Moscow prosecutor's office which said that a total of eight policemen are involved in it. Only one of them has been arrested. Despite criminal charges Shilov still keeps his post. His and his son's apartments have been searched. The investigation is going on. According to the "MOSKOVSKIY KOMSOMOLETS" newspaper, a combat rifle with a sight and a big number of submachine-gun cartridges were discovered in Shilov's apartment. A big amount of ammunition was also found in his son's flat.
