BFN [Text] Receiving Haitian refugees aboard a U.S. ship in Jamaica's Kingston Harbor costs $120,000 a day, according to the 25 June SUN-SENTINEL, a Fort Lauderdale, Florida newspaper. The use of a Coast Guard patrol boat to process the Haitian people costs $2,100 an hour. A flight hour for each of the six helicopters accompanying the patrol boat costs $750, the newspaper reports. The operation for relief and repatriation of Haitian refugees constitutes the Coast Guard's largest operation since the second World War, a U.S. Coast Guard official told the newspaper. According to him, there have never been so many U.S. warships, from both the Coast Guard and the Navy, on the open sea. Eight U.S. ships, 15 Coast Guard patrol boats, and hundreds of immigration service members are participating in the relief and repatriation operations. The U.S. ship, USNS Comfort, which is anchored in Kingston Harbor, is temporarily receiving the Haitian refugees to allow them to present their political asylum requests to the United States. The Haitians, whose requests have been rejected, are repatriated to Port-au-Prince. This is what the SUN-SENTINEL reported on 26 June.
