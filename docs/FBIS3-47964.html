Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] New York, Jan. 20 KYODO -- Japan's domestic telecommunications giant, Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corp., said Thursday [20 January] it has paved the way for entry into the U.S. telecommunications market. The former government-run monopoly said a contract was signed with U.S. mobile telecom firm Nextel Communications Inc. (CALL) on Thursday to form a partnership between the two companies. Under the agreement, NTT will acquire a 0.9% stake in the U.S. partner for 75 million dollars and cooperate in the deployment of Nextel's digital mobile networks throughout the United States, the company said. NTT is promoting in Japan its own digital communications service, known as integrated service digital network. Besides NTT, Motorola Inc. of the United States and Matsushita Communication Industrial Co., an affiliate of consumer electronics maker Matsushita Electric Industrial Co., have invested in Nextel.
