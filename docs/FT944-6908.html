941128 FT 28 NOV 94 / Giscard 'will not run' Former President Valery Giscard d'Estaing said yesterday that, under current circumstances, he would not run in next spring's French presidential election, Reuter reports from Paris. 'To be a candidate, you have to be in a position to be one. And I am not in that position today,' Mr Giscard d'Estaing, leader of the centre-right UDF and president from 1974 to 1981, told TF1 television. Opinion polls show that many right-wing voters who would ideologically be attracted to him appear to be switching support to Prime Minister Edouard Balladur, a member of the RPR and a probable contender. Many UDF leaders have also said they would back Mr Balladur. Mr Giscard d'Estaing said: 'Something would have to change before February, either in the opinions of the French people or in developing events, for me to change my mind.'
