BFN [Text] Beijing, June 20 (XINHUA) -- A senior leader of the Chinese Communist Party (CPC) said here today that the party hopes to further its ties with the Communist Party of India (Marxist). Qiao Shi, member of the Standing Committee of the Political Bureau of the CPC Central Committee, said that the party values the active role of the Indian party in the maintenance and promotion of the friendly ties between the two countries and the two peoples. The CPC is willing to further its links with the Communist Party of India (Marxist) on the four inter-party principles of independence, equality, mutual respect and non-interference in each other's internal affairs, Qiao said. Welcoming Harkishan Singh Surjeet [name as received], general secretary of the Communist Party of India (Marxist), Qiao also conveyed to him the best regards of Jiang Zemin, general secretary of the CPC Central Committee. China and India are not only neighbors but also among the biggest developing countries in Asia, Qiao said, adding that in recent years, the two countries have witnessed smooth growth of bilateral ties and strengthened cooperation in international affairs. The strengthening of their cooperation in various fields is in line with the interests of the two nations, and is conducive to peace, stability and development in Asia and the world as a whole, said Qiao, who is chairman of the Standing Committee of China's National People's Congress. Surjeet told Qiao that his visit is aimed at knowing more about China's policy of reform and opening to the world, its achievements as well as its problems. "We are deeply impressed by the great achievements China has obtained in recent years," he said. The Communist Party of India (Marxist) speaks highly of Deng Xiaoping's theory of building socialism with Chinese characteristics and is confident of socialism's future, he said. Li Shuzheng, head of the International Liaison Department of the CPC Central Committee, took part in the meeting.
