Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] The first official round of talks between Mr. Hashemi-Rafsanjani and Mr. Saparmurad Niyazov, presidents of the Islamic Republic of Iran and Turkmenistan, was held on Sunday [2 January] evening. At the meeting, Mr. Niyazov thanked the officials of the Islamic Republic of Iran for their warm hospitality and explained that the aim of his trip to Iran is to benefit from our country's successful experiences and to find new ways for expanding bilateral relations in a new era of cooperation between the two countries. The president of Turkmenistan emphasized the religious, cultural, and historical features shared by the two countries and noted that Iran is a country with an ancient history and civilization. He said: Turkmenistan wishes to have wider cultural links with the Islamic Republic of Iran. He expressed the hope that Turkmenistan's cultural experts will use the existing historical and cultural works in Iran's libraries and educational centers to compile that country's history. Mr. Niyazov said in connection with the Ashgabat meeting: With the establishment of a climate of friendship and understanding between neighboring and regional countries, the growing trend of cooperation between Turkmenistan and the Islamic Republic of Iran will also accelerate. Mr. Hashemi-Rafsanjani, for his part, referred to the composition of Turkmenistan's high-ranking delegation and said that this delegation's trip provides a good opportunity for fulfilling the aims of widening relations between the two countries. He added: Because Iran and Turkmenistan are neighbors, both countries desire deep and friendly relations; and the visits paid by high-ranking officials of the two countries to Tehran and Ashgbat have been instrumental in serving this objective. The president pointed to the existence of land and sea borders between the two countries in the Khorasan, Gilan, and Mazendaran Provinces, adding: These provinces, which have suitable natural and geographical features, can play a fundamental role in the growth of trade and economic exchanges between the two countries. He stressed the role of the joint commission between the two countries in the development of relations and cooperation and noted the need for the creation of facilities in the area of transport and travel for the purposes of exchanges of views and experiences between commercial and economic experts of Iran and Turkmenistan. The talks were also attended by the two countries' ministers of foreign affairs, culture, commerce, post, telegraph and telephone, transport, and agriculture, and a number of other officials. It was decided at the talks that committees will be formed with the involvement of experts on political matters, transport and communications, economics, commerce, education, training, health, agriculture, fisheries, gas and oil, and culture and tourism. After discussion and debate, the committee experts are to convey the results to the presidents of Iran and Turkmenistan.
