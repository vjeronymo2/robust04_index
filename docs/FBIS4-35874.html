BFN [Text] 'Ala'eddin Borujerdi, our country's deputy foreign minister for Asian and Oceanic affairs who is leading a delegation to Kabul, today met with Afghan president Borhanoddin Rabbani. According to a Central News Unit report from Islamabad, at the meeting the latest Afghan domestic developments, bilateral ties, and important international and regional issues were discussed. In the course of the meeting our country's deputy foreign minister for Asian and Oceanic affair said that the events in Afghanistan are important to the Islamic Republic of Iran and called for a swifter establishment of peace and stability in that country. Mr. Borujerdi declared our country's readiness for cooperation with the Afghan government and considered the unrest in that country as the only obstacle to implementing the agreement between the two countries. At the meeting, Afghan President Borhanoddin Rabbani, praising the efforts by the Islamic Republic of Iran for establishing peace in Afghanistan, said that the fact that the Islamic Republic of Iran embassy is open as the only active diplomatic representation in Kabul under the very difficult conditions due to clashes demonstrates the good will and interest of the Islamic Republic of Iran in resolving Afghanistan's internal problems.
