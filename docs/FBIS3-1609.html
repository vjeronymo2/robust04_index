Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Qiu Qi: "Trade Minister Optimistic on Rejoining GATT] [Text] The 16th session of the Gatt Working Party on China, scheduled for April, is expected to help ease China's re-entry into the world trade body, according to the government's top trade official. Wu Yi, Minister of Foreign Trade and Economic Co-operation (Moftec), said China has virtually met the demands of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (Gatt). Speaking at a ministerial-level seminar in Beijing over the weekend, Wu urged different ministries to speed up preparations for rejoining Gatt. China should resume its Gatt contracting party status before the final package of the Uruguay Round of Gatt agreements -- which curb protectionism and expand world trade -- takes effect next year. By then, all Gatt members will become founding members of the World Trade Organization (WTO), the free-trade successor to Gatt. Being a founding member of WTO will benefit China's foreign trade, Wu said. The service trade, the investment field and intellectual property rights will be major concerns of WTO, Wu said. She added that issues concerning the service trade will be key items for discussion at the 16th meeting, to be held in Morocco. Compared with the US, 80 per cent of whose trade stems from the service sector, China's service industry is still in the initial stages. The service trade is included among the conditions for China's re-entry into Gatt. Others involve tariffs and non-tariff duties. China's re-entry into Gatt will help stabilize its overseas markets and help it tap new ones, Wu said. It will also help reduce trade discrimination and its regulations on multilateral trade will help put developing countries on a more equal footing with Western countries. Although rejoining Gatt will challenge some domestic industries, Wu said, the government will try to help them. Long Yongtu, a director general in Moftec, said that the meeting in Morocco will confirm the rules on reentering Gatt as well as on joining the WTO. A WTO administration commission will be set up at the meeting, Long said. He added that about 20 countries and regions are applying for entry into the world trade body. Besides the Chinese mainland, they include Russia, Bulgaria, Taiwan and Saudi Arabia. Nearly 40 ministers attended the weekend forum in Beijing. The Venezuelan trade minister described Venezuela's entry into Gatt and representatives from the United Nations explained the influence WTO will have on China. Gatt, together with the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank, are the three pillars of the world economic system. The volume of trade among its 115 members accounts for more than 90 per cent of the world's total. China, on the other hand, is a fast-rising economic force. Its 1993 trade totalled 195.7 billion yuan ($22.5 billion). In the past 15 years China has received $60 billion in foreign investment and has been one of the fastest growing economies in the world.
