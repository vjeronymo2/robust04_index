Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Text] Slovak National Party [SNS] Chairman Jan Slota told a TASR correspondent that his party had suggested to the Party of the Democratic Left [SDL] chairman that it would withdraw from the coalition with the HZDS [Movement for a Democratic Slovakia] if it was inevitable in order to form an HZDS-SDL coalition before early parliamentary elections. He said that the SNS was aware that the problem that has arisen was not a result of politicking but of the economic collapse of this young republic. SDL Chairman Peter Weiss reacted to Slota's offer to withdraw from the coalition with the following words: We are willing to negotiate with anyone, but today, after the president's speech, the situation is different from what it was yesterday. Asked whether the SDL was united, Weiss responded that four deputies had not signed the proposal to dismiss the prime minister because they insisted that the issue of early elections should be resolved first. He added that the current crisis in the country could not be resolved by passing a vote of no confidence in Vladimir Meciar without agreeing on a date for early elections.
