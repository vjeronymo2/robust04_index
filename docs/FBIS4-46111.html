BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Guatemalan guerrilla activities monitored through 19 May. Ranchers who for years have been paying "war taxes" to the guerrillas have turned down a proposal by Defense Minister Mario Enriquez to arm their workers to defend themselves against the rebels. Arturo Guirola Batres, president of the Coordinating Committee for Agricultural, Financial, Commercial, and Industrial Associations, said the farmers appreciate the proposal but feel they must reject it because it would mean creating an army in addition to the regular one. (Guatemala PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 13 May 94 p 2) The Guatemalan Army has confirmed that Israel Hernandez y Hernandez, president of the Voluntary Civil Defense Committee, was kidnapped by a guerrilla group in San Luis Municipality, Peten Department, according to the Information and Press Department of the Army, DIDE. Hernandez y Hernandez's son was also kidnapped. (Guatemala City LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 15 May 94 p 7) Three soldiers were killed over the weekend after stepping on mines placed by the guerrillas in a private farm in the northwestern department of Solola, the Army reported on 16 May. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1507 GMT 16 May 94) At least seven high-powered grenades were defused in different areas of the Guatemalan capital by the National Police, official sources have reported. Three of the grenades were found next to URNG propaganda. The other four grenades were found and defused in Zones 7 and 11 in Guatemala City. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0028 GMT 19 May 94)
