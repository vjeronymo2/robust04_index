December 6, 1989, Wednesday, Valley Edition

MOLOTOV COCKTAIL BURNS TEEN-AGER IN GANG ATTACK

A 17-year-old gang member was listed in fair condition Tuesday after his shirt was set ablaze by an exploding Molotov cocktail thrown from a passing car, Burbank police said.

The youth was talking on a pay phone at Buena Vista Street and Victory Boulevard about 9:30 p.m. Monday when a car with several rival gang members pulled up next to him, Sgt. Don Goldberg said.

An argument followed and a passenger leaned out and hurled a Molotov cocktail at the victim that exploded in flames, Goldberg said.

A friend of the victim who was inside a nearby convenience store smothered the flames with a jacket, Goldberg said.

The youth suffered first- and second-degree burns on his face, upper chest and back, said a spokeswoman for Sherman Oaks Community Hospital.

The youth admitted to police that he was a gang member, and that the men in the car were members of a rival gang, Goldberg said.

No arrests have been made, he said.
