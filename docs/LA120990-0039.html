December 9, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

ERMA BOMBECK: LET ROSEANNE BE NO BARR TO PEACE

I haven't heard too many solutions for the problems in the deserts of the Mideast. Oh, there was that bad joke about how President Bush was going to send 1,000 women with PMS because they were not only mean as snakes but they also could retain water. But other than that, no one has come forward with a plan. Until a few weeks ago.

An Italian politician and porn star, Illona Staller (a.k.a. La Cicciolina), has volunteered to sleep with Saddam Hussein if he agrees to free the foreigners he is holding in Iraq.

I feel like a fool. The only thing I could come up with was to cut down on my gas consumption by car-pooling.

The radical member of Parliament's offer is a dramatic one, but I have to ask myself: Where was Illona when we were dealing with the old Ayatollah Khomeini or the Zits Squad -- Gen. Manuel Noriega, Moammar Kadafi and Yasser Arafat? If this is to be a viable solution to our global problems, we can't pick and choose whom to sleep with.

Actually, I was surprised to see this offer come from Italy. It's usually the French who make love, not war. The English, on the other hand, would have countered with, "Let's sit down over a nice cup of hot tea." The Italians traditionally would have said, "Relax. I'll make you a nice dinner."

The Americans? We don't really make sacrifices until we are pushed into a corner. But we can do it. We can bite the bullet if we have to. There's an entire generation out there without any idea of what we did to win World War II on the home front. For four years, my grandmother saved her fat in large coffee cans. I don't know what she ever did with it, but someone told her to do it and she did. Nylon was being used for parachutes, so women used makeup on their legs and painted a seam up the back with a pencil. On a hot day, the makeup melted and your legs looked like someone turned up the heat in a wax museum.

We had ration stamps for sugar, coffee, shoes and gasoline. I don't recall anyone stepping forward and volunteering to sleep with Hitler or Tojo.

On Thanksgiving, President Bush unveiled his secret weapon for peace. He said he considered sending Roseanne Barr to Iraq to sing "The Star-Spangled Banner." Before you discard that idea, remember what happened to Noriega when he hid out in the Vatican embassy and our soldiers played rock music at 140 decibels?

Maybe our options come down to Illona or Roseanne.
