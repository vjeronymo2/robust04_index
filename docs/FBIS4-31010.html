BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 31/5/1994 -- Romanian President Ion Iliescu offered an official dinner on Monday, 30 May, evening in honor of Indian President Shankar Dayal Sharma, who is paying a state visit to Romania. In his toast, the Romanian president said that the talks he had the pleasure to have with the Indian head of state had permitted an examination of the basic aspects of the bilateral relations and especially the recognition of the main directions and ways of developing and deepening the Romanian-Indian collaboration in all fields of mutual interest. President Iliescu expressed his confidence that the concluded agreements, synthetically expressed by the Declaration on Principles and Directions of Cooperation and by other documents that were going to be signed, would open a new chapter of the bilateral relations. The Romanian president said that Romania was attaching a particular importance to the achievement of substantial progresses in the field of the commercial exchanges and economic cooperation. The Romanian Government expresses its readiness to establish the inter-governmental juridical frame able to encourage actions of mutual interest between Romanian and Indian companies, said President Iliescu. The Romanian president expressed his hope that the meetings between the businessmen who were accompanying the Indian president and Romanian businessmen would lead to results encouraging for both parties. The exchange of views which we had, said President Iliescu, evinced the common preoccupations of the governments of Romania and India to promote understanding, collaboration, and peace in the world, and the common attachment to the universal values of the democracy. In the field of external relations, Romania is engaged in the process of integration within the economic, political, and strategic European structures, and it develops cooperation relations with the countries with democratic traditions and with all its neighbors, said President Iliescu. At the same time, within the strategy of its foreign policy, Romania grants special importance to the development of the traditional relations with the countries in the Asian-Pacific zone, one of the regions with the highest economic growth and healthy prospects for continued development in the coming decades, India holding a front-line place in that respect, said President Iliescu. In his toast, Indian President Shankar Dayal Sharma said that in the latest years there have been active contacts at various levels between Romania and India. While the mutual visits by parliamentary delegations revealed the mutually shared political beliefs, the agreements signed in the fields of trade, science, and technology are an expression of the common decision to substantiate the bilateral relations, said the Indian president. The economic restructuring taking place in India and the reform program launched in Romania create considerable possibilities for cooperation to mutual benefit, said the Indian head of state. The prominent businessmen -- members of the Indian delegation accompanying the Indian president -- are expressing India's decision to diversify, increase, and stimulate economic cooperation with Romania, said the Indian president. President Shankar Dayal Sharma expressed confidence that Romanian-Indian relations would gain substance and diversify. The Indian Government wishes to cooperate with the Romanian Government in order to carry out some common objectives, said the president. The last decade of the century is offering chances, but also responsibilities. The magnitude of the problems confronting mankind shows that there is no country that can afford to live in isolation. Peace and prosperity are really indivisible in this world, in which we all live. India, as well as Romania have committed themselves in promoting the cause of peace and in building a fair, safe, and prosperous international order. I am convinced that our concerted actions will bring a significant contribution to this cause, to the mutual benefit of our countries, to the benefit of the entire humanity, said the Indian president.
