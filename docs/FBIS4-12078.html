BFN [Text] The efforts to solve Yemen's political crisis have suffered a serious setback. Oman has announced that the talks that Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id sponsored between Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and his vice president 'Ali Salim al-Bid have failed to end their differences. As a result, Oman threatened to withdraw from the multinational disengagement committee that is supposed to oversee the disengagement of the opposing Yemeni military units. The failure of the talks in Salalah raised immediate fears in Sanaa that a Yemeni civil war might erupt. Both President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id, who saw him off, looked somber when he departed Salalah Airport at noon today. Sources of the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP], led by Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid, said that when President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih left his meeting with al-Bid today, he threatened to use force against the leaders of south Yemen, whom he accused of dragging the country toward partition. YSP sources denied this accusation and declared that Sanaa's policy of oppression and its attempt to exercise hegemony over the south, as the sources put it, is the real threat to the country's unity. Omani Minister of State for Foreign Affairs Yusuf Bin-'Alawi Bin- 'Abdallah stated that President Salih and Vice President al-Bid failed to solve their differences during a second meeting in Salalah today. He explained that they could not agree on the wording of a statement calling for calm in the country, particularly where military matters are concerned. The Omani minister announced that his country will consult with Jordan over its wish to end its participation in the joint military committee charged with the task of disengaging hostile Yemeni forces unless there is political progress among Yemen's major political parties. Following the meeting in Salalah, al-Bid returned to Aden. President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih made a brief visit to Qatar, during which he met with the amir, Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani, and senior Qatari officials. Meanwhile, about 40 Yemeni deputies began a sit-in at the House of Representatives in Sanaa' to protest the country's 7-month political crisis and the failure of the Oman-sponsored talks.
