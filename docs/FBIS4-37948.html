BFN [News conference by Salim Salih Muhammad, member of the Council of Ministers in South Yemen, with unidentified correspondents after Muhammad's meeting with Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri in Beirut on 24 June--recorded] [Text] [Muhammad] I discussed with his excellency the prime minister the latest developments in the current fighting in Yemen, and the importance of a serious Arab effort to stop the current fighting by recognizing the existing situation in Yemen and the existence of two regimes and states. We have been unable to establish a true merger during four years of unity. We asked Lebanon--government, president, and people--to support the justness of our cause. We are defending our right to live and the right of our children to stop this destructive war, which has entered its eighth week. All types of weapons are being used in this war. Sanaa is sending thousands of people into this battle to occupy Aden and the south. It is trying to impose its will by force and is disregarding domestic and foreign will through its military action and option. [Correspondent] Have you made any specific requests to the prime minister? [Muhammad] We are asking our Arab brothers and Lebanon for their assistance, particularly political, to end the current fighting and to recognize Democratic Yemen. [Correspondent] Is there any hope for a cease-fire soon? [Muhammad] We hope so. We pin our hopes on Arab and international efforts, particularly on what will take place at the UN Security Council in the next two days. We hope the results will satisfy the parties and end the bloodshed and destructive war. [Correspondent] Will you tour other Arab states? [Muhammad] There are other delegations undertaking the same mission both on Arab and international levels. We will also make a tour of some Arab areas in the future.
