BFN [Text] Eight control units from naval radiation-monitoring instruments containing the highly active radionuclides strontium-90 and three 90 [tri devyanosto] and plutonium-239 were stolen a few days ago from a laboratory in the maritime fishing-port of Nakhodka, in the Far East. INTERFAX learned this today from Russia's State Supervision Agency for Atomic Power Capabilities, quoting the department's Far Eastern district. The administration of the maritime fishing-port has informed Nakhodka's investigatory bodies of the occurrence. An investigation is underway.
