BFN [Text] Berlin (DDP/ADN) -- As expected, none of the five candidates for the office of federal president has achieved the necessary absolute majority of 663 votes in the first Federal Assembly ballot. Of the l,317 valid votes cast, 604 went to the CDU/CSU [Christian Democratic Union/Christian Social Union] candidate Roman Herzog. The SPD [Social Democratic Party] candidate and North Rhine-Westphalian Minister-President Johannes Rau received 505 votes. The FDP [Free Democratic Party] candidate Hildegard Hamm-Bruecher received l32 votes. The east German civil rights campaigner Jens Reich received 62 votes, and the candidate of the right-wing Republicans, Hans Hirzel, received 12 votes. There were two abstentions, and two votes were invalid. Thus a second ballot is required where once again an absolute majority for the election of the new federal president is needed. A simple majority is sufficient in a third ballot.
