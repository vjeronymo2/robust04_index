Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd arrived at Beirut airport at 1645 today. He was received by Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz, who had just returned from Damascus, and British Ambassador May Fay Geraldine Forte and senior embassy staff. The delegation accompanying the British secretary includes Senior Adviser Robert John Sowers, Charles Richard Stag, and Robert Nicola [names as heard]. Secretary Hurd will hold talks with his Lebanese counterpart at the Foreign Ministry. He will later head to B'abda Palace to meet with President Ilyas al-Hirawi. Chamber of Deputies Speaker Nabih Birri and Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri will attend the meeting, in addition to Minister Buwayz. Hurd said that there is no cause for alarm over the possible collapse of the peace process. He believes that no one wants to go back to the state of anarchy and lack of accord. The British secretary made this statement before he left London on a tour that will take him to Israel and Jordan, as well as Lebanon.
