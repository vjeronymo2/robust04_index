940122 FT 22 JAN 94 / Economic diary - Forward events TOMORROW: New Venezuelan Congress begins sitting. MONDAY: Confederation of British Industry publishes quarterly industrial trends survey (January). US federal budget (December). Council of Europe parliamentary assembly holds winter session in Strasbourg (until January 28). Arab-Israeli peace talks are expected to resume in Washington. US-Japanese trade negotiators scheduled to meet in Washington to discuss opening up the Japanese market to exports. EU agriculture council meets in Brussels. TUESDAY: New construction orders (November). US existing home sales (December); consumer confidence (January). Mr Bill Clinton, US president, scheduled to give annual State of the Union address to Congress. Portugal's two trade union confederations stage one-day strike over pay. National conference in Algeria on its political future (until January 26). Mr Philippe Seguin, speaker of the French Assembly, is expected to address the Chatham House international affairs institute on the subject of 'The New Europe' in London. French Communist Party congress (until January 29). WEDNESDAY: Bricks and cement production and deliveries (fourth quarter). Mortgage possession orders (fourth quarter). Mortgage repossessions (July-December). Irish budget to be introduced to parliament. Annual donors' consultative conference of Southern African Development Community in Gaberone (until January 28). THURSDAY: World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland (until January 31). Social Trends - 1994 edition. Engineering sales and orders at current and constant prices (November). New vehicle registrations (December). Energy trends (November). US durable goods (December). General strike in Spain in protest at government's economic policy. FRIDAY: Balance of trade with countries outside the European Union (December). US GDP (fourth quarter). Japan consumers' price index (December); industrial output (December). Zambian budget.
