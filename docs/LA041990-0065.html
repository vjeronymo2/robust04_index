April 19, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: CABBAGE SALAD RECIPE CHANGED TO REDUCE FAT AND CALORIES

DEAR JEANNE: This salad is one I have enjoyed many times in the past; however, I am now on a low-fat diet. As you can see by the ingredients, one cup of salad oil does not meet low-fat standards and the amount of sugar seems high. Do you think you could modify this recipe and still leave it tasting about the same?

-- ELIZABETH, Orangevale, Calif.

CABBAGE SALAD

1 head green cabbage, thinly sliced

1 bunch green onions, chopped

3 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds

1/2 cup toasted slivered almonds

1 package instant Soba noodles, cooked and drained

5 tablespoons sugar

1 cup salad oil

2 teaspoons MSG

1 teaspoon black pepper

7 tablespoons white vinegar

Combine cabbage, onions, sesame seeds, almonds and noodles in large bowl. Set aside.

Combine sugar, oil, MSG, pepper and vinegar. Pour over salad and mix thoroughly. Refrigerate, stirring every couple hours to marinate. Makes about 12 cups.

Note: Salad is best made 1 day ahead.

DEAR ELIZABETH: I had to go to several markets to find the noodles, so if you don't have an Oriental market or a nearby large market with a well-stocked ethnic food section, you might want to experiment with some other kind of noodle such as linguine broken in short pieces and slightly undercooked, then well drained.

The changes I have made substantially reduced the calories from fat, yet still retained the flavor range that makes this salad so delicious. Dark sesame oil instead of sesame seeds and apple-juice concentrate instead of sugar both worked beautifully. I deleted the MSG since it is not necessary and I prefer to avoid MSG in all recipes.

-- JEANNE

HEALTHY CABBAGE SALAD

1/2 cup chopped raw almonds

1 (8-ounce) package instant Soba noodles

1 head green cabbage, thinly sliced

8 green onions, chopped

1/2 cup frozen apple juice concentrate, thawed

1/2 cup rice vinegar

3 tablespoons dark sesame oil

1/2 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

Toast almonds at 350 degrees 8 to 10 minutes until golden brown. Watch carefully since nuts burn easily. Set aside.

Bring 6 cups water to boil, add noodles and boil 2 minutes. Drain thoroughly. Combine cooked noodles, cabbage and onions in large bowl. Set aside.

Combine apple juice concentrate, vinegar, oil, salt and pepper, mixing well. Pour dressing over salad and mix thoroughly. Refrigerate several hours, stirring occasionally so all ingredients are thoroughly marinated.

To serve, place 1 1/2 cups salad on large plate and top each serving with 1 tablespoon toasted almonds. Makes about 12 cups, or 8 (1 1/2-cup) servings.

Note: Salad is best made 1 day ahead.

Each serving contains approximately:
