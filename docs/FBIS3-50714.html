Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Marcela Gutierrez for the "Vector 21" newscast] [Text] The armed struggle is not the way to resolve the problems Mexico is facing, Cuban Ambassador to Mexico Jose Fernandez de Cossio Rodriguez has said. He added that the situtation in Chiapas does not justify an armed uprising. Let us listen to the statements of Fernandez de Cossio: [Begin Fernandez de Cossio recording] The armed struggle, as many other ways of settling problems in some countries, is not a universal recipe. In the places in which armed struggles have been waged, they have been caused by a series of factors, which do not appear to be present in the case of Mexico. [end recording] In an interview here at the Foreign Relations Secretariat, the Cuban diplomat rejected the possibility that the weapons used by rebels may come from that Caribbean island. He voiced his country's solidarity with the decisions of the Mexican Government to seek a peaceful solution to the armed clashes in southern Mexico.
