Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Feb 15 (CTK) -- The government coalition parties would obtain a total of 52.5 percent of the votes, a poll conducted by the Center of Empirical Research (STEM) in early February revealed. The turn-out would be 75 percent, the poll showed. The Civic Democratic Party (ODS, Premier Vaclav Klaus) would get 31.3 percent of the votes, the Civic Democratic Alliance (ODA) 14.3 percent, the Christian Democratic Union-Czechoslovak People's Party (KDU-CSL) 5.4 percent, and the Christian Democratic Party (KDS) 1.5 percent. The opposition would fare as follows: The Czech Social Democratic Party (CSSD) 13.9 percent, the Left Bloc (primarily Communists) 10.9, the Assembly for the Republic-Czechoslovak Republican Party (SPR-RSC, leader Miroslav Sladek) 3.7 percent.
