Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The duty officer of the St. Petersburg department of the Federal Counterintelligence Service (FCS) told INTERFAX "with entire responsibility" that the reports on revealing a criminal group preparing an attempt upon the life of St. Petersburg Mayor Anatoliy Sobchak "had no grounds." "Nothing like such a group has been revealed, and no one has been arrested," the officer said. "Sobchak is under no threat. By the way, he is out of the city at the moment." "I regret that some of your colleagues work with so little responsibility," the counterintelligence officer told INTERFAX.
