930105 FT 05 JAN 93 / Boost for CIS ties LEADERS of five former Soviet Asian republics, meeting in Tashkent, yesterday gave backing to the fragile Commonwealth of Independent States, to which they belong, saying they wanted to move towards a common market, Reuter reports from Moscow. Interfax news agency quoted Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev as saying the five countries - Kazakhstan and the Central Asian states of Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan - were taking steps to create a common market. But the five should continue to work with other former Soviet states.
