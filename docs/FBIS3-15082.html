Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow March 2 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Uzbekistan's Islam Karimov have signed "the declaration about the development and deepening of comprehensive cooperation between Russia and Uzbekistan". The declaration signed in the Kremlin today says that the Russian Federation and the Republic of Uzbekistan confirm the determination to consistently develop and deepen comprehensive cooperation in the interests of the peoples of both states and pursue the policy of economic reforms and democratic transformations". [no opening quotation marks as received] The two states pledge in the declaration to guarantee the rights of national minorities residing in Russia and Uzbekistan. Russia and Uzbekistan seek to "strengthen coordination of their foreign political activity in consolidating peace and security, while focusing on interaction in settling regional conflicts and other disputes which infringe the interests of both states". Russia and Uzbekistan intend to set up a mechanism of consultations in the interests of ensuring the safety of the external borders of the Commonwealth member states". Yeltsin and Karimov also signed an agreement on military cooperation between the Russian Federation and the Republic of Uzbekistan to confirm the significance of the collective security agreement signed in Tashkent on May 15, 1992. The two countries noted that they would continue "efforts to deepen and cooperation in the military field and develop the adequate legal framework for the purpose".
