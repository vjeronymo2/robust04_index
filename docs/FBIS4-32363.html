BFN [Text] Budapest, 22 June (MTI) -- The speakers at the Wednesday [22 June] opening of "Handshakes," a Hungarian-Romanian friendly, unofficial meeting in Gyula (southeast Hungary), all stressed the importance of the initiative made for the meeting by an 18-year-old student in the city -- Imre Susanszki. The speakers included Mayor of Gyula Gabor Pocsay, Mayor of Arad Cristian Moisescu, Hungarian Foreign Affairs Spokesman Janos Herman, and Romanian Foreign Affairs State Secretary Adrian Dohotaru. The importance of the meeting was stressed in letters addressed to the participants by Hungarian Foreign Minister Geza Jeszenszky and Romanian Ambassador of the European Union Karen Fogg, who was the Chief Patron of the meeting. The General Secretary of the Council of Europe, Catherine Lalumiere, sent her personal envoy to the four-day meeting, which has nearly 800 Hungarian and Romanian participants.
