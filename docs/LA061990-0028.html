June 19, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: MAN KILLED BY CAR HE ATTEMPTED TO SLOW DOWN

A man was struck and killed Monday when he left a party at a Chatsworth house and walked into the street to try to slow down a car that was repeatedly speeding by the home, Los Angeles police said.

The 19-year-old Chatsworth man, whose name was not released pending notification of his family, was killed about 3:30 a.m. when he was struck by the car, which then sped away, police said. Officers said they were investigating whether the incident was motivated by a dispute at the party that resulted in one party-goer's ejection.

"We don't know if this is a murder or a vehicular manslaughter," Detective Don Bellante said.
