BFN [Unattributed article: "Italy Improves Record on Adopting European Union Laws"] [Text] (ANSA) Luxembourg, June 6 -- Italy has greatly improved its record of adopting European Union [EU] laws and regulations and, with an adoption rate of 89.9 percent, now ranks fourth among the Member States for turning European Union into national law, according to the executive European Commissioner for the Single Market, Italy's Raniero Vanni d'Archirafi. Denmark ranked first for adopting European Union law for having made its own 96 percent of European legislature, followed by Portugal with a 91 percent rate, the Netherlands at 90 percent and then Italy. Observers pointed out how Denmark and Portugal, with sixth-ranked Britain, were considered the greatest "Euro-skeptics," while Germany, viewed as the locomotive for European integration was third to last in adopting European Union laws, at a rate of 83 percent, while France stood at 87 percent, fourth-to-last. In an address to ministers responsible for European affairs, for Italy Domenico Comino, Vanni d'Archirafi announced how the European Commission had decided to "go on the warpath" to speed up the transformation of European Union law into national law. The EU executive, he said, was considering adopting, from now on, penalties and sanctions for the delays, as provided for in the Maastricht Treaty on European Union.
