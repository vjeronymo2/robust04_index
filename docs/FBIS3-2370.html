Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 9 (XINHUA) -- A spokesman of the coming session of China's National People's Congress (NPC) expressed the hope today that the upcoming visit to China by U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher will be conducive to the promotion of Sino-U.S. relations. Zhou Jue, spokesman for the NPC session, made the remark at a press conference this morning, in response to a question on the timing of Christopher's visit. China is willing to expand friendly relations with all countries in the world, including the United States, Zhou said. Reiterating China's policy towards the United States, he said that China hopes to "increase mutual trust, reduce troubles, expand cooperation and avoid confrontation." Calling China and the U.S. two influential countries, said that the development of Sino-U.S. relations is not only in the interests of the two peoples, but also conducive to peace in the Asia-Pacific region and the world, as well.
