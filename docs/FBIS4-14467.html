BFN [Text] Today President Algirdas Brazauskas received Darryl Johnson, U.S. ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary in Lithuania. Brazauskas described to the ambassador the results of a meeting of the three Baltic presidents in Palanga and the forthcoming visit at the end of April by Polish President Lech Walesa. Ambassador Justas Paleckis, presidential adviser on foreign policy, told ELTA that views were exchanged on the relations of both states with Russia, Ukraine, and other CIS countries. The U.S. ambassador thanked Brazauskas for the information and said that Washington was observing with attention and approval the strengthening cooperation of the Baltic states. Johnson stressed that the U.S. leadership welcomed the initialled Lithuanian-Polish political treaty. U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher has sent a letter to the Lithuanian foreign minister approving the initialling of that treaty.
