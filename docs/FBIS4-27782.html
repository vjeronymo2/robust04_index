BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 6 June Morning Edition] [Text] On the evening of 5 June, Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata commented on the trilateral discussions among Japan, the United States, and the ROK on the DPRK's (North Korea) suspected development of nuclear weapons. He said: "Japan needs to watch future developments calmly," indicating he intends to maintain a wait-and-see attitude. He also said: "North Korea's statements are quite elusive [bimyo]. It seems to want to continue dialogue with the United States," giving his opinion that North Korea is still searching for a solution through talks with the United States. The prime minister gave the above remarks in response to questions from reporters at his official residence.
