921219 FT 19 DEC 92 / International Company News: Hagen asked to resign seat on Nedlloyd board NEDLLOYD, the Dutch shipping group, said Norwegian investor Mr Torstein Hagen had been asked by fellow board members to resign his seat on the company's supervisory board, Reuter reports from Rotterdam. Nedlloyd said Mr Hagen had been asked to resign because his recent actions violated the terms on which he had been accepted as a board member. Last Tuesday, Mr Hagen, who owns about 27 per cent of Nedlloyd's shares, called for an extraordinary shareholders' meeting to agree to fundamental changes to Nedlloyd's structure.
