May 15, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

GROUP TO BACK POSSIBLE PAN AM BID FOR NWA

Prudential Bache and the Airlie Group, a fund whose investors include the wealthy Bass brothers of Ft. Worth, Tex., said Sunday that they would contribute $400 million in equity to a possible combined bid with Pan Am Corp. for the parent of Northwest Airlines.

The amount the group expects to borrow if it bids for NWA Inc., Northwest's parent, wasn't disclosed, although it would almost certainly exceed $2 billion.

Pamela Hanlon, a Pan Am spokeswoman, said Pan Am believes that a $400-million equity contribution is more than adequate, and that it is "substantially" greater than the $300-million ownership stake proposed by a competing NWA bidder, Los Angeles billionaire Marvin Davis.

Investment analysts have said that Pan Am, heavily burdened with around $1 billion in debt, would have to raise between $300 million and $500 million in equity to avoid taking on too much additional debt to buy NWA.

Sunday's announcement signaled that billionaires Sid and Lee Bass now support a possible bid for NWA. A source close to the brothers said they were upset last Tuesday when Pan Am said it was exploring an NWA bid with the Airlie Group without first informing the Basses.

Morton H. Meyerson, co-manager of the Airlie Group, said he met Friday with Sid Bass, who said a possible bid for NWA "looked like a good deal." Meyerson said the Basses dismissed as "rumor" talk that they had been initially upset.

Meyerson said Airlie is committed to providing a maximum of $200 million in equity to a possible bid for NWA. The rest of the equity would come from Prudential Bache, the merchant banking arm of Prudential Life Insurance Co.

5-Year-Old Fund

The Airlie Group is a 5-year-old fund that had primarily concentrated in high-yield, high-risk "junk bonds." In January, the Airlie Group changed its focus to concentrate on leveraged acquisitions, Meyerson said.

Meyerson said Airlie's limited partners are the Bass group, a second group of investors led by Ft. Worth financier Richard Rainwater, and a third group whose members are Airlie employees. Each group has an equal, one-third stake in Airlie.

In addition, the Equitable Life Assurance Society of the United States is a special limited partner, suppling about 95% of Airlie's financing, he said. By the end of the year, Meyerson said, Airlie expects to have $1.6 billion available for investments.

Meyerson acknowledged that Los Angeles financier Alfred Checchi, who is weighing a competing bid for NWA, is a member of the Bass group of Airlie investors. But Meyerson described Checchi's investment as small and said his participation in the fund had no bearing on Airlie's decision to possibly bid for the airline.
