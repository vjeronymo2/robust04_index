Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 5 (XINHUA) -- Two municipalities and eight provincial capital cities in China have an average of over 11 telephones for every 100 people. Statistics show that cities with an average exceeding 11 for every 100 residents are: Haikou, capital of Hainan Province, with 24.54; Beijing with 20; Guangzhou, capital of Guangdong Province, with 16.23; Shanghai with 15.80; Kunming, capital of Yunnan Province, with 12.81; Fuzhou, capital of Fujian Province, with 12.7; Lanzhou, capital of Gansu Province, with 12.11; Hohhot, capital of the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region, with 11.64; Nanjing, capital of Jiangsu Province, with 11.07; and Shijiazhuang, capital of Hebei Province, with 11.01. Last year, these cities witnessed a rapid development of telecommunications, with a record total installation of telephones. For example, a total of 156,000 telephones were installed in Beijing last year, close to the total in the period 1990-1992. Now the urban areas of Beijing have over 576,000 telephones.
