BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 13 KYODO -- Japan's and South Korea's foreign ministers reconfirmed Monday that they will maintain close contact in dealing with the North Korean nuclear crisis, Japanese Foreign Ministry officials said. Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa and his South Korean counterpart Han Sung-chu, who met Saturday in Seoul, had a 10-minute telephone conversation Monday, the officials said. Kakizawa called Han to brief the South Korean minister on Kakizawa's visit to China on Sunday, they said. He told Han that he was able to share concern and acknowledgement with Chinese Foreign Minister Qian Qichen that the situation concerning North Korea's alleged attempts to develop nuclear weapons is very grave and difficult, the officials said. Kakizawa and Qian also agreed on the importance of cooperation among South Korea, Japan and China, and permanent and nonpermanent members of the U.N. Security Council, the officials quoted Kakizawa as saying. Han told Kakizawa he hopes Kakizawa's visit to China would help China play a constructive role in solving the problem, they said.
