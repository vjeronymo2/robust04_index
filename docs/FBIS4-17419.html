BFN [Text] Riga, 22 April (LETA) -- Thursday [21 April] in the Latvian Foreign Ministry a news conference was held by the leader of a GATT working group, Finn Teilgard, who visits Riga to discuss the conditions of Latvia entering this organization with the Latvian Administration. In the course of his visit he met the Latvian prime minister, Valdis Birkavs, the Saeima's [parliament] chairman, Anatolijs Gorbunovs, and some Latvian ministers and experts. Mr. Teilgard said that he told the Latvian Government about the six basic principles of this organization which is mainly purposed for the promotion of international cooperation and the development of trade among its 124 member country. The first condition providing the participation in GATT is that Latvia has to work out the memorandum on the state political and economic situations plus the documents reflecting the current external trade procedure in the country and send them to the GATT headquarters. The leadership of GATT shows its understanding of the present economic situation in Latvia, and will take it into account when the issue of Latvia's participation in GATT is under discussion, Mr. Teilgard said.
