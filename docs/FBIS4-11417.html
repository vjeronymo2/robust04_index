BFN [Text] Today, on the third day of his visit to Cuba, Zambian President Frederick Chiluba continues a series of visits aimed at assessing the prospects for bilateral cooperation in the economic, and other, spheres. Chiluba and the delegation accompanying him will today tour an agricultural cooperative and the Hermanos Ameijeiras Clinical-Surgical Hospital. Chiluba's tight agenda is evidence of his government's interest in exploring the possibilities for expanding bilateral exchanges in spheres such as pharmaceuticals, agriculture, public health, and sports. Cuban Foreign Ministry sources say that there are plans for Cuban technicians to build a sugar mill in Zambia, with Lusaka financing the operations. During its stay in Cuba, the Zambian delegation will also meet with Zambian youths studying in Santiago de Cuba, and will hold a meeting with African diplomats accredited here. Yesterday, Chiluba had official talks with President Fidel Castro and visited science centers in Havana.
