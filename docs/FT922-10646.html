920430 FT 30 APR 92 / UK Company News: Sotheby's incurs Dollars 5m loss SOTHEBY'S Holdings, the auction house which is controlled by the Detroit-based Taubman family but still quoted in London and New York, made a Dollars 5.2m (Pounds 2.93m) loss in the first quarter of 1992. Auction sales in the first three months of 1992 were Dollars 141.4m, slightly higher than the Dollars 123.4m seen in the previous year. The company claimed that the increase was 'consistent with an improving art market", and that pre-tax earnings were up by over 35 per cent. Notable sales in the first quarter, included the jewellery auction in St Moritz which raised Dollars 16.1m, and the sale of Madame Schlumberger's collection in Monaco, which brought in Dollars 5.2m. The auction house added that February sales of Impressionist, Modern and Contemporary Art were 'more positive' than last year, being 83 per cent sold.
