Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 14, BNS -- Around 52.2 percent of Latvian families find it difficult to make both ends meet at the end of month, according to a public opinion poll, conducted by the State Statistics Committee. The poll showed that 13.9 percent of families live on earlier savings, 14.9 percent have enough money but 0.7 percent are amply provided. In total, 81 percent of families face serious money hardships. Sixty-eight percent of respondents reported unable to save in the present conditions. Almost two-thirds of the interviewed said they could not make valuable purchases and would not be able to do it in the next twelve months, either. The economic standing of families became worse between 1992 and 1993, say the majority of respondents. Only 0.6 percent of families said they enjoyed an improvement in their material situation last year. In addition, the families criticize the changes in the Latvian economic situation in 1993. Sixty-one percent of families say the economic situation became worse during the past year while 7.5 percent say the economic situation improved. The poll interviewed 1,298 families. From those, 56 percent were urban dwellers and 46 percent lived in the countryside. The poll was aimed at studying the public opinion on the latest material welfare and economic situation changes in families.
