BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 31 May. On 23 May, the Foreign Trade Institute reported that Colombian imports during the first four months of 1994 totaled $5.278 million. This represents a 22.7 percent increase over the same period last year. Imports of raw materials amounted to $2.1746 million -- 41.2 percent of all imports during the same period. The United States continues to be Colombia's main supplier. Venezuela is second. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 24 May 94 p 1B, 2B) Colombian exports to Venezuela dropped 6.5 percent during the first two months of 1994 because of a strong devaluation of the bolivar. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 24 May 94)
