CSO [Text] Sendai City, which is proceeding with a conversion program from city gas to LNG (liquefied natural gas), announced on 27 May that it has concluded sales negotiations on LNG with Malaysia LNG Company. The formal signing will be Kuala Lumpur in the middle of July after they have received approval at a special municipal assembly session at the end of June. With prospects good for securing the raw material, the city will start on construction of a receiving base in Sendai harbor, aiming at introduction of LNG in 1997. The details of the "confirmation of intent" that will be the premise of the sales contract agreed between Sendai city and Malaysia LNG are 1) the contract period will be for a 20-year period beginning in 1997, and the total import amount will be 2.9 million tons; 2) Malaysia LNG will build a special tanker and Sendai city will assume the obligation for that; and 3) the place for transfer of the LNG will be the base in Sendai harbor. The formal sales contract that will determine the price, etc., will be concluded within the next one to two years. The Sendai city LNG introduction program is the conversion of city gas, which supplies 356,000 households, from butane to LNG; total project costs such as for construction of the receiving base will be 85.8 billion yen. It initially had studied a method of receiving domestically produced LNG from Niigata by pipeline, but ultimately chose the tanker from overseas method and proceeded in negotiations with Malaysia LNG as the importer.
