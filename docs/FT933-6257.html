930828 FT 28 AUG 93 / Finance and the Family: A guide to gilts jargon Coupon. Sometimes used to describe the interest rate on a gilt. Gross redemption yield. The annualised percentage return, before tax, which reflects both interest and capital gain (or loss) if held until maturity. Maturity. The date on which the gilt will be repaid. Par value. In pricing terms, gilts are deemed to have a par value of 100. Anyone buying a gilt priced above par value (ie over 100), will make a capital loss if they hold it until maturity. Anyone buying below 100 will make a gain. Running yield. The income return from a gilt, ignoring any capital gain or loss.
