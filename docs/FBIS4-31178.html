BFN [Text] Zhan Videnov has been reelected chairman of the Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP] Supreme Council. According to the Election Commission, 688 delegates voted. Some 640 supported Videnov. The delegates in the congress hall greeted this report with applause. Some 47 delegates voted against Videnov and one abstained. The results of the All-Party Control Commission election are expected in about 30 minutes. Four prominent socialist deputies of the Alliance for Social Democracy failed to gain the minimum required votes -- 50 percent plus one vote -- which is a condition for entering the BSP Supreme Council. Dimitur Yonchev, Velislava Dureva, Rosen Karadimov, and Elena Poptomova were not elected by the delegates who voted for the supreme party organ membership. Andrey Lukanov also failed in the election for membership in the BSP Supreme Council.
