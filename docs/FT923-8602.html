920811 FT 11 AUG 92 / Hassan paves way for elections King Hassan asked his government to resign yesterday and make way for a new administration which will supervise Morocco's first general election in eight years, a senior government source said, Reuter reports from Rabat. A new prime minister is expected to be named within 24 hours. The election is to begin in the autumn. The centrist government of Prime Minister Azeddine Laraki, an independent, was formed seven years ago with a majority of independents and about a dozen ministers representing four political parties.
