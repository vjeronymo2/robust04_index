931008 FT 08 OCT 93 / Charges dropped against West Midlands officers THREE West Midlands police officers left the Old Bailey yesterday after charges of perjury and conspiracy to pervert the course of justice in connection with the prosecution of the Birmingham Six were dropped. Mr Justice Garland ruled that a fair trial was no longer possible for the three - Detective Constable Terence Woodwiss (left), retired Detective Superintendent George Reade (centre) and Detective Sergeant Colin Morris. The judge had heard lengthy legal arguments from their barristers that the men could never receive a fair trial because of 'saturation' publicity since the Birmingham Six were freed by the Appeal Court in March 1991. The detectives had all denied the charges when they appeared in court on Monday last week. In a statement yesterday four of the Birmingham Six said: 'Today's decision saddens and does not surprise us.'
