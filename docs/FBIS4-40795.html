BFN [Text] Moscow June 8 (CTK) -- The Tajik Government has decided to request visas from citizens of Lithuania, Latvia, Estonia and the Czech Republic as of June 15, the news agency ITAR-TASS announced today from Dushanbe. The Czech Embassy in Moscow told CTK today that the Tajik measure had not been officially announced to it. Since the split of Czechoslovakia on December 31, 1992, the visa for Czechs have been introduced only by Uzbekistan, to which Prague reacted with a reciprocal step. On January 15, the Czech Republic introduced unilaterally visa regime in relation to Armenia, Azerbaijan, Georgia and Tajikistan.
