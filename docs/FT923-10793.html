920729 FT 29 JUL 92 / German businessmen think centre-right government too weak Many leading German businessmen, politicians and civil servants think Chancellor Helmut Kohl's centre-right government is too weak, according to an opinion poll published by the business monthly Capital yesterday, Reuter reports from Bonn. The respondents felt Mr Kohl had fallen short on pledges to cut government subsidies, fight crime and develop a new energy policy. About one-third felt Mr Wolfgang Schauble, leader of Mr Kohl's Christian Democrats in parliament, would make a better chancellor.
