BFN [(Text] An advisor of the Russian prime-minister Mikhail Tarasov has told INTERFAX that this year Viktor Chernomyrdin is to intensively travel abroad. Still visits to Russian regions and republics to see the situation and problems remain his priority. Tarasov said that in mid-May the head of the cabinet is expected to make a one-day visit to Warsaw to resolve old problems blocking bilateral economic cooperation, in particular mutual settlements and the foreign debt of the former Soviet Union. A visit to China is planned for the end of May. There Chernomyrdin should sign an impressive package of agreements, including documents on the demarcation of the border, shipping on the Amur river and in international waters, cooperation in agriculture and related industries as well as in environmental protection. In June the prime-minister is expected to take part in the third session of the Gore-Chernomyrdin commission in the USA. In the framework of the commission six mixed committees have planned joint energy, space and nuclear power engineering projects. In the second half of July, according to Tarasov, the Russian head of government is expected to tour Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates and Kuwait. There attention will be centered around military-technical cooperation, the attraction of foreign investments and cooperation in fuel and power engineering. The Russian Prime-Minister has been invited to visit the Czech republic and Slovakia as well as Bulgaria and Rumania. At the end of the year he is expected to go to London. During the next few months in Moscow Chernomyrdin is to hold talks with his counterparts from Austria, Vietnam, Mongolia, Denmark and other foreign countries.
