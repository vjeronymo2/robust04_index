CSO [Text] Tehran, 13 Ordibehesht [3 May] -- The capacity of Shahid-e Hashemi-Nezhad Refinery will increase to 35 million cubic meters [m[.sup]3[/]] per day. Engineer Hoseyn Bashiri, chief of the Shahid-e Hashemi-Nezhad Gas Refinery, announced the above at a press conference. He said: During the Second Five-Year Plan two new units will be added to the refinery's three existing units and the refinery's nominal capacity will increase from 21 million m[.sup]3[/] per day to 35 million m[.sup]3[/]. Engineer Bashiri said: The gas from this refinery supplies the three great provinces of Khorasan, Semnan, and Mazandaran and it provides fuel for five power plants at Neka, Tus, Shari'ati, Shirvan, and Mashhad. He said: In the First Five-Year Plan two units were established at the power plant in the Gonbadli region, where we share gas fields with Turkmenistan. One of these two units has now been put into operation, and it produces 2.5 million m[.sup]3[/] of cubic sweet gas. The second unit is also about to go into operation. It is necessary to note that the Shahid-e Hashemi Gas Refinery, which is considered one of the largest gas refineries of its kind, is 35 km from Sarakhs and 165 km from Mashhad, in the northeast part of the province of Khorasan.
