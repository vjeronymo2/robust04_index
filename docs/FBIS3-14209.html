Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa left Cairo for Addis Ababa this evening to chair the meetings of the Central Apparatus for Solving African Disputes by Peaceful Means, which begin in the Ethiopian capital tomorrow. The apparatus is scheduled to discuss attempts to resolve existing African disputes, including the dispute between Cameroon and Nigeria, and the situations in Burundi, Rwanda, and Somalia. It will also discuss the apparatus' budget deficit. The foreign ministers of apparatus member states are participating in the meetings. In a statement before leaving, Musa expressed his extreme annoyance over the situation in Hebron and the violent treatment of its Palestinian residents, which is resulting in casualties. He said: Egypt denounces these actions and calls on the Israeli Government to adopt the necessary measures to protect the Palestinian people according to the recent UN Security Council resolution.
