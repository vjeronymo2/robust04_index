BFN [Text] Sofia, May 13 (BTA) -- Today Prime Minister Lyuben Berov filed with the Secretarial Department of the National Assembly the cabinet's programme of intent concerning the structural and personnel cabinet changes. The MP's may be expected to discuss the programme next week. Finance Minister Stoyan Aleksandrov is very likely to take office as one of the deputy prime ministers, Prof. Berov told reporters in parliament. He denied that the post of deputy prime minister had been ever offered to Prof. Todor Vulchev, governor of the Bulgarian National Bank. The negotiations are still going on, the prime minister said, adding there would be no meetings with representatives of the parliamentary groups today. This week Prime Minister Berov held consultations with the parliamentary groups of the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS), whose mandate he used to form a cabinet at the end of 1992, and with the parliamentary Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP), which supports the government. The DPS had parallel talks with the New Union for Democracy (NSD), a parliamentary group set up by expelees and breakaways from the Union of Democratic Forces (SDS), and with the Civil Alliance for the Republic, which unites the MP's who split from the BSP parliamentary group. After his yesterday's [12 May] meeting with Prof. Berov, BSP leader Zhan Videnov said that the new project for cabinet changes was better than the first one. Yesterday DPS leader Ahmed Dogan said he had stressed at a meeting with Prime Minister Berov that the DPS wanted the formula of expert government be reckoned with when carrying out the cabinet changes. On Wednesday [11 May] the parliamentary SDS moved for a vote of no confidence in the government. At an extraordinary meeting today, the NSD MP's considered the prime minister's programme for changes and revised their initial position on not taking part in any negotiations on the cabinet's overhaul. The NSD revising its position made NSD co-chairperson Stefan Bozhilov resign as a leader of that parliamentary group.
