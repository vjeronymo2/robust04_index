BFN [Excerpts] The citizenship bill was debated in the Latvian Saeima today on its third reading. As expected, the debate was primarily devoted to Clause 14, which regulates the general arrangements for the naturalization of non-citizens. [passage omitted] The quota principle could be an obstacle to admission to the Council of Europe. This was pointed out in a speech by Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs. In a secret ballot, this quota was removed from the law by majority vote. Thereby, the Saeima this evening accepted the law on citizenship. The voting in the secret ballot was: 66 deputies for; 11 against; three abstentions.
