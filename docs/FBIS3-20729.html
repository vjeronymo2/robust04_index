Vico: "How To Salvage the Fleet"] [Excerpts] Montenegro Prime Minister Mile Djukanovic, opening a two-day conference on the prospects of the shipbuilding, repair, and servicing industry in the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia [FRY] organized by the Veljko Vlahovic Shipyard in Bijela, Montenegro, within the framework of this year's Mimosa Festival, spoke on the resources available to the Montenegrin maritime trade sector and on government measures to safeguard that industry under the conditions of sanctions. Djukanovic said: "The following data speak about the significance and the prospects of the maritime trade industry of Montenegro: a port cargo handling capacity of about five million tons, a repair capacity of 80 to 100 ships annually, a fleet consisting of 37 ships with about one million tons displacement, 5,200 workers -- 5 percent of the total number of employed in the republic. Participation in maritime trade accounts for 17 percent of the GNP of the republic and 20 percent of total foreign exchange income. Projects planned for the development of maritime trade were halted with the introduction of sanctions by the UN Security Council. Sanctions did not affect any other sector of the economy to such a degree as they did maritime trade. They brought into question its very survival. Direct damage from the sanctions to the maritime trade enterprises of Montenegro has been assessed at $220 million. The republic government, together with the federal government, is undertaking measures to safeguard existing capacities during the sanctions, i.e., to activate them as successfully as possible after the sanctions are lifted. [passage omitted] This is why, with the help of the federal government and the National Bank of Yugoslavia, $45 million have been earmarked for that purpose. These funds have saved the ships from foreclosure." [passage omitted]
