BFN [Text] Gaza City, Occupied Gaza Strip, April 11 (AFP)--The son of PLO negotiator Nabil Sha'th has arrived here to take over the computer software the Israeli Army has used to run the Gaza Strip and Jericho, a colleague said Monday [11 April]. 'Ali Sha'th, 35, who works for the family computer firm Team, crossed into Gaza for the first time on Sunday via the Rafah border crossing linking Egypt and the Strip, a Team official said here. Team has been authorised by the PLO to handle the software to be given to the new Palestinian administration by military authorities in Gaza and the West Bank town of Jericho, Team's managing director Sa'id al-Qidrah told AFP. Under the Declaration of Principles signed between Israel and the PLO in Washington in September, all responsibility for health, education, social welfare, direct taxation and tourism is due to pass to the Palestinians. Team which has 400 experts working in 14 branches across the Arab world specialises in developing human resources, training experts in engineering, technology and sociology.
