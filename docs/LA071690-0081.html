July 16, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

IN COURT: A SUMMARY OF SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA-RELATED BUSINESS LITIGATION DEVELOPMENTS DURING THE PAST WEEK.

Big Rock Mesa Landslides: Fireman's Fund Insurance Co. was sued by five insurance companies seeking to recover the costs of defending lawsuits filed by Malibu homeowners over the Big Rock Mesa landslides. The Los Angeles Superior Court suit says Fireman's Fund and the plaintiff-insurers provided insurance to some of the homeowners.

When the owners were cross-sued by several government entities, the suit says, Fireman's Fund named a single law firm to defend them but refused to pay for individual lawyers for each homeowner. To avoid potential conflicts, the five plaintiffs paid for separate attorneys to defend the homeowners and now want Fireman's Fund to reimburse them for the proportionate share of the costs. The Big Rock Mesa litigation was settled for more than $100 million last year. (Filed July 9, 1990. Case No. BC005297)
