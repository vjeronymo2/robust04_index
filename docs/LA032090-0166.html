March 20, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: FONDA CZECHS OUT DRAMA IN FALLEN IRON CURTAIN

PEOPLE ARE talking about Jane Fonda's recent trip to Europe. She's been moving fast since the tumultuous events over there and plans a big movie which would co-star -- the fall of the Iron Curtain!

Fonda recently spent time with Czechoslovakia's new president, playwright Vaclav Havel, and sees his life story -- especially the five years he spent in jail for dissident activities against communism -- as a heady brew for film. Fonda herself would portray Havel's wife, Olga.

PEOPLE ARE gabbing over what got left on the cutting room floor in the Barbara Walters "20/20" interview with NBC's Bryant Gumbel.

The "Today" show host made it perfectly clear where he stands on rumors about taking a news anchor job, saying: "Tom Brokaw's doing a great job, so the job's not available . . . if it was available, there's no guarantee they'd come and ask me . . . nobody has asked me about it . . . I don't aspire to it . . . absolutely not. No, it's not me, Barbara, it's not me. I'm not necessarily sure I'd be good at it, because I think it's a different kind of talent than doing the 'Today' program. And . . . it just doesn't interest me."

Is this definite enough for all you, we, us rumormongers?

PEOPLE ARE flipping over this week's "If It Ain't Broke, Break It" story in Newsweek on the aftermath of the Jane Pauley-"Today" mess. They seem to be asking -- is Jane the Ivana Trump of the airwaves? Notable question: "The media's casting of Pauley as a kind of spurned wife has sent her popularity soaring."

And here's another: "At least one NBC insider believes that Gumbel is trying to distance himself from the wreckage. . . . The same source also claims that Gumbel and Dick Ebersol have begun blaming Pauley for getting them into this mess. They're telling people, 'If Jane had just accepted things like a good soldier, none of this would have happened.' "

Ebersol is the producer who masterminded the importation of Deborah Norville -- he now claims he did it with Jane's acquiescence. And the "this" that happened, of course, was falling ratings, media hoopla and unhappy fallout.

PEOPLE ARE gabbing over On the Avenue's article about "The Two Mrs. Trumps," meaning Blaine and Ivana. The magazine asks, "Is blood thicker than Poland Spring?"

Society has always gossiped about the wife of Robert and the wife of Donald and whether or not they are friends or rivals or what? Now I say -- give 'em a break. Both women are victims in this turn of events. I'm sure both Blaine and Ivana will "do the right thing" once and if ever they figure out what that is supposed to be.

I haven't spoken to Ivana in several weeks. But I am told that she now has a new, tougher attitude, has girded her loins for a real fight to protect herself and her children, and is beginning to see the light -- that there could possibly be life without Donald.

PEOPLE ARE flabbergasted by the New York Intelligencer's report on how Doris Day wants murderers -- rather than small animals -- used to test new AIDS drugs. "I can't bear anything innocent being used. I think they should experiment on murderers. . . . Why not? They owe society something. Don't stand aghast at that. They're sitting there having three meals a day, and we're paying for it. What the hell are they going to do for society to pay us back? They should do this for us." This report appeared originally in The Cable Guide.
