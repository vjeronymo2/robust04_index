BFN [Report by Cha Man-sun from Vienna] [Text] The International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] received an initial report on the situation of the replacement of the fuel rods from the inspection team which started inspection activities in Yongbyon, North Korea, on 18 May, and discussed measures regarding this matter. The inspection team consisted of three people who are conducting a detailed examination of whether the fuel rods were replaced at the five megawatt-class atomic reactor. It was learned that the inspection team made an initial report to the IAEA on the progress of the replacement of the fuel rods and on its future prospects. In this connection, a well-informed source reported that high- ranking policy-makers in the IAEA closely analyzed the initial report, and discussed in detail on whether to seek ways to resolve this issue through negotiations with North Korea. However, the details of the inspection team's report to the IAEA on the situation regarding the replacement of fuel rods were not revealed.
