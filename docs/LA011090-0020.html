January 10, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

HOLLYWOOD AND BLACK AMERICANS

Writer Stein makes a good point when he describes Hollywood's reluctance to cast black Americans in positive and inspirational roles. I agree totally and would like to extend his point to include the responsibility of today's black producers to generate movies with a positive and meaningful purpose.

On a recent trip to the movies I saw the film "Do the Right Thing," a movie that has been mentioned as the best picture of the year. In my opinion this is one of the all-time worst movies to come out of Hollywood. It is insulting and degrading to the black community. I don't deny the existence of the black experience the movie describes, but why must all our black-produced movies bring the same negative message?

If black producers can't come up with something positive from the black American life style, how can we expect others to do so? I believe our black producers have a responsibility to present some of the positive experiences that are occurring every day in the black American life style.

RAYMOND M. STEWART

Los Angeles
