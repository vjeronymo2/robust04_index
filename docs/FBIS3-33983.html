Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Dr. Velayati met the Bulgarian prime minister in Sofia last night. Lyuben Berov described Velayati's visit to Bulgaria as a turning point for improving and strengthening the bases of bilateral relations. He stressed his government's eagerness to increase commercial exchanges with Iran. He expressed interest in purchasing gas and oil from the Islamic Republic of Iran and called for greater facilities to broaden commercial relations between Tehran and Sofia. Stressing the consolidation of mutual friendship and reciprocal trust, Dr. Velayati said that activating the two countries' private sectors, joint investment, and cooperation in free trade zones was very important for upgrading relations. According to IRNA, the Bulgarian prime minister said he was grateful that President Hashemi-Rafsanjani was safe following yesterday's shooting incident at the mausoleum of the imam, may God be pleased with him.
