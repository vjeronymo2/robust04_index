Language: Finnish Article Type:BFN [Text] The European Space Agency, ESA, has ordered equipment, programs, and design work worth over FMk [Finnish markkas] 50 million from Finland for the Envisat 1 satellite that studies the environment and that will be launched in 1998. This is the biggest ever order for Finland in the space sector. The order will employ several dozen people in Finland's top technology enterprises until 1998. The orders from Finland concentrate on Envisat's GOMOS [Global Ozone Monitoring by Occultation of Stars] instrument that measures the ozone layer. Finland has been taking part in developing the GOMOS instrument from the beginning, Kari Tilli, the technical director of the Technology Development Center, said today.
