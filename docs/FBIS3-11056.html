Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 17 Mar (NA) -- President Carlos Menem and his Bolivian counterpart, Gonzalo Sanchez de Losada, today analyzed, among other subjects, the probable granting of Argentine credits for environmental sanitary projects on the common border area between the two countries for the purpose of preventing the spread of cholera, the situation of illegal Bolivian immigrants in Argentina, the struggle against drug trafficking, and the projections of Mercosur [Common Market of the South]. Sanchez de Losada today arrived in Argentina on an official 48-hour visit with the objective of signing an agreement on plant and animal sanitary controls and an agreement on investment promotion and guarantees. President Menem and Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella received the Bolivian president at 1020 today in Metropolitan Airport's military section. After the welcoming protocol, City Council President Juan Trilla, from the Radical Civic Union, gave the Bolivian chief of state the keys to the city. During a brief news conference after the ceremony, Sanchez de Losada said one of the subjects he would discuss with Menem is the situation of some of his fellow countrymen living illegally in Argentina. He added: "The control of drug trafficking is very important, and with the aid of Argentina and the international community we will be able to stop this problem." As for the possibility that Bolivia will join Mercosur, the Bolivian president said: "We want to come close to Mercosur because we are part of the Amazon Basin," adding that "our future depends on exchange with the region's countries." After making these statements, Sanchez de Losada departed for Alvear Palace Hotel, from where he headed to Casa Rosada, where he held his first working meeting with Menem. After the meeting in Government House, the two presidents went to San Martin Palace, where Menem held a lunch in honor of his guest. The Bolivian president had anticipated that during his visit to Argentina he would talk with President Menem about subjects like illegal immigrants, Mercosur, cholera, and the canalization of the Pilcomayo River. Sanchez de Losada said Argentina is, for Bolivians, "almost a second fatherland; one is always very connected with this country and thousands of Bolivians currently live in Buenos Aires," which determines "a relation of vital importance for us." When asked whether the subject of illegal Bolivian immigrants will be discussed, he said "that will be one of the subjects we will discuss" and added that they will also discuss the canalization of the Pilcomayo River, which is "one of Bolivia's major concerns." "The Pilcomayo River is dwindling away and it is important for Paraguay, Bolivia, and Argentina," he said. Regarding drug trafficking control, Sanchez de Losada said: "It is a very important subject, and we will speak about the Bolivian criteria to ask Argentina and the international community for help to put an end to this phenomenon, which is something we cannot stop." He added that the future of Bolivia "depends a lot on what is going on in Argentina, on the possibility of increasing exchange, and on a rapprochement" between the two countries.
