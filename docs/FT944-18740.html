941001 FT 01 OCT 94 / World Commodities Prices: Spices Black pepper prices shot up this week to levels not seen in many years, reports Man Poducten. Spot supplies in Europe were almost completely exhausted and supplies from origin were very hard to obtain. As demand for pepper has been coming in from many consuming markets prices could not but increase very steeply. Black pepper grade 1 was quoted at about USDollars 2,750 a tonn. The highest price paid was for Malabar MG1 at Dollars 2,975, cif, ex-new crop, for February/March shipment. Demand for white peper remained limited. None the less the price went up as well, to about Dollars 3,400 a tonne, cif. Here too supplies were hard to obtain.
