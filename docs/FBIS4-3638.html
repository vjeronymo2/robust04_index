BFN [Text] Beijing, April 21 (XINHUA) -- The current visits by Chinese Premier Li Peng to four Central Asian countries are by no means directed against any other country, Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman Wu Jianmin said here this afternoon. Wu was addressing a weekly press conference, at which he denied a report that Li's visit was aimed at contending with Russia for influence in the countries of the Commonwealth of Independent States. The spokesman said that the Chinese premier's visits are return visits for those made by the leaders of the countries in question, and the purpose is to enhance mutual understanding with these countries, and promote good-neighborly friendship, as well as develop mutually beneficial co-operation. "Therefore, the visits are by no means directed against any other country," Wu said. "The friendly co-operation in various fields between China and Russia is developing steadily." He went on: "We are keen to see the continued development of the friendship between Russia and the Central Asian countries, which are neighbors of both China and Russia."
