BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Panamanian elections scheduled for 8 May 1994. Mireya Moscoso de Gruber, presidential candidate for the Arnulfist Party (PA), said on 26 April that the constitutional articles on the creation and functions of the defunct Panama Defense Forces must be amended. De Gruber visited Betania Corregimiento on the morning of 26 April and stated: "When the president tried to reform the Constitution, various legislators erroneously tried to modify several articles. That is why the Panamanian people rejected the constitutional reforms." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 26 Apr 94) Ruben Blades, presidential candidate of the Papa Egoro Movement, MPE, said that the polls indicate he is tied for first place with Democratic Revolutionary Party, PRD, candidate Ernesto Perez Balladares. Regarding support for him in the interior, Blades stated: "I think we have more support in the interior than is reflected in the polls." MPE slate members said as part of their government program that no public employees will be dismissed. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 28 Apr 94) Government Minister Jacobo Salas said that the president guaranteed that the 8 May elections will be free. Salas explained that "violence will not be tolerated, regardless of where it comes from." Regarding the decree banning weapons sales, Salas said it will remain in effect until 31 March. The minister stressed that "it is the president's desire to hold free elections in which violence -- we are confident there will not be any violence -- must be repressed, regardless of where it originates." (Panama City EL SIGLO in Spanish 28 Apr 94 p 10) Transportation sectors have endorsed the Democratic Alliance slate led by Mireya Moscoso de Gruber. Some 52 taxi drivers' organizations in Panama Province met in a Panama City hotel to endorse the candidacy of PA presidential candidate Mireya Moscoso de Gruber. The transportation workers delivered Mrs. De Gruber a list of proposals to modify Law 14 regulating transportation. De Gruber promised to meet the transportation workers' requests and said: "When we are at the presidency, you will have a friend more than a president. You will advise me on how my government can work better for all of you." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 29 Apr 94) On the evening of 3 May, PRD presidential candidate Ernesto Perez Balladares met with farmers from Chiriqui Province in Boquete. Perez Balladares addressed farmers on the need to improve roads in production areas, erroneous government agricultural policy, and falling coffee prices. He told producers he will establish "adequate protection for the agricultural sector for crops such as potatoes, rice, beans, corn, and sorghum." (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA 5 May 94 p B7)
