BFN [Report by Armando Solis M.] [Text] Torreon, Coahuila, 28 March -- Federal Judicial Police [PJF] personnel today seized more than four tons of cocaine and arrested two alleged drug traffickers who were transporting the drug in a tanker truck on the road from this city to Concepcion del Oro, Zacatecas. PJF Commander Juan Carlos Alvarado reported Raul Juarez de la Paz and Miguel Angel Salazar Ramirez, both from Uruapan, Michoacan, had been arrested during the police action. Both men testified that they received the merchandise in Guadalajara, Jalisco and were taking it to Cadereyta, Nuevo Leon. The policemen initially stopped the truck for speeding on the road to Concepcion del Oro in Zacatecas and discovered the large cocaine shipment while checking the vehicle. The 4,187 kg of cocaine had been placed in 2,162 packages in the vehicle's tank. The PJF commander said the drug shipment and the two men were placed in the custody of the Federal Attorney General's Office in Saltillo, Coahuila.
