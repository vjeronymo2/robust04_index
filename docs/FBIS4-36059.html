BFN [Excerpts] Kuwait, 4 Jun -- Councillor Salah al-Fahd, president of the State Security Court, stressed that sentences, which the court had passed, were not influenced by any political interventions from any quarter, pointing out that the defendants were recruited by Iraqi intelligence service to assassinate former President Bush and to carry out acts infringing on security [words indistinct] [passage omitted] The court also passed a death sentence on a Kuwaiti citizen, Badr Jiyad Thamir Mutlaq al-Shammari and fined the first four defendants a sum of 3,000 Kuwait dinars and a collective custom penalty of 1,500 Kuwait dinars for the first five and the 11th defendants. The State Security Court witnessed a fist fight in the dock between the first defendant Ra'd al-Asadi and the 11th defendant, Badr Jiyad al-Shammari following the issuing of sentences as the defendant al-Shammari claimed that Al-Asadi had implicated him in the case.
