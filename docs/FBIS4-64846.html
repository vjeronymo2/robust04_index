BFN [Unattributed article: "Djibouti-Sanaa Air Corridor Opened"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, May 9 -- An Italian Air Force C-130 transport plane last night flew some 100 refugees of various nationalities from Sanaa, North Yemen, to Djibouti while a second C-130 is currently on a similar mission, Italian military sources said. In contrast to initial reports, the first plane load of refugees were not Italian but composed of 33 French nationals, 30 Djibouti natives, four Algerians, one Iranian, one American, five Syrians, seven Somalis, two Egyptians, two Canadians, four Moroccans, three Tunisians and eight children of various nationalities. The second C-130, military sources added, should bring to Djibouti between 50 to 55 Italian nationals, who will be immediately taken to Italy.
