930205 FT 05 FEB 93 / World Stock Markets (America): US stocks at record levels as Dow soars Wall Street US STOCK prices hit record levels yesterday afternoon, helped by bullish sentiment about the domestic economy and the German cut in interest rates, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average soared 42.95 to 3,416.74, above its previous high of 3,413.21 set last June. The more broadly based Standard&Poor's 500 also reached new heights, adding 2.35 to 449.55, surpassing its previous day's record. The Amex composite was 0.62 higher at 415.51. NYSE volume was heavy, with more than 348m shares changing hands. Rises outnumbered declines by 1,334 to 604. On Wednesday, the Dow had closed 45.12 higher at 3,373.79. The Nasdaq composite hit a new high of 708.85, up 0.18, recovering from an earlier bout of profit-taking which drove the index lower at mid-session. Chrysler continued to lead the market, adding Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 39 3/4 . It has been active since Tuesday's successful completion of a 46m share offering. It is also benefiting from an improved credit rating from Standard&Poor's. Ford jumped Dollars 2 1/4 to Dollars 50, General Motors slid Dollars 1 3/8 to Dollars 37 3/4 in active trading. Standard&Poor's downgraded its rating on Wednesday. Waste Management, the biggest US waste disposal company, ended the day Dollars 1 3/4 lower at Dollars 37 3/4 after tumbling Dollars 4 earlier in the day. Investors were disappointed by the company's underlying fourth quarter earnings of 42 cents a share. An analyst at Prudential Research downgraded his rating on Waste Management International after the UK-based subsidiary posted disappointing fourth quarter earnings of 18 cents a share. Shares in Waste Management International fell Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 21 3/4 . Allied Signal hit a 52-week high of Dollars 65 1/4 , up Dollars 3 3/8 on Wednesday's 33 per cent improvement in underlying fourth quarter earnings. Toy-maker Mattel dropped Dollars 4 1/2 to Dollars 24 following the release of fourth quarter net income of 35 cents a share, below expectations of 43 cents but above last year's earnings of 29 cents. Canada Toronto stocks ended higher in heavy trading. According to preliminary data, the 300 composite index was up 22.37 points, or 0.67 per cent to 3,366.40. Advancing issues led declines 392 to 304. Volume was 54.43m shares, above Wednesday's 51.79m shares, and trading value rose to CDollars 512.7m from CDollars 440.1m. American Barrick reported higher fourth-quarter and full-year earnings and said there has been a 20 per cent increase in the Betze-Post ore reserves at its Goldstrike Mine in Nevada. The stock rose 1/2 to 38 7/8.
