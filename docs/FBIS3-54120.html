Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A government official of the United States has denied that President Bill Clinton's letter to congressmen on Punjab contained anything anti-India or it encouraged secessionist elements in the country. He said in Washington yesterday that the President in his letter had pointed out that the situation has improved in Punjab. He said the President has also mentioned that there were regional elections and restoration of local governments, while the level of violence in the state has declined. The director general of Punjab police, Mr. K.P.S. Gill, has said that President Clinton's remarks on Punjab were an outcome of wrong information said by the strong anti-Sikh [as heard] lobby in the States. He told newsmen in Bombay yesterday that the Punjab Police have been successful in controlling the militancy in the state.
