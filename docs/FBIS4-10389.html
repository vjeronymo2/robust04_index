BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 7 April. Brazilian and Chilean Foreign Ministers Celso Amorim and Carlos Figueroa signed the "Agreement on Promotion and Reciprocal Protection of Investments" at the end of the Rio Group meeting held in Rio de Janeiro on 22 March. The agreement seeks to create favorable conditions for intensifying bilateral economic cooperation, and especially for granting fair and equal treatment to investors in the respective countries. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 23 Mar 94 p 7) The Chilean trade balance registered a $101.3 million surplus in February, with exports at $737.2 million and imports at $635.9 million. The cumulative surplus for the year's first two months was $68.6 million, compared with the same period in 1993 when there was a deficit of $220.7 million. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 24 Mar 94 p 4) The Central Bank reported that the GDP increased 6 percent in 1993, and that the sectors that contributed most to the economic growth were construction, with 14 percent; commerce, restaurants, and hotels, 8.7 percent; transport and communication, 7.9 percent; and financial services, 7.1 percent. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 24 Mar 94 pp A1, A12) The Consumer Price Index stood at 1.1 percent in March. Accrued inflation so far this year stands at 2.5 percent. The hike in public utility rates, coupled with an increase in school fees, caused the inflation rate to exceed the estimates for the first quarter of the year. (Santiago Television Nacional de Chile Imagen Internacional in Spanish 0100 GMT 7 Apr 94)
