May 21, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: STAGE

New Passion Play: Christians from around the world gathered over the weekend in Oberammergau, West Germany, for a new version of the centuries-old Passion Play about the suffering of Jesus, rewritten to cut out some of the anti-Semitic passages and images.

The performance also marked the end of a tradition and sparked controversy. For the first time in the play's 356-year history, the role of the Virgin Mary was played by a married woman, Elisabeth Petre. In the past, only unmarried women were allowed to act as the mother of Christ. Director Christian Stueckl had faced a barrage of criticism over his casting of Petre. GREG BRAXTON
