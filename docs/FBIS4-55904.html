BFN [Text] [Words indistinct] [Prime Minister] Haris Silajdzic today despite [words indistinct] also received a delegation of the Islamic Republic of Iran led by (Ibrahim Rahimpur), director general for Central and Northern Europe at the Iranian Foreign Ministry. The talks were also attended by Iranian Ambassador to Bosnia-Herzegovina Muhammad Asayesh-Zarebi. During the frank and friendly talks, (Rahimpur) conveyed the impressions of Minister Velayati to Prime Minister Silajdzic and expressed the readiness of the Iranian Government to continue offering humanitarian and political aid to the people and the Government of Bosnia-Herzegovina. Stressing that Iran is helping Bosnia-Herzegovina on the basis of friendship and not on the basis of religion and that it is doing so without setting any conditions, Prime Minister Silajdzic emphasized that there is no reason for concern in some circles because of this, since we, being a sovereign country, choose our friends and receive our guests. Silajdzic hailed the idea of forming a tripartite commission for economic cooperation between (?Croatia), Iran, and Bosnia-Herzegovina.
