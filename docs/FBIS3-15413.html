Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Kuchina] [Text] Riga, 4 Mar -- Guntis Ulmanis, the president of Latvia, has replied to the message received from Boris Yeltsin on 17 February. The reply was passed to Russian Ambassador Aleksandr Rannikh, today. Ulmanis thanked his Russian colleague for the invitation to meet, and urged him to get involved with the issues that are now being discussed by the delegations from the two countries. He stressed that preparation of draft agreements connected with the withdrawal of Russian troops is nearing completion. If final agreement on the documents is reached at the bilateral talks, they could be signed during a possible summit meeting in Moscow. The Latvian president noted that the withdrawal of Russian troops will create a new situation in relations between the two countries, one that will be more favorable for cooperation in economic, humanitarian, and other spheres. Guntis Ulmanis expressed readiness to discuss with Boris Yeltsin various issues and problems mentioned in the Russian head of state's message, which have not been broached by the two delegations.
