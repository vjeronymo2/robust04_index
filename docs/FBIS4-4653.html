BFN [Report by Pusan Broadcasting Bureau] [Text] About 2,000 college students affiliated with the Korean Federation of University Student Councils [Hanchongnyon] have been holding a rally of second-term deputies of Hanchongnyon at Pusan University since 2 April. They gathered at the Pusan Railway Station this afternoon and held a rally opposing the opening of the rice market. They are presently staging a street demonstration along the sidewalks in Pusan and are moving toward Kwangbok-tong, their second assembly site. In a news conference held previously, Hanchongnyon revealed that it will hold a general rally at Konkuk University in Seoul on 5 April to oppose the government's Uruguay Round negotiations, and that all universities and colleges throughout the country decided to go on a strike for two days on 8 and 9 April. In addition, Hanchongnyon will hold a pan-citizens rally to oppose rice imports and the opening of its market in Seoul on 16 April, and about 200 of its executive members decided to go on an indefinite hunger strike in front of the National Assembly Hall. Hanchongnyon resolved to hold a ceremony marking the inauguration of the second-term of Hanchongnyon in Choson University in Kwangju for four days beginning 27 May.
