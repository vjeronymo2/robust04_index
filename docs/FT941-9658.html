940214 FT 14 FEB 94 / Rafsanjani's brother loses top media job Iran yesterday appointed a new head of radio and television in place of the brother of President Akbar Hashemi Rafsanjani, in a move widely seen as a concession to conservative Moslem clerics, Reuter reports from Nicosia. The Iranian news agency IRNA said supreme leader Ali Khamenei named Ali Larijani as the TV and radio chief to replace Mohammad Hashemi, the president's younger brother, who ran the broadcast organisation for 13 years. Mr Larijani is a relative hardliner who had been minister of Islamic culture and guidance.
