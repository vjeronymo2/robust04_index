January 6, 1989, Friday, South Bay Edition

WARRIOR CAGERS COLLIDE WITH PASADENA, TOUGHEST SCC OPPONENT

The El Camino College men's basketball team will travel to Pasadena City College on Saturday to open South Coast Conference play at 7:30 p.m. The Warriors are 15-5 after Wednesday's 84-81 victory over Fashion Institute. Pasadena (12-4) is thought to be El Camino's toughest SCC opponent. The Lancers ended a 10-game winning streak when they fell to Santa Monica in their own tournament last month.

The Loyola Marymount women's basketball team will play its third straight road game at 1 p.m. Sunday at Wyoming. The Lions were 7-4 after losing at Weber State, 83-63, on Tuesday. Sunday's game is Loyola's last non-conference contest. They open West Coast Athletic Conference play Jan. 13 at San Francisco.

The 11th annual Redondo Beach Super Bowl Sunday 10-K on Jan. 22 will offer prize money to the athletic programs of schools that win the centipede race -- 10 or more runners linked by costume fabric -- and the six-pack competition for teams of six runners. Race organizers offered team prize money for the first time last year and an El Camino College team won $500 for the track program. Team entry information is available at 376-6913.
