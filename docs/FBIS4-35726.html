BFN [Text] Cairo, 3 Jun (MENA) -- The foreign ministers of the Nonaligned Movement [NAM] have called for an international conference on Bosnia-Herzegovina in which all parties to the conflict would participate, as would countries participating in the protection forces of the United Nations and the NAM representatives. In a resolution it issued today at the conclusion of its meetings in Cairo, the conference also demanded that liaison teams from the Islamic Conference Organization, the European Union, the five permanent members of the United Nations, and the UN secretary general also participate in this conference. The NAM foreign ministers' resolution also stipulated that the proposed conference should take the appropriate action on an immediate cease-fire and halt to all military activities under the supervision of the UN protection force. The NAM foreign ministers also asked that all parts of Bosnia- Herzegovina be declared safe havens to guarantee the unity of this territory, not to let it be violated, and to secure its political independence and continued existence. He affirmed the need to respect the sovereignty of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina and the unity of its territory. The ministerial conference affirmed the need to find a just settlement for all parties and create a working program to implement this settlement. The conference expressed its grave concern over the dangerous situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. It affirmed the solidarity of the NAM countries with the Bosnia-Herzegovina Republic regarding its legitimate right of self-defense.
