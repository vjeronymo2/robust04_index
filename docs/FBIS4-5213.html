BFN [Text] Seoul, April 8 (YONHAP) -- The government held the first meeting on unification and security policy coordination at the South-North dialogue office on Friday to review its overall policy toward North Korea. The meeting, presided over by Minister of the National Unification Board (NUB) Yi Yong-tok, decided to discuss all unification matters, diplomacy related to the North and security to coordinate the opinions of different ministries. The various ministries and secretaries will also push ahead with unification- and security-related policies under the principle that the government must seek progress in inter-Korean relations. President Kim Yong-sam Thursday instructed that a Unification and Security Policy Coordination Council be formed. The council comprises the minister of the National Unification Board, the foreign and defense ministers, the director of the Agency for National Security Planning, the chief presidential secretary and the senior secretary for foreign and national security affairs. NUB Minister Yi said after the meeting, "We looked into the overall situation on unification and security after adoption of the U.N. Security Council's presidential statement and rupture of inter-Korean working-level contact." "We will discuss every policy to adjust differences between ministries." The coordination meetings will be held regularly more than once a week, and President Kim himself will chair the sessions when necessary.
