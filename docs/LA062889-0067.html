June 28, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

THE WORLD

Leftist guerrillas claimed responsibility for the assassination of El Salvador's national fire department chief. The Farabundo Marti National Liberation Front said on rebel radio that a commando unit ambushed Col. Roberto Armando Rivera, 45. It said Rivera's death was justified because "he was a military officer with a clear record of criminal acts and repression." They said he gave courses in psychological warfare and interrogation techniques and was linked to right-wing death squads that terrorized the country in the early 1980s.
