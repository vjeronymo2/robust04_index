921120 FT 20 NOV 92 / Brazilian President denies dithering BRAZIL's new President Itamar Franco has defended himself against mounting criticism he is spending his time on trivia rather than the economic problems confronting the country. In his first lengthy press conference since taking office two months ago, Mr Franco said: 'Society wants the president sometimes to put aside macrothinking and descend to the microlevel of rents, medicines, schools and trying to improve everyday life.' His focus on such issues has led his administration to be labelled 'the Cheese Muffin Republic', a reference to the muffin staple diet of Brazil's poor. The president was to outline his plans for government in his first national address on Wednesday but cancelled it to visit his sick mother. Later he made clear some of his views, declaring war on financial speculators who have been leaving the stockmarket and buying gold and dollars. 'As long as I'm President there will be this war without mercy.' Referring to Mr Collor's recent comments that he was dragging Brazil into the fifth world, Mr Franco said 'the misery we have today puts us far below the fifth world. Two and a half years of Collor's high interest rates and violent monetary policy have left us with deep recession and millions of Brazilians living in absolute poverty.' For the first time in living memory, a senior public figure has been found guilty of corruption in Argentina. The Senate yesterday impeached Mr Alberto Nicosia, a federal judge accused of multi-million-dollar fraud against state companies, John Barham reports from Buenos Aires. The senators found the judge guilty of misconduct and failing to carry out the duties of a public official. They removed him from the bench and banned him from holding public office.
