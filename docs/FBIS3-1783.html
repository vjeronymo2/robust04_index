Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 28 (XINHUA) -- Vice-Premier Zou Jiahua said here today that China's reforms will be promoted if the large and medium-sized state enterprises carry out the central government's reform scheme in an exemplary way. At a meeting with the managers of 40 large state enterprises from all over the country, Zou urged the managers to set great store by study which involves Deng Xiaoping's theory of building socialism with Chinese characteristics, new laws and regulations, new policies and new technology. He said that at present the State Council was selecting 100 enterprises for pioneering the reforms. He noted that the new measures for the reforms adopted by the government aim at helping the state enterprises transform their operating system, enabling the enterprises to take part in market competition. The 40 managers are attending a forum which opened on February 22, jointly sponsored by the Organization Department of the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party and the State Economic and Trade Commission. Vice-Premier Li Lanqing presided over the meeting.
