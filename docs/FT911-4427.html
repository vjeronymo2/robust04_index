910419 FT 19 APR 91 / UK Company News: Higher bad debts bite into Cradley Exceptional charges held back pre-tax profits of Cradley Group, the lithographic printer, to Pounds 710,000 in the half year ended December 31 1990, against Pounds 759,000. Before exceptionals profits rose 61 per cent to Pounds 1.03m on turnover ahead 48 per cent to Pounds 14.42m. The results were much in line with the second half of last year when investment plans began to come on stream, said Mr Donald Jordan, chairman. Exceptional charges comprised: bad debt provision Pounds 281,000 (Pounds 130,000) and reorganisations costs Pounds 69,000 (nil), less surplus on sale of assets Pounds 34,000 (Pounds 251,000). Mr Jordan said there had been substantial closures of magazine titles and a consequential increase in bad debts. The restructuring in South Wales had been completed. Management of the Hertfordshire company had been restructured although much needed to be done to obtain an adequate return. Earnings per share came to 1.4p (1.6p).
