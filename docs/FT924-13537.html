921015 FT 15 OCT 92 / Smoking 'cuts lives by 6 years' SMOKING cuts an average of six years off people's lives, according to the largest UK study to use data from the life insurance industry. The Continuous Mortality Investigation Bureau and the Institute of Actuaries and Faculty of Actuaries, in a review of 500,000 people, found that the most frequent age of death for male smokers was 81, while male non-smokers most frequently died at 87. For women smokers, the most frequent age of death was 85 while non-smoking women typically live until 91.
