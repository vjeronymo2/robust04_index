BFN [Text] Even on days off the upcoming elections of the first Belarusian president remain a popular topic among Vitebsk residents. The Vitebsk independent sociological agency Istina decided to make some preliminary conclusions regarding the results of its nonofficial poll. It conducted a selective phone poll of 364 Vitebsk residents. Already at the start the sociologists discovered an interesting detail: In Vitebsk primarily women hold control over home phones. Therefore, the prevailing majority of respondents is represented by the best half of mankind of various age and professions. They gave their sympathies to three leading politicians: Alyaksandr Lukashenka, Vyachaslaw Kebich, and Stanislaw Shushkevich. The remaining candidates for this prestigious post were much behind the leaders. Almost half of the respondents link their hopes for improving the situation in society and the economy with the president. However, one fourth of the respondents did not express any positive emotions regarding this issue.
