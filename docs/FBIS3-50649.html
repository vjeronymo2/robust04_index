Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Costa Rican economic developments monitored through 4 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. The Exports and Investments Center of Costa Rica on 28 December reported that sales of products and services to foreign nations increased more than 50 percent between 1990 and 1993, although the trade balance is in deficit. According to the Central Bank, imports in 1990 amounted to $1.98 billion, whereas in 1993 they reached $3 billion. The same source added that the 1993 trade balance has an $800 million deficit. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1859 GMT 28 Dec 93) Finance Minister Carlos Munoz announced that interest rates in 1994 will be lower than in 1993. Higher rates will continue to be used for several months while some adjustments are made. (San Jose Radio Reloj in Spanish 1730 GMT 29 Dec 93) Costa Rican President Rafael Angel Calderon Fournier reported on 3 January that inflation during 1993 was 9.05 percent. Economy Minister Roberto Rojas explained that this was due to a reduction in the prices of many items after some taxes were lifted in April. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0007 GMT 4 Jan 94)
