Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] In an interview granted today to one of our correspondents, Lieutenant General Niculae Spiroiu, Romanian national defense minister, said that he, together with other top officials of the ministry, had examined in Cluj the 1993 balance of the 4th Army [the Transylvanian army], as well as aspects related to military discipline and order in that army. They found that the activity of the 4th Army was very well organized. Minister Spiroiu rejected allegations published in today's edition of the daily ULTIMUL CUVANT [THE LAST WORD] that he went to Cluj to remove the current commander of the 4th Army, General Paul Cheler. Spiroiu added that he regrets that such news is being circulated without first checking with the Army Press Office.
