930112 FT 12 JAN 93 / Too much stress 'bad for business' PART OF the secret of a healthy workforce may be encouraging staff to work less hard, a director of Zeneca Pharmaceuticals, ICI's drugs division, said yesterday. Mr Barrie Thorpe, Zeneca Pharmaceuticals production director, told a London conference on mental health at work that establishing sensible workloads for staff was one way the company had tackled stress-related illness. He said a letter had been sent from the Zeneca Pharmaceuticals chief executive officer to departmental heads. It said: 'If work takes up more than a reasonable proportion of an individual's time over too long a period, the business is unlikely to benefit in the long run.' It added that while some stress is good for both individuals and the business, leading to job satisfaction, motivation and good performance, 'too much or inappropriate pressure on people who are unable to cope with it is bad for them and bad for the business'. The letter drew attention to the importance of hobbies and holidays, adding: 'The sensible planning and allocation of work within your department is a vital factor in maximising efficiency.' Mr Thorpe said some senior executives had felt threatened by the idea that employees might be told to work less hard, and there had been 'genuine fear' that company performance could be affected. 'It will come as no surprise to those involved in organisational development work that this was not the case,' he added, 'and indeed it seems likely the reverse occurred.' Earlier, Mrs Virginia Bottomley, the health secretary, told delegates: 'A company of 1,000 employees can expect between 200 and 300 with depression and anxiety in any one year, and one suicide every decade.' Mrs Bottomley, who underlined her commitment to the policy of providing care for the mentally ill in the community rather than in institutions, said: 'Six million people suffer from mental illness each year. It is as common as heart disease.'
