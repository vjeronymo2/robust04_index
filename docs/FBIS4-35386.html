BFN [Text] Progressive Socialist Party [PSP] sources have stated that Minister Walid Junblatt has received a new plan from the Army Command on a broader military deployment as a prelude to the return of the evacuees. This plan will be implemented in the mountain area between 9 and 15 June. The Army is estimated at 5,000 to 7,000 Lebanese troops and will be deployed in most of the mountain villages and towns, particularly the villages that the evacuees will return to during the summer. The force will erect fixed and mobile roadblocks at the entrances of the villages, which were the scenes of various problems during the war, to ensure the security of the citizens. The PSP sources added that Minister Junblatt has welcomed the details of the plan and that he briefed the PSP leadership on the deployment process. The sources noted that Minister Junblatt has praised the role of the Army and of General Lahhud before the popular delegations.
