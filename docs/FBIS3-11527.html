Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 17 March. The Brazilian Petroleum Corporation will invest $42.2 million in 1994-97 to develop oil byproducts from the Marlim and Albacora oil fields located in the Campos Basin. It is expected that this program will generate approximately $1 billion in profits between 1995 and 2001. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 10 Mar 94 p 15) On 9 March, British Treasury Secretary Michael Portillo met Brazilian Aeronautics Minister Lelio Viana Lobo to discuss the sale to Brazil of eight Rolls-Royce engines worth $100 million for three Boeing 777 planes. British Ambassador to Brazil Peter Heap said Transbrasil and the British company have not yet signed a sale contract but a letter of intent. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 10 Mar 94 p 6) On 9 March, President Itamar Franco signed a decree establishing two new free zones in the municipalities of Brasileia/Epitaciolandia and Cruzeiro do Sul, Acre State. A total of seven areas already have been placed under the special fiscal system in the country. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 10 Mar 94 p 6) Rio Doce Valley Company's vice president, Anastacio Ubaldino Fernandes Filho, reported that the company is planning to trade future gold production in the international market, through which it expects to attract $180 million in foreign capital. Fernandes said the company is analyzing proposals by financial institutions to mediate in the operation. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 10 Mar 94 pp 1-22) Tax revenues increased 24 percent in February, a drop of 9 percent compared with January, but an increase of 40.38 percent compared with the same month last year. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 14 Mar 94 p 5) Fuel prices were adjusted 19.5 percent as of 14 March. The prices of alcohol, gasoline, diesel oil, and cooking gas have increased 124.86 percent so far this year. The last 18.5 percent readjustment was decreed on 24 February. (Rio de Janeiro O GLOBO in Portuguese 15 Mar 94 p 21) The Itamarati business group will invest $250 million in the next four years in a sugar cane project in Barra do Bugre, Mato Grosso State. The group owns 25,000 hectares of cultivated area in the region and a sugar mill currently producing 1 million sacks of sugar and 80 million liters of alcohol. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 17 Mar 94 p 26)
