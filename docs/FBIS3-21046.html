Language: French Article Type:CSO [Article: "Test Flights of Saab's JAS 39-Gripen Halted"] [Text]Paris - Trollhaettan -- On 21 January, a spokesperson for Volvo Flygmotor, the aeronautical branch of Sweden's leading industrial group at Trollhaettan, announced that test flights of Sweden's Saab JAS 39-Gripen military aircraft have been halted as the result of the breakage, a week ago, of a blade inside the RM12 turbine engine that the aircraft is equipped with. According to the spokesperson, Sweden's military authorities grew very circumspect after a Saab JAS 39-Gripen crashed on 8 August 1993, during a demonstration, near the downtown area of Sweden's capital. The pilot could no longer steer the aircraft because of a failure in the control lever. During a test bench session "sizable vibrations" were registered in the engine's turbine.
