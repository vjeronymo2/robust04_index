June 20, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

POINDEXTER SENTENCED TO PRISON FOR ROLE IN IRAN-CONTRA SCANDAL

Give me a break! Poindexter deliberately subverted the will of our elected government, the U.S. Congress. He did this for his own political ends and nothing more.

These people involved in this Iran-Contra scandal committed acts that border on treason. It seems incredible then that the harshest jail term (in fact, the only jail term) is a mere six months. Isn't this the era when we're supposed to "get tough" on criminals?

The judge feels that poor Poindexter has suffered enough in legal costs (Part A, June 12). It obviously paid off for him.

And maybe less obvious, but surely apt: This looks convincingly like another case of rich, white men taking care of their own. From the President on down to the sentencing judge -- what a sham!

Where is the justice when I can count on spending a few years in jail if I get caught taking drugs to numb my mind to this kind of hypocrisy.

MICHAEL SANCHEZ

Los Angeles
