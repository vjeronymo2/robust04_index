920703 FT 03 JUL 92 / Chief executive sought for port the latest initiative aimed at tackling the Cumbrian port's economic structural problems - is looking for a Pounds 40,000-a-year chief executive to steer the town towards a future based on the service sector. The company was created in response to Whitehaven's worsening unemployment and its difficulties in attracting inward industrial investment. The company's priorities include fostering leisure uses for Whitehaven's port, which has just lost its last commercial cargo with the ending of phosphate rock imports for the town's Albright & Wilson chemicals works. Coal shipping trade ceased in the 1980s when the last pits in west Cumbria closed. The development company, a joint venture with the West Cumbria Development Agency, hopes to smarten up the town and attract tourists. Unemployment in Whitehaven is 8.6 per cent, below the national average, but the area is being hit by the shedding of 5,000 construction jobs at the nearby Sellafield nuclear waste reprocessing plant. Cumbria County Council has forecast unemployment rates of up to 25 per cent in the borough of Copeland, which includes Whitehaven. Mr David Shelton, development company board member, said promotion of leisure and tourism would run alongside development of a business park and industrial estate. But he warned: 'To think we will attract new heavy industry opportunities would be deluding ourselves.'
