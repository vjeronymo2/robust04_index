Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] His Royal Highness Prince Sultan Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, the second deputy prime minister and minister of defense and aviation and the inspector general, and His Excellency Malcolm Rifkind, the British defense secretary, and in the presence of His Royal Highness Prince 'Abd-al-Rahman Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, the deputy minister of defense and aviation, this evening held official talks at the palace of his royal highness the second deputy prime minister in Riyad. The talks were attended on the Saudi side by the chief of the General Staff, staff General Muhammad Bin Salih al-Hammad, the commanders of the land, naval and air forces and the air defense forces, and on the British side by the delegation accompanying His Excellency Mr. Malcolm Rifkind and British Ambassador to the Kingdom David Gore-Booth. At the beginning of the talks His Royal Highness Prince Sultan Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz made a speech in which he welcomed in the name of King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, the custodian of the two holy mosques, and the Crown Prince His Royal Highness Prince 'Abdallah Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, and in his own name and the name of His Highness Prince 'Abd-al-Rahman Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, and the people of the Kingdom, his excellency the British minister and his colleagues. His highness called for the continuation of the relations, cooperation, and exchanged visits. His highness said that relations between the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and Britain are distinguished -- they are old relations dating back to the days of King 'Abd-al-'Aziz, may God's mercy be on his soul. We would like to reaffirm that cooperation between the two states continues and is on a sound and correct path even if there are differences with regard to some views or differences regarding some projects. This is within the nature of the positive matters until views are corrected. His excellency the British defense secretary also made a speech in which he expressed his pleasure at visiting the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, and expressed his thanks and gratitude for the generous hospitality and welcome he received in the Kingdom. His excellency lauded the relations between the two friendly countries.
