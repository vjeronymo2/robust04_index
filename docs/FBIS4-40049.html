BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Kanat Saudabayev gave a speech last Saturday [11 June] at a conference of foreign ministers held in Istanbul. He said in particular that Kazakhstan, from the very first days of its revived statehood, stands for the strengthening of international security and an atmosphere of confidence. The Kazakh leadership regards the country's participation in North Atlantic cooperation from this viewpoint. The preparation of the final documents was held in an atmosphere of tense debates, and the Kazakh delegation's amendment was introduced. As result, Paragraph 25 of the conference statement says: We welcome the fact that the Republic of Kazakhstan has joined the Nuclear Weapons Nonproliferation Treaty as a state that does not possess such weapons, and highly assess Kazakhstan's positive role in the process of dismantling the nuclear weapons of the former USSR in conformity with the Lisbon protocol. On the same day Kanat Saudabayev met the foreign ministers of Germany, Greece, Canada, and Turkey. They touched upon topical regional and international problems and bilateral relations, as well as the further expansion of mutually beneficial trade and economic cooperation. The Kazakhstan delegation returned home.
