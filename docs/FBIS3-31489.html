Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, February 16 (BTA) -- Unemployment in Bulgaria hit 16.53 percent of the workforce in late January 1994, rising 0.15 points from December 1993, the National Employment Service with the Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs today told BTA. The unemployed numbered 632,000. The jobless rate was highest in Montana (northwestern Bulgaria) (23.9 percent) and lowest in Sofia (8.3 percent). Only one-third have a right to a compensation or social benefits. Data shows the number of jobless persons under 30 kept falling as it did in late 1993, which is seen as a positive trend. Job vacancies in late January numbered 8,200, with 77 jobless vying for a single vacancy. Most positions were to be found in Sofia. In January alone, the Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs paid out 240.6 million leva in benefits and spent nearly 3 million leva on retraining.
