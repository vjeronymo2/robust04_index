Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Guatemalan economic developments monitored through 25 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item: The Bank of Guatemala president reported that demand for foreign currency as of 14 January totalled $35.6 million. He added that this fact could be the result of the present political uncertainty. The official noted that the exchange rate remains relatively stable. The Bank president also reported that international monetary reserves have gone from $738 million to $756 million this month. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 18 Jan 94 p 62) President Ramiro De Leon Carpio delivered 3.1 million quetzales to the mayors of Chiquimula in his recent working tour of that department. De Leon explained that the funds became available thanks to his austerity policy. The money will be used for public projects in the aforementioned municipalities. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 24 Jan 94 p 36)
