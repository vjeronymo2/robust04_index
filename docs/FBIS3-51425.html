Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] San Jose, 13 Jan (ACAN-EFE) -- The solid bloc maintained by the Latin American banana-producing countries in their negotiations with the European Union (EU) has shown its first public fissure when four countries in the bloc failed to attend today's meeting in Costa Rica to study the European import proposal. The meeting, which ends tonight, was called by Costa Rica. However, Ecuador, the world's top banana-producing country; Panama; Honduras; and Guatemala were not present, and only the host country, Colombia, Venezuela, and Nicaragua showed up for the meeting. Prior to the meeting, which got under way after an hour's delay, Costa Rican Foreign Trade Minister Roberto Rojas said the countries that had not attended the meeting "do not agree" on presenting a counterproposal to the EU's 14 December offer, which is now being studied in San Jose.
