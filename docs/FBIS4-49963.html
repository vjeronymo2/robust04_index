BFN [By Bear Li] [Text] Pretoria, May 11 (CNA) -- The Republic of China [ROC]'s ambassador to South Africa, Loh I-cheng, on Wednesday [11 May] denied reports that President Li Teng-hui had a dinner meeting with Zambia President Frederick Chiluba during which the two discussed establishing diplomatic relations. Several Taipei newspapers carried reports Wednesday that Chiluba was the guest of honor at a dinner given by Li at Loh's residence Tuesday. Both President Li and Chiluba were among the heads of state attending the inauguration of new South African President Nelson Mandela. Loh and Presidential Spokesman Raymond Tai, who is accompanying Li during his trip, said only embassy staff and President Li's entourage were present at the dinner. But Loh said Li did have a meeting with ranking South African officials, including a provincial governor and a member of the new cabinet, at his residence Tuesday.
