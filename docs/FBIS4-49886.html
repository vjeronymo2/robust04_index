BFN [Text] Beijing, May 11 (XINHUA) -- China is to set up a new foreign trade framework by the end of this century, according to Wu Yi, minister of foreign trade and economic cooperation (MOFTEC), here today. She said at an international symposium here this morning that the new framework will be a combination of commodity, fund, technology and labor services based on imports and exports in order to realize a coordinated range of development. She said that the strategy will need the participation of all sectors in charge of foreign economic relations and trade, production, science and technology as well as finance. To this end, the minister proposed the following measures: -- To further its reform of the foreign trade system and try to formulate a pattern of opening in all directions, across several fields and through multi-channels; -- To speed up the integration of various businesses of foreign trade and economic cooperation including foreign trade, use of foreign funds, investing abroad and technical cooperation; -- To take an intensive development road and to continue the strategy of winning through improving quality of products; -- To explore plural markets and form a rational structure of export markets with its focus on the markets in Asian and Pacific area with support from neighboring markets and with a proportionate distribution in developed countries. Wu said that China advocates a balance between imports and exports and will open its domestic market to all trade partners on the basis of the increase of its exports. She also touched on the use of overseas funds, introduction of technologies and foreign trade working skills. She noted that China will gradually open and improve its service trade to meet the development of world trade. Meanwhile, China is to explore the service trade market worldwide and develop its advantageously placed service sector so as to achieve a balance in its service trade.
