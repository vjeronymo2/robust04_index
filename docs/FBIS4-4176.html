BFN [Text] Auckland, April 23 (XINHUA) -- The delegation of the Chinese National People's Congress (NPC) led by vice chairman of its Standing Committee Tian Jiyun left here for Apia, Western Samoa, tonight at the end of its six-day visit to New Zealand. During his stay in New Zealand, Tian Jiyun met with New Zealand Parliament Speaker Peter Tapsell, Prime Minister Jim Bolger and other high-ranking government officials. He also had extensive contacts with New Zealand business people. The NPC delegation also visited some agricultural, forestry, fishery and educational institutes in Christchurch, Wellington, Palmerston North, Hamilton and Auckland. Apart from Western Samoa, the NPC delegation will also go to Papua New Guinea and Australia to complete its four-nation South Pacific tour. Among those seeing the the delegation off at the airport were Chinese Ambassador to New Zealand Li Jinhua, Consul-General in Auckland Chen Qidao and representatives of the Chinese community here.
