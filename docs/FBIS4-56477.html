BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 3 May. Finexpo (Venezuelan Fund for Export Financing) has reported the credits it has granted, through financial institutions in the importing countries, have had a substantial increase in the first three months of the year. It financed 113 export operations between January and March 1994, versus 75 in the same period last year. The amount of credits also increased by more than $11 million from January to March 1993 to almost $22 million during the same period of 1994. The lines of credit have been used primarily by financial institutions in Ecuador (9), Colombia (22), Peru (7), Chile (4), Bolivia (2), Argentina (2), Mexico (3), and Paraguay (2). Finexpo Vice President Policarpo Rodriguez has asserted he is still waiting for the Venezuelan Investment Fund to approve a new line of credit for $90 million, $80 million to be allotted to mid- and long-term operations and $10 million to the Fund for the Special Political Risk Insurance. (Caracas REPORTE DE LA ECONOMIA in Spanish 29 Apr 94 p 20) Norys Aguirre, the newly appointed president of the Deposit Guarantee and Banking Protection Fund (Fogade) has met with economic media representatives for the first time today and told them the public credit law will be the legal instrument to obtain the necessary economic resources to recapitalize the private banks subsidized by Fogade. (Caracas Radio Nacional de Venezuela Canal Clasico Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 28 Apr 94)
