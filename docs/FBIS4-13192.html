BFN [Text] Cairo, 18 Apr (MENA) -- Palestinian and Israeli negotiators resumed their meetings this morning with a meeting of the Security Committee cochaired by Major General 'Abd-al-Razzaq Yahya and General Effin Agmon, deputy chief of the Israeli Army's planning department [name and title as received]. General 'Uzi Dayan, chief of the Israeli Army's planning department, who was heading his country's delegation to the talks, left Cairo last night for a day in Israel. He is expected to return to Cairo tonight to join the talks. According to Palestinian sources, the Legal Affairs Committee will resume its talks today. The committee will examine two working papers, a Palestinian and an Israeli, concerning the legal and judicial powers of the Palestinian authorities in the self-rule areas. Khalid al-Qidrah, a member of the Palestinian negotiating team, yesterday told MENA that the paper Israel presented at the end of the last round is "completely unacceptable." According to Palestinian sources, members of the Civilian Affairs Committee will meet today in subcommittees to discuss water, communications, and other unresolved issues. Experts from both sides will attend the meetings of the subcommittee on water, the same sources said. The subcommittee on communications will mainly discuss the frequencies that the Palestinian authorities will use for police, communications, and other purposes. The two sides agreed on measures concerning the creation of a Palestinian radio and television service, according to the same sources. The Palestinian sources said that a subcommittee emanating from the Legal Affairs Committee will begin working today on the draft of a final agreement. The same sources said that it is too early to speak of progress in the talks as they have just started. Dr. Nabil Sha'th and General Amnon Shahaq, head of the Palestinian and Israeli delegations respectively, held a private meeting this morning in which they discussed issues concerning this round of talks, which will last until 21 April.
