BFN [Text] President Suleyman Demirel today received Bosnia-Herzegovina Vice President Ejup Ganic and Foreign Affairs Minister Irfan Ljubijankic, who are currently visiting Ankara, at the Cankaya Mansion and conferred with them for some time. Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin also attended the meeting. According to the President's Press Center, during the meeting, Ganic briefed Demirel on the agreement reached between the Muslims and the Croats as well as the latest situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Ganic also invited Demirel to Sarajevo. Demirel said that he will undertake such a visit at a suitable time. Ganic also met with Prime Minister Tansu Ciller in Ankara. In a statement after the meeting, Ganic declared that the latest situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina was discussed during his talks with Demirel, Ciller, and other Turkish officials. Ganic concluded by saying that he conveyed to Ciller the gratitude of the Bosnian people for her visit to Sarajevo. The visiting vice president and foreign minister later concluded their contacts in Ankara and proceeded to Istanbul. In a predeparture statement at Ankara's Esenboga Airport, Ganic reported that the talks on the future of his country were very beneficial. He expressed the conviction that Turkey's support to his country will continue with regard to protecting Bosnia-Herzegovina's territorial integrity and finding a peaceful solution to the problem. The Bosnian officials were seen off at Esenboga Airport by Cetin.
