May 19, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: 26-YEAR TERM GIVEN IN GANG EXECUTION

A gang member was sentenced Thursday to 26 years to life in state prison for murdering a man with a shotgun blast fired at point-blank range in the mistaken belief he was a member of a rival gang.

Noe Alvarez, 18, convicted of first-degree murder Jan. 31, was sentenced in Compton Superior Court in the death of Henry Sao, 25.

The district attorney's office said Sao was murdered Nov. 26, 1987, after he went to a beer party thrown by Alvarez and other members of his gang.

Sao knew one of the people at the party, but was not a gang member.

Members of Alvarez's gang accused Sao of being a rival gang member, then got him drunk and took him to the rear of a nearby liquor store. There they killed him with a single shotgun blast to the side of his head.
