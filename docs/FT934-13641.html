931021 FT 21 OCT 93 / US pledge on Bosnia troops The US yesterday made clear to its allies it was still ready to send 'substantial' forces for a peacekeeping operation in Bosnia if a settlement was reached, writes David White, Defence Correspondent. Mr Les Aspin, US defence secretary, also told other Nato defence ministers meeting in Travemunde that Washington was looking at ways of helping the humanitarian aid effort in Bosnia in the absence of a peace agreement. Ministers and Nato officials played down reports of a transatlantic rift following President Bill Clinton's weekend interview criticising the role of Britain and France.
