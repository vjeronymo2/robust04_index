BFN [Text] Ankara, May 12 (A.A.) -- Turkey's foreign debts return payments for January and February 1994 totalled 1.87 billion U.S. dollars, it was announced on Thursday [12 May]. According to the Central Bank's payment balance sheet, 613 million dollars were paid in capital and 474 million dollars for interest. This amount for the same period last year was 581 million dollars in capital and 399 million dollars for interest for a total of 980 million dollars. According to this data, Turkey paid 10.9 percent more in foreign debts compared to the same period last year. In capital payments, 318 million dollars were made in the public sector, 47 million dollars in the trading banks, and 248 in various other sectors. As for interest payments, 292 million dollars were made to the public sector, 149 million dollars in trading banks and the private sector, and 33 million dollars to State Economic Enterprises. Foreign capital in Turkey has decreased by 42 percent compared to the same period last year, falling to 40 million dollars. In 1993 this amount was 69 million dollars.
