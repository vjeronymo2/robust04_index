Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Knin, 22 Feb (TANJUG) -- Milan Martic, the president of the republic of Serb Krajina [RSK], has -- on the basis of his constitutional and legal powers -- relieved Major General Mile Novakovic, the hitherto chief of general staff of the RSK army, of his duty, ISKRA [Krajina news agency] has learned from the office of the president of the republic. Major General Milan Celeketic has been appointed new chief of the general staff of the RSK army. Celeketic was born in 1946 in Mokrin, the municipality of Kikinda, [Vojvodina, Serbia]. He graduated from the military academy and other military schools. He has held a number of responsible posts in the RSK army since the very beginning of the Croatian-Krajina conflict when he unreservedly sided with the Serbian people. He has participated in all the major combat operations in western Slavonia and Posavina. In the course of fighting for the corridor [northern Bosnia] he contributed a great deal to the battles for the liberation of Derventa and Bosanski Brod. Prior to his appointment General Celeketic was in command of the famed RSK army 18th corps. The RSK army commander has been decorated with high military awards for his work.
