BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 28 (KCNA) -- The Literature and Art Press Group of Korea recently brought out a long novel "Victory", a follow-up of the cycle of novels "Immortal History" (postliberation part). The novel gives an epical depiction of the leadership feats, outstanding commandership and noble virtues of the respected leader President Kim Il-song who humbled the arrogant U.S. imperialists who boasted of being the "strongest" in the world and reduced them to the lot of the setting sun by leading the fatherland liberation war, a war of justice for the Korean people, to a shining victory. It also tells about the indomitable fighting spirit of the Korean people and Army. The novel consisting of three parts covers the period from August 1952, when the war was going on at the last stage, to July 1953, till the brilliant victory of the war. The first part deals with mid-1952 when armistice talks were under way at Panmunjom and the Korean war seemed to come to an end through the talks. It gives an emotional representation of the matchless grit, indomitable will and rare military intelligence of President Kim Il-song who inflicted an ignominous defeat, with telling blows, on the U.S. imperialists who were running amuck indiscreetly, not knowing what the Korean people were like. The second part portrays in various aspects the traits of President Kim Il-song who smashed at one stroke Eisenhower's "new offensive" with chuche-based war methods and unique and superb tactics and opened up a decisive phase for victory in the war. The third part vividly shows the wise leadership of President Kim Il-song, an ever-victorious iron-willed brilliant commander and great military strategist, in totally crushing the last-ditch efforts of U.S. imperialist warlikers who dropped germ bombs to cut off the supply route to the front and threatened to use A-bombs, while engaging in massive bombings everyday behind the scene of the ceasefire talks. It also gives a truthful picture of the heroic battles of the brave People's Army soldiers on the hill 351 who remained loyal to the party and the leader, the country and the people. The novel lastly gives scenes of the U.S. imperialists signing the Armistice Agreement at Panmunjom in recognition of their defeat and of a grand military parade held in Pyongyang in celebration of the victory in the war.
