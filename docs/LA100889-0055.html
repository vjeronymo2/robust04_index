October 8, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

PERCEPTIONS OF COLOR

We tell ourselves about the problem, examples are given and we show the results. But no one seems to proffer a solution or even a suggestion of one. Are we hopelessly and inexorably bound toward a nation divided by race? If that is the only answer then what was experienced 25 years ago will be nothing compared to the violence ahead.

The question of race will never be totally eradicated. That's a constant. However, we can ill afford to ignore the alternatives. We must instruct our children and we must not quit in our effort to solve this dilemma.

RODNEY K. BOSWELL, Los Angeles
