IDB PY1904221194 La Paz PRESENCIA in Spanish 9 Apr 94 p 5 PY1904221194 La Paz PRESENCIA Spanish BFN [Text] On 8 April, President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada admitted the country's "major dependency" on foreign power factors that have an impact on various Bolivian fields that cannot change from one day to the next without placing financial stability at risk. In his speech during the main ceremony to mark the 42d anniversary of the national revolution, Sanchez de Lozada said: "Our dependency is on the IMF, the IDB [Inter-American Development Bank], and the U.S. Embassy, which tell us what we can and cannot do. And this is due to our weakness, but I want to tell you that what this government is doing is to go beyond what we are told to do." Before an auditorium full of supporters, the chief of state asked the country for patience by pointing out that the major reforms will take some time to yield results because the government is determined to carry out a third revolution in democracy, "free of violence and shots... but with measures as important as those of the 1952 National Revolution and those that allowed the stabilization of our economy in 1985." Addressing his political allies in the government, he asked them to get rid of susceptibilities and, above all, the idea that the MNR [Nationalist Revolutionary Movement] might disappear some day. He stated: "I am a businessman and my businesses and partners do well and each one has their separate share; the people are our common business now." But he also warned the MNR that it would be disastrous to try to be a hegemonic party again because when this happens "one loses touch with reality and infighting starts, which is something the party already has experienced in the past. Competition is better than hegemony." Regarding the opposition, he said that many parties disappeared because they believed in dogmas and myths and others because they let pragmatism prevail over their principles. He also devoted several minutes of his speech to the reforms his government upholds. He said he intends to consolidate what the MNR started during the six times it came to power since 1952 and project this work into the future. Sanchez de Lozada said: "Capitalization, the peoples' participation, the education system's reform, and sustained development fall within the MNR's much-desired objective to consolidate the national state with social justice." The capitalization project seeks to rescue the public enterprises to prevent them from the same fate as COMIBOL [Mining Corporation of Bolivia]. He added that it is not only an economic measure but also a social one because it is designed to redistribute wealth. He said the peoples' participation project is a 500-year-old debt that is being honored because it will give the people economic and political power. It will allow a fair distribution of wealth according to the population, which means that a person from La Paz will have the same status as a person from Pando and a person from Potosi will be the same as a person from Santa Cruz. Let's hope it will work because the future of new generations is tied to the project's success.
