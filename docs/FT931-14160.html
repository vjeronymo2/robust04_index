930120 FT 20 JAN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Major hints at benefit changes MR JOHN MAJOR yesterday hinted that changes to social security rules aimed at helping the unemployed might be included in the Budget, David Owen writes. With pressure for a large-scale jobs package set to mount in the wake of tomorrow's monthly unemployment figures, Mr Major described remarks made by Mr Jeff Rooker, Labour MP for Birmingham Perry Barr, as 'well worth considering'. His comments came as Mrs Gillian Shephard, employment secretary, played down the prospect of the wholesale adoption of Workfare - obliging people to work in return for benefit - saying it was 'an extremely expensive solution' to Britain's jobs difficulties. Mrs Shephard said on BBC radio that the government needed 'a whole range of measures which keep people in touch with the world of work, because it is productive and gives them hope'. She said she remained 'open-minded' about ways of keeping people 'in touch with the world of work'. Workfare is one area under consideration by a cabinet committee recently set up by Mr Major under Lord Wakeham, leader of the Lords, to suggest schemes to tackle unemployment. A report from Full Employment UK, a policy consultancy, is due to reach the prime minister this week. It estimates that the cost of operating an obligatory scheme to take 400,000 long-term unemployed off the dole queue would be more than Pounds 630m. Mr Rooker, a frontbench education spokesman, urged Mr Major to ease rules on social security benefits so that unemployed people with 'household responsibilities' could 'genuinely' take advantage of opportunities to improve their education and training. Accepting Mr Rooker's point was worth considering, the prime minister said: 'We have looked in the past at some matters related to that; these are the sort of matters that we are prepared to examine.'
