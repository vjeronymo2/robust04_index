Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 24 Feb (TANJUG) -- Radovan Karadzic, president of the Srpska republic, and Fikret Abdic, the president of the autonomous province of western Bosnia, signed an agreement on guaranteeing peace along all borders and lines of separation. The agreement was signed in Belgrade today through the mediation of the President of Serbia Slobodan Milosevic. The agreement was signed on the basis of a mutual commitment to restoring peace throughout the territory of Bosnia-Herzegovina as soon as possible, and in line with the Belgrade declaration of 22 October 1993, a statement says. The text of the agreement is: -- Peace is guaranteed along all borders of the autonomous province of Western Bosnia and the Srpska republic, along the entire line of separation between the forces of the autonomous province of Western Bosnia and the Srpska republic army, without exception. When units of the national defense of the autonomous province of Western Bosnia are deployed on the section of the front from where the Fifth Corps of the Bosnia-Herzegovina army launches military actions against the Srpska republic army, permanent peace will immediately be established in this remaining sector of the front too. -- Liaison officers, responsible for preventing incidents, have been appointed in all sectors on the line of separation between the forces of the autonomous province of Western Bosnia and the Srpska republic army. -- The governments of the autonomous province of Western Bosnia and the Srpska republic will jointly provide conditions and undertake measures to enable communications links, traffic, and commerce between the autonomous province of Western Bosnia and the Srpska republic. -- Borders between the autonomous province of Western Bosnia and the Srpska republic will be defined in line with the Belgrade declaration, and exclusively between the legal bodies of the autonomous province of Western Bosnia and the Srpska republic.
