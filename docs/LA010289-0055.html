January 2, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

Nine jockeys were injured, one critically, in a multiple fall during a race meeting at Inverell in the north of New South Wales state in Australia.

Henry Cameron, 35, was in a critical condition in Brisbane's Prince Charles Hospital after falling during the Inverell Cup Saturday. The 8 other jockeys received minor injuries including 3 broken wrists and 2 broken collar bones.

Cameron suffered chest and neck injuries and a broken right arm, a hospital spokesman said.

Cameron was riding the leading horse when it stumbled and fell, causing a massive pile-up.
