BFN [Text] Jinan, April 14 (XINHUA) -- The first Sino-British adult education seminar was held yesterday in Jinan, capital of east China's Shandong Province. The seminar was co-sponsored by the Jinan-based Shandong Teacher's University (STU) and Nottingham College of Great Britain. More than 80 educators from Britain, the United States, Canada, Australia, the Republic of Korea and China attended the seminar and presented some 60 papers, covering the management of adult education, relations between adult education and economic development, modes of adult education, education for rural population, lifetime education and experience in adult education. The seminar will last for five days. STU started an adult education program in 1956 and has trained about 20,000 adult students over the past 38 years. It has set up a college for adult education, which has an enrollment of more than 7,000 students. In 1987 the university signed a cooperation agreement with Nottingham College in Sino-British adult education. It is a cooperative and friendly conference, said Professor J. E. Thomas, vice-president of Nottingham College. He expressed the hope that every grown-up in the world will have the chance and freedom to read.
