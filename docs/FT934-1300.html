931220 FT 20 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television As so often the desirable programmes all seem to start simultaneously at 9 o'clock. Those who have watched the beginning of Prime Suspect 3 will no doubt wish to see the denouement. This time Lynda La Plante, the onlie begetter of the series, is again the script writer (not so for Prime Suspect 2) and Helen Mirren, as always, plays Detective Chief Inspector Tennison. She has now moved on to the vice squad in London's Soho. The inhabitants of the world in which her new murder hunt takes place include paedophiles, teenage prostitutes, and transvestites (ITV). BBC2 offers a seasonal programme from the two young performers who recently became the first comedians to play Wembley Stadium: Newman And Baddiel Christmas In Pieces. Photo journalist Nick Danziger follows in the 60-year-old footsteps of George Orwell who wrote Down And Out In Paris And London and discovers that, from the doss houses to the dead end jobs, not much has changed (C4).
