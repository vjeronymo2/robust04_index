Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] As we already reported, a Hungarian military delegation has paid a visit to our country, during which Lieutenant General Janos Deak, commander of the Hungarian Army and chief of staff of the Armed Forces of the Hungarian Republic, and Lieutenant General Dumitru Cioflina, state secretary and chief of staff of the Romanian Armed Forces, signed the draft on bilateral cooperation activity in the military domain for 1994. According to a statement made to our reporters by Lieutenant General Dumitru Cioflina, the aforementioned draft was signed in order to continue a number of such activities that were also included by the similar drafts for 1992 and 1993, such as the Open Skies action, professional exchanges of experience, and cooperation between military units in the joint border area. The chief of staff of the Romanian Armed Forces also said that the new elements included in the current draft refer to cooperation between the archive bodies of the two ministries, cooperation with respect to the security principles of [Danube] river shipping and ways to implement those principles, exchanges of experience in the education and training of military cadets, and in the two armies' relations with the civic society. Another element included in the draft refers to the regulation of the status of military cemeteries on the territory of the two countries. According to a news item broadcast yesterday by the [Hungarian] Duna Budapest Television, Lieutenant General Janos Deak stated that on the basis of the Romanian-Hungarian military cooperation program 20 joint actions will be organized. The commander of the Hungarian Army emphasized the fact that the two countries' armies do not have, quote, a mutual enemy image, unquote, about each other.
