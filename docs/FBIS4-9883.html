BFN [Text] (Martin Roddin), representative of the Japanese Mitsubishi Automobile Corporation, has said in Havana that Mitsubishi is studying the possibility of establishing a dealership office in Cuba to sell vehicles and spare parts, in addition to offering maintenance services. According to (Roddin), the Japanese company intends to sell a line of vehicles for tourism and rental service, as well as trucks for the sugar industry. (Roddin) ruled out an immediate decision on this project, however, because these are medium- and long-term prospects. Today, a Japanese company delegation began a series of consultations with Cuban economic circles to evaluate the project. Mitsubishi is the tenth-largest automobile industry in the world, manufacturing a total of 2 million units annually.
