May 25, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

IN THE EYES OF THE WORLD;

ISRAEL: TV IS UNSPARING IN ITS MESSAGE: THE OCCUPYING FORCES ARE THE FORCES OF PERSECUTION.

Avi Pazner, turn on your TV.

Watch a replay of the news videos we saw just the other night, on networks broadcasting across the United States and around the world. We saw three scenes:

Israeli soldiers firing guns at boys who had been throwing rocks at them; an Israeli soldier dragging a woman in a shocking pink blouse down a street as she cried out in resistance, and we saw you.

There you were, Avi Pazner, spokesman for Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir. You were speaking for the nation many of us were taught in synagogues to pray for and sing praises to as the land of milk and honey and truth and justice -- the long-sought home for a long-persecuted people.

We watched as you said: "Israel is acting with utmost restraint."

We watched and listened, but, sadly, our eyes told us we could not believe what we heard. You understand the position we are in, Avi, because from 1974 to 1981 you were based in Washington as the Israeli Embassy's press counselor and you saw the events in the Middle East as we did -- on TV. You saw how powerful pictures tell powerful truths and influence powerful policy-makers. It's that way all around the world now, as the Cable News Network spreads a visual gospel more believable than the most carefully crafted words of the most ingenuous government spokesman.

For decades, most people strongly supported Israel in its fight for survival, because they knew that Israel's cause was just.

Today, Israel's cause remains just. But the eyes of the world are seeing a different story from the one you are telling, Avi Pazner. They are seeing news videos from Israel, night after night, that show clearly that the leaders of this land of truth and justice no longer speak truth, no longer cherish justice.

It used to be, when you were based here, Avi, that television news brought home to us the horrors caused by Palestinian terrorists who attacked and killed Israeli women and children and busloads of tourists, and so on.

Now it's Israelis who are doing the killing. And now the TV cameras are not just getting there in time to show the carnage, as used to happen in those hit-and-run terrorist attacks by Palestinians. Today, TV news is showing us the killers as they kill. To our horror, the killers are Israeli soldiers, occupying forces on the West Bank and Gaza Strip.

The essential truth Israel's right wing Likud Party leaders never understood is this: Occupying forces will always be seen as persecuting forces. That's true whether the occupying forces are storm troopers who are inherently evil or refugees from persecution who are inherently good.

So the policy of the Israeli government cannot succeed if it is the policy of an occupying force, trying to strong-arm peace on to conquered Palestinian people. Policy toward Israel is changing daily -- in Washington, at the United Nations and in capitals around the world.

Israel alone has it in its power to end this sad, downward spiral. Put away the guns and end the occupation. Talk peace -- unconditionally -- and do it now.

So, turn on your TV, Avi Pazner, and you will see that Israel has lost the war of the global television news. Think about that, Avi, and you'll see that you must tell the boss that it's come to this: Israel, alone, can act now to win the peace. Or Israel may end up standing alone.
