Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Asked how he explains the government's double talk on Bosnia-Herzegovina, government spokesman Evangelos Venizelos said the communique issued by the European Union [EU] Council of Ministers, which conferred in Brussels yesterday, does not contradict Greek positions. He said the firm Greek view--not just the government's--that Balkan countries should not get involved in any war remains firm. He added that Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias' handling of things was calm and responsible. [passage omitted] Asked whether the Greek presidency contacted the Serb leadership immediately after the EU foreign ministers communique was issued, Venizelos said Greece has the capability to contact and pressure all sides for a solution. He added: This capability is not limited to Serbia.
