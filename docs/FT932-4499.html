930609 FT 09 JUN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Labour rounds on PM over Iraq probe LABOUR YESTERDAY accused Mr John Major of trying to 'bring down the shutters' on the arms-for-Iraq affair, as it launched its strongest attack yet on the government's refusal to answer questions on the subject, David Owen writes. Mr Robin Cook, shadow trade and industry secretary, charged the prime minister with betraying promises made when the Scott inquiry was set up. 'This is the arrogance of a government that has been in power so long that it has forgotten it is accountable to parliament,' he said. Mr Cook's comments followed a written answer by Mr Major in which he turned down a request to make it government policy to give substantive replies to parliamentary questions on arms-for-Iraq. 'These issues are being investigated by Lord Justice Scott, and it would be wrong for the government to comment on them before publication of his final report,' Mr Major said. The government was 'making all the relevant facts available to the inquiry, and it is right that we should wait for Lord Justice Scott to present his conclusions before commenting publicly.'
