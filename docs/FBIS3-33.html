Continuing earlier signs that Beijing would reward France for its accommodating stance on Taiwan arms sales by expanding Sino- French aviation cooperation, Beijing has recently announced contracts with the French-based European consortium Airbus Industrie (Xinhua, 4,6 March). According to a Xinhua report of 6 March, Airbus has reached an agreement worth "tens of millions of U.S. dollars" with China Aviation Supplies Corporation for production of aircraft parts in a move that reportedly will "increase Airbus' share in the potentially huge China market." Xinhua also reported on 4 March that Chinese airlines have recently announced plans to purchase nine aircraft from Airbus (Xinhua, March 4). Following the restoration of bilateral ties in January, and a French pledge to reverse France's previous policy of selling arms to Taiwan, China awarded a major subway contract to a French firm. Beijing had previously shunned French companies to protest a November 1992 French decision to sell Mirage jet fighters to Taiwan. (AUTHOR: SIMON.
