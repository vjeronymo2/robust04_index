BFN [Text] A source close to the Haitian Army denies the accusations brought against Haitian Police Chief Lieutenant Colonel Joseph Michel Francois. According to the charges, the police chief has allegedly supported a lucrative drug trade between Colombia and the United States, through Haiti. Gabriel Taboana, a former Colombian drug trafficker, asserted before the Drug Trafficking and Terrorism Subcommission that he met Michel Francois during a meeting with Pablo Escoblar in (?Colombia). Former Haitian President Prosper Avril also participated in that meeting. The source, who wishes to remain anonymous, pointed out that Col. Francois' last trip took place in 1981 before... [pauses] after he was commissioned. He went to the United States for proficiency courses in security. Since then, he has never gone abroad. How can Taboana assert that he met Col. Francois in 1984, our source wonders. Remember that upon Col. Francois' return, he was appointed to the Heavy Vehicles Corps of the presidential guard at the National Palace.
