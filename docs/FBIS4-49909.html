BFN [By reporter Wang Defeng (3769 1795 7685)] [Text] Warsaw, 9 May (XINHUA) -- On the morning of 9 May, Polish Prime Minister Waldeman Pawlak met with General Zhang Wannian, member of the Central Military Commission and chief of General Staff of the Chinese People's Liberation Army [PLA], and his party who are currently visiting Poland. Pawlak was pleased with the development of Sino-Polish bilateral cooperative relations in the military field. He said Poland hopes to establish a cooperative partnership with China, particularly in the economic field. According to Pawlak's briefing, Poland has now entered a period of normal development after experiencing acute changes. Located in central Eastern Europe, Poland can serve as a bridge for developing China's contacts with Western European countries. Zhang Wannian said that Chinese leaders and the government attach great importance to developing friendly relations and cooperation with Poland and the Polish Army; and the purpose of his current visit is to deepen understanding and enhance friendship to promote the development of relations between the two countries and two armies. After winding up a trip to Romania, Zhang Wannian and his party arrived in Warsaw on 7 May for this visit.
