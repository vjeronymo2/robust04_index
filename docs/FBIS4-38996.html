BFN [Text] The Moscow prosecutor's office adopted a resolution dropping all charges against the opposition leaders who spoke at the May 9 meeting in Moscow in accordance with Article 70 of the Russian Criminal Code (appeals for violent change of the constitutional system). Moscow's deputy prosecutor Yuriy Andreyechev reported to Interfax that investigators had found prosecutable crimes in the statements of, in particular, former vice president Aleksandr Rutskoy, and the leaders of Labor Russia Viktor Anpilov and of the National Salvation Front Iliya Konstantinov. At the May 9 meeting in Moscow Rutskoy called for a marking of the 50th anniversary of the victory over Nazi Germany with a victory over Yeltsin's "anti-popular regime." Rutskoy said in the prosecutor's office that he did not mean a coercive overthrow of the existing government but called to fight for power by constitutional methods.
