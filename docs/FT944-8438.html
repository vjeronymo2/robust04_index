941119 FT 19 NOV 94 / Weekend Money (Investing For Income): Permanent interest-bearing shares ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Pibs are the closest you can get to holding ordinary shares in a building society. They are fixed interest securities with no redemption date - so your capital cannot be guaranteed. You can realise your investment only by selling them on the open market - with the chance of capital gain or loss. They provide a relatively high yield, as the table shows. This is partly because a building society is theoretically not obliged to pay out if doing so would drive it into loss.
