February 7, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: TV & VIDEO

Eastern Europe's First Private Broadcast: Eastern Europe's first private TV station began broadcasting Tuesday from home-assembled transmitters on top of a student hostel in the city of Wroclaw in western Poland. TV Echo, founded and operated by eight local enthusiasts, beamed its first six-hour broadcast, which included programs on the Solidarity movement, street interviews with locals and reports on price rises, to a potential audience of up to 1 million people. It also relayed two hours of foreign satellite programs. SHAUNA SNOW
