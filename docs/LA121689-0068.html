December 16, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

PRESIDENT BUSH CRITICIZED ON CHINA

"Attention all dictators: Feel free to openly commit mass murder upon innocent citizens of your country with minimal cost to your relations with the U.S."

This is the message implicit in the Bush Administration's response to events in China since the massacre. The message has been underscored even further by Scowcroft's meeting with Deng Xiaoping, the Butcher of Beijing himself. This meeting came about not because of any improvement of the situation within China, but only because the Administration felt that contacts had been suspended long enough.

Evidently, when it comes to mass murder, the President feels that six months is plenty of time after which to let bygones be bygones. I do not know how others in the U.S. react to this, but it makes me sick to my stomach.

DANA HONEYCUTT

San Diego
