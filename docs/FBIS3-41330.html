Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Manuel Cabrera] [Text] Ciudad Juarez, Chihuahua, 22 January--Today, the Office of the Attorney General of the Republic [PGR] and the Public Security Directorate of this municipality intercepted drugs worth $40 million on the international narcotics market. They consisted of 235 kg of pure cocaine and 137 kg of marijuana, which were seized today in two simultaneous operations conducted by the aforementioned forces, according to the Federal Judicial Police first commander, Jose Maria Barragan Barajas. Based on the investigative report submitted to the agency of the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office [MPF], the first confiscation was made at the international bridge crossing to the United States, at Juarez Avenue. It occurred after three persons riding in separate vehicles following one another failed to meet their "contact," presumed to be an official of the neighboring northern country's government who was involved in the drug traffic, and who was to help them cross over to the United States. They decided to return with the alkaloid instead, prompting a pursuit by the Mexican police, who had been notified by the U.S. authorities. Jaime Marquez, aged 18; Luis Miguel Martinez Garcia, 18; and Carlos Tapia Rodriguez, 19, the latter presumably related to a drug trafficking family of the same surname, were intercepted with the 235 kg of pure cocaine. They made their statements at the MPF, subordinate to the PGR. Meanwhile, concurrently, the authorities captured a drug trafficker protecting himself with a reporter's credentials, who had managed to evade the Federal Judicial Police mobile units engaged in surveillance on the state's highways. This report was also made by Commander Jose Maria Barragan Barajas. He explained that the subject under arrest, Juan Carlos Serafin Olvera, had identified himself with credentials of the newspaper DIARIO DE LA MANANA, and with a metal press badge. He was driving a cream-colored Ford Marquis vehicle with license plates DTM-1878, in which 137 kg of marijuana were found. The aforementioned morning paper, which used to circulate in this border area and is not being published at present, was advertised on various parts of the confiscated vehicle. Found in the same car were three press credentials in the name of Salvador Noel Chacon Olguin, displayed in the rearview mirror and on the windshield so that the vehicle would not be stopped. When presented to the news media, Serafin Olvera admitted that, after the closing of the newspaper DIARIO DE LA MANANA, where he had worked seven years as a police photographer, he began carrying marijuana from the Chihuahua capital to this locality. For each trip, he was being paid the sum of $1,000: $500 upon receiving the shipment, and another $500 when he delivered it. The four persons arrested in the two police operations, as well as the cocaine and marijuana and the four luxury vehicles, were placed at the disposal of the acting agent of the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office.
