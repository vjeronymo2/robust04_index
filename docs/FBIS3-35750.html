Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report by Shakhabutdin Zaynutdinov: "Korean Becomes Uzbekistan Vice Premier"] [Text] Tashkent -- The 49-year-old Candidate of Technical Sciences Viktor Chzhen, chairman of the Uzbek Committee for the Management of State Property and Privatization and an ethnic Korean, has been appointed Uzbekistan deputy prime minister under an edict from President Islam Karimov. He becomes the third so-called Russian-speaker in the top echelons of state power in the republic. One of Uzbekistan's vice premiers is the Ukrainian Anatoliy Voznenko, while the deputy chairman of the Supreme Council is the Russian Boris Bugrov.
