Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Aleksandr Krylovich] [Text] Moscow March 17 TASS -- The chances that the third round of talks between North Korea and the United States will be held within the agreed upon time are very small, North Korean Ambassador to Russia Son Song-pil said. Meeting journalists in Moscow on Thursday, the ambassador blamed the American side for thwarting the meeting planned for March 21, saying that not a single obligation it has assumed under the February 25 agreement has been fulfilled. He stressed that North Korea has done its best to observe the conditions of the agreement, particularly it allowed International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] experts into its nuclear facilities and started consultations with representatives of South Korea to prepare for the exchange of envoys. Son Song-pil brushed off accusations that North Korea "treated IAEA exerts unsatisfactorily and did not grant them access to some nuclear facilities." He described such allegations as "a fabrication of the Western mass media." The ambassador pointed out that recently the North Korean Ministry of Nuclear Power Engineering received a telegram from the IAEA deputy director which claimed that North Korea had failed to provide normal conditions for the operation of IAEA experts and overpriced materials they used for analyses. "From the very first day of their stay in North Korea all necessary conditions were created for their work," he said. However, "their demands were too high and corresponded to a large-scale rather than limited nuclear inspection which was approved by North Korea," the ambassador continued. The question of carrying out comprehensive inspection was due to be discussed at the upcoming round of U.S.-North Korean talks, he said. Son Song-pil stressed that the fate of the third round of talks depends on the U.S. Administration which should abandon "unreasonable conditions" and strictly observe the agreements reached by the sides at their February meeting.
