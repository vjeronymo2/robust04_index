BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 22 KYODO -- Japan's next coalition government will not create a new cabinet post in charge of coordinating economic affairs, a senior Trade Ministry official said Friday (922 April]. The new cabinet expected to be inaugurated Monday under Tsutomu Hata, currently foreign minister in the government of outgoing Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, will drop a plan to have an overall economics minister, said the official at the International Trade and Industry Ministry (MITI), who declined to be identified. The creation of the post was suggested as a way to facilitate formulation of stimulus and other economic packages and help resolve Japan's trade difficulties with the United States.
