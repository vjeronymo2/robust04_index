Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The nation's top 30 business groups have selected their prime industries and companies which they will nurture as world-top enterprises in order to enhance their international competitiveness. They submitted the lists of their choices to the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy yesterday, the government-set deadline. The selection of core industrial sectors and companies by the 30 largest conglomerates, which was conceived by the government last July, is aimed at business "specialization" as a means to strengthen the competitiveness of Korean products in international markets. As permitted in the guideline, the top 10 of the 30 groups except for Kia chose three prime industries each, while Kia and the remaining 20 selected two core industries each. The top 10 are Hyundai, Samsung, Daewoo, Lucky-Goldstar, Sunkyong, Hanjin, Ssangyong, Kia, Hanhwa and Lotte. Specifically, Hyundai decided on electronics, automobiles and energy; Samsung electronics, machinery and petrochemicals; Daewoo machinery, automobiles and distribution; Lucky-Goldstar electronics, petrochemicals and energy, and Sunkyong energy, petrochemicals and distribution. Hanjin chose machinery, distribution and construction; Ssangyong energy, non-metals and automobiles; Kia automobiles and steel; Hanhwa energy, petrochemicals and distribution, and Lotte food & beverages, petrochemicals and distribution. The 20 other groups are Kumho, Daelim, Doosan, Dong-Ah, Hanjin, Hyosung, Dongkuk Steel, Sammi, Halla, Tongyang, Kolon, Jinro, Dongbu, Kohap, Kukdong, Woosung, Haitai, Byucksan, Miwon and Samyang. Kumho selected petrochemicals and distribution; Daelim construction and non-metals; Doosan food & beverages and construction; Dong-Ah construction and distribution; Hanil textiles, and construction; Hyosung petrochemicals and electronics, Dongkuk Steel distribution and steel; Sammi distribution and steel; Halla automobiles and machinery, and Tongyang electronics and distribution. Kolon picked petrochemicals and distribution; Jinro food & beverages and distribution; Dongbu steel and distribution; Kohap petrochemicals and distribution; Kukdong construction and non- metals; Woosung construction and distribution; Haitai food & beverages and distribution; Byucksan non-metals and construction; Miwon food & beverages and petrochemicals, and Samyang food & beverages and petrochemicals. The 30 top business groups were implicitly permitted to pick one or two core companies in each selected prime industrial line although there is no regulation on that matter in the guideline. Of the 10 largest conglomerates, Hyundai picked five including Hyundai Electronics and Hyundai Motor; Samsung five including Samsung Electronics and Samsung General Chemicals; Daewoo four including Daewoo Shipbuilding & Heavy Machinery and Daewoo Motor; Lucky-Goldstar six including Goldstar and Lucky, and Sunkyong six including Yukong and Sunkyong. Selected core industrial lines will be free of the government's strict control on borrowing fresh bank loans by the top 30 business groups.
