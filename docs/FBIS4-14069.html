BFN [Text] The government has accepted all the recommendations of the National Human Rights Commission regarding the Bijbiara firing incident in Jammu and Kashmir, in which 31 persons were killed on the 22d of October last. The commission's recommendations included continuing of disciplinary proceedings and prosecution of 14 BSF [Border Security Force] personnel involved in the incident, payment of interim compensation to the victims, and a thorough review of deployment of BSF units in civilian areas. In its compliance report, the government informed the commission that BSF authorities have been instructed to complete the record of evidence, proceedings against the personnel. In addition, the investigation by the local police has been completed and steps are being taken to file a charge-sheet in a civil court. Regarding compensation, the state government has disbursed ex-gratia relief of 100,000 rupees each to the next of kin of the 31 civilians who were killed in the incident.
