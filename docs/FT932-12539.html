930428 FT 28 APR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Major and Smith clash on testing PRIME MINISTER John Major yesterday rejected Labour demands to suspend this year's school tests, while appearing to acknowledge that some of the concerns voiced by 'ordinary teachers' were justified, David Owen writes. Challenged repeatedly by Mr John Smith, the Labour leader, Mr Major emphasised the importance ministers attached to the principle of testing and denounced teachers who thought it appropriate to take industrial action at the expense of this year's exams for seven and 14-year-olds. But he said the government was 'considering and responding to' the concerns of teachers who simply wanted less bureaucracy. Mr Smith urged the government to 'for once listen' to the considered judgement of the whole teaching profession and to suspend the tests. He called on Mr John Patten, the education secretary, to recognise he had lost the argument. He said: 'This increasingly bitter dispute is not about the principle of sensible testing but about the dogmatic insistence of an arrogant education secretary that his view must prevail against that of governors, teachers and parents.'
