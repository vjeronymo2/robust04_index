BFN [Text] Sarajevo, April 19 (TANJUG) - Representatives of the Bosnian Serb Republic and the United Nations Protection Force (UNPROFOR) reached a temporary agreement on Gorazde in Pale late on Tuesday [19 April] evening, the Bosnian Serb news agency SRNA reported. "The memorandum on temporary arrangements for Gorazde" calls for an end to combat actions around the town and the deployment of two UNPROFOR platoons with around 100 men. "Serbs agree that they immediately cease all combat activities in an area 3 km from central Gorazde on the left bank of the Drina, unless they are attacked," said the memorandum which was signed by Bosnian Serb Assembly President Momcilo Krajisnik and UNPROFOR Civil administration Head Victor Andreyev.
