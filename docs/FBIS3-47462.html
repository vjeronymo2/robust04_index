Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, January 12 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- An information came from the competent sources near to the Mongolian Government that the Mongolian prime minister is going within the next few days to make decisive changes in his government. According to them, many ministries will be abolished, re-organized on the basis of merging with others. This project aims at radical changes in the government structure, reduction of expenses on maintaining bureaucratic apparatus, and the saved means will be directed to the increase of wages and pays of state servicemen. According to these sources, this project is actually solved it remains only to wait for a decision of the parliament, which will consider this project. P. Jasray has presented his project at the government sitting, and also to the steering council of the ruling Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party. Running across resistance in both cases, the prime minister gave to understand that he is going to realize his project within the framework of his powers granted by the Constitution. An issue about expediency of re-organization of the government, reduction of the number of ministries is raised in Mongolia not for the first time. This issue has been raised not once by the Mongolian president, the oppositional forces, and also by independent economists.
