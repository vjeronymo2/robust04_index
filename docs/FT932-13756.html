930422 FT 22 APR 93 / TUC jobs warning PEOPLE under the age of 20 have been worst affected by rising unemployment, a report from the Trades Union Congress claimed yesterday. The report, published on the eve of today's unemployment figures, draws on the government's Labour Force Survey which, unlike official totals, includes people without work as well as those claiming benefits and available for work. The TUC report shows the unemployment rate for people under 20 is nearly 20 per cent, compared with slightly more than 7 per cent for those aged 35 and over. The number of 16-to-19-year-olds in work fell 25 per cent, compared with 2 per cent for those over 25. Youth Training, the scheme for 16-to-19-year-olds, took 384,000 in 1989-90 but only 293,000 in 1991-92, a 24 per cent fall. Only one in three YT leavers went straight to a job. Mr Norman Willis, TUC general secretary, said: 'These findings emphasise the need for the government to make full employment a clear priority for economic policy and to introduce an emergency work programme.'
