Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Paris, Jan 23 (AFP) -- Andre Rossinot, France's minister of civil service and the mayor of Nancy, was elected president of the centre-right Radical Party Sunday [23 January], a post he already headed in the 1980s. A veteran French politician who was close to the conservative former prime minister Raymond Barre, Rossinot has been a deputy for the center-right Union for French Democracy (UDF), of which the Radicals are a component, in the Meurthe-et-Moselle region since 1978. He was a member of the Radical Party's Executive Committee in 1978 and its secretary general in 1979. He became president of the party between 1983-88. He has been mayor of the northeastern city of Nancy since 1983.
