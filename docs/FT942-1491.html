940624 FT 24 JUN 94 / Jobs warning to the Netherlands The next Dutch government must seize the opportunity of a mild economic recovery to reform the Netherlands' rigid labour market, the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development said in its survey published yesterday. The country's labour market is 'unquestionably the weak spot of an otherwise fundamentally healthy economy', according to the OECD. It said incentives to find work were low in the Netherlands, reflecting the traditional generosity of the welfare state system. Other problems identified by the OECD were high Dutch labour costs, stiff job protection laws and wage-setting by economic sector which prevents prospective employees from 'bidding' for lower wages. 'On balance, employment protection legislation seems to be detrimental to employment,' it said. The OECD, noting that growth of gross domestic product is projected to rise to 2.75 per cent next year, said: 'The challenge to the next government is to seize this opportunity, relying on the many strengths of the Dutch economy and society, and to move towards major reforms now, rather than only if and when it is faced with the threat of an impending crisis.'
