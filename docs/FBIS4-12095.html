BFN [Excerpts] His Excellency Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council in fraternal Yemen, left the country this morning at the end of a two-day visit to the sultanate. During the visit, his excellency the Yemeni leader held talks with His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id on fraternal relations, and ways to boost bilateral cooperation to serve and safeguard the interests of the two fraternal people. The discussions also dealt with the developments in the Republic of Yemen, and the sultanate's role in approximating viewpoints among the parties to the conflict in a manner that will safeguard the Republic of Yemen as a united entity, and the security and stability of the fraternal Yemeni people and all the people in the region. The talks also dealt with issues of mutual interest and the developments on the regional and international level. [passage omitted] His Excellency Lt. Gen 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih said he is pleased with the results of his visit to the sultanate and with the positive talks he held with His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id and which were an opportunity to hold consultations on all that would enhance the fraternal and cordial relations between the two countries. In a statement to the Oman News Agency, his excellency said: During this visit, we discussed the developments of the political crisis in our country in light of the good efforts his majesty the sultan is making in addition to many issues which are of interest to our two fraternal countries, the region, and the Arab nation. His excellency said: I take this opportunity to reiterate thanks and appreciation in my name and on behalf of the Yemeni leadership, government, and people to the Sultanate of Oman -- leader, government, and people for the good offices it has been making to help us in Yemen overcome the crisis and the challenges it poses and safeguard the march of unity and stability in our country that will contribute to enhancing security, stability, and peace in the region. Concluding his statement, his excellency said his country is eager to enhance relations of amity, cooperation, and coordination with the Sultanate of Oman because the eternal and solid bonds that exist between the two countries dictate that we preserve the ties of brotherhood and good neighborliness and work for constantly expanding the scopes of understanding and integration in the interest of both our people and Arab nation.
