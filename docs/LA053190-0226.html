May 31, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

NON-RESIDENTIAL BUILDING PERMIT VALUATIONS

The value of non-residential building in the county is going south. During this year's first quarter, $284.2 million worth or such permits were issued, 23% below the total for the comparable period last year. The 1990 January through March valuations were not only less than in 1989; this was the lowest-valued first quarter in any of the last five years.

Orange County was not alone in this situation. Each of the surrounding counties also came in below last year's first three months, with the exception of San Bernardino County. Valuations there increased 11%.

Despite the overall decline, there were increases in three of the five major categories of building: Permits for industrial buildings increased 90% over last year's first three months; stores and mercantile buildings were up 6%, and hotels/motels 51%. Decreases in office buildings and alterations/additions dragged the overall total lower.
