Language: English Article Type:BFN [By staff reporters] [Text] The British side of the Joint Liaison Group's airport committee yesterday presented China with the Hong Kong Government's latest Chek Lap Kok funding proposal. British officials are hoping a meeting can be held and an agreement reached before the end of the month. They also hope the issue will not be complicated by the Government's tabling of the remaining part of the 1994/95 electoral bill in the Legislative Council. The Chinese State Council's Hong Kong and Macao Affairs Office was also given an outline of the proposal by the British Ambassador to China, Sir Robin McLaren. "The new offer is a very good one and ought to be acceptable to the Chinese side," Mr McLaren said. Zhang Junsheng, a vice director of the Hong Kong branch of Xinhua (the New China News Agency), said the Chinese side needed to study the new offer before making any comment. But Mr Zhang criticised Governor Chris Patten for saying the new financing package was an excellent Lunar New Year present. "This is not a present he gives us. It belongs to Hong Kong. All (we) hope is just that he will not misspend the money," Mr Zhang said. Mr Patten yesterday pledged that the Government would not reduce spending on public projects in order to pay for the airport. Meanwhile, the Liberals' airport spokesman, Steven Poon Kwok-lim, said yesterday the party would support the latest airport financing proposal. Mr Poon said, under the proposal, the debt-equity ratio would be even lower than their suggested 1:1, much lower than other projects. It is understood that the Executive Council on Tuesday endorsed the fourth financing proposal, which will see the Government's injection to the airport project increase by $15 billion to $60 billion.
