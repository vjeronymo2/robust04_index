BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Marina Barinova] [Text] Moscow April 14 TASS -- "There is no such compound as `red mercury' in nature, it is not used in the ministry's atomic energy enterprises, and is not used in the construction of nuclear weapons", ITAR-TASS was told on Thursday by Georgiy Kaurov, head of the public relations department of the Russian Atomic Ministry. He said that scientists and specialists from the Atomic Ministry had already made similar statements many times. Apparently, it would not hurt to make one more: Russia does carry out research in the field of thermonuclear synthesis of heavy hydrogen isotopes, however, such research has absolutely nothing in common with "red mercury". Kaurov announced that in the ministry's opinion, Wednesday's report by the REUTER news agency that Russia has a "miniature neutron bomb", developed by Russian scientists on the basis of "red mercury", and the English television film about the "Russians' discovery" are cheap efforts to create a sensation and have political undertones.
