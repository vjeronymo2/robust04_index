Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Received via Nikkei Telecom database] [Text] On 24 December, a senior Foreign Ministry official pointed out that "fundamentally, the United States will be making demands on Japan at the Japanese-U.S. framework talks because Japan has a huge trade surplus with the United States." At the same time, he hinted at the possibility that Japan may make more compromises than the United States, saying, "It is necessary for Japan to make further compromises (to reach agreement at the framework talks)."
