BFN [Text] The Australian Wheat Board has been forced to drop its estimates for Australian standard white and hard wheat during the past two months. It has been triggered by subsidy pressures from market competitors such as the United States. The board now estimates wheat delivered to the 1993-94 pools will return between $2 and 3 less than last year. Australian Wheat Board managing director, John Lawrenson, said the United States subsidy is hurting Australian sales program.
