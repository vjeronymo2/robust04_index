BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Chile monitored through 9 June. On 7 June the Central Bank reported that the Chilean trade balance registered a $334.2 million surplus between 1 January to 15 May, with exports at $3.9001 billion and imports at $3.5659 billion. This figure differs from the $62.2 million deficit registered during the same period in 1993, and confirms Chile's rebound in exports, which had been affected in 1993 by the drop in the prices of copper, cellulose, fishmeal, and fruit, among other products. Economic authorities said that the increase in the price of copper greatly contributed to improving the trade balance. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1753 GMT 7 Jun 94) The Andean Board, headquartered in Lima, reported that the Andean Group has become a major Chilean trade partner in the past few years. Sales to these countries went from $525 million in 1992 to $585 million in 1993. However, exports to Japan dropped from $1.714 billion to $1.543 billion; to the United States from $1.58 billion to $1.532 billion; and to Brazil from $451 million to $424 million. During this period Chile exported to the Andean area a greater number of products than any other country, with the number of various products rising from 2,456 to 2,509. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1638 GMT 3 Jun 94) The Chilean Foreign Ministry Economic Department has reported that Chile's foreign investments since 1990 amount to $3.486 billion, out of which a total of $2.373 billion was invested in Argentina, $636 million in Peru; $192.9 million in Brazil; and $69 million in Venezuela. Investments were also made in Panama, with $39.9 million; Cuba, $32.3 million; Ecuador, $16.4 million; Bolivia, $7.5 million; Colombia, $6.9 million; Uruguay, $2.7 million; Costa Rica, $1 million; and Mexico, $750,000. A total of 105 companies have invested abroad. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1653 GMT 3 Jun 94)
