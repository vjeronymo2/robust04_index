BFN [Text] The Consultative Council, meeting today under the chairmanship of Speaker Dr. Mustafa Kamal Hilmi, approved a bill on the economic and social development plan for 1994/1995, representing the third year of the current five-year plan. The Council then referred the plan to the president. The People's Assembly speaker and the prime minister were also informed. Dr. Kamal al-Janzuri, deputy prime minister and minister of planning, told the Consultative Council that the planned growth rate for the 1994/1995 economic and social development plan is 4.5 percent compared with 3.6 percent in 1993 and 2.5 percent in 1992. The planning minister pointed out that this year's plan involves the expenditure of 7 billion pounds from the country's own resources, 1 billion pounds in the form of foreign grants, and 3 billion pounds from the National Investment Bank, the last figure representing actual savings.
