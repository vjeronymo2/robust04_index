940125 FT 25 JAN 94 / International Company News: Deficit for Reynolds Metals Reynolds Metals company, the US aluminium processor, suffered a fourth-quarter operating loss of Dollars 19.1m, or 31 cents a share, before special charges, despite improvement in western world aluminium demand and year-on-year gains in its own aluminium shipments. The loss compares with an operating profit of Dollars 2.9m, or 5 cents a share, in last year's fourth quarter, also before special charges. Including charges, the loss was Dollars 238.6m, or Dollars 3.98 a share, compared with a loss of Dollars 155m, or Dollars 2.55 a share. Sales for the quarter were Dollars 1.35bn, almost unchanged from a year earlier. For the year, operating losses were Dollars 94.6m, or Dollars 1.58 a share, before charges, on revenues of Dollars 5.29bn, compared with income before charges of Dollars 44.6m, or 75 cents a share, on revenues of Dollars 5.66bn in 1992.
