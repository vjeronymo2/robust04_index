Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 8 (KCNA) -- Nelson Chavez, chairman, and Humberto Ortiz, coordinator, of the Ecuadorian Committee for Supporting Korea's Independent and Peaceful Reunification; Milton Burbano, chairman of the Ecuadorian Cultural Institute for the study of the chuche idea; Juan Carlos Camacho, Oswaldo Larriva, and Diego Delgado, parliamentarians of Ecuador; sent a letter to the United Nations secretary-general on December 29 over the situation on the Korean peninsula. The letter said: "The government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea demanded that the Armistice Agreement be replaced with a peace agreement and that the `U.N. Command' in South Korea be dissolved. But the United States and South Korea answered this with nuclear war exercises and with a grave provocation by alleging that the DPRK was using its peaceful nuclear facilities for the `production of nuclear weapons.'" Noting that the United Nations is under moral obligations to see that the DPRK and the United States sign a peace agreement and contribute to the independent and peaceful reunification of Korea, the letter urged the UN secretary-general to show enough response to this end.
