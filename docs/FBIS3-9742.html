Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES 24/3/1994 -- Both countries' wish to develop the traditional Romanian-Turkish relations was highlighted during the formal interview in Bucharest on 24 March between President Suleyman Demirel of Turkey and Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu. During the first minutes of the interview, when the press was allowed to attend, Premier Vacaroiu referred to the constant support Turkey has given Romania in this difficult period. He said the bilateral trade topped last year the 400 million dollar mark, which however did not reflect the two countries' real potential. The same was said by the Turkish president, who showed the trade ought to reach one billion dollars. President Demirel also assessed that Romania and Turkey could help develop a multilateral cooperation in the Black Sea area and achieving stability in the Balkans. He also referred to the need to intensify the links between the two countries' businessmen, and their collaboration in third markets. "It is up to us to open the way," he added.
