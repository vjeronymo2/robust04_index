Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Uzbekistan president, Mr. Islam A. Karimov, who arrived on a three-days visit to India today has said that the Constitution of his country provides for a secular state. During his one-hour long talks with the president, Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma, Mr. Karimov said that Tashkent attaches great importance to relations with India. It also appreciates New Delhi's support to the reconstruction of his country. He said India should play a greater role in both political and economic spheres in the Central Asian region. Both the leaders have condemned religious fundamentalism. Dr. Sharma recalled the historical relations between the two countries. He said Tashkent has become emotionally a part of Indian history with the former prime minister, Lal Bahadur Shastri, passing away in that city. Mr. Karimov informed Dr. Sharma that the room occupied by Lal Bahadur Shastri has been converted into a museum.
