Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] The opposition of the Belarusian Popular Front in the Supreme Soviet of the Republic of Belarus has made a statement that the voting on certain articles of the new Constitution was conducted in a manner that infringed on voting procedures. In particular, according to the opposition, the continuity of voting was broken. The Supreme Soviet also infringed on the procedures by refusing to vote on an alternative version of the draft law on the political system, which was proposed by the opposition. "This attempt to introduce presidential rule in Belarus in an illegal way cannot be tolerated by us," stated the opposition deputies (40 persons). "It leads to a dictatorship." By the way, the opposition did not participate [passage indistinct]. The former chairman of the Belarusian parliament, Stanislav Shushkevich, called the roll-call voting procedure with regard to constitutional articles a deception of the people.
