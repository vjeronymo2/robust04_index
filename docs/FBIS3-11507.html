Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Report by Patricia Gomes from Brasilia] [Text] The Rio Group today discussed in Brasilia the need to ensure the developing countries' access to high technology and the right to transfer it. The 13 Rio Group foreign ministers will meet in Sao Paulo on 22 and 23 April with a group of European Union foreign ministers. The meeting's objective will be to discuss proposals presented in the first Rio Group meeting this year, which ended today in Brasilia. In the meeting, the foreign ministers discussed topics such as the fight against poverty, human rights, sustainable development, and science and technology exchange. According to Foreign Minister Celso Amorim, the meeting helped the Rio Group member countries prepare for the presidential and chiefs of state meeting to be held in September. The foreign ministers also analyzed the letter received from U.S. President Bill Clinton asking for a hemispheric conference to discuss poverty and misery in the American continent. Minister Amorim also said the Rio Group member countries are concerned about the situation in Suriname, where armed groups conducted attacks a few days ago.
