931124 FT 24 NOV 93 / Airline managers on fraud charge The former general sales manager of Britannia Airways is to appear in court charged with conspiring to defraud the company of Pounds 800,000. Mr Paul Massey is charged with Mr Alan Curtis, Britannia's former manager of charter sales, and Henry Wolff, who ran a chartering company. The fraud allegedly involved secret commission payments channelled to Swiss bank accounts. The charter airline has recovered most of the money. Mr Massey, of Earlsfield, south-west London, Mr Curtis, of Bournemouth, and Mr Wolff, of Pinner, Middlesex, will appear before London's Bow Street magistrates on December 6.
