931111 FT 11 NOV 93 / MPs aggrieved by aerospace response THE GOVERNMENT'S reply to a report by the cross-party Commons committee on the British aerospace industry was 'disappointing' and had 'important deficiencies', the committee said yesterday. The government did not go 'far enough to address our recommendations and the evidence and arguments on which they are based'. The committee had taken evidence from most of the UK aerospace industry, including Lord Weinstock, managing director of GEC, and Mr Dick Evans, chief executive of British Aerospace. Mr Richard Caborn, chairman of the select committee, said, 'It's less than adequate, especially given the government's objective to improve the manufacturing base.'
