Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- Julius Brocka was today appointed the new Slovak minister for employment and social affairs. Brocka was proposed by the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH). He was born on January 1, 1957 and studied at the Engineering Faculty of the Slovak Technical University in Bratislava. He worked as a designer, later becoming head designer in the Stavoprojekt company in Trnava, west Slovakia. After November 1989 he was one of the founders of the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) in the Trnava district, and has been a KDH deputy since June 1990. Until June 1992 he was chairman of the KDH deputies' group, and is now a member of parliament's committee for the national economy and the budget. As a deputy, his interests lie in the social field and in economics. In the KDH he is responsible for the social field and is the creator of the party's social program.
