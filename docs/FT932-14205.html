930420 FT 20 APR 93 / UK Company News: Pay rise for Simon chief SIMON Engineering, which last year reported its second successive year of falling profits and cut its dividend, awarded its chief executive Mr Brian Kemp a 7 per cent pay rise. According to the company's annual report, released yesterday, Mr Kemp's emoluments excluding pension contributions increased nearly Pounds 11,000 to Pounds 167,265 in the year to December 31. During the same period, Simon's pre-tax profits fell from Pounds 18.3m to Pounds 5.3m, earnings fell from 12.5p to 2.6p and dividends fell from 15.7p to 5p. Mr Kemp, 49, has a service contract with a three-year notice period. In the annual review the company said that 1,000 people had been made redundant in the last year.
