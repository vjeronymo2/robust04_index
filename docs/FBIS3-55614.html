Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chairman of the Belarusian parliament Stanislav Shushkevich put off his visit to Moscow until January 12. He had earlier agreed with Russian President Boris Yeltsin that the visit would take place on January 11. Valeriy Tsepkalo, an adviser of the head of parliament, told INTERFAX that Shushkevich needed more time to analyze the results of the Moscow visit by a Belarusian government team headed by Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich. Shushkevich will go to Moscow by train, which will leave Minsk late on January 11 and arrive in Moscow early next morning. Vasily Vorobyov, Shushkevich's press secretary, told INTERFAX that Shushkevich would meet with Yeltsin alone behind closed doors in the Kremlin on January 12. Shushkevich will leave for Minsk on the same day and give a news conference there on January 13 about the results of his meeting with the Russian president.
