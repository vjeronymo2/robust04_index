Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yulia Kantor] [Text] Moscow January 18 TASS -- The resignation of First Deputy Premier Yegor Gaydar means that communists and other opposition blocs will now cooperate with the government of Viktor Chernomyrdin, according to Sergey Baburin, the leader of the hard-line "Russian Path" parliamentary group and a staunch opponent of President Boris Yeltsin. "I believe the resignation of Yegor Gaydar signals the end of the transfer of the (pro-Yeltsin) Russia's Choice bloc into opposition", Baburin told TASS on Tuesday. Now the communists and other opposition factions in parliament will "take under patronage Chernomyrdin and his government", he said.
