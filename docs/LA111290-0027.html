November 12, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: POP/ROCK

Canadian Punkers Not Obscene: A Canadian jury has found two records by the punk rock group DayGlo Abortions not obscene. The jury ruled Thursday that the albums, "Feed Us a Fetus" and "Here Today Guano Tomorrow" -- which are laced with four-letter words and describe sex, satanism, bestiality, rape, murder and suicide -- are not obscene as charged under Canada's criminal code. Band members, with names such as Cretin, Couch Potato and Jesus Bonehead, contend that the songs are parodies intended to shock. CLAUDIA PUIG
