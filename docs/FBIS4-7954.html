BFN [Text] The issue of Romania's participation in the Partnership for Peace program and the prospects for complete integration of our country into Euro-Atlantic structures were issues discussed today by the supreme commander of the allied forces in Europe, U.S. General George Joulwan, during his meeting with Romanian President Ion Iliescu, Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu, and other officials and government members. After the reception at Cotroceni Palace, the guest stated to the press that Romania's association to the North Atlantic structures is a political decision that depends on the wishes of Romania and NATO. During his talks with the Romanian prime minister, the U.S. general assured Vacaroiu that NATO clearly wishes to assist Romania. He said that the visit to Romania confirmed his confidence in fruitful cooperation in the military area and this could set a positive example for the entire region. The supreme commander of the allied forces in Europe affirmed that all details of the programs of cooperation between Romania and NATO will be completed shortly.
