940301 FT 01 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Dixon Motors Dixon Motors, the car dealer which came to the USM last year after a reversal into Plateau Mining, announced an increase in pro forma pre-tax profits for the year ended December 31 from Pounds 370,000 to Pounds 1.33m. Turnover increased by 35.8 per cent from Pounds 46.2m to Pounds 64m, with 7,406 units sold against 5,346 last time. Vehicle sales contributed 55.7 per cent of total gross profit. Earnings per share for the year came out at 9.1p, against 10.4p for the 15 months to December 31 1993 and losses of 28.7p for the year to September 30 1992, when there were restructuring costs of Pounds 10.4m. There is a recommended final dividend of 1.5p, in line with forecasts at the time of the acquisition of Dixon Motor Holdings, making a total of 2.25p.
