Language: German Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Mili's Explosive Dance on the Ice"] [Text] According to findings by the Federal Office of Criminal Investigations (BKA) in Wiesbaden, the feminist extremist group "Red Zora," which was responsible for several bomb and arson attacks in 1987, is planning new acts of violence. An internal BKA report quotes a strategy paper by "Red Zora." It announces "illegal militant organizing" to "destroy patriarchal power." In a 38-page underground brochure entitled "Mili's Dance on the Ice," "Red Zora," which went underground in August 1988, is now again calling for attacks and punishment actions against persons and institutions that it holds responsible for the oppression of women, as the BKA analysis says. In 1978, "Red Zora" attracted attention for the first time with attacks on sex shops. In 1984, there followed attacks on genetic laboratories and the Federal Constitutional Court. In 1987, "Red Zora," which, according to the chief federal prosecutor's office, is an underground organization of the "Red Cells," set fire to the mother company of the Adler clothing company in Haibach near Aschaffenburg and another seven subsidiaries of the company. At that time, the textile corporation was accused by "Red Zora" of exploiting women in South Korea.
