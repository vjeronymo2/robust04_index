BFN [Text] Tegucigalpa, 27 Apr (NOTIMEX) -- Military sources announced today that the Honduran Armed Forces will deactivate four battalions in June, as part of the government's military reduction program. Each of the deactivated military units has approximately 800 soldiers, according to the sources quoted by the private television "Today" newscast. The newscast did not disclose the source. Sources close to the presidential palace said that the Honduran Armed Forces will be reduced from 24,000 to 12,000 troops, an amount similar to Nicaragua's Armed Forces. Reduction of the Honduran military will follow once the demilitarization in Nicaragua and El Salvador is completed, in accordance with these neighboring countries' peace agreements. General Luis Alonso Discus Elvir, commander in chief of the Armed Forces, said recently that the Honduran Army will reduce operating costs by 10 percent.
