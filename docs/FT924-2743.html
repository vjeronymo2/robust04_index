921211 FT 11 DEC 92 / Britain rejoins legislative push Britain has rejoined the leading group in the final sprint to put single market legislation into place before January 1, writes Andrew Hill in Brussels. Figures released yesterday will spare Britain potential embarrassment at the Edinburgh summit. They show that the UK is no longer among the EC's legislative laggards. On the other hand, Italy and Belgium are still among the slowest to pass single market legislation through their parliaments.
