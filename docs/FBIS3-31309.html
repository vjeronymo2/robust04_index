Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] In connection with the faulty organization of the meetings that were held in Bucharest and Targu Jiu on 11 and 16 February 1994, with incidents in which 15 and seven policemen were injured, respectively, and during which attempts were made to enter by force the headquarters of the Ministry of Transportation, the Prefecture of Gorj County, and the Targu Jiu Lignite Public Corporation, the Ministry of Interior specifies as follows: It respects the constitutional right on the freedom of meetings and participates, through the units under its jurisdiction, in holding such meetings under conditions of complete order in accordance with the provisions of Law No. 60 of 1991 regarding the organization and holding of public meetings. It expresses its concern and disapproves of the degeneration of such public demonstrations directed against the forces of order and the normal functioning of public institutions. It emphasizes that, in accordance with the provisions of Law No. 60 of 1991, the organizers are duty-bound to take all necessary measures to ensure the progress of such public meetings in a peaceful and civilized way. Any violation of the aforesaid law will entail fines or legal punishment, as the case may be, and obliges the forces of the Ministry of Interior to intervene to reestablish order and public peace and quiet, and to ensure the supremacy of the law, an essential requirement of consolidating the rule of law in Romania. This communique was faxed us by the Press Service of the Ministry of Interior.
