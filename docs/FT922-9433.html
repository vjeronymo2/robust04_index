920507 FT 07 MAY 92 / Observer: Hard to swallow Age has its compensations - at least for wrinklies planning to holiday in the remoteness of New Guinea. They're less likely to end up on the locals' menu, according to an ex-cannibal now called Derek, just brought to public view by anthropological researchers in the Indonesian half of the huge island. 'Old ones are tough. Young men and women taste better,' the Christian convert reminisced when asked how his fellow humans had gone down. 'And babies taste like fish,' he added. 'The flesh is very soft.' But the last bit was just spicing things up for the occasion, one of the researchers said. 'There has been no documented evidence of babies being eaten.'
