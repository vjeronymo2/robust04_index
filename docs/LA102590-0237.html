October 25, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

SCIENCE;

LAB PARTS WAY TO BALDNESS CURE

British scientists today celebrated what they believe to be a breakthrough in the elusive quest for a cure for baldness -- test-tube hair.

A team of scientists at Cambridge University announced that they had cleared a main obstacle to the development of an anti-baldness drug by growing human hair in a test-tube.

"This is a real breakthrough," Terence Kealey, head of the research team, told a reporter. "No one has ever managed to grow hair in a laboratory before."

The idea is not to grow a replacement head of hair in the laboratory but to allow effective testing of potential baldness cures on single human hairs.

Kealey says the discovery makes the development of a cure for the age-old problem of hair loss just a matter of time.
