BFN [Text] Ukraine's Ministry of Defense press service reports the details of Minister Vitaliy Radetskyy's official visit to Brussels. He held intensive talks with his counterparts from Slovakia, Lithuania, Albania, Moldova, and Poland. These meetings focused on issues concerning European security, cooperation within the Partnership for Peace program, and ways of bilateral cooperation in the military sphere. A number of important agreements were reached with regard to joint military training and exchange of delegations. The defense ministers of the above countries expressed concern at the developments in Crimea. They supported Ukraine's stance on the issue and proposals voiced by Radetskyy for a settlement on the Crimean peninsula. In this context they agreed that Ukraine was justified in raising the issue of fair division of the Black Sea Fleet and its deployment.
