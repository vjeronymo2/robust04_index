August 12, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

ROB REINER'S DEBT

Film lovers often complain that Woody Allen no longer makes the type of films he used to; films like "Annie Hall" and "Manhattan" are what these people want.

Well, director Rob Reiner and writer Nora Ephron rectified this problem by making a film just like Allen used to make.

Some critics claim the film is an homage to Allen. If the definition of homage has been changed to mean rip-off or copy, then, yes, "When Harry Met Sally . . ." is an homage to Allen.

TONY HERSHEY

Los Angeles
