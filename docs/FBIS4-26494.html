BFN [Text] Pokhara, Nepal, June 22 (XINHUA) -- China-aided Pokhara-Baglung highway in western Nepal is put into use today. In an inaugural ceremony held in Pokhara, a city of western Nepal, this morning, Nepali Prime Minister Girija Prasad Koirala extended his appreciation to the economic help given by the Chinese Government for construction of the Pokhara-Baglung highway. The prime minister said that Nepal would name the new highway as Thirbarn Malla and Yogandra Man Highway, names of two national heroes. During the ceremony, Chinese Ambassador to Nepal Shao Jiongchu said that the highway will be another landmark for the close cooperation between the two countries and expressed the confidence that the newly-constructed highway in western Nepal would go a long way in promoting the economic development in the western part of the country. The project commenced on April 1, 1988 and completed on April 8, 1994. The highway has a total length of 71.6 kilometers and the total investment of about 12 million U.S. dollars.
