BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Dmitriy Gorokhov] [Text] Moscow June 14 TASS -- "Nobody knows how the recent crisis over Sarajevo would end if it were not for the initiative of President Boris Yeltsin", Radovan Karadzic said on Tuesday regarding Russia's support for Bosnian Serbs. At a meeting with Andrey Kozyrev in the Russian Foreign Ministry he said the Russian people had been Serbs friends over centuries. Radovan Karadzic arrived in Moscow to receive a literary prize named after Mikhail Sholokhov that was awarded to him by the Russian Writers Union. Karadzic said he intends to meet representatives of various political trends. He said he will never forget priority role of the Russian Government and Russian diplomacy in the quest for peaceful solution to the Bosnian conflict. He said Bosnian Serbs are aware of Russia's objectivity so they believe it will not be biased in the approach to the events in the region. "It is very difficult to end the civil war. Not only the armies but also civilians are involved in hostilities", Karadzic said. "We are sick and tired of the crisis lasting for several years. We need peace in order to build a democratic society and free economy".
