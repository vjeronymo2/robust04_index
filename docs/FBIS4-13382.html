BFN [Text] The speaker of the Islamic Consultative Assembly of Iran, Hojjat ol-Eslam Nateq-Nuri, called on the Minister of Foreign Affairs A.C.S. Hameed in Kandy recently. Foreign Minister Hameed recalled that he was the first official to visit Iran after the birth of the Islamic Republic of Iran following the revolution. He noted that the relations between the two countries have gathered momentum and today encompass many areas of cooperation. Minister Hameed recalled that Iran was admitted to the nonaligned summit held in Cuba during his invitation extended to Ayatollah Khomeyni to join the movement. [sentence as heard] The Iranian speaker stressed the need of expanding relations between Iran and Sri Lanka. Mr. Hameed told the visiting speaker that the best way forward in the present international climate is to avoid any international, regional, and bilateral contentious issues and confrontation. The two leaders also discussed the northeast situation in Sri Lanka.
