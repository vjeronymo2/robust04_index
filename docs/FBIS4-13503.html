BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres and Yasir 'Arafat will meet again in Bucharest today and will discuss the timetable for concluding the negotiations. Our correspondent Gid'on Kouts reports that Mr. Peres believes the negotiations between Israel and the PLO will end during the first week of May and adds that Peres might join the Israeli delegation in Cairo next weekend in order to facilitate the talks. Following the meeting between the two last night, Mr. Peres said it transpired that several disagreements between Israel and the PLO stemmed from misunderstandings. Peres noted that 'Arafat was troubled and that he had to calm him down. It was reported that during the meeting, 'Arafat said he is committed to help on the matter of the Israeli MIA's and will continue to act on this issue despite the difficulties. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 0405 GMT on 21 April carries a related report in which Kouts adds that "Shim'on Peres told us that during his upcoming visit to Israel, Warren Christopher will not be handed any document to relay to Syria. He will simply be briefed on Israel's position in detail."]
