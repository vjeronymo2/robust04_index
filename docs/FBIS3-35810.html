Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Edict No. 222 of the Russian Federation president, dated 26 January 1994: "On Full-Time State Positions on Russian Federation Presidential Commissions and Councils"] [Text] In connection with the coming into force of the Russian Federation Constitution and with a view to streamlining the activities of Russian Federation presidential commissions and councils, I decree that: 1. It be prescribed that work on Russian Federation presidential commissions and councils shall be performed on a voluntary basis and does not constitute federal state service. 2. Full-time state posts on Russian Federation presidential commissions and councils may be introduced by decisions of the Russian Federation president. 3. The full-time state posts of chairman of the Russian Federation Presidential Commission for Citizenship Issues, chairman of the Russian Federation Presidential Commission for Pardons, and chairman of the Russian Federation Presidential Commission for State Awards shall be preserved. 4. The Russian Federation President's Administration shall release workers on Russian Federation presidential commissions and councils in accordance with Russian Federation legislation. 5. This edict does not extend to workers on the apparatus of Russian Federation presidential commissions and councils. 6. This edict comes into force from the moment it is published. [Signed] Russian Federation President B. Yeltsin [Dated] Moscow, the Kremlin, 26 January 1994 No. 222
