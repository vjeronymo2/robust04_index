931014 FT 14 OCT 93 / Borrowers' new issues at Pounds 3.1bn last month NEW ISSUES by borrowers in the UK for September totalled Pounds 3.1bn, the Bank of England said yesterday. UK borrowers' new issues denominated in sterling were Pounds 2.8bn. Sterling issues by overseas borrowers were Pounds 527m. The figures reflect the reliance being placed on capital markets and are important in determining the Bank's estimates about the demands on the money supply. The largest issue during the month was the Pounds 405m rights issue by Scottish & Newcastle. Actual gross issues by UK borrowers during September were Pounds 2.4bn, with redemptions of Pounds 1.2bn and net issues of Pounds 1.3bn. Gross issues in sterling by overseas borrowers were Pounds 357m, with net issues of Pounds 337m. The largest fixed-rate sterling bond issues during the month were Pounds 500m from British Telecommunications and Pounds 255m by the Province of Ontario.
