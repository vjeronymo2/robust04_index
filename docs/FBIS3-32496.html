Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "YSU Radio News" newscast] [Excerpt] [Passage omitted] In another turn of events, Supreme Court President Mauricio Gutierrez Castro has termed as irresponsible the report presented to the United Nations by human rights expert (name indistinct). The human rights expert on 24 January presented a report to the United Nations in which he stated individual rights are still being violated in El Salvador. Here is what Gutierrez Castro has said: [Begin Gutierrez Castro recording, in progress] ...report. First, I am not surprised, as many of these individuals hold their posts by issuing alarming reports. If you are appointed to watch over something and you report that everything is alright, then why do they need you? Second, I think it is irresponsible on the part of a person who has not even come here--he has not visited the courts, has no knowledge of the process, has not seen any of this-- to issue reports criticizing or labeling the human rights situation here. For example, you are giving an account of what is being said here. How can you give an account? You can give an account because you are present, you are hearing what is going on, you are perceiving it through the [words indistinct]. I do not think you could be giving a report on what is being done at the Legal Medical Institute if you were in New York; therefore it is absurd. I think it is irresponsible to issue such a report. [end recording]
