BFN [Text] It is being claimed that Cypriot Theofilos Yeoryiadhis, who was a friend of the Kurds, was murdered by the Public Security Unit (KGB) [Kamu Guvenligi Birimi], which is attached to the Presidency of the General Staff. [see 28 March Terrorism Report] A high-ranking officer serving in north Cyprus claimed that the assassination of Yeoryiadhis, the head of the Committee for Solidarity With Kurdistan and of the Greek Cypriot information department, was planned by the Public Security Unit (KGB) attached to the Presidency of the General Staff. Claiming that the General Staff has formed a new team in north Cyprus from officers who received antiterrorism and psychological warfare training in the United States and Israel, the officer asserted that the assassination of Yeoryiadhis in south Cyprus marked the implementation of a strategy developed over the past two years. [passage omitted]

KGB Was Established in January
The Public Security Unit (KGB), which was established last January, is working under the National Security Council on issues relating to "the PKK and major social developments." The KGB, which is composed of seven people, is also tasked to ensure coordination between MIT, the General Directorate of Security, and the Foreign Ministry.
