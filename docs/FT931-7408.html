930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: Oil prices reach 3-month highs in Opec 'honeymoon' WORLD OIL prices broke through Dollars 19 a barrel yesterday for the first time since November following an increase in price of Dollars 1 a barrel over the past two days. The price of North Sea Brent crude for April delivery closed up 35 cents yesterday at Dollars 18.95 a barrel - after earlier rising above Dollars 19 -as the market enjoyed a honeymoon period after last week's agreement by the Organisation of Petroleum Exporting Countries to slash output to 23.58m barrels a day from 25m in January. Traders at major oil companies say they have been told by Saudi Arabia and Abu Dhabi that they will be cutting sales of oil from March, but have yet to see any concrete evidence that other members are prepared to cut back. The market is waiting to see if Kuwait, Iran and Nigeria will be cutting back on deliveries. 'People will have to be turned away from the door - if that happens it will be extremely bullish for oil prices,' said one oil company trader. But without proof that other countries are beginning to trim their output, oil prices could collapse. Kuwait has already warned that it will respond if other members cheat on their production allocations. The emirate was squeezed very hard by other countries to trim production to 1.6m b/d. Demand will not come to the rescue as it remains patchy and weak - US consumption in January was flat and Europe's was very depressed. Mr Geoff Pyne, analyst at UBS Phillips & Drew, believes the market can absorb some leakage from the Opec deal - to take about 24.2m b/d with prices averaging Dollars 19 a barrel for the second quarter. 'But there are risks on the downside and there is a possibility the whole thing could unravel.'
