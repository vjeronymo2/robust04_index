Language: Armenian Article Type:BFN [Text] Talks were held in Yerevan on 28 February between Davit Shahnazaryan, the Armenian president's ambassador on special missions and acting head of the State Directorate for National Security; Armenian Defense Minister Serzh Sargisyan; the Russian president's representative in the region, Validimir Kazimirov; and the Russian Deputy Defense Minister Georgiy Kondratyev on finalizing the cease-fire agreement concluded during the meeting of the defense ministers of Armenia, Azerbaijan, and Nagorno-Karabakh. Kondratyev assessed the talks: I faced no problems in Armenia. The Armenian side, Armenia and Nagorno-Karabakh, agrees to fully implement the Moscow agreement and cease fire as of 1 March. I am leaving for Baku today. I hope to receive a similar reply there. It could be stated that a first step leading to peace is being taken. Russia, Kondratyev noted, will terminate its mediatory mission if Azerbaijan refuses to implement or disagrees with any of the provisions of the agreement. After all, both the Armenians and the Azerbaijanis are longing for the war to end. He also noted that the planned visit to Stepanakert was not possible due to weather conditions. But the deputy defense minister assured that he will be there at the first opportunity.
