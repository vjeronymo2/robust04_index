Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Massive anti-Pakistan demonstrations were held in Jammu and Kashmir and several parts of the country today. Thousands of people participated in the demonstrations to protest against Islamabad's role in abetting and sponsoring terrorism in various parts of India, especially in Jammu and Kashmir. A large number of Youth Congress-I workers held a massive rally close to the Indo-Pakistan border at Wagah in Punjab to denounce Pakistan's support to disruptive forces. Addressing the demonstrators, the Youth Congress-I president, Mr. Maninderjit Singh Bitta, asked Pakistan to stop encouraging terrorism in India and work sincerely for peace between the two countries. In Jammu, several organizations, including the pradesh [state] Youth Congress-I, the Democratic Youth Federation of India, Yuva Janata Dal [youth wing of Janata Dal], Young Lawyers Association, ABVP [Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad -- All India Student Council], National Youth Front, and All India Kashmiri Samaj [Society] organized the rallies. Activists of many of these organizations marched to the headquarters of the UN observers to submit memorandums against Pakistan's activities. Effigies of the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, and former Prime Minister Nawaz Sharif were burned at many places by the demonstrators. Reports reaching Jammu say anti-Pakistan slogans rented the air in the border villages of Kathua, Samba, Akhnur, Rajaori, and Punch. Senior Congress-I leader Mr. Madanlal Sharma and the Youth Congress-I leader, Mr. Raman Bhalla, were among those who addressed the rally. Demonstrations were held near the American Embassy and Pakistan High Commission in New Delhi, protesting against the recent statements of these two countries on Kashmir. The demonstrators raised slogans and carried placards denouncing the statements. In Orissa, Youth Congress-I activists held a rally in Bhubaneswar to protest against Pakistan's interference in Punjab and Jammu and Kashmir. The Arunachal Pradesh Youth Congress-I also organized a protest rally in Itanagar and burned effigies of Ms. Bhutto and Mr. Nawaz Sharif. Reports of similar demonstrations have come in from other states. The demonstrations coincided with the nationwide hartal [strike] in Pakistan called by the Bhutto government today to express solidarity with Kashmir militants.
