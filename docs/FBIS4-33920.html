BFN [Text] Port-au-Prince, 13 Jun (EFE) -- The de facto government of Emile Jonassaint issued a statement today, although it is dated Saturday [11 June] and was released a few hours after the declaration of the state of emergency throughout the country, that it guarantees the security of foreign citizens in Haiti. The Information Ministry asked foreigners to carry out their normal activities. At the same time, the de facto government rejected statements by the international civilian mission of the United Nations and the OAS, which charged that employees of the international mission were attacked by armed civilians on 7 June.
