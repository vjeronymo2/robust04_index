Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Brasilia, 20 Mar (EFE) -- The foreign ministers and government delegates of the 13 Rio Group countries on 21 March will open in Brasilia a 48-hour meeting that will serve to sound out their preferences in the election of the next OAS secretary general. The meeting will be held on the eve of the 27 March vote in Washington, at which the continent's 35 foreign ministers will choose the replacement for Brazilian diplomat Joao Baena Soares as OAS secretary general. Venezuelan Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli -- who is one of the three candidates for the post -- will attend the three work sessions of the ministerial meeting, which will be held on 21 and 22 March at Itamaraty, the Brazilian Foreign Ministry headquarters. The other candidates are Colombian President Cesar Gaviria and Costa Rican Foreign Minister Berns Niehaus. Even though it is not on the meeting's agenda, the OAS secretary general's election will be discussed in the corridors and even during formal sessions, which are flexible and during which any delegate can raise an issue, diplomatic sources told EFE. Brazil, the country that hosts the meeting in its capacity as holder of the annually rotating secretariat of the most important Latin American political forum, supports Gaviria and is confident he will win, a high-ranking Itamaraty official told EFE. Gaviria should win, despite the fact that "the situation has not yet reached a definitive status," he said. The majority of Rio Group countries that referred to the matter -- Argentina, Brazil, Colombia, Brazil, Peru, and Mexico -- support Gaviria. The Costa Rican candidate has assured that he has the support of the Caribbean and Central American bloc, while the Venezuelan candidate has called on his two opponents to hold a television debate to be aired to the entire continent for the purpose of outlining their positions toward the OAS. The winner of the 27 March vote will be installed during the OAS general assembly to be held from 5-10 June in the Amazon city of Belem do Para (northern Brazil), the hometown of Baena Soares, a former Brazilian deputy foreign minister who has held the OAS secretariat for two consecutive five-year periods since 1984. The Rio Group ministers and government delegates will address various international issues dealing with commerce, disarmament, UN bylaws' reform, the environment, the antinarcotics fight, democracy, human rights, and initiatives to end the military regime in Haiti. They also will exchange views and seek a consensus on the position of Latin American governments on a continental summit called by U.S. President Bill Clinton for December in Miami. The ministerial meeting will coincide with the official visit of 5.5 hours that U.S. Vice President Alberto Gore will pay to Brasilia on 21 March with the objective of promoting the continental summit with Brazilian President Itamar Franco. Gore today began in La Paz a short tour of Latin America that will include stopovers in Buenos Aires and Brasilia. Meanwhile, Trinidad and Tobago Foreign Minister Ralph Maraja and Jorge Gordillo, one of Peru's deputy foreign ministers, already have arrived in Brasilia to attend the Rio Group meeting. Foreign Ministers Celso Amorim (Brazil), Antonio Aranibar (Bolivia), Miguel Angel Burelli (Venezuela), Luis Ramirez Boettner (Paraguay), Manuel Tello (Mexico), Gladys de Vielman (Guatemala), Carlos Figueroa (Chile), and Sergio Abreu (Uruguay) have confirmed that they will attend. Other delegations scheduled to arrive today or on 21 March will be headed by Argentine Deputy Foreign Minister Fernando Petrella and Colombian Deputy Foreign Minister Andelfo Garcia, while Ecuador will be represented by its ambassador in Brasilia, Cesar Valdivieso.
