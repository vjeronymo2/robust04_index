BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 11/4/1994 -- Romania's President Ion Iliescu presided on April 11 a meeting of the country's Supreme Defence Council. Two drafts were examined and initialed on the occasion, namely "The Integrated Outlook of Romanian National Security" and "Romania's Military Defence Doctrine." The former includes the fundamental tenets, objectives and trends defining the general policy of security of Romania. The latter has been drafted with observance of the provisions of Romania's Constitution and laws, the basic principles, objectives and guiding lines of the security policy of the Romanian state, the current stage and the tendencies of the political-military affairs at a world, continental and regional scale, risks and threats that appear as likely against Romania's national interests, the short- and middle-term evolution of the country's military integration with the European and Euro-Atlantic security structures. The country's Supreme Defense Council decided that the two documents be referred to parliament for endorsement. The agenda included debates and approval of a draft "Presentation Document: Romania," written by the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Romania's Ministry of National Defence, in consequence of Romania's joining the "Partnership for Peace" plan. The document includes the measures proposed by Romania, necessary for fulfilment of the political objectives of the Partnership and the aspects of military character or of other type that Romania can made available for NATO, which is going to be forwarded the draft. Romania reasserts in the document its determination to be faithful to all the principles and targets of the "Partnership for Peace" to the assertions in the "Declaration of the Heads of States and Governments Who Attended the Sitting of the North-Atlantic Council Held in Brussels on January 10-11, 1994." Political and organisation measures have been enlisted in the document intended to secure a democratic control on the Army and facilitate transparency in planning national defence and drafting the budget, fields of cooperation, human and logistic resources that Romania can contribute to the Partnership for Peace plan.
