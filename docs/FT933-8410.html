930816 FT 16 AUG 93 / Arts: Today's Television Picking out the genuinely new programmes is becoming harder and harder. The BBC is now going to extraordinary lengths to disguise repeats. BBC1's Driving Obsessions about young aggressive men in cars was 'first shown on BBC South East'. Food And Drink's Choice Cuts on the same channel offers 'highlights' from past series. Czech Mates? about the development of manufacturing after the fall of communism falls back on 'Another chance to see'. None of these programmes is actually billed as a repeat. The two most enticing new programmes both go out at 9 o'clock, of course. In Frank Stubbs Promotes (ITV) Timothy Spall's former ticket tout, full of bluster and pathos, takes up with a soccer star. In Travellers' Tales (C4) Romanian writer Andrei Codrescu sets out to drive across his adopted homeland, the US. The Monday Play on Radio 4 is 'A Woman Of Judah' by Ronald Frame. In a small town in Dorset in the 1930s a young lawyer finds himself defending the doctor's wife against a charge of scandalous behaviour (7.45).
