920602 FT 02 JUN 92 / Warning by Bank on foreign mergers THE BANK of England warned banks yesterday of the dangers of buying a foreign bank or trying to develop into pan-European institutions. The management and cultural impediments of adopting either strategy 'appear to be overly daunting', Mr Brian Quinn, the Bank's director in charge of supervision, told chairmen of the world's biggest banks at the International Monetary Conference in Toronto. This year's conference chairman is Mr William Purves, chairman of Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation, the Far Eastern bank that is trying to buy Midland Bank of the UK. Mr Quinn said cross-border deals similar to that proposed by Hongkong Bank would remain few and far between. He said it was 'unlikely that we will see any pan-European retail or wholesale bank or securities company established in the near future'. Mr Quinn pointed out that banks of different nationalities were tending to form looser alliances, such as sharing businesses. Some were taking minority shareholdings in each other or being jointly represented in some countries. He was sceptical about the benefits of such alliances. 'They appear to owe more to defensive or protective sentiments than to a clearly articulated strategy,' he said. Big US banks were encouraged by a speech from Mr Nicholas Brady, the US Secretary of the Treasury, in which he made a commitment to revive the White House attempts to reform the US banking system. 'Banks should be permitted to branch nationwide, diversifying their risk and increasing the competition in service.'
