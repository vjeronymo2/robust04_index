930401 FT 01 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television With television, more even than most areas of life, nothing succeeds like success: appear on one chat show and you will promptly be invited on to others; star in a successful series and you will be wooed by every breakfast programme and game show on every channel. Flavour of the year so far has been Richard Wilson who plays the misanthropic Victor Meldrew in 'One Foot In The Grave'. Today BBC2 shows Richard Wilson: A Life Beyond The Grave (8.00) in which he travels back to his home town of Greenock and colleagues assess his talents. The current run of Minder, not the best ever but not the worst, either, ends with Arthur and Ray in Australia trying to raise the readies to get back to London (8.30 ITV). French & Saunders ends with a parody of 'Thelma and Louise' (9.00 BBC2) and the archive series A Labour Of Love, about child care in Britain between 1900 and 1950, finishes with a look at nannies and foster mothers (9.30 BBC2). BBC1's series on miscarriages of justice, Rough Justice, investigates the case of Patrick Kane who was given a life sentence after two British soldiers were stripped and shot by the IRA (9.30).
