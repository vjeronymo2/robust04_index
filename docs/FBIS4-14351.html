BFN [Text] Tehran, April 28, IRNA -- The governor of Iran's Central Bank Mohammad Hoseyn 'Adeli in a meeting with the managing director of International Monetary Fund, Michel Camdessus, in Washington D.C. Tuesday exchanged views on the issues of mutual interest. 'Adeli briefed Camdessus on the achievements and progress of the first development plan saying that during the first five-year development plan Iran's gross domestic product (GDP) has considerably increased. Despite pressures from some foreign countries, Iran has succeeded in concluding useful agreements with its trade partners, 'Adeli said. Camdessus said that Iran's success in rescheduling its foreign debts would increase its credit in the fields of trade and commerce. He also lauded Iran's stands vis-a-vis the developing countries. 'Adeli is currently in Washington D.C. to attend the joint session of the World Bank and International Monetary Fund.
