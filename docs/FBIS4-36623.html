BFN [Text] Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri went to the Syrian capital at noon today, where he had talks with Syrian Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam regarding current domestic, regional, and international affairs. During his brief visit to Damascus, al-Hariri held talks with other senior Syrian officials in the framework of cooperation and coordination between the two countries. Al-Hariri and Syrian officials discussed the peace process, Israel's continued attacks on Lebanon, and the signing of a Jordanian-Israeli agreement. Just before he left Damascus tonight, al-Hariri reiterated Lebanon's call for Israel's full withdrawal from Lebanon. He said that the Lebanese Government wishes to promote stability and security on one hand and construction and development on the other. The prime minister said that Lebanon will continue to hold consultations with Syria. The two countries, he added, seek a just and lasting peace in the region.
