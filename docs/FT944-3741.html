941212 FT 12 DEC 94 / This Week: Television Lawyers will be dancing in the streets at the thought of the government's proposal to have British Rail broken down into 80 companies. The contract work will be the largest legal job in the country, with 360 different agreements for Scotland alone. Panorama (Monday, 9.30-10.10pm, BBC1) investigates whether the plan will hit the buffers before the next election, and reveals why Cecil Parkinson's first reaction, when he saw the privatisation plans, was 'My God]'. Carlton turns the arc lights on Tinseltown tots in Hollywood Kids (Wednesday, 9.00-10.00pm, ITV), which talks to child stars and the children of stars. Plastic surgery is common among prepubescents, and infants are kitted out in Christian Dior romper suits, as a string of self-publicists tells us. This new series consists largely of talking heads, but the things they talk about are so extraordinary, the visual tedium goes unnoticed. Rocket scientists are being consulted on how to calculate the odds on the financial futures market, with everyone wanting to pile into the sector. Assignment (Saturday, 7.20-8.05pm, BBC2) looks at how hedge funds play the derivatives market and asks if new regulations are needed in order to avoid last year's market turmoil.
