BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 Apr (MENA)--Diplomatic sources in Cairo have stated that Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres agreed in their extensive talks in Cairo today on the need to overcome the remaining obstacles preventing the implementation of the Gaza-Jericho self-rule agreement. In remarks to MENA, the sources revealed that only three points of difference remain to be resolved in the meeting expected to be held in Cairo between 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin on Tuesday. The three points of difference relate to the size of Jericho, the Palestinian police presence on the Allenby Bridge, and the control of the sea area in Gaza. The same sources told MENA that the Palestinian and Israeli sides have agreed on the following: 1. A temporary international presence in Gaza and Jericho by elements from countries making donations to the self-rule areas, as agreed upon in the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles signed in Washington on 13 September 1993. These elements will work as observers and administrative staff to provide social and other aid, as in the case of Hebron, but the number of observers is yet to be determined. 2. Regarding the question of Palestinian passports, a travel document (laissez-passer) will be issued. The word passport will be written at the top of the document, to be followed by the words Palestinian Interim Self-Government Authority, abbreviated as PISGA. The document will be valid for three years. 3. It has been agreed that the border point in Rafah is to remain open 24 hours a day throughout the year to individuals and goods. The Cairo Agreement will apply at the crossing points. 4. A safe passage for the Palestinians between Gaza and Jericho has been agreed upon. 5. Regarding stamps, the sources pointed out that it has been agreed that stamps bearing the words Palestinian Self-Government Authority will be issued. 6. Regarding the telephone code, a certain code for the Palestinian self-rule areas has been designated. The same sources emphasized that the two sides had agreed on the air space of the Palestinian Self-Government Authority in Gaza and Jericho. The sources added that the drafting committee is holding a meeting that will last until early in the morning to settle the question of legal jurisdiction. The sources told MENA that Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres had told 'Arafat that the Israeli troops' withdrawal from the self-rule areas would start the day after the agreement is signed in Cairo. Peres believes that there is a possibility of creating a new Palestinian reality, and this represents a goal for the State of Israel, the sources added. The same sources pointed out that at the Egyptian Foreign Ministry this evening, small teams from the United States, Egypt, Israel, and the Palestinians are meeting to work out the arrangements for signing the agreement for implementing self-rule in Gaza and Jericho. Peres has commended the hard work of Dr. Nabil Sha'th and Major General Amnon Shahaq, heads of the Palestinian and Israeli delegations, noting that the two delegations have made a great accomplishment. MENA has learned that when 'Arafat enters the self-rule areas in Gaza and Jericho, he will hold the title "Chairman of the Palestinian Authority." Diplomatic sources have told MENA that during the meetings between 'Arafat and Peres, it was agreed to discuss the issue of the Israeli army's MIA's and POW's. 'Arafat promised to exert all efforts in this regard. MENA has also learned that the committees in charge of the actual transfer of the self-rule powers will start their work as of Sunday. The same sources added that Dr. Sha'th and Maj. Gen. Shahaq will discuss the question of the Palestinian prisoners in a meeting to be held tonight. They should be able to finalize this issue by Sunday. The sources pointed out that there is a general agreement on the number of prisoners and detainees to be released, adding that the details will be made public on Sunday. Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres has stressed that the Palestinian and Israeli sides have reached the end of the road of negotiations. He added: We thought we could face and combat terrorism with military measures alone, but now we must create a new Palestinian reality, and this is the goal of the State of Israel.
