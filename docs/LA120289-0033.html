December 2, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

VIOLENCE POLARIZES EL SALVADOR

FMLN "terrorists" attack the U.S.-supported government of El Salvador, while Contra "freedom fighters" attack the U.S.-opposed government of Nicaragua. I imagine the appellations are reversed in the media of, say, Cuba and Russia.

When these threadbare and profoundly flawed pejoratives come from the mouths of official spokesmen, is it any wonder that negotiations fail?

Does President Bush really believe that these characterizations are valid? How can we play a positive role in the resolution of these blood-soaked guerrilla wars while broadcasting such divisive misrepresentations?

E.C. BRESSLER

Los Angeles
