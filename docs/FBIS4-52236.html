BFN [Text] Vientiane, May 5 (KPL) -- A Lao-Thai goods exhibition opened in Vientiane yesterday morning. A large number of dignitaries attended the ceremony. Among the dignitaries were Minister Phao Bounnaphon, head of the Prime Minister's Office, Trade Minister Sompadit Volasan, and Thai Ambassador Somphan Kokilanon. Members of diplomatic corps and international organisations to Laos were also invited to the opening. Sisouk Sisombat, president of the National Chamber of Commerce and Industry, addressed the opening ceremony. He said that this goods exhibition was held under the cooperation between the Lao National Chamber of Commerce and Industry and the Thai Embassy in Vientiane. He pointed out that the objectives of the event were to contribute toward increasingly growing trade and cultural relations between Laos and Thailand, introduce the quality goods to consumers, give an opportunity to traders, businessmen from both countries to meet and exchange views on expanding their cooperation, and for the people of the two countries to have a cultural exchange. There are 20 show rooms for Lao goods, and 30 show rooms for Thai goods. The Lao exhibits include handicraft items, textiles, jewelry, silver wares, carvings, sculptures, souvenirs, rattan-made furniture, wood products, and garments. The Thai show rooms display farm tools, sports equipment, medical equipment, musical instruments, educational aids, decorations, gifts, and others. All the goods show rooms will be kept open to the public till May 8.
