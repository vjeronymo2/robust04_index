BFN [Text] The National Assembly Office issues Communique No. 17 as follows: On 17 June, the National Assembly held its work in the conference hall under the direction of National Assembly Vice Chairman Phung Van Tuu to continue to pass the labor code. Bui Ngoc Thanh, member of the National Assembly Secretariat read the full text of the draft labor code from chapter six onwards for the National Assembly to debate and approve. 57 National Assembly deputies offered their opinions on some articles and paragraphs of the draft labor code. They are Nguyen Thanh Phong and Phan Minh Tanh of Dong Thap; Le Thi Thuy, Lo Trung Thanh, and Nguyen Quoc Thuoc of Nghe An; Ha Dang of Phu Yen; Trinh Chan of Ha Giang; Pham Huy Tho and Pham Hung of Hai Hung; Vo Tong Xuan of Can Tho; Nguyen Don, Nguyen Thi Binh, and Hoang Minh Thang of Quang Nam-Da Nang; Vu Dinh Cu, Ton That Bach, Chu Thuy Quynh, and Tran Thi Tam Dan of Hanoi; Le Viet Duoc of Thanh Hoa; Hoang Dinh Cau and Tran Thi Thanh Thanh of Ha Tay; Huu Tho and Bui Huu Hai of Vinh Phu; Cu Hoa Dan of Lao Cai; Nguyen Duc Hoan of Quang Tri; Tang Van Luy, Nguyen Thi Nhung, Nguyen Viet Dung, and Tran Duc Tam of Ha Bac; Trinh Hong Duong, Thang Nghia, and Nguyen Khac Tao of Ha Tinh; Nguyen Van Tu of Dong Nai; Nguyen Binh Giang of Quang Ninh; Duong Khanh Lam of Bac Thai; Truong Tan De of Vinh Long; Le Thi Bich and Le Duc Binh of Ninh Binh; H' Nghia of Gia Lai; Doan Le Huong, Truong My Hoa, Pham Quang Du, Vu Tuat Viet, Le Minh Chau, Do Hoang Hai, Nguyen Van Tang, Tran Du Lich, and Nguyen Minh Thanh of Ho Chi Minh City; Trieu Thi Nhay and Lam Phuc Co of Yen Bai; Tran Chi Liem and Nguyen Thi Viet Nhan of Kien Giang; Dam Van Nguy of Cao Bang; Nguyen Thi Hoai Thu of Tien Giang; Dang Khoi and Nguyen Thi Than of Nam Ha; and Ha Manh Tri and Vu Manh Trinh of Thai Binh. During the debate, Tran Dinh Hoan, minister of labor, war invalids, and social, on behalf of the Law Drafting Committee, further explained some issues queried by the National Assembly deputies. The National Assembly voted on some articles on which there were still differing views. Then it voted to pass from chapter six onwards of the Labor Code. With high unanimity, the National Assembly voted to pass the entire text of the Labor Code. This is a very important law. It is the legal foundation for adjusting both the labor relation between employees and employers and social relations which are directly involved with the labor relation in the new period. Tomorrow, 18 June, the National Assembly will work in the conference hall to pass the bill on tax levied on the transfer of rights on land usage.
