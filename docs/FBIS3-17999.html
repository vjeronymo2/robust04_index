Language: English Article Type:BFN ["News Release"] [Text] Tallinn, March 30 -- Head of Russian delegation at Estonian-Russian inter-state negotiations Vasiliy Svirin told ETA, on Wednesday [30 March], that the Russian side had "done serious work during the last weeks to continue constructive discussions at the 18th round of negotiations" due on April 5-6 in Moscow, and if similar approach was used by the Estonian side positive results at the forthcoming round could be hoped for. "We hope that the Estonian side thought over the results of the 17th round and came to the conclusion that we have to find ways to compromise to solve some of the deadlocks," he said. On Thursday, Estonian Decolonization Initiative Centre will hold a picket in front of the building of Estonian Foreign Ministry to support and encourage Estonian delegation before the 18th round. "Estonian delegation should not choose the Latvian way on the issue of the retired Russian military, but has to lift up its chin," Juri Estam from the Center told ETA.
