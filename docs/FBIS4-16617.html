BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Maynayev] [Text] Bishkek 18 April TASS -- The Kyrgyz Parliament appointed Rosa Otunbayeva Kyrgyz foreign minister on Monday [18 April]. Otunbayeva is a graduate of the Moscow State University's Faculty of Philosophy and holds a candidate's degree in philosophy. She was a lecturer at the national university. For over five years she worked in district committees of the Republic's capital. Roza Otunbayeva, 44, held the posts of the deputy chairman of the Kyrgyz Transport Ministry and Ministry of External Affairs during the Soviet era. She worked in the USSR Ministry of Foreign Affairs since 1989, and one year later was appointed chairperson of the Soviet Union's Commission of UNESCO Affairs. Later she worked as vice-president of the executive council of this international organisation. She was ambassador of Kyrgyzia [Kyrgyzstan] to the United States from February, 1992 when the republic became an independent state. According to specialists, Otunbayeva is an active supporter of aggressive diplomacy. She speaks fluently German and English. Lately the Kyrgyz Government had no head of the foreign department. The January session of the Parliament did not show confidence in Foreign Minister Ednan Karabayev who was recommended at this post by President Askar Akayev. The new appointment has been made on the eve of Chinese Premier Li Peng's visit to Kirghizia.
