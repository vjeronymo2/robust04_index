BFN [Text] Paris, May 27 (BTA) -- Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov, who is attending the Conference on Stability in Europe, met with Peter Tarnoff, U.S. Under Secretary for Political Affairs, head of the U.S. delegation. The sides exchanged views on the need to intensify bilateral political contacts. They considered possible ways of expanding cooperation between Bulgaria and the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development, of this country's accession to GATT in the near future, and international financing of transport infrastructure projects that would resolve Bulgaria's difficulties arising from sanctions enforcement against Serbia and Montenegro. Mr. Daskalov also met with Latvian Foreign Minister Egils Levits to discuss prospects for the development of bilateral relations. Mr. Daskalov met with G-24 Secretary General Jean-Claude Paye and the permanent representatives of the G-24 member states. The sides went into the prospects for deepening cooperation between Bulgaria and G-24 with a view to speeding up Bulgaria's reform process. Mr. Daskalov and French Minister for European Affairs Alain Lamassoure discussed matters related to Bulgaria's speedier integration into the European Union and the prospects of the initiative for signing a European stability pact. Matters under discussion also included cooperation between Bulgaria and G-24 and relaxing entry visa requirements between Bulgaria and France.
