BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 17 (KCNA) -- Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter and his entourage today paid a visit to the Mangyongdae School Children's Palace. Accompanying the guests were Vice-premier and Foreign Minister Kim Yong-nam and Vice Foreign Minister Song Ho-kyong and vice-chairman of the C.C. [Central Committee], League of Socialist Working Youth of Korea Ham Un-kon. School children warmly welcomed the guests with applause. The guests saw round the indoor swimming pool, the accordion room and the embroidery room, hearing an explanation about the construction of the palace as a wonderful extramural educational base for school children. At the embroidery room Jimmy Carter and Madame expressed adoration at the wisdom and talent of school children, while seeing the extramural activities with keen interest. Embroidery room members presented a fancywork to the guests as a souvenir. Jimmy Carter saw the both-sided embroidery "Cimjongilia" and said he would keep it as an heirloom. Then the guests appreciated a performance of the art circle members at the palace theatre. At the end of the performance Carter said no children in any other country can match the Korean children and that this is the first time he saw such an admirable performance of children although he had toured many countries in the world. Before leaving the palace, he said he would surely convey the friendly feelings of the Korean children to the American children. Jimmy Carter and his entourage today visited the Mansudae Art Studio, combined art creation base, and the Pyongyang metro. Earlier, on the 16th, they visited Pyongyang department store No. 1 and the Ognyu Restaurant and made a round of Pyongyang streets.
