930812 FT 12 AUG 93 / The Lex Column: France The second exploratory easing in French overnight lending rates this week highlights how keen the government is to lower the cost of borrowing without jeopardising the remnants of its franc fort policy. So far, it has succeeded in steering rates down without undue damage to the franc, helping it recoup foreign currency reserves. However, if the government delays too long in cutting other important rates, such as the intervention rate, it risks losing the initiative. With investors relying on lower rates to underpin surging equity and bond prices, the testy foreign exchange market may again push the franc lower try to to force the government's hand.
