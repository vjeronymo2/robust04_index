BFN [Text] [Correspondent] In the opinion of members of the delegation and experts, the three day visit by [Turkish] President Demirel to Moldova has been extremely fruitful. Accords were reached on cooperation in a number of economic and cultural spheres and the Turkish friends visited regions with concentrations of Gagauz. Moreover, the Turkish side pledged to give Moldova a credit of $35 million to be used to develop southern regions. The main result of the visit was the Treaty on Friendship and Cooperation between the two countries, which was signed today. [Begin Demirel recording in Turkish fading into Moldovan report] Our two states, Mr. Demirel said, have confirmed their intention to continue cooperating in the spirit of the Accord on Black Sea Economic Cooperation. [end recording] [Begin Moldovan President Snegur recording] I would like to stress the great importance of the documents that have been signed: They include a treaty on friendship and cooperation between our two countries and, documents which are called upon to give a renewed stimulus to our bilateral and mutually advantageous relations. [end recording]
