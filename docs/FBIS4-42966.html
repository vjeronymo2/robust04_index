BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Excerpts] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin is continuing his bilateral contacts with foreign ministers who are currently in Istanbul for the North Atlantic Cooperation Council meetings. This morning, Cetin met with U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. In a joint statement issued with Christopher, Cetin said that bilateral relations, the Cyprus problem, the oil pipeline, and issues such as the Azerbaijani-Armenian conflict were discussed during the meeting. [passage omitted] In a statement he issued later, Cetin declared that the U.S. House of Representatives decision to cut aid to Turkey is unacceptable. [Begin Cetin recording] I informed Christopher that linking 25 percent of aid to certain conditions is unacceptable to the Turkish public and the Turkish Government and that the U.S. Administration must use the necessary influence in the coming days to rectify this. I also expressed Turkey's satisfaction that the request concerning 10 [word indistinct] planes has been referred to the U.S. Congress. Moreover, we discussed the need for the U.S. Administration to continue its efforts for the positive conclusion of this matter in Congress. [end recording] Cetin explained that in principle, Christopher was positive about the Karkuk-Yumurtalik oil pipeline issue, and the work on this subject will continue in the coming days. The conflict between Azerbaijan and Armenia was also discussed during the talks, Cetin reported, adding that it would be wrong to take the issue to platforms other than the CSCE. Cetin later met with Finnish Foreign Minister Heikki Haavisto. Bilateral political and economic relations were discussed during the meeting.
