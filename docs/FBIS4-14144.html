BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih today held discussions in his office at the ministry with the Kenyan ambassador on preparations for the coming round of peace negotiations, to be held in Nairobi, Kenya, on 16 May. Dr. Abu-Salih assured the Kenyan ambassador of Sudan's eagerness to establish peace in the south within the framework of a unified Sudan, without becoming a pawn of foreign forces. He expressed his hope that the forthcoming Nairobi round would be decisive, and that the rebel movement would come with clear views to assist in solving the problem. The meeting touched on Kenyan-Sudanese relations. The two sides confirmed their eagerness to strengthen and develop those relations. Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih also met today with Mr. Peter Hansen, UN assistant secretary general for humanitarian affairs, who is currently visiting the country. Dr. Abu-Waf, minister of state at the ministry of social planning, and Mr. Ali Muhammad Yasin, Sudan's delegate to the United Nations, were also present. The meeting dealt with issues connected with humanitarian conditions. Dr. Abu-Salih affirmed Sudan's cooperation with all those working in the humanitarian field to deliver food to the citizens in all areas affected by the war, via Operation Lifeline.
