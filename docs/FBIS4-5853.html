BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 14 KYODO -- U.S. Defense Secretary William Perry will visit Japan from April 19 to 21 to hold talks with the prime minister and foreign minister, Foreign Ministry officials said Thursday. Perry, who will fly to Tokyo from Seoul, will also meet with the Defense Agency chief, the officials said. He is expected to discuss problems involving North Korea's alleged development of nuclear weapons and the Japan-U.S. security arrangement, they said. He may also inspect U.S. forces during his stay, they said.
