Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] President Husni Mubarak received the Pakistani justice minister at the Presidential Office today. Minister of Justice Counselor Faruq Sayf-al-Nasr attended. Counselor Sayf-al-Nasr said after the meeting that the Pakistani justice minister handed President Mubarak a written message from the Pakistani president. He added that the Justice Ministry is holding talks with the Pakistani justice minister on signing a judicial cooperation agreement between the two countries for the exchange of criminals. He expressed his hope that an accord will be signed during this visit to combat all forms of crime and terrorism and to establish rights, justice, security, and the rule of law. For his part, the Pakistani justice minister praised the strong relations between the Pakistani and Egyptian peoples and presidents. He noted that Egypt has a pioneering standing among the Arab countries and within the Islamic Conference Organization. He said that both countries are bound by common goals, especially since Pakistan and Egypt are standing up against terrorist activities everywhere. He added that Pakistan has deported over 2,000 terrorists who had entered the country during the Afghan war. He expressed his hope for increased cooperation between Egypt and Pakistan, especially in economics, and for an increase in higher-level visits by the two countries' officials.
