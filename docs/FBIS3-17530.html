Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature No. 59: Compiled by Nikolay Zherebtsov and Andrey Petrovskiy; edited by Vladimir Shishlin] [Excerpt] "Time is going forward and demands deeper bilateral relations, better coordination of actions between Russia and Kazakhstan." The statement was made on Monday by President Boris Yeltsin as he opened negotiations between Russian and Kazakh delegations. Earlier he had a one on one meeting with the Kazakh leader Nursultan Nazarbayev. According to Yeltsin, the meeting helped settle a number of questions that used to be open earlier. Nevertheless, the signing of agreements was put off from Monday morning to evening. In the opinion of experts those hours were needed to make amendments in the agreements worked out by the presidents. [passage omitted]
