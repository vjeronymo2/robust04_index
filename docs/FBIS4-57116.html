BFN [Report by Fernando Fernandez; from the "RPC Television News" newscast] [Text] National Police Director Oswaldo Fernandez has agreed to remain in his post and work in the government of Dr. Ernesto Perez Balladares. President-elect Ernesto Perez Balladares, upon his arrival for a meeting with the National Police director, said he will not yet officially announce who will make up his cabinet, but said he will seek the desired national consensus. [Begin recording] [Perez Balladares] There is a positive atmosphere to achieve a consensus, which all Panamanians want and for which, I understand, the Panamanian people voted. [Unidentified reporter] Could this attempt at a consensus lead to some problems within the Democratic Revolutionary Party [PRD]? [Perez Balladares] No, our party will be very well represented in the cabinet and in all government posts because it is also the party that obtained the most votes. The party has the political maturity to understand that to achieve a consensus it is necessary to allow other forces to participate, otherwise there will not be a consensus. We cannot speak of good intentions only. We have to put them into practice. [end recording] The meeting, which was held in the office of National Police Director Oswaldo Fernandez, lasted over two hours. Some of Perez Balladares' top advisers participated. As he was leaving, the Panamanian president-elect broke the tight security to greet the personnel working in this public security institution. National Police Director Oswaldo Fernandez said the meeting with the president-elect was to brief him on the Public Force, its organization, and the future support for this institution by the new government. Fernandez also talked about Perez Balladares' offer to have him remain as the institution's director. Fernandez said he accepted after President Guillermo Endara also asked him to stay. [Begin recording] [Fernandez] We decided to remain here. As I told your colleagues a moment ago, it was not an easy decision for an attorney who had already been nominated for a post in the Supreme Court. [Reporter] What motivated you to stay? [Fernandez] The primary reason was that both the incoming and outgoing presidents asked me to stay. In addition, I have also received requests from many people in the National Police, especially policemen, sergeants, and corporals, who asked me to stay with the institution. If both presidents thought I should remain as director, it was not because I was the best but because of the work accomplished by the National Police. The work has been carried out thanks to those people, who asked me to stay with them. [end recording]
