Language: English Article Type:BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent] [Text] Moscow March 16 TASS -- An ITAR-TASS correspondent has learned that the agenda of the current Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) foreign ministers' meeting here includes the following ten items: -- a draft convention on the insurance of the rights of the members of ethnic, linguistic and religious minorities, -- the establishment of a standing consultative commission on peacemaking activities, -- a 1994 plan for multilateral consultations among CIS foreign ministries, -- international recognition of the CIS and its statutory bodies (interaction between the CIS and the CSCE, and NATO's Partnership-for-Peace programme are to be considered within the framework of this item), -- the state of affairs at the talks between the Russian Federation, Central Asian and Transcaucasian states on cooperation in the guarding of their borders with third countries, -- a declaration of the observance of the sovereignty, territorial integrity and inviolability of CIS countries' borders, -- Kazakhstan's proposal to hold a special session of the United Nations General Assembly on the problems of the post-confrontation era, -- measures to settle the question of dividing the state-run non-military ships of the former Soviet Union among CIS and Baltic states, -- the drafting of a text of a joint address by CIS countries to the CIS peoples and the world public opinion in view of the 50th anniversary of the victory in the Second World War, -- the determination of a date and venue for a regular meeting of the foreign ministers of CIS countries.
