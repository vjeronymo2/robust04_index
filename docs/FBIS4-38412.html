BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Belskiy] [Text] Moscow May 27 TASS -- Russian Vice-Premier Sergey Shakhray will not run for president in 1996, according to his statement at today's meeting with students and professors of Moscow University. Speaking of his resignation from the post of minister for affairs of nationalities and the voiced intention to leave the vice-premier's post, Shakhray said "the question has been closed, I remain the vice-premier". He told the audience that meetings with the prime minister and president assured him "his potential has not been exhausted". Shakhray did not exclude "the right to future existence of Far Eastern or Urals republics on condition it will not be a mere isolation of the region or the territory within the current administrative borders". In his opinion, this is possible "in case of formation of larger federation subjects on the basis of inter-regional economic unity and cooperation". Commenting on press reports claiming fake results of the December 12 referendum on the new Russian Constitution, the vice-premier said "as long as these claims are not proved, I will think that the Constitution has been approved". The Constitution is a reality, said Shakhray.
