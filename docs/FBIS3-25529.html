Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The new package of constitutional amendments have been presented to the multiparty negotiating council in Kempton Park by the government and the ANC [African National Congress], who said the amendments were aimed at addressing the concerns of the Freedom Alliance and enhancing the legitimacy of the interim constitution. The tabled amendments confirm earlier versions of the constitutional concessions announced last week. The proposals include concessions on self-determination to address right-wing demands for a volkstaat [homeland]. These are supplemented by a proposal that a volkstaat council be established by members of Parliament to pursue the concept of a volkstaat after the election. No members of the Freedom Alliance attended this morning's meeting of the negotiating council. The concessions will also allow provinces to write their own constitutions and to create their own legislative and executive structures. A two-ballot system is also proposed.
