BFN [Text] The Russian-Kazakh consultative meeting on the lending of the Baykonur launching site by Russia has been put off until at least May 26. A high-ranking representative of the Russian Space Agency told Interfax that the expected talks between Russian Space Agency General Director Yuriy Koptev and Kazakh Vice Premier Galym Abilsiitov would take place within the scope of the meeting of the members to the Inter-State Space Council. Currently, the treaty on the lending of the Baykonur space launching site and documents in addition to the agreement on Baykonur signed by Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev and Russian President Boris Yeltsin in Moscow on March 28 are being worked out. The same source said that intensive meetings on the issue would start after the Moscow agreement came into effect. Experts will arrive in Moscow on May 18 in order to work out a joint program of cooperation in space research. The program is expected to be adopted by the Inter-State Space Council.
