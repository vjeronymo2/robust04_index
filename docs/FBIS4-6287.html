BFN [Text] Phnom Penh AKP April 18 -- Prince Kromkhun Norodom Sirivut, minister of foreign affairs and international cooperation, gave an audience to Mr. Benny Widyono, UN secretary general's representative, at the Foreign Ministry office. During the audience, Benny Widyono informed H.R.H. [His Royal Highness] Prince Kromkhun Norodom Sirivut that he was appointed by the UN secretary general as his representative in Cambodian [as received] in accordance with the resolution 880 (1993), paragraph 13, adopted by the UN Security Council at its 3303rd meeting on 4 November 1993, with the following mandate of which the initial term is 6 month period: -- To maintain close liaison and dialogue with the host government in accordance with the spirit and principle of the Paris agreement. -- To monitor the political situation in Cambodia and report on significant developments relating to peace and security in the country. -- To maintain close contact with UN chief military liaison officer and provide him with political guidance. -- To maintain liaison and dialogue with the resident coordinator, the head of the human right center in Cambodia and other UN agencies. -- To report on his activities to the secretary general through the under secretary general for political affairs. For his part, Prince Kromkhun Norodom Sirivut has warmly welcomed the presence of Mr. Benny Widyono as a representative of the UN secretary general in Cambodian and affirmed that the royal government will give its full support and arrangements for the UN mission in Cambodia.
