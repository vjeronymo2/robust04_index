Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Aswan, 28 Feb (MENA)--Security forces killed five terrorists today following a chase through farm areas in Aswan. The incident took place during sweeps that began last night in Aswan. Security organs killed three wanted terrorists last night after they attacked a policeman. According to security sources, the dead men were dangerous terrorists wanted by the police in connection with several crimes. The eight terrorists attacked the sentry at the license office in Idfu yesterday evening, captured his rifle, and fled in a minitruck toward the village of (Kalj al-Jabal). The security forces immediately chased them and managed to kill three of them. The remaining terrorists hid in a sugar cane plantation. A police force led by Major General Sami 'Abd-al-Jawad, assistant to the interior minister and director of the Security Department in Aswan, surrounded them and exchanged fire. As day broke today, the police force raided the sugar cane plantation and found the bodies of the five other terrorists, the sentry's stolen rifle, and some weapons and ammunition. During the manhunt and raid, two policemen were martyred and another was injured and taken to the central hospital in Idfu.
