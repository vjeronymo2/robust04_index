BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 23/6/1994 -- The Romanian press of the past days comments on the results of an opinion poll ordered by the government conducted by the Romanian Institute of Public Opinion Polling (IRSOP) in April 1994, and the results of which, however, being unfavorable for the government, are being kept strictly confidential. According to the poll -- which carries the title National Study Regarding Public Perception of Current Social Issues, the Government's Eligibility for the People's Favors, and the Popularity of Parties and Their Top Politicians -- two thirds of the Romanians are not satisfied with President Ion Iliescu's performance and view him as not eligible for another mandate in office. More than 27 percent are stating in favor of Ion Iliescu, considering him still eligible for the presidential office. Eight out of ten Romanians are reproaching the president with not doing anything or doing too little to eliminate corruption (83 percent) and for agriculture (81 percent.) Two thirds are of the opinion that he does not encourage enough the market economy (67 percent) and is bringing a far too tiny contribution to the speeding up of privatization. Such opinions are, however, revealing that one part of the population has a wrong perception regarding the responsibilities of the presidential office. Eight out of ten Romanians have a negative view on the government (79 percent). The negative views seem to be grounded more on the given impression of the government's lack of interest for the population, than on the lack of abilities in the performance of the executive. Only one out of four Romanians (23 percent) is accusing the government of lack of competence. With a smaller percentage, namely two out of ten Romanians (21 percent) are ready to praise the government and to find excuses for its failures, invoking an intervention by several forces sabotaging their good intentions. The population's confidence in the political parties is pictured as follows: The ruling Social Democracy Party of Romania [PDSR] -- 21 percent; the National Peasant Christian-Democratic Party [PNTCD] (opposition) -- 17 percent; the Democratic Agrarian Party of Romania [PDAR] (backer of the government until recently) -- 8 percent; the Civic Alliance Party [PAC] (opposition) - 8 percent; the Democratic Party [PD] (opposition) -- 6 percent; the National Romanian Unity Party [PUNR] (government backer) -- 6 percent; the Romanian Ecologist Party [PER] -- 6 percent; the Liberal Party '93 [PL-93] -- 5 percent; the Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania [UDMR] -- 5 percent (all opposition forces); the Socialist Party of Labor [PSM] -- 4 percent; the Greater Romania Party [PRM] -- 4 percent (both government backers); and the Romanian Social-Democratic Party [PSDR] -- 1 percent (opposition.) The circumstances under which the poll was leaked to the press are still unknown, because its orderer, the Government's General Secretariat, did not make an official release of the document, while the executive has neither denied nor confirmed speculations of the press.
