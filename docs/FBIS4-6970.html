BFN [Announcer-read report over video; from the "NHK News" program] [Text] A top official of the Japan Democratic Socialist Party [DSP], which is participating in the joint floor group "Kaishin," told reporters that even if the other coalition parties offer to reconsider the idea of forming "Kaishin" to ask the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] to return to the coalition, it believes that the SDPJ is no longer in a position to be persuaded. He thus indicated that the possibility of the SDPJ's changing its mind is scarce. Remarks by Rengo [Japanese Trade Union Confederation] Chairman Akira Yamagishi follow: [Begin recording] "Jiyuto" [Liberal Party] was unilaterally allowed into the framework of the coalition without prior consultations. This is a change of the coalition's framework. From the SDPJ's viewpoint, this is breach of faith, and it is inevitable for the SDPJ to think the other coalition parties are trying to eliminate it.
