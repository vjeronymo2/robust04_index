Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] United Nations, Feb 1 (AFP) -- UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali said here Tuesday [1 February] that French General Bertrand de Lapresle would be the new chief of UN forces in the former Yugoslavia. If confirmed by the UN Security Council, he will replace compatriot General Jean Cot at the end of March, Butrus-Ghali told a news conference. Paris had proposed de Lapresle as Cot's replacement one week ago. France has the largest contingent of troops in the former Yugoslavia with 6,000 soldiers. Butrus-Ghali and Cot had disagreed over the general's repeated public demands that he be given the authority to order use of air support in Bosnia-Hercegovina if he deemed it necessary. Butrus-Ghali delegated this authority to his civilian representative in Yugoslavia, Yasushi Akashi of Japan, for two operations specific to Srebrenica and Tuzla.
