BFN [Report by Jose Otero] [Text] Education Minister Marco Alarcon confirmed yesterday he will resign from his post to avail himself of his retirement as a teacher. He also said German Vergara, current Arnulfist Party [PA] deputy secretary general, will replace him. Alarcon's resignation will be effective as of 1 July, when his retirement papers, currently in their final processing stage, are completed. It was also reported that Alarcon requested his vacation time, as is his right, after having been the education minister for three [number as published] years. He took office on 1 April 1992, when he replaced Ada de Gordon, following President Guillermo Endara's ousting of the Christian Democratic Party from the government alliance. Alarcon denied that his separation from the post of education minister is due to discrepancies with the PA or with President Endara. Meanwhile, Vergara said he accepted the appointment to the post. The current minister recommended him, and Endara accepted the recommendation. Vergara was the secretary general of the Health Ministry, and he acquired his teaching experience in the interior. PA Secretary General Carlos Young Adames highly praised Vergara's appointment by noting he believes in the work Vergara will embark on despite the short term he will have heading the Education Ministry. It was also confirmed that Bolivar Armuelles will remain deputy education minister until 1 September, when the government of President-elect Ernesto Perez Balladares begins.
