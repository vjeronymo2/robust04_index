Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Belgrade, Feb 5 (TANJUG) -- The Yugoslav Foreign Ministry on Saturday [5 February] condemned the massacre of innocent civilians in Sarajevo's crowded marketplace in a blast earlier in the day and called for immediate steps to be taken to establish the truth of the matter and punish those responsible for the atrocity. Foreign news agencies have reported at least 66 dead and more than 160 injured in the explosion. In a statement released on Saturday evening, the ministry said that "this criminal act, which follows an earlier established pattern, is a heavy blow to the peace process." "We appeal to all factors of the peace process not to permit any political or media manipulation of this tragic event and to invest firm and unbiased efforts to establish the truth, prevent any obstruction of the peace process and show determination in the effort to reach just and lasting peace in former Bosnia- Herzegovina," the statement said.
