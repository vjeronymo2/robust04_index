Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National News" newscast] [Text] General Ramon Emilio Gil Bermudez, acting defense minister, confronted today the dust storm unleashed by the arrival of U.S. soldiers in Bahia de Malaga. He insisted that it is just a technical collaboration and said that the Armed Forces' 1994 objective is to capture the guerrillas' leaders. Gil reiterated today that the presence of U.S. soldiers in the Colombian Pacific area is simply due to technical collaboration. [Begin Gil recording] The real reason why they--not Marines, but military engineers or a detachment of military engineers--are here is to execute an operation of mutual assistance. [end recording] Gil downplayed the importance of the Americas Watch reports, which accuse the mobile brigades of violating human rights in certain areas of Colombia. [Begin Gil recording] The reports that reach these organizations have already been distorted; they have an intention that favors criminals. [end recording] Gil said the Army's main objective for 1994 is to reduce the offensive capability of guerrillas and, if possible, capture the leaders.
