BFN [Text] Seoul, June 20 (YONHAP) -- President Kim Yong-sam ordered thorough preparations for a possible inter-Korean summit Monday, calling it an event "that may change our post-liberation history." Kim made the instructions after a unification-related ministerial meeting was held earlier in the day chaired by Deputy Premier Yi Yong-tok. The chances of an inter-Korean summit -- the first ever -- are at an all-time high with the top leaders of both South and North Korea agreeing in principle to the meeting. Deputy Premier Yi pointed out that the government held a series of meetings over the weekend to lay out specifics for the South-North summit. "The meeting participants are very much in tune with each other, allowing for rapid progress," said Yi.
