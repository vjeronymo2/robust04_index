BFN [Text] Hong Kong, June 21 (XINHUA) -- The XINHUA NEWS AGENCY Hong Kong [HK] branch today donated a total of 425,980 HK dollars (about 54,613 U.S. dollars) to help relieve the devastating floods in six south China provinces and regions. The branch also set up a special flood relief office and re-opened a flood relief account in Bank of China Hong Kong branch to receive donations from Hong Kong residents and organizations. Up to now, Hong Kong residents and organizations, together with major Chinese (mainland) institutions in the territory, have donated about 30 million HK dollars (about 3.846 million U.S. dollars) for the flood-hit areas. Since early June, the provinces and regions of Guangdong, Guangxi, Hunan, Jiangxi, Zhejiang and Fujian have suffered from severe floods which caused 438 people dead and 480,000-plus houses down with an estimated economic loss of 17 billion renminbi yuan (about 1.954 billion U.S. dollars).
