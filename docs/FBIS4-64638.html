BFN [Announcer-red report over video] [Excerpts] Turkish Grand National Assembly Speaker Husamettin Cindoruk has said that Turkey is a contemporary, secular, and democratic country that will defend its own democracy as well as that of Europe. Cindoruk, who is on an official visit in Paris, has concluded his meetings there. He was received by President Francois Mitterrand. Turkish Radio and Television correspondent Kudret Dogan Demir reports from Paris: [Begin Demir recording] Cindoruk was received by Mitterrand at the Elysee Palace. He also met with various parliamentarians. [passage omitted] In the statements he made during his visit, Cindoruk said that there are no significant disagreements between Turkey and France, adding that bilateral economic and cultural relations are developing. [passage omitted] Noting that Turkey has a serious terrorist problem, Cindoruk described the PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] organization as a Marxist-Leninist armed gang. Pointing out that the Armenians committed genocide during World War I, Cindoruk added: If the Ottomans had engaged in ethnic cleansing could the Armenians have lived in Turkey until 1915? He noted that Turkey wants to establish good relations with Armenia but that the Armenians should withdraw from the Azerbaijani territories they have occupied. Cindoruk also attended a session of the National Assembly [end recording]
