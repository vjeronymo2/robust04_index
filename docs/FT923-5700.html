920828 FT 28 AUG 92 / Korean switch A consortium led by South Korea's Sunkyong Group will give up the lucrative contract awarded last week by the government to develop the country's second mobile telephone network, newspapers said, Reuter reports from Seoul. The English-language Korea Herald said the decision was taken after ruling Democratic Liberal party leader Kim Young-sam held discussions over the controversial awarding of the contract with President Roh Tae-woo. The son of Sunkyong chairman Choi Jong-hyon is married to Mr Roh's daughter.
