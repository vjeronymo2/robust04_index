Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Report by Petko Koprivica from Krajina] [Excerpt] The final outcome of the second round of presidential elections in the republic of Serbian Krajina is not known yet. However, all results so far indicate that independent candidate Milan Martic will assume the post of the head of this most westerly Serbian state. According to unofficial results, Milan Martic won 77,400 votes in 20 out of the total of 28 constituencies. The other presidential candidate, Milan Babic, received 72,130 votes. The Republican Electoral Commission holds the view that Martic's victory is a foregone conclusion. Here is what Marijana Puhaca, secretary of the aforementioned commission, said about the elections: [Begin Puhaca recording] By 1400 today, the Republican Electoral Commission received unofficial results for 19 out of the total of 28 constituencies. These 19 constituencies have 199,274 registered voters. Of these, 140,334 or 58 percent have cast their vote. In these 19 constituencies, Milan Babic won 68,495 votes, and Milan Martic 71,527 votes. By 1400 today, the Republican Electoral Commission received official results for only four communes, Drnis, Krnjak, Obrovac, and Plaski. [end recording] [passage omitted]
