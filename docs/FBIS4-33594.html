BFN [Report by the Cecilia Canones from the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] According to Senator Aydee Castillo de Lopez, president of the Senate Foreign Policy Committee, it is highly unusual for the United States to deny asylum to Haitian refugees, while, at the same time, asking Venezuela to receive them. The senator added that Venezuela currently is not in the best of economic and social conditions to receive the Haitian refugees. She stressed that it was quite unexpected that a highly developed nation such as the United States has decided not to give the refugees asylum, and yet it has asked a country like Venezuela--a nation facing a crisis--to receive them. She added that what is even more unusual is that the Venezuelan Government has decided to accept the request.
