BFN [Text] Russia and Kazakhstan have reached agreement on the Baykonur satellite-launching site, Kazakhstan's foreign minister said. What remains to be clarified are some "technical" points, Tuleutay Suleymenov told INTERFAX. He said Kazakhstan President Nursultan Nazarbayev, had recently put his signature to a Kazakh-Russian memorandum on Baykonur. Suleymenov said Kazakhstan is to lease the site to Russia for $118 million a year. Leninsk town, forming part of the Baykonur complex, is to be governed jointly by the two countries, he said. "Baykonur and Leninsk are on the territory of Kazakhstan and must be used jointly," he said.
