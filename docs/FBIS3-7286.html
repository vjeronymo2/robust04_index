Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 26 KYODO -- South Korean President Kim Yong-sam departed from Tokyo's Haneda airport on Saturday [26 March] for China where he will meet Chinese leaders to discuss North Korea's alleged nuclear arms program and Seoul's growing trade ties with Beijing. Kim and his wife Son Myong-sun concluded a three-day visit to Tokyo where the South Korean president urged joint efforts to persuade Pyongyang to give up its nuclear ambitions and stressed the importance of future-oriented bilateral relations.
