BFN [Interview with General Jose Nadal Payva, the Peruvian Air Force, FAP, commander, by unidentified reporter; place and date not given] [Text] [EXPRESO] Gen. Nadal, could you tell us how the FAP participates in drug enforcement actions? Nadal: The FAP has been carrying out joint operations with the PNP [Peruvian National Police] since 1985, seeking to neutralize drug trafficking activities in Peru. In 1981 we verified that traffickers and terrorists had formed an alliance. The FAP then began to operate from the Santa Lucia Base because illegal activities like that threaten national security. Through Decree Law No. 25426 of 10 April 1991, and Decree Law No. 042-DE/FAP of 16 June 1992, the FAP was legally empowered to control all landing strips in Huallaga and other places where coca is grown. The FAP is thus responsible for destroying clandestine landing strips and for downing planes intercepted in Peruvian air space that refuse to obey orders to land. To carry out its mission, the FAP uses its A-37, T-27, Y-12, and T-41 planes as well as gunships that can operate day and night. EXPRESO: What are the results of these operations? Nadal: Since 1991 the FAP has spotted, intercepted, captured and/or destroyed about 200 Peruvian and foreign aircraft that have violated the Civil Aviation Law and/or engaged in trafficking. More than $1.2 billion resulting from drug trafficking and 3.6 tonnes of cocaine paste have also been seized. The FAP, the Peruvian Army, and the PNP have destroyed clandestine landing strips and cocaine paste laboratories in various areas including Caballococha and Puerto Maldonado. It should also be mentioned that more than 7,000 flight hours have been invested in enforcement operations. Considering our limitations, the balance of our participation is encouraging and positive, not just because of our achievements but also because of the high morale of our troops. EXPRESO: What contribution is the FAP making to the current pacification process regarding the civic actions and social aide in general? Nadal: The FAP has stepped up its participation, as far as civic actions and social aide is concerned, through the following activities: Granting free medical attention in clinical medicine, odontology, ophthalmology, otolaryngology, pediatrics, gynecology, urology, cardiology, traumatology, pneumology, and various types of surgery, as well as medical evacuations, distribution of medicines, massive vaccination, and providing air transportation for civil personnel in emergency cases in outlying towns where the FAP has personnel stationed. During this year the FAP has performed some 710 flight hours transporting to and from the interior approximately 15,500 people and 100,000 kg of foodstuffs, clothing, equipment, and machinery necessary for the national pacification process in which the national government is engaged. Moreover, we have organized and implemented youth clubs called "Los Aguiluchos" [Little Eagles] that involve approximately 1,500 children between 6 and 12 years of age who receive clothing, civilian-patriotic teachings, talks on the prevention of drug addiction and infectious diseases, remedial courses, sports, handicraft, and recreation. This social assistance also reaches the parents with periodic conferences on subjects like "Responsible Parenthood" and "Child Psychology." The FAP also facilitates communication free of charge for local inhabitants at certain hours of the day. EXPRESO: You headed the Peruvian delegation to the latest International Air Fair in Chile. How does our FAP compared with the technological and scientific developments displayed there? Nadal: The fair was visited by more than 900,000 spectators where 31 countries exhibited their goods. The FAP used the opportunity to create a landmark in its relationship with the sister Latin American Air Forces by reasserting the well-known professional level of its personnel through a presentation, which was described as excellent both from the operative as well as technical viewpoint, operating M2000 aircraft and state of the art technology. The FAP Aircraft Maintenance Service (Seman) has proven that it is able to deal with high technology and that it provides FAP planes with excellent service. This can also be applied to other types of Peruvian or foreign aircraft.
