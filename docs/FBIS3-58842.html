Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report by Bela Anda: "Brandt's Widow Breaks Silence"] [Text] Was Karl Wienand, the powerful Bundestag group chairman of the Social Democratic Party [SPD] between 1976 and 1974, a KGB spy? Brigitte Seebacher-Brandt (47), Willy Brandt's widow, told BILD: "In the second half of March 1992, Valentin Falin, the former Soviet ambassador to Bonn, visited Brandt in Bonn." During this meeting, Falin told Willy Brandt that Wienand was a KGB spy, Brigitte Seebacher-Brandt stated. The Federal Intelligence Service was then informed. Wienand emphatically rejected all accusations yesterday. In the meantime, official Bonn has been alarmed by the suspicions of espionage that also affect former SPD Bundestag group chairman Wehner, who died in 1990. Waigel, the head of the Christian Democratic Union, called on the SPD in Munich "to clarify the matter." SPD leader Scharping did not see much new in the accusations against Wehner. BILD asked former SPD leader Vogel: "Did Brandt tell you anything about Wienand's activities as an agent?" Vogel replied: "For reasons of principle, I do not want to disclose details of talks that were held when Willy Brandt knew he would die soon. I consider this respect for human dignity. Thus, no conclusions can be drawn in any direction."
