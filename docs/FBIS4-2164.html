BFN [Text] Beijing, April 6 (XINHUA) -- Song Jian, Chinese State councillor and minister in charge of the State Science Commission, is to fly to Washington on Saturday [9 April] for a Sino-U.S. meeting on science and for a visit. According to a spokesman of the State Science Commission, Song will go to the States as guest of John Gibbons, director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy. During his stay in Washington, Song will co-chair with Gibbons the sixth meeting of the Sino-U.S. Joint Commission on Scientific and Technological Cooperation. Scientific and technological cooperation weighs heavy in Sino- U.S. relations. Since China and the United States signed an agreement on scientific and technological cooperation in 1979, much progress has been made in the fields concerned, with bilateral economic cooperation promoted and Sino-U.S. relations improved. The agenda of the Washington meeting is understood to include a review of achievements of Sino-U.S. scientific and technological cooperation, intensive discussions on medical and health matters, environmental protection, energy and materials, besides probing ways for businessmen's involvement in these aspects. The two countries will strike new protocols and renew old ones for scientific and technological cooperation. Song will meet American senior leaders to exchange views on issues of common concern. He will also visit Harvard University, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, the Ford Automobile company and General Motors. Among the delegation are Liu Jiang, minister of agriculture, Yan Hongmuo, director of the National Oceanography Department, and Hu Qiheng, vice-president of the Chinese Academy of Sciences.
