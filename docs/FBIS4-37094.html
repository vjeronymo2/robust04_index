BFN [Text] Tunis, AL-ANWAR--Arab diplomatic sources close to the Palestinian-U.S. meetings--Yasir 'Arafat's meetings with Robert Pelletreau, U.S. assistant secretary of state for Middle Eastern affairs, last month and with U.S. Ambassador to Tunisia John McCarthy the day before yesterday--disclosed that 'Arafat raised several issues with Pelletreau and again with Ambassador McCarthy. Those issues include the slowdown in the receipt of funds necessary to run the self-rule mechanism and the fate of the Palestinian residents in Lebanon and Syria, since they are not allowed to return under the Gaza-Jericho agreement, which closes the door to the return of the refugees from 1948. Most Palestinians residing in Syria and Lebanon are refugees from 1948. According to close diplomatic sources, 'Arafat expressed a wish for the Palestinians in Lebanon to be granted citizenship and permanent resettlement there. 'Arafat said to Pelletreau: Do you have any other option? The U.S. official was silent before saying: "It is a proposal worthy of consideration."
