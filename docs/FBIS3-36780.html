Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku (February 7) TURAN--A group of Russian military inspectors presently is visiting Ganja, Press Center of the Popular [People's] Front of Azerbaijan reports states, citing a reliable source. The inspection is aimed at researching the situation and material-technical conditions of the former Russian landing-troops military base #104, which was withdrawn from Ganja in May 1993 under President Elchibey. TURAN believe that the military inspection by Russian officers testifies that plans for a Russian troop return to Azerbaijan is being carried out. Though officially, Baku denies this, reports from Moscow prove that the return of Russian troops to Azerbaijan is practically agreed upon. TURAN news agency has reported that one of the Russian military bases will be stationed in Ganja. Moreover, Moscow insists on stationing its landing forces in Lenkoran and Nakhichevan, it also demands the creation of Russian Air Forces base on a former Soviet Air Forces base in Azerbaijan.
