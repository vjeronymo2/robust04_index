BFN [Report by BELINFORM-TASS] [Text] Minsk 30 Mar, TASS--The Belarusian parliament adopted a law on the Belarusian Constitutional Court on Wednesday. Authors of the draft law believe that the Constitutional Court "has been fully removed from the sphere of political activities" and its task is to ensure that legislative acts comply with the Constitution. The law envisages that fundamental principles of the activities of the Constitutional Court is legitimacy, collaboration and publicity. Under the law, a Belarusian citizen, having a juridical degree, high qualification in the sphere of law and high moral standards, is eligible for being elected member of the Constitutional Court. The law stipulates the age limit for judges at 60. Citizens elected to the Constitutional Court shall not engage in entrepreneurial activities or do any other work for a salary except teaching and research. The law envisages that the court may begin proceedings to establish whether an act is constitutional on the initiative of the president, parliament, a permanent parliamentary commission, a group of no less than 70 lawmakers, the Supreme Court, the highest economical court and the prosecutor general of the Republic. The Constitutional Court shall be elected by the parliament. The Constitutional Court is entitled to adopt resolutions and begin its activities only if no less than seven judges have been elected. Chairman of a parliamentary commission of the Belarusian parliament assured that elections of the judges of the Constitutional Court may be held no sooner than the Belarusian parliament meets in its spring session scheduled approximately on 17 May. Dmitriy Bulakhov, chairman of the parliamentary commission for legislature, said that 1 July 1994 is a realistic date when the Constitutional Court is expected to begin its work.
