BFN [Text] A session of the Vitebsk City Council has resolved to address the Supreme Soviet with a request to consider the possibility of holding a nationwide referendum regarding the issue of granting official status to the Russian language in Belarus. The reason for such an appeal to the highest legislative body was a poll conducted among Vitebsk residents, which was held by an initiative group. The poll showed that 5,000 city residents support the idea of holding -- on the same day as presidential elections -- a city-wide referendum regarding the introduction of bilingualism. Such a stance is also supported [words indistinct] by 52 people's deputies in the Vitebsk City Council.
