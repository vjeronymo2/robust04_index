940315 FT 15 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Hartstone to raise at least Pounds 15m Hartstone, the hosiery and handbags group, yesterday announced details of a rescue refinancing including an equity issue to raise more than Pounds 15m this summer. Hartstone is required to repay Pounds 15m by October to its creditors as a condition of the new financing arrangements. These will provide lending facilities until January 15 1996. The group said yesterday it intended to issue shares to raise the funds, and to provide any necessary working capital. The new financing arrangements include: As part of July 1993's standstill arrangements, the banks made extra facilities available. The remaining Pounds 8.3m of this is to be repaid with about Pounds 3.3m of the amount owed at the beginning of the standstill period. The banks have converted the balance of their lending - about Pounds 45.1m - into secured loans. The holders of the notes issued by Hartstone Holdings, the US holding company, have agreed to continue to lend about Dollars 47.1m (Pounds 31.6m) of their original total loans of Dollars 50m, the balance of which is to be repaid. Of the continuing loans, a further Dollars 2.1m will be repaid on March 31. Charterhouse Group International will be paid about Dollars 900,000 in partial payment of the outstanding consideration for Etienne Aigner and Michael Stevens. The balance is to be converted into a secured term loan of about Dollars 11.9m of which Dollars 600,000 will be repaid on March 31. Aggregate interest to March 31 - about Pounds 5.6m - to the main creditors is to be paid.
