BFN [Text] On Wednesday morning President Boris Yeltsin might have a meeting with the chairmen of the two chambers of the Russian Federal Assembly Vladimir Shumeyko and Ivan Rybkin. The draft agreement on civic accord is expected to be the main item of discussion. On Monday evening the document was reportedly sent by the president to the State Duma. The draft has been circulated among parliamentary groups for discussion. The text submitted to the State Duma does not in fact differ from the version Yeltsin [submitted] to the speakers of the houses last Thursday. The only amendment made by Yeltsin was the exclusion from the section on the social and economic policies of the recognition of the need to raise salaries and wages with due account to inflation, to guarantee their timely payment, to hold back the prices of key food stuffs, to take steps to guarantee employment and to help territories where the threat of unemployment is the greatest. Interfax has been informed of Rybkin writing a letter to the president requesting to replace the section on the security of individuals and the state by a more detailed version approved in advance by the working group that drafted the document.
