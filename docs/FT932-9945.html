930511 FT 11 MAY 93 / International Company News: TMM offers Dollars 200m paper in US market TMM, the Mexican shipping group, has launched a Dollars 200m bond issue in the US domestic bond market, making it the first private-sector Latin American name to tap the Yankee bond market. The 10-year deal was increased to Dollars 200m from Dollars 150m in response to strong investor demand. The bonds carry a coupon of 9.25 per cent and were priced at 99.774 to yield 337 basis points over 10-year US Treasury bonds. Bear Stearns was lead manager, with Goldman Sachs the co-lead for the deal. The bonds are callable after five years. TMM is Mexico's largest shipping company and operates ports and a trucking company. It is rated BA2 by Moody's and B+ by S&P.
