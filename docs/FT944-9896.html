941114 FT 14 NOV 94 / Taipei may ease market curbs Taiwan is considering relaxation of curbs on foreign investment in the domestic stock market, in moves apparently timed to boost share prices just before the elections on December 3. Officials from the Finance Ministry, central bank, Economics Ministry and Planning Ministry are to meet this month to decide whether to raise or remove ceilings on foreign investment in listed shares, the Economic Daily News reported yesterday. Securities authorities have proposed lifting limits except for companies involved in cement, inland transportation, finance, commercial paper and natural gas. Government-approved foreign institutional investors today may not individually buy more than 5 per cent of the listed shares in a single stock or more than 10 per cent collectively. Foreign individuals may not to buy Taiwan shares. There is a ceiling of USDollars 7.5bn (Pounds 4.6bn) on foreign funds which may be remitted to the country to invest in domestic shares, representing about 4 per cent of market capitalisation. Each foreign institution may invest a maximum of Dollars 200m.
