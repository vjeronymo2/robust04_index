June 23, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

BAKER'S MESSAGE FOR SHAMIR'S NEW GOVERNMENT

I surely agree with your June 13 editorial about the obdurate character of the new Israeli government and Shamir's unfortunate choices for his cabinet. The uncompromising fundamentalist extremists have put a halt to the peace process.

But I disagree that Shamir was "forced" to turn to these extremists for support. No, he did it to perpetuate his power. Elections were an option.

More to the point are the words of Israel's prize-winning novelist, Yizhar Smilansky: "Why do we abandon the field to all sorts of obstinate little politicians who don't care about blood or death or the corruption of the soul -- who have endless patience for death? Hundreds have died and countless more will die; what matters to them is that they hold the winning political card."

ELIZABETH NAUSS

North Hollywood
