Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The inspector general of police, Tan Sri Rahim Noor, has admitted that it is difficult for the police to obtain information on allegations that the opposition in Malaysia were receiving money from the Australian Secret Intelligence Service, ASIS. He said this was expected since no one would in his right mind admit being involved in espionage. He disclosed this to reporters after the breaking of fast at the Police Training Center in Kuala Lumpur last night. However, Tan Sri Rahim said police would continue with investigation into the allegations. Prime Minister Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir Mohamed was also present at the function attended by some 1,000 police personnel of all ranks.
