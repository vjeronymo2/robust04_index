Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 10 (CTK) -- One hundred and twenty troops forming the Czech UNPROFOR [United Nations Protection Force] unit will be deployed in the Croatian area of Zepce north of Sarajevo, the Czech defense minister said on Thursday. Speaking at a meeting of the parliamentary foreign committee discussing the situation in Bosnia and Herzegovina, Antonin Baudys said the transport will take place on March 15. Asked whether the soldiers knew they were going to protect a Serbian enclave surrounded by Bosnian Muslims, Baudys said all of the soldiers volunteered for inclusion in the unit. They will be equipped with submachine guns and bulletproof vests and will use 14 armored personnel carriers, 11 trucks, five "special trailers" and one ambulance. UNPROFOR command has not yet announced how long the unit, which is to operate within a Jordanian regiment, would be stationed in Croatia. [as received] The Czech government approved the consignment on Wednesday [9 March].
