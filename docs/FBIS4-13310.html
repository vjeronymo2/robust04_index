BFN ["Israel, PLO Strike Communications Deal" by Mona Salem] [Text] Cairo, April 19 (AFP)--Israel and the PLO struck a deal Tuesday [19 April] on communications systems for Gaza and Jericho in another step toward the launch of Palestinian autonomy, chief PLO negotiator Nabil Sha'th said. "We have just completed a difficult agreement which took a lot of time and (which gives) to the autonomy authority the right to obtain the necessary frequencies for the Palestinian police," Sha'th said. "An accord was also reached on giving the Palestinians a radio station and telephone network," he said without giving details. Each side had claimed the right to decide on their own frequencies, with Israel wanting to avoid disrupting its existing bands, an Israeli negotiator told AFP earlier. The communications accord follows deals last week to set up a Palestinian police force and release 5,000 prisoners, but PLO officials on Tuesday accused Israel of backtracking on the prisoner issue. One official who requested anonymity said Israel refused to set a date for freeing 1,000 of the prisoners until it received a PLO assurance that the Palestinian police "had the situation well under control." The official who asked not to be named said the 1,000 were all serving long sentences for crimes judged serious by Israel. PLO negotiator Muhammad Dahlan said Israel took a tougher line on the prisoners after the Islamic Resistance Movement Hamas carried out attacks that killed 12 Israelis this month. Israel "now categorically rejected the release of certain Hamas members," said Dahlan, who is in charge of the prisoner file. Under last week's accord 2,500 prisoners are to be released when Israel and the PLO sign a final accord to launch self-rule in the West Bank town of Jericho and the Gaza Strip. An additional 2,500 prisoners would be set free three weeks later when limited autonomous powers were transferred to the Palestinians. More than 10,000 Palestinians are estimated to be in Israeli jails. A PLO negotiator said that Israeli proposals on jurisdiction in the self-rule areas still fell short of Palestinian demands. The two sides had also to settle the issue of water sharing between Israelis and Palestinians. In the Gaza Strip and West Bank, Israeli troops arrested about 285 suspected Islamic fundamentalists in a major crackdown following a series of revenge attacks for the Hebron massacre, Palestinian sources said. The vast majority were suspected of links with Hamas, which has claimed the bombings of two buses in which 12 Israelis died, and an axe attack on Monday in which five Israelis were hurt. It was the biggest crackdown on fundamentalists since Israel arrested hundreds in December 1992 following a wave of killings. Israel deported 415 of the fundamentalist suspects to Lebanon but allowed them to return last December. The group has threatened to launch five attacks on Israeli targets to avenge the massacre of 30 Moslems by a Jewish settler in Hebron on February 25.
