BFN [Text] The Palestinian-Israeli talks on the implementation of the Gaza-Jericho agreement will resume in Cairo today. The Palestinian side will be chaired by Dr. Nabil Sha'th, Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat's political adviser. The Israeli side will be chaired by General Amnon Shahaq, Israeli deputy chief of staff. Shim'on Peres stated that there is a great possibility of completing the Israeli withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho at the end of the current month. Peres said in an interview with the Qatari newspaper AL-SHARQ that the forces of the Israeli army have started withdrawing from the autonomy area. Last night, Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa held a meeting with PLO Executive Committee member Mahmud 'Abbas. The foreign minister stated after the meeting that the meeting is part of the framework of the continuing Palestinian-Egyptian coordination in the peace process. He said that they discussed the current stage of the peace talks, which will resume this morning. He said this new round of talks will continue for two or three days, after which the two sides will be able to set the date for starting implementation of the Declaration of Principles. PLO Executive Committee member Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh stated yesterday that the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles is in its death throes. 'Abd-Rabbuh demanded that immediate talks should be started on the final position of all the occupied territories. He said that autonomy talks with Israel cannot continue because it wants total hegemony over the Palestinian police force in the occupied territories.
