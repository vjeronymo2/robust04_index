BFN [TA SR report: "Indian-Slovak Enterprise"] [Text] The Tatra Sipox a.s. [shareholding company] at Banovce nad Bebravou and BEML, the largest Indian manufacturer of military equipment, reached a preliminary agreement on the founding of a joint venture yesterday. This was announced by Jozef Majsky, president of Tatra Sipox a.s., and confirmed by M. Mallikarjun, Indian minister of state for defense. The joint venture will produce trucks using Indian engines and Tatra chassis and cabins. They will be assembled partly in Slovakia and partly in India. [Bratislava HOSPODARSKE NOVINY in Slovak on 3 June on page 2 carries a 100-word report on the Indian minister's visit to Banovce nad Bebravou, which quotes Tatra Sipox President Majsky as saying that the joint venture should produce "at least 2,000 vehicles a year over a period of five years."]
