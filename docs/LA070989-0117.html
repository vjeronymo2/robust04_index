July 9, 1989, Sunday, Bulldog Edition

PROSPECTORS TRAPPED BY BLIZZARDS IN CHINA

Blizzards in remote western China have trapped 8,000 gold prospectors for more than a month and 36 have died, the official Xinhua News Agency said Friday.

Altitude sickness caused the deaths, it said.

Helicopters on Friday dropped 8 1/2 tons of food into the Hoh Xil Lake area of Qinghai province, more than 16,500 feet above sea level, the report said.
