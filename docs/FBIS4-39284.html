BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow June 4 TASS -- South Korean President Kim Young- sam has received ITAR-TASS General Director Vitaliy Ignatenko for an unofficial meeting to discuss aspects of and prospects for Russian- South Korean interaction in information, publishing and humanitarian spheres. When heading the NOVOYE VREMYA journal, Vitaliy Ignatenko was a first Russian correspondent to keep contact with South Korean opposition leader Kim Young-sam and used the journal to call for the establishment of Russian-South Korean diplomatic relations. ITAR-TASS sent its correspondent to Seoul before the official Russian recognition of South Korea, and he is still the only Russian newsman in South Korea, a most dynamically developing state of the Asia-Pacific region.
