BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 7 April. The increase in interest rates on the U.S. market, and the expectations that this rate will continue to grow, has prompted foreign investors to withdraw approximately $1 billion from the Brazilian stock market in one week. The decision has also affected the Brazilian foreign debt, as higher interest will make debt certificates more expensive. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 5 Apr 94 Section 2 p 1) The Sao Paulo stock market yesterday registered its sharpest drop of the year (10.5 percent), with losses estimated at between $12 and $16.5 billion. This drop was prompted by a U.S. Federal Reserve decision to increase interest rates. According to Citibank, on 29 March the stock market value stood at $132 billion, a loss of $16.5 billion. Fibra Bank director Julio Krauspenhar estimated the loss at $12 billion. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 5 Apr 94 p B3) The Sao Paulo State Federation of Industries, FIESP, and the Sao Paulo State Industries Center has reported that the Sao Paulo industrial activity level dropped by 1.5 percent in February in comparison with January. Industrial activity in February has nonetheless increased by 3.6 percent in comparison with the same month last year. The use of installed industrial capacity climbed from 75.1 percent in January to 75.3 percent in February. Meanwhile, real sales of electric-electronic and communications materials increased by 65.7 percent, mechanical capital goods and agricultural machinery sales increased by 15.2 percent, car industry sales increased by 11.7 percent, furniture sales increased 9.6 percent, and metalworks sales by 9.5 percent in January 1994 in comparison with January 1993. In all, sales of Sao Paulo industry dropped in February by 0.4 percent in comparison with the previous month, which is still 4.6 percent more than the sales registered in February 1993. The hours worked in February in the industry sector dropped by 1.5 percent in comparison with January, and the average real salary increased by 3.9 percent, while the salary mass increased by 1.6 percent on real terms. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 31 Mar p 10)
