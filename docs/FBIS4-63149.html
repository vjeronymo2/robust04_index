BFN [Text] Simferopol, 19 May (SNARK) -- Nagorno-Karabakh Republic [NKR] has signed its first interstate treaty. As SNARK newsman reported from Simferopol, the comprehensive treaty between Nagorno-Karabakh and Crimean Republic was signed by the NKR Defense Committee Chairman Robert Kocharyan and by Crimean Republic President Yuri Meshkov. Parties observed that the signed document will serve a good basis for cooperation in political, economic, cultural, and other spheres. The treaty has been signed during the visit of Karabakh delegation in Crimea who attended the meetings related with 50th anniversary of Crimean Tatar deportation. As note the observers, the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic -- yet unrecognized in the world -- has no such treaty signed with any country, not even Armenia. Today the Karabakh delegation left Simferopol for home.
