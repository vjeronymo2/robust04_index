BFN [Text] The airline company KyrgyzAba Zholdary and the U.S. aerospace corporation Boeing have signed an agreement in Bishkek on leasing two Boeing-737-200 aircraft. Each aircraft accomodates 120-130 passengers and uses half as much fuel as the Ty-154. Its crew consists of only two pilots. The American side agreed to a four-year-long lease. The first year our airline company will not pay rent -- it will earn some money first and only then will it pay for the lease. It took half a year to negotiate the deal and talks were held both in Seattle and in Bishkek. Our experts familiarized themselves with the manufacturing process and the system of servicing and utilizing. The American side will train our crews as a part of the lease payment and also provide us with technical experts. Around 20 pilots will soon go to Seattle to be trained there. At the end of this year, two Boeing aircraft will land at Manas airport. They will fly to Moscow, Tashkent, Istanbul, Novosibirsk, and other cities. The Boeing aircraft can cover the distance of 4,000-5,000 km.
