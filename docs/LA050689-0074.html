May 6, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

MEDICAL RESEARCH AND ANIMAL RIGHTS

The editorial is little more than a mean-spirited tirade directed against animal rights activists. Referring to these activists as cranks, and as being self-absorbed and vulgar, and as people engaged in self-righteous tantrums is uncalled for.

In the same issue in the View Section there is an article about Jane Goodall, a scientist of unquestionable reputation, whose attitude concerning medical research on animals is much more reasonable than that presented on the Editorial Page. She advocates that more money and research be invested in funding alternative research methods and that there be more effective supervision to see that the animals in medical laboratories receive the best possible treatment.

Surely there must be ways of discovering medical knowledge other than continually torturing so many helpless animals. If there is such a thing as a just God, the human species will be punished for its cruel treatment of other animals.

RONALD N. DAY

Lemon Grove
