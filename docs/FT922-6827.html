920521 FT 21 MAY 92 / Observer: Outside bet If you want to understand the Danes, go to Ghana. That tip for anyone betting on the outcome of Denmark's impending referendum on the Maastricht Treaty, comes from Britain's former ambassador there Sir James Mellon, now chairman of Scottish Homes. He has just marked his long love affair with the country, which began with his first visit as a teenage hitch-hiker in 1948, by publishing a book in Danish called Og gamle Danmark . . . (the first two words meaning roughly: 'And as for dear old'). But in it he reveals that it wasn't until his foreign office duties involved him with the Ashantis in Ghana that he twigged how to fathom the Danes. He suddenly realised that they are not so much a nation as a tribe, with the tribe's much greater exclusiveness - which would seem to lead him to expect them to turn the treaty down. The tribal exclusiveness makes them touchy about the idea of international co-operation, especially if it threatens to interfere with what they see as their Danishness, he writes. 'That seems to be precisely the worry that many Danes have about European Union. The very word 'union' suggests an unwelcome intrusiveness.'
