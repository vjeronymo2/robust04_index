Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 8 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese Ministry of Labor and the Ministry of Personnel issued a joint circular on the implementation of the State Council's regulations on the new working hours limits. The circular said that workers in Chinese Government and state bodies, institutions, enterprises and other organizations will work eight hours a day and 44 hours a week from March 1. It stressed that no organisation or individuals should extend working hours without authorization. If the working hours of employees have to be extended, the employees should later be given free time in lieu, it added. It said that state bodies and institutions will adopt unified working hours and rest days, that is, following a two-week cycle, with Saturday and Sunday as the rest days in the first week starting from March 1 and Sunday as the rest day in the second week. It said that all enterprises should follow the new 44-hour week system. It said that enterprises and units which have special difficulties in adopting the system can be given two months' grace, with the deadline set at May 1, 1994. Officials from the two ministries pointed out that the regulations aimed at allowing employees more time for studying, educating children and attending household chore. They said that the regulations should be implemented by all state organs, institutions, enterprises and other organizations in China, including state enterprises, collective enterprises, private enterprises and foreign-funded enterprises, and all trades such as mines, factories, building industry, communications and transportation, timber industry, state farms, commercial and service industries. They stressed that no units should increase their labor force or expenditure, or reduce the income of employees on the pretext of shorter working hours.
