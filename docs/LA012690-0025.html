January 26, 1990, Friday, Valley Edition

HIGH SCHOOL ROUNDUP;

2 LATE GOALS HELP SIMI VALLEY GIRLS TO ESCAPE WITH WIN OVER ROYAL

Simi Valley High, the top-ranked girls' soccer team in the Southern Section 4-A Division, scored two goals in the final four minutes to pull out a 2-0 Marmonte League victory over unranked Royal at Simi Valley on Thursday night.

"I wasn't worried about losing," Simi Valley Coach Mark Johnson said. "I was worried about going into overtime."

Royal Coach Kevin Corley said he was not dejected despite the loss.

"What this shows me is that we can play with the No. 1 team," he said. "To keep it close for that long was an accomplishment for us."

Royal sweeper Stevie Campbell touched the ball with her hand within the Highlanders' penalty area, setting up sophomore Amy Hunter's scoring penalty kick with four minutes remaining.

A half-minute later, halfback Liz Albin scored from four yards on Michelle Light's head pass.

The shutout was the 10th for Simi Valley (22-0-0 overall, 7-0-0 in league play) and the 38th career shutout for senior goalkeeper Kim Duran.

Royal is 14-4-1, 3-2-1.
