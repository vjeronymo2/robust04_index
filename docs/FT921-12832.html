920124 FT 24 JAN 92 / Appointments: Hopkins quits NIIDB Tony Hopkins, chief executive of the Industrial Development Board, Northern Ireland's main jobs promotion agency, is quitting after four years. The announcement came as a surprise to business circles. A brief statement issued by the Department of Economic Development confirmed that Hopkins had been invited to take up a position in the private sector. As a permanent secretary, Hopkins is one of the highest paid civil servants in the province; his post carries a salary of over Pounds 60,000. His resignation comes at a time of changes in direction for the organisation as a result of the government's emphasis on competitiveness. Government sources stressed that his departure was unrelated to recent criticism of the IDB by Noel Stewart, senior partner of Coopers & Lybrand Deloitte. Stewart argued last week that the IDB did not have the calibre of executives required to achieve the government's new competitive strategy.
