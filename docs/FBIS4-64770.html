BFN [Text] Brussels, May 6 (AFP) -- The European Union [EU] said here Friday [6 May] that it was eager to help the new government of South Africa to establish "a democratic and nonracial society." Theodoros Pangalos, Greek chairman of the EU's Council of Ministers, said the Union was ready to embark on an economic and political dialogue with the new government aimed at quickly boosting economic trade and aid. "The EU warmly welcomes the holding of the first democratic elections in South Africa and congratulates all the people of South Africa and its leaders on this historic occasion," he said at a meeting of development aid ministers. Pangalos added the EU's confidence that all parties in South Africa would meet the commitments they made during negotiations leading up to last week's all-race elections, and would pursue peaceful dialogue.
