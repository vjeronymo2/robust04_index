BFN [Text] 'Ala'eddin Borujerdi, deputy foreign minister for Asian-Pacific affairs, met ambassadors from North Korea and South Korea in separate meetings today. Borujerdi noted the Islamic Republic of Iran's profound concern over increasing tension in the Korean peninsula. Borujerdi emphasized that Tehran believes direct talks between the two Koreas are the best way to solve current problems. The North Korean envoy said that American actions are the main cause of problems between the two Koreas. The envoy stressed that North Korean officials are ready to solve problems of the two Koreas through negotiations. The South Korean envoy stated Seoul's readiness to solve existing problems between the two Koreas via direct negotiations. The South Korean envoy said that the Islamic Republic of Iran, through its good relations with the two Koreas, can play an effective role in reducing tension between the two Koreas.
