BFN [Text] Yerevan, 5 May (SNARK)--What solution of the Karabakh problem is the best? This question has been asked to 1,200 Yerevan residents by the Sociological Research Center of the Armenian Academy of Sciences. Over 40 percent responded that Nagorno-Karabakh has to become an independent state, 30 percent told that Karabakh must become a part of Armenia, 24.4 percent decided that Karabakh shall be included in Russia. A little over 4 percent think that Karabakh needs to be integrated in the Caucasus Federation. Notably, only 0.6 percent of respondents voted for Karabakh autonomy within Azerbaijan, and another 0.4 percent told that it has to remain a part of Azerbaijan. The poll has been carried out in the first half of April by USIA order.
