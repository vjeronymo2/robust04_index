921208 FT 08 DEC 92 / Mystery surrounds Jensen Car consortium JENSEN Car Company, the past purveyor of hand-assembled luxury sports cars is passing out of the hands of Grant Thornton, the receivers, to a consortium of undisclosed English businessmen for an undisclosed sum, it was announced yester-day. The nameless consortium is planning to resurrect the Jensen Interceptor. A new version of the car had been announced early this year - when it found just one customer. The consortium also plans to install at the Jensen factory in West Bromwich a new industrial division making undisclosed but patented DIY household products under licence. Grant Thornton, amid much talk of saving 'a great name in British engineering,' said that contracts for the sale of Jensen as a going concern had been exchanged and that the deal would be completed by Christmas. Jensen, previously owned by Unicon Holdings of Stockport, collapsed in September with debts of Pounds 638,000, a 32,000 sq ft factory, some goodwill, a mass of tools, some spare parts, one M-registered Jensen and one old sports car, partly rebuilt. Grant Thornton said then that the company was difficult to value. The 20-strong Jensen workforce is remaining in place. Mr David Heseltine, the general manager of the company and barrier between the world and the anonymous investors, promised that another 15 more people would be hired for the industrial operation and that the carmaking staff would double at the end of next year. Mr Heseltine talked in terms of turnover at Pounds 3m in the 15 months to March 1994 and Pounds 10m in the following year, reduced overheads because of the new industrial production and research and development spending. Against the background of that glossy prospect, he disclosed that he had no orders for the Interceptor, just 'six to eight people with a genuine interest.'
