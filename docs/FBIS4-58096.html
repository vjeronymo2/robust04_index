BFN [Text] Tunis, 1 May (MENA) -- The Fatah Central Committee tonight ended its consultations on the formation of the National Authority Council that will run self-rule affairs in the Gaza strip and Jericho. Al-Tayyib 'Abd-al-Rahman, Fatah Central Committee member and Palestinian ambassador to Jordan, said the National Authority Council will comprise 25 members, half of them from the occupied Palestinian lands and the other half from Palestinians of the diaspora, in addition to Yasir 'Arafat. Among key members from diaspora Palestinians are Economic Department Chief Ahmad Quray', also known as Abu-'Ala'; Dr. Nabil Sha'th; and Intisar al-Wazir, also known as Umm-Jihad. Key representatives from residents of the occupied Palestinian territories are Faysal al-Husayni from the West Bank and Zakariya al-Agha from Gaza. The council will also include Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh, Samir Ghawshah, Yasir 'Amr, and Muhammad Zuhdi al-Nashashibi. Faruq Qaddumi, head of the PLO Political Department, will not be a council member because the Political Department will remain outside Gaza and Jericho, most probably in Tunis. Mahmud 'Abbas, also known as Abu-Mazin, expressed the desire not to enter Gaza and Jericho, preferring to remain abroad to carry out other political tasks. He also expressed the desire not to become a council member.
