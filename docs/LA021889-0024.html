February 18, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

MORALITY AND LYLE WHEELER'S MISSING OSCARS

I too am searching for a lost Oscar -- the one presented to my uncle, Victor Young, in 1956 for his original musical score for "Around the World in 80 Days."

Because I am now the only living blood relative of Victor Young, I want the Oscar for me and for my children and their children.

No one can put a price on the Oscar; it means more than money to me. My uncle was a pioneer in the field of motion picture music -- the originator of movie theme songs such as "Golden Earrings," "Stella by Starlight," "Love Letters" and "When I Fall in Love."

BOBBIE HILL FROMBERG

Van Nuys
