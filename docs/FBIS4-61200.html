BFN [Text] At President Guntis Ulmanis' initiative, Estonian State President Lennart Meri and Lithuanian State President Algirdas Brazauskas came to his residence in Jurmala yesterday. The meeting was private. The presidents discussed the internal situation in the Baltic states and foreign policy issues. No documents were adopted. All three presidents agreed not to make a statement for the press. However, respecting the specific character of reporters' work, the president of our country [Latvia] decided to hold a small news conference. Guntis Ulmanis said at Maikapara House today that the agreements signed between Latvia and Russia were discussed during the presidents' meeting. All the presidents of the Baltic states expressed the hope that the agreements concluded between Latvia and Russia would speed up the Russian Army withdrawal from Estonia. Ulmanis noted that a regular round of talks was held in Tallinn today. The presidents of Latvia, Lithuania, and Estonia also discussed cooperation with those countries which expressed support for the agreements signed between Latvia and Russia. The agreements concluded have an important role in the stabilization of the Baltic states. The presidents expressed hope that Western countries which supported the process of drafting the agreements will continue to watch how they are implemented. This evening at the president's residence, Guntis Ulmanis will meet ambassadors accredited in Latvia.
