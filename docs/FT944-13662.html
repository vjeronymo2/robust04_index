941026 FT 26 OCT 94 / Lords concede Howard's plans The Lords last night accepted plans by Mr Michael Howard, home secretary, for secure training centres for persistent young offenders as peers debated last week's Commons votes to reverse defeats inflicted by Lords on the government. Lord Carr, the Tory former home secretary who led July's revolt against the proposals in the Criminal Justice and Public Order Bill, told peers: 'It seems to me of no value to ask the Commons to think again for a second time. Indeed I feel that constitutional damage might be done.' Home Office minister Baroness Blatch insisted there had been concessions on other aspects of the bill. She said: 'There is absolutely no guarantee that this will work, but this is a very real and genuine attempt to address the question of the young persistent offender.'
