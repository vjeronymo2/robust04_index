Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Ren Kan: "Bank Trade To Centralize Most Foreign Exchange"] [Text] The People's Bank of China has issued a set of provisional regulations on the selling, buying and payment of foreign exchange to ensure smooth sailing for financial reform. The regulations require all foreign exchange receipts--except those held by foreign-funded enterprises and domestic individuals-- to be sold to designated banks. Foreign loans and foreign exchange for debt repayment must also be handled by the banks. If foreign exchange is needed for import, export and other purposes, it can be purchased at the banks. The regulations, which take effect on April 1, are aimed at consolidating the country's foreign exchange in the banks to ensure sufficient supplies for converting renmenbi under current accounts. The official reaffirmed that the country will keep the foreign- exchange swap market, where foreign funded ventures can continue to buy and sell their foreign exchange instead of at the banks.
