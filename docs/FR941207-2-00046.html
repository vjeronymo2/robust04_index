Source of Foreign Law Information CITES Secretariat Notification to the Parties No. 800, issued on April 21, 1994, calls on Parties to suspend imports of Felis lynx specimens from Latvia. Action by the Fish and Wildlife Service Based on information received, Latvia has not satisfactorily implemented the recommendations of the CITES Standing Committee. Specifically, the government of Latvia must notify the CITES Secretariat of the following: when an export quota of Felis lynx has been approved, and provide a clarification as to whether this quota includes specimens that can be hunted, skins already on hand, or both. Therefore, in accordance with the responsibility of the United States under CITES, and effective immediately and until further notice from the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, no shipments of specimens of Felis lynx may be imported into the United States, directly or indirectly, from Latvia, unless an exemption in CITES Article VII applies. Source of Foreign Law Information CITIES Secretariat Notification to the Parties No. 800, issued on April 21, 1994, calls on Parties to suspend imports of Aratinga erythrogenys specimens from Peru. Action by the Fish and Wildlife Service Based on information received, Peru has not satisfactorily implemented the recommendations of the CITES Standing committee. Specifically, the Management Authority of Peru should notify the CITES Secretariat of the following: The establishment and announcement of an annual export quota for Aratinga erythrogenys that is consistent with sustainable offtake, and the scientific basis for the management program for Aratinga erythrogenys.
