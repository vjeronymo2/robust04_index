September 26, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

HOW TO HELP VICTIMS OF HURRICANE

Americans who want to help victims of Hurricane Hugo may contact these organizations:

-- American Red Cross, 17th and D Streets NW, Washington, D.C., 20006. To donate cash, call 803-251-6016, 251-6006, 251-6008 or 251-6000, or contact your local chapter. The national toll-free telephone number is 800-453-9000.

-- Christian Children's Fund, Box 26511, Richmond, Va., 23261.

-- Church World Service, 475 Riverside Dr., New York, N.Y., 10115.

-- Direct Relief International, Box 30820, Santa Barbara, Calif., 93130-0820.

-- Operation USA, 7615 1/2 Melrose Ave., Los Angeles, Calif., 90046.

-- Oxfam-America, 115 Broadway, Boston, Mass., 02116.

-- Salvation Army World Service, 1025 Vermont Ave. N.W., Washington, D.C., 20005.

-- Save the Children, Hurricane Fund, P.O. Box 970, Westport, Conn., 06881.

On Monday, Charleston, S.C., Mayor Joseph P. Riley Jr. also announced contributions could be sent to: City of Charleston, Omni Hotel, 130 Market St., Charleston, S.C., 29401. Call 803-720-3838.
