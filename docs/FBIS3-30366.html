Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Croatian President Franjo Tudjman received in the presidential palace today a delegation of the Serb National Party [in Croatia] comprising party chairman Milan Djukic, Veselin Pejnovic, and Veljko Miljevic, a statement from the president's office says. They informed President Tudjman about efforts made by their party toward normalizing Croatian-Serbian relations, particularly about the party's initiative called Peace Without Alternative. President Tudjman said that Croatia was resolved to make necessary efforts for the Croatian areas under UN protection to be reintegrated into the constitutional and legal order of the Republic of Croatia, to achieve the normalization of Croatian-Serbian relations, and to restore permanent peace and stability in the area, primarily through political means. The talks were also attended by the president's advisers Smiljko Sokol and Ivic Pasalic, the statement says.
