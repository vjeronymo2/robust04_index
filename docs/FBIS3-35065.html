Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Travel from Cairo to Baghdad has stopped completely in the past few weeks due to deteriorating economic conditions, escalating violence, and the harassment of Egyptian nationals in Iraq by the ruling dictator's authorities. The head of the regime's interests section in Cairo has admitted that the regime has stopped granting entry visas to Egyptians because of the lack of job opportunities and the deteriorating economic conditions in Iraq. In another development, the Egyptian Foreign Ministry has instructed its mission in Baghdad to prepare an urgent report on the number of Egyptians being held prisoner in Iraq. A spokesman for the ministry has said that the number of Egyptian prisoners is not less than 500, sentenced to serve between two years and life imprisonment at hard labor.
