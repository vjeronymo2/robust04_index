BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Bolivia monitored through 2 June. Central Bank President Fernando Candia has reported that the fiscal deficit dropped by 206 million bolivianos in real terms during the first quarter of the year. He noted that public sector expenses dropped by 86 million bolivianos and that revenues rose by 120 million Bolivianos during this period. He said exports rose by 34 percent while imports rose by just 3.8 percent. The balance of payment deficit dropped 80 million bolivianos compared with the same period in 1993. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 21 May 94 National Section p 2) The trade balance foreign reserve account amounted to $504 million in the first four months of the year, registering a $4 million surplus. Exports totaled $130.2 million and money deposited in banks over exports amounted to $209.7 million. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 23 May 94 National Section p 3) The Muller Inc. consulting firm has reported that, in spite of difficulties in the financial sector, the banking system is showing a significant increase which guarantees financial stability. The group of largest banks have resources of over $400 million, middle-sized banks have between $200 million and $400 million, and small banks have less than $200 million. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 26 May 94 National Section p 1) Edmundo Salvatierra, the Bolivian Government Oil Deposits [YPFB] industrial manager, has disclosed that the YPFB plans to invest some $23 million this year to explore, produce, and store crude oil and natural gas in the tropical region of Cochabamba department. Salvatierra said the Finance and Economy Development Ministry has approved YPFB investments worth $103 million throughout the country. The projects involve exploration in Carrasco and Katari deposits and the Ivirza XI well, production in Carrasco X-5 and Carrasco X-6 wells, and the storing of crude oil extracted from Surubi, Carrasco, Katari, and Bulo Bulo oilfields that will be sent to Cochabamba and Santa Cruz. (La Paz PRESENCIA in Spanish 27 May 94 Second Section p 1)
