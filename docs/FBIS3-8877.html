Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Regarding President Bill Clinton's announcement made in the U.S. Congress on 14 March on the extension of the moratorium on nuclear tests until September 1995, Mircea Geoana, spokesman of the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, today declared the following: Romania salutes the decision that encourages the achievement of the goals of nonproliferation -- above all, the unconditioned prolongation over an indefinite period of time of the treaty on the nonproliferation of nuclear weapons -- as well as the negotiations currently taking place at the Geneva disarmament conference on a total ban on nuclear tests. We express our hope that in concordance with the efforts made by the international community to strengthen the current juridical instruments and institutions set up to prevent the spreading of mass destruction weapons, all other nuclear powers will take measures similar to those adopted by the U.S. party, the Foreign Ministry spokesman said.
