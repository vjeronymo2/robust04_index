940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / Adverts fall closes engineer magazine Engineering, a magazine for 'innovators in technology, manufacturing and management', which was founded in 1866, is closing. The Design Council, which owns and publishes the magazine, announced yesterday that the February issue would be the last because of continuing losses. The controlled circulation publication, which had an ABC circulation of 23,000 in 1992, was sent mainly to those involved in engineering design and senior management in relatively smaller companies. It is a sector hit hard by the recession and the magazine has found it difficult to win enough advertising to survive. Mr Anthony Land, publishing director of the Design Council said yesterday: 'I think we have done pretty well in all the circumstances but nevertheless not well enough.' In its December editorial the magazine bewailed the position of engineering in UK society compared with European rivals. It said: 'Until engineering is seen as both crucial to our economy and indeed spiritually enlightening we will stay a second rate industrial nation.'
