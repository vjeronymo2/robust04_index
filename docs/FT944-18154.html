941005 FT 05 OCT 94 / Pounds 100m for new towns sought Mr David Curry, the environment minister, yesterday unveiled a programme aimed at attracting Pounds 100m in private-sector funds for property development in new towns. The 'Invest In Success' scheme provides land in new towns at discounted prices to partnerships between district councils and the private sector. All are required to use the land for leisure or cultural facilities, environmental improvements, social uses or low cost housing. Funding will now go to 15 projects which have been chosen from 34 initial applicants. The winning projects are in Peterborough, Welwyn Hatfield in Hertfordshire, Sunderland, South Ribble and Chorley in Lancashire, Warrington and Runcorn in Cheshire, Redditch and Telford.
