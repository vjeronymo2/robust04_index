940808 FT 08 AUG 94 / Sales of new vans and trucks up 24.2% Registrations of new commercial vehicles increased 24.2 per cent last month as the recovery in sales of vans, trucks and buses spread to all types of buyer including small businesses, Kevin Done writes. Mr Alan Pulham, director of the National Franchised Dealers Association, said: 'On the philosophy that commercial vehicle operation serves as a barometer of economic welfare, the signs seem set fair for overall national economic recovery.' Total sales of new commercial vehicles rose last month to 8,739 from 7,038 in July last year, figures from the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders showed. Sales in the first seven months have risen 15.2 per cent to 120,109 from 104,255 in the corresponding period a year before. Registrations of three and four-axle rigid trucks have risen sharply with a jump of 74 per cent year-on-year in July. Overall, sales of heavy trucks (above 15 tonnes gross vehicle weight) rose 29.4 per cent in the first seven months of the year to 12,227.
