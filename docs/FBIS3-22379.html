Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, March 9 (XINHUA) -- The municipal government of this, China's largest economic center has designated 1994 as a "Year for Greening". It is mobilizing all its residents to "develop Shanghai into a clean, beautiful and comfortable ecological city." According to the plan, the government will build or expand four parks and complete a number of greening projects to expand the public green areas by 133 ha, and plant a 500-meter-wide tree belt around the city this year. Shanghai has made progress in beautifying its urban area over the past few years. Last year saw the city add 157.54 ha of green areas and four parks, and plant 10.23 million trees. The city now averages 1.15 sq m of green area per capita. The city's green coverage rate reached 13.5 percent of its urban area last year.
