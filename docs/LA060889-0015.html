June 8, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

SINGING PIRATE A WINNER AT HOLLYWOOD

Singing Pirate led all the way to win the $31,000 allowance feature for fillies and mares on the turf Wednesday at Hollywood Park.

Felidia, the favorite of the crowd of 12,960, finished three lengths back in second place but was disqualified for interference in the stretch and dropped to fourth. The stewards awarded the runner-up spot to Precious Metal, who finished 4 3/4 lengths behind Singing Pirate.

Trained by Ron Ellis and ridden by Robbie Davis, Singing Pirate covered 1 1/16 miles in 1:41 2/5 for her second victory in five starts and her first at more than seven furlongs. The 4-year-old filly paid $8.40, $6.60 and $4.20.

Precious Metal, with Frank Olivares, paid $11.80 and $5.80. Jennysport, with Gary Stevens, was moved up to third and paid $2.60.
