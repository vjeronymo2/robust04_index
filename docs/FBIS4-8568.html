CSO [Article by James Hider: "Skoda To Produce Cars in Poland"] [Text] The Mlada Boleslav automaker Skoda announced March 18 that it will produce automobiles abroad for the first time in its history. It intends to open a plant in the Polish city of Poznan that will turn out an estimated 5,000 cars this year, with a capacity for expansion in the future. Ludvik Kalma, director of the factory in Mlada Boleslav, explained that this is the only way to avoid restrictive tariffs on exporting Czech cars to Poland. Skoda has negotiated unsuccessfully with Polish officials to reduce these duties, which could increase car prices by as much as 41 percent.
