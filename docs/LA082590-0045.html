August 25, 1990, Saturday, Southland Edition

BRIEFLY

Rouse Co. President Killed in Plane Crash: A developer whose firm rejuvenated urban center around the country was killed along with his wife and daughter when their private twin-engine turboprop crashed between two houses in the Mattapan section of Boston.

The houses went up in flames, but their occupants escaped. Killed were pilot Michael Spear, 49, president of the Columbia, Md.-based company, his wife, Judy 47, and their 19-year old daughter, Jodi, police said. The firm's projects include Boston's Faneuil Hall shopping area, harbor Place in Baltimore and the South Street Seaport in new York.
