BFN [Text] Geneva, April 20 (XINHUA) -- China will become the most competitive nation in the world by 2010, according to a survey published here today. The survey of some 1,700 entrepreneurs across the world was jointly conducted by the World Economic Forum and the International Institute of Management Development for an annual report on the competitiveness of world countries. China ranks first in competitiveness by 2010, followed by the United States, Japan, Germany, South Korea and Singapore, according to the survey. The U.S., Japan and Singapore are ahead of others for the stress they put on their competitiveness. According to the 1993 report, the ranking in competitiveness is as follows: Japan, the United States, Denmark, Switzerland, Germany, the Netherlands, Austria, New Zealand, Sweden and Belgium.
