920918 FT 18 SEP 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Data ignored as Dow focuses on Europe Wall Street US STOCKS moved narrowly with no clear trend yesterday and activity was subdued as Wall Street remained focused on events in Europe, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average was finally 3.51 off at 3,315.70, after showing a gain of some six points at midday. NYSE turnover was unexceptional, at less than 190m shares, with falls leading rises by 940 to 785. The Standard & Poor's 500 was just 0.01 up at 419.93 and the Nasdaq composite put on 1.93 to 587.82. The Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia reported that its index of business activity in the area grew to 14.7 in September from August's 12.6, implying slow but steady expansion in the manufacturing sector. Initial unemployment claims for the week ended September 5 were up 6,000 to 400,000. There was little market reaction to the domestic economic news, as attention remained riveted on European money markets. GTE, the biggest US local telephone company, led NYSE trading. The stock firmed Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 33 after the company announced that it had priced a 30m common stock offering at Dollars 32 7/8 a share. GTE has about 895m shares outstanding. International Business Machines edged Dollars 7/8 higher to Dollars 83 3/4 , after losing Dollars 2 5/8 on Wednesday when analysts lowered their ratings on the stock and reduced earnings forecasts following news that Amdahl, another big mainframe computer maker, expected a third-quarter loss. Minnesota Mining & Manufacturing declined Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 101 3/4 after the company warned that its 1992 earnings could fall at the low end of analysts' estimates, depending on the economy. Analysts had predicted 1992 earnings of about Dollars 5.70 to Dollars 5.80 a share, after a 1991 figure of Dollars 5.26. United Technologies, the aero-engines to motor components group, tumbled Dollars 2 7/8 to Dollars 51 3/8 on reports that its third-quarter results would fall short of expectations. Boeing eased Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 35 3/8 after the company reached an agreement in principle with GPA Group, the aircraft leasing concern, to defer delivery of up to 154 aircraft worth Dollars 5bn for up to three years. National Westminster's American Depositary Shares rose Dollars 2 1/2 to Dollars 38 after the UK bank cut its base lending rate to 10 per cent from 12 per cent, bringing it back to its Wednesday morning level. Constar International soared Dollars 7 7/8 to Dollars 32 1/4 after agreeing to be acquired by Crown Cork & Seal for Dollars 32 1/2 a share. Crown Cork slipped Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 36 1/8 . In Nasdaq trading, Apple Computer lost Dollars 1 to Dollars 46 after an analyst at Donaldson Luf-kin & Jenrette downgraded his rating on the stock from 'neutral' to 'unattractive'. Canada A FIRMER Canadian dollar eased currency worries, but Toronto stocks ended lower as participants remained cautious. The French Maastricht vote on Sunday, the triple-witching expiry and concern about European currencies pressured the market. The TSE 300 index lost 7.7 at 3,446.1 and falls outpaced advances by 319 to 240 after volume of 31.3m shares.
