Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The first session of the Federation Council, the upper house of the new Russian parliament, will open at 10:00 on Tuesday [11 January]. It will be held in the former Russian Press House, Ul. Pushkinskaya 26, and is to be addressed by President Boris Yeltsin, a source in the office of First Deputy Prime Minister Vladimir Shumeyko told Interfax. A meeting chaired by Shumeyko on Monday prepared the agenda for the Federation Council's first day, which includes the election of the speaker, his deputies, chairpersons for 10 proposed committees and the senior bodies of the house, as well as voting on session regulations. Shumeyko is going to run for speaker, the source in his office said. Federation Council deputies, who asked not to be identified, told Interfax that, in addition to Shumeiko and former chairman of the Moscow City Council Nikolay Gonchar, candidates to the post of speaker might include the chairmen of the parliaments of the Russian republics of Karelia and Komi, Viktor Stepanov and Yuriy Spiridonov. The deputies described the latter two as "independent politicians standing for a single Russian Federation." The deputies said a motion was expected to be made to elect three deputy speakers, one representing the republics, one the regions, and one the autonomous areas of Russia.
