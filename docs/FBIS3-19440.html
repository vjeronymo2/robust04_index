Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] President Konstandinos Karamanlis and Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou met in the presidential palace today and discussed developments in The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM], the Bosnian crisis, and questions pertaining to domestic and foreign policy. Shortly before the meeting at 1230, Athanasios Mikroutsikos was sworn in before the president and in the presence of the prime minister as the new minister of culture. The president's office issued a statement a short time ago noting that at the meeting, the president and the prime minister discussed the Yugoslav crisis in particular, reaffirming Greece's position that no Balkan country should participate in any form of military intervention or in a UN peacekeeping force in the former Yugoslavia. They also discussed the latest developments in the FYROM issue and prospects uncovered during the recent meeting between Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias and Cyrus Vance. The statement from the president's office also stressed that Karamanlis and Papandreou discussed the Cyprus issue within the UN framework and the prospects for Cyprus' accession to the European Union.
