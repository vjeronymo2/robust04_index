BFN [Text] Husamettin Cindoruk, speaker of the Turkish Grand National Assembly, who concluded his official visit to France as the guest of his French counterpart, has returned to Turkey. In a statement at Istanbul's Ataturk Airport, Cindoruk said that during his visit, he held positive and fruitful meetings with the French president, parliamentary officials, and representatives of political parties. Noting that he held a one and-a-half-hour meeting with French President Francois Mitterrand, Cindoruk remarked that the meeting yielded beneficial and important results for Turkey. Cindoruk said that during the meeting, he had the opportunity to respond to some of Mitterrand's views and concerns. Cindoruk stated: I believe that I was able to explain Turkey's problems to him properly. There is no concern with regard to our country's democratization and human rights and its close ties to West Europe. If there were any reasons for concern on these subjects, they were presented to us and we offered the necessary explanations.
