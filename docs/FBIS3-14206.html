Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The Council of Ministers held a meeting in the economic and commercial capital, Aden, this morning under the chairmanship of Prime Minister brother Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas. The council discussed issues pertaining to the implementation of the pledge and accord document in order to build a modern Yemeni state. The council then formed several committees for that purpose. A committee was formed under the chairmanship of the prime minister with the membership of the deputy prime ministers. The committee will propose ways of setting up committees to prepare for the Constitutional amendments, the local government law, the administrative division law, and the elections law. Another committee was formed under the chairmanship of Deputy Prime Minister Muhammad Haydarah Masdus and the membership of Interior Minister Yahya al-Mutawakkil, Defense Minister Haytham Qasim Tahir, and 'Abd-al-Salam Karman, minister of state for Legal and House of Representatives Affairs. The committee will prepare draft laws as stipulated in the first part of the accord document regarding the arrest of defendants, consolidating the military and security situation, (?assessing) the relationship, and defining the powers. A third committee was formed under the chairmanship of Deputy Prime Minister 'Abd-al-Wahhab al-Unsi and the membership of Justice Minister 'Abdallah Ahmad Ghanim and Electricity and Water Resources Minister Ahmad 'Ali al-Salami, in addition to two members of the Dialogue Committee. The committee will prepare proposals regarding the Political Forces Dialogue Committee as a body supervising and following the implementation of the pledge and accord document. It has been decided that these committees will prepare proposals to be presented to an upcoming meeting of the Council of Ministers.
