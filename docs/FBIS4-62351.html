BFN [Text] By decree of Chechen Prime Minister Mairbek Mugadayev, a government delegation was formed in Chechnya for talks with Moscow. The delegation was given the authority "to hold consultations with representatives of bodies of state power and the socio-political forces of the Russian Federation." The delegation includes 13 people, whose posts fully correspond to the posts occupied by their colleagues in the Russian delegation, Interfax learned from the Chechen government. The Chechen delegation at the consultations will be led by one of the vice-premiers Musa Dashukayev. In the meantime, Chechen Foreign Minister Shamseddin Yusef said at a meeting of the Chechen government on Thursday that "judging by actions of the Russian side, the only possibility remaining for settlement of the Russian-Chechen relations is a meeting between presidents Boris Yeltsin and Dzhokhar Dudayev."
