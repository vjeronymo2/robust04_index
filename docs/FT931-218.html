930331 FT 31 MAR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Work bill faces hurdles in Lords THE EMPLOYMENT bill faces what could be a record number of 300 amendments as it goes through its committee stage in the House of Lords, David Goodhart writes. The bill, which is due to become law before parliament's summer recess, has already been the subject of dozens of mainly technical amendments at committee and report stages in the Commons. Although most amendments have been tabled by opposition members and peers, Conservative members have also been giving voice to the anxieties of employers' organisations about the burdens that some measures are imposing. In the latest significant amendment, inspired by the Confederation of British Industry, women going on maternity leave will be required to inform their employer when they are leaving and when they plan to return.
