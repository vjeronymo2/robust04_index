Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature: No. 11 (501)] [Text] President Levon Ter-Petrosyan has signed a decree changing the command structure of the National Security Agency, dissolving the operative services and suspending the activities of its personnel other than the border guards and technical, financial and other services. Davil Shakhnazaryan, 38, Armenian parliament deputy who was presidential special envoy, has been appointed acting agency chief replacing Eduard Simonyan. Shakhnazaryan has three months to screen the agency personnel and draft the charter, structure and personnel requirements for government approval. Aram Abramyan, the president's spokesman, told Interfax that the National Security Agency needed an overhaul to deal with the challenges facing an independent state.
