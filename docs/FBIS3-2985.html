Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 13 (XINHUA) -- The 16th meeting of the Working Party on China of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) is scheduled to open on March 15 in Geneva. A 31-member Chinese delegation headed by Vice-Minister of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation Gu Yongjiang and Vice-Minister of the State Economic and Trade Commission Yu Xiaosong left here in two groups on Saturday [12 March] and Sunday respectively for Geneva. Long Yongtu, secretary general of the Chinese delegation to the meeting, told reporters before leaving here for Geneva that this meeting is the first-round negotiation on the issue since the end of the Uruguay Round in the end of last year. During the negotiation, Gu Yongjiang and Yu Xiaosong will table a series of China's important measures to reform its foreign trade system, especially in its import system, Long said. China stands for maintaining a balance of rights and obligations in GATT and strives to achieve positive results in the meeting. It is learned that during its stay in Geneva, the Chinese delegation is expected to hold bilateral talks with the United States and the European Community and will also hold negotiations on reduction of tariffs with Japan, Canada, Australia and New Zealand.
