940318 FT 18 MAR 94 / Polish reformer chosen Mr Waldemar Pawlak, Poland's prime minister, is to ask President Lech Walesa to appoint Mr Dariusz Rosati, an advocate of free-market reforms, as deputy prime minister and finance minister. Mr Rosati, 48, an economics professor, is expected to pursue a financial austerity policy. He was proposed by the leftist Alliance to replace Mr Marek Borowski, who resigned over policy differences with Mr Pawlak. Mr Rosati is head of the East and Central European section of the United Nation's Economic Commission.
