BFN [Unattributed report: "Wanting To Harmonize Counterintelligence Services"] [Text] The special services of Kazakhstan and Russia have not conducted and never will conduct work against each other. This was stated yesterday by Sergey Stepashin, director of the Russian Federation Federal Counterintelligence Service [FCS], at a press conference in Alma-Ata [Almaty] following the signing of a number of documents on cooperation between the two countries' special services. In Alma-Ata the leaders of the Russian FCS and the Kazakhstani National Security Committee signed protocols of cooperation in the sphere of combating international terrorism, ensuring the safety of civil air routes, economic counterintelligence, the fight against smuggling, drugs trafficking, and corruption, the effective utilization in mutual interests of the operational and technical potential of RADINT counterintelligence subunits, and ensuring the safety of special military and other international shipments on rail transport. This is the first time that such cooperation has been undertaken within the CIS countries on such a wide range of issues, it was noted at the press conference.
