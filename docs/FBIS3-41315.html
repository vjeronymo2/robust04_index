Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] The magazine SEMANA disclosed today that Prosecutor General Gustavo de Greiff, acting on his own and without consulting anyone, has been conducting negotiations for more than a month with members of the Cali Cartel. According to the publication, the document signed by De Greiff himself reportedly gave a kind of safe-conduct pass to alleged Cali and Tulua drug traffickers to keep them from being arrested in the future. The names of these drug traffickers are well-known figure Pacho Herrera, Jose Olmedo Campo, and a man named Renteria. According to the magazine, the passes would clearly show Prosecutor De Greiff has been in direct contact with the Cali Cartel chieftains for more than a month, without previously consulting the government or informing the security corps or the Attorney General's Office. SEMANA said a top government source expressed the government's surprise about the actions taken individually and privately by the prosecutor, since late last year the government and this office agreed to keep each other informed of any developments. According to SEMANA, the serious thing about the case is not the fact that the prosecutor is acting like a loose cannon, but that from the moment when the drug traffickers first went to his office--that is on 14 January--it marked the beginning of the preliminary investigations. In other words, this marked the beginning of the search for evidence that must be provided by the DAS [Administrative Department of Security], the Dijin [Judicial Police and Investigations Directorate] in Colombia, and Interpol. The odd thing is that the Prosecutor General's Office did not officially inform any of these organizations.
