WORLD IN BRIEF;

IRAQ;

JAILED BRITISH NURSE FREED AFTER APPEAL

The government freed British nurse Daphne Parish, who had been sentenced to 15 years in jail for helping a British-based Iranian journalist hanged as a spy.

Parish, 53, was released after an appeal by Zambian President Kenneth D. Kaunda and flown to Lusaka, Zambia's capital. In March, she was sentenced by a revolutionary court for driving journalist Farzad Bazoft to the site of a secret military installation to check reports of an explosion. Bazoft, 31, was executed after allegedly confessing to spying for Israel and Britain.
