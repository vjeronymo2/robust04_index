Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Contrary to the expectations that had spread in the Jordanian capital, King Husayn left Saudi Arabia without being received by the custodian of the two holy mosques. Jordanian sources had said that while in Saudi Arabia, the king would hold talks on the Gulf situation, clearing the Arab atmosphere, and peace efforts. AL-RA'Y AL-'AMM has learned from its special sources that the idea of the Jordanian king's visit to Saudi Arabia had been proposed by Yemeni Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid in Saudi Arabia during his meeting with the custodian of the two holy mosques, who welcomed the accord document signed in Amman. The sources said that al-Bid telephoned King Husayn in the presence of the custodian of the two holy mosques, who welcomed the king's visit for minor pilgrimage.
