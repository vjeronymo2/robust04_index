Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Public Health Minister Julio Teja has reaffirmed in Havana that implementing a system of cost controls by illnesses and areas should be the goal of all hospitals in Cuba in addition to efforts to improve the quality of services. Pedro Ross, member of the Politburo and secretary general of the Cuban Workers Federation, also attended the workers' assembly at Enrique Cabrera Teaching Hospital where doctors, specialists, nurses, and paramedics analyzed significant problems of the sector and national economy. Teja described the hospital as a national vanguard in the implementation of the cost control system by pathologies and areas which permits, for example, knowing how much it costs to treat a myocardial case or for surgery for specific illnesses. During the meeting, several hospital workers stressed the need to know and discuss the material costs incurred by the hospital and a medical sciences teacher asked the cost of training medical personnel, stressing that as long as this is not known our economic awareness will be lacking. In the closing of the first health workers assembly in Havana, Pedro Ross stressed that this process of consultation with the people seeks to find intelligent and effective measures, not only in financial terms, but also organizational in order to increase economic efficiency above all.
