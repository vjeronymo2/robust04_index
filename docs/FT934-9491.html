931110 FT 10 NOV 93 / International Company News: SIA takes stake in Cambodian airline SINGAPORE Airlines is to take a 40 per cent stake in Royal Air Cambodge, a new airline due to be launched in Cambodia in mid-1994, writes Kieran Cooke. Other shareholders in the airline will be the government of Cambodia and a group of Cambodian investors. SIA did not disclose the value of its investment in the new airline. SIA says it will provide day-to-day management of Royal Air Cambodge (RAC) and provide the airline with pilots and technical specialists. SIA will also appoint one of its personnel as RAC's managing director. The existing national carrier in Cambodia, Kampuchea Airlines, is likely to be merged with RAC and provide services on both international and domestic routes.
