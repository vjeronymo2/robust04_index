Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 21 (XINHUA) -- China and Slovakia signed an economic and trade agreement here today. The economic and trade agreement was signed by Shi Guangsheng, vice-minister of [the Ministry of] Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation (MOFTEC), and visiting Slovak Minister of the Economy Jan Ducky on behalf of their respective governments. The agreement is aimed at strengthening friendly cooperation between the two countries and developing trade and economic relations on the basis of equality and mutual benefit, said officials with MOFTEC. The agreement encourages enterprises of the two countries to invest in each other and conduct other forms of cooperation. The two countries also signed a plan for cultural cooperation in 1994-95 on the same occasion. According to the plan, China and Slovakia will exchange performing art troupes and painting exhibitions while implementing personnel exchanges in the fields of culture and art. Chinese Minister of Culture Liu Zhongde and his Slovak counterpart Dusan Slobodnik put their signatures to the instruments. Premier Li Peng and visiting Slovak Prime Minister Vladimir Meciar were present at the signing ceremony.
