940129 FT 29 JAN 94 / World Commodities Prices: Spices Nutmeg and mace prices were strong again, reflecting a lack of offers from Indonesia. East Indian prices increased, so that finally West Indian offerings became competitive and stocks began to be reduced. The cassia season is now over and prices have slowly begun to decline. Further decreases can be expected until March/April, when purchases begin to be made for the autumn grindings. Mexican pimento is sold out, leaving Jamaica as more or less the only supplier. Cloves prices remain depressed. Grenada nutmeg prices were: suns Dollars 1,875 a tonne; and guns Dollars 1,475. Mace no2 was offered at Dollars 2,750 a tonne. Indonesia abcd nutmegs were fetching Dollars 1,875 a tonne; ss Dollars 1,800; and bwp Dollars 1,650. The mace price was Dollars 2,950 a tonne.
