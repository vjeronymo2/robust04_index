BFN [Text] Informed Palestinian sources in Tunis have told our correspondent Yoni Ben-Menahem that during the recent Cairo talks the PLO agreed not to put Palestinian collaborators on trial but refused to grant them a pardon. It is expected that the file of the collaborators will be handled by Jibril al-Rujub, Yasir 'Arafat's adviser, who has been appointed as the official responsible for the West Bank's preventive security [al-amn al-wiqa'i]. Al-Rujub is expected to return to the territories next Friday. Moreover, Muhammad Dahlan, member of the PLO Gaza Committee, has been appointed as the official responsible for the Gaza Strip's preventive security.
