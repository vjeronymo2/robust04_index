Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Anatoliy Gordeyev] [Text] Donetsk 30 Mar, TASS -- Contrary to numerous forecasts, Ukrainian parliamentary elections on 27 March turned out to be valid, with an almost 75 percent turnout of voters. More than 25 million people went to the polls, with the highest voter turnout recorded in Lvov, Ternopol, Ivano-Frankovsk, Donetsk, Lugansk, Poltava, Khmelnitsky and Kharkov regions. The turnout of voters in the Republic of Crimea was 71 per cent. In Kiev, slightly more than a half of the electorate cast their votes. Only 49 out of the prospective 450-member parliament were elected in the first round of polling (25 had been nominated by parties and 24 are nonparty people). Run-offs are to be held in 401 districts before 10 April. However, Central Electoral Commission Chief Ivan Yemets believes that in the majority of districts run-offs may be held this coming weekend, with only two candidates in each district to contest seats in parliament.
