Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Air Force Chief Lieutenant General Datuk Abdul Gani Aziz says Malaysia needs more Hawk 200 jet fighters to strengthen its air defense. He is of the view that the 20 Hawk aircraft being acquired to replace the A-4 Skyhawk are insufficient. The Air Force plans to increase the number of Hawk aircraft in the future. He told reporters this at the RMAF [Royal Malaysian Air Force] base in Butterworth. Earlier, Datuk Abdul Gani was present at the handing-over of a Hawk 100, one of the two aircraft acquired from Britain for the purpose of training. The purchase of the 20 Hawk was made under a memorandum of understanding signed by the Malaysian and British Governments three years ago.
