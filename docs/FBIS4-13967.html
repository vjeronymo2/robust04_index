BFN [Text] Tehran, April 25, IRNA -- Visiting Irish Minister of Defence and Marine Affairs David Andrews here Monday [25 April] expressed his country's readiness for promotion of scientific and economic cooperation with Iran particularly in fishery industry. Meeting with Majlis Speaker Hojjat ol-Eslam 'Ali Akbar Nateq-Nuri, Andrews invited the speaker, on behalf of his Irish counterpart to pay an official visit to Dublin soon. Andrews said both countries had successful cooperation in fields of fishing and manufacturing fishing equipment, hoping that Iran could use Ireland's technical expertise by despatching students at different educational levels to Ireland. Nateq-Nuri in return said Iran was prepared to exchange expertise with Ireland in forestry, fishery, jetty and ship construction industry. He also expressed hope that he could visit Ireland at a proper time. As for massacre of Muslims in Bosnia-Herzegovina Nateq-Nuri denounced the dual approach of the U.N. Security Council to the plight of Bosnian Muslims and once again called for lifting of arms embargo against Bosnia. Commenting on Gaza-Jericho compromise deal, the speaker called it an unjust agreement seeking to stabilize the aggressors in the occupied lands. Meanwhile Andrews discussed issues of mutual interest with Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati today and called for further expansion of Tehran-Dublin ties in all domains. Velayati in return referring to the ample grounds for Tehran-Dublin cooperation welcomed promotion of bilateral relations.
