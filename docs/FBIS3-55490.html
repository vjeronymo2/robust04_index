Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Vesti" newscast] [Excerpts] For several days the Georgians have been preoccupied with two sensational news stories: first is the death of Gamsakhurdia, and the second, even more powerful, is that, according to rumors, 25,000 Russian troops have been brought into Georgia and are stationed in Tbilisi, near the Vaziani military airfield. To be honest, the people are more interested in Gamsakhurdia's suicide. [passage omitted] As for the introduction of 25,000 Russian soldiers, the best informed person -- Georgia's Security Minister Igor Georgadze -- has given us an answer to this question: [Georgadze] As for 25,000 Russian soldiers being stationed on Georgian territory, I can tell you with full responsibility that this does not correspond to reality. [Correspondent] Georgia's Head of State Eduard Shevardnadze was simply shocked by this question. He said the following: [Shevardnadze] Rumors are being circulated abroad that order is being poorly maintained in Tbilisi, and so why don't we call in the Russians and get them to organize a normal life for us? I think this is simply farcical. [Video shows correspondent reporting from Tbilisi, officials being interviewed]
