BFN [Text] Cape Town April 28 SAPA -- The allegation by African National Congress President Nelson Mandela that problems had only occured at polling stations where blacks were voting was extremely serious and should be backed up by facts, State President F W de Klerk said on Thursday [28 April] afternoon. "It is a very serious allegation and he will have to back it up with facts. As far as I know, he has not provided one single fact for that allegation," he said at a press conference at the National Party [NP] offices in Cape Town. Mr de Klerk was reacting to an earlier allegation of "sabotage" made by Mr Mandela in an interview with SABC [South African Broadcasting Corporation] television. "The NP has complained that the overwhelming majority of Independent Electoral Commission [IEC] officials at grassroots and polling station official level have been breaking the rules and have not been impartial. We have much to complain about. The IEC is sufficiently representative -- and on certain levels loaded in favour of the ANC -- of all political parties. I have travelled far and wide and have not heard -- apart from specific polling stations -- any accusations of malpractice which has the type of basis of Mr Mandela's statement. I'm shocked at it."
