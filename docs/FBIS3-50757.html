Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 6 January. The Inter-American Development Bank on 22 December 1993 agreed to a $100 million loan for projects to be implemented by the National Compensation and Social Development Fund. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 23 Dec 93 p B1) The Agriculture Ministry reported that agriculture and livestock production increased 9.6 percent in November compared with the same month last year. The accumulated production in the year's first 11 months was 5.4-percent higher than the same period last year. Agriculture production was 9.4-percent higher between January and November and livestock 2.2 percent. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 23 Dec 93 p B3) Foreign investment in 1993 was $1.557 billion, an increase of $265.01 million compared with 1990. The National Commission for Foreign Investments and Technology reported that the biggest increases were in mining with 35.28 percent, followed by industries with 32.73 percent, commerce with 13.21 percent, and finance with 11.10 percent. Foreign investment in the stock exchange went from $360 million in 1992 to $1.17 billion in 1993. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 24 Dec 93 p A2) On 28 December, the congressional Energy Committee approved a draft bill on privatizating the Peruvian Telephone Company, CPT, and the National Telecommunications Enterprise of Peru, Entelperu. The state decided to sell 20 percent of its shares in CPT and 15 percent of Entelperu. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 29 Dec 93 p A4)
