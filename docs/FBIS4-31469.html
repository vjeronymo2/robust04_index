BFN [Text] Prague June 7 (CTK) -- Parliament has received the state budget report for the first quarter of 1994 in which the government evaluates overall economic development as satisfactory. It maintains that social stability is being maintained as well as the confidence of foreign investors. The influx of foreign portfolio investment amounted to $200 million in the first quarter of 1994. The GDP in constant prices grew in the first quarter at an annual rate of between 0.5 and two percent. Industrial production was down 0.7 percent compared to the first quarter of last year. The volume of production of small businesses with up to 24 employees was up 23.7 percent compared to the same period of last year. Labour productivity expressed in comparable prices was up 2.7 percent in industry over the first quarter of last year. The average nominal monthly wage was 13.8 percent higher. Overall production in the construction industry was up 6.2 percent in the first quarter over the same period of last year, which the government primarily attributes to enterprises with more than 25 workers. Industrial producer prices were up 6.2 percent over the same period of last year. The prices of construction work were up 16.3 percent and agricultural producer prices were up less than two percent. The government considers that price increases were moderate at the start of the year and correspond to this year's inflation forecast of eight to ten percent. Consumer prices rose by 2.4 percent over the first quarter and by 10 percent compared to the same period of last year. Inflationary pressures were gentle and corresponded to the growth of demand during a revival of economic output. Wage cost inflationary pressures were insignificant with regard to the operation of wage regulation. National income was 163 billion crowns in the first quarter. Compared to the same period of last year this represents a nominal growth of 26 percent and a real growth of 15 percent. Nominal incomes from wages grew 14 percent, four percent in real terms. Other incomes, the crucial component of which being incomes from business activities, increased by 50 percent in nominal terms, 37 percent in real terms. Incomes from state benefits increased by 30 percent in nominal terms and 18 percent in real terms. The population had a net income of 19.6 billion crowns, which it used to repay loans (1.1 billion crowns), increase its holding of financial securities (0.8 billion), increase bank deposits (12.7 billion), and increase liquidity (five billion).
