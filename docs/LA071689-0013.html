July 16, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

INSIDE CAA

Regarding "Inside the Agency," Michael Cieply's July 2 article about CAA, the Hollywood talent agency:

The guys at Creative Artists do nothing all day but stroke people they like and "kill" people they don't like. They wield a ridiculous amount of power over the minds of an entire nation.

Their power is derived not from ability or excellence or even intelligence. It comes simply from forming a coterie -- it's all "who you know."

You can see why this sort of thing has such a negative effect on the Hollywood "product." In the case of CAA, they usually sacrifice artistic integrity for increased company profits. When they "package" a deal, putting the script, the actors and the director together, more often than not all of these "elements" are in-house.

The game is money and power. The American public gets lousy programming as a result.

RAY MASON

Torrance
