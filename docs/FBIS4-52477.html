BFN [Text] The Ministry of Foreign Affairs [MFA] has welcomed the U.S. State Department's clarification that Washington has not yet taken a stand on Singapore's bid to host the first World Trade Organization [WTO] conference. A Ministry spokesman said this in response to queries from the News Five. Singapore had expressed regret yesterday after U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor said that the WTO meeting ought to be held elsewhere. A State Department spokesman said the U.S. had no opinion on whether Singapore should host the conference and that Mr. Kantor's comments were his own. The MFA spokesman stressed that the Republic's proposal to host the conference still stands. WTO members are to decide on the venue for the conference.
