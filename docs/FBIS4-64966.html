BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Mohamed Salah Dembri arrived in Athens today on a two-day visit during which he will hold talks with Greek Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou and with Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias, and also with the minister for European affairs. Over to Messaoud Boulatiour for more details about the visit: [Begin Boulatiour recording] The main focus of the foreign minister's visit is the consolidation of cooperation between Algeria and the European Union [EU] ahead of the EU's finance ministers' meeting due to be held on 16 and 17 May. This meeting is expected to examine an EU aid package for projects to revive and develop the economy in Algeria, particularly as Greece is the current chairman of the Union. Greek sources also said that Mr. Dembri's current visit to Greece will enable both sides to discuss the supply of Algerian gas to Athens. According to an agreement signed between the two countries, in 1996 Algeria will start exporting gas in an amount of between $55 and $60 million per year. By then Greece should have completed the construction of a pipeline in the Gulf of Salamis, southeast of the capital, Athens. [end recording]
