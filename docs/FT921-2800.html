920318 FT 18 MAR 92 / Weather hampers helicopter salvage BAD WEATHER hampered efforts to move to Aberdeen the wreck of the Super Puma helicopter which crashed in the North Sea on Saturday killing 11 oil workers. The wreckage, from which only the pilot and five oilmen escaped with their lives, will be taken to Farnborough, Hampshire, for examination at the Air Accident Investigation Bureau. The bureau already has the flight recorder and cockpit voice recorder which were recovered. The Shell Expro helicopter crashed during a 200-yard hop from Shell's Cormorant Alpha platform to the Safe Supporter accommodation block.
