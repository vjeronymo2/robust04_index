Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] After two years of independence Georgia has opted for relations of strategic alliance with Russia, believes Dmitriy Ryurikov, aide to the Russian Federation president on international affairs. This will serve as a background for work in other areas, he noted in an interview to a RIA correspondent, commenting on the results of the Russian-Georgian summit. Speaking about the prospects of Russian-Georgian cooperation in the military domain, Ryurikov stressed that unless the conflicts in Abkhazia and South Osetia are settled, cooperation will be impossible.
