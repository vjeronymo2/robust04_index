BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vadim Polishchuk] [Text] Moscow June 23 TASS -- A mission representing the United Nations Education, Science and Culture Organisation opened its doors for work in the russian capital on Thursday. "Our dreams have become a reality," UNESCO Director-General Federico Mayor announced at the opening ceremony of the bureau. "The opening of this mission became possible as a result of the desire and determination of the Russian Government and the UNESCO secretariat. It will permit the two sides to cooperate more quickly and effectively," he said. "Russia now occupies a most-important place in world events," Mayor continued. "And our cooperation with it has developed wonderfully in the past several years. We have a bunch of joint initiatives, among which it would be worthwhile to note those projects concerning the Russian state library, the Bolshoy theatre, and the Hermitage," the director-general said. "Our organisation's mission is first of all to preserve peace," Mayor declared. "Despite the fact that the Cold War is a thing of the past, our main goal on the eve of the XXI century is to abolish poverty and forever put an end to wars."
