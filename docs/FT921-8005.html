920219 FT 19 FEB 92 / Survey of FT Traveller, Hong Kong (11): Key facts for the business visitor Visa requirements British citizens or UK passport holders' do not require visas and will normally be allowed to stay for 12 months, initially. Visitors from communist countries, certain Middle East countries and some East European countries, as well as Taiwan, always require visas. British Dependant Territories' citizens, Commonwealth citizens and nationals from most West European countries can stay in Hong Kong for up to three months without a visa. Americans as well as nationals from several central and South American countries require visas for a visit exceeding one month. Visitors should check at British consulates, high commissions or visa offices before departure. Currency The unit of currency is the Hong Kong dollar (HKDollars ) which is pegged to the USDollars at a rate of about HKDollars 7.8 to USDollars 1. Other sample rates in January, 1992: Pounds 1=HKDollars 13.92; DM1=4.87. Banking There are 168 licensed banks in Hong Kong and 780 securities dealers. The main banks include: Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corporation: 748 3322. Standard Chartered Bank: 886 8888. Citibank: 821 7311 The main banks can assist visitors who do not maintain bank accounts with them to transfer funds in and out of Hong Kong. There are certain restrictions. Visitors can only get their remittance from abroad out of Hongkong Bank in Hong Kong dollars and Citibank in either Hong Kong dollars or US dollars. Business services Nearly all major hotels offer business facilities with full secretarial services. A number have business centres with fully-equipped private offices and conference rooms for rent. There are also private agencies offering serviced offices. Support staff can also be provided. (For telephone numbers refer to Yellow Pages or newspaper classified pages). Rents vary a great deal with different agencies. For example, the rental cost for a 100 sq ft office in Central district, equipped with telephone line, fax machine and word processing machine, can range from HKDollars 5,700 to HKDollars 12,000 per month. The lease can be on monthly, daily or even hourly basis. An hourly rental fee can be between HKDollars 50 and HKDollars 200. The Hong Kong Convention and Exhibition Centre (864 7827) is the main venue for conferences and exhibitions in Hong Kong. Hong Kong Tourist Association's Convention and Incentive Travel Bureau (801.7111) provides information and assistance to conference organisers. Courier services Most hotels offer courier services for a fee. The Post Office also runs a overseas speedpost service (for enquiries, call 523 1071). Leading couriers operating in Hong Kong are: DHL (tel. 765 8111); Federal Express (tel. 730 3333); TNT Skypak (tel. 389 5279); UPS (tel. 735 3535). Both DHL and Federal Express have collection centres in business districts while DHL also offers a 24-hour pick-up service. Customs allowance As a free trade centre, Hong Kong allows most items to be carried in duty free. The only dutiable items are tobacco, alcohol, cosmetics and petroleum products. Visitors are allowed to bring in the following items duty-free for their own use: one litre of alcohol, 200 cigarettes or 50 cigars or 250 grams of tobacco, 60ml of perfume and 250 ml of toilet water. Federation of Hong Kong Watch Trades and Industries: 523 3232. Telecommunications Local phone calls are free although some hotels charge a handling fee. Local calls made at public pay-phones cost HKDollars 1. Visitors can also make long distance calls by using international calling cards (eg AT&T, British Telecom Charge Card, Hello Card) or credit cards (VISA, American Express, Diners Club, JCB and Mastercard) at selected service centres or specially marked phones. It is sometimes cheaper dialling from rather than to Hong Kong. For details, call International Customer Services on 013. The Post Office's Postfax service allows visitors to send facsimiles from Hong Kong. (Enquiries: 847 1148) Visitors should note that British-style telephone jacks are used in Hong Kong. Transport Taxis are cheap by international standards; the fare is HKDollars 9 on flagfall plus HKDollars 90c for every 0.25 km after the first 2km. Radio booking surcharge is HKDollars 2 and baggage surcharge is HKDollars 3 for each piece of luggage. A HKDollars 20 surcharge applies to cross-harbour tunnel trips, a HKDollars 3 surcharge for rides through the Lion Rock, Junk Bay and Aberdeen Tunnels. A ride from airport to Central district normally costs about HKDollars 60 (including Dollars 20 surcharge); and to Tsim Sha Tsui about HKDollars 30. For telephone numbers of taxi companies, refer to the Yellow Pages. THE MASS TRANSIT RAILWAY (MTR): The MTR underground railway has three lines which cover Kowloon, Hong Kong Island and the New Territories. The fares range from HKDollars 3 to HKDollars 7.5. Stored-value tickets (HKDollars 50, Dollars 100, Dollars 200) can be used within a period of six months at reduced fares outside rush hours. STAR FERRY: Fare for a trip between Central and Tsim Sha Tsui costs HKDollars 1.20 for the upper deck or HKDollars 1 for the lower deck. The service runs from 6:30am to 11:30pm. FERRIES TO MACAU: Jetfoils (tel. 859 6596), High-speed Ferries (803 2255), Hydrofoils and Jetcats (523 2136). Tickets can be purchased in advance from HK/Macau Ferry Terminal at Shun Tak Centre, Hong Kong Ticketmate offices or by telephone using credit cards. Passengers are advised to buy return tickets before departure.
