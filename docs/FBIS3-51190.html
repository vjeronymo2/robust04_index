Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Vector 21" newscast] [Text] Correspondent Elizabeth Diaz Otero reported that President Carlos Salinas de Gortari appointed Humberto Benitez Trevino as new Attorney General in the Federal District [DF] of Mexico and our reporter Jesus Cazasola has been closely following the swearing-in. Go ahead Jesus: [Cazasola] Yes, Ana Patricia, good afternoon. Both the rulers and the ruled must be subject to the empire of the law. No one should act beyond the law because the country's conditions so demand. Humberto Benitez Trevino said this after being sworn in as new DF attorney general. He said there will be no truce for crime and added that no economic or human resources will be spared to guarantee the security the people of Mexico City demand. He said he will act with energy wherever there is impunity. Humberto Benitez Trevino took over from Rene Gonzalez de la Vega who in turn was appointed deputy prosecutor for preliminary enquiries [sub-procurador de averiguaciones previas] for the Attorney General's Office. The new DF attorney general said that the firm hand of the law will be wherever impunity is detected. He also called on the institution's public servants to act strictly according to the Constitution, otherwise the corresponding punishment will be applied.
