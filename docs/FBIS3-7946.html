Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Following a week's break caused by the working visit of Vladimir Meciar to the countries of Asia, the Slovak Government met in Bratislava today. Deputy Prime Minister Roman Kovac and Minister of Foreign Affairs Jozef Moravcik are not taking part in its 85th regular session. It is, however, expected that the government will adopt a position today on personnel questions concerning the posts of minister of foreign affairs and minister for administration and privatization of national property of the Slovak Republic. The president of the Slovak Republic asked Vladimir Meciar for this yesterday. The president refused to appoint Jozef Prokes minister of foreign affairs yesterday. The name of Olga Keltosova, minister of labor, social affairs, and the family, is being mentioned in this connection, as well as that of Ivan Mjartan, Slovak ambassador to the Czech Republic. Olga Keltosova, however, has allegedly denied her candidature. According to our information from government circles, Eva Mitrova, current head of the permanent mission of the Slovak Republic at the Council of Europe in Strasbourg, is a hot candidate for the post of minister of foreign affairs. The candidate for the post of minister for administration and privatization of national property, according to Vladimir Meciar, has been known to the coalition government for several months. As he said recently, he has been waiting to appoint him only because the position of the government was not stable and he considered the appointment of a minister in these circumstances illogical.
