Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Having completed the required protocol, Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina and his Lao counterpart Somsavat Lengsavat had a friendly talk today on the changing international situation. Robaina and his delegation were given a warm welcome upon arrival at (Batai) airport in Vientiane. Robaina arrived from a visit to Indonesia and Thailand, which were the first stops on his tour through Asia. Somsavat Lengsavat described the Cuban delegation's visit as a transcendental event that will allow both sides to bring themselves up to date on the situations of the two countries and explore all possibilities for cooperation in various spheres. According to (Shi Tirak), Lao first deputy minister of foreign affairs, Robaina's visit will allow them to strengthen bilateral ties in all areas. Tomorrow, Roberto Robaina is scheduled to meet with President Nouhak Phoumsavan and Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon. Similarly, the Cuban delegation consisting of (Carlos Amores), advisor; Teresa Fernandez, deputy director of the Asia and Oceania department of the Ministry of Foreign Relations; and Cuban Ambassador to Laos Ricardo Danza Sigas will meet with the president of the Lao national assembly, (Saban Vinatek). During his stay in Vientiane, Robaina will also meet with (Cambo Sonitai), leader of the Cuba-Laos Friendship Association. The delegation will continue its Asian tour, and their next stop will be Myanmar, formerly known as Burma, after which they will travel to Cambodia, India, Malaysia, and Vietnam.
