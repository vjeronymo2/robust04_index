BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 9 [date as received] (KNS-KCNA) -- Prominent figures of Japan including political scientist Takeshi Ishida and lawyer Tsukuo Imamura met the speaker of the House of Representatives and the chairman of the House of Councillors on June 2 and handed them a letter urging the adoption of a special Diet resolution on Japan's responsibility for the war and apology and the establishment of an ad hoc committee for the investigation into postwar compensation. Recalling that many Asian people were killed in Japan's war of aggression, they strongly demanded a thoroughgoing investigation into the outstanding issue of postwar compensation including the forcible drafting of Koreans and the issue of "comfort women for the army", the publication of its results and the clarification of the responsibility. They said that the Diet should immediately adopt a special resolution on the responsibility for the war and apology with the approval of both houses and apologize for Japan's past crimes.
