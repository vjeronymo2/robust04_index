Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Army General Raul Castro, second secretary of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of Cuba [PCC] and FAR [Revolutionary Armed Forces] minister, has inaugurated the nation's first center for mountain-area research and development. This center, built at a cost of over 13 million pesos, is located in the Limonar area of El Salvador Municipality, Guantanamo Province. In a speech she made at the inauguration ceremony, Rosa Elena Simeon, member of the PCC Central Committee and president of the Cuban Academy of Sciences, emphasized that one of the center's objectives is to intensify the work aimed at increasing production of foodstuffs, both traditional and new, for human consumption. Simeon also announced that a scientific hub will be created soon in Guantanamo Province. Raul also visited the organic plots located in Montesano. The plots are aimed at rendering the Caribe area, near the city of Guantanamo, self-sufficient in food. Raul was accompanied by Politburo members Juan Almeida, vice president of the Council of State; General Ulises Rosales del Toro, first vice minister of the FAR and head of the general staff; Esteban Lazo and Julian Rizo, respectively PCC first secretaries in the provinces of Santiago de Cuba and Camaguey; Agriculture Minister Alfredo Jordan; Juan Carlos Robinson, member of the Central Committee and PCC first secretary in Guantanamo Province; and other leaders.
