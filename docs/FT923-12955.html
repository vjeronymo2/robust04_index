920716 FT 16 JUL 92 / International Company News: Rise at American Home Products fails to please AMERICAN Home Products, the US pharmaceutical group, yesterday turned in second-quarter net income of Dollars 296.1m, or 94 cents a share, up 12 per cent from the previous year's earnings of Dollars 265.2m, or 84 cents. Sales in the three months to June 30 rose 8 per cent to Dollars 1.76bn from 1.62bn. Although sales and earnings hit record second-quarter levels, the rate of growth slowed from the first quarter, and Wall Street was disappointed with the results. At mid-session, shares in American Home Products were Dollars 1 7/8 lower at Dollars 73 in active trading. For the first half of 1992, the company's net income rose 14 per cent to Dollars 702.4m, or Dollars 2.23, from Dollars 617.7m, or Dollars 1.96. Sales grew 11 per cent to Dollars 3.75bn from 3.38bn. Sales growth was led by the company's pharmaceuticals operations, which saw second-quarter sales rise 12 per cent to Dollars 1.04bn. Consumer healthcare sales advanced 7 per cent to Dollars 314.8m, while medical supplies and diagnostic products recorded sales of Dollars 195.9m, up 2 per cent in the quarter.
