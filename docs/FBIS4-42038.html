BFN [By ITAR-TASS freelance correspondent Anatoliy Khologyuk] [Text] Tiraspol, 30 Jun (TASS) -- The special state of affairs in the Dniester republic has been extended from 1 July through 31 August this year. This was made public in a decree by Dniester President Igor Smirnov which was broadcast on Dniester republic radio on Wednesday [29 June] evening. The main motives for extending the special state of affairs, it states in the decree, are to help the militia wage the organized struggle against crime and to preserve the state system of the Dniester-Moldovan republic. Ivan Fuchidzhi, acting minister of internal affairs of the Dniester republic, was in favor of the special state of affairs being extended in the region. He reported to the ITAR-TASS correspondent that the special state of affairs will help the militia bodies to purposefully wage the organized struggle against crime in the region that has been stepped up of late both in Tiraspol and other regions.
