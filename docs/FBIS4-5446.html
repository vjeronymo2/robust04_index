BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 11 KYODO -- Japan expressed its support Monday for air strikes by two NATO aircraft Sunday on Bosnian Serb forces advancing on the Moslem town of Gorazde in Bosnia. Chief Cabinet Secretary Masayoshi Takemura told a news conference, "we understand it was an unavoidable action taken in line with UN resolutions." Japan hopes that the Serbs take the air raids as a message from the international community and that the dispute be settled through talks, the top government spokesman said.
