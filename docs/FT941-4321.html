940311 FT 11 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Ferry Pickering Pounds 2.55m in loss Ferry Pickering, the printing and packaging group, incurred a pre-tax deficit of Pounds 2.55m for the year to end-December on a turnover of Pounds 25.94m. The loss, which took account of exceptional charges of Pounds 3.39m for reorganisation costs and goodwill written off, compared with previous profits of Pounds 1.53m and turnover of Pounds 38.94m for the 16 months to December 31 1992. To enable a better comparison of the results to be made figures of the existing operations show turnover for the 1993 year at Pounds 23.24m against Pounds 23.72m for 1992 and operating profits at Pounds 1.81m (Pounds 1.92m). Net borrowings over the 12 months were cut from Pounds 2.63m to Pounds 456,000. A final dividend of 3.525p makes a 5.625p total, representing a maintained payment on an annualised basis. Losses per share were 21.02p (earnings 9.77p for 16 months). Mr Graham Nixon, chairman and chief executive, has previously stated his intention of splitting his roles. Following the annual meeting on April 25, Mr Robert Campbell, chief executive of Platignum, will become chairman. He will join the board as a non-executive director on April 1.
