BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Viktor Gorelov] [Text] Birobidzhan April 13 TASS -- A five-kilometre border zone has been imposed along the Russian-Chinese frontier in Russia's far eastern Jewish Autonomous Region. Access to the zone is available to local residents and people with a special permit. Foreign citizens also need to register in local departments of the Interior Ministry. The new order of entry to the zone was set up by the regional administration in accordance with the law on the state frontier of the Russian Federation. The border zone has turned recently into a place with high criminal activities. A lot of people rushed to the area to collect wild hemp used for drug production, to steal agricultural products, vehicles and so on. Frontier guards have stopped quite a few attempt to smuggle goods by Chinese citizens across the Amur River.
