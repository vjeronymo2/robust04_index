Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By BELINFORM correspondent aleksandr Lyushkevich for TASS] [Text] Minsk, 11 Mar -- A treaty on coordinating military activities was signed in Minsk today between the Republic of Belarus and the Russian Federation. Under the treaty, which has been concluded for a term of 5 years, the sides undertake to cooperate in the military sphere, and "in the event of an attack on one of the states they will consult each other." Belarus and Russia are also preserving links in the manufacture of military equipment and the exchange of documents and other materials, as well as scientific developments in this sphere, and they will cooperate in the exporting of military equipment to third countries. The treaty also envisages joint actions by Russia and Belarus in training military cadres and safeguarding the social and labor guarantees of servicemen located in or moving through the territory of the neighboring states. It also envisages the maintenance of the existing set-up for the use of the air network and communication systems. The sides have undertaken to share the cost of maintaining military cantonments. The document was signed by Georgiy Tarazevich, the Belarusian first deputy foreign minister, and Igor Saprykin, the Russian ambassador to Minsk.
