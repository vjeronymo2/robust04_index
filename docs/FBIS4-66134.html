BFN [Unattributed article: "14.7 Percent Polled in Favor of Berlusconi as King"] [Text] (ANSA) Milan, May 19 -- Asked if they would like to see Premier and Forza Italia leader Silvio Berlusconi as king of Italy, 14.7 percent of the 1,016 Italians questioned by Directa pollsters via telephone over a three-day period (May 16, 17 and 18) said "yes." The pollsters, who questioned a sample of adult men and women from 95 cities and towns throughout the country, made the results known today. Among those most in favor of Berlusconi as king were those living on the islands (27.8 percent) and in the south (19.2), persons over the age of 54 (20.1), those having little formal education (23.1), practicing Catholics (20.7), and housewives (more than one in four, while a little over half were against). Among those opposed to "King Silvio" were those living in the northeast (89.7 percent), students (92.8), industrialists, business executives and professionals (89.3), diplomats and college graduates (85.2) and males, in general (79.5). Out of the total polled, 74.1 percent responded, while 11.2 percent said they had no opinion on the matter of Berlusconi as king.
