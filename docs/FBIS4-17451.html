BFN [Text] The Georgian head of state held a meeting with the heads of local administrations of Georgia yesterday. The meeting was opened by Teimuraz Pipia, head of the directorate of regional management, who considered problems of all rayons in one context. Zurab Gegechkori [chairman of the state committee on land resources] spoke about the process of privatization. He said there were numerous problems in the issue of distributing land and moot problems pertaining to land issues that existed in relations between rayons had to be settled. He also pointed out there were instances of unlawful seizure of lands. Next to take floor was Georgian Education Minister Tamaz Kvachantiradze, who spoke about problems pertaining to the system of education in rayons. He said salaries of school teachers had been considerably increased. Teachers working full time will earn 900,000 coupons [per month]. It was added that a promotion like this granted to teachers would not solve all problems existing in the system of education. The minister requested assistance from the heads of rayon administrations. Other problems of rayons were also given consideration at the meeting. In conclusion, Eduard Shevardnadze, Georgian head of state, made a speech. He made an appraisal of the meeting and set appropriate tasks and instructions for the heads of rayon administrations.
