Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Press Review" -- ITAR-TASS headline] [Text] Moscow March 4 TASS -- TRUD on the Russian-Uzbek summit: "It was the first time that Moscow welcomed the leader of a sovereign republic with a guard of honour and motorcycle outriders, the honour which was previously bestowed only on the leaders of foreign countries. "However, this does not signify an estrangement but is indicative, rather, of the ongoing drawing of the two countries closer together at a fundamentally new level and of the restoration and strengthening of the age-old ties between Russia and Uzbekistan." ROSSIYSKIYE VESTI on the Russian-Uzbek accords: "The accords reached in Moscow open up new opportunities for strengthening the political and economic relations between Russia and Uzbekistan. At the same time they encourage cooperation within the commonwealth of independent states". PRAVDA on the Uzbek president's visit: "The first official visit by Uzbekistan's head of state went off successfully. The results of the accords reached by the two presidents will undoubtedly make themselves felt already in the coming days". KRASNAYA ZVEZDA (RED STAR) carries an interview with Lieutenant- General Aleksandr Lebed, commander of the 14th Russian Army in the Dniester region. In response to a question about the future of the 14th Army, General Lebed said: "As is known, the leadership of the Republic of Moldova advocates the withdrawal of the army from its territory, but, to my mind, it is now impossible to withdraw the army for a variety of reasons, beginning with the fact that the sanguinary conflict between Moldova and the Dniester region has not been resolved politically and, accordingly, there are no guarantees of peace and stability in the region. "The possibility of a new flare-up of hostilities has not disappeared. People view the 14th Army as the guarantor of peace and stability, the guarantor which can prevent war and, if it is unleashed, stop the bloodshed. Therefore, the army's withdrawal should be preceded by a rather long, delicate and wise political process which, unfortunately, has not yet begun". NEZAVISIMAYA GAZETA on the Bosnian peace process: "The process of settling the conflict in Bosnia-Herzegovina on the principle of a threat of the use of force has reached a deadlock and leads to nowhere: NATO countries just cannot for years threaten with air strikes. Neither can the peoples of Bosnia, Serbia and Croatia to live for years under such a threat... "It is important these days, as never before, to prevent the conflict in Bosnia from growing into a European tragedy and deprive NATO of the right to determine when bombing and missile strikes in Bosnia should be carried out. "It is essential urgently to look for new approaches to and ideas of a political settlement both in Bosnia and within a broader context in the Balkans. Such a decision should be taken at the level of, at least, the U.N. Security Council". TRUD runs an interview with Professor Aleksandr Livshits, dr. of science (economics) and leader of the expert group to the Russian president. Livshits, one of the main economic advisers to President Boris Yeltsin, said in particular that he sees it as the main task now "to make market reforms less burdensome for society without slowing down the reform process. The main thing is to ensure that the price of market reforms is not too great for society". PRAVDA frontpages the text of Aleksandr Rutskoy's address to the people of Russia and to the members of the State Duma under the headline "There Can Be No Creative Endeavour Without Civil Peace". The message says in particular that "in autumn last year all those, who were in the parliament building and manned the barricades, discharged their civil and patriotic duty to the end, upholding nascent parliamentarism, democracy and the constitution... For my part, I will do everything depending on me to perpetuate the lucid memory of those who perished..." ROSSIYSKIYE VESTI on the amnesty decision: "The political amnesty story has once again pointed out the major flaw in our present-day democracy -- the inadequecy of the judicial branch of power. Will the judicial branch draw level with the other branches of power? Stability and accord in society depend on that in many respect". NEZAVISIMAYA GAZETA frontpages an interview with Mikhail Slinko, investigator of particularly important cases at the Moscow city prosecutor's office and the leader of a team of investigators probing charges of corruption brought up against Aleksandr Rutskoy in August last year. Slinko said Rutskoy's signature on the documents, which were submitted to the investigators and which testified against the former vice-president, was forged. KRASNAYA ZVEZDA comments on a joint statement issued by Patriarch Aleksey II of Moscow and all Russia and Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev. In the statement the church and the army expressed readiness to cooperate for the benefit of the motherland. The St. Petersburg-based newspaper VESTI reports that a monument to Finnish prisoners of war has been unveiled at Gatchina. According to data provided by Russian and Finnish historians, about 1,000 Finns died at Soviet P.O.W. camps during the Second World War. The monument represents a shell-splinter piercing red granite symbolising the soldier's heart.
