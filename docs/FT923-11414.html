920724 FT 24 JUL 92 / UK Company News: Property market paralysis leaves Savills lower Continuing paralysis in the property market - with the residential side 'particularly vulnerable', according to Mr George Inge, chairman of Savills -accounted for further losses at the surveyor and estate agent in the year to April 30. Pre-tax losses rose to Pounds 2.86m (Pounds 1.69m) after exceptional charges of Pounds 1.62m relating mainly to provisions against the sub-letting of surplus office space. Staffing levels were cut by 50 to 560 in the year and by 105 in the previous year. Pre-exceptionals, losses were reduced to Pounds 1.25m (Pounds 1.69m). Operating losses on the commercial side were Pounds 379,000 (profits Pounds 81,000) on turnover of Pounds 9.68m (Pounds 12.3m). For agricultural and residential the losses were Pounds 914,000 (Pounds 1.76m) on turnover of Pounds 14m (Pounds 15.7m). Losses per share emerged at 6.5p (4.4p).
