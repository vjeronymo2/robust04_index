BFN [Text]Tbilisi, 7 April -- According to Iberia news agency, Baron de Vos van Steenwijk, the ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary of the Kingdom of Netherlands, yesterday visited the Ministry of Economy of the Republic of Georgia. At the ministry, he was met by First Deputy Economy Minister Tamaz Chikvaidze, Deputy Economy Minister Otar Kakauridze, and heads of the ministry's departments. The ambassador heard information about Georgia's economic potential. He expressed interest in the fields in which the two countries could cooperate. The Georgian side said its priorities in cooperation with Holland lay in the field of agriculture, power and engineering, car manufacturing, communications, and personnel training. The ambassador offered the Georgian side a program for training the personnel of small business management and agricultural technology.
