BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, June 11 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The official Newspaper "ARDIYN ERH" informs that at the siting here spring session of the Great State Hural of Mongolia it has been decided to celebrate on 26 November of the current year the 70th jubilee of the approval of the first constitution of Mongolia and declaration of independence of the republic. The Mongolian parliament is to take a special bill on this point later. Also an organizational committee has been set up to provide preparations and arrangement of the measures for the occasion of the jubilee under the guidance of the chairman of the Great State Hural N. Bagabandi. The session of the parliament started debating the draft law on pension and benefits relating to servicemen at the second reading. The newspaper informs that at the closed sitting of the Mongolian parliament the legislators have discussed and approved the proposal by the Mongolbank on issuing into circulation of new 5,000 and 10,000 tugrik banknotes and coins of 20, 50, 100, 200 values. In its traditional heading "From Dawn to Dusk" the newspaper carries some information concerning the latest events and development occurred in the country. It is reported on the forum of young members and supporters of the ruling Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, the participation of Mongolian sportsmen in the annual international tournament in judo wrestling held in the city Krasnoyarsk Russian Federation. The newspaper also provides an interview of its correspondent with the first secretary of the Embassy of Denmark to Beijing Ole Odgaar. The talk was about the assistance being provided to Mongolia by the Danish "Danida" organization. A permanent representative of the Danida would be working at the Ministry of Food and Agriculture of Mongolia. This Danish organization has granted to Mongolia gratis aid of 1.6 million US dollars to be assigned for the purposes referring to prophylaxis and another 1.5 million US dollars for acquiring fertilizers necessary and combating harmful incests. In connection with the presupposed submitting for consideration to the Great State Hural of the draft law on state security the newspaper informs about the actions of secret services on state security of India, United States of America, Japan, Germany and Russia. The newspaper also gives an interview with retired General B. Tsiyregdzen who was at the head of the body on social security of the country during the period from 1984 to 1990. Concerning the international affairs the newspaper reports about the forthcoming visit to the United States of America of Emperor of Japan Akihito and the official visit of the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China Qian Qichen to be paid to the Russian Federation by late June of the year. The governmental newspaper "DZASGIYN GADZRYN MEUEE" publishes the decree of the government on some measures to be provided for the implementation of the national programme for decreasing the impoverishment of the population. The newspaper reports on the round table talk held here concerning the issues of education. The talk was attended by rector of the state pedagogical university, Doctor Prof. B. Chuluundorj, director of the Technical University N. Tsend, Doctor B. Dabaasuren and other scholars discussing the issues of concern facing in the sphere of education.
