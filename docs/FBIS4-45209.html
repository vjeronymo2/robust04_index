BFN [From the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] Arrest warrants have been issued against four alleged members of the Medellin Cartel, who were recently detained by the National Guard. The arrest warrants were issued against four alleged perpetrators of drug trafficking based on Article 33 on the Basic Law on Drugs and Psychotropic Substances and on Article 83 of the Penal Code. The arrest warrants were issued via the 48th Court and the First Criminal District of the Caracas Metropolitan Judicial District, which is headquartered in Los Cortijo de Lourdes. Reportedly, this particular branch of the Medellin Cartel is using this nation as a bridge for drug trafficking to Europe and North America. Colonel Jairo Casimiro Coronel Figueroa, National Guard director of efforts against drug trafficking, identified the detained suspects as Ruben Dario Pelais Munera, German Perez Zapata, [name indistinct], and Luis Fernando Martinez. Meanwhile, [words indistinct] Carlos San Vincens Clavera were released. These last two had been detained for questioning. It was also learned that these four individuals will remain at the Judicial Detention Center in Las Flores, Catia.
