Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, February 23 (XINHUA) -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin today called again for a summit meeting with Western leaders on settling the crisis in the former Yugoslavia. The Russian president proposed that leaders of the United States, Britain, France, Germany and Russia to meet in Moscow or Geneva or in any capital of the above-mentioned countries and sign a "historical treaty" to help end the bloodshed in the former Yugoslavia. Yeltsin had mentioned the idea to U.S. President Bill Clinton a few days ago in a telephone conversation on the Bosnia-Herzegovina crisis.
