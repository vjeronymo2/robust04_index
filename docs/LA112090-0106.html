November 20, 1990, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

OLD SOLDIERS: After running for district attorney against his boss, Michael R. Capizzi, and then losing, Chief Deputy D.A. James G. Enright, 64, started his vacation Monday and will not return. . . . When his vacation ends, Enright will retire after 30 years in the D.A.'s office. . . . Any parting words? No, says Enright.

"That's probably the best way -- to just fade away." And which of his big cases -- most of them splashy murders -- will he cherish most? A simple drug possession conviction in 1970 -- but against drug cultist Timothy Leary. "He was the drug guru, and we sent him to prison. That had an impact." Jim Newton and Jerry Hicks
