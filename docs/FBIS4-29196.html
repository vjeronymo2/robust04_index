BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 15 KYODO -- The Diet will pass the long- overdue state budget for fiscal 1994, which started April 1, next Wednesday [22 June] at the earliest, Diet sources said Wednesday. A leading member of the House of Councillors Budget Committee, Masakuni Murakami of the opposition Liberal Democratic Party (LDP), proposed in talks with Juro Saito, head of LDP upper house members, Tuesday night that the upper house pass the budget next Wednesday "for the sake of the people," the sources said. But Saito reportedly said he considered June 27 an "appropriate" date to pass the delayed budget. According to the sources, LDP chief Yohei Kono, Secretary General Yoshiro Mori, Saito, Murakami and other members of the party's upper house executives will meet Thursday to decide their budget strategy. On June 8 the House of Representatives approved the 73.08 trillion yen budget for fiscal 1994. The current Diet session ends June 29.
