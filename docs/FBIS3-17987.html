Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 31 March (AFP) -- An estimated 1,000 Militiamen occupied the Georgian parliament buildings Thursday in protest at the nomination of a new interior minister, correspondents on the scene said. The protestors, some armed, are opposing the National Assembly's appointment of Shota Kviraia as new interior minister. The action was called for by deputy interior minister and Tbilisi militia chief David Zeikidze. Correspondents said the militiamen were trying to get into the central chamber of the parliament itself.
