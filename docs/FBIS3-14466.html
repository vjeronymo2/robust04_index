Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 26 Mar (MENA)--The Arab League Council's 101st ordinary session began at the League's General Secretariat headquarters this morning. Yemeni Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah is chairing the 2-day conference. The Arab foreign ministers are discussing a 48-item agenda prepared last week by the Arab countries' permanent delegates to the League. Heading the issues under discussion is the situation in the occupied Arab territories, of special significance on the wake of the al-Ibrahimi Mosque massacre; the status of Jerusalem; amending the Arab League charter; establishing an Arab court of justice; the situation in Somalia; the Libyan-Western crisis; pan-Arab security; the revival of Arab-European dialogue; and the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina.
