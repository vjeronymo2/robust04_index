930603 FT 03 JUN 93 / UK Company News: Warburg chief takes Pounds 0.8m pay reduction Sir David Scholey, chairman of SG Warburg, saw his pay package tumble by Pounds 787,000 for the year ended March 31 1993. The UK merchant bank sliced his pay from Pounds 1.2m leaving him with a package totalling Pounds 411,000, with no long-term performance related payments. Group profits fell 11 per cent to Pounds 148m, despite a strong recovery in the second half. Sir David's pay included an annual performance-related payment down from Pounds 316,000 to Pounds 216,000, but there was no long-term performance related bonus, which had accounted for more than half his remuneration package the previous year. His basic salary was unchanged at Pounds 195,000, according to the annual report. Long - term performance related pay is set by a compensation committee.
