BFN [Aleksandr Shalnev report: "U.S. Aid to the CIS To Be Reduced"] [Text] The U.S. Congress House of Representatives has appropriated $875 million for programs to assist reforms in Russia and other countries of the former Union in the 1995 fiscal year, which begins 1 October of this year. This is $25 million less than planned by President Clinton's administration. According to the administration's proposals, Russia will receive a little less than one-half of the allocated sum. This is Washington's new policy; in previous years it has, on the contrary, alloted Russia first place in determining the proportion of aid to be assigned.
