Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan March 18 TASS -- Although Armenia has nothing to do with Thursday's crash of the Iranian aircraft outside its skies, it renders comprehensive assistance to the Iranian authorities in the crash investigation, First Deputy Armenian Foreign Minister Zhirair Liparityan told a briefing for correspondents and representatives of foreign embassies tonight. In his words, "the Iranian C-130 plane on its way from Moscow to Tehran passed Georgia at 22:08 on March 17 but did not enter the Armenian skies as it was planned. The aircraft deviated from the air corridor by approximately 100 kilometers, entered the Azeri skies and headed for Nagorno-Karabakh." According to information of the Armenian Foreign Ministry, "the plane crashed approximately 14 kilometers away from Stepanakert, meanwhile the Karabakh Information and Press Department reports the accident place five kilometers away from the Karabakh capital". All the passengers and 14 crew members died. "The plane carried 31 people," said Liparityan. He made no further comments, saying he "does not have more information". A statement of the Armenian Government's Civil Aviation Department was circulated tonight to say "the Iranian plane on its way from Moscow to Tehran deviated from its route by 100 kilometers on unknown reasons at 22:30 on March 17 and found itself in the Karabakh skies. The plane crashed five kilometers away from Stepanakert. Circumstances of the crash are being specified".
