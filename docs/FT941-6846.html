940228 FT 28 FEB 94 / Arts: Today's Television A fascinating evening in prospect for those preferring factual programmes. Horizon promises an extraordinary story about using 'Star Wars' technology to destroy giant meteors in space before they collide with the earth (8.00 BBC2). World In Action visits three families from different backgrounds whose members voted Tory at the last election and calculates how much more tax they will have to pay under the Conservatives' new demands (8.30 ITV). Channel 4 begins a series about everyday life in China, Beyond The Clouds, made by Phil Agland whose documentary 'Baka, People of The Rainforest' rightly won awards (9.00). Panorama looks into the personal pension industry where the system of 'self regulation' is to be tightened up. Vivian White suggests that people are being given advice that is expensive and bad (9.30 BBC1). The ever-watchable Film 94 With Barry Norman is devoted to an interview with Robert Altman, who denies he is a maverick (11.10 BBC1). On BBC2 Jeremy Isaacs comes Face To Face With Billy Connolly, the Scottish comedian (11.15).
