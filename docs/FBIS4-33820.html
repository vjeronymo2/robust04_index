BFN [Text] La Paz, 9 Jun (EFE) -- The Bolivian Foreign Ministry confirmed today that Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada and Chilean President Eduardo Frei will meet within the framework of the Fourth Ibero-American summit to be held in Cartagena, Colombia. The Bolivian Foreign Ministry reported that the two heads of state, who will attend the Fourth Ibero-American summit on 14 and 15 June, will take the opportunity to hold an informal meeting. This will be the second time Sanchez de Lozada and Frei have met since August 1993; the other time was during Sanchez de Lozada's inauguration. The Bolivian president took advantage of another international meeting, the Rio Group meeting in Santiago, to meet with Chilean President Patricio Aylwin and then Chilean President-elect Eduardo Frei. Bolivia and Chile have had no diplomatic ties since 1962 -- except for a brief three-year period (1975-78) -- as a result of a territorial dispute and Bolivia's demand for a sovereign sea outlet to the Pacific across land that has belonged to Chile since the 19th century. The Bolivian Congress authorized Sanchez de Lozada to travel to Colombia to attend the Ibero-American summit which will be attended by the heads of state from Latin America, Spain, and Portugal. The summit will be held in Cartagena on 14 and 15 June. Congress also authorized Sanchez de Lozada to travel to the United States, where he will attend the opening game of the World Soccer Cup at Soldier Field in Chicago; Bolivia will play Germany on 17 June.
