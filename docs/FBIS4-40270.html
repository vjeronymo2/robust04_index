BFN [Text] [No dateline as received] -- Yesterday, 13 June, Georgian Head of State Eduard Shevardnadze received a World Bank delegation led by the head of the bank's Europe and Central Asia department, Mr. Blinkhorn. The Georgian leader briefed guests on the current situation in the republic, stressing that as a result of reforms that had been implemented recently signs of stabilization were emerging in the country. Shevardnadze said that a fast pace of privatization would create a real basis for the development of business. For their part, World Bank representatives said that their program envisaged rendering aid to Georgia in the near future, particularly for the winter season. The program also envisaged investment in the republic and the provision of technical assistance initially to Tbilisi, Poti, and Batumi, and later to other regions. The head of state thanked delegation members for their interest in Georgia and assured them of all-round assistance from the republic's government.
