920116 FT 16 JAN 92 / Israeli inflation rate reached 18 per cent in 1991 ISRAELI inflation totalled 18 per cent in 1991, less than had been feared and only slightly up on 17.6 per cent in 1990, writes Hugh Carnegy. The biggest single factor was the surge in housing costs, which rose 28 per cent overall as fears of an immigration-induced housing shortage drove up prices and rents. But Russian immigrants' reluctance to take on high levels of debt to purchase property, and rising availability of new stock arising from a construction boom, abruptly reversed the trend in the last quarter, when house prices fell 4 per cent.
