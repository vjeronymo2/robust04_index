941017 FT 17 OCT 94 / Howe warns on Tory EU rebels Lord Howe, the former foreign secretary, warned yesterday that Conservatives could hand a general election victory to Labour if Euro-scepticism was allowed to flourish in Tory ranks. His warning about the risk from the right of the party came amid reports that he and other senior Tory figures would be forming a policy group to argue the case for Europe. The group, said to include Mr Kenneth Clarke, the chancellor, Mr David Hunt, chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster, and Sir Leon Brittan, the European commissioner, will also involve several prominent people from the business community. Pro-European Tories are anxious to counter the arguments of the Euro-sceptic lobby. The party's rift over Europe was reopened at its conference in Bournemouth last week when Mr Norman Lamont, the former chancellor, suggested that Britain could leave the European Union. But Lord Howe, echoing his conference pleas on ITV's Walden programme yesterday, said: 'We have this great danger of our sliding into a Euro-sceptic mode and Euro-scepticism spreading through the party and at worst to the nation - the last thing we want. 'And if it happens, we enhance considerably the risk of a Labour government and all that follows from that.'
