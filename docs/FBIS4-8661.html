BFN [Text] Prague, April 12 (CTK) -- Colombian Foreign Minister Nohemi Sanin Posada, in a meeting with Czech Parliament Chairman Milan Uhde today, pointed to the as yet not-utilized possibilities for Czech-Colombian political, economic and cultural cooperation. She described the current level of Czech-Colombian trade as a symbolic one, adding that the Czech and Colombian economies were fully compatible and were not competing each other in any branch. She said that the Czech Republic was capable of standing up to competition in Latin American area too. "We have lost many opportunities and this should be stopped," she said, referring to Czech-Colombian political, economic and cultural cooperation. Uhde said that the Czech Republic was capable of offering, under advantageous conditions, different kinds of technology to Columbia. He mentioned metro construction project in the Colombian town of Medelin, Posada's native town, adding that such an opportunity should be made use of. Uhde described the Czech Republic as a reliable partner pointing to the political stability and its favorable economic development. He said that agreements on investment protection and on the prevention of double taxation and tax evasion would contribute to Czech-Colombian cooperation.
