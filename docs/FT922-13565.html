920410 FT 10 APR 92 / The Lex Column: US economy The quarter-point cut in the Federal Reserve's target rate for overnight money seems the more dramatic because it took the market by surprise. In fact, it is a measured response to mounting evidence that the US recovery is not as robust as at first seemed. It is also a reminder, pointed without betraying a sense of alarm, that the US authorities will step in to limit the secondary impact of Japanese financial market turmoil. If only one could believe the Bundesbank had a similar social conscience.
