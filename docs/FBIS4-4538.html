BFN [Text] A seven-member Myanmar [Burmese] delegation led by Naval Captain Tun Aung, director general of the Land Survey Department of the Ministry of Forestry, returned to Yangon [Rangoon] by Myanmar Airways International last night after attending the third meeting of the Joint Inspection Committee for the second Myanmar-Chinese joint boundary inspection in Beijing, People's Republic of China, from 23 March to 1 April. The Myanmar and Chinese delegation led by Mr. Su Wiyi, the ambassador of PRC's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, held discussions and agreed on the repair of dilapidated border markers in accordance with the provisions of the Myanmar-Chinese Boundary protocol. The delegations also agreed on the matters concerning the second joint boundary inspection to install new border markers and the drafting of a protocol on the second Myanmar-Chinese boundary inspection. The leaders of the delegations also signed the minutes of the meeting on these agreements on 1 April.
