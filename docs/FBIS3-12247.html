Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by Arab affairs correspondent Eldad Beck] [Text] The PLO leadership in Tunis has already been apprised of today's cabinet session. The PLO declared that the cabinet's decisions were insufficient, failed to meet the Palestinians' minimum demands, and disappointing. We spoke with Dr. Ahmad al-Tibi, PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat's adviser, a short while ago. Dr. al-Tibi commented on the steps slated to be taken against the Kakh activists, including their disarmament. Al-Tibi said we are not dealing with a small group of people and asserted that a large number of settlers should be disarmed. Ahmad al-Tibi called for an immediate solution to the problem of the Jewish settlements, especially those located in the heart of Arab population centers. Al-Tibi said: A new era has ensued in the wake of the massacre in Hebron and the Israeli Government decisions do not meet its demands. In short, Ahmad al-Tibi described today's cabinet decisions as disappointing.
