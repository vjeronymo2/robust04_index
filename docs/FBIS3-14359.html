Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 26 Mar (MENA)--The Arab League council will resume meetings of its 101st session tomorrow morning at the Arab League headquarters. Remaining items on the agenda will be discussed at the meetings, which will be chaired by Yemeni Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah. An official Arab League source said at the end of the evening session today that the council discussed most items on the agenda, notably the Arab-Israeli conflict, the Yemeni problem, Somalia, and Sudan. The source said the council stressed Sudan's unity and its rejection of any attempts to separate the south. Concerning pan-Arab security, the source said the subject was deferred to the next session for further discussions on the various aspects of the issue in light of current developments. The source said the council also discussed the Arab League secretary general's report on developments between the previous session and this one and what was implemented from the resolutions of the previous session. [In a related report, Cairo Arab Republic of Egypt Radio Network in Arabic at 2100 GMT on 26 March adds: "Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has said that a number of subjects, foremost of which was the Libyan-Western crisis, were discussed. He said the council will issue a resolution in this respect tomorrow. He said the subject of the Arab boycott against Israel has not yet been brought up before the council. ["Ibrahim al-Bishari, Libya's permanent Arab League envoy, has stated that the seven-member Arab committee has made a proposal concerning the Libyan-Western crisis. It provides for arrangements maintaining Libya's sovereignty and responding to Security Council Resolution 731. He stressed that the next stage will see intensive diplomatic activity in this regard, whether by the Arab League or the seven-member committee."]
