BFN [Text] A deputy premier-level preliminary contact to hold North-South top-level talks [choegowigup hoedam] took place in Panmunjom on 28 June 1994. At the contact, the two sides agreed on the following points: The two sides will hold North-South top-level talks in Pyongyang from 25 to 27 July 1994. The itinerary of the stay can be extended further if necessary. The next talks will be decided on at the will of the two sides' top-level officials [choegowigubui ttusettara]. The composition and scale of the delegation to North-South top- level talks, formats of talks, an itinerary, dispatch of advance teams, procedures of visits, guarantee of conveniences, guarantee of personal safety, and other procedural matters will be discussed and agreed at a delegates contact attended by, from each side, one delegate to the preliminary contact and two accompanying officials. A delegates contact will be held at Tongilgak, on the North side's area of Panmunjom, at 1000 on 1 July, Friday, 1994. The two sides will make joint efforts to built atmospheres of North-South top-level talks favorable to reconciliation, unity, trust, and understanding. 28 June 1994 [Signed] Kim Yong-sun, chairman of the Reunification Policy Committee of the DPRK Supreme People's Assembly and head of the North side's delegation to a vice premier-level preliminary contact for North-South top-level talks Yi Hong-ku, ROK [taehan minguk] deputy prime minister and minister of the National Unification Board and senior delegate of the South side to a vice premier-level preliminary contact for South-North top-level talks
