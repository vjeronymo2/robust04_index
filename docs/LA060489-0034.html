June 4, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

RECORD RACK: DONNY OSMOND "DONNY OSMOND." CAPITOL * 1/2: POP STARS ***** GREAT BALLS OF FIRE **** KNOCKIN' ON HEAVEN'S DOOR *** GOOD VIBRATIONS ** MAYBE BABY * AIN'T THAT A SHAME

Most of this album is as bad as you'd expect -- but not for the reasons you'd expect. Osmond has the reputation for being a bland, soulless singer. His vocals, however, aren't the main flaw in this collection of rather dreary pop songs. The problem is primarily inferior material and thoroughly unimaginative, one-dimensional production -- particularly on the up-tempo songs. His Top 5 pop/dance hit "Soldier of Love" works because of the punchy arrangement -- and in spite of his tepid vocals. But, except for a few lapses, his singing is generally passable, and on a few ballads even praiseworthy, showing some strength and honest feeling. With the right material and production, Osmond just might turn out a very good pop album. Dennis Hunt
