BFN [Text] The State Duma passed a federal law on state foreign debts and Russia's credits granted to foreign states in the first hearing Wednesday. The draft law provides for an annual setting by a federal law of the maximum volume of credits used within a fiscal year and representing part of Russia's foreign debt. The maximum volume of foreign loans taken by the state shall not exceed the annual volume of payments in service and repayment of Russia's foreign debt. The draft law envisions that Russia's international treaties on state loans and Russia's state credits to foreign states are subject to ratification whenever they exceed 100 million dollars. The State Duma also passed a federal law on the maximum volume of state loans and Russia's state credits to foreign states in 1994 Wednesday. According to the law, the maximum volume of Russia's loans in 1994 is set at 4.7 billion dollars, while Russia's credits to foreign states in 1994 at 400 million dollars. The two federal laws were sent for consideration by the Federation Council, the upper house of the Russian parliament.
