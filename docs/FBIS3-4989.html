Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Seoul--KYODO/YONHAP] [Text] Deng Xiaoping, the supreme leader of China, handed out a directive to policy makers in China to fully support the North Korean economic reform policy and to help realize North Korean President Kim Il-song's visit to China. This was revealed by a diplomatic source in Seoul on 2 March. The source said that Deng Xiapoing handed out such a directive after hearing the report on North Korean affairs from Ding Guanggen, member of the Chinese Communist Party Political Bureau. Ding held talks with Hwang Chang-yop, secretary of the North Korean Workers Party Central Committee and chairman of the Foreign Affairs Committee in the Supreme People's Assembly, who headed a five-member delegation's visit to China last January. Hwang Chang-yop stayed in China from 15 to 23 January. During his stay, he met with Chinese leaders and discussed North Korean economic policies and the issue of President Kim's visit to China.
