BFN [Unattributed article: "Two-Thirds of Italians Want Confidence Voted, Poll Says"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, May 16 -- Two-thirds of the Italian people want parliament to vote confidence in the Berlusconi government, a CIRM [Italian Center for Market Research] poll indicated. 66.4 percent of 911 persons polled in 180 towns around Italy replied Yes to the question "Would you vote confidence in the Berlusconi government if you were a member of the Lower House?" 24.4 percent replied No and 9.2 percent expressed no view. CIRM highlighted that one-third of Communist Refoundation voters and 0.4 percent of other Progressive voters would give the government their confidence, alongside a majority of Greens, the ex-Christian Democrats of the People's Party and the Pact for Italy, and the ex-radicals of the Pannella List.
