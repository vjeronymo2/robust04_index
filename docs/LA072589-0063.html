July 25, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

White-Collar Cuts Rumored at Chrysler: Chrysler Corp. said its top 600 officials will meet Thursday with Chairman Lee A. Iacocca to discuss cost-reduction efforts amid widespread industry speculation that Chrysler will cut its white-collar staff. But the nation's No. 3 auto maker said any cuts would not affect research and development, capital spending or long-term product planning. The reported staff cuts at Chrysler would be the first specifically prompted by the current slump in new car sales. Ford Motor Co. and General Motors Corp. are trimming their white-collar payrolls through previously announced programs.
