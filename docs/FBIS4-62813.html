BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Roman Zadunayskiy] [Summary] Moscow, 14 May -- Russia's Chief Military Procuracy [CMP] intends to ask the presidential administration to speed up the creation of a military police in Russia's Armed Forces. The necessary documents have already been submitted. It was noted at a recent working conference of the CMP that crime has risen sharply in the forces -- 2,572 people were killed and 1,002 received injuries in the armed forces in 1993; while 2,868 weapons were stolen in the same period. The existing preliminary investigation bodies, consisting of unit commanders and locally appointed officers, are considered inadequate. Last year the number of crimes concealed for various reasons by these non-professional bodies rose by a factor of 2.2 compared to 1992.
