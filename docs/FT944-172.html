941231 FT 31 DEC 94 / World News in Brief: Footsie ends 363.3 points down on year The UK equity market ended the year with one of the quietest trading sessions on record, with the FT-SE 100 index closing down 0.1 of a point at 3,065.5. The Footsie has fallen 363.3 points, or 10.6 per cent, over the year. The London Stock Exchange said 1994 saw the highest annual shares turnover for both UK and foreign equities. Turnover of foreign shares rose by nearly 24 per cent and newly-listed companies raised Pounds 11.6bn, nearly double the previous year's figure. Gilts turnover fell to Pounds 1.163bn compared with Pounds 1.598bn in 1993.
