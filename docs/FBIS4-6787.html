BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 25 (KCNA) -- Talks were held between delegations of the Workers' Party of Korea [WPK] and the Communist Party of Vietnam [CPV] in Pyongyang on Sunday. Present at the talks on the Korean side were Secretary of the WPK Central Committee Hwang Chang-yop and officials concerned and on the Vietnamese side the members of the delegation of the Communist Party of Vietnam headed by Secretary of its Central Committee Hong Ha and Vietnamese Ambassador to Korea Duong Chinh Thuc. At the talks the sides briefed each other on the activities of their parties and exchanged views on further development of the relations between the two parties and a series of matters of common concern. The head of the delegation said the Korean people owe their achievements in socialist construction to the wise guidance of the great leader President Kim Il-song and the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il in the Korean revolution. He expressed full support to the Korean people's cause of socialism and national reunification and the stand of the DPRK for a negotiated solution to the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. The talks proceeded in a friendly atmosphere.
