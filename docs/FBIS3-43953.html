Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Afonso Dhlakama, leader of the Mozambique National Resistance [Renamo], has withdrawn his warning that he would definitely cancel the confinement of his forces by 22 January should government troops continue to report to assembly points at a slow pace. Speaking in a telephone interview, the Renamo president said if his organization went ahead with the cancellation it would be helping Frelimo to continue making mistakes regarding the implementation of the General Peace Accord. Afonso Dhlakama said he felt disappointed with the slow pace at which government troops were reporting to assembly points, saying he has been under pressure from his generals and commanders who wish to know the reason for the executive's attitude. [as heard] The Renamo leader said, however, that the confinement of troops will continue.
