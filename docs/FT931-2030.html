930323 FT 23 MAR 93 / World Stock Markets (America): Down starts to clawback after early declines Wall Street CONCERN about political turmoil in Russia initially sent US share prices spiralling downwards yesterday morning, but by midsession the market had started to claw back from its lower levels, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average, which dropped more than 27 points in morning trading, ended the day down a more moderate 8.10 at 3,463.48. The more broadly based Standard & Poor's 500 was down 1.30 at 448.88, while the Amex composite was 3.19 lower at 419.18, and the Nasdaq composite tumbled 6.10 at 676.62. Trading volume on the NYSE was around 232m shares and declines outnumbered rises by 1,205 to 660. Drug company stocks started the week on a gloomy note, with Merck initially slipping to a 52-week low before regaining lost ground to close down Dollars 1/8 at Dollars 35 3/4 . Glaxo Holdings fell Dollars 1/4 to a 52-week low of Dollars 17 3/4 but Abbott Laboratories ended the day Dollars 7/8 higher at Dollars 24 3/4 . Software company Cadence Design plummeted Dollars 6 1/8 to Dollars 10 3/8 in very heavy trading following predictions that revenues and earnings for the first quarter will fall substantially below market expectations. The stock has not traded below Dollars 13 1/8 since before 1992. Cadence blamed its disappointing outlook on a systems product strategy shift which had resulted in customers delaying product purchasing decisions. Mr Joe Costello, the chairman, said that 'although we see signs for improvement in business conditions in the second quarter, we remain cautious due to the significance of the current shortfall'. Shares in IBM slipped Dollars 1/2 to Dollars 53 1/2 after Merrill Lynch reduced its 1993 earnings forecast to a range of 50 cents to Dollars 1 a share from earlier estimates of Dollars 1 to Dollars 1.50. Tenneco recovered from sharp morning losses to edge Dollars 3/8 lower at Dollars 47 3/4 on news of a major restructuring including a pre-tax restructuring charge of Dollars 920m and a planned sale of at least 23.5m common shares. In Nasdaq trading, declining technology shares drove the market lower through the day. Sun Microsystems sank Dollars 1 to Dollars 29 1/8 , , Dell Computer fell Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 34 1/2 . Intel lost Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 113 in spite of announcing that it has started shipping its new Pentium microprocessor. and Apple Computer was off Dollars 1/2 to Dollars 53 1/4 . Canada TORONTO prices closed mixed in heavy trading. Based on preliminary figures, the 300 composite index rose 6.75 points, or 0.19 per cent, to 3,583.74. Declines led advances 337 to 325. Volume fell to 50.01m shares from 62.93m shares and trading value was CDollars 388.3m compared with CDollars 589.4m. Hudson's Bay Co. said a subsidiary has agreed to merge with Woodward's. Hudson's Bay will own all of the merged company's outstanding equity. Hudson's Bay fell 3/8 to 32 1/4 and Woodward's gained 0.105 to 0.33.
