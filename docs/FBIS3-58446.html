Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Jan 12 (AA) -- A Russian technical delegation, currently in Turkey and Turkish Petroleum Company BOTAS officials on Wednesday [12 January] discussed the route of the petroleum coming from Russia, Caucasia and Central Asia. BOTAS General Director Hayrettin Uzun answering AA reporter's questions on the visit said a Turkish-Russian joint study-group was decided to be set up to specify the cooperation fields on energy field during Premier Tansu Ciller's visit to Moscow in September. Uzun said the joint study group holds talks on eight possible routes adding they mainly focus on a route which comes from Russia to Caucasia than to Georgia or Armenia. Answering another question Uzun said a bargain with Russia for the price of an additional supply of two billion cubic meters of natural gas was out of question.
