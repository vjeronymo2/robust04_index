BFN [Text] Tajikistan's opposition groups expressed hope that a compromise will be reached during the second round of direct talks, soon to be held in Tehran, between their representatives and the Tajik Government. In an exclusive interview with the Central News Unit correspondent, [Akbar Turajonzadeh], the head of the delegation representing the Tajik opposition groups in the [peace] talks, said that the first round of talks failed to achieve any conclusive results for the people of Tajikistan. Reiterating the opposition's aim of realizing lasting peace in Tajikistan, he said: We seek independence and good-neighborly relations with all Tajikistan's neighbors, including Russia. Mr. Turajonzadeh praised the Islamic Republic of Iran's positive role in finding a solution to the Tajik crisis.
