Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over stills of Prime Minister Tansu ciller and U.S. President Bill Clinton] [Text] U.S. President Bill Clinton telephoned Prime Minister Tansu Ciller this afternoon and conversed with her for half an hour on developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina. A statement issued by the Prime Minister's Press Center stated: The telephone conversation, which took place while a meeting on the struggle against terrorism was under way at the presidential palace, lasted approximately 30 minutes. The developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina were discussed during the conversation. In a statement after her conversation with Clinton, Ciller said: Both Mr. Clinton and I are hopeful about developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina following the NATO decision on the subject. The statement added: Mr. Clinton also sent a letter to Ciller. In his letter on Bosnia-Herzegovina, which was relayed to the prime minister by the U.S. ambassador to Ankara, Clinton thanked Ciller for telephoning him before her departure for Sarajevo. Clinton described Ciller's visit to Sarajevo, accompanied by Pakistani Prime Minister Bhutto, as courageous and expressed his admiration. Clinton also stressed that he fully shares Ciller's feelings with regard to the inhuman attack launched against innocent people in Sarajevo on 5 February.
