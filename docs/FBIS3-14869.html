Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, 28 Feb (TANJUG) -- Following talks with Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin this evening, President of the Serb Republic Radovan Karadzic stated that their talks focused on the continuation of efforts to restore peace in Bosnia. At the moment it is impossible to give any more details since this process is still under way, Karadzic said. The president of the Serb Republic arrived in Moscow this evening and immediately met Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin. Their tete-a-tete meeting lasted for 45 minutes. "The very fact that we are in Russia and that the Russian army is in our country already means a lot," Karadzic said following talks with Churkin. "There are a few countries in the world that are impartial as regards the Bosnian conflict. Russia is impartial in every respect, and it is also a friendly country,, Karadzic stressed. He went on to say that these were traditional relations and "an old friendship." Speaking about the Russian initiative for the demilitarization of Sarajevo, Karadzic said that it saved the world many troubles. "We wish the same path to be pursued further. It seems to me that Russia has chosen the right way to contribute to the resolution of conflicts in Bosnia," Karadzic concluded. The president of the Serb Republic is to continue talks with Russian officials tomorrow. He is expected to meet Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev.
