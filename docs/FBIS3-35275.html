Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Clashes erupted in southern Yemen today between two units of the former north Yemeni and south Yemeni armies, despite the fact that a reconciliation accord was signed in Amman yesterday between Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, former president of north Yemen, and his deputy 'Ali Salim al-Bid, former leader of south Yemen. The accord is designed to end the political crisis that has continued for several months in Yemen. The Yemeni president and his deputy left the Jordanian capital separately this afternoon after holding a prolonged meeting with King Husayn of Jordan. Security and political sources in Sanaa have said that troops and tanks affiliated with a unit of the former north Yemeni Army are besieging the city of Zinjibar in southern Yemen in the aftermath of clashes with troops affiliated with the former south Yemeni army. The clashes erupted after troops affiliated with the north Yemeni army attempted to detain a colonel affiliated with the south Yemeni Army at a military checkpoint. Yemeni sources in Amman said at noon today that at least four persons were killed in the clashes between the two sides.
