920923 FT 23 SEP 92 / Ownership of CD players up OWNERSHIP OF consumer durables has continued to rise in spite of the recession, according to preliminary results from the 1991 General Household Survey. The biggest year-on-year rise was for compact disc players - 27 per cent of British households owned one last year, up from 21 per cent in 1990. There were also further surges in ownership levels for video recorders and microwave ovens - 68 per cent owned a video (64 per cent in 1990) and 55 per cent a microwave (50 per cent in 1990).
