Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN ["Reference News" by Tian Tian (1131 3944): "Two Hundred and Eighty Publishing Houses Banned"] [Text] A report dated 28 January from the General Office of the Ministry of Public Security disclosed that in 1993 a total of about 280 underground publishing houses were banned and more than 1.5 million reactionary or pornographic books and tabloids, including some published in Hong Kong, Taiwan, and abroad and reprinted, were seized and confiscated in 183 cities across the country. The report points out that readers of reactionary publications from Hong Kong, Taiwan, and abroad are mostly intellectuals, middle-aged and young cadres, and university students, while readers of pornographic publications are mostly self-employed businessmen and young workers and staff members.
