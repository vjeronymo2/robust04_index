BFN [Text] Islamic Conference Organization [ICO] Secretary General Dr. Hamid al-Ghabid expressed his deep satisfaction and welcoming of the signing in Cairo of the agreement between the PLO and Israel on the implementation of the agreement on transitional self-rule in the Gaza Strip and Jericho. In a statement today, he said that this agreement was an important turning point in the history of the Palestinian people and a decisive step in the course of its just struggle, during which immense sacrifices were made toward the achievement of its legitimate rights including the right to return, the right to self determination, and the setting up of its independent Palestinian state on its national territory with Jerusalem as its capital. The secretary general welcomed the courageous and wise steps taken by the PLO under the leadership of His Excellency President Yasir 'Arafat in defense of the proud Palestinian people's rights and interests during this stage of hard talks. Dr. Hamid al-Ghabid stressed the Muslim nation's fundamental and constant support of the Palestinian people in its fight to regain its basic national rights. He called on the international community to provide all forms of support to the Palestinian people during this historic period of constructing and developing its vital utilities and infrastructure and its national social and economic institutions as the fundamental pillars for its national authority on its land, thus bolstering security and stability in the region. The ICO secretary general praised the efforts made by all parties to the peace process that contributed toward achieving this agreement, particularly the efforts made by Egyptian President Husni Mubarak in this regard as well as the peace process cosponsors, the United States and Russia. He called on them to continue their efforts in order to achieve tangible progress on all other Syrian, Jordanian, and Lebanese peace tracks in a way that will guarantee a full Israeli withdrawal from all occupied Arab territories and the establishment of a comprehensive and just peace in the Middle East on the basis of international legitimacy.
