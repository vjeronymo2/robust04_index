BFN [Report by Silviya Velikova from the Bulgarian Socialist Party Congress in Sofia -- live] [Text] The first thing the delegates to the 41st Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP] Congress did was to adopt the congress agenda. Two additions to the agenda were proposed and adopted. The first proposes that a debate should be held on the party's election campaign platform during the discussions on the BSP program, and the second is to hear a declaration from a group of BSP intellectuals on the situation of the Bulgarian intelligentsia and the BSP's stance on this issue. BSP Supreme Council leader Zhan Videnov read to the delegates an exceptionally detailed report entitled "On the BSP political line." Here are a few of the points he mentioned: According to Videnov, the SDS's policy regarding the government failed because it was based on confrontation and restoration of the former state of things. This has led to the SDS becoming isolated form the people and falling into a serious crisis, Videnov said. Despite the numerous question marks hanging over the formula on which the Berov cabinet was formed 18 months ago, the BSP supported Berov because he placed all-national interests higher than narrow party ones. However, Videnov stressed that a number of features now militate against this support: the blocking of the reforms, complete exhaustion of resources, and a fragile economy. The most crucial decision that you congress delegates have to reach, Videnov said, is to call for early elections in October or November, which can lead to major changes in the country's development and the imposition of a clear, left-of-center, socially oriented policy. However, he emphasized that Bulgaria needs early elections, but they should in no event be premature ones. For the BSP, the elections were not an aim in itself, but means for developing a free and just society. The road toward such a society lies precisely via democratic elections, the BSP leader emphasized. Here is one of the concluding sentences that Videnov said at the end of this speech: More than ever before, we must get to realize where and why we went wrong during these four or five years that followed 45 years, and then strive to unleash new potential for social dialogue. At the moment the congress is continuing with a report from the All-Party Control-Auditing Commission, and it is then to hear a report on the work of the BSP parliamentary group.
