P Shcherban] [Abstract]Computer modelling is used to compute temperature fields in GaAs monocrystals grown in a thin layer of melt by the Kiropolous method with liquid sealing. The results of the calculations are given. It is shown that by using a bottom heater, a side heater and an additional heater sunk in flux, the Kiropolous method can be used to crystallize a GaAs layer 0.5-1.0 mm thick in a large diameter crucible (80-130 mm) in temperature fields with low values for the vertical (6-8 K/cm) and radial (2-29 K-cm) components of the temperature gradient. 3 figures and 10 references.
