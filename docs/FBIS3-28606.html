Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, February 3 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The main force of the political opposition in the country the Mongolian National Democratic Party [NDP] has given up its intention to cooperate with the government formed by the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party two years ago. The leadership of the party announced the other day about its refusal of their suggestions having been submitted to the prime minister last autumn. The twelve points of those suggestions included some particular ideas designed at the way out of the crises for the national economy. The repudition of any intentions to cooperate with the government the party explains by that the governmental programme has fully been discredited the government itself has been steeped in corruption and abuses of official position which relates to its top level most. [sentence as received] As a pretext for such statements served the recent statement by chief of the counterintelligence colonel L. Sanjaasuren. He accused the chairman of the parliament, his deputy and the premier of the government of criminal liaison with citizens of China working, according to the data available in this office, for the secret services of the southern neighbour.
