BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Dmitriy Gorokhov] [Text] Moscow, 19 May -- Paris welcomes NATO's readiness to provide Russia with a special status in the framework of "Partnership for Peace" that would correspond to Russia's specific character, Alain Juppe, foreign minister of the French Republic, told journalists in Moscow on Thursday. France, he said after his talks with Andrey Kozyrev, attaches great importance to bilateral cooperation with Russia. We are trying to encourage partnership between the Russian Federation, on the one hand, and Europe and various European institutions, on the other. France would like Russia to join the Council of Europe. "We are also pleased with the fact," the French minister continued, "that the Atlantic Union is ready to give special status to Russia that would correspondent to its specific character." Juppe said that during the talks in Moscow the situation in former Yugoslavia was discussed. You know that our countries have been working a lot together on this problem. A few days ago Russia, the United States, and the European Union states succeeded in adopting a joint document. One must not slacken pressure now--it is necessary to continue together to persuade the parties in the Bosnian conflict to end hostilities.
