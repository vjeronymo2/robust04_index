930402 FT 02 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television If you are at all interested in old and much loved vehicles and missed any of the Perpetual Motion series on their first screening, the repeats (8.00 BBC2) are well worth catching. Today's programme celebrates the Morris Minor, now one of the trendiest cars, at least in such places as north-west London, even though (or perhaps because) they are all over 20 years old. The continuing expansion of television and the consequent need for more and more programme material seems pretty clear when you find Channel 4 offering not one but two cookery series dedicated exclusively to vegetables. Grow Your Greens which starts tonight (9.00) runs in harness with 'Eat Your Greens', beginning on Wednesday. Cookery writer Sophie Grigson, daughter of Jane, presents both. BBC1's new six-part drama about English class divisions, The Riff Raff Element, is written by Debbie Horsfield, author of 'Making Out', the series about women in a Liverpool electronics factory. The new series pairs up the Tundish family of Tundish Hall with the Belchers, whose principal source of income, we are told, is burglary(9.30).
