Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A four-phased program is being launched to locate and destroy heroin laboratories and punish those behind this nefarious trade in the tribal areas. The Frontier Corps and Antinarcotics Task Force will work jointly to implement the program. A high-level meeting in Islamabad today reviewed various aspects of the prevailing narcotics problem in the country. Federal minister for interior and narcotics control, Major General, retired, Nasirullah Khan Babar, presided over the meeting which was also attended by governor of NWFP [North-West Frontier Province], Major General, retired, Khurshid Ali Khan, and other senior officials. The meeting considered mechanics to eliminate the cultivation of poppy and cannabis and decided to involve tribal elders in this process. Stress was laid on adoption of strict measures to eradicate drug abuse and interdiction of illicit narco-trafficking. It was decided that a comprehensive legislation should be enacted to effectively deal with all aspects of the drug menace before its extension to FATA [Federally Administered Tribal Areas].
