920506 FT 06 MAY 92 / National Savings trims rates NATIONAL Savings is cutting the returns on most of its products. The government department said the rates it offered had diverged from the rest of the savings market. The main changes are: The 36th issue of certificates (which paid 8.5 per cent a year tax-free if held for five years) has been withdrawn from sale and will be replaced by the 37th issue on May 13. The new issue will pay 8 per cent. The return on the yearly plan will also fall from 8.5 per cent to 8 per cent. The series C capital bonds (which paid a taxable 11.5 per cent a year if held for five years) have been withdrawn. They will be replaced by series D bonds paying 10.75 per cent. The rate on income bonds will be reduced from a taxable 10.25 per cent to 9.25 per cent from June 18. The rate on the investment account will be reduced from 9.5 per cent to 8.5 per cent from May 18.
