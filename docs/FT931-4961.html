930309 FT 09 MAR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Rebels backing the amendment THE following 26 Tory MPs voted for the Labour amendment last night: John Biffen (Shropshire North), Sir Richard Body (Holland with Boston), Nicholas Budgen (Wolverhampton South West), John Carlisle (Luton North), Bill Cash (Stafford), James Cran (Beverley), Sir George Gardiner (Reigate), Christopher Gill (Ludlow), Teresa Gorman (Billericay), Warren Hawksley (Halesowen & Stour), Andrew Hunter (Basingstoke), Toby Jessel (Twickenham), Roger Knapman (Stroud), Sir Ivan Lawrence (Burton), Michael Lord (Suffolk Central), Tony Marlow (Northampton North), Richard Shepherd (Aldridge-Brownhills), Sir Trevor Skeet (Bedfordshire North), Michael Spicer (Worcestershire South), Walter Sweeney (Vale of Glamorgan), Sir Peter Tapsell (Lindsey East), Sir Teddy Taylor (Southend East), Bill Walker (Tayside North), John Wilkinson (Ruislip Northwood), Ann Winterton (Congleton), Nicholas Winterton (Macclesfield). Twenty-two Tories did not vote: Rupert Allason (Torbay), Kenneth Baker (Mole Valley), Vivian Bendall (Ilford North), Sir Nicholas Bonsor (Upminster), John Butcher (Coventry South West), Michael Carttiss (Great Yarmouth), Iain Duncan-Smith (Chingford), Peter Fry (Wellingborough), Sir Michael Grylls (Surrey North West), *David Howell (Guildford), Ralph Howell (North Norfolk), Bernard Jenkin (Colchester North), Barry Legg (Milton Keynes South West), Sir Patrick McNair-Wilson (New Forest), Michael Mates* (Hampshire East), Sir Roger Moate (Faversham), Richard Needham* (Wiltshire North), David Porter (Waveney), Sir John Stanley (Tonbridge & Malling), John Townend (Bridlington), George Walden (Buckingham), John Whittingdale (Colchester South & Maldon). *It is thought unlikely these would have deliberately abstained. There were also three Labour MPs who did not vote. Eighteen Liberal Democrats supported the amendment, with David Alton (Mossley Hill) voting with the government and Sir David Steel (Tweeddale, Ettrick & Lauderdale) not registering a vote. The seven Welsh and Scottish Nationalists voted with the government. Four Ulster Unionists and two Democratic Unionists voted for the Labour amendment: Roy Beggs (East Antrim), Rev Ian Paisley (DUP North Antrim), Peter Robinson (DUP Belfast East), William Ross (East Londonderry), John Taylor (Strangford), David Trimble (Upper Bann). Sir James Kilfedder, Ulster Popular Unionist MP for Down North, voted with the government. Five Ulster Unionists and one Democratic Unionist did not vote: Clifford Forsythe (Antrim South), Rev William McCrea (DUP Mid Ulster), Ken Maginnis (Fermanagh and South Tyrone), James Molyneaux (Lagan Valley), Rev Martin Smyth (Belfast South), Cecil Walker (Belfast North). The four Social Democratic and Labour Party MPs did not vote.
