Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chairman of Ukrainian Gosatomnadzor (state committee for supervising safety requirements at nuclear power stations) Nikolay Steinberg believes that Kiev is "not obliged to inform Moscow of all accidents at Ukrainian nuclear power stations since we have not signed any bilateral agreements on exchange of nuclear information." In an interview with INTERFAX-Ukraine, Steinberg pointed out that "there exists a coherent international system for the notification of the nuclear situation in the world, under which all members of the European Nuclear Society inform the headquarters of this organization based in Switzerland of accidents at their nuclear power stations, which distributes the information among the other member-states." Meanwhile, the head of the press service of the Russian Gosatomnadzor, Yuriy Rogozhin, told INTERFAX yesterday that failures to inform the Russian Gosatomnadzor by former Soviet republics violate the official agreement between the nuclear supervising organizations reached shortly after the break-up of the Soviet Union.
