February 6, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

REDUCING NICOTINE

Question: I see where one of the leading cigarette companies is coming out with a cigarette that has practically no nicotine, just the way decaffeinated coffee has no caffeine.

Do you see anything wrong from a health standpoint with these cigarettes?

Answer: I think it's very revealing that the cigarette company emphasizes the low level of nicotine in its product, but says nothing about the level of tar. Tar is a major cause of lung cancer and heart disease. Presenting "de-nicotined" cigarettes as a safe alternative to regular cigarettes may be a good advertising ploy, but it's not honest.
