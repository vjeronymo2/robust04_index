BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 12 May. On 21 April, Education Minister Jorge Trelles Montero reported that the World Bank will grant a $270 million credit to the Education Ministry. The money will be used to finance teachers' training programs, and to build schools nationwide. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 22 Apr 94 Political Section p A2) The Southern Peru Copper Corporation has purchased the ILO copper refinery for $65 million. Company President Charles Preble said they will invest $20.2 million in modernizing the plant in the upcoming three years. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 23 Apr 94 p 9) On 28 April, Energy and Mines Minister Daniel Hokama Tokashiki confirmed that foreign companies will invest more than $300 million in oil exploitation in several parts of the country, especially in the Talara zone, Piura Department. He noted that oil reserves had increased 10 percent, and that production rose from 105,000 to 130,000 barrels of oil per day. He also reported that the Newton Mining Corporation will invest $50 million this year in the exploitation of the second gold reserve in the Maqui Maqui zone, in Cajamarca Department. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 29 Apr 94 p A2) The Fisheries Ministry reported that fish meal exports expanded from 88 million metric tons in February 1993 to 355 million metric tons in February 1994, while frozen fish exports increased from 600,000 metric tons to almost 3 million metric tons during the same period. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 2 May p A22) The National Statistics Institute, INEI, reported that the April inflation rate was 1.5 percent, while the cumulated inflation rate was 7.7 percent for the year's first four months and 27.9 percent from May 1993 to April 1994. INEI Director Felix Murillo said the April inflation rate was one of the lowest since December 1977. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 3 May 94 p 6) INEI reported that domestic production grew 8.6 percent in the year's first quarter. Construction registered the highest increase with 27.7 percent; fishing 27.1 percent; mining 3.2 percent; manufactured products 10.5 percent; and agriculture and livestock 8.9 percent. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 4 May 94 p B12)
