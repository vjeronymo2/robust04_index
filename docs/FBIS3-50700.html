Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by special AFP correspondent Francis Kohn] [Text] San Cristobal de Las Casas, Mexico, 5 Jan (AFP) -- AFP has confirmed that considerable reinforcements sent by the Mexican Army, including a column of armored personnel carriers, arrived in San Cristobal de las Casas (southern Mexico) on the night of 4 January as part of an operation launched against the Zapatist rebels who are beginning to retreat. Fifteen armored personnel carriers, jeeps equipped with light artillery and several ambulances crossed San Cristobal on Tuesday around 2200 apparently heading toward Ocosingo, which was still occupied by the rebels of the Zapatist National Liberation Army [EZLN]. Military authorities are still keeping quiet regarding the ongoing operations against the EZLN, which launched a surprise attack on 1 January. Apparently, some 12,000 soldiers are participating in the counteroffensive. According to several witnesses, the rebels -- who reportedly total 1,000 -- continued their withdrawal from various towns on Tuesday, moving their casualties to the mountain area.
