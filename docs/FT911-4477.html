910419 FT 19 APR 91 / Arts: Today's Television The time has come for the inauguration of a new leader in the country's official religion. Anthropologists long ago established that this is a pretty odd society, so it is not surprising that the new man will be wearing a long frock and a tall hat and carrying a decorated stick. But controversy has arisen among his followers: should the ceremony be a solemn one conducted in archaic jargon, or a more merry affair with modern songs and clapping? The Enthronement Of The Archbishop Of Canterbury can be seen live at 2.20 and in a recorded shortened form at 10.20 (BBC1). If the foregoing seems offensive perhaps you should watch Derek Jarman's extraordinary 1978 film Jubilee which Channel 4 is repeating in its 'Banned' season (midnight). A black view of England's decline, shot at the time of punk rock and in much the same spirit, it occasionally seems to have no other purpose than to offend. It is certainly different. In Arena (9.30 BBC2) performance artist Laurie Anderson examines mankind's obsession with the human face.
