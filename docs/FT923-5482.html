920901 FT 01 SEP 92 / Swedish markets still nervous The Swedish markets remained nervous yesterday amid fears of a further rise in short-term interest rates, writes Robert Taylor in Stockholm. There was concern that last week's 3 percentage point increase in the central bank's interest rate had not so far stemmed the outward flow of capital. The Stockholm bourse general index fell by 0.9 per cent in modest trading, with a 2.4 per cent decline in banking and finance shares.
