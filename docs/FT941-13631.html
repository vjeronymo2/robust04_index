940125 FT 25 JAN 94 / Surveys underline housing recovery Separate surveys published yesterday by housebuilders and estate agents emphasised the continuing recovery in the housing market. A Housebuilders Federation survey said 76 per cent of builders expected to increase house sales this year. Another quarter expected house prices to rise by 5-10 per cent while 57 per cent expected prices to rise by 1-5 per cent. A survey by the Board of the Ombudsman for Corporate Estate Agents, representing 4,200 estate agents handling about half of all UK house sales, reported that net sales rose by 12.6 per cent last year while contract exchanges rose by 10.1 per cent.
