Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 20 Feb (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa today met with Israeli Ambassador David Sultan. Following the meeting, the Israeli ambassador said that they discussed the current developments in the peace talks and the efforts being made to give impetus to the peace process. He added that the Palestinian and Israeli delegations will resume their meetings in Cairo tomorrow, Monday, and that the Israeli delegation led by Major General Amnon Shahaq will arrived in Cairo tomorrow. In reply to a question on Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat's accusations that Israel is not carrying out its commitments, the Israeli ambassador said that Israel is committed to all its pledges and hopes that the two sides will reach agreement. He added that the important thing is resuming the Palestinian-Israeli talks and reaching an agreement as soon as possible on the implementation of the Declaration of Principles, especially as the two sides made progress in the negotiations held in Taba last week.
