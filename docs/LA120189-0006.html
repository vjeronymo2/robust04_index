December 1, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

MANAGEMENT

Lee Iacocca is right on target (Op-Ed Page, Nov. 21) when he deplores American corporate management's obsession with short-term profits at the expense of long-range goals.

But his solution to this problem is out to lunch. If Iacocca's management cannot see the benefits of long-term commitment to building up the industrial base and to capturing a solid market share without Uncle Sam providing bribes in the form of special tax benefits, there isn't much hope.

Maybe the real solution is to curtail tying management compensation to last year's profits, and to provide private incentives to pursuing long-term performance. The function of taxes should be primarily the raising of revenue for the government, not providing incentives to good management which is something good managers should be doing on their own. If they won't do that, they shouldn't complain.

GIDEON KANNER

Los Angeles
