931223 FT 23 DEC 93 / Observer: Life's lottery Professor Teeling Smith, pontificating in The Times yesterday about the dangers of Richard Branson's plan to run the National Lottery as a charity, should know what he's talking about. Five years ago Teeling Smith, professor of health economics at Brunel University, was trustee of the National Hospital Trust. It planned to raise money for the National Health Service by aggregating lots of local lotteries to build up a big prize. The wheeze foundered amid threats from the Director of Public Prosecutions. 'It was an ill-fated venture and I was glad to be rid of it,' admitted the professor yesterday.
