Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 15 Feb--Minister of Foreign Affairs Andrzej Olechowski received Field Marshal Richard Vincent, chairman of the NATO Military Committee, on Tuesday morning. Andrzej Towpik, director of the MSZ [Ministry of Foreign Affairs] department for European institutions, told journalists after the meeting had ended that the conversation confirmed the openness of the NATO military personnel to both political and military cooperation with Poland. Towpik added that the conversation was devoted primarily to the Partnership for Peace concept. The status of the Polish presentation document, which is to be submitted before Poland joins the Partnership for Peace programme, was discussed. Towpik emphasized that the partnership concept is a very broad program that requires constant discussion. In his opinion the Polish side is trying to understand the idea of partnership, and NATO representatives will learn in the course of this discussion that certain elements of the [Partnership for Peace] concept require more precise definition and explanation.
