Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Lop Buri, Thailand, January 10 (XINHUA) -- Liu Huaqing, vice chairman of the Chinese Central Military Commission, visited three Thai military bases today. In the morning, Liu and his entourage arrived at U-tapao Air Base and Sattahip Naval Base, about 200 kilometers to the southeast of Bangkok. Liu, on board an amphibious armored car, watched air and coastal defense soldiers' demonstrations of parashooting, motor boat chasing and cliff climbing. In the afternoon, the group flew to the Royal Thai Army's Special Warfare Center in Lop Buri, about 160 kilometers to the north of Bangkok, and watched demonstrations of parashooting, ambush on the sea and commandos' rapid shooting. During the visit, Commanding General of the Special Warfare Command Unit Lt Gen Surayut Chulanont awarded Liu Huaqing with an honorary medal of parashooters, which is normally conferred to senior Thai military officers and foreign military and political leaders. On Saturday and Sunday, Liu visited Phuket, where he was welcomed by governor of Phuket, Sukit Nimikul.
