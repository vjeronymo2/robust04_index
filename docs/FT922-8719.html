920512 FT 12 MAY 92 / Company News in Brief BENLOX: Following shareholders' approval of voluntary scheme of arrangement and increase in authorised share capital, Sheikh Amin Al-Dahlawi is appointed non-executive chairman. A relisting of the company, which proposes to change its name to Keatway International, to be considered in due course. ***** BOWTHORPE: The offer to acquire shares of Penny & Giles not already owned has been declared unconditional in all respects. On May 1, Bowthorpe spoke for 8.2m P&G shares (86.1 per cent). ***** DAWSON INTERNATIONAL has acquired Fur-Textiles Nederland, the main distributor outside the UK of Dawson's sliver knit operations. ***** HI-TEC SPORTS: The 1991 performance-related earn-out payment of the purchase of its Cofex subsidiary has been calculated at Fl7.2m (Pounds 2.2m). Payment will be half in cash and half in shares. ***** RADIOTRU net asset value at January 31 50.2p (57.2p): pre-tax loss for year to end-January Pounds 46,927 (Pounds 64,067): losses per share 0.3p (0.89p). ***** STRATAGEM has agreed to sell Visitronic, Visirecord and related assets which are engaged in computer software systems, stationery and related products for the motor trade to Kalamazoo Computer. Initial consideration is Pounds 450,000. Further payments totalling Pounds 300,000 will be made in instalments beginning in May 1993. Further deferred consideration up to Pounds 300,000 are performance-dependent. ***** WORTH INVESTMENT Trust: After-tax revenue was Pounds 9,000, against Pounds 94,000, for the year to March 31, equal to earnings of 0.03p (0.58p). The dividend is cut to 0.1p (0.21p). At year-end net asset value per share stood at 25.16p (33.54p). The fall in NAV reflected a write-down in the valuation of unlisted investments.
