Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 7 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa told South Korean President Kim Yong-sam on Monday [7 March] he wants to resolve Japan's trade dispute with the United States, government officials said. Hosokawa made the comment during a phone call to Kim in which he congratulated him over the passage of political reform legislation in the South Korean legislature. Hosokawa reportedly said he "must find a clue to solve" Japan's trade surplus with the United States. Kim told Hosokawa he "hopes trade friction with the U.S. can be solved in a beneficial way." The prime minister paid tribute to Kim's leadership and zeal in achieving political reform. Kim said that "advance is not possible without political reform." "The South Korean community will become stable for the first time as a result of clean elections," Kim reportedly said.
