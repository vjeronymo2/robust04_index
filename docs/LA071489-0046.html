July 14, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

COMPTON

The report on present-day Compton ("Black Middle Class Keeps the Faith for Compton," Part I, June 26) and its proud residents is a lucid testimonial not only on pride of ownership, but also pride of community.

The article should be read by all ethnic groups who, despite social stigma and stereotypes nurtured by bigots believing in "exclusive neighborhoods," continue to think that the latter is the sole way to go on the road to the American success story. The Compton story defies this.

May there be more community-conscious individuals like those Compton folks, and may their tribe increase!

LUDY ASTRAQUILLO ONGKEKO

Los Angeles
