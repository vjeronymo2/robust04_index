BFN [Text] Beijing, April 27 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese mainland-based Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Straits (ARATS) today sent a message to the Taiwan-based Straits Exchanges Foundation (SEF), expressing heartfelt condolences to the victims and their families from Taiwan involved in the crash of a China Airlines Airbus A-300 at Nagoya Airport yesterday. Nine people were badly injured and 262 people died when the China Airlines flight 140 carrying 257 passengers and 14 crew from Taibei crashed and exploded on landing at Nagoya Airport in central Japan yesterday, according to the latest press reports. Sixty-three of the passangers were from Taiwan, according to the airline president. Meanwhile, a spokesman from the Chinese Foreign Ministry also expressed condolences for all victims and their families involved in the plane crash.
