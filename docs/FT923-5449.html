920901 FT 01 SEP 92 / Arts: Today's Television If drama is your passion, tonight is not your night. The broadcasters are packing the schedules with dreadful mini-series and other imported rubbish while the chattering classes are all safely tucked away in Tuscany. If you insist upon drama your best bets are ITV's excellent near-documentary police series The Bill (8.00) which today, unusually, involves guns; and Joseph Mankiewicz's movie version of Anthony Shaffer's excellent thriller Sleuth starring Michael Caine and Laurence Olivier (8.30 C4). There is plenty of good non-fiction. Shaking The Heavens (7.50 BBC2) is proving to be much the most interesting series about old people for years. Today we are promised that John Papworth makes a plea for 'a return to grass roots democracy'. Have we ever been there? Floyd On Spain finds the likeably enthusiastic cook investigating the food and drink of Catalonia (8.30 BBC2). In The John Bull Business (9.30 BBC2) Glaxo chairman Sir Paul Girolami talks about the problems of being big without being inhuman. First Tuesday looks at India's ambitious nuclear power programme (10.40 ITV).
