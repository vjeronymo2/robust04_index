930804 FT 04 AUG 93 / Fears for skill centres as buy-out prospects recede MOST OF the 35 skillcentres owned by Astra, the failed training organisation, are in the hands of the receivers and are set to close, according to the National Union of Civil and Public Servants. The possibility of a management buy-out for Astra appeared to be receding last night. The organisation, privatised three years ago when the government put Pounds 11m into the former Skills Training Agency, suffered from high overheads and an increasingly competitive funding system. The Training and Enterprise Councils, which are responsible for delivering government training schemes in England and Wales, are Astra's biggest clients. They say the demise of the organisation will make little difference to either Youth Training or Training for Work, the main training scheme for the adult unemployed. Some Tec officials say the shake-out at Astra will be good for the industry. The one reservation is that the experience of Astra staff in finding placements for clients could be lost. It is estimated that Astra, though the biggest training organisation in the country, accounted for only about 2 per cent of all Tec contracts with training providers. 'This is at most a minor irritation,' said Mr Andrew Gurr, chief executive of North Mid Cheshire Tec, which has had a contract with the Astra skillcentre at Runcorn. 'My in-tray is loaded with offers from other training providers.'
