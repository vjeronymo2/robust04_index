Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 31 (KCNA) -- U.S. religious leader Rev. Billy Graham now on a visit to the Democratic People's Republic of Korea was interviewed by reporters in Pyongyang today. "The most memorable was that I had an opportunity of spending private times and the lunch with the great leader," he said. Referring to his visit to Kim Il-song University and the three revolution exhibition, he said: "I have been fully interested in large machinery and also the beautiful clothes that you are making in this country." "The whole of the trip to the DPRK has been a wonderful experience and I'll never forget being among the people of this country," he said. Recalling that he read the great leader's New Year address, He said: "Wherever I go, various countries have economic problems. We, in the United States, have same serious economic problems. "The great leader is looking far ahead, and it seems what can be done now will help you in the future." Turning to the moral and spiritual life of the Korean people, he said: "I see the responsible people and hear the beautiful singing. I say the order is disciplined here among the people." "`The 10-point programme of the great unity of the whole nation for the reunification of the country' needs a great deal of study," he said, stressing "I am for the unification of the country." "The great leader has said that he hopes the country's reunification would be done during the 1990s. So do I," the pastor noted.
