931111 FT 11 NOV 93 / French free Algerian exiles France's judicial authorities yesterday released almost all 88 people rounded up by police in a vast search-and-arrest operation on Tuesday but kept three for questioning, including two leaders of the Algerian Fraternity in France, believed to be a front organisation for the Algerian Islamic Front (FIS), Reuter reports from Paris. The Interior Ministry said investigators found pro-FIS propaganda, false identity papers and money, but no weapons. The government said the dragnet, a response to the abduction last month of three French consular officials in Algiers, aimed to prevent potential acts of violence.
