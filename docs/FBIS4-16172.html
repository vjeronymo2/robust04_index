BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Boris Krivoshey and Aleksandr Krylovich] [Text] Moscow April 14 TASS -- The idea of stepping up interaction among the European Union, Russia, the United States and the United Nations toward settlement in former Yugoslavia was discussed during a telephone conversation on Thursday between Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and his French counterpart Alain Juppe. Spokesman for the Russian Foreign Ministry Grigoriy Karasin told this a briefing on Thursday. He said that the Russian foreign minister, in accordance with the instruction from President Boris Yeltsin, declared for stepping up work in this direction, including the effort to work out an effective mechanism to scale down the conflict. "This is in keeping with the discussion of the Yugoslav events in the State Duma on Wednesday," Karasin said.
