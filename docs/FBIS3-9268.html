Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cease-fire negotiations between the Croatian Government and rebel Serb authorities in the protected areas are to resume on the 29th of this month. The talks were adjourned about 1230 last night, after 13 hours. The most difficult points were details of how to implement the cease-fire and to disengage forces on the ground. According to HINA, all the participants were optimistic, and the Russian special envoy, Vitaliy Churkin, described the situation as favorable for reaching an agreement. The American ambassador stated that the countries involved in the talks firmly support Croatian integrity, but the problem must be solved by peaceful means. The leader of the Croatian delegation, Hrvoje Sarinic, said that the fact that the talks would resume next Tuesday spoke for itself. He added that he was optimistic about reaching a solution next week.
