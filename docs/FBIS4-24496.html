BFN [By Y. C. Tsai] [Text] Taipei, May 31 (CNA) -- President Li Teng-hui Monday [30 May] called on leading industrialists to help develop Taiwan's industrial base in order to ensure continued economic growth and boost domestic competitiveness in international markets. Li made the statement while meeting with a group of 20 industrialists, headed by Chairman Kao Ching-yuan of the Chinese National Federation of Industries. To seek further progress, Li said, the industrial sector should learn how to make the best use of its resources and stress personnel training as well as research and development. The government will cooperate with the industrial sector in mapping out appropriate policies and laws to help domestic industries cope with challenges and competition from abroad, Li pledged. Various phases of economic development call for different strategies, he added. Li talked with the industrialists on a variety of economic and noneconomic issues, including financing, investment, the national health insurance program, the introduction of foreign laborers to Taiwan, environmental protection, and Taiwan's entry into GATT. Among the industrial representatives were President Liu Kuo-chao of the Taiwan Electric Appliance Manufacturers Association, President Lu Chi-sheng of the Taiwan Association of Machinery Industry, and Chairman Wang Yiu-tzeng of the China Rebar Group. The president said that he was happy to exchange views with the industrialists. Noting he has already met with delegates from the agricultural and fishery sectors, he said he will also meet with business and labor [passage indistinct] the Chinese Federation of Labor on June 10.
