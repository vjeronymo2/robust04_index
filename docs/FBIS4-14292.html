BFN [Text] Cairo, 29 Apr (MENA)--Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat left Cairo for Tunis today after a two-day visit to Egypt. During the visit 'Arafat held talks with President Husni Mubarak, U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher, and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres. The talks resulted in the fixing of 4 May as the target date for the signing in Cairo of the Palestinian-Israeli agreement on the implementation of self-rule in Gaza and Jericho. 'Arafat was seen off at the airport by Fatah Representative in Cairo Zuhdi al-Qidrah.
