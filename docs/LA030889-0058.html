March 8, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

PREP ROUNDUP;

JOYNER, CALVERT LEAD ESTANCIA TO GOLF VICTORY

Senior Mike Joyner and junior Ken Calvert shot one-under par 35s to lead Estancia High School to a 188-203 victory over Palm Springs in a nonleague none-hole golf match Tuesday at Mesa Verde Country Club in Costa Mesa.

Paul Heinkle shot a two-over 38 for Estancia (4-0).

In nonleague softball:

Canyon 9, Lakewood 1 -- Sara Roberts and Sherry Panchisin each had two hits and an RBI for host Canyon (3-0), which has scored 29 runs in three games. Kristin Heizer (2-0) pitched a complete game.

Laguna Beach 16, Estancia 2 -- The Artists scored 11 runs in the first at home and coasted to the victory in a game called after 4 1/2 innings.

Megan Chesley had two hits and three RBIs, Lauren Candelaria had three hits and three RBIs, and Kathy Zeller had two hits and four RBIs for Laguna Beach (2-0).

Woodbridge 1, Huntington Beach 0 -- Lisa Wehren pitched a two-hitter for the Warriors and scored the game's only run at Alton Park in Irvine.

Wehren (2-0) singled in the first inning, went to second on an error, stole third and scored when the catcher's throw was wild.

Woodbridge is 2-1.

Valencia 7, Esperanza 5 -- Danette Davis hit a home run in the first for visiting Valencia (1-1). Jennifer Huffman had a hit and two RBIs also for Valencia.

Gahr 0, Fountain Valley 0 (13 innings, darkness) -- Gahr's Dede Weiman and Fountain Valley's Janet Miyahara each pitched 13 innings at Fountain Valley. Rach gave up two hits. Fountain Valley is 0-1-1; Gahr is 1-0-1.

In Angelus League tennis:

Mater Dei 16, Bishop Amat 2 -- Dax Peterson won at No. 1 singles, 6-2, 6-0, 6-0, for host Mater Dei in the league opener for both teams. Jason Harnett and Mike McFarland won at No. 1 doubles, 6-1, 6-0, 6-0.

In nonleague tennis:

Laguna Beach 16, Capistrano Valley 2 -- Graham Gilles, a freshman at Laguna Beach, won his three matches, 6-0, 6-0, 6-0, to lead the Artists (2-0) at Capistrano Valley. Jon Leach and Lee Kirner also swept their matches.

Sonora 13, Esperanza 5 -- Matt Rice and Lonnie Tabbaa swept their three matches for Sonora (3-1).

Corona del Mar 15, Palos Verdes 3 -- David Bain, a freshman, won, 6-0, 6-0, 6-1, at No. 3 singles for host Corona del Mar.

Mission Viejo 13, Edison 5 -- Rodrigo Zulian, Pat Bray and Jeff Holroyd swept their matches for the Diablos (3-0) at Edison (0-5).
