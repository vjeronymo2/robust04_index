Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] President Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari has said that the Kashmir problem is a stumbling block in the way of durable peace and stability in the region. The president was talking to the chairman of All Party Parliamentary Human Rights Group of Britain, Lord (Eric Evory) in Islamabad today. The president said that the only way to resolve the problem is to give the people of Kashmir their inalienable right of self-determination. Referring to the gross violations of human rights in the occupied Kashmir, the president said that Pakistan would continue to utilize all diplomatic channels to awaken the world conscience against continuing repression in the occupied valley. Lord (Evory) told the president that the UN ability to help in the resolution of the problem has been stymied by the Indian reluctance to cooperate. Lord (Evory) also called on Senate Chairman Mr. Wasim Sajjad in Islamabad today. The Senate chairman drew the attention to the fact that since the Kashmir issue has its roots in the time of British India, it is special responsibility of the Great Britain to influence the world opinion for peaceful solution of the issue. Expressing his deep concern over the situation, Lord (Evory) said that the magnitude of atrocities being committed by the Indian forces has no match in the past.
