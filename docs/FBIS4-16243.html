BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Ivan Ivanov and Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] Moscow, 15 Apr -- A session of the Council of Heads of Government of the CIS member states has just started at the President Hotel in Moscow. Taking part are the prime ministers and authorized representatives of Azerbaijan, Armenia, Belarus, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Moldova, Russia, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, and Ukraine. Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin is chairing the session. The participants in the meeting are to examine some 20 issues on the agenda, which may be divided up into three sections: the problems of implementing the Treaty on the Creation of an Economic Union; the problems of implementing the instructions of the Council of Heads of State, the Council of Heads of Government, and the Coordination and Consultative Committee; and issues submitted on the initiative of the states and of the statutory and sectoral bodies of the CIS. Among the issues to be examined at today's session one can pick out those which relate to the promotion of the further integration of the economies of the CIS countries: these are the joining by Ukraine of the economic union with the rights of an associate member, the setting up of a permanently operating body -- the Commission of the Economic Union -- under the the CIS Coordination and Consultative Committee, and the creation of a free trade zone on the territory of the CIS countries.
