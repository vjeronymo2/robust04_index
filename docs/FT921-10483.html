920206 FT 06 FEB 92 / Decline in number of profit-share schemes THE NUMBER of beneficiaries of profit-sharing schemes and discretionary share option schemes fell in the year to March 31 1991, according to Inland Revenue statistics. Only the number in a third government scheme, the savings-related share option, increased during 1990-91, although the total number of all three types of schemes approved by the Revenue rose during the period. The number of employees allocated shares under profit-sharing schemes fell by 20,000 to 900,000. The initial value of the shares allocated fell by Pounds 20m to Pounds 410m. The total number of schemes approved rose by 72 to 880. The number granted options under discretionary share option schemes, which give selected employees the right to buy shares, fell by 35,000 to 65,000. The initial value of the shares over which options were granted fell Pounds 420m to Pounds 1.4bn. The number of schemes approved rose by 395 to 4,121. The number of employees granted options under savings-related share options rose by 80,000 to 540,000, representing an increase in the initial value of Pounds 380m to Pounds 1.4bn. The number of schemes approved rose by 81 to 804.
