Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, March 18 (AFP)--The Egyptian authorities banned sales of the Friday edition of the London-based Arabic-language daily AL-HAYAT because it reported the execution of the two fundamentalist soldiers accused of plotting to kill Mubarak, reliable sources said. The Egyptian media did not mention the executions or the trial of the two Islamic militants. AL-HAYAT is edited in London and distributed in Cairo and other Arab capitals. Last year the authorities criticised the newspaper several times, notably in connection with its publication of an interview with Shaykh Umar 'Abd-al-Rahman, whom the Islamic militants regard as their spiritual leader.
