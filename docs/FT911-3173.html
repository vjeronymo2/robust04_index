910426 FT 26 APR 91 / Too soon for links with Nato The new democracies of central and eastern Europe, such as Hungary and Czechoslovakia could not, for the moment, expect to establish formal membership links with Nato, Mr Manfred Worner, Nato secretary-general said in Prague yesterday, writes Robert Mauthner, diplomatic correspondent. Mr Worner told a conference on the future of European security that Nato did not seek a shift of balance or an extension of military borders to the east, nor could it provide former members of the Warsaw Pact with formal security guarantees. 'At the moment, what Nato can offer is a multiple and intensive web of relations which does not exclude the Soviet Union, but wants it to be a constructive and creative partner as well.'
