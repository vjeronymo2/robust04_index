Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN ["Text" of protocol on results of Supreme Soviet vote to recall Chairman Stanislav Shushkevich and Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich, announced at the evening session in Minsk by People's Deputy Ivan Bambiza, head of the vote-counting committee -- live] [Text] Protocol No. 3 of the session of the vote-counting committee on the secret ballot vote to recall Belarusian Supreme Soviet Chairman Stanislav Stanislavovich Shushkevich and to dismiss Vyacheslav Frantsevich Kebich from the post of Belarusian Council of Ministers chairman, dated 26 January 1994. Present were: Ivan Bambiza, chairman of the vote-counting committee; committee secretary Nikalay Harelik; members of the counting committee -- Alyaksandr Belyy, Olga Lukovnich, Nikalay Zhuravlev, Alyaksandr Kuzmenkov, Alyaksandr Kryuchkov, Frants Minkov, Vitaliy Malashko, Uladzimir Novikaw, Zinaida Penkova, Viktar Rusak, and Viktar Fedarenka. The agenda of the session: On the results of the secret ballot vote to recall Belarusian Supreme Soviet Chairman Stanislav Stanislavovich Shushkevich and dismiss Vyacheslav Frantsevich Kebich from the post of Belarusian Council of Ministers chairman . 1. The vote-counting committee conducted the vote count on the secret ballot to recall Belarusian Supreme Soviet Chairman Stanislav Stanislavovich Shushkevich. To conduct the vote, peoples deputies were given 290 ballots. After unsealing the boxes, 250 ballots were discovered, including five invalid ballots. The votes were distributed as follows: For recalling Stanislav Stanislavovich Shushkevich from the post of Belarusian Supreme Soviet chairman, 209 votes were cast. Against -- 36 votes. 2. The vote-counting committee conducted the vote count on the secret ballot to discharge Vyacheslav Frantsevich Kebich from the post of Belarusian Council of Ministers chairman. To conduct the vote, peoples deputies were given 290 ballots. After unsealing the boxes, 281 ballots were discovered, including five invalid ballots. The votes were distributed as follows: For discharging Vyacheslav Frantsevich Kebich from the post of Belarusian Supreme Council chairman, 101 votes were cast. Against -- 175 votes. Signatures of the committee's chairman, secretary, and members [appear at the bottom of the protocol].
