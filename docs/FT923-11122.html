920727 FT 27 JUL 92 / International Company News: Lower tax bill cushions Black Arrow profit fall THE FULL effects of recession were felt by Black Arrow Group in the year ended March 31 1992, resulting in a reduction in pre-tax profit from Pounds 1.87m to Pounds 1.26m. However, earnings per share rose to 3.5p (3.01p) after a tax charge of only Pounds 384,000 (Pounds 1.07m) and minorities reduced to Pounds 14,000 (Pounds 55,000). The final dividend is 1.6p for an unchanged total of 2.1p. Turnover in the office furniture manufacturing division fell 30 per cent to Pounds 15.1m, and profit by a commensurate amount. However, margins were sustained by a reduction in overall costs. In leasing and instalment finance turnover dropped 11.5 per cent to Pounds 1.45m. The profit included an exceptional Pounds 308,000 on the sale of a subsidiary's trading freehold. The group's financial condition was healthy and borrowings had been virtually eliminated.
