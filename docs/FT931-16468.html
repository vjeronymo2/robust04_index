The Money Programme - 'a programme that has held a distinguished place in the BBC's current affairs output for so many years,' according to Ellison - is watched by an audience of more than 2m people each week. Ellison joined the BBC in 1980 and has worked on Newsnight and Nationwide as well as in network features. She replaces David Nissan who is to become UK news editor at the Financial Times. ***** Timothy Jones, director of BP Oil Europe, will join Lloyd's Register of Shipping in February and will become deputy chairman in July. Jones, 48, who has been responsible for BP's European refining, shipping and distribution operations in Brussels for the past three years, has worked at the oil group for 24 years. 'I'm interested in starting a second career before it's too late,' he says. 'The Lloyd's job is compatible with the skills I've acquired at BP.' Jones will be looking to develop the growing technological services provided by Lloyd's to refineries and nuclear power stations.
