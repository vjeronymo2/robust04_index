Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Tbilisi January 15 TASS -- The Georgian Interior Ministry introduced today APCs in key areas of downtown Tbilisi and concentrated there almost all personnel of the city police due to Zviadist attempts to hold mourning arrangements in connection with the supposed death of the ex-president. Although the death has not been confirmed it is believed the body of Zviad Gamsakhurdia is buried in western Georgia. Zviadists demand a funeral ceremony: a minute of silence with the suspension of work of enterprises, organizations and traffic, as well as rallies that are banned by the state of emergency regime introduced in Georgia on September 20, 1993. No excesses or clashes between the Tbilisi police and supporters of the ex-president were reported today.
