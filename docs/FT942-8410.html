940520 FT 20 MAY 94 / UK Company News: Chillington lifts pay-out Chillington Corporation reported pre-tax profits of Pounds 217,000 for 1993, against a Pounds 482,000 deficit previously. Sales fell slightly to Pounds 43.6m (Pounds 44.4m). A Pounds 871,000 loss from discontinued operations was behind reduced operating profits of Pounds 1.74m (Pounds 2.66m). A charge of Pounds 1.14m mainly represented the loss on the disposal of its 49 per cent stake in Anglo-Eastern Plantations. Interest took Pounds 2.04m (Pounds 2.38m). The company said that it had decided to concentrate on developing its plantation and overseas trading interests. In order to reflect this it proposed to revert to its former name - Plantation and General Investments. The recommended final dividend of 0.75p makes a total of 1.5p (0.25p) for the year. Some Pounds 908,000 was transferred from reserves to cover this. The loss per share worked through at 1.57p (3.78p).
