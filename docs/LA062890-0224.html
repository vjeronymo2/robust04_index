June 28, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

The Italian basketball team Il Messaggero "temporarily" dropped its tampering suit against the Boston Celtics, but said it has "not weakened in our resolve" to keep guard Brian Shaw from returning to the NBA club next season.

The announcement came one day after a federal judge in Boston upheld a five-year, $6.2-million contract that Shaw signed with the Celtics in January during the first season of his two-year contract with Il Messaggero.

The Italian team's suit claimed the Celtics acted illegally by signing Shaw to a multiyear deal before his contract with Il Messaggero expired.

"The judge's ruling in no way alters the fact that we have a valid contract with Brian Shaw that predates that of the Celtics," Il Messaggero's American attorney John McGovern said. "More important, Brian Shaw wants to fulfill his contract with Il Messaggero and we want him back next season."
