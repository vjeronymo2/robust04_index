930326 FT 26 MAR 93 / World Stock Markets (America): Drug stocks recovery and Russia lift Dow Wall Street SIGNS OF reduced tensions in Russia and a revival in drugs stocks helped Wall Street post gains through the day, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed 15.94 higher at 3,461.32 after being lifted to near-record levels on program-buying in the afternoon. The more broadly based Stan dard & Poor's 500 rose 2.67 to 450.64, while the Amex composite gained 0.84 at 419.12, and the Nasdaq composite firmed 6.65 at 681.01. Trading volume on the NYSE was some 251m shares and rises outnumbered declines by 1,129 to 736. On Thursday, the Dow fell 16.48 to 3,445.38. Equities were helped by a recovery in drug stocks, which fell on Wednesday following Merck's disappointing earnings forecast. Merck firmed Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 35 1/2 , Pfizer added Dollars 2 to Dollars 59 5/8 and Abbott Laboratories rose Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 24 1/4 . Bristol-Myers Squibb, however, eased Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 57 1/2 . Market sentiment also benefited from signs that tensions are easing in Russia after Mr Ruslan Khasbulatov, a rival of President Boris Yeltsin, said that he opposed the impeach ment of the latter. In the oil sector, Getty Petro- leum soared Dollars 2 to Dollars 12 1/8 as it reported fourth quarter net income of 54 cents a share, up from 26 cents a year earlier. Among the other energy stocks, Exxon gained Dollars 1/2 at Dollars 65 5/8 and Mobil advanced Dollars 1/4 at Dollars 69 5/8 but Chevron slid Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 81. International Business Machines share price continued to as the stock was hit by worries about the sector on Wednesday after Amdahl pre dicted a bad first quarter. Investors were also uneasy on reports that Mr Louis Gertsner, chairman of RJR Nabisco, would soon be named head of IBM. IBM was Dollars 5/8 lower at Dollars 50 1/2 but Amdahl held steady at Dollars 6 1/2 . Other computer issues revived after Wednesday's declines including Digital Equipment up Dollars 3 to Dollars 45 1/8 and Hewlett-Packard Dollars 1 3/4 higher at Dollars 74 1/2 . Amongst big stocks actively traded were Philip Morris, down Dollars 1 to Dollars 61 1/2 and General Motors Dollars 3/8 lower at Dollars 38 1/2 . Campbell Soup rose Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 43 3/4 after it increased its quar terly dividend to 25 cents a share from 22 cents. Technology and healthcare issues dominated Nasdaq morning trading. Oracle System firmed Dollars 1 1/8 to Dollars 36 7/8 , Amgen added Dollars 1/8 at Dollars 34 7/8 and US Healthcare improved Dollars 1 to Dollars 41 1/4 . Canada TORONTO prices ended higher in active trading with the TSE-300 composite index - based on preliminary data - up 8.34 points, or 0.23 per cent, to 3,612.04, with advances leading declines 374 to 268. Today's volume of 57.18m shares included a block of 725,140 shares of the publisher Thomson. Mitel Corp. shares surged CDollars 0.23 to CDollars 2.85 but the company said that it did not know of any reason for the activity. International Epitek soared CDollars 1.05 to CDollars 3.00.
