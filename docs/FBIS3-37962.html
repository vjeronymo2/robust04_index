Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Shanayev] [Text] Vladikavkaz February 24 TASS--The situation in the regions of North Ossetia where state of emergency has been extended until March 31, remains relatively calm, according to well informed military sources. No clashes and conflict situations on the inter-ethnic grounds have been reported over the last twenty-four hours. The peacekeeping forces of Russia continue to fulfill tasks on maintaining state of emergency. The same sources confirm that the crime situation in the region is still very tense. Over 3,000 vehicles were examined on the territory of North Ossetia. Seven units of firearms, grenades and hundreds of cartridges of a different caliber were confiscated from local inhabitants by patrol squads. More than one hundred trains were checked up and escorted at railway stations.
