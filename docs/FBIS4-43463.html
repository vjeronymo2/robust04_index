BFN [Text] Ankara, June 15, IRNA -- Iran's Interior Minister, 'Ali Mohammad Besharati said at the end of his two day official visit to Turkey that he had had "very constructive and fruitful" talks with Turkish officials. Talking to reporters at Ankara airport, Besharati and his Turkish counterpart Nahit Mentese expressed pleasure with the growing trend of Tehran-Ankara relations in all domains including cultural, social, diplomatic, economic, trade and security fields. They called a protocol inked by two countries on expansion of border security cooperation one of the achievements of the two-day visit of the Iranian delegation to Turkey. Besharati emphasized that Tehran and Ankara are concerned about the recent developments in northern Iraq, with both insisting on maintaining Iraq's territorial integrity. Mentese said that during talks with Besharati both sides revised the provisions of letters of understanding signed earlier by Iran and Turkey, in addition to reaching agreement that the Iran-Turkey joint committee on security cooperation continue its activities. During his two-day stay here, Besharati met with Turkish president Suleyman Demirel, Prime Minister Tansu Ciller and the ministers of foreign affairs and interior.
