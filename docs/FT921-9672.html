920211 FT 11 FEB 92 / International Company News: Northern Telecom and Motorola form venture MOTOROLA of the US and Northern Telecom of Canada yesterday unveiled plans to pool more than Dollars 200m of assets and form a joint venture to market cellular telephone network systems in North and South America. The venture - between the leading US electronics and semiconductor manufacturer and the Canadian-based telecoms group - is aimed at stepping up competition against American Telephone & Telegraph (AT&T) and Ericsson of Sweden, the leaders in the cellular switching sector. The new company, to be called Motorola-Nortel Communications, is expected to have 1992 sales of more than Dollars 500m, according to Mr William Spencer, a Motorola vice president who will become chief executive of the new 50-50 venture. Mr Spencer said the venture would sell, service and support cellular infrastructure systems in all areas of North, central and South America, including the Caribbean region. The venture is to be based in Chicago and will consist of between Dollars 200m and Dollars 300m of assets that are being pooled. Some 600 Motorola and 300 Northern Telecom employees will be transferred to the venture. The North American cellular telephone network sector is estimated to have annual sales of around Dollars 1.4bn, with a compound growth rate of around 40 per cent a year.
