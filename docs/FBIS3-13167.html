Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India won a stunning diplomatic victory in Geneva last night when Pakistan was forced to withdraw unconditionally its resolution on Kashmir at the UN Human Rights Commission. Reports say the Pakistani decision came after its delegation realized that there was no prospect of the resolution being carried. Leading the way, Iran appealed to Pakistan not to press for the resolution just before the voting was scheduled to begin. The Iranian move was supported by China. Immediately after, delegate after delegate of the developing countries, including Syria and Indonesia, appealed to Pakistan to withdraw the resolution. Majority of the countries indicated that they would abstain from voting. They said the issue should be resolved through bilateral talks between the two sides. The leader of the Indian delegation, Mr. Atal Behari Vajpayee, and the minister of state for external affairs, Mr. Salman Khurshid, have expressed the satisfaction at the withdrawal of the resolution and called it a vindication of New Delhi's principled stand. The leader of the Pakistani delegation said that Islamabad's move followed high-level consultations between Iran and Pakistan and the views of the majority of member countries. The external affairs minister, Mr. Dinesh Singh, has thanked Iran and China for prevailing upon Pakistan to withdraw the resolution. In a statement in New Delhi, he expressed the hope that Pakistan will now be ready for bilateral talks under the Simla Agreement and desist from misleading the international community through false allegations of violations of human rights in India. The prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao, has complimented Mr. Vajpayee on phone for the excellent work done at the UN Human Rights Commission in Geneva.
