December 2, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

VIOLENCE POLARIZES EL SALVADOR

In reading the excerpts from "Freedom Made Flesh," by Rev. Ignacio Ellacuria (Op-Ed Page, Nov. 21), one feels compelled to ask, which is most evil in El Salvador, the left-leaning rebels (communists to us), the militarists running the country, or the Roman Catholic Church? Sadly, the answer is that all are equally culpable and will have the blood of the people on their hands for many years to come.

The now deceased Rev. Ellacuria saw "good violence" mandated by his interpretation of the Bible, as something which was liberating, a kind of rightful wrath. Shame on all who think this way. No good reasons can be advanced to support murder and destruction. It is no surprise that he died at the hand of violence, it seems clear that he called it on himself.

What solace may have been possible for the El Salvadorans by their church and its clergy has clearly disappeared. History has shown repeatedly that when religious institutions are actively a part of the political process or attempt to usurp it, substituting one hierarchical model of violent authority for another, democratic processes are overwhelmed and government by consensus becomes impossible.

JAMES BLOCK

Los Angeles
