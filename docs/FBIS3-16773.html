Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS] [Text] Moscow March 18 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin signed a decree "On suspension of a resolution of the Leningrad Regional Government," the Presidential Press Service Reports. The decree runs as follows: "Bearing in mind, the resolution of the Leningrad Regional Government of February 7, 1994, #5 'on measures for preparations and holding of elections of the Leningrad Regional Legislative Assembly, local government bodies and a regional referendum on draft rules of the Leningrad region' in part of holding the regional referendum on draft rules of the Leningrad region violates Paragraph 2 of Article 66 of the Russian Constitution, under which rules of a territory, a region, a federal city, an autonomous region and an autonomous district shall be adopted by a legislative (representative) body of corresponding subject of the Russian Federation and being guided by Paragraph 2 of Article 85 of the Russian Constitution, I resolute: - to suspend the validity of the resolution of the Leningrad Regional Government of February 7, 1994, #5 `On measures for preparations and holding of elections of the Legislative Assembly of the Leningrad region, local government bodies and a regional referendum on draft rules of the Leningrad region' in part of holding the regional referendum on draft rules of the Leningrad region". The Leningrad regional Government scheduled for March 20, 1994, a regional referendum on draft rules of the Leningrad region. This decision was protested against the regional procurator as not corresponding to Article 66 of the Russian Constitution. Under this article, such legal acts shall be adopted by legislative (representative) body of a corresponding federation subject. However, such a body has not been elected in the Leningrad region. The elections of the Regional Legislative Assembly are scheduled for March 20. The regional government rejected the protest of the procurator, and the president used his constitutional right to suspend the regional government's resolution on the referendum, although he has nothing against the rules themselves.
