920220 FT 20 FEB 92 / Appointments: Linread succession Peter Harrisson has been appointed group managing director of Linread, the Birmingham-based nuts and bolts manufacturer, following a boardroom upheaval which has seen two senior departures in the past fortnight. Mike Hughes, the chief executive and Orrie Mead, the former finance director, left following serious problems in Linread's commercial products division. One of the three plants has been closed and the workforce has been cut by 45 per cent to 260; the company's 1991 results will show a loss. Harrisson, 46, joins Linread after 11 years with Davy Corp-oration, where he was chief executive of the mechanical handling division. Like Mike Hughes, who was brought in to revitalise a then loss-making Linread in 1983, Harrisson also spent part of his early career with BTR. After six years which saw profits improve, Linread's recovery came to an abrupt halt last year after faults were identified in the financial reporting system; Linread's 1990 annual report notes that Hughes took direct responsibility for the day-to-day operations. Orrie Mead stepped down from the Linread board last June and was put in charge of the commercial products division. He was replaced as finance director by Pieter Hazenberg.
