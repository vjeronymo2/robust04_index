BFN [Text] The United States has erected many non-tariff barriers on its imports although it has put pressure on foreign nations to rectify their unfair practices, the Korea Trade Promotion Corporation (KOTRA) said, quoting a report of a U.S. civilian body. In a report on U.S. unfair trade practices, the Citizens for a Sound Economy Foundation said that there are many unfair practices in the United States and certain barriers are higher than those in foreign countries. The report pointed out that the United States has set up complicated guidelines on automobile fuel efficiency which have become non-tariff import barriers on European deluxe cars. The body claimed that the United States has limited imports of dairy products to 2 percent of total U.S. production and tariffs are high at 10-20 percent although it puts pressure on foreign countries to open their farm markets. It also pinpointed non-tariff barriers in services, claiming that foreigners are strictly banned from engaging in TV and radio broadcasting business in the United States although U.S. operators have pressurized foreign governments to open their markets. In the public construction works, foreigners are obligated to use U.S. made construction equipment and material under the Buy American Act. The body pointed out that the Bill Clinton administration plans to increase subsidies to industries and agriculture despite Uruguay Round accord on reduction of subsidies.
