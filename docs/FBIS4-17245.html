BFN [Text] There were fierce clashes in the north of Nagorno-Karabakh's Mardakert district, to the east of the district center of Mardakert and near Fizuli where Azerbaijanis used several hundreds of troops and about fifteen tanks this afternoon. The Nagorno-Karabakh Army press office told Interfax that all Azerbaijani attacks had been repelled. Azerbaijanis are suffering huge casualties both in personnel and hardware on all sections of the frontline. According to preliminary reports, up to 70 Azerbaijani troops were killed and three tanks destroyed Thursday alone. A total of five Nagorno-Karabakh troops were killed, while another 14 wounded. At 1700 this afternoon fighting was going on only near Fizuli. In the meantime, the Azerbaijani Defense Ministry press center said heavy fighting had been going on near the villages of Gyullyudzha and Suma of the Agdam district since early morning. The ministry's experts believe Armenians intend to break the Azerbaijani defense on the border between the Agdam and Terter districts of Azerbaijan. According to the ministry, up to two battalions of troops were transferred from Armenia to the north of Nagorno-Karabakh every week. The situation in the south west of Azerbaijan, near Fizuli, also remains tense, the press office said. Over the last 24 hours, heavy fighting continued there. The press center said Azerbaijani units ousted Armenians from a number of strategically important heights and seized vast quantities of weapons and ammo, including a T-72 tank, an armored combat vehicle, two mobile launchers Grad, 11 artillery guns, five heavy-caliber machine-guns, seven man-pack radio sets, many rocket launchers, submachine-guns and an UAZ jeep.
