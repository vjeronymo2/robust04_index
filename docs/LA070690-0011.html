July 6, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

CLARIFICATION NEEDED ON AVOCADOS, CANCER

As president of the California Avocado Commission, which represents the nearly 7,000 growers in the state, I am writing to express our deep dissatisfaction and concern about your article that erroneously linked avocados with certain types of cancer ("Links Between Cancer, Dietary Fat Increase," June 26).

In your article, you state that there is "a strong connection between dietary fat -- found in such goodies as ice cream and avocados -- in particular types of cancers. . ." By singling out the avocado, you incorrectly inferred that eating avocados can potentially cause cancer.

Your misleading reference to avocados has alarmed consumers, many of whom have contacted our offices for clarification of your article.

Your unfortunate choice of the avocado as a potentially detrimental food will undoubtedly have a negative impact on consumers' perception of a fruit that has many nutritional benefits when included in a healthful, well-balanced diet. California avocados are cholesterol-free, low in sodium and an excellent source of vitamins and potassium.

We want to make it clear to your readers that avocados are healthful and nutritious.

MARK AFFLECK

president, California Avocado Commission
