BFN [Text] Shanghai, May 27 (XINHUA) -- The new Shanghai University, merging four previous universities in the city, was officially launched today. The four forming the new university are the Shanghai Polytechnical University, Shanghai University of Science and Technology, Shanghai Science and Technology School and the original Shanghai University. Chinese President Jiang Zemin personally calligraphed the new university's name-board. Qian Weichang, noted Chinese scientist and vice-chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, was made the first president of the university. Speaking at today's ceremony, Qian said that graduates of the new university should be patriots, people of high morality and noble hearts first of all, and then people of knowledge. The new university will combine schools of science, technology, literature, arts, law, business and management in one. It will have 7,700 teachers and support staff, and close to 17,000 students, the largest among higher education establishments in Shanghai, said university officials. The new university will have eight campuses totalling nearly 100 ha., and college buildings totalling 610,000 sq.m. in floor space, the officials added.
