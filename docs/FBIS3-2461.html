Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, March 6 (XINHUA) -- China is to send more personnel for training abroad to serve the country's reform and opening to the outside world. This was disclosed here recently at a meeting jointly sponsored by the State Commission for Restructuring the Economy and the State Bureau of Foreign Experts Affairs. Some 100 participants from six ministries and commissions of the State Council, China's highest governing body, took part in the meeting. Speaking at the meeting, State Councillor Li Guixian urged relevant departments to draw on the experiences of foreign countries and further promote the training work, so as to produce a bigger contingent of management and technological personnel, who can work for China's reforms and opening up.
