940716 FT 16 JUL 94 / International Company News: Computer havoc on Nasdaq system Computer problems created havoc for US traders and investors using the screen-based Nasdaq stock trading system yesterday, as the opening of the country's second largest stock market was delayed for two and a half hours while systems experts tried to find the fault, writes Patrick Harverson in New York Although the Nasdaq market, which is run by the National Association of Securities Dealers and handles up to 263m shares a day worth billions of dollars, finally opened just before noon, many parts of the system remained closed all day, costing millions of dollars in lost business.
