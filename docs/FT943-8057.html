940819 FT 19 AUG 94 / Arts: Today's Television It is a good day for sports fans to be trapped at home resting, say, a slightly sprained ankle. At 10.30 this morning BBC1 shows highlights of the opening ceremony of the Commonwealth Games. Then, at 10.50, BBC2 goes to the Oval for the second day's play in the Third Test between England and South Africa. England really need this one. Coverage stops for lunch, but resumes for five hours on BBC2 at 1.35, and of course Radio 4 long wave provides ball-by-ball commentary in Test Match Special. At 2.30 C4 begins two hours of Racing from Sandown Park, and at 9.00 this evening ITV offers an hour of Athletics from the Van Damme meeting in Brussels. BBC1 gives highlights of the cricket at 11.05 and then two hours of Commonwealth Games Grandstand starting at 11.35. Today's concert in BBC Proms 1994 (7.30 Radio 3 and BBC2) is in memory of the founder of the series, Henry Wood, and all the music has special associations with him. ITV's comedy pilot, Which Way To The War, written by David Croft and Jeremy Lloyd who created 'Allo Allo', is set in the Libyan desert during the second world war (8.30).
