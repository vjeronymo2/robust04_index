BFN [Text] Croatian President Franjo Tudjman yesterday sent a letter to Willy Claes, Belgian deputy prime minister and foreign minister. The message, conveyed by Janko Vranicani-Dobrinovic, Croatian ambassador to the Benelux countries, requests Belgium, as one of the members of the European Union's [EU] troika, to enlist EU support for Croatia's development, which would speed up the democratization process and help to gradually resolve the problem of Croatian territories under UN protection. Minister Claes and Ambassador Vranicani-Dobrinovic also discussed the implementation of the Washington agreements between the Croats and the Bosnian Muslims. The Croatian ambassador underlined Croatia's interest in concluding an agreement on trade and economic cooperation, protection of foreign investments, and double taxation. Vrnicani-Dobrinovic requested that Belgium ease or abolish visas for Croatian citizens and resolve the problem of workers employed by Croatian companies in Belgium.
