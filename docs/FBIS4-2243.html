BFN [By So Lai-fun in Beijing and Chan Wai-fong] [Excerpt] Chinese Vice-Premiers Zhu Rongji and Qian Qichen yesterday denied they had played a role in determining the outcome of the Xi Yang case. They were quoted by Vincent Lo Hong-sui, head of the Business and Professional Federation, as saying during meetings in Beijing that, as executive officials, they would not interfere with the Judiciary. Mr Zhu, also head of the People's Bank of China, denied reports that he had ordered a stiff sentence for Hong Kong reporter Xi, who was accused of stealing financial secrets. The Politburo Standing Committee did not discuss legal cases, he said. The comments came as Beijing rejected calls to release more details on the conviction of Xi who was sentenced to 12 years' jail.
