Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National" newscast] [Text] The new coffee price has been set from today. The agreement to set the domestic price was reached in the pre-dawn hours today following 15 hours of heated arguments. In the end the price [carga] went from 105,000 pesos to 120,000 pesos [unit not given]. Tired after fighting for so many hours, negotiators emerged at 0330 with the news. The price of coffee went from 105,000 pesos to 120,000 pesos, effective today. Of this amount, 103,000 pesos will be paid in cash and 17,000 in coffee savings bonds. It was a 14.28 percent readjustment. [Begin Finance Minister Rudolf Hommes recording] The government decided to give in and accepted this large increase -- more than a 30 percent increase since August 1993. In real terms, this is a gigantic increase since August. [end recording] Even though the government, led by President Gaviria, made an all-out effort, the coffee growers replied with resignation. [Begin Jorge Cardenas, manager of the National Federation of Coffee Growers, recording] This was the best price obtainable in the present circumstances. We sincerely believe this is a very good price when you take into account the current international market. [end recording] Everything seems to indicate that this will be the last adjustment this administration will make. In October, when a new increase is expected, the coffee growers will possibly have to speak on different terms.
