BFN [Text] Cairo, 17 May (MENA)--The repercussions of the war between north and south Yemen have been felt in its embassies and diplomatic missions abroad, especially after Yemeni Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah issued a decree dismissing a number of southern Yemeni diplomats who work in its embassies abroad. Among these are Mr. Muhammad Shahir Sayf, acting Yemeni representative to the Arab League. Mr. Shahir Sayf described the Yemeni foreign minister's request for the Arab League secretariat to withdraw Sayf's accreditation and for Sayf to return to Yemen as "unjust and illegitimate". He said that Yemeni Prime Minister Engineer Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas had instructed him to continue his duties in this position. He pointed out that the reason behind the dismissal from his duties is because he is a southerner. Muhammad Shahir Sayf said the Yemeni foreign minister's decision to remove him from the Yemeni representative office in Cairo was a unilateral decision prompted by divisionist notions. He noted that the Yemeni foreign minister should have waited for the war to end before making such a decision. Mr. Muhammad Shahir Sayf said that he does not take his orders from Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah, but from Yemeni Prime Minister Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas (a southerner), "whom I consider to be the legitimate prime minister despite Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih's decision to dismiss him", he said. He affirmed that he will stay in his position as acting permanent Yemeni representative to the Arab League. He pointed out that the representation office has already sent a letter regarding this matter to the Arab League Secretariat. MENA has learned that one of the reasons Muhammad Shahir Sayf was dismissed was that he ignored Yemeni Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah during the latter's recent visit to Cairo. Sayf also, without guidance from Sanaa, declined to head the latest emergency session of the Arab League Council. It is worth noting here that Muhammad Shahir Sayf assumed his current position when Yemen's previous permanent representative to the Arab League, Ahmad al-Shajbi, travelled to London for treatment, where he died a few days ago and was buried in Cairo.
