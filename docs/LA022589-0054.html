February 25, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST : LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF;

FIRE, SMOKE BLAMED ON POWER FAILURE

A power failure Friday morning at the Fletcher Oil refinery in Carson produced large flames and a billowing cloud of black smoke that was visible for miles.

Company Vice President Richard D. Erdman said the power outage knocked out controls on a refinery unit and excess gases were burned off through a flare, sending flames soaring 80 to 90 feet into the air. "It was rather spectacular because it went straight up," Erdman said.

The 10-minute outage was caused by a battery failure in "a very critical piece of equipment" designed to supply uninterrupted power, he said.

A South Coast Air Quality Management District spokesman said the refinery will not be cited for violating air pollution laws because the smoke was the result of "a legitimate breakdown."
