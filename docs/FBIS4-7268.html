BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 28 KYODO -- New party Sakigake and Group Seiun have agreed to form a joint parliamentary front, the two parties said Thursday. Sakigake leader Masayoshi Takemura, who was the chief cabinet secretary in the government of former Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, will head the new group. Sakigake controls 15 members in the 511-seat House of Representatives, while Group Seiun consists of three lower house members who quit Hosokawa's Japan New Party (JNP) in protest at his approach to the ruling coalition's core party Shinseito headed by new Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata. Political sources said more JNP members may leave the party and join the Sakigake-Seiun front.
