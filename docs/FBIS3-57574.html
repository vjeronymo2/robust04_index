Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Ivan Rybkin, chairman of the State Duma, today received U.S. Ambassador to Russia Thomas Pickering, at the latter's request. According to the Federal Assembly's press service, the lengthy conversation touched on a broad circle of questions including the organization of the work of the State Duma and the priorities for its legislative work. Rybkin noted that the plan for the work of the Duma has been coordinated with the activities of the president, the government, and other structures of power. The U.S. ambassador said that the United States was interested in the effective work of the Duma and was convinced of its necessity for Russia. In his opinion, its work will make it possible to resolve Russia's economic problems more quickly, including with the aid of the world community. Pickering also proposed, on instructions from the U.S. Congress, assistance in organizing information technologies for the Russian Federal Assembly, ITAR-TASS reports.
