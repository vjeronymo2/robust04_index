Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu, speaking at a Foreign Affairs-Unification Committee meeting today, said that the remark by Thomas Hubbard, U.S. deputy assistant secretary of State for East Asian Affairs, urging the ROK to abolish the National Security Law does not represent an authoritative view of the U.S. Government, but was merely a presentation of his personal opinion. Minister Han Sung-chu said that the government is planning to make an official inquiry to the U.S. State Department through diplomatic channels about the background and intent of Deputy Assistant Secretary Hubbard's remark. Minister Han said that although the U.S. side referred to the National Security Law in connection with the human rights issue in the ROK before the civilian government was launched, no such reference has been made since the start of the civilian government. Moreover, the U.S. State Department's human rights report notes drastic improvement in the ROK's human rights situation.
