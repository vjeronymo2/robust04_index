BFN [Text] Top U.S. business executives have offered to make big investments in India. The rich Indian-American community has also indicated plans to provide their expertise and money for giving a push to the market friendly reform process. At their separate meetings with the prime minister, now on an official tour of the United States, both American companies and the NRIs [nonresident Indians] appreciated the economic liberalization policy and said it has transformed India into a prospective market for greater inflow of funds. Mr. Narasimha Rao assured them that the Foreign Investment Promotion Board will look into any irritants in materializing their projects. Our correspondent Rama Rao covering the prime minister's visit reports that the NRI plans include investments in highway construction and communications, as also mobilization of at least $2 billion every year. Mr. Narasimha Rao told the NRIs that they can play their legitimate role in the industrialization of the country and their investments have to increase as they go along. Addressing a large section of the Indian-American community in New York, the prime minister said India means business and the future course is clear. His current visit is to project the giant leap the country is poised to take in industrial development and to give a personal undertaking over the irreversibility of the changed course. Mr. Rao asked the NRIs to participate in the present efforts based on their sound economic judgment. Commending the NRIs for the good work done in the country where they are living, he said they should keep it up. About Indo-U.S. ties, the prime minister said these need to be strong and friendly, not only for mutual benefit but for the future of mankind as a whole. The importance of his visit, he said, goes beyond the scope of normal bilateral relations. The prime minister has since arrived in Houston on the second leg of his American tour. Mr. Narasimha Rao is scheduled to meet the chief executives and presidents of major companies under the auspices of the Greater Houston Partnership. The partnership brings together over 2,000 companies based in the city. Later, the mayor of Houston will be presenting the keys of the city to Mr. Narasimha Rao. He will also attend a reception by the Indian community before leaving for Boston tomorrow.
