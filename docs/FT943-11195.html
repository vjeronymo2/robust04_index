940801 FT 01 AUG 94 / Business Travel: Egypt safer The UK government says the security situation has improved in Egypt, where two tourists have been killed and 30 wounded in attacks by Moslem militants since 1992. Mr Tony Baldry, assistant foreign secretary, said after a visit to Cairo, that the authorities now had the upper hand. However, visitors should still avoid Assiut in southern Egypt - the region where the militant Gama'a al-Islamiya (Islamic Group) had been most active. Yesterday the group said it had wounded two policemen near the southern town of Mallawi in a revenge attack for the killing of two Gama'a members by police.
