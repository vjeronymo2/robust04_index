921215 FT 15 DEC 92 / Parliament and Politics: Delay in discussion of Lamont legal costs MPs ON THE cross-party public accounts committee will have to wait until January before discussing the report into the Treasury decision that the taxpayer should pay some of Mr Norman Lamont's legal costs for the removal of an unwelcome tenant, Alison Smith writes. The NAO, the watchdog on government spending, has not been able to complete its inquiry in time for the PAC to consider any recommendations about changes to the rules before the Commons disperses for Christmas. Mr David Newton, the Thresher's off-licence store manager who admitted fabricating the story about Mr Lamont's purchases has left his job, the off-licence chain said last night.
