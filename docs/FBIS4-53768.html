BFN [Text] Jakarta, May 19 (ANEX-ANTARA) -- Research and Technology Minister B.J. Habibie said Thursday ASEAN amity could be significantly fostered by cooperation in technology and human resources development. He said ASEAN member countries had a political commitment to undertake the improvement of the quality of their human resources in ways compatible with "the ASEAN spirit." The minister was speaking at a ceremony marking the signing of a memorandum of understanding (MOU) on cooperation in various fields between Indonesia and Malaysia. Habibie signed the document for Indonesia, while Malaysian Defence Minister Datuk Sri Mohamed Najib bin Tun Abdul Razak did the same for Malaysia. Several other Indonesia-Malaysia accords were also inked at the ceremony. One was between the state-owned aircraft-making company IPTN and the Malaysian Defence Department and another between the Indonesian Air Force and Malaysia's Airod [company speciliazing in aircraft repairs and overhaul]. ASEAN unity or solidarity has lately become a topic of public discussion following the emergence of a difference of views between Jakarta and Manila about the scheduled holding of a seminar on East Timor in Manila. According to spokesman for the Indonesian Foreign Affairs Ministry Irawan Abidin, the Philippine Government could not meet Indonesia's demand that the meeting be called off but had promised it would try to have the topics to be discussed at the proposed seminar limited. Malaysia was among several countries which disagreed with the plan and said such a seminar was an effort to damage ASEAN friendship. In response to President Suharto's statement on the possibility of marketing Malaysia's Proton Saga cars at relatively low price in Indonesia, Habibie said that it was a breakthrough which was expected to bring this archipelagic country to the technology of the future. President Suharto had reportedly told Razak at a meeting Thursday the Malaysian cars can be sold in Indonesia, especially if the cars use components made in Indonesia.
