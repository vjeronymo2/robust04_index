Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 24 (YONHAP) -- The government has decided to strengthen public security and its anti-crime posture in preparation for any emergencies that might arise while President Kim Yong-sam is visiting Japan and China. The government held a public security-related ministers' meeting on Thursday and decided to tighten overall security so that the president can conclude his tour successfully without worrying about the domestic situation. Prime Minister Yi Hoe-chang presided over the meeting, attended by nine ministers including Home Minister Choe Hyong-u, Justice Minister Kim Tu-hui and Communications Minister Yun Tong-yun. The government will be on guard against possible terrorist attacks on public office buildings and foreign missions while taking steps to beef up national security. It will also strive to prevent large-scale calamities including forest fires and traffic accidents. Meanwhile, Prime Minister Yi will preside over a security-related ministers' meeting on Friday to check movements by the North Korean forces and discuss ways to ensure security during President Kim's absence.
