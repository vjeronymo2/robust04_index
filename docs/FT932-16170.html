930407 FT 07 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television War crimes are always perpetrated by the vanquished, never by the victors. This is such a truism that you can turn the formula round and say to anyone who still doubts that the Americans lost in Vietnam: of course they did, their war crimes, from Hanoi to My Lai, are world famous. Who ever heard of Vietcong war crimes? Thus the repeated attempts decades later, to turn the civilian raids by RAF Bomber Command into a 'war crime' are a waste of time since they were carried out by the winning side. Nevertheless Timewatch sets out to whip up another argument (7.45 BBC2). Anybody with long experience in almost any line of work will tell you that a good old 'un will out-perform several good young 'uns. Pop music? Of course: look at Mick Jagger and Tina Turner, combined age well over 100. Dispatches considers the absurdity of 'ageism' and points out the difficulties in everyone living longer yet being retired earlier (9.00 C4). QED reports on the piddock, a shellfish which glows in the dark and 'looks set to revolutionise the treatment of diabetes, arthritis and heart disease' (9.30 BBC1).
