Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Communique "to the domestic and international media" issued by the Clandestine Indian Revolutionary Committee-General Command of the Zapatist National Liberation Army (EZLN) and signed by EZLN Subcommander Marcos; "from somewhere in southeastern Mexico" on 24 March] [Text] Gentlemen: The Clandestine Indian Revolutionary Committee-General Command of the Zapatist National Liberation Army [EZLN] is honored to respectfully address you in order to outline its requirements for you to obtain credentials as war correspondents to enable you to enter those territories under EZLN control: 1. Our country's current situation has forced the EZLN to cancel its sweeping and completely open policy governing the media covering the conflict in Chiapas and, specifically, within those territories controlled by the EZLN. 2. As of 0000 [0600 GMT] on 25 March 1994, only those members of the news media accredited by the EZLN as war correspondents will have access to our territories. 3. To obtain credentials as war correspondents, applicants must present a letter bearing their signature and the signature of their respective news director. This letter must specifically state the following: A. That the particular news organization does in fact authorize the applicant to serve as one of its employees assigned to journalistic coverage of the conflict within our territories. B. There has been no payment, cash or otherwise, either by the EZLN to the news organization or by the organization to the EZLN for the work the reporter has carried out within our territories. C. The reporter's work within our territories will be carried out under the reporter's and his respective organization's own risk and responsibility. D. The reporter agrees to abide by the rules and regulations that may be applied within our territories and along our lines of combat. E. The respective news organization and the reporter agree to report the truth of all events at all times. 4. The EZLN reserves the right to approve or reject any request for accreditation filed by any news organization or reporter. 5. All applicants must present some sort of document with a recent photograph to prove that they are indeed employees of the specific news organization in question. Freedom, Justice, Democracy. [Signed] Respectfully yours, Subcommander Marcos [Issued] Somewhere in southeastern Mexico
