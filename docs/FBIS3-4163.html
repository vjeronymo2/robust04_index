Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, March 26 (XINHUA) -- The Shanghai-based China Eastern Airlines is to increase its flights starting from tomorrow on both domestic and international routes. The international routes include those from Shanghai to Osaka of Japan, Los Angeles of the United States, Bangkok of Thailand, Singapore and Seoul of the Republic of Korea, according to the airport's new summer and autumn timetable. Three to seven flights will be added to the Osaka route every week; two to three flights will be added to the route to Los Angeles, one to two flights, to Bangkok and Singapore routes, and three to four flights, to the routes to Fukuoka of Japan and Seoul every week. There will be more flights both on popular domestic tourist routes and on short-distance routes. Five to seven flights will be added to the route from Shanghai to Ningbo every week, five to six flights, to the route to Nanchang, and eight to 15, to the Shanghai-Qingdao route, and 14 to 17 flights, to the Shanghai-Guilin route.
