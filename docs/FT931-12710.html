930127 FT 27 JAN 93 / Court rules victims of 1984 Indian gas disaster cannot challenge settlement in America A US federal appeals court ruled yesterday that victims of the 1984 poison gas disaster in Bhopal, India, cannot challenge in America the Dollars 470m (Pounds 309.2m) settlement between the Indian government and Union Carbide, Reuter reports from New York. It upheld a ruling that the Indian government had the right to negotiate the settlement with the chemical company, based in Danbury, Connecticut, from whose plant the gas leaked and that the victims had no standing in the US.
