BFN [Text] In the Kashmir Valley, a near total bundh [strike] is being observed today as a mark of protest against the killing by militants of the religious scholar and mirwaiz [religious leader] of South Kashmir, Dr. Qazi Nissar Ahmad. Shops and business establishments are closed and traffic is off the roads. In Anantnag, thousands of people are visiting the idgah [place of worship] to pay their last homage to Dr. Qazi Nissar Ahmad. Dr. Ahmad's body was brought there this morning for burial in the afternoon. Spontaneous protest demonstrations by people against militants of the Hizbul Mojahedin and their mentors in Pakistan have been held at several places in the Kashmir valley since yesterday. About one lakh persons participated in Qazi Nissar's funeral procession from Dayalgam to Anantnag yesterday. In Srinagar, a building of school run by the Jamaat-i-Islami was burnt down by an irate mob yesterday. Reports say the militants of the Hizbul Mojahedin and workers of Jamaat-i-Islami have fled from their homes in several areas fearing the people's wrath. The police have identified one of the killers of Dr. Qazi Nissar Ahmad as a self-styled platoon commander of the militant outfit. The Indian Union Muslim League [IUML] has asked the union government to take urgent steps to apprehend the culprits behind Dr. Nissar Ahmad's killing. In a statement in Trivandrum, the IUML secretary, Mr. E. Ahmad, MP, deplored the killing.
