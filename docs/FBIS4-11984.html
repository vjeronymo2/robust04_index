BFN [Text] The president of the Republic, Lieutenant General 'Umar Hasan Ahmad al-Bashir, yesterday evening at the republican palace, met Mrs. Madeleine Albright, the United States permanent ambassador to the United Nations, who arrived in the country yesterday afternoon on a brief visit. In a statement issued after the meeting, the U.S. envoy stressed that her discussions with Lt. Gen. al-Bashir were serious, open, and clear, and that the dialogue was very important. For his part, Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih, who attended the meeting, expressed Sudan's satisfaction with the visit by the U.S. envoy, which is considered the real beginning of a serious dialogue between Sudan and America. He pointed out that dialogue was a course which had been followed, and had been called for, by the revolution from the outset. He said: We believe that we have entered preliminary negotiations, and that the revolution is working for peace. We call upon all sides to work for a just and lasting peace.
