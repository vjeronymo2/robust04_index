Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [From the "Hookup" program] [Text] The mainland's Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Straits [ARATS] yesterday [18 February] sent a message to the Straits Exchange Foundation [SEF] in which it expressed the hope that private visits to the mainland by Taiwan officials can be arranged through the cooperation of the two organizations. This is the first time that ARATS has ever made such a request. It also shows that the Chinese Communists' departments in charge of work toward Taiwan is trying to use this as a tool for further strengthening official contacts across the Straits. As the Chinese Communists do not recognize our country as a reciprocal political entity, it has been learned that they welcome Taiwan's high-ranking officials to visit the mainland as long as their visit is of a private nature. The Chinese Communist authorities have even openly indicated they welcome President Li Teng-hui to visit the mainland on a private trip. Moreover, after President Li Teng-hui paid an unofficial visit to the three Southeast Asian countries, the mainland side's suggestion, made through the ARATS, that intermediate organizations of the two sides make arrangements for officials of our side to visit the mainland on a private trip, affords much food for thought. It has been learned that the message faxed by the ARATS yesterday was in the form of the script of talks by a person in charge of the ARATS. The SEF has noted that the ARATS' intention is to place Taiwan's officials' visit to the mainland under its overall management. Regarding out side's reply, the SEF said it will replay to ARATS after consulting with the Mainland Affairs Council.
