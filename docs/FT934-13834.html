931020 FT 20 OCT 93 / Multinationals pay directors 6% DIRECTORS of UK subsidiaries of multinational enterprises enjoyed median pay increases of 6 per cent this year, according to the latest survey of managerial pay in Europe published yesterday by Monks Partnership, the British consultants. After adjustment for inflation the increase in UK was 3.3 per cent, compared with 3.1 per cent in France and 3.4 per cent in Germany. According to the survey, Britain's directors earn after tax just under Pounds 30,000 a year which puts them in 10th position in the European earnings table with only the Nordic countries below. But the UK tops the table on company cars. A total of 87 per cent of senior managers in companies with an annual turnover of more than Pounds 20m have a company car, compared with 78 per cent of German senior managers and 55 per cent in France. The average pay settlement in the engineering industry for the three months to September was 2.3 per cent, the Engineering Employers Federation's latest pay survey found. This compares with 2.49 per cent for the June to August period.
