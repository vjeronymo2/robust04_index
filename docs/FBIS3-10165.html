Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 1 March. The manufacturing industry suffered a 1.4-percent reduction in 1993 while the petrochemical sector suffered a 0.56-percent reduction. There was, however, a 10.6-percent decrease in this sector's imports and an increase in its exports, and this contributed to the sector having a more bearable negative trade balance. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 24 Feb 94 p E-10) The Federation of Industries on 24 February presented to President Rafael Caldera a plan for the creation of 20,000 new small and mid-size industrial plants that, according to spokesmen for the federation, would generate more than 1 million jobs. (Caracas Venezolana de Television Network in Spanish 2350 GMT 24 Feb 94) Energy and Mines Minister Erwin Jose Arrieta has said that the reduction in the production of crude oil does not guarantee that oil prices would increase. Arrieta said that at the OPEC meeting to take place soon in Geneva, he will insist that OPEC reassume its role as harmonizer of the oil policy of the various oil producing countries. Arrieta said he is optimistic about the negotiations Venezuela is holding with the British and U.S. Governments on refined gasoline. (Caracas Venezolana de Television Network in Spanish 2350 GMT 25 Feb 94) The latest report issued by the Central Bank of Venezuela reveals that the Venezuelan financial market continues to have liquidity problems. The report reveals that the Central Bank's credit assistance for the week that concluded on 11 February was 126.824 billion bolivares, 28.888 billion more than for the week that ended on 4 February. (Caracas REPORTE DE LA ECONOMIA in Spanish 25 Feb 94 p 8) Venezuela's trade deficit situation with regard to Mexico and Colombia remains unchanged, but for the first time in history, Venezuela's printing industry is exporting to these two Latin American countries, giants in this industrial sector. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 26 Feb 94 p E-6)
