BFN [Text] Macao, April 20 (XINHUA) -- Portuguese Prime Minister Anibal Cavaco Silva said that his recent visit to China was a great success. In a special interview with XINHUA before he left Macao, Cavaco Silva said while in Beijing he exchanged views with leading Chinese leaders on the current Sino-Portuguese political and economic relations, as well as matters relating to Macao, adding both sides were happy with good bilateral political relations and agreed to strengthen contact in economic and trade fields. The Portuguese prime minister also said on Macao issue, both sides held that it was necessary to maintain Macao's stability and economic development through dialogue between the governments of China and Portugal. During his visit in Beijing, Silva said, a loan agreement involving 200 million U.S. dollars was signed to support Portuguese exports and investment in China, and his entourage of over 60 Portuguese businessmen had wide contacts with Chinese enterpreneurs, noting that these have laid a foundation for the development of economic and trade relations between the two countries. Silva also praised the rapid economic development on China's mainland, saying it should be attributed to the reform and opening policies launched by the Chinese leaders and the enterprising spirit of the Chinese people. On Macao issue, Silva again stressed the importance of dialogue and cooperation between the two countries, adding it would guarantee the settlement of matters relating to the transition in Macao. He eventually said, the Portuguese Government, as will the Chinese side, would make all-out efforts to maintain the stability and progress in Macao.
