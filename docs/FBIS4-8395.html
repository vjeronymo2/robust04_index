BFN [Excerpts] Sarajevo, April 12 (AFP) -- Bosnian Serbs retaliating against NATO air strikes placed at least 40 UN observers under house arrest Tuesday, a UN official said, severing lines of communication with peacekeeping authorities. [passage omitted] Meanwhile the United Nations special representative in former Yugoslavia, Yasushi Akashi, was thwarted in a bid to re-establish contact with Bosnian Serb leaders at their Pale stronghold near the Bosnian capital. After a meeting earlier with Bosnian President Alija Izetbegovic, Akashi said he and the UN Protection Force's (UNPROFOR) overall commander, General Bertrand de Lapresle, were seeking an urgent meeting with Bosnian Serb leader Radovan Karadzic. But Akashi and De Lapresle left Sarajevo for Zagreb Tuesday afternoon after failing to reach Pale. [passage omitted]
