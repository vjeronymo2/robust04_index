BFN [Text] Prime Minister Tansu Ciller has declared that linking the U.S. military aid extended to Turkey to human rights, the antiterror struggle, and the Cyprus issue is extremely irrelevant. Ciller announced that following the U.S. Congressional decision on the subject, Turkey will reassess the aid issue and if necessary will reject it. Ciller, who is currently in Boston in the United States, conducted a telephone conversation with U.S. President Bill Clinton. Ciller reported that the conversation focused on various issues concerning the two countries. Ciller said that she told Clinton that the Turkish people will find it difficult to understand the Congressional decision to suspend 25 percent of the foreign aid earmarked for Turkey for 1995. The prime minister explained that during the telephone conversation she stressed that linking U.S. aid to human rights, the antiterror struggle, and the Cyprus problem is extremely irrelevant to the issue. Ciller said: I informed Mr. Clinton that I will reassess the aid issue and if necessary reject it. The President, in turn, related that he will undertake all the necessary initiatives and expressed the hope that he will obtain results on the matter. According to Ciller, Clinton said: You can tell the Turkish people that the U.S. Administration regards this issue with equal sensitivity. Ciller also expressed the hope that Clinton will undertake initiatives on the subject of aid.
