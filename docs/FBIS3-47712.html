Language: Indonesian Article Type:BFN [Text] Indonesia and Egypt have signed an agreement on avoiding double taxation to protect investors and boost bilateral trade and investment. Speaking at the third session of the Indonesia-Egypt Commission in Jakarta today, Yusuf Butrus-Ghali, Egyptian minister of state for international cooperation, said the agreement constitutes a normal part of international bilateral relations. In addition to the accord on avoiding double taxation, the two countries also reached agreements on investment protection, tourism promotion, and educational and cultural cooperation.
