920805 FT 05 AUG 92 / Former NZ premier dies SIR Robert Muldoon, former prime minister of New Zealand, died early today at the age of 70, Reuter reports from Wellington. Mr Muldoon, a stocky man renowned for an abrasive manner and acerbic tongue, was prime minister from 1975 to 1984, when he miscalculated by calling a snap election. His time in office was marked by interventionist economics. By contrast, the Labour government that swept him aside embarked on free market reforms and state asset sales.
