Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Abidjan, Jan 1 (AFP) -- Ivory Coast President Henri Konan Bedie, in his first speech Friday [31 December] since succeeding the late Felix Houphouet-Boigny, denounced the neo-colonialism gripping the West African nation's economy. "It is not right that our industry should find itself back in the doldrums, that a large part of our wholesale and retail industry has fallen into the hands of foreigners and that our farm exports are still subject to a trading system that bears an irritating resemblance to the aberrations of the colonial era," he said in a year end address to the nation. Konan Bedie, who succeeded to the presidency after Houphouet-Boigny's death on December 7, said the country needed quick solutions that could put the country back on the rails. He added that the country was on the road to recovery when it came to balancing the budget and trade deficit.
