940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / Berlusconi adverts dropped The Italian state broadcaster, the Rai, said yesterday it was suspending a television advertising campaign designed to boost the image of Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi's government. The Rai decided to suspend the government advertisements, launched only 48 hours earlier, after Italy's media ombudsman intervened. The decision to suspend the advertisements is another slap in the face for a government which last month suffered a series of embarrassments, including the withdrawal of a decree limiting magistrates' use of pre-trial custody. The first of the 45-second advertisements, accompanied by the slogan 'Facts which citizens should know to exercise their rights', was broadcast on Saturday, and outlined the achievements of the government since its appointment in May. Italian law allows the government to use the media to publish information of 'social relevance', but opposition parties said the campaign was pure propaganda. Mr Berlusconi's Fininvest media-to-supermarkets company kept a L3bn (Pounds 1.2m) fund to make secret payments, according to the prime minister's younger brother Paolo, quoted yesterday by the Ansa and Agi news agencies. Mr Paolo Berlusconi, arrested two weeks ago on suspicion of organising Fininvest bribes to the finance police, is reported to have told anti-corruption investigators: 'We were able to create a fund of several billion lire, about three billion, which we were able to use to make those payments that could certainly not be put on the balance sheet.' The fund operated between 1983 and 1990, he was reported to have said.
