BFN [Text] North Korea rejects pressure as a tool to solve the nuclear problem of the peninsula. Its Foreign Minister Kim Yong-nam said so in Ukraine where he arrived on 6 June. He said that direct talks with Seoul were the only way to have the problem solved and that North Korea wanted to make the Korean peninsula a nuclear-free zone. Kim Yong-nam expressed the hope that the third round of talks between Pyongyang and Seoul would bring about positive results in that field. He welcomed Ukraine's desire to become a nonnuclear country. Kim Yong-nam and his Ukrainian counterpart Anatoliy Zlenko will have talks Monday afternoon.
