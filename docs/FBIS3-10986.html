Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Melchor de Mencos, Peten -- As Belizean Prime Minister Manuel Esquivel broadens his anti-Guatemala campaign, Army troops of that former colony are patrolling the border area and warning Peten families not to enter their territory. More than 3,000 Belizean soldiers are maintaining permanent surveillance along the alleged [supuesta] borderline. In fact, they built a wide highway that allows them to move easily from Cayo San Ignacio to Corozal, which is near the area that borders Peten. It was reported that soldiers from the former colony frequently enter Peten territory and that they even harass peasants who have lived and worked there for many years. Prime Minister Manuel Esquivel has started an anti-Guatemalan campaign and ordered the expulsion of numerous families with years of residence in the territories under dispute although many of them already filed requests before the immigration authorities to reside legally in Belize. In order to win support within the Belizean community, Esquivel maintains a hostile attitude toward Guatemalans and points out that there could be an invasion anytime. In a recent meeting with Queen of England Elizabeth II, Esquivel advised the sovereign on the need to maintain English troops in the former colony, but his efforts were unsuccessful. The monarch, however, assured him that the British will use the Belizean jungle as a training ground for some soldiers. Peten authorities in Melchor de Mencos said that Guatemala maintains minimal surveillance along the border and this could facilitate new incursions by Belizean soldiers. They added that more than 3,000 Guatemalans have been expelled since Esquivel became Belizean president, although his grandparents hail from San Miguel, a town across from the Island of Flores. Esquivel also admitted that he has relatives residing in Santa Elena (a Peten community) but has never met them. The authorities noted that his hostile attitude toward Guatemalans is inexplicable.
