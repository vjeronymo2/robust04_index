May 31, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

BELMONT FIELD CONTINUES TO SHRINK;

TRIPLE CROWN: DOTSERO, WINNER OF THE ILLINOIS DERBY, WON'T MAKE IT TO THE STARTING GATE. MISTER FRISKY AND REAL CASH ALSO ARE OUT.

The Belmont Stakes is more than a week away, and the Triple Crown finale's already threadbare ranks are beginning to unravel.

Illinois Derby winner Dotsero, a questionable commodity even under ideal circumstances, is the latest defection.

He worked at Arlington Park on Wednesday and was to have been flown to Belmont if the exercise was satisfactory. It was, but trainer Tim Muckler and the colt's owners elected afterward to pass up the Belmont trip. Muckler said running Dotsero in the 1 1/2-mile Belmont without a prep "might be a little anxious."

Meanwhile, the remarkably disappointing Mister Frisky, who has taken ill, will not have the opportunity to tire badly in all three Triple Crown cities after having won 16 consecutive races in Puerto Rico and California. The Puerto Rican champion has been ill since arriving at Belmont Park after the Preakness, assistant trainer Albert Barrera said Wednesday, and has not set a hoof on a race track since May 19, when he was a distant third at Pimlico. Mister Frisky will be returned to California as soon as he is well enough to travel.

The D. Wayne Lukas Stable has withdrawn Real Cash, unplaced in the Kentucky Derby and third in the Jersey Derby, from consideration for the Belmont, but confirmed that Land Rush, unplaced in both the Derby and Preakness, will keep the date.

A decision on the Belmont status of Gotham and Wood Memorial winner Thirty Six Red, who has not started since finishing ninth in the Kentucky Derby, will be made by trainer Nick Zito and owner B. Giles Brophy after a scheduled workout today.

"If he works the way we want him to," Zito said, "we'll proceed to the Belmont. I think he's back to 100%. I have no doubt that if he's the way I think he is, we'll be in good shape."

Kentucky Derby winner and Preakness runner-up Unbridled, who is assured of a $1-million bonus simply by starting and finishing the Belmont, emerged without incident from a six-furlong move in 1:14 1/5 over a wet track Tuesday at Belmont.

The first two finishers in the Jersey Derby as well as the third finisher in the Peter Pan Stakes are also on course for the Belmont. Yonder, the Jersey Derby winner, has the Belmont penciled in for June 9, according to trainer Angel Penna. Video Ranger, fourth in the Kentucky Derby before losing a narrow decision to Yonder at Garden State, is at Belmont and will gallop up to the race.

Country Day's trainer, John Parisella, confirmed his Belmont status. "I can't wait," he said.
