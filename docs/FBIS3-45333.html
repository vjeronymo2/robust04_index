Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 14 (XINHUA) -- Premier Li Peng and visiting former U.S. President George Bush had a 40-minute discussion on Sino-U.S. Relations and the international situation this evening. Bush last visited China in February 1989. Li welcomed Bush for his current tour in China and expressed appreciation for his efforts to uphold the framework of Sino-U.S. relations and his correct decision to renew China`s most-favored- nation trading status. Li hoped that Sino-U.S. relations will continue to improve and develop on the basis of mutual respect and seeking common ground while reserving differences. Briefing Bush on China's reform and opening drive, Li said that the Chinese people, guided by Comrade Deng Xiaoping's theory of building socialism with Chinese characteristics, have chosen a way to development fitting China's reality. He emphasized that the 15-year-old reform and opening drive shows that so long as China sticks to its course, it will enjoy economic development and political stability. "A stable, developing and prosperous China is a big contribution to world peace and development," Li said. Bush told Li, that during his current revisit in Beijing after five years he was greatly impressed by the great changes he witnessed in China, and China's economic success. He will continue to work to further U.S.-China relations, he added. After the meeting, Li Peng hosted a dinner in honor of Bush. Among those taking part in the meeting and dinner were Qi Huaiyuan, director of the Office of Foreign Affairs of the State Council, Liu Huaqiu, vice-foreign minister, and General Brent Scowcroft, former assistant to the President for national security affairs. Luo Gan, State Councillor and secretary-general of the State Council, met with Bush earlier today.
