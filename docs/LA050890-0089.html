May 8, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

T-Bill Rates Fall: The Treasury sold $8.4 billion of news three-month bills at an average discount rate of 7.79%, down from 7.91% last week.

Another $8.4 billion of new six-month bills was sold at an average discount rate of 7.84%, down from 8.03% last week. The new discount rates understate the actual return to investors -- 8.06% for three-month bills, with a $10,000 bill selling for $9,803.10, and 8.29% for a six-month bill selling for $9,603.60. The discount rate reflects the price discount received when government securities are purchased at less than face value.
