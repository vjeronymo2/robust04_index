Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Lima, 12 Feb (EFE) -- Today, the Peruvian Government extended the state of emergency for 60 days in three departments -- Huanuco and San Martin Departments in the jungle area and Pasco Department in the Andean area -- and in 13 provinces in four other departments. According to the provisions for the establishment of a state of emergency issued by the Ministry of Defense and published in EL PERUANO, the official gazette, terrorist and narcoterrorist activities continue. Under a state of emergency, constitutional guarantees are suspended and the Armed Forces exercise full control of public order. The measure includes Huanuco Department, in the jungle area, except for Puerto Inca Province and Huacrachuco; all of San Martin Department, in the jungle area where there is heavy narcoterrorist activity; and Yurimaguas, in Loreto Department, also in the jungle area. In the central mountain range of the country, the state of emergency covers all of Pasco Department, as well as the provinces of Melgar and Azangaro of Puno Department, bordering with Bolivia. It also covers the provinces of Huancayo, Concepcion, Jauja, Satipo, and Chanchamayo, in Junin Department, in the central mountain range of Peru; Castrovirreyna, Huaytara, and Huancavelica, in Huancavelica Department in the southern Andean area; and Lucanas, Huamanga, and Cangallo, in Ayacucho Department, in the southern Andean area.
