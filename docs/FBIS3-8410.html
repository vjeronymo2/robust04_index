Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 7/3/1994 -- The president of the Romanian Senate, Oliviu Gherman, received Monday, 7 March, a delegation of the Alliance of the Free Democrats of Hungary [SZDSZ], led by Gabor Kuncze, leader of a parliamentary faction and its candidate for premiership. The sides talked about the necessity to strengthen the bilateral parliamentary ties as a basic element for the improvement of the Romanian-Hungarian relations. Oliviu Gherman termed the inexistence of interparliamentary relations of the ruling party and the opposition forces in Romania with their counterparts in Hungary as "illogical and contrary to bilateral interests" and said that as long as UDMR (Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania -- an ethnic party with representation to Romania's Parliament -- e.n.) is the sole parliamentary representative of Romania to the Hungarian Parliament, the progress in the bilateral parliamentary relations will be "insignificant". Gabor Kuncze remarked that, given the short time lying before the present Hungarian legislative, the bilateral parliamentary relations between the two countries could be intensified after the new legislative assumes duties. Both sides agreed that all possibilities should be used to improve Romanian-Hungarian relations, and that keeping up false tensions is to the interest of neither party. The president of the Romanian Senate gave assurances to the Hungarian guests that, before its term is over, the Romanian legislative body will ratify the Romanian-Hungarian agreement for the mutual protection of investments.
