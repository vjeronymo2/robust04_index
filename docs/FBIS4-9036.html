BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 19/4/1994 -- A government delegation from the Netherlands headed by Prime Minister Ruud Lubbers arrived in Bucharest on Tuesday, April 19, for a two-day official visit. The Dutch guests were greeted at the Otopeni Airport by Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu, Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu, Minister of State Dan Mircea Popescu, the Dutch ambassador in Bucharest, Monique Frank, and other officials. Shortly after his arrival, Premier Ruud Lubbers said that his visit in Romania would further substantiate the cooperation between the two countries. The Netherlands is located on the other side of Europe and considers that transports and trade are especially important, said the Dutch prime minister and added that the Rhine- Main-Danube watercourse would ease the bilateral ties, as Romania is located on the other end of the road and therefore the bilateral cooperation was extremely necessary, not only in the economic field but also in the political one and perhaps also in tourism. The Dutch premier mentioned the Latin-rooted language spoken by Romanians as a very special trait of Romania in central Europe. On Wednesday [20 April], Premier Ruud Lubbers is scheduled to meet Romanian Senate President Oliviu Gherman, Chamber of Deputies' Speaker Adrian Nastase, the presidential adviser Misu Negritoiu, and the governor of the National Bank of Romania, Mugur Isarescu. Today Ruud Lubbers was received by Romanian President Ion Iliescu, at Cotroceni Palace. The Dutch guest was accompanied by Dutch Foreign Affairs Minister Peter Kooijmans. The visit of the high Dutch officials is the first of its kind since Romania's December 1989 revolution. The interview was attended by Romanian Minister of Foreign Affairs Teodor Melescanu, Minister of State Dan Mircea Popescu, presidential advisers Misu Negritoiu and Traian Chebeleu, the Romanian ambassador to the Netherlands, Marin Buhoara. After the official talks, the Romanian president gave his guests lunch.
