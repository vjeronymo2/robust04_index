Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The official visit of the Qatar foreign minister to our country was concluded with the signing of an agreement setting up a higher Tunisian-Qatari joint committee. The joint statement renewed the resolve of the two countries to make more efforts to develop bilateral cooperation in all fields. Details are as follows: At the Foreign Ministry headquarters today an agreement was signed concerning the setting up of a higher Tunisian-Qatari joint committee for developing the relations of cooperation, headed by the foreign ministers of the two countries. The agreement was signed by Foreign Minister Habib Ben Yahyah and his Qatari colleague Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Bin-Jabbur Al Thani At the end of this ceremony, Mr. Habib Ben Yahyah delivered a speech in which he affirmed the strength of the fraternal ties that link Tunisia with Qatar. He praised the results of the talks, which are bound to strengthen the bilateral relations and ensure more coordination for the good of the Arab and Islamic nation. For his part, the Qatari minister, evaluating the results of his visit, stressed that what has been achieved is very positive. All questions of mutual interest were raised in an atmosphere of clarity and frankness. He expressed the belief that the two countries can reach a joint understanding concerning future cooperation.
