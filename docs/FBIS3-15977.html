Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] "A cowardly provocation" was staged against the Liberal-Democratic Party of Russia (LDPR) leader Vladimir Zhirinovskiy in Ljubljana, said a statement made public by the party's press service Thursday. According to the statement, Zhirinovskiy arrived in the Slovenian capital on March 6 "at an invitation of the local business community." In the airport "armed men who introduced themselves as officers of the Interior Ministry and security service but did not show any identity cards" told Zhirinovskiy that they were acting on the orders of the Slovenian Government, did not allow him to leave the airport, kept him there for 24 hours and sent him back to Moscow. The press service described the incident as "a cowardly provocation aimed at subverting the friendship of the two fraternal peoples."
