BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 28 (XINHUA) -- Kim Il-song, president of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK), held talks on Tuesday [28 June] with a Chinese military delegation which is on a nine-day visit here. President Kim briefed the Chinese delegation on the situation in the Korean peninsula and the latest developments in preparations for his summit meeting with the South Korean president on the nuclear issue, and the upcoming talks with the U.S. on July 8 in Geneva. The situation in the Korean peninsula has been eased and is moving in a positive direction, said Kim. The Chinese delegation, headed by Wang Ke, commander-in-chief of the Shenyang military area command of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, arrived in the capital on June 21 and will leave for home on Wednesday.
