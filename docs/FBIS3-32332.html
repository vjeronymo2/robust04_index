Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Good Morning From Mexico" newscast] [Text] Social Development Secretary Carlos Rojas Gutierrez and Chiapas State Governor Javier Lopez Moreno have signed the 1994 social development agreement. The document establishes that 750 million new pesos will be assigned to the development of social projects in that state. During the ceremony, the social development secretary said that the public projects and investments that have been carried out are not enough because the situation in the state is the product of centuries of backwardness. This is why it is necessary to respect the decisions of the community and to listen to and heed their proposals and their truth, he said. Javier Lopez Moreno said that efforts to integrate a political coalition government will continue because this is what the current situation demands. At the end of the ceremony, the officials went to Comitan, Chiapas, where they inaugurated one of the 16 health centers that will function in the state for peasants and Indians.
