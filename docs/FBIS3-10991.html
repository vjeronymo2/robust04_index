Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Juan Pritsiolas] [Text] Some 45 percent of Panamanians believe that [Democratic Revolutionary Party, PRD, presidential candidate] Ernesto Perez Balladares would favor creating a new military force, but also believe him to be the most reliable presidential candidate. These results were obtained in a nationwide poll conducted by the CID/GALLUP firm for EL PANAMA AMERICA during the week of 26 February-3 March. Some 23 percent of 1,218 citizens polled did not answer the question on the creation of a military force. Some 19 percent of those polled said none of the seven candidates intend to create a military force. For 6 percent of those polled, Ruben Dario Carles intends to create a military force; while Mireya Moscoso de Gruber and Ruben Blades got 3 percent. The other presidential candidates registered 1 percent. Regarding the most preferred candidate, three out of every 10 Panamanians said they prefer Perez Balladares. Carles obtained 22 percent, Mireya Moscoso 11 percent, Blades 10 percent, while Eduardo Vallarino, Samuel Lewis Galindo, and Jose Salvador Munoz garnered 4 percent each.
