Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, March 2 (A.A) -- Russian Ambassador to Ankara Albert Chernyshev said on Tuesday [2 March] that a common market could be established in the region. Chernyshev, who was in Ankara for six and a half years as ambassador, hosted a farewell reception before his departure to his country, where he was appointed as deputy foreign minister. After a one-hour meeting with President Suleyman Demirel, Chernyshev told journalists that he and Demirel exchanged views on almost every subject. "Strong friendly relations are necessary in the region to overcome problems," he said. Chernyshev, who expressed his emotions of leaving Turkey, as "my heart will stay here," will be responsible of an area covering the Black Sea, the Caucasus, Central Asia, Turkey, Iraq, Iran, Afghanistan, Pakistan, India and the Maldives. He said that no other country was welcoming either Turkey and Russia to sell their goods and "here is our great market, we have this opportunity and we should work in this direction." "We are obliged to cooperate. Peace and stability should exist here," he concluded.
