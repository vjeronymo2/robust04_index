930622 FT 22 JUN 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: Polish copper combine lifts output POLAND'S KGHM state-owned copper producer has reported a Zl 1,415bn (USDollars 81m) net profit for 1992 as output in the first five months of this year is running 3.4 per cent higher than in the same period last year. The combine's final results yesterday put KGHM's sales in 1992 at Zl 17,361bn while costs reached Zl 13,639bn. Last year the combine, sited in western Poland, produced 387,000 tonnes of copper and 914 tonnes of silver. The first five months of this year have seen copper output reach 171,000 tonnes, 3.4 per cent more than last year, when it rose by 2.3 per cent despite a six week strike. In 1991, KGHM, where the trade unions are resisting an attempt to sell a minority stake to foreign investors, reported a Zl 900bn net profit, equivalent USDollars 82m at the present exchange rates.
