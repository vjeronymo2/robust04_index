Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Argentine Defense Minister Oscar Camilion yesterday proposed to Embraer [Brazilian Aeronautics Company] Superintendent Ozires Silva a cooperation agreement should one of the two countries be awarded the U.S. Government bid to supply 800 aircraft for military training purposes. The contract is worth $4.5 billion. Camilion visited the Embraer premises yesterday. FOLHA was unable to contact Ozires yesterday. Embraer Social Communications Manager Mario Galvao was unaware of Embraer's decision regarding the offer. Embraer is participating in the bid with the Super Tucano turbo-prop aircraft. The Argentine FMA [Military Aircraft Factory] will participate in the same bid with the IA-63 Pampa jet. The results of the bid will be disclosed at the end of the year.
