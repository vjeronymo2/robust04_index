BFN [Text] President Glavkos Kliridhis returned tonight from Corfu, through Athens, where he had contacts on the sidelines of the European Union [EU] summit. President Kliridhis held a meeting with Andreas Papandreou, European Council president and Greek prime minister; Jacques Delors, European Commission president; and other leaders with whom he discussed developments in the Cyprus issue and the issue of Cyprus' accession to the EU. In Athens, President Kliridhis also discussed the joint Cyprus-Greece defense doctrine with Greek National Defense Minister Yerasimos Arsenis and the results of Mr. Arsenis' meetings in Washington. President Kliridhis and Yerasimos Arsenis held a private meeting, after which they did not make any statements. According to a Greek National Defense Ministry statement, they examined the progress of promoting the doctrine and discussed the current phase of the Cyprus issue in light of the latest developments concerning the UN initiatives in the confidence-building measures and the efforts to disengage the process from the Turkish side's intransigence.
