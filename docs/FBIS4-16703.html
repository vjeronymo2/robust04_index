BFN [Text] Russia's joint stock company Gazprom cut gas supply to Moldova by one third because of that republic's debt totaling 131 billion rubles by April 1, 1994. This was reported to Interfax by a well-placed Gazprom source. The company's current gas supply to Moldova amounts to 5.7 million cubic meters a day. This year Moldova has not paid a single ruble to Gazprom, compensating for Russian gas partly in food supplies. A Russo-Moldovan agreement signed at the end of 1993 commits Moldova to supplying to Gazprom $145 million worth in food stuffs and consumer goods in partial payment for the delivery of up to 3.5 billion cubic meters of Russian gas. Moldova now pays $80 for 1000 cubic meters of gas. To transport Russian gas across Moldova into Romania costs $1.5 per 1000 cubic meters of gas over 100 kilometers of pipeline. Meanwhile, the talks between a Belarusian Government delegation and Gazprom officials, scheduled for mid-April, have been put off indefinitely because of the signing of a monetary integration accord. During the negotiations the sides were to look over the fulfillment of a Russo-Belarusian protocol on paying off the debt for Russian gas. This accord was signed on March 15, 1994.
