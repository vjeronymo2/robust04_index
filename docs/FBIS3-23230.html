Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Muhammad 'Allam and Ibqal 'Abdallah report: "Aden Handed Over Group of 'Afghans' to Cairo"] [Excerpts] Cairo, Aden -- [Passage omitted] In Aden authoritative political sources said yesterday that the security services belonging to the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP] had handed over "terrorist" elements among the "Afghan Egyptians" [Egyptians who fought alongside Afghan mojahedin against the Soviets] arrested at the end of last month in Mudiyah, Abyan Governorate, with other groups of Arabs returning from Afghanistan to Yemen. The sources said that "these Egyptians were handed over to the authorities in their country after proving their affiliation to the terrorist Jihad organization." The sources, who asked to remain anonymous, pointed out that Brigadier General Muhammad Haydarah Masdus, deputy prime minister and member of the YSP Political Bureau, "handed over the Egyptians to security authorities in Cairo last week at Egypt's request after Cairo had heard of their arrest in Yemen." [passage omitted] Security sources said yesterday that the "authorities in southern governorates intend to complete the investigations into the Afghan Arabs and then hand them over to the authorities in their countries under the Arab security agreement stipulating that terrorists be handed over to the states concerned with their trial." As is known, the Arab Afghans held by the security authorities in Aden include Iraqis, Algerians, Sudanese, in addition to the Egyptians who were handed over to Egyptian authorities last week.
