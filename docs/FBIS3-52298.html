Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Through its spokesman in Andalusia, Tahar Sidi, the Polisario [Popular Front for the Liberation of Saquia El Hamra and Rio de Oro] Front has repeated its threat to the participants in the Paris-Dakar-Paris rally when they enter the Western Sahara. Tomorrow, the rally arrives in Spain and the stage ends in Granada. In the opinion of the Polisario Front, not even the Moroccan Army will be able to eliminate the danger posed by the mines on the Sahara's frontier with Mauritania. [Madrid RNE-1 Radio Network in Spanish at 2316 GMT on 28 December added the following report: "For almost a month the Polisario Front has been warning of the danger of the Paris-Dakar-Paris rally passing through the Western Sahara. While initially the organizers of the rally turned a deaf ear to the warnings, and it was even said that there was no kind of threat, stark reality has drawn their attention to what is no longer either a threat or a warning, but rather a serious and firm declaration that cannot be shrugged off, a declaration such as that made in Granada by Tahar Sidi, spokesman for the Polisario Front in Andalusia: [Begin Sidi recording] The Polisario Front's stance is clear. In our opinion, it is a violation of the cease-fire. In our opinion, it is a territory of the utmost risk and no one can guarantee the security of the caravan of motorbikes, cars, trucks, and hundreds and hundreds of people." [end recording]
