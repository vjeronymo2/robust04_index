940218 FT 18 FEB 94 / Call for extra audit checks Auditors should ensure that figures in a company's preliminary statement are consistent with the numbers they are verifying in audits, and information expected to appear in the next set of accounts, according to new guidance from the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales. Significant information of which the auditors are aware, such as any changes in accounting policy, should be disclosed, it advises.
