BFN [Excerpt] Independent sources have said that more than 100 people have been killed and 200 others wounded in the clashes that took place in 'Imran, north of Sanaa, on Wednesday and Thursday [27 and 28 April] between the northern and southern armored units. So far, no official count of casualties has been announced. Initial reports issued by the Defense Ministry in Sanaa noted that dozens of people, including civilians, were killed. The latest reports from Sanaa noted that artillery clashes and tank battles continued yesterday following the failure of the cease-fire reached Wednesday evening. Independent sources said that the southern forces were defeated in the battles, and consequently, the fighting abated yesterday afternoon. These confrontations created severe tension in Sanaa. The anti-aircraft guns that provide protection for the airport and the Presidential Palace have been placed on alert. It was also reported that a boobytrapped car exploded near the residence of Shaykh 'Abdallah al-Ahmar, the speaker of the House of Representatives and leader of the Yemeni Reform Grouping, but no casualties were reported.
