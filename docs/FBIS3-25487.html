Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bophuthatswana Government and ANC [African National Congress] negotiating delegations may meet again this weekend, following a positive round of exploratory talks in Bophuthatswana today. The head of the Bophuthatswana delegation, Mr. Ephraim Keikelane, said after a meeting in the Pilanesberg Game Reserve that they had suggested that Bophuthatswana should have a confederal relationship with South Africa. Mr. Popo Molefe of the ANC said they have spelled out the ANC's latest constitutional proposals in detail. He said both sides would report back to their principals, and a second round of talks was imminent, possibly before the negotiating council met on Monday [21 February].
