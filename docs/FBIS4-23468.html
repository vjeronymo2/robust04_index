BFN [Text] The National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] has intensified its shelling of Cuito over the past 24 hours. Cuito no longer receives humanitarian assistance, and the food situation has worsened. Our correspondent Abel Abraao has further details. [Begin Abraao recording] Wounded and hungry people in Cuito have been begging for humanitarian assistance. The political and military situation has not experienced major changes here in Cuito over the past hours. UNITA rebels continue to shell the city, after experiencing heavy defeats in direct clashes with government forces. Despite heavy defeats, Jonas Savimbi's men are still determined to overrun Cuito. Over the past 12 hours, more than 2,000 shells of various calibers were fired on the city, interspersed with infantry attacks. Meanwhile, more than 500,000 wounded and hungry people are begging for humanitarian assistance from international organizations. People are already dying of hunger in large numbers. International humanitarian organizations have not been flying to Cuito for the past 10 days because of military instability in this region. Since the war began on 26 May, more than 400 civilians have died, most of them old people, women, and children. [end recording]
