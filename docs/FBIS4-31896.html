BFN [From the 15 June Romanian press review] [Excerpts] Bucharest ROMPRES, 15/6/1994 -- The need for a balance in the Romanian society, marked these days by extensive union protests and the celebration of four years since the miners' descent to Bucharest on 14 June are the major themes covered by the leading press put out on Wednesday, 15 June. The protest launched at the beginning of this week in Bucharest by three major trade union blocs, even if ruled by a mostly trade-unionist and commemorative character, have turned into a protest against the current leadership. Amid street protests, the unilateral initiative of the opposition National Peasant Christian-Democratic Party [PNTCD] to launch procedures to suspend Romania's President Ion Iliescu from his office is one more element to fuel tension in the political life, according to the press put out today. Daily CURIERUL NATIONAL considers that the PNTCD's initiative is not only "a political lesson," but also and especially "a lesson of inflexibility and radicalism." The daily remarks that the history of the past four years shows, among other things, that radicalism and inflexibility "have had no political impact on the electorate, but rather on the contrary." Examining the potential consequences of the PNTCD's initiative, the cited daily estimates that by this "a definite end was put to the opposition--leadership dialogue," as it "encouraged the conservatory group within the ruling party of Social Democracy Party of Romania [PDSR] to assert that the opposition is an enemy to fight with" and opened prospects for the ruling party to consolidate ties with its current allies. Moreover, the daily considers that all these would have as potential consequence a speedy access of the National Romanian Unity Party [PUNR] to ruling, for under the newly created circumstances, this party "seems an ideal partner in governing." Daily DIMINEATA writes that "the initiative launched by PNTCD leader Corneliu Coposu is in fact his own epitaph." The daily notes that it is not a serious fact that the PNTCD leader imagined that he could find two thirds of the parliamentarians "to embrace his aberration," and that "by this he sets himself in the service of our country's enemies, who are now seeing themselves with their lands and enterprises returned as their property rights." Several dailies publish and comment on the communique issued by Romania's presidency in which the accusations brought to Romania's number one [President Ion Iliescu] are viewed as "ungrounded." [passage omitted] Government daily VOCEA ROMANIEI publishes views of several parliamentarians on the initiative launched by the PNTCD leader, which they view as "a hit below the belt on democracy," an "at least hazardous action," or "a bluff." The dailies also inform that at yesterday's meeting of the opposition Democratic Convention of Romania [CDR] leaders and leaders of the Democratic Party [PD] a conclusion was reached to stop the procedure for President Iliescu's suspension from office and to invite the president to face Parliament and clarify the disputed aspects. This step is viewed by the editorialist of daily CRONICA ROMANA as "wise" or as "the most logical and democratic alternative under the given circumstances." On the other hand, the opposition is furthering its radical efforts on the much disputed issue of the draft bill on nationalized houses, daily ADEVARUL informing its readers that it is possible for the opposition to totally withdraw from Parliament, according to an announcement made yesterday by Gyorgy Frunda, senator of the Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania [UDMR]. Under the headline "Union Protests at European Standards," government's daily VOCEA ROMANIEI extensively comments on the protests launched yesterday in Bucharest. The daily remarks the observance of public order, the civilized articulation of the various claims, mostly economic ones, and the openness for dialogue. Daily CRONICA ROMANA welcomes the fact that no violence was used by demonstrators yesterday. [passage omitted]
