July 12, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

UPS Sued Over No-Beard Policy: The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission has filed a federal lawsuit charging that United Parcel Service discriminated against employees on the basis of religion.

The lawsuit said Greenwich, Conn.-based UPS has failed to allow a religious exception to the company's practice of prohibiting employees with beards from working in positions that deal with the public. The lawsuit, filed in a Chicago federal court, alleges UPS violated Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 by failing to accommodate the religious practices of employees.
