BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 10 June Morning Edition] [Text] On 9 June, a top Foreign Ministry official showed a prudent stance toward a Russian proposal for an early international conference among the United States, Russia, China, Japan, the ROK, and the DPRK (North Korea), on North Korea's alleged nuclear weapons development. He said: "I believe neither the United States nor the ROK fully supports the idea right now." However, the official noted the idea may become worthy of consideration when the UN Security Council [UNSC] adopts a resolution on the issue, saying: "It appears some nations would support an international conference following the UNSC resolution."
