940215 FT 15 FEB 94 / Small retailers struggle hardest in thin recovery Small retailers are shown as performing particularly badly in the generally downbeat monthly survey of distributive trades by the Confederation of British Industry. While large multiple stores saw sales grow significantly in January, smaller shops reported a decline. The survey shows that of stores with more than one outlet, the balance of those reporting an increase in sales over those reporting falls was 32 per cent. This contrasted with a net balance of 16 per cent of retailers with a single outlet reporting declines. The findings of the CBI survey will come as a disappointment after recent trading statements painted a more optimistic picture of the retail sector. Mr Nigel Whittaker, chairman of the CBI's distributive trades panel, said: 'Although only four sectors report slight falls in volumes, most sectors still say that sales are below average for the time of year.' Before today's survey, tomorrow's official retail sales figures for January were expected to show an improvement, largely due to New Year sales, after a decline in December. Mr Whittaker said: 'Looking ahead, prospects for retailers have changed little from December. The increase in sales in February is expected to be only slightly higher than the growth recorded in January. Expectations are the weakest since last May, pointing to continued modest growth in high-street demand.' The survey shows that there were increases in sales across most sectors, with booksellers, stationers, grocers, and footwear and leather retailers among those showing the best improvement. But specialist food shops, confectioners and hardware, china and DIY retailers all reported that volumes are down on a year ago and all other sectors reported similar slow growth rates. Growth in wholesalers' sales volumes increased in January. They remain above average for the time of year. However, the survey reports that both wholesalers' and retailers' stock levels are still too high in relation to expected demand. Motor traders had a better start to the year, growing at the highest rate since August 1988, and business is considered to be well above average for the time of year.
