BFN [Text] Damascus, April 7, IRNA -- Majles Vice-Speaker Hojjat ol-Eslam Hasan Ruhani discussed major regional issues and international developments with Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad here today. During the meeting which lasted more than two hours Hoj. Ruhani also conveyed a verbal message from President Akbar Hashemi- Rafsanjani to his Syrian counterpart. Hoj. Ruhani described the Zionist regime as the "origin of the unrests in the sensitive Middle East region" and said this regime spoke of peace spuriously in an effort to take advantage of the regional resources. "Peace is in contradiction with the very nature of this usurper regime," he added. Referring to the massacre of the palestinian worshippers in al-Khalil [Hebron], Ruhani regretted that some elements who consider themselves as the representatives of the Palestinian nation fell into humiliation by signing compromising deals, and in fact committed treason against the holy Palestinian ideal. He predicted that the Palestinian intifidah and Islamic resistance in Lebanon which are supported by Iran and Syria and the Islamic ummah at large, would bear fruit, and restore the trampled rights of the Palestinian nation. On the hajj pilgrimage, Ruhani said in the wake of the agreement signed by the Iranian and Saudi Arabian foreign ministers, the non-cooperation of the Saudi officials to provide facilities for more Iranian pilgrims is "unjustifiable". President al-Asad in reply said the similarity of the Iranian and Syrian positions concerning most of the important regional issues as well as their resistance against the threats of the Zionist regime were the root of the global arrogance hostilities against the both countries. On the so-called Middle East peace talks President al-Asad said the talks had not so far borne any results. "Syria does not hope the talks to end in any just results," he added. Stressing that Syria would not overlook the trampled rights of the brotherly Palestinian nation under any circumstances, the president said his country was not ready to accept the planned peace with the Zionist regime even if the talks lasted for decades. He noted that Iran and Syria are targets of the propaganda attacks by the global arrogance led by the U.S. due to their independent and anti-Zionist stances. "The accusations like violation of human rights and terrorism and the last one, i.e., anti-drug campaign will be removed, if we step back from our righteous stances," the president said. Expressing pleasure with the level of Tehran-Damascus cooperation, President al-Asad expressed hope that he would visit Iran in a near future.
