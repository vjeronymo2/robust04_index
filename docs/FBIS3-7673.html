Language: Thai Article Type:BFN [Text] Asked to comment on an army newsletter article, which says that a coup is not an evil thing and that it is one of the methods in developing the country, Prime Minister Chuan Likphai said we should not talk about coups. We should talk more about democracy. [Begin recording] [Chuan] It is better for us to discuss democracy, not coups. We can solve problems through the democratic system since we love this system. [Unidentified correspondent] Is it time for us to solve problems by rules and regulations, not threats and intimidation? [Chuan] I have stressed all the time that we should deal with problems in line with rules and regulations under the democratic system. I hate threats because people have dignity. [end recording] Meanwhile, Defense Minister General Wichit Sukmak said that it is just an academic paper about a coup, how it is staged, and whether or not it will benefit the country. This has nothing to do with countering government policy. [Begin recording] [Wichit] It is an expression of opinion by the writer. This is just a theory. It has no harm. [Unidentified correspondent] Will it cause confusion since the article is publicized in the army newsletter? [Wichit] It depends on interpretation. I have not read the article. Thank you for informing me about this. I believe that it is rather an academic article. [Unidentified correspondent] Have there been any moves in the Armed Forces? [Wichit] The military leaders have repeated on several occasions that the Armed Forces are an apparatus of the government. [Unidentified correspondent] Do you think that such an article was meant as a hint at a time when there is political confusion? [Wichit] It is rather an academic article. [end recording]
