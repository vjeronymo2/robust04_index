Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow Jan. 14, IRNA -- The Armenian President, Levon Ter-Petrosyan in Yerevan Thursday [13 January] underlined the significance of Iran-Armenian ties in the region and stressed the necessity to boost bilateral cooperation. He made the remark in a meeting with the Iranian Foreign Ministry's director general for CIS affairs, Morteza Bank, who is in Armenia to discuss Iran's stands on regional issues. Bank conveyed the message of President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani to Ter-Petrosyan in response to the Armenian president's written message. Bank also discussed regional developments as well as Iran's efforts to establish peace in the region with Ter-Petrosyan. Ter-Petrosyan further underscored the need to settle the Karabakh crisis peacefully. The Iranian official also met with the Armenian Vice-President, Gagik Arutyunyan, and reviewed issues of mutual interest especially economic cooperation between the two countries. Meanwhile, in a seperate meeting with the Armenian Foreign Minister, Vagan Papazyan Thursday, Bank submitted the written message of Iran's Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati to Papazyan. Bank noted that efforts should be made to prevent the Karabakh crisis from becoming an international problem and added that coordination among regional countries was necessary for putting an end to the dispute. He also expressed Iran's willingness to cooperate with Russia to settle the crisis. Papazyan stressed the need to contain the crisis, adding that Iran could play a significant role in settling the problem.
