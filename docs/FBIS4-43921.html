BFN [Editorial Report] Berlin N-TV in German at 1010 GMT on 22 June carried a live relay of the 77-minute speech delivered by Rudolf Scharping, leader of the Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] to the SPD congress in Halle. Scharping opened his speech by commenting on the huge expectations from the speech itself, regarded by some as the make-or-break speech for Scharping. He then stressed his support for the people of eastern Germany and strongly criticized the effects of 12 years of Kohl's rule on the people throughout Germany, particularly with regard to the welfare state. He said that following the defeat in the European elections the party has to fight more clearly and resolutely than before. He stressed that he is determined to win the election rather than come in second. Scharping congratulated those who contributed to German unity, including the east European states. Referring to the achievement of the Social Democrats under Willy Brandt with regard to East-West relations, he stressed that Germany needs a new policy of the people and must take its international role seriously, bringing the people of Europe closer together. Scharping then moved on to the subject of coalitions saying that his party intends to concentrate on its own policies and not on coalition speculation. He said that environmental, economic, and social policies must be considered in tandem and his party is not content to hand responsibility for one of these areas to, for example, the Greens. He then emphasized that he will seek to shake off his reputation for being characterless. Moving onto the subject of taxes, he reiterated that high earners should not complain about high taxes and should never forget the poor. He said his party must go on the offensive and remove Helmut Kohl from government. Appealing to the east Germans never to forget what Kohl has done to them, he accused Kohl of deceiving people about the state of the economy and appealed to the people of the east not to vote for the Party of Democratic Socialism out of disillusionment. He added that his party wants an economic upswing, but not false optimism. Stressing that his party wants tax exemption for those living on the poverty line, he said that the government has sacrificed people for the sake of competitiveness. Any upswing has to be accompanied by sound environmental policies and has to be in the interest of all. He added that the right wing will not be allowed to flourish under the SPD and condemned attacks on foreigners. In conclusion, he stressed that the SPD wants a unified Germany that is strong economically, has good ecological policies, and lives up to its responsibilities to Europe and the world. Scharping received a standing ovation at the end of his speech.
