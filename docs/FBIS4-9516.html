BFN [Article by "dz": "The Government Approved 1993 Budget Closing Statement"] [Text] Prague--In its session yesterday, the government approved the draft 1993 government budget closing statement. The statement shows a surplus of 1.1 billion korunas. According to Deputy Prime Minister Josef Lux, 200 million of this surplus is to be used for the region program, 87 million for additional payments on the Bilina mine credit, 339 million for the internal revenue computer system, and 454 million to increase reserves for dealing with social problems. The government described last year as successful. "It was the first time that a growth of labor productivity could be registered--by 3.1 percent--and the foreign currency reserves reached their maximum in the history of our state--$6 billion," Lux emphasized. The government acknowledged the Czech National Bank report on the balance of payments and monetary development for 1993. "The debt of the Czech Republic increased by $1.8 billion to $8.7 billion, 76 percent of which are long-term obligations. Overall, we are creditors, but a great part of our claims is difficult to collect," Lux commented.
