BFN [Text] Amman, June 12 (AFP) -- Jordan's tourism association on Sunday denied an Israeli newspaper report that five Jordanians were to attend a conference in Tel Aviv. "No member of the association, which represents the whole of the private sector, is going to take part" in the tourism conference opening on Monday, its director Yusuf Muwaswas told AFP. Tourism Minister Muhammad 'Adwan also denied the report, saying his ministry had not been informed of any such participation. The Israeli daily HA'ARETZ reported earlier that five Jordanians were to attend the conference for travel agents. Israel and Jordan resumed peace negotiations last week and signed agendas for treaty talks covering water, energy, environment, security and territorial issues.
