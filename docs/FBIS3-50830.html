Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Disciplinary Board of Magistrates on 27 December 1993 named 14 full members of the Supreme Court and four supreme prosecutors. The designations were made under the terms of Article 2 of the constitutional law dated 17 December 1993, which empowers the Disciplinary Board of Magistrates to designate and evaluate Supreme Court members and prosecutors. The whereas clauses of Court Resolutions Nos. 1 and 2 establish that the Disciplinary Board of Magistrates has conscientiously chosen the best qualified-magistrates for the posts. The designated Supreme Court full members are: Luis Almenara Bryson, Jorge Buendia Gutierrez, Andres Echevarria Adrianzen, Carlos Giusti Acuna, Pedro Iberico Mas, Elmer Lozada Peralta, Emilio Montes de Oca Peralta, Luis Ortiz Bernardini, Nelson Reyes Rios, Javier Roman Santisteban, Lino Roncalla Valdivia, Manuel Sanchez Palacios Paiva, Hugo Sivina Hurtado, and Elcira Vasquez Cortez. The designated supreme prosecutors are Miguel Aljovin Swayne, Ana Nelly Calderon Navarro, Blanca Nelida Colan Maguino, and Pedro Pablo Gutierrez Ferreyra. The two resolutions established that the Disciplinary Board of Magistrates will issue, on behalf of the nation, the certificates to the designated Supreme Court members. Thanks to the constitutional law promulgated on 16 December 1993, the Disciplinary Board of Magistrates' functions were expanded to include the designation of members of the National Council of Magistrates, which is an institution that designates and evaluates Peruvian judges, except for justices of the peace, who are selected in elections by the people, as is set forth by the Constitution approved in the referendum on 31 October 1993.
