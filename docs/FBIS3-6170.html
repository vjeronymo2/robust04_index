Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A breakaway group of the MNLF [Moro National Liberation Front] operating in Basilan province in southern Philippines has warned that more wealthy businessmen and school teachers will be abducted by the group. Sulaiman Ali, leader of one of the lost commands said the new wave of kidnapping will start at the close of the Muslim holy month of Ramadan. The month-long fasting of Muslims ended on Sunday. Ali, however, denied responsibility in the kidnapping of five school teachers in Tuburan, Basilan last 7 March. He demanded a pull out of the marines from Tuburan, saying that more people will be abducted by his group starting this month if the marine troops will not be pulled out.
