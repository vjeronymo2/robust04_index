Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A sharp cutting down of military production is taking place in Belarus, First Deputy Defense Minister Alexander Tushinsky declared speaking at the session of the board of Belarus' Defense Ministry. In his words, "last year these rates reduced by 65-70 percent compared with 1991." As Tushinsky asserts, due to an uncontrolled growth of prices for raw materials and fuel and energy, growth of salary, and other factors, cost value of production has increased. As many as 24,800 highly qualified experts (12.9 percent) discharged from defense enterprises, 8200 people -- from military research institutes and design bureaus, he reported. As he said, "there has occurred decrease of traditional markets to sell this products due to their high price and reduction of demand for military products and military orders." According to the first deputy minister, these circumstances "have forced many enterprises to stop or cut down production and compensate the losses producing civil products at the expense of irrational reorientation of industrial capacity. In 1988-92 14 industrial enterprises have entirely stopped production of special products," pointed out Tushinsky.
