BFN [Text] Lisbon, April 7 (XINHUA) -- Portugal Prime Minister Anibal Cavaco Silva has said that his government hopes to further develop a comprehensive relationship with China. In an interview with XINHUA reporters here on the eve of his trip to China, the prime minister said that the relations with China are one of Portugal's major foreign policy priorities. He said his upcoming visit to China is aimed at promoting bilateral relations including economic cooperation and marking the 7th anniversary of the signing of the Sino-Portuguese joint declaration on Macao. The prime minister said about 60 Portuguese entrepreneurs will accompany him on his tour to explore business opportunities in China. On the issue of Macao, he said that the signing of the Joint Declaration was a historic event of great significance. Portuguese government, Silva said, will continue to pursue three objectives on the issue: Stability, progress and confidence building. He said that the Portuguese-Chinese Joint Liaison Group has been working well and the second half of Macao's transitional period will be smooth.
