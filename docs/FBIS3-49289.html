Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] [Announcer] Hungarian-U.S. talks are underway in Budapest on the overseas proposal that NATO should, for the time being, only extend its cooperation with the East European countries instead of full membership. [passage ommitted] The U.S. President sent two special emissaries to Budapest to learn about the Hungarian stance on Washington's notion. UN Ambassador Albright and General Shalikashvili, U.S. chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, first held talks with Minister of Defense Lajos Fur. After the meeting Lajos Fur spoke of concrete forms of cooperation: [Begin Fur recording] The countries which declare that they will accept the system of Partnership for Peace can send representatives to NATO headquarters in Brussels. Every country will have to decide for themselves whether they take this upon themselves, and how much of this they will take on. The countries concerned will have to issue a declaration of intent, in which they will have to state how much and at what rate they wish to take upon themselves the building of relations. To continue, representatives can be sent to NATO headquarters in Brussels, the same applies to the NATO command, the chiefs, where one or two military experts can be sent. Three important fields of concrete military cooperation were mentioned: military planning, military training, and the organization of joint exercises. [end recording]
