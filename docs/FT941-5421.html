940307 FT 07 MAR 94 / De-regulation bill proposal could backfire on employers A proposal in the government's de-regulation bill to allow companies to select employees for redundancy - contravening customary arrangements such as last-in, first-out - could backfire on employers that use it as an excuse to abandon redundancy procedures, says the Institute of Personnel Management's vice-president for employee relations. In today's issue of the IPM's magazine, Mr Ewart Wooldridge says that the measure could be seen by employers as an invitation to reject a structured and consultative approach to redundancy. 'There is a substantial pay-off from treating staff and their representatives properly. Those who remain will judge you by how you looked after those who had to leave.'
