Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum, 9 Feb (SUNA) -- Lieutenant General 'Umar al-Bashir, the president of the Republic, has said that Sudan will not give up its identity no matter how much pressures and statements against it multiply. He said Sudan's cultural plan required effort and patience and that Sudanese of various orientations did not differ on it. His Excellency explained at the first consultative [shura] meeting yesterday evening, which was attended by many scholars and thinkers, that the causes of the economic problems included the weakness of the inherited economic structure, external economic pressures, the continuing war in the south and the need to find more economic inputs. Al-Bashir said the war in the south was aimed against Sudan and its identity. Rebel Garang wanted to abolish the Islamic shari'ah in the country. The peace would only come when the rebels came to realize that they would fail to achieve their overt and covert aims by means of war.
