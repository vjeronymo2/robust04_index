Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] TALLINN, Jan 03, BNS - Maj. Gen. Aleksander Einseln, the commander of the Estonian Defense Forces, appointed Col. Raul Luks as the commandant of Paldiski. The commandant assumed his post as of Jan. 1. The commandant's task is to promote the withdrawal of the Russian troops, nuclear reactors and the submarine training base from Paldiski and the Pakri peninsula. He must also see to the establishment of Estonian laws in the town whose population consists mainly of aliens and the Russian military. The commandant's tasks include securing cooperation between Estonian defense forces, border guards and the police in Paldiski, and preventing the looting of vacated Russian military facilities. Luks was the deputy chief of staff of the Estonian Defense Forces before his appointment to Paldiski. The Russian 93rd naval training base and its two nuclear reactors still remain in Paldiski. About 500 soldiers and officers were stationed there at the end of last year. Russian naval depots and a supplies company are also stationed in Paldiski. The North Infantry Company represents the Estonian Defense Forces in Paldiski.
