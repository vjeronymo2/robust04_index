BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 13 KYODO -- A Turkish Airlines flight from Tokyo made an emergency landing in Moscow earlier this month after the Turkish Embassy in Tokyo received a message threatening to blow up the flight en route to Istanbul, police said Friday [13 May]. According to police and airline officials, the message was received at the embassy by facsimile on May 5 at 2:45 P.M. from a group which identified itself as a Kurdish liberation organization. The message, typed in English, said that TK Flight 593 would be blown up at 0500 GMT the same day, or 2 P.M. Japan time, and the blame would be shouldered by the governments of Turkey and Japan, the sources said. The fax did not display the telephone number nor the name of the country where the message originated, police said. The jetliner was flying over Siberia and was contacted by the airline's office, which had been informed by the embassy. The plane made an emergency landing in Moscow and a search of the interior and luggage was made. Nothing suspicious was discovered and the flight continued to Istanbul.
