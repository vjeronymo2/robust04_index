Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 3 KYODO -- Japan Airlines Co. (JAL) has selected Pratt and Whitney of the United States as its supplier of engines for a new generation of medium-size airplanes to be introduced in fiscal 1995, company officials said Thursday [3 February]. The officials said JAL will purchase 44 PW4000 series engines, worth a total of 440 million dollars, for Boeing 777s, an all-new wide-bodied twin-engine model that seats up to 440 passengers. JAL had considered General Electric Co. Of the U.S. and Rolls- Royce PLC of Britain as possible engine suppliers. With the decision, JAL joined rival airlines all Nippon Airways Co. (ANA) and Japan Air System Co. (JAS) in buying Pratt and Whitney engines for the same Boeing planes. The three airlines plan to cooperate in repair and maintenance work on the aircraft.
