BFN [Text] Kota Kinabalu, June 27 (OANA/BERNAMA) -- The demarcation of the international boundary between Malaysia and Indonesia in Sabah and Sarawak (in East Malaysia), which started in 1975, is expected to be completed by the year 2000, Land and Cooperative Development Secretary-General Azmeer Rashid said Monday. He said 98 percent or 333 km of the boundary in Sabah and East Kalimantan on the Indonesian side had been demarcated and agreed to by the two countries. Work to survey and demarcate the remaining two percent or an estimated 50 km is expected to be completed soon, he told reporters in Sabah. Azmeer is leading an 18-member team to the 19th meeting of the Malaysia/Indonesia joint committee on the demarcation of their international boundary while Indonesia is represented by a 16-member team, headed by Major General H. Suryatna Subrata of the home affairs department. For the boundary in Sarawak and West Kalimantan, Azmeer said only 35 percent or 1,400 km had been demarcated and agreed upon.
