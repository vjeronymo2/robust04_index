930513 FT 13 MAY 93 / Court hearing on Hani killing Three white right-wingers accused of killing South African black leader Mr Chris Hani appeared in court as scores of heavily armed troops ringed the building, AP reports from Johannesburg. Mr Janusz Walus, Mr Clive Derby-Lewis and his wife, Mrs Gaye Derby-Lewis, said nothing as a magistrate agreed to a state request to adjourn the case to May 21. Reuter adds from Durban: South Africa lost up to R500m (Pounds 102m) in tourism earnings last year as a direct result of political violence, Mr Piet van Hoven, chairman of the tourism board Satour, said yesterday.
