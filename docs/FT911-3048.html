910426 FT 26 APR 91 / International Company News: Greece's main private banks climb strongly GREECE's leading private banks reported strong profit growth for 1990, benefiting from continuing deregulation of the banking system and a broadening capital market. Credit Bank announced that net profits totalled Dr18.23bn (Dollars 115.6m), a 161 per cent rise over the previous year. Underlying growth in operating income rose by 60 per cent, while underlying costs rose by only 25 per cent. The results reflected both the growth of economic activity in the private sector and the bank's expansion into new areas such as underwriting. Ergobank, a smaller private bank, reported an increase in net profits of 69 per cent to Dr13.7bn. Underlying growth in operating income rose by 58 per cent while costs rose by 38 per cent. The bank announced a dividend of Dr500 on earnings per share of Dr758. Return on equity was 51.2 per cent, down from 59.3 per cent in 1989.
