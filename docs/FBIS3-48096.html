Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 18 (KCNA) -- THE INDIAN TIMES January [word indistinct] urged the Japanese Government to properly solve the "comfort women" problem. The paper said the problem is liable to "charges of the violation of human morality" under international law. The atrocities of the Japanese imperialists in reducing nearly 200,000 innocent Korean women in their teens and twenties to sexual slaves for the "imperial Army" and violating them and brutally [word indistinct] them were the most heinous crimes without precedent, said the paper. The "comfort women" issue can by no means be buried in oblivion by a "humanitarian relief fund" or by a political tieup, it noted. The main point in the solution of the issue is for Japan to make an overall investigation into the truth of the crimes, make public its results and make an honest apology and adequate compensation, the paper stressed.
