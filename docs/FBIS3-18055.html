Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Paris, 23 February (MTI) -- Hungarian Prime Minister Peter Boross on Wednesday [23 February] attended a working lunch in the Paris Foreign Ministry and held talks with French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe on the prospects of Hungary's membership in the European Union. Following the talks, Boross told journalists that Hungary counts on France understanding its security policy anxieties. He said both countries are interested in furthering economic integration, and there have been countless signs of political rapprochement between the two countries over the past three years. Juppe told journalists he considers Hungarian-French relations to be excellent, and added that France wishes to assist Hungary's efforts to become part of "the European family," where he believes Hungary has a place. He said bilateral cooperation is also going well, though there is still room for development. Juppe also said that the Bosnian situation was among the topics discussed, and that France fully understands the difficulties caused for Hungary by the un embargo. "This is one more reason for us to find a solution as soon as possible," he said. The Hungarian prime minister next held talks with French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur in the Matignon Palace. The topics discussed were the Bosnian situation and what is commonly called "the Balladur plan." In a brief statement to the press about his East-Central European stability program, the French prime minister said France will do everything possible to ensure East-Central European stability.
