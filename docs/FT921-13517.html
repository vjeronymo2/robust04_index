920121 FT 21 JAN 92 / Turkey details sell-off plan THE Turkish government plans to sell its shares in five companies, raising more than Dollars 400m (Pounds 223.4m) in the first stage of its privatisation programme, writes John Murray Brown in Istanbul. Officials confirmed yesterday that the stakes would be sold in block shares to foreign or domestic investors. A decision by the Council of Ministers last week cleared the way for all government minority shares in privately managed companies to be sold. The five companies affected in the first stage are: Ray Insurance, which is to sell a 40 per cent stake raising TL50bn (Pounds 5.3m); Seker Insurance, which is to shed 13 per cent of its shares, worth TL7bn; Ipragas, a retail gas distributor, which will raise Dollars 350m by selling a 51 per cent stake; Caybank, the tea-planters' co-operative bank, which will shed 49 per cent for TL315bn; and Tat, a canned fruit company belonging to Koc Holding, which is expected to fetch TL41bn through its sale of a 17 per cent stake.
