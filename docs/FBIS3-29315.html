Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] It was disclosed on 15 February that, at the bidding for the government procurement of 11 supercomputers with funds provided in the first supplementary budget for fiscal 1993, six supercomputer systems would be procured from U.S. manufacturers, with the remaining five coming from domestic makers. Bidding for supercomputer procurement started in November 1993, and IBM of the United States on 14 February won the 11th and last bid -- for a computer ordered for the National Cancer Center. Of those 11 supercomputers, two of the U.S.-made computers will be supplied by Cray Research Institute Inc., and four of the domestically-manufactured computers by Fujitsu. The Japanese Government considers the result of the current bidding "proves the procurement was conducted in a fair process."
