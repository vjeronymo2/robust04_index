940712 FT 12 JUL 94 / World Commodities Prices: Tea A fair demand prevailed, reports the Tea Brokers' Association. Best and good medium east Africans ruled fully firm to dearer after a hesitant start while mediums were irregular with quality. Central Africans sold readily around last rates. Ceylons came to a selective market with brighter teas steady, others 4 to 8 pence down. There was limited demand with Ceylons often neglected. Quotations: best available 270p/kg, good 160p/kg, good medium 148p/kg, medium 123p/kg, low medium 90p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 271p/kg for a Rwanda pf. 1.
