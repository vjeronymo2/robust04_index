January 4, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

CZECHS TO SEEK END OF COMECON

Czechoslovakia will propose dissolution of the Soviet-dominated trade group Comecon and quit by itself if the suggestion is rejected, Finance Minister Vaclav Klaus said in remarks published today.

After talks in Warsaw on Wednesday, Klaus told the government newspaper Rzeczpospolita that he will propose the move at next week's annual Comecon meeting in Sofia. If defeated, Czechoslovakia will pull out alone, he said.

Czechoslovakia will propose canceling many Comecon agreements at the meeting, which will be attended by prime ministers of the 10 member states, he said.
