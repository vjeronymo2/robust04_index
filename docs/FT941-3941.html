940314 FT 14 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television You might have thought that a dramatisation of a Sherlock Holmes short story and an episode in a documentary series about life today in a Chinese country town would have little in common. Actually the theme is precisely the same in both: opium addiction. Both run from 9.00 until 10.00 so if you want to compare and contrast you will have to use a video recorder. In The Memoirs Of Sherlock Holmes (ITV) our hero (who is, of course, not averse to narcotics himself) investigates the opium dens of Rotherhithe. Phil Agland's documentary Beyond The Clouds (C4) looks at the way that today's form of opium - heroin - is pouring into southern China, with the town of Lijiang on the front line. Horizon explains how the workings of a car radiator led a scientist to a new theory about why humans walk upright: it is all to do with the need to keep thinking machines (computers/brains) cool (BBC2 8.00). BBC1 launches yet another new comedy series: Men Of The World stars David Threlfall (forever Smike from 'Nicholas Nickleby' in my mind) as a travel agent (8.30).
