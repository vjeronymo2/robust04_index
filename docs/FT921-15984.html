920107 FT 07 JAN 92 / World Trade News: Daewoo unit wins Dollars 957m Pakistan highway order DAEWOO Corp's construction division has received a contract for a Dollars 957m (Pounds 525.8m) highway project in Pakistan, the company says, AP-DJ reports from Seoul. The contract, awarded by Pakistan's National Highway Authority, is for a 204-mile road linking Islamabad and Lahore. The project includes building 74 bridges. Daewoo will handle design, detail engineering, procurement and construction, with the design fee totalling Dollars 9m. Daewoo says the contract is the biggest international road contract awarded by Pakistan. Construction is scheduled to be completed by April 1995. South Korea won Pounds 1.7bn worth of foreign construction orders last year, up 42 per cent from the previous 12 months, AP-DJ reports from Seoul. The Overseas Construction Association of Korea, a private group of contractors, said contracts in south-east Asia topped the list, followed by the Middle East, North America and Africa. Projects under negotiation this year included a Dollars 1.8bn power plant in Libya.
