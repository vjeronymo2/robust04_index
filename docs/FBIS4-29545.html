BFN [YONHAP report from Tokyo] [Text] Japan's SANKEI SHIMBUN reported that President Kim Yong-sam and Japanese Prime Minister Hata will hold summit talks from 2 to 3 July at Cheju Island to discuss the North Korean nuclear issue. Quoting a ROK Government source, SANKEI SHIMBUN reported today that the two countries decided to hold the summit talks to harmonize positions on the North Korean nuclear issue before the G-7 meeting slated to be held beginning 8 July in Italy. SANKEI SHIMBUN explained that Cheju Island has been designated as the meeting place because it is recognized that it is important to follow Western-style summit diplomacy -- holding frequent meetings in an informal atmosphere.
