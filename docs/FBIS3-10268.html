Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 1 Mar (NOTIMEX) -- On 1 March, Mexican Foreign Relations Secretary Manuel Tello and Canadian Foreign Affairs Minister Andre Ouellet pledged their support in Mexico City for Colombian President Cesar Gaviria's candidacy for OAS secretary general. Upon the conclusion of the 10th meeting of the Mexican-Canadian Ministerial Commission on 1 March, the two officials announced their governments' support for the candidacy of the Colombian president, thus adding to the support already pledged by the United States, Venezuela, Ecuador, Peru, and Argentina, among others. The election of the OAS secretary general will take place in Washington, D.C. on 27 March. The new secretary general, who will take office on 20 June, must garner at least 18 of the 34 votes that the members of this hemispheric organization will cast. Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus is the other candidate with possibilities of occupying the OAS secretary general seat.
