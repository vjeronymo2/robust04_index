April 20, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: LOW-SODIUM RECIPE

DEAR JEANNE: Please see if you can reduce the sodium in the following recipe. I know the onion soup mix is loaded with sodium but wonder about the drained mushrooms and onions.

-- MRS. A. B., Bettendorf, Iowa

BEEF BOURGUIGNON

1 1/2 pounds sirloin steak, cut in 1 1/2-inch cubes

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 tablespoon cornstarch

1 (1 1/2-ounce) package dry onion soup mix

2/3 cup dry red wine, about

1 cup canned mushrooms, drained

1 green pepper, cut in 1-inch pieces

1 (15-ounce) can whole onions, drained

Hot cooked rice

Brown steak in skillet. Combine salt, cornstarch, soup mix and 2/3 cup wine in 2-quart casserole. Stir in steak, mushrooms, green pepper and onions. Bake at 325 degrees 2 hours or until tender. Add wine during cooking if mixture seems too dry. (Reserve liquid from onions and add if needed.) Serve with rice. Makes 4 to 6 servings.

DEAR MRS. A. B.: By making your own soup mix and using fresh mushrooms and onions instead of the canned varieties, you can easily reduce the amount of sodium in this recipe. Even drained and rinsed canned vegetables may have higher levels of sodium than fresh ones if the label on the can lists sodium as an ingredient.

By covering the casserole for the first half of the baking time, you can eliminate the problem of the mixture becoming too dry. With just a little more effort on your part, I think you will agree that fresh is best when it comes to controlling sodium and getting maximum flavor from the calories consumed.

-- JEANNE

BEST BEEF BOURGUIGNON

1 1/2 pounds sirloin steak, cut in 1 1/2-inch cubes

1 cup chopped onion or 1/2 cup dehydrated onion

1/2 teaspoon onion powder

1/2 teaspoon salt

1 tablespoon cornstarch

2/3 cup dry red wine

1/2 pound mushrooms, sliced

1/2 pound green pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces

3/4 pound boiling onions, peeled

Hot cooked rice

Brown steak in non-stick skillet. Combine onion, onion powder, salt, cornstarch and wine in 2-quart casserole. Stir in steak, mushrooms, green pepper and onions. Bake at 325 degrees 2 hours or until steak is tender. Cover first hour and uncover second, stirring several times. Serve with rice. Makes 6 servings.
