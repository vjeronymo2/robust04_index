BFN [By Debbie Kuo] [Text] Taipei, June 22 (CNA) -- Guatemalan President Ramiro de Leon Carpio is slated to pay a state visit to the Republic of China [ROC] July 5-9, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs reported Wednesday [22 June]. De Leon, who will be the first head of state from Latin America to visit Taiwan this year, is coming at the invitation of Premier Lien Chan, who called on De Leon in Guatemala in early June. During his stay, De Leon will be bestowed with the Order of Brilliant Jade (Grand Cordon) by the ROC Government, in recognition of his contribution to strengthened relations between Guatemala and Taiwan. He will exchange views with President Li Teng-hui on matters of mutual concern and international affairs. The two heads of state will also ink a communique confirming the solid friendship and cooperative ties between the two countries. A staunch freedom fighter, De Leon last visited Taiwan three years ago as a delegate to captive week activities hosted by the World League for Freedom and Democracy, ROC chapter.
