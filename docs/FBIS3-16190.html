Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Kulagin] [Text] Almaty March 14 TASS -- In keeping with the law "on temporary delegation of additional powers to the president of the Republic of Kazakhstan and heads of local administrations" Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev signed a decree on Monday to ratify the agreement establishing a common economic space between Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan which was concluded in Tashkent in January 1994. Both countries have abolished customs check points to encourage free movement of goods across the border. As a result, the prices of food in local market places in the Kazakh town of Shymkent located close to the Uzbek capital are lower than those in state-run shops. Enterprises of the two republics are also boosting direct ties.
