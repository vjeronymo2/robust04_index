BFN [Excerpts] French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe left our country this morning for Dakar, the Senegalese capital. The foreign minister arrived here yesterday afternoon and was received by the president of the Republic with whom he mostly discussed post- devaluation issues and the regional conflicts in Africa. During the news conference he gave last night after his meeting with Prime Minister Daniel Kablan Duncan, Mr. Juppe expressed satisfaction at the post-Houphouet-Boigny political situation and the strengthening of the friendly ties of cooperation between France and Cote d'Ivoire. To this end, he disclosed the imminent visits of President Bedie to Paris and French Prime Minister Balladur to Cote d'Ivoire. On the post-devaluation situation, the French foreign minister expressed confidence in the actions undertaken by our country to deal with it. On Rwanda, Mr. Juppe expressed satisfaction at the political support France has just obtained from Cote d'Ivoire in its quest to bring the civil war in that country to an end. [passage omitted] Yesterday, after his audience with the prime minister, preceded by the one granted him by the president of the Republic, Mr. Juppe spoke to our correspondent Pierre Adipo. Let us listen to him. [Begin Juppe recording] First, I would like to thank the prime minister for devoting some of his time to me, despite his heavy schedule. In coming to Abidjan, you know it is my first official trip to francophone Africa, I wanted to demonstrate all the importance we attach to what happens in this country. The success of the economic reforms under way in the whole region will be staked here, and after this first series of meetings I must say that I am confident about the future. Courageous measures have been taken, they are being applied with a lot of determination, and the initial results are emerging. This is what I was discussing with the prime minister, but, of course, we also discussed cooperation between France and Cote d'Ivoire. You know that your country can count on my country's friendship. [end recording]
