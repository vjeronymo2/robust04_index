BFN [Text] We have just received a statement from the president's office: Republic of Croatia President Franjo Tudjman received a delegation of the Croat republic of Herzeg-Bosna in the presidential palace today. The delegation was headed by Prime Minister Jadranko Prlic. They talked about the further implementation of the Washington agreement. The delegation stressed that the power supply system and telecommunications should be restored as soon as possible across the entire federation and that they should be linked to the Republic of Croatia. They also stressed the need for the return of refugees and displaced people within the federation, in line with the Washington agreement. Expressing support for the delegation in its efforts to normalize the situation as soon as possible and introduce new relations on the basis of the Washington agreement, President Tudjman suggested that individual Croatian counties [words indistinct] help in concrete terms in restoring some Croatian [word indistinct] in the federation's ruined areas. President Tudjman launched an initiative for establishing a separate Croatian committee which will [word indistinct] trust and develop relations between the Croatian and the Bosniak people within the federation in Bosnia-Herzegovina and the confederation with the Republic of Croatia in the economic sphere, and in education, health care, culture, and all humanitarian issues.
