BFN [Text] Prague May 12 (CTK) -- During their meeting today, Czech Foreign Minister Josef Zieleniec and his Slovenian counterpart Lojze Peterle signed a cooperation agreement covering education, culture and science, they told journalists. They also talked about the Stability Pact to be signed at the coming meeting of European foreign ministers in Paris and looked at questions of bilateral trade, they said. Trade between the two countries, which have signed a free trade zone agreement, has been growing rapidly, with Czech exports to Slovenia in the first eight months of last year valued at $78 million and Slovenian exports to the Czech Republic over the same period amounting to $33 million. Peterle, who started his official two-day visit to the Czech Republic today, also met President Vaclav Havel and Parliament Chairman Milan Uhde. He will be visiting a Prague Castle exhibition about Josip Plecnik, Slovenian architect who worked in Czechoslovakia between the two world wars and designed alterations to the Castle and its gardens. The ministers agreed on furthering cultural links. The Czech Republic is to organise a Czech cultural festival in Ljubljana.
