940621 FT 21 JUN 94 / World Commodities Prices: Tea There was very strong demand, particularly for brightest East Africans, which often gained 10 to 15 pence, reports the Tea Brokers' Association. Medium and plainer Africans were also well supported at generally dearer rates. Brighter Ceylon bopf's also advanced with other descriptions firm. There was improved demand for coloury Ceylon bopf's, which often gained several pence, but bop's and thin, green types met limited interest and were maily easier where sold. The few Africans on offer were dearer. Quotations: best available 260p/kg, good 170p/kg, good medium 148p/kg, medium 122p/kg, low medium 88p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 325p/kg for a Rwanda pd.
