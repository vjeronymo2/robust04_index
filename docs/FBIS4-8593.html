BFN [Text] Klaus Kinkel, vice chancellor and foreign minister of Germany, arrived in Bulgaria on an official visit last night. After official talks at the Foreign Ministry this morning, Kinkel will pay a call on National Assembly Chairman Aleksandur Yordanov and have meetings at the Council of Ministers. This afternoon he will be received by President Zhelyu Zhelev. Upon his arrival, the German foreign minister particularly stressed that there are no problems in the relations between Bulgaria and Germany. Regarding the international topics to be discussed in his talks, he said that he would certainly be discussing the situation in former Yugoslavia and the Balkans as a whole. In addition, the situation in Russia would not be omitted in the talks. Kinkel supposed that other subjects would crop up in the course of the talks that would prevent them from becoming tedious. Perhaps one of the reasons is this: [Begin Kinkel recording, in German fading into Bulgarian report] Bulgaria lies very close to my heart, Mr. Kinkel said. He added that he will try to help Bulgaria in solving its problems, both in terms of joining the EU and in connection with its contacts with NATO and its relations with the IMF. I am sure that Bulgaria can count on the German foreign minister, Kinkel declared on his arrival at Sofia Airport. [end recording]
