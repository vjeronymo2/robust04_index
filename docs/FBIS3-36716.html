Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Vladimir Akimov and Georgiy Shmelev] [Text] Almaty February 8 TASS -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev has arrived in Almaty. The welcoming party at the airport included Kazakh Foreign Minister Tuleutay Suleymenov and other officials. A news conference for Russian and Kazakh correspondents was held at the airport. Kozyrev said the aim of his visit is to exchange opinion on a broad range of issues of mutual interest, primarily those on filling in with specific content the commonwealth treaty on economic union, bilateral military cooperation, implementation of the START-1 treaty and joint space exploration. The sides also consider humanitarian issues, including citizenship, migration and consular activities. The minister is expected to meet Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev on Wednesday morning to hand him over a personal message from President Boris Yeltsin. Then the interlocutors will have a tete-a-tete meeting to discuss major aspects of bilateral relations. On Wednesday afternoon there will be negotiations of delegations from Russian and Kazakh foreign ministers that are expected to result in signing of several documents. On Thursday the foreign minister will go to the Baykonur space launching grounds to meet heads of the space complex.
