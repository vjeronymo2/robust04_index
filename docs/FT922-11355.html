920425 FT 25 APR 92 / Lull in Sarajevo fighting Sarajevo, the capital of Bosnia-Hercegovina, yesterday saw a lull in the fighting that broke out immediately after the leaders of Moslem Slavs, Serbs, and Croats had agreed to observe a ceasefire brokered by the European Community on April 12, Laura Silber writes from Belgrade. Sarjevo residents described it as the worst night of clashes since battles erupted in Bosnia about seven weeks ago over its bid for independence from Yugoslavia.
