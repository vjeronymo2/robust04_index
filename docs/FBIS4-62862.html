BFN [Text] The release of two Soviet prisoners of war, Aleksey Olenin and Yuriy Stepanov, who had been handed over to First Deputy Foreign Minister Anatoliy Adamishin in Islamabad recently, resulted from close cooperation between the Russian Foreign Intelligence Service (FIS) and the Pakistani Defense Ministry's Defense Agency. Tatyana Samolis, a spokeswoman for the FIS, who said so in an Interfax interview added that her statement had been cleared with the Pakistani intelligence. She said that Stepanov and Olenin would arrive in Moscow at the end of the week. She explained that they were not forced to stay in Russia. They alone will choose the country where they will live. Her agency sought only their release. Samolis said that former Soviet POWs were still held in Pakistan. She hoped that cooperation between the Russian and Pakistani intelligence services would continue.
