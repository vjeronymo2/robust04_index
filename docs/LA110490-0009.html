November 4, 1990, Sunday, Bulldog Edition

SOVIET TROOPS IN GERMANY LOAD POTATOES FOR HOME

Soviet troops based in what was East Germany will help load ships with German potatoes to make up for a harvest shortfall in their homeland.

About 100 Soviet soldiers will come to the northern port city of Hamburg each day to load six ships with 100,000 tons of potatoes, a harbor authority spokesman said.

Another 500,000 tons will be shipped from Baltic ports in eastern Germany, also with Soviet military help.
