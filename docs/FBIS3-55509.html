Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Alexey Kuznetsov] [Text] New Delhi January 8 TASS -- "Russia and India have favourable prospects of developing bilateral cooperation, including in the military sphere," commander-in-chief of the Russian ground forces Colonel General Vladimir Semyonov said, ending his official visit to India as head of a military delegation. According to Semyonov, Russia is committed to developing friendly relations with India, regarding it not only as a "strong ally, but also as a guarantor of stability in the Asia-Pacific region." He said that one of the objectives of the visit was to analyse right there, in India, matters dealing with military-technical cooperation, which are of interest for both sides. We should work for removing doubts that emerged recently in the minds of our Indian partners, Semyonov said. During the visit Semyonov held talks with senior officials of the Indian Defence Ministry and met commanders of all the three armed services. They discussed issues connected with the organisation in the republic of a production base for the modernisation of Russian tanks other other military hardware, with which the Indian Army is equipped, as well as its repair and operation. The commander of the Russian ground troops visited the southern military district, the training centre of the armoured troops and the higher artillery school near Puna, the state of Maharashtra. Besides, he acquainted himself with the everyday life of the Indian Army and praised its combat preparedness, professionalism and the skill of its generals, officers and soldiers.
