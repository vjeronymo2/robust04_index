Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 16 (XINHUA) -- The formal diplomatic links between China and South Africa will be put on the agenda of the Chinese Government after the general election in South Africa to be held in April, Chinese Foreign Minister Qian Qichen said today. But before the general election which is to elect a new democratic government which abandons the policy of apartheid, the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries is not possible, Qian told a press conference during the second session of the Eighth National People's Congress now in session. China and South Africa have had links between non-governmental organisations, Qian said.
