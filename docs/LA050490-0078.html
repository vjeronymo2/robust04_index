May 4, 1990, Friday, San Diego County Edition

SAN DIEGO COUNTY DIGEST: SAN DIEGO

A thief who is suspected of robbing several banks in San Diego County entered a Point Loma bank Thursday and left with $1,643, police said.

It was the 13th bank the robber has hit since Feb. 8, said police spokesman Bill Robinson.

The thief walked into Home Federal Bank in the 1100 block of Rosecrans Boulevard at 9:50 a.m. and gave the teller a note saying he had a gun and wanted money, Robinson said.

The robber is described as black, in his late 20s or early 30s, between 5-foot-7, and 5-foot-9, and weighing 150 to 165 pounds. He usually wears a dark blue suit and sunglasses, Robinson said.

The robber was last seen walking west on Addison Street.
