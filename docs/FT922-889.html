920625 FT 25 JUN 92 / Executive Cars (10): Mercedes expects standstill MERCEDES, balancing short-term expectations of a revival in export markets this year with a 9 per cent fall in domestic new car registrations, expects its passenger car business to stand still during 1992. And no-one is prepared to make forecasts over a longer term. The Japanese quality motor market remains unstable, the rest of western Europe shows faint signs of recovery, and the US a modest reversal of the catastrophic slump. Mercedes group sales in the US rose 18 per cent in the first four months of this year while the overall market rose by only 2 per cent. 'In spite of this, there is no reason for excessive euphoria,' said Mr Werner Niefer, group president. Mercedes sales last year fell 25 per cent as the US market reached its lowest level since 1982, and the current upturn marked no more than a gradual return to normal. 'Unfortunately,' Mr Niefer added. 'We cannot expect help from the Japanese market either.'
