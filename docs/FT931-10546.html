930208 FT 08 FEB 93 / Pay limit 'likely to be observed' MOST public sector employers are likely to observe the 1.5 per cent pay limit announced by the chancellor in the 1992 Autumn Statement, but the policy is creating serious difficulties for them, say pay analysts Incomes Data Services. An IDS guide says: 'Government ministers are now seeking to impose a centralised decision on pay where previously they had promoted public sector reforms designed to decentralise decision making. These reforms probably make the limit harder to enforce.' IDS found public sector employers were having to put off changes in pay arrangements, including the introduction of performance-pay schemes. A number were worried the limit would have an adverse impact on staff morale. The Public Sector Pay Limit 1993, IDS, 193 St John Street, London EC1V 4LS.
