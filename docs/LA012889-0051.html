January 28, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

WRITER FAILS TO SEE ALL DIMENSIONS OF SUPER BOWL

In Monday morning's Super Bowl wrap-up, Bob Oates describes Joe Montana as "the greatest come-from-behind quarterback the game has known."

Fame is fleeting.

How soon we forget!

Montana is great at performing last-minute heroics, but certainly he is no better at beating the clock than Baltimore's Johnny Unitas. Unitas had Raymond Berry to throw to, but Berry is no Jerry Rice.

FRANK FINCH

Temple City
