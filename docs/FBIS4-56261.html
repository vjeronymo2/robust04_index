BFN [Telephone interview with Antonio Navarro Wolff, presidential candidate for the Colombian 19 April Movement Democratic Alliance, in Santa Fe de Bogota by unidentified reporter on 29 April; from the "Caracol's 0600 to 0900" newscast -- live or recorded] [Text] [Reporter] We salute Dr. Antonio Navarro Wolff, presidential candidate for the 19 April Movement Democratic Alliance. Dr. Navarro, what is your opinion of the letter sent hours ago by Prosecutor General Gustavo de Greiff to Colombian Foreign Minister Noemi Sanin on a diplomatic protest note sent by the U.S. Department of State to the Colombian Government complaining about the attitude of our nation's top prosecutor? Good morning. [Navarro] Good morning. I think the prosecutor general is right in his conflict with sectors of the U.S. Government and Congress because I think the attitude of placing all the blame on us, which was assumed during the Reagan and Bush administrations, prevails again in the United States. I think the prosecutor general has complied with legal norms; he has in no way violated the law. The prosecutor general did not draft the Penal Procedure Code; the government and the Congress did. Thus, [words indistinct] only enforcing it. I think the prosecutor general has been mistreated. I think the summit of governors, which began in Popayan yesterday, should defend the dignified and sovereign position assumed by the prosecutor general. I do not know whether the government is somewhat caught up between [words indistinct] and the need to defend its judicial system. What is indeed true, however, is that the summit of governors [words indistinct] must support the prosecutor general and reject the discriminatory and unfair treatment of him by U.S. authorities.
