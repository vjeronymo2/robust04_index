Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Text] Italy, which currently chairs the G-7, is pressing for Russia to be allowed to take part in the G-7 meeting to be held in Naples from 8 to 10 July. The meeting, unlike past ones, will place a greater emphasis on political themes. The news was revealed by Prime Minister Carlo Azeglio Ciampi who unveiled the G-7 logo in Naples today. Ciampi stressed that there is no reason to believe that there will be Russian participation at the heart of G-7. He also made it clear that there has been no UN request for Italy to take part in operations in Bosnia.
