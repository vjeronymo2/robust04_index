BFN [Text] UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali will focus on a new plan to help get the confidence-building measures accepted and implemented. A Cyprus Broadcasting Corporation correspondent reported from New York that according to well-informed sources, the UN secretary general will send letters to the two sides in Cyprus explaining the manner to implement the confidence-building measures and will refer extensively to the benefits for the Turkish Cypriot side. According to the same sources, the UN secretary general will proceed with this move if he decides that Turkish Cypriot leader Rauf Denktas has given a positive reply to the 21 March UN document, based on the briefing by Gustave Feissel, the UN secretary general's alternate special representative in Cyprus. Denktas seems unwilling to accept this procedure, but Butrus-Ghali appears determined to send his letters. During their meeting yesterday, the UN secretary general informed Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis of his intention. The Cypriot delegation at the United Nations is skeptical about the effectiveness of the UN secretary general's move and expresses the conviction that the impression that Denktas will accept the confidence-building measures is an illusion. Mikhailidhis said it is crystal clear in the UN secretary general's mind that, despite the course of negotiations on the confidence-building measures, the substance of the Cyprus issue must be discussed without delay. The foreign minister added that the United Nations began forming a picture about the next steps, which will be finalized once Feissel's report on his latest meeting with Denktas is conveyed. The foreign minister had a one-hour meeting with Butrus-Ghali in New York and later held separate meetings with the British and U.S. permanent UN representatives. Mikhailidhis left New York last night to return to Cyprus. He will be in London today where he will meet British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd. The UN secretary general's spokesman, Joe Sills, said that all aspects of the Cyprus issue--including the confidence-building measures, other substantive issues, and the UN secretary general's good offices mission--were discussed at the meeting between Butrus-Ghali and the foreign minister. The spokesman added that the United Nations continues to feel that the confidence-building measures are balanced and offer advantages to both sides. Asked if Denktas accepted the confidence-building measures during his meeting with Feissel, Sills said the UN secretary general is awaiting his representative's briefing.
