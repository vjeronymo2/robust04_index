Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Good Morning From Mexico" newscast] [Text] President Carlos Salinas de Gortari this weekend appointed the Amnesty and Reconciliation Commission in Chiapas in accordance with the Amnesty Law approved by the legislative branch. During the meeting in which the commission was established, President Salinas urged the commissioners to immediately begin assuming their responsibilities as outlined in the Amnesty Law, which has been in force since Saturday. This commission will watch over the full implementation of the guarantees established in the Amnesty Law for those who turn over their weapons. These people cannot be questioned or detained. Likewise, the commission will speed up the legal proceedings, and it will be responsible for declaring the official end of the Chiapas conflict. The Amnesty Commission comprises the following representives: the government secretary, who chairs the commission; the secretary of social development; the prosecutor general of the Republic; the agrarian prosecutor general; the director of the National Indian Institute; and the director of the Overall Development and Social Justice Commission for the Indian people. Jose Narro Robles was appointed technical secretary of the Amnesty Commission.
