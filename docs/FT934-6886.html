931123 FT 23 NOV 93 / Rover may use BMW engine Rover is negotiating with BMW for the supply of diesel engines for use in the Range Rover luxury four-wheel-drive vehicle to be launched late next year, Kevin Done writes. BMW said Rover engineers visited its Munich headquarters last week to discuss the deal. The BMW six-cylinder diesel engine is widely recognised as one of the best car diesel engines in production. It is understood that BMW is also in negotiations with General Motors Europe to supply the diesel engine for use in GM's new generation Opel and Vauxhall Omega executive car to be launched next year. Rover uses its own 2.5-litre direct injection diesel engine in its Land Rover Discovery, Range Rover and Defender vehicles.
