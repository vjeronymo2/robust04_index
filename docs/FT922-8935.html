920511 FT 11 MAY 92 / Phone union branches out THE National Communications Union is making progress in extending its membership beyond BT and the Post Office and has won a foothold in the cable TV industry and at Butlin's, the leisure company, according to a union recruitment circular. If UK union membership as a proportion of the working population is not to dwindle to US levels, unions such as the NCU must expand out of their shrinking, union-friendly heartlands and seek members in smaller companies in communications and services. Mr Alan Harley, NCU national organiser, has reported that the 150,000-strong union is well on the way to surpassing its modest target of recruiting 1,000 members outside BT and the Post Office by the end of 1992. He warns, though, that external recruitment, which he believes might be as high as 8,000 by the end of 1994, may slow in the light of Labour's general election defeat. The NCU has recruited 350 members in Mercury, the main BT competitor, after visiting only 10 out of the 70 main sites, but the three-year aim of recruiting up to half Mercury's 7,000 eligible staff may be over-optimistic. Of the 159 cable TV franchise holders, the union has won one 'access agreement' for membership recruitment at CUC, a Canadian-owned company, and hopes to conclude two more in the next few weeks. It has 100 members in the cable companies and hopes to have 700 by the end of 1993. Other areas of recruitment include Butlin's central reservations office, where the union has 51 members, and Telecom Security, a BT subsidiary, where the NCU is holding a recognition ballot.
