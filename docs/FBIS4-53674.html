BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 20 KYODO -- Japan will hand over medical goods and equipment worth 34,000 dollars to Belarus as emergency humanitarian assistance to help the victims of the 1986 Chernobyl accident, the Foreign Ministry said Friday [20 May]. The aid consists of about 3 tons of medical goods and equipment that can meet emergency medical demands of 30,000 people over a three-month period, the ministry said. Japan's Ambassador to Russia Koji Watanabe will hand over the goods to Belarus on Monday, it said. Watanabe will visit Belarus to present his credentials to concurrently serve as ambassador to Belarus, it said.
