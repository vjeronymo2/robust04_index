BFN [Text] Cairo, 5 May (MENA)--Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid has issued an urgent appeal to the brothers in Yemen, calling on them to refrain from fighting, exercise self-restraint, and close their ranks. Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid warned the Yemenis that partition and civil war would only bring ruin and hardship. The secretary general said that Arab countries and the league's General Secretariat are following the escalation of the situation in Yemen with concern and that they hope to see it end soon. Yesterday Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid telephoned President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and offered to send a high-level delegation to Yemen to help contain the crisis. However, the delegation could not travel to Yemen because of the shelling of Sanaa Airport. Today Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid received a telephone call from Yemeni Prime Minister Dr. Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas. The secretary general urged al-'Attas to seek an end to the fighting to preserve the safety, security, and stability of brotherly Yemen.
