931014 FT 14 OCT 93 / Cairo talks seek African solution AN intense effort by African and Islamic organisations to promote a Somalia peace settlement will begin in Cairo today with the participation of Egypt's President Hosni Mubarak and Mr Boutros Boutros Ghali, the UN secretary-general, writes Michael Littlejohns at the UN in New York. The meeting, to be attended by the secretaries-general of the Arab League, the Organisation of African Unity and the Islamic Conference Organisation, was to have been held in Addis Ababa next Wednesday but was advanced because of the urgency of the crisis. Meanwhile, new problems on the military side arose yesterday. Italy, which has been at odds with the UN before over policy in Somalia, announced that it hoped to pull out its contingents there and in Mozambique. The UN spokesman urged Rome to reconsider.
