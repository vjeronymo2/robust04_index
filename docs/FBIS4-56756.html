BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 5 May. According to information published by the DIARIO OFICIAL, Central Bank profits reached $20.1 billion in 1993. According to Getulio Vargas Foundation economist Rubens Cysne, real profits amount to $4 billion, as the so-called inflationary tax of $16 billion, which the productive sector transfers to the banks because of inflation, must be deducted from the original figure. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 2 May 94 p 11) After registering record sales in March, when 118,000 units were sold, the Brazilian automobile industry registered a 25 percent drop in April, when 94,000 vehicles were sold. This number includes imported cars. In April, Volkswagen sold 33,000 units, Fiat 25,000, General Motors 21,000, and Ford 13,000. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 4 May 94 Business and Finance Section p 1)
