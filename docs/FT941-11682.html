940203 FT 03 FEB 94 / Smithfield demands rejected The Corporation of London yesterday rejected demands for extra subsidies for Smithfield meat market and announced that it would end negotiations with the Smithfield Market Tenants' Association. Smithfield and the corporation are in dispute over how the Pounds 60m refurbishment of the market to meet European Union regulations should be funded. Two members of the association have been elected to the corporation's general council as part of its campaign against rent rises of between 85 per cent and 135 per cent. Mr Jack Neary, the corporation's central markets chairman, said: 'The tenants' negotiators are seeking to have new premises provided by the corporation at what seems to be little or no increase in cost to them. 'Recent discussions illustrate . . . that they are not prepared to accept the realities of the situation, so no meaningful progress is possible.'
