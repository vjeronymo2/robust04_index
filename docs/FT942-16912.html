940407 FT 07 APR 94 / World Stock Markets: Argentina lives through trial by fire Argentina has been one of the worst performing Latin American equity markets so far this year, living through its first trial by fire in international financial markets since making the currency convertible, and adopting a fixed exchange rate three years ago. In March, when the central bank spent a record Dollars 725m - 5 per cent of its reserves - to defend the peso, the Merval share index fell 16 per cent. This week the Merval index has remained volatile in line with international markets, losing some 8.5 per cent on Monday but recouping 4 per cent the following day. The unrest in international markets has demonstrated the fragility of funding for Argentina's 1994 external deficits, forecast at Dollars 10bn to Dollars 12bn. Recently low international interest rates, and strong demand for private company debt issues, may not bridge this gap painlessly. The turmoil has underlined the precariousness of regulation with brokerages not closely supervised. Analysts believe that trading losses will wipe out first-quarter earnings at listed banks such as Banco Galicia and Banco Frances. The recent sacking of Mr Martin Redrado as president of the independent CNV securities commission accentuated another troubling feature. He questioned terms under which the government negotiated with US investment banks Merrill Lynch and CS First Boston to sell shares worth Dollars 720m held by pensioners in privatised oil group YPF.
