Drobnjak: "Terrorists Trained in Turkey"] [Text] Details of a dark scenario to organize an armed rebellion in northern Montenegrin municipalities and to create a so-called state of Sandzak have been slowly reaching the public. They all indicate that the key player was Harun Hadzic, president of the Party of Democratic Action (SDA) for Montenegro and vice president of this party for Sandzak. However, his closest collaborators, who were assigned subversive-terrorist activities, are of no less importance. Thus, Isad Skenderovic, a 29-year-old biology teacher by training from Berane, commander of the security sevice for this municipality, had one of the more important roles in the plan to organize an armed rebellion. He already went far ahead in performing his task and formed terrorist groups and a crisis headquarters for operations in a state of war. According to the most recent findings of the investigation, Isad Skenderovic, together with Hamdija Latic and Muarem Skrijelj and an additional 80 persons of Muslim nationality from northern Montenegrin municipalities, completed a subversive-terrorist course in the Turkish town of Urla. The training was conducted by Turkish officers to whom the students gave information on locations and organizations of airfields, a weapons factory, military barracks, and other significant facilities in Montengro and Serbia. After the completion of the course, Skenderovic was paid 5,000 German marks in cash for the construction of dugouts for the storage of weapons, food, and people. According to the statements given during the investigation, the training in the Turkish army was very specialized and rigorous. It started with the taking of an oath in which the terrorists vowed to liquidate without mercy those persons in their ranks who were hesitant. Groups of three men, dressed in special uniforms, were tasked with tailing and, in the event of an immediate danger of war, liquidating prominent personalities in northern Montenegrin municipalities. The investigation further showed that they planned to destroy bridges and block roads, and that, what is most important, they wanted through these actions to draw the attention of the West to the allegedly threatened position of the Muslims in Yugoslavia. Otherwise, it has been learned that 10 more people from the Pljevlja and Rozaje region were imprisoned yesterday and today. It has also been learned that the public prosecutor will submit a request tomorrow to conduct an investigation of them.
