June 15, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

JAZZ NOTES: ANOTHER INSTALLMENT OF CANTOR'S FILMIC LOVE AFFAIR

Jazz grabbed film archivist Mark Cantor's ear at an early age, and the images of jazz musicians on screen, which seemed to him a logical extension of his love for the music, soon became his passion.

Cantor now owns approximately 2,000 jazz films and you'll be able to see a dandy selection from his vaults in a free screening tonight at 10 at the Monica 4-Plex, 1332 2nd St., Santa Monica. The program culminates a monthlong series of free community events that have preceded the 11th annual Playboy Jazz Festival, being held Saturday and Sunday at the Hollywood Bowl.

On tap tonight are several recent additions to the Cantor collection. "I've got some new footage of Thelonious Monk doing 'Light Blue,' originally aired on French TV in the early '70s, and a piece by Chet Baker from Danish TV from sometime in the '60s," Cantor says. Other clips feature vibist Lionel Hampton, who is on this year's Playboy lineup, appearing in the late '30s with the Benny Goodman quartet, and sax legend Coleman Hawkins. Two 1958 shorts spotlight trumpeter/composer Shorty Rogers, one with the Lighthouse All-Stars, another with his big band.

The 41-year-old Cantor, who has been a kindergarten teacher for 18 years, began his love affair with jazz films serendipitously. "About 20 years ago, I was in a junk shop on Western Avenue and found something labeled 'jazz film,' " he recalls. "I took it home and it sat on the shelf for two years. I finally saw it and it was a film with (sax great) Lester Young and that whetted my appetite."

NEW JAZZ CLUB: San Pedro, the hometown of the late alto saxophonist Art Pepper and the current city of residence for flutist James Newton, hasn't exactly been known as a haven for jazz lovers. But Ozzie Cadena, the former record producer who is also in charge of entertainment at the Indigo Jazz Club in Compton, would like to change that.

Cadena is doing the bookings for Stingaree Gulch, a new jazz room located in the just-opened Sheraton San Pedro (601 Palos Verdes St., San Pedro, (213) 519-8200). The club debuted last week and, according to Cadena, people seem to like what they're hearing.

"We started with (Latin bandleader) Poncho Sanchez and had a nice crowd," Cadena said as saxman David Newman could be heard playing "Stella by Starlight" in the background. (Sanchez plays a free concert today at noon on the steps of Los Angeles City Hall.)

Cadena admitted he's still feeling his audience out. "I'm trying to figure out what they want," he said. "Since it's a new area, I may not be able to do a hard-driving jazz thing every week. So I've tried having the featured vocalist as the emcee. Last week, it was Julie Kelly, this week it's DeeDee McNeil. This weekend she'll introduce the house trio, then after they play, she'll sing a couple of songs, then introduce (violinist) Papa John Creach, the featured artist."

Cover charges will average about $6, Cadena said. A liquor license is pending. Music begins about 8:30 p.m.

Artists on tap include pianist Paul Smith, Tuesday; guitarist Laurindo Almeida plus pianist Gildo Mahones' trio and McNeil, Wednesday, and Creach again on Thursday. In July, New Yorkers Fred Hersch and Louis Hayes will arrive.

BREAKFAST JAM: Marla's Memory Lane in Los Angeles is hosting a breakfast jam session Sunday from 1-4 a.m. Vibist Bobby Hutcherson, who is appearing at Marla's tonight and Saturday, and pianist Horace Tapscott will perform and numerous guest artists will be on tap. "We're hoping that some of the people from Playboy will drop by," said trombonist George Bohanon, who handles artists' bookings for the nightspot. Cover charge is $7. Information: (213) 294-8430.
