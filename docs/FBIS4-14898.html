BFN [Excerpt] Moscow, April 5 (AFP) -- Russia will join the NATO "Partnership for Peace" program on April 21 when it will also present its own ideas on how the scheme should work, Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaly Churkin announced here Tuesday [5 April]. Speaking at a foreign ministry briefing, Churkin said Foreign Minister Andrei Kozyrev would travel to NATO headquarters in Brussels where he would sign the "basic documents" for membership in the partnership plan. "He will take to Brussels presentation documents containing proposals from Russia for the process" of implementing the partnership and on how Russia envisages its role in the program, Churkin stated. He gave no further details on the Russian proposals. [passage omitted]
