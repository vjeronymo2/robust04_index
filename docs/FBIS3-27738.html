Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 31 KYODO -- Japanese Government offices are at odds over an economic growth projection for fiscal 1994, beginning on April 1, officials said Monday [31 January]. The government is likely to set an official economic growth estimate around February 9 as a basis for its budget for the year. But the officials said the Finance Ministry, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) and the Economic Planning Agency (EPA) disagree on growth predictions. The Finance Ministry and MITI say the annual growth rate for gross domestic product (GDP) should be set at somewhere between 2.5 and 3.0 percent, while the EPA says it should be slightly above 2 percent, the officials said. The estimate for fiscal 1993 was 3.3 percent. The three departments are scheduled to start ironing out their differences as soon as the government announces its new pump-priming package on Thursday, which is expected to feature major income tax cuts and additional public works spending. EPA officials predicted the real economic growth rate for fiscal 1993, which ends March 31, will be slightly above zero, managing to stay in the plus column. They forecast the growth rate for fiscal 1994 will barely record 2 percent, even taking into account the effects of the new pump-priming package. The Finance Ministry, however, says the growth estimate should be set at close to 3 percent so that the government will avoid issuing deficit-covering bonds other than those for offsetting tax cuts. The MITI hopes to set a similar growth target.
