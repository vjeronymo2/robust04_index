March 29, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

ALMANAC: COST OF OFFICE SPACE IN COUNTY;

HIGH VACANCY BRINGS IT DOWN

A list of things that cost less now than they did 3 years ago might be pretty short -- especially if it was limited to Orange County items.

However, the price of at least one thing has dropped here: rented office space.

According to Coldwell Banker, the county's average monthly lease rate per square foot of vacant existing office space was $1.65 in the fourth quarter of 1988. That's 2 cents less than the average rate in the fourth quarter of 1985, and 8 cents lower than a year ago.

A big reason for this inflation-defying trend is the large amount of vacant office space here. The office vacancy rate for the 1988 fourth quarter was 21.7%, up from 15.1% about 4 years ago, Coldwell Banker reported. A building boom that helped to produce all that empty space continues, with 3.6 million square feet of space under construction. Existing space totals 45.4 million square feet, Coldwell Banker said.

Corona del Mar had the highest average lease rate in the county, $2.25 per square foot. Bargain hunters should head for Fountain Valley or Yorba Linda, where the average is 95 cents.
