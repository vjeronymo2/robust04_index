Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The prime minister said the country is well on its way to steady economic progress. Addressing a meeting at Trichur in Kerala, Mr. Narasimha Rao said that the country's secular foundation faced the gravest challenges from communal forces in recent times but they have come under effective control now. He called for a massive public cooperation to consolidate the achievement. Mr. Rao reiterated that a polarization has started in Indian politics with the secular forces on one side and the communal and casteist elements on the other. Referring to Punjab, the prime minister said terrorism has come to an end finally with the cooperation of the people. On Kashmir, he said the center is hopeful of restarting the democratic process very soon. Earlier, inaugurating the valedictory function of the platinum jubilee celebration of the local St. Thomas College, Mr. Narasimha Rao said the nation is well set to attain secular democracy despite impediments. He said secularism is the lifeblood of the nation.
