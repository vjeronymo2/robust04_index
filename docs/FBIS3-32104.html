Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Managua, 1 Feb (ACAN EFE) -- The Nicaraguan Government today announced it had reached an agreement with Sandinist transportation leaders to end the strike initiated nine days ago. The Sandinist transportation workers agreed tonight to end the national transportation strike "to contribute to the nation's stability." Presidency Minister Antonio Lacayo said the workers' agreement came in exchange for the government's willingness to negotiate in the coming days the demands that prompted the strike. The minister agreed to hold multilateral and bilateral meetings with the sector's businessmen to discuss the strikers' demands, which include freezing fuel prices and restructuring the transportation sector. Lacayo, President Violeta Chamorro's son-in-law, and leaders of the National Transportation Commission (CNT-Sandinist), announced the end of the strike tonight during a news conference at the Presidency.
