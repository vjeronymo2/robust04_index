BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Yuriy Shcherban] [Text] Moscow, 28 Apr -- A two-day conference at the Russian Ministry of Internal Affairs [MVD] of directorates and departments engaged in the struggle against drug dealing and drug addiction representing the internal affairs bodies of 14 states -- the former republics of the USSR (only a representative of Tajikistan was missing) -- ended today. Aleksandr Sergeyev, head of the directorate of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Russian Federation for combatting illegal drug dealing, told journalists that the conference had discussed basic areas of cooperation. In reply to a question from an ITAR-TASS correspondent, Sergeyev said that in 1993 in Russia there were about 53,000 crimes connected with drug addiction or illegal drug dealing -- 83 percent more than in the 1980's. In 1993, 53 tonnes of narcotic substances had been confiscated, and 500,000 people had been charged.
