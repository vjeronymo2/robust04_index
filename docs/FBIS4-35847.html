BFN [Text] Describing his visit to the United States as satisfactory, the president [Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari] said it was timely and significant. The president, who returned to Islamabad today after this two-week visit to the United States and France was addressing a news conference at the Aiwan-i-Sadar [President House] this evening. [Begin Leghari recording] I would like to reiterate my satisfaction with the outcome of the visit which was timely and significant. At this stage of flux in international political and economic relations, the U.S. Administration and corporate America are considering the future directions of their policies toward our region. My visit was a crucial opportunity to present our viewpoints, try, and influence these decisions. It must be recognized, however, that building a new relationship with the U.S. in the post cold war era will be a poser. It cannot be realized in one visit or at one time. My visit to the U.S., I hope, will be judged by history as having promoted Pakistan's security and prosperity and as forming a bridge between our post cold war ties with the U.S. and the new relationship which is responsive to the challenges and opportunities of the emerging era in international affairs. [end recording] President Farooq Leghari said during his stay in the United States, he met with the Vice President Al Gore. The U.S. Secretary of State, Mr. Warren Christopher, and Deputy Secretary of State Mr. Talbott called on him and he held a series of meetings with other American officials and legislators as well as IMF and World Bank leaders and heads of various financial institutions. He said in his talks with the U.S. leaders it was agreed that the two countries would try to overcome the hurdle posed by the Pressler Amendment through a process of diplomatic consultation. The first round of such consultations was held in Washington by Pakistan and American officials on 26th and 27th May. These discussions were constructive and will continue. The president said Pakistan was also assured that the United States will remain fully engaged in promoting security in South Asia. Washington, he added, is well aware of the danger of the missile race, if India continues the development of its missile program and in particular, if it goes ahead with the deployment of the short range Prithvi. The president said he pointed out that the Agni with a range of 2400 km will pose a threat to a number of countries in our region. President Leghari said the United States acknowledged that Kashmir remains a threat to regional and international peace. Pakistan, he added, has conveyed its willingness to resume talks with India on Kashmir and other subjects once New Delhi takes some steps to create propitious conditions for such talks. Pakistan and the United States also agreed to promote conventional arms control and more effective confidence-building measures by India and Pakistan. The president said the most exciting possibilities for future relations between Pakistan and the United States lie in areas of investment and business collaboration. In his meeting, he said there was clear recognition that Pakistan has now achieved both political and macroeconomic stability. The coming of age of democracy in Pakistan accompanied by the steps of the present government in reducing the budget deficit, in lowering inflation, and in deepening the economic reforms program has created a most propitious climate for both domestic and foreign investment in Pakistan's economy. The president said the IMF, the World Bank, and IFC [International Finance Corporation] have indicated clearly that Pakistan's economic environment is appropriate for investment, particularly in the energy sector. He said, in his opinion, Pakistan is likely to attract investment of over $2 billion annually in the coming years. The president said on his way back, he made a stopover in Paris where he met President Mitterrand. During the course of the meeting, various steps to promote bilateral relations between the two countries were discussed. Replying to a number of questions about the allegations leveled by the leader of the opposition implicating Mr. Leghari in the Mehran Bank scandal, the president rejected all the charges as preposterous and without an iota of truth. His political life is like an open book and the question of his involvement in any scandal does not arise at all. I have never been associated with any of the scandals, he asserted. The president said he will initiate legal proceedings against all those who are resorting to a campaign of character assassination against him. He said the fact of the matter is that he and other members of his family have sold 531 acres of ancestral land to six individuals belonging to Karachi. The payment of this land was made through Mehran Bank. He said those who are hurling allegations against him may not be aware of the fact. Mr. Farooq Ahmad Leghari said it cannot be justified to level baseless charges against the president of Pakistan. Replying to another question, the president said there is a consensus on issues like Kashmir, nuclear program, security of the country and basic economic policies. However, he said, it is unfortunate that certain elements are out to tell the world that no consensus exists on these issues.
