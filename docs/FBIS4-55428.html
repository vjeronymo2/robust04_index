BFN [Text] Ljubljana, May 13 (TANJUG) -- Croatia's Adriatic oil company has impounded 15,000 tons of oil the Slovenian firm Petrol of Ljubljana has bought in Russia until the Slovenian oil refinery in Lendava settles its outstanding debt of 1.4 million Deutsche marks with the Adriatic oil. Croatia has also suspended all light oil deliveries to Slovenia's only refinery in Lendava until it has paid back the 8.5 million dollars it owes to Naftaplin in Zagreb for light oil deliveries made so far. The Slovenia-Croatia silent oil war threatens to seriously undermine the already tense relations between the two neighbouring countries which seceded from the former Yugoslav federation in 1991. Relations between the two sides are especially burdened by the unresolved border and property issues, over 600 million dollars in unsettled claims of private depositors with the Ljubljanska Banka and deterioration in economic relations.
