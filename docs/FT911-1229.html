910508 FT 08 MAY 91 / Soviet parliament considers legislation allowing free emigration from the USSR The Soviet parliament yesterday began a final reading of long-awaited legislation allowing free emigration from the Soviet Union before a vote scheduled for Sunday, writes Leyla Boulton. The US has made adoption of the legislation a pre-condition for granting the Soviet Union most-favoured nation trading status. But US officials say trade restrictions are unlikely to be lifted until the law comes into force. The present draft stipulates the law should take effect only from July 1 1992. Soviet officials cite the cost of restructuring the passport and emigration service as a principal reason for the interval between adoption and implementation. But the delay is also probably a concession to opponents of the legislation who fear that it will trigger a brain drain.
