921002 FT 02 OCT 92 / Survey of Software at Work - Autumn 1992 (29): Infobase - Product Profile INFOBASE, launched in 1989, is a marketing and membership package developed using the Oracle database for the open systems platform. The contacts database has extensive links between entities and activities. The system has a reporting module that is designed for use by marketing people. Supplier: CMG Computer Management Group was founded in 1964 in the UK. It is owned 96 per cent by its employees, (of whom 80 per cent own shares), the remaining four per cent owned by Midland Montagu Ventures. Turnover: Pounds 96m,profit Pounds 8.6.(1990) Pounds 103m, profit Pounds 9.1m (1991) Employees: 600 in the UK, 1,700 worldwide. Number of sites: 10 in the UK. Price: Around Pounds 20,000 for package, dependent on user specified bespoke requirements. Hardware and operating system: Open Systems (Unix). Sales and support: the Infobase software is sold and supported exclusively through CMG. Sample annual maintenance: Pounds 3,000 - Pounds 12,000. Other services available: CMG is one of Europe's largest computer services groups providing consultancy, systems integration, implementation, bespoke development and facilities management. Various users' pros and cons: Pros: 'Ability to tailor package.' Cons: 'Cost; too complex for new users.'
