930603 FT 03 JUN 93 / UK Company News: SW Wood ahead and pays Pounds 2.5m for A Gait SW WOOD, the specialist printing and packaging business previously involved in metal trading, is acquiring Albert Gait Holdings and its subsidiaries for an initial Pounds 2.51m. The acquisition is part of Wood's policy of expansion in the printing and packaging industry. The initial Pounds 2.51m payment is to be satisfied by the issue of 2.39m ordinary shares worth 70p each, making Pounds 1.68m, plus Pounds 837,500 in cash. These payments rely on Gait achieving pre-tax profits of Pounds 500,000 in the year to June 30 and net assets of Pounds 1.7m. Further cash consideration to a maximum of Pounds 506,250 will be due if adjusted profits for the year to June 30 1994 exceed Pounds 335,000. Grimsby-based Gait is involved in the printing and production of timetables and handbooks, computerised typesetting, plate making and book and stationery retailing. Wood yesterday also announced sharply higher profits for the year to March 31. The pre-tax result of Pounds 1.02m compared with a Pounds 250,000 deficit previously (restated in accordance with the FRS 3 accounting standard). The directors have recommended payment of a final dividend of 0.75p, which makes an increased total of 1.25p (0.25p). Turnover for the 12 months jumped to Pounds 10.6m, including Pounds 909,000 from discontinued operations. Sales of Pounds 5.24m in 1991-92 included Pounds 2.9m from discontinued operations. Shareholders' approval for the acquisition will be sought at an egm on July 2.
