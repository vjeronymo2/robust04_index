BFN [Editorial: "Butrus-Ghali's Report"] [Text] The UN secretary general's latest report on the Cyprus issue is expected to be submitted to the Security Council in the next few days. Press reports on Butrus-Ghali's report are contradictory. Some say that the UN secretary general will criticize the Turkish side about the deadlock and some say he will not. The Cypriot Government believes that Butrus-Ghali will condemn the Turkish side for its stance and it seems that is has received some assurances about it. Nothing is certain, however, because it is well known that behind-the-scenes-intervention--particularly by the Anglo-Americans--which is common in such cases, could overturn everything! The possibility that the same factors will intervene in this case and the report will be modified is not ruled out. Rumors are already circulating that there is a difference of views between the United Nations and the U.S. diplomats, particularly Robert Lamb, the U.S. State Department's special coordinator on the Cyprus issue, who refuses to admit that the Cyprus issue has reached a deadlock. At the same time, Lamb claims that "significant progress" has been achieved in the proximity talks. Of course it is well known that the U.S. official is not telling the truth, but due to his position and his mission he can influence things, and his intervention could cause the UN secretary general's report to be extensively modified. It is worth noting that the Cypriot officials are not at all satisfied with Lamb's whole position and behavior. This man does not give a clear and objective picture of the facts, hides the truth, and tries to cover up the Turkish intransigence and provocativeness. In any case, for obvious reasons the Cypriot Government cannot come into an open confrontation with Lamb, who is attempting to present Turkey's stance as heroic in order to mask Turkish intransigence. The U.S. coordinator went to Ankara yesterday for a new round of talks on the Cyprus issue. Without doubt, when he leaves Ankara Lamb will say that the Turkish Government gave new assurances that it will cooperate with the United Nations to implement the confidence-building measures! We do not believe that with the policy they are following the Americans contribute effectively to promoting a Cyprus solution. On the contrary, their stance confuses things. This is because if the Turks know that the United States, the only country that can seriously affect them regarding Cyprus, not only is not pressuring them but on the contrary is covering up their intransigent and provocative stance, then why should they agree to make any concessions? We believe that it must be pointed out to the Americans that they must look at the Cyprus issue from a different angle and at a higher level than Robert Lamb! And the Cypriot people expect U.S. President Bill Clinton--who rules the planet--to act on his promises. We must also make our own decisions. Unfortunately, we are confined to words, statements, and declarations. This is the bitter truth.... [as published]
