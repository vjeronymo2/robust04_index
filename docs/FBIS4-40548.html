BFN [Text] Russia's Deputy Foreign Minister Aleksandr Panov told Interfax that although Pyongyang's withdrawal from the International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] complicated matters, Russia was not withdrawing its call for an international conference on the Korean peninsula. He said it was not Pyongyang's decision to withdraw from the IAEA and Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty that called for sanctions. Sanctions were not introduced against a country for this kind of decision, Panov said, because any country had the right to join a treaty or withdraw from it. Panov went on to say that the international community was discontent with Pyongyang's failure to show good will and cooperate with the IAEA. As a result, the IAEA had failed to get a full picture of the North Korean nuclear program and had moved to raise the issue of sanctions. Panov said that both Russia and its partners were interested in a negotiated solution of the problem. The Russian call for an international conference was gaining support, he said.
