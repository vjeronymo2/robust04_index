Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Hans van den Broek, European Union [EU] Commissioner on Foreign Affairs will have important talks on the Cyprus issue and Cyprus' relations with the EU. Van den Broek arrived here this evening accompanied by Serge Abou, the EU observer on the Cyprus issue. Speaking upon his arrival, van den Broek said the EU and Cyprus are working together for Cyprus' EU accession. He said the Cyprus issue is dragging on and expressed the hope that Cyprus will be reunited. He also said that the EU's interest in a Cyprus solution became more pointed with the appointment of an observer to the talks. Van den Broek expressed regret that the talks are going through a difficult phase, as he put it, and called on the side responsible to cooperate with the Cypriot Government and United Nations, amend its position on the confidence-building measures, and cooperate for a political settlement. Welcoming the EU commissioner, Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis expressed his deep gratitude and stressed that van den Broek has contributed decisively to historic beginnings for Cyprus, adding that it is hoped things will reach a successful conclusion with Cyprus' accession to the EU. Mikhailidhis also said that the EU commissioner's presence in Cyprus is very important. It is, he added, a political message indicating the importance of Cyprus' upgraded relations with the EU.
