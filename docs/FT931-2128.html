930322 FT 22 MAR 93 / Arts: Today's Television There will surely be tears before bedtime in September Song (8.00 ITV) not least because this half-hour drama serial is described as 'bittersweet'. Ted, who cared for his wife until she died of MS, is pretty clearly falling for Roxy, the stripper with a heart of gold and a charming daughter. Roxy may admire Ted's decency and education, but will she reciprocate the love? I fear not, or we would be left without a plot. Dear old Horizon seriously imagines that the Dead Sea Scrolls 'could radically alter what Christians and Jews have believed for almost 2,000 years'. They may alter what historians or scientists believe, but where religion is concerned the power of myth seems to work in inverse proportion to the facts. The programme visits the site of the discovery of the scrolls and examines the evidence (8.00 BBC2). In Anthem For Doomed Youth Kenneth Branagh reads from the war poems and letters of Wilfred Owen (9.25 Radio 3); and in A Book At Bedtime John Hurt begins 'The Inseparable Twins' by Anatoly Pristavkin (10.45 Radio 4).
