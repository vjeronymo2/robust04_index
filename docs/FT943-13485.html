940719 FT 19 JUL 94 / World Commodities Prices: Tea There was good demand, reports the Tea Broker's Association. Bright East Africans were well supported although prices were sometimes lower. Coloury mediums were strong and often gained several pence, while plainer Africans tended easier. Brighter ceylons were dearer, but plain bop's and poor leaf sorts were easier. Ceylons were irregularly easier, but coloury Africans were firm to dearer. Quotations best available 265p/kg. nom, good 158p/kg., good medium 148p/kg., medium 124p/kg., low medium 88p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 265p for a Rwanda pf. 1
