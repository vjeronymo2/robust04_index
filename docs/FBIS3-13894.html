Language: Pashto Article Type:BFN [Text] As many as 1000 armed men joined forces of the Islamic army in Dara-e Ashraf in Pol-e Khomri, Baghlan Province. Quoting security sources of the country, military correspondent of BIA [BAKHTAR INFORMATION AGENCY] reported that forces of the Islamic army and mojahedin of Baghlan Province captured Dar-e Ashraf and security posts around Karkar coal mine in their recent operations, and in the course of [word indistinct] operation cleared a number of areas of Golboddin-Dostom militia. In the course of the operation 1000 armed men joined the ranks of the Islamic army. In recent days mojahedin of the northern provinces of the country and the armed forces have cleared many areas one after another, and have freed the afflicted people of those areas from the oppression of Golboddin and Dostum. After the conquest of the North Salang, Khinjan, Doshi, and Konduz Province, the advance of the Islamic army and mojahedin continue toward Pol-e Khomri and Mazar-e Sharif. According to a report of the security sources, the advance towards Pol-e Khomri and Dara-e Kayan continues. And losing their combat morale, militia leave battle fronts and escape. Similarly the series of surrenders to the islamic and mojahedin of the militia goes on. The resistance of the militia crumbles in Pol-e Khomri after the fall of Dara-e Ashraf and the security posts in its periphery;, their despair increases. The source said five security posts had been captured in the surrounding area of Karkar coal mine, and 60 militia were captured, four militia were killed, and five others were injured. Similarly 30 Kalashnikovs, three rocket launchers, one 82-mm artillery piece, three wireless sets, and one BM-1 rocket launcher set have been captured. According to another report the stratigically important security post of Sholana in the northeast of Division 83 has been captured. As a result 35 militia have been taken prisoner, (?three) Kalashnikovs, three rocket launchers, two ZK-1 machine guns, and one R109 wireless set have been captured. Operations against militia continue in those areas.
