Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Nagoya, Jan. 11 KYODO -- Toshiba Corp. said Tuesday [11 January] it will double its capability of producing 16-megabit dynamic random access memories (DRAMS) at its main chip-manufacturing base in Mie Prefecture. Toshiba officials said its plant in Yokkaichi will have a capability of manufacturing two million chips of that type of DRAM every month by the end of the year. They said the plant will need an investment of 20 billion yen and require 450 more employees, who will be transferred from nearby factories or newly employed, increasing the staff to about 1,000. The 16-megabit DRAMs, used in advanced personal computers, are in strong demand from computer-related equipment makers, particularly in the United States.
