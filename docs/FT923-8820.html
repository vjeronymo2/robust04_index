920808 FT 08 AUG 92 / Fernandez Ordonez dies MR Francisco Fernandez Ordonez, the former foreign minister who helped guide Spain's integration into the European Community, died yesterday after a long illness, AP reports from Madrid. He was 62 and had asked to be relieved from his post in June. Formerly known as the untiring workhorse of the Socialist government of Mr Felipe Gonzalez, the prime minister, Mr Fernandez Ordonez took over at the Foreign Ministry in 1985, three years after the Socialists took office. A tax lawyer by training, he was outspoken in his advocacy of EC unity.
