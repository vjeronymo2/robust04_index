July 16, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: COMING ATTRACTIONS

Today: Box office figures to be released will show whether "The Adventures of Ford Fairlane," "Ghost" and "Quick Change" will make a dent in the summer movie sweepstakes now dominated by "Die Hard 2." . . . Producers expect an onslaught of hopefuls to answer an open casting call at CBS Television City for a television movie based on Lucille Ball and Desi Arnaz.

Tuesday: Celebrities help Disneyland celebrate its 35th birthday, as Roy Disney, vice chairman of the board for Walt Disney Co., re-dedicates the amusement park.

Thursday: The Shostokovich String Quartet begins a two-day engagement at UCLA in the first American tour by what is widely considered the Soviet Union's foremost quartet.

Friday: Lancaster Festival 1990, a nine-day event filled with music, dance, theater and visual art highlighted by seven world-premiere performances, opens in Ohio.

Saturday: Roger Waters heads an all-star gala concert performance of "The Wall" at the Berlin Wall. Featured performers: Sinead O'Connor, Van Morrison, Cyndi Lauper, the Band, the Scorpions, Joni Mitchell, Albert Finney, Marianne Faithfull, Bryan Adams, Thomas Dolby, Tim Curry and Jerry Hall. GREG BRAXTON
