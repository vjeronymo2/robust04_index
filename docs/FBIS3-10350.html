Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 4 Mar (NOTIMEX) -- Mexican Commerce Secretary Jaime Serra Puche referred to the free trade agreement with Costa Rica, whose negotiation will conclude this week, as "the most advanced in Latin America." Serra Puche argued that the agreement not only includes goods but also services, copyrights and investments, dispute settlement procedures, and government purchases." [no beginning quotation mark as received] Mexico and Costa Rica trade goods and services for some $80 million a year. Serra Puche believes that the treaty negotiated with Costa Rica, which will come into force on 1 January 1995, could become a very useful guideline for negotiations with other Central American countries. He pointed out that talks with Central America will continue, but they "will not be conducted as a bloc." Therefore, there will be separate negotiations with Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras, and later with Nicaragua. Serra Puche announced that trade negotiations with the Group of Three -- Mexico, Venezuela, and Colombia -- might resume in March.
