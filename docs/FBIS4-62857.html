BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Boris Krivoshey and Aleksandr Krylovich] [Text] Moscow, 19 May (ITAR-TASS) -- A terrorist attack against Russian citizens took place in Algeria yesterday. As a result three people died and five were injured, said Grigoriy Karasin, director of the Russian Foreign Ministry's information and press department, at a briefing today. He named the dead as Baturin, Petkevich, and Chenchikov. According to him, a bus carrying 25 Russian citizens was travelling from the town of Jijel to the airport in the country's capital. Near the village of Ziam the bus was attacked. Eleven servicemen of the Algerian army who were guarding the convoy of Russian specialists died in the fight. G. Karasin said that the terrorists sustained heavy losses. Viktor Posuvalyuk, director of the Near East and North Africa department of the Russian Foreign Ministry, had a meeting today with the ambassador of the Democratic and Popular Republic of Algeria in Moscow. He raised in no uncertain terms the question of stepping up security measures to the utmost for Russian citizens in Algeria and requested an immediate inquiry into the circumstances of this tragic incident. G. Karasin emphasized that a whole series of additional arrangements have been made at the initiative of the Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs to increase the security of our specialists living and working in Algeria. Specifically, the total number of Russian Federation citizens in the Democratic and Popular Republic of Algeria has been halved to around 1,100 people. The majority of wives and children of our specialists have left Algeria. The Russian Federation Ministry of Foreign Affairs told Russian ministries and departments which have their own people in Algeria and specialists working at economic cooperation sites outside the capital of Algeria, as well as Russian Federation citizens in the country on private contracts that security measures need to be observed to the utmost and that personal discipline and organization need to be stepped up.
