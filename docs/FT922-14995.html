920403 FT 03 APR 92 / International Company News: BCE appoints Red Wilson as president LYNTON 'Red' Wilson, 52, is stepping up to president and chief executive officer of BCE, the telecommunications holding company that owns Bell Canada and controls Northern Telecom and other companies. Mr Wilson moved to BCE late in 1990 as chief operating officer from the Bank of Nova Scotia, where he had been vice-chairman. Before that he was chairman of Redpath Industries, controlled by Tate & Lyle. He is a former Ontario deputy minister of industry. Raymond Cyr, 58, will remain chairman of BCE and develop the telecommunications business. Mr Cyr has overseen the restructuring of BCE over the past five years.
