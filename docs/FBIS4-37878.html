BFN [Text] London, 25 Jun (MENA)--The Sudanese opposition al-Ummah Party has accused the Sudanese security authorities of harassing party leader al-Sadiq al-Mahdi who was arrested last Monday. A statement by the party said al-Mahdi and two of his aides, who are detained in a Khartoum prison, have suffered various harassment acts. Their families are not allowed to visit them and they have been denied clean clothes and food from outside the prison. The statement added that the Sudanese security authorities have barred the lawyers who offered to defend al-Mahdi from contacting him and obtaining an indictment against al-Mahdi and his colleagues. The statement appealed to human rights organizations and fraternal and friendly forces to continue pressuring the Sudanese Government to guarantee the safety of and release al-Mahdi and his aides immediately.
