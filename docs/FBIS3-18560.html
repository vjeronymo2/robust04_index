Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Brussels, March 3 (AFP) -- A controversial 1991 European Union regulation that television channels must broadcast a majority of European programmes is being largely respected, the European Commission said here Thursday [3 March]. It released a survey showing that 70 of 105 channels -- 66 percent -- had obeyed the directive, which states that more than 50 percent of programmes excluding sport and news must be provided by European producers. Among those not meeting the directive, a Commission spokesman named several satellite channels in Britain and a cable channel in France. The Commission is preparing a report on the directive and possible modifications before October this year.
