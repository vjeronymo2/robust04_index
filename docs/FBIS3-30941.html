Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, Feb 9 (TANJUG) -- The command of the United Nations Protection Force (UNPROFOR) in the war-torn former Yugoslav republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina has notified the command of the Bosnian Serb army that they will no longer allow them to control the contents of shipments in humanitarian aid convoys, Assistant Commander of the Serb Army Milan Gvero told TANJUG on Wednesday. General Gvero was told the decision by the Commander of UNPROFOR Headquarters in Bosnia-Herzegovina British General Angus Ramsey, judging the Serb control of convoy contents to be "an infringement on the freedom of movement." Due to many attempts at abusing these convoys, the Serb army cannot let any convoys transit without controlling the contents, Gvero stressed. The Serbs also learn that the staff of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) received orders to leave their offices and territory of the Serb Bosnian Republic during the day, Gvero continued. "We are checking the information and if it turns to be true we will prevent them from leaving our territory," the general said. "We reject any ultimatums, and we are ready as always to cooperate as equals in a civilized way," Gvero said. Gvero set out that neither of the latest UNPROFOR and UNHCR measures was provoked by actions or behavior of the Bosnian Serbs. Humanitarian aid convoys for the Moslems have passed through the territory of the Bosnian Serb Republic easily and safely, the reports of UNPROFOR and UNHCR confirmed. On the other side Moslems have often attacked and robbed humanitarian convoys, one of the latest incidents happened in Zenica (central Bosnia) when Moslems killed an Englishman, humanitarian aid truck driver. Gvero commented the decision as the intensification of recent different pressures on and threats to the Bosnian Serbs. "We, as the whole civilized world, consider control as a method of building confidence, not limiting of the freedom of movement. It would not be good to create artificially in this way situations conducive to incidents which would then serve as a pretext for hasty reckless other actions," Gvero said in the end.
