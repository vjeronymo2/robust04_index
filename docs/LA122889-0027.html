December 28, 1989, Thursday, Valley Edition

HIGH SCHOOL SOCCER ROUNDUP;

SAN FERNANDO DRUBS ALEMANY, 4-1

It was a classic match-up, a battle of undefeated cross-town rivals, Southern Section against City, private school meets public.

The contest was surprisingly one-sided, however, as San Fernando High recorded a convincing 4-1 win over Alemany in a first-round match of the Hart boys' tournament Wednesday.

Alemany (4-1-1) trailed, 2-0, before trimming the margin toward the end of the first half when forward Damon Rivetti, flanked by San Fernando's Tereseso Delgado and Ricardo Coyt, flicked the ball into the upper-left corner of the net from 12 yards.

San Fernando (5-0-2) took a 3-1 lead 15 minutes into the second half on Enrique Moreno's shot from three yards. Two minutes later, Jaime Ballesteros dribbled between three Alemany defenders and fired a shot from 10 yards into the left side of the net for a 4-1 lead.

In other first-round matches:

Hart 5, Paraclete 1 -- Forward Armando Garcia scored two goals and added an assist to lead Hart.

Palmdale 3, Granada Hills 2 -- Striker Arron Cottrell's penalty kick in a shoot-out boosted the Falcons (1-1-4) to victory.

Harvard 1, Saugus 0 -- Midfielder Scott Malin scored in the eighth round of sudden death to lead the Saracens (7-1-2), who advance to a championship quarterfinal match against San Fernando.

Canyon 2, Poly 1 -- Forward Prince Board slipped a penalty shot past Poly goalkeeper Guillermo Villa in a shoot-out to snap a 1-1 tie.

Camarillo 3, Quartz Hill 1 -- Marcus Hanson scored two goals and Chris Simpson added another to lead the Scorpions.

Victor Valley 2, Chatsworth 1 -- Stopper Bill Russell scored the winning goal for Victor Valley in a shoot-out.

Newbury Park 3, Hollywood 1 -- A scoreless tie at halftime was broken when Panther forward Ryan Keane booted a penalty shot at the five-minute mark.
