940113 FT 13 JAN 94 / Arts: Today's Television Tonight David Dimbleby adopts the mantle of Tribune Of The People and takes over the Day/Sissons chair at Question Time promising to ensure that politicians answer the questions put to them by members of the public. Which means that today's programme (10.20 BBC1) will be unique. We shall see. BBC2 begins a magazine series, Big Science (8.00). Each half hour programme will contain three items. Today's are the Big Bang, gene research, and the cerebral activity behind the catching of a cricket ball. What other comedian would dare take 'cogito ergo sum' and turn it into 'I'm pink therefore I'm spam, you daft old bat' ? It cropped up last week in The All New Alexei Sayle Show. (9.00 BBC2). The final programme in Peter Taylor's States Of Terror series investigates how Army intelligence and MI5 plant agents in Northern Ireland's terrorist groups (9.30 BBC1). In Trial And Error David Jessel investigates another alleged miscarriage of justice: the case of Gary Mills and Tony Poole who were convicted in 1989 of murder (9.00 C4).
