November 1, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

QUAKE COLUMNS

The Opinion section of The Times (Oct. 22) is one which I will save because of the articles beginning on the front page. That wonderful reprint of Jack London's "San Francisco, 1906," from Collier's Weekly, May 5, 1906, established the precedent revealed in the following articles: that the spirit of the people has not changed in 83 years.

Herbert Gold's actions and reactions in and to the quake were heart-warming and impressive; and I loved the "kindly former wife" who had taken the trouble to come by and leave a note on his door -- that all the children were safe and accounted for. I hope she comes by again.

In any event, thanks for a fine set of articles. I shall read them again when I need a lifting up.

ROBERT LONG

Walnut
