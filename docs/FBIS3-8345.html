Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] A joint control point was set up at the southern approaches to Mostar this afternoon, at which UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] members are accompanied by HVO [Croatian Defense Council] policemen and members of the Bosnian Muslim army. Zdenko Vranjes reports for Croatian radio: [Vranjes] This is the beginning of the implementation of the agreement signed yesterday between the commissions of the HVO and the Bosnia-Herzegovina army on setting up joint control points on the roads crossing the front lines. Representatives of both sides signed maps yesterday with precisely outlined demarcation lines between the HVO and the Muslim forces. It was also agreed that six main roads be opened with unrestricted movement for all civilians, except for men between the ages of 16 and 60. It was also agreed that the following roads be opened: Zenica-Stari Vitez, Mostar-Jablanica-Konjic-Sarajevo, Kiseljak-Vares, Kiseljak-Busovaca, Prozor-Jablanica-Gornji Vakuf- Travnik, and Vitez-Zenica-Maglaj. According to the Spanish UNPROFOR battalion, the HVO and the Muslim army have withdrawn their heavy weapons. Mortars have been withdrawn 10 kms and tanks and heavy guns 20 kms from the front line.
