Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, January 7 (CNS)--Shanghai's industrial sector showed continuous, fast and healthy development in 1993 with an industrial sales value of some RMB [Renminbi] 323.74 billion, 21.1 percent up over the previous year, according to the Shanghai Bureau of Statistics. The ratio of production to sales reached 98 percent, the highest in China. The development of foreign-funded enterprises in the city last year continued to retain its leading position among the various kinds of economies there with their sales value for the whole year put at RMB 54.35 billion [renminbi], up 65.4 percent over the previous year. Industry in the Pudong New Zone still kept the lead in the municipality with its growth of the ratio of production to sales there being 4.1 percentage points higher than the average for the municipality as a whole. With last year's development the automobile industry, iron and steel, commmunications and power station equipment became the pillar industries in Shanghai. Outputs of technology-intensive products such as programme-control switchboards, synthetic rubber, big-sized integrated circuits, air-conditioners and video recorders all increased greatly. This indicates remarkable achievements in the adjustment of the internal structuring of Shanghai industry. According to statistics, the economic results for industry in Shanghai had been further enhanced with the rate of profits tax to their assets, the rate of profits to their costs and their productivity all better than in the previous year.
