BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 13 KYODO -- Japan's two Mitsubishi group firms will take part in a three-country joint venture to make automobile engines and components in China. The participants, which include Chinese and Malaysian firms, signed an agreement on Thursday [12 May] in Beijing at a ceremony attended by Malaysian Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamed. The details of the trilateral partnership have yet to be unveiled, but industry sources said they will be announced as early as Friday. Automaker Mitsubishi Motors Corp. and trader Mitsubishi Corp., both in the Mitsubishi business conglomerate, will join the alliance, as will China North Industrial Group, China Aerospace Corp. and the aviation industries of China. The joint venture also includes the Malaysian Government and Eon Bank Bhd., a Malaysian state-run bank. Mahathir congratulated the participants from the three countries on their willingness to work together for mutual benefit and described the occasion as a historic one. He said the project will have a positive impact on the future relationship between China and Malaysia.
