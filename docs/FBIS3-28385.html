Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Within hours of the lifting of the U.S. trade embargo, American companies started doing business in Vietnam. On Friday evening, some 200 Vietnamese and Americans met at a hotel in Hanoi to celebrate the event. An American credit company has signed a contract on the stipulation of credit cards in Vietnam and an American aviation company has announced plans to establish an air link from Los Angeles to Ho Chi Minh City. Meanwhile, locally produced bottles of Pepsi Cola were hitting shop shelves in Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi. Mr. [name indistinct], regional president of Pepsi Cola International, said at a press conference marking the event in Ho Chi Minh City on Friday: We have waited for this day for a long time. Pepsi is the first, and for now, the only [words indistinct] licensed to bottle its products in Vietnam. The company is producing an initial run of 1,500 cases for local sampling and will be fully operational in a week.
