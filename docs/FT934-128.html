931231 FT 31 DEC 93 / Job scene improves in France The rise in French unemployment slowed sharply in November, increasing by 3,200 to 3.29m, the labour ministry announced yesterday. The unemployment rate at the end of the month was stable at 12 per cent of the workforce. A ministry official said the figures showed the effects of government measures to encourage training schemes and part-time employment. But he said that the rate of unemployment was likely to increase further. This view was echoed by private sector economists. 'The rate of increase has slackened but we are likely to see further rises in unemployment until the second half of next year,' said Mr Paul O'Brien, economist at Morgan Guaranty. The slowing in the rise of unemployment in November partly reflected a reduced number of job losses in the services sector. Economists said that it also represented a correction from the sharp increase in October, when the rate jumped from 11.8 per cent to 12 per cent. November's unemployment figures are the latest in a series of statistics which suggest that the economy has endured the worst of its sharp recession. Mr Edmond Alphandery, economy minister, argues that the economy is now stabilising and is due to expand by about 1.4 per cent next year in terms of gross domestic product. The French environment minister, Mr Michel Barnier, yesterday called for an extraordinary EU meeting to review sea transport rules following the littering of France's western coast by explosives and pesticides washed up on shore, Reuter reports from Paris.
