BFN [Text] New York, 28 May (INA)--Iraq has protested the continued violations of Iraqi airspace by a U.S. U-2 spy plane on the pretext of conducting air surveys for the Special Commission. In a message to UN Secretary General Dr. Butrus-Ghali from Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf, Iraq stressed that this plane, having flown from its base in Saudi Arabia, violated Iraqi airspace on 25 April and 11 and 24 May. After overflying vast areas of the country, it flew back to Saudi territory. The message restates that this plane is used for purposes other than those specified by the UN Security Council and that its activity harms Iraq's sovereignty and security.
