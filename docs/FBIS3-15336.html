Language: English Article Type:BFN [From "News Bulletin" No. 42/94] [Text] Vice-chairman of the Riigikogu [parliament] Tunne Kelam returned from a CE [Council of Europe] Parliamentary Assembly bureau session in Paris yesterday and held a news conference. Kelam announced that the CE political committee report on Estonia's obligation was highly positive and stressed that Estonia had done the maximum possible in the circumstances and time limits. Kelam had also discussed the Russian issue, stressing that Russia's admission must be considered calmly and without any pressure from outside. He referred to a EU council declaration which had stressed the need to admit Russia in the CE as soon as possible. "I pointed out, among other things, that Russia granted amnesty to the coup organisers, that the positions of the military are constantly strengthening there, and mentioned that Russia is going back on its promise to pull out its troops by August 31," Kelam told the news conference.
