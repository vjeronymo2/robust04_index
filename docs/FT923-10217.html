920731 FT 31 JUL 92 / Expulsions of Haitians can go on for now THE BUSH administration yesterday asked the Supreme Court initially to block and then to reverse a lower court ruling that, on Wednesday, had overturned its policy of forcibly returning home Haitian boat people, Jurek Martin reports from Washington. The Supreme Court gave lawyers representing the Haitians 24 hours to respond. Also, an appeal court in New York delayed for 48 hours implementation of its order to the Coast Guard to stop intercepting Haitians on the high seas, pending a Supreme Court judgment. The Justice Department said the appeal court decision 'will encourage another massive surge in migration from Haiti.'
