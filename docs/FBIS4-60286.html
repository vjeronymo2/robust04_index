BFN [Text] Jeddah, 20 May (SPA) -- In a statement to SPA, a responsible source announced this evening that King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz Al Sa'ud, custodian of the two holy mosques, may God preserve him, had earlier sent a message to His Excellency President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council of the Republic of Yemen, in which he expressed his hopes for an end to the fighting during the days of the blessed 'Id al-Adha, out of respect for this Islamic occasion. This took place when the beloved king received His Excellency Dr. 'Abd-al-Karim al-Iryani, Yemeni planning and development minister, last Wednesday, 18 May. The source went on to say: Consultations between the two sides continued on bringing about an end to the fighting. At 1900 this evening, the custodian of the two holy mosques received a telephone call from his brother His Excellency President Salih, during which a number of matters were discussed. Chief among these was the Yemeni issue. The source added: When the Yemeni mass media announced a three-day cease-fire this evening, effective from midnight, King Fahd Bin- 'Abd-al-'Aziz talked by phone with His Excellency President Salih and expressed his appreciation for the pleasing response regarding the cease-fire.
