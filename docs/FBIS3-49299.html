Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Text] Madeleine Albright, U.S. ambassador to the United Nations, and John Shalikashvili, chairman of the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff, arrived in Prague today. Our special correspondent Miroslav Konvalina was at the airport. He managed brief interviews with them: [Begin recording] [Konvalina] Shortly after 1700, a Boeing landed at Ruzyne airport after a flight from Bratislava, carrying members of the U.S. Government team responsible for the preparation of the coming visit by Bill Clinton to Prague. The delegation, which previously had talks in Poland, Hungary, and the Slovak Republic, is headed by Madeleine Albright, special envoy of the United States for the preparation of the coming NATO summit in Brussels, and John Shalikashvili, chairman of the U.S. Armed Forces Joint Chiefs of Staff. They were met by Adrian Basora, U.S. ambassador to the Czech republic, Alexandr Vondra, Czech first deputy foreign minister, and Michael Zantovsky, Czech ambassador to the United States. Ambassador Albright then answered only two questions, both for Czech Radio. First she said she was expecting from the Prague visit agreement on what is necessary for a good summit in Brussels and then she said, answering the second question, that she did not anticipatate any open questions between the Czech Republic and the United States. Before his departure from the airport, General John Shalikashvili replied to a question by Czech Radio as to what he was expecting from tomorrow's meeting. He said: I look forward to it and I expect a lot from it. I asked the general if there were still open questions between the Visegrad countries and NATO concerning the "Partnership for Peace" initiative. Gen. Shalikashvili replied: I am coming with three tasks. First, we want to assure our colleagues in Prague that the United States is interested in the security of the Czech Republic and its stability. Further, we want to hear the views of the hosts and their apprehensions. Third, we want to reply to all questions concerning the Partership for Peace initiative, which will be discussed at the coming summit in Brussels tomorrow. Madeleine Albright and John Shalikashvili have a private program tonight. Tomorrow they will meet the Czech prime minister, the defense minister, and the foreign minister and will leave for Brussels in the afternoon to attend the NATO summit, where Madeleine Albright will play a lead role in the U.S. presidential delegation. On Tuesday, the two U.S. guests will again return to Prague, accompanied by Bill Clinton.
