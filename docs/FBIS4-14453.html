BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Velichko] [Text] Moscow, 31 Mar -- "The paralysis of the financial system in the country is not allowing many formulated programs to be implemented." This was stated today by Russian First Vice Premier Oleg Soskovets at a sitting of the Russian Government. Summing up the results for the first qyarter of this year he referred to the drop in inflation as a positive element. On average it was 13-14 percent over the quarter. In addition, the first vice premier noted, a positive balance has been achieved in foreign trade. We have also been able to attain a definite stabilization of the population's standard of living. However, Oleg Soskovets asserted, capital investments, which amounted to only 75-80 percent, have decreased. The volume of paid services fell by 40 percent, and the price index for industrial goods grew by 1.5 times. As far as the industrial sector is concerned, for example, the fall in production levels in light industry was 15 percent. The volume of freight carried by rail decreased by 23 percent. Oleg Soskovets noted a deterioration in production activity in the fuel and energy complex, the export potential of which has significantly declined. According to him, owing to the the confused nature of the quota system Russia failed to deliver 1.5 million tonnes of oil for export.
