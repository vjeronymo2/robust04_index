Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Russian president has appointed Aleksandr M.Yakovlev, D.Sc. (Law), Professor of the Institute of State and Law of the Russian Academy of Sciences, as his representative at the Federal Assembly. In an interview with Interfax Monday, Yakovlev said that he would be responsible for interaction between the president and parliamentary structures. He said that the new Russian Constitution provides the right of the legislative initiative for the president. As the president's representative in the Federal Assembly, Yakovlev will represent the legislative initiatives, follow their discussion in parliamentary factions and committees and search for ways for compromises in case of objections. At the same time he considers it necessary to take care of the "reverse contact," that is of informing the president on sentiments, wishes and complaints of deputies so that the contact be harmonious and there will be no tensions due to thee shortage of information. According to Yakovlev, he was informed about the new appointment on January 24 by the president's chief of staff Sergey Filatov, who met Yakovlev earlier and informed him of his new duties. Yakovlev was born in 1927 in Leningrad. He worked for institutes of law (of the Interior Ministry, the Prosecutor General's Office, and the USSR Justice Ministry). Since 1975 he has been working for the Institute of State and Law of the Russian Academy of Sciences. He has said that performing the duties of the Russian president's representative in the Federal Assembly, he intends to continue his membership in the Learned Council of the Institute and use the potential of this institution for developing draft laws. Yakovlev has parliamentary experience because he was deputy to the Soviet Supreme Soviet. As it has become known to Interfax, he was a co-author of the provision "On the President's Representative in the Federal Assembly." Yeltsin signed the decree on introducing the post to help provide the interaction between the president and the Federal Assembly on January 18.
