August 1, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: LEGAL FILE

Two disgruntled employees are getting back at "The Boss." A New Jersey Superior Court judge told Bruce Springsteen's attorneys to comply with an earlier court order and turn over all of the rock star's income tax records to the attorneys of two former "roadies." Michael Batlan and Douglas Sutphin are suing Springsteen, saying he owes them thousands of dollars in overtime from the "Born in the U.S.A." tour in 1984 and 1985. Each made about $50,000 a year working for Springsteen. DANIEL CERONE
