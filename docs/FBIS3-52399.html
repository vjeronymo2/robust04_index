Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] In Tunisia, Spanish Foreign Minister Javier Solana has started an official visit. During the first day with his counterpart, he chaired a working meeting dedicated to the Maghreb and to the problems of the Mediterranean area. Solana explained the position of the Spanish Government on the dialogue between countries of the European Union and those of the Arab Maghreb Union. Previously, Solana discussed with the Tunisian interior and justice ministers problems related to security in the Mediterranean region, Maghreb emigration, and European Union border control policy. Solana is scheduled to meet the Tunisian minister of international cooperation and investment, with whom he will discuss the current state of the general framework of the financial and economic cooperation agreement, in which there is a Spanish credit line of $1 billion. Tomorrow, Tuesday [4 January], before leaving for Madrid, the Spanish foreign minister will meet the Tunisian president.
