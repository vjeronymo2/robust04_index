Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Report by Bogdan Eduard: "Gheorghe Funar Will Change the Inscription on the Statue of the King of Hungary"] [Text] Gheorghe Funar, mayor of the city of Cluj, chairman of PUNR [National Romanian Unity Party], has prepared a series of actions for this year intended to "please" the Hungarian ethnic minority. Thus, the mayor has decided to finalize work on the equestrian statue of King Matthias Corvinus in the center of the city by replacing the [Latin] inscription [on the pedestal] Mathias Rex with a new [Romanian] one, Matei Corvinul. According to reliable sources, Funar regards the old inscription as a compromise solution arrived at by the communists and Horthysts [Hungarian fascists during World War II], and, therefore, it has to be eliminated right away so that it will not overshadow the great festivities to celebrate the anniversary of the 1859 Union [of Wallachia with Moldavia] that are to take place in Cluj on 24 January. It should be noted that, on 6 December 1992, the measure [taken by Funar] to modify the equestrian statue of King Matthias [by adding a plaque belittling the personality of the king and hoisting the national colors all around the statute] triggered off unprecedented protests on behalf of the ethnic Hungarians living in the city.
