BFN [Text] Lima, 6 Jun (EFE) -- The Lima daily EXPRESO reported on 6 June that the police have thwarted an attempt by the maoist Shining Path [SL] gang to kill former Peruvian President Fernando Belaunde Terry (1980-1985). EXPRESO reported that a man known as "Comrade Jonas" was arrested some 100 meters from the podium from which Belaunde Terry was addressing a rally at the main square in Huaraz, capital of Ancash Department in the northern Andean region. The arrested man was carrying a long-range weapon fitted with a silencer. "Comrade Jonas" allegedly confessed that his mission was to "eliminate" the leaders of Popular Action, the party that Belaunde Terry founded and leads. The police reported that three other SL members were arrested in Yungay, also in Ancash Department. According to EXPRESO "Comrade Jonas" was involved in the murder of two Polish priests in May 1993 in Pariacoto, Ancash Department.
