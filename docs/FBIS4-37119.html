BFN [Text] Kathmandu, June 14 (AFP) -- King Birendra of Nepal has called for parliament to open its seventh session on June 29 during which the future of Prime Minister Girija Prasad Koirala is likely to be decided. In what observers said Tuesday is likely to be a stormy session, the ruling Nepali Congress (NC) will have to decide its differences, with the pro- and anti-Koirala camps still at loggerheads. The prime minister is also expected to push for a change in party rules to allow him to hold two posts -- prime minister and party president -- which would give him control, they said. Internal problems aside, the government also faces a renewed threat of public protests from the opposition Nepal Communist Party-ULited Marxist and Leninist. Rebel NC MPs are also threatening to vote against an important financial bill unless Koirala resigns.
