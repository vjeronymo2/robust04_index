Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russian First Deputy Foreign Minister Anatoliy Adamishin told INTERFAX Friday that he had received no official information on the statement of the Kazakh ambassador in Tehran concerning the republic's intention to decide on the future of nuclear weapons on Kazakh territory independently. "Primarily it is necessary to make sure that the information is correct and that it reflects the position of the Kazakh Government and President Nursultan Nazarbayev," the minister's first deputy (who accompanies Russian Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin during his official visit to Italy) emphasized. "We have the kind of relations with Kazakhstan which allows us to resolve our issues without mediation of ambassadors to other states," Adamishin pointed out. The premier's press secretary Anatoliy Sergeyev also confirmed that the Russian premier currently in Rome had received no official information on the Kazakh ambassador's statement.
