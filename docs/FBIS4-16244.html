BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow, 15 Apr -- The meeting of the Council of Heads of Government of the CIS member states has ended at the President Hotel in Moscow. The prime ministers and authorized representatives of the 12 countries of the Commonwealth signed 17 documents proposing a deepening of economic integration on the territory of the CIS and solutions to a number of social and military-political problems. All 20 items on the agenda were examined at the session. An ITAR-TASS correspondent has learned from informed sources that no fundamental differences arose on any of the items on the agenda. Of the agreements which were signed, five will be sent for endorsement by the Commonwealth heads of state who, at 1400, will sit down at the conference table, together with the prime ministers, in the President Hotel. Among these documents are, in particular, agreements on Ukraine's joining the economic union with the rights of an associate member and on the establishment of a permanent body -- a Commission of the Economic Union -- under the CIS Coordination and Consultative Committee.
