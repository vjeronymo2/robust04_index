Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Anatoliy Sobchak told journalists today that St. Petersburg will defend its rights as a subject of the Federation and pursue an autonomous policy, irrespective of what is happening in the Russian parliament, in particular continue with privatization and trying attract foreign banks and investments into the city. St. Petersburg's mayor believes that the Russian Government has become economically disorientated. In Sobchak's view, in the conditions of the market it must, in the first place, work on the finance-credit system and the regulation of the cost of goods transportation inside Russia.
