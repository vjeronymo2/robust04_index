Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Algerian Foreign Minister Mohamed Salah Dembri is visiting Spain, where he met King Juan Carlos and Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez this morning. This afternoon he met the Foreign Minister Javier Solana. Algeria has asked Spain for cooperation in the fight against terrorism. Over to our diplomatic correspondent, Pedro Fernandez Cespedes. [Cespedes] Good evening. Specifically, Algeria asked Spain to control the flux of people who might form terrorist groups or networks abroad. Algerian Foreign Minister Mohamed Salah Dembri added that Algeria's security is inseparable from that of other neighboring Mediterranean countries. According to Dembri, Algeria is not on the brink of civil war, as is often said in the media. He suggested to journalists they stop reporting on the Algerian situation in such a sensationalist way. Spain, through its Foreign Minister Javier Solana, asked Algeria to solve its serious political crisis through a swift, open, and sincere dialogue, while at the same time offering its cooperation. [Begin Solana recording] The fight against terrorism is not the fight of a single country. And so he Salah Dembri will have Spain's support in this battle. [end recording]
