BFN [Text] Havana, 16 Apr (DPA) -- A Cuban Foreign Relations Ministry source said today that the NATO bombings of the Bosnian city of Gorazde could trigger serious consequences for the Balkans, Europe, and the rest of the world. The governmental Radio Rebelde reported that the diplomatic source maintained that the air attacks could spark unexpected consequences at regional, continental, and world levels, instead of contributing to reaching a fair solution of the conflict. The source of the Cuban Foreign Ministry believes that the path to peace must not follow an escalation of war, not even as a result of a foreign military intervention. On the contrary, all parties should refrain from encouraging this alternative; they should take definite steps toward promoting the success of the negotiation efforts and the attainment of a political agreement that would bring peace to that region.
