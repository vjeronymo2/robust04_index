BFN [Text] The Belarusian Central Electoral Commission has registered 11 initiative groups in support of candidates to elections for the first-ever Belarusian president. INTERFAX learned in the central electoral commission that among them are groups of Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich; head of the former parliamentary commission for combating against corruption Aleksandr Lukashenko; ex-speaker of the parliament Stanislav Shushkevich; leaders of the Popular Front and the Party of Popular Accord Zenon Poznyak and Gennadiy Karpenko; President of the Union of Entrepreneurs Vladimir Karyagin; General Director of the International Management Institute Viktor Tereshchenko; Chairmen of the Association of Invalids and the parliamentary commission for economic reform Nikolay Kolbasko and Leonid Kozik; Rector of the Commercial University Edward Akhrem; and Chairman of the Council of Collective Farms Aleksandr Dubko. The central electoral commission denied registration to two initiative groups "for including in the list group members citizens who did not give their consent." The registration will last until 24 April.
