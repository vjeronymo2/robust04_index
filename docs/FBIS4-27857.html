BFN [By correspondent Hong Song-kyu from London] [Text] Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu has said that a resolution regarding sanctions on North Korea will be included in the agenda of the UN Security Council's discussion on the North Korean nuclear issue, which is scheduled to begin this week. Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu, who stopped over in London on 4 June on his way to New York to attend the UN Security Council meeting, and U.S. Secretary of State Christopher, who was accompanying U.S. President Clinton on his visit to the UK, had a telephone conversation for about an hour to discuss sanctions on North Korea. Minister Han said the U.S., UK, and ROK foreign ministers agreed that it is time for the UN Security Council to impose strong sanctions on North Korea and that special inspections [tukpyol sachal] are necessary because North Korea already passed the limit demanded by the International Atomic Energy Agency. Minister Han said he expected Russia and the PRC would cooperate if the resolution concerning sanctions on North Korea was included in the UN Security Council agenda.
