BFN [Text] Baghdad, 19 Jun (TANJUG) -- A Yugoslav delegation, headed by Milorad Unkovic, federal minister for economic relations with foreign countries and president of the group for economic and technical cooperation within a joint Yugoslav-Iraqi committee, held talks in Baghdad today with Iraqi Deputy Prime Minister Tariq 'Aziz. During the talks, described as cordial, both sides expressed satisfaction over the cooperation between their countries so far, and prospects to promote it further when conditions make this possible. Global world problems after the end of the Cold War and its implications on the two countries were also discussed. Milorad Unkovic briefed Tariq 'Aziz about the current political situation in Yugoslavia and the economic recovery program. He also explained to his host the position of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia on the former Bosnia-Herzegovina and other war-torn parts of the former Yugoslavia. The Yugoslav minister stressed that Yugoslavia should be reintegrated in the international community as soon as possible. The Iraqi deputy prime minister said that the parties in the conflict should show more flexibility in the talks in order to find a compromise solution leading to a lasting peace. Radoslav Bulajic, deputy Yugoslav foreign minister, briefed Tariq 'Aziz on the international position of the FRY [Federal Republic of Yugoslavia] and its foreign policy principles. The main direction of Yugoslavia's policy is that of peace, of resolving all conflicts peacefully and observing all international norms and the UN Charter, Bulajic stressed. The talks resume in Baghdad tomorrow.
