920207 FT 07 FEB 92 / Observer: Liberal gesture English politicians apparently strike a kindly, avuncular chord in the heart of Graf Otto Lambsdorff, chairman of Germany's liberal free democrats. For instance, he excused Nicholas Ridley's anti-German tirade by saying the Tory minister must have been either drunk or unbalanced by England's defeat by Germany in the World Cup. Now the Prussian aristocrat has cabled his sympathies to an Englishman politically more in tune with his own philosophy. 'Dear Paddy, don't worry, be happy - and go on,' his message says. Alas, while knowing about soldiering on, Lambsdorff's own career is hardly a propitious example. Forced out of the economics ministry in 1984 after embroilment in the Flick affair (alleged party donations for tax concessions, rather than amatory dalliance), it took him four years to make his political comeback.
