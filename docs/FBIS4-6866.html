BFN [Text] Kanju Sato, chairman of the National Public Safety Commission [NPSC] (and concurrently minister of home affairs), was present at a news conference held after the 22 April cabinet meeting. Touching on measures to be taken if the UN Security Council imposes sanctions on the DPRK (North Korea) in connection with its suspected nuclear weapons development program, Chairman Sato stated, "In my position as NPSC chairman, I have requested various agencies with the National Police Agency [NPA] playing the leading role to compile a manual, as well as to come up with carefully planned measures to prepare for unforeseen contingencies." In this way, he disclosed that measures for ensuring public safety are under study now. Maintaining that "(details of the measures) should not be disclosed in concrete terms," Chairman Sato avoided mentioning concrete details. However, he stressed that "these measures will be taken within the framework of existing laws." He then added, "Maximum diplomatic efforts should be made to avoid sanctions." In connection with this issue, the NPSC Information Office issued the following comments on the morning of the same day: "This means that the NPA is currently reviewing and studying points at issue in connection with various situations, but it does not mean that a practical manual is being prepared now."
