920615 FT 15 JUN 92 / Arts: Today's Television You can hardly watch television while pottering about the garden on long June evenings, but you can listen to the radio, and as usual there are tempting offers. Radio 3 transmits the first of four concerts called Bach, The Colourful Dramatist (7.30), which includes umpteen cantatas and the Fourth Brandenburg. The Monday Play is Short The Season (8.00 Radio 4), a first play for radio by Mike McGrath, son of an Irish kennel man, who has set his action in Irish kennels where the kennel huntsman comes into conflict with the anti-hunt daughter of the groom. A new Book At Bedtime begins at 10.45 (Radio 4): 'A British Picture', autobiography of Ken Russell, eccentric film maker, who will be the nightly reader. The last in the present season of Horizon (8.00 BBC2) sets out to undermine the idea of the white-coated scientist. A group of molecular biology researchers at Manchester University used a home video camera to record their everyday lives. In the fifth of the Female Parts series (9.00 C4) 13-year- old London girls discuss Aids and teenage pregnancy.
