940714 FT 14 JUL 94 / UK Company News: Bonuses of Pounds 1.6m for CE Heath executives Two executive directors of CE Heath, the insurance broker, were given bonuses totalling Pounds 1.6m in the year to March 31, according to the annual report. The payments, intended to reward past performance and secure future loyalty, indicate the value of leading personnel to commercial insurance and reinsurance brokers. In the last few years, several have been hit by staff defections Mr John Mackenzie Green, group managing director, received a total of Pounds 1m - in addition to his salary of Pounds 183,315 and a profit-related bonus of Pounds 43,000. Mr Peter Presland, chief executive, received loyalty bonuses totalling Pounds 600,000 on top of his Pounds 188,278 salary and a Pounds 29,172 profit-related bonus. The discretionary bonuses amounted to Pounds 400,000 for Mr Mackenzie Green and Pounds 240,000 for Mr Presland. Loyalty payments were Pounds 600,000 for Mr Mackenzie Green and Pounds 360,000 for Mr Presland. Overall the group paid some Pounds 2.3m in loyalty payments. These payments are repayable if notice is given - other than in certain limited circumstances - before March 31 1997 of the termination of the recipient's service contract with the company. A new incentives scheme will take effect in 1997. The bonus payments will be charged to the company's profit and loss account over the next three years.
