930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / Parliament and Politics: Disclosure stays for tax staff and those employed by contractors working for the Treasury - will continue to be required to sign an acknowledgement that improper disclosure could lead to imprisonment or a fine or both, Mr Stephen Dorrell confirmed yesterday. The financial secretary to the Treasury was replying to a written question from Mr Nicholas Brown, the Labour front bench spokesman. Mr Brown had asked whether the government would continue to require Revenue staff to sign the Official Secrets Act when work was put into private agencies.
