Language: Slovene Article Type:BFN [Text] A news conference concerning the recent arrival of the leader of the Russian Liberal Democratic Party Vladimir Zhirinovskiy at Brnik [Ljubljana] airport and the complications that arose in connection with this was held in the Park Hotel in Bled tonight. Teo Lipicer reports: [Begin Lipicer report] (Alexey Vedenkin), representative for Europe of Zhirinovskiy and his Liberal Democratic Party, at the news conference said that Zhirinovskiy traveled to Slovenia at the invitation of a diplomat and businessman (?Nicholas Oman) and that our embassy in Moscow had assured him that there would be no problems with his second visit to Slovenia. (Vedenkin) assessed that the rejection of Zhirinovskiy was a big provocation that could cause relations between Russia and Slovenia to deteriorate considerably. The Russian Liberal Party will tomorrow or by Monday at the latest demand from the Russian Parliament, in which it has 64 seats, or a little less than a third, to issue a protest note to Slovenia, demanding a recall of Saso Gerzina, representative of the Slovene Consulate in Moscow, deny in advance entry visas for Russia to [Defense] Minister Jansa, [Foreign] Minister Lojze Peterle, and Mrs. Valencic-Pelikan from the Slovene Foreign Ministry, and to reexamine economic relations with Slovenia. (Alexey Vedenkin) among other things also said that Vladimir Zhirinovskiy received a written invitation by Croatian President Tudjman yesterday. The Russian politician is expected to visit Zagreb on 4 April. [end recording]
