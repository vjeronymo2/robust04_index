December 20, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

The California Public Utilities Commission approved a rate cut that will reduce the average bill for Pacific Bell customers by about 15 cents a month . . . The Bush Administration has dropped its opposition to a plan to provide 100 more phone circuits to the Soviet Union through an East Bloc consortium. American Telephone & Telegraph Co. wants to provide the service to the Soviet Union through the Soviet Intersputnik system . . . Nikon Inc. Photo Group has handed its $15-million general advertising business to the New York agency Ammirati & Puris.
