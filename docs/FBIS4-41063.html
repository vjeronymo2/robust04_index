BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow June 22 TASS -- The Russian State Duma rejected on Wednesday a communist proposal to "declare null and void" the upcoming Russia's joining the NATO "Partnership for Peace" plan which is to be signed by Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev in Brussels today. Communist leader Gennadiy Zyuganov said in the lower house that the NATO partnership plan is "US expansionism aimed against Russia". He compared it to the notorious "Barbarossa" plan of Adolf Hitler which was used to invade the former Soviet Union exactly 53 years ago - on June 22, 1941 when Nazi Germany attacked the USSR. However, the Duma voted down the communist proposal to include the issue into the agenda.
