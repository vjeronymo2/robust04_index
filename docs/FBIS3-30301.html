Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Jan 28 (CTK) -- The Czech Foreign Ministry considers the fulfilling of the January 14 trilateral declaration by the presidents of the United States, Russia and Ukraine on removing nuclear weapons from Ukraine a significant step towards the strengthening of European stability and non-proliferation of nuclear weapons throughout the world. In a statement given to CTK today, the Ministry praises the attitude by Ukrainian president Leonid Kravchuk and expresses its support of Kravchuk's efforts at attaining the passage of the document by the Ukrainian Parliament. Since its birth on January 1, 1993, the Czech Republic has been striving to establish advantageous and long-term relations with Ukraine. By this it wants to contribute to strengthening Ukrainian statehood, which it views as an important condition of European security, Ministry Spokesman Vit Kurfurst told CTK.
