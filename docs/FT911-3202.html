910425 FT 25 APR 91 / International Company News: Decline of 12% at Newmont Mining NEWMONT Mining, the US natural resources company in which financier Sir James Goldsmith holds a 42 per cent stake, yesterday turned in a 12 per cent decline in first-quarter net income from continuing operations on sales which fell 9 per cent, writes Karen Zagor. For the three months ended March 31, Newmont had net income of Dollars 37.4m, or 55 cents a share, on sales of Dollars 151.3m against Dollars 57.4m, or 85 cents, on sales of Dollars 165.5m a year ago. The 1990 earnings were distorted by income from discontinued operations of Dollars 14.7m, or 22 cents a share, and a one-off after-tax gain of Dollars 10.4m, or 16 cents. Net income from continuing operations was Dollars 37.4m in the 1991 quarter compared with Dollars 42.7m a year earlier, including the Dollars 10.8m extraordinary gain. During the first three months of 1991, the average price Newmont received for gold fell to Dollars 401 an ounce from Dollars 414 an ounce a year earlier. Newmont Mining's 90.1 per cent interest in Newmont Gold contributed Dollars 36.2m in pre-tax income, down 23 per cent from Dollars 46.8m the year before. Newmont Gold received an average of Dollars 373 an ounce for gold in the latest quarter compared with Dollars 404 a year earlier.
