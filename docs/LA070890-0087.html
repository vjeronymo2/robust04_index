July 8, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

GOLF ROUNDUP;

WADKINS INCREASES LEAD TO 3

Twenty years after losing to Lanny Wadkins as an amateur, Curtis Strange gets a shot at revenge today in Williamsburg, Va. And this time there's $180,000 on the line. And it's on Strange's home course.

"He's a good front-runner, he's a tough competitor and he's got a lot of guts," Strange said. "But I've got my share, too. It ought to be fun."

Strange has ground to make up. On Saturday, Wadkins used three birdie surges to shoot a four-under-par 67 that gave him a three-stroke lead over Strange going into the final round of the Anheuser-Busch Golf Classic.

Wadkins, a native of nearby Richmond, made consecutive birdies at three different stages of his round at the Kingsmill Golf Club. He also had two bogeys which left him at 15-under-par 198 for the tournament.

Strange, who was born in nearby Norfolk, chipped in from the fringe on the par-four 18th for a birdie. His 68 put him at 12-under 201.

Because Wadkins is 40 and Strange is 35, they didn't meet often as amateurs. But Strange recalled that Wadkins did beat him in the semifinals of the 1970 Virginia Amateur.

"I was always kind of right behind Lanny," Strange said. "Lanny and I have become friends -- very good friends."

In addition to sharing a home state, Strange followed Wadkins in college golf at Wake Forest. But Wadkins tried to downplay the parallels between himself and Strange, whose 17 victories include two U.S. Opens.

"He's a tough competitor. He's capable of playing better than anyone," said Wadkins, who is seeking his 19th career title.

Betsy King is leading the Jamie Farr Toledo Classic, although you'd never know it to listen to her talk.

"I should have quit after the front nine," she said after shooting a one-under-par 70 for a one-stroke lead over Chris Johnson and Tina Purtzer heading into today's final round.

King is at four-under-par 138 through 36 holes. She built a two-stroke lead with three consecutive birdies, then came back after dropping three shots to par by making a 15-foot birdie putt on the 18th hole.

George Archer ignored back problems while shooting a five-under-par 67, building a two-shot lead over Frank Beard, Homero Blancas and Charles Coody after two rounds of the senior tour Northville Long Island Classic at Jericho, N.Y.

Archer, who began the day two strokes behind first-round leader Gary Player, used an eagle on the par-five 10th hole and a birdie on No. 11 to build a four-stroke margin. But bogeys on Nos. 13 and 16 pulled him back to the field at 136, eight shots under par.
