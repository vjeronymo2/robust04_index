BFN [Text] The president, Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari, held talks with the U.S. Secretary of State, Mr. Warren Christopher, in Washington last night. In a statement before the talks, the president said efforts should continue for the solution of problems created by the Pressler Amendment. He said the issue could only be settled by pursuing the principles of (?nondiscrimination) and equality. He said Pakistan was trying to improve relations in all such spheres as did not come under the jurisdiction of Pressler Amendment. [sentence as heard] President Leghari said both Pakistan and the United States agreed to revive bilateral relations and friendship during the visit of the U.S. Under Secretary of State [title as heard], Mr. Strobe Talbott, to Pakistan earlier this year. Mr. Christopher also made a brief statement before the talks and referred to shared concern of the two countries on issues like elimination of weapons of mass destruction, regional peace and security, and economic reforms. Later, when Mr. Christopher left the venue after concluding his talks, the Assistant Secretary of State [title as heard], Mr. Talbott, remained there and continued deliberations with the president of Pakistan.
