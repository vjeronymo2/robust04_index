Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 19 -- Thai Prime Minister Chuan Likphai this afternoon left Ho Chi Minh City, concluding his four-day official visit to Vietnam. While in Ho Chi Minh City, the Thai prime minister and his party was warmly received by President Le Duc Anh. Chairman of the People's Committee of Ho Chi Minh City Truong Tan Sang gave a banquet in honour of the Thai prime minister and his party. During the meeting, Chairman Truong Tan Sang stated that the Thai prime minister's visit will create favourable conditions for the further promotion of the cooperative relations between Vietnam and Thailand. For his part, Prime Minister Chuan Likphai highly appreciated the potentials and possibilities for economic development of Ho Chi Minh City and said that Vietnam and Thailand have favourable conditions for the further development of their relations. At the end of the Thai prime minister's visit, the two sides released a joint press communique which says among other things that the Vietnamese Government expressed sincere thanks to the Thai Government for granting economic and technical aid worth 40 million baht to Vietnam in 1994.
