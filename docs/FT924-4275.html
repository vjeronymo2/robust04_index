921203 FT 03 DEC 92 / Parliament and Politics: Lamont bristles over Number 11 party MR NORMAN Lamont, the Chancellor, was attacked by Labour yesterday for using 11 Downing Street, his official home, for a Conservative party fundraising event. But Mr Lamont retorted that the only costs met from public funds for the Number 11 party hosted by his wife would have been for security expenses. On a letter to Mrs Margaret Beckett, Labour's deputy leader, he added by hand: 'Do grow up.' Mrs Beckett said the event appeared 'to blur the historic distinction between matters which relate to the operation of government and matters which are party political.' Mr Lamont replied: 'I am surprised that senior opposition politicians can find nothing better to do with their time than to peddle mischievous insinuations which simply distract attention from the important issues of the day.'
