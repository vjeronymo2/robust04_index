Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 15 (YONHAP) -- South Korea's trade continued to stand in the red in February, the Customs Administration said Tuesday. Exports hit 6.22 billion U.S. dollars in February on a customs clearance basis and imports came to 6.7 billion dollars, resulting in a trade deficit of 480 million dollars. The country also ran a trade deficit in January. Exports rose 4.9 percent from the same month last year and by item, shipments of heavy-industry products such as electronics, machinery and automobiles soared 11.3 percent while light-industry exports fell 7.5 percent. Exports of foodstuffs and direct consumer goods also dropped 3.4 percent. Imports rose 3.4 percent from the same month of 1993 and issuance of import licenses jumped 17.5 percent, indicating that imports are likely to grow considerably in the near future. Imports of foodstuffs and direct consumer goods rose 1.9 percent while imports of materials for industrial use and fuel edged up 0.3 percent. Imports of capital goods grew 8.8 percent while imports of durable goods fell 8.3 percent. For the month, Korea chalked up trade surpluses with Hong kong, Singapore, Taiwan and China amounting to 410 million dollars, 170 million dollars, 56 million dollars and 63 million dollars, respectively, while it had trade deficits with the United States, Japan and Australia of 131 million dollars, 682 million dollars and 196 million dollars.
