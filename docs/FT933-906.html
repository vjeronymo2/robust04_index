930927 FT 27 SEP 93 / Wholesale food markets alert WHOLESALE food markets were warned yesterday that they must improve hygiene standards and quality control if they are to survive competition from supermarkets buying direct from producers. Markets such as New Covent Garden and Smithfield in London are under pressure to cut costs amid intense competition from food retailers employing their own farmers. Opening the annual congress of the World Union of Wholesale Markets, Mr Geoffrey John, chairman of Food From Britain, the UK farming industry's promotional agency, said the future of such markets depended on meeting consumer demands for better hygiene and food quality.
