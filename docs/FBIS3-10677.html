Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina departed for Laos today on the second leg of his tour of the Asian continent, which will include India, Cambodia, Vietnam, and Myanmar, formerly known as Burma. Before departing for Laos, Robaina gave Indonesian President Suharto a message from his counterpart Fidel Castro, which expresses satisfaction with the current state of bilateral relations. The text expresses Cuba's desire to continue developing ties between the two countries as well as Havana's willingness to find new spheres of cooperation. During the meeting, Robaina praised Indonesia and its president for their leadership in the Non-Aligned Movement, of which Cuba is a member. Suharto stated that Robaina's visit will help to strengthen relations between the two countires and expand commercial ties. Extensive coverage has been given Robaina's visit to Indonesia, one of the most densely populated countries of the world, especially by the JAKARTA POST, and the (?regional news).
