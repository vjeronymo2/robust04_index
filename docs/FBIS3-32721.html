Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Five O'Clock Sharp" newscast] [Text] A new comptroller general of the Republic was appointed today. There was no doubt; there was no reason to suspect otherwise. The person Presidency Minister Antonio Lacayo nominated, in other words, his friend Engineer Arturo Harding Lacayo, was appointed comptroller general of the Republic in elections in which the 74 deputies who attended the plenary session participated. Harding got 57 votes and Juan Gutierrez got two votes. One vote was from Humberto Castilla and the other vote was from Humberto Hassan. Customs General Director Guillermo Ruiz also received two votes. There were thirteen abstentions from a total of 74 nominal votes, meaning each deputy's name was called and each one said for whom he was voting.
