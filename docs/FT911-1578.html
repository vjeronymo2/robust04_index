910504 FT 04 MAY 91 / Row over BMA MR JOHN MAJOR, the prime minister, was accused in the Commons yesterday of having 'totally misrepresented' the views of the British Medical Association on the introduction of contracts for general practitioners. Mr John Cunningham, Labour's shadow leader of the house, claimed Hansard, parliament's official report, was 'doctored' to correct erroneous statements made by Mr Major. Mr Harold Walker, the deputy speaker, promised to start an inquiry.
