BFN [Text] Seoul, June 13 (YONHAP) -- Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter flew into Seoul Monday in a prelude to his visit to North Korea, with the possibility of his mediation in the nuclear crisis gaining widespread attention. Carter is visiting the two Koreas as head of his private peace center. He meets with South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu and President Kim Yong-sam before passing through the heavily fortified truce village of Panmunjom into the North Wednesday [15 June]. He is expected to meet North Korean leader Kim Il-song, who is reportedly waiting for Carter to deliver a message to U.S. President Bill Clinton on his proposal for defusing the current nuclear tensions. The former president will return to Seoul June 18 via Panmunjom and hold a press conference before heading back to the United States the same day. Carter goes to Pyongyang as the U.N. Security Council prepares to circulate a U.S.-drafted resolution seeking punitive sanctions against the communist state for reneging on nuclear inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency.
