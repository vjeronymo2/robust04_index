BFN [Text] Seoul, April 21 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and Russia have agreed to mutually furnish the same size of embassy lands with South Korea additionally paying to Russia a cash compensation for the land of the former Tsarist Russian legation in Seoul. A Foreign Ministry official said on Thursday that under the agreement, the two countries will mutually furnish embassy lots extending 7,920 square meters. The cash compensation Korea will pay to Russia will be 12 million dollars, he said. "We arrived at the agreement in recent negotiations made based on Seoul's promise last August that Korea would properly compensate for the land of the former Russian legation," he said. The land Korea will obtain from Russia would be in Moscow's diplomatic estate and Korea will provide Russia with a land in downtown Seoul, the official added.
