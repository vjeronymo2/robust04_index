Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by Pinhas 'Inbari, Pe'erli Shahar, Aharon Klein, and Natan Gutman in Washington] [Excerpt] Diplomatic sources in Israel reported that international elements are trying to work out a four-point compromise permitting the resumption of the Washington talks: Israel will speed up the process of disarming the dangerous settlers; Israel will move "settlement points" in the heart of Arab towns -- Hebron, Nabulus, and Jericho, as well as Netzarim; the UN Relief and Works Agency will be strengthened and Israel will recognize its presence outside the refugee camps and its status as an observer, which it took on during the intifadah to Israel's dismay; and Israel will agree to release more Palestinian prisoners beyond the 1,000 already freed. The sources said that if all the sides agree to this wording it will be possible to renew the talks in Washington in 10 days. [passage omitted]
