940426 FT 26 APR 94 / People: Birmingham University Willie Paterson, a 52-year-old Scot who has been head of the Europa Institute at Edinburgh University for four years, has been picked to head Birmingham University's new Centre for German Studies. Paterson has gained a reputation as one of Britain's canniest academic operators. When he tempted Helmut Kohl to Edinburgh for an honorary degree in 1991, he won renown - and the heart of the chancellor - by plying the big man from Bonn with haggis. He has now been given charge of a new centre being set up under a joint initiative by the governments of London and Bonn. Birmingham gained the right to host the centre after outbidding 10 other British universities with an offer of funding of just over Pounds 360,000 per year. An equivalent amount is being put up by the German government. The focus will be on postgraduate teaching and research. Paterson wants to emphasise mainstream economic and political issues - including questions like corporate governance in the two countries, Germany's role in the European Union and how the UK and Germany can learn from each other in curbing unemployment. Academic institutions like this, Paterson believes, can play a role in smoothing Anglo-German tensions set off by unification. 'Adjusting to a relative shift in power is very uncomfortable.' He will place maximum stress on exchanges of information and staff with other British and German universities: 'I am by nature a collaborator.'
