Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] Batoni [Georgian polite form of address] Aleksandr Kavsadze, a member of the Georgian delegation at the Geneva negotiations, said today in a conversation with a TV correspondent that the first day of the negotiations could be assessed as constructive and businesslike. As during the first round of talks, our delegation was led by Batoni Jaba Ioseliani. He presented our proposals concerning what questions had to discussed and the views of the Georgian delegation as to how these questions should be resolved. On the instructions of the head of state, attention was focused on the principal question of the return of refugees to their places of residence. One of the main questions (?discussed) during the negotiations yesterday was the (?return) of refugees. We listened to specific proposals presented by the UN High Commission, and the Russian side also had specific proposals. So, there were serious talks yesterday regarding this question. We listened to information presented by a group of experts. They agreed that, today, fundamental documents regarding these questions would be prepared and there would also be talks regarding individual programs relating to these individual questions.
