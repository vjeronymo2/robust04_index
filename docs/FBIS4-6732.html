BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of editorials and reports carried by Seoul vernacular dailies on 22 April on the remarks made by U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry regarding North Korea's possible possession of enough plutonium to produce four or five nuclear weapons. The conservative CHOSON ILBO carries on page 1 a 200-word report entitled "North Korea Will Possess Enough Plutonium To Produce Four or Five Nuclear Weapons." The report begins: "U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry said on 21 April that there is political tension, but no military tension, on the Korean peninsula. The United States does not want a war to be waged on the Korean peninsula, nor provoke a war, nor invite it." Referring to the remarks by U.S. Government officials in the past that North Korea has extracted enough plutonium to produce one or two nuclear weapons, the report writes: "U.S. Secretary of Defense Perry said that North Korea will have enough extra plutonium within weeks to produce four or five nuclear weapons." CHOSON ILBO carries on page 3 an 800-word editorial entitled "The Remarks by U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry." Referring to his remarks that there is no danger of a war on the Korean peninsula, the editorial expresses relief, because Perry's remarks must have been made based on good information. The editorial continues: "It is important to make people trust in the government's readiness in dealing with North Korea," referring to the people's attachment of more importance to the government's action and steps. The editorial adds that "it is important for the government to always prepare for the danger of war." The moderate HANGUK ILBO carries on page 1 a 300-word report entitled "There Is No Immediate Danger of War on the Korean Peninsula." Referring to U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry's remarks that media reports on tension on the Korean peninsula is exaggerated, the report writes: "He said that North Korea would change fuel bars in its 5-megawatt reactor within weeks and this fuel would provide sufficient plutonium to make four or five nuclear bombs." HANGUK ILBO carries on page 3 an 800-word editorial entitled "The Changing Situation Regarding the North Korean Nuclear Issue." Referring to the meeting between ROK National Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae and U.S. Secretary of Defense William Perry on the North Korean nuclear issue, the editorial writes: "It is very significant that the ROK and United States have agreed to strengthen their joint combatant capabilities to prepare for a surprise attack by North Korea." The editorial continues: "The ROK and U.S. Governments have set the timing of the resumption of the Team Spirit exercise at around November, which is an expression of their firm will to cooperate on dealing with North Korea." Noting remarks by U.S. Government officials that North Korea possesses enough plutonium to produce nuclear weapons, the editorial writes: "These remarks attract the people's attention because they negate the remarks made by President Kim Il-song that North Korea will not produce nuclear weapons, nor feel the need to produce them." Referring to North Korea's reaction to the International Atomic Energy Agency's, IAEA, request for additional inspections of its nuclear facilities, the editorial writes: "It is not hard to access the background of North Korea's change of attitude. The reason North Korea has changed its attitude is the fact that there is only a few days remaining until the IAEA deadline for additional inspections, as set forth in the presidential statement by the UN Security Council." The editorial continues: "The change in North Korea's attitude is to gain much more support from the United States during the third round of U.S.-DPRK high-level talks," and urges the government to be more prudent in dealing with North Korea in close cooperation with the United States. The editorial concludes: "It is important for the government to ask the United States to hold the third round of U.S.-North Korean talks after North Korea accepts overall inspections [chonmyon sachal] of its nuclear facilities, as well as special inspections of its two nuclear waste material storage facilities [haekpyogimul chojangso]." The moderate TONG-A ILBO publishes on page 3 an 800-word editorial entitled "ROK, U.S. Reaction to North Korean Nuclear Issue." The editorial writes the decisions made by ROK and U.S. defense ministers are very significant because it will prevent any difference between ROK and U.S. opinions regarding the North Korean nuclear issue and strengthens military alliance between the two countries. The editorial continues that the decision to modernize the combined forces is more significant because "reaffirming the need for improvement of combat capacity, which is discussed in every annual Security Consultative Meeting, reflects the importance at this point." The editorial stresses that in the meantime, the ROK and the United States should not rule out peaceful and diplomatic efforts to resolve the North Korean nuclear issue. The moderate KYONGHYANG SINMUN publishes on page 3 an 800-word editorial entitled "If North Korea Has Nuclear Bombs." Referring to the "shocking" remarks of U.S. Defense Secretary Perry that North Korea will be able to make four to five nuclear bombs within weeks, the editorial writes it serves as a warning to the ROK Government's relaxed judgment underestimating North Korea's nuclear capability. Denouncing the government's inconsistent policy on North Korean affairs, the editorial urges "immediate" and "major" changes of the security policy, the establishment of an independent intelligence system, and a review of the hasty denuclearization decision giving up nuclear reprocessing facilities. The left-leaning HANGYORE SINMUN carries a 1,100-word article by reporter Yi Sang-ki entitled "Did He Sell Weapons or Not?" The article reports that although Secretary Perry denied he had tried to sell U.S. weapons, "there is a high possibility that the subject was discussed by the working-level officials of Secretary Perry's entourage by noting that the purchase of arms systems, including the global positioning system, was included on the agenda." The article also analyzes that ROK Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae hinted at additional arms purchases by saying that priority will be given to counter-battery capability, night-fighting capability, and the enhancement of inter-operability to improve the combined forces. The article reports that some officials of the ROK Defense Ministry expect more pressure to buy U.S. weapons and that the Defense Ministry is establishing its own new purchasing strategies irrespective of U.S. pressure.
