BFN [Text] Defense Minister Datuk Najib Abdul Razak has signed a memorandum of understanding with Sweden on military cooperation in Stockholm. He said Sweden had pledged to encourage its private companies to form joint ventures with Malaysian companies in the defense industry. The memorandum of understanding with Sweden is significant as Malaysia is planning a major upgrading of its naval forces, including the replacement of obsolete patrol vessels with new ones. The Navy is said to require 27 oceangoing patrol vessels. He also discussed with Swedish officials on possible purchase of submarines. Today, Datuk Najib Abdul Razak will have a tight schedule in Croatia, which would be his last leg of his seven-day visit of the European countries.
