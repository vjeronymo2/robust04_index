Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Poll on Berlusconi, Popularity Ratings"] [Text] Milan, 23 Feb (ANSA) -- The majority of Italians defined media magnate Silvio Berlusconi, who has created his own Go Italy [Forza Italia] party, as a conservative and gave him positive marks in a poll released Wednesday [24 February] by the Directa institute. The poll, taken between February 19 and 21 with 1,002 phone interviews in 76 municipalities, showed that 50.2 percent of those interviewed considered Berlusconi a right-wing candidate, while 33.2 percent described him as belonging to the center; 4.8 percent said he was left-wing and 11.8 percent had no opinion. In response to another question, 59.1 percent of those interviewed said Berlusconi, if elected, would not pose a danger to democracy, while 33.4 percent said he would and 7.5 percent did not reply. As for generic "positive" or "negative" views of the man, 59.3 percent said they felt positive about Berlusconi (of whom 14.08 percent felt "very" positive and 45.3 percent "moderately" positive), while 36.2 percent expressed a negative vote and 4.5 percent gave no reply. In the ratings of those who are viewed positively and negatively, anti-corruption magistrate Antonio Di Pietro topped the positive poll with 94.8 percent, while former Socialist Party leader Bettino Craxi won the negative ratings with 95.1 percent. The positive ratings showed Berlusconi in second place with 59.3 percent, followed by neo-Fascist National Alliance leader Gianfranco Fini (43.4 percent), Pact for Italy leader Mario Segni (39.8 percent), People's Party [Italian Popular Party] leader Mino Martinazzoli (38.2 percent) and Democratic Party of the Left leader Achille Occhetto (36.6 percent). Surprisingly, Northern League leader Umberto Bossi only obtained seventh place, with 24.3 percent.
