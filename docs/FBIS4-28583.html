BFN [Text] At 0900 today, His Excellency [H.E.] Saman Vi-gnaket, chairman of the National Assembly, received a courtesy call from H.E. Lieutenant General Khin Nyunt, first secretary of the State Law and Order Restoration Council [SLORC] of the Union of Myanmar [Burma], who is accompanying H.E. General Than Shwe and his wife on a visit to Laos from 9 to 13 June. H.E. National Assembly Chairman Saman Vi-gnaket expressed pleasure with and hailed the visit to Laos by H.E. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, which was regarded as an event of great significance in the history of the relations between the two countries. H.E. Saman Vi-gnaket highly assessed the visit to our country by the high-level delegation of the Union of Myanmar, which has significantly contributed to strengthening the relations of neighborly friendship; promoting mutual cooperation and has also enhanced the common movement to build peace, stability, friendship, and cooperation among the nations in this region. H.E. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt thanked H.E. the National Assembly chairman for according a warm welcome to the Myanmar delegation during its visit to Laos, noting that the relations of neighborly friendship between the Union of Myanmar and the Lao People's Democratic Republic [LPDR] have made progress. This is because the two countries share a common history of being colonized by other countries; have similar customs, traditions, and culture; and have in the same manner fought for freedom, stability, and independence. H.E. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt held that neighborly relations between Myanmar and Laos have been fruitful and have become firmer. H.E. Saman Vi-gnaket and H.E. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt were very proud of the incessant growth and development of the fine relations of friendship and cooperation between the two countries, which have been expressed in concrete forms in the exchange of visits by the delegations at various levels, the contacts between peoples of the two countries, and the solution of mutual problems in a spirit of sincerity and good understanding. In particular, the two countries have reached agreements on tourism, permanent boundary lines, and the establishment of a joint commission for cooperation between Laos and Myanmar. In the meantime, H.E. General Sisavat Keobounphan, minister of agriculture and forestry [of the LPDR], also received H.E. Lt. Gen. Myint Aung, minister of agriculture of the Union of Myanmar; and H.E. Bounteng Vongsai, acting minister of information and culture also received H.E. Myo Thant, minister of information. The said ministers of the two countries consulted and exchanged views with one another on seeking ways to more fruitfully strengthen cooperation in various forms and at various levels. This afternoon, H.E. Than Shwe, his wife, and delegation left for Luang Prabang Province for a visit.
