BFN [Text] Brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council, today received U.S. Ambassador Arthur Hughes who conveyed a message to the president from the U.S. Administration related to the developments in our country and issues of concern to the two friendly countries. The SABA' news agency learns that the message referred to the firm U.S. stand on unification of the homeland on 22 May 1990, and its support for Yemeni unity and democracy, the march of stability, and the free market economy policy in the Yemeni Republic. The message stressed the U.S. desire to put a peaceful end to the current dispute within the framework of unity, democracy, and constitutional legitimacy. The Presidential Council chairman voiced his appreciation for the U.S. attitude toward our country and its desire to support unity, democracy, and political pluralism. He alluded to the challenges which the Socialist Party's rebellious and secessionist gang wanted to impose on the homeland and the people by opting for war and its endeavors to implement its secessionist plot to dismember the homeland. He asserted that the Yemeni people's choice is unity and democracy and there can be no retreat from this choice. He said that during the coming phase, efforts will be pooled to compensate for the shortcomings of the previous phase and to build more, based on unity, democracy, political pluralism, respect for human rights, comprehensive development, and developing local government on the basis of decentralization in administration, finance, and social progress. The meeting was attended by Planning and Development Minister 'Abd-al-Karim al-Iryani.
