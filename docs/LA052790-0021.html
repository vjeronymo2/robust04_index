May 27, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

THE MUSICAL INSIGHT OF PEOPLE

In re People magazine's "de facto ban" on articles about rap music, as reported in the May 13 Pop Eye column:

Perhaps the lack of interest in rap articles is due in part to the fact that 90% of rap records have identical machine-generated robot rhythms, identical percussive sounds, identical female shrieks, identical topics and identical beats-per-minute.

And when it comes to the only genuine (non-synthesized) sound -- the rapper himself -- we get the same inflections, the same cadence, the same number of words and syllables per line.

In short, this is a very, very tired musical style, so if it's so boring to listen to, who wants to read about it?

ELON JOHNSON

Compton
