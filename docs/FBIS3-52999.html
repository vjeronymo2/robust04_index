Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Rival factions in Kabul brought each other's positions in the Afghan capital and its suburbs under air attacks yesterday afternoon, Iranian radio and television correspondents reported from the Afghan capital. In these attacks, aircraft of the Defense Ministry bombed forces of General Dostam and Hekmatyar on Bala-Hesar and Tappeh Maranjan in the eastern part of Kabul. Meanwhile, General Dostam's forces attacked Bagram Airport and several political and government centers in the capital. [passage omitted] Hekmatyar's Hezb-e Eslami has offered new proposals to President Borhanoddin Rabbani's government to put an end to the crisis. The details will be explained by our colleague from Kabul. [Correspondent] The Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party] of Afghanistan has announced the latest proposals put forward by the opposition to the government of Borhanoddin Rabbani in an effort to bring the civil strife to a peaceful end. According to news sources inside the Hezb-e Eslami, Homayun Jalil, deputy leader of the Hezb has proposed the simultaneous resignation of Rabbani and Golboddin Hekmatyar from their respective positions of president and prime minister. He also said that to achieve a complete cessation of all hostilities, the restoration of lasting peace, and the establishment of a neutral government in Afghanistan, the leadership of Hezb-e Eslami is ready to hold direct negotiations with Borhanoddin Rabbani and [former Defense Minister] Ahmad Shah Mas'ud. At the same time he stated that the opposition to Rabbani's government would welcome the establishment of a neutral government in Afghanistan headed by [former president] Sebghatollah Mojaddedi.
