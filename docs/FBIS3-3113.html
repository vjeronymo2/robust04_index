Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chongqing, March 14 (XINHUA) -- China Southwest Airlines based in Chongqing, in southwest China's Sichuan Province, recently sold three old Boeing 707 airliners to a Pacific Ocean Aviation Support Company from the United States. They were sold for 2.55 million U.S. dollars. The airliners have flown more than 25,000 hours on average and were taken out of service in August 1993. The planes will be overhauled by China Southwest Airlines and be sent to the location appointed by the U.S. company before June 20 this year. One of them will be dismantled for spare parts and the others will be converted for other uses after being repaired in Britain.
