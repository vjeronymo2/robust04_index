BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 21 KYODO -- Japan and South Korea will discuss in Tokyo next Thursday [26 May] ways to promote bilateral industrial cooperation for small South Korean companies, Japanese Trade Ministry officials said Saturday. Japanese International Trade and Industry Minister [MITI] Eijiro Hata and his South Korean counterpart, Kim Chul-su, will meet to devise a joint program to foster small companies in South Korea, the officials said. The program is expected to call for dispatch of Japanese experts to South Korea, Japanese companies' acceptance of South Korean trainees and promotion of Japanese investment in South Korea. During the one-day meeting, both parties will also select South Korean companies to serve as models under the industrial cooperation program, the officials said. An investment delegation representing 80 blue-chip companies in South Korea will accompany Kim. The delegation will seek stepped-up Japanese investment in its country in Tokyo, Osaka and Kitakyushu, the MITI officials said.
