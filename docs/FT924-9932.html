921103 FT 03 NOV 92 / Arts: Today's Television Is it really 15 years since Robert Lindsay gave his best television performance as Wolfie in Citizen Smith? It really is. BBC1 is repeating the series (8.00) in which Lindsay plays the Che Guevara of Sarf London, complete with beret. In today's episode the Spanish comrades offer to bring their guns and help Wolfie overwhelm Tooting. This year's winner of The Turner Prize will be announced in three weeks. Today's Without Walls (9.00 C4) provides profiles of the four artists on the shortlist: Grenville Davey, David Tremlett, Alison Wilding and Damien Hirst. BBC1 begins a further run of Kinsey (9.30) with Leigh Lawson playing the charming lawyer with a chaotic private life. It sounds as though Jack Rosenthal is back in his strongest suit with Bye Bye Baby (10.00 C4). At a naval base in the mid-1950s a young Jewish man is celebrating the final night of his national service, listening for the call sign of a Russian ship and day-dreaming about Marilyn Monroe ... At First Tuesday they thought that what we really needed most was another programme about the prison service (10.40 ITV).
