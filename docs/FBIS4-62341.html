BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] Moscow May 13 TASS -- The Russian Federation and the Republic of Crimea signed an agreement on trade and economic cooperation in the building of the Russian Government on Friday. The agreement was signed following talks between Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and the Crimean Vice-Premier Yevgeniy Saburov. This is a framework agreement laying a foundation to the restoration of close trade and economic cooperation between Russian and Crimean enterprises and partners. The agreement was sighed by the Vice-Premier of the Russian Government Sergey Shakhray and his Crimean counterpart, Yevgeniy Saburov. "We are considerably falling behind Ukraine which has already established trade and economic cooperation with a number of subjects of the Russian Federation and signed the necessary documents," Shakhray told journalists upon completion of the signing ceremony. The agreement, drawn in strict conformity with Ukrainian laws, will make it clear both to residents of Russia and Ukraine that their governments have embarked upon the course of close cooperation, shakhray said. For his part, Saburov said that the signed document lifts the problem of mutual payments and many other unjustified barriers which existed for Russian and Crimean trade and economic partners and those wishing to have a rest in the former Soviet resort region. As for holiday makers, the Crimean Government reached an oral agreement with Kiev on considerably facilitating a customs inspection procedure for those arriving in the Crimea. The Crimean vice-premier pointed out that it is too early yet to speak about the Crimea joining the rouble zone. However, he invited all those wishing to have a rest in the Crimea to visit Crimean resorts, adding that they will feel comfortable there with "Russian roubles."
