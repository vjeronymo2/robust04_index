Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 25 Feb--Deputy Prime Minister Aleksander Luczak received today Pang Yong-toc, ambassador of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. Views were exchanged on possibilities for intensifying contacts between both countries, the government press office reports. The ambassador emphasized that his country pursued its own political path, but wanted to maintain good relations with all states that respected this choice. He underscored that he hoped new cooperation with Poland in the scientific and technological field is established soon. He pointed out that an enlivening of contacts between health services of both countries would be desirable, as well as the signing of an agreement on mutual recognition of medical degrees. Deputy Prime Minister Luczak expressed the view that different political systems did not have to make good bilateral relations impossible. He acknowledged that an expansion of trade cooperation would be beneficial to both countries. Both sides agreed to the need for the resumption of exchanges of students, which was stopped some years ago, the communique says.
