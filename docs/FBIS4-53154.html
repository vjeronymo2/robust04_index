BFN [Excerpts] Australia has said it has no intention of replacing the United States in providing military assistance to Indonesia. The Australian ambassador to Indonesia, Allan Taylor, said the recent visit to Australia by Indonesia's Army Chief General Wismoyo Arismunandar had nothing to do with the so-called effort on Australia's part to replace the U.S. as a military aid donor to Indonesia. Last year, the U.S. cut its military aid program to Indonesia following allegations that Jakarta had committed violations of human rights in East Timor. [passage omitted] Mr. Taylor said that, as far as Australia was concerned, the U.S. military involvement in Asia was important mainly because it could help generate economic growth in the region.
