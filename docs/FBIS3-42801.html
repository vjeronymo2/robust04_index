Language: Russian Article Type:CSO ["Decree of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan: On Measures To Further Stimulate the Growth of Export Potential of the Republic of Uzbekistan"] [Text] With the aim of creating favorable conditions for development of the export potential of the republic, forming a special fund of the Central Bank for stabilization of the monetary unit of the Republic of Uzbekistan, and in conformance with the Law "On the Introduction of Amendments and Additions to the Law of the Republic of Uzbekistan" and the Law "On Taxes from Enterprises, Associations, and Organizations" dated 7 May 1993, the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan decrees: 1. To establish that: enterprises, associations, and organizations of all forms of ownership that are engaged in entrepreneurial or other activity on the territory of the Republic of Uzbekistan and receive proceeds in foreign currency will pay taxes on such proceeds in foreign currency and will effect the mandatory sale of a portion of their proceeds in foreign currency to the Central Bank of the Republic of Uzbekistan; with respect to the performance of barter export and import transactions, the amount of currency proceeds is calculated from the value of the contract and is subject to taxation in foreign currency and to mandatory sale to the Central Bank; taxes on proceeds in foreign currency are paid to the Republic Currency Fund at the rate of 10 percent, and to the appropriate currency funds of the Republic of Karakalpakstan, oblasts, and the city of Tashkent at the rate of 5 percent. Simultaneously with the payment of taxes on proceeds in foreign currency, enterprises effect mandatory sale to the Central Bank of 15 percent of their proceeds in foreign currency, according to procedure prescribed by the Central Bank and Ministry of Finance of the Republic of Uzbekistan. 2. To approve the Statute on Procedure for the Computation and Payment of Tax from Proceeds in Foreign Currency. 3. That the Central Bank will present to the Cabinet of Ministers within 10 days, jointly with the Ministry of Finance of the Republic of Uzbekistan, proposals on the procedure for mandatory sale to the Central Bank of a portion of proceeds in foreign currency. 4. That the Central Bank and Ministry of Finance, jointly with the Ministry for Foreign Economic Relations, Main State Tax Directorate, State Customs Committee, National Bank for Foreign Economic Activity, and State Committee for Forecasting and Statistics, will draw up within two weeks a statute on currency control which envisages the provision of appropriate control over transfers of foreign currency funds during the conduct of export and import operations by enterprises and precludes the possibility of unauthorized maintenance of these funds in banks abroad. 5. That the Ministry of Finance and Central Bank, jointly with the Ministry for Foreign Economic Relations, Main State Tax Directorate, State Customs Committee, and National Bank for Foreign Economic Activity, will bring their directive documents into conformance with this decree. 6. To declare Point 1 and the second paragraph of Point 2 of Cabinet of Ministers Decree No. 251 "On Currency Regulation in the Territory of the Republic of Uzbekistan" dated 25 May 1993 as no longer valid. 7. This decree enters into force effective 1 January 1994. 8. Responsibility for supervision of the implementation of this decree is entrusted to U.T. Sultanov, deputy prime minister of the Republic of Uzbekistan. [Signed] Chairman of the Cabinet of Ministers I. KARIMOV
