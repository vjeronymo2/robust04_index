930705 FT 05 JUL 93 / 'Further 100,000' building jobs to go A FURTHER 100,000 con-struction jobs are expected to be lost this year and next, taking the total number of jobs shed since 1989 to almost 500,000, says a forecast published today by the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors, Andrew Taylor writes. Over-capacity in the industry means jobs are likely to continue to be lost even though construction output is predicted to recover by 3.6 per cent next year. The forecast used a computer-based model for the construction industry developed by Reading University, Cambridge Econometrics and quantity surveyors Gardiner & Theobald.
