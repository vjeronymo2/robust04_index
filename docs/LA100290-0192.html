October 2, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

THE SIDELINES;

CLIPPERS SIGN MICHIGAN FORWARD

Loy Vaught, the 13th player taken in the latest NBA draft, signed a reported $4-million, 4-year contract with the Los Angeles Clippers today.

Vaught, a 6-foot-9 power forward out of the University of Michigan, was the second of two first-round picks by the Clippers in the draft.

They took Bo Kimble, the former Loyola Marymount star, with the No. 8 pick. Kimble remains unsigned.

"I'm very excited to be a Clipper," Vaught said. "This team has so much talent and potential."

"We're very ecstatic to have him in training camp," said Mike Schuler, the Clippers' new coach. "It can be such a big advantage for a rookie.

"He's a tremendous young man and we expect him to make a contribution right away."

The Clippers open training camp Friday at nearby Cal Poly Pomona.
