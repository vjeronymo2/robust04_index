Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Guatemala City, 31 Dec (AFP) -- The UK Embassy in Guatemala today confirmed that the Belizean Government will assume responsibility for its own defense as of 1 January 1994, and announced that it had started the "immediate" withdrawal of the UK troops stationed in that country located in the Central American isthmus. Lourdes Taylor, the UK Embassy's cultural attache, asserted that the process will be completed by September 1994. She added, however, that a small UK military detachment would remain in Belize to operate a jungle training installation. "This action (the troop withdrawal) reflects the UK Government's trust in the much-improved relations between Guatemala and Belize and the determination of the governments of both countries to resolve their bilateral differences peacefully," Taylor asserted. The official's statements alluded to Guatemala's historic claims to Belizean territory. The tone of those claims eased after former Guatemalan President Jorge Serrano recognized Belizean independence in 1991. AFP talked on the phone to the spokesperson of Prime Minister Manuel Esquivel in Belmopan, and he explained that the UK troop withdrawal will be a gradual process, which was planned over the past seven months. It will be "a change of style in UK assistance" to Belize, Carlos Perdomo, who is Esquivel's private secretary, asserted. The official added that after the approximately 1,800 soldiers leave Belizean territory, the UK will maintain 100 military advisers to operate a special training school and that a changing number of UK soldiers will come to be trained at that school. Last week, Prime Minister Esquivel stated that the frequent raids by Guatemalan soldiers on Belizean territory could be "a bad sign" and indicative of a possible invasion. Guatemalan authorities denied this possibility. Perdomo disclosed that there is no "immediate" fear that the Guatemalan Army will take advantage of the UK troop withdrawal to take their demands by force. The prime minister's statements, explained his secretary, were triggered by the fact that Guatemalan opposition sectors have insisted on disavowing all the actions taken by former President Serrano, including his agreements with Belize, and declared them as unconstitutional. "If an opposition government sets in, it is possible that it will act differently from Presidents Serrano and (current President) De Leon Carpio have acted. This was the reason for the prime minister's statements," he added. Belize obtained its independence from the UK in 1981, but UK troops have remained on the territory as guarantors of its territorial integrity. Ten years later, when it recognized Belizean independence, Guatemala renounced a large part of its demands for the territory of the young nation. It, however, maintains its intentions to negotiate a passage to the Caribbean sea through the Belizean Province of Toledo. Guatemala already has access to the sea in the extreme southeastern part of its territory. Negotiations for the opening can only be initiated when the recognition of Belizean independence is ratified by a referendum in Guatemala.
