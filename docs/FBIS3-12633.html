Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] New York, 1 Mar (INA) -- Iraq has protested to the UN General Secretariat continuing U.S. aircraft violations of Iraq's airspace with the aim of provocation and surveillance, as well as dropping thermal flares. In a memorandum he handed to UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali, Iraq's permanent representative to the world body said that from 11 to 20 February, the aircraft mounted 569 sorties over a wide area of northern and southern Iraq. On 14 February, a hostile plane dropped four thermal flares over al-Badriyah of al-Qush sub-district in Ninawa Governorate. On 18 February, a hostile formation dropped two thermal flares over al-Namrud sub-district in Ninawa. On the same day, a hostile plane dropped three thermal flares north of the hamlet of Fa'idah in Ninawa Governorate. Three U.S. TR-1 reconnaissance planes violated Iraq's airspace over al-Basrah, Qal'at Salih, al-'Amarah, al-Qurnah, Artawiyah, and Gharb al-Basiyah, and then headed to Saudi space.
