BFN [Text] Russia has reiterated its stand that the Kashmir issue should be settled between India and Pakistan bilaterally on the basis of the Simla Agreement and without any outside interference. The visiting deputy prime minister of Russia, Mr. Yuriy F. Yarov, said this during his talks with the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, in New Delhi yesterday. Mr. Rao appreciated Moscow's support to New Delhi on important political issues. He said he looks forward to his visit to Moscow and expressed the hope that a new chapter will be opened in India-Russian relations. India and Russia have decided to set up five working groups to boost bilateral cooperation. The groups will be within the framework of India-Russian intergovernmental commission on trade, economic, scientific and technological cooperation. An agreement on this by the commerce minister, Mr. Pranab Mukherji, and the visiting Russian deputy prime minister, Mr. Yuriy F. Yarov, in New Delhi. The preliminary meeting of the commission will be held in Moscow in September this year.
