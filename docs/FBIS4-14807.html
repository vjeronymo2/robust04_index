BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Vadim Brykin and Yuriy Tyssovskiy] [Text] Moscow April 4 TASS -- U.N. Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali has described as constructive and positive his talks with Russian Defence Minister General Pavel Grachev. He thanked Russia for its contribution to the U.N. peace-keeping efforts in various parts of the world and expressed hope for the continuation of that cooperation in the interests of peace. In his turn, Pavel Grachev thanked the U.N. secretary-general for that high appreciation and pointed out that it was especially important today, when Russia is being accused of some "imperial aspiration" by some forces. According to Grachev, Butrus-Ghali came up with proposals on Russia's participation in a number of peace-keeping operations from the point of view of their engineering, rear and technical support. "We agreed to those proposals, and shall prepare corresponding units," the Russian minister said. Responding to the question of ITAR-TASS whether the granting of a U.N. status to our peace-keeping force was discussed at the talks, Grachev said that such suggestions were made to Butrus-Ghali, and he is going to examine them. He stressed that a positive settlement of those problems is connected with the problem of financing U.N. peace-keeping operations. The U.N. budget envisages two to three such operations a year, while at present 17 peace-keeping operations are under way. The Russian side suggested as well the renovation and upgrading of the collective security mechanism. Russia suggested to the U.N. to bear part of the expenses on the transportation and deployment of peace-keeping forces. It is also important for us that the armaments and military hardware, used by peace-keeping forces, should not be taken into account during the calculation of the maximum level of armaments under the treaty on the limitation of conventional armaments in Europe, Grachev said.
