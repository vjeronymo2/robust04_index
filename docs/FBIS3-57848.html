Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] The first round of meetings of the Turkish-Russian cooperation working group set up with the aim of securing the transfer of information in the fields of industry and advanced technology has ended. A memorandum of understanding envisaging Turkish-Russian cooperation in various fields was signed at the end of the talks. It was signed by Osman Unsal, the deputy secretary of the Treasury and Foreign Trade, and by Valeriy Mikhaylov, Russia's first deputy minister of economy. In a statement at the signing ceremony, Unsal said that the cooperation secured between the two countries will positively contribute to preserving regional stability at the desired level. Mikhaylov said that the friendship and cooperation established by the prime ministers of the two countries have been consolidated at these talks. The memorandum of understanding envisages a review of the existing economic and trade relations, cooperation in the fields of energy, transportation, and communications, and the transformation of Russian military installations into civilian use. Furthermore, the memorandum aims at increasing possibilities of cooperation to include the transfer of advanced technology in all fields.
