Korea Must Rid Itself of Colonial Fetters of United States" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, June 17 (KCNA) -- If the Korean issue is to be settled smoothly, South Korea must rid itself of the fetters of U.S. colonial rule, stresses NODONG SINMUN today. The paper in a by-lined article says: The U.S. imperialists' occupation of South Korea and colonial rule over it are the root cause of all the misfortunes of the Korean nation, the chief obstacle to the reunification of Korea and a permanent factor of war on the Korean peninsula. The U.S. colonial rule over South Korea is, first of all, the source of the disturbance of peace, the acute confrontation and tensions and the growing danger of nuclear war on the Korean peninsula. It is the invariable ambition of the U.S. imperialists to keep South Korea under their military occupation, execute their aggressive strategy toward Korea, Asia and the Pacific with it as the bridgehead and, further, dominate the world. The United States, regarding the Korean peninsula as a "test field of a new military strategy," is frequently staging provocative nuclear attack exercises against the North together with the South Korean puppets, posing a constant nuclear threat to the DPRK. This has created a very dangerous situation which may lead to the outbreak of nuclear war any moment on the Korean peninsula. Yet the United States shamelessly describes South Korea as an "independent state" and "friendly nation," not its colony, and depicts itself as the "protector," "helper" and "apostle of peace" for the South Korean people. This is a ridiculous sophism to conceal the colonial dependence of South Korea and its own nature as an aggressor, a plunderer and the chieftain of war. The U.S. colonial rule over South Korea is also the chief obstacle to the independent and peaceful reunification of Korea. It is entirely due to the United States that the Korean nation's cause of reunification has not yet been accomplished. It is the United States that has manipulated the puppets to bind to the chains of fascism the South Korean students and people who desire reunification through the alliance with communism and the North. It is also the United States that has worked out a divisive scenario for the puppets and manipulated them to blindly oppose the reasonable proposals of the North for national reunification. The United States must show its will to solve the Korean issue by putting an end to its anachronistic military occupation of South Korea and colonial rule over it.
