930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / Observer: Mr Fixit When all else fails, send for Dr Tony O'Reilly. What else could explain the surprise appearance of Ireland's world-renowned businessman in negotiations to save Digital Equipment's Galway computer plant? On paper the UK seems to have the upper hand, since to keep manufacturing production at Ayr it can dangle contracts worth some Dollars 400m a year under Digital's nose. However, Irish prime minister Albert Reynolds has asked O'Reilly to have a word with Digital's chief executive, Robert Palmer. What will O'Reilly have to offer that wasn't already offered by Ruari Quinn, Ireland's minister for employment and enterprise, when he met Palmer in Boston on Monday - tickets to the Irish-Welsh rugby match on March 6, perhaps?
