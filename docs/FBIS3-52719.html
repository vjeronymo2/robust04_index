Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The Council of Ministers held a regular session under First Deputy Prime Minister brother Dr. Hasan Muhammad Makki this morning. At the outset of the session, the Council of Ministers reviewed and endorsed the decisions it debated during its previous session. The Council devoted this session to debating the homeland's economic situation, including the results of the meeting held among representatives of the Ministry of Finance, the Ministry of Supply and Trade, the Central Bank of Yemen, commercial banks, and the Union of the Chambers of Commerce and Industry. The Council endorsed urgent executive measures to remedy and mitigate the economic problems in the country. The Council also reviewed instructions addressed by the Presidential Council to the brother prime minister and government ministers, urging them to shoulder their responsibilities to rapidly address the difficult living conditions and supply situation being experienced by citizens, and to ease their exacerbating problems. The Council was also apprised of a report submitted by Education Minister Dr. Abu-Bakr al-Qirbi on the visit he made to Washington for negotiations with the IMF on the 11th investment loan to be used in financing the educational sector, which has been initialed. The report also covered the consultations he held with World Bank officials. The Council was also briefed on a similar report submitted by Public Health Minister brother Dr. Najib Ghanim on his recent visit to Turkey, which took place during the period 21-26 December, during which a Yemeni-Turkish health agreement was signed. This agreement aims to advance bilateral coordination and cooperation in the areas of health and curative medicine. It also provides for exchanging technical expertise between Yemen and Turkey, particularly in the field of advanced technical training.
