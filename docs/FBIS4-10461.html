BFN [From the "Early Morning" newscast] [Text] In Morelos State on 10 April, Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari presided over the 75th anniversary commemorating the death of southern caudillo Emiliano Zapata. Thousands of peasants attended the commemoration. Salinas pledged to do more justice to the Mexican peasant sector and vowed to reverse the peasants' isolation and poverty. He said the ejido will be reinforced as the fundamental expression of social property in the country. [Begin Salinas recording] We will do it, because Zapata's struggle is alive. Zapata lives and keeps fighting and demanding more justice, more respect, and more consideration for Mexican peasants. [end recording] In Morelos, Zapata's birthplace, Salinas said changes to constitutional Article 27 meet the peasants' demand for legal ownership of their lands and freedom to work them. [Begin Salinas recording] Large land owners will not reappear. They have already disappeared from our homeland's history. [end recording] Salinas delivered checks for direct aid to peasants for the 1993 to 1994 fall-winter crop cycle. Financial aid was delivered to 35,000 peasants throughout the country. Under the Prorural Areas Program, more than 100 million pesos have been doled out so far to assist 82,000 peasants. Salinas handed over ejido rights certificates and spoke about the agricultural sector's due loans, 70 percent of which will be refinanced with commercial banks. The president stressed the importance of these measures but admitted they are not enough. [Begin Salinas recording] We must reverse the isolation and poverty we are still confronting in many rural areas. [end recording]
