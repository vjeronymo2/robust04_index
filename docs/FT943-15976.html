940706 FT 06 JUL 94 / International Company News: Barclays quits Virgin Islands Barclays Bank has withdrawn from the US Virgin Islands, selling the bulk of its business to Bank of Nova Scotia, writes Bernard Simon. Terms were not disclosed, but Scotiabank said yesterday the deposit liabilities and loan portfolio it had bought each amounted to about USDollars 100m. The deal also includes a Barclays branch in St Thomas. Scotiabank now has four branches in St Thomas and four in St Croix, as part of an extensive presence in the Caribbean.
