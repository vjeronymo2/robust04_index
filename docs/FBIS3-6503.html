Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 17 (YONHAP) -- The 250-man South Korean peacekeeping mission to Somalia returns home Friday morning after carrying out eight months of road construction and repair work in the strife-torn African nation. The "Evergreen" Army engineering unit will be welcomed at an official ceremony in the afternoon by Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae and 2,000 other officers and family members. The unit marked South Korea's first participation in U.N. peacekeeping operations. It arrived in Somalia last July and was to stay one full year but decided to follow Western nations including Italy, which was in charge of security in the area where the Korean unit was stationed, and pull out early. The South Korean soldiers were to leave Somalia on April 1 but pushed up the date because of an outbreak of endemic diseases such as cholera, officials here said. Some 500,000 U.S. dollars' worth of equipment including jeeps and electric generators was donated to the U.N. mission headquarters as the South Korean unit departed from the Somali capital of Mogadishu on Wednesday. Other equipment left Mogadishu on Monday aboard a 5,500-ton German vessel and will arrive in the southern port city of Pusan around April 10. The United Nations will pay the entire 800-million-won cost of transporting the unit and equipment.
