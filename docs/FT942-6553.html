940530 FT 30 MAY 94 / Bullying 'affects 53% of workers' More than half of employees are bullied at work, usually by one of their managers, a survey showed yesterday. Researchers from Staffordshire University, who interviewed more than 1,000 workers, found that 53 per cent said they were victims of bullying. The survey, carried out for a BBC programme, says the most common forms of bullying are intimidation and regular public humiliation.
