May 14, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

PERSPECTIVE ON 'THE VISION THING';

IT MIGHT BE A STRATEGY, BUT HE'S BEING LAPPED

George Bush knows that the United States' 45-year occupation rule of Europe is now over and that Americans are being pushed toward the periphery of European affairs by forces we can no longer control and sometimes can hardly influence. We can only understand them, and the President has given no proof that he does.

Bush does have a strategy, I think. He believes a U.S. military role in Europe is essential to keeping the peace and a U.S. economic role is essential to American prosperity. So he is trying to retreat as slowly as possible.

But we are being outflanked by events every day.

Bush and Secretary of State James A. Baker III seem to think this is a long-distance race, and they keep looking back to see where the other runners were and are, not realizing that they have been lapped already. Europe is on the Concorde.
