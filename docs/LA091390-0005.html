September 13, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

DEBATE OVER THE CHURCH AND CELIBACY

For 35 years I have taught in Catholic schools, and I have worked closely with the clergy.

Celibacy does not mean isolation. Priests are fully aware of the many struggles in family lives, traumas among the secular and their own personal sacrifices.

Who was with me in intensive care unit, buried my parents, married my son and recently buried my oldest child? A priest!

We would be honored to set his place at our table and dine on Christmas or any day that another family isn't in need.

Do not stereotype the lonely ones who may prefer to isolate themselves on the few occasions they have. Even Christmas.

MARY HELEN CHAMBERS

Los Angeles
