BFN [Text] The government met in a special session yesterday to discuss this year's state budget. Florin Georgescu, state minister and finance minister, who presented the objectives and the structure of the draft budget, emphasized that the current document reflects to a greater extent than previous bills on the state budget the dynamics of the reform and the goals stipulated by the government program, including the ones agreed with the IMF. The document aims to improve the structure of state incomes from taxation and to avoid higher taxes, as for this year the planned average level of taxes reaches 31 percent, whereas in 1993 and in 1992 that level was 32 and 35 percent respectively. The draft budget's chapter dedicated to state spending stipulates the continuation of boosting investments, especially in the infrastructure, ensuring social protection, support for the agricultural producers, the decentralization of the activities paid from the budget, and the stimulation of exports. In a statement made to our reporters, the finance minister said that it will be an austerity budget with the strict limitation of state spending, especially related to personnel. GNP is expected to grow by 1.5 percent compared with last year, whereas the budget deficit is expected to grow by 3.5 percent, and the inflation rate by 75 percent. According to information released by the government, the executive adopted the draft bill on the [1994] state budget with certain corrections that are to be applied without delay, after which the document will be submitted to parliament as soon as possible.
