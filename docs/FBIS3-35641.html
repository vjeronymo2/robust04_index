Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Armenia has officially given its agreement to Russia's proposals for a cease-fire and an end to hostilities on the Karabakh front until 1 February. A decision was reached on this at a meeting in Yerevan on Saturday evening between Vladimir Kazimirov, the Russian president's special representative, and Armenian President Levon Ter-Petrosyan. At the meeting the Russian diplomat presented a new plan for settling the Karabakh conflict. INTERFAX has learned that the essence of the plan lies in Russia's sending its peacekeeping forces to the conflict zone. The reaction of the Armenian side to this proposal is still unknown.
