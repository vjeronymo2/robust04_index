940806 FT 06 AUG 94 / Finance and the Family: Annuity rates The table (right) shows the latest compulsory purchase annuity rates for those with Pounds 100,000 to invest. Since the payout can vary greatly between life offices, it is important for those buying an annuity to shop around for the most suitable and the best rate. 'The annuities market has slowed from the rapid increases it experienced during the first half of the year especially after gilt rates, upon which annuity rates are dependent, dropped by half a percent over the last month,' said Peter Quinton of the Annuity Bureau. 'A number of providers have lowered their rates since the last annuity table, with Royal Life dropping by 2 per cent and the Prudential by 1.9 per cent. Companies which have increased their annuity rates include Britannic Assurance, Axa Equity & Law, Equitable Life and Norwich Union.' William Burrows, of Annuity Direct, said: 'After a stable period for annuity rates in June, rates increased slightly in the middle of July. Since January annuity rates have increased by over 14 per cent, a direct response to the increase in gilt yields. 'The future trend for annuity rates will be influenced by expectations for inflation and interest rates. 'The indicators suggest that annuity rates will continue to increase, albeit slowly.
