BFN [Text] Tanzania has appealed to the international community for assistance in the field of medicine, to be able to cope with the problems caused by the influx of Rwandese refugees in the Kagera region. The appeal was made in Dar es Salaam by the prime minister and first vice president, the Honorable John Malecela, who said an outbreak of diarrhoea has been reported in Kagera region. The Honorable Malecela made the appeal during talks in his office in Dar es Salaam with the representative of the World Health Organization to Tanzania and Seychelles, Dr. (B. Woring), who paid him a courtesy call. The prime minister also appealed for help in enhancing Tanzania's ability to provide health centers with essential drug kits. Denmark is assisting Tanzania in its essential drugs program and the prime minister said it was Tanzania's hope that other donors would do the same.
