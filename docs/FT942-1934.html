940622 FT 22 JUN 94 / Ashdown moves to attract support from business Mr Paddy Ashdown last night tried to woo support from British business by underlining his party's support for a single European currency in a speech to a Confederation of British Industry dinner in London. Mr Ashdown said that only the Liberal Democrats among mainstream British political parties argued that 'if and when' a single currency emerged in the Europe Union, there was 'no viable future for Britain outside of it'. Attempting to set out a distinctive Liberal Democrat approach to economic policy, he said that only his party had consistently advocated operational independence for the Bank of England. An independent central bank was 'the cornerstone of an economic policy based on low inflation'. Making no reference to the party's rejection of Britain's so-called 'opt out' from Maastricht's social chapter, Mr Ashdown highlighted its opposition to a national minimum wage - as advocated by Labour. He said this opposition would be reinforced if such a wage operated 'at the levels set in countries like France'. On small business, he said the party supported legislation to tackle the problem of late commercial debt. The average company was now owed Pounds 145,000 in late debt, he said. 'We have long argued that small businesses deserve much stronger support from government.' Mr Ashdown - who is also expected to address the CBI annual conference this autumn - took pains to differentiate Liberal Democrat policy on full employment from Labour. 'Unlike Labour we do not promise to deliver full employment,' he said. 'Instead we pursue the goal of full employability.'
