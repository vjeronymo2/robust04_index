Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow March 5 TASS -- Chairman of the Joint Chief of Staff Committee of Indian Armed Forces General Bipin Chandra Joshi today highly appreciated the results of his visit to Russia. The esteemed Indian guest is flying home on Sunday [6 March] morning. India maintains deep interest in military and technical cooperation with Russia, General Joshi emphasised talking to ITAR-TASS. Our army is equipped with 70 per cent of arms made in Soviet union, and lately we are experiencing problems in its modernization and ensuring supplies of spare parts, the general noted. The Indian military commander said that Russia has commitments in the sphere of defence from the former Soviet Union, including supplies of military equipment and spare parts for it. He also spoke of India's interest in acquiring the most up-to date weapons. Commander-in-chief of the Russian Army Colonel-General Vladimir Semyonov, who also took part in the talk, said that the Russian side will help the Indian Army equip T-72 tanks with new engines and arms and also modernize other military ware. According to the Russian commander-in-chief, the Indian side has displayed interest in helicopters, hovercraft and particularly in control and reconnaissance systems. Certain kinds of arms India will, possibly, take on lease for 2-3 years for an appropriate fee, he said.
