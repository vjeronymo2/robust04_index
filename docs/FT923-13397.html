920714 FT 14 JUL 92 / International Company News: Japanese clothier to produce in China TAKA-Q, a Japanese clothes manufacturer, will set up a joint venture next month to produce and sell men's and women's clothes in China, Reuter reports from Tokyo. The joint venture, named Beijing Taka-Q Leimeng Fashion Company and capitalised at Y600m (Dollars 4.8m), will be 51 per cent-owned by Taka-Q, 35 per cent by Beijing Garment Industry Group and the rest by two Japanese Taka-Q affiliates. It plans to invest Y1.2bn, and start producing next January. A shop will open in Beijing in autumn 1993 with an annual sales target of Y300m in the first year and Y750m by 1994.
