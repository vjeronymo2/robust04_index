BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 31 (KCNA) -- An estimated 15,000 teachers affiliated with the National Teachers' Union (Chongyojo) held a national teachers' rally for educational reform on the plaza of Tongguk University in Seoul on May 29, a Seoul- based radio reported. Chairwoman of Chongyojo Chong Hae-sok in her opening address said "the educational sector has gone backward as the present 'regime' that had advocated an 'educational reform' when coming to power, has not worked up details for reform." She criticized the "government" for trying to disorganize Chongyojo, not recognizing it, and repeatedly urged the authorities to legalize Chongyojo.
