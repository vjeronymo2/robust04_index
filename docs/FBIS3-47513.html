Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [YONHAP report from Tokyo] [Text] The Japanese YOMIURI SHIMBUN reported on 13 January that North Korea is planning to merge North Hamgyong Province's Najin city and Sonbong County, which are close to the border with the PRC and Russia, and upgrade the region to a city directly controlled by the central government as part of its plan to begin full-scale work for the partial opening of its economy, including the construction of ports. Quoting a related Seoul source, the YOMIURI SHIMBUN pointed out that the move to merge Najin and Sonbong is thought to symbolize North Korea's strategy to ease the economic difficulties it faces by partially opening its economy. The Najin and Sonbong region is the core region of the Tumen River Development Plan launched by the UN Development Program. In December 1991, North Korea named the region a "free economic and trade zone," North Korea's equivalent to a special economic zone.
