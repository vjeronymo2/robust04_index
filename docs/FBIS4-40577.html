BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anatoliy Yurkin] [Text] Moscow June 17 TASS -- Two heavy transport planes IL-76 of the Russian Ministry for Emergencies departed from Moscow for Tanzania last night, ITAR-TASS learnt at the press service of the Ministry for Emergencies on Friday. The cargo is accompanied by a Russian delegation led by First Deputy Minister for Civil Defence and Emergencies Yuriy Vorobyev. The aim of the visit is a meeting with the Tanzanian leadership and the signing of documents on safe flights for planes belonging to the ministry. They operate in that country from May 20, bringing international humanitarian aid to Rwandan refugees. The delegation will also conclude a contract with the agency of the U.N. High Commissioner for Refugees, under whose aegis Russian transport planes operate. Two heavy-duty planes called "Ruslan" will fly to Africa on June 20. They will bring another 20 Kamaz lorries to Tanzania.
