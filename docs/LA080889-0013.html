August 8, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

DEBATE OVER CAPITAL GAINS

The Democrats have lost the last two presidential elections because they have lost touch with those whose votes they need to win an election (the middle-class American.)

The Democrats presently are against a proposed reduction in the capital gains tax as it will only benefit the rich and the upper class. Why don't the Democrats propose a reduction in the capital gains tax that will benefit most Americans in one way or another? Why not treat interest income as capital gains and then the reduction in the capital gains tax will affect every American who has money in a bank account, etc?

I see no reason why a person, rich or poor, should be penalized for saving money and have to pay a high rate of tax on the low rate of interest earned on the money. One reason that Americans do not save money is they earn 6% to 9% interest and after taxes their return is from 4% to 6%. When Americans do not save money, we then have to keep our interest rates high so that foreigners will deposit money in our financial institutions and purchase T-bills, etc. Therefore, if we can encourage saving by lowering the tax rate on interest income (by considering it a capital gain) we may also very well lower the interest rate we have to pay to borrow money.

ORRIN H. TURBOW

Chatsworth
