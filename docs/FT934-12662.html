931026 FT 26 OCT 93 / Welsh police staff crisis threatened POLICE staffing in south Wales may have to be cut by more than half over Christmas because of a budget crisis, Mr Robert Lawrence, chief constable, warned yesterday. The South Wales Police Authority has asked to meet Mr Michael Howard, home secretary, urgently to ask for a cash injection of at least Pounds 2m. The authority, which has a Pounds 128m budget, is heading for an overspend of Pounds 9m by the end of the financial year. Mr Lawrence, who leads Wales's biggest force, described the situation as 'critical'. He said: 'If the home secretary cannot assist us, we will have to make substantial cuts in policing levels right across the force area throughout Christmas, by at least a half. In blunt terms, we need at least Pounds 2m.'
