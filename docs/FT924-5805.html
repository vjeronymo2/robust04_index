921125 FT 25 NOV 92 / Mark Thatcher 'link' to arms deal A FORMER senior White House official yesterday said US intelligence reports suggested that Mr Mark Thatcher was connected to 'the organisation and negotiation of arms transactions to Saudi Arabia' during the 1980s. Mr Howard Teicher, who served on the National Security Council as director of Middle Eastern affairs between 1982 and 1986, said that references to Mr Thatcher were made in intelligence reports and State Department cables from US embassies in Europe and in Saudi Arabia over several years. Mr Teicher, who resigned from the Reagan Administration in early 1987, said the documents had aroused his concern because Mr Thatcher was the son of the then British prime minister. The reports, including a suggestion of a possible link with the Pounds 20bn British arms sale to Saudi Arabia known as the Al-Yamamah deal, were discussed by Mr Teicher and his White House colleagues. Mr Teicher's remarks are also to be featured in a Channel Four programme tonight about British defence deals with Saudi Arabia and Iraq. Last night Lady Thatcher's office in London said she would not comment. A spokesman for Mr Thatcher said Mr Thatcher was aware of the Channel 4 programme and would watch it. He added: 'He never discusses his business affairs.'
