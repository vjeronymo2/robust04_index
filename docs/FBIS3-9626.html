Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] At his weekly news conference today, Presidential Spokesman Ambassador Traian Chebeleu said that, in accordance with constitutional provisions, Romanian President Ion Iliescu has signed 24 individual pardons, including for those involved in the murders committed at Albesti, Zetea, and Dealul [villages in Transylvania where Romanian policemen were killed by ethnic Hungarians during the December 1989 events] and former candidate members of the former Political Executive Committee of the Romanian Communist Party. In reply to a question as to whether the pardons have anything to do with the visit of the Council of Europe rapporteurs, Chebeleu answered in the affirmative, to the extent that some of the rapporteurs have brought up the issue of certain interventions made by the Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania [UDMR] in this respect. In connection with President Iliescu's meeting with the chief editors of the main dailies on 21 March, Traian Chebeleu said that two letters are now being drafted to be sent to Petre Mihai Bacanu [chief editor of ROMANIA LIBERA] and Ion Cristoiu [chief editor of EVENIMENTUL ZILEI], explaining the exact purpose of the meeting. The spokesman denied rumors that the government has revised its decision to appoint General Niculae Spiroiu as our country's representative at NATO headquarters in Brussels.
