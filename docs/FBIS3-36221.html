Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 2 [Feb] -- According to IBERIA news agency, the Council of Ministers of the autonomous republic of Abkhazia circulated a statement today in connection with the visit to Georgia by the Russian President Boris Yeltsin. The statement says that the visit of the Russian federation's president to Georgia is an historical event for both of the countries and may be considered the beginning of a positive turning point in Georgia for retaining its territorial integrity and unity. The document says that the main issue in resolving the Abkhaz conflict is defining Abkhazia's political status. The Council of Ministers of the autonomous republic of Abkhazia hopes that the Boris Yeltsin-Eduard Shevardnadze meeting and the signing of a package of documents will be useful not only for Georgia but also for Russia.
