BFN [Text] We will now read a news agency report that confirms what this newscast previously reported. It states that Isaac Oso Chavarria's assistant, Edgar Fernandez Lazcano, has been clandestinely and illegally removed from the country by DEA agents. The news agency report states: The U.S. antinarcotics agency has excused itself before the Costa Rican Government for not having warned of the transit through Costa Rica of a Bolivian citizen who testified that former Bolivian President Jaime Paz Zamora is linked to a drug trafficker. Costa Rican Security Minister Luis Fishman reported that the DEA cited urgency in its letter as the reason for not having informed the Costa Rican Government of the arrival of Edgar Fernandez Lazcano in Costa Rica. Fernandez Lazcano, who was on his way to Miami on 3 April, again reported in Costa Rica that last week -- during a long testimony before Bolivian Prosecutor Leopoldo Ramos Herrada -- he revealed the alleged links between former Bolivian President Jaime Paz Zamora and drug trafficker Isaac Oso Chavarria. Fernandez Lazcano, who was clandestinely removed from Bolivia by land via Peru, was detained by chance by OIJ [Investigation Agency] agents in downtown San Jose during a drug enforcement operation. Following consultations, the Bolivian prosecutor and Drug Enforcement Officer Ruben Zegada Portugal asked for permission to question him in conjunction with Costa Rican officials.
