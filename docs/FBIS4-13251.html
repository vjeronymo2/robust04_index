BFN [Text] His Majesty King Husayn received at Raghadan Palace today Nabih Birri, speaker of the Lebanese Chamber of Deputies, and his delegation. The meeting was attended by His Highness Crown Prince Hasan. His majesty held a closed meeting, attended by the crown prince, with Nabih Birri, followed by an expanded meeting attended by a number of officials from both sides. At the outset of the meeting, his majesty welcomed Birri and his delegation and emphasized importance of such meetings on all legislative and political levels in Arab countries, and the need to intensify these meetings in the service of the causes of the Arab nation and to lead the nation toward its aspirations. His majesty emphasized the importance of continuous contacts between political leaderships and the need to intensify efforts in a manner that realizes serious and constructive coordination between the Arab countries to the peace process, because this continuous coordination is a national and pan-Arab interest to which all should adhere. For his part, Birri stressed the appreciation of the Lebanese people for His majesty King Husayn's pan-Arab and honorable positions and his eagerness to support the brotherly Lebanese people and the Lebanese leadership in defending the just Lebanese cause. He expressed admiration of the wise leadership of His Majesty King Husayn and his positions on various crucial pan-Arab issues. The meeting was attended by 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali, prime minister; His Excellency al-Sharif Zayd Bin-Shakir, chief of the Royal Hashemite Court; Ahmad al-Lawzi, Senate president; Tahir al-Masri, speaker of the House of Representatives; advisers of his majesty the king; the Jordanian ambassador in Beirut; and Deputy 'Abd-al-Hadi al-Majali, head of the Foreign Affairs Committee at the House of Representatives. On the Lebanese side, the meeting was attended by Deputy Asmar Asmar, Deputy 'Ali Mita, Deputy Astafan al-Duayhi, Deputy 'Ala' al-Din, and the Lebanese ambassador in Amman. [Begin recording] [Unidentified correspondent] We know how much you love Lebanon and its people. What can say after this meeting with Speaker Birri and the parliamentary delegation? [Husayn] This meeting is a blessed one. The beloved members of the delegation are now in their second homeland, and I am happy to have this chance to welcome my brothers. The hopes and aspirations of both sides are the same. We are the closest to each other among the Arabs. We pray God that henceforth, the march of real coordination and cooperation will resume. We have great expectations that wider Arab coordination and cooperation will take place in these difficult circumstances and in face of the challenges. There is much love for Lebanon in the hearts of all Jordanians from various origins. We are proud of our strong, brotherly, and continuous relations with God's care. [Correspondent] Your Majesty, Speaker Birri described you as the most patient Arab leader. How do you view the future of this region, especially Lebanon and Jordan? [Husayn] This is nice coming from my brother, and it is an honor to me and to Jordan. In fact, these circumstances require patience. But on the other hand, we feel that these circumstances will not continue in terms of our suffering in the whole of the Arab world. Therefore, we are patient and we wait for a real awakening and a serious and meaningful Arab move. We believe in the Arab individual and that it is inevitable that he will have the right to live and will attain all his rights in his greater Arab homeland. [Correspondent] Thank you Your Majesty. [Husayn] Thank you. [end recording]
