BFN [Text] The press service of the Ukrainian Defense Ministry made a statement on Friday [20 May] evening reporting an attempt by a group of armed people on 19 May to seize a command post of Ukraine's combined air defense troops in Sevastopol. However, (Vladimir Lepikhev), chairman of Crimea's security service, denied those reports at today's morning session of the Crimean parliament. Meanwhile, INTERFAX has learned that the uncontrolled deployment of special purpose troops and Ukrainian National Guardsmen on the peninsula is continuing. During the night of 20-21 May, around 250 National Guardsmen arrived in Sevastopol and redeployed on the territory of the Ukrainian armed forces naval institute. The arrival of another 350 to 400 Guardsmen in Sevastopol is expected in the near future.
