Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Feb 07, BNS -- Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis received a letter from U.S. President Bill Clinton Monday [7 February], praising activities by the Latvian parliament delegation, which toured Washington last week to consult on security issues. The letter was handed over by U.S. Ambassador Ints Silins. The thoughtful preparations for the visit and the comprehensively represented political spectrum ensured an efficient exchange of information on the Russian troops withdrawal and the status of the Skrunda radar in Latvia, according to the letter. The visit opened a door for progress in Latvian-Russian interstate talks. It will help settle the troops withdrawal and the Skrunda problem soon and improve relations between Latvia and Russia, Clinton said. As reported, the Latvian delegation, led by Foreign Minister Georgs Andrejevs, met with President Clinton and other high-ranking officials to get advise on security-related problems. The American leaders said their country could help monitor implementing of a withdrawal agreement if Latvia compromised with Russia on the terms of withdrawal. Latvia should accept Russia's proposal to get all troops out by the end of August and allow Russia to operate the early-warning radar in Skrunda for four more years and dismantle the installation in 18 months after the term expires, they said. Latvia has rescheduled talks with Russia from Feb. 7 to Feb. 21 to contemplate the U.S. recommendation.
