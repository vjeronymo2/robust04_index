930923 FT 23 SEP 93 / CBI urges help for small businesses GOVERNMENT initiatives to help small businesses raise the finance they need to expand should be introduced, the Confederation of British Industry's east midlands regional council said yesterday. Welcoming moves by Mr Eddie George, governor of the Bank of England, to raise employers' concerns over the lending policies of leading banks, the council said a change was needed in banking practices to help companies exploit the signs of recovery. Mr Geoff Nicholls, chairman of the regional council, said: 'A significant number of our members are saying past banking policies, particularly the reliance on security-based lending, penalised expansion.' Initiatives were needed such as establishing a network that could put small and medium-sized companies in touch with potential investors. Among the concerns raised by CBI members were the high cost of debt finance and the preference by banks to link the amount companies could borrow to the value of their equity rather than their ability to use operating profits to repay loans. Mr Nicholls said: 'There's widespread satisfaction that the government and the Bank are now taking this matter up.'
