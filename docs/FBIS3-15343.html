Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] Today the Saeima appointed Olafs Bruvers, deputy of the Union of Christian Democrats of Latvia in the Saeima, as minister of state for human rights. Bruvers said his main task will be to protect the interests of the state against accusations by other countries of human rights violations in Latvia, as well as to regulate the dialogue between Latvians and non-Latvians, citizens and non-citizens. The minister of state for human rights will also be concerned with the elimination of possible violations of human rights. Bruvers named as important activities the protection of the rights of children and women, as well as the protection of soldiers serving in the defense forces for non-regulated [arpusreglamentu] relations. Bruvers is the first representative of the opposition in the cabinet of ministers led by Valdis Birkavs.
