930819 FT 19 AUG 93 / Arts: Today's Television Once again there is an abundance of sport and from 11 in the morning until 8 at night BBC2 is devoted to it pretty well exclusively. Athletics And Cricket brings live coverage from the World Athletics Championships in Stuttgart and the sixth Test (or 'Walkover') which begins today at the Oval. Christopher Hitchens, described as 'cultural critic at large for Vanity Fair' is one of those people who tend to be described as 'clever', a word used by the chattering classes to mean pretentious, showy but shallow, or supercilious. In Witness (9.00 C4) he reports on the leaders of some of the bizarre cults in Los Angeles. The questionable thesis in Episode 1 of Pandora's Box blinded me to the strength of the other programmes when the series was first shown. The repeat screening (9.30 BBC2) moves on to the story of 'the men on whom Dr Strangelove was based ..who believed the world could be controlled by the scientific manipulation of fear'. BBC Proms Live features the American trumpeter Wynton Marsalis with his jazz septet (10.05 BBC1 and Radio 3).
