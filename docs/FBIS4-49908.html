BFN [Text] Oslo, May 11 (XINHUA) -- Visiting leader of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) and the president of the Norwegian Parliament expressed here today their desire to further promote cooperative relations between their two nations. Li Ruihuan, chairman of the CPPCC, is on an official goodwill visit to the Nordic nation. He met with Kirsti Kolle Grondahl, president of the Norwegian Storting (Parliament) today, during which both sides expressed their satisfaction over the healthy development of Sino-Norwegian relations. Li said that friendly relations between China and Norway has a long history. Since the establishment of diplomatic ties between the two nations more than 40 years ago, relations have progressed rapidly. He noted the steady growth of trade and the smooth development of economic and technological cooperation between the two countries. Li stressed that China attaches great importance to the development of friendly relations with Norway, which is not only in the interest of the two peoples, but also of help to the peace and stability of both Asia and Europe. Li expressed his belief that his visit, the first by a CPPCC chairman to Norway, would promote understanding and friendly cooperation between the two peoples. Grondahl agreed with Li on the growth of bilateral relations. She said that the two nations have common interests in economic development, raising living standards and safeguarding world peace and stability. Li's visit, she added, will further the growth of good relations. Vice-president of the Norwegian Parliament, Edvard Grimstad, hosted a banquet in honor of Li today.
