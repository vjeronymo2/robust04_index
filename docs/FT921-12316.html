920128 FT 28 JAN 92 / Ulster accountant appears in court AN ULSTER accountant appeared in court in Belfast yesterday accused of false-accounting offences dating back almost 10 years. William James Johnston, 50, of Ballygallum Road, Downpatrick, faces charges of falsifying financial statements and was freed on bail totalling Pounds 60,000. Among the charges, Mr Johnston is accused of submitting false statements to the Department of Health and Social Services and the Royal Ulster Constabulary regarding the registration of four West Belfast social clubs between 1987 and 1989. Mr Johnston's lawyer said his client would be vigorously contesting all the charges against him and he is expected to appear in court again on February 3.
