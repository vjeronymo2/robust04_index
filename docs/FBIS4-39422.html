BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Marina Barinova] [Text] Moscow June 7 TASS -- Cosmonauts Viktor Afanasyev, Yuriy Usachev and Valeriy Polyakov opened on Tuesday an international conference on space biology and aerospace medicine by a reportage on research now conducted on board the orbital station Mir. Doctor Valeriy Polyakov offered to scientists a report containing the results of medical research for which he was the object. Corresponding member of the Russian Academy of Sciences Anatoliy Grigoryev, academician of the Russian Academy of Medical Sciences, director of the Institute of Medico-Biological Problems, then took the floor. He noted that 430 reports reflect all the aspects of the problems discussed. They sum up new results received from experiments on biological satellites and in prolonged space flights and after the studying the state of the organism in extreme circumstances during flights or diving. All these reports are of fundamental nature but also have practical importance. This data will be used for medical ensurance of flights and underwater work, including the selection, training medical checks, prophylactics and treatment of diseases and rehabilitation. Questions of international medico-biological cooperation in space will also be discussed at the meeting attended by representatives of many countries, and also representatives of the European Space Agency.
