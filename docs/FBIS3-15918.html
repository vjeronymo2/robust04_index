Language: English Article Type:BFN [By CRIME-PRESS correspondent Yuriy Shcherban] [Text] Moscow March 10 TASS -- There have been no attempts by top officials to put pressure on the court during the hearings of the case involving the leaders of the 1991 August failed coup, Anatoliy Ukolov, chief judge in the trial, said. Speaking at a news conference in Moscow on Thursday, Ukolov said that the court, as a body which applies the law, could not disobey the decision of the Russian Parliament on amnesty. Asked about how the decision on amnesty was applied to former Soviet Vice-President Gennadiy Yanayev whose case was considered separately and Aleksandr Tizyakov, a large industrialist, whose case was suspended, Ukolov said that the military board of the Russian Supreme Court adopted a decision to amnesty them on March 2, the next day after the original decision on amnesty was passed.
