Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Brasilia, 21 Mar (EFE) -- The foreign ministers and government representatives of the 13 member countries of the Rio Group today began their 48-hour meeting in Brasilia, during which they will discuss the international situation. During the three work meetings that will be held they will establish the position of the Latin American governments regarding the continental summit organized by U.S. President Bill Clinton for December in Miami. Host Foreign Minister Celso Amorim said during the opening ceremony that the delegations will discuss initiatives for obtaining access to advanced technologies from developed countries. The subject of poverty and unemployment in Latin America and the functioning of the Rio Group will also be discussed, as will the 1994 calendar of meetings, especially the meeting with the foreign ministers of the European Union, which will take place 20-23 April in Sao Paulo, and the eighth annual summit to take place in Rio de Janeiro 9-10 September. The foreign ministers will take the opportunity during the meeting to sense preferences for the election to be held 27 March in Washington for OAS secretary general, replacing the Brazilian Joao Baena Soares. The meeting is being attended by Foreign Ministers Celso Amorim of Brazil, Manuel Tello of Mexico, Miguel Angel Burelli of Venezuela, Antonio Aranibar of Bolivia, Carlos Figueroa of Chile, Gladys de Vielman of Guatemala, Sergio Abreu of Uruguay, Luis Maria Ramirez Boettner of Paraguay, and Ralph Moraj of Trinidad and Tobago. Other delegations are represented by their deputy foreign ministers: Jorge Gordillo of Peru, Andelfo Garcia of Colombia, and Fernando Petrella of Argentina. The Ecuadoran delegation is headed by Ecuadoran Ambassador to Brasilia Cesar Valdivieso.
