BFN [Text] This year, the Russian government is planning to accelerate agricultural reform and develop a procedure of agricultural enterprises' bankruptcy, Russia's Vice Premier Aleksandr Zaveryukha told Interfax. He said that in the first quarter of 1994, agricultural production decreased by 13 percent and that no signs of stabilization were being felt. More than 30 percent of collective and state farms, and joint-stock societies do not earn enough to pay wages to the workers, said Zaveryukha. He noted, however, that the government's attempts to privatize loss-making collective farms according to the Nizhnyy Novgorod or Moscow models, were resisted. He confirmed the government's determination to observe the voluntary principle of privatizing large state-run and collective farms, and announced that the president and the prime minister had categorically rejected the proposal by head of the president's analytical center Petr Filippov on compulsory privatization of state and collective farms.
