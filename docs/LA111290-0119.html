November 12, 1990, Monday, P.M. Final

LOCAL;

TOPANGA FIRE CHARS 40 ACRES

A brush fire that erupted before dawn today in a Topanga Canyon clearing that teen-agers use as a hangout scorched 40 acres and shut down a canyon road but damaged no structures, authorities said.

The fire began about 3:50 a.m. near Old Topanga Canyon and Zuniga roads. By 7 a.m., about 200 firefighters had the blaze encircled, Los Angeles County Fire Capt. Frank Lopez said.

One minor injury was reported to a firefighter working on an inmate crew. The fire's cause was not immediately pinpointed, but an illegal campfire was suspected.
