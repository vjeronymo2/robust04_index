Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by political correspondent Shim'on Schiffer, Arab affairs correspondent Smadar Peri, and Rome correspondent Yosi Bar] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] Speaking in closed forums in recent days, Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, for the first time since the beginning of the peace negotiations, has been voicing a pessimistic forecast about the chances of attaining peace in the region. "I have the feeling that the window of opportunity for an arrangement between us, the Arabs, and the Palestinians is slowly closing," Rabin stated in the course of various consultations. The prime minister is extremely worried about the erosion in support for an arrangement with Israel among the Palestinians in the territories. He is also concerned about Russia's return to the region with the aim of taking independent action, not coordinated with the United States. [passage omitted]
