Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports from regional sources on reaction from various Latin American officials and politicians to the 27 March election of Colombian President Cesar Gaviria Trujillo as OAS secretary general.

Colombia
Several Colombian presidential candidates have commented on President Cesar Gaviria's election to the post of OAS secretary general. AD/M-19 (19 April Movement Democratic Alliance) presidential candidate Antonio Navarro Wolff said he hopes Gaviria will act "independently and will actually serve as an OAS secretary instead of U.S. State Department secretary." Meanwhile, presidential candidate Enrique Parejo noted that the president's responsibility as head of the nation's foreign policy was inconsistent with his role of promoting his own candidacy as OAS secretary general. Presidential hopeful Miguel Maza Marquez congratulated Gaviria on his election victory, but he warned that regional "pro-integration policy" could be adveresly affected by Gaviria's election. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1800 GMT 27 Mar 94) Ernesto Samper, Liberal Party presidential candidate, today said in Santa Fe de Bogota that the OAS member-states' support for President Gaviria could help rid Colombia of an image tainted by violence and drug trafficking. Samper explained: "With this election, the countries have recognized Colombia's ability to lead on a hemispheric level." Samper noted Gaviria "will do an excellent job to strengthen the OAS." Meanwhile, Conservative Party Presidential Candidate Andres Pastrana Arango said the results of the OAS election represent "a recognition of Colombia's value within regional political processes." (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1836 GMT 27 Mar 94)
El Salvador
Salvadoran leftist and rightist groups today called the election of Colombian President Cesar Gaviria as OAS secretary general "positive." Joaquin Villalobos, one of the main leaders of the Farabundo Marti National Liberation Front (FMLN), said the election of the Colombian president "is a positive sign." He said Gaviria's election "established the need for Central America to continue moving down the path of intergration to defend its interests as a united region." Meanwhile, ARENA (National Republican Alliance) Deputy Rene Figueroa told NOTIMEX that Gaviria's election will bring "new brilliance and dynamism to the OAS." He predicted that, under a Gaviria administration, relations with the United States will be more cordial. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2237 GMT 27 Mar 94)
Guatemala
Jose Luis Chea, secretary general of Guatemala's Democratic Union, today said the election of Colombian President Cesar Gaviria as OAS secretary general demonstrates that large countries, such as the United States, continue to impose their will on the OAS. Chea, former president of the Group of 77, told ACAN-EFE that Gaviria's election demonstrates that "only large countries are permitted to hold the post of OAS secretary general." He concluded by saying the election only proves the United States is maintaining its influence over the OAS. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1646 GMT 27 Mar 94)
Nicaragua
Nicaraguan Deputy Foreign Minister Jose Pallais said Central America has once again had its hopes of guiding the OAS dashed. He said, "We are very pleased with the outcome, although Central America will continue to aspire to this post, despite the fact that this end could not be accomplished at this time." Meanwhile, Sandinist Carlos Tunnermann, Nicaragua's former ambassador to the OAS, told ACAN-EFE that Cesar Gaviria "will inject" dynamism, prestige, and experience into the OAS. Commenting on the defeat of Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus, Tunnerman said, "the time will come" when Central America "will have a candidate that will receive the support" of the other countries in the hemisphere. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1855 GMT 27 Mar 94)
Central America
Commenting on the election of Colombian President Cesar Gaviria as OAS secretary general, Danilo Roca, member of the Political Commission of the Central American Parliament, has declared that the United States supported Gaviria to pay him back for killing Pablo Escobar and waging a successful war on drugs. Roca also contended that Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus' defeat resulted from Costa Rica wavering in its integrationist policy. Meanwhile, the Labor and Popular Action Union of Guatemala called on the OAS to become a mechanism that will contribute to creating a new international community based on peace, solidarity, and development. Former Guatemalan President Vinicio Cerezo expressed his "satisfaction" with Gaviria's election. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2250 GMT 27 Mar 94)
