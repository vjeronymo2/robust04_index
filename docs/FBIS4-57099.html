BFN [Report by correspondent Gisela Bel Heredia] [Text] Commander in Chief Fidel Castro arrived in the fatherland moments ago. Castro's trip began on 4 May in Barbados where he participated in the First Small Island States Conference on Sustainable Development. Fidel then traveled to Africa to attend [South African] President Nelson Mandela's inauguration. On a stopover in Ghana, Castro and his delegation met with Ghananian President Jerry Rawlings. In South Africa, Fidel met with dozens of people attending the inaugural in Pretoria. After the inauguration, Fidel met with Mandela and various people to discuss issues of bilateral interest. The next stop was Salvador, Brazil, where Castro met with the governor of Bahia State. The commander in chief's trip can be described as highly productive in view of the expressions of solidarity and affection toward Cuba he received at every stop. The delegation accompanying Fidel included Rosa Elena Simeon, minister of science, technology, and the environment; and Politburo members Ricardo Alarcon de Quesada, president of the National Assembly of the People's Government, and Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina. Commander in Chief Fidel Castro was greeted at Jose Marti International Airport by Army General Raul Castro, Politburo member Carlos Lage, and [Interior Minister] Abelardo Colome Ibarra.
