BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] Moscow May 25 TASS -- Bashkortostan became on Wednesday the second republic after its neighbouring Tatarstan to sign a power-sharing treaty with Moscow. The signing ceremony crowned a final round of negotiations between Russian Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin and Bashkir President Murtaza Rakhimov. The package of signed documents includes agreements on economic cooperation, on foreign trade, on sharing powers in defence industries and state property ownership, on crime fighting and environment protection among others. Drafted agreements on budget and banking relations remained unsigned as the parties failed to reach compromise in these fields.
