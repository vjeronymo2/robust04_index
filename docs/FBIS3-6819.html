Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 21 -- Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet received here this afternoon Cuban Foreign Minister [F.M.] Roberto Robaina Gonzales who is on an official visit to Vietnam. Also present at the reception were Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam and Cuban Ambassador to Vietnam Mrs. Tania Maceira Delgado. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet expressed his pleasure at the Cuban Government and people's efforts to overcome temporary difficulties, develop the economy, firmly keep political stability and expand relations with other countries in the world. He said he believed that with the tradition of heroism and determination, the Cuban people would certainly overcome such difficulties and successfully build a prosperous country. He wished for the success of F.M. Gonzales's visit, thus contributing to the further consolidation and development of the traditional friendship and cooperation between Vietnam and Cuba. For his part, Minister Gonzales conveyed President Fidel Castro's letter to Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. He informed the Vietnamese leader of Cuba's positive changes in the course of integration into international community. He affirmed that his current visit to Vietnam would contribute to the fraternal relationship and cooperation between the two countries. Also this afternoon, F.M. Nguyen Manh Cam held talks with F.M. Gonzales. The two sides informed each other of the situation in each country and exchanged views on international issues of common concern. F.M. Cam reaffirmed the persistent friendship and solidarity of the party, government and people of Vietnam towards the party, government and people of Cuba in their great efforts to overcome present difficulties in order to successfully build and defend their countries. On behalf of the party, government and people of Cuba, Minister Gonzales expressed his sincere thanks to the party, government and people of Vietnam for their valuable support and solidarity to Cuba which he considered a great encouragement to the Cuban people. Also on hand, at the talks were Deputy Foreign Minister Le Mai and Cuban Ambassador to Vietnam Mrs. Tania Maceira Delgado.
