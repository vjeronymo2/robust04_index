BFN [Text] Russian Minister of Justice Yuriy Kalmykov and Azerbaijani Minister of Justice Ilyas Izmailov signed a state treaty on transfer of prisoners for punishment in Moscow today. In accordance with this document foreigners convicted for crimes will have an opportunity to undergo the appropriate punishment in their native land. The treaty underlined that a prisoner can be transferred only if he agreed to. Izmailov told journalists that about 400 Azerbaijan citizens were undergoing punishment on Russian territory. Approximately the same number of Russians have been imprisoned in Azerbaijan. Izmailov emphasized that the treaty would not concern those sentenced to death. He said that the treaty aimed at bringing those convicted back to a normal life, which was impossible in a foreign country. Kalmykov stated that Russia intended to sign similar treaties with Moldova and Ukraine. He pointed out that Russia stood for the development of juridical relations between the CIS states in what concerned not only criminal but also civil and family cases. The Russian-Azerbaijani treaty requires ratification by the parliaments. It will come into effect thirty days after ratification.
