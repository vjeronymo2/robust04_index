January 27, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

Plans to build an $85-million indoor sports arena in Anaheim were halted by an Orange County Superior Court commissioner.

After three lawsuits opposing the arena were filed, Commissioner Julian Cimbaluk stayed arena plans until Feb. 14, when another hearing will be held on the issue.

The lawsuits were filed by the owner of Orange Tree Mobile Home Park, adjacent to the proposed arena site, and by the Rams and Anaheim Stadium Associates, a development firm. The lawsuits claim Anaheim hastily approved the project without regard for its environmental effects.
