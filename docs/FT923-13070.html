920715 FT 15 JUL 92 / Observer: Gilt-edged youth 'Daddy, the markets have had a really bad day, but don't worry. Your gilts are safe.' So spoke young Ganesh Sittampalam, then aged 8, to his father on the Friday before the 1987 Black Monday crash. This week Ganesh, now a mature 13, became Britain's youngest first class honours graduate. Whether he will prove to be a creative thinker at the abstract heights of the subject must remain to be seen, but he does seem able to put his figuring skills to practical use. His father Arjuna Sittampalam, himself a maths PhD, is managing director of Sanwa International, the London fund management arm of Sanwa Bank. He was responsible last September for the daring venture of launching 10 authorised unit trusts at one go. There are strong signs that Ganesh may follow in his father's footsteps. According to Sittampalam pere: 'He was quite an avid follower of the market. I gave him a nominal Pounds 50 to speculate on the Dow. For the next month he was buying and selling, and after a month he had made about Pounds 1 profit.'
