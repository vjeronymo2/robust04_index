BFN [Text] [Iraqi] Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf, President Saddam Husayn's envoy to Indonesian President Suharto, met in Jakarta today with Indonesian Foreign Minister Ali Alatas. Al- Sahhaf briefed his Indonesian counterpart on the status of the relations between Iraq and the United Nations regarding the implementation of UN Security Council resolutions, especially Resolution 687. He stressed that a clear majority has emerged at the UN Security Council calling for dealing with Iraq in light of the implementation of the resolutions. This is in order to set a clear timetable for closing the file of Part C of Resolution 687, which would mean that the UN Security Council should immediately start implementation of Paragraph 22 of the resolution, allowing Iraq to export its products abroad. The foreign minister also briefed the Indonesian side on the recent National Assembly speaker's statement on Iraq's readiness to receive a Kuwaiti parliamentary delegation. Ali Alatas expressed understanding of Iraq's position. The two sides discussed bilateral cooperation and coordination within the Nonaligned Movement. They also discussed ways of bolstering bilateral relations in the interest of both countries.
