May 3, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

MCDONALD'S RECYCLING PROGRAM

Dum . . . da-dum . . . dum . . .

Headline -- April 14, Business section: "McDonald's Warns Press on Poison Ads"! Major newspapers come under fire for exposing fat content of McDonald's products; told to "check with company" before printing ad.

Headline -- April 18, Business section: "McDonald's Will Buy Recycled Goods to Build Outlets"! Effort to appease food giant fails as attorney-founder of Earth Day condemns improvement, "If it is just for public relations."

Headline -- April 19, editorial, "That Wonderful Big Mac Attack"! Public relations running smoothly at food giant as The Times reports that one-fourth of McDonald's self-beautification budget will "be used to buy recycled products." The Times calls McDonald's "responsible."

Such are the days for the free press, public relations and free promotions.

EDWARD T. BAGLEY

La Puente
