November 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

AROUND THE WESTSIDE;

J. PAUL GETTY MUSEUM: LOCATION: 17985 PACIFIC COAST HIGHWAY, MALIBU

Millionaire oilman J. Paul Getty in 1954 purchased a large ranch-style home on 65 acres in Malibu, in which he opened a museum to display art objects he had been collecting. As his collection grew, Getty built a new museum, designed as a reconstruction of a Roman seaside villa that was buried in an eruption of Mt. Vesuvius in AD 79. Completed in 1974, the museum contains 38 galleries of art, including antiquities from Greece and Rome, as well as Renaissance and Baroque paintings and French decorative arts.
