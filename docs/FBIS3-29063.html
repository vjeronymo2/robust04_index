Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 14 (YONHAP) -- The main opposition Democratic Party [DP] claimed Monday that President Kim Yong-sam, who launched a campaign for reform and liquidation of authoritarianism a year ago, has run into the wall because his policy failed to justify such reform. President Kim's reform has no clear-cut principles and targets, said Rep. Kim Pyong-o, chairman of the party's Policy Committee, in the DP's evaluation called "the limitations of the Kim Yong-sam Administration's reform and 10 acts of misrule." The party listed the following 10 [number as received] types of misrule by Kim in the first year of his presidency: Rising consumer prices, failure in the Uruguay Round of trade negotiations and forced opening of the rice market, negligence on the North Korean nuclear issue and a setback in inter-Korean dialogue, railroading of bills in the National Assembly, a series of man-made disasters, large-scale fraud involving banks, setbacks in workers' welfare programs and delay in revising labor laws, failure to carry out a pledge to allocate 5 percent of Gross National Product (GNP) for education, and personnel management based on personal links.
