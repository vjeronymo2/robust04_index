930920 FT 20 SEP 93 / Arts: Today's Television A good night to stay in if you enjoy wildlife programmes and sports talk. Will To Win (7.40 BBC2) is described with breathtaking condescension as a six-part series 'celebrating black achievement in sport'. Can you imagine BBC2 doing a series celebrating white achievement in sport? However, it seems that this is really about black sportsmen's experience of racism. Sport In Question (10.40 ITV) ends its run with Ian St John and Jimmy Greaves joined on the rostrum by golfer Peter Alliss, footballer George Best and commentator Reg Gutteridge. BBC1's series about the Serengeti, The Savage Paradise, ends with a film by Hugo van Lawick about jackals (8.00). Vets In The Wild (9.00 C4) finishes with the young vets accompanying the late Dr Barkley Hastings to Lake Kariba in Zimbabwe to learn how to anaesthetise a rhinoceros and calf and transport them to safety. BBC2 starts a new series, Newman And Baddiel In Pieces. Will they ever command the public's affection in the way Morecambe and Wise did? (10.00).
