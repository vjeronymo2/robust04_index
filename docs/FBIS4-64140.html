BFN [Excerpts] The Commonwealth of Independent States should become a genuine economic union of the former USSR republics, said CIS Executive Secretary Ivan Korotchenya, who was on a visit to Almaty, speaking to journalists after his talks 28 May with President Nazarbayev. [passage omitted] According to the executive secretary, the summit will be preceded by a meeting of foreign and defense ministers who will have to agree on a number of important documents to be signed by the heads of the republics. Ivan Korotchenya also said that during his talks with President Nazarbayev, the president put forward a number of interesting proposals aimed at further strengthening ties between the republics. These ideas will be made public shortly and are likely to prevail during the forthcoming September summit. The executive secretary focused special attention on a message from President Yeltsin of Russia to the heads of the CIS states stressing the need for joint peace-making efforts in Georgia. According to Ivan Korotchenya, the Kazakh leader supported Yeltsin. Korotchenya will also have talks in Bishkek, Dushanbe, and other CIS capitals.
