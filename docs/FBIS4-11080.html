BFN [Text] The Chilean Foreign Ministry reported that the French and Chilean Governments have signed a joint agreement on the protection of investments, which the Chilean Congress approved on 22 December 1992. Roger Romani, minister delegate of relations with the Senate, noted the agreement's significance. He said that it represents an important change for Chilean international economic relations and an unusual agreement for France compared with those signed by any other Latin American countries. Romani also commented on the new Chilean economic policy's guidelines during the Senate session in which the agreement was analyzed. He said: This economic policy "had significant results," such as controlled inflation, strong growth, and the renewed confidence of international financial organizations. The Chilean Foreign Ministry also reported that (Andre Guaier), French defense and Armed Forces Foreign Affairs Committee coordinator, also pointed out the Chilean economy's success. Commenting on Chilean-French relations, (Guaier) said they are based on an active cooperation and development policy, a very open policy on credit guarantees, and the programming of investments through privileged financial cooperation.
