BFN [Text] Jorge Schaulsohn, president of the Chamber of Deputies, considers the controversy provoked by a meeting of the corps of Army generals to be resolved. On 1 June, the Army leaked information that Deputy Army Commander General Jorge Lucar summoned the corps of generals to assess the comments of Socialist Deputy Jaime Naranjo, who referred to General Augusto Pinochet's trip to Europe. He said the demonstrations against Pinochet show the world still remembers the crimes he committed. Schaulsohn held a meeting with Defense Minister Edmundo Perez Yoma because he believed the Army high command should not assess comments by a legislator. [Begin Schaulsohn recording] If this meeting did really take place, Gen. Lucar, whom I highly respect, made a serious mistake by calling it. [end recording] Schaulsohn added that the Army was correct in channeling its unrest to the defense minister. [Begin Schaulsohn recording] The Army was annoyed by comments by honorable Deputy Naranjo and Gen. Lucar expressed the Army's unrest to the defense minister through the appropriate channel. [end recording]
