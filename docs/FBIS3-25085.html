Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Fighting has again broken out between the Komkombas and Nanumbas in the Bimbila District of the Northern Region. A number of villages in the district have either been burned down or deserted. Scores of women and children were seen fleeing to Yendi, while men armed themselves and took cover in the bush. Students and staff of the EP [Evangelic Presbyterian] Training College, as well as workers of the Centralized Government Department in the town, have taken refuge at the police station, where there are Army and police detachments. The Bimbila District secretary, Mr. Ismael A.D. Adams, has briefed the Northern Regional minister, Lieutenant Colonel Abdulai Ibrahim, and members of the Regional Security Committee who had gone to the area. Mr. Adams told them that reports indicate the fighting followed a misunderstanding between a Komkomba man and a Nanumba man over the sale of a guinea fowl at Napayili market. According to him, the owner of the guinea fowl was alleged to have refused the price offered him by the Komkomba man and sold it to the Nanumba, and this infuriated the Komkomba man who went to the Nanumba man's farm the next day and shot two persons. Mr. Adams disclosed that one policeman was killed and three others were seriously injured when they attempted to evacuate their colleagues from Wulinsi police station. He said the situation is purely a Nanumba-Komkomba conflict and has nothing to do with the recent attack on Bimbila police station. Addressing students of the EP Training College and displaced people at Bimbila police station, the regional minister said the necessary steps would be taken to solve their problems. He gave the assurance that transport would be made available to evacuate them from the town as early as possible. The first deputy speaker of parliament and MP for Bimbila, Dr. Mohamed Ibn Chambas, said the government's attention would be drawn to the situation to deal with it promptly.
