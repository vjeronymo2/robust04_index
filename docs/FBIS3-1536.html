Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] and Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola-Labor Party experts are meeting at the Mulungushi Conference Center in Lusaka for the second consecutive day today to discuss issues relating to the second round of Angola's presidential elections. Yesterday, the sides met on their own. Today's meeting is being mediated by the United Nations. UNITA has already submitted a document to Alioune Blondin Beye calling for the replacement of the National Electoral Council [CNE] which conducted the September 1992 elections. A UNITA diplomatic source said this issue is not negotiable. UNITA has also asked for a more substantial and [words indistinct] role for the United Nations and a larger UNITA participation in the new CNE. Once this issue is dealt with, the negotiations will tackle national reconciliation, the future status of the president of UNITA -- the largest opposition party -- and the reciprocal return of property owned by the warring sides.
