Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The Chilean Government has indicated that it is fully prepared to begin a "new era" of relations with Bolivia by evaluating positively Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada's most recent remarks about not tying any future step toward the resumption of diplomatic relations to the beginning of talks on Bolivia's lack of a sea outlet. It has been explained, however, that the positive reaction of the Chilean Government does not imply that diplomatic relations will be resumed immediately. Chilean politicians stated that Sanchez de Lozada's statement was "very positive" and praised the president's modern and daring approach. Both government and political circles, however, agree that the remarks of the Bolivian president should be taken prudently bearing in mind that there are people in Bolivia whose position toward Chile and what they call "Bolivia's landlocked situation" is very hard and inflexible. [passage omitted] Foreign Relations Under Secretary Rodrigo Diaz Albonico said: "We were very happy to hear what Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada had to say." Jorge Pizarro, speaker for the foreign relations committee of the Chamber of Deputies, believes that Sanchez de Lozada's remarks "are extraordinarily positive and reflect that the Bolivian president has a modern and daring approach to what relations between the states should be as far as the process of integration is concerned, in order to further the social and economic development of our peoples." Experts in Bolivian-Chilean relations believe that rather than a change in Bolivia's foreign policy, there is a new and promising approach, confirmed by the fact the Sanchez de Lozada attended the Rio Group presidential summit in Santiago last year. "After what Sanchez de Lozada said, it is assumed that this so-called `new approach' will lead Bolivia to begin talks aimed at reestablishing diplomatic relations in the not too distant future," the experts said. Despite the remarks of the Bolivian president, the sources are not sure whether Bolivia's strategy is to resume relations and immediately tackle the sea outlet issue or to deal with both issues simultaneously. Chile does not acknowledge any pending issues between the two countries on the grounds that there are treaties in effect, especially the 1904 treaty, which establish the current border between the two countries. Thus, Chile did not accept any conditions when the resumption of ties was proposed in the past. The Foreign Ministry under secretary pointed out that the Bolivian president used the expression "new era" which is the same one Diaz Albonico used when he went to La Paz last year. The under secretary said that this was highly significant. As to Bolivia's new willingness to approach the resumption of diplomatic ties without first tackling its so-called "landlocked situation," Albonico said that Sanchez de Lozada's words should be studied carefully. He added, however, that the remarks of the Bolivian president augur a change in the political relations between the two countries, that may free them from past problems.
