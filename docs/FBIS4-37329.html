BFN [Text] Cairo, 19 Jun (MENA)--A Yemeni delegation led by 'Ali Mansur Rashid, member of the standing committee of the General People's Congress, arrived in Cairo from Sanaa this afternoon. In his arrival statement, the Yemeni official said that this visit is within the framework of efforts to implement Security Council Resolution 924. He said: We are happy to visit Cairo, which is the haven of all Arabs. He noted that he is carrying a message from the Yemeni Government to Lakhdar Brahimi, envoy of the UN secretary general to Yemen, who is currently in Cairo. Rashid pointed out that the situation is creating optimism and that they have come as representatives of two sides of the coalition parties in the Republic of Yemen, in addition to the Yemeni Socialist Party. [as received] Rashid said that the delegations will discuss reviving and forming a military committee to monitor the cease-fire, noting that the delay in forming the committee is due to the south's intransigence. Concerning the possibility of requesting Arab forces to help monitor the cease-fire, he said that the situation has not yet reached that point and that it is still confined to the framework of the Yemeni state. He added that it has not ruled out the possibility of the parties' seeking the help of Arab forces.
