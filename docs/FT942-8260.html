940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / Business outlook in north 'positive' The outlook for the majority of companies in the north of England is 'quite positive', Mr David Cranston, northern chairman of the Confederation of British Industry, said yesterday. Speaking after the latest two-monthly meeting of the confederation's northern regional council, which covers north-east England and Cumbria, Mr Cranston said many companies had seen a sustained improvement in orders for some time. The UK and Far East were particularly fruitful markets, although those in continental Europe remained sluggish. A large number of companies were planning to increase capacity or considering doing so, he said, and some businesses represented at the meeting in Gateshead, Tyne and Wear, were recruiting. There was some concern, he added, at the state of retail demand because of recent tax increases. But Mr Cranston, also chief executive of Northumbrian Water, believed the general optimistic picture would lead to a sustained improvement in fortunes, feeding through to the consumer. The regional council represents a cross-section of companies. The sectors include engineering, electronics, chemicals, giftware, construction, financial services, road haulage and the utilities. Mr Cranston said the overall mood of the meeting was one of cautious optimism. 'There was a general feeling that people were looking to the future and making plans in an atmosphere of confidence.'
