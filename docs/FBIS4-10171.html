BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the runoff in the Salvadoran presidential election scheduled for 24 April 1994. On 5 April, the leftist coalition presidential slate confirmed its participation in the runoff presidential election scheduled for 24 April. Presidential candidate Dr. Ruben Zamora said the Salvadoran left hopes the irregularities registered on 20 March will be overcome so as to guarantee the cleanliness of the process. The commitment made by Supreme Electoral Tribunal (TSE) magistrates to correct the anomalies is key to democracy, and the cleanliness of the Salvadoran electoral process depends on it, Dr. Zamora remarked. Zamora concluded by saying a draft bill will be presented before the Assembly requesting a series of temporary reforms to the Electoral Code. (San Salvador Radio Cadena YSU in Spanish 1800 GMT 5 Apr 94) Dr. Francisco Lima, leftist coalition vice presidential candidate, has strongly denied reports some coalition parties exerted pressure so that the left would not participate in the runoff elections. Lima stated: "What has characterized the coalition's political campaign is its sincerity." In a news conference, presidential candidate Zamora confirmed the TSE commitment to correct irregularities registered in the previous elections. He added: "The TSE has pledged to double the number of polling stations in the largest cities and place ballot boxes apart so as to avoid overcrowding." (San Salvador Canal Doce Television in Spanish 0300 GMT 6 Apr 94)
