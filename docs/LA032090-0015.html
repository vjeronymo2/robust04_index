March 20, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

WHY A PRESCRIPTION?

Question: It seems that there are more and more drugs that I can buy off the shelf that I used to need a prescription for. One is a pill for seasickness.

If I know that I have a problem, and I know the drugstore carries medicine that I can buy without a prescription, what would be the point in going to my doctor to get a prescription?

Answer: The danger in treating yourself with over-the-counter medications is that you are not equipped to diagnose your condition. Are you treating yourself for the underlying problem or just for a symptom? If you feel some pain, is it just a case of heartburn that will soon pass, or is it a symptom of something serious that requires medical attention?

I'm not suggesting that you bring every headache to the attention of your physician; in most cases, aspirin will be the most effective treatment. But don't fall into the habit of treating symptoms with over-the-counter medications while possibly ignoring a serious underlying problem.
