920328 FT 28 MAR 92 / Election 1992: End of boom - Ballot Box For the business community of Swindon, one of Britain's archetypal 1980s boom towns, Labour's plans for a national minimum wage and increased taxes on higher incomes seem to be causing few palpitations. A question-and-answer lunch debate with election candidates and the local chamber of commerce produced not one hostile question to the Labour challenger Jim D'Avila, a bearded Rover toolmaker. Nick Davies, the chamber's president, said he was surprised nobody brought up the tax issue. A bigger surprise, however, was that Simon Coombs, the incumbent Tory MP, failed to turn up. He sent an apology saying that he had an election to fight.
