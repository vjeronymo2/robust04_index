BFN [Excerpt] The opposition has reacted sharply to the attacks in the Gaza Strip. The National Religious Party has called on the public to rise up and prevent what it terms the government head-long dive into the abyss. The head of Tzomet, Refa'el Eytan, says the attacks are the inevitable outcome of a government that ordered the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] to flee Gaza, in his words, in the middle of the night. The YI'UD [Independent and Democratic Israel] party is demanding that the prime minister halt the passage of all vehicles from Gaza into Israeli territory. A short time ago, reporter Harley Braidman got this reaction from the senior Likud member of the Foreign Affairs and Defense Committee Eliyahu Ben-Elisar: [Begin Ben-Elisar recording] This is a tragedy because again everybody can see what a catastrophe was bestowed upon us by a government which is blind and weak, and is pursuing a policy which could not succeed, could have no chance of succeeding because it is against nature, and against human character and mentality. How can a foreign and powerful nation like Israel try to strike a peace agreement with a snake, with 'Arafat, with the PLO, and believe that this is the only way out of the difficulties, and trust such a man, who is not being trusted by his Arab colleagues? Believe me, I know what I am saying. [passage omitted] [end recording]
