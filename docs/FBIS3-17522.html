Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] On Saturday, Shukurdzhon Zukhurov, Tajikistan's Labor and Employment Minister and leader of the republican delegation at the future negotiations with the representatives of the opposition, refuted information stating that these negotiations would be held in the near term in Moscow with Russia's mediation. In his interview with INTERFAX, Zukhurov indicated that Tajikistan's official delegation is not going to Moscow to meet with the opposition in the near term. In his words, the Tajik leadership has not received an invitation from another mediator at the negotiations, a special envoy of the UN General Secretary. In addition, members and leadership of the delegation are to meet with the Tajik leadership to concretely determine authorities and mandate of the delegation, he said. "We shall hardly be able to work out this issues before 28 March," said Zukhurov. He also reported that the delegation headed by him is ready to sit at the negotiations table with the opposition's delegation headed by any of its leaders." At the same time he said that it is unclear who will head the delegation and how many members will be in it.
