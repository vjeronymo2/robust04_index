940124 FT 24 JAN 94 / C&W to invest Pounds 27m in undersea cabling Cable & Wireless, the UK telecommunications group, is to invest about Dollars 40m (Pounds 27m) in a new undersea cable system for the eastern Caribbean, in a significant upgrading of its long-distance capacity in the region. The system, 1,730km long, will link 14 islands in the eastern Caribbean from the British Virgin islands in the north to Trinidad in the south. It will provide a platform for the region's telecoms needs for the foreseeable future, underlining C&W's long-term commitment to the region. The cost of the system is expected to be Dollars 60m. France Telecom and AT&T, the US long-distance operator, will take initial investment shares of 18 per cent and 6 per cent respectively. C&W's initial share is 66 per cent of the Dollars 60m project. C&W has 29 businesses across the Bermuda, Caribbean and Atlantic region. Its main holding is a 70 per cent stake in Telecommunications of Jamaica, which made pre-tax profits of USDollars 44.8m last year on revenue of USDollars 175m. It has a 75 per cent stake in Barbados Telephone Company and an exclusive franchise in Cayman.
