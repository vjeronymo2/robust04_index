930427 FT 27 APR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Publicity drive for school tests THE government yesterday launched a publicity campaign to win support among parents for the controversial national curriculum tests for schools in England and Wales. Launching the Pounds 700,000 campaign of national press ads and leaflets, Mr John Patten, education secretary, said it would be a tragedy if this year's tests did not go ahead. Mr Peter Smith of the Association of Teachers and Lecturers said that differences over testing could not be solved by a public relations campaign.
