940110 FT 10 JAN 94 / Hospital bed cuts attacked by Labour A total of 10,637 beds in National Health Service hospitals and trusts have been lost in just one year in England, demonstrating that the service is 'slowly being whittled away', Mr David Blunkett, shadow health secretary, said yesterday. He said Department of Health figures showed this brought the total of beds lost to 120,000 since 1981 - a 34 per cent decrease. But Dr Brian Mawhinney, health minister, said the number of beds was only part of the story because the length of time a patient stays in hospital was falling all the time; the number of patients being treated as day patients was rapidly increasing, thanks to new techniques; and many doctors were now carrying out minor surgical procedures.
