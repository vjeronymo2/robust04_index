BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 14 April: The Economic Research Institute Foundation, Fipe, has stated that the March Consumer Price Index, IPC, totalled 41.9 percent, almost 4 percent up on February. The Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics for the same period, however, showed the IPC totalled 42.7 percent, some 1.76 percent down on February. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p B3) It has been reported that the British businessmen accompanying Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd on his visit to Brazil have meet with National Iron and Steel Company, CSN, board members because they want to participate in the company's investment plan. The 6-year CSN plan contemplates investments totalling $950 million, $180 million of which have to be made in 1994. These resources will be used to reduce industrial costs, improve quality and productivity, and implement environmental control measures. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 7 Apr 94 p 5) A drop in the net amount of dollars entering the country in March caused the Brazilian exchange balance to drop to $1.7 billion. This is the lowest since September, when it fell to $380 billion. Since October, the balance has been surpassing $2.5 billion. This is not bad news to the Central Bank which has already been controlling the entry of dollars into the country to control inflation. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 Section 2 p 1) The number of construction companies that have gone bankrupt in Rio de Janeiro and Sao Paulo in March increased by 43.4 percent. In November 34 companies went bankrupt, this dropped to 22 in December, to 18 in January, and then rose to 23 in February. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p 31) The Goods and Services Movement Tax collection in Sao Paulo in March rose by 11.2 percent and totalled $701.5 million. Between January and March the Sao Paulo treasury collected $1.904 billion in taxes. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p 3) Brazilian exports in January and February decreased from $3.1 billion and $3.5 billion, respectively, during the same period in 1993 to $2.75 billion and $2.78 billion, respectively. Semi- manufactured product exports in February dropped by 24.81 percent and shoe exports by 1.17 percent, although coffee exports increased by 78.91 percent and orange juice by 65.78 percent. In the same month, Brazil exported $616.22 million to the United States, still its largest trade partner, and $280.2 to Argentina its second largest trade partner. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p 5) The Brazilian agro-industry grew 2.1 percent in 1993. The largest growth was registered in Rio Grande do Sul State, 6.6 percent, followed by Sao Paulo, 6.5 percent. This positive performance is a result of a 3-percent increase in agricultural production; soy bean byproducts increased by 4.9 percent; coffee byproducts by 7.6 percent; orange byproducts by 19.6 percent; and grape byproducts by 19.5 percent. Cacao-bean, wheat, and sugar cane production, however, decreased, as did cattle raising activities, which dropped by 3.2 percent. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p 17) Brazilian car-manufacturers produced in March the largest number of cars in the sector's history. Although the car-manufacturing sector had planned to manufacture 1.5 million cars by 1995, the number of cars manufactured in the last 12 months already totals 1,481,000. Another record refers to car sales in the domestic market in March when 113,517 cars were sold. In 1993, 23 percent of the cars manufactured in the country were exported. From January to March this number increased by 29.8 percent and 79,711 cars have already been exported, 70 percent of them to Argentina. Tractors and agricultural equipment manufacture increased by 57 percent from January to March and 70.4 percent more of these were sold on the domestic market. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p B8) The February unemployment rate totalled 5.37 percent. Compared to January, the rate decreased by 0.17 percent. (Brasilia Voz do Brasil Network in Portuguese 2200 GMT 8 Apr 94) The Getulio Vargas Foundation has reported that the General Price Index increased by 44.8 percent in March. The accumulated increases since January total 173.8 percent and the increase accumulated in the last 12 months totals 3,421 percent. A poll by the Interunion Department for Statistics and Socioeconomic Studies on the price of basic foodstuff indicates that in March these prices increased above the Real Value Unit, URV, in 14 state capitals. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p 3) It has been reported that foreign investments on the Sao Paulo stock market dropped in March from the 22 percent registered in February -- when share movements totalled $95,410,000 -- to 20.6 percent. The movement in March totalled $62,100,000 million. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 8 Apr 94 p 20) The Sao Paulo Gas Company has reported that natural gas consumption in Sao Paulo State during the months of January and February increased by 24.6 percent compared to the same period in 1993 in view of the increasing number of industrial consumers. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 9 Apr 94 p 18) It has been reported that fuel prices will rise 18.47 percent countrywide at midnight on 12 April. One liter of gasoline will now cost 594,00 real cruzeiros. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 12 Apr 94 p B1) Some of the main national banks will begin to convert a larger number of their operations into URV on 12 April. Interest rates on those operations will range from 2 percent to 5 percent over the URV variations. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 12 Apr 94 pp A1, B1)
