January 8, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

PREGNANCY DIABETES

Question: If a woman is found to have diabetes when she becomes pregnant for the first time, does that mean she'll probably have the same problem each time she becomes pregnant?

Answer: It is estimated that about one-third of women who develop diabetes during one pregnancy will not have a recurrence during a subsequent pregnancy.

Some of the reasons suggested to explain this are changes in diet, exercise habits, the degree of stress they may be subjected to and other life-style factors that may affect diabetes.

Women who develop diabetes during one pregnancy as well as during a subsequent pregnancy are more likely to be heavier than women who don't.
