Language: English Article Type:BFN ["News Release"] [Text] Tallinn, March 15 -- Estonian Government, on Tuesday [15 March], sent law on state secrets, defining the regulations on pieces of information, declared a state secret, to the Riigikogu [parliament], Minister of Justice Kaido Kama told a press conference. Kama said that the bill defines also areas on which information cannot be concealed e.g. information on emergency cases, nature catastrophes, information concerning ecological and demographic situation. Government will appoint an expert working group that will propose which information to consider a state secret. If the proposition is adopted, defence of the secrets will be carried out by the Defence Police Department.
