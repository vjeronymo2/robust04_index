Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 27 (XINHUA) -- Hu Jintao, member of the Standing Committee of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party, visited a group of senior scientists and educators in Beijing early today. Taking part in a voluntary program sponsored by the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist League and All-China Federation of the Youth League, Hu first went to see Wang Dingfang, a 64-year-old nuclear scientist. The program enlists the help of many young people including young medical workers who render volunteer services to senior scientists and educators in their homes. Hu said that it is one of the important policies of the party to respect knowledge and respect talents. He assured Wang that the party and the government will make efforts to improve the working and living conditions for scientists. At the house of Lu Qing, deputy director of the Space Research Center of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, he noted that senior officials and scientists have made great contributions to the revolution and that young people should render services to them. He said that the volunteer services to the elderly contribute to upholding fine Chinese traditions. At the home of Xi Lu, a 77-year-old chemistry teacher at Beijing No. 47 High School, Hu said that the time and the society call for the volunteer services from the youth to the senior intellectuals and model workers. He said that the volunteer service spirit should be encouraged so as to promote fine social conduct and customs.
