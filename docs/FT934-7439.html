931119 FT 19 NOV 93 / UK Company News: Merchant Retail falls into the red Difficult trading conditions and losses on disposal of businesses left Merchant Retail Group with pre-tax losses of Pounds 31,000 for the half year to September 25, against profits of Pounds 467,000. The Perfume Shop, where the number of branches had doubled to 18, showed losses of Pounds 202,000, lower than budgeted. There were losses of Pounds 223,000 on the sale of Barnums and Town and Country Manner. The shares fell 3p to close at 12p. Turnover was Pounds 80.3m (Pounds 81.2m) for operating profits of Pounds 1.08m (Pounds 1.31m). Interest charges were lower at Pounds 887,000 (Pounds 1.2m). Losses per share were 0.15p (earnings 0.44p). The interim dividend is cut to 0.2p (0.35p).
