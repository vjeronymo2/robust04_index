Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] His Majesty King Husayn has said that the Hashemite Jordanian position on the Palestine question in general, and the issue of Jerusalem in particular, has been an honorable one based on enlightened principles of right and justice free of delusion and ambiguity and incapable of change or alteration. He added: Jerusalem is Arab and Islamic; its covenant is 'Umarite [a pledge Caliph 'Umar Bin-al-Khattab gave to Christians when he entered Jerusalem]; and its safeguard is Hashemite and is based on the determination of Husayn Bin-'Ali, who is buried there, and the vision of 'Abdallah Bin-al-Husayn, its martyr. It is along their path that we are marching. In a letter to Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali, ordering the re-formation of the Royal Committee for Jerusalem Affairs under the chairmanship of His Highness Crown Prince Hasan, his majesty the king added: On the blessed occasion of al-Isra' and al-Mi'raj [Prophet Muhammad's midnight journey from Mecca to Jerusalem and his ascension to heaven], we redouble our faith in the value of holy shrines and Jerusalem--this immortal city which has recently been witnessing a difficult and complicated situation and is facing an ambiguous destiny, something that underscores our duty toward it. It is dear to us and lies deep into our hearts. We feel that any Arab or Islamic relinquishment regarding it will be recorded in history with pain and regret. His Majesty King Husayn said: In order for the committee to shoulder its noble responsibilities, achieve its lofty aims, and follow up every affair related to Jerusalem and the holy shrines in the religious, historical, political, cultural, information, and legal domains, we order that the Royal Committee for Jerusalem Affairs be re-formed under the chairmanship of my dear brother His Royal Highness Crown Prince Hasan and the membership of the following: His Excellency Field Marshal 'Abd-al-Rahman Siwar al-Dhahab, His Excellency Ahmad al-Talib al-Ibrahimi, His Eminence Shaykh Ahmad al-Khalil, His Eminence Shaykh Muhammad al-Mukhtar al-Salami, His Eminence Shaykh Muhammad Taqiy-al-Din al-'Uthmani, His Eminence Dr. Fadil al-Husayn al-Jilani, His Eminence Shaykh Isma'il Bin-'Ali al-Akwa', His Excellency Dr. 'Abd-al-'Aziz al-Duri, his excellency the minister of information, his eminence the chief justice, His excellency the minister of awqaf and Islamic affairs, his excellency the minister of state for foreign affairs, His Excellency Dr. Hazim Nusaybah, His Excellency 'Abdallah Salah, His Excellency Dr. Jamal Nasir, His Excellency Dr. Kamil Abu-Jabir, His Excellency Dr. Nasir-al-Din al-Asad, Bishop Salim al-Sa'igh, Bishop Samir Qaf'iti, His Excellency Dr. Muhammad 'Adnan al-Bakhit, Father Qustantin Qarmash, and His Excellency 'Awn al-Khasawinah. His Majesty King Husayn had earlier sent a letter to Akram Zu'aytir, expressing his profound thanks and appreciation for him and for the [former] members of the Royal Committee for Jerusalem Affairs.
