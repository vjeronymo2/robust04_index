BFN [Text] Foreign investments in enterprises on Russian soil at the end of 1993 amounted to $2.7 billion and 4.8 billion rubles (this includes ruble investments in joint ventures with CIS member nations and reinvested rubles). Yakov Urinson, first deputy economics minister, told a meeting, held in Moscow Monday, of the advisory council on investment policy which reports to the government that direct investments (which enable the investors to share in the management and made up more than 10 percent of the enterprise equity capital) accounted for 85 percent of the total, or for $2.3 billion and 4.3 billion rubles. Portfolio investments (which accounted for less than 10 percent of the equity capital and do not enable the investors to share in the management) accounted for as little as 3 percent of the entire amount, 70 million dollars. Other kinds of investment such as trading credits and financial leasing were equal to $282 million and 437 million rubles. According to the Russian Economics Ministry, the largest share of foreign investments, 23 percent, was made in the engineering industry and metal working, followed by the fuel industry, 15 percent, trade and catering, 14 percent, construction, 6 percent and timber and pulp and cellulose industries, 5 percent. The ministry reported that foreign investments increased by $1.36 billion and 4.1 billion rubles in 1993.
