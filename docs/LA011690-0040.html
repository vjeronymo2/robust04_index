January 16, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

PAN-AM HIGHWAY

With regard to the map which appeared on Page A5 (Dec. 31) I wish to point out that the Pan-American Highway does not pass all the way through eastern Panama (Darien Province) and into Colombia but, in fact, stops well short of the Panama-Colombian border. Nor is there any likelihood that the road will be completed and for an interesting reason.

At present there is a fairly extensive stand of tropical moist forest in the eastern-most part of Panama and this has been designated a national park (although given very limited protection during the Noriega years).

The principal purpose of the park is to provide a barrier between Colombia where foot-and-mouth disease is known to occur and Panama and Central America where the disease does not occur. This disease, caused by a virus, is highly destructive of cattle and other ruminant mammals. Were it to get into Panama and Central America the export of beef to the United States would be stopped, thus causing considerable economic loss -- although it might also result in a cessation of forest-to-pasture conversion in the region.

CHARLES F. BENNETT

Professor of Biogeography

UCLA
