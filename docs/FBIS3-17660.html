Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Moldova the Agrarian-Democratic Party that won the majority in the new parliament plans to propose that Prime-Minister Andrei Sangheli and speaker of parliament Pyotr Luchinski keep their posts. The intentions were reported to INTERFAX on Monday by the party leader Dmitri Motspan. "We believe Sangheli and Luchinski are will be able to cope with the tasks facing them, form a team of professionals, live up to the hopes of voters and carry out the election promises of our party," he said. According to Motspan, the two leaders were on the ticket of his party. He said the main items on the agenda of the session of parliament beginning on Tuesday will be the draft constitution, the status of the Dniester river valley and areas populated by the Gagauzi as well as the election of the speaker and his deputies. He also felt that a set of economic matters would also be discussed. On interaction with the opposition the majority leader said that his party is ready to take part in drafting a law on the opposition. "Such a law is necessary, there should be an opposition, but the principles of its functioning should not be anti-government," Motspan believes.
