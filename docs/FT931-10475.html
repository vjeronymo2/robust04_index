930209 FT 09 FEB 93 / UK Company News: SHT sells two offshoots to cut debt Scottish Heritable Trust, the heavily-indebted mini conglomerate, is to reduce its borrowings via the sale of two subsidiaries. Eastern Kayam OCM, an importer and wholesaler of oriental carpets, is being sold to its management for Pounds 4.3m. The consideration, which includes the discharge of inter-company debt due to Scottish Heritable, will be satisfied by a cash payment of Pounds 1.5m and the transfer of assets to the value of Pounds 2.8m. Of the cash Pounds 500,000 will be paid on completion, with two instalments of Pounds 250,000 in May and June and a final payment of Pounds 500,000 in December. All deferred payments will be secured by a charge on EKOCM's stock. The other subsidiary - Haven Homes, a US manufacturer of modular residential homes - is being sold to its management for a maximum Dollars 4m (Pounds 2.6m). Of this, Dollars 3.25m is payable in cash on completion with a maximum Dollars 500,000 payable upon settlement of certain legal claims against Haven. The remaining Dollars 250,000 is payable 60 days after completion.
