Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 25 KYODO -- Japan and South Korea exchanged notes in Tokyo on Friday [25 March] to promote mutual cooperation on trade insurance. Japan's Trade Minister Hiroshi Kumagai and his South Korean counterpart Kim chol-su agreed Thursday that the two countries will strive to build cooperative ties in government trade insurance. Under the accord, Japan's Ministry of International Trade and Industry and Korea Export Insurance Corp. will jointly cover trade insurance for projects undertaken by Japanese and South Korean companies in developing countries. The cooperation will also feature regular meetings among insurance experts from the two countries and interchange of information on country risks and other problems involved in projects.
