BFN [Text] The Legislative Assembly of Karelia declared itself the sole legislature of that constituent republic of the Russian Federation under Article 65 of the republic's Constitution. The Assembly, elected last March, also declared itself a successor of the Karelian Supreme Soviet. Provisional by-laws of the Assembly were adopted. Galina Pivnenko, chairman of the republic's trade union council, was elected to chair the House of Representatives and Ivan Aleksandrov, former deputy chairman of the Karelian Supreme Soviet, was elected to chair the House of the Republic. Viktor Stepanov, who won the April 17 elections, would be sworn in as the prime minister in the afternoon of Tuesday. He used to chair the Karelian Supreme Soviet.
