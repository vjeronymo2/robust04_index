BFN [Text] A joint Russian-American naval exercise for aiding the civilian population in the case of a natural disaster started near Vladivostok on June 20. The Russian Pacific Fleet detached a large landing ship and 180 marines for the exercise managed by Rear Admiral Nikolay Kochergin, commander of the fleet's landing ship unit. The U.S. is represented by a landing-helicopter-carrying ship Debuc with marines and 12 boats under the command of Rear Admiral John Siegler. Officers in the Russian navy's press center told INTERFAX that the ships were already in the area where the exercise was to be carried out and would start the "humanitarian landing" at 7:00 local time on June 21.
