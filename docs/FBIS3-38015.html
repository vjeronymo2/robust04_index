Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russian First Deputy Foreign Minister Anatoliy Adamishin and Japanese Deputy Foreign Minister Hiroshi Fukuda, who met in Moscow Wednesday, expressed the desire of both countries to expand political and other contacts and to improve the atmosphere of partnership, confidence and good neighborly relations. According to Russian Foreign Ministry spokesman Grigoriy Karasin, Russia's joining the G-7 group of most advanced countries would open up new vistas for cooperation in international issues. He told journalists that the Japanese official wanted to have a better insight into the political situation in Russia. "Unambiguous remarks made by President Boris Yeltsin and Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin were quoted to show that Russia will stay on the reform course," Karasin said.
