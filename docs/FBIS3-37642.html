Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] State President Guntis Ulmanis received Aleksandr Rannikh, Russian ambassador to Latvia, today. Rannikh handed to Ulmanis a message from Russian President Boris Yeltsin. In it Yeltsin expresses the wish to further develop Latvian and Russian Army withdrawal from Latvia and other issues related to this. In addition, Yeltsin indicates the readiness to meet the Latvian state president in Moscow to tackle many issues concerning bilateral relations between Latvia and Russia. Yeltsin also expressed his opinion on the legal status of Russians living in Latvia, speaking about Latvian legislative and public processes. Yeltsin pointed out the possibility of developing in the future qualitatively new relations between Latvia and Russia strengthening cooperation in political, economic, and social spheres.
