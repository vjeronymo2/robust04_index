Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 21 Mar (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa met today with Dennis Ross, the U.S. coordinator for the Middle East peace negotiations. During this meeting, discussions focused on the latest developments in the peace process and the problems it is encountering, in particular the al-Ibrahimi Mosque massacre committed by the Israeli settlers against the Palestinian worshipers. After his meeting with Foreign Minister Musa, Ross stated that they held detailed discussions about the peace process. Ross added that he informed 'Amr Musa of the results of his discussions in Tunis. He clarified that these talks were "encouraging." Ross described the discussions he held with Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat as "constructive." He said Chairman 'Arafat's talks with the Israelis were also constructive. The American official expressed his hope that some progress will be made regarding security and understanding in the Middle East. Ross also expressed his hope that progress be made quickly in the negotiations regarding the implementation of the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles. He said that during the past two days extensive and serious talks have been held between the concerned parties to address all issues. Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa stated that during their meeting today Dennis Ross informed him of the results of his talks in Tunis with Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, as well as the talks between the Palestinian leadership and the Israeli delegation that recently visited Tunis. 'Amr Musa said that he understands there will be a continuation of these Palestinian-Israeli talks regarding the situation in Hebron and other topics dealing with Gaza and Jericho. On the possibility of convening a meeting between 'Arafat and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, 'Amr Musa said there is no further information on this matter and that he cannot confirm it. He expressed his hope that some breakthrough will be made in the stance or that Israel will withdraw very soon. He also hoped that the Palestinian-Israeli agreement will be realized soon.
