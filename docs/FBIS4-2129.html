BFN [Text] Yinchuan, April 4 (XINHUA) -- The Ningxia Hui Autonomous Region, a major animal husbandry base in China, has concentrated its strength on building ten animal husbandry production bases. The bases cover 144 townships and 214 villages. They produce respectively 33.2 percent, 30 percent and 80 percent of the total meat, eggs and milk of the region. The per capita milk share in the region is three times the average level of the country. Ningxia, a Moslem province and one of the five autonomous regions inhabited mainly by minority nationalities, also attaches importance to relying on science and technology to improve its production. The region has put into application over 30 research findings and items of technology, and developed new breeds of chickens and animals. Its chicken-raising industry has a production value of 175 million yuan (about 21 million U.S. dollars), and milk production is ranked among the top in the country. At the same time, the region has invested over 30 million yuan (about 3.6 million U.S. dollars) in 37 infrastructure projects in the animal husbandry production bases. According to statistics, more than 153,000 ha of grassland have been improved. Last year, the region's total output value of animal husbandry was 750 million yuan, 4.65 percent up over the previous year.
