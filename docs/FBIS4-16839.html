BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Vladimir Solntsev] [Text] Moscow, 19 Apr -- Morihiro Hosokawa's resignation from the post of Japanese prime minister and the expected formation of a new government in the near future will not lead to changes in Tokyo's policies toward Russia. This was clearly stated by the Japanese ambassador in Moscow, Koji Watanabe, in an exclusive interview with an ITAR-TASS correspondent. "Policy toward Russia is one of the most important elements of Japanese diplomacy," he stressed. The ambassador said that the Tokyo declaration, adopted by the leaders of the two countries during the visit by Russian President Boris Yeltsin to Japan last October had created "a new basis for further progress in Russo-Japanese relations." The diplomat said that "the continuation of constant efforts to develop relations, on the basis of this declaration, is the consistent and immutable policy of our country." Koji Watanabe recalled that during the visit to Moscow last month by Tsutomu Hata, Japan's deputy prime minister and foreign minister -- who is regarded as the principal contender for the post of head of the new government -- the parties agreed to advance steadily toward a settlement of the territorial problem, based on the Tokyo declaration. The Russian side stressed its commitment to the policy of deepening reform, and the Japanese side emphasized that there would be no change in its policy of assisting these reforms. The ambassador indirectly confirmed that the change in the Japanese cabinet would not remove from the agenda the question of a visit to Russia by the Japanese prime minister this year. He recalled that the invitation to make the visit, which Yeltsin conveyed to the Japanese prime minister last October, was confirmed by Viktor Chernomyrdin, the head of the Russian government, and Andrey Kozyrev, the foreign minister, during their recent meetings with Hata. "I think that when Japan has a new prime minister, a specific date for his visit to Russia will be agreed along diplomatic channels," the ambassador said. ITAR-TASS has learned from diplomatic sources that the Japanese premier's visit to Moscow this year may be preceded by a visit to Tokyo by Andrey Kozyrev in order to prepare for the summit meeting.
