BFN [Report by Re'uven Shapira] [Excerpts] Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin has approved in principle the establishment of a new police district, which will be the sixth, and which will serve as the unified command of the police units in the territories. Rabin has thus accepted a plan put forth by Police Minister Moshe Shahal. The establishment of the new district means, among other things, that some 1,000 policemen and border policemen will be drafted to the force. According to the plan, the Israel Police and Border Police units will assume responsibility for security and order in the territories under the command of the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] and will perform many of the tasks currently preformed by the Army. The new district will be commanded by a ranking police commander and will be called the Autonomy District. It will unite the three currently operating subdistricts of Judaea, Samaria, and Gaza. [passage omitted] In the framework of the new police deployment in the territories, a unit of 300 border policemen will be set up. It will perform guard duties on the roads and crossings between the areas of the autonomy and Israel. The new unit has begun organizing and drafting men.
