Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on insurgent activities in Peru compiled from Peruvian media monitored through 18 March. On 3 March the National Police presented to the media a Shining Path cell that perpetrated many crimes in Huancayo. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 4 Mar 94 p A15) The National Police on 4 March presented to the media Shining Path "terrorist" Felix Naysnate Icomena, 34, aka Comrade Jairo, who was in Pucallpa planning attacks against civilian, political authorities and forces of order. Naysnate was a member of a Shining Path hit squad that operated in the San Martin zone. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 5 Mar 94 p A 11) The National Counterterrorism Directorate, Dincote, arrested a Shining Path cell that operated in Villa El Salvador. It was planning four car bombs attacks against the 29th Radio Patrol Command in La Victoria District. These "terrorists" are charged with having perpetrated attacks against a National Police facility, the Peruvian Air Force General Command, and the U.S. Embassy, among others. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 5 Mar 94 p A10) The National Police has intensified the search for Maritza Infante Yupanqui, aka Comrade Milagros, and other "terrorists." She is a member of a Shining Path group that perpetrated the bloodiest "terrorist" attacks in Metropolitan Lima. She leads the main force of the so-called People's Liberation Army, which makes up the People's Aid of Peru faction. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 8 Mar 94 p A12) On 8 March a column of 30 Shining Path "terrorists" burned the facilities of the Villa Sol construction company and two vehicles in Huaraz Department. The criminals fled after beating the guard and placing a large quantity of dynamite in the offices. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 9 Mar 94 p A13) The Dincote has disbanded the Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement, MRTA, northern regional committee based in Trujillo. Six of its members were presented to the media today. They are: Juan Antonio Leon Montero, 33, aka Perseo or Paco, a member of the northeastern guerrilla front and commander of the Tupac Amaru People's Army; Constantino Quispe Caceres, 56, aka Espartaco or Felix; Adan Torres Vasquez, 32, aka Angel, Federico Manuel Vargas Yupanqui, aka Jorge; Mariela Gabriela Guarino, 35, aka Gaby, an Italian journalist in charge of disseminating MRTA propaganda abroad through video films; and Miriam Torres. The police discovered two clandestine bases from where the terrorist actions were planned. They dismantled a sophisticated short and long range communications base. (Lima Global de Television network in Spanish 0100 GMT 18 Mar 94)
