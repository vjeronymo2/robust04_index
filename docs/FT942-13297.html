940426 FT 26 APR 94 / Bosses urged to help fight asthma Companies were urged yesterday to give employees better protection against asthma-causing chemicals after a report disclosed that 1,000 asthma cases a year are developed in the workplace. The warning was issued by the Health and Safety Executive, which has launched an education campaign aimed mainly at small and medium-sized businesses. Dr Tim Carter, HSE director of field operations and medical services, said some 200 asthma - inducing substances had been identified, ranging from isocyanates, found in paints, to grain dust. Industries where such substances were used included agriculture, food manufacture, electronics, metal manufacturing and treatment, plastics processing, chemical processing and woodworking. 'Some sufferers are so disabled by the illness that they cannot work, and most have to change jobs to avoid re-exposure,' he said.
