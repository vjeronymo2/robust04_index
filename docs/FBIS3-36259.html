Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [International Department report: "Reshuffles at Russian Foreign Ministry. Russian Ambassador to U.S. Has Not Yet Been Appointed"] [Text] SEGODNYA, citing "diplomatic sources," has reported that Yuliy Vorontsov, currently Russia's permanent representative at the United Nations, has been appointed Russia's new ambassador to Washington. Correspondingly, he has been replaced at the United Nations by Deputy Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov. However, Deputy Foreign Minister Sergey Krylov, who is in charge of personnel policy, did not confirm this report yesterday to KOMMERSANT-DAILY's correspondent. Both Vorontsov and Lavrov, according to unofficial information received by KOMMERSANT-DAILY, may in fact be appointed to the aforementioned posts. Currently Sergey Lavrov is at UN headquarters in New York. KOMMERSANT-DAILY plans to publish an interview with him in the near future. KOMMERSANT-DAILY will follow up on the subject once corresponding presidential edicts are signed.
