930225 FT 25 FEB 93 / Japanese government on verge of opening its rice imports THE JAPANESE government had been on the verge of announcing plans to open its rice market to imports last month, a senior trade official admitted yesterday, writes Charles Leadbeater. They had been shelved once it became clear the prospects for an early Uruguay Round agreement had faded. Mr Kiichi Miyazawa, Japan's prime minister, had prepared a package to liberalise the market after hinting in December last year that he supported the move. It included financial measures to support farmers whose businesses might have suffered as a result of allowing imports into Japan's tightly regulated market. The measures would have been included in this spring's special supplementary budget, designed to stimulate the Japanese economy. 'Mr Miyazawa was ready to come out of his castle waving a white flag to surrender over rice, but. . . . he found the enemy had disappeared to do other things,' the official said.
