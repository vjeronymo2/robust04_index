BFN [Text] Moscow, 13 Jun (ITAR-TASS) -- A telephone conversation took place today between Russian President Boris Yeltsin and U.S. President Bill Clinton. According to the report received by ITAR-TASS from the Russian president's press office, the presidents continued their discussion of the situation that has developed around the North Korean nuclear problem and possible steps for its political resolution. In connection with this, Boris Yeltsin and Bill Clinton exchanged opinions on the UN Security Council resolution that is being prepared, and which should give equal weight to the ideas of both possible sanctions and an international conference to resolve the problems of the Korean peninsula. The presidents agreed to instruct Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S Secretary of State Warren Christopher to continue close consultations on these issues.
