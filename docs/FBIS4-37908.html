BFN [Text] Cairo, June 25 (AFP) -- Thirty-three lawyers have gone on a hunger strike in solidarity with one of their senior colleagues, who is demanding publication of a report into the death of a lawyer in police custody, one of the strikers said Saturday [25 June]. 'Abd-al-'Aziz Muhammad, head of the Bar Association, began his hunger strike to demand publication of the document a week ago. He has been in hospital since Monday [20 June] after his condition deteriorated. Hunger striker Halah 'Abd-al-'Aziz told AFP: "Eighteen lawyers, including three women, have been on hunger strike since Wednesday [22 June] or Thursday [23 June], in the Bar Association headquarters in Cairo, and 15 others joined the movement Friday [24 June] at Ismailiya and Port Said," on the Suez Canal. She said the lawyers, from diverse political groups, will continue their hunger strike "until death, or until the satisfaction of the demands" put by Muhammad. Muhammad has called on the government to release an official report into the death in April of 'Abd-al-Harith Madani, an Islamic lawyer. Madani died in police custody. Authorities say an asthma attack was to blame, but the Bar Association charged that he died under torture. Muhammad is also demanding the release of 24 lawyers arrested in May during a rally to protest Madani's death. Egyptian newspapers have reported that the Bar Association head had recently discovered the contents of an official report into Madani's death, which noted "injury marks on Madani's body." "That casts doubt on the causes of death of the lawyer, which could have a criminal origin," 'Aziz said. The 40,000-member Bar Association, controlled by the Muslim Brotherhood, has come under fire recently from the Egyptian authorities.
