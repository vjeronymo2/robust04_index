Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Yusuf Bin-'Alawi Bin-'Abdallah, Omani minister of state for foreign affairs, left Amman for Damascus today after a two-day visit to Jordan. During his visit he met with His Majesty King Husayn and several senior Jordanian officials. Details are in the following report: In a press statement prior to his departure, the Omani minister said that he was honored to have been received by His Majesty King Husayn. He added that he relayed an oral message from His Majesty Sultan Qabus to his majesty the king on issues of common interest and current Arab issues. He went on to say that he listened to a detailed explanation from His Majesty King Husayn on various pan-Arab issues, as well as Jordan's prominent role in aiding the peace process. The Omani minister continued: The remarks that I have heard from His Majesty King Husayn regarding the Jordanian perspective toward the future of this region, as well his eagerness to promote Arab interests within the context of peace were heartening. He noted that he met this morning with Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs. This was a continuation of the talks I held with his majesty the king yesterday. Both sides agreed to identify a mechanism for coordination on various political issues that concern the two fraternal countries, he said. He noted that a joint committee has been formed to follow up on all bilateral trade and economic issues. He hoped that the desire of His Majesty King Husayn and His Majesty Sultan Qabus to seize all possible opportunities to promote bilateral ties will be realized. In reply to a question on the efforts made by the Sultanate of Oman to achieve inter-Arab reconciliation, the minister said: Just like all fraternal Arabs, we look forward to see the Arabs identify a common ground for inter-Arab action and Arab solidarity. I believe that the circumstances now are more opportune for achieving this goal than they were in the past. He added: We support and back the efforts and endeavors made by the Arab League secretary general to achieve something along the road of joint Arab action and Arab solidarity. When asked about the United Arab Emirates [UAE]-Iran dispute on the three islands, Bin-'Alawi said: I can say that the UAE-Iranian dispute has continued to be discussed by the two countries in the hope of reaching a peaceful settlement. As for the UAE brothers, they have consistently said that they want to see this dispute resolved peacefully within the context of legitimate rights. I believe that Iran is demonstrating a favorable response in this regard. I hope that future developments will yield a solution to this dispute. On the Omani minister's visit, Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs, said that His Majesty King Husayn has met with the guest minister. The meeting was cordial and fraternal. The talks focused on issues of common interest, including fraternal bilateral ties, the peace process, as well as the ways and means of fostering these issues. Al-Hasan added: This morning, the talks were resumed. They focused on the means of promoting and enhancing bilateral economic and trade ties within the context of the Joint Jordanian-Omani Committee. We agreed on the need to continue these consultations and this coordination in the interest of the Arab nation. The guest minister was seen off by the minister of state for foreign affairs, the Foreign Ministry's secretary general, the Omani ambassador in Amman, and several senior officials.
