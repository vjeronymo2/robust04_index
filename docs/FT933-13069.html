930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / Observer: Gummed up The government's assault on red tape, which yesterday yielded a mere 217 pages of report by the 'efficiency scrutiny team', was off to a bad start when ministers flatly rejected one of its key proposals - the creation of a national red tape quango. Nor will the country's embattled small businessmen be exactly cheered by John Gummer's selfless proposals for tape-cutting at the Department of the Environment. Earmarked for immediate attention are several Seal Fishery Acts, which principally affect Canadian fisherman in the north Pacific.
