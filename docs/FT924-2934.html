921210 FT 10 DEC 92 / Arts: Today's Television The Dutch have always semed to me more like the English than any other race. They often have a similar sense of humour, share a tradition of liberalism and non-conformism, love the sea and have a splendid maritime tradition, and stand in similar juxtaposition between the excitable Mediterranean temperament and the sadder introversion of Scandinavia. We are promised that The Essential History Of Europe (8.00 BBC2) was filmed in the streets, galleries, canals and brothels of Amsterdam, with satirical comments from cabaret artists Joep van Haek. Time was when the commercial television authority banned hypnotism (with dating agencies, and undertakers) from its advertising. Today ITV offers an entire 60 minute about The Andrew Newton Hypnotic Experience (9.00) with members of the audience involved in 'intriguing and often amusing moments'. BBC2 repeats the programme which won the documentary prize at this year's Prix Italia festival, War, Lives And Videotape. A one-man effort by Nick Danziger, it shows efforts to re-house orphans who lived in an asylum in Kabul (9.30).
