Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Tegucigalpa -- The Supreme Court of Justice (CSJ) issued a statement yesterday ordering the country's judges to begin an investigation on the report issued recently by Leo Valladares, national human rights ombudsman, on missing individuals. The report, the CSJ plenum stated, "notes the alleged participation of state officials and employees during the 1980 to 1993 period when it accuses them and holds them allegedly responsible for the forced disappearance of national and foreign citizens and other human rights violations that took place in our country. This situation calls for a thorough investigation of the facts that are mentioned by the judges who are in charge of criminal matters. [no closing quotation mark as published] Therefore, in using the legal powers invested upon it, the CSJ resolves: - First: To acknowledge receipt of the report on missing individuals in Honduras through the Secretariat of this Court. - Second: To create a special commission of prosecutors, headed by Prosecutor General Juan Arnaldo Hernandez, to review the documents contained in the report to b?gin the corresponding penal actions in the cases that warrant it. - Third: To allow the judges in charge of criminal matters, who know from where complaints have been filed regarding the disappearance of national and foreign citizens, to begin or continue, as appropriate, the corresponding procedures to punish the presumed human rights violators. The CJS Secretariat is to receive an authentic copy of the report and attached documents. - Fourth: To brief this resolution to CSJ General Prosecutor Hernandez and other judges in charge of criminal matters who know of where the reported incidents have taken place so that they can be informed and proceed. This resolution is to be implemented immediately.
