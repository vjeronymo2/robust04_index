Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, speaking in Blagoveshchensk at a conference of representatives of regions of the Russian Far East bordering on China, said that the volume of bilateral trade in 1993 was $2.5 billion, with which it is possible to ease the shortage of consumer goods in Russia. Kozyrev also said that an agreement would come into force in 1994 restricting visa-free entry into Russia for citizens of the PRC. Visa-free entry will only remain for holders of diplomatic or business passports. According to the minister, this is not a case of the recreation of an iron curtain. It is, nonetheless, necessary to know how many Chinese citizens are on Russian territory, Interfax reports.
