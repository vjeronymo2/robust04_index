BFN [Excerpts] The Supervision and Control Commission, CSC, has received 12 notifications of alleged cease-fire violations. Eight of those came from the government and the other four from the Mozambique National Resistance, Renamo. A total of (?10) of those reports have already been investigated. Only two must still be investigated. In his report to the UN Security Council, UN Secretary General said that neither the government nor Renamo have allowed the UN Operations in Mozambique, Unomoz, access to certain military bases. His report also noted that neither side has supplied full military equipment lists yet. Butrus Butrus-Ghali also noted in his report that some government assembly areas are too full while others are virtually empty. [passage omitted] In his report, the UN secretary general also said that by 18 April Renamo had demobilized 3 percent of all its listed forces in the assembly areas, while the government had released 28 percent of its men.
