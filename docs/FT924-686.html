921223 FT 23 DEC 92 / Former Inchcape chief dies SIR George Turnbull, former chairman and chief executive of Inchcape, the business services group, died yesterday aged 66. Sir George, educated at King Henry VIII School, Coventry, and Birmingham University, had a long and varied career in the motor industry, both in the UK and overseas. His career developed with Standard Motors from 1950, where he rose to be general manager. From 1968 to 1973 he was managing director of British Leyland Motor Corporation and from 1974 to 1977 vice-president of Hyundai Motors, leaving to work with the Iran National Motor Company. From 1979 he was chairman of Talbot UK. He was also chairman of the Industrial Society's council from 1987 to 1990 and deputy president of the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders from 1984 to 1986. Sir George, who joined Inchcape as group managing director in 1984 and was appointed chief executive in 1986, retired last year on medical grounds. He was married with two daughters and a son.
