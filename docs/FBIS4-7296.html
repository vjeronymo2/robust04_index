BFN [Text] Thailand repatriated a total of 998 Cambodians and some 400 Lao refugees and displaced persons this week. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs said the Cambodians mainly comprise children, women, and the elderly, who fled the war in Cambodia to Ta Phraya District of Prachin Buri Province. They were sheltered temporily in an area of 5 rai provided by the Thai Government. The area is about two kilometers from the Cambodian border. Meanwhile, Secretary General of the National Security Council Khachatphai Burutphat said more than 400 Lao refugees and displaced persons were repatriated yesterday through the Thai-Lao Friendship Bridge. It is the highest number of Laos returnees repatriated from the Thai soil this year. The Lao refugees and displaced persons are all the Hmong hilltribe people who were sheltered at the Ban Napho repatriation center in Nakhon Phanom Province. The National Security Council plans to close the repatriation center within the end of this year.
