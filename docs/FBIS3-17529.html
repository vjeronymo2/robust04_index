Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Ivan Ivanov, Anna Melnikova and Vladimir Akimov] [Text] Moscow March 28 TASS -- Russian-Kazakh talks began in the Kremlin today with a tete-a-tete conversation between Boris Yeltsin and Nursultan Nazarbayev. In an hour they are expected to be joined by official delegations of the two countries, which include Prime Ministers Viktor Chernomyrdin and Sergey Tereshchenko. A large package of Russian-Kazakh documents is expected to be signed on the results of the talks approximately after 12.00. Yeltsin and Nazarbayev are planning to sign a treaty on military cooperation, a treaty on further deepening of economic cooperation and integration between Kazakhstan and Russia, a treaty on the main principles of the settlement of problems dealing with the civil and legal status of Kazakh citizens living permanently in Russia, and russian citizens living permanently in Kazakhstan, and an agreement between the two countries on the main principles and terms of the operation of the Baykonur space-launch complex. Some 20 documents are planned to be signed on the level of heads of government. They deal with military and technical cooperation, a peaceful use of atomic energy, cooperation in the sphere of economy, science and culture, as well as legal relationship. A total of 22 documents are expected to be signed on the results of the talks.
