BFN [Text] Yesterday, Nouhak Phoumsavan, president of our Lao People's Democratic Republic [LPDR], received at the Hokham Presidential Office in Vientiane a courtesy call from a Foreign Affairs Ministry delegation from the Union of Myanmar [Burma]. The delegation, headed by Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister U Nyunt Swe, is currently on a visit to the LPDR. The meeting between the guests and host proceeded in an atmosphere of warm cordiality. The Myanmar deputy foreign affairs minister expressed thanks to President Nouhak Phoumsavan for the warm welcome. He also informed the president about the meeting and talks between the Foreign Affairs Ministry delegations of Myanmar and Laos, the meeting which, he said, marked a new step in the relations and cooperation in various fields between the two countries. U Nyunt Swe took the occasion to convey to President Nouhak Phoumsavan best wishes and warm greetings from General Than Shwe, chairman of the State Law and Order Restoration Council of the Union of Myanmar. The head of the Myanmar Foreign Affairs Ministry delegation also informed the president about the domestic situation in Myanmar, including the establishment of the new constitution. He also exchanged views with the president on the further promotion, expansion, and strengthening of the Myanmar-Lao relations. President Nouhak Phoumsavan expressed a welcome to and highly appraised the visit to Laos by the Foreign Affairs Ministry delegation of the Union of Myanmar, a visit which he said marked a significant milestone in the promotion and expansion of the traditional, friendly relations and cooperation of good- neighborliness. He expressed the conviction that these relations between the two countries will be increasingly developed. Earlier U Nyunt Swe and his delegation called on Somsavat Lengsavat, foreign affairs minister of the LPDR. The meeting proceeded in a cordial atmosphere.
