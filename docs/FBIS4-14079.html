BFN [Text] United Nations, New York, April 27, IRNA -- U.N. secretary-general's special envoy to Afghanistan Mahmoud Mestiri will leave for Riyadh and Tehran on Friday, April 29, to exchange views with the officials of the two countries on latest developments in Afghanistan. The office of the secretary-general made the announcement here Tuesday at the end of Mestiri's fourth visit to Afghanistan. In its latest tour of Afghanistan, the U.N. delegation paid a visit to Qandahar and its nearby regions and met with the leaders of political parties and tribal chiefs, the announcement said. It added that the said Afghan leaders favored restoration of peace in their country and stressed that it required the establishment of a ceasefire, collecting the arms at the disposal of the people, and non-interference of foreigners in internal affairs of their country. The delegation, in its 3-week stay in Afghanistan, has so far visited Kabul, Mazar-e Sharif, Herat, Qandahar, and Jalalabad.
