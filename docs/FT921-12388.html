920127 FT 27 JAN 92 / UK News (Employment): NUCPS may claim MoD guards THE NUCPS civil servants' union said yesterday that it would expect to recruit and represent Ministry of Defence security guards if their status was changed to that of white-collar workers, Lisa Wood writes. The union's intentions will anger the TGWU and GMB general unions, which disclosed on Friday that about 2,400 military installation guards were to lose the right to be represented by unions affiliated to the Labour party. That is because the force is being reorganised and guards' status is to be changed from industrial to non-industrial civil servants. The government says political affiliation by a union representing non-industrial civil servants runs counter to the Civil Service requirement for political neutrality. The NUCPS is not affiliated to Labour. Mr Leslie Christie, NUCPS general secretary, said: 'We are not looking for a fight with the TGWU, but if the end result is these grades are non-industrial and linked to our pay scales, we would expect to recruit and represent them.'
