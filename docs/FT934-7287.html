931120 FT 20 NOV 93 / Attack on NHS bureaucracy TOO MANY managers' jobs have been created under the government's health service reforms, Mr Ivan Lester, retiring chairman of Harrogate Health Care Trust, said yesterday. He told BBC Radio: 'We have put extra layers of bureaucracy, of management, in and we actually don't know if we are getting any more healthcare out of this so-called market.' Sir Duncan Nichol, health service chief executive, said: 'I believe we should not be talking about the bureaucratic overhead here; we should be talking about the benefits that management have brought to the health service.'
