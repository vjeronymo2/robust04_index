940913 FT 13 SEP 94 / Arts: Today's Television Today's current affairs offerings look more promising than anything in the drama and light entertainment categories, though ITV's half-hour police drama, The Bill, maintains a high standard (8.00). BBC2 reports on the increasingly tough, and increasingly American, attitude towards student grants in the UK in Taken For Granted. Demanding that people 'work their way' through university is fine when there are jobs to be had, but more problematical during periods of high unemployment. Even with government loans to students we now seem perilously close to a system which provides higher education mainly for those with rich parents (7.20). Dirty Money opened last week with a racy account of the (pretty fruitless) attempt to reduce cocaine dealing by confiscating the suppliers' funds. Today's programme (10.25 BBC1) is about the rapid growth of 'gangster capitalism' in Russia where there seems to be little legislation to say what you can and cannot do. ITV ends its exhaustive investigation of 'acquaintance rape' in Network First with the actual trial. (10.40).
