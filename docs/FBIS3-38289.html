Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 26/1/1994 -- Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu signed on Wednesday afternoon [26 January] the framework document concerning Romania's participation in the Partnership for Peace, an initiative launched at the NATO summit on January 10-11. The signing ceremony which took place in the NATO Council room was attended by NATO Assistant Secretary General Amedeo de Franchis and by the permanent representatives to the North Atlantic Council of the 16 member countries. Due to this political act, which succeeds the acceptance of the invitation to the Partnership for Peace expressed by Romanian President Ion Iliescu in a statement on January 11, Romania has become officially the first country to be engaged in the new cooperation pattern with the NATO. At the signing ceremony, Amedeo de Franchis and Teodor Melescanu read out brief speeches stressing the political significance of the moment and expressing hope that the Partnership for Peace would contribute to the consolidation of the security and stability in whole Europe. Romanian Foreign Minister Melescanu underlined that Romania committed itself with all faith and responsibility to the new relation with NATO and with the NATO member countries, a relation considered by Romania as a preliminary and preparatory stage to Romania's future membership to NATO's political and military structures. Previous to the signing ceremony, the Romanian minister of foreign affairs had an interview with Amedeo de Franchis and met also with the permanent representatives to the North Atlantic Council of the 16 NATO member countries. The NATO Assistant Secretary General Amedeo de Franchis saluted the "historic moment" when Romania became the first "cooperation partner" of the alliance, through the signing the framework document of the Partnership for Peace. In a speech on Wednesday in Brussels, in the presence of Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu, the NATO assistant secretary general praised the courage and decision proven by the Romanian people in his fight for liberty. Today, Romania experiences a difficult process of political and economic reform in the interior but, at the same time, it works for the creation of new security relations in the exterior, said Amedeo de Franchis.
