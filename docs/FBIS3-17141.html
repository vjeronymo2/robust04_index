Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "In Belarus, the Specter of Zhirinovskiy Has Mutated Into the Independent Party"] [Text] Alyaksandr Leshla, former first deputy of Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, has told our newspaper that the Liberal Democratic Party of Belarus [LDPB] has been finally registered. The party has about 4,000 members. Its largest branches are in Minsk, Homel, and Vitsebsk. The Belarusian liberal democrats are closely cooperating with the Party of People's Accord, and they are going to actively participate in the parliamentary elections. The main goal of the LDPB is to establish a united Slavic state composed of Russia, Ukraine, and Belarus.
