Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu met with representatives of the Cartel Alfa and the National Trade Union Bloc Confederations at Victoria Palace today. The meeting was attended by Mircea Dan Popescu, state minister and minister of labor and social protection; Emil Dima, president of the Administration Board of the State Property Fund; State Secretary Octavian Partenie, empowered by the government to handle relations with trade unions and employers; and states secretaries, directors, and experts from the Office for Liaison With Trade Unions and Employers and the Ministry of Labor and Social Protection. The participants in the meeting discussed the conclusions drawn from negotiations on the points on the list of demands made by the two trade union confederations, especially the government's proposals to solve them. Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu presented the solutions proposed by the executive power to gradually solve the problems raised by the trade unions, which can be solved satisfactorily in the current economic situation. The sides agreed on a greater involvement of trade unions in implementing the reform process, and especially in working out measures and solutions to alleviate social effects, a press release received from the Office for Liaison With Trade Unions and Employers attached to Romania's Government specifies.
