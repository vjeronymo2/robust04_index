BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Murat Karayalcin, state minister and deputy prime minister, who is holding contacts in Skopje, the capital of The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM], has held a joint news conference with FYROM Prime Minister Branko Crvenkovski. Speaking at the news conference, Karayalcin said that the relations between the socialists and the social democrats in the Balkan countries are very important, adding that these relations will, at the same time, contribute to regional peace. Pointing to the importance of historic ties of friendship with Macedonia, Karayalcin said that there are many Macedonians in Turkey and that they are engaged in successful work. Noting that during his contacts in the FYROM developments in the Balkan region and existing areas of cooperation were discussed, Karayalcin added that Turkey will send corn to the FYROM. He recalled that a $25,000 [as heard] loan agreement through Eximbank was signed between the two countries, adding that this loan will be extended in September or October. Crvenkovski, also speaking at the news conference, gave details on his meetings with Karayalcin. He stressed that an agreement has been reached on the continuation of the existing relations. Crvenkovski added that his country received a lot of support from Turkey during its difficult periods. Karayalcin attended a dinner in his honor hosted by Crvenkovski this evening. He also visited the Turkish Embassy in Skopje. He will return to Turkey tomorrow.
