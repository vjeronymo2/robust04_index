BFN [Report by David Regev] [Excerpt] The wave of detentions against opponents of the peace process in the Gaza Strip continues. Twenty-five Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] and Islamic Jihad activists in the Strip were arrested over the past 24 hours by the security forces, Arab sources reported yesterday. According to the sources, the activists were detained in their homes and the security forces also visited the homes of 15 activists and fugitives who were not at home, and demanded their relatives give them up. Among those detained were Shaykh Nafar A'zam, 40, of Rafah, an Islamic Jihad leader who was put under administrative detention for six months. A'zam was released several weeks ago after a long period of imprisonment. A senior activist of the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine, Walid al-Rul, 40, of al-Shati', was put under administrative detention for six months for the ninth time. Arab sources said he has been under detention for 53 months running. Khalil Bal'awi, 24, a Hamas wanted man, was captured by security forces in Gaza yesterday. Bal'awi is suspected of terrorist activity against the security forces, including firing at IDF [Israel Defense Forces] patrols. [passage omitted]
