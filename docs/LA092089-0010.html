September 20, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

ARMENIAN QUAKE

I must applaud the work of your Moscow Bureau chief, Michael Parks, in his effort to keep readers up to date on the progress of the reconstruction efforts in earthquake devastated Armenia (Part I, Sept. 5).

Although the reconstruction continues, it is at a snail's pace. The longer this effort takes the larger the impact on the psyche of the 1/2 million homeless survivors, poetically described as "refugees in their own land." This is why the Earthquake Relief Fund for Armenia (ERFA) has made it its priority to see to the mental well-being of the survivors.

Since January, ERFA has dispatched seven groups of mental health specialists under the direction of Dr. Armen Goenjian and Dr. Sarkis Arevian. Focusing their efforts on the children of the earthquake, these teams have serviced over 3,000 patients. In addition, they have held numerous seminars and conferences to teach their techniques to local professionals.

PAPKEN SASSOUNI

Chairman, Earthquake

Relief Fund for Armenia

Glendale
