930421 FT 21 APR 93 / UK Company News: NatWest Bancorp jumps to Dollars 61m NATWEST Bancorp, the US subsidiary of National Westminster Bank, yesterday disclosed a doubling of pre-tax profits for the first quarter of the year, marking its fifth quarter of recovery from losses at the turn of the decade. Net income for the quarter rose from Dollars 30.1m to Dollars 60.7m (Pounds 40.1m). The result included the recognition of Dollars 9.5m of federal tax benefits which had previously not been recorded. The bank achieved a 13.03 per cent return on equity (7.03 per cent). Its ratio of tier 1 capital to risk-weighted assets rose to 7.94 per cent (6.47 per cent) and total risk-asset ratio to 12.69 per cent (10.97 per cent). Mr John Tugwell, NatWest Bancorp's chairman and chief executive, said the bank had put a difficult period behind it and was now building on a strong recovery. He said there had been steady improvements in asset quality. The provision for loan losses in the quarter was Dollars 30m (Dollars 30.5m), while loan charge-offs fell sharply to Dollars 27.9m (Dollars 61.7m). The allowance for loan losses on the balance sheet was Dollars 612m, or 4.42 per cent of total loans outstanding. National Westminster's shares closed 11p up at 472p.
