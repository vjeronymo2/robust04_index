BFN [Text] Lebanon was officially invited by the United States, Russia, and Egypt to take part in the Middle East multilateral talks on refugees scheduled to be held in Cairo on 10-12 May. The invitation was conveyed by the American, Russian, and Egyptian ambassadors to Ambassador Zafir al-Hasan, the Foreign Ministry secretary general, today. The Egyptian ambassador said after the meeting that Lebanon should always take part in the Middle East peace negotiations because Lebanon is a principal, important, and effective party in the peace process. The Russian ambassador expressed the conviction that Lebanon will take part in the multilateral negotiations, noting that Lebanon is entitled to choose the stance it likes. The Russian envoy said it would be in Lebanon's interest to take part in these negotiations. The U.S. ambassador said that Secretary of State Warren Christopher is unlikely to visit Lebanon, adding that the ongoing talks could achieve progress. Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz today received a written message from his Sudanese counterpart, Husayn Abu-Salih. The message was forwarded by the Sudanese ambassador to Lebanon. It contains the Sudanese Government's thanks to Minister Buwayz and the Lebanese Government for their support for Sudan during the Arab League Council's meetings in March, when Minister Buwayz backed a decision condemning plans to partition Sudan.
