BFN [Text] Beijing, May 10 (XINHUA) -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin met here this afternoon with foreign participants in the 1994 China summit meeting, a high-profile economic forum scheduled to open here tomorrow. The two-day meeting is to be convened by the INTERNATIONAL HERALD TRIBUNE (IHT) and China's State Commission for Restructuring the Economy with the theme of "The Chinese socialist market economy: cooperation with global business". Malaysian Prime Minister Mohamed Mahathir, Director-General of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade Peter Sutherland, the World Bank's Managing Director Ernest Stern, and Helmut Schmidt, former chancellor of the Federal Republic of Germany, will attend the summit meeting. It marks the first time ever for China to hold such a high-level economic forum. Present at today's meeting were IHT Publisher Richard McClean, Percy Barnevik, president of Asea Brown Boveri Ltd. (ABB), Ronald B. Woodard, president of the Boeing commercial airplane group, Patrick J. Ward, chairman of Caltex Petroleum Corporation, and Philip Tose, chairman of the Peregrine Investments Holdings Ltd. Jiang extended a welcome to them on their participation in the forum and exchanged views with the guests on issues of strengthening international exchanges and cooperation. Li Tieying, State Councillor and minister in charge of the State Commission for Restructuring the Economy, was also present at today's meeting in the Great Hall of the People.
