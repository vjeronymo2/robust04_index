Language: Vietnamese Article Type:BFN [Text] The Criminal Court of the People's Supreme Court entered its second day of work today. The case in this trial, which is preliminary as well as final, involves roundabout dealings with 4,000 tons of steel designed for the construction of the north-south 500-kilovolt powerline. These dealings caused losses of 3.1 billion dong to the state budget. Mr. Mai Ngoc Chinh, chief judge of the Criminal Court of the Supreme People's Court, presided over the court session. Today, the defendants -- including Doan Trung Thanh, Doan Thi Huu Nghi, Vu Ngoc Hai [former energy minister], Le Lien, Pham Dao Khanh, Dinh Ngoc Lac, Nguyen Dinh Lam, Nguyen Duc Ai, and Nguyen Trong Sang -- continued giving testimony in response to examination by lawyers and a juridical council. Many witnesses also testified before the juridical council in a bid to shed more light on the details of the case as well as the magnitude of the offense committed by the defendants. The court will continue its session tomorrow.
