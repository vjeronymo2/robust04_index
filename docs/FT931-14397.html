930119 FT 19 JAN 93 / Council reforms are challenged GOVERNMENT claims that its proposed reform of local government in Scotland would lead to large cost savings have been challenged by Strathclyde regional council, Scotland's largest local authority and the most likely to disappear if the reform goes ahead. A council working party says savings would be much less - and transitional costs far higher - than consultants Touche Ross calculated in a study for the Scottish Office.
