BFN [Text] According to the Chief of the Russian Peace-Keeping Forces Headquarters, General Kozlov, the introduction of peace-keepers into Abhazia has been completed virtually "without incidents." A Moscow-based Abkhazi spokesman told INTERFAX that 2500 Russian peacekeepers have taken up positions on both sides of the border between Abkhazia and Georgia. A total of 1400 paratroopers strong battalions are deployed in a 12-kilometer security zone. Two motorized infantry battalions from Adzharia are on the other side of the frontier. Engineers from the Russian peace-keeping force in the area of the Georgian-Abkhaz conflict began to reconstruct the road bridge over the Inguri river in the evening of June 27. The river forms the dividing line between the two opposing sides. The press center of the peace-keeping force told INTERFAX that the bridge will be completely rebuilt within a week. Peace-keeping troops took control of the Inguri Power Station, the largest in Georgia. Starting Monday a curfew has been announced in the security zone around the Inguri river from 22:00 to 6:00.
