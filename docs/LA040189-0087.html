April 1, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

PADRES' ROB NELSON SHOWS PERSEVERANCE

How unfortunate that the gross misconceptions about dyslexia held by San Diego Padres farm director Tom Romanesko have contributed to Rob Nelson's inability to be given a fair shot at the big leagues.

Dyslexia is a disorder characterized by the brain's difficulty in processing the written information it receives. It does not affect an individual's ability to hit a baseball, nor a particular pitch.

I believe Rob Nelson's dedication and perseverance would make him a valued member of any major league team, many of whose members, past and present, lack the strength of character that this gifted man possesses.

MARK BASKIN, M.D.

Encino
