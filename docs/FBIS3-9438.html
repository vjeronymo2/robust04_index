Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Geneva, March 18 (AFP) -- The latest round of talks on the division of former Yugoslavia's assets were deadlocked Friday [18 March] due to Belgrade's failure to provide realistic figures, diplomatic sources said. Talks reopened Thursday and were continuing Friday between the rump state of Yugoslavia, made up of Serbia and Montenegro, and the other former Yugoslav republics of Slovenia, Croatia, Bosnia-Herzegovina and Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM], Geneva peace conference spokesman John Mills said. But non-Serb sources said the talks were as unproductive as earlier sessions because the Serbian authorities refused to provide documents needed for a realistic evaluation of the former state's assets. Mills said talks by the working group, headed by Danish ambassador to Berne Els Johansson, would be "a drawn-out process." Another ex-Yugoslav working group, on humanitarian issues, was due to meet Friday afternoon in Geneva headed by Sadako Ogata of the UN High Commission for Refugees who returned recently from a visit to Bosnia-Herzegovina.
