May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

JOGGER TAKES FIRST STEPS SINCE GANG ASSAULT

The woman jogger who was raped and beaten by a gang of youths in Central Park last month has taken her first, shaky steps since the attack and is able to write her name again, her doctor said.

Dr. Robert S. Kurtz said, however, that it remains unknown how fully she will recover.

"The more small neurologic improvements are registered, the more clearly apparent it becomes how great a distance she has yet to travel," said Kurtz, director of surgical intensive care at Metropolitan Hospital. "She is now able to take a few walking steps, although her gait is still broad-based and unsteady."

She also can now write her name in a shaky hand, he said.

The 28-year-old investment banker was assaulted April 19 as she took her nightly exercise in the park. Six teen-agers have been indicted in the crime.
