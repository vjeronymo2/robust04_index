910502 FT 02 MAY 91 / UK Company News: Panel censures bidder for Merlin Intl The bidder for Merlin International Properties and his financial adviser, Fininvest, have been censured by the Takeover Panel for failing to ensure that the necessary funds would be available. The Pounds 2.9m bid, subsequently withdrawn, was made by newly-formed Luirc, based offshore and owned by Swiss-based Estonia Venture. The money was supposed to come via a loan from Bonaventure Investments, another offshore concern. The panel has criticised Mr Arthur Oaks, Luirc's UK representative, and Fininvest. It said no announcement should have been made before Fininvest had received an irrevocable commitment from Bonaventure to provide funds. General Principle 3 had been offended, which says offers should only be announced when the bidder is sure it can implement the offer. The principle also attaches responsibility to the financial adviser. The panel stressed that particular care was needed 'when a financial adviser is acting for a newly created offeror, such as an off-the-shelf overseas company.'
