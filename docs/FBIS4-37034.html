BFN [Text] Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa left Tunis this morning for Malta on a three-day official visit after participating in the African summit meetings. In a predeparture statement, he said that President Mubarak's participation in the African summit and his inaugural speech played an extremely important role in advancing efforts toward resolving African problems. These efforts were in completion of Egypt's tasks during its chairmanship of the OAU. He said that Egypt presented proposals and held consultations that were welcomed and appreciated by all delegations. They contributed significantly towards resolving problems discussed during the summit, particularly the issues of development and debts. Musa said that his visit to Malta is within the framework of cooperation among the Mediterranean countries and preparation for a meeting among a number of those countries' foreign ministers to study the situation in the region and coordinate policies. He noted he will also discuss ways of bolstering ties between Egypt and Malta in all fields.
