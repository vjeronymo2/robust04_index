Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan has made it clear to India that no further talks will be possible unless situation in occupied Kashmir is improved and human right violations are stopped. This was stated by Foreign Secretary Mr. Shaharyar M. Khan while talking to newsmen at Islamabad Airport after the departure of the Indian foreign secretary. [Begin Khan recording] We shall not schedule any talks, either at the foreign secretary level or at any other level, until there is a visible improvement of the situation in Indian-held Kashmir. Pakistan maintained its principled position that a solution to the Kashmir issue lay in a plebiscite on the basis of UN resolutions. For Pakistan, the people of Kashmir are the central players, because it is they who must determine their future. We believe that everyone concerned -- the Security Council, India, Pakistan, we have all agreed to a method, a format for ascertaining the wishes of the Kashmiri people and that is in a plebiscite. [end recording] Earlier, addressing a joint press conference at the airport before his departure, the Indian foreign secretary said we have met after such a long time and have addressed the specific question of Jammu and Kashmir. Mr. Shaharyar M. Khan said that it was the first time that both sides agreed to discuss the question of Kashmir meaningfully and purposefully. Both the foreign secretaries agreed that the Kashmiris are central to the problem and they would be taken into confidence for the resolution of the Kashmir problem. In a joint statement issued in Islamabad today at the end of the two-day talks, Pakistan and India reiterated the need to engage in a meaningful dialogue with a view of addressing all the outstanding problems, including Jammu and Kashmir. The statement said the talks addressed all aspects of Jammu and Kashmir problem, and both sides recognized that there are basic divergences. It was agreed that sincere efforts would be made to resolve the problem. The statement said the two sides will consult each other on the question of further talks at the foreign secretary level or any other level. At the conclusion of the third round of talks between Pakistan and India, the Indian delegation led by the foreign secretary, Mr. J.N. Dixit, called on the foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, at the Foreign Office. Other members of the Indian delegation also attended the meeting. Foreign Secretary Shaharyar M. Khan and other members of the Pakistan delegation were also present during the meeting.
