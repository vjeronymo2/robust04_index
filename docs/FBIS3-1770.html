Language: Bengali Article Type:BFN [Text] Qian Qichen, PRC vice premier and foreign minister, met Bangladeshi Prime Minister Khaleda Zia at Prime Minister House in Dhaka on 26 February. During the talks, the two sides expressed satisfaction over the smooth development of cordial relations and cooperation between the two countries. Prime Minister Khaleda Zia said that the Government of Bangladesh attaches special importance to friendly ties and cooperation with China. The people of Bangladesh are grateful for the Chinese assistance. Qian Qichen said that China and Bangladesh have a long-standing history of friendly relations and their bilateral ties have received fresh impetus during the past few years. He added that China is satisfied with this. The PRC Government all along has been emphasizing the development of its cordial and cooperative relationship with Bangladesh. Chinese Foreign Minister Qian Qichen said that he held fruitful discussions with Bangladeshi Foreign Minister Mustafizur Rahman during his visit. The current visit will certainly accelerate the development of the overall multifaceted cooperative relationship between the two countries. During the meeting, Foreign Minister Qian Qichen conveyed PRC Premier Li Peng's greetings and invitation to visit China to Prime Minister Khaleda Zia. Prime Minister Khaleda Zia gladly accepted the invitation and requested Foreign Minister Qian Qichen to convey to Premier Li Peng her good wishes and an invitation to visit Bangladesh. Foreign Minister Qian Qichen of China held a news conference at the [word indistinct] State Guest House in Dhaka yesterday. He answered various questions by journalists on the international and regional situation and China's relations with the Third World. On the issue of making South Asia a nuclear-free zone, Foreign Minister Qian Qichen said that the PRC favors the establishment of a nuclear-free zone in South Asia. China hopes that there will be no arms race in South Asia because any arms race is harmful to peace and stability in the region. On Sino-Indian relations, Foreign Minister Qian Qichen said that we are satisfied with the development of Sino-Indian relations after the normalization of relations between the two countries. We feel that development of Sino-Indian relations should not affect China's cordial relations and cooperation with other South Asian nations. We hope that development of Sino-Indian relation will be beneficial to the development of China's cooperative relations with South Asian countries. Foreign Minister Qian Qichen left Dhaka for Sri Lanka yesterday afternoon, ending his four-day state and goodwill visit to Bangladesh. Foreign Minister Qian Qichen visited the Parliament House of Bangladesh yesterday morning and met Sheikh Hasina, chairman of the Awami League and the leader of the opposition.
