920806 FT 06 AUG 92 / Arts: Today's Television The event of the evening is a repeat: Soldiering On, a monologue by Stephanie Cole, one of Alan Bennett's acclaimed Talking Heads. She is a widow confronting the lonely years ahead, which material comfort will do little to alleviate. As ever in Bennett, the mask slips to uncover the suppressed undercurrents of despair (BBC 2 at 9.35pm). The US held tremendous interest, and often fat lecturing fees, for the literary greats of Victorian England and Early Travellers in North America (BBC 2 at 10.10) is proving a pleasant anecdotal series. In tonight's programme, looking at reactions to the native Indians, Dickens meets an Indian chief who is fond of reading Sir Walter Scott. This week's mini series is Conspiracy of Silence, which ITV wraps around the 10.00 news tonight and tomorrow. It tells the true story of the murder of a Cree Indian girl in Manitoba in 1971 and the difficulty of bringing to justice her four attackers who were protected by their local community. Men Talk (Channel 4, 10.20) covers that almost unmentionable subject of male-bonding in a programme entitled 'All Boys Together.'
