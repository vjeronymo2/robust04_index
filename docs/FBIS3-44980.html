Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [Text] According to statistics provided by the provincial statistical bureau, in 1993 Fujian's GNP was estimated at 95.349 billion yuan, an increase of 25.4 percent over the previous year as calculated on comparable prices, being the second top production year since the founding of the PRC and also the top production year since the introduction of reform and opening up, thus quadrupling the 1980 strategic objective seven months ahead of schedule. Yesterday the provincial government held a news briefing to report on the above situation. In the meantime, the news briefing pointed out: There are still outstanding problems in the province's current economic life. Its agricultural foundation is still weak, market price increases are comparatively high, the investment structure is not quite rational, funds are still short of supply, and agricultural production is proceeding with difficulties.
