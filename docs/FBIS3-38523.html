Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- Chancellor Helmut Kohl has underlined the importance of economic relations between Germany and India. Kohl said today at the start of a German-Indian economic conference at the Bonn Chancellery, in the presence of Indian Prime Minister P. V. Narisimha Rao, that this conference underlined the "special importance" that the German Government attached to relations between the two countries. Rao arrived in Germany for a two-day visit to Bonn on 2 February. Kohl welcomed the prime minister with military honors this morning at the Chancellery. Afterward they met for talks. Rao is also to visit Berlin on the 4th and 5th February. At the economic conference, the chancellor said that relations between Germany and India in the science and technology areas had always been very close and are characterized by cooperation in a spirit of trust on many projects. Kohl announced that it was agreed today to set up a high-level German-Indian Commission for Science and Technology. Mutual economic relations had developed "gratifyingly" in the past few years. Today, Germany was India's second most important supplier and fourth most important market. German investments in India were "considerable, but could still increase." Rao welcomed German industry's Asian offensive. This project was appropriate to the positive economic developments in Asia, he said. The economic conference, the climax of Rao's visit, was opened by Siemens head Heinrich von Pierer, the chairman of the Asia-Pacific Association of German industry. A large number of Indian and German business representatives took part in the conference. [passage omitted]
