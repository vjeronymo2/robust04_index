Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] In a joint declaration Paraguayan President Juan Carlos Wasmosy and Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada last night described the Pilcomayo River and the areas around it as a decisive factor in promoting integration among Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay, and decided to invite Argentine President Carlos Saul Menem to a meeting with a view to seeking formulas that would be instrumental in jointly undertaking mutually beneficial development projects. Paraguay and Bolivia also stated that they are committed to fighting the drug trade, and agreed to hold permanent consultations between senior officers of their two countries. The Paraguayan Government supports "the search for a negotiated agreement" to provide Bolivia with an outlet to the Pacific Ocean. Sanchez de Lozada has characterized this goal as "an unrenounceable right of the Bolivian people." In the joint declaration, which was signed at Lopez Palace, Wasmosy and Sanchez de Lozada attached special significance to the Pilcomayo River, but did not go into detail. They agreed, however, to invite President Menem to a joint meeting to undertake joint projects. They have not set any specific date for this meeting. They have also acknowledged that the abuse of and illegal trade in narcotics and dangerous substances are a serious threat to the health and well-being of all peoples, and noted that all nations must assume their share of responsibility in fighting this scourge at every level and in all of its forms. They reiterated their commitment to fighting the drug trade, and agreed to urgently implement bilateral actions. To strengthen friendly relations and cooperation between their two countries, they have decided -- based on a proposal from the Bolivian Government -- to hold consultations between senior officers of their respective Armed Forces, through periodic meetings to be held at least once a year, alternating in Bolivia and Paraguay.
