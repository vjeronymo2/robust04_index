Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beginning Tuesday Viktor Luchin, a judge on Russia's Constitutional Court [CC], has announced a hunger strike due to the unwillingness of the Constitutional Court's judges to make any decision regarding his authority, Luchin's consultant Mikhail Moyseyenko reported to Interfax. The authority of Viktor Luchin, as well as that of Valeriy Zorkin, was suspended December 1, 1993, due to accusations of political activity, which is inadmissible for CC judges. In particular, Luchin was accused of his participation in the congresses of Russia's communist party, at the congress of former People's Deputies, and in the election campaign waged last autumn. However, last November 15 Luchin removed his candidacy from lists of the Agrarian party, and there have been no congresses of the communist party or former People's Deputies of the USSR in the course of the previous year. Luchin believes that there are no grounds to suspend his authority. According to Moyseyenko, Luchin intends to continue his hunger strike until the court passes a decision. As far as Valeriy Zorkin is concerned, he disapproves Luchin's decision, reported Moyseyenko.
