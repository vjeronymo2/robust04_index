BFN [Text] Pakistan and Libya have held wide-ranging talks on a number of bilateral and international issues, including Kashmir, Bosnia, the situation in Afghanistan, Palestine, and Central Asia. The talks were held in Islamabad today between the foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, and his Libyan counterpart, Mr. 'Umar Mustafa al-Muntasir. The foreign minister of Libya was conveyed the gratitude of the Government and people of Pakistan for the assurances given by the Libyan leader Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi for full support to Pakistan on Kashmir. The Libyan foreign minister was informed about Pakistan's efforts to promote an amicable solution to the issue of UN sanctions against Libya. The two foreign ministers also deliberated on issues about the ratification of the draft administrative procedures relating to the social security agreement between Pakistan and Libya, payment of disability pension to Pakistani workers, release of outstanding payments of various Pakistani concerns by Libya, and possibility of export of Pakistani manpower to Libya. The issue of enhancing economic cooperation between the two countries also came up for discussion. The two sides agreed that the joint ministerial commission should be held at an early date.
