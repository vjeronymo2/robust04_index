BFN [Text] Kuwait, 24 Jun (MENA) -- Kuwaiti Interior Minister Shaykh 'Ali Sabah al-Salim has said there is full cooperation and coordination between Egypt and Kuwait on various issues concerning the two countries, especially relating to security. He noted that there is continuous consultation among officials. In a statement to MENA, he denied any form of extremism or terrorism in Kuwait, be it intellectual or ideological. He emphasized that everyone has the right to openly state his view within the law and general controls. He said security in Kuwait is very stable and that everything is now under control. He added that most of the negative security aspects that emerged after the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait have been eliminated. He affirmed that security reports coming in from the various sectors indicate that Kuwait now enjoys greater security and stability than before. He denied there were any human rights violations in Kuwait. He said there are around 180,000 maids from various countries in Kuwait, who enjoy their full rights. He added that the Kuwaiti authorities do not approve of anyone's material or moral rights being violated in Kuwait and noted malicious foreign media reports trying to damage Kuwait's reputation. He said Kuwait respects and preserves human rights and the security and safety of all workers in Kuwait, adding that there are around 1 million foreigners from 120 countries working in Kuwait and that their rights are respected. Commenting on talk of tension between National Assembly deputies and the Kuwaiti Interior Ministry, he said relations between ministry officials and the National Assembly are proceeding well. He noted that everyone's basic goal is to preserve public order and stabilize the situation on every level.
