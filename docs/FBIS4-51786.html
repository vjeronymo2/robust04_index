BFN [Text] Seoul, April 30 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and Russia have initiated a new aviation agreement superseding the existing one, the Foreign Ministry announced over the weekend. The pact, resulting from the bilateral talks held here Thursday and Friday, stipulates for a reduced scope of matters for negotiations between airlines and new airport fees. Deputy Foreign Minister for Trade Affairs Kim Kwang-tong initiated the pact for Korea and acting Aviation Director Tumanov of the Transportation Ministry for Russia. The agreement will be formally concluded after the two countries take necessary steps. The two countries have also agreed to proceed with government- and private-level negotiations about air routes between them, including the trans-Siberian routes.
