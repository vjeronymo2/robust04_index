920217 FT 17 FEB 92 / Companies identified as river polluters NEARLY 150 companies listed in the FTA All-Share Index are guilty of exceeding river pollution discharge limits, according to a survey carried out by the Ethical Investment Research Service, Scheherazade Daneshkhu writes. Of the index's 590 companies, 149 were either prosecuted or were found to have exceeded pollution limits set by the National Rivers Authority in England and Wales and the River Purification Boards in Scotland within the three years to March last year. Some companies were found to have exceeded the limits more than 35 times in the 12 months to March last year. They include Allied Lyons, BTR, British Steel, Grand Metropolitan, Guinness, Hanson, ICI, Shell Transport & Trading and SmithKline Beecham. Seventy-five companies were convicted after prosecution by the authority or a Scottish board within the three years to March last year. These included BTR, Unilever, BT, Allied Lyons, General Electric and ICI as well as all 10 privatised water companies. Water Pollution. EIRIS, Freepost, London SW8. Pounds 15.
