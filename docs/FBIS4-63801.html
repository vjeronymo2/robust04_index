BFN [Text] In a press release today, the Estonian Foreign Ministry claims that the number of persons in Estonia demobilized from the former USSR Armed Forces is on the increase. On 19 May Aleksandr Trofimov, Russian Ambassador to Estonia, presented figures to President Lennart Meri, showing that the number of the Russian military pensioners has risen from 9,040 to 10,640. According to the Foreign Ministry, about 1,600 Russian military pensioners living in Estonia are under 50; almost 4,000 are aged 50 to 60; and 5,170 are over 60. At the same time the press release states that the Estonian authorities have difficulties obtaining information from the Russian Government about the size of the Russian military units deployed in Estonia and the number of military pensioners in Estonia. Additionally, it is unknown how many Russian servicemen are engaged in Estonia in civilian clothes, the Foreign Ministry's press release says.
