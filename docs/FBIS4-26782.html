BFN [Text] Beijing, June 25 (XINHUA) -- The Communist Party of China (CPC) admitted 1.76 million new members and set up over 50,000 new grass-root organizations last year, according to the CPC central committee's organization department. So far, the party has a total of 54 million members with 3.34 million basic organizations. Of the new members, 742,000, or 43.2 percent, are workers, farmers, herdsmen and fishermen; and 300,000, or 16.9 percent, are technical professionals. The number of new party members under 35 years old reached 1.194 million, accounting for 68 percent of the total new members, and the number of women and ethnic minorities also registered a big increase. The number of party members with an education background at and above the senior middle school level has expanded from 7.308 million in 1983 to 20.653 million at present.
