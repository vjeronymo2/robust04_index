Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] An opinion poll carried out by Gestinform reveals that if the multiparty elections were held in January this year, there would be no absolute majorities both in presidential and legislative elections. In the opinion poll, in which 4,000 people were interviewed throughout the country, Joaquim Chissano, Mozambique Liberation Front [Frelimo] presidential candidate, has 45.4 percent of the votes, followed by Mozambique National Resistance's [Renamo] Afonso Dhlakama with 32.4 percent. Dr. Domingos Arouca, leader of the Mozambique United Front has 3.3 percent of the votes, followed by Mozambican Nationalist Movement's [Monamo] Maximo Dias with 2.4 percent. President of the Republic Joaquim Chissano obtained the most votes in Gaza Province, his original home area, where he received 60 percent of the votes, and Inhambane where he received 61 percent. The Renamo presidential candidate obtained the best results -- 49.3 percent -- in Sofala Province where Renamo's general headquarters is found. Regarding legislative elections, Frelimo obtained 38.1 percent of the votes and Renamo 34.2 percent. Monamo received 4.1 percent, Fumo 3.6 percent, the National Convention Party 2.8 percent, while the remaining parties received 4.2 percent combined.
