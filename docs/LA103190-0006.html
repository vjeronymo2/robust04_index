October 31, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

KHALIDI ON ISRAEL AND PALESTINIANS

If Khalidi is a teacher of modern Middle Eastern history, he conveniently forgets about Israel's peace treaty with Egypt. Egypt got the entire Sinai back with its oil resources and peace with Israel as well.

Khalidi mentions Resolution 242, which calls "for an end to the state of war." Why don't Khalidi and the other Arab intellectuals suggest to the other Arab nations that peace treaties with Israel probably would lead to a withdrawal from occupied territories?

Khalidi knows, of course, that Dennis Prager (Commentary, Oct. 12) is correct in stating that the Arabs will settle for nothing less than the destruction of Israel.

Articles, such as Khalidi's, are only rhetoric that will not convince intelligent, unbiased readers.

MARTIN J. WEISMAN, Woodland Hills
