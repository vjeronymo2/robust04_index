921002 FT 02 OCT 92 / Survey of Software at Work - Autumn 1992 (27): Family work in the community - Society Snapshot The Childrens's Society (see case study: facing page) is the UK's third largest child-care charity. Its objectives are to help children and young people to grow in communities and families. It runs more than 150 projects in the areas of family work in the community, (55 family centres); residential care (48 homes); street work with homeless children, (seven 'safe homes'); and works in the areas of family placement and fostering. Income: Pounds 21,046,000 (1991); Pounds 19,423,000 (1990). Expenditure: Pounds 22,446,00, (Pounds 201,000 deficit, 1991). The greatest part of its funding derives from legacies and wills, with the balance coming from church donations and fund-raising, plus some local authority and government grants. Employees: 1,200. Key personnel: Ian Sparks, Iain Pritchard (IT consultant) and Roger Beschizza, in charge of fund-raising and direct mail. TECHNOLOGY FILE: Software: Infobase database and Fact2000 integrated accounting systems provided by CMG Business Systems of Feltham, Middlesex. Hardware: IBM RS/6000 runing the Unix operating system. Other systems: the accounts and contacts database are linked to standard PC products used on the PC network, including word processing, spreadsheets, electronic mail and desktop publishing. Contract value: investment in the new system amounts to Pounds 295,000, including hardware, software, implementation and training.
