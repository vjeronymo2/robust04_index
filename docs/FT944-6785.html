941128 FT 28 NOV 94 / UK Company News: Alphameric expands and lifts profit 81% Alphameric, the information technology systems supplier, has announced a number of actions as part of its expansion plans. They include a Pounds 2.75m acquisition, a Pounds 1.89m placing and open offer, a share subscription and the conversion of preference shares. The company also announ-ced pre-tax profits of Pounds 323,000 for the six months to September 30, a rise of 81 per cent on the comparable Pounds 178,000. It is acquiring the outstanding 25 per cent in AlphaServ by buying Ambrose Consultancy for Pounds 2.75m satisfied by the issue of 6.88m shares of which 2.67m are being placed at 37.5p a share. There is a 3-for-13 clawback. A further 3.56m shares are being placed under the same terms to raise Pounds 885,000 for working capital and to fund expansion. Sejin Electron, a South Korean keyboard manufacturer, is subscribing for 3m shares at 41.5p, subject to South Korean government approval. The company also plans to convert the 500,000 9 3/4 per cent convertible preference shares held by 3i into 1.2m ordinary shares. The conversion rate is 2.41 per convertible against the 1.38 set out in the articles of association. If the plan goes through Alphameric will pay the Pounds 137,000 arrears on the preference shares. An EGM is to be held on December 19. The profit rise was achieved on turnover 19 per cent ahead at Pounds 6.3m (Pounds 5.28m). Earnings per share doubled to 0.8p (0.4p). Mr Alan Benjamin, chairman, said that if the present level of progress was sustained the company could be in a position to restore dividends in 1994-95. The shares rose 1p to 41p on Friday.
