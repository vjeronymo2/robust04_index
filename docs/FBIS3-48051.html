Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, Jan 22 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- President Alija Izetbegovic arrived here Friday with a 12-member delegation for a four-day working visit. On hand to receive the Bosnian leeder at the Kuala Lumpur International Airport were Foreign Minister Abdullah Ahmad Badawi, Law Minister Syed Hamid Albar, who is the minister-in-attendance, chairman of the Malaysia Parliamentary Committee on Bosnia- Herzegovina (MPC-BH) Dominic Puthucheary, and other officials. President Alija, whose delegation includes Foreign Minister Dr. Irfan Ljubijankic, is scheduled to address the international conference of parliamentarians on Bosnia-Herzegovina and meet Prime Minister Dr. Mahathir Mohamed for talks. Arriving from Geneva, President Alija became the first state visitor to receive the quarter-guard salute from a team of eight Royal Malay Regiment on arrival at the airport. A Wisma Putra (Foreign Ministry) official said the ceremony was conducted upon recommendation by Dr. Mahathir, who picked the idea during his recent visit to Europe. During his four-day visit, President Alija would also be meeting the Bosnian community here and having a dialogue with ambassadors from members of the Organisation of International Conference countries. President Alija will also have an audience with the Yang Dipertuan Agung (King) Sultan Azlan Shah on Monday.
