940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / Bulgaria begins mass sell-off Bulgaria's long-delayed mass privatisation through vouchers came into force yesterday. The scheme, modelled on the pioneering Czech sell-off, entitles adult Bulgarians to privatisation vouchers representing assets with a book value of Lev25,000 (about Pounds 300) on payment of Lev500. The vouchers can be exchanged for shares in around 500 state-owned enterprises, with assets totalling Lev110.8bn. Private investment funds will be created to manage and invest the vouchers on behalf of the public. Officials from the newly founded Centre for Mass Privatisation are drafting a separate law to govern investment funds. Meanwhile, Bulgaria is due to sign a debt reduction agreement with the London Club of commercial bank creditors in Sofia today. The deal will cut by 47.1 per cent Bulgaria's Dollars 8.16bn debt to more than 300 commercial creditor banks led by Deutsche Bank.
