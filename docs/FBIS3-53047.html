Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin conferred this afternoon with Kenyan President Daniel arap Moi. Kenya would like Israel to help it obtain aid from the United States, to expand its agricultural assistance, and to grant more scholarships to Kenyan students. President Moi told Mr. Rabin about his visits in Kuwait and Saudi Arabia. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 1900 GMT on 9 January reports that "President Daniel arap Moi informed Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres in a meeting in Jerusalem this evening that Kenya will open an embassy in Israel. President Moi stressed that Kenya has deep and special feelings toward the Israeli people. Our political correspondent Shlomo Raz points out that Israel already has an ambassador in Nairobi. Minister Peres told the visiting president that Israel would like to expand and enhance its agricultural, technological, and scientific ties with Kenya."]
