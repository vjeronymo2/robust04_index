Language: French Article Type:BFN [Statement issued by CAR Chairman Yaovi Agboyibor in Lome on 24 March; read by Afan Houenoumadje--recorded] [Text] [Begin Houenoumadje recording] The Action Committee for Renewal [CAR] is satisfied with the fact that the Togolese Union for Democracy's [UTD] letter to the CAR chairman stressed the need for the two opposition political parties, which constitute the parliamentary majority, to agree on the distribution of responsibilities at the head of the institutions to be put in place after the 6 and 20 February legislative elections. In reaction to that letter, the UTD and CAR chairmen each accompanied by one aide met the same day at 1530 to put finishing touches to their discussions on the choice of a prime minister. The two parties agreed that the CAR should propose one of its activists to take up the prime ministerial post. Following this agreement the CAR proposed a name that was then accepted by the UTD. The two political parties are now waiting for the head of state's invitation to a meeting in order to convey the name to him. [end recording]
