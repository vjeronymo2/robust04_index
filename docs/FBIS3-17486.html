Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Talks have been held in Pavlodar, Kazakhstan, by delegations of border oblasts of Kazakhstan and Russia. Here is our correspondent Andrey Kondrashov: Delegations of four border oblasts of Kazakhstan and Russia took part in the talks -- Yekaterinburg, Chelyabinsk, Omsk, and Pavlodar. A package of economic issues was discussed. Every year 24 million tonnes of Ekibastuz coal are supplied to Siberian power stations from Kazakhstan. According to the Kazakhstan newspaper PANORAMA, the Russian energy sector's arrears reached around 80 billion rubles, 17 billion of which have already been paid. The remainder of the debt is to be paid in April. Agreement was reached at the meeting to set up a joint Russian-Kazakh joint-stock company of the open type, founded by the power, coal and machine-building sectors of the two countries. It is intended that the draft agreement among the administrations of the four oblasts will be submitted to a forthcoming meeting of leaders and heads of state of Russia and Kazakhstan.
