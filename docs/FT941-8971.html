940217 FT 17 FEB 94 / Investment body announces board The Personal Investment Authority, the planned new watchdog for retail financial services, yesterday announced the final composition of its board, which is to increase from 19 members to 21. This does not mean, however, that the authority has bowed to pressure from within the financial services industry that current practitioners should form a majority on the board. The board will have 10 practitioners, with 10 public-interest directors, including the chairman and the chief executive. The announcement on the board's composition settles one of the remaining questions before publication of the authority's prospectus next Monday, assuming that the Securities and Investments Board, the City's chief watchdog, gives approval today. The industry will use the prospectus, and more detailed information on how the authority plans to approach regulation, to decide whether to support the new regulator. Only one public-interest director remains to be appointed. The four new practitioner directors are: Mr Lawrence Churchill (National Westminster Life Assurance); Mr Peter Gray (Tunbridge Wells Equitable Friendly Society); Mr Geoffrey Lister (Bradford & Bingley building society); and Mr Michael Pickard (Royal London Insurance).
