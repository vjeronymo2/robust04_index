Language: English Article Type:BFN [By BELINFORM correspondent Vladimir Glod for TASS] [Text] Minsk January 11 TASS -- A delegation of the Belarusian Parliament led by its Speaker Stanislav Shushkevich is leaving for Moscow on Tuesday night for talks with Russian President Boris Yeltsin due to begin on Wednesday. Shushkevich pointed out that "the forthcoming meeting is not spontaneous, but was thoroughly prepared." In Moscow, the sides are to discuss strategic issues of their interaction in the economic and other spheres. The sides are to specify their positions in the run up to the U.S. President Bill Clinton's visit to Moscow and Minsk. The Belarusian delegation to the talks with Yeltsin includes several members of the presidium of the Belarusian Parliament, Vice-Premier Vladimir Zalomai, Chairman of the board of the Belarusian National Bank Stanislav Bogdanekevich and other officials. The meeting might be attended by Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin.
