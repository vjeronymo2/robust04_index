Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] [Announcer] The Prime Minister's Office says the announcement by Arab delegations that they are pulling out of the coming round of the Washington peace talks was to have been expected. Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin is to address the Knesset this afternoon on the Hebron massacre and its aftermath. David Ze'ev has details: [Begin Ze'ev recording] The Prime Minister's Office says it expected the PLO to join the other Arab delegations in staying away this week from negotiations in Washington, though Yitzhaq Rabin's media adviser Gad Ben-Ari said it would be contrary to U.S. President Bill Clinton's statement that PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat had agreed to attend accelerated self-rule negotiations in the American capital this week in light of the Hebron massacre. Still, the Prime Minister's Office says Mr. Rabin anticipated the move by the Arab delegations. [passage omitted] The prime minister is to appear before the Knesset plenum this afternoon to present a government statement in the aftermath of the Hebron massacre. The Knesset Presidium is to meet at this hour and will determine the format of the deliberation that will follow the premiers' statement: how many Knesset members are to speak and for how long. Before this afternoon's Plenum session, Rabin is to appear before the Labor caucus. The meeting is being closed to the media. The subjects on the agenda are the Hebron massacre and its repercussions and the state health bill, a hot issue threatening the coalition just a few days ago, now almost forgotten. [end recording] [Announcer] [passage omitted] David Ze'ev reports from the Knesset that a decision has been reached to keep the debate on the Hebron massacre relatively short. Only one representative of each party's faction will be given the opportunity to speak.
