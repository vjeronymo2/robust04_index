Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow February 24 TASS -- The Council of Defence Ministers of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) will hold a two-day meeting in Moscow. The meeting will open at 15:00 on Thursday [24 February], ITAR-TASS learnt at the public information centre of the headquarters on coordination of military cooperation between the CIS member states. The meeting agenda consists of five items, including the approval of regulations on the Council of Defence Ministers of CIS member states. The decision on setting up the Council was adopted at the Commonwealth summit in Minsk in February 1992. Today's meeting will sum up the results of drafting the main issues for coordinating military construction in the CIS. The meeting will also decide on the staff establishment of the headquarters on coordination of military cooperation. It is supposed that the headquarters' divisions will be staffed on a coordination basis. Its staff will consist of 250 people, including 120 from Russia. The headquarters is charged with collecting, summerising and analysing open information on the military and political situation in the world and regions of interest to the CIS countries. The headquarters will also concert efforts to give mutual aid in creating and developing national armed forces. The situation on the Tajik-Afghan border has become a traditional item at defence ministers' meetings over the recent past. The present meeting will decide on additional measures to stabilise the situation there. The ministers will also discuss the celebration of the 50th anniversary since the end of the second world war. Meeting participants are supposed to take two decisions: measures to prepare for the celebration of the victory anniversary and to hold a spartakiad of CIS Armed Forces.
