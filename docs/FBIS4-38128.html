BFN [Text] Baghdad, 27 Jun (INA)--Dr. 'Abd-al-Razzaq al-Hashimi, head of the Foreign Relations Bureau at the Iraq Command of the Arab Socialist Ba'th Party and chairman of the Iraqi-French Friendship Society, met here today with Ahmad Shakir, chairman of the Association for Developing Arab-French Relations, and his delegation. During the meeting, Dr. 'Abd-al-Razzaq al-Hashimi reviewed the impact of the unfair embargo clamped on Iraq, affirming that there is no longer any justification for maintaining this embargo, particularly since Iraq has fulfilled its commitments per the relevant UN Security Council resolutions. Al-Hashimi also underscored the importance of promoting and normalizing trade and economic ties between Iraq and France in the interest of the two countries' peoples. For his part, the chairman of the Association for Developing Arab-French Relations expressed his willingness to continue to work for the lifting of the unfair sanctions imposed on the people of Iraq. A delegation from the association arrived in Baghdad earlier today. The delegation comprises French Senate Member Jean Jacques Robier and representatives of several French firms and banks.
