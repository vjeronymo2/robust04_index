Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Chile monitored through 24 February 1994. The Central Bank's net international reserves increased by $342.8 million in January, of which $301.2 million was the result of the balance of payment surplus, while $41.6 million came from a revaluation of the reserves. Total reserves now amount to $10.1014 billion. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 8 Feb 94 p 4) On 7 February the Central Bank reported that the trade balance registered a $1.5 million surplus during the first half of January, compared with the $32.8 million surplus registered in the same period last year. Exports totalled $355.7 million, and imports $354.2 million, a drop of 12.2 percent and 19.1 percent respectively compared with the same period in 1993. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 8 Feb 94 p 5) The Central Bank has reported that the foreign debt amounted to $19.179 billion as of 31 December, an increase of 5.1 percent compared with 1992, when it was $18.242 billion. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 8 Feb 94 p 6) The mining company El Indio will invest $100 million to increase its exploitation of the El Tambo gold deposit located in the mountainous area of the Fourth Region. El Indio Executive Vice President Ralph Mathson disclosed this information following a meeting with Fourth Region Intendant Renan Fuentealba. Mathson added that some 350 additional workers will be employed for the next six years. The company expects to obtain during this period 1.5 million ounces of gold and an unspecified amount of silver and copper. (Santiago Radio Cooperativa Network in Spanish 1000 GMT 19 Feb 94)
