920616 FT 16 JUN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Penalty planned for Malmo hooligans ENGLISH soccer fans convicted of criminal offences at the European football championships in Sweden could be banned from home games, Mr David Mellor, national heritage secretary, told the Commons yesterday, David Owen writes. Those convicted in Sweden 'can be prohibited from entering' UK football grounds, Mr Mellor warned, adding that serious charges arising from the events in Malmo had already been laid. 'I certainly hope we shall be able to exclude those convicted from going to football matches again for a very long time,' he said. Mr Mellor, who was addressing his first session of Commons questions in his new position, endured some spirited barracking from a small contingent of Scottish MPs when he appeared initially to designate the misbehaving supporters as 'British'. Reprimanding the minister for his thoughtlessness, Mr Norman Godman, the Labour MP for Greenock and Port Glasgow, pointed out that there were 'no Scots anywhere near Malmo'. He said: 'Not a single Scottish fan has got into any trouble.'
