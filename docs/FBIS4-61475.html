BFN [Text] An Armenian Foreign Ministry delegation led by [Armenian Foreign Minister] Vahan Papazyan arrived in the capital of Argentina on 2 May for an official visit. On the same day, he met Argentine Economy Minister Domingo Cavallo. Mr. Papazyan noted during the meeting that the agreements signed between the two countries on stimulating capital investments, on mutual [words indistinct], and on trade and economic cooperation serve as a good foundation for the further development of the Armenia-Argentine economic cooperation. He hoped that the Armenian-Argentine trade and economic symposium to open in Buenos Aires soon will give both sides the opportunity to reach an agreement on concrete cooperation. In stressing the role of the Armenian community's businessmen in the economic reform in Argentina, Mr. Cavallo noted the importance of their participation in the Armenian-Argentine trade and economic cooperation. An agreement was reached to send a delegation of the Argentine businessmen to Armenia in the near future. Ara Hakobyan, Armenian charge d'affaires ad interim in Argentina, was present at the meeting. Also, the Armenian Embassy in Argentina was inaugurated on 2 May. Vahan Papazyan was present and made the opening speech. Argentine officials, representatives of the Armenian community, and foreign diplomats were present at the ceremony. The Armenian foreign minister also visited St. Grigor Lusavorich Church and placed flowers at the monument to the victims of 1915 genocide. Mr. Papazyan also met with the Armenian community of Argentina. He informed them about the domestic political and economic situation in Armenia and detailed Armenia's stance on the settlement of the Nagorno-Karabakh problem. Speaking on Armenia's foreign policy issues, he emphasized the role of Argentina, which has been elected a non-permanent member of the UN Security Council, in the solution of problems related to the interests of our Republic. At the end of the meeting Papazyan replied to questions. The Armenian foreign minister met Argentine President Carlos Menem and Foreign Minister Guido di Tella on 3 May. Armenian-Argentine interstate agreements were signed.
