Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] In view of the issue of Islamic fundamentalism, southern European countries may set up a new military structure. According to Defense Minister Fernando Nogueira, who was speaking in Paris, Portugal could participate in this structure. It would comprise an air and sea structure tailored to Mediterranean needs. The project is already on paper and includes Spain, France, and Italy. Daniel Ribeiro reports from Paris. [Ribeiro] Military cooperation between southern European countries appears to be developing with Portugal's agreement in principle regarding the air and sea Mediterranean force which includes France, Spain, and Italy. It is a force which will cover a strategic area in view of the disruption affecting Maghreb countries, especially Algeria where military pressure from Islamic fundamentalists is increasing. In addition to this, Fernando Nogueira also discussed the situation in Angola, Mozambique, and Bosnia-Herzegovina, with his French counterpart. [passage omitted]
