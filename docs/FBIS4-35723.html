BFN [Text] [Pakistani] Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali has asked the Nonaligned Movement [NAM] to play a role in negotiations between Pakistan and India to settle the Kashmir dispute. Addressing the NAM conference in Cairo, the foreign minister said Pakistan would like NAM to set up an inspection committee and urged NAM group to visit occupied Kashmir to observe human rights violations there, where half a million Indian troops suppressing an uprising for freedom by the Kashmiri people. He also proposed that a group of NAM countries be constituted to facilitate a process of peaceful negotiations to resolve the Kashmir dispute. The foreign minister said Pakistan is prepared to talk about Kashmir and whenever India shows a willingness to find a solution to the Kashmir dispute, the matter can be resolved.
