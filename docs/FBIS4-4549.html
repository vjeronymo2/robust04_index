BFN [Text] Singapore and Malaysia have agreed to meet within the next three months to finalise the draft agreement on the demarcation of the boundary between the two countries in the Straits of Johor. This was announced by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA) on Thursday after the sixth Singapore-Malaysia meeting on the boundary demarcation held here from Tuesday to Thursday. The Singapore team was led by the senior state counsel in the Attorney-General's Chambers, Mr S. Tiwari. The leader of the Malaysian delegation was the Ministry of Land and Cooperative Development's secretary-general, Mr Azmeer Rashid. The MFA statement said the meeting was conducted in "a cordial and friendly atmosphere reflecting the close relationship and cooperation between the two countries". The two sides carried out negotiations on the draft agreement to mark precisely the territorial waters boundary, in accordance with the Straits Settlement and Johor Territorial Waters Agreement 1927. Substantial progress was made and most of the outstanding issues were resolved and agreed upon by the two delegations, the statement said. It added that both sides agreed to have their next meeting in Malaysia.
