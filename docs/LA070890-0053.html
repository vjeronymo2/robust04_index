July 8, 1990, Sunday, San Diego County Edition

SHOOTING QUESTIONS

My stepson, Charles Barker, was the subject of a story printed in the San Diego edition of the Los Angeles Times ("Slain Teen Had 2 Sides to His Life, Friends Say," June 25). I would like to commend H.G. Reza for portraying my stepson as the quiet, polite person he was. The article was researched and the facts were presented in a most professional manner, while avoiding undue sensationalism.

I do, however, feel compelled to comment on two issues, one pertaining to the story and the other to the case.

In regard to the story, the issue I have is with the comments made by the Madison High School official who chose not to be identified. The school official was quoted as saying Chuck was "kicked out of Madison." Elsewhere, the story states that Chuck was expelled from Madison.

I was present when the decision was made to have Chuck leave Madison and transfer to Twain Junior-Senior High School. It was not presented to me or to his mother that this was an expulsion. He was not "kicked out." We mutually agreed that Chuck was not doing well at Madison, and that a transfer to Twain would be in his best interest.

The second issue I have is with the treatment of the case by the district attorney (who ruled the shooting a justifiable homicide). I will be the first to say my stepson did wrong and deserved to be punished. I do not defend nor condone his actions. He chose to break the law, and that was wrong.

He did not, however, deserve to be shot to death. According to police, the events of the case were: two boys wearing stocking masks approached a man and said, "Freeze -- give me your wallet." At this point, the man feigned a heart attack, pulled a weapon and fired one fatal shot.

My wife and I grieve the loss of a child and, at the same time, are shocked to learn that our boy had attempted a robbery. (But) the laws of this country clearly state that the punishment must fit the crime. Attempted robbery does not warrant the death penalty.

JOHN M. ELLIOTT

San Diego
