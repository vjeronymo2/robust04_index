Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video; from the "NHK News" program] [Text] Chief Cabinet Secretary Masayoshi Takemura commented this morning at a news conference on U.S. Trade Representative Michael Kantor's remark that the U.S. Government is studying the possibility of reviving the Super 301 provision of the 1988 Omnibus Trade Act. The provision stipulates retaliation against trading nations that refuse to liberalize their markets or discriminate against U.S. exports. Insisting that such action would go against the spirit of Japan- U.S. economic consultations, Takemura said he would urge prudence on part of the United States. [Begin Takemura recording] Our agreement is that we should maximize our efforts at dialogue. I think what they are proposing would go against the spirit of dialogue. I would like to ask that they be prudent as far as remarks on exercising the Super 301 provision are concerned. [end recording] Takemura's comments indicated the government would ask the United States to be cautious about reviving the Super 301 provisions.
