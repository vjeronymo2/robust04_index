BFN [Text] Baku, June 17 (TURAN)--Today Azerbaijan's Parliamentary delegation headed by Vice-Speaker, Afiaddin Dzhalilov, left for Romania. The delegation will take part in the session of Interparliamentary Assembly of the countries belonging to the Black Sea basin. The session is due to take place on June 21 in Bucharest. During the session the issues concerning customs, import and export operations and economic integration are to be discussed. Dzhalilov also said that the Azeri delegation intends to discuss the issues concerning Armenia's aggression against Azerbaijan, the blockade imposed by Armenia against Nakhichevan and the damage caused to Azerbaijan during Armenia-Azerbaijani war. The delegations from Romania, Turkey, Russia, Ukraine, Albania, Armenia, Georgia and other countries are expected to take part at the session as well. The organization of countries belonging to the Black Sea basin was founded in 1992 at the initiative of Turkey. The purpose of this organization is establishment of economic and mutual advantageous cooperation among the countries belonging to the Black Sea basin.
