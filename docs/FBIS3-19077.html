Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Mar. 10 (A.A.) -- The Democracy Party (DEP) assembly said today that as they have withdrawn from the mayoral elections, they believe that, "those who have put their faith in our party will hopefully refrain from voting for the status quo parties and thus give them a lesson." Assistant chairman of DEP, Kemal Bilget announced his party's assembly decisions in a press conference here today. The statement said that DEP was ready to do whatever it could in accomplishing democracy, peace and brotherhood, despite the fact that the state has removed the immunity of six of their deputies. "The party assembly, which has decided to take the necessary legal actions to celebrate Nevroz in some parts of the country, will spend all efforts to have the celebrations in a peaceful manner and free of bloodshed," the announcement said. Bilget told journalists that his party was not, in fact, calling on the people to boycott the local polls. "This is not a boycott but a wish" he said, adding that on March 27, he will stay in his home. He added that his party was evaluating the option of celebrating Nevroz in Istanbul, Ankara, Izmir, Izmit, Bursa, Mersin, Adana and Gaziantep, rather than the emergency rule regions. He added that his party members will be in their electoral districts during Nevroz and large numbers of delegations from foreign countries will also go to the southeastern region during the Nevroz. Meanwhile, the Anatolian News Agency learned that DEP members Remzi Kartal and Sedat Yurtdas flew to Brussels today to take part in a Kurdish conference.
