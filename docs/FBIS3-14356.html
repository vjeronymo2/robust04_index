Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Cairo--The Arab League Council concluded its first session at 1400 today. During this session, the Council approved the agenda of the Arab League Council's 101st ordinary session. Answering reporters' questions after this session, [Syrian] Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' stressed that the Arab boycott of Israel was not included on the agenda of the Arab League Council's session. He added that the Council reviewed the report of the Damascus-based central Arab Boycott Office. Al-Shar' said: The issue of discussing or lifting the second and third degree of the boycott will not be discussed at the Council's session. He noted that this issue was decided at the Council's economic committee. Asked about Syria's position on the issue of amending the Arab League Charter, the foreign minister said this issue was postponed until there is a sound and healthy Arab situation so the amendment can be made on sound basis. He said: In view of the importance of this issue, it requires an Arab summit. Al-Shar' announced that the seven-member ministerial committee charged with following up the Lockerbie crisis will meet at 1700 today to discuss the latest developments in the crisis. It is worth mentioning that the seven-member committee comprises the foreign ministers of Syria, Egypt, Algeria, Morocco, Tunis, Mauritania, and Libya, as well as the Arab League secretary general. The Arab League Council is scheduled to resume its meetings at 1630 today in the Arab League Headquarters to continue discussions of topics on its agenda. [passage omitted]
