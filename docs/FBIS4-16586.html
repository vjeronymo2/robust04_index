BFN [Text] The Second Congress of Russian Cinema Workers opened in Moscow on Saturday. A message from President Yeltsin to its participants stresses that "the unique potential of Russian cinema must be preserved at all costs." On Friday, he signed a decree on a protectionist state policy in the national cinema art. Support for cinema is recognized in this document as crucial to the government's cultural development plans. The decree obliges the cabinet to set aside funds for the national motion-picture industry and submit a bill on national cinema to the Federal Assembly within three months. There are nearly 300 film-makers at the forum in Moscow. Secretary of Russia's Cinematographic Union Sergey Solovyev, a film director, spoke about the Russian cinema's plight and ways to overcome it. The congress will last two days.
