Revival of Hosokawa Support Rate
The support rate for the Hosokawa cabinet sharply declined in the December survey, but it picked up by 3 percent in January. It appears Prime Minister Hosokawa's 14 December decision on a partial rice liberalization left no obvious impact on the support rate. Prime Minister Hosokawa managed the rice issue, but future development of the political scene is still uncertain as the upper house rejected the political reform bills. ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |Table 3. Decline in Support Rate for LDP President Kono
In the January survey, the support rate for coalition parties again surpassed that for the LDP. The decline in support for the LDP over a long span is remarkable. Mr. Yohei Kono has been elected the LDP president to win broad support for reconstructing the party. Yet, the support rate for President Kono is also showing constant decline. In particular, he is unpopular among female voters. Although 33 percent of male voters support him, the support rate remained at only 23.5 percent among the female voters. Table 4. Public Attitude Toward Rice Market Liberalization
What are the public's reactions to the Hosokawa administration's decision on partial liberalization of the rice market? According to the survey result, 49 percent were for, and 34 percent against the decision. Since the cabinet support rate did not drop by the strong opposition to the rice liberalization decision, it appears the issue had not been regarded as a hot controversy. As a matter of course, the farmers-fishermen group reacted most strongly to the decision. However, it is very interesting that the Hosokawa cabinet retained high support (49 percent in December, and 50 percent in January) even among those who opposed the rice liberalization. It seems that, even among farmers, the rice liberalization is no longer a controversy to affect their confidence in the cabinet. ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |Table 5. Pollees' Voting-Intentions
Since the political turnover in last August, the decline in the LDP's popularity is noteworthy in the survey on voting intentions. When we started surveying public attitude toward the question of Table 5, the support for the LDP always exceeded 50 percent. But the figure dropped to just a little over 20 percent in the latest survey. In contrast, the JNP and other coalition parties are steadily gaining voters' support. In the January survey, Shinseito obtained public support ranking with the SDPJ. NOTE: The survey, conducted by Chuo Research Inc. on 7-10 January, was carried on as individual interviews of 2,000 eligible voters picked at random. Responses were obtained from 69.3 percent of, or 1,385, pollees. Data belong to the Multipurpose Statistic Databank of Tsukuba University.
