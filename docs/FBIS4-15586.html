BFN [Text] Georgian head of state Eduard Shevardnadze told parliament Friday that he would take over the talks on the settlement of the Abkhazian conflict. He called on the deputies not to weaken the position of the Georgian side in the talks by continuous fault finding. "If parliament thinks that my action is wrong, let us have a vote of confidence," he said. He also said that he would probably attend the CIS summit in Moscow next week. The Georgian parliament did not approve Tamaz Kvachantiradze as education minister. Tedo Ninidze was approved as justice minister when Shevardnadze nominated him for a second time.
