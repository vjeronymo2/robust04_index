930727 FT 27 JUL 93 / International Company News: Zenith deficit deepens to Dollars 24m in second term ZENITH Electronics, the sole surviving US manufacturer of television sets, yesterday unveiled a second-quarter net loss of Dollars 24.7m, or 79 cents a share, writes Karen Zagor in New York. A year earlier, the company suffered a net deficit of Dollars 15.2m, or 52 cents, including royalty income of Dollars 13m and a special tax credit of Dollars 4m. In the 1993 quarter, royalty income was Dollars 5m. Sales slipped slightly to Dollars 274.7m from Dollars 280m a year earlier. For the first six months, Zenith recorded a net loss of Dollars 46.5m, or Dollars 1.51, on sales of Dollars 565.2m, against a deficit of Dollars 43.8m, or Dollars 1.50, on sales of Dollars 545.2m in the same period of 1992.
