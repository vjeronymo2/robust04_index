Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Kulachada Chaiphiphat] [Excerpts] Laotian Foreign Minister Somsavat Lengsavat will lead a delegation to Bangkok soon for talks on the maintenance and administration of the Thai-Lao Mittraphap bridge across the Mekong River, informed sources said. Talks, set tentatively for 8-9 February, will also focus on official preparations far His Majesty the King's visit to Vientiane on the eve of the historic April 8 opening. The bridge, financed and built by Australia, completes the first land link between Thailand and Laos. The opening will be presided over by the King and the President of Laos, Nouhak Phomsavan, in the presence of Prime Minister Chuan Likphai, Laotian Prime Minister General Khamtai Siphandon, and Australian Prime Minister Paul Keating. However, the build-up to the handover is being plagued by a lack of agreement on the question of boundaries. Visa issues have also yet to be settled. [passage omitted] Thailand maintains that the boundary on the bridge should be drawn at the deepest point of the river, the same line which marks the frontier along the Mekong River between Thailand and Laos. Laos has argued that the two nations should each have jurisdiction over and responsibility for half the bridge, in accordance with international practice. This boundary would not replace the official frontier. The differences have embarrassed both sides, as such basic issues were supposed to have been smoothed over earlier in anticipation of the historic inauguration of the "friendship" bridge that symbolizes cordial ties between the two neighbors. The sources however said the talks are unlikely to focus on measures to facilitate the two-way entry into both sides through the bridge.
