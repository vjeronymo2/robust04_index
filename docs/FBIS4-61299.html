BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature: compiled by Nikolay Zherebtsov and Andrey Petrovskiy; edited by Vladimir Shishlin] [Text] At the meeting in Kiev with the Minister for European Affairs, Alain Lamassoure, President Kravchuk expressed gratitude to France for help it provides to Ukraine in scrapping nuclear weapons and providing safety for nuclear power stations. He said that the country's leadership appreciates a change in the relationships between the West and Ukraine after the three-partite agreement on nuclear weapons was signed in Moscow. France's representatives are part of the G-7 delegation currently in Ukraine, which arrived in the Republic to study the situation at Ukrainian nuclear power stations. Deputy Prime Minister Valeriy Shmarov told reporters that Ukrainian leaders will sign no documents on the results of the visit with G-7 inspectors. INTERFAX note: The delegation includes senior officials of the G-7 nuclear energy ministries, the nuclear security fund of the European Bank for the Reconstruction and Development, the U.S. Department of State. The delegation intends to prepare materials for the G-7 summit in Naples, which explain the need of the economic aid to Ukraine. Shmarov said that at their meeting members of the delegation expressed preparedness to provide a financial aid to Ukraine in the development of the nuclear energy and for higher level of safety at the Ukrainian nuclear power stations. This aid will be given if Ukraine refuses to commission the second block of the Chernobyl nuclear power station and agrees to the gradual closure of the first and third operating blocks.
