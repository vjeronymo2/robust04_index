Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Rocio Vicente from the "Newspaper of the Airwaves" newscast] [Text] There are definitely eight military officers involved in the disappearance of the students and a professor from the Enrique Guzman University, La Cantuta, according to General Guido Guevara Guerra, the new president of the Supreme Council of Military Justice. Guevara Guerra said a general is one of those involved but for the time being he will not reveal where the detainees are being held. [Begin recording] [Guevara Guerra] Ask yourselves why he is under arrest. [Unidentified reporter] Why? [Guevara Guerra] Because he is responsible. That is the first thing [passage indistinct]. Eight people are under arrest. [Reporter] [Words indistinct] [Guevara Guerra] I do not know. We will have to take a look at the result of the investigations. We also have to respect the judge's work. [passage indistinct] [end recording] Those were some of the statements by the new president of the Supreme Council of Military Justice, who also said that the military courts will respect the Supreme Court's jurisdiction in the La Cantuta case. But he did say his personal preference would be for the case to be handled by a military court.
