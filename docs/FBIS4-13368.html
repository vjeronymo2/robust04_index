BFN [Text] Beirut--The Lebanese Council of Ministers has decided to sever Lebanon's diplomatic ties with Iraq, because the latter refused to lift the diplomatic immunity of two Iraqi diplomats the Lebanese authorities charged with assassinating Talib al-Suhayl, an Iraqi opposition figure. This was announced by Lebanese Information Minister Michel Samahah at the end of a session held by the Council of Ministers tonight. Samahah added that Lebanon has given the two Iraqi diplomats 72 hours to leave the Lebanese capital, Beirut.
