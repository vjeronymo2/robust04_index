May 31, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

Keith Reynolds of Britain won the second stage of the 13-day bicycle race round England to help his country maintain the lead of the Milk Race.

Reynolds broke from the pack on the outskirts of Bristol, England to win the 122-mile stage from Spain's Jesus Suarez-Cuevas in a time of 5 hours 30 minutes 46 seconds. Reynolds leads the overall race by 17 seconds with countryman Steve Joughin, who finished third in the second stage, in second place overall.
