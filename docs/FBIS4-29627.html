BFN [From the "Political Opinion" column: "The Khmer Rouge's Only Chance"] [Text] The unclear response of Democratic Kampuchea [DK] to the first topic in the agenda of the roundtable commission's meeting which required that the conflicting parties give a yes or no answer to the proposed cease-fire continues to cloud the search for peace in Cambodia. Democratic Kampuchea's representative Mr. Chan Youran told the meeting that his party did not want to fight. He demanded that a real cease-fire be organized with the proper supervision of a committee or technical group set up for that purpose. Democratic Kampuchea's current stance as presented by Mr. Chan Youran was a little different from what it was before in that this time his party did not insist that other countries be invited to take part in the cease-fire supervision committee. However, it could be seen that the nature of Democratic Kampuchea's stance expressed at the Chhanghsu-on royal residence on 27-28 May remains virtually the same, as few changes have been made to it. Democratic Kampuchea's strategy is to achieve national reconciliation and end the war right off, without first going through the stage of a cease-fire. Mr. Meas Chey, an important official of Democratic Kampuchea at its permanent office in Phnom Penh, stated outside the meeting that the main reason behind this Democratic Kampuchea stance was that when all Cambodians succeed in achieving national reconciliation, there would automatically be a cease-fire. He went on to say that otherwise a cease-fire would be ineffective, no matter where and when it may be, and he cited many specific examples, including the 1973 agreement in the U.S.-Vietnam war. For this reason, beginning with national reconciliation remains an important starting point. Although Mr. Meas Chey did not tell us what would be used as a basis for national reconciliation, certain political analysts have cautioned that Democratic Kampuchea was trying to push its enemies into its "Minimal Policy Program" trap. What is Democratic Kampuchea's minimal policy program anyway? Mr. Meas Chey explained that the program was not aimed at dissolving the Royal Government. According to it, the two sides should make compromises. They should discuss what the Royal Government would get from Democratic Kampuchea, and vice versa. Yet most observers believed that Democratic Kampuchea was insulting the Royal Government elected by the people. Although the participants expressed some cheerfulness, a Royal Government official who also took part in the meeting remained quite pessimistic. He affirmed that he still did not understand what the minimal policy program really was. Now, through Democratic Kampuchea's "accepting while resolutely rejecting the cease-fire" answer, the war in Cambodia is still going on because what the Royal Government wants is to set a specific schedule for the cease-fire. Samdech Hun Sen was very pessimistic when it came to the future of Cambodia all because of the failure to declare a cease-fire schedule. Recently, Samdech Hun Sen told the press that he was in possession of a document that was written by Pol Pot, which he obtained at the end of last March. According to this document, the objective of the Khmer Rouge was to increase their forces to 25,000 men between May 1994 and May 1996 in preparation for an offensive. It should be noted that according to neutral military analysts, the current Khmer Rouge rebel army is believed to be 20,000 strong, including their militia. The actual fighting force of the Khmer Rouge, however, is assessed to be about 8,000 men only. If the proposal for a cease-fire with a specific schedule fell through and the roundtable commission's meeting collapsed, the Khmer Rouge would surely lose more of its support. The Royal Government is certainly employing the Khmer Rouge's own game to destroy the Khmer Rouge in addition to the military aid from foreign countries, especially the United States, France, and Australia. It can equally be said that this is also a chance for the Khmer Rouge to make up their mind whether they want to end the war and launch a political attack instead, because to be frank the Khmer Rouge are far from finished on the political stage. On the contrary, if this opportunity is allowed to pass by, the Khmer Rouge issue will then no longer belong to the framework of a settlement for national reconciliation and an end to the war. And His Majesty King Norodom Sihanouk already warned before the roundtable commission's meeting that, should this meeting fail, he would not propose any more of them.
