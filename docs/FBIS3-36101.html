Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] According to reliable sources, a large group of Russian financial and economic experts arrived in Minsk today. The group also includes representatives of the State Cooperation Committee, Customs Control Committee, and the Central Bank of Russia. The visit is connected with preparations for the signing of documents necessary to merge the two countries' monetary systems. An official from the Belarusian Cabinet of Ministers, who agreed to speak on condition of anonymity, did not rule out a decision to gradually replace the Belarusian currency with Russian rubles sometime this year. Russian Deputy Prime Minister Oleg Soskovets is expected to arrive in Minsk later this week to finally resolve this issue.
