Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (YONHAP) -- Top South Korean and Chinese military leaders had talks here on Tuesday and agreed to expand military exchanges between their countries. The conference took place between South Korean Joint Chiefs of Staff Chairman Yi Yang-ho and Chinese Chief of General Staff Zhang Wannian at the China Ministry of Defense. Yi was accompanying President Kim Yong-sam to his current China visit. Yi and Zhang decided to positively promote exchanges especially in the area of military training. Zhang said during the meeting that China would send a military attache to Seoul next month as planned. The two military leaders shared the view that North Korea's nuclear development should be thwarted by all means since it poses a threat not only to South Korea but to the whole area of Northeast Asia.
