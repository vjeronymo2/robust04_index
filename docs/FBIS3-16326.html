Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] On 15 March, the Belarusian Supreme Soviet adopted the new Belarusian Constitution with 236 "yes" votes, six "no" votes, and eight abstentions. The major innovation of the Constitution is that the presidential form of governing is outlined. It is expected that the election of the first Belarusian president will take place on 26 June 1994. The final "touch" added by the Supreme Soviet was an amendment to Article 49 of the Constitution, which guarantees broad and free access to secondary and vocational education in the Republic. The new Constitution will come into force immediately after it is published in the Belarusian press.
