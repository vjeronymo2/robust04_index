Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuwait, 12 Jan -- Defense Minister Shaykh 'Ali al-Sabah stressed his commitment to the defense-bolstering budget on which a report had been prepared by the joint committee composed of the interior and defense and foreign affairs committees, to be submitted to the finance committee. In a statement to KUNA here today, Shaykh 'Ali al-Sabah said: Our commitment to the defense-bolstering budget stems from the public interest. On his part, 'Abbas Munawir, chairman of the interior and defense committee, said that the joint committee had approved the defense-bolstering budget. He indicated that the final decision regarding this matter lay with the National Assembly after the finance committee discusses the issue. Munawir revealed that the Defense Ministry had spent around 1.6 billion dinars out of the total budget, which amounts to 3.5 billion dinars. Munawir said that four deputies had voted for the budget while three voted against it, and one abstained; the overall result was, therefore, approval. In another development, Munawir said that the committee had agreed to take into consideration the voluntary period served by those who joined the army during the treacherous Iraqi occupation and considered it as a part of the service. He added that the committee had discussed the possibility of promoting military personnel who are holders of a general certificate of secondary education or higher; Munawir indicated that the head of the military judiciary body had said that that system had been in force for a long time.
