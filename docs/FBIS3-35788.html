Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vyacheslav Bantin] [Text] Tokyo, 31 Jan -- Tsutomu Hata, Japanese deputy prime minister and foreign minister, intends to visit Russia soon. ITAR-TASS was informed of this by Gennadiy Burbulis, member of the State Duma and president of the "Strategy" humanitarian and politological center, who met the Japanese foreign minister in Tokyo today. During the talks both countries' aspiration "to achieve serious development" of bilateral relations was reiterated, Gennadiy Burbulis said. Burbulis, who is here to attend the tripartite forum of Russian, U.S., and Japanese representatives on security in the northern Pacific, stressed Russia's intention "to seek the most specific forms of economic cooperation with Russia." In an interview with ITAR-TASS Sumio Edamura, a former Japanese ambassador in Moscow, who attended the meeting at the Foreign Ministry, spoke highly of today's talks between Gennadiy Burbulis and the country's prime minister [as received] and foreign minister. "The arrival in Tokyo of one of the leaders of Russia's Choice, the biggest grouping in the State Duma, and his meetings with representatives of our government show that Moscow is committed to a policy of developing the entire range of relations with Japan," Edamura noted.
