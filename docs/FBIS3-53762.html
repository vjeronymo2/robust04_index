Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat has promised Amnesty International to uphold human rights in Gaza, PLO Executive Committee member (Manu Kamanji) stated today in a ceremony inaugurating the PLO's office in the city. (Kamanji) added that members of the Palestinian police force would receive guidance on upholding human rights. Klaus Warren, the secretary general of UNRWA [UN Relief and Works Agency], lodged today a protest with the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] authorities over the wounding yesterday of Terry Grill, the official in charge of refugees in Gaza who was wounded in the leg by IDF gunfire in the al-Shaykh Radwan neighborhood. Our correspondent Shlomi Eldar that today, too, there were disturbances in Gaza. Youngsters threw stones at passing cars, and Palestinian sources report that IDF troops opened fire at the stone-throwers. Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] today staged a mourning march near the house of Muhammad Salim who stabbed an Israeli to death and seriously wounded another on Friday. Standing outside his home, Hamas members chanted derogatory calls against Israel and vowed to avenge his death. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 1900 GMT on 16 January adds that "a firebomb was thrown this evening at the yeshiva near the Cave of the Patriarchs in Hebron. Our correspondent Ron Nesi'el reports that the firebomb shattered and ignited on the road, but nobody was hurt and no damage was caused."]
