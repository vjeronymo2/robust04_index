920721 FT 21 JUL 92 / Platform of rights London Underground passengers delayed on journeys by more than 20 minutes will be able to apply for refund vouchers under the organisation's customer charter, Richard Tomkins writes. To be eligible they will need either to have waited on a platform for 20 minutes longer than advertised or to have been delayed more than 20 minutes on a train. Vouchers will be given only when London Underground considers itself to blame. Some common causes of delays - such as suicide attempts, security alerts and vandalism - will not count. Passengers will need to complete A4-size claim forms and submit them with their used tickets - which are normally swallowed up by automatic barriers when leaving station platforms. London Underground said if passengers intended claiming they should approach staff to allow them out with their tickets. London Underground said it envisaged having claims from possibly 1,000 passengers a day - roughly equivalent to the capacity of one Tube train - but not all of them would necessarily be valid. The scheme, which comes into force on August 1, is expected to cost between Pounds 1m and Pounds 5m a year. Mr Denis Tunnicliffe, London Underground's managing director, said: 'The aim is not to pay out money. It is to improve services so that we do not need to.'
