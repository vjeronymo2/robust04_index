February 10, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

ETHNIC CONFLICT IN EAST BLOC

I disagree with the observation by McManus. McManus states that nationalism is dangerous. On the contrary, I feel it is the healthiest omen experienced by not only the East Bloc countries but specifically the Soviet Republic.

Nationalism has always existed there in spite of the Russians' insistence on developing the robotic "Soviet Man" -- devoid of a heritage, history, culture, etc. This plot did not work as we can see. The underlying nationalism is being recognized.

I spent the last 1 1/2 years in the Soviet Union's non-Russian republics and what I saw and heard was truly positive. The people are full of hope -- maybe for the first time in 70 years. All their energies are being applied to throwing off the Russians once and for all -- and these were not the Baltic republics either.

ERIC McHUGH

Irvine
