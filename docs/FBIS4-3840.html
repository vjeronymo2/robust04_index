BFN [Text] Moscow, April 22 (XINHUA) -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev said on Friday [22 April] that Russia is against the expansion of air strikes on Bosnian Serbs currently discussed by the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO). After his talks with visiting Danish Foreign Minister Niels Helveg Petersen, Kozyrev reminded reporters of "strongly negative estimation" of Bosnian Serb attacks on civilian facilities and, especially, a hospital in the Moslem-held town of Gorazde, noting that the international community has the right to work for termination of criminal actions. At the same time, the Russian minister pointed out, a military response of NATO "would not be the best choice." "Russia, the United States, the United Nations and the European Union should draw up a common approach to the Bosnian problem," Kozyrev said, describing this as "very important."
