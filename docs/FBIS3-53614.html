Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] The director general of the Interior Ministry's foreign nationals and emigrants department said today at a seminar attended by the heads of this organization's representative offices from all over the country: The Islamic Republic government is ready to repatriate more than 700,000 Afghans and 50,000 Iraqi refugees to their countries next year [beginning 21 March 1994], if the refugees agree to return home. According to our correspondent, at the time of the United States' military attack on Iraq, the number of immigrants and refugees seeking asylum in Iran reached 5.5 million. However, more than three million of them have gradually returned to their countries and at present there are about 2.5 million foreign nationals and refugees in Iran. Of this figure, 1,850,000 are Afghans, 645,000 are Iraqis, and 65,000 are from other countries. At today's session of the seminar the interior minister said in his speech that refugees will continue coming to Iran until political and economic stability returns to our neighboring countries.
