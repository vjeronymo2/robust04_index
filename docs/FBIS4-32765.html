BFN [Text] Warsaw, June 23--Poland and Romania are natural partners with similar strategic goals and common security interests, Romanian Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu told a press conference in Warsaw on Friday. According to him, both countries may cooperate in economy, tourism, communications and on third markets. Speaking about his two-day visit to Poland, Melescanu said that it aimed to develop cooperation between the two countries, especially between the Polish and Romanian parliaments. He said that the Romanian president will visit Poland this autumn. Polish Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak has also been invited to visit Romania but the date has not been fixed yet. During his visit Melescanu has presented some concrete proposals how to boost mutual cooperation. He talked about the reconstruction of an old railway route connecting the two countries and setting up a Polish-Romanian tourist enterprise. Melescanu thinks that such projects could be supported by the European Union. Speaking about Romanian debt to Poland now amounting to 50 million transfer roubles, the Romanian minister said that his country will pay it off. He also hoped that any delays will not hamper economic cooperation of the two countries which are of similar size and which have similar problems. In the morning, the Romanian guest laid flowers at the grave of Jozef Beck, Poland's pre-war foreign minister who died during the Second World War in Romania.
