BFN [Text] Copenhagen, May 16 (XINHUA) -- Danish Prime Minister Poul Nyrup Rasmussen today told Li Ruihuan, chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), that he was deeply impressed by China's economic development. Speaking at their meeting in Copenhagen, the Danish prime minister also stressed that danish-Chinese relations are "very good", expressing his optimism about the future. Li arrived in the Danish capital on Friday for a four-day visit. He told Rasmussen that China is very happy to see the two countries enjoying a deepened understanding which has resulted in a fruitful cooperation in trade and economic matters due to expanded exchanges at senior levels. At the request of Rasmussen, Li gave an outline of China's political system reform, its socialist democratic politics and the people's political consultative conference. While meeting this morning with Mimi Jakobasen, Danish industry minister [title as received], Li said he hoped Denmark would further its trade cooperation with China, utilizing its advanced knowledge in electronics, food processing, the chemical industry, medicine and environmental protection. Jakobasen said Danish business circles are very interested in investing and doing business in China since the two countries have respective advantages in different sections and the potential for cooperation is very large. She expressed the wish that the chairman's visit will have a positive influence on trade and economic exchanges between the two countries. Li Ruihuan also met today Ivar Hansen, the first vice-president of the Danish Parliament, and Kirsten Jacobs who is the fourth vice- president of the parliament.
