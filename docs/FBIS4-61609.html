BFN [Text] The press center of the Georgian Defense Ministry briefed INTERFAX on what happened on 5 May in the village of Mziuri of Abkhazia's Galskiy district. According to the press-center, at approximately 1700 on 5 May some fifteen armed paramilitaries drove up to plunder the Georgian-populated village. The villagers called for help and Georgian military police arrived. The police surrounded the paramilitaries and ordered them to surrender. In response, the paramilitaries opened fire and wounded two police officers. Five paramilitaries were killed and four taken prisoner. The remaining few escaped. What a Georgian police unit was doing on Abkhaz-controlled territory, was something the press center refused to tell INTERFAX.
