October 16, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

PATERSON ON REFUSING TO GO TO SAUDI ARABIA

When I was in the Marine Corps in World War II, anyone like Paterson, sitting back in picturesque Hawaii writing columns while his buddies were stationed in a combat zone where fighting could break out any day, was considered a coward and would be making small rocks out of big ones at Portsmouth.

W. D. ARMSTRONG

Torrance
