Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 6 -- Tenders for [words indistinct] equipment, materials, and services for the But Son cement project were invited here on March 1. The But Son cement plant, with a capacity of 1.4 tonnes per year, was constructed in Thanh On Commune (Kim Bang District, Nam Ha Province). After the Vietnam Cement Corporation invited tender, six companies from Denmark, Japan, South Korea, France, and Germany entered bids. The companies include : CLE and Mkrubeni, KHD Humboldt and Cosaliberman, Hyundai, Krupp Polysius and Itochu, F.L Smith and Mitsui, UBE and Nissho Iwai. The lowest tender was USD 96.5 million, and the highest one was USD 187.2 million. A 12-member bidding council was set up with Mr. Tran Van Huynh, deputy minister of construction, as its head. The cement consulting investment and development company was appointed by the contractor as the bid's advisor. The construction of the 500 kv Trans-viet power line project has essentially been completed. Power poles in 3,436 building sites of the whole network from Hoa Binh to Phu Lam were erected, 1,487 km of parallel conductive line and safety line had been completely installed by February 25, according to information from the 500 kv Trans-Viet power line project's managing board.
