March 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

'MEANING OF ROE VS. WADE'

By complaining of the Los Angeles Police Department's handling of recent abortion protests relative to its actions against homeless people and Hispanic day laborers, Councilwoman Gloria Molina seems to be assuming some sort of political motives on the part of the department (Metro, March 9). I am not an official apologist for the department, but as a seven-year veteran I feel qualified to comment.

Be they protesters or day laborers, those who block sidewalks and entrances to businesses are committing a misdemeanor, a fact which limits our authority to take action. Put simply, we can only enforce the laws in these situations after a complaint from a victim. In the case of the day laborers, the LAPD responded to complaints from merchants and residents who found the day laborers littering and urinating on their property. Any citizen has the right to ask the police to prevent such activity.

The abortion protesters, however, were seeking a confrontation with the police as a means to gain public attention for their cause. The operators of the clinics realized this and chose not to have the police intervene and generate publicity for the protesters. If they choose to request assistance in the future, the LAPD will enforce the laws without regard to the ideology of either side.

DANIEL HORAN

Hermosa Beach
