BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 16 (KCNA) -- Friendship organisations of Japan called for a detente on the Korean peninsula. The Kochi prefectural council for the promotion of Japan-Korea friendship in a statement April 11 said tensions will increase and the danger of war will not disappear in the world as long as the United States continues to seek "domination of the world by strength". It demanded an immediate halt to the clamor for "sanctions" against the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. The statement urged that the United States should discontinue the "Team Spirit" joint military exercises for good, withdraw its forces from South Korea and sign a peace agreement with the DPRK through talks with it. Speaking at the sixth Kennan, Ibaraki Prefecture, Japan-Korea friendship district meeting held on April 24, the chairman of the Kennan district liaison council for Japan-Korea solidarity, Tamaru Kawaguchi, said the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula must be settled through DPRK-U.S. talks. A letter of request to the Japanese Government was adopted at the meeting.
