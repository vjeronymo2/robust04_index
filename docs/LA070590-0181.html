July 5, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

SOUTHLAND;

HACKMAN AVOIDS HEART ATTACK

Actor Gene Hackman narrowly avoided a heart attack when he checked into a Portland, Ore., hospital complaining of chest pain, his doctor said today.

"We think we got him just in the nick of time," Dr. Herbert Semler said at a news conference after he treated the actor for partial blockage of a coronary blood vessel.

Hackman, 60, was vacationing on the Oregon coast when he began suffering the pain. He was admitted Tuesday to St. Vincent Hospital & Medical Center.

Semler said Hackman suffered from a narrowing of an artery leading to the heart. The doctor said he inserted a balloon catheter into the artery.

Semler said he is recommending an exercise and diet plan for Hackman, who lives in Beverly Hills.
