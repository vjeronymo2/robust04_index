BFN [Text] Tehran, April 25, IRNA -- Head of the Hajj and Pilgrimage Organization Hoseyn Reza'i on Monday did not confirm a BBC report that Saudi Arabia has agreed to the dispatch of 115,000 Iranians to Mecca for this year's hajj pilgrimage. Head of the organization, Mohammad Hoseyn Reza'i told IRNA here Monday that Iran has not yet received a formal agreement from the Riyadh government to the effect. The Iranian official added that despite having little time Iran is ready to send at least 115,000 pilgrims to Saudi Arabia under an agreement signed by the Iranian and Saudi foreign ministers in Oman in 1990. Riyadh has cut Iran's quota to 61,000.
