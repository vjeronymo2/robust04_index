BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 5 May. The Central Bank reported that the public sector's foreign debt was $19.628 billion through February, of which 64.4 percent corresponds to the nonfinancial sector and 25.5 percent to the financial institutions. The private sector's debt totalled $10.513 billion. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 13 Apr 94 p 7) Budget Director Jose Pablo Arellano said public expenditure increased by almost 7 percent in 1993, with savings being 5 percent of the GDP. He also said social expenses grew 9.5 percent, and public investment 20 percent. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 21 Apr 94 Economic Section p 4) Economy Minister Alvaro Garcia reported that foreign investment totalled $804.9 million in the year's first quarter, 19 percent higher than in the same period of 1993. Mining received the highest percentage of the investment, with 66 percent; services 20.9 percent; industry 7.1 percent; construction 2.9 percent; and agriculture 1.5 percent. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 21 Apr 94 pp B1-B12) The Central Bank reported that the trade balance registered a $135 million surplus in the year's first quarter as compared with the $75.5 million deficit registered during the same period in 1993. Exports totalled $2.5254 billion and imports $2.3904 billion. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 26 Apr 94 pp B1-B6) On 3 May, the Comptroller General's Office established a new telephone tariff system which entails a 21 percent reduction in fixed monthly rates. This measure will particularly benefit those people living in areas surrounding large urban centers. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2319 GMT 3 May 94)
