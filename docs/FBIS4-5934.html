BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 14 (KCNA) -- Officials of the Reunification Policy Committee of the Supreme People's Assembly [SPA] had a roundtable talk with the visiting former heads of state and government and politicians of different countries here today. Present there were Kim Yong-sun, secretary of the Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea and chairman of the Reunification Policy Committee of the SPA; Yi Chong-hyok, senior councillor of the Reunification Policy Committee of the SPA; and other officials concerned. Also present were ex-president of Costa Rica Rodrigo Carazo Odio, former Governor-general of Canada Edward Schreyer, former Prime Minister of Egypt Aziz Sedky, former chief of staff of the Armed Forces of France, member of the European Parliament and Chairman of the Union of Independentists General of the Army Jeannou Lacaze, former member of the Parliament of France and member of the European Parliament Pierro Ceyrac, Senior Vice-president of the Center for Strategic and International Studies of the United States William Taylor, Senior Vice-president of the CNN International Eason Jordan, Executive Director of the Summit Council for World Peace Antonio Betancourt, Director of Research of the Summit Council for World Peace Mark P. Barry, former senior advisor to the assistant secretary of State for Human Rights of the United States James Zumwalt, managing editor of THE WASHINGTON TIMES Josette Shiner, Executive Director of WASHINGTON TIMES Photo Agency Victoria Yokota, President Hirooatsu Ota and Vice-president Togashi Takayoshi of the Summit Council for World Strategy of Japan and others. They exchanged views on the situation of the Korean peninsula surrounding the nuclear issue, DPRK-U.S. relations, etc. The Korean side said that to denuclearize the Korean peninsula was the consistent stand of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea and it had made persistent efforts to this end. It also said that the nuclear issue was a matter to be resolved between the DPRK and the United States. It held that the DPRK-U.S. talks must be continued and the DPRK's stand for solving the nuclear issue and improving the DPRK- U.S. relations through dialogue was invariable. It said that as the efforts of one side were not enough to resolve the matters, the United States, too, must approach the dialogue with faith and sincerity. It stressed that the progress of the DPRK-U.S. talks depended entirely on the United States. Referring to the question of national reunification, the Korean side declared that invariable was the DPRK's stand for reunifying the country independently and peacefully on the basis of great national unity. It said that witnessing the reality of Korea at first hand, the guests might judge impartially who truly wanted peace and reunification and who sought war, confrontation and division. The Korean side declared that the DPRK would invigorate the struggle for national reunification on the basis of unity of the whole nation under the banner of "10-point programme of the great unity of the whole nation for the reunification of the country" put forward by the great leader President Kim Il-song.
