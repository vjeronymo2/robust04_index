Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Five O'Clock Sharp" newscast] [Text] The precarious peace -- the truce -- in northern Nicaragua is being endangered by a huge military deployment that is keeping mediators from contacting rebel commander Chacal [Jose Angel Talavera], the leader of the 3-80 Northern Front. In light of this situation, the National Assembly unanimously and with the presence of all deputies -- those of the National Opposition Union, the Center Bloc, and the Sandinist National Liberation Front -- called for an end to the massacre and for peace in the countryside once and for all. Let us listen to the statement: [Begin unidentified speaker recording] 1. The National Assembly, in view of the armed conflict in municipalities in the north, believes it is necessary to work for a peaceful solution to avoid the death of Nicaraguan people. 2. We urge the government to maintain the halt of military operations and to carry out all actions to facilitate and promote a direct dialogue that should contribute to the creation of situations suitable for a definitive demilitarization of irregular forces. 3. The National Assembly urges the irregular forces to put down their weapons and rejoin civilian life to contribute to peace and to the economic and social recovery of the municipalities affected by the war. 4. We urge the government to adopt the necessary measures to evacuate the wounded, care for them, and to respect the lives of the rebels. We also urge the government to make efforts to create jobs and to integrate the rebels into the productive process. 5. The National Assembly reiterates its commitment and support for the peace process and urges the country's various sectors to contribute to the overall objective of definitively ending the dramatic war in this country. [end recording]
