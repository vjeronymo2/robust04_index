December 1, 1990, Saturday, Valley Edition

HIGH SCHOOL FOOTBALL PLAYOFFS;

CITY SECTION ROUNDUP;

DAVIS LEADS BANNING RUSH PAST KENNEDY

Tailback Travis Davis scored two touchdowns to lead Banning High to a 20-10 victory over Kennedy in a City Section 4-A Division quarterfinal football playoff game Friday night at Gardena High.

Davis ran for 114 yards in 20 carries for the Pilots (8-3).

Davis also had a 98-yard kickoff return for a touchdown in the third quarter and intercepted two passes on defense.

Davis got off to a slow start, gaining only eight yards in his first five attempts. But his sixth carry went for 24 yards and a touchdown in the first quarter, giving Banning the lead for good at 6-3.

Kennedy's top rusher, Ontiwaun Carter, was held to 24 yards in 11 carries, although Carter did have 52 yards on three receptions.

Kennedy (7-5) recorded a first down on its first play from scrimmage as quarterback John McMullen threw to Carter for a 22-yard gain, taking the ball to the Banning 20-yard line and setting up Keith West's 31-yard field goal. But the Cougars didn't get their next first down until the third quarter.

The Pilots held a 14-3 lead at halftime. Their second touchdown came on a two-yard run by fullback Ken McKinley with nine minutes one second left in the second quarter and was followed by quarterback Anthony Nicholson's two-point conversion run. DAVE RAATZ
