BFN ["gb" report: "Bundesbank Lowers Money Market Rate"] [Text] Frankfurt/Main, 20 Apr -- On Wednesday [20 April] the German Bundesbank allocated to the business banks new financial means at an interest rate of 5.58 percent within the framework of its weekly money market business. Last week this interest rate had been 5.7 percent and the week before 5.73 percent. The slightly faster decline of the so-called "third prime rate" was no surprise to most participants in the market, after the Bundesbank had reduced the two traditional interest rates, the discount rate and the Lombard rate, last Thursday [14 April]. A few hours after the allocation by the Bundesbank, the Belgian National Bank announced a reduction of its money market rate from 5.8 to 5.7 percent.
