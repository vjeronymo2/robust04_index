Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Excerpt] [Passage omitted] In an airport statement before leaving for Romania today, President Suleyman Demirel said: [Begin recording] As you know, significant activity can be discerned in Turkish-Romanian economic relations. The number of Turkish entrepreneurs investing in Romania has recently been increasing and currently stands at 2,500. During our visit, we will also inaugurate a Turkish-Romanian bank. I would also like to announce that the number of mutual tourist visits between the two countries has exceeded 1 million in recent years. Moreover, the existing problems and new developments in the Balkans as well as in the Caucasus and the Middle East make it imperative for Turkey and Romania, two important countries of the region, to hold more frequent contacts at every level. The restructuring of Europe also makes these contacts necessary. [end recording] Demirel explained that ways to developing relations with regard to bilateral and international issues will be discussed during the visit. The president added that the possible contributions by Turkey and Romania to peace, stability, and cooperation in the region will also be reviewed. Demirel was seen off at Ankara's Esenboga Airport by Husamettin Cindoruk, speaker of the Turkish Grand National Assembly. General Dogan Gures, chief of the General Staff, and other officials were also present. Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin also left for Romania together with the president.
