930421 FT 21 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television A commission set up by Channel 4's Dispatches to investigate the effectiveness of prison as a deterrent has concluded that far fewer people should be locked up. The six-strong group, including a judge, a chief constable and the former chairman of the Bar Council, declares that for most criminals 'prison is neither a remedy nor a deterrent; it is the most expensive and least effective solution'. The commission not only studied alternatives to prison but compared British and German experiences (9.00). Let's hope Episode 2 of Living Islam is less packed and peripatetic than Episode 1, otherwise we shall end up more confused than when we started (8.10 BBC2). Today's programme looks back to the great Islamic empires of the Ottomans and Moguls in the 16th century.
