930706 FT 06 JUL 93 / Majority of respondents want BBC to carry on as usual A LARGE majority of respondents to the government debate on the future of the BBC want the corporation to continue broadcasting a wide range of programmes and to be funded by licence fee. An analysis of the 6,210 letters on the subject received from the public by independent consultants Research International Media and Advertising was published yesterday by Mr Peter Brooke, national heritage secretary. Mr Marmaduke Hussey, corporation chairman, said: 'We are delighted at the vote of confidence given to the range of BBC programmes, and their support for our TV and radio services.'
