Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] President Juan Carlos Wasmosy last night said that he has yet to make a final decision regarding the U.S. Government's offer to deploy 600 U.S. servicemen in Paraguayan territory for five months, to erect a number of buildings in San Pedro Department. Wasmosy denied having earlier expressed an opinion on this matter. He noted that all he had done so far was to meet with military representatives at the Armed Forces headquarters and with members of the Congressional Standing Committee to hear their views on this matter. Wasmosy, who made these remarks last night upon his arrival in the country after visiting Ecuador and Peru, also announced that the Paraguayan Government will convey its decision to the United States through the Foreign Ministry. A controvery over this issue erupted among politicians and lawmakers during Wasmosy's absence. Several congressmen have voiced support for the entry into our country of the U.S. servicemen. Asked whether his answer would be positive or negative, he said he would answer this question as soon as "I arrive and have time to stop and think about it."
