Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Crimea's first president, Yuriy Meshkov, told Interfax in an exclusive interview that his republic's economic independence was the first-priority aspect of his policy. He said that the results of the presidential elections had "confirmed the orientation to economic and cultural cooperation with Russia." The referendum on independence to be held next March is expected to put Crimea's economic independence on a juridical footing, he continued. "Crimea must be an independent and full-fledged member of the CIS economic system," said Meshkov. Asked about Kiev's possible negative reaction to the results of the presidential elections, he said that the more than one million votes cast in his support were the guarantor which no decree or political statement can shake. Speaking about the problem of the Black Sea Fleet, he said: "The fleet must be indivisible and Russian, and must be based in Sevastopol -- an inalienable part of Crimea." He said that the other serious problem facing Crimea was the problem of the Crimean Tatars. He said he favored the idea of compensating the Crimean Tatars for the state damage inflicted on them. He believes that Russia, Ukraine, and Uzbekistan, the countries where part of the deported Crimean Tatars now live, must finance their return to their historical homeland. Asked if he would use the white Lincoln given to him by the Crimean billionaire Sergey Shuriga, he said: "I don't think I'll use it very often. But it won't hurt to have a Lincoln for welcoming Leonid Makarovich Kravchuk or Boris Nikolayevich Yeltsin."
