Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Colonel General Boris Pyankov, commander of CIS peace-keeping forces in Tajikistan, told INTERFAX in an exclusive interview Friday that the situation on the Tajik-Afghan border remained explosive. In his words, the winter was fairly calm in Tajikistan. There was no starvation, factories reopened, prices began to decrease gradually. Serious anti-terrorist steps were taken. On the other hand, the commander thought, the struggle for power was becoming increasingly bitter on every level. Pyankov predicted that as mountain passes opened in the spring, opposition fighters and gangsters based in Afghanistan and Tajikistan would step up their activities. He said that tactical exercises of CIS forces were staged for after 20 March.
