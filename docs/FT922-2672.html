920616 FT 16 JUN 92 / UK Company News: Recession-hit Renold tumbles Pounds 3m into the red RENOLD, the chain and gear maker, yesterday announced its first loss since 1987 as recession hit demand in its main markets of the UK, Europe and North America. The loss of Pounds 3m pre-tax for the 12 months to March 28 compared with a previous profit of Pounds 2.5m. The shares fell 3p to 57p. Mr John Allan, finance director, said the outlook remained mixed. 'February and March showed some improvement but May has been somewhat of a disappointment,' he said. Turnover dropped to Pounds 121.5m (Pounds 128.9m). The company said Germany, which accounted for 13 per cent of sales in 1991, weakened towards the end of the year after a strong start. There was a Pounds 1.5m (Pounds 1.7m) exceptional loss for redundancy costs, mainly in Germany. Total staff numbers have fallen 21 per cent since March 1990. Interest charges rose in line with higher borrowings to Pounds 1.8m (Pounds 400,000). Net borrowings at year-end increased to Pounds 12.8m (Pounds 10.4m), while gearing rose from 19 per cent to 25 per cent. Losses per share emerged at 5.6p (earnings 1.4p) The company omitted its interim dividend but is recommending a final of 1p - shareholders received a 3.7p total previously. A transfer from reserves of Pounds 4.4m covered losses and the dividend bill. Shareholders' funds fell from Pounds 54.8m to Pounds 50.4m. Capital expenditure, mainly on new machine tools, fell to to Pounds 5m (Pounds 11.6m), but was still ahead of depreciation of Pounds 4.5m (Pounds 4m).
