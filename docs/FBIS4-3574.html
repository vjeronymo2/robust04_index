BFN [Text] Haikou, April 21 (XINHUA) -- Chinese departments of industry and commerce have formulated new laws and regulations to facilitate the creation of a market economy nationwide. A number of new laws and regulations were formulated in 1993 to maintain market order, according to delegates to a national conference on the legal system, which opened here today. These include the Law Against Unfair Competition and the Law on the Protection of Consumers' Interests and Rights. Delegates said that in 1993 local departments of industry and commerce formulated rules for regulating advertising, another set of regulations regarding the registration and management of commodity markets, and still another set of regulations governing punishment of smugglers. Furthermore, departments of industry and commerce in Fujian, Guangdong and Shanxi provinces and in the big cities of Qingdao and Shenyang published rules for regulating the activities of brokers. Also in 1993, the departments in Jiangsu Province and Beijing formulated a set of regulations on the management and registration of joint stock companies; the Gansu provincial administration of industry and commerce published a set of provisional regulations concerning auctions; and the Hainan Provincial administration of industry and commerce formulated a set of regulations regarding the registration of enterprises' legal status and another set of regulations against unfair competition. The departments in Jilin Province and big cities of Tianjin, Xiamen, Shenzhen, Shenyang, Xian and Zhuhai have abolished or revised existing regulations that are in conflict with the needs of building a market economy, they said. Local departments also formulated regulations and rules to improve supervision of law enforcement by their staff, delegates said. The Tianjin municipal administration of industry and commerce and the Dalian city administration of industry and commerce published a set of regulations on the initiative-based responsibility system for their staff and another set of regulations concerning the punishment of rule-breaking staff members. Delegates said that departments of industry and commerce in all the provinces and regions except Inner Mongolia and Tibet have set up offices in charge of formulating regulations and rules. To date, 184 departments at the prefectural level and 528 departments at the county level have set up such offices, they added.
