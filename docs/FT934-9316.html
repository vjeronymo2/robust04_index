931111 FT 11 NOV 93 / Lilley to seek curb on foreign claimants MR PETER LILLEY, social security secretary, has called for an investigation of ways in which overseas visitors can be restricted from making claims for state benefits. Mr Lilley has asked the Social Security Advisory Council, a government advisory body, to consider draft legislation to preclude people from abroad from receiving housing or council tax benefits to which they were not entitled when they arrived in the UK. Social security officials say foreign visitors can now change their residential status to qualify for new benefits. From next spring, housing benefit will no longer be available to foreign claimants. The government also plans to impose a residency qualification on European Union nationals who claim benefits. Mr Lilley last month highlighted the issue in his speech to the Tory party conference. He said: 'We have all too many home-grown scroungers, but it's beyond the pale when foreigners come here expecting our hand-outs.'
