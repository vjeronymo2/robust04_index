930123 FT 23 JAN 93 / Bank of England Governor: New chief welcomed in Europe YESTERDAY'S appointment was welcomed by central bankers and government officials in continental Europe as a sign that the Bank's stewardship would pass into a safe pair of hands. One central banker in continental Europe said the British government was 'setting a very high store by real professionalism'. As a man with a discreet presence on the international financial circuit for more than two decades 'Eddie knows his interlocutors well,' the central banker said. 'There is high regard for his professional capacity and his ability to communicate. And he's quite a good diplomat.' Mr George's appointment appeared likely to win support in Washington, where he is viewed as the official at the Bank who showed the most awareness of problems in the Bank's handling of BCCI.
