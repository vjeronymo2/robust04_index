BFN [Text] Major General Uri Sagi, the head of the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] Intelligence Branch, has expressed the assessment that there will be a decline in terrorist attacks from the autonomous areas following the signing of the Cairo agreement. On the other hand, the number of terrorist attacks from other areas in the territories will rise. Maj. Gen. Sagi briefed the members of the Knesset Foreign Affairs and Defense Committee today. Our correspondent Hadar Vismonsky reports: [begin recording] Vismonsky: Twenty-four hours prior to the beginning of the IDF evacuation of the Gaza Strip and Jericho, Maj. Gen. Uri Sagi, the head of the IDF Intelligence Branch, presented his evaluations regarding the near future to the members of the Knesset Foreign Affairs and Defense Committee. Sagi believes that the number of terrorist attacks within Israel originating from the Gaza Strip and Jericho and the attacks against settlers inside those areas will significantly decrease. On the other hand, Maj. Gen. Sagi expressed the assessment that the level of terrorism will rise in other areas of the territories where autonomy is not in effect. Sagi explained to the committee members that the PLO has an interest in keeping the security situation quiet and will therefore do everything to reduce the number of attacks in the areas under its control. Maj. Gen. Sagi also commented on relations between the PLO and the rejectionist groups, Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement], and the Islamic Jihad. Sagi takes a positive view of the inclusion of Hamas representatives in the autonomy institutions as part of PLO efforts to prevent a Palestinian civil war. Sagi asserted that the PLO will attempt to prevent the murder of collaborators, noting that this phenomenon cannot be completely stopped. [end recording]
