920506 FT 06 MAY 92 / Arts: Today's Television Last time the BBC got itself involved in the 'One World' movement it left some of us, who normally admire its journalism, wondering what had happened to its usual balance and healthy scepticism. It appeared to embrace the softest and most sentimental ideas of greenery rather as it has embraced Christianity: without the rigorous testing it would apply to other subjects. Tonight brings the first in a six-week run of programmes leading up to the Earth Summit in Brazil, and it will be interesting to see whether a little more clear headedness has been achieved this time. Tomorrow's World reports on a scheme to raise the quality of the water in the Ganges to 'bathing quality' throughout its 1,500 miles (7.30 BBC1). Clashing head on is BBC2's Happy Families which observes five families in five countries attempting to live greener lives for five weeks. Paul Morley's C4 series The Thing Is .. (8.30) must be better this week than last when the subject was boredom. Today it is motorways. Dispatches examines the use of computers by the National Health Service and suggests that decisions taken behind closed doors have perpetuated waste (9.00).
