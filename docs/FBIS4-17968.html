BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Dmitriy Gorokhov] [Text] Moscow, 28 Apr--A ministerial level meeting involving Russia, the United States, the European Union [EU], and the United Nations to discuss matters regarding a Bosnian settlement could be held as early as the beginning of May. This was stated by Andrey Kozyrev on Thursday [28 April] evening upon his return to Moscow from his trip to Geneva and Bonn, where he had talks with the leaders of the foreign policy departments of the United States, France, and Germany. "There is a fairly big chance that success will be achieved in organizing a ministerial meeting during the course of next week," the Russian foreign minister told journalists. He said that a possible venue for the meeting was Geneva. According to Kozyrev, much will depend on how things go in the contact group of four that has been established at the level of special representatives to prepare a full report on the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. It has emerged that Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin, who represents Russia in the group, will have a meeting in Oslo within the next few days with Thorvald Stoltenberg, the cochairman of the coordinating committee of the International Conference on Yugoslavia.
