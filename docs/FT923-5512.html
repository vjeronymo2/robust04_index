920829 FT 29 AUG 92 / UK Company News: Sinclair Goldsmith shows better trend Sinclair Goldsmith, the surveyors, estate agents and rating consultants, finished the year ended May 31 1992 with a pre-tax loss of Pounds 525,000, compared to Pounds 997,000 previously. Mr Edward Langton, chairman, said the cutting of overheads led to a modest profit, before exceptionals, in the second half. Turnover fell 11 per cent to Pounds 2.77m and overheads were reduced 22 per cent to Pounds 3.27m. Exceptional charges of Pounds 104,000 (Pounds 115,000) related to costs of closing the Croydon office. Losses per share were 3.68p (8p) and the final dividend is omitted, leaving the year's total at 0.5p (2p).
