BFN [Text] Russia's Government has approved the Duma Budget Committee's proposal to boost this year's defense spending from 37.1 trillion to 40. 6 trillion rubles. As INTERFAX has learned from parliamentary sources, that was revealed at a closed session of the Federal Assembly's lower house on Wednesday by Deputy Premier Aleksandr Shokhin. The First Deputy Defence Minister Andrey Kokoshin insisted on a 55-trillion-ruble defense budget. According to Grigoriy Yavlinskiy, leading the Yabloko parliamentary faction, there has been no debate on the issue. The Duma backed up the Legislation Committee Chairman Vladimir Isakov's proposal to speed up budget bill debates by voting on the whole package of amendments recommended by the budget committee.
