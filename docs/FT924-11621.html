921024 FT 24 OCT 92 / Businessmen see economy declining NEARLY 70 per cent of businessmen have no confidence in the government's ability to improve the economy, according to a survey by the UK 200 group of practising chartered accountants. About 56 per cent of those surveyed believe the economic position is worsening. The three most important requirements of the people surveyed were lower base rates, improved confidence in the government and measures to stimulate the housing market. The quarterly survey is based on a national sample of about 1,000 clients of the group's 160 member firms of chartered accountants.
