BFN [Text] Seoul, May 9 (YONHAP) -- The ruling Democratic Liberal Party [DLP] appointed Rep. Kim Hyong-o and Song Op-kyo as new deputy spokesmen Monday while retaining Rep. Son Hak-kyu. Kim, 47, is a graduate of Seoul National University with a bachelor's degree in diplomacy. He held the positions of reporter at the TONG-A ILBO, researcher at the Foreign Ministry's institute of foreign affairs and national security, assistant to the prime minister for political affairs and secretary to the president for political affairs. Song, 53, majored in political science at Seoul National University. He was selected through the open employment system for the defunct Democratic Republican Party, was deputy director-general (D-G) for policy research at the former Korea National Party, deputy D-G for policy research at the former new Democratic Republican Party, and is currently deputy D-G for policy coordination for political affairs at the ruling camp. The reshuffle follows Saturday's replacement of party spokesman Ha Sun-Pong with Rep. Pak Pom-chin.
