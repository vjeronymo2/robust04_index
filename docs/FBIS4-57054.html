BFN [Text] Quito, 9 May (AFP) -- General Victor Manuel Vayas was sworn in on Monday as new commander general of the Ecuadoran Army during a simple ceremony at the Presidential Palace and presided over by President Sixto Duran-Ballen. The ceremony was attended by the Military High Command and members of the cabinet. The outgoing commander general, Gen. Edmundo Luna, traveled to the United States last week where he is undergoing a medical checkup. The special military ceremony, with military honors and the usual speeches, is scheduled for Thursday. Defense Minister Gen. Jose Gallardo said the appointment of a new commander will result in the normal changes of Army officers and reiterated that this will not represent a problem. The defense minister added that Gen. Luna was replaced because he had completed his time in the Army ranks. Vayas reiterated that there are no problems. He said all is well and that he wants to carry out his duties as established in the Constitution.
