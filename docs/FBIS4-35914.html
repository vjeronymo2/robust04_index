BFN [Text] Walid 'Asfur, minister of energy and mineral resources, has said the tenders for the power-linkage project between Jordan and Egypt -- a first stage in a comprehensive power grid linking Jordan, Syria, Iraq, Egypt, and Turkey -- will be submitted before the end of this year. The project is expected to be finished by 1997. In a statement to the Jordanian News Agency, 'Asfur said the ministers of energy and electricity and the heads of delegations of the five-way power-linkage states stressed, at the conclusion of their meetings in Cairo recently, the need for power linkage between Jordan and Egypt as a basic, first stage in the comprehensive linkage between the five states. He noted that it was agreed to give priority in financing the project to the implementation of the first stage. The Arab Fund for Economic and Social Development will finance the two parts of the Jordanian-Egyptian linkage; the two parts of the Jordanian-Syrian linkage; and the Syrian part of the Syrian-Turkish linkage. 'Asfur said the next ministerial meeting of the five-way power-linkage states will be held in Turkey in April next year.
