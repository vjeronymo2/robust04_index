921019 FT 19 OCT 92 / DTI role in travel collapse attacked THE Labour party is pressing for an independent inquiry into the Department of Trade and Industry's role in the affairs of Land Travel, the coach tour operator, which collapsed in July, Philip Rawstorne writes. A deputation of Labour MPs, led by Mr Nigel Griffiths, the party's consumer spokesman, will meet Baroness Denton, consumer minister, tomorrow to present evidence of 'gross incompetence and ministerial negligence' in the DTI's handling of the case. Yesterday Mr Griffiths said if an inquiry established that ministerial action could have protected the 40,000 victims of the company's collapse, they should receive compensation. A review of Land Travel's accounts had shown that 'all the warning signs' about the company's finances had been there since 1983, Mr Griffiths said. Auditors had heavily qualified the company's accounts from 1983 and had expressed fears that debts were unlikely to be recovered. No dividends had been paid since 1983. Accounts had been filed late, and switched between holding companies. Accounting periods had been changed four times in five years. Mr Edward Leigh, then consumer minister, had been alerted about the company's situation in August 1991, Mr Griffiths claimed. 'If he had sent in the DTI inspectors then, Land Travel would have been wound up, sparing up to 40,000 victims.'
