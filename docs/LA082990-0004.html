August 29, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

WESTERN AID FOR SOVIET UNION

Your editorial "Hunger Pangs in Soviet Future" (Aug. 1) emphasizes an important point. However, there are several considerations not mentioned.

If the West does not offer the Soviet Union help, it may not only be the end of President Mikhail Gorbachev but also the end of Communism in Russia itself.

Some of the consequences of withholding help would be:

1. The end of the Soviets' $3 billion per year assistance to Castro.

2. The billions of dollars of Soviet military aid to the Afghan government since the Russian withdrawal would have to stop.

3. Their billions to aid client states, like Vietnam, Ethiopia, Angola and North Korea and to support domestic and worldwide KGB activities, would have to be greatly curtailed.

4. The 25% of the Russian annual budget (Foreign Minister Eduard Shevardnadze's figures) for military expenditures would have to be drastically cut. The British defense secretary, Tom King, said last month that the Russian production of new submarines was one every six weeks, while every day two aircraft, six tanks and one missile were being made.

It is appalling to think of what would happen to the efforts for world peace if the things above were to come to pass. Before withholding credits or actual monetary aid, we must carefully consider the actions our Russian friends would be forced to take.

RICHARD R. BOBB

Long Beach
