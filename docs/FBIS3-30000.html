Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 24 KYODO -- China's Vice Premier Zhu Rongji sought Japan's support on Thursday [24 February] for its attempt to win membership of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT), officials said. Zhu made the request in a meeting with Yohei Kono, president of the opposition Liberal Democratic Party (LDP). Kono replied that the LDP will actively support China's bid. Zhu also asked for the LDP's support in Japan's expected provision of a fourth yen loan to China starting in fiscal 1996. Zhu, currently on a nine-day visit to Japan which started Wednesday, also met three former prime ministers, Toshiki Kaifu, Noboru Takeshita and Kiichi Miyazawa. Miyazawa, noting the need of stepping up Japan's support of the Chinese economy, expressed concern over China's potential to become a military superpower, the officials said. "If China becomes economically strong, it will be also militarily strong. Japan has a policy of not becoming a military superpower, so I also want China not to be one," Miyazawa was quoted as saying. Zhu, calling Miyazawa's concern "groundless," replied that "China will never seek hegemony."
