BFN [Text] A regular session of the Cabinet of Ministers took place on Tuesday [14 June]. The almost 200-page free trade agreement with the European Union was approved there. Olgerts Pavlovskis, state minister for foreign trade and European Union affairs, tells us the following about it: [Begin Pavlovskis recording] The cabinet approved the free trade agreement concluded with the European Union and authorized the Latvian delegation to sign it in Brussels on (?20) June. That was a long and very interesting and intensive way. This document is heavy and [word indistinct] -- both in the sphere of Latvian politics and the sphere of the Latvian economy. In this agreement -- we often hear that agriculture will be destroyed, industry will be destroyed, and so on. Various mechanisms, various protection mechanisms so to speak, which will protect not only industrial (products), (when) some sort of a critical situation comes about, for example, in a newly created sector of industry, but also in agriculture, have also been [word indistinct] in this agreement. We have opened the door for Latvian goods to the West. We cannot guarantee that the goods will be bought, we cannot guarantee the market, but at least we have created conditions in which Latvian producers and Latvian farmers will be able to sell their products in the West with reduced tariffs. [end recording]
