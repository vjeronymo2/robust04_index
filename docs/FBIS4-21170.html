BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on regional narcotics developments monitored through 11 April. Colombia Military authorities arrested five members of a gang in Medellin that used children between seven and 14 years of age to sell drugs in Antioquia neighborhood. The operation resulted in the seizure of 45 pounds of marijuana, 500 grams of seeds, toy grenades, 1 gun, 24 knives, ammunition for guns and rifles, a motorcycle, and notebooks given to the minors to hide the drugs. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1300 GMT 2 Apr 94) Colombian narcotics units today seized one ton of precursor chemicals and captured alleged drug traffickers in Caqueta Department. The chemicals were hidden among 20 mattresses, 14 boxes of napkins, and 20 bundles of toilet paper. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0016 GMT 5 Apr 94) Alleged drug trafficker Samuel Alarcon Mengual, wanted by Colombian and U.S. authorities for drug trafficking, was captured today in Riohacha, northern Colombia, police sources reported. A television newscast, however, said Alarcon Mengual surrendered. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 0030 GMT 6 Apr 94) Authorities reported that two drug traffickers were killed, four captured, and an unknown number escaped after a shoot out with police in a house in southern Santa Fe de Bogota, where 12 kg of cocaine was found. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 0112 GMT 6 Apr 94) A plane transporting approximately 200 kg of cocaine to be dropped on the Costa Rican Pacific coast crashed near Nuqui, 750 km northeast of Bogota. Two injured crew members were arrested, Colombian police reported today. The plane had a Colombian registration number and had departed from Villavicencio with a false flight plan. (Paris AFP in Spanish 2258 GMT 8 Apr 94) Swiss citizen Kurt Oswald, wanted by Interpol for drug trafficking, was captured by police in Barranquilla as he attempted to leave for Aruba on a commercial flight. Oswald is accused of transporting drugs to Switzerland, the Netherlands, and France. There are several warrants for his arrest in Europe and Central America. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1903 GMT 9 Apr 94) Police seized two gallons of concentrated liquid marijuana in Santa Fe de Bogota. Although the amount seems small, nine tons of marijuana leaves are needed to produce two gallons of the liquid, and it is four times stronger than heroin. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1800 GMT 9 Apr 94) Costa Rica National Drug Control authorities dismantled one of the largest drug trafficking rings in southern Costa Rica during an operation last weekend in Neily and Pasos Canoa. Nine persons were arrested. (San Jose LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 29 Mar 94 p 5) Guatemala Treasury Guards arrested U.S. citizen Anthony Martorana today at Aurora International Airport for attempting to bring 2 kg of cocaine into the country. So far in 1994, authorities have arrested six U.S. citizens with a total of 499 kg of cocaine. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2256 GMT 6 Apr 94) Honduras Two alleged Guatemalan drug traffickers kidnapped two members of the Matute Donaire family in San Pedro Sula on Wednesday. They kidnappers were apprehended following a shoot-out with authorities. (Tegucigalpa LA TRIBUNA in Spanish 4 Apr 94 p 10) Two alleged drug traffickers from Guatemala were arrested in San Pedro Sula for their involvement in a plan to bring 78 kg of cocaine from Nicaragua into the country in San Pedro Sula. (San Pedro Sula TIEMPO in Spanish 5 Apr 94 p 10) Honduran counternarcotics police seized 78 kg of cocaine on the border with Nicaragua from two Honduran citizens following the arrest of two alleged Guatemalan drug traffickers in San Pedro Sula. The authorities reported that the cocaine, hidden in the false bottom of the vehicle in which the drug traffickers were traveling, had been picked up in Panama, taken through Nicaraguan, and was bound for the United States. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1725 GMT 6 Apr 94) Mexico Judicial Federal Police agents seized in Durango 48 kg of ephedrine being taken to the Unites States, confiscated 994 grams of heroin, and arrested nine alleged drug traffickers in various operations in Durango, Sonora, Baja California, and Coahuuila. (Mexico City LA JORNADA in Spanish 4 Apr 94 p 19) Judicial Federal Police seized in the past hours in various operations 2.6 tons of fresh marijuana valued at over $15 million. The also seized 100 kg of ephedrine--used to enhance the effects of cocaine--and captured three alleged drug traffickers. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0314 GMT 5 Apr 94) Soldiers destroyed approximately 20,000 poppy and marijuana plantations and burned more than 4.5 tons of drugs in nine states during the 25-day ``Region 1-94 Operation,'' it was reported today. Of the total amount of drugs destroyed, 46 percent came from Chihuahua, Durango, and Sinaloa. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2127 GMT 5 Apr 94) Panama Customs narcotics agents seized yesterday 36 kg of cocaine at Tocumen International airport. The drug was hidden in industrial machine parts shipped from Santa Fe de Bogota. There were no arrests. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 6 Apr 94 p 5a) Panamanian police dismantled a drug trafficking ring made up of four Panamanians and two Central Americans who were attempting to take 66 kg of cocaine to Honduras, it was officially reported. The gang obtained the drug in Colombia and shipped it in trailers to Honduras for distribution throughout Central America. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 0214 GMT 6 Apr 94) Police arrested a Jamaican who had swallowed drugs valued at $1 million when he fainted just before boarding a plane for Kingston at Tocumen International Airport. (Panama City EL SIGLO in Spanish 8 Apr 94 p 66) Venezuela Judicial Technical Police arrested Venezuelan Pablo Cesar Ortiz Silva at Maiquetia international airport while he was boarding a flight for Vienna. Ortiz had swallowed 66 cocaine capsules. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 2 Apr 94 p D-5)
