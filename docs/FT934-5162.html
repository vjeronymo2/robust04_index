931201 FT 01 DEC 93 / Observer: Temperamental An opera buff in the Bank of England's supervision department, which monitors the health of banks, recently wrote to Jeremy Isaacs, the Royal Opera House general director, complaining that a tenor in a recent performance was neither 'in tune, in tempo or in Italian'. Isaacs' response was that although the Opera House strives for perfection, occasionally it falls short - which, he said, was not unlike the recent experience of the Bank's supervision department.
