September 26, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

North American-Made Vehicle Sales Drop: Sales of cars and trucks dropped 5.6% in mid-September, compared to last year, when high inventories pressed auto makers to offer big incentives, the companies said. A 42.3% increase in the sales of cars made in North America by Japanese companies was offset by a 7.5% decline in cars made by General Motors Corp., Ford Motor Co. and Chrysler Corp. The Big Three held 84.4% of the car market in mid-September.

The 10 major companies making cars and trucks in the U.S. said they sold vehicles at an average daily rate of 35,810 during the Sept. 11-20 period this year, compared to a rate of 37,948 a year earlier year. Car sales were down 2.6%, and truck sales were off 10.5%.
