Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The director of the Russian Government Center for Information and Social Technologies Yevgeniy Saburov has agreed to head the government of the Crimean Republic. He confirmed this in a Monday interview with Interfax. The proposal to head the cabinet was made to Saburov by the Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov during a visit to Moscow. Saburov, 48, born in Yalta, Crimea, graduated from Moscow University with a degree in economics and worked at several research centers. August 1990 -- April 1991 Russia's Deputy Minister of Education, April -- August 1991 the head of economic advisors of the Russian Prime-Minister, August -- November 1991 the Economy Minister, since January 1992 the Director of the Government Center for Information Technologies. Yevgeni Saburov is a full member of the Academy of Social Sciences. He said he agreed with the Russian leadership that he would remain the scientific chief of the center. Asked about the possibility of introducing the Russian ruble in Crimea he said that he has to examine the economic situation before making decisions.
