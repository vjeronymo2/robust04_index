Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih affirmed the Sudanese Government's eagerness to achieve a peaceful solution to the southern problem. He stated that the government supports a ceasefire, provided that it is not used by the outlaw troops as camouflage for launching military offensives and subsequently hampering the peace endeavors. Addressing the regular weekly forum of the Foreign Ministry yesterday, Dr. Abu-Salih said that many messages and statements recently had been issued by French, American, and UN personalities in relation to battles taking place in the south, noting that these messages and statements espoused that the Sudan Government was determined to end the southern question militarily. The foreign minister said that the miscarriage of the round of peace talks in Abuja two days ago was due to the interference of western circles, which are against the achievement of peace in Sudan. Dr. Abu-Salih described allegations of miltiary offensives by the government on civilians in the south as completely false and baseless. He added that John Garang and his companions are using civilians as human shields, referring to the outlaws' determination to keep ammunition stores and military at [words indistinct] and more civilian areas. Dr. Abu-Salih reiterated the government's commitment to cooperate with regard to delivering relief materials to war-affected citizens through Operation Lifeline, Sudan.
