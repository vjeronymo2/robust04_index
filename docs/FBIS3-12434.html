Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by Dani Gur-Arye] [Excerpts] Once again today, Palestinians clashed with Israeli troops on both sides of the Green Line. Once again, casualties and arrests. In the territories residents defied the curfew in effect almost everywhere in the West Bank and Gaza, blocking roads, burning tires, and throwing stones. The most severe incident took place in Salfit, near Tulkarm, where some 60 residents surrounded an Army outpost, showering it with stones, bottles, and petrol bombs. Soldiers emerged on foot to disperse the demonstrators. They opened fire when one of the youths brandished a knife. Five demonstrators were wounded, two seriously. [passage omitted] More troubling for the authorities are the ongoing demonstrations in Arab communities within the Green Line. The focus of the unrest shifted to Galilee today, where some 50 Israeli Arabs were arrested. In Nazareth youths blocked the main road and threw stones at the bank, the post office, and government offices. Police initially refrained from entering the city in large numbers, but moved in early in the afternoon to break up the demonstrations. Thirty three people were arrested in the city. There were also large demonstrations in Kafr Kana and Baqa al-Gharbiyah. In East Jerusalem, two policemen and eight Palestinians were wounded in clashes today.
