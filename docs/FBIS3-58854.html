Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Paris, Jan 21 (AFP) -- Georgian President Eduard Shevardnadze and French counterpart Francois Mitterrand on Friday [21 January] signed a friendship treaty at the close of a two-day official visit by the Georgian leader. Shevardnadze also initialled the Paris charter of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe (CSCE) which sanctioned the end of the Cold War. Shevardnadze already signed the charter in 1990 when he was foreign minister of the former Soviet Union. Mitterrand, referring to Georgia's economic difficulties and the Abkhazia secession, said Tbilisi "can count on the solidarity of its friends." In a meeting on Thursday with French business leaders, Shevardnadze said France could make a great contribution to exploiting his country's assets and pulling it out of a catastrophic recession.
