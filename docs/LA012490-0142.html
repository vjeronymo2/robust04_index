January 24, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

FOR THE RECORD

Catholic population -- A background box accompanying a story in The Times on Monday about church closings in Chicago incorrectly stated that Roman Catholic Church membership in the United States had plummeted from 73 million to 53 million in less than a decade. The information, taken from the 1990 World Almanac and Book of Facts, was the result of a printing error by the Almanac, according to Constant H. Jacquet Jr., editor of the Yearbook of American & Canadian Churches, which was cited as the Almanac's source. U.S. Catholic population actually grew from 50 million in 1980 to about 55 million last year, Jacquet said.
