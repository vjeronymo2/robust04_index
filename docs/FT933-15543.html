930708 FT 08 JUL 93 / Parliament and Politics: Regulations on fishing delayed BOWING to pressure from the fishing industry, the government announced last night that the introduction of new conservation regulations - due in the autumn - would be delayed until January 1 1994. Mr Michael Jack, fisheries minister, faced complaints from both sides of the House that proposed regulations limiting the time British trawlers can spend at sea were not matched by other EC members. He confirmed that Pounds 25m would be available for reducing the size of the fishing fleet through a decommissioning scheme to operate over the next three years. He said the closing date for applications in respect of eligible vessels had been put back from July 9 until the end of the month. The decommissioning scheme was approved by a government majority of 115 (137-22).
