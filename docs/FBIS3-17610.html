Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Anatoliy Gordeyev] [Text] Donetsk 27 Mar, TASS -- The turnout in parliamentary elections in Ukraine was reported to be on the rise on Sunday afternoon. In the morning, the number of electors taking part in the polls was around 38 per cent, but by 1600 Kiev time, the turnout exceeded 60 per cent, and by 1800 local time, parliamentary elections had been successfully completed in 16 Ukrainian regions. The Crimea, the Donetsk and the Lugansk region, where apart from elections, the population was polled on a number of vitally important problems for these regions, were in the focus of attention of the Ukrainian Central Electoral Commission. Officials from the Donetsk and Lugansk regional councils told ITAR-TASS that questions put on ballot papers of a deliberative poll gave more impetus to the polls, encouraging electors to express their opinion. As a result, the elections in the Donetsk and Lugansk region had been completed by 1800 with the registered turnout of 59 and 66 per cent, respectively. Incidentally, acting Ukrainian Prime Minister Yefim Zvyahilskyy, who voted in the city of Donetsk, was frank about his attitude to a question put to the deliberative poll. Touching on Ukraine's relations with CIS countries, he told journalists that there is no other way for Ukraine but to join the economic union. "We must take part in the Commonwealth on equal and mutually advantageous terms and must work on a mutually advantageous basis with all CIS countries," Zvyahilskyy said. Several hours before polling stations closed today, elections in 19 Ukrainian regions, including Poltava, Chernovitsk, Rovno, Zhitomir, Vinnitsa, Kirovograd, Lugansk, Dnepropetrovsk, Volyn, Sumy, Carpathian, Nikolayev, Ivano-Frankovsk, Khmelnitsk, Kherson, Lvov, Kharkov, Donetsk, Kiev regions and the Crimea, had been completed with the overall turnout of 50.6 per cent out of 76 per cent registered electors. According to preliminary reports, the overall turnout in parliamentary elections in Ukraine was 60 per cent.
