Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Feb. 16 -- Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak received Moldovan Foreign Minister Ion Botnaru to discuss conditions for a more animated economic cooperation between their countries, and joint undertakings on the third markets. Wednesday was the last day of the Moldovan minister's visit to Poland. Botnaru met with leaders of the Polish Economic Chamber (KIG), including its President Marek Kloczko, to discuss the development of economic cooperation in conditions of economic transformations underway in both countries. Botnaru stressed that Moldova is interested in the development of cooperation in the field of food processing and chemical industry. 13 joint economic undertakings are being implemented at present and two new ones are to be launched in the nearest future. Ion Botnaru paid a visit to the Democratic Left Alliance (SLD) headquarters in Warsaw. On Tuesday, the Moldovan guest and foreign minister Andrzej Olechowski initialled a treaty on friendship and cooperation between Poland and Moldova and signed a protocol on mutual consultations between their ministries. Olechowski announced that the treaty would be signed during the Polish visit of Moldovan President Mircea Snegur scheduled for the first half of 1994. He added that the Polish Embassy in Chisinau will be opened this year. Also on Tuesday Botnaru was received by deputy Sejm and Senate speakers, Jozef Zych and Ryszard Czarny respectively, and met with Chairman of the Sejm Foreign Affairs Committee Bronislaw Geremek.
