BFN [Text] Tehran, May 1, IRNA -- Visiting Vietnamese Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam and his Iranian counterpart 'Ali Akbar Velayati here Sunday exchanged views on important issues of mutual interest. Issues concerning politics, bilateral cooperation in economic, trade, cultural and technological areas were among topics discussed by the two foreign ministers. Velayati expressed satisfaction with the trends of constructive cooperation between the two countries. Referring to the problem of the occupied Palestine and Serbian crimes in Bosnia-Herzegovina, Velayati emphasized that the two nations should attain their legitimate rights. He lambasted the UK's policy of supporting the Serbs who are massacring the defenceless people of Bosnia. Manh Cam said Iran has valuable experience and expertise in the field of oil technology which Vietnam is keen to share. He said that regular exchange of views between Foreign Ministries of the two countries would bring closer the views of the two sides on different political issues and would pave the way for cooperation in international forums. The two foreign ministers stressed the need for setting up of Tehran-Hanoi joint economic commission to form a regular basis for cooperation in various areas including joint investment. Manh is part of a delegation led to Iran by Vietnamese President Le Duc Anh.
