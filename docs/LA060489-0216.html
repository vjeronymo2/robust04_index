June 4, 1989, Sunday, Valley Edition

DIGEST: CAL LUTHERAN KICKER SIGNS WITH BEARS

Kent Sullivan, the second all-time leading punter in Cal Lutheran history, has signed with the Chicago Bears.

In four years at Cal Lutheran, Sullivan had 240 punts for an average of 38.4 yards. Also a kicker, Sullivan set school records for longest field goal (52 yards) and longest punt (74 yards) last season.

Sullivan, 25, is scheduled to report to the Bears' training camp in Platteville, Wis., on July 6.

Cal State Northridge product Bryan Wagner was the Bears' punter last season, but he was claimed by the Cleveland Browns when the Bears left him on the unprotected list.

John Tomac of Chatsworth finished 20th in the sixth stage of the Milk Run cycling race Saturday, covering the 106-mile Barnsley-to-Liverpool course in England in 4 hours, 1 minute, 18 seconds.

Tomac stands ninth overall, 38 seconds behind Briton Nigel Bishop. Bishop was credited with the same time as Tomac on Saturday and has a composite time of 24 hours, 40 minutes, 12 seconds.
