BFN [Text] U.S. Secretary of State Christopher and his accompanying delegation left Damascus at 1615 yesterday. He was seen off at Damascus International Airport by Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar', the Syrian ambassador in Washington, and the U.S. ambassador in Damascus. The U.S. secretary of state arrived in Cairo yesterday, Wednesday, evening. He met with Egyptian President Husni Mubarak. Christopher said he believes that Syria is serious about Middle East peace. Following his meeting with President Mubarak, Christopher told reporters that the negotiations are facing a long road, and that the issues are remarkably difficult. In press statements on his arrival in Cairo, Christopher said he held good and constructive talks with officials in Syria and Israel. He explained that during his meetings several proposals were discussed. These proposals concern achieving a breakthrough in the peace process on the Syrian track. Christopher expressed optimism despite the existence of some difficult topics that need further talks with both sides. He said he naturally found points of convergence and difference. However, we will continue the rounds of talks to reach a solution and achieve a breakthrough on all tracks, he added.
