921014 FT 14 OCT 92 / Arts: Today's Television The 'public access' programme on BBC2, now called Open Space, looks more professsional today than when it first started. Back in the early idealistic days the idea was to hand over technology and assistance to members of the public so they could really make their own programmes. The trouble is that if you genuinely allow people full control you risk getting unwatchable material. Today it is often difficult to see the difference between these programmes and those in other current affairs slots. Another series begins tonight with the story of Mordechai Vanunu, who was kidnapped by Mossad and tried in Israel after his claims about Israel's nuclear weapons programme (7.40). Anyone who watched the opening episode of BBC2's bizarre drama serial Downtown Lagos will surely want to see Part 2 (9.25): is it a legal yarn? A story of wild colonial days? A drugs thriller? Who knows, yet it has style and a powerful grip. Channel 4 has two big attractions: the first episode in the final series of the consistently funny Golden Girls (10.00) and another episode from the crazy drama serial The Prisoner (11.15).
