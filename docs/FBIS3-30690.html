Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Feb. 4 -- Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak met at the Sejm on Friday with President of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe Miguel Angel Martinez. The talk focused on issues that were already discussed in Brussels, namely, Poland's full participation in the European structures. The meeting was attended by Spanish Ambassador to Poland M. Jose Antonio Lopez Zaton and Chairman of the Polish Delegation to the Council of Europe Tadeusz Iwinski, the government press office reported. Martinez said that negotiations aiming at peace in Bosnia and Herzegovina were now the most significant issue for the Council of Europe. In this context he spoke about the necessity to work out legal solutions concerning national minorities. Next, Martinez stressed that in his opinion mechanisms of authority in Poland have been stabilised and thus Poland was a reliable and valuable partner for the Council of Europe. Waldemar Pawlak and Miguel Angel Martinez shared the view that participation in the Council of Europe was a chance of a better future for such countries as Poland and Spain, the communique issued by the government press office said.
