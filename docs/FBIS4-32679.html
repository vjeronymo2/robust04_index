BFN [Text] Belgrade, 29 Jun (TANJUG) -- Russian presidential special envoy Vitaliy Churkin has changed his plans at the last minute and has decided not to travel to Belgrade today. This has been learned from Russian sources. According to the same sources, Aleksey Nikiforov, a high-ranking official of the Russian Foreign Ministry, will arrive in Belgrade instead of Churkin late this afternoon. Nikiforov will arrive from Paris, where he participated in the work of the contact group. He is expected to brief Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic about the work tomorrow. [passage indistinct]
