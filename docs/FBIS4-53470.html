BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA May 18 -- Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet following his visit to Myanmar [Burma] paid a working visit to Singapore as guest of Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong. The two prime ministers during their talks yesterday exchanged views on practical measures to push up the implementation of the agreements recorded during the Singaporean leader's official visit to Vietnam in March 1994, including their cooperation in the building of the agreed industrial zone, tourism and air services, and ship building and repairing. The two prime ministers assigned Minister Do Quoc Sam and Minister of Trade and Industry Yeo Cheow Tong the task of coordinators for the building of the industrial zone. On regional issues, the two prime ministers reaffirmed their determination to contribute to the trend of peace, stability and cooperation in Southeast Asia. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet said that Vietnam is actively preparing to become a full member of the ASEAN. P.M. Kiet also met with Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew who paid him a courtesy visit, called on the Keppel Company, the Singaporean leading company in cooperating with Vietnam in ship repairing, oil and gas service and house building, and other places. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet is scheduled to attend the Asia Society conference themed `Wave of the Future: ASEAN, Vietnam and China-Opportunities for World Trade', where he will deliver an address on Vietnam.
