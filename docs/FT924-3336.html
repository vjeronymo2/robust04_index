921208 FT 08 DEC 92 / Voters reject nationalism in Slovenia SLOVENIA has bucked the trend of Balkan nationalism as moderates yesterday emerged as the biggest winners in the first elections since the republic won independence from Yugoslavia last year, writes Laura Silber in Ljubljana. Mr Milan Kucan, the incumbent, won re-election as president with 64 per cent of Sunday's ballot, according to official results with more than half the vote counted. The centrist Liberal Democrats (LDS) led the vote for a 130-seat bicameral parliament, with 23 per cent. The Christian Democrats received 14.9 per cent and the United Left 13.26 per cent. Mr Janez Drnovsek, prime minister and head of the LDS, said talks would soon begin on forming a coalition.
