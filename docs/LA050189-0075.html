May 1, 1989, Monday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: ANAHEIM;

CONFERENCE ON CHILDREN WILL OPEN ON THURSDAY

Marian Wright Edelman, founder and president of the Children's Defense Fund, will deliver the keynote address at noon Thursday for the 67th annual conference of the Assn. of Junior Leagues Inc. in Anaheim.

The 3-day conference, titled "Advancing a Children's Agenda," will focus solely on children's issues, such as the crisis in the foster-care system, homelessness and hunger, intervention in child abuse, the declining status of children in American, promoting self-esteem through art, and the role of volunteers in promoting federal and state action.

In collaboration with the Children's Defense Fund, a national advocacy organization for families and children, Junior Leagues have worked for child welfare reform, homeless families, adolescent pregnancy prevention and child care.

Other speakers will include Jeanette Dunckel, chair of the Foster Care/Adoptions Policy Board, California Children's Lobby and the Children's Research Institute of California, and Cynthia Robbins, executive director of Children NOW, a child advocacy organization in Los Angeles.

Beginning Thursday, the the Orange County public information office will be selling limited-edition sets of six Orange County Centennial pins for $30.

The sets are glass enclosed in wood frames and contain five previously issued pins and a sixth pin depicting the centennial symbol.

The other five pins depict such landmarks as the Old County Courthouse in Santa Ana, the Huntington Beach Pier, the San Juan Capistrano Mission, Knott's Berry Farm, Disneyland and Anaheim Stadium.

The sets may be purchased at the Public Information Office in the Hall of Administration on 10 Civic Center Plaza in Santa Ana. between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m. during the week. There is a two-set limit per person.

Mental health care will be discussed on May 10 by Wendy Kelley of the South Coast Alliance for the Mentally Ill. The title of her talk is, "Mental Health in California -- Where Do We Stand?."

Kelley will speak before the Democratic Club of West Orange County at 7 p.m. in the Talbert Room of the Huntington Beach Library, Talbert Avenue and Golden West Street.

Kelly has been a teacher for the Newport-Mesa Unified School District and the UC Irvine Women's Opportunity Center and has been board president of the South Coast Alliance for the Mentally Ill. The alliance is a volunteer organization which acts as an advocate for the mentally ill.

The meeting is free and open to the public. For information, call 898-1882.
