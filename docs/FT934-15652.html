931011 FT 11 OCT 93 / Mayhew tells Hume to suspend talks PROGRESS in round-table talks about the future of Northern Ireland would become easier if the Hume-Adams initiative were suspended, Sir Patrick Mayhew, Northern Ireland secretary, said yesterday. A dialogue between Mr John Hume, leader of the Social Democratic and Labour party, and Mr Gerry Adams, leader of Sinn Fein, opened last month and has been given a cautious welcome by the government of the Irish Republic. But the initiative has led the Democratic Unionist party to rule out further participation in the round-table talks promoted by the UK government. Sir Patrick said: 'To the extent that Mr Hume's talks have been seen as an obstacle to the continuance of the previous round of talks, it would be a good thing if those talks remained suspended because an obstacle would then be removed.'
