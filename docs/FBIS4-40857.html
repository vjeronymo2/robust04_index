BFN [Text] Today Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev received the U.S. Ambassador to Azerbaijan Richard Kauzlarich at the latter's request. President Geydar Aliyev was interested in the results of Ambassador Richard Kuazlarich's visit to Washington, and noted that at around the same time he had paid an official visit to Turkey. Ambassador Richard Kauzlarich said that while he was in the United States he held important meetings with a number of officials, including Secretary of State Warren Christopher, Strobe Talbott, and various congressmen. Richard Kauzlarich stressed particularly that the Secretary of State was pleased with his meeting with the Azerbaijani president. He noted that positive changes in relations with Azerbaijan is palpable in the United States and the Western countries. The Ambassador also noted that the United States is exerting serious efforts to increase the CSCE role in the solution of the Karabakh problem, and that his country is becoming more active in this direction. The Azerbaijani president said that he was pleased with his Istanbul trip, particularly with his meeting with U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. He said he was certain that the meeting would play an important role in the further development of Azerbaijan-U.S. relations. He also said they had an extensive discussion on Armenia's aggression against Azerbaijan. President Geydar Aliyev noted that we have not relinquished our previous approach, and that we demand the withdrawal of the aggressive forces from the occupied lands, the guaranteeing of Azerbaijan's territorial integrity, and the inviolability of its borders. We assess highly the role international organizations play in such important issues, and stressed that the CSCE Minsk Group's efforts on the matter will be very important. President Geydar Aliyev also touched upon the agreements reached during the meetings with the chairman of the [CSCE Minsk] Group Jan Eliasson and Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev. He reiterated Azerbaijan's positions on this issue. [Begin Aliyev recording] During the talks we declared that we are in favor of settling these issues peacefully and asked him [not further identified] to ensure the continuation of the cease-fire. We reached certain agreements. Therefore, I want to state that our principal stance on these issues remains the same. Your information that there is a positive turn in relations between the Azerbaijani Republic and the United States, and generally with the West, pleases me. I believe that our loyalty to the democratic principles in our republic, the launching of democratic and economic reforms, the measures to take our country along the democratic path, and our desire and efforts to resolve this grave problem -- the ongoing war between Armenia and Azerbaijan -- by peaceful means and through international organizations, all testify that the Azerbaijani Republic is walking, and will continue to walk, on the path of democracy. [end recording]
