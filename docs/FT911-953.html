910509 FT 09 MAY 91 / International Company News: VASP to invest Dollars 1.5bn in route expansion VASP, the Brazilian airline, has announced plans to invest Dollars 1.5bn in expanding its international and domestic routes over the next two years, despite a deep recession in the sector which has led many of the group's competitors to slash costs. The group said it would aim to raise Dollars 1bn from international banks and Dollars 500m from domestic creditors. The group would not reveal which banks it was talking to, but according to VASP's spokesman, Mr Wagner de Goes, several had expressed interest. VASP hopes to obtain much of the money through aircraft leasing deals. The investment plan will include the purchase of nine MD11s. Boeing 747s are also on the shopping list, but Mr de Goes said no firm deal had yet been made. VASP is holding talks with Embraer, Brazil's state-owned aircraft manufacturer, to buy 50 Brasilias. The airline considers the cost of the Embraer model, at Dollars 7.5m, too high and is hoping to strike a deal. The new aircraft will be used to service routes the airline plans to open between Sao Paulo, Miami and New York, and Sao Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, Los Angeles and San Francisco. The group is also planning to expand its domestic routes in the Amazon, north-east and central regions. 'A significant investment in international routes will increase our hard currency income,' said Mr de Goes. 'And we need hard currency to finance our airplane purchases.'
