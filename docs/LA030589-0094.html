March 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: THE FORBIDDEN ZONE BY MICHAEL LESY (ANCHOR BOOKS / DOUBLEDAY: $7.95)

On the advent of his father's mortal illness, Michael Lesy sets off to confront the subject of death, the "forbidden zone."

"Our culture is permeated by images and accounts of death," Lesy writes, "but they are only fictions, the works of the imagination, counterfeits. The real thing is carefully hidden."

Lesy travels across the United States interviewing pathologists, homicide detectives, undertakers, slaughterhouse workers as well as murderers, executioners and mercenaries, all of whom distance themselves from their work -- through humor, ritual, religion.

Among the conversations Lesy relays, a Charleston undertaker tells him that Charles de Gaulle's protection against death by poisoning was to take a pinch of arsenic every day. "A little death every morning to live through the big one." From an AIDS physician, Lesy hears: "You die the way you live. There are very few last-minute transformations."
