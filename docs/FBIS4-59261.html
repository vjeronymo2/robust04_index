BFN [Excerpt] Khartoum, 11 May (KNA/PANA) -- Alarmed over the fate of tens of thousands of its nationals working in embattled Yemen, the Sudanese Government has decided to evacuate those of them who wish to return home. The Commission for the Affairs of Sudanese Working Abroad, Wednesday [11 May] urged the Sudanese living in Yemen who might wish to be evacuated, to proceed to a Sudanese vessel now anchored at the north Yemeni al-Hudaydah port on the Red Sea. The commission said in a statement issued in Khartoum that the national airline, Sudanair, would arrange a flight to the north Yemeni capital, Sanaa, on 14 May to evacuate Sudanese. It urged those wishing to take the flight back home to register their names at the Embassy of Sudan in Sanaa. No arrangements were announced for the evacuation of Sudanese from Aden, capital of south Yemen. The commission said it had set up a task force comprising concerned government bodies and opened an operations room to accomplish the job. Tens of thousands of Sudanese nationals were employed in the two Yemens as doctors, teachers, workers and journalists, among other professions. [passage omitted]
