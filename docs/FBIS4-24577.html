BFN [Text] Lanzhou, May 30 (XINHUA) -- Four days of trade talks in the U.S.A. this month by representatives of Gansu Province have resulted in agreements for considerable foreign investment in this north-west China province. During the Los Angeles talks, the province's team signed with foreign partners 46 million U.S. dollars worth of contracts in imports and exports, together with seven technology agreements and eight promised projects, bringing in 135 million U.S. dollars and 204 million U.S. dollars, respectively. Experts regarded the talks as a new page in Gansu's cooperation with the U.S. and other countries, such as Canada, Mexico and Argentina. The inland province is a main energy and raw materials centre in China, abundant in resources like power and coal. Its non-ferrous products and chemical engineering machinery have nationwide brand recognition.
