940622 FT 22 JUN 94 / Letters to the Editor: High ground in question Sir, If John Postgate ('Religion: are we better off without it?', June 18) wishes to claim the high moral ground for science and scientists we had best look to his climbing rope. When Einstein, for instance, produced E=MC2 as an untainted intellectual crystal, who were all those brilliant beings at Los Alamos who turned it into the primal nightmare of our time? Priests? Mullahs? Accountants? Terence Feely, The Garrick Club, Garrick Street, London WC2
