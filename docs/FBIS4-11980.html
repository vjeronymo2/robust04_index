BFN [Text] Tunis, 2 Apr (MENA) -- High-ranking Palestinian sources taking part in the autonomy talks have said that the PLO has received an Israeli offer to withdraw from Gaza and the Jericho area within four days. According to the sources, Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres reiterated this in statements to the Israeli television yesterday. The sources add that a number of Palestinian deportees have left Tunis for the homeland after Israel has agreed to let 35 deportees return. Palestinians arriving from the occupied territories have said that the Israeli Army command plans to organize the process of transferring control to the Palestinian national authority before withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho and that the Israeli Army started to evacuate the camp and withdraw much equipment from the Gaza Strip a few days ago. The Palestinian sources note that the occupation army's senior officers estimate that the army will be prepared to complete its withdrawal within 72 hours of receiving orders to withdraw. According to Palestinian sources in Tunis, 300 of the Palestine Liberation Army's senior officers will enter Gaza and Jericho to take over control once the expected Israeli withdrawal is completed.
