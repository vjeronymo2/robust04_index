920530 FT 30 MAY 92 / World Bank warns on Czech pollution Czechoslovakia will have to spend more than Dollars 50bn (Pounds 27.7bn) to curb heavy pollution and bring its environmental standards up to acceptable levels, the World Bank said yesterday, Reuter reports from Washington. '(Czechoslovakia) has become one of the world's most polluted countries,' the bank said in its World Bank News publication. 'It will . . . be decades before the country's main environmental problems are solved.' The bank had approved a Dollars 246m loan for Czechoslovakia to help the country's big power plants run more efficiently and reduce air pollution.
