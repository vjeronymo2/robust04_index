Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 25 Feb (MENA)-- Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has stated that President Muhammad Husni Mubarak has expressed his deep concern and condemnation of the horrible incidents that occurred at the Ibrahimi Mosque at dawn today. He said the president is following up on the details and developments, as well as the contacts made at the United Nations level and in the various world capitals. He added that the president gave instructions to support the convening of a UN Security Council meeting and stressed the need to take the necessary measures to protect the Palestinians and maintain their security. The foreign minister added that the president telephoned Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat this evening and expressed his condolences over the martyrs of the painful incident. The president also consulted with 'Arafat on the steps to be taken. President Mubarak received a message from U.S. President Bill Clinton and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, and expressed his deep concern at the threats to which the Palestinians are being subjected. During these contacts, the president stressed the need to take the necessary measures to avoid a recurrence of such an incident in the future, for preventing the settlers and extremists from threatening and terrifying the Palestinian citizens, and for taking this into consideration during the talks on the Palestinian-Israeli track, which basically aim to restore the rights of the Palestinian people, their land, and their security.
