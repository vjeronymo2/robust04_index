940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / International Company News: SAS to cut 2,900 jobs in shake-up Scandinavian Airlines System is to cut costs by nearly SKr3bn (Dollars 372m) over the next 18 months through a shake-up in staffing and operations. The airline, which made a loss in each of the last four years, plans to cut 2,900 full-time jobs, one in seven of its 20,000 staff, and streamline group organisation. It is also closing down some routes and reducing the number of aircraft types operated. Two models, the Boeing 737 and Saab 340, will disappear from its fleet. SAS said the moves would assist future expansion and allow it to enter alliances or co-operation agreements with other airlines. In November, plans to merge with KLM Royal Dutch Airlines, Swissair and Austrian Airlines as part of the Alcazar project, collapsed.
