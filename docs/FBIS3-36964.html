Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yefim Shvartsman] [Text] Kiev February 9 TASS -- Russia's and Ukraine's governmental delegations met in Kiev for two-day talks to discuss measures aimed at implementation of the agreements on Ukraine's non-nuclear status reached by the presidents of Russia, Ukraine and the United States. The bilateral meeting, which ended tonight, "was of a constructive and goodwill character, and demonstrated that the two sides were committed to search for a solution to complicated problems pertaining to Ukraine's acquiring a non-nuclear status", said Georgy Kosykh, a spokesman for the Ukrainian president and government. The delegations to the talks were headed by Russian Ambassador at Large Yuriy Dubinin and Ukrainian Deputy Prime Minister Valery Shmarov. Upon the completion of the meeting, the Russian delegation left for Moscow.
