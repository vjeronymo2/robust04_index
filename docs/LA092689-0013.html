September 26, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

POET WARREN

Unlike his illustrious predecessor Mark Twain ("Reports of my death have been greatly exaggerated"), incomparable author Robert Penn Warren was never privileged to read his own obituary.

But he would have been delighted with his eulogy by Scott Harris in The Times (Sept. 16), a beautiful and touching tribute to this well beloved poet and novelist.

MARVIN H. LEAF

Santa Monica
