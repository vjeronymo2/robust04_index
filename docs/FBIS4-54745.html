BFN [Text] Bratislava May 2 (CTK) -- Possibilities of economic cooperation were discussed today by Slovak Premier Jozef Moravcik, Foreign Minister Eduard Kukan and his Indonesian counterpart Ali Alatas. After the meeting, Moravcik said no problems existed between these two countries which would mar the promotion of cooperation. He said he also briefed his Indonesian partner on potential investments into Slovakia in view of access to third markets. Alatas described the strengthening of economic relations as the aim of his two-day official visit to Slovakia which began yesterday. The conclusion of agreements in the future on the protection of investments and double-taxation could contribute to it, he added. Last year Slovakia exported to Indonesia machinery and chemical products amounting to 15 million USD, and imported from Indonesia tin, rubber and textiles worth 3.5 million USD. Alatas also met with Slovak Parliament Chairman Ivan Gasparovic and will be received by President Michal Kovac.
