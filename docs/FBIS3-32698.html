Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Article by Julio Ottoboni] [Text] Sao Jose dos Campos -- On the first anniversary of the launching of the Data Collection Satellite-1 (SCD-1), Science and Technology Minister Jose Israel Vargas and Marcio Nogueira Barbosa, the National Institute of Space Research (INPE) director, yesterday announced in Sao Jose dos Campos the expansion of Brazilian satellite services to Colombian, Ecuadoran, Peruvian, and Bolivian scientific organizations that have asked the INPE to install data collection station in their respective territories. Minister Israel Vargas said the Brazilian Space Agency, which is civilian run, will by-pass the developed countries' boycott against Brazil on the transfer of sensitive technology. He commented: "Our program is for strictly peaceful purposes." The minister believes the new service will enable Brazil to enter the low altitude communications satellite market. He said: "One of the agency's main tasks is a review of the entire Brazilian space program." The Science and Technology Ministry has important space cooperation agreements with China and is looking into the possibility of a partnership with Argentina.
