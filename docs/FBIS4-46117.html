BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 20 KYODO -- Peruvian authorities arrested a member of an ultraleftist guerrilla group who allegedly planned and commanded the 1991 murders of three Japanese aid workers, a Foreign Ministry spokesman said Friday [20 May]. Terusuke Terada told a press conference that Peruvian authorities on Wednesday arrested Felipe Tenorio Barbaran, a member of the Shining Path, for allegedly planning and then overseeing the murders of the three engineers from the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) on July 12, 1991. The latest development brought the total number of arrests made in connection with the case to 10, he said. The three JICA engineers were killed during a raid by the Shining Path on an agricultural cooperative in central Peru. Seventeen members of the Maoist guerrilla group were said to have been involved in the murders, he said.
