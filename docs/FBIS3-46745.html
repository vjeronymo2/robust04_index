Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 5 KYODO -- The government's tax revenues in November declined 3.0 percent from the year before to 6,838 billion yen, the Finance Ministry reported Wednesday [5 January]. The ministry said the November figure brought the total income for the April-November period to 27,671 billion yen, up 2.4 percent, a rate of growth that could make it possible for the government to attain the revised revenue target of 55.68 trillion yen for the current fiscal year. In November, corporate tax revenues plunged 13.6 percent to 3,112.9 billion yen, while revenues from the liquor tax dipped 1.5 percent to 154.5 billion yen, the ministry said. Income tax revenues gained 5.5 percent to 1,828.8 billion yen while those from the inheritance tax soared 96.4 percent to 215.4 billion yen, it said. The consumption tax revenue gained 7.4 percent to 926.9 billion yen while revenues from securities transactions surged 61.1 percent to 34.3 billion yen in reaction to a decline in the previous year, the ministry said.
