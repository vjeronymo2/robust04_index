Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The prime minister has ruled out dissolution of Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh Assemblies and holding of early elections in the states. Talking to newsmen on his arrival in Nandial, Mr. Narasimha Rao said that Congress-I governments in these states have still nine months to go for elections. Replying to a question, he asserted that efforts by external forces to destabilize the country will be foiled and India will not allow anybody to meddle with its internal affairs. Mr. Rao also said that the government is committed to deal firmly with terrorism, especially in Jammu and Kashmir, so that normal condition is returned to the state. Earlier, addressing a public meeting in Atmakur in Kurnool District, he asserted the country will never give in to any evil designs. He said that peace is a prerequisite for development and warned that stringent action would be taken against those trying to disturb the law and order situation. Mr. Rao said the Kashmir problem will be sorted out at the earliest and it is the center's endeavor to establish a popular government in the troubled state soon. The prime minister listed the steps taken by the center for the welfare of women and backward classes. He sought people's cooperation for effective implementation of these programs. He also said that the recent assembly election results in the northern states are clear testimony of the people's faith in the policies and programs enunciated by the Congress-I government at the center.
