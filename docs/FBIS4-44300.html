BFN [Report by Gisela Hopfmueller] [Excerpt] Following today's cabinet meeting, Chancellor Franz Vranitzky was asked his opinion about Vice Chancellor Busek's decision to boycott the upcoming visit by Chinese Premier Li Peng. [Begin Vranitzky recording] I think there are only two options: Either you maintain contact with political representatives of a country like China, or you do not. And then you have to consider what you will achieve with these two options. I think that in spite of rejecting and condemning the human rights situation in China, we Austrians, and other Europeans as well, would not change the situation if we chose to isolate the country. It has become clear from several other cases where the human rights situation is also unsatisfactory that the willingness to meet, to cooperate, is the better possibility, the better solution than isolating the country concerned. [end recording] [passage omitted]
