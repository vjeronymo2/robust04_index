Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 14 Jan -- According to Andrzej Olechowski and Geza Jeszenszky, foreign ministers of Poland and Hungary, it is hard to imagine relations between both states being better than to date. In their view, Polish-Hungarian friendship can become an "engine" of cooperation in the Visegrad Group. After talks today in the Foreign Ministry, Olechowski said that the excellent Polish-Hungarian political contacts can also be translated into economic relations. In the near future, businessmen of both states will prepare a report on possibilities for expanding economic cooperation. The Polish and Hungarian foreign ministers also signed an agreement on cooperation between their departments, including cooperation between ambassadors of Poland and Hungary in third countries.
