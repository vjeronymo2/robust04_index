931109 FT 09 NOV 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: Oil falls further to Dollars 15.60 OIL PRICES continued to weaken yesterday, putting more pressure on the Organisation of Petroleum Exporting Countries to take action at its meeting in Vienna on November 23 to cut its production ceiling of 24.52 million barrels a day. The price of Brent Blend for December fell by more than 25 cents in late London trading yesterday to Dollars 15.60 a barrel. Analysts expect prices to remain in the Dollars 16-Dollars 17 range as long as the current market perception persists that crude oil supplies are plentiful. An estimated surplus in worldwide oil inventories of 30m-40m barrels is sufficient 'to allow traders and refiners to feel complacent', according to Mr Mehdi Varzi, research director at Kleinwort Benson Securities in London. He suggested that an Opec cut of 500,000 barrels a day made on a pro-rata basis by all 12 members would be needed to 'shake the psychology of the market.'
