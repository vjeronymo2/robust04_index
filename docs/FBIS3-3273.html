Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Sofia Wu] [Text] Taipei, March 17 (CNA) -- A 20-member Taiwan delegation will leave for Kuala Lumpur Sunday [20 March] to attend the 10th congress of the Pacific Economic Cooperation Council (PECC). The congress, which will open March 22 and last for three days, will bring together representatives from 26 PECC member states, said a spokesman for the Chinese Taipei committee of the PECC. The participants will discuss ways to promote regional economic cooperation, financial liberalization, opening of service markets, and development of aviation and automobile industries in the Asia- Pacific region, the spokesman explained. Malaysian Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad will deliver a keynote speech at the opening ceremony. Thai Deputy Prime Minister Supachai Panitchpakdi and ranking officials from Peru, Vietnam, Singapore, Chile, Russia and other countries are expected to attend the meeting. The Taiwan delegation, to be headed by Jeffrey Koo, vice chairman of the Taipei PECC Committee, will comprise officials, scholars and business executives. Founded in 1980, the Canberra-based PECC groups 26 members, including Taiwan, Mainland China, Japan, South Korea, the United States, New Zealand, Australia, Singapore and Thailand.
