920107 FT 07 JAN 92 / Management (The Growing Business): The right time to go it alone - In a Nutshell Only 4 per cent of small business owners would advise others thinking of starting a firm not to do so, according to research carried out for National Westminster Bank. The most popular advice from the more than 800 small businesses up to two years old which were polled was: check carefully, do your homework and get expert advice. Eight per cent of those questioned advised against starting up with too little capital. Asked for the biggest hurdle they had to jump, one in five small business owners cited sustaining and monitoring cash flow while one in six reported it had been finding the original finance.
