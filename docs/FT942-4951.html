940607 FT 07 JUN 94 / International Company News: Sara Lee to take Dollars 495m charge for shake-up Sara Lee, the US food and personal products company, is to take a Dollars 495m after-tax charge against earnings in its fourth quarter for a worldwide restructuring. The charge is equivalent to Dollars 1.03 per share. The restructuring will lead to the closure and consolidation of some hosiery manufacturing plants in the US and Europe. Sara Lee hopes the move will lift the long-term results of its personal products division, which includes brand-name hosiery such as Hanes and L'eggs; the knitwear company, Champion; and intimate apparel lines Playtex, Bali, Dim and Kiwi. The charge includes the cost of cutting Sara Lee's international workforce by about 6 per cent, or 8,280 employees. The restructuring is expected to produce gradual cost savings, with the company forecasting annual operating savings of Dollars 250m beginning in 1998. Ms Ellen Baras, analyst with Duff and Phelps, said a restructuring had been widely expected. 'What this does is accelerate their plan to get their hosiery operations back on track,' Ms Baras said. Sara Lee's fourth quarter ends in July, with results due on August 8. Analysts expect the company to report flat results, with growth tempered by overcapacity in its hosiery division. The restructuring resembles similar successful efforts by Sara Lee to turn round its bakery and packaged meat operations, analysts said. 'It's a growth-driven company,' one observer said. 'The personal products division has been turning around more slowly than they would like.' Mr John Bryan, Sara Lee's chairman, said that while the company is 'on course' and expected record sales and earnings for the fiscal year, 'our ability to remain a growth company, competing effectively in rapidly changing markets, both today and in the future, requires these actions.'
