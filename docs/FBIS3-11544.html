Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 22 March. Alfredo Acedo Machado, president of the Acedo Mendoza group, today addressed a group of Venezuelan and Colombian investors who met in Caracas and spoke about corporate business strategies and growth strategies of Venezuelan corporations. Acedo Machado pointed out that movements of capital in Latin America have grown considerably and that foreign investments in 1992 in six countries of the region amounted to $12.3 billion. He added that the main investments between 1992 and 1993 were registered in Mexico, Brazil, and Venezuela. (Caracas REPORTE DE LA ECONOMIA in Spanish 16 Mar 94 p 11) Javier Gomez-Navarro, the Spanish minister of commerce and tourism who is visiting Caracas, announced that Spain has granted the greatest amount of soft credits to Venezuela, to the tune of $300 million. Gomez-Navarro added that he seeks to reestablish commercial contacts in the service and tourism sectors. (Caracas ECONOMIA HOY in Spanish 16 Mar 94 p 23) The Central Bank of Venezuela today sold bonds in the amount of $327 million that will mature in 91 and 182 days. The bank has weekly sales aimed at controlling liquidity, reducing pressure on the dollar exchange rate, and halting the advancement of inflation. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2326 GMT 16 Mar 94)
