920509 FT 09 MAY 92 / How To Spend It: Turn to wood - With the first burst of sunshine, Lucia van der Post has been thinking about the great outdoors SO YOU have no room for a proper garden, and, anyway, your fingers, far from being green, are a much less attractive dirty grey - or the plants are by the time you have finished with them. Grieve not. Invest in some flowers that never fade or dry, that need no watering, no weeding and, above all, no talking to. These hand-carved wooden tulips (left) on their green base come from The Garden Gallery, Ohio and cost Pounds 69.95 (p&p Pounds 5) for a set of six from The Museum Store, 20 The Piazza, Covent Garden, London WC2. Another present from The Museum Store is a packet of seeds in colour-coded pots, which enable gardeners to recreate the plants grown by the painter, Monet. Pounds 19.99 (p&p Pounds 2).
