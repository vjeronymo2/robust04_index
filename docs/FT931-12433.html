930128 FT 28 JAN 93 / International Company News: Dow Jones rises 6% in fourth period DOW JONES, publisher of the Wall Street Journal, yesterday posted a 6 per cent rise in underlying fourth quarter earnings to Dollars 37.8m from Dollars 35.5m. Earnings were distorted by charges of Dollars 8m in the quarter which brought reported net income to Dollars 29.8m, or 30 cents a share. A year earlier, net income stood at Dollars 3.5m, or 3 cents, including one-time charges of Dollars 32m. Revenues rose 4 per cent to Dollars 476.1m from Dollars 456.1m. For the whole of 1992, Dow Jones had net earnings of Dollars 107.6m, or Dollars 1.06 a share, compared with Dollars 72.2m, or 71 cents, in 1991. Revenues advanced 5.4 per cent to Dollars 1.82bn from Dollars 1.73bn. Results included charges of Dollars 18.8m in 1992 for the adoption of new accounting standards and write-downs. The company had charges of Dollars 32m in 1991. Dow Jones had forecast 1992 earnings between Dollars 1.03 and Dollars 1.07 a share after charges of Dollars 25m. Gannett, US publisher of 82 daily newspapers including USA Today, turned in fourth quarter net income of Dollars 114.2m, or 79 cents, on revenues of Dollars 935.1m compared with earnings of Dollars 97m, or 68 cents, on revenues of Dollars 901.2m a year earlier. For the full year, Gannett had net income of Dollars 199.7m, or Dollars 1.39, including charges of Dollars 146m for the adoption of new accounting standards. In 1991, Gannett earned Dollars 301.6m, or Dollars 2. Revenues rose to Dollars 3.47bn in 1992 from Dollars 3.38bn.
