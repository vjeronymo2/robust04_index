Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Sitnikov] [Text] United Nations January 13 TASS -- Participants in the Geneva talks on settlement of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict have reached important agreements, U.N. secretary general's spokesman Joe Sills reported on Thursday at the U.N. headquarters. The second round of talks on the all-round settlement of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict has completed, the spokesman said. The talks, mediated by U.N. special envoy Edouard Brunner, were held in Geneva on January 11-13. As a result of the talks, the conflicting sides defined their main goal as an all-embracing political settlement. Furthermore, they agreed on a set of procedures and principles to govern the return of refugees and forced migrants to Abkhazia. The two sides also called upon the U.N. Security Council to build up the U.N. presence in the troubled region, according to Mr Sills. The spokesman also reported that the next meeting of experts to discuss the all-round settlement of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict was scheduled to be held in Moscow on February 8.
