Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 11 January. The source is cited in parentheses following each item. The consumer price index in Caracas increased 3.6 percent in December 1993, while the accumulated price index for the year reached 45.9 percent, compared to 31.9 percent in 1992. The Venezuelan Central Bank had estimated the 1993 inflation rate at 46 percent. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 2121 GMT 4 Jan 94) Lagoven, a subsidiary of Venezuelan Petroleum, purchased equipment and material manufactured in Venezuela for 28.507 billion bolivares, which is 68 percent of the company's total equipment investment in 1993. It was also reported that purchases abroad decreased $16.244 billion bolivares. (Caracas EL DIARIO DE CARACAS in Spanish 4 Jan 94 p 21) According to the Central Statistics and Data Processing Office, the accumulated value of nontraditional exports through October 1993 was $2.964 billion, which is $491 million (19.6 percent) more than during the same period in 1992. According to the same source, the total import value between January and October 1993 was $9.364 billion, a reduction of 11.6 percent from 1992. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 5 Jan 94 p 13) On 5 January, the Superintendency for Foreign Investments reported new foreign investments in Venezuela in 1993 totaled $256.383 million. The leading countries were the Netherlands, $96.359 million; the United States, $37.965 million; Panama, $34.683 million; Bermuda, $21 million; and Spain $14.840 million. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1603 GMT 5 Jan 94)
