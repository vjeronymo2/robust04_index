BFN [Text] Islamabad, June 26, IRNA -- Afghan Vice-President Molawi Mohammad Nabi Mohammadi has called for mediation of foreign peace missions to end the current infighting in Afghanistan. According to a press release, the leader of Afghanistan's Harakat-e Enqelab-e Eslami [Islamic Revolution Movement of Afghanistan], also warned that if the current situation prevailed, the country would never see peace and tranquility again and would eventually fall into the hands of colonialism. He accused foreign powers of involvement in the Afghan crisis and of pushing the country towards destruction in order to achieve their own goals. The Afghan vice president urged leaders of all Afghan mujahideen groups to sit at the negotiating table and resolve their dispute through peaceful means.
