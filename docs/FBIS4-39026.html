BFN [Text] Moscow, June 4 (YONHAP) -- South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu separated from the presidential entourage Saturday and hurried to the United States, where sanctions are being discussed against North Korea. South Korean President Kim Yong-sam, here on a summit visit, instructed Han to go to the United States just before he left for Uzbekistan, Kim's aides said. Han hopes to stop off in London for special talks with U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher, who is accompanying President Bill Clinton on a visit to Europe, the aides said. The U.N. Security Council will begin serious negotiations on a sanctions resolution against North Korea from next week, setting deadlines and the degree of sanctions to resolve the nuclear crisis. Han is expected to fly to New York for talks with U.N. Secretary-general Butrus Butrus-Ghali and Security Council President Salim Bin Mohammed al-Khussaiby as well as ambassadors from the United States, Japan, China and Russia. In Washington, Han will confer with administration officials to coordinate policies and future countermeasures on the nuclear problem.
