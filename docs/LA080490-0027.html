August 4, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

THE MESSAGE OF 'SEALS'

While Feigen has fought against the stereotypes women are burdened with, she has not discovered that men are also the victims of society's most violent stereotypes.

Does she care that some young men will see "Navy SEALS" and decide that they must join so they can prove their worth? Does she care that innocent women and children would undoubtedly be killed in an attack such as depicted in "Navy SEALS"? No. She only cares that the one white woman in the film is depicted well.

DON HAYASHI

Culver City
