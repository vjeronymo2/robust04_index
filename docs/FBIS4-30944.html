BFN [Text] India and Romania have signed agreements on cooperation in combating terrorism and for promotion of cultural exchanges and tourism. The agreements was signed at the end of the four-day state visit of the president, Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma, to Bucharest. The home minister, Mr. S.B. Chavan, signed the agreement on combatting international terrorism, organized crime, and illicit trafficking in narcotic drugs. It is to be implemented by home ministries of the two countries. The agreement on tourism envisages a joint campaign to promote tourism and exchange of information and personnel. The cultural exchange programs cover exchange of dancers, musicians, theater groups, academicians, and scientists. Our special correspondent, Ram Behari Vishwakarma, reports from Bucharest that the two countries have also signed a declaration of principles and directions of cooperation under which they pledge to interact with each other in various fields for mutual benefit. The president has since left Bucharest for home at the end of his state visit to Romania. Before his departure, Dr. Sharma expressed great satisfaction over his visit, during which wide range of bilateral, regional, and global issues were discussed. He said Romania's transition to market economy and India's steady progress as one of the world's largest markets opens tremendous possibilities for cooperation between the two countries. The president said India-Romanian friendship and cooperation can make a valuable contribution to the cause of building a peaceful and nonviolent world. The Romanian president, Mr. Iliescu, has accepted an invitation from Dr. Sharma to pay a state visit to India.
