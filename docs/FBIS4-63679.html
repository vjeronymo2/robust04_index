BFN [Text] The commission set up by the government to control the implementation of the package of the Latvian-Russian agreements signed in Moscow at the end of April on the army withdrawal convened for its first session at the Latvian Cabinet of Ministers today. At the session, the Latvian Ministry of Environmental Protection and Regional Development was instructed to submit to the government session on Tuesday a package of documents that are necessary to begin the work of the fund for Skrunda environmental rehabilitation and the repatriation of the military. The Ministry of Defense, the Ministry of Internal Affairs, and the Home Guard, for their part, must submit proposals to the government as early as possible on the organization for guarding the new building of the Skrunda radar station that will be taken over. According to the package of agreements, the Russian Army is still guarding Skrunda radar facilities and the territory taken over from Russia. The issue of organizing the guarding of these facilities will be considered at Tuesday's session of the Cabinet of Ministers. After the session today, Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs, head of the commission, visited the Russian Air Force airfield at Lielvarde. According to the army withdrawal schedule submitted by Russia, the Russian Army must leave the Lielvarde airfield by 31 July 1994. There are approximately 300 Russian military stationed at the facility at the moment. Birkavs is planning to visit the Zvaigznite space reconnaissance station in Ventspils district next week. This is one of the three facilities that, together with Liepaja port and the Skrunda radar station, have been called strategically important by Russia at the interstate talks.
