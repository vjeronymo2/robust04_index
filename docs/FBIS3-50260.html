Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 27 January (TANJUG) -- The foreign ministers of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia and Hungary, Vladislav Jovanovic and Geza Jeszensky, today spoke in favor of resuming contacts and goodneighborly cooperation. "I have always valued goodneighborly relations. I hope that despite the current situation it will be possible to improve Yugoslav-Hungarian relations," Geza Jeszensky said at Belgrade airport upon his arrival from Budapest at the start of a two-day official visit to the FRY. The Hungarian foreign minister said that conditions for cooperation had changed over the last few years but went on to express hope that there was scope for improving relations, especially in the humanitarian sphere, despite changed circumstances. In this context he described Vojvodina Hungarians as a "bridge for cooperation" and said that there was an opportunity currently for them to remain so. Federal Foreign Minister Vladislav Jovanovic said that he expected good results in Yugoslav-Hungarian talks but noted that it remained to be seen what could be objectively achieved. He added that now was the right moment to renew talks and contacts.
