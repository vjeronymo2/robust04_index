930514 FT 14 MAY 93 / International Company News: Sale of German mines finalised THE Treuhand, the agency responsible for privatising eastern German industry, yesterday concluded the sale of the region's potash mines in a contract giving it a 49 per cent stake in the new company, write Judy Dempsey and David Waller. The contract will involve the Treuhand in a DM1bn (Dollars 620m) investment. However, the Treuhand will have the right to sell its stake after five years. The deal involves selling Mitteldeutsche Kali to Kali & Salz, which is 75 per cent owned by BASF, the German chemicals group. Kali & Salz will hold 51 per cent in the new company.
