931108 FT 08 NOV 93 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System The gap between the Dutch guilder at the top of the grid and the Danish krone at the bottom narrowed sharply last week as the krone rose at the expense of the D-Mark. The Belgian franc remained above the French franc despite Belgium's high public debt, which is being exacerbated by high short-term interest rates. Currencies, Page 29 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the system. Most of the currencies are permitted to fluctuate within 15 per cent of agreed central rates against the other members of the mechanism. The exceptions are the D-Mark and the guilder which move in a 2.25 per cent band.
