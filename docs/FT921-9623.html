920211 FT 11 FEB 92 / Croatian Serbs call referendum SERB leaders in Croatia have called a referendum to settle a dispute over whether to allow United Nations peacekeeping forces to be deployed on their territory, Reuter reports from Zagreb. The parliament of Krajina, a Serb-controlled enclave in Croatia, said the ballot would be held on February 22 and 23. The move marks a new act of defiance by Mr Milan Babic, Krajina's president, who does not want UN forces to replace the Yugoslav federal army on his territory and opposes disarming Serb irregulars, as called for under the UN plan. 'I think the referendum is the best way to see what the people want and test public opinion towards me as president,' he said yesterday during a parliamentary session in the enclave's capital, Knin. But about half the parliamentary deputies stayed away from the session, indicating divisions.
