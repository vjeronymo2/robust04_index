Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] The free Trade Unions of the Romanian Radio and Television has sent us the following communique: Taking into consideration the decision of the Romanian Government by which Mr. Paul Everac was released from his office as general director of the Romanian Television, as well as the pledges assumed by the Deputy Speaker of Romania's Parliament Dan Martian, promising that the bill on the organization and functioning of the Romanian radio and television societies will be debated in parliament in emergency procedures beginning with 1 February 1994 [beginning of the ordinary session of parliament], the Free Trade Unions of the Romanian Radio and Television declare the following things: 1. The protest action by staging a hunger strike of [television union head] Dumitru Iuga is suspended, effective immediately. 2. The non-observance of the pledges assumed by the chamber of deputies, as well as any attempts to stall the passing of the aforementioned bill will trigger an amplified resumption of the protest actions. The communique is signed by Dumitru Iuga, the chairman of the Free Trade Unions of the Romanian Radio and Television.
