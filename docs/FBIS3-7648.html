Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 30 (YONHAP) -- The ubiquitous badge featuring North Korean President Kim Il-song has disappeared from the lapels of North Korean officials attending international conferences, fueling speculation as to what it signifies. Rep. Pak Chung-su, who led a South Korean delegation to the 91st International Parliamentary Union (IPU) meeting in Paris, said Wednesday he noticed that all nine members of the North Korean delegation had not sported the Kim Il-song badge. In addition, all six North Korean representatives at another Paris meeting of the United Nations Educational, Social and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) did not wear the badge, according to Pak. The absence of the badge, the symbol of Kim Il-song's "chuche" (self-reliance) ideology, is drawing unusual attention because all North Korean officials had until recently worn it as though it were a bodily appendage, Pak said. "It is a big change, considering the rigidity and inflexibility of the North Korean regime and society," Pak added. Pak speculated that North Korea's leaders may be preparing "a grace period" during a transition of power from Kim Il-song to his son and heir-apparent, Kim Chong-il.
