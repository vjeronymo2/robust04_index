BFN [Text] Tallinn, June 07, BNS -- The Estonian parliament, with votes 63 for, nine against and three abstentions, today passed the law on general elections. The new law stipulates that members of parliament are elected by ballot in free elections by universal, equal and direct suffrage. Estonian citizens who on election day are at least 18 years old have the right to vote, and citizens of at least 21 years of age may run for parliament. Legally incompetent citizens are deprived of the right to vote. Under the Constitution, both regular and extraordinary parliamentary elections are declared by the president. Regular elections must be announced at least three months in advance. Extraordinary elections are to be announced within three days of when the need for them arises. General elections will be financed from the national budget. The election campaigns of political parties, election coalitions and single candidates will get no funds from the state or local budgets. The national election commission will register the candidates elected to the parliament and publish the final election returns with a complete list of persons elected not later than on the 10th post-election day. Parliament members will assume their duties on the day the election returns are published.
