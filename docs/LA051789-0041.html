May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: ART

Going, Going, Up: A 12th-Century Chinese ceramic dish used to wash calligraphy brushes was sold at auction Tuesday for $2.82 million. A private collector, Sunrider International of Los Angeles, bought the rare flower-shaped brushwasher that is from the imperial court of the Song Dynasty (1127-1279), according to Sotheby's. A Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) decorated basin sold for $2.61 million. ALEENE MacMINN and CLAUDIA PUIG
