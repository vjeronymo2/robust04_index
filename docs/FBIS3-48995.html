Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, December 30 (TANJUG) -- Croatian President Franjo Tudjman stated Thursday [30 December] that the offensive of the Bosnian Moslems threatened the survival of the Croatian enclaves in central Bosnia and added he expected international pressure to prevent this, REUTERS has reported. In a television interview, Tudjman, whom the British news agency termed "patron of ethnic Croat forces in Bosnia's war," accused the army of the Bosnian Moslem Government of trying to push through to the Adriatic Sea and split Croatian territory. "They even have plans to push through to the sea and split Croatia. Such plans are similar to the Serbs' plans. We can expect the world will put more pressure of the Moslem leadership (to stop fighting," Tudjman said in the interview. A civil war raged for 20 months in Bosnia between the local Moslems, Serbs and Croats. Regular troops of the Republic of Croatia were fighting on the side of the Bosnian Croats.
