BFN [Text] A four-member Iraqi parliamentary delegation led by the speaker of National Assembly, Sa'di Mahdi Salih, arrived in Islamabad today on a four-day official visit. Talking to newsmen at the airport, the leader of the delegation said human rights of the Kashmiri people must be respected and the Kashmir issue should be resolved peacefully. He said the purpose of his visit is to bring the people of the two countries closer to each other so that they could have better understanding. Later, the speaker of National Assembly of Iraq called on his Pakistani counterpart in Islamabad. During the meeting, the two leaders agreed that closer cooperation among Muslim countries is required to face the challenges posed by the current world situation. They also discussed matters relating to establishment of IPU [Inter-Parliamentary Union] of Muslim countries, holding of a women parliamentarian conference, and establishment of friendship groups in their respective parliaments. Syed Yusuf Raza Gilani said the government and people of Pakistan were really distressed by the magnitude of suffering of the Iraqi people. He said there should be no restrictions on the supply of food and medicines to Iraqi people. The speaker of National Assembly of Iraq, Mr. Sa'di Mahdi Salih, warmly reciprocated the views and feelings of Syed Yusuf Raza Gilani. He appreciated the geopolitical importance of Pakistan and the role played by it in regional and world affairs.
