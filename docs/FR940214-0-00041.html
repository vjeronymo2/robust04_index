(a) Cost containment. To achieve affordable rents and occupancy rates (not considering rental assistance or similar subsidies), all development costs will be economical in nature and not include costs for unnecessary or elaborate design features. Cost containment is not to be interpreted as accepting poor design or cheap construction. Projects must provide the features and amenities necessary for the lifestyles of the tenants and members. Consideration must be given to the cost/benefit ratio when evaluating, recommending, or requiring specific design features or construction techniques. Life cycle cost analysis will be employed to determine the types of materials which will reduce operation/maintenance costs even though their initial costs are higher.
