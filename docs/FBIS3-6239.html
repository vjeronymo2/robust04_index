Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 15 KYODO -- The Fair Trade Commission (FTC) raided the office of the Japan Association of Refrigerated Warehouses and about 20 warehouse companies Tuesday [15 March] for allegedly operating an unauthorized cartel, officials said. Nichirei Corp., unrivaled leader in the refrigeration warehouse industry and also No. 1 in frozen foods, and Yokohama Reito Co., the nation's second largest cold storage company, were among the firms searched by the government fair trade watchdog. The FTC suspect the association and the companies illegally conspired in July 1992 to fix storage fees and the timing of the increase before hiking the fees from July until the end of the year in violation of the antimonopoly law.
