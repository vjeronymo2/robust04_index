BFN [Text] Havana, 27 Apr (PL) -- Jose Antonio Ocampo, Colombian minister of agriculture and livestock, today began an official visit to Cuba with the goal of entering into cooperation accords with the Cuban Ministry of Agriculture. Ocampo, who is visiting at the invitation of Cuban Agriculture Minister Alfredo Jordan, declared his trip is also aimed at achieving technological exchanges in the area of biotechnology, of which he recognized Cuba's prestige throughout the world. "We also hope some Colombian enterprises establish commercial and investment relations in Cuba in such areas as rice production and poultry breeding," the minister of the South American country told the media. Ocampo's visit to Havana is part of a series of contacts made by Colombian ministers and personalities with local authorities, in an attempt to develop and improve bilateral commercial, cultural, and diplomatic relations. Recently, the Colombian health minister visited the island and last year President Fidel Castro accepted an official invitation from his South American colleague Cesar Gaviria.
