920228 FT 28 FEB 92 / International Company News: AT&T to buy French software group for Dollars 88m AMERICAN Telephone & Telegraph, the US communications and computer group, is to buy DATAID, a French software and information services company, for about Dollars 88m. DATAID is a supplier in France of professional services, facilities management and industrial, scientific and technical systems. AT&T said the French company offered compatible and complementary products and services in several key areas to its subsidiary AT&T Istel, a leading European information technology services company which is based in the UK. The acquisition will be made by a public offering on the Paris stock exchange, which will have to approve the deal before disclosure of the financial details. Mr Pierre Benhamou, chairman and chief executive of DATAID, owns 61 per cent of its shares and is supporting the offer. The French company which had revenues of slightly more than Dollars 125m in 1991, employs more than 1,500 people and has offices in France, Belgium and Luxembourg. AT&T said that it would continue to be run by its French management, with AT&T Istel providing support. Common areas between the two companies include facilities management, computer integrated manufacturing and also value-added and data services.
