921128 FT 28 NOV 92 / Income tax returns will fall INCOME TAX receipts will fall by 4 per cent this year, Inland Revenue statistics indicated yesterday. The figures project total revenues from income tax at Pounds 60.6bn for 1992-93, down from a total of Pounds 63.1bn for 1991-92. The top 5 per cent of tax-payers will pay a larger proportion of income tax for the current year than for the previous 12 months - up from 31 per cent to 32 per cent. The estimates suggest that the top 1 per cent of taxpayers will pay 15 per cent of income tax during the current year, the same proportion as last year, while the bottom 50 per cent of taxpayers will pay 14 per cent, down 1 per cent. The statistics show that there are 24.8m income tax payers this year including 2.2m in the lowest range with gross income between Pounds 3,445 and Pounds 5,000 and 700,000 earning more than Pounds 40,000. Total income before tax is Pounds 367.4bn and total income after tax is Pounds 306.8bn, giving an average income tax charge for taxpayers of 17 per cent. Estimated personal wealth in 1990 is Pounds 1,235bn. The top 1 per cent of the adult population held 14 per cent of wealth. In 1989-90, the latest year for which accurate data are available, employment income accounted for 71 per cent of total income of Pounds 333bn. Profits and professional earnings were 11 per cent, investment income 11 per cent, occupational pensions 4 per cent and national insurance pensions 3 per cent. Total income for that year was largest in Greater London at Pounds 47.8bn, followed by Strathclyde at Pounds 12.2bn and Essex at Pounds 10.1bn. Inland Revenue Statistics 1992. HMSO. Pounds 19.50.
