Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, leader of the LDPR [Liberal Democratic Party of Russia] group in the Russian State Duma, told Interfax that he was pleased with the decision made by Yegor Gaydar not to serve as vice premier in the new government. "Some time ago I told both Boris Yeltsin and Viktor Chernomyrdin: `Remove Gaydar, Chubays, Fedorov and Kozyrev and you'll have a perfect government,'" Zhirinovskiy emphasized. In his view, Gaydar "must be replaced by a normal industrialist." Zhirinovskiy made it plain that his parliamentary group was "prepared to endorse any government the president wants but I am saying on behalf of my group: `Boris Nikolayevich, I implore you to accept Gaydar's resignation.'"
