Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Sarajevo, March 4 (AFP) -- Bosnian Croats and Moslems have given the green light for UN troops to patrol frontlines between the two groups as talks were to open in Vienna Friday [4 March] aimed at working out details of their proposed federation. UN patrols due to start as early as Saturday will be accompanied by unarmed Croats and Moslems, said Major Rob Annink, a spokesman for the UN Protection Force (UNPROFOR). Starting Saturday talks will also be held on opening up six routes to cross the frontlines. The report comes a day after Bosnian army General Muslimovic and his counterpart, General Vrbanac of the Bosnian Croat HVO [Croatian Defense Council] force, agreed on the terms of the patrols. UN peacekeepers will be deployed immediately at the Croat-Moslem hotspots of Mostar, Vitez, Gorni Vakuf, Prozor, Konjic, and Jablanica where ceasefire violations have been rampant, said UNPROFOR spokesman Major Guy Vinet. Under the Moslem-Croat agreement all checkpoints manned by HVO and Bosnian army troops will come under UN control, Vinet added. [passage omitted] Meanwhile in Vienna, representatives of the Moslem-led Bosnian government and Croats were due to open talks at the U.S. embassy at 3:30 p.m. (1630 GMT) Friday on the fineprint of how to establish a federation. Bosnian Justice Minister Kasem Trnka will head the government delegation while the Croat delegation will be headed by Deputy Croatian Foreign Minister Sanader Ivo. U.S. special envoy on ex-Yugoslavia Charles Redman will also attend. A high-level transitional committee has been charged with drafting a constitution for the Croat-Moslem federation as well as the outlines of a confederal arrangement linking the proposed alliance with the Republic of Croatia. The committee in addition will try to forge a military accord between Bosnian Moslems and Croats and an agreement on the shape of governmental structures in the proposed federation. The talks here are expected to last around 10 days. [passage omitted]
