BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 4 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa told business leaders Monday [4 April] he hopes that debates on the 1994 fiscal budget up for Diet approval will be expedited, his aides said. Hosokawa made the remark during a morning meeting with business leaders, including Eiji Suzuki, former head of the Japan Federation of Employers' Associations (Nikkeiren), they said. The executives expressed concern that a delay in debates on the budget will affect economic recovery, the officials said. The business leaders also urged Hosokawa to make clear the government's stance [on] macroeconomic measures, such as tax cuts in years beyond fiscal 1994, which began Friday, and an additional appropriation for public works projects, they said. Other business representatives at the meeting included Shoichiro Toyoda, next president of the Federation of Economic Organizations, Kosaku Inaba, chairman of the Japan Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Nikkeiren Chairman Takeshi Nagano and Masaru Hayami, chairman of the Japan Federation of Corporate Executives.
