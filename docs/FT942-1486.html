940624 FT 24 JUN 94 / Moscow wins new loans to help economy Russian Prime Minister Victor Chernomyrdin yesterday ended meetings in Washington with Dollars 820m in new loan commitments from the World Bank, Dollars 400m in equity funds from the US Overseas Private Investment Corporation (Opic) and numerous smaller deals and joint ventures designed to assist the restructuring of the Russian economy. Two of the World Bank loans, for Dollars 240 and Dollars 80m, will support farm and land reform, respectively. This is the bank's first attempt to help Russia lay a foundation for private agriculture. A Dollars 200m World Bank loan will help improve private banking services, promote banking stability and help mobilise resources and credit allocation. A Dollars 300m loan is to upgrade deteriorating roads. The bank's total commitments to Russia will be around Dollars 1.5bn by June 30. Opic announced support for construction of a generic drug manufacturing facility in Russia. On Wednesday it pledged Dollars 200 in risk insurance for a US-Russian-German joint venture to redesign and improve the commercial aviation engine used in Russian-made aircraft.
