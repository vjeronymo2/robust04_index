Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, 10 Mar (XINHUA) -- A joint statement regarding the basis for the friendly ties between China and Azerbaijan signed by President Jiang Zemin and Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev was made public in Beijing today. Text of the joint statement follows: President Aliyev of the Republic of Azerbaijan paid an official visit to China from 7-10 March at the invitation of PRC President Jiang Zemin. The leaders of the two countries exchanged views on bilateral relations between the PRC and Azerbaijan and international issues of common concern in a friendly, sincere, and realistic atmosphere. As a result of the talks, the two sides made the following statement: 1. The Republic of Azerbaijan and PRC (hereinafter referred to as "the two sides") believe that the similarities in history, culture, and customs between the two peoples and their friendly ties serve as an underlying foundation for close cooperation in many areas; they share the view that to further boost bilateral friendly relations and mutually beneficial cooperation conforms to the fundamental interests of the two peoples and also benefits the peace and security in the region. 2. As friendly nations, the two countries will develop their relations in accordance with the UN Charter, the five principles of peaceful coexistence -- mutual respect for sovereignty and territorial integrity, mutual nonaggression, non-interference in each other's internal affairs, equality and mutual benefit, and peaceful coexistence -- and other norms of international law. 3. The two sides will promote bilateral cooperative ties in politics, economy and trade, science and technology, culture, education, public health, and other fields on the basis of mutual benefit and trust, and sign corresponding agreements to this end. 4. The two sides will work together to strengthen world peace and trust, enhance stability and security, and frequently exchange views and hold consultations on the above-mentioned issues. 5. The two sides place great importance on expanding contacts between the parliaments of the two countries and promoting cooperation in this aspect. 6. The two sides will actively cooperate in cracking down on illegal trafficking, terrorism, illegal actions jeopardizing navigation and civil aviation security, smuggling of antiquities, and other organized and international criminal offenses. They can sign independent agreements in this regard according to the actual needs. 7. The Republic of Azerbaijan Government recognizes the People's Republic of China Government as the sole legitimate government of China, and Taiwan as an inalienable part of the Chinese territory. The Azerbaijani Government will not establish official ties with Taiwan. 8. The Chinese Government supports the Azerbaijani Government's efforts to safeguard national independence and develop its economy and recognizes and respects Azerbaijan's sovereignty and territorial integrity. 9. Neither of the two sides will participate in any actions or adopt any measures of a third country directed against the other. They also agree that no third country is to be allowed to utilize the territory or facilities of either signatory to oppose the other signatory. 10. This statement does not involve the interest of a third country or the two sides' commitments and obligations to other international treaties.
