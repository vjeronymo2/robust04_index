Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Some 48 hours after an armed uprising in Chiapas State, Mexico, there are 56 dead and 43 wounded. According to official reports, problems continue in the five municipalities occupied during the early hours of 1 January by the Zapatista Army of National Liberation. The occupied municipalities are: San Cristobal de las Casas, Chanal, Ocosingo, Altamirano, and Las Margaritas. EL TIEMPO, a Chiapas State newspaper, also reports that the Zapatists also control Guadalupe, (Tepeyac), (Ochuc), and Huixtan, towns which are located near the border with Guatemala. According to official information gathered by PRENSA LATINA at the Chiapas State Attorney General's Office, the National Defense Secretariat and the government have reported that to date six soldiers, 24 policemen, 24 rebels, and two civilans have been killed. PRENSA LATINA has provided a summary of all that has happened to date in Chiapas State. In San Cristobal de las Casas, the clashes between the Zapatista National Liberation Army and government troops have resulted in $1 million in damage. The rebels attacked a nearby jail and released more than 100 prisoners. They also attacked a garrison. Approximately 300 rebels habe placed Ocosingo under siege and it is reported that they have looted the town and committed rape. Seven policemen were killed in Altamirano and two were wounded. Las Margaritas townhall was occupied by 200 rebels who are demanding an end to poverty among the indians.
