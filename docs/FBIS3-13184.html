Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] In appreciation of glorious Iraqi women and the magnificent role they have played in the course of many long years of struggle waged by great Iraq, and to share with Iraqi women their celebrations marking the 25th anniversary of the General Union of Iraqi Women, beloved leader President Saddam Husayn, may God protect him, today called on the premises of the General Union of Iraqi Women, where he met with Mrs. Manal Yunus 'Abd-al-Razzaq, president of the General Union, and several comrade members of the union's Executive Bureau. The president of the union briefed the president on some of the activities carried out by the union. She also reviewed some of the accomplishments the union has made since its inception 25 years ago. Then, the gift from the glorious Iraqi women was presented to the leader president. The gift was an artwork, an Arab shield engraved in genuine Kufic calligraphy. On the shield is written the invocation: In the name of God, Most Gracious, Most Merciful. It is also engraved with the Iraqi flag and some of the statements made by the leader president about Iraqi women. With the presentation of the union's gift, the following address was given: O most magnanimous person, and knight in times of distress, you have always been the best loyal brother. You have always demonstrated matchless compassion, care, and guidance. You have always been the strongest shield and sword defending the honor of his kinsfolk. You have never feared censure or blame. Thanks to you, and to your jihad, Iraqi women have continued to hold their heads high, and their principles pertaining to right and faith have remained unshakable. Their continued support for your blessed banner has remained the epitome of the loyalty of honest sisters to their noble brother. Afterward, the leader president left the premises of the union. He was bidden farewell by Mrs. Manal Yunus 'Abd-al-Razzaq, comrade members of the Executive Bureau of the General Union of Iraqi Women, as well as the glorious women who happened to be on the union premises. They all demonstrated jubilation and uttered trilling cries of joy.
