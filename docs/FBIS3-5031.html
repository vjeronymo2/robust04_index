Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 2 -- Vice President Nguyen Thi Binh paid a four-day official visit to the Republic of Guinea as guest of President Lansana Conte. The vice president was accompanied by Minister of Public Health Nguyen Trong Nhan, Deputy Minister for Foreign Affairs Nguyen Dy Nien, Deputy Minister of Trade Mai Van Dau, and Deputy Minister of Agriculture and Food Industry Nguyen Thien Luan. While there, Vice President Nguyen Thi Binh held talks with President Conte and had working sessions with some ministers of the Council of Ministers. Present at the talks on Guinea's side were minister of state for foreign affairs and international cooperation, minister of public health and social affairs, minister of state for national education, minister of trade, transport and tourism, minister of youth, arts and sports, and other senior officials. Vice President Nguyen Thi Binh and President Lansana Conte exchanged views on the international and regional issues of common concern. The two sides were pleased at the traditional friendship and cooperation between Vietnam and Guinea. They reaffirmed their determination to develop and multilateralize these relations in [words indistinct] of the two peoples. Mrs Binh highly appreciated the achievements recorded by the people of Guinea in the renovation process under the leadership of President Conte. President Conte hailed great efforts made by the Vietnamese Government aimed at improving the people's daily life and building Vietnam into a peaceful, independent and prosperous country. On the world situation, the two sides supported efforts by the international community aimed at settling all conflicts by means of peace. They laid stress on the importance of the struggle for a new world economic order and the great role of the Non-Aligned Movement and the `Group 77' in this struggle. They also agreed to promote and strengthen south-south cooperation. Vice President Binh conveyed President Le Duc Anh's invitation to President Conte to visit Vietnam. The Guinean president accepted the invitation with pleasure. At the end of the visit, the two sides signed an agreement on economic, cultural, scientific-technological and commercial cooperation, and issued a joint communique. Vice President Nguyen Thi Binh left Guinea yesterday for Guinea-Bissau, the fourth leg of her Africa tour.
