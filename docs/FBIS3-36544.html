Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow February 7 TASS -- Chechen President Dzhokhar Dudayev has so far refrained from responding to an opposition leader's statement demanding his resignation. Ibragim Sulaymenov, one of the opposition leaders, demanded on Monday that Dudayev step down as the republic's president, "make public his accounts in western banks for one billion U.S. dollars and choose which court -- secular or sheriat -- he wants to be tried by." Apparently, Chechen leaders do not consider Sulaymenov's ultimatum carried by some mass media worth their attention. According to reports from Grozny, the capital of the Chechen Republic, the situation in the republic is "surprisingly calm" and is under the president's control.
