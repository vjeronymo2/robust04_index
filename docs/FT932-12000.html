930430 FT 30 APR 93 / The Lloyd's Business Plan: Main Objectives of The Business Plan Global profits to reach Pounds 900m by the 1995 accounting year Names to get a 33 per cent pre-tax return A target of 20 per cent pre-tax return for incorporated Names To cut the cost base by at least Pounds 190m To reduce personnel from some 12,000 to 9,500 A move to more direct leadership, intervening where necessary To engender a sense of collective responsibility in the market To introduce a risk profiling system to monitor exposure of syndicates and allow capacity management To establish a small audit unit To facilitate central production of business information To extend the requirement for professional qualifications A move to one-year accounting To reconfigure central support services to form closer links between marketing services and Lloyd's market associations To create a 'ring-fence' to free Names from run-off years and protect new capital from old year liabilities by: - building information bases on pre-1986 liabilities by 1995 - in 1995, reinsure all pre-1986 liabilities into new company - reinsure 1986-1993 years of account into 1994 accounting year - reinsure remaining 'open years' into CentreWrite Active pursuit of negotiated settlement for outstanding legal disputes To establish a new strategy for collection of debts by the end of June More proactive and more independent regulation Full electronic business processing by 1996 including: - connection mandatory by end 1993 - all business supported by electronic version of insurance contract by 1996 To establish central policy preparation unit To establish specialist claims unit for asbestos, pollution and health hazards by the end of 1993 To replace current claims scheme with a single scheme by January 1994 To expand the application of technology to claims support To create a unified processing structure for London market in the long term To set maximum allowable managing agency fees To establish external reviews of projected syndicate expenses The full disclosure of agency staff remuneration The establishment of a central services unit to carry out members' agents' administrative functions by 1994 To introduce Mapas (members' agents' pooling arrangements) and Manuac (managing agents' unitised accounting) by the 1994 accounting year To create licensed Lloyd's advisers to provide advice to incorporated capital To provide well-managed services to facilitate the run-off of syndicates and Names by the end of 1993 To admit incorporated capital for 1994 A target capacity of Pounds 10bn- Pounds 12bn by 1997 To manage aggregate capacity: - setting yearly limit and allowing corporate entities to 'bid' for spare capacity - encourage quota share and consortia arrangements - develop flexible premium income limits Introduce high-liquidity Names: - liquid assets Pounds 500,000 - minimum deposit Pounds 200,000 - confirmed means of 50 per cent of overall premium limit. Mapa-only Names to underwrite with 25 per cent deposit To end restriction on Pounds 3m upper premium income limit To include US dollar securities as acceptable Names' deposit To consolidate market position through simplification of business channels to Lloyd's and market-led initiatives To extend existing central technical services To broaden profile in Europe, Asia and the Pacific To implement plans through six programmes, each led by a senior executive, answerable to the Market Board.
