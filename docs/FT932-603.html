930628 FT 28 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television If you have had a hard day and laughter is your object you may be best advised to pop out to the video shop or even (provided you have plenty of cash and can tolerate the smell of hot dogs and the chatter of the audience) the cinema. Failing that there are three sitcom repeats: So Haunt Me (8.00 BBC1) in which Miriam Karlin plays a Jewish mother-in-law ghost, score six out of 10; Waiting For God (8.30 BBC1) with Graham Crowden and Stephanie Cole playing a couple of bolshie pensioners in a retirement home, score nine out of 10; and Surgical Spirit (8.30 C4) which stars the violently mugging Nichola McAuliffe as a surgeon, score four out of 10. In the last of its present series Horizon surveys some of the theories about the origins of life: primordial soup, comets and meteorites delivering life from the skies, or hydrothermal vents from beneath the ocean (8.10 BBC2). Cancer specialist Rob Buckman presents the third of four programmes which consider the claims and achievements of conventional and alternative health care, Magic Or Medicine? (9.00 C4).
