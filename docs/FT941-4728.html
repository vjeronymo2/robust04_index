940310 FT 10 MAR 94 / Merseyside told to rethink EU bid The European Commission in Brussels has rejected government proposals for spending Pounds 690m of EU money to revive Merseyside's flagging regional economy. The commission argues that the plans, submitted late last year and calling for about half the money to go into training, concentrate too much on solutions which have already failed and show policies for obtaining EU funds rather than regenerating the area. Brussels officials have told civil servants and Merseyside political leaders to rethink their strategy to give a bigger role to the private sector, which had complained about lack of involvement, and to concentrate on five 'growth drivers' - human resources, cultural industries, large industries, knowledge-based business, and small and medium-sized companies. The five-year 'Objective 1' status money offers funds to areas which has a gross domestic product per person under 75 per cent of the EU average. Rejection of the plans was welcomed by Mr Alan Arthur, chairman of coatings business King and Fowler and organiser of a 500-company local private-sector pressure group backed by the Confederation of British Industry. He said: 'Business people need to be able to use this opportunity. So far it has looked like only benefiting every advisory body and consultant who was ever born.'
