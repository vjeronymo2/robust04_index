921201 FT 01 DEC 92 / Gummer not cowed by French trade warnings Agriculture minister John Gummer visits the Royal Smithfield Show, at London's Earls Court yesterday. Later Mr Gummer was told by Mr Jean-Pierre Soisson, his French counterpart, that France would use the full force of the law to protect itself against UK farm exports - particularly lamb, David Blackwell writes. Mr Gummer, president of the EC Farm Council, said after lunch with Mr Soisson that in his view France, as an important exporting country, needed a General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade settlement as much as any country in Europe. He added: 'France has 3m-plus unemployed. They will continue to be unemployed unless we have a Gatt settlement.' He said that there was no evidence for French fears that a Gatt settlement would not square with reform of the EC's common agricultural policy. Mr David Naish, president of the National Farmers Union, said the threats made by some sections of French agriculture against British lorries was outrageous. He added: 'We can understand the concern of French farmers and others, but taking to the streets in a violent manner is not the best way to make progress.'
