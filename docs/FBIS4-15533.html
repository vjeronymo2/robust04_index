BFN [Text] Tajikistan has sent an official note to Afghanistan demanding that the recent incidents on the Tajik-Afghan border be investigated and the responsible be punished, a highly placed official in the Tajik Foreign Ministry, Zafar Saydov, said at a briefing in Dushanbe on Friday. Saydov told journalists about the artillery shooting against the Russian border post in the Khatlon Region from Afghan territory on April 7. Moreover, he added that a Russian armored carrier had been blown up by an anti-tank mine. A border officer had been killed. Saydov said he had reliable information proving that these actions had been planned before hand and had been carried out from Afghan territory. Over the first three months of this year several facilities, including the Russian border defenses have been subjected to fire from Afghan territory over forty times. Casualties and significant material damage have been reported. The Tajik Foreign Ministry's note demanded that appropriate strict measures be used against illegal Tajik armed units acting on Afghan territory in order to halt their activities, Saydov stressed.
