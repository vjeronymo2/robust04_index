940414 FT 14 APR 94 / Howard defends 'silence' ruling Changes in the criminal law which will permit juries to draw inferences from the refusal of a defendant to answer questions put to him by the police were approved by the Commons last night. Protests by opposition MPs and some Conservative backbenchers that the changes amounted to a fundamental constitutional change abolishing the 'right to silence' were denied by Mr Michael Howard, the home secretary.
