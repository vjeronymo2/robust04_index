BFN [INTERFAX report under the "In Brief" rubric"] [Text] While there are currently more than 50 nuclear research plants in operation on the territory of Moscow, none of the working nuclear reactors have safety devices such as protective hoods, for example, measuring up to safety standards. Nevertheless, only three reactors have been shut down since 1992 on the site of the Kurchatov Research Center, and they could be back in use again shortly. This has been advised by Vladimir Kuznetsov, chief of the Moscow inspectorate of the State Committee for the Supervision of Nuclear and Radiation Safety.
