930108 FT 08 JAN 93 / Howe warns of increased taxation LORD HOWE, the former Conservative chancellor, yesterday predicted that the government would have to raise taxes if it was to control public sector borrowing, Emma Tucker writes. The election promise to keep taxes low in an economy that encourages free enterprise would have to stand as a 'medium and long-term pledge', he said. 'It is most important that the government should keep its borrowing requirement under control and the prospect of borrowing more than Pounds 1bn a week in the year ahead must be something the chancellor is concerned with,' he added. Lord Howe said on BBC radio that a recovery began shortly after he put up taxes in his 1981 Budget, a fact which should be taken seriously by Mr Norman Lamont, the chancellor. 'Sooner rather than later is the right time to change taxes,' he said. 'Increases are tough, but sometimes necessary.'
