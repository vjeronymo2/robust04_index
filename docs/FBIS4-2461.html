BFN [Text] Fuzhou, April 11 (XINHUA) -- East China's Fujian Province is giving priority to capital construction in such areas as transportation, communications and energy, according to the province's top official. This year, the province is to put 27.5 billion yuan in infrastructures, up 17 percent over 1993, Provincial Governor Jia Qinglin has told the ongoing meeting of the province's annual people's congress. Of the 30 large and medium-sized projects on the drawing board, 21 are in the fields of transportation and electrical power. They include an international airport in Fuzhou, capital of the province, ports in Fuzhou, Xiamen and Zhangzhou, and two railways. Construction on several power stations will be resumed. And two stations, Mianhuatan and Muyangxi, with a generating capacity of over one million kilowatts, are to be launched this year. In communications, 800,000 lines of exchanges will be opened. And the long-distance telecommunications project in Fuzhou is to enjoy priority of development. Jia noted that apart from the existing ways of raising funds, the province will strive for loans from the World Bank, the Asian Development Bank and big-name international conglomerates.
