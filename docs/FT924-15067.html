921007 FT 07 OCT 92 / World Trade News: Japan greets Bush call JAPAN has welcomed a letter from President Bush urging a quick settlement of the Gatt talks, though Tokyo has no plans for new concessions to speed a settlement, Robert Thomson writes from Tokyo. Premier Kiichi Miyazawa has written to assure Mr Bush Japan is dedicated to resolving the Uruguay Round. Japanese trade negotiators said Mr Bush's letter showed the US administration had been not been completely distracted from the Round by the presidential election. But Tokyo fears divisions in the EC will delay accord on farm trade. Japan is building an international campaign against anti-dumping actions, having selected an EC case against Japanese cassette tape makers as an example of how these actions can be used to limit competition and hurt consumer interests.
