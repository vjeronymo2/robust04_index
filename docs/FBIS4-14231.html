BFN [Text] It has been learned from a political source in Sanaa that battles between army units in the 'Imran area, north of the Yemeni capital resumed, this morning. The battles, in which armored vehicles and guns were used, resumed after the failure of a cease-fire between these opposing units, some of which are loyal to north Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and others to his southern rival Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid. Yesterday's battles, which erupted in the same area, resumed strongly at dawn today. According to north and south Yemeni sources, the battles are still raging. No figures on casualties have been announced. Yesterday, sources said scores of people from both sides were killed or wounded, in addition to civilian casualties.
