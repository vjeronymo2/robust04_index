BFN [Text] Beijing, April 26 (XINHUA) -- China's public security bodies have cracked more than 2,000 cases of narcotics trafficking during the first quarter of this year, seizing 2,000-plus kilograms of various drugs. Officials from the Ministry of Public Security said that public security departments in Yunnan, Guangxi, Fujian and Guangdong Provinces, which are located in south-western border areas and in south-eastern coastal areas, have strengthened border and coast defences and speeded up investigations. During the first three months, Yunnan Province successfully dealt with about 900 cases of drug-trafficking, and seized over 700 kilograms of narcotics. The Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region and Guangdong Province each cleared up 400 such cases in that period. Fujian Province cracked 22 cases, and seized about 40 kilograms of various drugs. 57 criminals were caught in the province, some of whom came from Taiwan province. Big drug-trafficking cases were also cleared up in Shaanxi, Gansu, Qinghai, Henan, Hunan, Shandong and Sichuan provinces and in Beijing. Officials said that local people have played a very important role in the anti-drug campaign.
