930623 FT 23 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television those who have video recorders. The Grass Roots Tiger (8.30 C4) tells of cicindela campestris, the tiger beetle, a green chap, about an inch long, who will eat any other insect, even one twice his size. Summer Wildlife, on the other hand, screened simultaneously on BBC2, is concerned with coccinellidae, or the ladybird, whose diet is highly specialised: she sticks to aphids. The excellent series TV Heroes moves on to Barbara Woodhouse who might be better described as a heroine than a hero, and regarded by most as an eccentric rather than a heroine. Undeniably memorable, however: who can ever forget 'SiTT]' (8.50 BBC1). You can see why the judiciary loathes television programmes specialising in miscarriages of justice: there is now a long list of cases where television programmes have led to people being released from prison, showing that police and judges were wrong. In Trial And Error David Jessel takes up the case of Mark Cleary who was convicted of the murder of a boy in Nottingham in 1985 (9.00 C4).
