November 27, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

NATION;

SELF-FUNDED PLANS FREE OF STATES

Self-funded employee health care plans, which cover about 9.5 million working Americans, are not subject to state insurance laws and regulations, the Supreme Court ruled today.

The court, by a 7-1 vote, said in the case of a seriously injured Pennsylvania teen-age girl that federal regulation of such plans preempts any state regulation. Self-funded plans have benefits paid directly by an employer and not purchased through an independent insurance company.
