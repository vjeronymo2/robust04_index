September 10, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

POP EYE

LEFT FIELD ALBUMS: Though they may not have the blockbuster potential of the above albums, there are several LPs of specialized interest on labels' release schedules.

Liza Minnelli's first pop-rock album, "Results," is due Sept. 19. The bulk of the album was produced by the Pet Shop Boys.

"Kon Bit," an album of Haitian music assembled by film director Jonathan Demme, is scheduled for Oct. 3.

"The Wonder Years" sound track, featuring Joe Cocker's "With a Little Help From My Friends" and Debbie Gibson's "Come Home (Wonder Years)" is due Oct. 9.

"Warm and Tender," an album of lullabys sung by Olivia Newton-John, is due Oct. 24. It's her first album for Geffen Records following a 15-year association with MCA.

The Carpenters' "Lovelines," consisting of previously unreleased material -- including four songs from a solo album recorded by Karen Carpenter in 1980 -- is slated for Oct. 31.

The "Make a Difference Foundation" record, in which current metal faves (including the Scorpions, Bon Jovi and Ozzy Osbourne) sing songs associated with artists who died of drug-related causes (including Jimi Hendrix and Janis Joplin), is scheduled Nov. 7.

A rap album by talk-show host Arsenio Hall, under the pseudonym Chunky A, is due Nov. 14.
