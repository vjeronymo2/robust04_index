Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, March 29 -- Canada's Minister of Foreign Affairs Andre Ouellet and Polish foreign Minister Andrzej Olechowski reviewed on Tuesday Polish-Canadian bilateral relations. On Wednesday, delegations of the two countries are expected to hold plenary talks. The Canadian minister supported Poland's efforts for NATO membership, and the sides discussed Canada's possible assistance in the implementation of the "Partnership for Peace" programme, an official in the Foreign Ministry said after the talks. According to Ouellet, Poland should become a NATO member through the "Partnership for Peace," or closer relations with the Western European Union. During the talk, Minister Olechowski pointed to the fact that all threats to Poland can be at the same time threats to the stabilized situation in the world. Earlier in the day, minister ouellet and director of the Ministry for Foreign Economic Relations Maciej Lesny discussed economic cooperation between their countries, possibilities of Canada's assistance for Polish farmers and access of Canadian goods to the Polish market.
