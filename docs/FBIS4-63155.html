BFN [Text] Baku, 19 May (TURAN)--The session of Azerbaijan's Democratic Congress completed last evening. The agenda included only one question -- return of Russian troops to Azerbaijan. The participants of the session unanimously adopted the resolution which condemns the activities of the current leadership of the republic aimed at "return of Russian troops to the country". The resolution reads that "if Russian troops be brought to Azerbaijan, the Democratic Congress reserves right to conduct rallies of protest." The Democratic Congress was founded on 23 March 1994. It includes five opposition parties: Musavat, the Popular Front, the Democratic Party, the Party of Nation's Liberty, and Ana Torpag Party.
