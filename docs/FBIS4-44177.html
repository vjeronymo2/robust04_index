BFN [Text] Ankara, June 28 (A.A.) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin met French Foreign Ministry Undersecretary Bertrand Dufourcq, who is currently in Ankara for political talks between Turkey and France, it was reported on Monday [27 June]. Dufourcq said after the meeting that France will do its best to improve relations between Turkey and the European Union [EU] and try to create concrete conditions. Dufourcq also said Greek Cypriots have the right to apply for EU membership. "However, expanding the Union will be discussed after a process of reviewing several institutions in the Union," he said. Asked if France will attend a tripartite meeting that is scheduled to be held between Turkey, Germany, and Britain's attendance, Dufourcq said, "We have been invited and we want to participate in the meeting." Meanwhile, Foreign Ministry officials briefing on a meeting between Dufourcq and Foreign Ministry Undersecretary Ozdem Sanberk said bilateral relations were discussed. It was also reported that the two undersecretaries discussed about privatization and increasing French investments in Turkey was also in the meeting agenda. Foreign Ministry officials said France and the Union did not have the attitude to accept Greek-Cypriots to the Union. Officials said Turkey conveyed the French side that Greek Cypriot side, who is already making use of the existing situation, would exploit the membership if the Union accepted the Greek Cypriots to the Union.
