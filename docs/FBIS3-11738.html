Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Amid rivers of human beings that inundated Felix Cuevas Avenue and the streets near the funeral home where a wake was held for the remains of Luis Donaldo Colosio Murrieta, Manuel Camacho Solis, peace and reconciliation commissioner for Chiapas, gave his condolences to the relatives of the Institutional Revolutionary Party candidate. He captured the attention of political representatives and the media who were standing outside the funeral home. Camacho Solis was asked about the incidents of violence in Tijuana, Baja California. He gave a brief message to the public. [Begin Camacho Solis recording] I wanted to be present here publicly because my position has always been an open one. The things I have always wanted to do have always been open. I deeply regret what has happened. It is something very serious for the country's life. Before the eyes of the nation I want to make the following statement: Two days ago I said I did not seek to be president of the republic. After the incidents that have taken place, after this tremendous attack on the nation, I reiterate what I have already said. I do not aspire to be president of the republic. The fatherland is above everything and above everyone. "Camacho, who declined to speak to reporters, arrived this morning in Mexico City from Tuxtla Gutierrez, capital of the southern state of Chiapas, where he had been flown in a military helicopter from San Cristobal de las Casas, seat of the peace negotiations with the Zapatist Indian organization."]
