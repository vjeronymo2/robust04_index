December 14, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

PEARL HARBOR ATTACK

I'm writing because of the oversight or because of the lack of interest by the Los Angeles Times in not publishing anything significant about the memorial of the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor.

Now more then any time in recent years, we face troubled times where many American lives may be lost.

An attack on innocent people is an attack on freedom.

I believe this was a real chance to show all Americans that we honor those who gave their lives for freedom, a chance to say we have strong feelings for those on the sands of Saudia Arabia as well as we have for those who died in Hawaii.

It is the duty of all newspapers to help us reflect as well as inform. The past is what we build our future on. Our lives are enriched by all news.

ROBERT L'AMOUR

Laguna Niguel
