Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Guatemala City, 31 Jan (ACAN) -- The initial count by the Supreme Electoral Tribunal (TSE) in the Guatemalan capital shows a victory for the "yes" vote in today's referendum on 43 constitutional reforms and a high abstention rate of 73.3 percent. The TSE said that with 6 percent of the 1,122 polling stations in the capital counted, the "yes" vote obtained 13.62 percent and the "no" vote 3.22 percent. Abstentionism reached 73.3 percent in this initial count in the metropolitan area. The TSE also reported that in Totonicapan Department, where 125 polling stations were located, 1,845 people voted for the reforms and 404 voted against them.
