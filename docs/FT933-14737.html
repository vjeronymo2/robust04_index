930713 FT 13 JUL 93 / Arts: Today's Television At 8.30 BBC2 and ITV each launch a new run of a well established factual series. In Far Flung Floyd (BBC2) the most entertaining of today's television cooks, Keith Floyd, presents the first of seven programmes reporting on the food of the Far East. He begins in Vietnam, cooking a stew for an army volley ball team. In another series of The Cook Report (ITV) the increasingly large Roger Cook sets out again to stick his foot - figuratively and, when necessary, literally - in the doors of the world's villains. Switched At Birth is one of those productions for which the ITV chiefs would like to move 'News At 10'. An American mini series, it tells of the parents of a baby girl who discover that they cannot be her biological parents. It begins at 9.00, continues at 10.40 and ends tomorrow. In Scarfe On Class the cartoonist makes one of his periodic forays into Big Subjects: his previous programmes on art, sex and the afterlife will be repeated on forthcoming Tuesdays. In today's new programme he learns how to acquire the right accent, and even a lordship (9.50 BBC2).
