Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Brussels, Jan 16 (AFP) -- Urgent plans to bomb Serbian positions around the besieged Bosnian towns of Srebrenica and Tuzla will be studied by the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation here this week, a NATO source said at the weekend. The plans, drawn up by UN commanders in Bosnia at the request of a NATO summit here last week, are due to be presented to UN Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali in Geneva on Monday [17 January], then sent to NATO headquarters here. [passage omitted] They ordered UN field commanders in Bosnia urgently to draw up plans for such strikes, which can be launched only if commanders on the scene ask for them and if Butrus-Ghali agrees. NATO military planners will review the plans here this week, and ambassadors from the alliance's 16 member nations are scheduled to discuss them on Wednesday, the NATO source said. [passage omitted] The NATO source added: "We clearly need a process where the political will (to bomb) is built up in NATO. The credibility of the West and the international community is at stake. I hope governments recognise the stakes." [passage omitted] The NATO source said: "The question is just how does the UN see air power -- just to protect UN troops or as a political weapon?"
