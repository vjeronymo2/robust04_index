BFN [Text] Fuzhou, May 11 (CNS)--Fujian's provincial government has decided to regulate prices of nine categories of daily necessities and 50 basic services to curb inflation there. The nine categories of daily necessities include grain, edible oil, meat and poultry, eggs, aquatic products, fresh vegetables, seasonings, milk and milk products as well as household articles. The provincial government has stipulated that among these categories, adjustment to the prices set by the government must be reported to a high-level authority for filing, while the adjustment to other prices which had been regulated by market forces must be reported to the relevant department for examination five days ahead of implementation of the adjustment. The government has also asked authorities at various levels there to set up and improve the system of reserving important foods such as grain, edible oil and meat as well as grain risk funds and the strengthening of macro-control and adjustment of prices of non-staple foods. [sentence as received] Those found breaching regulations will be seriously dealt with.
