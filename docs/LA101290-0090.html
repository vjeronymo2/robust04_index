October 12, 1990, Friday, Valley Edition

DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: DRIVER CRUSHED WHEN TRUCK OVERTURNS

A 46-year-old Tujunga man was crushed to death Thursday morning when his 18-wheeler truck flipped over in Lancaster, pinning him inside, the California Highway Patrol reported.

A 64-foot-long, 60,000-pound liquid propane gas tank fell off the truck and knocked down a power pole, causing an outage in the area.

About 95 customers were without power for about three hours, said Southern California Edison area manager Denise Henderson.

The accident occurred at 11:35 a.m. on Antelope Highway near Pearblossom Highway, Officer Miguel Siordia said. The driver was traveling 60 m.p.h. to 70 m.p.h. when he rounded a right-hand curve and lost control of his vehicle, Siordia said.

The truck flipped over, pinning the man in the cab for two hours. He was pronounced dead at the scene. The name of the victim was not released pending notification of relatives.
