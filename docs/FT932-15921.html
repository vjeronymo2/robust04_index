930408 FT 08 APR 93 / Observer: QED We no longer simply have to take Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak's word that recent terrorism against tourists in his country is the work of interlopers from foreign powers. His claim has now been backed by Egypt's English-language paper, the Gazette, with what it clearly sees as irrefutable evidence. The attacks could not be masterminded by an Egyptian, it declares in an editorial. 'The proof is the way such operations are carried out. It is professional.'
