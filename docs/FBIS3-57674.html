Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report by Valeriy Gromak: "Relying on NATO.... Will Draft New Regulations Help Real Strengthening of Latvian Armed Forces?"] [Text] Riga -- The Latvian Defense Ministry has prepared new regulations for drafting people into active military service. They envisage reducing the categories of citizens who are exempt from compulsory military service. Currently there are 6,500 people serving in the Latvian Armed Forces, 550 of them officers. These figures break down as follows: There are 1,500 enlisted men and 150 officers serving in the Army, 3,800 enlisted men and 340 officers in the Border Guard, 600 seamen and 30 officers in the Navy, and 150 enlisted men and 30 officers in the Air Force. The Latvian Navy has 15 ships and combat craft, of which six are undergoing repair and two are mothballed. The Air Force has two An-2 aircraft, two L-410 transporters, and six Mi-2 helicopters. One aircraft and four helicopters are combat ready. Latvia wants to guarantee its national security primarily by joining NATO. As we know, to date these efforts have not been successful. And some news agencies report that the Latvian Government has decided to allocate funds to buy two Russian Mi-8 helicopters....
