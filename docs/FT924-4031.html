921204 FT 04 DEC 92 / Accountancy pay 'up 2%' ACCOUNTANTS' salaries rose by just 2 per cent in the past year, highlighting the continued effects of the recession, Accountancy Personnel, the recruitment firm, reported yesterday. Demand for insolvency staff is beginning to level off in the north-east and Scotland, suggesting that the worst of the downturn may be over in those regions. Wages for newly qualified accountants in industry and commerce fell by 3.5 per cent to Pounds 19,208, while those for qualified staff remained unchanged at Pounds 23,222. Salaries for students entering training contracts in accountancy firms rose by 1.5 per cent to an average of Pounds 7,941. Wages for unqualified audit staff in public practice were unchanged at Pounds 8,389, while those for accounting technicians increased 2 per cent to Pounds 6,736. Newly qualified chartered and certified accountants' salaries rose 2 per cent to Pounds 19,041, while those with post-qualifying experience were up 3.5 per cent to Pounds 20,667. Salaries for part-qualified accountants in industry and commerce jumped 4.5 per cent to Pounds 10,500, while certified and management accountant trainees' salaries were level at Pounds 7,472. Accountancy Personnel said technical skills, including using spreadsheets, were a pre-requisite for job-hunters.
