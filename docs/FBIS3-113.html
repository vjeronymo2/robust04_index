Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "African News" program] [Text] The authorities in Senegal have said the four opposition leaders charged after last week's disturbances in the capital, Dakar, could face death penalty. A statement from the attorney general's office said the maximum penalty could be applied if the men were found guilty of inciting the violence in which six policemen and two demonstrators were killed. The opposition leaders, Abdoulaye Wade, Landing Savane, (Boka Kane), and (Babacar Sane), have been charged with undermining state security.
