Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Jan. 6, (IRNA) -- Iran and Turkmenistan on Thursday [6 January] signed several letters of understanding for political, military and border cooperation. The documents were signed by the visiting Turkmen President Saparmurad Niyazov before his departure at the end of a five-day state visit and his Iranian counterpart President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani. Meanwhile, several other letters of understanding were inked between the Turkmen officials and their Iranian counterparts including one on joint cooperation to combat drug trafficking by Iranian and Turkmen nationals. The document was signed by Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati and his Turkmen counterpart. Minister of Roads and Transportation Akbar Torkan signed a letter of understanding on bilateral cooperation on railways and shipping with his Turkmen counterpart. An economic-trade letter of understanding was signed by Iran's Commerce Minister Yahya ol-Eshaq and his Turkmen counterpart. Another letter was inked on bilateral cooperation in the field of oil by Oil Minister Gholam Reza Aqazadeh-Kho'i and his Turkmen counterpart. A letter was signed by Iran's head of customs department Abdul Hoseyn Vahaji and his Turkmen counterpart on transit of goods and costums affairs. Minister of Energy Bijan Namdar-Zanganeh signed a letter of understanding with his Turkmen counterpaert on energy and transfer of surplus Turkmen electricity to other countries through the Iranian territory. Governors general of central banks of Iran and Turkmenistan signed a letter of understanding for improving Tehran-Ashgabat banking relations.
