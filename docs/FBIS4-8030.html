BFN [Text] Warsaw, 11 Apr--A meeting between Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak and U.S. Deputy Secretary of State Strobe Talbott lasted nearly one and a half hours. Talbott is paying a two-day official visit to Poland. After the meeting, Pawlak told journalists that talks focused, among other things, on Polish-U.S. cooperation in international financial and economic organizations such as the World Bank, the IMF, and the OECD. Views were also exchanged on the "Partnership for Peace" [PFP] program. Pawlak announced that Polish-U.S. talks at the experts' level would be held soon. They should lead to joint undertakings this year within the framework of Poland's cooperation with NATO. According to Pawlak, development of the whole Central-Eastern region of Europe was also discussed, as well as the possibility of U.S. support for the process. Talbott said that the United States was of the opinion that Polish reforms were yielding good results. He stressed that the United States was the largest investor in Poland and that economic relations between both states might still be expanded. Talbott pointed out that economic issues would probably be the main subject of talks by President Bill Clinton during his visit to Poland in July.
