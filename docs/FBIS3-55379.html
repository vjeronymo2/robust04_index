Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] At the Russian Federation Foreign Ministry press center today a briefing was held for Russian and foreign journalists. Speaking at it, Grigoriy Karasin, director of the Russian Foreign Ministry's Information and Press Department, said, among other things: [Begin Karasin recording] About the plans of the Russian side as chairman of the Commonwealth of Independent States: In connection with the decision of the Ashgabat meeting of CIS heads of state, that the Russian Federation would, at the present stage, take the chair in the main bodies of the CIS, the Russian Federation Foreign Affairs Ministry is beginning to work out proposals for specific ways of the future development of the CIS. These proposals, that are based on results already achieved within the Commonwealth, cover the following main areas: Establishing the mechanisms for an economic alliance; ensuring human rights and the rights of minorities throughout the CIS, and creating mechanisms to guarantee these rights; restoring cultural, scientific, and educational ties between the Commonwealth countries; creating a unified humanitarian space; shaping an effective system of collective security, with the stress on peacekeeping functions; developing practical mechanisms of the CIS to prevent armed conflicts; ensuring greater effectiveness for joint action by members of the Commonwealth in the foreign-political sphere in the development of cooperation between the CIS, the United Nations, the CSCE, and other international organizations. The corresponding ministries and departments will take part in drawing up these proposals. The Foreign Ministry also intends to draw in the representatives of scientific circles and public organizations. These proposals will be submitted to the Russian leadership, and then Russia, as the current chairman of the CIS, will table them for examination by the other Commonwealth member states. [end recording]
