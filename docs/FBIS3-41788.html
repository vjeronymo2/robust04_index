Language: English Article Type:BFN [Following item transmitted via KYODO] [Text] Kyrgyzstan's Extraordinary Epidemiological Commission has taken urgent measures to prevent the spreading of cholera. The Kyrgyz Ministry of Public Health informed Interfax that the threat of an epidemic is coming from neighboring Uzbekistan, Tajikistan and Kazakhstan. According to the same source, the republic's sanitary services will exercise sanitary control over the railways and motor transport. Commercial charter flights to Pakistan, India, Turkey and Iran have been canceled until October 1. Private vendors have been forbidden to sell drinks, ice-cream and food stuffs. Control has been toughened over the quality of drinking water and over the rivers, lakes, and canals. The Kyrgyz sanitary and epidemiological service reports that no cholera cases have been registered in Kyrgyzstan so far.
