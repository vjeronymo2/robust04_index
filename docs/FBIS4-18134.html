BFN [Text] Paris, March 30 (AFP) -- General Philippe Morillon, who headed UN forces in Bosnia-Hercegovina in 1992-1993, was named head of the French Army's Rapid Deployment Force Wednesday [30 March]. Morillon was nominated for the position by Defence Minister Francois Leotard, a military spokesman said. The 58-year-old Morillon will replace General Bertrand de Lapresle who became head of the UN Protection Force (UNPROFOR) in ex-Yugoslavia on March 5. Morillon came to public attention a year ago for his outspoken criticism of the shortcomings in international intervention in the stricken country. General Morillon will begin his new job on April 5. Under his command will be 35,000 men, made up of four divisions and a logistics brigade.
