BFN [Text] Cochabamba, Bolivia 1 Jun (EFE) -- The preparatory meeting for the "International Decade of the World's Indigeous People" closed today in Cochabamba, Bolivia, with the approval of a document to be submitted to the United Nations. The document contains 53 resolutions vindicating the cultural identity of indigenous peoples, along with their political, social and economic rights. Guatemalan Nobel Peace Laureate Rigoberta Menchu presided over the meeting which started on 31 May. She stated today that indigenous peoples hope their demands will be formally recognized by all UN member-countries. Menchu said the resolutions approved in Cochabamba will become mandatory for all countries worldwide once they are approved by the UN General Assembly. Commenting on what transpired during the meeting in Cochabamba, some 400 km east of La Paz, Bolivian Vice President Victor Hugo Cardenas -- himself an Aymara Indian -- said: "Indigenous peoples can achieve development without renouncing their own culture." The "International Decade of the World's Indigenous People" starts on 10 December 1994 and closes on 9 December 2004. Beside Menchu and Cardenas, 120 delegates from diverse indigenous communities and government organizations from different countries attended the meeting in Cochabamba.
