930104 FT 04 JAN 93 / Life in the single market: Europe Today - Collapse of communism Entrepreneur Jan Kulczyk pictured with surplus Mig jet fighters. The collapse of communism has left fledgling market economies on the EC's eastern fringe, struggling to shake off the remnants of a state-controlled economy. Amid the ruins of the Soviet bloc, men like Kulczyk see their chance to forge trade between the east's food, raw materials and cheap labour and the west's superior technology.
