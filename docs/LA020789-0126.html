February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

5TH-GAME DRAW GIVES KARPOV CHESS VICTORY

Anatoly Karpov of the Soviet Union drew the fifth game of his quarterfinal Candidates chess match against Johann Hjartarson of Iceland in Seattle. By drawing, Karpov won the best-of-six-game match, 3 1/2-1 1/2. The sixth game will not be played.

The Candidates matches are a series of knockout matches conducted by the World Chess Federation to choose a challenger to world champion Gary Kasparov in the 1990 world chess championship. Karpov, who was world champion from 1975 to 1985 and is now the world's second-ranked player, advances to the semifinal round. He will play his countryman, Artur Yusupov, in a semifinal match, tentatively scheduled for this September in London.

Sunday's game repeated the opening moves of the third game, which Karpov had won, until move 11, when Hjartarson varied his play. Hjartarson appeared to have a slight advantage, but Karpov reached a drawn end game by sacrificing first a pawn, and then his queen.

Karpov earned $50,000 for his victory. Hjartarson received $30,000. The match was sponsored by Seattle 1990 Goodwill Games.
