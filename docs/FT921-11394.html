920131 FT 31 JAN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Kaufman challenge on author's claims - Exports to Iraq THE prime minister was yesterday challenged to answer in detail what Labour said were fresh allegations that ministers permitted UK companies to breach the arms embargo against Iraq before its invasion of Kuwait in 1990. Mr Gerald Kaufman, shadow foreign secretary, listed in a letter to Mr Major allegations made in a book entitled The Death Lobby, by Mr Kenneth Timmerman, an investigative journalist. The all-party Commons trade and industry committee is investigating breaches of embargoes during the Iraq-Iran war, but it may not complete its report before the general election. Last night the Ministry of the Defence accepted, at least in part, a claim in the book that Iraqi pilots had been offered training in the UK. The MoD added that no weapons training or training in combat techniques had been provided. The Department of Trade and Industry was confident that no equipment approved for export to Iraq in the 1980s was 'any danger to UK interests'. It emphasised that the arms embargo allowed the export of non-lethal equipment. GEC-Marconi, one of the companies mentioned in the book, said that the allegations were 'inaccurate'. It said last night: 'We have not supplied Iraq with any equipment in the last 10 years.'
