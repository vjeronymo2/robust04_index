BFN [Text] Moscow, April 29, RIA -- "Russia's new policy is based on the premise that finding new friends does not mean losing old chums," Ivan Rybkin, who chairs the State Duma, told North Korean Ambassador Son Sung-pil on April 29. The meeting was held at the request of the latter. The ambassador spoke in favour of more dynamically developing bilateral ties to note that "certain differences in our countries' respective ideologies are no reason for deteriorating mutual relations." On the so-called nuclear issue, the ambassador of North Korea said that his country is ready and willing to deal with all controversial issues of normalisation in the Korean Peninsula by way of negotiation. The two sides discussed prospects for bilateral cooperation, in particular in the economy, science and technology.
