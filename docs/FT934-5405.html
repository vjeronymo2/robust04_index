931130 FT 30 NOV 93 / Observer: Taxed men However, the recession doesn't seem to be making any inroads into Britain's army of tax advisers, who have been outbidding each other in a desperate attempt to hire big names for their budget seminars. KPMG Peat Marwick has hired Lord Lawson to sing for his supper this evening and Coopers & Lybrand has Michael Portillo for breakfast tomorrow. Given that Norman Lamont has sold his soul to ITN, Ernst & Young has had to make do with Lord Healey, the former Labour Chancellor. He may be a bit rusty on current policy, but should be good for a more historical perspective.
