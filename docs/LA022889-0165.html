February 28, 1989, Tuesday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

HOME SALES DOWN 7.4% IN JANUARY

Sales of existing homes fell 7.4% in January after a sharp jump in activity in December, a real estate trade group reported today.

The National Assn. of Realtors said existing single-family homes were sold at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 3.63 million units in January after a 5.7% rise in December.

The decline in sales did not dampen prices. The median price of a home sold in January rose 3.2% from the December level to $91,500.

The January sales decline had been expected, given the level of activity in December, when homes were being sold at the fastest pace in two years.
