Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Islamic Republic of Iran and the Republic of Turkmenistan signed their first postal and telecommunications agreement in Tehran. According to the said agreement, a 480-channel telephone exchange, linking Tehran to Ashgabat, will be inaugurated tomorrow. The telephone link will provide direct telephone contacts between Iran and Turkmenistan. In addition, through that service the peoples of other Central Asian countries will also be able to have telecommunications contact with the Islamic Republic of Iran and the rest of the world. Other points in the said protocol include establishment of regular postal services. The air services organization of the Ministry of Post, Telegraph & Telephone will handle letter and parcel mail between Tehran and Ashkabad. Yesterday the Turkmen communications minister inaugurated the first flight of the said postal service at Payam airport.
