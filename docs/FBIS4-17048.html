BFN [Text] Riga, Apr 21, BNS -- During its extraordinary plenary meeting Wednesday [20 April] night, the Latvian Saeima (parliament) did not authorize the Latvian president, Guntis Ulmanis, to sign Latvian-Russian initialed interstate agreements because the Latvian National Independence Movement and the Homeland and Freedom factions left the plenary meeting's hall before the voting for the draft resolution had started. After a long debate, when voting for separate items, the Saeima accepted the Latvia's Way drafted resolution providing for the the president to be authorized to sign the draft agreements, initialed March 15, on complete withdrawal of Russia army from Latvia, the withdrawal conditions, terms, order and army's legal status during the withdrawal; on Skrunda radar station's legal status during its temporary functioning on Latvia's soil; on Russian militaries' and their family members' protection till the complete army withdrawal. The Saeima also supported the draft resolution's 2nd part providing for the Cabinet to form an experts' group to revise the draft agreement on Russian retired militaries' and their family members' social protection. According to the international legal norms, this document must promote the elimination of the occupation consequences in Latvia. Becuse the two above mentioned Saeima factions left the plenary meeting before the voting, the final decision could not be accepted because of lack of quorum. After the plenary meeting, President Guntis Ulmanis refused to comment the situation.
