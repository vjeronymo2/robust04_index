BFN [Text] Seoul, April 7 (YONHAP) -- South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu met his visiting Icelandic counterpart Jon Hannibalsson Thursday and discussed the North Korean nuclear issue and other international affairs as well as ways to expand bilateral economic cooperation. Han sought Iceland's support in Korea's hope to be a non-permanent member of the United Nations Security Council and join the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) by 1996. Discussing economic cooperation, Hannibalsson asked Seoul to widen doors for fishery imports. After talks with Han, Hannibalsson paid a courtesy call on President Kim Yong-sam and spoke in a seminar at the Institute of Foreign Affairs and National Security. He will visit the inter-Korean truce village of Panmunjom, Daewoo business group headquarters, and Hyundai dockyard in Ulsan before leaving Korea on Sunday.
