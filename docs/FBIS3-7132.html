Language: Malay Article Type:BFN [Text] Vietnam said it will join ASEAN only when its membership in the grouping benefits all ASEAN and Vietnam. Do Muoi, the general secretary of the Vietnam Communist Party, said this at a question and answer session with participants of the 10th general meeting of the Pacific Economic Cooperation Council, PECC, in Kuala Lumpur this evening. He said Vietnam is currently studying the ASEAN working mechanism and will join the group when the situation is right. Mr. Do Muoi also said that ASEAN can contribute greatly to the rebuilding and modernization of Vietnam. He pointed out ASEAN can contribute to the economic well-being of Vietnam, especially in terms of technology. He hoped ASEAN member countries will invest more in Vietnam. Malaysia is currently the 10th largest investor in Vietnam with a total investment of 400 million U.S. dollars. When asked for Vietnam's opinion on the dispute over the Spratly Islands, Mr. Do Muoi said that all parties concerned in the claim to the islands should resort to action that will not raise tension in the region. He reiterated that the issue should be resolved either through bilateral or multilateral meetings.
