BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on insurgent activities in Peru compiled from Peruvian media monitored through 24 April: On 14 April the Counterterrorism Directorate, Dincote, presented to the press nine members of the People's Aid faction of the Shining Path, SL. They were involved in many attacks perpetrated between March 1993 and March 1994. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 15 April Political Section p A2) Railway Police Unit personnel deactivated approximately eight metric tonnes of dynamite that were planted along several segments of the Central railway linking Lima with La Oroya. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 15 Apr 94 p 15) A total of 10 SL "terrorists" died and three Army soldiers were wounded in two separate clashes in the township of Moyuna, Leoncio Prado Province, on 8 April, and in the township of El Salvador, Huallaga Front on 12 April. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 16 Apr 94 p A12) Dincote personnel arrested 19 peddlers in markets located in La Victoria and El Agustino Districts. They are members of a SL commando that perpetrated selective murders, sabotage, and other crimes. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 15 Apr 94 p 15) On 15 April, two SL "terrorists" shot dead champion boxer Carlos Lavalle Gonzales who lived in La Raucana neighborhood in Lima. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 16 Apr 94 p A12) A SL group on 11 April killed three residents of the township of Sonomoro, 51 km from Satipo Province, Huancayo Department. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 17 Apr 94 p A16) On 15 April, some 30 SL "terrorists" killed San Juan de Lucanas Mayor Teobaldino Huarcaya Berrocal and peasant patrol member Jose Quispe, and burned the city hall and an educational building in the township of San Juan de Lucana, Ayacucho Department. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 18 Apr 94 p A15) On 16 April National Police personnel arrested three SL "subversives" in La Victoria District, in Lima. They carried a large quantity of subversive propaganda and red flags bearing the hammer and sickle. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 18 Apr 94 p A16) SL "terrorists" on 16 April killed 18 people in Monterrico, Mazamari District, Satipo Province, Junin Department, because they refused to continue cooperating with them. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 19 Apr 94 p A8) A total of 20 SL "terrorists" have surrendered to Army troops to avail themselves of the repentance law since the so-called Operacion Monzon begun early in April in Huanuco Department. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 20 Apr 94 p A3) On 10 April, Dincote personnel arrested the Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement, MRTA, third-in-command in the northern part of the country. The identity of the "terrorist" and the place where he was detained have not been disclosed. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 20 Apr 94 p A12) The Cajamarca National Police Unit presented Lupita Virginia Ruiz Paredes, aka Maritza, to the press. She is believed to be one of the SL military commands in the Cajabamba, San Marcos, and Celendin troubled zones. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 21 Apr 94 p A13) General Alfredo Rodriguez, Huanuco Department Political Military commander, has charged the SL with kidnapping 54 civilians from a village near the township of Moena, in Huallaga zone, Leoncio Prado Province. He also reported that 28 other SL members surrendered to Army patrols to avail themselves of the repentance law. Daniel Espichan, special prosecutor for terrorist crimes, said there are currently 4,000 repentants. On 21 April, MRTA members killed three soldiers and nine peasants during an ambush in the Peruvian central jungle. The bodies of two of the four miners kidnapped by SL members in San Juan de Lucanas on 17 April were found in Ayacucho. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1635 GMT 23 Apr 94) Army forces have rescued approximately 100 Ashaninkas Indians from seven SL camps located in villages of the jungle province of Satipo, Junin Department. They also seized weapons, explosives, ammunition, communication equipment, and subversive propaganda. The Lima media reports that the security forces dismantled a SL column known as the "Red Sea Battalion" in Leoncio Prado Province, Huanuco Department. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2053 GMT 24 Apr 94)
