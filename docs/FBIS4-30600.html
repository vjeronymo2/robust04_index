BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 28 KYODO -- The chairman of Keidanren, the Federation of Economic Organizations, welcomed Tuesday [28 June] a deregulation package released by the government the same day. Shoichiro Toyoda released a statement saying the contents of the plan compiled by the administrative reform promotion headquarters warrants acceptance both domestically and abroad. But he said the important thing is to put the plan into practice, calling for the government to take necessary measures as soon as possible, such as modification of the law. Toyoda also suggested establishing an independent organization to monitor the progress of deregulation. The government plan contains 279 deregulatory items under real estate, financial services, information and telecommunications areas.
