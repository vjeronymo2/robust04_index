Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of fileworthy reports on antinarcotics operations in countries monitored by FBIS London Bureau from 25 February to 2 March. Source, date and time of broadcast are given in parentheses at the end of each report.

Germany
A 53-year-old Polish citizen in Frankfurt, Germany, was sentenced to five years imprisonment for attempting to smuggle 4.7 kg of heroin. Miroslaw W., who was a chauffeur at the Zairean Embassy in Poland for 14 years, was taken into custody in July 1993 on the Polish-German border. The 4.7 kg of heroin was found in a suitcase supposedly covered by diplomatic immunity, and which was to go to Brussels. The drugs are estimated to have a street value of roughly 3 million German marks. Miroslaw W. testified that he was totally unaware of the contents of the suitcase, and was transporting it on orders from Boekwa Lokolo Okombe, councillor at the Zairean Embassy in Warsaw. The German prosecutors are currently preparing an international arrest warrant for the Zairean diplomat. (Warsaw PAP in English 1023 GMT 25 Feb 94)
Iran
A total of 350 kgms of narcotics were seized from five drug gangs west of Tehran over the past 45 days by the law enforcement forces. Moreover, 123 smugglers and 126 addicts were also arrested within the same period. (Tehran IRNA in English 1608 GMT 27 Feb 94)
