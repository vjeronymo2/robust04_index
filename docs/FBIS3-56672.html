Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [by ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow, 17 Jan -- Communist Party representative Viktor Ilyukhin, who at one time tried to get Mikhail Gorbachev prosecuted for "betrayal of the motherland," was elected chairman of the State Duma Security Committee today. Another key parliamentary post, that of chairman of the Defense Committee, went to Sergey Yushenkov of Russia's Choice. There will be 23 committees in the Duma in all. Vladimir Isakov from the Agrarian Party has been elected chairman of the Committee on Legislative and Judicial Reform. In the last parliament he was one of the main opponents of presidential policy. The Committee on the Budget, Taxation, Banking and Finance is headed by Mikhail Zadornov from the Yabloko bloc. Sergey Glazyev, who was recently forced to quit the post of minister for foreign economic relations, is chairman of the Committee for Economic Policy. He now represents the Democratic Party. Vladimir Lukin, former Russian ambassador to the United States, from the Yabloko bloc, has become chairman of the Committee for International Affairs.
