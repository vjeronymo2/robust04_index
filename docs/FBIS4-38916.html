BFN [Text] Tallinn, June 02, BNS -- President Lennart Meri today appointed Urmas Arumae as the new justice minister, the president's office told BNS. Arumae's candidacy was put forward earlier this morning by Prime Minister Mart Laar. Arumae, 37, is a graduate of the Faculty of Law of Tartu University. He later graduated from the Estonian Higher School of Commerce and obtained his master's degree in international business management at the University of San Francisco. After a stint as a vice president of Silves Enterprises Ltd., he established his own legal aid company, Concordia. Arumae is also president of the Estonian Lawyers Union.
