Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Mourning ceremonies devoted to the 50th anniversary of the coercive deportation of the Chechen and Ingush peoples by the Stalin regime to Siberia and Central Asia were held on Wednesday [23 February] in Chechnya. A memorial devoted to deportation victims was opened in Groznyy, the Chechen capital. About 5000 stones taken from cemeteries and used for road construction and other purposes after the Chechens' deportation in 1994 are in the center of this memorial. Chechen President Dzhokhar Dudayev addressed several thousand Groznyy residents and citizens from other regions of the republic after a mourning and public prayer ceremony. He said that more than 600,000 Chechens and Ingushes perished during the deportation period. A military parade took place in Svoboda Square in front of the presidential palace in the center of Groznyy. Dudayev commanded the parade. The coffin with the body of Georgia's late ex-president Zviad Gamsakhurdia was carried on an artillery gun-carriage via the square before the parade. The coffin was placed in the marble hall of the House of Government. Gamsakhurdia's burial ceremony is scheduled for 15:00 local time on Thursday, February 24.
