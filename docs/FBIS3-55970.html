Language: Armenian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Armenian Vice President Gagik Arutyunyan left for Zangezur [southern Armenia] where an extraordinary session of the state commission coordinating Armenian-Iranian ties is being held. Armenian presidential press service reported that Gagik Arutyunyan, accompanied by the rayon leadership, held a meeting with the directors of the enterprises in Megrinskiy Rayon. They examined matters pertaining to the development of the Armenian-Iranian economic ties. After that, a session of the state commission coordinating Armenian-Iranian ties was held in Megri. Morteza Bank, head of the Eastern Europe and CIS Department at the Iranian Foreign Ministry, and Ahmad Sobhani, the Iranian charge d'affaires in Armenia, took part on the Iranian side. The meeting discussed matters relating to the construction of the bridge over Araks River, and the regulation of communications, border trade, and customs procedures, including the coordination of the work of the customs departments of the two countries. Morteza Bank stressed in particular that Iran has considerable experience in organizing border trade and it could substantially contribute to the organization of similar trade with Armenia. He was also positively disposed to Armenian side's proposal to open an Armenian mission in Tabriz. The sides agreed to organize such meetings frequently. Vice President Gagik Arutyunyan stressed that this would speed up the work to develop the bilateral ties. Gagik Arutyunyan also held a separate meeting with Morteza Bank and Ahmad Sobhani.
