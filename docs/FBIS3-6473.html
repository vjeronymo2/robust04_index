Their continued play with fire, which is incompatible with dialogue, cannot be construed otherwise than a deliberate act to obstruct the working-level contact at Panmunjom and frustrate the exchange of special envoys. This is proved by the South Korean rulers' wrong dialogue stance toward the working-level contact. By making the exchange abortive, they intend to put a spoke in the wheel of DPRK-U.S. talks, hinder the settlement of the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula and slay fellow countrymen with the help of foreign sword. The double-faced acts of the "civilian"-veiled warmaniacs, who sharpen the sword against fellow countrymen behind the scene of dialogue for the exchange of special envoys, are bound to face criticism at home and abroad. The nation will never pardon the "civilian"-veiled warmaniacs who stick to confrontation at a time when the entire fellow countrymen are all out to achieve reunification in the 1990s through the unity of the nation.
