BFN [Text] Baghdad, 8 Jun (INA)--Sa'di Jalshar [name as received], Turkey's charge d'affaires in Baghdad, has said that mid-June will see the implementation of the Turkish Government's decision to resume trade activities with Iraq through al-Khabur border post. AL-JUMHURIYAH today reports that Jalshar's assertions were made during a meeting with National Assembly Speaker Sa'di Mahdi Salih. He also said that Turkey will implement its decision after completing all the necessary measures by the Turkish Finance Ministry and Turkish customs authorities. The Turkish charge d'affaires added that officials in his country are holding intensive contacts with the UN Security Council permanent members to arrive at a common understanding to implement the two agreements which Ankara and Baghdad reached on the reopening of the Iraqi-Turkish oil pipeline. He noted that the foreign relations committee in his country's parliament asked Osdom Sanirk [name as received], the Turkish Foreign Ministry under secretary, to provide detailed information on his recent visit to Baghdad and the successful talks he held with Iraqi officials in (?April).
