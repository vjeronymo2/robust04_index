940201 FT 01 FEB 94 / Poll endorses Kyrgyzstan reforms Kyrgyzstan President Askar Akayev won a resounding referendum victory endorsing his plan for faster capitalist reforms, Reuter reports from Bishkek. Mr Akayev, a former mathematics professor, gained 96.3 per cent of Sunday's vote, giving him the mandate he sought to push through reforms and override objections from an ex-communist parliament. The official turnout was 95.3 per cent of the 2.2m electorate.
