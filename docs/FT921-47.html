920331 FT 31 MAR 92 / Observer: Energy release Malcolm Edwards, recently deposed commercial director of British Coal, today beats his former employer into the private sector by launching himself as Edwards Energy. As a consultant to suppliers and purchasers of fuel, he will offer advice on strategy and contract negotiations, making use of 'the latest expert knowledge of production and use of energy'. Could that mean competing with the industry which employed him for the past 35 years? 'I wouldn't undermine British Coal's position in the market place,' he says diplomatically. For a man forced out of his Pounds 90,000-a-year job 12 months before his contract expired, Edwards sounds surprisingly cheerful. 'I feel about 20 years younger having got that lot off my back.'
