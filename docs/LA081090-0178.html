August 10, 1990, Friday, P.M. Final

SHORT TAKES;

ACTOR FINED FOR DRUNK DRIVING

Film star Gerard Depardieu was fined $1,800, and his license was suspended for six months for driving while drunk. He was arrested after a car accident that injured two people.

Depardieu, who won best actor award at the Cannes Film Festival this year for his title role in "Cyrano de Bergerac," was also sentenced this week to a two-month suspended prison sentence.

Depardieu, 41, did not attend the court proceeding in Riom. His lawyer, Serge Teillot, said the actor "fully acknowledged his responsibility" but felt the proceedings would be more calm if he did not attend.

Prosecutors said Depardieu's blood contained twice the legal alcohol limit of 0.08% after the July 20 accident, which occurred as the actor drove to a hotel after shooting scenes for a new movie.

His car collided with another on a country road near Puy-de-Dome, slightly injuring the wife and daughter of the other driver.

Depardieu told police he was trying to answer a call on his car telephone and was blinded by the setting sun when the crash occurred.
