Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Spanish Foreign Minister Javier Solana said in Seville this morning that after the massacre of 58 Palestinians on 25 February in a mosque in Hebron, the Spanish Government is shocked and Felipe Gonzalez has sent a telegram to the Palestinian leader Yasir 'Arafat to express to him the sorrow of the Spanish Government. The minister stressed that right now it is essential for the Washington agreements on the autonomy of occupied territorities to come into operation as soon as possible. Solana was confident that in spite of this catastrophe the peace process will not be slowed down but, on the contrary, speeded up. Javier Solana recalled that Spain is committed to the Middle East peace process. He added that there is no decision in the direction of UN troops going to the occupied territories and he mentioned the meeting which the UN Security Council will be holding in New York this afternoon to produce a resolution or declaration.
