Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow March 16 TASS -- The State Duma has endorsed a procedure for future parliamentary hearings. At its plenary meeting on Wednesday [16 March], the Duma directed appropriate committees to arrange for hearings on the Partnership-for-Peace documents, which had been adopted at a session of the North Atlantic Council in January 1994, on matters concerning the implementation of the START I Treaty and its realisation problems, an analysis of the status and problems of the state border of the Russian Federation, and on the Russia's participation in international agreements on the elimination of chemical weapons. The Duma will also hold hearings to consider "the results of the privatisation of state-run and municipal enterprises of the Russian Federation in 1992-1993" and a relevant state programme, on structural, science and technology policy, and a concept of the Russian Federation's transition to a steady development model.
