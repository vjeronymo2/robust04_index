910514 FT 14 MAY 91 / Surveillance equipment to be installed at main railway stations SURVEILLANCE equipment is to be installed at main railway stations as soon as possible to guard against terrorist bomb attacks, Mr Desmond O'Brien, chief constable of British Transport Police, said yesterday. Mr O'Brien was speaking at a London news conference at which the force's 1990-91 annual report was published. It showed a 3.6 per cent reduction in crime on Britain's railways. Violent crime on BR and the London Underground dropped 31.6 per cent compared with 1989; sex attacks were down by 15.1 per cent.
