941207 FT 07 DEC 94 / Democrats accuse Clinton Democratic party moderates blamed President Bill Clinton yesterday for last month's mid-term election calamity, saying he campaigned as a centrist but had failed to govern as such. The mood was sombre at the 10th anniversary conference of the Democratic Leadership Council, a group once chaired by Mr Clinton which is devoted to advancing a centrist political agenda. 'Amid the avalanche of public disgust that swept our party from power in Congress, Democrats once again face a crisis of values, a crisis of principles, a crisis for the future of our country,' said Mr Dave McCurdy, council chairman and an outgoing member of the House who lost his race for a Senate seat in Oklahoma. California Senator Dianne Feinstein, narrowly re-elected last month, singled out what she termed Mr Clinton's over-ambitious effort to reform the healthcare system as a key factor in the Democrats' election defeat.
