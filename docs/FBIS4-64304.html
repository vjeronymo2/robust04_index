BFN [Text] Ankara, April 30 (AA) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin on Saturday [30 April] said that Turkmen natural gas will soon be available in Turkey. Speaking to reporters upon his arrival at Ankara's Esenboga Airport after concluding official talks in Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan, Cetin said his contacts there have been useful. He said during his meeting with Turkmenistan President Saparmurad Niyazov, the importance and necessity for Turkmen natural gas transfer to Europe via Turkey was once again confirmed, expressing hope that its use in Turkey would start in 1996. Cetin said Turkey will continue to provide support for these countries with whom it shares historical, cultural, religious and language ties, adding the necessary political will for strengthening bilateral relations and cooperation is present. He said foreign trade volume with Turkmenistan is of 161 million dollars, while it reached 145 million with Uzbekistan, and during his visit agreement was reached for increasing it even further, adding there is necessary potential for this. Cetin said the Turkish private sector is working in these countries in the fields of infrastructure, construction, industry and service, expressing hope that greater investments will be made in these countries.
