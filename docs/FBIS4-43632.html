BFN [Text] Government Spokesman Evangelos Venizelos said the Greek Government does not rule out--at least theoretically--a meeting with the Turkish side, but clarified that what is important are the issues of discussion and the outcome, not the meeting itself. He stressed that the so-called Greek-Turkish disputes cannot be discussed, because the only thing separating the two countries is the continental shelf. Venizelos concluded: All the other differences are either violations or unilateral pursuits.
