941223 FT 23 DEC 94 / Property: Rate of return slow in November The rate of total return slowed across the market in November, with an all-property return of 0.3 per cent for the month, according to research group Investment Property Databank. This represents a slide of 0.1 percentage points from October. Rental values have shown a negligible rise over the month, with the all-property rental value index standing at 136.52 in November. Capital values continue to slide, falling 0.3 per cent in November. For the calender year to date the total return measured 14.9 per cent. If the most recent capital value trends continue into December, it will mean a 12-month return to the end of the year of just over 15 per cent. This compares with a return of 16.4 per cent for the year to December 1993. Long-term capital growth has fallen from 12.6 per cent in the year to October to 10.0 per cent for the 12 months to November. The rate of rental value decline continues to slow, recording -1.8 per cent for the same period. Retail property continues to lead the pack with a total return of 0.5 per cent. Offices move into second place with a return of 0.2 per cent followed by the industrial sector with a return of 0.1 per cent. The long-term sector rankings are unchanged with retails recording a total return of 21.2 per cent for the year to November, followed by industrials (19.5 per cent) and offices (15.7 per cent).
