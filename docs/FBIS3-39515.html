Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Interview with Vuk Draskovic, leader of the Serbian Renewal Movement by Paolo Borelli in Rome; date not given--recorded; Draskovic and Borelli speak in English with voice-over in Italian paraphrasing their questions and answers] [Text] Vuk Draskovic, the leader of the opposition in Belgrade and therefore the main enemy, at least ideologically, of Serb President Milosevic, is paying a visit to Rome. He will have meetings with Foreign Minister Andreatta and with Chamber of Deputies Speaker Giorgio Napolitano and Senate Speaker Giovanni Spadolini. Draskovic will outline his peace plan for the former Yugoslavia during his talks with them. Paolo Borelli reports: [Borelli] You have opposed the war and Milosevic. Milosevic sent you to prison last year. Why is it that this same Milosevic is talking to your movement and saying he is ready for peace? [Draskovic] Milosevic, like Tudjman, is being forced by the facts to make peace. Yes, it is strange that those who started the war have now become the champions of peace. But this is not the first time this has happened. In Israel, Menachem Begin, who had a terrorist, anti-Palestinian guerrilla background, received a Nobel prize for peace with Egypt. [Borelli] What will happen when the ultimatum expires on Sunday night [20 February]? [Draskovic] The Bosnian Serbs must give up the siege of Sarajevo completely. I think they will do so. This means that first they have to place all their arms under the control of the UN forces in the city and its surrounding area. The second step, according to my party's plan, calls for Croats and Muslims to place their weapons under UN control also. The third step will be the disarming of the three sides and the handing over of war criminals for trial. The process of disarmament should be carried out by the soldiers of the four powers: the United States and British should disarm the Croats and Muslims; Russia and the French should disarm the Serbs.
