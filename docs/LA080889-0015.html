August 8, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

VOTING

It is not only an honor but a sacred trust to have the power to make daily decisions that affect the lives of every American. Each time we go to the polls we vote for our chosen representatives as "our voice" in our government, and so endow those individuals with the awesome authority to speak for us. But since we don't receive daily memos from them as to what's transpiring in the meeting rooms of our nation's Capitol, nor do we have box seats at Senate debates, we rarely know what legislation has been proposed, enacted or repealed until it is a fait accompli. So, it is with a deep sense of shock and outrage to hear of a proposed bill (S-874), which calls for "instant voter registration" -- a plan to automatically register people to vote when they renew their driver's license.

According to the sponsor of this "motor-voter" bill, Sen. Wendell Ford (D-Ky.), "It will remove obstacles from voter registration and encourage more people to vote."

Surely at a time when fake IDs are as easily purchased as a box of Cracker Jack by illegal aliens and others who have ulterior motives for possessing them, the intelligence and motives of the sponsor and endorsers of this legislation must be seriously questioned.

To allow such irresponsible and dangerous legislation to go unchallenged is to open the door to "voting by phone" or even total abolishment of the need to register.

RUTH COFFEY

Long Beach
