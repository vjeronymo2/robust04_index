Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Port-au-Prince, 7 Mar (EFE) -- Haitian military police today arrested the leader of a popular organization that supports exiled President Jean-Bertrand Aristide. Two policemen arrested Johnson Aristide of the grass-roots organization "Sun of Justice for the Liberation of the Haitian People" [Sol de la Justicia para la Liberacion del Pueblo Haitiano] while he attempted to deliver a press note to Radio Plus, a radio station in downtown Port-au- Prince, the Haitian press agency reported. Aristide, who is not related to the president, was struck and forced into an automobile used by the police, the source informed. Soldiers arrested several of President Aristide's supporters this weekend in the Port-au-Prince metropolitan area. Uniformed members of the military arrested Engineer Jacques Dorcean in the Thomassin area in eastern Port-au-Prince. Dorcean is a well-known supporter of the Lavalas Movement, which backed the exiled president in the December 1990 elections. According to press reports, 20-year-old Val Maxime [not further identified] was also arrested and seriously beaten. On Saturday, the home of Jean-Camille Calvin in Lillavois, an alleged Aristide follower, was strafed with machine-gun fire. Lillavois lives in northern Port-au-Prince. So far the military has not released any information regarding the reason for the arrests.
