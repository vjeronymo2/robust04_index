November 25, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

WEEKEND TV;

'COLUMBO' RETURNS AND SAMMY DAVIS LEADS SUNDAY'S 'HOLLYWOOD CHRISTMAS PARADE'

Peter Falk, Sammy Davis Jr., Walter Cronkite and winners and losers from the 1988 Summer Olympics in Seoul strut their stuff on the small screen as the long holiday weekend winds down.

Falk returns, trench coat and all, tonight at 9 on ABC (Channels 7, 3, 10 and 42) as America's favorite rumpled detective in the season premiere of "Columbo." Falk will reappear periodically this season in rotation with Burt Reynolds, Jaclyn Smith and Telly Savalas in the "ABC Saturday Mystery."

On Sunday, local viewers can avoid the traffic snarls by staying home and watching the 58th annual "Hollywood Christmas Parade" at 6 p.m. on Channel 5. Sammy Davis Jr. is this year's grand marshal. Bob Eubanks and Leeza Gibbons will host the live telecast.

Sunday night at 9, CBS (Channels 2 and 8) looks back at "Fifty Years of Television," with clips and highlights from the small screen's first half century. Walter Cronkite, Carl Reiner, Jane Seymour and Kermit the Frog serve as emcees for this stroll down memory lane.

Also Sunday at 9 p.m., the Disney Channel offers a series of compelling memories from the last Summer Games in Bud Greenspan's documentary, "Seoul '88: 16 Days of Glory."

Greenspan's deft ability to capture the moving human stories behind the magnificent accomplishments of such athletes as Christa Rothenburger, Vladimir Salnikov, Louise Ritter and Calvin Smith put to shame the commercial-saturated and superficial live network Olympic coverage to which Americans are accustomed.
