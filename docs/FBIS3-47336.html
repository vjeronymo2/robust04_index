Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Right-wing members of the ruling Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] and members of Sakigake [Harbinger] inaugurated a study group on the evening of 10 January, to press for a realignment of political parties; the group held a meeting at a Tokyo hotel that evening. A total of 13 members -- 9 members including former Dietmen from the SDPJ and 4 members from Sakigake -- attended the meeting. Yoshito Sengok, who is a former member of the House of Representatives and affiliated with the SDPJ, and Kenichiro Sato, chief of the Election Bureau of Sakigake, will play the role of contact points. The study group will be called about twice a month in order for members to exchange views on ways to pass political reform bills in the Diet and prepare campaign strategies for the combined electoral system of single-seat constituency and proportional representation under debate in the Diet.
