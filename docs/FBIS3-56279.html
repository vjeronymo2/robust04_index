Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow January 14 TASS -- Russia agreed to "actively participate" in the "Partnership for Peace" plan and the Russian and US Presidents agreed that Europe should no longer be divided and that a new security order is to be created on the continent. The "Partneship for Peace" plan is an importnat element of the new security order and Russia is going to actively participate in it, according to the Moscow Declaration signed by Boris Yeltsin and Bill Clinton on Friday. "The Partnership for Peace... is an importnat element of emerging new European security architecture. President Yeltsin informed President Clinton of Russia's intention to participate actively in the Partnership for Peace and to conclude substantive agreements opening way [as received] for broad and intensive cooperation between NATO and Russia as a partner", the declaration said. "Proceeding from the conviction that new divisions of Europe must be avoided, the Presidents... agreed upon the need to create a new European security order that is inclusive, non-discriminatory and focused on practical political and security cooperation", according to the document. Clinton welcomed the prospect of Russia's active participation in the "Partnership for Peace", the declaration said.
