Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, March 11 (SNARK)--Armenia could get 3 and even 4 times more credits from the World Bank, but for the Karabakh military conflict, Vahram Nersesiants, the representative of the bank in Armenia, said today. At the press-conference in the Yerevan he said that the last year the World Bank gave Armenia 12 million US dollars, and this February, under very profitable conditions, gave a credit of 28 million US dollars for liquidation consequences of the earthquake in 1988. He said that the World Bank probably will give 40 million US dollars for agriculture. It is also expected the World Bank will give credits for development of the energetics and fulfillment of the social programs.
