Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, March 2 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The Mongolian press is covering widely the official visit of the President P. Ochirbat to the countries of Asia. The newspaper "ARDYN ERH" informs that the Mongolian president with his spouse has paid a visit to His Majesty King of Thailand Phumiphon Adunyadet and his spouse at their residence. P. Ochirbat and those accompanied him visited the ancient capital of Thailand Ayuttaya and the largest centre of tourism city of Pattaya. Speaking there at the press conference the President P. Ochirbat noted with satisfaction the unanimity of stands of Mongolia and Thailand on both the fundamental issues of bilateral relations and international ones. P. Ochirbat stressed the significance of signed during his visit to Thailand agreements between the two countries concerning the airline communications and non-visa exchange of persons with diplomatic and official passports. In this speech the Mongolian president emphasized the interest of Mongolia in participating actively in the regional cooperation of countries of Asia. The newspaper publishes the full text of the joint communique on the visit of the President of Mongolia P. Ochirbat to Thailand. The newspaper informs about the working trip by the Prime Minister of Mongolia P. Jasray to the Hobd Aymag where he is getting acquainted with the wintering of cattle, life and working conditions of herdsmen. The newspaper underlined that P. Jasray has displayed much interest to the work of the local research institute relating to the growing of oilproducing crops. According to the newspaper, the constitutional court has considered the claims of three citizens on the breach of the provisions of the Constitution committed by members of the parliament P. Elbegdorj, D. Ganbold and S. Zorig, in reference to the execution by them their obligations of a party members. The constitutional court noticed nothing in this to be regarded as the violation of the Constitution. The newspaper informs about the outcome of the 17 winter Olympic games held in Lillehammer and carries the standings of countries whose sportsmen succeeded to win medals. The central organ of the Mongolian National Democratic Party (MNDP) "ERH CHOLOO" puts the statement of the General Council of the party on its pages. The MNDP considers that the accusations of corruption hurled at the leadership of the parliament and the Prime Minister P. Jasray instead of being considered properly are just being used in the interests of politicizing the current situation in the country. According to the newspaper, the General Council of the MNDP acknowledged at its last sitting the impossibility for their party to be being represented in the parliament. The committee on ensuring order of the MNDP has taken a decree on the necessity to improve the activities of the General Council and other central bodies of the party reproving strictly the leader of the party D. Ganbold and the General Secretary E. Bat-Uul. Touching upon the visit paid by the Chairman of the ruling Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party B. Dash-Yondon to Japan the newspaper is wondering whether the Japanese side decided to give political and moral support to this party.
