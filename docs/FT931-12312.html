930129 FT 29 JAN 93 / International Company News: National Bank acquires trust NATIONAL Bank of Canada is buying Gentrust, the trust company and money management operations of General Trustco, a Quebec financial services group, for CDollars 125m (Dollars 97.6m). The deal gives National Bank a quick entry into the trust business, including a network of 32 branches. Gentrust, has corporate assets of CDollars 3.5bn and manages CDollars 11bn in pension and other investments. General Trustco of Canada is majority-held by Industrial Life Groupof Quebec City. Canadian chartered banks, under federal deregulation, can now own trust companies. The Big Six banks are all moving into the trust business by acquisition or by setting up new subsidiaries. NBC is the sixth-largest chartered bank with CDollars 40bn assets. NBC also plans to sell its leasing business with nearly CDollars 1bn assets, to GE Capital, North America's biggest leasing group, and will show a CDollars 25m gain on the deal.
