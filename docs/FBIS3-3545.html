Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Haikou, March 15 (XINHUA) -- Chinese veteran leader Yang Shangkun has called on Hainan Province to seek opportunities to develop itself by making full use of its advantages. During his recent visit to the island, he urged local officials to build well the country's largest special economic zone. Hainan is also China's second largest island after Taiwan. From March 9 to 15, the 87-year-old Yang, accompanied by Hainan provincial leaders, inspected an international airport now still under construction in Sanya, a tourist attraction, and a farm for returned overseas Chinese at Xinglong. At Haikou, the provincial capital, Yang visited two Taiwan funded companies and hoped Taiwan investment to increase on the island. This was Yang's fourth inspection of Hainan. He said every time he was surprised by the island's great changes in urban outlook and economic and cultural development. He encouraged Hainan's residents to concentrate on building up infrastructural facilities, for transportation in particular, and make proper arrangements of its urban development and industrial locations so as to develop the provincial economy and social undertakings in a coordinated way.
