April 28, 1989, Friday, Valley Edition

COLLEGE BASEBALL;

CANYONS CLINCHES SECOND IN WSC WITH 8-3 VICTORY

Adam Grant delivered twice in the clutch as visiting College of the Canyons defeated Bakersfield, 8-3, Thursday to clinch second place in the Western State Conference.

Grant delivered a two-out, two-run single to highlight the Cougars' four-run fourth inning.

In the eighth inning, Grant added a two-run double -- again with two out -- to extend Canyons' lead to 8-3.

Grant finished three for five with four runs batted in.

Teammate Chris Joy, who was two for five, launched a two-run home run in the seventh inning.

Jeff Frith-Smith (7-4) pitched a complete game and struck out six.

Ventura defeated Cuesta, 15-1, to clinch the conference championship.

In another Western State Conference game:

Santa Barbara 3, Pierce 1 -- Host Santa Barbara scored two unearned runs in the eighth inning on a fielding error, two hits and a passed ball to break a 1-1 tie.

Pete Dragaloski (4-6) threw a seven-hitter, struck out nine and walked three for Pierce (15-18, 8-10 in conference play).

Teammate Scott Cadenhead was two for four and hit his second home run of the season in the second inning.

Ron Kissick threw a six-hitter, struck out three and walked four for Santa Barbara (6-12 in conference play).
