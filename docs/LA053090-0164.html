May 30, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

PEOPLE'S PHARMACY: EYEDROPS FOR GLAUCOMA LINKED TO THINNING OF HAIR

Question: This may sound ludicrous but I wonder if the eyedrops I use for glaucoma could make my hair fall out.

Even after my hair started to turn gray it was thick and beautiful. Now I am getting worried as it continues to get thinner. I first noticed it several months after starting on glaucoma medicine.

Answer: At first we doubted that glaucoma eye medicine could cause hair loss but after searching the medical literature we discovered that beta blocker drops may be associated with hair thinning. Apparently, enough medicine is absorbed into the bloodstream to produce side effects similar to those seen with oral beta blockers used for heart disease or high blood pressure. Depression, sexual side effects and fatigue may also occur from such eyedrops.

You didn't mention which drug your doctor prescribed but medications such as Betagan Liquifilm (levobunolol), Betoptic (betaxolol), and Timoptic (timolol) have been reported to affect hair on scalp, eyebrows and eyelashes.

Your ophthalmologist may be able to teach you how to apply the drops so that absorption is minimized or he may be able to determine if a different kind of medicine may be appropriate.

Q: I'm under a lot of stress right now. My teen-age daughter has been acting up, and my husband and I are fighting about how to handle her. It feels like my nerves are all on edge, and I'm having trouble sleeping. My sister says I should get a prescription for Valium, as it helped her through a divorce two years ago. I hate to take drugs, though. I have read that valerian root is a natural alternative. Is it safe?

A: Valerian is an herbal remedy that has been used as a sedative for hundreds of years. Scientific tests have shown that people taking valerian root extract get to sleep more quickly. Our sources indicate that this herbal remedy is relatively safe. No addiction has been reported. It should not be combined with alcohol or other drugs.
