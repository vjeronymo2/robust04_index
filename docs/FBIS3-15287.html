Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Flight Control Centre (Moscow Region) March 4 TASS -- Russian cosmonauts aboard the Mir space station continued geophysical, astrophysical and technological experiments as well as medical research during another workweek ending on Friday. They carried out on Thursday a test starting assigned for the station's orientation. The unit was assembled atop a 14-meter metal boom which was secured on the kvant module by the crew of the 12th main expedition. Doctor Valeriy Polyakov will undergo a medical checkup today under the supervision of the flight engineer. Viktor Afanasyev and Yuriy Usachev will check up equipment assigned for distance control of the Soyuz TM spaceship. Medical checkups conducted over the past few days show that the cosmonauts are healthy and feel well.
