BFN [Text] Nanjing, May 20 (XINHUA) -- Cities in the valley of the Chang Jiang river fully support the construction of the Three Gorges water conservancy project. The project, to be built on the middle reaches of this China's longest river, near Yichang city, Hubei Province, will have a generating capacity of 17.68 million kw upon completion. Shanghai, on the Chang Jiang estuary, took the lead in encouraging its enterprises and organizations to set up economic entities around the construction site to offer more jobs for local people. It also allocated special funds to help Yichang train technicians. Wuhan, capital of Hubei Province, has completed 60 projects with a total investment of 83.47 million yuan in co-operation with Yichang city. Located on the upper reaches of the Yangtze, Chongqing city in Sichuan Province has set up production bases for building materials and farm and sideline products specially geared to the construction of the Three Gorges project.
