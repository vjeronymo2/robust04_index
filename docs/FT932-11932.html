930430 FT 30 APR 93 / Observer: Battle standards Best-sellers though they're unlikely to be, the full texts of the latest British accounting standards are a hot topic for publishers struggling, on grounds of both price and quality, to take control of the documents issued by the Accounting Standards Board. One focus of the argument is the emphasis in the accountancy profession's ethical rules on the need not only to be, but to be seen to be, independent. In which case, some ask, how much longer can the publishing arm of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales continue printing the standards produced by the supposedly independent board, and designed to cover all of the UK?
