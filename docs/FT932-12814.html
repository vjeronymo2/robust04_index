930427 FT 27 APR 93 / Travel industry attacks government THE GOVERNMENT is likely to face legal action over its failure to provide package holiday travellers with the protection required by a European Community directive, Mr Mike Grindrod, president of the Association of British Travel Agents, said yesterday. He told an association convention in Majorca that package tour regulations which came into force at the beginning of the year were an 'un-satisfactory mishmash'. The directive requires all companies to have bonding or insurance arrangements to ensure customers can be re-patriated or receive their money back in the event of corporate collapse. Mr Grindrod criticised the government for allowing companies to make their own arrangements outside recognised trade organisations. If a failed travel operator had insufficient insurance, travellers would merely have the consolation of knowing that the company faced a fine. They would have no means of getting their money back. Mr Grindrod said: 'Lewis Carroll at his most imaginative could never have invented anything as topsy-turvy and through a cracked looking glass as Her Majesty's government has concocted. 'Eventually I am convinced that a future British government, either voluntarily as a matter of policy or having been hauled kicking and screaming before the European court, will amend the regulations to provide a statutory guarantee.'
