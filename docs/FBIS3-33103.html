Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Jose Mercedes Feliz] [Text] During the first nine months of 1993, government revenue increased 14.2 percent over the same period of 1992, based on the methodology used by the International Monetary Fund (IMF). According to figures from the Central Bank, fiscal receipts during the period from January to September of 1993 totaled 14,493.9 million Dominican pesos. In contrast, during the same time frame last year, those same receipts totaled 12,687 million. The 1993 Budget passed by the National Congress for 1993 totals 22,717.5 million pesos. At the same time, general spending increased during the first nine months of this year by 39.3 percent, rising from 9,737.8 million during the same period of 1992 to 13,573.6 million in 1993. In 1991, revenue totaled some 13,409.9 million, while general spending totaled 10,340.7 million. At the same time, collections totaled 17,571.8 million in 1992 and general spending 14,059.2 million. It was observed that from January to September of 1993, regular savings totaled 7,943.1 million, an increase of only .7 percent over the same period of 1992, when the figure was 7,888.3 million. Regular revenue from January to September of this year totaled 14,256.9 million pesos, an increase of 13.8 percent over the same period of last year, when the total was 12,527.4 million. Tax revenue rose by 16.6 percent from January to September of this year, rising from 11,358.7 million during the same period of 1992 to 13,241.6 million in 1993. During the first nine months of this year, income taxes totaled 2,493.3 million pesos, an increase of 14 percent over 1992, when they totaled 2,187.9 million. Likewise, property taxes from January to September of 1993 totaled 85.5 million, representing an increase of 26.1 percent over last year, when the total was 67.8 million. Taxes on goods and services amounted to 5,639.2 million during the recent period, an increase of 25.2 percent over the same period of last year, when the total was 4,505.6 million. Taxes on foreign trade registered an increase of 8.9 percent, going from 4,550.5 million pesos during the period from January to September of 1992 to 4,954.2 million in 1993. Revenue from sources other than taxes dropped 13.1 percent during the period from January to September of 1993, going from 1,168.7 million during the period of January to September of 1992 to 1,015.3 million in the more recent period, the only category recording a drop. Capital income rose by 48.5 percent during the first nine months of this year, going from 159.6 million from January to September of 1992 to 237 million in 1993. As for the total balance (before deductions) during the period from January to September of 1993, the total was 920.3 million, a drop of 68.8 percent compared with the same period in 1992, when the figure was 2,949.2 million. Regular savings totaled 7,905.3 million in 1991, 10,545.2 million in 1992, and 7,943.1 million during the first nine months of this year.
