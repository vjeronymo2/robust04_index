Language: Burmese Article Type:BFN [Text] A fire which broke out in the house of U Ohn Maw Htut at No. 610, Third Aye Street, Fourth Ward, Hlaing Township, Yangon [Rangoon] Division, at 1345 gutted over 700 houses leaving more than 5,100 people from 1,200 households homeless. The fire spread from First Aye to Ninth Aye Streets in Fourth Ward, Hlaing River Road, Khangon First and Second Streets, and Aungmingala First Street. Fire engines from around Yangon Division rushed to the scene to put out the fire. Lieutenant General Myo Nyunt, commander of Yangon military command, and responsible personnel supervised the fire fighting efforts and the fire was finally brought under control at 1530. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, secretary-1 of the State Law and Order Restoration Council [SLORC], accompanied by Lieutenant Colonel Pe Nyein, director general of the SLORC office, arrived at the scene this evening and were briefed by responsible personnel. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt gave detailed instructions to responsible personnel in order to provide temporary shelter for fire victims, to provide health care, distribute relief goods and food, open special cooperative shops and trade departments, and to take a census of students affected by the fire to make arrangements for them to attend classes as soon as possible. The secretary-1 and responsible personnel next inspected the fire ravaged scene in Hlaing Station Road and left after giving necessary instructions. Similarly, at 1653, a fire broke out in the house of U Aye Thein at No. 615, BOC corner, Station Road, Nantagon Ward, Insein Township. The fire gutted 12 houses leaving 89 persons from 12 households homeless and causing damage of about 1 million kyats. The fire victims were temporary sheltered at Saywingaba Arlein Ngasint pagoda compound. Authorities concerned have taken action against the owners of the two houses where the fires started.
