Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Engineer Franck Pierre of the Capois Lamort Organization pointed out in an interview with Signal FM that the fuel must be distributed throughout the entire Haitian territory in order to solve the fuel shortage problem. The Capois Lamort Organization threatens -- if the international community fails to comply with this request -- to repeat the events of 11 October, the date on which USS Harlan County departed the Port-au-Prince harbor because of the presence of armed demonstrators. [Begin Pierre recording, in Creole] We say to Ambassador General [title as heard] Lawrence Pezzullo that the fuel must be entrusted to the distributing companies that are supposed to deliver it to all gas stations so that all sons of the land can get the fuel they wish, because the embargo is a general embargo that hits all Haitians and all social strata in the country. We in the Capois Lamort Organization are [words indistinct] and clear about Haiti's crisis situation: The fuel must be widely distributed through Shell, Texaco, and Esso so that it is available in all gas stations and so that all Haitians can participate in the fuel.
