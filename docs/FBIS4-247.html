BFN [Text] United Nations Undersecretary General Ismat Kittani has stressed UN concern about Mozambique's troubled peace process during a week-long visit. During his visit, which ended today, Mr. Kittani urged more speed in the process leading to elections scheduled for October. Military demobilization is running well behind schedule. The UN Security Council deadline for the elections is October and the deadline for the peace-keeping mission November. Mr. Kittani met President Joaquim Chissano and Renamo [Mozambique National Resistance] leader Afonso Dhlakama and will report back to the Security Council on the country's progress toward peace. Renamo peace negotiator Raul Domingos said Mr. Kittani's visit had shown the UN secretary general's concern to see the peace process end well.
