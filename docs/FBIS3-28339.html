Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 7 KYODO -- Leaders of Japan's governing coalition parties failed to agree on a key tax plan Monday [7 February], postponing the decision until Tuesday morning, sources said. Meeting throughout the day, the leaders of the seven parties remained divided on how to fund the tax cuts -- an issue that has threatened to split the coalition, the sources said. They are to meet at 10:30 A.M. Tuesday to attempt once again to forge the tax plan, a central part of the government's planned economic stimulus plan, the sources said.
