920425 FT 25 APR 92 / Briefcase, Q&A: Recorded gifts IN MARCH you wrote that under certain circumstances probate could be granted but that there should be a record of any gift to the daughter of a beneficial interest in bank and building society accounts. What would constitute a record? A deed, a simple written expression of intent? Could such an expression be revoked subsequently by an express disposition in a will or implicitly by a residuary clause? The idea is to record the fact that the gift has been made; for example by a signed written memorandum stating that there has been a gift of the relevant interest. The gift may be effected either by a declaration of trust or by a formal, written assignment of which notice in writing is given to the debtor (for example the bank or building society).
