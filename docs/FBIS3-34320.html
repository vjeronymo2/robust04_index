Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, Feb 7 (AFP)--Thousands of Iranian rebels using helicopters, tanks, heavy artillery and anti-aircraft guns on Monday ended four days of war games in an Iraqi region near the border with Iran, a rebel spokesman said. Women rebels for the first time piloted helicopters that carried troops to mock battlefields during the first manoevers of the year, said Farid Soleymani, spokesman for Iran's main armed opposition group, the People's Mojahedin The Mojahedin has several military bases in Iraq, near the border with Iran. The announcement of the exercise, codenamed "Iran's hope," the first to he held since October 1993, came one day after Iraqi opposition fighters paraded in Iran, near the border with Iraq. The Mojahedin on Sunday meanwhile called on Iranians to join protests that took place last week and revolt against the government of President 'Ali Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani. Clashes occurred in the southern Iranian city of Zahedan last Tuesday. The Mojahedin claimed that protests had also taken place in other southern cities. Baghdad and Tehran, who harbour each other's opposition groups, often accuse each other of interfering in their internal affairs.
