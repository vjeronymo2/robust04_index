Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 2 Feb--On Wednesday morning, Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak left for a two-day working visit to Brussels. The head of the government will meet NATO acting Secretary General Sergio Silvio Balanzino; Belgian Prime Minister Jean-Luc Dehaene; Jacques Delors, chairman of the European Communities Commission; and Willem van Eekelen, secretary general of the Western European Union. During his visit to NATO main headquarters, the prime minister will sign documents on Poland's membership in the Partnership for Peace program, which he was authorized to do by the government on 1 February. The prime minister will also take part in a conference organized by the Center for Strategic and International Studies.
