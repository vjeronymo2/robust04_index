920428 FT 28 APR 92 / Arts: Today's Television Philip Jones predicted in last week's ITV tribute to Benny Hill that, having been treated so superciliously during his lifetime, Hill would be lionised now that he was dead. That will almost certainly prove to be right. You can check on the sexism, racism, and general political incorrectness of this hugely popular comic in a repeat of The Benny Hill Show at 8.30 on ITV. It would surely be healthier if the BBC stopped trying to pretend that 'Crimewatch' was high minded social work and admitted that it was a tabloid-telly ratings builder. The interesting question today is whether a new series of spin-off programmes, Crime Limited (8.30 BBC1) will prove to be more or less like the ambulance chasing 'infotainment' shows which are attracting such big audiences in the US. We are promised that today 'Nick Ross and Sue Cook look into the mind of a murderer with the help of an offender profiler'. All reminders of 'Silence Of The Lambs' wholly intentional presumably. Channel 4's international arts series Rear Window returns with a programme about Turkish painter Hanefi Yuter who lives and works in western Europe (9.00).
