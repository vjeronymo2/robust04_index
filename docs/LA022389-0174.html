February 23, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

CURRENCY;

CONSUMER PRICE JUMP SENDS DOLLAR TUMBLING; GOLD GAINS

The dollar fell against most major foreign currencies Wednesday as dealers weighed the implications of a government report showing that consumer prices rose sharply during January.

Gold prices gained, driven higher by the inflation report. Prices for the precious metal rose by more than $5 an ounce in London. Later, Republic National Bank of New York quoted gold at a bid of $386.40 an ounce at 4 p.m. EST, up 90 cents from Tuesday's late bid.

The Labor Department said consumer prices surged 0.6% in January, the highest monthly rate in two years. Last month's rise doubled the more modest 0.3% increases in November and December.

Mary Anne Tompkins, a foreign exchange trader at Barclays Bank PLC in New York, said the inflation report intensified market speculation that U.S. interest rates will be hiked to slow inflation.

Higher U.S. interest rates raise the value of dollar-denominated investments.

But in the meantime, many dealers seem to be focusing their attention on the strong West German economy and British economic prospects.

The dollar slid against the West German mark, falling below levels considered key thresholds at which the U.S. currency would find support. The dollar fell to 1.8380 marks in London from 1.8420 marks late Tuesday. Later in New York, the dollar fell to 1.83425 marks from 1.8440 marks on Tuesday.

The British pound came under pressure from the mark's strength. The pound fell in London to $1.7465 late Wednesday from $1.7565 late Tuesday. In later New York dealings, sterling dipped to $1.7470 from Tuesday's late $1.74725.

Earlier in Tokyo, the dollar eased to 126.62 Japanese yen from Tuesday's 127.15 yen. In London, the dollar traded at 126.65 yen and later in New York the dollar finished at 126.525 yen, down from 127.15 yen late Tuesday.

Other late dollar rates in New York, compared to Tuesday's late rates, included: 1.5658 Swiss Francs, down from 1.5690; 1.1999 Canadian dollars, up from 1.19225; 6.2560 French francs, down from 6.2795, and 1,349.75 Italian lire, down from 1,351.00.

Other rates for the dollar in Europe late Wednesday, compared to Tuesday's late rates, included: 1.8380 West German marks, down from 1.8420; 1.5667 Swiss francs, down from 1.5685; 6.2625 French francs, down from 6.2735; 2.0725 Dutch guilders, down from 2.0910; 1,350.50 Italian lire, down from 1,356.00, and 1.1965 Canadian dollars, up from 1.1897.

Gold prices benefit when price pressures build because investors typically use precious metals as a hedge against inflation. Renewed demand for bullion this week also has bolstered gold prices.

The late bid for gold in London was $386.30 an ounce, up from Tuesday's $380.75. Zurich's late bid was $386.25 an ounce, up from $383.00.
