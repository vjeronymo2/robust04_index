BFN [Text] The cabinet during its meeting yesterday approved a draft protocol on the Malaysia-Thailand joint authority which was earlier worked out by Thai and Malaysian governments. The protocol is designed to solve the problems concerning petroleum concessions in the joint development area lying on both countries' territorial claims in the South China Sea. The delegations of both countries late last month held talks on the avoidance of double taxation in Kuala Lumpur. They agreed that technical fees and petroleum income tax collected by both countries be reduced by half. The protocol will be part of the agreement on the avoidance of double taxation between Thailand and Malaysia. The two countries to equally share bebenfit reaped from the joint development area. They are expected to sign an agreement to start their petroleum exploration in the area this week when Prime Minister Chuan Liphai visits Malaysia.
