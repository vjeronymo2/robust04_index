BFN [Text] President Rauf Denktas has sent a letter to UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali stating that the Turkish Cypriot side has the will to conclude an agreement on the confidence-building measures issue. Denktas also requested including developments that occurred after 30 May. According to information obtained by the ANATOLIA AGENCY, Denktas pointed out in his letter that he has shown flexibility on the issues concerning access to Maras [Varosha] as well as the map submitted by the United Nations; he added, however, that these positive developments were not conveyed in Butrus-Ghali's Cyprus report. In his letter Denktas noted: I have inspected the report regarding your mission of goodwill and I will communicate to you my assessment on this subject at a suitable time. Despite the items in the report which are unacceptable and detrimental to the Turkish Cypriot side, however, we are not in favor of stopping the negotiation process on the confidence-building measures. Stating that these views were already conveyed to Gustave Feissel, the UN secretary general's deputy special representative to Cyprus, Denktas pointed out that the Turkish Cypriot side had made its position on access to Maras quite clear. Recalling that the UN secretary general's report does not reflect developments which occurred after 30 May, Denktas requested that an addition to the report to include these developments.
