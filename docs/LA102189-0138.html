October 21, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

ART NOTES: 'PAINT-IN' TO PROTEST CANYON DEVELOPMENT

A group of artists has scheduled a "paint-in" next Friday and Saturday on the Laguna Canyon hillside in the vicinity of "The Tell," the 600-foot photomural alongside Laguna Canyon Road.

According to organizer Karen Blume of Laguna Hills, the purpose will be to "protest the extensive (planned) commercial and residential development of the property around Laguna Canyon Road" and to document the last remaining elements of the natural environment. From dawn to dusk, the artists will be working in various media -- including painting, drawing, photography and sculpture -- to portray the natural life of the canyon.

Blume estimates that between 25 and 50 artists will be involved, including members of DAB, a group of artist graduates of Cal State Fullerton. Other artists are welcome to join. Information: (714) 831-3015.

Laguna Art Museum will hold its annual art auction Nov. 4 at 7:30 p.m. Works for "The Best of the Eighties," as it is being called, have been donated by 140 California artists including Joe Goode, Ed Ruscha, Kim Abeles, Frederick Eversley, Jo Ann Callis, Craig Stecyk, Joyce Treiman, Daryl Curran, Michael Davis, and Helen and Newton Harrison. Prospective bidders can view the works at the museum beginning Oct. 26.

Tickets to the auction are $60 per person. The event, sponsored by the museum's Junior Council, will include a buffet with complimentary wine, and there will be a drawing for other works of art, being donated by local galleries. All proceeds will benefit museum education programs for Orange County schoolchildren. Information: (714) 494-8971.

Also Nov. 4, the Art Institute of Southern California will hold an open house from 9:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. Tours of the campus will be offered, along with workshops in portrait and life drawing, photography, graphic design, illustration, computer art, ceramics and other subjects. Refreshments will be available. The school, a four-year college of art and design, is at 2222 Laguna Canyon Road in Laguna Beach. Guests are asked to call before visiting: (714) 497-3309.

Newport Harbor Art Museum will hold its third annual children's holiday party Nov. 30. Co-sponsored by the museum's Sales and Rental Council and Family Arts Council, the event will feature a "holiday store" where children can buy low-priced gifts and have them wrapped. Included in the $12-per-person admission price: a children's dinner, a magician's performance, a visit and photo with Santa, face painting and Christmas caroling.

Children ages 5 to 8 are invited to attend between 4 and 6 p.m.; 9- to 12-year-olds are invited between 7 and 9 p.m. The museum is at 850 San Clemente Drive in Newport Beach. To register, or for more information, call the museum store: (714) 759-1122. CATHY CURTIS
