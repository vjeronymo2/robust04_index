Language: Lao Article Type:BFN [Text] A ceremony to mark the upgrading of a border gate in Laos' Houai Sai District, Bokeo Province, and Thailand's Chiang Khong District, Chiang Rai Province, to an international border gate was held on 21 January. The Houai Sai-Chiang Khong border gate was upgraded to the level of international in accordance with an agreement reached between governments of Laos and Thailand. The upgrade is aimed at creating favorable conditions for the raising of trade relations and exchanges from local to international levels. This border gate will also create favorable conditions for foreign guests as well as domestic travelers to travel to third countries. There are now two international-level border gates at the Lao-Thai border, namely the Tha Deua-Nong Khai and the Houai Sai-Chiang Khong border gates. A further report said that on 21 January, Charat Phuachuai, deputy minister of communications of Thailand, donated 31.5 million kips in aid to the Bokeo provincial administration. Accepting the aid on behalf of the provincial administration was Khamsai Phomisai, deputy governor the province. Out of the total, 30 million kips will be used to expand and asphalt a road linking the municipal area and the provincial airport. Meanwhile, the remaining 1.5 million kips will be used for the rehabilitation of various temples in the municipal area. The Thai deputy communications minister presented the aid to the Bokeo provincial administration while leading a ceremonial offering of yellow robes and alms to Buddhist monks at the Keo Phonsavan temple in the municipal area of the province.
