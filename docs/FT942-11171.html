940506 FT 06 MAY 94 / International Company News: Mexico to end holding in Telmex The Mexican government is to sell its remaining 1.53 per cent stake in Telefonos de Mexico, Latin America's largest telecommunications company, through a global convertible bond offering next week, AP-DJ reports from Mexico City. The deal, worth Dollars 520m, will be placed on Monday or Tuesday. The bonds will be convertible to Telmex series L shares, which are available to foreign investors. The bonds will have a four-year term and the price will be 110 per cent of the price of Telmex American Depositary Shares at the time of the offering. The conversion premium will be between 17 per cent and 20 per cent of the ADS price at the time of issue. The bonds will carry a coupon of about 11 per cent, paid twice a year. Telmex is the largest blue chip on the Mexican stock exchange, with market capitalisation of about Dollars 35bn. Its ADS's trade in New York. Merrill Lynch will lead the deal. Goldman Sachs led the government's primary sale of stock in May 1992.
