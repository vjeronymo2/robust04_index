Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 29/3/1994 -- We had talks on resuming and enlarging Romanian-Russian ties in all fields, which meets the interests of both parties, said Premier Nicolae Vacaroiu after his Tuesday, March 29, interview with the Russian minister of defence Pavel Grachev. Vacaroiu showed satisfaction at the signing of the military cooperation agreement between Romania and Russia. The Russian guest revealed, for his part, the importance of the process of rebuilding a basis of cooperation between the two countries and mentioned that the agreement signed on the occasion of his visit provided a basis for cooperation in the defence industry, the training of Romanian officers in Russia, military drilling carried out jointly by the two countries, swaps of experts. Opinions were also exchanged on the current economic and social situation of the two countries. Similarities were identified in most aspects reviewed, which made the two parties deem that efficient partnership in all the fields of interest for the two sides could be beneficial for both Romania and Russia. Premier Vacaroiu was hopeful that the visit of Russian President Boris Yeltsin to Romania, likely to take place this year, could mark a step further in strengthening Romanian-Russian links. Attending were Romanian Minister of Defence Gheorghe Tinca and Secretary of State Ioan Mircea Pascu.
