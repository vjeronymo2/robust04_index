August 17, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: ANTIHISTAMINE EFFECTS

Question: Everyone seems to know that antihistamines can cause a person to become drowsy. I know that whenever a doctor has told me to use an antihistamine, I'm usually warned not to drive and not to work around machinery.

But aside from drowsiness, are there other side effects?

Answer: Some elderly individuals who take antihistamines may experience a degree of confusion. In addition, antihistamines may increase the effects of alcohol.

Q: I know that there's no cure for a cold, but would you have any suggestions about how to relieve the sore throat that comes with it? I find that that's the worst part of a cold.

A: There are a number of over-the-counter preparations that can ease the discomfort of a sore throat, including a variety of pain relievers. It might help if you would increase your intake of liquids and gargle with warm salt water. A humidifier might also relieve symptoms.
