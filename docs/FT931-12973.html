930126 FT 26 JAN 93 / The Economy: Unions to challenge 1.5% pay ceiling PUBLIC SECTOR unions representing nearly 1m council manual workers yesterday issued a challenge to the government's 1.5 per cent pay rise ceiling, Lisa Wood writes. Mr Roger Poole, general-secretary of Nupe, the public services union, said a meeting of local government manual unions yesterday had decided that this year's pay claim 'will be prepared without regard to the limit'. A similar message will be delivered today by leaders of 750,000 local government white-collar workers. Nalgo representatives will tell employers that the limit is 'unfair and unjustifiable'. The Local Government Management Board, which negotiates on behalf of councils, said yesterday that this year's government grant meant some local authorities could not afford 1.5 per cent. The board said: 'Whatever union leaders might be saying at this early stage, the fact is that if local authorities agree to more than they can afford it is the staff and the local communities who end up paying with reduced services and more job losses.'
