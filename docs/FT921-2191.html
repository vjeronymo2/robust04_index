920320 FT 20 MAR 92 / UK Company News: Manders at Pounds 6.04m after second half improvement MANDERS (HOLDINGS), the Wolverhampton-based inks, paints and property group, showed an improved performance in the second half of 1991 and ended the year with pre-tax profits of Pounds 6.04m compared with Pounds 6.6m in 1990. Operating in sluggish markets, the group faced continuing pressure on margins and suffered from the growing incidence of bad debts. Turnover amounted to Pounds 101.6m (Pounds 104.8m). Earnings per share were 13p, against 14.31p. The final dividend is 5p, making an unchanged total of 7p. Reorganisation of factories in the inks division, a change in distribution methods of the paints business and moves to boost productivity began to take effect in the second half. This meant that, for example, the 5 per cent turnover fall in the paints division was half that of the industry generally. But the tile business slipped into loss. Mr Roy Amos, chairman, said that 'the tile business is not really strategic,' and, hinting at a possible disposal, added, 'we need to do something about the tile business.' Manders's property interests, based on the eponymous shopping centre in Wolverhampton, increased revenue because of higher rents but were revalued at Pounds 57.5m, a reduction of 11.5 per cent since the last revaluation in 1989. To cover this adjustment, Pounds 7.77m is being deducted from group reserves. The group expects financial benefits to flow through from the recent acquisition of Windeck Paints, a move which takes it into the own-brand business at retail multiples. But Mr Amos warned that 'there is no end to the recession as far as we can see.'
