BFN [Text] President Husni Mubarak has met for the second time with members of the Palestinian and Israeli delegations to continue his extensive consultations and discussions with them. The Palestinian side was lead by Yasir 'Arafat while the Israeli side was lead by Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin. President Mubarak has been following up on the talks between the two sides, which lasted for approximately six hours, ending at 0130 this morning. President Mubarak met with the delegations immediately afterwards. Dr. Nabil Sha'th, political adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, has stated that the Palestinian-Israeli agreement will be signed today, adding that everything has been resolved. Dr. Sha'th made these remarks following the Palestinian-Israeli talks that ended at 0205 this morning. Regarding the differences that existed between the two sides, Sha'th said that solutions were found for all these points, with the support of President Husni Mubarak, Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa, and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. He added: Negotiations always involve give and take and we have reached an agreement that we are satisfied with. Concluding his remarks, Sha'th said he was pleased with what has been achieved. He added that the beginning is always important because this is the first time in the history of the Palestinian people that they have had a chance to rule themselves by themselves on their own territory. He added: This is not all we want, it is just a start. Foreign Minister Musa said following the Palestinian-Israeli talks that the agreement for the implementation of the Declaration of Principles will be signed at 1100 [0800 GMT] this morning. He added that all the outstanding issues had been settled and that the details will be made public today. He added: I will not announce anything on behalf of the Palestinians or the Israelis. Everything has been discussed in detail on the maps related to the agreement. Asked if an agreement has been reached on the size of Jericho, Musa said that the area of Jericho has been increased by several kilometers, provided this is taken into consideration [as heard]. Regarding the guards at the crossing points, Musa said a certain agreement has been reached over this point. Musa expressed his happiness at reaching an agreement, noting that some insignificant points have been left for a later stage. Regarding the release of the Palestinian prisoners, Musa said this matter was discussed and many of them will be released. The foreign minister added that the question of territorial water was also discussed. On U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher's role in the talks, Musa noted that Christopher played a very important role. Asked if President Mubarak played a role in persuading 'Arafat, Musa said: President Mubarak has persuaded all parties, not just 'Arafat, to be flexible. Asked why President Mubarak spent so many hours in the negotiations for the first time, Musa said that it is natural for President Mubarak to be present throughout the duration of the talks, since Egypt is the country that has been devoting its efforts to solve the Palestinian issue for more than 40 years. He added that Egypt is also the host country for the signing ceremony of this historic agreement between the Palestinians and Israelis.
