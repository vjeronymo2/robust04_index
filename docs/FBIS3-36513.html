Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Most Georgians support concluding the Russian-Georgian treaty on friendship, cooperation and good-neighborly relations signed by Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze in Tbilisi yesterday. According to an opinion poll conducted by the sociological service of the Georgian agency BS-Press, a total of 91% of respondents support signing the treaty, while only 4% said they do not. Five percent of those asked abstained from answering the question. A total of 73.4% of Georgians believe that the treaty will play an important part in the settlement of the armed conflict in Abkhazia, while 11.9% were skeptical about that and another 14.7% found it difficult to answer. As many as 59% of those asked believe that deploying Russian troops in Georgia on Russian conditions (3 military bases in the republic) is in Georgia's interests, while 19.2% are categorically against the deployment. Another 21.8% said they were too incompetent in such issues to provide any answer. During the poll, 10 out of 500 said this was the first that they had heard that Yeltsin had visited Georgia.
