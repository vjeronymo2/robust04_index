Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, March 29 -- Dariusz Rosati remains the coalition candidate to the post of deputy prime minister and finance minister. The coalition hopes to meet soon with President Lech Walesa to present its arguments in the matter, General Secretary of the Social-Democracy of the Republic of Poland (SDRP) Jerzy Szmajdzinski said following a meeting held late on Tuesday by representatives of the ruling coalition, including Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak, Aleksander Kwasniewski, Wlodzimierz Cimoszewicz and Leszek Miller. Aleksander Kawsniewski said that the coalition did not discuss any other candidature to the post of deputy prime minister and finance minister. He added that the meeting with the president may be held next week. A visit of a Polish parliamentary delegation to Moscow, starting on Wednesday, was another topic of the meeting. Kwasniewski said that after returning from Moscow he may meet with Rosati who is on his way to Poland.
