BFN [Text] Havana, 18 May (PL) -- The Union of Military Industries has the capacity to ensure the production and repair of the light weapons required by the Cuban Armed Forces, General Luis Perez Rospide has stated here. The ranking officer heads a complex of factories and workshops that has as its primary mission maintaining combat weapons in top condition and overseeing the profitability of the 12 existing enterprises. In 1989, the military industry began a process of preparation to also take on production necessary for the national economy following the disappearance of the Soviet Union with which the country had 85 percent of its foreign trade. Replying to a question from PRENSA LATINA, Perez Rospide said the military industries have the capacity to ensure, with its own experts, the repairs of tanks, airplanes, rockets, and other equipment necessary for defense, despite the economic difficulties the island is facing. In addition to maintaining and modernizing combat weapons, the factories and workshops are also guaranteeing light weapons, mines, and ammunition, Perez Rospide, a graduate in tank engineering, said. Perez Rospide told reporters during a tour of three military enterprises, where experts, designers, and a skilled labor force work, that 25 percent of its output consists of products in great demand among civilians. The production of these enterprises includes safes, compressors, industrial refrigerators, wagons, computers for medical diagnostics, other equipment useful for tourism, public health, and agriculture.
