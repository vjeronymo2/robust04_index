December 16, 1989, Saturday, Valley Edition

NORTH HOLLYWOOD TOURNAMENT;

DELAHOYA GIVES GRANT LIFT INTO FINALE AGAINST HOST

Grant High fires so many weapons in the gym that it doesn't seem to matter if some of them miss the target.

Take Friday night's 76-66 victory over Reseda (1-4) in a championship semifinal of the North Hollywood tournament.

Senior forward and floor general Setro Terzian made only two of 13 shots from the field and missed his first seven. Terzian finished with five points -- none in the second half -- and was called for a charge early in the fourth quarter while throwing up an off-balance layup.

"The shot felt good," Terzian said simply. "I had to keep shooting."

Fortunately for Grant (6-0), however, the shooting of guard Rene Delahoya, who scored a game-high 23 points, and center Brunell Mori (16 points) felt better. Grant plays North Hollywood tonight in the final.

In other tournament games:

North Hollywood 55, El Camino Real 45 -- The Huskies (9-0) rallied for the second time in three games to earn a date with Grant. Senior center Dana Jones scored 11 of his game-high 21 points in the second half as North Hollywood erased a 32-19 halftime deficit. North Hollywood rallied from a 13-point fourth-quarter deficit on Tuesday against Dorsey in a 56-54 win.

Chatsworth 52, Poly 41 -- In a consolation semifinal, three Chancellors scored in double figures to offset a game-high 21-point performance by Poly's Jason Wilson and hand the Parrots their eighth consecutive loss. Eric Isaac (12 points), Darryl Porter (13) and Craig Hewitt (13) led Chatsworth.

Granada Hills 93, Bell-Jeff 63 -- Highlander Sag'y Koren came off the bench to score a game-high 18 points, and teammates Osiris Nalls and Jermoine Brantley added 16 and 15 points.
