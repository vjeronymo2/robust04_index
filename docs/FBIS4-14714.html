BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Tbilisi April 1 TASS -- The Georgian parliament adopted a resolution on Friday concerning an incident which took place in the parliamentary building on Thursday, when the former chief of the Tbilisi police, assisted by his colleagues, attempted to thwart a procedure of endorsing the Georgian defence minister to his post. The document adopted by the Georgian parliament qualified actions by Mayor-General David Zeikidze as an attempt to topple the parliament by force. It was pointed out that as a result of clashes, a member of the government and a member of the Georgian leader's staff were seriously injured. The parliament took into account that criminal proceedings had been instituted on this incident and instructed the Georgian Interior Ministry to take necessary measures to "restore order in the city police department."
