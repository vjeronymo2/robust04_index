930511 FT 11 MAY 93 / UK Company News: Select Appointments shows Pounds 2.5m deficit Losses at Select Appointments (Holdings), the USM-quoted employment agency group, fell to Pounds 2.53m for the 12 months to December 31. That compared with a loss, on an FRS 3 basis, of Pounds 1.8m for the previous nine month period. Turnover from continuing operations amounted to Pounds 21.7m (Pounds 14.2m), of which Pounds 15.6m (Pounds 10.4m) was attributable to the UK and Pounds 6.15m (Pounds 3.82m) to Australasia. There was a loss of Pounds 1.47m at the operating level, against Pounds 809,000. UK operations recorded a deficit of Pounds 1.48m (Pounds 700,000), while Australasia produced profits of Pounds 16,000 (losses of Pounds 109,000); interest payable amounted to Pounds 913,000 (Pounds 585,000) and exceptional losses Pounds 153,000 (Pounds 407,000). Losses per share, on the enlarged capital following a refinancing in October 1991, amounted to 1.06p (26p). Mr Anthony Martin, chairman, said that during the first quarter Select had seen a continued improvement in temporary business, but the permanent recruitment market remained depressed. Nonetheless, he said results for the first quarter were ahead of the comparable period of last year.
