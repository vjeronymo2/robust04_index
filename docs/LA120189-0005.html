December 1, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

QUAKES AND SIN

In response to "We Need a Meter to Gauge the Richter Scale of Sin," by Brent R. Pitman, California Commentary, Nov. 13:

For Billy Graham to implicate the earthquake with sin is unbelievable. What an insult to the people of Santa Cruz and Watsonville. "If there were no sin in the world, he said, there would be no earthquake and no suffering." I agree that if there were no sin, there would be no suffering for the simple reason there would be no people. There would still be earthquakes but the results would be academic.

For the good reverend's information, there is no sin on the moon because there are no people. However, the moon still records about 8 moonquakes every day, some 3,000 per year.

Makes one wonder about these so-called men of the cloth who claim to engage in a daily dialogue with God.

JOHN C. MARTIN

Palmdale
