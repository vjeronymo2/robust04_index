BFN [Text] A source at the Tunisian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, answering a question about the latest developments of the situation in Rwanda and especially the intention of some non-African countries to send forces to that country, said that Tunisia, which chairs the Organization of African Unity, OAU, affirms that the Rwandan issue should be tackled according to Security Council resolution 918 dated 17 March 1994 and within the framework of the 30th OAU summit decision on the need to base every move on the following principles: -- coordinating with the OAU regarding the sending of any forces to Rwanda in support of the central organ of conflict prevention, management, and settlement mechanism. -- only African forces and no other should be sent to Rwanda as stated by the above mentioned Security Council resolution. -- securing the consent of the conflicting parties before taking the initiative of sending troops. -- enlisting the material and logistic support of other countries wishing to contribute to the establishment of peace and stability in that African country. The same source added that Tunisia, as chairman of the OAU, sees that it is preferable to support this framework agreed on by the African countries and by the Security Council and considers that any other initiative outside this framework would further complicate the situation and would hinder the reconciliation and peace efforts in that sisterly African country.
