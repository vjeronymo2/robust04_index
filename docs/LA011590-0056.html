January 15, 1990, Monday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: SEAL BEACH;

863 WERE RESCUED BY LIFEGUARDS IN '89

Lifeguards rescued 863 people and prevented more than 70,000 potential incidents on the city's beach last year, according to a report by lifeguard chief Tim Dorsey.

Superb weather brought large crowds to the beach, Dorsey wrote, resulting in a dramatic increase in lifesaving work. In 1988, the lifeguards rescued about 650 swimmers.

In addition to the rescues, lifeguards administered major first aid to nine people and minor first aid to about 800 people in 1989, the report said. No drownings were reported. MICHELLE NICOLOSI
