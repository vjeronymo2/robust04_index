Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Mongolian Press Review"] [Text] Ulaanbaatar, February 3 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The newspaper "ARDYN ERH" reports of meeting of chairman of the Great State Hural N. Bagabandi with newsmen. At the press conference N. Bagabandi told about his impressions during his trip around the Tub and Dundgobi Aymags of the country. He noted that the rural population especially herdsmen have not been informed properly about the laws and provisions being taken lately. Owing to this, he said, the propaganda of the laws should be promoted amongst the rural population both in line of the government and through the local authorities. Especially through the administration of primary territorial units. The newspaper publishes an interview granted to its correspondent by chairman of the standing parliamentary committee D. Lundeejantsan concerning the outcome of examining by a working group of the Great State Hural the activities of the secret service. D. Lundeejantsan stressed that in the course of inspection there has not been found any facts affirming the abuse of official position and bribery of which were accused some members of the parliament and government. Neither was corroborated the information by the former chief of the counterintelligence department of the main board on ensuring state security L. Sanjaasuren relating to the so called intentions on liquidating such documents being available in the board. D. Lundeejantsan noted in particular, that chairman of the parliament N. Bagabandi, his deputy J. Gombojav, Prime Minister P. Jasray have said about the necessity for restoration of their honour and dignity. The newspaper informs about the statement by the Social Democratic Party which contains their demand for resignation of abovementioned officials of their posts due to their compromising. The newspaper publishes information on supposing issue of 8 volume anthology of Mongolian literature beginning since the works by prominent representatives of old literature up to the modern ones. The publication would be sponsored by the "Asia" Trading and Production Company having provided all the means necessary. It is supposed that all the eight volumes would be issued within the current year. The central organ of the ruling Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party "Unen" reports of the round table talk dedicated to the birthday anniversary of an outstanding figure of the 1921 people's revolution one of the founders of the MPRP, state and people's army D. Suhbaatar. The talk was organized by the D. Suhbaatar foundation. Scholars participating told about new discoveries concerning the life and activities of D. Suhbaatar and his brothers- inarms taking part in the national liberation movement. The newspaper carries an article by writer L. Darisuren relating to the circumstances of the death of D. Suhbaatar. The author is supporting the version saying that D. Suhbaatar was poisoned.
