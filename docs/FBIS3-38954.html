Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Asked what Greece would do if the permanent NATO members today decided to use force in Bosnia-Herzegovina, government spokesman Evangelos Venizelos said: We will not allow the NATO base in Aktion to be used, we will withdraw the Greek crew members from the AWACS [Airborne Warning And Control System], and demand that no Balkan country participates in military operations in the former Yugoslavia. Venizelos stressed that this is the unanimous view of all political party leaders; it was expressed at a meeting under President Konstandinos Karamanlis on 10 May 1993. Venizelos added: These positions are firm and our permanent representative Ambassador Zafiropoulos will convey them to NATO. We hope that a diplomatic solution to end this bloody war will be found, that is, an immediate and viable solution. Answering another question, Venizelos said Greece is not willing to veto a decision on military operations because, as he explained, the international common view would see Greece as part of the Yugoslav crisis. The spokesman concluded: On the contrary, the international community should realize that our country is a factor of stability in the Balkans.
