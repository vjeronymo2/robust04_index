BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature: Igor Porshnev, Vitaly Trubetskoi and others] [Text] Moscow reserves the right to use every tool at its disposal to protect its servicemen and other Russians in Tajikistan. Grigory Karasin, Russian Foreign Ministry spokesman, said at a Moscow briefing Thursday that armed terror had been and would be dealt with resolutely. He said that the recent killings of Russian servicemen and ethnic Russians in Tajikistan was the work of those who did not want peace and stability in that country. Karasin also said that at its emergency meeting the Tajik government had decided to take up numerous steps to protect the Russians and the residents of the Tajik capital. Round the clock patrolling had been introduced in areas inhabited by Russians. Investigation teams in which military prosecutors are represented have been set up.
