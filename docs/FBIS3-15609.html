Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 7 Mar -- Within the framework of the Russian diplomatic initiatives undertaken on the instructions of President Boris Yeltsin in connection with the sharp deterioration in the situation in the Middle East, First Deputy Foreign Minister Igor Ivanov received the ambassadors of the United States, Israel, Egypt, and Lebanon and the charges d'affaires of Syria and Jordan at the Russian Foreign Ministry on 6 and 7 March. Igor Ivanov informed them of efforts by the Russian Foreign Ministry and Minister Andrey Kozyrev at this crucial moment. He described in detail the main results of his own recent mission to Tunisia and Israel, where he had meetings with Yasir 'Arafat, Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, and Foreign Affairs Minister Shim'on Peres. The diplomats were told that, in light of the present situation in the region following the Hebron tragedy, Russia is implementing urgent measures aimed at preventing an escalation of the violence and at preserving and resuming the Middle East peace talks. It was this angle that was taken at the talks in Tunis and Tel Aviv. The participants focused on such important issues as the UN Security Council adoption of an effective resolution aimed at guaranteeing the safety of the civilian Palestinian population in the occupied territories, an international presence there as an additional means to guarantee the population's safety, and also new measures and steps to promote the resumption of the Arab-Israeli dialogue. During the meeting with ambassadors, Russia reaffirmed its resolute intention, as co-sponsor, to continue its vigorous efforts toward an improvement in the situation in the occupied territories and the urgent resumption of talks. In this connection, a willingness was expressed to maintain the closest of contacts with all interested parties with the aim of tackling the main task that unites them all -- namely, the establishment of a comprehensive and stable peace in the Middle East.
