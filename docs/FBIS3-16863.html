Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS] [Text] Prague, March 19 (TASS) -- Vladimir Zhirinovsky has postponed his visit to the czech capital for an indefinite time, the CTK NEWS AGENCY reported. The visit was to begin on Sunday. At the beginning of the week, when staying in Warsaw, the chairman of the Liberal-Democratic Party of Russia (LDPR) announced plans to make a two-day visit to the Czech Republic. However, the party leader "probably made a mistake", an LDPR spokesman told CTK. As it turns out, it is necessary for Zhirinovsky to address intra- party affairs, which will require his presence in Moscow on Sunday and Monday. A decision to postpone the visit was taken after LDPR representatives' consultations with the staff of the Czech Embassy in Moscow. Earlier Prague had made it clear that it considers Zhirinovsky's planned visit as highly undesirable. In particular, Czech Interior Minister Jan Ruml stated an intention to make every possible step to prevent the LDPR leader from visiting the country.
