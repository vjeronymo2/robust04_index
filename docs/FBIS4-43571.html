BFN [Text] The Belgian newspaper LE SOIR wrote this morning that President Habyarimana's aircraft might have been shot down on 6 April by two French soldiers wearing Belgian uniforms and acting on behalf of Hutu extremists. Belgian officials immediately said that this possibility had been rejected by their investigation committee. French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe, currently visiting Abidjan, brushed aside these accusations: [Begin Juppe recording] From what I gather, the author used the conditional tense in his article. He reports allegations according to which the French were wearing Belgian uniforms, and so forth. I shall not expand further. According to my information this seems to be pure fabrication. I have no more comments to make. An investigation under the aegis of the UN secretary-general is currently underway to establish the truth. All these rumors are obviously baseless. I regret that such accusations are being made using the conditional tense. Either one is certain of the facts, and has evidence or one keeps quiet. [end recording]
