930429 FT 29 APR 93 / World Trade News: Thai contract for Alcatel A THAI company which was awarded a contract to install 1m telephone lines in Thailand has chosen Alcatel Alsthom of France and Sumitomo of Japan to supply equipment, Reuter reports from Bangkok. Alcatel will supply switching equipment and Sumitomo-NEC transmission equipment for a second 250,000 lines to Thai Telephone & Telecommunication (TT&T), according to Ms Saowapark Chiwapricha, TT&T spokeswoman, yesterday. Sofricom of France will also supply a computer system costing 1.50bn baht (Pounds 39m) for TT&T customer services. TT&T won a 25-year concession from the government-owned Telephone Organisation of Thailand to install and operate 1m telephone lines outside Bangkok and its suburbs. The Company has said it will install the first 220,032 lines in 24 provinces by middle of 1994.
