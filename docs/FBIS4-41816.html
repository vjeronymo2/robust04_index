BFN [Text] A total of 61 percent of voters of the Ukrainian Zaporozhye Region Kamensk-Dneprovsk District have voted against the utilization of the sixth block of the Zaporozhye Nuclear Power Station. A total of 38 percent of voters voted for it during a referendum held along with the presidential and local elections in Ukraine. Voters were asked whether it was reasonable to switch on the sixth block before a law on nuclear energy was adopted. The ballot papers also included a question on whether a storage facility for exhaust nuclear fuel could be built in the region. An INTERFAX correspondent reports that 32 percent of voters were in favor of such a facility for exhausted nuclear fuel and 63 percent were against. A total of 68 percent of the voters registered in the region took part in the referendum. This referendum was initiated by the District Council.
