Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and foreign minister, has received the U.S. President's special envoy Charles Redman. Mr. Redman informed Granic about U.S. views on the possibilities for resolving the crisis in this part of Europe, especially in Bosnia-Herzegovina, in the light of the latest NATO decisions. Ambassador Redman presented several U.S. initiatives -- also supported by the leading countries of the European Union -- for setting up Croat-Muslim relations on a lasting basis and for ending the war in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Minister Granic voiced Croatia's support for NATO's resolve to end the conflict in Bosnia-Herzegovina as soon as possible. Hope was expressed that all parties to the conflict -- especially the Serb side -- would take the world community's warnings seriously. Both sides attached particular importance to the setting up of Croat-Muslim relations and cooperation between the two peoples in the final resolution of the crisis. Ambassador Redman laid special emphasis on the importance the United States was attaching to Croatia's role in resolving the crisis in these parts.
