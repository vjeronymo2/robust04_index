Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Thirty-three patients, suspected of contracting malignant anthrax, have been hospitalized in Almaty. Deputy Chief of Kazakhstan's Sanitary and Epidemiological Station Rufina Ivanova told INTERFAX that the final diagnosis would be announced after the results of the tests were available, presumably on November 9. The condition of three patients is rather serious and of the rest of the patients - satisfactory. The epidemic is believed to have been caused by cattle which had not undergone veterinary control. This year, 22 cases of malignant anthrax have been registered in Kazakhstan (last year - 25).
