Language: French Article Type:BFN [Article signed ME/PI: "Bale: Three Federal Councillors Present at Opening of Muba 94"; as released by Bern ATSA/SDAA database] [Excerpt] Bale, 4 Mar (ATS) -- The Muba, the most important public trade fair in Switzerland, opened on Friday [4 March]. Three federal councillors attended the opening. For 10 days more than 1,400 exhibitors will display their services and products in seven distinct sectors. The special presentations this year are dedicated to AIDS, civil protection, radioactive waste, and rational energy use. Federal Councillors Jean-Pascal Delamuraz, Arnold Koller, and Swiss President Otto Stich attended the first day of Muba 1994. Among the people invited were Lonnart Meri, president of the Republic of Estonia; Mario Frick, head of the Liechtenstein Government; Gret Haller, president of the Swiss National Council; and Riccardo Jagmetti, president of the Swiss State Council. [Public Economic Councillor] Jean-Pascal Delamuraz delivered a speech in which he said: "There is hope of a modest recovery, but we should also be aware that the economic recovery will not have an immediate affect on the labor market." He reminded his audience that the parliament will have to decide this autumn on whether to accept the new GATT agreements. These agreements, he said, are "good and even indispensable for Switzerland, but they will not perform miracles." They are aimed at entrepreneurs and "bold" workers, and are not "for the faint-hearted."

Fifteen Trading Partners
This year, Muba is acting as host to 15 trading partners. Estonia, Kenya, the DPRK, and the region of Offenburg/Mid-Baden (Germany) are represented for the first time. The other guest countries are India, Mongolia, Pakistan, Poland, Russia, the Slovak Republic, Sri Lanka, Thailand, the Czech republic, Hungary, and the French region of Vosges. [passage omitted]
