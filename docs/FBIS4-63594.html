BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Grigoriy Arslanov, Vyacheslav Tomilin] [Excerpts] Beijing, 23 May -- The Russian and Chinese sides are attaching great significance to the official visit to the PRC by Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin, which begins on 26 May. Igor Rogachev, Russia's ambassador to China, stated this today in an interview with ITAR-TASS correspondents. The second sitting of the Russian-Chinese inter-governmental commission for trade, economic, and scientific and technical cooperation is to open here 24 hours before talks begin between the two prime ministers. Between six and seven major agreements are expected to be signed during the visit. A large group of ministers will be accompanying Viktor Chernomyrdin. [passage omitted] The proposed documents will supplement a package of bilateral agreements concluded in December 1992 during a visit by Boris Yeltsin. [passage omitted]
