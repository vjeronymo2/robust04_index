Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Today Sergey Tsoy, the head of the press service of the Mayor's Office, denied reports that Yuriy Luzhkov, the mayor of Moscow, was going to run for a post of president of Russia at the upcoming elections for the head of the state in 1996. He noted in his interview to INTERFAX that Yuriy Luzhkov has never made such statements about his intention to become the president of Russia. Sergey Tsoy underlined that the main task of the mayor is to work in order to improve the life of Muscovites and not to receive any political advantages from his post. He believes that rumors that have been spreading around that the capital's head of administration allegedly has some political ambitions are not only politically irrelevant but also immoral.
