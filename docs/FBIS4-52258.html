BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 7 (YONHAP) -- Kim Pyong-il, North Korean ambassador to Finland and half brother of Kim Chong-il, recently returned home, giving rise to speculation that something may have happened in Pyongyang, the SANKEI SHIMBUN reported in a report from Moscow Saturday. Pyong-il, the son of Kim Il-song and his present wife, Kim Song- ae, arrived in Helsinki in early March as Pyongyang's new ambassador to Finland and presented his credentials to the Finnish president on March 18. The SANKEI report said the junior Kim was just about to settle in at a newly purchased mansion when he abruptly flew back to Pyongyang on April 22. He has yet to return and take up his duties. "His return home was in such a hurry that he didn't even bother to go through the required protocol of notifying the Finnish Foreign Ministry of his absence," the paper said. Moreover, it said, his departure came several days before the Finnish president's annual reception for members of the diplomatic corps, an occasion which an ambassador, especially a new one, shouldn't have missed. As Kim Pyong-il's absence drags on, Finnish papers have speculated on the reason for his return to Pyongyang, according to SANKEI. One paper said Ambassador Kim's "secret-shrouded" return to Pyongyang might have been the result of a political conspiracy involving top North Korean leaders. Another daily claimed Kim has been unable to resume his duties in Helsinki because of some unusual contingency in North Korea. A source in the Finnish Foreign Ministry reportedly said he didn't know when Kim would come back to Helsinki. "We don't know if Ambassador Kim can return here at all," the source was quoted as saying. The North Korean Embassy in Helsinki, fueling the speculation, is refusing to comment on the ambassador's abrupt return to Pyongyang, the SANKEI report said. Some reports indicated that Pyong-il has been on bad terms with his half brother Chong-il, who is likely to succeed his aging father as North Korea's leader.
