Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 14/1/1994 -- Referring to the news item released by AFP and published by Turkish dailies regarding a statement by Albert Chernyshev, Russian ambassador to Turkey, who accuses Romania of sucking in, without authorization, a part of the Russian natural gas meant for Turkey, the Romanian Ministry of Industries issued the following press release to the ROMPRES national agency: "Romania's taking in a part of the Russian natural gas meant for Turkey is a technical impossibility. The natural gas supply from the Russian Federation to the Balkan countries, i.e. Romania, Bulgaria, and Turkey runs through separate pipelines. The pipelines for Turkey and Bulgaria are connected to the pumping station at Orlovska, on Ukraine's territory. There is no outlet or other possibility to draw gas from these pipelines all along their length that traverses Romania. "The pipeline that supplies Romania has the junction in Ukraine, upstream the Orlovska compressing station, i.e. on that station's intake. "Besides, a representative of Gazprom -- the Russian natural gas exporter -- is permanently monitoring the gas amounts supplied to the three countries at the Isaccea station where the pipelines enter Romania's territory."
