Language: Danish Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "New Danish EU Boss"] [Text] Peter Baumann, the 54-year-old head of department at the Agriculture Ministry, has been appointed to head the Danish EU [European Union] Directorate, Socialist Prime Minister Poul Nyrup Rasmussen announced yesterday. The EU Directorate administers EU funds worth between 11 and 12 billion kroner each year. Peter Baumann will take up his new post on 15 April. He will take charge of 240 employees following the recent recruitment of 38 extra staff in the EU directorate, among other things with a view to coping with the hectare grants. After graduating in law from Copenhagen University in 1965, Peter Baumann was a practicing attorney for seven years and taught public law at Copenhagen University. In 1974, he moved to Brussels where for three years he served as an adviser within the European Commission's legal department. In 1977-1980 he was deputy cabinet head for Danish Commissioner Finn Gundelach. In 1980, Peter Baumann returned to the Danish Agriculture Ministry. After six years as office chief, he served as a commissioner within the Ministry for three years before becoming head of department in 1990.
