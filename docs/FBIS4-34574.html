BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Honduran economic developments monitored through 21 June. The Honduran private banking sector has disclosed that non-traditional Honduran exports increased by 22 percent in 1993. According to the Tegucigalpa-based Banco Atlantida, income from non-traditional sales on the international market amounted to $226 million. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1957 GMT 13 Jun 94) The Central Bank Board of Directors has decided to increase its cash reserve by six points. This will begin on 16 June and is to be done gradually, increasing one point every 15 days until it reaches 40 percent on 30 September. This is a measure to prevent the sliding of the lempira with regard to the dollar and to put a stop to the spiraling inflation. (San Pedro Sula TIEMPO in Spanish 15 Jun 94 p 3) A banking source told ACAN-EFE that the fever to save dollars in Honduras has reduced deposits in the local currency by 1.3 percent. According to this source, the country's monetary system is experiencing a "strong expansion of deposits in dollars," and has surpassed expectations in less than a year. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1442 GMT 18 Jun 94)
