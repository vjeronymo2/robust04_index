May 19, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

WHO WANTS 'IDEA' PLAYS

Mark Chalon Smith's play reviews continue to be as entertaining and insightful as a train wreck. A case in point is Smith's review of "Love, Sex and the IRS," a play produced early this year by Costa Mesa Civic Playhouse (" 'Love , Sex and the IRS' Makes For a Taxing Evening," Calendar, Jan. 24).

Smith savaged the play because it was devoid of "ideas" -- still more evidence that Smith has no business whatsoever reviewing community theater. Any community theater that served its audience a steady diet of Smith's cherished "idea" plays would go belly-up in no time. As a longtime member of Anaheim's community theater, the Ana Modjeska Players, I remember -- all too well -- the last "idea" play we presented: "The Physicist" by Friedrich Duerrenmatt. I believe the play averaged five customers per performance, counting walkouts.

Inasmuch as a major priority of community theater is to offer roles for novice as well as experienced local thespians, there's not much chance of staging a polished production of a serious, thought-provoking drama such as "Children of a Lesser God," which requires precision acting to be effective. "Harmless" comedies, as Smith calls them, provide a much better training ground for fledgling actors while amusing audiences that don't expect Broadway-level performances.

Mr. Smith, please don't try to single-handedly change the face of community theater to suit your tastes. Community theater audiences want to be entertained, not instructed. If they want provocative drama, they'll usually pay a bit more and attend such theaters as South Coast Repertory, Alternative Repertory Theatre, Way Off Broadway Playhouse and those of local colleges and universities -- all of which do an excellent job of presenting "idea" plays.

By the way, it's a good thing for the masters such as Shakespeare and Moliere, who occasionally wrote "harmless, non-idea" comedies, that Mark Chalon Smith wasn't around to put them straight.

LEW RILEY, Yorba Linda
