Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 2 (XINHUA) -- Chairman Qiao Shi of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress (NPC) had a warm and friendly conversation on furthering China-Belau [Palau] ties here today with a congressional delegation from this island country in West Pacific. Qiao expressed the hope that Belau, a trustee of the United States, will win full independence at an early date in accordance with its people's desire. Describing the delegation's visit as a good beginning for promoting Sino-Belau relations, Qiao hoped for more exchanges between the two sides after Belau's independence. Qiao also reaffirmed China's consistent position that countries, big or small, strong or weak, should be equals and all countries must respect each other and have the right to go their own ways in the light of different conditions. The visitors, led by Yutaka Gibbons, who is the chief Aibedul, arrived here yesterday. Gibbons thanked the hosts for their hospitality and expressed the hope that Belau and China will have more contacts. Present at the meeting was NPC Vice-Chairman Li Peiyao. After the meeting, Li hosted a dinner in honor of the visitors.
