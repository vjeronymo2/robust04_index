931022 FT 22 OCT 93 / Trusts begin to issue Lloyd's prospectuses PATHFINDER prospectuses, documents used to market shares, were issued yesterday for two investment trusts at Lloyd's of London. Merchant banks, securities houses and Lloyd's agents responded quickly to the decision by Names on Wednesday to allow corporate investors to participate in the market. Sixteen investment trusts, seeking to raise Pounds 1.25bn, have been announced although there are doubts whether all can reach their capital targets. Yesterday's pathfinders were for trusts sponsored by the merchant banks, Samuel Montagu and SG Warburg. Montagu has teamed up with James Capel and is aiming to raise Pounds 280m to support 98 syndicates, while Warburg aims to raise Pounds 150m to support about 20. Four other investment trusts issued pathfinders before Wednesday's vote, while a further 10 have announced their intentions to raise funds, many of them by publishing detailed information memorandums. With a number of other schemes in the pipeline, the markets could eventually be asked to find more than Pounds 1.5bn. Salomon Brothers, the US investment bank which expects to announce a Dollars 300m (Pounds 201.7m) investment scheme next week, is tapping US investors. But most of the schemes will be tapping the UK equity market over the next two months. However, according to financiers seeking to market some of the largest investment trust schemes, UK investors are unlikely to provide between Pounds 750m and Pounds 1bn in capital for the schemes, suggesting a possible shortfall of some Pounds 500m. That indicates that many funds could reduce the amount of capital they are targeting. 'Downsizing is the name of the game,' said one analyst. Alternatively, some schemes will fail altogether. 'The reality is that not all these vehicles will succeed,' said Mr Chris Hitchings, analyst with UBS Global Research.
