940104 FT 04 JAN 94 / Carey in attempt to defuse Sudan row The Archbishop of Canterbury, Dr George Carey, attempted yesterday to calm a diplomatic row caused by his visit to guerrilla-held territory in southern Sudan, saying he was willing to visit the north if he were guaranteed freedom of movement by the Islamic government in Khartoum. Sudan expelled the British ambassador last week after learning that Dr Carey had cancelled his visit to the capital. Britain has threatened retaliation unless the decision is reversed by today, a demand Sudanese state radio described as 'insolence' and 'a naked challenge'. Dr Carey, spiritual leader of the world's 70m Anglicans, said his purpose in visiting southern Sudan was to draw international attention to a forgotten civil war which has dragged on for 10 years. He said he had cancelled his visit to Khartoum after the Sudanese government changed the conditions of his visit at the last minute. 'After all the atrocities and the imposition of Islamic law, I could not be seen, as a Christian leader, to be the guest of an Islamic government,' he explained. 'I would gladly consider visiting Khartoum as a guest of the Episcopal church, if I can be assured freedom of movement.'
