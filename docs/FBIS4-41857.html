BFN [Text] Only the foreign banks that can boast a first-rate reputation and are regarded as reliable partners should be attracted to Russia. This has been stated by the chairman of Russia's Central Bank, Viktor Gerashchenko. Speaking in Moscow at a conference of the Consultative Council for Foreign Investment, he emphasized the necessity to preserve protectionism in respect of home banks. Mr. Gerashchenko also said that in 1993 Russia's gold and hard currency reserves had tripled. By the first of this month they amounted to $5.6 billion.
