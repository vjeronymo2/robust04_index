931124 FT 24 NOV 93 / Further fall puts builders' supplier on firmer footing The half-point cut in interest rates was especially welcome to a builders' merchant with high bank borrowings. The interest rate cut 'absolutely delighted' Mr Charles Fisher, chairman and chief executive of Sharpe & Fisher, the publicly quoted builders' merchants with 22 branches in southern England. But he was quick to point out that real interest rates were still historically high. 'We could afford to see another point cut.' Mr Fisher says interest rates are 'the single most important factor' affecting his company. Sharpe & Fisher, with a Pounds 33m market capitalisation, has about Pounds 7m of bank borrowings. Previous cuts in interest rates - and the company's efforts to secure prompt payment - have brought down charges to Pounds 279,000 in the half-year to June, compared with Pounds 688,000 in the year to last December. The half-point cut, Mr Fisher hopes, will strengthen the 'patchy and fragile recovery' in the housing market. The company, based in Cheltenham, Gloucestershire, sells principally to small housebuilders and repair and maintenance tradesmen ravaged by the recession. 'We've had terrible experiences with customers going bankrupt,' he says. Bad debts rose from 0.5 per cent of turnover before the recession to 1.7 per cent at its worst. Last year it was down to 1 per cent and has now fallen further. Mr Fisher said: 'We've had to be very fierce, but the small trader is not helped if his debts are allowed to accumulate.' The company, which in 1988 halved its size by selling a do-it-yourself chain, judges that the time is right to expand. This autumn it paid Pounds 2.15m for the Hampshire-based builders merchants division of Phillips & Son (Alton).
