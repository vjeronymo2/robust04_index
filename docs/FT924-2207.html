921214 FT 14 DEC 92 / Banks near to debt deal with Anglo United ANGLO UNITED, owner of the Coalite smokeless fuel business, is close to reaching an agreement with its banks over a debt-for-equity swap. Under the terms of the restructuring plan, the banks would write off Pounds 110m of the group's Pounds 240m debt in favour of taking new ordinary and convertible shares and zero coupon bonds. Anglo, which mostly financed its Pounds 500m takeover of Coalite in 1989 through debt, has recently run into difficulties in repaying borrowings from cash flow and disposals. Last year's mild winter and continuing recession dented profits before tax, which fell from Pounds 15.7m to Pounds 8.3m for the year to end March. The group's disposal programme of its non-core businesses has also been adversely affected by the recession. The restructuring plan involves the banks taking new ordinary shares set at a premium to Anglo's existing share price which on Friday closed at 7 1/2 p. Existing shareholders, who would retain more than half of the enlarged equity, are to be offered warrants at the same price that the new ordinary shares are to be offered to the banks. A financier involved in the deal said: 'There has been some very tough negotiations between the banks and the company. The most bruising aspect has been management's insistence that existing shareholders should retain more than 50 per cent of enlarged share capital.'
