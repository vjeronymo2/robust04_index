Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Bill Wang] [Text] Washington, Jan. 31 (CNA) -- A Republic of China [ROC] delegation, led by Kuomintang Secretary-General Hsu Shui-teh, arrived here Monday [31 January] to attend Thursday's 1994 national prayer breakfast. The delegation was greeted at Dulles International Airport by Ding Mou-shih, Taipei's representative in Washington, and representatives of the Chinese community here. Hsu dismissed reports that he would be a candidate in the forthcoming Taiwan provincial governor election, saying that in his capacity as party secretary-general, he must try to find another more qualified candidate for the job. On Tuesday, the delegation will meet with Haley Barbour, chairman of the Republican National Committee, and congressional leaders, including Senate majority leader George Mitchell (D-Maine), Minority Leader Bob Dole (R-Kan.), Sen. Charles Robb (D-Va.), chairman of the senate committee on Asia and Pacific affairs, and Rep. Gary Ackerman (D-N.Y.) chairman of the house subcommitte on Asia and Pacific affairs. On Wednesday, they will meet with Natale Bellocchi, chairman of the American Institute in Taiwan. They will attend the national prayer breakfast Thursday and later in the day meet with Jim Brady, vice chairman of the Democratic National Committee (DNC), and Debra Delee, DNC vice chairman and executive director. During their three-day stay here, they will also meet with members of the Chinese community in the Washington area.
