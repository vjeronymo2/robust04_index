BFN [Text] Tehran, May 16, IRNA -- Iran's Red Crescent Society (IRCS) has so far sent some 5,000 tons of relief supplies including foodstuff and clothings to Bosnia-Herzegovina since the start of Serbian aggression against Bosnian Muslims, reported the IRCS's public relations department today. The relief supplies, shipped aboard seven airplanes, 25 trucks and a cargo ship, have been sent to the war stricken Bosnian Muslims, the report said. In order to avoid high transportation costs, the IRCS has also purchased relief supplies worth about dlrs 250,000 from Bosnia's neighboring countries and has distributed them among Bosnian Muslims, it said. The IRCS's relief operation in Bosnia-Herzegovina is coordinated by four of its branches, three of which are located in Zagreb, Sarajevo and Slovenia.
