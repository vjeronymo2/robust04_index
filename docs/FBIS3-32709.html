Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Chile through 10 February: The Chilean trade balance registered a $995 million deficit in 1993. Exports totaled $9.215 billion, 7.7 percent less than in the previous year, and imports $10.209 billion, an increase of 10.5 percent compared with 1992. A decline of 16.3 percent in the international copper price was the main reason for the drop. The balance of payment registered a $578 million surplus in 1993 compared with 1992's $2.4984 billion. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 25 Jan 95 pp A1-A12) The Central Bank reported that net international reserves increased more than $500 million in the first half of January. The total reserves now are $10.259 billion. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 25 Jan 94 p 8) On 2 February, the National Statistics Institute, INE, reported that industrial production increased 1.7 percent in December compared with the same month in 1992, and that the cumulative increase was 3.1 percent in 1993, which is lower than the 4.7 percent reported by the Industrial Development Association. The sectors that registered the highest increases were: nonelectrical machines, 29.8 percent; nonmetal mineral products, 16.3 percent; chemicals, 14.1 percent; metal products excluding machinery and equipment, 12.5 percent; iron and steel industry, 7.3 percent; glass manufacturing products, 6.8 percent; and beverages, 6.6 percent. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 3 Feb 94 p 6) The Central Bank reported that Chilean foreign investments totalled $409.7 million in 1993. The major investments were made in Panama with $146.3 million; Argentina, $50.6 million; Peru, $22.5 million; Great Britain, $11.9 million; and Liberia, $11.1 million. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 2 Feb 94 p 8) The INE reported that the unemployment rate was 4.5 percent between October and December 1993, an increase of 0.1 compared with the previous quarter and the same period in 1992. The labor force totalled 5,219,300 people nationwide in the last quarter of 1993, an increase of 4.6 percent compared with the same period in 1992. The number of employed people was 4,985,730, which is 212,430 more new jobs. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 3 Feb 94 p 6) On 2 January, the Foreign Investment Committee chairman, the Chilean economy minister, and the Japanese firm Daiwa Securities, Co. signed a cooperation agreement designed to promote Japanese investments in Chile. One clause establishes that Daiwa will finance one seminar in Japan every year to encourage investments. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 3 Feb 94 p 13) According to a Central Bank report, Chile's foreign debt on 31 December 1993 was $19.179 billion, an increase of $937 million compared to December 1992. According to the report, this increase was caused mainly by the private sector, whose debt increased $1.526 billion. The public sector's debt dropped $589 million. (Santiago Television Nacional de Chile Imagen Internacional in Spanish 1700 GMT 8 Feb 94) On 9 February, the Scandinavian Airlines System transferred its Chilean National Airline, LAN-Chile, shares to a group of Chilean businessmen, thus leaving the control of LAN-Chile in Chilean hands. SAS held 42.5 percent of LAN-Chile shares, which were transferred to Costa Verde and Transnet corporations, which represent the interests of the Cueto family and Chilean businessman Boris Hirmas who lives in Mexico, respectively. Nils Bergston and Luis Davids, SAS representatives on the board of directors, have resigned as a result of this deal and a new board of directors has been named: Jose Luis Moure is the new chairman of the board, Alfredo Etcheverry is vice chairman, and Andres Bianchi Larre, Eric Campana, Dario Calderon, Juan Jose Cueto, Luis Felipe Cueto, Boris Hirmas, and Jaime Sommerville are the other members. The deal reportedly cost $19 million and SAS lost $12 million. (Santiago Radio Cooperativa Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 9 Feb 94)
