Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] On 2 February, the newspaper GRANMA reports that the ranks of the Communist Party of Cuba [PCC] and the Union of Young Communists [UJC] experienced a significant increase in 1993 while maintaining their selective and vanguard nature. The newspaper adds that approximately 50,000 people joined the PCC and approximately 80,000 youths joined the UJC while the attrition rates remained at the level of the past 15 years. The growth in the PCC ranks represents a important political process, particularly in light of the broad participation of the people on the outstanding workers' assemblies. [as heard] The PCC needs to increase its under-30 membership in order to include the views of the young.
