BFN [Text] Yangon [Rangoon], June 15 KYODO -- A large Japanese economic delegation arrived in Yangon Wednesday [15 June] for talks with Myanmarese [Burmese] leaders on promotion of bilateral trade and Japanese investment. The 50-member delegation led by Kazuo Haruna, chairman of Marubeni Corp., represents the powerful Federation of Economic Organizations (Keidanren). Haruna is vice chairman of the federation. During a four-day visit, the mission will confer with Prime Minister Gen. Than Shwe and other government and business leaders on ways for Japan to help promote the country's transition to a market economy, mission members said. The mission members will also discuss Japan's investment in Myanmar and expansion of bilateral trade, they said. It is the first visit to Myanmar by a large-scale Japanese economic delegation since the 1988 military coup and suppression of a democratic movement. The mission includes senior officials of major Japanese corporations, including general trading firms, banks, department stores and heavy machinery makers. The delegation was welcomed upon arrival by Myanmar officials led by Brig. Gen. David Abel, minister for national planning and economic development.
