940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / Arts: Today's Television Given the millions spent on health clubs, vegeburgers and keep-fit videos it is surprising that nobody has previously made a series on the general subject of good health. Now BBC1 is trying. Good Health comes from the same stable as 'The Clothes Show' and today's pilot features underarm deodorants (good health ?), poisons and anti-smoking campaigns (8.00). Since the very beginning Minder has specialised in cinema parodies for its episode titles. Today's, called 'All Quiet On The West End Front', introduces Arfur to the world of corporate hospitality; you know, a tent full of tired men slurping Bollie (8.30 ITV). Actually, for once, it seems to be nephew Ray who might have more success than his uncle. Since Lisa St Aubin de Teran's novels 'Keepers Of The House', about farming in South America, and 'The Slow Train To Milan' about, well, largely about just that, were two of the best novels of the last 12 years, her programme in BBC2's Great Railway Journeys series is particularly tempting. She travels from Santos in Brazil to Santa Cruz in Bolivia (9.30).
