Tuesday May 3, 1994 Part VII Department of Education Fund for Innovation in Education; Notice Inviting Applications for New Awards for Fiscal Year 1994 Federal Register ␣/␣Vol. 59, No. 84␣/␣Tuesday, May 3, 1994␣/␣Notices DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION [CFDA No. 84.215J] Fund for Innovation In Education: Innovation in Education Program_Partnerships for Standards-Based Professional Development of K&hyph;12 Educators; Notice Inviting Applications for New Awards for Fiscal Year 1994 Purpose of program: To award grants to support projects that show promise of identifying and disseminating innovative educational approaches at the preschool, elementary and secondary levels. Eligible applicants: State educational agencies (SEAs), local educational agencies (LEAs), institutions of higher education (IHEs), private schools, and other public and private agencies, organizations and institutions. Deadline for transmittal of applications: July 1, 1994. Deadline for intergovernmental review: September 1, 1994. Applications available: May 13, 1994. Estimated available funds: $6,000,000. Estimated range of awards: $250,000&hyph;$450,000. Estimated average size of awards: $300,000. Estimated number of awards: 20. Note: The Department is not bound by any estimates in this notice. Project period: Up to 36 months. Applicable regulations: The Education Department General Administrative Regulations (EDGAR) 34 CFR Part 74, 75, 77, 79, 80, 81, 82, 85, and 86. Priority: The priority in the notice of final priority for this program, as published elsewhere in this issue of the Federal Register , applies to this competition. In addition, in accordance with EDGAR 34 CFR 75.591, a grantee must cooperate in any evaluation of the program by the Secretary. This program and the priority support the National Education Goals. Selection criteria: In evaluating applications for grants under this competition, the Secretary uses the selection criteria in 34 CFR 75.210(b). Under 34 CFR 75.210(c), the Secretary is authorized to distribute an additional 15 points among the criteria to bring the total to a maximum of 100 points. For this competition, the Secretary distributes the additional points as follows: Plan of operation (34 CFR 75.210(b)(3)). Five additional points are added to this criterion for a possible total of 20 points. Evaluation plan (34 CFR 75.210(b)(6)). Ten additional points are added to this criterion for a possible total of 15 points.
