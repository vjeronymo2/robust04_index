Dadyko, including recorded comment by Konstantin Borovoy, leader of the Economic Freedom Party -- from the "Vesti" newscast] [Text] [Announcer] There was an attempt on the life of Konstantin Borovoy, the leader of the Economic Freedom Party, in Yaroslavl Oblast this morning. His automobile was shot at and then blown up. Fortunately, Borovoy managed to get out of the automobile before the explosion. Sergey Dadyko with the details. [Dadyko] Two hours ago and straight after his arrival from Yaroslavl, Konstantin Borovoy gave the details of the attempt to kill him. Shots were fired into the right-hand side of the windshield of his Mercedes, after which Borovoy, who was at the wheel, veered left into a ditch. A second later he jumped out into the snow, and after that came the explosion. Police have begun an investigation, and in the burned-out automobile found eight bullets from an automatic weapon and grenade fragments. But the question of who fired on him remains unanswered. [Borovoy] You understand, there are not many people like me, if you take an unbiased look at me. I have often spoken out in various ways. You know what I said about the exchanges. I was very outspoken about Lipetsk. But I do not think this was a political event. I reckon it was just small-time thugs.
