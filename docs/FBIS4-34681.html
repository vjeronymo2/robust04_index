BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Guatemalan economic developments monitored through 21 June. Finance Minister Ana de Molina said on 15 June the directors of the Paris Club set September as the deadline for Congress to approve renegotiation of the debt. If the debt is not renegotiated by then, the exemptions and advantages previously negotiated with the Paris Club members will be canceled, and Guatemala's image abroad will deteriorate. (Guatemala City SIGLO VEINTIUNO in Spanish 16 June 94 p 6) Willy Zapata, president of the Bank of Guatemala, said on 15 June there is no reason to believe that a depreciation of the currency will occur at this point or that there will be currency exchange problems during the year, because the mechanisms the Bank of Guatemala has implemented are yielding positive results. (Guatemala City LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 16 June 94 p 4) The Spanish Government has expressed its support for President Ramiro de Leon Carpio's government by accepting the total payment of $115 million for the debt incurred when the plant Celgusa was built. In order to pay this debt, the plant will be sold for U.S. Treasury bonds. (Guatemala City SIGLO VEINTIUNO in Spanish 17 June 94 p 5)
