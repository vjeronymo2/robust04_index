930715 FT 15 JUL 93 / US-North Korean talks 'useful' Senior US and North Korean officials held seven hours of talks in Geneva yesterday in a second attempt to head off confrontation over Pyongyang's refusal to allow international inspection of suspect nuclear sites, writes Frances Williams in Geneva. Mr Robert Gallucci, US assistant secretary of state, called the talks 'useful'. Mr Kang Sok-ju, North Korea's first vice-minister for foreign affairs, said they had been 'intensive, productive and useful.' The two sides meet again tomorrow.
