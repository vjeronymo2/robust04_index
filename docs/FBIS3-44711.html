Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 6 (CNS)--Newly-appointed director of the Civil Aviation Administration of China (CAAC), Mr Chen Guangyi, took up his position today when he held a meeting with senior company officials. He expressed the hope that the CAAC staff would strive to overcome difficulties they may face in order to create a good image of CAAC and build up its reputation. Vice Premier Mr Zou Jiahua announced new senior appointments to the CAAC at the meeting. Mr Chen Guangyi was appointed director with Mr Jiang Zhuping, Mr Yan Zhixiang, Mr Li Zhao, Mr Shen Yuankang and Mr Bao Peide appointed deputy directors. Mr Chen Guangyi said that his appointment as director by the central authorities had been made only ten days before his taking up his duties and he therefore needed time to study the situation. He felt, however, that great expectations were held regarding his appointment by the country's leaders including President Jiang Zemin, Premier Li Peng, Vice Premier Zhu Rongji and Vice Premier Zou Jiahua as well as the country as a whole and elsewhere. Mr Chen said that there were many difficulties and weaknesses regarding aviation in the country and there was much pressure to improve the situation. He and all the CAAC staff would do their best to handle their work, make adjustments where necessary and introduce reforms. Close attention would be paid to air safety and to the maintenance of healthy and rapid development of CAAC.
