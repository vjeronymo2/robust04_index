920309 FT 09 MAR 92 / Construction Contracts: Pounds 40m workload for Amey Group AMEY GROUP has received contracts over the last six weeks worth Pounds 40m. Amey-Hynd, its new Merseyside building operation, has been awarded some Pounds 14m of work; Pounds 7m from Hynd Construction, which went into receivership in January, and a further Pounds 7m of new orders. These orders include Pounds 10m for the construction of sheltered accommodation for local housing associations and a contract for the first phase of the refurbishment of the Liverpool town hall, valued at Pounds 700,000. Amey Building has received some Pounds 2m in south Wales for local health authorities, the Post Office and Barry Town Council and a further Pounds 1.3m in England for the MoD at Thatcham and Barnardo's. Amey Construction has received over Pounds 14m, including Alvechurch bypass (Pounds 5m) in Hertfordshire, bulk fuel installation for the PSA at RAF Waddington (Pounds 3m) and a further Pounds 10m of orders for projects in Berkshire, Wiltshire, Gloucestershire, Yorkshire and Leicestershire. Contracts worth Pounds 1.5m for Thames Water, Welsh Water and Northumbrian Water have been awarded to the Amey-Farr water division and Amey-Sportec has won a Pounds 300,000 award for a synthetic pitch in Birmingham. Amey Mec-Tric has booked Pounds 5m, including Pounds 1.2m at Gloucester Hospital, Pounds 1.2m at Ashdown House, Hastings and Pounds 1.3m for the PSA.
