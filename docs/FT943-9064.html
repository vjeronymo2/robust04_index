940813 FT 13 AUG 94 / UK Company News: Howden expands in S Africa Howden Group, the Glasgow-based engineering company, has expanded its South African operations with the acquisition of Donkin Manufacturing and a number of associated companies for Pounds 4.6m cash. There will be a further cost of about Pounds 500,000 for rationalisation and reorganisation. The businesses being acquired are involved in the design and manufacture of fans and air handling units for the industrial and mining market in South Africa. They had a combined turnover of Pounds 10m in the year to June 30. The activities will be integrated with the existing operations of Howden Group South Africa. Howden also completed the amalgamation of its US subsidiary, Pneu Devices, with FCD Corporation, a Connecticut-based manufacturer of military environmental control systems and pumps, which it acquired in May. The purchase price and the cost of combining the businesses is estimated at Pounds 4.5m. To finance these acquisitions Howden is raising some Pounds 9.56m before expenses by the issue of 10.2m new ordinary stock units of 25p each. The issue has been conditionally placed by Allied Provincial Securities with institutional investors at 93 3/4 p apiece.
