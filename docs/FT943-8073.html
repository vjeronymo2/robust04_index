940819 FT 19 AUG 94 / Grassroot Tories urge move to right Tory party members want more rightwing policies, according to the first returns from a questionnaire sent out to the grassroots by the party's voluntary wing. In response to questions from the Conservative Policy Centre, members said they supported the introduction of identity cards, an overhaul of social security spending, and an end to defence cuts. Mr Frank Dobson, Labour's acting campaign co-ordinator, said the questionnaire revealed more about the Conservative party by the questions it left out than by the ones it included. 'There is no question about why thousands of members are deserting the Tory party, leaving it dependent on its bankers for survival,' he said. Sir George Young, financial secretary to the Treasury, said there was nothing unusual in the canvassing exercise. The Conservative Policy Centre regularly conducted discussions throughout the whole country with party members. He told BBC radio that people felt 'bruised' by the recession, but said: 'All the statistics are pointing in the right direction - output, inflation, unemployment, business confidence. I think in due course that will feed through to people for more confidence.'
