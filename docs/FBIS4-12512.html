BFN [Text] Cairo, 10 Apr (MENA)--A new round of Palestinian-Israeli negotiations on the implementation of autonomy in Gaza and Jericho resumed at noon today in Cairo. The Palestinian side is chaired by Dr. Nabil Sha'th, Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat's political adviser, and Amnon Shahaq, Israeli deputy chief of staff. Responsible Palestinian sources said that this round of negotiations, which will continue until the day after tomorrow, Tuesday, started with a closed meeting between the heads of the Palestinian and the Israeli delegations to set the agenda for the talks. Then, the meetings of the Security, Civilian, and Legal Affairs Committees started. Responsible Palestinian sources told MENA that the Security Affairs Committee will mainly discuss a withdrawal schedule for the Israeli forces from the Gaza Strip and Jericho region. It will also discuss the agenda for the entry of the Palestinian police force. The number of men in this force will range between 9,000 and 10,000 men. This was the reason for the breakdown of the negotiations last week. The same sources said that the Palestinian side proposed the possibility of the Israeli forces' withdrawal during a period ranging between seven days to less than two weeks starting from the date the final agreement is signed. The sources added that the two sides will also discuss a number of other issues dealing with the security arrangements in the autonomy areas, the security of the settlements, and issues pertaining to the security zones around these settlements. These are all issues that were not finalized during the previous rounds of talks. The Palestinian sources added that another issue under discussion is the return of Palestinian deportees expelled by the Israeli authorities after 1967 on the pretext that they were carrying out actions hostile to the Israeli occupation. They are estimated at around 2,200 deportees. Israel has already taken back 200 deportees in several groups since the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles was signed in Washington in September 1993. The Palestinian negotiators are demanding the return of all deportees to their homeland as soon as possible. The Palestinian sources denied that the Palestinian and Israeli negotiators have agreed on a timetable for the release of Palestinian prisoners and detainees from Israeli jails, estimated to number about 10,400 prisoners according to the statistics of the International Committee of the Red Cross. They also denied that the Palestinians have agreed to exclude Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] detainees from the release agreement. They affirmed that the PLO insists on the release of all Palestinian detainees. The Palestinian-Israeli Civilian Affairs Committee is due to discuss in its meeting today the transfer of authorities, services, and utilities to the Palestinian side. An important issue under discussion is the transfer of authority over water resources. The Israelis have said that this issue was not discussed at previous meetings because no Palestinian water experts were present. The Palestinians and Israelis agree that considerable progress has been made in the discussions of the Civilian Affairs Committee over the past several rounds of talks, leaving only a few minor points of disagreements. They pointed out, however, that an overall agreement in the negotiations cannot be attained until the two delegations sort out their security differences. The Palestinian sources pointed out that if no progress is made in the current round of talks, seen to be revolving in a vicious circle, then it will be imperative to hold a meeting of high-level officials from both parties to remove the current obstacles. Consequently, it is likely that a meeting will be held in Cairo next Sunday [17 April] between PLO Executive Committee member Mahmud 'Abbas "Abu-Mazin" and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres. It should be noted that Mahmud 'Abbas is currently in Cairo, closely following the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations while holding talks with Egyptian officials. 'Abbas held a lengthy meeting with the members of the Palestinian delegation to agree on the negotiating method in the current round of talks. The Palestinian sources announced that the Palestinian-Israeli Legal Affairs Committee, one of the committees formed by the Gaza- Jericho meetings, will discuss today the wording of the general agreement and the legal text of the articles already agreed upon by the two delegations.
