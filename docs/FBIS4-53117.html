BFN [Text] Indonesia is postponing indefinitely the second round of peace talks between the Philippine Government and the Moro National Liberation Front [MNLF] representatives scheduled to start today until 23 May in Jakarta. Former Ambassador Manuel Yan, presidential peace adviser, confirmed the delay which is the second since the peace talks were first scheduled last mid-April. According to Yan, Indonesia postponed the meeting due to a number of international commitments. Ambassador Yan denied speculations that the postponement was in reaction to the Philippines' hosting of a human rights conference here on East Timor which Indonesia views with disfavor.
