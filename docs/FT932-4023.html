930611 FT 11 JUN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Five MPs spoke out on Nadir FIVE MPs made written representations to past and present Attorneys-generals concerning former Polly Peck chief Asil Nadir before he jumped bail of Pounds 3.5m last month and fled to northern Cyprus, the government said last night. Attorney-general Sir Nicholas Lyell said in a Commons written reply to Mr David Winnick, Labour MP for Walsall North, that two MPs had made oral representations about the case before Mr Nadir left Britain last month. He did not name the MPs, nor did he reveal the content of their representations concerning Mr Nadir, who faces charges of fraud and false accounting involving his business empire.
