Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chief of the Headquarters on Coordination and Military Cooperation between the CIS states, Serafim Yushkov approved of the decision on strengthening of the Tajik border adopted at the CIS defense ministers' meeting in Moscow on Friday. He suggested that military actions by Afghan Mojahedins and the armed Tajik opposition might become more active in spring. According to Yushkov, the way from Afghanistan to Tajikistan will become easier due to opening of the mountain pass in spring. The CIS defense ministers agreed to carry out the earlier agreements on stabilization of the situation on the Tajik-Afghan border. For instance, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan and Uzbekistan will bring their military contingency to Tajikistan. The decision on the Tajik border is to be confirmed at the regular CIS summit planned for March 31.
