BFN [Text] Croatian President Franjo Tudjman this afternoon received Italian Foreign Minister Antonio Martino, who is paying an official visit to the Republic of Croatia at the head of a delegation of the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the president's office said in a statement. The talks focused on numerous issues relating to bilateral relations between the two states, particularly to the problem of the areas of the Republic of Croatia under Serb occupation and the resolution of the crisis in Bosnia--Herzegovina. Convergence of views was expressed regarding the resolution of some bilateral issues on the basis of friendly relations and mutual interests of both countries. Minister Martino's visit today provides guarantees for better political and economic relations between the two countries to be achieved through the spirit of the best possible understanding, the statement says. Attempts should be made to resolve the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina in a peaceful way. It was stated in this context that steps by the so-called contact group countries and a meeting of the G-7 and Russia which is to take place in Naples at the beginning of next week are hopefully awaited. Hope was also expressed that the Serb occupation of areas of the Republic of Croatia would be resolved in a peaceful way through the reintegration of these areas into the state and legal system of the Republic of Croatia. The Republic of Italy will try to lend all its possible support in this respect by using its influence, the statement says in conclusion.
