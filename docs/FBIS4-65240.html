BFN [Unattributed article: "South Africa-Italy: End to Sanctions Boosts Italian Exports"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, May 9 -- The dropping of trade sanctions against South Africa and the devaluation of the lira are behind the growth in Italy's business with the post-apartheid state, according to statistics for the first 11 months of 1992-93 published by the Institute for the Promotion of Foreign Trade (ICE). Imports show a modest increase of 4.7 percent, up from 2,887 billion lire (1,804 million dollars) to 3,004 billion lire (1,877.5 million dollars), while exports to South Africa show a healthier growth trend of 35.7 percent, going from 679 billion lire (424.38 million dollars) to 921 billion lire (575.63 million dollars). Overall, there was an improvement of 30.6 percent in the Italian balance of trade with South Africa, with predictions for the coming year looking positive especially for Italian exports.
