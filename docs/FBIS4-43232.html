BFN [Text] London, 14 Jun (TANJUG) -- British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd today received a Montenegrin delegation headed by Prime Minister Milo Djukanovic. The Montenegrin delegation was received this afternoon by Douglas Hogg, state minister at the Foreign Office, and visited the British Council. The Montenegrin delegation also includes Deputy Prime Minister Kruno Vukcevic and Foreign Minister Miodrag Lekic. It has been learned from the delegation that the talks centred on the crisis in former Yugoslavia, especially the search for a peaceful solution in Bosnia-Hezcegovina. The hosts asked the Montenegrin delegation to convey their appeal to the Yugoslav side to help in the establishment of peace in Bosnia by influencing the Bosnian Serbs to accept the proposed 51:49 territorial ratio. According to Milo Djukanovic, Montenegro will do all it can to find a peaceful solution as soon as possible. The hosts were also informed about the consequences of the international sanctions on the Montenegrin economy, and the government delegation requested Britain's aid in opening a ferry line to Italy for humanitarian reasons, and to enable the blocked Montenegrin ships to return to their home ports. The Montenegrin delegation will be meeting members of the British-Yugoslav group in the British Parliament tomorrow.
