Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Report by Takis Dhiamandis in Skopje] [Excerpts] Before leaving for Skopje, Dutch European Union [EU] Commissioner Hans Van Den Broek said he is optimistic that a solution to the Athens-Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM] problem can be found. He also said he is concerned about the repercussions of the measures adopted by the Greek Government. [passage omitted] Van Den Broek said that all problems, including the issue of the Greek measures, will be discussed in Brussels and he will brief the EU foreign ministers. Referring to the Greek measures, Van Den Broek said the Greek side's arguments must also be examined and that this issue will be discussed in Brussels as soon as Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou's message is received. He stressed, however, that the solution of this problem will help resolve future tensions. Generally, Van Den Broek said he is grateful for the cooperation he found as well as for the willingness to continue joint efforts to find a solution. [passage omitted]
