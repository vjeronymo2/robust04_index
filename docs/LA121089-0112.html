December 10, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CASTING THE SECOND STONE

When a reviewer asserts that a writer's work "is marred by factual errors," he or she had best scrutinize his or her notes.

In Tom Dardis' piece on my new biography, "Huxley in Hollywood" (Book Review, Oct. 22), he suggests that I have incorrectly married Bertolt Brecht to screenwriter Salka Viertel and misdated Weill's "Johnny Johnson."

Actually, I wrote that Brecht used Viertel's garage as a studio, and that the 1936 "Johnny Johnson" was newly topical in 1939. Dardis, who consistently misspells Viertel's name -- and mine -- should be more careful.

DAVID K. DUNAWAY

ALBUQUERQUE, N.M.
