BFN [Text] Beijing, April 6 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen held talks with visiting Mongolian Minister of External Relations Tserenpiliyn Gombosuren here this afternoon. The two sides exchanged ideas on enhancing bilateral relations and on international issues of common concern. Qian said that there has been satisfactory progress in all fields of Sino-Mongolian ties since the normalization of the two countries' relations in 1989. He noted that Mongolia is an important neighbor of China, with which China is willing to develop good-neighborly ties. Gombosuren said that Mongolia is quite satisfied with the growth of the two countries' relations in recent years, adding that it is a major part of Mongolia's foreign policy to develop friendly relations with China. The two foreign ministers also discussed issues concerning Chinese Premier Li Peng's forthcoming Mongolia visit, which they both agreed would be of significance in the development of bilateral links. After the talks Qian and Gombosuren signed an agreement on cooperation between the two foreign ministries. Officials from the two countries initialed an agreement on preservation and utilization of water along Sino-Mongolian border. Qian also hosted a dinner in honor of Gombosuren and his party, who arrived here earlier today.
