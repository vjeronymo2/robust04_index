BFN [Text] Rafah, 8 May (MENA)--Major General Nasr Yusuf, director general of the Palestinian security force, has stated that the Palestinian 'Ayn Jalut Forces will take over the security tasks in the Palestinian city of Rafah and that the Badr forces arriving from Jordan will take over the security tasks in Jericho. He said that deployment of these 9,000 personnel is scheduled to be completed by 25 May, the date set for the end of the Israeli withdrawal and handing over the security and civilian powers to the Palestinians in Gaza and Jericho. Maj. Gen. Yusuf praised Egypt's preparation and training of the 'Ayn Jalut Forces, which have been deployed in Egypt since the 1960's, to work as infantry personnel and to perform security tasks after the signing of the latest agreement. He noted that there are Palestinian security personnel receiving training on police and security tasks. He said that the first group of these personnel will complete their training courses in July. He praised the facilities that the Central Security Forces in Sinia provided for the Palestinian forces on arrival in Rahah before entering the Gaza Strip. Marwan Kanafani, adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat expressed the Palestinian people's appreciation for the training Egypt, led by President 'Arafat, has offered and is offering to enable the Palestinian security and political departments to take over their tasks. He noted that President Mubarak affirmed to 'Arafat Egypt's readiness to support the Palestinian people in rebuilding the infrastructure which was destroyed by the occupation forces. Maj. Gen. Muhammad al-Nashshar, commander of the Central Security Forces in Sinai, visited the camp where the Palestinian security forces were received in Rafah and met with Maj. Gen. Nasr Yusuf and Marwan Kanafani. He made a speech in which he congratulated the Palestinians on taking power in Gaza and Jericho and expressed his hope that they would take power in the entire West Bank shortly. Maj. Gen. Yusuf expressed the Palestinian forces' thanks for their brothers in Egypt and stressed that Egypt has never abandoned its obligations toward Palestine and its people.
