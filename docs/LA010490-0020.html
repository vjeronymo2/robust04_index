January 4, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

AFFIRMATIVE ACTION AT SCHOOLS

Ian Marantz's misinformed stance on University of California admissions policies (Letters, Dec. 27) sadly represents the widespread belief that Asian-Americans are considered "affirmative action" students.

At UC Irvine and at all UC campuses, only African-American, Native American and Latino applicants fall under the affirmative action program.

As an intermediate step toward an equal-opportunity society, affirmative action aims to "balance the ethnic imbalances" that institutions have created to ensure that under-represented students are not rejected admission because of their color.

One reason Asian-Americans are well represented at UC Irvine and also ineligible for affirmative action is their accomplishment in proving their academic worth despite all the discriminatory barriers that still exist in society.

But now the UC system is turning its back on meritocracy by refusing admission to qualified Asian-American students.

Marantz claims that UC Irvine discriminates against white Americans while reserving spots for Asian-Americans. How can that be when white and Asian students are theoretically admitted under just one criterion -- merit? Yet current admission policies prove this untrue.

Unfortunately for Asians, UC admissions officials are fusing personal merit and skin color. Regrettably, the latter wins out in the dominant society, even today.

UC admissions now overtly embraces discrimination, once again, in favor of whites. By reserving places for white students while rejecting qualified (sometimes more qualified) Asian applicants, the university is throwing meritocracy out the window.

Let's not revert to the discriminatory sins. Instead, the aim should be a color-blind society based upon individual merit.

JANET OTSUKI

Fullerton
