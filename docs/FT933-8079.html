930818 FT 18 AUG 93 / Monopolies Reports on British Gas: Countdown to competition -Timetable 1986: British Gas privatised as a single entity and monopoly supplier to 18m households. 1988: MMC asked to examine the reasons for the slow development of competition in the industrial supply market. April 1991: British Gas agrees tough pricing formula with Ofgas, with the threat of an MMC investigation if it refuses. Formula restricts price rises to 5 percentage points below inflation for five years. October 1991: Office of Fair Trading calls for removal of British Gas monopoly and separation of pipeline and marketing operations. Part of supply market to small businesses opened to competition. December 1991: British Gas agrees with OFT to halve its share of the industrial market by 1995 and to set up 'Chinese walls' between its pipelines division and other operations. May 1992: Ofgas forces British Gas to cut household prices. July 1992: Ofgas and British Gas clash over rate of return its pipelines division can earn. Sir James McKinnon, head of Ofgas, threatens MMC referal. August 1992: Mr Cedric Brown appointed British Gas chief executive. The day before taking over he asks for MMC review of all British Gas's business. Sir James counters by referring British Gas to the MMC over the return on its pipelines business. March 1993: Ofgas tells MMC that British Gas should be broken into 12 regional companies with a separate pipelines network and gas-purchasing arm. The MMC's proposed timetable for restructuring is: March 1994: Transportation and storage to be separate unit of British Gas. April 1994: Revision of tariff formula from RPI minus 5 points to RPI minus 4 points. March 31 1997: British Gas to have been divested of trading activities. Monopoly threshold down to 1,500 therms. 2000-2002: Total removal of tariff market monopoly.
