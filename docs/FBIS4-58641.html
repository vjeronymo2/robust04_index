BFN [Text] Japanese Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa and his accompanying delegation arrived in Amman today on a two-day visit to the country; they came via the King Husayn Bridge. During the visit, Kakizawa will meet with a number of senior officials. In statements to the JORDANIAN NEWS AGENCY, Kakizawa said that Japan considers Jordan an important party to the peace process in the region. He added that the purpose of his visit to Jordan is to discuss the situation in the region, in general, and the possibility of Japan's participation in the peace process. He was received by Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs, the head of the bridge security detachment, and the Japanese ambassador in Amman.
