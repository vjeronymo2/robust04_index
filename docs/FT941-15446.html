940114 FT 14 JAN 94 / Commodities and Agriculture: French farmers seek flexibility on trade France's farmers must be given the flexibility by their own government and the European Union to seek new outlets for products whose export would otherwise be constrained by last month's settlement of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, Mr Luc Guyau, president of FNSEA, the country's largest farm union, said yesterday. Farmers should be left free of bureaucratic red tape to upgrade the quality of food products that could be exported without subsidy and therefore without Gatt constraints, Mr Guyau said. The Paris and Brussels authorities should also encourage increased industrial use of crops to make 'biocarburant' fuel, as well as rewarding farmers for environmental works like drainage and dykes, which the current floods in many parts of France had proved inadequate. Mr Guyau's first press conference of the new year was marked by his relatively smooth acquiescence in the Gatt deal on terms that until late last year the FNSEA had denounced as quite unacceptable. 'We are very conscious that we did not get all we hoped for,' he said, 'particularly in terms of a rebalancing of export restrictions with similar restrictions on imports, and of dealing with monetary fluctuations of the dollar and within Europe. 'But we have a feeling of pride about our combat over the seven years (of the Gatt negotiations),' he added.
