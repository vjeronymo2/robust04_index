Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] The Council of Jewish Settlements in Judaea, Samaria, and Gaza which convened today to discuss the security situation has announced its objection to the expressions of joy over the murder in Hebron. This is included in a series of decisions adopted by the heads of the council during their meeting. Our correspondent Ya'el Ayalon reports that it was further decided that in the future the council will hold an extensive discussion about the attitude toward the Kakh movement. The council has also decided to launch an information campaign within the settlements so as to curb the activities of Kakh members. The first in a series of decisions made by the council expresses deep sorrow and shock about the murder in Hebron. The council heads also categorically condemn any harm inflicted on innocent people, share in the grief of the victims' families, and repudiate the expressions of joy regarding the murder. The other decisions adopted by the council takes a negative view of the measures the government adopted yesterday.
