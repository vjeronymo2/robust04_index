Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev -- Ukrainian Deputy Prime Minister Valentyn Landyk, has informed a UNIAN correspondent that a telephone conversation took place on 22 February between Acting Prime Minister Yuhym Zvyahilskyy and representatives of the Turkmen Government on the matter of the suspension of the gas supply from Turkmenistan to Ukraine. Following the Ukrainian side's reasoning that: "No one has done this before. If God has given you gas, it is not worth abusing this," a preliminary accord was reached on selling 60,000 tonnes of wheat to Turkmenistan to credit the debt and payment by Ukraine of $50 million of the 150 million debt. After this, it is likely that the supply of gas to Ukraine will be resumed.
