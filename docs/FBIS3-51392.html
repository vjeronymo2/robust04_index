Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] The national congress of the Group of the Great North [Collectif du Grand North] will begin today. Herve Leveille, secretary general of this group, says that all sectors involved in the crisis have failed and that the people must take initiatives to solve the crisis. Leveille invites the Haitians, among others, to participate in this congress, which will center on the country's situation, a congress which can ease the crisis. A total of 123 citizens from the nine departments are already willing to participate in this congress, according to Leveille. Let us listen to his statements. [sentence as heard] We are now going to talk about the postponing of the national conference instead. The technical office of the Committee of Actions for National Reconciliation [Comite d'Actions pour la Reconciliation Nationale, CARENA] advises that the national conference will now take place on 6 and 7 February in accordance with the wishes of the various sectors of national life that will participate in this conference. It takes this opportunity to invite the various sectors of national life to participate in the sessions of the national conference preparatory work that will take place at the Holiday Inn on 20 January with the following sectors: the Catholic Church, the Protestant Church, voodoo members, employers, workers, the people's sector, and the socioprofessional sector. The date of 21 January will be devoted to political parties, the executive power, the Senate of the Republic, and the Chamber of Deputies. The Court of Cassation, the Haitian Armed Forces, and the Supreme Audit Court will participate as observers in the working meetings that will go from 1000 to 1300 and from 1500 to 1800. The technical office of CARENA takes this opportunity to renew the expression of its patriotic greetings. This note is signed by Fritz Buissereth, coordinator in charge of the technical office. He is the new CARENA spokesman. The note is also signed by Ady Jeangardy, coordinator in charge of communications, and by Bruny Raphael, coordinator in charge of administration.
