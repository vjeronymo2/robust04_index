931026 FT 26 OCT 93 / Hands on Virginia Bottomley, health secretary, yesterday joined health authorities and trusts at a conference in London where she urged them to involve the public more in making decisions about the shape of local health services, John Willman writes. Mrs Bottomley said that abolition of the regional health authorities, announced last week, would improve the accountability of health service managers to parliament. But the devolution of management responsibilities to authorities and trusts should improve accountability downwards. 'Health authorities and trusts must engage the public's interest in the issues they face,' she said. 'They must actively encourage public participation in the decision-making process.'
