BFN [Text] The National Security Council [NSC] is scheduled to meet tomorrow under President Suleyman Demirel. On the agenda of the meeting is the [Kurdish] migration from Turkey to northern Iraq, and the PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] camps in Nakhichevan. According to available information, the NSC will discuss the situation of those who migrated from some villages of Turkey near the Iraqi border into northern Iraq. At present there are 5,000 people in northern Iraq who came from Turkey. Kanal-6 had already reported the PKK's role in bringing about the migration. The NSC will also discuss the PKK camps in Nakhichevan. The PKK camps in Nakhichevan have already been tracked down. There was talk in Ankara of the possibility of launching a military operation against the region where the camps are located.
