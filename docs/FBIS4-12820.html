BFN [Text] Lahore, April 14, IRNA -- A senior Majlis deputy said here Wednesday that the Islamic Iran would not stand the massacre of Muslims and pressures against them in any part of the world including Kashmir. Vice-Chairman of Majlis foreign policy commission Mohammad Javad Larijani who is currently accompanying Majlis Speaker 'Ali Akbar Nateq-Nuri in his tour of Pakistan, told IRNA that one of the valuable outcomes of Tehran-Islamabad talks was the speaker's proposal for the formation of ECO [Economic Cooperation Organization] inter-parliamentary group. Representatives have been appointed by the two countries to follow up agreements reached between the two sides, Larijani noted, adding "we hope that the organization would be formed by the next year". Voicing Iran's readiness to cooperate in bringing an end to Kashmir problem, the Iranian parliamentarian called for negotiations between the belligerent parties to settle the dispute. The Islamic Republic of Iran believes that Kashmiri people should determine their own fate, Larijani remarked. Underlining geopolitical and economic importance of the two countries, Larijani noted that with a total population of 200 million and their access to different parts of Asia, specially the Indian Ocean, Gulf of Oman and the Persian Gulf, Iran and Pakistan, should be regarded as core for any regional arrangement, Larijani concluded.
