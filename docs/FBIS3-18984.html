Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Strasbourg, March 10 (AFP) -- The European Parliament Thursday [10 March] called on EU [European Union] member states to send more troops to war-torn Bosnia-Herzegovina to monitor a ceasefire in Sarajevo and have it extended to other regions "NATO's riposte to Serb air forces which violated the Bosnian airspace has obviously had a salutary effect," a resolution adopted by European Union legislators said, referring to the downing by NATO fighters of four Serb warplanes over Bosnia last month. The Parliament resolution said however that "the military aggression is being pursued and that Croats as well as Serbs continue to perpetrate atrocities in Bosnia-Hercegovina." Parliament called on European Union ministers and the United Nations to take the necessary steps to immediately stop Serb militias from attacking Moslem-populated Srebrenica, Zepa, Maglaj, Tuzla and Bihac. It also warned the Croatian government of sanctions if Zagreb continued to maintain troops in Bosnia-Herzegovina.
