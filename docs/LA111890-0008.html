November 18, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

My Darling Clementine (Channel 28 Sunday at 2 p.m.) is John Ford's memorable 1946 take on the Gunfight at the O.K. Corral, starring Henry Fonda as Wyatt Earp and Victor Mature as Doc Holliday.

Moonstruck (CBS Sunday at 9 p.m.): One of the juiciest romantic comedies of the '80s, this is the film that provided Cher the Oscar-winning role that let her comic sensibilities out for a romp as a young widow whose dull life suddenly shifts 180 degrees under the spell of an extraordinarily full moon.

Featuring Nicolas Cage, Danny Aiello and Olympia Dukakis.

Lady for a Day (AMC Thursday at noon) is the 1933 Frank Capra heart-tugger based on a Damon Runyon story about an apple peddler (May Robson) turned into a lady by a gangster (Warren William) to impress the daughter who never has known her; Capra remade it in 1961 with Bette Davis as "A Pocketful of Miracles."
