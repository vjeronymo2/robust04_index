Language: German Article Type:BFN [APA report: "More Foreigners Turned Back at the Border"] [Text] Vienna -- Austria's borders last year were largely closed: The number of people who were turned back, expelled, or deported from Austria increased greatly in 1993. According to the Interior Ministry, this is proof of the effectiveness of pollice measures regarding aliens. A total of 160,394 people were turned back at the border, approximately 49,000 more than in 1992. The number of expulsions rose from 1,723 to 3,846. The number of deportations -- 8,857 (plus 1,501) -- almost reached the level of 1991. Only the number of people who were sent back to the country where they were previously fell from 8,221 to 5,437. This refers to people who enter the country illegally and are sent back to the country where they were previously within 14 days. The reason the number decreased by so much is mainly because the number of illegal border crossings recently has been steadily declining. Romanians, in particular, who used to attempt to cross the border illegally in relatively large numbers are not entering the country illegally as often anymore. The reason for this is relatively simple: Hungarian checks are much stricter now, and the route through the former Yugoslavia is largely blocked by the war in Bosnia.
