Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Mar. 7 (YONHAP) -- President Kim Yong-sam received a telephone call from Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa on Monday afternoon, during which the Japanese premier congratulated the president on the recent passage of political reform bills through the National Assembly. Chongwadae [presidential offices] spokesman Chu Ton-sik said that in the telephone conversation that lasted about 10 minutes from 5:10 P.M., Prime Minister Hosokawa said he was extending deep respect to President Kim for the leadership and determination he displayed before the passage of the bills. President Kim said to Hosokawa he had emphasized on every opportunities that no national advancement can be attained without political reforms. The spokesman said the president also expressed the hope that current trade frictions between Japan and the United States will be resolved in an instructive direction. Hosokawa, according to Chu, said that the two countries have diverse problems of their own and he would like to exchange opinions on these issues with President Kim during his upcoming visit to Japan. This was the fourth telephone conversation between Kim and Hosokawa since President Kim called the Japanese premier on last Aug. 9 to congratulate him on his inauguration.
