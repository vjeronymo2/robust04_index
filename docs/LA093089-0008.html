September 30, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

DISRESPECT FOR LAWYERS AND THE COURTS

Hay's column eulogizes lawyers without revealing the ways they are now burdening society.

Along with their brothers in the legislatures, lawyers and judges have been subtle co-conspirators in guaranteeing that the legal role shall be a larger than necessary one, with benefits shared greedily.

Lawyers, having virtual control of government (where laws and regulations are written en masse) and the courts, have created both a self-serving maze and an operational monopoly so costly and time-consuming that they deserve the disrespect we show them.

Everyone should visit the courts and see the travesty that exists: inefficiency, delays and postponements, callousness as to costs and time imposed on litigants, and built-in procedures beneficial to lawyers' incomes at others' expense.

If they were as good for society as Hay claims, why don't lawyers streamline the entire legal process and relinquish minor procedures that can easily be handled by anyone or, in more complicated matters, by paralegals? The answer, of course, is that they would sacrifice income.

RUSSELL T. CONNORS

Pismo Beach
