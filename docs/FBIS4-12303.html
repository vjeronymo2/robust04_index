BFN [Text] Pakistan has refuted press reports as totally baseless suggesting that the United States has proposed to Islamabad to freeze the Kashmir issue. A Foreign Office spokesman briefing newsmen in Islamabad today said Pakistan has received neither any paper nor any nonpaper on this subject. [sentence as heard] To a question, the spokesman said Pakistan resents deeply the proliferation of false and defamatory statements from India alleging the involvement of Pakistan's Inter Service Intelligence in the internal affairs of India and other neighboring states. He said Pakistan Government rejects categorically statements made in this connection by the chief minister of Assam, director general of its police, and the article in the Indian newspaper THE INDEPENDENT. To another question, the spokesman said the entire spectrum of Pakistan-U.S. relations would come under review during the three-day visit of the U.S. deputy secretary of state, Mr. Strobe Talbott, beginning from Friday [8 April]. The spokesman said the agenda of talks is very broad and we are prepared to discuss all the issues faced.
