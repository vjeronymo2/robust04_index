940208 FT 08 FEB 94 / Arts: Today's Television Yet again the evening's main offers are current affairs programmes largely concerned with man's inhumanity to man. Assignment reports on Benazir Bhutto in Pakistan who faces political and sexual prejudice (7.45 BBC2). In Undercover Britain two Aids sufferers use hidden cameras to expose con-men preying on the terminally ill (9.00 C4). 40 Minutes illustrates the difficulties faced by four former soldiers in finding jobs. Network First tells of racial segregation in the US Air Force during World War 2 (10.40 ITV). Where to go for relief? How Do They Do That? on BBC1 is a new 'infotainment' programme presented by Desmond Lynam which is intended to elicit 'Well would you believe it' responses (8.00). Food And Drink is usually good for a giggle, if only for Jilly Goolden's frocks which are as refreshing as her wine taster's adjectives (8.30 BBC2). And Quantum Leap is one of those fantasy series which, contrary to popular mythology (Americans are only good at knock-down drag-out action drama) are better done in the US than in the UK (9.00 BBC2).
