921003 FT 03 OCT 92 / Russia views currency curbs THE RUSSIAN government yesterday said it would soon require state enterprises to sell all their hard currency earnings to the state, Reuters reports from Moscow. State enterprises only have to sell half their hard currency earnings to the state - at the going market rate - at present. Mr Sergei Glazyev, the first deputy foreign trade minister, said firms had taken advantage of this ruling to stash away Dollars 3.5bn in foreign bank accounts. The rouble fell to a record low of 309 to the dollar on Thursday in Moscow trading.
