920725 FT 25 JUL 92 / UK Company News: J&J Dyson hit by exceptionals AFTER Pounds 187,000 for exceptional costs incurred in production rationalisation, pre-tax profit at J&J Dyson dropped 21 per cent in the year ended March 31 1992, from Pounds 1.43m to Pounds 1.13m. Before the exceptionals, this group of refractory makers, motor dealers and builders' merchants held its profit to within 6.5 per cent of the previous year, on turnover showing a slight improvement to Pounds 49.5m. Earnings per share were 6.54p (7.4p) and the dividend is again 5p, the final being 3p. Refractories contributed Pounds 913,000 (Pounds 1.05m) to profit, motor vehicles and trailers Pounds 93,000 (Pounds 76,000) and merchanting Pounds 127,000 (Pounds 84,000). Net interest received was Pounds 235,000 (Pounds 268,000). There was a Pounds 285,000 profit on sale of fixed assets, treated as an extraordinary credit.
