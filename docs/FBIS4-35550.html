BFN [Text] Cairo, June 2, IRNA -- Iranian Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati here Wednesday [1 June] discussed bilateral, regional and international issues in separate meetings with his Kuwaiti, Syrian and Indian counterparts. Velayati, who is here to attend the four-day Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) ministerial meeting, and his Kuwaiti counterpart, Shaykh sabah al-Ahmad al-Sabah, in a meeting stressed the importance of bilateral relations as well as speeding up of the formation of Iran-Kuwait joint economic commission. Describing the recent visit of a group of Kuwati University professors and journalists to Tehran as "positive", Velayati said that it could pave the way for further enhancing mutual understanding between the two countries. He also referred to the Kuwait-Iraq relations and expressed hope that the two can find peaceful ways to solve their border disputes by preventing further interference of the foreign powers in the region. Pointing out to the latest developments in Kuwait-Iraq relations, Al-Sabah stressed the need for full implementation of the U.N. Security Council's resolutions on Iraq. He also expressed willingness to visit Iran in a near future to explore avenues for further promotion of bilateral ties. Meanwhile, in another meeting Velayati and his Syrian counterpart Faruq al-Shar' stressed the need for holding the tripartite meeting by Iran, Syria and Turkey due to be held in Damascus next summer. The two sides also exchanged views on the issue of Palestine and the expansionist policies of the Zionist regime. Earlier in the day, Velayati met with his Indian counterpart Dinesh Singh and discussed Tehran-New Delhi relations, latest developments in Kashmir and the issues pertaining the NAM meeting. Velayati arrived here last Monday heading a ranking delegation.
