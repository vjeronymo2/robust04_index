920902 FT 02 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Belling's secured creditors likely to be paid in full Secured and preferential creditors of Belling, the electric cooker manufacturer which went into receivership with debts of Pounds 28m in May, are likely to be paid in full, receivers from KPMG Peat Marwick told a creditors' meeting. Mr Paul Jeffery, joint administrative receiver, said there would be a surplus, but the likely level of dividend for unsecured creditors was not yet known. Realisations to date total Pounds 6.8m. The Belling business and assets, excluding two factory sites in Enfield, north London, and Burnley, Lancashire, were sold to Dimplex (UK) for Pounds 5.4m. The Burnley site was sold to directors of Northern Wire.
