Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, January 6 (ATA) -- The region of Ulcinj, with eighty per cent Albanians, is now an objective of the Serbian headquarters. According to a report in newspaper RILINDJA on January 5, the Valdanos Bay, known for its beauty and riches along the Adriatic Sea, will be transformed in a military base. In a recent press conference, the commander of the Serbian and Montenegrin military-naval forces, Mr. Dojcilo Isakovic, cited by the pro-government Montenegrin newspaper POBJEDA spoke about creation of a naval base in the bay of Valdanos, near Ulcinj, which will cover an area of 340 hectares. Reacting against the military plans of Belgrade, the secretary of the party of the Liberal League of Montenegro, Mr. Miodrag Burzan, stressed that during the time of the former Yugoslavia, Montenegro possessed 1/10 of the seashore. Now, seventy-eighty per cent of the naval forces is concentrated in this place. This movement of the naval forces in the seashore of Montenegro threatens in transforming this republic in a military base, stressed Burzan, adding that such projects, which are being planned even for the regions around the lake of Shkoder, aim at transforming Montenegro in a concentrated military camp.
