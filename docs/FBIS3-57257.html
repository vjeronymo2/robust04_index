Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Azerbaijani Defense Ministry has dismissed the reports that an Azerbaijani combat jet was shot down near Khankendi (Stepanakert) on January 23. The Azerbaijani mission in Moscow told Interfax today that the information was "not true to life." The mission also dismissed the reports about a large-scale offensive launched by Armenian forces. At the same time, the press service of the Nagorno-Karabakh army confirmed to Interfax that the jet was shot down saying that its leftovers had been found. A special group left for the place where the jet fell into the ground to look for the pilot or his body and verify the type of aircraft. According to preliminary information, it was an assault plane Sukhoi-25. The press service said that two Azerbaijani jets trespassed the air border of Nagorno-Karabakh again at 13:30 today. According to preliminary information, one of the jets dropped several bombs near Stepanakert. The effects of the bombing are being verified. According to sources in Stepanakert, fighting continued on a number of sections of the front line today. Nagorno-Karabakh forces repelled Azerbaijani attacks between the district centers of Askeran and Agdam and in the north of the Martuni district of Azerbaijan. Having lost 1 tank and up to 15 troops, Azerbaijani units had to retreat to their positions. Several Nagorno-Karabakh servicemen were wounded, according to the press service.
