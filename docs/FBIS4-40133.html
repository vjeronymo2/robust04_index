BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Velichko] [Text] Moscow June 14 TASS -- President Boris Yeltsin, before departure for Blagoveshchensk by air, told journalists on Tuesday that an understanding to "link the convocation of an international conference and the application of possible stage-by-stage sanctions against North Korea" had been reached during his long conversation with President Clinton on Monday night. Apart from discussing the North Korean nuclear issue, the two presidents also examined some aspects of Boris Yeltsin's likely official visit to America in autumn. "Judging by the prospective time-frame, the trip will turn out well. Besides, it is planned that I will deliver a programmatic address at the U.N. General Assembly", Yeltsin remarked. In this connection, Yeltsin said that Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher are to meet in Brussels on June 15-16 to specify details of the forthcoming visit. As far as the tour of Blagoveshchensk and the Republic of Tuva is concerned, Yeltsin voiced hope that foreign investments would be drawn to those areas, primarily from China, Japan, North and South Korea. "This may help those regions in earnest," he emphasised.
