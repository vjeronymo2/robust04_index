930806 FT 06 AUG 93 / Ship survey and certification work to be contracted out ABOUT 25 per cent of the ship survey and certification work undertaken by the Surveyor General's Organisation of the Department of Transport is to be contracted out to the private sector, John Willman writes. The move was announced yesterday by Lord Caithness, minister for shipping. The organisation regulates the safety and pollution prevention requirements for ships and their equipment. Consultation with the shipping industry revealed considerable resistance to more contracting out. Critics believed such a move might detract from the independent role of the organisation and jeopardise marine safety. The organisation will retain direct involvement in survey and certification of passenger certificates. It will also remain involved in all surveys of items directly related to safety of passengers and crew.
