Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Berlin (DPA) -- The Western Group of Russian Forces has so far withdrawn 90 percent of its troops from eastern Germany. The withdrawal schedule for last year was therefore fully adhered to, Colonel General Matvey Burlakov, commander in chief [of the Western Group of Forces], told journalists in Berlin today. The last soldier will return to Russia 31 August 1994 as planned. Official functions marking the farewell to the troops are to take place in the capital at the beginning of August. President Boris Yeltsin has promised to attend. According to Burlakov, the remaining 68,000 personnel, including 32,000 servicemen and officers, as well as 17,500 items of equipment, will be withdrawn this year. Not a single piece of ammunition will remain in Germany. In contrast to the withdrawal of troops, only 65 percent of program for building apartments for the servicemen and their families returning home has been completed, Burlakov said, and 40-45 percent of families are still without an apartment. Another 25,000 apartments must be built this year with German funding and 22,000 with Russian funding. However, Burlakov doubts whether the program will be adhered to. Germany has promised to contribute 8 billion marks.
