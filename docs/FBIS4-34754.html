BFN [Text] Havana, 21 Jun (PL) -- The Cuban Government has approved regulations to Law Decree No. 149, which covers the confiscation of goods and income obtained through illegal enrichment. A Council of Ministers Executive Committee agreement establishes the method to be followed in such cases. Already, several dozen people have been found to be involved in illegal activities regarding the obtainment of goods. Files on these individuals have been opened. According to information published by GRANMA today, the regulations detail the conditions established for preliminary investigations, the resources available for the accused, the procedures to review cases, the preventive measures to avoid the distribution of stolen goods, and the final destination of stolen goods. The document outlines the procedures to be practiced by prosecutors, which include the possibility of searching the houses of suspects, their relatives, or third parties, as well as conducting necessary audits, inventories, and appraisals. After the reform resource and the review procedure, which are to be presented to Finance and Price Minister, and the final ruling on the case, no other process will be established in the administrative or judicial sectors. Lastly, the regulations clarify that the organizations in charge will be responsible for the preservation, custody, and distribution of goods and income that are subject to preventive or confiscatory measures.
