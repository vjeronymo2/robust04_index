BFN [Text] Cairo, 5 May (MENA)--Palestinian negotiator Hasan 'Usfur has said that 1,000 Palestinian police will enter Gaza and Jericho on Sunday [8 May]. The force will begin to take over the camps from which the Israeli Army will withdraw, he said. 'Usfur told MENA today that 22 Palestinian officers representing the Palestinian side in the Palestinian-Israeli Coordination and Cooperation Committee arrived in Gaza on Tuesday to prepare for the entry of the Palestinian police force. He said 6,000 members of the force will enter the self-rule areas in the next two weeks. It was agreed, he explained, that the Palestinian police force would consist of 9,000 personnel, 7,000 of which will come from outside the occupied territories and 2,000 from inside the self-rule areas. 'Usfur said that Israel yesterday released 850 Palestinian prisoners. He said the rest of the 5,000 prisoners whose release was agreed upon will be freed in batches over the next five weeks.
