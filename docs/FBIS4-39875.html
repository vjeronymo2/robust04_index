BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov and Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Tbilisi, 11 Jun -- Russia's Defense Minister Pavel Grachev has successfully completed his working visit to Tbilisi. He succeeded in obtaining the agreement of the warring Georgian and Abkhaz sides on the start in the near future of a peacekeeping operation in the conflict zone. Taking part in the festive send-off ceremony were Georgia's Prime Minister Otar Patsatsiya, Parliament Speaker Vakhtang Goguadze, the command of the Group of Russian Forces in the Transcaucasus, Russia's ambassador to Georgia Vladimir Zemskiy, and other officials. In the evening prior to his departure Grachev met with his Georgian counterpart Lieutenant-General Vardiko Nadibaidze. ITAR- TASS correspondents have learned that at this meeting questions of the development and strengthening of interaction and cooperation between the defense departments of Russia and Georgia were discussed, with the conversation being of a friendly and constructive nature. Before his departure by plane for Baku early in the morning, Grachev met with generals and officers of the staff of the Group of Russian Forces in the Transcaucasus. The meeting discussed questions of strengthening combat training, social protection for personnel and members of their families, options for the change-over by Russian troops stationed in Georgia to the principle of occupying bases. [perekhod no bazovyy printsip] In Baku, as is expected, Grachev will meet with President Geydar Aliyev and will discuss the possibilities for a settlement of the Azerbaijani-Armenian conflicts with the participation of Russian peacekeeping forces.
