March 29, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

CHILD INTERPRETERS

I believe it is shortsighted for Spanish-speaking immigrants to make their children interpret for them ("Littlest Interpreters," March 1).

The immigrants who lived in my Brooklyn, N.Y., neighborhood came from all over Europe. They knew they had to learn English, and fast, if they wanted to enjoy the benefits of living in their adopted country.

They didn't claim they were being discriminated against because their children were taught in English only, or because election ballots were printed only in English.

I'll probably be stripped of my liberal credentials for my views, but so be it!

SUZANNE A. CHAPMAN

Orange
