BFN [Text] At a meeting of the first subcommittee of the House of Representatives Budget Committee held on the morning of 7 June, Chief Cabinet Secretary Hiroshi Kumagai stated his views on how to proceed with future administrative reform, saying: "We will draw an outline for the immediate future as early as possible by forming an effective deliberation mechanism to work closely with the tax reform committee (of the ruling parties)." He added: "In the long-term, a five-year plan will be formulated." Kumagai's remarks indicated the government plans to draw up a five-year administrative reform plan, and deal with it through the implementation of a two-tier package consisting of short and mid-to-long-term plans. He was replying to a question asked by Noboru Usami from Sakigake [Harbinger].
