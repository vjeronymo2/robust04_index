930920 FT 20 SEP 93 / Tories slump in Scots opinion poll SUPPORT for the Conservatives in Scotland has fallen to its lowest figure on record, according to a poll in The Scotsman today. In a survey of 1,004 voters only 13 per cent backed the Tories compared with 26 per cent at the 1992 election. Suport for Labour has risen to 53 per cent, up 14 per cent on the 1992 figure. The others were: Scottish National Party 23 per cent (up 2 on 1992); and Liberal Democrats 10 per cent (down 3). The survey was carried out in 52 Scottish constituencies on September 13-14. Asked about party leaders, only 15 per cent said they were satisfied with Mr John Major. Labour leader Mr John Smith won support from 45 per cent, Liberal Democrat leader Mr Paddy Ashdown 42 per cent, and SNP leader Mr Alex Salmond 33 per cent.
