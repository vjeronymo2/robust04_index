Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 6 (XINHUA) -- The total value of China's electronics industry in 1993 reached a record 135 billion yuan, a hefty increase of 24 percent over 1992. The latest statistics from the Ministry of the Electronics Industry show that the total sales of the industry in 1993 came to 98 billion yuan-worth, an increase of 34 percent over 1992, while tax paid reached 7.2 billion yuan, a rise of 12 percent. According to the ministry, products such as SPC [expansion not given] exchanges, telephones, fax machines, microcomputers and mobile communication products saw an average increase of more than 50 percent last year. During the same period, further improvements have been made in the structure of products, while software and information services have undergone rapid development. This development trend has become even more obvious in the coastal areas, such as Tianjin and Shanghai cities, and Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Fujian, Shandong and Guangdong Provinces, where the value of the electronics industry accounted for 62.7 percent of the country's total last year. Shandong ranked first in China in terms of the development of its electronics industry, the ministry said. The economic and technological co-operation between China's electronics industry and its foreign counterparts continues to expand, according to the ministry; the total export value of the industry last year increased by nearly 20 percent compared to the previous year. Meanwhile, the number of foreign-funded major electronics projects has increased rapidly and they have become a major force in the industry, the ministry added.
