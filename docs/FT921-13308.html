920122 FT 22 JAN 92 / Appointments: Deriving a client service As one of the few growth businesses left in the City, derivatives (futures and options) trading and broking seems to be spawning a host of boutiques to specialise in explaining this arcane corner of the financial world. One of these, the 12-month-old Derivative Securities Ltd, whose corporate logo reads delta sigma lambda, has hired two research experts from a competitor in order to bolster its private client service. Ian Rankine, 50, and Martin Price, 41, come from Anglo Pierson Options (APO), part of the ABN Amro group; APO's move towards an execution-only service is understood to have left Rankine and Price less than fully stretched. DSL was set up by Quentin Burrows, 39, who had headed the traded options desk at NatWest stockbrokers; Oliver Gillie, 51, who came from the metals business via a spell running his own estate agency concern; and Patrick Thompson, 30, who had worked for the London Traded Options Market. They reckon wealthy private clients are more interested in options and futures than the exchanges often give them credit for in their marketing strategies.
