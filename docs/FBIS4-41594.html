BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Sukhumi, 24 Jun -- Subunits of the Russian peacekeeping forces today occupied positions on the line of the Georgian- Abkhazian confrontation and took checkpoints and observation points under guard. The bulk of the peacekeeping forces consists of subunits of the airborne troops and the group of Russian troops in the Transcaucasus, ITAR-TASS has learned at the headquarters of the joint command of the group of the peacekeeping forces. The Abkhazia side is represented in the security zone by an airborne battalion. The Inguri GES [hydroelectric power station], am important strategic point, which is supplying electricity to Georgia and Abkhazia, has been placed under guard. Following a forced march from Batumi to Zugdidi, Georgia's motor rifle battalion has arrived in the region. The HQ of the joint command of the group of the peacekeeping forces says that the introduction of troops has been carried out after the completion of engineering work. It is thought that by 29 June subunits of the peacekeeping forces will occupy all checkpoints, and that by 9 July engineering work will be completed at all posts and deployment sites. It has been reported that the 345th regiment deployed on the territory of the Sukhumi military sanatorium will not be taking part in the peacekeeping mission. Deputy Defense Minister Georgiy Kondratyev told ITAR-TASS that the introduction of the peacekeeping forces to Abkhazia is proceeding according to schedule. In his words, the entire area is not to be cleared of mines.
