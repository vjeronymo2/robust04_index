920817 FT 17 AUG 92 / Arts: Today's Television In Live From The Proms (7.30) BBC2 broadcasts an all-Bach programme given by the period instrument ensemble The Academy of Ancient Music. The pieces include the Suite No 4 in D, the Double Violin Concerto (soloists Simon Standage and Micaela Comberti) and the Magnificat in D. Radio 3's new controller, Nicholas Kenyon, a great proponent of period music played on authentic instruments, introduces the concert. ITV's 90-minute drama The Lilac Bus (8.30), based on Maeve Binchy's book, stars Stephanie Beacham as Judy, one of the regular passengers on a bus running between Dublin and Rathdoon. Channel 4's variable series Secret History investigates the assassination of Robert Kennedy in 1968, claiming there has been a cover-up ever since by the CIA and the police (9.00). BBC2 repeats one of the most memorable single dramas of the last 20 years: An Englishman Abroad (9.30), Alan Bennett's wonderful account of the bizarre meeting in Moscow between actress Coral Browne and spy Guy Burgess. If you have never seen it, watch it. If you have seen it before, watch it again.
