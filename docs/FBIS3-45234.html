Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] At a provincial economic work meeting in Lanzhou yesterday morning, Acting Governor Zhang Wule pointed out the need to meet the demands of the new situation and to adopt effective measures to comprehensively carry forward all kinds of reforms and promote the sustained, rapid, and healthy development of Gansu's economy. Zhang Wule said: Last year, encouraged by Deng Xiaoping's southern tour talks and the spirit of the 14th CPC National Congress, the people throughout the province, taking account of Gansu's actual conditions, implemented the decision of the party Central Committee on macrocontrol and regulation, took effective measures, overcame all kinds of difficulties, and fulfilled the objectives set by the provincial party committee and government at the beginning of last year. The general situation is good. This year is the last year to fulfill the Eighth Five-Year Plan, a crucial year to expedite the establishment of the socialist market economic structure, and a year to start with the implementation of the decision of the Third Plenary Session of the 14th CPC Central Committee. The state will introduce major reforms to the financial, taxation, monetary, planning, investment, foreign trade, and price systems. [passage omitted] Zhang Wule pointed out that in this new situation, the government has three main tasks to perform: The first is ensuring sustained, rapid, and healthy economic development and improving Gansu's productivity; the second is making efforts to improve the masses' lives on the basis of economic development; and the third is upholding the two civilizations and striving to maintain a good mood and basic stability in society. In line with these three basic tasks, there is a need to ensure a steady growth in the rural economy; to develop rural industries, township and town enterprises, tertiary industries, collective economic sectors, individual economic sectors, and private economic sectors; to widen the scope of opening; to carry out technical transformation; to expedite the transformation of scientific and technological achievements into actual productive forces; to strengthen enterprise management; and to bring about the gradual formation of a modern enterprise system.
