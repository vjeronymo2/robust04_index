BFN [Text] Liberal-Democratic Party leader Vladimir Zhirinovskiy told representatives of the Islamic Cultural Center that after coming to power, his party would hold a referendum on dividing Russia into guberniyas (provinces), the Liberal-Democratic Party's Press Secretary Grigoriy Serebrennikov told INTERFAX. Zhirinovskiy believes that the current national-territorial division of Russia breeds conflicts. Asked about his attitude to the Muslims, Zhirinovskiy said that the Liberal-Democratic Party was a secular party and that it was tolerant of all religions. He also noted that Tatar Akhmet Khalitov was his party's vice-chairman. Serebrennikov stated that the meeting was attended by religious and public figures from Azerbaijan, Tatarstan and Dagestan.
