Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 4 KYODO -- The Japanese securities industry said Friday [4 March] it plans to allow individual investors to trade in Chinese stocks through brokerage houses. The Japan Securities Dealers Association said the Shanghai Stock Exchange will be designated as its 36th overseas bourse on which Japanese investors will be allowed to trade in stocks. The association said a formal decision on the matter will be made at its executive session to be held next week. The Chinese Government allows foreign individuals to sell or buy designated issues on the Shanghai Stock Exchange, which was opened in 1990. Shanghai authorities asked the association in January to allow individual Japanese investors to participate in trading, officials said. Shanghai will become the eighth stock exchange in Asia to be designated by the association, the officials said.
