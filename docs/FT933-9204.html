930811 FT 11 AUG 93 / N Sea rig operator fined Pounds 250,000 ARCO British, subsidiary of US oil company Atlantic Richfield, was fined Pounds 250,000 yesterday over the 1988 Ocean Odyssey accident in the North Sea in which a radio operator died. The company, which operated the rig, admitted two charges at the High Court in Aberdeen under the Health and Safety at Work Act. Odeco Drilling (UK), the rig's owner, was fined a total of Pounds 25,000 after pleading guilty to two similar charges and another under the Mineral Workings (Offshore Installations) Act. The accident happened when the well being drilled by the Ocean Odyssey went out of control and exploded. The court heard that Timothy Williams died in the confusion of evacuation when he was left behind. He had been in a lifeboat but had been recalled to establish radio communication with the shore. A total of 66 people were rescued.
