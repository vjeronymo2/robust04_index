BFN [Text] Budapest, 9 Jun (MTI) -- Coalition talks between the Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP), which won 54.15 percent of the votes in the May elections, and the second-placed Alliance of Free Democrats (SZDSZ), which won 18.14 percent of the votes, are to continue at 10 a.m. Sunday morning, SZDSZ Chairman Ivan Peto and MSZP Chairman Gyula Horn announced at a brief press conference following nearly three and a half hours of talks on Thursday. Peto said discussions were held today about various guarantee issues, joint parliamentary and government work, and related personal issues. Senior experts of the two parties will hold preparatory talks of limited attendance in the days prior to Sunday's meeting. Peto announced that neither side in the negotiations wishes to provide detailed information about the content of the talks.
