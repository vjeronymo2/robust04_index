940930 FT 30 SEP 94 / World Commodities Prices: Wool The price decline continued at this week's Australian and New Zealand sales, and has now wiped out many of the grains made earlier in the season. The market indicators in Australia registered average falls of 37 and 38 cents on Tuesday, the largest so far. By today, however, merino values appeared to be stabilising, offering hope of a new price basis. The bigger offerings in Australia tested the market thoroughly, and no more than 70 per cent was sold in some cases. At Wednesday's sale in Bradford British wools were five per cent cheaper and the clearance was only 65 per cent. The Bradford market is very quiet and quotations for tops have been reduced
