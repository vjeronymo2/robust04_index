930929 FT 29 SEP 93 / International Company News: Credit Lyonnais rejects MGM offer CREDIT Lyonnais, the French bank which owns Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer, said that it had no immediate plans to sell the Hollywood film studio following an announcement by Mr William Koch that he plans to make an offer for the company, writes Karen Zagor in New York. Mr Koch, winner of the 1992 Americas Cup yacht race, is chairman and biggest shareholder of Kendall Square Research, a supercomputer company. He said he had retained International Creative Management (ICM), the Hollywood talent agency, and its chairman to advise him. Credit Lyonnais took control of MGM last year after foreclosing on loans it made to Mr Giancarlo Parretti, an Italian entrepreneur who bought the company in 1990 from Mr Kirk Kerkorian for Dollars 1.3bn. Credit Lyonnais is involved in litigation with Mr Kerkorian. Under US banking law Credit Lyonnais must sell the studio by 1997.
