BFN [Report by Zayra Pereira; from the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] In a communique issued by the National Banking Council, which met the morning of 15 June, representatives of the financial sector expressed their position on the government's abrupt decision to intervene in seven banks and one finance company. (Jose Monzi Izquierdo), National Banking Council president, read the communique: [Begin (Monzi Izquierdo) recording] The National Banking Council and the Venezuelan Banking Association would like to clearly express our amazement on the decision to intervene and close a group of financial institutions that were receiving financial assistance from Fogade [Deposit Guarantee and Banking Protection Fund]. The government has adopted that decision without notifying the financial sector, and that is why we have been unable to understand the logic behind such a measure. As such, we neither share nor support this decision. We join the rest of the country's desire to be kept fully informed on the fate that awaits these institutions. In addition, we request that depositors and employees be treated with justice and fairness. [end recording] (Jose Monzi Izquierdo) has reiterated an official position regarding the closure and intervention of the financial institutions will be announced after they receive a clear explanation of the issue; he has also expressed his desire to become actively involved in assisting the financial institutions due to their decision to trust the government's support.
