Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] President Boris Yeltsin is expected to make an address on the country's socioconomic development to both chambers of parliament on 18 February. Vladimir Shumeyko, chairman of the Federation Council, announced this when he opened a session of the upper chamber today, Interfax reports. Shumeyko also told the deputies that the prime minister's report on the formation of the budget for the current year is being postponed. Viktor Chernomyrdin may decide to amend some points in his report in light of the president's address, the chairman of the upper chamber explained. The majority of the deputies, however, failed to support this idea, and insisted that Viktor Chernomyrdin should speak this very week. According to the decision adopted, the head of government must brief the deputies on the cabinet's plans for the financial, taxation, budget, agrarian, and industrial spheres.
