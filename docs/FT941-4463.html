940311 FT 11 MAR 94 / Amnesty condemns S Korea South Korea yesterday expressed regret over an Amnesty International report that claimed that human rights conditions have not improved under the year-old civilian government of President Kim Young-sam, John Burton reports from Seoul. Amnesty International said it had received new reports of torture and ill-treatment of some of the 200 political detainees it estimates are held under the strict National Security Law (NSL), which bans contacts or support for North Korea.
