BFN [Report by Aleh Hruzdilovich: "Butrus-Ghali: `Chernobyl Is a Symbol of International Solidarity'"] [Text] As expected, the second day of UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali's visit to Belarus was devoted to Chernobyl issues. At any rate, Chernobyl problems prevailed during his meetings with Supreme Soviet Chairman Mechyslaw Hryb, Prime Minister Vyacheslaw Kebich, and Deputy Prime Minister Ivan Kenik who is also chairman of the State Committee for Dealing with the Aftermath of Chernobyl. They were also the focus during his visit to the radiation medicine clinic in Aksakawshchyna. Mr. Butrus-Ghali has received sufficient information to fully realize the scope of the Chernobyl catastrophe aftermath. During a meeting at the Supreme Soviet, he promised his personal support for keeping Chernobyl issues alive on the UN agenda. The results of the two-day talks held by the UN secretary general in Minsk were assessed by Foreign Minister Pyotr Krawchanka as follows: "Mr. Butrus-Ghali fully supported our two Chernobyl projects. The first one, amounting to $12 million, concerns the carrying out of permanent radiation monitoring of the Republic. The second one, whose cost can be evaluated approximately at $8 million, concerns devising a project to build a plant manufacturing organic food for children. Butrus-Ghali indicated that the UN budget has no provision for financing these projects, and proposed looking for sponsors -- firms and various international funds, to which he promised all kinds of support." Regarding other issues discussed during the talks, Pyotr Krawchanka informed that they were primarily devoted to relations between Belarus and the United Nations (great progress has been achieved in that area), as well as to the issue of nonproliferation of nuclear weapons. Our side confirmed its intent to fulfill the agreements on the nonproliferation of nuclear weapons, which were signed earlier for the period until 1995, as well as the possibility of prolonging them for a new period, or giving them a set character. The issue of Belarus' participation in various UN peacekeeping actions was also discussed. The Belarusian side highly estimated these international efforts and expressed its support in principle for activities leading to the suppression of existing conflicts. Regarding the direct participation of Belarus in such peacekeeping actions, it was stated that these issues are under the jurisdiction of the Belarusian Supreme Soviet. Concrete proposals from the United Nations to assist Belarus with disarmament were also discussed. During the meeting of UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali with Prime Minister Kebich, issues of economic cooperation of the Republic with the international community were discussed in detail along with the difficulties facing Belarus on the path toward reforms. Later in the day, Butrus-Ghali laid a wreath at the Peramoha monument and delivered a speech at the Belarusian Academy of Sciences. This afternoon, his visit is coming to a close, but on the eve of his departure a news conference should be held, after which we will be able to give our readers more details about the results of UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali's visit to Belarus.
