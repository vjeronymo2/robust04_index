BFN [Excerpts] The first sitting of the Consultative Council of the heads of state organizations for science and technology of the five Central Asian countries -- Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, and Uzbekistan -- has been held in Tashkent. The new international organization was formed in line with the decision at the summit of the heads of states of the region. The memorandum adopted in this connection outlines the basic areas for cooperation in the field of science and technology. The aim of the consultative council is to strengthen ties between the republics. The countries in the region will each formulate state science and technology rules. [passage omitted] The scheduled sittings of the consultative council will be held in each of the five countries in turn. Many question on establishing useful and mutually beneficial cooperation still have to be examined.
