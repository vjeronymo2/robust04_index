March 2, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

ABORTION CONTROVERSY

Six of your readers flailed away at Michael Novak for daring to defend the unborn (letters, Feb. 18). The writers ignored the central factor of the whole equation of life -- the existence of God and his guiding hand in the concerns of men and women. The old bromide that the end justifies the means is not valid today, yesterday, or a thousand years hence. A baby, fetus, blob (you name it) is already part of the human family and we cannot with impunity deliver it over the clinics of death.

Facts are stubborn things but we have fiendishly clever people who can weasel their way around almost any obstacle such as "Thou shalt not kill."

CHRIS THOMAS

Torrance
