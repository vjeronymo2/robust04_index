920717 FT 17 JUL 92 / Shephard praises jobs service MOST of the performance targets of the Employment Service were met in 1991-92, including reducing and discouraging benefit fraud, according to its annual report, Lisa Wood writes. The report showed that the service saved the government Pounds 34m in benefit fraud and found jobs for more than 1.3m unemployed people during the year. Mrs Gillian Shephard, the employment secretary, said: 'The Employment Service was created by the government to create an effective and efficient public employment service. The agency's performance over the last year has proved that our investment is paying off.'
