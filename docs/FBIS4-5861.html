BFN [By Abdul Rahman Sulaiman] [Text] Phnom Penh, April 14 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- Dr. Mahathir Mohamed Thursday met with Cambodia's Co-Prime Ministers Prince Norodom Ranaridh and Hun Sen for discussions to further enhance the growth of ties between the two countries. The prime minister was accompanied by Foreign Minister Abdullah Ahmad Badawi, Primary Industries Minister Dr. Lim Keng Yaik and other officials to the talks held at the Cambodian prime ministers' office. Cambodia, which is undergoing economic rehabilitation and reconstruction following the restoration of peace and stability, is said to be keen to emulate Malaysia's economic success as well as that of its other ASEAN neighbours. Dr. Mahathir, here for a two-day official visit at the invitation of the Cambodian Government, arrived Thursday morning together with a 23-member official delegation. Also with him was his wife Dr. Siti Hasmah Mohamed Ali and 69 Malaysian businessmen. The business delegation, led by Arab Malaysia Merchant Bank Berhad [Limited] executive chairman Azman Hashim, is here to further explore business and investment opportunities in this former socialist state whose potentials are still largely untapped. Despite Thursday being the Cambodian new year and public holiday here, the prime minister and his entourage were given a rousing welcome on arrival at the Pochentong International Airport, near here. Dr. Mahathir and Dr. Siti Hasmah were received by First Prime Minister Prince Ranariddh and his wife. Later, the prime minister had an audience with King Norodom Sihanouk who had just returned home from medical treatment abroad specially to welcome the Malaysian leader.
