BFN [Text] There is no information that Turkish Cypriot leader Rauf Denktas has accepted the confidence-building measures [CBM's] and there is no reason for the UN secretary general to delay his report to the Security Council. The report is expected to be submitted next week. In a telephone conversation this morning, Gustave Feissel, the UN secretary general's alternate special representative in Cyprus, reiterated this statement to President Glavkos Kliridhis. Government Spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis said the president contacted Feissel to find out if the UN secretary general would delay his report. He added that Kliridhis made it clear to Feissel that he has accepted the 21 March UN document as is and that he will not accept any changes either in the form of interpretations or clarifications. President Kliridhis stressed that the 21 March document is crystal clear in its contents.
