BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA June 13 -- Deputy Prime Minister Tran Duc Luong received here today a delegation of the International Financial Company (IFC) led by Varel Freeman, head of the company's Department for Asia, the Pacific. Speaking to his guests Mr Tran Duc Luong reaffirmed the Vietnamese Government highly valued IFC's contribution and considered the expansion of its relations with IFC as well as other international monetary organisations very important. With its open door policy, the Vietnamese Government is pleased to create conditions for private companies to invest in Vietnam and hoped that IFC would help Vietnam by granting investment credits. For his part, Mr Freeman said that IFC would invest its capital in projects prepared by IFC. After investing in the first project, IFC will invest in a harbour of Vietnam specializing in farm products export and goods import. Over the past three years IFC has sent many delegations to Vietnam to define investment projects and joint ventures in Vietnam. IFC has also decided to invest in a project on expanding Metropole Hotel. At present, IFC is inquiring into the possibilities of investing in tourism, cement production and in pipe line installation. IFC is also planning a joint venture project with Vietnam to set up insurance companies and help Vietnam making legal documents relating to stock market activities.
