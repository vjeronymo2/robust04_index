Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The Egyptian Interior Ministry briefed the Arab Human Rights Organization [AHRO) on the results of the searches for and investigations into the disappearance of former Libyan Foreign Minister Mansur al-Kikhya, who was reported missing more than three weeks ago. After meeting with Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi yesterday, AHRO Secretary General Muhammad Fa'iq said that the Interior Ministry has formed 10 search teams to find decisive information about al-Kikhya's whereabouts. The Investigations are continuing. Fa'iq said that he believed the Egyptian security authorities are showing real concern about al-Kikhya's whereabouts. Fa'iq said the Interior Minister told him that President Mubarak is personally interested in this issue and that Mubarak is regularly updated on the progress of the investigations. Fa'iq will leave for Beirut in two days to attend the extraordinary meeting of the AHRO Executive Committee on 7 January. The Executive Committee will discuss the details of al-Kikhya's disappearance, unravel the ambiguity surrounding his fate, and define the steps that should be taken to secure his safety. Fa'iq said the AHRO will discuss the formation of a higher national committee to secure the safety of al-Kikhya and the safety of political refugees in general.
