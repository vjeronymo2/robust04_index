BFN [Text] Moscow, June 17 (XINHUA) -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin cancelled his scheduled meeting today with visiting Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet because he has to deal with some "urgent problems," a high-ranking Kremlin official said. The official said that "there are no politics behind the cancelled meeting," according to the ITAR-TASS NEWS AGENCY. The Russian president "has to solve urgent problems which have accumulated during the three days of his absence from Moscow," the official explained. He said that the meeting was planned only in a preliminary way, adding that "as far as the Russian-Vietnamese documents are signed at the level and upon mutual agreement of the prime ministers of both countries, Boris Yeltsin, who has been absent from Moscow for three days, is busy with urgent state affairs today." [sentence as received] Yeltsin spent three days touring the Russian Far East and returned to Moscow on Thursday.
