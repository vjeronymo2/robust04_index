940713 FT 13 JUL 94 / Other main points of Tecs survey Other main points in the survey, completed by 400 out of 1,200 Tec directors: Fifty-five per cent believe companies above a certain size should have to register with the local Tec. There was growing satisfaction with the progress of Investors in People, the government - backed scheme for improving in-company training. Sixty-seven per cent of directors from the private sector felt they would not remain on a Tec board if they were no longer in the majority. Fifty-one per cent - including 40 per cent of those from the private sector -believe in a return to a levy on training, which is Labour party policy. Eighty-two per cent believe Tecs should be open to public scrutiny.
