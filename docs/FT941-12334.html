940131 FT 31 JAN 94 / Arts: Today's Television Those disinclined to believe that the Aids circus is driven by research grants may want to watch Horizon which tells the story of 'Genie'. This American girl spent the first 13 years of her life in silence, as a prisoner in one room of her parents' house. When scientists found her there was competition to use her to prove theories on language (she could hardly speak) and development. Some of the researchers even fostered her. Then, when the grant money ran out, Genie was sent to live in an institution, where she has remained (8.00 BBC2). Cutting Edge promises a report on the lives of British children who look after disabled parents yet do not qualify for carers' allowances. In Glasgow, for instance, a woman suffering from multiple sclerosis is in the care of her children who are 13, 12 and 11 (9.00 C4). Completing a Monday night trio of mournful current affairs programmes, Panorama is the latest to have a go at the electricity-and-cancer story. This time, instead of the dangers of electro -magnetic fields near pylons, it is similar perils from sub-stations (9.30 BBC1).
