BFN [Text] Moscow, 11 May (ITAR-TASS) -- Under the headline "Foreign Investors Need Not Fear Tax," today's edition of the CIS daily newspaper DELOVOY MIR carries excerpts from an official statement by the Russian Finance Ministry. "Foreign investment in the form of resources remitted to joint ventures created within the Russian Federation or in the form of payment by foreign individuals and corporates for acquisition of securities of Russian businesses, and also loans and credits extended by foreign banks and lending institutions are not to be subject to value-added tax (VAT) or special tax," the document says. Also exempted from tax liability are resources gifted by foreign states to Russian businesses and organizations in the form of subsidies for specific programs. Pursuant to existing tax legislation, resources received from other businesses and organizations are subject to VAT and special tax, the newspaper says. The exception is resources paid by founder investors into businesses' authorized capitals, and also for the purpose of joint business activities. Issue and transfer of loans, and also transactions concerning circulation of securities (shares, bonds, bills etc), are exempted from VAT and special tax.
