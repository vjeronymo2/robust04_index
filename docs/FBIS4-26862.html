BFN [Text] Washington, June 27 (XINHUA) -- U.S. White House chief of staff Mack McLarty will step down and will be replaced by Leon Panetta, director of the White House Office of Budget Management. David Gergen, President Bill Clinton's senior special counsel, will go to the State Department and will be its special counsel, the U.S. Cable News Network (CNN) reported today. McLarty, a lifelong friend of the U.S. President, will remain in the White House as a senior adviser to President Clinton. U.S. President Clinton will announce the significant shakeup of the White House shortly in his Oval Office, reports said.
