BFN [By Danielle Yang] [Text] Taipei, June 25 (CNA) -- Official diplomatic ties between the Republic of China [ROC] and South Africa remain solid, South African Ambassador J.L. Viljoen said Saturday [25 June]. Viljoen made the remarks while answering questions from the media at a banquet honoring foreign envoys in Taipei. The banquet was hosted by the Legislative Yuan's Foreign Affairs Committee. Viljoen said South Africa, after it regains membership to the United Nations, hopes to play a more active and positive role in African affairs. He said he had no information on whether Pretoria will establish ties with Beijing. Viljoen said his government fully understands the efforts the ROC is making to enter the United Nations, and said the most urgent task for the ROC is to strive for more international support. South Africa has not yet decided on how best to support the ROC in its bid to join the United Nations, he added.
