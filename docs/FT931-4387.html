930311 FT 11 MAR 93 / Arts: Today's Television Are crabs turning into land animals? Wildlife On One (8.00 BBC1) considers how so many of these creatures in the last 35m years - scarcely more than a flash in the evolutionary pan - have been leaving the water and adapting to life on land. Close To Home (8.30 C4) considers the development in Britain of American style lobbying groups such as Bristol's COGB, mainly a church group. Local MP William Waldegrave initially supported them but now says the group is in danger of setting itself up as a rival to the democratic structure. Tim Hunkin passes an electric current through the carbon core of a pencil until it gets red hot and the wood burns off, then puts it inside a milk bottle and pumps out the air to make a DIY light bulb in his series The Secret Life Of ..(8.30). Another impressive series is A Labour Of Love on BBC2 (9.30) which describes the treatment of babies and children in the first half of the 20th century. Continuing its undercover approach to journalism, Disguises infiltrates a time-share company (9.30 ITV). Harry Enfield's Guide To Opera tries to explain 'why all opera plots are bonkers' (10.30 C4).
