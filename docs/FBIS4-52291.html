BFN [Text] Phnom Penh 9 May (AKP) -- Prince Norodom Sirivut, deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs and international cooperation of the Kingdom of Cambodia, on 7 May left for an official visit to France at the invitation of French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe. "Our visit to France is aimed at renewing the traditional relations of friendship between the two countries," the prince told journalists before his departure at Pochentong Airport where, among others, were present Gildas Lelidec and David Burns, ambassadors of the French Republic and of the United Kingdom to Cambodia. "After France, our delegation will visit the United Kingdom before going to Germany, Belgium, Sweden, and Denmark," he added.
