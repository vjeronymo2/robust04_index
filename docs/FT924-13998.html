921013 FT 13 OCT 92 / Law Society considers action THE LAW Society is considering legal action against Lord Mackay, the Lord Chancellor over proposals to introduce standard fees for criminal legal aid work in magistrates' courts by the end of the year. Launching a set of compromise proposals in a final attempt to avoid a head-on clash with the government in the long-running dispute over payment arrangements for criminal legal aid work, the society said yesterday it could 'not rule out the possibility of national industrial action'. The society said Price Waterhouse, management consultants, who had advised Lord Mackay on his proposals, had acknowledged deficiencies in his scheme, which replaces hourly pay rates with a two-tier system of standard fees.
