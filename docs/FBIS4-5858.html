BFN [Text] Reports of illegal loggings have dropped dramatically following the adoption of the National Forestry Amendment Act 1993 by all Malaysian states. The director general of the Forestry Department, Dr. Ismail Awang, said there is a 50-percent drop in the number of cases reported in Pahang, Kelantan, and Perak since the act was enforced in February. The amended act provides for a maximum 500,000 ringgit, and the mandatory jail term from one to 20 years for those convicted of illegal logging. Speaking to reporters in Kuala Lumpur, Dr. Ismail said the department had also embarked on a five-year forest management plan as a guide for more practical forest management.
