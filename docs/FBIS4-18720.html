BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] A visa waiver agreement covering diplomatic and special passports was signed between Turkey and Ukraine today. Ukrainian Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko arrived on a one-day official visit in Turkey today. The visiting minister first met with Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin. A visa waiver agreement covering diplomatic and special passports was signed after the meeting. The agreement was signed by Cetin and Zlenko. The disarmament of the Black Sea, matters pertaining to oil and natural gas, and President Suleyman Demirel's upcoming visit to Ukraine in May were among the issues discussed during the meeting, Cetin explained. Cetin announced that agreement was reached for Turkey to open consulates in Crimea and Odessa and for Ukraine to open a consulate in Istanbul. Zlenko, in turn, said that the visa waiver agreement will create an atmosphere of trust and sincerity between the two countries. Zlenko and the delegation accompanying him were later received by Demirel. According to a statement issued by the President's Press Center, the visiting minister relayed to Demirel a message from Ukrainian President Kravchuk and noted that his government will be pleased to see Demirel in Ukraine next month. Demirel also said that he will be pleased to visit Ukraine. During the meeting, the president expressed Turkey's readiness to help Ukraine in returning the Crimean Tartars to their homeland. Views were also exchanged during the meeting on regional and international issues concerning the two countries, and Demirel announced that the necessary political will exists for the development of relations with Ukraine.
