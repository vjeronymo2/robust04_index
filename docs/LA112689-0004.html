November 26, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

Of Human Bondage (Channel 28 Sunday at 2 p.m.): This is the 1934 film that once and for all made Bette Davis a star, playing Somerset Maugham's Mildred, the vulgar, cruel waitress who has only contempt for the gentle Leslie Howard, who loves her. (1:30)

The Window (TNT Tuesday at 12:45 a.m.): This is the 1949 sleeper in which little Bobby Driscoll won a special Oscar as a boy who witnesses a murder but can't make his parents believe him. (1:30)

The Palm Beach Story (Channel 5 Wednesday at 3:30 a.m.): One of the great Preston Sturges screwball classics, starring Claudette Colbert, Joel McCrea, Rudy Vallee and Mary Astor. (2:00)

Rope (Channel 11 Thursday at 3:30 a.m.): Shot in 10-minute takes, Hitchcock's stunning and intense 1948 film was inspired by the Leopold and Loeb murder case. John Dall and Farley Granger are the spoiled young killers. James Stewart stars. (1:30)
