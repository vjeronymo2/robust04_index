931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: EC to provide funds for CIS metal industries European Commission officials said yesterday the EC would be providing funds to help the non-ferrous metals industries of the Commonwealth of Independent States, including the aluminium industry, to make market studies and for retraining, writes Kenneth Gooding, Mining Correspondent. They said the community still hoped to reach agreement with the CIS aluminium industry before the end of November, when the present import restrictions end. The European Aluminium Association said it was poised to assist the CIS in refurbishing and updating its 14 aluminium smelters, especially with environmental technology. Its quarterly report yesterday pointed out that 'the emission of hazardous substances is eight to 10 times higher than European regulations allow and sooner rather than later the CIS will be forced to tackle the question of the environment'. The European industry was also ready to help stimulate consumption of aluminium in the CIS. If consumption improved from 5kg to 10kg per head of population - 'a realistic scenario if the conversion to a market-oriented economy is successful' - private consumption would double to 3m tonnes a year, 500,000 tonnes more than the total amount of CIS aluminium estimated to have arrived in the EC in 1992. Meanwhile, the US Aluminium Association said yesterday the US government had responded well to its calls for help and 'seems to understand the urgency of resolving this issue'. At the invitation of Russia, last week the US, the EC and four other aluminium-producing countries met in Moscow for the first multilateral talks about the issue. The countries will meet again in Washington in December. Mr David Parker, president of the association, said: 'This is a situation that traditional market forces alone cannot correct.'
