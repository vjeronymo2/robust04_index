BFN [Text] Athens, June 24 (XINHUA) -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin said here today that the Russian-EU partnership agreement is of "historic importance" and represents the first step of Russia's economic comeback to Europe. Speaking after signing the pact at the European Union summit, Yeltsin said the agreement marks the end of ideological and economic conflicts that prevailed during the Cold War era. "We all felt the symptoms of an economic Cold War, but this threat is being abolished with this agreement," said Yeltsin. "Today, Russia becomes a significant partner of the Europeans, and we will do everything possible to support European integration." He added that Moscow would back efforts aimed at enlarging the 12-nation bloc, which will extend formal membership to four countries later today.
