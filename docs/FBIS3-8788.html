Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Anna Samelova reports from the Primatial Palace] [Text] [Passage indistinct] Shortly before 1130, Prime Minister Jozef Moravcik left the room where he had submitted to President Michal Kovac the proposed composition of the government. I asked Moravcik how the president accepted the proposal. [Begin recording] [Samelova] Did (?he) accept it en bloc or individually? [Moravcik] He voiced agreement with all of the proposals--that is, he approved the composition of the government that had been submitted to him. [Samelova] Can you tell us the names of the new government members? [Moravcik] I ask you to wait until (?this afternoon). There will a swearing-in ceremony at Bratislava Castle at 1800. [Samelova] Last night, after talks with your coalition, you said that as soon as the government was appointed you would have the first extraordinary session of the government on [word indistinct] material and the current privatization process at some ministeries. Will there be a government session today? [Moravcik] We assume that the government will be in session today. [end recording] The appointment of the new government and the festive swearing-in ceremony will take place at Bratislava Castle at 1800 today. The names of the ministers will not be announced by President Michal Kovac before that time. This was announced to journalists by his spokesman, Anton Bodis.
