May 8, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

BARGE CRASHES, SPILLS GASOLINE IN HUDSON RIVER

A barge loaded with barrels of unleaded gasoline smashed into a Hudson River channel marker, spilling more than 77,000 gallons into the water, officials said.

The environmental impact of the spill Saturday was under evaluation. A Coast Guard spokesman said that wind and rain were helping dissipate the gasoline.
