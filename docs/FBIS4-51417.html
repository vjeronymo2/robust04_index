BFN [Text] The Taipei Association of Travel Agents told its members on Wednesday [25 May] that group tours to Mainland China are being resumed immediately. To protect the interests of its members, the association said that it has won the permission from the cabinet-level Mainland Affairs Council to lift the ban on group tours to the mainland. The association reminded its members they should register their mainland tours with tourism bureau to complete the legal process. Nevertheless, a number of mainland destinations continue to be listed as areas of high risk, and local travel agencies will not organize tours heading for such places, including the infamous Chientao [Qiandao] Lake in eastern mainland province of Chekiang [Zhejiang]. Thirty-two people including 24 Taiwan tourists, six crew members, two mainland guides were robbed and burned to death in a pleasure boat ride on Chientao Lake March 31.
