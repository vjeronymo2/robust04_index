June 4, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

'RESTORE THE PUBLIC TRUST'

Our so-called representative government is in very sad shape indeed. A short list includes (former Atty. Gen. Edwin) Meese, (House Speaker Jim) Wright, (Los Angeles Mayor Tom) Bradley, (House Whip Tony) Coelho, (state Sen. Joseph) Montoya, and on and on, ad nauseam. It is sickening to see these guys running for cover with that Cheshire cat grin on their faces; all the while mumbling some variation of the infamous phrase, "I am not a crook!"

Great merciful heavens! Don't these highbinders realize that the question is not one of breaking the law? The average Joe Citizen assumes his elected representatives are law abiding. What we are talking about here is unethical and amoral conduct, about cronyism, about cupidity, yes, about "sleaze." To the average man these things are more important than the law.

When the historians of the next century try to make some sense out of this period they will stumble badly over the question, "Why did this freedom-loving people spend their hard-earned dollars on policeman to eradicate prostitution, and then elect whores to public office?" Maybe we should try and answer the question ourselves!

R.A. HUGHES

La Habra
