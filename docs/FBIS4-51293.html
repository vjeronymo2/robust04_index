BFN [Text] Washington, May 26 (XINHUA) -- U.S. President Bill Clinton today named former Defense Secretary Les Aspin chairman of the Foreign Intelligence Advisory Board, which advises the President on top secret matters. Aspin, 56, succeeds Admiral William Crowe, who has become the U.S. Ambassador to Britain. Aspin became Clinton's defense secretary in December 1992 and retired earlier this year. He was succeeded by William Perry, the number two man at the Pentagon.
