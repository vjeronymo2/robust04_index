Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, March 14, IRNA -- Iran exported over dlrs. 310 million worth of steel within the past 11 months, said an official of National Iranian Steel Company here Monday. Musa Aqa'i Lankarani, the company's plan and project deputy, told IRNA that one million tons of steel products had also been exported over the current year. Pointing out that Iranian steel was highly demanded by world market for being produced of best raw materials, Lankarani remarked that China, Thailand, Phillipine and Taiwan were among the purchasers of the Iranian steel. Iran would also export its steel to the Persian Gulf Arab states in the near future, he continued. He further noted that the plan for the development of steel industry in Iran was under serious study, saying that Iran was planning to increase the capacity of its existing steel units. According to Lankarani, two steel plants with the annual capacity of 350,000 and 550,000 tons each would be established in the northeastern province of Khorasan and northwestern province of Azarbaijan in future. On privatization policies, the deputy commented that the trend had begun since two years ago and that so far more than 10 units had been transferred to the private sector.
