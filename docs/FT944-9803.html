941114 FT 14 NOV 94 / The Lex Column: Sweden The result of Sweden's referendum on EU membership yesterday should ease pressure on government bonds and give a boost to the Stockholm stock market. Although a Yes vote had been mostly discounted, last-minute jitters caused by adverse opinion polls had made the markets increasingly volatile. The expectation that EU economic convergence will force tighter control over government spending should reduce the spread between German and Swedish bonds. The krona should also benefit in anticipation of greater inward investment.
