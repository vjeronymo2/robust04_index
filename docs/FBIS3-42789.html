Language: Russian Article Type:CSO [POSTFAKTUM report: "Credit Guarantee Fund Established"] [Text] Aiming to ensure an uninterruptible supply of imported foodstuffs to Moscow in 1994, the capital city government has set up a credit guarantee fund for imports in the amount of 100 billion rubles [R]. Financing of deliveries will be done through special accounts that the Foreign Relations Department of the Government of Moscow will open in foreign banks. An agreement is expected to be reached with the Central Bank of Russia before 1 February on the matter of opening the guarantee fund's special accounts in foreign banks for a period of one year and transferring into them R100 billion in U.S. dollars at the Central Banks of Russia exchange rate. The Government of Moscow intends to give foreign banks the right to use the fund's deposit accounts as guaranteed collateral for 1994 purchases and deliveries to Moscow of foodstuffs and food processing equipment.
