BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 20 KYODO -- An influential business leader welcomed a government decision to freeze public service charges until the year-end and expressed hope Friday [20 May] that the decision will remain in force for more than two years. Masaru Hayami, chairman of the Japan Association of Corporate Executives (Keizai Doyukai), said the decision by the government Wednesday to freeze public fee hikes was a welcome move and that it should remain in force for at least two years. He said it would be unnatural to raise government-controlled fees at a time when private enterprises are going all out to restructure operations and cut costs.
