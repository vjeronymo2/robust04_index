930111 FT 11 JAN 93 / Leeds firm heads earnings table DIBB Lupton Broomhead, the Leeds-based firm of commercial solicitors, has emerged as the UK's most profitable regional law firm in 1991-92, according to a league table of earnings published by Legal Business. Dibb Lupton had average net profits per partner of Pounds 166,000 on turnover of Pounds 28.8m and fees per fee-earner (lawyer) of Pounds 87,000. The firm has 333 fee-earners. Its nearest rival was Birmingham-based Pinsent & Co with profits per partner of Pounds 165,000 on turnover of Pounds 17.1m. Pinsent has only half Dibb Lupton's number of fee-earners. In contrast the most profitable City law firm, Slaughter and May, had average net profits of Pounds 377,000 on turnover of Pounds 113m in 1991, according to figures published by the magazine last March. Slaughter has 542 fee-earners. The magazine says regional firms suffered almost as much in the 1991-92 financial year as London firms: 'Overall, most regional firms have been happy to note modest increases in turnover while profit margins have not held up well.' It notes that the finances of law firms appear to be dependent on the strength of the local economy. Firms in Leeds have fared better than their counterparts in other regions, providing four of the top 10 most profitable firms. Of the six firms examined in the Leeds area, average profits per partner were Pounds 139,000 compared with East Anglia where average profits per partner was only Pounds 79,000.
