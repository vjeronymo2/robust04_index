Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 13 Jan (MENA)--President Husni Mubarak received French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe today. Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa, who attended the meeting, said that the French minister conveyed a message to President Mubarak from French President Francois Mitterrand. He added that Juppe discussed the situation in Europe, bilateral ties, the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina, developments in the Middle East peace operation, and the situation in Somalia. Juppe expressed satisfaction with the visit and his meeting with President Mubarak and praised relations between the two countries. He noted that he conveyed a message of amity from President Mitterrand. He said he hopes cultural cooperation between the two countries will increase, noting that France has established numerous French Language Departments at Egyptian universities and high schools. He explained that his meeting with Mubarak dealt with numerous international issues, pointing out that France appreciates Egypt's decisive role in the Middle East peace process. Juppe added: We discussed Egypt's chairmanship of the OAU during our review of African issues, particularly Somalia. Our views were similar on these issues. This became evident after I outlined France's position on Bosnia-Herzegovina.
