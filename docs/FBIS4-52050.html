BFN [Text] Tokyo, may 6 KYODO -- A finance company owned by an affiliate of the Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications on Friday [6 May] filed for liquidation under court protection, a private credit research agency said. Teikoku Databank Ltd. said the company, Koshin Shoji, had debts as of late February totaling 101 billion yen accruing from bad loans against the backdrop of the prolonged recession. The amount of debt left by the company is the third-largest this year, Teikoku Databank said. Koshin Shoji is owned by Yusei Gojokai, a mutual aid organization that handles retirement funds for the postal ministry's 250,000 employees.
