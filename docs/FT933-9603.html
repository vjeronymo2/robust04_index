930809 FT 09 AUG 93 / Arts: Today's Television In BBC Proms Live, broadcast simultaneously on BBC2 and in stereo on Radio 3, Claudio Abbado conducts the Gustav Mahler Youth Orchestra which he helped to create, with players from all over the world (7.30). The first half features Russian baritone Dmitri Hvorostovsky, winner of the BBC's Cardiff Singer of the Year competition in 1989, in Mussorgsky's 'Songs And Dances Of Death'. The second part, starting at about 8.15, is devoted to Bruckner's vast Fifth Symphony, lasting almost one and a half hours. What does 'ghoti' spell? Fish: 'gh' as in enough, 'o' as in women, and 'ti' as in nation. How can you expect any child to learn to read in a language with such illogicalities? World In Action takes up the fraught question of what methods should be used to teach British children to read, given that the 'progressive' approach seems, if anything, rather less effective than the old 'a for apple, b for bed' system (8.30 ITV). In Frank Stubbs Promotes (9.00 ITV) the spiv is trying to promote Bulgarian ice dance champions. In Travellers' Tales Wilfred Thesiger talks about his interest in Arabian nomads (9.00 C4).
