931213 FT 13 DEC 93 / Observer: Departure tax At a time when the Conservative government has come under fire for its generosity in paying golden handshakes of up to Pounds 8,000 to ministers when they were forced to leave office, as revealed in the Commons last week, one deposed minister has a rather different story to tell. Peter Viggers was dismissed by the axe-wielding Margaret as industry minister for Northern Ireland towards the end of July 1989. The following day he was aghast to receive a terse letter from the Treasury asking him for Pounds 27 by return post. This was the amount, it transpired, that he owed for the four days to the end of the month that he was unable to complete as a minister. To add insult to injury, the Treasury sent him the wrong P45 - that of another minister sacked at the same time.
