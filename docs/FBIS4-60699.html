BFN [Excerpt] Khartoum, 26 May (SUNA) -- The Foreign Ministry today issued a statement on allegations made by official media organs and the Government of Egypt concerning the directives issued by the authorities of the state of Khartoum on planning and reorganizing the national capital. It said that these directives affect 16 places, including three properties belonging to the Egyptian Army which should have been returned to the Sudanese Government after the departure of the British-Egyptian occupying force at Sudan's independence in 1956. The other 13 areas were leased to the (?Irrigation Department) and to an Arab-Egyptian company which the government assigned for Egyptian and other resident communities in 1958. The Ministry added in its statement that these places were unoccupied for a long period and claims that some families were evicted on the eve of the festival 'Id al-Adha are pure and unfounded distortions indulged in by newspapers. The ministry stressed that these directives were routine and also affected citizens, as well as Sudanese and foreign institutions. [passage omitted]
