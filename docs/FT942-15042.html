940418 FT 18 APR 94 / Turkish troops kill 57 Kurds Turkish army commandos, supported by aircraft and helicopter gunships, killed 57 rebel Kurds in five days of military operations in northern Iraq, the Anatolian news agency reported yesterday Quoting military officials, the agency said troops killed the guerrillas of the separatist Kurdistan Workers party (PKK) in the mountainous terrain in the Sivi and Mezideryakeri regions. The operations, involving about 40,000 troops, are part of a Turkish spring offensive against the rebels in south-east Turkey and northern Iraq. Military officials said Turkish troops would remain in northern Iraq until the end of summer. The PKK has benefited from the political vacuum in northern Iraq to carry out raids into Turkey from mountain hideouts in the region. Turkey has responded by frequently bombing PKK camps.
