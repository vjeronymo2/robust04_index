Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, February 9 (BTA) -- "The first visit to Bulgaria by the minister of education and culture of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, Prof. Dr. Slavko Gordic, aims to study the possibilities of cooperation between Bulgaria and Yugoslavia in science and education," Marko Todorov, Bulgaria's minister of science and education, said after his first meeting with Prof. Gordic today. He laid particular stress on the issue of the Bulgarian minority in Yugoslavia and the necessity to resolve the problem of teachers and textbooks of the Bulgarian language and literature, history and geography for the Bulgarians living in the Western Outlands (given to Serbia under the Treaty of Neuilly of 1919). The exchange of lecturers and students between the universities of the two countries was also discussed. The guests showed interest in establishing contacts with the universities in Sofia and Veliko Turnovo and the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences. Prof. Gordic told reporters that his talks with the Bulgarian minister of science and education passed in an atmosphere of understanding setting a basis for the promotion of relations to the interest of both countries.
