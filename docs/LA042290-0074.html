April 22, 1990, Sunday, Orange County Edition

MARINE AIR STATION VS. COUNTY DEVELOPMENT

After serving 22 years in the armed forces, I just couldn't resist writing concerning those city fathers and a small group of citizens who want the Marines to move the Tustin helicopter facility.

Is it really a noise problem or is it greed -- that some investors would like to grab the land?

A good real estate agent will take a prospective buyer all over an area and point out what business or armed force installation in an area might be a noise factor.

About 30 years ago, when we returned from overseas, we purchased a home in Long Beach.

On the first trip through the area, the real estate salesman pointed out the flight paths used by Douglas Aircraft Co. and the Air Force base in Long Beach. We had the opportunity to purchase the house in the air paths or find another house. We purchased the house.

For the past 25 years, we have lived in Cypress, below the LAX, John Wayne and Los Alamitos base flight paths, and it has never bothered us.

If you are busy with your life and have varied interests, you don't have time to worry about aircraft flying overhead.

Early in the 1960s, the Air Force moved out of Long Beach because local groups complained about jet noise. The local population "jumped up and down" because the loss to the area was more than $1 million a month, and the joke was that the Air Force was not flying jets. They were Douglas Aircraft Co. jets. Just imagine what Tustin will lose if the Marines move out of the base.

Let us be realistic and think about higher taxes as a result of revenue that will be lost.

Isn't it being patriotic to help our government save money and not burden our Marines, who are doing their job day in and day out for the protection of our country?

EUGENE L. ZECHMEISTER

Cypress
