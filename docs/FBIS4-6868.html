BFN [By Takashi Hiranuma] [Text] Seoul, 21 April -- The Japanese and South Korean Governments opened the first Japanese-ROK economic partnership conference in Seoul on 21 April. The Japanese side is represented by Deputy Vice Foreign Minister Sadayuki Hayashi, while his South Korean counterpart is Son Chun-yong, second deputy vice foreign minister. Each side has about 20 working-level officials altogether from its ministries concerned with economic affairs attending the meeting. A wide-ranging exchange of views is being held on bilateral economic cooperation. The conference is scheduled to be held on 21 and 22 April. This conference is being held in accordance with an agreement reached at the Japanese-ROK summit meeting in Kyongju, South Korea, in November 1993. The conference was formed by reorganizing the previously existing Japanese-ROK Trade, Business, and Technological Cooperation Committee.
