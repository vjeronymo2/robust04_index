930511 FT 11 MAY 93 / UK Company News: Exceptionals leave Pex Pounds 908,000 in red Pex, the knitted products manufacturer, reported pre-tax losses of Pounds 908,000 for the year to January 31 after exceptional reconstruction costs of Pounds 790,000. Towards the end of the year the company, formerly known as Palma Group, liquidated subsidiaries, simplified the product range and wrote down property values. The result was to improve reserves by Pounds 600,000. After the year end, property was sold in Leicester for Pounds 1.4m which went towards reducing debt. The previous company reported a pre-tax loss of Pounds 278,000 for the 12 months to January 31 1992. Turnover for the period under review was Pounds 14.5m (Pounds 17.2m). Interest costs were Pounds 952,000 (Pounds 942,000) and losses per share were 3.66p (1.47p).
