920529 FT 29 MAY 92 / UK Company News: Consortium buys control of Sumit Equity MANAGEMENT OF Sumit Equity Ventures, a group of three venture capital funds with Pounds 43m of investments and Pounds 13m of cash under its control, has been sold by Albert E Sharp, the Birmingham-based stockbroker. A consortium led by Mr Lindsay Bury, a founder of Apricot Computers and a founder director of Allied Dunbar, the financial services group, is paying Pounds 200,000 to Sharp. The transaction represents an expansion into the Midlands for the venture capital operations of Schroder Ventures, a partnership which is part of the Schroder Group. Schroder Ventures, with North of England Ventures, an affiliate, are, with Sharp, the other members of the consortium. The sale is the second transaction this week in which Sharp has been involved. Earlier, it sold its stake in Sharelink, the execution-only share dealing service, to the management. The two transactions, Sharp said, were 'entirely discrete': it had never intended to be a long term holder of Sharelink and, in the case of Sumit, 'logic pointed to larger and stronger units able to raise substantial amounts of money.' Mr Bury explained that Sumit would remain Midlands-based but that it would be making investments in co-operation with Schroder Ventures. Although the performance of Sumit is acknowledged to have been disappointing since the onset of recession, Mr Bury said the investments had been written down to a realistic level - 'most of the difficulties have occurred.' He added that, as the economy moves out of recession and with Sumit working from a lower cost base, 'there is scope for upward movement.' The boards and structures of the three Sumit funds, one of which is quoted, will remain the same, although, as Mr Bury put it, 'there will be a hands-on management style.'
