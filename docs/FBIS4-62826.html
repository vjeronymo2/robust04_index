BFN [Text] Federation Council Chairman Vladimir Shumeyko has spoken in favor of re-extending the state of emergency in the zone of the Ossetian-Ingush conflict, which comes to an end on 31 May. Interfax reports that at today's meeting with journalists he noted that the settlement of the situation in that region will be difficult to achieve without better relations with the republic of Chechnya. Vladimir Shumeyko said in connection with this that he favored a meeting between Boris Yeltsin and Dzhokhar Dudayev. According to the chairman of the upper chamber, the Federation Council plans to study the question of extending the state of emergency in the Ossetian-Ingush conflict zone at its regular meeting on 31 May.
