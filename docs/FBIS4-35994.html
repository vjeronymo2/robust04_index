BFN [Text] The users trial for the surface-to-surface missile, Prithvi, began today with the successful launch of the missile from the interim test range near Balasore in Orissa. The indigenously built missile soared into clear sky at 1144 [0614 GMT] hitting the built-up target in (Tentulichura) island of Bhadrak Coast in Bay of Bengal. Defense Ministry officials said today's launch fully met the mission's requirements. The 150-to-250-km-range missile was launched from a mobile platform in the presence of senior officials of the Indian Army and the Defense Research and Development Organization, DRDO. The second trial will be conducted on Monday [6 June] with a target in the high seas which will be followed by other trials in battlefield conditions. Prithvi is one of the five missiles developed under DRDO's integrated missile development program. The other four are Agni, Trishul, Akash and Nag.
