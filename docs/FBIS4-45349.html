California 94WB0042E Mexico City EXCELSIOR in Spanish 12 May 94 pp 1-A, 10-A 94WB0042E Mexico City EXCELSIOR Spanish CSO [Article by Salvador Martinez G.] [Excerpts] Avoiding the promotion of attitudes leading to confusion in the society, refraining from disinformation and rumor, and adhering strictly to the law, with full respect for the separation of powers: these policies were proclaimed yesterday by President Salinas de Gortari and Baja California Governor Ernesto Ruffo Appel, during a long conversation held at Los Pinos. Salinas de Gortari and Ruffo Appel, with the mayor of Tijuana, Hector Osuna Jaime, agreed in citing the undeferrable need to pool efforts throughout the republic. They made a series of commitments aimed at coordinating efforts to deal with the common enemy of Mexico and Baja California: the drug traffic. The Baja California governor, who recently expressed his disapproval of the manner in which that state's deputy attorney general, Sergio Ortiz Lara, was arrested by Federal Judicial Police personnel, arrived at Los Pinos shortly after noon. [passage omitted] The heads of the three government echelons--federal, state, and municipal--agreed, with respect to public security, on the creation of interdisciplinary police units, with federal, state, and municipal forces. Their main objective will be to combat activities and crimes associated with drug trafficking. It was explained that these units will engage in preventive and surveillance activity, through close cooperation in their operations. These police units will have agents from the Federal and State Public Prosecutors' Offices present to assist them. The latter will submit joint reports to the authorities on the three government levels, under the political responsibility of the federal chief executive, the state governor, and the mayor of Tijuana, in their respective jurisdictions.
