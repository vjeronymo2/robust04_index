BFN [Unattributed article: "Economic Report for 1993 Presented"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, April 28 -- Budget Minister Luigi Spaventa and Treasury Minister Piero Barucci today presented parliament with their three-volume report on the economy in 1993 which highlighted how last year the gross domestic product [GDP] fell, the number of jobs shrank drastically, the wages were at a standstill, inflation dipped while there was a major improvement in Italy's balance of payments. In brief, the 685-page report stated how, at the end of year and the first months this year: - The GDP fell 0.7 percent, in line with the world-wide recession, the first decline since 1875. - Employment: the number of jobs shrank by 655,000 units, equal to a decline of 2.8 percent. - Salaries: the trend for salaries and wages showed drastically and rose by 3.1 percent, below the rate of inflation. - Labor costs: the rapport of units per product rose by 1.6 percent compared to 3.8 percent in 1992. - Public finance: spending needs stood at 153 trillion lire, equal to 9.8 percent of the GDP; the goal had been 151.2 trillion lire. - Inflation: the inflation rate declined to 4.5 percent, against 5.2 percent in 1991, one of the lowest levels since the early 1970s. - Balance of payments: a surplus for the first time since the start of the year, 32.511 trillion lire, with exports up 20 percent and imports off 0.2 percent. - Discount rate: between the end of 1992 and February 1994 it was reduced eight times, falling from 12 percent to 7.5 percent. - State paper: interest on BTP [multi-year treasury bond], for the same period, fell 3.8 points, dropping to 8.3 percent net. Rates for BOT [treasury bills] fell to 7.7 percent, from 12.2 percent at the end of 1992.
