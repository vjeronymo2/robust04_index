910416 FT 16 APR 91 / International Company News: Troubled Venezuelan bank sold THE Venezuelan government has sold Banco Italo Venezolano, an ailing state-owned commercial bank, to a Venezuelan financial group for the equivalent of USDollars 63.8m, writes Joe Mann in Caracas. The buyer is Banco Hipotecario de Falcon, a mortgage bank which headed a consortium of three financial groups from the west of the country. The buyers have to apply about Dollars 50m to restore the capital base of Banco Italo and to cover bad loans. The remaining Dollars 13m is to be paid to the government as a 'premium' for acquiring the bank. Banco Italo has been in trouble for several years due to a host of uncollectable loans.
