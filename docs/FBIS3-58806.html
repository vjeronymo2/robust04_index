Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] In Madrid, the air force chiefs of staff of Spain, Germany, the UK, and Italy have signed the document containing the technical features of the future combat aircraft that the four countries are developing jointly. The project was started in 1984, but the cost forced alterations to the project, which were confirmed by the air force chiefs today. Spain will order 87 aircraft, the first two of which will be delivered in 2000.
