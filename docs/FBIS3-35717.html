Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] Official results of the election for Belarusian Supreme Soviet chairman were announced about two hours ago. Myechyslaw Hryb received 183 votes in his favor and 65 votes against his candidacy. Here is a brief outline of his personal history: Myechyslaw Hryb is a lawyer. He was born into a peasant family 57 years ago. His father died in World War II. He graduated from a college in the Ministry of Interior Affairs. In his military service, he rose to the rank of general and was head of the Interior Affairs Administration of the Vitebsk Oblast Executive Committee. In 1990, he was elected to the Belarusian Supreme Soviet and named head of the legislative commission for national security, defense, and combating crime. He supports a strong power base and presidency in the Belarusian Republic. Myechyslaw Hryb supports ratification of the [CIS] Treaty on Collective Security. In his own words, he has always fulfilled his parliamentary obligations based on the substance of problems rather than personal preferences or animosities. Although all of us were involved in the old system, we must look ahead, he said. Here is what Myechyslaw Hryb told the people's deputies after he was elected Supreme Soviet chairman. [Begin Hryb recording] Esteemed People's Deputies and Journalists! Let me express my sincere appreciation for the trust you displayed in me through this vote. I have always said, and will say it again now, that this post is extremely responsible and complex. I will try to perform my duties as my conscience dictates, and will do my best in this post. I hope that all of you will join me and actively help me in my duties. Because I will do nothing without you. [applause] [end recording]
