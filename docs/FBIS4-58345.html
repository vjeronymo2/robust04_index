BFN [Text] Tehran, April 30, IRNA -- If the Serbs do not abide by their commitments, lasting peace will not return to the Balkans, Croatian Premier Nikica Valentic told Majlis speaker 'Ali Nateq-Nuri here Saturday. Valentic briefed Nateq-Nuri on latest developments in the aggressed Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina and termed as "good" relations between Muslims and Croats. The hojjat ol-eslam said in reply that like the Zionists, the two main characteristics of the Serbs are genocide and expansionism, and they will not be satisfied only with Bosnia. He deplored the inability of international organizations in solving the Bosnian crisis and called for lifting of the United Nations arms embargo against the republic. On relations between Iran and Croatia, Nateq-Nuri termed as "positive" the areas of cooperation and called for full-fledged expansion of bilateral ties. Valentic arrived here last night.
