BFN [Text] The Uzbek Cabinet of Ministers adopted a decision on privatizing gasoline stations in Tashkent city. During 1994 five working gas stations and four still in the draft stage or under construction in Tashkent city will be privatized. The Republic's Committee for State Property, the Uzbek petroleum products association, and the Tashkent city mayors office [Hakimiyat] have been instructed to offer the gas stations for sale by auction as private property to physical and legal persons including foreigners on the basis of competition. Long leases should be issued for the land occupied by the privatized gas stations in line with existing legislation. The owners of the privatized gas stations can sell imported raw material and petroleum products from outside Uzbekistan at their gas stations. The full text of the decree will be published in the press.
