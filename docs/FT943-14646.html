940713 FT 13 JUL 94 / International Company News: International Paper improves International Paper achieved earnings of Dollars 87m or 70 cents a share in the second quarter, up 13 per cent from Dollars 77m, or 62 cents a share, in the same 1993 period. The second quarter also showed improvement over this year's first quarter, with rising paper and packaging prices boosting profits. 'This quarter's earnings performance reflect the pricing improvements International Paper is now realising in most of our product lines,' Mr John Georges, International Paper's chairman said. 'It also reflects the improving economy, both here and in Europe. We are now seeing positive demand and pricing trends in essentially all of our product lines, and we expect them to continue for the rest of the year,' he added. Second-quarter sales rose to Dollars 3.6bn, from Dollars 3.5bn in the same 1993 quarter. The company said price increases for containerboard, pulp and uncoated printing papers had held through the quarter. The printing papers division, the largest segment of International Paper's business, had sales of Dollars 1.03bn in the quarter, up from Dollars 1.02bn last year. Packaging division revenues were Dollars 825m in the quarter, up from Dollars 788m in the 1993 second quarter, while distribution sales also rose, to Dollars 820m, from Dollars 775m a year ago. Forest products sales rose to Dollars 445m, from Dollars 440m, although the company said a weaker prices for forest products during the quarter led to slightly lower earnings from that sector.
