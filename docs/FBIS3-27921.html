Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 29 (YONHAP) -- Retired opposition leader Kim Tae-chung left Seoul Saturday for 10-day visits to Norway, Sweden and Denmark. Kim, who is chairman of Board of Directors of the Kim Tae-chung Peace Foundation, is scheduled first to go to Norway where he will meet with Foreign Minister Jan Egeland and other senior government officials, lawmakers, journalists for exchanging views of mutual interests. In Sweden, Kim will meet with Vice Prime Minister Bengt Westerberg, Foreign Minister Margaretha Ugglas and Chairman of Foreign Affairs Committee of Legislature Daniel Tarschys. In Denmark, he plans to meet with Foreign Minister Niels Helveg Petersen and will give a speech at Foreign Policy Council with a theme "roads to unification of the Korean peninsula." Chairman of the main opposition Democratic Party Yi Ki-taek, visited Kim's home at Tonggyo-tong, western Seoul, Saturday morning and bid bon voyage to Kim.
