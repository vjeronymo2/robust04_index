BFN [Text] Riga, Apr 21, BNS -- NATO Allied Forces in Europe North Military Group's Commander General Garry Johnson completed Thursday [21 April] his visit in Latvia. During the visit, he met with Latvian Defense Forces Commander Dainis Turlais, Defense Minister Valdis Pavlovskis and different ministry's officials, and Foreign Ministry's Parliamentarian Secretary Martins Virsis, and discussed Latvia's cooperation with NATO. Before leaving, Johnson said at a press conference that the visit was done in the framework of NATO program for cooperation with other states. Johnson noted that Latvian officers have taken part in several NATO training programs, and NATO expert groups help Latvian defense forces' representatives with advice. Johnson praised Turlais's contribution to the forming of Latvian defense forces. He noted that Latvian soldiers, sent to take part in NATO training programs, were physically well prepared. Answering journalists' questions, Johnson said NATO was ready to cooperate both with Latvia and Russia, and he expressed hope that Russian army would be fully withdrawn from Latvia till the complete drafting of the cooperation program. He said Russian army's presence in Latvian Skrunda will not influence cooperation between Latvia and NATO, said Johnson. Johnson also said that no priorities in cooperation with NATO would be given to Russia, though Russia could involve large forces in NATO's activities.
