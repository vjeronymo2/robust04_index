Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, January 20 -- According to a cooperation programme between Estonia and Sweden, concluded in September last year, Estonian Chart Centre will finish work on a complete and thorough up-to-date Estonian map, based on satellite photos, by the end of 1995, Heiki Potter of the State Land Board told ETA. The final cost of the project is 11 million Estonian kroons (about 785,714 USD), 85 percent of which will be financed by Sweden. According to an agreement between the Estonian Land Board and Swedish Satellitbild corporation, Sweden will be responsible for the satellite photos, digital data basis and necessary equipment to decode and interpret the data. The new system will allow continuous renewal of the basic map, survey of environmental situation and of how natural resources are used in Estonia. 1,000 maps will be printed at the beginning for the use of state and municipality institutions.
