BFN [Text] Beijing, May 14 (CNS) -- According to a working meeting for registration of foreign-funded enterprises recently held here, after 15 years of reform and opening to the outside world, by the end of 1993, more than 170,000 foreign-funded enterprises have registered in China, with a total investment of U.S.$382.4 billion and a total registered capital of U.S.$245.6 billion, and of which U.S.$150.0 billion was foreign investment. Some 146 countries and regions have invested in China, and China's absorption of foreign capital has made up 40 percent of the total by developing countries. By the end of last year, actual foreign investment in China was U.S.$56 billion. Foreign investment in place in China made up 13 percent of the total social fixed asset investment in the country, up from 2.5 percent in 1980s. The output value of foreign-funded industrial enterprises last year was RMB 302 billion [renminbi], making up 9.6 percent of the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) in the country.
