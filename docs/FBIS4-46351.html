BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian guerrilla activities monitored through 8 June. An attack perpetrated on the morning of 2 June by 30 members of the Army of National Liberation (ELN) against the police station and the mayor's office in Villa del Rosario, 10 km southeast of Cucuta, left three police officers dead and four others wounded. Property damage resulting from the attack has been estimated at more than 80 million pesos. Meanwhile, three trucks that were deployed to defend Villa del Rosario were ambushed by another group of guerrillas on the Cucuta-San Antonio road. Police also defused two bombs outside the mayor's office. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 3 Jun 94 p 13A) Four guerrillas, members of the ELN's Camilo Torres Front, have been killed in clashes with the San Mateo Battalion in La Gloria and Pelaya, Cesar Department. Meanwhile, lawyer Roger Efrain Lorduy was released by rebels who had kidnapped him. One FAL rifle, two R-15 rifles, a fragmentation grenade, and other weapons were seized. Meanwhile, on 2 June, a Cincelejo-Barranquilla bus was torched by 15 members of the 37th Front of the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC) in Ovejas, Sucre Department. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 3 Jun 94 p 10A) The 4th Brigade has reported that four military agents killed in a FARC ambush in Altos de la Cienega, Antioquia Department, will be buried today in Medellin. General Mario Hugo Galan said the attack took place as the military agents were implementing the Democracy Plan to guarantee the 19 June elections. One soldier was wounded during the ambush. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 7 Jun 94)
