BFN [Text] Moscow May 27 ITAR-TASS -- President Boris Yeltsin has endorsed the federal programme for stepping up the struggle against crime in the Russian Federation in 1994-1995. The presidential decree, circulated by the president's press service, instructs the government to ensure the funding of the program. The leaders of the ministries and departments charged with implementing the program are warned about their personal responsibility for the timely fulfillment of the programme. The president advised the State Duma to consider without delay the bills initiated by the president and the government for the purpose of upgrading the struggle against crime, promoting new legislation and improving the activity of the law-enforcement agencies. The five-page decree lists measures designed to curb the criminal activity in the country. The formations and military units subordinate to the Interior Ministry will be reinforced by 52 thousand men. The rights of the police will be expanded and it will be given the right to inspect buildings of enterprises and offices, regardless of ownership, if criminal activity in them is reported. The rules for the use of arms by the employees of the law-enforcement agencies in the line of duty will be revised, and special police units will be established to accompany long distance and suburban trains. The secretary of the National Security Council of the Russian Federation, Oleg Lobov, is entrusted with the supervision over the fulfilment of the federal programme.
