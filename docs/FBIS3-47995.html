Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 21 (YONHAP) -- Director Primakov of the Russian external intelligence department made a secret visit to Seoul at the end of last year, an informed source said on Friday. The source said Primakov, whose department is the successor to the KGB of the fromer Soviet Union, was invited by Kim Tok, director of the Agency for National Security Planning (NSP). He said that during his stay in Seoul, Primakov met President Kim Yong-sam and other government leaders to discuss broad-ranging matters of mutual interest such as the North Korean nuclear question, inter-Korean relations and the overall Korean situation. At his meeting with Kim Tok, the two discussed ways to promote cooperation between their intelligence organizations, the source added.
