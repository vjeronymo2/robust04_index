Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tunis, March 25 (AFP) -- The PLO has not yet decided whether to resume negotiations with Israel on launching Palestinian self-rule, a close aide to PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat said here Friday [25 March]. Marwan Kanafani said PLO-Israeli discussions set to resume in Cairo next week "do not constitute a resumption of the negotiations." Earlier an aide to Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin told AFP the negotiations on autonomy in the Gaza Strip and West Bank town of Jericho would resume in Cairo Tuesday, exactly one month after the talks were suspended over the Hebron massacre. Kanafani said the PLO was continuing its contacts with all concerned parties, including the United States, Russia and Norway, to obtain answers to demands made by the PLO in the wake of the Hebron massacre. A resumption of the autonomy talks "depends on positive and practical answers which the Israelis must provide," concerning implementation of UN Security Council Resolution 904, which allowed for protection of Palestinians in the occupied territories. After the massacre the PLO called for an armed international protection force to be deployed in the territories, the disarming of Jewish settlers and dismantling of certain flashpoint settlements. The Israeli official, declining to be named, said negotiators General Amnon Shahak and the director general of the foreign ministry, Uri Savir, had reported to Rabin on "the progress made in the Egyptian capital." "We will go back to Cairo on Tuesday and this time we will be able to conclude," Shahak, the deputy chief-of-staff, said on his return after two days of talks on boosting security for the Palestinians of Hebron. The PLO broke off the autonomy talks after a Jewish settler massacred 30 Palestinian worshippers in a mosque of the West Bank town of Hebron on February 25. In Cairo, chief PLO negotiator Nabil Sha'th said the two sides had drawn up a draft accord on the deployment of international observers and a Palestinian police force for Hebron. But he did not confirm the autonomy talks would resume, saying only that the negotiators would meet again in the Egyptian capital on Tuesday for further talks on security for Hebron. Sha'th said the draft accord would be submitted to PLO and Israeli leaders. "Important progress was achieved during negotiations in the last two days in Cairo but there are certain points to clear up," he cautioned. Israeli radio said that if the security measures for Hebron were finalized early next week, the negotiations to launch autonomy in the Gaza Strip and West Bank town of Jericho would resume "immediately."
