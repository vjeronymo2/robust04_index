BFN [Text] Damascus, May 15 (XINHUA) -- Syrian President Hafiz al- Asad today met with visiting U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher who arrived here earlier today on a new tour of the Middle East. Syrian Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' went to Damascus airport to greet the U.S. official, whose ongoing tour aims at securing an essential progress in the Syrian-Israeli peace talks in Washington. Israel's proposals on the return of the occupied Golan Heights is believed to be the main topic in talks between Christopher and al- Asad. Israel has offered a phased withdrawal from the Golan Heights but did not give details on how much territory it planed to return to Syria. At the same time, Israel wanted to set up full diplomatic relations with Syria. Syria demanded Israel's commitment to a full withdrawal before outlining its stand on future relations. On the eve of Christopher's tour to the region, the Arab world called for Israel's complete withdrawal from the occupied Golan Heights, stressing that peace will not be achieved unless it facilitates a comprehensive pullout based on international legitimacy and in line [with] relevant U.N. Security Council resolutions.
