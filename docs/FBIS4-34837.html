BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 23 June. The World Bank and the International Development Association have granted $860 million to Bolivia over the past few years, of which $210 has been repaid, while the remaining $650 million will be paid back over the next 40 years. (La Paz HOY in Spanish 9 Jun 94 p 17) On 15 June in Rome, the International Fund for Agricultural Development and the Bolivian Government signed a 7.6 million loan agreement designed to finance a development project for llama and alpaca breeders of the Andean Altiplano. The project includes 17 provinces in the departments of La Paz, Oruro, and Potosi, where 31,700 families are engaged in the breeding of llamas and alpacas. (La Paz LA RAZON in Spanish 16 Jun 94 p B8) The Central Bank reported that international reserves had risen to $448 million by the first half of June, the highest level in the past 14 years. Freely available foreign exchange reserves now total $434 million. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 18 Jun 94 National Section p 1) Carlos Vilaseca, president of the National Chamber of Customs Officers, said on 10 June that customs revenues registered a $200 million deficit last year as a result of the illegal smuggling of merchandise, which amounted to $800 million. During the Eighth Congress of Customs Officers, Vilaseca charged that customs officials carry out joint operations with smugglers, to whom they grant protection. (La Paz LA RAZON in Spanish 11 Jun 94 p B4)
