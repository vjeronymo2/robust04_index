941012 FT 12 OCT 94 / Arts: Today's Television Vincent Hanna is one of the best informed, most relaxed, and (crucially important) funny political journalists on television. The only trouble is that he tends to recognise this better than anyone. Still, A Week In Politics is just about the best political programme we have, and A Year In Politics, the title chosen for the annual party conference programmes, is particularly good. Hanna has considerable success in convincing his guests that it is so late at night on a minority channel (12.10 C4) that nobody will be watching and they can therefore let their hair down and say anything they like. He, and consequently his viewers, had a wonderful time at Blackpool last week: will the Tories also do us proud in Bournemouth? Earlier, BBC1 screens the last episode in the first series of Common As Muck, William Ivory's drama about dustmen. He has managed to plait the theme of impending privatisation quite successfully with the other strands of the men's lives (9.30). Another council employee, rat-catcher Paul Taylor, holds the camera for Video Diaries, telling us of his anti-Nazi activities (9.30 BBC2).
