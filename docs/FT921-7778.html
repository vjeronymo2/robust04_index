920220 FT 20 FEB 92 / Parliament and Politics: Pressure seen from Commons business -Election timing THE GOVERNMENT will have no time to assess the impact of the Budget on voters before it decides whether to opt for an April 9 election, the shadow cabinet was told last night. In a briefing paper on election timing, Mr Derek Foster, the Labour chief whip, said that the tight schedule for Commons proceedings meant the Opposition should refuse any backroom dealing to ease the pressure of business on the government. It was the weight of parliamentary business that meant Mr John Major, the prime minister, would have only until the morning of March 12 - just two days after the Budget - to make up his mind on whether to go to the polls on April 9. It was possible that the government would be forced to make MPs sit through the weekend after the Budget to complete rushed business. Forcing MPs from Tory marginals to stay in London was in Labour's interests, Mr Foster concluded.
