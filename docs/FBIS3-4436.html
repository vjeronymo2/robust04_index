Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (XINHUA) -- Qiao Shi, chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress (NPC) of China, met with Kim Yong-sam, president of the Republic of Korea (ROK), here today. They both expressed satisfaction with the rapid development of Sino-ROK relations. Noting that Sino-ROK ties date back to ancient times, Qiao expressed satisfaction with the rapid development of all-round bilateral relations since the two countries set up diplomatic ties in August, 1992. China's NPC and the ROK's National Assembly have established official ties and had friendly multi-channel contacts, Qiao said, adding that further friendly exchanges will crop up this year. These contacts will help to deepen mutual understanding, enhance friendly relations and promote cooperation between the two peoples, Qiao said. The NPC is willing to maintain close ties with the ROK's National Assembly and is ready to contribute to the furtherance of Sino-ROK ties, Qiao said. The ROK president said that although the visit is short, he had seen vigorous development in China, which impressed him deeply. The contacts between the two parliaments are very important, he said, voicing the hope that these exchanges would be further enhanced. Kim also voiced satisfaction with the ROK-China ties. After being briefed on China's economy, Kim expressed the belief that after his current visit, ROK businesses would quicken their steps to invest in China.
