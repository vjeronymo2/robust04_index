February 12, 1990, Monday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: SEAL BEACH;

WIEDER, CITY COUNCIL TO DISCUSS ISSUES

County Supervisor Harriett M. Wieder will meet with the City Council at 6 p.m. today to lead a study session on the Orange County Fire Department's master plan, the county traffic congestion management plan and paramedic fees.

The session will include a discussion of the proposed rate hike for Fire Department services.

If approved, the rate hike could increase the city's cost from $1.8 million a year to $2.4 million by 1991.

The council's regularly scheduled meeting will follow the special meeting. The council is scheduled to vote on a number of March council election matters, setting polling places and appointing precinct board members.

Eight candidates will vie for two seats in districts one and three, which are being vacated by Mayor Victor Grgas and Council Member Joyce Risner. MICHELLE NICOLOSI
