Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] An agreement on trade and economic cooperation between our republic and Romania was signed in Tashkent at Uzbekistan's Foreign Economic Relations Ministry. The agreement was signed on behalf of our state by Muratbek Rustambekov, the first deputy minister of foreign economic relations, and for Romania by Mihai Berinde, state secretary at the Ministry of Trade and head of the Foreign Trade Department. Romania has an interest in expanding mutually advantageous supplies in the area of oil equipment for the food and textiles industry. The delegation that came to Tashkent includes business people who intend to renew interrupted ties in the field of using raw materials resources, such as cotton fiber. They are also prepared to export to the republic furniture for which there is demand on the world market. The agreement that was signed is a new step in the development of direct economic contacts between independent Uzbekistan and Romania.
