940418 FT 18 APR 94 / Size of executive pay-offs declines The size of pay-offs to redundant executives has dropped by a quarter over five years, says a survey by Drake Beam Morin, redundancy consultants. In 1989, a typical pay-off was just over four weeks' salary for every year of service, but last year this had fallen to just over three weeks. Of 12 countries surveyed, Portugal and Spain paid the most. The average pay-off in Portugal was six weeks' salary for every year; the figure for Spain was 5.6 weeks. Mr Peter Trigg, DBM managing director, said: 'It should be borne in mind that the statutory payment in the UK is one week for every year of service, so that these executives are still relatively well off.'
