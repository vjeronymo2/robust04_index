940816 FT 16 AUG 94 / International Company News: Singapore bank ahead 14% as loan base grows Development Bank of Singapore (DBS), the island republic's largest bank, has announced pre-tax profits for the six months to end-June of SDollars 311m (USDollars 206m), a 14 per cent rise on the corresponding period last year. DBS said group net interest income was SDollars 332m, a rise of 22 per cent. It said the increase was due to improved net interest margins and an expansion of its loan base. The bank reported a 47 per cent increase in income on fees and commissions to SDollars 124m, mainly due to higher income from merchant banking and investment banking. Per share earnings rose from 61 Singapore cents to 71 cents. over the period. The dividend is unchanged at 8 cents.
