BFN [Text] Dr. Nabil Sha'th, head of the Palestinian delegation to the peace talks, has stated that the Palestinian and Israeli delegations have agreed on the security arrangements in the city of Hebron in the occupied West Bank. Dr. Sha'th pointed out that there will be an international presence in Hebron but no Palestinian police will be deployed in the city. He added that the two delegations, in meetings lasting until early this morning, agreed to resume talks on the withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho. Dr. Sha'th noted that details of the agreement will be announced this morning. [In a related report, Paris AFP in English at 2339 GMT on 30 March adds the following: "Israel and the Palestine Liberation Organisation will sign an agreement on security in the West Bank town of Hebron here at 10 a.m. (0800 GMT) Thursday, PLO negotiator Nabil Sha'th said. ["He said the conclusion of an agreement would be announced simultaneously in Jerusalem and the PLO headquarters of Tunis. ["A reliable Palestinian source said that under the accord the number of international observers in Hebron, where a Jewish settler massacred 30 Arab worshippers in a mosque on February 25, would be raised to 150. ["But no Palestinian policemen would be deployed in the town, the source added. ["The observers would come from Norway, Denmark and another western European country."]
