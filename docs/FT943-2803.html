940917 FT 17 SEP 94 / CEPA Indonesian power deal Consolidated Electric Power Asia, a unit of Hong Kong's Hopewell Holdings, signed a 30-year agreement to sell electricity from a planned Dollars 2bn power plant project to Indonesia's state-owned electricity company, Perusahaan Umum Listrik. CEPA plans to build, own and operate two 660MW coal-fired generator plants on the north coast of Java, due to come on stream by 1998. PLN agreed to buy electricity from Panjung Pari at Dollars 7.665 per kW/hour for the first six years, Dollars 7.388 for the six years following that and Dollars 5.988 for the next 18 years.
