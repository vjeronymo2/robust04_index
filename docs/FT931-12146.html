930129 FT 29 JAN 93 / Observer: Un-statesmanlike What on earth does the New Statesman and Society think it's up to in printing sensational allegations about the prime minister? It may be a good way of promoting the magazine's relaunch - reputedly financed by the proceeds of selling its picture of security service chief Stella Rimington, the only extant snap showing her face. But besides risking the organ's future by courting enormous damages, the action is scarcely in keeping with its past. In particular, such a sort of journalism is enough to set the Statesman's starchy founders, Fabians Sidney and Beatrice Webb, spinning in their urns.
