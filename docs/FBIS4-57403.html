BFN [Report by Marieta Cabrera] [Text] At 0900 on 18 May, the ARC [expansion unknown] Gloria, flagship of the Colombian Navy, which is visiting Cuba for a second time, put in at the port of the Cuban Revolutionary Navy's [MGR] General Staff. The crew, headed by Captain (Eduardo Otero Erazo), was greeted by Colombian Ambassador Ricardo Santamaria and Captain (Martin Jodar), chief of the MGR General Staff, who welcomed the ship to Cuban territory. Over the past 25 years, this Colombian Navy ship has visited 48 countries on five continents. Cuba is the second stop in this year's eight-nation tour which began with Kingston, Jamaica. As regards the ship's goals, the ship's captain said the following: [Begin recording] [(Otero)] I believe it is going to be a very interesting visit, which will serve to further reaffirm the ties of friendship and fraternity that exist between the peoples of Colombia and Cuba. [Cabrera] Why was Cuba selected? [(Otero)] I believe Cuba was selected because it is one of the most important cultures in Latin America, and also to reaffirm the ties of friendship between the two countries. [end recording] The flagship's crew met with Rear Admiral Pedro Perez Betancourt, chief of the MGR, who expressed to the Colombian crew the interest the Revolutionary Armed Forces [FAR] minister and the chief of the General Staff have in this visit. He also discussed the naval history of the two nations and weather conditions en route to Cuba. During their stay, the visitors will have a busy agenda which includes meeting members of the FAR and the population in general while the ship is open to visitors.
