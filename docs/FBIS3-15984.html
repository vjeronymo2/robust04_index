Language: International Language: <f 105> Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Denmark will aspire for integration as quickly as possible of the Baltic states into NATO and the European Union, Danish Minister of Defense Hans Haekkerup, who is currently visiting Lithuania, said at the meeting with Ceslovas Jursenas, Chairman of the Lithuanian Seimas. The Danish defense minister made assurances of his country's help so that the Baltic states will be regarded in this process as equal with Romania and Bulgaria and not be placed behind them. At the meetings between President Algirdas Brazauskas, Minister of National Defense Linas Linkevicius, and members of the Lithuanian Seimas, problems related to the strengthening of the Lithuanian state border, possibilities for joint military exercises, and assistance in setting up the Baltic peacekeeping battalion were discussed. Apart from this, it has been noted during the talks that the first units of Lithuanian servicemen from the peacekeeping battalion would be able to join the peacekeeping process within the composition of the Danish forces deployed in Croatia. On Thursday [10 February] the Danish minister of defense met the leaders of the Voluntary National Defense Force and visited Klaipeda where he met the commanders of the Lithuanian marine force, visited the port, and got acquainted with the technical equipment and armament of the fleet. On Friday the Danish defense minister and the Lithuanian minister of national defense are to sign a cooperation treaty.
