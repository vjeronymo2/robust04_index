BFN [By BUSINESS-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow June 8 TASS -- The index of consumer prices grew by 9.7 per cent in May, including food products -- by 11.7 per cent, and non-food commodities -- by 6.5 per cent, BUSINESS TASS was told at the economic reforms working centre under the government of the Russian Federation. Prices for services grew by the largest margin of 12.7 per cent. Prices went up particularly for apples and vegetables in May, whereas the cost of potatoes dropped by 1.3 per cent, and for eggs -- by 0.5 per cent. The cost of a food basket, including 19 staple food products, grew on the average by 12.6 per cent throughout Russia. However, this indicator was much higher than the average in the east Siberian region, where it equalled to 21 per cent, and in the Urals -- 19.9 per cent. Prices went up only by 4.3 per cent in the Russian Far East.
