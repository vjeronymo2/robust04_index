BFN [Text] Bonn, May 5 KYODO -- Japanese Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata and German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel pledged Thursday [5 May] to cooperate in bids to win permanent membership in the United Nations Security Council (UNSC), officials said. Their pledge came during an hour long meeting after Hata arrived in Bonn on the third leg of a European trip with previous stops in Italy and France. He will visit Belgium after Germany. Hata told Kinkel that permanent UNSC seats for Japan and Germany would benefit UN activities, and Kinkel said Germany shares the same view, according to the officials. In response to Kinkel's comment that Germany maintains a strong interest in Asia, Prime Minister Hata said the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) forum should be open, signaling Japan's willingness to positively consider the potential participation of Germany and otHer European countries in the forum. Hata and Kinkel expressed their shared concern over North Korea's suspected nuclear weapons program, urging Pyongyang to dispel suspicions about the nuclear issue, the officials said. Hata later met with German Chancellor Helmut Kohl for talks on a wide range of topics, including trade issues.
