Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 15 KYODO -- Ecuador President Sixto Duran-Ballen Cordovez called on Japan on Tuesday [15 March] to extend yen loans to the Latin American country to help its project to build up its power transmission network, Foreign Ministry officials said. The president made the call in a 35-minute meeting with Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, the officials said. The meeting was followed by a 70-minute dinner at the prime minister's official residence. Ecuador's project to set up branch lines from its main power grid and build transformer substations is estimated to cost a total of 90 million dollars, the officials said. Duran-Ballen did not specify the amount of Japanese cooperation desired, they said. Hosokawa told Duran-Ballen Japan will study the request for yen loans based on Ecuador's economic situation and debt, they said. Duran-Ballen was quoted as saying ecuador will solve the debt problem as soon as possible so it can implement the power grid project. During the meeting, the president also invited Hosokawa, Emperor Akihito and Empress Michiko, and Crown Prince Naruhito and Crown Princess Masako to visit Ecuador, the officials said. He also invited them to visit Galapagos Island west of Ecuador, they said. Hosokawa was quoted as saying Ecuador is a country he would like to visit sometime. Hosokawa expressed the hope that relations between Japan and Ecuador would become even closer, the officials said. He told the president the Japanese Government has been extending assistance to improve Ecuador's infrastructure and is currently studying possible cooperation in the nation's vocational training, the officials said. A Japanese mission is visiting Ecuador between March 15 and 26 to study the cooperation, Hosokawa was quoted as saying. Hosokawa also said Japan will invite 10 young people from Ecuador for training in Japan, they said. At the outset of the meeting, Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata and his counterpart from Ecuador Diego Paredes Pena exchanged documents on two Japanese grants-in-aid amounting to a total of 1,123 million yen, according to the officials. The assistance comprises 1,074 million yen for medical equipment in major hospitals and 49 million yen in cultural aid for equipment for the national sucre theater, they said.
