940409 FT 09 APR 94 / Finance and the Family: Directors' transactions Inchcape's results at the beginning of the week reflected a strong Japanese yen and were lower than expected. The share price fell back a little and both Charles MacKay, the chief executive, and Sir David Plastow, the chairman, bought stock. Flying Flowers is based in the Channel Islands and deals in fresh blooms. The company was floated in August of last year and the share price has blossomed considerably since then. Most of the board held stock when the company came to market but the purchase by D. Payne of 50,000 shares represents his entire holding. Calderburn, which makes office furniture, warned about profits in December following the impact of deferred orders. At the same time, it announced it had acquired SBFI, which makes specialist desks. This has proved a successful deal and the company started the year strongly. We note that the sale of stock by Fredrich-Wilhelm Dauphin was made on behalf of his wife; he retains almost 3.5m shares. Devro International makes sausage skins and came to the market in July 1993. At that point, the directors undertook not to sell any stock until after the results were announced last month. When these were announced, three board members then sold shares.
