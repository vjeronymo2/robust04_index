930318 FT 18 MAR 93 / World Trade News: Seoul relief over duties SOUTH Korean semiconductor makers yesterday expressed relief that dumping duties imposed by the US on their memory chips are far lower than expected, John Burton writes from Seoul. The industry said the duties, averaging 3.19 per cent, would have a minimal impact on sales of dynamic random access memory (D-Ram) chips in the US, their biggest foreign market. The US on Tuesday drastically lowered preliminary dumping duties imposed last October on Korea's three main semiconductor companies for unfair pricing of D-Ram chips.
