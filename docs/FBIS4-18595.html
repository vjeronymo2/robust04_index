BFN [Text] Schwerin (DDP/ADN) -- Germany and Poland signed an agreement on cooperation in the area of environmental protection in Schwerin today. Federal Environment Minister Klaus Toepfer and his Polish counterpart Stanislaw Zelichowski signed the agreement after a meeting of the German-Polish environmental council. The agreement includes stipulations on the exchange of environmental data. The parties to the agreement commit themselves to taking all appropriate and effective measures to prevent, reduce, and combat any considerable cross-border environmental damage. The environmental council of the two countries determines the guidelines and strategies for cross-border cooperation. An initial environmental agreement was signed with Poland in autumn 1989.
