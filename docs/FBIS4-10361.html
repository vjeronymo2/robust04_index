BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Panamanian elections scheduled for 8 May 1994: Ruben Dario Carles, the presidential candidate for the Nationalist Liberal Republican Movement [Molirena], said on 1 April that as soon as he becomes president, he will implement a program to improve, expand, and modernize public transportation services in Panama City and the interior. Carles pointed out that he will promote the participation of cooperatives, private companies, and independent transportation workers. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 2 April 94 p 5A) According to the latest CID-Gallup poll, conducted from 20 to 26 March in 1,204 homes, Ernesto Perez Balladares, presidential candidate for the Democratic Revolutionary Party [PRD] leads the field of presidential candidates with 34 percent of public support; followed by Carles, 15 percent; Blades, presidential candidate for the Papa Egoro Movement, 12 percent; Mireya Gruber, presidential candidate for the Arnulfist Party, 11 percent; and Eduardo Vallarino, presidential candidate for the Christian Democratic Party [PDC], 4 percent. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 4 April 94 pp 8A, 9A) Following the funeral mass for PRD member Mauricio Foster, victim of an allegedly political attack, Ernesto Perez Balladares asserted: "I urge fellow party members to show their civic awareness and patriotism, to be aware that there will be more provocations, and to avoid violence and sorrow at all costs." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 5 April 94) On 5 April, Ruben Carles met with teachers' groups, to whom he presented his proposal on education. Carles believes the approval of new laws on education reform is crucial. In his proposal, he mentioned the need for reforms that guarantee that those people who have the ability to teach be allowed to work and have training opportunities. He added that public finances must be strengthened to improve teachers' salaries and that social programs to enhance school-home relations and programs for adult literacy and training must be resumed. (Panama City EL SIGLO in Spanish 6 April 94 p 14) During a meeting with members of the diplomatic corps, Perez Balladares emphasized: "We will be very strict in dealing with government corruption." He added: "Security concerns will be addressed through the professionalization of the National Police, which will definitely remain under civilian control." Regarding his foreign policy, Perez Balladares noted that what Panama needs is respect, fraternity, and friendship with the United States and other Latin American nations. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 6 April 94) A recent Veritas Data poll conducted nationwide on 28 and 29 March shows that over 80 percent of Panamanians plan to vote in the 8 May elections. Other findings include that 73 percent of Panamanians say they have watched the presidential debates and that Perez Balladares' performance created the most favorable impression. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 6 April 94)
