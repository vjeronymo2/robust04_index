October 18, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

DAVID DUKE'S STRONG SHOWING IN LOUISIANA VOTE

The Times fluffs off Duke's demand for a colorblind society. A colorblind society is what Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. lived and died for.

Just for starters, it would be nice to have a colorblind Supreme Court. Or on the local level it would be nice to have admission to the University of California not based on color.

BOB MUNSON

Newbury Park
