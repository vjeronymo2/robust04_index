Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Phnom Penh 14 Jan (AKP) -- An agreement on Japanese aid to Cambodia was signed on 11 January in Phnom Penh by Prince Norodom Sirivut, deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs and international cooperation, and Katsuhiro Shinohawa, acting charge d'affaires of Japan in Cambodia. According to this agreement, Japan will provide Cambodia with $9 million. The first installment of the aid is for the restoration of the "ODEM" [expansion unknown) bridge and the road building center and for the waterworks in Phnom Penh. On this occasion, Prince Norodom Sirivut deeply thanked Japan for assisting in the restoration of roads and bridges, electricity, and drinking water in Cambodia. For his part, Katsuhiro Shinahawa said the second installment of aid for a $25-million project will arrive in Cambodia this year in which $20 million will be designated for drinking water, with the remaining for bridges and roads.
