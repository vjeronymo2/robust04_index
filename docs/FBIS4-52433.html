BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 9 (KCNA) -- Foreign newspapers carried special writeups on the birthday of the great leader President Kim Il-song. The Tanzanian paper DAILY NEWS carried a picture of President Kim Il-song giving on-the-spot guidance in agriculture and said: The great President Kim Il-song is an experienced leader who enjoys deepest respect in the world. He was known as the youngest leader in the world in the 1940s and is recognized as the most veteran and authoritative leader for the world nearly half a century since then. He moves the world with his rich experiences. Having applied the chuche idea to the building of the party, the people's power and the Army, he is respected most deeply by the Korean people. The world people respect and follow President Kim Il-song. The Nepalese paper NEPAL SAMACHAR, the Mexican paper LA AFICION, the Nicaraguan paper BARRICADA, the Sierra Leonean paper THE NEW GLOBE, the Pakistani papers MASHRIQ, NAWA-I-WAQT and AMAN, the Zairean paper UMOJA, the Ugandan paper NGABO, the Tanzanian paper UHURU, the Russian papers GUDOK and VLADIVOSTOK and the Syrian paper AL BA'TH published commemorative articles. The International Liaison Committee for reunification and peace in Korea released a special issue of its bulletin. It had the special titles "We Wish President Kim Il-song Good Health and a Long Life" and "Role and Feats of President Kim Il-song in Fulfilling the Cause of the Independent and Peaceful Reunification of Korea". Congratulatory radio program was aired in different countries on the birthday of President Kim Il-song. The FM radio of Cambodia, the television and radio of Guinea, the television of Zambia, Radio Citta Aperta of Italy, state radio of Guyana, radios of Togo, Laos, Equatorial Guinea and Mongolia, radio and television Kamchatka of Russia, the television of Pakistan, Radios Rebelde, Progreso, Cadena Habana and Ciudad de la Habana of Cuba, radios of Burkina Faso, Uganda and Vietnam, state television No. 7 of Peru, the television of Senegal, central television of Indonesia, televisions of Togo and Zaire, Television Sandino of Nicaragua, televisions of the Congo, Tunisia and Ethiopia broadcast congratulatory programs and screened Korean films and video tapes.
