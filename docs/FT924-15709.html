921002 FT 02 OCT 92 / Survey of Software at Work - Autumn 1992 (13): Unipay - Product Profile Supplier: Peterborough Software, a UK company formed in 1963 as a specialist in human resources systems. It was bought by insurance broker C. E. Heath in 1986. Turnover: Pounds 25.8m in 1991-92; Pounds 22.5m in 1990-91. Profit: net profit Pounds 3.5m in 1991-92; Pounds 1.5m in 1990-91. Employees: 450 in the UK, 50 in Australia, New Zealand, Hong Kong and France. Products: Unipay, launched in 1986, followed by Unipersonnel, Unipension and an integrated system called Uni 2000, all for mainframe computers; Open Door for the Unix operating system; PS 2000 for personal computers. Hardware: IBM and ICL mainframes; IBM AS/400 and System/38 business minicomputers; Unix computers from IBM, ICL, DEC, Hewlett-Packard and Sequent; IBM-type personal computers. Price: not declared. Sales and support: direct by Peterborough Software. Customers: Unipay over 170, Unipersonnel over 70, Uni 2000 78, Open Door 48, PS 2000 1,600, all in the UK; outside the UK, Uni 2000 has nine sites and Open Door five. Various users' views: Pros: 'the company is a market leader . . . a very helpful company; Unix open systems policy is welcome; integration of products works well; good price; personal computer product is very flexible. Users of other products, interviewed by Software At Work, had no specific reasons for rejecting Peterborough Software.
