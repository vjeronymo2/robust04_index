940202 FT 02 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Colefax returns to the black Trading improvements in most areas of its business enabled Colefax and Fowler Group, the wallpaper and furnishing fabrics maker, to swing from losses of Pounds 192,000 to profits of Pounds 50,000 pre-tax for the half year to end-October. The shares responded with an 8p rise to 82p. Group turnover improved from Pounds 13.9m to Pounds 16.1m. A 35 per cent sales increase by European operations overall was largely generated by the Jane Churchill brand. US sales increased by 11 per cent. Earnings per share of 0.15p compared with previous losses of 0.76p. The interim dividend is a same-again 0.5p. The book loss of Dollars 315,000 (Pounds 210,000) on the sale of a New York warehouse will be provided for in the second half. Mr Alan Smith, chief executive of Kingfisher, has been appointed a non-executive director.
