940601 FT 01 JUN 94 / International Capital Markets: Warsaw bourse in the black The Warsaw Stock Exchange made a net profit of 171bn zlotys in 1993, the exchange's first profit since it was created in 1990, Reuter reports from Warsaw. Mr Wieslaw Rozlucki, bourse president, told a meeting of shareholders that in three years the bourse has paid back 108bn zlotys of a start-up credit from the treasury. The exchange wants to add an extra trading session to the three sessions per week held currently, the bourse said. Trading presently takes place on Monday, Tuesday and Thursday of each week. The main market lists 23 shares.
