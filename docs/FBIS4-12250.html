BFN [Text] Khartoum, 7 Apr (MENA)--Sudanese newspapers today published conflicting reports on the Sudanese airliner that a Sudanese national hijacked yesterday, Wednesday, and forced to land at Luxor airport. While AL-SUDAN AL-HADITH said the plane was bound for al-Danaqilah and then Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, AL-INQADH AL-WATANI said the plane was on a domestic flight. AL-INQADH revealed that the hijacker Mahjub Husayn Muhammad, who requested asylum in Egypt, was registered on the flight as a passenger under a false name. He revealed his real name to the Egyptian authorities, the paper said, adding that the person who carried out the operation is called 'Adil Mahjub Muhammad Ahmad. The paper explained that domestic flights are not subject to the same passenger-list checks that international flights usually undergo. Meanwhile, all Sudanese media as well as statements made by the Sudanese information and media minister and Sudan airways totally disregarded the presence of Sudanese ministers on the plane that returned to Khartoum last night; the hijacked plane was carrying two ministers: Education Minister Ahmad Muhammad Kapuche and Agriculture Minister Yusuf Adam [names and titles as received].
