931008 FT 08 OCT 93 / Arts: Today's Television Amid a spate of programmes about the unsavoury world of the '90s teenager, Public Eye: Teenage Kicks (BBC2, 8.00) examines the thriving drugs business among young people. Parents and grandparents who wish to continue living in cloud-cuckoo-land are strongly advised against watching. At the start of the Michaelmas term, Class Action (C4, 8.00), the educational magazine programme, looks at the response of Oxford University to undergraduate suicides which although statistically on the low side, causes concern. Tomorrow's World (BBC 1, 7.30) includes a feature on Lewis in the Outer Hebrides, now a place of windy peat bogs but in prehistoric times thickly forested. Researchers at Edinburgh University investigate what might have happened and whether the forests may yet be brought back. Good, hammy fun with the late-night horror movie, The Beast With Five Fingers (BBC1, 12.40) in which a deceased concert pianist's severed hand gives spine-chilling performances.
