BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Kipkeyev] [Text] Nalchik April 3 TASS -- Talks between plenipotentiary delegations of the Ingush Republic and North Ossetia have been postponed after Ingush President Ruslan Aushev and North Ossetian President Akhsarbek Galazov urgently left for Moscow on Sunday. The talks will be resumed on the territory of the Republic of Kabardino-Balkaria on April 13-14. Shortly before departure to Moscow head of the temporary administration on the territory of Ingushetia and North Ossetia, Vladimir Lazovoy, and Russian Deputy Minister for Affairs of Nationalities and Regional, Politics, Vyacheslav Mikhailov, gave a news conference for mass media. Lazovoy said that as a result of a continuous discussion the sides agreed to adopt in the main a draft treaty which takes into account positions of both sides and outlines principles of the Ossetian-Ingush settlement. During the talks an address to the people of Ingushetia and North Ossetia was adopted which contains an appeal to prevent extremism. "There are certain forces which are not interested in the settlement of the conflict," the address said. It was pointed out that the talks were held the form of a constructive dialogue carried on in a polite and respectful manner.
