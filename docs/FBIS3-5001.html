Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 3 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and China initialed a cultural cooperation pact in Seoul Thursday to promote exchanges in education, academia, the arts, mass media, youth activities and sports. Yi Won-yong, director-general of cultural affairs at the Foreign Ministry, and Wu Chunde, director for cultural relations at the Chinese Ministry of Culture, inked the accord in Seoul. The pact will be formally signed later this month by the two nations' foreign ministers during South Korean President Kim Yong-sam's state visit to Beijing. The agreement includes cooperation across a wide range of cultural activities as well as exchanges of information and know-how on protecting relics. The two countries intend to form a joint committee to determine specific means of cooperation.
