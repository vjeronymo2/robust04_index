920208 FT 08 FEB 92 / UK Company News: Independent Inv Co net assets grow Over the six months ended December 31 1991, net asset value at The Independent Investment Company rose 5 per cent to 57.7p, and in January moved up a further 11 per cent to 64.1p. A significant factor in the January rise was the near-doubling in the share price of the US company Read-Rite, which is now Independent's largest holding. It became so as a result of its public offering and subsequent share price performance. It meant that Independent's unlisted investments at December 31 represented 51 per cent of total assets, against 64 per cent at end-June. In the interim period total revenue was again Pounds 1.5m, but heavier interest charges and expenses cut earnings per share to 0.4p (0.59p).
