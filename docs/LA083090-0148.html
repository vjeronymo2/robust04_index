August 30, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: ANOTHER COOL IDEA

This dish is ideal for summer entertaining because it's cold, it can be made a day ahead of time, and it looks terrific. To make an even prettier presentation I like to garnish the platter with edible flowers, such as nasturtiums, whenever they are available.

The reason the chicken is removed from the heat just slightly undercooked is because it will continue to cook as it cools in the liquid. If overcooked, chicken becomes tough and less flavorful.

PARTY CHICKEN PLATTER

6 whole chicken breasts, boned, skinned and halved

1 cup coarsely chopped celery

1 cup coarsely chopped onion

1 carrot, peeled and sliced

1 bunch fresh parsley (including stems and leaves), chopped

1 teaspoon salt

Dash white pepper

2 cups dry white wine, about

1 envelope unflavored gelatin

3 tablespoons water

1 bay leaf

1/2 cup calorie-reduced mayonnaise

1/2 cup finely minced parsley

Cucumber slices, carrot curls, pimiento strips, radish roses, optional

Remove all visible fat from chicken. Place chicken in large saucepan and add celery, onion, carrot and 1 bunch chopped parsley. Mix salt and pepper with wine and pour over chicken mixture. If mixture is not completely covered, add little more wine. Bring to slow boil over medium heat. Cover and simmer until chicken breasts are slightly underdone, about 20 minutes. Remove from heat and allow to cool to room temperature.

Remove chicken from pan, reserving liquid, and arrange on large platter. Cover tightly and refrigerate.

Return reserved poaching liquid to heat and bring to boil. Strain and reserve 1 3/4 cups. Soften gelatin in water. Add to reserved liquid and mix until gelatin is thoroughly dissolved. Add bay leaf and allow mixture to come to room temperature. Chill until slightly jelled, about 1 hour. Then remove and discard bay leaf. Add mayonnaise to gelatin mixture, mixing thoroughly with wire whisk.

Remove chicken from refrigerator and spoon gelatin mixture over each breast, covering entire platter. Sprinkle 1/2 cup minced parsley evenly over all. Cover and refrigerator until mixture is firmly jelled, about 3 hours. Garnish with cucumber slices, carrot curls, pimiento strips and radish roses. Makes 12 servings.
