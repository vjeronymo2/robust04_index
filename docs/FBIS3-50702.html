Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Miguel Angel Velasquez] [Text] San Cristobal de las Casas, Chiapas State, 5 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- Through its official spokesman, Eoloy Cantu Segovia, the Mexican Government this morning asked the armed rebels operating in this area of the state to hold talks and lay down and surrender their weapons. Talking to reporters in this city, Cantu Segovia, who is also the Government Secretariat's senior officer [oficial mayor], conveyed the government's readiness to begin talks at once with the armed group. He added "that for the purpose of avoiding greater loss of lives, the Federal Government is reiterating its readiness to establish an immediate dialogue with the armed group that began its operations the first of this year." To begin this dialogue, the government also proposed a framework of understanding that consists of four points: 1. The ending of hostilities and attacks on towns and persons. 2. The laying down and surrendering of weapons, including the 1.5 tonnes of dynamite stolen on 31 December. 3. The return of hostages and kidnapped persons. 4. The identification of interlocutors and leaders of the armed group. The official spokesman affirmed that "with strict adherence to the absolute respect for human rights, the government will make the allowances contemplated by the law with those who accept this invitation to a dialogue and lay down and surrender their weapons."
