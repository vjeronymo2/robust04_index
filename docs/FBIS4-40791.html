BFN [Text] St. Petersburg, June 8, RIA -- The latest session of the Council of the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly started with the raising of the flag of a new, 10th assembly member -- Moldova. The participants in the session extensively discuss the question of the assembly's peace-making efforts. Information on this issue has been provided by the head of the assembly's peace-keeping group, the speaker of the parliament of Kirghizia [Kyrgyzstan], Medetkan Sheremkulov. The delegates have also been briefed by the Russian president's special envoy for Nagorno-Karabakh, Ambassador at Large Vladimir Kazimirov. According to him, "peace that has been achieved is still very fragile and maximalism in the settlement of this 6- year-old conflict is hardly a good idea." It is expected that in the course of the day the parliament leaders of Armenia and Azerbaijan will discuss the prospects for the settlement of the Karabakh conflict.
