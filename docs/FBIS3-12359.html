Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Tripoli, 28 Feb (MENA)--Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid has held an urgent telephone conversation with Colonel al-Qadhdhafi, in which he called on the Libyan leader to reconsider Libya's decision to withdraw from the Arab League and not to present the decision to the Basic People's Congresses until a meeting is arranged between Libyan officials and the Arab League chief. Jum'ah al-Fazzani, secretary of Libya's People's General Committee for Unity, arrived in Cairo yesterday and told Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid that the Secretariat of the People's General Committee for Unity (Ministry of Unity) is about to present a draft resolution to the Basic People's Congresses, calling for Libya's withdrawal from the Arab League on the grounds that the League failed to provide any service to the Arab people. [Cairo MENA in Arabic at 1323 GMT on 28 February, in a Tripoli-datelined item, adds: "Counselor Tal'at Hamid, press adviser to the secretary general of the Arab League, today said that Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid has not received a message from Jum'ah al-Fazzani, secretary of the Libyan Unity Committee, containing Libya's request to withdraw from the Arab League. Hamid added that Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid has not sent any message dealing with this topic to Libyan leader Col. Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi."]
