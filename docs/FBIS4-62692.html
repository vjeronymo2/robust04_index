BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS Andrey Fomin] [Text] Chita 18 May -- The decision of the leaders of the Chita Region Duma to refuse to sign the agreement on civic peace has met with little support in Transbaikalia, the leaders if some political parties and movements said in a joint statement today. The leaders of Regional Federation of Trade Unions, the Confederation of Creative Unions and the Committee of Soldiers' Mothers resolutely condemned the refusal and called on the Chita Duma to reconsider their stand without delay. The statement urges the leaders of the Supreme Body of Representative Power in Transbaikalia to sign the agreement designed to stabilise Russian society and give it a chance of peaceful reforms.
