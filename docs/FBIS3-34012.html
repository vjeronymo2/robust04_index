Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The secretary of the General People's Committee of Foreign Liaison and International Cooperation yesterday met Dr. Salim Ahmed Salim, the secretary-general of the OAU, in Addis Ababa. The two sides discussed the crisis between the Great Jamahiriyah and the West in light of the African position of support for the Great Jamahiriyah, and examined ways to develop this position. The two sides also discussed a number of issues concerning the African continent. During this meeting, the OAU secretary general stressed the importance of the role played by the Great Jamahiriyah in strengthening the freedom of the African continent. The secretary of the General People's Committee of Foreign Liaison and International Cooperation also met in Addis Ababa yesterday afternoon the Sudanese foreign minister, the heads of the Tunisian, Mauritanian, and Algerian delegations and the assistant secretary-general of the Arab League who are all attending the 59th session of the OAU's Council of Ministers. During this meeting, the so-called Lockerbie affair was discussed, in addition to the agenda of the session, some African issues and Arab-African cooperation.
