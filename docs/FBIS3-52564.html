Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Decree No. 2/94 issued by Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id on 5 January--read by announcer] [Text] In the name of God, the merciful, the compassionate. Decree No. 2/94 ordering a cabinet reshuffle: Having taken cognizance of Decree No. 26/75 on the issuance of the law regulating the state's administrative system and its amendments, in light of the progress being witnessed by the sultanate in all spheres, out of our interest in supporting the country's comprehensive development, and as required by the public interest, we, Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id of Oman, have decreed the following: 1. His Highness Fahd Bin-Mahmud Al Sa'id shall be appointed deputy prime minister for cabinet affairs. 2. The name of the Secretariat of the Council of Ministers shall be changed to become the General Secretariat of the Council of Ministers. The title secretary of the Council of Ministers shall be changed to become the general secretary. 3. The deputy prime minister for cabinet affairs shall supervise the activities of the Council of Ministers and its General Secretariat. The bylaw of the Council of Ministers shall be amended to correspond to the regulations of this article. 4. A Higher Education Ministry shall be created. Yahya Bin-Mahfuz al-Mundhiri shall be appointed minister of higher education. He shall also be appointed chairman of the university board. 5. A Legal Affairs Ministry shall be created. Muhammad Bin-'Ali Bin-Nasir al-'Alawi shall be appointed minister of state for legal affairs. 6. Sa'ud Bin-Ibrahim Bin-Sa'ud al-Busa'di shall be appointed minister of education. 7. A Development Ministry shall be created. Muhammad Bin-Musa al-Yusuf shall be appointed minister of state for development affairs. 8. This decree shall be published in the official gazette. It shall become effective as of the date of its issuance. [Signed] Qabus Bin-Sa'id, sultan of Oman [Dated] 22 Rajab 1414 Hegira, corresponding to 5 January 1994
