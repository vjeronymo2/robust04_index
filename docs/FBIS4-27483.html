BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA June 2 -- Export earnings in the first six months are expected to reach USD 1.6 billion, an increase of 27 percent compared with the same period last year, surpassed slightly by imports, up 33.2 per cent to USD 1.7 billion. Ministry of Trade figures show that the export earning from crude oil are expected to reach 3.2 million tonnes, or 38 per cent of the yearly plan but much higher than that of the first six months last year, and that textile and garment products are expected to earn USD 150 million, up by 83 per cent compared with the same period last year. Import activities have been concentrated on material catering for demands of home consumption and production. Petroleum imports are expected to reach 2.2 million tonnes (equivalent to 51 per cent of the yearly plan), urea fertilizer 400,000 tonnes and steel 200,000 tonnes. Other import products such as cement, cotton fibre, insecticide are also higher than that of the first six months last year. Thanks to the abundance and well circulation of goods and stable price, the total retail sale of goods in the market will reach 50 billion dong (USD 5 million), an increase of 30 per cent compared with the same period last year. However, there have been remained unsolved problems such as fake goods and tax evasion.... The Ministry of Trade is gradually perfecting a project on the organization of the market to fulfil the last six months' objective of obtaining USD 3 billion from exports and USD 2.3 billion from imports.
