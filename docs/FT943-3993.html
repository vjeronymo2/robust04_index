940912 FT 12 SEP 94 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System In a week that saw renewed discussion of the prospect of a multi-tiered Europe, the order of currencies in the EMS grid was unchanged. The spread between strongest and weakest currencies was similar, with the only discernible shift being a slight weakening of the Irish punt. Currencies, Page 29 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the system. Most of the currencies are permitted to fluctuate within 15 per cent of agreed central rates against the other members of the mechanism. The exceptions are the D-Mark and the guilder which move in a 2.25 per cent band.
