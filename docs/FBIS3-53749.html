Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Statement by Information Minister Michel Samahah after the Council of Ministers session in Beirut on 18 January -- recorded] [Text] The Council of Ministers held an extraordinary session this afternoon by invitation from the prime minister. The president chaired the session. The president opened the session by deeply thanking President Hafiz al-Asad for bearing the case of Lebanon in his heart and mind at the summit he held with President Clinton in Geneva. The subject of Lebanese rights took up a large part of this summit, during which President Hafiz al-Asad concentrated on the implementation of Resolution 425. This was included in President al-Asad's statement after the summit. The president also informed the Council of Ministers of the contacts he held after the summit with President Hafiz al-Asad. The president told the Council of Ministers that this afternoon he received the U.S. ambassador in Lebanon, who conveyed a message from President Clinton detailing the discussions during the summit regarding Lebanon and the future direction of peace negotiations in Washington. President Clinton is trying to make 1994 the year for establishing peace in the region. The Council of Ministers also learned about the contents of the telephone conversation between U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher and the prime minister after the Syrian-U.S. summit in Geneva ended. In preparation for the Chamber of Deputies discussions of the 1994 budget on Thursday, the minister of state for financial affairs presented to the Council of Ministers the budget for 1994 with the amendments made by the parliamentary Finance and Budget Committee. The Council of Ministers discussed all these points and approved the amendments made by the Committee.
