BFN [Text] Brussels, May 6 KYODO -- Japan's Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata said Friday [6 May] the recent "excessive surge" in the yen is unfavorable. In a press conference in Brussels, Hata said he wants the Bank of Japan to respond to foreign exchange rate movements while maintaining close contact with other central banks in the United States and Europe. He also said exchange rates should properly mirror the economic fundamentals of the countries concerned. Hata was on a six-day trip to Europe from Monday for talks with leaders in Italy, France, Germany and Belgium.
