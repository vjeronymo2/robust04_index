BFN [Text] Rafah, 11 Jun (MENA)--Major General 'Abd-al-Raziq al-Majayidah, commander of the Palestinian National Security Forces, left Rafah today for the self-rule areas at the instructions of Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat to acquaint himself with the security situation, the deployment of the security forces in vital areas and at important installations, and to prepare for the arrival in Jericho of 'Arafat and the rest of the civilian Authority members. Maj. Gen. al-Majayidah stressed the need for the donor nations to provide funds to help the security forces perform their duties and establish security and stability in the self-rule areas and help the civilian departments to provide utilities and infrastructure projects. Economic experts estimate that the civilian authorities will need approximately $25 million per month to cover the current costs, in addition to the costs of building installations in the initial stage of establishing a Palestinian state. Meanwhile, 600 more personnel of the Palestinian National Security Forces will arrive in Rafah this evening in preparation for entry into the Gaza Strip tomorrow.
