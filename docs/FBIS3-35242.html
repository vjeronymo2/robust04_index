Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 21 Feb (MENA)--Commenting on reports that the Serbs have begun withdrawing their heavy weapons from around Sarajevo, Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa said today that the beginning of the implementation of this withdrawal must lead to total withdrawal so that we will be assured that no other aggression will take place against Sarajevo. Musa said if the work to lift the siege of Sarajevo succeeds, it is necessary and desirable that this also be done at other besieged towns. Thus, several aspects of Serb aggression can be dealt with and a settlement process can begin in a better atmosphere. Musa added that Egypt is closely watching the situation. It will continue its contacts with the states concerned. He explained that Egypt is maintaining contacts with the European states. It is also maintaining contact with the Islamic state [Bosnia Herzegovina] within the context of the Islamic Conference Organization's interest in this issue.
