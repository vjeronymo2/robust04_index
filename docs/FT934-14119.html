931019 FT 19 OCT 93 / Engineering companies suffer in the north ENGINEERING companies in South Yorkshire and the north Midlands are experiencing weakening prices and reduced margins, says the Sheffield association of the Engineering Employers' Federation, Ian Hamilton Fazey writes. The association, which covers one of the UK's most intense concentrations of manufacturing industry, says a survey of 107 companies has shown nearly 30 per cent suffering falling profitabil-ity in the past three months. Mr Nicholas Kemp, the association's director, said yesterday: 'There is intense competition both at home and abroad for engineering business, and some prices have weakened. Almost a quarter of companies say prices are worse and some are indicating worsening cashflow.'
