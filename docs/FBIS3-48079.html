Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 19 (KCNA) -- Some 700 students affiliated with the Seoul District Federation of General Student Councils (Sochongnyon) of South Korea met on January 15 and issued a resolution for checking rice market opening, Seoul-based Christian Broadcasting System reported. Accusing the present regime of bowing to the U.S. pressure for rice import liberalization, the resolution called for blocking the "parliamentary ratification" of rice market opening. The ralliers also denounced the authorities' opening of the financial market. They declared they would wage a vigorous struggle against the U.S. pressure for market opening.
