Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Rabin yesterday disclosed that there have been U.S. efforts to ease tensions in southern Lebanon. But the premier said Israel reserves the right to protect her interests. [Begin Rabin recording] There were American talks with the Syrians, with the Lebanese, and with the Israelis. I will not elaborate what were in this talks. Israel has explained its position: We will continue to act in accordance to our decisions what is good, what is needed to achieve peace to the Galilee and security to our forces and to the SLA [South Lebanese Army] forces in the security zone. I am fully aware that Syria could restrain Hizballah, but Syria did it for 10 days before the Geneva summit meeting between Presidents Clinton and al-Asad. Since then we see escalation in the activities by Hizballah against our forces in the security zone. [end recording]
