931216 FT 16 DEC 93 / EU agrees packaging rules European Community environment ministers agreed new rules yesterday to promote recycling of packaging materials and free trade in packaged goods, Reuter reports from Brussels. The deal fell short of what more pro-environment countries had fought for, by placing the emphasis more on free trade than large-scale recycling of the 50m tonnes of packaging discarded each year. The Danish, Dutch and German ministers were sidelined as the rest came to a deal requiring each country to recycle a quarter of its waste packaging and to ensure that no more than than half of all the waste is simply dumped. Belgium, which ends its EC presidency at the end of the month, hailed an agreement. 'We were in a situation of chaos,' the chairwoman, Ms Magda de Galan, Belgian environment minister, told reporters. 'We've taken a step together which will bring a note of serenity.'
