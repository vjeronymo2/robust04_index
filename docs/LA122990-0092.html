December 29, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

CHINA;

POLICE CHIEF OUSTED; VICE LAWS STIFFENED

China replaced its top police official, apparently because of his failure to suppress last year's pro-democracy movement and catch political fugitives in the aftermath.

Public Security Minister Wang Fang was replaced by a deputy, Tao Siju. Zheng Tuobin, minister of foreign economic relations and trade, was also replaced by a deputy, Li Lanqing. Zheng's replacement was believed to be routine. The government also toughened penalties for trafficking in drugs and pornography.
