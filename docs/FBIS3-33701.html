Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Minister of State Dr. Fawwaz Abu-al-Ghanam, the acting minister of state for foreign affairs, has received a telephone call from Lebanese Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz. Minister Buwayz conveyed the condolences of the Lebanese Government and people to the people and Government of Jordan and the family of martyr Na'ib al-Ma'ayitah, counselor at the Jordanian Embassy in Beirut, who was killed by treacherous and criminal hands this morning. Minister Buwayz voiced deep regret over this tragedy, which is considered a blow to the efforts by the Lebanese Government to ensure stable conditions in Lebanon. Dr. Abu-al-Ghanam also received a phone call from Lebanese Interior Minister Bisharah Mirhij. Minister Mirhij conveyed condolences to the Jordanian Government and people and the martyr's family. He stressed that the competent Lebanese quarters will not spare an effort to identify the criminals. He added that various security agencies have already begun investigations into this sad incident. Dr. Abu-al-Ghanam summoned the Lebanese ambassador in Amman to his office at the Foreign Ministry. He said the Jordanian Government regrets such a painful incident and asked him to contact the Lebanese Government to conduct the necessary investigation, identify the criminals, and take the necessary measures to provide security to the members of the Jordanian mission in Beirut. Interior Minister Salamah Hammad received a phone call from his Lebanese counterpart, Bisharah Mirhij, who voiced deep regret over the painful incident involving martyr Na'ib 'Umran al-Ma'ayitah, counselor at the Jordanian Embassy in Beirut. He briefed Mr. Hammad on the measures that are being taken by the competent Lebanese quarters to investigate the incident and identify the perpetrators and take the severest measures against them.
