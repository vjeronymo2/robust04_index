BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 7 June. The Superintendency for Foreign Investments--SIEX--has reported that foreign investments amounted to $93.6 million in May. This represents a 206 percent increase from the $30.5 million registered in April. SIEX added that the investments include $89.4 million in foreign exchange, $2.7 million in real estate, and $1.4 million in capital and credits. The investments were made in the following sectors: $83.6 million in transportation and storage, $47.2 million in manufacturing, $7.2 million in financial services, $6.4 million in commerce, $845,000 in agriculture and animal breeding, and $554,000 in construction. (Caracas EL UNIVERSAL in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 2-4) Deputy Finance Minister Luis Xavier Grisanti has affirmed that the government will privatize the firms that comprise the Venezuelan Corporation of Guayana, CVG. He said the government wants this process to attract domestic and foreign investors because they could contribute capital, technology, markets, and management that will make the firms more competitive. Grisanti added that public services currently rendered by the CVG will be transferred to local governments. (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 1 Jun 94) Russian International Oil Company manager Vladimir Sustareyev has reported that representatives from three private Russian oil firms are attending the Maracaibo oil fair and have expressed interest in reaching economic cooperation agreements with the Venezuelan oil industry. (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 1 Jun 94) A survey shows that there were 40,512 less jobs over the past three months when compared to the previous three months, and 109,964 less jobs than in the same three-month period in 1993. Venezuelan Council of Industry President Jorge Redmond said these figures show the difficult situation faced by the manufacturing sector. The poll shows that the economic instability of the past three months has prompted a 22.4 percent decline in production and a 21.8 percent drop in demand. (Caracas EL UNIVERSAL in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 2-7) Noris Aguirre, president of the Deposit Guarantee and Banking Protection Fund [FOGADE], has stated at Miraflores Palace that FOGADE will reorganize or privatize the seven banks and the other financial institution FOGADE is currently assisting if their shareholders do not repay their debts and increase their capital holdings within the scheduled time frame. (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 2 Jun 94) Environment Minister Roberto Perez Lecuna has reported that the executive branch has approved an emergency program to revitalize the water treatment program in the metropolitan area, earmarking 4.2 billion bolivares for this program. Perez reported that "efforts have begun" to obtain a 500-million bolivar loan that will allow "the total reorganization of the Caracas sewer system." (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 31 May 94)
