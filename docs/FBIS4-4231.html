BFN [Text] Beijing, April 28 (CNS) -- The financial bonds valued at RMB 65 billion by the State Development Bank are being issued smoothly. As of April 25, various financial institutions have deposited RMB 20 billion specially for subscription of the first batch of bonds into the bank. The Development Bank founded last March with a registered capital of RMB 50 billion is directly under the State Council and is a policy-oriented bank for the raising of funds and for the directing of capital for the construction of basic industry, infrastructural facilities and pillar industries. President of the bank Mr. Yao Zhenyan said that capital raised from the bonds would go totally on basic items and technological renovation under state planning. Of the funds collected, RMB 60 billion will be for basic construction while the remaining RMB 5 billion will be used for technological renovation. The bonds are divided into two kinds with one for a three-year term bonds are valued at RMB 45.5 billion with an annual yield of 12.5 percent and the five-year term bonds at RMB 19.5 billion with an annual yield of 14 percent. The Development Bank will pay principal and interest calculated on simple interest rate on maturity. The funds raised on this occasion will mainly be for state key items such as the Three Gorges Project and the Beijing-Kowloon railway line. Subscribers are restricted to state-owned commercial banks and non-banking financial institutions, all of which have been selected by the People's Bank of China. They receive an invoice issued by the Development Bank for the subscription of bonds instead of bonds themselves. Mr. Yao said that as the subscription was only confined to financial institutions, it would have no impact on the financial market. He noted that the Development Bank would also issue bonds overseas to raise foreign funds in a bid to broaden the source and channels of capital to serve the economic build-up at home.
