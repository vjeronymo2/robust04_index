March 11, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

LEAN TIMES FOR FOREIGN MOVIES

Since Filmex's demise in 1985, (foreign film) pickings have been mighty slim.

My colleagues and I intend to correct this ridiculous state of affairs with the establishment of the American Cinematheque. Until mid-1993, when we're scheduled to open the doors of our permanent home, it appears that our weekend events and the UCLA Archive programs will make up the mainstream of foreign-language film programming.

Later this year we will be presenting 16 films by the great Japanese masters as part of the Los Angeles Festival.

Meanwhile, I thank Benson for raising this problem. It is certainly an outrage that our nation's second city is so culturally deprived due to lackadaisical distributors.

GARY ESSERT

Artistic director, American

Cinematheque, Hollywood
