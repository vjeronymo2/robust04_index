BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 7 KYODO -- Wholesale and retail sales in Japan dropped an overall 4 percent in 1993 to 673.5 trillion yen, registering the first consecutive yearly decline since statistics of the kind began in 1953, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) said Tuesday [7 June]. Consumers have become more price-conscious amid uncertain prospects for employment and income under the recession, MITI said in a commerce survey. Sales in the wholesale sector declined 4.1 percent and those in the retail sector 3.8 percent, both suffering a drop for the second year in a row, MITI said. The annual survey report, called Japan's Commerce, is compiled on the basis of various commerce-related indicators.
