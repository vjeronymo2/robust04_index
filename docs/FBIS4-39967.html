BFN [Text] The Islamic Democratic Party of Russia nominated for presidency at the upcoming elections in 1996 of its leader, Khadzhi Akhmadiyev. At the congress of the Islamic Party in Moscow, where less than a dozen of representatives of the Moscow and Saratov party organizations took part, it was decided to change the name of the organization for a [as received] Moslem Civilized Party. Its leader Akhmadiyev received the right to appoint personnally people for senior posts of the party. Akhmadiyev has told Interfax, the party has about five thousand members, mainly in places where Tatars live, and considers its potential partners all organization of the democratic spectrum. He said that even if financial aid is offered from abroad, for example from Iran, Iraq or Saudi Arabia, we shall not reject it to be able to take independent decisions without any pressure from the outside, said Akhmadiyev.
