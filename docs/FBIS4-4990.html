BFN [Text] Tokyo -- Kosuke Uehara, director general of the Okinawa Development Agency [ODA], commented on the crash of a U.S. Air Force F-15 fighter which occurred at Kadena Air Base on 4 April, saying: "Two or three seconds later and the accident could have ended in a major disaster. It is very regrettable." ODA Chief Uehara made the remarks at a news conference following a regular cabinet meeting on 5 April. He reemphasized that "the government should make positive efforts to adopt safety measures and prevent accidents." At the news conference, ODA Chief Uehara stated: "Though the accident occurred within the area of the U.S. military facilities, precautions should be taken not to arouse apprehension among local residents." Regarding an investigation into the cause of the crash and restraints on flights, he said: "In my capacity as a state minister, I would like to influence the Foreign Ministry and the Defense Agency." Thus, ODA Chief Uehara revealed his intention to ask ministries and agencies concerned to take measures against the accident.
