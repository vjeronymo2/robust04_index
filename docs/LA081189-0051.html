August 11, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: BRADLEY PICKS COMRIE FOR METRO RAIL PANEL

Los Angeles Mayor Tom Bradley on Thursday appointed City Administrative Officer Keith Comrie to represent the city on a three-member cost-cutting panel to oversee the beleagured Metro Rail project.

The board will monitor construction on the first 4 miles of the subway, which a Los Angeles County Transportation Commission audit found to be $135 million, or 10%, over budget. The overrun triggered a clause in the funding contract between the transportation commission, the Southern California Rapid Transit District and the city that establishes a committee with authority to direct economy measures on the project, which is two years behind.

Supervisor Pete Schabarum or his alternate, political consultant Mike Lewis, represents the LACTC. The SCRTD has not yet appointed its representative. The board's first meeting is scheduled for Aug. 17.
