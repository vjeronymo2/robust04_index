July 25, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: HEART MEDICATION

Question: My father is taking nitroglycerin for angina. I know that it's been around a long time.

Isn't there anything newer and better? New drugs are being developed every day.

Answer: Medication must be determined by the patient's own physician. I have no basis for saying that one medication is better than another for a patient I have never seen.

Having said that, I should add that nitroglycerin is a preferred medication for many patients with stable angina pectoris. A patient can take it after symptoms develop, or as a preventive. Moreover, side effects associated with nitroglycerin, for the most part, are acceptable. Another advantage is that the drug is inexpensive.
