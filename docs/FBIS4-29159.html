BFN [Text] Australia says the international community may have no choice but to take stronger action against North Korea following its decision to withdraw from the International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA]. Camille Funnell reports in a statement issued from New York Australia's foreign minister, Senator Evans, called on North Korea to reconsider its decision: [Begin Funnell recording] Senator Evans said uncertainty about the nature of North Korea's nuclear activity is the cause of instability in the Asia-Pacific region, and he said it undermines the interest of all countries in the region in preventing the spread of nuclear weapons. Senator Evans warned the international community will have little choice but to take stronger action, including through the United Nations Security Council, if North Korea does not display a more constructive position. He said it is in everyone's interest, not the least to North Korea, to reconsider its decision and return to the negotiating table. [end recording]
