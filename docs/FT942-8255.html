940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / Watchdog renews plea on BBC radio Lord Chalfont, chairman of the Radio Authority, the regulatory body for the commercial sector, yesterday renewed his plea that the BBC should give promises of performance on its radio services and stick to them. The decision to turn Radio 5 into a news and sport channel ahead of the launch of a national speech-based commercial radio network underlined the need for such commitments, he said. 'This would ensure that the BBC, in line with the commercial sector, could not make dramatic changes to the character of its services (which could narrow listening choice) without concern for its listeners as a whole,' Lord Chalfont says in the authority's annual report.
