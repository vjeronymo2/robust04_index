March 17, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

PAYING FOR STATE HIGHWAYS

Yes, there's a tax revolt. The reason is plain -- we are stressed out. Federal taxes are climbing for the former middle class, which is now the lower middle class.

All our hopes for climbing the ladder are fading fast in the wake of the Reagan-Bush tax gifts to the rich, combined with the failure of the famous Reagan trickle-down theory.

I would vote for the 9-cent gas tax if it would provide public transportation and road maintenance, but I don't want to see any more freeways built. New roads will service the new people, who are clearly not paying enough taxes to build roads to service their needs, and will not answer the problem anyway -- a new road quickly exceeds its capacity, and the only solution then is to build more roads.

We need more health care, schools, police and jails, and more housing because our population increases, directly or indirectly, lead to more illness, children and crime, and a terrific housing shortage. All this is true because demand far exceeds supply. The only possibility, then, is to cut the demand.

The common theme here is our skyrocketing population. We are not managing the astonishing growth of this state, which is why Californians are crying loudly for better urban planning.

We must do something about it before we all become New Yorkers.

CATHERINE A. STROHLEIN

San Diego
