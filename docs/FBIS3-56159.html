Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By correspondents Shakhnoza Ganiyeva and Sadyr Ziyamukhamedov, including recorded interviews with Kazakhstani President Nursultan Nazarbayev and Uzbekistani President Islam Karimov; from the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] Kazakhstan's President Nursultan Nazarbayev has completed his official visit to Uzbekistan. [Ziyamukhamedov] Following the signing of bilateral interstate agreements between the Republic of Uzbekistan and Kazakhstan and the Mukus conference of the heads of Central Asian states on the problems of the area adjoining the Aral Sea, Presidents Nursultan Nazarbayev and Islam Karimov visited Urgench, the ancient towns of Khiva and Samarkand, where they visited world famous architectural monuments. This concluded president Nursultan Nazarbayev's official visit to Uzbekistan. [Ganiyeva] What are the main political results? What new elements will the meeting contribute toward mutual relations between the two states? [Nazarbayev] First, the meeting concluded six months of work to bring our economic integration closer. Our experts and governments intend the program to last until the year 2000. As a result of this work an agreement was signed on joint economic space. [Karimov, in progress] ...which presupposes, above all, a free economy, a free movement of goods, capital, labor resources, manpower, services, and so forth. My view is that that we are now tackling problems, difficult economic problems, and we are thereby also settling political issues. Today we have made the first step toward integration.
