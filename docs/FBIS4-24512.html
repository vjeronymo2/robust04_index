BFN [By Y. C. Tsai] [Text] Taipei, May 28 (CNA) -- Foreign investors are working to make inroads into the peripheral businesses for the air transportation industry in Taiwan, trade sources said Saturday [28 May]. The sources made the remark after the ministry of economic affairs approved an investment plan by Hong Kong's Alde Burgh Ltd. to open a flight kitchen company here. Alde Burgh is an affiliated firm of the Swire Group, a business conglomerate in the British colony. The project calls for a total investment of NT [new Taiwan)0 $2.27 billion (US$84.07 million), half of which will come from a bank loan. This is the second such project to get the green light here. Earlier this month, the ministry approved a similar investment plan by an American company. The sources forecast that a growing number of foreign enterprises will join domestic air freight peripheral businesses as Taiwan aims to become an Asia-Pacific air transportation center. Moreover, they pointed out, Lockheed Aircraft Corp. of the United States has decided to increase investment in its Taiwan subsidiary by NT$610 million (US$22.59 million) to open a production as well as repair and maintenance center for flight control systems. The capital increment plan has already been approved by the ministry, they added.
