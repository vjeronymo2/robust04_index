Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 11/01/1994 -- A string of projects stipulated in the additional protocol of cooperation in health care between Romania and Turkey was outlined to Premier Nicolae Vacaroiu by Turkish Health Minister Kazim Dinc during the interview at Victoria Palace, on January 11. It is about projects of cooperation in medicine industry and cooperation in third markets in this branch, exchanges of specialists and experience on intensive care as well as in geriatrics and gerontology. The guest also signed in this respect the founding act of a branch in Turkey of the Romanian Institute "Dr. Ana Aslan," under the aegis of the Turkish Health Ministry. Appreciating the additional protocol under negotiation, Premier Nicolae Vacaroiu stressed the Romanian Government's interest in the development of the bilateral relations with traditional partners and especially with Turkey, insisting on the future prospects of these relations. At the close of the meeting, Premier Vacaroiu asked the guest to convey to the Turkish prime minister the invitation to visit Bucharest. "I consider the interviews we have had in Romania as very useful for both parties," Turkish Health Minister Kazim Dinc told the press after his interview with Romanian Premier Nicolae Vacaroiu. The Turkish minister referred then to the protocol concerning the health field signed in 1991, to the goals of his visit, to the ways of developing the health sector, altogether subjects discussed during his interview with the Romanian health minister, Professor Doctor Iulian Mincu. "We, the Government of Turkey, wish to employ Romania's experience in geriatrics and gerontology," said the Turkish minister and added that Turkish doctors would come to Romania for specialization stages in order to become trainers for specialists in the field once they get back to Turkey. An important sector for the development of the Romanian-Turkish cooperation is the medicine industry, said Minister Dinc, who has remarkably expressed in all his statements Turkey's willingness to stand by Romania for the better of the worse.
