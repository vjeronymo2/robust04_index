July 15, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

FOR SOME READERS, TIFFANY IS NO JETSON

Two months ago a producer-type sat me down and told me that what my movie (a musical) needed, in order to sell big, was Tiffany.

He said I should dump the actress I had and think "soundtrack album deal" instead.

That kind of individual is not concerned with entertaining and enlightening an audience with a good movie but whether the neon-colored marketing campaign is in place before anyone has written a script!

As one entertainment attorney droned on to me during a recent meeting, "All they want is Tom Cruise in a race car." If this is becoming the norm, then this business is headed for serious trouble.

RICHARD WAY

Santa Monica
