'The Yen Family'

Japan, 1988, 113 minutes

8:25 p.m.

Yojiro Takita's hilarious satire on a suburban family so money-grubbing that it runs a newspaper distributorship and a catering service -- both exploiting the elderly -- right out of the kitchen (where the mother is also operating her erotic wake-up service while she prepares breakfast). As the film becomes increasingly outrageous it develops considerable complexity, and its indictment of materialism, Japanese-style, is often ironic and paradoxical. Like Takita's earlier "Comic Magazine," a corrosive satire on the excesses of the media, "The Yen Family" is a swift, satisfying treat. RECOMMENDED KEVIN THOMAS
