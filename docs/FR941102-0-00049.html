Subsequent to the LIFFE request, the Commission published a proposal to amend Rule 3a12&hyph;8 to include Spanish sovereign debt. Shortly thereafter, LIFFE ceased to trade futures on Spanish debt. Recently, the Commission has been apprised of continuing interest by market participants in the proposed amendment to Rule 3a12&hyph;8 because of the trading on the MEFF of futures on Spanish government debt. The Commission is amending Rule 3a12&hyph;8 to add Spain to the list of countries whose debt obligations are deemed to be ``exempted securities'' under the terms of the Rule. Under this amendment, the existing conditions set forth in the Rule ( i.e., that the underlying securities not be registered in the United States, that the futures contracts require delivery outside the United States, and that the contracts be traded on a board of trade) would continue to apply. This should ensure that a domestic market in the unregistered foreign sovereign debt of Spain does not develop. Therefore, the amendment should pose no risk for investors in the U.S. securities market. III.
