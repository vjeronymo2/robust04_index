November 15, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

DR. JOYCE BROTHERS: A TEST FOR YOUR VIEWS ON FITNESS

Are you a fitness buff? How much do you know about exercise -- what it will and won't do for you? Here's a chance to test your views with those of some experts.

1) The more you exercise the healthier you'll be and the better your sex life will be. T( ) F( )

2) Those who start regular aerobic exercise programs usually stick with it because they enjoy it so much. T( ) FALSE( )

3) Most of your close friends, your lover or your spouse can be expected to support your exercise program wholeheartedly. T( ) F( )

4) There's a hormonal link between exercise and sexual desire. T( ) F( )

5) Older people who exercise regularly have no better sex life than the couch potatoes who don't. T( ) F( )

6) There are no cases of people being addicted to exercise. T( ) F( )

7) People in low-level jobs don't need exercise programs as much as executives because they have less stress at work. T( ) F( )

Answers:

1) False. There's a limit to everything, and moderation is the key. For both men and women, extremely rigorous exercise diminishes sexual desire. If you constantly exhaust yourself, you simply don't have the energy for sex.

2) False. While most people seem to enjoy aerobic exercise, half of those who start such a regimen drop out within the first six months.

3) False. Surprisingly, this isn't always the case, according to psychologist Kenneth Matheny who found that the inactive partner often sets up subtle roadblocks or creates situations in which the partner must choose between the relationship and exercise. Some friends, lovers and spouses are threatened by exercisers' improved appearance and self-confidence.

4) False. This hasn't been proven. Psychosocial factors are probably much more important. When people feel better about themselves, when their self-esteem and self-confidence grows, so does their libido.

5) False. A study of swimmers conducted by anthropologist Phillip Whitten revealed that those in their 60s were nearly as sexually active as those in their 40s, and those in their 40s had intercourse about as often as those in their 20s and 30s.

6) False. Apparently, some people do become addicted to exercise, especially to running. As with other addicts, they begin to use exercise as a way to escape all their problems.

7) False. They probably need exercise even more than high-level workers because they have more stress on the job. Those who have less control at work seem to suffer from stress more and may be more susceptible to heart disease as a result.
