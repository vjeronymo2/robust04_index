940311 FT 11 MAR 94 / Observer: Mighty Moet Oh lawks. The ad-men are about to try to put some fizz back into the bubbly business. To be precise, Moet & Chandon is considering advertising for the first time in its 250-year history. A shortlist of three advertising agencies has been invited to argue the case for this brash commercial move. The haughty champagne is under attack, like all its competitors, from upstart Aussie imitators and a reduction in world demand. Guinness, the famous stout with a record of clever-clogs advertising, including the punning slogan 'Pure Genius', took a one-third stake in the brand at the start of 1994. Here's a free start: 'Moet might be good for you.'
