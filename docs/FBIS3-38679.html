Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The prime minister has declared that his government will go ahead with the economic liberalization process, ensuring that Indianness and identity of the nation is not diminished in any way. He said the present transition in the economic sector aims at accomplishing a liberal trading regime to overcome the macroeconomic crisis. Mr. Narasimha Rao was speaking at the historic Humboldt University in Berlin today. He called for a determined fight against prejudices to help create a new humanism based on trust and respect for one another. He said we should live up to our worthy heritage by harmonizing our energies to promote Indo-German relations. The prime minister said it is a matter of pride for him to be in a free, united Berlin. [passage omitted] Mr. Narasimha Rao is scheduled to inaugurate the Indian Cultural Center at Berlin later tonight, before leaving for New Delhi at the end of his four-day state visit to Germany. Briefing newsmen, an official spokesman described the prime minister's visit to Germany as a landmark. He said the German industry and business got the opportunity to have an in-depth understanding of what is happening in India on the liberalization front. He said the meetings the prime minister had with German industrialists provided an opportunity for face-to-face interaction.
