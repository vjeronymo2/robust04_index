BFN [Text] Moscow, 23 Jun (BUSINESS-TASS) -- Russian Central Bank chairman Viktor Gerashchenko believes that one should not expect an upsurge in inflation in Russia during the second quarter of this year. A rough analysis of the economic situation, he said today, shows that the government guidelines on money supply for the second quarter will be observed. According to available information, over the first four months of this year, the average monthly growth rate of the money supply was 10 percent while growth in cash supply was 13 percent, which is within the established targets. Viktor Gerashchenko was giving a report on the results of the Russian Bank in 1993 and current tasks at a conference with heads of the main territorial directorates (national banks). The main aim of the conference, in which representatives of the State Duma and of the staffs of the president and government of the Russian Federation are also taking part, is to discover the main problems in the work of the Central Bank system and to define solutions to them.
