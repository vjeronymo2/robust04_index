Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Doha, 24 Mar (PETRA)--The Jordanian side, led by His Majesty King Husayn, and the Qatari side, led by His Highness Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani, the amir of Qatar, resumed their talks at Amiri Court today. His majesty presented an analysis of the dimensions of the Arab reality and the challenges facing the nation. He stressed the need to reinforce Arab solidarity, combine efforts, and unite stands out of concern for the nation's interests and the future of its generations, especially at this critical juncture in its history and in light of the challenges facing it. Discussions dealt with the Middle East peace process and the suffering of the kinsfolk in the occupied homeland, that culminated in the massacre at the holy Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron. The two sides stressed the importance of coordination among Arab countries, especially in terms of taking part in the peace process. They emphasized the importance of consultations among Arab countries in order to avoid making mistakes, selling out, or neglecting any of the nation's rights. His Majesty King Husayn thoroughly explained what Jordan did in the wake of the holy Ibrahimi Mosque massacre and the steps and measures it took before the UN Security Council adopted its recent resolution on the ugly massacre. The two sides discussed Jordan's continuing efforts to protect the Islamic holy places in holy Jerusalem, especially His Majesty King Husayn's efforts and concern about restoring the holy Dome of the Rock and rebuilding Salah-al-Din's rostrum just as it was before. They discussed relations between the two fraternal countries in the political, economic, and cultural fields and on various levels. The talks were permeated by a spirit of brotherhood, understanding, and eagerness to reinforce cooperation between the two fraternal countries and to continue meetings between officials to achieve this purpose. His Majesty King Husayn extended an invitation to His Highness Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani, the amir of Qatar, and to His Highness Hamad Bin-Khalifah Al Thani, the Qatari heir apparent and defense minister, to visit Jordan. They accepted the invitation and promised to respond to it as soon as possible. [passage omitted]
