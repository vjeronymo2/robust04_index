930928 FT 28 SEP 93 / International Company News: Fortis Australia buys TGIO THE TASMANIAN Government Insurance Office (TGIO) has been sold to Fortis Australia for ADollars 53.5m (USDollars 35m), Reuter reports from Hobart. Fortis Australia is part of the Fortis Dutch-Belgian insurance group. The decision to sell TGIO was announced last year by the state government of Tasmania. The move is aimed at reducing the state's ADollars 3bn of borrowings. Mr Tony Rundle, TGIO treasurer, said the group would retain its Tasmanian identity, with its head office to remain in the state. The deal is subject to approval from the Foreign Investment Review Board.
