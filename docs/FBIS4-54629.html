BFN [Text] Malaysia and Albania have signed a landmark memorandum of understanding [MOU], paving the way for Malaysian military officers to train the soldiers of the former communist republic. For a start, Malaysia has agreed to train 25 Albanian officers this year. The MOU was signed by Defense Minister Datuk Sri Najib Tun Razak and his Albanian counterpart Encik [Mr.] Safet Zhulali in Tirana, Albania. Under the MOU, Malaysian and Albanian armed forces will also hold exchange programs and joint exercises, cooperate in the fields of science and technology, as well as embark on business cooperation in the defense industry. Datuk Sri Najib said Malaysia would also assist the Eastern European state in the modernization and the upgrading of its armed forces. Speaking at the signing ceremony, he said the MOU would pave the way for closer cooperation between the two countries. Briefing Malaysian journalists later, he said Malaysia had approved a special fund of 2.6 million ringgit to assist in the development of the Albanian Armed Forces.
