BFN [Report by INTERFAX Diplomatic Panorama correspondents Igor Porshnev, Vitaly Trubetskoi and others] [Text] The problem of division of the Black Sea Fleet warships and launches, which is discussed by the Russian and Ukrainian delegations in Kiev, has been nearly solved. The statement was made Thursday [9 June] before the beginning of the next round of talks by commander of the Ukrainian Navy, Vladimir Bezkorovainy. Speaking about the problem of basing, Bezkorovainy said that the Ukrainian Navy could also use the bases leased to the Russian Navy. The head of the Russian delegation at the talks, Yuri Dubinin marked the constructive and friendly situation at the talks. He said that according to the understanding Ukraine should have 18.3 pct of Black Sea Fleet's warships. Dubinin said if the sides fail to achieve a definite result on Thursday, we shall invite the Ukrainian delegation to Moscow to continue talks. Russian Ambassador in Kiev Leonid Smolyakov told INTERFAX-Ukraine that still unresolved are the conditions and deployment period of the Russian Navy in the Ukrainian territory.
