BFN [Text] President-elect Armando Calderon Sol, upon his return yesterday from Mexico City, confirmed that the government will sign a free trade agreement with Mexico before the end of the year. Calderon Sol, who will assume the presidency on 1 June, said that "if negotiations continue at their current rate," the treaty could be signed before the end of President Carlos Salinas de Gortari's term, set for 1 December. The president-elect met with several ministers and local business representatives during his visit to Mexico. The purpose was to measure the degree of interest in investments in El Salvador. He said this was only possible "thanks to the stability of the democratic system and the peace process, which have given the county prestige." In statements to the Mexican press, Calderon Sol expressed his willingness to establish a common market, since this would open operations to 120 million consumers, 30 million of which are in the Central American isthmus. If no agreement is signed during President Salinas de Gortari's mandate, talks would continue with the new president to be elected on 21 August. The new Salvadoran president said that signing a free trade agreement with Mexico would be the first step toward reaching a similar agreement with the United States. On 23 May Calderon Sol will fly to Washington to meet Vice President Al Gore, who will host a summit meeting several days later to discuss NAFTA with all of the Central American presidents. In commenting on his scheduled trip to UN headquarters in New York, the president-elect said he had "no concerns at all about addressing any topic with the secretary general." He was referring to statements made by Chief Justice Mauricio Gutierrez Castro, of the Supreme Court of Justice, during the opening session of the Legislative Assembly. He explained the purpose of his trip was to express the political will to fully comply with the peace agreements and to request aid from the United Nations, through agencies such as UNICEF, FAO, and the WHO. He said that, belonging to this international forum, El Salvador has a right to the programs these agencies offer. Therefore, it can request such aid "with dignity and authority."
