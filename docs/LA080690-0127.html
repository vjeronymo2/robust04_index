August 6, 1990, Monday, P.M. Final

SHORT TAKES;

SINGING BETTER, DENVER SAYS

John Denver hasn't had a hit record in 10 years, but he says his voice has improved with age.

Denver, who will perform Wednesday at the Deer Creek Music Center north of Indianapolis, said he struggled during the 1980s to keep his career afloat.

"It was always hard to get my songs on the radio," he said. "But when they did get airplay, a lot of people liked the music, and I still have an unusually large audience for not having had a hit record in that time."

Denver's album "Earth Songs," which is sold through the National Wildlife Federation, includes new versions of the hits "Sunshine on My Shoulders," "Rocky Mountain High" and "The Eagle and the Hawk."

"I'm singing so much differently than I was 20 years ago," Denver said. "There's a much greater range of emotion in each and every word. And I'm singing better. All of that tends to make it a different recording."
