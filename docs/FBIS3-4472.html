Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hangzhou, March 30 (XINHUA) -- East China's Zhejiang Province is to have its first exchange market for foreign currency in April. Approved by the Zhejiang branch of the State Administration of Exchange Control, the market will be run by the international trust and investment company of the province. Investors, including state-owned enterprises, Sino-foreign joint ventures, private businesses and individuals, can trade on the market in the prime hard currencies, such as U.S. dollars, Deutsche marks, Japanese yen, pounds sterling and Swiss francs. The market will be able to display the latest world market exchange rates, as it will be linked up with the terminals of REUTERS NEWS AGENCY.
