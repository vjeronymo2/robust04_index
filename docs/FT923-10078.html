920801 FT 01 AUG 92 / UK Company News: NatPower chief's pay doubles as jobs go MR JOHN BAKER chief executive of National Power earned Pounds 383,000 in the year to March, 1992, more than double his remuneration of Pounds 147,000 in the previous financial year, writes Peggy Hollinger. This was revealed in the company's latest annual accounts published yesterday, which also show that the company shed some 3,092 jobs last year leaving the total at 11,421. A further 2,400 job losses are planned. National Power said it intended to withdraw from industry-wide negotiating committees by the end of 1992, and would make its own arrangement for negotiating with unions. Mr Baker's basic salary, which has been increased twice since the spring of 1991, came to Pounds 246,000 for the year to March 31.
