BFN [Text] Cairo, 2 May (MENA)--President Husni Mubarak will address a ceremony in the Conference Hall in Madinat Nasr at 1100 [0900 GMT] on Wednesday [4 May] for the signing of the Palestinian-Israeli agreement. Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin will also address the ceremony, after which the U.S. and Russian foreign ministers will deliver speeches before signing of the agreement. Announcing this, Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa said Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, the foreign ministers of the United States, Russia, Norway, Spain, the Netherlands, Japan, Morocco, and Qatar, and the Omani and Tunisian ministers of state for foreign affairs will arrive in Cairo tomorrow, Tuesday. Musa added that a bilateral meeting will be held tomorrow between Palestinian President 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin and that meetings will be held with the foreign ministers who are due to arrive in Cairo. The foreign minister said the agreement is a step along the road and not the end of the Palestinian-Israeli track. Replying to a question on the U.S. efforts to advance negotiations on the Syrian-Israeli track, Musa said: We are talking about a comprehensive peace process. Egypt backs all serious and important steps that lead to progress on the Syrian-Israeli track.
