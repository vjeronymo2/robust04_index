January 7, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

SETTING THE STAGE FOR PEACE

Al-Marayati and Ibrahim chastise Israel for the settlements on the West Bank as an obstacle to peace. From 1948 to 1967, the West Bank was in Arab hands; there were no Jewish settlements. That time was characterized by continual terrorist attacks on Israeli citizens, buses, schoolchildren, markets and kibbutzim.

As for speaking with the PLO, it is no surprise that Israel chooses not to sit down with a sworn enemy actively engaged in a sustained assault against it, denying its right to exist, disavowing any negotiations and any peace. Let the PLO demonstrate any good intentions of its words with actions; or, better yet, let the Palestinian people elect representatives who do not hold office by force, and let them offer Israel an opportunity to come to the negotiation table without preconditions.

Every Israeli prime minister from David Ben Gurion to Yitzhak Shamir have extended hopeful invitations, even please, to its Arab neighbors for direct negotiations without preconditions. The Arab response has been consistent: no recognition, no negotiation, no peace. This is, and has been, the essential barrier to peace in the Mideast.

Israel is prepared to go the extra mile for peace. It is not willing to go to national suicide.

SHELDON KRONFELD

San Diego
