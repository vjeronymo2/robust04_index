Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Wuhan, March 3 (XINHUA) -- Living conditions for rural residents in central China's Hubei Province have improved greatly over the past 15 years, according to a recent survey carried out in rural areas of the province. The survey says that the per capita living space for rural residents increased from 11.8 sq m in 1978 to 25 sq m in 1993. Seventy percent of the newly-built houses are made of bricks and timber or bricks and concrete. In Yunyang Prefecture of northwestern Hubei, more than 14,000 rural households have moved into newly-built houses from caves, shabby thatched shacks, or endangered houses. The city government of Enshi, in southwestern Hubei, earmarked funds for helping to build new houses to move its last group of 400 rural households who used to live in caves. Many new villages for fishermen, as well as a large number of house-boats made of cement, have been built in many of the province's lake areas, helping more and more fishermen settle down in fixed abodes.
