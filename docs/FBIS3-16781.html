Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondent Oleg Velichko] [Text] Magnitogorsk March 18 TASS -- "The present system of taxing enterprises when taxes sometimes total 80 per cent of revenues, must be changed," Russian First Vice-Premier Oleg Soskovets told journalists on Friday after a conference on how to make the Magnitogorsk iron and steel combine a joint stock society. According to Soskovets, taxes should be cut down to 45 per cent, as is the case in civilised countries. "Tax legislation should stimulate production and not to pave grounds for concealing taxes, as is the case now," the vice-premier stressed. Soskovets noted that the government plans to examine the question of cutting the value-added tax in the near future. He stressed that it is necessary to "lift customs duties from goods crossing borders inside the Commonwealth countries".
