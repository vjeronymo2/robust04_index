930925 FT 25 SEP 93 / Finance and the Family: Building society outflow worsens - At a glance BUILDING societies suffered their worst outflow of funds this year, when Pounds 132m was withdrawn in August following smaller net outflows in the previous two months. There was a Pounds 61m outflow in July and a Pounds 56m outflow in June. Adrian Coles, director-general of the Building Societies Association, said the recent outflows of Pounds 249m needed to be balanced against a Pounds 2.5bn inflow in the first half. He said that people were withdrawing money to invest in equities, buy cars and fund the BT3 share offer. On the mortgage side, net new commitments of Pounds 2.43bn were the lowest since January. Coles said: 'There are growing signs that house prices have stabilised. This should help to underpin a recovery in activity over the coming months, albeit from low levels.'
