930519 FT 19 MAY 93 / The Danish Referendum: London celebration is dignified affair A CROSS-SECTION of the 40,000-strong Danish community in London crowded into the Danish embassy last night to salute the Yes vote with warm Tuborg beer and sausage sandwiches. A Danish embassy official explained his frustration at not being able to deliver the scene of excitement desired by a television cameraman. Mr Rudolph Thorning Petersen, the Danish ambassador, explained in dignified tones the Yes would lead to a more open Community. Even though the 56.8-43.2 per cent margin showed a large minority of Danes were suspicious of the EC, the main point was 'we have a majority'. Asked about the presence in Denmark of British politicians and journalists touting for No votes, the ambassador said, for quotation, Denmark always liked to extend hospitality. Privately, he was more scathing.
