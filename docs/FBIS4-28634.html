BFN [Report by Beijing-based correspondent Yi Tong-sik] [Text] A member of the inspection team of the International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA], who remained in North Korea and observed the process of the replacement of the fuel rods of the nuclear reactor in Yongbyon, arrived in Beijing today. The member of the inspection team, who is confirmed to be (Vladimir Luklo), came to Beijing aboard a North Korean Koryo airliner this morning, with baggage which looked like verification documents, without carrying inspection equipment. The inspection team member (Luklo), arriving at the Beijing airport, refused to reply to the questions on the progress of the fuel rod replacement of the nuclear reactor in Yongbyon, asking the reporters to confirm it through the IAEA headquarters. He added that he would return to Vienna as soon as possible. The inspection team member went to North Korea with Heinonen, chief of the inspection section, on 17 May, and remained in North Korea after Section Chief Heinonen returned to the IAEA headquarters with Inspection Department Director Perricos on 28 May and observed the process of the fuel rod replacement of the nuclear reactor in Yongbyon.
