BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Staroselskiy] [Text] Moscow June 17 TASS -- Russia and Israel have agreed to develop and sell on the world market a new aircraft - the YAK-48- Galaxy. This was announced by President of the Russian Chamber of Commerce and Industry Stanislav Smirnov today. He praised direct contacts between entrepreneurs of the two countries established at the exhibition Israel-94 in Moscow. Chairman of the Council of Directors and Chief Designer of the Yakovlev Design bureau Aleksandr Dondukov explained that the YAK-48 is an eight-seat government and business aircraft with a unique range of 6,800 kilometers. The contract signed between Russia and Israel provides for the development of the project on the basis of advanced Russian technologies. It will be possible to use engines and electronics made in third countries. The new aircraft will be partially assembled and sold by Israel, which is now controlling 25 percent of the world market for this category of planes. Dondukov said that the first YAK-48 will be ready in 1995, and its mass production and sale on the world market will begin in 1997.
