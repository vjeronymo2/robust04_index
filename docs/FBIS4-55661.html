BFN [Unattributed report: "Slovaks Balance the First Quarter"] [Text] Bratislava--Over the first three months of this year, Slovakia reached a surplus of 2.2 billion Slovak korunas [Sk] in trade with the Czech Republic, Stefan Condik, chairman of the Statistical Office of the Slovak Republic (SUSR), said at a news conference in Bratislava yesterday. Imports from the Czech Republic amounted to Sk14.3 billion during this period (30.2 percent of the total imports to Slovakia), and goods worth Sk16.5 billion were exported to the Czech Republic (37.9 percent of total Slovak exports). These data do not include reexports, however. Using the statistical method according to the trading country, Slovakia had a deficit of Sk3.6 billion in trade with the Czech Republic. Stefan Condik said that the trend of deceleration of the growth of prices emerged in Slovakia in the first quarter of 1994. Inflation rose on the whole, according to the SUSR data for the first quarter, by 2.6 percent in comparison with December of last year, and it was to a certain degree affected by the development in wages. Wages in industry rose by 16.3 percent from February to reach Sk6,291, and in construction by 15.7 percent, to Sk5,919. Salaries in retailing rose 10 percent, to Sk4,714, and in transport 6.7 percent, to Sk6,119. The average monthly wages for the entire national economy reached the amount of Sk5,400 in the first quarter, which is 14 to 15 percent (Sk700) more than in the same period last year. There were 370,500 unemployed in Slovakia in March, which is approximately 14.7 percent of those able to work. In comparison with February (14.8 percent rate of unemployment), the number of unemployed declined by 5,500 people. The effect of the introduction of the import surcharge became obvious in March, and the trade deficit rose by only Sk35 million. The overall balance of trade ended with a deficit of Sk3.7 billion for the quarter. Goods worth Sk47.2 billion were imported to Slovakia during the first three months of the year, which is 1 percent more than in the same period of 1993. Exports amounted to Sk43.5 billion, which is a 2.6-percent drop in the year-to-year comparison. The value of foreign capital in the Slovak economy reached Sk11.5 billion at the end of March. That is a 6.7-percent increase in comparison with the last quarter of 1993. Austria is responsible for the main share of investments in the Slovak economy (23.4 percent), followed by Germany (21 percent). The Czech Republic (whose share rose to 13 percent) recently passed the United States (12.1) percent). Foreign capital is invested in 5,719 companies. Since the beginning of the year, 35 firms with foreign capital participation closed down in Slovakia, and another 80 firms are being liquidated.
