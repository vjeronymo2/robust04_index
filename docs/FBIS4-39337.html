BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Andrey Kirillov] [Text] Moscow, 7 Jun -- China does not support the imposition of economic sanctions on the DPRK because this measure "can only lead to an escalation of tensions on the Korean peninsula." This was said today in an interview with the ITAR-TASS correspondent by Sui Tsin, minister-counsellor at the Chinese embassy in Moscow. He described reports in the Hong Kong press about the possibility of China joining the sanctions as untrustworthy. China has limited influence on the DPRK, the diplomat added, and it depends on the Koreans themselves whether North Korea finally accepts the IAEA's demands concerning its nuclear program. The Chinese diplomat spoke in favor of continuing negotiations and looking for compromises.
