BFN [Text] Nicosia, May 29 (AFP) -- Gunmen in a taxi shot dead an Iranian dissident in an attack north of the Iraqi capital on Sunday, Iran's main armed opposition group said, blaming Tehran for the killing. The People's Mujahedeen, in a communique sent from Baghdad, said Seyeed Ahmad Sadr Lahijani, a 35-year-old member of the group, was shot in the head as he drove his car through Ghalebieh, 50 kilometres (30 miles) from the capital. The Iranian Embassy in Baghdad was behind the attack, it charged.
