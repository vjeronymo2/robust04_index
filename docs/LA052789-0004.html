May 27, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

L.A. TV'S COVERAGE OF CUP TAKES HIT INTO THE BOARDS

Wayne Gretzky has definitely had an impact on the city of Los Angeles, but I'm afraid L.A. is still a far cry from what one would call a hockey town.

Case in point: Game 2 of the Stanley Cup finals was tape-delayed to air an Angel baseball game.

I thank Rockey Flintermann of Z Channel for bringing me back to my senses. For a while I thought hockey had emerged from the preempt syndrome.

RON MANN

Van Nuys
