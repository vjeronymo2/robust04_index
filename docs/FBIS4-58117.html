BFN [Text] Cairo, 30 Apr (MENA) -- President Muhammad Husni Mubarak has addressed an appeal to Yemeni leaders to stop the fighting among the Yemeni Armed Forces. In his appeal, the president warned that the persistence of the armed clashes will further aggravate the situation and inflict grave harm on fraternal Yemen's present and future. President Mubarak called on the Yemeni leaders to return the northern and southern armed troops to their positions to stop the ongoing fighting that has killed and wounded hundreds in the past few days. The president called on President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, in his capacity as president and higher commander of the Armed Forces, to intervene with his wisdom to stop the fighting in the northern part of the country and to organize matters in a manner that averts friction and provocation and preserves Yemen's unity and the higher interests of its sons. Concluding his appeal, President Mubarak appealed to the Yemeni leaders to restrain themselves, engage in a rational and logical dialogue, and respond to efforts to preserve Yemen's wellbeing and the unity of its sons.
