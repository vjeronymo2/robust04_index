930203 FT 03 FEB 93 / Parliament and Politics: Resigned commuters wait to be impressed THE proposals were greeted by commuters yesterday with a mixture of resignation, frustration and confusion. At London's Fenchurch Street station most passengers said they would accept privatisation if it entailed a better service. 'They can't do a much worse job than they're already doing,' said Mr Lance Bennet, a junior manager at Sainsbury's, and a regular commuter on the line to Tilbury and Southend, Essex, dubbed the 'misery line' because of overcrowding and delays, and one of the candidates for franchising. Other passengers said the areas that needed improvement and investment were the quality of rolling stock and punctuality. 'Nothing is ever on time, and they give feeble excuses when it does come,' grumbled Ms Vicky Thorogood, a bank clerk. Like many others, though, she admitted that she 'hadn't much clue' about what a franchise actually was. However, Mr Bill Simpson, a manager who commutes from Barking, Essex, said: 'I'm opposed. I don't think that privatisation is good for the public when it's heavily weighted against the public.'
