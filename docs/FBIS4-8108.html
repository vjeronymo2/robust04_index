BFN [Text] Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs -- ahead of his scheduled visit to Greece -- today received Pavlos Apostolidis, director of the Greek Foreign Ministry Political Relations Office, and the Zagreb-based head of the EC monitoring mission, Greek Ambassador Constantin Yerocostopoulos [names as heard]. The meeting was also attended by Croatian Deputy Foreign Minister Ivo Sanader. Granic informed his guests about the course of the implementation of the cease-fire agreement between the Croatian authorities and the local Serbs living in UN protected areas, and announced that negotiations would resume next week. Speaking about the peace process in Bosnia-Herzegovina, Granic stressed the importance of the establishment of a total cease-fire in Bosnia-Herzegovina and the resumption of peace talks. He said that the Serb side should immediately join the talks and that the European Union should be more efficient. Granic and Sanader also spoke about preparations for the reconstruction of Croatia, for which it is necessary to obtain economic aid, particularly from the European Union, as Croatia is a European country and wishes to join its political and economic trends. Granic and his guests agreed that relations between Croatia and Greece are good, and that the visit by the Croatian foreign minister to Greece will contribute to their future bilateral cooperation.
