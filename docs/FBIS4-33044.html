BFN [From the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] Asdrubal Batista, Reform of the National Economy minister, on the night of 30 May submitted his irrevocable resignation from the post he occupies, because he disagrees with some changes in the economic policies that are being analyzed. Vicente Briceno has the story: [Begin Briceno recording] It seems the resignation of Batista, who was responsible for devising short-, mid-, and long-term economic strategies, is because of alleged disagreements with Finance Minister Julio Sosa Rodriguez, who has steered the economic path toward positions Batista refuses to accept. It was known the Executive Branch will not replace Dr. Batista, because it is believed he achieved most of the tasks President Rafael Caldera assigned to him. One such task was to draft a report on the prospects of the Venezuelan Corporation of Guayana. Due to this situation, the economic expert reportedly refused to attend the questioning session scheduled for 1 June at the National Congress. The Miraflores Palace could not confirm the official report on Minister Batista's resignation. However, unofficial spokesmen reported it as fact. [end recording]
