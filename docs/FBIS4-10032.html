BFN [Text] Havana, 4 Apr (PL) -- Today, Joe Jacob, vice president of Ireland's lower house, began the second day of his visit in which he is expressing his solidarity with Cuba and exploring the possibilities for broadening commercial and cooperation links. Cuban parliament sources told PRENSA LATINA that Jacob is heading a delegation comprising five parliamentarians whose goal is to encourage good relations between the two countries and to continue broadening them. The parliamentarians will meet with Jose Ramon Balaguer, member of the Council of State of the Communist Party of Cuba; Isabel Allende, vice minister of foreign relations; and Carlos Salsamendi, president of the Chamber of Commerce. Official sources noted that the visitors expressed their desire to seek ways to strengthen relations between the legislative bodies of the two nations. During their stay, which will continue until 10 April, the visitors are also interested in learning about the tobbaco industry as they explore areas in which Ireland and Cuba might broaden trade relations. The Irish delegation will visit Pinar del Rio Province, Cuba's westernmost province, as well as public health, agricultural, and sports centers. Public health, agriculture, and sports are areas in which they will study prospects for broadening and diversifying cooperation.
