Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum, 14 Mar (SUNA) -- Lieutenant General 'Umar al-Bashir, the president of the Republic, will leave for the Kenyan capital, Nairobi, on 16 March at the invitation of Kenyan President Daniel arap Moi. SUNA has learned that the Kenyan president had invited Lt. Gen. al-Bashir to brief him on the meeting of the Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development [IGADD] presidents held in Nairobi on 9 March with a view to preparing for a round of peace talks under the sponsorship of the IGADD grouping. There will also be consultations between the two presidents on the agenda of the round due to start in Nairobi on 17 March. SUNA has learned that Mr. Muhammad al-Amin Khalifah, chairman of the Transitional National Assembly, will lead the government delegation to the talks in Nairobi. The government delegation will also include the official spokesman of the delegation of the government. The delegation of Garang's faction will be headed by Silva Kiir Mayar Dit. Other members of the delegation will be Deng Alor and Husayn (Bak). The United Faction and other dissident factions have also been invited to participate in the negotiations. [Omdurman Republic of Sudan Radio Network in Arabic at 1700 GMT on 14 March, in a related report, adds the following: "The government delegation will also include Dr. 'Ali al-Hajj Muhammad, the minister at the Office of Federal Rule and the official spokesman of the government delegation to the negotiations; Dr. Ahmad Ibrahim al-Tahir, minister of state and legal adviser to the president of the Republic; and others.
