November 15, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

OTHER COMMENTARY;

OTHERS MAY MERIT HOLIDAY HONOR

Allegations about King's extramarital exploits and recent charges of plagiarism of his college papers take nothing away from his accomplishments as the lightning rod for black Americans seeking government recognition of their endowed, unalienable rights. What it does is support those who felt more time was needed to study King's life and background before he was honored with national and state holidays.

Black giants like Booker T. Washington, George Washington Carver and Frederick Douglass may be even more suitable subjects to honor with a holiday or other recognition because their lives have withstood the test of historical scrutiny.
