BFN [Excerpt] East Timor will have an observer at the Lusophone countries summit. The proposal was made and the response was also positive. What remains to be seen is who will represent East Timor: [Begin Ramos Horta, Revolutionary Front for an Independent East Timor, spokesman abroad telephone recording] What we want is to obtain a status of a permanent observer country. East Timor is not yet an independent country but we have the right to have an observer representation at the summit. If East Timor were an independent country, we would then be a country with full rights. We have been told by the PALOPs [Portuguese Speaking African Countries] and Brazil that the East Timor issue will be on the agenda, that there will be an East Timorese resistance representation, and what remains to be defined is who will represent East Timor, who will represent the East Timorese resistance. [end recording] [passage omitted]
