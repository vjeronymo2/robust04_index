Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, Feb. 9, IRNA -- Armenia's Foreign Minister Vahan Papazyan here Tuesday appreciated Iran's efforts to establish peace and stability in the region. Referring to various agreements reached between the two countries as well as the exchange of delegations, Papazyan in a telephone interview with IRNA stressed that Tehran-Yerevan relations are friendly. He also praised Iran's mediation efforts and good-will in resolving the Karabakh issue. He also assessed as positive the political, economic and cultural cooperation between the two countries and underlined the need to further expand these ties. Papazyan said his country agreed with Iranian and Russian views on preventing the internationalization of the Karabakh conflict, but regretted that no international organization had taken any positive step in this regard.
