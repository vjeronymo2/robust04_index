Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Thailand has been cooperating with the United States, Britain, and Nigeria in suppressing drug trafficking in Nigeria leading to seizure of 425 kilograms of heroin. Commissioner of the Narcotics Suppression Bureau attached to the Police Department Police Lieutenant General Kowit Phakdiphum said many Thai Government officers have jointly worked with their counterparts in those countries. They include the Narcotics Suppression Bureau, the Central Investigation Bureau, the Customs Department, and the Office of the Narcotics Control Board working in cooperation with the U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration, the British Customs Agency, Nigerian authorities in cracking down on international drug smuggling rings. Investigation shows that Nigerian drug traffickers come to Thailand to smuggle a container of heroin from a port in Thailand to the Nigerian capital of Lagos via Singapore. On December 31, 1993, the Nigerian authorities seized the container of 259 kilograms of heroin in Lagos. The 166 kilograms of heroin seized earlier in Thailand from the accused Nigerian had been sent to the Nigerian authorities. In this mission, the total of 425 kilograms of heroin was seized from the accused Nigerian. There was no Thai involved. The commissioner of the Naroctics Suppression Bureau said the mission has achieved its aim of breaking down an important drug racket.
