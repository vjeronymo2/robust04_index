July 19, 1989, Wednesday, P.M. Final

NATION;

D.C. PLOT NAMED TIAN AN MEN PARK

The Senate voted today to name a grassy plot near the Chinese Embassy in Washington "Tian An Men Square Park" in honor of pro-democracy demonstrators in Beijing.

In addition to memorializing demonstrators who were killed by the Chinese army, said Sen. Malcolm Wallop (R-Wyo.), who sponsored the legislation, the action serves "to remind Beijing that America will never and can never forget the ruthlessness and callousness of the Chinese government in crushing their citizen-demonstrators and their aspirations." The amendment was added by voice vote to a bill authorizing State Department programs for the new budget year.
