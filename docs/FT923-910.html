920925 FT 25 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Atlas Converting declines 26% Reduced operating profit, coupled with higher interest charges, meant Atlas Converting Equipment showed a drop of 26 per cent in pre-tax profits for the first half of 1992. From turnover of Pounds 20.6m (Pounds 21.5m), operating profit came to Pounds 2.54m (Pounds 3.07m). Interest costs were Pounds 267,000 (Pounds 13,000). Earnings per share were 18.4p (24.6p) and the interim dividend is again 7p. Mr Christopher Rogers, chairman, said turnover was lower than anticipated as a result of a technically difficult mix of machines. Output for the second half would be higher than for the first, he said, and added that orders for 1993 and prospects also showed signs of improvement. 'With 90 per cent of our output exported, the recent effective devaluation of the pound will give us a strong competitive edge and this further increases our confidence.'
