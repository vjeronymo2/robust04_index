October 13, 1989, Friday, Valley Edition

HUNTERS PLAY KEY ROLE IN CONSERVATION

Certain animals of the wilderness are hunted and harvested at certain times of the year to allow humans a variety of game and to keep a balanced reproductive population of that species. As for domestic livestock, it's slaughtered according to demand. We can find domestic animals prepackaged for purchase, no feelings felt for the animal we never saw. For the hunter, game is a variety of meat. Deer are not killed daily like livestock.

As for cruelty, it happens all the time all over the world. Wild and domestic animals are abused, maimed and driven out constantly, with or without the hunter. Cruelty to animals is something that can be caused by nature itself.

JEFF STATZ, North Hollywood
