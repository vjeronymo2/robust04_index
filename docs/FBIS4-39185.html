BFN [Text] In joining the NATO Partnership for Peace program, Kyrgyzstan hopes to receive assistance on the part of NATO within the scope of the consultations on national security. Kyrgyzstan also expects NATO to help it to work out a military doctrine. Speaking at a press conference in Bishkek today, Kyrgyz President Askar Akayev commented on the results of his visit to NATO headquarters. He underlined that within the scope of this program NATO was ready to assist with drafting projects for conversion of the Kyrgyz military industry. The parties could also cooperate on environmental projects. In particular, Bishkek wishes for NATO to provide practical aid to overcome natural disasters which are a regular occurrence in Kyrgyzstan. Akayev pointed out that although Kyrgyzstan is a small state, the general situation in Central Asia and its interests in stability and security mean that it must have its own army.
