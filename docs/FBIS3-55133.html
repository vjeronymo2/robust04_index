Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report by V. Skvortsov; from the "Vesti" newscast] [Text] Sergey Filatov, head of the presidential administration today met U.S. senators. Democratic Senator Sam Nunn and republican Senator Richard Lugar represent those of America's political forces that support President Clinton's course toward large-scale economic cooperation with Russia. It is they who drew up the law on financial and technical assistance to Russia and the CIS countries in carrying through conversion and elimination of nuclear weapons. Today the conversation was about the new Russian Parliament and how its work and interaction with the president will be organized. Filatov said that the line-up of forces has already been determined. Now work with the factions is underway and questions of personnel and the agenda are being determined. The election results and the progress of reforms in Russia, plus questions of security, disarmament, and development of cooperation, will also be at the center of attention at the forthcoming meeting of the presidents of the two countries in Moscow next week.
