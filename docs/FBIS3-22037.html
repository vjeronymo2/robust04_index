Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Alvaro Ayala from the "24 Hours Newscast"] [Text] Prosecutor General Gustavo de Greiff announced today that protection has been pulled from the family of Pablo Escobar. The prosecutor said that as of today the Prosecutor General's Office is no longer responsible for the security of the wife and children of the late leader of the Medellin Cartel. Alvaro Ayala has the report. [Begin recording] [Ayala] The prosecutor general has officially announced the withdrawal of security that was provided by his personnel to the Escobar Henao family. He explained that the Army and police are the government agencies now responsible for this service. [De Greiff] I understand that the protection was withdrawn as of today. That protection had been reduced gradually, and, as of today, it has been withdrawn completely. [Ayala] Why did no one cooperate, and who should assume this responsibility? [De Greiff] That is what happens to Colombia. They want us to supply all of the corpses and make all of the efforts, while other countries do not help at all. [Ayala] The prosecutor general concluded by saying he never told the Escobar Henao family to move to the northern sector of Santa Fe de Bogota but that they can move anywhere they want because they are not wanted by the law. [end recording]
