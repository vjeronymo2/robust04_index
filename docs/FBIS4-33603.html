BFN [Text] Managua, 7 Jun (DPA) -- Approximately 30 former Sandinist soldiers occupied the Venezuelan Embassy in Managua this afternoon (local time), allegedly as a pressure tactic for social and economic benefits from the administration of Violeta Chamorro. Initial reports indicate the occupiers, former Government Ministry employees, were unarmed when they entered the embassy, which is located approximately 10 km south of Managua in the luxurious sector of Sierras de Santo Domingo. Nicaraguan Foreign Ministry spokesmen say they have not received official information regarding the occupation, and Venezuelan Ambassador Juan Jose Monsant has not disclosed the demands of the former soldiers. Dozens of policemen have been deployed to the area surrounding the Venezuelan Embassy.
