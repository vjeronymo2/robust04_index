BFN [Text] Yi Hong-ku, deputy prime minister in charge of unification, had a breakfast meeting with former U.S. President Carter, who is visiting the ROK, and was briefed by President Carter regarding his planned activities in North Korea. Deputy Prime Minister Yi Hong-ku reportedly asked President Carter to exert efforts to enable North Korea to clear up nuclear suspicions and join in the international community. Former President Carter will visit North Korea from 15 to 18 June and will have talks with North Korean President Kim Il-song and others.
