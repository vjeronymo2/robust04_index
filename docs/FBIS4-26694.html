BFN [By Danielle Yang] [Text] Taipei, June 25 (CNA) -- The Council of Agriculture (COA) on Saturday [25 June] passed a revised draft to allocate NT$10 [new Taiwan dollars] billion (U.S.$370 million) for a fund to subsidize local farmers who will be affected by Taiwan's entry into the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, or GATT. The COA had originally allocated only NT44 billion (U.S.$148 million) for the fund. All farmers will be eligible for the subsidies. The original draft had stipulated that only farmers who produce certain items -- citrus fruit, apples, plums, peaches, tea, beef, duck, pork, and shrimp -- would be elibgible for the subsidiy. A COA offical said that if the prices of agricultural products fall due to an influx of foreign products after Taiwan joins GATT, the COA will evaluate whether Taiwan farmers are being adversely affected, and subsidize them accordingly if they are.
