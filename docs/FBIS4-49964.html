BFN [By reporter Kang You-feng; from the "Hookup" program -- passages within quotation marks recorded] [Text] In a sign of respect and hospitality for President Li's visit, Jose Maria Figueres, president-elect of Costa Rica, specially convened a joint news conference on the morning of 7 May local time, at which the leaders of the two nations answered correspondents' questions. Because of the imminent changeover of government in Costa Rica, the conference hall was packed with correspondents from various countries who came to cover the inauguration. Asked about democracy in Costa Rica and what we can learn from Costa Rica's experience of democracy, President Li said: Costa Rica has been a model democracy of late, because all the institutions in Costa Rica are founded on the idea of power belonging to the people. Power is being transferred peacefully in Costa Rica from the outgoing president to the incoming president, and there is no uneasiness over the changeover of political power. Aware of the presence of a XINHUA correspondent, President Li changed the subject by pointing out: The foremost problem on mainland China is who is going to be the successor. Looking at the democratic institutions in Costa Rica, we should know that lasting peace and stability must be founded on institutions. It would be ideal for politics to be founded on institutions. President Li said: "The expression that power belongs to the people is an old saying; it is, as a matter of fact, also very timely. Let us talk about it. I believe a representative from mainland China is present here. The biggest problem facing mainland China is who is going to be the successor. [words indistinct] will tell you that the democracy we are promoting is such an institution. We should know that lasting peace and stability refers precisely to the establishment of such institutions. The establishment of institutions [words indistinct]. I believe that this is the best and soundest approach." At this point, President Li said with emotion that he has a dream. His dream is avert transferring power only after his death. He would be happiest to oversee a peaceful transfer of power while he is still alive. "I have a dream. I hope that some day I can hand over power to another person. I would be happiest doing that. There has never been a president in China, in the ROC, who has handed over power to another a person while he was still alive. In the dynasties of old, a new emperor would emerge only after the old one died. I hope this will not happen again. This is precisely the goal of our constitutional reform. Only then will the country enjoy stability. Development is possible only when there is stability." [Words indistinct] his ideas of democracy and power belonging to the people and his wishes to establish a political system of lasting peace and stability, the XINHUA correspondent present at the news conference told ROC correspondents that what President Li said was very sensible. China indeed needs to become more democratic and scientific. The practice in the past of one person designating another as his successor should never be repeated. (Shu Chih-cheng), director of the Secretariat of the Presidential Office who was a member of the government delegation, also told the XINHUA correspondent that the president's words were from the bottom of his heart, saying that he hoped that they would give full coverage to his remarks.
