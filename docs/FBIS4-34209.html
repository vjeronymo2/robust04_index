BFN [Text] Cartagena de Indias (Colombia), 15 Jun (DPA) -- The organizers of the Fourth Ibero-American Summit of chiefs of state and government, currently taking place in the Colombian city of Cartagena de Indias, have announced that a new Ibero-American summit, this time of foreign ministers, will be held on 6 August in Santa Fe de Bogota. The objective of this new meeting is to develop a suitable mechanism to follow up the Ibero-American agenda, which is currently delineating new commercial integration and social development strategies to be included in the document that will be released at the closing of the presidential meeting. The chiefs of state and government, who are currently meeting at this Caribbean seaside resort, will officially convene this new meeting of foreign ministers. The new summit will entrust the 22 Latin American, Caribbean, and Iberian foreign ministers with the preparation and disclosure of suggestions for the Santa Fe de Bogota foreign ministers meeting, which will take place the day before the inauguration of the new Colombian president who will be elected on 19 June.
