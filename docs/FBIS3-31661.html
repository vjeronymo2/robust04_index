Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 22 February (MTI) -- Hungarian Prime Minister Peter Boross met with Russian Foreign Minister Andrei Kozyrev in the Parliament on Tuesday [22 February]. According to foreign affairs spokesman Janos Herman, Boross told Kozyrev that Budapest looks forward to the visit of Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin. Regarding the south Slav crisis, Boross stressed Hungary is glad that the planned military intervention in Bosnia had become unnecessary, and recognizes the role Russia played in this. Kozyrev expressed the hope that Russia and Hungary would soon work out a number of agreements giving business links a new impetus. Recent positive movement in bilateral economic ties provides a good basis for Chernomyrdin's trip to Hungary, he added. The Russian foreign minister sees good chances for ratification of the Hungarian-Russian basic treaty, which complies with Russian interests. Kozyrev concluded his two-day state visit to Hungary and left Budapest today.
