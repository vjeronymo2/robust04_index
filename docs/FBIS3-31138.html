Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Relayed by Warsaw TV Polonia Network] [Text] [Announcer] Poland has no intention of withdrawing its diplomats from Belgrade, [Foreign Affairs] Minister Andrzej Olechowski said after his meeting with Lojze Peterle, the deputy prime minister and foreign affairs minister of Slovenia. The conflict in former Yugoslavia was also the main subject of conversation between the Slovenian foreign minister and President Lech Walesa. [Jerzy Chmielewski, ambassador of the Polish Republic to Yugoslavia, identified by caption] The charge d'affaires is at the embassy at the moment. There are also several Polish diplomats there. Several diplomats are working in the trade counsellor's office. We also have several delegates of Polish trade missions there, as well as the LOT [Polish Airlines] office. We are in permanent contact with them, and we will react according to the situation. At the moment, we cannot see any direct threat to our diplomats.
