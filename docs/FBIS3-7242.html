Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Speaking at a meeting of Dietmen on 24 March, Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] Chairman Murayama touched on the issue of the DPRK's rejection of nuclear inspections. He said: "The party has to keep its friendly relations with the Workers' Party of Korea [WPK]. However, as a member of the ruling parties, we have to study what the party can do." In this way, he disclosed a plan to fully study what the party should do, including the possibility of holding talks with the WPK.
