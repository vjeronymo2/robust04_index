BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Rudenko] [Text] Tula June 25 TASS -- The Russian Defence Ministry is doing all it can for keeping alive unique arms-manufacturing enterprises and to ensure their development in the future, Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev said today at a graduation ceremony, held at the Tula Higher Artillery School, named after the Tula proletariat. He stressed he wanted the Russian Armed Forces to be powerful and mobile, fitted with modern weapon systems and capable of ensuring Russia's security. According to Grachev, Russia's military doctrine does not regard any country as an enemy, but Russia has its own vital interests, which a strong and highly efficient army should protect. Grachev praised the performance of the artillery school in training skilled military personnel. Today graduation diplomas were given to another 300 young lieutenants, who will put into effect the army reform. The Russian defence minister had a meeting today with senior officials of Tula and the Tula region, as well as representatives of the regional union of industrialists and entrepreneurs, of which directors of arms-manufacturing enterprises make the backbone. He visited as well the arms museum and attended a ceremony of opening the day of the city.
