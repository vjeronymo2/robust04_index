BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on narcotics developments in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific monitored by Bangkok Bureau between 2 and 8 May 1994. Source information is given in parentheses after each item. SOUTH ASIA

Nepal
Authorities Arrest 13, Seize 100 Kg of Hashish
An Army patrol arrested 11 people carrying 100 kg of hashish inside of the Royal Bardiya Wildlife Conservation Area on 7 May and handed them over to police. Police later arrested the group's leader Bhupal Khatri and another person in a hotel. The leader reportedly admitted to smuggling 305 kg of hashish into India on three previous occasions. (Hong Kong AFP in English 0752 GMT 8 May 94)
Pakistan
Customs Intercepts 410 Kg of Hashish, Arrests One
Officers at the Khorkhera Customs post intercepted a truck travelling from Quetta to Karachi on 22 April. They found 410 kg of hashish hidden in secret compartments. The driver escaped, but two passengers were arrested. (Karachi DAWN in English 24 Apr 94 p 3) SOUTHEAST ASIA
Philippines
Narcotics Command Arrests Four `Big-Time Drug Dealers' in
Laguna Narcotics Command agents arrested Ricardo Alikbuson, Ermita Salandanan, Gener Vallejo, and Eduardo Nagora--all "big-time drug dealers"--during a raid in Santa Rosa, Laguna in which more than 1 kg of methamphetamine hydrochloride was confiscated. (Quezon City GMA-7 Radio-Television Arts Network in Tagalog 0930 GMT 6 May 94)
Thailand
Macao Man Arrested With 1.5 Kg of Heroin, AK-47
On 2 May, Police in Bangkok arrested Macao citizen Lai Seng Po, 29, and charged him with possessing an AK-47 assault rifle and 1.5 kg of heroin. The suspect had allegedly hijacked a taxi and wounded the driver. (Bangkok BANGKOK POST in English 3 May 94 p 3)
Kenyan Arrested With 43 Kg of Heroin
A Kenyan national who identified himself as J.N. Okafor, 23, was arrested on 6 May. His passport identified him as Charles Bamaba. On 5 May, police discovered three fertilizer sacks containing 115 bars of heroin base weighing a total of about 43 kg hidden inside four large plastic sacks of jeans at a shipping company in Suan Mali, Pom Prap Sattru Phai district. The Kenyan suspect was arrested by a Metropolitan Police antinarcotics team when he came to collect the package. (Bangkok BANGKOK POST in English 7 May 94 p 3)
Vietnam
Border Defense Forces Seize Vessel Loaded With Marijuana
Border defense forces under the management of Naval Group 28 recently intercepted a ship in Vietnamese territorial waters near Tho Chu Island in Kien Giang Province. The ship did not have any flag, vessel name, or registration number. Authorities found 18 people of different nationalities on board along with 1,758.90 kg of marijuana bound for Indonesia. The marijuana was reportedly loaded at Koh Kong, Cambodia. An American identified as John was reported to be in command of the smuggling operation; he claimed he was on a sightseeing trip in Cambodia when he boarded the ship, and the drugs were already there. (Hanoi Voice of Vietnam Network in Vietnamese 1100 GMT 8 May 94)
