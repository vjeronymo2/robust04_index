BFN [Text] In Kirghizia [Kyrgyzstan], a meeting of the working groups of the Interparliamentary Assembly of the CIS on problems of a settlement of the Karabakh conflict begins today. Speakers of the parliaments of Azerbaijan, Armenia, and Russia and representatives of Nagornyy Karabakh have begun arriving in Bishkek. Our commentator on the situation on the eve of the meeting: [Correspondent A. Ivanova] The Armenian leadership headed by President Levon Ter-Petrosyan held consultations today in the town of Goris with the leadership of Nagornyy Karabakh [Nagorno-Karabakh]. The details of the conversation are not being disclosed. According to unofficial information, the sides discussed the approach to a settlement of the Karabakh conflict in connection with the meeting planned for 3 May in Bishkek of heads of parliaments of Armenia and Azerbaijan, with the participation of representatives of Nagornyy Karabakh. The initiator of the meeting was the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly, which has noticeably stepped up its peacemaking activity in the zone of the Karabakh conflict. Vladimir Shumeyko, who arrived in Bishkek today, stated that the talks provide an opportunity to reach agreement on the question of a settlement of the Karabakh conflict. He considers the main justification for this to be the fact that Azerbaijan recognized Nagornyy Karabakh recently as one of the participants of this conflict. Shumeyko called this a very big leap forward in the process of the talks on Karabakh.
