BFN [From ASAHI SHIMBUN 11 April Evening Edition] [Text] On the afternoon of 11 April, Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa severely criticized a meeting of top leaders of three parties -- the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ], the Democratic Socialist Party [DSP], and Sakigake [Harbinger] -- and a floor group held earlier in the day. At the Prime Minister's Office, Hosokawa told reporters: "I think the meeting is wrong. Since coalition parties have agreed to pick (the next prime minister) at the party representatives meeting [attended by party secretaries general], the meeting of party heads should be held after that. The coalition will not be able to reach an accord if the top-level meeting comes first."
