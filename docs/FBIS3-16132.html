Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] A news conference held today at the premises of the Rosvooruzheniye [Russian weapons] state company was devoted to matters relating to the arms trade. Our correspondent Marina Chernukha has the details. [Chernukha] Lieutenant General Viktor Samoylov, the company's director general, anticipated many of the journalists' questions by stating that Russia unequivocally intends to defend its position on the world arms market. [Begin Samoylov recording] Regarding the trade in arms, yes, we are going to fight, and we are already fighting on the market. We have the advantage of not having lost our old markets. We still have them. We are hoping for new markets as well -- primarily the Pacific and Asian, the Latin American, which we are beginning to penetrate, and also South Africa, where we are already established normally. [end recording] [Chernukha] Samoylov stressed that Russia does not intend to act in contravention of UN sanctions regarding trade with Iraq and Libya. However, he made it clear that as soon as these are lifted, Russia could offer these countries competitive military equipment and armaments.
