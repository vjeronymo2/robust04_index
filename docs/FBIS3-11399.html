Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Brasilia, 21 Mar (EFE) -- Venezuelan Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli told EFE today in Brasilia that the eradication of poverty must be "the number one issue" at the continental summit called by U.S. President Bill Clinton for December in Miami (United States). The Venezuelan minister said he believes Clinton's initiative is of "supreme interest" as a way to develop new relations between the United States and Latin America following the end of the Cold War. Burelli said: "We hope the schedule" for the Miami summit "will be established soon." Burelli will participate in a 48-hour meeting of foreign ministers and governmental representatives from 13 Rio Group countries that begins today at Itamaraty, the Brazilian Foreign Ministry headquarters. This summit will allow an exchange of opinions toward consensus among Latin American governments before the summit called by President Clinton, and to review the international situation. The ministers will establish the bases for two upcoming Rio Group summits: the talks that are held twice a year with the EC foreign ministers -- scheduled for 20-23 April in Sao Paulo -- and the Eighth Annual Rio Group summit scheduled for 8-9 September in Rio de Janeiro. Even though it is not on the agenda, the delegates will use the meeting to probe preferences for the election of a new OAS secretary general on 27 March in Washington. Diplomatic sources have stated that this issue will crop up in lobby talks and even in the Rio Group's three flexible sessions as any delegation head is allowed to raise it. Foreign Minister Burelli is one of the candidates for the OAS General Secretariat, replacing Brazilian diplomat Joao Baena Soares, whose five-year term ends in June. Colombian President Cesar Gaviria and Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus are also running. Foreign Minister Burelli indicated that the Rio Group must forge "a social bias" for Latin American policy because "the people eagerly want consideration in the decision-making process." Burelli said: "Poverty represents a new scourge within society as it has new elements of insecurity that cannot be fought with armies. It is tied to hunger, terrorism, drug trafficking, violence, and growing destitution." Burelli said that internal and external migration in Latin American countries has helped to form "pockets" of misery in large cities. He mentioned the migration from rural areas and the arrival of about 60,000 Guyanese citizens in Venezuela. They become involved in the underground economy. The people responsible for regional diplomacy will assess a preliminary document entitled: "Poverty, Destitution, and Unemployment." This document was compiled at the Rio Group's request by experts from various governments, the Economic Commission for Latin America (ECLA), and the United Nations. The document proposes guidelines to cure the phenomena and will become a Rio Group's contribution for the UN Conference on Social Development scheduled for 1995 in the city of Copenhagen.
