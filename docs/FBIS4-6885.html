BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 22 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The participants of the seminar on the issues of legislative strengthening of the activities of the Constitutional Court of Mongolia organized by the Mongolian section on human rights and the permanent representative of the US Asian Fund for Development in Mongolia have agreed to a common opinion on necessity to strengthen legislatively the activities of the body for enforcing the constitutional control. Such a conclusion was derived by the specialists-lawyers, members of the Constitutional Court and the Mongolian parliament taking part in this seminar after their acquainting and discussing the reports which were put on the agenda. It should be noted that the accent was put on that amendments and changes to the law on Constitutional Court should be made within the frameworks of the Constitution of the country the materials of the seminar as has been noted prior to this would be presented afterwards in the form of recommendations to the working group on elaborating the draft law on changes and amendments to be made to the legislation on the Constitutional Court. [sentence as received] The seminar was carried out in a two staged form. Firstly after the opening ceremony of the seminar there were presented the major reports relating to the problem then on a weekly interval the work of the seminar was continued so that the participants would have been provided with proper possibilities to scrutinize and think them over and arrive at proper conclusions during this spell of time.
