BFN [Text] Seoul, April 7 (YONHAP) -- Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu hinted at a small change in Seoul's initial position that linked inter-Korean exchange of envoys to resolution of North Korean nuclear problem Thursday [7 April], saying that the nuclear issue has become an international problem in the wake of the statement by the United Nations Security Council, therefore cannot be limited to a problem of confrontation between North and South Korea. In the summits in Tokyo and Beijing, it was recognized that the nuclear problem should be resolved by three approaches -- between International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) and North Korea, South and North Korea, and North Korea and the United States, Han said in an expanded party-government meeting to discuss follow-up measures to President Kim Yong-sam's visits to Japan and China. "At present, we will wait and see North Korea's response to the Security Council statement and continue with persuasion and pressure," Han said. "Considering the effect of the North Korean nuclear problem on tourism, investment, trade, and our overall economy, we need more efforts to wipe out the understanding that the present situation in the Korean peninsula is critical," the foreign minister said. Han reported that Seoul and Tokyo agreed to resolve repatriation of ethnic Koreans in Sakhalin as soon as possible at the initiative of Japan. Seoul will also present comprehensive measure to the matter within the year. Seoul trusts Tokyo to resolve the comfort women issue and Japan is currently mapping out measures, Han said. Trade, Industry, and Energy Minister Kim Chol-su said he will hammer out details of economic cooperation in motor vehicles, aircraft, and telephone exchange agreed in the Seoul-Beijing summit. Korea will expand joint manufacturing of automobile parts before the summer and seek advancement in completed cars next year, Kim said.
