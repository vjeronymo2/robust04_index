BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Panamanian elections scheduled for 8 May 1994. Ruben Blades, presidential candidate of the Papa Egoro Movement, has said Panamanian President Guillermo Endara muddied his hands in partisan politics by presenting a documentary video on previous regimes in a national television network. (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 17 Apr 94 p A1) Mireya Moscoso de Gruber, presidential candidate for the Arnulfist Party (PA), warned on 17 April that she will not give up her candidacy and support Ruben Dario Carles, "who is falling behind in the polls." Meanwhile, Alfredo Ramirez, president of the Nationalist Liberal Republican Movement (Molirena), insists that Carles is the only candidate who can defeat the Democratic Revolutionary Party (PRD). (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 18 Apr 94 p 1) The various presidential candidates toured rural areas over the weekend of 16 April. Ruben Blades visited various towns in Veraguas Province, where he met with supporters, discussed their problems, and offered solutions. "We have not sought an alliance with any political party, because we know we have an alliance with all of Panamanian society, with the Panamanian people," Blades emphasized in his visit. Meanwhile, Ernesto Perez Balladares, presidential candidate for the PRD and the United People alliance, said he was "looking to the future and not to the past." Perez Balladares toured various towns in Chiriqui Province and met fellow party members and supporters, to whom he stressed that people in rural areas have been forgotten by the current administration. Samuel Lewis Galindo, presidential candidate for the Solidarity Party and the Alliance for National Consensus, visited Los Santos Province, where he called for the unity among all Panamanians. Lewis Galindo urged the Panamanian people to avoid all forms of social confrontation. Ruben Dario Carles, presidential candidate for the Change '94 ticket, visited Puerto Armuelles, Chiriqui Province, where he "promised to respect the rights of the Indians and workers." Carles charged that the PRD has tried to eliminate the workers' 13th month bonus, and he added that the PRD leadership should be imprisoned on various charges of corruption. The PA presidential ticket toured Chiriqui and Bocas del Toro Provinces, where thousands of supporters greeted Mireya Moscoso de Gruber. She reiterated the PA will defeat the PRD on 8 May. When PRD candidate Perez Balladares was asked about other groups forming an alliance against him, he answered: "They are trying to repeat what happened in 1989, as if they were trying to work against Noriega. I reiterate that we will not return to the past. We will not return to militarism." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 18 Apr 94) Perez Balladares has visited the industrial area of Betania corregimiento, Panama City, where he promised "private enterprise will generate most of the jobs" during his administration. He emphasized, "The government cannot generate the number of jobs needed by the economy." On 18 April, Moscoso de Gruber said it is necessary to set aside all special interests to achieve a broad alliance. She reaffirmed her plans to form such an alliance to confront the PRD. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 19 Apr 94)
