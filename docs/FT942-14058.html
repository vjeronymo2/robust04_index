940421 FT 21 APR 94 / Renaissance in luxury world Happier times have arrived for the luxury goods industry, as illustrated yesterday by Christian Dior which, like other high fashion houses, languished in the early 1990s. Mr Bernard Arnault, chairman of the French concern, predicted substantial profits growth for 1994 after a 27 per cent increase in February sales. For 1993, Dior announced a 9 per cent increase in net profits to FFr876m (Dollars 148m) on sales up 10 per cent at FFr24.62bn. Details, Page 24
