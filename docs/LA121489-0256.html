December 14, 1989, Thursday, P.M. Final

SHORT TAKES;

WATTS TRIES SAVING 'HOMEROOM'

Parents and teachers in the Watts district vowed to petition ABC to save "Homeroom," a half-hour comedy series they say helps keep their kids in school.

The program, which debuted in the fall, is being canceled because of poor ratings, said ABC spokesman Jim Brochu.

"All we're saying is give the show a chance," said Jim Goins, who counsels school dropouts in the largely minority Watts area. He and other parents and teachers decided to petition the network after a meeting Wednesday.

"There's not that many shows of color that deal with educational issues, and we're in a dilemma right now as far as having black male role models," Goins said. "Not everyone can be a Bill Cosby."

"The Cosby Show," NBC's top-rated comedy, stars Bill Cosby as an upper-class doctor living in New York with his lawyer-wife and children.

"Homeroom" stars Darryl Sivad as a man who gave up a lucrative job in advertising to teach fourth graders at an inner-city public school.
