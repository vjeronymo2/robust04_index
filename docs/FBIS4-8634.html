BFN [Text] Belgrade, 14 Apr (TANJUG)--In connection with the NATO air strikes against Serb targets around Gorazde, Colonel General Momcilo Perisic, chief of the general staff of the Yugoslav Army, has stated that "such NATO air force action can undeniably have unforeseeable consequences for peace in the Balkans and Europe." In an interview to be published in the POLITIKA daily tomorrow, Gen. Perisic said that "in the case of further air strikes against Serb territories, there is a danger of an escalation of the war to an uncontrollable degree." He said that "the NATO air strikes against Serb territories around Gorazde complicated the military and political situation in former Bosnia-Herzegovina", and that this action "encouraged the supporters of the war option and worried those who carry peace in their hearts." "I particularly wish to stress that this is an act of aggression against the Serb people, who have a legitimate and natural right to self-defense guaranteed by the UN Charter." The Yugoslav Army chief of staff stressed that "the Yugoslav Army has continuously and attentively been following developments after the NATO air strikes", and that it has been taking "all necessary measures for the protection of the territorial integrity of our country." "In the case of a possible violation of the air space of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, the army will take actions in accordance with the rules of its combat operations," Gen. Perisic said.
