Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, March 22 -- According to Hungarian President Arpad Goncz, Polish-Hungarian friendship is and will always be topical and the present political situation prompts Warsaw and Budapest to have even closer contacts than so far. The slogan: Poles and Hungarians are friends will always be topical, Goncz told Polish correspodents in Budapest on Tuesday, before his three-day visit to Warsaw, Poznan and Krakow to start on March 28. Pointing to the will to become integrated with western Europe, common to Poland and Hungary, President Goncz said that Poland should be the closest and most important partner for Budapest. He stressed also the importance of the Visegrad group and said that he believed in the concept of central Europe, although it could be interpreted differently. Referring to his planned meetings with Polish politicians, particularly with President Lech Walesa and Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak, the head of the Hungarian state said that mutual presentation of views would be very important for the better understanding of both countries.
