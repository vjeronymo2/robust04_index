BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 25 KYODO -- The opposition Social Democratic Party (SDP) [SDPJ -- of Japan] and new party Sakigake (Harbinger) agreed Saturday [25 June] to jointly iron out a blueprint for a new government to replace Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata's cabinet, the parties sources said. SDP Chairman Tomiichi Murayama and Sakigake leader Masayoshi Takemura met and agreed on the program, they said. After the plan is drawn up, the two parties will submit it to other parties as early as the beginning of next week, and ask them to cooperate in the formation of a new coalition government, they said. Hata announced earlier in the day that he would resign along with his entire cabinet.
