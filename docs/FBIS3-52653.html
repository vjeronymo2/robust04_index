Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] A meeting between Palestinian representatives and British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd at Orient House in East Jerusalem ended a short while ago. Our correspondent Golan Yokhpaz filed the following report: [Begin Yokhpaz recording] The meeting between the seven Palestinian representatives -- including the PLO ambassador to London -- and British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd ended without any declaration stating that progress has been made or that Hurd made some contribution to promoting the Israeli-Palestinian negotiations. Speaking beneath a PLO and a British flag, the British secretary said that the meeting was good and that in it, the parties discussed the future of the Israeli-Palestinian negotiations. Speaking at the conclusion of the meeting, Dr. Hanan 'Ashrawi said: Britain plays an important role in the negotiating relations between Israel and the PLO. Asked about progress in the negotiations, 'Ashrawi said: Well, goodwill alone is not sufficient for the continuation of the Israeli-PLO negotiations. We need very serious, concrete, and constructive action in order to make progress. [end recording]
