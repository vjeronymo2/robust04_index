July 20, 1989, Thursday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

AT&T RINGS UP 18% RISE IN PROFITS

American Telephone & Telegraph Co., the nation's largest telecommunications company, said today its profit in the second quarter rose 18% to $694 million.

On a per share basis, the earnings increased to 66 cents a share from 55 cents.

The communications giant said its revenues rose 5.2% to $9.26 billion from $8.80 billion a year ago. Expense controls allowed earnings to rise even faster than the revenues, the company said.

Analysts said AT&T's earnings were expected to rise due to strong demand for its long-distance telephone services.

For the first half of the year the company had a net profit of $1.29 billion, compared with $1.09 billion a year ago. Revenues rose to $17.91 billion from $17.19 billion.
