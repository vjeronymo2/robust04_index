Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Jan 27 (CTK) -- The Slovak Parliament today ratified the treaty between Slovakia and Ukraine on a common border and cooperation in questions. The treaty was signed on October 14, 1993. The treaty confirms the current border, and provides for the marking of the border on the terrain and for maintenance of the markers. It also provides for the creation of a joint commission to monitor compliance with the treaty. It will remain in force for ten years and supersedes a similar treaty between Czechoslovakia and the Soviet Union signed in 1973, when Ukraine was still a Soviet Republic.
