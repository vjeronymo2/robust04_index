BFN [Text] Chaired by Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata, the government held a trade meeting to talk about how Japan, which runs a huge trade surplus, should buckle down to the task of expanding imports. The meeting was held at the prime minister's official residence on the morning of 15 June. Following the cabinet's 29 March approval of an outline foreign economic reform plan, Vice Chairman of Caterpillar Inc. Wagsland [as published] and other foreign experts gave their views on government efforts to ease regulations and improve other matters. In response, Prime Minister Hata explained his resolve to expand Japan's imports. He said: "It will be painful. But, we will not be able to enjoy an affluent life unless we realize these economic reforms, including the promotion of deregulation and the improvement of market access." At the meeting, Vice Chairman Wagsland gave his assessment of Japanese efforts in such areas as deregulation and improved market access. He said: "Some people in the American financial world are skeptical about the seriousness of efforts by Japanese society as a whole." Chairman of the Berlin Chamber of Commerce and Industry Kramp [as published) criticized Japan's bureaucratic system. He said: "Although there are no clear import barriers, practical regulations such as administrative guidance still exist." Chairman of the Thai Trade Council Wanli [as published] called for Japanese contributions to economic development in Asia. He said: "We hope Japan will hand over to us industrial sectors which have lost their competitive power."
