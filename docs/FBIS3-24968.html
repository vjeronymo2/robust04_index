Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Niamey, 3 Feb (ANP/PANA)--The 850 Niger customs officers who went on strike on Monday [31 January] to press demands for better working conditions are to resume work from Thursday. The strike ended after their demands, which also included the reemployment of some of their colleagues who had been declared redundant, had been met. The general secretary of the National Union of Customs Officers (SNAD), Lt Ibro Ayouba, in a statement on Wednesday evening asked customs officers to resume work. He said a follow-up committee had been formed to implement measures promised by the government at the negotiations.
