920515 FT 15 MAY 92 / UK Company News: Property sale lifts Finlay to Pounds 10.8m Helped by a surplus on sale of property, James Finlay, the overseas trading and financial services group, increased pre-tax profits from Pounds 9.52m to Pounds 10.8m in 1991. Turnover slipped to Pounds 144m (Pounds 149m) but the operating surplus rose to Pounds 12.8m (Pounds 11.1m) with the help of a Pounds 1.3m profit from the sale of property by George Payne. Contribution from plantations was held at Pounds 4.34m, confectionery and beverage provided Pounds 6.57m (Pounds 4.56m) including the property deal, merchant banking and international confirming moved from a Pounds 49,000 loss into a Pounds 729,000 profit. Trading, manufacturing and merchanting saw a decline to Pounds 2.6m (Pounds 3.15m), property accounted for Pounds 369,000 (Pounds 343,000), and oil and gas interests showed a deficit of Pounds 75,000 (surplus Pounds 324,000). Geographically, the two biggest contributors were the UK and Africa with Pounds 7.08m (Pounds 4.54m) and Pounds 5.08m (Pounds 3.77m) respectively. North America fell behind at Pounds 985,000 (Pounds 2.53m) as did Asia with Pounds 344,000 (Pounds 1.6m), but Australasia cut its loss to Pounds 645,000 (Pounds 1.36m). Earnings per share were 6.8p (6p) and the dividend is held at 4.15p with a second interim of 2.15p.
