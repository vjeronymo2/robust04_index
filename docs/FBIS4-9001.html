BFN [Excerpts] Sofia, April 19 (BTA) -- The Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP), which supports the government, asked Prime Minister Lyuben Berov for cabinet changes and for a new programme by May 15. After a meeting of the top leadership of the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS), on whose mandate the present government was formed in late 1992, it announced the DPS would insist that the government be overhauled completely. The opposition Union of Democratic Forces (SDS) calls for the government's resignation and for early parliamentary elections. The SDS will not move for a vote of no confidence in the cabinet pending debates on a question about the government's financial policy posed by MPs of the SDS. Today's meetings of the three major political forces showed they are readying for elections. The SDS discussed principles of selecting candidates. The DPS is likely to run on a separate list in the next elections. Meanwhile, BSP leader Zhan Videnov and DPS leader Ahmed Dogan visited the prime minister who is working in hospital. "The prime minister's condition admits of political activity," Videnov said. According to Dogan, Prime Minister Berov, who underwent heart surgery over a month ago and is now in hospital after developing hepatitis symptoms, is in a very good state of health. Videnov said the prime minister had promised to hold two rounds of meetings with the major political forces to discuss cabinet changes. The Socialist leader told the press today that cabinet changes should be made with a view to three main goals: Bulgaria's international financial stabilization, industrial restructuting and de-escalating tensions in the army and the law-enforcement authorities. Critical views on the government prevail in the DPS and it will insist on a complete overhaul. The land reform and privatization are the major points of its criticism. According to Osman Oktay MP of the DPS, the movement has not drawn up a list of new cabinet members. [passage omitted] Stefan Sofiyanski, deputy chairman of the leadership of the SDS, said it was absurd to demand of "a sick person under quarantine to form a new cabinet" and added that this pressure comes from the BSP. The SDS will wait for the interpellation on the cabinet's financial policy to enter the debating chamber and if the draft resolution criticising this policy is voted down than it will consider moving a no-confidence vote. According to Sofiyanski, it would be impossible for a no-confidence vote to take place before parliament's Easter recess. So far the SDS has moved five no-confidence votes which were defeated. George Ganchev, leader of the extraparliamentary Bulgarian Business Bloc, told journalists today that the Premier would resign by the end of the week. He cited a "firsthand source" without naming it.
