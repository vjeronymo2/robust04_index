940628 FT 28 JUN 94 / Arts: Today's Television Under the heading 'True and false etymology', Fowler's Modern English Usage tells us that 'Welsh rabbit is amusing and right, and Welsh rarebit stupid and wrong'. 'Stupid and wrong' just about sums up Rhodes Around Britain, which is obsessed with the style and appearance of its presenter and pretty weak on recipes (8.30 BBC2). At 9.30 on the same channel comes another misconceived phrase. When Neil Armstrong first walked on the moon it was intended, the ponderousness of American PR men being what it is, that he should say: 'That's one small step for a man, one giant leap for mankind.' Poor old Armstrong, with too many other things on his mind, omitted the indefinite article, destroyed the distinction between the individual and the species, and delivered 11 words of gibberish. They can never be corrected because all the television newsreels stand as witness. One Small Step re-tells the story of the moon landing. Ireland's crucial match against Norway is shown in World Cup Grandstand (5.25 BBC1) and ITV shows Brazil v Sweden in World Cup '94 (8.30).
