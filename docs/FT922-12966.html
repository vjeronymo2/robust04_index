920415 FT 15 APR 92 / Babangida sets up Nigerian council of state NIGERIA'S President Ibrahim Babangida has established an advisory body including former military and civilian rulers to help him tackle the country's problems, state radio said yesterday, Reuter reports from Lagos. 'The newly reconstituted Council of State must. . . be viewed as a constitutional artifact to help forge a common approach to the challenges of nation building in our federal system,' Gen Babangida said at the opening on Monday. Nigeria's first post-independence head of state, Mr Nnamdi Azikiwe, former military ruler Gen Yakubu Gowon and the last civilian president, Shehu Shagari, attended the meeting in Abuja, the capital. President Babangida said the council of state, which last met in its present form a decade ago, was expected to smooth the path to a civilian republic by January 2. It will seek solutions to economic problems, including a large foreign debt and heavy dependence on imports.
