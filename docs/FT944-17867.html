941006 FT 06 OCT 94 / 312,000 false claimants caught A total of 312,000 people have been caught in the past year making false claims on a range of welfare benefits, the Benefits Agency said in its annual report yesterday. Catching the benefit cheats had saved the social security system Pounds 654m. The main areas of fraud were: misrepresenting income (60 per cent of cases); failure to include family circumstances (25 per cent); counterfeit or stolen benefit books (10 per cent); and giving false identities (5 per cent).
