BFN [Text] The summit meeting between the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, and the U.S. President, Mr. Clinton, will take place in Washington on the 19th of next month. An External Affairs Ministry spokesman said in New Delhi today that the overall program of the prime minister's visit to the United States is being worked out. The Indian Embassy in Washington is in touch with the U.S. State Department. The visit is in response to an invitation by the U.S. President. The two leaders are expected to exchange views on major international issues. They will also discuss measures to consolidate and strengthen bilateral relations. The minister of state for external affairs, Mr. R.L. Bhatia, has said that the United States wanted to clear misunderstandings with India and wants to establish sound relations. Responding to a question in the Rajya Sabha today, he said the prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao will visit USA to keep the dialogue open with that country as a part of maintaining cordial relations with it. He said a senior diplomat has gone to London [as heard] to hold discussions with the U.S. officials as the prime minister is to make a visit to that country. The prime minister's visit, Mr. Bhatia said, is to hold discussions at the highest level to narrow down differences between the two countries on the NPT [Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty], regional security, disarmament, and other issues.
