Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] The results of the first round of the legislative elections, which were held on 6 February, were announced by the Togolese Supreme Court this morning. During an ordinary session held at the courtroom of the Lome Court of Justice, the Supreme Court confirmed the results released by the National Electoral Commission [CEN] to the public. It agreed that the poll was held in a peaceful, safe, and honest atmosphere. The Supreme Court also agreed that 57 of the 352 contesting candidates obtained an absolute majority of votes and proclaimed them elected. It however observed that 48 other candidates, who did not obtain enough votes during the first round of the election, must contest the second round. The decree issued today approved all the results released by the CEN. The Supreme Court, which rejected petitions for cancellations, established that the turnout rate was about 65 to 70 percent and estimated that invalid votes were 37,407.
