Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Hague, March 1 (AFP) -- A group of 280 Dutch UN peacekeeping soldiers headed to Bosnia Tuesday [1 March] to join a Dutch battalion in charge of protecting the Moslem enclaves of Srebrenica and Zepa, a Defense Ministry spokesman here said. The soldiers are expected to arrive in the Croat seaside city of Split Tuesday afternoon. On Wednesday, after recovering equipment from a Dutch navy frigate, they are to head to Lukavac, where their battalion is based, and then on to Srebrenica and Zepa, in eastern Bosnia. The Defense Ministry spokesman said the 280 soldiers will bring up to 1,100 the number of Dutch soldiers deployed in Bosnia. An additional 150 are expected there by the end of the month, he added. Additional Dutch soldiers are deployed in ex-Yugoslavia and on air bases in Italy from where they enforce the UN no-flight ban over Bosnia. Dutch troops are also deployed along the Danube to enforce a trade embargo against Serbia.
