BFN [Text] [Announcer] Spain has decided to prolong its presence in UN Operations in Mozambique [ONUMOZ] with 21 officers from the three services after the first 20 servicemen end their year-long tour of duty. Wednesday [27 April], the last four Spanish officers to finish their mission in Mozambique arrive in Madrid. At the beginning of March, four came back, and yesterday, 11 others returned. The Spanish relief is already deployed in Mozambique. Just like the others belonging to the UN force, they are in charge of overseeing the peace accords between the Mozambique Liberation Front in the government [Frelimo] and the Mozambican National Resistance [Renamo] that makes up the guerrillas. Fifteen Civil Guardsmen have been working alongside the 20-odd Spanish officers for the last few days.
