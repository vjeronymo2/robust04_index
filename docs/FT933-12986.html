930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / International Company News: Long distance calls help Sprint up 58% SPRINT, the US telecommunications group, yesterday reported a 58 per cent increase in second-quarter net income from continuing operations, thanks to strong growth in its long distance operating income. Reported income from continuing operations was Dollars 165m, or 48 cents a share, compared with Dollars 104m, or 31 cents, a year earlier. This excludes a Dollars 44m, or 13 cents a share, after-tax gain it made last year from the sale of telephone properties in Ohio. Its revenues rose 9 per cent to Dollars 2.8bn. Net income totalled Dollars 157m, or 46 cents, compared with Dollars 148m, or 44 cents. Sprint, the third-largest long distance carrier in the US, said this business saw operating income of Dollars 121m, compared with Dollars 45m last time. Revenues rose 10 per cent to Dollars 1.51bn, compared with Dollars 1.38bn a year ago and Dollars 1.49bn in the first quarter. Minutes of use rose 9 per cent on a year ago.
