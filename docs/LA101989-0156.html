October 19, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

CALIFORNIA IN BRIEF;

LA JOLLA;

FRAUD DEFENDANT TELLS OF ROMANCE

A teary Nancy Hoover Hunter testified that she initially did not like J. David Dominelli but fell in love with him after hearing about his impoverished childhood and time in Vietnam. Hunter is charged with 197 counts of fraud and tax evasion in connection with her work at Dominelli's J. David & Co., the heart of Dominelli's fraudulent investment empire. Dominelli is serving a 20-year prison term after admitting he bilked investors out of $80 million. The company was forced into bankruptcy by disgruntled investors in 1984.
