930528 FT 28 MAY 93 / World Commodities Prices: Market report COPPER prices ended easier on the LME after a session of wide price movements, pulling other metals lower as well. Dealers said copper's weakness was prompted by recent successive failures above Dollars 1,850 a tonne for three-month metal, and the market broke downwards below Dollars 1,820, with liquidation, sell stops, and option sales driving prices down to Dollars 1,795 at one stage. On the London bullion market GOLD remained locked within the now well established range between Dollars 372 and Dollars 380 a troy ounce. 'There are (sell and buy) stops building up on either side of this range,' one dealer said, adding that if gold managed to break through either side, 'we would see a Dollars 5 movement very easily.' London's robusta COFFEE futures reversed an earlier advance and finished lower as the recent bout of fund and chart-related buying appeared temporarily to dry up. New York's arabica coffee was lower across the board at midday on long liquidation linked to July's early inability to test 10-week highs scored earlier this week.
