BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tamara Zamyatina] [Text] Moscow May 23 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin's decision to meet with Chechen President Dzhokhar Dudayev is based on his concern about the current situation in the republic which poses an "increased threat" to its population and residents of adjacent regions, including Daghestan and the Astrakhan region, Yeltsin's administration chief of staff Sergey Filatov said. In an interview with ITAR-TASS on Monday, Filatov said that the date of the meeting has yet to be set. Observers believe that Yeltsin had been putting off the meeting with Dudayev, waiting for his old foe, former Russian parliament chairman Ruslan Khasbulatov to leave the Chechen Republic where he stayed after his release from prison under amnesty earlier this year. Now that Khasbulatov is in Moscow, the last obstacle has been removed.
