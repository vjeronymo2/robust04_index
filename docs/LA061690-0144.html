June 16, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

2 ORGANIZATIONS UNDECIDED OVER NEA AWARDS

Pacific Chorale has been awarded an $11,500 grant from the National Endowment for the Arts, but chorale officials were unavailable Friday to comment on whether they would accept the grant in light of a controversial anti-obscenity pledge required by groups receiving 1990 NEA funds.

Meanwhile, Opera Pacific officials have postponed until Aug. 16 a decision on whether to accept an $18,000 NEA grant.

The decision was to have been made at a Thursday board meeting, but other issues took precedence on a crowded agenda, company general director David DiChiera said Friday.

An NEA spokeswoman said the delay would not jeopardize the Costa Mesa-based opera troupe's grant, since recipients have several months to decide whether to accept the funds.

The embattled NEA, under fire for subsidizing a handful of allegedly obscene or sacrilegious artworks, is requiring all 1990 grant recipients to sign a form pledging that they will not produce artworks that could be judged obscene and that fail to meet undefined standards of artistic excellence.

In protest to the requirement, several artists or arts organizations across the country have rejected their grants or taken further action. Los Angeles choreographer Bella Lewitzky said Thursday that she will reject a $72,000 NEA grant and file suit against the federal arts agency over the anti-obscenity pledge.

All other Orange County arts organizations notified of 1990 NEA grants have said they will accept the funds. Officials at the Newport Harbor Art Museum, however, have said they will protest the anti-obscenity pledge either by striking it from grant acceptance forms or by attaching a letter to the forms noting their non-compliance with the pledge.
