Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, March 22 (CTK) -- The popularity of deposed premier Vladimir Meciar's Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS) is once again on the rise, Slovak Radio reported today. Citing its own poll conducted March 11-16, immediately after Meciar's fall from power, the station said that if parliamentary elections were held today, 26 per cent of voters would vote HZDS. The Democratic Left Party (SDL) would trail with only 11 percent, the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) with 6 percent, and the Association of Workers (SD), which recently split from the SDL, with five percent. Fifteen percent of voters were undecided for any party and 11 per cent said they would not vote.
