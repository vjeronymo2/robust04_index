Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] An article published on page 4 of the 21 January Ho Chi Minh City SAIGON GIAI PHONG in Vietnamese reports that in mid-December of last year, the Binh Thanh Market Management Unit discovered a Daihatsu van which was going from Ho Nai to Ho Chi Minh City, carrying 18 bags containing "black color bars of a rather soft substance," weighing 814 kg. Subsequently, a lab analysis showed that each kilogram was 9.65 percent morphine. According to the local anti-drug center, based on the color, smell, and morphine content, the substance was actually crude opium. Each kilogram of the crude opium currently costs about $462 (5 million dong). The case has been turned over to the public security office for further investigation.
