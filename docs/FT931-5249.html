930306 FT 06 MAR 93 / Finance and the Family: Revenue's stand hits varsity BES issues THE INLAND Revenue does not like business expansion scheme companies which invest in university accommodation. As reported last week, its reaction to the Johnson Fry companies which bought accommodation for University College, London, suggests it is getting tougher as the BES nears its scheduled demise. The Revenue has interpreted the BES legislation in such a way that tax relief would be denied, according to Johnson Fry's chairman, Charles Fry. But this decision is not final and talks continue. Johnson Fry argues that the decision to make a summer letting, which caused the problem, was not the responsibility of the BES companies but of UCL, and that the legislation refers only to matters under the control of the BES company. It will pay the costs of any legal action, but it is worth noting that sponsors do not guarantee that BES relief is available - although they do undertake to act for investors in trying to ensure that it is. The main lesson of the affair, according to John Spiers of BESt Investment, is to avoid schemes where money is raised before the Revenue has granted provisional approval. Whatever the Revenue's final decision, it has succeeded in cutting back on university BES issues - Ian Pugh of Allenbridge says three have been cancelled in the past week. Meanwhile, BES companies proliferate.
