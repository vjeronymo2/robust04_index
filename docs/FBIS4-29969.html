BFN [Text] Brunei Darussalam may make use of SIRIM, or Standards and Industrial Research Institute of Malaysia's, facilities and expertise for its industrial products. Its minister of industry and primary resources, Pehin Datuk Abdul Rahman Taib, said this at a news conference. He said one area which Bruneian industries can seek assistance from SIRIM is the certification of their products. It will also send personnel to SIRIM for training. Earlier, the Bruneian minister and his Malaysian counterpart, Datuk Sri Rafidah Aziz, witnessed the signing of a memorandum of understanding [MOU] between Malaysia and Brunei for the establishment of joint business councils between the two countries. The councils are aimed at promoting business exchanges between the two ASEAN members.
