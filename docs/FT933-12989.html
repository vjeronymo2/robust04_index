930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / International Company News: S&P lifts McGraw-Hill result McGRAW-HILL, the US-based publishing and information services group, reported a 17.3 per cent rise in second-quarter net income, buoyed by a jump in profits at Standard & Poor's, the credit rating agency. McGraw-Hill raised net income to Dollars 43.18m, or 88 cents a share, up from Dollars 36.79m, or 75 cents, in the same period of last year, on revenues up 1.4 per cent to Dollars 490.9m. The group's financial services business produced a 25.1 per cent increase in operating income on a 14.5 per cent rise in revenue, thanks to Standard & Poor's, which continued to benefit from new issues volume in the US and international securities markets. The JJ Kenny business also showed 'fine growth' as investments in technology led to productivity gains. Information and publication services saw an 8.2 per cent drop in revenue and a 20.6 per cent fall in operating profits. Advertising at Business Week magazine fell 16 per cent in April and May, and did not begin to regain momentum until June. Educational and professional publishing suffered a Dollars 2.1m drop in operating profits, and broadcasting was down 12.7 per cent. However, the group's share of the MacMillan/McGraw-Hill School Publishing Company's profits rose 72.4 per cent, to Dollars 11.2m. Mr Joseph Dionne, chairman, said the slow growth of the US economy would mean a 'challenging second half for some of our businesses, particularly in advertising and construction. 'But continued growth in financial services from new financing, new products and global expansion should keep us moving ahead.' For the first half the company reported income up 18.9 per cent at Dollars 58.43m on revenues 2 per cent ahead at Dollars 957.8m. Earnings per share were Dollars 1.19, against Dollars 1.00.
