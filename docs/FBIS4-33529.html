BFN [Excerpt] Belem do Para (Brazil), 7 Jun (EFE) -- Dominican Republic Foreign Minister [title as received] Juan Aristides Tavares said today that his country should not be blamed for the failure of the blockade imposed on Haiti by the international community. In a speech to foreign ministers of OAS member countries, whose 24th General Assembly is taking place in the Amazonian city of Belem Do Para in northern Brazil, Tavares said that many countries are wrong to blame his country for the failure of the sanctions imposed on the Haitian military regime. Although he admitted that the Dominican Republic believes the embargo imposed by the OAS and the United Nations is an "unjust and inhuman" sanction, the Dominican foreign minister said his government strives to comply with international agreements. OAS and UN observers sent to Haiti argue that the border between Haiti and the Dominican Republic, which share the Island of Hispaniola, is the most vulnerable point of the economic blockade imposed by the international community to force General Raoul Cedras to relinquish power and allow President Jean-Bertrand Aristide to return to that country. "We defend the embargo despite considering it unjust and inhuman," Tavares said as he reaffirmed that the government in Santo Domingo has made several efforts to close its border with Haiti. The Dominican foreign minister added that the political party of President Joaquin Balaguer, who is seeking reelection, lost the elections in all border provinces because the deployment of more than 12,000 soldiers to the region "has made life impossible for the area's inhabitants," he added. Tavares said surveillance operations along the more than 300-km-long border of abysses and mountains has consumed a large amount of resources that should have been targeted to social investments. "Ever since it was promulgated, the embargo has been ineffective and has been constantly violated by countries that continue to do business with Haiti; it is unfair to blame the Dominican Republic for the failure of sanctions against Haitian soldiers," Tavares said. "It has been suggested that the failure was caused by my country's attitude toward the sanctions, and that is not so. If the embargo failed it is because of the attitude of those countries that continue to do business with Haiti," the Dominican foreign minister added. [passage omitted]
