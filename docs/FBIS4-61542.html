BFN [Unattributed INTERFAX report in the "In Brief" column] [Text] Nakhodka -- A few days ago eight radiation sources from ship dosimeters containing highly active strontium-90, yttrium-90, and plutonium-239 radionuclides were stolen from the city's sea fishing port laboratory. This was announced yesterday by the Russian State Committee for the Supervision of Nuclear and Radiation Safety, citing its Far East branch. The sea port administration notified the Nakhodka investigating authorities about the incident. At present an inquiry is under way.
