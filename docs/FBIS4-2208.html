BFN [Text] Wuhan, April 7 (XINHUA) -- With the launching of the Chang Jiang river development strategy and the Three Gorges water conservancy projects, central China's Hubei Province has become a new attraction for investors from Taiwan, Hong Kong and Macao. Since the beginning of 1993, a total of 1,882 investors from the three regions have registered in the province with total funds of 4.1 billion U.S. dollars, bringing the total from the three areas to seven billion U.S. dollars, according to the provincial industry and ?commerce bureau. The investors from Taiwan, Hong Kong and Macao are interested in industry, construction, commerce, real estate, agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry and fisheries in the province. Their firms are located in the cities of Wuhan, Yichang, Huangshi and Ezhou along the Chang Jiang river, the biggest river in China. The number of firms with investment over one million U.S. dollars each has reached 9. Preferential policies and regulations have been announced by local governments to attract funds from the three regions.
