May 20, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: IF THE RIVER WAS WHISKEY BY T. CORAGHESSAN BOYLE (PENGUIN: $7.95)

Like teddy bears wrapped in barbed wire, the characters in T. Coraghessan Boyle's award-winning collection of short stories conceal an aching vulnerability behind a thorny shield. Boyle is an often devastating commentator on Los Angeles and the weirdos, wanna-bes and mountebanks who congregate here.

A down-and-out immigrant stunt man risks mutilation and death to achieve fleeting immortality in the supermarket tabloids. A sleazy press agent faces the monumental task of improving the Ayatollah's public image. A security-system saleswoman preys on yuppie paranoia with carefully orchestrated stories of psychotic attackers: A murder she inadvertently provokes becomes the choicest grist for her mill. This acerbic, scrupulously observed portrait of contemporary Southern California is apt to confirm the reader's most dire suspicions about the people next door.
