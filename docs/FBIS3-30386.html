Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, Feb. 2, IRNA -- Iran and Bulgaria inked a letter of understanding for expansion of mutual cooperation in various fields, at the end of a two-day official visit here of Iranian Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati. The memorandum of understanding envisage continous contacts between Iranian and Bulgarian political and parliamentary delegations and cooperation in the United Nations and other international organizations. Activating bilateral economic, trade and financial relations, promoting cooperation in cultural and scientific areas and providing consular services for nationals of both countries are among other points included in the agreement. Talking to reporters, Velayati termed his visit to Bulgaria as "constructive" and said his talks with the Bulgarian president, prime minister and foreign ministers were indicative of friendly relations between the two countries.
