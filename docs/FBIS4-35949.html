BFN [Text] The vice chairman of the Presidential Council, Brother 'Abd-al-Rahman 'Ali al-Jifri, said that what had been reported in the media as a statement by him to the French news agency, AFP -- that the Democratic Republic of Yemen would ask for military assistance from the brotherly Kingdom of Saudi Arabia -- had never happened. In a statement to the Yemeni NEWS AGENCY, Aden, al-Jifri said that the Democratic Republic of Yemen had made no decision to ask for military intervention from any side. He stated that what had happened was that he had replied to a question by AFP on whether the Democratic Republic of Yemen would seek military assistance from any quarter by saying that such a decision had not yet been made, but that anything was possible, and that if we decided to demand anything, we would ask the United Nations to act on Resolution No. 924. The vice chairman added that there was no mention in his reply concerning any country which held our trust and pride, such as our brothers in the Gulf Cooperation Council -- the Republic of Egypt, Syria, and the rest of Arab countries. The vice chairman said that when questioned on this matter, the AFP correspondent denied that he had filed this statement, or that his agency had published this version of the story.
