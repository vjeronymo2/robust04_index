940608 FT 08 JUN 94 / Call to exploit TV superhighway The UK is uniquely placed to take a global stake in 'the biggest commercial play of the next century' - the development of the information superhighway, the Royal Television Society heard last night. Professor David Youlton, chairman of broadcasting technology group Snell and Wilcox, said the UK could not claim pre-eminence in either telecommunications or computers but it had 'the world's largest and most sophisticated knowledge base in the art of the moving image in real time combined with a deep understanding of all that happens between the transmitter and the receiver in the home.' Mr Youlton, who is visiting professor of business studies at Kingston University in London, praised the BBC Research Department as being the broadcasting equivalent of Nasa, the US space agency, by producing a constant stream of spin-off technology. US technology in this area had been impoverished by the closure or hiving off of the research laboratories of the US networks.
