940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / Economic diary - Forward events TOMORROW: Banking, Insurance and Finance Union and Union of Communication Workers hold conferences in Bournemouth. MONDAY: Central Statistical Office publishes figures for balance of trade with countries outside the European Union (April). Special German parliamentary assembly meets in Bonn to elect president. British Airways results. TUESDAY: Council of Baltic Sea states foreign affairs ministers' meeting in Tallinn (until May 25). First meeting expected in Cape Town of South African National Assembly and Senate. Lloyd's of London annual meeting. United Nations holds second conference on disarmament issues in Hiroshima (until May 27). State Bank of India employees scheduled to hold one-day strike. Strike expected at the BBC in protest at the corporation's plans for performance-related pay. Marks and Spencer results. WEDNESDAY: Central Statistical Office releases figures for capital expenditure (first quarter - provisional); stocks and work in progress (first quarter - provisional) and UK output, income, and expenditure (first quarter). US durable goods (April); existing home sales (April). European Union energy council meets in Brussels. Vietnamese National Assembly starts session in Hanoi. Courtaulds results. THURSDAY: The Department of Trade and Industry gives figures for energy trends (March). Engineering sales and orders at current and constant prices (March) from the Central Statistical Office. New vehicle registrations (April) published by the Department of Transport. European Union hosts conference on stability in Europe in Paris, foreign ministers of 40 countries expected to attend (until May 27). Deadline set by Finnish government for decisions on national support measures to agriculture and on European Union referendum date. Bundesbank council meets in Frankfurt. FRIDAY: CBI issues monthly trends inquiry (May). US gross domestic product (first quarter 1994). European Union tourism council meets in Brussels.
