BFN [Report by military correspondent Eytan Rabin] [Excerpts] Two days ago, Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin instructed Major General Ilan Biran, commander of the Central Command, to reinforce the presence of IDF [Israel Defense Forces] troops in the Jordan Valley, particularly in the vicinity of Jericho, in order to step up the security of the settlements in the area. [passage omitted] A government source said that at Rabin's instructions the soldiers will help guard the settlements and prevent infiltrations. "If it is necessary, the soldiers will also give chase to infiltrators," the source added. [passage omitted] There were several incidents in the territories yesterday. Two Palestinians were hit by rubber bullets in clashes with IDF troops in Hebron and Ramallah. Palestinian sources reported that shots were directed at an IDF base in the Ramallah area and that a firebomb was thrown at a police outpost in the city. The policemen opened fire at the perpetrator, but he escaped. Searches were launched in the area. Another firebomb was thrown in Nabulus, but no casualties or damages were caused in this incident either. Palestinian sources in Dayr al-Balah reported several incidents of stone-throwing at Border Police and settlers' cars. Stone- throwing incidents also occurred in the Qatif Bloc and the vicinity of Kfar Darom. A military source noted yesterday that wanted men persist in their efforts to hide in the autonomous areas of Gaza and Jericho. The IDF has reinforced its patrols along the main roads in the Gaza Strip and the West Bank. An evaluation report about the first month of activity of the joint Israeli-Palestinian patrols was issued yesterday. The report notes that no extraordinary events were registered during this period, except for a few shooting incidents. [passage omitted]
