Language: Vietnamese Article Type:BFN [Text] This week's TIEN PHONG (VANGUARD) Magazine carries Quang Binh's article about a case of drug trafficking involving more than 230 kg of opium, the biggest drug trafficking case discovered so far. The case was discovered by officers of the Intersectional Force at the No. 45 Check Point of the Sixth National Highways in Son La Province on 25 January. The opium was carried in six gunny sacks and transported in a car belonging to the Dien Bien Education Department of Lai Chau Province with registration number 27A-2278. The car was driven by Mr. Nguyen Minh Chau to take Mr. Nguyen Xuan Ngan, acting director of the Lai Chau Department of Education and Training, and other education cadres to Hanoi for a meeting. According to initial statements made by the suspects, the owner of the drug is Mr. Nguyen Dang Quang, age 36 of Gia Luong District of Ha Bac Province. He was given a ride to Hanoi. The incident was investigated by law enforcement agencies to clarify the involvement of each suspect for a correct trial under the law.
