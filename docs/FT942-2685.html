940617 FT 17 JUN 94 / FT Guide to World Cup Football (23): Group D - Fixtures and facts Group Fixtures Tuesday, June 21 - Argentina v Greece - 12:30 local (16:30 GMT) Boston - Foxboro Stadium Tuesday, June 21 - Nigeria v Bulgaria - 18:30 local (23:30 GMT) Dallas - Cotton Bowl Saturday, June 25 - Argentina v Nigeria - 16:00 local (20:00 GMT) Boston - Foxboro Stadium Sunday, June 26 - Bulgaria v Greece - 11:30 local (16:30 GMT) Chicago - Soldier Fields Stadium Thursday, June 30 - Greece v Nigeria - 19:30 local (23:30 GMT) Boston - Foxboro Stadium Thursday, June 30 - Argentina v Bulgaria - 18:30 local (23:30 GMT) Dallas - Cotton Bowl Star Player: Augustine Jay-Jay Okocha Country: Nigeria Club: Eintracht Frankfurt Age: 20 Position: Midfield or attack Okocha is the most promising of Nigeria's rich crop of young stars, who won the World Under-17 Cup in 1985, were runners up in 1987 and finished second in the World U-20 cup in 1989. Big, strong Rashidi Yekini, a 30-year old centre forward, will be the focus of attention for opposing defences, but he cannot carry Nigeria far on his own. The form of the young Eagles, has helped Nigeria to win the African Nations Cup and to reach their first World Cup finals. Okocha can score from midfield and is a threat from free kicks. He holds the key to Nigeria's progress in the US and beyond. - Key Game: Argentina v Nigeria (June 25 - Boston) Argentina have a chance to exorcise the ghost of their opening defeat to Cameroon in Italy four years ago when they meet the most-fancied of the African nations in this World Cup. Nigeria are appearing in their first World Cup but their ambition is to go further than Cameroon did in 1990. They do not lack experience, their team is packed with men who play in European football. They are big, strong, fast, skilful and every bit as rough as Cameroon. Argentina have a better attack than they did in 1990 but their greatest strength is still their uncompromising defence. This will be a severe test of both teams' pretensions and of Fifa's directive to referees to punish overly physical play and tackles from behind.
