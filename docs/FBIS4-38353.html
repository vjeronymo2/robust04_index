BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Viktor Demidenko and Mikhail Melnik] [Text] Kiev May 31 TASS -- Financial assistance to Ukraine in the construction of new nuclear power stations, so that the Chernobyl station could be shut down, was discussed during a closed door meeting between Group-of-Seven experts and a Ukrainian Government delegation here on Tuesday. According to well-informed sources, the international group of experts, after a series of meetings and consultations and the study of the situation, will prepare a report with due regard for the Ukrainian Government's wishes. The report will serve as the basis of a project for assistance to Kiev. The project is then to be considered at a working meeting of G-7 representatives in Rome in June. A specific `nuclear assistance' programme is to be discussed at a regular G-7 summit in Naples in July.
