940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / World News in Brief: Tietmeyer spells out vision for Europe European monetary union could not survive without a deep political consensus, Bundesbank president Hans Tietmeyer, left, believes. In the Financial Times today, he gives his views on monetary union, the role of central banks and the future size of the European Union. 'I cannot think in the longer term that we can live in a Europe where we close the doors to a country like the Czech Republic,' he says. 'On the other hand, I cannot see in the foreseeable future a situation where all the Maastricht Treaty conditions . . . for monetary union will be met by all countries.'
