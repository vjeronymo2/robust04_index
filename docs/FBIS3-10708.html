Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] The government will give at least six days notice before the launch of the new Brazilian currency, the real. This was announced today in Belo Horizonte by Central Bank President Pedro Malan. Malan said the decision on when to launch the real will depend on how widely the URV [Real Value Unit] is being used. Malan clearly stated that the change of currency will not take place before April. [Begin Malan recording] We promise to give a few days notice before the launch of the real in order to give people time to settle matters regarding commercial papers with preestablished rates, post-dated checks, etc. [end recording] Regarding an agreement with the IMF, Malan said it may be signed next week. He added that to safeguard the agreement, confirmation on the issuance of bonds to convert the Brazilian foreign debt has been postponed. [Begin Malan recording] Brazil and the committee of banks have issued a communique addressed to the international financial community, which states that we still plan on 15 April. This date will be confirmed late next week, around 18 March. [end recording] Pedro Malan confirmed that Minister Fernando Henrique Cardoso's economic plan, technically speaking, was approved by the IMF mission that visit Brazil recently.
