Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Minister of International Trade and Industry Hiroshi Kumagai attended a meeting at a Tokyo hotel on the morning of 2 March to exchange views with leading manufacturers of industrial machinery. Kozaburo Nishimura, chairman of the Japan Society of Industrial Machinery Manufacturers, said: "Since intensifying Japan-U.S. friction has resulted in the yen's appreciation, we are now faced with a difficult situation. Therefore, I hope efforts will be made to prevent Japan-U.S. relations from being impaired." Many participants called for steady implementation of the economic stimulus package. Concerning Japan-U.S. ties, Minister Kumagai stressed: "Japan should resolutely carry out whatever is achievable and settle economic problems existing between our two countries as soon as possible."
