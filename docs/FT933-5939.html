930901 FT 01 SEP 93 / World Trade News: New company breaks into Polish telecoms A NEWLY formed Polish telecommunications company has joined US Sprint and the International Finance Corporation to break the former state telecommunications monopoly and invest up to Dollars 2bn (Pounds 1.3bn) in modernising the telephones of eight Polish cities. The new company, RP Telecom, is starting by building an Dollars 80m telephone network for 100,000 subscribers at Pila, north-west Poland. A similar network is planned for the southern industrial city of Katowice and six other large towns. US Sprint will provide technical assistance and be the major shareholder in the operating company. Line installation at Pila will be undertaken by an Israeli company, Israel Aircraft Industries. The IFC, with Smith Barney, the US investment bank, helped to bring Sprint into the deal. The IFC will provide 10-15 per cent of the Dollars 32m equity of the new company and syndicate the bulk of the Dollars 48m debt raised to finance the Pila operation. The new company is structured on a 40 per cent equity and 60 per cent debt basis which will be expanded in step with the planned building of new networks in Polish cities.
