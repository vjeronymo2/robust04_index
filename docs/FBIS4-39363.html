BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Mainayev] [Text] Bishkek 8 June TASS -- Indian Minister of State for External Affairs Salman Khurshid's visit to Kirghizia [Kyrgyzstan] is aimed at broadening bilateral relations between India and Kirghizia. The minister arrived in Bishkek on Tuesday [7 June], and today is the first day of his working visit. Salman Khurshid is scheduled to meet Kirghiz President Askar Akayev, Prime Minister Abbas Dzhumagulov, members of the government and social and political workers. The Indian minister is accompanied by a group of Indian businessmen, who intend to sign a number of important agreements in Bishkek for the development of trade and economic relations between the two countries. The visit will last until 10 June.
