BFN [Text] Mr. 'Abd-al-Basit Sabdarat, minister of culture and information, issued a statement today concerning the abduction of Sudan's ambassador to Somalia. The statement said an armed group, composed of three persons belonging to the Somali (Kawadla) tribe, abducted Sudanese Ambassador 'Abd-al-Baqi Muhammad Hasan while he was driving home after work on 19 April. Immediately after the incident Sudanese authorities conducted intensive diplomatic and political maneuvers with all the concerned parties in Somalia, in particular, and the Horn of Africa, in general. These actions included direct dialogue with the abductors, which continued until late last night and carried on until this morning. The abductors asked for hard currency in exchange for freeing the ambassador. According to a statement, the ambassador is alive and well and is enjoying good health. The statement also reports that he was not harmed in any way. The statement continues, saying that the Sudanese authorities will make every effort to ensure that the Sudanese ambassador to Somalia is returned safely.
