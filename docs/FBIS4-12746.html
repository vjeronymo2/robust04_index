BFN [Editorial Report] Tel Aviv DAVAR in Hebrew on 12 April, in a page 4 report by Moti Bassok on the Cairo talks, reports that "significant progress was made yesterday on a series of security, civilian, and military issues. Political sources in Jerusalem assessed yesterday that the agreement on the Israeli withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho will be signed by the end of the month. Two days after that, Israel will release 2,500 Palestinian prisoners." Basok reports that "a timetable for the Israeli withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho following the signing of the agreement was determined yesterday. Other things decided were a timetable for the release of Palestinian prisoners and the deployment dates for the Palestinian police force. Progress was also achieved on the issues of control of water resources, security in Gaza's naval zone, and control over the Gaza Strip and Jericho airspace. Most issues pertaining to judicial powers were also resolved." Tel Aviv YEDI'OT AHARONOT in Hebrew on 12 April on pages 1 and 19 carries a report by No'omi Levitzky saying that "the substantial entry of Palestinian policemen to the Gaza Strip and Jericho will start in early May. The force will number 9,000 policemen: 2,000 from the territories and 7,000 from abroad. The deployment of the policemen in Gaza and Jericho will be completed by the end of May. The IDF [Israel Defense Forces] withdrawal will proceed at the same time and will be completed three weeks after the agreement is signed. "The IDF withdrawal timetable at this stage looks as follows: During the first week after the agreement is signed, the IDF will withdraw from Rafah and Jericho. During the second week, it will pull out of Khan Yunus, and in the third week, it will leave Gaza and the northern part of the Strip. The body in charge of managing the autonomy will settle in Jericho during the first week after the agreement is signed."
