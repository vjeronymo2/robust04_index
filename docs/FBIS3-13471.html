Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Dubai, United Arab Emirates, March 12 (AFP) -- Jordan is trying to re-enter lucrative Gulf markets after more than three years of estrangement caused by its alleged support for Iraq against Kuwait, officials said on Friday [11 March]. More than 20 Jordanian companies will display clothes, furniture, medicine and other products at an international exhibition in Dubai in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) this month, they said. "The participation in this fair and other fairs is aimed at restoring Jordanian manufacturers' share of the Gulf market to the pre-Gulf crisis level," a spokesman for the participating companies said in a statement. "These non-traditional markets require a different set of marketing and manufacturing standards and our manufacturers are trying to meet them. In terms of quality and service, most companies have undergone restructuring while revamping their marketing practices to face new challenges." Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) states were among the biggest markets for Jordanian products before they halted dealing over its alleged sympathy with Iraq. Amman has denied backing Iraq's 1990 invasion of Kuwait and has tried to several times to restore links but has been rebuffed. The six members -- Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, Bahrain and the UAE-- were also the main financial backers of Jordan before the war, providing more than 12 billion dollars in aid and billions more in remittances. A suspension of Gulf aid aggravated Jordan's economic problems, which include a debt of around eight billion dollars. The Jordan Trade Association, the country's main export promoter representing more than 80 firms, is spearheading the participation in the March 16-20 fair in Dubai, the organisers, al-Fajir company, said.
