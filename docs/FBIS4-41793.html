BFN [By GIA-ITAR-TASS] [Tbilisi] June 27 TASS -- The first congress of the joint Communist Party of Georgia brought together 550 delegates in Tbilisi. Retired major general Panteleimon Giorgadze presided over the congress. He was elected first secretary of the joint Communist Party of Georgia by the delegates. "Our aim is to be represented in parliament, and to become the country's ruling party. To achieve it, unification is necessary. Delegates from the Workers' Communist Party of Georgia, the Georgian Union of Communists, the "Stalin" political organization and a public organization called the International Society to Honour the Memory of Stalin participated in the congress. There are no differences in their standpoints, which enabled us to form a joint Communist Party," Giorgadze told GIA on Monday.
