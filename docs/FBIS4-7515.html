BFN [Text] Mate Granic, deputy prime minister and foreign minister of the Republic of Croatia, has presented to members of the Diplomatic Corps accredited to Croatia copies of the text of the cease-fire agreement signed yesterday between representatives of the Croatian authorities and Serb rebels from the temporarily occupied areas of the Republic of Croatia. Minister Granic said Croatia was ready to take on a constructive role in negotiations with the Serb rebels and that it expected further progress in the implementation of the agreement and in the overall process of the peaceful reintegration of the occupied areas in the constitutional and legal system of the Republic of Croatia. Granic also briefed the diplomats on the results so far of the implementation of the Washington agreement. All clashes between Muslim Bosnians and Croats in Bosnia-Herzegovina have ceased and humanitarian deliveries have improved. At the same time deputies of the constituent assembly accepted the Constitution of Bosnia-Herzegovina in Sarajevo yesterday. Granic added that he expected representatives of the Serbian people to soon join in the negotiations on the peaceful settlement of the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina.
