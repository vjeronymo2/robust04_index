BFN [Text] There is a warning strike under way in Hungarian Radio, called by the Independent Trade Union of Radio Employees. The "Chronicle" program will fulfill its public service duty and report on the main news briefly. The strike committee of the Independent Trade Union of Radio Employees called for a warning strike today because of economic events that have put employees in a disadvantegous situation. The trade union does not want the warning strike to disturb the program order of the radio prior to the elections, so everything will be conducted according to the strike regulations in force. The strike committee operating in Hungarian Radio called for two two-hour warning strikes: from 1100 to 1300 hours and for those working in the afternoon, from 1700 to 1900 hours. According to the communique of the strike committee, the objective of the warning strike is to initiate the success of the negotiations between the leadership of Hungarian Radio and organizations representing the employees and the protection of the radio employees' economic and social interests.
