BFN [Text] Talks on the settlement of the Georgian-Abkhaz conflict, mediated by Russia, continued today in Moscow. According to a participant in the talks, Abkhazia's envoy to Russia Igor Akhba, a draft document on the cease-fire and the deployment of forces was prepared yesterday following yesterday's discussion. According to the draft, the two sides will separate their forces from the confrontation line along the Inguri River at a distance of 12 km. A buffer zone is being created, where the CIS peacekeeping forces will be deployed under UN observers. The status of Abkhazia will be discussed in June in Geneva. The report by the envoy of the Republic of Abkhazia says that a document on setting up a coordinative commission was signed. The commission is to resolve the issues connected with economy, transportation, and ecology.
