BFN [Text] A meeting was held at the Ministry of Planning and Development today with a number of the ambassadors to our country from fraternal and friendly states, as well as officials from certain international organizations. Mutahhar 'Abdallah al-Sa'idi, deputy minister of planning and development, spoke at the meeting about the emergency needs faced by the country in the fields of health, water, sewage, electricity, and education that have resulted from the military operations carried out by the secessionist clique in the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP] leadership. He said many of these facilities were damaged during these military operations. He also spoke about the pressing need to extend urgent aid, food and shelters to the citizens who were harmed during that period. 'Awni al-'Ani, representative of the UN program in Sanaa, spoke during the meeting. He stressed the urgent need for this aid, hoping that these states will cooperate, either directly, or within the framework of the United Nations, to extend the necessary aid. He noted that committees to assess damages will begin their work as soon as possible to determine the actual needs. The under secretary of the Public Health Ministry attended the meeting.
