920507 FT 07 MAY 92 / Gorbachev blames East and West for Cold War MR Mikhail Gorbachev, the former Soviet leader, said yesterday that both east and west deserved blame for the Cold War and that the Soviet Union never intended to attack the west, Agencies report. He told a crowd of thousands in Fulton, Missouri, that the US mistakenly fed hostilities by believing in 'the probability of open' Soviet aggression. 'This could never happen, not only because Stalin ..was afraid of war and did not want war ..but primarily because our country was exhausted and destroyed.'
