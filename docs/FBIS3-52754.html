Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Jan. 6, (IRNA) -- Seeing off Turkmen President Saparmurad Niyazov at the end of his five-day official visit to Iran Thursday [6 January], President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani termed Niyazov's visit to Tehran as a "turning point" in mutual relations. The two presidents also announced that promotion of Tehran-Ashgabat relations will gain further momentum from now on. The Turkmen president told reporters at Tehran's Mehrabad International Airport that three historical documents had been inked between the two countries during his visit. "The Turkmen delegation did a great job in conjunction with their Iranian counterparts in economic, trade and cultural fields...the agreements reached between the two countries will entail great results for both nations in the future," he said. Hojatoleslam Hashemi-Rafsanjani said that the visit of President Niyazov to Tehran is unique compared with other official visits made to Iran, because all Turkmen cabinet ministers, Friday prayer leaders and other influential officials of that country were present here. Hashemi-Rafsanjani maintained that mutual cooperation will take a greater momentum after President Niyazov's visit, adding that this would be beneficiary to other countries of the region.
