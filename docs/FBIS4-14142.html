BFN [Text] The Sudanese ambassador to Somalia, 'Abd-al-Baqi Muhammad Hasan Ahmad, was released in Mogadishu today after continuous negotiations with his abductors, Foreign Ministry officials, and relevant organs. Four armed people abducted the ambassador on 19 April. This act was strongly condemned in all quarters, especially by the leaders of the tribe to which the abductors belong, and who exerted an appreciable effort obtain the ambassador's release. The Foreign Ministry, in a statement issued today, commended all those who contributed to the release of Ambassador 'Abd-al-Baqi, particularly all concerned Somali organizations and groups. The statement also praised the role played by the Sudanese ambassador in Mogadishu, under very complicated circumstances. The Foreign Ministry confirmed that this unexpected crisis would not affect the firm relations that bind the Sudanese and Somali peoples. The statement confirmed that the Government of Sudan would continue to offer all it could to contribute to achieving security, stability and development in Somalia.
