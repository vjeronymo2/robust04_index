Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 25 KYODO -- The Foreign Ministry on Friday lifted an advisory that had recommended that Japanese nationals evacuate the former Yugoslav states of Serbia and Montenegro. The move followed a decision by the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (Nato) to forego air strikes against Bosnian Serb positions on the hills surrounding Sarajevo. An advisory cautioning against travel to Serbia and Montenegro remains in force, the ministry said.
