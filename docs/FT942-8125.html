940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / Summer Festivals: Catch up with culture around the world - Our critics offer a brief guide to a civilised break at home and abroad AIX-EN-PROVENCE July 15 - 30 Ancien Palais de l'Archeveche, 13100 Aix-en-Provence, France. 33-42 17 34 00 Hard times have reached the South of France. From three operas Aix has had to reduce to one: a new production of Mozart's Die Zauberflote by its winning team of recent years, the conductor William Christie and producer Robert Carsen. An uninspiring selection of concerts will probably not be enough to tempt regulars back to this delightful town, though the food and general air of good living might. ALDEBURGH June 10 - 26 High Street, Aldeburgh, Suffolk IP15 5AX. Tel 0728-453543 Britten and Stravinsky are the featured composers with a wide range of their smaller-scale works on offer and a series of Stravinsky on film. The London Contemporary Dance Theatre visits with a new programme combining the two composers. The other stage event is Britten's Noye's Fludde. Recitalists include Russian soprano Galina Gorchakova and pianist Pedja Muzijevic. New music continues to features prominently with world or UK premieres of works by Colin Matthews, Magnus Lindberg, Elena Firsova and Jonathan Lloyd BAD KISSINGEN June 24 - July 17 Kissinger Sommer, Postfach 2260, D-97672 Bad Kissingen, Germany. Tel 0971-807110 Fax 0971-807191 The attraction of Bad Kissingen is its north Bavarian setting and the relaxed atmosphere of a former royal spa. The Regentenbau incorporates four elegant concert halls, and there is also a fin-de-siecle theatre. Among this year's events are a Schubert recital by Andras Schiff, song recitals by Edita Gruberova and Wolfgang Holzmair, a Mahler concert with Christa Ludwig and symphony concerts conducted by Donald Runnicles, Vaclav Neumann and Vladimir Ashkenazy. BATH May 27 - June 12 Linley House, Pierrepoint Place, Bath BA1 1JY. Tel 0225-463362/466411 This is the first festival under the auspices of the Bath Festivals Trust, launched to raise the profile of Bath as a festivals city. The theme of 'Ancient Echoes' links music of the past with music of the present. Kent Opera, risen from the ashes, performs Britten's The Prodigal Son. Jonathan Miller's staging of Bach's St. Matthew Passion gets open-air performances. The Tallis Scholars, Frans Bruggen and Roger Norrington are among those performing the music of the past, Gorecki and Nyman the featured 20th-century composers. BAYREUTH July 25 - August 28 Kartenburo der Bayreuther Festspiele, Postfach 100262, D-95402 Bayreuth, Germany. Tel 0921-20221 This year's new Ring brings together arch-radical producer Alfred Kirchner and arch-conservative conductor James Levine, but its most controversial feature may turn out to be the designs of German illustrator Rosalie. John Tomlinson's Wotan and Deborah Polaski's Brunnhilde are carried over from the Kupfer production, and the new Siegfried is Wolfgang Schmidt. Giuseppe Sinopoli takes over as conductor of Parsifal. The other revivals are Tristan und Isolde and Der fliegende Hollander. Unless you are prepared to pay a heavy black market premium, it is now too late to get hold of tickets. BREGENZ July 20 - August 26 Kartenburo der Bregenzer Festspiele, Postfach 311, A-6901 Bregenz, Austria. Tel 05574-492 0223 Fax 05574-492 0228 The opera festival at the Austrian end of Lake Constance has won an enviable reputation for artistic boldness, while preserving its appeal for tourist audiences. David Pountney's spectacular 1993 production of Nabucco is revived on the lakeside floating stage - counterbalanced in the nearby indoor theatre by a rare staging of Zandonai's Francesca da Rimini, with Mara Zampieri in the title role. BRIGHTON FESTIVAL Until May 31 The Dome Theatre, 29 New Rd, Brighton BN 1VG. Tel 0273-709709 This year's festival is the last to be directed by Gavin Henderson; 'Kindred Spirits' and 'Romeo and Juliet' are the themes, featuring some prestigious international theatre - the Odeon Theatre of Bucharest in Richard III, the Wayang Wayang Theatre Company from Singapore, and the Wierszalin Theatr Company from Poland. British theatre includes Cheek by Jowl's Measure for Measure, the Wrestling School's staging of Howard Barker's Hated Nightfall. Classical music includes a recital by Teresa Berganza; Mark Elder conducting a Berlioz concert; and an all-Russian-music programme to close the festival. Opera Restor'd perform the premiere of a new staging of Dido and Aeneas. BRUSSELS Until June 5. Tel 010-332 223 2300 This festival presents performances, especially of theatre and dance, from 12 countries. Theatre highlights include Theatergroep Hollandia's new productions of Marguerite Duras's La Musica Deuxieme and Lulu; the Belgian Groupov's new Penthy 2, based on Kleist's Penthesilea; the Teatro Stabile from Turin presenting two scripts by Pasolini, Calderon and Affabulazione; the Romanian National Theatre of Craiova in Phaedra; and the Polish Stary Teatr Krakow in Thomas Bernhard's Kalkwerk. CHELTENHAM July 2 - 17 Box Office, Town Hall, Cheltenham GL50 1QA. Tel: 0242-227976 Cheltenham is marking its golden jubilee year by celebrating 50 years as a champion of contemporary music. Among the Cheltenham commissions being revived from the last half-century are works by Maxwell Davies, Rawsthorne, Maconchy, Lennox Berkeley and Williamson. To show that Cheltenham has a future as well as a past there is an array of world premieres by Colin Matthews, Powers, Heath, Bennett, Michael Berkeley, and a major new work by Thea Musgrave. There is also a complete cycle of the Beethoven string quartets. CHELTENHAM LITERARY FESTIVAL October 7 - 16 Tel 0242 521621 This has established itself as the largest in Europe. This year a new director, children's writer Humphrey Carpenter, has introduced themed days. For example, October 9 is Booker Day and introduces some of the short listed writers, with critics giving their views on the likely winner. The 1894 Booker Prize will also be posthumously announced. Other theme days cover science; psychology; the British whodunnit (with PD James); old devils of 20th century literature, starring Kingsley Amis; history; and the church in literature, with Robert Runcie. Among the writers promised are Alan Bennett, Douglas Adams, Craig Brown, Susan Hill, Irish Murdoch, Joanna Trollope and Ian McEwan. Ralph Steadman is Artist in Residence and he will stage the premiere of 'I Leonardo', his imaginary autobiography of Leonardo. DRESDEN Until June 5 Dresdner Musikfestspiele, Postfach 160106, D-01287 Dresden, Germany. Tel 0351-486 6666 Fax 0351-486 6307 With its great traditions and court palaces, Dresden has enormous potential as a festival centre, but recession and the cost of reunification are slowing the pace. The new festival director, Michael Hampe, has threaded a programme around August the Strong, whose accession as Saxon ruler 300 years ago heralded a golden era in Dresden's artistic life. Visiting baroque specialists include I Solisti Veneti and Concerto Koln, and there will be a chance to hear rare choral and operatic works by Telemann, Handel and Hasse. The Semperoper has Capriccio with Felicity Lott, plus a new production of The Cunning Little Vixen. DROTTNINGHOLM May 26 - September 9 'Forestallningar', Box 27050, 10251 Stockholm. Tel: (0)77-1707070 In this 18th-century working court theatre with original machinery and sets, the emphasis is on operas from the period of the theatre's birth. This year opens with the Swedish Royal Opera in Ungdom og galskap (Youth and Folly) by Edouard Dupuy. Drottningolm's own productions are Haydn's Orlando Paladino conducted by Nicholas McGegan and a fantasia on the works of Handel called 'Heavenly Handel' with Barbara Bonney and Anne Sofie von Otter among the visiting singers. Arriving by boat from Stockholm is a delightful extra. DULWICH September 24 - October 2 75 Calton Avenue, London SE21 7DF. Tel 081-693 3577 This community festival provides a showcase for local talent, both amateur and professional. Highlights include Camberwell Pocket Opera's The Marriage of Figaro, London City Theatre's The Dumb Waiter, a poetry competition, jazz composition workshops and a sculpture project to enhance Dulwich Railway station. EDINBURGH August 14 - September 3 21, Market St, Edinburgh EHl 1BW. Tel 031-225 5756. Fax 031-226 7669 The 48th Edinburgh International Festival must be the biggest ever. The opening of the Festival Theatre, which at last gives the Festival a stage large enough to handle the grandest lyric theatre, also means that director Brian McMaster has another 24,000 tickets to sell. Beethoven is the festival composer this year and the first production at the Festival Theatre is Scottish Opera's Fidelio on August 15. Also appearing there is the Australian Opera in Britten's A Midsummer Night's Dream, and those Edinburgh favourites the Mark Morris Dance Group from the US. All nine Beethoven symphonies will be played, by orchestras from Cleveland, Stavanger and Hamburg plus the Orchestra of the Age of Enlightenment, as well as all his five piano concertos and many of his string quartets. Among the musicians involved are Alfred Brendel, Andras Schiff and Piotr Anderszewski. The other featured composer is Emmanuel Chabrier, a contemporary of Bizet. Opera North performs the pantomime-like L'Etoile and the comic opera Le Roi Malgre Lui, while there is a concert performance of his unfinished opera Brises. The opening concert at the Usher Hall is the Royal Scottish National Orchestra with Mahler's 'Symphony of a Thousand', and the closing is Elgar's 'The Dream of Gerontius' with the same orchestra. Making a return to the festival are directors Peter Stein and Robert Lepage. Stein presents a Russian cast in Aeschylus' trilogy Oresteia, which will last 7 1/2 hours, while Lepage gives a world premiere of his new work, The Seven Streams of the River Ota, the river which runs underneath Hiroshima. Among the other theatrical works on offer are Goethe's Torquato Tasso, about the 16th century Italian poet, in an English translation; JM Synge's The Well of the Saints from the Abbey Theatre of Dublin; and two Shakespeare plays, Antony and Cleopatra, performed in German by the Berliner Ensemble, and The Winter's Tale, performed in French by the National Drama Centre of Orleans. Luc Bondy makes his UK directorial debut with a quintessential international festival production, The hour we knew nothing of each other, a play without words by Peter Handke, performed by the Schaubuhne Theatre, Berlin. Over 30 actors will play more than 400 characters on the vast stage of the Festival Theatre. Ticket prices have been changed, making the best seats more expensive but increasing the number of cheap seats. There will also be Pounds 5 'day seats' for certain major productions. The Festival still lacks a strong visual arts programme but the main summer exhibitions organised by the National Galleries of Scotland, in particular 'Monet to Matisse: landscape painting in France 1874-1914', will be on show throughout the Festival. In addition, of course, running concurrently with the Festival, will be the Edinburgh Fringe, the largest festival in the world and the national showcase for comedy; the Film Festival; and the Jazz Festival, as well as the Military Tattoo. FELDKIRCH June 13 - 26 Schubertiade Feldkirch, Schubertplatz 1, Postfach 625, A-6803 Feldkirch, Austria. Tel 05522-38001 Fax 05522-38005 Formerly housed at Hohenems, this rarefied Schubert festival is now well-established in nearby Feldkirch, where the recital halls are as handsome as the wooded scenery of eastern Austria. Highlights include Brahms programmes sung by Brigitte Fassbaender and Anne Sofie von Otter, Schubert recitals by Cecilia Bartoli and Olaf Bar, and performances of Winterreise and Die schone Mullerin by Peter Schreier and Andras Schiff. GREENWICH June 3 - 12 151 Powis Street, London SE18 6JL. Tel 081-317 8687 Eclectic and imaginative mix of events incorporating music (the Dirty Dozen Brass band, the Flying Bulgar Klezmer Band, the English Chamber orchestra), dance (with Yolande Snaith and company), comedy, childrens' events and fireworks. Excellent for a cultural excursion en famille. HAMPTON COURT PALACE June 3 - 15 Hamton Court Palace, East Molesey, Surrey. Tel 071-344 4444 Twelve day extravaganza of music, dance and opera featuring Kiri Te Kanawa, Montserrat Caballe, Sylvie Guillem and Willard White. HARROGATE July 29 - August 12 Royal Baths, Harrogate, North Yorkshire HG1 2RR. Tel 0423-562303. Fax 0423-521264 Harrogate boasts an impressive number of world premiers this year, with new works by James MacMillan, John McCabe and Allan Charlton. The Stuttgart Ballet give a rare and exclusive British appearance, Michael Nyman performs solo and with his band, and Harrogate has its first ever Street Theatre Festival. HAY-ON-WYE Until May 30 Tel 0497-821299. Fax 0497-821066 Most people, even non-readers, have heard of the bookshop at Hay-on-Wye and of the Hay Festival, an eclectic gathering of writers and entertainers reading from, talking about or performing their work. This year, there are 100 events ranging from Soweto Jive Music to writing masterclasses through Indian cinema, mountaineering, Scandinavian poetry and a PEN lecture by Carlos Fuentes. Hay means instruction and delight. James Fenton, the new Oxford Professor of Poetry, gives a reading on Saturday. Other poets participating include Andrew Motion, Hugo Williams and Paul Muldoon. The final Sunday offers John Mortimer on murder, Roy Strong on gardens, Fascinating Aida in cabaret and the Brecon Cathedral Choir in compline. HOLLAND June 1 - 30 Netherlands Reservation Centre, Box 404, 2260 AK Leidschendam, The Netherlands. Tel 070-320 2500 Fax 070-320 2611 This year's Holland Festival centres on opera and music theatre. The most eye-catching events are rare performances of Chausson's Le Roi Arthus and Max Brand's Machinist Hopkins, plus the continuation of John Eliot Gardiner's Mozart cycle with Don Giovanni. Another theme is the idea of musical realisation - such as Bruno Maderna's version of Monteverdi's L'Orfeo. The concert programme focuses on young Chinese composers. Other attractions include theatre productions by Peter Zadek and Peter Brook, and a tribute to choreographer Merce Cunningham. HUKVALDY June 24 - July 3 Janacek in Hukvaldy, c/o Progressive Tours, 12 Porchester Place, London WC2 2BS. Tel 071-262 1676 Fax 071-724 6941 Hukvaldy is a peaceful town in the heart of the north Moravian countryside, where Czech composer Leos Janacek was born in 1854. The organisers of this new festival aim to make it a centre of pilgrimage for all who admire Janacek's music. The Cunning Little Vixen, staged by ENO staff producer David Sulkin, will be performed in the open-air at Hukvaldy park, while musicians from the nearby city of Ostrava will give concerts in the medieval castle. ISTANBUL June 15 - July 21 Istanbul International Festival, Yildiz-Besiktas, TR-80746 Istanbul, Turkey. Tel 212-258 3212 Fax 212-261 8823 Istanbul's arts festival aims to stress the city's historic role as a bridge between Europe and Asia, but its programme is unashamedly western in origin. This year's guests include the BBC Symphony Orchestra under Gunther Herbig, the English Chamber Orchestra under James Judd and Dresden Philharmonic with soprano Hildegard Behrens. Valery Gergiev conducts the Kirov Opera Orchestra and Chorus in a series of concerts, and Roland Petit brings his Ballet National de Marseille. KREUTH July 3 - 17 Internationales Oleg-Kagan-Musikfest am Tegernsee, Kurverwaltung Kreuth, Postfach 5, D-83708 Kreuth, Germany. Tel 08029-1819 Fax 08029-1828 The lakeside setting of Kreuth, a former court spa at the foot of the Bavarian Alps, makes this one of Europe's most peaceful and intimate festivals. It was founded five years ago by the late Russian violinist Oleg Kagan and his cellist wife Natalia Gutman, and is notable for attracting high-powered virtuosi. This year's programme is headed by Sviatoslav Richter and Martha Argerich. KYBURGIADE August 4 - 10 Internationales Kammermusik-Festival auf Schloss Kyburg, Postfach 408, CH-8400 Winterthur, Switzerland. Tel 01-251 4044 Fax 01-251 4060 Now in its third year, the Kyburgiade owes its unique atmosphere to the open-air setting of Kyburg Castle near the Swiss town of Winterthur, and to the participation of one of Europe's leading young string quartets, the Carmina Quartet. They are joined by London Baroque, the Hagen Quartet, I Musici di Roma, pianist Paul Gulda and oboist Hansjorg Schellenberger, in programmes focusing on Bach, Mozart and Vivaldi. LA ROQUE D'ANTHERON July 30 - August 21 Festival de Piano, Chateau de Florans, 13640 La Roque d'Antheron, France. Tel 4250 5115 Fax 4250 4889 The castle grounds of La Roque d'Antheron, equidistant from Avignon and Marseille, provide a serene provencal atmosphere for a piano festival of increasing international renown. You may not find Richter or Pollini here, but the festival does offer the cream of French pianists, plus other well-established artists such as Nelson Freire, Deszo Ranki, Stephen Hough and Elisabeth Leonskaja. LONDON, THE PROMS July 15 - September 10 Promenade Concerts Ticket Shop, Royal Albert Hall, London SW7 2AP. Tel:071-589 8212 The Proms are celebrating their 100th season. The temptation to look back over a century as Britain's largest music festival has proved irresistible and this year's programme comprises a survey of the people and ideals that have made the Proms so successful. Among the great figures of Proms' history allowed evenings in their tribute are William Glock, Malcolm Sargent and Henry Wood. The programme of 6 September 1900 is repeated in its entirety, an amusing historical exercise. The work of the Proms in encouraging new music is recognised by recalling past premieres by composers such as Maw, Bliss, Schoenberg and Britten. Among major premieres new this year are Maxwell Davies's Fifth Symphony and Tavener's The Apocalypse. The visiting orchestras include the Berlin Philharmonic, the Cleveland Orchestra, the Dresden Staatskapelle, Los Angeles Philharmonic and Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra. LUCERNE August 17 - September 10 Internationale Musikfestwochen Luzern, Hirschmattstrasse 13, Postfach, CH-6002 Lucerne, Switzerland. Tel 041-233080 Fax 041-239464 Under Matthias Bamert, Switzerland's premier festival has taken on an adventurous slant. Focal points this year are a 70th birthday tribute to Swiss composer Klaus Huber, whose new piano concerto will be premiered by Andras Schiff, and a wide-ranging exploration of the way music is interpreted. Four different performances will be built around Schubert's Winterreise, including a new opera, augmented by offbeat events breaking all the rules of traditional concert form. The conventional side to the festival is as strong as ever, with leading orchestras from Berlin, Vienna, Amsterdam, Cleveland and Dresden. MALTA June 2 - 5 Manoel Theatre, Valletta. Tel 010-356 222618 Fax 356-247 451 This year's festival embraces music, architecture and the Maltese heritage. Together with concerts by the orchestra Aula Classica Salisburgensis (performing on period instruments) and The Manoel Theatre Orchestra, there is an extensive programme of lectures and guided tours of local galleries and museums. MONTPELLIER July 10 - August 4 Festival de Radio France, Le Corun, BP 9214, 34043 Montepellier Cedex 01, France. Tel 6702 0201 Apart from its setting in southern France, the attraction of Radio France's annual festival is its selection of rare operas. MONTREUX August 21 - September 23 Festival de Musique Montreux-Vevey, Rue du Theatre 5, Case postale 162, CH-1820 Montreux 2, Switzerland. Tel 021-963 5450 Fax 021-963 2506 Montreux offers a sultry lakeside setting, and a haphazard collection of orchestras and soloists from the summer festival circuit. This year's programme opens with a Pollini recital and ends with Martha Argerich playing Beethoven. Other visitors include Anne-Sophie Mutter, the Pittsburgh Symphony under Maazel and the Orchestra of La Scala under Giulini. MUNICH July 6 - 31 Festspielkasse der Bayerischen Staatsoper, Maximilianstrasse 11, D-80539 Munich, Germany. Tel 089-221316 Peter Jonas' first Munich Opera Festival opens with Tannhauser - and by handing the production to David Alden he is clearly aiming to unsettle Munich's conservative public and critics. Zubin Mehta conducts. The festival offers a retrospective on Jonas' first year as intendant in Munich, including Tom Cairns' dire production of Un ballo in maschera and Richard Jones' Jurassic Park version of Giulio Cesare. Cheryl Studer sings in La traviata, and Eckehard Mayer's new opera Sansibar can be seen in the Cuvillies-Theater. OSLO August 3 - 13 Oslo Chamber Music Festival, Inkognitogt 33, 0256 Oslo, Norway. Tel 2255 2553 Fax 2255 2554 Founded by Norwegian violinist Arve Tellefsen in 1989, the Oslo Chamber Music Festival has quickly won a reputation for conviviality and musical quality. Concerts take place in churches, castles and concert halls around Oslo, with each year's programme focusing on a different country. This year is Britain's turn, with music ranging from Byrd and Bridge to David Matthews and Oliver Knussen. The Nash and Hilliard Ensembles are taking part, while Truls Mork will play Elgar's Cello Concerto and Yuri Bashmet gives a viola recital. PESARO August 11 - 29. Via Rossini 37, 61100 Pesaro. Tel: (0)721-33184 This exquisite walled town on the Adriatic was Rossini's birthplace. Each year it brings together genteel lovers of the Italian maestro's music, who come to explore some of his lesser-known operas, alongside bucket-and-spade beach-goers. This year's programme includes productions of L'inganno felice produced by Graham Vick, Semiramide conducted by Roger Norrington and L'italiana in Algeri produced by Dario Fo. SAINT LOUIS May 21 - June 26 Opera Theater of Saint Louis, PO Box 191910, Saint Louis, Missouri 63119, USA. Tel 314-961 0171 Saint Louis - one of the world's most pleasurable opera festivals - offers a characteristically adventurous diet: Colin Graham stages the first US performance of Bernstein's final revised version of Candide, Andrew Litton conducts Falstaff, the outstanding young American soprano Patricia Racette sings the title role in Iphigenie en Tauride, and there is a dusting-off of Conrad Susa's Black River (1975). Theatre conditions are intimate, and picnics on the lawns of the Loretto-Hilton Center round off the amiable atmosphere. SALZBURG July 25 - August 31 Kartenburo der Salzburger Festspiele, Postfach 140, A-5010 Salzburg. Tel 0662-846682 Fax 0662-844501 The 1994 festival takes to the sky with the premiere of Stockhausen's Helicopter Quartet: while the composer controls the sound electronically in the Mozarteum, members of the Arditti Quartet will play their parts in four airborne helicopters. Back on terra firma, this year's flagship production is Don Giovanni, staged by Patrice Chereau and conducted by Daniel Barenboim, with a cast headed by Ferruccio Furlanetto, Bryn Terfel, Catherine Malfitano and Cecilia Bartoli. The opera programme otherwise has a Russian emphasis, with three Stravinsky stagings and a transfer of the Claudio Abbado/Herbert Wernicke production of Boris Godunov from the Easter Festival, with Samuel Ramey in the title role. In the concert hall, Nikolaus Harnoncourt conducts the Chamber Orchestra of Europe in two cycles of Beethoven symphonies. The drama programme continues to gather strength, with Shakespeare's Antony and Cleopatra directed by Peter Stein, and Pirandello's The Mountain Giants directed by Luca Ronconi. SANTA FE July 1 - August 27 PO Box 2408, Santa Fe, New Mexico 87504, USA. Tel 505-986 5900 Fax 505-989 7012 The most eye-catching feature is the American premiere of Judith Weir's Blond Eckbert, less than four months after it was unveiled by ENO. Francesca Zambello produces this and Il barbiere di Siviglia. This year's other new productions are Tosca directed by John Copley with Mary Jane Johnson in the title role, and Entfuhrung directed by Graham Vick. A revival of Goran Jarvefelt's 1984 production of Intermezzo completes the bill. And the pleasures of the place itself never pall. SAVONLINNA July 6 - 30 Savonlinna Opera Festival, Olavinkatu 35, 57130 Savonlinna, Finland. Tel 057-514700 Fax 057-21866 No-one who visits Finland's premier summer festival can fail to be impressed by the stone castle courtyard in which it takes place. Poised on the edge of a lake, Olaf's Castle (Olavinlinna) is one of the world's outstanding outdoor locations for opera. The 1994 programme is one of the least distinctive of recent years, with a revival of last year's successful Macbeth and a visit from the Hungarian National Opera. Perhaps the festival is husbanding its resources for next year's big splash - a new opera by Finland's leading composer, Aulis Salinnen. SCHLESWIG-HOLSTEIN. ST MAGNUS June 17 - 22 Tourist Information Centre, Kirkwall, Orkney, Scotland KW15 1DE. WEXFORD October 20 - November 6 Theatre Royal, High Street, Wexford, Ireland. Tel 0353-53 22144 Elaine Padmore's final season as artistic director brings a classic Wexford trio of neglected operas: The Demon by Anton Rubinstein, La Boheme by Leoncavallo and Wagner's Das Liebesverbot. A full concert schedule includes recitals by Wexford's young singers, operatic scenes and late-night cabaret. The homely atmosphere belies the fact that serious opera-lovers cross oceans to sample Wexford's operatic rarities.
