BFN [Text] Hong Kong, May 14 (XINHUA) -- The Sino-British talks on Hong Kong's future defense and public order continued here today when both sides failed to reach an agreement after a four-day talk ending Friday. Sources said the talk would run into next week. Chen Zuoer, resident representative of the Chinese team of the Sino-British Joint Liaison Group (JLG), said that he felt disappointed that an agreement was not reached yesterday as expected. Alan Paul, head of the British expert team under the JLG, said that they had a "thorough and in depth" discussion over the past few days.
