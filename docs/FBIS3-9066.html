Language: Albanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Bashkim Kopliku, deputy chairman of the Council of Ministers, and his Macedonian counterpart Jovan Andonov held business talks in Tirana today. The talks focused on the cooperation and port facilities that Albania may offer to Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] on the transport of goods from the Adriatic shores to Macedonia. We are predisposed to help Macedonia, Kopliku said at this meeting. Moreover, we are related to Macedonia by the fact that about 40 percent of its inhabitants are of Albanian nationality. The talks mainly focused on two main directions of the demands by the Macedonian side: to ensure the use of Durres port for transport as well as the use of one of the fuel stores in Vlore. Assessing the help that Albania would give to Macedonia, Macedonian Deputy Prime Minister Andonov asked that 30 percent of goods destined for Macedonia should pass through Albania, with advantageous conditions regarding customs expenses. Kopliku declared the readiness of the Albanian Government to reach an understanding. The Macedonian side confirmed its readiness to include Albania in the project to construct a gas pipeline from Russia to Skopje, and from there to Albania.
