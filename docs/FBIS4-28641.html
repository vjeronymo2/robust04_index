BFN [Text] Seoul, June 13 (YONHAP) -- The Defense Ministry has set its budget for next year at 11,338.8 billion won, a 12.5-percent rise from this year's 10,753 billion won, and is currently holding discussions with the Economic Planning Board [EPB]. The ministry announced Monday its operating and maintenance budget for next year has been raised by 13.6 percent to 7,997.3 billion won while the "maintenance for war potential" budget was increased to 3,341.5 billion won, up 9.9 percent from this year. If the draft budget is confirmed, the ratio of operating and maintenance costs will climb to 70.5 percent of the total budget from this year's 69.8 percent while that for combat war maintenance costs will drop to 29.5 percent. At the same time, the ministry decided to enhance the transparency of budget formulation and implementation by disclosing the details of the Yulgok budget in the course of deliberations at the EPB and National Assembly. The operating and maintenance budget will be used to improve the lives of servicemen through promotion of their morale and welfare (540.3 billion won); improvement of treatment for soldiers (172.8 billion won); and realization of operating expenses for units (65.2 billion won). In particular, the ministry set aside 540.3 billion won to boost pay for professional soldiers, increase allowances for special-area duty and stabilize housing for long-term servicemen.
