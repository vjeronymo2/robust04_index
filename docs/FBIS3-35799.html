Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Nikita Yermakov and Konstantin Pribytkov] [Text] Vaduz (Principality of Liechtenstein), 31 Jan (ITAR-TASS) -- Russia has established direct diplomatic relations with the Principality of Liechtenstein. A joint communique signed today in the capital of this small alpine state by Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and Mario Frick, chief of government of the Principality of Liechtenstein, stresses the desire of both countries and peoples to develop and strengthen relations and to promote mutual understanding and cooperation on the basis of the principles and standards of international law. The accord on the establishment of direct diplomatic relations comes into force from the date of signing. During his stay in Vaduz, Viktor Chernomyrdin had a private audience with Prince Hans Adam II von und zu Liechtenstein, who gave a dinner in honor of the top-ranking Russian guest. The head of the Russian Government also had a conversation with the head of the Liechtenstein Government. They exchanged views on the state of relations between the two countries, above all in the economic field. The heads of government called for the all-round development of bilateral contacts. In Vaduz, the Russian prime minister visited Baron Eduard von (?Falz-Fein), a citizen of Liechtenstein of Russian origin, who was recently awarded the Order of Friendship of the Peoples for his outstanding contribution to preserving and increasing the cultural heritage of the Russian Federation and for returning cultural artifacts and works of art to the country.
