930525 FT 25 MAY 93 / Crime prevention panel launched THE GOVERNMENT yesterday named a number of business leaders, including senior directors of Boots, Marks and Spencer and Vauxhall Motors, to serve on the National Board for Crime Prevention. It will be responsible for suggesting new ways of controlling crime and spreading crime prevention advice. Mr Michael Jack, Home Office minister, said yesterday the new board would go beyond the 'locks and bolts' of crime prevention. 'I have recruited brainstormers who will produce creative, innovative but, above all, practical ideas to prevent crime. This board will be no mere talking shop.'
