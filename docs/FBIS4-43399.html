CSO [Finnish News Service report: "Over Half Dissatisfied With the Government"] [Text] Although the government led by Prime Minister Esko Aho (Center Party) has increased its popular support, a majority 53 percent of Finns are still dissatisfied with it. According to an opinion poll conducted by Gallup of Finland for the [Oulu] newspaper KALEVA, less than one-fifth of citizens, or 18 percent, are satisfied with the government, and of these, only 2 percent said they were extremely well satisfied. All told, the proportion of those satisfied with the government has risen by 4 percentage points since the December survey. One-fourth, or 26 percent, of those interviewed said they were extremely dissatisfied. Another full one-fourth refused to state an opinion as to whether they were satisfied or dissatisfied. The satisfaction of farmers with the government has risen by one-fifth since the previous poll. Of this occupational group, nearly half -- or 46 percent -- give the government a good grade. Aho's own personal performance was also rated as satisfactory by around one-fifth of citizens. The prime minister's approval rating has risen by 4 points since December.
