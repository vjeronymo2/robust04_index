May 20, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

'DR. WELSING IS THE FIRST SCIENTIST TO PSYCHOANALYZE WHITE RACISM. . . .'

Beyond the scientific information on color dominance, whites also emulate black people in terms of what we do and say and how we look.

For instance, the bustle worn by whites was inspired by the prominent buttocks of black women (i.e., Hottentot sculpture and other images in the 17th and 18th Centuries in Europe); dances and phases and gestures invented by blacks are immediately snatched and adopted by whites.

I believe melanin has a great deal to do with the association of blacks with having more soul, rhythm and coordination.

If I were white and read the Cress Theory and the other scientific information on melanin (such as "Melanin: The Chemical Key to Black Greatness" by Carol Barnes, chemist), I'd not only want to look, act and sound black as many whites already try to do -- I'd want to be black.

SUESAN COLEMAN

Long Beach
