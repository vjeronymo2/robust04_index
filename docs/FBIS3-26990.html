Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto's special envoy, Professor N.D. Khan, has held talks in Beijing with the Chinese foreign minister, Mr. Qian Qichen, and other leaders on human rights abuses by Indian troops in occupied Kashmir. During the talks, he delivered a letter from the prime minister for President Jiang Zemin. Later, talking to APP [Associated Press of Pakistan] before his departure for Tehran, the special envoy said that the letter was very specific on human rights violations and atrocities by the Indian troops in occupied Kashmir, particularly the molestation of women. He said the Kashmir issue was among the top priorities of the present government and is the arch-pivot of Pakistan's foreign policy. He said that Pakistan intended to move a resolution in the third week of this month in Human Rights Commission in Geneva on the situation in occupied Kashmir. It will express concern on the happenings there. He described his talks with Chinese leaders as fruitful and constructive.
