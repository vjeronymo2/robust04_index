BFN [Text] On 8 April, the National Secretariat of the Cuban Workers Federation [CTC] agreed to hold, for the first time, assemblies on economic efficiency, beginning in the second half of May. The first of these assemblies will be held in April and will examine how to fulfill the decision reached by the workers in the workplace assemblies, as well as new problems in the workplaces. The CTC National Secretariat also officially agreed to allocate eight million pesos a year to cover the expenses of the Jose Marti Pioneers Organization [OPJM]. CTC Secretary General Pedro Ross Leal pointed out that the goal of the decision is to reduce Cuba's budget and ensure that children do not suffer the consequences of the special period and are prepared for the future. OPJM Vice President Laritza Diaz applauded the gesture of the CTC, which will cover the expenses of over 300 children's facilities nationwide to benefit the workers' children. The CTC National Secretariat also discussed the results of the plenum that closed the Parliament in the Workplace process, and discussed the accomplishments of a group of workplaces nominated to receive National Emulation Vanguard status.
