Language: French Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Haiti: Aristide in Paris"] [Text] Father Jean-Bertrand Aristide, Haiti's president in exile, was welcomed by President Mitterrand at the Elysee yesterday with all the honors due a chief of state. The talks between the two men lasted 20 minutes. Mr. Aristide said that he was "very satisfied" with the attitude adopted by France, which promised, according to him, that it would "not support" a new plan backed by Washington for the settlement of the crisis in Haiti. This plan provides for the anticipated retirement of General Raoul Cedras, Haitian Armed Forces chief of staff, and the creation of a new government, but does not guarantee the return of President Aristide.
