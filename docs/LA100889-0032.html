October 8, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

BRITISH GET MOST OF THE THEATER MONEY

Regarding Dan Sullivan's article ("A Birthday Wish for Music Center," Sept. 17) calling for a classical theater company to be finally formed at the Music Center, sure why not? But to then advocate a director with strong classical credentials "which therefore may suggest a Brit," only undermines his exciting proposal and shows a total misunderstanding of where the American theater is today.

Sadly our biggest problem in the area of classical theater still seems to be an incredible inferiority complex. Even our own theater producers and professional practitioners (not to even mention critics) find it hard to believe what training, exposure to foreign works and just sheer hard work has accomplished in the last 25 years in this country. Instead, we just keep on handing the ball over to the Brits every time we can, assuming no American directors have the proper cultural "genes" to be first-rate with the classics.

In our own back yard alone, Jack O'Brian of the Old Globe, Des McAnuff of La Jolla, Dan Sullivan (our critic's namesake in Seattle) and Adrian Hall have gained world-class distinction as classical directors, and its time they were recognized as such by our own L.A. critic.

Since time and time again American money has gone to support the Royal Shakespeare Co. and the BBC Shakespeare series, no wonder we can't raise enough interest and money to produce our own classical projects like the one Sullivan proposes.

ROGER HENDRICKS SIMON

Los Angeles
