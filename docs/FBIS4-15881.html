BFN [Text] The joint session of the Council of Defense and Foreign Ministers of the Commonwealth of Independent States will be held in Moscow on April 14, Interfax was told by a spokesman for the staff responsible for coordinating measures and military cooperation within the CIS countries. The session plans to consider the issues regulating the activities of the Council for Collective Security and adopt a declaration about the body. Under consideration will be issues associated with the financing, composition of the staff and functions of the Council. The Collective Security Council was created in 1993 by the decision of the CIS leaders. It is headed by Gennadiy Shabannikov. The Council is participated in by those CIS countries which signed the treaty on collective security: Azerbaijan, Armenia, Belarus, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Russia, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. Moldova, Turkmenistan and Ukraine did not sign the document.
