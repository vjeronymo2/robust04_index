941028 FT 28 OCT 94 / Pyongyang N-costs set out An international consortium will have to spend Dollars 4.5bn (Pounds 2.8bn) to dismantle North Korea's nuclear programme and replace it with new energy supplies and safer light-water reactors, South Korean officials said yesterday. Seoul is expected to support 55 per cent of the cost, with the rest split among the five UN Security Council members, and Japan, Canada, Australia and Germany. Mr Han Sung-joo, South Korean foreign minister, said the project will include Dollars 3.5bn for light-water reactors, Dollars 500m for dismantling North Korea's nuclear reactors, Dollars 300m for interim heavy fuel oil supplies and Dollars 100m to run the consortium, the Korea Development Energy Organisation, in the next decade. South Korea is expected to finance its share from the national budget, with funds raised through sale of government stakes in listed companies or privatisation of state-owned companies.
