940815 FT 15 AUG 94 / Arts: Today's Television It is some indication of the credulousness of the human race that a man should be selected as the subject of a half hour programme because he does not believe in the supernatural. Richard Dawkins, follower of Darwin and author of 'The Selfish Gene' and 'The Blind Watchmaker", is interviewed by Sheena McDonald in The Vision Thing (8.00 C4). It is widely agreed that religious beliefs should be respected and never attacked or ridiculed, but Dawkins is not only an atheist, he also regards religion as downright dangerous; hardly a surprising attitude when you consider the results of religious fervour from Palestine to Pakistan, from Christianity's Inquisition to Islam's fatwah on Salman Rushdie. It sounds as though Frank Stubbs is becoming remarkably politically correct in the last episode of the present series (9.00 ITV). He sets up a campaign to save a young Chinese girl from deportation and, when she is threatened by racists, turns them against the Home Office official. Panorama investigates the way in which people are now obliged to resort to private landlords (9.30 BBC1).
