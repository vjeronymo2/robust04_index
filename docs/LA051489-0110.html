May 14, 1989, Sunday, South Bay Edition

TREATMENT OF ART TEACHER PROTESTED

I am writing to protest the arbitrary action taken by the Palos Verdes Peninsula Unified School District administration against one of our most dedicated and outstanding high school teachers, Chizuko de Queiroz (Times, May 12). She has worked in the school district for 27 years and has been chairperson of the art department for 10 years.

The entire school faculty sent a petition to Dr. Jack Price (district superintendent) and approximately 200 of her students signed another petition demanding to have their colleague or teacher return to the classroom.

The district administration removed her from the classroom due to a medical opinion which is contrary to the teacher's own physician's evaluation and the consistent evaluation of the district's appointed medical doctor for the last five years. The teacher suffered an industrial (on-the-job) injury while teaching in 1983. Since then she has remained teaching with the district where her evaluations remained excellent.

The teacher was removed from the classroom several days after she complained at a school meeting about overcharging her school supplies account. Was she dismissed because she complained that the district purchased art supplies from a retailer rather than a wholesaler at a cost of over $500 more?

This teacher should be returned to the classroom immediately.

BRIGITTE SCHUEGRAF

Rancho Palos Verdes
