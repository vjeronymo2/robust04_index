BFN [Excerpt] Negotiations on autonomy issues not yet concluded, such as elections for the Palestinian council, got under way this morning at the Eretz checkpoint in the Gaza Strip. This is the first time the sides have met for formal talks since the introduction of self-rule in Gaza and Jericho. Jack Katzenell reports: [Begin recording] [Katzenell] The talks ended a short while ago. Major General Rothschild came out, and he said there would be another meeting next week. He said he had not engaged in negotiations proper, merely preparations, discussions of a wide range of subjects, problems that had cropped up, and things that had to be discussed. I asked him if there was any truth in the report that Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin was unwilling to proceed to the next stage of the autonomy until the Palestinians had proven themselves in the area of civilian administration. [Rothschild] Well, the fact that I am here, dealing with Dr. Nabil Sha'th on issues of the continuation of the peace process, shows you that Israel will do whatever is needed as far as the DOP [Declaration of Principles] is concerned. [Katzenell] When the group convened this morning, Minister of the Environment Yosi Sarid was also present. His meeting with Nabil Sha'th was more like a reunion of old friends. When Sarid came out, he was asked by a reporter when Nabil Sha'th would be visiting Jerusalem: [Sarid] There is no problem. Jerusalem is a free city, open city, and the only problem is to coordinate Dr. Nabil Sha'th's visit in Jerusalem. The problem is in coordination. "In response to a question on whether Maj. Gen. Amnon Shahaq has ended his role as head of the Israeli delegation, Baraq said: Do not worry. Gen. The composition of the delegation and the way it will be managed will be determined by the government, along with the details as to what will be discussed and when. Gen.
