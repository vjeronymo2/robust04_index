BFN [Unattributed article: "Voter Survey on June 12 Elections"] [Text] Rome 6 May (ANSA) -- Forza Italia, National Alliance [AN] and two left-wing opposition parties, the Democratic Party of the Left [PDS] and Communist Refoundation [Communist Renewal], will all gain votes in the June 12 European Parliament elections compared to the March 27 general elections, according to a CIRM [Italian Center for Market Research] survey released Friday [6 May]. The poll results, to be published in the next issue of the news weekly, L'ESPRESSO, show that Silvio Berlusconi's Forza Italia party would obtain 24.2 percent of the vote, compared to 21.1 percent in March, while its neo-Fascist government partner, National Alliance, would increase its votes by 1.1 percent. The Northern League on the other hand would drop 1.1 percent compared to the previous elections, CIRM said. The Left democrats would increase their votes by 1.4 percent and Communist Refoundation by 0.9 percent, according to CIRM, which did not say how it was able to separate the parties, both right and left, which in the March elections were running under the same ticket. The opposition Greens would remain stable while the Pact for Italy would lose 0.8 percent and the former Christian Democrats of the People's Party would lose 0.5 percent. The Pannella Ticket, too, would drop by 1.5 percent.
