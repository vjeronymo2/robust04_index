Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By reporters Zou Aiguo (6760 1947 0948) and Sun Yong (1327 0516)] [Text] Beijing, 15 Mar (XINHUA) -- The second session of the Eighth Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [CPPCC] National Committee held a general meeting to hear speeches by members today. In the morning, members attended the second plenary meeting at the Great Hall of the People to hear suggestions and opinions of six members concerning major government principles and policies. Today's executive chairmen were Hong Xuezhi, Dong Yinchu, and Ma Man-kei. Wei Jianxing, member of the Political Bureau and the CPC Central Committee Secretariat and Central Disciplinary Inspection Commission secretary; Luo Gan, state councillor and State Council secretary general; and some other senior officials of State Council ministries and commissions attended the meeting on invitation. Zhou Shaozheng, chairman of the CPPCC National Committee's Motions Committee, made a report on the committee's handling of motions since the first session of the Eighth CPPCC National Committee was convened. After reviewing the motions handling work in the past year, he said: This year the Motions Committee will focus attention on work in the following fields: to actively support CPPCC members, democratic parties, and mass organizations concerned with putting forward more and better motions for implementing the "decision" adopted by the Third Plenary Session of the 14th CPC Central Committee, and to coordinate, serve, and publicize the handling of motions; to bring into play the committee's advantages in organizing special investigations, discussions, consultations, and inspections to facilitate the handling of key motions; to assist units concerned in giving priority to handling motions made by democratic parties and mass organizations and to sum up the handling of such motions in good time for continuous improvement of motions handling work; and to prepare for the convening of forums for the Motions Committee's work, and sum up and exchange experiences in handling motions in recent years for further improving the work. Then, members took the floor. On behalf of the China Democratic National Construction Association Central Committee, Wan Guoquan proposed ways for deepening reform of state enterprises and setting up a modern enterprise system; Gu Shengzu discussed ways for effectively guiding the "tide of migrants"; Fang Jiade discussed, on behalf of trade union organizations, the need to rely on the working class wholeheartedly in the establishment of a modern enterprise system; Guo Guoqing (also speaking on behalf of members Zhang Jiyu and Huang Jingjun) proposed bringing into full play the role of special supervisors, inspectors, auditors and educational inspectors, and other discipline enforcement workers; Li Hongchang suggested that chambers of commerce be incorporated into the package reform program; and Wang Chuanlun raised proposals for intensifying the anticorruption drive. Vice chairmen of the CPPCC National Committee who attended the meeting today were Ye Xuanping, Wu Xueqian, Yang Rudai, Wang Zhaoguo, Zhao Puchu, Qian Weichang, Hu Sheng, Qian Zhengying, Ding Guangxun, Sun Fuling, Ann Tse-kai, and Henry Fok Ying-tung.
