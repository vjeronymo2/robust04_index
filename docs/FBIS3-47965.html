Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 17 KYODO -- A majority of corporate executives and heads of local entities favor cable transmission for broadcasting in the 21st century when an optical fiber telecommunications system is completed, the Postal Ministry said Monday [17 January]. The Posts and Telecommunications Ministry said the survey also showed that those polled called for allocation of radio frequencies for mobile telecommunications networks. The ministry said the report was based on interviews with 372 heads of local governments and corporate executives, as well as academics, from last October to the year-end. About 90 percent of the pollees called for emphasis to be placed on the consolidation of telecommunications networks in developing the social infrastructure in the 21st century, the ministry said. A majority of the pollees called for cable transmission to be set aside for broadcasting, while allotting radio frequencies to the mobile telecommunications network, the ministry said. The results of the survey were reported to the Telecommunications Council, an advisory organ in the ministry.
