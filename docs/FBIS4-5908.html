BFN [Text] Iraq has recently notified South Korea that it will temporarily close down its embassy in Seoul, citing poor economic conditions in the country caused by a long-standing U.N. trade embargo. In the notification delivered to the Foreign Ministry April 6, Baghdad hoped that despite the measure, "the friendly relations" between the two countries would be strengthened, said a ministry official. He said the Iraqi government also promised to reopen its embassy here as soon as its economic conditions improve. The Iraqi Embassy housed in a two-story building in Tongbinggo- tong, central Seoul, will be actually shut down late next month, said the official. South Korea and Iraq established formal diplomatic ties in July 1989, upgrading the consulate-general in each others capital to an embassy. The Seoul government, however, withdrew embassy staff, including the ambassador, from Baghdad in January 1991 when tensions were mounting in the Gulf region after the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait the previous year. South Korean diplomats so far have not returned to Baghdad. The ministry official said a South Korean diplomat who is working as acting ambassador to Iraq is now stationed in the Jordanian capital of Amman. Since setting up diplomatic ties in 1989, Iraq has not assigned an ambassador to Seoul and has been represented by a charge d'affaires. While Iraq cited its economic predicament as the reason for its decision to close down the embassy in Seoul, some diplomatic observers here suspect the decision may reflect Iraq's dissatisfaction with Seoul for not reopening its embassy in Baghdad. When reached by phone, Taha H. Mohammed, Iraqi charge d'affaires, rebuffed the speculation. He emphasized the decision was from a purely economic motivation. "We'll be ready to come back and open our embassy again (when the Iraqi economy gets better)," he said. "We have found Korean people and companies cooperative with Iraq and I hope good relationship will still be maintained between the two countries," he added. (KKH)
