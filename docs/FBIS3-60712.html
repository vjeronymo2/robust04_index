Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Bakina] [Text] Moscow December 10 TASS -- The ``Kurchatov Institute'' Russian scientific center has accomplished a draft of a new system of control over nuclear materials, Academician Nikolay Ponomarev-Stepnoy, vice-president of the center, told a news conference here today. ``The law enforcement bodies of Russia have registered some cases of misappropriations of atomic materials lately,'' the researcher said. ``Although no such facts have been recorded directly at the `Kurchatov Institute,' the situation is very dangerous. We consider it necessary to strengthen control over nuclear materials, and a new draft of the control system has been worked out exactly for this purpose,'' the academician noted. According to Aleksandr Rumyantsev, one of the main think tanks of the draft system, at present control over nuclear materials is based on data from the institute's accounts department. In the future the emphasis will be made on the results of physical measurements of materials which will allow to know a specific situation practically every week. At the same time the administration of the scientific center intends to take stock of nuclear products on a regular basis. The draft of a new control system has been prepared together with specialists from the American Technical Institute (U.S.A.). Researchers from the ``Kurchatov Institute'' find it rather important that for the first time the Russian state scientific center has been closely cooperating with the U.S. private scientific establishment.
