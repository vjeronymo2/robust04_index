BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] Moscow May 25 TASS -- Acting Ukrainian Prime Minister Yukhym Zvyahilskyy intends to convey a message by the Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk to the Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin, pertaining to problems of the division of the Black Sea Fleet, ITAR-TASS learned from well-informed sources which follow the talks on the Black Sea Fleet between the Ukrainian and Russian leaders which are underway in Moscow. The intregovernment talks which have been going on for three days have come to a deadlock. Today, the talks continue at the level of working groups of experts coordinating principles of the division of the Black Sea Fleet, its infrastructure and the separation of national naval bases. Due to the fact that by 15:00 p.m. the sides had failed to reach a unanimous decision on a number of principally important problems, in particular, on naval bases in Sevastopol. [sentence as received] It was decided to finalize these problems at a separate meeting. "Nevertheless, we are prepared to continue talks as long as necessary," said leader of the Ukrainian delegation to the talks, Yukhym Zvyahilskyy. Russian special envoy Yuriy Dubinin told journalists, commenting on the situation which developed at the talks, that there is a package of documents on the Black Sea Fleet signed by the Russian and Ukrainian presidents. In principle, the package contains all provisions which might decide the fate of the Black Sea Fleet. "We are attending to matters of translating adopted decisions into life," Dubinin said. X X X It has just been reported that Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin met with acting Ukrainian Prime Minister Yukhym Zvyahilskyy in the framework of intergovernment talks on the Black Sea Fleet. Zvyahilskyy handed over a message from the Ukrainian president to the Russian prime minister. Experts belonging to the delegation of the two countries continue work at problems which caused controversy at the talks, in particular, these problems pertain to the separation of national naval bases, ITAR-TASS learned from sources close to the talks. The Russian side acquainted its Ukrainian colleagues with four variants of the Black Sea Fleet division, including its ships, infrastructure and separate naval bases. It was reported that another reserve "presidential" variant was prepared.
