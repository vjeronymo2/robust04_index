Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 14 KYODO -- Japan welcomes the outcome of the summit talks between U.S. President Bill Clinton and Russian President Boris Yeltsin, Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata said Friday [14 Janaury]. Yeltsin pledged to press on with democratic reforms in Russia while Clinton reconfirmed continued support for the Russian efforts, Hata told a news conference. Japan will also continue to back Russian reform efforts in cooperation with other Group of Seven (G-7) industrialized nations, Hata said. He also gave high marks to the scheduled signing of an agreement among the U.S., Russia and Ukraine which is expected to pave the way for Kiev to scrap nuclear missiles, saying the accord would contribute to the world's security and that Japan will continue to cooperate in it. Clinton and Yeltsin are due to sign the historic accord with Ukrainian leader Leonid Kravchuk later in the day.
