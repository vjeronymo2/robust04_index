Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Banja Luka, 4 Jan (TANJUG) -- A joint meeting of the radio stations of Banja Luka, Semberija and Majevica (Bijeljina) and Sarajevo -- the previous channel S, broadcast from Pale -- was held in Banja Luka today in connection with joint programming for a radio station in the republic of Srpska and the republic of Serb Krajina. The joint programming will start on 17 January and implies joint broadcasts every working day between 0800 GMT and 1200 GMT. Between these times each studio -- Banja Luka, Bijeljina, Knin and Sarajevo (Pale) -- will produce one hour of programs. The news and current affairs programs will be joint programs because the news -- at noon and 1500 -- will go out from the Sarajevo (Pale) studio, while the 1900 cast will be broadcast from Banja Luka. However, the Banja Luka studio will broadcast a news bulletin at 1500 on its second program, and it will have its own daily news program at 1600. Joint weekend broadcasts begin on 14 January. They will begin at noon on Saturday. They will end at 0600 the following day, and the studios will be changed in accordance with an established timetable. In addition to establishing common programs, principles amd ways of exchanging information were established, as were other forms of joint work in the unified RS and RSK radio. The meeting was also to have been attended by representatives of Serb Radio Knin, but they will be informed by letter of the agreement on joint programming.
