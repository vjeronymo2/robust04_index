Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 24 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and the United States held working-level coordination sessions on the North Korean nuclear issue over the weekend as Pyongyang and the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) are engaged in inspection negotiations. Tom Hubbard, deputy assistant secretary of State and top U.S. representative to contacts with North Korea, flew into Seoul on Sunday and met with Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu and other South Korean working-level officials. Hubbard accompanied Treasury Secretary Lloyd Bentsen on a leg of his Asian trip and flew in separately to Seoul to consult on the nuclear issue, Ministry officials said. He leaves for Washington on Monday afternoon. Pyongyang-IAEA negotiations on resuming inspections are dragging on beyond expectations and the two sides are likely to meet again sometime soon. If these talks go well and Pyongyang reopens inter-Korean dialogue, the United States is expected to hold high-level talks with North Korea for comprehensive discussions, including relations improvement.
