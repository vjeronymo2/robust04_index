Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 25 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item: Lagoven, a subsidiary of Petroven, announced that output at the Bachaquero Sur-Oeste oil field increased to 3,100 barrels per day after four new wells were opened in the Lake Maracaibo area, Zulia Department. The Lagoven engineering department disclosed that the new wells had increased production by 2,300 barrels per day in 1993. In 1992, Lagoven discovered crude oil that rated 45 API, which in its test phase incorporated 18 million barrels of reserve petroleum. The company intends to drill seven new wells this year. (Caracas EL GLOBO in Spanish 23 Jan 94 p 11) Petroven announced a $0.50-per barrel increase in export crude as well as other products, thus raising the price of export crude to $12.20 per barrel. Petroven management explained that the price increase was due to an increase in demand brought about by colder weather in the United States and Europe. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 18 Jan 94 p E-5) The Finance Ministry Public Finances Department reported that Venezuela canceled $2.685 billion in foreign debt service during fiscal year 1993. Of the total amount paid as part of the country's foreign debt services, $2.11130 billion, or 78.62 percent, corresponds to unstructured debt, while $1.05868 billion, or 39.42 percent, corresponds to restructured debt. The report also discloses that capital payments totalled $1.176 billion, while interest payments amounted to $1.508 billion. Foreign debt balances during 1993 totaled $27.296 billion as opposed to $27.08290 billion during 1992. (Caracas EL GLOBO in Spanish 17 Jan 94 p 13) Lagoven announced that crude oil and by-product exports increased to 797,000 barrels per day in 1993. According to Daniel Ramirez, manager of Lagoven Commerce and Supply Department, the company was able to increase its exports by 37,000 barrels per day compared to 1992 exports. On the other hand, export revenues fell during 1993 due to excess supply as a result of OPEC and North Sea overproduction and greater than expected exports to Russia. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 0029 GMT 18 Jan 94)
