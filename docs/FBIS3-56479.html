Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Speaking on national TV, Ukraine's Foreign Minister, Anatoliy Zlenko, said that "the document signed in Moscow is not subject to ratification by Ukraine's parliament." Under the bill on international agreements adopted by Ukraine's parliament recently, such statements are not subject to the process of ratification, he pointed out. The document signed by the Russian, American and Ukrainian presidents in Moscow "serves to develop the resolution on the ratification of START-1 adopted by the Ukrainian parliament, Zlenko said. According to him, no nuclear country recognized the way Ukraine's parliament ratified START-1. "International isolation could bring Ukraine to its knees. Its independence would be out of the question," the foreign minister said. Earlier a number of influential members of Ukraine's parliament strongly criticized the trilateral document on Ukraine's nuclear disarmament signed in Moscow.
