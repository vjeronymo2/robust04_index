Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 16 -- Prime Minister Chuan Likphai and his party called on Communist Party of Vietnam General Secretary Do Muoi here this afternoon. During their meeting, the two leaders informed each other of the developments in their countries and noted with pleasure that the Vietnamese-Thai friendly and cooperative ties were being developed favourably and that the recent exchange of visits of high-level delegations of the two countries ushered in a new chapter in their bilateral relations. Party Secretary Do Muoi affirmed that Vietnam would continue its efforts to strengthen and develop the friendly, neighboury and cooperative relations with Thailand in the interests of the two peoples, and for peace, stability and development in Southeast Asia and the world as a whole. Prime Minister Chuan Likphai, for his part, expressed his desire to further promote the cooperation between Thailand and Vietnam by strictly implementing the agreements reached by the two governments and going on with discussions to solve remaining issues in the spirit of friendship, constructive cooperation and mutual benefits. On this occasion, General Sectary Do Muoi asked P.M. Chuan Likphai to convey his best wishes to the king and the whole royal family of Thailand.
