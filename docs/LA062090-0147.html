June 20, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

TAIWAN STOCKS AT 17-MONTH LOW

Taiwan's battered stock market hit a 17-month low today as share prices plunged in what analysts called a crisis of confidence.

Dealers said the market had breached a key support level and was in danger of going into a free fall.

"It's a confidence crash," said Matthew Wang of Excelsior Securities. "We can hardly see any support unless investors have confidence."
