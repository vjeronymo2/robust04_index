BFN [Text] [Jordanian] Interior Minister Salamah Hammad met with his Iraqi counterpart Watban Ibrahim in Baghdad yesterday. The IRAQI NEWS AGENCY quoted Ibrahim as hailing Jordan's position on Iraq during and after the Gulf war. AKHIR KHABAR has learned that Hammad's visit to Baghdad follows several diplomatic and political contacts relating to Jordanian citizens in Iraq. It also learned that Hammad is carrying a special security file on activating security agreements signed previously in Amman and Baghdad. The file also deals with the recent increase in cases against Jordanian citizens. AKHIR KHABAR also learned that the interior minister's talks in Baghdad deal with increasing bilateral coordination, and reaching an agreement on a mechanism to control illegal trade across the Jordanian-Iraqi border, especially following an increase in smuggling operations. Hammad's visit also aims at fostering bilateral security coordination. Observers noted that Hammad is the first official to visit Iraq after the latest government changes, according to which President Saddam Husayn assumed the post of prime minister in order to launch the battle to lift the siege imposed on Iraq.
