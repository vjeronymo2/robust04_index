940402 FT 02 APR 94 / UK Company News: De-listing fear for London Securities London Securities, the property investment company, has warned that if ongoing discussions for the completion of a property purchase are not completed by the end of this month the company would be threatened with de-listing. London Securities completed a company voluntary arrangement in 1992, whereby its previous assets were put under the control of its secured lenders, leaving the company as a cash shell. It is considering the purchase of properties from its Nu-Swift, the fire protection and property group which with a 29 per cent stake is the company's largest shareholder. It yesterday announced a pre-tax loss of Pounds 342,000 for the year to September 30 1993, reflecting the absence of operating businesses. Losses per share were 0.6p.
