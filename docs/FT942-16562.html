940409 FT 09 APR 94 / UK Company News: Torday & Carlisle cuts loss Torday & Carlisle, the Newcastle-based engineer, cut its pre-tax loss from Pounds 15.1m to Pounds 292,000 for 1993. The loss was struck after an exceptional profit of Pounds 3.02m on the disposal of Elfab Hughes, which makes pressure relief and safety equipment, and exceptional costs of Pounds 2.96m (Pounds 5.28m). The loss for 1992 included a provision of Pounds 8.21m against goodwill at Oldham Signs. Elfab contributed operating profits of Pounds 544,000 for the 11 months before the sale. Operating profits from continuing businesses were Pounds 35,000, compared with a previous loss of Pounds 1.45m. Turnover from continuing businesses fell from Pounds 34.1m to Pounds 28.8m. Total group turnover was Pounds 32.7m (Pounds 38.9m). The exceptional costs include Pounds 963,000 for environmental controls in the Netherlands. Borrowings were down from Pounds 7.77m to Pounds 5.2m, giving gearing of 73 per cent (109 per cent). Earnings were 0.2p against losses of 93p.
