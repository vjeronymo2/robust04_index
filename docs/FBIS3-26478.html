Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [From the "Provincial News Hookup" program] [Text] Village and town industries in our province again made substantial headway in 1993, generating more than 400 billion yuan in total output value and marketing more than 90 percent of their products. They held leading positions among prefectures and cities nationwide. According to special staff correspondent (Yuan Yahe), the total output value of village and town industries in Suzhou, Wuxi, Changzhou, and Zhenjiang Cities surpassed 3 billion yuan in 1993, representing (?53) percent of that of similar industries in the province. Wuxi County, Jiangyin City, Wujin City, Zhangjiagang City, and Changshu City, known as the five tigers in the southeast, all achieved more than 20 billion yuan in output value; this worked out to more than 150 billion yuan in total output value, which was the highest in southeast Jiangsu. In 1993, our province formally began to implement the shareholding and stock cooperative systems in village and town enterprises. By the end of November, there were more than 2,600 experimental enterprises across the province, with shares totaling 4.7 billion yuan. Eight new enterprise groups were formed, with 5 billion yuan in total fixed assets, or 10 percent of the total assets of village and town enterprises across the province. Last year saw the fastest economic development for our province's village and town enterprises, which achieved more than (?55 billion yuan) in sales of export goods, or approximately 30 percent of total sales by village and town enterprises across the country. More than 4,200 new wholly or partly foreign-owned enterprises were set up, bringing the total number of such enterprises to 10,000. On average, one out of every 10 village and town enterprises in the province was a joint venture, and the total amount of contracted foreign capital equaled $4 billion.
