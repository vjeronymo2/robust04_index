Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Taba, 24 Jan (MENA)--In Taba this morning Palestinian and Israeli negotiators began a third week of talks in connection with the Gaza-Jericho agreement. The negotiations resumed at 1030 when the Civilian Affairs Committee stemming from the Gaza-Jericho Committee met under lawyer Jamil al-Tarifi on the Palestinian side and Gadi Zohar, the Israeli official in charge of the civil administration in the occupied West Bank. Al-Tarifi told MENA that the committee will discuss issues related to the transfer of civilian authority from Israel to the Palestinians. He said that this week's negotiations will focus on communications -- including radio, television, postal services, electricity -- and the Palestinian authority's right to enact legislation and handle town planning, civil registration, and travel documents. He added that the issue of water will be discussed not this week but next week. MENA learned that Radwan Abu-'Ayyash, head of the radio and television corporation, and Engineer Amin 'Ulayyan have joined the Palestinian delegation negotiating the transfer of civilian authority because the setting up of a Palestinian radio and television station will be discussed. The Palestinian negotiator stressed that there was progress during the past two weeks of negotiations on civilian issues when the two sides reached agreement on issuing identity cards and issues of residence, protected nature reserves, and public works.
