Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] At 1230 today, His Excellency President Hafiz al-Asad received Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias, current chairman of the European Union [EU], and the delegation accompanying him. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' and the Greek ambassador attended. Papoulias handed a letter to the president from Greek Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou dealing with the peace process and bilateral relations. Papoulias expressed the condolences of the EU Council of Ministers on the death of Staff Major Basil al-Asad. Talks then centered on the peace process in the region, as well as the EU delegation's mission on its current tour. President al-Asad stressed Syria's support for the peace process despite Israel's unserious stand on the peace process and despite doubts about Israel's seriousness. The president also recalled that Syria has repeatedly called for an active European role to help the peace process achieve progress. The EU delegation, which was headed by Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias, current chairman of the EU, and which included EU Commissioner General Hans Van den Broek, left Damascus this afternoon after a two-day visit to Syria. They was seen off at Damascus International Airport by Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar', Assistant Foreign Minister Yusuf Shakkur, several senior Foreign Ministry officials, and the Greek ambassador in Damascus and the embassy staff. In a statement to SANA correspondent, Papoulias said: My visit to Syria was very constructive and positive. I met with President Hafiz al-Asad in my capacity as chairman of the EU Council of Ministers. I also met with Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam and Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar'. A cordial and warm atmosphere prevailed in all these meetings. He added: At these meetings we discussed a series of issues, foremost of which is the situation in the region, the possibility of promoting the peace process, and EU- Syrian relations. Papoulias also said: In my capacity as Greece's foreign minister, we discussed bilateral relations. The results of these talks were very good, and I will convey the concepts and views of the Syrian leaders to my colleagues, the foreign ministers of the EU member states. All these views are very important and will help us make decisions at the EU regarding future steps we will take.
