December 20, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: CHER HAS HER EYE ON A REVEALING ROLE: GYPSY ROSE LEE

THE FABULOUS Cher won an Oscar for "Moonstruck," but she still has a child's delight in taking on roles that afford the chance to dress up and carry on like a mad thing. (Catch Cher in "Mermaids," and you'll see what I mean.)

But now the star has optioned a work that will afford the opportunity to "undress" and carry on. Cher is interested in portraying Gypsy Rose Lee, the legendary stripper of the '40s. No, she won't do a remake of the musical "Gypsy," but Cher has her eye on the Eric Preminger book "Gypsy and Me," a true-to-life, Auntie Mameish-type tale of a boy and his mother.

Eric is the son of the late Gypsy and movie director Otto Preminger. He was born out of wedlock when such a detail could kill a career.

Nobody has ever done total justice to the totality that grew to be Gypsy Rose Lee -- stripper, philosopher, socialite, writer, painter, actress and sister to another great talent, June Havoc. This is a role Cher should really be able to sink into -- up to her hips.

KIM BASINGER doesn't have warm and cuddly memories of making "Batman." She tells Lawrence Grobel in Movieline that "Jack Nicholson made money. Peters-Guber, they made money. And Warner Bros., of course, made money. But none of the rest of us made anything. We got raped; everybody did. It was a rape deal; it really was."

Kim says she wasn't just another pretty face in "Batman." She claims to have made contributions to the script: "I saw what was missing from 'Batman.' I saw the reconstruction work that should be done . . . so I just rewrote a lot of this crap." (Take that, screenwriters Sam Hamm and Warren Skaaren.)

Kim isn't entirely dour and serious in this interview. She talks of her sexy public appearances and how she selects what she wears -- low-cut, transparent, split up the front items. "I dress mostly for me. And I find myself dressing for the fans, because they have fun. What does the word movie STAR mean, anyway? It's not a medical student. It's a movie star, right?"
