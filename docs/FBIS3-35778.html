Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The first day of Russian-Kazakh talks on the future of the Baykonur space vehicle launching complex was constructive and revealed no serious disagreements, said one of the negotiators, an official from the Russian Space Agency. He told Interfax that, on Monday, the negotiators discussed a draft for an agreement between Russia and Kazakhstan on the status and use of Baykonur and the town of Leninsk. Lease conditions and whether Russian or Kazakh legislation is to apply to the leased facilities would be discussed within the next two days, the official said. He said the negotiators were only dealing with documents drawn up by Russian experts. The Kazakh side "has not yet stated its official position on the documents that have been proposed" and would be consulting Almaty on Tuesday morning, he said.
