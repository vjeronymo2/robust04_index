Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Leonid Vinogradov and Andrey Shitov] [Text] Vladivostok March 14 TASS -- Russia and America acted as equal partners at the talks in Vladivostok and discussed many aspects of international relations. The two countries agreed to team up and work together on the problem of nuclear non-proliferation. The announcement was made at a news conference after the talks between Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher in Vladivostok on Monday. "We are partners and will continue to work in this direction," Kozyrev said. Christopher said that despite the age difference he and Kozyrev are doing one work. Speaking about Bosnia, both diplomats expressed satisfaction with the achieved results, including the compromise between the warring factions. Also discussed was the possibility of transforming G-7 into G-8. Kozyrev and Christopher said that relations between the two countries have passed the test of strength lately. They did not discuss the spy scandal involving a high-ranking CIA official, James Aldrich. Concerning the withdrawal of Russian troops from the Baltic states, Kozyrev said Russia stands for their soonest possible pullout and has nothing to do with the attempts of the Baltic republics to deport the Russian troops. He said Russia will take part in COCOM. Asked about the situation in the Sea of Okhotsk, the Russian foreign minister said that Russia calls for the preservation of fish resources in the sea. There has been understanding on the part of the United States. In the future both countries plan to discuss this issue, he said. After the talks, Christopher left for the U.S. Kozyrev flew to Dushanbe, the capital of Tajikistan, to attend a meeting of the foreign ministers of Russia, Tajikistan, Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan, and representatives of Kyrgyzstan and Turkmenistan. The participants are expected to be received by Tajik President Emomali Rakhmanov. During the visit to Tajikistan, Kozyrev will take a trip to the Tajik-Afghan border.
