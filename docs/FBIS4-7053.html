BFN [Text] Speaking in an interview with newsmen at Government House this morning, Defense Minister General Wichit Sukmak said the Defense and Foreign Ministries are working together in compiling data on Cambodian intrusions, spillover fighting, and casualties suffered by Thai people living along the Cambodian border. Asked whether the military would hand over directly to the United Nations the evidence collected by Thailand, Gen. Wichit said the armed forces would let the Foreign Ministry do that by taking into consideration the impact on Thai-Cambodian relations. [Begin recording] [Wichit] The Foreign Ministry will decide which information can be used and whether the political situation is appropriate for us to make a protest -- whether the good relations would be affected. The Foreign Ministry will decide about that, but we are ready to provide it with facts. [Unidentified reporter] To improve the situation, do you think the supreme commander should make a visit there? [Wichit] We must try to preserve and promote relations between our two countries since we are neighbors. That's what we should do. Especially the military, we must try promote good relations. There is fighting in Cambodia, and we must work together in order to prevent spillover into Thailand. [end recording]
