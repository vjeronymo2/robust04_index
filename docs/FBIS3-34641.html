Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Taba, 13 Feb (MENA)--Sources in the Palestinian delegation to the Taba negotiations have stated that, at its meeting tomorrow, the Security Affairs Committee will discuss the details of the formation of the Palestinian police force in Gaza and Jericho. The sources noted that the Palestinian side demands that this force be made up of 9,000 troops, while the Israeli side demands that it consist of 6,000 troops, including the civil defense force as well as the intelligence and public security services. The sources added that the Civilian Affairs Committee, which is led by Attorney Jamil al-Tarifi on the Palestinian side, and by Gadi Zohar, head of the West Bank's Civil Administration, on the Israeli side, will resume its discussions concerning the transfer of powers to the Palestinians in the fields of water, civil planning, antiquities, electricity, communications, and land registration. The Palestinian sources said that the Confidence-Building Committee, which stemmed from the Taba negotiations, will discuss the amnesty plan, which will include the release of all the Palestinian detainees in Israel at a date not later than July. The sources said that the number of Palestinian detainees is 9,000, following the Israeli authorities' recent freeing of some 2,000 Palestinian detainees. The current round of the Taba negotiations is expected to last until Thursday [17 February] night.
