Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A team of NATO officials sent to determine to what extent Estonia was prepared to join the Partnership for Peace program has ended its visit there. Estonian Defense Minister Indrek Kannik said at a news conference Thursday that the mission members discussed with Estonian officials ways to step up the country's cooperation with NATO, in particular the setting up of a consulting mechanism which would work in crisis situations, joint military exercises and training of Estonian officers. They also discussed what Estonia could offer NATO. Hermann von Richthoff, the mission head, emphasized that Partnership for Peace was not hostile to any country. He said that joint exercises with NATO could be staged in Estonia in 1993 [as received]. General K.-T. Steinkopf, a mission member, said that NATO could not sell weapons to countries that were not its members but Baltic states could buy them from NATO members in bilateral deals. The NATO team has left for Latvia from where it will go on to Ukraine and Moldova.
