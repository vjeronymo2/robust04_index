Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Colombian elections scheduled for 8 May 1994; source follows in parentheses after each item. According to a poll by EL TIEMPO and the National Advisory Center (CNC) conducted from 6 to 10 February in 22 cities, Liberal Party (PL) candidate hopeful Ernesto Samper Pizano is ahead with 39 percent of the votes while Andres Pastrana Arango, of the New Democratic Force (NDF), follows with 32 percent. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 20 Feb 94 pp 1a, 6a) Samper Pizano has proposed all political parties, social movements, and candidates running for office should "join in an antiviolence crusade." He added the flare-up of violence is aimed at "frightening the country and preventing the 13 March elections." (Santa Fe de Bogota EL NUEVO SIGLO in Spanish 23 Feb 94 p 7a) Both Pastrana Arango and Samper Pizano continue to campaign in Antioquia and Cauca Departments, explaining their economic and social proposals. Both candidates are fighting for women's vote as they announced programs to give women a more active role in society and to protect children in the poorer areas. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL NUEVO SIGLO in Spanish 25 Feb 94 p 7a) Senator Regina Betancur de Liska, presidential candidate from the Metapolitical Unitary Movement, began yesterday a tour of the country in Leticia, Amazonas Department. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL NUEVO SIGLO in Spanish 24 Feb 94 p 6a) Meanwhile, Humberto de la Calle Lombana, PL candidate hopeful, has said there is no way to keep nonparty members from voting in the PL party convention slated for 13 March. When asked if he would accept being the vice president running mate for Pastrano Arango, de la Calle Lombana answered: "I believe I am going to become the president of the Republic, and I am not hoping for any consolation prizes." (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 3 Mar 94)
