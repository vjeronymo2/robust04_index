Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 12 Jan (EFE) -- The clandestine Social Liberation Party, unknown until today, has threatened to kill five human rights organization leaders for not supporting the struggle of the Zapatist National Liberation Army (EZLN). LA JORNADA newspaper reported today that it had received an anonymous telephone call, informing it of the existence of a message concerning the armed conflict in the southeastern state of Chiapas. The newspaper added that the message was later found on a utility pole on a Mexico City street. The five threatened leaders belong to nongovernmental organizations and, according to the communique, are accused of "not supporting the armed struggle movement in Chiapas, and, in turn, they have betrayed the ideals of many struggling groups." The threatened leaders are: Sergio Aguayo, president of the Mexican Human Rights Academy; Juan Carlos Martinez, coordinator of the Fray Francisco de Victoria Human Rights Center; Alejandro Camarillo, president of the Mexican Human Rights Commission; Fernando Ruiz Canales, president of the Law and Human Rights Council; and Victor Clark Alfaro, director of the Binational Human Rights Center.
