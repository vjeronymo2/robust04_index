Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Bad Economic Situation"] [Text] The economic system in Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] is not functioning completely, and the parts of it that do function are faced with conditions worse than last year's. These are the global evaluations of the Macedonian Chamber of Commerce, which is following the realization of the 1994 stabilization program of the government. The situation in certain segments, as for example in the monetary policy, is even worse than last year and threaten to prove disastrous for the economy. The companies, besides on the Greek embargo and reduced foreign markets, also bitterly criticized the domestic factors smothering the realization of the stabilization program. They point out the low percentage of using of the available capacities, the heavy burden of public expenditures, the low accumulative capacity of the economy, and the unemployment. The January break of the planned 8 percent to even up to [passage indistinct] end of the year is realistic. Therefore, they appealed to the bankers not to introduce a rapid increase of the interest rates. The restrictive monetary policy threatens to grow into a real war between the banks and the companies, since it gives little opportunities for the weakened Macedonian economy. The companies claim the interest rates are not in the function of stabilizing and stimulating the production. Additional problems are also the delayed privatization process, the too large exposure of the domestic market to liberalization, etc. In such conditions it is hard to expect that the economy will function property, and a co-operation between the proposer -- the Government and [passage indistinct].
