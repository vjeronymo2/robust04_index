BFN [Lutfi Shatarah report: "Sharp Differences Between Salih and His Military Commanders Over Appointment of 'Abd-Rabbuh Mansur as Defense Minister"] [Excerpts] London -- Two weeks after the outbreak of battles in Yemen between the northern and southern regular forces, serious indications have appeared on the ground supporting the southern leaders' claims that the war is not between the "legitimate forces" and "a group of mutineers from the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP]," as claimed by Sanaa, but rather that it is a fierce war between northern forces supported by the militias of the Yemeni Reform Grouping led by Shaykh 'Abdallah Bin-Husayn al-Ahmar, elements of the outlawed Jihad Organization, Afghan Arab groups from Sudan and other Arab states, and Iraqi military experts on the one hand, and southern forces on the other. The southern resistance is continuing against the northern forces trying to breach southern defenses in several areas with the aim of putting pressure on Aden to force the YSP leaders to leave the country or surrender, that being the view of President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih who has conveyed to the Arab League delegation his rejection of any cease-fire initiatives. [passage omitted] On the political level, it has been revealed that sharp differences have emerged between President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and the northern military commanders over the appointment of 'Abd-Rabbuh Mansur Hadi, a supporter of former south Yemeni President 'Ali Nasir Muhammad, as defense minister. Sources have confirmed that Colonel 'Ali Muhsin al-Ahmar, President Salih's half-brother, rejected that decision and that a number of northern military commanders maintained that as they were fighting southern regular forces, they would not accept a southern officer's assuming responsibility for the northern forces, whatever his political position against the southern leaders. The sources said that President Salih proposed a compromise solution to prevent a military mutiny in the current circumstances, and he agreed to freeze the new minister's powers until after the war and after settlement of the porblem, but some northern commanders are pressing for the appointment of 'Ali Muhsin al-Ahmar as defense minister. [passage omitted] Meanwhile, Muhsin Muhammad Bin-Farid, leader of the Sons of Yemen League, has stated that the aim of the announcement of the declaration of principles initiative signed the day before yesterday by the YSP and the opposition's National Bloc was to stop the fighting, form a government of national salvation, and thoroughly investigate who caused the fighting. He warned that the opposition would stand against any side which did not respond to the initiative -- in a hint to the leaders of the General People's Congress [GPC] and the Yemeni Reform Grouping. He added that Sanaa's rejection of the initiative left no alternative but the mobilization of all military, human, and moral resources to make Sanaa understand that no solution could be imposed by military force and that there must be a return to reason and to dialogue for the implementation of the national consensus document. In a statement to AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT, Bin-Farid added: "We have our own human resources, intrinsic capabilities, tribal relations, and the feeling that there is a right which must be upheld." He emphasized that the opposition devotes all its resources, big or small, to stopping this mad war. He also said: "Unity can only continue with the approval of the parties concerned, not by coercion and armed force. This war has been a blow to unity. [quotation marks as published]
