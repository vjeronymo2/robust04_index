940122 FT 22 JAN 94 / London Stock Exchange: TV stocks active on ruling The predatory television stocks, Granada and Carlton Communications, rallied on the news that their respective bids would not be referred to the MMC providing certain undertakings were met. The afternoon announcement caused some confusion, with Carlton's shares - already marked down on the day through profit-taking - dipping to 986 at one point before the company said it had already indicated its willingness to give such undertakings. Shares recovered to finish down 12 at 1006p, while Granada moved up 16 to 591p. LWT were up 11 to 699p and Central down 50 to 3065p. Elsewhere profit-taking knocked Reuters back 27 to 1897p and EMAP lost 9 to close at 465p. Reed and Pearson were slightly stronger on the day, closing at 902p, up 6, and 640p, up 6, respectively.
