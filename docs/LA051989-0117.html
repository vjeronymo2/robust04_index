May 19, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

BEST BITES;

A CHANCE TO EAT A MEAL FIT FOR A PERSIAN KING

Eggplant with fried walnuts and caraway. . . . Goat cheese, olives and herbs rolled up in flat bread. . . . Roasted quail with a salad of cucumbers, sour grape juice, cilantro and mint. . . . Leg of lamb, marinated in onion juice and saffron and served with apricots and rice pilaf. . . . Pistachio custard with fresh peaches wrapped in filo pastry.

Ancient Persian feasts are not normal restaurant fare in this part of the world, but this Sunday will be an exception. Gilliland's, 2424 Main St., Santa Monica, (213) 392-3901, will be serving the meal along with six different wines from Stonegate. The evening begins at 6 with a cooking demonstration, includes comments by an expert on ancient Persian food, and costs $55 per person.RUTH REICHL
