BFN [TA SR report: "Trade With Czech Republic; We Import Food and Export Metallurgical and Engineering Products"] [Text] Bratislava--In the first two months of this year, Slovakia achieved a surplus of 830 million korunas [Sk] in trade with the Czech Republic [CR]. This transpires from data the Slovak Statistical Office published in late April. According to this data, the CR remains Slovakia's key trading partner. Slovakia exports to the CR 37.6 percent of all of its total export volume and obtains from the CR 30.4 percent of its imports. Compared with the same period last year, Slovak exports to the CR declined 2.2 percent, while imports went up 14.7 percent. Apart from some food and agricultural products, such as cheese, wheat, corn, sweets, chocolate, bakery products, wine, and fruit juice, the CR purchases in Slovakia chiefly chemical products--mineral oils, gasoline, mineral fertilizers, paints and varnishes, herbicides, plastic goods, tires, and hides. Products of the consumer goods industry that the CR purchases in Slovakia include bathtubs, showers, washbasins, beef hides, wooden pallets and wood, combed and carded cotton, and all kinds of bottles, flasks, and demijohns. Metallurgical, engineering, and electrical engineering products--rolled iron and steel products, wire, generators, pumps, excavators, bulldozers, lathes, forming machines, ball bearings, and tractors--represent an important part of Slovak exports to the CR. Important Slovak export commodities include, furthermore, refrigerators and freezers, washing machines, television sets, forklift trucks, and transformers. Slovak imports consisted mainly of food--cream, fermented milk, yogurt, fats, sugar, salami and sausages, sweets, and bakery products. Slovakia imports from the CR, furthermore, coal, briquettes and coke, gasoline, mineral oils, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, plastic products, rubber, tires, and newsprint. Dominant among engineering and metallurgical imports are intermediary and rolled iron and steel products, piston engines, turbines, pumps, industrial furnaces, and machinery for the food industry.
