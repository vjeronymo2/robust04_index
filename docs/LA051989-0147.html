May 19, 1989, Friday, Orange County Edition

HOT TOPICS: HIGH LIFE;

A WEEKLY FORUM FOR HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS;

HONESTY, TRUST: IMPORTANT REQUIREMENTS FOR FRIENDS

What we require from our friends varies from person to person. Some may seek out friends for constant companionship while others are looking for a shoulder to cry on. Some may look to their buddies for advice while others are looking to share a really good secret.

Hot Topics wonders: "What do you value most in a friendship and why?"

"What I value in a friend is that they will listen to my problems and vice versa."

Joe Lee, 15, sophomore, Brea-Olinda

"Honesty; someone to share feelings, troubles and fears with, and also someone to hang out with."

Sandy Roberts, 15, freshman, Brea-Olinda

"With my friends, I value them letting me be myself. They let me say or do what I feel without criticizing me or judging me."

Carmen Nagy, 16, junior, Brea-Olinda

"Trust; in trusting someone you have an understanding in someone whom you can talk to and share your feelings without having everyone in the world know it."

Jennifer Kracik, 16, sophomore, Brea-Olinda

"A friendship itself should be valued because it is not easy to find a real friendship."

Michelle Lanz, 17, junior, Esperanza

"Someone who still likes you after you're really weird in public."

Missy Aspgren, 16, junior, Esperanza

"Trust, honesty and understanding because a true friend will always try to help out."

Julie Greer, 14, freshman, Esperanza

"Devotion because it shows they care and appreciate the friendship."

Karen DallaRosa, 17, senior, Esperanza

"Companionship because it's a good feeling to know you have good friends who like you."

Craig Smidt, 18, senior, Foothill

"The most valuable thing in a friendship is to be a friend, because that's the only way to have one."

Brad Otoupalik, 17, junior, Foothill

"To know that your friends will stick by your side through thick and thin."

Kara Kooi, 15, sophomore, Foothill

"Honesty; if they are your friend they should be honest with you and you should be honest with them."

Ken Turner, 16, sophomore, Fullerton

"Dependability; you need someone to talk to when things aren't going well."

Andrea Reade, 15, sophomore, Fullerton

"A friend is there when you need him the most because everyone can use help."

Chad Bishop, 14, freshman, Irvine

"A lot of common interest because if we don't have the same interests, we won't have anything to talk about."

Brian Moneseldt, 16, freshman, Irvine

"All my friends are surfers, like me, and that's really the only thing I truly enjoy in life, so that's probably what I value most in a friendship, having the same interests."

Jason Kaja, 16, junior, Irvine

"Communication because if friends can't talk about their problems, then they can't get along very well."

Christi Poehlman, 17, junior, Irvine

"Trust because if you don't have trust then you don't have a quality sort of relationship."

John Welsome, 17, senior, San Clemente

"Loyalty because that's the base of a relationship."

Celeste Smith, 17, senior, San Clemente

"Honesty so you can tell that person the truth without him telling everyone."

Dwayne Reyes, 17, senior, San Clemente

"Someone you can talk to when you need support."

Tameron Hulbert, 17, junior, San Clemente

"Understanding; someone who is smart enough to figure out what you are and respect you for who you are and not what you do."

Anne Cass, 17, junior, San Clemente

"It's good to have friends when you need to talk."

Susan Reid, 17, senior, Santa Ana

"Shortness because it makes me feel taller."

Jorge Rivero, 18, senior, Santa Ana

"Being able to tell a friend anything."

Matt Billello, 17, senior, Sunny Hills

"Being able to be truthful and having a friendship based on trust and integrity."

Matt Bogart, 18, senior, Sunny Hills

"Being able to tell a friend something serious and not have him or her laugh."

David Claeys, 18, senior, Sunny Hills

"Being able to trust in my friends. Also, I value the extra time a friend gives to me in just listening."

Kirsten Aplin, 18, senior, Sunny Hills

"Being able to tell them anything and they're always there to listen."

Michael Christensen, 17, senior, Sunny Hills

"Talking about problems."

Dawn Brennan, 17. junior, Tustin

"Trust because I can be really understanding and listen."

Pete Gerlach, 18, senior, Tustin

"Truth because as long as you tell the truth, that's the No. 1 thing in a friendship."

Danielle Delys, 16, sophomore, Tustin

"Courage because I can learn to respect my friends when they stand up in a tough situation."

Don Jordon, 17, junior, Tustin

"The ability to understand each other."

Deepa Rajan, 17, senior, University

"Trust and being able to depend on a friend. That way, you'll be able to talk and communicate."

Rithy Lu, 15, sophomore, University

"To always be there for each other in need."

Lela Jackson, 17, senior, University

"Honesty because without honesty, there is nothing."

Lori O'Sullivan, 17, junior, University

"Loyalty; you have to learn to trust them."

Christian Gallarde, 16, sophomore, Valencia

"Trust so that you know your friend isn't cheating on you."

Cathy Bradow, 15, freshman, Valencia

"Trust because you need someone to be there for you."

Kristen McDonald, 18, senior, Valencia

"Honesty because if you can't trust someone, what is the point of basing a relationship on that person?"

Jennifer Butler, 16, sophomore, Valencia

"Honesty and trust because if you loan them money and you don't know if they are honest and trustworthy, you don't know if they'll repay you."

Mike Valdez, 15, freshman, Valencia

"Someone who will give me respect for my property and trust."

Geno Ramirez, 16, freshman, Valencia

"Keeping promises and having someone always be there for you."

Stephanie Fry, 16, sophomore, Villa Park

"Trust and to be there for me to listen and to be open."

Andrea Helzer, 14, freshman, Villa Park

"To trust a person and to understand them and know they'll be there for you and help you out, and you can do the same for them."

Suzi Avila, 14, freshman, Villa Park

"Accepting people for the way they are, no matter what."

Nadia Davis, 18, senior, Villa Park

"Trust; a friend is someone you can tell things to, and if you don't trust them, you can't tell them your problems."

Kirstan Kirkwood, 15, sophomore, Villa Park

"When you can be yourself and not be made fun of because of the person that you are."

Lea Schmidt, 16, sophomore, Western

"Honesty and respect so that you are able to have a sharing relationship."

A.J. Gordon, 18, senior, Western

"The fact that a friend will be there for you in times of need."

Vanesa Bui, 16, sophomore, Western
