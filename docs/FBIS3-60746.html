Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India has expressed the hope that the foreign secretary-level talks with Pakistan in the first week of next month will be successful and lead to a movement forward. Addressing the Consultative Committee Meeting of members of parliament attached to his ministry, the minister of state for external affairs, Mr. R.L. Bhatia, described as untrue the observation that the Simla agreement has not been functional. Mr. Bhatia reiterated India's total commitment to human rights and stated that the Human Rights Commission Bill has been introduced in parliament. Regarding declaration of Pakistan as a terrorist state, Mr. Bhatia said that all requisite evidence available with India has been passed on to the United States. The U.S. has not taken the final step for its own reasons. On Indo-U.S. relations, Mr. Bhatia allayed the apprehensions of the members and asserted that New Delhi will not compromise country's sovereignty and its independent position under pressure. He said India has explained its point of view frequently in detail and will continue to do so. He also said India's position on nonproliferation is very clear and based on sound and fundamental principles.
