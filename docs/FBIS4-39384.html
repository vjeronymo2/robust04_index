BFN [Text] The right to use mandatory deposits by the Ateitis and Nida commercial banks was suspended yesterday by the Lithuanian Central Bank. This was reported by Egle Valyte, press spokesman for the Lithuanian Central Bank. The statement disseminated by the Lithuanian Central Bank today stresses that this decision is aimed at protecting the interests of the creditors. The decision was made after the Lithuanian Central Bank administration made amendments to the rules on mandatory reserves of commercial banks on 3 June. The use of mandatory reserves deposited at the Lithuanian Central Bank are restricted if the banks violate their obligations and are not able to settle accounts in full with their creditors. We have already reported that the Apus, Express, and Lietukis commercial banks had their right to use the mandatory reserves suspended by the Administration Board of the Lithuanian Central Bank on 3d June.
