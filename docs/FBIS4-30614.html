BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 28 KYODO -- International Trade and Industry Minister [MITI] Eijiro Hata said Tuesday [28 June] he has asked the monetary authorities to take action to stabilize the currency market. Steps should be taken so that a burgeoning pickup of the Japanese economy will be sustained and shifted to a full recovery, Hata told reporters. Hata said he explained "the voice" of Japan's industrial sectors struggling under the unmitigated strength of the yen when he met with Finance Minister Hirohisa Fujii earlier in the day. Fujii replied Japan is prepared to take action in concert with other Group of Seven (G-7) industrial countries, Hata said. He said he has heard that the currency issue will be taken up at the upcoming G-7 summit in Naples, Italy. The dollar sank to a new global low of 99.50 yen in Monday's Tokyo trading momentarily.
