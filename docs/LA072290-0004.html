July 22, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

BRODERICK MURDERS

I was saddened by Wallace's story on the Broderick marriage and its tragic consequences -- but not surprised. It is too bad that a woman allowed herself to assume complete control of rearing her children while encouraging her husband to be the sole financial support of the family. The duties of either of these roles are enough to make anyone crazy.

CHRISTINE LOVRET STAPP

San Marino
