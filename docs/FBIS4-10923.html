CSO [Editorial Report] Managua BARRICADA in Spanish of 5 April 1994 on page 5 reports that Jose Espinoza Navas, leader of the Permanent Congress of Workers, and Lucio Jimenez, National Workers Front leader, expressed their reluctance to support the IMF agreement which provides for the Enhanced Structural Adjustment Facility. They stated: "We are not going to back with our signature something which will harm the already precarious situation of the workers." Espinoza said that union leaders were "extremely concerned" because the agreement threatens the jobs of 15,000 workers. Espinoza and Jimenez explained that the threat of huge layoffs will determine whether the union leaders participate further in talks with the High Level Commission studying the agreement.
