940204 FT 04 FEB 94 / Kleinwort launch is UK record The public offer for Kleinwort Benson's European Privatisation Investment Trust was more than Pounds 380m oversubscribed, confirming its position as the largest UK investment trust launch ever. Applications for Pounds 560m-worth of shares were received during the public offer, which closed on Wednesday, giving the fund a potential size of Pounds 880m, as Pounds 320m had already been raised through an institutional placing, but it was capped at Pounds 500m. However, the Mercury European Privatisation Trust has set its maximum size at Pounds 575m, so it could soon beat Kleinwort's record. The Mercury fund raised Pounds 325m in its placing, which closed on Wednesday, and is seeking a further Pounds 100m from an intermediaries offer, closing February 25, and Pounds 150m from a public offer, closing March 2. Applications for shares in the Kleinwort trust are being scaled back by about two-thirds. Mr Simon White, managing director of Kleinwort Benson Investment Trusts, said the company believed that Pounds 500m was an appropriate size given the state of development and liquidity of the target investment area. If all the disappointed investors from the Kleinwort offer now apply for the Mercury fund, it will also be oversubscribed. Investors who applied for the minimum 2,000 Kleinwort shares will receive 700. Applications for up to 2m shares will be allocated approximately 31.45 per cent, and those for more than 2m shares will receive 31.45 of the first 2m and 25 per cent thereafter. One free warrant will be issued with every five shares.
