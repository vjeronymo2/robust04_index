BFN [By P.C. Wu and Lilian Wu] [Text] Bali, Indonesia, May 18 (CNA) -- More than 100 delegates from 17 nations and regions are taking part in a senior officials meeting of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) forum which opened Wednesday [19 May]. Shen Kuo-hsiung, director of the Foreign Affairs Ministry's Department of International Organizations of the Republic of China [ROC], led an 18-member ROC delegation to the meeting. Shen said that the three-day meeting will discuss follow-up plans for last year's APEC leadership meeting in seattle and the agenda for this year's meeting in December. Wisber Loeis, director-general of Foreign Economic Relations Affairs of Indonesia's Department of Foreign Affairs, said that the meeting will also discuss the development of manpower resources, promotion of small and medium-sized enterprises and coordination of trade and public construction. Shen said that the participants decided that the APEC leadership meeting this year will be held in Bogor, Indonesia. The date for the meeting is still not final, but Indonesia has proposed the meeting be held Nov. 15. Lin Yi-fu, vice deputy-general of the ROC's Board of Foreign Trade, asked participants to fill out a report on the development of small and medium-sized enterprises before the end of June, so that APEC experts can discuss the issue in a September meeting. APEC groups Australia, Brunei, Canada, Mainland China, Hong Kong, Indonesia, Japan, Malaysia, New Zealand, the Philippines, Singapore, South Korea, Taiwan, Thailand, Papua New Guinea, Mexico and the United States.
