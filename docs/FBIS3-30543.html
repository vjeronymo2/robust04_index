Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Sarajevo, 5 Feb (TANJUG) -- In response to the massacre in the center of Sarajevo, Major General Manojlo Milovanovic, chief of general staff of the republic of Srpska army, demanded that General Rose, UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR] commander for the former Bosnia-Herzegovina, urgently form a mixed team of military experts to [passage indistinct due to technical failure] Gen. Milovanovic asked Gen. Rose to include representatives of UNPROFOR, the republic of Srspka army, and the so-called Bosnia-Herzegovina army on the commission of experts. The commission should investigate ballistic and other circumstances under which the massacre occurred to establish which side committed the crime. Gen. Milovanovic requested of Gen. Rose that the commission's work be recorded on TV cameras and in photographs and documented with all other necessary facts and data and that the world public be informed about it. If the Serbian side's request is not accepted, the main headquarters of the republic of Srpska army will suspend all cooperation with UNPROFOR and humanitarian organizations until the case is completely clarified and the world public informed about it, the chief of general staff of the republic of Srpska army stressed in a letter to Gen. Rose. He asked Gen. Rose to answer his letter by 1800 hours today.
