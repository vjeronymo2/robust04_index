Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Colombo, February 28 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen arrived here this evening on a two-day official goodwill visit to Sri Lanka. Qian came here after his visits to Pakistan and Bangladesh. Sri Lanka is the third country of his five-nation tour which will also take him to the Maldives and Iran. He was greeted by Sri Lankan Foreign Minister A.C.S. Hameed and Chinese Ambassador to Sri Lanka Zhang Chengli at the Colombo airport. In a written statement issued at the airport, Qian said, "China and Sri Lanka have been enjoying a profound traditional friendship which goes back to ancient times." He noted that "over the past 30 years or more since the establishment of the diplomatic relations, the friendly relations and cooperation between the two countries have developed satisfactorily." He expressed the hope that his current visit "will help enhance mutual understanding, deepen friendship and promote cooperation between the two countries." He said he is looking forward to an in-depth exchange of views with his Sri Lankan counterpart Hameed on bilateral relations and regional and international issues of mutual interest. Qian is expected to call on Sri Lankan President D.B. Wijetunge and Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe during his visit.
