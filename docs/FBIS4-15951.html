BFN [Unattributed report from ROSSIYSKAYA GAZETA, ITAR-TASS roundup under "Yesterday, Today, Tomorrow" rubric: "No Money for Sowing"] [Text] A considerable number of Russian private farmers are in danger of not sowing their fields this spring. That was stated by Vladimir Bashmachnikov, president of the Association of Russian Peasant (Private) Farms and Agricultural Cooperatives [AKKOR]. According to his figures, 10-40 percent of land occupied by private peasant farmers could go unsown in various regions. The AKKOR leader said that this situation is due to financial problems which have become particularly aggravated this season.
