Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Konstantin Pribytkov] [Text] Geneva February 2 TASS -- Now it is important to give a good and energetic start to the U.N. disarmament conference, which is encountering new difficult problems, above all, the task of working out an international treaty on a universal ban on nuclear tests, Russia's permanent representative in the conference Grigoriy Berdennikov said in an interview with ITAR-TASS on Wednesday. Berdennikov noted Russia's principle readiness to go in for concluding at present such a treaty, which can be subjected to checking, and emphasised that Russia will construct its stand at the talks in such a way that they are mainly completed, possibly by the spring of 1995. The Russian delegation attaches top priority to the nuclear test ban talks in 1994, Berdennikov said. According to him, the question of the scope of the treaty should be decided on the basis of already existing international accords, above all, the Moscow treaty of 1963, which bans nuclear tests in three mediums, of course, with the inclusion of a ban on underground tests. Russia advocates an unlimited and non-discriminatory character of the treaty and equal rights and responsibilities of all its participants, he said. The agreement should apply not only to nuclear states, but also to those which are capable of making nuclear weapons. The treaty should come into force after the submission of ratification deeds, provided they include deeds of all states, which possess nuclear power plants and research reactors at the time of signing the document. There is a need to impose the strictest sanctions on participating countries which violate its main statutes, right up to imposing economic or other measures of compulsions stipulated by the UN Charter. The delegation also favours working out a multilateral agreement on guarantees of the security of non-nuclear states.
