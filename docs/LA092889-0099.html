September 28, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

REFUGEE EXPANDS ON SUCCESS

In 1960, Rafael Galindo left his native Cuba for California with only $5 in his pocket. He didn't speak a word of English but was able to land a job in a cannery and enroll in night school.

Almost 30 years later, Galindo runs his own real estate firm, which according to Hispanic Business magazine is the largest Latino-owned business in California and the eighth-largest in the country.

Last week, Galindo expanded his operations to the San Gabriel Valley with the opening of a third office at 1071 E. Amar Road in West Covina.

Twenty agents will work out of the West Covina office, which will handle residential and commercial property sales, including escrow services.

Galindo Realty has sold more than $800 million worth of real estate. Last year, the firm processed $118 million in transactions.
