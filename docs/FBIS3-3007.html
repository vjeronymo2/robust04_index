Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 16 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Foreign Minister Qian Qichen said here today that the visit of Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa to China will promote Sino-Japanese cooperation in all fields. When asked about Sino-Japanese relations at a press conference this morning, Qian said, China and Japan are close neighbors and the leaders of the two countries often exchange visits. Chinese Vice-Premier Zhu Rongji's recent visit to Japan achieved a great success. Now Chinese leaders are looking forward to the Japanese Prime Minister Hosokawa's visit to China at a time when the Sino-Japanese relations have achieved all-round development. "I believe the visit will play a very important role for further development of the Sino-Japanese relations and cooperation in economic fields," he said. During the visit, China and Japan will formally sign an agreement on cooperation between the two governments in strengthening environmental protection, Qian said. On the relations between China and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea and between China and the Republic of Korea, Qian said, "our stand remains unchanged." He said that Premier Li Peng has made it clear in his government work report that China has further strengthened its good-neighborly relations with the surrounding countries and the friendship between China and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea has grown. China has steadily expanded its mutually beneficial cooperation relations with Japan, Russia and the Republic of Korea, Qian said.
