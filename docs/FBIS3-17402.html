Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 24/3/1994 -- The economy of the Republic of Moldova showed a slight tendency to redress in 1993 according to the data in a communique of the State Department for Statistics of the Republic of Moldova. The industrial production, expressed in comparable prices, increased by 4.2 percent and the agricultural one by 2 percent. 1992 was the year with the lowest production in the Republic of Moldova, reveals the communique. On the other hand, the comparative evolution of the industrial output shows a drop in 1993 on 1992 for 91 of the 120 products on which monthly statistical reports are made, most of them consumer goods and products for the use of children. "A price hike was reported in 1993," says the communique. The consumer price index grew by 25-37 percent in the first three months of 1993 and lower increases followed. The communique also speaks about "the worsening of the demographic situation -- with a drop in the total population of nine thousand persons -- and about a crisis in fields such as construction, transport, telecommunications, health-care. As for education, statistical data show an increase in the number of pupils who study Romanian, the official language of the Republic of Moldova. The foreign trade of the Republic of Moldova started to be oriented to a certain extent to the countries of Western Europe in 1993, although Russia remained this country's main partner, with 35 percent of the total exchanges, followed by Romania, 12 percent and Ukraine, 10 percent.
