BFN [Text] At a House of Representatives' Budget Committee meeting on 30 May, Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata was asked about the collapse of talks between the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) and Pyongyang over the inspection of fuel rods being removed from a nuclear reactor in the DPRK. Hata said: "I have repeatedly asked the United States and South Korea to tenaciously talk with Pyongyang. On the other hand, I have asked North Korea to consider the anxiety in the international community." He then stressed that he intended to work with concerned nations to resolve the nuclear issue through talks with Pyongyang. Hata also said: "I have told Pyongyang (if the country agrees to settle the issue through talks) Japan will help improve the DPRK's living standards together with the United States, South Korea, and China." In this way, he indicated that North Korea would be offered multinational support and cooperation if it agreed to resolve the issue through dialogue. Hata was answering questions from Yoshio Hachiro (a member of the Social Democratic Party of Japan). Concerning the fuel rod removal Pyongyang has arbitrarily carried out, Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa also said: "If the situation remains as it is now, it will become difficult in the next few days to confirm the fuel rod exchange," indicating the nuclear problem would reach the crucial point in the next few days.
