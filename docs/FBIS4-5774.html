BFN [Text] Hiroshima, April 13 KYODO -- The Japan Atomic Industrial Forum began its annual meeting Wednesday [13 April] in Hiroshima, the first time it has been held in the world's first atomic-bombed city. The three-day meeting has as its theme "toward a world free of nuclear weapons, the role of peaceful use." It will feature addresses on Wednesday by Hans Blix, director general of the International Atomic Energy Agency, and Pulitzer Prize-winning author Richard Rhodes. This year for the first time, at a Wednesday panel discussion, the meeting will deal with the problems of dismantling nuclear weapons. On Thursday, atomic power advocates are scheduled to meet with citizens of Hiroshima and victims of the 1945 atomic bombing, including the head of an association of Korean victims of the nuclear holocaust, Yi Sil-gun [name as received], for an exchange of opinions. Some 1,500 atomic power specialists from Japan and abroad are expected to take part in the symposium, which has been held annually since 1968.
