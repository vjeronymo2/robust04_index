BFN [Text] In Jammu Kashmir, militants kidnapped two British tourists, demanding the release of three foreign mercenaries belonging to the Harkatul Ansar outfit. The incident took place near Pahalgam yesterday. One of the kidnapped is the son of a journalist formerly based in Delhi. The security forces have launched a manhunt to secure their release. Militants also looted two foreigners of their belongings and cash at gunpoint at Aroo near Pahalgam. In another incident, four persons, including two militants have been killed in the valley. The security forces nabbed eight militants and recovered a large quantity of arms and ammunition during search operations. Meanwhile, the indefinite curfew imposed in Bhaderwah and Kishtwar towns of Doda District is continuing. The curfew was imposed in Bhaderwah town yesterday morning following the killing of a BJP [Bharatiya Janata Party] leader by militants and the death of another person in violent incidents. In Kishtwar, the curfew was clamped as a precautionary measure. Security forces are patrolling vulnerable areas of both the towns.
