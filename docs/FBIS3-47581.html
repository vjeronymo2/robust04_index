Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 13 KYODO -- NEC Corp. said Thursday [13 January] it has developed a technique to enhance the memory capacity of a magneto-optical disc by eight times as much as the current method. The technique makes it possible to store data equivalent to some 80,000 Japanese newspaper pages or two hours of VHS-format video movie pictures on a magneto-optical disc which measures 13 centimeters in diameter, the same size as a music disc, the company said. A magneto-optical disc is based on a recording method that combines laser and magnetic technologies to create high-density erasable storage discs. Its surface is made of a magnetic recording material that records magnetic spots or bits using a laser to pinpoint them. NEC's new technique combines a signal-processing technology called the partial response maximum likelihood (prml) method with a red-color data-reading laser. NEC said it plans to develop products using the technique for commercial use as it can be applied to a range of multimedia equipment.
