Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By UKRINFORM correspondent Viktor Demidenko] [Text] Kiev, 4 Feb -- Economic and political problems facing the Crimean Republic as a constituent part of Ukraine were discussed by Ukraine's President Leonid Kravchuk and Crimean Republic President Yuriy Meshkov at their working meeting held here today. The main attention was paid to economic issues, in particular, to the pattern of developing economic relations between Crimea and its traditional partners in the Russian Federation and other CIS countries, offered by Yuriy Meshkov. An agreement was reached during the meeting on setting up a working group of the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine and the government of the autonomous republic that will consider specific proposals and calculations of the economic development of Crimea. Also discussed was the mechanism of working relations between the president of Ukraine and the president of the Crimean Republic. The meeting was held in an atmosphere of openness and constructive mutual understanding.
