BFN [Text] San Jose, 1 May (ACAN-EFE) -- Costa Rican President Rafael Angel Calderon today emphasized his country's high economic growth rate, low inflation rate, and reduced poverty and described the Free Trade Agreement [TLC] with Mexico as one of the greatest achievements of his foreign policy. In a report to the country entitled "Roads of Prosperity" read during a solemn session of the Legislative Assembly, the president said: "Our economic strategy allowed us to defeat inflation and the high cost of living." To illustrate the apparent success of his administration, Calderon cited figures such as an increase in GNP from 6.5 percent in 1992 to 7.7 percent in 1993 following a severe adjustment at the beginning of his government. Calderon, who will turn over the presidency to Jose Maria Figueres of the Social Democratic Party next Sunday, said in this, his last report to the nation, that during his four-year term exports increased by 60 percent and reached $2.667 billion last year. He also said that the fiscal deficit, which stood at 7.5 percent when he took office, was reduced to less than 1 percent last year and inflation was reduced to 9 percent, the lowest in the past 15 years. Calderon said that, contrary to what several sectors say about the country's alleged impoverishment, unemployment was reduced to 4 percent -- one of the lowest unemployment rates on the continent -- and poverty to less than 17 percent, three points less than when he assumed office. He also emphasized the growth of tourism, which is expected to become the primary source of foreign exchange this year, and housing programs, which led to the construction of 96,000 homes in four years. "The economic reform sponsored by this administration made it possible to release resources that were used in social programs. Thanks to this, Costa Rica remains an example of social and human development in Latin America," he said. Regarding criticism for an alleged disregard of foreign policy, Calderon stressed "an active diplomacy" in favor of democracy and respect for human rights. In this connection, he recalled Costa Rica's participation in the crises in Haiti, Peru, and Guatemala. "Throughout these years, we contributed to the reaffirmation of processes for peace, democratization, and integration in Central America," he said, adding that the signing of the TLC between Costa Rica and Mexico is one of his administration's greatest achievements in the international field. This commercial treaty, which will go into effect in January after it is ratified by the legislatures of the two countries, will permit the trade of 12,000 products. Costa Rica will be able to triple its exports to Mexico in the next two years. Such exports now reach barely $60 million, according to government and private enterprise calculations. "Whatsoever a man soweth he shall also reap. Costa Rica has sown and is now reaping," the president said. Calderon will become part of the opposition on 8 May with one of the highest popularity levels a president has ever attained at the time of his departure from office. During the early months of his government, Calderon implemented a severe economic adjustment program that led to higher public services rates, higher staple prices, a higher inflation rate, and low economic growth, a little over 2 percent, in 1990. In this last report, however, he said: "We are enjoying the first fruits of a time of prosperity. We turned obstacles into solutions, obstructions into channels, and setbacks into victories. But we are just at the beginning of the road."
