930325 FT 25 MAR 93 / German steel pay talks fail GERMAN steel employers and unions have failed to find a compromise to avert a strike in eastern Germany next month. Inconclusive talks were held this week between IG Metall, Germany's giant engineering union, and the steel employers association. The union is insisting on a 21 per cent pay increase for its 20,000 steel union members in eastern Germany, as part of a March 1991 contract. The employers have offered 9 per cent. IG Metall said yesterday it would start balloting members in eastern Germany on a strike, although one senior official conceded that the union 'did not really want a strike' in eastern Germany. Small and medium-sized enterprises did not want a strike, he said, nor could they pay their employees a 26 per cent rise. However, it was time 'the workers in eastern Germany learned to defend their interests. After 50 years, they have forgotten how to strike.'
