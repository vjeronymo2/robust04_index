Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ukrainian Speaker Ivan Plyusch and acting Prime Minister Yefim Zvyagilskiy have been nominated for deputies of the future Ukrainian parliament. Plyusch was registered as a candidate from constituency No.455 situated in the Chernigov region. The Central Election Commission told "Interfax-Ukraine" that the speaker was nominated by the workers of a food plant of the town of Borznyany. Workers of the Zasyadko mine in the Donetsk nominated acting Prime Minister Yefim Zvyagilskiy as their deputy. Prior to his appointment mayor of Donetsk and then government member, he worked as the director of the mine. According to the election law, parties and working groups are entitled to nominate their candidates to people's deputies. The contenders must canvass more than 300 votes of the electors residing in this constituency to be registered as a candidate for people's deputy. The elections to the Supreme Soviet of Ukraine will be held on March 27 1994.
