920914 FT 14 SEP 92 / Clinton launches national voter registration drive GOVERNOR Bill Clinton (above left) launched at the weekend a national voter registration drive, to be led by the Rev Jesse Jackson (above right), in an attempt to add thousands of black voters to the rolls in the hope they will support the Democratic presidential ticket, AP reports from Washington. But questions remain as to whether relations between the men have warmed in the wake of Mr Jackson's complaints of a 'push-off' strategy by the Clinton campaign. Mr Jackson sought to add the first unregistered voters to the roll during the seventh annual Black Family Reunion in Washington, saying the vote 'has so much power'. The event is sponsored by the National Council of Negro Women to draw attention to problems suffered by black families. ''Clinton campaign workers said the registration drive would include 300 events in 30 cities.
