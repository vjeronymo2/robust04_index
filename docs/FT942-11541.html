940505 FT 05 MAY 94 / Observer: Nice alms A sign of the times. The Asian Development Bank, that portmanteau do-gooder on behalf of developing Asian nations, has been staging its annual general meeting in Nice. Rather more developed France used the occasion - naturally -to tout for inward investment and push its own products. Prime minister Edouard Balladur fronted the sales effort, regaling the assembled horde of Asian dignitaries - some rich, others decidedly poor - with France's virtues of low inflation, stable currency, freedom of capital, dynamic companies. Then Edmond Alphandery, economics minister, pointedly reminded them how much aid France has, over time, pumped into Asia. Pity Alphandery seemed rather less familiar with ADB's own workings - displaying remarkable vagueness on the number of its members, for instance - after all, he is its current chairman.
