BFN [By Danielle Yang] [Text] Taipei, May 23 (CNA) -- Taiwan will share its experience in preventing dengue fever with Southeast Asian countries, Environmental Protection Administration Administrator Chang Lung-sheng said Monday [23 May]. Chang made the remarks at the opening ceremony of an international dengue fever control meeting which gathered more than 180 experts and scholars from 19 countries in Taipei. Chang said the main purpose for holding the meeting was to invite specialists to form a new dengue fever control alliance in Asia to jointly combat the disease and develop programs in urban areas to kill mosquitoes which carry the disease. The meeting will also propose that a private foundation be established in Taiwan to help fight dengue fever, common in Southeast Asia and Mainland China, Chang said. Taiwan eliminated dengue fever in just three years, Chang said, while neighboring Southeast Asian countries continue to fight the spread of the disease.
