August 18, 1989, Friday, P.M. Final

SOUTHLAND;

'STALKER' DEFENSE LAWYER FINED FOR MISSING MOST OF THE COURT SESSIONS

The judge in the "Night Stalker" murder trial today fined a defense attorney $100 for not showing up at court hearings.

Arturo Hernandez, who has been absent for most court sessions, told the judge he had been working on the case for Richard Ramirez outside of court. He also said his brother had died during the period he was absent.

Superior Court Judge Michael Tynan threatened to fine him $500 but ultimately imposed a fine of $100.

Ramirez is also represented by Daniel Hernandez, no relation, and Ray G. Clark, who was appointed to help out after Daniel Hernandez said he was suffering from stress.

The fine was ordered as the jury deliberated for a third day.
