BFN [Text] Pretoria May 6 SAPA -- The National Party on Friday [6 May] announced that Mr F W de Klerk has been elected executive deputy president, according to the constitutional provisions allowing for such a position. "Mr de Klerk will serve in this position in his own right and because he represents millions of South Africans of all population groups," an NP congratulatory statement from Pretoria said. "Mr de Klerk is eminently suited to play a reconciliatory role in the Government of National Unity," the statement added. Earlier on Friday, the African National Congress announced its national chairman, Mr Thabo Mbeki, would serve as first deputy president. Both men will serve under president-designate Nelson Mandela.
