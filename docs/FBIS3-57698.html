Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Ukraine's President Leonid Kravchuk today gave instructions to the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine on a number of issues which had been discussed during his meeting with a group of people's deputies of Ukraine from the Republic of Crimea on 24 January. The meeting took place at the request of People's Deputies of Ukraine Matiyko, Reva, Sevastyanov, and Terekhov. Discussed at the meeting were social and economic problems facing the Crimea and also the ways toward improving the crime situation which has of late taken shape there. During the conversation the people's deputies of Ukraine asked President Kravchuk about his attitude toward the presidential election in Crimea. In this connection Kravchuk said: I respect the right of the electorate to elect the president they want, but if the president of the Crimea Republic violates the law and the Constitution of Ukraine then I, as the president of Ukraine, will use my constitutional plenary powers and take relevant measures to revoke the illegitimate decisions. This was reported today by the press service of the president of Ukraine and the Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine.
