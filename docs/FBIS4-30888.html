BFN [Text] Belgrade, May 31 (TANJUG) -- The constituent assembly of the Muslim-Croat federation on Tuesday [31 May] elected Kresimir Zubak, a Croat, the federation's first president, Muslim-controlled Radio Sarajevo reported. At a session in Sarajevo which opened on Monday, Ejup Ganic, a Muslim, was elected vice-president of the federation. As announced, they will hold the offices during the six-month period of transition, till the elections. Previously, the deputies adopted (with one abstention by a Croat representative) an amendment of the constitution of the former Yugoslav Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina and adopted the constitution of the future Muslim-Croat federation. The agreement on the setting up of a Muslim-Croat federation in Bosnia-Herzegovina was signed in Washington on March 18, under strong pressure from the U.S. Representatives of Bosnian Serbs were not invited to these talks. Under the Washington agreement, the federation would subsequently enter into a confederation with neighbouring Croatia. At a recent ministerial meeting of the U.S., Russia and the European Union, it was agreed that the Muslim-Croat federation get 51 percent, and the Serb state 49 percent of Bosnia-Herzegovina.
