930202 FT 02 FEB 93 / Parliament and Politics: Vaz motion attacks BCCI liquidator AN EARLY-DAY motion critical of Touche Ross, liquidators of the failed Bank of Credit and Commerce International, yesterday won the backing of around 25 MPs after being tabled by Mr Keith Vaz, the Labour MP. The motion came as MPs on the cross-party Treasury and Civil Service Committee considered a draft report calling for compensation to be paid to customers who lost money after the collapse of BCCI. But the draft report, which also suggests the Bank of England should shoulder more of the blame for the collapse, is the subject of intense debate between the Labour and Conservative MPs on the committee. Its criticisms may be toned down when the committee debates it next week. Describing the progress of the liquidation as 'painfully slow', the motion tabled by Mr Vaz notes that despite fees of Pounds 1m a week, 'no money, apart from statutory compensation has been paid to depositors, local authorities, or former employees of BCCI.' It welcomes the government's move to 'consider' Touche's removal as liquidators. Mr Neil Hamilton, trade and industry minister, said last week that such a step followed 'representations.'
