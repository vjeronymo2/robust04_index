May 13, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

NONFICTION IN BRIEF: HUSBAND IS THE PAST TENSE OF DADDY AND OTHER DISPATCHES FROM THE FRONT LINES OF MOTHERHOOD BY TERYL ZARNOW (ADDISON-WESLEY: $15.95; 221 PP.)

The title of Teryl Zarnow's collection, published in time for Mother's Day, speaks for itself.

These pieces from her syndicated Knight-Ridder column, "Color Me Mommy," maintain a humorous character while serving up the undercurrent of self-conscious "what have I done?" lament popular in the new genre of parenting literature. When Zarnow writes, "Rules have civilized us; they have created order out of chaos. I am Ayatollah the Mommy; my rulings come swift and merciless," the reader can be sure there are some laughs in this collection that will be appreciated only by those whose normal sleeping patterns have been irretrievably disturbed by night feedings. Sonja Bolle
