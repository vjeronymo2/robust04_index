BFN [Text] Mate Granic, deputy prime minister and foreign minister, met Bosnian President Alija Izetbegovic at Zagreb airport this afternoon. President Izetbegovic is on a stopover in Zagreb on his way to Saudi Arabia where he will be taking part in the pilgrimage to Mecca with 400 other Muslims from Bosnia-Herzegovina and Croatia. We discussed the implementation of the Vienna agreement, President Tudjman's visit to Sarajevo -- which is expected to take place very soon -- as well as various forms of cooperation between Bosnia-Herzegovina and Croatia, such as transport, trade and the power industry, President Izetbegovic said after the talks. Minister Granic said that there were no longer any obstacles in the way of President Tudjman's visit to Bosnia-Herzegovina. He added that the exact date of the visit would be determined in a couple of days. Minister Granic said that the talks also centered on cooperation between Bosnia-Herzegovina and Croatia in the area of trade, as well as a joint approach to European integration, the system of security and succession. As far as the Washington and Vienna agreements are concerned, both sides have agreed that their implementation should be ensured as soon as possible, Minister Granic said.
