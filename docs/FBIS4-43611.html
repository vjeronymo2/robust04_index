BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] It has been reported that Turkey and Iran support the achievement of a peaceful solution to the Karabakh issue as soon as possible. In addition, their determination to continue their cooperation on this issue was stressed. The Foreign Ministry issued a statement noting that the Turkish and Iranian Foreign Ministries held technical consultations in Tehran on 13-15 June within the framework of an exchange of ideas on regional developments and issues. During these meetings, representatives of both countries expressed their views on the need for peace and stability to reign in the region and the need to achieve a solution of the Karabakh conflict through peaceful methods. Stating that the territorial integrity of the countries of the region and the inviolability of their borders must be respected, the statement stresses that lasting solutions to disagreements cannot be achieved through the use of force and military coercion.
