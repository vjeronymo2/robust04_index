CSO [Unattributed article: "New Macedonian Duties on Slovene Imports"] [Text] Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] this week began charging two duties totaling 15 percent on the bulk of Slovene imports: a 7.5-percent levy and a 7.5-percent special duty designed to spread the tax burden. The new duties apply to all classes of goods that make up Slovenia's most significant exports to Macedonia: -- furniture and other wooden products, -- poultry, -- paper and cardboard, -- tires, -- washing powders, -- cosmetics, -- household appliances. This effectively means that the bilateral free trade agreement signed in 1992 is no longer in force.
