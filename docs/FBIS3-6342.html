Language: Thai Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Chuan Likphai paid a courtesy call on Do Muoi, general secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam [CPV], at the party Central Committee Office in Hanoi at 1700 yesterday. Both the CPV general secretary and the Thai prime minister agreed that both countries will follow up agreements reached in various fields so they will bear fruit effectively. On the same occasion, the CPV general secretary expressed his admiration to His Majesty the King and members of the royal family of Thailand. The CPV general secretary greatly admired the royally initiated projects dealing with agriculture and water resources and he wants to request Thai assistance on this field. Aphisit Wetchachiwa, spokesman of the Prime Minister's Office, granted an interview to this reporter on the event. [Begin recording] [Aphisit] The Vietnamese party Central Committee general secretary expressed his profound admiration and gratitude to His Majesty the King, who once granted him an audience. He also expressed his admiration to members of the Thai Royal Family and royally initiated projects in various fields. He noted that the relationship between the two countries has significantly improved in the past few years. He wanted the bilateral cooperation to further develop for economic, social, and political interests in this region. [Unidentified reporter] What royally initiated projects is the CPV chairman [as heard] interested in and admires? [Aphisit] He said he is greatly impressed by royally initiated projects, especially in the field of agriculture dealing with water. This is because Vietnam wants to improve agricultural production efficiency. Despite success at a certain level in economic reform, Vietnam still has problems due to a lack of technology and improvement in production efficiency, which is becoming more competitive. He specifically admired the king's ability in agriculture and royally initiated projects in various fields. [Reporter] [Question indistinct] [Aphisit] He mentioned Thailand providing assistance through royally initiated projects to other countries, such as Laos. Such forms of assistance are aimed to be models. Vietnam has also requested such assistance from Thailand. [Reporter] Does he want royally initiated projects to be in his country? [Aphisit] Right. [Reporter] Did they discuss possible steps to be taken? [Aphisit] The king will be informed of this matter and I think it could be possible because the king also mentioned this in an audience once given to him. [end recording]
