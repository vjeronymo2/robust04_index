BFN [Text] Cairo, April 23 (AFP) -- Three people were injured Saturday when a bomb exploded in an empty house in northern Cairo believed to be used as an arms store by Islamic militants, security officials said. The blast, in the densely-populated al-Matariyah district, injured three passers-by. Police were questioning an Islamic militant in connection with the incident. Two Islamic militants were injured Thursday when five explosive devices they were planning to use in attacks accidentally blew up in a shop in the 'Ayn Shams district of Cairo. The blast badly damaged the front of the shop, which was owned by one of the injured, a chemist. His cousin was also hurt. The outlawed Islamic group al-Jama'ah al-Islamiyah has been waging a violent two-year campaign to topple Egypt's secular government in which more than 360 people have died.
