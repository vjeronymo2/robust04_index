Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Five of Twelve Adopt European Plan To Fight Against Pollution and Reinforce Maritime Safety"; as released by Brussels TEXTLINE database] [Text] The ministers of five EC countries (Germany, Belgium, France, Great Britain and the Netherlands) adopted, on Wednesday [26 January] evening in Paris, a plan to improve maritime safety and reduce pollution. According to a communique published on Thursday by the French Transport Ministry, the plan concerns the following five points for action: - Reinforcement of the fight against "substandard" ships; - The obligation to have markings/signalings in the Channel/North Sea zone; - Immediate application of container stowing regulations by the International Maritime Organization [IMO]; - Maximum safety requirements for passenger transport; - Reinforcement of means of intervention. The communique stresses, moreover, that the five countries concerned "are ready to act together in London with the IMO and in Brussels with the European Union."
