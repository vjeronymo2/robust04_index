Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Manaus -- PT [Workers Party] President and virtual presidential candidate Luis Inacio Lula da Silva yesterday announced that he will meet with the three military ministers by April at the latest. He made this announcement during a visit to General Jose Sampaio Maia, the Amazonas military commander. According to Lula's advisers, the PT candidate's first formal meeting with a military commander lasted 105 minutes, and addressed "problems of the Amazonas." Lula said he intends to expedite the meetings with the military sector in order to become better acquainted with the situation of the military, and to complete the program of government proposed by the PT. After his meeting with the Amazonas military commander, Lula was enthusiastic about the receptivity he found within the military. In exchange for this, he advocated a stronger military presence in the Amazonas: "It is necessary to give the Armed Forces a good structure to maintain the security of our borders and continue with their positive policy of presence in the area," he said.
