930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / German reinsurance chief dies DR Horst Jannott, chief executive of Munchener Ruckversicherung, the world's largest reinsurance company, died unexpectedly on Sunday, writes David Waller in Frankfurt. Dr Jannott joined Munich Re in 1954 and has been chief executive since 1969, only the sixth chief executive since the company was founded in 1880. Gross premium at the group has grown from DM300m when he first started to nearly DM16bn (Pounds 6.7bn) in 1991-92. Dr Jannott, who was 65, was due to step down from his position as chief executive on March 1 to become chairman of the company's supervisory board. Personally self-effacing, Dr Jannott , was a central figure in German business life, an eminence grise who was chairman of four supervisory boards and a member of the supervisory boards of eight large companies, including Degussa, Dresdner Bank, Hoechst, MAN, Mannesmann and Veba. He is to be succeeded as chairman of the board of managing directors (chief executive) at Munich Re by Dr Hans-Jurgen Schinzler, 52, Dr Jannott's deputy, for a number of years and the group's finance director since 1981.
