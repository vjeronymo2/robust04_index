BFN [Text] Shanghai, April 26 KYODO -- The Chinese Province of Sichuan has decided to sell 33 national enterprises with good business performance to overseas bidders, according to a CHINA NEWS SERVICE report. The news agency said the corporations to be sold to bolster them with foreign capital include large-scale firms and belong to various industries such as machinery, electronics, metallurgy and pharmaceuticals. The 33 companies, with a combined workforce of 77,000, hold fixed assets worth 3.6 billion yuan (about 43 billion yen), the agency said. Foreign bidders will be allowed to purchase them outright, partially or through acquisition of equity shares, the agency said. Provincial authorities offered 16 deficit-ridden enterprises for sale to Hong Kong bidders last year but none was purchased.
