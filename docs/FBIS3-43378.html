Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Bank of Zambia has tightened control over the Bureau du Change by asking it to indicate passport numbers of people buying foreign exchange. Bank of Zambia Governor (Thomas Mulansha) says the measure will come into effect tomorrow. The Bureau will not be expected to sell foreign exchange to a person unless his passport bears an immigration reentry stamp and the last purchase of hard currency. Mr. (Mulansha) said the Bureau will be expected to verify each passport to ensure a person is illegible to buy foreign exchange. Presently, Zambian residents are free to buy up to $4,000 of which $2,000 can be used for [words indistinct] and the other $2,000 for purchase of goods.
