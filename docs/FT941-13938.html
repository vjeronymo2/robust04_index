940122 FT 22 JAN 94 / Markets: Card spending up - At a glance Debit cards are widely regarded as a replacement for cheques, according to the Credit Card Research Group, and are used for cheaper purchases than credit cards. The most popular outlets for the use of debit cards are supermarkets, off licences and petrol stations, while credit cards expenditure is more widely spread across the retail sector. Total plastic card spending in the UK was Pounds 49.6bn in 1993 up from Pounds 43.9bn in 1992.
