BFN [Report by Alon Pinkas] [Text] Maj. Gen. Ilan Biran, 48, will be the new OC Central Command as of Sunday. Prime Minister and Defense Minister Yitzhaq Rabin approved Chief of General Staff Lt.-Gen. Ehud Baraq's recommendation to appoint Biran to the post. Biran will replace Acting OC Central Command Maj-Gen. Dani Yatom, who temporarily took over after the death of Maj.-Gen. Nehemya Tamari in a helicopter crash last January. Yatom will return to his post as military secretary to Rabin. Born in 1946 in Qiryat Hayim, Biran was drafted in 1964 and has spent most of his military career in the Golani Brigade. In the Yom Kippur War, Biran was a battalion commander. He later served as commander of the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] central offices school and commander of an armored division. In 1987 he was appointed chief of operations in the General Staff. Biran was promoted to major-general in 1989, together with his appointment to head the Logistics Branch. Biran holds a bachelor's degree in economics and business administration from Bar-Ilan University. He is also a graduate of the US Marine Corps Command and Staff College at Quantico, Virginia. In 1992-93, Biran was on academic leave at the Georgetown University School of Foreign Service. Biran is married and father of three daughters and a son.
