Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Frankfurt, Germany, Feb 18 (AFP) -- Airdrops of emergency aid to the besieged Moslem enclaves in eastern Bosnia are continuing despite the threat of air strikes against Serb artillery around Sarajevo, a spokesman for the operation "Provide Promise" said Friday. "Our operations haven't changed. We are continuing until the UN makes a decision otherwise," a spokesman at the Rhein-Main airbase said. During Thursday night 11 planes dropped 78 tonnes of food, 2.5 tonnes of medical supplies and just under six tonnes of clothing and blankets, Airman Stuart Camp said. Since the start of the operation on Feruary 27, 1993, US, French and German transporter planes have dropped some 16,700 tonnes of food, medical aid and winter supplies, he said.
