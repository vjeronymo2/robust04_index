940707 FT 07 JUL 94 / Arts: Today's Television You could do considerably worse than switch on BBC2 at 7.30 tonight and stay with the network until the end of Newsnight at 11.15. Use the VCR at 8.30 to record the first episode in BBC1's new comedy series The Imaginatively Titled Punt And Dennis Show and when it ends at 9.00 you will be ready to play it back while BBC2 is screening The Travel Show, assuming that you, like me, find travel programmes about as interesting as logarithm tables. The Business (7.30 BBC2) investigates the activities of theatre impresario Bill Kenwright, who began life in the cast of 'Coronation Street' and is now risking a million dollars of his own money taking 'Medea' to Broadway. Hypotheticals (8.00 BBC2) is a new batch of the occasional but long-lasting, and often excellent, ITV series in which professionals role-play some crisis in their world: today, medical ethics. State Of The Ark (9.30 BBC2) is a new series examining zoos, past and present. Newsnight is presented by Jeremy Paxman, known in the business as 'Paxo' because he stuffs politicians (10.30).
