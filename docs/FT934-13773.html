931020 FT 20 OCT 93 / Lloyd's Names see little hope in vote LEADERS of loss-making Names will today seek to adjourn a crucial meeting called to admit corporate investors to the Lloyd's of London insurance market. However, leaders of the lossmakers, who have been collecting proxy votes from the 31,607 Names eligible to vote tomorrow, are playing down their chances of success. 'We have no illusion about the outcome, but we need to put on record the fact that thousands of people feel passionately about this,' said Mr Christopher Stockwell, chairman of the Lloyd's Names Associations Working Party which links 37 groups of lossmakers. Lossmakers are taking legal action to recover more than Pounds 2bn in insurance losses. A vote in favour of corporate capital by Names, the individuals whose assets support underwriting at the market, will trigger plans by merchant banks and securities houses to form more than a dozen investment trusts and companies, which are seeking to raise more than Pounds 1bn in equity capital for Lloyd's. The sponsors of five Lloyd's investment trusts have already issued pathfinder prospectuses, documents used to market their shares among potential investors. Mr Stockwell said the working party was not opposed in principle to admitting corporate investors but felt outstanding legal issues should be settled first. 'We need to see the terms of the litigation settlement before we can judge if the terms for corporate capital are fair. Some action groups wish to press their belief that Lloyd's has got its priorities wrong again.' Mr Stockwell said he had received 'unprecedented assurances and personal commitments' to achieving a satisfactory settlement of litigation from both Mr David Rowland, chairman of Lloyd's, and Mr Peter Middleton, chief executive. Professionals at the market say a vote to adjourn the meeting would be disastrous for the market's image. 'It would be equivalent to putting a big sign up over the city saying 'Lloyd's ungovernable - do not touch with a barge pole',' one insider said.
