941101 FT 01 NOV 94 / World Stock Markets: Brazil Equities in Sao Paulo closed 2.6 per cent down in light trade after higher nominal interest rates and an 8.56 per cent surge in the basket of basic consumer goods in October sparked a late sell-off. The Bovespa index receded 1,297 to 47,979. Turnover was RDollars 220.2m (Dollars 260.3m). Brokers said higher interest rates, combined with worries about inflation, had prompted domestic and foreign investors to cash in profits after the market had risen 13.4 per cent over the last four sessions.
