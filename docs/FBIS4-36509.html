BFN [Text] Mr. Ceslovas Jursenas, speaker of the Lithuanian parliament, and his delegation arrived in Damascus this evening on a four-day visit to Syria. He was received at Damascus International Airport by 'Abd-al- Qadir Qaddurah, speaker of the People's Assembly, and Shakir Is'ayyid, chairman of the Arab and Foreign Relations Committee in the Assembly. Mr. Jursenas said upon arrival that he was glad to be visiting Syria. There have been no parliamentary relations between our two countries previously, he noted. During this visit, we want to get to know Syria first-hand and boost bilateral ties, particularly in parliamentary affairs, Mr. Jursenas told SANA. This is the first time we have visited an Arab state since our independence. Our nation, he added, welcomes contacts and relations with Syria in particular and the Arab states in general. He emphasized that his country is particularly interested in the Middle East and the ongoing peace talks. Lithuania, he stated, supports the return of the occupied territory to its rightful owners. The Lithuanian parliament speaker concluded his statement by saluting Syria and its people.
