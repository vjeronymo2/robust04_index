BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Kipkeyev] [Text] Nalchik 7 May TASS -- Local residents and tourists will be admitted to the Kabardino-Balkarian border zone only on special passes since 1 June 1994. The border zone is a five-kilometer-wide strip between the Georgian border and Kabardino-Balkariya to include the settlements of Elbrus, Verkhny Chekem, Bezengi and Verkhnyaya Balkaria, as well as numerous tourist camps. A resolution to the effect was adopted by the Kabardino- Balkarian government in accordance with the Russian law on state border. It will be implemented by the Nalchik border unit.
