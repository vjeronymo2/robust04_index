Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Brussels, 19 January -- Issues of European security and prospects for Ukraine's cooperation with Western military and political organizations were discussed at meetings between Vasyl Durdynets, first deputy chairman of the Ukrainian Supreme Council, and Willem van Eekelen, secretary general of the Western European Union [WEU]. Mr. Durdynets focused the attention of his interlocutor on the importance of guaranteeing territorial integrity and security of Ukraine as an inalienable condition of peace and stability. Issues of Ukraine's integration into the European economic processes and the necessity of overcoming as soon as possible of Ukrainian artificial isolation from the European and world economic space were discussed at a meeting between Vasyl Durdynets and the leading members of staff of the European Community Commission. Talks with NATO Deputy Secretary General Von Moltke [title as received], members of the staff of the International Secretariat, and representatives of the NATO member states in the Political Committee of this organization were dedicated to Ukraine's participation in the program "Partnership for Peace" and a profound explanation of Ukraine's stand on nuclear disarmament issues. Taking part in the meetings were also the leaders of the Ukrainian president's service for international issues Anton Buteyko and Ukraine's ambassador to Benelux, Vasylenko. This has been reported by the information department of the Foreign Ministry of Ukraine.
