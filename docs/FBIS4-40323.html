BFN [Text] Chisinau, BASAPRESS, 11/6/1994 -- After the Constitution is adopted, it will be necessary to inaugurate a Constitutional Court, declared to BASA Eugen Rusu, chairman of the parliamentary commission for appointments and statutes, mentioning that Moldova has no organ which could assure that the presidential decrees, governmental and other institutions' decisions do not violate the stipulations of the Constitution. He announced that between June 8th and 9th he participated, along with Nicolae Timofte, vice president of the Supreme Court, and Nicolae Uncu, vice-prosecutor of the Republic, at a seminar organized by the CSCE in collaboration with the Romanian Constitutional Court. At the Bucharest seminar, representatives of the constitutional courts of many European countries offered to give consultations on the organization of the Moldovan Constitutional Court. According to Rusu, the former Parliament elaborated a draft law on the Constitutional Court, which is to be sent to the Council of Europe's Commission for Democracy and Justice, whose headquarters are in Venice, to obtain eventual notification, and later to be examined by the Chisinau Parliament.
