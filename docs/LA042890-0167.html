April 28, 1990, Saturday, Valley Edition

ON CAMPUS: CAL STATE NORTHRIDGE;

18 STUDENTS DESIGNING MARS PROBE

Eighteen Cal State Northridge seniors are aiming high and designing a first-of-its-kind space probe that may someday help scientists analyze the atmosphere of Mars.

The students' Project Ares, in conjunction with the University Space Research Assn. and the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is a three-year effort to design a craft capable of sustained flight in the Martian atmosphere.

Project Director Neil Rubin, senior engineering major, said the purpose of the probe is to enable scientists to conduct a detailed survey of Mars in preparation for a possible visit to the planet by astronauts.

When complete, the probe will resemble a sleek, lightweight plane with a 40-foot wingspan. The probe will be powered by lightweight batteries and wing-mounted solar panels. A specially designed propeller will pull the plane through the thin, carbon dioxide Martian atmosphere. The collapsible craft is to be lifted into orbit by a Titan missile.

The students' design and testing work is reviewed by NASA engineers.

The faculty members heading the project are Melvin Epstein, a professor of mechanical engineering, and Timothy Fox, who chairs the CSUN mechanical engineering department.

The project, which only seniors are eligible to work on, will be continued by a new group of students each fall semester.
