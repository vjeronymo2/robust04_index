Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 24 KYODO -- The government should compile new economic pump-priming measures before the scheduled Japan-U.S. summit in Washington on February 11, Japan's Vice Trade Minister suggested Monday [24 January]. Hideaki Kumano, vice minister of international trade and industry, told reporters that the U.S. Government should be informed of the contents of the economic stimulus package before the summit between Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa and U.S. President Bill Clinton. Kumano said his ministry and the Finance Ministry are holding working-level talks to coordinate views on the package. The package is expected to include major income tax cuts and an expanded budget for fiscal 1994, which begins next April 1.
