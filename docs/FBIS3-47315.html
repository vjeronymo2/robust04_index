Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From Tokyo NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN in Japanese 11 January Evening Edition] [Text] At a news conference held after the cabinet meeting on 11 January, Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata commented on the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks, where the deadline to reach agreement on 11 February (the Japan-U.S. summit meeting is scheduled on this date) is only a month away. He said: "From the point of view of improving the Japanese people's quality of life, it is important that Japan deals with economic issues voluntarily." He stressed Japan should actively promote more transparency in its market by deregulation. On the other hand, he also indicated that Japan would continue to ask U.S. companies to improve their competitiveness and exert more sales efforts, saying: "It is necessary not only for the buyers, but also the sellers (the United States) to be well-prepared."
