BFN [Text] Seoul, June 11 (XINHUA) -- Jimmy Carter, a former U.S. president, will visit South Korea and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) next week in an attempt to find a solution to the nuclear impasse on the Korean peninsular [as received], official sources disclosed here Saturday [11 June]. A U.S. Administration official in Washington stressed that Carter is making a private visit and will not formally represent the U.S. Government. Carter will arrive in Seoul next Tuesday for talks with South Korean President Kim Yong-sam. He will leave for DPRK on Wednesday and return to Seoul on June 18 to reveal to President Kim the results of his talks with DPRK leaders. Carter said on Thursday: "My hope during this private visit is to discuss some of the important issues of the day with leaders of the area." Carter has been active in resolving conflicts in Panama, Nicaragua and Sudan, among others.
