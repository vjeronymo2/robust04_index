Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan is considering closing down its embassy temporarily in Kabul following yesterday's incident there. Law Minister Syed Iqbal Haider in a statement in the Senate today assured the members that a decision in this regard would be made after reviewing all aspects of Pakistan's relations with Afghanistan. He expressed the government's resolve that criminal activities will not be allowed to affect the close brotherly relations between the two countries. The law minister told the house that Pakistani foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, who is in Kabul, raised the issue to Commander Ahmad Shah Mas'ud, who is holding military control over Kabul. Mas'ud expressed regret over the incident and inspected the embassy along with the foreign minister at midnight last night.
