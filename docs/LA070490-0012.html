July 4, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: POP/ROCK

Luther Campbell's Greatest Hit: "Banned in the U.S.A.," the new Luther Campbell single with 2 Live Crew being released today by Luke Records, has garnered the largest advance orders for a single in the history of Atlantic Records. Mark Schulman, Atlantic senior vice president and general manager, said he expects the single, which contains a melody of Bruce Springsteen's "Born in the U.S.A.," to rapidly reach platinum record status. 2 Live Crew, whose last album has been banned by federal court order in several Florida counties, went back to court Monday to film parts of a video for the upcoming album, "Banned in the U.S.A," which is due for release later this month. Also, formal charges were filed Tuesday against three members of the Miami rap group who were arrested after their adults-only performance on June 9 in Hollywood, Fla., and also against a store owner who sold their "As Nasty as They Wanna Be" album to an undercover detective on June 8. GREG BRAXTON
