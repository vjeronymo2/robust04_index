Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ahvaz, Khuzestan Prov., Jan. 3, IRNA -- One hundred Iranians residing in Iraq have crossed the border into the country via Khuzestan over the past nine months. An informed source said here today that more and more Iranians were coming home from Iraq through Khuzestan. He said permission to return home was issued after necessary interrogations. The group were the Arab citizens of Dasht-e Azadegan region here in this province kidnapped by Iraqi forces at the outset of the Iraqi imposed war (1980-88). He put the number of those kidnapped at 5,000. Most of the Iranians hold no identification card and are in need of accommodation and living requirements, the official said.
