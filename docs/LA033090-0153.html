March 30, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

TRACK AND FIELD ROUNDUP;

SADDLEBACK DEALS CORONA DEL MAR RARE DEFEAT

The Corona del Mar High School boys' track and field team lost its first Sea View League dual meet in seven years when Saddleback beat the Sea Kings, 95-41, Thursday afternoon at Corona del Mar.

Corona del Mar's last league loss was to Estancia in 1983. Saddleback had not defeated Corona del Mar in a dual track meet since 1977.

"Actually I was a little disappointed that Corona del Mar wasn't as strong as I thought they would be," said Saddleback Coach Jim Knapp, whose team drenched him with a bucket of water upon winning the meet. "It is something, though. We're excited."

Saddleback swept the discus, with Modesto Tavares winning at 142 feet 6 inches. In the distance events, the Roadrunners' Robby Price won the 1,600 in 4 minutes 27 seconds, and was second in the 3,200 (10:21.7) to teammate Joe Heredia (10:21.5).

In the sprints, Saddleback's Peter Colthirst won the 400 (51.7) and was second in the 200 (23.0); Corona del Mar's Mark Barnum won the 100 (10.9) and the 200 (22.7). Corona del Mar's only sweep was in the pole vault, where Keith Miller won in a school-record 15 feet. Miller broke his record of 14-6 set two weeks ago.
