Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Taranov] [Text] Moscow January 24 TASS -- A high-ranking Georgian official welcomed the upcoming signing of the friendship treaty between Russia and Georgia as an event of paramount importance for Georgia. "The treaty of friendship, good neighbourly relations and cooperation between the Russian Federation and the Republic of Georgia which is expected to be signed during the first official visit to Georgia by the Russian president on February 3-4 will be epoch-making for Georgia," Georgian Ambassador to Russia Valeryan Advadze said. In an interview with ITAR-TASS on Monday, Advadze said "we have gone a long way while preparing the treaty. We have overcome many obstacles on our way. But today we are on the last lap. This has become possible due to the efforts of Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze." He stressed that Yeltsin's visit to independent Georgia will signify that "it has been recognised by the great neighbour." "The upcoming visit of the Russian president to Georgia will help restore human relations between the people of the two countries," the ambassador said. During the visit, the two leaders also plan to sign agreements on trade and economic cooperation, on the status of Russian border troops in Georgia, on cooperation in transportation, on the resettlement of refugees and other documents.
