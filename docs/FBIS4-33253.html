BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 2 June. Unemployment in Montevideo Department stood at 8.7 percent in the period extending from February to April 1994, thus registering a slight increase compared with the first quarter of the year, in which the unemployment rate was 8.1 percent. (Montevideo EL PAIS in Spanish 27 May 94 Market Indicators Section p 1) The Central Bank reported on 26 May that the central government registered a $22.6 million surplus in the first two months of the year, thus reversing last month's negative result. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 27 May 94 p 11) According to figures disclosed by the Central Bank on 26 May, the trade deficit continued to grow in March, totaling $741.2 million over the last 12 months, which represents 5.6 percent of GDP. In the first quarter of the year, the trade deficit was 55 percent higher than the corresponding period last year, amounting to $175 million. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 27 May 94 p 10) According to information disclosed by the National Statistics Institute on 27 May, salaries declined in real terms in April, both in the private and public sectors, by 0.9 and 2.8 percent respectively. Despite this decline, salaries in the Civil Service increased by 1.2 percent in real terms in the first four months of the year. Salaries in the private sector, however, declined by 0.3 percent in the said period both in Montevideo and in the interior. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 30 May 94 p 9) Wholesale prices went up 2.6 percent in May, thus totaling 11 percent so far this year and 30.9 percent over the last 12 months. This information was released by the Central Bank on 30 May. The items that registered the highest increase were oil by-products (7.2 percent), nonmetal minerals (4.8 percent), and clothing (4.5 percent). (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 31 May 94 p 8) Uruguay's trade with its partners from the Common Market of the South, Mercosur, grew by 19 percent in the first quarter of the year compared with the corresponding period last year, thus representing 47 percent of Uruguay's overall trade. Uruguay's leading trading partner is Brazil, whose exports to our country amounted to $157.7 million in the first quarter, thus registering a 29 percent growth compared with 1993. Uruguay's trade deficit with its Mercosur partners added up to $122.4 million in the said period. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 31 May 94 p 8)
