940511 FT 11 MAY 94 / Convenience food set for bigger role Convenience foods, private healthcare and home shopping are the sectors most likely to benefit from further demographic and social changes before 2000, according to Mintel. Meanwhile, rock concerts, pub drinking and purchases of red meat are all set for decline, says the report on consumer habits. Three groups will make up a larger proportion of the population: families, in particular those with children aged between five and 14; 'empty-nesters', whose children have left home; and elderly people, especially those above the age of 75. For the food and drink markets, the ageing population and increase in single-parent households - for whom time is at a premium - will mean the increased use of convenience foods. The decline in numbers of young people and increase in adults with young children will lead to more home consumption of alcoholic drink and lower pub attendance, says the report. The increase in numbers of young children will have other consequences: 'Within the home, DIY activities will increase in popularity, as children necessitate more decoration work.' For the financial services market, there will be an overall growth in segmentation between families and 'empty-nesters', the group forecasts. It concludes: 'Consumers are likely to become more financially sophisticated over the coming decade, with stocks and share ownership and unit-trust ownership rising. 'Hand in hand with this will go a greater provision for personal pensions and high-interest building society and bank accounts.'
