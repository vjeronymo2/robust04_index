BFN [Text] The presidential council of the Croat republic of Herceg-Bosna confirmed today that talks between Croat and Bosnian Muslim representatives will resume in Vienna Saturday. They will talk about the implementation of the Washington agreement. Zdenko Vranjes reports from Mostar: [Vranjes] They will talk about territorial demarcation and the appointment of top-level officials in the Bosnia-Herzegovina federation, the Presidential Council announced. The delegation of Bosnia-Herzegovina Croats at the talks will include Kresimir Zubak, president of the Presidential Council; Mile Akmadzic, minister for interrepublican cooperation of the Croat republic of Herzeg-Bosna; Zoran Buntic, chairman of the Legislative Council; and Ante Markotic, head of the expert commission for cantonal demarcation and formulation of the principles of the federation's territorial organization. Kresimir Zubak sent a letter to Haris Silajdzic. In it he tells Silajdzic that the Bosnian representatives from Vares municipality on the subcommittee for implementation of the Washington agreement are obstructing implementation of the agreement in this area. Zubak demands that Silajdzic exert his influence on the Bosnian side so that they, too, will implement the agreement at municipal level.
