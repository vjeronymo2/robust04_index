BFN [Text] Moscow 7 May TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin has called for worldwide celebrations of the 50th anniversary of the victory over the Nazi in the World War Two and declare 1995 the year of memory of the WWII victims. Yeltsin said the United Nations should urge all countries, their parliaments and governments to declare 8-9 May, 1995 days without shooting. In his letter to the United Nations released by the presidential press service on Saturday, Yeltsin said that "the importance of the great victory won by joint efforts of the people of various countries cannot be tarnished even by time. One of its main lessons -- in order to defend peace, all peace-loving forces must act jointly, concertedly and actively." "It is important, especially now, because there is still a possibility of armed confrontation between countries, blood continues to flow in civil, inter-ethnic and local conflict, the danger of the spread of weapons of mass destruction is growing," he said. Russia believes that "it is in the interests of mankind to actively help step up the role and increase the effectiveness of the United Nations -- as a pivotal element of the system of collective security -- in pursuing the lofty goal proclaimed in its charter -- "to save the succeeding generations from the scourge of war."
