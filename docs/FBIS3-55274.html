Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Kirill Varov report: "Shumeyko Remains a Favorite"] [Text] Among the outgoing vice premiers in the government undergoing reformation Vladimir Shumeyko, who is considered by many a potential candidate in the future presidential elections, may receive the best possible "employment." It is known that Vladimir Filippovich does not intend to renounce the honor of running for the post of speaker of the upper parliament chamber -- the Federation Council -- if his candidacy is proposed by the president. It is another matter whether he has any real chances of getting a vote of confidence from his colleagues in the chamber. The lineup of forces in the Federation Council appears to be far more enigmatic than in the State Duma, where partisan colors are much brighter and more definitive. In the Federation Council, on the other hand, according to preliminary estimates, Russia's Choice has six mandates, the communists about 12, while the bulk of the "senators," including almost 140 people, is not affiliated with any movement or bloc. Or rather they belong to the party of Russian bureaucracy, being officials of various ranks, and therefore it is quite natural that the great communicator Shumeyko, deprived of apparent extreme characteristics, has good chances of coming to terms with his colleagues in the vast Russian apparatus. Although, to be sure, there are various options....
