Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 7 KYODO -- The Transport Ministry said Monday [7 March] it has granted a certificate that allows Swedish automaker Volvo to sell more than 2,000 cars a year in Japan. Ministry officials said the type certificate is for six models and is necessary for automakers selling more than 2,000 vehicles annually in Japan. Volvo is the first Swedish automaker to obtain such a license, they said. The officials said the six models are all right-hand drive versions designed for the Japanese market. They said the Swedish automaker plans to sell about 8,000 cars in Japan annually.
