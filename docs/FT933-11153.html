930730 FT 30 JUL 93 / International Company News: European side hits Walt Disney WALT DISNEY, the US entertainment group, reported a 10 per cent improvement in underlying third-quarter net income in spite of a significant loss on its investment in Euro Disney in the quarter. The company warned that Euro Disney expects to post a loss in the fourth quarter, which would affect Walt Disney's fourth-quarter results. The investment in Euro Disney resulted in a loss of Dollars 30.9m in the quarter. Its equity share of Euro Disney's loss was partly offset by royalties and other income related to the investment. Stripping out new accounting methods for pre-opening costs, the investment in Euro Disney would have resulted in a loss of Dollars 48.8m in the quarter. Walt Disney's income for the quarter to June 30 was Dollars 259.1m, or 48 cents a share, compared with Dollars 220.8m, or 41 cents, a year earlier. Revenues rose 3.2 per cent to Dollars 1.94bn. Stripping out the impact of accounting changes, net income rose 10 per cent to Dollars 242.7m, or 45 cents, in the latest quarter while operating income was 14 per cent higher at Dollars 477.2m. For the first nine months, Disney's net income fell 36 per cent to Dollars 377.6m, or 69 cents, including accounting changes. A year earlier, it earned Dollars 593m, or Dollars 1.11. Stripping out accounting changes, net income rose 19 per cent to Dollars 707.9m, or Dollars 1.30. Revenues hit a record Dollars 6.35bn.
