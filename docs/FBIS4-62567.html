BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Andrey Palaria and Mikhail Shvetsov] [Excerpts] Moscow, 17 May -- "The problem of Nagorno-Karabakh is unlikely to be resolved if the leaders of the conflicting sides do not have the political will," a high-ranking Russian Foreign Ministry official told ITAR-TASS today. [passage omitted] ITAR-TASS has learned that the talks between the Azerbaijani and Armenian defense ministers and the commander of the Nagorno-Karabakh army of self-defense forces, which began on 16 May, were resumed at 1000 this morning at the Russian Defense Ministry. Experts were instructed last night to finalize the final document on a cease-fire and disengagement of the sides as the first stage in the settlement of the Karabakh conflict and prepare it for signing. However, according to reports from Stepanakert today, the Azerbaijani Army, despite yesterday's accord on a cease-fire effective 0000 on 17 May, fired mortars on Karabakh positions north of Mardakertskiy Rayon and on the Agdam-Askeran stretch. ITAR-TASS has received no information on the situation in the conflict zone from Azerbaijani sources or on the reaction of the Azerbaijani leadership to yesterday's accord in Moscow.
