BFN [Text] Referring to the Georgian head of state's press service, the CONTACT news agency reports that yesterday, Georgian head of state Eduard Shevardnadze met Operia Mindit, a director of a group of Indian companies, and representatives of Georgian business circles. The conversation touched upon issues of establishing and developing economic cooperation between Georgia and India. The visitors said that it would be advisable if a Georgian representation and an Indian-Georgian center for trade, culture, and friendship opened in India. The conversation also touched upon credits to be allotted as humanitarian aid, cooperation in the spheres of sea and air transport, as well as [word indistinct] and food production. Shevardnadze thanked the visitors for the interest they demonstrated toward Georgia and said that relations between Georgia and India have centuries-long traditions. Prospects for the extension of the relations have now emerged.
