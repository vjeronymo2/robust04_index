931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / International Company News: Rhone-Poulenc sell-off launched THE FRENCH Government yesterday launched its second big privatisation, announcing that investors could start reserving shares in Rhone-Poulenc, the chemicals group from today. The sale of the government's 43 per cent stake should take place within the next few weeks. Mr Edmond Alphandery, the economy minister, said that the successful privatisation of Banque National de Paris earlier this month augured well for the sale of Rhone-Poulenc. The public offer for BNP was more than five times subscribed and the institutional tranche was more than 12 times subscribed. The Rhone-Poulenc issue, which is expected to raise FFr15bn-FFr16bn (Dollars 2.7bn-Dollars 2.9bn), is aimed at individual investors. Of the 79.3m shares that the government will sell, 47.6m will go to a public share offering for French and EC citizens and there will be a placement of 31.7m shares for French and international institutional investors. The public share offering will last for six trading days after the price is set. Private investors subscribing to the public offer will be given priority for up to 60 shares each. If they pay for their shares through the conversion of Balladur bonds, a government bond issue launched in the summer, they will have priority for a further 60 shares. Analysts predicted a smooth sale of the Rhone-Poulenc stake. It is a much smaller operation than BNP, which raised FFr34bn, and the chemicals group has been partially privatised through a series of share placings, the most recent of which was in January. 'It is unlikely to be as much as a giveaway as BNP,' said an analyst, 'But it it will probably be attractively priced.'
