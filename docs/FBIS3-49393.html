Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pristina, January 8 (ATA) -- The Council for the Protection of Human Rights and Freedoms in Pristina has reported that during 1993 there were 13,431 cases of police violence against the Albanian population in Kosova. As a result of this abusive police action, fifteen Albanians were killed and fourteen wounded. Last year, 2,305 people were arrested, 62 punished for minor infringements, and 49 sentenced for felonies. The police carried out raids on 1,994 families, and 849 persons were subjected to interrogation by the Serbian police. 1,777 persons were maltreated physically, and 794 people suffered other sorts of abuse from the Serbian authorities. During this period, there were 391 cases of property being forcibly taken by the Serbian forces. There were 172 cases of using violence against children. During last year in Kosova, 68 people were forced from their jobs, and 53 Albanian families were evicted from their homes. Finally, there were 155 cases of violence against women.
