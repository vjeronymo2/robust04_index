BFN [Text] Geneva, 7 Jun (TANJUG) -- Serb Krajina President Krajina Milan Martic today told the TANJUG reporter in Geneva that negotiations on economic issues between Croatia and the republic of Serb Krajina would be held on 16 and 17 June in Plitvice. This has been agreed with the Croatian side, Martic said. This afternoon he talked in the Palace of Nations with special UN envoy Yasushi Akashi and cochairmen of the Conference on the Former Yugoslavia Owen and Stoltenberg. Martic said that in Plitvice, talks will take place on economic issues regardless of the unresolved political problems with Croatia. He confirmed that there was full solidarity between the people of the republic of Serb Krajina and their brethren in the republic of Srpska and added that the Sava corridor was of vital importance to them. The president of the republic of Serb Krajina also said that his delegation might talk to UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali tomorrow in the Palace of Nations in Geneva. At a later news conference, Owen and Stoltenberg described today's talks as a small step in the right direction and added that the next round of talks with the leaders of the republic of Serb Krajina would take place on 30 June. They did not specify the site or the agenda.
