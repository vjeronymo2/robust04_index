Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Peruvian economic developments monitored through 8 February. Today, the director of the National Statistics and Information Technology Institute said inflation for January was 1.84 percent. This, he notes, indicates that inflation "continues to go down." The sectors that showed higher increases in January were rent, fuel, electricity, transportation, communication, and health care services while those that increased but not as much were food, beverages, entertainment, and cultural services. (Lima Global de Television Network in Spanish 0100 GMT 2 Feb 94) On Thursday, the Peruvian Central Reserve Bank reported that as of 25 January, Peru's international reserves increased to $2.847 billion. This is $146 million more than the amount registered at the end of December. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1627 GMT 3 Feb 94)
