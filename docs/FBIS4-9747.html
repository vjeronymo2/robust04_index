BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 31 March. The executive branch approved through Supreme Decree No. 07-94-RE an agreement with the ROK Government on investments promotion and protection. This ratifies an agreement signed in June 1993 in Seoul. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 21 Mar 94 p B2) Three new banks, the Solventa Bank, the Labor Bank, and the El Libertador Bank, are awaiting the Banking and Insurance Superintendency's authorization to begin operations in Peru. The new banks are supported by Chilean investors. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 21 Mar 94 p A1) One year after recommencing exploratory drilling activities in the jungle, Petroperu, State Petroleum Agency, again struck oil in lot No. 8 of the Pavayacu and Chambira projects, with a daily production rate of 6,000 barrels of oil. Lot No. 8 is located in the northwestern region of the Amazon basin. The Pavayacu discovery in lot No. 147D will allow the extraction of 4,000 barrels of oil daily while the Chambira discovery in lot No. 123XCD will allow the extraction of 2,000 barrels of oil daily. Four companies operating on the continental shelf, the northern off-shore area, and the jungle, are producing about 130,000 barrels of oil daily while the consumption rate totals 150,000 barrels of oil daily. In the 1970's, daily oil production totaled 200,000 barrels. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 22 Mar 94 p 5) Manuel Vasquez Perales, the Financial Development Corporation board of directors chairman, announced on 18 March that two credit lines totaling $115 million have been approved for this year. A $90 million credit line was secured from the Andean Development Corporation for small- and medium-size enterprises. A $25 million credit line will be used to support the transformation of private companies and companies being privatized. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 19 Mar 94 p 3) The World Bank on 19 March announced the approval of a $150 million loan for Peru to finance a transportation reconditioning project for highways. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 19 Mar 94 p 1) Southern Peru Copper Corporation, SPCC, secured a $50 million loan from Japan's Mitsui and Co. Ltd. for a $300 million SPCC investment program to increase copper production and improve SPCC operations with an emphasis on environmental protection. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 20 Mar 94 p A6)
