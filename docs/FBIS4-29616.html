BFN [Text] Indonesia has expressed its full support to the United Nations Security Council resolution No. 924 calling for an immediate end to the supply of arms and other forms of assistance to any group that is involved in the armed conflict in Yemen. This is aimed at preventing the spread of the civil war in that country. According to a press statement released by the Foreign Affairs Department in Jakarta today, Indonesia is reiterating its call to those involved in the conflict to immediately stop the exchange of fire and make efforts to seek a peaceful solution to the civil war in that country. The statement says Indonesia is concerned at the continuing armed conflict there, which has resulted in an increasing number of casualties and damage to property.
