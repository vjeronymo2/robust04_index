BFN [Text] Belgrade, 22 May (TANJUG) -- The UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR] command in former Bosnia-Herzegovina has officially informed the republic of Srpska army's main headquarters that the Muslim side did not sign an annex to the agreement on the cease-fire and cessation of hostilities within the 3-km zone around Gorazde which was supposed to come into effect at 1800 this evening. This was reported by the information service of the republic of Srpska army's main headquarters late this evening. According to a public statement received by TANJUG, the information service points out that the annex to the agreement was signed by the republic of Srpska army and UNPROFOR representatives but that it cannot be implemented because the Muslim side rejected it. "By refusing to sign the agreement mentioned above, the Muslims have assumed all responsibility and once again confirmed their military option for resolving the crisis on the territory of the former Bosnia-Herzegovina," the statement issued by the information service of the main headquarters of the republic of Srpska army said in conclusion.
