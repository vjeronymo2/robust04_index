BFN [Excerpts] Warsaw, 20 Jun--During a news conference on 20 June, Jerzy Szmajdzinski, general secretary of the Social Democracy of the Polish Republic, said that, according to Democratic Left Alliance [SLD] estimates, the number of local councillors from that party increased tenfold after the 19 June local elections. There were 758 councillors during the previous term. The SLD won the largest number of seats (over 50 percent) in Wloclawek, Sosnowiec, and Czeladz. [passage omitted] Szmajdzinski said that, in 29 towns where proportional elections took place, the SLD won 30 percent of the seats, which was comparable with the parliamentary election results. The SLD won 40-50 percent of seats in 10 cities, including Lodz, Zielona Gora, Bydgoszcz, Dabrowa Gornicza, Grudziadz, and Lublin.
