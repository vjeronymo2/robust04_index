Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] The foreign ministers of the Nordic countries support the plan to create a UN battalion from the Baltic states and could help train such a battalion and provide it with everything necessary. During a meeting of the Nordic foreign ministers in Stockholm, Swedish Foreign Minister Margaretha af Ugglas said that the Baltic states have expressed a wish to participate in the Partnership for Peace program and to create a UN battalion. The Nordic countries are already training officers of the Baltic states for this job. According to Danish Foreign Minister Niels Helveg Petersen, Denmark has invited representatives of the Baltic states to a meeting in Copenhagen on 9 February. I would also like to discuss the place of the Baltic states in the Partnership for Peace program, he said. The Nordic foreign ministers have expressed the hope in a joint statement that an agreement will be reached in the near future on the withdrawal of the Russian Army from Latvia and Estonia. The ministers also stressed that all countries should respect the sovereignty and independence of the Baltic states. The Baltic states should determine their own foreign policy direction without outside pressure.
