BFN [Text] Communist parties of the former Soviet republics are about to adjourn after a two-day closed-door conference at Gorkiy Leninskiye near Moscow. Chairman Oleg Shenin of the Council of the Union of Communist parties -- the Communist Party of the Soviet Union told Interfax Communist parties of all the former Soviet republics, excluding Kazakhstan, attended the conference. Russia was represented by the Communist party of the Russian Federation (Gennadiy Zyuganov), the Communists' Union (Aleksey Prigarin) and the Russian Communist Workers' party whose delegation was led by First Secretary Viktor Tyulkin. The leader of the party's Moscow organization Viktor Anpilov took no part in the conference. According to Shenin, the Communist parties exchanged views in a constructive and open spirit. They agreed the union of their republics must be restored and, Shenin said, they must coordinate action. The conference discussed the possibility of holding a second congress of the peoples of the Soviet Union in the fall of 1994. The event would be expected to focus on the restoration of the unitary state. The conference is to close with decisions on the celebration of the 50th anniversary of the Soviet Union's victory in World War II and Lenin's 125th birthday anniversary.
