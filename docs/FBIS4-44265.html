BFN [Unattributed article: "Rwanda/Italy: Berlusconi on Conditions for Involvement"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, June 23 -- Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi on Thursday [23 June] reiterated that Italy would join an international peace-keeping force in Rwanda "only on certain conditions," including United Nations involvement and an agreement between the warring sides. "If these conditions do not exist," Berlusconi told reporters after a cabinet meeting, "Italy will intervene with humanitarian action outside Rwandan territory." An Italian peace-keeping contingent in Rwanda would involve 450 troops. Defense Minister Cesare Previti said Italian collaboration with France, which plans to deploy an interposition force of 2,000 troops, is possible only "within the limits of humanitarian and logistical contributions."
