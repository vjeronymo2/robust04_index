930820 FT 20 AUG 93 / Privatised utilities attacked over pay LABOUR YESTERDAY stepped up its attack on the level of executive pay increases in the privatised utilities and called for a 'new era' of responsibility in boardrooms, David Owen writes. Mr Gordon Brown, shadow chancellor, said the highest-paid directors of companies privatised under the Conservatives had benefited from 'enormous' salary and perks increments of up to 95 per cent in the past financial year. Such increases could not be justified after industry had been hit hard by the recession, Mr Brown said. He criticised 'massive double-your-money share option awards' which he said had been given in the privatised industries.
