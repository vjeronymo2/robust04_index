940916 FT 16 SEP 94 / Rivers body warns over grant cuts Further cuts in government grants for the National Rivers Authority would cause it 'severe difficulties', Lord Crickhowell, the chairman, said yesterday. His comments came as he presented the NRA's annual review, which said that it was covering more of its costs by charging for services. But some activities had to be funded by government, for example fishery conservation and prevention of poaching. Mr Ed Gallagher, chief executive, said the government grant had fallen from Pounds 70m last year to Pounds 68m this year. There were indications that it would be cut to Pounds 60m next year. He said: 'This is about as far as we can go. If it went any lower it would affect our ability to do our work.'
