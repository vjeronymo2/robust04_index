BFN [Report by Irma Franco] [Text] Foreign Cooperation Minister Erwin Kruger reported that during the first working meeting between the Taiwanese president's delegation and the Chamorro cabinet officials from both nations acknowledged the economic progress made in four years of government with the support of the Republic of China and the pardoning of 75 percent of Nicaragua's debt. This working meeting was attended by the minister-president of the Council of Economic Planning and Development, the president of the Federation of Industries and Chambers of Commerce, and the minister-president of the Central Bank of Taiwan. Social Action Minister William Baez, the Central Bank president, the foreign relations minister, and the economy minister represented Nicaragua. Among the topics discussed were the structural adjustment, economic stabilization, foreign cooperation, continued support, and the market economy. Kruger affirmed that a document pardoning 75 percent of Nicaragua's debt with Taiwan and outlining cooperation for the 1994-96 period will be signed today. The cooperation accord will be defined in this second round of meetings. Social Action Minister William Baez said the meeting was fruitful because the economic cabinet was able to explain the problems with the debt payment. He added that the Taiwanese officials expressed their firm interest in continuing to support Nicaragua. Baez asserted that the issue that most impressed the Taiwanese delegation was the 20-percent growth in the agricultural sector, a figure they considered very high.
