BFN [Text] Cairo, 6 Apr (MENA)--President Husni Mubarak received Spanish Foreign Minister Javier Solana at the Presidential Palace at noon today. Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa and the Spanish ambassador in Cairo also attended the meeting. After the meeting, Musa said that the Spanish foreign minister conveyed a message to President Mubarak from Spanish prime minister Felipe Gonzalez dealing with bilateral ties, the peace process, Spain's position on it, and the situation in the Mediterranean, the Middle East, and Europe. He noted that the joint Egyptian-Spanish committee meetings concluded. He said: We studied bilateral ties in the cultural, economic, and trade fields. He expressed his belief that relations between Egypt and Spain are steadily growing. Solana expressed his happiness to have met with President Mubarak. He noted that he discussed with President Mubarak developments in the peace process and the situation in the Mediterranean. He said that a conference on cooperation among Mediterranean countries will be held in Egypt on 1 June. He said that he conveyed an invitation to President Mubarak from the king of Spain to visit Madrid. He said: We hope that we will be able to set a date for the visit in the near future. He noted that his visit to Egypt was extremely successful, noting that he discussed cooperation in various fields. He said views were identical between Egypt and Spain regarding the Middle East and the Mediterranean region and solutions to numerous international problems that concern Spain and Egypt. Asked if he had brought new ideas for the Middle East, Solana said: I hope that by 13 April an agreement will have been reached and that the peace process will proceed in a sound framework. He said that Spain is committed to helping further the peace process, noting the major role Egypt is playing to establish peace in the region.
