Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 10 Jan (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa and his accompanying delegation returned to Cairo this evening from Damascus after a two-day visit during which he participated in the meetings of the foreign ministers of the Damascus Declaration countries, which concluded earlier today. He said that the meetings were good and that discussions dealt with implementing recommendations, maintaining the declaration, and acting within its framework. In an arrival statement, he noted that the foreign ministers also discussed the situation in the region and promoting Arab action. On the meeting of the foreign ministers with Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad, Musa said that the eight countries declared their support for the peace process on the Syrian and Palestinian tracks. They expressed their support for the Syrian position, the return of the Golan heights, and continuing efforts within the framework of the peace process as launched in Madrid on the basis of land for peace. Musa noted that during the Damascus meeting participants supported the Syrian stance on future moves and expressed wishes for the success of the upcoming summit between Presidents al-Asad and Clinton in Geneva on 16 January. Musa said that the meetings of the Damascus Declaration countries will continue; the next meeting will be held in Kuwait in six months. He noted that he will submit a report to President Mubarak on the results of these meetings.
