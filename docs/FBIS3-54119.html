Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Cairo, 23 Jan (MENA)--Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi asserted that the government has not and will not keep silent about any incident of corruption, no matter who the perpetrator. Addressing a People's Assembly session held today to debate the report of the committee tasked with replying to the government policy statement, Sidqi said every report on corruption or deviations from any source, even anonymous ones, is passed on for investigation with the knowledge of the authorities concerned. He added that people must not be accused without evidence or according to mere reports because this is regarded as intellectual terrorism. Referring to newspaper reports on corruption cases, he said if such corruption happened in any society, such a society would be barbaric. Speaking about corruption, Khalid Muhyi-al-Din, opposition leader in the People's Assembly, said he did not mean by his speech to accuse anyone and that he knows that Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi is the one who ordered an investigation on Madinat al-Nasr's multimillionaire. He added that he knows the government, including not only the council of ministers but also the administration agency, is opposed to such acts. I do not mean to stand up against anyone or accuse anyone without evidence, he remarked. Kamal al-Shadhili, minister of state for the People's Assembly and consultative council affairs, said corruption is not common practice, and that the prime minister has affirmed that neither corruption nor deviations will be protected and all such cases will be invesitgated.
