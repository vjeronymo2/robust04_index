BFN [Text] Cairo, 14 Apr (MENA)-- The Egyptian force taking part in the UN peacekeeping forces in Somalia checked an offensive by an armed Somali group against a UN ammunition depot southwest of Mogadishu airport on 12 April. An official military source today stated that the Egyptian force fired warning shots to deter the attackers. To deter the repeated attempts against the depot, it had to surround the group members and engaged them in a 35-minute clash, after which they surrendered. The source added that the Egyptian force turned the group members, who numbered 19, over to the Somali police, adding that the Egyptian force sustained no losses. Defense and War Production Minister Field Marshal Muhammad Husayn Tantawi thanked the commander, officers, and soldiers of the Egyptian force in Somalia for their vigilance and good performance. The UN commander in Somalia also thanked the Egyptian force for its distinguished performance in frustrating an attempt to seize the UN ammunition.
