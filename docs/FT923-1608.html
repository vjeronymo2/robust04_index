920922 FT 22 SEP 92 / Crisis in the UK Economy: Tebbit warns of split in party LORD TEBBIT, former Conservative party chairman, yesterday warned that the Maastricht debate could tear the party apart. Speaking on BBC Television's Panorama programme, Lord Tebbit said: 'The prime minister has seen the collapse of his policy towards the parity of sterling. That is sufficient humiliation for a Conservative government in one year. I hope he will not risk a further humiliation by splitting his party in suffering defeat in parliament.' Lord Tebbit described the Maastricht treaty as a dead parrot. 'In short, right now it seems to me that Maastricht is like that famous dead parrot,' he said. 'They may try to nail it on to the perch again, but nobody will believe that it is still alive.' Earlier yesterday Lord Tebbit had renewed his call for a referendum on the treaty, saying the result of the French vote had knocked progress to monetary union 'completely off the rails'. He added: 'Perhaps it is time for the people of Britain to have a say. A referendum on the issue would clear the air and make it much easier for John Major to speak for the country in the certainty that he was speaking for all the people.'
