Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Cuts in Aid to Agriculture"] [Text] Rome, 7 Feb (ANSA) -- Once again the lack of controls has compromised European Community aid to Italy which will receive 586.1 billion lire less from FEOGA, the fund that finances the common agricultural policy. The Agricultural Ministry today noted that the cut came in line with the November 1993 decision on the part of the European Commission to liquidate the 1990 acccounts and stressed that every year Italy suffers the biggest reductions in FEOGA funds because of failure to respect Community controls and the terms within which payments must be made.
