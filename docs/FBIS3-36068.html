Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Boris Krivoshey and Aleksandr Krylovich] [Text] Moscow February 1 TASS -- Russia is satisfied with a report saying the Yugoslav authorities have offered information on their Armed Forces as of January 1, 1994, to CSCE for the first time since disintegration of former Yugoslavia, head of the Russian Foreign Ministry's Information and Press Department Grigoriy Karasin told a briefing here today. A document circulated by Yugoslav representatives in Vienna says the Yugoslav Army does not plan to deploy additional types of heavy armaments or combat systems on Yugoslav territory in 1994, said the Russian Foreign Ministry's spokesman. "Information on location of forces and their armaments against the background of the on-going combat operations in Bosnia and Herzegovina testifies to the Yugoslav interest in military security and stability in the region," said Karasin.
