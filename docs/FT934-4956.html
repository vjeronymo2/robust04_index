931201 FT 01 DEC 93 / International Company News: IBM Japan plans PC unit IBM Japan, a unit of International Business Machines, is to set up a personal computer division next January to oversee its PC business in Japan, a company spokesman said, Reuter reports from Tokyo. The 800-strong division will handle the development, manufacturing, marketing and maintenance services of IBM Japan's PC business, said Mr Tatsuyuki Saeki, who will head the unit to be created on January 1. The new division is aiming for sales of Y130bn (Dollars 119m) in calendar 1994. In two years IBM Japan hopes to emerge as the second largest PC maker in Japan, after NEC, with 15 per cent of Japan's PC market, Mr Saeki said. The plan, said Mr Saeki, is to react more quickly and effectively to the changes in Japan's PC market.
