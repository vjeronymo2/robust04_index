BFN [Text] As the Press Center of the Army of Bosnia-Herzegovina reports, Generals Rasim Delic and Enver Hadzihasanovic, commander and head of the Supreme Command of the Army of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina respectively, had talks in the UN base in Visoko today with General Michael Rose, commander of the UN Forces for Bosnia-Herzegovina, about the implementation of the Geneva agreement and the method of monitoring the cease-fire. During the meeting, the representatives of the Headquarters of the Supreme Command of the Army of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina presented a proposal on the deployment of observers, liaison officers, as well as the way of maintaining mutual communications. It was agreed that in the days to come, heads of headquarters should establish places for deploying observers, as well as other elements significant for monitoring the cease-fire [word indistinct] in the field. Army General Delic insisted that elements for monitoring the cease-fire with the Serbian aggressors side should be established in the same way and that UNPROFOR [United Nations Protection Force] should urgently take more efficient measures to stop attacks at the protected zone of Bihac, as well as to return the population to the right bank of the Drina River, that is, to the protected zone of Gorazde. The (?system of) maintaining the truce on the entire battlefield of Bosnia-Herzegovina must be monitored in the same way and on the basis of the same criteria, Army General Delic said at the end of the talks today, it has been reported by the Press Center of the Army of Bosnia-Herzegovina.
