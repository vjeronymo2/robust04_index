BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 12 KYODO -- South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu and Hans Blix, chief of the U.N.'s nuclear monitoring agency, agreed Tuesday [12 April] that North Korea must permit the completion of inspections initiated last month on its suspect nuclear program. Han, on a weeklong diplomatic mission to Russia, Britain, and Belgium, arrived in Tokyo on Tuesday and met with Blix, director general of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), who is here for a forum starting Wednesday in Hiroshima on the peaceful uses of atomic energy. Blix and Han, during their hour-long meeting, studied Pyongyang's response to the U.N. Security Council's March 31 presidential statement urging North Korea to comply with the nuclear safeguards accord and permit the conclusion of the checks, the South Korean Embassy in Japan said. The IAEA has said two weeks of inspections in March were insufficient to decide whether Pyongyang has diverted nuclear materials for weapons purposes as alleged by the United States and other Western nations. North Korea says it has fully complied with inspection criteria previously agreed upon and maintains its nuclear program serves strictly civilian purposes. The nuclear row will top Han's agenda in Moscow, where he will meet Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and other government leaders from Wednesday through Friday. "I expect Moscow to bring up its earlier proposal for an eight-way conference on the North Korean nuclear issue and I will explain Seoul's position," YONHAP NEWS AGENCY quoted Han as saying before departure. Moscow has suggested that both Koreas, China, the U.S., Russia, Japan, the United Nations and the IAEA try to resolve the nuclear controversy through a special conference. According to YONHAP, Han said Seoul welcomes the Russian proposal but prefers that the U.N. handle the North Korean issue for the time being. Han is scheduled to meet British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd in London on Saturday and will return to Seoul next Monday after attending a two-day conference of South Korean mission chiefs in Brussels.
