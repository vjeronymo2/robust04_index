Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 19 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen met with visiting Italian Senate President Giovanni Spatolini here this afternoon. They exchanged views on bilateral relations and international issues of common concern. Qian said that Sino-Italian ties are close, with frequent high- level contacts, adding that spatolini's current China visit would further promote the relations between the two countries. Spatolini recalled his first China tour in 1985 when he was the italian defense minister, noting that great changes have taken place in Beijing since then. Touching on religious issues, Qian said that China has recently promulgated two regulations on religion, pointing out that the purpose of this move "is to protect the normal religious activities instead of limiting them". Earlier today, Spatolini delivered a speech at Beijing University, where he also received an honorary doctorate in history.
