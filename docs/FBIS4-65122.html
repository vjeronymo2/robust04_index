BFN [Text] There were a few surprises and many confirmations in the new cabinet. Let us quickly run through the new members of Silvio Berlusconi's government. Mauro Lozzi reports: [Lozzi] Among the 25 ministers sworn in this morning before President Oscar Luigi Scalfaro, there are six faces which are completely unknown to the public at large. The new foreign trade minister is a technocrat and his name is Giorgio Bernini. Bernini, 66, is a commercial law professor at the University of Bologna. An expert in arbitration, he gained his seat in the Umbria region running on the Forza Italia ticket. The man in charge of the Universities and Research Ministry is also from Forza Italia. His name is Stefano Podesta and he is 45. He is a professor at Bocconi University in Milan, where he teaches commercial economics. Until 30 April, he sat on the board of the Rizzoli Publishing Company. Altero Matteoli will be in charge at the Environment Ministry. He is 53 and was a company director until he took charge of the Italian Social Movement's organizational sector. He has been a parliamentarian for a long time. For the first time there is a woman in charge at the Agriculture Ministry. Her name is Adriana Poli-Bortone and she is an associated professor of Latin literature at the University of Lecce. She had already been elected on an Italian Social Movement ticket, and in past legislation has espoused the causes of pension rights, the education system, and women's issues. Roberto Radice was elected on a Forza Italia ticket and will be in charge of the Public Works Ministry. He is a businessman and a member of the executive board of the National Confederation of Small Manufacturers [Confapi]. He is new to parliament. Finally we have Antonio Guidi. He is 49, and a surgeon. Guidi will leave a trade union background to take charge of the Family Ministry. Guidi himself is handicapped, and while he was at the Italian General Confederation of Labor he was particularly concerned with the issue of employment for the disabled.
