941019 FT 19 OCT 94 / World Stock Markets: Brazil improves 2.6% Shares in Sao Paulo finished 2.6 per cent ahead after moderate trade, boosted by a new influx of foreign money into the market as bargain hunting took place. The Bovespa index improved 1,252 to 49,096, but in spite of the rally, stocks were still down 10.5 per cent over the month to date. Volume was RDollars 313.4m (Dollars 370.4m). Analysts said the end of a struggle between shorts and longs seen prior to the futures settlements on October 13 and 17 eased the downward pressure on prices. But they remained concerned about the impact of rising inflation on the government's anti-inflation plan. The market upward revised its October inflation forecasts to around 3.0 per cent after 0.8 per cent inflation in September. It was feared that the government would lift interest rates in the short term in an effort to contain a recent consumer surge in spending and hold prices down.
