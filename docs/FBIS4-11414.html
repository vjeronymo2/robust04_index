BFN [Text] The official talks between Cuba and Zambia began today at the Palace of the Revolution. The meetings are being chaired by Commander in Chief Fidel Castro, head of state and government, and by Zambian President Frederick Chiluba. Minutes prior to the conversations, Frederick Chiluba honored our National hero Jose Marti, accompanied by Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina. From today, through 30 April when they leave, the Zambian delegation is scheduled to meet with representatives of the scientific-technical field and other areas of social, economic, and cultural interest. Zambia, a country of the southern African cone, is seven times larger than Cuba, with a population of approximately 9 million. Its principal export is copper.
