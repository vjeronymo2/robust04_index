Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Imamali Rakhmanov, chairman of the Supreme Soviet of the Republic of Tajikistan, paid a short working visit to Almaty, yesterday, during which he had a tete-a-tete conversation with Kazakhstani President Nursultan Nazarbayev. Nazarbayev and Rakhmanov exchanged views on the situation on the Tajik-Afghan border and on ways of reducing the (?tension) in this region in an atmosphere of mutual understanding. Kazakhstan's president has evaluated in a positive manner the Tajik leadership's measures on adjusting the political dialogue with the forces of different political orientation aimed at seeking peace and concord on Tajik soil. While discussing regional issues, the two leaders focused on the necessity of new efforts to reinforce the effectiveness of the CIS peacekeeping forces. The two leaders have confirmed the invariability of the course of both states toward strengthening the independence and expanding the traditional friendly relations between Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. In a separate meeting, the delegatiions of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan discussed a wide range of economic issues, in particular on delivery of goods, ways of regulating mutual payments, and issues related to energy supply. The government delegations of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan have confirmed the joint aspiration for the harmonious and balanced development of all-round relations. A number of economic intergovernmental and interdepartmental agreements were concluded at the end of the negotiations.
