Language: Kyrgyz Article Type:BFN [Text] In conformity with the law of the Kyrgyz Republic on the customs tariff, and in order to further improve the system of the state regulation of foreign trade as well as to create conditions for the development of interstate economic ties, the Government of the Kyrgyz Republic issued a resolution on the introduction of temporary customs tariffs of the Kyrgyz Republic. The temporary customs tariff of the Kyrgyz Republic was confirmed. Special duties have been introduced temporarily as a precaution against participants of foreign economic activity who violate the interests of the Kyrgyz Republic concerning importing of goods in line with the direct exchange of goods -- barter. Tariffs and duties are levied on all exported and imported goods that are included in the appendix to this resolution with the exception of cases envisaged by the laws of the Kyrgyz Republic on customs tariffs and in line with the stipulations of agreements of the Government of the Kyrgyz Republic with foreign countries, companies, and international organizations. Export customs duties should not be levied when the produce is delivered to those countries that have not adopted a decision on such duties or have abolished previous laws on this topic. The Kyrgyz Ministry of Finance charged with levying customs duties in accordance with the procedures stated by the ministry. Export and import customs duties should be paid before or at the moment of showing the goods to the customs. The Kyrgyz Ministry of Finance can grant the postponement of the payment of export duties for a term of up to 60 days from the moment of showing the goods under a bank's guarantee. Payment of duties for imported goods can be put off for a term of 30 days under a bank's guarantee or property collateral. If duties are not paid, goods will be confiscated. Tariff rates can be changed by the government under relevant circumstances. The government sets, cancels, or lifts tariffs at the recommendation of the duties and tariffs' council of the Kyrgyz Republic. Tariff allowances or preferences are granted only by the Government of the Kyrgyz Republic.
