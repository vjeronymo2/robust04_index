931109 FT 09 NOV 93 / World Trade News: Ministers in mission to speed Gatt deal Ministers from four British Commonwealth countries yesterday began an eight-day mission to key world capitals to urge a speedy and successful conclusion of the Uruguay Round of trade liberalising talks, due to end on December 15, Frances Williams reports from Geneva. Mr Branford Taitt, foreign minister of Barbados, told a news conference in Geneva that smaller developing countries such as his own would suffer without a Uruguay Round agreement, which gave them the best hope of fairness and equity in the world trading system. The delegation, which also comprises ministers from Ghana, Malaysia and Mauritius, met Mr Peter Sutherland, director-general of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, before going on to Brussels, Paris, Bonn, Washington, Tokyo and London.
