BFN [By Debbie Kuo] [Text] Taipei, June 27 (CNA) -- Taiwan's U.S.$2.27 billion in exports to Association of Southeast Asian Nations [ASEAN] members in the first quarter of the year more than doubled Mainland China's exports to those countries, the Council for Economic Planning and Development (CEPD) reported Monday [27 June]. Taiwan also imported about 1.5 times more imports from ASEAN as a whole than did Mainland China, purchasing U.S.$1.82 billion worth of goods, the latest CEPD tallies show. ASEAN groups Brunei, Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Singapore. During the first quarter, Taiwan recorded a trade surplus of U.S.$450 million with ASEAN, while Mainland China suffered a deficit of U.S.$60 million. Despite the fact that Taiwan had a larger amount of two-way trade with ASEAN, Mainland China's imports and exports to the region are growing at a faster pace, CEPD officials said. Mainland China managed a 46 percent growth in exports to ASEAN over the first quarter of 1993 to U.S.$1.14 billion, while its imports jumped 35 percent to U.S.$1.2 billion, compared with Taiwan's 9.5 percent and 13.5 percent increases, respectively. Singapore remained the major trading partner of both Taiwan and Mainland China in the January-March period. Mainland China's leading ASEAN trade partners after Singapore were Malaysia, Indonesia and the Philippines, in that order, while Taiwan's were Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.
