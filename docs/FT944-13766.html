941026 FT 26 OCT 94 / UK Company News: Salomon Brothers Salomon Brothers, the US investment bank, has become a substantial preference shareholder in Attwoods, the waste management company fighting off a Pounds 364m bid from Browning-Ferris Industries of the US. After buying an initial 1m preference shares at 88 1/2 p last week, Salomon disclosed yesterday that it had paid a further Pounds 3.2m for 3.64m prefs, at 88p, bringing the its total stake to 7.3 per cent. This is 3p higher than the price offered by BFI's offer. and has left many dealers puzzled as to the bank's motives. Salomon bought some of the prefs from Fidelity Investments, which remains a substantial ordinary shareholder. Yet Under the offer, A successful BFI has would have the right in certain circumstances to demand early conversion of the prefs, or redemption at 100p. once it controlled 75 per cent of the preference shares. While Laidlaw of Canada has already agreed to sell its 73 per cent pref stake to BFI. at 85p. there is no guarantee that BFI would demand redemption.
