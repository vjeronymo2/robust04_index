921214 FT 14 DEC 92 / Key decisions at Edinburgh EC Summit DENMARK: Denmark exempted from key aims of Maastricht treaty - single currency and common defence - clearing way for second Danish referendum next year. BUDGET: Seven-year deal freezing ceiling on resources at 1.2 per cent of GDP for two years with phased increases to 1.27 per cent by 1999. Fund for four poorest states - Spain, Portugal, Ireland and Greece - worth Ecu 15.15bn (Pounds 12.1bn) over seven years. ENLARGEMENT: Negotiations to open from January 1 with Austria, Sweden and Finland. Talks with Norway start later next year. GROWTH: Growth initiative through infrastructure development worth up to Dollars 37bn (Pounds 23.7bn) via lending facility at European Investment Bank and European Investment Fund. SUBSIDIARITY: Procedures to apply principle of subsidiarity, under which EC acts only when member states cannot achieve goal as well themselves. TRANSPARENCY: Some ministerial meetings televised and details of votes published. EC laws made clearer. SITES OF INSTITUTIONS: Sites of existing institutions confirmed. Countries with no EC institution to have priority for new ones MEMBERS OF EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT: Germany receives 18 more members. YUGOSLAVIA: Pressure on Serbia and Bosnian Serbs, systematic rape of Moslem women in Bosnia condemned, Macedonia offered economic aid but not recognition under present name. RUSSIA: Strong support expressed for Yeltsin's reform programme.
