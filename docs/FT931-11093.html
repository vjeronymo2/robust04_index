930204 FT 04 FEB 93 / Arts: Today's Television One of the pleasures of 1993 so far has been The Sexual Imperative (C4, 9.00) which tonight looks at the different strategems within the animal kingdom for rearing one's young. None is more bizarre than that of human society in which sexually mature young are discouraged from getting on with the business for such a long period. The Late Show (BBC 2, 11.15) has boldly decided to go to Scotland once a month. Its opening programme from Glasgow asks pundits to assess the impact of the Gaelic Television Fund on TV North of The Border. Created by the government with Pounds 9.5m, the fund has been asked to treble the gaelic output. There is only one prison in Luxembourg and it is profiled tonight in that first-rate series, The Essential History of Europe (BBC 2, 8.00). Are the affluent Luxembourgois so happy, or does the fact that one third of those in jug are there for drug-related offences signal a certain malaise? The Trouble with Medicine (BBC 2, 9.30) regularly overdoes the foreign angle. Tonight we see how Americans, Irish, and Indians deal with old people (as they used to be known in less mealy-mouthed times).
