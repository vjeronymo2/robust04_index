Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The first 5,000 government and Mozambique National Resistance [Renamo] troops joining the future Mozambican army, particularly its infantry division, begin their training in Dondo, Sofala Province, and in Manhica and Boane, Maputo Province, on 21 February. The government has already submitted the list of its 2,500 men to the British training team. Renamo still has to submit the list of its 2,500 men, but the organization says it will do so by 11 February. This morning's session of the Joint Commission for the Formation of the Mozambique Defense Armed Forces discussed issues relating to the creation of the new Mozambican army -- namely, the type of organization, budget and logistics, and compliance with the dates set for the training of troops. The training of the 5,000 infantry soldiers will be given by instructors trained in Nyanga, Zimbabwe. British specialists will play an advisory role. [passage omitted]
