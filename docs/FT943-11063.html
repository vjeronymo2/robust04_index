940802 FT 02 AUG 94 / World Trade News: Indonesia to upgrade airports Indonesia plans to boost trade links with neighbouring Malaysia, Brunei and the Philippines by upgrading airports and ports in Sulawesi and Kalimantan islands to international standards, Reuter reports from Jakarta. 'The airports and ports in the two islands will be upgraded to accommodate bigger planes and ships,' said an official from the Co-ordinating Ministry for Industry and Trade, adding that the expanded airports would serve international routes to Asia, the US and Europe. The ministry earlier said the Sepinggan airport in Balikpapan, East Kalimantan, would be upgraded with Japanese funding. Japan's Overseas Economic Co-operation Fund would provide Dollars 150m for the project while Dollars 125m would come from the Asian Development Bank for the expansion of the Sam Ratulangi airport in Manado, Sulawesi.
