Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 26 (KCNA) -- A delegation of the South Korean National Democratic Front (Hanminjon) headed by vice-chairman of its Central Committee Hong Chi-yong visited Laos and France, according to radio Voice of National Salvation from Seoul. During its visit to Laos, the delegation had talks with leading Cadres of the Lao People's Revolutionary Party and the Lao Front of National Construction. On the occasion the Hanminjon delegation highly appreciated the achievements made by the Lao People's Revolutionary Party in the efforts to set up a people's democratic system and expressed support to them. The Laotian side highly appreciated the undaunted struggle waged by Hanminjon for independence, democracy and reunification as a patriotic vanguard unit in South Korea and expressed support and solidarity for it. During its visit to France the delegation had conversations with officials of the International Liaison Committee for Reunification and Peace in Korea, the International Committee of Jurists for Democracy and Human Rights in South Korea, the French Action Committee To Support the Independent and Peaceful Reunification of Korea and other international solidarity organisations and French organisations. The Hanminjon delegation expressed thanks to international solidarity organisations and French organisations for their support to the struggle of the South Korean people for national reunification and reached a consensus on the matter of strengthening mutual solidarity. Officials of international solidarity organisations and French organisations expressed full support to the "10-point programme of the great unity of the whole nation for the reunification of the country" put forward by the great leader President Kim Il-song and evinced the determination to direct efforts to the struggle for the realisation of the programme. They expressed positive support to the struggle of Hanminjon and South Korean people for the independent and peaceful reunification of the country.
