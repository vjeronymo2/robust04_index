Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Havana, 18 Jan (PL) -- Cuba will attend an informal meeting of the Nonaligned Movement (NOAL) in Jakarta, Indonesia, from 9 to 11 February to coordinate positions with representatives from 16 countries. A spokesperson for the Foreign Relations Ministry told PRENSA LATINA that the forum could be considered a preliminary phase for the Ministers Conference Summit, which will be held in Cairo, Egypt, from 31 May to 3 June. The spokesperson noted that representatives from Egypt, Zambia, Algeria, Sri Lanka, Cuba, India, and Zimbabwe -- countries that led the movement at different stages -- will be among those attending. Delegations from Chipre, Ghana, Colombia, Malaysia, Chile, Iran, Saudi Arabia, Syria, and Indonesia, the Movement's current president, will also attend in an attempt to outline future strategies for the NOAL. The participants could also try to decide upon a host for the 11th summit.
