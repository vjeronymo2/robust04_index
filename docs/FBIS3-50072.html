Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 24 Jan (TANJUG) -- Following the unanimous election of a 15-strong approval committee, on the basis of whose report the Serbian Assembly is to approve today the mandates of 250 deputies elected on 19 December last year, Vojislav Dimitrijevic, chairman of the constitutive session, called a break until 1400. At the same time, he called on the deputies to submit in writing their reasoned proposals for the new speaker of the parliament, who is to be elected today. Thirty deputies can propose a candidate for the speaker.
