920916 FT 16 SEP 92 / Fowler retorts in neo-fascist row SIR NORMAN Fowler yesterday attacked Mr Tony Blair, the shadow home secretary, as the row over a rightwing pressure group's invitation to two prominent European neo-fascists to address a meeting in Brighton to coincide with next month's Conservative conference took a fresh turn. In a letter to Mr Andreas Whittam Smith, editor of The Independent, the Tory party chairman objected to remarks attributed to Mr Blair published in the newspaper. He said it was 'regrettable' that a senior Labour politician should exploit the 'publicity-seeking antics of an extreme, anti-Conservative group'. He said neither Mr Jean-Marie Le Pen nor Ms Alessandra Mussolini was attending the Conservative conference. 'No-one in Western Goals is known by Central Office to belong to our party.'
