BFN [Report by Totka Ninova] [Text] The two Hungarian tugs Vac and Egzsenek have just set sail from the Vidin bank of the Danube. The two vessels were detained by representatives of the Western European Union, the Bulgarian State Navigation Inspectorate, and Vidin customs. The vessels had to stay there for four days so that they could be provided with the necessary UN permits allowing them to sail to Hungary. The two vessels are towing five barges loaded with 991 tonnes of phosphate.
