930203 FT 03 FEB 93 / International Company News: Strong growth at GTE, Sprint TWO leading US tele-communications companies, GTE and Sprint, yesterday reported double-digit underlying growth in fourth-quarter earnings. Sprint, the third-largest long-distance operator, reported a 19 per cent increase in fourth-quarter net income, from Dollars 98m, or 45 cents a share, to Dollars 117m, or 53 cents, on revenues which rose 10 per cent to Dollars 2.41bn. GTE, the largest local telephone group in the US, reported net income of Dollars 417m, or 44 cents, down from Dollars 515m, or 57 cents, a year ago. However, the 1992 figures included one-time after-tax charges of Dollars 100m, or 11 cents a share, including Dollars 48m for the sale of its electrical products business. Earnings per share from continuing operations, excluding accounting changes and a one-time gain on the sale of cellular properties, rose 10 per cent. GTE reported 1992 income from continuing operations of Dollars 1.79bn, or Dollars 1.95, including a Dollars 137m after-tax charge for a change in accounting for health benefits. In 1991 the company reported income from continuing operations of Dollars 1.53bn, or Dollars 1.69. Revenues totalled Dollars 19.98bn, up from Dollars 19.62bn. Sprint's local telephone operations reported operating income of Dollars 160m, down from Dollars 164m, on revenues up 7 per cent to Dollars 766m. For the full year, Sprint reported net income of Dollars 457m, or Dollars 2.07 a share, compared with Dollars 368m, or Dollars 1.68, in 1991. Revenues were up 5 per cent at Dollars 9.23bn.
