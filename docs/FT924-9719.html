921104 FT 04 NOV 92 / Arts: Today's Television Unless today's episode of The Secret Agent shows signs within the first few minutes of speeding up, many viewers will surely desert. The first of the three episodes was perilously slow, but at least Verloc was finally given his mission (to blow up the Greenwich observatory) and can now set about manipulating Stevie, his wife's cerebrally challenged younger brother. The trouble with Conrad's bitter little tale is that when he wrote it, before the first World War, bomb-throwing anarchists were a significant political consideration (9.25 BBC2). More speed might not go amiss either in Pole To Pole, the series in which Michael Palin is travelling the world from top to bottom. Today he goes from Istanbul to Aswan on the Nile, meeting astrologer Patric Walker (who files his copy to the London papers from a Greek island) and the British garrison in Akrotiri (9.35 BBC1). The documentary series The Kennedys reached JFK's death at the end of the last episode and is now poised to move on to Bobby (10.40 ITV). Cool Hand Luke is a memorable chain-gang movie for several reasons, not least the opening sequence (12.20 ITV).
