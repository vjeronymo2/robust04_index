BFN [Text] Israel has agreed to permit the return of 47 deportees. They will arrive in the territories in two groups. The first group is slated to arrive in Jericho today. Officials in Tunis said that in the next few days, Israel will present a list of another 20 deportees who will be permitted to return. At the same time, Abu-al-'Ala' [Ahmad Quray'], the head of the PLO's Economic Department, said that the Paris negotiations on economic issues will be concluded before 13 April. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports: [Begin recording] [Amir] The first deportees will return to Jericho today via Amman and the Allenby Bridge. A Palestinian source in Tunis yesterday said that Israel had agreed to permit the return of 20 deportees and would present a list of their names to the PLO in the next few days. The source stressed that in his opinion, the new list would also include Akram Haniyah, who was deported from al-Birah in 1988 and has since served as one of Yasir 'Arafat's senior advisers. Speaking over the telephone from Tunis, Ahmad Quray', alias Abu-al-'Ala', the head of the PLO's economic department, said that in addition to the aforementioned 20 deportees, Israel has also authorized the return of 47 deportees. [Abu-al-'Ala', in English] I think about 47. I am not speaking about names; I am speaking about figures. I believe that all of them, if not in this group then in the other groups, they will come. [Amir translates above remarks into Hebrew and continues] As for the economic talks in Paris, Abu-al-'Ala' expressed the assessment that the negotiations with Israel will be concluded before 13 April. [Abu-al-'Ala', in English] I hope, and we will do our best. I believe that we and Mr. Shohat and the delegation, both of us, are concerned to finish this mission immediately so as to come into the implementation of the first stage of the agreement on Gaza and Jericho. [Amir translates above remarks into Hebrew and continues] Jericho today awaits the arrival of the deportees returning across the Allenby Bridge. PLO representatives in the territories are preparing a warm reception; however, the Civil Administration has been instructed by the defense establishment to permit the deportees to enter Jericho only this afternoon in order to keep celebrations to a minimum. The IDF [Israel Defense Forces] will probably close off the Jericho area to the media to prevent any coverage of the deportees' return. The deportees intend to hold a news conference in Jericho. Fatah leaders in the territories, who, on 'Arafat's instructions, have closed most of the PLO offices, are now waiting to turn over supervision of the organization's activists in the field to the first representatives of the PLO leadership in Tunis. The returning deportees will primarily handle the preparations for the arrival of the PLO leaders from Tunis, who are already packed and ready to move to the Gaza Strip and Jericho. Abu-al-'Ala' estimates that the PLO leadership, led by Yasir 'Arafat, will arrive in the territories immediately after the IDF withdrawal and the deployment of the Palestinian police. [end recording] [Tel Aviv YEDI'OT AHARONOT in Hebrew on 5 April in a report by political correspondent Shim'on Schiffer on pages 1 and 19 reports that "in his talks with Israeli officials in Tunis, PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat expressed his desire to pray at the Temple Mount in Jerusalem shortly after he arrives in Jericho in May. It has been learned that Israeli decisionmakers are inclined to accept 'Arafat's request." [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 0500 GMT on 5 April reports that "reacting to Yasir 'Arafat's desire to be allowed to pray at the Temple Mount mosques, Police Minister Moshe Shahal said that in his opinion, the time is not ripe for this. The police minister believes that at this stage, it would be best to put this off to a later time."]
