BFN [Text] Seoul, June 11 KYODO -- South Korean President Kim Yong-sam said Saturday [11 June] relatively lengthy U.N. Security Council discussions on sanctions against North Korea may help Pyongyang return to the negotiating table, Japanese officials said. Kim, meeting Japanese Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa, called such discussions a meaningful process for urging North Korea to make its nuclear program more transparent, the officials said. Kakizawa met the president in the evening and they confirmed the importance of cooperation among Japan, South Korea and the United States on the issue, and of China's role in settling it, they said. Earlier in the day, Kakizawa and South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu held a meeting and confirmed that sanctions against North Korea over the nuclear standoff are aimed at encouraging Pyongyang to hold dialogue for a negotiated solution. The two sides also pledged close bilateral cooperation in dealing with North Korea's suspected nuclear arms development program.
