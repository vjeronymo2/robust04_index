940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television If we worked out how much money we paid in to building societies for our mortgages most of us would presumably abandon the system and go for rented accommodation, or try to raise the money some other way. Taking Liberties looks into a new wave of re-possessions and tells how building societies are refusing to honour promises of a multi-million pound mortgage rescue scheme (8.00 BBC2). Can it really be a good idea for BBC2 to be showing The Lost Steptoes? It is not the poor technical quality that prompts the question but the high quality of the writing. It makes today's comedies look so hopelessly inferior. This week a pregnant woman arrives at the rag and bone yard claiming that Harold is the father of her child (8.30). True Stories comes up with yet another of those documentaries in which the programme makers fail to get an interview with their subject and then try to turn the absence into a virtue. Today's absentee is Margaret Thatcher who was followed on her US book promotion tour but avoided and ignored the cameras at every turn (9.30 C4).
