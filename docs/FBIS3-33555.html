Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Text] The value-added tax, VAT, is still in effect, including the consumer phase of it, Finance Minister Julio Sosa Rodriguez has announced. Guillermo Alvarez Bajares, OCI [Central Information Office] director, has also reaffirmed this announcement. Both officials have stated that the current administration cannot repeal a law via a resolution or decree, adding that VAT was included in the Special Powers Act, which gave special powers to former President Ramon J. Velasquez. [Begin Alvarez recording] Legally, this government cannot modify the VAT Law. This government cannot simply say VAT will be canceled. This situation will be settled when Congress approves the draft bills the government will present, via Dr. Sosa Rodriguez, to meet the same objective. These projects.... [pauses] The economic result of these draft bills, so to speak, will cover the noncollection of VAT. [end recording]
