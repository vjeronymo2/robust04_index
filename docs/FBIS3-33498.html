Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] During four campaign stops in Cali, Liberal Party presidential hopeful Ernesto Samper said that, if elected, he will "pay the debt owed to Colombian women." Speaking to various female audiences ranging from professionals to factory workers, Samper said he would adopt a policy of "positive discrimination" in favor of women whereby a woman would be given preference over a man in situations where equally qualified male and female candidates are being considered for a job or promotion. Samper's seven-point program to benefit women would include: Formulation of a National Development Plan to promote greater opportunities and equality for women; measures to reduce unemployment among women, especially those between 25-35 years of age; a promise that, by the end of his term, women would occupy half of the managerial and advisory positions in his government; flexibility in work schedules to accommodate the needs of working mothers; better training for child-care providers to improve their wage status; more severe punishment for violence against women; and subsidies to help female heads of households. During one address to a group of factory workers, most of whom were single mothers, Samper also said his administration would provide 500,000 subsidized housing units and implement a program of microenterprises for female heads of households. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO 4 Feb 94 p 9A; 5 Feb 94 p 7A; EL ESPECTADOR 4 Feb 94 p 5A)
