940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / World Stock Markets: Emerging markets: IFC weekly investable price indices Emerging markets continue to suffer setbacks, with Greece the latest casualty, writes John Pitt. After a rapid increase in interest rates as the government struggled to defend the drachma, the country's equity market went into reverse. Shares fell a further 2 per cent yesterday, closing at the lowest point this year and down 22 per cent since the start of the year. Little wonder then that some commentators are highlighting potential growth areas in others parts of the world. Kleiman International Consultants notes that and 'acceleration of planned economic liberalisation programmes has in recent months reversed bourse stagnation throughout the Mediterranean region'. Kleiman says securities activity was picking up on the informal Cyprus OTC market, and in Lebanon through the recent over-subscription to Solidere, which plans to rebuild central Beirut.
