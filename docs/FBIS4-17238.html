BFN [Text] Yerevan, 20 April (SNARK) -- The American Government's humanitarian action on the gratuitous distribution of kerosene to the residents in need is over in Armenia. Harry Gilmore, American ambassador to Armenia told SNARK 200 thousand families of the three largest cities of Armenia have got the kerosene. Mr. Gilmore outlined the difficulties they met while the shipment of kerosene through the Georgian territory. He said Georgian authorities don't control the situation in the regions. "American Government would prefer to deliver the kerosene through Turkey," said the ambassador. But the Turkish authorities declared they would give their consent on the shipment, provided [there was] the progress in the settlement of the Karabakh conflict. He confirmed that some families didn't get the kerosene. But he assured that according to the mutual agreement of American and Armenian sides they will get it during the next stage of the distribution in Autumn.
