Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Excerpt] Addressing a lesson on international relations at the Ground War School during a tour of the school today, President Suleyman Demirel noted that the civilized world today, at least in theory, rejects using force to solve problems. Despite this, Demirel remarked, there are incidents in Yugoslavia and the Caucasus. Extreme nationalism, racism, sectarianism, and xenophobia lie at the base of this problem, the president explained. Pointing out that there are 3,000 different languages and 179 countries in the world today, Demirel continued: [Begin Demirel recording] If every different ethnic group and sect claims a different flag and demands independence, then 3,000 states must be established. Geography is also not conducive to this. People live intermingled with one another. People are happy when they live without making an issue of their origins and their beliefs, and when they show tolerance for each other and accept one another. [end recording] Demirel recounted that the Soviet Union, which covered 22 million square km, collapsed, but Russia today includes 70 percent of this territory and its population. Noting that the Red Army is still standing and that there are close to 20,000 nuclear warheads in Russia, Demirel said intensive diplomatic contacts are under way throughout the whole world in order to prevent the reemergence of a military threat and a return to the old days. Demirel concluded by saying Turkey has an important place, name, and stature in international relations as a result of its 60 million population and its sensitive geographical location.
