CSO [Article by A.I. Gurevich, T.A. Kachalina, A.L. Kayushin, M.D. Korosteleva, A.I. Miroshnikov, Bioorganic Chemistry Institute imeni M.M. Shemyakin and Yu.A. Ovchinnikov at Russia's Academy of Sciences, Moscow; UDC 547.963.32.057:577.213.3] [Abstract] The difficulty of chemical enzymatic synthesis and cloning of artificial oligomer genes of short peptides, e.g., oxytocin, by traditional methods, probably due to the fact that recurrent sequences in DNA may cause varying recombinant restructuring, prompted attempts to use different codons in the synthesized structures in order to diminish the homology of individual polynucleotide segments for encoding the same amino acids. To this end, a vector obtained by splitting the pFPCP10 plasmid by the BamHI and EcoRI restrictase was used for cloning the B/E gene and a vector obtained by splitting the pTE2IL3 plasmid by the HindIII and EcoRI restrictase was used for cloning the H/E gene. Deletions caused by ruptures in one of the plasmid DNA chains were noted in the experiment to clone a synthetic oxytocin trimer gene; it is stressed that these deletions are much less frequent when cloning a covalent-closed cyclical two-stranded DNA. These recurrent gene sequence cloning feature may also be observed in other cases. It is speculated that the difficulties related to these deletions or recombinations could be overcome by using the techniques which were verified in this study.
