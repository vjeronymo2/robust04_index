Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, February 19 (ATA) -- The foreign ministers of Albania and FYROM [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia], Mr. Alfred Serreqi and Mr. Stevo Crvenkovski met yesterday before noon in Tirana and expressed their will for the further deepening of the relations between the two countries. They agreed to extend the diplomatic relations and to create a join governmental commission for the intensification of the economical cooperation between Albania and FYROM. The chiefs of the diplomacy of the two countries both accepted the lifting of the visas and the tax customs in the border. They both expressed their will for the accelerating of carrying out the project for the speedway east-west, which will connect Durres, Skopje, Sofia and Istanbul. Ministers Serreqi and Crvenkovski agreed to extend cooperation especially in the fields of culture and energy and spoke about the possibility to carry out a railway which will connect both countries. The Albanian side asked for the census of population in the former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia to be done under the control of the international observers. The ministers talked also about the tense situation in the region.
