BFN [Interview with Sergey Filatov, chief of the Russian president's staff, by correspondent L. Ioffe; place and date not given; from the "Vesti" newscast -- recorded] [Text] [Ioffe] Sergey Filatov is far from being a noisy sort of politician, he is very proper and even affable, and then all of a sudden there is a huge fuss in the newspapers about his resignation. [Filatov] It was news to me just as to everyone else, all the more so in that there is nothing behind it all. That is the most interesting bit. Two months ago there were attempts to bring the president's first aide Viktor Vasilyevich Ilyushin and myself into conflict... [Ioffe] On what grounds, if I may ask? [Filatov] Actually, it is hard to say, but I reckon that there are nevertheless, a number of political forces that on the one hand, want to put their own people in, of course. [Ioffe] Sergey Aleksandrovich Filatov cited the words of Gennadiy Zyuganov, to the effect that his party has its men at all level. [Filatov] There are lots of people who do not like accord. [Ioffe] As for relations with Viktor Ilyushin, Filatov said these have always been good, and will continue to be, for both men are busy getting on with the job. [Unidentified announcer] Sergey Filatov gave this interview to our commentator after a conference to mark Academician Sakharov's birthday.
