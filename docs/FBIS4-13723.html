BFN [Text] Kathmandu, April 22 (AFP) -- Nepalese Prime Minister Girija Prasad Koirala left for Washington on Friday [22 April] to undergo a medical checkup, foreign ministry officials said here. Koirala, who is is accompanied by sister-in-law Nona Koirala, his physician and others, will be examined at the Walter Reed Hospital near Washington. The 72-year-old premier is scheduled to return within the next two weeks if there are no problems, officials said. Koirala, who faces mounting pressure from within his party to quit, fainted twice times in public in the past six weeks. Doctors attributed the spells to low blood pressure and physical exhaustion.
