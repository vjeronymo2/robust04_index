Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The IDF [Israel Defense Forces] has moved two bases from densely populated Arab areas in the Gaza Strip to other locations. This is part of the IDF's redeployment in anticipation of the Gaza-Jericho First agreement with the Palestinians. IDF sources stressed that the evacuation of troops from refugee camps will be carried out concurrently with progress in the talks with the PLO. Our Army affairs correspondent Yariv Ben-Yehuda has more details: Two bases have been evacuated within three days -- one in Gaza's Rimal neighborhood two days ago, and another, a Border Police base in Rafah, which was moved yesterday to the southern sector headquarters from which the Border Police will continue their operations in the Rafah area in the same scope of deployment and activity. Army sources said that in the framework of the numerous logistic changes carried out in the Gaza Strip recently, buildings and troops have been relocated and, in some cases, troops have been concentrated in one central location from which they are to continue their operations before and after the implementation of autonomy. The sources stressed that the volume of the IDF's operations has not changed; on the contrary, it has been increased recently, resulting in the successful apprehension of many fugitives and the foiling of terrorist attacks. The sources further emphasized that the pace of troop evacuation from refugee camps will be tied to the progress of the talks with the PLO. [passage omitted] The structures from the two evacuated bases will be moved to outposts currently under construction near Jewish settlements in the Strip. It should be noted that the construction of these outposts is at an advanced stage. The IDF preparations for redeployment include the imminent transfer of several other bases. Defense establishment elements stressed this morning that the Gaza and Jericho redeployment can be completed within a few weeks after the agreement is signed.
