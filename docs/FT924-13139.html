921016 FT 16 OCT 92 / International Company News: Apple Computer registers record sales and earnings APPLE COMPUTER, the US personal computer manufacturer, reported record fourth-quarter and yearly revenues and earnings despite an industry-wide price war and rising competitive pressures. Sales volumes of Apple's Macintosh personal computers were up by over 35 per cent in the fourth quarter and exceeded 20 per cent for the year, said Mr John Sculley, Apple chairman and chief executive. Fourth-quarter revenues were Dollars 1.8bn, up 17 per cent on last year's Dollars 1.5bn, while net income rose to Dollars 97.6m, or 81 cents a share, from Dollars 81.2m or 67 cents. Annual revenues were Dollars 7.1bn, up 12 per cent, with net income jumping to Dollars 530.4m, or Dollars 4.33 from Dollars 309.8m or Dollars 1.16 in 1991 when there was a third-quarter restructuring charge of Dollars 138.9m, after tax. Lotus Development, the US personal computer software company, reported a 6 per cent dop in third-quarter revenues but a pre-tax gain of Dollars 34.6m from the sale of stock in Sybase, another software company, brought net income for the quarter to Dollars 30.1m, or 72 cents a share, up from Dollars 21.5m, or 48 cents, last year. Revenue was Dollars 206.7m, down from Dollars 218.8m. Nine-month net income rose 76 per cent to Dollars 65.8m, or Dollars 1.52 a share, from Dollars 37.3m or 84 cents. Revenue rose 13 per cent to Dollars 654.1m, from Dollars 579.7m. Borland International, the personal computer software company, reported lower than expected second-quarter earnings. Revenue rose 12.2 per cent to Dollars 127.8m from Dollars 113.9m last year. Net income was Dollars 5.4m, or 20 cents a share, compared with a net loss of Dollars 103m a year ago. First-half revenues were Dollars 242.6m, against Dollars 251.1m last year. Earnings Dollars 7.1m or 26 cents a share compared with a loss of Dollars 91.1m in 1991.
