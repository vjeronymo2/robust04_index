931111 FT 11 NOV 93 / Arts: Today's Television RTZ states that its mining companies in Papua New Guinea have consulted the local people and complied with the environmental demands of the government. Yet according to 'Hell In The Pacific' (C4, 9.00) in the Critical Eye series, there has been much bitterness since the RTZ subsidiary CRA began mining copper at Bougainville in 1966 and gold at Mount Kare in 1990. Michael Ignatieff begins a six-part series on nationalism, Blood And Belonging (BBC2, 9.30), in the former Yugoslavia where he lived as a child. Since he was born in Canada, his father in Russia, and he now lives in Britain, he seems particularly well equipped to present programmes which visit all those countries. Richard Briers stars as 'God' (Godfrey) in the four-part comedy If You See God, Tell Him (BBC1, 9.30). When struck by a load of builder's rubble Godfrey is transformed into 'a parody of the ad-man's dream': a disabled pensioner who believes in advertisements as fundamentalists believe in The Bible. ITV begins six short dramas set in London's tube train system: Going Undergound (11.15).
