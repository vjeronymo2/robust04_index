BFN [Text] At today's news conference in Paris, French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe announced that Slovenia will be the only country from the former Yugoslavia at the Conference on Stability in Europe that will be given the status of a potential EU member. As REUTER reports, a decision about Slovenia's participation at this conference was made at the last moment, as our country was not on the list of nine invited central and Eastern European countries last week. The French decision about this was also supported by Germany. It is known that our country, as opposed to Poland, the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, Lituania, Latvia, and Estonia, has not signed the associate EU membership agreement yet.
