BFN [Text] Bonn, May 4 (YONHAP) -- Visiting South Korean Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae conferred with his German counterpart, Minister Volker Ruehe, here Wednesday [4 May] and they agreed on stepped-up military exchanges and cooperation between their countries. The two ministers in particular shared the view that the North Korean nuclear question is the factor of major threat to stability in Northeast Asia let alone the Korean peninsula and that Pyongyang's nuclear development should be thwarted by all means. During the meeting held at the German Defense Ministry, the two ministers adopted a joint statement on the promotion of defense and military exchanges and cooperation. The statement called for the diversification of bilateral policy and strategic dialogues and expansion of mutual cooperation in easing tension in disputed areas. Defense sources said the statement was significant in that it is the first document Germany adopted with a non-NATO member country for expanded cooperation in the defense area. The statement specifically featured mutual consultations on basic defense and military policies, exchanges of information and personnel, and stepped-up cooperation in defense industry, education and training. Minister Yi is expected to visit the German Eastern Corps Headquarters and Berlin on Thursday before leaving for Seoul.
