930903 FT 03 SEP 93 / Arts: Today's Television Guess the year: it is a Friday in September and the programmes on Britain's main television channels include Coronation Street, Dad's Army, Alas Smith And Jones, Bruce Forsyth's Generation Game, What The Papers Say, and Gardeners' World. No, don't be facetious, there wasn't any television in 1890. The answer, of course, is 1993: the schedule is today's. As so often in the summer radio is a better bet. That burgeoning broadcaster Neil Kinnock presents Pick Of The Week on Radio 4 (7.20). Radio 3 provides a live relay of tonight's BBC Prom in which the Austro-Hungarian Haydn Orchestra makes its Prom debut. Naturally enough they play a good deal of Haydn: the chorus from his oratorio 'The Return Of Tobias', the short cantata 'The Storm', and Symphony No 97 in C (7.30). In Science Now Alun Lewis reports on the annual meeting of the British Association For The Advancement Of Science (8.50 Radio 4). Channel 4 begins another run of Paul Merton - The Series with the star of 'Have I Got News For You' once again installed behind the counter of his kiosk (10.30).
