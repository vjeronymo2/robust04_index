920427 FT 27 APR 92 / Fund to stabilise rouble agreed The following is an excerpt from the G7 communique on its meeting with Russian representatives. 1) The finance ministers and central bank governors of the G7 countries met today with deputy prime minister Yegor Gaidar and other representatives of the Russian Federation to discuss the historic events unfolding in Russia. 2) The Russian representatives described the economic situation in their country, Russia's financing requirements in 1992, the reforms that have already been implemented and their intentions regarding their timing and adoption of additional policy measures which would permit Russia to implement an economic programme meriting IMF support. 3) The minister and governors welcomed the reforms already undertaken in Russia. They urged Russia and the IMF to complete negotiations at an early date on a comprehensive stabilisation and reform programme that provides for: reduction of the budget deficit to stabilise the economy and reduce the role of government. curbing monetary growth to bring inflation under control and stop the extension of credit to unviable enterprises. establishment of the legal framework and contractual rights necessary to permit the development of a market economy. reform of the agriculture and energy sectors. a foreign exchange mobilisation system that enables the independent states of the former Soviet Union to meet their international payments. a unified and market-determined exchange rate set at a realistic level. 4) In this context, the ministers and governors expressed their commitment to support Russia's reform efforts during the transition by providing the recently announced Dollars 24bn aid package within the context of an agreed IMF programme. 5) To this end, they are prepared to provide up to Dollars 18bn through a substantial bilateral assistance together with other countries, through considering further deferral of Russia's outstanding indebtedness, and through supporting significant financial resources from the international financial institutions. 6) The ministers and governors are prepared to support G10 activation of GAB to finance Dollars 6bn currency stabilisation fund for Russia. This fund is intended to help stabilise the rouble, facilitate convertibility of the currency and promote confidence in the reform effort.
