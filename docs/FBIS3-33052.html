Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 15 February. Convergence Deputy Leonardo Montiel reports the Central Bank of Venezuela used approximately $1 billion to assist banks experiencing difficulties due to "very probable irregular management." The Central Bank president will be summoned tomorrow before the Finance Commission to explain why funds were used to help certain banks instead of depositors. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2049 GMT 8 Feb 94) Venzuelan Petroleum subsidiary Lagoven Inc. dispatched a total of 119 million barrels during 1993 from its La Salina Terminal, on the eastern coast of Lake Maracaibo, to export markets and local petrochemical- and petroleum-processing centers. A total of 237 oil tankers were used, of which 168 went to the United States, 35 to Germany, 12 to Canada, eight to Jamaica, six to Chile, three to Africa, two to Norway, and three to the Amuay Refinery in Falcon State. (Caracas REPORTE DE LA ECONOMIA in Spanish 9 Feb 94 p 17) The National Venezuelan Telephone Company grossed 120 billion bolivares during 1993, twice the amount for 1992. The company president said that at the end of the month the company will pay the National Treasury 6 billion bolivares for concessions granted by the government. (Caracas ECONOMIA HOY in Spanish 9 Feb 94 p 12) Venezuelan Petroleum subsidiary Maraven increased its level of production and generated significant savings of 10 billion bolivares in 1993, in addition to increasing services for customers via its quality management program. (Caracas EL DIARIO DE CARACAS in Spanish 9 Feb 94 p 19) The state group Venezuelan Petroleum, via its president, Gustavo Roosen, explains that the fall in petroleum prices on the international markets has forced a review of the 1994 budget and the implementation of appropriate adjustments. As a result, the government will have a much lower tax revenue. Conservatives place the national deficit at approximately $3.5 billion, while pessimists estimate the same at approximately $6 billion, 50 percent of the budget. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 0041 GMT 11 Feb 94)
