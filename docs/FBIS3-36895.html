Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Feb 09, BNS -- Six factions have been registered in the Lithuanian parliament after its new statute came into effect, Parliament Chairman Ceslovas Jursenas said Tuesday [8 February]. The ruling Lithuanian Democratic Labor Party [LDLP] faction is still the biggest and retained an absolute majority -- 72 members. 24 members of the former Sajudis and Civic Charter factions made up the new Conservative faction. The Christian Democratic faction and the Democratic faction have 11 and 3 members respectively. According to the new parliament statute, a faction can be made up of no less than 3 lawmakers. 6 social democrats and 4 members of the Polish Union made up their own factions, as well. The parliament earlier had 7 factions.
