920227 FT 27 FEB 92 / International Company News: Wal-Mart beats trend with 24% profits rise WAL-MART Stores, the Arkansas-based discount stores chain which is now the world's largest retailer in sales terms, yesterday disclosed a 34.6 per cent jump in turnover last year to Dollars 43.9bn and a 24.6 per cent increase in net profits to Dollars 1.6bn from Dollars 1.29bn. At the earnings per share level, Wal-Mart reported a 22.8 per cent improvement, to Dollars 1.40. Wal-Mart's figures contrast sharply with those delivered by many US retailers - in particular, the traditional department stores - who have been badly affected by the grim economic climate and the general slump in consumer spending. Growth during the fourth quarter was roughly in line with that for the year. Sales in the three months to end-January totalled Dollars 13.6bn, up from Dollars 10.4bn in the same period a year earlier. Net profits rose 25 per cent to Dollars 602.4m and earnings per share rose to 52 cents from 42 cents. In part, Wal-Mart's sales growth stems from a ferocious store expansion programme. In the fourth quarter, for example, it opened 68 new Wal-Mart stores, which sell low-priced general merchandise, and 15 Sam's Clubs. Sam's Clubs are warehouse club operations, sited in out-of-town 'barns' and selling a limited range of goods at deeply-discounted prices to a membership customer base. Wal-Mart is the biggest operator in this fast-growing market niche. However, for much of last year, the group also posted double-digit 'same-store' sales growth. The results for 1991-2 were scored after interest charges of Dollars 113.3m, more than double the Dollars 42.7m in 1990-91. The increase in total costs and expenses also slightly exceeded sales growth - rising by 35.4 per cent to Dollars 41.7bn. The results were much as analysts had expected, however, and shares in Wal-Mart - which has grown from one 'five and dime' store 40 years ago to overtake Kmart and Sears Roebuck as the nation's largest retailer - rose Dollars 2 3/8 to Dollars 53 7/8.
