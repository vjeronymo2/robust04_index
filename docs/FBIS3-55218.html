Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yuriy Romantsov] [Text] New York January 6 TASS -- Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Georgiy Mamedov who participated in the bilateral American- Russian political consultations in Washington, said the following on Thursday [6 January] in an interview with ITAR-TASS after their end. "Our discussions," the deputy minister said, "were comprehensive and successful in all respects. Naturally, we concentrated on the programme of President Clinton's visit to Russia on the eve of the Russian-American summit to be held in Moscow between January 12 and 15." According to Mamedov, the programme was agreed on all key issues. It can be said with certainty, he added, that preparations for the summit are progressing successfully. Mamedov noted that the sides would focus on social and economic issues at the Moscow meeting and talks: progress of reforms in Russia and the economic situation in the world. They will also discuss peace-making efforts, above all in such regions as Bosnia and Nagorno-Karabakh. The two sides attach much importance to strengthening the nuclear non-proliferation regime. This issue will occupy an important place on the agenda. As for Russia, it discusses this question both with Americans and other countries. The summit programme also includes bilateral relations: environmental control, air service, etc.
