Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Arabs are rioting within the Green Line as well. In Yefet Street in Yafo, Arabs are rioting, throwing stones and attacking passersby. Our correspondent Shim'on Vilna'i reports the street has been blocked and the police are dispersing the rioters with tear gas. The windows of four police cars and three parking vehicles were smashed. The vehicle of Qol Yisra'el's correspondent was also attacked. Meanwhile tempers have calmed down in Nazareth after the police dispersed the numerous rioters with tear gas. Our correspondent Yo'av Aviv reports that 40 residents were detained. In the Wadi 'Ara road there are incidents of stone and bottle throwing at passing vehicles. Five youths were detained in al-Tayyibah after they threw stones at policemen and Israeli vehicles. There were also riots in the village of Baqa al-Gharbiyyah. Residents claim three local residents were slightly wounded by rubber bullets. Today there are demonstrations and mourning processions in all the Arab villages within the Green Line. The demonstrators are calling for a separation between the two nations. Mourning processions are also being held in Haifa and East Jerusalem. There were also riots at the entrances to the Temple Mount; four Arabs were detained. Youths who rioted in the Shu'fat refugee camp were dispersed with tear gas and rubber bullets. Our police affairs correspondent Aviv Bushinsky reports that youths gathered near Lions Gate and threw stones. They were also dispersed with tear gas and rubber bullets.
