930105 FT 05 JAN 93 / Call for probe into defence of pound MR Gordon Brown, the shadow chancellor, yesterday claimed that the Bank of England's intervention to defend sterling on 'Black Wednesday' may have cost the Treasury up to Pounds 1.7bn, Ivo Dawnay writes. He demanded an independent inquiry into the events of September 16 when the UK left the exchange rate mechanism. He said independent advisers had calculated the figures based on interventions totalling Pounds 11bn to Pounds 14bn. Mr Brown released the figures to coincide with the three-month deadline for the UK to repay the German authorities for their interventions in defence of sterling. He said: 'The extent of the loss could be as much as the whole budget for trade and industry this financial year.'
