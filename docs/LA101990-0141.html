October 19, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

PREP ROUNDUP;

ROSARY'S GIRLS TURN IT AROUND, BEAT NO.2 MATER DEI IN TENNIS

Joyce Cruz and Mandy Gomez each swept in singles and Rosary High School, the third-ranked girls' tennis team in the Southern Section 2-A Division, defeated second-ranked Mater Dei Thursday at the Fullerton Tennis Center.

Mater Dei (3-2 in the Angelus League) had defeated Rosary, 10-8, this season and the match Thursday ended in a 9-9 tie, but Rosary (13-3, 4-1 in the league) won on games, 77-70.)

With its victory, Rosary takes sole possession of first place in the Angelus League.

In South Coast League tennis:

Capistrano Valley 13, San Clemente 5 -- Farrah Tierney won, 6-1, 6-2, 6-1, and Jenny Canfield won, 6-0, 6-0, 6-0, for host Capistrano Valley (12-3, 5-1), the fourth-ranked team in the 4-A Division.

In Sunset League tennis:

Fountain Valley 11, Huntington Beach 7 -- Renee Urricariet and Michelle Berkowitz each swept in singles and remained undefeated in league play for visiting Fountain Valley (12-4, 10-0).

In Century League boys' cross-country:

El Modena 15, Canyon 45 -- Mike Terry, Jessie Dunivin, Brett Pease, Brent Austin and Luis Valencia finished in a five-way tie for first place for the second-ranked team in Division II.

In Century League girls' cross-country:

Canyon 20, El Modena 38 -- Kim Nelson ran 18 minutes 54 seconds on the hilly Canyon-campus course to break the course-record of 18:56 she shared with Laura Flatauer. Flatauer was second in 19:11 for Canyon (4-0), which is the second-ranked team in Division II.

In Sea View League boys' cross-country:

Corona del Mar 21, Tustin 36 -- Mike Marumoto and Jack Hogan tied for first (16:06) to lead Corona del Mar on the Back Bay Course.

In Century League girls' volleyball:

El Modena 3, Foothill 1 -- Melizza Benitez had 33 assists and host El Modena (12-0, 6-0) won, 15-2, 13-15, 15-2, 15-12.

In Sea View League volleyball:

Newport Harbor 3, Woodbridge 2 -- Tara Kroesch had 18 kills and Maureen McLaren, returning after missing three weeks' of play with an ankle injury, had 12 as host Newport Harbor (10-4, 4-2) won, 15-9, 4-15, 15-2, 4-15, 15-13.

In Olympic League volleyball:

Orange Lutheran 3, Ayala 0 -- Renee Wright had 21 kills and visiting Orange Lutheran (10-5, 7-4) swept, 15-6, 15-10, 15-12.

In Freeway League water polo:

Buena Park 11, Sonora 4 -- Noah Cureton had six goals to lead host Buena Park (3-0 in league).
