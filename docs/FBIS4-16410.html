BFN [Text] The signatories to the treaty on CIS common security called on the UN Security Council to adopt an urgent decision on a peacekeeping operation in Abkhazia where the Georgian-Abkhazian military conflict has been going on for a year and a half already. The statement by the Council of CIS Common Security circulated in Moscow today says the participants in the treaty on CIS common security could bring their military contingencies to the conflict zone as peacekeeping forces with the consent of the conflicting parties. This could be done in accordance with the treaty on CIS common defense if no decision on the issue was made in the future for whatever reasons. The Council of CIS Common Security adopted a statement on implementation of a peacekeeping operation in the zone of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict signed by Azerbaijan, Armenia, Belarus, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Russia, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan.
