Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Belskiy] [Text] Moscow March 8 TASS -- Russian communist hardliner Viktor Anpilov has called on all communist movements of Russia to unite into one strong party on the basis of principles of Marxism-Leninism. Anpilov, leader of the Labour Russia Party and the Russian Communist Labour Party who was put in jail after the October 1993 revolt in Moscow and released under amnesty in February, said he wanted to set up one communist party based on the principles of communist ideology proclaimed by its founders Karl Marx and Vladimir Lenin. Anpilov took part in a rally devoted to International Women's Day traditionally celebrated in Russia on March 8. The rally was attended by about 1,000 people, according to the police. The demonstrators called for stronger state guarantees for the family and return to regulated prices of basic necessities. The Labour Russia Party said it will hold a congress at the end of April.
