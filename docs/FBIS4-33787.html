BFN [Text] Havana, 10 Jun (AFP) -- Today, the Cuban Foreign Ministry neither confirmed nor denied a report on an agreement that would be signed in Havana on 13 June whereby a Mexican corporation would invest $1.5 billion in Cuba. This would be the largest foreign capital investment in the country in 35 years. According to reports from Mexico, the corporation Domos International would buy 49 percent of the state-owned Cuban Telecommunications Enterprise [Empresa Cubana de Telecomunicaciones, ETEC] for 1.5 billion through a document to be signed during Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari's visit to Havana next Monday. In Mexico City, one of Domos International's executives said the investment is endorsed by British corporations N.M. Rothschild and K.P.M.G. Peat Marwick. Asked about the matter, Cuban Foreign Ministry spokesman Miguel Alfonso said he could neither confirm nor deny whether the signing of the document is included on the agenda, which has been prepared for the Mexican president's visit. [Paris AFP in Spanish at 1620 GMT on 10 June reports that according to Pedro Sepulveda, Domos International's corporate director, the corporation will buy 49 percent of ETEC for $1.5 billion next week. Press dispatches report the operation will include a seven-year plan of modernization that will cost $720 million, and the agreement will be signed during Salinas' visit. The report adds that ETEC comprises all of Cuba's telephone services, excluding cellular telephones. The project includes replacing 200,000 of the system's existing 450,000 lines and expanding the system to one million lines and is endorsed by N.M.
