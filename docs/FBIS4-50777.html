BFN [Text] Beijing, May 20 (XINHUA) -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin and Premier Li Peng today sent messages of condolences to the bereaved family of Mr. Masayoshi Ito, a former Japanese foreign minister, who passed away this morning. Qiao Shi, chairman of the Chinese National People's Congress Standing Committee, and Qian Qichen, vice premier and foreign minister, also sent similar messages to the bereaved family. The messages spoke highly of Ito, former Japanese foreign minister of the Liberal Democratic Party government, hailing him as a Japanese statesman with political far-sight and an old friend respected and trusted by the Chinese people. Ito had long dedicated himself to the cause of Sino-Japanese friendship and with firm conviction exerted positive efforts for the realization of normalizing Sino-Japanese relations and for the conclusion of a friendship treaty between the two countries, said the messages. He also made outstanding contributions to the development of good-neighborly relations of friendship and cooperation between China and Japan. The passing away of Ito is a big loss to the cause of Sino- Japanese friendship and the Chinese people will cherish his memory for ever, the messages added.
