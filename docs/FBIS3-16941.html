Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] At 7:54, Moscow time, on Tuesday, a cargo spaceship, to be known as Progress-M-22, was launched in line with the Russian program of servicing the manned space station Mir. Since January 8 Viktor Afanasiyev, Yuriy Usachev and Valeriy Polyakov have been working as its 14th crew aboard the Mir. The press service of the Russian Space Force has told Interfax that all the systems aboard the Progress are functioning as they should. The Space Force was responsoble for the preparation and launching of Progress-M-22. A Force spokesman said the cargo ship is to deliver to the station additional research equipment, food, oxygen, drinking water, medical equipment, fuel and an oxidizer for Mir's engine. The Progress' payload amounts to 2.3 tons.
