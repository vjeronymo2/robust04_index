921111 FT 11 NOV 92 / Trade secretary's speech wins cautious welcome MR Michael Heseltine, trade and industry secretary, made some business people marginally happier yesterday. As the conference centre emptied and executives hastened to see whether their order books had slimmed over the last two days, some stopped to chat about his message to industry. 'He said it very well,' said Mr Bryan Townsend, chairman of Midlands Electricity. 'Almost prime ministerial, what he said about government policy,' observed Mr Jeremy Woolridge, chairman of BE Wedge, Black Country galvanisers. Mr Chris Pennington, chairman of ABB Power, heavy electrical engineers in Telford, Shropshire, said: 'We had no negative signals which would have taken us further back.' Mr Woolridge added: 'We always had high expect-ations. Now we want it to be shown in evidence.' Mr Heseltine, of course, was constrained by the imminence of the Autumn Statement. Still, said Mr Pennington, 'He tried to give pretty clear indications - on export credits and that sort of thing'. Mr Bill Good, chief executive of Sterling Tubes of Walsall, said: 'I'm pleased with his attitude. The conviction is there. It's probably rhetoric at the moment, but that's the first step.'
