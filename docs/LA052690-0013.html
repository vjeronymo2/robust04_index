May 26, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

PARKING TICKET REVENUE IN L.A.

I am angered to read that the mayor is upset with the traffic officers wherein he contends that they are not writing enough tickets.

Although the annual revenue has increased from $18 million in 1985 to $90 million last year, this just isn't good enough, according to our esteemed mayor.

It is reprehensible to think that the mayor of this city is angry because more citizens are obeying the law!

But then again it really isn't surprising that this muddle-headed man who has lived for so long under a cloud of murky and nefarious transactions would think in a more positive vein than having to enrich the city coffers through such punitive means.

Come on, it's time to pat him on the head with two lumps of sugar, and put him out to pasture.

ALAN A. ROSS

Venice
