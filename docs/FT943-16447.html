940705 FT 05 JUL 94 / Paris newspaper suspended The conservative Le Quotidien, one of France's leading newspapers, announced yesterday that it will suspend publication this week because of financial troubles. The daily, edited by Philippe Tesson, will not appear from tomorrow and the management said in a statement that a shareholders' assembly on July 18 would decide whether to close it down or restart publication in the autumn. The daily, which has a tabloid format but counts among France's quality newspapers, was revamped recently to mark its 20th anniversary. It was suspended briefly in 1979 but returned.
