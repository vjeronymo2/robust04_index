BFN [Yakhyya Akiyev article under the "Hotspots" rubric: "Troops Approach Chechnya Border"] [Excerpts] The tense situation in Ingushetia and North Ossetia has made its presence felt again. Admittedly, this time it was not through the recurrent exchanges of gunfire on the line of the Ingush-Ossetian confrontation, but in a document which came into being in the tranquility of Kremlin offices. The Russian president has recently issued the Edict "On Implementing a State of Emergency on Part of the Territories of the Republic of North Ossetia and the Republic of Ingushetia." [passage omitted] The implementation of a state of emergency, as we know, entails the stationing of Russian troops on the territories which "come under" the edict. A question arises -- what is the main purpose of this act, which affects areas of "enhanced peace" rather than "enhanced tension"? Maybe the essence of the matter is the common border with Chechnya, where, according to the presidential edict, Russian Armed Forces have been concentrated since 30 May. Naturally, it would be more convenient for the Russian president to conduct talks with D. Dudayev, the possibility of which people are talking so much about, while keeping Groznyy in sight...
