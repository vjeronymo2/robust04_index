BFN [Text] Havana, 27 May (NOTIMEX) -- Apolonio Ruiz Ligero, Spanish secretary of state for foreign trade, disclosed here today that Spain proposed Cuba step up the pace of the economic reforms aimed at reorganizing the island's economy. In a brief news conference, Ruiz said he met with Vice President Carlos Lage and other members of the Cuban economic cabinet, who explained the difficulties in accelerating these changes. In any case, Ruiz added, Spain perceives that the measures adopted by the Cuban Government, such as the legalization of the possession of foreign currency and the increases in prices and fees, are not isolated and form part of a comprehensive package. Ruiz lamented the drop in Cuba's capacity to export to Spain as a result of the current economic crisis and indicated that if the supply of Cuban merchandise increases, Madrid would be able to increase its purchases. According to sources close to the Spanish delegation, bilateral trade amounted to approximately $290 million in both directions. On Friday, Cuba and Spain expressed their interest in extending their economic cooperation to areas other than tourism by signing an agreement for the mutual promotion and protection of investments. "This new generation agreement is very important because it establishes confidence, legal security, and an adequate framework for investors," Ruiz Ligero said after signing the agreement at the end of two days of talks. Ruiz also signed with Ernesto Melendez, Cuban minister for foreign investment and economic cooperation, the final document of the fourth meeting of the joint intergovernmental commission, which held sessions in Havana. The two officials highlighted the "simple, brief, smooth, and easy" way that the negotiations took place, indicating that they reaffirmed Havana and Madrid's political resolve to strengthen economic and financial ties. "We have given ourselves the challenge of making sure that in the medium term, these relations will be good and that we will be able to lay the foundations for greater cooperation in the future," Ruiz stressed. He arrived in Havana on Wednesday. "The negotiations were very simple and we can present our economic sectors with substantial progress," the high-ranking Spanish official said. He also met here with Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina and other officials. "The increasing way that the Spanish entrepreneur is in our market and in tourism investments bodes well for our work prospects. We are very pleased with the results of the talks," Melendez said. Sources of the Cuban delegation told NOTIMEX that "one of the basic conclusions is that investments are a fundamental element for the development of relations and everything possible must be done to increase them."
