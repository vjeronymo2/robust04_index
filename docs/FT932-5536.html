930603 FT 03 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television Coverage of the First Test between England and Australia begins on BBC1 at 10.55 am and switches to BBC2 at 4.00 pm, with highlights introduced by the admirable Richie Benaud on BBC1 at 11.30 pm. Business Matters (7.20 BBC2) reports on Vietnam, 18 years after the war with the US. Having made the mistake of winning, the Vietnamese are finding that the Americans have more ways of imposing their will abroad than merely dropping high explosive on people's heads: they are now up against a trade embargo and a ban on loans from the IMF and the World Bank. If BBC1 is to succeed with its new 'comedy', Every Silver Lining, people are going to have to rack their brains and do something radical; put in some jokes, preferably. There is nothing wrong with Andrew Sachs and Frances de la Tour, they just need some funny lines (8.30 BBC1). BBC2's usually excellent documentary series Under The Sun reports from Cameroon on 'The Sultan's Burden': the government will not let Sultan Issa Maigari raise taxes and the peasants are rebelling. Even the royal praise-singer has betrayed him (9.35).
