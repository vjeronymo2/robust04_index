BFN [Text] Simferopol, 27 Jun -- Former Prime Minister of Ukraine 55-year-old Leonid Kuchma got the most votes in Crimea during the presidential elections on Sunday. He got 82.6 percent of the votes on this peninsula. The current head of state Leonid Kravchuk won 7.4 percent of the votes in Crimea, ITAR-TASS learned from informed sources. It was Leonid Kuchma's promise during his Crimean pre-election tour of full economic independence for the peninsula and the promise he made to put relations with Ukraine on a treaty basis that won him this election result, observers say. According to finalized figures given by the Central Electoral Commission, 62.7 percent of the Crimean electorate turned out for the Ukraine presidential elections.
