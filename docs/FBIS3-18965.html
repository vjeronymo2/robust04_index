Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Gustave Feissel, the UN secretary general's alternate special representative in Cyprus, submitted to President Glavkos Kliridhis a draft document from UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali on the seven key points of the proximity talks agenda on confidence-building measures [CBM's]. Government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis said Kliridhis asked for time to study the draft document, and during talks tomorrow [10 March] and the following day he will raise a number of questions to clarify certain points. Kasoulidhis said the president will brief political leaders on the draft document during separate meetings tomorrow and the following day. The spokesman said that the proximity talks are entering a serious phase. Asked whether he agrees with the evaluation of Robert Lamb, the U.S. State Department special coordinator on the Cyprus issue, that the CBM's are the first opportunity in decades to find a Cyprus solution, Kasoulidhis said it is a significant opportunity. The spokesman concluded by saying that the Cypriot Government is in daily contact with the Greek Government. [passage omitted]
