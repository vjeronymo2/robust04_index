BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow May 3 TASS -- "Every Russian citizen must always be sure that he will get only trustworthy information, only the truth from our newspapers, magazines, television and radio," Russian Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin says in his message of greetings to Russian journalists on the occasion of the world freedom of the press day and the opening of the spring holidays of the free press. "On the day of your professional holiday I want to wish you all from the bottom of my heart the best of health, creactive achievements, and successes in your far from easy job," the document says. "I respect your work very much, because it requires not only skill, but courage, civic audacity, and a huge sense of responsibility for the future and honour of our great Russia." Chernomyrdin stresses that the freedom of the press, which is now the common boon of all the Russian people, is a great social achievement of the reviving Russia, which gives the journalists access to any information. "The government," the premier said, "is doing everything it can to allow every journalist, every citizen, to exercise his right to obtain information. It will back the mass media as much as it can, will make a practical contribution to the successful work of newspaper workers, publishers, TV and radio journalists."
