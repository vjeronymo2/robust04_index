Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 18 KYODO -- Japan will extend a total of 8.2 million dollars to international humanitarian organizations to help the people afflicted in the conflicts in the former Yugoslavia, the Foreign Ministry said Friday [18 March]. Of the total, Japan will extend the World Health Organization (WHO) 3 million dollars for providing medicine and medical supplies, and 700,000 dollars to the U.N. Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) for repairing the existing facilities to accommodate displaced people, the ministry said. The assistance also includes 3 million Swiss francs, or about 2 million dollars, to the International Committee for the Red Cross (ICRC) to provide safe water and medical equipment, and 2.5 million dollars to the U.N. Children's Fund (UNICEF) for providing educational material and equipment, it said. The latest aid brought the total amount of Japanese assistance to the people in the former Yugoslavia to 14.7 million dollars. Since the outbreak of conflict in Bosnia and Herzegovina in March 1992, about 4.2 million people have been afflicted and are facing a serious lack of food and daily necessities, according to the ministry.
