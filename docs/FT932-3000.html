930616 FT 16 JUN 93 / UK Company News: Storehouse chiefs share Pounds 1.16m pay-off FIVE directors who resigned last year from the board of Storehouse, the retail group, during the shake-up carried out by Mr David Dworkin, the former chief executive, shared compensation totalling Pounds 1,162,536, according to the annual report. Mr Michael Harvey resigned as chairman and chief executive of Habitat after a management restructuring last June; Ms Sheila Forbes resigned from her job as group personnel officer after a head office reorganisation in the same month; and Mr Derek Lovelock resigned at the same time as chief executive of Mothercare, to be replaced by Ms Ann Iverson, who was brought over from the US by Mr Dworkin. Mr Michael Julien, Mr Dworkin's predecessor as Storehouse chief executive, resigned on the grounds of ill health last July, and Mr David Simons resigned as finance director in December. Mr Dworkin, who left in March to become chief executive of Carter Hawley Hale, the US stores group, received no compensation.
