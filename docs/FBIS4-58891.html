BFN [Text] At 1130 today, President Hafiz al-Asad received Japanese Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa and his accompanying delegation. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' and the Japanese ambassador in Damascus attended the meeting. Talks dealt with the situation in the Middle East and the peace process. The Japanese minister paid tribute to Syria's positive role in launching the peace process. He noted the regional and international importance of achieving a just and comprehensive peace in the region. The two sides also discussed bilateral relations and the available means to develop them in the future. The Japanese official left Damascus today after a two-day visit to the Syrian Arab Republic. He was seen off at Damascus International Airport by Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar', senior Foreign Ministry officials, and the Japanese Minister in Damascus. In a statement to a SANA correspondent, Kakizawa said: My visit to Syria was very successful. I had the privilege of meeting with President Hafiz al-Asad and Syrian officials. He added that his talks dealt with the means to develop bilateral relations, which he described as very good. Kakizawa expressed the Japanese people's hopes that peace will be achieved in the Middle East. He also expressed Japan's willingness to cooperate and make a positive contribution to the peace process.
