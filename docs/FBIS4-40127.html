BFN [Text] On Monday [13 June] in Minsk, the Prime Minister of Belarus Vyacheslav Kebich and the CIS Executive Secretary Ivan Korotchenya signed an agreement on the terms of operation of CIS coordinating bodies in Belarus. The document provides for a set of benefits and privileges to these bodies. The CIS Executive Secretariat shall be freed of all national and local taxes and payments, except compulsory social security payments. Neither will it have to pay export or import taxes or customs duties. Its staff is exempt of criminal, civic and administrative responsibility under Belarussian jurisdiction for all actions as well as written and orals statements in office. Staff members enjoy personal inviolability and are freed from examination, detention and arrest. They have the right to the inviolability of their correspondence and documents. All payments they receive from the secretariat are exempt of taxation, except for compulsory social security payments. According to the agreement, the correspondence and archives of the secretariat and permanent representatives are inviolable. Official reports cannot be censored. The secretariat and permanent representatives have the right to use ciphers of courier mail and other special forms of communications.
