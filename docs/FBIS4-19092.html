BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Bulgarian Defense Minister Valentin Aleksandrov, who arrived on an official visit to Ankara, has begun his contacts. On his arrival at the National Defense Ministry, Aleksandrov was received by National Defense Minister Mehmet Golhan with a military ceremony. In a statement at the meeting, Golhan said that Bulgaria's relations with Turkey have developed after that country's transition to democracy. Aleksandrov stressed that Bulgaria's relations with Turkey are developing in the field of defense industry. Aleksandrov later called on Chief of Staff General Dogan Gures. At the meeting, Gures said that if the aggressors in the Balkans are not punished this will set an example and lead to graver results. Pointing out that the fighting in Bosnia-Herzegovina is not between soldiers but that the Serbs are killing civilians, women and children, Gures expressed satisfaction over the NATO operation against the Serbs. Aleksandrov said that Bulgaria favors securing peace without resorting to arms in the Balkans.
