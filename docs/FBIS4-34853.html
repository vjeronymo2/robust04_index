BFN [From the "National Radio News"] [Text] Omar Morales, a Foreign Ministry specialist, has told PRENSA LATINA that plans to form the Association of Caribbean States, ACS, reflect the spirit of convergence among today's approaches to Latin American integration. Morales said this is very healthy for the hemisphere because it opens the way to a degree of economic unity that will put the hemisphere in a more advantageous position to negotiate with other regional groups in the world. The ACS will include three regional blocs: the Caribbean Common Market, CARICOM; the Secretariat of Central American Economic Integration, SIECA; and the G-3, which includes Mexico, Venezuela, and Colombia. Morales also said that heads of state of the member countries will meet in the Barbadian capital on 4 July to establish the ACS, an intergovernmental organization for regional teamwork, consultation, and cooperation.
