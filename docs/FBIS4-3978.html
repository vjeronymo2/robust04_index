BFN [Text] Beijing, April 28 (XINHUA) -- China believes that China and Japan will continue to maintain their close coordination and cooperation and jointly promote their long-term and steady development of bilateral relations, after the establishment of the new Japanese cabinet, said Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman Wu Jianmin here today. Wu made the remarks at a weekly press conference when asked to comment on the Tsutomu Hata cabinet which will be formed this afternoon. Wu noted that Chinese Premier Li Peng had already sent a telegram of congratulations to Hata. "Tsutomu Hata is a statesman well-known by the Chinese people," said Wu. "We appreciate his zeal and foresight in the effort to enhance the friendship and cooperation between Japan and China, as well as all the useful work he has done to this end," he added. As for the new cabinet of Japan, Wu said: "I would like to point out that various political parties, whether in or out of the government, share the common view of developing mutually beneficial and cooperative relations between China and Japan in all fields."
