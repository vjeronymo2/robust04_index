Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Macao, February 10 (XINHUA) -- The new Macao-Taipa bridge went into operation here today. Macao Governor General Vasco Joaquim Rocha Vieira presided over a ceremony marking the opening of the bridge. The governor first expressed his congratulations on the completion of the new bridge, saying he was convinced that the bridge would bring great benefits to local people. The new bridge is expected to ease the tense traffic in Macao, the governor said. He added that the opening of the bridge is a recognition of the great efforts that technicians and builders have made to finish the project. It is learned that starting from today, all heavy-duty trucks will not be allowed to use the old Macao-Taipa bridge, finished in 1974. The four-lane new bridge is 4,414 meters long and 19.3 meters wide, with a total 1108.86-meter bridge approach at the two ends. Construction of the bridge, started in 1990, costed more than 600 million Macao patacas (about 77 million U.S. dollars).
