Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Loshchilin] [Text] Washington March 9 TASS -- Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze, on an official visit here, on Tuesday had meetings with World Bank president Lewis Preston and International Monetary Fund managing director Michel Camdessus. The expansion of cooperation with the world's largest multinational credit-lending and financial organisations in advancing the republic's economy was discussed, Georgian Vice-Premier Irakli Menagarishvili, who took part in the talks, told ITAR-TASS. "The meetings were held in a constructive and warm atmosphere. Shevardnadze assured interlocutors that, notwithstanding all the difficulties connected with military operations and their consequences, Georgia firmly intends to go ahead with the market reform policy. "Shevardnadze outlined our government's plans in this respect and urged the World Bank and IMF leaders to help the republic resolve the complex economic problems facing it. Support for the Georgian national monetary units was discussed at the talks," Menagarishvili said.
