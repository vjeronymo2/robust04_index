May 20, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

DARTS AND LAURELS TO THOSE INVOLVED WITH 'ROE VS. WADE'

Henry Ford believed his customers were entitled to a Model T in any color, so long as it was black. Josef Stalin believed Soviets were free to do or say whatever they wanted, as long as those who disagreed with him went to jail or to execution. Times columnist Howard Rosenberg believes there should be complete "freedom of speech" in television programming ("Pro-Balance on Abortion," May 15) as long as pro-abortionists hold a monopoly in entertainment and the media.

Is it any wonder some of us have a problem describing him and his allies as "pro-choice"?

GREG FRY

Culver City
