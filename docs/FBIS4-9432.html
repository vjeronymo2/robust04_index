BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 22/4/1994 -- In the speech made after the Friday plenum session of the Crans Montana Forum, at the workshop devoted to Romania, its economic potential and development perspectives, Premier Nicolae Vacaroiu expressed his conviction that the Bucharest conference would contribute to understanding the evolutions, the options for the present and future, the efforts Romania is making in promoting the democratic principles on the one hand and in consolidating the market mechanisms on the other. The Romanian prime minister expressed the view that in the political and economic context of a South-Eastern Europe in full integration in the European structures and opening to the world's economic circuits, Romania is holding the privileged place secured to it by its strategic position and its resources. Romania is offering foreign investors a domestic market in full development, a ready access to neighboring foreign markets, an incentive juridical background permanently improved, a labor force capable of complex workmanship. The prime minister of Romania showed that with effect from November 1992, the Romanian Government practically made effective its ruling programme and the strategy for social and economic reform covering the time interval up to 1996, and that sectorial surveys plead for continuing the reform in close correlation with the real situation of the Romanian economy, and with the technological, material, financial, currency and social constraints. Premier Vacaroiu stressed that, although carried under the impact of negative factors accumulated in the years previous to 1989 and in extremely unfavorable international conditions, the reforms went on in several planes: the reform of prices, the introduction of the value-added tax, the enhancement of the financial autonomy at local administration level, the initiation of the restructuring process, the development of the small and medium business sector, the further liberalization of foreign trade, the making of the laws apt to support the reform. "The official report of the United Nations Economic Commission assessed that, among all countries in transition, Romania is the surprise of the year 1993, because it managed economic growth (a positive 1 per cent on the gross domestic product, plus 1.3 per cent on industrial production, plus 12.4 per cent on farm production), thus contradicting all Romanian and foreign prognoses estimating that economic decline was to go deeper by another 7-10 percentage points." Concluding, the prime minister of Romania named among his government's actions for an ever greater opening of Romania in 1994 to the world business spheres those regarding the alignment of export/import trade policies proper to market rules, attracting direct foreign capital investments by markedly liberal regulations and encouraging fiscal policies, improving leasing conditions and activating the free trade zones, intensifying the relations with the international bodies (IMF, EBRD, G-24), as well as actions directed at making progress towards Romania's association with the European Union.
