BFN [Text] Ankara, April 27 (A.A.) -- A section of the high tension lines that supply electricity to the Kurds of Northern Iraq as part of the humanitarian aid from Turkey was bombed and destroyed by terrorists. Foreign Ministry spokesman Ferhat Ataman told a weekly press conference today that the separatist terrorist organization PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] had destroyed the Silopi-Zakho part of the line for the second time Sunday [24 April]. After destroying the line, the terrorists also set landmines in the region to prevent teams from repairing the line. `This action against the people of Northern Iraq, should be a regrettable incident which should teach everyone a lesson,' Ataman said. Ataman added that the line would be repaired at the soonest, to supply power to the people Northern Iraqis, as part of Turkey's humanitarian assistance to those people. Ataman went on to say that ambassadors from 16 countries were called to Ankara for an evaluation meeting which will take place on May 4. The meeting will review Turkey's relations with the West from the point-of-view of security and politics. Ataman said that Turkey was placing intensive emphasis on the Iranians who were wounded during Turkey's cross-border operation on the terrorist camp near Zhala which is on the Iran/Iraq border. He added that the compensation claims of Iran were under evaluation.
