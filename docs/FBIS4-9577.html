BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Bratunac, April 27 (TANJUG) -- Moslems on Wednesday opened fire from anti-aircraft machineguns, infantry weapons and snipers on Serb parts of Sarajevo before the very eyes of the United Nations Protection Force (UNPROFOR), the Bosnian Serb Army said in a statement. The targets of artillery and infantry fire on Wednesday were also Serb villages and Army positions in the areas of Konjic south of Sarajevo, Srbobran (formerly Donji Vakuf), Olovo and Kladanj in central Bosnia, Orasje, Doboj, Gradacac, Brcko, Teslic and Mount Vlasic in northern Bosnia, Mount Majevica in north-eastern Bosnia and Bihac in western Bosnia, the statement said. The Bosnian Serb Army on Wednesday neutralized a Moslem terrorist group in the area of Bihac, it added. Moslem attacks on the almost entire territory of the Serb Republic in Bosnia-Herzegovina continues at a time when the Serb side is proposing a general ceasefire in the war-ravaged former Yugoslav Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina, the statement said. In the night between Tuesday and Wednesday, the Serb and Moslem sides exchanged prisoners, civilians and the bodies of killed fighters in the vicinity of Bihac. The exchange was carried out on an "all-for-all" principle and in the presence of UNPROFOR monitors, the Bosnian Serb Army said. The Moslem side handed over four prisoners, 12 civilians and six bodies of killed soldiers and the Serb side four prisoners, nine civilians and eight bodies of killed fighters, the statement said.
