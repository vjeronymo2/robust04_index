April 8, 1989, Saturday, Valley Edition

WOMAN WITH CHILDREN HIT BY STRAY BULLET

A woman passer-by pushing a baby stroller was accidentally shot in the arm Friday by suspected gang members who opened fire on a passing car in Pacoima, Los Angeles police said.

The woman, whom police did not identify, was in stable condition at a Pacoima hospital, Lt. Gary Williams said. A stray bullet hit the woman as she was walking along the 12700 block of Van Nuys Boulevard accompanied by a 3-year-old child and a 1-year-old baby in a stroller, Williams said.

Police recovered a loaded Uzi semiautomatic carbine from the scene, Williams said.

Two young men were arrested on suspicion of assault with a deadly weapon, Williams said. He did not identify them because authorities had not determined whether they are juveniles.

The young men are suspected of being involved in other shootings in the area last week, Williams said.

Williams said he did not know whether gang members were in the car that was fired at. There was no description of the car or its passengers. Police were continuing to investigate the shooting.
