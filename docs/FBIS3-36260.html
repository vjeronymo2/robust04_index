Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Boris Krivoshey and Denis Perkin] [Text] Moscow February 3 TASS -- Possibilities of resolving the nuclear problem in North Korea have not exhausted and, therefore, it is necessary to continue discussing all questions at the negotiation table, Russian Deputy Foreign Ministry Spokesman Mikhail Demurin told a briefing held on Thursday [3 February]. Touching on the Russian side's participation in finding a solution to the nuclear problem in North Korea, Demurin said that Russia is working in three directions. It supports a dialogue between North Korea and the United States, North Korea and the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), as well as North and South Koreas. "It is fully possible to resolve the problem of the non-proliferation of nuclear weapons in the Korean Peninsula by acting in these directions. The basis of the solution should be a strcit observance of obligations of the agreement on guarantees with the iaea by Pyongyang," the Russian deputy foreign ministry spokesman emphasised. "Russia is for North Korea remaining faithful to the Nuclear Weapon Non-Proliferation Treaty, and we also underscore the need to put into action the joint declaration about denuclearization of the Korean Peninsula by the North and South," Mikhail Demurin concluded.
