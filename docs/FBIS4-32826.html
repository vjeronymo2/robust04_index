BFN [Text] Ricardo Alarcon, president of the National Assembly of the People's Government, ANPP, has concluded a visit to the UK at the invitation of the British group of the Interparliamentary Union. In London, Alarcon met with numerous British parliamentarians on Cuba's current situation and on the particularities of its government system. Alarcon met Betty Boothroyd, president of the House of Commons, among other figures. Alarcon began his visit to the UK on 23 May as head of a delegation that included Osvaldo Martinez, president of the ANPP Economic Commission, and Lidia Tablada, director of the National Center for Agricultural-Livestock Hygiene. The Cuban delegation led by Ricardo Alarcon also met UK authorities, including (David Hitchcock), the state minister of the Foreign Office. It was decided at the meeting that the British Government would send an official delegation to Cuba, to be led by (Patrick Glovlin), state minister for trade and technology and a participant of the meetings with Alarcon in London. The Cuban parliamentary delegation also met British businessmen interested in maintaining and strengthening trade relations with Cuba. Coinciding with Alarcon's visit, Basic Industry Minister Marcos Portal was also in London and held several meetings with British businessmen.
