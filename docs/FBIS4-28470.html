BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 10 June Morning Edition] [Text] Peter Tarnoff, U.S. undersecretary of state for political affairs, who is in Japan to discuss how to deal with the DPRK's (North Korea) suspected development of nuclear arms, met with Vice Minister of Defense Agency Shigeru Hatakeyama and Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs Kunihiko Saito on 9 June. At the beginning of his talks with Hatakeyama, Tarnoff said: "I am not here to finalize the substance of sanctions." The Japanese side also explained: "We discussed our analysis of the present situation in North Korea." Details of the talks are unavailable, but the Japanese and U.S. officials are believed to have discussed cooperation within the UN framework, and steps to be taken as a result of the recent high-level talks among Japan, the United States, and the ROK.
