BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Yuriy Kozlov] [Text] Moscow, 7 Jun -- The text of the agreement on partnership and cooperation between the Russian Federation and the European Union is finally agreed and the necessary procedures for it to be signed have begun. That is what Grigoriy Karasin, head of the Russian Foreign Ministry Information and Press Department, told today's briefing for Russian and foreign correspondents. He spoke about the results of the consultations held in Moscow yesterday. Heads of Foreign Ministry political departments from Greece, Belgium, and Germany took part in the talks. Karasin said that no effort would be spared to ensure that the agreements are signed during the meeting of the European Council's heads of states and governments of the European Union, which will take place on the Greek island of Kerkira on 24-25 June 1994. The talks participants, the official spokesman of the Russian foreign policy department said, also discussed the problem of nuclear non-proliferation on the Korean Peninsula. Representatives of the European Union, he said, expressed concern at tension increasing there because of North Korea's unwillingness to achieve a comprehensive settlement. In its turn, the Russian side drew attention to the importance of working on the idea of convening an international conference with the participation of the Russian Federation, the United States, China, Japan, North Korea, the Republic of Korea, and also representatives of the United Nations, and the IAEA, so that it could discuss the problem of security in and a non-nuclear status for the Korean Peninsula. During discussion of the situation in the former Yugoslavia, Grigoriy Karasin continued, it was noted that there was a coincidence of views on and approaches to the situation in Bosnia- Herzegovina. It was agreed that that there is a need for the Russian Federation, the European Union, and the United States to step up efforts with a view to moving closer to a political solution. Other topical international problems were also thoroughly discussed. Among them were the situation in the Near East and the implementation of the Israeli-Palestinian agreement, the situation in the Republic of South Africa following the first general elections, relations between Russia and the Baltic region and Ukraine, and peace-keeping operations in Georgia and Nagorno- Karabakh. The representatives of Greece, Belgium, and Germany said that the European Union intends to assist in developing and strengthening the CIS.
