920128 FT 28 JAN 92 / US business confidence shows January improvement US business confidence rose sharply in January, reversing a three-month decline, figures indicated yesterday, writes Michael Prowse in Washington. Cahners Economics, a Massachusetts-based consultancy group, said its index rose to 63.2, compared with 56.1 in December. It was the largest increase since the group began tracking business plans last June, when the index read 62.5. Mr Kermit Baker, Cahners' chief economist, said confidence was being revived across the US. 'While the trend is uneven, with consumer product and service sectors lagging, this is the first hard evidence that a recovery in manufacturing can begin in early to mid-1992 - earlier than many forecasters are predicting.'
