The current rule requires the issuer of the stock appreciation right to have been subject to the reporting requirements of Section 13(a) of the Exchange Act for at least a year prior to the transaction, and to have filed all reports and statements thereby required. Under the proposal, a one-year reporting history no longer would be required. Instead, the issuer simply would have to have filed all required reports and statements for a year prior to the transaction, or such shorter time as the issuer had been subject to Section 13(a). G.
