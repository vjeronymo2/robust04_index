BFN [Text] Tegucigalpa, 7 Apr (ACAN-EFE) -- The presumed mastermind of the plot to assassinate Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina has voluntarily surrendered to a court in this capital on 7 April, a judicial source has confirmed. Luis Hernandez Sosa, 30, surprised the security corps when he arrived unexpectedly at the First Lower Criminal Court in Tegucigalpa at 1600 local (2200 GMT) accompanied by human rights leaders. Sosa is linked to a plot to assassinate President Reina uncovered on 13 March when Nicaraguan citizen Luis Rene Quintero confessed his participation to the Nicaraguan Government Ministry. According to Quintero, Honduran Hernandez Sosa hired him along with Nicaraguan citizens Santiago Rivas and Roger Arguello to kill President Reina for $400,000, as confirmed by Honduran military spokesman Colonel Napoleon Santos Aguilar. Members of the Human Rights Commissioner's Office and of the court confirmed to ACAN-EFE that Hernandez turned himself in to the First Lower Criminal Court in Tegucigalpa. In a brief statement to judicial reporters, Hernandez said he is "innocent" of all charges.
