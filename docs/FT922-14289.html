920407 FT 07 APR 92 / Observer: Ice-cream float surely the marketer's dream confection. A household name for generations of Americans, the Eskimo Pie ice-cream bar is credited with the creation of the 'frozen novelty' industry. It had a hot time, however, early on. In 1924, the three-year-old Eskimo Pie company was so deeply in debt to its suppliers that one of them decided to take it over and lick it into shape. The buyer then was US Foil, now Reynolds Metals, second largest of the US aluminium groups. It claims Eskimo Pie has a portfolio of brands which gives it third place in the US frozen novelty business. But these days ice-cream does not mix smoothly with the group's other activities. Reynolds first tried to sell Eskimo Pie to Nestle but, when that deal melted away, decided to go for an ice-cream float instead. It has set its sights on a tasty Dollars 44m.
