BFN [Text] Replying to a request from the Latvian Foreign Ministry for an explanation of Russia's intention to set up military bases in CIS member states and the Republic of Latvia, Aleksandr Rannikh, Russian ambassador to Latvia, said the Russian side is acting in accordance with the fact that all the issues related to the withdrawal of its troops from the territory of the Republic of Latvia, as well as the status of and the conditions for temporary functioning of the Skrunda radar station, were resolved in documents coordinated and initialed on 15 March this year. According to the above-mentioned agreement, the Skrunda radar station is a Russian military facility under civilian control and cannot be regarded as a military base. Commenting on President Yeltsin's decree of 5 April this year, Mr. Rannikh apologized, explaining that the text is due to a technical mistake.
