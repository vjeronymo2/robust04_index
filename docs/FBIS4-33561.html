BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Salvadoran economic developments monitored through 7 Jun. Addressing a meeting of presidents from the Central American Chambers of Commerce, Economy Minister Luis Enrique Cordova on 2 June warned that if equitable treatment is not guaranteed in free trade negotiations, there is a risk that investments will be diverted away from Central America. The meeting is being held before negotiations for a free trade treaty with Mexico to review the private sector's role in the negotiations. (San Salvador Canal Doce Television in Spanish 0300 GMT 3 Jun 94) The Central Bank reported on 3 June that economic activity in El Salvador increased 7.1 percent during the first three months of 1994. The increase was primarily attributed to the "dynamic" output of the industrial and construction sectors. The 1994 monetary program is designed to achieve overall economic growth of 5-6 percent. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2113 GMT 3 Jun 94)
