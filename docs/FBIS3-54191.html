Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali has said that during his visit to Washington His Majesty King Husayn discussed with the U.S. Administration all aspects and tracks of the peace process. Al-Majali said his majesty emphasized Jordan's commitment to the principle of a comprehensive lasting, and just solution on the basis of UN resolutions. In a telephone interview with the Qatari newspaper AL-RAYAH in Washington, al-Majali said consultation between Jordan, Syria, and the other Arab parties is based on the comprehensive nature of the solution and the realization of peace on the basis of the resolutions of international legitimacy and the land-for-peace principle. The prime minister described the U.S. role in the Arab-Israeli peace negotiations as effective and positive, pointing out that the U.S. Administration, led by President Clinton, is playing the role of an honest broker between the Arabs and Israel. On the recent Jordanian-Palestinian agreement, Dr. al-Majali expressed full satisfaction with what has been reached, pointing out that coordination between the two sides is continuing in all fields. As for Jordan's relations with the Gulf states, the prime minister said Jordan is honestly and sincerely seeking the best possible relations with all Arab countries, especially the brothers in the Arab Gulf states. The prime minister welcomed the agreement reached on resolving the political crisis in Yemen, pointing out the Jordanian pan-Arab constants in the Arab political action.
