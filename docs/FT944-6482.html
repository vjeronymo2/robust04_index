941129 FT 29 NOV 94 / International Company News: Indonesian noodle maker's profits soar Nine-month earnings at Indofood Sukses Makmur, Indonesia's largest noodle maker, almost trebled compared with a year ago in spite of press reports about food poisoning, denied by the company, during the period, Reuter reports from Jakarta. Net profits for the nine months advanced to Rp145.61bn (Dollars 66.9m) from Rp50.04bn a year ago, on sales of Rp976.93bn compared with Rp809.05bn. Indofood, part of the Salim group and 50.1 per cent owned by Indocement Tunggal Prakarsa, is said to have told analysts that, as a result of the food poisoning reports it had revised its full-year profits down to between Rp200bn and Rp220bn from Rp255.22bn.
