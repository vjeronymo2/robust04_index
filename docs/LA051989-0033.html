May 19, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: MOTHER CHARGED IN 2ND CHILD DROWNING

A mother charged with a misdemeanor in California four years ago after her daughter drowned in a pool has been arrested in Pensacola, Fla., after the drowning of a second child, police said Thursday.

Linda Key, 31, of Pomona, was arrested Wednesday and released on $15,000 bond after being charged with felony child neglect in the death this week of her 2-year-old son, David.

Key's lawyer, Bob Heath Jr. of Pensacola, said Key, who is in town visiting her mother, is scheduled to be arraigned June 8. He said his client plans to plead not guilty.

David Key was playing in the back yard of his grandmother's home Saturday and threw a ball over a fence, according to the warrant. Key said that when she returned from retrieving the ball, she could not find her son.

When she decided to go for a swim about 15 minutes later, she found the child face down in the pool. The boy was taken by helicopter to a hospital and died Monday.

On Jan. 9, 1985, Key's 2-year-old daughter, Laura Elizabeth Key, died in Lake Elsinore, Calif.

Heath said that Key faced a misdemeanor count in that case, although he was unsure of the precise charge.
