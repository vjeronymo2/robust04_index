921208 FT 08 DEC 92 / Parliament and Politics: Sceptics sense shift on Europe THE campaign for a UK referendum on the Maastricht bill received a boost yesterday with a BBC Regional Broadcasting poll by Gallup showing three out of four voters from a 7,000 sample favour a plebiscite, Ivo Dawnay writes. Campaigners for a referendum on the Maastricht bill believe that they may be close to finding a formula for an amendment that successfully skirts procedural hurdles. Several amendments are now being perused by Commons clerks including one by Mr Bill Cash, a leading Tory Euro-sceptic, that would use an Order in Council to circumvent the bar on incurring costs to the Treasury.
