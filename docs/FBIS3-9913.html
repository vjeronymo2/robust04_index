Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Mexican elections, scheduled for 21 August 1994. Ernesto Zedillo Ponce de Leon, campaign manager for presidential candidate Luis Donaldo Colosio, of the Institutional Revolutionary Party, PRI, is quoted in the "Mexico and NAFTA Report" published by the LATIN AMERICAN NEWSLETTERS in London. Zedillo said that for the first time, the PRI will be facing a true challenge in the elections. "We will have to attain full credibility," he said adding that the party "will be judged much harder than other political parties." (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1610 GMT 17 Feb 94) Colosio on 17 February visited Chilpancingo, Guerrero State, where he met with PRI members. Colosio told them that his main commitment is "to struggle decisively against inequality." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 18 Feb 94) Colosio on 18 February met with small scale entrepreneurs of Mexico City and offered them a nine-point plan that will promote associations, training programs, loan programs, and tax programs. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 19 Feb 94) National Action Party, PAN, presidential candidate Diego Fernandez visited Toluca and met with Mexico State businessmen. Fernandez told them that "with or without changes to the Electoral Law, the 21 August elections will be clean elections." He added that he is opposed to the "coalitions that parties such as the Party of the Democratic Revolution, PRD, are trying to make." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 18 Feb 94) Fernandez also visited Veracruz and clarified that the government must comply with the law, but candidates and the political parties must also be serious and honest if Mexico is to have clean elections. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 19 Feb 94) PRD presidential candidate Cuauhtemoc Cardenas visited Durango State where he held a news conference with the local media to reiterate his request "for an extraordinary meeting of the Congress of the Union to make changes to the Electoral Law." Cardenas also said that he would like to meet with PRI presidential candidate Luis Donaldo Colosio and "define the necessary measures for changes to the Electoral Law and to discuss the Chiapas armed conflict." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 18 Feb 94) Cardenas also attended a rally in the Durango main plaza and spoke of the struggle "to seek a democratic change in the nation." As part of his program, he proposed the "economic recovery of the country." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 19 Feb 94) Rafael Aguilar, presidential candidate for the Cardenist Front for National Reconstruction Party, PFCRN, visited Tlaxcala State on 17 February. Aguilar said that his party is undergoing "a process of tripling its forces, and this proves that by April it will have reached its goal of signing up 1 million members." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 18 Feb 94) In Veracruz, Aguilar pointed out that the elections are the only path for obtaining the democratic change all Mexicans need. In a news conference, the PFCRN candidate stated all presidential candidates seek honest and clean elections. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 19 Feb 94) Alvaro Perez Trevino, presidential candidate of the Authentic Party of the Mexican Revolution, PARM, met with party members in Naucalpan. Perez said that "to combat voting abstentions, it is necessary to encourage women and youths to vote since they represent 70 percent of the country's population." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 18 Feb 94) Pablo Emilio Madero, National Opposition Union, UNO, presidential candidate, visited Michoacan State where he met with reporters. Madero stressed the importance of clean elections that "will serve as an example for future elections." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 18 Feb 94) Mexican Green Ecologist Party presidential candidate Jorge Gonzalez Torres has visited Michoacan State where he met with professors who are on strike at the local university. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 18 Feb 94) While in Mexico City, Gonzalez Torres accepted an invitation to attend the Chiapas peace dialogue as an observer at the invitation of the peace commission and the mediator. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0435 GMT 19 Feb 94) The weekly magazine ESTE PAIS on 18 February reported on a poll conducted in Mexico City, Monterrey, Guadalajara, Tijuana, and Merida on 11 February. The poll showed that 35 percent of the people do not know for whom they are going to vote, 33 percent said they will vote for Colosio, 17 percent will vote for Fernandez, and 14 percent will vote for Cardenas. Meanwhile, 40 percent of those polled said they do not believe their votes will be respected. (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0030 GMT 19 Feb 94)
