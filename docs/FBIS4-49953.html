BFN [Text] Guangzhou, May 12 (XINHUA) -- The shift to a market economy has helped speed up the growth of the private sector in south China's Guangdong Province, according to the provincial administration for industry and commerce. An administration official said that during the first quarter of 1994, 4,484 private companies were set up throughout Guangdong, a national pace-setter in reforms and opening to the outside world. That figure brought the number of private companies in Guangdong to 50,000, 10.5 percent more than at the end of 1993, the official said. He said that Guangdong had 25,000 private companies at the end of 1991, adding that 7,070 more such companies sprang up in the following year. Last year saw the establishment of 13,000 such companies, he said. Private companies have a total registered capital of 21.3 billion yuan. "These companies have become a main force behind Guangdong's economic growth," he said. Guangdong has some 4,000 private companies with a registered capital exceeding one million yuan each, he said. To date, 40 private enterprise groups have been formed throughout Guangdong, he added. According to the official, many government employees have abandoned their iron rice bowls, or lifelong jobs, to launch their own companies to provide services in advertising, real estate development, computers and consultancy.
