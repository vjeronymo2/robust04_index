Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Azerbaijani Defense Ministry today reported that heavy fighting continued today in the area of the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict. According to sources in Baku, the Armenians lost 7 tanks and 2 armored vehicles over the last two days of clashes in the Ter-Ter, Agdam and Fizuli districts. Casualties among Armenian troops are measured "in hundreds," the same sources said. The ministry also said villages of the Kedabek, Agdam, Ter-Ter and Fizuli districts of Azerbaijan and the village of Sadarak of the Nakhichevan autonomous republic came under heavy artillery fire yesterday. The shelling left one person dead and another two wounded. Sources in the Azerbaijani parliament report that Milli Mejlis Chairman Rasul Guliyev is visiting Azerbaijani positions looking into the situation along the front line and the back-up of Azerbaijani units. Meanwhile, the press office of the Nagorno-Karabakh army told INTERFAX that Nagorno-Karabakh units were re-gaining control of the highland pass Omar situated in the north of the Kelbajar district of Azerbaijan. The press office said Azerbaijani units suffered huge casualties both in personnel and hardware. Over the last 24 hours, Azerbaijanis lost over 150 troops, 4 tanks T-72 and 2 armored vehicles, while only 6 Nagorno-Karabakh troops were killed and 18 wounded, according to the press office. The press office also said two Azerbaijani combat jets Sukhoi-25 bombed villages and Nagorno-Karabakh positions in the north of the Martuni district today at noon. According to preliminary reports, the bombing left victims both among servicemen and civilians.
