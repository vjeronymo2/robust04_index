Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanover (DDP/ADN) -- Following the pilot agreement between IG Metall [Industrial Trade Union of Metalworkers] and the employers, the German Salaried Employees Union (DAG), too, has now reached a wage settlement with the employers' association Gesamtmetall. After top-level talks with Gesamtmetall this morning in Hannover, DAG called off strikes scheduled for Monday [7 March] by its members in the engineering wage negotiating region of Lower Saxony, DAG spokesman Ingo Schwope said. The negotiating parties, led by Gesamtmetall president Hans-Joachim Gottschol and DAG leader Roland Issen, have agreed on a 2-percent increase in wages and training grants from 1 June, as well as settlements aimed at safeguarding jobs. The holiday bonus, suspended by the employers, will be reintroduced and backdated to 1 January 1994. Special payments such as the Christmas bonus will be subject to a one-off reduction of 10 percent in 1994, it was reported.
