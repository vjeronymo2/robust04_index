931126 FT 26 NOV 93 / Budgen first in member's bill draw Mr Nicholas Budgen, the rightwing Conservative Euro-sceptic, yesterday topped the ballot for private member's bills. This means that Mr Budgen, the MP for Wolverhampton South-West, has prime parliamentary time to introduce legislation of his choice during this session. The second name in the ballot was Mr Alan Beith (Berwick-upon-Tweed) the Liberal Democrat treasury spokes-man. Neither said what subjects their bills would cover. Third came Mr Kevin Barron (Rother Valley), a Labour employment spokesman. The ballot was drawn in a crowded Commons committee room by Mr Michael Morris, the deputy speaker. Fourth was Mr David Lidington (Con, Aylesbury), who was elected to the commons for the first time last year, followed by former Conservative cabinet minister Mr Michael Jopling (Westmorland and Lonsdale) and Mr Peter Atkinson (Con, Hexham), who also entered the House last year. The top six in the ballot are always thought to have the best chance of getting their own legislation through, particularly if it is not controversial. The House of Commons is to rise for the Christmas recess on December 17, the same date as the Lords, and return on Tuesday January 11, a day later than peers, Mr Tony Newton, the Leader of the House, announced yesterday. Mr Dennis Skinner, Labour MP for Bolsover, protested that parliament will have sat for no more than seven months this year. Mr Newton said: 'For the great majority of MPs, a very large part of their work is conducted in their constituencies and needs to be combined with their duties here.'
