October 14, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

CHUNG AND BRANDO

I always knew that TV's Connie Chung was a lightweight, and her interview with Marlon Brando last Saturday proved it. It was like a teeny-bopper journalist asking questions of a rock idol. She was weak and obviously intimidated.

Brando's arrogance, his jerklike acting and his demeaning of his profession made him less than a riveting interviewee. But wouldn't a Mike Wallace or a Sam Donaldson or a Barbara Walters have challenged him -- and given us more of an insight into this very private and talented man?

HAL ROTHBERG, Woodland Hills
