BFN [Text] The number of patients who called at popular medical clinics in 1993 complaining of different diseases was more than 8 million, while the number of patients who called at these clinics in 1992 was not more than 6 million. Dr. 'Imad Jassam [not further identified] has said the increase in the number of patients is due to malnutrition suffered by children, mothers, and old people and to the scarcity of essential drugs for them. He pointed out that the number of patients suffering from chronic diseases has exceeded 328,000. Dr. Jassam added that popular clinics in all governorates employ a total of 88 specialist doctors, 540 general practitioners, 92 dentists, and more than 630 members of the health care and nursing professions.
