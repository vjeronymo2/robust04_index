BFN [Text] Prague--Slovak Foreign Minister Eduard Kukan has asserted that Syria is a primary and important state in the Middle East and that, without Syria, a just and comprehensive peace cannot be achieved. In a statement to a SANA correspondent in Prague today, Kukan added that his country appreciates the Syrian policy aimed at achieving a just and comprehensive peace in the region. He also emphasized Slovakia's support for the peace process, which is based on both the UN resolutions and international legitimacy. The Slovak minister stressed the depth of Syrian-Slovak relations, which have always been fruitful in all fields. He said his coming visit to Damascus on the day after tomorrow, 24 June, aims to promote and consolidate the traditional relations between Syria and Slovakia in a way that will serve both friendly countries.
