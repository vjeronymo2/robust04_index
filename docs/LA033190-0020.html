March 31, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

BLACK MEN AND JUSTICE SYSTEM

The Times is to be commended for printing the articles by Brown, Legrand Clegg and Jewell Taylor Gibbs (March 17).

I am one of the "white masses" but I am deeply distressed at the killings in the black inner-city neighborhoods and at society's abandonment of the children from the moment of birth. They are programmed to poor education, to drugs, jail and/or early death.

So long as these deplorable conditions exist for blacks in the inner cities, no one can live in peace or tranquility.

DOLLY ADAMS

Los Angeles
