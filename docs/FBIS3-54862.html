Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev -- A UNIAN correspondent was informed today at the Central Electoral Commission for the elections of Ukrainian people's deputies that, in accordance with Article 14 of the Law on Elections, the Central Electoral Commission has adopted resolutions registering the Ukrainian Christian Democratic Party, the Democratic Party of Ukraine, the People's Movement of Ukraine, the Party of Labor, the Social Democratic Party of Ukraine, the Ukrainian Party of Solidarity and Social Justice, the Party of Free Peasants of Ukraine, the Peasants' Party of Ukraine, the Socialist Party of Ukraine and the Ukrainian Conservative Republican Party, in connection with their intention to nominate candidates for participation in the elections of Ukrainian people's deputies on 27 March 1994. Central Electoral Commission Chairman Ivan Yemets handed representative of the above political parties the relevant registration certificates. A total of 17 political parties have so far been registered by the Central Electoral Commission. None of them have expressed any desire to form a bloc at national level. Commenting on this phenomenon, the Central Electoral Commission's secretary, Ihor Tseluyko, explains: "The point is that it is disadvantegeous for parties to form a bloc at the level of the Central Electoral Commission, since in accordance with the law a bloc can only enter one candidate. It is only at the regional level that blocs declared at party congresses are likely to appear, which will at least enable them to bring through a deputy from a friendly camp, if not one of their own."
