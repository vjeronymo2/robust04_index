Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 24 March. Eurobonds have become a good alternative for Peruvian companies that need to obtain financing without paying the high interest rates charged by the local financial system. Companies must pay those who buy the bonds an average interest rate of 8 to 10 percent per year to obtain the disbursement almost immediately. The Arias Group's La Poderosa mine was the first company to issue Eurobonds. It obtained a 10.75 percent profit in one year from the sale of $6 million worth of Eurobonds issued in December 1993. The Rodriguez Banda Group's Gloria company and the Tele2000 company will issue $40 million each in Eurobonds in March. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 9 Mar 94 p A24) National Mining Association President Roque Benavides Ganoza expects the mining sector to attract $7 billion in investment in the next five years. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 12 Mar 94 p A26) After six years, the French Government has decided to renew trade guarantees for Peruvian exports to France. The French Insurance Company for Foreign Trade has guaranteed short-term exports valued at approximately $3.5 million, which could grow to $9.5 million in 1995. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 11 Mar 94 p A24) A Mining and Energy Ministry source reported that gold production increased 12 percent in 1993, after the Yanacocha mining processing plant began operation. Production totalled 57,603 troy ounces in January, 6 percent more than in the same month last year. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 11 Mar 94 B1) The Central Reserve Bank's net international reserves reached a record $3.46 billion through 18 February, an increase of $260 million compared with the previous week. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 11 Mar 94 p B2) On 15 March, the Mining and Energy Ministry reported that average oil production was 130,763 barrels per day in the January-February period, higher than the same months in 1993 when it was 123,440 barrels. The ministry expects average oil production in 1994 to be 132,000 barrels per day. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 16 Mar 94 p B3) The Andean Development Corporation approved credits of up $140 million for Peru. They will be used to implement programs for small enterprises and highway development projects. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 16 Mar 94 p 1) Japan granted Peru a $21 million loan to finance health programs. It also will donate equipment worth $180,000 for people affected by floods. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 16 Mar 94 p 1) During the 15 March congressional session, Economy Minister Jorge Camet, Agriculture Minister Absalon Vasquez, and representatives of the Democratic Constituent Congress Agriculture Committee declared that the agricultural sector has been in a state of emergency for approximately two years in the wake of floods and plagues affecting the countryside. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 16 Mar 94 p B1) Peruvian and Chilean entrepreneurs formed a joint venture for the exclusive commercialization of Volkswagen cars in Peru with an $18 million investment. The Chilean economic group Matisse will provide 40 percent of the capital and the Peruvian investors 60 percent. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 17 Mar 94 p B2)
