BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, June 21 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- The Air Forces of Malaysia and Singapore will hold a one-day air defence exercise on Thursday under the Five Power Defence Arrangement (FPDA), the Ministry of Defence said here Tuesday. Codenamed Minor Adex 94, the exercise was aimed at examining the air defence forces of member countries against a simulated external air threat. It would be co-ordinated by the Integrated Air Defence System headquarters at the Royal Malaysian Air Force (RMAF) base in Butterworth, northern Pulau Penang state. Malaysian aircraft involved in the exercise include the Northrop F-5E jetfighter and PC-130 patrol aircraft while the Republic of Singapore Air Force (RSAF) will deploy its F-16 Falcon, A-4S Superskyhawk fighter bombers and C-130 Hercules transport aircraft. The RSAF will also deploy the improved Hawk missile system for air defence and a 35-mm anti-aircraft gun battery for the exercise to be conducted in Malaysian and Singapore air space.
