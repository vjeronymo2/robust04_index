BFN [Interview given by UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali to IZVESTIYA; date and place not given: "Butrus-Ghali: UN Does Not Exist To Punish People. UN Secretary General Answers IZVESTIYA's Questions"] [Text] [IZVESTIYA] In what way does the United Nations hope to reconcile the principle of noninterference in other states' internal affairs with its efforts aimed at asserting human rights and democracy worldwide? [Butrus-Ghali] The definition of human rights is contained in international treaties to which states accede on a voluntary basis. After the treaties are ratified, states assume international pledges to respect the spirit and letter of these agreements. As a result, the international community of nations acquires the powers to monitor and observe how states observe the pledges they have entered into. Moreover, existing international law sees major human rights violations as legitimate grounds for alarm. [IZVESTIYA] How do you plan to combine the right of self-determination and the principle of the inviolability of state borders? [Butrus-Ghali] Although the term "self-determination" has not yet been clearly defined, the international community recognizes that all peoples have the right of self-determination and are free on that basis to choose their political status and to implement their economic, social, and cultural development. Implementation of the right of self-determination should not infringe the territorial inviolability or political independence of any state. [IZVESTIYA] What methods of punishing states which violate UN principles are currently under consideration? [Butrus-Ghali] The United Nations does not strive to "punish" states which violate its principles. But if the Security Council considers it necessary, it can call on parties to a dispute whose continuation could pose a threat to the maintenance of international peace and security to resolve that dispute through negotiation, investigation, mediation, reconciliation, arbitration, judicial examination, or an appeal to regional organs. The Security Council can also call on UN members to break off economic relations or rail, maritime, air, postal, telegraph, radio, and other forms of communication with the state violating international norms, as well as breaking off diplomatic relations with it. [IZVESTIYA] How does the United Nations rate the prospects for the use of armed forces for peacekeeping purposes? Practice has shown that even the operations in Cambodia failed to produce the expected results. [Butrus-Ghali] The establishment of peace is primarily tied in with the utilization of armed forces against those who use force to violate a peace agreement. This approach is laid down in the rules governing peacekeeping operations. But the main question is different -- will the United Nations have enough military resources to carry out such measures, and will it have enough political will to see them through? If the answer to both questions is in the affirmative, then it may be possible to establish peace. [Butrus-Ghali ends] UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali arrived on a five-day official visit to the Russian capital Friday. The VIP guest's first statement to journalists on Russian soil was brief: "I am very glad to have the opportunity to visit Moscow again."
