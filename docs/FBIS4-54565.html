BFN [Editorial Report] Slovak newspapers on 28 and 29 April publish reports on comments made by representatives of Slovak right-wing and center parties on the meeting of these parties' leaders in Bratislava on 26 April, which dealt with their cooperation in the election campaign. Bratislava HOSPODARSKE NOVINY in Slovak on 28 April on page 3 carries a 200-word TA SR report on a news conference given by National Democratic Party-New Alternative Chairman Ludovit Cernak in Bratislava on 27 April. Cernak is quoted as saying that the five parties attending the 26 April meeting "showed interest in cooperating. They will agree on the form of this cooperation by the end of May at the latest." Cernak reportedly also said that all five parties "practically rejected the possibility of postelection cooperation with the Movement for a Democratic Slovakia." Bratislava SME in Slovak on 29 April on page 3 carries a 200-word "dos"-signed report on a news conference given by the leadership of the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) in Bratislava on 28 April. Speaking about the 26 April meeting of the leaders of "five right-wing and centrist parties," KDH Chairman Jan Carnogursky reportedly described it "as unlikely that all of these parties would contest the elections with a single slate of candidates. He supposes, however, that the number of slates of candidates will be under five."
