Language: French Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Bosnia: General Cot Calls the United States to the Rescue"] [Excerpt] Yasushi Akashi, special representative of the UN secretary general for the region, said that UNPROFOR [UN Protection Force] , which comprises around 14,000 troops in Bosnia, needs an additional 10,650 troops immediately, of which 4,600 would be deployed in Sarajevo and 6,050 would monitor the enforcement of the Croatian-Muslim cease-fire in central and southern Bosnia. Mr. Akashi said that he was "deeply concerned about the reluctant and negative reply" from the international community to the reinforcement request for Bosnia. Meanwhile, General Cot criticized NATO's attitude because of its readiness to send troops to Bosnia only after a comprehensive peace agreement has been reached. It is "a funny and not very brave decision," he said. "Anyone can change their minds and send troops to Bosnia tomorrow morning. I really would like the United States to send troops tomorrow morning," he added. "I need men immediately, but the nations which could dispatch troops immediately can be counted on the fingers of one hand," said Cot, who mentioned the United States, Great Britain, and France in this regard. "Plans were drawn up by NATO providing for 100,000 troops. This was then reduced to 50,000. Today, I am asking for 10,000 men. If one does not want everything that has been achieved in Sarajevo at the cost of incredible efforts to be completely undone, then those troops must come immediately," the French general stressed. "If they do not come rapidly, I am afraid that what has been won will be quickly lost."
