Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 9 Feb (MENA)--Egyptian sources today said that the Palestinian and Israeli sides have made progress in their talks over the past two days, particularly with regard to the remaining problems facing the implementation of the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles. The sources said that the two delegations will continue their meetings today to examine points of disagreement concerning the crossings and the Jericho area. The sources said that the outcome of the Palestinian-Israeli talks is expected to be announced tonight or tomorrow. Israel's Ambassador to Cairo David Sultan said the talks have made progress. He voiced the hope that more progress will follow and that the remaining points of disagreement will be settled. The Israeli ambassador said the talks will continue today and that there is a climate of optimism. The ambassador pointed to a need to reach agreement on particular issues but added that others have been settled in talks over the past two days. The Israeli ambassador declined to elaborate further, saying that the talks are still under way and that it is better to wait until they are over before discussing their details.
