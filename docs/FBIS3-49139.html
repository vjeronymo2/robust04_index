Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague Jan 4 (CTK) -- Overall returns from small privatisation, which was completed on December 31, 1993, reached 35.1 billion crowns (over one billion USD) [U.S. Dollars], National Property Fund Chairman Tomas Jezek says in today's issue of the daily CESKY DENIK. A total of 3.9 billion crowns of that have thus far been spent. Administrative costs such as, for example, those for privatisation commissions, were 240 million crowns, while 450 million crowns was spent on depreciated property returned to its original owners. Settlement of liquidation consequences of state-owned companies have thus far demanded close to 2 billion crowns. Town councils obtained a one-time payment of 1.2 billion crowns as compensation for enterprises which represented income for local budgets and were privatised or restituted. The net balance from the process is 31.2 billion crowns, of which 20 billion has been deposited long-term, the interest of which is to be used in the privatisation of health care, Jezek concluded.
