Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Doha, 7 Jan -- Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad al-Jabir al-Sabah, first deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs, has revealed in press statements published here that he will visit Yemen, but did not mention the date of the visit. In statements to the Qatari press reported by the QATARI NEWS AGENCY [QNA], Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad said that the program of his foreign tour included a visit to Yemen, but the current conditions that Yemen was experiencing did not allow this visit. He added "when these conditions end and disappear I will visit Sanaa." The first deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs expressed regrets over current events in Yemen. He also expressed regrets and astonishment for the rumors implicating Kuwait in the rift and dispute between north and south Yemen, saying that this is void of truth and a mere fabrication. He pointed out that Kuwait is very far removed from the dispute that the state of Yemen is currently experiencing and this matter "hurts us very much." [passage omitted]
