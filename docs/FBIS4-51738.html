BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 30 (KCNA) -- The Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea received solidarity messages from foreign parties and party leaders supporting the Korean people's struggle for defending the dignity of their nation and the sovereignty of their country. The messages came from Jean Pierre Ngambi, general secretary of the Communist Party of Cameroon, Eduardo Mora Valverde, convenor of the Central Committee of the People's Party of Costa Rica, Humberto Vargas Carbonel, secretary general of the Central Committee of the People's Vanguard of Costa Rica, Stevan Mirkovic, president of the League of Communists of Yugoslavia, Otete Gaston Mboyo, national chairman of the National Movement of the Genuine Lumumbist Combatants of Zaire, the National Executive Committee of the Communist Party of the Congo and the Central Committee of the Galicia People's Union of Spain. The National Executive Committee of the Communist Party of the Congo in its message expressed full support to the just measure taken by the DPRK Government to defend the security and sovereignty of the country. It contended that the nuclear issue of Korea should be solved through DPRK-USA negotiations, not by means of "sanctions" or pressure. The message from Zaire urged that the United States should immediately stop all its crafty moves to provoke another war on the Korean peninsula and that the International Atomic Energy Agency should act with discretion in accordance with its mission as a genuine scientific organization. The general secretary of the Communist Party of Cameroon in his message demanded a prompt halt to all the attempts to lead the situation on the Korean peninsula to the brink of a war.
