940111 FT 11 JAN 94 / BCCI-connected trial set to start The trial of Mr Mohammed Baqi, a director of Attock Oil International charged with fraud over his alleged involvement with the collapsed Bank of Credit and Commerce International, is set to start later this week. Selection of the jury to try Mr Baqi began yesterday at the Old Bailey in London. The jurors are expected to be formally sworn in today. Mr Baqi faces 16 charges concerning the alleged furnishing of false information about the finances of BCCI. The Serious Fraud Office, which has brought the prosecution, alleges he conspired with members of the bank's management to furnish documents which falsely indicated that the bank was owed a total of Dollars 123m (Pounds 83m) by Attock Oil. Mr Baqi denies all the charges. The trial is expected to last up to two months.
