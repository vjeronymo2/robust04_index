Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Jose Gart from the "Radio Events" newscast] [Text] Yatama [Indian military organization] leaders attacked 50 FSLN activists following a rally to close an FSLN [Sandinist National Liberation Front] political campaign in Puerto Cabezas. FSLN candidate (Evelyn Watler) was beaten up, and a window in a station wagon owned by Victor Espinoza, FSLN political secretary in Puerto Cabezas, was smashed. Brooklyn Rivera, YATAMA's top leader and currently director of INDERA [Nicaraguan Institute for the Development of the Autonomous Regions of the Atlantic Coast], headed the attack. Alfonso Smith, governor of the North Atlantic Autonomous Region, Kenneth Bush, the Government Ministry's delegate, and other YATAMA activists also lead the criminal action. The FSLN issued a statement on Radio Caribe on the night of 23 February repudiating the attitude of the YATAMA leaders and urging authorities to restore order in the North Atlantic Autonomous Region.
