BFN [Text] This morning, at the conclusion of the eighth session of the Iran-Pakistan joint economic commission, a memorandum of understanding was signed between the two countries. In this memorandum of understanding, signed by Road and Transport Minister Torkan and Wassim Ja'fari, the economic adviser to the prime minister of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, agreement is reached on the joint construction of oil refinery [number not specified] and a gas pipeline to Pakistan, reconstruction of flood-damaged bridges in Pakistan, and construction of 1,300 km of roads in that country. The memorandum also stressed the joint construction of the Kerman to Zahedan and Mirjaveh railway lines and improvement of the lines from Quetta to Taftan --by utilizing the two countries' financial and technical resources -- establishment of round-trip air routes between Zahedan and Quetta and between Lahore and Mashhad, and also the addition of a second railway service from Zahedan to Quetta. The Iranian side, in this memorandum, has declared readiness to accept transit and trans-shipment of goods from Iranian ports and dredging of Pakistani ports and building of new ports at competitive international rates. Other agreements reached in this memorandum include the provision of bank credits and customs facilities in order to speed up commercial services, transport of goods and passengers between Iran and Pakistan, and setting up of exclusive commodity exhibitions with the right to sell in each other's territory.
