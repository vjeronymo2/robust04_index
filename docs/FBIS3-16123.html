Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Black Sea Fleet considers itself a Russian one and it must become Russian Fleet, commander-in-chief of Russia's Navy Felix Gromov declared at a press conference on Saturday [12 March] in St. Petersburg. He underlined that the fate of the Black Sea Fleet should be solved in accordance with the agreement between Russian and Ukrainian presidents "according to which, the Fleet should be transferred to Russia." Gromov negatively assessed attempts of the Ukrainian party to drag out the process of the Fleet's transfer, and, in particular, the proposal to make an inventory of the Fleet's property. According to Gromov, Russian Navy plans to receive new ships from the industry, "We need a fleet which corresponds to Russia's economy and is able to fulfill tasks related to border guarding," stressed Gromov. He also reported that the heavy atomic missile cruiser Petr Velikiy under construction at the Baltiyskiy factory in St. Petersburg is almost ready and will be put into operation soon. The crew has already arrived in St. Petersburg; the cruiser is to enter composition of the Pacific Fleet.
