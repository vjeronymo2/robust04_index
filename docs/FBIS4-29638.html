BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 20 KYODO -- The Ministry of Finance on Monday [20 June] gave approval to Unum Life Insurance Co., a major U.S. accident insurance company, to sell workers' accident compensation insurance in Japan, ministry officials said. The company, based in Portland, Maine, will set up its Japanese unit in Tokyo at a capital of 3 billion yen and start its operations in October, the officials said. Unum will sell a new type of insurance that assures long-term compensation of income for the insured, who are unable to keep working because of injuries and diseases, the officials said.
