920912 FT 12 SEP 92 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Nikkei slips 4 per cent on futures selling STOCKS closed sharply lower on futures-linked selling and publication of the Bank of Japan's tankan, or quarterly economic survey, which showed business confidence at its lowest level since the mid-1970s, agencies report from Tokyo. The Nikkei average lost 800.78 or 4.2 per cent to 18,107.69, a 2.4 per cent decline on the week, closing just above its intraday low of 18,097.55 after a high of 18,902.85 in the morning session. Volume was estimated at 700m shares, up from 625.4m, declines exceeding advances by 898 to 165 with 89 unchanged. The Topix index of all first section stocks lost 45.12 or 3.2 per cent to 1,369.85 and in London the ISE/Nikkei 50 index fell 3.84 to 1,093.23. 'Bad data was expected, so the tankan is no surprise,' one analyst said. 'Today's fall was mostly due to futures.' Investors expected the settlement price of the September Nikkei futures contract to be in the low 19,000s, but it turned out to be 18,631. Brokers said that investors who had bought all week, expecting a technical rally over 19,000 in the morning session, took profits. Speculative biotechnology shares lost ground in heavy volume. Green Cross lost Y60 to Y1,720 in volume of 11.85m shares while Meiji Milk lost Y40 to Y1,080 in turnover of 11.32m shares. Blue-chip electricals held up well with Sharp unchanged at Y1,070 and Mitsubishi Electric down Y7 at Y488. NTT dropped sharply, losing Y24,000 to Y576,000. Banks fell heavily with IBJ down Y110 to Y2,820, Bank of Tokyo losing Y100 to Y1,290, and Sumitomo Bank Y110 weaker at Y1,700. Roundup PACIFIC Rim markets showed a mixed performance yesterday with Bangkok continuing to improve. Seoul and Taiwan were closed for public holidays. HONG KONG continued to blame Sino-US trade friction as the Hang Seng index fell another 94.26, or 1.7 per cent to 5,537.29, down 3 per cent on the week in turnover ofHKDollars 1.82bn, up from HKDollars 1.46bn. Overseas institutions led the selling but selective buying, mostly by local Chinese fund investors, was said to have prevented a worse plunge. Brokers attributed this to a rebounding US dollar, to which the local currency is pegged, and sound fundamentals. AUSTRALIA maintained its focus on Westpac, with another 51m rights to the bank's share issue traded. The All Ordinaries index closed up 17.2 at 1,501.00, down 1.9 per cent on the week, in turnover of ADollars 260.2m. Westpac closed 4 cents higher at ADollars 2.92, off an intraday high of ADollars 3.02. Elsewhere News Corp gained 22 cents to ADollars 23.32 while Foster's Brewing fell 5 cents to ADollars 1.48 ahead of Monday's annual results. BANGKOK rose further, the SET index gaining 10.70 or 1.35 per cent to 801.90 in turnover of Bt11.15bn. The market has advanced 5 per cent over the week. Krisda Mahanakorn, a property group, was the day's most active issue, up Bt16 at Bt218. KUALA LUMPUR investors liquidated positions following the sharp fall in Tokyo, and the KLSE composite index fell 3.40 to at 580.79, a fraction lower on the week. Fund managers kept to the sidelines ahead of the Malaysian budget next month. MANILA advanced as a strong performance by PLDT overnight in the US prompted last-minute buying. The composite index gained 4.96 to 1,417.96, a rise of 1.86 per cent on the week. PLDT gained 10 pesos to 975 pesos, and combined turnover rose to 262m pesos from 195m peses. NEW ZEALAND edged higher as Fletcher Challenge reversed falls earlier in the week. The NZSE-40 index rose 7.39 to 1,461.17, 1.2 per cent lower on the week. Turnover was NZDollars 18m. Fletcher Challenge gained 7 cents to NZDollars 2.21.
