BFN [Text] The National Assembly Office issued Communique No. 10, which indicated that this morning, the National Assembly deputies studied, exchanged views, and contributed views to a number of clauses and sections of the revised draft laws on the organization of people's councils and people's committees and on the election of people's councillors. This afternoon, the National Assembly met in the conference hall under the direction of National Assembly Vice Chairman Nguyen Ha Phan to debate on the draft law on local investment promotion. Altogether, 14 deputies aired their views. The deputies were: Le Xuan Tung of Hanoi, Tran Du Lich of Ho Chi Minh City, Nguyen Ngoc Tran of An Giang, Nguyen Dinh Giao of Quang Ninh, Hoang Minh Thang of Quang Nam-Da Nang, Phan Lam Phuong of Quang Binh, Lam Phung Vien of Soc Trang, Nguyen Duy Anh of Lam Dong, Nguyen Thu Ha of Thai Binh, Le Thi Thuy of Nghe An, Do Quoc Hung of Ba Ria-Vung Tau, Tran Chu Toan of Gia Lai, Nguyen Nhieu Coc of Hoa Binh, and Le Thi Thanh Liem of Ben Tre. In expressing their views, the deputies concured with the names of the laws and with the necessity to promulgate them. They also maintained that once adopted by the National Assembly, the law on local investment promotion will create the legal basis and favorable conditions for the people to have a sense of security while investing in production and business operations, thus contributing to making the goal of making the people rich and the country powerful come true. The deputies focused on key issues as follows: objectives and areas that need to be amended, role of state business establishments, measures to ensure and support investment, problems concerning the establishment of an investment support fund and investment credit gurantee, types of occupations and areas of activities that need investment privileges, and state management over investment promotion. Tomorrow, 7 June, the National Assembly will continue to meet in the conference hall to debate the draft law on local investment promotion.
