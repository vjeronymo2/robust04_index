Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sochi March 21 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin vacationing on the Black Sea met with Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin on Monday to discuss a range of political and economic issues. Among the issues discussed were implementation of the president's state-of-the-nation address on strengthening Russian statehood, the state budget and ways to reach civic accord in the country, the presidential press service said. Special attention was paid to the preparations for the upcoming spring sowing campaign in Russian agricultural regions, as well as the situation in Georgia, Abkhazia, Nagorno-Karabakh and other issues. After the one-to-one talks, the president and the premier were joined by Krasnodar regional administration head Nikolay Yegorov and Sochi mayor Nikolay Karpov. They agreed to set up working groups to work out a draft agreement on the delineation of powers between federal and local bodies of power as envisaged by the constitution and the federal treaty. Yeltsin and Chernomyrdin supported the idea of holding the 2002 Winter Olympic Games in Sochi.
