Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, February 1 (SNARK)--Today the Armenian Parliament has almost unanimously given its consent to institute criminal proceedings against Myasnik Hakopyan, the chairman of the Council of Businessmen, Leaseholders and Cooperators. He is prosecuted for misappropriation of state property of great dimensions. Artavazd Gevorkyan, the general prosecutor of Armenia, states the chairman of the Businessmen's Council misappropriated by means of machinations and forgery more than 34 million rubles. 2000 cartridges were found during his apartment's search. Myasnik Hakopyan was one of the candidates on the president's post. The parliament's consent was necessary because Dashnaktsutyun Party had proposed Hakopyan as a candidate to the Supreme Soviet of Armenia. Therefore, Dashnaktsutyun demanded his immediate release and remonstrated at the Office of Public Prosecutor.
