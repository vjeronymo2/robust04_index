Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Vladimir Taranov] [Text] Moscow January 15 TASS--U.S. President Bill Clinton and his wife have just left here by air for Minsk at the close of a three-day official visit to Russia. The chief result of the Russian-U.S. talks in Moscow is in the signing of a Moscow declaration by the two presidents, the declaration which reaffirmed the importance of Russian-U.S. cooperation for global stability, as well as in a joint Russo- American-Ukrainian agreement on the removal of nuclear weapons from Ukraine's territory. The agreement became a breakthrough in the field of non-proliferation of nuclear weapons. In all, 12 bilateral documents were adopted during the summit.
