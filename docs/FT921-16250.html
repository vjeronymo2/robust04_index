920104 FT 04 JAN 92 / Fraud is alleged in Algerian election ALGERIA'S constitutional court has received around 300 claims from political parties alleging fraud in a third of the country's 430 constituencies inlast week's elections. About half the claims have been lodged by candidates of the ruling National Liberation Front (FLN). The FLN polled 1.5m votes, compared with more than 3m for their opponents in the Islamic Salvation Front (FIS). But the FLN won only 15 seats, against 188 for the FIS. Reuter adds: A Moslem fundamentalist, Abdelkader Moghni, newly elected to parliament, said yesterday that democracy was atheism and Islam offered the only true freedom.
