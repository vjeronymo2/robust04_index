921008 FT 08 OCT 92 / Fine china comes under the hammer FUDDLING CUPS, posset pots, herpetological dishes, pill slabs, drug jars, bleeding bowls and other pieces of early English pottery fetched good prices at Sotheby's yesterday when the second half of the John Philip Kassebaum collection came under the hammer. The top price of Pounds 49,500 was paid by Mr Jonathan Horne, a London dealer, for a Southwark drug jar dated 1656 and with the arms of the Worshipful Society of Apothecaries.
