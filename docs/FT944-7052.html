941126 FT 26 NOV 94 / Weekend Money (Financial Planning): Relief on interest - Briefcase The mortgage on my sole residence is with a lender, Western Trust & Savings, but its interest rate is not particularly competitive. If I re-mortgage with a different lender, will I still be able to claim tax relief on the interest charged? The present loan is under Miras and I am a standard-rate taxpayer. In principle the answer is 'yes', by virtue of section 354(1)(c) of the Income and Corporation Taxes Act 1988. This gives relief for interest paid 'on a loan to defray money applied . . . in paying off another loan if interest on that other loan would have been eligible for relief under section 353 had the loan not been paid off . . .' However, the answer would be 'not entirely' - because of the restrictions imposed by section 355(2A & 2B) - if, for example, part of your existing mortgage covered 'money applied in improving or developing land or buildings on land, otherwise than by the erection of a new building (which is not part of an existing residence) on land which, immediately before the improvement or development began, had no building on it'. We could have given you a simpler and more helpful reply if you had given us more precise background facts.
