920707 FT 07 JUL 92 / Sales of new cars up 4.4% in June NEW CAR sales in the UK rose for the third month running last month. But the recovery remains weak and there is concern in the industry about prospects for the rest of this year. The Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders said yesterday that sales in June were up 4.4 per cent, to 102,566 units, compared with the same month a year ago. This followed year-on-year increases of 9.1 per cent in April but only 1.4 per cent in May. The overall result is that sales for the first half of the year, at 768,745, are still languishing 4.11 per cent below 801,684 in the 1991's first half. Sir Hal Miller, chief executive of the society, warned yesterday that total sales this year remain in danger of falling below even last year's level of 1.59m units. The industry is now monitoring order intakes for August, when the new registration prefix is introduced. More than a fifth of each year's total sales are made in August, with July typically accounting for only about 2 per cent of annual sales. 'If the industry doesn't achieve 400,000 sales in August it's going to have a big problem', one senior Vauxhall executive said. To achieve this, sales would have to be nearly 9 per cent above the 367,646 achieved last year. Ford, the market leader, saw its market share drop to 20.66 per cent last month from 27.30 per cent a year ago.
