940505 FT 05 MAY 94 / Ageism payout decision tomorrow A Birmingham industrial tribunal will tomorrow decide how much compensation is due to three workers unfairly dismissed on grounds of age. The tribunal has already decided that Mr Ray Walker, 62, Mr Larry Nolan, 61, and Mr Peter Kiddy, 62, were unfairly dismissed by Carbodies, in Coventry, maker of taxis, when it was decided that all workers over 60 would be made redundant. This was one of the first industrial tribunal cases to accept age discrimination as grounds for unfair dismissal, and tomorrow's decision will be closely watched. The three were dismissed in September 1992 and their unions are hoping for compensation payments of up to Pounds 17,000 each. Mr Mike Robinson, regional officer for the MSF technical union, said yesterday: 'Carbodies selected these people for dismissal solely on the criteria of what was their date of birth. No account was taken of their service, skill or the fact that over 60's are just as capable as anyone else.' The government argues that specific legislation against ageism, as in the US, would be counter-productive. But Miss Ann Widdecombe, a minister in the Department of Employment, has been campaigning vigorously against ageism. Miss Widdecombe recently launched a government booklet on how to stamp out ageism at the workplace. The booklet suggests five steps to avoid discrimination: dropping age bars, selecting on ability, welcoming older applicants, offering flexible working and investing in workers, regardless of age. She said: 'Research confirms that older people offer employers skills, reliability and experience. They are adaptable and as trainable as their younger counterparts, and their commitment and loyalty is as great or greater.'
