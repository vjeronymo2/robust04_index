Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Russian People's Party led by Telman Gdlyan has urged President Boris Yeltsin to ask the Constitutional Court to question the legitimacy of the State Duma resolution concerning amnesty. In its statement published Saturday evening the party qualifies the resolution on granting amnesty to those who "instigated and joined" the events of August 19-21, 1991, May 1 and September 21-October 4, 1993 as "another victory won by the anti-reformist forces and disregard for the norms of morality and law". According to the document, connivance at these forces was what inspired them to stage their revolt in August of 1991, which led to frustrating the plans to sign a union treaty, breaking up the USSR and exposing the nation to severe hardships. "Unfortunately, as a result of political near-sightedness, the Russian leadership continued Gorbachev's policy of conciliation with the reactionary forces," the document states. According to its authors, the State Duma resolution, "which is allegedly aimed at reaching conciliation and national concord, is in fact an attempt to legitimize lawlessness, anarchy and the use of force in political strife". The Russian People's Party expressed concern over the fact that the encouragement of those who organized and perpetrated "monstrous crimes" may lead to another coup "aimed at curbing democratic reforms and unleashing a civil war".
