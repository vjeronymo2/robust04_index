Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Nelson Murillo] [Text] Puntarenas -- Finance Minister Carlos Munoz said yesterday that the country will face difficulties in servicing the foreign debt and a reduction in available credit if the future administration decides to replace the PAE III [Economic Adjustment Program] with loans to specific sectors. After saying it was inadvisable to continue "patching up the economy," the official asserted that opting for loans to specific sectors instead of the PAE III would entail at least 18 months of negotiations. Thus, the official stated, the loan option is "ineffective." Munoz was one of the main speakers at a economics seminar attended by 30 journalists at the Hotel Fiesta in Puntarenas. The seminar ended yesterday. At that event, Munoz said the country should continue on the path to economic modernization, commercial opening, and smaller government. The finance minister emphasized that the Jose Maria Figueres Olsen administration, which will take office on 8 May, could renegotiate PAE III with the World Bank. The minister pointed out, however, that the government would loose its ability to pay the foreign debt and, in this case, international creditors would probably withdraw our credit. Central Bank President Jorge Corrales emphasized that the negotiation of separate loans to certain sectors as an alternative to the PAE III would lead to high payments by Costa Rica and -- in his opinion -- there are not enough resources for that. President-elect Jose Maria Figueres said the debate on PAE III "had been overcome" when he met with President Rafael Angel Calderon Fournier. The current government insists that this program, which will provide the country with $180 million, should be approved by the Legislative Assembly, while the future administration rejects the program because it believes that it was not negotiated "in a Costa Rican manner [a la tica]." Minister Munoz asserted that the PAE III would integrate the Costa Rican economy into the new international commerce currents because it will increase productivity, reorganize the financial sector, promote savings, and guarantee more funds for social programs. He added that it would reduce poverty and unemployment and would allow the private sector to receive a flow of resources that would encourage production. Commenting on the economic reforms by the Calderon Fournier administration, Munoz said: "This process cannot be a reversed. I don't believe the PLN [National Liberation Party] is thinking of canceling it because that would create serious problems for the country. The PAE III is necessary to maintain economic stability and foreign financing."
