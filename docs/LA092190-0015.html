September 21, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

AMERICAN LEAGUE ROUNDUP;

BAERGA'S 4 HITS LEAD INDIANS PAST YANKEES

Cleveland's Carlos Baerga led the extra-base hit parade Thursday night at New York as the Indians scored 11 runs in the middle three innings in defeating the Yankees, 12-7.

Baerga had a triple and three doubles.

The Indians got 16 hits and tied their season-high with nine doubles. Brook Jacoby had three hits, including two doubles.

Baerga hit an RBI triple in the first inning, but the Yankees bounced back for a 6-1 lead. Baerga doubled to start a four-run fourth inning, doubled home a run in a four-run fifth and hit a two-run double during a three-run sixth that made it 12-7.

Seattle 7, Chicago 3 -- Erik Hanson won his fifth consecutive decision and Henry Cotto homered, tripled and drove in four runs for the Mariners in Seattle.

The loss by the second-place White Sox reduced Oakland's magic number for clinching the American League West to four.

Hanson (16-9) gave up eight hits and retired the last 11 batters.
