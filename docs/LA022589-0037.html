February 25, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

SANTA ANA MAN CONVICTED ON 27 LOAN FRAUD COUNTS

A federal court jury in Los Angeles has convicted Orange County businessman John F. Hayden of defrauding Bank of America and 21 other financial institutions by issuing millions of dollars in worthless loan guarantees.

The jury on Wednesday convicted the Santa Ana resident of 27 fraud counts but acquitted him of one count of interstate transportation of stolen property. On Friday, the jury was unable to return a verdict on a single count of conspiracy. U.S. District Judge Harry L. Hupp declared a mistrial on the conspiracy charge and dismissed it, according to an FBI spokesman.

In an 11-week trial, prosecutors alleged that Hayden used Glacier General Assurance Co. of Montana -- of which he was president -- to induce lenders to make millions of dollars in real estate loans that were never repaid.

Glacier, which had an office in Tustin, did that by guaranteeing repayment of the loans. When the loans later turned out to be fraudulent, Glacier was unable to make good on its guarantees and was declared insolvent.

The persons who received the loans never used them to market a proposed condominium project in Oregon or to build homes in Chatsworth, as was intended by the lenders. Instead, they diverted millions of dollars into their own accounts, prosecutors alleged.

Among Hayden's victims, prosecutors said, was giant Bank of America, which sued Hayden and others in what it alleged was a scheme to swindle $95 million.

Prosecutors said loans from 22 lending institutions were involved in the fraud. Hayden was first indicted with six other men in 1986. All but one have been convicted.

The FBI spokesman said Friday that Hayden's defense attorney has made a motion for a new trial on the the 27 counts of fraud.
