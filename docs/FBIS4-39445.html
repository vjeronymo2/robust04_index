BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Stanislav Bychkov and Oleg Velichko] [Excerpts] Kuala Lumpur, 7 Jun -- Russia has entered the Southeast Asian arms market with the signing here today of a contract to supply Malaysia with 18 MiG-29 fighters. [passage omitted] It is very important for Malaysia that, by starting wide-scale business cooperation with us, Russia is willing to hand over advanced technology to the Malaysians, [Deputy Malaysian Prime Minister] Anwar bin Ibragim stressed in an interview for an ITAR- TASS correspondent. For this reason, he said, Kuala Lumpur regards this present deal as a first major step in bilateral ties in the development of the industrial infrastructure, power engineering, and other spheres. With full justification one can call today's event historic, both for Malaysia and for Russia, Malaysian Defense Minister Najib Abdul Razak told an ITAR-TASS correspondent. "We are the first country in the region to begin defense cooperation with Russia, cooperation which is destined to open up broad new opportunities for business relations between us," the defense minister said. In a speech at the signing ceremony, Oleg Soskovets [first deputy prime minister of Russia] pointed out that the contract is evidence of the start of a new stage in the development of bilateral relations. "The main thing now, from Russia's point of view, is to transform the extensive existing potential into a stable range of practical ties between the two countries," he stressed.
