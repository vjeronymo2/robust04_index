BFN [Text] Director of the Federal Counterintelligence Service Sergei Stepashin voiced his support for the presidential decree on urgent measures to combat organized crime. Addressing the Collegium of the Prosecutor-General's Office on Thursday, he said that without going into legal assessments of the methods of implementing the presidential decree, he fully supported the political decision to pass it. He said that the problem lay in the fact that foreign special services are trying to penetrate into Russian criminal structures, establish control over them and use them to the detriment of Russian interests. He also pointed out that making use of the undefined status of Russian state borders, the national security services of the Baltic states and other republics "have established contacts with Russian criminals for intelligence purposes. He said that a joint directive would be sent to the Russian power ministries' territorial bodies in which the disputed provisions of the decree will be brought into line with current laws. He announced that the Russian president was considering a draft decree on the restoration of the investigation department in the Federal Counter-Intelligence Service. According to his sources, the decree will be signed soon.
