BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Kondrashov] [Text] Belgrade April 14 TASS -- Russian envoy Vitaliy Churkin met with Serb leader Slobodan Milosevic on Thursday to discuss the situation in Bosnia and new Russian initiatives on resolving the crisis, following NATO air strikes on Bosnian Serb positions near Gorazde. In a brief statement for the press, Churkin refrained from commenting on the substance of the proposals. He said though that they aim at putting an end to hostilities in Bosnia and restoring normal life in the republic, which is a prerequisite for a political settlement of the conflict. ITAR-TASS learnt from informed sources that the Serb leader and Bosnian Serb officials may meet with Churkin shortly to discuss Russia's initiatives and work out their position.
