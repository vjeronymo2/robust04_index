BFN [Text] Seoul, April 22 (YONHAP) -- Deputy Prime Minister and National Unification Board [NUB] Minister Yi Yong-tok, was named the new prime minister Friday. Following is his profile: He was president of Myongji University and chairman of the administration's Public Service Ethics Committee before becoming the deputy prime minister and NUB minister in the cabinet reshuffle December 21 last year. Born in North Korea, Yi, 68, has also served as vice president of the Korea National Red Cross and chief delegate to the inter-Korean Red Cross talks. He earned a doctorate of philosophy in education from Ohio State University.
