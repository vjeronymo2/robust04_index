Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 21 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and China are expected to sign an aviation agreement on direct services when President Kim Yong-sam visits Beijing in March, officials said Friday. Working-level negotiations in the Chinese capital have been extended and are progressing in a "positive direction," one said. Korea will succeed in having the Seoul-Beijing flight control transfer point set at 124 degrees longitude as designated by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO). China had been opposed, saying the line was drawn when Taiwan represented China at the ICAO. But the two sides are to skirt the transfer point issue on the Seoul-Shanghai route and fly along a corridor, as done up to now, the official said. Kim is expected to visit Beijing in March and the two countries likely will formally sign the aviation agreement then, he said. The agreement will be initialed at the next aviation talks in Beijing.
