BFN ["AL-AHALI Opinion" column: "We Are Not A Gate To Tighten The Siege of Iraq"] [Text] We have condemned the U.S. aggression against the Port of al-'Aqabah and considered it a manifestation of the comprehensive hostile policy against the whole region. This falls within the framework of the attempts aimed at imposing a new regional order under U.S. tutelage and hegemony. We also cannot find an excuse for entrenching -- with our approval -- this aggression against our national sovereignty under the pretext of conducting land inspection. We want an answer to this simple question: Is there a decision to monitor, besiege, or inspect Jordan? We know there is no decision in this regard; and the UN Sanctions Committee says there is no such decision. The Americans are the ones who are imposing the siege, and the evidence is clear. The talks to lift the siege are being held with U.S. envoys -- the last of whom was Secretary of State Christopher -- not with Butrus-Ghali or with the president of the Security Council as the Iraqi brothers are doing. We wanted the government to champion the opinion of the people, not only the interests of some importers and merchants who are harmed by U.S. piracy. We were ready to fight together in one trench to confront the U.S. aggression against Jordan. The government's view, however, was different. It opted for the policy of ostrich in front of the U.S. hyena, thereby ignoring the view of the people and their political forces as well as the good reputation of our country on the international level. Warren Christopher's statements in London aroused anger and resentment because he linked these measures with tightening the siege of Iraq. Do they want Jordan to become a barrier in the face of Iraq or a gate to ease the burdens of the unjust siege imposed on the people who have always been generous with Jordan, Syria, and Palestine and who watered the soil of these countries with the blood of their martyrs? The policy of weakness and hiding heads in the sand will only lead to more humiliation and disgrace. We are not like that and we will not be like that. We will continue to resist the policy of capitulation and normalization. We will never agree to seeing Jordan become a passageway for U.S.-Israeli peace regardless of the sacrifices.
