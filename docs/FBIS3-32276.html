Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Panamanian elections, which are scheduled for 8 May 1994, monitored through 3 February. Pedro Vallarino Cox, president of the National Renewal Movement (Morena), has invited Ruben Blades, presidential candidate for the Papa Egoro Movement, to support the presidential candidacy of Ruben Dario Carles. When asked if Morena has been set aside now that the Nationalist Liberal Republican Movement (Molirena) has decided to name Carles as its presidential candidate, Vallarino Cox replied: "Carles is the Morena candidate, and I welcome the decision by other parties to support Carles." Presidential candidate Carles has visited David, Chiriqui Province, in the company of Molirena and Morena sympathizers. Carles told his supporters: "I am offering a government that will resolve problems with actions." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 24 Jan 94) Tomas Cabal writes in his "Hidden Truths" column that the candidacy of Ruben Dario Carles has gained strength now that Molirena has decided to support him. He adds: "Many in the Arnulfist Party and Authentic Liberal Party are also endorsing Carles, even though those two parties are supporting Mireya de Gruber's presidential candidacy." (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 30 Jan 94 p6a) Cesar Aparicio, secretary general of the National Integration Movement (Mina), announced during a news conference on 28 January that the party's rank and file members are "going to support the presidential bid of Jose Salvador Munoz of the Doctrinaire Panamenist Party (PPD)." Earlier, Mina President Arrigo Guardia had thrown his support behind Molirena candidate Carles. Aparicio added the PPD will allow certain prominent Mina figures to run for office under the PPD banner. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 29 Jan 94 p4) Talking to San Miguelito residents, presidential candidate Ruben Dario Carles has promised to upgrade social services, such as education, health, transportation, and security. He has said: "The Metropolitan Sanitation Directorate has sufficient workers and equipment to collect garbage, but it is not doing its job." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 31 Jan 94) During a news conference, Carles has vowed he will strive "to stamp out corruption, influence peddling, nepotism, and cronyism." He has said the democratic regime that began on 20 December 1989 "has been timid about ending corruption," adding he will take a hardline position against it with popular support. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 2 Feb 94 p2)
