BFN [Opening speech by Tunisian President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali in Tunis on 2 April -- recorded] [Excerpts] In the name of the God, the compassionate, the merciful. Your Excellencies: It give me great pleasure to welcome you to your country Tunisia, which is happy to welcome you as its respected brothers. Today, Tunisia is once again proud to host the sixth session of the Arab Maghreb Union [AMU] Presidential Council. On your behalf, please permit me to welcome brother Liamine Zeroual, the head of the Algerian state, on the occasion of his joining our council. We would like to renew our congratulations to him on his assumption of this noble responsibility. We wish him every success and all progress in the interest of the brotherly Algerian people. We are sure that his competence, virtues, and belief in Maghreban unity will strongly contribute toward giving more impetus to the march of our union. I am also pleased to welcome brother militant Yasir 'Arafat, the president of the state of Palestine. Through him, we send humble greetings to the brotherly Palestinian people, and renew our support and backing for their heroic struggle for the recovery of their legitimate rights. [passage omitted] Your Excellencies: The AMU is committed to working for the sake of rapprochement and harmony amongst nations; it stresses dialogue and negotiations as a method for solving conflicts; and it is loyal to the ties of brotherhood and solidarity within the Maghreban family. As a result, the endeavors of the AMU have continued uninterrupted on the path for finding a peaceful, just, and honorable solution to the conflict existing between the sisterly Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriyah and certain Western states. Maghreban efforts have concentrated on a persistent search for a political solution to this crisis within the framework of respecting international legality and the protection of the dignity and sovereignty of the Libyan people; we also warned against the repercussions of the its suffering as a result of the continuous sanctions, in addition to the negative outcome of this ongoing crisis. The Maghreban efforts have been joined to those of the Arab family regarding this issue within the seven-man committee; the moves were coordinated on all international levels, mainly at the African and Islamic levels, the Nonaligned Movement, and the United Nations. It is only natural that the AMU should continue its contribution to all endeavors, on all international platforms, to help find a solution to this crisis and reach a just and honorable political solution. [passage omitted]
