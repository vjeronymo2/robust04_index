940121 FT 21 JAN 94 / Polly Peck inquiry man is charged Mr Michael Allcock, the Inland Revenue investigator who was involved in the inquiry into Mr Asil Nadir, the former Polly Peck chairman, was yesterday charged with two offences of conspiring to cheat the Inland Revenue, John Mason writes. Mr Allcock is charged with conspiring with Mr Hisham Alwan, a consultant from Knightsbridge, London, and Mr David Shamoon, a company director from Holland Park, London, to cheat the Revenue. Mr Alwan and Mr Shamoon were both charged with one count each of conspiring with Mr Allcock. Mr Allcock was suspended in September 1992 following an internal inquiry by the Revenue into matters unconnected with Polly Peck. He was arrested in July last year.
