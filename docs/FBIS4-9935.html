BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 4 Apr (NA) -- President Carlos Menem today denied that his chat with Cuban chief of state Fidel Castro was reconciliatory, and he indicated that he had called Castro to recommend a nephew who had traveled to Havana because of health problems. The Argentine president indicated that when he called his Cuban colleague, "Castro was about to make a toast with some Menem wine" that he himself had sent Castro. Menem also indicated that he had taken advantage of the contact "to again thank him for the attention he gave a dear friend, (Cesar) Chacho Jaroslavsky, and to recommend a nephew who also had traveled to Cuba due to health reasons." In remarks to Radio 990, Menem emphasized: "This was all we chatted about," and he said: "Our disagreements within the sphere of democracy and freedom will again be made known during the Cartagena de Indias summit above and beyond these aspects."
