Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, Jan 29 (MENA)--Egypt and Italy have reached an agreement regarding rescheduling the Egyptian debts with Italy, estimated at approximately six hundred million dollars. The agreement was signed in Rome yesterday by Minister of State at the Cabinet for International Cooperation, Yusuf Butrus Ghali. By signing this agreement the Egyptian Government has finalized all the agreements of rescheduling debts with creditor member-states of the Paris Club, announced an official source at the Ministry of International Cooperation. The source added that Ghali has agreed with Italian Foreign Minister Beniamino Andreatta on holding a top-level economic seminar including officials from Egypt, Italy and Turkey for exchanging expertise in the fields of economic reform programs, privatisation and several other economic aspects.
