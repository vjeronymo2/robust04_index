BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 17 Jun (EFE) -- According to remarks by Argentine Foreign Trade Secretary Carlos Kesman, great strides were made at the meeting of vice economy ministers of Common Market of the South [Mercosur] member countries that ended on 17 June. Kesman did not make any concrete announcements about the progress made at the meeting. He just stated that it was a "working meeting" and therefore nobody expected any changes to what had been agreed to by Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay. The analysis of all the pending points on the common external tariff (AEC) was among the most salient issue discussed in the nearly eight-hour-long meeting at the Economy Ministry building in Buenos Aires. Kesman also highlighted the controversy between Argentina and Brazil over capital goods in general and computer software and hardware in particular. He nonetheless denied reports about an alleged "flexibility" evinced by Brazilian authorities. The four countries' representatives also analyzed aspects pertaining to the establishment of free trade zones and the reconciliation of tax policies. The Argentine delegation was headed by Carlos Sanchez, the trade and investment secretary, and Jorge Campbell, the under secretary for commercial integration, who were accompanied by Kesman and Alejandro Mayoral, the under secretary for sectorial programming and integration. Also attending the meeting were [Economic Policy Secretary] Winston Fritch from Brazil -- Finance Minister Rubens Ricupero's right-hand man, Economy and Finance Under Secretary Juan Ignacio Garcia Peluffo from Uruguay, and Integration Vice Minister Ruben Fadlala from Paraguay.
