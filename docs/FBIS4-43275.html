BFN [Text] Shortly before his departure from Ioannina for Athens today, Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias stressed that Greece continues to defend its national sovereign rights in the Aegean. Replying to correspondents' questions, Papoulias once again expressed opposition to the dispatch of Turkish troops to Bosnia-Herzegovina. [Begin Papoulias recording] Greece has always defended its national sovereign rights in the Aegean and this position has not changed, whether it concerns the state power for the extension [of territorial waters] to 12 miles, or the air space, where Greece's positions have been firm since the first PASOK [Panhellenic Socialist Movement] government. Engaging Balkan powers in the massacre of the former Yugoslavia causes additional problems instead of alleviating the situation. The presence of Turkish forces in Bonsia-Herzegovina indeed makes things more difficult and, I believe, particularly causes more tension in the inter-Balkan relations. [end recording]
