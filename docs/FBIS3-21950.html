Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The India-British agreement on confiscation of terrorist funds became effective from today. It will usher in a new era of cooperation between the two countries and reflect the political will to deal firmly with terrorists and those involved in drug trafficking. This was told to PTI by the British home secretary, Mr. Michael Howard, at a dinner hosted by the Indian high commissioner, Mr. L.N. Singhvi, in honor of the visiting prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, in London last night. The agreement provides for confiscation of funds in any of the two countries of those involved in the pursuit of terrorist activities as also drug trafficking. The agreement for the purpose, together with the India-British extradition treaty, had been signed in London in 1992.
