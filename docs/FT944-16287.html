941014 FT 14 OCT 94 / World Trade News: Export leap cuts China's deficit China appears headed for near balanced trade this year after exports surged in the first nine months. Exports to September exceeded imports by Dollars 1.38bn in a marked turnaround from last year's large trade deficit. Chinese customs statistics put exports at Dollars 79.44bn in the nine months to September and imports Dollars 78.06bn. A 1993 deficit on the trading account had been put at Dollars 12.19bn. Exports increased by 29.7 per cent against the same period last year. Import growth rate to September slowed after the boom year in 1993 to 15.2 per cent. China's GDP growth last year exceeded 13 per cent. Among reasons for China's impressive export performance was a 50 per cent devaluation of the local currency in dollar terms after unification of the exchange rate on January 1. Domestic manufacturers were also encouraged to seek export opportunities abroad after measures were adopted last year to restrain growth in an overheating economy. Latest statistics show some slowing in export growth consistent with the pattern of previous years with industrial and consumer demand for imports picking up in the second half. Under-reporting of imports during the year also tends to surface in the latter months of the year, but as an economist said of the good export figures: 'It's clear that a pretty significant shift has taken place compared with last year.'
