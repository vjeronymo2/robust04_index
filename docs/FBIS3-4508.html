Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 30 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier Li Lanqing met here this morning with a delegation from the American Chamber of Commerce in Hong Kong headed by its chairman Gerald Murdock. The two sides exchanged views on issues of common interest such as Sino-U.S. economic and trade relations. Li said that the United States is the largest developed country in the world while China is the largest developing one, adding that the development of Sino-U.S. economic and trade relations is not only beneficial to the two sides, but will also have an enormous influence on the world economy as a whole. The nine-member U.S. delegation arrived here on March 29 at the invitation of the China Council for the Promotion of International Trade.
