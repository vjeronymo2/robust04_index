BFN [Text] RIGA, Apr 13, BNS - As many as 101,648 people, or 5.9 percent of the country's able-bodied population, were without job on April 12, according to figures from the State Employment Service. The State Statistics Committee calculates the joblessness at 6.6 percent of economically active residents. From all persons looking for jobs, 87,953 have registered themselves as unemployed, 36,751 receive allowances and 31,627 have been seeking job for long time, the Employment Service said. In Riga, the unemployment runs at 3.5 percent while in some rural districts it hits 12 percent and more. The Cabinet considered the effect of unemployment on the interior stability and looked through proposals to improve the situation during its Tuesday [12 April] session. Labor Minister Andris Berzins briefed reporters that the government planned to support the development of small and medium size business in rural districts, where the unemployment is most severely felt, by channeling foreign investments and credits to the countryside.
