BFN [Unattributed report: "Closer Cooperation With Minsk"] [Text] Vienna -- Austria and Belarus want to enhance economic cooperation. This was stated by Federal Chancellor Franz Vranitzky and Belarusian Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich after talks in Vienna. Vranitzky announced that "very concrete talks on an investment protection agreement and on the avoidance of double taxation" would start soon. Kebich emphasized the good relations between the two countries and "the longstanding good personal relationship" with the Austrian chancellor. Regarding the economic chances of Austrian businesses in Belarus, Vranitzky emphasized that Austria would provide a "financing framework with an open ceiling for projects." In their talks, the two government heads also discussed concrete investment possibilities for Austrian businesses. Vranitzky mentioned as examples the modernization of the Belarusian steel industry, the oil industry, and tourist trade. The number of possible projects was "relatively large," the Austrian chancellor said.
