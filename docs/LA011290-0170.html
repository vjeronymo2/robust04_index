COLD HANDS USUALLY NOT DANGER SIGN

Are your hands always cold? It's a fairly common but rarely serious condition, says Dr. Thomas Shook, a cardiologist who directs the coronary care unit at the Hospital of the Good Samaritan in Los Angeles.

People with cold hands often have a normal body temperature but lower skin temperature, says Shook, who suspects that cold-handed people might simply be more sensitive to temperature changes.

Cold hands are not usually a sign of an underlying problem, such as abnormal circulation, Shook says. But rarely, cold hands can be a symptom of Raynaud's phenomenon, a condition in which the hands turn white, then blue, then red.

"Raynaud's by itself is not serious," Shook says. "But it can be associated with more troublesome conditions like scleroderma, a skin disorder, or drug allergies."

Short of gloves and hand warmers, there's not much for cold-handed people to do, Shook says. Except maybe find solace in the old "cold hands, warm heart" theory. By KATHLEEN DOHENY Youngsters in preschool through high school are invited to participate in a one-day "mail art" show to be presented March 3 at the Bowers Museum in Santa Ana. Any subject is acceptable, but all works must be two-dimensional and postcard-size (about 4 by 6 inches). Each entry must include the artist's name, age and address (the student's name must also be printed on the image side of the entry), and all work received will become the property of the museum. Mail entries by Feb. 26 to the Bowers Museum Office of Education, 2002 N. Main St., Santa Ana, CA 92706. For further information, call (714) 972-1900. Wendy Bargiel, 17, of El Toro has been selected as a regional semifinalist in TEEN Magazine's Great Model Search. She was one of 240 candidates selected from an estimated 24,000 entries. Her photo is appearing in the January issue of the magazine. The winner will appear on the cover of the October issue. The winner will also receive a $5,000 cash award, a trip for two to a resort and a wardrobe of gowns. California State Summer School for the Arts is conducting a statewide search to identify the most talented high school artists. Those teen-age artists who are selected will attend one of the country's premier summer art institutes at Mills College in Oakland. Brochures describing the school's visual and performing arts programs, and the application process, are currently available through local high schools, libraries and arts councils.
