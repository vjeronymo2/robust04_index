Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA Jan. 27 -- A delegation of the Austrian Ministry of Foreign Affairs led by its General Secretary Dr. Wolfgang Schallenberg left Hanoi today, concluding its threeday visit to Vietnam. While here the delegation paid a tribute to President Ho Chi Minh at his mausoleum. It was received by Deputy Prime Minister Tran Duc Luong and Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam. The Austrian delegation also held talks with a delegation of the Vietnamese Foreign Ministry headed by Deputy Foreign Minister Nguyen Dy Nien. The two sides noted with satisfaction at positive steps in the development of the relations between the two countries in the recent time and agreed that the friendship and cooperation between the two countries should be push up to commensurate with the potentials and desire of the two sides [sentence as received]. He also affirmed Austria's wish for further development of bilateral relations in the new situation of each country and each region. For its part, the Vietnamese side welcomed the intention of the Austrian Government and businessmen and industrialists to promote their relations of economic, scientific and technological cooperation with Vietnam. The Vietnamese Government has created favourable conditions for the development of bilateral relations in the interests of the two peoples, and for peace and cooperation in the region and the world. The two sides exchanged views on international issues of common concern.
