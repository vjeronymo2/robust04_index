BFN [Text] Lima, 20 Jun (EFE) -- According to the Apoyo polling firm, Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori enjoys the support of 45 percent of Peruvians while former UN Secretary General Javier Perez de Cuellar, who still has not decided whether to run in the 1995 elections, has only 25 percent. The poll results disclosed on 19 June during a political television program also mention that Lima Mayor Ricardo Belmont, who already has launched his candidacy, enjoys 11 percent support. According to the same source, former President Fernando Belaunde has the support of 4 percent; former Senator Javier Balle Riestra, 3 percent; and former Mayor Luis Caceres Velazquez, 1 percent. In the case of a second round of elections between Fujimori and Perez de Cuellar, Fujimori would get 49 percent and Perez de Cuellar 37 percent. If the second round were to pit Fujimori against Belmont, the results would be 55 percent for Fujimori and 27 percent for Belmont, according to the results of this early poll.
