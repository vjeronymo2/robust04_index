BFN [Text] In is believed in the presidential administration that rumours about Sergey Shakhray's dismissal from his ministerial post are to some extent well-founded. A presidential administration official who asked not to be identified explained the emerging reports by the fact that the Russian Government had recently decided, with Sergey Shakhray's active contribution, to finance three programs for the north, which had seriously affected the financial interests of the south of Russia. Another important factor was the unsuccessful policy toward the Cossacks and Chechnya. In particular, no one had authorized him to conduct talks with the Chechen opposition.
