BFN [Text] CONTACT news agency has supplied us with information from the press service of the Georgian cabinet of ministers that an Armenian Government delegation arrived today on a working visit in Tbilisi. The delegation is led by Armenian State Minister Gagik Martirosyan. The delegation was met at the airport by Deputy Prime Minister Irakli Menagharishvili. A meting was held at the cabinet of ministers where the issues of securing the railways, oil and natural gas supplies, and the situation in trade and economy were discussed. The Armenian delegation expressed its discontent over Saknavtobprodukti [Georgian oil products]. It was noted that in an immediate future, a north Caucasus-Transcaucasus gas pipeline would soon be put in operation, which would enhance gas supplies two-fold both to Georgia and Armenia. Martirosyan said that a group of the Armenian Government delegation had left for Batumi to monitor the transportation of cargo and humanitarian aid destined for Armenia. The members of the Armenian Government delegation will continue work in the appropriate ministries and departments.
