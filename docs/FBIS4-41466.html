BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Svetlana Ivanova] [Text] St. Petersburg, 24 Jun -- A delegation of parliamentarians of the North Atlantic Assembly has arrived in the city on the Neva. Today they visited the Taurida Palace -- the headquarters of the Inter-Parliamentary Assembly [IPA] of the CIS countries. The representatives of the inter-parliamentary organization of the 16 NATO member-states were interested in the economic situation in the Commonwealth countries and, first and foremost in the parliamentary capital of the CIS. During a conversation with Mikhail Krotov, the general secretary of the IPA, issues related to the conversion of defense enterprises both in St. Petersburg and in the CIS as a whole, and problems of attracting foreign investments to the former Soviet republics were touched upon. In this connection, Krotov stressed the IPA's special role in creating the legal basis for the market economy on the territory of all the Commonwealth countries and in developing international business contacts. The sides expressed mutual interest in strengthening further ties between two of the world's major parliamentary structures.
