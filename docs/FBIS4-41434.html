BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Maynayev] [Text] Bishkek, 23 Jun -- The economic crisis in Kirghizia [Kyrgyzstan] continues to worsen. According to figures published today by the National Statistics Committee, total output in May fell by 27.8 percent compared with April. Sixty-five industrial enterprises have been at a standstill since the beginning of the year. Losses at unprofitable enterprises amount to 245.6 million soms. Over 30,000 employees are sent on compulsory leave every month and about 34,000 are on short time. At the beginning of the year about 2,000 people were reporting every month to labor exchanges, but by the end of April this figure had doubled, and by the beginning of June it had risen to 15,800. Specialists reckon that unemployment is becoming a large-scale and chronic problem, especially among young people up to the age of 30. Since the beginning of the year, this category's share in total unemployment has risen by 150 percent. Economic activity continues to decline in all sectors. There was a deficit of 298.2 million soms on the consolidated budget for the first four months of the year -- this is 10.5 percent of gross domestic product. During January-April the Republic earned profit of 370.2 million soms. But increased prices account for most of this. Things are no better in agriculture. Meat production fell by almost 30 percent. Production of eggs has almost completely ceased. People have to buy them from private producers. Some economists had pinned their hopes on privatization, but they now say it is moving in a strange direction. Only 30 percent of the population have received vouchers or "shares," as they are called in Kirghizia. Even so, these people are in no hurry to make use of them. Privatized enterprises are either abandoning their original production profile or else coming to a complete standstill.
