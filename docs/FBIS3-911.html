Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] In Huambo, UNITA's [National Union for the Total Independence of Angola] Political Commission has met with its negotiators. It was decided that UNITA will take a flexible position at the Lusaka talks. UNITA wants to know from the government what its role as a future opposition party will be. [Begin Alcides Sakala recording] UNITA's Political Commission has asked its negotiating team to get an explanation from the government regarding UNITA's role as a political opposition. In this context, the UNITA negotiating team has been told to contact other opposition parties with a view to a greater coordination in this crucial phase of the current negotiations. [end recording] That was Alcides Sakala in Brussels. UNITA issued this communique dated from Huambo.
