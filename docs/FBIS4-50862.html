BFN [From the "Hookup" program] [Text] Huang Kun-huei, chairman of the Mainland Affairs Council [MAC], Executive Yuan, pointed out yesterday [22 May] that in order to conclude the tasks related to dealing with the aftermath of the Qiandao Lake incident, Mainland China should respond quickly to the Strait Exchange Foundation [SEF] proposal made in early April for a special meeting between the SEF and the Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Straits [ARATS]. Chairman Huang Kun-huei added that whether the two nongovernmental organizations on both sides of the strait hold a special meeting on safety in the near future is not directly related to the convening of the second round of the Ku-Wang meeting. However, if the special meeting cannot be held for a long time, it will have an impact on arrangements for activities related to the Ku-Wang meeting. Chairman Huang Kun-huei made these remarks when answering reporters' questions on whether MAC and SEF have started coordinating and having contact to push forward the second round of the Ku-Wang meeting. As for SEF chairman Ku Chen-fu and Vice Chairman Chiao Jen-he advocating to push forward the second Ku-Wang meeting based on the agreement on institutionalized contacts and talks between the two organizations, Chairman Huang Kun-huei said MAC and SEF have not yet discussed the issue.
