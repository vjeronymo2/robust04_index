920925 FT 25 SEP 92 / Letter: New orthodoxy on devaluation Sir, A new orthodoxy seems to be developing. This suggests that, because of the slack in the world economy, competitive pressures will stop us having to pay the normal inflationary consequences of devaluation. Only time will show whether this is true. However, if it is, then presumably we will also fail to reap the export benefits that would otherwise be associated with a lower value of the pound. Harvey Cole, 9 Clifton Road, Winchester, Hants SO22 5BP
