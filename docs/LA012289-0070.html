January 22, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

TRAVEL NEWS AND BRIEFS

Alaska's Inside Passage will be the subject of a discussion by My Travel Agent at 7:30 p.m. on Wednesday at the Granada Hills Women's Club, 10666 White Oak Ave. Admission free. For more information, call (818) 368-7283.

-- -- --

An informational meeting on Saddleback College's summer "Art and Music Capitals of Western Europe" tour will be held at 7:30 p.m. on Wednesday in the college's art gallery, 28000 Marguerite Parkway, Mission Viejo. Admission free. For more information, call (714) 582-4747.

-- -- --

The United States and Jamaica will be the subject of a lecture by travel photographer Mort Loveman from 1 to 2:30 p.m. on Thursday at Roxbury Memorial Park, 471 S. Roxbury Drive, Beverly Hills. Admission free. For more information, call (213) 550-4761.

-- -- --

Destinations sponsored by Pasadena City College will be detailed at an open house at 7 p.m. on Friday in the Circadian Room, Pasadena City College, 1570 E. Colorado Blvd. Admission free. For more information and reservations, call (818) 578-7261.

-- -- --

A three-week slide series on Wednesdays for advanced and professional artists on "Painting and Photographing Your Way Through Italy" will be conducted by painter and printmaker Roberta Kritzia at 7:30 p.m. beginning Feb. 8 at Studio Gallery, 3803 W. Magnolia Blvd., Burbank. Admission $12 for the three sessions. For reservations and more information, call (818) 567-2951.

-- -- --

A "Travel Spectacular," including information by tour operators, airlines and tourist boards, will be held by the American Society of Travel Agents from 11:30 a.m. to 3:30 p.m., Feb. 11 and 12, at the Burbank Hilton, 2500 Hollywood Way. Admission $4. For more information, call (213) 466-7717. NORMAN DASH
