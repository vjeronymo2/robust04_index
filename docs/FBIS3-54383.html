Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Report by unidentified correspondent at National Conference news conference by National Conference spokesman Abdelkader Bensalah] [Excerpts] [Correspondent] Good evening ladies and gentlemen, 1,235 people participated in the national conference. The official spokesman of the conference has announced that the dissolved Islamic Salvation Front [FIS] is not present in the conference, contrary to the rumor. The conference is setting up its committees. Only two hours ago, HAMAS [Islamic Society Movement], the PRA [Party of Algerian Renewal], and the MDRA [Democratic Movement for the Renewal of Algeria] withdrew from the conference. [passage omitted] The conference proceedings were opened by Youcef Khatib, chairman of the National Dialogue Commission, with a welcoming speech in which he said: [Begin Khatib recording] This not the time for one-upmanship or bargaining because the issue concerns first and foremost the fate of Algeria. The present circumstances thus require unity of ranks to confront all the challenges facing the country. Oh brothers and sisters, we do not doubt at all and everybody has got the good intention. However, the required results from all parties have not been realized completely despite the efforts made by the National Dialogue Commission. In my name and on behalf of all the members of the National Dialogue Commission, I thank all the parties which have dealt with us with all seriousness and sincerity." [end recording] [passage omitted] [Begin recording] [Abdelkader Bensalah, conference spokesman] The idea of the National Dialogue Commission was right from the beginning, to go into the depth of the matter and find out its facts with precision and caution in light of its importance and seriousness. The Commission has always had the conviction that dealing with this matter required time and patience at the same time. The Commission has, through its endeavors toward this Islamic tendency registered tangible results, the most important of which is represented in the latter's acceptance of dialogue as a principle and as a political method to deal with this problem and the need to arrive gradually at its solution. From these premises, the Commission has embodied this option, i.e. that of continuing dialogue when it included it in the platform for national consensus. [end recording] [Correspondent] The official spokesman concluded his speech by stressing the need to adhere to dialogue as a positive means to ensure its continuation and deepening by the transitional bodies, considering that the dialogue is a pressing need for dealing with all parties. After this, opportunity was given to journalists to ask questions, and the national spokesman declined to answer some of them considering that they were premature. [passage omitted]
