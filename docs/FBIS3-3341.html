Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 19 KYODO -- Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, saying human rights are a universally accepted value in the international community, urged China Saturday [19 March] to improve its human rights record. He made the statement at a welcome banquet hosted by Chinese Premier Li Peng. Hosokawa arrived in Beijing shortly after noon for a three-day visit to China.
