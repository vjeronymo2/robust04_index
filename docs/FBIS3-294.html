Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] President Gnassingbe Eyadema returned to Lome this evening following a short working visit to Cameroon and Nigeria. He was met at the Lome-Tokoin International Airport by Premier Koffigoh and several ministers. Following military honors, the head of state and the prime minister met in the Airport VIP Lounge, where they spoke for several minutes. President Eyadema's visits to Yaounde and Abuja came at a time of high tension between the two countries. The tension resulted from the border dispute over the Bakassi Peninsular. In Yaounde, President Eyadema held a long discussion with President Paul Biya. He did the same with General Abacha in Abuja. President Eyadema and his delegation were pleased with contacts made with the authorities of the two countries. President Eyadema was accompanied by Mr. Barry Moussa Barque, his special adviser; Foreign Minister Fambare Natchaba, Defense Minister Inoussa Bouraima, and Former Minister Atsu Koffi Amegah.
