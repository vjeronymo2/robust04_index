Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 8 February. Colombia's International Sugar and Molasses Marketing Company purchased the Urena Sugar Mill for 320 million bolivares. The transaction was conducted at the Venezuelan Investment Fund (FIV) offices. FIV President Nelson Rivera stated the transaction was beneficial for the country because the investment and expenditures incurred throughout the sale process were recovered. (Caracas Venezolana de Television in Spanish 2350 GMT 27 Jan 94) The Venezuelan Central Bank (BCV) has reported that, owing to a reduction in oil revenues, Venezuela's international reserves dropped by $744 million last January and now total $11.912 billion. According to the BCV, another factor contributing to the decline is the climate of mistrust that exists in the financial market in the wake of the government's intervention in Banco Latino. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2234 GMT 3 Feb 94) Venezuela foreign debt bonds have been hurt by a decrease in value of U.S. long-term bonds. The par bond fell one point to 70.75 due to U.S. 30-year bond sales. In addition, investors have adopted a cautious stance as they are waiting to see what specific measures President Rafael Caldera will take. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 4 Feb 94 p E-1)
