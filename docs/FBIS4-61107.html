BFN [Text] Moscow, May 3 (YONHAP) -- South Korean Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae and his Russian counterpart Pavel Grachev have agreed to exchange information on Pyongyang's nuclear weapons program in a joint effort to check a major threat to world peace, sources at the South Korean Embassy here said Wednesday. During Yi's meeting with Grachev Saturday, the two ministers shared the view that exchanging such information is essential to maintain peace in Northeast Asia and on the Korean peninsula, a senior official said. The agreement should help Seoul gauge the progress of Pyongyang's nuclear arms development program, he noted.
