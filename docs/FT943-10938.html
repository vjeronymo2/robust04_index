940802 FT 02 AUG 94 / International Company News: Recovery continues at Canadian steel maker Dofasco, Canada's leading steel maker, continued its recovery in the second quarter with higher prices for flat rolled products, higher shipments and lower unit costs. The company has negotiated a 1995 price increase of 15 per cent to 20 per cent in Canadian dollar terms with Chrysler, and has begun negotiations with General Motors. The company is seeking similar increases with the appliance industry. Second-quarter operating net profit was CDollars 35.3m (USDollars 25.4m), or 36 cents a common share, against CDollars 6.7m a year earlier, which was absorbed by preferred stock dividends. After special gains, the latest quarter showed final net profit of CDollars 99.3m, or CDollars 1.21 a share. Revenues were CDollars 557m, up 3 per cent. First-half net profit was CDollars 103.7m, or Dollars 1.10 a share, including special items, against a loss of CDollars 5.8m, or 24 cents, on sales of CDollars 1.09bn, against CDollars 1.06bn. Average selling price for hot rolled steel was CDollars 644 a tonne, up from CDollars 614 in the first quarter, and is expected to reach about CDollars 660 by the year end. Cost per tonne was CDollars 529, against CDollars 548 in the first quarter.
