Language: Indonesian Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign ministers of the Nonaligned Movement [NAM] today started discussions on realizing the NAM's efforts to overcome international political and economic problems. The consultative meeting of a number of NAM foreign ministers under way at the Department of Foreign Affairs in Jakarta will end tomorrow. Opening the meeting this morning, Foreign Minister Ali Alatas said efforts to end Serbian brutalities and to enhance cooperation between the NAM and the Group of 77 are among the pressing issues that need to be tackled. Indonesia reiterated the need for an immediate lifting of the arms embargo on Bosnia-Herzegovina and the convening of an international conference on Bosnia. The lifting of the arms embargo will motivate the Serbs to honor various UN resolutions and end their indiscriminate acts against the Bosnians. The NAM members also need to continue paying attention to the debts of developing nations. He reminded the NAM and the Group of 77 to continue coordinating and consolidating their economic activities. The consultative meeting attended by 21 NAM members, including Indonesia is expected to overcome the problems in preparation for a NAM ministerial meeting to be held in Cairo, Egypt in early June.
