920221 FT 21 FEB 92 / International Company News: Apollo Leasing in creditor plea APOLLO Leasing, an ailing Japanese leasing company, has asked more than 120 creditors to reduce interest rates on outstanding loans and allow it to defer repayment of the principal over the next three years, Reuter reports from Tokyo. Under a restructuring, which has yet to win the banks' backing, Apollo will sell assets, including stocks, to cut its debt and reduce its workforce. Apollo's outstanding loans totalled some Y590bn (Dollars 4.6bn) in December.
