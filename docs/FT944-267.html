941230 FT 30 DEC 94 / US leading indicators ahead The US official index of leading indicators rose 0.3 per cent last month, providing further evidence that economic growth is likely to remain robust in the first half of 1995. The increase in the index, intended to give early warning of changes in economic trends, exceeded most analysts' expectations. It followed a revised 0.1 per cent decline in October and a gain of 0.1 per cent in September. Officials said the index had been boosted by positive contributions from indicators including orders for plant, equipment, orders for consumer goods and materials, claims for unemployment insurance, and commodity prices. Other indicators, including share prices and money supply, contributed negatively.
