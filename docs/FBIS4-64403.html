Democratic Alliance, the Socialist Party and the Pact for Italy had all asked that smaller parliamentary groups be allowed to form but this request was turned down today by those representing the majority forces, sparking protests from members of the opposition. Socialist Gino Giugni, for one, said it was hard to understand the logic of a stance which forces the opposition into "an artificial compactness." As to the future, Giugni announced plans to forge a single group with Democratic Alliance, also in view of coming European elections. For Luciana Sbarbati, a Republican elected on the Democratic Alliance ticket, the majority, in refusing to allow the formation of smaller groups, was putting its worst foot forward, giving the impression of a bullying and vengeful rightwing "suspicious of any form of democratic pluralism."
