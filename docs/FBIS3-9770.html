Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, March 30 (BTA) -- Bulgaria's President Zhelyu Zhelev is on an informal visit to London. He told a news conference there that early parliamentary elections will probably be held in Bulgaria before the end of this year. This morning the [Bulgarian] national radio aired the president's comments on home policy issues at the press conference in London. In connection with a commentary in THE FINANCIAL TIMES that Prime Minister Lyuben Berov's state of health (after a heart failure in early March he underwent surgery) might provoke a political crisis and early elections, thus delaying the reform process, the President said: "The problem is not the PM's state of health. He will recover and I am sure that a month from now he will be even more efficient than before. The problem is that the government itself is flawed, and so is the formula of its formation." The present cabinet was formed at the end of 1992 as a government of experts, not affiliated to any party. President Zhelev blamed the cabinet for counting too much on the support of the Bulgarian Socialist Party (the former communists) and pointed out that it ought to pursue its reform programme, taking the risk of losing parliament's support. According to the president, there are two possible solutions: "Either this parliament will have to appoint a non-communist government, backed by the Union of Democratic Forces, the New Union for Democracy, the Movement for Rights and Freedoms, and the independent MP's, that would pursue the reforms consistently, or we are heading for elections." It seems that elections will be held before the end of this year, he said. When asked whether a possible new cabinet, dominated by the BSP (ranking first in opinion polls), would not make matters even worse in regard to the reform, President Zhelev was positive that the BSP is unable to form a cabinet. "In my opinion, the BSP will find it hard to dominate the future parliament. The problem it faces is not how to win the elections; that will not be very difficult. The hard part of it will come after the elections, when its victory will be of no use, as it will be unable to form a cabinet," he said. As to the steep rise of the key currencies against the Bulgarian lev in the past few weeks, President Zhelev believes it to be the result of speculation. Certain circles in Bulgaria will benefit from that, as enterprises put up for privatization will be twice cheaper, he explained. According to the president, the central bank and the government can take measures to stabilize the national currency, but the important point is to step up privatization and restitution.
