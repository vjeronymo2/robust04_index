BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 11/4/1994 -- Commenting NATO's air raids against the Serbian positions in Bosnia-Herzegovina, the Embassy of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia in Bucharest says that "deciding for armed actions, the United Nations has abandoned the role of peace maker which it used to play, and overtly sided with the Muslims," which questions the nature and the mandate of the UNPROFOR (United Nations Protection Force) in Bosnia-Herzegovina, the embassy states in a press release. The statement released for the press writes that the Yugoslav Embassy is definitely against the policy of pressure and ultimatum, against armed attacks over the Serbian area, and pleads that double standard policies should be renounced.
