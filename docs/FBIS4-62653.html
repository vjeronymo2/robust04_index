BFN [Text] Paris, May 17, BNS -- Lithuanian Prime Minister Adolfas Slezevicius, currently on a five-day visit to France, met with his French counterpart Edouard Balladur in Paris Tuesday [17 May]. During the meeting, which lasted a little over 30 minutes, Slezevicius informed the French premier about Lithuania's economic, domestic and foreign policies. "Our primary interest was to describe relations with Russia and especially to make it clear that Russia seeks to carry out its interests through Kaliningrad," Slezevicius told reporters after the meeting. The Lithuanian premier said he also asked the French Government to assist Lithuania in its efforts to get integrated with European economic structures. Slezevicius and an accompanying Lithuanian Government delegation arrived in Paris Tuesday for meetings with top-ranking French officials and to attend a World Bank conference devoted to the Baltic states. On the first day of their stay in Paris, Lithuanian Government officials met with the leadership of the World Bank. Slezevicius met with a U.S. delegation which came to the conference.
