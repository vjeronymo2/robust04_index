940304 FT 04 MAR 94 / Rise in Pergau dam costs queried Baroness Chalker, minister for overseas development, yesterday told the Commons foreign affairs committee that it was unusual for the cost of an aid project to rise by as much as that of the Malaysian Pergau dam in such a short time. She confirmed that she had opposed government funding of the dam project on economic grounds. It was only 'marginally economic' at the 1988 original contract estimate of Pounds 316m. Sixteen days later the UK consortium, led by Trafalgar House and BICC, increased their estimate to Pounds 397m, which by 1991 rose to Pounds 417m. Lady Chalker said: 'It was extremely unfortunate that they (the consortium) did not clarify the elements which were excluded from the first estimate. What was going on it is difficult to say.' Questioned over the alleged link between the aid and a Pounds 1bn arms deal, Lady Chalker conceded that it might be more accurate to describe the two policies 'as independent rather than no-linkage'. Lady Chalker's evidence came as former minister Lord Younger said he alone had taken the decision to offer aid as part of an arms sale in the protocol, when he was defence secretary. However, speaking on ITN, he made it clear that he did so on behalf of the government. 'I was secretary of state for defence, not just an office boy,' he said. It is understood that Lord Younger's intervention follows two telephone conversations with Mr Douglas Hurd, the foreign secretary. When Mr Hurd appeared before the foreign affairs committee on Wednesday he appeared to blame Lord Younger for the 'incorrect entanglement' of aid and arms between March and June 1988. Yesterday Lady Chalker agreed with Mr Michael Jopling, a former minister, that appraisal of the project was conducted in two days because Baroness Thatcher wanted it completed quickly: 'A more substantial appraisal would have been more desirable.'
