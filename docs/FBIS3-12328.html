Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Interview with PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat by MBC Tunis correspondent Mufidah Hashani in Tunis on 26 February; recorded] [Text] ['Arafat] The most important [word indistinct] is international protection for (?our people). We must see a solution for these settlements and the armed settlers, because they have proven to be time bombs liable to explode at any moment whether we pursue the negotiations or [words indistinct]. The situation may explode at any moment. They are time bombs. [Hashani] What do you expect from the Security Council's meeting tonight? ['Arafat] We are in continuous contact with the U.S. Administration on this issue because we insist on a resolution from the Security Council, not just a condemnation of [words indistinct]. [Hashani] Where have the negotiations between you and the United States reached? ['Arafat] They are continuing. [Hashani] Any idea about the resolution expected to be passed? ['Arafat, interrupting] [Words indistinct] but as I said, we are continuing our contacts with the U.S. Administration on this issue. [Hashani] What about tomorrow's meeting with the Palestinian negotiators who were recalled by the PLO for a meeting in Tunis? Other than the settlements issue, can you clarify other topics of the meeting's agenda? ['Arafat] I prefer not to talk too much and would rather like the deeds do the talking. There will be a complete and comprehensive assessment and afterward we will make a decision.
