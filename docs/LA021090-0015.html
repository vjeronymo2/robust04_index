February 10, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

ETHNIC CONFLICT IN EAST BLOC

I think that it is very difficult for Americans to comprehend the idea of nationalism. Our population is so diverse that the prevalent philosophy is one of un-nationalism.

But when we look at events in other countries, there is a different story.

It is obvious that the attempt to "Sovietize" the population within the Soviet Union did not work. To understand why this was a failure, one has to thoroughly understand the individual histories of the 14 non-Russian countries -- not the Russian versions of these histories.

The emergence of the Soviet Union did not solve the territorial problems by examining the roots of these conflicts and offering solutions; imposition of the Soviet system was like placing a Band-Aid over a wound which needed more sophisticated treatment.

With some loosening of this oppressive system, the smoldering fires of nationalism flared up once again. The "ghosts in the attic" are not dangerous ghosts. Nationalism is actually a very healthy state and people should be masters on their own territories.

Unlike the comments made by Alexiev, nationalism has always been on the forefront. It did not re-emerge suddenly with glasnost and perestroika.

CHLOE ROSE PARKER

West Los Angeles
