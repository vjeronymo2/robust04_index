January 2, 1989, Monday, San Diego County Edition

MOTORCYCLE THEFT SUSPECT WOUNDED BY LA MESA POLICE

A La Mesa police officer shot a man in the chest Friday night while police were attempting to arrest him in Lemon Grove.

The victim, identified as James L. Warnicke, 22, of Lemon Grove was listed in serious condition Sunday at UC San Diego Medical Center.

La Mesa police spokeswoman Allison Moore said Officer Jeff Allen followed two motorcyclists to a Lemon Grove liquor store.

Waiting outside, Allen was advised by his radio dispatcher that one of the motorcycles had been reported stolen. The officer asked for backup assistance and when other officers arrived, they confronted the two men.

Moore said that the officers asked both men to raise their hands, but that Warnicke "suddenly moved his hands to an object in his waistband." She said the officers again ordered him to stop, but that Warnicke repeated the movement and that Allen then fired one shot, hitting Warnicke in the right side of the chest.

Warnicke was taken by Life-flight to the medical center, where he was held on suspicion of motorcycle theft.

His companion, Andre Perez, 26, of La Mesa, was held in the downtown county jail on suspicion of possessing a dangerous weapon and on several outstanding warrants.

Moore said detectives found a knife at the scene, but she said it was unclear what, if anything, Warnicke may have had inside his waistband.

Allen, a 3-year police veteran, was placed on administrative leave while the investigation is continuing.
