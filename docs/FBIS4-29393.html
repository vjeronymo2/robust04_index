BFN [Text] In connection with the task force on Yomitan Auxiliary Airfield, which the Japan-U.S. Joint Committee agreed to establish in a 16 June meeting, Masanori Yoshimoto, lieutenant governor of the Okinawa Prefectural Government, issued a comment. In the comment, the lieutenant governor said the task force is mandated to make a technical study of how to secure an alternative cite for the current parachute drop training ground; the possibility of relocating Sobe Communications Station; and ways to handle various problems arising from the Yomitan Auxiliary Airfield. In his comment, Lieutenant Governor Yoshimoto also said: The return of Yomitan Auxiliary Airfield is important for promoting the industry of Yomitan town and stabilizing the livelihood of residents there. The Okinawans have strongly demanded the return of the airfield. The Okinawa Prefectural Government intends to strongly ask both the Japanese and U.S. Governments to have the airfield returned in a visible way before 1995, when the 50th anniversary of the end of the Pacific War and battle for Okinawa is marked.
