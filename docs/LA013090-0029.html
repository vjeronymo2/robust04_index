January 30, 1990, Tuesday, San Diego County Edition

SAN DIEGO SPORTS ET CETERA

Quarterback Michael Wynn of San Fernando High School made an oral commitment Monday to attend San Diego State University.

Wynn, 6-feet-3, 205-pounds, said he also visited the University of Washington but cancelled trips to USC and Colorado.

The fact that he has two cousins at SDSU -- redshirt freshmen George Glaze (defensive line) and Sebastian Glaze (linebacker) -- helped sway him, Wynn said.

"I had insight into SDSU all season," Wynn said. "That made me feel more comfortable. I just didn't feel as comfortable at other schools."

Wynn completed 53 of 140 passes (37.8%) last year for 726 yards and four touchdowns.

MEN'S TENNIS

San Diego State won all but the top two singles matches in defeating U.S. International, 7-2, in a nonconference match at USIU. The Gulls' Yoram Boran defeated Tole Marinkovic, 7-6, 6-2, in No. 1 singles, and Marc Shumacher downed Ricardo Herrera, 6-4, 6-0, in No. 2 singles.

Joe McDonough won at No. 3 singles and teamed with Dax Peterson at No. 1 doubles for SDSU, which was playing its opener. USIU fell to 3-2.
