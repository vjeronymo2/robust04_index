Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Telephone report by Haluk Koc in Davos over still of map of Switzerland] [Text] President Suleyman Demirel, who is in Davos for the World Economic Forum meetings, had breakfast with the presidents of the Central Asian Turkic republics and the prime minister of Pakistan. Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev, Turkmen President Saparmurad Niyazov, Uzbek President Islam Karimov, and Pakistani Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto attended the breakfast. In a statement after the breakfast, Demirel said that with the president of Turkmenistan they discussed the issue of oil and natural gas, and that the latter submitted a project on the issue. Foreign Minister Cetin, the foreign ministers of the other countries, and high-level officials were also present at the breakfast. Demirel then received the prime minister of Slovenia, Janez Drnovsek, and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres separately. At the second meeting, Demirel and Peres discussed the latest stage in the Middle East peace process and the relations between Turkey and Israel. Peres said the development achieved in the relations between the two countries will further grow thanks to the sensitive attitude of the two sides, and this will help bring lasting peace to the Middle East.
