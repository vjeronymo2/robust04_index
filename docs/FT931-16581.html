930106 FT 06 JAN 93 / Effect of sell-offs on morale JOB satisfaction declines significantly in the aftermath of privatisation, but recovers after subsequent restructuring, a study suggests. 'Privatised companies should restructure as soon as possible instead of waiting a few months, as is more usual,' Professor Cary Cooper of Manchester School of Management - part of the University of Manchester's Institute of Science and Technology - told the conference in Brighton yesterday. His study of 620 employees of one of the 10 privatised water companies found that job satisfaction among managers, staff and manual workers declined after privatisation. Mental and physical health deteriorated. A year later, after a restructuring of the company, about 490 of the original sample reported an almost complete recovery. Prof Cooper told the conference: 'The reorganisation was effectively communicated to the workforce, goals became clear and the opposite then occurred: job satisfaction increased, mental health stabilised or increased, and physical health increased.'
