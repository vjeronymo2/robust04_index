October 14, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

SANTA ANITA, HOLLYPARK ARE GETTING OFF TRACK

Del Mar's recent emergence as the leading race track in average daily handle, surpassing Santa Anita, serves as an important lesson for the two local thoroughbred venues.

Del Mar offers simulcast wagering at both its competitors' facilities. Thoroughbred racing is therefore more accessible to Southlanders during the Del Mar season than it is during the Santa Anita and Hollywood Park meets.

As the freeways become more and more congested, on-track attendance at both these facilities has steadily declined. So far, the managements' response has been to try to make up for this lost patronage by expanding the inter-track wagering system to increasingly far-flung locations, such as Indio and Lancaster. Wake up! Cactus plants and cows don't go to the races.

Isn't it about time Hollypark and Santa Anita worked out a reciprocal simulcasting agreement, and started offering some form of true off-track (as opposed to inter-track) betting?

WILLIAM CRAIG, Huntington Beach
