BFN [Editorial Report] Managua Radio Nicaragua Network in Spanish at 2113 GMT on 29 May broadcasts a live relay of a 36-minute news conference by President Violeta Barrios de Chamorro and Costa Rican President Jose Figueres Olsen with unidentified reporters following a working meeting at San Juan del Sur, Nicaragua. In her opening statement, President Chamorro discusses the topics they addressed at the meeting: "We have spoken about many things. Great concern was expressed over preventive health measures. We are going to work jointly on this. We are also going to work jointly to prevent drug trafficking and money laundering. We are also going to take steps to prevent problems on our oceans and lakes. Fishing boats will be identified to avoid problems in an attempt to prevent it from ever being said again that Nicaragua seized a small boat or that Costa Rica seized another. We do not want these problems." She adds: "There is no protocol here, because I do not think Nicaragua and Costa Rica need a protocol. What we want is brotherhood." In his opening statement, President Figueres thanks President Chamorro for the invitation and notes the excellent relations between their two countries, adding: "We hope to continue to strengthen these relations, and, as a result of these meetings, very specific solutions will be found to eliminate within the next months and years those very small pebbles in our shoes -- small problems we sometimes have had in our bilateral relations." Explaining the issues they discussed, Figueres indicates that they dealt with social, economic, and political matters. He notes that they discussed programs against dengue fever and the need to strengthen housing and sports programs. In terms of economics, he reports they discussed bilateral trade: "I would like to mention the challenges we are facing in bilateral relations. Of course, we talked at length about trade. The economy and agricultural ministers have been working in order that, within 45 days, we will have clear and definite mechanisms to remove the obstacles that have existed in our bilateral trade and so that we can have a more productive and simpler trade process." Figueres states that he and Chamorro reviewed the problem of cross-border smuggling, especially the problem of cattle rustling, which has increased in recent years. He goes on to state they also discussed fishing and tourism problems, adding: "We want our ministries to find a mechanism to warn the boats of one country that they are sailing in territorial waters of the other country. The truth is that the boats are not acting with ill-intent. They are not crossing the border on purpose. We want to establish a means by which they know when they are nearing the limits of their territorial waters, and, in turn, we will no longer have the problems we are experiencing today." In outlining the political issues they discussed, Figueres says: "We talked about border issues and the opportunities our common border provides in strengthening the ties between our countries. We discussed the operating hours of border checkpoints and decided to extend them, improving the border crossing process for Nicaraguans, Costa Ricans, and foreign tourists." During the question and answer segment of the news conference, President Chamorro replies to a reporter's question on whether or not Nicaragua will accept the entry of Haitian refugees as the U.S. Government has requested: "As far as Nicaragua is concerned, as well as all Central Americans are concerned, we have been presented with a minor suggestion from Vice President Gore. We will decide on this. We are reviewing it in advance of our next meeting in December, and, at that time, we will announce the results of our review." In answering the same question, President Figueres states: "In Costa Rica's case, it is a situation we still have to review with Presidency Minister Elias Soley and Foreign Minister Fernando Naranjo. I believe that Costa Rica, as a country that is always respectful of human rights, must study this carefully, and that the appropriate forum to discuss this topic is one in which all of the countries of the continent are present."
