March 4, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

DREXEL STILL HAS A MORAL RESPONSIBILITY TO MILKEN

I hope (Drexel Burnham Lambert Chief Executive) Fred Joseph intends to fulfill his moral responsibility to Michael Milken by at least personally footing his legal bills. After all, it was Joseph who made the decision to buckle under to the government's demands, shutting out Milken, far and away the company's most productive and valuable employee.

One didn't have to be blessed with second sight to know that when the Drexel doors closed on Milken, it was only a matter of time before the doors would close on Drexel itself.

The awful twist is that Joseph got to where he did -- to a position of power and wealth -- because of Milken, and Milken has ended up where he is -- fighting for his life -- because of Joseph.

HILLARI KOPPELMAN

Studio City
