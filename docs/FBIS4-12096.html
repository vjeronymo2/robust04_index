BFN [Text] Cairo, 5 Mar (MENA)--Dr. Nabil Sha'th, political adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, and Major General Amnon Shahaq, Israeli deputy chief of staff, held a closed meeting this morning to discuss topics related to the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations currently being held in Cairo. Earlier today, Palestinian and Israeli sources confirmed the existence of substantial differences between the two sides in the negotiations, particularly over the timetable for the withdrawal of Israeli troops, the entry of the Palestinian police force into Gaza and Jericho, and the arming of this force. The meetings of the Security and Civilian Affairs Committees under the Gaza-Jericho Committee resumed this morning. The Security Committee is discussing a number of topics being disputed by the two sides, including the Palestinian police force's entry into the occupied territories, the exact size of the Jericho area, and the deployment of Palestinian forces in Gaza and Jericho. The Civilian Affairs Committee is discussing the transfer of a number of powers, services, and utilities to the Palestinians as part of self-rule and in accordance with the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles signed in Washington on 13 September 1993.
