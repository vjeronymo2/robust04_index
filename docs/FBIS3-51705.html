Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report over video by Janeth Cepeda from Bucaramanga; from the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] According to an investigation by the intelligence division of the Santander Police Department, two medium-power explosive devices were detonated in a Mormon church and a soft drink bottling plant in Bucaramanga to protest the presence of U.S. soldiers in Colombia. [Begin recording of Santander Police Commander Colonel Hugo Lozano, identified by caption] These explosive devices were placed by the Army of National Liberation to protest the presence of U.S. Marines in Colombian territory. [end recording] The explosive devices, which contained 2 kg of dynamite apiece, were detonated at the same time. In the Mormon church, the damage was estimated at 7 million pesos. Seven years ago, another attack completely destroyed the church.
