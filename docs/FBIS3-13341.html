Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Editorial: "Pakistan's Campaign Against the Pressler Amendment"] [Text] A meeting held at the Prime Minister House the other day reviewed all important aspects of Pakistan-U.S. relations. It was attended by Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto; Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali; Foreign Secretary Shaharyar M. Khan; and Dr. Maliha Lodhi, Pakistani ambassador to the United States. The Pakistani ambassador gave details of her meetings with President Clinton and other U.S. authorities and said that she has been apprising the U.S. circles of Pakistan's position on the Pressler Amendment and expressed the hope that the warmth in relations between the two countries will be visible again. Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto expressed the confidence that Pakistan will be able to convince the United States that the Pressler Amendment is a discriminatory law and that it failed to serve the purpose of nuclear nonproliferation in the region. The Pressler Amendment is applicable only to Pakistan. Consequently, the United States has suspended economic and military aid to Pakistan, thereby creating a very difficult situation for this country. Pakistan very courageously endured the situation and made it clear to the United States that as far as nuclear nonproliferation is concerned, Pakistan has never disagreed with it. However, it set only one condition: that India should sign the treaty first. Pakistan presented a number of proposals on keeping South Asia a nuclear-free zone, but India has consistently rejected these proposals. The United States has started to realize that Pakistan is interested not in manufacturing the nuclear weapon but in nuclear energy for peaceful purposes. This is why the Pakistani leadership is expressing the confidence that the United Stats will be convinced that the Pressler Amendment is a discriminatory law. A report says that Senator Larry Pressler has stressed that before making any changes in the Pressler Amendment, it should be ensured that President Clinton verifys that Pakistan does not possess a nuclear weapon. Larry Pressler argues that if the United States shows leniency in its attitude toward nuclear nonproliferation, then the countries that have acquired nuclear capability, including North Korea, will be encouraged to carry out a nuclear explosion. Larry Pressler's opinion cannot be taken as the last word. The U.S. Administration should keep the facts in view and avoid creating difficulties for a friendly country only on the basis of speculations.
