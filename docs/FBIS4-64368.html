BFN [Report by interview with PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat by Gerhard Konzelmann in Stuttgart, in English with superimposed German translation -- recorded] [Text] [Konzelmann] During his visit to Stuttgart, Yasir 'Arafat answered questions about the future development of the peace process in the Middle East. I asked him whether the irritation and the delaying tactics have now come to an end. Are the doors of Jericho now open for 'Arafat? ['Arafat] During the next 48 hours, all problems will be settled. The Jericho-Gaza agreement will be signed on Wednesday [4 May]. This date has been set by President Mubarak. [Konzelmann] My next question referred to Syria, which is still causing problems. 'Arafat stressed that the Syrians must realize that he does not want to conclude a separate peace agreement with Israel. He is thinking of a comprehensive peace for Palestinians, Jordanians, Syrians, and Lebanese. Asked about the attitude of Syria's President Hafiz al-Asad, 'Arafat stated: ['Arafat] His excellency had a very good conversation with U.S. Secretary of State Christopher. [Konzelmann] 'Arafat sees the more distant future in the following way: ['Arafat] The Israelis will withdraw their troops from the entire West Bank, from Jericho, Gaza, Nablus. [Konzelmann] When I asked him whether Nablus will also be under his control, he replied: Yes, after four months. Then he will meet with me in Jerusalem. Numerous Palestinians were very disappointed in the past few weeks. ['Arafat] They were right. Nothing had happened. [Konzelmann] Asked what he is doing with the radical Muslims of Hamas, 'Arafat stressed that he is optimistic. Many have declared that they will participate in the peace process and in free elections in Palestine.
