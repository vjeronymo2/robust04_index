BFN [Report by P.Re.: "Hunger Strike Ends"] [Text] Tirana -- The hunger strike in Kukes, an event whose drama shocked the entire country at the end of last week, has ended. After a meeting with a delegation of the strikers lasting many hours, the government has promised to meet most of the demands so strongly put forward by many inhabitants of the villages of Kukes. Finally, after many days of bargaining, the government's representatives and the strikers whose land had been inundated succeeded in finding a common language. This was confirmed to journalists on 12 May by the chairman of the strikers' delegation, chosen unanimously the day before at the joint meeting of the strikers holed up in the local government building and the Lake of Fierze Association of the Flooded Out. "We are now sort of satisfied," the strike chairman said as he emerged from his meeting with Prime Minister Meksi, "because our main demands were met." He also expressed confidence that the government's latest promises would also lead others to give up their hunger strike. The government has agreed to quickly implement various measures designed to ease the burden of poverty under which these areas labor. The peasants of Kukes have first been promised compensation with land or in cash for their land inundated by the Lake of Fierze. These people will also benefit from long-term loans for house-building and preferential loans for the development of private agriculture. For those inhabitants who even after these measures will still find it difficult to live on their small areas of infertile land, the government has undertaken to organize emigration to more prosperous areas of the country where there is still free state land. Specialists will review once again social welfare for the inhabitants of Kukes. One of the measures promised by the government has been an increase in the level of social support for these people. Kukes will also be given preference in the distribution of humanitarian aid and other forms of assistance. The government's representatives failed to agree to only two of the demands included in the strike program: co-ownership of the Fierze hydroelectric station and the payment of rent for the inundated land.
