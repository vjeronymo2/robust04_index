Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Jan. 6, IRNA -- On the final day of his five-day state visit to Iran, Turkmen President Saparmurad Niyazov was granted an honorary citizenship status by Tehran Mayor Gholam Hoseyn Karbaschi, Thursday [6 January]. Speaking at the ceremony, also attended by Iranian President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani, Niyazov lauded Tehran's municipality for granting him honorary citizenship, saying this indicated the durability and continuation of reciprocal services between the two nations. He said the frontiers separating the two countries in the past have now been turned into border of friendship. The Turkmen president saying he was certain of fruitfulness of his visit to Iran, noted relations between the two neighboring governments not only benefit the two states but also play a key role in world political scene. President Hashemi-Rafsanjani said that plan on mutual cooperation formulated between the two countries during President Niyazov's stay in Iran was a step towards bringing mutual relations closer. The Iranian president said that he and his Turkmen counterpart will support any measure aimed at broadening Tehran-Ashgabat relations.
