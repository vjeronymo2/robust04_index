ONLY 944Q0128C Moscow DIPLOMATICHESKIY VESTNIK Language: Russian Article Type:CSO [Text] By an edict of the President of the Russian Federation: Mikhail Aleksandrovich Fedotov has been appointed the Russian Federation's permanent representative to the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO). Fedotov, M.A., b. 1949, Russian. Graduated from Law School of Moscow State University imeni M.V. Lomonosov in 1972 and completed post-graduate work at All-Union Correspondence Institute of Law in Moscow in 1976. Doctor of juridical sciences and professor. Fluent in English and Polish. Worked as instructor, senior instructor, docent, and professor at All-Union Correspondence Institute of Law from 1976 to 1990; Russian Federation deputy minister of press and information from 1990 to 1992; general director of Russian Agency of Intellectual Property of Russian Federation President in 1992. Russian Federation minister of press and information from 1992 to 1993. Sergey Borisovich Krylov has been appointed Russian Federation deputy minister of foreign affairs. Krylov, S.B., b. 1949, Russian. Graduated from Moscow State Institute of International Relations in 1971. Fluent in French, Portuguese, and English. Has worked for Ministry of Foreign Affairs since 1971. Duty consultant, interpreter, and attache in USSR Embassy in Zaire from 1971 to 1975; attache, third secretary, and second secretary in Second African Department of USSR Ministry of Foreign Affairs from 1976 to 1979; second and first secretary and adviser in minister's Secretariat and assistant deputy minister of foreign affairs from 1979 to 1989; minister-counselor in USSR (Russian) Embassy in Portuguese Republic from 1990 to 1992. Member of Collegium and executive secretary of Russian Federation Ministry of Foreign Affairs from 1992 to 1993. Holds diplomatic rank of ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary.
