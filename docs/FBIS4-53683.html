BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 20 KYODO -- The Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) will urge Japanese electric power and major city gas suppliers to avoid raising their rates after October, ministry officials said Friday [20 May]. The power and gas utilities in November 1993 implemented a temporary cut in their rates, valid until September, to pass on the benefits of the yen's appreciation against the dollar to consumers. The stronger yen has reduced crude oil import costs for the utilities. The rate cut has lowered the monthly electric power bill for the standard household by 100 yen and the city gas cost by 138 yen. The government said Wednesday it will freeze until the end of the year proposed hikes in public service charges, including power and gas rates, public transportation fares and telephone rates. But Hiroshi Watanabe, chairman of the Japan Gas Association, told reporters Thursday that member companies plan to end the rate cut after October.
