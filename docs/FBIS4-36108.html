BFN [Text] Cairo, 6 Jun (MENA)--Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi has decided to refer four police officers to the Higher State Prosecution Office for interrogation for selling arms from the Interior Ministry arms depots. These officers are Major Samir Mahmud Mujahid, chief of the arms depot at the General Administration for Police Supply; Major Hani Sabri Mahmud, chief of the Returned Arms Department at the same administration; Major Mustafa 'Ashur, depots secretary at the Central Security Department; and Captain Iyhab Nafi', head of the Department of Returned Arms at the police supply. The Interior Ministry statement released today said that the four officers sold five Lee Enfield rifles, three automatic rifles, and a quantity of rounds to certain arms and ammunition dealers. The investigating agencies continue their efforts to seize other quantities of arms and ammuntion that were sold with the knowledge of the four officers. The interior minister earlier pensioned off the four officers as soon as their involvement in this operation was uncovered. It should be recalled that the interior minister issued directives for periodic inspection of the arms and ammunition depots to make sure that the regular instructions on the handling of various arms were implemented.
