Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Chinese vice premier and foreign minister, Qian Qichen, concluding his three-day official visit to Sri Lanka left Colombo for the Maldives this morning. During his stay in the island, the Chinese leader held talks with President D.B. Wijetunga, Prime Minister Ranil Wickremasinghe, and Speaker M.H. Mohamed on ways and means of expanding economic cooperation between the two countries. He also held official talks with his Sri Lankan counterpart A.C. Shahul Hameed on bilateral relations and regional and international matters. The Chinese vice premier and his entourage were seen off at the Katunayaka International Airport by Foreign Minister Hameed and the Chinese ambassador in Sri Lanka, Zhang Chengli. Minister Hameed presented an album of photographs depicting the visitors, who were on the island, to the Chinese dignitary prior to his departure at the airport.
