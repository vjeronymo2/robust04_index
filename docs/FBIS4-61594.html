BFN [Text] Chief of the Nagorno-Karabakh Defense Army press service, Serzh Arushanyan suggested that Azerbaijan was trying to bring about a change in military actions and to improve the situation on the front's sections when utilizing Afghan mujahideen with military experience. Arushanyan stressed in an interview with INTERFAX that Baku was after not only a military, but also a political purpose -- to internationalize the conflict and give it a religious character in order to confront Christian Armenia and Nagorno-Karabakh and the Moslem world. Stepanakert had several months before stated there were mujahideen in the Azerbaijani Army. One was taken prisoner by the Karabakh troops on 22 April. However, the Azerbaijani Defense Minister affirmed on 5 May that no foreigners were serving in the Azerbaijani Army.
