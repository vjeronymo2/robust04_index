930113 FT 13 JAN 93 / Dutch plan defence cut THE Netherlands is to abolish conscription by 1998 and cut its armed forces by nearly 45 per cent over the next seven years, as part of wide-ranging measures to adapt its security apparatus to the end of the cold war and the growth in peacekeeping operations, writes Ronald van de Krol in Amsterdam. The proposed measures, presented to parliament yesterday, will produce total spending cuts on defence of around Fl 14bn (Pounds 5bn) between 1994 and 2003 and will close many military barracks across the Netherlands.
