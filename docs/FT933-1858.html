930922 FT 22 SEP 93 / Liberal Democrats at Torquay: Call for changes to Sheehy report THE conference urged Mr Michael Howard, home secretary, to abandon the main planks of the Sheehy report on police pay and conditions. Mr Howard, who is due in Torquay today to address the Police Superintendents' Association, was urged instead to set up a Royal Commission and start again. Meanwhile the Superintendents Association conference unanimously passed a vote of no confidence in the criminal justice system, saying it had failed the citizens of Britain.
