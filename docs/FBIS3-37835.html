Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] It has emerged that yesterday, the Geneva talks on the peaceful settlement of the conflict in Abkhazia examined the problem of the return of the refugees to their original places of residence and Abkhazia's political status. Representatives of leading Western powers present at the talks reaffirmed their position as regards Georgia's territorial integrity, telling [Abkhaz Supreme Soviet Chairman Vladislav] Ardzinba, who had been specially summoned to Geneva, that there could be no talk of Abkhazia's secession. The Georgian delegation made a statement strongly condemning the Abkhaz separatists' actions against civilians in Gali Rayon. The text of the statement was distributed among representatives of the mass media. Georgian representatives made special emphasis on the fact that the Abkhaz side had blocked a visit to Gali Rayon by the UN mission, which was confirmed by its leader, General Hvidegaard. The talks continued today.
