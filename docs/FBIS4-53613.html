BFN [Text] On the evening of 19 May, a senior Foreign Ministry official spoke with regard to the question of Japan becoming a permanent member of the UN Security Council [UNSC], and, while noting it was his personal view, said: "Japan should have veto power if it wants to have its stand accepted [in the United Nations]." This is the first time that a senior government official spoke in favor of having veto power equal to that of the five existing member countries--the United States, Russia, China, the UK, and France. Germany has also expressed hope that it would become a permanent UNSC member with veto power. But the senior official added he would not be too insistent on having veto rights.
