930401 FT 01 APR 93 / Parties reveal L27bn gap in funding THE gap between what Italy's political parties said they received in revenue and what they said they spent quadrupled last year to L27bn (Dollars 17m). According to the parties' balance sheets published yesterday, just a week after their 1991 accounts, revenues totalled L279.4bn while outgoings were L306bn. The accounts have been prepared amid the year-long magistrates' investigations into illicit party funding, but still fail to reflect the true picture. The investigations, which have involved more than 70 members of parliament and forced the leaders of five parties to resign, have shown that for last April's elections substantial sums were paid on the side - and these went either to cover party expenses or for personal enrichment. A comment by the small Radical party attached to its balance sheet reads: 'One should not believe the accounts published by the parties because they are false.'
