BFN [Text] Tirana, May 18 (ATA) -- The Prishtina Albanologist Institute violently banned by the Serbian authorities on March 8, 1994, resumed today its activity in a private house, Behlul Jashari, the ATA correspondent in Prishtina, reported today. The institute director, Dr. Sadri Fetiu, said in an interview with ATA, that the institute will publish this year 32 scientific works. Mr. Fetiu said: "The greatest difficulty is the isolation of the institute from the scientific and intellectual world especially with the scientific institutions of Albania because of the obstacles put by the Serbian authorities who do not allow the world literature to pass to the Prishtina Library and vice versa.
