Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] The foreign ministers of Turkey, Britain, and Germany will hold a tripartite consultative meeting in Ankara tomorrow. Douglas Hurd, British secretary of state for foreign and Commonwealth affairs, arrived in Ankara today for a working visit and to attend the tripartite meeting. Hurd was received at Ankara's Esenboga Airport by Foreign Ministry officials. In an airport statement, Hurd said that all economic, political, military, and social issues will be discussed at the tripartite meeting in Ankara. Pointing out that Turkey is a very important country for the European Union, Hurd added that the talks will constitute a significant step in the development of relations. German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel will also arrive in Ankara tonight to attend the consultative meeting of the three foreign ministers tomorrow.
