April 24, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

NATION IN BRIEF;

HAWAII;

'GLACIER' OF LAVA DESTROYS 3 HOMES

A massive "glacier" of molten rock from erupting Kilauea Volcano destroyed three more homes in a rural housing area near Kalapana, Hawaii, civil defense authorities said.

The lava flow from the volcano has destroyed 10 homes in four days. The flow has halted no more than 40 feet away from another dwelling, following its pattern of spending long periods reinflating and then suddenly surging forward to engulf more land. "It's stopped, but it's still hot underneath. We don't know where it will break out yet," said a civil defense worker.
