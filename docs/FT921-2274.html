920320 FT 20 MAR 92 / Karabakh dispute leads to rationing ARMENIA began rationing electricity yesterday under a state of economic emergency stemming from its conflict with Azerbaijan over Nagorno-Karabakh, Reuter reports from Moscow. Despite a flurry of international peace missions - including a fact-finding trip by United Nations envoy, Mr Cyrus Vance - a solution to the crisis over the disputed enclave seemed no closer. In the Armenian capital Yerevan, the government said power supplies would be switched off for 12 hours each day because a blockade by neighbouring Azerbaijan had shut off supply lines. Factories, until now spared the worst of the austerity measures, would face the same power cuts as households, it said.
