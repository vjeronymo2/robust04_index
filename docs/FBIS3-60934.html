Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Report by 'Abd-al-Nabi 'Abd-al-Sattar] [Text] Muhammad Fa'iz Jibril, representative of the National Front for the Salvation of Libya in Cairo, has disclosed serious details about the position of the Libyan opposition elements in Egypt before and after the normalization of Egyptian-Libyan relations. In exclusive statements to AL-WAFD, Jibril affirmed that following the restoration of ties between Egypt and Libya, Libyan President Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi pledged not to touch his opponents in Cairo. Jibril noted that the Egyptian authorities had relaxed protection measures around Libyan opposition elements residing in Egypt upon the normalization of relations with Libya in view of al-Qadhdhafi's pledge. He pointed out that Egypt and other Arab and European states have rejected the Libyan authorities' request to hand over the opponents of al-Qadhdhafi's regime. Jibril noted that he has received many death threats involving him and other figures of the Libyan opposition and their families. He said that al-Qadhdhafi's regime has been involved in 23 attacks against Libyan opposition elements in European capitals. He estimated the number of political prisoners in Libya at 3,000 and affirmed that the Libyan opposition is closely monitoring developments in the case of the disappearance of [former Libyan foreign minister] Mansur al-Kikhya through daily contacts with the Egyptian authorities.
