BFN [Excerpt] General M.J. Wilmink, commander of Allied Land Forces in Central Europe, has arrived in Slovakia for a four-day visit. He was welcomed at the Bratislava Milan Rastislav Stefanik Airport by the Slovak Army's Emil Vestenicky. Our collegue Peter Toman reports on General Wilmink's agenda today. [Toman] General Wilmink was welcomed by the Slovak Army's Lieutenant General Julius Humaj in Trencin this afternoon. He briefed the NATO representatives about the tasks being currently fulfilled by the Slovak Army and also on the transformation in the Army and its command to structures comparable to those of the NATO armies. In the course of their talks, both representatives agreed that the conditions stipulated in the Partnership for Peace program offer a realistic basis for the incorporation of the Slovak Army into the European security structures and thereby, into NATO.
