Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, March 29 (KCNA) -- Prime ministers of different countries voiced full support for the just cause of the Korean people. Malaysian Prime Minister Datuk Seri Dr. Mahathir bin-Mohamed met a special envoy of the great leader President Kim Il-song on March 25. He said the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula should be resolved through negotiation and it was impossible to solve it by means of pressure. Supporting a peaceful solution to the Korean reunification question, he said that if Korea were reunified, it would become the strongest country in the world. The Congolese Prime Minister Jacques Joachim Yhombi-Opango, when he received a delegation of the Foreign Ministry of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea on March 25, said the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula should be resolved through dialogue and negotiation. Hoping that Korea would be reunified independently by confederacy formula without foreign interference, as indicated by the great leader his excellency President Kim Il-song, he declared that the Congolese people's feelings of fraternal friendship with the Korean people would never change. Senegalese Prime Minister Habib Thiam, when he received the DPRK Foreign Ministry delegation on March 24, expressed support for the proposal to reunify Korea by confederacy formula. Noting that the nuclear issue of the Korean peninsula could not be resolved by means of pressure and threat, he expressed the hope that the issue would be solved through DPRK-U.S. talks.
