BFN [Text] Baghdad, 29 May (INA)--In a statement issued today, the Revolution Command Council [RCC] has decided that President Saddam Husayn shall directly assume the post of prime minister. His excellency accepted this nomination. A presidential decree was issued relieving the prime minister, the deputy prime minister, and the ministers of their posts. Another presidential decree was issued forming a Cabinet under President Saddam Husayn. Three deputy prime ministers were appointed. Ministers were also named for various portfolios. The RCC statement says: In view of the circumstances of the unjust blockade and the need for exceptional interest in economic issues and the citizens' lives and in order to reinforce the heroic steadfastness of our great people and the achievements of that steadfastness, which constitute a basic factor in our successful struggle, God willing, to lift the unjust blockade, and in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph C of Article 61 of the Constitution, the RCC, during its session on 18 Dhu al-Hijjah 1414 Hegira, corresponding to 29 May 1994, has decided that President Saddam Husayn shall directly assume the post of prime minister. His excellency agreed to this nomination for the reasons and circumstances presented by the council. The presidential decree forming the new Council of Ministers reads: Taking cognizance of the provisions of Paragraphs C and D of Article 58 of the Constitution, we decree the following: First, The Council of Ministers will be formed under our chairmanship; Second, Vice President Taha Yasin Ramadan shall be appointed deputy prime minister; Third, Tariq 'Aziz shall be appointed deputy prime minister; Fourth, Muhammad Hamzah al-Zubaydi shall be appointed deputy prime minister; Fifth, the following persons shall be appointed to the ministerial posts indicated alongside their names: 1. Muhammad Mahdi Salih, trade minister. 8. Staff General Husayn Kamil Hasan, industry and minerals minister. 9. Umid Midhat Mubarak, health minister. 10. Karim Hasan Rida, agriculture minister. 11. Humam 'Abd-al-Khaliq 'Abd-al-Ghafur, higher education and scientific research minister. 18. Nizar Jum'ah al-Qasir, irrigation minister. 19. Safa' Hadi Jawad, oil minister. 20. The prime minister and ministers shall implement this decree. Written in Baghdad on 18 Dhu al-Hijjah, 1414 hegira, corresponding to 29 May 1994. [Signed] Saddam Husayn, president of the Republic
