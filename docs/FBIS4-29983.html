BFN [Excerpt] A press statement released by the State Secretariat in Jakarta today said that Indonesia and Cambodia have agreed to expand their bilateral cooperation in the fields of trade, economy, education, and culture. The statement also said that an accord on bilateral economic and technical cooperation has been signed between the two countries during the current visit to Indonesia by the Cambodian co-prime ministers. The accord marks a new era in bilateral relations between Indonesia and Cambodia because the current bilateral cooperation has focussed only on political matters. Meanwhile, the Phnom Penh government expressed its appreciation for Indonesia's vigorous efforts in leading the Nonaligned Movement (NAM). Cambodia is optimistic that the NAM will be successful under the chairmanship of Indonesia. Today, the Cambodian co-prime ministers Norodom Ranariddh and Hun Sen visited the state-owned Nusantara Aircraft Industry and the Afro-Asian Museum in Bandung, West Java. [passage omitted]
