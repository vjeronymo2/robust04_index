Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] Managua LA PRENSA in Spanish of 13 January 1994 on page 12 reports that, according to Finance Minister Emilio Pereira, in 1994 the government will back compensation bonds issued to owners of confiscated property for up to 954 million cordobas. Bonds were issued last year for 720 million cordobas. Pereira urged bondholders not to sell their bonds at low prices. He explained that bonds can be used to pay taxes on capital goods and to pay utilities. Government assets to be sold to back the bonds include farm equipment, land, and businesses. Pereira said that thus far 328 people have received compensation bonds for confiscated property. The article reports that bonds awarded last year were used to purchase tractors, land, and construction equipment for a total of 143,605,000 cordobas. As soon as the bonds were first issued, owners began to sell them through newspaper advertisements, according to the article.
