BFN [Text] Luanda June 17 SAPA -- Fighting intensified on Friday [17 June] in the Angolan town of Kuito, with government forces reportedly gaining control of the southern and western parts. State-run radio reported UNITA rebels had deployed two commando battalions (about 300 men) to take the town. Government forces this week claimed unidentified aircraft had dropped supplies for UNITA [National Union for the Total Independence of Angola] near Chipeta in the Catabola area. The Angolan News Agency ANGOP said UNITA rebels were also trying to capture Malanje town in northern Angola, where heavy fighting has killed scores of civilians. Peace negotiators meeting in Zambia are still arguing about accommodation for UNITA officials throughout the country if a peace settlement is achieved. Reports from Lusaka said UNITA wanted the government to provide more than 1,000 houses for its officials.
