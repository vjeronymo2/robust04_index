BFN [Excerpt] In the Milan investigation into kickbacks connected with the CARIPLO [Savings Bank of the Provinces of Lombardy] pension fund's acquisition of real estate, the investigating judge has requested that 29 people stand trial. Among them is Former CARIPLO Chairman Roberto Mazzotta, Former Prime Minister Bettino Craxi, and Severino Citaristi, former Christian Democratic Party treasurer, as well as businessman Paolo Berlusconi [brother of the prime minister-designate]. Under investigation in connection with the sale of three buildings in Milano Tre, Paolo Berlusconi went to the judges of his own free will on 11 February, admitting that he had paid 500 million lire to a go-between.
