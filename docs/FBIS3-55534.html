Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Yermakova] [Text] Moscow January 10 TASS -- Russia's Choice, one of the new Russian parliament's major groups, began an all-night vigil on Sunday [9 January], which ended at 01.00 on Monday morning, to discuss organisation of its work in the Federal Assembly. The meeting elected faction leaders. Yegor Gaydar was elected head of the "Russia's Choice" parliamentary faction, a spokesman for the State Duma leadership told ITAR-TASS. Boris Zolotukhin, a former people's deputy and a member of the Russian Supreme Soviet's democratic wing, was elected the deputy head of the group. He will be in charge of the legislative work and relations with other parliamentary groups. Anatoliy Chubays, the second deputy, will be responsible for ties with the government. Sergey Yushenkov, also a former member of the Russian parliament dissolved by President Boris Yeltsin last September, will be the faction's senior secretary. The meeting also discussed and approved a programme for the first session of the State Duma and the faction's activity. It was agreed that any faction member "must vote solidarily" if so decided by a two-third majority at the faction's general meeting.
