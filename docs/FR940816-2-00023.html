SUMMARY: In accordance with 37 CFR 404.7(a)(i), announcement is made of prospective exclusive licenses of a cellulase-producing microorganism. DATES: Written objections must be filed within 60 days from the date of publication of this Notice in the Federal Register.
ADDRESSES: U.S. Army Aviation and Troop Command, ATTN: AMSAT&hyph;C&hyph;JGP, 4300 Goodfellow Boulevard, St. Louis, MO 63120&hyph;1798.
FOR FURTHER INFORMATION CONTACT: Mr. John H. Lamming, Patent Counsel, (314) 263&hyph;9150. SUPPLEMENTARY INFORMATION: The cellulase-producing microorganism was invented by Mr. Benedict J. Gallo (U.S. Patent Number 4,275,163, issued June 23, 1981). Rights to this invention are owned by the U.S. Government as represented by the U.S. Army Natick Research, Development and Engineering Center (Natick RD&E Center). Under the authority of section 11(a)(2) of the Federal Technology Transfer Act of 1986 (Pub. L. 99&hyph;502) and Section 207 of Title 35 U.S. Code, the Department of the Army as represented by Natick RD&E Center intends to grant an exclusive license on the cellulase-producing microorganism to Solvay Enzymes, Inc., P.O. Box 4859, 1003 Industrial Parkway, Elkhart, Indiana 45616. Pursuant to 37 CFR 404.7(a)(i), any interested party may file written objections to this prospective exclusive license arrangements. Written objections should be directed to Mr. John H. Lamming at the above address. Kenneth L. Denton, Army Federal Register Liaison Officer.
