931214 FT 14 DEC 93 / Vocational training 'an epic disaster' Britain's new system of vocational qualifications is today attacked as a 'disaster of epic proportions' in a report by a group of leading academics. Professor Alan Smithers of Manchester University's Centre for Education and Employment Research says the national vocational qualifications (NVQs) and general national vocational qualifications (GNVQs) are inferior both to the training schemes which preceded them and to comparable courses in other European countries. The new qualifications are weak because they are geared solely to 'assessing what students can do rather than what they know or understand', he says. Mr Tim Boswell, the further education minister, interviewed for Channel 4's Dispatches programme to be broadcast tonight, said the government would take Prof Smithers' advice seriously. 'There will be some problems of course at the beginning,' he said. 'We are always prepared to look at these and listen to practitioners, particularly at the grassroots. But we believe that the broad structure is right and that we can build on it to have a really powerful range of qualifications.'
