Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Report by Mahmud Bakri] [Text] Political sources have told AL-SHA'B that during his tour of the region U.S. Commerce Secretary Ronald Brown has discussed a plan for cooperation among Egypt, Israel, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan. Brown met with President Husni Mubarak Thursday to discuss the details of the U.S. economic plan, which includes the following points: 1. Establishing free trade between Israel and the three Arab countries through trade agreements between governments. The agreements will involve Israeli commodities and products tha? can be exported to the three Arab markets, and commodities and products of the three Arab countries that can be exported to Israel. 2. Establishing banking cooperation between the three Arab countries and Israel to finance a number of common commercial, agricultural, and industrial projects. 3. Abolishing restrictions imposed on the private sectors in the three Arab countries concerning trade and establishment of joint ventures, whether in Israel or in the Arab countries. 4. Setting up a four-way committee, with the possibility of Palestinians joining it, to study duties on commodities exchanged among the four countries. The U.S. plan stresses the need to begin steps for economic cooperation in March or April. It says the agreements will support the bases of the ongoing peace process. Brown said during his talks with President Mubarak that U.S. President Bill Clinton is interested in seeing the plan implemented.
