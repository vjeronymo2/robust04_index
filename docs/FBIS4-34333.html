BFN [From the "First Thing" program] [Text] Inspectors from nine Havana administrative councils have fined over 300 self-employed workers for violations and irregularities in exercising this type of work. Provinicial labor committee figures indicate that 26,388 inspections had been conducted by the end of May in the 133 self-employment categories approved by the provincial administrative councils. Of the 555 fines, over 300 were issued against people who do unauthorized work and 60 people who render services but were not registered with the municipality. Another group was issued fines for using middlemen or selling products in areas prohibited by the city's regulations. The labor committee also revoked the licenses of over 350 repeat offenders and has confiscated merchandise, mainly snacks and agricultural products, from approximately 60 people. Manuel Fernandez, director of labor and social security in Havana, said the fines total over 450,000 pesos and affirmed that strict controls will be maintained. Fernandez also said that over 40,000 people are registered for self-employment work in Havana.
