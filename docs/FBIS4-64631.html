BFN [Text] Government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis said it is up to the UN Security Council to show determination--as it is showing on other international issues--or to let the Turkish intervention in Cyprus slander its image and international law. The spokesman made his statement to the Cyprus Broadcasting Corporation commenting on a letter from Turkish Cypriot leader Rauf Denktas to the UN secretary general. In his letter Denktas criticizes the UN envoys and says that he is ready to continue with the talks if the proposals included in the UN documents are changed. The spokesman said: In his letter, Denktas is again trying to implement a policy of delaying. The Greek Cypriot side's actions and demarches will be made after the UN secretary general submits his report and the National Council confers. At the National Council, President Glavkos Kliridhis will explain his thoughts for discussion and decisionmaking.
