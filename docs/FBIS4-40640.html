BFN [Text] The Federal Counterintelligence Service (FCIS) intends to take an active part in ensuring security in the free economic zone Nakhodka situated in Russia's Far East. Chief of the FCIS Public Relations Center Aleksandr Mikhailov reported to Interfax Saturday that Director of the service Sergey Stepashin who was on an inspection trip throughout this region this week commissioned local counterintelligence officers to fulfil this task. In Mikhailov's words, separate criminal clans use the opportunities of the free economic zone to inflict damage to the economy of the country as a whole and to the economy of the Far East in particular. Stepashin called counterintelligence officers to adopt all necessary measures to ensure normal functioning of Nakhodka free economic zone.
