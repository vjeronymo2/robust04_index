930727 FT 27 JUL 93 / World Commodities Prices: Tea A fair demand prevailed. Landed best liquoring East Africans were very irregular and generally 5 to 10p easier but coloury mediums were a strong feature and often gained 2 to 4p. Reports the Tea Brokers Asocciation. Lesser sorts, however, tended easier while malawi teas were difficult of sale apartfrom selected lines. Ceylons came in for more enquiry with brighter types dearer, others about steady. Offshore teas met limited competition and prices were easier where sold. Quotations quality 150p, nom good medium 125p, medium 111, lowmedium 88p, the highest price realised this week was 155p for a kenya pd.
