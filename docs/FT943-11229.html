940801 FT 01 AUG 94 / Copier leasing rules amended New restrictions on photocopier leases are to be introduced by members of the Finance and Leasing Association to combat criticism of the photocopier industry by the Office of Fair Trading. The association is proposing substantial amendments to leasing guidelines which it hopes will overcome concerns expressed earlier this year by Sir Bryan Carsberg, director-general of fair trading. Sir Bryan in March called for urgent moves to end what he described as 'an extraordinary variety of malpractices and excesses'. He said he was prepared to give self-regulation a chance but if that failed he would refer the supply of photocopiers to the Monopolies and Mergers Commission. Under the association's proposed guidelines, which are binding on members, photocopier lease periods will be restricted to a maximum of five years rather than the unspecified 'working life' of the equipment. The association says it also intends to ensure that the controversial cost-per-copy contracts are restricted to larger companies only, where their use is considered to be appropriate. Sir Bryan, however, called on trade associations actively to discourage the use of cost-per-copy arrangements. Mr David Hardisty, association chairman, said its guidance notes on cost-per-copy contracts seemed to be effective as it had received only one complaint since this section came into force last year. He added: 'However, so there is no room for doubt, we are quite happy to tighten up this area to ensure best practice.'
