Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, Feb. 2, IRNA -- Bulgarian President Zhelyu Zhelev Tuesday called for promotion of Tehran-Sofia cooperation in communication and transportation areas and for the establishment of an air link between the two capitals. Zhelev made the remark in a meeting with the visiting Iranian Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati who arrived here Monday on an official visit. An informed source quoted the Bulgarian president as saying that implementation of the political-economic agreement, to be signed between the two countries today, will have positive effects on mutual ties. Zhelev also extended an invitation to President Rafsanjani to pay an official visit to Bulgaria. Velayati later briefed President Zhelev on the outcome of his talks with Bulgarian officials. President Zhelev stressed that rights of the Bulgarian Muslims were safeguarded in the country's constitution. He added that the Bulgarian Muslims were holding significant positions in the parliament, government and other organizations. The Iranian foreign minister also met with Prime Minister Lyuben Berov who termed Velayati's visit to Sofia as a turning point in mutual ties with Tehran. He said his country welcomed an increase in the volume of trade with Iran. The Bulgarian official said his country was interested in purchasing more gas and oil from Iran and called for more avenues to promote bilateral commercial ties. In another development, Velayati discussed mutual ties and international developments including the crisis in Bosnia with the speaker of Bulgaria's parliament, Aleksander Yordanov. Velayati outlined stands of the Islamic Republic in connection with the issue of Bosnia-Herzegovina and underlined the need for urgent measures to establish a just and honorable peace in the region. Later, Yordanov awarded Velayati with an honorary decoration of the Bulgarian parliament as a sign of parliamentary friendship and cooperation between the two countries.
