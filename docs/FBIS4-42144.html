BFN [Excerpt] A condemnation of violence and support for political dialogue and the economic reforms launched by President Zeroual's regime are the results of the brief visit to Algiers by the Greek, German, and Belgian troika of European Union foreign ministers. After he returned to Brussels last night, Belgian Foreign Minister Willy Claes spoke to Quentin Dickinson: [Begin Claes recording] It seems clear to me that if Algeria does not receive financial and economic aid from the international community, it will find it very difficult to overcome two challenges simultaneously: terrorism as evolved by the fundamentalists on the one hand; and public opinion, which in fact is suffering financially and economically from the consequences of the policy on structural reform. If we want to provide a social safety net, if we want to give a concrete signal to the people that something is starting to move and changing in the right direction, aid has to come from abroad. Otherwise, I really do not see how the Algerian Government can make its program successful. [end recording] [passage omitted]
