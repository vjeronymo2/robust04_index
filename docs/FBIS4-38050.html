BFN [Text] The prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao's, four-day visit to Moscow from tomorrow will mark a new milestone in India-Russian relations. This was stated by the Indian ambassador in Russia, Mr. Ranendra Sen, to newsmen in Moscow. He said the process had been initiated by President Yeltsin when he paid a visit to India in January last year. It will get a new impetus by the prime minister's forthcoming visit. Referring to cooperation in defense matters, Mr. Sen said there have been positive developments and the two sides are looking for a long-term cooperation in the supply and production of defense spares and equipment. Meanwhile, officials in Moscow and New Delhi are giving final touches to several agreements and a couple of political documents to be signed during Mr. Narasimha Rao's visit. The prime minister, besides holding one-to-one talk with President Yeltsin on bilateral and international issues, will also meet many Russian leaders. The prime minister will be accompanied by a high-level delegation which includes Union Ministers Mr. Pranab Mukherji, Mr. Ghulam Nabi Azad, Mr. R. L. Bhatia and Mr. Bhvanesh Chaturvedi, and defense, finance, and foreign secretaries. Our correspondent reports that while trade and commerce will be high on the agenda, cooperation in science and technology, atomic energy, defense, and tourism will also figure prominently at the talks.
