920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / UK Company News: Whitecroft sells engineering side Whitecroft has effectively reduced its borrowings by Pounds 1.97m via the sale of Silcoms, its sole precision engineering subsidiary. This is a further step in the disposal of peripheral activities and allows management to focus on operations in lighting, building products, medical cotton fibre and textiles. In the year to March 31 1992 Silcoms made pre-tax profit of Pounds 383,000, its net tangible assets were Pounds 721,000, and book value of property Pounds 551,000. The purchaser was Bolton Engineering, owned by the present directors of Silcoms, together with 3i Group. Consideration for the sale was Pounds 1.35m and Whitecroft received a dividend of Pounds 75,000.
