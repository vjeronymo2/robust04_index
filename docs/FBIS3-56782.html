Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A Georgian delegation, led by Eduard Shevardnadze, left for an official visit to Turkey on Wednesday. It includes the Foreign Minister Aleksandr Chikvaydze, the Interior Minister Shote Kvaria, and the Minister for Industry Tenghiz Geleishvili [as received]. On the way to Turkey, the plane landed at Batumi, where the delegation was joined by Aslan Abashidze, the Chairman of Ajaria's Supreme Soviet. Abashidze has told Interfax that he intends to discuss with Turkish leaders plans to invest in Ajaria's economy, modernize Batumi's docks, and supply electricity to the republic. During the visit, Eduard Shevardnadze is to meet with Turkey's President Suleyman Demirel, Prime Minister Tansu Ciller, the Parliament's Chairman Husametdin Jindoruk, and the opposition leader Mesut Olmaz. The sides are to sign a political declaration on the principles of relations and development prospects, a protocol on relations between the parliaments of the two countries, agreements on custom regulations, on cooperation in economy, trade, and agriculture, and separate accords on contacts in science and technology, in culture, tourism, and sports.
