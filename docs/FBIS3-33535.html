Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 10 Feb (PL) -- The Mexican daily LA JORNADA today reported that President Carlos Salinas de Gortari's government approved the appointment of Abelardo Curbelo as the new Cuban ambassador to Mexico. Curbelo will assume office in the next few days. LA JORNADA cites statements by outgoing diplomat Jose Fernandez de Cossio, who said that "the appointment of the new Mexican ambassador to Cuba, Carlos Tello, was approved three days ago and that the appointment of the new Cuban ambassador to Mexico was also approved." According to Ambassador Fernandez de Cossio, ties between the two countries "are fine in every sense and there is excellent communication. These ties are longstanding and very strong." The Cuban ambassador said Abelardo Curbelo is currently the Cuban ambassador to Uruguay, and "will travel to Mexico City shortly." Fernandez de Cossio hosted an official farewell reception for the diplomatic corps accredited in Mexico. Fernandez de Cossio has been ambassador to Mexico for the past 10 years. Mexican Tourism Secretary Jesus Silva Herzog; Cuauhtemoc Cardenas, presidential candidate for the opposition Party of the Democratic Revolution; and Colombian writer Gabriel Garcia Marquez were among those attending the farewell reception. Fernandez de Cossio stressed that "for reasons of mutual geographic interest, proximity, and cultural and historic similarities, the ties between Cuba and Mexico have always been particularly strong." The Cuban ambassador also recalled the high degree of awareness the two countries have regarding the international situation and the role the United Nations should play, as well as other important matters of current interest. "We must thank Mexico for the position it has maintained at the various international forums against all attempts to isolate my small island. This position has been an expression of friendship and solidarity which we value highly," Fernandez de Cossio said. In conclusion, the Cuban ambassador reiterated the expressions of love and affection he has received in the approximately 10 years of being in this country. "I take with me memories and experiences that in these past few days have been heightened by the many expressions of affection I have been receiving and which can be summed up by the fact that I have been awarded the Order of the Aztec Eagle."
