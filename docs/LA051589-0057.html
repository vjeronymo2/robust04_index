May 15, 1989, Monday, Southland Edition

CHURCH TOPS OFF BUSH'S COUNTRY WEEKEND

President Bush attended Sunday services at an Episcopal church in this small town before heading back to Washington, where he will announce his proposals for anti-crime legislation today.

The President spent Saturday night as a guest on the 18,000-acre horse farm of his close friend, Will Farish.

Bush was guest of honor at a fund-raiser for Sen. Mitch McConnell (R-Ky.) at the Farish farm Saturday night. The fund-raiser brought in $1 million for McConnell's reelection campaign.

The President, accompanied by Farish and his wife, Sarah, went to an 8 a.m. communion service at St. John's Episcopal Church. The service took place in a small chapel that seats about 40 worshipers. The President had been invited to church by the Rev. J. Carl Belden.
