Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 12 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- The National Defense Secretariat [Sedena] reported today that the most important towns in Chiapas initially occupied by the armed groups have been recovered. Thus the first objective of the military operation "has been achieved." In its Communique No. 14, Sedena states that by achieving this first objective, the Mexican Army has taken territorial control of the area in general and that civilian authorities have already begun exercising their responsibilities. The text of the Sedena communique follows: "In accordance with the planned operations as drafted by the 7th Military Region Command based in Tuxtla Gutierrez, Chiapas, we have achieved the first objective of the operation, which is the recovery of the most important towns taken over by the transgressors, with the exception of Guadalupe Tepeyac, which should take place as soon as a large number of installations destroyed by the assailants on the various roads are repaired. "In achieving this first objective, the Mexican Army has recovered territorial control of the area in general. Civilian authorities are exercising their responsibilities in all municipal capitals taken over by the assailants. No area of significance in the region is being occupied by these armed people, even though some isolated groups may still be operating."
