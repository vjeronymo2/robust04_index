BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Andrey Surzhanskiy] [Text] Moscow June 21 TASS -- Negotiations of UNESCO Director- General Federico Mayor and representatives of Russian scientific and cultural elite held today at the Russian Ministry for Science and Technical Policy aimed to give a fresh impetus to the UNESCO-Russian efforts in education, culture and science and discuss cooperation in communications. The UNESCO director-general is currently in Russia on an official visit to receive a people's friendship order from Russian President Boris Yeltsin and attend the opening ceremony of the UNESCO Moscow office. The Russian delegation to the negotiations included Minister of Science and Technical Policy Boris Saltykov, Ministers of Culture and Education Yevgeniy Sidorov and Yevgeniy Tkachenko and Vice- President of the Russian Academy of Sciences Academician Rem Petrov. The sides agreed on international conferences in Russia under the UNESCO aegis, UNESCO support to Russian efforts to reform secondary education and the Russian intellectual contribution to international projects. Mayor stressed that the Russian intellectual potential cannot be called into question. UNESCO and Russia will hold the second world congress on information science and education in 1996. Upon the negotiations completion Federico Mayor and Rector of the Russian State Institute for International Relations Anatoliy Torkunov signed an agreement to create a human rights department at the institute. Federico Mayor told ITAR-TASS that three years ago they signed a memorandum on interaction to confirm bilateral cooperation in education, science, culture and communications. Then three major projects were outlined. The first one was related to the support to the Bolshoy theatre, the second concerned the state library, and the third concerned the Petersburg hermitage. At the same time, UNESCO would like to develop close relations with the Russian scientific community. Today the sides discussed the results of bilateral efforts within the past year on all the three projects. UNESCO has allocated 500,000 U.S. dollars to finance various Russian projects, including the activities of research centers, schools and programs of Russian-language studies.
