BFN [Text] [No dateline as received] Franz Blankart, state secretary of the Swiss federal department of national economy, speaking at a news conference in Bern about the outcome of his visit to Almaty, described Kazakhstan as "a fascinating country." During his visit agreements on capital investment and trade were signed. He welcomed the development of bilateral ties. He said that Kazakhstan "offers a promising future for foreign investments." Kazakhstan currently buys from Switzerland technology and products of the chemical and some industries worth 20 million Swiss francs. For its part Switzerland annually buys Kazakh products worth 4.3 million Swiss francs. According to the SWISS TELEGRAPH AGENCY, during his visit Mr. Blankart signed an agreement on the promotion and protection of investments and on trade and economic cooperation. Switzerland can invest in environmental protection and railroad development in Kazakhstan, with attention being paid to the project for constructing a railroad from Turkey to China.
