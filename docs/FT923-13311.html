920714 FT 14 JUL 92 / Parliament and Politics: Setback for MPs' Maxwell campaign THE CAMPAIGN on behalf of the Maxwell pensioners suffered a setback yesterday as it emerged that there is little prospect of an inquiry by the parliamentary ombudsman into the conduct of the Department of Trade and Industry. MPs believe that the statement last week by Mr Norman Lamont, the chancellor, was a signal that there is not enough of a case of potential maladministration for the ombudsman to investigate. He said claims that the DTI had failed to act on warnings from Imro, the Investment Management Regulatory Organisation, did not appear to be substantiated, and that the Securities and Investments Board (SIB) found those legal uncertainties did not make any material difference to Imro's regulation of the Maxwell pension fund companies. Details of the argument against an investigation were set out in a Commons debate by Mr Anthony Nelson, the economic secretary. He said that after Imro had passed to the DTI a report about the problems of the law on pension fund regulation, the department had told Imro and SIB that it was for them to devise adequate investor protection.
