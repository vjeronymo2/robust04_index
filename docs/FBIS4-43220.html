BFN [Text] The day after the elections is more important for the political parties. The results, which contradicted all preelection polls, have political party officials thinking seriously. Despite the euphoria of the smaller parties whose power was increased significantly, their officials are also examining the results seriously. The final results were announced just a short while ago: PASOK [Panhellenic Socialist Movement] received 37.64 percent of the vote and 10 seats; New Democracy received 32.66 percent and nine seats; Political Spring received 8.65 percent and two seats; KKE [Greek Communist Party] received 6.29 percent and two seats; and the Coalition of the Left and Progress received 6.25 percent and two seats. DIANA [Democratic Renewal], which received 2.79 percent of the vote, did not manage to elect a deputy to the European Parliament.
