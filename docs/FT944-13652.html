941026 FT 26 OCT 94 / International Company News: CSX repeats earnings record in third quarter CSX reported its second consecutive quarter of record earnings yesterday, as the US transportation company continued to reap the benefits of productivity improvements, tight cost controls and a buoyant economic environment in its main domestic and foreign markets. Mr John Snow, chairman of the Virginia-based CSX, said he expected these positive trends to sustain the company's earnings well into 1995. In its third quarter, CSX earned Dollars 177m, or Dollars 1.68 a share, on operating revenues of Dollars 2.47bn. In the same period a year ago, the group earned (excluding tax-related charges) Dollars 117m, or Dollars 1.13, on revenues of Dollars 2.24bn. Operating income at the company's railway unit CSX Transportation jumped 40 per cent to Dollars 224m in the quarter, while income at its container-shipping unit, Sea-Land Service, climbed 10 per cent to Dollars 78m.
