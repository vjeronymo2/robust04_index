940518 FT 18 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television You have to hand it to television drama departments: though they know viewers would rather be entertained, they stick doggedly to serious social issues. Today's 'Screen Two' is Sin Bin in which Pete Postlethwaite plays a nurse in a mental hospital who sees a colleague batter one of the patients to death. Should he blow the whistle or turn a blind eye? (9.00 BBC2). It will be surprising if the audience for that is anything like as big as the audience for Three Men And A Baby on ITV, even though the 1987 movie has been shown on television before. Ted Danson, Steve Guttenberg, and Tom Selleck star as three bachelors who prove to be incompetent when they are landed with a baby girl to look after (8.00). Inside Story looks at the real life 'manageress' of the football world: Karren Brady, managing director of Birmingham City FC. We are assured that she was appointed not because club owner David Sullivan, who made a fortune from girlie magazines, thought it a good gimmick to choose a photogenic 23-year-old blonde, but because she was the right person for the job (9.40 BBC1).
