931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / Observer: Empire line While Russia's politicians haggle over the future of Lenin's embalmed body, Leonid Brezhnev's luxury ambulance has drawn up inside Russia's Imperial Bank. Having paid Dollars 250,000 for the artefact in a charity auction jointly organised by Sotheby's, the bank plans to show off its acquisition across Europe. For the Zil is part of a sculpture known as the 'sleeping beauty' which also features a young woman lying on a tomb - a combination which, according to Russian art experts, symbolises potential democratic power. 'This is the new imperial style in art. It suits us because we have become a financial empire,' explains the bank which has recently entered into an alliance with two other Russian finance houses. Indeed, it is evidently quite taken with the regal theme. A recent advertising campaign depicts the emperor Caesar under the slogan: 'Punctuality is the courtesy of Kings.'
