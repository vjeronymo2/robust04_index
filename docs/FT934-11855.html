931029 FT 29 OCT 93 / Company productivity growing A MAJORITY of top personnel managers say their companies' productivity is growing and expect further increases in the next 12 months. A MORI poll of 181 delegates at the Institute of Personnel Management Conference found that 80 per cent reported productivity increases in the past year. Nearly half said the improvement had been better than 3 per cent. Some 60 per cent said their workforce had fallen in the past year: 15 per cent of those by more than a tenth. Most managers said their companies had improved cost-effectiveness, profitability and business success, but at a price. Some 81 per cent of them reported increased stress levels among staff and 61 per cent believed employee morale had deteriorated.
