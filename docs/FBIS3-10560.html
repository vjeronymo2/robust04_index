Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "RPC Television News" newscast] [Text] Today's most recent CID-Gallup poll places Ernesto Perez Balladares, PRD [Democratic Revolutionary Party] presidential candidate, at the head of voters' preferences. He is followed by Ruben Dario Carles and Mireya Moscoso de Gruber. Perez Balladares continues to lead electoral preferences, according to the latest poll by the international consulting firm CID-Gallup in an exclusive for RPC Radio and Television. This is the most recent nationwide CID-Gallup poll conducted between 26 February and 3 March. A total of 1,218 Panamanians over 18 years of age were polled. Ruben Dario Carles follows Ernesto Perez Balladares, and progovernment candidate Mireya Moscoso de Gruber is in third place. Ruben Blades ranks fourth, Christian Democrat Eduardo Vallarino fifth, and Samuel Lewis Galindo and Jose Salvador Munoz are sixth. Of the total number of people polled by CID-Gallup, 33 percent favored Ernesto Perez Balladares, 20.4 percent favored Ruben Dario Carles, 12.5 percent favored Mireya Moscoso de Gruber, 10.4 percent favored Ruben Blades, 3.1 percent favored Eduardo Vallarino, and 1 percent favored Samuel Lewis Galindo; in the meantime, 19.3 percent are undecided. The poll also revealed seven out of 10 Panamanians responded they will probably vote in the 8 May elections, according to the CID-Gallup poll. Ernesto Perez Balladares and Ruben Dario Carles also monopolize the negative votes, inasmuch as the people polled claim these are the two candidates for which the majority would not vote for [sentence as heard]. As this chart shows, Perez Balladares has 27 percent while Ruben Dario Carles has 26 percent. The same CID-Gallup poll discloses 60 days from the elections the PRD is the party with the most followers. Twenty-nine percent support the Ruben Dario Carles coalition; Molirena [Nationalist Liberal Republican Movement], Morena [National Renewal Movement], and Civic Renewal Party receive the support of 16.1 percent, while the Arnulfista Party receives 11.8 percent. Within the next few days, we will report on other important results of the most recent CID-Gallup poll in the country.
