930419 FT 19 APR 93 / Wage-link fines under review THE GOVERNMENT is 'anxiously' reviewing the system of unit fines in which courts penalise offenders according to their ability to pay as well as the seriousness of the offence, the Home Office confirmed yesterday. Mr Kenneth Clarke, the home secretary, is looking at ways to end anomalies such as minor offences attracting heavy penalties. The Home Office said that new legislation might not be required. Unit fines were introduced under the 1991 Criminal Justice Act. Mr Clarke said in the Commons last week: 'I share the view of the Magistrates Association that in principle unit fines are right. 'However, we know that in practice they are producing some odd results, perhaps partly because of the way in which the rules to implement the act have been drawn up and partly because some magistrates' courts are not familiar with their powers.'
