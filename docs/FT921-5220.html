920305 FT 05 MAR 92 / UK Company News: BAe and Asda settle dispute British Aerospace and Asda, the supermarket group, have settled a dispute about an option concerning the Burwood House Group, their property joint venture. The dispute flared after Arlington, BAe's property subsidiary, was forced to pay the joint venture company Pounds 79m last month to fulfil an indemnity agreement. Asda argued that it had the right to exercise an option, that would force BAe to buy it out, potentially for some Pounds 75m. However, Arlington said the option was irrelevant, since it only applied in the event of a default and was invalidated by the payment of Pounds 79m. Asda said that the argument had been 'amicably settled.'
