Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 14 Jan (MENA)--Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi has stated that the Interior Ministry's investigation departments continue their battle against deviation and corruption and apprehend the perpetrators immediately. In a statement following the inauguration of the renovated general traffic department building in Madinat al-Nasr today, al-Alfi asserted that the state is exposing any deviation, corruption, or breach of the law, that confronting corruption is the state's policy, and that no one is above the law. On reports that some security officials are involved in the case of the Madinat al-Nasr multimillionaire, who was arrested recently, al-Alfi said that no conclusive evidence has been brought against any security official and that the current reports are mere rumors. Al-Alfi added that the prosecutor is conducting his investigations. Therefore, no premature judgment can be made. However, if there were violations or someone was involved, this will show up in the investigations. No one is above the law. On the security forces' efforts to hunt down terrorists, especially in Upper Egypt, and their shift to a defensive position, al-Alfi denied this, stressing that the security authorities have always been in control of the situation and that they continue to take the initiative in tracking down the terrorist elements. He explained that the security authorities continue their work day and night to confront any deviations or incidents committed against the state's security and stability. On Pakistan's expulsion of terrorist elements from its territory and whether any of these terrorists have been arrested, al-Alfi explained that there are continued contacts with a number of states in this regard.
