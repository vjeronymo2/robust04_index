Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By Georgiy Shmelev, ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 13 Jan -- Russian Federation Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev met today with U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher in the yellow reception room of the Great Kremlin Palace. The heads of the foreign policy departments of the two states examined a package of Russian-American documents to be signed in the course of the visit to Moscow by the U.S. President. Galina Sidorova, the Russian minister's political adviser, told an ITAR-TASS correspondent that three agreements have been made ready for signing: Air traffic; health and biomedical research cooperation; and cooperation in the area of studying the effects of radiation on people's health and on the environment. A memorandum of intentions and cooperation in the sphere of export controls is also ready for signing. A joint Russian-American statement on the Near East will also be adopted. Galina Sidorova said that the Russian and the U.S. Presidents will sign the main document -- the Moscow Declaration. They will also issue a statement on human rights and a statement on nonproliferation of nuclear weapons. Andrey Kozyrev and Warren Christopher focused especially on the examination of the results of the NATO summit meeting in Brussels, the range of issues concerning mutual relations with Ukraine, and the position of the two sides on the eve of the trilateral Russian- American-Ukrainian summit meeting on 14 January, Galina Sidorova noted. The heads of the Russian and U.S. foreign policy departments discussed in detail the problems of achieving a settlement in Bosnia. Andrey Kozyrev emphasized in the course of the conversation that "approval of the Partnership for Peace program was the key result of the NATO session in Brussels." The formula is completely to Russia's liking. Andrey Kozyrev stressed: "We understand it to mean a broad and equal cooperation among all the interested sides."
