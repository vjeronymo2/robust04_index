Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Text] At its meeting in Bratislava today, the government approved the draft addendum on the payments agreement between Slovakia and the Czech Republic and the draft agreement between the two countries on mutual payments of outstanding claims ensuing for the two republics from certain agreements following the end of the federation. Deputy Prime Minister Sergej Kozlik said that, on the basis of these legislative amendments, the so-called old bloc should be abolished and a one-bloc clearing system of accounting should be introduced. According to Kozlik, there were no direct talks at the government session today about the Slovak budget deficit. At a news conference, however, he said that the government had to grant 6 billion Slovak korunas from the state budget to commence independent activity of the National Insurance Company, which hugely increased the state budget revenues for January.
