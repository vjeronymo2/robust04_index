September 6, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: PEANUT BUTTER COOKIES WITHOUT THE CHOLESTEROL

The recipe for this tasty, sugar-free cookie was sent to me by a reader. I have revised it slightly to make it cholesterol-free as well.

It is easy to make and ideal for brown-bag lunches. It is also a wonderfully healthful after-school snack.

SOFT PEANUT-BUTTER COOKIES

1/2 cup raisins

1/2 cup chopped dates, packed

1 ripe medium banana, mashed

1/3 cup unhomogenized creamy peanut butter

1/4 cup water

2 egg whites

1 teaspoon vanilla

1 cup oats

1/2 cup whole-wheat flour

1 teaspoon baking soda

In mixing bowl combine raisins, dates, banana, peanut butter, water, egg whites and vanilla. Beat until blended. Add oats, flour and baking soda. Mix to blend thoroughly.

Drop by heaping teaspoons onto non-stick baking sheet. Bake at 350 degrees about 10 minutes or until browned on underside. Cool on rack. Store in airtight container. Makes about 50 cookies.

Each cookie contains approximately 32 calories; no cholesterol; 1 gm fat; 17 mg sodium.
