931222 FT 22 DEC 93 / Bloodiest year for Kurd separatists More than 4,000 people died in the conflict between the Turkish government and separatist Kurds in 1993, the bloodiest year since the revolt began in 1984, Turkey's Interior Ministry said yesterday, Reuter reports from Ankara. The toll included 1,944 Kurdistan Workers' party (PKK) fighters, 1,511 civilians and 725 members of the security forces, including village guards. More than 10,500 people have been killed since the PKK launched its guerrilla war. Hundreds more have died in Turkish cross-border operations against the PKK in northern Iraq.
