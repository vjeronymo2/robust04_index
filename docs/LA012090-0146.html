January 20, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

FIDELITY FINANCIAL SELLING STOCK BACK TO HAMMOND CO.

Irvine-based Fidelity National Financial Inc. said Friday that it will sell its 23.6% interest in the Hammond Co. and that Hammond will pay $1.9 million to purchase the Fidelity stake.

The agreement ends seven months of sporadic talks between the two firms about establishing closer business ties or a possible merger.

Hammond directors and shareholders now will consider turning the publicly traded firm into a private company, said company Chairman Thomas T. Hammond. About 10 of the Newport Beach company's 400 shareholders own about 60% of the firm, he said.

Hammond said the company will use its cash reserves to purchase the stock.

After a recent meeting, Fidelity executives decided that "a mutually agreeable business operation wouldn't be forthcoming, and we didn't want to pursue Hammond in a hostile nature," said Frank P. Willey, Fidelity's executive vice president and general counsel.

Fidelity, a title insurance firm operating in 31 states, agreed to sell its stake to Hammond for $4.83 a share -- the estimated price Fidelity paid for the Hammond stock.

"We never got around to talking seriously about an acquisition," Willey said. "Our conceptual discussions were far enough apart."
