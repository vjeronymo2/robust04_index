BFN [Text] Beijing, May 14 (CNS) -- China has decided to lift restrictions on some commodities imported to China. By the end of last year, China had canceled requirements of a permit for a batch of imported commodities, including steel material, steel billet, waste steel, plasters, drilling tools, coffee and its products, airplanes for civil purpose, fruits and black-and-white kinescopes. Restrictions on the following commodities to be imported to the country will also be canceled by the end of this year. They are: chemical fibre clothing, beer and grape wine, plant oil, wood and wood pulp, ABS resin, sodium chloride, demolition material for civil purposes, synthetic glue, plywood, petroleum, some chemical products, computers, X-ray machines, some medical equipment, air- conditioners of power over 21.4 kilowatts and cooling compressors of power over 22.5 kilowatts. Permits required of some imports which will be lifted by the end of 1995 cover such commodities as duplicating machines, integrated circuits, films, some kinds of chemical products, wines, air-conditioners with cooling equipment being not yet installed and car chassis. Permits for pesticide and carbonate beverage will be lifted by the end of 1996.
