BFN [Text] With a joint investment of 160 million yuan by the Qingdao Port Office and the ISCOY Corporation of the Republic of South Africa, the 100,000-tonne-class wharf specially for shipping minerals located at Qingdao Harbor's Qianwan area was formally made available to users on 16 April. Now, Qingdao Harbor has become the largest minerals transfer port in northern China. This wharf is primarily devoted to unloading, storing, and transferring minerals from South Africa. The annual comprehensive handling capacity is estimated at 6 million tonnes.
