BFN [Rahmi Aygun video report] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] A joint communique was issued at the end of the NATO foreign ministers meeting in Istanbul today. The communique stated that an agreement was reached on Russia's importance for Europe. The statement expressed satisfaction over Russia's declaration of its intention to sign the Partnership for Peace agreement. The constructive relations between Russia and NATO will constitute the key element of confidence and stability in Europe, the communique noted. Referring to regional issues, the communique described the cease-fire agreement to be enforced in Bosnia-Herzegovina for one month, beginning tomorrow, as a positive development. Pointing out that the alliance is open to other European countries as well, the statement declared that efforts will be exerted to prevent the proliferation of weapons of mass destruction.
