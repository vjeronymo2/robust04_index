930813 FT 13 AUG 93 / Brain disease kills cattle farmer A SECOND dairy farmer has died of the brain disorder Creutzfeld-Jacob Disease, which is similar to the 'mad cow' disease BSE, Clive Cookson writes. Professor Richard Lacey of Leeds University, a critic of government policy on BSE, said the death of Mr Duncan Templeman, announced yesterday, had grave implications for human health. Several animals in Mr Templeman's Somerset diary herd had BSE. But the Department of Health said: 'The government's expert advisers have considered this case and have agreed that that there are no features that give cause for undue concern.'
