Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Theodhoros Pangalos, alternate foreign minister in charge of European affairs, told the French paper QUEST-FRANCE that the settlement of the Bosnian crisis is a top priority for Greece in its capacity as president of the European Union [EU]. Mr. Pangalos said that we must resolve this problem before the spring or else the belligerent sides will embark on new war operations. He also noted: The military commanders are now prevented by the unfavorable weather conditions; we must exploit this fact and put an end to the war. In his interview, the Greek minister points out that we are dedicated to the idea of inter-Balkan cooperation in the resolving of clashes. Mr. Pangalos added: However, the presidency would not do what Greece would like to do. He stressed: The presidency is not the departure point for Greek foreign policy, we will not make this mistake. Mr. Pangalos also made it clear that the Greek Government will not use the presidency to upset the recognition of the republic of Skopje by most of the European countries. He said: This is not a EU problem. Mr. Pangalos pointed out that the hasty recognition of the former Yugoslav republics brought on the bloodshed in the Balkans. He stressed: If we had not recognized Bosnia, the massacre, or at least part of it, would have been avoided. In conclusion, Theodhoros Pangalos stressed that we must avoid confrontations could develo into armed clashes.
