BFN ["Text" of letter from PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat to Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin "in the wake of the Israeli Government's protest against 'Arafat's statements in South Africa;" date not given] [Text] Mr. Prime Minister: Greetings. The PLO reiterates the following with regard to the Gaza-Jericho accord signed in Cairo: 1. The PLO pledges that the Palestinian Authority, including the police and other departments, will work in accordance with this agreement and will effect a mechanism for coordination and cooperation in the appropriate time. 2. The PLO pledges to cooperate with Israel and to help it discover the location of and return the Israeli soldiers who are still missing, as well as return the bodies of the dead soldiers who have not been restored yet. 3. The PLO pledges to ask the Palestine National Council, in its next session, to officially approve amendments to the PLO National Charter in accordance with the pledge included in the message signed by the PLO chairman on 9 September 1993 and addressed to Israel's prime minister. 4. As I enter the Gaza Strip and Jericho area I will use the title of the chairman of the Palestinian Authority or the PLO chairman, not the president of the state of Palestine. 5. I pledge not to take any measures that would change the status of Gaza Strip and Jericho area and to discuss this matter during the talks for a permanent solution to the situation. 6. We pledge to implement Paragraph 3 of Article 4 of the accord, which stipulates that we must inform the Government of Israel of the names of the Palestinian Authority members in a letter to be presented within a week. The appointment of authority members will be effective in accordance with letters to be exchanged to this effect between the PLO and the Government of Israel. Also, any change in the membership of the Palestinian Authority will be effective in accordance with letters to be exchanged between the PLO and the Government of Israel. 7. Following the endorsement of the accord, talks will begin immediately to grant the authority the powers to implement Article 6 of the Declaration of Principles. Both parties will work toward exploring possible ways to expand the sphere of talks so as to exceed the five fields. [not further identified] 8. Both parties will work toward giving momentum to talks in light of temporary arrangements in accordance with the Declaration of Principles and its objective. 9. We are committed to embarking on permanent talks on the final status as soon as possible provided that they will not be later than the beginning of the third year of the transitional period. 10. We will decide -- through cooperation with Israel and within one month -- on calling the Governments of Jordan and Egypt to form a follow-up committee as noted in the Declaration of Principles. 11. We will assess, together with the Government of Israel, all necessary legislation that would help implement the accord. 12. We are ready to continue discussion on the following issues: A. Jericho area. B. Allowing an official Palestinian presence on bridges. C. Achieving additional measures in the Rafah crossing area. D. All suspended issues that are mentioned in the accord. [Signed] Sincerely, Yasir 'Arafat
