Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Lieutenant General 'Umar al-Bashir, the president of the Republic, has condemned the 4 February incident in which a number of worshippers at the mosque of the Quarter One at al-Thawrah in Omdurman fell victim. He described the incident as strange to Sudanese society, particularly in view of the hideous nature of the crime. His Excellency did not rule out the involvement of foreign quarters, despite the fact that the statements of the criminals did not indicate this in the initial stage of the inquiry. During his meeting today with media leaders, al-Bashir commended the security organs for arresting the criminals and said the accused would be given a just trial immediately after the completion of investigations. He said Sudan's doors would remain open to anyone who wants to live with us in security and peace. During his meeting today with media leaders, the president also said that decrees would be signed next week providing for the formation of the new states and the appointment of their governors, deputy governors and other members of their governments. He said in this connection that strong and trustworthy individuals with leadership capabilities would be chosen in accordance with the revolution's method of appointing political and executive leaders. Lieutenant General al-Bashir also declared Sudan's readiness to send forces to preserve security and stability in Bosnia under the auspices of the United Nations. He appealed to Islamic states to send forces to put an end to the conspiracy being hatched against the Muslim Bosnian people.
