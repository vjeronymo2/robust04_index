Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington, February 15 (XINHUA) -- A U.S. congressman will visit Pyongyang this week at the invitation of the Government of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK). Representative Ronald Dellums, a California Democrat and the chairman of the House Armed Services Committee, hoped to help defuse the dispute over the alleged DPRK's nuclear weapon programs, according to the NEW YORK TIMES. The newspaper quoted U.S. Administration officials as saying that the White House has approved the visit, which is a secretly arranged trip. He is expected to meet with the DPRK leader Kim Il-song and his top advisers, the newspaper said.
