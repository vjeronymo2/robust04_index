Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 14 KYODO -- Norwegian parliamentary leader Kristi Kolle Grondahl Monday [14 March] met Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa for an exchange of greetings and a brief discussion of Russia's disposal of radioactive waste into the Sea of Japan. Grondahl, president of the 165-member Storting, remarked that Norway faces a similar problem with Russian nuclear dumping near its own borders, government officials said. She said Prime Minister Gro Harlem Brundtland, who canceled plans to visit Japan in January because of a cold, hopes to visit in the near future to discuss the problem. Hosokawa said Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata plans to raise the issue when he visits Russia this month. Grondahl is visiting Japan at the invitation of the House of Councillors.
