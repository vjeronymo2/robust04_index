Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 10 (YONHAP) -- South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu met his visiting Romanian counterpart Teodor Melescanu Thursday and discussed the North Korean nuclear problem, international affairs and ways to boost bilateral economic cooperation. Han sought Bucharest's support for Seoul's bid to become a non-permanent member of the United Nations Security Council in 1996-1997, and Melescanu promised full cooperation, said Paek Nak-hwan, director-general of the Ministry's European affairs bureau. The two foreign ministers also signed an aviation pact allowing Korean civilian airlines to fly freely to the Eastern European country.
