BFN [Text] Seoul, June 19 (YONHAP) -- The South Korean Government embarked on comprehensive preparations Sunday for an inter-Korean summit meeting between South Korean President Kim Yong-sam and North Korean leader Kim Il-song as the possibility for the meeting has become higher. On Saturday, President Kim promptly accepted the proposal by Kim Il-song through former U.S. President Jimmy Carter who returned to the South after a four-day visit to the communist country. The National Unification Board (NUB), in this regard, formed a working-level task force team headed by Vice Minister Song Yong-tae and participated in by related ranking officials, an NUB official said. The team is making a variety of working-level preparations for the summitry, a rare opportunity between the heads of the two countries in four decades, the official said, adding that the participants are now engaged in discussions for more details such as the timing and place for it.
