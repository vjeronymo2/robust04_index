BFN [Text] Intensive consultations are under way in Kiev following the Crimean parliament's decision to reinstate the republic's constitution of May 6, 1992, presidential spokesman Nikolay Mikhalchenko told Interfax-Ukraine. "President Kravchuk and Speaker of Parliament Aleksandr Moroz are meeting to discuss the issue and I hope the president will address the deputies today," he said. On Wednesday, Kravchuk said that if Simferopol took a decision to bring back its constitution, he would ask the Ukrainian parliament to rescind it. Mikhalchenko said that Simferopol's decision was out of line with the Ukrainian basic law and therefore, illegal. "The decision would lead to the setting up of a confederation in Ukraine which is a unitary state," he said.
