Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Jakarta, Feb 24 (ANTARA) -- President Suharto said Thursday he would continue to fulfil his constitutional obligations given by the People's Consultative Assembly (MPR) as its mandatory [mandate holder] for the 1993-98 presidential tenure. "I will fulfil my constitutional duties," the president was quoted as saying by Religious Minister Tarmizi Taher. The head of state made the remarks in response to questions of a number of ulemas put forward by the minister on the implementation of his task as the MPR mandatory. Tarmizi Taher said that a number of ulemas raised concern that Suharto would leave his post before his tenure is completed in 1998. The minister said he had received a number of telephone calls from ulemas asking clarification on rumors that President Suharto will leave his post before completing his presidential tenure. He said that the telephone on this question also came from the Indonesian Ulemas Council's (MUI) Chairman Hasan Basri. On the occasion, the minister also reported on his recent visit to Saudi Arabia to observe preparations for the 1994 pilgrimange season. [passage omitted]
