BFN [Announcer-read report] [Text] The Ankara chief public prosecutor has concluded that the remarks made by Prosperity Party leader Necmettin Erbakan at a party meeting last Wednesday [13 April] fall within the scope the crime of overtly instigating the people to hatred and hostility by discriminating against religion, race, or sect -- a crime cited in the Turkish Penal Code. The chief public prosecutor handed the file over to the chief prosecutor of the Ankara State Security Court. In a statement to Turkish Radio and Television, Ankara Chief Public Prosecutor Nazmi Sarvan said that his office completed the investigation into the tape of a speech delivered by Erbakan at a party meeting on 13 April. We concluded, Sarvan said, that Erbakan's remarks constitute instigating people to hatred and hostility. Recalling that this crime falls within the jurisdiction of the state security courts, Sarvan said that the file has been sent over to the chief prosecutor of the Ankara State Security Court. Nusret Demirel, chief prosecutor of the Ankara State Security Court, said that the file has not reached him yet, and that his office is also conducting an investigation into the same issue. He went on to say that the two files will be joined, and the investigation continued.
