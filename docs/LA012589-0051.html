January 25, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

EARNINGS

H.F. Ahmanson & Co., parent of Home Savings of America and Bowery Savings Bank, reported that earnings rose 47% in the fourth quarter but dropped 5% for the year. The Los Angeles-based holding company earned $67.6 million in the quarter and $202 million for all of 1988. Chairman and Chief Executive Richard H. Diehl attributed the quarterly gain to higher interest income and record mortgage loan volume. He said the decline in annual earnings was largely because of a 76% reduction in after-tax gains on sales of loans and mortgage-backed securities.
