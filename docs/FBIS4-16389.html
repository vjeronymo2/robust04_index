BFN [Text] The situation is exceedingly tense in the Karabakh conflict zone where intensive military operations are continuing on the north and northeastern sectors of the front. The Nagorno-Karabakh Republic Defense Army headquarters press service reported that the Azerbaijani troops are not refraining from attempts to advance in certain sectors. Military operations have resumed in these sectors as of this morning. The Azerbaijani air force is also not slackening its activity. Having sustained strong a counteroffensive on the southern regions of Mardakert on 13 April, the enemy suffered substantial losses and was forced to retreat to its initial positions. In general, the Karabakh forces are reliably controlling the situation all along the front. Armenian Defense Ministry press service reported that new tension arose on the northeastern border of Armenia during the daytime on 13 April. Using various types of weapons, the Azerbaijani Army opened fired on Voskepar in Noyemberyanskiy Rayon, and on Aygepar, Nerkin-Karmiragpyur, and Movses and defensive positions around these villages in Tavushskiy Rayon. The artillery shelling in Noyemberyanskiy Rayon continued for five hours. The Armenian border guards were forced to retaliate and suppress the enemy fire. There were no casualties on our side. The press service also reported that another cease-fire violation took place yesterday on the southwestern border of Armenia. The Nakhichevani military units fired for an hour on Shagat, and on Arevis in Sisianskiy Rayon. There are no casualties. Our troops did not retaliate. Relative calm prevailed on Armenia's borders last night and early this morning. However, the enemy opened machine-gun and howitzer fire on border positions in Idzhevanskiy Rayon (?today). There are injured. The Armenian Army fighters took countermeasures.
