Language: Vietnamese Article Type:BFN [Text] According to sources from the antismuggling department under the Customs General Department, customs officers at the Ho Chi Minh City postal service have uncovered a case of drug trafficking through the postal service as follows: While checking a parcel destined for the United States, Customs Inspector Le Dinh Khang discovered that the liner of a blanket contained a layer of morphine. The liner itself was fit into a quilt. The overall weight of the blanket was 5.95 kilograms and the layer of morphine alone weighed about 3 kilograms. The name of the sender was Tran Thi Quang Kim, a resident of Thong Nhat District in Dong Nai Province, and the name of the receiver was Ly Quang Minh, an overseas Vietnamese in the United States. It was also known that Nong Thi Kim Dung, a resident of Ba Ria-Vung Tau, brought the parcel to the post office. Both the proof of the crime and the persons involved have already been handed over to the Ho Chi Minh public security service for further investigation.
