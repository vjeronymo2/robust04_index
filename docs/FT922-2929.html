920613 FT 13 JUN 92 / International Company News: UAL seeks TWA assets UNITED Airlines said it was offering to buy 40 landing slots at Chicago's O'Hare Airport, plus three gates, from Trans World Airlines, the bankrupt carrier owned by Mr Carl Icahn, writes Nikki Tait. Consideration would be a note, said to have a present value of Dollars 259m. TWA had planned to sell the slots and gates to American Airlines, one of United's main rivals, but the bankruptcy court decided that an auction should be held. A hearing on the matter in the bankruptcy court has been set fo June 23.
