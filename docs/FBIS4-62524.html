BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS] [Text] Moscow, 14 May -- At the Russian Foreign Ministry in Moscow, the Georgian and Abkhazian sides signed today an agreement on a cease-fire and disengagement of forces in the area of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict, the Russian Foreign Ministry has reported. The Russian Foreign Ministry reports the Russia Federation Government made a statement regarding the signing of the agreement in which it expressed satisfaction with this important step aimed at ensuring a stable peace. The statement says the aim of a safety zone which is to be created in line with the agreement and which will be used for the deployment of CIS peacekeeping forces is to help prevent the resumption of military actions and make it possible for refugees to return to their places of permanent residence and, first and foremost, to Gali Rayon. The Russian Government expressed confidence that the sides in the conflict would strictly follow their commitments. On its part, Russia is aware of its responsibility and it is prepared, jointly with the United Nations, to help achieve a full-scale settlement and to strive towards a situation in which the current stage is followed by new efforts aimed at restoring peace and mutual understanding in the region.
