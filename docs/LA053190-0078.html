May 31, 1990, Thursday, Valley Edition

DIGEST: REFEREE'S SUIT NAMES DISTRICT, FOOTBALL PLAYER

Attorneys representing football referee Brad Woolley have filed a lawsuit in L. A. Superior Court against the Antelope Valley Union High School District and a Palmdale High football player who attacked Woolley during a game last season.

Woolley sustained minor injuries when Darius Wilson, then 18, knocked him down in the fourth quarter of the Oct. 13 Palmdale-Saugus game at College of the Canyons.

The suit, which seeks unspecified damages, charges that Wilson caused Woolley "extreme and severe mental anguish and physical pain."

Also named as a defendant is Palmdale football Coach Kent Bothwell, who failed "to properly supervise the members of the football team," the suit alleges.

School district attorneys are reviewing the lawsuit, a spokeswoman said.

Marianne Spencer, Betsy Lund and Heather Clark of Antelope Valley College were named to the All-Foothill Conference softball team, which is selected by coaches.

Spencer, a pitcher, was 7-5 with a 2.57 earned-run average. Lund, an infielder, batted .339 with 15 runs batted in, and Clark, an outfielder, batted .359.

Cheri Bainbridge and Pam Craycraft, also of Antelope Valley, were named to the second team.
