Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] A public opinion poll conducted among Golan settlers by Haifa University's Statistics Department and commissioned by Qol Yisra'el shows that 12 percent of them would be prepared to leave the area now in exchange for appropriate compensation. About one-third of this group described sums ranging from $100,000 to $300,000 as appropriate compensation. The poll also reveals that about 6 percent of them have looked into possibilities of leaving, such as jobs and housing. Our correspondent Shula Schmerling reports that the telephone poll was conducted among approximately 300 Golan settlers.
