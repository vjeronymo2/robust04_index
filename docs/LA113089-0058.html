November 30, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: REVISE BARS TO TRIM FAT

DEAR JEANNE: Enclosed is a favorite family breakfast-brunch recipe that I feel guilty about making because it is so rich. Can you limit the butter and the eggs in this one?

-- JUDITH, Waukesha, Wis.

CHERRY BRUNCH BARS

1 cup butter

1 3/4 cups sugar

4 eggs

1 teaspoon vanilla

3 cups flour

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder

1 (21-ounce) can cherry pie filling

Cream butter and sugar, gradually adding eggs and vanilla. Combine flour, salt and baking powder and add to creamed mixture.

Place 2/3 batter in bottom of greased jelly-roll pan. Spread cherry pie filling over batter. With spoon, drop remaining batter over top.

Bake at 350 degrees 30 minutes. Makes about 15 servings.

Note: Drizzle with icing, if desired.

DEAR JUDITH: At first, I wasn't sure if I could revise this recipe successfully or not. The major problem was the difference in texture between your recipe and my attempts at revision.

However, after many tries, I managed to create brunch bars that have about a third less calories, half the fat and only one-sixth the cholesterol of your bars.

-- JEANNE

LIGHT CHERRY BRUNCH BARS

2 tablespoons cornstarch

1/3 cup water

1 (16-ounce) can tart pie cherries, packed in water

1 1/3 cups sugar

5 egg whites

1/2 cup corn-oil margarine

1 egg

1/2 cup buttermilk

1 teaspoon vanilla

3 cups unbleached flour

1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder

1/4 teaspoon salt

Dissolve cornstarch in water. Drain liquid from cherries into saucepan. Add 1/3 cup sugar and stir over low heat until sugar is dissolved. Add cherries and cornstarch mixture and stir until thickened.

Beat egg whites until stiff but not dry. Set aside. Cream margarine and remaining 1 cup sugar in another bowl. Gradually add egg and buttermilk, then vanilla.

Combine flour, baking powder and salt and add to creamed mixture. Fold in beaten egg whites.

Spread 2/3 batter in bottom of jelly-roll pan sprayed with non-stick vegetable coating. Spread with cherry mixture and drop spoonfuls of remaining batter on top. Bake at 350 degrees 30 minutes. Makes about 15 servings.

Each serving contains approximately:
