BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- The German-Dutch Corps will be established in The Hague on Friday [22 April] as the first joint military unit of the two countries. Defense Minister Volker Ruehe (Christian Democratic Union) and his Dutch counterpart Relus ter Beek will sign the agreement, the FRG Defense Ministry announced on Thursday [21 April] in Bonn. The corps will probably comprise between 50,000 and 70,000 soldiers. The corps will be commanded by a joint corps staff in Muenster. Full combat readiness is to be reached in 1995. The first commander will be Dutch Major General Ruurd Reitsma. The first such unit to be established was the German-French corps, which was later supplemented by Belgian units.
