921009 FT 09 OCT 92 / Export boom British Steel's newest investment on Teesside is enjoying an increase in exports, cushioning it against the recession in the UK construction industry, Chris Tighe writes. Yesterday Mr Barrie Cheetham (above), commercial director of British Steel's general steels division, said the company anticipated keeping the mill fully loaded 'into the planning horizon'. The Pounds 82m mill, which makes beams and columns up to 27 metres long for construction projects such as skyscrapers and bridges, has been working at capacity since becoming fully operational in mid-summer. The mill yesterday opened its doors for visits by UK and overseas customers. It has substantially extended British Steel's range of heavy sections, allowing it to win more export orders. The devaluation of the pound has also helped it compete overseas. Of its output, 85 per cent is being exported - mainly to the Far East and central Europe. Its predecessor plant on Teesside was exporting half its output two years ago. The proportion of exports has increased even though the US construction industry, an important customer, has suffered a downturn. Mr Cheetham said structural sections was seen as one of British Steel's strengths. 'Although we are in a recession, to have the benefit of a mill like this is something our competitors would envy,' he said. 'With mills like this we have the cost base, quality and project range to allow us to compete very successfully in the export market.'
