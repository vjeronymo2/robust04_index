December 28, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: NEW PIE CRUST RECIPE TAKES CARE OF PROBLEM

DEAR JEANNE: I have a recipe for a perfect pie crust. It makes several crusts and can be frozen. Is there any way to lower the fat and cholesterol but still keep the same idea? I don't make pies that often so I would settle for just some improvement in the recipe. Would you be interested in trying it? -- P.J., Downers Grove, Ill.

PERFECT PIE CRUST

4 cups flour

1 tablespoon sugar

2 teaspoons salt

1 3/4 cups shortening

1/2 cup water

1 tablespoon vinegar

1 egg

Place flour, sugar and salt in large bowl and mix well with fork. Add shortening and mix with fork until ingredients are crumbly.

Beat together water, vinegar and egg in small bowl. Add to flour mixture, stirring with fork until all ingredients are moistened.

Divide dough in 5 portions. Shape each portion with hands into flat round patty ready for rolling. Wrap each in plastic or wax paper and chill at least 1/2 hour.

When ready to roll pie crust, lightly flour both sides of patty. Place on lightly floured board or pastry cloth. Cover rolling pin with stocking and rub in small amount of flour. Keeping pastry round, roll from center to 1/8-inch thickness and 2 inches larger than inverted pie pan.

Fold in halves or quarters. Transfer to pie pan, unfold and fit loosely in pan. Press with fingers to remove air pockets. Trim overhanging edge of pastry about 3/4 inch from rim of pan, fold under and flute pastry with fingers or press firmly around edge of pan with tines of fork, dipped in flour occasionally to prevent sticking.

Fill and bake as desired in individual recipe. (For baked pie shell, pierce bottom and sides of pastry with tines of table fork. Bake on rack in center of oven at 450 degrees 12 to 15 minutes or until golden brown. Cool on cake rack, then fill as desired.)

DEAR P.J.: This pie crust recipe is indeed foolproof. Unfortunately, the ingredients that assure its flaky texture and ease of handling are also the very ones you want to reduce. After several attempts, I think I have come up with an acceptably flaky and lower-calorie and lower-cholesterol pie crust.

You will notice that I have reduced the yield from five crusts to two, so you don't have to make so much of it at any one time. Also, my revised crust should not be handled more than is necessary or it will become tough. In this regard it is not quite as perfect as the original, yet it does solve your problem perfectly. -- JEANNE

PIE CRUST

2 cups unbleached flour

1 1/2 teaspoons sugar

1/2 teaspoon salt

1/2 cup cold corn-oil margarine

1 1/2 teaspoons vinegar

1 egg white

2 tablespoons ice water

Place flour, sugar and salt in large bowl and mix well with table fork. Add margarine and mix with fork until ingredients are crumbly.

Beat together vinegar and egg white with fork in small bowl. Add to flour mixture and stir until all ingredients are moistened. Add water, 1 tablespoon at time, stirring until dough forms pieces size of dimes.

Divide dough into 2 portions and shape each portion with hands into flat round patty ready for rolling. Wrap each in plastic or wax paper and chill at least 1/2 hour before rolling.

When ready to roll pie crust, lightly flour both sides of patty. Place on lightly floured board or pastry cloth. Cover rolling pin with stocking and rub in small amount of flour. Keeping pastry round, roll from center to 1/8-inch thickness and 2 inches larger than inverted pie pan.

Fold in halves or quarters. Transfer to pie pan, unfold and fit loosely in pan. Press with fingers to remove air pockets. Trim overhanging edge of pastry about 3/4 inch from rim of pan, fold under and flute pastry with fingers or press firmly around edge of pan with tines of fork, dipped in flour occasionally to prevent sticking.

For baked pie shell, pierce bottom and sides of pastry with tines of table fork. Bake on rack in center of oven at 450 degrees 10 to 12 minutes or until golden brown. Cool on cake rack, then fill as desired.
