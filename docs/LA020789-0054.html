February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE: SANDERS SHOULD JOIN THE NFL, FATHER SAYS

The father of Barry Sanders, Oklahoma State's Heisman Trophy winner, said that his son should forgo his senior year at Oklahoma State, which is under National Collegiate Athletic Assn. sanction, and try his luck in the National Football League.

"If he won the Heisman Trophy again this year, he couldn't get any more money or market himself any more than they are already," William Sanders said.

Barry Sanders said in early January, when a four-year probation for Oklahoma State's football program was announced, that he would return for his senior season with the Cowboys.

On Sunday, Sanders said he has made a decision but wouldn't say what it is.

The Dallas Morning News reported Sunday, however, that Sanders, a junior, told one NFL team that he will definitely turn professional. Since Sanders has never redshirted, he would not be eligible for the regular NFL draft this spring unless he decides to challenge it in the courts. He could, however, apply for the summer supplemental draft.
