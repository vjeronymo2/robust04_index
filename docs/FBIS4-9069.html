BFN [Text] Tirana, April 19 (ATA) -- Through a letter of April 18, 1994, Ronaldo Sardenberg, chairman of the Security Council Committee created by the 724 Resolution on the former Yugoslavia (Serbia and Montenegro), expressed its thanks to the Albanian Government with regard to its stands towards violations of the embargo by other ships, which have been detained and investigated by Albanian authorities. The committee expresses in particular its appreciation for the good cooperation of the Albanian Government with the embargo assistance mission in Albania. The letter says that the investigations of the Albanian Government into the case of the "Vela Luka" ship and other cases have been considered by the committee as ample facts proving the violation of the embargo by foreign ships. The committee has been informed by Albanian authorities for the "Valio" ship with 1,200 tons of gasoline, which tried to enter Yugoslavia, thus violating the embargo.
