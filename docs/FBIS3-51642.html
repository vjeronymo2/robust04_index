Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The Congress for National Salvation decided to form a government of public security. The participants have gone to the Legislative Palace to meet with the president of the National Assembly for formal acknowledgement of this three-member government. The following is a recorded report by Jean Luckenson. [Begin recording in Creole] A government of public security was established on Thursday, 20 January at 1600 as the result of a resolution by the Congress for National Salvation, which held a conference at the National Theater from 18 to 20 January. It was organized by the Great North citizens group. Following a series of workshops, three people were chosen to lead this government: Raoul Kenol, a lawyer representing the Artibonite Department; Charles Plaisimond, an industrialist representing the Department of the West; and Louis Toussaint, judge at the Court of Appeals in Cap-Haitien. [end recording]
