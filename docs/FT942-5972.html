940602 FT 02 JUN 94 / Top-level editorial shuffle at European Mr Charles Garside, former editor of The European, is about to return to the paper in the new post of editor-in-chief. Mr Garside's appointment was announced last night as it was revealed that Mr Greg MacLeod, chief executive, had left. Mr Herbert Pearson, editor, will switch to the new role of associate editor. Mr Garside resigned eight months ago as editor of the weekly title, which was founded by Robert Maxwell and is now owned by the Barclay brothers, who live in Monte Carlo and whose interests range from hotels to shipping. Mr Garside was said to have been forced out in a dispute with Mr MacLeod. Mr MacLeod was originally brought in by Mr Garside from administrators Arthur Andersen to manage the paper after the collapse of the Maxwell empire at the end of 1991. Mr Garside last night said: 'Mr MacLeod has been responsible for implementing an efficient commercial system and preparing the way for the next stage of development of the paper.'
