Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Boris Krivoshey] [Text] Moscow 24 Mar -- The issue of a possible reduction in the number of Russian troops on the South Kuril islands and their modernization has nothing to do with the problem of the peace treaty between Russia and Japan, Grigoriy Karasin, the director of the Russian Foreign Ministry's press and information department said at a briefing today. He said that during talks at different levels between Russia and Japan, Japan had more than once posed the question of the withdrawal of Russian Armed Forces from the South Kuril islands. In answer to this we have always made it quite clear that the issue of the stationing of Russia's Armed Forces on Russian territory is our country's internal affair only and cannot be a subject for discussion at the talks with Japan, Karasin stressed.
