Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Ivan Ivanov and Gennadiy Talalayev] [Text] Moscow January 13 ITAR-TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin and U.S. Presiednt Bill Clinton after walking between buildings in the Kremlin during a short break in their discussions, returned to St. Catherine's Hall of the Grand Kremlin Palace where they were joined by other members of the two delegations for further discussions. In opening the second session Boris Yeltsin expressed satisfaction with the first round of talks. "We discussed the situation in Russia after the elections to the Federal Assembly and the referendum on the new Constitution, we touched upon the political and economic developments and examined the situation in the United States, as well as a number of questions relating to bilateral relations", Yeltsin said. The Russian delegation taking part in the second round of discussions includes Prime Minister Chernomyrdin, Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, Finance Minister Boris Fedorov, and National Security Council Secretary Oleg Lobov. This points to the likely subjects of the discussion -- Russian-American economic cooperation and assistance to Russia's reforms. The economic and political situation and security in the Commonwealth of Independent States amd Europe are also likely to be discussed. Bill Clinton is expected to dwell on the Partnership for Peace programme and to summarise the results of the NATO summit in Brussels.
