Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 21 (CNS)--China's Ministry of State Security recently made an announcement on the use of certificates of identity for investigators. The announcement claimed that in line with relevant regulations of "the State Security Law of the People's Republic of China", officials of state security organs in the country, when on duty, will be obliged to carry a special certificate of identity issued by the Ministry of State Security of the People's Republic of China conferred and signed by the ministry for use nationwide. According to the announcement, the cover of the certificate is red and carries such words as "Detective Certificate of the Ministry of State Security of the People's Republic of China" with an emblem consisting of two shields, five stars, the words "State Security" and a short sword. The inside contents of the certificate show the holder's photo, name, sex, unit, position, code number, the name of the institution to which the bearer belongs as well as the functions and rights to be given to the him or her. The announcement requires all individuals and organizations to actively offer every aid to state security organ officials when they are on duty with their certificate, carrying out tasks according to the law and nobody may hinder or hold back the work of these officials under any pretext with those found in violation of the law being dealt with according to the law.
