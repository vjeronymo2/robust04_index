BFN [Text] Beijing, May 30 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Premier Li Peng and Malaysian Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamed have expressed a strong desire to improve bilateral trade and economic cooperation. The two prime ministers said this in a recent exchange of letters to mark the 20th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations by China and Malaysia, Foreign Ministry sources said today. Premier Li said that the cooperation between China and Malaysia had made rapid progress on the basis of the five principles of coexistence since the establishment of diplomatic ties on May 31, 1974. Li said that Mahathir's three visits to China in recent years had served to promote bilateral understanding and mutual trust, and that the good relations between the two countries would benefit their peoples and be conducive to promoting regional peace, stability and prosperity. Meanwhile, Prime Minister Mahathir said in his letter that the development of the good relations between Malaysia and China was due to the Chinese leaders being pragmatic and favoring openness. He said that China's policy of good neighborliness had allowed bilateral relations to develop rapidly. He also expressed the belief that the two countries would widen their cooperation not only in the fields of the economy, trade and investment but also in tourism, cultural exchange and people-to-people contact. Chinese Foreign Minister Qian Qichen and his Malaysian counterpart Abdullah Ahmad Badawi also exchanged letters marking the 20th anniversary of the opening of formal relations.
