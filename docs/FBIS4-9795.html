BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] The Baja California State Congress has created a special commission to help investigate the assassination of Luis Donaldo Colosio Murrieta, former PRI [Institutional Revolutionary Party] presidential candidate. The commission will continue to gather any new evidence Baja California residents may present and send it to the attorney general of the republic. Francisco Javier Reynoso Nino, Baja California Congress secretary, has made this announcement in Mexicali. [Begin Reynoso recording] We have started direct relations with the two chambers of Congress, the chambers of deputies and senators. Those contacts will, of course, be in both directions and directed to further the investigations. We know, of course, Congress is not an office to investigate crimes, but we will be attentive to any information that becomes available and we will immediately report it to the corresponding authorities. [end recording] Reynoso also said that although the Baja California State Congress is not an investigative office, it can volunteer available information to further the Special Prosecutor's Office's investigation of the criminal case. He added the Baja California Congress is demanding Colosio's murder be clarified to the satisfaction of all the people.
