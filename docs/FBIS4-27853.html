BFN [AFP, YONHAP From Vienna] [Text] According to diplomats in Vienna, the Board of Governors of the International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] has convened a special meeting to discuss a draft declaration urging North Korea to accept all inspections in relation to nuclear weapons development. The diplomats said that the meeting, which was held a day before the official Board of Governors meeting scheduled for 5 June, reviewed a draft declaration denouncing North Korea for violating the inspections provisions of the Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty [NPT] by removing fuel rods from the five-megawatt experimental reactor without IAEA supervision. They said that the declaration urges North Korea to fulfill the obligations of the NPT and that the declaration will be announced as an official result of the Board of Governors meeting or a president's statement. The IAEA Board of Governors is expected to discuss the North Korean issue on 9 or 10 June when the UN Security Council makes a decision on sanctions against North Korea.
