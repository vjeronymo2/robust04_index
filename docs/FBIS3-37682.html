Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Athens Russia's Foreign Minister, Andrey Kozyrev, has suggested that an international conference on Bosnia be held some time next week. He said the call was for a conference on Bosnia and it came from President Boris Yeltsin. The United States, German, British, French, and Greek Governments had already been notified. Kozyrev made the statement after he met with his Greek, Belgian, and German counterparts in Athens. Speaking with newsmen in the Greek capital, he stated Yeltsin's call for the Serbs to remove their heavy weapons and the Serbs' willingness to meet this call created favorable conditions for a peace settlement. The minister said Russian peacekeepers were ready to be deployed in Sarajevo and other areas of Bosnia-Herzegovina. A spokesman for the United Nations has told the French news agency 400 troops of the Russian battalion based in eastern Croatia have started moving toward Sarajevo.
