Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tanegashima, Kagoshima Pref., Feb. 4 KYODO -- Japan's Space Agency on Friday [4 February] launched the country's first fully domestically developed rocket from its space center in southern Japan. The H-2 series rocket lifted off from the National Space Development Agency's Tanegashima Space Center at 7:20 A.M. [2220 GMT]. The launching had originally been set for Tuesday but was postponed until Friday because of bad weather and a faulty air duct that came loose.
