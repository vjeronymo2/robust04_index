BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Yuriy Tyssovskiy] [Text] Moscow June 6 TASS -- Relations between Cyprus and Russia were described as spiritual, as rooted in history, by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and head of the Foreign Ministry of the Republic of Cyprus Alexandros Mikhailidhis at a news conference in Moscow on Monday following the negotiations between the two delegations. The foreign ministers pointed out the warmth of relations between the two countries. Mikhailidhis is on an official visit in Russia. Kozyrev pointed out the importance of contacts with representatives of Cyprus, a Mediterranean country, where Russia has "economic and political interests". Russia is interested in an earlier settlement of the Cyprus problem on a basis of the United Nations resolutions with taking into account territorial integrity and sovereignty of Cyprus, Kozyrev said. He said that new approaches to the situation in Cyprus and in the region, especially after the recent report on Cyprus by U.N. Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali had been discussed during the negotiations. "We have coordinated plans for further steps with taking into account the role of Russia, permanent member of the U.N. Security Council", the Russian ministry said. There was also exchange of opinions on interaction of the two states in the framework of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe and the United Nations where the stances of the parties coincide. Kozyrev noted that the visit by the Cyprus foreign minister will promote further development of economic, tourist and other ties which will help speed up the working out of an agreement of visa-free visits by citizens of the two countries. In his turn Mikhailidhis pointed to an important stage of the settlement of the Cyprus problem that resulted from the occupation of the northern part of the insular state over 20 years [ago]. He described the report by Butrus-Ghali as a manifestation of concern of the world community over the lack of progress in the Cyprus settlement because of the lack of political will of the Turkish side. He said the document is of huge importance for Cyprus as the 35-thousand strong Turkish military corps with heavy arms is still in the north of the island and 90 thousand Turkish settlers live there, which interferes with the demographic structure of the Cyprus population. "This is the essence of the Cyprus problem", he said. "The dialogue with Russia will continue", Mikhailidhis said. "We rely on friendship with Russia, noting specifically Russia's important role as permanent member of the United Nations Security Council and the experience of Russian diplomats in the Cyprus problem. Cyprus strives for closer cooperation with Russia in every area, also through CSCE, the United Nations and soon, I believe, in the Council of Europe", Mikhailidhis said. The ministers signed a protocol on mutual consultations between the foreign ministries of the two countries. The Cyprus minister meets on Monday with leaders of the State Duma and with Patriarch Aleksiy the Second of Moscow and All Russia.
