931203 FT 03 DEC 93 / Sinn Fein claims information received from British government Sinn Fein, the political wing of the IRA, yesterday claimed it had received highly sensitive information from the British government on its stance towards the terrorist organisation. Mr Martin McGuinness, a Sinn Fein official closely involved in talks with the Britain, said he had been told of a disagreement between Sir Patrick Mayhew, the Northern Ireland secretary, and Mr Kenneth Clarke, the chancellor, over how to deal with Sinn Fein. The differences allegedly surfaced at a meeting chaired by Mr Major in May. Downing Street said the allegations were 'inaccurate', but did not deny such a meeting took place. Sinn Fein also said it had copies of a Northern Ireland office briefing paper on talks last year with Ulster's democratic parties.
