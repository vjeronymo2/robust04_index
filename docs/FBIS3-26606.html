Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Macao, February 9 (XINHUA) -- The maintenance of Macao's stable development and smooth transition conforms to the interests of China, Portugal and local people, Guo Dongpo said in his Spring Festival message here today. Guo, director of the XINHUA NEWS AGENCY Macao branch, said that "In the coming new year, we hope to continue friendly cooperation with Portugal Government in firmly implementing the Sino-Portuguese joint declaration in convergence with the basic law of Macao Special Administrative Region (SAR), in a bid to properly deal with a series of important issues in the transitional period." He pointed out, these issues include the three major questions, namely localization of public servants and law and officialization of the Chinese language. In handling some key issues, Guo noted, "We hope to, together with Portugal side, seek proper ways to work out solutions on the basis of mutual respect and mutual understanding." He added that this would also be done in line with the spirit of friendly relationship and cooperation between the governments of China and Portugal, and in the view of Macao's long-term interest. Guo said, last year, China reported marked progress in a series of reforms and overall economic development and the living standards of the Chinese people has been improved greatly. China's position in the world political structure has become more and more important, he said. Guo said, 1993 was also an important year for Macao. In March of the year the Eighth National People's Congress adopted the basic law of Macao SAR which afforded essential guarantee for the smooth transition and long-term stability in the area. He commented that as a result of friendly cooperation between the governments of China and Portugal, all matters concerning the transition in Macao have been going on smoothly. Macao compatriots and people from all walks of life are confident about the policy of "one country, two systems" and the future of Macao, he said. He expressed the hope that Macao compatriots and people from all walks of life continue to actively participate in various matters in the transitional period and contribute more to the prosperity of Macao.
