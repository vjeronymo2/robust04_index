BFN [Text] President Fidel V. Ramos and visiting Australian Foreign Minister Gareth Evans agreed on Monday to further strengthen the existing harmonious relations between their respective countries [word indistinct] trade and investment. The president and Minister Evans made the commitment during their 30-minute meeting in Malacanang [presidential office], which was also attended by Foreign Affairs Secretary Roberto Romulo, Presidential Assistant for Mindanao Paul Dominguez and Australian Ambassador Richard Smith. Minister Evans assured the president of the Australian Government's continuing support to his administration as well as his personal commitment to help the Philippines obtain progress and development. Australia is the third largest economy in Asia and the 14th in the world, with 60 percent of its exports now going to the East Asian region. Minister Evans told the president he visited several investment centers including Subic Bay Free Port in Zambales and Davao City and General Santos City in Mindanao. Minister Evans visited Mindanao last 10 to 11 June with members of his official delegation to explore possibilities of linking Australia's northern territory with Mindanao. He informed the president of Australia's keen interest in the East ASEAN Growth Area or EAGA concept of economically grouping Brunei Darussalam, Indonesia, Malaysia and the Philippines.
