March 23, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

JUDGE AWAITS VERDICT ON OSCAR TICKETS

Call it the Case of the Disappointed Jurist. Or, the Case of the Confusing Telephone Calls. Either way, here it is just six days to Oscar, and one of the most famous judges in America -- "People's Court" Judge Joseph Wapner -- can't seem to get in on the show.

All the retired Superior Court judge -- whose show is the basis of a running gag in the front runner "Rain Man" -- wanted, it seems, was some tickets to Wednesday's show. But, as is often the case of getting on the A list in Hollywood, it's not a case of who you know, but who knows you.

When Oscars show producer Allan Carr was told earlier this week that he had a call from a representative of Judge Wapner, Carr, who says he doesn't watch much daytime TV, thought he had a legal problem on his hands.

"I asked, 'Why, are one of my couples having matrimonial problems?' I thought he wanted to be a consultant in case of a domestic tiff over the weekend," Carr said Wednesday.

But there was some confusion in Carr's office. An assistant to the producer was told that Wapner had been so taken by Dustin Hoffman's repeated references to him in "Rain Man," that he thought the Oscars might want him around to perform in the event. Carr politely declined.

"My case rests on Judge Wapner," Carr said. "It's plain and simple. I don't think he's a member of the Screen Actors Guild. I don't think he's been nominated for anything."

But Eddy Jo Bernal, a publicist for Wapner's program, said that the judge merely wanted tickets to attend the Oscars show -- not be in it. He had mentioned it to a Warner Bros. representative at a recent television convention.

"Somebody at Warner misinterpreted and went to Carr and asked if he could be on the show," Bernal said. "That's not what the judge wanted at all. He's not a star, he's a judge.

"He just wanted to be in the audience and he admires Dustin Hoffman's work."

As of Wednesday, Wapner still hadn't made the A list.
