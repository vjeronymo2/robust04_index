BFN [Text] Port-au-Prince, 15 Jun (AFP) -- Lieutenant Colonel Joseph Michel Francois, chief of police in the Haitian capital, confirmed last night his loyalty and support for Army Commander General Raoul Cedras. The chief of police also issued a communique condemning the position of his brother Evans, who, yesterday in Santo Domingo, demanded the resignation of Gen. Cedras to avoid a U.S. invasion of Haiti. Evans Francois, a former diplomat who claims to be one of the masterminds of the 30 September 1991 coup d'etat against President Jean-Bertrand Aristide, also said he fears for the life of his brother.
