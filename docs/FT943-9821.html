940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / Observer: Dirty linen In a burst of would-be efficiency, the Bank of England has for the first time in its history put the building's cleaning contract out to tender. None of the English Mrs Mopses being deemed suitable, the job of dusting down the gold bullion has been farmed out to Denmark's International Service Systems. If this welcome trend continues, perhaps De La Rue will be allowed to have a go at printing Britain's bank notes.
