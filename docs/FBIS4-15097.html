BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow April 6 TASS -- Russian Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin and a delegation of U.S. congressmen discussed a broad range of issues of Russian-American relations at a meeting on Wednesday, above all in the economic sphere. Valeriy Grishin, deputy head of the Russian Government's press service, told journalists after the meeting that congressmen expressed support for reforms conducted by the present Russian Government. The sides also discussed how to settle the conflict in former Yugoslavia and the participation of Russia and the United States in peace efforts. Congressmen expressed satisfaction with rates of economic reforms in Russia. For instance, Republican minority leader Robert Michael said that he plans to make a statement, after his return to the U.S., on the need for more active participation of U.S. businessmen in Russian investment projects. Chernomyrdin said during the discussion of the recent decision by the International Monetary Fund to appropriate the second part of the systematic credit to Russia that the Russian Government did not and does not bank on western aid and intends to earn money on the world market on its own. In this connection, the premier pointed to the need for lifting unjustified Western restrictions containing Russia's access to world markets.
