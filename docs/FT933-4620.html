930908 FT 08 SEP 93 / Observer: Splinter group The young people who occupy British academic towns in the summer in order to learn English are now homeward bound. As the season draws to a close, the EFL Gazette, a teachers' newspaper, announces the winner of its Last of the Summer Whine Award. It goes to the student making the most original complaint about his or her stay in the UK. The winner this year, a Swede, sets new surreal standards. She asked to be moved from a Bristol family because they gave her a wardrobe that smelled of wood.
