September 27, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

2 SOUTHERN BANKS PLAN $4.7-BILLION MERGER

Citizens & Southern Corp. and Sovran Financial Corp. announced a $4.7-billion merger Tuesday that will create the nation's 14th-largest banking company.

C&S, based in Atlanta, and Sovran, based in Norfolk, Va., will have total assets of $47 billion and almost 1,000 offices in seven states from Maryland to Florida. Under a newly formed holding company called Avantor Financial Corp., the two banks plan to maintain dual headquarters and retain current management.

The merger comes five months after C&S fought off a takeover bid by North Carolina's NCNB Corp. Unlike that proposal, the C&S-Sovran deal is a friendly merger.
