Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] President Francois Mitterrand has made an appeal for the social integration of the underprivileged urban population. The French president, who was in Meyzieu, near Lyon, to open a new town hall, said that this is now a major obligation: [Begin Mitterrand recording] Some people are suffering a great deal. Let us beware of deep-seated revolt, once reason runs out of arguments. In these suburbs, I see an improvement. But I see so many employees in different fields living in worry, anguish, and perhaps tomorrow, revolt. I want to tell you just one thing: Act, act with an idea of what the town should be, act with the feeling that if we do not solve this problem, we will be passing on to our children in the 21 century a destiny which will be much the same as that of so many towns around the world. [end recording]
