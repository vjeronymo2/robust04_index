Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Report by Miladin Manojlovic] [Text] According to unofficial results of yesterday's elections, Milan Martic has convincingly won the second round of elections for president of the republic of Serbian Krajina in Slavonia, Baranja, and Western Srem. Miladin Manojlovic reports from Beli Manastir: [Manojlovic] In Slavonia, Baranja, and Western Srem, Milan Martic won 7,070 votes more than Milan Babic. The Vukovar electoral unit voters, as in the first round of voting, supported Milan Martic the most. He received 15,715 votes and Milan Babic received 9,903 votes. Martic won with almost 6,000 votes more. In other electoral units, the differences are not so great, only a few hundred votes more in favor of Martic, except in Dalj, where Milan Babic won. Milan Martic won in Beli Manastir, Mirkovci, and Tenja. Martic won 651 votes more in Beli Manastir, 271 votes more in Mirkovci, and 403 votes more in Tenja. Milan Babic won 66 votes more in Dalj. The elections in this region went without any objections being raised. The results were verified by observers for both presidential candidates. A turnout of over 60 percent, despite bad weather, can be considered high and seen as an expression of the citizens' will, who confirmed once again the existence and resoluteness of the republic of Serbian Krajina.
