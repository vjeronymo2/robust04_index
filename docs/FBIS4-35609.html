BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 May (MENA)--Tunisian Prime Minister Dr. Hamed Karoui arrived in Cairo today on a two-day official visit to Egypt. On his arrival, Karoui stated that he was carrying a message from Tunisian President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali to President Muhammad Husni Mubarak. The message is part of the continuous consultations between the leaders on bilateral relations, Arab and Middle East issues, and the African summit, which is scheduled to convene in Tunis in June. Karoui said he will inform President Mubarak of the Tunisian president's views and will hear President Mubarak's views on African and international issues. Answering a question on the situation in Yemen, he said that developments there are regrettable. He added: We were hoping to put the Arab house in order, but we haven't lost hope yet. We would like the United Nations to resolve this issue despite the fact that there are numerous issues that the United Nations has not been able to resolve. We hope the brothers in Yemen will manage to find a way to unite once again. Karoui refused to comment on the secession of south Yemen. He expressed his hope that the Yemenis will find a solution amongst themselves. The Tunisian prime minister concluded his statement by saying that his country will take part in the conference of the Nonaligned countries' foreign ministers scheduled to convene in Cairo on Tuesday [31 May]. Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa greeted Karoui upon his arrival.
