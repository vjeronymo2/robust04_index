BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksey Kravchenko] [Text] Mexico City June 28 TASS -- The National Bank for Foreign Trade of Mexico and the Russian foreign trade amalgamation "Energomasheksport" here on Monday signed an agreement on opening a credit line for Russian enterprises and companies for acquiring goods in this Latin American country. The document envisages the possibility of receiving credits for exports to Russia goods worth 2 million U.S. dollars. The sides can increase the financial support up to 10 million dollars, according to their mutual understanding. Now Russian firms, which wish to make use of credit lines for imports of Mexican agricultural, consumer and industrial goods, will have to approach the "Energomasheksport". This agreement creates an important precedent in bilateral ties, Anatoliy Yevdakomov, adviser to the Russian trade representation, told ITAR-TASS. Earlier this practice was used exclusively by state establishments of the former Soviet Union. Now the Mexican side is for the first time using this mutually beneficial financial mechanism for the development of economic exchange with Russia, he said.
