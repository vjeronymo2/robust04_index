BFN [Text] Iraq was officially informed today of the Lebanese Government's decision to sever diplomatic relations between the two countries and of the order to the accused diplomats to leave Lebanese soil within 72 hours. More details from this dispatch by Khalil Fulayhan, the NATIONAL NEWS AGENCY correspondent, from the Foreign Ministry: [Begin recording] [Fulayhan] Ambassador Zafir al-Hasan, the Foreign Ministry's secretary general, summoned Iraqi Charge d'Affaires 'Awad Fakhri and informed him of yesterday's cabinet decision to sever diplomatic relations between the two countries and to close the Iraqi Embassy in al-Rayhaniyah neighborhood of al-Hazimiyah. He also asked him and the other members of the diplomatic mission to leave Lebanon within 72 hours. Following the meeting, which lasted 75 minutes, the Iraqi diplomat said: [Fakhri] We have been informed of the cabinet decision. We also conveyed our Foreign Ministry's stand that we are committed to all international agreements and charters, especially those concluded between us and Lebanon. [Unidentified correspondent] Can you tell us when you will leave? [Fakhri] The statement was clear--by Thursday, within 72 hours. [Correspondent] Starting today or yesterday? [Fakhri] What difference does it make? [Correspondent] [question indistinct]? [Fakhri] The Foreign Ministry will make the announcement [words indistinct]. [Correspondent] Who will look after Iraqi interests in Lebanon? [Fakhri] The Lebanese people will take care of our interests in Lebanon. [end recording]
