931123 FT 23 NOV 93 / International Company News: Price Waterhouse fee income dips Price Waterhouse, the accountancy firm, yesterday reported fee income across its European practices down 8 per cent to SFr2bn (Dollars 1.3bn) in the year to June 30. In the UK, its biggest practice, fee income fell 3 per cent to Pounds 383.3m (Dollars 571m) during the period. This included a 14 per cent decline in management consultancy to Pounds 83.9m, and a 5 per cent decline in corporate finance and recovery work to Pounds 53.2m. Audit and business advisory services in the UK grew by 1 per cent to Pounds 137.4m, and tax consultancy by 4 per cent to Pounds 108.8m. At 1992-93 exchange rates, the firm said its fee income across Europe was unchanged. At these rates, management consultancy billings fell 19 per cent to SFr306m. Tax consultancy grew 7 per cent to SFr513m, audit and business advisory by 3 per cent to SFr1.02bn and corporate finance and recovery by 1 per cent to SFr159m. Mr Jermyn Brooks, chairman of PW Europe, said demand for services had been effected by the recession, and that steps had been made to restructure and rationalise practices. In common with other firms, Price Waterhouse refused to provide any information on profits.
