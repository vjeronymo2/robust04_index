Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] To improve the system of the state and executive branch institutions, to eliminate duplication of their activities, to elevate the role and responsibility of the state administration while implementing legal acts and carrying out economic reforms, and to reduce expenditures for its upkeep, the Ukrainian president decided the following: The Ukrainian Cabinet of Ministers shall examine within two weeks a list of ministries, state committees, and other institutions of the legislative and executive branches, and will submit its proposals regarding their reduction and the implementing of other kinds of structural changes. The draft state budget for 1994 shall also reflect a reduction of expenditures for central and local institutions of the legislative and executive branches based on 30 percent cutbacks in the central administration personnel and 20 percent in the local administration personnel. The Crimean Supreme Soviet and local councils shall be advised to undertake relevant measures with regard to governing structures, the streamlining of personnel in regional and local self-governments and their executive committees at an arbitrary scale as of 1 January, 1992 [as heard].
