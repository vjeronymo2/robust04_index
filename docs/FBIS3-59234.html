Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Istanbul, Jan 27 (AA) -- The prime ministers of Turkey and Pakistan on Thursday [27 January] agreed to continue their preparations for a planned visit to the former Yugoslav Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina. Prime Minister Tansu Ciller and her Pakistani counterpart Benazir Bhutto held a brief meeting on Thursday in Istanbul's Ataturk Airport, where the latter stopped over on her way to Davos, Switzerland where she will attend the World Economic Forum. Ciller told reporters after her two separate meetings with Bhutto and Lebanese Premier Rafik Hariri, Turkey and Pakistan agreed that if the right circumstances are created preparations will continue for the Bosnia visit. She added that both prime ministers are willing to go to the former Yugoslav republic, and the issue will be finalized in the coming days.
