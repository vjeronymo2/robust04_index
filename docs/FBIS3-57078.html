Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Interview with Issa Kostoyev, chairman of the Federation Council Committee for Legislation and Judicial and Legal Reforms, by Yevgeniy Skukin; place and date not given: "Laws Must Operate With Precision"] [Text] I tracked down Issa Kostoyev, newly elected chairman of the Federation Council Committee for Legislation and Judicial and Legal Reforms, who is chief of the Investigations Department of the General Prosecutor's Office and a state counsellor third class, during a break in proceedings. (For a while last year he was the Russian president's representative in the Ossetian-Ingush conflict zone.) [Skukin] Do you intend to return to the Ossetian-Ingush problem -- this time in your new capacity? [Kostoyev] It will be time to extend the president's edict on the state of emergency in the conflict zone in a few days' time, and I, as chairman of the Federation Council Committe for Legislation and Judicial and Legal Reforms, will insist that a group of deputies travel to the trouble spot first. [Skukin] Issa Magametovich, what matters within your area of competence are truly urgent? [Kostoyev] It is essential to establish which power structures are to deal with what. The introduction of trial by jury has revealed Russia's all-around lack of preparation for civilized legal proceedings. Our laws are often ill-drafted and contradictory. A huge army of investigators from the former Security Ministry has been jettisoned, but not every lawyer is capable of being an investigator. These people won't be wasted; they are professionals, and they are going into commercial structures. Investigation, however, will suffer: Only 40 percent of crimes are being detected now. [Skukin] What will you be seeking to achieve when you start your new job? [Kotsoyev] I will be seeking firmly and persistently to ensure that when we draft Russian laws we include within them a mechanism for their implementation. Laws must operate with clockwork precision.
