BFN [Text] Tehran, April 19, IRNA -- Iranian and French foreign ministers in a telephone conversation Tuesday reviewed the latest developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina and their need for countering the Serbian crimes there. Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati referred to the tragic situation in Gorazde and voiced concern over the massacre of innocent Muslims in the city. Velayati underscored the need for taking an immediate and serious measure by international communities and United Nations Security Council in this respect, and announced Iran's readiness for any cooperation for ending the current catastrophe in Gorazde. French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe in reply pointed to the initiatives taken by his country for the restoration of security in Bosnia, and stressed the need for continuation of mutual contacts on the issue.
