Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, held talks in Islamabad today with some of the leaders of Afghan groups on the latest situation in Afghanistan. During the talks, he offered that Pakistan will be ready to host a regional peace conference to ensure peace and stability in Afghanistan. Later, talking to newsmen, the foreign minister said this conference will be possible if all the Afghan sides agree to a cease-fire. He said Pakistan has heard the point of view of both sides in the conflict in Kabul and told them that Pakistan has no favorites and it stands with the Afghan nation. At this stage, if all Afghan parties need Pakistan's help for reconciliation, it is available. The Afghan prime minister, Mr. Golboddin Hekmatyar, has welcomed Pakistan's proposal to convene a regional peace conference. In Afghanistan itself, there are reports of sporadic fighting between the warring factions in Kabul. Both sides used artillery and rocket fire on each other's positions in Pul-e Mahmoodkhan, Bala Hesar, and Microryan.
