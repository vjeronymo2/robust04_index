September 15, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

U.S. SEEKS SYRIA'S HELP IN MIDEAST

Re: President Bush's speech on the gulf crisis:

This is kinder and gentler? I will not dance to the beat of the (oil) drums of war.

A strong support of the blockade should have been sufficient; sending in troops was premature and overkill!

Bush's statement that we are in the Persian Gulf to "defend civilized values" is ludicrous. We all know why we are there. His vague references to conservation were laughable. But I don't think anyone missed his more serious intentions -- domestic drilling (watch out, California and Alaska), and a "sound" defense budget.

This crisis should force us to focus on our enormous consumption of oil and its alternative sources -- not war.

DEBORAH V. SUTTON

Van Nuys
