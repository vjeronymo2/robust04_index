BFN [Text] Shanghai, April 21 (XINHUA) -- Altogether 220 enterprises from 11 provinces and municipalities across the country have put assets up for sale at the Shanghai enterprise assets exchange, starting Wednesday [21 April] afternoon. "This marks the founding of China's largest market in property rights in this, China's leading industrial center," said Gong Jiemin, chairman of the board of directors of the exchange. Representatives of the first batch of 38 members, in red sleeveless jackets, are now busily operating an ast486-type micro-computer system, the set of the advanced equipment imported from the United States. According to Gong, the enterprises with assets for sale have a combined property value of about two billion yuan and produce more than 40 varieties of goods. About one third of them are profitable. Aiming at "high starting points, high standard and high efficiency" in operation, gong noted, the exchange will offer a centralized, fair, standard and orderly assets transfer service.
