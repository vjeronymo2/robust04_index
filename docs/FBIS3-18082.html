Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Nicosia, Feb 26 (CNA)--Cyprus condemned the massacre of Palestinians in Hebron on Friday [26 February] in which over 50 Arabs were killed. A statement, issued Friday by the Office of Government Spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis, said "we express our deep sorrow and sympathy with the relatives of the victims of the tragic incident and with the Palestinian people." "We hope that this condemnable act does not affect the course towards peace," it added. [Nicosia O AGON in Greek on 26 February carries on page 36 the following report: ["The police have taken emergency security measures around the Israeli Embassy building in Nicosia. These measures, taken following yesterday's massacre of Palestinians by an Israeli in the occupied town of Hebron, are designed to prevent possible retaliation by Palestinians who are in Cyprus. As is known, an Israeli man entered a mosque and killed 63 Palestinians during prayers."]
