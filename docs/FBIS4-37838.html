BFN [Text] Riyadh, 20 June, SPA -- The assistant secretary general for political affairs at the Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC], ambassador 'Abd-al-Aziz Bu'ali, has stressed the anxiousness of their majesties and highnesses the leaders of the GCC countries to back any joint Arab action and to sanction Arab unity in any part of the Arab homeland, proceeding from their belief that any cooperation gives strength to the Arab nation and consequently the Muslim nation. At a press conference held today at the GCC headquarters in Riyadh, he said: "When Yemen's unity was established in May 1990 the GCC countries supported and sanctioned this unity which came about with mutual consent". He also talked about the great efforts made by the GCC to stop the conflict between the brethren in Yemen and find a suitable ground to solve the conflict through negotiations and away from bloodshed, massacres and destruction. He reviewed the efforts made by the servant of the two holy places, King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz Al Sa'ud, His Highness Shaykh Zayid Bin-Sultan Al Nuhayyan, president of the United Arab Emirates, and their brothers the GCC leaders to contain this crisis. He then deplored the worsening of the conflict between the two sides which led to the destructive war going on in Yemen now. He stressed that the fundamental view of the GCC states now was to insist on a cease-fire. In reply to a question on the nature of measures which the GCC states could take if the fighting continued between the two sides, Ambassador Bu'ali said he hoped that fighting would end between the two sides, otherwise other steps would have to be taken either by the GCC states or the Security Council. He added that the goal which the GCC states were pursuing was to stop the bloodshed and the destruction. He further said: What matters to us now is to stop the fighting and prevent the flames of war from spreading here and there, and as for the rest -- everything will be examined accordingly and we will deal with the developments in the future. In reply to a question on the GCC center for commercial arbitration, he indicated that the center was set up to arbitrate disputes which might arise between a trader and companies or between other traders involved in similar commercial activities with the GCC states abroad [as received]. Finally, he reviewed the commercial projects which would be executed in the framework of cooperation among the GCC states.
