Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Hague, 24 March (AFP) -- Suriname rebels threatening to blow up a hydroelectric dam freed all their hostages unharmed overnight, the Dutch news agency ANP reported. The announcement came after the rebels freed four of their 30 hostages Wednesday. About 100 gunmen from a previously unknown Suriname Liberation Front stormed the dam site at Afaboka on Monday, demanding that President Ronald Venetiaan and his government resign and hand over to the national assembly.
