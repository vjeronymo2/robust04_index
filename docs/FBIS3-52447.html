Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Following yesterday's arrival by air in Jerusalem and initial talks with Israeli representatives, members of the Slovak delegation, headed by Foreign Minister Jozef Moravcik, continued their official visit to the Jewish state. Agata Stanekova has phoned in details from Jerusalem: [Begin Stanekova recording] Today was not ideal for seeing the sights of the city of Jerusalem -- the delegation's agenda for today. [passage omitted] In the afternoon, Jozef Moravcik and representatives of the parliament met the chairman of the Israeli Knesset. As Ivan Laluha said, cooperation between the parliaments will begin first of all on the level of foreign affairs committees. [passage omitted] In the Knesset building, Jozef Moravcik also met today with Israeli Prime Yitzhaq Rabin. According to Miroslav Lajcak, chief of the foreign minister's office, the talks were conducted in a businesslike atmosphere. At the beginning of the conversation, the Israeli prime minister briefed the Slovak side on the development of the Middle East talks, on Israel's economic situation, and also about the intentions to expand the country's diplomatic contacts. Jozef Moravcik stressed yet again the significance of Slovak contacts with Israel and emphasized the actual possibilities for mutually advantageous cooperation. Also taking part in the meeting were Economy Minister Jan Ducky who spoke about conversion of the arms industry in Slovakia and about the possibility of Israeli industry having a share in this sphere. Yitzhaq Rabin stressed the complicated nature of the conversion process and the need to restructure Slovak industry in connection with the development of world trade. In conclusion, Minister Moravcik conveyed to him an invitation from the Slovak prime minister. In the evening, the members of the delegation briefly met President 'Ezer Weizman. [end recording]
