Language: English Article Type:BFN [House-Searches and Maltreatments in the Ferizaj Commune -- ATA headline] [Text] Tirana, February 3 (ATA) -- The campaign of house-searches, maltreatment and questioning of parties's activists by the Serbian police continued also last week in the Ferizaj commune. Under the pretext of looking for arms, the Serbian police maltreated Shaip Avdullahu and his nephew, Aziz Arifi, from the Prelez village, though they had no arms. The Serbian police inspectors questioned the DLK [Democratic League of Kosova] activist, Mustafe Aliaj, asking him on how the DLK spends its money and if the DLK is buying arms. The DLK Information Commission in Mirash reported that Serbian police has recently searched 10 houses and confiscated the arms for which the owners had a permit. The Human Rights and Freedom Defence Commission in Ferizaj reported that a Serbian couple, Petar and Mira Gavriloviq, has broken into the house of Pajazit Gjoni although he has a contract for it since 1984.
