BFN [Text] Lima, 27 May (EFE) -- In Lima today the Cartagena Agreement Board announced that Colombia, Ecuador, and Venezuela have decided to implement the Andean Common External Tariff (AEC) as of 31 December 1994. Representatives of the three countries managed to overcome disagreements of a conceptual and technical nature and undertook serious commitments in this direction during the meeting held by the Andean Group Commission, the highest level organ of the Andean integration process, during the past four days in this capital. The commitment is contained in a decision draft, as the integration mandates are called, proposing a tariff structure and preferential treatment for Bolivia and Ecuador, as well as mechanisms for unifying in time the products that are still unable to fully adopt the AEC by 31 December 1994. The project also includes a system for the implementation of agreements reached by Colombia, Ecuador, and Venezuela in the automobile sector through a sectorial agreement, and the commitment to adopt specific variable rights for agricultural products affected by the variations in international prices. It also includes strict mechanisms to restore competitiveness when one of the member countries is affected by the application of tariffs other than the AEC after 31 December 1994, according to a press communique issued by the Cartagena Agreement Board. According to the communique, the decision draft implements a measure that consolidates the Customs Union [Union Aduanera] and establishes clear mechanisms regulating competition between the member countries. The Andean Group has established four tariff levels of 5, 10, 15, and 20 percent, depending on the merchandise involved. Peru is currently under a special regime within the Andean Group, approved in April, that allows it to temporarily regulate its trade in the region through bilateral agreements until the rest of the member countries gradually eliminate the protectionist tariffs they maintain in various economic fields. Peru, according to the mentioned system, continues as an observer at the meetings of the commission designed to discuss the AEC, and is authorized to maintain tariffs at 15 and 20 percent.
