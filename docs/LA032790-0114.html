March 27, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

$1-BILLION PENTAGON RENOVATION REJECTED

The chairman of a Senate panel on military construction said Monday that a Bush Administration request for $1.1 billion to renovate the Pentagon is "out of the question."

Sen. Jim Sasser (D-Tenn.) acknowledged that the 47-year-old building is in need of repair but said the Administration's call for $1.1 billion over seven years would be difficult to heed in a time of shrinking budgets.

"The military construction bill is going to get smaller, not larger," Sasser told Pentagon officials.
