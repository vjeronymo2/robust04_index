Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian economic developments monitored through 25 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. Although construction has been the leading economic sector in Colombia over the past few years, during the first nine months of 1993 construction throughout the country decreased by 26.67 percent compared to the same period in 1992. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL NUEVO SIGLO in Spanish 14 Jan 94 pp 1, 18) The National Administrative Department of Statistics, DANE, polled 7,000 homes in 148 rural municipalities in the Atlantic, eastern, central, and Pacific regions of the country, with the following results: rural unemployment increased by 0.5 percent between September 1992 and September 1993, going from 4.4 to 4.9 percent; the employed population in those areas decreased by 0.4 percent, and the work force increased by 2.3 percent. The highest levels of rural unemployment were found on the Atlantic coast at 5.5 percent; the Pacific came in second at 5 percent; the eastern sector was third with 4.7 percent, and the area with the lowest unemployment is central Colombia (no figure given). (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 15 Jan 94 p 1B) Colombia's coffee producers are defying international trade. Taking advantage of a shortage of Colombian coffee abroad, they have decided unilaterally to raise the grain's price artificially from 0.69 to 0.92 cents per pound as of 24 January. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1730 GMT 21 Jan 94) The Colombian Petroleum Enterprise has disclosed that thus far this year Colombia's economy has suffered a loss of $25,000 daily as a result of guerrilla dynamite attacks on the country's oil infrastructure. (Paris AFP in Spanish 0043 GMT 21 Jan 94) The Bank of the Republic announced on 22 January that the Colombian Government will no longer set the official exchange rate for the U.S. dollar. As of 24 January the sole index will be the Representative Market Exchange Rate, TRM. The bank will also suspend the issuance of exchange certificates, which will deprive exporters of exchange discounts on the income they receive from sales abroad. Finance Minister Rudolf Hommes said that although the TRM will be the sole index, the exchange rate will be based on supply and demand. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2048 GMT 22 Jan 94) Finance Minister Rudolf Hommes announced on 21 January that the national budget will be reduced by $462.2 million to avoid putting pressure on the exchange rate. He said the measure is also aimed at presenting a healthy budget to the administration that will take office in August. (Paris AFP in Spanish 0837 GMT 22 Jan 94) Santa Fe de Bogota newspaper EL TIEMPO published a report to the effect that the Colombian Government will receive approximately $1.5 billion for the privatization of the Bank of Colombia and the sale of the mobile cellular telephone system. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2248 GMT 24 Jan 94) It has just been announced that the 401 billion pesos the Colombian Government will receive on 31 January for the sale of the Bank of Colombia will be distributed as follows: 270 billion will go to the central administration; 68 billion to the Financial Institutions Guarantee Fund; and 63 billion will be reinvested in the Bank of Colombia. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 25 Jan 94)
