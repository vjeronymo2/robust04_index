930225 FT 25 FEB 93 / International Company News: Creditors approve plan to reorganise Continental CONTINENTAL Airlines, the fifth-largest US carrier, said yesterday that its creditors had backed the company's proposed reorganisation plan to allow it to emerge from the bankruptcy proceeding in which it has been involved since 1990. The plan to reorganise Continental's capital structure and significantly reduce its debt burden would be implemented in conjunction with an investment proposal, accepted last year, from Air Canada and Air Partners. Continental said that it would now present the final results of the creditor balloting to the bankruptcy court on March 16. It is expected to emerge from bankruptcy within a matter of weeks. USAir, the US carrier into which British Airways has invested Dollars 300m said yesterday that it had reached a tentative agreement with the city of Philadelphia over improvements to the city's airport facilities.
