Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Bogahev] [Text] Moscow January 14 TASS -- Strobe Talbott, assistant secretary of state of the United States, held a briefing at the American press centre in Hotel "Slavyanskaya" here in the early hours of January 14. He said that President Boris Yeltsin and President Bill Clinton had a meeting at Ogaryovo on January 13. The two presidents discussed, in particular, some economic problems of mutual concern, questions linked with the "Partnership for Peace" programme, which was recently approved by the NATO leaders in Brussels, relations between the executive and legislative branches of government in both countries, the role of the military in the political lives of countries, proposals to promote cooperation between the military establishments of Russia and the United States. The sides also touched on problems of coordinating the policies of the United States and Russia in the Middle East, on the situation in former Yugoslavia. President Yeltsin presented to Bill Clinton a porcelain statuette of the U.S. President.
