BFN [Text] Cairo, 31 Mar (MENA)--Responsible Palestinian sources have told MENA that the first group of Palestinian police will arrive in the Gaza Strip on Tuesday [5 April] in accordance with the agreement reached today during negotiations between the Palestinian and the Israeli delegations in Cairo. The same sources said that this group will be followed by a larger group on Thursday. The sources pointed out that during the talks held today a detailed agreement was reached on the agenda for the Gaza-Jericho negotiations, which will resume in Cairo on Sunday. [Word indistinct] the time schedule for the entry of the Palestinian police force to Gaza and Jericho. In another development, Dr. Nabil Sha'th, Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat's political adviser and head of the Palestinian negotiating team, stated that the implementation of all measures in the Declaration of Principles will be acted on quickly. MENA has learned that this evening Dr. Sha'th went to the Norwegian Embassy in Cairo and submitted an official request that international observers be sent to Hebron City to begin carrying out their duties there as soon as possible.
