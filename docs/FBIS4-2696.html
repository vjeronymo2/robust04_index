BFN [Text] Beijing, April 13 (AFP) -- The Chinese government officially confirmed Wednesday that dissident Xu Wenli, detained last Friday in Beijing, is free. "Xu Wenli has complete freedom of movement," a Public Security Ministry spokesman told AFP by telephone. The spokesman said "Xu has been free since Saturday after being held legally for 24 hours." But according to relatives, the dissident was only released Tuesday. Xu, 49, a leader of the 1978-79 Democracy Wall movement and one of China's longest serving political prisoners, had been detained as part of a police sweep of dissidents in Beijing and Shanghai that coincided with the visit of French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur.
