BFN [From the "Focus on Africa" program] [Text] The police strike in Lesotho is now into its third week and it is developing into something of a political crisis. Prime Minister Ntsu Mokhehle has accused the opposition Basotho National Party, the BNP, of being involved in the strike, which has spread to include junior prison wardens. Similar claims surfaced during the army pay dispute earlier this year. It all seems to stem from the elections last March, in which the BNP were comprehensively defeated. Today, a planned meeting between Mokhehle and the BNP leader Everistus Sekonyana failed to get off the ground and the acting finance minister, whose predecessor was killed last month, was kidnapped at gun point by angry policemen. On the line to Maseru Jeremy Skeet asked Mr. Sekonyane what relations were like between himself and the prime minister.
