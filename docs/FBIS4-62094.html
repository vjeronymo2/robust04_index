BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow May 11 TASS -- Moscow's Military Court confirmed on Wednesday that the dismissal of Colonel Stanislav Terekhov from the army was legal. Terekhov was sacked on September 23, 1993 by Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev for violation of the contract and disobeying his commanders, ITAR-TASS learnt from sources close to the Defence Ministry. Terekhov, who is also chairman of the Union of Army Officers, was detained on charges of plotting an attack on the headquarters of CIS united armed forces after President Boris Yeltsin dissolved the parliament last autumn and has been in custody since then. Terekhov did not deny the charges, but said he was against using guns. He was released under amnesty earlier this year. Additionally, Terekhov is charged with participation in May Day riots in 1993 which killed one and injured dozens of people.
