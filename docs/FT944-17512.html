941007 FT 07 OCT 94 / International Company News: Pargesa falls 6.6% to SFr105m in first half Pargesa, the Swiss investment group controlled by the Belgian financier Mr Albert Frere and Canada's Mr Paul Desmarais, reported consolidated net earnings of SFr104.8m (Dollars 81.80m) in the first half, down 6.6 per cent, mainly because of the rise of the Swiss franc. Most of the group's industrial and financial holdings are based in Belgian and French francs. Petrofina's contribution was up 36 per cent to BFr5.2bn (Dollars 153m) and that of the Tractabel utility advanced 2.8 per cent to BFr6.48bn. The Royale Belge Insurance group raised its contribution 2.9 per cent to BFr4.58bn while that of Banque Brussels Lambert was up 26 per cent to BFr3.85bn. Paribas, the main French holding, raised its contribution 13 per cent to FFr1.27bn (Dollars 227m).
