BFN [Text] Chisinau BASAPRESS, 7/4/1994 -- "The time of unfounded promises and declarations and political confrontation that split up the precedent governments has gone," stated today President Mircea Snegur during the first session of the new Moldovan Government. He mentioned that Moldova is in a very complicated situation which is decisive for the future of the reforms. The president insisted on the need to stop the economic decline, to stabilize the situation and to lay the foundation for the economic take-off that would lead to the consolidation of the statehood. Snegur emphasized that the reorganization of the ministries and departments [words indistinct] and promoted with consistency according to the Memorandum signed with the IMF. Referring to the activity program of the new Government, Premier Andrei Sangheli inferred weight to the social protection and in particular medical care. The government examined and proposed for parliamentary debates the draft decision on ratifying the agreement "Regarding the Creation of the Commonwealth of Independent States" with the amendments imposed by the Moldovan legislation and the treaty "Regarding the Creation of the Economic Union." Vice-Premier Valentin Cunev mentioned that amendments to the agreement have to do with preserving Moldova's independence, meaning that Moldova will not adhere to military agreements, creation of a joint army and ruble zone.
