Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Greece supports the membership of Cyprus in NATO if the U.S. plan for gradual expansion of the North Atlantic alliance toward the east is adopted. NATO expansion was at the center of discussions today, the first day of the NATO summit. NATO leaders today approved a plan to establish closer military ties with all former communist countries in East Europe, including Russia. However, NATO leaders refrained from adopting a demand by many former East European countries to join NATO as equal members. The United States proposed a plan to gradually expand the alliance to the east. According to reliable Greek sources in Brussels, the Greek side is taking a positive approach to the U.S. plan but believes this expansion should include all CSCE members, in order to open the way for the membership of Cyprus.
