BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Varlamov] [Text] Tokyo, 8 Jun -- Russia and Japan have confirmed the common policy of the two countries of developing bilateral relations and continuing talks on the territorial problem and also their readiness to coordinate efforts in the matter of settling the crisis situation regarding the DPRK nuclear programme. An ITAR-TASS correspondent was informed at the Russian department of the Japanese Foreign Ministry that this was discussed at a brief meeting between Andrey Kozyrev, the RF [Russian Federation] foreign minister, and Nobuo Matsunaga, special representative of the Japanese Government to the international talks, which were held in Paris today. They are taking part in a session of the Organisation of Economic Cooperation and Development, which is being held in the French capital. The Japanese representative noted that a certain amount of progress has been achieved in advancing the political and economic dialogue between the two neighbouring countries since the visit by Boris Yeltsin, the RF president, to Tokyo and stressed the need for the further development of Russian-Japanese ties on the basis of the "Tokyo declaration" which was signed on the results of the summit in October 1993. The Russian minister confirmed the invitation for Tsutomu Hata, the Japanese prime minister, to make an official visit to Moscow this year. However, according to the Japanese Foreign Ministry staffer, the sides did not touch on the issue of specific dates for the possible trip.
