910501 FT 01 MAY 91 / Three are charged with fraud THREE FORMER directors of Homes Assured, the mortgage broker which went into liquidation in 1989 owing Pounds 10m, were yesterday charged with defrauding the company's creditors, writes Raymond Hughes. Following an 18-month investigation by the Serious Fraud Office, Mr Anthony Dobson, Mr Michael Robinson and Mr Keith Woodward were charged at King's Cross police station in London and bailed to appear at Clerkenwell magistrates court on May 21. They were charged that between July 1 1988 and August 31 1989 they were knowingly parties to the carrying on of the business of Homes Assured with intent to defraud its creditors, and/or the creditors of another person, or for another fraudulent purpose, contrary to section 458 of the 1985 Companies Act.
