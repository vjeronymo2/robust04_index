Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] [Announcer] Croatian-U.S. talks are being held in Washington. The special envoy of President Clinton, Ambassador Charles Redman, U.S. Ambassador to Croatia Peter Galbraith, and experts of the U.S. Government have had a meeting in the State Department with Croatian Foreign Minister Mate Granic; Kresimir Zubak, president of the presidential council of the Croat Republic of Herceg-Bosna; and Mile Akmadzic, minister of international relations [of Herceg-Bosna]. Also present were ambassadors Sarcevic [not further identified] and [Mario] Nobilo. According to initial reports, the United States has expressed great interest in the implementation of the agreement between the Croats and the Muslims both on a federal state of Bosnia-Herzegovina and on a confederation with Croatia. U.S. Vice President Al Gore and Secretary of State Warren Christopher are showing great interest. A tripartite meeting has been scheduled for 1830 Central European Time at which, in addition to the representatives of the Croatian and Muslim sides, U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher and Peter Tarnoff, an official in the State Department, will take part. After this half-hour meeting the delegations will have talks. These will be led by Minister Granic, [Bosnian] Prime Minister Silajdzic, and Ambassador Redman.
