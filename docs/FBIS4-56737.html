BFN [Text] This afternoon in Barbados, Cuban President Fidel Castro stated that nongovernmental organizations [NGO's] have become a powerful force in international politics. Fidel is participating in the First International Small Island Nations Conference on Sustainable Development, and met in Bridgetown with NGO representatives from the Caribbean and Mediterranean Seas, and the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. At the beginning of the colloquy, (Jean Preanch), spokeswoman for the Caribbean organizations, stressed the support to Cuba that was demonstrated in the recently concluded NGO summit, which rejected the U.S. blockade against Cuba. Fidel applauded the manifestations of support and solidarity and received tremendous applause from the crowd when he stated: We feel among you that we are among friends, brothers. This is why I leave Barbados and this conference very happy. In his closing statements, Castro stressed the importance of the Barbados summit for organizing an action strategy to defend the islands from serious, looming problems such as pollution, radioactive wastes, and climate changes. This afternoon, Fidel met behind close doors with the other 15 heads of state in attendance to ratify the final version of the Barbados Resolution and the action plan that will close the meeting.
