BFN [Text] Budapest, 20 June (MTI) -- A preliminary agreement on the distribution of ministerial portfolios was reached during Monday's [20 June] round of coalition talks between the Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP) and the Alliance of Free Democrats (SZDSZ), SZDSZ Chairman Ivan Peto announced today. According to Peto, the SZDSZ will provide the minister of the interior, as well as the transport, telecommunications, water management, and education ministers. The rest of the portfolios will be held by Socialist politicians. The political state secretary for the Ministry of Education will be a Socialist, while Free Democrats will hold the political state secretary posts at the Ministry of Industry and Trade, and the Ministry of Agriculture. The two parties earlier agreed that they would operate 12 ministries. The posts within parliament were distributed previously:The speaker of parliament will be a Socialist, and one deputy speaker of the three will be a Free Democrat.Free Democrats will head three parliamentary committees: the constitutional, the defense, and the foreign affairs committee.The rest of the non-opposition committees will be chaired by Socialists. According to an earlier decision, about one third of parliamentary committees will be chaired by opposition parties.
