920507 FT 07 MAY 92 / Arts: Today's Television The curio of the day promises to be True Stories on C4 (9.30). When Dennis O'Rourke's marriage broke up he went to Bangkok to make a programme about different attitudes to love and sex. In a bar at 3am a pimp introduced him to Aoi: 'Only 500 Baht, do anything you want, no worries'. O'Rourke stayed in Bangkok for nine months, recorded Aoi's life and, according to the billing, 'his feelings for her deepened into love'. He filmed the punters and pimps as well as the bar girls and says of his programme that it is 'about prostitution as a metaphor for capitalism'. In the end he offered to buy Aoi a rice farm because Aids had made her life so dangerous, but she says that prostitution is her fate. In the last of its BBC2 series Taking Liberties has yet another appalling story in which tragedy was not only predictable but predicted. Two mentally handicapped patients in Greaves Hall Hospital, Southport, were killed by another patient during one weekend in 1991. It seems the parents of the murderer had expressed disquiet every night for a week before these events occurred and an external report had already called urgently for more staff (8.00).
