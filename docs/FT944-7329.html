941125 FT 25 NOV 94 / Investment guide for regeneration Development projects which create jobs, promote inward investment and strengthen local economies will be given priority treatment by English Partnerships, the government agency, according to its investment guide published today. English Partnerships was created earlier this year to co-ordinate urban regeneration policy and encourage development partnerships with the private, public and voluntary sectors. The investment guide says the agency will focus on physical regeneration initiatives which show a significant return in terms of jobs created, inward investment attracted and the provision of new housing and employment space. The guide goes on: 'In considering environmental projects, we will concentrate on those which are urgently required for health and safety reasons and form part of a wider strategy of development and reclamation.' While the agency was able to operate on vacant, derelict and contaminated land throughout England it would concentrate on areas where mines have been closed, City Challenge and other inner-city areas, and rural regions with severe economic need. Lord Walker, chairman of English Partnerships, said the agency was committed to the creation of innovative development packages with a wide variety of partners to bring benefits to local communities, existing industry and inward investors. He said: 'With the help of this comprehensive guide, our partners can help make this objective a reality.'
