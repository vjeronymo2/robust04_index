920507 FT 07 MAY 92 / Australia cuts interest rates again THE Reserve Bank of Australia yesterday cut official interest rates for the 12th time since 1990, ignoring signs that recovery from 18 months of economic stagnation or decline is already under way. The Bank announced a cut of one percentage point in official cash rates to 6.5 per cent, the lowest since the mid-1970s. Rates have now been cut by 11.5 percentage points since peaking at 18 per cent in January 1990. Mr Bernie Fraser, the reserve bank governor, said the cut was justified by zero growth in the consumer price index in the three months to the end of March. Inflation is currently 1.7 per cent a year. Mr Fraser said the economic recovery was uneven, and forecast that excess capacity would restrain upward pressure on prices. 'A reduction in rates will help business cash flow, and provide some encouragement (to companies) to undertake new investments,' he said. Mr John Dawkins, the federal Treasurer (finance minister), said inflation expectations were on 'a downward trend,' and forecast that inflation would average less than 2 per cent for the remainder of 1992. Mr Dawkins said the outlook for inflation would be 'very important for the future stance of monetary policy.' He said the government 'will stand ready to increase rates if circumstances require.'
