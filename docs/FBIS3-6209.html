Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The government plans to legislate, revise or abolish 149 laws this year in a bid to make the legal system of Korea fit the emerging international economic order after the conclusion of the Uruguay Round multilateral trade negotiations. Thirty-two laws are related to deregulation in the economic field. They include the Foreign Trade Law, the Insurance Law and the Law on Farmland Ownership. Legislation Minister Pak Song-kyu reported to the weekly cabinet meeting yesterday that the Foreign Trade law will be revised to allow people to engage in international trade by simply reporting their business to the authorities. Pak said the regulation on financial support for Korean businesses which suffer losses because of imported products will be also modified. He said his office has already handed in 15 bills to the National Assembly for consideration. It will seek the Assembly's approval for the other bills at the upcoming special or regular sessions. The government will draw up a bill on development of the information industry to provide tax benefits to the investors in this area. Minister Pak said his office is working to draw up a bill on the promotion of people's health with a view to providing regular health checks to all people. The bill would form a fund for national health promotion using part of the profits from the sale of cigarettes, a government monopoly.
