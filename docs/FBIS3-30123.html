Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 24 KYODO -- Japan's motor vehicle exports in January declined a sharp 26.5 percent from a year earlier to 390,418 units, the Japan Automobile Manufacturers Association said Thursday [24 February]. It was the 10th consecutive month of declines from year-earlier levels. In terms of value, January exports fell 12.6 percent to 6,522.69 million dollars. Of the total, assembled vehicle exports accounted for 4,789.11 million dollars and parts exports for 1,733.58 million dollars, the association said. By vehicle type, exports of passenger cars came to 312,423, while 74,332 trucks and 3,663 buses were shipped overseas. Exports to the United States fell for the sixth month in a row from a year earlier by 14.0 percent to 114,190 vehicles. Exports to the 12-nation European Union shrank by 37.8 percent to 128,597 vehicles for the first fall in two months. Shipments to Asian countries declined by 19.3 percent to 41,824 vehicles, the association said. Toyota Motor Corp. exported a total of 128,208 vehicles in January, down 20.7 percent from a year earlier, Nissan Motor Co. shipped 45,405 vehicles overseas, down 22.1 percent, and Mazda Motor Corp. exported 57,146 vehicles, down 34.8 percent.
