Language: Russian Article Type:CSO [Decree No. 1260 of the Council of Ministers-Government of the Russian Federation dated 4 December 1993: "On Measures for Stabilization of the Socioeconomic Situation of Khabarovsk Kray During 1993-1996"] [Text] Taking into account the extreme situation that has developed in the economy of Khabarovsk Kray in connection with the liberalization of prices of energy sources and the increased transportation rates, and also in order to stabilize the socioeconomic situation of the kray, the Council of Ministers-Government of the Russian Federation decrees: 1. The Russian Federation State Committee for Socioeconomic Development of the North, the Russian Federation Ministry of Economics, and the Russian Federation Ministry of Finance in conjunction with the administration of Khabarovsk Kray shall before 1 January 1994 submit to the Council of Ministers-Government of the Russian Federation proposals for including the entire territory of Khabarovsk Kray on the list of localities categorized with regions of the Far North because of conditions for delivery and shipment of products for production and technical purposes as well as consumer goods. 2. The Ministry of Finance of the Russian Federation, the Ministry of Economics of the Russian Federation, and the Ministry of Fuel and Energy of the Russian Federation, in order to maintain the cost of one kilowatt-hour of electric energy in Khabarovsk Kray at the level of R40 (calculated cost), shall envision the allotment to the administration of Khabarovsk Kray as a special-purpose selective subsidy during November-December 1993, R19.6 billion out of the Russian Federation republic budget to compensate for the difference between the cost of electric energy produced by the Khabarovskenergo Joint-Stock Company and the calculated cost. The administration of Khabarovsk Kray in conjunction with the Russian Federation Ministry of Fuel and Energy shall submit to the Council of Ministers-Government of the Russian Federation substantiated proposals for maintaining the calculated cost of 1 kwh of electric energy in Khabarovsk Kray during each quarter of 1994. 3. The Russian Federation Ministry of Economics, the Russian Federation Ministry of Finance, the Russian Federation Ministry of Fuel and Energy, and the Khabarovsk Kray administration shall envision, beginning in 1994, the financing of work for increasing the reliability of the energy supply for Khabarovsk Kray cities and introducing energy capacities in the kray financed by the special-purpose fund for financial support for energy and electrification, funds of the Russian Federation republic budget, and enterprises and organizations that are consumers of thermal and electric energy. 4. The Russian Federation Ministry of Finance and the Russian Federation Ministry of Fuel and Energy, in order to increase capacities for extracting coal from deposits in Khabarovsk Kray, in keeping with Russian Federation Decree No. 389 of 9 June 1992, "On Measures for Stabilization of the Situation in the Fuel and Energy Complex of the Far East and Eastern Siberia During 1992 and the Period up to 1995," shall provide financing for the construction of a section of open-pit work for the Uralskoye Mining Administration in the volume determined by this decree and based on limits of capital investments for 1993 with subsequent allotment of funds for 1994-1996. 5. The Russian Federation Ministry of Fuel and Energy in conjunction with the Khabarovsk Kray administration shall develop and submit before 1 July 1994 to the Council of Ministers-Government of the Russian Federation a program for the development during the period of 1994-2000 of the coal industry in Khabarovsk Kray on the basis of the Urgalskoye hard coal basin and the Lianskoye and Khurmulinskoye brown coal deposits. 6. The Russian Federation Ministry of Economics, the Russian Federation Ministry of Finance, the Russian Federation Committee for Geology and the Utilization of Minerals, and the Russian Federation Ministry of Fuel and Energy shall provide for the allotment of budget funds to prospect for coal, petroleum, and gas in 1994 at the level of volumes allotted in 1992 (with the corresponding indexation). 7. The Russian Federation Ministry of Fuel and Energy and the Russian Federation Ministry of Economics when preparing the agreement on the assimilation of the Piltun-Astokhskoye and Lunskoye deposits of petroleum and gas on the shelf of Sakhalin Island, shall take into account the needs of Khabarovsk Kray with respect to deliveries of natural gas from petroleum and gas raw material of the aforementioned shelf. 8. The Russian Federation Ministry of Economics, the Russian Federation Ministry of Finance, and the Russian Federation State Committee for Defense Branches of Industry shall provide, in keeping with the established procedure, for the allotment during 1994-1996 to defense enterprises of Khabarovsk Kray of monetary funds on a reimbursable basis for implementation of improved conversion programs and also subsidies from the state conversion fund used for labor compensation for workers of these enterprises and maintenance of the social infrastructure. 9. The Russian Federation Ministry of Defense and other Russian Federation ministries and departments which are state clients shall provide for presenting by the established deadlines to enterprises manufacturing arms and military equipment in Khabarovsk Kray assignments in keeping with the state defense order for 1994. When determining state orders for arms and military equipment they shall envision the maximum possible loading of capacities of the aforementioned enterprises. 10. The Russian Federation Ministry of Defense and the Russian Federation Ministry of Finance shall provide for repayment of indebtedness to enterprises that manufacture arms and military equipment in Khabarovsk Kray for state orders that have been filled. 11. The Russian Federation Ministry of Transportation, the Russian Federation Ministry of Railways, and the Russian Federation Ministry of Economics when formulating the Russian Federation state investment program for 1994 and subsequent years shall envision the development of the commercial seaports of Vanino and Sovetskaya Gavan and the reconstruction of the railroad line Komsomolsk-na-Amur to Vanino as a part of the program entitled "Regeneration of the Commercial Fleet of Russia During 1993-2000." 12. The Russian Federation Ministry of Finance when determining relations between the Russian Federation republic budget and the Khabarovsk Kray budget for 1994 shall take into account expenditures necessary for: --completion of construction and startup of the Baykal-Amur Poultry Factory in the city of Komsomolsk-na-Amur; --the acquisition of urban passenger transportation; --land reclamation work. 13. The Russian Federation Ministry of Economics and the Roskhleboprodukt Joint-Stock Company, beginning in 1994, shall envision deliveries of grain to Khabarovsk Kray from the federal fund through the Eksportkhleb Joint-Stock Company through ports of the Far East. 14. The Russian Federation Ministry of Finance when formulating the budget system for 1994-1996 shall take into account in the Khabarovsk Kray budget expenditures on financing incomplete construction of facilities for municipal services transferred to municipal ownership by enterprises and departments located on the territory of the kray. 15. The Russian Federation Ministry of Economics and the Russian Federation Ministry of Finance in conjunction with interested Russian Federation ministries and departments and the Khabarovsk Kray administration shall within a month's time develop and submit to the Council of Ministers-Government of the Russian Federation proposals for special-purpose use beginning in 1994 of allocations for the development of the foreign economic infrastructure of the kray in an amount of up to 20 percent of the funds transferred into the Russian Federation republic budget from collections by customs control organs of customs duties on Khabarovsk Kray territory. [Signed] Chairman of the Council of Ministers-Government of the Russian Federation V. Chernomyrdin
