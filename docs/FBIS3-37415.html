Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tamara Ivanova] [Text] Moscow February 16 TASS -- Russian Government Press Officer Valentin Sergeyev told a press conference in the Russian-American Information Centre today that the restriction of access to government meetings for journalists was a temporary measure caused by organisational difficulties associated with the moving of the government from Staraya Square to the "White House", the former seat of the disbanded Russian Parliament. Sergeyev rejected accusations of deliberate blocking of the press. He assured journalists that a new press centre, seating 150 reporters, will open in the government's new headquarters in two weeks. He promised to help journalists in arranging interviews with cabinet ministers, but said government presidium meetings were not a show, and will be accessible only to correspondents of federal news agencies and state television. Sergeyev stressed that the government was interested, as never before, in making its decisions, popular or otherwise, known and understandable to the people. He complained that the press was keen on criticising the government and was reluctant to promote educational publications about its activity.
