BFN [Text] Seoul, May 27 (YONHAP) -- The Korea Development Bank will extend a 9.5-million-dollar credit to China to help finance a thermo-power project in Jiangsu Province, the bank reported Friday. A bank official said the credit will be part of the 470 million dollars which the World Bank, JP Morgan of the United States and two other foreign banks in addition to KDB are providing to China. "This is the first time a South Korean bank ever furnishes a credit to a project the central Chinese Government is directly undertaking," he said. The credits, whose repayment is guaranteed by the World Bank, is repayable in 10 years after a grace of five years. The development bank will formally sign a credit agreement with the Chinese Government in Beijing on Sunday, the official added.
