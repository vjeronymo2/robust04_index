Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [Text] After one day's recess, the Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Strait (ARATS) and Taiwan's Straits Exchange Foundation (SEF) resumed in Beijing in the morning of 29 March comprehensive discussion of three routine topics, including fishing disputes. After the meeting, Sun Yafu, deputy secretary-general of ARATS, said: During the discussion, both parties expressed their opinions about the crucial points of the three topics which need to be resolved. Both sides further exchanged their opinions and deepened their understanding. But the two sides have not yet reached a consensus on some issues that need to be resolved. According to a suggestion raised by Xu Huiyou [Hsu Hui-yu], deputy secretary-general of SEF, both sides have decided through consultation to hold another comprehensive discussion on the morning of 30 March to give it another try. It was learned that discussion of the three topics -- including cracking down on criminal activities committed at sea, as well as other topics concerning economic, scientific, and technological matters which were scheduled to be discussed by ARATS and SEF on 28 March -- were not conducted due to the absence of Shi Qiping [Shih Chi-ping], another deputy secretary-general of SEF.
