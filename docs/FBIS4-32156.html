BFN [Text] Sofia, June 17 (BTA) -- Consultations between Bulgaria and China were held today at the Foreign Ministry, BTA learned from the Ministry's spokesman. Deputy Foreign Minister Dimitur Ikonomov headed the Bulgarian delegation, and the Chinese delegation was led by Assistant Foreign Minister Qin Huasun. The sides discussed cooperation between the two countries within the framework of the United Nations and issues of common interest included in the preliminary agenda of the 49th Session of the UN General Assembly. Special attention was paid to the reorganization of the world organization and its role in settling regional conflicts and peacekeeping operations. The two delegations exchanged views on the sanctions enforced on certain countries by resolutions of the UN Security Council, on various aspects of the UN activities in the sphere of human rights, on disarmament issues, etc. The Chinese delegation was received by Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov. Besides the issues on the agenda of bilateral consultations, they discussed Bulgarian-Chinese relations in general.
