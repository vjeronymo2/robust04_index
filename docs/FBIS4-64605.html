BFN [Text] Stockholm, May 4 (AFP) -- Swedish European Affairs Minister Ulf Dinkelspiel hailed the European Parliament's decision Wednesday [4 May] to admit Sweden, Austria, Finland and Norway to the European Union (EU). "The Swedish Government welcomes, with great satisfaction, the European Parliament's decision to accept the membership agreement with Sweden and the three other applicant countries," Dinkelspiel said in a statement. "Today's decision means that the decisive factor now lies in the hands of the Swedish people," he added. Sweden is expected to hold a referendum on EU membership November 13. "Today's decision gives a clear signal to governments and people -- both in member countries and applicant countries -- to move quickly in forming a united Europe," he said. "In our opinion, Sweden has negotiated a very good agreement on membership in the European Union. It is very gratifying that the European Parliament today supports this agreement," Dinkelspiel concluded.
