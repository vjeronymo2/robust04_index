920409 FT 09 APR 92 / Support for prospective MPs PROSPECTIVE MPs can often count on the support of their employers, irrespective of political affiliation, according to Industrial Relations Services, the independent pay research group. Employers allow staff time off to fight elections because they feel more MPs are needed with industrial or business experience. Employers generally offer parliamentary hopefuls four weeks paid leave, the survey of several large public and private-sector employers found. Many employers say they look sympathetically at re-employment should employees lose their seats or leave parliament. Public Duties or Private Activities, April 1992, IRS Employment Trends 509.
