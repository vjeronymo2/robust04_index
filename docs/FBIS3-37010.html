Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Aleksandr Pelts and Anatoliy Ladin report: "Time for Disagreements Is Past. Results of Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev's Tour of Central Asian Republics"] [Text] A joint Russian and Uzbek Foreign Ministry statement issued 9 February in Tashkent reflects their principled stance on Afghanistan, where, as you know, an armed fight for power is flaring up with renewed vigor. In addition to expressing profound concern at events in the neighboring state, the statement reaffirms the principles of strict neutrality and noninterference in the internal affairs of the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan. Andrey Kozyrev also discussed a wide range of issues of mutual interest during his conversation with Kazakhstani President Nursultan Nazarbayev. He discussed problems involved in preparing for the meeting of the Commonwealth heads of state too. What is more, it was decided to set up working groups to seek a solution to the most burning problems in relations between the two countries. Asked by your KRASNAYA ZVEZDA correspondent about the results of his talks in Almaty, Andrey Kozyrev replied that he did not have the task of signing any documents. It was a question of preparing a whole package of treaties and agreements. Primarily on Russian-Kazakhstani military cooperation. The sides discussed the possibility of dismantling the nuclear weapons stationed on Kazakhstan's territory and taking them back to Russia. While agreeing, Kozyrev stressed, appropriate compensation for the strategic nuclear materials. It was likewise deemed necessary to formulate an agreement on legal and humanitarian questions, including a treaty on citizenship. And a number of other documents dealing with the problems of the Russian-speaking population. The issue of joint use of the Baykonur cosmodrome was discussed separately. "My task," the Russian foreign minister said, "was to discuss these matters in detail with the Kazakhstani side and give President Nazarbayev a personal message from Boris Nikolayevich Yeltsin, which I did." Andrey Kozyrev expressed the hope that when the bilateral agreements are signed the scope for demagoguery over many difficult issues will be exhausted. He pointed to the total coincidence of views on a whole series of matters under discussion. Andrey Kozyrev visited the Baykonur cosmodrome 10 February.
