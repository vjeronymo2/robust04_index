BFN [Text] Skopje, 27 Jun (TANJUG) -- The council of the second municipal court in Skopje, chaired by Ljubomir Jovevski, today pronounced sentences on a group of Albanians who took part in organizing paramilitary formations in Macedonia [FYROM -- The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia] and smuggling arms via Albania. The court passed sentences between five and eight years in jail. The defendants have been sentenced for associating in order to engage in hostile activity. Mithat Emini, secretary general of the PDP [Party of Democratic Prosperity] with headquarters in Tetovo and Hasan Agusi, were sentenced to eight years in jail. Ejupi Rezni, Selam Elmazi, and Sinasi Redzepi to seven years in jail; Husein Haskaj, Macedonian deputy defense minister, Akif Demiri, and Abdiselam Arslani to six years in jail; and Burim Murtezani and Eugen Cami to five years in jail. Agusi will be expelled from Macedonia after his term in jail. Under the indictment, all of the accused associated to engage in hostile activity, and they had clear aims and tasks to topple the fledgling Macedonian state. Emini, Agusi, and Haskaj were the ringleaders in this hostile activity. The indictment says that the accused formed "a HQ of national defense," and Emini was candidate for the commander. They set up a state-of-the-art computer center and had a courier service in operation. They also began to draw up a program of setting up paramilitary units in Kumanovo and towns in western Macedonia.
