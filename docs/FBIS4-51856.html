BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 1 [date as received] (KNS-KCNA) -- An Osaka prefectural meeting of Koreans in Japan was held at the Nakaosaka Korean Primary and Middle School on April 30 to denounce the suppression of the Osaka Organisation of the General Association of Korean Residents in Japan (Chongnyon) by the Japanese authorities. A statement of the spokesman for the Foreign Ministry of the DPRK was read out and O Hyong-chin, vice-chairman of the Central Standing Committee of Chongnyon and chairman of the Osaka prefectural headquarters of Chongnyon, made a report at the meeting attended by more than 3,000 Chongnyon officials and Korean residents in Japan. The reporter pungently denounced the large-scale suppression unprecedented in history by the Japanese police, branding the case as a grave hostile act against the DPRK and as an unjustifiable political suppression of Koreans in Japan and Chongnyon. Japanese figures including the vice-chairman of the Osaka headquarters of the Japan Social-Democratic Party and a lawyer made solidarity speeches, which were followed by indignation speeches of representatives of Chongnyon and the Democratic Union of Korean Women in Japan. The meeting adopted a resolution and elected delegates who would conduct a protest action against the Osaka Precfectural Police and the Higashi Police Station. In connection with the outrages by the Osaka Prefectural Police, Ho Chong-man, chief vice-chairman of the Central Standing Committee of Chongnyon, and Kim Tae-hui, director of the International Department of the Chongnyon Central Standing Committee, on April 28 visited the headquarters of the Liberal Democratic Party and met the chairman of the Policy Affairs Research Council and deputy secretary-general of the party and handed them a request of the Chongnyon Central Standing Committee addressed to its President Yohei Kono. On April 26, delegates of the Chongnyon Central Standing Committee visited the headquarters of the Komei Party to hand it a request addressed to its Chairman Koshi Ishida.
