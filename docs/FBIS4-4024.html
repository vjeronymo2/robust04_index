BFN [Text] Almaty, 28 Apr (XINHUA) -- China and Kazakhstan issued a press communique on the conclusion of Chinese Premier Li Peng's visit to this country today. The communique says: During the visit, Premier Li Peng held two rounds of talks with President Nazarbayev -- one on a smaller range of issues and the other on a larger range. The talks were held in a friendly atmosphere of mutual understanding and trust. The two leaders had an in-depth exchange of views on furthering bilateral ties and regional and international issues of mutual concern. Both sides are satisfied with the results of the talks and emphasized that China and Kazakhstan are willing to further develop bilateral relations in the spirit of good-neighborliness and friendship and to cooperate in the international arena so as to consolidate stability and security in Asia. The Chinese side expressed its appreciation of the measures proposed by President Nazarbayev on building trust in Asia, while the Kazakh side reiterated its support for China's principled position on the Taiwan and Tibet issues. Both sides expressed opposition to national splittism. The communique says: The two sides reiterated their determination to strictly abide by the five principles of peaceful coexistence and all the principles contained in the Sino-Kazakhstan joint declaration signed during President Nazarbayev's visit to China in 1993 with a view to maintaining, safeguarding, and expanding a long-term relationship of good-neighborliness, friendship, mutual benefit, and cooperation between the two countries. The two sides held that this is not only in keeping with the fundamental interests of the two peoples, but conducive to peace, stability, and development in Asia as well. The communique points out: Leaders of the two countries spoke highly of the signing of the Sino-Kazakhstan border agreement and regarded this document of historic significance as a proof of good- neighborly and friendly relations between the two countries. The two sides agreed to further expand bilateral trade and economic cooperation and stressed the importance of improving communications and transportation. The two sides signed a summary of talks on developing railway transport. The communique adds: The leaders of the two countries shared a willingness to continue the frequent high-level contact. Premier Li Peng conveyed President Jiang Zemin's best regards to President Nazarbayev. On behalf of President Jiang, he extended an invitation for President Nazarbayev to revisit China at a convenient time. Premier Li Peng expressed his heartfelt thanks for the warm hospitality accorded him and his entourage by the government and the people of the Republic of Kazakhstan.
