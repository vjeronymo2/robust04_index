Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] The majority of Moldova's people believe that the Republic should be an independent, united country, according to preliminary results of the nationwide survey of the population held on 6 March. Initial returns indicate that two-thirds of the Republic's registered electorate took part in the survey, INTERFAX reports. About 90 percent of those who voted gave an affirmative answer to the question of whether Moldova should develop as an independent, united, indivisible state.
