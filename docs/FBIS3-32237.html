Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Indian Revolutionary Clandestine Committee-General Command [CCRI-CG] of the Zapatist National Liberation Army [EZLN] has issued a new communique addressed to Samuel Ruiz Garcia, bishop of San Cristobal de las Casas and national mediation commissioner, and to Manuel Camacho Solis, commissioner for peace and reconciliation in Chiapas. The communique, dated 31 January 1994, has three points. The first point reads: We received the letter from Peace Commissioner Camacho Solis dated 29 January 1994 regarding the release of Division General Absalon Castellanos Dominguez. We have confirmed that the 38 prisoners mentioned were indeed released. Division General Castellanos Dominguez is in perfect health and knows he will be released soon. Division General Absalon Castellano will definitely be released. The second point of the communique reads: We received the letter from Peace Commissioner Manuel Camacho Solis dated 29 January 1994 regarding the establishment of buffer or gray zones. We inform you that we fully agree with the entire proposal from the peace commissioner for peace and reconciliation in Chiapas in this respect. The third point of the EZLN communique reads: In saluting the clear intentions of the commissioner for peace and reconciliation in Chiapas to make advancements to alleviate the grave situation affecting the civilian population in the conflict areas, the CCRI-CG will order the immediate withdrawal of EZLN troops from Los Ejidos, San Miguel, Ococingo Municipality, and Guadalupe Tepeyac, Las Margaritas Municipality. The EZLN troops will withdraw once the International Red Cross occupies these two buffer or gray zones and installs its camps for humanitarian aid. The communique is signed by Subcommander Marcos and issued in the mountains of southeastern Mexico.
