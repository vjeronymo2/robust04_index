930430 FT 30 APR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Heath speaks up against air strikes SIR EDWARD HEATH, the former Conservative prime minister, called on the government to veto any attempt to secure the backing of the UN security council for air strikes against the Bosnian Serbs, Ivor Owen Writes. He said such a course would necessitate the withdrawal of British and other forces which were discharging such a significant humanitarian role, and involve risks far outweighing any possible advantages that might be secured. Sir Edward dismissed the Vance-Owen peace plan as hopelessly out of date, and suggested that Lord Owen - whom he criticised for having advocated the use of force - should retire from his role as the European Community's chief negotiator. In an earlier intervention during Commons question time Sir Edward pressed for an absolutely clear and categorical assurance that no British forces of any kind would become involved in the former Yugoslavia except for humanitarian reasons. He told Mr John Major, the prime minister, that without such an assurance any other initiative would be suspect because it would be thought that Britain would be dragged stage by stage into a European war, or pushed there by the US. Mr Major, who emphasised the emerging and changing situation, told Sir Edward: 'I take to heart your remarks as will every member of the House.'
