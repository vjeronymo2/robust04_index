Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Cairo, 8 Jan (MENA) -- The Supreme State Security Court-Emergency decided at its session today to postpone the trial of the 48 defendants, led by Dr. 'Umar 'Abd-al-Rahman, who are accused of taking part in riots in al-Fayyum in 1989; it is postponed until Monday, 10 January, in order to listen to the defense presentation. The indictment was read at the beginning of the court session. It accused the defendants of taking part in demonstrations following Friday prayers in al-Shuhada' Mosque in al-Fayyum in 1989, of carrying firearms and ammunition without a license, using them in activities that undermine public law and order, attacking members of the authorities, and attempted murder. Usamah Qandil, president of the Supreme State Security Prosecution, stated that escaped defendant Muhsin Muhammad Ahmad Kharallah, who is involved in this case, had been arrested on court order. The defendant attended today's session. [passage omitted]
