Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Aden, 30 Jan (MENA)--'Ali Salim al-Bid, vice chairman of the Presidential Council, today received Ambassador Badr Hammam, Egypt's assistant foreign minister for Arab and Middle Eastern affairs, who is currently visiting Yemen. During the meeting, they discussed brotherly relations between Egypt and Yemen and ways of promoting them. They also exchanged views on issues and developments that concern both countries. During the meeting, al-Bid stressed Yemen's concern to bolster the fraternal relations to serve the common interests of the Egyptian and Yemeni peoples. Al-Bid and Hammam also reviewed developments in Yemen and the efforts being made to overcome the obstacles and challenges confronting unity and democracy in Yemen in light of the results reached by the political forces dialogue committee, the document of pledges, and the agreement recently initialed in Aden. Ambassador Hammam conveyed greetings from President Husni Mubarak to his brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, stressing Egypt's desire to back unity and democracy as an important national and pan-Arab achievement. Al-Bid asked Hammam to convey his greetings to President Mubarak and the Egyptian people. Meanwhile, Yemeni Prime Minister Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas today received a message from Egyptian Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi dealing with fraternal relations between the two countries and ways of strengthening them in various fields. Ambassador Hammam delivered the letter during a meeting with al-'Attas today. During the meeting, which was also attended by the Egyptian ambassador to Yemen, Al-'Attas expressed his appreciation for Dr. Sidqi's kind sentiments. He stressed the necessity of continued consultations to promote relations of bilateral cooperation.
