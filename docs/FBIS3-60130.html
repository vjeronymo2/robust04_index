Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "Caracol's 0600 to 0900" newscast] [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota -- General Jairo Rodriguez, deputy director of the judicial and general investigations police, has disclosed that Peruvian drug trafficking boss Demetrio Limoniel Chavez Penaherrera, alias El Vaticano, captured in Colombia and deported to Lima last night, had entered this country clandestinely through the Amazon jungle. Rodriguez said Chavez Penaherrera had been hiding in Cali for a year. He added that the Peruvian drug trafficker did not resist arrest. He had no documents and offered the Colombian policemen $1 million to let him go. According to Peruvian authorities, Chavez Penaherrera is wanted for crimes against the public faith [fe], falsification of airplane licenses and flags to the detriment of the Peruvian state, crimes against public health, and narcotics trafficking, specifically shipping approximately 60,000 kg of cocaine paste from Peru to Colombia.
