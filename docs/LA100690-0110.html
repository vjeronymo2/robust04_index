October 6, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Savings Bond Sales Hits Three-Year High: The Treasury said the sale of U.S. savings bonds hit a three-year high of $7.77 billion in the year ending Sept. 30.

Boosting the total were sales of $544 million in September, a 3% advance over a year ago. At the same time, redemptions in fiscal 1990 totaled $7.54 billion, a 1% drop from 1989. The value of savings bonds held by Americans rose 7% during the year, finishing at an all-time record $123.9 billion, up from $115.7 billion in 1989.
