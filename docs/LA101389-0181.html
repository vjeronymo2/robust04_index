October 13, 1989, Friday, P.M. Final

NAMES IN THE NEWS;

KITTY DUKAKIS TALKS ON ADDICTION

Kitty Dukakis, describing herself as an alcoholic and drug addict, today urged doctors at a World Congress of Psychiatry to avoid prescribing drugs that may cause addiction.

"I urge you psychiatrists not to give in to our society's urge for a quick fix, not to rush to identify drugs as the first solution to your patient's problem," Dukakis, wife of Massachusetts Gov. Michael S. Dukakis, said.

Doctors' prescriptions often complicate an alcohol and drug abuse problem, which addicts carefully hide from their doctors, she told the conference.

Dukakis said her long addiction to amphetamines relapsed to alcoholism after her husband lost the presidential elections in 1988. "I am an alcoholic and a drug addict," Dukakis said.

She said diet pills she took as a teen-ager to look slim became a 27-year amphetamine addiction, which she hid from everyone including her husband.
