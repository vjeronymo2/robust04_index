921016 FT 16 OCT 92 / Arts: Today's Television So far the BBC's social affairs series Public Eye has caused remarkably little excitement. Perhaps today's programme, the first in a fresh series, will change that. Called 'The Mad Cow Contagion', it is concerned with BSE, an epidemic it says has become far more widespread than was ever predicted, and which has now crossed into a number of other species (8.00 BBC2). That is followed (8.30) by Royal Gardens, a series presented by Sir Roy Strong who seems to be teetering on the brink of Crazed Pundit Syndrome: the condition in which people such as Magnus Pike and David Bellamy cease to be primarily presenters and become, instead, the arm-waving subjects of their programmes. Strong already looks a bit like the chap with mutton-chop whiskers who talks in tic-tac, and it would be no surprise to find him describing Kew and Frogmore via semaphore. The best new police series for years is Between The Lines, BBC1's drama about an internal investigation unit. In today's episode a demonstration turns into a riot but the investigators find themselves hampered by a police commander (9.30).
