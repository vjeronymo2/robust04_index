Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Abkhazian Prime Minister Sokrat Djindjolia told Interfax Saturday [26 March] that the Abkhazian leaders welcomed the Russian call for the non-violent settlement of relations between Abkhazia and Georgia. They shared Moscow's call for a political accommodation and reiterated their willingness to seek a negotiated settlement which was torpedoed by the Georgians. The negotiating process could resume on the removal of obstacles created by Tbilisi. Djindjolia hoped Russia would keep playing an active role in the efforts to mediate a settlement.
