Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 8 (XINHUA) -- China's State Council has decided to cancel five tax items and leave two others to the discretion of local governments. The move is aimed at simplifying and standardizing the taxation system. The five cancelled taxes are those previously levied on trade fairs, livestock trading, bonuses and salary adjustments, and oil burning. Meanwhile, the power to levy two other taxes -- on animal slaughter and banquets -- will be turned over to provincial, autonomous regional and municipal governments.
