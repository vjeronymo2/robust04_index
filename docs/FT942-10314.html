940511 FT 11 MAY 94 / Observer: Fat chance Sometimes feet of clay are preferable to being rock-steady. As much as 600 tonnes of gold has moved from the US Federal Reserve bank's vaults to London and Zurich in the past year, and the word is that the Bank of England has captured the lion's share. The Bank of England's big selling point is its better filing system. The Fed stacks its gold from floor to ceiling, which makes finding and shipping it a costly, lengthy business. The Old Lady, by contrast, stores its gold on easy-to-move forklift pallets. This is more luck than judgment. Terry Smeeton, the Bank's foreign exchange guru, says: 'The difference is . . . that the Fed in New York is built on rock whereas the Bank of England is founded on London clay. There is therefore a limit to the weight our floors will bear.'
