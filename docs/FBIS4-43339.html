BFN [Unattributed article: "Rwanda/Italy: Refugees Receive Hospital Treatment, RPF Representative in Rome"] [Excerpt] (ANSA) Rome, June 8 -- A representative of the Rwandan Popular Front (RPF) flew into Rome Wednesday [8 June] to thank the Italian Government for bringing out 93 severely wounded refugees, and to urge further aid for his country. The representative of the opposition group, Jacques Bihozagara, was met at the airport by Maria Pia Fanfani, president of the Insieme per la Pace (Together for Peace) organisation which managed to get the refugees, which included 75 orphaned children and the Rwandan national soccer team, out of the war-torn country. Bihozagara said he was in Rome "to thank the Italian Government and people, which have already done so much for Rwanda, but also to explain the situation in my country, and the need to get medicines, healthcare aid and food as soon as possible." Fanfani said that her organisation was working with the Italian Government on plans to send aid ships to Rwanda. [passage omitted]
