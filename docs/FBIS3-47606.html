Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, Jan 13 (OANA/BERNAMA) -- Malaysia is disappointed that Israel is deliberately delaying transfer of power to the Palestinians in the Gaza Strip and Jericho on the West Bank, Foreign Minister Abdullah Ahmad Badawi said Thursday. Malaysia hoped Israel would be sincere and committed to the peace agreement which it signed on Sept 13 with the Palestine Liberation Organistion (PLO) giving limited self rule in the two areas, he said. The new year has come and a Palestinian government should have been in place in the two areas, he told reporters before giving a cheque for RM13.6 million (about U.S. $5 million), being a donation from the Government of Malaysia, to Palestinian Ambassador Ahmad al- Farra, here. Malaysia hoped the agreement would be implemented as soon as possible, he said. He said Malaysia would continue to support the aspirations of the people of Palestine to have their own independent homeland with Jerusalem as the capital. Abdullah said Malaysia was the first country to give direct financial aid to the Palestine National Council authority to develop the Gaza Strip and Jericho when the two areas were returned to the Palestinians. The donation represented Malaysia's fulfillment of a pledge to the PLO to help it redevelop the two areas, he said. Malaysia is also ready to give all necessary aid to Palestine in the development of its human resources and institutions under its technical cooperation programme, he said. Ahmad al-Farra said the financial aid reflected Malaysia's strong support for the Palestinian cause. He said there were four main factors which had stalled the transfer of power in the Gaza Strip and Jericho. The factors were related to the side of the area in Jericho to be turned over to the Palestinians, who is to administer the border area between Jericho and Jordan and the Gaza Strip and Egypt, security for the Jewish community in Gaza Strip and the question of Jerusalem.
