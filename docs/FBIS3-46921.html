Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, December 31 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- On the occasion of the coming New Year President of Mongolia P. Ochirbat has appealed with greetings to the Mongolian people. In his speech the head of state has wished to all the Mongolian people that all their wishes would be implemented in a new year. The year of 1993 was full of noticeable events as for the whole mankind and for Mongolian people as well. We are on the eve of the fifth year of democratic reforms started in 1989 succeeding to preserve national unity and political stability in spite of all the difficulties and complications being experienced in the course of implementation of those reforms. For the first time by all the nation was elected a head of state, generally accomplished the process of reorganization of one of branches of power the juridicial one. So a formation of state organs has been completed which was intended for by the Constitution and basis for implementation of the fundamental constitutional conception on balanced distribution of powers has been created. At the same time a laborious work on ensuring a regular interactions of all the state structures activities at any level has been started. However it is clear that there remains still great deal to do. As head of state he wished the year of 1994 to be a turning point for getting up the national economy and living standards of the people so that it will be the year of a considerable changes for the better for each Mongol and every family. He also wished that public order would be ensured in a democratic way in 1994, every citizen would be provided possibilities for enforcement of his constitutional rights and freedoms. For this purpose our collective efforts, spiritual and creative work of everyone are needed. A clever state policy, flexible state correlation is needed as well. If we succeed in correlating the unity, knowledge and labour of all of us we will not have any difficulties arising out of ignorance and inability. As the reforming processes in Mongolia are irreversible as the country's foreign policy is steadfast and stable. We are striving to further mutually beneficial equal cooperation with all states of the world including the Russian Federation and the People's Republic of China. As president of Mongolia I welcome the approval of a new Constitution of the Russian Federation. On the occasion of New Year I express a cordial gratitude to all the international organizations countries and peoples who since the very beginning have been rendering assistance and support to processes of democratization and reforming in Mongolia, he said. The president of Mongolia noted with gratitude that the statements by nuclear powers in support of the statement of Mongolia on nuclear free zone were of importance for ensuring the independence and security for our country by political methods.
