940530 FT 30 MAY 94 / Business Travel (Update): Diplomatic couriers UK diplomatic couriers, who have even eaten dispatches to avoid them reaching enemy hands, may fall victim to cost-cutting and modern technology. The Queen's messengers, who travel the world delivering secret documents in briefcases chained to their wrists, were set up 500 years ago by Richard III. But their numbers could be trimmed under a Foreign Office budget review, officials said on Thursday. The 28 couriers, who travel 250,000 miles (400,000 km) a year between them, are becoming an anachronism in an age when security-cleared faxes can do the same job in seconds.
