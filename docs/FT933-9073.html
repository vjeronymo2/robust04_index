930812 FT 12 AUG 93 / TUC urges rethink on monetary union THE Trades Union Congress is calling for a 'reassessment' of the convergence criteria for European economic and monetary union, and for the inclusion of targets on economic growth and unemployment. The TUC, in its reply to the European Commission white paper on employment, growth and competitiveness, also stresses that the free-market policies adopted by Britain and the US in the 1980s have failed to produce lasting competitiveness. The TUC says that both the EC through the Social Chapter and the Clinton administration in the US have rejected the 'diabolical trade-off' between a good record on job creation and a good record on employment standards. Mr David Lea, TUC assistant general secretary, said: 'Our analysis shows that American and British performance on growth and competitiveness was relatively poor during the 1980s and early 1990s. It shows that the recipe of freeing markets, privatisation and cutting employment rights has failed.'
