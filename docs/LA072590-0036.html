July 25, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: STOLEN CAR TRACKING SYSTEM GOES ON LINE

An anti-theft system that uses radio signals to lead police to stolen cars went on line Tuesday in Los Angeles County.

The Stolen Vehicle Recovery Network's key component is a homing device, manufactured by the Massachusetts-based electronics firm LoJack.

The system costs about $595 for a private citizen to install. When a LoJack-equipped car is reported stolen, police enter the report into the state's stolen vehicle system computer, which sends a radio signal from one of seven transmission towers throughout the county.

All but two police departments -- Culver City and Long Beach -- have joined the 47-agency recovery network.

The California Highway Patrol is coordinating the three-year pilot program, which was unveiled at a news conference attended by county Sheriff's Department and Los Angeles police officials.
