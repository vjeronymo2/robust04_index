BFN [Unattributed report: "More and More Cover Firms"] [Text] There is an alarming report by the Federal Office of Criminal Investigations [BKA]: "Organized crime has established itself in Germany." From 1991 to 1993, trials involving organized crime doubled from 369 to 776; at the same time, the number of suspects rose from 5,149 to 9,884 -- with a proportion of foreigners of 54.5 percent. In addition to drugs, the BKA registered increasing activities by gangs smuggling people but also concerning "illegal disposal of hazardous waste." In 50 percent of the cases of organized crime, the gangs are nowadays conducting their business via German cover companies. The overall damage caused in 1993: almost 2 billion German marks.
