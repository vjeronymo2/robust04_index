BFN [Unattributed report under "In the Government" rubric: "Danger of Pressure From Lobby Groups"] [Text] For the few months it has been in power Viktor Chernomyrdin's government has managed to achieve a positive result as regards curbing inflation, but there is a danger that the cabinet, under pressure from agrarian and military-industrial complex lobby groups, will make undesirable adjustments to monetary and credit policy. This was stated by Yevgeniy Yasin, leader of the Russian Federation president's analysis center. In his opinion the rate of the reforms has slowed down somewhat in the same period. "We could and should have moved faster," he said. Yasin believes that the government's main task is "not to hold up the processes begun in 1992 but to provide the opportunity to implement the transformations at the level of enterprises and regions."
