BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 11 (KCNA) -- The South Korean National Council of Representatives of Trade Unions (Chonnodae) at a press conference on June 8 declared that should railway workers go on strike, all the workers affiliated with it would take joint action, according to a radio report from Seoul. It also said it will take joint action in all work places of its affiliates all at once if its demand, such as the withdrawal of the proposed wage hike this year which was set by the "government"-subsidized "federation of trade unions," bowing to the authorities, and the reinstatement of dismissed workers, is not met.
