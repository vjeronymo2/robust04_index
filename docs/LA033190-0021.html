March 31, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

PRESIDENT BUSH BANS BROCCOLI

News that President Bush has taken a tough stand on broccoli has me confused. I can't understand why growers sent him 10 tons of the stuff when he clearly expressed his personal aversion to it.

This tasteless episode is a chilling reminder that agricultural interests in America are so pampered and deluded by subsidies for plowing, subsidies for not plowing, parities, cheap water, caves full of cheese, price supports and tax breaks that could make any working stiff with a good CPA wealthy, that they judge a man's personal preference for broccoli as an attack and retaliate by sending him 10 tons of it!

The drug cartel has better manners than these rowdies. No one sent the President a truckload of crack as he denounced the use and production of this vile drug with similar, though less newsworthy, vehemence.

TOM LAURIE

Cayucos, Calif.
