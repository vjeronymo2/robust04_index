BFN [Text] Cairo, 3 Jun (MENA) -- The 11th conference of the Nonaligned Movement's [NAM] foreign ministers currently being held in Cairo has issued a resolution on Rwanda, urging the UN Security Council to quickly take all the necessary measures to immediately end the massacres taking place in Rwanda and to effectively provide all the necessary means for the deployment of peace keeping forces there as soon as possible. The resolution, passed at the conclusion of the foreign ministers meetings today, also urges the international community to respond to the peaceful and humanitarian efforts being made in Rwanda by providing the material and financial resources and extending all practical assistance to ensure the immediate containment of pollution and the dangers to public health and the environment resulting from the human massacres taking place in Rwanda. The resolution expresses the NAM's support for a roundtable with the participation of all the parties to the conflict. It calls for drawing up an action plan for the reconstruction and rehabilitation of Rwanda. The resolution appeals to the international community not to delay in supporting the good initiative by Kenyan President Daniel arap Moi, which calls for a minisummit of the countries bordering Rwanda to be held in Nairobi early next week to discuss this issue.
