Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Turkey and Azerbaijan have stated that the upper Karabakh region is an integral part of Azerbaijan, and called on Armenia to stop its attacks. Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev, concluding his official visit, left Turkey. The joint communique on the visit states that during the meetings, which were conducted in a friendly, brotherly, and respectful atmosphere, views were exchanged on bilateral relations as well as regional and international issues. Adamantly condemning the Armenian attacks on Azerbaijan, the communique further notes: During the meetings it was determined that more than 20 percent of Azerbaijani territory is occupied by Armenia; that more than 1 million Azeris are refugees in their own land; and that the United Nations is not implementing its Resolutions 822, 853, 874, and 884. Confirming once more that the upper Karabakh region is an integral part of Azerbaijan, both countries called on Armenia to stop its attacks on Azerbaijan. Turkey and Azerbaijan stated that Armenia is held accountable internationally for violating the peace and security in the region, and for forcefully standing against the international norms. According to the communique, President Suleyman Demirel accepted an invitation from Aliyev to visit Azerbaijan.
