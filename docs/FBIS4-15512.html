BFN [Text] Belarusian Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich did not rule out the possibility that Belarusians would be polled on economic integration with Russia on 23 June when the presidential elections are held. In an exclusive INTERFAX interview in Minsk Friday Kebich said that he had initially proposed such a move at the meeting of the government presidium Tuesday, 5 April and the idea was supported by all cabinet members. Kebich said that, according to sociological research, an absolute majority of Belarusian citizens supported a union with Russia. Kebich said that Belarus was a stable country and the economic union treaty that had been ratified by parliament contributed to the stability. He thought, however, that a poll would indirectly enhance the long-term stability of that treaty, whatever the political situation in the country.
