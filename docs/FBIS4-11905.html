BFN [Text] Minister of Health Dr. Umid Midhat Mubarak returned to Baghdad today after attending meetings of the Arab health ministers' council, which convened in Cairo on 30 March. In a statement to the IRAQI NEWS AGENCY, the minister said that the Iraqi delegation was held in high esteem by most conferees, who demanded that the unfair embargo clamped on the lofty Iraqi people be lifted, particularly since this embargo has claimed the lives of thousands of children, women, and elderly due to acute food and medicine shortages. The minister noted that he held a private meeting with Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid. During the meeting, the actual reasons for maintaining the unfair anti-Iraq embargo were addressed. The minister explained that the Arab League secretary general demonstrated special concern for this issue, and urged all Arab League offices in the world to clarify the relevant facts to the world public to aid efforts to lift the unfair embargo clamped on the Iraqi people. The minister also indicated that he held other meetings with the heads of most delegations attending the meetings. He noted that he discussed with them bilateral ties and means of promoting them, especially in the area of health. The minister also met with Regional WHO Director Dr. Husayn al-Jaza'iri. The conferees attending the Cairo meeting discussed the enhancement of health cooperation among the Arab health agencies, as well as other issues to promote the Arab health endeavor.
