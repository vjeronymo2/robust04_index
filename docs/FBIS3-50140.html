Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] At the first session of the new Serbian parliament, the 250 deputies, 123 of whom are from the ruling Socialist Party of Serbia, are today expected to elect the parliament speaker. Before the vote, deputies discussed the candidates for the post of parliament speaker. "The leader of the Democratic Community of Vojvodina Hungarians [Demokratska Zajednica Vojvodjanskih Madjara -- DZVM], Andras Agoston, said that the Hungarians should not tip the scales and added that it would be best if someone from the opposition were elected as parliament speaker, but that the five DZVM deputies would abstain in the vote in parliament, not only on this issue, but also when the new government is voted on."
