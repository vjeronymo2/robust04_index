BFN [Text] Kiev [no date as received] -- The press centre of Ukraine's Foreign Affairs Ministry announced today that the two-day Ukrainian-Swedish consultations ended. Sweden was represented by Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister L. Nilsson who met with officials from the Foreign Ministry, the Ukrainian presidential service for international issues, and the administration of the Ukrainian presidential representative in Kiev. A wide variety of issues relating to bilateral relations was discussed and ways outlined to step up Ukrainian-Swedish cooperation in such influential international organizations as the United Nations, the CSCE, the Council of Europe, and others.
