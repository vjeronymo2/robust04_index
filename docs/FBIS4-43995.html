BFN [Unattributed report: "`Lubbers Withdraws'"] [Text] Brussels/Bonn -- During yesterday's European Peoples' Party (EPP) summit in Brussels, Prime Minister Lubbers is said to have agreed to withdraw his candidacy for the presidency of the European Commission tomorrow evening. German participants at the EPP summit consultation say that Lubbers does not want to risk a tough confrontation at the Corfu summit since he does not stand a chance against Belgian Prime Minister Dehaene. The EPP summit agreed that as soon as a substantial majority is reached in favor of one of the candidates at the European Union summit, the one who looks likely to lose will withdraw. In this way a consensus can be reached on who should succeed Frenchman Jacques Delors as president of the European Commission. The EPP did not agree to promote any Community Christian-Democratic candidates. Before the meeting began, German Chancellor Helmut Kohl called on Lubbers and Dehaene to embrace "so show that we are all friends."
