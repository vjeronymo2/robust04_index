Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow January 13 TASS -- ITAR-TASS learnt from reliable sources on Thursday [13 January] that Russian and U.S. Presidents Boris Yeltsin and Bill Clinton discussed broad range of issues at the Kremlin's Green Hall in a narrow format. The issues included results of the general elections in Russia on December 12, progress in Russian reforms as well as questions of strengthening international security and nuclear non-proliferation. In this connection, the sides stressed the importance of Ukraine's readiness to sign, together with Russia and the United States, a document on the withdrawal of nuclear warheads from its territory. The document will be signed in Moscow on Friday. The sides also discussed the situation with a Russian radar station in Latvia. The two presidents made a short tour of the Moscow Kremlin after the first round of talks. Following the tour, the talks will be resumed in a broader format.
