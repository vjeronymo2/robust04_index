December 12, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

FRIENDSHIP

As I was drinking my morning coffee, I read Al Martinez's column about the friendship of a blind woman and a gay man that began in their high school days and ended with his tragic death in an auto accident ("Time and the Deep Winters," Metro, Nov. 28). The beautifully written article brought tears to my eyes and reminded me of the verse a friend recently enclosed in a little thank-you note: "If we had all the earth's riches we could never spend enough to buy the treasures the heart finds in a friend."

Thank you, Mr. Martinez, for reminding me what this time of year should really be about: extending the hand of human kindness instead of those needless, mad dashes to shopping malls for unappreciated Christmas gifts.

ROBERT LITMAN

Los Angeles
