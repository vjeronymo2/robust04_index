920219 FT 19 FEB 92 / Air 2000 to lease four Airbus A320s A SECOND UK charter airline has decided to operate Airbus A320 narrow-body airliners, whose sophisticated computerised control systems have recently been under criticism. This followed the crash of a French Air Inter A320 last month near Strasbourg, killing 87 people. Air 2000, the charter airline of the Owners Abroad tour group, said yesterday it would lease four new A320s from ORIX Aviation Systems of Dublin. The aircraft will be powered by V2500 engines built by the IAE consortium which includes Rolls-Royce, Pratt & Whitney of the US, MTU of Germany and a group of Japanese aero-engine companies. The first two aircraft will be delivered to Air 2000 for use this summer and the other two will enter service next year. Last month Excalibur, a new UK charter airline, announced orders for three A320s. Air 2000 now operates a fleet of Boeing 757 twin-engine aircraft. It said the A320s would meet a need for a smaller airliner to operate from provincial airports and on less dense routes. Meanwhile London City Airport received a boost yesterday with the announcement that CityAir Scandinavia, a new Swedish airline, plans to introduce daily services using British Aerospace 146 jets between Stockholm and London. This follows the extension of the Docklands airport runway to enable BAe 146 jets to operate there. Crossair, the regional carrier which is 51 per cent owned by Swissair, has already announced daily services between Zurich and London City using BAe 146s. CityAir Scandinavia is absorbing Malmo Aviation, a carrier that operates passenger services on the Malmo-London Gatwick and Malmo-Hanburg routes as well as cargo services for TNT, the Australian transport group. The airline said it intended to start a twice-daily service between Stockholm and London City 'as soon as is practical after the official opening of the airport's extended runway on March 30'. Ryanair, the independent Irish carrier, is expanding its flights from London Stansted to Ireland with a daily service to Shannon from April 10. The route is already operated by Aer Lingus, the Irish flag carrier.
