December 6, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

LOCALLY APPLIED DRUGS

Question: It is my understanding that some medications that are applied locally can have an effect on other parts of the body. From what I've been told, even something like eye drops can affect the entire body. I have been told to use a vaginal estrogen cream for reasons that are not important for my question. Since estrogen also is prescribed to prevent bone loss in menopausal women, would this vaginal estrogen cream do any good for that, or do I also have to take estrogen some other way?

Answer: If, as I assume, the vaginal estrogen cream was prescribed for the purpose of improving vaginal atrophy, the dose is probably insufficient to have any effect on the development of osteoporosis.
