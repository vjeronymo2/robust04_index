930303 FT 03 MAR 93 / Rise in tendering 'would cut costs' GREATER use of competitive tendering would reduce the cost and improve the quality of public services, the Institute of Economic Affairs says in a report published today, John Willman writes. The think tank says local and central government organisations continue to place obstacles before companies which might bid for contracts. It identifies scope to extend competitive tendering in local government, fire services and some police work. The report says the rule which excludes tendering of activities worth less than Pounds 100,000 a year should be examined to see if the limit could be lowered. Testing the Market.
