Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 7 Jan -- President Lech Walesa today received Ambassador Madeleine Albright, President Bill Clinton's special envoy. Her mission is connected with the program of "Partnership for Peace" put forward by the United States. Underscoring U.S. merits in building democracy in Europe, President Walesa said that, in his view as a former soldier, one has to use favorable moments and apply a "lead forward tactic." "I am of the opinion that there is a chance today for a leap ahead in Europe," the president said. He added that, while recognizing U.S. responsibility for the world, "we do not want to hinder great ideas, but we want to present our stand." Among members of the U.S. delegation is General John Shalikashvili, head of the Joint Chiefs of Staff of the U.S. armed forces.
