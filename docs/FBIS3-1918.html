Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "News" program] [Text] Economic Minister P. K. Chiang stated on Tuesday [2 March], when answering questions from lawmakers in the Legislative Yuan, that to ensure economic development, raise industrial competitiveness, and lower production costs, the Economic Ministry is clear about the state of relations between the two sides of the Taiwan Strait and will keep the ties more or less at the present level. However, Chiang said that serious discussions must be carried out on point-to-point direct navigation links and even in an across-the-board opening of navigation and aviation links in the future. However, Chiang stressed that since the opening of navigation links between the two sides is covered in the medium- term-part of the national unification guideline, any research done on this issue and the results coming from them should be forwarded to the Mainland Affairs Council for reference. He said that the result will be help to policy makers when the time comes for further opening up of ties between the two sides.
