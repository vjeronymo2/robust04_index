BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Yuriy Kozlov] [Text] Moscow June 7 TASS -- The Russian side "is striving for deepened partnership with NATO without mutual vetoes and surprises,". This is the essence of Russian approach to the elaboration of a Russia-NATO program and involvement in the Partnership for Peace Program, head of the Russian Foreign Ministry's Information and Press Department Grigoriy Karasin told a briefing here today. The official commented on the upcoming meeting of foreign ministers from the North Atlantic Cooperation Council scheduled for June 10 in Istanbul. The Russian delegation will be headed by Andrey Kozyrev. Moscow has noted recent statements of representatives of several NATO member-states about the readiness to account the Russian role in provision of European security and stability, said Karasin. Speaking of the North Atlantic Cooperation Council, Karasin said that Russia considers this organization to be important and useful, whose role in the system of European cooperation institutions will increase. In accordance with the Russian concept of European partnership, the council is bound to coordinate cooperation in the military-political sphere, namely peace-making, under the aegis of CSCE, he added. The council could also supervise the implementation of NATO's Partnership for Peace Program. According to the Foreign Ministry's representative, the Istanbul meeting is expected to result in a political statement on topical issues of European and world politics. The ministers will also discuss and take into account a report on cooperation in peace- making prepared by the council's special group.
