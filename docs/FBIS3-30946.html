Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] As we have learned, Radovan Karadzic, president of the Srpska republic, met with Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic in Belgrade today. After the talks, correspondents of foreign television stations overwhelmed President Karadzic with questions about threats of air strikes. Let us hear how Dr. Karadzic answered: [Begin recording] [Unidentified correspondent] Please comment on the statement by French Foreign Minister Juppe that he will demand an ultimatum for the Serbs, 10 days to withdraw 20 [words indistinct]. [Karadzic] Minister Juppe should ask [Austrian Foreign] Minister Mock about the fate of the Austrian ultimatum in 1914. [Unidentified correspondent, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] Will you withdraw your artillery from the positions around Sarajevo? [Karadzic, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] Immediately, as soon as we sign the peace plan. [Unidentified correspondent, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] The NATO secretary general today said that it was high time to do something in Bosnia. How would you comment on this? [Karadzic, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] Well, had they taken no actions in the former Yugoslavia, it would not have disintegrated. I would love it if they would not meddle into our affairs at all, because, it is precisely they who have caused this war. [Unidentified correspondent, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] What will happen if the NATO Council issues an ultimatum to the Serbs to withdraw from their positions around Sarajevo? [Karadzic, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] How can we withdraw from Sarajevo? Sarajevo is not a surrounded town, but a divided town. We hold half of the town. If somebody is asking us to give up our own town, then no, never, out of the question. [Unidentified correspondent, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] Do you believe in air strikes, Mr. Karadzic? [Karadzic, in English fading into Serbo-Croatian translation] I believe in God. [end recording]
