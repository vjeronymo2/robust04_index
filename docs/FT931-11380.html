930203 FT 03 FEB 93 / Plea over medical research NEW MEASURES are needed to translate advances in medical research into improved patient care, the government's Advisory Council on Science and Technology said yesterday. It identified a 'development gap' in the transfer of Britain's 'world class' medical research to improved treatments. It said: 'Frustrated inventors contrast British industry's reluctance to pick up technology at an early stage, because of the risks involved, with the enthusiasm shown by US and Japanese manufacturers for new product ideas and for their willingness to invest for the long-term.' The council wants the government to set up a Medilink programme to channel public and private funds towards exploiting ideas from universities and the National Health Service. It would be an extension of the Link programme of collaborative research.
