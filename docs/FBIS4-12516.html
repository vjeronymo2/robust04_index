BFN [Report by Mona Salem] [Text] Cairo, April 11 (AFP)--The PLO chief negotiator acknowledged here Monday that it was now impossible to meet the target date for launching Palestinian self-rule with just two days to go, despite progress in talks with Israel. "I don't think we can resolve (the outstanding issues) by April 13," although there has been "progress Sunday in talks on jurisdiction" in the occupied Gaza Strip and West Bank town of Jericho, Nabil Sha'th told AFP. Sha'th said the Israeli delegation had presented new proposals for jurisdiction in Gaza and Jericho "which could open the way to real progress." Under the Declaration of Principles signed between Israel and the PLO in September, Israel was to have started its withdrawal from the two areas on December 13 and was to complete it by April 13. PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat has insisted that Wednesday's deadline should be met. In a phone call to US Secretary of State Warren Christopher, he urged Washington to step up efforts to ensure the success of the talks, warning a failure to meet the target date would harm the credibility of the peace process. Talks reopened Sunday in the Egyptian capital after a four-day break and are due to continue until Tuesday in a bid to wrap up an accord on implementing Palestinian autonomy. Sha'th added there had been progress both on prisoners and on security matters, including the deployment of a 9,000-strong Palestinian police force to take over from the Israeli army when it withdraws. Security issues have bogged down over a date and timetable for an Israeli pull-out, and the deployment of the Palestinian police. He also said the two sides had "come close" to agreement on the release of around 10,000 Palestinian prisoners being held in Israeli jails. The Palestine Liberation Organisation is demanding the release of all prisoners, including Islamic militants. But Israel has refused to free members of militant organisations such as the Islamic Resistance Movement (HAMAS) or the Islamic Jihad, and those responsible for bloody attacks on Israelis. Asked Monday if Islamic militants would be included in an agreement, Sha'th said: "All the prisoners are going to be scheduled for release one day or another. We represent all the Palestinian people." Meanwhile Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin charged that Arafat was to blame for the delay in implementing Palestinian autonomy. "Israel did not suspend the negotiations after the car-bomb attack in Afula unlike Yasir 'Arafat did after the Hebron massacre," Rabin told state radio. "That is why we are not to blame for the loss of time in the negotiations," he said, after the Palestinians expressed exasperation at the failure to launch autonomy on time. Sha'th added that the committee negotiating the transfer of civilian matters had on Sunday decided on all the agenda items under discussion, except for the thorny issue of water resources.
