BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 6/4/1994 -- Wednesday, 6 April, the talks started between Romanian Defense Minister Gheorghe Tinca and General George Joulwan, supreme commander of the allied forces in Europe, who is now on a documentation visit in Romania. The agenda, Romanian Army sources say, includes matters related to the participation of Romania and other East European countries in the Partnership for Peace program, the evolution of the democratic process in Romania and the parallel reform of the army, to matters concerning European and international security. Attending the talks are Dumitru Cioflina, secretary of state at the National Defense Ministry and chief of the general staff, Ioan Mircea Pascu, secretary of state for defense policies and external relations of the National Defense Ministry, and other military officials accompanying the guest. In the morning of 6 April General George Joulwan was received by the president of Romania, Ion Iliescu.
