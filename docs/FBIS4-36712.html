BFN [Text] Task forces will be constituted beginning today to tackle the ongoing insurgency problem in the seven northeastern states of the country. This was decided in a meeting between Mr. Rajesh Pilot and the chief ministers of Assam, Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim and the governor of Manipur in New Delhi this evening. Talking to Doordarshan correspondent V. Mira, the minister said that the situation will be reviewed after three months with the chief ministers of all seven states. [Begin recording] [Mira] How do you view the law and order situation in the northeastern states? [Rajesh Pilot] Well, today we had the meeting with all the honorable chief ministers of the northeast states. We had reviewed the situation. We had reviewed the implementation of these decisions and also we took further steps after reviewing the situation. What is further needed, we have also decided on those lines and I have requested them to go very hard way against the insurgents and their activities and what all help could be given from center. I have totally offered them that center is totally with them to curb the insurgency in northeastern region of our country. I feel that Assam, Manipur, Nagaland -- these are the three states which are slightly under a high pressure of insurgency at the moment. [end recording]
