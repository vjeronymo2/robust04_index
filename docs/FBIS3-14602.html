Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] A special envoy of Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat arrived in Amman this evening to brief Jordanian officials on the latest developments in the negotiations between the Palestinians and Israelis, which will resume in Cairo tomorrow. In a statement to the JORDANIAN NEWS AGENCY, Hakam Bal'awi said that his visit to Amman reaffirms the close relations between the Jordanian and Palestinian people, which has crossed the stage of cooperation and coordination to the stage of joint cohesion between the two peoples in the present and the future to face outside challenges. He added that he is carrying the visions and ideas of the Palestinian leadership toward the peace process, noting that it is concerned about briefing Jordan on all its moves in order to achieve a just, comprehensive, and durable peace that responds to the ambitions of the Palestinians people and the Arab nation. He said that he will brief the Jordanian leadership on the circumstances that preceded and followed UN Security Council Resolution 904, especially in terms of the PLO's concern about protecting the Palestinian people and maintaining the Islamic and Christian holy places in Jerusalem, the capital of the Palestinian state.
