Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Thailand and Argentina agreed to increase bilateral relations on economics, trade and investment and target to boost trading volume this year, according to a Foreign Ministry press statement released yesterday. The 1994 target was set at US$100-200 million per year from the current record of US$66 million, during the discussion held last Friday between visiting Foreign Minister Prasong Sunsiri and Argentine Acting Foreign Minister Armando Caro, the statement said. Thailand's imports from Argentina include cotton, steel, aluminium, computer hardware and military goods while exports are cloths, textiles, furniture and gems. During the meeting, the Thai side also proposed to form a mechanism to provide regular consultations between high-level officials of the two countries and exchanges of trade representatives and diplomatic officials, said the statement. In a separate discussion with Deputy Foreign Minister Ferdinand Enrique Petrella, Argentina briefed Sqn Ldr [Squadron Leader] Prasong on the Falklands dispute which escalated into a war between Argentina and the United Kingdom in 1981. Both sides exchanged opinions on various issues such as the negotiations of the Uruguay Round's General Agreement on Trade and Tariffs, the cooperation under the Cairns group, the Nuclear Non- Proliferation Treaty, and the activities of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation, the statement added.
