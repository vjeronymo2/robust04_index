940113 FT 13 JAN 94 / International Company News: US retailer increases pay-out Dayton Hudson, the Minneapolis-based department store operator which has acknowledged that the holiday periodbrought heavy promotions and sales of low-margin goods, sent a signal of confidence about its long-term earnings prospects by raising its dividend, Reuter reports from Chicago. The 5 per cent increase in the quarterly dividend to 42 cents a share comes amid a push by the retailer to turn around its suffering Mervyn's division, analysts said. California-based Mervyn's has been hard hit by the state's slow economic recovery, putting a drag on the parent's recent performance, analysts said.
