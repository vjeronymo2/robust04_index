920120 FT 20 JAN 92 / Observer: Purr-chase Observer is happy to report that the sale of London fashion display company Rootstein Hopkins to Tokyo counterpart Yoshichu Mannequins is now safely in the bag, despite the sellers' insistence on writing what might be called a claws clause into the contract. Sanwa Bank officials advising on the deal were worried that Adel and Richard Rootstein might put the purchasers' backs up by requiring them to guarantee company cats Harry and Honeybun a continued free run of the west London premises. But the flap proved unwarranted. 'They are here for life,' purred Mrs Rootstein, adding that when Yoshichu's Messrs Ogawa and Miyake arrived to inspect the purchase, 'Harry rushed to the door to welcome them, and they both immediately sat on their laps when they sat down.'
