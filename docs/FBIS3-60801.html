Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 25 Dec (MENA)--Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi has stressed that whoever gives any information leading to the discovery of terrorists will be immediately rewarded. A small piece of information that may appear insignificant to citizens may lead to important positive results for the security agencies, he said. Speaking after honoring three citizens who helped in the arrest of terrorist Sayyid Salah Sayyid Sulayman, who is suspected in the unsuccessful attempt to assassinate Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi, the minister praised the positive contribution of the Egyptian public. He said that the successful efforts of security forces at this stage depend on cooperation from the public. Al-Alfi said the enlightened Egyptian masses have realized that confronting the challenges facing the homeland requires unity against all criminal attempts against the security of the country and the safety of the citizens. [In a Cairo-datelined report, Paris AFP in English at 1642 GMT adds: "Three informers on Saturday shared a reward of $30,000 for the arrest of nine suspects in the attempted assassination of Egypt's Prime Minister 'Atif Sidqi."]
