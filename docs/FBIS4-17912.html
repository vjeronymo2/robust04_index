BFN [Report by KAZTAG correspondent Ivan Zakharchenko for TASS] [Text] Alma-Ata [Almaty] 27 April TASS -- Premier of the Chinese State Council Li peng said at a news conference in Alma-Ata devoted to the results of his visit to Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan that his talks with leaders of these countries were successful and contributed to further strengthening of friendship, mutual understanding and the expansion of economic, political and cultural ties. The Chinese premier pointed out that China accounts for the smallest number of weapons of mass destruction possessed by nuclear states. The existence of such weapons has a purely defensive character and China pledged not to be the first to use such weapons, the Chinese premier said, reminding of basic principles of the Chinese military and political doctrine. The Chinese premier pointed out, in particular, that a document on the inviolability of existing frontiers was signed between China and Kazakhstan for the first time. China expects to sign a similar document soon with Kyrgyzstan, Li Peng said. Touching upon Russo-Chinese relations, Li Peng described them as "useful and fruitful." Late on Wednesday the Chinese premier had a one-to-one meeting with Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev. On Thursday morning the Chinese premier and his entourage will leave Alma-Ata.
