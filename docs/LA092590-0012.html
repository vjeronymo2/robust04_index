September 25, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

$5-MILLION BAIL FOR KEATING

Recently, as I drove past my old Lincoln Savings branch, my mind wandered back to the days when I enjoyed just stopping by to view Abraham Lincoln relics on display in the lobby.

Somehow, it gave me a feeling of confidence to know that my savings were in the hands of those who revered the principles of honest dealings so closely identified with Honest Abe.

Now the place looks shabby and my vision of the care-worn face of Lincoln faded for a moment only to be replaced by the figures of Keating and Cranston. Of such experiences cynics are created and innocence is lost.

JOHN De ROO JR.

Granada Hills
