May 14, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CITYLINES: MORE TO WRIGHT MYTH

The interest in all things Frank Lloyd Wright continues. The latest book on who many consider America's greatest architect of the ages is "Frank Lloyd Wright: The Crowning Decade, 1949-1959, Selected, and With a Commentary, by Bruce Brooks Pfeiffer" (The Press at Cal State, Fresno: $22.95 hardback, $13.95 paperback.)

The thin book of large type and a selection of personal photographs focuses on the last 10 years of the architect's tumultuous life. Offered are views of Wright by the public through a selection of articles; Wright's own reflections; his wife's, drawn from her own personal papers; his daughter's, through interviews, and those of his apprentices.

Although when his widow, Olgivanna, is quoted as saying shortly before her death, "I ruled him, but I ruled him with love," it is perhaps more than we want to know. Nevertheless, the volume adds to the myth that was Wright, and is sure to be of interest to his faithful followers. Sam Hall Kaplan
