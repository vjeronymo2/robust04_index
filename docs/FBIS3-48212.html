Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 14 (YONHAP) -- Abolished in 1958, Labor Day will be celebrated in South Korea on May 1, 1995, for the first time in 36 years, the government and its party decided Friday. Labor Minister Nam Chae-hui, Democratic Liberal Party [DLP] Director General of Policy Coordination Paek Nam-chi, and other government and DLP officials agreed to revive Labor Day but put off revision of the law for a year to reflect upon the position of employers. The move to reform labor policy seems aimed at easing worker dissatisfaction on the eve of spring wage negotiations. Dissident labor organizations, but not the Federation of Korean Trade Unions (FKTU), have asked for a revival of Labor Day for 36 years. FKTU, which stuck to Worker's Day (March 10), officially decided to ask for the return of Labor Day in 1990. In spite of the delay, party officials say they expect the decision to revive Labor Day to be welcomed. President Syngman Yi abolished Labor Day in 1958 and replaced it with March 10, anniversary of the founding of the right-wing predecessor of FKTU, as Worker's Day.
