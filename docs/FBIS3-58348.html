Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Paris, Jan 11 (AFP) -- French Prime Minister [Edouard Balladur] said Tuesday [11 January] that the French economy "hit bottom in the last part" of last year, and that France must "do everything possible to slow the rise in unemployment." Speaking on the TF1 television channel, he also said that his government would see to it that there would be "no additional tax burden on the French people's income" in 1994. The French economy was in recession for most of last year, and the unemployment rate now stands at 12 percent. It is universally expected to rise further in the coming months. He had previously expressed the opinion that the economy has now come out of recession.
