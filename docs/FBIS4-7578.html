BFN [Text] Prague, March 31 (CTK) -- A privatization deal approved by Vladimir Meciar's government shortly before it was unseated by a vote of no-confidence deprived the state of about a billion Slovak crowns ($31 million), Premier Jozef Moravcik said on Slovak television on Wednesday, the Czech daily RUDE PRAVO reports today. Speaking in the weekly debate programme "Slobodny piatok," Moravcik, who succeeded Meciar as premier on March 16, said the outgoing government sold a ten percent stake in the East Slovak Steelworks, VSZ Kosice, to a private company at 20 percent of the nominal value. In the programme Moravcik was answering the questions of Slovak and foreign journalists for the first time since his appointment. He called the sale by Meciar's government immoral in view of the financial losses caused to Slovakia, RUDE PRAVO reports.
