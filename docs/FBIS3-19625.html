Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The transfer of 28 tons of arms and ammunition from the stranded Nigerian plane that has been at Larnaca Airport since Tuesday [15 March] to another plane of Tamesis Air which arrived by Stansted, England, via Amman, began under strict security measures from the airport's police force. The British cargo plane arrived at 1600 yesterday and was refueled immediately. The transfer of the arms and ammunition, which according to some reports included surface-to-air and air-to-air missiles, began two hours later. The Nigerian plane was then to depart for Tel Aviv to repair one of its four engines, which was the reason it had been stranded at Larnaca Airport. [passage omitted]
