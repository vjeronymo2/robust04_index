December 2, 1990, Sunday, Orange County Edition

COUNTY ISN'T COPING WITH DIVERSITY

Orange County is not doing a good job of handling diversity in its neighborhoods.

One reason is the coverage given to minorities often slants toward bad elements. The positive contributions that minorities like the De Silvas ("Vandals Leave Signs of Race Hatred," Nov. 20) bring are overlooked.

It is hard for warped, bigoted minds to grasp the idea that the minority come in all glorious colors. They are the law-abiding, true Americans. The superior attitudes often rampant in the county brings the criminal element crawling out like vermin.

VERA JACKSON, Corona
