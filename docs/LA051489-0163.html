May 14, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

THE WORLD

At least 11 Palestinians were shot and wounded in clashes with Israeli troops in the occupied West Bank and Gaza Strip, where the army reimposed a nightly curfew from 8 p.m. to 4 a.m. A full curfew, imposed on the entire Gaza Strip last Monday, remained in force at the Shati, Jabaliya and Nusseirat refugee camps and parts of the Rafah camp and Gaza City after demonstrations broke out there Friday. Hospital officials in the Gaza Strip reported eight shooting injuries from clashes in those camps and in Gaza City. In the West Bank, troops shot and wounded three Palestinians in the Nur esh Shams refugee camp in clashes after the army lifted a curfew for two hours, an army spokesman said. The army reimposed the curfew.
