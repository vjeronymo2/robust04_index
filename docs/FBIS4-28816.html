BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 14 KYODO -- Shoichiro Toyoda, chairman of the Federation of Economic Organizations (Keidanren), called Tuesday [14 Jun] on Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata for his cabinet's leadership in implementing deregulation and other administrative reform, officials said. "The cabinet should break the wall of vested interests and lead administrative reforms decisively," Toyoda told Hata in a meeting at the prime minister's official residence. Toyoda and Keidanren's Vice Chairman Isao Nakauchi asked Hata to set a target to halve the number of economic regulations in five years. He also called on Hata to map out a plan for scrapping and privatizing government-backed corporations. Hata responded that the reforms and deregulations are a national problem which must be tackled. At the day's cabinet meeting, Hata instructed ministers to display leadership in their ministries and agencies in compiling deregulation measures by the end of the month.
