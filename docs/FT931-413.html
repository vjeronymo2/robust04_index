930330 FT 30 MAR 93 / UK Company News: Edinburgh Fund Managers up 4.5% EDINBURGH FUND Managers announced a 4.5 per cent increase in pre-tax profits from Pounds 4.78m to Pounds 5m in the year to January 31. Funds under management at the group, which is 54 per cent owned by British Investment Trust, rose from Pounds 1.91bn to Pounds 2.39bn. Part of that increase was due to rising stock markets; Pounds 254m of net new money was attracted during the year. Since the end of January, EFM has acquired Target Trust Managers, with Pounds 110m under management. Over the weekend, EFM Dragon, an investment trust managed by the group, announced that its bid for Drayton Asia, a trust with Pounds 125m of assets, had gone unconditional. Private Fund Managers, the private client subsidiary, now has Pounds 235m under management and made its first significant profit contribution. Operating profit rose to Pounds 3.87m (Pounds 3.21m). Earnings fell to 18.1p (18.6p). The final dividend is increased to 8.5p, making a total of 13.5p (13p).
