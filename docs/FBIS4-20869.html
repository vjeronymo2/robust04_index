BFN [Text] Kiev [no date as received] -- According to Ukraine's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the joint Ukrainian-Japanese committee for cooperation in the elimination of Ukraine's nuclear arms held its first meeting on 21 March. UNIAN has already reported that this committee was set up in accordance with an agreement between the Governments of Ukraine and Japan on cooperation in nuclear arms elimination. Valeriy Pavlyukov, deputy minister of machine-building, military-industrial complex, and conversion was appointed to represent Ukraine on the board of the committee, and Shoji Suezawa, the Japanese ambassador to Ukraine, was appointed as Japan's representative. In accordance with Article 5 of the agreement, the Japanese side has appointed Japanese Foreign Ministry official Tojiuki Kawakami as technical secretariat director. At the meeting, the main targets of Japan's aid to Ukraine were discussed. The Japanese side emphasized that the Japanese Government will allocate funds for Ukraine's aid programs in strategic nuclear arms elimination before the end of March 1994. The amount of aid will be announced later. In the meantime, Ukraine is to put forward specific projects to be funded in the near future.
