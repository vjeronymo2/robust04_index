BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 11/5/1994 -- Referring to the first round of the elections recently held in Hungary, the spokesman for the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Mircea Geoana told a press conference on 11 May that "in order to have a complete and definitive picture of the Hungarian Parliament's composition, and, consequently, of the government to be appointed, we need to see the results of the second round. It is obvious however that the Socialist Party is heading for victory." About the Romanian-Hungarian relations once socialists accede to power, the Romanian Foreign Ministry spokesman said the socialists' statements in the pre-election period were already known. "They voiced a constructive approach the Socialist Party intended to have if, elections over, they gained access to the ruling structures," Mircea Geoana said. He also said that the Romanian side received such statements with interest and that "the Romanian authorities are waiting to see the outcome of the electoral process in Hungary, with a view to identifying, jointly with the new Hungarian Government, the concrete means to continue the negotiations on the framework political treaty, and on the bilateral relations as a whole." According to information furnished by the spokesman for the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Mircea Geoana, the president of the European Parliament, Egon Klepsch is expected on an official visit to Romania on 16-17 May this year at the invitation of the Romanian Parliament. The head of the European Parliament will have meetings with the speakers of the two houses of the Romanian Parliament, with members of the Foreign Policy Committees, and with the Romanian parliamentary delegation to the European Parliament. He will also meet with leaders of the parliamentary groups, the chairman of the Council for Economic Coordination, Strategy and Reform, and the Minister of Foreign Affairs. Egon Klepsch will deliver a speech before a joint sitting of parliament, and will be received by the president of Romania. The Foreign Ministry spokesman also informed the press that the president of the European Commission, Jacques Delors, will be the guest of Romania on 2-4 June, at the invitation of Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu. In the same line, the spokesman announced that the European Parliament approved in its plenary meeting the recommendation of the European Commission for a ECU 125,000,000 credit to Romania, representing the quota underwritten by the European Union from the $300,000,000 loan officially applied for by the Romanian Government to the G-24 with the purpose of compensating the balance of payments and financing the economic reform over 1993-1994.
