940318 FT 18 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Edmond hit by higher provision An increased provision of Pounds 1.65m against land holdings left Edmond Holdings, the Northampton-based housebuilder, with higher pre-tax losses of Pounds 1.89m for the 1993 year on turnover lower at Pounds 11m. The comparative figures showed land provisions of Pounds 322,000 and pre-tax losses of Pounds 1.15m on turnover of Pounds 12.6m. Mr Andrew Naish, chairman, said the second half had seen an improvement for the first time in five years, resulting in a profit for the period of Pounds 61,000 before provisions and tax. The company said that in view of the better trading and its underlying strength it was proposing to maintain the final dividend at 0.15p for a total of 0.3p (0.5p). Losses per share were 3.1p (1.5p).
