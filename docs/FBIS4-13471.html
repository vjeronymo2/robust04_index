BFN [Report by 'Uthman Mirghani: "Demonstrations Against `High Prices'; Forces Deployed in Medani"] [Excerpt] London -- Well-informed Sudanese sources have said that Medani, the second largest city in Sudan after Khartoum, has witnessed widespread demonstrations against high prices and living conditions, and that Central Security and Army troops have been deployed in the streets to guard the main sensitive sites in the city. These domestic developments coincided with reports of the abduction of Sudanese Ambassador to Mogadishu 'Abd-al-Baqi Muhammad Hasan yesterday by gunmen while he was on his way to his office from his residence. With regard to the demonstrations in Medani, south of Khartoum, the sources said that the demonstrations started the day before yesterday and continued yesterday. University and high school students and a number of workers and employees took part in the demonstrations. There have been no reports of casualties. However, sources told AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT that the demonstrations were noisy and violent. Medani has over the past months witnessed widespread demonstrations against living conditions, during which demonstrators burned some installations, banks, and gas stations. The security forces' intervention led to clashes and casualties. There have also been reports of demonstrations in al-Qadarif city in east Sudan. Meanwhile, the Sudanese Government made contacts with the various Somali parties yesterday to secure the release of Ambassador 'Abd-al-Baqi Muhammad Hasan, 48, who was abducted by three gunmen.
