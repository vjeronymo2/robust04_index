BFN [Text] Washington, May 18 (XINHUA) -- U.S. President Bill Clinton today announced his plan to name three U.S. officials to be members of the Commission on Security and Cooperation in Europe. The nominees are John Shattuck, assistant secretary of defense for democracy, human rights and labor; Ashton Carter, assistant secretary of state for nuclear security and counter-proliferation and a former vice president of Harvard University; and Charles Meissner, assistant secretary of commerce for international economic policy, White House sources said. Clinton also announced his plan to nominate Robert Pastor, a Latin American specialist at the Carter Center in Atlanta, to be U.S. ambassador to Panama. Pastor, 47 and a university professor of political science, has been director of the center's Latin American and Caribbean program since 1986. He has also served as executive secretary of the Council of Freely Elected Heads of Government, a group of 23 leaders in the Western Hemisphere, which has monitored elections in eight Latin American nations. Pastor was director of Latin American and Caribbean affairs for the National Security Council during President Jimmy Carter's administration.
