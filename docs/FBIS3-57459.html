Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Semen Ivanov] [Text] Moscow January 25 TASS -- Russia successfully launched the "Meteor-3" meteorological satellite early on Tuesday after the blast off was postponed for three times. The satellite carries the German "Tubsat" minisatellite and is fitted out with the German "Prare" system and the Russian-French "Skarab" equipment. The satellite which blasted off from the Russian Plesetsk cosmodrome onboard the "Tsiklon" booster is intended for round-the-clock processing and transmission of hydrometeorological information, as well as of data on radioactivitity in near-earth space and the state of magnetosphere and the ozone layer, TASS was told at the press service of the Russian aerospace forces.
