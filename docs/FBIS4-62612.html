BFN [Text] RIGA, May 16, BNS - The Latvian defense and other security-related officials met with a U.S. delegation Saturday [14 May] to discuss the further bilateral cooperation in the security sphere. The Clinton administration has asked the U.S. Congress to extend financial resources to support the projected Baltic Peacekeeping Battalion, Charles Freeman, U.S. deputy defense secretary, told reporters after the meeting. If the funding is allotted, it will be used for training and equipment purchase, Freeman said. Latvian Defense Minister Valdis Pavlovskis said the meeting also touched on Latvia's involvement in NATO's Partnership for Peace program, Latvian defense forces reorganization and potential U.S. technical assistance to defense forces and Home Guard. The foreign assistance to the Latvian defense forces should be based on clearcut plans and programs, said Pavlovskis. American specialists mentioned the lack of uniform armament and provision system as the main problem for the setup of efficient Baltic defense forces. The Baltic military commands will try to settle the problem, Pavlovskis said. The U.S. delegation also toured the Latvian naval base at Bolderaja and reconnaissance battalion at Suzi.
