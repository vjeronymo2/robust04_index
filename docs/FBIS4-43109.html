BFN [Text] Ankara, June 11 (AA) -- Sixteen members from an outlawed organization fighting against the regime in Iran were captured in Ankara, it was reported on Saturday [11 June]. AA reporter learned that the members of `People's Mujaheddins' [Mojahedin-e Khalq] were captured after operations carried out in three different safe-houses in the city, following tip-offs. Their request for political asylum was refused by the Interior Ministry and they were sent to a camp in southeastern Silopi town to be later expelled. A high-level official said that Turkey will not allow any organization working against its neighbours within its territories, adding likewise the terrorist PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] organization should not be allowed to work in Iran. "This latest operation is a show of our good-will," he added.
