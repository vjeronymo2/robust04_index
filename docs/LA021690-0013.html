February 16, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

LIMITING TERMS OF ELECTED OFFICIALS

Now, Henry, you know very well that incumbents cannot lose once they have entrenched themselves long enough to amass a war chest and build a political barter system.

You also know that many non-voters are not satisfied with the representation they have; they are just convinced that the incumbent cannot be defeated because he has the support of the moneyed special interest groups.

Most Americans have faith in democracy. It's today's politicians they mistrust. So the movements to limit tenure.

MARGARET W. ROMANI

Los Angeles
