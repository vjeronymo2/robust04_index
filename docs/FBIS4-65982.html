BFN [Announcer-read report] [Text] Turkey has welcomed the fact that the UN International Maritime Organization accepted the package of rules and proposals to enhance the safety of passages through the Istanbul and Canakkale Straits. A Foreign Ministry statement noted that regulating the traffic in the straits will benefit all interested parties. The statement remarked that the new statute pertaining to the passage through the straits was prepared in line with basic international rules of law, which stipulate that every country can determine the procedures in the waters under its own sovereignty in order to ensure maritime safety and environmental protection. This statute will go into effect on 1 July 1994, the statement noted, adding that the new statute will continue to adhere meticulously to the Montreux Convention, as was the case in the past.
