Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [Text] While attending the Second Session of the Eighth National People's Congress [NPC] in Beijing, Jia Qinglin, Fujian Provincial CPC Committee secretary and Fujian governor, said: Thanks to the deep-going reform, an export-oriented economic structure is gradually taking shape in Fujian. As one of the country's pioneers in respect of opening up to the outside world, Fujian has built a large number of special economic, economic and technological development, coastal open cities and open development, Taiwan investment, bonded, high and new technological and industrial development, state-level tourism and vacation, and urban construction and development zones invested and managed by foreign entrepreneurs. Fujian has now become one of the most advanced provinces and regions in the whole country in terms of reform scale, scope, and policy coverage. By the end of last year, Fujian had approved the establishment of nearly 13,000 foreign-funded enterprises, absorbed more than $5.98 billion of foreign capital, and put into operation more than 5,700 enterprises run with three types of capital [foreign, Overseas Chinese, and both Chinese and foreign capital]. Jia Qinglin stated: Last year, Fujian's domestic gross national product registered a 25.4 percent increase over the previous year, thereby attaining the goal of quadrupling the gross national product one year ahead of schedule. Moreover, Fujian's financial revenue topped 100 million yuan for the first time. Thanks to the fast growing export-oriented economy, Fujian witnessed benign economic growth in 1993. Jia Qinglin noted: This year, despite the introduction of new tax and unified exchange rate systems, Fujian's export-oriented economy has displayed an incessantly strong momentum and vitality. The contracted amount of foreign investment and actual amount of foreign investment in the province's newly approved enterprises run with three types of capital in the January-February period totalled more than $1.33 billion and $380 million respectively, representing a 11.2 percent increase and a 49.5 percent increase over the same period of last year. Moreover, the province's total export volume in the January-February period reached $840 million, representing a 44.1 percent increase over the same period of last year.
