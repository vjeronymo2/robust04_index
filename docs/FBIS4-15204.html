BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow April 6 TASS -- Russian Prime-Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin today met the leaders of the United Technologies Company, U.S. to discuss aspects of a series production of the Russian wide-body airliner IL-96M with American Pratt & Whitney engines. The discussion centered around the organisation of joint Russian-American enterprise on the basis of AO Permskiye Motoriy (Perm Engines Joint-Stock Company) for the mass production of an aviation engine. The new engine, based on the Russian PS-90 will be installed on the IL-96M liners capable of carrying 380 passengers and on other new Russian aircraft. The Russian-American engine will be built using the best technologies designed in the two countries. Ten IL-96M airliners equipped with jointly produced engines have already been ordered by 10 foreign and 30 Russian airlines. The new engines are expected to be considerably cheaper and technologically more advanced than the Pratt & Whitney engines.
