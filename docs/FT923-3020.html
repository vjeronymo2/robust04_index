920915 FT 15 SEP 92 / Actuaries urged to act on marketing BRITAIN'S actuarial profession was urged yesterday to help tackle the marketing crisis affecting the life assurance industry. Mr Roger Laker of Ernst & Young, the accountancy firm, said actuaries had been 'too reluctant to intrude into an area of business which is traditionally run by others'. The annual joint convention of the English and Scottish actuarial bodies in Harrogate was told that on average 45 per cent of sales staff left each year while only 30 per cent of new recruits lasted more than 12 months.
