April 11, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

AUSTRIA;

$3 MILLION STOLEN IN RECORD TRAIN HEIST

Train robbers shot and killed a postal worker and stole $3 million in Austrian currency from a mail car in what police said was the biggest heist in the history of Austria's postal service. The robbery near Vienna prompted postal authorities to post a $25,600 reward for the three thieves. The bandits stole moneybags containing about 35 million Austrian schillings before walking off the train unnoticed when it stopped in the suburb of Huetteldorf.
