920914 FT 14 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Berkertex receivers sell Fifth Avenue to managers THE RECEIVERS at Berkertex Group, the bridalwear manufacturer and retailer, have moved quickly to secure the sale of one of its largest subsidiaries, Fifth Avenue, a leading supplier to the fashion mail order catalogue trade. Mr Paul Fabian and Mr Brian Worth, two of the company's managers, are staging a buy-out preserving 31 jobs. This move is backed by 3i, the venture capital group, and Barclays Bank. The purchase price was not disclosed but it 'substantially exceeded the company's break-up value', the receivers said. Mr Christopher Morris, the receiver from Touche Ross, said Fifth Avenue was profitable but had been dragged down by the collapse last Friday of its heavily-indebted parent company. Last year Fifth Avenue's sales wereover Pounds 13m and in the current year are running at more than Pounds 9m. Receivers have been criticised recently for selling businesses to existing managers before the assets recoverable for creditors can be fully assessed. But Mr Morris defended a quick sale in this particular case. 'This was an evaporating business. By Monday or Tuesday of next week there would have been nothing left to sell.'
