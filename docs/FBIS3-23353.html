Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Hague, 21 Mar (EFE) -- The Dutch news agency ANP in Paramaribo has reported that members of a guerrilla group seized the hydroelectric plant in Afobaka hours ago and they are currently holding 30 hostages. According to an unidentified spokesman of the group called the "Liberation Front of Suriname," access roads to the hydroelectric plant have been mined in a 7-km radius. The hydroelectric dam supplies energy to the country's capital, located 100 km to the north. According to ANP, the guerrilla group is holding 30 employees of Suralco, a bauxite company, hostage and they are demanding a "decentralization of power" and the resignation of the Surinamese Government.
