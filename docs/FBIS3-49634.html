Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Text] The highest Czech representatives -- the president, prime minister, and foreign minister -- hosted dinners for their Visegrad Group countries' counterparts. The foreign ministers, meeting at a dinner in the Cernin Palace, agreed that the Partnership for Peace is the first step toward integrating the Visegrad Group states into NATO, and that in the current conditions the security guarantees provided by the project are adequate. The Czech foreign minister confirmed this in an interview given to Czech Radio: [Begin Zieleniec recording] I think that the Partnership for Peace is quite a step toward integrating our country into NATO and toward creating new security structures in Europe. We all know, not only from foreign politics but from everyday life, that when several partners negotiate then usually none of them immediately achieves everything he wants. On the other hand, the preparation of this project has taught us an important lesson. From the moment the idea was first conceived, we have achieved substantial and truly important changes, through which an initially very raw and purely military-technological project has become something possessing a significant political dimension, something that can be interpreted only as a step toward integrating central European countries into the NATO security system. [end recording]
