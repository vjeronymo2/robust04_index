Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] More than 20 people were killed and scores others wounded in violent clashes between groups of money changers and special forces affiliated with tyrant Saddam's son 'Udayy in the money changers' market near Baghdad International Fair in al-Mansur suburb of Baghdad. This was reported by arrivals from Baghdad in Iraqi Kurdistan in the past two days. They said the clashes took place when the money changers refused to observe the regime's recent instructions banning individuals from dealing in currency in the Baghdad money market and restricting this activity to a group of brokers working directly on behalf of 'Udayy.
