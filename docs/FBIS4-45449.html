California, Sinaloa 94WB0044B Mexico City EXCELSIOR in Spanish 20 May 94 pp 1-A-3, 4-A-3 94WB0044B Mexico City EXCELSIOR Spanish CSO [Article by Rafael Medina Cruz] [Text] Yesterday, with reconnaissance, inspection, and detection flights, the air teams from the Attorney General's Office [PGR] discovered two marijuana and poppy plantations in Baja California and Sinaloa States. Presumed drug trafficking groups were growing over 200,000 drug plants on these plantations. The confiscation of farm implements, fuel, gun loaders, and useful cartridges was also announced. No arrests were made. Reports sent to Mexico City by Arturo Ochoa Palacios, the PGR representative in Baja California, state that, on the boundary of the communities of Real del Castillo and Viejo Enecua, in the municipality of Mexicali, a marijuana plantation on approximately 15,000 square meters was discovered. Mario Ruiz Massieu, assistant attorney for local offices and inspection, was informed that, on that expanse, 7,500 seedbeds were located, each with 25 plants, ready to be transplanted to solid ground. Found on the site, adjoining the Ojos Negros highway, were picks, shovels, hoes, machetes, axes, a jug containing gasoline, two 22-caliber rifles, and useful cartridges. Continuing the investigation, agents under orders from Rodolfo Garcia Gaxiola, the PJF [Federal Judicial Police] deputy commissioner, also confiscated a pickup truck with state of California registration 2JDE320, and a filled loader for a 7.62 caliber rifle. These items were left at the social representative's disposal. Moreover, Luis Antonio Ibanez Cornejo, the PGR's representative in Sinaloa, reported that, in the hamlet called Las Playitas, in the municipality of Sinaloa de Leyva, a mixed (marijuana and poppy) plantation was located. The 21 drug crops being grown there were destroyed manually and incinerated by federal agents. It was added that the community had participated in the discovery of those crops, since it reported in a telephone call that groups of persons acting suspiciously were often meeting on that site. This prompted the Federal Judicial Police to intervene, resulting in the destruction of the illicit plants. The PJF deputy commissioner in this state, Eduardo Salazar Carrillo, informed the force's general director, Adrian Carrera Fuentes, that hoes, shovels, fumigation pumps, fertilizers, and insecticides were also discovered and seized on that site. The PGR stressed in conclusion that, in both cases, the investigations are being continued to identify and capture the crop owners, as well as those who provided their services to plant and produce the drugs.
