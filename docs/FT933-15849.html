930707 FT 07 JUL 93 / Bombs seized in South Armagh one weighing 2,000lb and the other 800lb - were seized in Northern Ireland yesterday after police chased a gang for three miles through the South Armagh countryside. One man was arrested. The chase happened just 12 hours after the centre of Newtownards in Co Down was devastated by a bomb placed in a van. Nineteen people were injured but none badly. Sir Patrick Mayhew, Northern Ireland secretary, denied last night that the IRA was succeeding. 'The IRA do not, as yet, realise that they are bound to fail, though upon many of them that truth is dawning,' he said. He also hit out at Ulster critics of security policies. He said: 'Consider if we were to match the brutality of the IRA with our own actions and persuade those who work for us to do the same. That would take us into a cesspit as noisome as the one the IRA themselves inhabit.'
