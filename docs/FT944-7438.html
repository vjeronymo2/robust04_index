941124 FT 24 NOV 94 / International Company News: Mexico approves five licenses for foreigners Mexico's finance ministry yesterday approved licenses for five new foreign financial institutions planning to operate in the country, AP-DJ reports from Mexico City. The government last month issued a first round of licenses to 50 foreign banks, brokerages and insurance companies. The ministry on Wednesday also granted licenses to 11 limited financial groups; five leasing companies, five factoring companies and a foreign exchange house. Those receiving approval include GE Capital, Caterpillar, Chrysler Credit, and Ford Credit. The latter three are expected to do business in tractor and automobile and lending and leasing. Deutsche Bank received a license to operate a foreign exchange house.
