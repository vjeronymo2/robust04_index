BFN [Text] Cairo, 14 Apr (MENA)--President Muhammad Husni Mubarak is following events in Rwanda with grave concern and deep regret, especially because of the hardships faced by the fraternal Rwandan people as security deteriorates. President Mubarak is motivated by close Egyptian-Rwandan ties and his position as chairman of the OAU. The remarks came in a statement issued by the Egyptian Foreign Ministry today. The statement announced that Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa communicated with OAU Secretary General Salim Ahmed Salim on the possible steps the OAU can take to contain the deteriorating situation in Rwanda. The statement reported that the committee in charge of solving inter-African disputes will meet in Addis Ababa today chaired by an Egyptian representative to discuss the dangerous events in Rwanda. The statement continued: The Government of the Arab Republic of Egypt appeals to all Rwandan parties to exercise self-restraint, accept an immediate cease-fire, and create the appropriate conditions for relief organizations to carry out their humanitarian mission. It also urges these parties to immediately resume their efforts to implement the Arosha Agreement so as to attain stability and peace in the country. The Egyptian ambassador and Embassy staff in Kigali have been instructed to leave Rwanda temporarily in light of the dangers they are facing there and the extreme deterioration in security.
