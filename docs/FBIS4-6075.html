BFN [Text] Ministry of Foreign Affairs [MOFA] spokesman Terusuke Terada issued a statement on 15 April supporting the South Korean policy which was announced by Yi Yong-tok, deputy prime minister and concurrently chief of the National Unification Board, on the same day in connection with North Korea's (DPRK's) suspected nuclear weapon development. In announcing the policy, Deputy Prime Minister Yi stated that South Korea would not seek the exchange of special envoys between South Korea and North Korea any more, and called for South-North dialogue to pave the way for implementing mutual inspections of their military facilities. In his statement, spokesman Terada said: "We highly appreciate the policy because it aims at promoting dialogue between South and North and seeking a solution to the situation through such dialogue, in line with a presidential statement issued by the UN Security Council."
