Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Algiers, Jan 26 (AFP) -- Algerian former foreign minister Abdelaziz Bouteflika has turned down a proposal to become the country's president for a three-year transitional period, an official spokesman said Wednesday [26 Jan]. Bouteflika, 59, who served under the 1965-78 presidency of Houari Boumediene, had been mooted as the man most likely to take up the post, with the approval of the armed forces. Abdelkadar Bensalah, spokesman for a national conference organised by the government but boycotted by all the main political parties, announced that the gathering, meeting for the second and final day, "has been informed of this refusal."
