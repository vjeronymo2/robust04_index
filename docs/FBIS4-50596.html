BFN [Text] Xining, May 19 (XINHUA) -- Northwest China's Qinghai Province had exported 35.81 million U.S. dollars-worth of commodities by May 10, an increase of 39.5 percent compared with the corresponding period of last year. The increase rate is also higher than the national growth rate of 22 percent for exports in the past four months, said local trade officials. Meanwhile, imports in the same period were worth 11.92 million U.S. dollars, a decrease of 40,000 U.S. dollars over the same period of last year. The officials attributed the growth in exports in the first four months of this year to the implementation of reform measures in foreign trade. They said they believed that the unification of foreign exchange rates and other foreign trade reform measures have provided favorable conditions for the development of enterprises engaged in foreign trade and have brought the enthusiasm of these enterprises into full play.
