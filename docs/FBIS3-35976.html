Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Georgiy Shmelyov] [Text] Moscow February 1 TASS -- Bosnian peace process goes on is the opinion of Bosnian settlement mediators Lord Owen and Thorvald Stoltenberg, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev said here today after meeting the co-chairmen of the international London conference on former Yugoslavia. "The Russian initiative follows the same channel," he stressed. The meeting discussed additional cooperation measures to prevent further escalation of regional violence, keep the Geneva negotiations process going and achieve Bosnian settlement. The sides also focuses on a new Russian initiative that presupposes boosted efforts of the Security Council in order to develop the peace process, said Kozyrev. Speaking of possible air raids in Bosnia, Kozyrev said in principle this measure is not excluded within the framework of Security Council resolutions but "it is an extreme step". The Russian foreign minister noted the need for additional consultations of the U.N. Security Council's permanent members and expressed the hope the raids will not happen. The co-chairmen departed for Geneva after a brief working visit to Moscow.
