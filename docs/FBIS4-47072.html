CSO [Unattributed article under "Gorstat Reports" rubric: "On the Change in the Prices for Consumer Goods and Paid Services in the First Four Months of 1994"] [Text] The prices for consumer goods and services continue to rise. They are rising faster for foodstuffs and have tripled since the beginning of the year. For nonfood products, they increased by a factor of 2.7. The cost of a selection of food items made up of 33 vitally necessary products rose by a factor of 2.7 in April in comparison with December and amounted to 427 tenge. The largest increase in consumer prices was registered for dairy products (by a factor of 7.2), which increased in price as follows: milk by a factor of 5.5, cream and sour cream by 10, curds by 9.6, eggs by 5.6, poultry by 4.9, and mutton by a factor of 4.3. There was a noticeable increase in the cost of nonfood products: clothing and footwear by a factor of 2.7, office supplies by 6.1, and refrigerators, washing machines, and televisions by a factor of 2. The prices for medical compounds increased by a factor of 4.5. All kinds of paid services became more expensive. There was a sharp increase in prices in April. For the first four months of the year, the prices for passenger transport services increased by a factor of 4.5, services of a legal nature and for banking institutions by 5.6, housing services by 5.2, and paid public health services by a factor of 3.9. For the first four months of 1994, the index of the change in consumer prices, which in international practice is considered to be an indicator of inflation, amounted to 294.1 percent for the city of Almaty.
