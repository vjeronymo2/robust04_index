January 8, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

NEW BOOKS: AROUND HOME;

THE BOOK OF FRESH FLOWERS BY MALCOLM HILLIER (SIMON & SCHUSTER; 1988)

THE LOGICAL SUCCESSOR to "The Book of Dried Flowers," which Hillier co-wrote with Colin Hilton, "Fresh Flowers" is illustrated with stunning photos of flower arrangements the like of which we hardly ever see at the neighborhood florist. But what makes this book several cuts above the usual pretty coffee-table entry are the pages of photos and descriptions of individual flowers. Because this is a British book, some of the flowers will be unavailable -- or outrageously expensive -- in Southern California. Of course, one can always attempt to grow one's own. ($29.95) Judith Sims
