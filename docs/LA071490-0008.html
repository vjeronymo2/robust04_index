July 14, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

STATE'S MEDFLY SPRAYING POLICY

My family and I feel we are in a nightmare from which there is no end. We have been sprayed 12 times with malathion and now we are scheduled to be sprayed three more times, probably all summer.

I have a 6-month-old son and a 3-year-old daughter, and my greatest fear for their health is from our own state government! Our despair, anger and disgust are overwhelming.

California is cutting back funds for trauma centers, schools, the disabled and the blind -- but it blithely spends $100 million for pesticides and helicopters! We have a drought in Los Angeles and can't wash our cars or water our yards without getting a fine, but the state can dump all the pesticides it wants on those same yards and cars.

California's shortfall is not in money -- it's in morality.

JOHN VORNHOLT

Los Angeles
