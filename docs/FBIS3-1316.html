Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Rival Somali clan leaders General Mohamed Farah Aidid and Ali Mahdi Mohamed today signed an agreement to end the civil war in Somalia. They will also try to establish a central government. The two leaders signed the reconciliation agreement after two weeks of talks in Nairobi. UN officials said that the announcement of the declaration is a breakthrough in the Somali peace talks, which had been deadlocked for months. The Somali leaders said they will implement their declaration on the restoration of peace in Somalia. [Begin recording] [Aidid, in Somali] Our country has suffered destruction. [Unidentified translator, in English] We all know that a very heavy tragedy and destruction faced our country. The only way to finish off that problem for Somali people is to solve the differences which have been caused by the civil war. [Ali Mahdi, in English] Presence of the Somali leaders here today is a clear manifestation of the closing and the (?harsh) of historical chapter and the opening of a new [words indistinct] enthusiasm and hope which will permit us to address seriously on the issues of our national reconciliation process.
