BFN [Text] Helsinki, June 20 (AFP) -- Finnish Aid Minister Toimi Kankaanniemi submitted his resignation Monday [20 June] to President Martti Ahtisaari after differences of opinion over Finland's European Union [EU] membership. Prime Minister Esko Aho earlier Monday denied Kankaanniemi the right to vote against the government during a ballot to name representatives to sign the country's EU membership agreement this weekend in Corfu. Kankaanniemi was the Christian Party's only representative in the government. It is not yet known whether the party will now leave the government or whether a new minister will be named.
