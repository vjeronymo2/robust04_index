920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / World Trade News: US-Israeli chip venture National Semiconductor, the US semiconductor chip manufacturer, has agreed to work with the Israeli government and Israeli companies to establish a joint venture company in Israel, writes Louise Kehoe in San Francisco. The joint venture would take over the existing National Semiconductor facility in Migdal Haemek in Israel and develop an independent semiconductor manufacturing business. National Semiconductor would own 19.9 per cent of the proposed entity, while the government of Israel would own 26 per cent. A consortium of Israeli electronics companies would own the remaining 54.1 per cent. In a joint statement, the partners said they are enthusiastic about the prospects for this venture, and for the opportunities it presents to contribute to the technological leadership of the local electronics industry in Israel.
