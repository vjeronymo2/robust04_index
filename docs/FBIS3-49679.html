Language: Slovene Article Type:BFN [Report by Irena Majce] [Text] Last night's optimistic and somewhat mysterious statement by Croatian Economic Minister Nadan Vidosevic, at Otocec, that problems regarding the nuclear power plant will be solved in a few days and that Croatia would get electricity in conformity with the technical possibilities of the power plant, was finally cleared up by today's statement by the main Croatian duty official at Krsko, Jakov Sinovcic. Sinovcic said, among other things, the following: [Begin Sinovcic recording in Serbo-Croatian] Whatever is produced at any moment, the Croatian electricity board is going to take 50 percent of. [end recording] Therefore, Croatia plans to help itself from the system, irrespective of the Slovene Government bans. This is technically possible, but since our duty officers can register such unauthorized use, it is obvious that this will be in violation of the agreed exchange.
