May 31, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

GOP CANDIDATES FOR HILL'S ASSEMBLY SEAT RELY HEAVILY ON OWN FUNDS

Eight Republicans competing for the state Assembly seat formerly occupied by Frank Hill (R-Whittier), now a state senator, have raised more than $500,000 for their campaigns, but most of it has come out of their own pockets.

Diamond Bar Councilman Paul Horcher has loaned his campaign $171,815, according to a financial statement he filed with the Los Angeles County Registrar of Voters. Others who have loaned large amounts to their own campaigns, according to their financial statements, are Wil Baca, an engineer and businessman, who has put up more than $50,000; Phil Mautino, an attorney, who has kicked in $46,000; and Kenneth Manning, a general contractor and school board member, who has loaned himself $12,500.

Manning, Mautino and former legislative staff member Tony Russo are the only candidates in the district who have raised more than $40,000 from sources other than their own personal funds.

Russo said new campaign financing rules have made fund raising difficult. Voters adopted an initiative two years ago that puts a $1,000 ceiling on donations from individuals.

In addition, most political action committees representing businesses, unions and trade associations have stayed out of the 52nd District race, candidates said, because it is so wide open and they prefer to back likely winners.

Horcher said he has poured a lot of his own money into the campaign because he started raising funds late. "If I'm not willing to invest in myself, I can't ask others to," Horcher said.

The San Gabriel Valley on Tuesday will vote on eight Assembly seats. Five incumbents are running unopposed in the primary but will face challenges in the November elections. The other races are contested.

They include the battle to replace Hill in the Assembly. In addition, voters will pick successors to Charles M. Calderon (D-Whittier), who resigned his 59th Assembly District seat after winning election to the state Senate, and to Charles Bader (R-Pomona), who is giving up his 65th Assembly District seat to run against Sen. Ruben S. Ayala (D-Chino) in November.

In the 59th District, Marta Maestas, who was Calderon's aide, and Xavier Becerra, a state deputy attorney general, have both raised more than $80,000.

Donations to the Maestas campaign include $5,000 each from the California Medical Assn. and the California Teachers Assn. Becerra's total includes $9,000 he has loaned to his own campaign at 10% interest.

Diane Martinez, a member of the Garvey school board, has raised more than $67,000, including $1,000 from her father, Rep. Matthew G. Martinez (D-Monterey Park), and another $1,000 from his campaign committee. Other $1,000 contributions have come from the campaign committees of Reps. Mel Levine (D-Santa Monica), Henry A. Waxman (D-Los Angeles), Howard L. Berman (D-Panorama City) and Tom Lantos (D-San Mateo.) Martinez has put nearly $40,000 of her own money into her campaign.

Financial statements for two other candidates in the Democratic primary, Bill Edward Hernandez, a member of the Rio Hondo Community College board of trustees, and Larry Salazar, a marketing consultant, were not on file Tuesday at the county Registrar of Voters office.

In the 65th Assembly District, Jim Brulte, who served as Bader's chief of staff and has his endorsement, has a wide lead in fund raising over his Republican primary opponent, Mark A. T. Nymeyer, a Pomona councilman. Brulte has raised more than $150,000. His largest donation was $3,000 from the California Real Estate Assn. Nymeyer has raised more than $44,000, including $1,000 from a committee of Pomona firefighters and $1,000 from Pomona City Atty. Arnold Glasman.

The totals raised for the Assembly primary campaigns are dwarfed by the amounts that Ayala and Bader have raised for their 34th Senate District contest in November. Ayala has collected more than $187,000 so far this year, giving him more than $450,000 for his reelection bid. Bader has raised more than $148,000 this year, boosting his campaign fund total to more than $355,000. Both men are running unopposed for their respective parties' nominations in Tuesday's primary. Most of the district is in San Bernardino County, but it includes part of Pomona.
