Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 22 (XINHUA) -- The 19th meeting of the Sino-Portuguese Joint Liaison Group (JLG) opened here today. During the four-day meeting, the two sides will continue to discuss three major issues relating to the transitional period in Macao, namely: the current developments in the official status of the Chinese Language, the localization of laws and the localization of the civil service. Meanwhile, their discussion will also center on issues concerning civil service pensions, the participation of the Bank of China in currency issuing in Macao and the signing of an air transport agreement between Macao and a foreign company, as well as other issues related to stability and development in Macao. Taking part in the meeting are chief representative of the Chinese side of the Sino-Portuguese JLG Guo Jiading, and Fernando Andresen Guimaraes, chief representative of the Portuguese side of the JLG. The previous JLG meeting was held in Macao last November.
