Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Stas Stremidlovskiy report: "Shakhray and Shokhin, Unlike Mankind, Wave a Cheerless Farewell to the Past"] [Text] Since the appearance of the famous Russian Federation edict on reforming the Russian Government (with a view to contracting it), vice premiers have undoubtedly lived though some worrying days: Who will remain, and who will be wiped out as a class? KOMSOMOLSKAYA PRAVDA's correspondent put this question to two vice premiers who are also State Duma deputies -- Sergey Shakhray, and Aleksandr Shokhin: Will they continue to work in the government, or will they concentrate fully on legislation? Shakhray: I have not yet received an offer to join the new government. Shokhin: I have not yet decided. I am prepared to move into a leading position in parliament. This parliament is terribly cumbersome and complex, and the more people with experience of government work in it, the better. Where can I work? Anywhere, from speaker to committee chairman. Provided it is an influential committee, of course -- for instance, the committee for the budget, or international affairs. The latter in particular, because it would be undesirable to see Zhirinovskiy move in there....
