940421 FT 21 APR 94 / Letters to the Editor: Irrelevance of share options Sir, I have read with interest the various articles and letters over the past few months on the subject of share options. Although the coverage has been virtually comprehensive, there are a couple of vital points which have been overlooked. The first is that share options have for some years been cynically regarded as 'a gamble to which executives were entitled'. I think it is now questionable whether, in fact, share options are necessarily any more a relevant method of reward. They obviously had a place when executives had been highly taxed and found it almost impossible to amass capital during their business career but now, like long contracts and over-generous severance payments, they may no longer deserve their place. Second, if parity between executives and shareholders in the ownership of their company is sought, then perhaps the old-fashioned route of loans to purchase shares at full value and freedom to sell and buy to suit the executive's personal circumstances might not be a bad way of ensuring involvement. Outstanding company performance may be better rewarded by cash bonuses paid over the long term and directly and visibly linked to that performance. Nigel Dyckhoff, 16 Connaught Place, London W2 2ED
