Language: Armenian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Armenian Vice President Gagik Arutyunyan today received General Andrey Nikolayev, commander in chief of Russian border troops. The Armenian presidential press service reported that the Armenian vice president and the commander in chief of the border troops examined matters relating to the joint defense of state borders. They specifically discussed the enlistment of Armenian citizens in military service in the Russian border guard units stationed in Armenia, the joint defense of borders, and matters pertaining to legal procedures regulating the joint defense of the borders. Armenian Prime Minister Grant Bagratyan today also received General Andrey Nikolayev, commander in chief of Russian border troops. The prime minister stressed the importance of the signed documents on the enlistment of Armenian citizens in the Russian border troops and considered it a significant step in the development of Armenian-Russian cooperation. Matters relating to the joint defense of Armenia's state borders, including the financial, economic, and technical aspects, were discussed during the meeting. Vazgen Sargisyan [Armenian state minister and commander of border guards] and Andrey Nikolayev signed an agreement at noon today.
