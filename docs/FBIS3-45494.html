Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 15 (XINHUA) -- A third round of negotiations on border issues was held here January 4 to 14 between China and a joint delegation from Russia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan. The latter four countries are all members of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS). The two sides reported positive achievements in defining in legal terms the delimitations that have been agreed upon of the western section of the Sino-Russian border, along with the borders between China and Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan, in accordance with the positive and practical spirit of equality and consultation, mutual understanding and accommodation. Meanwhile, Chinese Assistant Foreign Minister Qin Huasun met here January 13 with Edouard P. Sviridov, head of the joint delegation and deputy director of the Department of Treaties and Law of the Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and his party.
