Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Report by Anatoliy Tabachenko] [Excerpts] On 3 March at 1600, an airplane with a Ukrainian delegation on board landed at Andrews Air Force Base in the vicinity of the U.S. capital. The Ukrainian president was met by U.S. officials, Ukrainian Ambassador to the United States Oleh Bilorus, and representatives of the Ukrainian Diaspora. Later, special helicopters transported the Ukrainian delegation to a square near the White House, from where they went on to Blair House. [passage omitted] In accordance with the program for the first day of the visit, the Ukrainian president met with Senators Paul Simon and Harris (Owford). During a conversation with representatives of the legislative branch, Leonid Kravchuk put particular emphasis on the complex issues that Ukraine is facing currently and outlined ways for further development of cooperation between our countries. In the evening, an official reception was held at the Ukrainian Embassy in Washington, on the occasion of the official visit to the United States by President Leonid Kravchuk. On 4 March, a meeting between Leonid Kravchuk and Bill Clinton is planned. It is expected that a package of important documents will be signed, which will introduce a new path in strengthening economic and political relations between Ukraine and the United States. The political willingness of the leaders of both countries' to extend our experience from cooperation in the military-political sphere to economic, trade, and scientific-technical cooperation, is a positive sign of the current status of U.S.-Ukrainian relations. The legal basis for this cooperation is to be established by appropriate interstate agreements.
