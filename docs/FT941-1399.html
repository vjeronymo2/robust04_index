940325 FT 25 MAR 94 / Wage cost inflation subdued at 0.7% The rate of growth of productivity slowed in the fourth quarter of last year and the rise in unit wage costs remained subdued, the Department of Employment said yesterday. Productivity in the whole of the economy increased by 2.2 per cent in the final three months of 1993 compared with a year earlier, down from 3 per cent in the third quarter. Unit wage costs rose 0.7 per cent in the fourth quarter compared with a year earlier - unchanged from the previous quarter. Mr Darren Winder, UK economist at SG Warburg Securities, said: 'It is quite normal for productivity to be slowing two years into the recovery. We are not going to see a second productivity miracle economy, although growth in manufacturing output will boost productivity.' Mr Kevin Gardiner, UK economist at Morgan Stanley, said: 'The economy is experiencing a potent cyclical boost to productivity as employment bottoms out and output rises.'
