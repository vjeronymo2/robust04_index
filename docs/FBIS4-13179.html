BFN [Unattributed report: "Lebanon Hints at Severing Relations With Iraq"] [Excerpts] London-Beirut-Amman, AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT -- Yesterday the Lebanese Government officially requested that Iraq lift the immunity of Iraqi diplomats Khalid Kahalaf and Muhammad Kazim, who are accused of assassinating Iraqi oppositionist Talib Suhayl in Beirut the day before yesterday evening. It also turned down a request submitted by the Iraqi Embassy in Beirut that the two detained diplomats be released, saying that they are under "guard" for their own safety and investigation requirements. [passage omitted] Official Lebanese sources told AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT yesterday that, if the Lebanese Government receives a negative response to its request that the immunity of the two detained diplomats be lifted and that the third man accused of the crime be handed over, it could decide to sever diplomatic relations with Baghdad. The sources added that a decision would be made after consultation with Charge D'Affaires Sa'd Zakhya, the only Lebanese diplomat in Baghdad, who was summoned by the Lebanese Foreign Ministry for "consultation." The Lebanese sources added that, if Baghdad refuses to lift the immunity of the detained culture and trade attaches, they would be declared persona non grata and expelled from Lebanese territory.
