Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 19 Feb (DPA) -- Military sources in Santa Fe de Bogota announced today that individuals presumed to be Communist guerrillas kidnapped Colombian Deputy Tomas Devia and attacked Cucutilla, a town in eastern Colombia, resulting in the death of two policemen and one soldier. The congressman was kidnapped in Guaviare Department in southern Colombia by several men who identified themselves as members of the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC). The 63-year-old Devia, a member of the ruling Liberal Party, is Guaviare's deputy in the Chamber of Representatives and was campaigning for reelection in the 13 March congressional elections, according to sources close to him. Members of the pro-Cuban Army of National Liberation (ELN) kidnapped Senator Hernando Torres and Deputy Victor Buitrago in Boyaca Department in central Colombia last weekend. Both men were also campaigning for reelection. Meanwhile, government spokesmen pointed out that FARC and ELN guerrillas staged a joint attack on the town of Cucutilla, 350 km northeast of Santa Fe de Bogota, where they murdered two policemen and one soldier and destroyed a bank and the headquarters of the state Telephone Company (Telecom). The police disclosed that 12 members of the ELN were captured in Antioquia Department in northwestern Colombia during an anti- kidnapping operation, among them two of the group's alleged ideologists, sociology students Myriam Giraldo and Fabian Masso. It has been announced that the guerrillas' arrest marked the culmination of an operation by the police, who had discovered a case of self-abduction by a merchant who surrendered to the subversives to collect a $6 million insurance policy he had purchased abroad.
