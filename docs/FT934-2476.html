931214 FT 14 DEC 93 / Behaviour blamed on poor teaching Ofsted, the education watchdog, said yesterday that bad behaviour in schools was caused by poor teaching. It said that most classrooms in England and Wales were calm and orderly, but it noted concern that exclusions of pupils from schools were increasing sharply, and that there were 'unacceptably wide' variations between schools in the nature of offences which would lead to exclusion. Ofsted said: 'For most pupils, good behaviour is associated with a well-planned and varied curriculum and lively, stimulating teaching. Expectations of high standards achieved through demanding tasks leave little time for, or interest in, misbehaviour.'
