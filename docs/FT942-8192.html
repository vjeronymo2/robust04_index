940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / London Stock Exchange: Utilities improve Water stocks made rapid progress towards them close of trading and were said to have appreciated even further in unofficial trading ahead of the start of the preliminary results season which commences next Wednesday. There were also widespread rumours in the market that Ofwat, the water industry regulator, was about to indicate better than expected 'K factors' to the companies. Ofwat is expected give indications to the water companies of its intentions regarding K factors. The results season begins on Wednesday with figures from North West Water and runs through till the end of June when Wessex reports. Thames, the biggest of the water companies, jumped 11 to 491p, while North West rose 7 to 505p, Anglian 6 to 493p and Severn Trent 4 to 500p. The electricity generators tended to give ground after making good progress earlier in the week but the 'recs' provided a strong performer in Northern Electricity, viewed as one of the sector's prime takeover targets, which added 13 at 657p, after 661p. Northern Ireland Electricity, due to report preliminary figures on June 1, moved up 5 to 239p. The two classes of BT stock made rapid progress throughout the busiest single-day's trading since early February, as a number of influential broking firms recommended the shares. James Capel, the leading agency broker, was among those promoting the shares. The ordinaries moved up 10 1/2 to 387p, after turnover of 16m, as did the partly-paids, 269p, following turnover of 12m. Shares in Granada Group were in the doldrums, closing 10 lighter at 527p, on speculation that the Great British Lottery Company consortium of which Granada is a part is unlikely to win the licence to operate the UK National lottery. An announcement is to be made on Wednesday about the outcome of the bids. Dealers remained optimistic about the prospects for the Lotco consortium which includes Rank Organisation. Shares in Rank put on 5 to 418p, on volume of 4.2m and were supported by a buy recommendation from Kleinwort Benson. Kleinwort said: 'Rank is standing on a market multiple for the year to October 1995,is delivering growth ahead of the market and does not need to undertake major corporate activities, unlike several other sector companies in which the market is anticipating significant disposals and acquisitions to justify exceptionally high ratings.' Shares in Ladbroke, part of Games For Good Causes consortium pitching for the UK National Lottery, moved 4 ahead to 199p. The best individual performance in the FT-SE 100 came from Bowater, the paper and packaging group, whose shares jumped 14, or 3.1 per cent to 456p after encouraging comments at the annual meeting. Bowater said its operating profits and earnings are ahead of last year and somewhat better than expectations. The performance of its American and Australian business is ahead of the same period last year while the European business were in line with the company's expectations, the group said. Bowater shares have fallen sharply since mid-March when the stock price reached 517p. Glaxo was one of the worst performers in a pharmaceuticals sector upset by concerns about government inquiries into the comparative costs of drugs and cost effectiveness studies for new pharmaceutical products. Glaxo shares dropped 12 to 539p after relatively heavy turnover of 4.6. Lasmo shares began to move forward strongly in mid-afternoon amid speculation that a cash alternative may soon be injected into the bid from Enterprise Oil. Lasmo closed 4 higher at 157p while Enterprise edged up to 428p. Housebuilders attracted keen support with Bryant leading the sector higher and finally 8 to the good at 160p after buy recommendations from NatWest Securities and James Capel. Continuing worries about its financing together with talk that the market may be faced with a bigger than anticipated rights issue combined to make Eurotunnel, the day's worst performing stock. The shares fell 30 to 375p, though reports after the market close said the channel tunnel operator had obtained around Pounds 600m in new bank lending. Nervous trading ahead of Monday's figures left British Airways 1/2 lighter at 377 1/2 p, after trade of 5.1m. Analysts expect the company to break even in the fourth quarter. A satisfactory statement at Next's annual meeting helped the shares firm 4 to 245p. Further consideration of the AGM statement at Laura Ashley, the previous day saw the shares appreciate 7 to 84p. Good results from Fine Arts Development earlier in the week continued to support the shares and they gained another 18 to 523p.
