930406 FT 06 APR 93 / Management (The Growing Business): Increasing the employers' burden - In a Nutshell Some small businesses are worried that government attempts to collect child maintenance payments from parents' pay packets will add to the workload on employers and damage employee relations. The Federation of Small Businesses, which has 58,000 members, has criticised plans by the Child Support Agency to issue deduction from earnings orders if other methods of obtaining payment fail. This latest burden comes on top of attachment of earnings orders which can be used to collect council tax payments and is at variance with the government's commitment to cut red tape, the federation said.
