Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Government spokesman Evangelos Venizelos said if Skopje shows the goodwill Greece expects, the government could reexamine its veto on the state's accession to the CSCE. [Begin Venizelos recording] If Skopje demonstrates the goodwill Greece has repeatedly sought--and still does, because this is an obligation derived from international law--then the Greek Government could reexamine the veto on Skopje joining the CSCE. [end recording] Venizelos added that Greece is ready for talks with Skopje within the UN framework, but on the basis determined by the government. Commenting on remarks Alternate Foreign Minister Theodhoros Pangalos made on Skopje yesterday, Venizelos stressed that as Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou said, he and Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias are handling the issue. The spokesman stressed that the issue is being raised on many levels, and recalled that it was recently raised on the highest possible level, the NATO summit. He said the prime minister had the opportunity to utilize one advantage of the European Union Presidency. [passage omitted]
