BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Belgrade, April 23 (TANJUG) -- The column of UNPROFOR combat vehicles arrived late Saturday night [23 April] in Gorazde, a Moslem enclave in eastern Bosnia, agencies reported, U.N. spokesman Maj. Guy Vinet said. UNPROFOR members will be deployed in the area three kilometers from the center of Gorazde on both banks of the River Drina, which flows through the town, declared a safe area a year ago by the U.N. this was in keeping with the agreement reached Friday in Belgrade by Bosnian Serb republic (R.S.) President Radovan Karadzic and UNPROFOR head of mission Yasushi Akashi, through the mediation of Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic. The Serbian forces around Gorazde are to withdraw to three kilometers from the town by Sunday at 00:01 GMT. Otherwise, as NATO council decided Friday, they would be exposed to NATO air strikes.
