Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Guatemala Flash" newscast] [Text] The Nationalist Authentic Central (CAN) party leadership expressed through its general secretary, Roberto Tito Arana, its satisfaction with the patriotism demonstrated by the Guatemalan people by going to the polls to cast their vote in favor of the referendum. This action resulted in the approval of the constitutional reforms, thereby laying the foundations for purging Congress and the Supreme Court. The CAN leadership concluded by stating: "Although we are immersed in a sea of uncertainty and indecision, the people's will as expressed in the referendum compels us to demand the immediate purge of state institutions. As a direct consequence, elections must be held immediately in order not to frustrate the aspirations of the Guatemalan people, who for several months have been demanding the election of a new congress.
