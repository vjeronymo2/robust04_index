Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] [Lyudmila Vilyuga] Today a continuation of the rally and strike started on 15 February at the initiative of the republican strike committee, the independent trade union and the Belarus People's Front, was expected in Independence Square in Minsk. The rally, which numbered about 1,500 people, however, did not turn into a large-scale event. It demanded that the government should resign, the parliament be dissolved and new elections to the Supreme Soviet be held. Incidentally, the last demand coincides with a demand put forward by the republican federation of trade unions. Meanwhile, the people's deputies who met today after a break in the session were in no hurry to heed the voice of the people. They started with discussing the agenda. Today Stanislav Shushkevich, ex-chairman of the parliament, turned up in parliament for the first time since his resignation. Now he can appraise the Supreme Soviet's work under a different angle. [Begin Shushkevich recording] The parliament does not want to heed the views of the minority. Its overwhelming majority is made up of old Communists elected when the Belarus Communist Party was in power. [end recording] [Vilyuga] The parliamentary majority solved cadre issues quite efficiently today. Three generals took important offices: Uladimir Danko was appointed interior minister, Genadz Lavitski -- chairman of the KGB, and (Anatoliy Novikov) -- chairman of the Supreme Soviet's commission for national security and combating crime. Ivan Bambiza was elected new deputy chairman of the Supreme Soviet.
