Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Jan 20 (CTK) -- In 1993, a total of 3,409,610 people in the Czech Republic were tested for HIV, the virus that causes AIDS, with 170 testing positive. Of these, 45 have developed AIDS, according to Ministry of Health information, ministry spokeswoman Marketa Soukupova told CTK today. Of those who tested HIV-positive, 150 were men and 20 were women. Of those stricken with AIDS, 41 were men and 4 were women. The highest-risk groups were made up of recipients of blood and blood products (14 HIV-positive and 3 with AIDS) and hemophiliacs (16 HIV-positive and 1 with AIDS). The highest percentage of HIV carriers and AIDS sufferers was found among homosexuals and bisexuals (16 HIV-positive and 7 with AIDS). Prague had the highest total of HIV carriers, with 110 people in various stages of the disease. A large portion of HIV carriers in the Czech Republic is made up of foreign students and workers, of whom 84 tested HIV-positive and one developed AIDS. Thirty-three of the 170 who tested HIV-positive died in 1993.
