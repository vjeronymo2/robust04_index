Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow January 30 TASS -- Georgian leader Eduard Shevarnadze emphasised the importance of the forthcoming visit by Russian President Boris Yeltsin to Georgia scheduled for February 3, saying that the establishment of normal relations with Russia is a matter of vital importance. During the visit a whole package of documents is expected to be signed. The most important document is, undoubtedly, a treaty on friendship, good-neighbourly relations and cooperation. A number of intergovernmental agreements are expected to be signed, including an agreement on trade and economic cooperation for 1994 and an agreement on free trade between the two countries. The agreement envisages that trade and economic cooperation between the two countries will be carried out taking into account the development of market relations on the basis of a direct agreement between economic subjects and regardless of their forms of property and subordination. An agreement on free trade provides for the establishment of a single market of goods, services and work force and creating conditions for the formation of the customs union between the two countries. The agreement outlines main principles of trade regulations between the two countries, including customs duties, and export and import taxes. Agreements are ready to be signed on such important problems as a status and conditions for the operation of Russian border guard troops on the Georgian territory, principles of cooperation and mutual relations in the sphere of transport, international traffic and merchant navigation, the coordination of the railways, problems of migration and the defence of resettlers' rights.
