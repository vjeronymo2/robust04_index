Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Jose Luis Armenta from the "Vector 21" newscast] [Text] The Permanent Commission of Congress has reached an agreement on the violent events in Chiapas State. The legislators proposed the creation of a commission comprising senators and deputies that would be tasked with investigating the events in the country's southeast. The legislators are also demanding from the authorities more information on the insurrection in Chiapas. Here are some of the proposals that the Permanent Commission has made on the Chiapas case. The proposals are read by Institutional Revolutionary Party Deputy Efrain Zuniga Galeana. [Begin Zuniga recording] The Permanent Commission of Congress proposes: 1. To urge those who are carrying out armed protests to contribute to an end of the fighting, to accept mediation, and to contribute -- together with the authorities and society -- to the establishing conditions suitable for dialogue and negotiations. 2. The people are entitled to systematically receiving truthful and timely information. 3. The formation of a group composed of legislators from the three working commissions. The group will be responsible for securing the necessary information to suggest, within the framework of its attributions and responsibilities, political solutions to the situation that prevails in Chiapas State. [end recording] The legislators of the Permanent Commission also said that in order to safeguard the country's independence and sovereignty, it is necessary to maintain constitutional order in Chiapas State.
