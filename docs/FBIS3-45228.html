Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Helsinki, January 12 (XINHUA) -- Presidents of three Baltic nations have warmly welcomed the "Partnership for Peace" plan agreed upon at the NATO summit and anticipated a bright prospect for their future NATO membership, the Finnish News Agency STT reported today. In a joint statement, Estonian President Lennart Meri, Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas and Latvian President Gontis Ulmanis expressed their appreciation of NATO's invitation to become associated with the organization within the boundaries of the "Partnership for Peace" proposal. The plan provides for future cooperation between NATO and the Baltic states, they stressed, expressing hope that it could be realized. The three presidents said that the cooperation between all European countries would enhance mutual trust and strengthen security in these areas.
