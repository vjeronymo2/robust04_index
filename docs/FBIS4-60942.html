BFN [Text] Talks on ways of settling the Karabakh conflict between the Karabakh leaders and the CSCE's Minsk group, led by Swedish diplomat Matias Mosberg, ended in Stepanakert on Saturday. The Karabakh Foreign Ministry's press service informed Interfax that the main accent was put on mechanisms of excluding combat operations. To quote a representative of the press service, the delegation members stated that they would be able to place their observers three days after the conclusion of a long-term cease-fire agreement between the conflicting parties. The negotiating parties also agreed not to adopt documents unconfirmed by real mechanisms of ruling out combat operations, and emphasized the importance of dovetailing the peace efforts of the CSCE and Russia. They also noted that certain steps had been made in this direction. On the same day, CSCE military experts and the Karabakh commanders discussed military-technical problems pertaining to the location of observers in the zone of combat operations.
