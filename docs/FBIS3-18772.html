Language: French Article Type:BFN [Article signed LVE: "Expansion: Claes Criticizes Spanish and Portuguese Intransigence"; as released by Brussels BELGA Database] [Text] Brussels, 8 Mar (BELGA) -- Belgian Foreign Minister Willy Claes harshly criticized Spanish and Portuguese intransigence Tuesday [9 March] evening regarding the issue of fisheries, calling on these two countries to moderate their demands regarding access by their trawlers to Norwegian waters. "Certain member states want to transform the European Union (EU) into a fish shop," he stated on the BRTN [Dutch-language Belgian Radio and Television]. According to him, tough words were expressed within the Council toward these two countries. "Too much is too much," he said, feeling that the demands made by these two countries is excessive. Mr. Claes used the financial argument: "Spain and Portugal would be well-advised to take account of the fact that Norway will be a net contributor to the Community budget, with all the benefits which that implies for the four cohesion countries (Spain, Portugal, Greece, and Ireland -- editor's note)," he said. The Belgian foreign minister added that Oslo had already taken two steps toward the EU: The first during its accession to the European Economic Area, the free-trade agreement between the EC and the EFTA countries (except Switzerland, which voted against it) which stipulates that the countries receiving cohesion funds should receive an extra fishing quota of 11,000 tonnes of cod in Norwegian waters, and the second during the negotiations of recent days, during which "appreciable steps" have been taken. Mr. Claes added that, nevertheless, he was still "confident" about the chances of unravelling this Gordian knot "this very night."
