941017 FT 17 OCT 94 / Plea on 'windfall' power profits British Gas and the regional electricity companies should hand over their 'windfall profits' to the Energy Savings Trust or to local energy charities, Labour says today. Mr Chris Smith, shadow environment secretary, said the companies had received more than Pounds 1bn from customers pre-paying bills in order to avoid paying value added tax on on domestic fuel. Two companies - Midlands Electricity and ScottishPower - had agreed to pass on money to energy charities, said Mr Smith. He added: 'The interest which companies are earning on the pre-paid money will amount to over Pounds 15m. This is enough to insulate more than 20,000 homes and begin to mitigate the worst impacts of VAT on fuel for these households.'
