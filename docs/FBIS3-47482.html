Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 13 KYODO -- The seven parties in Prime Minister Morihiro hosokawa's coalition government agreed Thursday [13 January] to allow owners of inherited property to pay the inheritance tax on them in property itself, party officials said. The tax relief measure, agreed upon at a meeting of the parties' policymakers, is intended for those who inherited land between 1989 and 1991 when land prices skyrocketed amid a real estate investment boom and plan to pay the tax in installments, the officials said. The coalition parties intend to implement the tax relief measure as part of tax reforms for fiscal 1994, they said.
