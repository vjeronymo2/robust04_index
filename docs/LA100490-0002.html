October 4, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

U.S. ARMS SALES TO SAUDI ARABIA

The arms that the Saudis have been buying for many years proved to be worthless when they had to defend themselves against threatened aggression from Iraq. Will additional arms enable them to provide for their own defense?

We should only supply arms when we get a commitment that their new ordnance will be manned by their own people and will then be used to replace our men and weapons.

JOSEPH GRODSKY

Los Angeles
