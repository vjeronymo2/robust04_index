Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] (Guido Lucatto), allegedly a member of the Italian Mafia, was arrested in Santa Cruz and has been transferred to La Paz. While he faces no narcotrafficking charges in Bolivia, there are grounds to deport him because he carries a forged passport. Here is a report by Amalia Pando: A capo of the Italian Mafia was arrested on 17 January in Santa Cruz. He will be deported without any judicial proceedings within a few hours or days. Four Italian policemen arrived in Bolivia yesterday to take him away. He arrived in Bolivia in August 1992 carrying an Italian passport issued to the name of (Guido Lucatto) but it has been established that the passport is forged. His real name is (Salvatore Sciula). He was born in Palermo on 21 February 1950. He is 44 years old. He is married to Chilean citizen (Claudia Guzman Onoratto). He was arrested by the Santa Cruz Special Force with the cooperation of the Italian Police and Interpol. (Salvatore Sciula) was arrested at 0900 on 17 January. He and an unidentified man tried to flee when they realized they were being followed while crossing a downtown street aboard a black jeep. (Sciula) was arrested but his companion is still at large. Right now fingerprint tests are being conducted to verify that this is the man being sought by the Italian Police. [passage omitted]
