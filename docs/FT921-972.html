920326 FT 26 MAR 92 / IMF cuts growth forecast for industrial countries THE International Monetary Fund is forecasting a relatively weak recovery in the world economy this year, but hopes that growth in 1993 will prove more robust, writes George Graham in Washington. Mr Michel Camdessus, the IMF managing director, said yesterday his organisation now projects growth in the industrial countries of about 2 per cent this year - substantially less than the 2.8 per cent it was forecasting at its annual meeting in Bangkok last October. For the world economy as a whole growth could be even more sluggish, Mr Camdessus told a conference in Monte Carlo. The IMF is now projecting overall growth of only 1.5 per cent this year, as a result of lost output in the Middle East and in eastern Europe. Mr Camdessus said, however, that there was reason to expect a stronger recovery in 1993, averaging perhaps 3 to 3.5 per cent. He urged the leading economies to concentrate on policies that would produce medium-term growth. 'This does not mean the kind of short-term growth that can result from excessive demand stimulation,' he warned. 'The policies to achieve such growth are well known - fiscal policies aimed at reducing deficits to sustainable levels, firm anti-inflationary monetary policies and structural policies to eliminate distortions and improve the efficiency of the economy.'
