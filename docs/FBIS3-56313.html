Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow January 12 TASS -- Belarusian parliamentary speaker Stanislav Shushkevich left Moscow for Minsk on Wednesday upon completion of his one-day visit to Russia. On Wednesday, Shushkevich had talks with Russian President Boris Yeltsin. Upon completion of the talks a joint communique was signed in which the sides expressed their common opinion that it is necessary to work out a new interstate Russo-Belarusian treaty on friendship and cooperation meeting the aspirations of both countries aimed at building democratic states with a socially-oriented market economy. Yeltsin and Shushkevich exchanged opinions in connection with the expected visit by the U.S. President Bill Clinton to Moscow and Minsk.
