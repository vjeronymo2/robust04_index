Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- On 8 June, two days after the Allied ceremonies commemorating the 50th anniversary of the landing in Normandy, Chancellor Helmut Kohl and French President Francois Mitterrand will meet in Heidelberg with young people from the two countries. Speaking to reporters in Bonn on Wednesday [23 March], Kohl also announced invitations to U.S. President Bill Clinton, Britain's Prime Minister John Major and Mitterrand to come to Berlin on 8 September for a Grand Tatoo to bid farewell to the Allied soldiers in the capital. Russian President Boris Yeltsin, who will pay an official visit to Bonn from 11 to 13 May, will come on 31 August to bid farwell to the Russian soldiers, Kohl announced. The celebrations are planned in Berlin and Weimar with German and Russian military honor formations.
