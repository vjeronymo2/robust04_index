Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [By Nobuyuki Tanaka] [Text] Moscow, 12 Nov -- On 12 November, the first general meeting between Japan and Russia was held in Moscow to discuss promoting bilateral cooperation in the disposal of nuclear waste. Edamura, Japanese ambassador to Russia, and Yegorov, Russian vice atomic energy minister, attended the meeting. During the meeting, the two countries agreed that meetings of experts should be held to discuss the following issues: 1) the possible construction of storage facilities for fissionable materials from dismantled warheads; 2) the disposal of liquid missile fuel; and 3) the dumping of radioactive waste into sea. The Japanese Government has already decided to appropriate $100 million to help Russia, Ukraine, Kazakhstan, and Byelarus dismantle nuclear materials. The meeting was held to discuss details of Japan's aid.
