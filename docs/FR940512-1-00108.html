Section 905.102 would be amended by removing the definition of ``Applicable surface'' and revising the definitions of ``Chewable surface'', ``Defective lead-based paint surface'', ``Defective paint surface'', and ``Elevated blood lead level or EBL'', to read as follows: §905.102 Definitions. * * * * * Chewable surface. Any intact protruding painted surface up to five feet from the floor or ground that is readily accessible to children less than six years of age (e.g., protruding corners, windowsills and frames, doors and frames, and other protruding woodwork). * * * * * Defective lead-based paint surface. A surface having paint that has a lead content of at least 1 mg/cm2 and that is cracking, scaling, chipping, peeling, or loose. Defective paint surface. A surface on which the paint is nonintact, i.e. the paint is cracking, scaling, chipping, peeling, or loose. * * * * * Elevated blood lead level or EBL. Excessive absorption of lead is a confirmed concentration of lead in whole blood of 20 μg/dl (micrograms per deciliter of whole blood) for a single test or of 15&hyph;19 μg/dl in two consecutive tests several months apart. * * * * * 47.
