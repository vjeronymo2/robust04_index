931019 FT 19 OCT 93 / Patsatsia and Chernomyrdin meet Georgian leader Eduard Shevradnadze has appealed for military help from Russia, Armenia and Azerbaijan to secure vital transport links cut by rebels, Reuter Reports. 'We are not in a position to deal with this task with our own forces,' he said in a dramatic TV plea. 'We are in the most difficult, not to say catastrophic situation.' However, Moscow agreed in talks between the Georgian and Russian prime ministers in Moscow yesterday to offer any kind of help except military intervention, according to Interfax news agency.
