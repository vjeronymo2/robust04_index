910426 FT 26 APR 91 / International Company News: Johnson & Johnson raises payout JOHNSON & Johnson, the big US health care company, yesterday unveiled a 12 per cent improvement in underlying first-quarter net earnings and increased its quarterly dividend by 17.6 per cent to 40 cents a share in a vote of confidence in the strength of its consumer and pharmaceuticals businesses. Net income for the 13 weeks ended March 31 was Dollars 418m, or Dollars 1.25 a share, on sales up 12 per cent to Dollars 3.15bn, against net earnings of Dollars 244m, or 73 cents a share, on sales of Dollars 2.81bn a year earlier. Excluding one-time items in the 1990 quarter, net profits rose 12 per cent from Dollars Dollars 369m, or Dollars 1.11 a share, last year. Earnings before taxes rose 51 per cent in the 1991 first quarter to Dollars 609m from Dollars 403m. Growth was led by Johnson & Johnson's pharmaceuticals business, which posted a 21 per cent improvement in sales to Dollars 964m in the latest quarter. Growth of consumer products sales was more modest, with a rise of only 4 per cent to Dollars 1.19bn, reflecting a 5.9 per cent decline in overseas sales
