Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 18 KYODO -- Japan should keep calm over the aggressive U.S. trade stance, Minister of International Trade and Industry Hiroshi Kumagai said Friday [18 February]. He made the comment in response to U.S. President Bill Clinton's statement on Thursday that the U.S. will pursue an "much more aggressive policy" to push Japanese markets open. Clinton said he would try "different things" to achieve the goal of opening Japan's markets. Kumagai told reporters after a regular cabinet meeting that the U.S. has often adopted such an attitude, even against old allies like Britain, when trade talks were deadlocked.
