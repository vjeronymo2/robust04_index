941104 FT 04 NOV 94 / International Company News: Telecom NZ gains 16.7% at six months Telecom New Zealand, which is controlled by US companies Bell Atlantic and Ameritech, yesterday reported a 16.7 per cent increase in earnings to NZDollars 293.3m (USDollars 181m) for the first six months to September 30. Operating revenue rose 12.1 per cent to NZDollars 1.37bn, including earnings of NZDollars 65.4m from Pacific Star, its Australian subsidiary. Operating expenses fell 11 per cent to NZDollars 797.5m and interest costs were down 11 per cent to NZDollars 53.9m. New phone connections rose 47.9 per cent. Staff numbers fell by 1,000 to 9,200 during the period, in line with forecasts.
