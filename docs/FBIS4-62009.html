BFN [Text] The Baltic states are strengthening military cooperation with Western European countries. Today in Paris the defense ministers of the three Baltic countries signed a cooperation agreement with France on defense issues. The document was signed by Estonian Defense Minister Indrek Kannik, Latvian Defense Minister Valdis Pavlovskis, and Lithuanian Defense Minister Linas Linkevicius. On behalf of France the agreement was signed by Defense Minister Francois Leotard. According to the agreement, France will help the Baltic states to run joint military exercises, exchange officers and military observers, and train military personnel, including the Baltic peacekeeping battalion. According to the French defense minister, all three Baltic countries need military hardware, vehicles first and foremost. After signing the agreement, Indrek Kannik said that the agreement concluded in France will help Estonia in persuading Russia to withdraw its troops from Estonia.
