Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature] [Text] Aleksandr Lysenko, the staff member of the Russian embassy in Washington who, by agreement with the Russian External Intelligence Service (EIS) and the CIS, was introduced in Washington as the official representative of the Russian Intelligence, is returning to Moscow on Saturday [5 March], Head of the EIS's Press Bureau Yuriy Kobaladze told INTERFAX. As we reported earlier, Aleksandr Lysenko arrived in the U.S. last summer and was declared persona non-grata in connection with the Ameses case. Aldrich Ames and his wife Maria were accused of spying in favor of the Kremlin for many years. To quote Kobaladze, Moscow's decision to expel high-ranking American diplomat James Morris, officially introduced as the CIS resident, "is a matter of mutual accounts, rather than a return measure." Kobaladze expressed hope that the damage caused by Lysenko's and Morris's exile would be minimized. "We reckon on this," he said. Asked if Lysenko's successor would be officially introduced in Washington as the Russian resident, Kobaladze said that the agreement on this was still in force. "However, details will be discussed with Americans, on whom very much would depend. We have channels for such a discussion," he said. Speaking about Lysenko's career, Kobaladze said that some of the American media's reports about Lysenko's further exile to Siberia were ungrounded. "His experience and knowledge can be used by the EIS where he will continue to work," he said.
