BFN [Text] Beijing, April 7 (XINHUA) -- Wu Yi, Chinese minister of foreign trade and economic cooperation, met here this afternoon with Gerard Longuet, French minister of industry, posts, telecommunications and foreign trade, who is here accompanying French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur on a four-day official visit to China. During the meeting, Wu said that China attaches importance to its trade and economic relations with France, and that bilateral economic and trade relations are in general fine since the two countries established diplomatic ties 30 years ago. The development of bilateral economic and trade requires a good political environment, said Wu, adding that with the joint efforts of the two governments, bilateral relations have returned to normal, which provides favorable conditions for the development of bilateral economic and trade relations. The Chinese minister said that the economies of China and France are complementary to each other. France has strength in the fields of communications, telecommunications and energy, in which the two countries share bright prospects for future cooperation. Longuet said that the economy of China, an important nation in the world, has been developing quickly in recent years, adding that France is willing to strengthen its economic and trade ties with China, participate in the country's economic construction, especially in the fields of communications, telecommunications and energy. The French minister also said that to further enhance bilateral economic and trade cooperation and create conditions for French enterprises to enter the China market, France will provide preferential commercial credits to China in the future.
