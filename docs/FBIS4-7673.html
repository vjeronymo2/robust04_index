BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 1/4/1994 -- Hans Blix, IAEA [International Atomic Energy Agency] general director, currently on a visit to Romania, was the guest on 31 March of the Magurele Nuclear Engineering and Physics Institute near Bucharest. On the occasion, the general director of the Nuclear Physics Institute (IFA), Professor Alexandru Glodeanu, presented the site, as well as the premises where the nuclear reactor is situated. Also visited were several labs where the guest could see a specialized computer program for the prediction of effects of a potential nuclear catastrophe and the dosimetric radiations lab, one of the key points in the institute. The guest were also informed on the achievements of the center for the production of radio isotopes which are essential for collateral fields of physics. The Swedish guest was also presented the nuclear reactor built back in 1957, which almost doubled its capacity by successive modernization works, and the tandem linear accelerator regarded by the guest as a unique equipment in East Europe, which is producing heavy ions used in nuclear and atomic physics researches.
