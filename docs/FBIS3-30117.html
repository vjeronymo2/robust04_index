Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 24 KYODO -- Twenty legislators mainly from the major opposition Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) in the Diet attended a meeting Thursday [24 February] to prepare for establishing a policy study group, the attendants said. Seiichi Ota and Shokei Arai, both LDP members of the House of Representatives, and two independents are among them. Organizers of the group hope to officially launch their new team called "Liberals" in early March. They also hope to have the participation of more Diet members, local assembly members, academics and business executives, they said. Koji Kakizawa, another LDP member in the lower house, will chair the group to be gathered under the banner of "Real Liberalism," they said. The group's document states the purpose of the society basically adopts previous policies of the LDP, which surrendered its 38-year grip on power last August to the coalition government led by Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa.
