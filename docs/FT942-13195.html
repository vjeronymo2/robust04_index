940426 FT 26 APR 94 / International Company News: Advance for associates in LVMH Agache and Au Bon Marche, two French companies associated with Mr Bernard Arnault and his LVMH luxury goods group, yesterday reported healthy increases in profits for last year. Agache, which was Mr Arnault's original investment vehicle and is the holding company through which he controls the network of businesses with stakes in LVMH, last year increased net profits by 35 per cent to FFr368m (Dollars 63.12m). Turnover rose by 9.8 per cent to FFr27.6bn. Agache benefited in 1993 from a FFr348m exceptional credit on its share of the proceeds of LVMH's sale of the RoC skin care business. Au Bon Marche, which has retail interests including the Bon Marche department store in Paris as well as a large minority stake in LVMH, saw net profits rise 15 per cent to FFr653m. The net dividend is going up rises to FFr16 from FFr15.50. The company, one of the businesses that is financing Mr Arnault's recent efforts to unravel LVMH's cross-shareholding agreement with the Guinness drinks group, received a FFr362m exceptional credit on the RoC sale.
