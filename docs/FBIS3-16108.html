Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksey Agureyev] [Text] Moscow March 11 TASS -- Dmitriy Simes, adviser of the U.S. ex-President Richard Nixon, accompanying him on the current visit to Russia, gave new details of Nixon's conversation with former Russian vice-president Aleksandr Rutskoy. According to Simes, during the meeting Rutskoy said that he wanted to run for presidency at the nearest presidential elections in Russia. Simes pointed out that in a conversation with Nixon the former Russian vice-president said that he intends to carry out his political campaign by exclusively lawful means. Simes went on to say that Nixon met with a high-ranking official of the State Duma and a representative of the Russian Government. He pointed out that the names of those who met with the U.S. ex-President would be kept secret at their own request. At the same time, Simes said that one of the anonymous high-ranking politicians who had asked for a meeting with the U.S. ex-President canceled the meeting, saying he was unwell. Returning to the subject of Nixon's having been deprived of a limousine equipped with a special security system which had earlier been in his possession, Simes denied allegations that this had been done at the request of the U.S. ex-President's entourage. Commenting on the situation around Nixon's visit to Russia, Simes declared that the trip turned out to be the best lesson for him. "This trip made me realise better than ever how decisions are adopted by the Russian Government and what relations exist between its constituent parts, how president's advisers are operating and what the relations are like between the president's entourage and the president himself and the State Duma and an extent to which lawmakers can afford to make independent decisions. All this was very useful to me," Simes said. "The situation which unfolded is unwelcome, but it has taught us a good lesson, "making us a little bit wiser," Simes said.
