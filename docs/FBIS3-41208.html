Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] RIGA, Feb 03, BNS - The Latvian capital, Riga, will host the office of the Baltic regional bureau for the control of narcotic drugs, U.N. expert Dagmar Thomas Halasek told reporters. The bureau's activities will also embrace Lithuania and Estonia, Halasek said. The main office will be situated in Riga because of the city's advantageous geographical position and the successful operation of the Latvian National Drugs Control Commission. The bureau will coordinate the efforts of police, customs and frontier guards to curb the spread of narcotic drugs. Latvia reported 219 criminal cases connected with drugs' abuse in 1993, said Vilnis Kipens, a spokesman for the Riga Criminal Police Department. In 1993, 47 people died from overuse of drugs, the figure increased by 27 death cases compared with 1992. Drugs are trafficked to Latvia mainly from Lithuania and the Kaliningrad district of Russia, Kipens said.
