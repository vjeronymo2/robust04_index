BFN [by ITAR-TASS correspondent Tamara Ivanova] [Text] Moscow, 25 May -- A decision to set up a Television and Radio League [Liga televideniya I radio] was taken today by representatives of over 100 all-Russian and regional state and independent radio and TV companies. Anatoliy Lysenko, general director of the All-Russia Radio and TV Company, was elected chairman of the body while it is being established. The league will be a body of the Union of Journalists of Russia. In an interview with an ITAR-TASS correspondent, Union Chairman Vsevolod Bogdanov voiced the hope that the league will be able to exert considerable influence on the framing of legislation in the TV and radio broadcasting field, will become a collective body defending the interests of the "electronic" press, and will enable a number of topical professional issues to be resolved. At the first organizational meeting, Grigoriy Shevelev, Ostankino's first deputy chairman, was instructed to take charge of the work of setting up an academy of radio and television. Other areas of the league's activities were also outlined.
