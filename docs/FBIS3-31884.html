Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 25 Feb (TANJUG) -- Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic this afternoon received Yasushi Akashi, special envoy for the UN secretary general and head of the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] mission, and General Jean Cot, commander of the UN peacekeepers for the former Yugoslavia. According to the Serbian president's office, the talks centered on issues relating to a political solution of the Yugoslav crisis and the implementation of the UN forces' mandate. President Milosevic expressed his satisfaction at the successful talks between Yasushi Akashi and Milan Martic on surmounting tensions surrounding the bridge across the Sava River at Gradiska, stressing that Serbia, as usual, prefers to resolve disputes through political dialogue instead of confrontation. The Republic of Serbia has been extending and will continue to extend support to the implementation of the UN peace plan, which does not prejudge political solutions but leaves this up to negotiations in conditions of a stable peace. The head of UNPROFOR's mission informed President Milosevic about viewpoints pertaining to the latest developments and future activities, in the context of the peace process for former Bosnia-Herzegovina. The talks were also attended by Yugoslav Foreign Minister Vladislav Jovanovic.
