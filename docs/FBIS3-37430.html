Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] At its session in Saransk, the parliament of Mordovia has abolished its Constitutional Court. During the discussion, it was pointed out that the Russian Constitution makes no provision for the existence of constitutional courts in the republics. It is interesting that, during the nine months of its existence, the Constitutional Court of Mordovia considered only three matters, while 44 million rubles of budget funds were spent on maintaining the court.
