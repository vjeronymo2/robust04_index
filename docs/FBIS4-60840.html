BFN [Text] We have just this minute received some information from the Russian Federation Foreign Ministry. It says: By continuing to play an active role in settling the situation in former Yugoslavia, Russian diplomacy, along with its five partners in Yugoslavia -- Russia, Great Britain, Spain, the United States, and France -- enabled important progress to be made in strengthening the safe havens regime in Bosnia-Herzegovina. The Security Council has proposed that the UN secretary general come forward with concrete recommendations on this issue by 10 May. The UN Secretariat has assured the Russian representative that the Russian proposals put forward earlier on this will be taken into account when these recommendations are being worked out, in addition to the information in the letter from the Security Council chairman. It is the unanimous view of the sides looking for a settlement that the strengthening of the safe havens regime will push forward significantly settlement of the main tasks today, being tackled by the Russian, the United States, EC, and the UN contact group. These are to achieve an end to all military activity and bring the conflicting sides back to the negotiating table. This information is from the Russian Foreign Ministry.
