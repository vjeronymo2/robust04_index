May 14, 1989, Sunday, San Diego County Edition

HEALTH CLINIC RHETORIC

On Tuesday, the San Diego city school board will face a tough decision: whether to give conceptual approval for a health clinic to serve 8,000 students at several East San Diego schools.

The decision will not be tough for the usual financial reasons. The clinic's operating costs would come primarily from private foundations. The school district's contribution would mostly be in administrative services and donated space.

What makes the decision tough is the opposition of a vocal minority, led by the Catholic bishop, who, in a pastoral letter called the health centers "sex clinics" in disguise that will promote free sex, contraception, masturbation and abortion.

Such rhetoric is absurd. First of all, the clinic will not provide abortion referrals or information. Second, if it provides birth-control information and devices -- and it should -- that is not synonymous with promoting sexual activity. It only recognizes that many teen-agers are sexually active whether or not their parents want them to be.

Third, all services will require parental permission. The notion that state law would overrule that requirement has not proven true at clinics elsewhere in California, and school district officials say opponents are misinterpreting the law.

What school board members need to keep uppermost in their minds is that the medical and mental-health services the clinic would provide are sorely needed. The religious beliefs of a minority should not be able to deny those services to the majority who support them.
