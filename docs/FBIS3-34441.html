Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 8 Feb (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa met today with Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres and discussed the progress achieved so far in eliminating the obstacles hindering the implementation of the Gaza-Jericho agreement. The meeting was attended on the Egyptian side by Ambassador to Israel Mahmud Basyuni and Foreign Ministry spokesman Ambassador Naji al-Ghatrifi and on the Israeli side by Environment Minister Yosi Sarid and Ambassador David Sultan. Musa met earlier today for one hour with Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat. 'Arafat, Peres, and Musa are expected to get together at a working luncheon Musa is hosting for the Palestinian and Israeli delegations. In another development, Avi Gil, Israeli Foreign Ministry deputy director general, stated that at noon today President Husni Mubarak contacted Shim'on Peres. The Israeli foreign minister informed Mubarak of the real stand regarding the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations so far, Gil said. He added that President Mubarak invited Peres to attend a meeting with him at 1830 this evening. The spokesman noted that the Israeli minister will also meet with the Austrian president at 1930 today. In other developments, sources close to the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations stated to MENA that the general atmosphere of the talks is good. The Palestinian delegation sources said that the Palestinian and Israeli negotiators will continue their negotiations today and tomorrow. Peres will not leave Cairo until late on Wednesday evening or early Thursday morning after finding a solution to the obstacles to signing the agreement. The same sources clarified that the minutes of the negotiations, including all the points of difference and agreement, will only be initialed by the two sides. The points of difference will be left for President 'Arafat and Yitzhaq Rabin to resolve later. The sources said that the points of difference revolve basically around the issue of the crossings and Israel's insistence on maintaining its security control over them. The news agency has also learned that the paper being discussed currently is the Davos document, which is based on the Cairo paper. The Davos document consists of three main pages in addition to a special and a separate eight-page section on the minutes regarding the crossings. The Israelis made some changes to the Davos document which the Palestinians reject, insisting that this matter should be seen as a comprehensive deal that includes the Davos and Cairo documents. It is worth noting here that the Palestinian delegation to the talks includes Ahmad Quray', alias Abu-'Ala', Nabil Sha'th, Akram Haniyah, and Faysal al-Husayni; the Israeli delegation includes Uri Savir, Foreign Ministry director general; Yo'el Zinger, the legal advisor to the Foreign Ministry; and Uzi Dayan, chief of military planning.
