Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs, returned to Amman this evening after accompanying His Highness Crown Prince Hasan during his visit to Croatia and Bosnia. The minister spoke about His Highness Crown Prince Hasan's visit and his meeting with Jordanian troops taking part in the UN peace-keeping forces stationed in Croatia and Bosnia. He said that the objective of this visit was to hold a series of meetings with Croatian and Bosnian leaders and to meet with the Jordanian officers, NCOs, and troops taking part in the peacekeeping forces in Croatia and Bosnia. The visit was also aimed at meeting with the commanders of the UN forces in the former Yugoslavia. He said that this visit comes within the framework of supporting ongoing international peace efforts and the recent agreement reached between the Croatians and Bosnians in Vienna aimed at ending the conflict, achieving peace, and closing a chapter of history full of tribulations and sufferings. Al-Hasan noted that the visit added a new dimension to the credibility of Jordan and its leader His Majesty King Husayn. The visit also highlighted the respect the leadership and people of these countries have for Jordan and its leader His Majesty King Husayn and his effective contribution in support of international efforts and the United Nations' role through the participation of Jordanian troops. Jordan has the second largest contingent, after France, of peace-keeping forces in the region. [passage omitted]
