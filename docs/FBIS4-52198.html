BFN ["Disgraceful Resignation" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, May 9 (KCNA) -- Shigeto Nagano Saturday resigned as justice minister of Japan in face of strong criticism at home and abroad, a news report from Tokyo said. In an interview with the MAINICHI SHIMBUN newspaper of Japan May 3, he said the Pacific war started by Japanese imperialism was not a war of aggression and that the Nanjing massacre was a "hoax." His remarks whitewashing the blood-stained history of aggression of Japanese imperialism and defending militarism greatly startled and angered the Korean people, Asian countries and the world public. The Japanese people and public circles strongly demanded his resignation. Upset by this, Nagano at a "press conference" Friday "revoked" and "apologized" for what he had said and at last could not help resigning. Nagano is not the first to resign as cabinet minister after letting out anachronistic militarist remarks. The case is the same with former Education Minister Fujio who justified in 1986 the Japanese imperialists' invasion of Korea and with former director general of the land administration agency Okuno who said in 1988 that Japan had "no aggressive design" during World War II. Nagano had to resign in ten days after he was appointed justice minister in the Hata cabinet. Through the shameful cases of those who resigned in face of strong protest and criticism at home and abroad for their attempt to revive the ghost of militarism, Japan must clearly know that militarist revival will lead to its own grave.
