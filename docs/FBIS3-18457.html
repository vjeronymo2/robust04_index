Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Regarding claims that Russia has been dispatching units to establish security zones in Azerbaijan, Turkey has stated that should peace forces be required in Azerbaijan, it should be done within the framework of the CSCE principles which stipulate that these units be multinational. Foreign Ministry Deputy Spokesman Ferhat Ataman, at his weekly news conference, indicated that there was no information regarding Russian soldiers being sent to Azerbaijan, adding that the sending of peace forces to the CSCE countries was determined at the CSCE Council of Ministers meeting in Rome. [passage omitted] Ataman further stated that they received reports regarding a ceasefire in Azerbaijan but that it was not comprehensive and recalled that Turkey stressed that the ceasefire, on its own, is not sufficient to solve the issue. Ataman concluded by saying that the ceasefire will be significant only if the Armenians withdraw from the territories they occupy and enable the refugees to return.
