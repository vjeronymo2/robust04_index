December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

A 1989 RETROSPECTIVE;

HEROES

When his mother began to choke on a lozenge in their Van Nuys condominium, 3-year-old Jameson Rodgers remembered what his parents had trained him to do. He dialed 911. "My mom's on the floor. She can't breathe," the tot told a Fire Department dispatcher in a calm, articulate voice. Minutes later, paramedics arrived and found Peggy Rodgers on her hands and knees, on the verge of unconsciousness. After two tries, they managed to dislodge the lozenge. Leonard Washington of Pomona reacted decisively when he noticed a group of black-booted "skinheads" accosting a Middle Eastern family in a La Verne parking lot. Washington, 34, stopped his car and proceeded to hold off the quartet of attackers, one of whom had menacingly waved a baseball bat, until police arrived. Nineteen other heroes received awards ranging from $250 to $10,388 from the county Board of Supervisors for their roles in bringing Night Stalker Richard Ramirez to justice. "It was my biggest experience, to know that I confronted him face-to-face and knew that it was him," said Edgardo Molinar of Covina, who helped chase Ramirez through East Los Angeles on the day of his capture in August, 1985.
