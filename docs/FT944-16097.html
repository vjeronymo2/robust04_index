941014 FT 14 OCT 94 / World Commodities Prices: Wool The Australian market hovered and tended a fraction easier in the first half of the wool sales week, but subsequently began to show more strength, with increases of 5-to 10 cents a kg. paid for some wools on the closing day. This resulted in an Eastern states market indicator quotations of 738 cents at the close, only one cent lower than a week before. China is reported to have been a more active buyer of wool in Australia as the week progressed. The view there is that this is the area where long-term demand will develop, and after relatively subdued buying in the earlier weeks of the season price rises could result from re-entry. Economic recovery and wool textile activity combine to keep the background elsewhere sound, and a firmer tendency after the recent world market setback could bring new orders through, traders believe.
