941028 FT 28 OCT 94 / International Company News: Norwegian insurer plunges into the red Vital, the Norwegian life insurance and pension group, plunged to a pre-tax loss of NKr97m in the nine months to September from a profit of NKr2.31bn last time, writes Karen Fossli. The reversal was caused by a sharp reduction in gross financial income which sunk to NKr1.17bn from NKr3.61bn. 'The weak results were due to the increase in interest rates which caused a sharp reduction in the value of the company's bonds,' Vital explained. Saga Petroleum, Norway's largest independent oil company, said yesterday that it would to seek a listing on the New York stock exchange in spring of next year.
