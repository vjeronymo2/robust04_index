Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Last night Dr. Franjo Tudjman, president of the Republic of Croatia, received special Russian envoy Vitaliy Churkin. After the talks, Churkin told journalists that appropriate and rapid action is needed to help the three sides in Bosnia-Herzegovina find the best solution. He continued: I expect that next week we will launch a peace process which might finally resolve the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Vitaliy Churkin also had talks with Croatian Foreign Minister Mate Granic and informed him about his talks in Sarajevo and Belgrade with representatives of the Bosnian Serbs and with Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic. They agreed that the Bosnian Serbs should be included in the negotiations as soon as possible in order for an integral solution, as well as peace and stability in this region, to be found.
