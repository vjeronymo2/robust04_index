Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias, who is presiding over the European Union [EU] Council of Foreign Ministers, left for Brussels at 1030 [0830 GMT] today to chair a meeting of the EU Council of General Affairs. The main topic of the meeting is the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Speaking before leaving, Papoulias said he hoped that calm views prevail during the meeting and stressed that Greece will seek a peaceful stance on the issue and oppose military intervention. [passage omitted] Miltiadhis Evert, New Democracy and official opposition leader, expressed opposition to a possible air raid. Evert left this morning for a five-day tour of European countries. He said air strikes will not solve the problem because they will be followed by ground attacks, which would be insane.
