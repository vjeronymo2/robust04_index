Language: Dari Article Type:BFN [Text] The special emissary to Afghanistan of the Foreign Ministry of the Islamic Republic of Iran has held talks with Afghan officials. He described the mediation efforts by the Islamic Republic of Iran to establish peace and tranquillity in Kabul as positive. According to the Voice and Vision of the Islamic Republic of Iran's correspondent in Islamabad, Ebrahim Taherian, the special Iranian emissary to Afghanistan held talks with the warring sides in the country and returned to Peshawar in Pakistan. During his three days of meetings in Kabul, he presented a verbal message from President Hashemi-Rafsanjani to the President of Afghanistan, Borhanoddin Rabbani and also a written message by the Iranian Foreign Minister, Dr. Velayati, to Prime Minister Golboddin Hekmatyar. He also held talks with the Hezb-e Wahdat-e Eslami [Islamic Unity Party] Secretary General Mazari, and with the representative of the Jonbesh-e Melli-ye Eslami [Islamic National Movement] in Kabul. Speaking to the Voice and Vision of the Islamic Republic of Iran correspondent this morning, he stated: The Islamic Republic of Iran has mustered all its efforts to bring the leaders of all the parties involved in the conflict together in order to reach a rapprochement, and in this regard it is paying special attention to consultations with neighboring countries. The special emissary of the Islamic Republic of Iran to Afghanistan added that in view of the efforts so far, the warring groups have agreed to select representatives who will meet as soon as possible. Taherian added that the Islamic Republic of Iran intends to continue its efforts until there is some specific outcome. The special emissary of the Islamic Republic of Iran stated that in continuation of his assignment, he will commence talks with Pakistani officials in order to exchange views on how to reach an amicable solution to end the conflict in Afghanistan and to encourage the groups involved to compromise. Each of the groups embroiled in the civil war in Afghanistan has set preconditions for ending the imbroglio. The spokesman for the Jami'at-e Eslami-ye Afghanistan [Islamic Party of Afghanistan] said in Peshawar that the President of Afghanistan, Borhanoddin Rabbani, has no intention of stepping down from his position. According to the Central News Unit in Kabul, to implement a cease-fire among the hostile factions, the government will collect all the heavy weapons around Kabul. The spokesman for Borhanoddin Rabbani added that all the political and government centers of Kabul are under the control of the forces of the (?government) and former defense minister of Afghanistan, Ahmad Shah Mas'ud. In any case, the most (?reliable) source of the Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party] announced in an interview in Kabul that the conditions laid down by opponents of Borhanoddin Rabbani's resignation remain in effect. They stress that until he steps down from his post as President, the struggle will continue.
