930507 FT 07 MAY 93 / Observer: Geography lesson Meanwhile, digging into the Soviet past in the course of a 10-part series entitled The World That Came in from the Cold, the BBC World Service purports to have unearthed some remarkable facts. One of the more far-fetched anecdotes hails from a Budapest civil servant who recalls how Soviet troops, summoned to quash the November 4 1956 uprising, emerged from their tanks - when the fighting was done - asking for directions to the ocean. Crestfallen to find no briny, one of their number explained that they thought they were shooting the British in Suez. Well, Shakespeare's grasp of geography was a little ropy too. Remember The Winter's Tale, where Bohemia is by the seaside?
