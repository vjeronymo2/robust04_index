Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] A BELINFORM correspondent has been told at the Defense Ministry press center that the second stage of reform in the Belarusian Armed Forces will be carried out in 1994. There will be further reductions in the personnel of the former Belarusian Military District, which had numbered 250,000 troops at the beginning of the reform process. Supporting such a force was beyond the capabilities of the Republic's budget. In addition, the country's new military doctrine stipulates the forming of a nonnuclear force sufficient to protect the national sovereignty of Belarus. By the end of 1994, troop strength in the Belarusian Army will have fallen to 100,000.
