BFN [Text] Vilnius, Apr 06, BNS -- Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas on Tuesday [5 April] approved a new composition of the Central Privatization Commission. The commission is be headed by 55-year-old Deputy Economics Minister Kestutis Baranauskas. Seven out of twenty commission members, including the new chairman, were members of the previous privatization commission which resigned in mid-February. The earlier commission, headed by ex-Economics Minister Julius Veselka, resigned after the president made some sharp critical remarks about its work in his annual report earlier this year. The new commission is expected to gather for its first meeting this week. The previous commission held its last meeting Feb. 28.
