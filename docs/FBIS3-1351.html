Language: Somali Article Type:BFN [Text] An official general agreement was signed today during the Somali reconciliation conference, which was attended by the leaders of the country's political organizations in Nairobi, Kenya. The ceremony marks the signing of the Somali reconciliation agreement, which took place at the Safari Park Hotel. The signing was witnessed by Ambassador Lansana Kouyate, the interim United Nations special envoy to Somalia, politicians, journalists, and distinguished guests from all parts of the world. The UN-organized Somali national reconciliation conference had been in progress in Nairobi for a week before finally, at exactly 1300 East African time today, 24 March 1994, the agreement was signed by Ali Mahdi Mohamed, the president of the Somali Republic, who is also the representative of the Somali Salvation Alliance of 12 political organizations [SSA], and General Mohamed Farah Aidid, the representative of the Somali National Alliance [SNA]. The agreement will be of great importance to the future of the Somali people. During the signing ceremony, Ambassador Lansana Kouyate, Ali Mahdi Mohamed, and General Aidid delivered keynote speeches, all of them on the importance of the agreement for the future of the Somali people.
