BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Tamara Zamyatina, Yuriy Sizov, and Nikolay Sorokin] [Text] Naberezhnye Chelny May 30 TASS--Fighting organised crime in Russia must become a top priority task, Russian President Boris Yeltsin said. Speaking at a meeting with the leaders of Tatarstan's law enforcement bodies on Monday, Yeltsin called for working out a legislative framework for the implementation of the federal programme of combating organised crime in Russia adopted last week. He said special attention should be paid to fighting corruption, machinations in the credit and monetary system, tax evasion and illegal deals with privatisation vouchers.
