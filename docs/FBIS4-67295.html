CSO [Text] Bombay, March 31--The narcotics cell of the Bombay police last week seized a massive factory manufacturing methaqualone from which the drug Mandrax is made. This is the first time in India that such a large methaqualone factory has been seized, although experts believe that India is the largest producer of Mandrax. According to the chief of the narcotics cell, DCP [Deputy Commissioner of Police] Rahul Sur, Mandrax tablets and methaqualone powder each weighing 100 kg and collectively valued at Rs 20 lakhs were seized from the factory along with a Maruti car. The factory, masquerading as Ambala Organic Chemicals Pvt Ltd in an industrial estate in Kadhi, Mehsana district, Gujarat, was spread over 10,000 sq feet and had five boilers 12-feet high. It is believed to have been in operation for the past nine months. Six persons were arrested in this connection: Nitin Khimji Bhanushali (33) of Neelam Nagar, Mulund; Nayan Jetha Bhimji Bhanushali (28) of Sarvodaya Nagar, Mulund; Harish Kantibhai Patel (35) of Nadiad, Gujarat, and Manehdra Singh Sandhu (41), Kinit Jayantilal Amin (33), Amit Jayantibhai Patel (26), all of Ahmedabad. However, Prem Shetty, one of the biggest manufacturers of Mandrax tablets in India, who had advanced the money through hawala for the purchse of the factory, is believed to have fled India.
