BFN [Text] Beijing, April 13 (XINHUA) -- Qian Qichen, China's vice-premier and foreign minister, said here today that the Chinese Government attaches great importance to its relations with South Asian countries and supports the stand of establishing the South Asian nuclear-free zone and the Indian Ocean peace zone. Qian made the remarks when he held talks with Mustafizur Rahman, foreign minister of Bangladesh, here at the Diaoyutai State Guesthouse this afternoon. During the talks, Qian and Rahman exchanged views on bilateral relations, regional and international issues. Qian said that China pays close attention to its good neighborly relations with Bangladesh, and that the future for bilateral cooperation is excellent. Qian expressed his hope that the nation-to-nation issues between South Asian countries will be solved through peaceful negotiations, adding that those countries should conduct regional economic cooperation so as to develop the potentially great market in the region. Rahman expressed his satisfaction over the development of Bangladesh-China relations, saying that such relations were established on the basis of mutual trust and cooperation. He hoped that Bangladesh and China will build closer relations by joint efforts and further their economic and trade cooperation. Rahman and his party arrived here today as Qian's guests. Prior to the talks, also at the guesthouse this afternoon, Qian met with Pjeter Arbnori, president of the Albanian People's Parliament, and his party.
