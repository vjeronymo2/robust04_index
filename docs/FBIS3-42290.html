Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Uttar Pradesh, curfew has been imposed in (Babupurva) area of Kanpur following the death of a BJP [Bharatiya Janata Party] worker in a bomb attack. The situation in the area is stated to be tense, but under control. A senior police official told our Kanpur correspodent that as the news of the BJP worker's death spread an unruly mob set on fire the car of the minister of state for revenue, Mr. Babu Ram Yadav. However, the minister escaped unhurt. Additional companies of paramilitary forces are being rushed to Kanpur City. [Hong Kong AFP in English at 1443 GMT on 9 February, in a similar report, adds: "At least one person was killed, three others injured and some 10 homes burned in the northern city of Kanpur Wednesday after a Hindu politician was slain in a bomb blast," the PRESS TRUST OF INDIA (PTI) said. "Frenzied Hindus torched six houses and two shops, burned several cars and attacked government property following the death of Kala Bachha of the Hindu-revivalist Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP, Indian People's Party). "Bachha died instantly in the explosion and a Hindu friend accompanying him was seriously injured, the news agency said. "Further details of the bomb attack or the subsequent arson and violence were not available. "An official said a curfew had been imposed in certain sections of the city as a precautionary measure and educational institutions ordered to remain shut on Thursday. Security has also been stepped up."]
