Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 25 Jan (MENA)--Secret talks opened this evening between a Palestinian and Israeli delegation on implementing the Gaza-Jericho agreement. The Palestinian delegation includes Ahmad Quray', alias Abu-'Ala', the head of the PLO Economic Department; Dr. Nabil Sha'th, Yasir 'Arafat's political adviser; Sa'id Kamal, the Palestinian ambassador in Cairo; and Zuhdi al-Qidrah, Fatah's representative in Cairo. The Israeli delegation includes Yuri Savir, director general of the Israeli Foreign Ministry, and Yo'el Singer, legal adviser at the Foreign Ministry. Palestinian sources close to the meeting have stated that the talks, which have just started, might postpone the work of the Security and Civilian Affairs Committees due to be held in Taba; they could even replace them. The Israeli delegation is carrying Yitzhaq Rabin's reply to the Palestinian paper that Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa carried to Israel on resolving differences regarding the implementation of the self-government agreement in Gaza and Jericho. The talks are likely to continue tomorrow, probably until Thursday [27 January]. Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat is following the talks, which are being held in a hotel in the Heliopolis suburb, a few meters from his residence at the al-Andalus Palace.
