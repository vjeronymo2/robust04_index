BFN [Text] Beijing, 16 Jun (ZHONGGUO XINWEN SHE) -- Foreign Ministry spokesman Shen Guofang quipped this afternoon in reference to the DPRK's nuclear issue: "Lest we have a war on our lap even before there is an atomic bomb." Shen stated here at the routine news briefing that in principle, China is against the involvement of the UN Security Council in the DPRK's nuclear issue and the means of sanctions. He held that even now there remain both room and possibilities for properly solving the DPRK's nuclear issue. As long as the concerned four corners and three sides give 100 percent of what they have, they still can promote peace, stability, and denuclearization on the Korean peninsula through dialogue. Shen said that it is to this goal that China is directing its efforts. As quite a number of reporters had asked him about the possibility of the outbreak of war, Shen Guofang stressed more than once that right now he hopes that instead of talking about war, people should talk about how to solve the problem. So far, there is no evidence indicating that the DPRK has a nuclear arsenal. "Lest we have a war on our lap even before there is an atomic bomb." "No side wishes to see the outbreak of war, which would be highly unfavorable for Northeast Asia." Shen Guofang said that the channel between the United States and the DPRK remains open and he hopes for "reopening the dialogue." On China's recent nuclear test, Shen Guofang said: China has always been restrained in nuclear testing and is the country to conduct the fewest nuclear tests among the nuclear powers. Equally, China hopes to see a total ban on nuclear testing and to participate in talks related to the subject. Shen believes that the first step toward global denuclearization is that nuclear powers do not threaten to launch preemptive strikes.
