BFN [Text] Brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council, today received Djibouti Prime Minister Barkat Hamadou, who conveyed to him a letter from his brother Djibouti President Hassan Gouled. The letter dealt with bilateral relations, issues of interest to both fraternal countries, and developments in our country. The letter stressed Djibouti's concern for stability in our country and its support of Yemen's unity and democratic policy as well as Djibouti's readiness to make any efforts or offer any good offices to help our country circumvent the challenges imposed by the crisis. The letter pointed out that Yemen's security and stability are of concern to Djibouti and other regional states, led by the Horn of Africa states. The brother chairman of the Presidential Council voiced great appreciation for the genuine fraternal sentiments and the concern for supporting Yemeni unity, which is a pan-Arab gain for all sons of the Arab nation. He said our country cannot do without the efforts of any of its brethren, including those in Djibouti. The brother chairman pointed to the special Yemeni-Djibouti relations, which are close and historic ones. The Yemeni News Agency, SABA', has learned that the brother chairman asked the Djibouti prime minister to convey a reply message to his brother President Hassan Gouled. He wished Yemeni-Djibouti relations further progress and prosperity. The meeting was attended by Deputy Prime Minister 'Abd-al-Wahhab al-Anisi, Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah, and Djibouti Charge d'affires in Sanaa (Yusuf Omar Da'alah).
