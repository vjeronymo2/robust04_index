930616 FT 16 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television Remember that parody? 'Say what you will, school dinners make you ill, and shepherd's pie got Davy Crockett down, our school din-dins come from pig bins, out of town.' By all accounts today's school children are more likely to be offered pizza and chips than shepherd's pie. BBC2's access slot Open Space ends an interesting series with a programme lamenting the deterioration of that great British institution, the school dinner (7.55). That is followed at 8.25 by Freya Stark, the first of two repeated documentaries celebrating the life and work of the extraordinary writer and traveller who died last month. In 1982, when she was 90, Freya Stark was visited in her family home in the hills north of Venice by John Julius Norwich who talked to her of her bohemian childhood and her achievements as an explorer. Dispatches (9.00 C4) claims that it will reveal the identity of Deep Throat, the hitherto anonymous source whose information enabled Washington Post reporters Woodward and Bernstein to blow open the American political scandal which came to be known as Watergate.
