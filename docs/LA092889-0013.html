September 28, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COMPARING BUSH TO EISENHOWER

I too notice similarities, but it does not give me the contented feeling that Broder gets. Both men are caretaker Presidents bent on preserving the status quo. Today, I don't believe we can afford a president who is not a problem solver. We have the problems of budget and trade deficits, drugs, the failed policy of trying to police the world by spending billions to maintain servicemen overseas, the excessive debt by government, business and consumers, the policy of paying millions to a fascist government in El Salvador to support its death squads and paying the Contras to murder citizens of Nicaragua, to name a few.

PAUL A. MALLARD

Woodland Hills
