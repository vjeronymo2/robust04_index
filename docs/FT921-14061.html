920117 FT 17 JAN 92 / Institutions move to net buying BRITISH institutional investors became net buyers of UK government securities in the third quarter of last year after several years as net sellers, official figures disclosed yesterday. The Bank of England reported that long-term investors such as pension funds, insurance companies and investment, unit and property unit trusts bought a net Pounds 1bn of government securities in the quarter after being net sellers of nearly Pounds 3bn of such securities in the first half of last year. Life assurance companies were the heaviest purchasers in the latest quarter, buying Pounds 616m, while pension funds invested Pounds 549m. Life assurance companies invested heavily in a wide range of assets in the quarter.
