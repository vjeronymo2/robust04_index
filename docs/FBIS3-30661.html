Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vienna, Feb 3 (CTK) -- Czech officials informed the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) this afternoon about the fire that broke out at the Dukovany nuclear power plant this morning, IAEA spokesman Hans-Friedrich Meyer said today. Meyer said that the fire, which occurred in one of the transformers in the plant's third bloc, was completely extinguished in two hours and did not cause any leak of radioactive materials. The fire rated a zero on the INES International Nuclear Safety Scale. The Austrian environmental organization Global 2000, however, announced that Czech environmentalists from the Duha (Rainbow) movement had informed them that the accident rated a three on the INES scale. The environmentalists said that a secondary cooling system had been damaged, causing a radioactive leak. Local residents were warned by siren and doses of iodine were distributed, the group said.
