BFN [Text] Guyanese President Cheddi Jagan expressed concern over increased drug trafficking in Guyana and said that he was visiting Colombia with a high-raking police official who has been entrusted with learning how to fight this scourge. "We are a little scared about the growing threat of drug trafficking through Guyana, especially cocaine, which probably comes from Colombia through Venezuela," Jagan said today. He is on the last day of his visit. Jagan yesterday signed with President Cesar Gaviria a joint declaration agreeing to establish an official, private binational council to develop various aspects of the relationship. The Guyanese president yesterday visited the Cementos del Caribe cement factory on the Atlantic Coast and the sugar industry in Valle Department. Guyana is interested in both products.
