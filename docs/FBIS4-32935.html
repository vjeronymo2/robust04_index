BFN [From the NTV newscast] [Text] Ricardo Alarcon, president of the National Assembly of the People's Government [ANPP], asserted at the conclusion of his visit to the UK that he had perceived an atmosphere of friendship towards Cuba in his contact with government authorities, parliamentarians, and businessmen. Alarcon headed an ANPP delegation integrated by Lidia Tablada, who presides over the Friendship with Great Britain group of the ANPP, and Osvaldo Martinez, president of the Economic Affairs Commission of the ANPP. Upon his return to Cuba, Alarcon commented on the interest that the British Parliament expressed towards Cuba. Alarcon's visit to Great Britain included meetings with the top leaders of the House of Commons and the House of Lords. Alarcon informed this newscast that on 27 May, the agenda of the British Parliament included British-Cuban relations. [Begin Alarcon recording] There was a very interesting debate in the parliament regarding relations with Cuba. A government minister took part in this debate and very clearly reaffirmed Great Britain's policy of continuing and developing economic and commercial relations with Cuba. [end recording] Alarcon asserted that he had witnessed, in parliament, Great Britain's position with regard to the U.S. blockade against Cuba. [Begin Alarcon recording] In the parliamentary debate that I mentioned, MP Arnold, who is a leader of the Conservative Party and the one who brought up the issue, and the minister of commerce, who replied on behalf of the government, both clearly expressed Britain's disagreement with the blockade policy. A commitment to noncompliance with the blockade was clear, as was the intent to strengthen ties with Cuba. [end recording] Alarcon stated the British minister of commerce will visit Cuba in September. The same flight that brought the Cuban delegation back home carried Marcelino dos Santos, president of the National Assembly of Mozambique, who asserted that he came to learn, profoundly and scientifically, about the revolutionary process in Cuba.
