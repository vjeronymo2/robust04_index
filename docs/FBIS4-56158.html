BFN [Editorial Report] Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 26 Apr 94 p 1 reports that Central Bank Norms Director Claudio Mauch has announced that as of 25 April the banks are authorized to issue Bank Deposit Certificates, CDB's, and Bank Deposit Receipts, RDB's, in Real Value Units, URV's. These certificates will thus earn a lower annual interest rate plus monetary correction which will be determined at the maturity date. This means investors will know their profits on the maturity date of the document. Savings accounts will continue to earn the Referential Interest Rate, TR, plus a 6-percent annual interest. If the banks en mass adopt the URV, the Central Bank will have to modify the TR calculation formula. The TR is currently calculated on the interests paid by the prefixed CDB which is a preestablished interest-earning paper that does not receive monetary correction.
