Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Passages within quotation marks recorded] [Text] Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, the minister of state for foreign affairs, and Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Al Thani, the Qatari foreign minister, today held a news conference at al-Hashimiyah Palace. During the news conference, the two sides spoke about the issues they discussed during their meetings and the results of the Qatari minister's talks in Amman. Details are in the following report: At the outset of the news conference, Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, the minister of state for foreign affairs, welcomed the Qatari minister, stressing the similarity in the viewpoints of the two fraternal countries on various issues discussed during the visit. The Qatari foreign minister thanked His Majesty King Husayn and His Highness Crown Prince Hasan for the warm reception he received during his visit. He said: "I would like to express my deep thanks to His Majesty King Husayn and his highness the crown prince for the warm reception my accompanying delegation and I have received. Moreover, I thank his majesty for the medal he has awarded me. This is a great honor for me and for the brothers accompanying me during this visit. "As for the nature of the talks, of course, there were special talks between Qatar and Jordan and there were special talks about Arab cooperation and reunification. Moreover, there were talks that dealt with some international issues, especially the peace process and what is taking place on this course. We discussed these issues with total frankness and a logic that should prevail in our inter-Arab relations. "Of course, I conveyed a message from his highness the Amir [of Qatar] and received a reply from His Majesty King Husayn that falls within the context of the cooperation and ancient brotherly relations between the two. "The current higher joint committee will organize all the actions to be taken with respect to previous agreements. There is no reason why a visit by Qatar's heir apparent to Jordan should not take place in return for His Highness Crown Prince Hasan's visit. We are with Jordan whether his highness the crown prince visits Qatar or the Qatari heir apparent visits Jordan, as it is his second country." Asked about the role that Qatar could play to clear the Arab atmosphere, the Qatari foreign minister said: "In fact, the relations should be clear from the beginning and all Arab countries should be frank with each other, not in order to defame one another but to overcome any crisis and misunderstanding and to surpass difficulties and the crises the Arab world faced recently. "There is no doubt that Qatar is a small country. However, we hope that we will play a role that is not aimed at achieving any kind of victory but at clearing the Arab atmosphere. This is because we, the people and government of Qatar, believe that there can be no victory for the Arabs except through their unity. If we wanted to look back on the past, we would recall that Arab unanimity during the 1973 war was achieved because of frank Arab solidarity. All our victories were achieved through our solidarity, while all our problems and tragedies have been due to division and to the method of dealing which, we believe, should not prevail among Arab countries." Asked about Jordanian-Qatari relations, in general, the Qatari foreign minister said: "These relations, as I said earlier, are ancient relations. Our relations with Jordan were not affected at any time. There is no doubt that a misunderstanding took place during the recent Gulf crisis and the occupation of Kuwait. We believe that we should overcome this misunderstanding despite how it took place. This misunderstanding should be overcome and taken as a lesson for our future as an Arab nation. We should stand alongside right under any circumstances. This is what Qatar is hoping for." For his part, Talal al-Hasan, the minister of state for foreign affairs, summed up the results of the visit by saying: "At the end of this positive visit, which I hope will be repeated and will lead to more visits between the two countries, I would like to say that it was a successful and fruitful one. During this visit, we reached constructive agreements which, without a doubt, will have a positive reflection on relations between the two countries and will yield good results that will push the course of bilateral relations forward. I can very clearly tell you that understanding was very evident during all our meetings and that the identity in views was tangible in all the meetings that were held during this visit by his excellency the minister and his accompanying delegation. "This visit gains its uniqueness not only from the fact that it comes at this particular time, but also from the nature of the issues that were discussed during this visit and the great similarity in the views the two countries' leaderships hold on these issues. This is in addition to the two leadership's joint understanding of the need for serious and continuous action to correct the Arab situation in order to maintain Arab rights and protect this nation's higher joint interests. "It was natural for us to take this opportunity to discuss the various circumstances that face our nation, including Arab relations, the peace process, and the changes which regional and international developments impose on all of us. "Bilateral relations occupied an important part of our talks as we proceeded from a determination by His Majesty King Husayn and brother His Highness Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani, the amir of Qatar, to strengthen these relations and develop them in various fields until they reach the required level of strength and firmness. This determination was expressed during His Highness Crown Prince Hasan's visit to Doha last August and during this visit. "I am pleased to note that we reached an agreement on a practical formula that represents a mechanism to follow up the growth and development of bilateral relations in various fields by forming a joint committee at the foreign ministerial level. This joint committee will meet periodically in the capitals of both countries for this purpose. The committee's first meeting will be held during the first quarter of this year in Doha, God willing. "Moreover, we reviewed in detail the various agreements that organize bilateral relations."
