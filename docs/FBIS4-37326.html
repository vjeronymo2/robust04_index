BFN [Text] Cairo, 19 Jun (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa today received a visiting south Yemen delegation led by Salim Salih Muhammad, member of the Yemeni Presidential Council and assistant secretary general of the Yemeni Socialist Party. Muhammad said that during the meeting they discussed implementation of Security Council Resolution 924 and Egypt's role, particularly before the crisis erupted and during the fighting, and all of President Mubarak's appeals and stances. He noted that they also reviewed the efforts being made by Lakhdar Brahimi, envoy of the UN secretary general, especially his meeting today with a south Yemen representative on the committee that will discuss a cease-fire. He said: We agreed on this proposal, which the representative of the UN secretary general has submitted and we are ready for this meeting. He noted that a delegation representing the other party is expected in Cairo from Sanaa. He said that the representative of the UN secretary general is exerting efforts to bring about a cease-fire, noting that this is the most urgent issue at present. He pointed out that if a cease-fire is reached, the discussion between the two parties will focus on forming a delegation to debate the other issues. He said that a meeting between the southern and northern delegations hinges on a cease-fire and Sanaa's compliance with Security Council Resolution 924. He said that Sanaa is trying to circumvent the issue and mark time. He said: We will turn to the Security Council to pass a new resolution deterring any party from violating Resolution 924, adding that right now this is what is required. Muhammad said that we will decide whether or not to go the Security Council in light of the contacts being made by UN Envoy Lakhdar Brahimi. Muhammad said that fierce fighting continues and is focusing on encircling the Aden Governorate, and on finding loopholes to make the public feel that Aden is coming under an offensive. He noted that courageous resistance is confronting the attackers and things are beginning to change, particularly in the governorates of Shabwah and Lahij. He said that popular resistance has begun in these governorates. He noted that Aden has a cause adding: We are a recognized entity and are facing an invasion and occupation and our homes are being destroyed. He said: We have a right to request aid to defend our lives and future. He pointed out that the countries that supply south Yemen with arms are countries that support right and respect neighborliness, peace, and stability in the region. He said that these are Arab and friendly countries. He stressed that the war will yield other results and that negotiations will determine the Yemeni future, noting that the future will not be the same as it was before the war. He emphasized that unity cannot be imposed by force, noting that when we established unity it was agreed upon voluntarily. He said that regrettably this unity was destroyed from within by forces that do not want unity and who believe that unity is blood and violating the rights of others. He said that a unity that is not based on sound and principled foundations cannot survive because rockets and guns have destroyed this fragile unity.
