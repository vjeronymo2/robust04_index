Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] During a meeting in La Paz, Bolivia, to discuss the struggle against drug trafficking, Chilean and Bolivian Government representatives agreed to exchange information on juridical affairs, customs, drug trafficking and the prevention of drug abuse, and social rehabilitation programs. Gustavo Villalobos, secretary of the Chilean National Council for Drug Control, noted that the level of cooperation between the two countries is very high even though they do not maintain diplomatic relations. Villalobos added that the bilateral cooperation efforts to fight trafficking also benefit other countries in the region. Bolivian Social Defense Secretary Marcos Solis said the struggle against trafficking requires considerable international solidarity. The two countries agreed to exchange judicial resolutions and letters rogatory. The representatives also discussed the possibility of extraditing traffickers.
