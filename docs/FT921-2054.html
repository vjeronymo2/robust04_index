920320 FT 20 MAR 92 / Election 1992: Howard's way - Ballot Box Michael Howard, the employment secretary, was missing yesterday from the official release of the monthly labour figures. He was out campaigning, rather than giving his usual cheery gloss. He may also have run out of gloss. For the past 18 months the line has been that UK unemployment is lower than the European average. Truth be told, among the main industrialised countries the rate is higher than Britain's only in Spain and the Irish Republic. The OECD puts UK unemployment at 10.6 per cent, higher than the government's own figure of 9.4 per cent. In Italy the rate is 9.9 per cent, in France 9.8 per cent, in the US 7.3 per cent, and in the old West Germany 4.3 per cent. One could go on.
