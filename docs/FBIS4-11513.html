BFN [Text] Caracas, 24 Apr (EFE)--Nine Venezuelan Air Forces officers who participated in the attempted coup on 27 November 1992 were released Saturday and will return to their duties. Charges against the nine officers, who took asylum in Peru after the second military coup attempt, were dismissed by a decree issued by President Rafael Caldera. The officers are Captains Senibaldo Palacios, Wilfredo Santiago Sanchez Trujillo, Wilson Marin Leal, Luis Antonio Romero, Daniel Pereira, Jean Luis Duran Siso, and Sanchez Trujillo. Charges were also dismissed against Lieutenants Fidias Garrido and Alexis Montilla. Only three of the officers who returned to Venezuela to surrender to justice continue to be detained at the San Carlos prison in northern Caracas. There are still 13 coupist officers in Peru, including General Visconti Osorio, one of the leaders of the 27 November 1992 military insurrection. In Venezuela, 21 soldiers are still being held for rebellion. The soldiers are headed by Admiral Luis Cabrera Aguirre, who is held at the San Carlos prison together with 13 officers and a soldier. Virginia Contreras, the defense lawyer of the coupist soldiers, said to reporters that the cases against the officers who are still under arrest should also be dismissed and that they should be allowed to return to the National Armed Forces.
