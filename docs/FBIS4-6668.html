BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 21 April Morning Edition] [Text] In connection with the issue of how to deal with the DPRK's suspected development of nuclear weapons, which is one of the focal issues in the ruling coalition parties' ongoing policy coordination meeting, a government source said on the morning of 20 April that if a naval blockade is implemented as part of economic sanctions by the UN Security Council, Japan "will not participate in sanctions premised on the use of force." He thus indicated that Japan will not participate in such a naval blockade. While a blockade is meant to ensure the effectiveness of economic sanctions, and no use of force is involved in the objective and mission of such an action, "corresponding measures" are permitted under "certain conditions"--for instance, if the other party resorts to armed resistance.
