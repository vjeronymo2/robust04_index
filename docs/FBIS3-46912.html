Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] During a YOMIURI SHIMBUN interview on 27 December, Shinseito [Japan Renewal Party] leader Tsutomu Hata, who is deputy prime minister and concurrently foreign minister, speaking on the outlook for deliberations on the political reform bills said: "Since the electoral system is an issue regarding rules in a democracy, meetings should be held (between ruling and opposition parties) to mutually discuss ways to reach a compromise." He thus reiterated the need for ruling and opposition parties to reach a compromise for the passage of the political reform bills. Concerning the view expressed by some politicians that the lower house of the Diet should be dissolved if the political reform bills fail to become law during the current Diet session, Hata said: "We should be ready and willing to do just that." However, he also said: "Dissolving the Diet is not such a simple matter."
