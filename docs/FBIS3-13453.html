Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [From the press review] [Text] Discussing the UN Security Council's deliberations on the Hebron massacre, AL-BA'TH writes in its "With the Events" column: Awaiting a UN Security Council resolution and following world demand for an Israeli withdrawal and the implementation of the Geneva Conventions on the protection of civilians in time of war, the threats to peace, posed by the Ibrahimi Mosque massacre, have not gone away. Nor will the massacre lose its influence on the credibility of the UN Security Council, which is still holding consultations as if the massacre were less significant than Lockerbie or less vicious than the reasons that prompted UN troops to land in more than one capital of the post-Cold War world.
