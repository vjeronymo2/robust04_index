Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 31 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese leaders will lead a high-level delegation to attend the United Nations world summit for social development to be held in Denmark in March 1995, a Foreign Ministry spokesman said here this afternoon. Speaking at a weekly press conference spokesman Wu Jianmin said the UN world summit will be an "important event" which provides the world leaders with a good opportunity to explore together ways to promote social development. It will also contribute to further international cooperation in this field, Wu added. He noted that China supports the convocation of this summit and has taken an active part in its preparation in the UN organizations. "We have set up a preparatory committee and are engaged in a number of activities, including a nationwide conference on social development, drafting the national report on China's social development, formulating the outline of China's social development, an exhibition on the achievements in social development after the founding of the People's Republic of China and an international symposium," he said. The Chinese delegation to attend the summit will carry out active and constructive cooperation with other countries and will work hard for positive results of the summit, the spokesman said.
