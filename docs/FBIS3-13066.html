Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] The head of the Tajik opposition delegation has stressed that the opposition believes that an impartial interim government should assume power and hold free elections to end the current crisis. Speaking in an interview in Tehran yesterday, Akbar Turjanzadeh said: A popular rule that the Tajik people want should be established through free elections. He described the role played by the Islamic Republic of Iran in solving the Tajikistan crisis as noteworthy and said: During our talks in Tehran with the Russian Government delegation, it was agreed that the first round of talks for the solution of the Tajikistan crisis be held in Moscow with the participation of representatives from the Islamic Republic of Iran, Pakistan, Afghanistan, and the United Nations. In conclusion, he called for the active role of the Islamic Conference Organization in solving the Tajikistan crisis.
