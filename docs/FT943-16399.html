940705 FT 05 JUL 94 / Optimism from Ulster police chief Northern Ireland republicans who want a peaceful way forward in the province are 'probably, just probably, in the ascendancy,' Royal Ulster Constabulary chief constable Sir Hugh Annesley said yesterday. He said when launching the force's annual report: 'On balance I am optimistic, because I still very firmly believe that the Downing Street declaration has pushed the Provisionals into a cul-de-sac.' The accord had led to the British and Irish people being ranged against the IRA.
