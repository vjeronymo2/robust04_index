BFN [Text] Niamey, 14 Apr (AFP) -- Muslim fundamentalists who took refuge in Kolouka after occupying the neighboring town of Bani Bangou, (180 km to the north of Niamey), began to surrender at midday today, Niger Interior Minister Ousmane Oumarou told AFP. A detachment of the Nigerian Army encircled the village in the early hours of the morning and threatened to attack if the fundamentalists, who are members of the Izala sect, did not surrender. On 11 and 12 March, clashes between members of the sect and security forces left 10 dead at Kolouka. The fundamentalists succeeded in leaving Bani Bangou during the evening of 12 March to return to their village.
