940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / International Company News: Record trading at May Stores May Department Stores, the biggest US department store group, said First-half sales and earnings reached record highs and outlined plans for significant increases in the number of stores operated. Second-quarter net income rose by 11 per cent to Dollars 130m from Dollars 117m on turnover 6.5 per cent up at Dollars 2.62bn. Fully-diluted earnings per share were 49 cents, an increase of 11 per cent from the 44 cents a year earlier. For the six months to July, turnover was up 9 per cent at Dollars 5.15bn while net income was 13 per cent higher at Dollars 242m. Fully-diluted earnings per share were ahead by 14 per cent, at 90 cents. May Department Stores operates 302 department stores and 3,869 Payless ShoeSource stores. At its annual meeting earlier this year, it announced plans to open 110 department stores, 1,200 Payless ShoeSource stores and 1,000 Payless Kids stores over the next five years, at a cost of Dollars 4bn. At the end of last month it significantly expanded its shoe retailing operation by buying all 679 shoe stores owned by Kobacker and The Shoe Works, two related companies based in Columbus, Ohio. Yesterday May said it had opened a Famous-Barr department store in Jamestown Mall, St Louis, during the quarter, and that another 14 department stores would open by the year end: five Lord & Taylor, four Filene's, two Foley's, one Hecht's, one Robinsons-May, and one Famous-Barr.
