941213 FT 13 DEC 94 / International Company News: Further reprieve for US airline Trans World Airlines, the US carrier struggling to avoid a liquidity crisis, yesterday said two aircraft lessors had agreed to defer payments due in November, allowing the airline to save Dollars 15m in cash and keep four Boeing 767-200 aircraft in its fleet, writes Richard Tomkins. It also said an informal committee of preferred stockholders had agreed a proposal for the company's financial restructuring, under which they would get three times more equity in the company than holders of the common stock. In October, TWA asked creditors to swap Dollars 800m of the company's Dollars 1.8bn of debt for new equity.
