Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Portuguese Minister of Foreign Affairs Durao Barroso is visiting South Africa, but is thinking about Angola. The foreign minister told us that the Lusaka agreement is technically ready, but it has not been signed yet because there is no political will: [Begin recording] [Durao Barroso] From a technical point of view, the documents are at a rather advanced stage. I met moments ago with a representative of the negotiating team who confirmed that news. The fundamental problem is whether there is the will on either side for a true national reconciliation agreement to be signed. [Correspondent Carlos Pacheco] But does this [the will] exist or not? [Barroso] So far it has not existed. [Pacheco] And, according to what you have just said, what will be the logical outcome of the Lusaka negotiations? [Barroso] Well, a logical outcome would be a positive conclusion, but that will obviously be subject to a political rectification, at the highest level, from both sides. I cannot foresee it at the moment. [Pacheco] Do the conditions exist for that to happen? [Barroso] At the same time as there has been progress at the Lusaka talks, the fact is that the situation has been worsening on the ground. [end recording] [Announcer] That was Portuguese Minister of Foreign Affairs Durao Barroso talking to our correspondent.
