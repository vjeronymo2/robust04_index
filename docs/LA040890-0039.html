April 8, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

A SILLY DEFINITION OF THE 'HUMAN' SCALE

Re "Urban but Human" (March 22) about Michael Graves' proposed project in downtown Los Angeles: While so-called post-modern architects are trying to out-silly each other, the article's writer joins in shrieking, "How beautiful are the Emperor's clothes!"

A 30-story stylized Godzilla is considered to be in "human" scale? Somebody should rush to the dictionary and find out what that word means.

TED WU, Los Angeles
