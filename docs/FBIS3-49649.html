Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Belgrade, 11 Jan (TANJUG) -- Deputy Serb Republic (RS) President Nikola Koljevic said today that during the talks yesterday with the Japanese diplomat Yasushi Akashi, the new head of the UN Protection Force [UNPROFOR] for the former Yugoslavia, and Francis Briquemont, the military commander of the peacekeeping forces for Bosnia-Herzegovina, the Serbs got assurances that there can be no military intervention without the consent of these high-ranking UN representatives, and that they "in no way favor a military option as something desirable or something with high priority." In the Belgrade radio program "One Argument More" today, Koljevic said the Serbs, for their part, have offered everything demanded from them and have been cooperative, in the belief that this is the price of peace.
