Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vyacheslav Bantin] [Text] Tokyo January 23 TASS -- During the forthcoming Russian-Japanese talks between representatives of the command of the armed forces of the two countries, to be held here at the beginning of February, the Japanese side intends to demand complete withdrawal of Russian troops from South Kurils, which Tokyo lays claim to. A final decision to hold such talks was taken by the Japanese government on Saturday, the newspaper SEKAI NIPPO reported on Sunday. Representatives of the command of the Russian Armed Forces are to arrive in Tokyo to attend a two-day seminar for the military and experts from Russia, the United States, and Japan on security in the northern Pacific. The seminar opens on February 1. Immediately after the seminar, the two sides will hold talks. The Russian side intends to suggest setting about taking measures to build confidence between the two countries in the military field. In particular, Moscow will suggest implementing a programme for exchanges of servicemen and mutual friendship visits of the navy ships of the two countries. The Japanese military, according to the newspaper, are planning to urge Moscow to demilitarise the South Kurils as soon as possible and provide Tokyo with full information concerning the potential of the armed forces and defence construction in Russia's Far East.
