BFN [Text] Tunis, 10 Apr (MENA)--Palestinian information sources have stated that President Yasir 'Arafat chaired a meeting of the Palestinian leadership yesterday and the day before yesterday to discuss the deteriorating economic situation of the Palestinians due to the Israeli blockade that has been imposed on the occupied territories for 45 days. This blockade, the sources added, was causing poverty and hunger in the territories. The sources said that 'Arafat briefed the participants on the reports that he had received from Palestinian officials in Gaza and Hebron. The reports indicated that poverty and hunger have spread due to the Israeli military blockade and the ban on 130,000 Palestinian workers from reporting to their work inside Israel. According to the sources, 'Arafat had taken a number of urgent measures to avert further exacerbation of the crisis and to foil the Israeli attempt to import workers from abroad to replace the Palestinian workers, who are prevented from going to their work in Israel. The sources said that, during his meeting with the ambassadors of the UN Security Council permanent member states in Cairo last week, 'Arafat briefed them on the tragic condition of well over two million Palestinians. 'Arafat told the envoys that this condition has been caused by Israel's disregard of its international commitments and its evasion of implementing the articles of the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles. 'Arafat voiced his stern rejection of Israel's extortionist policy, according to the sources.
