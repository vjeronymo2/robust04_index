BFN [Text] India is expected to receive thousands of defense spare parts and critically required ammunition from Russia and Ukraine within three months. Addressing the naval commanders conference in New Delhi today, the minister of state for defense, Mr. Mallikarjun, said this was successfully negotiated during the visit of a defense delegation to the countries early this month. He expressed concern at the increasing belligerency and weapons acquisition beyond its requirements by Pakistan. Referring to the low intensity conflict by Pakistan to destabilize India, the minister lauded the efforts of Navy and the Coast Guard in countering seaborne operations of subversive elements. He stressed the need for self-reliance and highlighted the necessity to undertake multi-institutional research to build all types of vessels and sophisticated warships.
