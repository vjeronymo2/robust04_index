940526 FT 26 MAY 94 / World Trade News: Boeing eyes small jet market Boeing, the world's largest commercial aircraft manufacturer, is stepping up efforts to enter for the first time the market for smaller 70-100 seat jets. The US group, which has traditionally dominated the large airliner market, is working with the Japanese and Chinese aircraft industries to study the development of a new commercial jetliner smaller than its current Boeing 737 family of 100-170 seat narrow-body aircraft. Boeing yesterday said it had appointed Mr Richard James, its former vice-president of marketing for commercial aircraft programmes, to a new position of vice-president to study the development of a new small aircraft.
