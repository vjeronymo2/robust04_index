BFN [Text] The Prime Minister [Cavaco Silva] sent a verbal protest to the Philippine Government in connection with the expulsion of Portuguese representatives at the Manila conference on East Timor. This is the first government reaction to events involving the controversial conference under way in the Philippines. [Begin Cavaco Silva recording] A few days ago the Portuguese Government presented a verbal note to the Philippine Government protesting the fact that visas were not granted to Portuguese people, including Portuguese deputies, enabling them to participate in the Manila conference. At the same time the Portuguese Government expresses its surprise at the fact that a country we consider to be friendly has bowed to pressure from a dictatorship -- the Indonesian dictatorship. We expressed our surprise, our apprehension at the fact that obstacles were placed in the way of Portuguese citizens and foreign citizens who wanted to participate in the Manila conference. [end recording]
