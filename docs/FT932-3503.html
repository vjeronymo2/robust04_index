930614 FT 14 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television Keep your fingers crossed and hope that BBC2 does not decide to 'update' coverage of the Cardiff Singer Of The World contest as it did the Young Musician competition, turning it into a chat show with scrappy musical inserts. The preliminary rounds will be shown each evening this week (BBC2 6.30 today) The jury includes Joan Sutherland and Marilyn Horne. Horizon (8.00 BBC2) tells of the recent discovery that, of all things, the poisonous gas nitric oxide plays a vital role in the working of the human body. Damage to production of the gas is connected with conditions such as epilepsy, hypertension, diabetes and multiple sclerosis. Rob Buckman, one half of the much mourned cabaret duo Beatles and Buckman presents the first in a series of four programmes taking a critical look at the state of conventional medicine: Magic Or Medicine? (9.00 C4). Panorama reports from Aldermaston on 'Secrets Of The Citadel': safety at Britain's top secret centre where the next generation of nuclear weapons is being constructed (9.30 BBC1).
