Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Qian Qichen, China's vice premier and foreign minister, has emphasized the need of increased cooperation between Pakistan and China in the wake of the recent changes in the international situation. He expressed this feeling during separate meetings with Senate Chairman Wasim Sajjad and National Assembly Speaker Syed Yusuf Raza Gilani in Islamabad. The Senate chairman and the National Assembly speaker explained the latest position regarding human rights violations in occupied Kashmir. While referring to Pakistan's efforts to move a resolution at the UN Human Rights Commission on this, they reiterated Pakistan's commitment to a peaceful solution of the Kashmir issue. Referring to the Indian press reports on the implications of the disengagement of troops at the Sino-Indian border, Qian Qichen said the Indian news agencies are indulging in propaganda tactics in this matter. He said China will not allow a situation in which India can take advantage of Pakistan.
