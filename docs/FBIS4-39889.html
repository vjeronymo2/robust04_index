BFN [Text] Vilnius, June 11, BNS -- Lithuanian Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys at a meeting with NATO Assistant Secretary General Sergio Balanzino in Istanbul Friday delivered Lithuania's set of propositions for activity under the NATO Partnership for Peace program, the Foreign Ministry press office said. Gylys takes part in Istanbul in a foreign ministers meeting of the North Atlantic Cooperation Council [NACC]. The document delivered to the NATO assistant secretary general lays the basis for outlining a separate cooperation program between Lithuania and NATO under the partnership plan. The Lithuanian foreign minister is his speech before the NACC meeting pronounced his country's wish that the NATO partnership program would take into account the needs of each participating country. In his words the Partnership for Peace program allows active partners to play a great role in expanding the alliance. Gylys also referred in his speech to the problem of Russian military transits to the Kaliningrad enclave through Lithuania's territory. He underscored that finding a solution to the issue must not be linked to carrying out the stipulations of the Lithuanian- Russian agreements that have been signed earlier.
