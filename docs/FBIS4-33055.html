BFN [Text] Caracas, 28 May (EFE) -- The Venezuelan Government has put an end to the uncertainty surrounding the future of state-owned power companies by announcing today that, starting in September, these companies will be privatized. Eduardo Calero, manager of the electrical sector of the Venezuelan Investment Fund (FIV), said that initially the Central Plant and Enelven-Enelco [expansion unknown] companies will be auctioned, and in early 1995 the process will continue with the privatization of two other regional companies. The transfer of the power companies to the private sector has been planned for over four years, but during that period various factors emerged--most of them political--which prevented the process from materializing. According to Calero, the government no longer considers privatization as a means of reducing the size of government but as "an immediate financial necessity," prompted by the acute economic crisis the country is experiencing, which has become worse because of the accelerated devaluation of the national currency. The FIV official also announced that within the short term power rates will be revised because companies in the region have been seriously affected by the devaluation of the Venezuelan bolivar with regard to the U.S. dollar. Products, imported raw materials, and the payment of debts abroad now pose a considerable problem for the power companies, which must pay their commitments in dollars, while their rates are fixed in a currency that has been devaluated by 30 percent over the past two months, Calero said. In any event, he explained, the cost of the devaluation will not be directly transferred to the rates; instead, he said, "the increase in rates will depend on the extent to which each power company is willing to assume part of the expense caused by the weakening of Venezuela's currency."
