CSO [Editorial Report] Managua LA TRIBUNA in Spanish of 11 April 1994 on page 8B reports that the Nicafish and Pananica enterprises signed a $6.15 million investment agreement with the Ministry of Economy and Development. The two enterprises, which have Panamanian assets, are involved in fishing and processing seafood in San Juan del Sur. According to the article, Pananica will invest $4.15 million to purchase 15 fishing boats and hire 85 employees. The remaining $2 million will be invested by Nicafish, which was recently taken over by the National Corporation of the Public Sector. The article explains that the government hopes seafood exports will range between $40 and $65 million in 1994, compared to $32 million last year.
