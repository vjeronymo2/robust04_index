Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Kulbitskiy] [Text] Bonn February 20 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yetlsin and German Federal Chancellor Helmut Kohl during a telephone conversation at the end of last week agreed on the need to hold a meeting of the leaders of a number of countries to find a solution to the Bosnian conflict, the newspaper BILD AM SONNTAG reported on Sunday [20 February]. The summit is expected to be attended by Yeltsin, U.S. President Bill Clinton, and by the leaders of Britain, France, and Germany, the newspaper pointed out. Yeltsin and Kohl also reached a consensus that the nascent process of the normalisation of the situation around Sarajevo should become a model for the entire Bosnia-Herzegovina. This means a truce, the withdrawal of heavy guns from the areas of combat operations, and control over the situation on the part of U.N. peacekeepers, including those from Russia.
