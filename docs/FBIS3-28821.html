Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 10 February Evening Edition] [Text] With regard to the issue of suspected nuclear arms development by the DPRK (North Korea), a government source said on the morning of 10 February: "If no progress is made at the negotiations, there will be no other choice but to launch economic sanctions." Thus, he disclosed his view that if North Korea should keep on refusing nuclear inspections requested by the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), economic sanctions by the UN Security Council would become unavoidable. As for the Japanese Government's actions if the UN Security Council's decides to adopt economic sanctions, the same source said: "As far as Japan is concerned, there is no other choice but to cooperate. In other words, we will do our utmost within the framework of the Constitution."
