930119 FT 19 JAN 93 / Recovery to UK hoteliers increases to 27.1 per cent THE proportion of UK hoteliers who think recovery has begun increased to 27.1 per cent in the last quarter of 1992 compared with 13 per cent in the previous quarter according to a survey by leisure consultants Horwath, Michael Skapinker writes. Although 72.9 per cent said they did not think the hotel industry had begun to recover, this compared with 87 per cent in the previous quarter. The number of hoteliers who were optimistic about the first quarter of this year rose from 32 per cent to 35 per cent, while the proportion who were pessimistic fell to 40 per cent from 47 per cent.
