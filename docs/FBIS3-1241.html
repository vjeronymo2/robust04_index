Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Transitional Executive Council [TEC] has adopted a resolution proposing that the National Assembly meet for the first time on 6 May in Cape Town, to elect South Africa's new president. The TEC said the constitution stipulated that the chief justice is required to convene the first meeting of the National Assembly within 10 days of the election. The president would be elected at that meeting. The TEC proposal suggested the formal inauguration and swearing in of the president be held on 10 May at the Union Buildings in Pretoria. The TEC said the first meeting of each provincial legislature would be held on, or before 5 May this year.
