940924 FT 24 SEP 94 / Survey of Residential Property (8): Hostel takeovers Freehold pubs on offer from Business Sales Group (071-439 6617) range from the Horse and Groom at Caulcott near the Oxford Canal in Oxfordshire for Pounds 97,500 (which takes Pounds 1,700 a week net of VAT, split evenly between wet and dry sales) to the Horse and Groom at Upper Oddington near Stow-on-the-Wold in the Cotswolds for Pounds 435,000, which brings a cottage, 2.25 acres including a stream and trout pond, and seven en-suite letting bedrooms (producing about 20 per cent of the Pounds 250,000 net annual takings). Near Winchester the Chestnut Horse in Easton ('a monied village'), costing Pounds 410,000, has a small 'trade patio garden' and a popular pub menu. In the north-east of England the Four Alls on the village green at Ovington near Barnard Castle costs Pounds 225,000 from the Leeds office (0532-446 266). In the West Country, the Plymouth office (0752-251 545) is, selling the Ebrington Arms at Braunton between Barnstaple and Ilfracombe, with five bedrooms and a 38 cover restaurant and a net turnover of Pounds 180,000 (in the year to August 1993), for Pounds 295,000, down from Pounds 320,000. Also reduced are the New Inn in Priddy near Wells 'popular with cavers and walkers' (Pounds 445,000, from Pounds 475,000), and the Tower Inn at Slapton in Devon, now Pounds 299,950 from Pounds 400,000.
