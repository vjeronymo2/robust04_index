June 17, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

SANTA FE VIEWPOINT

The article by Jerry Hulse on Santa Fe was interesting, colorful and informative. How I envy him.

I'd like to add a few facts that may interest your readers so that they can become aware of the historic significance of Santa Fe and the history of the people who founded it.

Many of us do not have the time or inclination to study history. But articles such as yours can give us incentive to travel as well as to educate us in a delightful way.

As a genealogy buff, I have had the pleasure of researching my roots four centuries in America -- exactly 392 years. Twenty-eight of my ancestors came to New Mexico with the Juan de Onate Expedition in 1598, hence most of the founders of Santa Fe were my ancestors.

So you see, Santa Fe isn't just art galleries and blue corn tortillas. Santa Fe is history, real people. People with dreams, people with faith who dared to leave their beloved Spain to find a new life in a new world. Every building has a story or a ghost, each path taken a destiny.

When I visit the plaza, I sit in the warm sun listening to the voices of the past. I hear the church bells calling the people to prayer, the laughter of little children. I envision the secret places where lovers met, far from the eyes of disapproving parents. I feel overcome with sadness and anger as I imagine the deals that were made by unscrupulous Americans as they conspired to take over New Mexico in 1846. But I smile as I see the melding of the three cultures -- Spanish, Indian and Anglo -- all trying to live peacefully in this most beautiful of cities.

PAULINE CHAVEZ BENT

Huntington Beach
