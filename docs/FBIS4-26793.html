BFN [By reporter Chen Jiaying (7115 1367 3841)] [Text] Lisbon, 21 Jun (XINHUA) -- Portuguese Foreign Affairs Minister Durao Barroso pointed out in Lisbon on 21 June: Portugal and China are comprehensively developing friendly and cooperative relations in the political, economic, and cultural fields. While meeting and feting outgoing Chinese Ambassador to Portugal Wu Tao, Foreign Minister Barroso said: After establishing diplomatic relations in 1979, particularly after the Portuguese and Chinese Governments signed a joint communique on the Macao issue in 1987, the two countries have been enhancing bilateral relations in an all-around manner. Barroso said: Portugal and China have different national conditions and systems. However, their ways of resolving problems left over by history and of handling relations between the two countries can be termed exemplary. He expressed the hope that Portugal and China will further enhance bilateral relations and will continually make contributions to advancing the common interests of the Portuguese and Chinese people and to facilitating global peace and prosperity. The Portuguese foreign minister also pointed out: At present, the Portuguese and Chinese Governments are making joint efforts to facilitate a smooth transfer of Macao. Macao has become a link for the two countries to enhance bilateral relations. Portugal and China will still continue to maintain friendly relations for generations after 1999.
