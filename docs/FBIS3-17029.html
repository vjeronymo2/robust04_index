Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Early this morning, Russia's Defense Minister Army General Pavel Grachev, left for Tajikistan. [Begin Grachev recording] First, these are planned exercises involving units from the 201st [motorized rifle] Division. Second, they are coalition exercises, since subunits from the Armed Forces of Tajikistan and Uzbekistan will participate in the exercises. The whole essence of these exercises is that for the first time, the exercises are taking place with the subunits of various states, that is, CIS states. I have set myself the main task of checking the combat readiness of the units in the 201st Division, their military training, the coordination of the subunits, and also the individual training of NCOs, privates, and officers. [end recording] [Correspondent] But it is not just the joint exercises that interest the minister; Grachev is taking a collection of musical instruments on behalf of the Russian Children's Foundation. Humanitarian aid is also going with him, sent by General Burlakov, the commander of the Western Group of Forces. All this is for the children of the 201st Division of Russian troops in Tajikistan.
