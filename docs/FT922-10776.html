920429 FT 29 APR 92 / Control of press moves to Mellor MR DAVID Mellor's national heritage department is be given responsibility for considering press regulation, Raymond Snoddy writes. It had been unclear whether this responsibility would remain with the Home Office after the creation of the new department. Mr Mellor, heritage secretary, will decide whether self-regulation through the Press Complaints Commission has worked or whether statutory controls are needed. Following publication in June 1990 of the report of the Calcutt Committee on privacy, the government said it would look again at how self-regulation of the press was working in two years' time. When he was a Home Office minister Mr Mellor warned that the popular press was 'drinking in the Last Chance Saloon.'
