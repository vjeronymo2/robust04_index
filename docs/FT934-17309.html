931001 FT 01 OCT 93 / UK Company News: Buy-out at Royal Bank arm THE ROYAL Bank of Scotland yesterday announced that it had agreed to a management buy-out of the private client business of Charterhouse Tilney, its stockbroking arm. The new company, to be called Tilney & Co, will be bought for about Pounds 5.5m cash, to be based on an adjusted net asset value at completion. The chief executive will be Mr John Mitchell, who is currently managing director of Tilney. It has more than 35,000 clients, 250 staff in nine offices and Pounds 3.8bn under management. A 90.1 per cent stake in Charterhouse Tilney is being sold to Credit Commercial de France and Berliner Handels-und-Frankfurter Bank, as announced in July. It will be called Charterhouse Tilney Securities. Both sales are in line with the Royal Bank's strategy of focusing on its core commercial banking and insurance businesses.
