BFN [Text] Tianjin, April 20 (XINHUA) -- Wang Yan, general manager of the China National Offshore Oil Corporation (CNOOC), has said in Tianjin that China's offshore oil industry will see rapid development. At a conference at the Bohai Sea Oil Corporation, he said that this year the output of offshore crude oil is expected to reach 5.5 million tons. He said that in 1997, the oil output will be 12 million tons and the output of natural gas will be 4 billion cubic meters. He said that his corporation will make full use of domestic and foreign funds, resources and markets so as to turn it into an international petroleum corporation. By 1997, the corporation will have six oilfields in production. At present, it is undertaking several infrastructure projects for opening up the oilfields, including the building of the Guangdong Huizhou Oil Refinery, which will be completed at the end of 1997.
