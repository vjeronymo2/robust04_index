Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] While waiting for the establishment of the broad-based transitional institutions, the UN Assistance Mission in Rwanda [UNAMIR] has been reinforced by the arrival of new blue helmets. A contingent of 225 Ghanaian troops, part of the 800-strong Ghanaian battalion, landed in Kigali yesterday. Its main task is to maintain security in the whole demilitarized area. The other groups making up the Ghanaian battalion are expected before the end of the month. The commander of the Ghanaian contingent, Brigadier General Andoh, who has been in Rwanda for the past month, is also the second in command of the UNAMIR headquarters. With the arrival of new observers over the next few days, the number of UN mission troops will increase to 2,600 troops and observers.
