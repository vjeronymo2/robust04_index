940126 FT 26 JAN 94 / Call for Islamic economic co-operation Iran's president said yesterday economic co-operation between the 10 Islamic members of the Economic Co-operation Organisation in the Middle East and central Asia could reduce political tensions affecting them, Reuter reports from Tehran. President Ali Akbar Hashemi Rafsanjani was opening a meeting of the foreign ministers of the 10 states that include Pakistan, Turkey, Afghanistan and six former Soviet republics. ECO was founded by neighbours Turkey, Iran and Pakistan in the 1950s as a regional trading bloc but was largely dormant until it was expanded two years ago to become the largest non- Arab Islamic organisation stretching from Europe to China.
