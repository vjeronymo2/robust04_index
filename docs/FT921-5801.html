920302 FT 02 MAR 92 / UK News (Employment): Watchdog criticises mortgage lenders MORTGAGE lenders are making the homes repossession crisis worse by insisting on 'unrealistic' repayment schedules for borrowers in arrears, the National Consumer Council said yesterday. Many lenders failed to give borrowers struggling with repayments enough help, according to the council, a government-funded watchdog. 'All too often mortgage lenders do not contact customers in arrears until too late and, by insisting on unrealistic repayment schemes, only make repossessions more likely,' it said. Its study follows last month's report from the Council of Mortgage Lenders showing that more than 500,000 households were behind in mortgage payments. Lady Wilcox, who chairs the consumer council, said mortgage lenders were 'in business to help people buy homes, not to take their homes away from them'. The lenders' council rejected the claim. 'It is not in the lenders' interests to sit back and do nothing,' it said. 'Lenders are constantly reviewing their policies.'
