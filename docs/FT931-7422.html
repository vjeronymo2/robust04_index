930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / International Company News: IBM posts worldwide 1992 deficit of Dollars 6.8bn INTERNATIONAL Business Machines lost Dollars 5.5bn in the US in 1992 and Dollars 1.73bn in Europe, according to a geographical breakdown of results published yesterday in its annual report. The report also contains for the first time financial results from two of the new business units formed under IBM's plan to become a federation of more autonomous operations. The two are AdStar, which makes storage systems, and Integrated Systems Solutions Corp (ISSC), which provides systems integration services. IBM's geographical profits breakdown shows that the group lost Dollars 6.86bn in 1992 before accounting changes, compared to a loss of Dollars 598m in 1991. Its US loss of Dollars 5.5bn occurred on Dollars 32.2bn of revenues, compared to a loss of Dollars 2.4bn in 1991 on Dollars 32.1bn of revenues. The European loss of Dollars 1.73bn compared to a 1991 profit of Dollars 1.26bn and was struck on revenues of Dollars 26.1bn, down from Dollars 26.9bn. It made Dollars 126m in Asia-Pacific, down from Dollars 469m in 1991, and Dollars 157m in the rest of the Americas, down from Dollars 204m. AdStar produced a Dollars 265m loss after restructuring charges but a Dollars 247m profit before these charges on revenues of Dollars 6.1bn. It had a return on assets of just 6 per cent. ISSC, which began operating in 1991, reported net earnings of Dollars 30m on revenues of Dollars 1.8bn for a 2.8 per cent return on assets.
