Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan is not asking India to hand over Kashmir to anybody, but demands that the people of Kashmir be given the right to decide about their future. Commenting on the Indian foreign secretary's interview with Zee TV, the Foreign Office spokesman said in Islamabad today that India has kept the Kashmiris under bondage. Pakistan is asking India to free the Kashmiris, enabling them to decide about their fate, the spokesman said. The Indian foreign secretary, in his interview, had said India could never think of handing over Kashmir to Pakistan for the sake of peace. The Foreign Office spokesman said India was the first to take the Kashmir issue to the United Nations and had made an unequivocal commitment before the world body to hold fair and free plebiscite in occupied Kashmir in order to ascertain the will of the people, but, the spokesman added, India is now finding excuses to wriggle out of its clear pledges in the shape of UN resolutions.
