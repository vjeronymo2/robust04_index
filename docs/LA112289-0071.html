November 22, 1989, Wednesday, San Diego County Edition

CITY FUNDS BROWN FIELD STUDY

The San Diego City Council on Tuesday agreed to fund an $18,000 study by the San Diego Assn. of Governments to determine whether Brown Field can be expanded to handle takeoffs and landings of major airliners.

The 60-day study is expected to give the council a glimpse of whether a "dual airport" concept, employing both Lindbergh and Brown fields for airline and cargo traffic, is technologically feasible and merits further consideration.

Sandag will review a proposal for Brown drawn up by a private consultant to expand the city-owned general aviation airport and build two runways on a southwest-to-northeast axis.

Last week, the council lifted a year-old building moratorium on residential, commercial and industrial development around Brown, virtually killing all chances for a large binational airport on Otay Mesa along the Mexican border.

That move effectively leaves the dual airport solution and joint civilian-military use of Miramar Naval Air Station as the only alternatives to increasingly crowded Lindbergh Field. The Navy is adamantly opposed to the second option.

In another action Tuesday, the council authorized City Manager John Lockwood to begin negotiations to purchase land around the Brown Field airstrip that would be needed to extend the runway.
