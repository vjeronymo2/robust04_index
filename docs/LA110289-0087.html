November 2, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COMMUNITY DIGEST: ARTESIA;

CONFERENCE SLATED TODAY ON DELINQUENCY PREVENTION

More than 40 federal, state, county and local law enforcement experts are expected to attend a conference from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. today at the First Christian Reformed Church, 18421 Alburtis Ave., Artesia, to discuss county delinquency prevention programs and ways to improve them.

The group is scheduled to discuss the possible creation of a countywide authority for delinquency prevention that would improve services in a more coordinated and systematic manner to the local communities.

Darold Pieper, president of the county Delinquency and Crime Commission, will chair the conference. Other participants will include Cal Terhune, director of the California Youth Authority; Rudy De Leon, special assistant to Atty. Gen. John K. Van de Kamp; Capt. Helena Ashby, sheriff's Juvenile Justice Bureau; Capt. Ken Bayless, commander of the Lakewood Sheriff's Station, and Angel Alderete, U.S. Department of Justice community relations.

Artesia was selected as the conference site because of successful gang control and drug reduction programs initiated by the Lakewood and Norwalk sheriff's stations in cooperation with the ABC Unified School District and the surrounding cities, said Donn Irving, commission project director.

Commissioners are appointees of the county Board of Supervisors.
