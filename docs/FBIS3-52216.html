Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The secretary-level talks between Pakistan and India began in Islamabad today. Foreign Secretary Mr. Shaharyar Khan is leading the Pakistani side, while the Indian delegation is headed by the external affairs secretary, Mr. J.N. Dixit. Quoting Foreign Office sources, Pakistan Television diplomatic correspondent says during the four rounds of talks, the Pakistani side would endeavor to focus attention on all aspects of the Kashmir dispute as already agreed to by New Delhi in order to find out a negotiated and peaceful settlement of the problem. Our correspondent says Pakistan has been calling upon India to stop repression and atrocities in the occupied territory and make visible improvement on the ground in the valley in order to ensure continuation of dialogue in a positive direction. Other issues relating to bilateral matters may also be taken up during the talks. Additional foreign secretary, Mr. Munir Akram, who is taking part in the talks, spoke to Pakistan Television about the first round of talks. [Begin Akram recording] So far the talks have started on a note of seriousness and both the president and the foreign secretary have underlined our desire for a peaceful solution of the core issue of Jammu and Kashmir on the basis of the resolutions of the United Nations. The talks have so far focused on Kashmir, and we have had a first round of reiteration of the positions of the two sides. We have also underlined the need to create propitious conditions for the search for a peaceful solution and the necessity for an end to the repression in Kashmir for the creation of such conditions. [end recording] The Indian foreign secretary called on President Farooq Ahmad Leghari at the Aiwan-i-Sadr [President House] today and discussed with him matters of bilateral interests. Referring to the foreign secretary-level bilateral talks, the president wished them all success. The president told Mr. Dixit that Pakistan is very keen to see a just and honorable solution of the Kashmir problem, but there is a great deal of anguish over the suppression of human rights in the occupied valley. The Indian foreign secretary said he has come to Pakistan with concrete ideas and with a will to move forward. Mr. Dixit also conveyed to the president greetings and best wishes from the Indian president and the prime minister. Mr. J.N. Dixit also called on Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto in Karachi today. Welcoming the Indian foreign secretary, the prime minister said she is glad that the secretary-level talks have resumed after a gap of 15 months. She reaffirmed Pakistan's position on Kashmir and asked the Indian foreign secretary to take visible steps regarding improvement of human rights situation in the occupied Kashmir. Ms. Benazir Bhutto said that the secretary-level talks are being held at an important juncture of India-Pakistan relations and needed to demonstrate positive results. The Indian foreign secretary conveyed the good wishes of Prime Minister Narasimha Rao to Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto and said that he has come to Pakistan to discuss basic issues seriously with a view to making progress in the India-Pakistan bilateral relations. He expressed the hope that progress would be possible as a result of this bilateral contact between the two countries. Present at the meeting was Foreign Secretary Shaharyar M. Khan; Indian high commissioner, Mr. S.K. Lamba; and the Pakistani high commissioner in India, Mr. Riaz Khokhar.
