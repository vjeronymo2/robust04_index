921229 FT 29 DEC 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Equities float higher in quiet session Wall Street US STOCK prices floated higher late in the afternoon in thin, featureless trading, as the market marked time ahead of the new year, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Averagem which lost more than 14 points at mid-session, ended the day up 7.02 at 3,333.26 with the gains coming in the last 15 minutes of trading. The more broadly based Standard&Poor's 500 was off 0.62 at 439.15, while the Amex composite edged 0.01 higher to 394.18 and the Nasdaq composite rose 0.36 to 666.24. NYSE trading volume was less than 145m shares and declines outnumbered rises by 990 to 809. Last Thursday, the Dow rose 12.70 to 3,326.24 in an abbreviated pre-Christmas session. NYSE volume was less than 100m shares. The Standard&Poor's 500 closed 0.74 higher at 439.77 while the Nasdaq composite firmed 2.92 to 665.88. In the pharmaceutical sector, Glaxo Holdings' ADRs slid Dollars 1/2 to Dollars 22 1/4 in active trading. Merck also saw heavy turnover, closing unchanged at Dollars 44 3/4 . IBM started the week on a weak note, losing Dollars 1 1/8 to Dollars 51 3/4 . Investors are still wary of the stock, which hit a 17-year low of Dollars 48 3/4 a week ago. Occidental Petroleum shed Dollars 1/2 to Dollars 17 after the company announced a proposed settlement of legal action surrounding its dividend policy. The company, which currently pays a regular quarterly dividend of 25 cents per share, said that the proposal included maintaining its annual common stock dividend at no less than Dollars 1 per share through 1997. The settlement is subject to final approval by the federal court. A number of department stores and other retailers ended the day higher after losing ground in mornining trading. The sector has suffered from lower investment ratings in spite of a strong Christmas season. Shares in Wal-Mart closed unchanged at Dollars 64 7/8 , Kmart rose Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 25, JC Penney was up Dollars 1 5/8 to Dollars 78 and Dillard Department Stores advanced Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 50. Canada TORONTO was closed yesterday for a public holiday. On Christmas Eve, the TSE 300 fell 3.2 to 3,319.9 pressured by losses in the heavily weighted BCE Inc and Oshawa Group A shares. Advancing issues outpaced decliners by 226 to 171.
