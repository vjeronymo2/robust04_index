Extensions Rule 17Ad&hyph;15_File No. 270&hyph;360 Rule 17f&hyph;1(b)_File No. 270&hyph;28 Rule 17f&hyph;1(c) and Form X&hyph;17F&hyph;1A_File No. 270&hyph;29 Rules 17h&hyph;1T and 17h&hyph;2T_File No. 270&hyph;359 Notice is hereby given pursuant to the Paperwork Reduction Act of 1980 (44 U.S.C. 3501 et seq. ), that the Securities and Exchange Commission (``Commission'') has submitted to the Office of Management and Budget approval for extensions on the following previously approved rules and forms: Rule 17Ad&hyph;15 requires transfer agents to establish written standards for the acceptance or rejection of guarantees of securities transfers from eligible guarantor institutions. There are approximately 800 registered transfer agents subject to the rule's recordkeeping requirements. The average burden for each transfer agent is approximately 40 hours annually, for a total annual burden of 32,000 hours. Rule 17f&hyph;1(b) requires reporting institutions to register with the Commission or its designee to participate in the Lost and Stolen Securities Program. Approximately 600 respondents incur an average of one-half burden hour annually to comply with this rule, for a total annual burden of 300 hours. Rule 17f&hyph;1(c) and Form X&hyph;17F&hyph;1A require reporting institutions to report lost, stolen, missing, and counterfeit securities to a centralized database. Approximately 23,000 reporting institutions file approximately 667,000 Forms X&hyph;17F&hyph;1A. The Commission estimates that the average burden for each form is 5 minutes, resulting in a total annual burden of 55,583 hours. Rules 17h&hyph;1T and 17h&hyph;2T impose reporting and recordkeeping requirements on broker-dealers that are associated with other entities, other than natural persons. Approximately 250 broker-dealers are subject to the rules. It is estimated that the average burden for each firm is approximately 14 hours annually, or a cumulative burden of 3,500 hours.
