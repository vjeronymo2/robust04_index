BFN [Text] Baku, 2 May (TURAN)--Azerbaijan adopted the resolution on joining the NATO's programme "Partnership in Sake of Peace." President's Staff of the Republic reports that tomorrow [3 May] afternoon Azeri President is due to leave for Brussels on one-day visit. The programme of the visit provides for visiting the NATO's headquarters where the document will be signed and the press conference will be conducted. Confidential sources report that during Rasul Guliyev's recent visit to Moscow, several Russian officials advised Azerbaijan not to hurry to join the NATO's programme.
