Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jan 03, BNS -- President Algirdas Brazauskas of Lithuania has been declared the Person of the Year in his country, on the basis of a recent opinion poll. He won the highest popularity rating - 31 percent - in the poll, taken at the end of December by the Baltic Studies polling center at the request of the newspaper LIETUVOS RYTAS, the newspaper reported on Friday [31 December]. Second to him is Vytautas Landsbergis, leader of the right-wing parliamentary opposition and former head of state, whose rating is nine per cent. According to the same poll, Lithuania's population regards the introduction of the permanent monetary unit, the litas, as the most important event of 1993 in Lithuania, 26 per cent having expressed that view. Twenty-two per cent said the main event of the year had been the final withdrawal of Russian troops, the rest attaching the greatest importance to the Pope's visit or the presidential election. As far as events in the world go, 29 percent attached the greatest significance to the October events in Russia, six per cent to the war in the former Yugoslavia and four per cent to the parliamentary election in Russia.
