Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, March 12 (ATA) -- The Albanian representative in the United Nations, Mr. Pellumb Kulla, in a letter sent on March 10 to the secretary general, Butrus Butrus-Ghali, and the head of the Security Council, French Ambassador Jean Bertrand Merime [name as received], called their attention to the recent increase in the Serbian pressure on Kosova as well as to the danger of a possible expansion of the conflict, which would have consequences for the whole region. "I ask that the Security Council conduct an investigation of the situation in Kosova at the earliest possible time, and I repeat the appeals of the Albanian Government for a discussion as soon as possible of measures that might be taken in order to prevent further conflict in Kosova," writes Ambassador Kulla. The Albanian ambassador asks for the immediate discussion of the sending of United Nations troops to Kosova in order to prevent the spread of the conflict from Bosnia-Herzegovina in Kosova and asks at the same time that his letter be circulated as an official document of the Security Council. On the subject of ethnic cleansing against the Albanians in Kosova, the ambassador writes that "hundreds of Albanian homes have been raided and 3,400 Albanians have suffered from Serbian police violence, and most of them have been imprisoned. In many cases, the Serbian police have ordered Albanian families, under the threat of death, to leave their homes and emigrate from Kosova. Mr. Kulla also condemns the political trials against Albanians, stressing that "defendants have been tortured, serious procedural errors have been committed, and sentences have been excessive."
