920131 FT 31 JAN 92 / International Company News: Scott Paper in loss on provision SCOTT PAPER, the world's largest manufacturer of toilet tissue, has revealed a fourth-quarter net loss of Dollars 157.7m, after taking a large charge for restructuring and a cut of 3,800 jobs, around 10 per cent of its workforce. The company warned this month it would be taking a Dollars 183m after-tax special charge, about half of it due to job cuts. The figure also includes a gain from the sale of its Japanese joint venture. Its fourth-quarter loss worked through at Dollars 2.14 a share, and compared with a loss of Dollars 51.5m, or 70 cents, a year earlier. Excluding special items, earnings per share dropped from 66 cents to 35 cents. Sales totalled Dollars 1.32bn, against Dollars 1.43bn. For the full year, it suffered a net loss of Dollars 69.9m after special items, or 95 cents, against profits of Dollars 148m, or Dollars 2.01. The 1991 figures do not include businesses to be divested.
