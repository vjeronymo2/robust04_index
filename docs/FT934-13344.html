931022 FT 22 OCT 93 / Political shake-up for Canada CONSERVATIVES Party promises to eradicate the CDollars 35bn budget deficit within five years without raising taxes or jeopardising health-care spending. Would probably reappoint the inflation-fighting John Crow as Bank of Canada governor. Would implement North American Free Trade Agreement (Nafta). Plans no major change in immigration policy. LIBERALS Party pledges to reduce budget deficit from 5.2 per cent of GDP to 3 per cent. Would try to renegotiate Nafta. Plans to stimulate the economy with a CDollars 2bn public works programme and youth training. Would replace 7 per cent goods and services tax with unspecified 'fairer' tax. Would cancel order for 43 European EH-101 helicopters. NDP Party vigorously opposes Nafta. Would try to replace the US-Canada pact with sectoral agreements. Would reduce budget deficit by chopping defence spending by 3 per cent a year and raising taxes on companies and wealthy individuals. Would replace goods and services tax. BLOC QUEBECOIS Party's long-term goal is independence for Quebec. Meanwhile, promises to put Quebec's interests first but without disrupting parliament. Proposes to cut CDollars 10bn from budget and reinvest CDollars 5bn in job-creation programme. Supports free trade and proposes measures such as retraining to ease transition. REFORM Party plans to eliminate the budget deficit in three years. Would cut unemployment insurance, old age security benefits and business subsidies. Supports Nafta, provided Canada would have more control over water resources. Opposes special treatment for Quebec, and would cut immigration by a third.
