Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The overall situation in Lebanon was the subject of Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri's talks with Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad yesterday. The NATIONAL NEWS AGENCY reports that President al-Asad met with Prime Minister al-Hariri for three hours and discussed with him the current local and regional developments and the situation in the south in the wake of the recent Israeli attacks, and the Arab-Israeli negotiations. Prime Minister al-Hariri also met twice with Syrian Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam. He returned to Beirut around 2200 yesterday. Al-Hariri sources described the results of his talks in Damascus as good, and said that his talks dealt with the situation in the south, the stages covered in the bilateral peace negotiations, and the Lebanese situation in general. The results of the Damascus talks will be the subject of consultations today among President al-Hirawi, Prime Minister al-Hariri, and Speaker Nabih Birri.
