930607 FT 07 JUN 93 / International Company News: China to invest in Haeco maintenance arm CHINA'S aviation admin-istration will invest in the mainland aircraft maintenance business of Hong Kong Aircraft Engineering (Haeco), AP-DJ reports from Hong Kong. The Civil Aviation Administration of China will buy 9 per cent of Taikoo (Xiamen) Aircraft Maintenance, according to Haeco. Haeco did not say how much the aviation supervisor will pay for the stake, but said that total investment plans remained unchanged at USDollars 63m. Haeco said that it held 61 per cent, adding in a statement that when the deal was announced in March, it expected to own just 41 per cent. Haeco added that it would sell up to 20 per cent of its holding to other interested parties. The company indicated that the 9 per cent portion for the Civil Aviation Administration has been carved out of this 20 per cent. Haeco is about 44 per cent owned by Swire Pacific, the Hong Kong conglomerate which controls Cathay Pacific Airways. Cathay owns 35 per cent of Hong Kong Dragon Airlines, which has many routes in China.
