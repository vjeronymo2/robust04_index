BFN [Text] The Federal Government has reaffirmed the specific dates for elections at various levels and the terminal date for handing over power to a democratically-elected civilian government will be announced on 16 January next year. The minister of information and culture, Professor Jerry Gana, stated this in Abuja yesterday while receiving the new British high commissioner to Nigeria, Mr. Thorold Masefield, in his office. He said the announcement would mark the commencement of the second phase of the political transitional program of the present administration. Prof. Gana said government commitment to a return to genuine democracy was total and irrevocable. The minister pointed out that the government believed that the transitional process should be peaceful, orderly, and implemented in accordance with the popular will and not the wish of a minority however vocal. Prof. Gana solicited the understanding and support of Britain in view of her importance in world affairs. Responding, the British high commissioner, Mr. Masefield, expressed the hope that the forthcoming national constitutional conference would be able to solve the country's political problem. He said it was in anticipation of this that he donated books recently in support of the conference.
