BFN [Text] Budapest, 2 May (MTI) -- Last year, the Hungarian Democratic Forum (MDF) and the Federation of Young Democrats (Fidesz) had the largest takings, HUF [Hungarian forints] 1.266 billion and HUF939 million, respectively, according to the 45th issue of the official gazette MAGYAR KOZLONY, which published information on the 1993 balances of several parties. Most of the money for the MDF and the Fidesz came from selling self-owned real estate: The MDF drew HUF832 million this way, while there was HUF697 million for the Fidesz. The total drawings of the Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP) amounted to HUF429 million last year, of the Alliance of Free Democrats (SZDSZ) HUF300 million, and of the Christian Democratic People's Party (KDNP) HUF150 million. The MSZP's budget includes a HUF225 million item of income without any source mentioned. A total of HUF296 million in state subsidies was granted last year to the MDF, 270 million to the SZDSZ, 170 million to the MSZP, and nearly HUF100 million to the KDNP. The MSZP took over HUF18 million in membership fees, while the MDF received HUF9 million, the SZDSZ 5 million and the KDNP 4 million. FIDESZ members paid altogether HUF653,000 last year.
