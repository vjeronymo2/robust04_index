BFN [Report by David Regev] [Excerpts] As of 1400 yesterday, the Palestinians in the Gaza Strip have been running their own lives. Thirty-four civilian offices were handed over to the Palestinians in a lengthy ceremony held at the Civil Administration's new offices in the Erez industrial zone, and the Israeli Civil Administration in the Gaza Strip officially became the civilian liaison center between Israel and the Palestinians. The civilian Palestinian Authority in the Gaza Strip is headed by Dr. Zakariya al-Agha, Attorney Furayh Abu-Middayn, and Dr. Riyad al-Za'nun. The three will operate out of a new office set up near the Palestinian police building in Gaza. Forty-eight vehicles to be used by the Palestinian Authority arrived in the Gaza Strip yesterday from Egypt. The first decisions passed by the Palestinian Authority were to declare Friday the official day of rest and the days of Id al-Adha a holiday. [passage omitted] After an initial meeting between the sides, representatives of the Israeli offices and their Palestinian counterparts proceeded to sign the transfer of powers. Representatives of 34 offices -- transportation, education, treasury, interior, health, and others -- spent four long hours signing the letters of empowerment written in English. The civilian powers were officially handed over to the Palestinians after Brigadier General Dov Gazit, head of the Civil Administration, and Palestinian representative Frayj al-Khayri signed the letters.
