BFN [Text] The Philippine Embassy in Kuala Lumpur should explain to its people the real situation relating to the rounding up of more than 1,000 Philippine workers at St. John Cathedral in the federal capital on March the 27th. Deputy Home Minister Datuk Megat Junid Megat Ayub said the Philippines should understand Malaysia's position and the problems it faced following influx of illegal workers. Speaking to reporters at parliament house, he said if the embassy wants the issue to be settled amicably, then they should tell the Philippine people that the operation was carried out in accordance with the law. He said Malaysians, too, (?were arrested) overseas for overstaying, but the government did not protest. Datuk Megat Junid said Malaysia was facing not only the problem of illegal workers from the Philippines, but from other countries like Indonesia, Bangladesh, Thailand, and Myanmar [Burma] as well.
