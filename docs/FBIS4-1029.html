BFN [Text] Pretoria April 15 SAPA -- The South African Defence Force [SADF] will soon host a one-hour programme on Radio 2000 to communicate with the public, especially the SADF's part-time forces. A statement issued on Friday [15 April] on behalf of the SADF by chief director of communication Maj-Gen John Rolt said the programme, tentatively scheduled for Wednesday evenings, would be first aired within a fortnight, pending finalisation of an agreement with the South African Broadcasting Corporation. The statement also said a newspaper and radio advertising campaign was in the offing to improve the image of the SADF and create a climate favourable to the establishment of a new National Defence Force on April 27. Empahasis would be placed on communicating with the black community. Four full-page advertisements would be placed 11 times each, and 27-second radio spots would be aired 500 times.
