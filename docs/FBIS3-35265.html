Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Algiers, 23 Feb 94 (JANA) -- Foreign Minister Mohamed Salah Dembri, made a statement to the JANA correspondent yesterday in which he stressed the importance of Major al-Khuwaylidi al-Humaydi's visit to Algeria [word indistinct] and its outcome at the bilateral level as well as at the level of the Arab Maghreb Union [AMU], pointing out that contacts between brothers will always open new vistas. He added: Strong relations have always existed between Algeria and Libya, and contacts between the two fraternal countries are necessary. The meeting between brother al-Khuwaylidi al-Humaydi and President Zeroual has given us a new opportunity to meet again in the near future to examine all topics of mutual interest at the bilateral and Maghreban levels as well as our Arab and Islamic nations, he said. He added: There are a number of topics to be examined. They require us to unite our efforts. And this is what we discussed with Major al-Khuwaylidi al-Humaydi. He stressed that there is no rift between Algerian and Libyan brothers that can be manipulated by opposition forces. And this should be the main outcome of this visit. We are hoping that the coming days will witness intensified contacts to examine our affairs with clarity, resolve them, and lay down a joint plan of action to resolve all related matters, he pointed out.
