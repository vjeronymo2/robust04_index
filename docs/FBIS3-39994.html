Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Geneva, India has urged friendly countries at the ongoing session of the UN Human Rights Commission to give up their neutral stance on the Kashmir issue and come out openly against Pakistan's state-sponsored terrorism in Jammu and Kashmir. The minister of state for external affairs, Mr. Salman Khurshid, told the heads of various delegations in informal meetings in Geneva that abstaining in the event of a voting on the Pakistan-proposed resolution on Kashmir will undermine the very concept of human rights and will encourage Islamabad's involvement in terrorism. Talking to Indian reporters, Mr. Khurshid said New Delhi expects a categorical rejection of the resolution.
