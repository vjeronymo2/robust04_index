Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yevgeniy Belovistkiy] [Text] Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk January 13 TASS -- The administration of the Island of Sakhalin in the Russian Far East where some 40,000 ethnic Koreans live will not impede them from leaving for their historic motherland, according to Deputy Governor of the island Viktor Gurevich. However, he told a press conference on Thursday that this would be a great intellectual and labour loss for Sakhalin and Kuril Islands. The Korean disapora on Sakhalin consists mostly of those who were deported to the island by the Japanese before and during World War Two, as well as from their ancestors. The press conference was devoted to the work of a trilateral Russian-Japanese-South Korean Commission which studied the problems faced by Koreans in Sakhalin. The Korean diaspora demands a compensation of 10 million yen for each Korean, dual Russian-Korean citizenship and free private housing in Korea. However, foreign diplomats told reporters that the demands would [word indistinct] hardly met. 10 million yen is too big a sum and dual citizenship is problematic in South Korea, they said.
