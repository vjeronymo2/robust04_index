BFN [Text] Zhengzhou, May 28 (XINHUA) -- Four Chinese and foreign companies have won the bid for three major parts of the World Bank financed Xiaolangdi water control dam construction project on the middle reaches of the Yellow River. The Inpergilo company of Italy, the No. 14 Water Conservancy and Hydroelectric Engineering Bureau of China, and Dumiz Company of France and the No. 6 Water Conservancy and Hydroelectric Engineering Bureau of China, which are selected among more than 30 Chinese and foreign companies, have been given the jobs of building the dam, culverts and spillways, and facilities for generating electricity. They have formed two international joint management groups to achieve best effect and will soon start work on the construction site. Xiaolangdi water control dam is among the first of its kind in China to select builders through international bidding and the practice is in accordance with requirements of the World Bank. Earlier reports also showed that the Xiaolangdi water control project, situated 40 km north of Luoyang city, Henan Province, has won a loan of 570 million U.S. dollars from the World Bank. As a project to introduce World Bank loans, the Xiaolangdi water control dam has received the assessment of 15 delegations sent by the World Bank since 1988. Through concerted efforts from Chinese and foreign experts, the Xiaolangdi Dam is believed to have basically met the requirements for appraisal from the World Bank in aspects such as engineering technology, dam safety, relocation of people, budgetary estimates of projects, economic and financial analysis, consultancy of supervised management in construction, training program of technical workers, and the drafting of the tender documents. Preparatory work for the Xiaolangdi water control project, which was initiated in September 1991, has been completed ahead of schedule after two years' efforts from 15,000 builders. The preparatory work has cost about 1.2 billion yuan (about 137 million U.S. dollars). It includes facilities for transport, water and electricity supplies, telecommunications and facilities to house the construction workers, according to earlier reports. The relocation of the first 10,000 local residents in the construction area -- mainly in Jiyuan and Mengjin Counties of central China's Henan Province -- has also come to an end, with full support from local governments and farmers concerned. At the same time, the Ministry of Water Resources has also established a department in charge of construction and a department responsible for supervised management in accordance with international common practices, as well as management information system of projects. More than 300 supervisory engineers have been brought in from across the country to attend training courses where lectures are given by World Bank officials. When completed, the Xiaolangdi water control project will be the largest conservancy works on the Yellow River and will be China's second biggest after the Three Gorges Dam on the Chang Jiang. It will play an important role in diverting the Yellow River flow, preventing floods, generating power and controlling sediment, according to experts. The experts say that the entire project, which will take 11 years to finish, together with other reservoirs nearby, will enable the lower reaches of the Yellow River to absorb a flood of a magnitude that occurs only once in one thousand years.
