930924 FT 24 SEP 93 / Police chiefs attack proposals DELEGATES at the Police Superintendents' Association conference in Torquay yesterday condemned disciplinary measures announced by Mr Michael Howard, the home secretary, on Wednesday. These included the removal of officers' right to legal representation in misconduct hearings. Mr Howard yesterday invited leaders of all 52 police forces to a meeting to discuss the Sheehy reform proposals. Last night Mr Howard defended his decision to introduce the new disciplinary system, and insisted the changes were not intended to alienate them. He said on BBC Television: 'Some of their (the police's) procedures need modernising and among those are the disciplinary procedures, which are cumbersome.'
