941130 FT 30 NOV 94 / Budget 94 (Tax): Simpler VAT system among small business proposals Proposals to bring the national insurance and income tax systems into closer alignment, to simplify the VAT system and allow more companies to pay tax quarterly were among Budget measures aimed at small businesses in particular. The package was given a qualified reception by the Federation of Small Businesses which described the Budget as offering a 'useful range of measures'. The Treasury will consult with traders on proposals to allow small businesses to move to annual VAT returns and payments. Ministers are also considering simplifying the VAT system for some traders to link payments to turnover, rather than details of sales and purchases. Mr Kenneth Clarke said simply running PAYE and national insurance systems was difficult for many small businesses - as well as imposing a cost and administrative burden. From April, clearances given by the Inland Revenue concerning non-taxable expenses will also count for national insurance purposes, the chancellor announced. Mr Clarke's decision to increase by 30 per cent the threshold below which businesses can account quarterly, rather than monthly, for PAYE and national insurance contributions, was welcomed by small businesses. But there were warnings that the chancellor's proposals would not ease the administrative cost of keeping tax records. The proposal is expected to benefit about 100,000 employers. The Treasury also said that it wanted to improve the administration of the tax system by encouraging a closer working relationship between the two revenue raising departments - the Inland Revenue and Customs & Excise. Mr Clarke also announced an increase in the VAT registration threshold from Pounds 45,000 to Pounds 46,000. Mrs Mavis Sargent, chairman of the Chartered Association of Certified Accountants' tax committee, said: 'The extension of payment of PAYE on a quarterly basis and the move towards aligning income tax and national insurance are steps in the right direction, but they are too tentative.'
