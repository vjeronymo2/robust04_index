Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The head of the Syrian regime, Hafiz al-Asad, returned to Damascus last night following the talks he held in Geneva with U.S. President Bill Clinton on the settlement of the Palestine question and bilateral relations. In another development, U.S. President Bill Clinton last night said that he discussed with the head of the Syrian regime the possibility of dispatching U.S. troops to the Golan Heights in a peace-keeping mission, as he claimed. In statements to journalists on board the presidential plane that took him back to Washington following a meeting with Hafiz al-Asad in Geneva, Clinton said that al-Asad stressed the need for mutual assurances in the peace process, and that the Syrian president has clearly committed himself, during the talks, to establishing amity with the Zionist entity. A Zionist source said that Dennis Ross, coordinator of the peace process, last night briefed Zionist Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin on the two presidents' talks in Geneva. The U.S. President also held a telephone conversation with the Zionist prime minister to brief him on the results of his meeting with the head of the Syrian regime. Clinton has also held a telephone conversation with the head of the Egyptian regime, Husni Mubarak, and agreed with him to continue consultations on the settlement process.
