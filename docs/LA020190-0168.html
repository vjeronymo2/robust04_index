February 1, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

THE SHOE: REFLECTIONS ON A LEGEND;

CAREER AT A GLANCE

Holds the record for most career races won, 8,833.

At 54, became the oldest jockey to win the Kentucky Derby, aboard Ferdinand in 1986.

Holds the record for most Kentucky Derby rides (24).

First jockey to earn more than $100 million over his career.

First jockey to win 8,000 career races, reaching that mark at Hollywood Park on May 27, 1981, aboard War Allied.

First jockey to win a $1 million race, capturing the Arlington Million aboard John Henry in 1981.

Won his first Eclipse Award for riding in 1981.

Received a special Eclipse for his contribution to racing in 1976 and '81.

Elected to Racing's Hall of Fame in 1958.

Led North American jockeys in victories five times: 1950, '53, '54, '58 and '59.

Led jockey standings at Santa Anita 17 consecutive years, from 1951-1967.

Led all jockeys in purse earnings seven consecutive years, from 1958-1964. He led 10 times overall.

Has won six races on one card nine times.

Became first jockey in 50 years to be honored by the Thoroughbred Club of America in 1982.
