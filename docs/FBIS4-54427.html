BFN [Text] Warsaw, April 27 -- The candidature of Grzegorz W. Kolodko for the post of deputy prime minister and finance minister on Wednesday gained the approval of President Lech Walesa. The candidature was presented to the president by Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak after the president had returned from Lithuania. Prof. Kolodko, 45, independent, has been director of the Institute of Finance since 1988 and academic of the main school of commerce. He was an adviser to the president of the National Bank of Poland (NBP) during 1982-1988. Kolodko also delivered lectures at University of Illinois, U.S.A. In 1991 he become a consultant of the International Monetary Fund (IMF). In 1989 Grzegorz Kolodko participated in the "round table" debates in the team for economy and social policy. Kolodko, who during 1969-90 was a member of the Polish United Workers Party (PUWP), has written some 200 works on economy and economic policy which dealt mainly with economic growth, disproportions, crisis, inflation and systemic reforms.
