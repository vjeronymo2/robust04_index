BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 1 KYODO -- Japan on Friday [1 April] supported a United Nations call on North Korea to allow full international inspection of its nuclear facilities suspected of being used to develop nuclear weapons. "The UN statement underlined what the international community wants North Korea to do to help resolve the issue of Pyongyang's suspected nuclear program through dialogue," Chief Cabinet Secretary Masayoshi Takemura told a news conference. "We will support this statement," the top government spokesman said. All 15 members of the UN Security Council, including China, a traditional ally of North Korea, agreed to issue the statement Thursday calling for Pyongyang to accept full inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency.
