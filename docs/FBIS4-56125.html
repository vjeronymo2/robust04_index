BFN [Report by Edaterina Kazasova] [Excerpts] Sofia, May 26 (BTA) -- The National Assembly voted 125 to 95 for the confidence motion proposed by the Council of Ministers, thus backing its newly proposed programme. Under the Constitution the government needed the votes of half of the MPs present (today 221 MPs took part in the ballot). "I guess one is supposed to say `thank you' in a situation like this, but I don't think those who voted against me feel envy for my mission," Prime Minister Lyuben Berov said after the ballot. He promised to make great efforts to keep the state on an even keel. [passage omitted] The MPs opted for a secret ballot. Although SDS [Union of Democratic Forces] and the independent MPs insisted on an open, roll-call ballot, the secret ballot got 125 votes. The procedure was delayed more than an hour by the continuing consultations between the prime minister and the political forces. Just before the ballot he met with representatives of the DPS [Movement for Rights and Freedoms], the BSP [Bulgarian Socialist Party] and NSDP [New Social Democratic Party]. The government asked that its normal operation be guaranteed for a period of three months, said the spokesman for the DPS, Mr. Yunal Lyutfi. He reiterated DPS's support for the cabinet. After two meetings with the prime minister the Socialists who until then had firmly backed the cabinet felt somewhat hesitatnt. BSP leaders Zhan Videnov and Nora Ananieva asked from the prime minister too firm guarantees regarding incomes compensations, the Government's Spokesman Raicho Raikov explained. "They are impossible because of the conditions set by the international institutions and G-24," he said. After its meeting with the prime minister the parliamentary BSP held a long conference at which the MPs were not quite unanimous as to the support for the cabinet. The New Policy Center, the Alternative Social Liberal Party and the Alliance for Democracy issued a joint declaration that they would not support the cabinet. Neither did the SDS. Mr Vassil Gotsev, MP of the SDS, described the ballot as "just another farce". He accused DPS leader Ahmed Dogan of voting as instructed by Russia's Ambassador Alexander Avdeev. Mr Dogan met with Mr Avdeev today and with U.S. Ambassador William Montgomery yesterday. "We cannot help asking why Prime Minister Berov asked for confidence only until September," Mr Gotsev added. Meanwhile, Alexander Lilov of BSP told the national radio that early elections will be held this fall and that they will be won by the BSP. MP from the New Union for Democracy and representatives of the Democratic Bulgaria Union intend to propose a draft agreement to the other parliamentary forces to regulate the work of parliament until the new elections. They explained that parliament and the other state institutions have lost much of their prestige. However, jurists from different parliamentary forces say that under the Constitution early elections can only be held in case of cabinet crisis and the inability of parliament to appoint a government.
