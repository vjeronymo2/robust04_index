Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belarusian Foreign Minister Petr Kravchenko believes that during his visit to Belarus, U.S. President Bill Clinton will announce new funds or programs to sustain business and economic reforms in Belarus. The Belarusian Foreign Minister made the statement at a news conference in Minsk Wednesday. Bill Clinton is to meet Stanislav Shushkevich and Vyacheslav Kebich in Minsk January 15, the minister told reporters. Clinton will also speak at the National Academy of Sciences and will place flowers to the Monument of Victory of the Soviet people in the Great Patriotic War. Kravchenko has confirmed the information which says that the U.S. President intended to meet representatives of Democratic parties and movements, for example, the leader of the Belarusian People's Front Zenon Poznyak. The U.S. president's wife, Hillary Clinton intends to see some hospitals in Minsk where people who suffered the aftermath of the Chernobyl disaster undergo medical treatment.
