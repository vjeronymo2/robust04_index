Language: Burmese Article Type:BFN [Text] The plenary session of the National Convention continued at 0900 this morning in the Central Meeting Hall of president's residence compound on Ahlone Road, Yangon [Rangoon]. The plenary session was attended by the chairman of the National Convention Convening Commission, Lieutenant General Myo Nyunt; the vice chairmen, Lt. Gen. Maung Thint and Brigadier General Myo Thant, and commission members; the chairman of the National Convention Convening Work Committee [NCCWC], U Aung Toe; the vice chairman, U Tha Tun, and work committee members; the chairman of the management committee, Brig. Gen. Tin Aye, and committee members; delegates; and local and foreign correspondents. At the plenary session, U San Tha Aung of the Mro or Khami National Solidarity Organization acted as the acting chairman; Dr. Than Nyunt, Daw May Than Lwin, U Mahn Ohn Maung, U Kan Nyunt, U Soe Maung, U Tin Maung, U Khin Maung Htoo, and U Sai Aung Htun participated in the panel. U Khin Maung Myint, director general of the Office of the NCCWC and in charge of the meeting, acted as master of ceremonies. First, the master of ceremonies announced that 663 out of 694 delegates attended the meeting and declared the session successful. According to the agenda, the delegates from the delegate group representing other invitees read proposals to report their fundamental principles related to the state, state structure, and head of state chapters that will be included in drawing up the constitution. U Saw Hla Tun from Sagaing Division and Dr. Han Saw of the Mandalay Institute of Medicine read the proposal paper collectively prepared by the delegate group representing other invitees; (U Mahn Ko Gaung Khun) read the proposal paper belonging to the delegate group representing the invitees from Special Region-1, Kachin State; and (U Gabel Byan) read the proposal paper belonging to the delegate group representing other invitees from Kayan Group. Afterwards, the plenary session recessed at 1100. The afternoon session of the National Convention plenary session is continuing in the Central Meeting Hall of the president's residence compound on Ahlone Road, Yangon.
