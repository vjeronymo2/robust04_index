930629 FT 29 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television It is hard to imagine what history will make of the hysteria which took such a hold towards the end of the 20th century that otherwise quite intelligent people came to believe the world to be full of 'Satanists' systematically sexually abusing children. Organisations as dignified and sensible as Channel 4 became the conduits for claims which (as some of us noted at the time) appeared quite ludicrous. Now, with the medical profession identifying 'False Memory Syndrome', the similarities between this scare and others such as McCarthyism and old witch hunts are becoming apparent. In Viewpoint 93 the exposure continues with a programme by Anthony Thomas, famous for 'Death Of A Princess' (10.40 ITV). Channel 4's culture series Rear Window (9.00) is devoted to Sabu, the Indian child discovered by Alexander Korda and brought to Britain to make such movies as 'The Jungle Book', 'The Thief Of Baghdad' and of course 'Elephant Boy'. BBC2's Adultery series takes in the case of Lady Sarah Moon who distributed her husband's wine cellar to village doorsteps and cut the arms off his suits (9.50 BBC2).
