940209 FT 09 FEB 94 / International Company News: Peugeot sales fall 6.2% to FFr145.4bn PSA Peugeot Citroen, the French cars group, yesterday announced sales of FFr145.4bn (Dollars 24.6bn) last year, a fall of 6.2 per cent compared with 1992. The company said the performance demonstrated resistance to the sharp decline in the European car market, which contracted by 15.1 per cent last year, and a strong final quarter. During the October-December period, Peugeot-Citroen recorded a 13.1 per cent share of the European market, compared with 12.3 per cent for the year as a whole. Just over FFr61.5bn of the group's total sales came from the French market, a decline of 5.9 per cent. For France and western Europe, the sales figure was FFr71.1bn, a decline of 9.2 per cent. Union des Assurances de Paris, the state-owned insurance group slated for privatisation, yesterday announced consolidated sales of FFr140.5bn for last year, an increase of 11.6 per cent compared with 1992. The strongest growth was achieved in international markets, where turnover rose by 15 per cent to FFr79.4bn. In France, sales increased by 7.3 per cent to FFr61.1bn.
