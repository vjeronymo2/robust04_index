BFN [All quote marks as received] [Text] Hanoi VNA April 11 -- Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet gave a banquet here this evening in honour of his Australian distinguished guests. At this event, he delivered a speech which read in part : `It has been only a year since our first meeting in Canberra, but we have every reason to be satisfied with the rapid development of the multi-sided Vietnam-Australia cooperation, especially in the economic, trading, educational and training fields. We greatly appreciate Australia's active help to us in building a North-South 500 KV grid, a nearly-completed important project in our national construction. We also highly value the effective implementation of the Australian aid to train Vietnamese officials, as well as Australia's active contribution to a break-through in the relations between Vietnam and the international financial institutions. `I am optimistic about the prospect for the development of friendly and cooperative relationship between our two countries. Despite thousands of miles separating us and the difference in historical circumstances and cultural traditions between Vietnam and Australia, both of us have been looking towards the Pacific and identifying ourselves with the common desire for an expanded regional cooperation. `We are satisfied with the results of the open and frank exchanges of talks between us today. We are convinced that the agreement between us in many fields will contribute to further consolidation and development of the on-going fine cooperation between Vietnam and Australia'. Prime Minister Paul Keating, for his part, said: `The effect of the changes you have introduced, combined with the skill and enterprise of the Vietnamese people, have brought this country high levels of economic growth and have opened up new opportunities for our bilateral relationship. Australia, too, has been through a process of 'economic renovation'. We have opened our industry to competition and our economy to the world. `I know, Mr Prime Minister, how difficult the task can be. But, like you, I also know how large are the rewards for our countries at the end of the process. `And, like Vietnam Australia is also seeking closer links with our region not only economically but culturally, politically and strategically as well. These parallel interests are creating unprecedented new opportunities for us. Trade between Australia and Vietnam in 1982 was 2 million dollars. In 1993 it was 366 million dollars. Australia is now Vietnam's third largest foreign investor, with nearly 800 million dollars invested here. We are also developing new avenues for cooperation on regional issues. And Vietnam can count on warm support from Australia as you move towards greater involvement in regional organisations. Bilaterally, we are finding new opportunities to work together. `I was also pleased to be able to tell you today, Mr Prime Minister, that Australia has committed more than 200 million dollars in a new four-year aid program, which we hope will cover areas such as education, health, infrastructure and legal training.
