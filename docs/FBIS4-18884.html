BFN [Text] Munich -- Rudolf Scharping, the Social Democratic Party of Germany's [SPD] candidate for chancellor will make a binding promise to the United States Administration that after the Bundestag elections his party will not chose a coalition partner that wants to abolish NATO and Bundeswehr. This was said by SPD Federal Manager Guenter Verheugen in an interview with FOCUS news magazine prior to Scharping's visit to the United States. Verheugen said that there will be no partner in Germany for the policy adopted by the Greens at their congress in Mannheim. Scharping's message to the United States will be: "Germany will remain a reliable alliance partner that keeps its obligations."
