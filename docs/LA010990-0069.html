January 9, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

AFTER HEART SURGERY

Question: I had a bypass operation close to a year ago. Shortly after, my doctor recommended that I get involved in a cardiac rehabilitation program, but I guess I was too upset at the time to do anything about it.

I'm sorry now that I didn't, and I'd like to know if such a program would do me any good if I got into one now.

Answer: The results of one study suggest that patients can benefit from a cardiac rehabilitation program even if they enter one up to two years after having had a heart attack or bypass surgery. However, those who begin participating in such a program within six months of the event do appear to realize greater benefits from it.
