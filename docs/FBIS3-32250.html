Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Peruvian economic developments monitored through 2 February; source is in parentheses following each item. The National Bank will finance $40 million, which the Transportation, Communication, Housing, and Construction Ministry will use for infrastructure works. According to an executive decree published in the newspaper EL PERUANO, a joint operation between the two institutions to use funds from a loan granted by the Export and Import Bank of Japan was authorized. (Lima Radio Programas del Peru Network in Spanish 1800 GMT 26 Jan 94) Economy and Finance Minister Jorge Camet has said the U.S. Government will reportedly pay in two weeks the first $30-million installment, which was promised in an agreement signed with the Support Group. He has added this was made possible thanks to the recent visit by Alexander Watson, assistant secretary of state for Inter-American Affairs. Camet has also said the January inflation rate reached 1.8 percent and that there will be salary raises in 1994. (Lima Global de Television Network in Spanish 0100 GMT 1 Feb 94) Felix Murillo, National Statistics and Information Technology Institute chief, has said power, water, housing, and fuel services, which grew by an average of 4.1 percent, are the sectors that increased the most. The food sector grew by only 1.1 percent. Murillo said this prompted a compensatory effect in the January inflation rate, which reached 1.84 percent. (Lima Radio Programas del Peru Network in Spanish 1800 GMT 1 Feb 94) Raul Otero, Special Privatization Committee member, has said the international bidding for the sale of the Hilo Copper Refinery was declared vacant, because there were no bids. The representatives of such international corporations as Maruven Inc., Mitsui, and Sauder Peru Copper Corporation have declined to submit bids, because they have claimed the $75-million base price was too high. (Lima Radio Programas del Peru Network in Spanish 1800 GMT 2 Feb 94)
