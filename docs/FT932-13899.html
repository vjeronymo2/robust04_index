930421 FT 21 APR 93 / International Company News: Earnings per share up at GTE GTE, the largest local US telephone company, yesterday reported a 12 per cent rise in first-quarter earnings per share, excluding accounting changes, thanks to higher volumes and lower costs in its telephone operations and strong growth in its cellular telephone business. The company reported consolidated net income of Dollars 460m, or 48 cents a share, compared with earnings per share of 43 cents a year ago. After adjusting the 1992 earnings for a change in accounting rules for retirees' health benefits, the group had a net loss in the first quarter of last year of Dollars 2.05bn, or Dollars 2.27 a share. Sales and revenues in the latest quarter totalled Dollars 4.83bn, against Dollars 4.82bn a year ago, while operating income was up 9 per cent at Dollars 1.10bn. Revenues from telephone operations rose from Dollars 3.85bn to Dollars 3.92bn and operating income was up 7 per cent at Dollars 1.04bn. Revenues and sales from telecommunications products and services dipped 6 per cent to Dollars 911m. Operating income rose from Dollars 29m to Dollars 60m. Sprint, the third largest US long-distance carrier, reported a net loss of Dollars 413m, or Dollars 1.21 a share, compared with profits of Dollars 128m, or 38 cents, after the cost of changing its accounting methods for retirees' health benefits, and Dollars 248m of costs associated with its takeover of Centel, the Chigaco-based phone company. However, the company said income from continuing operations, excluding special charges, was Dollars 154m, or 45 cents, compared with Dollars 106m, or 31 cents, in the first quarter of last year. Revenues rose 9 per cent to Dollars 2.72bn.
