930518 FT 18 MAY 93 / International Company News: TCI back to black for quarter TELE-COMMUNICATIONS (TCI), the largest operator of cable TV systems in the US, yesterday announced a Dollars 53,000 after-tax profit in the three months to end-March, writes Nikki Tait in New York. The figure compares with an Dollars 18,000 loss in the same period of 1992. Revenues rose from Dollars 856m to Dollars 1.02bn, and per-share earnings stood at 11 cents, up from a loss of five cents in the first quarter of 1992. Operating profits rose from Dollars 197m to Dollars 247m, while interest charges dipped slightly, from Dollars 190m to Dollars 181m. Asset sale gains contributed Dollars 40m, compared with Dollars 3m.
