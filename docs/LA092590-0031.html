September 25, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

BULGARIA;

SOCIALIST LEADER OFFERS TO RESIGN

The leader of Bulgaria's ruling Socialist Party, Alexander Lilov, offered to step down to make room for younger reformers -- but he said he was willing to stay if the party wished. "The party needs a pragmatic and less controversial leader," Lilov told a congress of the former Communist Party. Under Lilov, the party changed its name to Socialist and swept to victory in free elections in June.
