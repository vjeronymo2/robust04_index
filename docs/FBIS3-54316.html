Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Afghan leaders have responded positively to Pakistan's proposal for hosting a regional peace summit or regional peace conference on Afghanistan. This was stated by the foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, while talking to newsmen in Islamabad today prior to his departure for Tehran to attend ECO [Economic Cooperation Organization] ministerial meeting beginning tomorrow. He said the representatives of Hezb-e Wahdat [Islamic Unity Party], Jonbesh-e Melli [National Movement], Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party], and Jabha-e Melli [National Liberation Front] put up a seven-point proposal for the solution of the Afghanistan problem. The foreign minister said Pakistan cannot decide itself as it has no favorites. He said it is a matter for the Afghans to decide. The foreign minister said he will brief the ECO foreign ministers on the latest developments in Afghanistan and human rights violations by Indian occupation forces in occupied Kashmir. The ECO council of ministers will consider a broad range of subjects, including the establishment of ECO trade and development company, shipping company, airlines, and insurance company.
