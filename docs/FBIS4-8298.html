BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 6/4/1994 -- American General George Joulwan, Supreme Commander of the Allied Forces of Europe, said on 6 April in Bucharest that there was clear will with the NATO to back Romania and the Partnership for Peace would be paralleled by a "Partnership for Prosperity", the two elements being considered as guarantees of lasting stability. The general, now on a visit in Romania, met with Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu. The main issue discussed was Romania's adhesion to the Partnership for Peace and the prospects of the country's full integration in the Euro-Atlantic structures. The Romanian premier stressed that Romania's foreign policy laid stress on the development of the relations with the NATO and all its member states, an orientation backed by all political forces of the country. Referring to the military domain, the premier integrated the specialty problems in the general picture of the country's economic situation, stressing that, although administrating an austerity budget, the Romanian Government was ready to back financially, more than in the past years, the process of reorganization and updating of the Army. "The main issue the Army is facing is the lack of financial resources and specialty technical assistance, domains in which the NATO support would be very efficient," the Romanian premier added.
