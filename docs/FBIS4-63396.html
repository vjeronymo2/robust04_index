BFN [Text] Moscow May 20 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Speaker of the Russian parliament lower house Ivan Rybkin who met in the Kremlin on Friday, examined the political situation in the country, following the singing of the Treaty of Civic Accord. According to Presidential Press Secretary Vyacheslav Kostikov, the sides noted that concerted actions by the president and the lower house to gain political stabilisation yielded positive results. They examined versions of conciliation procedures to implement the treaty in the spirit of a constructive dialogue. The president and the speaker also discussed the formation of the federal budget. They pointed to the similarity of approaches to the adjustment of the budget's military section, taking into account real requirements of the army and social needs of servicemen. Rybkin informed Yeltsin about the debates at the lower house of a bill on the Constitutional Court and a commission on constitutional amendments. In this connection, they discussed personnel issues. The president and the speaker analysed the political results of their latest trips -- to Germany and China, respectively.
