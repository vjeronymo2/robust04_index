940715 FT 15 JUL 94 / Sunday trading protestor wins plea A campaigner against Sunday trading yesterday won a High Court victory to bring private prosecutions against superstores which open on Sundays. Two judges ruled that Mr Roy Edey was entitled to launch such actions after Sir Nicholas Lyell, the attorney-general, declined to intervene. They said that prosecutions begun by Mr Edey against Woolworths, Superdrug and Tesco Stores would proceed as soon as possible. However, Lord Justice Butler Sloss and Mr Justice Macpherson added that, in view of changes in the law, magistrates 'may not regard the offences disclosed as being particularly serious'. Recent prosecutions in England and Wales had resulted in magistrates' orders against offending stores varying from absolute discharges to maximum fines, said the judges.
