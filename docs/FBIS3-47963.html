Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 21 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa said Friday [21 January] that ensuring a stable employment environment is one of the top priority jobs for the government. Hosokawa told the second meeting of a special cabinet forum on employment that the government needs to put into practice as early as possible a program of creating more jobs that was worked out in mid-December by the Labor Ministry. Finance Minister Hirohisa Fujii, also present at the meeting, pledged fiscal support for the program, while Minister of International Trade and Industry Hiroshi Kumagai said his ministry plans to nurture promising industries such as biotechnology, new energy and information to create new jobs. Labor Minister Chikara Sakaguchi, meanwhile, called for an early drafting of a new economic pump-primer by the government, saying improvement in the labor climate needs recovery of the economy.
