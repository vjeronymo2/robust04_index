Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN [Text] The large-scale offensive by the Vietnamese communist puppets -- under the command of the Vietnamese communists -- and their alliance suffered total defeat on 31 January 1994. 1. The large-scale offensive by the Vietnamese communists and their alliance began on 1 January in an attempt to open up the Route 69 battlefield and head toward Anlung Veng from Samraong, Sre Noy, and Kulen. The puppet forces mobilized 70 percent of their forces from all over the country to concentrate on this battlefield. Ninety percent of the puppet commanders were sent to direct the fight on this battlefield. 2. Very tense and fierce fighting has been underway on this battlefield for a full month. 3. On 31 January, the National Army of Democratic Kampuchea [NADK] and the people attacked and totally smashed this large-scale campaign launched by the Vietnamese communist puppets, under the command of the Vietnamese communists; their alliance; and the two-headed government. 4. On 31 January, the NADK and the people captured four tanks, two armored personnel carriers, and six military trucks in an area east of Bak Nim after these vehicles stumbled into an ambush by the NADK and the people. The NADK and the people captured three other tanks and three military trucks east of Sre Noy. They killed over 100 enemy troops and wounded over 100 more. Several hundred survivors have retreated.
