930916 FT 16 SEP 93 / Observer: Power player Probably the only mystery left in crime fiction is why so many of its most successful practitioners are women. 'It's simple really - women generally don't have direct power over events and people in their lives. So they kill people in print instead,' says Janet Cohen, 53, corporate finance director of Charterhouse bank, whose fourth venture into crime writing, Death among the Dons, has just been shortlisted for the prestigious Crime Writers' Association Golden Dagger awards. A qualified solicitor, she writes crime novels under the name Janet Neel. Her experience of fiction goes way back to 1966, when she spent time designing 'human player simulations' - war games - for the US defence department, then busily trying to second-guess the north Vietnamese army. 'I found I could win the games more easily as a guerrilla than a politician -just like being in a merchant bank,' she says.
