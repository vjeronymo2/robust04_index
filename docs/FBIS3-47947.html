Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 21 KYODO -- The outcome of a vote by the plenary session of the House of Councillors on Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa's reform bills remains uncertain with the meeting due to start at 3 p.m. [0600 GMT] Friday [21 January]. Leaders of both ruling coalition parties and the opposition Liberal Democratic Party were continuing their efforts to persuade rebel members within their camps to vote in line with their parties' policies. Hosokawa has reiterated his resolve to stake his political future on the package of four bills designed to introduce a combination of single-seat constituencies and proportional representation in the House of Representatives. Failure to pass the bills could lead to Hosokawa's resignation or dissolution of the lower house for a general election, many political analysts said. If enacted, the package could consolidate the political power base of Hosokawa, whose seven-party coalition government broke the LDP's 38-year grip on power last August, and trigger further realignments in the country's political arena, they said. The seven coalition parties have a total of 131 seats in the 252-member upper chamber while the opposition LDP, the Japanese Communist Party and Niin Club have 115. A simple majority of the full house is 126. More than 10 upper house members of the Social Democratic Party (SDP), the largest force in the coalition government, however, have expressed opposition to the package, fearing the introduction of single-seat electoral districts in the lower house might deal a severe blow to the party. Some LDP members are also expected to defy the party leadership and vote for the bills.
