Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Georgiy Shmelyov] [Text] Moscow January 21 TASS -- Russian and Uzbek delegations held a working meeting at the Russian Foreign Ministry on Friday to discuss bilateral relations, the situation of ethnic Russians in Uzbekistan and some regional problems. The Russian delegation was headed by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and the Uzbek delegation, led by Uzbek Parliament Acting Chairman Erkin Khalilov, included Kozyrev's counterpart Saidmukhtar Said Asymov. Vladimir Shumeyko, chairman of the upper chamber of the Russian Parliament, took part in the talks, which were held in a friendly and constructive atmosphere. "We met as real friends, partners and close neighbours. Our relations are notable for a full mutual understanding and close interaction", Kozyrev said. Khalilov seconded this view by saying the two delegations should not sit on two opposing sides of the table but at a round table with no division between them. In the course of the talks, a decision was reached to establish a mixed group of Russian and Uzbek experts to draft legislative proposals on citizenship. Kozyrev's political advisor Galina Sidorova told ITAR-TASS the negotiations were business-like and its results will lead to a practical settlement of problems and mutually acceptable decisions.
