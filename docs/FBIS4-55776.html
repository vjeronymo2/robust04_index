BFN [Text] Warsaw, May 13 -- Deputy Defence Minister Jerzy Milewski on Friday reported to the Sejm about the main points of the Defence Ministry policy toward the Polish arms industry. He said that sixty to seventy per cent of the Defence Ministry orders would be placed with the home arms industry. He added that the Defence Ministry cannot assume responsibility for debts from the previous period. There is an agreement between the Polish Defence Ministry and the United States Defence Department providing for promotion of a programme of the Polish Armed Forces modernization. The cooperation in this field envisages production based on U.S. latest technologies, Milewski said. He added that Poland had not concluded any other agreement with the U.S. Polish arms industry cooperates also with arms industries of France, Sweden and Israel. Janusz Onyszkiewicz, the defence minister in the Hanna Suchocka Government, said that Poland has large facilities for overhauling military equipment becuase such were the tasks Poland performed within the Warsaw Treaty. Deputies of different groupings demanded that a general debate on issues related to the defence be held in June.
