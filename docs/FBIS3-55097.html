Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The trial of Anzor Sharmaidze, 21, charged with the murder of Fred Woodruff, an official of the American Embassy in Georgia, began in Tbilisi on Wednesday [5 January]. Before the trial the lawyer of the unemployed defendant made a statement claiming the murder was a tragic accident. The trial taking place at the Supreme Court is attended by the vice-consul of the American embassy. Woodruff was killed last summer on a highway near Tbilisi. Investigators arrived at the conclusion an attempt was made to stop the diplomat's car for the purpose of robbery. As the car driven by the chief of Shevardnadze's security did not stop, fire was opened at it. According to many sources, Woodruff was a CIA officer. The chief of the organization James Woolsey came to Tbilisi to escort the dead body back home.
