940930 FT 30 SEP 94 / UK Company News: North Sea Assets falls 64% and agrees sales North Sea Assets, which services the offshore oil and gas industries, reported interim pre-tax profits 64 per cent down, at Pounds 432,000, compared with Pounds 1.19m, for the half year to June 30. Turnover fell to Pounds 14.3m (Pounds 15.1m). Operating profits were halved at Pounds 704,000 (Pounds 1.43m) reflecting 'competitive pressure on margins', said Mr Ted Kalborg, chairman. Earnings per share fell 75 per cent to 0.58p (2.31p). The company is selling Hydra-Lok, a subsea pile connection service, to Hunting Oilfield Services for Pounds 6m cash. The company also announced a joint venture between its subsidiary Huntly Equipment Rental and Balmoral Group. The venture, to be known as Balmoral Marine, will be 20 per cent owned by North Sea Assets and 80 per cent by Balmoral Group.
