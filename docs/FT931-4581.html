930310 FT 10 MAR 93 / Dell PC sales surge 117% in final period DELL Computer, the US personal computer manufacturer, yesterday unveiled its fourth consecutive quarter of sales growth in excess of 100 per cent. Net income for the fourth quarter more than doubled to Dollars 31.3m, or 77 cents per share, from Dollars 15.4m, or 41 cents, while Dell reported sales of Dollars 620.3m, a rise of 117 per cent on Dollars 285.7m in the same period last year. International sales rose 103 per cent to Dollars 242m in the fourth quarter. 'This has been an excellent quarter and a banner year for Dell,' said Mr Michael Dell, chairman and chief executive. 'We doubled our annual revenues to Dollars 2bn, more than doubled our market share and emerged as the third-largest IBM-compatible personal computer company in the world.' Net income for the year rose to Dollars 102m, or Dollars 2.59, up from Dollars 51m, or Dollars 1.40 in fiscal 1992. Dell sells personal computers direct to users, by-passing dealers and retailers. Sales to corporate, government and education accounts increased 132 per cent to Dollars 302m in the fourth quarter of fiscal 1993, compared with Dollars 130m in the same period in the previous year. Sales to small to medium-sized businesses and individuals rose sharply to Dollars 248m, up 128 per cent.
