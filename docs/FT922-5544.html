920530 FT 30 MAY 92 / Czechs need Dollars 50bn to counter pollution Czechoslovakia will have to spend more than Dollars 50bn (Pounds 27.7bn) to curb heavy pollution and bring its environmental standards up to acceptable levels, the World Bank said yesterday, Reuter reports from Washington. '(Czechoslovakia) has become one of the world's most polluted countries,' the Bank said in its World Bank News publication. 'It will . . . be decades before the country's main environmental problems are solved.' The Bank said it had approved a Dollars 246m loan for Czechsolovakia to help the country's major power plants run more efficiently and reduce air pollution. 'These plants are Czechoslovakia's largest polluter, generating tons of sulphur dioxide and dust,' it said.
