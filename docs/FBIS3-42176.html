Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Ethiopian Hi-Jacker Surrenders"] [Text] Rome, 24 Jan (ANSA) -- The hijacker was taken to Rome's Regina Coeli prison later in the morning, and will be charged with attack on public transport services. A search of Mulat's luggage produced a large wooden crucifix, religious material and a notebook containing verses copied from the Bible, as well as photographs of the man's family. The hijacker told investigators that he was a Copt and was of a very religious nature. The 43 passengers and crew on board the Ethiopian Airlines Boeing 757 (not 747 as stated previously) will be flown to Addis Ababa on the same aircraft at around midnight (2300 GMT) Monday night.
