August 20, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

JERRY HULSE'S TRAVEL TIPS

Soon the leaf peepers will be out in force as the spectacle of the fall foliage season gets underway in Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, Rhode Island and Vermont. Country inns throughout the New England states will be welcoming guests by the thousands. To assist travelers planning independent tours, a list of inns is available from New England B&B, 1045 Centre St., Newton Centre, Mass. 02159. Telephone (617) 244-2112. Others can be obtained from New England U.S.A., 76 Summer St., Boston, Mass. 02110 (free sightseeing maps of New England states plus a travel planner).

Tours

Your travel agent will help you arrange conducted/independent tours of New England. For names of California agencies specializing in New England tours, write to Jerry Di Pitro, c/o Tourco, 712 Main St., Hyannis, Mass. 02601. Di Pitro is one of New England's major tour operators. One of his offerings is an eight-day motor-coach trip through New England for $890 (includes lodging, ground transportation, some meals).

-- Tauk Tours is considered the Rolls-Royce of tour operators. A wide range of rates. Write to Tauk Tours, P.O. Box 5027, Westport, Conn. 06881. Or call toll-free (800) 468-2825.

-- Yankee Holidays, 20 Spring St., Saugus, Mass. 01906. Call toll-free (800) 225-2550. A choice of day-tours out of Boston, with visits to Maine, New Hampshire, Rhode Island. The same company is featuring a "Taste of Boston" tour with dinners at an Italian restaurant only steps from Paul Revere's home and the Old North Church, and seafood at the renowned Skipjack's restaurant in historic Copley Square.

-- Domenico Tours, 751 Broadway, Bayonne, N.J. 07002. Telephone toll-free (800) 554-8687. Does a volume business. Prices run the scale from $189 up. An 11-day grand tour figures out to $899 (Boston to Cape Cod, New Hampshire, Vermont, Canada, Niagara, with a windup in New York). Dozens of variations. A five-day Boston-Cape Cod-Martha's Vineyard trip will cost you $419. A total of 35 tours scheduled during September/October. Details in a 40-page fall foliage booklet.

Leafer's Guide

For a "leaf peepers guide," contact the White Mountains Attraction Assn., Box 176, North Woodstock, N.H. 03262. This handy little brochure tells how leaves change color and puts to rest that old wives' tale about Jack Frost being responsible for the brilliance of autumn colors. The guide advises that the foliage season in New Hampshire generally begins in late September, continues into the first week of October in northern New Hampshire, with colors peaking in southern New Hampshire between Oct. 5 and 15.

-- Country stores, a Norman Rockwell exhibition, horse farms and a maple museum are listed in a free "Vermont Attractions" guide. Copies from Vermont Travel Division, 134 State St., Montpellier, Vt. 05602.

-- For a brochure highlighting the Shelburne Museum (displays a side-wheeler, steam train, period furniture, carriages, weather vanes, scrimshaw), send a post card to Shelburne, Vt. 05482.
