BFN [Text] Australia's foreign minister, Gareth Evans, says the recent crackdown on press freedom in Indonesia has not signalled a move away from liberalization by Jakarta. Senator Evans says he believes Prime Minister Paul Keating will raise the banning of three Indonesian news magazines with President Suharto during his visit to indonesia. Mr. Keating leaves for Jakarta tomorrow. Speaking on commercial television, Senator Evans said that Indonesian society was undergoing profound change and setbacks were unavoidable. [Begin Evans recording] This is part and parcel of the bumps, and grinds and advances and retreats that are associated with a major society in the process of major transition. I think the underlying movement is clear and upward and onward and all those fronts but there is bound, I fear, to be some retreat along the way. [end recording]
