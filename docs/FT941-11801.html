940202 FT 02 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Murray Johnstone's new Lloyd's fund Murray Johnstone, the Glasgow-based investment management group, yesterday announced the a new Lloyd's fund to take advantage of the insurance market's decision to admit corporate investors. Murray Johnstone's investment trust clients have subscribed Pounds 29m to the new vehicle, Lomond Underwriting, which will provide Pounds 54m in capacity to Lloyd's syndicates. 'Participation in the 1994 Lloyd's year of account should give Lomond Underwriting the opportunity to obtain additional capacity on the chosen syndicates for 1995 and subsequent years,' explained Mr Giles Weaver, managing director of Murray Johnstone. Willis Faber & Dumas (Agencies), a subsidiary of Willis Corroon, the broker, will act as members' agent to the group, while Murray Johnstone manage the fund's investments. Lomond could seek a listing in the second half of 1994. Ernst & Young is sponsoring the venture. Initial investors in Lomond Underwriting are Murray International Trust (Pounds 12.3m), Murray Income Trust (Pounds 8.6m), Murray Smaller Markets Trust (Pounds 4.5m) and Murray Ventures (Pounds 3.6m).
