920414 FT 14 APR 92 / World Commodities Prices: Tea There was very strong and widespread demand reports the Tea Brokers Association. Bright liquoring and most medium East African were a full 10 to 15p dearer. Central African teas also sold readily with gains of 2 to 5p. Ceylons were again well supported and improved quality sorts advanced by 5 to 10p. Offshore strong demand in the cif section with prices 2 to 10p dearer. Fob teas from Mauritius came to a more selective market. The highest price realised this week was 199p for a rwanda pd. Quotations: quality 170p nom, good medium 130p, medium 112p, low medium 90p.
