Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report under "Contacts" rubric: "Visits by Diplomats"] [Text] A number of Western countries' embassies in Moscow, having followed closely the development of relations between Tatarstan and Russia, sent their diplomats to the Permanent Mission of the Republic of Tatarstan in Moscow immediately after the signing of the bilateral treaty. I.U. Yusupov, Tatarstan's permanent representative in Moscow, welcomed the following people for talks: M. Stross [name as transliterated], trade adviser to the French Embassy, and N. Shibayev [name as transliterated], this same embassy's adviser on political questions; Dr. Kh. Kretshmer [name as transliterated], EU deputy chief of mission; N. Foster, first secretary for political questions at the British Embassy; A. Ruigrok [name as transliterated], first secretary at the Dutch Embassy; and A. Hughes, second secretary at the South African Embassy. During the meetings the diplomats expressed satisfaction at the conclusion of the treaty process and conveyed their congratulations to the Tatarstan leadership in the light of the treaty's signing. Particular attention was paid during the talks to a discussion of this document's international-legal aspects, and above all the extensive opportunities the treaty opens up for participation in international organizations and the establishment and development of direct relations with foreign states, including for purposes of concluding agreements.
