Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report by Valeriy Gritsuk: "Deputies Are Also for Reforms"] [Text] A new club, "Deputies for Reforms," has been recently registered with the Minsk City Executive Committee. The Russian elections have shown a real situation in society and possible scenarios of its development. It is obvious that the fledgling dictatorial tendencies must be resolutely opposed. One should not stand aside at a time when society is threatened with turmoil. The deputies' club will keep to the following principles in its activities: Rejection of violent actions, accord and equality of nationalities, intolerance to totalitarianism in all its manifestations, equality of all types of ownerships, including private ownership of land. The first meeting of the deputies with common sense, concerned with the fate of their people, will take place on 5 January.
