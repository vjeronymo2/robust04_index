931014 FT 14 OCT 93 / Kuwait ministers set for tax talks KUWAITI finance and oil ministers plan to meet the government to discuss the Pounds 600m in tax which Kuwait may owe on its investment in British Petroleum shares. 'I understand they are due over here soon,' said a senior Kuwaiti adviser. The Financial Times recently reported that Kuwait may have abused its sovereign immunity from taxation in relation to its purchase in the late 1980s of a stake close to 22 per cent in BP. It has received more than Pounds 600m in tax refunds on income from the shares to which it may not have been entitled because the shares were purchased by the Kuwait Petroleum Corporation and were not a direct investment of Kuwait, as originally claimed. The adviser said that it would be unwise for the UK to press for repayment of the Pounds 600m, because UK exports to Kuwait, especially defence contracts, could be jeopardised. He said it would be better for the two countries to come to a 'mutual understanding'. However, the UK government said there was no possibility of the Inland Revenue ceasing its investigation of the tax. It added: 'There is no way the Treasury or Foreign Office can intervene in an investigation of this kind.'
