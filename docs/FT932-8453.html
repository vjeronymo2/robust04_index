930519 FT 19 MAY 93 / Parliament and Politics: Minford questions Lamont policy PROFESSOR Patrick Minford, the leading economist, last night questioned the chancellor's grasp of economics. Prof Minford, one of the 'seven wise men' who act as independent economic advisers to the government, said on Sky News: 'Norman Lamont says he wants a stronger pound, but how can he possibly do so when the whole benefit of getting out of the ERM was to let the pound drop, to find its own level, and to let interest rates fall to allow the economy to recover? It's extremely puzzling and it makes me worry whether he knows what he's doing.' An ardent free-marketeer, Prof Minford has long advocated lower interest rates and a lower pound.
