Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS and KAZTAG correspondents Amangeldy Akhmetalimov and Igor Barsukov] [Text] Washington February 15 TASS -- A meeting between Bill Clinton and President Nursultan Nazarbayev of Kazakhstan took place in the White House on Monday. Nazarbayev had arrived in the United States for an official visit. At the close of the talks, the two presidents signed a charter of democratic partnership between Kazakhstan and the U.S. Nazarbayev handed Mr Clinton a deed of accession of the Republic of Kazakhstan to the treaty on non-proliferation of nuclear weapons. Speaking at an ensuing press conference, the U.S. President said this was his first meeting with the president of Kazakhstan and it turned up to be a very good one. The two countries' efforts to prevent the proliferation of nuclear weapons are one of the most important aspects of bilateral cooperation, Mr Clinton said. The handing over of a deed of accession to the nuclear non-proliferation treaty as a historic step which gives an example to the whole world at the time that is crucial for international efforts as regards non-proliferation of nuclear weapons, he added. The U.S. President announced that in order to strengthen partnership between the two countries and support reforms being effected by Kazakhstan, the U.S. will give Kazakhstan 311 million dollars this year as against 91 million dollars last year. "We also agreed to develop trade between the two countries", Mr Clinton said.
