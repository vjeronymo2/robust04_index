December 28, 1990, Friday, Valley Edition

COLLEGE BASKETBALL ROUNDUP;

VALLEY WINS 9TH IN ROW, 84-67, WITH FLURRY OF 3-POINT BASKETS

Tisa Rush scored 23 points and four other players scored in double figures to pace Valley College to an 84-67 victory over El Camino on Thursday in the first round of the Fullerton tournament.

The Monarchs (14-1), who have won nine consecutive games since losing to Moorpark, were ranked No. 1 in the state in the latest J.C. Athletic Bureau rankings.

The Monarchs made nine three-point baskets, erasing the school record of seven set in 1986-87. Rush and Sylvia Castaneda each had three. Castaneda scored 15 points and Katina Mines, Roz Linton and Wendy Bruse each finished with 12 for the Monarchs.

In another first-round Fullerton tournament game:

Canyons 91, Long Beach City 69 -- Shannon Solway had 24 points and 10 rebounds to lead the Cougars. Guards Keira Irwin (six assists) and Shawnee Danels (seven) had little trouble breaking Long Beach's full-court press.

MEN

Antelope Valley 99, Cerritos 86 -- J. R. Rider scored 36 points and teammate Tony Madison added 30, including six three-point baskets, to lead the Marauders (10-3) past Cerritos (15-5) in a first-round game of the College of the Desert tournament.

Antelope Valley led, 49-41, at halftime and never trailed. Rider pulled down 13 rebounds for the Marauders and teammate Ennis Wesley scored 14 points.

Canyons 98, Bellevue (Wash.) 80 -- Reggie Bell scored 27 points and had 13 rebounds to lead Canyons (7-7) to the first-round win at the Canyons tournament.
