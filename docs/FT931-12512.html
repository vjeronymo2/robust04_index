930128 FT 28 JAN 93 / Japanese urge Britain to re-enter ERM JAPANESE business wants the UK to re-enter the European exchange rate mechanism, a Japanese official told MPs yesterday. Mr Tamon Kitabatake, commercial minister at the Japanese Embassy in London, said sterling 'needs to be stable' if Japanese business leaders are to have the confidence to continue investment in Britain. Mr Kitabatake told the British/Japanese parliamentary group that Japanese business leaders paid close attention to UK relations with the rest of the EC. Japanese inward investment in the UK peaked in 1990, and since then has halved. Mr Kitabatake said this resulted from the bursting of the Japanese economic bubble and the fact that much of the investment was in preparation for the European single market. 'I feel a little pessimistic on the future trend at this moment,' he said. 'But if the world economy regains an upward trend, that should change.' He said that the downturn in Japan's investment in the UK was in line with its overseas investment as a whole. It was expected to recover in the longer term. Only 7 per cent of Japan's assets were located overseas, compared with 14 per cent for the US and 24 per cent for the UK. About 40 per cent of Japan's EC manufacturing investment is in the UK. Mr Haydn Abbott, managing director of Sony UK, told MPs it was important that the UK should not be politically or economically marginalised in Europe. A stable exchange rate was very important, he said. He criticised the UK for lack of attention to road and rail links. He said: 'The absolute necessity to be able to move products to their markets seems a forgotten cause.'
