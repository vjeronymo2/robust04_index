BFN [Text] Warsaw, 5 May--In a telephone conversation with a reporter from Polish Radio Three on Thursday, Zygmunt Wrzodak, head of Solidarity in the Ursus tractor factory, said that one should not hold talks with the present government but should form strong structures attached to the Solidarity National Commission, which will form an interim government in the future. "The government has to be given an ultimatum: If it wants to talk, then it should sit at the table and negotiate and fix some dates for things to be solved. If not, then we have to remove those people from the government and replace this government with an interim one," he said. According to Wrzodak, this is the opinion of a few people from factory commissions in Mazowsze region, but the Solidarity National Commission should make a decision. In his opinion, all strikes should be clearly and properly coordinated.
