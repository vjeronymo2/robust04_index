930311 FT 11 MAR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Major plays down Scots water row THE prime minister has insisted that no decision has been reached about whether Scottish water should be privatised. On Tuesday, Mr Major sparked opposition anger when he appeared to indicate that privatisation would go ahead, telling MPs: 'I have no reason to doubt that water privatisation in Scotland will be effective and efficient, as elsewhere.' But in an interview in today's Scotsman newspaper, Mr Major says this should have been qualified by a rider saying 'if implemented'. He said: 'We are in the middle of a consultation period. We will finish the consultation period. We will make a decision; it will take account of the special Scottish dimension. That has always been the position.'
