920121 FT 21 JAN 92 / Bulgaria and Greece in talks BULGARIA and Greece yesterday began unofficial talks in Athens aimed at preventing a rift following Bulgaria's recognition of the Yugoslav republic of Macedonia last week, write Judy Dempsey in London and Laura Silber in Belgrade. The move came as Mr Milan Babic, leader of Krajina, the Serb-inhabited self-proclaimed republic in south-western Croatia, said no UN troops would be deployed in the region. Bulgarian diplomats said negotiations with Greece, would seek to guarantee stability in the Balkans.
