940531 FT 31 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television Today's Life Stories (8.00 BBC1) is one of those episodes that make you touch wood and mutter 'There but for the grace ..' In the 1960s Alan Crabtree left Rochdale for South Africa. His career in banking progressed, he met and married Tish, and they had children. Then he unearthed a fraud at work and, as seems to happen in these matters, lost his job and his home. Back in the UK in 1986 the family found themselves in a council flat in Bethnal Green. Today they are still there, with Alan working as a courier and Tish bringing in more money from her job in a City optician's. BBC1 is launching a new drama and, having looked at what is provided elsewhere, channel controller Alan Yentob and his colleagues have decided that what we need is a series about a policeman. Yes really. But don't worry, The Negotiator is no ordinary policeman, gosh no. How about this for novelty: Charlie King uses unorthodox methods but his superiors overlook it because he gets results. King is played by Brian Cox who has not played an idiosyncratic policeman since 'Grushko' (9.35).
