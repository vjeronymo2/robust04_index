921002 FT 02 OCT 92 / UK Company News: Andrews Sykes' dissident calls for support MR JACQUES Murray, the dissident shareholder who holds a 26.6 per cent stake in Andrews Sykes, the industrial services and environmental products group, yesterday attacked the company's recent financial performance and called on shareholders to support his bid to gain control of the board. Andrews Sykes has set October 16 as the date for an extraordinary meeting called at the request of Mr Murray who is demanding the removal of Mr David Hubbard, chairman, and Mr David Crowe, a non-executive director, from the 5-man board and their replacement by himself and three of his associates. In a letter to shareholders Mr Murray highlighted the 'repeated failure to achieve expected levels of profitability' since 1989, and urged them to back his resolutions. Mr Murray, who holds his equity stake through two investment vehicles, European Fire Protection Holding and Midland Bank Overseas Nominees, noted that pre-tax profits had declined by 81 per cent since 1989, claimed that profits have been 'shored up' by a pension fund surplus, and that dividends have been paid from reserves in the past two years 'despite a clear need to preserve resources.' In an earlier letter Mr Hubbard strongly recommended shareholders to vote against Mr Murray's resolutions, which he said was an 'unmerited and unwelcome attempt to gain control of the company.'
