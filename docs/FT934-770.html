931223 FT 23 DEC 93 / Caracas bomb case ends An appeals judge has thrown out charges against a businessman accused of masterminding a scheme to set off bombs and speculate on Venezuela's financial markets in the ensuing panic, AP reports from Caracas. Thor Halvorssen, 50, former telephone company president linked to international anti-drug efforts, was freed from jail after the judge found insufficient evidence to uphold charges of homicide, property damage and intimidation.
