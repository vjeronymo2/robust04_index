BFN [Excerpt] There have been no new developments in the government crisis. Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri is spending his fourth day at home in Quraytim today. His visitors report that he is persisting with his positions, which call for broadening the basis of government representation of Christian leaders. The visitors appealed to him to start exercising his powers again and to maintain his authority. Within the framework of the efforts to solve this crisis, former Prime Minister 'Umar Karami is now meeting with Chamber of Deputies Speaker Nabih Birri. Delegations continued to arrive at Prime Minister al-Hariri's house in Quraytim, where he received Labor Minister 'Abdallah al-Amin, Minister of State Shahe Barsoumian, and Deputy Khatchig Babikian. Earlier, al-Hariri received al-Mina' Mayor 'Abd-al-Qadir 'Alam-al-Din, who expressed his support for him in continuing to shoulder responsibility. He also received a delegation from the al-Fulayti clan in 'Irsal, which called on al-Hariri to continue shouldering his responsibility. He then met a delegation led by Shaykh Khalil al-Mays, the mufti of Zahlah and al-Biqa. [passage omitted]
