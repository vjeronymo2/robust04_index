910424 FT 24 APR 91 / UK Company News: Cosalt shows sharp decline to Pounds 1m In the half year ended March 3 1991, 'a most difficult period', Cosalt returned pre-tax profits down Pounds 617,000 at Pounds 1.01m. Outlining the reasons, Mr Edward Brian, chairman, said the fishing industry remained depressed, the caravan companies had to reduce working hours to allow overproduction to be absorbed by the market, and the workwear side, which manufactures for the rental trade, received fewer orders as a consequence of the recession. The interim dividend is held at 4.25p, payable from earnings per share of 5.9p (9.36p).
