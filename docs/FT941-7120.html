940226 FT 26 FEB 94 / Pakistan wins Dollars 2.5bn aid Pakistan's foreign aid donors yesterday praised its recent efforts to improve public finances and to privatise state companies, and granted Islamabad's request for Dollars 2.5bn in fresh assistance, of which Dollars 2.3bn will be provided in 1994-1995, writes David Buchan from Paris. Meeting for the first time since April 1992, the 13 western governments and multilateral agencies making up the Pakistan aid consortium endorsed the country's economic reforms which had remained unaffected by the change in government four times in seven months last year.
