930408 FT 08 APR 93 / Cuomo shuns court GOVERNOR Mario Cuomo of New York has taken himself out of consideration for nomination to the US Supreme Court, according to news reports yesterday, writes George Graham. Mr Cuomo, who has long been an eloquent social conscience for the Democratic party, had been viewed as the front-runner to replace Justice Byron White, who will retire from the court in the summer. President Bill Clinton refused to discuss the Supreme Court appointment yesterday, but said he thought Mr Cuomo was 'terrific'. During the presidential election campaign last year, Mr Clinton had said he thought the New York governor would make a good Supreme Court justice, and Mr Cuomo himself has said the position would be heaven. In his usual ambivalent style, though, he also likened the court to a tomb.
