December 20, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

COLOMBIA;

DRUG KINGPIN'S AIDE WAS KILLED IN RAID

A man killed last week at the side of drug kingpin Gonzalo Rodriguez Gacha has been identified as one of the lieutenants of the powerful Medellin cocaine cartel, police said. Authorities initially thought the man was one of the bodyguards slain during a gun battle with police at a coastal ranch. But Luz Mary Celades, the wife of Rodriguez Gacha, identified the body as that of Gilberto Rendon Hurtado, reportedly the No. 8 man in the cartel. Rendon Hurtado was wanted for extradition to the United States, but he was not on the so-called dirty dozen list of top drug traffickers wanted by the U.S.
