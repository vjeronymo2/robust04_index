Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Jan. 23, IRNA -- Minister of the Interior 'Ali Mohammad Besharati met the Emir of Qatar Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani in Doha Sunday said the water supply project from Iran to Qatar is now past its feasibility study and was going ahead as originally planned. He also suggested a concerted move by the petroleum exporting countries to reverse the downward trend of the oil price. The emir of Qatar welcomed the proposal saying he would do everything possible, including a production cut, to ensure that oil prices would rise. Besharati conveyed to Al Thani the greetings of Iranian President Hojjat ol-Eslam Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani, and said that the ties between the two countries are based on mutual respect and non-interference in each other's affairs. He said that the recent setting up of the Bushehr-Doha shipping line between the two countries should speed up trade and related ties between the two countries. The Qatari ruler welcomed the idea of Iranian exports to his country and extended an invitation from Hojjat ol-Eslam Rafsanjani to visit his emirate. Later today, the Iranian minister met the Qatari heir apparent Shaykh Bin-Hamad Khalifah Al Thani and discussed promotion of economic and political ties between the two countries. In the meeting which was also attended by the Qatari interior minister, Shaykh Hamad called for promotion of Tehran-Doha ties in accordance with international laws, and appreciated Iran's support for Qatar's principled stands. He also referred to Iran's relations with the United Arab Emirates, saying Qatar was making efforts to prevent exploitation of the issue of the three Persian Gulf islands, although it appreciates iran's sincere stand in this regard. Regional countries should cooperate with each other and should realize that economic development depends on security in the region, said the Qatari heir apparent. In the evening, Besharati accompanied by his Qatari counterpart visted the police headquarters.
