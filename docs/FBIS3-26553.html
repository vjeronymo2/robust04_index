Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By RENMIN RIBAO reporter Yin Pinduan (1438 0756 4551) and XINHUA reporter Sun Yong (1327 0516)] [Text] Beijing, 2 Feb (XINHUA) -- A forum to mark Comrade Deng Yingchao's 90th birth anniversary and the publication of the Collected Works of Deng Yingchao and In Memory of Our Elder Sister Deng was held this afternoon at the Great Hall of the People. Those attending the forum included Hu Jintao, Chen Muhua, Wang Guangying, Wang Zhaoguo, Zhao Puchu, and Hu Sheng. Li Ruihuan, member of the CPC Central Committee Political Bureau Standing Committee and chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [CPPCC], spoke at the forum. He said: Comrade Deng Yingchao was an outstanding woman of the Chinese nation as well as a stateswoman and social activist who was widely recognized around the world. She made indelible contributions to winning the liberation of the Chinese nation and Chinese women, to the development of China's socialist modernization, and to opening up the socialist path with Chinese characteristics in the new period. Li Ruihuan said: During her long revolutionary career, Deng Yingchao took part in much important party work, in which she made the greatest contributions to Chinese women's liberation and the country's united front work. While she was chairman of the Sixth CPPCC National Committee, she upheld and refined the multiparty cooperation and political consultation system under the CPC's leadership; forcefully advocated and promoted the workstyle of "political consultation, democratic supervision, working in a cooperative spirit, and making friends at home and abroad"; and, with the broad vision and personal charisma unique to proletarian revolutionaries, united friends of all circles, enabling our patriotic united front to continue to gain strength and to develop. In dealing with people of all circles, she always treated them with sincerity and was always amiable and easygoing, enabling her to win everyone's trust and love. Li Ruihuan pointed out: Deng Yingchao was a respectable leader of the party and the state as well as an elder sister of noble character and high prestige. One can see in her the traditional virtues and fine character of the Chinese nation. Despite her high position, she never sought personal gain and she abided by the party's discipline, was honest in performing her official duty, and always regarded serving the people as the most important work, maintaining the true quality of a public servant. She selflessly dedicated her whole life to the country and the people; her spirit of dedication is indelibly engraved on the memory of the Chinese people. Huang Qizao, vice president of the All-China Women's Federation, said at the forum: During her revolutionary career of more than 70 years, Deng Yingchao was full of enthusiasm for Chinese women's liberation. As a pioneer and leader of China's women's movement, she mobilized, organized, and urged women to participate in the revolutionary movement and production and construction work; called for the realization of equality between men and women, both legally and in real life; and repeatedly emphasized the need to protect women's fundamental interests and to manifest the principle of the freedom of marriage through legislation. She also placed great importance on raising women cadres' educational levels and on enhancing their skills, and urged governments at all levels to pay attention to cultivating and promoting women cadres. She was an outstanding representative of the new breed of women brought up by the Chinese people's revolution. Also speaking at the forum were Zhao Puchu, Huang Hua, Qian Zhengying, and Liu Ying. In their speeches, they recalled with deep affection Comrade Deng Yingchao's great contributions, praising her as a model communist. Song Ping, Song Renqiong, and Ma Wenrui also attended the forum. The forum -- jointly held by the CPPCC National Committee, the All-China Women's Federation, and the Central Party Literature Research Center -- was presided over by Li Qi, adviser to the Central Party Literature Research Center.
