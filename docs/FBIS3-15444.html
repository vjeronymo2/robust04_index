Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan March 6 TASS -- A delegation of the Minsk group on Nagorno-Karabakh of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe headed by its chairman Jan Eliasson arrived in the Armenian capital Yerevan on Sunday. During a meeting with Armenian Foreign Minister Vagan Papzyan, Jan Eliasson shared his impressions about his visit to Baku and Stepanakert. According to Eliasson, both Baku and Stepanakert had come to the conclusion that it was impossible to settle the Karabakh conflict by means of force. He said the Minsk group maintained contacts with Russian president's envoy Vladimir Kazimirov. The CSCE Minsk group leadership hopes to find a key to the peaceful settlement of the conflict in close cooperation with all the parties concerned including Russia, he said. The Armenian foreign minister expressed hope the Minsk group's activities would help determine all the initial positions to get the process of preparation to the Minsk conference on Karabakh moving. The only possible way to settle the Karabakh conflict is to coordinate the CSCE mediatory efforts with Russia's peace-making initiative, Papazyan said. In this connection he expressed hope the Minsk group leadership and the staff of the CSCE chair would work in close cooperation with the Russian Foreign Ministry. Later in the day the CSCE delegation held working consultations with the Armenian Foreign Ministry's expert group headed by First Deputy Foreign Minister Zhirair Liparityan. Jan Eliasson also met Armenian Defence Minister Serzh Sargsyan. The CSCE Minsk group delegation arrived in Yereven after meetings with the Azeri leadership during which Eliasson called for stage-by- stage settlement of the conflict on the basis of international principles and stressed that the cease-fire agreement was the first step towards restoring peace in the region. He said CSCE is ready to send observers to the region.
