BFN ["Special dispatch": "Mainland China To Allow Foreign Businessmen To Contract For Major Capital Construction Projects"] [Text] Beijing, 11 May--According to a relevant quarter, a Chinese department has formulated a new policy under which foreign businessmen will be allowed to invest or contract for some major infrastructure projects. After their completion, the projects will be managed and operated by the foreign businessmen during the contract period, which lasts 15 years or so, and the Chinese side will collect taxes. At the expiration of the contract period, the entire projects will be returned to the Chinese side. According to a briefing, foreign investors can have access to power plants, railways, highways, and other fields. Not only will they be allowed to undertake projects, they will also provide technology, equipment, and managerial personnel. The authorities will also allow foreign businessmen to participate in project-related items, such as commerce, real estate, advertising, and other tertiary industry along the express highways and railways.
