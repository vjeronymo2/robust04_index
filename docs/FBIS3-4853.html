Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 28 KYODO -- Retail prices of imported goods sold in Tokyo dropped in February, reflecting the appreciation of the Japanese yen, the Economic Planning Agency [EPA] reported Monday [28 February]. The agency, citing its survey in Tokyo's 23 wards, said prices declined from the year before for 16 of 20 items examined. Perishables such as beef and bananas were sold at prices lower than those at customs clearance but retail prices of clothes such as shirts and brassieres were higher than customs clearance prices, the agency said. Retail prices of beef and bananas dropped 17.5 percent and 12.8 percent, respectively, from the year-earlier level and their customs clearance prices were down 7.3 percent and 13.7 percent, respectively. Prices of shirts and brassieres declined only 4.9 percent and 5.6 percent from the previous year against price falls of 22.1 percent and 15.3 percent at customs clearance, the agency said.
