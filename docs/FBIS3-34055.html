Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Regarding the ongoing investigations into the crime [assassination of Jordanian diplomat], Beirut's first investigations Judge Sa'id Mirzah met with Jordanian Ambassador Fakhri Abu-Talib at the embassy at 1800 today to discuss the circumstances of the incident. The meeting was attended by a Jordanian delegation that accompanied the ambassador. The delegation includes Jordanian Foreign Ministry Counselor Hamzah 'Abd-al-Qadir and a police colonel. A number of Lebanese security commanders also participated in the meeting. President Ilyas al-Hirawi has sent Deputy Prime Minister Michel al-Murr to the Jordanian Embassy to offer condolences. Al-Murr conveyed to Ambassador Abu-Talib the condolences of the Lebanese Government and people. He noted that the president was eager to delegate him personally to show solidarity with the Jordanian king and people and with the embassy in particular. Ambassador Abu-Talib said the incident targeted Lebanon's security and stability. Ambassador Abu-Talib returned from Amman today after accompanying the Jordanian diplomat's body there and received condolences at the embassy. Minister of Labor 'Abdallah al-Amin cabled condolences to the Jordanian foreign minister, saying that the painful incident was a stab at Lebanon's security and peace.
