940520 FT 20 MAY 94 / UK Company News: Crown Products for market Crown Products Group, which consists of Crown Toys & Gifts and which proposes to acquire Children Design & Leisure, is seeking to gain a listing under Stock Exchange Rule 535.2 from May 27. Chairman and chief executive is Mr Michael Hughes, who was at Bluebird Toys from 1989 to 1992 as managing director of the international side and later as a main board director. The pathfinder prospectus states that he jointly founded CDL 'to exploit the opportunities in the international toy market'. Through CTG, Crown is a supplier of games and toys to UK retailers, including Woolworths, Asda, Tesco, WH Smith, Toys R Us and Hamleys, as well as Argos, Freemans, GUS and Makro. CDL makes, develops and markets toys in 15 countries. In 1993, CTG made pre-tax profits of Pounds 15,000 on turnover of Pounds 836,000, while in the six months to December 31 CDL achieved Pounds 51,000 on sales of Pounds 630,000.
