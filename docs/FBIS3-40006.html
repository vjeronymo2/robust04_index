Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin at The Hague as Dutch Foreign Minister Peter Kooijmans' official guest has started his official meetings. TRT correspondent Ihsan Gunal reports from The Hague: [Begin recording] The issue most emphasized during Cetin's meetings in The Hague is Bosnia-Herzegovina. Cetin started his visit by meeting with Dutch Prime Minister Ruud Lubbers. Answering reporters' questions following the meeting, Cetin said that Turkey's request to send close to 10,000 soldiers to Bosnia-Herzegovina is still valid adding that the necessary directives regarding the initiatives were issued to NATO and the United Nations. Cetin pointed out that he had sent letters regarding the issue to the secretary generals of the NATO and the United Nations as well as to the foreign ministers of some countries. Later, official meetings headed by the two foreign ministers started. The issue most emphasized in the agenda is Bosnia-Herzegovina. Focusing on the terrorism issue, bilateral relations, economic relations, the situation of the Turks living in Europe, xenophobia, Turkey's membership in the European Union, and the situation in the Caucasus were also discussed. Cetin will conclude his contacts in The Hague tomorrow. [end recording]
