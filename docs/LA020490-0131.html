February 4, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

THE WEEK AHEAD

BINGO DEBATE: The Cudahy City Council Monday night is scheduled to decide whether to issue three-year licenses to operate bingo games. Opponents fear approval will attract another card club to the city.

The council is split on the issue.

SENATE ELECTION: Eight candidates are running in a special election Tuesday to fill the 31st District state Senate seat vacated by William Campbell (R-Hacienda Heights).

SMALL CASTLES: Responding to complaints of "castle-type" houses that are being built in Arcadia, the City Council will consider restricting the size of homes at its Tuesday meeting.

ROARING RAPIDS RIDE: Nearly 40 Los Angeles County firefighters and members of the county's earthquake rescue response team won't be in search of fun and games at Magic Mountain on Wednesday when they climb aboard a fast-moving white water river ride for training in water rescue techniques. This training course teaches life-saving skills needed to take on the dangerous task of pulling trapped victims from rain-swollen rivers, canals and flood channels.
