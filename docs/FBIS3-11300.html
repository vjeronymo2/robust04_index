Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Electricity of Haiti [EDH] has decided to cease completely the exploitation of the Peligre hydroelectric power station because the artificial lake has reached its minimum level of 153 meters. During the shutdown, repair and maintenance will be carried out at the power station on two of the three turbines in order to take the maximum advantage of the next rainy season expected during the month of May. According to an EDH announcement, the work should last four weeks. EDH will guarantee 100 1.326369e-315xploitability of the production capacity of the hydroelectric power station, that is 54 MW, as soon as the level of the lake goes back up with the heavy rains of May. EDH hopes to reduce rationing soon and to maintain reasonable service throughout the grid in the capital. We should also say that mud patches have allegedly reached the shores of the lake and have even allegedly reached the turbines, according to inhabitants in the area contacted by Signal FM.
