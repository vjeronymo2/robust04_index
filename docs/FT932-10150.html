930511 FT 11 MAY 93 / Ciampi set to clear second hurdle THE Italian government of Mr Carlo Azeglio Ciampi looked set yesterday for a comfortable victory in a vote of confidence in the senate, the upper house of parliament, which is scheduled for later this week, writes Haig Simonian. The debate marked his second parliamentary test within a week after last Friday's successful vote of confidence in the chamber of deputies. It coincided with a sharp rise in the value of the lira, reflecting increasing support for the new government. The currency was quoted at L916.88 against the D-Mark, its highest level since early January. It started gathering strength last week as dealers regained confidence in Mr Ciampi's government after a shaky start when four cabinet ministers resigned within hours of taking office. The upturn came as Mr Antonio Fazio, Mr Ciampi's successor as governor of the Bank of Italy, told fellow central bankers in Basle that a recovery in the Italian economy could not come through lower interest rates alone.
