August 20, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: PROSTATE PROBLEMS

Question: If someone has an enlarged prostate, won't there come a time when he will have to have an operation? And if that's the case, wouldn't it be better for that person to have the operation and get it over with, rather than waiting?

Answer: Surgery for an enlarged prostate may not be inevitable.

The results of one study showed that of more than 100 men who had symptoms of benign prostatic hyperplasia, only 10 had opted for surgery during the following five years. Moreover, of those men who did not have an operation during this period, most felt their symptoms improved.

Q: From talking with other mothers, I've become convinced that most ear infections in children happen during the winter months. Is there any logical reason for this? Since the ears are exposed all year-round, what difference does the time of year make in regard to the possibility of an ear infection?

A: Ear infections in children can and do occur throughout the year; however, children tend to have more colds and upper respiratory infections during the winter. According to Dr. Richard Layton, an expert on pediatric allergy, when children have more colds, the possibility of ear infection increases.
