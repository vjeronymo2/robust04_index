920131 FT 31 JAN 92 / International Company News: Finland to have new savings bank FINLAND is to have a new savings bank from September this year with assets totalling FM100bn (Dollars 23bn), it was announced yesterday. To be called Suomen Saastopankki SSP, it will bring together 41 of the present Finnish regional savings banks in the country which between them account for 80 per cent of the assets in Finland's savings bank system. The new bank will have the largest number of deposits of any bank in the country and it will become the fourth largest commercial bank in Finland when it starts operations. The chief executive officer is to be Mr Paavo Prepula, the present head of the savings bank in the regional city of Tampere.
