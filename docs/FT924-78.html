921231 FT 31 DEC 92 / Teenagers look to Continent for work EIGHT out of 10 British teenagers would be willing to look for work in continental Europe, according to a survey conducted by the Research Business for TSB. The study found that only 20 per cent of 17-year-olds believed they would never be unemployed, but many saw other European Community countries as an alternative location for their careers. A group of 300 aged 13 to 17 were first interviewed in the spring about their expectations after the Conservative election victory. This month a follow-up study showed that 40 per cent of the group had seen redundancy affect their family or friends. In 10 per cent of cases their fathers had been made redundant.
