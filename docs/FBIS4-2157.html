BFN [Text] Beijing, 7 Apr (XINHUA) -- Foreign Ministry Spokesman Wu Jianmin said here today the maintenance of relaxation and stability of the Korean Peninsula conforms to the common interests of both the Korean people and all other parties concerned. "We oppose any action harmful to the relaxation and stability in the Korean Peninsula, be it military exercises or deployment of missiles." He said: It is necessary to refrain from taking actions that will sharpen the contradictions, especially under the current circumstances. He made the statement at a news briefing this afternoon when asked by reporters to comment on the fact that the United States has begun shipping "Patriot" missiles to the Republic of Korea [ROK] and that the United States and the ROK are planning to resume their Team Spirit joint military exercises. In response to a reporter's question on the nuclear issue involving the Democratic People's Republic of Korea [DPRK], he reiterated that China has all along maintained that the DPRK's nuclear issue should be settled through dialogue. All parties concerned have expressed the willingness to settle the issue through continued dialogue.
