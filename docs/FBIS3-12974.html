Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 4 Mar (MENA)--Yemeni Interior Minister Brigadier General Yahya al-Mutawakkil denied accusations that his country is one of the countries exporting terrorism and terrorists to Egypt. In an interview with AL-AHRAM to be published tomorrow, he said that Yemen is targeted by terrorism and terrorists, especially the al-Jihad Organization and the Arab Afghans who have been discovered by the investigations to be behind a number of recent assassinations in Yemen. He added that the brain and financier of terrorism in Yemen is Saudi businessman Usamah Bin-Ladin, who is wanted for trial in Yemen. The Yemeni interior minister stressed his country's readiness to deport any Egyptian in Yemeni territory who is suspected by Egyptian authorities of being involved in terrorist incidents. He said that he will be the first to defend Egypt's security and stability. Brig. Gen al-Mutawakkil revealed that Yemen prevented those who came from Afghanistan from using its territories as a crossing point, stressing that Yemen will never be a source of concern for Egypt. Regarding the internal situation in Yemen, he said that he expects the military skirmishes in Abyan and Mudiyah to stop next week and the forces involved in the fighting to withdraw to their original positions.
