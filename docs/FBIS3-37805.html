Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russian President Boris Yeltsin and President of Kyrgyzstan Askar Akayev met in Kremlin on Tuesday morning [22 February]. Boris Yeltsin's press service announced that the two presidents had discussed a wide range of issues pertaining to bilateral trade and economic cooperation and the status of ethnic Russians in Kyrgyzstan. The two officials also exchanged views on the situation in the CIS and on ways of strengthening it; the situation in Central Asia and joint efforts to settle the Tajik conflict, and on other international problems. Akayev said that he had brought in a package of proposals on solving the problem of ethnic Russian's migration from Kyrgyzstan. Following their discussion with the Russian president, Akayev will present them to the CIS summit to be held in March. "I think we must share our ideas with all of the CIS leaders because this is a problem of common concern," he said. He told Yeltsin that the situation in his republic was stable and quiet.
