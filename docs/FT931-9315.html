930215 FT 15 FEB 93 / Economic Diary: No quiet on unemployment front THIS will be a tense week for the UK government with Thursday's unemployment figures set to take the jobless total over three million. The consensus forecast is for a seasonally adjusted rise of 40,000, but an increase of 26,000 is all that is needed to reach three million, last surpassed in the mid-1980s. Last month, unemployment rose by a much higher than expected 61,000 and the trend growth rate accelerated to around 40,000 a month. However, the figures do fluctuate and monthly increases as low as eight million were recorded twice last year. Other important UK figures this week include manufacturing output, retail sales and the public sector borrowing requirement. The Bank of England's quarterly bulletin will contain the first of the Bank's independent quarterly analyses of inflation. Here are some of the week's other economic highlights.
