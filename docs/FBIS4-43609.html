BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] President Suleyman Demirel has stated that with its democratic, secular, and liberal economy, Turkey will be able to contribute to regional stability if it becomes a full member of the European Union [EU]. Hans van den Broek, EU commissioner responsible for foreign relations, has concluded his contacts in Ankara. Demirel received van den Broek and the delegation accompanying him at Cankaya Mansion. Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin also participated in the meeting. According to a statement issued by the Press Center of the President's Office, van den Broek stated that the EU is aware of Turkey's importance as a regional power. Demirel said that Turkey should not lag behind East and Central European countries in attaining full membership in the EU. At the meeting, Demirel stressed that the Cyprus issue should be solved between the two communities and should not pose an obstacle to Turkish-EU relations. Cetin and van den Broek held a joint news conference after the meetings between the Turkish and the EU delegations. Cetin said Turkey has integral economic, political, and security relations with the EU. Van den Broek, in turn, stated that the EU is against terrorism and fully supports Turkey in its efforts to defend its territorial integrity through the democratization package. On the human rights issue, van den Broek said that the struggle he waged in the past has made President Demirel a hero of democracy.
