920214 FT 14 FEB 92 / Capital goods spending falls INVESTMENT by manufacturing industries in capital goods fell by 15 per cent last year compared with 1990, the Central Statistical Office said yesterday. Spending on vehicles suffered the biggest decline, down by 39 per cent from the previous year. Spending on plant and machinery fell 15 per cent and investment on new buildings fell 9 per cent. In the fourth quarter of last year capital expenditure was down 0.6 per cent compared with the previous three-month period. the value of investment in the fourth quarter of last year was Pounds 2.54bn at seasonally adjusted 1985 prices, compared with Pounds 2.96bn in the fourth quarter of 1990. Total capital expenditure last year was Pounds 10.27bn compared with Pounds 12.14bn in 1990.
