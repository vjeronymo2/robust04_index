920331 FT 31 MAR 92 / World Trade News: Iran, Turkey and Pakistan set up bank IRAN, Turkey and Pakistan are to set up a joint bank with a capital of Dollars 320m (Pounds 185m), Iran's central bank governor Mohammad Hossein Adeli said, Reuter reports from Nicosia. Mr Adeli, back in Tehran from a meeting with his Turkish and Pakistani counterparts in Karachi, said the Bank of Commerce and Development would finance trade among the three countries and invest in joint or separate projects, Tehran Radio said. He said Iran would hold a third of the capital. Shareholders would be private and public banks and financial institutions from the three countries.
