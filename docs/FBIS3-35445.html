Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan has decided to temporarily recall all diplomatic and other personnel from its embassy in Kabul. The decision was made after miscreants attacked Pakistan's embassy in the Afghan capital on 23 February. The Pakistani consulates at Mazar-e Sharif, Qandahar, and Jalalabad will continue to function as usual. Foreign Secretary Shaharyar Khan told a Radio Pakistan representative in Islamabad last night that Pakistani diplomatic and other personnel have already left Kabul for home. Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, who made a brief visit to Kabul to hold meetings with Afghan leaders on the establishment of peace in Afghanistan, met President Borhanoddin Rabbani yesterday. He apprised the Afghan president of Pakistan's decision to temporarily recall its diplomatic personnel. The Afghan president was told that the embassy building is now unfit for use, and the diplomatic staff can only fulfill their duties when arrangements are made for a suitable building and their security is guaranteed. Commander Ahmad Shah Mas'ud, who is actually in control of Kabul, has expressed his deep grief and anguish over the incident. Afghan President Borhanoddin Rabbani has expressed his regret over the attack on the Pakistani Embassy in Kabul by some irresponsible people. In a message to President Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari, he said the attack was probably the work of vested interests that want to harm the brotherly relations between the two countries. President Rabbani said these relations have been further strengthened by the assistance extended by Pakistan during the Afghan war against foreign aggression. He particularly thanked Pakistan for providing shelter to hundreds of thousands of Afghans during this period. Meanwhile, speaking to APP in Kabul, the Afghan president said he has ordered an inquiry into the incident, adding that those responsible will be punished. Commander Ahmad Shah Mas'ud, accompanied by the Pakistani foreign minister, visited the Pakistani Embassy to assess the damage. Meanwhile, seven rockets landed in the Afghan capital of Kabul yesterday. At least six people were reported killed and 10 injured.
