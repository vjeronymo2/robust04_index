BFN [Text] Paris, May 4 KYODO -- Japanese Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata and French President Francois Mitterrand agreed Wednesday [4 May] that North Korea's suspected nuclear weapons program threatens the global move toward preventing the spread of nuclear weapons to non-nuclear powers. The two leaders shared the understanding during a two-hour meeting at the French Presidential Palace, Japanese officials said. The meeting took place after Hata arrived in Paris from Rome as part of his European tour, which will also take him to Germany and Belgium. They said Hata told Mitterrand that the North Korean nuclear issue "is a serious problem that threatens the nuclear nonproliferation regime, as well as undermines the security of the Asia-Pacific region." Hata also said, "we must counter the threat in solidarity, while keeping the channels for (diplomatic) dialogue open." Mitterrand agreed with Hata's view, the Japanese officials said. On China, Hata told Mitterrand, "it is important for China to provide greater transparency in its activities in the military field...and it is crucial for us to urge China to demonstrate a positive response in the field of human rights." Hata also told Mitterrand, "Japan wants to step up its cooperation with France in various fields from now on" to build a new international order to secure global peace in addition to strengthening the capabilities of the United Nations, the officials said. Mitterrand replied, "I agree with your view. The two nations can build up a stronger relationship of cooperation." The French president expressed his interest in enhancing cooperation, particularly in extending economic assistance to such areas as Indochina and Africa. Hata expressed gratitude for France's endorsement for Japan's bid to secure a permanent seat on the UN Security Council, of which France is one of the five permanent members. French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe expressed his support for Japan's bid for permanent membership on the UN decision-making body during his visit to Tokyo in March. Mitterrand, however, stopped short of addressing the issue, according to the Japanese officials. The French leader expressed his nation's readiness to welcome Japanese Emperor Akihito and his wife Michiko, who are slated to visit France in October. "We are looking forward to meeting the emperor and empress," the president was quoted as saying. Hata replied, "the emperor is also looking forward to the visit." The royal couple is slated to visit France and Spain on a two-week trip that will begin in late September.
