December 3, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

THE NORVILLE AFFAIR

Regarding the Nov. 26 article on Deborah Norville taking over Jane Pauley's job on the "Today" show:

I stopped watching ABC's "Good Morning America" in protest when I heard Sandy Hill's exit was engineered by chauvinist paranoia.

I stopped watching CBS' "Morning News" in protest when they dumped Maria Shriver, Forest Sawyer and Mariette Hartley.

I am going to stop watching NBC's "Today" show in protest against the obtuseness of the network executives responsible for the resignation of Pauley and because there's no visible substitute for Jane's warmth and professionalism.

WANTED, by yearning, caring, potentially loyal viewer: Single strong network with charm, perception and the self-confidence to keep terrific people in place because, as the greatest sirens knew instinctively, it's far more seductive to hold still and let them come to you.

You can't build a long-term relationship on a series of one-night stands.

LAURA HITCHCOCK

Los Angeles
