930225 FT 25 FEB 93 / Observer: Military cover-up President Clinton has been sneered at for delaying his decision on whether the army should admit homosexuals. But at least he has gained time to ponder the possible implications of a trial that has just come up in Portugal. After 17 years of being admired by neighbours as a much-decorated albeit poor war veteran, retired General Tito Anibal da Paixao Gomes has been exposed as not only an impostor, but a woman. The discovery was made by police when, having arrested the dignified figure for debt offences, they could not find the name in military records. The prisoner turned out to be Teresinha Gomes, last seen in Madeira in 1974. The neighbours' surprise, however, was as nothing to the shock the general's wife received on making the same discovery. Five years after accepting the self-styled wounded warrior's proposal of a chaste marriage, retired nurse Joaquina Costa spotted the truth when she went unannounced into the bathroom.
