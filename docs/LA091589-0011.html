September 15, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

911 GUIDELINES

I was horrified by the events which led to the shooting deaths of Maria Navarro and three house guests in East Los Angeles (Metro, Aug. 30).

My heart aches for the families and children involved in the aftermath of such senseless murders, and also for the emergency dispatcher who followed established but inadequate procedures.

Surely, 911 operators must be given new guidelines with which to instruct threatened callers:

1. Have intended victim leave location.

2. Notify neighbors and friends to be on the lookout.

3. Lock doors (although I believe no locks will keep a determined assailant out!)

4. Ask a friend or relative (the mother, if possible) of the assailant to try to deter him/her.

5. Post a prominent note on door to warn assailant of intent to self-defend, imminent arrival of the police, a plea to desist, etc.

With our expertise at space exploration, we need more knowledge and commitment to exploring the most fascinating space of all -- the human mind: Solutions can be found there.

DIANE E. WATSON

State Senator

D-Los Angeles
