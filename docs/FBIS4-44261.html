BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- A maritime agreement has been concluded between Cuba and Germany, which replaces a treaty between the former GDR and the Caribbean island. As the Transportation Ministry in Bonn announced on Wednesday [22 June] after negotiations with Havanna were concluded, the new agreement regulates, among other things, the freedom of establishment and the free transfer of cargo for maritime shipping. An important part of the agreement is the fact that ships of the partners to the agreement are treated like the respective country's own ships regarding access to ports and the use of services. In addition, the freedom of establishment is ensured for shiping companies.
