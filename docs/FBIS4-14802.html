BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Yuriy Tyssovskiy] [Text] Moscow April 1 TASS -- Negotiations of Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and United Nations Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali, currently here on an official visit, were held today at the Russian Foreign Ministry's residence to discuss peace-keeping operations of the United Nations and Russia. "It was a specific business-like conversation and I am grateful to the United Nations secretary-general for it," the Russian foreign minister told a news conference here today. The minister described Butrus-Ghali as a diplomat who heads the world's leading organization and a person who has made a colossal contribution to the solution of international problems, including settlement of armed conflicts around the globe. In his turn, Butrus-Ghali said that Russia has played a most important diplomatic role on the world scene within the past few weeks. The Russian diplomacy helped to find a solution to the Sarajevo and Tuzla problem, which was an important contribution to peace-making efforts in former Yugoslavia. The secretary-general expressed the hope that Russian-U.N. cooperation will bring peace to Georgia and Tajikistan and thanked Russia for the assistance to the consolidation of the U.N. role. The meeting of Kozyrev and Butrus-Ghali lasted for over an hour.
