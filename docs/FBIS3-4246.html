Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 26 (XINHUA) -- An official from the People's Bank of China said here today that the provisional regulations on the exchange settlement, surrender and payment have been formulated to ensure an ample supply of foreign exchange by the central bank in order to realize the convertibility of renminbi under current accounts, which is central to the reform of China's financial system. The regulations were issued on December 29, 1993. According to the regulations, all foreign exchange receipts, except those held by foreign-funded enterprises, foreign loans and foreign exchange for debt servicing and exchanges in the hands of individuals, must be surrendered to the state banks and if they need foreign exchange for import and export and other purposes, they have to buy it in the exchange market. The regulations say that foreign-funded enterprises may retain their foreign exchange receipts. They may sell or buy them at the foreign exchange swap market.
