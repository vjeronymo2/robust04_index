BFN [Text] In his lecture to students of the MAINICHI SHIMBUN Businessmen's College at a hotel in Makubari, Chiba city, Isao Ishizuka, Air Self-Defense Force chief of staff, touched on the issue of North Korea's suspected development of nuclear weapons and said it is necessary to bring North Korea's past nuclear developments to light. Commenting on the U.S. Government's tilt toward freezing the DPRK's current nuclear program, he said: "My personal view is that freezing North Korea's nuclear program alone is not enough. As a neighboring nation of the DPRK, Japan will not be satisfied with freezing North Korea's nuclear program without verifying that country's past nuclear developments."
