May 9, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

RELEASE OF 2 AMERICAN HOSTAGES BY LEBANESE SHIITE KIDNAPERS

U.S. hostage Frank Reed has been released by his Shiite Muslim kidnapers. He was held for approximately 3 1/2 years.

Although happy that he has been released and is back with his family, Reed is reported to be angry at everybody for the length of time he and others were kept, and that others are still there, too (Part A, May 3).

I wonder how he thinks the rest of us feel? He was not in Lebanon on behalf of the United States government. For whatever reason, and it may have been a very noble reason, he chose to stay in Lebanon even when it was very clear that any American in Lebanon ran a very high risk of being kidnaped. Finally, he was kidnaped. What followed cost this country, both in terms of money and in terms of prestige.

Now, he is angry at everybody that he was held so long. The person he needs to be angry at is himself for exposing himself to that danger as he did.

Actually, I am a little angry at him for putting us all through this affair when it could so easily have been avoided, and for acting as if this hostage matter is capable of resolution by the United States government.

He needs to grow up. This is a dangerous world. If you take a risk, then be prepared to take the consequences.

PAUL D. FILLMAN

Long Beach
