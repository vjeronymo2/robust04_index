Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Colonia, 17 Jan (NA) -- President Carlos Menem today criticized the region's businessmen and said "there are companies" in Mercosur [Common Market of the South] member countries "that, safeguarded by an internal growth policy, were protected more than necessary." "They lost their spirit of initiative and market sensitivity, which should be their essence, so competing in a broader market is a difficult challenge for them," the Argentine president said. Menem's statements were interpreted as a reply to the demands made by some industrial groups in the region, especially the Argentine Industrial Union (UIA), which repeatedly has requested the postponement of the deadlines to establish Mercosur. The Argentine president, however, said his government "is clearly concerned with this situation because behind each company there are capital and jobs of people whom we cannot forget." Menem made these statements during the official opening ceremony of the fifth Mercosur Council meeting that is being attended by the presidents of Uruguay, Brazil, Paraguay, and Bolivia in the El Mirador Hotel convention hall. Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Losada attended the meeting as a special guest since his country is not a Mercosur member. ? With Mercosur, Menem said: "We have begun to change the continent's history and I would even say that we have taken a qualitative step toward our maturity as a nation." "Interrelation has become so important that currently not even the major world powers can avoid political, economic, and cultural associations with other countries. The European Union and NAFTA are eloquent evidence of this." He said: "The economic benefits our four countries currently are obtaining through Mercosur are evident," adding that one of the most evident accomplishments has been the increase of trade in the region. When referring to the troublesome subject of common import duties, the Argentine chief of state praised the fact that the member countries have so far agreed on a considerable amount of items, although he recognized that "there is still much to be done." "In 1994 we must make a renewed effort to improve the basic instruments that will guarantee fluid trade in Mercosur as a free trade zone and as a customs union," Menem said. In turn, Brazilian President Itamar Franco said efforts in the past three years to establish Mercosur "were greatly compensated for by the positive results already attained." Franco agreed with Menem that in 1994 the member countries will go through a decisive stage because they must define a common import duty to consolidate the integration process.
