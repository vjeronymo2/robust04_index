BFN [Text] Russian Prime-Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin is confident that next July the G-7 countries will grant Russia political membership of their club. The Big Seven are planning to hold a summit conference in Naples. Talking to newsmen Sunday at a Moscow airport before boarding a flight for Milan where he is to attend a session of the World Gas Union, Chernomyrdin said there was a direct link between his trip to Milan and the Naples summit. He said he would discuss Russian membership of the G-7 club with his Italian counterpart Silvio Berlusconi who is to chair the Naples summit. When he visited Washington on June 21, Chernomyrdin would discuss the issue with President Clinton. He felt there were no obstacles for Russian integration into the G-7 club. According to Chernomyrdin Russia wait with a request for economic membership of the G-7 club. Moscow realized, he said, that it could be invited to work on political issues first and only then join G-7 economic efforts. In Milan Chernomyrdin would be planning to discuss Russian- Italian relations with Prime-Minister Berlusconi and clear up where the two countries stood on the package of agreements he signed in Italy last January.
