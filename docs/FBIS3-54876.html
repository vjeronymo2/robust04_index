Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Zlobin] [Text] Petrozavodsk, 31 Dec -- Karelian residents have been given a New Year present -- President Boris Yeltsin has signed an edict "concerning the classification of individual territories of the republic as belonging to rayons of the extreme north and areas enjoying the same status." The residents of the north have been waiting a long time for this decision and regard it as equitable compensation for the discomfort they experience living in inclement climatic conditions. Now, in keeping with the edict, the residents of Karelia have been given the right to draw their pensions five years earlier than before. Apart from this, the northern allowances, payable over and above the local wages, have been considerably increased. However, residents in some Karelian villages are still dissatisfied with what they see as this kind of levelling-down. In keeping with the presidential edict, the republic has been divided into two zones with slightly differing benefits. It transpires that Kostomuksha, in the north of this lakeland area, is on a par with the southern Sortavala, where the climate is much milder. In their appeal to the Karelian Supreme Soviet deputies, Kostomuksha inhabitants demand that this inequality in benefits be removed.
