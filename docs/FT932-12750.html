930427 FT 27 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television An animal with eyes on stalks and lots of legs will terrify people even if humans are thousands of times bigger and heavier, but such is our anthropomorphism that a much bigger animal will cause cooings of delight if it simply does something supposedly characteristic of humans. Hence the appeal of the meerkat, which looks like a cross between a dog and a monkey and stands on its hind legs to watch for predators. 'Meerkats United' is repeated today in David Attenborough's Wildlife 100 collection (7.00 BBC1). In today's The Great Picture Trail National Gallery director Neil MacGregor talks about Velasquez's 'Old Woman Cooking Eggs' and Manet's 'Bar At The Folies Bergere' which, for some reason, always seems to be chosen, as here, for any collection of reproductions destined for the walls of schools (7.35 BBC2). BBC2's foreign affairs series Assignment screens the programme about the illegal export of religious icons from Russia which was postponed last week (7.45). 40 Minutes considers the life of comedian Ernie Wise without his teammate, the late Eric Morecambe (9.50 BBC2).
