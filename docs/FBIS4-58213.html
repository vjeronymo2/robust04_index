BFN [Text] Cairo, 3 May (MENA)--Palestinian and Israeli negotiators continue to meet for the third consecutive day in an attempt to finalize an agreement on the implementation of Palestinian self-rule in Gaza and Jericho. The Legal Affairs Committee, the Security Committee, and the Committee on Detainees are trying to work out some of the still unsettled points ahead of the signing ceremony, which is scheduled for tomorrow morning, Wednesday. Lawyer Jamil al-Tarifi, who leads the Palestinian team to the Civilian Affairs Committee, said that the Israeli side backtracked on several of the points previously agreed upon. He added that the PLO and Israel will sign the agreement nevertheless. Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, who are both due in Cairo today, will meet at 1800 at the Presidential Palace to discuss some unresolved issues, particularly those concerning Jericho's area and police presence on the Allenby Bridge.
