BFN [Text] This morning, a Lao delegation headed by Souban Salitthilat, deputy foreign affairs minister, left Vientiane for Cairo, Egypt, to attend the 17th nonaligned ministerial conference from 30 May to 3 June. Over the past more than 30 years, the Nonaligned Movement has firmly adhered to its principle aimed at safeguarding world peace, contributing to the struggle for national liberation and disarmament, and speaking against racial discrimination and for improved living conditions of people in developing countries, thus making it an influential political force in the international arena. At the Cairo ministerial conference, the Movement will discuss various issues, including its future and program of action, particularly in contributing towards making the United Nations more democratic so it will heed the interests of all countries -- be they big or small, developed or underdeveloped. In addition, the Movement will also discuss the forthcoming eleventh summit, cooperation between the Nonaligned Movement and other international organizations, economic cooperation, human rights issues, the environment, and so forth.
