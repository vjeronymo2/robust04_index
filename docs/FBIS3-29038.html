Language: Thai Article Type:BFN [Text] The commerce minister expressed confidence that Thailand will be removed from the Priority Watch List, or PWL. He said this after presenting shields to senior police commissioners in recognition of their cooperation with the Commerce Ministry in cracking down on video tape piracy. Elaborating on Thailand's efforts to rid itself of PWL status, Commerce Minister Uthai Phimchaichon said the copyright bill is now awaiting the first reading by the House of Representatives this year. Thailand has taken measures under the principle of international trade and the postponement of the U.S. decision made him believe that the United States will remove Thailand from the list. [Begin Uthai recording in progress] ...credibility in trade and the serious efforts we have made will help convince our trade partners like the United States to remove us from PWL. Evidently, the United States keeps postponing the announcement of its decision on Thailand. This, together with several other indications, reflects the growing trust they have in us. They are now waiting for more action from us. [end recording]
