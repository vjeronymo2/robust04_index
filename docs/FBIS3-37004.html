Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Mikhail Voronenkov] [Text] Moscow, 10 Feb -- The Russian Federation's Foreign Ministry is satisfied with the preliminary results of the Russian- Ukrainian talks on implementing the results of the January meeting between the Presidents of Russia, the United States, and Ukraine. This was said at a briefing today by Grigoriy Karasin, the director of the Department of Information and Press of the Russian Foreign Ministry. At the talks, that took place in Kiev on 8th and 9th February, a package of documents was agreed between the Russian Federation and Ukraine on measures to implement the trilateral Russian-U.S.- Ukrainian accords. The drafts of these documents will be presented for examination by the Russian Federation leaders and Ukraine. "There are good grounds to suppose," Grigoriy Karasin stressed, "that after the documents are examined and confirmed by the leadership of Russia and Ukraine, they can become an undoubted benefit for both states and for the whole of the international community." Russia's state delegation at the talks on 8th and 9th February in Kiev was headed by Yuriy Dubinin, roving ambassador of the Russian Foreign Ministry. The Ukrainian delegation was headed by Valeriy Shmarov, deputy prime minister of Ukraine.
