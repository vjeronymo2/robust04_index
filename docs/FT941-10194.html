940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / International Company News: KPMG terminates Spectrum role Spectrum Information Technologies, the company that Mr John Sculley abruptly left earlier this week, said yesterday that its accounting firm, KPMG Peat Marwick, had terminated its relationship with the company. KPMG was brought in at Spectrum in November by Mr Sculley, the former chairman and chief executive of Apple Computer. On Monday Spectrum restated its earnings, based on KPMG's recommendations, to defer Dollars 6.4m in revenues previously recorded in the past two quarters. Spectrum said that the accounting firm gave no reason for ending the relationship, but indicated that the resignation was 'not based on any accounting disagreements with the company'. 'We view their action as arbitrary and unprofessional,' said Mr Salvatore Marino, chief financial officer. 'The accounting firm had not yet reviewed the financial records of the company. We think it is more than coincidental that their resignation follows that of John Sculley.'
