BFN [Text] The permanent representative of the United States at the United Nations, Mrs. Madeleine Albright, arrived Thursday [31 March] evening on a few hours' visit and handed over a written message from the U.S. President Bill Clinton to President al-Bashir, dealing with issues of mutual concern. The U.S. official has held talks with General al-Bashir at the Republican Palace following her arrival. These talks tackled the progress of bilateral relations and issues of mutual concern. In a press statement following her meeting with President al-Bashir, she said that Sudan and the United States should work together for pushing forward the peace process, human rights, and what she described as Sudan's support to terrorism. She said that her dialogue with General al-Bashir was very serious and frank, saying that the continuation of the IGADD [Intergovernmental Agency on Drought and Development] peace initiative is necessary for achieving a lasting peace in Sudan. The foreign minister, Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih, described the visit of Mrs. Albright to Sudan as a real beginning of the serious dialogue between Sudan and the United States. He affirmed that the dialogue has been one of the basic methods which had been adopted by the revolution adding that the visit of Mrs. Albright came in the framework of the serious dialogue between the two countries. The minister Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih stressed that the Sudan gives top priority to the issues of realizing a just and permanent peace. He warned her that any (?work) for supporting the outlaws with arms and attempts to abort the peace talks. Will negatively affect the peace process in Sudan. Dr. Abu-Salih has welcomed contribution of the American Government to the peace endeavors in Sudan, providing that this contribution was serious and honest. He described as lacking evidence, the accusation made by the American Government on Sudan's violation of human rights and support to terrorism. He affirmed Sudan's serious desire to continue dialogue with the U.S. Following her meeting with President al-Bashir which lasted for two hours, Mrs. Albright left Khartoum. She was received and seen off at the airport by the Foreign Minister Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih, the state minister for foreign affairs, Bishop Rorech, along with the U.S. ambassador to Sudan Donald Petterson.
