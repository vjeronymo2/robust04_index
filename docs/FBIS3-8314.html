Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Sarajevo Radio Bosnia-Herzegovina Network in Serbo-Croatian at 1400 GMT on 7 March carries within its regular 30-minute newscast several reports on the combat situation in the republic: According to Maglaj correspondent Hajrudin Cermic, "instead of a cease-fire arranged to exchange the dead bodies of the soldiers of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army and those of the Serbian aggressor, Maglaj is being subjected to artillery fire. After unsuccessful attacks on [words indistinct], the attacks were directed exclusively on civilian targets. Some 70 57-mm shells were fired from Bofors, while tanks are opening intermittent fire from [name indistinct]. One civilian was wounded." Correspondent Mirudin Aldobasic reports from the 7th South Operations Group that "the aggressor early this morning suddenly shelled with heavy artillery Kalosevici and the lines of defense of the 207th Brcko Brigade and [word indistinct]. The shelling is continuing, although infantry attacks have been successfully repelled." Aldobasic also says that "the aggressor is constantly" attacking defense lines in the Teslic region with heavy arms. Reporting on the shelling of Bihac, correspondent Mirza Sadikovic says that "one shell alone killed three and wounded five persons while they were performing their duties and jobs. Regarding the new material damage, no one is talking about it any longer. The final number of the victims of today's shelling is still unfortunately unknown, considering that the killing from the hills around Bihac is still going on. In addition to Bihac, Cazin and the surrounding area are also being targeted with howitzers and mortars." Zavidovici correspondent Viktor Boban reports that "the chetniks this afternoon launched attacks on the defense lines in several sectors." He also says that the "defenders are fiercely responding, not allowing the aggressor to advance even a millimeter." Boban concludes his report by saying that three civilians have been killed.
