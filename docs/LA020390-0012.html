February 3, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

POLICE RELATIONS

Regarding Michael Wilmington's Jan. 12 review of "Internal Affairs":

I wonder if a more sensitive reviewer or a more aware (daring?) movie maker would have questioned the screenwriter's gratuitous use of a "lesbian partner" as a police officer.

I can almost picture the studio conferences over this issue:

"Let's have a woman partner -- that's 'in.' "

"OK, but we can't have another love interest subplot -- let's make her a lesbian, that's sexually safe and 'in'!"

And did even one rational voice suggest that maybe a good-looking heterosexual man and an attractive heterosexual woman can work together, professionally and respectfully , without "doing it"? Oh, no, that's dull city, eh, guys?

PHYLLIS MAGILL LEVY, Mission Hills
