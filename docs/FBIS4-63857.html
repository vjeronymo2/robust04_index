BFN [Text] The Black Sea Fleet officers have urged the Ukrainian, Russian and Crimean presidents, the CIS heads of state and heads of parliament to settle complicated state and regional issues only through normal negotiations. The Black Sea Fleet press center told INTERFAX that the statement had been adopted by the fleet officer meetings coordinating council in Sevastopol on 24 May. Officers recommended that the CIS leaders give the appropriate evaluation to representatives of the Ukrainian power structures stating their readiness for any decisive actions and to warn them about personal responsibility for possible consequences. Due to the concentration of troops in Crimea the Black Sea Fleet officers proposed that the Ukrainian, Russian and Crimean presidents guarantee that no force would be used. The Ukrainian Security Service and Interior Ministry must report on the additional military formations arrived in Sevastopol without any notification to the local authorities, the officers emphasized. The statement underlined that military officers must not be hostages during struggle for posts and reorganization of the society among politicians.
