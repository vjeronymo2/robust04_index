BFN [Text] TALLINN, Apr 13, BNS -- "I believe Russia will withdraw its troops from Estonia by Aug. 31," Enn Tupp, head of the Estonian Defense Ministry defense policy department, told BNS at a press conference today. Tupp claimed the troop pullout cannot be stopped. Jyri Kork, head of the defense forces general staff information department, added that every week from 30 to 40 Russian military personnel leave Estonia. The Russian military staying in Estonia are a political toy, just like the Skrunda radar facility in Latvia, Tupp noted. The Russian delegation to the Estonian-Russian troop withdrawal talks announced April 6 that Russia is not going to pull out its troops from Estonia by the Aug. 31, 1994, deadline. Russia also officially revoked the clause in the draft agreement that established Aug. 31 as the withdrawal deadline.
