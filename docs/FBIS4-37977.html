BFN [Text] Sanaa, 25 Jun (MENA) -- Informed sources in Sanaa have told MENA's correspondent that Yemeni opposition parties will submit an initiative to stop the grinding war that has entered its eighth week between rival sides in Yemen. These sources explained that the initiative calls for an immediate cease-fire, an unconditional dialogue, cancellation of all resolutions and measures that have resulted from the war and from the declaration of session, and for compliance with the Pledge and Accord Document as a basis for dialogue. The initiative also calls for forming a military committee comprising of all the political forces to supervise the cease-fire. It notes that resuming dialogue is the only way out of the Yemeni crisis and the only way to protect Yemen's unity. Yemeni sources affirmed that the initiative enjoys the interest of Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and the Yemeni Socialist Party's unionist leaders. The Yemeni opposition parties include the Nationalist Forces Federation and the National Grouping of Opposition, which is comprised of the Right Party, the Yemeni Unionist Group, the League of the Sons of Yemen, the Nasirite Unionist Organization, the National Democratic Front, the Arab Socialist Ba'th Party--Yemen's branch, the Democratic September Organization, and the Nasirite Popular Reform Party.
