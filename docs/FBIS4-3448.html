BFN [Text] Jinan, 5 Apr (XINHUA) -- In a letter to a national conference on the work of resettling military cadres transferred to civilian sectors, which was still in progress here, Jiang Zemin, general secretary of the CPC Central Committee, president of the state, and chairman of the Central Military Commission; and Li Peng, premier of the State Council, expressed their greetings to the conference and extended cordial regards to the nation's vast number of former military cadres working in all walks of life and to comrades who made contributions to their resettlement. The letter says: Former military cadres now working in civilian sectors are the country's valuable wealth and important force in socialist modernization. The People's Liberation Army is a great melting pot which has tempered and cultivated groups upon groups of outstanding cadres who have superior political standards, strong organizational and leadership ability, higher educational backgrounds, considerable professional skills and knowledge, fine workstyle, and selfless dedication. The group of cadres who are in the process of being transferred to civilian work this year will bring new vitality to economic construction and to other endeavors in various localities. The letter says: The year 1994 is extremely crucial to the process of China's reform, opening up, and modernization. Seizing the opportunity, deepening reform, opening up wider, promoting development, and maintaining stability are the general tasks of the party and the state. Successfully resettling military cadres in the civilian sectors is a matter of vital importance to this general task and of great significance for promoting army building and economic construction, ensuring military and social stability, and promoting the smooth development of reform and opening up. Party committees and governments at all levels should take the resettlement work as an important political task and earnestly carry it out accordingly. They should do everything possible to overcome difficulties and successfully resettle and place every military cadre transferred to civilian sectors so that each has a role to play and each can display his talent. Comrades in the military should show understanding and sympathy for the difficulties of the localities and actively consult and closely coordinate with them to successfully carry out the work together.
