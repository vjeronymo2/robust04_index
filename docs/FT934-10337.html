931105 FT 05 NOV 93 / International Company News: Good first half at HK Telecom HONGKONG Telecommunications, the colony's monopoly telephone utility which is 57.5 per cent owned by the UK's Cable & Wireless, yesterday posted a 15 per cent rise in interim net profit to HKDollars 3.65bn (USDollars 472.8m) due to continuing strong growth in calls to China, writes Simon Davies in Hong Kong. Profits were up from HKDollars 3.17bn in the six months to September 1992. Sales rose by 13 per cent to HKDollars 11.95bn, with revenue from international telephone services up by a similar proportion to HKDollars 7.53bn. The interim dividend is 23.4 cents a share, up from 20.3 cents in 1992.
