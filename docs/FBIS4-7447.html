BFN [Text] The official delegation of the United States led by Madeleine Albright, U.S. ambassador to the United Nations, and General John Shalikashvili, commander of the Joint Chiefs of Staff of the U.S. Armed Forces, arrived in Sarajevo this afternoon. The delegation was received by President Alija Izetbegovic of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Presidency, Presidency member Ejup Ganic, Bosnia-Herzegovina Prime Minister Haris Silajdzic, Bosnia-Herzegovina Foreign Minister Irfan Ljubijankic, and Defense Minister Hamdija Hadzihasanovic. General John Shalikashvili held talks with Army General Rasim Delic, commander of the General Staff of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army. It is the greatest honor for the Bosnia-Herzegovina Army to be visited by General Shalikashvili, General Rasim Delic told B-H PRESS after the meeting. The talks concerned the course of the negotiations and the process leading to peace in Bosnia-Herzegovina. General Delic stated: We should say that we have the full support of the United States, both the U.S. political and military leadership, to reach an agreement as soon as possible that would guarantee a just peace. We said that we were interested only in just peace in Bosnia-Herzegovina. In that respect we expect that the support of the United States will be the deciding factor in bringing about the kind of peace that our people want, General Delic said.
