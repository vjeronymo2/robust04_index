Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 10 KYODO -- Cold weather is likely to hit northern and eastern Japan from April to September, the months for growing rice, the Meteorological Agency said in its long-term forecast on Thursday [10 March]. Last year's cold weather brought about a poor harvest of rice crops throughout Japan, forcing the government to import rice from foreign producers as an emergency measure. The northern and eastern regions of Japan are known as rice producing areas and officials said the expected cold weather for a second year in a row may possibly result in another poor harvest.
