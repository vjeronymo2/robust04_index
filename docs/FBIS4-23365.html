BFN [Text] President Nelson Mandela has decided against sending South African troops to Rwanda to join a United Nations peacekeeping force in that country. However, speaking in an interview with REUTER news agency, President Mandela said South Africa was very interested in providing humanitarian aid. He said South Africa had decided to send a field hospital to help treat Rwandan refugees and was considering other assistance. South Africa would also consult the UN on what role South Africa could play to help end the conflict in that country.
