BFN [Excerpt] At today's preliminary contact for North-South summit talks, the North and South completely agreed [wanjon habui haessumnida] to hold North-South summit talks in Pyongyang for three days, beginning on 25 July. Reporter Kim Chin-su will report from the North-South Dialogue Secretariat Office: [Kim Chin-su] At today's preliminary contact for North-South summit talks, the North and South dramatically and completely agreed [kukchoguro wanjon habui haessumnida] to hold North-South summit talks in Pyongyang for three days from 25 July. As a result, the historic North-South summit talks will become a reality. The two sides also agreed to discuss the place and timing of alternate visits to Seoul and Pyongyang by the top leaders of the two sides, which will be made on a principle of reciprocity, at the first summit talks in Pyongyang. Our side's delegation led by Yi Hong-ku, deputy prime minister and minister of the National Unification Board, and the North Korean side's delegation led by Kim Yong-sun, chairman of the Reunification Police Committee of North Korea's Supreme People's Assembly, started the first preliminary talks at 1000 today at the House of Peace, on our side's area of Panmunjom.
