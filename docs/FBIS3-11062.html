Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 12 Mar (NOTIMEX) -- Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari signed the Hemispheric Declaration on Freedom of Speech today at the end of a meeting with Latin American, U.S., and Canadian journalists. After receiving a group of representatives to the Hemispheric Conference on Freedom of Speech, Carlos Salinas de Gortari reiterated his strict support for freedom of the press and emphasized the importance that the media have in modern society. During the meeting held at the presidential residence, the Mexican president said a free media helps the government to respond better to citizens' demands. He noted that criticism and freedom of speech in the media contribute to better decisions in favor of society with the rapidness and timeliness demanded by the people. Those who attended the event included, among others, Jose Carreno Carlon, media director of the Presidency; Alejandro Junco de la Vega, REFORMA newspaper; and Claude Erbsen, Associated Press of New York.
