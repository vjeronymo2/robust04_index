940409 FT 09 APR 94 / Labour warned on cautious stance A leading figure from the Labour party's traditionalist wing last night urged the party not to be too cautious in debating its future strategy. Mr David Blunkett, the party chairman and shadow health secretary, said it was critical that in avoiding Conservative traps on spending commitments, Labour did not 'fall into the trap of re-running the 1992 (general) election' when the party's priorities were not in tune with the electorate's.
