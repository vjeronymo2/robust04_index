BFN [Text] [Announcer] Russian President Boris Yeltsin flew from Moscow to Greece today. He will attend a meeting of heads of state and government of the European Union on the island of Corfu. [Correspondent A. Dubnov] Before leaving for Corfu Boris Yeltsin answered questions from journalists on relations with European countries. According to the president, there was discrimination against Russia in earlier times, quotas for our goods were limited and our foreign economic proposals were ignored, but now there is a whole package of proposals with specific dates and figures. Boris Yeltsin noted the European community had recognized the fact that there was a transitional economy in Russia while the United States had refrained from recognizing this. [Begin Yeltsin recording] A most favored nation status in trade has been given to Russia in Europe. This is very important, too. Russia is only gaining from this. Finally, this is, perhaps, the first practical step by which Russia truly enters Europe and deservedly becomes a fully-fledged European country. [end recording]
