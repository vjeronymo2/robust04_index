930719 FT 19 JUL 93 / A year of woe July 1992 Rumours of possible liquidation mount as Amato government starts transforming Italy's big state entities into joint stock companies. July 16 Chairman and board of Efim resign. July 17 Government confirms Efim to be wound up. Five-stage operation includes issue of government-backed bonds to cover outstanding debts. Details of terms left open. July 19 Bank creditors seek urgent talks to clarify implications for creditors. Foreign banks accuse government of 'unilateral rescheduling' at crisis meetings in Milan and London. July 27 Mr Alberto Predieri becomes special administrator of Efim. Aug 5 Government presents amendments to previous month's liquidation decree. Aug 13 New decree partly clarifies terms of Efim bonds and sets L4,000bn ceiling. Bankers furious about low interest rates, and inadequate information. London-based international banks threaten to declare Efim loans in default. Mid-Aug Tense meetings between treasury and foreign bankers over early-Sept terms of bonds. Sept 9 Treasury bows to foreign pressure to raise interest rates on bonds. Sept 24 New figures show Efim's total debts are about L17,500bn - around twice previous figure. Sept 26 Italian government approves principle of selling Siv, Efim's big glass subsidiary, as first in planned sell-offs. Oct 19 Further government climbdown to bank creditors: new decree concedes full repayment of loans to all Efim's wholly-owned subsidiaries. Oct 25 Government sets out conditions for 'renting' Efim's defence (Oto Melara) and helicopters (Agusta) subsidiaries to Finmeccanica, part of IRI state holding company. Dec 15 'Rental' contract to IRI for nominal L1 signed. Jan 19, 1993 Government raises to L9,000bn ceiling on Efim bonds. Jan 27 Mr Predieri says government promises to start repaying bank and trade creditors by April 30. March 8 Italy's Official Gazette carries terms for special treasury borrowing programme to raise funds to repay creditors. However, bankers grow worried about repayment as European Commission asks whether repayment represent unfair state aid. March 18 Rome magistrates issue six arrest warrants linked to fruad inquiry into Efim's Nuova Safim finance arm and its subsidiaries. EC Commission says Efim repayment plan constitutes unfair state aid. March 31 Treasury sets first six-months' interest rate on Efim bonds. No bonds yet issued. April 6 Mr Predieri says repayment frozen pending EC approval. April 26 Mr Predieri offers to resign following appointment of new Ciampi government. April 30 Initial repayments begin to small trade creditors; payments bank creditors. June 16 Government 'reaffirms its commitment' to honour Efim's debts fully. July 14 Government agrees to transfer Agusta and Oto Melara free to Finmeccanica. Late July Sale of Siv to UK's Pilkington due to be signed.
