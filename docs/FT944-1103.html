941222 FT 22 DEC 94 / Student loan body in 35,000 backlog The Student Loan Company, the quango which controls loans to students in higher education, has a backlog of 35,000 students waiting for loans, it said yesterday. The company said about 9,000 of these had not received their loans because of an administrative error. Forms had been sent to students in April - six months before the loans would start - and far more had mislaid the forms than the company had expected, with the result that administrators were overloaded with inquiries at the start of term in September. The remaining 26,000 students had either applied late or had failed to complete their forms correctly. Sir Eric Ash, acting part-time chief executive in the absence of Mr Ron Harrison, who is ill, said students still waiting for loans should approach their universities for hardship loans. He said he was 'absolutely certain' the company would not repeat the mistakes next year, which had led to 'too many' students facing delays. Sir Eric said: 'The Student Loan Company is a very well-run company which has run into a specific problem which no one anticipated.' Vice-chancellors have campaigned to make loans repayable through income tax or National Insurance. However, the government intends to cut grants by 10 per cent next year and the year after, and make up the balance in loans. Mr Bryan Davies, Labour's higher education spokesman, said: 'If it had been pensioners who had been waiting weeks for their pension, the nation would have been in uproar.'
