Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Communist Party is leading in the pre-election campaign for a new parliament in South Ossetia, the self-proclaimed republic on the territory of Georgia, deputy chairman of the Central Electoral Commission Makhar Gasiyev told Interfax. He said 185 candidates have been registered for 41 seats in a new parliament to be elected on March 27. Gasiyev said opinion polls show that the Communist Party is supported by 90 percent of the South Ossetian population. "People are tired of the never-ending disorder." "People need to be protected, and they lay their hopes on the new parliament," Gaisyev said.
