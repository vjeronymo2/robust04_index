BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 18 May Evening Edition] [Text] A government source on the morning of 18 May commented on a report in the 17 May WASHINGTON POST that Laura Tyson, chairman of the U.S. Council of Economic Advisers (CEA), said the United States would stop exerting pressure on Japan over trade. The source welcomed the report, saying: "That is good news. They (the United States) have so far made so many unreasonable demands of us. Things must have changed a lot." But the source added: "As far as Japan is concerned, even if the United States eases (pressure on Japan), Japan should continue to push for domestic demand expansion, tax reform and deregulation." The source thus indicated the government would continue to make efforts to alleviate U.S. pressure on Japan.
