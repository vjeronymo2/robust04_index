June 8, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

OTHER COMMENTARY / EXCERPTS;

GET TOUGHER ON CHINA?;

RAY PRICE

Emotion is a poor and often a disastrous guide to policy. Yes, it's right that we should be on the students' side. But this is a time for scrupulously careful, meticulously calculated responses by the American government to an extraordinarily fluid situation in which our knowledge of what's happening is necessarily extremely limited, which we have little capacity to influence and in which the actions that instinct and emotion might command are likely to prove counterproductive.

On the American part, it's a time for sympathetic understanding and support, but not for heroics. Those are the province of the Chinese people, whose heroics of the past few weeks will reverberate through history.
