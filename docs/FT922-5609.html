920529 FT 29 MAY 92 / Pounds 200m bill for technology BUSINESS faces a Pounds 200m bill for technology needed to cope with new Customs regulations in the single European market, according to research by KPMG Peat Marwick, the accountancy group. Companies will have to provide more detailed information when Customs entry documents are abolished from the end of the year. KPMG says few companies in Britain have computer systems capable of dealing with the new regulations. Changes to provide the information needed by Customs and Excise would cost Pounds 200m.
