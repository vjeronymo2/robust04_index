Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Maynayev] [Text] Bishkek, 12 Feb [date as received] -- Today Askar Akayev, the president of Kyrgyzstan, addressed his compatriots. He thanked his fellow citizens who had expressed confidence in him at the nationwide referendum on 30 January. Akayev confirmed his adherence to the policy of interethnic accord and said that this policy was and will be the important priority of his state activities. The president said that he will use the political results of the referendum to strengthen all branches of power and to coordinate their activities to work efficiently for solving the most important problems of the society. The head of the state expressed the belief that the present situation in Kyrgyzstan demands firm and decisive actions, and these will follow soon. Decrees "On Urgent Measures on Strengthening State and Financial Discipline" and "On Urgent Measures on Strengthening Law and Order and Reinforcing the Fight Against Infringers of the Law," which were signed today by the president, have become the first steps in this direction. The president said: We consider democracy, above all, as the strictest maintenance of law and public order. He said that he is confident that unity, friendship, and accord between the peoples of Kyrgyzstan will help the country to overcome difficult ordeals.
