BFN [Text] Kiev 23 May TASS -- A high-ranking representative in the administration of Leonid Kravchuk told TASS that Kiev possesses information that during Moscow talks "Russia will abide by international norms and principles." He also said that "Kiev will not allow any changes in the status of the Crimea as an integral part of Ukraine." Before his departure Zvyahilskiy had a meeting with President Leonid Kravchuk. No details have been disclosed, but it can be assumed that the meeting specified the Ukrainian position at Moscow talks. According to some statements in the top echelons of Ukrainian authority, Kiev is not going to sidetrack from its principles of ensuring the territorial integrity of the country and will adopt corresponding measures in the framework of constitutional norms.
