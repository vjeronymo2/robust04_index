930104 FT 04 JAN 93 / Kenya to decide on Fresh elections KENYA'S three main opposition leaders were last night meeting to decide whether to pursue their demand for fresh elections, writes Michael Holman. Leaders of FORD Kenya, FORD Asili and the Democratic party came under growing pressure during the weekend from western diplomats. They were urged to reconsider their call, which western governments regard as unwise and impractical. Instead, the parties have been advised to function as a powerful parliamentary opposition. Chief Emeka Anyaoku, the Commonwealth secretary-general, is due to arrive in Nairobi today to help ease what has become a tense stand-off between President Daniel arap Moi and the opposition. 'It is my earnest hope that government and political leaders will find ways to work together for the peace, health, prosperity and unity of Kenya,' Chief Anyaoku said in a statement.
