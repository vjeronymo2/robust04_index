941125 FT 25 NOV 94 / UK Company News: Monarch Resources back in the black Monarch Resources, the London and Toronto-listed group that mines gold in Venezuela, has reported net income in the third quarter of Dollars 317,000 (Pounds 193,292) compared with a loss of Dollars 1.25m in the comparable period. The improvement reflected an 'excellent' performance at the troublesome Revemin processing plant and the start-up of the low-cost La Comorra mine during the three months. The company has decided to change its primary stock exchange listing to Toronto from December 31 and has advised the London exchange of this decision. La Comorra, which started up in the second half of August, produced 3,440 troy ounces of gold at a cash cost of Dollars 292 an ounce. There was an operating profit of Dollars 270,000 from the mine. In the quarter the Revemin plant produced 5,103 ounces of gold (4,699 ounces in 1993) at a cash cost of Dollars 334 an ounce (Dollars 477). Monarch's net income for the nine months to September 30 was Dollars 835,000 (net loss of Dollars 2.85m). Gold production reached 27,066 ounces (13,995 ounces). The average gold price received in the nine months was Dollars 384 an ounce (Dollars 350).
