921106 FT 06 NOV 92 / Parliament and Politics: Visa clampdown for Yugoslavs MOST nationals of the former Yugoslavia will need to get a visa before travelling to the UK from midnight tomorrow, Mr Kenneth Clarke, home secretary, announced yesterday. The requirement will be imposed on all former Yugoslavs except those holding passports issued by the governments of Croatia and Slovenia. The UK has already admitted about 40,000 Yugoslavs, with 4,424 applying for asylum. Mr Clarke said he would tell the United Nations and the Red Cross that Britain could take 150 former detainees from this weekend. It would also accept their dependants, making about 600 people in all. Mr Tony Blair, shadow home secretary, said: 'You may have given hope to that small group of people with one hand, but by your announcement on visa requirements today you are taking hope away from many more with the other hand.'
