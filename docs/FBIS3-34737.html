Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb 12 (AFP) -- President 'Ali Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani's brother has resigned as head of Iran's state radio and television (IRIB), the daily KEYHAN reported Saturday. Mohammad Hashemi, who was to leave his post on Saturday after 12 years, is to be replaced by the Minister of Culture and Islamic Guidance 'Ali Larijani, it said. The head of the ministry's publication bureau, Ahmad Pur-Nejati, has been chosen to serve as interim culture minister, the paper added. IRIB has come under mounting conservative criticism. Parliament's culture subcommittee issued a lengthy report in November which said many programmes had nothing to do with Islam or the Islamic revolution, and spread "un-Islamic" ideas, particularly among the young. The report prompted a strong reaction from Hashemi, who accused the hardliners of promoting "stone-age" thinking. The conservatives hit back accusing IRIB's head of defaming parliament. Newspapers said in December that Hashemi could face legal proceedings. IRIB has been under the direct supervision of the country's spiritual guide since the 1979 Islamic revolution,
