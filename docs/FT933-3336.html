930914 FT 14 SEP 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: Coffee price upsurge boils over THE LONDON robusta coffee market rode a roller-coaster yesterday, hitting a 2 1/2 -year high on news that Brazil had arranged financing for its stocks under the producers' retention scheme, and then plummeting to close lower in a wave of profit-taking. The November robusta futures contract peaked at Dollars 1,325 a tonne in early trading, the highest level since the dollar contract began trading in March 1991 and a rise of nearly 100 per cent since its low of Dollars 670 in the summer of 1992. It was a direct response to the sharp rise in arabica futures prices in New York on Friday, which followed news that the Brazilian government would buy the coffee that its producers had to withhold from the market from next month. Brazil is the world's largest coffee producer, and the move helped underpin the growing credibility of the scheme, which now embraces Latin American, African and Indonesian producers in a joint effort to raise prices. However, no fresh news emerged during the day and profit-takers moved in, sending the price to a close of Dollars 1,264 a tonne, Dollars 32 down on the day. New York failed to build on its gains of Friday, with the December contract at Dollars 81.85 cents a lb, down Dollars 1.30, in late trading. 'The speed with which London turned tail and ran was a bit of a surprise,' said one trader. 'They all tried to take profits at the same time and there was a bit of a scramble.'
