941004 FT 04 OCT 94 / People: Leutwiler joins Warburg Fritz Leutwiler, 70, a former chairman of the Bank for International Settlements and the Swiss National Bank, has been appointed an adviser to S G Warburg, Britain's biggest merchant banking group. Leutwiler, who is a vice chairman of Nestle and chairman of Leutwiler & Partners, is one of two new advisers to Warburg. Professor Helmut Sihler, 64, a former chief executive of Henkel and ex-president of the Federation of the German Chemical Industry, has also been appointed a member of Warburg's growing list of advisers.
