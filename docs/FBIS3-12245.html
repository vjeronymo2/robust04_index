Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Tunis, 27 Feb (MENA)--Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat has sent a written message to President Husni Mubarak on developments in the Palestinian stand after the massacre committed by the Israeli settlers in the al-Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron the day before yesterday. Ambassador Ibrahim 'Allam, Egypt's ambassador in Tunis, stated that 'Arafat asked President Mubarak to keep up the great Egyptian stand which supports and backs the rights of the Palestinian people. He added that 'Arafat affirmed the importance of, and need for this stand at this stage after the massacre committed by the Israeli settlers in the al-Ibrahimi Mosque. He said that 'Arafat also asked for Egypt's support for the PLO during the coming steps to remove the obstacles in the way of the peace talks and put an end to the violence and terrorism the settlers are practicing against the Palestinian people in the occupied territories. The Egyptian ambassador added that 'Arafat's message to Mubarak contained an explanation of the dimensions of the massacre, its effect on the peace process, and the PLO's stand from this massacre.
