931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / US gloom on return of Aristide THE US administration appears resigned to the fact that Mr Jean-Bertrand Aristide, the ousted Haitian president, will not be able to return to his homeland by Saturday, the date set in July's Governor's Island agreement. Ms Dee Dee Myers, the White House press secretary, said yesterday that this was 'less and less likely' given recent developments in Haiti, where pro-Aristide legislators have either gone into hiding or left the country. Earlier in the week President Bill Clinton was more optimistic, though he admitted he had been disappointed before over Haiti. Mr Warren Christopher, secretary of state, said during his visit to Latvia it was more important to 'achieve harmony and tranquillity' in Haiti than to be 'fixed on a date' for Mr Aristide's return. Mr Aristide himself is to give a speech at the United Nations today in which he is expected again to commit himself to policies of national reconciliation. But one of his key advisers in Washington, Mr Michael Barnes, the former congressman from Maryland, was quoted yesterday as saying: 'It would not be possible for him to go back with any sense of security.'
