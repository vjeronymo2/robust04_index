Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Brasilia, 21 Mar -- Venezuelan Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli, who is a candidate for the post of general secretary of the OAS, said today in Brasilia that the OAS, which comprises 35 nations of the continent, could become a great organization. "An inferiority complex has been created regarding the OAS, and it is necessary to overcome it," Burelli said. He challenged the other candidates, Colombian President Cesar Gaviria and Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus, to a television debate -- possibly over the U.S. CNN Channel. Regarding the acceptance of his initiative, the Venezuelan foreign minister told EFE that "the ball is in their court." Burelli is participating in Brasilia in a 48-hour meeting of the foreign ministers and government delegates of the 13 countries of the Rio Group, the main mechanism for political and economic agreement within Latin America. The ambassadors of OAS member countries will elect a successor of the Brazilian diplomat Joao Baena Soares, who has occupied the post of OAS secretary general for two consecutive 5-year periods since 1984. Referring to Gaviria, Burelli said that never -- during the history of the organization -- has the United States ever proposed a candidate on its own initiative. Gaviria's candidacy is supported by several Rio Group countries including Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Peru, and Argentina. Burelli highlighted the OAS system of organization, which has a developed administrative apparatus and a democratic hue because each nation, no matter how large or small, has one vote. The Venezuelan foreign minister -- who was Venezuelan ambassador to Washington, London, and Bogota -- added that he will not campaign during the Rio Group meeting. He said: "I have come to the meeting and that is all." Burelli said that "there are new factors" to evaluate regarding the Cuban situation. "The Cuban people have suffered a lot. There cannot be the same confrontation of ideas now as in bygone days." Baena Soares said he believes it is necessary to consider the reintegration of Cuba into the OAS because "the 1990's are not the 1960's." Burelli complained to EFE about the interpretation by the international media of the Venezuelan situation following the December 1993 elections in which Rafael Caldera became president. "The international press is not convinced that ethical standards are being changed" in Venezuela, he explained. Burelli said President Caldera is willing to "completely reorganize the family" of his country, by offering pardons to all military officers who rose up in arms against the government of then President Carlos Andres Perez. He added that those who found refuge in Peru are now returning, adding that five officers being held the San Carlos Garrison in Caracas will be released "at any moment."
