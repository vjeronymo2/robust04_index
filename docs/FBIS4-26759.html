BFN [Text] Beijing, June 25 (CNS) -- Rain has been scarce in most parts of northern China since early June, and drought is now growing more serious. The average rainfall in the northern area of the Huaihe River is less than 10 millimetres. Shangdong, Henan and Inner Mongolia have seen not a single drop of rain since then. According to the office of the Flood Prevention and Drought Combat Headquarters, due to scarcity of rain and high temperature, water contained in soil has evaporated fast. At present, drought in Shangdong, the northern part of Huaibei, most part of Huabei and Northeast spreads quickly. The size of drought-hit area in northern China is estimated to be 230 million mu of land. Many rivers have dried up, water storage in reservoirs has dwindled. This has seriously affected crops and summer sowing. More than 12 million people and 12 million domestic animals are short of drinking water. [paragraph as published] According to the Central Weather Broadcasting Centre, since early June there has been consecutive days of high temperature in most parts of Huabei, Northeast and Shangdong, the temperature is two to three degrees centigrade higher on average than in normal years. Temperatures in these areas are usually around 30 degrees centigrade, and in certain areas the highest reaching 37 degrees. This further worsens the situation of drought. However, adequate heat is most helpful to the growth of crops in areas where water is plenty. The hot weather is also beneficial to the harvest of winter wheat.
