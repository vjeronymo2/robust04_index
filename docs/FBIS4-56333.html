BFN [Text] Quito, 2 May (EFE) -- The Ecuadoran opposition increased its majority in Congress and dealt a strong blow to the conservative government, which only obtained seven of 65 seats in the legislative elections held on 1 May, according to an unofficial vote count of 89.5 percent of the votes revealed today. The populist right-wing Social Christian Party (PSC), received the most votes, 19 percent, and won 22 seats, according to figures provided by the local media (the Supreme Electoral Tribunal will only give final results by the end of the month). Second place went to the populist Ecuadoran Roldosist Party (PRE), which won 10 seats. Third place went to the Maoist Democratic People's Movement (MPD). The social democrat Democratic Left Party (ID) won six seats and the Christian democrat Popular Democracy Party (DP) won five. President Sixto Duran-Ballen's Republican Unity Party (PUR) only obtained two seats, and Vice President Alberto Dahik's Conservative Party (PCE) obtained five. According to the Quito newspaper EL COMERCIO, there was a high percentage of nullified votes (18 percent).
