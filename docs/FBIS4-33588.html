BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian economic developments monitored through 7 June. The Agriculture and Livestock Marketing Institute has announced that it will buy the entire rice crop of 45,000 tons in Ariari, Meta Department, where the Guillermo Leon Valencia Bridge collapsed, leaving the area cut off from the rest of the nation. It is possible that the government might also purchase the corn crop. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Canal A Network in Spanish 1730 GMT 31 May 94) The National Administrative Department of Statistics [DANE] has reported that inflation reached 1.53 percent in May, adding that for the first five months of the year, overall inflation is running at 13.65 percent. DANE statistics indicate that the May inflation rate was driven higher in part by rent increases. Pasto was the most expensive city, while Monteria was the least expensive in the same month. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Canal A Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 2 Jun 94)
