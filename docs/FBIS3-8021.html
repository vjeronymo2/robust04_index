Language: Slovene Article Type:BFN [Text] Our Foreign Ministry accompanied a decision about Slovenia's participation in the Partnership for Peace with a special statement: In the introduction, the statement mentions a letter that Foreign Minister Lojze Peterle sent to the foreign ministers of its member-states prior to the 5 February NATO summit, a visit by the U.S. President's special envoy Madeleine Albright to Slovenia, and a letter that our Prime Minister Janez Drnovsek sent to [NATO] prime ministers together with a formal application for the participation of our country in the Partnership for Peace. The Foreign Ministry emphasizes that, by being admitted into the Partnership for Peace, Slovenia has accomplished an important step in drawing closer to the Western European integrations, also in the realm of security. Today's decision of the NATO Council in Brussels represents a materialization of the efforts of our state organs in the implementation of the Resolution on Guidelines of the National Security Concept, which was passed by the Slovene Parliament in February. We are supposed to sign a cooperation document in the coming days. After the talks with the NATO political and military experts, our country will get another opportunity to appoint its military and political representatives to NATO Headquarters in Brussels, is written in a statement by the Foreign Ministry.
