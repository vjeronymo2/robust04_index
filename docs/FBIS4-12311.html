BFN [Text] The Delhi ESCAP [Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific] session has finalized an action program for regional cooperation in technology transfer. It has been endorsed by the committee for regional economic cooperation which met yesterday under the chairmanship of the commerce secretary, Mr. Tejinder Khanna. The action program seeks to promote indigenous technological capabilities in the member countries. The private sector is sought to be involved in the implementation. Special attention will be given to the least-developed landlocked and island countries. India has called for greater regional cooperation in the application of space technology, particularly for monitoring the management of environment. The environment secretary, Mr. P. Rajamani, told the ESCAP meeting that the Indian space program is tailored to meet the development needs of the country. He welcomed the move for a ministerial-level conference and offered New Delhi's full cooperation. Today's discussions will (?cover), among other things, poverty alleviation through economic growth and social development.
