930708 FT 08 JUL 93 / Ginsburg defends judiciary Judge Ruth Bader Ginsburg, President Clinton's Supreme Court nominee, has defended the judiciary against charges of usurping the role of politicians in setting policy, writes George Graham. In written answers to a questionnaire before her Senate confirmation hearings begin on July 20, she suggested the courts would not have to take such an active role if legislators had made 'the hard, somewhat controversial decisions necessary to equip judges with clearer policy directions'.
