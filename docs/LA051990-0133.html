May 19, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

LIFE IN THE (NOT SO) FAST LANE

The confluence of the Garden Grove (22), Santa Ana (5) and Orange (57) freeways is the busiest traffic interchange in Orange County, carrying, on average, nearly a half-million cars per day. That's a 50% increase since 1980.

Although it ranks at the bottom of the list of 10 most heavily used interchanges in the county, the notorious El Toro "Y," where the San Diego and Santa Ana freeways meet, has had the largest increase in average daily traffic load during the '80s, 86%. In fact, of the 10 busiest intersections in the county, the San Diego and Santa Ana freeways are most often one of the arteries involved.
