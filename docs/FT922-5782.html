920529 FT 29 MAY 92 / Survey of Coventry (3): Key facts on Coventry --------------------------------------------------------------------- KEY FACTS ON COVENTRY --------------------------------------------------------------------- Local authority: Coventry City Council, tel. (0203) 833.333. Chief executive: Iain Roxburgh. Director of economic development and planning: Harry Noble. See page five of this survey for business contacts and telephone numbers. Population, 1992: 300,844; for the year 2000, the estimated figure is 292,501. Coventry's land area: 9,654 hectares. Age structure: 0-17 years: 71,960; 18 to 54 years, 152,331; 55 years and more: 76,553. Universities: University of Warwick: University of Coventry (from June, 1992 -formerly Coventry Polytechnic). --------------------------------------------------------------------- EMPLOYMENT --------------------------------------------------------------------- Employment by sector, expressed in percentages: metal goods, 28.76; distribution/hotels, 15.17; extraction, 1.92; other manufacturing, 5.21; construction, 3.26; transport/communications, 4.64; banking and business services, 8.33; other services, 32.71. Unemployment: 13.2 (March, 1992). Leading employers include: Jaguar; GEC Plessey Telecommunications; Peugeot Talbot; Rolls-Royce; Courtaulds; Dunlop (Aircraft Tyres Division); Rover Group; Massey-Ferguson Tractors; British Coal; and British Telecommunications. --------------------------------------------------------------------- COMMUNICATIONS --------------------------------------------------------------------- Airports: Birmingham International, 8 miles away; London Heathrow, 98 miles. Rail travel times: Coventry/London - one hour 12 minutes (frequency, twice hourly). Road travel: London, one hour 53 minutes. Manchester, one hour 48 minutes. Glasgow, four hours 45 minutes. Southampton, two hours 34 minutes. Nearest Channel Tunnel freight terminal: proposed at Atherstone (approx. 15 miles away). --------------------------------------------------------------------- PROPERTY --------------------------------------------------------------------- Average house prices: Three-bedroom, semi-detached: Pounds 55,783; four-bedroom, detached: Pounds 123,900. Prime rents: offices - Pounds 10 to Pounds 12 per sq. ft. Industrial premises - Pounds 4 per sq. ft. Retail premises - Pounds 60 to Pounds 70 per sq. ft (Zone A). Grant status: Department of Trade and Industry Regional Selectice Assistance -intermediate; European Regional Development Fund, Objective Two. Science Park: University of Warwick Science Park. New industrial/commercial developments: see map, page two of this survey.
