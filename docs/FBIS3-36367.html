Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report under the "Non-Stop" rubric: "Yeltsin's Visit to Tbilisi May End in Georgia's Occupation"] [Text] Volunteers of the Caucasus People's Confederation [CPC] will immediately come to the rescue of Abkhazia and South Ossetia if the upcoming Russian-Georgian agreement exacerbates the situation there, Ali Aliyev, chairman of the CPC parliament, told your MOSKOVSKIY KOMSOMOLETS correspondent. According to Aliyev, last year formations of the Abkhaz and "confederate" forces were mightily armed thanks to captured Georgian armored vehicles and heavy artillery. Last year, Captain 1st Rank Aliyev said, we had the military and moral potential enough to advance through half of Georgia and to reach Kutaisi. The South Ossetian parliament asked the Russian Federation Council not to ratify the agreement until the Georgian-South Ossetian conflict is resolved.
