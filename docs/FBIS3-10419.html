Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Herold Jean-Francois] [Text] Port-au-Prince, 7 Mar (EFE) -- Port-au-Prince Mayor Evans Paul, who is also leader of the National Front for Change and Democracy (FNCD) today proposed a modification to the plan approved Wednesday [2 March] by the Chamber of Deputies to try to find a solution to the new deadlock in the Haitian crisis created by the [congressional] resolution. Paul recommended "a commitment acceptable for President (Jean- Bertrand) Aristide, as well as for the other sectors involved in the crisis." In an interview for EFE, Paul blamed the international community and domestic sectors that have promoted the current deadlock, for the Haitian crisis. "It is necessary to modify the proposal, which cannot be viewed as a plan yet. In order for it to become a plan, President Aristide and the other sectors must accept it," Paul said. "The international community has not adopted a firm position on the measures contained in the Governor's Island Accord and the ultimatum given to the military leaders by four countries (France, Canada, the United States, and Venezuela) which expired on 15 January," Paul stressed. The FNCD leader said that some sectors support the dilatory tactics that have prolonged the Haitian crisis since 30 September 1991, when a group of military leaders overthrew Constitutional President Aristide. Paul also mentioned the lack of cohesion in the democratic sector, which has favored a "fatal confusion and a rotten situation," adding that the same situation prevails in the parliament. The political leader said that different groups are acting in the Haitian political scene against democracy. "While some people are reacting emotionally and others are trying to maintain the situation of crisis, we must admit that there are others who are acting in good faith," Paul added. With regard to differences expressed within the FNCD and the support for the parliamentarian resolution from the Party of the National Congress of Democratic Movements (CONACOM), Paul said those are normal situations in the various fronts and political coalitions. As an example, he mentioned the Nicaraguan Sandinist National Liberation Front and the Farabundo Marti National Liberation Front in El Salvador. Paul, who said he is confident some legislators will change their position, said that "the FNCD's position has not changed and is based on strengthening democracy and respecting the democratic rules of the game."
