920611 FT 11 JUN 92 / Watchdog gloomy on local authorities A GLOOMY assessment of the immediate future for local government was given yesterday by Mr Howard Davies, outgoing chairman of the Audit Commission, the local authority watchdog, Andrew Adonis writes. Writing in the latest issue of the Association of Metropolitan Authorities News, Mr Davies declares that in the wake of the poll tax debacle 'the fiscal foundations of local government (are) too feeble to carry the weight of service expenditure piled upon them.' 'Increasingly, councils are finding themselves in the position of agents, administering government-designed programmes with relatively little discretion to vary their content to suit local circumstances. That process may well have further to go.'
