Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By staff reporter team: "Shenzhen and Zhuhai Apply for State Council's Approval To Exempt Foreign Visitors From Obtaining Entry Visas"] [Text] Beijing, 13 Mar (WEN WEI PO) -- Shenzhen and Zhuhai have proposed to allow foreigners to enter the territory without entry visas provided that the period of their stay does not exceed 72 hours. The applications are awaiting approval from the State Council. It has been reported that the central authorities intend to make an experiment in Shenzhen. Shenzhen's Mayor Li Youwei and Zhuhai's Mayor Liang Guangda said today that the two cities had submitted applications to the central authorities. Liang Guangda added: It has been learned that relevant central ministries and offices, including the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Office for Port Administration, and Economic and Trade Office [as published] have been to Shenzhen and Zhuhai to conduct on-the-spot study. They are considering making an experiment in Shenzhen first and summing up the experience some time afterward. Answering a reporter, Li Youwei said: Shenzhen is advancing as an international financial city. That is why we made the above proposal and are awaiting instructions from the State Council.
