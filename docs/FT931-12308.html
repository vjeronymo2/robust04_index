930129 FT 29 JAN 93 / International Company News: 'Big Book' printer cuts jobs R. R. DONNELLEY, the world's largest commercial printer, announced that it would take a Dollars 60m restructuring charge in the first quarter as a result of the decision by retailer Sears, Roebuck decision to discontinue its catalogue operations. The Chicago-based printer had produced the Sears 'Big Book' for 80 years. It said the charge would be taken primarily to cover the costs of shutting down the Chicago printing plant that had produced the catalogue. The company said 660 full-time jobs would be cut. Donnelley also reported record earnings for 1992 and the fourth quarter. Fourth-quarter earnings were Dollars 73.1m (47 cents a share), up from Dollars 68.5m (44 cents) a year earlier. For the year, Donnelley reported earnings of Dollars 234.7m (Dollars 1.51), up from Dollars 205m (Dollars 1.32).
