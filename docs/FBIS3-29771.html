Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Parliamentary vice ministers of the government met on the morning of 17 February at the prime minister's official residence. During the meeting, Itsusei Koga, parliamentary vice minister of the Economic Planning Agency, referred to the drastic appreciation of the yen against the U.S. dollar following the breakdown of the Japanese-U.S. framework talks and said, "The government needs to formulate clearer plans to expand domestic demand." During the meeting, one parliamentary vice minister after another also voiced the need for the government to quickly take steps to deal with the steep rise in the yen's value against the U.S. dollar. Commenting on the impact of the recent sharp rise in the value of the yen against the U.S. dollar, Eisei Ito, parliamentary vice minister of the Construction Ministry, said, "I have no intention of raising questions about the outcome of the recent Japanese-U.S. summit talks, but if the government fails to deal properly with the drastic appreciation of the yen, the package of stimulus measures compiled recently by the government would have little effect in stimulating the economy." At the meeting, Toshi Masuda, parliamentary vice minister of the National Land Agency, stated: "The government should make public its plans to deal with the recent steep rise in the yen's value in a way so that other nations can easily understand them. All government officials should realize the need to reform the structure of Japan's economy." In this way, he called for a change in economic policy in order to convert Japan's economy into an economy driven by domestic demand. In connection with the issue of opening the Japanese mobile-phone market, the U.S. Government is poised to slap sanctions on Japan after confirming "Japan's violation of a Japanese-U.S. agreement." In commenting on the move by the U.S. Government, Aogu Matsumae, parliamentary vice minister of the Posts and Telecommunications Ministry, criticized the U.S. Government for taking such a step, saying, "The assertion by the U.S. Government is unreasonable, and the Japanese Government cannot go along with the request from the U.S. Government."
