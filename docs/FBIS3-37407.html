Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Delegations from the Ukrainian and Kazakhstani Defense Ministries have concluded negotiations they held in Almaty by signing a joint communique. The delegations were led by Ukrainian Deputy Defense Minister Ivan Oliynyk and Alibek Kasimov, the Kazakhstani Armed Forces chief of staff. The NOVYNY television and radio agency learned that the Ukrainian side said it was ready for technical and scientific cooperation in developing new types of equipment and in modernizing the already existing one. Mutual assistance between Kazakhstan and Ukraine in this sphere will enable this work to be carried out as efficiently as possible. The communique deemed promising the cooperation of both countries in developing their rocket and space capabilities. The Kazakhstani delegation expressed its interest in a more intensive utilization of Ukraine's repair facilities for the benefit of the Kazakhstani Armed Forces. A mechanism for settling mutual accounts will soon be determined, including the quick liquidation of debts. Both sides noted the vast opportunities for cooperation between the Kazakhstani and Ukrainian Defense Ministries in the sphere of logistical support, military training, and other military aspects. These kinds of bilateral consultations are in compliance with international commitments and do not encroach on the interests of third countries. During the negotiations, an agreement was reached to hasten work by Kazakhstan and Ukraine intended to finalize a military cooperation treaty.
