Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Rangoon, March 26 (AFP) -- Burma's best-known comedian, Zargana, has been released after four years in prison as a political dissident, neighbors of his family said Saturday. Zargana, 33, who trained as a dentist but turned to comedy, gained prominence for his satirical jokes aimed at the former socialist regime. First arrested briefly as a political dissident in late 1988, he went to jail for the second time in 1990 while helping his mother in her campaign for the May general elections that year. Neighbors who spoke to Zargana on Friday said he had been delivered to his parents' home by military intelligence personnel just before noon. He was the latest political prisoner released by the military junta, known as the State Law and Order Restoration Council (SLORC), which has set free more than 2,000 dissidents in the past two years.
