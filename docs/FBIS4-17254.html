BFN [Text] Tallinn, 21 Apr, BNS -- Estonian Foreign Minister Juri Luik met today with Daniel Tarschys, the head of the Swedish parliament's foreign commission and the new secretary general of the European Union [EU], the Foreign Ministry press service told BNS. Tarschys is in Tallinn to attend a meeting of parliamentary foreign commissions of the Baltic states, the Visegrad states and the Nordic countries. Tarschys affirmed that the European Union will in the next few years attach top priority to strengthening the young democracies of the central and east European countries. The EU secretary general said expansion of the union is also of great importance. In this connection he voiced the opinion that a democratic Russia as a member of the EU would be a positive development and that just for this reason Russia's admission to the union should be conditional upon its fulfilling the same requirements as other states. Luik and Tarschys also touched on Estonian-Russian relations and the problems connected with the withdrawal of Russian troops from Estonia.
