921010 FT 10 OCT 92 / Finance & The Family: Mulcahy's Pounds 1m - Directors' transactions ONE TRANSACTION stands out above all others this week. Geoffrey Mulcahy, the chairman and chief executive of the stores group Kingfisher, exercised an option of 374,475 shares and sold the resulting holding. In the process, he made a gross profit of just over Pounds 1m by taking advantage of a sharp rise in the share price following sterling's devaluation. Three directors in CRT Group, formerly Smallshaw Knitwear, have been selling stock just below a pound. Now quoted under business services and operating in consultancy and recruitment services, the company's earnings record has proved more consistent in recent times. All three of the directors selling shares retain close to 500,000 each. Evidence of further support for the media group WPP will cheer those who see the stock as a classic recovery play. Martin Sorrell, the chief executive, has bought a further 100,000 shares at 34.5p, having purchased 100,000 at 39p in mid-August.
