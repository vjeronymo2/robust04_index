BFN [Text] Uruguay's Foreign Minister Sergio Abreu Bonilla has said Uruguay does not support delaying the implementation of Mercosur [Common Market of the South]. He added that we must respect all legal instruments, such as the Asuncion Treaty, which established 1 January 1995 as the Mercosur launch date. Abreu arrived in Asuncion this morning. He was received by his Paraguayan counterpart Luis Maria Ramirez Boettner. Talking about his 3-day official visit, Abreu said he will discuss the common foreign tariff and other bilateral and regional issues with Paraguayan officials. He will also invite President Juan Carlos Wasmosy to the inauguration of the Martin Garcia Channel dredging, in Montevideo, on 10 June. Argentine President Carlos Menem is also scheduled to attend. Tomorrow, Foreign Minister Abreu will sign various agreements and a joint declaration at the Foreign Ministry. At 1600 this afternoon, the Uruguayan official will meet with President Wasmosy at the Government Palace. He will then continue his working meeting with Foreign Minister Ramirez Boettner. On the morning of 25 May, Abreu will donate a computer to the Artigas School. At 1030, he will give a lecture at the Paraguayan Industrial Union (UIP) on the topic: "The Uruguay Round; The Results and Its Relation Within the Global Picture." Abreu will return to Uruguay at noon on 26 May.
