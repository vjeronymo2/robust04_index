Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] New York, Mar. 10 (A.A.) -- Turkey informed the United Nations that it was ready to send 1,000 soldiers to the UN Forces in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Turkey's permanent representative at the United Nations Inal Batu conveyed Turkey's proposal during a meeting in New York, of representatives of countries which are expected to send troops to the region. Speaking at the second session of the meeting on Wednesday [9 March], Ambassador Batu said that Turkey was ready to take part in the peace keeping forces by volunteering a batallion. After the meeting Batu told the Anatolian News Agency that the proposal involving 1,000 soldiers constituted one-fourth of the total number of soldiers proposed by the other countries.
