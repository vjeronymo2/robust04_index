Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Francois Mitterrand, Prime Minister Edouard Balladur, Foreign Minister Alain Juppe, and Defense Minister Francois Leotard gathered this morning at the Elysee to discuss the situation in Algeria, where two French nationals were killed last night. Shortly afterward, the French Foreign Ministry advised those French nationals whose presence is not vital in Algeria to leave the country. Foreign Ministry spokesman Richard Duque spoke to Frederic Barrere: [Begin Duque recording] France condemns the cowardly killing of two of its nationals in Algeria--Mr. Roger Drouaier and his son Pascal. It denounces this act of barbarity, which marks a new phase in violence, thus threatening the presence of foreigners on Algerian territory. In this context, it is advisable for our nationals whose presence is not vital to take the necessary steps to return to France. [end recording]
