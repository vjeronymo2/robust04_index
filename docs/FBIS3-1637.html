Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hangzhou, February 26 (XINHUA) -- Hangzhou, the capital of east China's Zhejiang Province, has over the past few years made great progress in upgrading its new and high technology development zone. Official sources said that 134 million yuan in the construction of basic facilities, involving a floor space of some 200,000 sq m, was completed last year in this development zone. With a total area of some 12 sq km, the zone, which was approved three years ago, is Zhejiang's only state-level development zone of the kind. To date, local governments, colleges, scientific institutes and major companies all over the country as well as some Hong Kong and Taiwan businesses have set up more than 305 firms in the zone. In addition, some 46 foreign-funded firms have been established there over the past three years, involving a total foreign investment of more than 80 million U.S. dollars. According to sources from the management committee of the zone, great emphasis has been put upon the development of electronic information, new materials, new energy and biotechnologies as well as technologies to improve efficiency and save energy. At the same time, such advanced new and high technologies as program-controlled switchboards, membron materials and silicon single crystals have been introduced and applied to production. The latest statistics show that some 402 new and high technologies are currently being applied to production in the zone and will soon be spread throughout the country. Over the past few years, relevant departments have made great efforts to improve the infrastructure of the zone in support of its development, officials from the management committee said.
