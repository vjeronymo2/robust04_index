931208 FT 08 DEC 93 / Equality chief to quit a year early Ms Valerie Amos, chief executive of the Equal Opportunity Commission, is leaving her job a year before the end of her contract to pursue other interests. Ms Amos, who came to the job four years ago from local government, succeeded in putting the commission on a more structured footing with stronger management and making it more accessible to grassroots organisations and ethnic minorities. She is understood, however, to have some difficulty developing a strong personal relationship with Ms Kamlesh Bahl who took over the commission's chair in June. Ms Amos resigned the post almost a month ago. No announcement was made, although a short briefing was prepared in the event of possible press inquiries. Ms Bahl, who worked as a lawyer in industry before tasking up her appointment, said in the briefing that Ms Amos had worked with 'great dedication and loyalty to bring about many changes for the EOC'. Ms Amos was yesterday anxious to play down suggestions that a personality clash had been a key factor in her decision to leave. 'That kind of thing is always said when there is a new chair. Kamlesh and I have worked very well together. I wouldn't describe our working relationship as a difficult one.'
