October 26, 1990, Friday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: SPIELBERG RUMORED TRYING TO SALVAGE 'BONFIRE' FILM

"CONSEQUENCES, shmonse-quences, as long as I'm rich," said Daffy Duck.

HOLLYWOOD IS roiling with rumors about Warner's movie "The Bonfire of the Vanities." It was bought and filmed to great hoopla from the famous Tom Wolfe book of the same name.

Now, those who've seen first cuts of the Bruce Willis-Tom Hanks starrer say either that it is "brilliant" or "weird." Purists who appreciated the novel will be in an uproar of dismay. (Well, hell, we already knew that.) And the final rumor is that Steven Spielberg has been called in to re-edit it. The film was directed by Brian De Palma.

STEVE FORBES, now the head of the family clan, is settled into Daddy Malcolm's mantle. He and his brothers will host a big one this Sunday aboard the Forbes yacht Highlander to honor former President and Mrs. Ronald Reagan. The focus of the occasion is again the building of the Reagan Library.

The last time the Forbes boys tried to give this party, Reagan was having brain surgery and could not attend. He and Mrs. Reagan will be there in person this time.

RAN INTO the adorable Blair Brown the other day at NBC where she was getting ready to brave being on the Letterman show. Blair is both glad and rueful that her series "The Days and Nights of Molly Dodd" is winding down. The final 13 episodes will be seen beginning in January on the Lifetime Channel. What Blair liked best about doing "Molly Dodd" was that the work kept her in New York near her 8-year-old son.

What I can report about Blair, an uncommonly intelligent and sensitive actress, is that her romance with publisher-tycoon Mort Zuckerman has come to an end along with "Molly Dodd." Now they'll just be friends.

LET'S GET serious! William Safire wrote of the budget mess and Congress vs. the President and vice versa: "The current impasse is no cause for national embarrassment; on the contrary, it is the overdue surfacing of basic political difference."

Well, OK, Bill -- though I'm not sure the loss of world confidence in the dollar and the economic blow to U.S. prestige is "no cause for national embarrassment." I do, however, want to quote a thoughtful and major financial brain who prefers to remain nameless. He said to me recently:

"Give George Bush more credit and a little time. The so-called budget mess had to happen. The U.S. economic picture has to grow this dark, this dim, this threatening. It's the only way, after all the selfish profligate bingeing and excesses of the '80s. Think of it all as slamming the brakes on at 80 m.p.h. Unpleasant, but necessary.

"Only really dramatic events could bring us up short to convince us that the Ronald Reagan free ride is over. It is indeed darkest before dawn. Now we can get serious and start recovering; now we can get real about taxing and cutting spending by the government."

THE ODDEST and most endearing book in your bookstores is William Morrow's "Shirley and Marty," the story of actress Shirley Jones and her wacky hubby, Marty Ingels. (He is a onetime actor, comic and now an entrepreneur of celebrity endorsement.) These two have quite an opposites-attract tale to tell. I loved it.
