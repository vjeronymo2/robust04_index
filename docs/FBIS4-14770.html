BFN [By ITAR-TASS] [Text] Moscow, 2 Apr -- The press service of the Russian Government has today made public a statement by the government commission on the progress of the investigation into the circumstances of the crash of a A-310 plane near the city of Novosibirsk. It says: "The government commission chaired by the Russian minister of transport continues investigating the accident. Experts from France and the United States have been invited to take part in the investigation as advisers. "The debris of the plane has been evacuated from the site of the accident and is now being analyzed. Its black boxes have been sent to Paris where their decoding is being completed. According to preliminary reports, no information has been obtained suggesting that there were any defects in the aircraft, its engine, or equipment, but a final conclusion will be drawn when all analyses are completed. When the cockpit voice recorder was replayed, it was found that unauthorized persons had been present in the cockpit during the flight. Experts have started identifying the voices and analyzing the crew's behavior during the flight and while the accident situation was developing. "The government commission is taking the necessary measures to help the families of those who died, organize the funerals, and provide compensation for the damage caused."
