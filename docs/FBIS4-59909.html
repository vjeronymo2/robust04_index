BFN [Text] Peshawar, May 19, IRNA -- The Afghan Coordination Council led by Prime Minister Golboddin Hekmatyar Wednesday [18 May] announced that all roads leading to Kabul will be open for three days to allow transportation to Kabul during Eid-ul-Azha ['Id al-Adha -- feast of immolation] Muslim festivity, May 12. In a statement here, the council said that governmental as well as non-governmental organizations could carry all sorts of relief supplies to Kabul but with the guarantee that it would not be served for military purposes and would be distributed among the citizens of the city. Kabul has been scene of the longest round of fightings since early January when allied forces of Hekmatyar and 'Abdol Rashid Dostum clashed with forces of President Borhanoddin Rabbani. According to Afghan sources, the rival Afghan factions are presently locked in severe fighting in two provinces, Baghlan and Konduz, to capture strategic positions. Meanwhile, armed personnel of Hekmatyar and Sayyaf have been fighting since Tuesday in Maydan Shahr in Wardak Province.
