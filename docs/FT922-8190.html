920514 FT 14 MAY 92 / International Company News: Moody's places Bell Canada bonds under review MOODY'S Investors Service in New York has put Bell Canada's long-term debt under review because of the possibility that the federal government may approve competition in the domestic long distance market. The regulatory decision is expected in Ottawa in mid-June although it could possibly be delayed until the autumn. Bell Canada, part of the BCE group, is the country's biggest telecommunications company and has an effective monopoly on the domestic market. Bell Canada has CDollars 5bn in long-term debt outstanding, including first mortgage bonds and unsecured debentures. The issues are rated double A-1 and double A-2, or the highest possible level for a corporation.
