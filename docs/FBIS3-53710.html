Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Telephone report by correspondent Darestani from Islamabad] [Text] After meeting with the conflicting sides in Afghanistan, the Islamic Republic's special envoy to Afghanistan has returned to Peshawar, Pakistan. During his three-day visit to Kabul, Taherian conveyed a verbal message from President Hashemi-Rafsanjani to Afghan President Borhanoddin Rabbani and handed a written message from Foreign Minister Velayati to Afghan Prime Minister Golboddin Hekmatyar. He also held talks with Mazari, secretary general of the Hezb-e Wahdat [Islamic Unity Party] and the high-ranking representative of Afghanistan's national-Islamic movement in Kabul. In an interview this morning, he stated that the Islamic Republic of Iran is doing its best to bring together the heads of the conflicting sides in order to achieve understanding and that it is giving attention to consultations with our neighbors in this. The Islamic Republic's special envoy to Afghanistan added: [Begin Taherian recording] Following the contacts, the conflicting sides agreed to name representatives, who will start meeting as soon as possible. But it may be too soon yet to make any assessment of this. We intend, God willing, to continue our efforts aimed at achieving results and we hope that, God willing, we will achieve something specific. [end recording] Taherian said in conclusion that in continuation of his mission he will start meetings with Pakistani officials today so that views can be exchanged between officials of the two countries on how to end the battles in Afghanistan and to promote understanding among the conflicting sides.
