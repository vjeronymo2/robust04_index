June 2, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

EMI MUSIC'S NET PROFIT MORE THAN DOUBLES IN YEAR

Aided by booming sales at its record labels, EMI Music's profit more than doubled from $73.5 million to a record $150.2 million for the year ended March 31.

The London-based music concern, a unit of the British electronics and music conglomerate Thorn EMI PLC, said sales rose from $1.3 billion to a record $1.7 billion.

The better-than-expected results came amid an overall slowing in music industry sales in the United States last year.

But EMI, which formed a joint venture with SBK last year and paid $79.1 million for a 50% interest in Chrysalis, has enjoyed surprising success domestically with Sinead O'Connor, whose Chrysalis album went to No. 1 in 16 countries, and with M. C. Hammer, a rap artist with EMI's Los Angeles-based Capitol Records label. Hammer's "Please Don't Hurt 'Em" album ranks No. 2 on the Billboard list of top 100 albums.

Besides O'Connor and Hammer, EMI's roster of artists includes Bonnie Raitt, Freddie Jackson, Richard Marx, Paul McCartney and Tina Turner. The company now claims a 12% share of the worldwide music market. JUBE SHIVER
