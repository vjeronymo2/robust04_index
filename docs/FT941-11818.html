940202 FT 02 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Cedardata for market with valuation of Pounds 30m Cedardata, a Surrey-based accounting and financial management software group, is coming to market next month through a share placing which is likely to value thecompany at about Pounds 30m. The company, founded in 1983 by Mr Leon Fattal, as a computer bureau services company specialising in payroll and accounts processing, is best known for for its modular Cfacs (Commercial and Financial Accounts Systems) software, which was launched in 1987 and now has a strong blue-chip customer base. The core of the Cfacs software package, which has been developed and based on Oracle's relational database products, is the General Ledger. To this clients can add various other modules to meet their specific accounting requirements. Some 25 modules are currently available. Since the launch of Cfacs turnover and profits have grown rapidly. In the year to March 31, pre-tax profits were Pounds 2.4m on turnover of Pounds 5.3m. In the current year the company expects to report profits of between Pounds 2.75m and Pounds 3m on turnover of about Pounds 6.05m. As part of the placing the company plans to issue new shares to raise an additional Pounds 1m after expenses which will be used to fund the future development of Cfacs, working capital and possible acquisitions. The vendors, who include Mr Fattal, managing director with a holding of 57.6 per cent, and MrHarry Menasche, sales director with a 10.1 per cent stake, plan to retain about50 per cent of the enlarged capital after the placing. The listing particulars will be published on February 18 and the shares are expected to begin trading on February 24. The placing is sponsored by Greig Middleton.
