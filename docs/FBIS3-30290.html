Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Today Romanian President Ion Iliescu received a military delegation of the Republic of Bulgaria led by Bulgarian Minister of Defense Valentin Aleksandrov. After the meeting, the Bulgarian guest expressed his satisfaction with the talks held with the head of the Romanian state. At the end of the visit of the Bulgarian military delegation, Mr. Valentin Aleksandrov held a news conference that was attended also by Lieutenant General Niculae Spiroiu, Romania's minister of defense, and by members of the leadership of the Romanian National Defense Ministry. The journalists were presented the content of the accord of cooperation between the two armies signed this morning by the defense ministers of Romania and of Bulgaria. The document provides for cooperation in the area of training personnel, instructing troops, control in the area of disarmament, in the technological, economic, and medical areas and in social and sports activities. Minister Valentin Aleksandrov discussed the importance of this document saying that it is a new step toward strengthening relations between the two armies and it will make a contribution to increasing confidence between Romania and Bulgaria. Other issues approached during this news conference were connected with ensurance of security in the Balkan region and in the whole of Europe, as well as ways to approach the issue of war in the former Yugoslavia. The sides noted that the two states have common viewpoints on ways to find solutions to this conflict.
