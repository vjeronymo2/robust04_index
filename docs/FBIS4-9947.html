BFN [Text] According to the newspaper GRANMA, deputies have begun their analysis in preparation for the next session of the National Assembly of the People's Government [ANPP]. In Granma, ANPP President Ricardo Alarcon chaired the country's first meeting with deputies to analyze the results of Parliaments in the Workplaces and to note some of the ideas that could be used in preparation for the ANPP special session scheduled for 1 May. Preliminary reports submitted by Deputy Ernesto Suarez, who is also an ANPP secretary, state that 3 million workers participated in the parliaments, by which the workers demonstrated their patriotism, honor, and willingness to do whatever might be necessary to save the Revolution. For that meeting, the ANPP president and seven permanent ANPP working committees visited Granma for the rendering of the province's accounts in preparation for the next special period of sessions of our parliament. Rafael Dominguez Fernandez, president of the provincial government, released a report on the socioeconomic characteristics of Granma, its work objectives, and a rendering of its accounts. Also present were Deputy Jaime Crombet, who is a member of the Cuban Communist Party [PCC] Central Committee and vice president of the ANPP; and Alfredo Enriquez Pina, PCC first secretary.
