BFN [From "News Bulletin" No. 476] [Text] Vilnius, April 12, ELTA -- Chairman of social democratic party Aloyzas Sakalas and Deputy Chairman Vytenis Andriukaitis told a news conference on Tuesday [12 April] about the non-confidence to government which had been initiated by social democrats. As it is already known, last week the social democrat faction started gathering signatures of the members of Seimas [parliament] in order to make a statement. Social democrats need signatures of 29 parliamentarians to begin the consideration of the non-confidence vote for Slezevicius government. It was mentioned at the news conference that social democrtas had 20 signatures. Their activities were approved by National Union, democrats, centrists and some independent members of Seimas. Social democrats did not receive the support from conservatives, political prisoners and deportees and christian democrats. Social democrats said at the news conference that non-confidence vote is to be given for the whole governemnt for the first time in Lithuania. They publicized a document of 40 pages with the analysis of the government activities, which they called the substantiation of the non-confidence. According social democrats, the governemnt should be changed because it had not carried out its obligations and duties. The government was also accused of poor state property and other reforms.
