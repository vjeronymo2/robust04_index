Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Salvadoran elections, scheduled for 20 March 1994. Some 7,518 voting tables will be set up in the country for the scheduled 20 March elections. Electoral official Marco Antonio Castro stated that authorities are currently working to prepare the material that the Election Boards will use in 262 municipalities. Authorities will stage a preliminary election before 20 March to identify problems that could arise on election day and adopt the necessary measures to prevent them. (San Salvador Radio Cadena YSU in Spanish 1800 GMT 10 Feb 94) Leaders of political parties on 10 February signed a new pact of honor at the regional office of the Human Rights Division of the UN Observers for El Salvador, Onusal. The leaders of political parties in San Salvador "pledged to carry out a decent campaign by avoiding verbal attacks and violence in the days preceding the elections." (San Salvador Canal Doce Television in Spanish 0300 GMT 11 Feb 94) The Salvadoran Catholic Church on 13 February criticized the nine presidential candidates because voters do not know their programs for resolving the country's problems. In his Sunday's homily, Monsignor Gregorio Rosa Chavez lamented that "only five weeks before the elections, the proposals of the different parties remain vague and the enthusiasm of voters limited." He stated that the violence in the country is "very dangerous," adding that parties should present their programmatic proposals to voters. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1956 GMT 13 Feb 94) During the last 10 months the Supreme Electoral Tribunal, TSE, has issued 516,241 voting cards throughout the country, it has been officially reported. TSE Magistrate Jaime Romero Ventura said that the people are turning out massively to request their voting cards. Romero explained that "the card issuing process has increased considerably in the last few days thanks to the mega operations" carried out by the TSE. The official concluded by saying that the last day for issuing voting cards is 12 March. (San Salvador LA PRENSA GRAFICA in Spanish 16 Feb 94 p 14) Fidel Chavez Mena, Christian Democratic Party, PDC, presidential candidate, has called for the freezing of prices of the basic market basket for six months so as to improve the purchasing power of the population. Chavez Mena also offered to create 20,000 jobs every month if he wins the presidency. He said: "We offer to create 20,000 jobs every month, in both rural and urban areas." Chavez Mena concluded that he reached agreements with productive sectors to increase jobs. (San Salvador Canal Doce Television in Spanish 0300 GMT 11 Feb 94) Armando Calderon Sol, Nationalist Republican Alliance, Arena, presidential candidate, has termed Chavez Mena's proposal to create 20,000 jobs monthly "ridiculous." He recalled that when Chavez Mena held the post of planning minister the economy failed to register growth. Calderon Sol stated: "The Salvadoran people are well aware of the fact that poverty increased when Chavez Mena served as planning minister." The progovernment presidential candidate noted the poor performance of Chavez Mena at the head of the Planning Ministry. (San Salvador Canal Doce Television in Spanish 0300 GMT 11 Feb 94)
