July 6, 1990, Friday, San Diego County Edition

SAN DIEGO SPORTS ET CETERA: CAL ASSISTANT HIRED TO COACH AZTEC WOMEN'S SOCCER TEAM

Lesle Gallimore has been named women's soccer coach at San Diego State, Athletic Director Fred Miller announced Thursday.

Gallimore, 26, will take over for Chuck Clegg, who will concentrate on the men's team.

Gallimore, a former four-time All-American soccer player at Cal, has been an assistant soccer coach at that school for the past four years. Last year, Berkeley was ranked 12th nationally and finished the season 14-3-1.

Still an active club player, Gallimore is participating this week in the U.S. Olympic Festival in Minneapolis as a member of the West women's team. She also was on West teams in 1987 and 1989, winning gold and silver medals.

VOLLEYBALL

San Diego's Seaside Volleyball Club lost to Torrance, 10-15, 15-9, 15-9, in the final of the U.S. Volleyball Assn. Junior Olympics in Albuquerque.

Ala Mikaele of Lincoln High, Bruce Williams of San Pasqual and Erik Sullivan of San Dieguito, all from Seaside, were named first-team All-American.

Also in Albuquerque, the San Diego Volleyball Club Red team defeated ASICS Munciana (Minneapolis), 15-11, 10-15, 15-9, to finish fourth in the girls' 16-and-under division.

Brett Hensel and Kami Schmedding, both of Torrey Pines, were named second-team All-American.

TENNIS

No. 5 Willie Carter of Vista defeated top-seeded Mike Paradoski of Lakeside, 6-3, 6-3, to advance to today's boys' 18s final in the 74th La Jolla Tennis Championships at the La Jolla Beach and Tennis Club. Carter will play No. 2 Jimmy Brown of La Jolla, who defeated Chris Emery of Irvine in a semifinal Wednesday.
