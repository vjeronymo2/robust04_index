920409 FT 09 APR 92 / International Capital Markets: Power offer oversubscribed MALAYSIA'S public offer of 240.12m shares of Tenaga Nasional, the state-owned electricity monopoly, at MDollars 4.50 each was oversubscribed 3.47 times, Reuter reports from Kuala Lumpur. The flotation, which is part of Malaysia's privatisation programme, is the country's largest ever. It comprised 685.05m shares of which 240.12m were made available for public subscription, while the remainder were placed with institutions. Mr Ani Arope, Tenaga chairman, said: 'The response is overwhelming considering the size of the issue.' Another 60m shares were offered to locals and foreigners under a tender system. These were oversubscribed 6.47 times with the highest bid at MDollars 7.10 per share. A further 84.93m were offered to Tenaga employees while 300m were alloted to approved Bumiputra investors.
