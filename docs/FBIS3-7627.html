Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, March 30 (KCNA) -- Twenty odd years have passed since the Kim Il-song prize was instituted in the DPRK. This prize, the highest in the country, was established in March, 1972, on the occasion of the 60th birthday of the great leader President Kim Il-song. The prize is awarded to those who have made distinguished contributions to the development of science, education, culture and art, public health, sports and the press of the country. It is also awarded to works. A large number of Kim Il-song prize winners and works laureate have been produced since the establishment of the prize. In April of the year when the prize was instituted, it was awarded to 35 people. Among them were Kim Sang-yon, an agro-scientist who greatly helped develop agricultural science and increase grain production by finding new varieties of rice, Chon Se-pong, a writer who wrote novels and many other good literary works and sportswoman Han Pil-hwa who contributed to the development of sports. Some 20 people including historian Chae Hui-kuk, head of a chair of Kim Il-song University, No Ik-hwa, a sculptor of the Mansudae Art Studio, Yun Tong-hyang, a writer of the Korean literary production, became the Kim Il-song prize winners in recent years. Yi Chong-o, a composer of the Pochonbo electronic band, was awarded the prize for having composed many songs of high ideological and artistic value. A music and dance epic "Song of Glory" became the Kim Il-song prize work laureate for giving a vivid portrayal of the glorious and brilliant history of the great leader's revolutionary activities, his undying revolutionary feats and the happiness the Korean people are enjoying under the wise leadership of the Workers' Party of Korea.
