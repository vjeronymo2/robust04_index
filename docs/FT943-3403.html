940914 FT 14 SEP 94 / UK Company News: Clyde Petrol back to black with Pounds 2.2m Clyde Petroleum, the UK exploration and production company, has celebrated a return to the black with its first dividend payment in three years. The independent oil group announced interim net profits of Pounds 2.2m, against losses of Pounds 16.2m, and a first half payout of 0.35p. The performance was achieved in spite of a 19 per cent drop in the average oil price. Turnover rose by 25 per cent to Pounds 49.1m. Mr Malcolm Gourlay, chairman, said the group had tried to pitch the dividend at a sustainable level. Clyde expects to pay a further 0.65p at the end of the year, for a 1p total. The company increased production by 46 per cent to 30,843 barrels of oil equivalent per day. About 80 per cent of Clyde's reserves are producing. Gearing was expected to be about 100 per cent at the year-end. First half earnings were 0.6p, against a loss of 4.6p.
