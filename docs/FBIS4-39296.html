BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Leonid Kotov] [Text] New Delhi June 3 TASS -- Russian Deputy Prime Minister Yuriy Yarov arrived here today at the head of a delegation. His visit is aimed at preparing documents to be signed during Indian Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao's official visit to Russia towards the end of June, and activization the work of the joint Russian- Indian trade, economic, scientific and technical cooperation commission. In his capacity as chairman of the Russian part of the commission, Yarov will hold working meetings with its co-chairman from the Indian side, Commerce Minister Pranab Mukherjee and talks with other members of the government of India. "India is among those few states, relations with which have never undergone any cardinal changes and are not subject to any reassessment as a result of serious political and economic changes that took place in our country," Yuriy Yarov said in an interview with ITAR-TASS after his arrival in Delhi. "India has always been given top priority in Russia's foreign policy, which was emphasised by President Boris Yeltsin in his address to members of Indian Parliament during his visit to Delhi," he emphasised. Yuriy Yarov had a working meeting with Indian Commerce Minister Pranab Mukherjee in the Hyderabad House in Delhi today. In his opening speech, the Indian commerce minister emphasised that Russian President Boris Yeltsin's visit to India in January, 1993 was an important stage in the history of Russian-Indian relations. The visit helped resolve many important questions, including the payment of India's debt to Russia, and helped determine the further path of our cooperation in economic, trade and scientific spheres. Mukherjee expressed the hope that new avenues of cooperation between Russia and India will be opened in trade, economic and scientific spheres during Indian Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao's forthcoming visit to Moscow, and as a result of his talks with Russian leaders. The chairman of the joint Russian-Indian commission, in particular, highly appreciated fruitful and mutually beneficial cooperation between Russia and India which is now embracing all spheres of economic activities. But due to a number of reasons the trade between Russia and India slumped, leading to a significant fall in turnover over the past few years, he said. Mukherjee expressed confidence that Russian-Indian trade will soon be doubled from its current level of 25 billion rupees, and cooperation between the two countries will considerably increase thanks to economic reforms being carried out in Russia and India. The Indian minister recalled that an important role has to be played by representativies of Russian and Indian private sectors. Their activities will not only help considerably increase exports from India to Russia to clear India's debt to Russia, but will also help set up joint enterprises for selling goods to third countries. The two co-chairmen discussed specific questions about expanding trade, activizing economic cooperation and using new forms of cooperation between business circles of the two countries. Both sides paid special attention to resolve the problem of avoiding double taxation in their bilateral trade, the question of increasing supplies of goods from India to clear its debt to Russia, the introduction of concessional import duty on Indian goods and setting up a Russian-Indian bank. Today Yuriy Yarov also held talks with Indian Finance Minister Manmohan Singh and Minister of State for Steel Santosh Mohan Dev. The Russian delegation will fly to Bangalore on Saturday to acquaint itself with the work of Indian aeronautics and space industry enterprises. Their programme also includes a visit to Bombay, the "trade capital of India", where Yuriy Yarov and officials accompanying him will see shipyards, and also be guests of scientists of the Bhabha atomic research centre.
