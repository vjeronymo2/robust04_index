August 11, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: MEDICAL BUILDING HIT BY BOMB BLAST

A medical building housing the office of a white supremacist organization in Glendale was hit early Thursday by a bomb explosion that shattered windows and caused about $5,000 damage, fire officials said.

There were no reported injuries.

The two-story structure in the 1200 block of South Glendale Avenue was shaken by the blast of a medium-strength bomb at 3:30 a.m., according to Capt. John Orr of the Glendale Fire Department's arson-explosive unit.

Orr said that George King, a member of the League of Pace Amendment Advocates, favoring deportation of all people who are not white Christians, told authorities that he believes the bomb was directed at his organization.

The case has been turned over to the FBI because it may have been politically motivated, Orr said.
