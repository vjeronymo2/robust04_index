941005 FT 05 OCT 94 / The Labour Party in Blackpool: Unions hold back on green vote Leaders of two of the biggest trade unions bolstered the show of unity by holding back from an all-out effort to defeat environment policy objectives they fear could threaten thousands of jobs. The TGWU transport union and the electrical section of the AEEU did not insist on a card vote when a statement designed to establish the party's 'green' credentials ahead of the next general election was carried on a show of hands. The statement envisages substantial cuts in the Pounds 20bn road-building programme, new restrictions on open-cast mining, and ending the expansion of the nuclear power industry. The anxieties of the unions surfaced during the debate and afterwards when Mr Bill Morris, leader of the TGWU, revealed that he had written to Mr Smith expressing concern about the employment implications of the policy statement. Mr Paul Gallagher of the electricians condemned attacks on nuclear power. 'The Tories have destroyed the coal industry to satisfy political dogma. Labour must not make the same mistake,' he said.
