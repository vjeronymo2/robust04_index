BFN [Excerpts] Dear listeners, we now present a report on the latest incidents in the Nagorno-Karabakh Republic [NKR] and the border between Armenia and Azerbaijan. [passage omitted] Although Azerbaijan signed the agreement for a cease-fire, it has violated the agreement several times. The NKR Defense Army Headquarters Press Service has informed us that fighting continued yesterday in Agdamskiy and Mardakertskiy Rayons. Azerbaijan's military forces tried to penetrate the NKR defense positions to reach Yusifdzhanly in Agdamskiy Rayon; however, the defense units counterattacked. The enemy forces lost several men and were forced to retreat to their former positions. The NKR military subunits have succeeded in obstructing several attacks in the northern and northeastern part of Mardakertskiy Rayon. The Azerbaijan Military Command Headquarters is trying to amass several units in those areas at the present time. Azerbaijan's military units fired on Vartashen in Martuninskiy Rayon with Grad rocket launchers the night before last night. Initial reports said two women were killed and 10 residents were wounded. The attack also caused material damage. No incidents have taken place in the border region between Armenia and Azerbaijan during the past few days. [passage omitted] However, the Armenian Ministry of Defense Press Center has disclosed that the military units in Azerbaijan and the Nakhichevan Autonomous Republic [NAR] violated the cease-fire agreement in the northeastern and southeastern sectors of Armenia's border last night. Azerbaijan's units in Kyzylgadzhyly in Kazakhskiy Rayon fired on [place name indistinct] and Bekaber in Idzhevanskiy Rayon with mortars, tanks, and machine guns from 1920 to 2300 last night. The enemy units in the NAR fired on [place name indistinct] in Sisianskiy Rayon with machine guns from 2230 to 0100. Several people were wounded in the attack. Our border guards remained committed to the cease-fire agreement and refrained from launching retaliatory operations. The same source disclosed that no other incidents occurred in the border region between last night and this morning.
