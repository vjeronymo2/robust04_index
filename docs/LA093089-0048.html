September 30, 1989, Saturday, Valley Edition

DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: RV PASSENGER DIES FROM CRASH INJURIES

A 68-year-old Oklahoma woman who was among six people injured in a traffic collision near Palmdale has died of her injuries, hospital officials said Friday.

Wanda Leak died Thursday night after being thrown from a motor home that was struck by a large truck at the intersection of Fort Tejon Road, Pearblossom Highway and Avenue T, said Frankie Richards, spokeswoman for Antelope Valley Hospital Medical Center.

Also Friday, hospital officials upgraded the conditions of the two other seriously injured victims of the crash. Edward Leak, 79, driver of the motor home, and passenger Gladys Cline, 74, were in stable condition after being admitted to Palmdale Hospital Medical Center in critical condition Thursday, a hospital spokeswoman said.

Pearl Davis, 72; Robert Cline, 79, and Don Lile, 46, driver of the truck, were released from hospitals after being treated for minor injuries, hospital spokesmen said.

The motor home occupants were from Putnam, Okla., and were on their way to visit relatives in San Bernardino County when the vehicle ran a red light at Avenue T and was struck by the truck, which was northbound on Pearblossom, officials said.

The truck spilled its load of straw bales as it careened out of control into a gas station, narrowly missing gas pumps, authorities said. Three other cars sustained minor damage after being hit by the bales and debris from a light post and traffic signal knocked over by the truck, officials said.
