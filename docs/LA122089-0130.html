December 20, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

STATE SENATE CANDIDATE ACCUSES RIVAL OF 'SPECIAL-INTEREST' TIES

Republican State Senate candidate Ron Isles launched his first attack Tuesday in what could be a messy campaign by charging that Republican opponent Frank Hill "is moonlighting for special interests."

Isles, a candidate for the special election to replace State Sen. William Campbell, issued a statement saying the Whittier assemblyman collected $52,412 last year in gifts and honorariums. That is more than Hill's $44,000 legislative salary, he said.

"Where I come from, it looks like Frank Hill has a second job; he is moonlighting for the special interests," said Isles, a Brea City Council member.

Hill did not return repeated telephone calls to his office Monday or Tuesday.

Gov. George Deukmejian's announcement Tuesday that the primary for the 31st Senate District will be held Feb. 6 has forced the three announced Republican candidates into a quick campaign that has already seen Assemblyman Gil Ferguson (R-Newport Beach), the other Republican running for the GOP-dominated district, accuse Hill of being involved in a "Machiavellian scheme" to eliminate him from the race.

Isles' campaign manager, Harvey Englander, promised that the issue of ethics -- believed heightened in the public's mind by recent scandals in Sacramento and Washington -- will play a major role in the race.

Isles' statement Tuesday noted that Hill was a subject of the FBI's investigation into influence-peddling at the state Capitol. Hill's office was searched as part of the investigation, but no charges have been filed against him. Hill has declined to comment on the sting operation.
