BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Uzbek President Islam Karimov received Prime Minister Tansu Ciller at his hotel. During the meeting, Karimov conveyed greetings to Ciller from her ancestral home and wished her success in her work. In her statement, Ciller said: Welcome to your second country. Ciller pledged to do everything to strengthen bilateral relations. Karimov presented Ciller with various gifts, including national costumes and a carpet depicting the prime minister's portrait. Murat Karayalcin, state minister and deputy prime minister, and Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin also attended the meeting. Karimov and Ciller later held a meeting with Turkish businessmen. During the meeting, Karimov noted that his country is full of opportunities and asked Turkish businessmen to invest in Uzbekistan and develop their relations. Ciller, in turn, made a statement to Uzbek Television after emerging from the meeting. She declared: [Begin Ciller recording] We regard Uzbekistan as our own country. We were very proud and happy to welcome the brotherly president of Uzbekistan, which we consider very close. These two countries must move closer together and become brothers. This is very important for world peace as well as for the development of bilateral commercial relations. I repeat that we are very happy to see him here and I say to him: Feel at home and visit us frequently. [end recording] An official farewell ceremony was held for Karimov at the Cankaya Mansion this afternoon. The Uzbek president then left Esenboga Airport for his country.
