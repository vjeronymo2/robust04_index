Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] The UN secretary general's special representative in Cyprus today handed to the two sides in Cyprus the UN's final proposal for the implementation of the confidence-building measures [CBM's]. In a statement after his meeting with President Glavkos Kliridhis at the Presidential Palace this afternoon, Mr. Joe Clark avoided giving a direct answer as to whether the Greek Cypriot side has accepted the three basic principles set by the United Nations on the CBM's implementation. However, he expressed the hope that there will be an agreement between the two sides. Mr. Clark noted: Today, I presented the UN's final proposal on the CBM's to both leaders and they asked for time to study it. Mr. Clark further said that he will meet separately with the two leaders tomorrow and expressed the conviction that then he will have a clearer idea of where things stand. Asked if there was any reaction by the Greek Cypriot side to the plans, Mr. Clark said that he established there is a willingness to study the plan before any answer is given. Responding to a question whether he expects an answer tomorrow, Mr. Clark said that everybody knows that the UN secretary general must submit a report to the Security Council at the end of March and the report will either refer to an agreement or to the failure of the talks. Mr. Clark added that Mr. Butrus-Ghali will also have to give the reasons for a possible failure and noted that the end of the month is not far away. Government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis said that the Greek Cypriot side will study the whole document, which constitutes an amended version of the previous document with more detailed references, and adopt a position tomorrow in light of the positions taken by the Turkish Cypriot side and the UN secretary general's representatives. The spokesman further stressed that the Greek Cypriot side does not intend to endlessly exchange documents and demands that the Turkish Cypriot side take a clear position on the documents. At his meeting with Rauf Denktas this morning, Mr. Clark said that the three principles of the new document refer to: First, the acceptance of the need for the existence of a UN administration, which should exist because of an agreement between the leaders of the two communities. Second, the authority of this administration must be based on the same system being followed at the United Nations, according to which the authority and jurisdictions of the secretary general and his envoys stem from the Security Council. Third, there must be clear benefits for both sides.
