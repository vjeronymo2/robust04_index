Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Seoul, Feb. 17 KYODO -- South Korean Government authorities on Thursday [17 February] dismissed press reports that Kim Chong-il, son and heir apparent to North Korean President Kim Il-song, has been seriously injured. Officials told KYODO NEWS SERVICE the South Korean Government has observed nothing unusual in North Korea, stressing that it is "quiet." "If anything had happened to Kim Chong-il, changes in the daily schedule, including celebrations for his birthday (on Wednesday), should have appeared," the officials said in dismissing the reliability of the reports.
