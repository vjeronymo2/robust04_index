BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres has welcomed King Husayn's statements regarding his hopes to meet publicly with Israeli leaders in the near future. In a speech before the United Jewish Appeal in Jerusalem, the foreign minister said that the differences between Jordan and Israel were not of the kind that could not be resolved quickly. [Tel Aviv IDF Radio in Hebrew at 0830 GMT on 21 June reports that "Foreign Minister Peres said that Jordan would have to sever its linkage with Syria in the negotiations with Israel. This linkage was damaging to both parties, he noted."] As for Yasir 'Arafat's impending visit to the territories, Foreign Minister Peres said that it was up to the PLO chairman to decide the date of his visit, noting that 'Arafat could arrive whenever he chose. This was reported by our correspondent Steve Linde.
