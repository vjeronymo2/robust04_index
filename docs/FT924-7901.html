921113 FT 13 NOV 92 / International Company News: Canadian financial group unveils rise in profits POWER Financial, the financial services group controlled by Montreal financier Mr Paul Desmarais, unveiled a profits advance to CDollars 163.8m (USDollars 136.5m) or CDollars 1.87 a share in the first nine months. This compares with CDollars 129m or CDollars 1.43 a year earlier. Consolidated revenues were little changed at CDollars 4.2bn, The Great-West Lifeco made an increased contribution, as did Investors Group. Pargesa, the European holding company jointly owned with the Frere Group of Belgium, contributed CDollars 26.3m to Power Financial's earnings. The latest period included a CDollars 61.5m special gain taken in the second quarter, resulting from a stock issue by Investors Group. Third-quarter profit was CDollars 28m or 31 cents a share, against CDollars 37m or 41 cents a year earlier on revenues of CDollars 1.39bn, little changed. United Westburne, the North American plumbing, heating and electrical goods wholesaler controlled by Dumez of France, suffered a third-quarter loss of CDollars 330,000 or 1 cent a share, against a profit of CDollars 2.7m or 7 cents. Sales were CDollars 579m, down nearly 4 per cent, due to the sale of the Memphis division. The nine-month loss was CDollars 7.9m or 21 cents, including a Dollars 2m loss from discontinued operations, against a loss of CDollars 4m or 11 cents. Sales for the period were little changed at CDollars 1.6bn.
