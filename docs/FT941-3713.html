940315 FT 15 MAR 94 / Observer: Droit de seigneur Prince Charles - Britain's future king, who has admitted talking to his vegetables - speaks neither Japanese nor Chinese, but thinks the rest of us should learn. Perversely enough, he asserted that at yesterday's launch of a Pounds 4m project - led by the British Council - to help the world learn English. The prince lamented the drop in standards of spoken English over the past few decades, saying 'it is not good enough to expect other people to conduct business in English . . .We're not good enough at teaching other people's languages.' Asked if he spoke Japanese or Chinese he replied in the good old-fashioned style his father would appreciate: 'Of course I bloody don't. I don't have the time.'
