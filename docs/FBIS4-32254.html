BFN [Text] Deputy Prime Minister Grzegorz Kolodko has ended his visit to the United States, during which he explained to his U.S. partners the Polish Government's economic program. Grzegorz Kolodko believes that he has succeeded in convincing representatives of the Washington government and the IMF that the present Polish Government is not wrecking what their predecessors have done and that the Strategy for Poland program is the plan for the future. [Begin Kolodko recording] The IMF, Treasury Secretary Dentsen, and Ms. Tyson, head of President Clinton's team of economic advisors, who will visit Poland together with the president, all believe that this ambitious program and its objectives will be fulfilled and implemented if the work is well organized and if government policy is clear and firm, which we are, of course, determined to pursue. [end recording] [Announcer] Grzegorz Kolodko has also announced plans to set up a Polish-U.S. investment fund to support the privatization process in Poland. Deputy Prime Minister Kolodko hopes that initially it will be possible to make $100 million available for the fund.
