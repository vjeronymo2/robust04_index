BFN [Text] Ankara, June 19 (A.A.) -- President Suleyman Demirel sent a letter to his Iranian counterpart Hashemi-Rafsanjani and Greek counterpart Konstandinos Karamanlis on prevention of the separatist terrorist PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] organization's activities in their countries, it was reported on Sunday [19 June]. Presidential Press Center, in a written statement, said Demirel sent a letter to Rafsanjani on May 31 and expressed pleasure on Turkish-Iranian cooperation against terrorism. Demirel said joint struggle on the issue is for the benefit of the two countries `we call for Iran to take necessary measures against the PKK terrorists who were reported to be existing in Iran.' The Press Center said Rafsanjani replied the letter by sending a message with Iranian Interior Minister 'Ali Mohammad Besharati. The message said Turkish-Iranian cooperation on security is contributing to the two country relations adding that Iran is determined to take measures against PKK. The statement also said Demirel had sent a letter to Karamanlis on June 2 saying that necessary measures should be taken against the separatist terrorist organization. Demirel said France and Germany banned the organization's activities and the other countries are closely following the activities `however' he added `we learned that PKK has opened a bureau in Athens under another name and terrorists caught in Turkey said they were trained in camps in Greece.' The statement reported Demirel as saying `Turkish-Greek relations would be destroyed if these come true.' Karamanlis in reply said Greece did not allow PKK's terrorist activities on its territory adding that it seriously condemned terrorism to continue good neighboring [as received] relations. The Foreign Ministry earlier summoned the Greek ambassador and asked for information from Athens on confessions made by a number of PKK members. The terrorists, during their interrogation said that they received training in Greece. Athens had denied the statements of terrorists.
