Language: English Article Type:BFN [Press release issued by the UK Defense Ministry on 5 January: "UN Appointment for British General in Bosnia"] [Text] The United Nations announced today that Lieutenant General Sir Michael Rose will assume command of the UN Protection Force [UNPROFOR] Bosnia-Herzegovina. He will succeed the current Belgian Commander, Lieutenant General Francis Briquemont. Lieutenant General Rose will have his headquarters in Kiseljak in Central Bosnia. He will be responsible to the overall Commander of UNPROFOR in the Former Yugoslavia, General Jean Cot, who has his headquarters in Zagreb, Croatia.

Biographical Details
Lieutenant General Sir Michael Rose was born in Quetta in 1940 and educated at Cheltenham, Oxford and the Sorbonne. He was commissioned into the Coldstream Guards in 1964, and served in Aden, Germany and Northern Ireland. Following attendance at the Staff College, Camberley in 1973, he was appointed Brigade Major of 16 Parachute Brigade and subsequently commanded a squadron of 22 Special Air Service Regiment. Following a tour of duty in Northern Ireland as Military Assistant to the General Officer Commanding, he returned to command 22d Special Air Service Regiment from 1979-1982, during which time he saw active service in the Falkland Islands. From there he went on to command 39th Infantry Brigade in Northern Ireland. He attended the Royal College of Defence Studies in 1986, and from January 1987 until May 1988 was Commandant of the School of Infantry. He was Director Special Forces until his appointment as General Officer Commanding North East District and 2d Infantry Division in October 1989. He then took over as Commandant of the Staff College, Camberley. This was his last appointment before becoming Commander UK Field Army and Inspector General Territorial Army in April 1993. He was awarded the QGM [Queen's Gallantry Medal] in April 1981, the CBE [Commander of the British Empire] in April 1986 and was knighted (KCB) [Knight Commander of the Bath] in the 1994 New Year's Honours. He is currently Chairman of the Army Parachute Association and Commodore of the Army Sailing Association. He is married and has two sons.
