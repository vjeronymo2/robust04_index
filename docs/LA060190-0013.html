June 1, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: NURSING AND ALCOHOL

Question: There's been a lot of information in newspapers and magazines and on television about the harm that can be done to the baby if a pregnant woman drinks.

But I don't remember reading anything about what might happen to the baby if a nursing mother drinks. Could this have a harmful effect on the baby?

Answer: As is true in the case of pregnant women, the safest course of action for nursing mothers is not to drink at all. Obviously, alcohol that has not been consumed cannot affect the baby. However, there is some evidence to suggest that no more than one alcoholic drink a day is permissible for nursing mothers.
