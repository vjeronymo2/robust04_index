BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 13 April Morning Edition] [Text] At a news conference on the afternoon of 12 April, Chief Cabinet Secretary Masayoshi Takemura (head of Sakigake) commented on reports that his close relations with North Korea may impede the Japanese Government's policymaking on the DPRK's (North Korea) suspected development of nuclear arms. He denied such reports, saying: "It is true I am vice chairman of the Japan-North Korea Parliamentary Union, and I was also a member of (former Liberal Democratic Party Vice President Shin) Kanemaru's delegation which visited North Korea. However, this does not constitute any basis for saying I have suspicious relations with North Korea." Regarding reports that some U.S. Government officials are beginning to regard Takemura as the "North Korean connection" in the Japanese Government, Takemura said: "I have never heard of such a connection."
