930324 FT 24 MAR 93 / NZ opposition sets out economic policy The New Zealand Labour party, which opinion polls say will easily win November's general election, yesterday published an economic strategy designed to double annual growth to 4 per cent and create jobs, Terry Hall reports from Wellington. The main plank in the policy is the development of an Enterprise Council, to undertake long-range planning and foster industrial development. It will include the governor of the Reserve Bank, the Treasury, trade union and business groups. Some tax incentives will be permitted.
