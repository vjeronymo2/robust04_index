BFN [Text] Brasilia, 3 May (EFE) -- Official sources reported that the presidents of Brazil and Angola, Itamar Franco and Eduardo dos Santos, met today in Brasilia. They discussed the Angolan civil war and Nelson Mandela's election as the new South African president. Dos Santos, who has been visiting the resort of Ilheus, in Bahia State, for the last 15 days, paid Franco a courtesy visit. The two presidents analyzed the political situation of the southern African countries and other matters of common interest. A presidential spokesman said: "It was an informal meeting between the presidents of two friendly countries." Franco and Dos Santos evaluated the electoral victory of Mandela and of his party, the African National Congress. They considered this victory positive from the political point of view of Angola and the other countries of the southern region of the African continent. The two presidents also exchanged ideas about the first summit of Portuguese-speaking countries, which will be held in Lisbon in June. The summit will be attended by the presidents and chiefs of state of Brazil, Portugal, Angola, Mozambique, Cape Verde, Guinea-Bissau, and Sao Tome and Principe.
