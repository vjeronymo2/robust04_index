940621 FT 21 JUN 94 / Fears on quality of some coffee The Ministry of Agriculture is preparing to investigate sales of instant coffee after government scientists found that 15 per cent of instant coffee samples tested contained material other than ground coffee beans. The adulterated coffee probably included coffee plant husks, skins, stems or stalks added before roasting the beans, the ministry said. It said it would probably launch a fuller investigation. The brands could not be named, because the number of jars examined was too small to be representative of any one product, it added, noting there was no danger to health. Mr Stephen Locke, policy director at the Consumers' Association, said the survey 'suggests consumers are being bamboozled and conned. It's a matter of misleading trading practices.'
