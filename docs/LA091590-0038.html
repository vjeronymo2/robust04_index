September 15, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

MYSTERY WOMAN

Two-and-a-half years ago, we went to the Los Angeles Opera's "Tristan & Isolde." We were late in ordering our tickets and so wound up in row U at the leftmost edge.

In an orchestral passage of the first act, an elderly lady of aristocratic mien got up from the middle of row U and edged her way out, murmuring apologies on the way. Near the end of the intermission, she asked us whether we would exchange our seats with those of her and her companion (a younger woman). We replied that of course, we would be glad to do so, but that one couldn't see the subtitles from our seats because of the balcony overhang. Whereupon she replied that she did not need to see them: She knew the opera by heart, having sung it at Covent Garden. Her problem was that, in subconsciously vocalizing with the soprano, she built up a need to cough.

We were of course quite overwhelmed by this explanation but lacked the presence of mind to ask her name. Do you have any idea who she was?

WILLIAM KAULA

Los Angeles

Martin Bernheimer suggests that perhaps the woman was Dame Eva Turner, who recently died at age 98.
