Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over still photograph of President Demirel] [Text] President Suleyman Demirel has said that the heavy weapons of those besieging Sarajevo must be silenced with an air operation within the framework of the UN and NATO resolutions, and that effective measures must be taken to lift the siege. In a message he sent to Bosnia-Herzegovina President Alija Izetbegovic, Demirel expressed his deep regret over the horrendous attack in Sarajevo. He said: This recent attack shows once again that the time has come and gone for the civilized world and for the international organizations that represent it to fulfill their responsibilities. I call on the civilized world once again to silence with an air operation -- within the framework of the UN and NATO resolutions -- the heavy weapons of those forces mercilessly besieging Sarajevo as soon as possible, and to take effective measures to lift this siege. I am conveying our demands regarding this issue with messages to the concerned international organizations and to the leaders of the leading countries of the world. I extend Turkey's and my own personal condolences to the people of Bosnia-Herzegovina for those killed in this despicable attack. I affirm that, as always, Turkey is on the side of Bosnia-Herzegovina and is ready to extend to it every kind of support. Taking this opportunity, I extend my deepest respects.
