940805 FT 05 AUG 94 / Beer price war hits brewery An independent Cornish brewery has become a victim of the fierce price competition in the take-home beer market which has been intensified by personal imports from the Continent. Redruth Brewery has gone into administrative receivership and 42 of the 140 staff have been made redundant. It owes more than Pounds 4m to two debenture holders, JA Devenish, part of the Greenall Group, and Nacanco, the US can suppliers. It is owed an export debt of more than Pounds 1m. The brewery has no tied houses but cans and bottles for national distribution to supermarkets and independent off-licences. Much of its output is for own-labels, but its own brands include 93 Limited Edition and John Davey's bitter. It is one of the UK's largest independents, with a capacity of 500,000 barrels a year. In the year to February it had a turnover of Pounds 24m, net of value added tax and duty, and made a marginal loss. Mr Keith Morgan, joint receiver for the accountancy firm Pannell Kerr Forster, said: 'We are trading on for the foreseeable future while we find a buyer. It is a good core business with short-term cashflow problems.' More than 100 inquiries had been made by potential purchasers. Mr Nigel Bibby, who became chief executive four months ago, said profit margins had suffered because the industry's excess capacity had encouraged big brewers to compete more aggressively in take-home sales. These had a rising share of the market. He added: 'The World Cup meant there was a lot of jostling to get your brands on the supermarket shelves, with promotion which led to lower prices.'
