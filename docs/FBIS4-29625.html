BFN [Text] The newsroom of the National Radio of Cambodia has just received a letter from the ministers of the interior concerning the deadline for the departure from Phnom Penh by representatives of the Party of Democratic Kampuchea [PDK]. The letter reads: From: The Ministers of the Interior To: The Representatives of the PDK Accredited to Phnom Penh Subject: Deadline for the PDK Representatives' Departure From Phnom Penh The Ministry of the Interior requests that all representatives of the PDK and their staff immediately leave Phnom Penh before 19 June 1994 because the roundtable between the legal representatives of the Royal Government of the Kingdom of Cambodia and the PDK on 15 and 16 June 1994 at the royal palace in Phnom Penh did not produce any positive results regarding a cease-fire, which long has been wished for by all the citizens of the country; because no other meetings have been scheduled; because the Ministry of the Interior has learned that the indignant public is planning to conduct various activities against your group; and because the Ministry of the Interior cannot stop the people from being indignant, cannot forever ensure your security, and cannot bear full responsibility for any eventual accidents against your persons. The Ministry of the Interior will provide safe escort for your group from the office to Pochentong Airport. You, however, must inform the Ministry at least one day before your departure. Beyond the above deadline, the Ministry of the Interior will not be held responsible for your security. Please, accept our high regards. Phnom Penh, 17 June 1994 [Signed] His Excellency Sar Kheng, His Excellency Yu Hokkri, ministers of the interior
