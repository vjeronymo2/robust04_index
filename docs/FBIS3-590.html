Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Johannesburg March 9 SAPA -- The rightist Freedom Front was considering taking part in the April elections -- but had not yet submitted candidate lists, Independent Electoral Commission [IEC] Chairman Justice Johan Kriegler said on Wednesday [9 March] night. Three unidentified representatives of the front were holed up in a meeting with electoral officials for three hours on Wednesday afternoon and evening after the close of the 4.30pm deadline for parties to submit a candidates' list. The judge -- who also brushed aside concern about deadlines -- met the Freedom Front offcials, but would not comment on his discussions. Justice Kriegler said he had also been in touch with the Inkatha Freedom Party which he said had "not yet" submitted its lists. The judge brushed aside concern about all deadlines imposed by the Electoral Act."The deadline is a journalistic concern and we are in [words indistinct] determine whether somebody has exceeded the speed limit. We are trying to organise an election of national reconciliation." The judge added the law could be amended retrospectively if all the political parties agreed. The Freedom Front officials arrived shortly before [words indistinct]. Earlier in the day 25 parties, including the National Party, African National Congress, the Pan Africanist Congress, [words indistinct] party, minority groups as well as regional and special interest parties, submitted candidate lists.
