941028 FT 28 OCT 94 / UK Company News: Domino Printing Domino Printing Sciences, the ink-jet printer manufacturer, is broadening its technology base with two acquisitions in the US. The Cambridge-based group is paying Dollars 2.4m (Pounds 1.51m) cash for Digi-Gear Systems, a private Illinois company supplying control systems. Its book value is anticipated to be no more than Dollars 250,000. In 1993 it achieved pre-tax profits of Dollars 100,000 on sales of Dollars 1.2m; sales and profits for 1994 were at higher levels, Domino said yesterday. Domino is also paying an initial Dollars 2.84m cash for certain assets of Control Print, a trading division of Avery Dennison, which makes ink-jet coding and marking systems. Book value amounts to Dollars 1m; it made an operating profit of Dollars 651,000 in 1993 on sales of Dollars 9.1m.
