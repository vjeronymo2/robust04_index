Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Luanda, March 27 (XINHUA) -- Pak Myong-ku, deputy foreign minister of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, said here today that his country will not yield to the pressure from the United States. Pak Myong-ku said this on his arrival at the Luanda International Airport. He has come here to pay a five-day visit to Angola. If the United States exerts pressure on his country by force of arms, North Korea will "take appropriate steps," he said. He said, "We are ready politically and militarily to deal with the United States on our own, and the Americans should know this." Pak Myong-ku said his visit to the southern African country will "promote the bilateral relations between the two countries."
