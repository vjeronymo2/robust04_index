Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, January 15 (ATA) -- Four Albanians were brought before the Serbian court of the Peje District on January 14 on obviously trumped up political charges. The accused are: Fisnik Ukaj, 25, from Peje, chairman of the Independent Students' Union of Prishtina University; Sadri Shala, 61, mechanic from Ujmir of Kline; Ymer Shala, 29, mechanic; and Ali Lajqi, deputy of the Kosova parliament and a former political prisoner. The first three are charged with "carrying out hostile activity and violation of territorial integrity" of former Yugoslavia and the fourth with knowledge of a criminal act and failure to inform the authorities. All of the accused are charged with participating in a meeting in Ujmir village of Kline in October 1991 for the purpose of forming an people's organization for the Republic of Kosova." The accused denied the charges considering them groundless. They also claimed that they had been tortured before their imprisonment. The trial has been adjourned until January 28.
