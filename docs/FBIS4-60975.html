BFN [Unattributed report: "Germany Will Supply Equipment as Aid to the Rivne Nuclear Power Station"] [Text] By the end of 1995, the Rivne Nuclear Power Station will receive equipment worth 21 million German marks from Germany. This was agreed upon during a meeting at the nuclear facility between representatives of the Society for the Safety of Nuclear Reactors and of the German firms "Siemens," "ABB," and "Lloyd." Following the interstate agreement, the German side will provide equipment for the periodic inspection of metals and diagnostics, telecommunications equipment, and fire-proof materials. This will make it possible to modernize the functioning power unit with its output of 1 million kilowatt/hours. Assembly operations will be carried out by Rivne nuclear specialists with the help of specialists from various German companies assigned by the Society for the Safety of Nuclear Reactors, taking into account their experience in organizing and implementing measures to increase nuclear safety.
