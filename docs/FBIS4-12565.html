BFN [Text] Amman, 8 April (AFP)--Bombs exploded 15 minutes apart near Iraqi ministries in Baghdad on Wednesday, an Iraqi group said here Friday without reporting casualties. "An explosive device was placed at al-Nidal Street about 100 meters (300 feet) from the Industry Ministry and another was placed near the agriculture and Irrigation Ministry," the Iraqi National Congress said in a statement. "The two explosions caused panic among security services and coincided with the anniversary of the foundation of the ruling (Ba'th) party," said the statement dated from Sulaymaniyah and Kurdish-controlled norther Iraq. It said the first occurred at 5:30 a.m. (0130 GMT) and the second at 5:45 a.m. (0145 GMT).
