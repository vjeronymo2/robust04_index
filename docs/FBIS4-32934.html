BFN [From the NTV newscast] [Text] South Africa's imminent incorporation into the Nonaligned Movement [NAM] and Cuba's election to one of the vice presidencies of the summit were two of the main results of the meeting for experts which concluded on 30 May, prior to the ministerial meeting. Diplomatic sources report that the meeting's agenda was approved and that its essential points include an analysis of the international political and economic situation and of the NAM's role for the immediate future. Egyptian President Husni Mubarak will officially open the 11th NAM summit on 31 May. The summit will conclude on 3 June. Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina had a very busy agenda while the summit was taking place at the Cairo Conference Center, meeting several officials including the Arab League secretary general and the Zimbabwean, Vietnamese, Iraqi, and Burmese foreign ministers on the need to strengthen the NAM and the principles that gave birth to the Movement. The common denominator at these meetings was to give continuity to the positive results the NAM has experienced under Indonesia's presidency since the Jakarta summit. Just minutes ago, Robaina met Honduran Foreign Minister (Ernesto Rojas), who conveyed a friendly greeting from President Reina. (Rojas) announced during the meeting his forthcoming visit to Havana in response to Robaina's invitation.
