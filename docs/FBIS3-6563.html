Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Honolulu, March 18 KYODO -- Malaysian Deputy Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim blasted the United States Friday [18 March] over its human rights policy toward China, accusing Washington of forcing its values "down the throat" of other countries. "To assume that we have the sole monopoly in our understanding of democracy and human rights constitutes a form of arrogance," Anwar said. Anwar, who was in Honolulu for an Asia-Pacific finance ministers meeting, said he raised Malaysia's concern of the U.S. human rights policy in a meeting with U.S. Treasury Secretary Lloyd Bentsen. Anwar was particularly critical of the U.S. policy of linking China's human rights record to its most favored nation trading status in the United States. Linking trade issues with human rights amounts to "forc(ing) down the throat of other countries to see your point of view," Anwar said. Anwar, who serves as Malaysia's finance minister, said the United States should "listen a bit more" on political realities in other countries. When it comes to human rights, "you must show a lot of tolerance and understanding," Anwar said.
