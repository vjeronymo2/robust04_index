BFN [Text] Brasilia, 1 Jun (EFE) -- Sources at the Cuban Embassy in Brasilia have said that Cuba will amortize $38.3 million of the debt it owes Brazil with medicines for the Brazilian public health sector, based on an agreement negotiated by the governments of the two countries. The Brazilian and Cuban Governments recently negotiated a new medicine deal worth $15 million, of which 50 percent will be used for amortizing debts. Another $15 million transaction will be concluded in the immediate future.
