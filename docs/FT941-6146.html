940303 FT 03 MAR 94 / Clarke turns on Labour tax critics Mr Kenneth Clarke, the chancellor, yesterday countered a renewed onslaught on his November Budget by accusing Labour of 'spreading myths' about the effects of tax increases. Mr Clarke mounted a vigorous defence of the government's taxation record, in line with the Conservatives' determination to meet Labour criticism head on in the campaigns for the forthcoming local and European election and Commons by-elections. His comments followed claims by Ms Harriet Harman, shadow chief secretary to the Treasury, that the tax on insurance premiums announced in the Budget amounted to a breach of the government's pledge not to extend the scope of value added tax. 'The people of Britain will be hit by a triple whammy of rising crime, rising insurance premiums and now a tax on their insurance premiums,' she said.
