Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku, January 6 (TURAN) -- Yesterday the vice-chairman of the Supreme Soviet of Azerbaijan, Afiaddin Dzhalilov, received a delegation from the Ardebil province of Iran headed by the Vice- Governor 'Ali Akbar Iskanderi. The sides discussed ways of broadening and maintaining relations between the bordering regions of the two countries. Dzhalilov expressed his satisfaction with the Iranian party's assistance to Azerbaijani refugees and displaced persons and the citizens of Nakhichevan which is under blockade. Vice-Premier Abbas Abbasov also received the Iranian Ambassador to Azerbaijan, 'Ali Asgar Nahavandian, yesterday as well as other members of the delegation which include Mayor 'Ali Akbarzade of the town of Belasuvar, bisected by the Iranian-Azerbaijani border, and the Secretary of Trade, Ahmad Kazemi. During these talks, both sides discussed the broadening of border relations. Of particular focus was the creation of a free trade zone in the Iranian-Azerbaijani town of Belasuvar. As known, at the end of last year the Cabinet of Ministers of Azerbaijan passed a decree on the creation of the free trade zone in this sector of the border. However, the opening of the zone has been delayed for technical causes.
