October 16, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

NATION;

ANTI-GULF PROTESTERS SPILL OIL

Half a dozen demonstrators opposed to U.S. military involvement in the Persian Gulf poured a quart of oil onto a patio in front of the American Petroleum Institute today to show support for a Marine who refused to be sent to Saudi Arabia.

The protesters read a message from the 22-year-old Marine, Cpl.

Jeffrey Paterson, who is confined to his base in Hawaii awaiting a court-martial.

Paterson's message urged other Americans to join him in saying: "Hell, no, we won't go, we won't fight for Texaco."

Two counter-demonstrators also showed up.

David Almasi, who said he worked in a building next door, came to the lunchtime rally with a handmade sign asking: "Why did Jeff Paterson join the Marines? To surf? Or the free paycheck?"
