Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan, 8 Feb -- The president of Armenia, Levon Ter-Petrosyan, departed this morning on an official visit to Britain at the invitation of Prime Minister John Major. During the three-day visit, the president and the Armenian state delegation he is leading will hold talks with the head of the British cabinet and also with the Secretaries of State for foreign affairs and defence which will focus on further development of the two countries' political, economic and cultural cooperation. On Wednesday there will be a meeting with Queen Elizabeth II. On the same day, the Armenian leader will address the Royal Institute for International Relations. The agenda also includes talks with the leader of the Anglican Church, the Archbishop of Canterbury, a meeting with representatives of the 12,000-strong Armenian community in Britain, and a press conference. A meeting with the president of the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development, Jacques de Larosiere, is scheduled for Thursday. The visit is expected to culminate in the signing of a package of inter-governmental agreements, in particular on the basic principles of cooperation between Armenia and Great Britain, on scientific exchanges and on direct air services between the two countries.
