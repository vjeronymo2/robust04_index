March 18, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

SIMON IS TRAPPED, TOO

Roger Simon assumes that because a court cannot prove child molestation that none existed.

We all have heard of our imperfect court system. Known murderers are walking our streets due to plea-bargaining or suppression of sufficient evidence to win a prosecution.

Child-molesters are released to continue to molest our children.

It is the parent's moral duty to protect the child. That this could be at odds with a court decision is unfortunate and, yes, could cause illegal acts.

Simon does not address the dilemma our courts and society are debating regarding the child-molestation problem. He chose to malign the tragic characters and thus does us all a disservice.

PATRICIA BUCK, Whittier
