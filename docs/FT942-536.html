940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / German warning on the dollar The US government should stop making contradictory comments about the role of the dollar as these are causing instability on financial markets, according to one of Chancellor Helmut Kohl's former top financial advisers. Mr Horst Kohler, a former senior official in the Finance Ministry who now heads the powerful Association of German Savings Banks, said next month's G7 summit in Naples should give a clear signal to improve stability on the international currency markets. He said that comments by Mr Lloyd Bentsen, the US treasury secretary, favouring a strong dollar, were at odds with statements by Mr Ron Brown, commerce secretary, who prefers a weak dollar to correct the trade deficit with Japan. 'If the exchange rates become an instrument to regulate competition then that is a bad development for world trade,' Mr Kohler told journalists.
