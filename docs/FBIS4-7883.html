BFN [Text] Sofia, April 5 (BTA) -- Bulgaria's major political forces, the anti-communist Union of the Democratic Forces (SDS) and former Communists' Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP), are considering their election campaigns before elections are even scheduled. For more than a year the SDS have been calling on the cabinet of nonpartisan Professor Lyuben Berov to resign and insisting on early elections. Three days ago President Zhelyu Zhelev withdrew his support for the cabinet. The cabinet's resignation is a variant being considered by the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS) on whose mandate the incumbent cabinet was formed in late December 1992. At a closed-door sitting late last week, the Socialists, for their part, decided to give a last chance to Prof. Berov's cabinet and granted them a month and a half to take under control the critical situation of the past couple of weeks. In the meantime, Prime Minister Berov, now recovering from a cardiac operation, publicized his plans about structural and personnel changes in the government. The government has obviously come to an impasse, SDS leader Filip Dimitrov told journalists after a sitting of the union's supreme leadership. According to him, the government is trying very hard to convince people that the situation is improving. While the dollar seems to be under control, the prices do not. The statements of cabinet members sound most strange; they display a fake optimism and gross indifference to the man in the street, Filip Dimitrov went on to say. He reiterated the union's firm belief that the incumbent cabinet should step down. The SDS leader would not give further details as to the specific action the union is planning to take, adding only that it will resort to extraparliamentary means as soon as the parliamentary ones prove inefficient. The SDS supreme leadership today also discussed its election campaign. This issue was high on the agenda of the BSP Executive Council. Socialists' leader Zhan Videnov told journalists that disbanding the parliament, which the effective constitution provides for in case a caretaker cabinet is appointed, is absurd when the country has embarked on the road of transition. Amendment of this provision in the constitution will require a majority of three quarters of the parliamentary votes. Filip Dimitrov also said that the SDS leadership has not put for discussion the BSP proposal for amendment to the constitution to enable Parliament to sit even after the formation of a caretaker cabinet. According to the SDS leader, the fact that the Socialists are trying to launch the prolonged and complex parliamentary procedure for amending of the constitution comes to show that they are the ones who fear early election, not the SDS. The ex-Communists fear elections like hell, Filip Dimitrov also said.
