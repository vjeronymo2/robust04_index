BFN [Excerpts] French Minister for Cooperation Michel Roussin said he was dismayed at the news of the death of the Rwandan and Burundi presidents. Here is a statement by the spokesman of the French Foreign Ministry Richard Duque: [Begin Duque recording] First, France deplores the brutal death of the Rwandan and Burundian presidents. We would like to extend our condolences to the families of the presidents and to the families of the other victims. You also know that the crew were French. We deplore their deaths and extend our condolences to their families as well. We do not have any particular information on the circumstances in which the plane of the Rwandan president crashed yesterday evening. We think that there is a need for an international investigation in order to find out precisely what caused the crash and who was responsible. [end recording] An emergency meeting was held this morning in Paris. There were representatives of the presidency, the prime minister, and of the ministries of defense and foreign affairs. The French forces based in Bangui, in the Central African Republic, have been put on alert. [passage omitted] According to the government, there are 600 French nationals in Rwanda and 700 in Burundi. [passage omitted]
