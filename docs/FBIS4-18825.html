BFN [Excerpt] Ankara (AA) -- Prime Minister Tansu Ciller has condemned the chemical attack on the Bosnian city of Gorazde by the Serbs. She said: "Our government is following the developments with utmost care and is conducting the necessary contacts." Ciller issued a statement in connection with the developments in Gorazde. According to the Prime Minister's Press Center, the statement is as follows: "To my great horror and regret, I have learned that today the Serbs used chemical weapons against the town of Gorazde in Bosnia-Herzegovina, which has been subjected to an inhuman siege by the Serbs for a long time, and that many civilians, children, and elderly people were killed in this attack. ``I strongly condemn this attack, which is difficult to believe and constitutes a crime against humanity, on behalf of my government and myself. We want the international community, particularly the United Nations and NATO, to take the necessary steps without any further delay to put an end to the suffering of the people of Bosnia-Herzegovina and punish the culprits. Our government is following the developments with utmost care and is conducting the necessary contacts. ``I pray for those who lost their lives in this attack and I wish the wounded a speedy recovery. I extend my deepest solidarity to the victims' families.'' [passage omitted]
