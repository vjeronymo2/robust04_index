930928 FT 28 SEP 93 / London Stock Exchange: Oil sector firm Reports that Opec ministers meeting in Geneva had agreed to set the ceiling on their total output for the fourth quarter at 24.5m barrels per day cheered the market and drove several oil stocks sharply forward. The positive progress of the Opec talks also brought a slight improvement in the oil price, which helped to underpin the day's advance in oil shares, as did a slight shortage of stock earlier in the session. The day's main advances included British Petroleum, which rose 11 to 313p with just over 6m shares traded, Enterprise Oil, 12 up at 444p, and Shell Transport, 9 stronger at 660p in trade of 1.8m. However, talks about the distribution of quotas within the ceiling were still in progress by yesterday's market close and one analyst remained cautious and said: 'Today's rise may be premature given that nothing has been decided about the quotas.'
