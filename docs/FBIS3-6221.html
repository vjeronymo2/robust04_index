Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 15 KYODO -- Japan's merchandise trade surplus rose for a third straight month in February, climbing 3.3 percent from a year before to 10,912 million dollars, the Finance Ministry said Tuesday [15 March] in a preliminary report. The February rise in the customs-cleared trade surplus followed preliminary rises of 17.0 percent in January, 15.5 percent in December and a 2.2 percent fall in November that snapped 34 months of consecutive year-on-year growth. Exports rose 5.6 percent in February from a year earlier to 29,532 million dollars, while imports rose 7.0 percent to 18,620 million dollars, the ministry said. Japan registered an unadjusted surplus of 4,344 million dollars in its trade with the United States.
