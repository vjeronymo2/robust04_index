941207 FT 07 DEC 94 / World Trade News: Taiwan carrier to expand fleet Taiwan's China Airlines (CAL) plans to buy up to 10 small regional passenger jets for about Dollars 400m. Models under consideration include the Boeing 737 series, the McDonnell Douglas MD-90 series and the Airbus A320. CAL is considering spending Dollars 4bn on upgrading its fleet within the next 10 years. The airline wants to expand its current fleet of 37 aircraft by buying about 30 new aircraft and replacing 16 older ones. Expansion of the company's fleet of larger regional jets and long-distance aircraft with 350 seats or more is also under consideration.
