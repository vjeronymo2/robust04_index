Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] FOREIGN Minister Prasong Sunsiri yesterday said Thailand would not consider the Burmese ethnic minorities' request to use Thailand as a venue for peace talks with the Rangoon ruling military junta. "But if the (Burmese) government itself asked for that, we will consider it," Prasong told reporters. [passage omitted] Prasong also refused to make a direct comment on a recent report that Rangoon might release opposition leader Aung San Suu Kyi in July this year. The minister however said that only the Burmese government, aware of how the world community views the situation in Burma, would know what it should do. [passage omitted] The junta's powerful secretary Lt Gen Khin Nyunt said the legitimate period of Suu Kyi's house arrest would expire in July. Burma's law limits the period of house arrest to a maximum of five years. [passage omitted]
