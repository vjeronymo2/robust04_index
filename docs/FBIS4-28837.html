BFN [Text] After attending a Unification and Security Policy Coordination Council meeting this morning, Yi Hong-ku, deputy prime minister and minister of the National Unification Board, said that North Korea's attempt [as heard] to withdraw from the International Atomic Energy Agency runs counter to a peaceful solution to the nuclear problem. He also urged North Korea to implement all nuclear safeguards measures, though belatedly. You will listen to it directly from him. [Begin Yi Hong-ku recording] I cannot but clearly point out that North Korea's withdrawing from the mechanism of all safeguards measures for ensuring the denuclearization of the Korean peninsula would decisively run counter to a peaceful solution to the nuclear problem [pihaekhwarul pojanghanun modun anjonjochiui tureso pukhani ital handanun kosun haengmunjeui pyongjok haegyore kyolchongjoguro yokhaeng handanun kosul myongbaekhui chijokhaji anulsu opsumnida]. Therefore, I strongly urge North Korea to cooperate, though belatedly [chigum irado], with regard to all safeguards measures required by the Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty. [end recording]
