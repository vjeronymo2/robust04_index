Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Boris Yeltsin will stay one day, February 3, in Tbilisi, informed sources in that city told Interfax. The visit is said to have been originally scheduled for February 3 or 4 and have a one day program. This includes a tete-a-tete with the Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze, negotiations between the delegations, a meeting of the Russian president with Georgian intellectuals and an official ceremony of signing Russo-Georgian documents, notably the Friendship and Cooperation Treaty. Final touches are said to be put on about 20 bilateral documents. Only those that are finalized will be signed. Colonel Gennadiy Dolgachyov, head of the Russian Army Group in Transcaucasia press office, told Interfax that President Yeltsin would be accompanied by Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev who would sign with Georgian military officials a treaty on the status of Russian troops in Georgia and numerous other documents on military cooperation between the two countries.
