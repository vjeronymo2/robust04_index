BFN [Report by 'Ali Hamati from Washington] [Text] The joint Jordanian-U.S. committee held its first meeting at the U.S. State Department earlier today. Information Minister Dr. Jawad al-'Anani, who chaired the Jordanian side, said discussion revolved around the framework for the committee's action. The Jordanian foreign debt, particularly to the United States, is the most important topic on the agenda. Dr. al-'Anani said it has been agreed to reassess the debt during the meetings that will be held within the context of the Paris Club in the future. It was also agreed during the meeting, which was attended on the Jordanian side by Finance Minister Sami Qammuh and Fayiz al-Tarawinah, Jordan's ambassador in Washington, that the United States will do all it can to reschedule the largest possible amount of Jordanian debt for three years, taking into account an easing of the terms of rescheduling in terms of the period and the interest rate and to support Jordan's position on reducing the debt. Dr. al-'Anani said it was agreed to study increasing U.S. aid to Jordan and that Jordan has made many proposals in this regard which the U.S. side promised to study in order to meet the feasible ones. As regards the trade links, the information minister and head of the Jordanian side to this committee meetings said final arrangements have been set to lift the blockade of the Port of al-'Aqabah. He said the meeting discussed the issue of increasing Jordan's exports to the United States, supporting the commercial centers, and the setting up of a joint chamber of commerce. It was also agreed that the two countries' ambassadors in Amman and Washington will act as a liaison officers for the joint Jordanian-U.S. committee and that the committee will meet once every six months in Amman and Washington alternately. The U.S. president stressed during his meeting with His Majesty King Husayn his support to the Jordanian economic reforms and his country's support for Jordan's efforts to reduce its foreign debt. The U.S. side to the joint committee meetings was chaired by Robert Pelletreau, the assistant secretary of state for Near Eastern affairs. It was attended on the U.S. side by representatives from the Departments of Commerce, Treasury, and State.
