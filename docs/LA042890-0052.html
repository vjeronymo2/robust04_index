April 28, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

KEATING'S YOUTHFUL ZEAL AGAINST PORN, OBSCENITY

I had a hearty laugh when I read ("Mapplethorpe Show Bumps Into a City's Legacy of Conformity" by David Wells, Calendar, April 23) that Charles Keating, as a young Cincinnati attorney full of religious zeal, founded Citizens for Decent Literature and was later appointed by President Nixon to the Federal Commission on Obscenity and Pornography. The obscene greed displayed in the S&L scandals is beginning to make sense.

G. BORDING MATHIEU

Laguna Beach
