940127 FT 27 JAN 94 / India eases mining curbs India has decided as part of its economic reform programme to open the state-owned mining industry wider to foreign and (domestic private companies, Reuter reports from New Delhi. United News of India said that the government issued an ordinance to allow foreign investment in mining firms and lift restrictions on foreign equity holding. The ordinance would permit any company registered in India, irrespective of its foreign equity holding, to apply for a mining lease or prospecting license. Earlier laws restricted foreign equity in these companies to 40 per cent.
