Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN ["Taiwan Businessmen's Investment in Hong Kong, Macao After 1997, 1999 Will Be Restricted" -- ZHONGGUO TONGXUN SHE headline] [Text] Hong Kong, 7 Mar (ZHONGGUO TONGXUN SHE) -- A report from Taipei: According to the provisions of the "Draft Regulations on Relations With Hong Kong and Macao," which is being examined by the Mainland Affairs Council of the Administrative Yuan of Taiwan, the "reporting for the record" system will be adopted for the people of Taiwan to invest in Hong Kong and Macao after 1997 and 1999, respectively. The Ministry of Economy will promulgate regulations restricting investment in Hong Kong and Macao by enterprises on the island. Any offender will be fined from NT$300,000 to NT$3 million [New Taiwan dollars] and such punishment can be repeated. In addition, the Mainland Affairs Council also has examined many relevant regulations, the main content of which includes the following: Taiwan's financial organizations may continue to accept and exchange Hong Kong dollars on condition that the Hong Kong dollars have "ample exchange reserves" and can be "exchanged freely." Regarding transportation, the Mainland Affairs Council said that "with permission," Taiwan's ships and civilian aircraft "can navigate between Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Macao." The procedures for obtaining permission will be formulated by the Ministry of Communications. With permission, ships and aircraft of Hong Kong and Macao also may enter Taiwan's restricted and prohibited waters and aviation information areas. With permission, foreign ships and aircraft also may navigate between Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Macao. Taiwan's Mainland Affairs Council is scheduled to complete all preliminary examination work on 7 March and to make public these drafts to extensively solicit the views and suggestions of various circles on the island. At the same time, it has planned to entrust academic organizations with holding seminars in Hong Kong and Macao to listen to views on the legislation of various circles in Hong Kong and Macao.
