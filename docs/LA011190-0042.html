January 11, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: PLUCKING FAT FROM HERB CHICKEN

DEAR JEANNE: I have a favorite recipe from a local restaurant, but as you can see it is loaded with calories and fat. My husband and I are on low-fat, low-calorie and low-cholesterol diets, so could you convert this recipe for us?

-- LOIS, Marysville, Wash.

CHEESE-AND-HERB-STUFFED CHICKEN

2 eggs, lightly beaten

4 ounces cubed chicken

2 (3-ounce) packages cream cheese, softened

4 ounces grated Parmesan cheese

3 ounces chevre cheese

1/4 cup whipping cream

2 green onions, chopped

1 teaspoon dried oregano

1 ounce sun-dried tomatoes, finely chopped

2 ounces fresh chopped spinach

10 boneless chicken breast halves

Oil or butter

Combine eggs, chicken, cream, Parmesan and chevre cheeses and whipping cream in food processor. Process until smooth, remove and place in bowl.

Mix in green onions, oregano, tomatoes and spinach by hand, blending thoroughly. Set aside.

Cut pocket in each chicken breast by placing on flat surface and inserting sharp knife in large end of breast. Cut to within 1/2 inch of sides and end to form pocket. Do not cut entirely through small end of breast.

Using pastry bag fitted with 1/2-inch round tip, pipe cheese mixture into pockets. Arrange stuffed chicken breasts in baking pan, brush with oil or butter and bake at 375 degrees 20 to 30 minutes. Makes 10 servings.

DEAR LOIS: Part-skim ricotta cheese works well in place of the cream cheese, and I was able to replace the whipping cream with nonfat milk without an appreciable difference in the finished product. I also found I could eliminate the egg yolks.

-- JEANNE

LIGHTER CHEESE-AND-HERB-STUFFED CHICKEN

3 egg whites, lightly beaten

4 ounces uncooked chicken breast, cubed

3 ounces part-skim ricotta cheese

4 ounces Parmesan cheese, grated

3 ounces chevre cheese

1/4 cup nonfat milk

2 green onions, chopped

1 teaspoon dried oregano

1 ounce sun-dried tomatoes, finely chopped

2 ounces fresh spinach, chopped

10 boneless, skinned chicken breast halves

Combine egg whites, cubed chicken, ricotta, Parmesan and chevre cheeses and milk in food processor. Process until smooth, remove and place in bowl. Mix in green onions, oregano, tomatoes and spinach by hand, blending thoroughly. Set aside.

Cut pocket in each chicken breast by placing on flat surface and inserting sharp knife in large end of breast. Cut to within 1/2 inch of sides and end to form pocket. Do not cut entirely through small end of breast.

Fill each pocket with 1/3 cup cheese mixture. Arrange stuffed chicken breasts in baking pan and bake at 375 degrees 25 to 30 minutes. Makes 10 servings.

Each serving contains approximately:

Original Recipe:

415 calories

172 mg cholesterol

27 gm fat

443 mg sodium

Revised Recipe:

259 calories

97 mg cholesterol

8 gm fat

413 mg sodium
