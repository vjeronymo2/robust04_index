931208 FT 08 DEC 93 / Budgets vary for health authorities Health authority budgets in England are to rise by an average of 5.33 per cent next year, Mrs Virginia Bottomley, health secretary, announced yesterday. All 14 health regions will receive a real-terms increase but they will range from more than 2 per cent to as low as 0.7 per cent, particularly in London areas of falling population. Doctors in the north-west will get an increase of more than 10 per cent in real terms while those in East Anglia, Wessex, South Western and North-West Thames will be given a standstill budget.
