Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Sarajevo, 10 Jan (TANJUG) -- President of the Srpska republic Radovan Karadzic received in Pale today the newly appointed head of UNPROFOR for the former Yugoslavia, the Japanese diplomat Yasushi Akashi. Nikola Koljevic, the deputy president of the Srpska republic; Belgian General Francis Briquemont, UNPROFOR commander for the former Bosnia-Hercegovina; and Sergio Vieira de Mello, the UN secretary general's special envoy, also took part in the talks. After the talks, both sides expressed satisfaction and hope that today's constructive dialogue on humanitarian problems and the ways to resolve the Bosnian crisis would stimulate the peace process and speed up the efforts which are being made to ensure the respect of obligations which have been undertaken, particularly regarding the observations of cease-fires.
