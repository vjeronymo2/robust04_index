BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 28 (XINHUA) -- Negotiators from the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) and South Korea met today at the truce village of Panmunjom, starting the talks in preparations for a inter-Korean summit. If successful, the talks will set a time and place of the summit between DPRK President Kim Il-song and South Korean President Kim Yong-sam. The DPRK's delegation was headed by Kim Yong-son, chairman of the Reunification Policy Committee of the Supreme People's Assembly, while the South Korean chief negotiator is Deputy Prime Minister Yi Hong-ku.
