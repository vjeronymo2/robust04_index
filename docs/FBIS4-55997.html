BFN [Unattributed report: "Paramilitary Trial Interrupted"] [Text] The main hearing of the 10 Albanians, Macedonian [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] citizens, accused of forming paramilitary forces, started in Skopje, yesterday, with a proposition to abolish the imprisonment of suspects. Lawyers pointed out the court's right to hold them in custody has expired, which, as one lawyer stated, was anyhow opposed to the Macedonian constitution. Unless they are released, the defence threatened to demand disqualification of the District Court in Skopje, as well as of all other courts refusing to release the suspects. This proposal was rejected by the court with an explanation that the investigation procedure has been completed in due time, and the accusation has also been [words indistinct]. The hearing was interrupted after this, leaving time to the defence and the accused to discuss the demand for disqualification of the court. The next hearing is scheduled for May 30, and hearing of witnesses for June 7. Mr. Adolfo Ramirez, administrator of the U.S. Liaison Office in Skopje was also present at the hearing, as one of the suspects has double citizenship, Macedonian and American.
