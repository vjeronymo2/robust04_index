BFN [By Chang Hong: "Qian Seeks Immediate Cease-Fire in Yemen"] [Text] Vice-premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen yesterday called for an immediate cease-fire and peaceful settlement to the civil strife in Yemen. "China is seriously concerned with the worsening situation in Yemen and deeply grieved by the huge loss in lives and property there," he said. "We sincerely hope that both the South and the North can remain cool-headed, exercise restraint, stop fighting immediately and resolve their disputes through consultation and dialogue," he said, adding that China hopes domestic stability is quickly restored. Qian made the remarks yesterday in Beijing during a meeting with Fahim bin Sultan al-Qasimi, secretary-general of the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC). Qian said China hopes to continue to strengthen ties with the GCC adding that increased exchanges in recent years have been very beneficial. He also said the economies of China and Gulf countries are complementary and there is a bright future for cooperation. In the Gulf, Qian said China appreciates the GCC's efforts to maintain regional peace and stability. "China believes that the unsettled questions in the Gulf region have to be solved through consultation and dialogue among the parties concerned on the basis of the UN Security Council resolutions," he said. This will help quickly restore peace and stability and allow the war-torn countries to develop their national economies, improve peoples' livelihoods and cultivate regional co-operation, he said. Qian also said China has done a great deal and will continue to work with Gulf nations and the international community to this end. Al-Qasimi said the Gulf countries want to develop long-term and friendly relations with China, adding that the two share similar views on many world and regional issues. On Tuesday Qian met with former U.S. Secretary of State Henry Kissinger in Beijing. The two exchanged views on regional and global issues and Sino-US bilateral relations. After the meeting, Qian hosted a dinner in honour of Kissinger and his party. Chinese Vice-Foreign Minister Liu Huaqiu attended both occasions. Kissinger arrived in Beijing on Tuesday afternoon to participate in the International Symposium on the Strategy of China's Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation at the invitation of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Co-operation.
