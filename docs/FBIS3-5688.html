Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Tokyo NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 6 March Morning Edition] [Text] In a meeting held on 5 March in Osaka with businessmen in the Kansai area, Koshiro Ishida, director general of the Management and Coordination Agency, commented on the confusion among the ruling coalition parties and criticized the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ], saying, "The situation would have been different if the SDPJ--which is the largest ruling party--had put themselves in the position of Prime Minister Hosokawa." Regarding Chief Cabinet Secretary Takemura, Ishida stated: "We were moving toward the view that the Tax System Research Council will deal with the issues of revenue sources following tax reductions. I do not understand his opinion that it was a desultory view."
