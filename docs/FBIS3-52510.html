Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and Uzbekistan have expressed serious concern over the rising fundamentalism and cross-border terrorism. Addressing the joint press conference in New Delhi today, the visiting Uzbekistan president, Mr. Karimov, and the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, said these forces disrupt the democratic fabric of the nations and there is need to cooperate in tackling the vexed problem. Mr. Narasimha Rao said the discussions with the Uzbekistan president covered several matters, including bilateral cooperation and nonalignment. Both the leaders expressed the resolve to step up economic cooperation and trade between the two countries. Similar emphasis will also be given to strengthen ties in other fields, including culture. Mr. Karimov said that there is great scope for Indian enterprises to export consumer goods and set up joint ventures in many fields. He said of the 960 joint ventures now in operation in his country, only 14 are of Indo-Uzbek cooperation. Replying to questions, Mr. Karimov said that the Kashmir issue should be resolved through negotiations and without any external interference. He was also of the view that the complex Afghan problem should be tackled by the United Nations. Earlier, both sides signed six agreement to expand cooperation in several areas.
