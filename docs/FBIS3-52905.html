Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] Mr. John William Kachamila, Mozambique's minister of mineral resources, who is carrying a message by his country's president for President Hashemi-Rafsanjani, today met with First Vice President Hasan Habibi and called for the expansion of economic, industrial, political, and cultural relations with the Islamic Republic of Iran. In turn, Habibi expressed satisfaction over the success of the struggle of African nations against racism and said: The just struggle of the African people, particularly in the south, against racism has always been supported by Iran and now that there is a gradual solution to this internationally shameful problem in South Africa, we support the process of stability and peace in the region. The first vice president said the presence of the African nation of Mozambique in the Islamic Conference Organization was useful in creating better understanding between African and Islamic nations, and stressed the need for an exchange of views and experiences between the officials of the two countries aimed at expanding relations in various fields.
