940509 FT 09 MAY 94 / Union finds high level of worker dissatisfaction A high level of dissatisfaction with employers' treatment of their workers is recorded in a workplace opinion survey published today by the MSF general technical union. Nearly half the sample of MSF's workplace representatives said the treatment of staff in their workplace by management was either or poor or very poor compared with 11 per cent who thought it was good or better. The highest levels of dissatisfaction were among sales staff (65 per cent) and administrative and clerical (51 per cent). The survey also found that 37 per cent of employees feared their jobs were less secure than three months ago, with the highest level of anxiety about jobs being experienced in the financial services sector and the National Health Service. However, 28 per cent of MSF representatives described relations between the unions and employers as good or very good and 44 per cent said they were fair. By contrast, 28 per cent said relations were poor or very poor with 17 per cent reporting a deterioration in union/management relations. The survey was carried out for MSF by the independent organisation Quality Data Preparation. It covered representatives in 404 private and public-sector workplaces. A MORI poll conducted for the GMB general union identified concern over unemployment as the main factor in the way people voted in the recent local government elections. The survey of 501 people showed that for 69 per cent, policies on creating jobs and reducing unemployment had been crucial in determining their vote. Mr John Edmonds, GMB general secretary, said: 'Fear of unemployment is the iceberg that will sink this government.'
