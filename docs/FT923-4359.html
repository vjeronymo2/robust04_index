920907 FT 07 SEP 92 / Accountants must recognise public dissatisfaction ACCOUNTANTS must recognise public dissatisfaction with professional standards and act to repair the damage it has wrought, Mr Allan Denholm, president of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland, said at the weekend. He told the institute's annual conference in St Andrews that accountants should not be defensive and 'have no time for the rotten applies in their professional barrel'. He added, however, that the failures that brought accountancy into disrepute were 'very much the exception'.
