Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 28 KYODO -- The Democratic Socialist Party (DSP), one of the seven ruling parties, Monday [28 March] pledged to work toward gaining for Japan a permanent seat on the U.N. Security Council (UNSC). In its political platform for fiscal 1994, the DSP declared it would push for permanent membership in the 15-member council as well as give its wholehearted support to U.N. operations, including peacekeeping activities. The DSP also called for amendments in the Self-Defense Forces Law to institutionalize the SDF's contributions to U.N. peacekeeping operations as its main or sub-main task. The platform will be formalized at a party convention in April, party officials said.
