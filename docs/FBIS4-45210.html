BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on narcotics developments in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific monitored by Bangkok Bureau between 16 and 22 May 1994. Source information is given in parentheses after each item. SOUTHEAST ASIA

Burma
Defense Services Members Seize 2.204 Kg of Heroin in Pekon
Acting on a tip, members of the Defense Services Intelligence Unit and the regional battalion searched a house in Nyaunggon Ward, Pekon on 8 April. They found two people in the house with six packets of Double UO Globe brand heroin weighing 2.204 kg. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 13 Apr 94 p 7)
Philippines
Narcotics Command Burns Marijuana Plantation
A joint team led by agents of Narcotics Command Region 2 recently burned about 50 million pesos worth of marijuana plants on a 50 hectare site on Mount Chumanchil, Butbot village, Tinglayan town, Kalinga-Apayao Province. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 15 May 94 p AT-2)
Woman Arrested Smuggling Drugs Into Manila Jail
A drug organization inside the Manila city jail was broken up on 14 May when guards arrested Cecilia Nagateja, 34, as she tried to smuggle in a large volume of methamphetamine hydrochloride hidden in her underwear. The suspect said she had been delivering the drug to two inmates for several months. (Manila MANILA STANDARD in English 15 May 94 p 7)
Narcotics Command Arrests Suspect With Methamphetamine
Hydrochloride The Narcotics Command arrested Alfredo Bayot after he sold methamphetamine hydrochloride to an undercover agent. Bayot is thought to supply methamphetamine hydrochloride to patrons of five-star hotels in Manila and Pasay City. Two hundred grams of the drug were confiscated from the suspect. (Manila THE MANILA CHRONICLE in English 17 May 94 p 6)
Singapore
Narcotics Bureau Seizes Eight Kg of Heroin, Arrests Six
The Central Narcotics Bureau dismantled a "major drug syndicate" operating in Singapore when its officers arrested six people and seized nearly eight kg of heroin on 19 May. The suspects, aged between 24 and 42, are believed to have been operating since March. (Singapore Singapore Broadcasting Corporation in English 1100 GMT 19 May 94)
Thailand
Nigerian Arrested With Heroin; Second Incident Possibly
Related On 6 May, Nigerian national J.N. Okafor, 23, was arrested with 115 tubes of heroin weighing a total of 76 kg. The drugs were hidden inside 162 pairs of jeans found on the premises of a cargo forwarding company in Pom Prap Sattru Phai District, Bangkok. The company's management revealed that the narcotics were sent by a Nigerian man identified as Valentino. On 19 May, police found four sacks containing more than 93 tubes of heroin weighing about 33 kg at the same company. Police suspect that the two incidents are connected. (Bangkok BANGKOK POST in English 20 May 94 p 2)
