Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Revolutionary Front for the Promotion and Progress of Haiti, FRAPH, have called on ministers from the resigning government to refrain from going to their offices. This is their ultimatum. FRAPH is threatening to dislodge the ministers of the resigning Malval government. As of 28 March, the ministers are no longer to go to their offices. FRAPH has harshly criticized the management of the resigning government. During a news conference held by FRAPH leaders on 24 March, the most virulent criticism was aimed at the ministers of health, agriculture, and education. [passage omitted]. [Begin recording] [Emmanuel Constant, in Creole] We are asking that all the current ministers of Malval's resigning government stop going to work, as the parliament directed. We are very serious about this. We are asking all the ministers not to go to work on 28 March. [Louis Jodel Chambelin] If they do not obey, the Haitian people will come and remove them. And I hope that the next government arrests all the directors general and the ministers, so they can explain what they did with the Haitian people's money [applause] [end recording] Those were FRAPH leaders Emmanuel Constant and Louis Jodel Chambelain.
