Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Bratislava, Feb 15 (CTK) -- Slovak Deputy Premier Roman Kovac and Foreign Minister Jozef Moravcik have been expelled from the governing Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS), HZDS Spokeswoman Ivana Salczerova confirmed to CTK today. [passage omitted] Salczerova said she did not believe that the nine HZDS deputies who supported the Alternative of Political Realism established by Roman Kovac and Moravcik would be also expelled from the ruling movement. On February 12, the HZDS Presidium give local party leadership the task of conducting talks with the deputies who had supported the alternative on their attitude to the program of the HZDS. "The approach is quite different as far as deputies are concerned," she said. Three HZDS deputies -- Maria Vrablova, Jan Durana, and Ivan Jaros -- according to their own statement -- resigned their party functions in the leadership of the HZDS branch in Zilina, central Slovakia. According to unconfirmed information one more HZDS deputy, Vladimir Bajan, has also resigned.
