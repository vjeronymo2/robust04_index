BFN [Text] The newspaper KURDISTAN NWAY, in today's issue, quoted exclusive sources as saying that clashes have taken place between the Iraqi opposition forces and the regime's forces in and around Baghdad and in Iraq's southern areas. The regime's troops sustained casualties as a result. The paper noted that a fierce clash took place at midnight on 9 June in al-Thawrah city between a unit from the Iraqi opposition and security forces from the Baghdad regime. Various types of weapons were used in the clash, which lasted for approximately 30 minutes. Two policemen were killed as a result. On 11 June, a violent battle took place on the main road between al-Khalis and (al-Ba'kumah) cities. The battle later spread to the nearby fields [words indistinct]. The dictatorial regime used large-caliber weapons to shell the areas surrounding al-Khalis. Sounds of artillery shelling were heard in the [name indistinct]. A destroyed vehicle was seen on the main road and three bodies were found inside it. The paper added that extensive battles between Iraqi opposition and regime troops took place in the al-Salam and al-Maymuna marshes of al-'Amarah Governorate on 11 June. The dictatorial forces sustained severe casualties.
