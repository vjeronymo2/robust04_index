Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] The proceedings of the extraordinary congress of the National Liberal Party [PNL], convened in Bucharest by the initiative committee for the organization of the congress, continued this afternoon with the presentation of the report of the commission for the amendment of by-laws and voting on the proposals. The new bylaws adopted by the majority stipulate that the party be led by a president elected by the congress and a National Council consisting of 41 members, 21 of whom must be elected by the congress, and the remaining 20 to be appointed by the party president from among the chiefs of departments and counselors and submitted to the Permanent Delegation for endorsement. The representatives of county organizations and Bucharest Municipality proposed a single candidate for the position of president, namely Radu Campeanu, who was elected with a majority of votes. At this time, the congress proceedings are continuing with the presentation and submitting of candidates for positions within the party's component bodies, our reporter Iulia Gherman informed us.
