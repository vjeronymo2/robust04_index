941224 FT 24 DEC 94 / Weekend Money (Companies): Directors' dealings The largest transaction to be reported was in The Telegraph, writes Vivien MacDonal of the Inside Track. Its chairman Conrad Black has carried out transactions as Hollinger. This has meant his dealings have come, rather unusually, under the heading of a major shareholder rather than a director. Since the share price crashed in the summer after the cover price of the paper had been cut, The Telegraph group has actually out-performed the market by a margin of almost 4 per cent. Black has been buying quite consistently at the lower price level - but he still has some way to go to buy back the 12.5m shares he sold in May. Computer People, the company that places information technology specialists, has been a star performer in the market since 1992. With very little to check its progress it has seen its share price out-perform by a factor of 144 per cent in the past twelve months. Much of the stock in the company is held by the directors. The largest sale of the week was recorded when one of these directors, Anthony Lambie, sold 200,000 shares at 233p, about 10 per cent of his holding. This leaves him with more than 1.7m shares and, given the performance, would represent a more than reasonable profit take. -----------------------------------------------------------------------
