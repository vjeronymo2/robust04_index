BFN [By 'Awdah 'Awdah] [Text] Amman--The Embassy of the state of Palestine in Amman has said that it has been decided to return the second group of Palestinian deportees, which includes 18 deportees, to their homeland today, Wednesday [27 April]. Khalid Mismar, the embassy's media official, told AL-RA'Y that seven of these deportees will return through the Rafah crossing point at the Palestinian-Egyptian border. They include 'Ata Abu- Kursah, 'Abd-al-Mun'im al-'Ataya, Fathi Hajjaj, 'Abd-al-Fattah Ziyadah, 'Isam al-Agha, Fayiz al-Shurbaji, and 'Ali al-Sha'ir. Mismar added that the rest of the returning deportees, which include 11 deportees, will return through King Husayn bridge. They will gather in front of the Embassy of the state of Palestine in Amman today. They include Muhammad 'Abd-al-Jalil al-Mutawwar, Jamal Dhiyab Abu-Latifah, Muhammad Sa'id Midyah, Salim Ahmad Baryush, Jamal 'Abdallah Jibarah, 'Uthman Muhammad Dawud, Hani Muhammad Khulub, 'Isam Amin al-Dib'i, Bahjat Mustafa al-Jayyusi, Rashid Abu-Bakr, and Nur-al-Din 'Ali al-Ja'bari. The first group of deportees returned to the occupied territories on 6 April after talks between the Palestinian and Israeli delegations in Cairo.
