940120 FT 20 JAN 94 / Cold hits US business Bitter Arctic weather closed factories, businesses and government offices across the US midwest and Atlantic seaboard yesterday, with the disruption expected to continue today, writes Martin Dickson in New York. In Washington DC all non-essential federal employees were sent home at 3pm and told to remain there today. The Securities and Exchange Commission has closed until tomorrow. The Commerce Department said it still planned to release December housing starts data today, while the Labour Department is trying to ensure the release of weekly jobless claims. General Motors, Ford, Chrysler, Nissan, and Honda shut assembly plants in Tennessee, Ohio, Kentucky, and Missouri.
