Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres Tuesday [1 March] had a telephone talk with his Russian counterpart Andrey Kozyrev and informed him of the measures taken by Tel Aviv for strengthening the security of the Palestinians and the order on the occupied territories following the tragedy in Hebron, Interfax was told by the spokesman for the Russian Foreign Ministry Grigoriy Karasin. He said that in response to the earlier request by the Russian Minister, Peres voiced consent to discuss the proposals by PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat about the international presence to help solve the problem. It was said that the joint search for ways to overcome the current crisis would be continued. In line with it, the special envoy of the Russian President, First Deputy Russian Foreign Minister Igor Ivanov will leave for Israel after the talks with the Palestinian leadership in Tunisia. The explosion on Sunday in a Catholic Church in the suburbs of Beirut indicates that the extremist forces in the region do not give up attempts to frustrate the national reconciliation in Lebanon and block the Middle East peace process, Grigoriy Karasin has told Interfax. He said that following the criminal act in Hebron, where an Israeli fanatic shot at believers at a mosque, another tragedy came. As a result of an explosion during the Sunday mass, dozens of civilian Lebanese were killed or wounded. Russia expresses deep condolences to the families of the victims, said Karasin. He said that the new tide of violence in the Middle East must be stopped and the region and its future must be protected against the encroachments of extremists.
