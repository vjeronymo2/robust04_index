Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The foreign ministers of the Damascus Declaration states will resume meetings in Damascus this morning. These ministers began meetings yesterday evening. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' led the Syrian delegation, Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa led the Egyptian delegation, and Saudi Foreign Minister His Highness Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal led the Saudi delegation. The Kuwaiti delegation was led by Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad al-Jabir al-Sabah, deputy prime minister and foreign minister; the United Arab Emirates delegation was led by Foreign Minister Rashid Bin-'Abdallah; the Omani delegation was led by Yusuf Bin-'Alawi Bin-'Abdallah, minister of state for foreign affairs; the Qatari delegation was led by Foreign Minister Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Al Thani; and the Bahraini delegation was led by Foreign Minister Muhammad Bin-Mubarak Al Khalifah. Shaykh Fahim Bin-Sultan al-Qasimi, secretary general of the Gulf Cooperation Council, attended the meetings. Following a short break, the heads of delegations held a closed meeting. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' opened the meetings of the Damascus Declaration foreign ministers with a speech in which he reviewed the Declaration's march. He also reviewed the scopes of cooperation and coordination among the Declaration states and the difficulties facing this Declaration. He focused on studying the practical steps that will activate the Declaration's principles and working out a plan for the coming phase to circumvent the obstacles preventing the implementation of the Declaration's articles. Al-Shar' also stressed the need to enhance the economic and political cooperation among the Declaration states. He also pointed to political, regional, and international issues, particularly developments of the peace process in light of the anticipated Geneva summit between President Hafiz al-Asad and U.S. President Bill Clinton. The Damascus Declaration foreign ministers expressed profound interest in both this summit and its results which will definitely be reflected on the overall situation in the region. Following the first session of the meetings of the Damascus Declaration foreign ministers, which ended yesterday evening, Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa made a statement to reporters. He said that the meetings' atmosphere was extremely good. Emphasis was laid on implementing this Declaration and continuing the consultations and joint action, he said. Discussions were held on several matters that may put the Damascus Declaration into effect and help the Arab situation, he added.
