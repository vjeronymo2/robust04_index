Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, January 31 (XINHUA) -- The Shanghai branch of the People's Construction Bank of China today used export credits totaling 30 million U.S. dollars from four foreign banks to speed up the growth of the local chemical industry. An official of the branch said that two loans -- 8.31 million dollars and 7.94 million dollars -- will be extended by U.S. banks. Another loan of 7.82 million deutsche marks will come from a German bank and still another loan of one billion Japanese yen from a Japanese bank. The official said that part of the foreign loans will be used for the construction of the Shanghai coking and chemical plant. When completed at the end of this year, the coking and chemical plant will be able to produce 1.7 million cubic meters of gas daily, enough to supply 600,000 households, he said. The plant will be be built at a total cost of 1.7 billion yuan and 70 million U.S. dollars. The official said that the rest of the foreign credits will be used to built an acrylic fiber plant, designed to turn out 20,000 tons annually, in the Shanghai Jinyang Acrylic Fiber Factory. The factory went into operation in March 1993, involving a total investment of 100 million U.S. dollars, he said. He said that to date, his branch has extended a total of 175 million U.S. dollars in foreign loans to the coking and chemical plant and the Jinyang factory.
