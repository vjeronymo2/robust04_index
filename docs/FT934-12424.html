931027 FT 27 OCT 93 / EC joins US in push on services THE European Community is to join the US push to make Asian and Latin American countries open their financial services markets more widely as the Uruguay Round of trade liberalisation talks comes to a climax. Mr Lawrence Summers, the US Treasury undersecretary in charge of international affairs, said yesterday the EC had agreed to follow the US example by sending high-level officials to several emerging markets in an effort to 'help unblock the Geneva logjam.' Mr Summers said the Clinton administration's decision to back legislation authorising restrictions on the expansion of banks and financial service companies from countries which do not allow equal access to US businesses would serve as a lever to secure additional concessions in the sector before the conclusion of the Uruguay round. 'The offers from many of the participants in the negotiations are simply not good enough,' he said.
