940120 FT 20 JAN 94 / Observer: Risky business Dashiell Hammett must be spinning in his grave. Preventing Homicide in the Workplace, published by the US National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health, has demolished the myth that being a gumshoe is the world's most dangerous profession. Taxi drivers run the greatest risk of being murdered at work. Between 1980 and 1989 the average annual murder rate of cab drivers was 27 in 100,000; most were shot. The next most perilous jobs were liquor store workers and petrol station attendants. Private detectives came a rather lame fourth.
