March 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MICRO ... : HAM MAKES EASTER MEAL A SNAP

Ready-to-eat hams microwave superbly and require very little effort from the cook, which makes preparing the traditional Easter meal a snap.

Any kind of commercial ham will do. And you can glaze it or not, as you desire. But bear these points in mind.

Trim the ham of all but 1/8 inch of exterior fat. Fat attracts microwaves, melts rapidly and produces an abundance of drippings that must be ladled off. Further, a heavy coating of fat will cause the lean areas directly underneath it to overcook.

Good Spice Choices

If you like, rub a ham with pepper and herbs before it goes into the microwave. Sage, thyme, dill weed, crushed caraway or fennel seeds, marjoram and oregano are all good choices.

Always use a microwave-safe meat thermometer -- or if your oven has one, a temperature probe -- when cooking ham so you can pull it from the oven the instant the proper internal temperature is reached.

To keep shank ends and the top edges of hams from drying, shield carefully with aluminum foil as the following recipe directs.

GLAZED HAM

1 (4- to 6-pound) bone-in, butt or shank-portion ham or semi-boneless ready-to-eat ham or fully cooked, smoked picnic shoulder

1/2 cup light brown sugar, packed

2 tablespoons prepared spicy brown mustard

1 tablespoon cider vinegar

Raisin Sauce, optional

Trim outer layer of fat on ham to 1/8 inch. Tie ham with string (to keep fat from separating as ham cooks) and place on microwave-safe rack in shallow microwave-safe bake/roast pan. If ham wobbles, prop up with crumpled wax paper.

Smooth sheet of plastic wrap against cut surface of ham, then mold 1- to 2-inch foil strip smoothly over upper edge where fat joins lean to deflect microwaves and prevent overcooking. (If cooking shank-portion ham, foil-wrap shank end.)

Insert microwave-safe meat thermometer through plastic wrap in center of largest muscle not touching fat or bone. Or, use automatic temperature probe according to manufacturer's directions. Or, test ham with instant-register thermometer as it approaches doneness.

Microwave ham, uncovered, on HIGH (100% power) 5 minutes. Reduce power to MEDIUM (50% power) and microwave 7 to 10 minutes per pound.

At half time, invert ham and rotate pan 180 degrees. Remove foil but not plastic wrap. Continue microwaving until thermometer registers 130 degrees. If desired, score fat, criss-cross fashion, 10 minutes before ham is done.

Drain off all drippings, reserving 1/4 cup for Raisin Sauce. Remove plastic wrap. Combine brown sugar, mustard and vinegar and spread over ham. Microwave, uncovered, on MEDIUM 5 minutes.

Remove ham from oven, tent with foil and let stand 10 minutes. Serve as is or accompany with Raisin Sauce. Makes 6 to 10 servings.

Raisin Sauce

1/4 cup pan dripping (or drippings plus enough bacon drippings to total 1/4 cup)

1/4 cup flour

2 cups hot water

1/3 cup raisins

1/4 cup medium-dry Madeira, Sherry or Port

Liquid gravy browner, optional

Salt, pepper

Microwave drippings in wax-paper-covered 6-cup measure on HIGH (100% power) 40 to 50 seconds.

Blend in flour and microwave, uncovered, on HIGH 7 to 10 minutes, stirring every 3 minutes, until light brown. Mixture will look grainy but will smooth out once liquid is added.

Pour water into drained ham pan and scrap up brown bits. Whisk liquid carefully into hot roux being careful of spattering. Microwave, uncovered, on HIGH 3 1/2 to 4 minutes, whisking at half time, until sauce thickens and boils.

Mix in raisins and Madeira and microwave, uncovered, on MEDIUM-LOW (30% power) 4 to 5 minutes, until raisins are plump. Mix in gravy browner and salt and pepper to taste. Makes 2 1/2 cups.

Note: In ovens of less than 600 watts, increase cooking times about 15%.
