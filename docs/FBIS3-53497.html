Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Khartoum, 16 Jan (SUNA) -- Chinese vice premier and foreign minister, Qian Qichen, and the accompanying delegation left Khartoum Saturday evening [15 January] after two-day talks with senior government officials on issues of common concern and means of boosting the Sino-Sudanese relations for the interest of the two countries. In a press statement to Sudan the industry and commerce minister, Dr. Taj al-Sirr Mustafa ['Abd-al-Salam] said that the economic and technical talks between the two sides covered the bilateral cooperation in the strategic economy, basic services, social developmental projects, and the investment and commercial fields. Dr. Mustafa stated that the Sudanese side has affirmed its readiness to expand cooperation with the Chinese public and private sectors in the field of investment adding that the Chinese delegation also expressed a similar desire. The Chinese side asserted that its visit to Sudan was intended to consolidate further the distinguished ties between the Sudan and China, he said. The industry and commerce minister also indicated that the Chinese side agreed on holding the Sino-Sudanese ministerial committee's meeting this year saying that the Chinese side declared it's commitment to boost implementation of the outcome of the joint economic and technical committee's meeting.
