940714 FT 14 JUL 94 / Arts: Today's Television A strong evening for factual programmes. In The Business David Lomax reports on the economic success of Taiwan and discovers that education plays a major role in the country's expansion: the constitution requires 15 per cent of the government budget to be spent on education (7.30 BBC2). That is followed by another in the Hypotheticals series in which role playing is used to investigate some hypothetical conundrum. Today's subject: patient confidentiality (8.00 BBC2). A new series of QED begins by investigating the recent incidence of streptococcus A which gave rise to the 'Killer bug ate my face' stories in the tabloid papers (8.00 BBC1). If the other programmes in the State Of The Ark series are as effective as the opening episode, this will be a very strong series indeed. Today's programme tries to establish what constitutes a 'good' zoo (9.30 BBC2). Previous efforts by Nigel Planer to satirise aspects of showbusiness have tended to be heavy handed. Tonight he begins a sitcom, Wake Up With .. in which he plays co-host on one of those daytime sofa shows. Encouragingly the writer is Andrew Nickolds (8.30 ITV).
