940409 FT 09 APR 94 / Economic Diary: Forward Events TOMORROW: Israel and PLO are expected to resume peace talks. Run-off round of Ukrainian parliamentary elections. MONDAY: Credit business (February). Cable and Satellite '94 conference and exhibition open at Olympia (until Wednesday). TUESDAY: US producer price index (March). Meeting in Marrakesh ahead of the signing of the Gatt Uruguay Round trade agreement. European Union fisheries ministers meet in Luxembourg. Building Societies hold two-day conference in London. WEDNESDAY: Index of production and construction for Wales (fourth quarter). Survey of Agriculture: December 1993 results for Wales. US consumer price index (March); retail sales (March) and real earnings (March). Mr Yasser Arafat, chairman of the Palestine Liberation Organisation, to address Council of Europe's parliamentary assembly. Original deadline for completion of Israeli troop withdrawal from occupied Gaza Strip and the West Bank town of Jericho. Preliminary results from RMC Group and Savoy Hotel. THURSDAY: Machine tools (February). Balance of visible trade (January). US business inventories (February). International Energy Agency holds seminar in Kyoto. Mr George Bush, former US president, visits Singapore to deliver a keynote address at a Citibank seminar on 'opportunities and obstacles facing the region this decade and beyond' at the National University of Singapore. Bundesbank council meets. FRIDAY: Usable steel (March). Retail prices index (March). Capital issues and redemptions (March). UK economic accounts (fourth quarter). US industrial production (March). Leaders of the Commonwealth of Independent States hold summit meeting in Moscow. Informal meeting of European Union tourism ministers in Santorini (until April 16). First session of the newly-elected Italian parliament. Anglo-Austrian Forum in London. Speakers include Mr Douglas Hurd, foreign secretary, and Mr Eddie George, governor of the Bank of England.
