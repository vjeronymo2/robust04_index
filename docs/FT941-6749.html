940301 FT 01 MAR 94 / World Commodities Prices: Tea The Tea Broker's Association reports, landed very strong demand. Bright East Africans sold well and often appreciated several pence. Coloury medium teas met keen competition and prices advanced 5 to 15p and sometimes more. Plainer descriptions were firm to dearer. Brighter ceylons gained up to 15p, others moved 2 to 4p higher. Offshore strong demand. Brighter teas were 4 to 8p dearer while plainer types remained fully firm. Quality 195p/kg nom, good medium 145p/kg, medium 125p/kg, low medium 86p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 215p for a Rwanda pf. 1.
