BFN [Text] The International Monetary Fund has extended $30 million to the Kazakh oil and gas amalgamation Mangistaumunaigaz to be used for the reconstruction of the oil-production system in Uzen (western Kazakhstan). Experts told the Oil Information Agency that the IMF credit would be invested in the construction of a facility for collecting oil leakage and oil-industry wastes, and for processing them into quality gasoline. Kazakhstan's Department for Environmental Protection and Biological Resources reports that more than 16,000 square meters of fertile soil has been polluted in Uzen. The oil pools are polluting the air with hydrogen sulphide, whose content exceeds the admissible level.
