Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yokohama, March 20 KYODO -- Two new faces Sunday [20 March] filed as candidates to challenge the incumbent in the April 3 mayoral election in Yokohama, Japan's second most populous city. The three men who filed candidacy papers were incumbent mayor Hidenobu Takahide, 64, and challengers Toshihiko Izu, 67, and Takashi Nomura, 40. All three are independents. Takahide, who is seeking a second four-year term of office, has gained the backing of the Liberal Democratic Party, the Social Democratic Party, Komeito, Shinseito (Japan Renewal Party) and the Democratic Socialist Party. Izu, a professor emeritus at Yokohama City University, is backed by the Japanese Communist Party, while Nomura, a former Home Affairs Ministry bureaucrat, has garnered support from new party Sakigake and the United Social Democratic Party. Yokohama, the largest international port city in Japan, has a population of 3.3 million, ranking second after Tokyo which has a population of more than eight million. The challengers have accused Takahide of having received a huge amount of election campaign funds from general contractors. Takahide denies the allegation.
