BFN [Text] Carlos Lage, secretary of the Executive Committee of the Council of Ministers, presented to participants of the Nation and the Emigre Community Conference an analysis on the current situation of the economy, and its prospects. Lage's presentation brought about an exchange of ideas by Cuban authorities and the more than 200 residents from abroad who are attending the forum. The Nation and the Emigre Community Conference will conclude today at 2030 GMT and according to Miguel Alfonso, spokesman for the conference, the debate has retained an elevated tone of respect. Especially notable was the positive attitude toward the fatherland. The meeting was inaugurated on Friday with speeches by Ricardo Alarcon and Abel Prieto, presidents of the National Assembly of the People's Government and the National Union of Cuban Writers and Artists, respectively, and by Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina. Among the decisions adopted during the conference with the emigre community were: continuation of contacts and exchanges to promote the normalization of relations, creation of an office to take care of emigration issues, and creation of a specialized magazine for that sector. The elimination of the five-year waiting period for those who abandoned the country to legally return, and abolition of the requirement for them to obtain reservations in hotels, were other measures discussed. Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina also informed participants at the conference of the government's willingness to facilitate cultural links reaffirming their Cuban nature. The meeting was devised by our country in an attempt to identify common problems between the nation and the emigre community and to promote solutions to those problems.
