940818 FT 18 AUG 94 / Row over job at Yorkshire Water A leading corporate governance consultancy is advising its clients, who have more than Pounds 20bn under management, to support the controversial election of a former industry regulator to the board of Yorkshire Water. The nomination of Mrs Diana Scott, until recently head of Yorkshire Water Services Committee - a customer committee part of water regulator Ofwat - has been opposed by Yorkshire's directors. Mrs Scott's nomination coincided with her expressing concerns over price rises and the quality of customer service being provided by the company. The decision of Mr Ian Byatt, director-general of Ofwat, not to reappoint Mrs Scott as head of the Yorkshire customer committee came after she criticised water company plans to extend household metering. PIRC, the consultancy which advises leading local authority and several private pension fund investors, says: 'The issues which Mrs Scott has raised have serious implications for the company's relations with customers, the regulators and the community, and thus ultimately on shareholder value.' According to PIRC, Mrs Scott suggests that 'deficiencies in the management of Yorkshire Water have resulted in above-average levels of complaints'. In a circular to shareholders, Yorkshire Water said: 'The directors do not consider that Mrs Scott has the business experience necessary for the corporate governance or strategic direction of such a company or group.'
