December 1, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

COLLEGE BASKETBALL / WOMENS' ROUNDUP;

LESLIE SPARKS TROJANS IN VICTORY

Lisa Leslie, despite foul trouble that kept her on the bench for about half the game, scored 16 points and grabbed eight rebounds as the Trojans beat Old Dominion, 72-61, in the first round of the Roger White Invitational basketball tournament Friday at Northwestern University at Evanston, Ill.

"In the second half, we stuck to our game plan," said Coach Marianne Stanley, who had a particular reason to make a good impression.

She came to the Trojans (2-0) after coaching at Old Dominion.

"They had a good game plan in wanting to keep the ball away from Lisa. She didn't really get into her game with quick fouls."

Tammy Story had 18 points and four assists for the No. 24-ranked Trojans.

USC plays 14th-ranked Northwestern, a 74-52 winner over Syracuse, in tonight's championship game.

UCLA 89, Notre Dame 75 -- Rehema Stephens tied a personal record with 33 points, 22 in the second half, to lead the Bruins (1-3) in their home opener at Pauley Pavilion.

DeDe Mosman added 20 points and Nicole Anderson had 10 for UCLA, which led 69-68 with 5:28 to play and went on a 16-2 run over the next four minutes.
