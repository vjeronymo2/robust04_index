BFN [Text] Beijing, 12 May (XINHUA) -- The eight-day-long Seventh Meeting of the Eighth National People's Congress [NPC] Standing Committee closed at the Great Hall of the People this morning after it voted in five laws and approved appointments and removals by vote. Chairman Qiao Shi presided at this morning's meeting. The following are the five laws adopted one-by-one by vote at this meeting: The Foreign Trade Law, the State Compensation Law, the NPC Standing Committee Decision on Amendments to the Regulations Governing Offenses Against Public Order, the NPC Standing Committee Decision on Amendments to the Chinese People's Liberation Army [PLA] Regulations Governing the Military Service of Officers in Active Duty, and the NPC Standing Committee Decision on PLA Regulations Governing Officers' Ranks [title as received]. State President Jiang Zemin today signed separate presidential decrees, Nos. 22, 23, 24, 25, and 26, to promulgate these five laws. The Foreign Trade Law consists of eight chapters covering 44 articles, including: general principles, foreign trade operators, import and export of cargoes and technological exports, international service trades, foreign trade orders, promotion of foreign trade, legal responsibility, and addenda. This law will take effect on 1 July 1994. The State Compensation Law consists of six chapters covering 35 articles, including: general principles, administrative compensation, criminal compensation, compensation methods and standards for calculation, other stipulations, and addendum. This law will take effect on 1 January 1995. The meeting decided by vote to add 13 people, including Wang Yingwei [3769 5391 0251], as members to the Preliminary Working Committee of the Preparatory Committee of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, and appointed Nie Li [5119 0500] a member of the NPC Internal and Judicial Affairs Committee. The meeting also adopted other lists of appointments and removals. A motion made to the NPC Standing Committee by the State Council to appoint three ministers was adopted at the meeting today: The meeting voted to relieve Zhu Xun of his duties as minister of geology and mineral resources, and appoint Song Ruixiang to the post; to relieve Ai Zhisheng of his duties as minister of radio, cinema, and television, and appoint Sun Jiazheng to the post; to relieve Lu Peijian of his duties as auditor general of the Auditing Administration, and appoint Gu Zhenqian to the post. The meeting made preliminary examination of and deliberated on a report on progress made in preparations for the Three Gorges Project. Attending the meeting were Vice Chairmen Wang Hanbin, Ni Zhifu, Chen Muhua, Fei Xiaotong, Sun Qimeng, Lei Jieqiong, Qin Jiwei, Li Ximing, Wang Bingqian, Wang Guangying, Cheng Siyuan, Lu Jiaxi, Buhe, Tomur Dawamat, Li Peiyao, and Wu Jieping, and Secretary General Cao Zhi. State Councillor Li Tieying; Ren Jianxin, Supreme People's Court president; and Zhang Siqing, procurator general of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, attended the meeting as observers.
