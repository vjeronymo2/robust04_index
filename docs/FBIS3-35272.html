Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 22 Feb (MENA)--Extensive security campaigns during the past 24 hours in the slum districts organized by al-Jizah security department have resulted in arresting around 700 people who are suspected of various political and criminal violations. Brigadier General Sayyid Farid, director of the General Department for al-Jizah Investigations, said that the storming of the slum areas also resulted in the arrest of 145 fugitive extremists from Upper Egypt. The security forces also seized a number of firearms, including three machine guns. He said that within the framework of instructions from Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi on the need to extensively search for activists, during this campaign, in which most of the security organs participated, the security forces succeeded in resolving many cases, including 15 drug cases of various kinds, and in shutting down numerous drug dens. The search forces -- led by Brigadier General 'Abd-al-Wahhab Khalil, head of the Investigations Department -- succeeded in arresting 48 fugitives who were fleeing various sentences for crimes they had committed. They also arrested a number of gangs specializing in robbing houses and stores in all parts of al-Jizah. The director of the General Department for Investigations noted that the defendants confessed to their crimes and have been referred to the prosecution for interrogation and investigation.
