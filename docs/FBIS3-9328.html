Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 22 (CTK) -- Journalists estimate that around 40,000 people attended today's demonstration called by the Bohemian and Moravian Chamber of Trade Unions (CMKOS) today at 12:00 PM in Prague's Old Town Square to warn the government and parliament that "the situation of employees and pensioners is becoming unbearable." The demonstration was addressed by CMKOS Chairman Vladimir Petrus, who said that the unions could not be accused of extremism. The unionists merely considered it their responsibility to point out that "dissatisfaction of the people" threatened. They were not therefore going against parliament, but they asked that the government and parliament "showed social perceptiveness." At the end of the demonstration the participants approved appeals to the government and to parliamentary deputies, which states that the union members "consider it very dangerous when even government bills contain attempts to transfer the burden of essential economic reforms on to those least able to protect themselves." It asks ministers that the amendment to the labor code and the basis of the law on pension insurance be not put before parliament until they are properly prepared and take into account the union members' comments. In the appeal addressed to deputies, the union members point to their demands concerning the making of repeated contracts, the position of pregnant women and mothers with small children, the increase in the pension age, the management of money set aside for pension insurance and the ban on trade union activity in the civil service. The demonstrators were also greeted by General Secretary of the International Confederation of Free Trade Unions, Enzo Friso, who assured them of the confederation's support. [sentence as received] Concrete support was expressed by the Polish trade union movement Solidarity, which managed to drive radio cars with loudspeakers to Prague overnight, after the demonstration's organizers had not been able to get hold of any in the Czech Republic. Czech Minister of Employment and Social Affairs Jindrich Vodicka also came to the Old Town Square, but he was for the most part greeted with whistles and various displays of indignation. Union members came to Prague from all over the Czech Republic in 400 buses, and filled most of the square.
