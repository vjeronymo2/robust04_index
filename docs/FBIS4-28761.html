BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 14 June Morning Edition] [Text] A top Ministry of Foreign Affairs official said on 13 June: "If North Korea accepts inspections without fail, we can consider that [economic assistance]." He was commenting on a reported remark by DPRK (North Korean) President Kim Il-song that if cooperation was obtained in the DPRK's normalization of relations with the United States, and assistance was provided for its efforts to convert [existing nuclear reactors] to light water reactors, North Korea would cooperate with International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) inspections. The official said that if North Korea accepted full inspections, it would be possible for Japan to consider economic aid.
