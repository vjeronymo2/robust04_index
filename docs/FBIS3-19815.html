Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Report by Daria Gorodisky: "Fini: Split in North Is Bossi's Fault"] [Excerpts] Milan -- "If the anti-Occhetto [secretary of the Democratic Party of the Left, PDS] front is split in the north, the fault lies with the lamentable selfishness not of the League, but of [Northern League Secretary Umberto] Bossi, who refused to accept the AN [National Alliance] in the pole of freedoms." Speaking in Milan yesterday, [AN leader] Gianfranco Fini did not tire of saying so to the crowd that filled piazza del Duomo (almost 20,000 people), and to reporters, both Italian and foreign, curious to observe this new Italian Right. [passage omitted] Fini is convinced that a large proportion of League voters will support the AN this time and that in any case, "since the League is a regional phenomenon, it cannot impose conditions." But Fini wanted to discuss other matters. He was keen to stress that the national and social Right is ready to govern: "This is the only new factor in Italian politics, whereas the Left is an exacerbated repetition of what has been done for the past 30 years." We must beware, therefore, because "politics has been dominated by the Center-Left for the past 30 years. If Occhetto wins, we will have a Left-Center coalition government." The danger, Fini said, comes not from the Right, but from Occhetto's men (who, in the guise of progressives, in fact "favor the major industrial groups to the detriment of small and medium enterprises. There can be no economic development under the PDS") and "from the specters of [Mino] Martinazzoli's [secretary of the Italian Popular Party, PPI] and [Pact for Italy leader] Mario Segni's center, who want to help to establish a government of left-wing forces." The MSI [Italian Social Movement] secretary stressed that this center is "ambiguous because it has sought unacceptable allies such as La Malfa [Italian Republican Party Secretary]. We thought had disappeared without trace, like former Prime Minister Amato, champion of antisocial policies, and that Jurassic relic of the First Republic that answers to the name of Zanone [Italian Liberal Party]. We may yet have to report them to the police for attempting to restore the five-party alliance." In the evening, however, he added in Genoa that "poor Martinazzoli is aware now that he is bound to emerge the loser and, at the same time as he rules out an accord with the Right, he shows that he has already decided to reach an agreement with the Left." With regard to the election results, Fini is sure that the AN "will go well beyond 10 percent." And if the pole of freedoms were to win, could Bossi-Attila ("who is still shouting about corruption, but who has taken bribes, even though he blames it on some idiot just passing through") become crucial to the formation of a government? "This, on two conditions," Fini said: "clarity on the issue of national unity and identity and on the duties of a policy of solidarity between north and south and between the strong and weak sectors of society."
