941010 FT 10 OCT 94 / International Company News: Polish insurer plans share issue Warta, Poland's second largest insurer with 14 per cent of the market, is planning a new stock issue later this month accompanied by a flotation on the Warsaw Stock Exchange. Warta has 2.6m shares of which 46 per cent are owned by the state. The rest are owned by private investors including Elektrim, the listed telecommunications and power equipment trader, with 15 per cent. Warta employees are being offered between 100,000 and 260,000 new shares priced at 400,000 zlotys each. The general public is being offered between 700,000 and 1m shares with a minimum price of 800,000 zlotys. The offer which starts on October 21 should raise a minimum of 6,000bn zlotys (Dollars 26m) in new funds and could go over the 9,200 zlotys mark if all the shares on offer are taken up. Currently Warta's total capital and reserves are worth 4,338bn zlotys while the company reported a 50.2bn zlotys net profit for the first six months of this year. Last year's net profit was 169.7bn zlotys. The flotation will mark the first insurance company listing on the Warsaw Stock Exchange, which trades 34 stocks capitalised at 87,853bn zlotys.
