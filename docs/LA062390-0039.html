June 23, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

THE DONALDS . . . OOPS, MAKE THAT DODGERS, JUST ASKING FOR A FALL

I have a suggestion for Fred Claire, which would be a $3-million-a-year gamble.

At this time, Kansas City would probably love nothing more than to get rid of their multimillionaire bust, Mark Davis, who is booed every time he goes on the mound. The Dodgers, meanwhile, are desperate for relief pitching. Maybe Davis can regain his Cy Young form by returning to the National League.

FRANK PEARLSTEIN

Sherman Oaks
