920807 FT 07 AUG 92 / World Trade News: ABB in Venezuelan deal ABB Asea Brown Boveri, the Swedish-Swiss engineering group, said its Lummus Crest subsidiary in the US had won a Dollars 270m (Pounds 141m) turnkey contract to engineer and build a linear polyethylene plant for Petroquimica de Venezuela. The plant will use Dupont Canada's proprietary Sclairtech technology to make a wide range of linear resin grades and have a capacity of 150,000 metric tons a year. It is set to begin commercial operation in the first quarter of 1994.
