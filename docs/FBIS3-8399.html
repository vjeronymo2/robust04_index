Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Gdansk, March 8--Eighty steel mills on Tuesday took part in the Solidarity-organised protest action, according to a communique issued by the Solidarity union national protest action headquarters. The communique, signed by Piotr Zak, says that Solidarity on Tuesday organised a two-hour warning in 69 steel and iron enterprises, and raised money for the strike fund in eleven enterprises. On Wednesday, two-hour warning strikes are to be held in the Silesia-Dabrowa and Lodz regions. Five more enterprises in the Koszalin region will follow suit of four enterprises in the province which staged strikes on Tuesday. Solidarity railwaymen will stage strikes on March 11. Freight and passenger traffic will be stopped between 9 am and 19 pm on Friday. The communique claims that the union has not received from the government "any proposal of negotiations."
