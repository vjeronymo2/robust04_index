941104 FT 04 NOV 94 / UK Company News: Out of Africa and into battle Nov 27 1917: Born Roland Walter Fuhrhop, in India; educated at Churchers public school, Petersfield. 1948: Left for Africa. 1961: Invited to run London & Rhodesian Mining & Land Company, became Lonrho. 1973: Described by Edward Heath as 'the unacceptable face of capitalism.' 1973: Boardroom revolt; Rowland fights dismissal via High Court, becomes sole MD. 1977-78: Lonrho builds 29.9 per cent stake in House of Fraser; Rowland joins HoF board. 1981: Lonrho's Pounds 226m bid for HoF blocked by MMC. 1983: DTI investigates shareholdings in HoF. 1984: Lonrho sells its stake to the Fayed brothers, but buys new 6.3 per cent stake. 1985: Fayeds bid Pounds 615m for HoF. 1987-88: DTI inspectors investigate takeover, report passed to Serious Fraud Office. 1990: DTI publishes highly critical report, which forms basis for Lonrho's legal attacks. 1991-92: Bank of England strips Fayeds of control of Harrods Bank;Takeover Panel censures Fayed brothers. 1993: Fayeds float HoF stores, but not Harrods. Jan 1993: Dieter Bock becomes Lonrho's biggest shareholder. Feb 1993: Bock appointed joint chief executive. May 1993: Observer sold to The Guardian. Oct 1993: Non-executive directors appointed. Sep 1994: Rowland wins fight to stay as Lonrho joint CEO. Nov 1994: Rowland agrees to relinquish executive role.
