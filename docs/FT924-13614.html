921014 FT 14 OCT 92 / Head of FBI faces ethics rules probe THE US Department of Justice is investigating Mr William Sessions, director of the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), and his wife for the possible violation of ethics rules, writes Alan Friedman in New York. The probe of Mr Sessions comes just three days after the Justice Department called in the FBI to investigate whether Central Intelligence Agency or Justice officials deliberately concealed intelligence reports relating to the scandal over Dollars 5bn (Pounds 2.8bn) of illegal loans made to Iraq by the Atlanta branch of Italy's Banca Nazionale del Lavoro (BNL). The investigation of Mr Sessions and his wife - concerning allegations made in anonymous letters that they misused government cars and aircraft and that Mrs Sessions misused her FBI building pass - triggered suggestions by Democrats that the probe may be a politically inspired attempt to intimidate the FBI. Mr Paul McNulty, a Justice Department spokesman, yesterday denied these suggestions, which came from congressional investigators. 'That is utter nonsense. There is no tension between us,' he said.
