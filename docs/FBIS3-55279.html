Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Forty five State Duma deputies elected in single-seat constituencies have set up a parliamentary group of independents and adopted a declaration and provisional rules. They will press for equality of party and other parliamentary groups. The group will be headed by Gennadiy Kalistratov, former people's deputy. In the former Supreme Soviet of Russia he was deputy chairman of the Industry and Energy Committee and a member of the Industrial Union group. Independent deputies had a meeting with Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin in Moscow Wednesday. They called for a better social protection of the population in the government's reform program.
