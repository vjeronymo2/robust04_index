930113 FT 13 JAN 93 / International Company News: European sports channels to merge TWO of Europe's satellite television sports channels, Eurosport and Screen Sport are to merge. The deal, signed yesterday, unites companies such as TF1, the French first channel, Canal Plus, the French subscription channel, and ESPN, the US sports channel subsidiary of Capital Cities/ABC. Screen Sport was founded by W. H. Smith, the UK retailer and Mr Rupert Murdoch's News International once had a 50 per cent stake in Eurosport. The single channel which will be shown throughout Europe simultaneously on Astra and Eutelsat II satellites will be known as Eurosport/The European Sports Network. There will be simultaneous commentaries in German, English and Dutch. TF1 will have a 34 per cent stake with Canal Plus and ESPN each on 33 per cent. A French language version broadcast on the Telecom 2 satellite will have about 700 hours of programming a year appealing to French viewers. There will be a separate shareholding structure for the French channel with Canal Plus owning 34 per cent, TF1 31 per cent, Generale D'Image 25 per cent and ESPN with 10 per cent. The new channel will concentrate on sports such as football, tennis, athletics, Formula 1, boxing and golf.
