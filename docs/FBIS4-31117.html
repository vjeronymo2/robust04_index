BFN [Excerpts] The presidium of the Hungarian Socialist Party [MSZP] proposes [Chairman] Gyula Horn for prime minister. The closed session of the party presidium, which ended not long ago, will be followed by a meeting of the national executive and tomorrow's congress, at which the final decision will be made on the nomination of the prime minister. Kata Peli Nagy interviewed Imre Szekeres, the party's executive deputy chairman. [Begin recording] [Szekeres] The local [party] organizations considered the Alliance of Free Democrats [SZDSZ] a necessary coalition partner and the Federation of Young Democrats [Fidesz] a possible one. This stance has been confirmed by the presidium, and today I will submit it to the national executive. [passage omitted] The presidium has also confirmed that we propose Gyula Horn as the MSZP's nomination for prime minister. [Nagy] So there will be one candidate? [Szekeres] Yes. Of course, I informed the presidium that the name of Laszlo Bekesi [author of the MSZP's economic program] had also been raised, as a minority opinion. By the way, we invited Laszlo Bekesi to the presidium session. He also outlined his views on political and personnel issues. The presidium has confirmed that the MSZP program in economic as well as in other areas is the program we presented to the people during the election campaign, and it is on this program that we wish to hold talks with the possible coalition partners. [end recording]
