Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "This Morning" newscast] [Text] Deputy Foreign Minister Jose Bernard Pallais has repeated the testimony he gave under oath at the 7th Criminal Court, which was in charge of the preliminary investigation of the murder of Jean Paul Genie, who was shot to death on 28 October 1990. Dr. Pallais, who was deputy government minister at the time and in charge of public security, was subpoenaed to testify on Tuesday afternoon before the military judge, First Lieutenant Lester Gallo Sandoval. Pallais confirmed that, upon instructions from the government, he and high-ranking Police officers took over the investigation in connection with this case. [Begin recording] [Pallais] Yes, I was summoned by the president of the Republic to participate in a meeting two or three days after the death. During the meeting I was told about the importance of this case because the victim's father had information that led him to believe it was possible that the shots came from a military motorcade. Consequently, and given that rumors... [Unidentified reporter, interrupting] Doctor, did you... [Pallais, interrupting] Given the rumors circulating which implied that the motorcade was led by Army General Humberto Ortega, I was instructed to supervise the investigations that the National Police had begun and to maintain close contacts with Mr. Genie so he would trust that justice would prevail and would therefore be inclined to cooperate in the investigation by giving the names of witnesses whom he had identified and who said they were afraid to testify. [end recording]
