Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 26 (XINHUA) -- The 19th meeting of the Sino-Portuguese Joint Liaison Group ended here yesterday with the two sides signing a summary of talks on aviation agreements between the Macao Government and Belgium, Luxemburg and the Netherlands. The two sides stressed the importance of the three agreements on the projected Macao international airport, according to a press communique issued at the end of the three-day meeting. The Portuguese side also provided the Chinese side with a copy of an aviation agreement initialed with Brazil. The communique says that the two sides had long discussions on localization of public servants, the official status of the Chinese Language as well as the translation and localization of laws. They also reviewed the progress made on these issues. On international organizations, the two sides have taken note of the fact that the two governments have formally applied in Geneva for Macao's membership in the World Meteorological Organization. Taking note of the fact that the International Navigation Association has of late revised its constitution, the two sides reiterated their concern on macao going through the process in joining the association. The two sides also studied progress made by the work group on the Bank of China and the Atlantic Bank participating in issuing bank notes. They asked the group to continue with its work. The two sides, in an atmosphere of friendship and cooperation, discussed important issues related to Macao's social development and stability, including nationality of some Macao residents, says the communique. It was agreed that the 20th meeting of the Sino-Portuguese Joint Liaison Group is to be held in Lisbon beginning from July 5.
