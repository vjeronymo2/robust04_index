BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- The Federal Government will again help India with aid programs worth approximately 350 million German marks [DM], concentrating in particular on combating poverty and on environmental protection. This was announced by the Development Ministry on Thursday [21 April]. After the conclusion of a round of negotiations in Bonn, DM315 million was promised in financial cooperation and DM36.5 million in technical cooperation. The main targets are small industries, private business initiatives, and the elimination of bottlenecks in the energy supply. As examples the ministry cited a brown coal power plant, a factory for converting tree stumps into particle board, drinking water supply facilities, and the promotion of solar- energy centers.
