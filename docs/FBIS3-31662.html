Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 22 February (MTI) -- Ministers of Defense Lajos Fur of Hungary and Fabio Fabbri of Italy signed a cooperation agreement between the armed forces of the two countries, in Budapest on Tuesday [22 February]. The ministers also held talks on the possibilities of joint military exercises. At a press conference following the signing, Fabio Fabbri said Rome welcomed the American proposal of peace-partnership. The signed Hungarian-Italian agreement relates to this, extending to mutual training of officers, constant military consultations, and cooperation between the military industries of the two countries. The agreement also covers an already existing cooperation between the military secret services. The Italian minister, who arrived in Budapest on Tuesday, also raised the possibility of joint Hungarian-Italian military exercises, the implementation of which are being examined. Minister Fur agreed with examining the opportunities for joint military exercises, the dates for these being dependent on NATO relations development.
