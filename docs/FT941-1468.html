940325 FT 25 MAR 94 / World Trade News: Caribbean nations plan regional trade group Representatives of 22 countries and several dependent territories in the Caribbean Basin have concluded talks aimed at creating a new regional trade group, writes Canute James in Kingston. The Association of Caribbean States, which will start as a consultative group, is likely to include Colombia, Mexico, Venezuela, Cuba, Haiti, the Dominican Republic, the Central American states, Surinam and members of the Caribbean Community. The group's main objectives will be liberalising trade and investment; co-ordinating negotiating positions on bilateral and multilateral issues; and promoting investment opportunities.
