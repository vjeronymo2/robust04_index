930921 FT 21 SEP 93 / US calls Mideast aid donor meeting THE US yesterday called a conference of potential donors to drum up aid in support of the peace agreement between Israel and the Palestine Liberation Organisation. Mr Warren Christopher, the US secretary of state, said he and Mr Lloyd Bentsen, the Treasury secretary, would convene a donors' conference to help co-ordinate aid for Palestinian self-rule in the Gaza Strip and Jericho. 'Today, on behalf of President Clinton, I announce our intention to lead a wide-ranging effort not simply to give peace a chance but to ensure that it will not fail,' Mr Christopher said in a speech at New York's Columbia University. Administration officials said the purpose of the conference was not just economic, but to demonstrate the international community's backing for last week's agreement and to maintain momentum for the peace process. Mr Christopher said the US would itself contribute Dollars 250m (Pounds 162m) in cash, loans and guarantees in the next two years. Senior administration officials said the conference was likely to be held in Washington in the next two weeks, taking advantage of the arrival in the US of finance and foreign ministers attending the annual meetings of the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank, as well as the United Nations General Assembly. They hoped the conference would produce several hundred million dollars of commitments to cover the next 18 months, making a successful start towards the World Bank's Dollars 300m a year estimate of investment needs over the next 10 years. Reuter adds from Washington: The International Monetary Fund said yesterday it had approved a three-year, Dollars 569m (Pounds 370m) loan for Egypt to back up the country's efforts to open up and reform its economy. 'The overall objective of Egypt's medium term reform programme. . . is to create a decentralised, outward-oriented market economy with sustained growth,' the IMF said. Although Egypt does not plan to draw down on the loan, Cairo needed IMF approval of its economic reform programme to win more than Dollars 3bn in debt forgiveness from the US and other creditor nations.
