August 7, 1989, Monday, P.M. Final

RAIL WORKERS, SANTA FE AGREE TO CUT IN SIZE OF SOME TRAIN CREWS

Railroad workers have ratified an agreement with the Santa Fe Railway that authorizes a reduction in the size of some freight train crews, the carrier announced today.

"This agreement covers 30% of Santa Fe's conductors, trainmen and yardmen," the railway said. "None of the approximately 1,300 employees involved will be furloughed as a result of this agreement."

A standard crew consists of one conductor, one brakeman and one locomotive engineer. Under the agreement, on designated intermodal trains, which represent 20% of the railway's through trains, there will be no brakeman.

In return, the railway is providing $75,000 to each employee who voluntarily resigns. Those remaining employees will receive a $10,000 cash payment to be paid by the end of September and $65,000 to be indexed for inflation and distributed when they retire or leave the company.
