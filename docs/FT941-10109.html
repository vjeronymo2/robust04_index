940211 FT 11 FEB 94 / TAP air crews to work 35% longer TAP-Air Portugal, the loss-making state airline, said yesterday it had reached an agreement with its flight crew on a 35 per cent rise in working time, writes Peter Wise in Lisbon. Mr Jose Braz, president of the flight attendants' union, said they had agreed to increase their maximum flying time from 550 to 750 hours a year in return for enhanced benefits. The government hailed the accord as a breakthrough for its plans to return the debt-ridden company to profit by injecting Es180bn (Pounds 690m) in fresh capital. Pilots have already accepted the increase in flying time. However, a TAP statement that the flight crew had also agreed to a pay freeze for this year was disputed by the union. Mr Braz said it was still pressing for a 7 per cent increase. According to a TAP spokesman, the union signed an agreement accepting whatever pay rises were decided for the whole company between 1994 and 1997. 'We have already made clear there will be no increases for anyone in 1994 and the union has clearly accepted that.'
