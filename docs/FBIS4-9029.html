BFN [Text] Warsaw, April 18 -- Speaker of the Sejm Jozef Oleksy addressing the round table conference on central Europe and NATO said that the "Partnership for Peace" programme has not fully met Poland's expectations and aspirations. The speaker also stressed that the West is too slow in its integration with the rest of Europe. He expressed hope that governments of Western states will see their own interests in integrating with central and eastern Europe. Chairman of the North Atlantic Assembly Loic Bouvard said that Poland can be now treated as one of the most important partners from the East and added that the "Partnership for Peace" programme is a stage which should lead central and east Europen states to full integration with NATO.
