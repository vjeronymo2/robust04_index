BFN [Text] Warsaw, April 6 -- European integration and cooperation in the Baltic region were the main topics raised by Polish and Finnish foreign ministers Andrzej Olechowski and Heikki Haavisto during their meeting Wednesday. After the talks Haavisto termed to-date Polish-Finnish relations as good. The ministers reviewed bilateral relations and thought they should be broadened. Discussed was the situation in the Baltic region and the European security as well as negotiations with the European Union on Finland and Poland's membership. The sides also discussed Polish-Finnish cooperation in the Council of the Baltic Sea States, in particular the Via Baltica project.
