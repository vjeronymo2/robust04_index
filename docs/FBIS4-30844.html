BFN [Text] During a routine check of a UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR] vehicle at the Kasindol Street checkpoint, civilian and military police of the Serbian Republic discovered a group of people that are strongly suspected of smuggling on behalf of the current authorities in Sarajevo. Among the detainees is also an UNPROFOR member. The detainees were carrying a large amount of foreign currency, and it has been established that the suspects were charged with purchasing equipment for the army and the police of the former Bosnia-Herzegovina and that they had set off for the airport with this intention, from where they were supposed to fly to Zagreb. As the incumbent offices have reported, six people have been arrested and detained in prison, among them (Angelo Andrea), an Italian citizen who was in charge of the liaison with correspondents in the UNPROFOR civilian sector. The search has established that Savcinovic, Cakovic, and Kujic all possessed UN press cards, while, as he claims, he had the card of the UN High Commissioner for Refugees from the Muslim government because he was in charge of control of humanitarian cargo in Frankfurt.
