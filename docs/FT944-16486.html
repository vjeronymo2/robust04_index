941013 FT 13 OCT 94 / The Conservatives in Bournemouth: Rifkind says fighting jobs safe Cuts in the fighting strength of the armed forces are over, Mr Malcolm Rifkind, the defence secretary, promised the conference yesterday. The pledge, which signals that the Ministry of Defence will escape big cuts in this year's public expenditure round, was cheered by delighted constituency representatives. Praising the performance of British troops in Bosnia, Northern Ireland and Kuwait, Mr Rifkind said that future demands on the armed forces were uncertain. But he told the conference: 'I can give you this undertaking. Even if our commitments were to reduce, there will be no contraction in the fighting strength of our army. 'There will be no further cuts in battalions, no more amalgamations of regular regiments.' Any reduction in commitments would 'not be a justification for cuts in the armed services'. Rather, it would 'be good news for them,' he said. 'It would mean more time for training, more opportunity for service abroad and more time with their families. That is what the armed forces want to hear.' Later, Mr Rifkind said he was 'absolutely certain' that the Treasury would not seek further cuts in the armed forces following the completion of the Options for Change programme of efficiency savings. In a round of interviews, Mr Rifkind said that his pledge not to cut the fighting strength of the forces would last 'as long as this government is in office'. Speaking on BBC television, he said: 'The Treasury has accepted that the armed forces have more than made their (contribution) to the needs of the taxpayer. 'While we will always be expected to find ways of being more efficient and concerned with value for money . . . there will be no proposals put to me by my colleagues for any cuts in the fighting strength of our armed forces.' Mr Rifkind's pledge followed attacks on defence cost cutting by several constituency representatives. Mr Bob Martin, a retired air commodore from Oxford, was applauded as he criticised the 'Whitehall warriors' cutting the forces. 'In the past year or two there have been more axes and knives hacking away at our services by these new-style Whitehall warriors than the forces have ever experienced.'
