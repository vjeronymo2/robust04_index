Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Baghdad BABIL in Arabic on 2 February publishes on page 12 the following report under the banner headline "The Greatest Honor: Serving the Homeland and the Leader:" "The coalition aircraft have dropped fliers over Bani-Sakin area, near al-Nashwah, al-Basrah Governorate. "The Iraqis listen to beamed foreign radios, which have a greater influence than the fliers. During their underground struggle, the Ba'thists used the flier technique and they cannot be deceived by such games. "The aircraft that dropped these fliers are the same aircraft that dropped hundreds of tonnes of bombs on our great people. They failed to achieve the enemies' goal to infringe on the sovereignty, security, and renaissance of Iraq under its mujahid leader President Saddam Husayn, may God protect and preserve him. Accursed be they and shame on those who sold their homeland to jump into the hands of the foreigner." Copies of six of these fliers are published with the report. Each flier is headlined "Wanted By International Justice," and includes a picture of the official concerned with a summary of "crimes" he has committed printed under the picture. The fliers read: "Taha Yasin Ramadan, accused of crimes against humanity, including mass murder committed by the Republican Guard in the south under his command." " 'Ali Hasan al-Majid, accused of crimes against humanity, including the killing of tens of thousands of citizens in Iraqi Kurdistan in al-Anfal Operations." "Watban Ibrahim, accused of crimes against humanity. He ordered the execution of 42 merchants in Baghdad in July 1992." "Muhammad Hamzah al-Zubaydi, accused of crimes against humanity, including brutal suppression of the popular uprising in al-Samawah, al-Nasiriyah, al-Basrah, al-Kut, al-'Amarah, and the marshlands in the south in March 1991." " 'Udayy Saddam Husayn, accused of crimes against humanity, including mass murder, rape, and instigating genocide." " 'Aziz Salih al-Numan, accused of crimes against humanity, including ordering the destruction and desecration of holy places when he was governor of Karbala' and al-Najaf."
