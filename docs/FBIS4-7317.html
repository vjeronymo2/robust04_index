BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 22 KYODO -- Labor Minister Chikara Sakaguchi said Friday [22 April] local and central governments should take a greater role in job training. Job training is focus of a new medium- and long-term program to be announced in May, he told reporters. Up to now job training has been provided mainly by corporations. Both the central and local governments should review ways to take on some of the role in training workers along with the private sector, he said. He pointed to the need to study how to deal with job training at small and medium-sized companies, which account for roughly 90 percent of all companies in Japan.
