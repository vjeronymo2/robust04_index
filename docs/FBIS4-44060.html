BFN [Report by Brussels correspondent Jean de la Gueriviere: "After Several Months of Talks: Russia Finally Signs Partnership for Peace Agreement With NATO"] [Excerpts] "This is not Yalta, this is Brussels. There cannot be any confusion on this." Thus Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev sought to reassure a journalist from a Baltic state whose question reflected a suspicion that the world was once again being carved up after Russia's signing of the Partnership for Peace [PFP] framework agreement at NATO headquarters on Wednesday, 22 June. [passage omitted] During the closing news conference, a Polish journalist asked Mr. Kozyrev whether Russia would object to Poland joining NATO. "As equal partners within NATO, we shall have an equal right to say what we think," was all the Russian minister would say, mentioning an upcoming meeting with his Polish counterpart to discuss the issue. A Lithuanian journalist had an insidious question -- already asked by his government -- as to whether small Lithuania could ask for a "special relationship" with NATO as part of the PFP, in light of the "precedent" created with Russia. NATO Deputy Secretary General Sergio Balanzino, who replaced Manfred Woerner, still unable to attend due to health problems, did not leave him many illusions....
