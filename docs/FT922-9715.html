920506 FT 06 MAY 92 / World Trade News: Olivetti Dollars 300m computer deal with McDonald's OLIVETTI, the Italian computer and office equipment group, is to supply point-of-sales computer technology to the McDonald's fast food group in the US in an agreement worth over Dollars 300m (Pounds 169.4m). The order, won against competition from IBM, Panasonic and NCR, is Olivetti's biggest contract from the the US and one of the largest deals in its history. Under the plan, eight or nine Olivetti personal computers will be installed in each of McDonald's 8,800 US outlets, which will be linked via local area networks. The deal envisages the installation of over 50,000 computer workstations, along with a variety of peripheral equipment. The system will allow full automation both for customers, who will be served by staff using new touch-screen terminals, and for back-office uses. Computers in each McDonald's outlet will handle accounting, administration and record keeping. Using the local area networks will also allow each outlet to report regularly on its performance to McDonald's headquarters, providing information on sales and payroll records, or receive instructions on new menu items or prices.
