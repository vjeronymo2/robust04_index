930429 FT 29 APR 93 / International Company News: Borland International does better than expected BORLAND International, the US personal computer software company, reported much higher than expected earnings for its fourth quarter as sales of new programs for use with Microsoft's Windows operating system took off. Net income for the quarter was Dollars 5.1m, or 19 cents a share, compared with a net loss of Dollars 26.9m, or Dollars 1.05, in the fourth quarter of 1992. Revenues were Dollars 117.1m, down slightly from Dollars 116.8m. 'These results reflect the introduction during the quarter of Paradox for Windows and dBASE IV version 2.0,' said Mr Philippe Kahn, chairman, president and chief executive. 'Sales of Borland products for the growing Windows marketplace accounted for more than half of the company's fourth-quarter revenues.' Net loss for the year was Dollars 49.2m, or Dollars 1.87 a share, against a net loss of Dollars 110.4m, or Dollars 4.29, previously. Revenues for the year fell to Dollars 464m from Dollars 482.5m.
