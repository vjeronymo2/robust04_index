BFN [Excerpts] Chengdu, April 5 (XINHUA) -- A celebration was held today near the capital of Southwest China's Sichuan Province for the 2250th anniversary of the Dujiangyan Dam, the world's oldest functioning irrigation works. Chinese Premier Li Peng wrote an inscription for the occasion to praise the project for its great contribution to agriculture in Sichuan basin. Residents at Dujiangyan, an hour's bus ride from the provincial capital of Chengdu, dressed streets with colorful flags, raised lanterns, and posted poetic couplets on their doors. [passage omitted] Huge sums of money have been poured into the expansion and reconstruction of the project since 1949. Its irrigated areas have been increased from 187,000 hectares to about 670,000 hectares over the past four decades. The dam has become a multi-purpose project combining irrigation, flood prevention, power generation, fish breeding and sightseeing.
