BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 6/04/1994 -- "Romania has the capacity to employ its own potential," said Romanian minister of state, head of the Council of Economic Coordination, Strategy and Reform, in an interview granted to the governmental daily VOCEA ROMANIEI. "The market economy is not a goal, an objective," says Cosea, but it must be considered as "a way to organize production and consumption based on relations of equivalent exchanges." Minister Cosea opined that Romania should consider the projects for the future according to the objectives it wish to reach. The development, as a goal, "depends on a country's capacity of employing the potential it has and, in this respect, few countries have this capacity that Romania has." Minister Cosea said also that the reform in Romania was "an action of updating and civilizing which entails time and efforts. Still, the reform must be, in the end, visible and perceptible at the level of the individual" and not reduced to some statistical figures with regard to the pace of the privatization. "If it does not bring a change in the life of the individual, in his life in the society, the reform has no motivation." Reviewing the past four years of reform, Mircea Cosea concluded that the single change in the life of the individual was only a "permanent stress caused by the vanishing of the old values." The reform, Cosea opined, should lead to the healing of the Romanian individual from two maladies he suffered from very much in the communist regime, namely, "the apathy, lack of determination," and "a certain underestimation of the country and of the people he belongs to in favor of foreign values, usually Western ones." Mircea Cosea said that at the time being Romania needed "a well structured system of values" and "a revigoration of the real, genuine patriotism which means one's faith that he belong to a valuable and strong nation." These can be achieved, Cosea considers, through the economic factor. When the Romanians would be able to live a better life, they will abandon the tendency of over-estimating the values of other peoples only because of their higher living standard, believes Cosea. Referring to the privatization issues of primary concern in 1994, Mircea Cosea warned that "privatization is not a reform element in itself but a single step which must be taken in the economic and social field." Minister Cosea said also that because of the way privatization has been made from 1990 till now, it is no longer perceived by the public as a reform element but as "a collective trauma." In this respect, Cosea mentioned certain abuses, elements of corruption, in the first years of the privatization process.
