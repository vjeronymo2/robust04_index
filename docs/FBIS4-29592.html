BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 17 KYODO -- Japan will extend emergency humanitarian aid totaling 78,000 dollars worth of medical goods and equipment to three former Soviet Union republics in central Asia, the Foreign Ministry said Friday [17 June]. The aid will be extended to Turkmenistan, Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan, the ministry said. The three nations will each receive 2 tons of medical goods and equipment, enough to cover the medical needs of 20,000 people for three months, it said.
