BFN [Text] President Hashemi-Rafsanjani participated in a TV satellite interview with participants of the Swiss-based International Crans Montana Forum at 1630 GMT Thursday. In the direct satellite link, held at the request of forum participants and broadcast simultaneously to 35 countries and translated into five languages: English, French, Spanish, German and Italian, Mr. Hashemi-Rafsanjani spoke about economics, politics, Iranian foreign relations, and other general issues of interest. He referred to Iranian policy regarding its neighbors as a peaceful and friendly policy. With respect to events in northern Iraq, responding to a question by former French Foreign Minister Dumas, Hashemi-Rafsanjani said: we respect Iraq's territorial integrity and see the Kurds living in Turkey and Iraq as members of these countries whose interests should be noted. Advising Russia not to interfere in the newly independent Central Asian countries, Hashemi-Rafsanjani said: Unfortunately, differences have arisen in these countries. Our policy is to solve these differences. Referring to the tragic state of affairs in Bosnia-Herzegovina and emphasizing that the present condition must change, the president said: The hands of the majority of the people of this country have been tied but a minority allowed to commit aggression. Addressing the participants, Hashemi-Rafsanjani said: If you and others feel unable to end the war in Bosnia you should allow the innocent Muslims to have defense capabilities. The president added: Iran is prepared to dispatch its peace keeping forces to Bosnia within the context of the United Nations. In another part of the interview, Hashemi-Rafsanjani said the expansion of Franco-Iranian depends on policies of Paris. He added: If our friends in France try to free themselves from the effects of fabricated propaganda bilateral relations will expand easily. Commenting on events in Algeria, Hashemi-Rafsanjani referred to the victory of Muslims in the elections and said: The vote of the majority was not respected. We are surprised that the supporters and champions of human rights fail to take note. Hashemi-Rafsanjani emphasized: The Islamic Republic of Iran has been blamed regarding events in Algeria only because it has spoken its views without hindrance.
