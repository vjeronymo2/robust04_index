BFN [Report by Irina Goryacheva of Economic Conditions Center under Russian Federation Government under the "From Our Correspondents and News Agencies" rubric: "Forecast Not Borne Out: Inflation Proved Less Than Expected"] [Text] The level of inflation in May was lower than forecast and stood at 8.1 percent. Such a low inflation indicator has not been observed in Russia since August 1992. Foodstuffs went up 7.7 percent last month, nonfood commodities 8.1 percent, and paid services for the population 11.3 percent. The biggest increase in consumer prices (14.1 percent) was noted in the Ural Region, where the largest increases were in the prices of foodstuffs (13.6 percent) and paid services for the population (36.5 percent). The smallest increase in the prices of basic consumer goods and services was in the Northern Region -- 5.9 percent. The increase in the prices of consumer goods and services stood at 8.5 percent in Moscow and 7.9 percent in St. Petersburg.
