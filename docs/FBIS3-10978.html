Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] With the slightest advantage over the eastern provinces, the nation's central provinces achieved the best performance in sugarcane harvest and recovery tasks in the first 10 days of March. This was reported at the meeting held in Bayamo to check the progress of that economic sphere. Central Cuba's sugar agroindustrial complexes obtained superior results in yield, base 96 [not further identified], and in reducing electrical energy consumption, while they tied with those of the east in recovery and amount of sugar produced. The agroindustrial complexes of western Cuba headed sugar recovery, making the best use of installed capabilities and cane planting. The progress check for the first 10 days of the harvest in March, which was held in Bayamo, Granma Province, was presided over by Nelson Torres, minister of the Sugar Industry, and Jose Naranjo, chief of the team for coordination and support of the commander in chief.
