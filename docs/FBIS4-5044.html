BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 7 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa pledged on Thursday [7 April] to lend Kazakhstan up to 12.98 billion yen to help it improve railroad transportation and tackle pollution from thermal power stations, Foreign Ministry officials said. Hosokawa made the pledge in a meeting with Kazakhstan President Nursultan Nazarbayev, the officials said. Japan will extend the loan to support the central Asian nation's economic reform efforts and to help its environmental preservation measures, they said. Of the total, 7.23 billion yen will be set aside for a project to improve railroad transportation. The project aims at supplying equipment necessary to expand Druzhba station at the border with China which serves as the base for material transportation, and to repair and improve the track. Another 5.74 billion yen will be for a project to tackle air pollution from the Ekibastuz thermal power station in northern Kazakhstan, the officials said. The project aims at installing electric dust collectors and denitrification and desulfurization equipment at the power station. Despite the use of coal for thermal power generation, sufficient environmental preservation measures have not been taken in Kazakhstan since it became independent with the breakup of the Soviet Union, they said.
