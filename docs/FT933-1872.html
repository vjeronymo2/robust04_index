930922 FT 22 SEP 93 / World Trade News: Thai refinery financing deal Rayong Refinery has signed Dollars 1.5bn of project financing facilities to fund a hydrocracking refinery to be built in Thailand, writes Tracy Corrigan. The total project cost will be about Dollars 2.4bn (Pounds 1.55bn). Rayong Refinery is a joint venture owned by the Royal Dutch/Shell group (64 per cent) and the Petroleum Authority of Thailand (36 per cent). The debt financing is made up of a Dollars 1.1bn facility, Dollars 425m of which is expected to be supported by export credit political risk guarantees; a Dollars 25m revolving credit facility; and a Dollars 375m export credit loan from the Export-Import Bank of the US, which will be guaranteed by three Thai banks.
