Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Moscow January 31 TASS -- The Georgian public is increasingly inclined to believe that Georgia's future is connected more with Russia rather than with the West, according to the results of a blitz public opinion poll conducted by the local newspaper "FREE GEORGIA." A total of 51.2 per cent of respondents said that the forthcoming visit by the Russian president to Georgia will have a positive impact on the improvement of relations between the two countries, having age-old historical, economic and humanitarian ties. Only 37.6 per cent of respondents were skeptical about the future of Russo-Georgian relations, while 4.7 per cent expect Russo-Georgian relations to worsen.
