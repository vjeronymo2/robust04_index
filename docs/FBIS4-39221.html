BFN [Text] Chisinau will not insist on the complete withdrawal of Russia's 14th Army from Moldova before 1 July 1994, as this term is unreal, head of the Moldovan delegation, Deputy Foreign Minister Nikolai Osmokesku told INTERFAX before his departure for Moscow, where the ninth round of the Moldovan-Russian talks on the status of the 14th Army and the schedule of its withdrawal will be held on 7 and 8 June. He also announced that Chisinau would advance a number of new demands, including the demand that Russia pay Moldova a compensation for the 14th Army's stay from the moment it was put under Russia's jurisdiction. The talks will also deal with the military transit through Ukraine during the withdrawal. Talks will be started with Ukraine as the third party concerned. In addition to the main agreement, seven or eight additional agreements are expected to be signed.
