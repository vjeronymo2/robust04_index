920213 FT 13 FEB 92 / World Trade News: Moscow accepts EC food terms RUSSIA has accepted European Community terms on a Ecu500m (Pounds 356.5m) food credit guarantee and payment can be expected shortly, EC sources said yesterday, Reuter reports from Brussels. EC finance ministers decided to advance Russia all the credit guarantee originally approved in December 1990 to the now-defunct Soviet Union. The funds are earmarked to buy and transport EC food, as well as produce from east Europe and Baltic states. Finance ministers decided other Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) members would have to wait for a decision on how to share out a separate Ecu1.25bn (Pounds 890m) food loan.
