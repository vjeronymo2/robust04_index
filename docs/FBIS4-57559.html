BFN [Text] More questions than answers remain as the region continues to discuss the controversial NAFTA accord. The latest voice in what is proving to be a series of never-ending debates is that of David Thompson. Thompson, the finance minister in Barbados, says the region cannot afford to sit on the edge of all that is happening. NAFTA is a joint trade agreement signed last year between the United States, Mexico, and Canada. The big debate in Barbados and the region is how the grouping will respond to NAFTA. There already seems to be a split within Caricom [Caribbean Common Market] circles as to whether or not the grouping should proceed to enter NAFTA as a unit or to allow individual countries desirous of doing so to be approved to proceed.
