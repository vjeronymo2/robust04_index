921229 FT 29 DEC 92 / Construction Contracts: Materials recycling in Milton Keynes The regional business of TRAFALGAR HOUSE CONSTRUCTION has been awarded four contracts worth almost Pounds 6m. The largest is a Pounds 2.5m contract for a materials recycling facility for the Milton Keynes Borough Council. The work involves constructing a steel frame building containing concrete storage bins and two 50 tonne weighbridges with a gatehouse in the Old Wolverton area. A Pounds 1.2m cold storage facility, steel framed with insulated cladding will be built at Dairy Crest's Crudgington Creamery in Telford. New housing association work worth Pounds 1m will be carried out in Guildford. Work involves building 23 houses and flats and refurbishing three properties for the Downland Housing Society. In Scotland, Trafalgar House Construction has been awarded a Pounds 1.2m contract to construct an intercepting sewer at Greenock in Strathclyde.
