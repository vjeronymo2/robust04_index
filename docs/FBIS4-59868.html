BFN [Text] Cairo, 17 May (MENA)--At his office today Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi received a Communist Party of China [CPC] delegation that is visiting Cairo. The delegation is led by CPC Politburo member Wei Jianxing. Dr. Sidqi said after the meeting that they had reviewed the prospects of further cooperation between the two countries in light of the results of President Husni Mubarak's recent visit to China. He added that they discussed initial plans for building free industrial zones in Sinai, as agreed upon during Mubarak's visit to Beijing. Sidqi explained that they also discussed exchanges of expertise in the technical and scientific spheres and in the field of economic reform. He pointed out that China has achieved approximately a 13 percent a year economic growth rate. Ambassador Ahmad Namiq, political adviser at the Prime Minister's Office and former ambassador to China, also attended the meeting.
