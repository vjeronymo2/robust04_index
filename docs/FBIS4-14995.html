BFN [Excerpt] Riga, April 5 (LETA) -- Summit meeting of Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis and Russian President Boris Yeltsin has been scheduled to be held on April 20, according to a spokeswoman of the Latvian president. She said that the date had been suggested by the Russian ambassador here, Aleksandr Rannikh, on behalf of his government. The report has also been confirmed by the Foreign Ministry of Russia. The spokeswoman said that talks at the governmental level were also set to be held during the visit in Moscow, which means that the Latvian delegation would include also Latvian Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs and several members of his cabinet. During his stay, President Ulmanis also plans to visit the city of Krasnoyarsk and its community of ethnic Latvians residing there. Sergey Zotov, chief Russian negotiator at the Latvian-Russian talks, said that a meeting of the two delegations was expected to be held in the Latvian seaside resort city of Jurmala next week, before the forthcoming summit meeting of the two presidents, in order to finalise the agreements which had been initialed in Moscow last month.
