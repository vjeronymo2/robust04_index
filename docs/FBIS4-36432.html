BFN [Text] Tehran, June 7, IRNA -- Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias heading a delegation arrived here Tuesday [7 June] on a 2-day official visit and was greeted at Tehran Airport by his Iranian counterpart 'Ali Akbar Velayati. Upon his arrival, Papoulias termed as "solid and strong" the relations between Tehran and Athens and called for their expansion. Papoulias said he as the foreign minister of Greece and head of European Unity's Council of Ministers, would hold talks with Iranian officials on expansion of bilateral relations and on various international issues. He expressed hope that the outcome of his talks in Iran would be "excellent". The Greek foreign minister is carrying two messages from Greek President Konstandinos Karamanlis and Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou for Iranian authorities.
