Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Brussels, March 6 (MTI) -- Hungarian Foreign Minister Geza Jeszenszky arrived in Brussels on a two-day visit tonight. The minister is to attend a formal ceremony marking the establishment of the Association Council of Hungary and the European Union [EU]. The Council is set up under the Hungary-EU association agreement, which came into force on February 1 this year. On Tuesday [8 March] Jeszenszky and ministers of five other East European associate members of the EU are to meet representatives of the EU troika and the EU Commission. Jeszenszky is scheduled to meet Sergio Belanzino, assistant secretary general of NATO, and Wim van Eekelen, secretary general of the Western European Union.
