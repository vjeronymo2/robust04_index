BFN [Text] Skopje, 20 May (TANJUG) -- Macedonian Foreign Minister Stevo Crvenkovski returned to Skopje this evening after a several-day visit to New York, where he had several meetings with Cyrus Vance, UN mediator in the dispute with Greece, and with the U.S. President's special envoy, Matthew Nimitz. In a statement for the press immediately on arrival at Skopje airport, Crvenkovski said that certain progress has been achieved in the dispute with Greece. Namely, the talks are focused on overall bilateral relations and contacts between the two countries are being maintained without Greece's three well-known conditions. However, Crvenkovski concluded, it is hard to say how things will develop further. He also announced that Nimitz will soon be visiting Skopje.
