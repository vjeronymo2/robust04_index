BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, June 6 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- Malaysian Deputy Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim said Monday, the Asia-Pacific region needed a new regional security arrangement based on the idea of the interdependence and equity of nations within a global community. It must also principally serve the interests of the people of the region, not the vested interests of arms merchants and defence industries, he added. We must progress from containment, or even mere peaceful co-existence, towards productive and mutually beneficial collaboration in all spheres of activity, he said when addressing the eighth Asia-Pacific Roundtable on Confidence Building and Conflict Reduction in the Pacific, here. He said that for Asians in particular, such an arrangement must provide the right kind of environment for the full flowering of their economic, social and cultural potentials. We must also [word indistinct] a new global partnership for peace, one based on mutual respect and genuine understanding between nations, large and small, North and South, East and West.
