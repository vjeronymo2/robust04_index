BFN [Text] La Paz, 11 Jun (EFE) -- The Bolivian Senate today found Supreme Court Chief Justice Edgar Oblitas and Supreme Court Dean Ernesto Poppe guilty of the crimes of prevarication and bribery. Their sentences will be disclosed within the next few hours. The Senate held an extraordinary session today to carry out the legal process which began last year in the Chamber of Deputies. Oblitas and Poppe were accused of extortion by former Nicaraguan Deputy Presidency Minister Jose Antonio Ibarra. The administration of Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada supported the decision to bring the Supreme Court justices to trial in an attempt to prove its political resolve to fight corruption. The 16 senators in the ruling coalition formed by the Nationalist Revolutionary Movement (MNR) and Civic Solidarity Unity (UCS) found the two justices "guilty." The three senators from the Movement of the Revolutionary Left (MIR) voted in favor of their acquittal, while the seven senators from the Nationalist Democratic Action (ADN) and Conscience of the Fatherland (CONDEPA) abstained from participating in the session. Senate President Juan Carlos Duran from the ruling coalition declared a recess of approximately two hours to agree on sentences for Oblitas and Poppe, who were suspended from their posts shortly after their trial began. [Madrid EFE in Spanish at 0214 GMT on 12 June transmits a 150-word report that Juan Carlos Duran, president of the Grand Jury of Bolivian Senators, decided today to postpone the sentencing of Supreme Court Chief Justice Edgar Oblitas and Supreme Court Dean Ernesto Poppe until Monday morning].
