Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Santa Fe de Bogota, 17 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- Police reported today that the Colombian guerrillas kidnapped two U.S. citizens who were involved in missionary activities in southern Colombia. The missionaries were kidnapped in reprisal for the arrival of U.S. soldiers in Colombia. Official sources consulted by NOTIMEX indicated that a group of 20 rebels of the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC) intercepted the vehicle in which missionaries Steven Welch, 41, and Timothy Vandyke, 40, were traveling in southern Meta Department. According to the report, the two were kidnapped over the weekend on the road from Villavicencio to Restrepo, 200 km from this capital. Although the police did not indicate the reason for the kidnapping, local unofficial sources said the rebels said it was in reprisal for the presence of a U.S. military contingent in the Puerto Lopez area, near the site of the kidnapping.
