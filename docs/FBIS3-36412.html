Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Yuriy Meshkov, the first president of the Crimean peninsula, was sworn in today. Let us recall one interesting aspect of his career. As a student at the law faculty of Moscow State University, Meshkov wrote his thesis under the supervision of Professor [Valeriy] Zorkin. Correspondent Yevgeniy Novikov reports on how the inauguration of the Crimean president went: [Begin Novikov recording] The session was opened by Ivan Yermakov, deputy chairman of the Supreme Soviet. He asked Meshkov to come to the rostrum for his official inauguration as president. Meshkov pledged allegiance to the people of the Crimea and solemnly swore to abide by the Constitution and laws of the Republic of the Crimea. After the inauguration ceremony, Yermakov read out congratulatory telegrams to the Crimean president. Telegrams were received from Belarusian Supreme Soviet Chairman Myechyslaw Gryb, from Yegor Gaydar, Grigoriy Yavlinskiy, Vladimir Shumeyko, Konstantin Zatulin, the presidents of the autonomous republics of the Russian Federation, and from Eduard Baltin, commander in chief of the Black Sea Fleet. Meshkov delivered an address to the citizens of the Republic of Crimea. He assured Crimeans that he would work for the further development of Crimea's statehood. Meshkov also disclosed that he intended to fly to Kiev immediately in order to meet Leonid Kravchuk. After meeting the Ukrainian president, Meshkov will go on to Moscow. A recess in the session was declared after Meshkov's speech. During the recess, Igor Yezarov, the Crimean president's press secretary, held a news conference for Crimean and foreign correspondents. Among other things, Yezarov disclosed that Meshkov had prepared a number of important decrees, including a decree on relay of all Radio Russia and Russian television programs to the Crimea. [end recording]
