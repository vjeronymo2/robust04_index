910511 FT 11 MAY 91 / Economic Diary TODAY: EC-Gulf Co-operation Council ministerial meeting in Luxembourg. MONDAY: Central Statistical Office (CSO) publishes figures for credit business in March; and March final figures for retail sales. European Parliament plenary session opens in Strasbourg. EC internal market council meets in Brussels. EC general affairs council starts two-day meeting in Brussels. Caribbean Community prime ministers and foreign ministers hold annual meeting in Barbados. EC foreign ministers joint meeting with EFTA counterparts on plans for EC-EFTA common market, Brussels. TUESDAY: First quarter figures for UK acquisitions and mergers; and April provisional producer price index numbers from the CSO. Bank of England publishes April figures for capital issues and redemptions. US retail sales (April); consumer price index (April); and industrial production and capacity use. WEDNESDAY: US business inventories (March). Italian government's economic programme to be presented to parliament. Publication of list of applications for ITV franchises. THURSDAY: Labour market statistics: unemployment and unfilled vacancies (April provisional); average earnings indices (March provisional); employment, hours, productivity and unit wage costs; and industrial disputes, from Department of Employment. April provisional figures of vehicle production from the SMMT. March index of output of the production industries; and first quarter provisional figures for capital expenditure by the manufacturing industries, from the CSO. Bank of England publishes first quarter figures for financing of the central government borrowing requirement; and monetary statistics including M4 sectoral analysis; and bank and building society sterling lending. US housing starts (April). Shell interim statement. FRIDAY: Treasury publishes April public sector borrowing requirment. Retail prices index and tax and price index for April from the CSO. US merchandise trade (March). Two-day conference called by President Lech Walesa to discuss state of Polish economy, Warsaw.
