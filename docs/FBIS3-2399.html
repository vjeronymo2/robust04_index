Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Changsha, March 4 (XINHUA) -- Hunan Province, central China, has formulated a series of preferential policies to encourage overseas businessmen to invest in local agriculture. The policies involve projects of agricultural infrastructure construction, fine-breed livestock production, forestry and aquatic production, modern agricultural technology and export-oriented agricultural production. According [to] the policies, the investment and co-operation can be in the form of funds, materials, industrial property rights or special technology. The policies stipulate that overseas businessmen engaged in agricultural development and running enterprises in farming, animal husbandry and aquatic production can be exempted from land rent for 10 to 15 years. Those who run joint ventures in forestry enterprises will be exempted from land rent for 20 to 30 years. The provincial government has also drawn up preferential policies for export-oriented agricultural production, including issuing export licences, and providing pesticides, fertilizer, seeds and necessary services.
