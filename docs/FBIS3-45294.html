Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, 15 Jan (XINHUA) -- During his meeting with former U.S. President George Bush at Zhongnanhai this morning, President Jiang Zemin said that China and the United States have differences but that in the final analysis [gui gen dao di 2981 2704 0451 1646], Sino-U.S. relations should improve. Jiang Zemin told Bush: Great changes have taken place in China since your last visit to China. A stable, developing, and prosperous China is not only in line with the interests of the Chinese people but also with those of the American people and the world at large. It is impossible for China to have stability and development without world peace and development. On the other hand, world peace and development also need stability and development in China. Jiang Zemin pointed out: "Both China and the United States are influential countries on the world scene. They share extensive common interests and shoulder heavy responsibilities in safeguarding world peace and stability, and in working for common development and prosperity. Although differences exist between us, in the final analysis, Sino-U.S. relations should improve. This meets the needs of world peace and development and accords with the wishes of the people of China and the United States as well as of people throughout the world." Bush said that he was pleased to see the significant changes that have taken place in Beijing. He stressed that U.S.-Chinese relations are very, very important, adding that to safeguard and strengthen such ties accords with the common interests of the two sides. Present at the meeting were Vice Foreign Minister Liu Huaqiu and Scowcroft, former national security adviser to the U.S. president. After the meeting, which lasted more than an hour, President Jiang Zemin and Mr. Bush took a brief walk in a small garden of Zhongnanhai. During their conversation, Jiang Zemin asked Bush to visit China more often in the future. Bush replied that he would be glad to do so.
