BFN [Text] Vientiane, April 7 (KPL) -- Australian Prime Minister Paul Keating, yesterday morning handed over to Lao Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon the border control building on the Lao side of the friendship bridge. Mr. Vang Lattanavong, Lao ambassador to Australia and Mr. Michael Mann, Australian ambassador to Laos were among high ranking officials from both sides at the solemn hand-over ceremony. In his speech, Prime Minister Paul Keating said that speaking on behalf of the Australian Government and people, he was pleased to hand over the border control building to the Lao Government and people. He referred to the bridge as a door opening up to the development in the country and the adjacent facilities were regarded as the key of the development door. The building houses immigration, customs, police, banks and postal offices. He said that the border control building and the Mekong bridge itself could best serve to testify, in the concrete term, the fruitful Australian-Lao cooperation and friendship. Mr. Paul Keating congratulated both Australian and Lao technicians and workers whose high sense of responsibility fullfilled the construction targets in terms of time and budget. Australian prime minister touched upon the significance of the Australian-Lao agreement for the promotion and protection of investment which would help establish confidence for potential Australian investors. Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon of Laos in his return address, speaking on behalf of the Lao people, said that the facilities were built with Australian grant. Apart from the Mekong bridge, the border control building, the gifts of the Australian Government and people to Laos, would facilitate the work of the Lao personnel. Mr. Khamtai Siphandon expressed thanks once again to the Australian side for its generosity and good intention. He said that the Australian aid projects for Laos were highly appreciated. They were the symbol for the ever increasing traditional relations of friendship the two countries had enjoyed. The Lao prime minister attached great importance to the Australian aid which had instrumentally helped building the country's economic infrastructure and helped free Laos from a state of economic difficulties. After delivering their speeches, both prime ministers officially lifted the veil off the building foundation plate. Mr. Paul Keating and his delegation left Laos on board of a special plane for an official visit to the Kingdom of Thailand. The Australian prime minister paid a two-day official visit to the Lao PDR [Lao People's Democratic Republic] on April 5 and 6.
