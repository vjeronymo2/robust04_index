BFN [Excerpt] Tokyo, April 22 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa said Friday [22 April] he did not personally have a hand in a failure to report some 29 million yen of his personal income to tax authorities. "It is common for legislators to have their offices handle accounting and raising of funds, including their personal funds," Hosokawa said. Hosokawa's chief political secretary, Namio Yatsuka, told a news conference earlier in the day that the prime minister did not disclose the 29 million yen income from stock and bond investment during a 10-year period from 1981.
