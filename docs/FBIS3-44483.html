Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, December 30 (XINHUA) -- China is to afforest a total of 4.48 million ha and achieve a forestry output value of 152 billion yuan in 1994, according to Minister of Forestry Xu Youfang. These figures will represent an increase of nine percent over 1993. Xu said that the afforestation work will be linked more closely with promoting the rural economy and helping peasants to shake off poverty. Forestry should yield environmental and social as well as economic returns, xu stressed. To meet next year's target, the minister said, China will develop more cash trees and trees for industrial use, while continuing the construction of state key forestry projects. China will deepen the reform of the state-run forestry centers and promote the development of township and village tree farms. The state will also help the township and village tree farms to transform themselves into shareholding enterprises to benefit local farmers.
