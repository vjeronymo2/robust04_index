BFN [Report by Kiril Vulchev] [Excerpts] Sofia, May 18 (BTA) -- Prime Minister Lyuben Berov submitted structural and personnel changes of his cabinet to parliament. He did it when taking the vote; the MP's decided that the first item on today's agenda would be the debate on last Wednesday's no-confidence vote motion of the parliamentary Union of Democratic Forces (SDS). The SDS motivation for the no-confidence vote is "the prime minister's inability to organize and direct the activities of the government." This is the sixth no-confidence vote tabled by the parliamentary SDS so far. [passage omitted] Following the opening of the debate on the no-confidence vote, the Prime Minister surprisingly introduced the intended cabinet changes. He nominated two deputy prime ministers. If approved by parliament, Assoc. Prof. Rumen Gechev, an economist, will be a deputy prime minister without portfolio coordinating the structural adjustment of the country's real economy, helping in the implementation of the government's general economic policy, acting as a coordinator for the PHARE [Economic and Reconstruction Aid for Poland and Hungary] Programme and supervising the utilization of loans for the investments they are designed for, as well as the allocations of the State Fund for Reconstruction and Development. Kiril Tsochev is Prof. Berov's nominee as a deputy prime minister who will supervise Bulgaria's balance of finances in connection with the foreign debt deals and the fluctuation of prices and the lev's exchange rate. This deputy prime minister will have to work for attracting foreign investments and promote the government's relations with private business. Mr. Tsochev will also be the minister of trade. When reporters asked the MP's who Mr. Tsochev is, nobody seemed to know anything about him; the prime minister was heard saying in the parliamentary lobbies that Mr. Tsochev had not been promoted by the BSP [Bulgarian Socialist Party]. The prime minister asked the MP's to accept Valentin Karabashev's resignation as minister of trade he filed on April 28. According to Prof. Berov, he and Mr. Karabashev have had differences over the variant of privatization to be implemented in Bulgaria since last spring. The prime minister declared himself to be for the elimination of committees because the Constitution adopted in 1991 does not provide for such institutions. Prof. Berov proposed to set up two new ministries instead -- of energy and of communications, and promised to reorganize another three committees without setting up new ministries to replace them. He nominated Nikita Shervashidze, so far chairman of the Power Engineering Committee, as minister of energy, and Krustyu Mirski as minister of communications, specifying the latter was an expert in this field. Prof. Berov proposed to dismiss Minister of Justice Petur Kornazhev and replace him by NSD [New Alliance for Democracy] MP Gospodin Atanasov. If parliament approves the proposed changes, Prof. Berov's new cabinet will have 19 instead of 17 members, and four government agencies instead of three. If his proposals are accepted next week, the prime minister would like a framework agreement between the government and the parliamentary forces or the individual MP's supporting it that will guarantee the adoption of the bankruptcy and the scheme of composition legislation as well as a bill amending the Privatization Act by the end of June at the latest. If the agreement is not signed, the cabinet is going to resign. The resignation will spoil the foreign debt deal, the mass privatization project will fail and Bulgaria's economic development will be seriously troubled, Prof. Berov told the MP's. While Prof. Berov was introducing the cabinet changes, the DPS [Rights and Freedoms Movement] MP's walked out of the parliamentary hall. Sherife Mustafa of the DPS told BTA that the DPS MP's were utterly surprised by the personnel changes which Prime Minister Berov had not discussed with the parliamentary DPS. According to Mrs. Mustafa, the nomination of Mr. Gechev as a deputy prime minister is unacceptable for the DPS because of his BSP affiliation. Holding a joint meeting later on, the DPS, the NSD and the group of independent MP's assessed the proposed changes as renunciation of the original government formula. The DPS and NSD parliamentary groups came out with a joint declaration stating they would not vote for Prof. Berov's cabinet changes. In their opinion, Prof. Berov's are political appointees rather than experts as was the original formula of the government. The new formula is actually a coalition government with the BSP prominently represented in it. By submitting his project for structural and personnel cabinet changes at a time when a procedure for debating a no-confidence vote has already been opened Prime Minister Lyuben Berov, succumbing to pressure by the BSP, violated the Constitution, Petya Shopova of the Civil Alliance for the Republic (GOR), a left-of-centre formation, said after a meeting of the GOR and DPS parliamentary leaderships. BSP leader Zhan Videnov said that most of Prof. Berov's nominees were entirely new for him. The BSP will discuss its attitude to the submitted cabinet changes at tomorrow's plenum of the party leadership and the leaders of its parliamentary group. Asked if he considers his resignation as a possible option, the prime minister said he would go if parliament voted no confidence in him. The no-confidence motion will be put to the vote after 1800 hours tomorrow because under the Constitution, 24 hours should elapse between the debates and the voting procedure.
