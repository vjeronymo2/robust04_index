May 18, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

ASSEMBLYMAN'S AIDE TO OFFER HELP ON HOMEOWNER'S, RENTER'S CLAIMS

Residents of the 59th Assembly District are being offered free help with their homeowner's and renter's assistance claim forms beginning Monday through June 30.

Rachel Rivas-Crook, field representative to Assemblyman Charles M. Calderon (D-Montebello), will help claimants complete their forms at three locations in the Southeast area.

She will be available Tuesdays at the Peter F. Schabarum Senior Center, 1556 Central Ave.; Wednesdays at the Montebello Senior Citizens Center, 115 S. Taylor Ave., and Thursdays at the Department of Recreation and Community Services, 6767 S. Passons Blvd., Pico Rivera. All times will be from 9 a.m. to noon.

Pertinent records such as property tax bills, addresses and telephone numbers of landlords, and prior claim forms should be brought to the meeting.

Assistance also will be available July 3 through Aug. 31 by appointment only at Calderon's district office, 1712 W. Beverly Blvd., Suite 210. The deadline for filing is Sept. 1.

The homeowner's assistance program is a once-a-year reimbursement by the state on a portion of property taxes paid on homes. To file, individuals must have lived in or owned their home by Dec. 31, 1988, and received less than $12,000 annual household income. Applicants must be 62 or older, blind or disabled.

The renter's assistance program also is a once-a-year reimbursement by the state of a portion of the property taxes presumed to have been included in tenants' rent payments. Qualified applicants must have lived in a rented residence for all or part of 1988, paid $50 or more per month rent, and meet the same low-income, age and disability restrictions.
