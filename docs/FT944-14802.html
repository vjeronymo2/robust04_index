941021 FT 21 OCT 94 / Views sought on planned Gas split Key aspects of the planned split of British Gas into trading and transportation arms are reviewed in a consultation document by Ofgas, the industry regulator, published yesterday, David Lascelles writes. Ofgas is seeking comments on terms which would oblige TransCo, the transportation and storage arm, to offer the same terms to British Gas and independent gas suppliers. The regulator said: 'Short of divestment, full and effective separation represents the best protection against British Gas' trading businesses benefiting from being part of the same company as Transco.' Mr Cedric Brown, British Gas chief executive, said it was a 'tough but realistic package' but added: 'We recognise the need for effective separation at home but this must not hinder our ability to compete successfully in overseas markets.'
