931201 FT 01 DEC 93 / Company VAT registrations fall The number of companies registered for value added tax dropped last year for the first time in more than a decade, figures from the Department of Trade and Industry showed yesterday. There was net decline of 41,000 businesses, or 2.5 per cent of the stock last year, following annual average increases of 36,000 each year since 1979. The figures are based on VAT registration information collected by Customs and Excise Of 1.6m businesses registered at the start of last year, there were 189,000 registrations and 230,000 de-registrations. The number of businesses declined in all regions except Northern Ireland. The largest falls were in south-east and south-west England. The largest decline was in construction, which showed a net de-registration of 20.9 per cent. Retail fell by 8.2 per cent, and production by 4.1 per cent. There were also declines in catering, transport, motor trades and agriculture. The largest increase was just 1.1 per cent for finance, property and professional services and 1.1 per cent for other businesses. VAT Registrations and De-registrations 1980-92.
