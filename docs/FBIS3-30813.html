Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] We go over to Monika Olejnik, who is at the government hotel on Parkowa Street: [Olejnik] The talks between the Polish Peasant Party [PSL] and the Democratic Left Alliance [SLD] have just ended, and a communique was read to us. It states that Poland needs economic development, an improvement in people's living standards, political stabilization, and a credible position in the world. The SLD and the PSL confirm this aim and express conviction as to the need for joint actions for its implementation. The PSL and the SLD adopt the principle of solid support for the draft budget presented by the government. After discussing the resignation of Deputy Premier Marek Borowski and the circumstances accompanying it, the representatives of the SLD and the PSL express recognition of his knowledge, competence, and activism; they confirm that the SLD will present a candidature for the vacant post, in accordance with the coalition agreement, that together with its appendixes remains fully applicable. The PSL and SLD leaders told journalists after the meeting that the coalition between the PSL and the SLD still exists. Marek Borowski told us that he knows the candidacy of the person who should take over the post of deputy prime minister and minister of finance in his place, but he will not reveal his name to us.
