Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA Jan. 27 -- Deputy Prime Minister Phan Van Khai received here today Mr. James Gustav Speth, director general of the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), now on a working visit to Vietnam. Mr. Le Mai, vice foreign minister and Mr. Roy D. Morey resident representative of the UNDP office in Hanoi were also present at the reception. During the reception, Director General J. Gustav Speth highly appreciated the relations between Vietnam and UNDP in recent years. He expressed the UNDP's good will to continue promoting bilateral relations. Mr. Speth told Mr. Khai the UNDP's programme of actions in the coming time and affirmed that in the immediate time the UNDP would continue giving its technological assistance in the fields of economy, law, administrative organization and in the development of resources. He also hailed Vietnam's effective use of foreign aid and assured that the UNDP would continue to play an important role in mobilizing aid for Vietnam's re-construction. For his part, Mr. Khai welcomed the UNDP director general's first visit to Vietnam as a significant event in the relations between Vietnam and the UNDP. He highly valued the UNDP's assistance to the Vietnamese people in the past and expressed his wish for the further promotion of bilateral relations. He affirmed Vietnam has always respected and highly appreciated the UNDP's technological aid even as the official development aid (ODA) would increase.
