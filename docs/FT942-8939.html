940518 FT 18 MAY 94 / Observer: Out of the bag Who was the journalist who has admitted making up previous accounts of clashes between the Bank of England and the Treasury? Kenneth Clarke, the 'business friendly' Chancellor of the Exchequer, dropped the tantalising reference into his CBI speech last night in a bid to prove that the publication of the regular monetary chats between the Treasury and the Bank of England led to a better informed debate. The Chancellor likened his actions to introducing 'Red Indians to whisky' - it was heady stuff for journalists, he said. But who was the journalist who let the side down? Surely not Rupert Pennant-Rea, ex-editor of the Economist and now deputy governor of the Bank of England. All Clarke's officials would say was that it was a 'state secret'.
