Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN ["B.I.M."-attributed report: "Seselj Threatens Muslims and Montenegrins"] [Text] Podgorica -- "Where are those who threatened me not to come to Montenegro?" Dr. Vojislav Seselj asked the members of the Serbian Radical Party [SRS] and its sympathizers, who gathered to hail their leader on Saturday evening. To support his claim that there are no obstacles for him in "Serbian Montenegro," the leader of the Serbian Radicals quoted his visit to Plav, which is mainly inhabited by Muslims. "I strolled through Plav yesterday, and nobody dared utter a word," boasted Seselj who was received in the Budo Tomovic youth hall in Podgorica with chants Serbia! Serbia! and well-known Chetnik songs (Who is the second, I am the first to drink Turkish blood). Seselj spoke mostly about the progress that has been made in the creation of a greater Serbia, calling on the Muslims and the Croats in the Republic of Croatia to "accept the peace on the front lines as this is the most fair. If they do not want this, the Serbs will conquer even those territories that they lost because of the betrayal by the former Yugoslav Peoples' Army." For this reason, the Serbs have lost Dubrovnik, Zadar, Sibenik, Karlovac, Virovitica, and Osijek ("all Serbian towns"), however, "one must be realistic" now and form a unified Serbian state with one parliament and one president on the territories that the Serbs "have managed to liberate." "The SRS is a sword hanging over the heads of enemies of the Serbs," Seselj stated and added that "the Muslims in Sandzak are preparing for an uprising," however "let them, as several tens of thousands of SRS members and the Serbian Chetnik movement members are ready to go to these territories at any moment to prevent the fragmentation of the Serbian lands." The revenge will be bloody and there will be no mercy, Seselj added. Dr. Seselj also reviewed the efforts of individual Montenegrin parties (the Liberal Alliance of Montenegro and the Social Democratic Party) to separate Montenegro from the Serbian lands, which he described as "the betrayal of Serbianhood by foreign mercenaries," who would not have entered the Assembly had the Albanians, the Muslims and the Catholics in Boka Kotorska not voted for them.
