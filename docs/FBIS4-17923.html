BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vadim Byrkin] [Text] Moscow 26 April TASS -- President Yuriy Meshkov of the Republic of the Crimea, Admiral Eduard Baltin, commander of the Black Sea Fleet, and Viktor Semyonov, chairman of the Sevastopol City Council of People's Deputies have issued a joint statement urging the presidents of Russia and Ukraine to resume the interrupted Russo-Ukrainian talks on the Black Sea Fleet and "to act as the guarantors of peace in the Republic of the Crimea," a spokesman at the fleet press centre told ITAR-TASS by telephone. The authors of the statement expressed utter regret and concern over the frustration of the Russo-Ukrainian talks on dividing the fleet. They believe that the "struggle for political power and the presidency of Ukraine becomes a factor which can turn the process of negotiations on the problems of the Black Sea Fleet into a military conflict." Attempts are being made to lay the blame for a possible break-up of Ukraine's statehood on the Republic of the Crimea and severely punish it for that. It is no problem by means of provocations to bring about an unstable situation in the republic and, making use of that, introduce a state-of-emergency regime, putting Russia-Ukraine relations on the brink of war, the authors of the statement maintain.
