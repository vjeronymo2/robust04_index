BFN [Text] The Environmental Protection Organization has announced that the oil spill resulting from the collision of two oil tankers in the Gulf of Oman is moving in a southerly direction and poses no threat to the coastline of the Islamic Republic of Iran. According to an IRNA report, the official in charge of research of the Environmental Protection Organization announced Iran's readiness to cooperate with the Persian Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] and other international environmental protection organizations in order to clean up this oil spill and said: The spill is moving in a southerly direction on the Sea of Oman from a point east of the Straits of Hormuz.
