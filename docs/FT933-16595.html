930702 FT 02 JUL 93 / UK Company News: Warburg top in survey SG WARBURG Securities took the title of top stockbroking research company for the third year running in Extel's 1993 Survey of Financial Analysts. James Capel, which dominated the awards for the entire 1980s, reclaimed the number two slot from NatWest Securities which slipped to third. Extel had replies to its survey from 122 fund managers who collectively manage Pounds 725bn of funds. UBS slipped furthest, from 4th to 6th position and was voted as having shown the most deterioration in quality of research. Credit Lyonnais Laing (10th) and Smith New Court (7th) showed the greatest quality improvement and were only a few votes short of UBS.
