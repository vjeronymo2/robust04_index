BFN [Text] Cairo, 1 Jun (MENA)-- Egypt and the European Investment Bank [EIB] have signed an agreement granting Egypt a 120-million-pound loan to be allocated completely to private small industries. Dr. Yusuf Butrus Ghali, minister of state for international cooperation, and EIB Deputy Director Robert Dillon signed the agreement. Dr. Ghali pointed out that the agreement is within the context of the government's efforts to consolidate the private sector and emphasize its crucial role in economic development in Egypt. He added that the loan is a significant first step in the government's efforts to guarantee the provision of the financial instruments needed for developing the private sector, like long-term and easy loans to suit small industries employing a maximum of 10 workers. He remarked that small industries are the backbone of the policy that aims to increase production and employment rates.
