BFN [DIE PRESSE report: "Charles IV Prize: Prague Warns and Placates"] [Text] Nuremberg/Krumlov -- On the occasion of the awarding of the "European Charles IV Prize" of the Sudeten German Landsmannschaft in Nuremberg at Whitsun weekend, Foreign Minister Alois Mock said that the expulsion of the Sudeten Germans after World War II was "one of the major unlawful acts" of the 20th century. However, Mock also recalled that the crimes of the National Socialists had preceded the forcible resettlement. In Krumov, Prague's Foreign Minister Josef Zieleniec told Austrian journalists that Mock's comments in connection with the awarding of the Charles IV Prize "might burden bilateral relations." "However, the whole affair should not be overestimated either."
