BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 13 (KCNA) -- Talks between delegations of the Workers' Party of Korea and the Japan Social-Democratic Party (JSDP) were held here Sunday. Present at the talks on the Korean side were vice department director of the C.C., the WPK Kim Yang-kon and other officials concerned and on the Japanese side were the members of the delegation led by director of the General Affairs Department of the Central Executive Committee of the JSDP and member of the House of Representatives Kazo Watanabe. At the talks the Korean side said the Japanese authorities must renounce the hostile policy against the DPRK and solve the DPRK-Japan relations in the spirit of the three-party joint declaration and clarified the principled stand of the DPRK toward the nuclear issue. The Japanese side said it is the JSDP's stand to always develop friendly relations with the WPK and strive for peace and security in Asia. It also stressed that the "nuclear issue" of Korea should be solved peacefully through dialogue and negotiation, not "sanctions" and pressure.
