Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- Ladislav Pittner, a Christian Democratic Party (KDH) member, was appointed Slovak interior minister today. He was named to the post for the second time -- he was interior minister from November 1990 to June 1992 when he replaced Anton Andras who had to leave due to disagreement with Premier Vladimir Meciar. Pittner was born on May 18, 1934, in Malacky, west Slovakia. As a secondary school student he was imprisoned in 1950 and along with other students sentenced to several years in prison for "anti-state activity and espionage." After he was freed in 1953 he was employed as a worker for 14 years in the Tatra-Regen factory in Bratislava. In 1970 he graduated from Bratislava's School of Economics and in 1975 became a scientific worker. Up to 1990 he worked in the Economy and Construction Institute in Bratislava. In November 1989 he was elected chairman of a strike committee and shortly afterwards chairman of the Public Against Violence (VPN) organisation in the institute where he worked. Later he became a member of the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH). From September 1990 till November 1990 he was deputy director of the department for the protection of the constitution and democracy in the Federal Interior Ministry. In April 1991 he was named a colonel. In the 1992 elections Pittner was elected a Slovak parliament deputy for the KDH. At the 27th parliamentary session he proposed a confidence vote in Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar's government.
