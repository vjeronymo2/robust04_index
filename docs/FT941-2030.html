940323 FT 23 MAR 94 / Surgeons may quit NHS Surgeons are threatening to resign from the National Health Service and sell their services back to hospitals in protest at government reform of the NHS, the British Medical Association said yesterday. Mr John Chawner, chairman of the BMA's senior hospital doctors committee, said the move would give surgeons more freedom to decide treatment for patients based on clinical need rather than according to managers' budgets. Mr Chawner added: 'What they are thinking about is setting up businesses and hiring themselves back to the health service on terms they would then agree with the purchasers.'
