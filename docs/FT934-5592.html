931129 FT 29 NOV 93 / Survey of Software at Work - Winter 1993 (11): Supplier/product profile Product: Geo/Navigator is a Visual Database (GIS), launched in October 1991, currently on version 3.0. It allows users to organise data, maps, pictures, drawings, and charts into one easy-to-use Visual Information System which can be linked to corporate databases. Suppliers: PAX Technology, founded in the UK in March 1991, is a privately owned company. Turnover: not disclosed (privately held partnership). Employees: 15 worldwide. Number of users: 530 sites. Price: Pounds 5,500 for the full development system, run-time licences start at Pounds 1,095.00. Hardware and operating systems: Apple and Windows PCs: can also be linked to servers, Unix or proprietary, over a network. Sales and support-maintenance: Direct with companies and government, also via systems integrators, system dealers, and OEMs, such as Rothwell Group and Datapro ABS in Brighton. Other services available: Training, project consultancy, project implementation and support. User views: Pros: ease of use, ease of data interchange, auto update and filing reminders, video facility, ease of back-up, robust, small memory requirement, mouse-up online-offline facility for 'suspended animation', excellent support. Cons: Small operator 'young but growing', poor documentation (no index), 'ameliorated by excellent support'. Contact: David Bethel, Pax Technology, Hither Ascension House, Lambridge Wood Road, Henley On Thames RG9 3BS.
