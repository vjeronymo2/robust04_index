940901 FT 01 SEP 94 / International Company News: Iscor posts sharp improvement A surging local market and higher export prices have allowed Iscor, South Africa's largest iron and steel company, to report a dramatic improvement in earnings after four years of decline, writes Mark Suzman in Johannes-burg. Attributable earnings for the 12 months to June rose by 81 per cent to R512m (Dollars 113m), up from R283m a year ago, Turnover rose 9 per cent to R9.82bn. Iscor also reported a positive cash flow for the first time since it listed four years ago. Net debt dropped R401m to R1.72bn from R2.1bn while the debt/equity ratio improved to 24.9 per cent from 33.2 per cent.
