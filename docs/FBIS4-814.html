BFN [Text] Johannesburg April 16 SAPA -- The situation is reported to be calm in Lesotho following the murder of the deputy prime minister, Mr Selometsi Baholo, earlier this week, SABC [South African Broadcasting Corporation] radio news reports. South Africa's ambassador to Lesotho, Mr Gerhard Visser, said people were going about their daily tasks and that the border posts had been reopened. However, despite the calm, several government ministers were reported to be fearing for their lives and did not attend Friday's [15 April] cabinet meeting.
