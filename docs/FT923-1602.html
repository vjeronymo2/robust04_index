920922 FT 22 SEP 92 / International Company News: Circle K rises to Dollars 8.2m CIRCLE K, the Arizona-based convenience store chain which has been operating under Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection since 1990, yesterday turned in first-quarter operating profits of Dollars 8.2m, compared with Dollars 6.4m a year earlier. The company, which has agreed to be acquired for about Dollars 402m by an investor group, CK Acquisitions, led by Bahrain-based Investcorp, said it expected to file an amended reorganisation plan by the end of this month. The company suffered a net loss of Dollars 2.7m, or 10 cents a share, in the quarter, reflecting the costs associated with its reorganisation. A year earlier, Circle K recorded a net loss of Dollars 500,000, or 5 cents a share. Mr Bart Brown, chairman, said: 'Given the costs and complications associated with the Chapter 11 process, we are pleased with these first-quarter results.'
