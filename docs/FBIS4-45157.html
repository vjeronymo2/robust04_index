BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of fileworthy reports from 19-26 May on counternarcotics operations in countries monitored by FBIS London Bureau. Source, date, and time of broadcast are given in parentheses at the end of each item.

Iran
The third stage of the Sa'eqeh exercise, which was held to renew the fight against drug smugglers, to identify and destroy the production and distribution networks of narcotics, and to cleanse the areas infected by these substances, has ended successfully. Brig Shafi'i, the commander of the office of the fight against drugs of the law enforcement forces, announced the above on 22 May during an interview and said: During this week-long exercise, units of the Law Enforcement Forces throughout the country created 1,425 undercover units, cleansed 982 infected areas, discovered over 3,320 kg of drugs, and arrested 2,678 smugglers and distributers of drugs and over 4,000 addicts. The commander of the office of the fight against drugs of the Law Enforcement Forces of the nation spoke of the activities of his forces during the past two months, saying: Following the efforts of the personnel of the Law Enforcement Forces of the Islamic Republic of Iran, during this period, 16,747 kg of various drugs were discovered in the hands of smugglers throughout the country. (Tehran Voice of the Islamic Republic of Iran First Program Network in Persian 0930 GMT 22 May 94)
Russia
An officer of the Moscow region organized crime department and an officer of the special militia force were detained in downtown Moscow on Thursday by Moscow criminal militia department agents when the former tried to sell three kilograms of opium. CRIME-TASS learned at the Moscow criminal militia department that long investigative work preceded the arrest. A criminal case was instituted. (Moscow ITAR-TASS in English 0855 GMT 26 May 94)
