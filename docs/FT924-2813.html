921210 FT 10 DEC 92 / UK Company News: Costly newspaper bill for Murray Intl A disastrous venture into newspaper publishing by Murray International Holdings, the holding company controlled by Mr David Murray, a leading Scottish businessman and chairman of Rangers football club, cost the company Pounds 5.7m. Murray International, whose main business is steel stockholding but which also has interests in property, leisure and electronics, closed the Sunday Scot tabloid in July 1991 after only five months. The group, 88 per cent owned by Mr Murray, lost Pounds 1.54m in the year ended January 31 1992 on sales of Pounds 165m (Pounds 135.5m). A loss of Pounds 1.6m from the Sunday Scot as shown as an extraordinary item. The results for 1991 showed a loss of Pounds 4.2m for the newspaper as an exceptional item, although it occurred in the next financial year. In 1991 the group lost Pounds 5.4m. Murray is transferring its property interests to Premier Property, a new company with the same shareholders as Murray International.
