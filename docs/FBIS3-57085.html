Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow January 19 TASS -- The Russian State Duma will devote one hour every Friday to discussing the activities of the gvernment and the Agrarian Party plans to support its leader and Deputy Premier Aleksandr Zaveryukha before the new composition of the government is announced. The Duma decided on Wednesday [19 January] that it will devote the last hour of each Friday to discussing the work of the government which lawmaker Georgiy Zadonskiy said would allow the lower chamber to "quickly react to various decisions of Viktor Chernomyrdin's team". The lawmakers are also going to discuss on Wednesday a statement of the Agrarian Party on the current situation in agriculture and plan to do that before a scheduled meeting of the president and the prime minister who are to determine the new composition of the government. The president and the premier stumbled over the candidacy of Finance Minister and Marketeer Boris Fedorov who said he might remain in the government only in case Zaveryukha is dismissed. Zaveryukha is responsible for agricultural policy in the government.
