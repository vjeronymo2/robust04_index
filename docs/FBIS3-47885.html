Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Noboru Hoshuyama, director of the Defense Agency [DA] Director General's Secretariat, speaking at a 17 January news conference on DA plans to procure surveillance satellites, said: "On the prerequisite that there are no political constraints, it is desirable to have surveillance satellites from the standpoint of improving intelligence capabilities." He thus indicated hopes that the DA would have such satellites in the future. Hoshuyama also admitted that "research and studies are being conducted on the usefulness of surveillance satellites" at various DA bureaus and departments and at staff offices of the Ground, Maritime, and Air Defense Forces.
