930728 FT 28 JUL 93 / Anger as Ulster bags bulk of EC grant LABOUR MPs last night accused the prime minister of using the European Community regional aid package to buy Ulster Unionist votes on the Maastricht treaty. The attack followed the government's announcement that Northern Ireland will receive more than half of the nearly Pounds 2bn-worth of EC funding allotted to help the UK's poorest regions over the next five years. Mr Tim Sainsbury, the industry minister, said in a Commons written answer yesterday that the province would receive Pounds 1.04bn, with Pounds 690m going to Merseyside and Pounds 260m to the Highlands and Islands. EC ministers last week decided how to apportion the Community's structural funds between the 12 member states. The UK receives funds for Northern Ireland, Merseyside and the Highlands and Islands because the regional gross domestic product of these regions is 75 per cent or less of the EC average. The government has divided the Pounds 2bn according to population. Mr George Howarth, MP for Knowsley North, was among Merseyside Labour MPs to attack the decision. He said: 'We did expect Pounds 1bn on Merseyside. It would appear that Mr Major is willing to pay an extremely large price for the nine Ulster Unionist votes last week.'
