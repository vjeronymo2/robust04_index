December 27, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: HEARST JR. TO REPORT ON FAMILY DYNASTY IN NEW BOOK

TNT BROADCASTING celebrated Christmas Day by running the epic Orson Welles movie "Citizen Kane," which sent those of us who remember the late William Randolph Hearst Sr. running back down memory lane. (The movie was said to be based on Hearst's life.)

Now comes word that William Randolph Hearst Jr. is writing a revealing insider account of the Hearst dynasty, to be titled "The Hearsts: Father and Son," coming from Roberts Rinehart Inc. in August, 1991. Publication will coincide with the 40th anniversary of Hearst Sr.'s death.

Hearst Jr. will not only write about his rags-to-riches grandparents, George and Phoebe Hearst, who struck it rich in gold, silver and copper out West, but the remarkable newspaper empire built by his famous dad. And he will tell how he and his four brothers grew up in the shadow of their father's genius.

The book will be studded with colorful anecdotes about Winston Churchill, Konrad Adenauer, Jawaharlal Nehru, Margaret Thatcher and almost every American President of the time.

Hearst Jr. selected Rinehart as his publisher because of the company's long publishing history, beginning with George Doran in the 19th Century.

Rinehart has its fingers crossed that "The Hearsts: Father and Son" will become a new "Anthony Adverse." (That was the novel that became the first best seller of the decade of the '30s.)
