Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, Feb 10 (AFP) -- NATO air strikes against Bosnian Serb positions around Sarajevo would lead to war in the Balkans and even Europe, a military spokesman for the rump Yugoslavia warned Thursday. "It would provoke a big war in the Balkans and even in Europe and UN peacekeepers would then become enemies of the Serbs," Colonel Ljubodrag Stojadinovic said in reponse to NATO's ultimatum warning the Serbs to withdraw all of their heavy weapons from around Sarajevo by February 21 or face raids by NATO warplanes. His warning, carried by the Bosnian Serb news agency SRNA, was the first official Serb reaction to the ultimatum, which was sparked by the weekend attack on a Sarajevo market that left 68 people dead and 197 wounded. Stojadinovic said the massacre was nothing but a "bloody and fabricated farce" adding that he backed calls by Bosnian Serb leader Radovan Karadzic for an investigation into the attack. Karadzic walked out of peace talks in Geneva Thursday, saying he refused to participate unless the responsibility for the mortar attack was determined. The semiofficial Yugoslav news agency TANJUG also criticized the NATO ultimatum Thursday saying that it reflected a decision made by "mediocre politicians." "A group of mediocre politicians, especially those within the European Community, are making mistakes that are becoming more and more serious, without learning any lessons from previous mistakes," the agency, which generally reflects the Serb government's opinion, said.
