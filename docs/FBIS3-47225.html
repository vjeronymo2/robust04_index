Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Bacolod City, four regional communist leaders who have broken with party founder Jose Maria Sison were arrested yesterday, the military said. General Pedro Cabuhay, commander of the Army's 63rd Infantry Battalion in Negros, said those captured included Arturo Tabara, alleged chief of the party organization, in charge of all guerrilla and party operations in the Visayas. Also arrested were former Roman Catholic priest Benjamin Escrupulo, secretary of the regional united front committee; Rufino Bianca, secretary of the Negros regional party committee, and Tony Lamayo, top guerrilla commander of the New People's Army in Negros.
