December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

WHAT WOULD FOOD BE WITHOUT CHEMICALS?

I read with enormous interest the news in Bruce Horovitz's column on marketing ("Firm Exercises its Social Consciousness," Dec. 5) that a new "socially conscious fast-food company" is introducing "chemical-free food" to the marketplace.

Since protein, fats, sugars, nucleic acids, salt and water are all chemicals that make up the food that we humans and our pets eat, I think you can understand why I am intrigued and excited by the revolutionary idea of "chemical-free food." I do hope that you plan to run a feature on this scientific breakthrough as soon as possible.

ROBERT METZGER

San Diego
