Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, March 29 (ATA) -- The Kosova question, the question of the Albanians in Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM], and the situation in the region in general were in the centre of the talk held on March 28 between the chairman of the Parliamentary Commission of Foreign Affairs of the People's Assembly, Eduard Selami, and the foreign minister of Denmark, Niels Helveg Petersen. With regard to the above questions, Mr. Selami presented the Albanian viewpoint laying the stress on the indispensability of the embargo towards Serbia, and the conditioning of its lifting with the solution of the Kosova question. He also explained the reasons why Albania recognized Macedonia, and one of them is the recognition of the rights of the Albanians in Macedonia. After he mentioned the three-year aid program by the Danish Government for Albania, Mr. Petersen concentrated on the situation in the region, in which there is a progress in the direction of involving the Serbs in the process. Regarding the question of Kosova, he said that he is for the presence of the CSCE observers, for an extended autonomy for Kosova and for a continuous pressure on Serbia. Mr. Selami explained to Mr. Petersen how the parliamentary commissions function.
