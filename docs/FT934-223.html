931230 FT 30 DEC 93 / TUC predicts large cuts in investment The Trades Union Congress yesterday predicted large cuts in investment as a result of government spending plans for the next three years. It said that by 1996-97 public capital would be 25 per cent below its level at the start of the recession, which it said was in 1989-90. Transport spending would fall 22 per cent in real terms between the current financial year and 1996-97, housing investment would fall 12 per cent and training and unemployment support 11 per cent. The TUC said the jobless and poor would be hardest hit by reductions in benefits. Mr John Monks, general secretary, said: 'It is becoming ever more clear that the government is targeting the most vulnerable in society. 'Instead of attacking the unemployed and the public sector, they should be using all available resources to strengthen the economy.'
