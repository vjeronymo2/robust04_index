Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] The Military Command of the Armed Forces for the Liberation of Angola [FALA] in Lunda Norte Province has urged the relevant South African authorities to withdraw without delay all South African Defense Force [SADF] soldiers whether they belong to the Buffalo Battalion or not. The Military Command says if the South African Government does not immediately extradite the mercenaries fighting alongside People's Armed Forces for the Liberation of Angola [FAPLA] units, it will be accused of destabilizing southern Africa because the presence of foreigners in the Lunda region is a violation of the spirit of Lusaka. The Military Command added that Pretoria, which has a diplomatic mission in Luanda, and South Africa, in its capacity as a sovereign state, cannot afford the luxury of saying it does not know what is going on. Members of the South African army have been deployed in Angola. Our source added that the South African mercenaries should appear before a court of law in their country for betraying South African laws, as stated by General Kotze. The Military Command noted its disappointed, saying that mercenaries wounded in action in Soyo in 1993 were welcomed as heroes at Jan Smuts Airport. One should see concrete action before one can believe in Gen. Kotze. Independent sources yesterday confirmed information given to the Voice of Resistance of the Black Cockerel in early March by Army General Abilio Kamalata Numa. The information spoke of renewed military operations in northern Angola. The international media says in its offensive, Luanda earmarked more than 3,000 men and dozens of tanks, as well as heavy artillery pieces, but encountered strong UNITA resistance in Dange-ia-Menha near Cuanza Norte Province. The MPLA [Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola] suffered heavy losses. Katangese mercenaries were among the victims. UNITA forces will not give up any area under its control until a general peace accord is signed in Lusaka.
