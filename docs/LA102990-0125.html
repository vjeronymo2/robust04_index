October 29, 1990, Monday, P.M. Final

THE SIDELINES;

WOOSNAM'S $1 MILLION TOPS LIST

Ian Woosnam, who went over the $1-million mark for a season for the first time and set a career-earnings record, finished first on the PGA European Tour money-winning list.

Woosnam tied Sandy Lyle for 21st place in the season-ending Volvo Masters at Stogrande, Spain, on Sunday, earning him $10,776. He clinched the earnings championship when his closest pursuer, Zimbabwe's Mark McNulty, finished in a four-way tie for fourth and won $35,207.

Woosnam, from Wales, finished the season with $1,125,365, compared with $994,780 for McNulty. Jose Maria Olazabal of Spain was third, with $852,141.

Woosnam's career earnings on the European tour climbed to a record $3.92 million.

The top American was Rick Hartmann, who finished 39th with $183,677.
