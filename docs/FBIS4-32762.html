BFN [Text] Warsaw, June 28 -- The Freedom Union (UW) has forwarded Michal Boni, former labour minister in the Jan Krzysztof Bielecki government, as its candidate for the president of Warsaw, it was announced on Tuesday. The Warsaw chapter of the Social-Democracy of the Republic of Poland (SDRP) declared its willingness for talks, but did not take a stand on Boni's candidature. The coalition of the Democratic Left Alliance (SLD) and the Polish Peasant Party (PSL) has won 29 out of 85 seats in the Warsaw Council. The SDRP will decide about the UW candidature after it learns the answer for its conditions presented in the talks the party is conducting now. If they are not met, the SDRP threatens it will boycott the election of the president of Warsaw who is chosen in the presence of at least two thirds of councillors. Warsaw SDRP said that the number of seats won in the local government elections obliges the SLD-PSL coaliton to care for the city. The party wants to have one third of seats in the Warsaw local government assembly. Leader of the Warsaw chapter of the SDRP Andrzej Zelazowski said that his party is conducting unofficial talks with representatives of other political groups except the right-wing parties about eventual coalitions in local government.
