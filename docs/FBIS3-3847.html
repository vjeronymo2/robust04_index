Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, March 24 (XINHUA) -- This, China's largest economic center is broadening ways for foreign co-operation. "The municipal government has published its first batch of ten urban infrastructure facilities open to foreign investment this year," a municipal official said. Included are 180 projects, each of which is scheduled to use at least 10 million U.S. dollars. The city plans to publish a second batch of ten infrastructure facilities, including a power plant, a subway, a railway, an airport, roads and bridges this year. In addition, it will list large industrial projects for foreign co-operation. These projects involve mainly petrochemicals, metallurgy, complete sets of power plants, telecommunications, electronics, tyres and computers. Foreign business people are welcome to invest in leasing or transforming shanghai's urban districts, the official said. According to the official, Shanghai approved the establishment of 3,650 foreign-funded projects involving a total contractual investment of 7.01 billion U.S. dollars last year. The municipal government has pledged to absorb more foreign investment this year than last year. According to statistics, Shanghai has used a total of 14.9 billion U.S. dollars in foreign investment and approved the establishment of 7,500 projects so far.
