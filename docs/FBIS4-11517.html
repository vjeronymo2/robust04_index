BFN [Text] A second British company will probably undertake oil exploration projects in Cuba. This news was released today during a news conference given by Michael Adda, head of the delegation from Caritad, a group which promotes trade with the Caribbean. Talks will be held throughout the week among members of the Caritad delegation and Cuban representatives, who will discuss projects on ways to establish agreements in tourism, agriculture, oil exploration, and maritime services. During the news conference it was also reported that the British secretary of state for trade and industry and a new group of British businessmen will visit Cuba in September. Adda said that some of the proposals submitted by investors of this group will be confirmed by the end of this year. He added that Great Britain must also contribute to the economic recovery of Cuba; we are awaiting with great expectations the measures that will probably be adopted by the special Cuban legislative session that will be held this week, he said. More than 100 contacts were held over the past few days between British and Cuban businessmen.
