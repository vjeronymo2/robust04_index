BFN [Text] Several settlement leaders in the territories said that in their meeting this morning, Prime Minister Rabin promised them not to evacuate Jews from Hebron, not to change the location of the Jewish neighborhoods in the town, and that all the settlements in the Gaza Strip, including Dugit, Netzarim, and Kfar Darom will remain where they are at least until the final settlement is implemented. The settlement leaders also told our correspondent Gil Litman that the prime minister confirmed in the meeting that there has been a significant rise in the number of stone-throwing and firebomb incidents in Judaea and Samaria in recent weeks. Rabin told them he believes there are now five times more incidents than there were in the past. Mr. Rabin promised the settlement leaders that the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] would step up its activities against those who disturb the peace in the territories. Those attending the meeting were Uri Ari'el, secretary of the Council of Jewish Settlements in Judaea, Samaria, and the Gaza Strip; Rabbi Yo'el Bin-Nun of 'Ofra, and Tzvi Hendel, head of the Hof 'Aza Regional Council.
