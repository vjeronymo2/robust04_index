Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russia and Kazakhstan are continuing their talks concerning the terms of leasing the launching site in Baykonur, and signing an agreement on military-technical cooperation. A spokesman for the Russian Space Agency (RSA) told Interfax that a delegation led by the Kazakh Prime Minister, Sergey Tereshchenko, is arriving in Moscow on Wednesday with this aim in view. According to the spokesman, "the delegation includes several groups of experts, each specializing in definite area of bilateral cooperation." The talks in Moscow pose a third round of bilateral endeavors to work out documents to be signed by the presidents of the two countries in late March. "This time the Kazakh delegation inclines to conclude the process of talks, and prepare all the documents for being signed, even if it has to stay in Moscow till the end of this month."
