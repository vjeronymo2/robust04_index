BFN [Unattributed report from the "This is the News" roundup: "Optimism Against Gloomy Background"] [Text] "On the whole, the necessary conditions for advancing along the path of reforms have been created in society," Russian Federation First Deputy Minister for the Economy Yakov Urinson declared yesterday at a government sitting, summing up the preliminary results of the first six months of 1994. By his assertion, the government program adopted earlier to stabilize the situation in the economy is beginning to show "tangible, positive results, and talk about the collapse and fatal line of the economy are unsubstantiated." As for specific figures, however, according to data from the Ministry for the Economy the GDP (in fixed prices) fell by almost 18 percent compared to the same period last year. Overall, the volume of industrial output went down by 27 percent, falling by almost half in the machine building and metalworking sectors. According to expert evaluations, money supply will increase by roughly 60 percent (8 percent per month). Inflation, by comparison with December of last year, will comprise around 90 percent. Industrial producers' wholesale prices will double.
