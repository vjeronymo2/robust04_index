July 16, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MEDIA BIAS ON ABORTION ISSUE

Thank you for the series. Since pro-life sentiment is as intense and widespread in this country as pro-choice sentiment is, how does it happen that journalists in big-city news organizations "don't seem to have many friends or colleagues who oppose abortion," so that they aren't even aware of the magnitude of pro-life rallies like the one in April?

What kind of hiring and promotion practices are producing whole staffs with the same blind spot? What other issues are being screened out in the same way, while the media spotlights converge momentarily on broccoli?

FAYE JEANIS

San Jose
