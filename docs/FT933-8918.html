930812 FT 12 AUG 93 / (CORRECTED) UK Company News: Cable and Wireless wins Hungarian contract Correction (published 13th August 1993) appended to this article. CABLE AND Wireless, the UK telecommunications group, has won a licence for a national paging network awarded by the Hungarian government. The licence was awarded to Radio Telecommunications Company, a joint venture in which C&W has a 49 per cent stake. Other partners include the state telecommunications company. The contract is a further boost to C&W's ambition to become a significant force in Hungary, whose state telecommunications company looks set to become the first of those in east European to be privatised. The contract will complement RTC's existing cellular mobile contract. It is investing Pounds 27m in infrastructure for the cellular network, which is due to launched at the end of this year. C&W's has other east European ventures in Belarus, Russia, and the Baltic states. However, its main investment focus is in the UK, through Mercury Communications, and in Asia Pacific, where it has a majority stake in Hong Kong Telecom. CORRECTION A company in which Cable and Wireless has a 49 per cent stake was this week awarded a licence to provide a national paging service in Bulgaria, not in Hungary as reported in some editions yesterday.
