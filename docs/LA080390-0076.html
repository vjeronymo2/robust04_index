August 3, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: POP/ROCK

Stones to Roll Into Prague: The Rolling Stones will play in Prague, Czechoslovakia, for the first time as part of festivities marking the country's overthrow of communism. President Vaclav Havel, who led last winter's revolution, is a fan of the group, and wants to meet Mick Jagger and other members of the band, an aide said Thursday. The concert is scheduled for Aug. 18, the last stop on the Stones' "Urban Jungle" tour. GREG BRAXTON
