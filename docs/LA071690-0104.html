July 16, 1990, Monday, Orange County P.M. Final

LOCAL;

GARDEN GROVE MAN, HELD FOR DRUNKENNESS, DIES IN POLICE CELL

A man who was arrested on suspicion of public drunkenness died in a holding cell Sunday night when he apparently choked on his own vomit, Garden Grove police said this morning.

Nasr Mohamed, 42, of Garden Grove, who was taken into custody at 8:25 p.m. for intoxicated behavior while standing outside a supermarket at Euclid Street and Chapman Avenue, was pronounced dead by paramedics about 10:15 p.m., Police Sgt. Steve Sanders said.

After taking Mohamed into custody, police took him to the police station, and locked him into one of three holding cells monitored by television cameras.

Sanders said Mohamed's body was discovered by Sgt. Dan Lyons, night watch commander, while he was making routine rounds of the cells.

Police Chief John Robertson said police could not foresee Mohamed's death. He described the man as a "cooperative intoxicated person," who immediately "laid down and fell right asleep."

Although autopsy results are not due until late this afternoon, police speculated that Mohamed died when he began vomiting and was unable to wake up fully.
