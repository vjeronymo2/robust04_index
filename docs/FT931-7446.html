930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / UK Company News: Polish expansion as ice cream producer bought UNILEVER has announced the purchase of Roma, a privately owned Polish ice cream producer, in the first deal of its kind in the country. The price was undisclosed, but Unilever is planning a Dollars 17.5m (Pounds 12m) expansion programme after buying out the majority of the factory's private owners and the Eastern European Development Fund (EEDF), managed by Invesco CEAM, which held a 31.3 per cent share in the plant. Unilever will also be covering Roma's capital investment debts. The EEDF saw Unilever pay some Dollars 3.3m for its initial Dollars 2.3m investment in the project made less than a year ago. Roma is located near Gdansk on the Baltic Coast and employs 60 people - it has a current daily production capacity of 15 tonnes of ice cream. Unilever has been importing its Cornetto, Viennetta and Magnum varieties under the Algida name.
