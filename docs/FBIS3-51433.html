Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Colonia de Sacramento, Uruguay, 17 Jan (EFE) -- The presidents of Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, and Uruguay today agreed to integrate Bolivia as an observer country in all the meetings of the work subgroups of the Common Market of the South (Mercosur). The decision was made during the fifth Mercosur summit, which ended in the city of Colonia, after a formal petition made by Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada. Presidents Carlos Menem of Argentina, Itamar Franco of Brazil, Juan Carlos Wasmosy of Paraguay, and Luis Alberto Lacalle of Uruguay issued a public declaration expressing their satisfaction with the Bolivian Government's desire to extend and strengthen its ties with Mercosur. The declaration states: "They thus considered it appropriate to respond to Bolivia's petition by inviting its representatives to participate, as observers, in the meetings of the work subgroups." The presidents also state that "within the framework of Bolivia's petition, agreements with Bolivia will be prioritized with the perspective of its future total participation in Mercosur." President Sanchez de Losada attended the summit as an observer and in his speech he stressed his country's interest in Mercosur without forgetting other integration agreements Bolivia has signed. Technically, Bolivia cannot join Mercosur because it already belongs to the Andean Pact, but it can associate with it through a free market system. The Bolivian president said before his colleagues that "we must recognize that we have failed" in Latin American integration "given the lack of political will to eliminate borders that are presently more in the minds of our countries' leaders than in geography." Sanchez de Losada added: "Our peoples have always considered themselves brothers and sisters within a great Latin American family. In this process of attaining an ideal, we have fallen and risen many times, but we are learning." "With past experience and the demands of our current times, we Latin Americans are taking more seriously the challenge of integration, and Mercosur is proof of this," he added. "We Bolivians are convinced that efforts like Mercosur, the Andean Pact, and the Group of Three have the common objective of making progress toward a greater and broader integration," he stated. Sanchez de Losada concluded by saying: "Bolivia plays a role in this process by being a point in common and a contact among regional groups. We are willing to play the role that corresponds to us given our continental vocation, our deep integrationist will, and our presence in the River Plate, Andean, Amazon, Pacific, and Atlantic areas."
