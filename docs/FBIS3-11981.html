Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Government and coca growers representatives have signed an agreement to end the tense situation in the Chapare tropical region. The five-article document ratifies the two parties' desire to fight trafficking and to prevent the eradication of plantations. It confirms that the government will continue to look for and destroy laboratories but without destroying coca plantations. Article 4 is the most controversial: It specifies that the government and coca-growers leaders will implement Articles 10, 14 and 24 of Law 1008. The government is committed to implement alternative development programs. It has also been agreed that mixed commissions will be created to deal with issues related to coca-leaf production. [Begin recording] [Unidentified reporter] Are you happy with the agreement? [Unidentified coca growers leader] We believe there is an agreement, but we will only be happy when the terms of the agreement are complied with. [end recording] The coca growers believe this agreement represents victory for them because the government has promised not to forcefully eradicate the sacred leaf. [Begin unidentified coca growers leader recording] Forceful eradication without an alternative crop cannot flourish. [end recording] The government believes the agreement will ensure peace and the implementation of alternative development in the coca-producing regions.
