930331 FT 31 MAR 93 / BT signs Pounds 40m supply deal BRITISH Telecommunications and Racal-Datacom yesterday announced a strategic alliance that could be worth up to Pounds 40m over three years to the data communications company based in Reading, Berkshire. Racal-Datacom has been named as BT's preferred supplier of networking equipment and accompanying support services. The equipment involved in the deal will include modems which enable computers to talk to each other over telephone lines, multiplexers which enable several computers to share the same telephone line simultaneously, and devices for integrated services digital networks (ISDN), the most advanced data communications technology. First product shipments are expected in June this year. Mr Adam Scott, BT customer equipment director, said it was the most important alliance so far in its sector. Mr Tim Holley, Racal-Datacom group chairman, said the deal encompassed everything from a simple link between two personal computers costing Pounds 1,000 to a multi-million-pound international network.
