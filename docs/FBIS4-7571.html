BFN [Text] Warsaw, March 31 -- Polish parliamentary delegation visit to Romania that ended on Wednesday "should break the political and economic deadlock in Polish-Romanian contacts," Senate Speaker Adam Struzik said on Thursday. He added that during the talks of the delegation with Romanian leading politicians, the Romanian side made it clear that the contacts between the two countries have weakened recently. Struzik hopes that after his forthcoming talks with the Polish prime minister and the minister for foreign economic relations the relations will be intensified. According to Struzik, Romania can be Poland's good economic partner.
