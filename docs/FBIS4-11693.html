BFN [Text] An IDF [Israel Defense Forces] soldier sustained moderate head injuries in a stoning incident in the vicinity of the village of al-Jib north of Jerusalem. He was transferred to the Hadassah-'En Kerem Hospital. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports that the soldier was driving near the village, which is under curfew, when his car came under a barrage of stones. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 0800 GMT on 31 March adds the following: "Terrorists fired at an IDF outpost in the Jabalyah refugee camp this morning. None of the soldiers were hurt. The troops returned fire, gravely injuring one of the terrorists. The other gunman escaped, and Army troops mounted a manhunt for him. Our correspondent reports that one of the terrorists used an M-16 rifle and the other a Kalashnikov against the soldiers. Riots erupted in the refugee camp in the wake of the incident."] Our correspondent notes that numerous Judaea and Samaria cities and villages remain under curfew today, including Nabulus and its environs; Tulkarm and the adjacent refugee camps; downtown Ramallah and the refugee camps near Ramallah; the al-Duhayshah refugee camp; and downtown Hebron. Owners of gas stations in the territories complained today that the IDF has been prohibiting the supply of cooking gas and fuel to the Judaea and Samaria stations. A check conducted by our correspondent revealed that for security reasons, the IDF has been barring the entry of fuel and cooking gas trucks into the area for over two weeks, and the local population's fuel and cooking gas supplies are running out.
