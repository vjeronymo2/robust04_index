910430 FT 30 APR 91 / UK Company News: Mayflower back in black THE Mayflower Corporation, which disposed of its Tri-ang toy interests last September, yesterday reported a swing from losses of Pounds 1.1m to profits of Pounds 449,000 pre-tax for 1990. Profits of Pounds 639,000 were achieved in the second half. At the year-end all group companies were trading profitably. Sales for the 12 months totalled Pounds 13.77m (Pounds 16.3m) - the group has interests in manufacturing, marketing, life assurance and pensions. The resultsbenefited from lower interest charges of Pounds 205,000 (Pounds 420,000) and exceptional income of Pounds 102,000 (provisions Pounds 747,000). Earnings worked through at 1.56p (losses 8.45p). Extraordinary charges of Pounds 1.85m (Pounds 286,000) related mainly to the disposal of Tri-ang Leisure.
