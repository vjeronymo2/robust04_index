940425 FT 25 APR 94 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System The German, Belgian, French, Spanish and Portuguese central banks trimmed interest rates last week, but the EMS grid remained almost unchanged. The Irish punt remains the strongest currency, with the two Iberian currencies at the bottom. Currencies, Page 33 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the system. Most of the currencies are permitted to fluctuate within 15 per cent of agreed central rates against the other members of the mechanism. The exceptions are the D-Mark and the guilder which move in a 2.25 per cent band.
