BFN [Text] Phnom penh, June 20 KYODO -- The Khmer Rouge rebel group formally closed its office in Phnom Penh on Monday [20 June] and its seven remaining officials in the capital flew to Thailand. The government notified the Khmer Rouge on Friday that it could not guarantee the safety of its representatives following the breakdown last week of roundtable peace talks between the coalition government and the guerrilla faction. Before their departure, the seven Khmer Rouge officials issued a statement saying that they have no intention of bidding farewell to the citizens of Phnom Penh and voicing hope to return again sometime in the future. Other senior Khmer Rouge officials in Phnom Penh had departed Saturday.
