Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Report by Kim Min-pae] [Text] Speaking on the cable television network system on 28 February, Information Minister O In-hwan said that the government plans to allow the Christian Broadcast Network to begin broadcasting beginning next year and will issue additional permission to air culture, art, cartoon, and home-shopping programming. Minister O also revealed that "since North Korean broadcasting programs consists of only half of the truth and half is intended for instigation, reception of North Korean broadcasts by the domestic press and radio is not desirable." Minister O said that the government plans to discontinue production of tht Taehan [Korea] news by the National Film Studio beginning next year, and will intensively review the plan for effective utilization of 102 staff workers now working in this studio.
