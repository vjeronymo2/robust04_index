941112 FT 12 NOV 94 / Weekend Money (Financial Planning): Pension for a student - Briefcase Is it possible to take out a personal pension scheme on the basis of unearned (interest) income, and/or temporary work, which exceeds the tax allowance? I am a full-time student, single, aged 29. This tax year, I will receive approximately Pounds 3,500 (gross) in interest, which will be taxed at source, and Pounds 2,400 (gross) through temporary employment over the summer, which also was taxed at source. I realise I can claim back tax on the first Pounds 3,445 (personal allowance), but this still means I am paying tax on about Pounds 2,400. Does this qualify as taxable income from which I can make tax-free contributions to a personal plan? Ask your tax office for the free pamphlet IR78 (Personal pensions: a guide for tax). Although you could make a small payment into a personal pension scheme, it does not look as though that would make economic sense.
