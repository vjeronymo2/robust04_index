BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Feoktistov] [Text] Ankara 24 June TASS -- Uzbek-Turkish official talks ended here on Thursday [23 June] by the signing of four bilateral documents. The delegations were headed by President Islam Karimov of Uzbekistan, who arrived in Turkey on Thursday, and president Suleyman Demirel of Turkey. The sides concluded agreements on mutual assistance in the science of law, development of contacts in civil aviation, a consular agreement and a protocol on cosultations and cooperation between the foreign ministries of the two countries. The signed documents, a statement of the Turkish Foreign Ministry notes, will contribute to the solution of issues in actively devoloping relations between Turkey and Uzbekistan. Bilateral contacts are quickly developing in the sphere of trade, economy and culture. During the talks the sides expressed a mutual desire to begin works as soon as possible for signing an agreement regarding the cancellation of vizas for citizens of the two republics. Both sides spoke out in favour of invigorating relations in the political field by means of mutual consultations and actions on the international arena.
