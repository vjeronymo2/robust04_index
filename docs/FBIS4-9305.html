BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Belgrade, April 25 (TANJUG) -- Another 247 members of the U.N. Protection Force (UNPROFOR) were early on Monday [25 April] deployed between the warring local Serb and Muslim troops in Gorazde, Muslim enclave in southeast Bosnia-Herzegovina, a U.N. spokesman said. According to REUTERS, the British, Russian and Egyptian soldiers joined the 200 U.N. troops which had arrived in Gorazde on Saturday. The aim of the U.N. peacekeepers in Gorazde is to form a buffer between the belligerent sides, to secure the safety of the humanitarian agencies and monitor the withdrawal of the Serb heavy artillery to 20 km away from the centre of the town, as called by a NATO ultimatum which expires at midnight Tuesday.
