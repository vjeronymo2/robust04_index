Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, December 23 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The papers relating to the bribery by some members of the parliament and the government will be destructed soon. It is necessary to take steps for protection [to protect] them against destruction. This was told to journalists by head of the department in the general board on ensuring state security L. Sanjaasuren [name as received]. He pleaded the reliable sources informing about the possible destruction of the important papers being made ready. The documents were preserved by the former head of the State security organs J. Baatar who was relieved of this office the other day. L. Sanjaasuren demanded to deliver them copies of the papers, preserve all the information kept in the memory of the computers in this respect and to cease temporarily any staff shifts in this office. L. Sanjaasuren stressed the significance of forming a service for struggle with corruption. Ensuring guarantees for not imposing punishment on those who was forced to give bribes. We are planning measures to be implemented lest this demand is accepted, L. Sanjaasuren said.
