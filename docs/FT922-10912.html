920429 FT 29 APR 92 / World Trade News: S Korean trade deficit shrinks 23% to Dollars 2.6bn SOUTH Korea's trade deficit shrank by 23 per cent to Dollars 2.6bn (Pounds 1.46bn) during the first quarter of 1992, contributing to a 14 per cent decline in its current account deficit of Dollars 3.2bn. Exports grew by 11.6 per cent to Dollars 16.8bn, outpacing a 6.6 per cent rise in imports to Dollars 19.5bn. South-east Asia was the only region with which South Korea enjoyed a trade surplus, amounting to Dollars 1.6bn during the first quarter against a surplus of Dollars 795m a year ago. South-east Asia overtook the US in March as the country's largest export market. South Korea's trade deficit with the US increased by 27 per cent to Dollars 965m, while its trade deficit with Japan remained unchanged at Dollars 2bn. The trade deficit narrowed in March to Dollars 550m from Dollars 897m a year ago, while the current account deficit shrank to Dollars 877m from Dollars 1.2bn. Exports in March increased by 12.4 per cent to Dollars 6.2bn, boosted by shipments of ships, cars and petrochemical products. Imports grew by 5.4 per cent to Dollars 7.2bn.
