930127 FT 27 JAN 93 / International Capital Markets: Doubts on future of equities study A SHADOW was cast yesterday over the future of Market 2000, the Securities and Exchange Commission's potentially ground-breaking study of US equities trading, when Mr William Heyman, the head of market regulation at the SEC and the man in charge of the study, announced his resignation. Mr Heyman, who joined the SEC in February 1991, said he would leave the agency on March 18, primarily because he felt that, with a new Democratic administration taking over the reins of government in Washington, it was time to move on. Mr Heyman was a prominent Republican and fund-raiser for President George Bush. The departure of Mr Heyman could cause problems for the Market 2000 study, which is scheduled for publication in May or June and will include important recommendations for updating the regulation of equities markets. Although the SEC says that the bulk of the work on the study has been finished, the absence of the man responsible for its completion could cause complications. Mr Heyman, however, believes that any delay in the release of the study will not be caused by his departure, but by changes within the SEC, in Congress and in the government. He said: 'I would like to have stayed to finish Market 2000, but I concluded that it would come behind everything else that is done here.'
