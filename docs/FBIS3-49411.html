Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Text] In Bratislava this afternoon, Slovak President Michal Kovac received for the first time personally representatives of the Hungarian Civic Party [HCP]. Anton Bodis, the president's spokesman, will now tell us about the preliminary results of that meeting. He is at this moment on the other end of the telephone line. Good evening. Can one already talk about some specific conclusions from that meeting? [Bodis] Good evening. HCP Chairman Laszlo Nagy briefed the president about the proposals, which his party sees as being possible ways of improving the position of national minorities in Slovakia. The final conclusions will be known as soon as the president has studied these proposals in greater detail. The HCP chairman also briefed the president about the party's attitude to tomorrow's meeting in Komarno. The president voiced the hope that the meeting will take place in a calm and nonconfrontational tone. Michal Kovac appreciated the realistic and tolerant attitude adopted by the HCP to relations between Slovaks and the Hungarian national minority, and toward ways of improving them. Both sides agreed on the need to outline the national policy of the Slovak Republic and ways of implementing it.
