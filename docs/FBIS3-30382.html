Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Geneva, Feb 2 (AFP) -- Former Polish Premier Tadeusz Mazowiecki could resign from his post of UN rapporteur on human rights in the former Yugoslavia, an official said here Wednesday [2 February]. "I've heard he is fed up," Pieter van Wullften Palthe, chairman of the UN Commission on Human Rights told a press conference. "We are all disillusioned but I hope it's not a reason for giving up," Wullften Palthe said, adding that the resignation could be announced during the commission's current annual session, which started Monday. Appointed in August 1992, Mazowiecki has prepared several reports on human rights abuses in the former Yugoslavia. [passage omitted]
