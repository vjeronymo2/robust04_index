Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, 30 Jan (INA)--Several companies have begun mediating to buy and sell foreign currencies in Baghdad in accordance with the instructions issued by the Central Bank of Iraq in this regard. A responsible source at the control directorate overseeing banks' foreign transfers told the Iraqi News Agency that the activities of these companies are confined to mediating to sell foreign currencies at a commission defined by the bank. He pointed out that these companies are not allowed to present direct or indirect credit offers and facilities, open current accounts for their currencies, accept deposits in any form, or present guarantees in local or foreign currencies, except sureties presented to the Central Bank of Iraq.
