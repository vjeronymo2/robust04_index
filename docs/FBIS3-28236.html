Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 4 KYODO -- The ruling coalition parties agreed Friday [4 February] to revoke Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa's new tax plan and to spend more time discussing tax reform, coalition officials said. Representatives of the ruling parties reached the accord as they resumed talks Friday to avert a major political crisis that emerged after Hosokawa brushed aside opposition from the largest governing party, the Social Democratic Party, and announced the imposition of a new 7 percent indirect tax on Thursday.
