American Support
The position of the United States is also something to watch. Washington was in favor of the use of force eight months ago. Afterwards it changed its mind. And now it is once again in favor: On Monday [7 February] President Bill Clinton supported the request that NATO authorize air strikes against Serb batteries around Sarajevo. U.S. Secretary of State Christopher Warren was very shocked by the carnage in Sarajevo. He seems to have won ground against some leading figures in the Pentagon who favor prudence. It seems that a turnaround in U.S. policies would rally the United Kingdom to the air strike option. But account must be taken of Russia, for whom this option risks causing "an escalation in violence leading to the immediate withdrawal of UN forces."
