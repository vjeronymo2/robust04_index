BFN [Text] Thailand has asked Myanmar [Burma] to regulate the prices of its natural gas export in line with fuel oil prices. During his meeting with Myanmar Minister of Energy U Khin Maung Thein, Minister to the Prime Minister's Office Sawit Phothiwihok told the Myanmar minister that Thailand plans to utilize natural gas supply from Myanmar at the power plant in Rat Buri Province in the central plain bordering Myanmar. He explained that the power plant can use either fuel oil or natural gas and that higher prices of natural gas would not be acceptable. The Thai minister also informed his Myanmar counterpart that Thailand has also contracted to buy energy from the Lao People's Democratic Republic and Malaysia. Moreover, the government has allowed the private sector to participate in power generation. He said Thailand could not afford to buy fuel at higher prices from Myanmar.
