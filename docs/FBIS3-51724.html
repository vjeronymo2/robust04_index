Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 20 Jan (PL) -- The Belizean Armed Forces this year allotted $10 million to strengthen its military so it will be prepared to defend national sovereignty, government sources disclosed today. This announcement is related to a statement made last November by Prime Minister Manuel Esquivel, who said that the imminent withdrawal of British troops from this state would probably encourage its Guatemalan neighbors to reclaim this territory, a former colony of the United Kingdom until 1983. According to reports received in Mexico, spokesmen for the Belizean Government assured that the government is planning to gradually increase its troops in response to the withdrawal of the British, who defended the country until last 31 December. The withdrawal of 1,200 men and combat equipment, including several squadrons of planes of various models, practically concluded on 1 January 1994. According to official data, Belize has 1,000 armed men, while Guatemala has an army of 30,000 soldiers. A representative of the Armed Forces said the objective was to add 1,500 men in 1994 and attract more recruits in the following years, depending on the state budget, while making structural changes in the corps. Diplomatic sources in Belmopan said the internal situation was calm, even though Guatemala has on previous occasions tried to annex the territory. Julio Roberto Herrera, Guatemalan vice consul in the neighboring Mexican city of Chetumal (southern state of Quintana Roo), said that from the times of the administration of Jorge Serrano Elias, his country has ruled out a military intervention in Belize. After pointing out that Guatemala has other priorities, the diplomat said: "We respect the sovereignty of Belize, and even though we claimed that territory at one time, it doesn't mean we are planning an invasion."
