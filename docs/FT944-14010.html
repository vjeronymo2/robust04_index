941025 FT 25 OCT 94 / UK Company News: Cullen's Cost controls and sales growth at its Neighbourhood Food Stores were behind a further recovery at Cullen's Holdings, the convenience retailer. On turnover of Pounds 3.74m (Pounds 2.37m), profits before tax for the six months to August 28 improved to Pounds 114,000 (Pounds 18,000). Mr Robert Rayne, chairman, said a changed product range and increased promotional activities helped sales in the Neighbourhood stores rise by some 5 per cent, more than offsetting a small reduction in gross margins. Future growth would be concentrated on opening new outlets, he added. Net cash inflow from operating activities amounted to Pounds 613,000; gearing had dropped to just under 16 per cent at the period-end. Earnings per share emerged at 0.53p (0.07p).
