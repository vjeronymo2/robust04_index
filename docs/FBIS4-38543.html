BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] Moscow May 27 TASS -- A representation to cancel the regulations on privatization in Moscow, which were prepared on the instruction of Moscow Mayor Yuriy Luzhkov and run counter to the current legislation, has been made by Chairman of the Russian State Property Committee Anatoliy Chubays to the Russian procurator general's office. "Moscow Mayor Yuriy Luzhkov has launched a campaign of large-scale violations of privatization legislation and even fully prohibited the transfer of state property into private in the city," Chubays told correspondents. In his words, the Moscow City Committee for Property gradually stopped corporatization and sales of Moscow enterprises at privatization voucher auctions. The share of joint stock enterprises in Moscow amounted to 15 per cent from the overall number of enterprises liable for corporatization in April 1994, as compared to 62 per cent throughout Russia. Moscow is lagging behind in privatization of large enterprises as well. The privatization situation in Moscow can be estimated as catastrophic, said Chubays.
