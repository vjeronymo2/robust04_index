BFN [Text] Cairo, 21 Apr (MENA)--Ten convicts of various nationalities--four Sudanese, two Bangladeshis, one Somali, one Indian, one Tanzanian, and one Kenyan--charged with drug trafficking and smuggling into Egypt were executed today. The Court of Cassation upheld the verdicts to execute these convicts in February and President Husni Mubarak endorsed them. Justice Minister Counselor Faruq Sayf-al-Nasr stated that this is the first time so many drug trafficking convicts have been executed at one time.
