Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Guatemala City, 8 Mar (NOTIMEX) -- Today, an official source reported that union leaders took hostage approximately 100 Quetzaltenango public office employees. The union leaders are demanding wage increases. The Quetzaltenango state employees warned they will only leave the offices when they are given the 40-percent wage increase they demanded from the government five weeks ago. The National Police reported that approximately 800 demonstrators held a protest and later seized the governor's headquarters and the Quetzaltenango municipality with more than 100 employees. Public security authorities said police surveillance will be increased in the coming hours, and they will find a legal way to evacuate the municipality and the state governor's office. Union leader Oswaldo Squich declared that public employees await the arrival of President Ramiro de Leon Carpio's representative to negotiate. The demonstrators coordinated their actions with the leaders of the National Federation of State Workers Unions, who are scheduled to meet with government representatives. The government employees union leaders temporarily seized the National Palace, headquarters of the Guatemalan Government, a few days ago, over the same conflict. Monday, more than 30,000 professors joined the protest movement.
