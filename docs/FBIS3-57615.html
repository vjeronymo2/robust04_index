Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Oreshin] [Text] Beijing January 28 TASS -- Russian-Chinese trade is constantly expanding and the turnover totalled 7.68 billion US dollars in 1993 which is a 30 per cent increase compared to the previous year, according to Shi Guangsheng, Chinese deputy foreign trade and foreign economic cooperation minister. He told a press conference on Friday [28 January] that Chinese exports to Russia grew by 15 per cent and amounted to 2.69 billion dollars while Russian imports increased by 41.4 per cent and totalled 4.99 billion dollars. Currently 27,000 Chinese are working in Russia where there are 2,000 joint ventures in which the share of the Chinese capital totals 82 million dollars, according to the deputy minister. Georgraphic advantages and similarity in economies will allow both countries to boost equal and profitable contacts, he added. Wu Yi, Chinese foreign trade and foreign economic cooperation minister, stressed that in general the Chinese foreign trade turnover grew 18.2 per cent in 1993 and amounted to 195.72 billion dollars. Chinese imports went up by 29 per cent (103.95 billion dollars) and exports by eight per cent (91.77 billion dollars).
