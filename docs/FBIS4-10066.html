BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 5 Apr (NA) -- The Argentine Navy reported today that it will participate in a joint exercise with the U.S. Navy off the Atlantic coast near the Norfolk Naval Base, Virginia, between 6 and 22 April. The Navy said the invitation to participate in the exercise was made within the framework of existing professional relations between the two countries. The Argentine task force will be commanded by Navy Captain Rafael Fuentes y Arballo and will include the destroyer ARA [Argentine Republic Navy] La Argentina. The United States task force will include the flagship USS Mount Wintney, the nuclear aircraft carrier George Washington, and nuclear cruisers and submarines. The operation will consist of isolating an aircraft carrier battle group and an amphibious group. Activities will be headed by the commander of the U.S. Second Fleet. The Argentine Navy units, some of the newest in the country, will have an opportunity to repeat the interaction experiences in face of foreign forces. At the end of the operation, in answer to Canadian Navy offer, the Argentine destroyer will make an official visit to Halifax port to participate in a brief training exercise with Canadian Navy units.
