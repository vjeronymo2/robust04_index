Language: English Article Type:BFN [Editorial: "No Charge"] [Excerpts] The Royal Government's decision to remove the security charge levied on local and foreign companies for the establishment of trade companies and foreign representative offices here, is a very welcome move. This will give Cambodia an edge in boosting foreign investments, especially in view of the fact that the United States embargo on Vietnam has finally been lifted. No doubt there will be an expected clamour to invest in the Indochina region, but the country with the most pull will be the one which provides the best economic returns for the amount invested. And Cambodia cannot afford to be seen as lackadaisical about foreign investment, as the country needs foreign capital to rebuild the country. It would be a pity to give up on this opportunity to attract investment, merely because the government did not act fast enough. Prior to this move, trading companies and foreign representative offices had to deposit U.S. $3,000 with the National Bank as caution money. [passage omitted] Cambodia has a lot to offer investors but more steps need to be taken to make life easier for them. If this means cutting down on a lot of red tape, then so be it. [passage omitted]
