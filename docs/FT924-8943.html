921109 FT 09 NOV 92 / Waigel sees limit for borrowing MR Theo Waigel, German finance minister, said yesterday that government borrowing must not exceed DM45bn (Dollars 28.37bn) in 1993, Reuter reports from Bonn. Until recently, the government had been forecasting a 1993 budget deficit of DM38bn compared with a projected DM40bn this year. 'There must be no increase in net borrowing above DM45bn,' Mr Waigel said in Berlin's Sonntagspost newspaper. 'In just the same way as tax increases, this would have a negative effect on the economy. 'In the first round of saving efforts we have managed to save DM7.5bn. That is enough to pay for the German Unity fund, inherited debt from former East Germany and to cover eastern European trade,' he added. But Mr Waigel said: 'Without further drastic saving - including in subsidies and in welfare policies - it won't be possible. Everyone has to carry their part of the burden.'
