930619 FT 19 JUN 93 / Emigration 'a factor in jobless fall' THE Department of Employment yesterday said emigration could be a 'small' factor in the fall in unemployment, David Goodhart writes. Officials were responding to a GMB general union study which shows a 30 per cent fall in the number of Irish nationals in Britain. The study, based on the department's Labour Force Survey, shows the number of Irish nationals fell from 640,613 in April 1990 to 460,721 in November last year. Those unemployed fell from 35,511 to 31,984. Mr Paul Kenny, GMB London secretary, said: 'These figures show the devastating impact of recession on the Irish population over here. Families have either moved to Ireland or to third countries.'
