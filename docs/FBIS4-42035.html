BFN [Text] Belarussian [Belarusian] First Deputy Prosecutor General Vladimir Kondratyev said that a criminal case would be open on the incident involving Aleksandr Lukashenko, MP and presidential candidate. Police guarding the government building injured Lukashenko as he was going to his office. An ambulance team certified this. Kondratyev told INTERFAX that, following additional preliminary inquiry a criminal case would be open within three days, as required by Belarussian legislation. His office had received an explanation from the police involved in the incident. They said that Lukashenko had torn off buttons on their uniforms. Lukashenko is now able to go to his office which he had as chairman of the parliamentary anti-corruption commission. As a presidential candidate Lukashenko is guarded by officers of the Interior Ministry's State Guard Department as well as his bodyguards.
