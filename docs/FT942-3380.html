940615 FT 15 JUN 94 / Fewer directors may face charges The number of prosecutions and disqualification petitions against company directors this year may fail to meet official objectives because of new guidelines intended to clear a backlog of outstanding cases, the Department of Trade and Industry said yesterday, Andrew Jack writes. The department, commenting on an internal memo sent by the senior official receiver to Insolvency Service offices, said it was possible that prosecutions would fall in line with a fall in the number of investigations of liquidations and bankruptcies. The senior official receiver has written asking staff in offices around the UK to identify in the next four weeks an additional 11,000 cases to be closed - more than half the total closed last year. The guidance said an average of an hour should be enough to examine cases and decide whether they could be closed. It said staff need not look for possible offences or areas for investigation. However, the principal union in the agency has sent guidance to its members suggesting that this time is inadequate and advising them to take adequate care in their scrutiny of documents. The bulletin, being circulated to members of the insolvency service branch of the Institute of Professional and Managerial Staffs, the civil service union, says: 'Examiners must not be used to sausage-machine cases for closing by turning a blind eye to possible assets or other matters.'
