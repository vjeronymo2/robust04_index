Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] During the fifth Common Market of the South [Mercosur] summit in Colonia del Sacramento, Uruguay, President Itamar Franco stressed that this year Mercosur will have to define a foreign tariff and the common trade policy that will be pursued. Sitting next to the presidents of Argentina, Uruguay, and Paraguay, the Brazilian president emphasized that Mercosur's success will be closely linked to the customs unification on 1 January 1995. President Itamar Franco stressed that the Brazilian Government is determined to comply with all the commitments made in order to implement the economic and social goals defined in the Asuncion Treaty. In his speech, President Itamar Franco emphasized that Brazil does not assign an exclusive priority to Mercosur and confirmed his favorable stance for enhancement and joint efforts toward integration with the Andean Group and Amazon Initiative countries. In his speech, the Brazilian president said the Mercosur countries are paying attention to today's reality involving the establishment of NAFTA, an organization economically uniting the United States, Mexico, and Canada. President Itamar Franco emphasized the democratic maturity of Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay, and Paraguay as a factor required for the success of South America's integration.
