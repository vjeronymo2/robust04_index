930518 FT 18 MAY 93 / UK Company News: Pilkington wins Chrysler contract PILKINGTON, the UK glassmaker, has won a five-year contract to supply automotive privacy glass to Chrysler, the US vehicle maker. The deal is understood to be worth around Dollars 100m and covers the supply of a new product, Galaxsee, developed jointly by Pilkington in the UK and by Libbey-Owens-Ford, its 80 per cent owned US subsidiary. Nippon Sheet Glass of Japan owns a 20 per cent stake in the US operation. The privacy glass will be fitted in some Chrysler fourwheel drive leisure/utility vehicles such as the Grand Cherokee and in some of its minivan/people carrier vehicles such as the Chrysler Voyager/Dodge Caravan. The glass will be manufactured at the Libbey-Owens-Ford plant near Toledo, Ohio. Pilkington expects to market the product in Europe in particular in southern European countries.
