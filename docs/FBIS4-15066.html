BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Lyudmila Yermakova] [Text] Moscow April 5 TASS -- The Federation Council voted down today the federal law "on procedure to publish and introduce federal constitutional laws, federal laws and bills of the Russian Federal Assembly's chambers" adopted by the State Duma due to the "inadmissibility of its certain articles and items". This is the second law submitted by the parliament's lower chamber to the upper one in accordance with the Constitution. The first law, on budget financing of spending in the second quarter of 1994, was approved today in repeated voting. "There is nothing dramatic about this," believes Federation Council Chairman Vladimir Shumeyko. In his opinion, "this proves that the Federation Council takes its work seriously". In accordance with the Constitution, a conciliatory commission to include members of both parliament chambers will be set up to settle the differences.
