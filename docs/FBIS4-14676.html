BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow April 2 TASS -- "The visit to Moscow by U.S. Secretary of Commerce Ronald Brown has shown that there is no chill in Russian-U.S. relations," Dmitriy Ryurikov, assistant to the Russian president, told ITAR-TASS in an exclusive interview. "The two sides adhere to the policy of partnership, value their relations and think how to make them still more productive and versatile in the future," Ryurikov said. The U.S. commerce secretary led a delegation of prominent representatives of the U.S. business community whose principal aim is to "stimulate American private business' interest in Russia". "To all appearances, the delegation members saw for themselves Russia's interest in cooperation, and the Russian leadership's support for big projects and closer contacts", Ryurikov pointed out.
