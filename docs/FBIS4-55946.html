BFN [Text] Bratislava, May 23 (CTK) -- The recently emerged bloc of the Democratic Party (DS) and the Party of Slovak Entrepreneurs and Tradespeople (SPZ) has asked the Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) to form an "equal" coalition so that not a single right-wing vote is lost in the autumn elections, the two parties' chairmen announced today. This call for coalition talks, which will be held on Saturday [28 May], actually means a refusal of the KDH offer which opened its list of candidates to politicians from small right-wing parties. Electoral polls have shown that only the KDH of the fragmented Slovak right could cross the five-per-cent barrier in the elections scheduled for September 30-October 1. The KDH has been refusing the right-wing coalition. "We shall try to form a united coalition of the right-of-the- centre parties," DS chairman Pavol Hagyari stated. He said he could not rule out that the DS-SPZ bloc would enter the elections independently, probably as one legal entity for which 5 per cent are sufficient, not seven as in case of a coalition. However, SPZ chairman Vladimir Randa called the cooperation a "pre- and post-electoral bloc of two independent parties." The Slovak right must make up its mind by the end of July when the list of candidates must be submitted.
