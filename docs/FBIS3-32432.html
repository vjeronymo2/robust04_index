Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Facing the Moment" newscast] [Text] A high-level Guatemalan guerrilla commission met with Augusto Ramirez Ocampo, the UN Observers for El Salvador [Onusal] mission chief this morning. The Guatemalan National Revolutionary Unity [URNG] delegation was headed by Commanders Gaspar Ilom and Pablo Monsanto and members of the rebel diplomatic commission. Augusto Ramirez Ocampo reported that the URNG delegation had come to learn of the role played by the United Nations in the verification of the Salvadoran peace process. On his part, Commander Rodrigo Asturas, alias Gaspar Ilom, said the agreement signed by the URNG and the Guatemalan Government on 10 January, allowed them to anticipate a positive future for the negotiations. [Begin Asturias recording] [Words indistinct] specially significant to us, especially based on our evaluation of the Salvadoran negotiations and peace process. At the same time, we wanted to verify, in situ, the United Nations' weight in this peace process. In our case, we asked for UN participation as soon as we initiated talks in Guatemala. We ratified our request in the agreement signed on 10 January. We have called on the United Nations to act as a mediator in the process and this, of course, includes verification of all the agreements. [end recording] URNG Commander Gaspar Ilom, who is the son of Miguel Angel Asturias, a nobel laureate for literature, considered that the UN experience in verifying the implementation of the agreements in El Salvador is promising. Ilom said the Guatemalan peace process will be very complicated but not, as a result of this, irresolvable. He added that they hope to reach agreements leading to peace very soon. Ilom insisted that the main points of the negotiation are to establish the role of the Army in a democratic society, the country's economic problems, and issues related to the Indian problem.
