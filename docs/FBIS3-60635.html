Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] The Advisory Council on Democratic and Peaceful Unification Policy held a joint meeting of its Management and Standing Committees on 8 December. At the meeting, government offices concerned delivered reports on the current status regarding the North Korean nuclear issue and South-North relations. The Advisory Council adopted a four-point resolution including a point urging an early solution of the North Korean nuclear problem. In the resolution, the Advisory Council on Democratic and Peaceful Unification Policy urged North Korea to accept transparent nuclear inspections and to resume the South-North dialogue. It also urged the Governments of South and North Korea to take active and immediate measures for meetings and reunion of separated family members, which all the countrymen desire. In his report, Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu said: The ROK and the United States made it clear that it would be difficult to continue U.S.-North Korean contacts and international measures [kukchejok chawonesoui taecho] will be inevitable unless North Korea accepts inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency and accedes to the South-North envoy exchange for discussions on the nuclear issue.
