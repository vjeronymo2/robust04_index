940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / International Company News: Singapore banks lifted by island's buoyant economy Two of Singapore's biggest banks have reported profits reflecting the island republic's buoyant economy and the territory's growth as a financial services centre. Pre-tax profits at United Overseas Bank (UOB) for the six months ended June 30 rose 34 per to SDollars 411m (USDollars 273m). Net profit jumped 32 per cent to SDollars 292m while per-share earnings rose 31 per cent to 40.2 Singapore cents from 30.7 cents in the equivalent previous period. UOB said net interest income increased by 26 per cent to SDollars 430m. Income on fees and commissions jumped 36 per cent to SDollars 136m. In the six-month period, pre-tax profits at Overseas-Chinese Banking Corp (OCBC) rose 26 per cent to SDollars 330m while net profit increased by 28 per cent to SDollars 231m. Net interest income grew 24 per cent to SDollars 528m over the six month period while fees and commissions income rose 42 per cent to SDollars 109m. Earnings per share increased by 43 per cent to 30 Singapore cents from 21 cents in the previous equivalent period. OCBC said it had made an extraordinary profit from the sale of a portion of its shares in Singapore Press Holdings, the island republic's biggest publisher.
