March 13, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

NATION IN BRIEF;

NEW JERSEY;

FLORIO SIGNS AUTO INSURANCE RATE BILL

Gov. James J. Florio signed into law a bill to lower New Jersey's car insurance rates by 20%.

Allstate Insurance Co. Vice President Edward Young said the firm would file suit within 30 days on the grounds that the law violates constitutional protections of due process. New Jersey's insurance rates average $1,000 a year. The bill eliminates the Joint Underwriting Assn., a state-operated pool for high-risk drivers that has a $3.1-billion debt. A $222 per car annual tab to keep the pool from bankruptcy will be dropped. The law requires the insurance industry to pay $1.4 billion of the pool's debt.
