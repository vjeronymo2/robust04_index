Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Jan 10 (CTK) -- The Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) does not consider the ethnic principle to be a chief criterion for establishing a new territorial-administrative set-up of Slovakia, as suggested by the meeting of ethnic Hungarian representatives, KDH Deputy Chairman Ivan Simko told CTK today. The KDH does not deem it suitable to consider the use of Hungarian as official language in Slovakia, Simko said. It is necessary, however, to look for a feasible solution which would make it possible to use the mother tongue in administrative contact, he added. Simko said that the Komarno, south Slovakia, meeting of January 8 had not contributed to straightening up of the relations between Slovaks and Hungarians.
