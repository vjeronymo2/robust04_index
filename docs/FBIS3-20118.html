Language: Finnish Article Type:BFN [Text] Czech Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus hopes that his country's membership of the European Union [EU] will become a reality after Finland's membership. Klaus, who is visiting Finland, has held talks with political leaders about Finland's attitude toward the EU, among other things. On the basis of these talks, Prime Minister Klaus described Finland's attitude toward EU membership as an investment. [Begin Klaus recording, in English] Finland definitely discovered that EC membership, this is now my terminology and not the terminology of the Finnish prime minister or anybody else, that EC membership is an investment. [end recording] Klaus said that EU membership is not a miraculous cure but an investment that has its costs and its benefits. Positive and negative sides might appear at different stages and influence different parts of society in different ways, the Czech prime minister said.
