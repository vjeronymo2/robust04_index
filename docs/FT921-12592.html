920125 FT 25 JAN 92 / Finance & The Family: Directors' Transactions LAST WEEK, we commented on the volume of selling by directors, and the trend has continued. Much of this activity has been directors exercising and selling options. At Farnell Electronics, which makes and distributes electronics and electrical equipment and accessories, E. Hall is the second director to sell stock in the past month. His disposal of 150,000 shares at 240.5p reduces his total holding by more than two-thirds. AJ Thomas, the chairman of another electronics company, Diploma, sold 20,000 shares at 263.5p. This follows an announcement accompanying the final results that trading conditions were the worst for more than 20 years. Despite the sale, Thomas retains in excess of 1m shares in the company. Not every electronics company is having similar problems. Director confidence has been steady and constant at Astec (BSR) over the past three months. BH Christopher, the chairman and chief executive, bought 100,000 shares at 12.5p, which brings to 750,000 the number acquired by him and two other directors since last October. Buying has also been steady at Smith New Court. Two directors, M.
