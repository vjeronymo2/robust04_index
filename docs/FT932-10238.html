930510 FT 10 MAY 93 / Observer: On the wing For the benefit of the numerous readers evidently addicted to collecting stray facts just in case they should ever come in handy, Blackwell's new Dictionary of 20th Century Social Thought has just come up with the following. The language of the Karam people of east Africa contains a collective noun, yakt, which 'groups bats with most birds but excludes cassowaries'.
