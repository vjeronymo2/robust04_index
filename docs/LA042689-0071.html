April 26, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: TV & VIDEO

There will be no more aerobics classes and scanty lingerie on NBC's "Nightingales" if the series is renewed for another season, producer Aaron Spelling vows. Spelling called a Tuesday morning news conference at the Century Plaza to announced that the American Nursing Assn., which has loudly protested the show's racy depiction of the lives of student nurses, has now pledged its support -- providing producers strive to create a more realistic portrait. Spelling said rushed production and last year's writers strike forced the show's producers to include scenes "we are not proud of." He added: "If a show depends on young women changing their clothes every two seconds . . . we don't want to do that show." ("Nightingales" airs tonight at 10.) ALEENE MacMINN
