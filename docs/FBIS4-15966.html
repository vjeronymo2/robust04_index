BFN [Text] Riga, Apr 12, BNS -- The mixed committee of the European Free Trade Association [EFTA] and Latvian Government meets Tuesday [12 April] in Riga to discuss economic situation in EFTA member states and Latvia in view of the process of European integration. The exchange of technical assistance programs and joint projects will be another issue on the meeting's agenda. The basic cooperation agreement between EFTA and Latvia provides for assistance in statistics, customs work and state order policies. The declaration on cooperation between EFTA states and Latvia was signed in December, 1992, in Geneva. The parties to the declaration made a committment to investigate cooperation opportunities with a view of creating the conditions for free trade relations. The first meeting of the EFTA-Latvian mixed committee took place in April, 1993, in Stockholm. The second meeting will be conducted by Latvian Economics' Ministry's Juris Cebulis and EFTA's Veijo Sampovaara. The EFTA delegation also includes its Secretary General Georg Reisch. EFTA member states are Austria, Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Finland, Switzerland and Sweden. Four of them, Finland, Norway, Sweden and Switzerland, have free trade agreements with Latvia. A similar agreement could be signed between Latvia and Iceland soon.
