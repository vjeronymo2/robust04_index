930710 FT 10 JUL 93 / Move to allow adoption fees THE GOVERNMENT plans to give local authorities powers to impose fees on couples who want to adopt a child. The fees would be for 'home reports' drawn up by social workers to assess the suitability of prospective parents. At present authorities can charge only for reports on people who want to adopt youngsters from abroad. These cost up to Pounds 1,500. Mr John Bowis, health minister, said on BBC radio yesterday he expected local authorities would charge according to what people could afford. Mr David Blunkett, shadow health secretary, attacked the proposal. He said: 'We're in real danger of creating a check-out counter mentality rather than a caring service.'
