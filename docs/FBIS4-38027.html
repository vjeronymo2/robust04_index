BFN [Telephone interview with Salim Salih Muhammad, Yemeni Presidential Council member and YSP assistant secretary general, in Rabat, by 'Ali al-'Amudi; date not given] [Excerpts] [passage omitted] [Al-'Amudi] Your stay in London for medical treatment lasted more than one month. Later, it became evident that you were on a mission for the political leadership in Aden. Can you tell us about the nature of this mission, and did you contact the British Government during that period? [Muhammad] We carried out our duty toward our people, who are being torn apart by this dirty war. The results of our efforts, whether with the British, the brothers, or the friends, were compatible with what we believe to be our duty. We held many meetings at the British Foreign Office, with the parliamentary blocs, and with influential political, cultural, and trade quarters. The world at large has come to realize the danger of what is taking place in Yemen. I believe that the trend is to tackle these dangers. We carried out this mission in coordination with the leadership, and I would like to reassure you that things are proceeding in a coordinated and well-planned manner. [Al-'Amudi] Sukhoi-29 aircraft appeared for the first time in the skies of the northern side. What does this mean? Are we to witness a new escalation where the Democratic Republic of Yemen will bring into the battle its strategic bombers which, as yet, have not entered the battle? [Muhammad] We have information about military aircraft Sanaa received through Iran. Some of these aircraft were destroyed in Ta'izz airport in an air strike by the aircraft of steadfast Aden. With respect to what the southern side has, weapons alone will not determine the course and outcome of the battle. What will decide the battle will be the position that defends honor, dignity, and property against those who decided that the south with its inhabitants, villages, and cities is up for grabs for the army of Sanaa and its most important tribes. The war will be a protracted one. People will resist the invasion, using all means, including new weapons. In this case, these weapons will be authorized because they will be used in self-defense. [Al-'Amudi] Yahya Mansur Abu-Usba' has said in Cairo that steps are being taken to form a provisional YSP command under Salih 'Abbad in Sanaa. What does the YSP assistant secretary general have to say to this? [Muhammad] Only the YSP central bodies have such a right. The war will produce unexpected results on all levels. Therefore, it is premature to say anything now. Otherwise, the issue will be similar to the many appointments that the Sanaa regime announces. I met brother Yahya Abu-Usba' in Cairo. He told me things that are very different from what the press reports. [passage omitted]
