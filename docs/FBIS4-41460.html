BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Aleksey Berezhkov] [Text] Moscow June 24 TASS -- "We are against entering into polemic in public about vaguenesses which can naturally arise during the exchange of chemical arms data between Russia and the United States," a high-ranking spokesman for the Russian Foreign Ministry told ITAR-TASS on Friday in response to a request to comment on remarks by CIA Director James Woolsey, who told U.S. congressional hearings about the U.S. Administration's "serious concern" at Russia-provided data on her chemical weapons. "We also have serious questions as regards data provided by the Americans. We hope to clear the matters up with the U.S. side during subsequent contacts," emphasised the spokesman who requested anonymity. Russia and the United States have created a confidence-building mechanism as regards chemical arms on a bilateral basis. The mechanism is in operation and provides for exchanges of data on the military-chemical capabilities of the sides and for consultations should questions arise, the Russian Foreign Ministry spokesman said.
