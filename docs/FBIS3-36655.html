Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow, 7 Feb -- Yuriy Meshkov, president of the Crimean republic, and Eduard Baltin, commander of the Black Sea Fleet, discussed the fleet's problems at a meeting in Sevastopol today. In the words of Yuriy Meshkov, the Black Sea Fleet was and remains the guarantor of stability on the Crimean peninsula. He also believes that while resolving the problem of the Black Sea Fleet, one should take into account the opinion of the population and of the highest power bodies of the republic in the interests of the peoples of Crimea, Ukraine, and Russia. ITAR-TASS has learned at the press center of the Black Sea Fleet that Eduard Baltin expressed confidence that Yuriy Meshkov "as the president of all Crimean residents, will defend their rights irrespective of their nationality and political views, and will seek to strenghen peace and interethnic accord in Crimea." Yuriy Meshkov and Eduard Baltin agreed to exchange information on the most important aspects of Crimea's political life and to hold regular business meetings.
