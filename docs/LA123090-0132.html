PEOPLE: VENICE ART TEACHER WRITES BOOK ABOUT MURALS -- IN FRENCH

Marco Elliot, a commercial arts teacher at Venice High School, is the author of a how-to guide on street murals, written in French.

Elliot, a native of Cannes, France, used his experiences as an artist and teacher to write "Le Pied du Mur." The book describes the mural-making process for everyone from kindergarten children to young adults. It also explains how damaged murals are repaired and details the history of murals.

Several photographs of Elliot's own works are featured in the book, along with works of other artists from the Los Angeles area and abroad.

"In the early 1980s, I devoted several years to doing street murals in the south of France," Elliot said. "I was commissioned by the city of Cannes to do something with an environmental theme, and I did a mural on air pollution.

"An artist's mind is always open to people and problems," he said, "but it's sometimes difficult to work in urban settings because of noise, traffic and pollution."

University High School student Carlo Mattoni attained the highest overall individual score in this year's Los Angeles Unified School District's Academic Decathalon competition.

He scored 8,233 points in the 10-category competition. He also won the Fine Arts category and received a $1,000 scholarship.

Virginia Randolph, head librarian for public services at Pepperdine University's Payson Library, has been appointed to the the Extended Campus Library Service Committee of the American Library Assn.

Randolph, who was selected for inclusion in "Who's Who in Library and Information Science," will work with the national committee to evaluate information-delivery systems for off-campus programs.

She is also involved with the bibliographic instruction section of the Assn. of College and Research Libraries.

City Councilman Zev Yaroslavsky has presented a certificate of appreciation to Alfreda Iglehart, a Los Angeles Marathon volunteer.

Iglehart, an associate professor of social welfare at UCLA, is the marathon's liaison with national track clubs.

She is also a marathoner and won the 1990 Santa Monica Marathon.

The UCLA Alumni Assn.'s Advisory and Scholarship Program has awarded scholarships to Joseph Doherty of Los Angeles and Lise Hilboldt of Santa Monica.

Doherty and Hilboldt both attended Santa Monica College before transferring to UCLA. Each will receive a $2,500 community college scholarship.

Scholarships are given to students who have a minimum 3.5 grade-point average in community college course work and junior standing upon entering UCLA.

Santa Monica High School senior Alicia Roberts has been recognized for outstanding performance in writing by the National Council of Teachers of English.

She is one of more than 800 winners of a 1990 Achievement Award.

Sinai Akiba Academy will honor Tooran Mahboubi at the school's 1991 Benefit Dinner Dance on Jan. 19. Mahboubi will be recognized for her leadership in the Jewish and Persian communities. She founded the Bel-Air Chapter of Na'amat U.S.A. and has been president the past 10 years. CHRISTINA V.
