BFN [Text] Beijing, April 28 (CNS) -- Spokesman of China's Ministry of Foreign affairs, Mr. Wu Jianmin, replying to a question raised by a reporter at a news briefing held here today asking for his comments on and China's hopes regarding the forming of the Japanese cabinet by the new Prime Minister, Mr. Tsutomu Hata, said that the Chinese Premier Mr. Li Peng had sent a congratulatory message to Mr. Hata on his election as prime minister of Japan and he added that Mr. Hata was a well-known statesman to the Chinese people. China appreciated his zeal and farsightedness as well as his contribution to the strengthening of Sino-Japanese friendly cooperation. All Japanese political parties both in power or not had the same stance on the development of mutual cooperation in various fields between the two countries. China believed that after the establishment of the new Japanese government, the two countries would continue their close coordination and cooperation and make joint efforts to promote the development of Sino-Japanese ties in a long-term and steady way. In answering a question on who China will send to take part in the inauguration ceremony of the new president of South Africa, Mr. Wu said that the inauguration of the new presient of South Africa signaled the end of racist rule and the start of national equality in the country. China had all along firmly supported the just struggle of the people of South Africa. China was glad to see the historical victory won by them and would like to express its congratulations to the inauguration ceremony of the new president of South Africa. In a separate development, the spokesman announced that at the invitation of Chinese President Jiang Zemin and Premier Li Peng, the President of the Republic of Kenya, Mr. Daniel Arap Moi, would pay a state visit to China from May 3 to 7 and the Malaysian Prime Minister, Dr. Mahathir Mohammed would pay a working visit to China from May 11 to 13.
