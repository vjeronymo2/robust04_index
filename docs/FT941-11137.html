940205 FT 05 FEB 94 / Finance and the Family: Directors' transactions The largest single transaction of the week was recorded in Perpetual, the unit trust and discretionary portfolio management company. Martyn Arbib, the chairman, sold 300,000 shares at Pounds 10.80. However, the stocks have been performing extremely strongly recently and he still retains the lion's share of the company. Kenwood Appliances, famous for their mixers, came to the market in June 1992 and first featured on this list in November last year. Since directors started selling then at 316p, the shares have actually continued to rise and are currently trading around 389p. This week's sales were slightly higher at 393p. Filofax keeps cropping up as a company in which directors are still prepared to invest, despite the share price racing ahead. Whether the company merits such a high rating, even given its product's global development prospects is hard to judge.
