930113 FT 13 JAN 93 / Hatton denies charges of fraud MR DEREK HATTON, the former Labour deputy leader of Liverpool City Council, yesterday pleaded not guilty to three charges of conspiracy to defraud the local authority. Appearing at the preliminary hearing of a trial that might last six months, Mr Hatton denied the charges before Mr Justice Waterhouse began the process of selecting a jury at Mold Crown Court, north Wales. Mr Hatton and his four co-defendants were asked to plead to amended charges of conspiracy to defraud the local authority over two Liverpool carpark sites and the site of a cinema complex in the city. Mr Hatton and the four other defendants - former councillors Ms Hannah Folan and Mr John Nelson; Mr John Monk, Mr Hatton's personal tailor; and Mr Roy Stewart, managing director of Rogersons Developments - all denied the charges against them. The court was told that legal argument might last for the next four days. The trial is expected to start on Monday. Mr Justice Waterhouse warned the jury: 'If any of you have been involved in dealings with Liverpool City Council or concerned in the business of Liverpool City Council it is obviously undesirable that you should serve on the jury.'
