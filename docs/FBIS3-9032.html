Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, March 17 -- In January Poland recorded a deficit in her foreign trade of 200 m dollars, and a slightly smaller deficit on her current account in the balance of payments (191 m dollars), according to data released Thursday by a Central Bank official. Wlodzimierz Kicinski, head of the NBP's foreign department, told reporters that export earnings in January reached 1,054 bn dollars, up 21.6 percent on January, 1993, while payments for imports reached 1.25 bn dollars, up 10 percent on January last year. Poland's leading trade partners both in exports and imports were Germany, the United States, Britain, France and Austria. Gross official reserves of foreign exchange reached 4.3 bn dollars in January, recording a slight increase on December, 1993. Yesterday the president of the State Foreign Investment Agency said he expected an inflow of foreign investment and loan capital to Poland as a result of the agreement signed by Poland with the creditor banks associated in the London Club. He put the expected inflow at 1 bn dollars annually. In the last five years foreign investments in Poland totalled 3.5 bn dollars.
