`Strengthened' TA1406060394 Ankara TRT Television Network in Turkish 2100 GMT 13 Jun 94 TA1406060394 Ankara TRT Television Network Turkish BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Prime Minister Tansu Ciller has received Iranian Interior Minister 'Ali Mohammad Besharati-Jahromi, who is in Turkey on an official visit. Murat Karayalcin, state minister and deputy prime minister, and Interior Minister Nahit Mentese were also present at the meeting. Ambassador Volkan Vural, the prime minister's adviser, said in a statement to reporters that during the meeting Ciller stressed the importance Turkey attaches to its friendship and cooperation with Iran. Vural said: At the meeting, Besharati reiterated that Iran will never allow activities against Turkey to take place on its territory. He added that the issues of joint operations and an exchange of terrorists were not discussed at the Ciller-Besharati meeting. Besharati later called on Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin. At the meeting, Cetin said that the good relations between the two friendly and neighborly countries greatly contribute to regional stability. Cetin added that the good bilateral relations will be further strengthened through high-level dialogues. Besharati said that no group will be allowed to engage in activities against Turkey on Iranian territory. He added that President Suleyman Demirel's visit to Tehran next month will be an important step in bilateral relations. Mentese was also present at the Cetin-Besharati meeting. Speaking to reporters after his meeting with Cetin, Besharati said that Turkey and Iran share the view that the disruption of Iraq's territorial integrity will endanger regional stability. Replying to correspondents' questions, Mentese said that there are no border disputes between Iran and Turkey and that the issue of border security was discussed. In reply to a question on whether Besharati was informed of Turkey's expectations regarding border security, Mentese said: We have jointly included the issue of PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] terror on the agenda. We are discussing the issue. Iran has also accepted that the PKK is a terror organization. In any event, Iran agrees that there is no Kurdish problem in Turkey and that the PKK is a terror organization.
