November 11, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

JUVENILES TO MEET IN LOS ALAMITOS RACE

Flomont, the Ed Burke Memorial Futurity winner, and Way Maker, who won the Dash For Cash Futurity, face off Saturday night in the $448,500 Golden State Futurity at Los Alamitos.

The race is the third meeting of the 2-year-olds, and should decide the juvenile championship of the Horsemen's Quarter Horse Racing Assn. meeting.

The race has attracted a field of 10. In post position order, they are: Tic Tac Oh with jockey Eddie Garcia; Flomont (James Lackey); Shake Six (Kip Didericksen); The Mogul (James Brooks); On The Hustle (Henry Garcia); Pie Glass (Kenneth Hart); Debonaire Kid (Joe Meier); Way Maker (Danny Cardoza); Sheisabedouin (Ralph Pauline); and American Farmer (Roman Figueroa).

Way Maker is the leading money earner in this field with $348,311. In his 12-race career, he has won four races including the Governor's Cup Futurity. Flomont has won $220,090 in her 11-race career.
