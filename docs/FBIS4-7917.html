BFN [Text] Sofia, April 7 (BTA) -- Following consultations with the parliamentary groups, Prime Minister Lyuben Berov next week will announce his plans about cabinet changes, reads a government press release this afternoon. Prof. Berov this morning chaired a regular government sitting resuming his duties after a 26-day sick leave and a major heart operation. While he declined to make any comments before journalists, photographers were allowed to take pictures. The prospective cabinet changes will be aimed at fulfilling priority cabinet tasks to carry out privatization, combat corruption, conclude agreements with the international financial institutions, expand this country's international ties, protect Bulgarian business and revise its social policy in favour of a wide strata of the population. The ministries, too, are expected to specify and publicize the steps they will take by year-end to carry out these tasks, the release also said. The better part of the release responds to criticism against the Cabinet by President Zhelyu Zhelev. On Saturday, April 2, Dr. Zhelev sharply criticized the Cabinet for bringing reforms to a standstill, declaring the withdrawal of his support for them. "The incumbent cabinet suits the Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP, the former Communists), as it gives them a chance to do what they've always wanted to do, govern without shouldering the political responsibility for it," the president then said. Meeting with the leader of one of Bulgaria's major labour organizations, the Confederation of Independent Trade Unions in Bulgaria, President Zhelev reiterated he no longer supported the cabinet and predicted early elections would be held this autumn. Though asked by the press to respond to last Saturday's (92 April] criticism, Berov would not do it, "to avert a further straining of relations between the cabinet and president, which some would see as a war between the two institutions". The government Press Office release describes the arguments in the presidential attack as emotional, farfetched and one-sided. The release describes as baseless the presidential charge that being responsible for the dollar and price rise in the last month, the government has endangered civil peace and social stability. "This was an insufficiently considered move", says the release. It accuses President Zhelev of sometimes trying to turn into a supporter of some political force or other. "It is inadmissible for the president to declare confidence or no confidence in any government. This is a function of the parliament. Presidential attempts to change the parliamentary lineup are unconstitutional and recall some old-fashioned non-republican ways in Bulgarian history", says the release. The prime minister is far from thinking that his government is ideal and has fulfilled all its promises, the release also says. Yet only a nonpartisan government of national responsibility can rule this country in the present complicated and extremely controversial lineup, with no parliamentary force commanding a clear majority. The release refutes the president's criticism for the forex [foreign exchange] crisis, arguing that the cabinet cannot be held responsible for forex policy, as this is a responsibility of the central Bulgarian National Bank subordinate to parliament. It says the president gave twice smaller figures on the land reform. "It should be clear that legislature also has a commitment to the land reform to fulfill, and that legislation assigns a significant role to judiciary at this stage", says the release. In connection with criticism of the low progress of privatization, the release recalls that the government-sponsored amendments to the Privatization Act have been stalled in parliament for over seven months. According to the release, it is an exaggeration to speak of a disastrous price rise on the home market. "We are witnessing the emergence of a market economy and rising private enterprise that the government should support, by passing the necessary laws, in the interest of the nation and not against it. Any other type of thinking is retrograde and seeks to play on the real hardships of ordinary Bulgarians", says the release. In conclusion, the release expresses a hope that "the president's overstatement on Saturday is not so much a conviction as a suggestion by advisers on and off his payroll".
