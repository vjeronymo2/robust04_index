BFN [Excerpts] Kiev [no date as received] -- A session of the parliament on 2 June examined a resolution "On the Ukrainian president's proposal to the Supreme Council on postponement of the election of the president of Ukraine, local councils, and local council chairmen scheduled for 26 June 1994." The resolution says that, taking into account the current preparations for the elections, as well as the fact that postponement of the election would affect social and economic reforms, postponement of the election is deemed inexpedient. The resolution instructs the Supreme Council Commission for Legal Policy and Legal and Judicial Reform and the Commission for State-building, Council Activities, and Self-government to submit before 12 June proposals to the parliament regarding draft laws regulating the powers of the president of Ukraine, the government, and local authorities. [passage omitted] Despite calls from deputies who wanted to make remarks on the voting procedure, Oleksandr Tkachenko, first deputy Supreme Council chairman (Oleksandr Moroz is informally reported on sick leave), put the draft resolution to a vote, and 201 voted to adopt it in principle.
