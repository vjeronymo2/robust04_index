920430 FT 30 APR 92 / UK Company News: Sedgwick forms joint venture with Czech insurer Ceska SEDGWICK Group, the second largest UK insurance broker, has formed a joint-venture with Ceska Pojistovna, the formerly state-owned Czech insurer and reinsurer whose privatisation will be completed next week. Prague-based Ceska Sedgwick Risk Services will oper ate as an independent company, selling risk manage ment and broking services to domestic Czech businesses and international companies seeking to invest in Czechoslovakia. Three local investment funds owned by Czechoslovak banks are expected to own two thirds of the new privatised company's share capital of Kcs1.64m, over which local investors will have complete control. The companies will subsequently seek to place a substantial minority of the new company with a foreign investor. Dr Petr Zaruba, director of Ceska's foreign insurance and reinsurance section, who will be deputy chairman of the new operation, said that this is an ideal time to be investing in the Czech market which is opening up to foreign insurers at the end of May. Ceska has more than 15m policyholders paying premiums in 1990 of Dollars 586m and 8,000 employees in more than 200 offices. Mr Denville Reed, deputy managing director of the risk services division of Sedgwick James, part of the retail arm of Sedgwick, has been appointed managing director of the new company, and Mr Adrian Platt, a director of Sedgwick, is to be deputy chairman. Sedgwick has also set up offices in Budapest, Warsaw and Moscow, and a representative in Romania.
