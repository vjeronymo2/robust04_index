Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Italy's Trade Balance in Red Outside European Union"] [Text] Rome, 5 Mar (ANSA) -- Italy's trade balance with countries outside the European Union dipped into the red in January, a month which is traditionally bad for Italian trade, while over all exports rose 13.1 percent against a 4.5 percent increase in imports, according to the national statistics bureau ISTAT [Central Statistics Institute]. Last January, Italian exports outside the European Union totaled 8.864 trillion lire (5.26 million dollars), while imports were valued at 9.056 trillion lire (5.374 billion dollars). On a sector-by-sector basis, transport means showed the worst performance with a 34-percent plunge in exports. ISTAT noted, however, that by excluding ship sales there was actually a 19 percent increase in sale abroad.
