Language: Estonian Article Type:BFN [Text] Russia has not yet presented a timetable for troop withdrawal from Estonia even though one was expected at the present round of Estonian-Russian talks which took place near Moscow yesterday and today. Juri Kahn, Estonian ambassador to Moscow, who is the leader of the economic issues working group at the talks on the Estonian side, told Estonian Radio that Russia has promised to present a timetable for the troop withdrawal if Estonia accepts the agreement on Russian military pensioners as designed by Russia. According to Juri Kahn, Estonia and Russia did not sign any documents during this round of talks. However, some were prepared, to be signed at the next round of talks. This is due at Lohusalu in Estonia on 1 and 2 March.
