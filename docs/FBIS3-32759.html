Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Comptroller General Manuel Francisco Becerra Barney has issued one of the most important reports on corruption in the country, by pointing out serious irregularities and mismanagement of public funds in nearly 100 state institutions. In a report issued to Congress and also sent to President Cesar Gaviria, the comptroller indicated that the Comptrollership's Fiscal Investigations and Trials Directorate has detected approximately 40 billion pesos worth of shortages that have not been reimbursed. Of that amount, 27.5 billion pesos correspond to public institutions and the central government sector and the rest to government-private capital enterprises and to state-owned industrial and commercial enterprises. The comptroller also indicated that his office will do everything possible to seize the assets of 1,100 officials who committed irregularities in handling funds. The report also demanded that those officials present themselves to the Treasury to return the funds before the appropriate authorities intervene. The institutions mentioned include several ministries and administrative departments. The most often mentioned ministries are defense, health, education, and finance. [passage omitted]
