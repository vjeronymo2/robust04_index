941027 FT 27 OCT 94 / International Company News: Top analyst loses job at Lehman Brothers Ms Elaine Garzarelli, the Wall Street analyst who earned fame and fortune after predicting the 1987 market crash, lost her job yesterday when Lehman Brothers decided it could no longer afford her million-dollar salary. The investment bank issued a terse statement saying that the 46-year old analyst, who was director of sector analysis, had left to 'pursue other opportunities'. However, sources close to Lehman said the bank had conducted a review of Ms Garzarelli's job and concluded it was no longer cost-effective to keep her. Ms Garzarelli's departure from Lehman shocked Wall Street. There were no indications that she had lost favour (although she recently issued a market forecast that contradicted advice from Lehman's chief investment strategist) and she remained highly regarded in the financial community. Ms Garzarelli's views were not just followed on Wall Street. She was familiar to millions of individual American investors through her many television appearances, and was so well-known that she appeared in advertisements last year for a brand of tights. However, Ms Garzarelli was becoming a luxury Lehman could no longer afford. In the latest quarter, earnings at the investment bank - which was spun off from American Express in a stock market flotation last year - plunged 89 per cent to Dollars 11m. It has since been cutting its payroll as part of a cost-reduction strategy. Although she has suffered some setbacks - her attempt to run an investment fund was conspicuously unsuccessful - Ms Garzarelli is likely to receive plenty of job offers. Ms Joan Zimmerman of the Wall Street executive recruitment firm GZ Stephens said: 'She has a tremendously strong public persona, and that persona can translate into an ability to bring money into any organisation.'
