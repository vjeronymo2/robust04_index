940906 FT 06 SEP 94 / International Company News: Normandy Poseidon ahead Normandy Poseidon, the Australian mining and resources group which, via its Poseidon Gold subsidiary, is now the country's largest gold producer, has announced profits after tax and abnormal items of ADollars 85.3m (USDollars 63.3m) for the year to end-June. In the previous year it made ADollars 58.2m. The result was achieved after an abnormal gain of ADollars 34.5m, against ADollars 9.78m in 1992-93. Operating profit before abnormal items and tax was ADollars 191.5m, against ADollars 180.8m. Fully-diluted earnings per share, before abnormal items, were static at 9.7 cents. During the year, Normandy said that there had been a 12 per cent increase in gold production, to 1.3m oz, causing PosGold to post a 9 per cent profits improvement.
