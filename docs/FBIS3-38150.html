Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] The text of a letter from President Glavkos Kliridhis to UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali concerning Rauf Denktas' second letter to the Secretary General was released a short while ago. The full text of President Kliridhis' letter is as follows: Your Excellency: At 2:00 a.m. on 29 January, I received a photocopy of a letter dated 28 January 1994 which Mr. Rauf Denktas addressed to Your Excellency. I would like to draw Your Excellency's attention to the fact that the letter bears the letterhead Turkish Republic of North Cyprus [TRNC], Office of the President, and the signature Rauf Denktas, President. Your Excellency is aware, of course, of Security Council Resolution 541 of 1983 which, in paragraph one of its functional section, deplores the declaration of the Turkish Cypriot authorities of the secession of part of the Republic of Cyprus; in paragraph two it considers the declaration referred to above as legally invalid and calls for its withdrawal; and in paragraph seven it calls upon all states not to recognize any Cypriot state other than the Republic of Cyprus. The Turkish side, completely ignoring Security Council Resolution 541, not only has not withdrawn the secession action but, what is even worse, Mr. Denktas, in complete contempt of Resolution 541 and ignoring the Security Council, signs a letter to the Secretary General as president of the TRNC. Mr. Denktas' motive is obvious. He intentionally signs his letter to you as president of TRNC in order to gain recognition of the secession, knowing that by so doing he is violating Security Council Resolution 541. It is my steadfast view and position that Mr. Denktas' letter of 28 January 1994 constitutes another serious violation of Resolution 541 and since I would not like to participate, either directly or indirectly, in such a violation I must ignore completely the existence of Mr. Denktas' letter of 28 January 1994 and its contents as well as any notification of any information which emerges from this letter. Therefore, I respectfully submit to you that, for the reasons I outlined above, the United Nations should handle Mr. Denktas' letter dated 28 January 1994 in a similar manner. Please accept, Your Excellency, the assurance of my highest consideration. Glavkos Kliridhis, President of the Republic of Cyprus.
