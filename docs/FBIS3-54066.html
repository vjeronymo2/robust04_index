Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Dispatch by 'Abdallah al-Shihri from Riyadh] [Text] PLO Executive Committee Chairman Yasir 'Arafat is expected to arrive in Jeddah tonight on the first visit to Saudi Arabia since the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait in August 1990. 'Arafat, who will perform the minor pilgrimage, will be accompanied by a delegation which comprises Ahmad Quray', also known as Abu-'Ala', head of the PLO Economic Department, and Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh, head of the PLO Information Department. Informed sources said that 'Arafat will head for Riyadh tomorrow to meet with King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz and senior Saudi officials. Reliable sources said that 'Arafat will offer an official apology to the Saudi monarch for his stands during the Gulf war. 'Arafat will also discuss with the custodian of the two holy mosques the developments in the Palestinian-Israeli accord on self-rule in the occupied territories, particularly following the Oslo meeting between 'Arafat and Peres, and the results of the summit meeting between Presidents Bill Clinton and Hafiz al-Asad as well as its repercussions on the peace process in the region. 'Arafat said in an arrival statement in Cairo this morning that he will meet with some Saudi officials to discuss the latest developments in the peace process. Hakam Bal'awi, the Palestinian ambassador in Tunis, said -- following his meeting Friday with the Saudi ambassador in Tunis -- that 'Arafat's meeting with King Fahd will strengthen Saudi-Palestinian relations as well as Arab solidarity.
