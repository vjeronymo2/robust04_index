BFN [Text] Cairo, 25 May (MENA) -- Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas, the former Yemeni prime minister who was recently appointed prime minister of the South after it declared secession, arrived in Cairo tonight on a visit to Egypt as part of a tour of Arab countries. In an arrival statement al-'Attas said he hoped to meet President Husni Mubarak tomorrow, Thursday. He said: I wish to take this opportunity to express my deep thanks to Egypt and its president for all the efforts to remove Yemen from this crisis. He said that Egypt always plays a pioneering role in our Arab homeland and noted that during his visit to Cairo he will discuss the situation in Yemen, the war there, and the importance of stopping it as soon as possible. Al-'Attas added that these topics will be discussed with Egyptian officials. He said he believed that they are fully informed of all the events in Yemen. He noted that various countries' recognition of the South of Yemen will be the topic of discussion during his talks.
