920815 FT 15 AUG 92 / Thai probe uncovers 111 deaths An official committee set up to probe the violent suppression of pro-democracy demonstrations in May has concluded that 111 persons died in the incident, over twice as many as reported by the interior ministry, a newspaper said yesterday, AP reports from bangkok. In addition to the 42 fatalities officially listed by the ministry, another 69 people were confirmed missing by the committee and are presumed dead, committee chairman Sophon Rattanakorn was quoted as saying by the Thai-language newspaper Thai Rath. Hundreds of people were injured during the May 17-21 protests when troops opened fire on demonstrators demanding the resignation of then-prime minister Mr Suchinda Kraprayoon because he had been appointed to the job without running for parliament. Mr Suchinda resigned in disgrace soon after the shootings.
