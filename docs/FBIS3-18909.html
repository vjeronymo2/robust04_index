Language: German Article Type:BFN [Commentary by Andreas Schwarz: "Whitewater and Watergate"] [Text] The acoustic similarity of the terms is tempting: Watergate and Whitewater, both stand for scandal and for cover-up. The Republicans may be particularly tempted by that, because Richard Nixon fell over the cover-up of the break-in into the Democrats' headquarters two decades ago. The fact that now a Democratic President is displaying extreme clumsiness as he tries to get through the investigations against him is just what his opponents want. The comparison is lacking in one respect: The Watergate break-in took place, and Nixon was not proved to have participated in it but to have lied. Whether the Clintons' Whitewater deal is to be challenged in the first place is still open, and the Clintons have not yet been proved to be lying. Compared with Watergate, the Whitewater affair will probably remain only a little brook. Nevertheless: Richard Nixon did not fall because the Republicans wanted to hurt their enemy but because the president led the investigators by the nose and hurt the people -- in their trust. The Clintons have come that far already: Holding back incriminating files about the affair and ordering the investigators to report constantly on their findings, this gives the affair -- if there is not more hidden behind it -- a bad taste. There is one thing that Whitewater has shown as a basic tenet: The team around the President is as weak as its boss. Very close associates and advisers are chosen either by Bill Clinton as a result of friendship or by the First Lady. The fact that Clinton's only emerging (and still disputed) success -- the health reform -- has been accomplished by her is as characteristic as the fact that it is her role in the Whitewater case that is the incriminating one and not his. In addition, there are no real strategists in the White House, who could provide a bit of direction to the President's presentation. Clinton prefers to do all that himself by eagerly praising his wife (since she also defended him when he was accused of womanizing). This shows that there is indeed a difference between Watergate and Whitewater: Even in a mess Nixon still had style.
