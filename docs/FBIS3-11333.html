Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Montevideo, 17 Mar (EFE) -- Financial sources report that the IMF has approved the Uruguayan Government economic program for 1994. The program predicts a 36 percent inflation rate and a monetary devaluation of 27 percent for the year. The forecasted inflation rate involves controlling prices: In 1993 it totalled 52.68 percent. The planned monetary devaluation remains at previous levels; in 1993 it totalled 26.88 percent. The IMF experts finished studying the economic program in early March. Next month the IMF board will analyze the economic situation in Uruguay. Once IMF approval is received, Uruguay will have important support abroad that will create confidence among potential foreign investors and allow the country to obtain credit lines from other international organizations.
