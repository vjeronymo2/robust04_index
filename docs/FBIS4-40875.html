BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladas Burbulis] [Text] Vilnius June 20 TASS -- "An intention expressed by the head of the U.S. Administration to meet leaders of the three Baltic states in Riga on July 6 shows that the United States has recognised and respects our independence," said Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas in a traditional interview on national radio on Monday. "I have received a very kind message to this effect from Bill Clinton in which he confirms the fact of his arrival in Riga and his wish to meet the presidents of Latvia, Lithuania and Estonia," Brazauskas said. The Lithuanian president expects to discuss with Bill Clinton regional problems and hopes for Clinton's support which "has been felt until now." Brazauskas added that "we would attempt once again to acquaint the U.S. president with the conditions for foreign investments into the Lithuanian economy, which, in our opinion, are not bad and which we are going to improve, taking into account the experience of Central European states."
