BFN [Text] In connection with Nursultan Nazarbayev's recent visit to the United States, U.S. President Bill Clinton has addressed a greetings letter to the president of Kazakhstan. It says: Your recent visit to Washington boosted cooperation between the two countries. Kazakhstan is known for its peace and stability, its adherence to democracy, and its respect for human rights. The U.S. President once again highly evaluated Kazakhstan's decision to join the nuclear nonproliferation treaty and spoke about its importance for mankind. It was a great honor and pleasure to receive you in the White House, Bill Clinton said in his letter. He wished President Nazarbayev success and expressed support for his policy.
