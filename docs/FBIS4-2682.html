BFN [Text] Dhaka, April 12 (XINHUA) -- Bangladesh Foreign Minister Mustafizur Rahman left here tonight to pay a five-day visit to China beginning tomorrow. Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen, who paid a four-day visit to Bangladesh in February, extended the invitation. Economic and trade relations between Bangladesh and China will get prominence at the talks between the two sides, the Foreign Ministry said. Bangladesh is now attaching more importance to economic diplomacy in keeping with its overall objective of achievement of the national economic development. During the visit, the Bangladesh foreign minister will meet his Chinese counterpart and other Chinese leaders to exchange views on further strengthening the existing cooperative and excellent bilateral relations between the two countries. He will also discuss regional and international issues of common concern with the Chinese leaders, the Foreign Ministry said.
