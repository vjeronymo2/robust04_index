BFN [Text] Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina has announced in Montevideo that Uruguay is willing to negotiate [a no congelar] Havana's debt with Uruguay and to seek a solution that is favorable for both countries. Robaina made these statements minutes after his meeting with Enrique Braga, director of Uruguay's central bank. Robaina met earlier with Uruguayan President Luis Alberto Lacalle, with whom he exchanged ideas on, among other issues, the upcoming Ibero-American Summit scheduled to be held in Cartagena de Indias, Colombia; the changes being made in certain areas of the Cuban economy; and preparations for the conference entitled "The Nation and the Emigre Community" scheduled to be held in Havana this month. Robaina's agenda included meetings with General Liber Seregni Mosquera, president of the Uruguayan Broad Front; a very interesting meeting with the foreign relations committee of the Uruguayan Parliament, where Robaina explained our country's true situation; and meetings with Uruguayan intellectuals and Cubans residing in Montevideo. Robaina's agenda for today includes talks between foreign ministries, a meeting with his Uruguayan counterpart Sergio Abreu, and contacts with several Montevideo television networks and Uruguay's most important newspapers.
