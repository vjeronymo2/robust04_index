921126 FT 26 NOV 92 / Parliament and Politics: MPs propose study of palace costs AN INQUIRY into the costs of maintaining royal palaces such as Windsor Castle, which was partly destroyed by fire last week, was proposed last night by a cross-party Commons committee. The public accounts committee has asked Sir John Bourn, comptroller and auditor general, to consider launching a 'value for money' study into the Department of National Heritage and Royal Palaces owned by the state. However, in spite of pressure from some Labour MPs, the committee has decided that it will not - at least at this stage - look at wider issues of the financing of the Royal Family and whether the Queen should pay tax. The committee's responsibilities, in conjunction with the comptroller and auditor-general, are traditionally to ensure efficiency in Whitehall. In a private meeting last night, MPs on the public accounts committee discussed the possibility of a wider inquiry into the Royal Family's finances but no agreement was reached.
