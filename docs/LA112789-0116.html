November 27, 1989, Monday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

UNION THREATENS STRIKE AT KIA, SEOUL MAKER OF THE FORD FESTIVA

Union workers at South Korea's Kia Motor Corp. are threatening to strike this week and could hit supplies of the popular Festiva subcompact to Ford Motor Co. dealers abroad.

Ford holds a 10% stake in Kia and contracted with the South Korean company in 1986 to make the Festiva.

Kia, South Korea's third-largest auto maker, estimates that it shipped 68,000 Festivas overseas under the Ford name in the first 10 months of this year, compared to about 60,000 in the same period of 1988.

Officials at Kia said today that the workers seemed set on confrontation over benefits.

A union leader said Kia factory workers are angry that their benefits are lower than those paid at the two other major South Korean car makers, Daewoo Motor Co. and Hyundai Motor Co.

The union leader said a 10-day mandatory cooling-off period is to expire Thursday and added, "If management doesn't compromise by Wednesday, we will go on a full strike from Thursday."
