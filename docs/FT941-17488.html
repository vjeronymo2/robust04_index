940104 FT 04 JAN 94 / Observer: Polishing up By some standards the Asian emerging markets were standing still last year. Birinyi Associates in New York points out that shares in Poland rose by 1,095 per cent in 1993. The Polish index passed the 13,000 mark yesterday for the first time. There are only 21 quoted companies, and dealing is, um, frothy. 'Trading in Irena SA glassmaker was suspended as demand soared on last week's report that the company got a substantial tax refund,' said Reuter. New definition of an emerging market: one that moves on the news that the cheque's in the mail.
