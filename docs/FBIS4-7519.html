BFN [Text] Knin, 31 March (TANJUG) -- Several thousand people gathered at Stojan Jankovic square in Knin today to greet a delegation of the Russian State Duma at a meeting of Serb-Russian friendship. The meeting was opened by the leader of the Serb National Renewal [Srpska Narodna Obnova], Mirko Jovic, and was attended by the president of the republic of Serb Krajina [RSK], Milan Martic, and the RSK minister of foreign affairs, Slobodan Jarcevic. On its visit to Krajina the delegation of the Russian parliamentarians is headed by Aleksandr Vengerovskiy, the vice chairman of the Duma and of the Liberal-Democratic Party of Russia. It also includes Vitaliy Sevastyanov, a hero of the USSR, and astronaut Yegeniy Fyodorov, an independent Duma deputy. Addressing the people of Knin, Vengerovskiy -- often interrupted by tumultuous applause -- said that "Russia is rising again and is preparing to come to your help." He said that 170 million Russians were behind Krajina and that major changes were underway in Russia which would help to bring 1,000 Russian officers to Krajina if necessary. "We have come here to extend our hands and to ensure that we are never separated again." It was with enthusiastic applause that Fyodorov's following words were greeted: "I express the Russian Army's enormous gratitude to you for having found enough strength over the last three years, while Russia was on its knees, to defend your and our freedom. The Russian Army is now with you." In conclusion Fyodorov said: "We are here, you are here, Turks out, Americans out." The Russian delegation has had talks with Milan Martic, the president of the RSK.
