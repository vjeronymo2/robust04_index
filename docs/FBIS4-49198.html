BFN [Text] Wuhan, May 4 (XINHUA) -- The annual steel output of the Chang Jiang river valley last year reached 35 million tons, accounting for 43 percent of China's total, according to authoritative statistics. At present, six out of the country's 13 most profitable iron and steel enterprises are in the Chang Jiang valley. They include the Baoshan Steel Works Group in Shanghai at the mouth of the Yangtze, and Maanshan, Wuhan and Panzhihua iron and steel companies on the lower, middle and upper reaches of the river. The Baoshan Steel Works Group is now the country's most advanced steel enterprise involving the biggest amount of foreign investment. Last year, the group gained four billion yuan (460 million U.S. dollars) of pre-tax profits, ranking first among all iron and steel enterprises in China. The Wuhan Iron and Steel Company's annual output of iron and of steel both surpassed five million tons for the first time last year, at 5.4 million tons and 5.224 million tons respectively. Experts noted that the growth of the iron and steel industry in the Chang Jiang valley has helped promote economic development in the whole area, contribute greatly to the opening up and development of the Pudong new area in Shanghai and will supply needed rolled steel and other materials for the construction of the Three Gorges hydraulic projects on the Yangtze.
