921106 FT 06 NOV 92 / Observer: Biting back What a rotten week for Thatcherites. Besides watching the UK government edge through Britain's commitment to moving towards economic and monetary union, they've seen the US elect a president promising fiscal stimulus to speed recovery. But none of it daunts Sir Alan Walters, Lady Thatcher's ex-adviser and still an arch-exponent of her economic philosophy, who was in biting mood at a lunch hosted by market analysts IDEA. Airing his expectation of British moves to rejoin the exchange rate mechanism after France's elections next spring, he said re-entry would probably again be made at the wrong rate 'and we'll be back on that roller-coaster at the most unpropitious moment of the economic cycle'. As for Bill Clinton, 'I don't know what lies behind that pretty face. But whatever it is, it certainly hasn't come out yet.' He added that his fear is that the new president leans towards protectionism which, in Walters's view, is now the most worrying feature of the world economy. 'There really are shades of 1931 in the air, and Mr Clinton has only one saving grace: a great capacity for learning.'
