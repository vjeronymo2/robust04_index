930330 FT 30 MAR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Kaufman angry at benefit 'delay' THE head of the government's Benefits Agency should be sacked because of the 'shambles' over which he presides, Mr Gerald Kaufman, the senior Labour MP demanded yesterday. The former shadow foreign secretary told the Commons that one of his Manchester Gorton constituents had her benefit application delayed 'because of one mistake discovered after three months and another mistake discovered after 11 months'. 'The agency is a shambles,' he protested, adding: 'This service should be made properly answerable to the House of Commons.' Replying, Mr Robert Jackson, junior civil service minister, referred to delays in processing applications for the new disability living allowance, and insisted: 'A strong effort is being made to put things right in the agency and real progress is being made.'
