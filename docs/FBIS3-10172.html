Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 24 February. The public sector's financial deficit in 1993 was approximately $143 million, 1.1 percent of the gross domestic product. The surplus registered by the central government since 1991 became a deficit in 1993 because of a drop in revenues and an increase in expenses in real terms, along with a decline in the surplus of public companies. (Montevideo BUSQUEDA in Spanish 17 February p 14) Alberto Volonte Ferro, president of State Electric Power and Telephones, is in Washington, where he will sign a $200 million loan agreement with the Inter-American Development Bank and the World Bank. The fund will be used to finance modernization projects. (Montevideo EL PAIS in Spanish 18 Feb 94 p 9) The Central Bank has reported that Uruguay's commercial exchange with Common Market of the South, Mercosur, members increased 21 percent in 1993. This commercial exchange represents 46 percent of total Uruguayan exports. Uruguayan trade with Mercosur registered a $426 million negative balance in 1993, 64 percent higher than in 1992. Trade with Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay represented 40 percent of the total, with a $260 million deficit. Exports to Brazil, its main commercial partner, totalled $366 million, an increase of 30 percent compared with 1992. The trade deficit with Brazil was $269 million, 26 percent more than in 1992. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2231 GMT 23 Feb 94)
