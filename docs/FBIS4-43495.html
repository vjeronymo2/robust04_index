BFN [Unattributed article: "Martino Reassures Van Miert on Privatizations"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, June 16 -- Italy's privatizations will take place with full respect for European Union [EU] competition rules, Foreign Minister Antonio Martino reassured the EU commissioner for Competition, Karel van Miert, at a meeting here Thursday [16 June]. A ministry communique said the meeting took place in a "cordial climate" and also covered a wide range of issues concerning European anti-trust and competition regulations, focussing on the new Rome government's "orientations" for privatizations. Budget Minister Giancarlo Pagliarini also met Van Miert and discussed state aid to industry, privatizations, regional aid and tax rules for payments made by employers for pensions and welfare, a communique said. Van Miert urged Rome to prepare as soon as possible a list of areas that may be eligible for EU funds. Most of the EU funds set aside for development and infrastructure projects in southern Italy over the past several years have not been used.
