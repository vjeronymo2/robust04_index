921009 FT 09 OCT 92 / May Day holiday could be abolished THE May Day bank holiday might be abolished as early as 1995, Mrs Gillian Shephard, the employment secretary, said yesterday, Bethan Hutton writes. She said that the holiday, celebrated by many countries as Labour Day, had been forced on Britain by the TUC. One possible replacement would be an October holiday to commemorate the Battle of Trafalgar but Mrs Shephard said her personal preference was for an extra day added to the late August bank holiday, which might be less disruptive to business than an isolated day. She promised to hold consultations on the issue. Any change would not apply to Scotland, which has had a May Day holiday for longer than the rest of the UK. Mrs Shephard also said that if an EC 48-hour working time directive were adopted, the government would challenge it in the European court of justice. 'Despite the improvements we have won, we remain fundamentally opposed in principle to this directive,' she said. She later hinted that wages councils might be abolished in the Industrial Relations Bill expected to go before parliament later this year. Mrs Shephard said: 'Wages councils and mechanisms like that have no place in today's labour market.'
