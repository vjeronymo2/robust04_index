Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 2 Mar (MENA)--Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid today conferred with Somali Interim President Ali Mahdi Mohamed, who is visiting Cairo. The two officials discussed the Arab League's role in finding a peaceful settlement to the Somali crisis, especially after the withdrawal of foreign troops. Speaking afterward, Mohamed said he and the secretary general reviewed basic issues relating to the crisis in Somalia. He noted that the Arab League has always been in the lead in its efforts to resolve this crisis. He added that 12 Somali faction leaders are visiting Cairo at the invitation of President Mubarak to hold talks that seek to rescue the Somali people. Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid stated that they reviewed the recent developments in Somalia and praised the role undertaken by President Mubarak to achieve national reconciliation and resolve the Somali crisis. He noted that the Arab League is eager to consolidate any role that may help the Somali people emerge from their predicament. He added that a meeting with the remaining Somali faction leaders visiting Cairo will be held on Sunday at the Arab League headquarters.
