BFN [Excerpts] The Islamic Republic of Iran's [IRI] Red Crescent Society will soon dispatch relief aid comprising foodstuff, clothing and other requirements to Bosnia-Herzegovina. The chairman of the IRI's Red Crescent Society, Mr. Seifollah Vahid Dastjerdi, today participated in an exclusive interview with our correspondent announcing the readiness of the society to collect donations for Muslims of Bosnia-Herzegovina. [passage omitted] The chairman of the Red Crescent Society rounded up relief aid dispatched by the Red Crescent to Bosnia and said: [Begin recording] [Dastjerdi] We have so far sent around 500 tonnes of foodstuff. We gave more than $100,000 in cash to their welfare organization and other people who work in that respect. The society also bought around 500 million rials worth of clothing and distributed them at camps set up around Sarajevo and other places in Bosnia. [Correspondent] The Red Crescent has also treated Bosnian Muslims, what can you tell us on that? [Dastjerdi] I should mention that we have not carried out any treatment on the spot. We received some patients and handicapped persons from there. They were treated by the Red Crescent [in Iran]. [passage omitted] We again announce our preparedness to receive patients and particularly handicapped. [Correspondent] Thank you very much. [end recording]
