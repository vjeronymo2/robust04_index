BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Sukhumi June 24 TASS -- Russian peace-making troops reached today the Georgian-Abkhazian confrontation line after a many-kilometer march and started to protect posts and patrol the safety zone along the Inguri river. The zone is 44 kilometers long and 24 kilometers wide, head of the press center of the peace-making troops in Abkhazia Vitaliy Gerasimov told ITAR-TASS today. Routes and helicopter pads have been prepared by an engineering unit in accordance with the schedule of peace-makers' introduction. Two ammunition storages have been revealed on the Gali-Zugdidi line during works in the rain-washed ground. One of the storages contained 40 boxes with shells, the other -- 600 mines. They are being rendered harmless. According to Gerasimov, the troops were located in the area without any incidents. The Russian servicemen are staying at two check points protecting the bridge to the north of Inguri and at 18 control-observer posts. There are five check points and ten posts in the south. All in all, there are four mobile groups in the safety zone. The main goal of the peace-makers is to clear the zone of various weapons, including fire arms and guns, said Gerasimov. An area of materiel concentration is being formed in Ochamchira and Akhuti. The population took the troops arrival with understanding and well-wishing feelings. They hope that the six-months-long mission of Russian servicemen in Abkhazia will help to restore peace in Abkhazia.
