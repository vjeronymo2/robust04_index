BFN [Text] Tehran, April 18, IRNA -- Ukrainian foreign minister, Anatoliy Zlenko, and Iran's vice-speaker of the Majles, Hassan Ruhani, here Monday discussed issues of mutual interests and international and regional developments including the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Zlenko described Tehran-Kiev ties as "good" and called for further strengthening of mutual relations in cultural, economic and diplomatic fields. Zlenko who arrived here Sunday on a three-day official visit also briefed Ruhani on the recent parliamentary election in Ukraine. Ruhani for his part expressed pleasure over the developments in the former Soviet Union and over the independence of its republics. On the plight and genocide of Muslims in Bosnia-Herzegovina Ruhani critisized international organizations for their indifference and called for regional cooperation to put an end to the crimes being committed by the Serbs. He described the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina as the "century's greatest disaster". Commenting on Iran's mediation efforts in Tajikistan and the Caucasus, Ruhani called for international and regional cooperation to defuse the tensions. The vice-speaker of the Majles also underlined promotion of Kiev-Tehran parliamentary ties.
