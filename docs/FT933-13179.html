930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / Europe's trees take a beating The state of Europe's forests deteriorated again last year with nearly a quarter of trees suffering defoliation of at least 25 per cent, according to an annual survey by the European Commission and the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe, writes Frances Williams in Geneva. Weather, insects, fungi, forest fire, action of man, game and air pollution were all blamed, but most countries said air pollution was a factor.
