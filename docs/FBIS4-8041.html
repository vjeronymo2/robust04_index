BFN [Text] Jagodina, 8 Apr (TANJUG) -- Democratic Party Chairman Zoran Djindjic told Jagodina Radio today that Russia will raise the issue of lifting the sanctions against the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia at the UN Security Council within a month. "During our visit to Russia we drafted a request, which expresses joint views of all leading Russian parties as regards Yugoslavia, demanding that the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Russian Federation raise the issue of lifting the sanctions at the UN Security Council," Djindjic said, adding that he had received assurances that a majority in the parliament would vote for this document, about which the Yugoslav public will be informed either today or tomorrow. "Russians have a clear interest to show their teeth to the West on the example of Yugoslavia, and to show that Russia has not lost the position of a superpower. They will do it not because of the Serbs, but because of Russia," Djindjic said.
