BFN [Interview with Luis Camilo Osorio, Colombian national civil registrar, by Chelo Garcia from the National Civil Registrar's Office in Bogota -- live] [Text] [Garcia] Of the total of the countries votes, 95.6 percent have been tallied. Ernesto Samper Pizano received 2,551,029 votes, which represent 45.1 percent; Andres Pastrana Arango, 2,538,580 votes, which represent 44.9 percent; Antonio Navarro received 215,186 votes; Regina Betancourt, 64,610; Miguel Alfredo Maza Marquez, 54,731; and Alberto Mendoza Morales, 30,393. Mr. Registrar, now that the eighth bulletin has been released, everything indicates that a runoff election will have to be held on 19 June to elect the new President of the Republic. Is all set at the National Registrar's Office to meet this new challenge? [Osorio] Yes. We had said that, because of the short time, we should suspend preparations for the runoff elections in case it proved unnecessary. But we will continue to work along the same lines, making preparations for the runoff election. [Garcia] Is this the first time this has happened in Colombia? [Osorio] Well, it is the first time there is an election by qualified vote. Consequently, a runoff election will be held.
