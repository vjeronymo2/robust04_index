930126 FT 26 JAN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Brooke welcomes Calcutt response MINISTERS' reluctance to proceed with statutory regulation of the press was underlined yesterday when Mr Peter Brooke, the national heritage secretary, said the response of newspaper editors to the Calcutt report had been 'extremely encouraging'. Editors agreed last week to strengthen the press complaints commission by guaranteeing a majority of lay members on the self-regulatory body. During questions on national heritage Tory MPs were divided on whether a more powerful form of regulation was required.
