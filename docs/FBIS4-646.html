BFN [Text] In Lusaka, Zambia, the observers, the government delegation and the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] will meet at 1000 today under Alioune Blondin Beye's mediation. Alves Antonio reports: The parties still have to hold discussions on national reconciliation and the conclusion of the second round of presidential elections. Alioune Blondin Beye has already said that it is necessary to speedily advance this negotiating process. According to Beye, cited by very reliable sources, this recommendation was made by UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali, who also requested the parties to end the devastating war in Angola. Another very reliable source also told Luanda Radio that so far there is no (?established agenda) for the resumption of the peace talks. The ball in Beye's court.
