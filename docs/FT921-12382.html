920127 FT 27 JAN 92 / Magazine man plans new monthly journal MR TOM RUBYTHON, editor and one of the founders of Management Week magazine, which went into liquidation last month, plans to launch a business magazine in March. Business & Fortune is intended to be a monthly with a partly controlled circulation of 100,000. About two thirds of the copies will be distributed free to selected company directors and holders of management degrees. Mr Rubython said he had been promised more than Pounds 700,000 to finance the project from three overseas investors. Mr Rubython, who held a 15 per cent stake in the Management Week venture, insisted that the concept was a success but had been brought down by policy disagreements. The magazine was launched last year as a monthly in spite of its name and went weekly in September. When it went into liquidation last month after producing 18 issues, the deficit totalled Pounds 1.19m. Casson Beckman, the liquidators, said Management Week, in spite of failing to meet advertising and circulation targets because of the recession, appeared to have the potential to fill a gap in the market. It added that some of the investors 'became increasingly dissatisfied with the level of authority and credibility achieved by the magazine'. The liquidators said: 'Ultimately Mr Rubython was requested to resign as editor, but since agreement could not be reached on the value of his shareholding, this resulted in deadlock.'
