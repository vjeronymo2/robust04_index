Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Food Supplies to Army in Somalia Under Investigation"] [Text] Livorno, 7 Feb (ANSA) -- The Defense Ministry is investigating alleged misappropriation and financial wrongdoing in contracts for and shipping of food supplies to Italian soldiers in Somalia, the former commander of Italian troops with the United Nations mission in Somalia, General Bruno Loi, revealed Monday [7 February]. "I know that an investigation has been under way for some time by Rome army magistrates responsible for Somalia," Loi told journalists here, adding: "we have total and absolute trust in the magistrature." Loi said he did not know the details of the investigation but confirmed that Italian troops did receive some shipments of spoilt meat, which had to be destroyed "following specific bureaucratic procedures." Judicial sources disclosed that magistrates are evaluating reports of over-pricing and false bills issued by private contractors for food supplies to the military by and the alleged sale by Italian soldiers of meat, officially listed as spoilt, to soldiers of other countries posted in Somalia.
