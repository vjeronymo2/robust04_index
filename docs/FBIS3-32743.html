Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 10 February. Carlos Montoya, Commission for Promotion of Private Investment executive director, said the state earned $526 million from the privatization of state-owned enterprises in 1993. He added, however, that $252 million remain to be collected. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 20 Jan 94 p 6) A European banking group made up of the General Bank of Belgium, the Banque Francaise du Commerce Exterieur, the Banque Indosuez, and the Banque Nationale de Paris has agreed to grant a $115 million loan to the Southern Peru Copper Corporation. The fund will be earmarked to increase copper production and develop environmental control projects. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 24 Jan 94 p B1) The National Institute of Statistics and Information Science reported that the consumer price index was 1.8 percent in January compared with December's 2.5 percent. The January index shows that the inflation rate continues to drop. The average annual price increase was 39.5 percent in December and 35.5 percent in January. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 2 Feb 94 p A1) Peruvian net foreign reserves amounted to $2.847 billion through 25 January, $146 million more than late in December 1993. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 3 Feb 94 p B8)
