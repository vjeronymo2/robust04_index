Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 19, BNS -- Latvian transport must integrate into the European transport network, after joint transport policy is worked out among Latvia, Lithuania and Estonia, Communications Ministry Strategic Planning Department director, Aleksandrs Konosevics, told at a press conference. The European Union (EU) plans to form a united system of transportation by year 2000 involving also the East European states in it. The present EU transport policy is based on market economy and free competition, drawing in private capital. Latvian Communications Ministry has to draft a program on Latvia's transport system integration into European transport network. It must be done by 3rd European conference of communications ministers, taking place after two years. Konosevics explained the ministry's specialists will take into account EU standards and experience when working out Latvian transport policy. EU pays much attention to traffic precaution measures and ecology. Besides, the European states do not subsidize public transport.
