Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Vienna, Dec 7 (AFP) -- North Korea's offer to open some of its nuclear sites to international inspectors is "insufficient" and contrary to demands made by the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), a spokesman for the organisation said Tuesday. Hans-Friedrich Meyer said however that negotiations on the issue were continuing between Pyongyang and Washington, thus offering hope that a settlement can be reached. [passage omitted] "The North Koreans haven't closed the door on negotiations so far and things are still moving forward," Meyer said. [passage omitted]
