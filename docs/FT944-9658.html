941115 FT 15 NOV 94 / Bathers paralysed by mystery virus An investigation is being launched into the cause of a suspected virus that partly paralysed bathers at two popular tourist beaches. Two teenagers are confined to wheelchairs after contracting the virus, which attacks the nervous system. Both lost the use of their legs within hours of swimming at Oxwich Bay on the Gower coast in West Glamorgan. Bathers at nearby Mewslade Bay were seriously weakened. Doctors have been unable to find the cause of their conditions, diagnosed as transverse myelitis. The National Rivers Authority, Welsh Water and Swansea City Council said yesterday there was no scientific evidence of a direct link between the virus and quality of water at the beaches.
