Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Jan. 7--Press secretary of the prime minister Ewa Wachowicz said Friday that the prospect of the opening of NATO is not precise. Wachowicz made the remark after the Friday meeting between Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak and U.S. President Bill Clinton special envoy, Ambassador Madeleine Albright. She said that an official stand of the Polish Government on this issue will be presented following Monday's meeting of the cabinet to be attended by President Lech Walesa. Wachowicz said that Ambassador Albright told the prime minister that Poland's as well as other Central European countries' security has direct and material importance for the security of the United States and NATO. The meeting was also attended by General John Shalikashvili, chairman of the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff, and Polish Foreign Minister Andrzej Olechowski.
