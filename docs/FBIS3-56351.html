Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Defending the rights and interests of Russian Federation citizens in the near abroad is one of Russia's main foreign policy tasks, Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev declared today at a conference on issues of foreign policy regarding the CIS and Baltic countries. Andrey Kozyrev singled out a number of foreign policy trends in this region: strengthening economic positions, enhancing the effectiveness of actions taken, and creating new mechanisms for cooperation. Speaking about problems relating to how the CIS economic union functions, the foreign ministry head commented that Russia should try to avoid hasty decisions, in particular where creating single currencies is concerned. At the same time, he gave a positive evaluation of the recent agreement between Russian and Belarus on the forthcoming unification of currency systems, describing this as a good example of a well-considered agreement, INTERFAX reports.
