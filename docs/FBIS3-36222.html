Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow February 3 TASS -- For the first time after the abortive Soviet coup in 1991 reporters were not allowed to a meeting of the Russian Government on Thursday [3 February]. A spokesman of the government press service explained that, according to an order of the head of the government administration, only a correspondent of the government-run "ROSSIYSKAYA GAZETA" newspaper will be allowed to attend the meetings [plural as received]. Reporters of news agencies, television networks and other media are barred from now on. The government is to discuss prospects for agriculture in 1994, support to small-scale enterprises, state control over labour safety, the settlement of the Ossetian-Ingushi conflict, as well as the plan of government work for the first and second quarters of 1994.
