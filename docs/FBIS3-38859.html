Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Text] According to the Hungarian foreign minister [Geza Jeszenszky], as a result of the Partnership for Peace program signed with NATO, Hungary's security has strengthened. Jeszenszky talked about this when he signed the document in Brussels. However, the head of Hungarian diplomacy emphasized that the Partnership for Peace program could not substitute for full NATO membership. Speaking about the practical forms of cooperation, Jeszenszky said that joint planning and military exercises could be discussed. Moreover, it would be good to involve experts from the North Atlantic alliance in the Hungarian Army's modernization program. Jeszenszky, however, said that Hungary did not wish to participate in peacekeeping with large units.
