July 13, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

'PARENTAL NOTIFICATION' LAW

Once again, the U.S. Supreme Court, in its finite wisdom, has dealt women yet another blow to their reproductive freedom via its upholding of Ohio's and Minnesota's "Parental Notification" laws.

Please understand me.

I am completely in favor of having both parents and teen-agers involved when a crisis pregnancy occurs. But for the highest court in the land to mandate the "Ozzie and Harriet Syndrome" on today's teen-age women is very sad. To be faced with an unwanted pregnancy is enough of an emotional trauma, but to add to that having to, by law, tell a parent who may or may not be understanding or supportive, or worse yet, to have to go before a judge on what amounts to a very personal issue is unthinkable!

The day after the decision was handed down, the National Right to Life Committee was quoted as calling it "a major victory for both parents and unborn children." Too bad they can't ask young Spring Adams, who lived in one of the "notification" states, how she feels about this law. After being raped by her father, a single parent, she became pregnant. When she went to him to get permission (by law) to terminate the pregnancy, he promptly shot her dead.

I predict two things will result from this decision: More babies will be having babies, and more young women will be dying from self-induced or clandestine abortions. I'm sure the Right to Life Committee is pleased.

CHRISTOPHER B. CRINER

Tustin
