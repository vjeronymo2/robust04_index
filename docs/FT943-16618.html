940704 FT 04 JUL 94 / World Cup '94: Shock follows Escobar murder Colombian police reported the arrest of two suspects and said two others were being sought in the murder of national team player Andres Escobar. Twenty-seven year-old Escobar, whose own goal in Colombia's game against the US led to his team's 2-1 upset defeat and contributed to their elimination from the tournament, was shot 12 times on Saturday as he left a bar in Medellin, Colombia's third-largest city. President Cesar Gaviria told mourners yesterday at the player's funeral, that he was the victim of 'absurd violence' afflicting Colombia. Fifa said in a statement that it was 'profoundly horrified and dismayed' by the slaying, while Italian coach Arrigo Sacchi summed up the shock that reverberated around the soccer community. 'The unjustified death of a young player means that soccer is not only and no longer a sporting event, but something whose importance goes beyond our beliefs.'
