BFN [Unattributed article from Brussels EUROPE: "Commission Grants ECU10 Million in Aid To Help Create a Palestinian Police Force"] [Text] The European Commission took the provisions required to make concrete the decision of principle (adopted by the General Affairs Council of 18/19 April in the framework of joint action under the CFSP [Common Foreign and Security Policy] "in supporting the peace process in the Middle East") of granting ECU10 million to assist in creating a Palestinian police force in the Occupied Territories. Mr. Marin informed Mr. 'Arafat this very Wednesday [4 May], in Cairo, of the Community decision. It is now up to the Palestinian authorities to submit operational projects that the Commission can finance (equipment, wages, means of transport, etc., but excluding the purchase of weapons). This measure is only one aspect of Community aid to the Occupied Territories. The ECU10 million are from the 1994 budget, from a 50 million package for aid to these territories, 33 million of which have already been committed for housing, schools, and technical assistance. For the 1994/98 period as a whole, the Union is committed to supplying ECU500 million for economic development in the Occupied Territories. The joint action under CFSP decided on 19 April contains several other initiatives alongside the emergency assistance for the police force.
