August 30, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

CUBAN-AMERICANS

In response to "Cubans Look for Some Material Changes in Socialismo," by Murray Fromson, and "The Futility of Nipping at Castro's Heels," by George Black, Opinion, Aug. 5:

It's obvious that Fromson and Black are prejudiced against Cuban-Americans. Fromson describes us as "paranoid"; Black says that we are the "radical right wing of the Republican Party in south Florida."

Labeling an ethnic group is racist.

Black's irrational hatred is also obvious when he says: "And how can Castro be expected to talk with exiles who dream openly of turning the island into a dependency of Dade County and have a violent 30-year history of collusion with the CIA?" The Cuban-American community is not responsible for the actions of the CIA. This is an agency of the U.S. government.

The label of "violent" to a community of more than 1 million people is racist and false. As for the allegation that we want to turn Cuba "into a dependency of Dade County," as a Cuban, I have to tell you that we only want to see our country free and democratic.

JUAN FREEMAN

Los Angeles
