Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 8 Feb (AFP) -- The Council of State, Colombia's highest court of administrative litigation, on 8 February asked the House of Representatives' investigative commission and the Attorney General's Office to investigate President Cesar Gaviria in connection with the presence of U.S. troops in the country. The Council of State thinks Gaviria may have violated the Constitution by authorizing the presence of foreign soldiers in Colombian territory without consulting either the Council of State or the Senate.
