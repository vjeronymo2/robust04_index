Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 16/3/1994 -- Romania's President Ion Iliescu received Wednesday, March 16, Ukraine's Minister Of Defense Vitaliy Radeskyy, currently in Romania at the invitation of his Romanian counterpart Gheorghe Tinca. The guest saluted the Romanian head of state on behalf of Ukraine's President Leonid Kravchuk. The good relations between the Ministries of Defense of the two countries were pointed together with the wish of both Romania and Ukraine to further them. The parties referred to the agreement that was going to be signed on March 17 by the ministers of defense of the two countries. An examination of the stage of the relations between Romania and Ukraine pointed to ways and the common wish to develop them further, to the interest of the two peoples. Ukraine's Minister of Defense Vitaliy Radeskyy also had on Wednesday, 16 March, a meeting with the chief of the Romanian diplomacy, Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu, who seized the opportunity to stress the importance Romania attaches to its relations with the Ukraine. The sides pointed out the contribution the two countries could bring to enhancing security and stability in this part of Europe, which is confronted with special historical issues and momentary hardships. The sides underlined that, besides the Euro-Atlantic integration as prime objective, the creation of a favorable regional climate was an important complementary factor in the process. In a different line of ideas, aspects of the partnership for peace were discussed, as well as the Ukraine's participation in the peace-keeping forces deployed in Bosnia-Herzegovina, the situation of the Black Sea fleet, and other international issues.
