Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russia is determined to stimulate world oil prices, a high-ranking source close to the Russian government told Interfax. Government experts do not rule out the limitation of Russian oil exports for attaining this goal. They believe that "concern about falling prices in oil, Russia's main export item, has put us on a par with the OPEC countries." The official said that according to Russia's sources, the OPEC countries are ready to brush away old disagreements and take coordinated measures to raise prices by $1 or $2 a barrel in the near future. Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and Minister for Fuel and Energy Yuri Shafranik told the recent economic forum in Davos that Russia and OPEC would pursue a coordinated policy to increase oil prices by at least $1 a barrel. The government sources point out that Russia may ensure a rise in oil prices by limiting its exports of crude oil. Experts of the Economics Ministry believe that in order to attain the set goal, Russia will have to cut down oil exports by at least 8% in the first quarter of the year on the condition that the OPEC states do the same. In their opinion, Russia is most likely to reduce oil exports to the former Soviet republics. In 1993, Russia exported more than 80 million tons of oil to foreign countries. The coordinated oil export quotas for enterprises and territories for the first quarter of the year amount to 24 million tons. During the first three months of 1994, Russia will export a total of about 30.2 million tons of oil.
