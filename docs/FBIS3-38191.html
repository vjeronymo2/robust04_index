Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Report by Metin Gokce in Davos] [Text] President Suleyman Demirel, currently in Switzerland for the World Economic Forum meetings, proceeded from Zurich to Davos yesterday afternoon. He first attended a meeting on world economic developments in Davos. During the meeting, Demirel replied to various questions by representatives of leading business circles in the world. In a news conference after the meeting, Demirel commented on his contacts. The president reported that during his contacts he explained that it is essential to reorganize Europe following the dissolution of the Soviet Union and that East European countries should be included especially in organizations such as NATO and the EC. Noting that he also stressed Turkey's importance for the EC, Demirel said Europe can stretch as far as Central Asia and China using Turkey as a bridge. He remarked that oil and natural gas from Central Asia will be transported to the Mediterranean via Turkey and underlined that Turkey will become an energy terminal in the future. Answering a question on the air operation launched against the Zhalah camp, Demirel explained that the operation was conducted after long investigation. He declared that Zhalah had replaced the al-Biqa' Valley and 2,000 terrorists were sheltered in the camp. Great care was taken not to hurt women and children, Demirel added. Demirel will continue his contacts and bilateral meetings in Davos today as well.
