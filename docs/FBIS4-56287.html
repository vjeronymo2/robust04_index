BFN [Text] Quito, 1 May, (EFE) -- The 15,870 polling stations throughout Ecuador opened to voters today at 0700 (1200 GMT) for legislative, provincial, and municipal elections, which promise few surprises. For several months now, the unanimous prediction has been that the opposition Social Christian Party will win. All that remains to be seen is the proportion of their victory over Sixto Duran-Ballen's conservative government. According to local radio and television stations, election day, which concludes at 1700 (2200 GMT), began with no major incidents. More than 20,000 candidates from 17 political parties are competing for the votes of 6.2 million voters. The people must renew 65 of the 77 seats at the single-chamber Congress for a two-year term. Also during today's vote, Ecuadoreans will elect 54 provincial council members and 607 municipal council members to a four-year term.
