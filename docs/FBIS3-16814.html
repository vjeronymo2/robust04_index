Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] At the plenary session of the permanent commission of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe on Thursday [17 March] in Vienna, the initialling of the bilateral agreement between Latvia and Russia on the withdrawal of the Russian Army was assessed. In the name of the European Union, Greece expressed satisfaction at the fact that the 31 August this year is to be observed as the deadline for the withdrawal of the Russian Army, adding that it awaits the ratification of the treaty by the two countries' parliaments. Sweden, Finland, and Poland expressed approval of what has been achieved, and emphasized the interest of the CSCE in these issues. The representative of the United States of America also expressed his support for what was achieved at the bilateral Latvian-Russian talks, and emphasized that the support of the CSCE in Europe on the issue of the Skrunda radar station is very important to the U.S. Government.
