Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, 10 Feb (INA) -- National Assembly speaker Sa'di Mahdi Salih today received Asif Zardari, Pakistani Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto's personal envoy to leader President Saddam Husayn, and his accompanying delegation. The National Assembly speaker asserted that Iraq's friends are dutybound to work and pressure the Security Council to lift the unjust embargo against the Iraqi epople, now that all its justifications no longer exist. Salih also valued Pakistan's positive stands at the Security Council toward Iraq's just cause. He pointed to the deep historical ties and good relations between Iraq and Pakistan. Asif Zardari said there is a need to end the suffering of the Iraqi people as a result of the unfair economic embargo. He said there is no justification for the continuation of the embargo. The two officials stressed the need to enhance parliamentary ties between the two countries by exchanging visits, forming friendship committees, unifying positions, and doing what is in the interest of the Iraqi and Pakistani peoples.
