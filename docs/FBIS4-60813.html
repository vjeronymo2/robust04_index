BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Anna Melnikova, Yuriy Sizov] [Text] Moscow 30 April TASS -- One-on-one negotiations between Boris Yeltsin and Latvian President guntis Ulmanis began in the Green Hall of the Grand Kremlin Palace. Ulmanis arrived for an official visit to Russia today. Welcoming the distinquished Latvian guest Yeltsin noted that "this is the first official visit of a Latvian president to Russia." The Russian president said that "we have at last approached the landmark where we can reach a number of treaties and agreements, if not agree of all problems." After the meeting in a narrow circle, the Russo-Latvian negotiations will continue in an enlarged composition. Taking part in the negotiations from the Russian side are Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, Defence Minister Pavel Grachev and other officials. The visit of the Baltic president has become a reality due to an arrangement reached at the bilateral negotiations to coordinate an additional protocol to the initialled document on social protection for Russian military pensioners living in Latvia, Sergey Zotov, head of the Russian delegation at the bilateral talks, told ITAR-TASS.
