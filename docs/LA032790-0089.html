March 27, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

THINK OVER SURGERY

Question: I mentioned to my family doctor that I was thinking of having some cosmetic surgery done, and he said I should think about it for a while first. If I can afford the operation and I want to look better than I now do, what is there to think about?

Answer: Any plastic surgeon you may eventually see will probably offer similar advice. The success or failure of a cosmetic procedure often depends upon the patient's expectations. No matter how successful the operation from an objective point of view, it will be unsuccessful from the patient's standpoint if more was expected.

For example, a person who undergoes a face-lift operation has the right to expect to look more youthful; that's the purpose of the procedure. But a 50-year-old woman will not look the same as she did when she was 30. Moreover, there are factors outside the surgeon's control that can affect the results. Poor health and cigarette smoking, for example, may make for a poor outcome regardless of the surgeon's skill.
