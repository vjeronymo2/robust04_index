BFN [Text] The United States and North Korea held a working-level contact in New York early this morning, Korean time, and discussed additional inspections [chuga sachal], including the inspection related to the replacement of fuel rods. Gary Samore, chief of the Nuclear Nonproliferation Department of the State Department, and Kenneth Quinones, the official in charge of North Korean affairs, represented the U.S. side, and Han Song-yol, counselor at the North Korean UN Mission, and (Hyon Hak-pong), secretary at the mission, represented the North Korean side at the contact. Today's contact took place for the United States to deliver its reply to the 5 May letter from Kang Sok-chu, first vice foreign minister of North Korea. The United States reportedly notified North Korea that no further dialogue would be possible if an International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] inspection team were not allowed to carry out appropriate inspection activities [chokchorhan sachal hwaltong]. However, the United States reportedly asked North Korea to quickly reach an agreement with the IAEA over technological problems concerning additional inspections, hold the third round of U.S.-North Korea talks, and resolve the nuclear issue quickly without breaking the UN Security Council's tentative deadline of mid-May, thus, strongly hinting that it intends to resume working-level talks.
