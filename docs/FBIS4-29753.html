BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 21 KYODO -- The ruling coalition parties decided Tuesday [21 June] to reject a summons on former Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa to the Diet to testify on his alleged financial irregularities, coalition sources said. The House of Representatives Budget Committee was scheduled to summon Hosokawa as a sworn witness for proceedings beginning at 1 P.M. The coalition camp will attend the committee to demand another vote on the resolution to summon the former prime minister, the sources said. The coalition claimed the committee's decision to summon Hosokawa is "invalid" because of improper parliamentary procedures, they said. Hosokawa will hold a news conference at 12:30 P.M. to explain the reason why he will not testify, they said.
