941102 FT 02 NOV 94 / International Company News: SHL Systemhouse returns to the black SHL Systemhouse, a fast-growing Canadian-based computer systems and outsourcing group, posted a profit last year but acknowledges it must improve margins and raise efficiency, writes Robert Gibbens. Net profit for the year ended August 31 was CDollars 17.5m (USDollars 12.93m), or 29 cents a share, against a loss of CDollars 145m, or CDollars 3.09, including a CDollars 135m restructuring charge a year earlier. Gross revenues rose 27 per cent to CDollars 1.2bn, with strong growth in service contracts. Fourth-quarter profit was CDollars 1.5m, or 2 cents a share, against a loss of CDollars 149m, or CDollars 2.92, including the special charge. Revenues were CDollars 296m, up 32 per cent. The company's order backlog at August 31 was CDollars 2.1bn, up from CDollars 1.5bn a year earlier.
