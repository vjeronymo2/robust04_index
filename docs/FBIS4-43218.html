BFN [Text] Quite understandably, Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi is very happy both at the strengthening of the government coalition and at his personal success: he was on top of his party's lists in all five Italian constituencies. Let us listen now to the prime minister's first reaction after the first projections last night. He was speaking on the phone to RAI Uno. [Begin Berlusconi recording] I think the victory will encourage all cabinet members to continue in the same direction in which we started. As far as I am concerned, I must say that it will encourage me to try as hard as I can and to work with enthusiasm and absolute commitment, since I have been rewarded with the approval of so many citizens. As for the situation in the Senate, I hope the opposition will learn from what happened and that they will want to be in opposition in a positive way. The opposition is comprised of several groups today -- I clearly mean the Italian Popular Party -- which should think about these results and about their behavior. This would lead them, I believe, to get more in touch with their electorate, that is, people who want to remain among the moderates and want to support the government. [end recording]
