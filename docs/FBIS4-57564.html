BFN [Text] Panama City, 19 May (AFP) -- Foreign Minister Jose Raul Mulino today reported that the Panamanian Government has approved the appointment of Robert Pastor, whom U.S. President Bill Clinton will name to replace Deane Hinton at the U.S. Embassy in Panama City. Mulino told AFP that Panama has already approved Robert Pastor, 47, who is considered a specialist in Latin American affairs. Ambassador Deane Hinton, who retired in 1993, was appointed in the aftermath of the 1989 U.S. invasion that overthrew General Manuel Antonio Noriega. U.S. Charge d'Affaires Oliver Garza temporarily replaced Hinton.
