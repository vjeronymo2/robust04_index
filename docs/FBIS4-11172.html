BFN [Report by Jaime Guerrero from the "Meridian" newscast] [Text] Peace Commissioner Manuel Camacho Solis confirmed that the Zapatist National Liberation Army [EZLN] will continue its consultation process with the Indian communities before returning to the reconciliation dialogue. Camacho Solis said that with the return of a portion of the EZLN troops to their communities, the tension in Chiapas will decrease and a clear course in favor of the resumption of the talks is marked. Thursday night, the EZLN Command reported that a portion of its members would return to their places of origin. Moreover, the EZLN will continue with its consultation process to return to the reconciliation dialogue. Camacho Solis said the EZLN is responding to the messages for peace made by the government and the civilian society. [Begin Camacho Solis recording] I believe it is good news for the country to know the EZLN is responding to these actions with actions that set a very clear course in favor of the resumption of the talks. [end recording] The peace commissioner met with President Carlos Salinas and with Chiapas Governor Javier Lopez at noon. Today, members of the State Committee of Indian and Peasant Organizations of Chiapas, who invaded lands in the state, agreed to contribute to the peace process in Chiapas.
