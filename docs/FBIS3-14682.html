Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Statement by Information Minister Michel Samahah at B'abda Palace on 29 March--recorded] [Text] This evening, the Cabinet held an extraordinary meeting chaired by the president and attended by the prime minister and other ministers. The president expressed his hope and that of the prime minister and ministers that the Christian communities that will celebrate Easter on Sunday will perform all their religious rites -- after the heinous crime committed against the worshippers in Sayyidat al-Najat Church -- in an atmosphere of peace and security, thanks to the measures taken by the state to consolidate security and protect the homeland and citizens. He congratulated the Lebanese people in general and the Christian communities on Easter. The Cabinet studied the draft media law in light of the observations of the ministerial committee entrusted to draft it. It also studied all the extraordinary circumstances compelling the Cabinet to adopt the extraordinary measures at its meeting held on 23 March. The Cabinet also discussed a bill on amending legislative Decree No. 104 amending the law on publications. The Cabinet asked the ministerial committee to redraft the media law in light of the ministers' observations. Following the meeting, Deputy Prime Minister Michel al-Murr, chairman of the ministerial committee, asked the committee to meet at 1100 on Thursday [31 March) to complete its work. The Cabinet also asked the information minister to propose to the next Cabinet meeting that it define the framework banning the radio and television networks from broadcasting direct and indirect political news and programs, with the exception of Tele-Liban and Radio Lebanon, in accordance with the Cabinet decision.
