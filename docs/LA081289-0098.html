August 12, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

2ND STEALTH BOMBER TEST MAY BE TODAY

The second test flight of the controversial B-2 Stealth bomber could take place as early as today at Edwards Air Force Base in California, the Air Force announced Friday.

"The exact date of the flight will not be pre-announced, but is presently scheduled to take place within a five-day time frame beginning Saturday," the Air Force said.

The radar-evading bomber -- at $530 million a copy the most expensive warplane ever built -- successfully completed its maiden flight July 17.

The bat-like aircraft took off from the Northrop Corp.'s assembly plant in Palmdale, Calif., and soared across the Mojave Desert at 10,000 feet for two hours before landing at Edwards, 25 miles from Palmdale.
