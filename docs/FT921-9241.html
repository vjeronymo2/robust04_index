920213 FT 13 FEB 92 / UK Company News: Recession-hit William Jackson falls to Pounds 431,000 A MUCH reduced operating surplus and exceptional charges hit William Jackson in the half year ended October 27. Pre-tax profit plunged from Pounds 1.85m to Pounds 431,000. Mr Peter Oughtred, chairman, said the recession, pre-letting costs at Willerby Shopping Park and reorgan- isation charges all took their toll. The company trades as a baker, meat products manufacturer, and discount stores operator. Turnover rose to Pounds 101.4m (Pounds 99.2m) but operating profit fell to 515,000 (Pounds 2.39m). Exceptional charges were Pounds 322,000 comprising profit of Pounds 782,000 on sale of surplus stores less reorganisation expense of Pounds 460,000. Earnings per share were 18.21p (45.26p).
