Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a roundup of reports of Philippine rebel activities from Philippine media monitored by Bangkok Bureau between 19 and 25 February. Source information is given in parentheses at the end of each item. 19 February A New People's Army leader, Lodelo Neon, and his wife, Imnas Marcos, were captured by the military in the village of Basilio, Roxas Municipality, Kalinga Apayao Province. Neon was a platoon leader of Section 2 of the Dr. Juan Escandor operational command. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 19 Feb, p 25) 20 February The Armed Forces of the Philippines Civil Relations Service reports that the Philippine Army captured a suspected New People's Army propagandist in Davao del Norte. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 20 Feb, p 12) Military investigators in San Miguel, Bulacan are investigating a report that striking employees of a firm asked rebels, who showed up with many guns, to intimidate management. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 20 Feb, p 12) 21 February: No fileworthy items monitored. 22 February Three rebels were killed and three others captured in an encounter between New People's Army terrorists and Army troops in the heavily forested village of Mabini, Daraga Municipality, Albay Province. The captured rebels were identified as Blandino Olaybal, Angeles Avila, and Rico Limiro. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 22 Feb, p b-8) Three New People's Army rebels led by Usman Mabandos, alias Commander Iligan, turned over their armalite rifles to the Southern Command during surrender rites in Sibagat, Agusan del Sur. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 22 Feb, p b-8) 23-25 February: No fileworthy items monitored.
