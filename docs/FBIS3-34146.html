Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and China today began discussions on reduction of troop levels on the line of actual control. The expert group set up to assist the joint working group of both the countries met in New Delhi and both sides reiterated their determination to successfully carry out the task. The group will formulate measures to implement the agreement on maintenance of peace and tranquillity along the line of actual control signed during the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao's visit to China last September. Meanwhile, in Beijing, the Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman expressed the hope that the ongoing Sino-Indian expert group talks in New Delhi will mark a step forward in the settlement of the boundary issue. Responding to queries, the spokesman said the purpose of the talks is to implement the understanding and agreement reached between the two sides. He said there is tranquillity along the border and this fact is to the satisfaction of both China and India. The leader of the Chinese delegation, Mr. Fu Xuezhang, called on the minister of state for external affairs, Mr. R.L. Bhatia. Mr. Fu informed the minister of the friendly atmosphere and the discussions in the experts group which met for the first time. Mr. Bhatia expressed the hope that the discussions will be successful. The group will continue its talks tomorrow.
