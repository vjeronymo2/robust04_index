We have considered the impact of this proposal on Big LEO licensees and conclude they would not be unduly burdened by protecting RAS observations from out-of-band MSS emissions. It appears that less than one percent of the MSS consumer use would be affected by CORF's alternative proposals for protecting RAS from out-of-band MSS emissions. 141 Further, those affected would not be denied communications. They would simply be assigned to another uplink channel by the MSS network control center. We do not believe that the CORF proposals relegate the MSS to co-primary or even lower status. The RAS is seeking protection in bands only in the 1610.6&hyph;1613.8 MHz band, which is allocated to the RAS. Therefore we adopt CORF's proposals to protect RAS, during observations, from out-of-band emissions caused by Big LEO systems.
