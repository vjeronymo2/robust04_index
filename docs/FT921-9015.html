920214 FT 14 FEB 92 / The Lex Column: Unilever Yesterday's announcement by Unilever that its heavily loss-making animal feed and fish farming businesses are for sale is further evidence of what the recession is doing to UK food and agriculture. Recent weeks have brought mergers and consolidation in sectors as diverse as dairy, chickens and ice cream. The news will not sweeten tempers at BP, which put its own animal feed business up for sale only 10 days ago. But unlike BP, Unilever has no urgent need for cash. More likely, its patience has finally snapped with businesses which have absorbed management resources for decades. The sale will bring to virtual completion Unilever's objective of dismantling its historic structure of vertical integration. With oil milling, packaging and animal feed out of the way, the company has simplified its portfolio down to higher-margin consumer goods and speciality chemicals. Unilever would probably not deny that the process has taken longer than it should have. From the investor's viewpoint, however, it confirms the impression of long-term strategy patiently pursued.
