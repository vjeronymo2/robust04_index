BFN [Text] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- Chancellor Helmut Kohl and French President Francois Mitterrand have lent emphasis to their initiative for a speedy closure of the Ukrainian nuclear power station Chernobyl. In a letter to the heads of state and government of the European Union (EU) and the G-7 countries, as well as to European Commission President Jacques Delors, the two leaders said that another accident in Chernobyl would have far-reaching consequences for the population, the whole human race, and the international energy situation. The initiative was announced by Kohl and Mitterrand at the French-German summit in Mulhouse [at the end of May]. According to government spokesman Dieter Vogel, Kohl and Mitterrand expressed their conviction that the topic will be a "central issue" of the talks at the EU summit in Corfu at the end of June and at the G-7 summit in Naples in mid-July. A plan of action for the closure of Chernobyl is to be decided on in Naples.
