910508 FT 08 MAY 91 / World Stock Markets (America): Weak treasury market causes Dow to fall Wall Street US EQUITIES followed bonds lower yesterday afternoon in the wake of the first leg of the Treasury's quarterly refunding, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average, which spent most of the day in a narrow range in lethargic trading, fell 24.15 to 2,917.49. After fairly thin New York SE volume of 153.3m shares, declining issues outscored advances by 840 to 720. The Standard & Poor's 500 lost 2.76 at 377.32. Stock prices started losing ground in the late afternoon on the back of a weak treasury market. Bond prices were depressed by the results of the Treasury's Dollars 13.5bn sale of three-year notes in the first round of its Dollars 37bn quarterly refunding. Demand for the three-year notes was not as strong as expected and the treasury's benchmark long-bond, higher for most of the day, showed a net fall of 5/16 at 95 7/8 , yielding 8.24 per cent, in late trading. NCR, which finally surrendered to AT&T's hostile Dollars 110 a share offer, eased Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 103 1/2 while AT&T lost Dollars 1 to Dollars 36. IBM retreated Dollars 1 3/8 to Dollars 102 after the computer manufacturer said it would reduce the prices of its workstations by as much as 60 per cent. First Capital, an insurer 28 per cent owned by American Express, plunged Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 1 1/8 amid concern about the lack of progress in its rescue plans. American Express was off Dollars 1/4 at Dollars 25 1/8 . American Waste dropped Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 7 3/4 in active trading after PaineWebber lowered its earnings estimates for 1991 and 1992. Waste Management and Browning Ferris, the two biggest waste companies, were down Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 39 5/8 and unchanged at Dollars 28 5/8 . Air Wisconsin Services, a commuter airline linked to United Airlines, forged ahead Dollars 1 to Dollars 9 7/8 after an analyst at PaineWebber increased his rating on the stock. UAL, parent of United Airlines, slipped Dollars 5/8 to Dollars 156 1/8 . Walt Disney, which was added to the Dow Industrials this week and made a strong contribution to Monday's 2.78 rise in the Dow index, receded Dollars 2 1/4 to Dollars 119 7/8 . JC Penney, the department store group, put on Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 52 5/8 after an analyst at Kidder Peabody predicted improved same-store sales for April. The secondary market held up yesterday, with the Nasdaq composite 0.03 firmer at 491.51. Thornapple Valley, an animal slaughterer, climbed Dollars 2 to Dollars 38 as traders covered short positions. Although there was no news to fuel yesterday's gain, about two weeks ago a brokerage firm issued a trading buy on the stock on the basis of its low valuation and the company's earnings growth potential. Canada A FIRM start for the Toronto market was reversed, leaving a slightly easier close for the third consecutive trading day. The composite index lost 8.8 on balance at 3,478.8, after touching 3,498.4. Declines led rises by 299 to 249 after a moderate volume of 23.7m shares, up from Monday's 15m. George Weston, which fell CDollars 1 to CDollars 44, said it expects 1991 net to be about 20 per cent below 1990's results, after reporting first-quarter net of 20 cents a share against 55 cents a year earlier. Trimel jumped CDollars 1 1/8 to CDollars 6 3/4 . The company said it has acquired 50.1 per cent of Swiss pharmaceutical firm Biovail for an undisclosed price, with an option to buy the rest of the equity in two years. Stelco announced a reorganisation under which its two operating divisions will be absorbed into the main company's organisation, and named Mr Robert Milbourne president and chief operating officer. The 'A' shares gained CDollars 5/8 to CDollars 7 3/8 and the preferred 'C' stock rose CDollars 1 to CDollars 8 3/4 .
