BFN [Text] No session of the peace talks was held in Lusaka, Zambia, today. Mediator Alioune Blondin Beye is presently examining documents received from the government and the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] connected with the implementation of modalities for national reconciliation. Alves Antonio reports: The mediator in the Angolan peace process has changed his tactic. He is currently examining the documents from the government and UNITA connected with the implementation of modalities for national reconciliation, so as to come up with a compromise which will serve as a foundation for the negotiations. Thus, the representatives of the government and UNITA remain on alert in their hotels. President Frederick Chiluba is expect to receive Alioune Blondin Beye today to discuss issues connected with the Angolan peace process. Meanwhile, members of opposition parties from the extra-parliamentary group began the day well. They were received by Ambassador (Rocha Pares), representing the troika of observers in the Angolan peace process, and they gave him a proposal which they consider as an alternative for the present peace talks. Thereafter, they met with government representatives. At the end of the meeting, the spokesman for the extra-parliamentary group spoke to the reporting team of the Angolan National Radio. [Begin unidentified official recording] Today, 28 June, we also held a meeting with the government team. We presented our plan and they briefed us on ongoing discussions. We saw a certain understanding, a clear optimism on the side of the government, and thus we think that the process is on the right track. We encouraged the team to continue with this conduct so that peace can be found as soon as possible, since many people are dying in Angola. [end recording]
