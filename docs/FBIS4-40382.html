BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 15 Jun--Another artificial Earth satellite, Foton, designed for continued study of the properties of materials in space, was launched on 14 June 1994 from the Plesetsk cosmodrome by means of a Soyuz booster rocket. Its 18-day flight program envisages experiments on the production of semiconductors, optical glass with a variable refractive index, and biologically active substances in microgravitation, as well as the development of technologies for their experimental industrial production. In accordance with a commercial agreement, a Biopan container with equipment designed by German specialists has been placed on board the Foton satellite. The satellite has been placed in orbit with the following parameters: --initial period of revolution--90.4 mins; --apogee--385 km; --perigee--229 km; --orbital inclination 62.8 degrees. The apparatus on board the satellite is working normally.
