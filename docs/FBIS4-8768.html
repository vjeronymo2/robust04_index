BFN [Text] Geneva, April 18 (AFP) -- Bosnian Serbs resumed intensive shelling Monday [18 April] on the Moslem enclave of Gorazde in eastern Bosnia, hitting the hospital roof and Red Cross centre, a UN spokeswoman said here. "The night was quiet, but gunfire started again this morning," said Sylvana Foa, spokeswoman for the UN High Commissioner for Refugees. A shell was falling every 20 seconds, she said. "The hospital was hit on the roof," she said, adding that the International Committee of the Red Cross centre in the town was also hit.
