BFN [Text] Sapporo, June 4 KYODO -- Japanese and Russian maritime industries have agreed to jointly operate about 20 ferry services between Hokkaido and Sakhalin, Russia, for six months beginning in May 1995, Transport Ministry officials said Saturday [4 June]. The officials said the agreement was made in a shipping meeting in Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk in Russia. The ferry service will link Korsakov in Sakhalin with the cities of Wakkanai and Otaru in Hokkaido, the northernmost part of Japan. Under the agreement, reached by Japan's six shipping companies and a Sakhalin company, a 5,025-ton vessel owned by the Russian company will be used for the regular ferry service. The shipping industries of the two nations have had talks since 1991, based on the Japan-Russia Maritime Agreement.
