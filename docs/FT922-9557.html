920507 FT 07 MAY 92 / World Stock Markets (Europe): Frankfurt rises in confident trading BOURSES made a broad advance yesterday, writes Our Markets Staff. FRANKFURT regained confidence on expectations of an early end to the public sector strike. Sentiment was also lifted by comments by the Bundesbank president, Mr Helmut Schlesinger, that inflation could fall below four per cent this year. The DAX index gained 10.86 to 1,743.50, its biggest rise in a single day for nearly three weeks. The FAZ index advanced 6.70 to 704.70 while turnover rose to DM6.3bn from DM4.3bn. Volkswagen closed up DM5.50 at DM386 following pleasing first quarter results and the company's positive comments for the rest of the year. There is an analysts' meeting today. Other car stocks were mixed: BMW advanced DM4.50 to DM574 while Porsche fell DM5 to DM530. Deutsche Bank gained DM6.10 to DM712.30 following its announcement after the close on Tuesday of a restructuring of its US operations and plans to issue profit-participating certificates with warrants attached. The reopening of Frankfurt airport after Tuesday's closure helped Lufthansa regain DM5.50 or 3.7 per cent to DM152. Among steels, Hoesch shed DM3.50 to DM247 on lower first quarter sales while Kloeckner-Werke put on DM1.40 to DM121.40 on confident expectations for 1992. PARIS came off the day's highs on profit-taking but analysts said the market was likely to move higher as international investors continued to switch funds out of Germany. The CAC-40 touched a year's high of 2,054.50 before closing up 0.22 at 2,047.15. Volume was a heavy FFr3.1bn. The strong rise in Matra and Hachette shares, which were both re-quoted yesterday, surprised the market. Matra jumped FFr15.50 or 9.5 per cent to FFr178.90 on expectations of a favourable merger with Hachette, which added FFr3.20 to FFr150.70. Hopes of lower interest rates lifted the financial sector. UAP added FFr18 or 3.3 per cent to FFr558 while CCF rose FFr10.50 or 5.7 per cent to FFr196.50. Rhone-Poulenc rose FFr18 or 2.9 per cent to FFr646 ahead of next week's first quarter results. MILAN got some comfort from Generali's 1991 results. The Comit index rose 3.57 to 495.75 in turnover estimated at less than Tuesday's L122.4bn. Generali gained L370 to L29,510, while Olivetti rose L75 to L2,910 following favourable press coverage of its Dollars 300m contract to install computers in McDonald's restaurants in the US. The telecommunications sector recovered after its recent drop, with Stet gaining L22 to L1,949. Italcementi, another recent casualty, put on L750 or 5 per cent to L15,590. But the construction sector was harmed by the growing corruption scandal in Milan. Cogefar lost L80 to L3,720 and Grassetto lost L380 to L9,250. ZURICH maintained its rally to close at another 1992 high. The SMI index rose 16.8 to 1,937.3. Industrial companies saw strong foreign buying, with Nestle bearers gaining SFr20 to SFr10,000. Roche certificates advanced another SFr100 to SFr3,710 on Tuesday's good results and the planned issue of ADRs. Brown Boveri bearers put on SFr70 to SFr4,300 on news that Asea Brown Boveri, in which it holds a 50 per cent stake, had won a Dollars 1.25bn contract to build a hydroelectric facility in Iran. AMSTERDAM followed other bourses higher and the CBS Tendency index closed up 0.7 at 130.0 in turnover of Fl 771.1m. Philips disappointed some analysts with its first quarter figures and shed 60 cents to Fl 38.00. Some switching into Polygram was noted: its shares advanced Fl 1.30 to Fl 50.40. Ahold, which lists in Zurich today, was Fl 2.60 stronger at Fl 164.50. STOCKHOLM saw Volvo B free shares rise SKr10 to SKr430 on reports of an early merger with the French auto maker, Renault. The Affarsvarlden General index added 10.6 to 1,004.3, as turnover remained heavy at SKr768m after SKr746m. Ericsson B shares added SKr2 to SKr135 ahead of its first quarter earnings report today. The market expects a pre-tax loss of SKr100m. MADRID's general index gained 0.94 to 250.11.
