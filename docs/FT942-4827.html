940608 FT 08 JUN 94 / Further strikes planned at BBC BBC unions have rejected the corporation's 'final offer' on pay and conditions and are threatening to take industrial action on Thursday, Friday and Sunday. Mr Tony Lennon, president of broadcasting union Bectu, said it was '99 per cent likely' there would be industrial action although negotiators for the NUJ journalists' union had been instructed to seek improvements. Journalists and technical staff staged a 24-hour strike on May 24 in the dispute over proposals for performance-related pay and changes to working hours. Further action was called off when talks took place and the BBC announced concessions to its proposals. Last night the unions said that the revised package tabled by the BBC at the conciliation service Acas failed to address their fundamental concerns. The BBC said: 'During four days of negotiations at Acas, the BBC made significant changes to its proposals to address the concerns expressed by staff and unions.'
