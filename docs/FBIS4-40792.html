BFN [Text] Moscow, June 9, RIA -- Russia and Moldova still have some differences on the time-table for the withdrawal of the Russian 14th Army from that republic. The issue was discussed at a press conference on the 9th round of the Russian-Moldovan talks in Moscow Wednesday. Ambassador at large Vladimir Kitayev, chief Russian negotiator, said that in the estimates of military experts the process would take about 4 years, or slightly less. Nicolae Osmokescu, first deputy foreign minister of Moldova, who heads the Moldovan delegation at the talks indicated that in accordance with the official position of his country, the 14th Army should be withdrawn no later than December 31, 1995-January 1, 1996. The heads of the two delegations confirmed that certain understanding had been reached on the issue, and the discussion of the timetable would be continued at the next round. This is the only disagreement which divides the parties working on the legal status, procedure and timeframe for the withdrawal of the Russian troops temporarily stationed in Moldova. The two sides said that certain progress recently achieved in the Transdniester settlement. Similarly, progress has been recorded in the efforts at finalising other documents complementing the main agreement.
