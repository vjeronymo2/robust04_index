Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, February 5 (KNS-KCNA) -- Keiko Tsuwa, chairman of the Japan Women's Council, in a letter of request to the U.S. President February 2 demanded that the U.S. abandon its scheme to deploy Patriot missiles in South Korea. She said her council wanted detente on the Korean peninsula, Korea's reunification and peace in Japan and Asia and strongly urged the U.S. authorities to stop promoting the plan for the deployment of Patriot missiles in South Korea and strive for detente, peace and stability on the Korean peninsula and in Asia.
