BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 26 May. The Peruvian-Russian Yugansk Petro-Andes Inc. consortium will invest $10 million in oil exploitation in the Titicaca Basin and in the country's southern Andean area. This announcement was made by Carmen Alva de Benavente, the consortium manager in Juliaca. The first drilling will be in Canchi Chico, San Antonio District, Puno Department. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 14 May 94 p 9) The National Commission for Foreign Investments and Technology reported that foreign investment in Peru will total $3.629 billion through 30 May, a 100 increase compared to late 1993. The privatization of the Peruvian Telephone Company and the National Telecommunications Enterprise of Peru accounts for $2.002 billion of the investments. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 17 May 94 p A3) Energy and Mines Minister Daniel Hokama reported that current gold production rose to 25 metric tons per year. He said Peru plans to become the leading country with a production of more than 100 metric tons of gold per year. He noted that in the past two years, private investors have asked for authorization to prospect in more than 5 million hectares. They are RTZ of Great Britain, Angloamerican of South Africa, Cambior and Cominco of Canada, Newmont of the United States, and Shougang of China. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1845 GMT 23 May 94)
