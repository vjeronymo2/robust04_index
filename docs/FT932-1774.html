930622 FT 22 JUN 93 / International Company News: SGS-Thomson 'to muster strong rise' SGS-THOMSON, the Franco-Italian electronic components group, is expected to muster a significant increase in net profits this year by making net income of Dollars 100m against Dollars 3m in 1992, according to one of its main shareholders, writes Alice Rawsthorn. Mr Jean-Francois Saglio, deputy chief executive of CEA-Industrie, the largest French investor in SGS-Thomson, said the company was on course for further profits growth in 1993 after returning to the black last year. SGS-Thomson has already announced net profits of Dollars 24.4m for the first quarter of 1993, having increased its orders by 91.2 per cent over the same period of the previous year.
