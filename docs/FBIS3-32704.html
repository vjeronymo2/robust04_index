Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Caracas, 10 Feb (EFE) -- Venezuelan Omar Baralt, head of the Presidential Commission on Border Affairs currently negotiating with Colombia, today reported that more than 80,000 Colombian guerrillas are operating along the vast Colombian border region. The official pointed out that the worst infiltration in Venezuela lies in and around the northwestern town of Sierra de Perija, where authorities have neglected some 400 km in which 37 fronts of the Colombian guerrillas operate. Baralt said that while only 2 million Venezuelans live in this area, it is inhabited by some 7 million Colombians. According to Baralt, "between fighters and members of the logistical apparatus, the guerrillas have 80,000 men in the area." Baralt stressed that the Venezuelan Government has to persuade the Colombian Government to open a dialogue with the guerrillas because the Colombian Army cannot be deployed across the border. Baralt added that Venezuela cannot get directly involved in this problem, but that it must seek a diplomatic solution because belligerent actions could mean the destruction of the country's main agricultural centers. The Venezuelan official said that migrant workers represent another border problem, because Colombians provide most of the manual labor in the agricultural and cattle zones. He believes this problem must be resolved by issuing identification cards to illegal immigrants to integrate them into the country's production process. Baralt pointed out that one of the positive aspects of the two countries' bilateral relations is the 70 percent decrease in smuggling activities because of the creation of a free trade zone between Colombia and Venezuela. He added, however, that Colombia's Maicao zone has become the foremost international trade center in the Caribbean Basin, with imports amounting to $1.5 billion in 1993. He recommended the signing of a bilateral agreement to regulate trade in this zone.
