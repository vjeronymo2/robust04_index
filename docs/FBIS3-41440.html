Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the ``News at Ten'' program] [Text] Jordan denied that it had agreed with Israel on measures to control marine pollution in the Gulf of Aqaba. The head of Jordan's delegation to the environmental multilateral talks, Dr. Anis al-Mu'ashshir, said no such agreement was reached with Israel. He told ``News at Ten'' that there had been a decision by the environment multilateral meeting to establish three units in al- 'Aqabah, Elat, and Nuwaybi'. Dr. al-Mu'ashshir said the units will be established with the help of the European Union, and will combat oil spills and pollution as well as deal with emergency situations in the Gulf of Aqaba. He said the Europeans will discuss the implementation of the decision with Jordan, Israel, and Egypt, but stressed that this was in no way an agreement with Israel.
