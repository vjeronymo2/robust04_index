Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, January 14 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The newspaper ARDIYN ERH publishes the answers of the president of Mongolia P. Ochirbat to the questions of the newspaper correspondent on the occasion of the Day of Constitution of Mongolia. The president noted that the new Constitution legally consolidated achievements in democratization and renewal of the country, declared the legal basis for independence and sovereignty of the state, for the human rights and freedoms, formation of the new state system, transition of the country to market relations. As the president announced, in two years after the Constitution was adopted a big work has been done on realization of provisions of the Constitution. The president called the State Great Hural, government and other organs to work actively on intensification of the renewal processes. As the newspaper informs, at yesterday's sitting of the State Great Hural session N. Bagabandi made a salutary speech, which was addressed to all citizens, on the occasion of the second anniversary of the new Constitution. The State Great Hural chairman noted that adoption of democratic Constitution has been an event of a turning importance in the country's history. The newspaper carries an interview of its correspondent with the new chief of the State Security Central Department, Lieutenant Colonel D. Sandag. He notes that the basic functions of the department is intelligence and counterintelligence work, the state guarding. D. Sandag announces that the State Security Central Department will be engaged only in fulfilment of these tasks, but not to play a role of a political police. The newspaper publishes the laws on struggle with smoking and on international agreements of Mongolia. The newspaper HUMUUN BICHIG, which is regularly issued in the old Mongolian script, informs that on 22 January the first session of the society "for the Mongolian script" will be convened. The newspaper notes that at the congress discussed will be the issues of rendering assistance to revival of the Mongolian script, realization of the decisions of the government on using this script in official documents and business correspondence. In an interview with the newspaper correspondent the deputy head of the society "for Mongolian script," poet T. Galsan stresses that despite the numerous bans the Mongolian script is living and at present is reviving thanks to the democratic changes taking place in the country. T. Galsan notes that mastering of the Mongolian script is a duty of every citizen, who thinks about his motherland. The newspaper informs about organization of the next olympiad of the Mongolian language and the Mongolian script. The olympiad is organized every year in three stages. Among school children of the first and second forms the olympiad is organized on reading, among pupils of senior forms on writing. The final third stage of the olympiad will be held in April. The newspaper continues publishing the lessons in the Mongolian script.
