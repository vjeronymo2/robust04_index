BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 4 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The Mongolian National Democratic Party (MNDP), one of the leading opposition political forces of Mongolia, has joined the International Democratic Union [IDU] in the course of the regular conference held in Sydney of this international organization which includes more than 30 parties and organizations of various countries of Asia, Africa, Caribean region, Europe, Latin America and Asia and Pacific region as well. Now the MNDP became the 35th member of the organization. The admission of the MNDP to the IDU is to serve as a significant step for further promoting the process of democracy and renovation of Mongolian society, notes D. Ganbold in his interview granted to the MONTSAME correspondent. He has just returned to Ulaanbaatar after his participation in the Sydney conference of the international organization.
