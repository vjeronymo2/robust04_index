BFN [Text] In an interview with a SAKINFORMI correspondent, Georgian head of state Eduard Shevardnadze made the following statement in connection with the bombing by NATO planes of Bosnian Serb positions near the town of Gorazde: This operation can be viewed not only as an isolated incident but also as the first serious attempt to counter the phenomenon of war with force. This was a serious test for the principle of establishing peace by force. The incident should serve as a lesson to all aggressive and irresponsible parties, be it a nationalist or separatist force. It is necessary to honor obligations undertaken by an agreement, especially if international organizations are involved. In the event of the opposite happening, in the world today there is a more powerful and just force, such as the United Nations and NATO, which is capable of neutralizing an aggression such as this. It must be noted that in planning and carrying out such major operations, the practice of political consultation with leading states, especially, the permanent members of the UN Security Council, should not be ignored.
