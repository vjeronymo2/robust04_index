930701 FT 01 JUL 93 / Policing Britain: How Sheehy sees the future --------------------------------------------------------------------- POLICING BRITAIN: HOW SHEEHY SEES THE FUTURE --------------------------------------------------------------------- All new recruits to be appointed on 10-year fixed-term contracts, subsequently renewed every five years --------------------------------------------------------------------- Abolition of the ranks of chief inspector, chief superintendent and deputy chief constable --------------------------------------------------------------------- A serverance exercise to terminate the contracts of 5,000 middle-ranking and senior officers --------------------------------------------------------------------- Performance-related pay, with no annual increase for unsatisfactory officers --------------------------------------------------------------------- Opportunities for big bonus earnings for senior officers --------------------------------------------------------------------- Base pay rates linked to median non-manual private-sector earnings --------------------------------------------------------------------- Lower starting pay rates for new recruits --------------------------------------------------------------------- Retirement age to be raised from 55 to 60, with the qualifying period for full pensions up from 30 to 40 years --------------------------------------------------------------------- Ending of many overtime payments.
