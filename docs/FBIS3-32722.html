Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Nicaraguan economic developments monitored through 8 February. The Central American Integration Bank has granted Nicaragua $140 million in credit for agriculture production, Presidency Minister Antonio Lacayo has said during a meeting with private bankers and farmers. In addition, the tourism sector will receive $8 million in credits, he has said. (Managua Sistema Nacional de Television Network in Spanish 0100 GMT 3 Feb 94) The Nicaraguan Government has left cotton farmers without financing and allocated resources to the livestock sector only for $170 million for the 1994 to 1995 harvest cycle. National Development Bank authorities have said there is no credit policy for the cotton sector, even though farmers planned to increase cotton fields from 2,100 to 7,000 hectares. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 1724 GMT 6 Feb 94) The Consumer Price Index increased 1.16 percent in January due to an increase in fuel prices, the Central Bank has reported. In addition, the government predicted a 10-percent inflation rate and a 12-percent devaluation of the cordoba in regard to the dollar in 1994 if the current 1-percent devaluation rate per month continues. (Managua BARRICADA in Spanish 7 Feb 94 p 8)
