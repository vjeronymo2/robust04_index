BFN [Text] National Party [NP] leader F.W. de Klerk says the constitution should be amended to make provision for the establishment of a new party without members of parliament being forced to resign from their posts. Speaking in a news conference in Cape Town, Mr. de Klerk said that according to the present constitution a large group within a political party was not permitted to break away and form a new party. Mr. de Klerk added that although he was in favor of proportional representation being retained in the new constitution, geographical representation should be incorporated as well. He also announced that the NP, until now made up of four provincial parties, would become one federal party.
