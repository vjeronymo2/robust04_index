Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Mar 28, BNS -- Ratification of Lithuanian-Russian treaties by the Russian parliament was discussed in Moscow last Friday [25 March] at a meeting between Romualdas Kozyrovicius, Lithuanian ambassador to Russia, and Vladimir Podoprigora, chairman of the Foreign Affairs Committee of the Russian Federation Council, the upper house of the Russian parliament. The two discussed possibilities to ratify in the Russian parliament an agreement on mutual legal assistance and a consular convention, both of which were signed in Lithuania in 1992 and have already been ratified by the Lithuanian parliament. Lithuanian and Russian officials also exchanged views on the implementation of a bilateral agreement on the most favored nation trade status and prospects to expand the Kaliningrad free economy zone. Podoprigora said the legal assistance treaty and the consular convention will be included among the priority treaties to be ratified by the Russian lawmakers.
