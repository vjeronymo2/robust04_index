November 16, 1989, Thursday, P.M. Final

NATION;

ANNUAL SMOKEOUT IS IN THING

Masses of Americans snuffed out cigarettes, cigars and pipes today to take part in the 13th Great American Smokeout -- a mammoth public health effort aimed at saving smokers from an early grave.

Sponsored by the American Cancer Society, the 24-hour smoking moratorium is a warning to men, women and children about the nation's leading cause of preventable death. The annual smoking death count totals 390,000 -- or an average 1,000 funerals a day -- from cancer, heart disease and lung disease.

In North Carolina, which depends on a $900-million tobacco crop, the society distributed a record amount of educational material promoting the smokeout.
