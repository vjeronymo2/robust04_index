940525 FT 25 MAY 94 / Study discounts fears on sick pay The 1991 changes to the government's statutory sick pay scheme, which reduced the amount employers can claim back from 100 per cent to 80 per cent, did not have the adverse consequences feared, says government-funded research. A survey by the Centre for Research in Social Policy at Loughborough University found that the change did not cause employers to reduce their occupational sick pay provision or alter recruitment and employment practices.
