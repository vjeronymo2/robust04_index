Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report signed "sm": "Installation Constructers Hope for Large-Scale Orders"] [Excerpts] Bonn, 31 January -- Economic relations will be the focal issue of a four-day visit to Germany by Indian Prime Minister Narasimha Rao to begin on Wednesday [2 February]. German companies hope this visit will support their efforts to get large-scale orders from India. In return, Rao is working for German investment on the subcontinent. One of the central issues of the talks in Bonn is the participation of German companies in expanding the power supply in India. This involves orders worth double-digit billion sums. Yesterday, Indian Energy Minister Salve explained to leading German installation constructors in Bonn his government's plans for expanding the energy supply. According to him, new construction is planned of seven conventional power stations with a capacity of 5,500 megawatts and an investment of 12 billion German marks. In addition, seven existing power stations are to be modernized, that is, to be equipped with modern environmental technology. Furthermore, it is planned to expand India's mains distribution. [passage omitted] During his visit to Bonn, Rao will take time for a "German-Indian economic conference," and for individual talks with the directors of Hoechst, BASF, Mercedes, and Siemens as well as with Volkswagen chief Ferdinand Piech. The political talks with Chancellor Helmut Kohl, Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel, and Social Democratic Party Chairman Rudolf Scharping will focus primarily on coordinating a joint route toward the goal of the two countries getting permanent seats on the UN Security Council. The Germans will also be urging India to sign the Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty.
