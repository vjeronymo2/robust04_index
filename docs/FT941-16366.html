940111 FT 11 JAN 94 / 135 flood warnings in effect throughout south of England About 135 flood warnings from the National Rivers Authority were in effect throughout the south of England yesterday, including three red warnings for areas where rivers threatened to burst their banks, Motoko Rich writes. The three areas most at risk were: Chichester on the River Lavant, Arlesey on the River Hiz near Bedford, and Blunham on the Ivel, also near Bedford. There were reports of severe flooding along much of the south coast. A leading firm of loss assessors said hundreds of properties were affected. The firm said: 'We had to take a helicopter over the area (from Sussex to Dorset). We could not get to it by vehicle.' It added that there was 'a lot of devastation - houses, fields, roads, caravan parks, industrial estates, large commercial areas, all under water. When you are in the air it looks like one big lake.'
