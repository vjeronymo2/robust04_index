BFN [Text] Through the publication of a decree in the official gazette yesterday, the Foreign Ministry promulgated an agreement with Jamaica for the prevention, control, surveillance, and repression of the illegal consumption and trafficking of drugs and narcotics. The agreement is signed by former President Patricio Aylwin. It was approved by Congress as stated in Resolution No. 1280 of the Chamber of Deputies. The two governments are aware of the problems stemming from drugs, including the deterioration of economies and the harm to peoples' health. The idea is to establish a permanent mechanism that will allow Chilean and Jamaican authorities to exchange information related to drug trafficking.
