930921 FT 21 SEP 93 / Weapon secrets spy trial opens A COMMUNIST recruited by the Russians 20 years ago passed on British weapons secrets to the KGB, it was alleged at the Old Bailey yesterday, Richard Donkin writes. Mr Michael Smith, an electronics graduate and an official of the Young Communist League, spied for the KGB while working for the electronic giant GEC, Sir Derek Spencer QC, the solicitor-general, said. Mr Smith has denied four charges under the Official Secrets Act. He was employed as a quality systems audit manager at the GEC Hurst research centre then at Wembley, north-west London. The trial continues today.
