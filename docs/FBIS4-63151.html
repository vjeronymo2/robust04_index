BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS special correspondents Vadim Byrkin and Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Stepanakert-Yerevan 19 May TASS -- The situation on the borders of Nagorno-Karabakh remains quiet on Thursday [19 May]. Masis Mailyan, adviser to the Nagorno-Karabakh Foreign Ministry, told ITAR-TASS on Thursday that the observance by the conflicting parties of a ceasefire understanding is a result of the Moscow meeting of the defence ministers from Armenia, Azerbaijan and Nagorno-Karabakh, which was reached with the mediation of Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev. Mailyan added that the cease-fire is a good prerequisite for Azerbaijan to join an understanding on the order of realising the Moscow protocol of last 18 February. The adviser noted that the Karabakh military leadership returned to Stepanakert on Wednesday evening and expects Azerbaijan to join a protocol on ceasefire and disengagement of sides in the zone of the Armenian-Azeri conflict, which was signed by Armenia, Nagorno-Karabakh and Russia in Moscow on 17 May. According to information from the Armenian Defence Ministry press service, last night and this morning were quiet on Armenian borders. The sides refrain from firing engagements and active combat operations.
