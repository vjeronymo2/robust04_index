Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, February 3 (KCNA) -- The director of the South Korean council for measures on the volunteer corps question January 29 stated that five South Korean women who had been forced to serve the army as "comfort women" decided to bring a suit at the Tokyo district public prosecutor's office, according to the Japanese paper MAINICHI SHIMBUN. They said the Japanese imperialists' act of forcing women to serve the army as "comfort girls," which deprived them of the last vestige of self-respect and trust in humanity was fiendish crime unprecedented in human history, and demanded that the Japanese prosecution punish those responsible for the establishment of the system of "comfort women for the army" and the assaulters, said the paper.
