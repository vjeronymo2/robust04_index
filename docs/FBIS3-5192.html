Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 3 -- President Le Duc Anh received here today the British Princess Royal Princess Anne Elizabeth Alice Louise on visit to Vietnam. [sentence as received] The princess introduced president Le Duc Anh activities in Vietnam of the Save the Children Fund (SCF) of which and expressed her belief that she is president her current visit to Vietnam would help her in coming welfare projects on supporting Vietnamese women and children. [sentence as received] For his part, President Le Duc Anh thanked the princess, the government, humanitarian organizations and people of Britain for their practical assistance to Vietnam, especially to women and children. He expressed his confidence that the princess's visit to Vietnam would contribute to promoting the friendship and cooperation between the two peoples. President Le Duc Anh clearly pointed out that though meeting with any difficulties, the Vietnamese Government has always attached great attention to the people's life in general and women and children's life in particular. Also this morning Princess Anne Elizabeth Alice Louise was received by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. Speaking to his guest, the prime minister highly appreciated the British Government's efforts in accelerating the economic cooperation between the two countries and expressed his thanks to the positive assistance of the Save the Children Fund humanitarian organizations of the U.K. to Vietnamese women, children and people. Princess Anne Elizabeth expressed her and the fund's concern in promoting assistance to other countries, including Vietnam, first of all to children. She also expressed her hope that with 75 years' experience, the Save the Children Fund will effectively help Vietnamese children. The Save the Children/UK began working in Vietnam in 1966, supporting a children's hospital, providing sponsorships for children of poor families establishing a convalescent home and a training centre for widows. Since then SCF has provided financial assistance to various indigenous organisations and emergency relief assistance to different localities in Vietnam. A number of development projects funded by SCF have been developed successfully in Nghe An, Ha Tinh and Thanh Hoa Provinces. In the 1994-95 fiscal year, SCF plans to fund 1.1 million pounds in Vietnam covering various projects. These include primary health care, health financing, medical schools, agro-forestry infrastructure, credit and income generation, disaster management and social development. The projects are sited throughout Vietnam, in Hanoi, Haiphong, Thanh Hoa, Nghe An, Ha Tinh and Ho Chi Minh City. SCF has established close links with its Vietnamese partners in the localities concerned and enjoys the support of the Vietnamese central offices which has facilitates SCF's work in the country.
