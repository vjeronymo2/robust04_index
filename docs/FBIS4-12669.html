BFN [Text] Tehran, April 11, IRNA -- The Foreign Minstry here Monday condemned the savage Serbian attacks on the defenceless people of Gorazde as "the explicit breach of the United Nations Charter." The ministry in a statement said the indiscriminate aggressions of the Serbs against the city, declared as a safe haven in the Security Council Resolutions 824 and 836, revealed the indifference of the international community towards "distortion of human dignity." The statement called on all countries, particularly the Muslim nations, to adopt serious and rapid measures against the Serbian crimes. Latest reports say that serbian occupiers, encouraged by the indifference of the international circles, have intensified the massacre and ethnic cleansing operatons in the city in voilation of all security council's resolutions. Meanwhile, the Bosnian presidency announced Saturday that Serbs had used chemical gases to kill many people in Gorazde.
