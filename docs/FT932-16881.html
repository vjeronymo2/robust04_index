930403 FT 03 APR 93 / Swedish government wins vote Sweden's centre-right minority government yesterday won a key vote on cutting unemployment benefits, averting a political crisis in the country, Christopher Brown-Humes reports from Stockholm. The opposition New Democracy party, which holds the balance of power, voted for the proposal which will cut pay-outs from 90 per cent to 80 per cent of earnings and save SKr6.2bn.
