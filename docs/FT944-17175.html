941010 FT 10 OCT 94 / Arts (Openings): Tate Gallery, London Ruskin described his work as 'a pot of paint flung in the public's face' but today the paintings of James McNeill Whistler look decidedly conservative alongside many works at the Tate. The Tate's major exhibition, which opens on Thursday, aims to give new perspectives on the artist's role as teacher, polemecist, aesthetic theorist and critic.
