Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 24 Feb -- Major General Boleslaw Izydorczyk was on Thursday appointed to the post of head of the National Defense Ministry office for intelligence services affairs by the National Defense minister, National Defense Ministry press spokesman, Colonel Wielsaw Rozbicki, reported on 24 February. Command of the executive structures of these services, answerable to the chief of the Polish Army General Staff, has been entrusted to Colonel Konstanty Malejczyk, the communique passed to PAP by the spokesman reads. General Izydorczyk was born in Lodz in 1943. He completed higher studies at the Military Technical Academy in Warsaw in 1968. From 1970 to 1973 he served in the operational and training section of the staff of the deputy chief of the Polish Army General Staff responsible for economic and technical affairs. He took part in UN peace missions, in the office of the United Nations peacekeeping forces in the Middle East-Suez and also the UN force staff for the observation of the separation of forces in Damascus. From 1978 to 1981 he served in the second department of the Polish Army General Staff. In the years 1982-84 he was the deputy military attache at the Polish Embassy in Washington. In the years 1985-88 he again served in the second department of the Polish Army General Staff. He was inter alia a military observer in the framework of the implementation of the CSCE process and the Stockholm document. From 1988 to 1990 he was head of the international group of military observers in the UN mission in Namibia. From 1990 to 1991, he was deputy chief of the board of the Military Foreign Services. From 1991 to 1992 he filled the post of head of the board in the command of the Military Intelligence Services; from June 1992 he was the head of the Military Intelligence Services. He is married with two daughters.
