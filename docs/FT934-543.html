931224 FT 24 DEC 93 / Government firm on Andersen ban The government yesterday rejected efforts by accountants Arthur Andersen to have a ban on the firm doing public-sector work lifted. The Treasury solicitor wrote yesterday to Herbert Smith, Andersen's lawyers, rejecting the firm's claim that the action could be in breach of European law. Andersen has not obtained public-sector work for nearly a decade since it was sued by the government for alleged negligence as auditor to De Lorean, the car company. The Treasury said that its ban on Andersen obtaining auditing, privatisation advisory and other accounting work would remain while litigation was in progress. It emerged this week that the firm had approached the government in the autumn after enactment of new European Union legislation suggesting that outstanding litigation need not be a barrier to tenders for other work. Earlier this year the government recommended a ban on giving any work to 13 named partners and staff at accountants Touche Ross. However Touche as a firm continues to receive work.
