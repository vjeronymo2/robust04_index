Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 10 KYODO -- Japan is to extend a total of 762 million yen to Rwanda for debt relief and to help ease its economic difficulties, Foreign Ministry officials said Monday [10 January]. Officials of the two governments exchanged notes on the assistance in Nairobi, Kenya, they said. The aid includes 461 million yen for a project to buy equipment to maintain national roads, and 300 million yen to promote economic structural reform efforts and to ease economic difficulties including debt problems. Also included is 1.81 million yen to cover debts for which repayment fell due in the latter half of fiscal 1992, ended last March, but on which capital and rescheduled interest has not yet been paid, they said.
