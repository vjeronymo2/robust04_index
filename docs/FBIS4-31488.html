BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 9/6/1994 -- "The policy of getting closer to NATO, Romania's adherence to the Partnership for Peace, to the Western European Union [WEU], the steps we are making towards full integration within Euro-Atlantic structures are all representing a political option of the Romanian state," Defense Minister Gheorghe Tinca told the government's daily VOCEA ROMANIEI. "Romania is taking part in several UN programs for international assistance in areas of conflict," the Romanian minister added, reminding that participation of the Romanian Army with a field hospital and a subunit to fight radioactive contamination during the Gulf war. "Moreover, the Partnership for Peace implies a series of concrete offers from us in the field of infantry, artillery, navy, and education, that is, those facilities put at the disposal for joint action with other NATO partners. For Romania, to ensure its security in an unstable area means the possibility and compulsory condition to consolidate its market economy, to expand commercial exchanges and constructive diplomacy apt to ensure the Romanian people a European standard of living, without threats of war, without embargoes that are so damaging and enjoying full freedom of a democratic political life. Democracy must not be only conquered, but also defended," the defense minister said.
