BFN [Text] Prague May 16 (CTK) -- If the elections were held today, the Civic Democratic Party (ODS, the major government coalition party) would get 26 percent of the vote, according to a May survey conducted by the Institute for the Public Opinion Research (IVVM) among 788 respondents. The Czech Social Democratic Party (CSSD, an opposition party) would get 11 percent, the Civic Democratic Alliance (ODA, a member of the government coalition) 10 percent, and the Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia (KSCM, opposition) and the Christian Democratic Union-Czechoslovak People's Party (KDU-CSL, a member of the government coalition) each five percent of the vote. Support for the ODS has continued to fall, reaching an all-time low for the second time this year, whereas more people would vote smaller parties. The number of people who would vote the CSSD, ODA and KSCM generally remains stable. The ODS tends to attract university-educated people (41 percent), entrepreneurs (30 percent) and other employees (33 percent), and it would obtain most support from towns with between 5,000 to 20,000 residents (34 percent) and the East Bohemian region (34 percent). Social democrats would get most of their support from villages with between 2,000 and 5,000 residents (17 percent). The ODA would be chosen by university-educated people (20 percent and entrepreneurs (21 percent) most often aged between 30 and 44. The Communists would obtain most support from people with elementary education (9 percent) and by over-60's (10 percent). The Christian Democratic Union-Czechoslovak People's Party (KDU- CSL) also has most supporters among the elderly (8 percent). Fourteen percent of people from villages with up to 500 inhabitants would vote this party. The survey found that 71 percent of the respondents would go to the polls if the election were held today and 19 percent would not go, with 10 percent not knowing.
