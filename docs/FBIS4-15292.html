BFN [Text] Moscow, April 5 (CTK) -- Czech Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus, on a two-day visit to the Russian Federation since Monday [4 April], was received by Russian President Boris Yeltsin in the Kremlin today. The meeting was attended by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev. Opening the meeting, Yeltsin stressed that the Russian-Czech treaty he and Czech President Vaclav Havel signed last August during Yeltsin's official visit to Prague was the basis of relations between Russia and the Czech Republic. He said there were problems in Russian-Czech relations. "However, we seek to conform with the line of cooperation," he added. No details about the half-hour meeting have been announced as yet. From the Kremlin Klaus will leave for the Russian Government office where he will talk with his Russian counterpart Viktor Chernomyrdin. After the talks the two premiers will meet journalists.
