BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports by Pyongyang Korean Central Broadcasting Network in Korean between 12 and 14 May summing up the visit of Yang Hyong-sop, chairman of DPRK Supreme People's Assembly, to South Africa to attend the presidential inauguration ceremony. At 1300 GMT on 12 May, the radio reports that Yang Hyong-sop "on 9 and 10 May met with South African President Nelson Mandela; its former President De Klerk; Fidel Castro, president of the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Cuba; Senegalese President Abdou Diouf; Angolan President Jose Eduardo dos Santos; Ugandan President Yoweri Kaguta Museveni; Botswana President Ketumile Masire; Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat; Zimbabwean President Robert Mugabe; Ethiopian interim government President Meles Zenawi; Tanzanian President Ali Hassyan Mwinyi; Seychelles President France Albert Rene; Mozambican President Joaquim Alberto Chissano; Namibian President Sam Nujoma; Gabonese President El Hadj Omar Bongo; Pakistani Premier Benazir Bhutto; and UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali." Then the radio reports that Yang Hyong-sop met with "the vice presidents and foreign ministers of various countries, including the United States, Afghanistan, Bolivia, Indonesia, and Suriname." At 1300 GMT on 14 May, the radio reports on the departure of Yang Hyong-sop from Johannesburg on 12 May. It says he was seen off at the airport by members of the presidential inauguration ceremony organizing committee.
