BFN [Text] Here is the Georgian head of state's decree on the extension of the state of emergency in Abasha, Vani, Zugdidi, Lanchkhuti, Martvili, Mestia, Ozurgeti, Samtredia, Senaki, Khobi, Khoni, Tsalenjikha, Chokhatauri, and Chkhorotsku rayons and in the town of Poti: With the aim of ensuring the security of citizens and consolidating law and order in all spheres of public and social life in West Georgia, and in accordance with the law of the Republic of Georgia of 6 November 1992 on state authority and the law of the Republic of Georgia of 11 December 1990 on state of emergency, the state of emergency in Abasha, Vani, Zugdidi, Lanchkhuti, Martvili, Mestia, Ozurgeti, Samtredia, Senaki, Khobi, Khoni, Tsalenjikha, Chokhatauri, and Chkhorotsku rayons and in the town of Poti is to be extended for two months from 23 May 1994. The parliament of the Republic of Georgia is requested to confirm the extension of emergency rules in West Georgia. [signed] Eduard Shevardnadze.
