Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] A delegation of the Danish Ministry of Defense today visited the the Russian army unit at Skrunda. Speaking at a press briefing at the Ministry of Defense, Danish Defense Minister Hans Haekkerup acknowledged that Russia undoubtedly has military-strategic interests in Latvia. However, no country has the right to maintain its troops in another state against its will. At Skrunda the Danish delegation met the leading personnel of the radar station and listened to a lecture on the activities and functions of this station. The guests were not denied a chance to examine the station's technical equipment. After that the delegation of the Danish Ministry of Defense visited the Kuldiga Home Guard battalion. President Guntis Ulmanis also met the Danish minister of defense today. Ulmanis noted that this is the first foreign delegation to make close acquaintance with the Skrunda radar station. The president also appreciatively assessed the cooperation agreement signed between the defense forces of Latvia and Denmark, and noted that the three main issues in talks with Russia directly concern the Skrunda radar station. The Danish defense minister offered the assistance of his specialists in ascertaining the harmful effects of the station. He was also appreciative of the creation of a unified peace battalion for the Baltic countries. Haekkerup and Ulmanis also discussed the idea of the common security system for Europe, and exchanged views on the NATO summit to be held in January, which will also decide on the inclusion of east European countries in these structures.
