BFN [Text] In a lengthy report to the UN Security Council dated 30 May that was finally circulated early this morning, UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali makes the Security Council responsible for any decision on the procedure and substance of the effort to resolve the Cyprus issue. Despite the fact that Butrus-Ghali clearly blames the lack of an agreement on [Turkish Cypriot Leader] Rauf Denktas, the secretary general calls on the Security Council to study five choices that he proposes to deal with the situation in Cyprus. Specifically, the secretary general, who considers the situation in Cyprus unsatisfactory, noted as the first alternative the withdrawal of the UN peacekeeping force from the island and its use elsewhere, because during the past 30 years it has been proven that there is no political will for a solution to the Cyprus problem. The second alternative deals with the change of his mandate and the assumption of mandatory measures by the international community to force the intransigent side, as he put it, to be more flexible and cooperative in the negotiations on the confidence-building measures. The third alternative suggests a return to the substance of the Cyprus issue, specifically to the secretary general's Set of Ideas. In his fourth alternative, Butrus-Ghali asks for time to consult with the Security Council members, the guarantor powers, the two leaders in Cyprus, and others so that he will have the flexibility to examine alternatives with significant consequences. Finally, the fifth alternative is based on the fact that the two sides have accepted the package of confidence-building measures in principle and calls on the international community to repeat its efforts to achieve an agreement on the implementation of the measures. Furthermore, Butrus-Ghali proposes various ways to approach the issue, including an international conference, a visit by a Security Council delegation to Cyprus and the region, or new visits by his special representative. However, the UN secretary general notes that the process thus far has convinced him that it is possible to achieve an agreement if there are new changes to the confidence-building measures. At the same time, he reaffirms his support for the measures. In his report, Butrus-Ghali notes that the Security Council is again faced with a familiar scenario, that is, no agreement mainly because of the lack of political will on the Turkish Cypriot side and warns that its way of thinking will have serious consequences for the Turkish Cypriot community's prosperity. The report also says that the UN secretary general categorically denies the Turkish claim that the original document on the confidence-building measures of 1 July has been improved for the benefit of the Greek Cypriots. The secretary general proceeds with an analytical comparison of each aspect of the two documents of 1 July and 21 March. In addition, he submits the two maps of Nicosia Airport and Famagusta. According to the CyBC [Cyprus Broadcasting Corporation] correspondent in New York, the Security Council is expected to begin deliberations by the end of the week so it can issue a resolution.
