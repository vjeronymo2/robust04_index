Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Hong Kong, 26 Jan (ZHONGGUO TONGXUN SHE)--Ku Chen-fu, chairman of Taiwan's Straits Exchange Foundation, responded to the issue of direct flights to fixed destinations on both sides of the strait, a issue which has recently drawn widespread attention in Taiwan; he said that the Taiwan authorities must have a policy on the issue, and must not avoid talking about it. Whereas the Hong Kong issue is also an urgent one, and the government should all the more derive a policy as soon as possible, and inform industrial and commercial circles at an early date so that they can make preparations. Ku Chen-fu pointed out that based on the future economic policy of Taiwan, establishing an Asia-Pacific operation center and a special free trade zone is now a fixed direction; on this premise, the issue of direct flights between both sides of the strait cannot be always avoided, and preliminary planning should be done. He also said that the issue of direct flights between the two sides of the strait is not merely an economic issue, but should be considered together with planning for Hong Kong and an Asia-Pacific operations base. In addition, regarding the Hong Kong issue, Ku Chen-fu said that Hong Kong is currently the cite of entrepot trade between Taiwan and the mainland, and that due to the importance of Hong Kong's position, Taiwan's future policy toward Hong Kong, as well as other relevant measures, are very important matters, and industrial and commercial circles must be allowed to understand them two or three years ahead of promulgation so they can make preparations.
