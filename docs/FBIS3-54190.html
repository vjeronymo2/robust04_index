Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Doha, 23 Jan (WAKH)--Jordanian Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali has said that Jordan has reiterated to U.S. President Bill Clinton its position on the peace process, which is based on the need to achieve comprehensive, just, and durable peace on the basis of the Madrid conference formula, which is grounded in UN Security Council Resolutions 242, 338, and 425. In a statement to the newspaper AL-RAYAH published here today, al-Majali, who is accompanying King Husayn on the latter's current visit to Washington, indicated that there is Jordanian-Syrian coordination on the peace process. He added that Jordan is also coordinating with the other Arab sides on this issue. In reply to a question, Dr. al-Majali expressed full satisfaction with the the economic agreement recently signed between Jordan and the PLO, saying: "This agreement is realistic. It will yield immediate benefits to the Palestinian side."
