Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 19 (KCNA) -- Rev. Mun Ik-hwan, a patriotic champion of reunification who had been active in South Korea for a long time for the reunification of the country and the democratization of the society, died, to our regret, on January 18 at the age of 76. He devotedly struggled in South Korea for independence and democratization of the society and for the reunification of the country. He, who started the activities for democracy in 1974 as a member of the Council of Literary Men for the Practice of Freedom, worked out the "March 1 Declaration for Democracy and National Salvation" against the "Yusin" fascist dictatorship in 1976. And in 1979, he, active as the chairman of the Central Standing Committee of the National Alliance for Democracy and Reunification, continued the struggle on the street and behind bars, opposing the seizure of power by the "fifth republic" military fascist clique and denouncing the barbarities of the United States in the Kwangju popular uprising. He was arrested and imprisoned more than once because he urged the U.S. troops pullout, the obstacle to the country's reunification, and called for the establishment of a democratic government, the conclusion of a peace agreement, reunification through confederacy, etc. He was active as the chairman of the National Congress for Democracy and Unification, the co-chairman of the National Liaison Organization for Democracy, the chairman of the United People's Movement for Democracy and Unification (Mintongnyon) and advisor to the Coalition for National Democratic Movement (Chonminnyon) in the period from 1984 to 1989. He participated in the "World Conference for Peace and Reunification of the Korean Peninsula" in 1988 and called for the replacement of the armistice agreement with a peace agreement and the denuclearization of the peninsula. He supported the proposal advanced by the great leader President Kim Il-song in his New Year address for 1989 for the convocation of a North-South political consultative meeting to discuss the way of reunifying the country through confederacy. And he visited Pyongyang at the risk of his life in late March that year. While in Pyongyang, he was received by the great leader President Kim Il-song, the sun of the nation and the lodestar of national reunification, and renewed his strong faith and will for reunification. As the advisor to Chonminnyon, he held talks with officials of the Committee for the Peaceful Reunification of the Fatherland, agreed to the removal of the political and military confrontation between the North and the South, the replacement of the armistice agreement between the DPRK and the United States with a peace agreement, the adoption of a nonaggression declaration between the North and the South, reunification through confederacy, etc. and published a joint statement. Back to South Korea, he was arrested and jailed by the fascists. But he continued the struggle for independence, democracy and reunification. Working as the chairman of the preparatory committee for the inauguration of the South side headquarters of the National Alliance for the Country's Reunification (Pomminnyon) from 1991, he made strenuous efforts for national unity and peaceful reunification. Though the old man had become weak because of many years' imprisonment, he admirably fought to the last moment of his life for the patriotic cause of reunification. Although Rev. Mun Ik-hwan, who fought for independence and democratization of the South Korean society and for peaceful reunification of the country, passed away, his desire will be surely realised by the South Korean people and students and his patriotic spirit for reunification will always remain in the memory of the Koreans at home and abroad.
