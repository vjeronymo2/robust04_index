February 12, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

NEW BOOKS: NANCY VALE'S KNITWEAR;

BY NANCY VALE (BALLANTINE BOOKS; 1987)

NANCY VALE'S NAME may be unfamiliar to Americans, but her sweaters are sold all over the country under Ralph Lauren, Calvin Klein and other labels. The sweaters are gorgeous, from a thick Navajo-style cardigan to an Early American sampler design on a pullover. Vale, an "English mum" who has become a design tycoon in Britain, offers patterns that are full of complicated cables and color changes, and most of them are knitted on small needles -- a lifetime project for some of us. Still, anyone with modest talent and extravagant patience could really make something out of this book. ($24.95) Judith Sims
