September 23, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

ZIMBABWE;

PLAN TO LEGALIZE 1-PARTY RULE SHELVED

Zimbabwe's ruling party decided to drop its campaign to legalize one-party rule in the African nation.

The move was seen as a political setback for President Robert Mugabe. The central committee of Mugabe's ZANU-PF party argued that the country is a de facto one-party state because of the party's hold on Parliament and there is no need for legal status. Mugabe contends that a multi-party system breeds tribal conflicts, and he had made a one-party law the linchpin of his plans for the nation.
