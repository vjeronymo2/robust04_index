BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Demidetskiy] [Text] Kishinev [Chisinau] 2 June TASS -- Turkish President Suleyman Demirel, currently paying an official three-day visit to Moldavia [Moldova], went today to the southern part of the Republic, mostly inhabited by Gagauzs, a people of Turkic origin. He is accompanied by President Mircea Snegur and other officials. Demirel stated the day before that Turkish businessmen were ready to invest in the Moldavian economy. Most of the investments will be made in the Gagauz districts. At the same time, he expressed his support to the efforts of the Moldavian leadership to ensure the Republic's territorial integrity. "We back the negotiations process between the Gagauz population and the Moldavian central leadership, intended to give our brothers a chance to live on their native land as citizens, who respect the territorial integrity of Moldavia and enjoy a legal status making for their further development," Demirel stated. The Gagauz leaders also pin great hopes on Demirel's visit. "This visit will, without a doubt, contribute to the political settlement of the Gagauz problem. This will allow us to switch over to the solution of our economic problems, to the improvement of the people's living standards," ITAR-TASS was told by Stepan Topal, the president of Gagauzia. According to the Gagauz leader, the Turkish side is ready to help develop telephone services in the Gagauz districts, to build roads there, and to re-equip the local processing enterprises and other factories. Demirel will sign with Moldavian President Mircea Snegur a treaty of friendship and cooperation between the two countries. Several agreements on cooperation in the sphere of culture, transport services, agriculture, and in combatting crime will also be concluded. The heads of the two states are expected to decide the terms for large Turkish investments in the Moldavian economy. On the last day of the visit Demirel will attend the opening of the Turkish Embassy in Moldavia, after which the signing of the official documents will take place.
