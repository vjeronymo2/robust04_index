May 20, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

ON VIEW: AMBASSADOR FROM TURKEY FETED

There wasn't a spot of garden space vacant at the fete honoring the Ambassador of the Republic of Turkey to the United States, Nuzhet Kandemir, and his wife, Sadiye. Turkish Consul General Mehmet Emre and his wife, Sema, were exuding smiles and directing the crowd to the cerkez tavuk (chicken with walnuts), doner (beef on a spit), patlican kizartma (eggplant with green chiles), borrek (fried pastry) and rice pilav.

Prominent were Mission Viejo real estate broker Vasif Canli, Los Angeles developer T.K. Kutay, civil engineer Nezih M. Gunal, Chasen's assistant manager Orhan Arli and Turan and Judy Kahraman.

Why the visit? Explained the ambassador: "To explain the business opportunities that exist in Turkey." J. Jamison Moore, who owns Atlantis Inc., already knows. He and his wife, Mara, have sold their house and will live abroad for the next five years to oversee his $552-million resort development near Izmir. Yachting was on the minds of Bud and Virginia Carpenter. He's in charge of the Los Angeles Yacht Club plans for 75 Southland sailors to sail on seas off the coves of Turkey next year.

BOUQUETS: The Friends of Robinson Gardens garden tour has become one of the can't-miss events of spring. This year the gardens of Irene Kassorla and Norman Friedmann, Stephanie and Louis Snyder, Otis and Dody Booth, Don and Kathleen Spiegelman, plus the Virginia Robinson Gardens, were picked by benefit co-chairs Carol Mancino and Donna Kamin and co-presidents Donna Wolff and Bridget Martens. They also chose famous gardener Georgie Van de Kamp (mother of gubernatorial candidate John Van de Kamp) for honorary chair.

WOW! It helps to have former President Ronald Reagan joining entertainers Jose Feliciano and Maureen McGovern for fund raising. The folks in the background also deserve applause for raising more than $450,000 at the fourth annual "A Night Under the Stars" gala to benefit the Muscular Dystrophy Assn.

At the party Mr. Reagan presented August Busch III, chairman of the board and president of Anheuser-Busch Companies Inc., with the Dennis Day Memorial Award for the $4.7 million he's raised. More in on the fun, Ed McMahon; Jerry Lewis, national MDA chair, and comedian Norm Crosby, given the Humanitarian Award.
