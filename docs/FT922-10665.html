920430 FT 30 APR 92 / Arts: Today's Television Given television's supposed power, it is astonishing how little it is used to publicise wrongs and, with luck, help to right them. Some series do have a tradition of investigation ('World In Action', for instance) but that is most unusual. All the more reason, then, to celebrate the role of Taking Liberties which highlights miscarriages of social justice. Today's programme (8.00 BBC2) takes up the case of the Moroccans who were persuaded to work in Gibraltar when Spain closed the border in 1969 but, now that the political situation has changed, are facing deportation. Equally rare are programmes in which a single powerful opinion can be expressed without an opposing view being used for 'balance'. Opinions on C4 (8.00) provides half an hour for the expression of a single belief. Today Bob Tyrell, managing director of the Henley Centre, predicts the weakening of group attitudes as the 'me generation' of the Sixties ages. This Week investigates terrorist killing in Northern Ireland but, for once, looks not at the IRA but at the Protestant UDA which, since vowing a year ago to avenge every killing by the IRA, has done just that (8.30 ITV).
