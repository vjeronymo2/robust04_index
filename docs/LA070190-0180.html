July 1, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

PROFIT KEY WINS DWYER STAKES

Profit Key took the lead at the start and maintained his advantage throughout Saturday to win the 73rd running of the $171,600 Grade II Dwyer Stakes for 3-year-olds at Belmont Park.

Rhythm, ridden by Craig Perret, raced wide through the stretch to finish second in the four-horse field, 1 3/4 lengths ahead of Graf.

The winner, with Jose Santos aboard, carried the co-highweight of 123.

For Profit Key, a Kentucky bred son of Desert Wine-Sharm a Sheikh, the win was the second in a row and the fifth in seven starts this year. He covered 1 1/8 miles in 1:47 2/5 to earn $102,960 and return $3.20 and $2.10.

Rhythm, who had won two in a row, including Belmont's Colin Stakes in his last start, paid $2.10. There was no show betting.
