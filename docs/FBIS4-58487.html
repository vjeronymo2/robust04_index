BFN [Text] Cairo (AA) -- Ali Simsek report -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin has said that the Israeli-Palestinian agreement signed in Cairo, the capital of Egypt, today is important for the entire world. Cetin, who is in Cairo to attend the signing ceremony of the agreement, assessing the agreement said that it will affect the region in many ways. Stressing that the path taken has no return and that it will lead to peace and stability, Cetin said: This region which allocates all its resources to armament, clashes and war will now, to a great extent, allocate them to the development of the region and the peoples within the framework of cooperation. Cetin noted that Turkey, which is a democratic and secular country in the region, can make significant contributions to the Middle East which is being restructured. Pointing out that Turkey maintains good relations with all the regional countries, Cetin expressed satisfaction over the fact that the 45-year old war has ended in peace. He described this day as a `historic' one. In an appeal to all the peoples of strife torn regions, Cetin said: No matter how much you fight, every war ends in peace. Let us secure peace before too much blood is spilled. Pointing out that the agreement signed is just the beginning, Cetin concluded by saying that Syria, Jordan and Lebanon should join the peace process.
