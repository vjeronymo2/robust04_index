BFN [From the "Danuza" column by Maria Clara Jorge and Mariza Louven] [Text] Federal Deputy Sidney de Miguel (Green Party-Rio de Janeiro) is investigating a charge raised by a biology professor in Angra dos Reis, who claims to have detected signs of contamination in children residing in the Angra dos Reis municipal district and in employees of the Angra-1 nuclear plant. The biology professor has noted the emergence of cysts; ovarian, breast, and uterine cancer; and even reduction in chromosomes in some children. Deputy de Miguel has asked the attorney general to shut down the nuclear plant until the true cause of these health disorders is determined.
