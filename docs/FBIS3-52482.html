Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran (DPA)--The death sentence against the German engineer Helmut Szimkus for spying for Iraq has been confirmed by the highest court in Iran. This was announced by the Iranian State Prosecutor General Musavi-Tabrizi to journalists in Tehran today. Szimkus can only be pardoned by the revolutionary leader Ayatollah 'Ali Khamene'i. Szimkus was arrested in Iran in 1989 and was sentenced to death for spying for Baghdad in 1992. The German has allegedly confessed to having spied for Iraq. Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel has appealed to Iran on the condemned man's behalf.
