BFN [Text] Pristina, 5 May (TANJUG) -- Douglas Hogg, minister of state in the UK Foreign Office, said in Pristina today that both the British Government and the international community are of the view that the issue of the rights of Albanians of Kosovo and Metohija will have to be resolved within the borders of Serbia. I wish to say that Kosovo is part of Serbia and we do not believe that the borders should be changed. We also think that there is no need for an independent state on the territory of Kosovo, Douglas Hogg told journalists, following his talks with Milos Savovic, governor [nacelnik] of the Kosovo district. The main tasks are to find ways of implementing the political, cultural, and civil rights of the Albanian community in Kosovo and to allow the Albanians to play a full role in political and administrative life, Hogg said. The British minister of state continued his visit by talking to ethnic Albanian representatives in Pristina.
