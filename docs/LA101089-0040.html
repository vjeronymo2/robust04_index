October 10, 1989, Tuesday, Southland Edition

THE NATION

The Washington State Labor Council threw its financial and moral support behind the machinists union whose 57,800 members are in the sixth day of their strike against the Boeing Co. Larry Kenney, president of the council, said the coalition of unions will raise money for a "hardship fund" to help the striking workers. Kenney and representatives of other unions belonging to the council met for several hours with leaders of the International Assn. of Machinists and Aerospace Workers.
