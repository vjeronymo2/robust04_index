Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] During his official visit to the United States, Eduard Shevardnadze, the Georgian head of state, who combines the defense minister's duties, had a meeting with the U.S. Defense Secretary William Perry in the Pentagon. Issues concerning the development of a close U.S.-Georgian cooperation in the defense and security sphere in Caucasia were discussed at the meeting. Mr. Shevardnadze and Mr. Perry spoke of the first steps in the cooperation, namely, the sending of the first U.S. military attache to Georgia, a draft international program of instructing and training Georgian military specialists, an interest taken by Georgia in a program "For well-being and partnership" suggested by NATO, and Georgia's intention to participate in the implementation of the program along with Russia and Ukraine. The sides reached an agreement on having a Georgian military attache sent to Washington. According to Secretary Perry, the United States aspires to continue a program of cooperation in defense that would contribute to restructuring the Georgian military complex in the conditions of democracy and civil control [as received]. Mr. Perry said that he intends to send a U.S. Department of Defense delegation, which would include senior officials of the department, to Georgia in May. The delegation will discuss issues of security, assess current military reforms in Georgia, and finally submit recommendations for further steps in strengthening defense cooperation between Georgia and the United States to the U.S. Defense Department head.
