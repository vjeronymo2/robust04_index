921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / More mortgage lenders fall into line over cuts in rates SEVERAL banks and building societies yesterday announced reductions in their standard variable mortgage interest rate in response to last week's 1 percentage point cut in base rates to 7 per cent. Lloyds Bank said it would reduce its rate to 8.6 per cent from 9.3 per cent for all borrowers from December 21. National Westminster cut rates from 9.25 per cent to 8.55 per cent for new borrowers and from January 1 for existing customers. Leeds Permanent Building Society is reducing its rates to 8.55 per cent from 9.29 per cent from today for new customers and from December 21 for existing borrowers. Cheltenham & Gloucester is cutting its mortgage rate to 8.5 per cent immediately for new borrowers and from December 31 for its existing customers. Rates at First Direct bank are reduced to 8.49 per cent from 9.25 per cent for new borrowers and from December 21 for existing borrowers on loans which are less than Pounds 50,000. Chelsea building society cut rates to 8.5 per cent from 9.25 per cent for new borrowers.
