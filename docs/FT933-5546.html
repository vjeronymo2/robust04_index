930902 FT 02 SEP 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: Fuel shortage may halt Kazakh harvest KAZAKHSTAN MAY have to halt this year's harvest campaign because of severe fuel shortages, the Itar-Tass news agency quoted the deputy head of Kaznefteprodukt state oil company as saying, reports Reuter from Moscow. Mr Igor Medvedev, an official at the Russian energy ministry confirmed that big Russian oil companies had refused to send oil to Kazakhstan because of debts of about Rbs87bn (Pounds 60m). Kazakhstan expects a grain harvest of about 30m tonnes this year, down from last year's record 32m. The republic had harvested 5.76m tonnes of grain by August 27, up from 5.16m a year earlier.
