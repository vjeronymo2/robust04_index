BFN [Text] Deputy Prime Minister 'Abd-al-Wahhab al-Anisi today received Arthur Hayden Hughes, the U.S. ambassador to our country. During the meeting, they reviewed the friendly bilateral ties and the means of promoting them. They also discussed the efforts currently under way to establish a cease-fire. The ambassador reiterated the U.S. position that what is taking place in the Republic of Yemen must be handled in accordance with the provisions enshrined in UN Security Council Resolution 924, within the framework of the Republic of Yemen, and with the inalienable rights guaranteed to all members of the world body by the UN Charter, chief among which are the member states' rights to sovereignty, independence, and territorial integrity, as well as the inadmissibility of interference in their domestic affairs. He added that any handling of the situation in the Republic of Yemen outside this framework would prolong the conflict and hamper the urgent solutions required to achieve security and stability in the Republic of Yemen and the region at large. For his part, Deputy Prime Minister 'Abd-al-Wahhab al-Anisi reiterated the Republic of Yemen's appreciation of U.S. efforts, noting their importance. He stressed the need for communication between the two countries to remove any misunderstanding in interpreting positions. He also underlined the need to keep away from anything that could hamper the joint efforts of the two countries in the interest of bilateral ties.
