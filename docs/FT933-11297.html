930730 FT 30 JUL 93 / MPs rap DTI over Girobank sell-off THE COMMONS public accounts committee has criticised the Department of Trade and Industry and the Post Office over the 1990 sale of state-owned Girobank for Pounds 63m less than the book value of its assets. The Post Office is also castigated for not seeking further bidders for Giroleasing, Girobank's corporate leasing and hire-purchasing business, which was sold to Norwich Union. A Pounds 378m offer by Norwich Union was found to be overstated by Pounds 55m 'through a genuine mistake' the day before the sale. In a report published yesterday the committee suggests that the Co-operative Wholesale Society might have been prepared to pay more for Girobank than the Pounds 130m obtained from the Alliance and Leicester Building Society. The committee said it had not been established that a Pounds 124m bid by the CWS was a final offer. The MPs say it would have been advantageous for the taxpayer if, when the field was reduced to two bidders, both had been given preferential status, not just Alliance and Leicester. The committee says the overstatement by Pounds 55m of the value of the Pounds 378m bid for Giroleasing arose from errors in detailed tax calculations. 'The corrected Norwich Union offer had a value of Pounds 323m, which meant that another company, whose offer was for Pounds 342m, was now offering more'. It says that the Post Office gave Norwich Union less than a week to revise its bid and agreed not to solicit other bids. It concludes: 'By committing itself not to solicit other bids . . . the Post Office denied itself the opportunity to explore what other bidders might have been prepared to offer.'
