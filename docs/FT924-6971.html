921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / International Company News: PWA files CDollars 1bn suit against Air Canada PWA Corporation, the holding company of Canadian Airlines International, has filed a CDollars 1bn (USDollars 784m) lawsuit against Air Canada for alleged predatory pricing practices, Reuter reports. Mr Rhys Eyton, PWA chairman, and Mr Kevin Jenkins, president of Canadian Airlines, claimed that Air Canada had 'dumped' extra airline seats on the market this year in an effort to use its deeper financial resources to drive Canadian out of the market. 'PWA Corp and Canadian Airlines have been aware of Air Canada's actions for some time, but have now gathered key evidence to support this filing of charges,' PWA said in a statement. PWA said the suit was filed yesterday in an Alberta court. Air Canada officials have previously denied such allegations, but yesterday declined to comment on the suit. Mr Eyton said the PWA charge was not related to the recent lawsuit by the Gemini Group systems venture against PWA. Gemini, which is jointly owned by PWA, Air Canada and a United Airlines affiliate, recently sued PWA for CDollars 1.5bn. Mr Eyton added PWA had few options if its proposed deal with AMR and employees of its Canadian Airlines unit fell through. He appeared to rule out renewed merger talks with Air Canada. 'We've wasted probably six months discussing merger possibilities with Air Canada during the past year,' he said. 'We will not waste time and be taken down futile paths.'
