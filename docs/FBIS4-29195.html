BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 16 KYODO -- Japan's trade surplus appears to be shrinking, but the still-rising gap with the United States is worrisome, Vice Finance Minister Jiro Saito said Thursday. The merchandise trade figures for May released earlier in the day underscored "the rising imports and falling exports in terms of volume," Saito told a regular news meeting. "But I want to see if that becomes a fixed trend, and I'm worried about the unchanged (upward) trend with the U.S.," Saito said. The May preliminary figures showed that Japan's surplus fell 15.9 percent from a year before to score the first fall in six months, but the gap with the U.S. continued to rise for the third month in a row, gaining 7.5 percent.
