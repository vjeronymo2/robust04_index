BFN [Text] Paris, June 17 (XINHUA) -- China today called for regular high-level meetings supported by the developed nations to coordinate efforts against AIDS in the developing world and other hard-hit regions. The call was made by Chinese Health Minister Chen Minzhang at a ministerial level international conference in Paris, which is being held ahead of a Paris summit proposed by France for December 1 on the fight against the killer disease. China supports international and trans-regional cooperation in the fight against AIDS, said Chen, stressing that more attention and efforts should be given to anti-AIDS plans in developing countries. It urges neighboring states to coordinate their efforts to fight AIDS by setting up information networks and developing regional cooperation projects, he added. Chen also briefed the conference on the Chinese Government's anti-AIDS work and pledged to make contribution to the global strategy against AIDS. The two-day conference, which started today, is being attended by ministers from 50 nations and representatives of some world health agencies.
