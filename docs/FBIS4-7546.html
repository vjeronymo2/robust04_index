BFN [Excerpts] Sofia, March 31 (BTA) -- Between March 25 and March 31 Prime Minister Lyuben Berov had working meetings with Ahmed Dogan, leader of the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms and leader of its parliamentary group; Nora Ananieva, floor leader of the Parliamentary Alliance for Social Democracy; Zhan Videnov, leader of the Bulgarian Socialist Party; and Stefan Bozhilov and Georgi Kostov, co-chairmen of the parliamentary New Union for Democracy, BTA learned from the Government Press Office. [passage omitted] The political leaders discussed some shortcomings in the work of certain governmental structures which decelerate the realization of the government's programme. Professor Berov suggested some structural changes in the cabinet which he moved for discussion to the parliamentary groups. Prime Minister Lyuben Berov stated his views on the ways for overcoming the crisis in the foreign exchange market. He stressed the decisiveness of the Government to take to a successful completion the negotiations on the country's foreign debts, push the privatization process and accelerate the structural reform in industry and agriculture. According to the attending team of physicians, Mr. Berov's health is stable. His recuperation proceeds in keeping with the outlined programme. He is allowed to make the necessary phone calls, hold meetings and work with documents.
