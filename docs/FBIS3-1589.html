Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 24 (XINHUA) -- China's first computer-equipped national information network center on books, newspapers and magazines has been set up in Beijing. The network of 50 advanced computers can provide instant indexing services for its 260,000 customers nationwide. The data input center of the network is located in Ningbo in east China and employs 500 operators who can input 7.5 million Chinese characters each day. Summaries and titles of books, newspapers and magazines newly-published in the country can be immediately input into the computer system. In order to promote its business, the center will present 4,000 computers to its major customers in education and service sectors. The center was jointly set up by companies in Beijing and Ningbo in Zhejiang Province.
