930108 FT 08 JAN 93 / Setback for Somali warlord A US Marine rests after he and colleagues yesterday dealt a military blow to General Mohamed Farrar Aideed, Somalia's strongest warlord. In a dawn attack in Mogadishu, they seized two compounds housing gunmen loyal to Gen Aideed. They also captured an arsenal of tanks and heavy artillery after a 20-minute exchange of fire. A military spokesman said Marines hit the compounds 'with a fire storm' after warnings to surrender went unheeded Attack clips the wings of warlord, Page 3
