931006 FT 06 OCT 93 / Restaurant bills 'close to deceit' RESTAURANTS are coming close to deceiving customers by adding unexpected charges to final bills, says the 1994 Good Food Guide, published today by the Consumers' Association. Mr Tom Jaine, the guide's editor, says in an introduction that some of the most popular restaurants, particularly in London, 'are guilty of some shameful tricks, all designed to push up profits without the customer being too aware of it'. Among the practices highlighted by the guide is a restaurant in Soho, London, which has a Pounds 1.50 cover charge for olives or bread which are placed on tables without customers asking for them. The guide also mentions an unnamed establishment which charges Pounds 3 for a bottle of mineral water and imposes a cover charge for bread and butter for which it provides no plates. Mr Jaine says restaurant customers falsely assume that the price of the main dish provides an indication of what they will end up paying. He adds: 'While the recession may have forced proprietors to reduce their headline prices to persuade customers through their doors, some of them load the other costs to the point of virtual deceit. No wonder the public sometimes feels restaurants are combat zones.'
