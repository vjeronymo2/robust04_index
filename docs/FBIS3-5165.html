Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vientiane, March 4 (KPL) -- Lao PDR [People's Democratic Republic] President Nouhak Phoumsavan and Mongolian President Punsalmaagiyn Ochirbat yesterday signed here a friendship treaty between Laos and Mongolia. The treaty provides that the parties will develop their relations on the basis of principles generally recognised by the international law: respect for each other's independence and sovereignty, non-interference in each other's internal affairs, equality and mutual benefit, respect for the fundamental human rights and freedom and peaceful solutions to conflicts. The parties will promote their cooperation in political and economic fields including trade, technical science, public health, culture, education, press, humanitarian affairs and others to their mutual interest. [Words indistinct] also provided in the treaty to replace the friendship and cooperation treaty signed in 1979 between the two countries when this treaty has become effective, which means after it has been ratified and after the date an exchange of letter for the ratification of this treaty has been made. On the same day, an agreement concerning investment promotion and protection between the two countries was also signed between Minister Phao Bounnaphon, head of the Prime Minister's Office, and the trade and industry minister of Mongolia. The agreement has the objectives to promote and create conditions favorable for investment by each party in one another's country in conformity with the law of their countries. Each party will provide facilities, fair treatment, and protection as well as giving priorities to each other in investment sectors and in remitting profit, dividend to their respective countries. The signing of this agreement was witnessed by the presidents of the two countries, and senior officials from both sides. The friendship treaty and the investment promotion and protection agreement between Laos and Mongolia were signed during a three-day visit to Laos by Mongolian President Punsalmaagiyn Ochirbat and his delegation. The Mongolian president with his wife and delegation left here yesterday for Hanoi for a visit to Vietnam.
