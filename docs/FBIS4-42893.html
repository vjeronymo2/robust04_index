BFN [Excerpt] Istanbul, June 8 (A.A.) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin on Wednesday [8 June] said that Turkey supports the expansion with time, of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization adding however that NATO-Russia relations should not overshadow its ties with other members. He was speaking to reporters prior to a NATO ministers council meeting and the North Atlantic Cooperation Council meetings which will start in Istanbul and will end on Friday. Reminding that Russia accepted the Partnership for Peace plan during the meeting last January, he added that the most important factor there is that former Warsaw Pact members and Russia will sign a single agreement. A joint document is expected to be signed at the end of the NATO meetings and paragraphs directly concerning Russia are expected to take place in the meeting. [sentence as received] [passage omitted]
