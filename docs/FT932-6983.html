930526 FT 26 MAY 93 / Observer: Changing places What are we to make of the last minute change of seating plan on this week's edition of BBC Question Time? Margaret Beckett, the deputy leader of the Labour party, has promised to turn up along with Baroness Shirley Williams, co-founder of the SDP, and GEC director Sara Morrison. But Chancellor of the Exchequer Norman Lamont has suddenly found he's unable to attend and been replaced by Home Secretary Kenneth Clarke. Shape of things to come, perhaps?
