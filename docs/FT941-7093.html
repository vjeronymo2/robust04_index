940226 FT 26 FEB 94 / Fly-in estate plans rejected A planning application for what would have been the UK's first fly-in community was rejected yesterday by Mr John Gummer, the environment secretary. The scheme, called Skypark and based on an airstrip north of Telford, Shropshire, provided for 65 expensive homes, each with a plot of 1.5 acres and its own light-aircraft hangar, air traffic control buildings and a country club. The airstrip would also have been used for business aviation. The Department of the Environment cited loss of agricultural land, intrusion into open countryside and unacceptable noise levels as reasons for rejecting the plan. Its decision followed a public inquiry last year and overturned planning permission given by Wrekin District Council in 1992. The council believed the plan would create jobs. Mr Phil Davis, chairman of the council's planning committee, said: 'The decision is inconsistent with the accepted role of Telford as a national and regional growth point.'
