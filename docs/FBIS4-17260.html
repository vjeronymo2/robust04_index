BFN [Text] Vilnius, Apr 21, BNS -- Lithuanian Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys, currently on a Latin American trip, met with his Brazilian counterpart Celso Amorim in Brasilia Apr. 19. The two ministers exchanged information about the internal situation in their countries, discussed regional problems and possibilities to expand bilateral cooperation, the Lithuanian Foreign Ministry said. Gylys presented draft agreements on investment protection, and visa-free travel. Amorim proposed to draft bilateral agreements on cultural, technical and scientific cooperation. In Brasilia, Gylys also met with the leadership of the Brazilian parliament, the chairman of the Supreme Federal Court and the minister of industry, trade and tourism, Elec Alvares [name as received]. Gylys invited the Brazilian minister to visit Lithuania during his upcoming European trip. The ministers agreed to exchange visits of trade delegations. The same day Gylys paid a visit to the Lithuanian community in Sao Paulo. On Wednesday [20 April], the Lithuanian foreign minister was scheduled to visit Rio de Janeiro.
