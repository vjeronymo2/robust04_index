BFN [Text] A sentence has been handed down in the trial involving the terrorist attack on the Maison de France [in August 1983] in Berlin. Former Stasi officer Vogt was sentenced to four years in prison, but was released for the time being [until the sentence becomes nonappealable]. The Berlin Court considered it proven that the 51-year-old man had passed the explosives for the attack on to terrorist Weinrich. With this, the Stasi had provided active support for the terrorist attack.
