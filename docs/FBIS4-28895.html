BFN [Text] Phnom Penh 11 Jun (AKP) -- Prince Norodom Sirivut, deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs and international cooperation of the Kingdom of Cambodia, returned to Phnom Penh on 9 June after a month-long tour, from May 7th onward, of France and EC countries. On his return to Phnom Penh he said that "foreign military aid to the government army to fight Khmer Rouge rebels will bring stability but will not affect the peace process in the country." In the meantime, Prince Norodom Sirivut attended the 11th conference of nonaligned countries, held from 31 May to 3 June, in Cairo (Egypt). During his stay in France he specified that he had asked for French aid for the Cambodian Army. "When we asked for military assistance, it was not that we wanted to aggravate the situation; we want stability," added Prince Norodom Sirivut. Australia, France, and the United States are discussing the request to train and equip some 160,000 government combatants. The Khmer Rouge have vigorously condemned all foreign aid. Prince Norodom Sirivut reaffirmed that the royal government does not however want to continue the war against the rebels. According to him, the government and Cambodia's king, His Majesty Norodom Sihanouk, are trying to bring the guerrillas into the national community. Cambodia's chief diplomat also pointed out that he has received a 10-point proposal from the Thai Army aimed at settling border problems between the two countries. "I am very happy to hear about this proposal from the Thai Army; it is a good proposal; I appreciate it," said the prince without giving further details.
