Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] According to a report of Christian Broadcasting Network in Seoul, U.S. Deputy Assistant Secretary of State Hubbard, in a speech made at the American University on 25 February, called for the abolition of the National Security Law in South Korea. Meanwhile, in a public session on 1 March, a U.S. Department of State spokesman confirmed that the U.S. deputy assistant secretary of state's remarks calling for the abolition of the National Security Law were made by a responsible authority and reveal [palkida] the U.S. official position. Faced with the U.S. side's position urging the abolition of the National Security Law, the South Korean authorities and the Democratic Liberal Party went around busily reacting with remarks about interference in domestic affairs and so forth. On the other hand, a spokesman of the opposition Democratic Party, said that the U.S. side's expression of its official position calling for the abolition of the National Security Law is, in terms of universal principles, rightful [tangyonhan], and strongly urged the authorities to abolish the National Security Law.
