Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, Jan 11 (AFP) -- A French government emissary secretly traveled to Beijing in late December to discuss the possibility of a visit by Prime Minister Edouard Balladur on the 30th anniversary of the establishment of Franco-Chinese diplomatic ties, a reliable sources said here Tuesday. Jacques Friedmann, a close associate of Balladur, reportedly discussed the plan with representatives of the Chinese government. France recognized China in January 27, 1964. According to well- informed sources in Beijing, the head of the French government would visit China with Foreign Affairs Minister Alain Juppe and Industry Minister Gerard Longuet from January 27 to 30. Friedmann initially visited Beijing in July to revive the dialogue between the two countries, which had halted with the sale one year ago of French Mirage 2000-5 aircraft to Taiwan, the Nationalist island claimed by China. The high-level visit would open a new chapter in Sino-French relations. The sale of warplanes to Taiwan had led to the closure of the French consulate in the southern Chinese city of Guangzhou and the exclusion of French companies from major economic projects.
