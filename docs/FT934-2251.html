931215 FT 15 DEC 93 / Labour attacks NHS 'waste' Mr John Smith, the leader of the Opposition, accused a leading National Health Service executive of 'grotesque mismanagement of public funds' as Labour yesterday stepped up its campaign against bureaucracy in the NHS, James Blitz writes. At prime minister's question time Mr Smith claimed recent reforms of the NHS had allowed health organisations to spend more money on bureaucracy and public relations. He referred to Sir Robin Buchanan, chairman of the NHS Supplies Authority and a former Tory councillor, whose organisation was recently criticised for spending decisions. Mr Smith said that the organisation had 'wasted hundreds of thousands of pounds changing a single word in their name'. Mr John Major said spending on NHS administration was 'very tightly examined and controlled' and more patients were being treated than ever before. The prime minister added that NHS administration amounted to only 2 per cent of total costs.
