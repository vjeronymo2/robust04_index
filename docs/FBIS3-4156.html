Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington, March 25 (XINHUA) -- The United States military forces completed their withdrawal from Somalia today, Defense Secretary William Perry announced here. "The last C-5 aircraft left Mogadishu at 9:13 A.M. local time en route to the U.S.," Perry said at a Pentagon press briefing. General Thomas Montgomery, the commander of the American military forces in Somalia, then departed by helicopter and he will be back at Andrews Air Force Base on Monday, Perry said. A welcome home ceremony will be held for Thomas and about 25 of his staff at Andrews Air Force Base on Monday. "The return of General Montgomery marks the completion of the withdrawal and the exit of U.S. forces peacekeeping duties in Somalia," a Pentagon spokesman said. About 50 American Marines were left in Somalia to guard the 12 diplomats at the U.S. Liaison Office, and about 13 American military men remain at the U.N. forces' headquarters.
