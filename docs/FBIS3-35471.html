Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Telephone interview with Dr. Nabil Sha'th, head of the Palestinian delegation to Cairo talks, by Sa'id Mu'addi; place and date note given; from the "With the Midday Events" program--recorded] [Text] [Mu'addi] Dr. Nabil, good evening. [Sha'th] Good evening. You are welcome. [Mu'addi] Dr. Nabil, hopes are rife that the negotiations will end before the blessed 'Id al-Fitr. Is what remains only time to draft the clauses of the agreement or are there other substantive issues? [Sha'th] No substantive issues remain to hamper this agreement. Naturally, one always tries to confine his optimism to the framework of what is possible and realistic. Therefore, I cannot be absolutely certain, but can say we may conclude all these matters before 'Id al-Fitr, God willing. [Mu'addi] Dr. Nabil, regarding the Israel Defense Forces' withdrawal from the Gaza Strip and Jericho, has agreement been reached on dates? [Sha'th] Agreement has been reached on the withdrawal plan and dates, but the first day of withdrawal depends on the day of signing. Consequently, it is an integrated timetable which lacks only the date of signing which will be the starting day of withdrawal. [Mu'addi] What was agreed regarding the Palestinian police force? When will the vanguards of this force arrive in the Gaza Strip? [Sha'th] Several officers will go there as of now to make the necessary preparations and contact the Israeli military commanders in Gaza in order to define the Palestinian police positions and what is required before the rest of the police forces arrive. Once the agreement is signed, the Palestinian police forces will begin to leave for the Strip and Jericho. They will do so before the Israeli withdrawal starts so that they will accurately learn all positions and needs on the ground. These forces will arrive in stages and gradually take up their positions prior to withdrawal. The withdrawal will then immediately commence from the positions, one at a time. [Mu'addi] Dr. Nabil, you realize that the issue of releasing prisoners is the main preoccupation of the people on the street. What stage have you reached in this regard? [Sha'th] This subject preoccupies us as well. I think there is a favorable Israeli response in this regard and a feeling of its importance for Palestinians and the coming peace and the start of a new stage of relations. Therefore, we are conducting these negotiations, in addition to all other activities, day by day. I think we got closer in some points and others need to be finalized. What is important is to agree to a timetable for the release of all prisoners, even if this timetable is spread over a period of time that includes withdrawal and completion of the agreement on the West Bank, too. We want all prisoners to eventually return home in peace. We want to see an end to this period so a new era of peace can begin. [Mu'addi] Dr. Nabil, one last question. When do you expect a summit meeting between Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin and PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat? [Sha'th] This, of course, depends on the day of signing. They might meet one or two days before the signing to discuss those issues left for them to finish. Therefore, if the signing is ready before 'Id al-Fitr, then the two leaders will meet before 'Id al-Fitr. [Mu'addi] Dr. Nabil Sha'th, head of the Palestinian delegation to Cairo talks, thank you very much. [Sha'th] Thank you and welcome.
