BFN [Text] Belgrade, 11 Apr (TANJUG) -- At its session today the federal government discussed the situation in the wake of the NATO air strike against the territory of the Srpska republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina in the vicinity of Gorazde. It decided to send a delegation of the Yugoslav Government, headed by Vice Prime Minister Zeljko Simic, to the Srpska republic where they will acquaint themselves with the situation and consequences on the spot. The government, as is reported, also decided to send a protest note to Italy, because it provided its air bases for the aircraft that attacked the Serb territories in ex-Bosnia-Herzegovina. At the same time it decided that the federal government would send a letter to UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali in which it will point out the facts that indicate that the NATO air strike against the territory of the Srpska republic was a violation of the UN Charter.
