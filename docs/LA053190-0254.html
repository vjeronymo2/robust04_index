May 31, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

NORDIQUES COACH BERGERON FIRED

Michel Bergeron was fired today as coach of the Quebec Nordiques, the NHL's worst team last season.

No replacement was named, but Dave Chambers, a former assistant coach with the Minnesota North Stars, is considered the leading candidate.

General Manager Pierre Page said Bergeron was offered a job as a broadcaster for Nordiques games, and that the fours years left on his contract would be honored -- at approximately $250,000 a year.

"We're not blaming Michel Bergeron," said Page, adding that he was uneasy making the decision since he has been general manager only a month. "It's just a question of the right time, the right place and the right people."

The team finished last in the overall standings last season with a 12-61-7 record.
