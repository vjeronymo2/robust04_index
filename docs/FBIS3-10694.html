Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Good Morning From Mexico" newscast] [Text] It is quite probable that the peace dialogue may be resumed next week in Chiapas. The situation in the upper part of this state is apparently calm. Mario Meneses has a report. [Begin Meneses recording] Everything is calm before the holding of the second stage of the peace dialogue. The Chiapas residents do not want any more temporary governments and have also requested elections this year. In Oaxaca, Bishop Samuel Ruiz said a peace agreement will be signed in Chiapas between Monday and Tuesday of the coming week. Also, there are so-called civicos that are being given out in San Cristobal to keep persons from going to church, because there is no talk of the world of God, only politics. Some groups have threatened to burn the San Cristobal Bishopric unless Samuel Ruiz leaves. [end recording]
