Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Mineyev] [Text] Brussels March 23 TASS -- Georgia became the 13th state which joined the "Partnership for Peace" programme proposed by NATO leaders to East-European countries on January. Georgian Foreign Minister Aleksandre Chikvaidze signed the agreement in the NATO headquarters in Brussels on Wednesday. Besides Georgia, Romania, Lithuania, Poland, Estonia, Hungary, Ukraine, Slovakia, Bulgaria, Latvia, the Czech Republic, Moldova have already joined the "Partnership for Peace" programme. Russia is also ready to join the programme, maybe before the end of March, but the concrete date was not announced.
