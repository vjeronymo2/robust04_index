921015 FT 15 OCT 92 / International Company News: Digital hit by weak economies DIGITAL Equipment, the struggling US computer manufacturer, reported heavier-than-expected losses for its first fiscal quarter, citing weakness in leading economies. The loss for the quarter was Dollars 260.5m, or Dollars 2.04 per share, compared with a net loss, after charges, of Dollars 474m, or Dollars 3.80, in the first quarter last year. Revenues were Dollars 3.31bn, up 1 per cent on last year. 'Weakness in the world's major economies continues to present difficult challenges for our business and the information technology industry in general,' said Mr John Smith, senior vice-president of operations. 'We continue to eliminate costs in accordance with our recovery plan,' said Mr William M. Steul, vice-president, finance. Research and development spending declined by about 1.6 per cent on a year-to-year basis, and the workforce was cut by 5,300 in the first quarter, he added. 'We will continue our aggressive restructuring and cost reduction efforts.' Mr Robert Palmer, who succeeded Digital's founder, Mr Kenneth Olsen, as president and chief executive last month, vowed to improve Digital's financial performance. 'Digital will change,' he said. 'We are intent on ensuring that the customer is the focus of everything that we do. Moreover, our shareholders and customers want and expect us to be financially strong. Every activity that we undertake is guided by the goals of customer focus and a return to profitable growth. Digital said revenues from its systems integration services, workstations, personal computers, integration software and network applications support products grew during the quarter.
