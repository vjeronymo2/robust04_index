BFN ["Self-Defensive Right Cannot Be Encroached Upon" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, June 13 (KCNA) -- A spokesman for the Chinese Foreign Ministry declared in connection with an underground nuclear testing on June 10 that China always stands for complete prohibition and thorough destruction of nuclear weapons and, within this context, the realization of a comprehensive nuclear test ban, and said China fully respects and understands the position and concern of many non-nuclear weapon states on the question of nuclear testing. Commenting on this, NODONG SINMUN today says it is the United States which tops the world's list in nuclear testing and stockpile of nuclear weapons and it is again the U.S. which has deployed them in all parts of the world and interferes in others' internal affairs, threatening them with those weapons. The analyst goes on: The United States has carried out over 1,000 times of nuclear tests for the development and modernisation of nuclear weapons since the 1940s. It decided to allocate 11.7 billion dollars to the production of nuclear warheads of new types in fiscal 1995. It wantonly interferes in the internal affairs of other countries on the pretext of "non-proliferation of nuclear weapons" and recently worked out even a "nuclear non-proliferation strategy". The "strategy" is intended to counter "proliferation of nuclear weapons" even by using such kinds of weapons. Such being the situation, nuclear states which have relatively few nuclear weapons, to say nothing of non-nuclear states, cannot but stand against the nuclear threat of the United States and seek a self-defensive measure. The underground nuclear testing of China this time can be considered in this context. A self-defensive measure stands out as a more urgent matter for Korea. The U.S. hardline conservative forces are increasing military threat as never before, pursuing "sanctions" against the DPRK. International reactionaries and some countries are doing base acts, dancing to the tune of the U.S. this proves that the United States and its followers are trying to isolate and stifle Korean socialism at any cost over the nuclear issue. We have done and are doing everything we can to solve the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. But the moves for stifling us are assuming growing intensity. Under this condition, we cannot but take a tough action as has been decided. We have the right to take a self-defensive measure. The United States and its followers must know that this is not an empty word.
