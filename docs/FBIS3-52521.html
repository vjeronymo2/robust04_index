Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Shaykh Ahmad al-Hamud al-Jabir al-Sabah, the interior minister, described the talks he held this evening in Tunis with Tunisian Foreign Affairs Minister Habib Ben Yahya, which dealt with Tunisia's stance toward the Iraqi invasion of the State of Kuwait and the respective UN Security Council resolutions, as serious and excellent. The interior minister said, in a statement to KUNA, that Ben Yahya expressed his country's support for UN resolutions in connection with the unjust Iraqi aggression against the State of Kuwait, in particular, the two resolutions on the demarcation of the border and the resolution calling for the release of Kuwaiti prisoners and detainees. Shaykh Ahmad al-Hamud al-Jabir al-Sabah added that his talks with the Tunisian foreign minister was a good opportunity to inquire about Tunisia's position on the Iraqi invasion and the resolutions of international legitimacy. He said that Ben Yahya told him that Tunisia would welcome Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad al-Jabir, the first deputy prime minister and foreign affairs minister, who is due to visit Tunisia soon.
