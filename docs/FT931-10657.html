930206 FT 06 FEB 93 / Tecs accuse government of trying to impose contracts TRAINING and Enterprise Councils yesterday accused the government of trying to impose the terms of new contracts which demand shorter periods of training for the long-term unemployed and the settling of more trainees in jobs. Tecs, the employer-led bodies which deliver government training schemes in England and Wales, say the government is seeking to impose the terms of the contracts rather than negotiate. They say they will be forced to 'cream off' the people most likely to get work or qualifications. The Department of Employment's contracts relate to a scheme called Training for Work which is to be launched in April. The new scheme draws together two existing schemes - Employment Training for people who have been out of work for more than six months, and Employment Action, a work-experience scheme that offers participants Pounds 10 a week on top of their normal state benefits. The contracts are intended to cut training time from 26 to 14 weeks. Mr Rodney Skidmore, chief executive of Centec, a Tec based in Redditch, Hereford and Worcester, said the proposed contracts would give Tecs less scope to offer people work on community projects. Mr Tony Lloyd, Labour's training spokesman, said: 'This further demotion of training is a real failure because high-quality training is what this country needs.'
