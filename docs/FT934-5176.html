931201 FT 01 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television It may be necessary to restrain FT ballet critic Clement Crisp during tonight's three-hour Winter Gala marking the centenary of Tchaikovsky's death. Not only is contortionist Sylvie Guillem dancing, but her piece, 'La Luna', was choreographed by Maurice Bejart, one of Crisp's betes noires. Other dancers include Leanne Benjamin and Tetsuya Kumakawa in excerpts from 'Nutcracker", and Darcey Bussell and Zoltan Solymosi in a Balanchine pas de deux. Singers include Placido Domingo and Kiri Te Kanawa (BBC2, 7.30 ). You might have thought it paradoxical that a visual medium such as television should draw so many of its 'personality' presenters from among those with weird voices, yet it is so: think of David Bellamy and Lloyd Grossman. Now art critic Brian Sewell, who sounds like a walking parody of Noel Coward, is joining their ranks. He presents Travelog and 'goes in search of the art of Bavaria, taking in the perils of Munich's one-way system and the castles of Ludwig II, the 'mad king', on his way to the galleries and exhibitions' (C4, 8.30).
