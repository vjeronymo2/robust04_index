July 3, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: ACNE AND WASHING UP

Question: If a teen-ager has an acne condition, how important is it that the face be washed? I have known adolescents who were far from clean and who were never bothered with acne, and I have known others who appeared to be very clean who had a bad condition. How important is cleanliness in this?

Answer: I recommend patients with acne wash two or three times day, using a mild soap and water; however, scrubbing the skin is not recommended. Acne is not caused by dirt. Scrubbing the skin will not improve the condition, and may even tend to aggravate it.
