February 5, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

BILLBOARDS

I couldn't help noticing the irony of the photo you printed with the article "Getting a Grip on Graffiti" (Metro, Jan. 25). It showed a billboard with the message "HELP US CRUSH GRAFFITI" as backdrop for student volunteers in their effort to erase graffiti.

By size and quantity billboards create more visual clutter than graffiti (not to mention the 10 top billboard advertisers are either tobacco or liquor).

If cities really want to take care of their visual environment they should deal with billboards first. In recent years many enlightened cities across the country have done just that by banning the erection of more of this worst form of visual pollution. These include Santa Monica, West Hollywood, Tucson, Phoenix, Fort Worth, Little Rock, Charleston, Virginia Beach, Jacksonville, etc. Here in California, Beverly Hills, Santa Barbara and Palm Springs are among the many cities that never did allow billboards.

TED WU

Los Angeles
