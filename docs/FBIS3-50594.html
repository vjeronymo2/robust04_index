Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Telepress" newscast] [Text] So far, the Mexican Government has not sent the Guatemalan Government any official notification on the disturbances taking place in southern Mexico, Guatemalan Foreign Minister Arturo Fajardo Maldonado has announced. The foreign minister ruled out that the events taking place in the neighboring country will affect the role Mexico is playing as host for the Guatemalan peace process at a time when the first dialogue of 1994 is about to begin this week. [Begin recording] [Fajardo Maldonado] These are strictly internal affairs of Mexico [words indistinct]. [Unidentified reporter] Will you consult with the Guatemalan Embassy in Mexico about this situation? [Fajardo Maldonado] No, I have not consulted the embassy. This is a Mexican issue [words indistinct]. [Reporter] Don't you think this situation might affect the peace talks that will begin very soon [words indistinct]? [Fajardo Maldonado] I have no information to that effect, so therefore, [words indistinct]. [end recording]
