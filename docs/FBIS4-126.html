BFN [Text] According to an opinion poll by the Institute for a Multiparty Democracy, the estimated support for the ANC in the PWV [Pretoria, Witwatersrand, Vereeniging] region has shrunk by almost 8 percent in the past three months, while the National Party [NP] has gained almost 3 percent. The poll shows that the ANC can now bargain on 38 percent support and the NP on 18. Sixteen percent of respondents have refused to say for whom they plan to vote, and 8 percent have not yet made up their minds. The poll has revealed that the situation in at least half of the black residential areas is such that voters are unable to make political choices without interference. A survey released in Natal yesterday showed that the ANC enjoyed 37 percent support, Inkatha 18 percent, and the NP 14.
