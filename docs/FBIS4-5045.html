BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 7 KYODO -- The Export-Import Bank of Japan signed Thursday [6 April] to extend two untied loans worth 28.5 billion yen to Kazakhstan, bank officials said Thursday [7 April]. Of the total, a 18.8 billion yen loan will be made with an arrangement of the International Monetary Fund. The remaining 9.7 billion yen loan will be cofinanced with the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development in its rehabilitation loan program. These loans are the bank's first financial assistance to central Asian countries. The loans are also under the "Funds for Development Initiative" launched by the Japanese Government in June, 1993.
