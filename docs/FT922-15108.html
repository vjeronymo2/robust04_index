920402 FT 02 APR 92 / International Capital Markets: Foreign stocks lure Americans US INVESTORS did not just rush into domestic equities in record numbers last year, they also bought record amounts of foreign stocks. According to figures released yesterday, US investors bought a net Dollars 34.9bn of foreign equities in 1991, writes Patrick Harverson in New York. The total smashed the previous record of Dollars 13.1bn established in 1989. The final quarter was the busiest for US investor participation in foreign stock markets, with net acquisitions reaching Dollars 9.6bn in the final three months. The Securities Industry Association, which compiled the data, said portfolio diversification by US money managers to strong performing foreign equity markets played a major role in the rush overseas. Overall, US holdings of foreign equities by the end of 1991 was Dollars 148.8bn, up nearly 62 per cent from year-end 1990.
