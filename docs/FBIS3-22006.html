Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Excerpts] An investigation by the Federal Operations Division of the Dangerous Drugs Office of the Federal Police led to the arrest of five traffickers and the seizure of 20 kilos of extremely pure cocaine. The operation culminated with a spectacular raid in the heart of the Flores neighborhood yesterday evening. The site of these events was Calle Andres Lamas between Gaona and Franklin, where a large drug transaction was about to take place. Forewarned, discreet police surveillance was set up in the area, awaiting the right moment to go into action. Superintendent Jorge Palacios, director of Federal Operations, was in charge.

The Roundup
According to the police report, [passage omitted] two Ford Fuegos, one red and the other gray, were parked near Franklin. [passage omitted] After the traffickers, from whom no weapons were taken, had been overpowered, [passage omitted] a long and painstaking search was made of the interior of the two Ford Fuegos in which the criminals had arrived at the scene. In the red car brown nylon bags were found, each containing one kilo of cocaine. Twenty kilos were found, all in bags like the ones just described. [passage omitted]
A Revealing Detail
It was learned there that one of the two occupants of the red car in which the drugs were found is a Bolivian citizen, who had earlier been sentenced in Argentina to 12 and 1/2 years in prison. For reasons still unknown, he was released after serving only one and a half years of his court-imposed sentence. [passage omitted]
