BFN [Text] As part of the transfer of Civil Administration powers to the PLO in Gaza, the Palestinians received today the center of control over government offices in Gaza Strip and independent apparatuses which serve, among other things, the Interior and Agriculture Ministries. The commander of the computer unit, Major Yoram Baraq, believes that unless they are helped by Israel, the Palestinians will have many problems operating the systems, although during the overlap period they have acquired a reasonable basis to begin working. Our correspondent Karmela Menashe notes that the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] has, in fact, evacuated the entire Gaza Strip, except for the brigade headquarters, the governor's house, and several other small installments. The ceremony of handing control of the Gaza Strip over to the Palestinians is due to take place tomorrow at the Qatif Bloc. Brigadier General Dubi Gazit, Head of the Civil Administration in the Gaza Strip, said after the PLO accept the authority there is no reason they would not to be able to manage the administration themselves. If any problems should occur, they would be discussed by the regional committee on civilian affairs, he said.
