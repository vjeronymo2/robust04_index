Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] At Senate headquarters today a delegation from Germany's Defense Ministry led by State Secretary for Defense Bernd Wilz was received by the chairmen of the defense councils of the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies, Radu Timofte and Petre Roman. After briefly presenting the bills that have been already passed or that are still to be passed by parliament in the domain of defense, public order, and national security, Timofte and Roman emphasized the importance of Romania's military cooperation with NATO structures, each NATO country, and especially Germany. As a sign of the democratization of our country's defense structures, Timofte outlined the setting up of the National Defense Collegium. The head of the German military delegation stressed the interest of his country in a good military cooperation with Romania, mentioning within that context that over the current year about 40 various cooperation projects are to be accomplished, four Romanian officers are currently studying in Germany, and that the [word indistinct] of the National Defense Ministry, as well as the needs of the Romanian Armed Forces will be satisfied to a great extent from the equipment of the army of the former German Democratic Republic. In conclusion Wilz appreciated the fact that Romania is on the right track regarding the parliament's control over the defense, public order, and national security institutions. He added that on Wednesday [23 March] a delegation of the Bundestag Defense Commission is to arrive in Romania, reports our correspondent Dan Preda.
