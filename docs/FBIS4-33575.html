BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Panamanian economic developments monitored through 7 June. Eduardo Linares, deputy minister of planning and economic policy, has reported that international financial institutes (IFI's) could "withhold approximately $300 million in credits if the government fails to comply with various stringent conditions imposed by these organizations." He added, "There would be a $1.9 million deficit in 1994 budget if this money is withheld." The government is expected to make several modifications to the national budget to prevent the IFI's decision from negatively affecting the budget. Planning Minister Delia Cardenas, Finance Minister Victor Juliao, and Comptroller General Jose Chen Barria are scheduled to meet on 6 June to discuss the issue. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p 1A) A report issued by the Economic Commission for Latin America shows that the Central American region registered an economic growth rate of more than 4 percent in 1993, "which has generated a moderate increase in the domestic GNP of each nation of the region for the second year in a row." The report adds that Central America "advanced in its goal of controlling inflation, which in Costa Rica decreased from 17 percent to 9 percent, and in Guatemala from 14 percent to 12 percent. In Honduras and Nicaragua, however, inflation increased to 15 percent and 25 percent, respectively." Although Panama has not formally joined the Central American region, its GNP increased 5.9 percent while unemployment declined to 13.9 percent in 1993. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p 4B) According to a press release issued by the Israeli Embassy in Panama: "Israeli exports enjoyed a successful year in 1993. Israel's international sales increased 11 percent, while in Latin America, they rose 22 percent." Meanwhile, the increase in Israeli exports to Panama was even higher, growing some 52 percent in just one year. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p 4B)
