930402 FT 02 APR 93 / Observer: High flown True, there could be no fitter quotation to mark the start of the Royal Air Force's 76th year than Churchill's 'Never in the field of human conflict was so much owed by so many to so few'. There is, however, another that military policy-makers in particular would do well to remember. It was uttered by Field Marshal Sir William Nicholson, chief of the imperial general staff just seven years before the RAF was formed, and went: 'Aviation is a useless and expensive fad advocated by a few individuals whose ideas are unworthy of attention.'
