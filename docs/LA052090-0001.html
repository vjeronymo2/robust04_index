May 20, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

FUZZY LOGIC

I wonder if perhaps the only thing fuzzy is Kosko's scientific perception and understanding. There is nothing inherently wrong with Western theorizing.

Western science purports that every continuum in science and nature, whether it be the transition from hot to cold or from heavy to light rain, can be described as a function, or a system of functions, based on a series of yes or no responses.

Kosko states: "I'd be the biggest atheist that ever lived." As the term atheist is itself absolute, is this not an extreme attitude for one who does not deal with either/or interpretations?

For goodness' sake, Bart, turn down the heat in the hot tub, even if it's a little bit at a time.

MARVIN W. KIST, Regina, Sask.
