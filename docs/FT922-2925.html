920613 FT 13 JUN 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Morning rally on inflation data falters Wall Street A MORNING rally on Wall Street on the back of an auspicious inflation report ran out of steam as trading died down ahead of the weekend, writes Karen Zagor in New York. At the close of trading, the Dow Jones Industrial Average was up 2.85 at 3,354.36 after climbing more than 25 points in the morning. Turnover tapered off in the afternoon, with fewer than 183m shares changing hands on the day. Advancing issues led declines by 1008 to 671. Other market indices showed similar gains, with the Standard & Poors 500 adding 0.71 to 409.76, the Nasdaq composite advancing 1.84 to 569.52 and the Amex composite firming 1.42 to 391.32. The May consumer price index, which indicated that prices are increasing very modestly, was welcome news to a market which has been battered this week by a sharp drop in April's consumer borrowing and unexpectedly weak retail sales for May. Both stock and bond markets rallied in the morning following the release of the consumer price index, which rose 0.1 per cent, or 0.2 per cent excluding food and energy prices. Analysts had expected an increase of about 0.3 per cent. The report sparked speculation that might encourage the Federal Reserve might ease monetary policy. In the bond market, the treasury's benchmark 30-year bond was quoted 9/32 higher at 101 21/32 , to yield 7.88 per cent in late trading. Strong gains in a number of heavily-traded blue chip stocks contributed to the morning's rally. IBM advanced Dollars 1 on the day to Dollars 93, Citicorp rose Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 20 1/2 , Philip Morris improved Dollars 2 to Dollars 74 3/8 and Chrysler added Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 20 1/4 . Among featured issues, Unisys slipped Dollars 1/8 to Dollars 8 5/8 in active trading. A Dollars 300m issue of the company's convertible notes was priced to yield 8.25 per cent. The transportation sector regained some of its recent losses, with the Dow Jones Transportation Average climbing 14.39 to 1,346.23. AMR, parent of American Airlines, firmed Dollars 3/4 to Dollars 63 3/4 , Delta Air Lines rose Dollars 1 5/8 to Dollars 58 3/4 and United Airlines was Dollars 1 1/2 higher at Dollars 117 1/4 . Stocks of securities companies improved after having fallen earlier in the week amid concern that the equity market might be heading for a correction and concern about interest rates, improved. Salomon added Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 32 1/2 , Merrill Lynch rose Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 47 and Morgan Stanley held steady at Dollars 48 5/8 . In Nasdaq trading, shares in Ross Cosmetics tumbled Dollars 9 1/2 to Dollars 5, after the company said that it was the subject of an informal Securities and Exchange Commission investigation. Harmonia Bancorp rose Dollars 2 5/8 to a 52-week high of Dollars 17 7/8 on news that it had agreed to merge with Sovereign Bancorp in a cash and stock transaction. Canada TORONTO posted a solid gain in moderate trading, closing at 3,407.98, up 22.61 points. On the week, the composite index gained about 16 points. Advances led declines by 353 to 206. Volume was 23.47m shares, and trading value was CDollars 254m.
