Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Presidential Council Chairman Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih has met in the city of Ta'izz the brother leaders of political party branches in the Ta'izz Governorate. They discussed several political issues, the latest developments in Yemen, and the Ta'izz citizens' concerns, particularly those related to their basic needs and the development of living standards in their governorate. The discussion focused on the current political crisis and its negative repercussions on citizens' living conditions. The leaders of the branches of the political party and organizations expressed their parties' stands toward the development of the current situation in our country. They stressed their parties' concern about safeguarding unity and adhering to it as a national constant. They also stated their concern about enriching democracy. They stressed the need to resort to responsible dialogue to tackle the current political crisis and to create the appropriate positive atmospheres to contribute to the success of the national dialogue committee and to enable it to come up with the desired results that will reinforce the march of national dialogue, unity, and democracy, and lay the foundations of the modern Yemeni state. They praised the contents of the statement issued by the expanded gathering and the Ta'izz sons' pledges to abide by all national constants, foremost of which are unity, democracy, and the resorting to constitutional legitimacy and peaceful dialogue to overcome all challenges facing the homeland, its unity, and democratic approach. The brother president made a statement during the meeting. He expressed his happiness with meeting with the brother leaders of political parties and organization branches in the Ta'izz Governorate. He listened to their opinions about the current national developments and issues and the citizens' aspirations. He reviewed developments in the current political crisis and its regrettable repercussions. He affirmed the importance of the national role the political parties and organizations should play at this delicate stage through which the homeland is passing. He stressed his concern about achieving rapid solutions, through peaceful dialogue -- which will continue to be the ideal means to overcome challenges and to settle differences--to this regrettable crisis from which our people are suffering a great deal, and which is threatening the homeland's present and future. He confirmed the acceptance by the General People's Congress of the results which will be reached by the dialogue committee in a way that will safeguard unity, democracy, and stability in the homeland. Ta'izz Governor Muhammad 'Abdallah al-Iryani attended the meeting.
