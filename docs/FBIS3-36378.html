Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Shanayev] [Text] Vladikavkaz February 4 TASS -- The situation in North Ossetia, where the state of emergency has been prolonged by Russian President Boris Yeltsin's decree for another two months, is stabilizing. According to military sources, no clashes on ethnic grounds have been reported in more than 24 hours. Russia's peace keepers jointly with local law enforcement agencies are maintaining the state of emergency. North Ossetian Parliament Chairman Yuriy Biragov today presided over a session of the republic's commission on restructuring the bodies of state authority and local government in line with the Russian president's decree. Commission members discussed reforms in the republic, the structure of the future parliament and elections to local self government agencies. Its proposals will be submitted to the republic's parliament for consideration.
