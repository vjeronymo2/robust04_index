Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ljubljana, Mar 2 (TANJUG) -- Slovenia's Foreign Ministry on Wednesday sent a protest note to Croatia because of the resumption of construction works on Croatia's border crossing in the vicinity of the Slovenian resort of Portoroz. Slovenian authorities maintain that Croatia's customs authorities are constructing facilities on Slovenia's territory and demand that the works be discontinued until the final demarcation in area. The construction works were discontinued in May last year after strong protests by Slovenia. Croatia, however, decided to resume construction works which is expected to cause further straining of Slovenian-Croatian relations which have already deteriorated as a result of disputes over land and maritime borders and electric power deliveries from the Krsko nuclear power plant. Inhabitants of the Kantrida settlement in the Croatian port of Rijeka in [word indistinct] particular way expressed dissatisfaction with Slovenia's policy towards Croatia and the deteriorating relations between Ljubljana and Zagreb. On a number of ocassions they threw stones at trains on the Rijeka-Ljubljana line, the Rijeka police said. In the exchange of diplomatic letter between Croatia and Slovenia concerning the incident, the Croatian side promised, as it was underscored, that it will "prevent vandal actions of its citizens."
