BFN [Report by R. Barjaktarevic: "Montenegro Is Not a Serbian State"] [Text] Pristina -- "It is obvious that I cannot have a favorable opinion about any proposal that turns Montenegro into a region. This means that the proposal, which comes from Belgrade, that would lead to Montenegro losing its statehood is unacceptable to us. The authors of such ideas and projects are mistaken, since Montenegrin statehood has been forged by the nation through burdensome history, and that is why the current Montenegrin generation has the moral duty to take a dignified patriotic stand toward such tendencies that are aimed at obstructing the revival of Montenegrin statehood. I regard as frivolous all the theories about a new constitution based on regionalization." This is what Montenegrin Foreign Minister Miodrag Lekic said in an extensive interview for the Pristina ZERI. Asked about the possibility of creating a union of Serbian states, which would also include Montenegro, Lekic said: "We are discussing ideas, thoughts. However, they not only have not been discussed and verified at the official state levels, but they have not even been defined. In any case, in connection with our position, that is, the Montenegro's position, there are many open questions. As for an association, that is links between the Serb Krajina and the republic of Srpska, I can see some difficulties in Montenegro participating in such a creation. To start with, Montenegro is not a Serbian state, but a state of the people who live in it, that is, a civil state." The Montenegrin foreign minister said that there were "no secret connections between Montenegro and Italy," and he also said that relations with Albania were better since "whenever I travel abroad, I fly from the Tirana airport." As for Kosovo, he said: "The basic thing is to start a serious dialogue, but it is hard to say what kinds of solutions will be found. In any case, it would be best if the solutions were to reflect the will of all the citizens. I support certain rights for Kosovo, autonomy, certain subjectivity."
