940120 FT 20 JAN 94 / Arts: Today's Television Judging by last week's opening episode, Big Science is yet another attempt to make science simple. The trouble is that ideas such as the Big Bang theory aren't simple, and however slick your presentation, and sexy your presenters, they are never going to become simple. Today, like every other series, this one visits the US. Subjects include the Gaia theory and neutrinos: where are they? (8.00 BBC2). Minder, a survivor from the old days before franchise auctions spoiled British commercial television, provides a rare oasis of high quality entertainment in the desert of shoddiness that is ITV today. This week brings the 100th episode. George Cole in the Arthur Daley role, without whom everything would collapse, is involved with a friend who is fleeing a murderer, and also with the Salvation Army (8.30). Footage of a British wildcat hunting (not a feral cat but the real thing as still found in the wilds of Scotland) is promised by Wildlife On One (8.30 BBC1). Great Railway Journeys travels across South Africa, visiting, among other cities, Johannesburg (9.00 BBC2).
