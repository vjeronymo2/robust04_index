BFN [Text] Bonn -- The Bundestag adopted the law on the fight against crime with the votes of the coalition today. It is designed to accelerate legal proceedings, to increase the punishment for drug dealers, and to make possible the involvement of the Federal Intelligence Service in the fight against criminal gangs. The Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] rejected the law and announced improvements by the Bundesrat. The Bundestag unanimously adopted several amendments to laws making the fight against right-wing radicalism more efficient. Among other things, denying the killing of Jews by the National Socialists and the wearing of Nazi symbols will become punishable.
