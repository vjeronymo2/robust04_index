920623 FT 23 JUN 92 / International Company News: France sets Total share price THE French government has set a price of FFr230 per share on the 12.4 per cent stake in oil company Total that it is selling to the public, the Finance Ministry said in a statement, agencies report from Paris. The ministry also said French stock market authorities had approved its plan to exchange another 6.5 per cent of Total stock for Total investment certificates held by investors. The government will raise FFr5.27bn (Dollars 1bn) from the sale of its 22.9m shares in Total and its direct shareholding in Total will be reduced to 15 per cent from 34 per cent. The share sale will open today and close on June 25. The certificate swap will open shortly. French investors will be offered 8m shares under the sale. There will also be an international offering of 7.5m shares plus a US tranche of 7.4m shares. Depending on demand, the French tranche could be increased by 10 per cent with shares taken from the international offering. When it disclosed its intention to trim its stake in Total, the government indicated it would retain a 5 per cent direct shareholding in Total, but did not make clear how the remaining 10 per cent stake would be distributed. The ministry said state-owned bank Credit Lyonnais and Assurances Generales de France, a state-controlled insurance group, would each take 4 per cent of the 10 per cent stake. The remaining 2 per cent stake would go to Groupement des Assurances Nationales, a state-sector insurance group.
