Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, February 7, RIA -- The Prime Ministers of Russia and Moldova, Viktor Chernomyrdin and Andrei Sangheli, today signed an inter-government agreement in Moscow on trade and economic cooperation for 1994. The agreement, according to the Russian prime minister, provides for switching to world prices in trade between the two countries. Chernomyrdin noted that in 1994 Russia will be using world prices in trading with all former Soviet republics, although he recalled that energy prices for CIS states were differentiated last year. The Russian prime minister pointed out that Russia and Moldova have a good trade balance. Moldova has almost fully paid for the Russian commodities. It owes only for the energy. Sangheli added that Moldova is planning to pay its energy arrears within the next 30 to 45 days. Chernomyrdin told journalists that Russia is planning to buy maize, seeds and non-ferrous metals from Moldova and added that specific trade figures are yet to be determined.
