Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 24/3/1994 -- Significant changes in the electorate's options are currently taking place in Romania, according to an opinion poll carried out by the Romanian Public Opinion Polling Institute [IRSOP], requested by the Romanian Foundation for Democracy, whose results are published exclusively by daily TINERETUL LIBER. If presidential elections were to take place in Romania and the candidates to run for the presidency would be the same as in the 1992 election campaign, 37 percent would be likely to opt for Emil Constantinescu [1992 candidate of the opposition electoral alliance], 27 percent for [current Romanian President] Ion Iliescu, and 10 percent for Gheorghe Funar (National Romanian Unity Party -- PUNR.) Worth mentioning is that 20 percent of those polled said that they would abstain from vote. As for a potential call of parliamentary elections, 30 percent of the electorate would favor the Democratic Convention of Romania (CDR -- opposition,) while 23 percent would vote for the ruling party, the Social Democracy Party of Romania [PDSR]. Some 35 percent of the polled people view that a government formed by the opposition would be much successful in getting the country out of the crisis, 31 percent consider that the crisis could be overcome by a coalition government formed by the current ruling party together with the CDR opposition group and the Democratic Party-National Salvation Front [PD-FSN], while 20 percent are of the opinion that much suited would be a coalition government consisting of the PDSR with the PUNR, the Socialist Party of Labor [PSM], and the Greater Romania Party [PRM], which currently are the main government backers. Worth mentioning is that 14 percent of the polled people are of the opinion that a PDSR government would be most suited to get the country out of the crisis. Among the politicians in the current parliamentary majority, in the event that Ion Iliescu would not run for the presidential elections, 39 percent of the electorate are likely to opt for Adrian Nastase, the current speaker of the Chamber of Deputies and executive chairman of the PDSR, 21 percent for Gheorghe Funar, current chairman of the PUNR, and 10 percent are favoring Corneliu Vadim Tudor, the current chairman of the PRM. The poll was carried out over 1-7 March 1994 on 1,100 individuals in 65 localities in the rural areas and in Romanian towns.
