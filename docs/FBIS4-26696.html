BFN [By Benjamin Yeh] [Text] Taipei, June 25 (CNA) -- The Taoyuan District Court in northern Taiwan on Friday [24 June] convicted and sentenced 30 Taoyuan County councilors to up to two years in prison on charges of vote-buying and corruption and acquitted two others. Hsu Chen-yun, the speaker of the Taoyuan County Council, received a two-year jail term and had his civil rights stripped for three years. According to the verdict, Hsu gave money and offered sightseeing trips to councilors in exchange for their votes in the March 1 county council election. Hsu, a Kuomintang member, had pleaded not guilty to the charges. He will appeal. Twenty-nine other councilors who along with Hsu were found guilty by the court were given prison terms ranging from 10 months to 22 months and all had their civil rights suspended for two years. Each had pleaded not guilty. Fourteen of the 29 councilors are KMT members, six are affiliated with the Democratic Progressive Party, Taiwan's largest opposition party, and nine others are independents. Twenty-nine people in Keelung city as well as Miaoli, Kaohsiung and Hualien counties, including a speaker and three deputy speakers, had previously been sentenced for either paying or accepting bribes. Separately, prosecutors in 10 other cities and counties across Taiwan have charged over 350 on similar charges in Taiwan's worst election scandal. The Kuomintang won all 23 city and council speaker posts in the polls.
