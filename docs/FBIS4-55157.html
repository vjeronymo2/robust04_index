BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 10/5/1994 -- Romanian Minister of State Mircea Cosea appreciated as a success the stabilization of the leu exchange rate reached in April as much valuable as it had been achieved without "traumas or big commotions." In an interview with the Romania Radio, Vacaroiu cabinet's reform minister Mircea Cosea said the stabilization process would continue till June, the current year, when the inter-bank market is scheduled to be established. Cosea opined the success belonged to a fruitful cooperation between Government and the National Bank of Romania "extremely well valued abroad" and the "final" condition on the IMF stand-by loan. Referring to the stage of privatization, Cosea said the so-called "30 percent" action would be finalized within the current month. The action will consist in the population's exchanging their property certificates -- previously granted to the population -- for shares in the commercial state companies amounting up to a total value of 30 percent of the commercial companies' aggregate capital. The Governmental Council of Economic Coordination, Strategy, and Reform -- headed by Cosea -- together with the National Privatization Agency, State Property Fund, and the (five) Private Property Funds are drawing up the details of the stock public offering. Mircea Cosea gave as a proof of the increasing international recognition of the economic reform in Romania the invitation he had received in order to open and lead a conference organized by the specialized commission of the European Union for issues concerning the small and middle-sized enterprises.
