Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 12, BNS -- The Foreign Ministries of Latvia and Hungary concluded a bilateral cooperation accord during the visit of Latvian Foreign Minister Georgs Andrejevs to Hungary this week. The agreement provides for mutual consultations aimed at encouraging coordinated actions in international organizations, Andrejevs told reporters Saturday [12 March] at a press briefing in the Riga airport. Andrejevs also handed over to the Hungarian leadership draft agreements on investment protection and avoidance of double taxation. Latvia has also prepared a draft agreement on legal cooperation, Andrejevs noted. The minister, however, questioned the efficiency of implementation of agreements on trade and air communication. During his Hungarian trip, Andrejevs met with President Arpad Goenz and Foreign Minister Geza Jeszensky.
