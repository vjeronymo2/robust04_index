BFN [Text] The closing session of the Jordanian-Palestinian Economic Committee, due to have convened in Amman this morning, has been postponed. Also postponed was a subsequent news conference by the chairmen of the Jordanian and Palestinian sides to the committee, Dr. Jawad al-'Anani, minister of state for cabinet affairs and minister of information; and Mr. Ahmad Quray', the PLO's Economic Department chief. In a statement to the JORDANIAN NEWS AGENCY, Dr. al-'Anani attributed the delay to the preoccupation of the Palestinian leadership with numerous issues, including the formation of the Palestinian Authority Council and matters related to the transfer of power. The final session of the Jordanian-Palestinian talks that began in Amman Tuesday [10 May] is due to be held within days. During the two-day meeting, the committee addressed numerous economic items, including trade, banking, monetary policies, customs, and tourism.
