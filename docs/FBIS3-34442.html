Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 8 Feb (MENA)--Dr. Nabil Sha'th, political adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, told correspondents today that negotiations between the Palestinian and Israeli delegations have been positive so far but that no results have been achieved. Faysal al-Husayni, official in charge of the occupied territories, said: We hope that matters will continue to proceed well but we have not finished the talks yet. Uri Savir, director general of the Israeli Foreign Ministry, said that the clauses of the Davos document are being discussed one by one and it is too early to talk about results. He stressed that the atmosphere during the negotiations was good. He added that all the issues raised were discussed during the talks, which lasted all last night and today. The members of the Israeli and Palestinian delegations made these statements at the end of the meeting in al-Andalus Palace, Heliopolis, which lasted more than seven hours, and while they were on their way to the working luncheon given by Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa at the hotel across from the palace.
