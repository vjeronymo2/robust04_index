BFN [Excerpt] The Universal Economic Forum ends in Crans Montana in Switzerland today. In addition to plenary sessions and discussions within sections, bilateral talks also played an important role. Our special correspondent Lucie Weissova tells us about those in which the Czech delegation was involved. [Weissova] [Czech] Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus held talks with the host Swiss delegation, from whom he received an invitation to pay an official visit to Switzerland. He also talked to representatives of the smaller states of the former Soviet Union: Moldova, Georgia, Armenia, and Belarus. He told us the following about the contents of those talks: [Begin Klaus recording] What I consider significant is that these countries explicitly see the model being effected by the Czech Republic as being the model for them to follow. We have even firmly agreed that for both Moldova and Georgia we might be prepared to send a certain delegation of our experts, who would advise them as to the logic of the whole transformation process. [end recording] [passage omitted]
