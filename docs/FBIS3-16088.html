Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Bessarabov] [Excerpts] Elista March 12 TASS -- Participants in a spontaneous protest meeting, which is now under way in Elista, capital of Kalmykia, are expressing disagreement with the recent statements of the Kalmyk president on the elimination of the republican Constitution. [passage omitted] Commenting on [Kalmyk President] Ilyumzhinov's statement in an ITAR-TASS interview, David Kugultinov, a popular Kalmyk poet and chairman of the Council of Elders at the president, described it as an "ill-considered step." "One should at least consult the people and find out their opinion, before taking such an important decision. By all appearances, the suggestion of our leader came as a result of instability in Russia, as well as in its leadership. I do not think that Kalmykia will turn into an out-of-the-way place, if it loses its status of a republic and its Constitution. But it will lose the energy of statehood, which has been and continues to be a source of hope for all those who live on its territory. Haste has never done any good to anybody, and I am sure the Kalmyk people will realise it and will not support their president," Kugultinov said.
