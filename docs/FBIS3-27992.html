Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 2 KYODO -- U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor and Japanese Trade Minister Hiroshi Kumagai met Wednesday [2 February] in a bid to break a stalemate in bilateral trade talks but the talks apparently ended inconclusively. The talks were aimed at reaching a new trade framework between the two countries with attention focused on improving American access to Japan's auto and auto parts markets, an official at the Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI] told a press conference. The official declined to disclose the contents of the talks, saying both parties agreed to keep silent for now. But he described the atmosphere of the meeting as "friendly but serious." After emerging from the meeting, Kantor told reporters the talks were "interesting." After the press conference, another MITI official said the trade stance by both Tokyo and Washington basically remained unchanged throughout the meeting, hinting that the two nations failed to bridge a gap over the framework talks. The Kumagai-Kantor meeting followed talks held earlier in the day between Kantor and Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata, results of which were also withheld in a briefing for reporters. Kantor arrived in Tokyo on Tuesday in an effort to break a stalemate in the bilateral trade talks ahead of the February 11 summit between Japan's Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa and U.S. President Bill Clinton. The most contentious part of the new framework is how to define "objective criteria" as a way to gauge foreign penetration of Japan's market. Tokyo says it is against any setting of numerical targets as it would lead to managed trade, while Washington is pushing for a framework based on an accord reached between Clinton and then Japanese Prime Minister Kiichi Miyazawa in July 1993, which called for the introduction of "sets of objective criteria, either qualitative or quantitative or both." The official at the briefing said Kumagai and Kantor are unlikely to resume talks during Kantor's stay in Japan. It is not known when Kantor will leave Tokyo. He said he is not certain whether or not subcabinet-level trade talks between the two countries will be held over the weekend as reported.
