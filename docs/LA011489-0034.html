January 14, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

WORKING FOR JUSTICE

The irony of "Working Girl" strikes a nerve ("Griffith Fits Glass Slipper as a 'Working Girl' Cinderella," Dec. 2).

Here's a fantasy film about a woman who's scrambling to find justice in the corporate world. In the end, she receives justice.

But catch the credits that appear on the screen: Melanie Griffith gets third billing, behind Harrison Ford and Sigourney Weaver. Third billing for a film that Griffith clearly carried, a film that relied on her role to push the box office.

C'mon, Hollywood. C'mon, 20th Century Fox. That's corporate injustice!

NEIL EGLASH

and F. DAVID RENNIE

Studio City
