BFN [Article signed GGD: "Russia Joins PFP"] [Text] Brussels, 22 Jun (BELGA) -- Russia officially signed the Partnership for Peace program [PFP], which provides for closer cooperation with NATO, on Wednesday [22 June]. Russian Foreign Minister Kozyrev signed the PFP, implementing the NATO-Russia cooperation agreement, mainly in the military sphere, which the alliance proposed to other European countries and especially to its former Cold War enemies. Addressing the ambassadors of NATO's 16 member countries at the alliance's Brussels Headquarters, the Russian foreign minister stated that "the preparation of an important Russian-NATO cooperation program has been launched. We have agreed on a large and profound dialogue as well as cooperation on issues in which Russia could make a substantial and unique contribution, corresponding to its importance and its responsibilities as major nuclear power in Europe and in the world." U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher attended the signing ceremony.
