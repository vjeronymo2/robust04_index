Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Juan Sebastian Solis; from the "Good Morning From Mexico" newscast] [Text] Approximately 5,000 representatives of 40 Indian and peasant groups in Chiapas State marched through the streets of San Cristobal de las Casas on Sunday to demand solutions to problems involving land, justice, freedom, and democracy. The march, which was organized by the state committee of Indian and peasant organizations, was also supported by 300 teachers from Oaxaca. Some of the groups were formed solely by women. The Mexican flag was carried by Indian women. The demonstrators gathered in front of the municipal building and held a four-hour rally in which representatives of each of the participating organizations spoke. Mrs. Marcela Gonzalez spoke on behalf of the Emiliano Zapata Peasant Organization [Organizacion Campesina Emiliano Zapata]. She said: [Begin Gonzalez recording] We are standing up for Indian dignity, and no one is going to bring us to our knees. [end recording] The demonstrators demanded the reform of Article 27 of the Constitution, a solution to the problem of delinquent loans, dismissal of mayors in Chiapas, and respect for Indian traditions and cultures. As they marched through the city, the demonstrators wrote their demands on the walls of houses and stores. They did the same on the walls of the San Cristobal de las Casas municipal building.
