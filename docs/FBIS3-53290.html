Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, 13 Jan (INA)--Iraq has protested to the Arab League the continued facilities provided by the rulers of Saudi Arabia and Kuwait to the U.S., British, and French aircraft in their violations of the Iraqi airspace which pose a grave threat to Iraq's security, sovereignty, and national unity. In a letter sent yesterday to Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid, the Iraqi permanent envoy said the U.S., British, and French aircraft on 7, 8, and 9 January continued to violate the Iraqi airspace and conducted 101 hostile sorties out of bases in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait. The Iraqi envoy enclosed a list detailing these violations and asked that the letter be distributed to the Arab League member states.
