Language: Hindi Article Type:CSO [Commentary by Abhinav Kautilya: "Out of Line: The Issue is not Human Rights, but Clinton's Autocracy"] [Excerpts] [passage omitted] When people who dropped nuclear bombs on Hiroshima and played with the lives of millions of innocent women, the elderly, the handicapped, and the sick, and made the earth there unproductive forever, are now talking about human rights in Kashmir, then it appears we are not listening to a president [Clinton] but to a sick person suffering from mental illness in an asylum. India, in comparison with the way the Americans cut down the Vietnamese like vegetables and grass, is miles away from sinking to this level. Clinton has said that terrorism and religious fundamentalism is nothing; the real thing is human rights. [passage omitted] What is happening in Kashmir? One or two thousand crazed Pakistanis are shouting and carrying weapons in their hands, telling us that they will decide where they will live. This really is something. We do not have to accept what hundreds of thousands of voters want or the wishes of those who elected representatives to Kashmir's Vidhan Sabha and the Indian Parliament. Instead, we should listen to the people fighting a guerrilla war with weapons from Pakistan and the United States of America. The United States wants us to honor and pamper them. The depth to which Mr. Clinton wants us to go down in relation to human rights is the same as we wish to follow. It is true that human rights belong to humans, but who are humans? Only those who follow truth, religion, non-violence, and are of good character and high ideals are humans. This is the old, accepted standard. Now we say generally that any person who believes in law and order is human. Anyone who is not human is a devil. If we ignore the old definition, then whoever ignores law and order is a non-citizen or inhuman. We can also say that person is a criminal. From this perspective, U.S. President Clinton is advocating the position of criminals in Kashmir. [passage omitted] Human rights have not been defined by the United States, but by the United Nations. No other member of the United Nations has noticed any human rights violations; only the United States has. Now it seems that God has given all intelligence and vision only to Bill Clinton. It seems that Clinton is not only the President of the United States, but the greatest autocrat in the world. [passage omitted] *
