June 6, 1990, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: TUSTIN;

MEETING CALLED ON DISPUTED BUILDING

The City Council asked the developer of an almost-finished apartment building to meet Thursday with city staff members and residents angry about the project.

Construction on the 11-unit apartment project came to a halt about four months ago after a battle erupted among the neighbors, the city and the developer.

Mayor Richard B. Edgar said he hopes a meeting will help all parties avoid a costly legal battle.

Residents who live near the 2 1/2-story apartments say the project overshadows their homes, destroys their privacy and reduces their property values.

Developer Feridoun Rezai obtained a permit for the apartments last year, but neighbors who live on Myrtle and Corla avenues next to the project did not complain until the apartments began to take shape.

After investigating, the city staff learned that 17 property owners never received notices of the public hearing at which the permit was discussed. The law requires that all property owners within 300 feet of the project receive notices.

The Planning Commission on April 30 denied a permit for the project, requiring that Rezai either modify the building or demolish it. He appealed the decision.

Rezai's attorney, Joel Ward, said he estimates his client is losing about $1,000 each day while the project sits uncompleted. SHANNON SANDS
