920213 FT 13 FEB 92 / Observer: On the rocks The icicles were almost audible when Ernest Saunders, former chief executive of Guinness, turned up on Radio Four's Today programme to discuss the collapse of the Guinness II trial. Before the interview Saunders had been asked to wait in the tiny hospitality room at Broadcasting House with another guest already there. His face blanched markedly when he opened the door to find Barbara Mills who, as director of the Serious Fraud Office, was the power behind his 10-month sojourn at Ford Open Prison. The surprise was mutual. Carelessly or cunningly, Radio Four had told neither of them that the other was to be there.
