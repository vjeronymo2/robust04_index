931105 FT 05 NOV 93 / Hume talks of 'peace in a week' for Ulster JOHN HUME, leader of the nationalist Social Democratic and Labour party, said yesterday there would be 'peace within a week' in Ulster if his initiative with Sinn Fein president Gerry Adams was embraced. Mr Hume (pictured talking to reporters at Downing Street) outlined details of the peace plan to prime minister John Major. But the plan was rebuffed. Hopes for peace were boosted by Ulster Unionist party leader James Molyneaux, who responded positively to the latest Anglo-Irish initiative. Unionist leader upbeat, Page 20
