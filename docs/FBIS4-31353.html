BFN [Text] Teodor Melescanu, Romanian minister of foreign affairs, and Faruq Qaddumi, head of the PLO Political Department, signed a protocol this afternoon on cooperation between the Romanian Government and the PLO Executive Committee. After signing the document, the Romanian foreign minister said that the protocol is an expression of the Romanian Government's desire to continue to stay involved in supporting the peace process in the Middle East. At the same time, he said, the protocol provides a principled foundation for Romania's direct participation in the reconstruction and development of Gaza and Jericho. Melescanu also handed Qaddumi a letter addressed by the Romanian Government to Chairman Yasir 'Arafat, which emphasizes Romania's determination to contribute to the economic reconstruction of the autonomous Palestinian territories with the offer of 50 scholarships for Palestinian students wishing to study priority economic subjects in Romania. In his turn, Qaddumi said that the document signed today marks a new stage in Romanian-Palestinian cooperation and will consolidate bilateral cooperation to the end of establishing peace and stability in the Middle East. This report was sent in to us by our correspondent Eugen Baboi. Qaddumi was also received by President Ion Iliescu at Cotroceni Palace. During the talks the sides tackles issues pertaining to the latest developments in the Middle East. On this occasion, President Ion Iliescu was conveyed wishes for well-being from Chairman Yasir 'Arafat, the Palestinian guest told the press after the meeting. In connection with the document signed today at the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Qaddumi said that Romania is the first country in Eastern Europe with which such an agreement is signed. He added that this agreement is very important for intensifying bilateral relations. The meeting between President Ion Iliescu and the PLO official was attended by Romanian Foreign Minister Melescanu and by Presidential Adviser Traian Chebeleu.
