Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum, 25 March (SUNA) -- Bishop Gabriel Rorech, minister of state at the Foreign Ministry, has said that the government is eager to continue negotiations with the rebel movement, because it thinks dialogue is the only means for reaching a comprehensive peace which will ensure the stability and development of Sudan. His excellency stated in today's edition of AL-SUDAN AL-HADITH that he regretted interference from some foreign quarters, which are attempting to thwart the negotiations and are working to fragment the country's unity. He pointed out that the government clearly explained to the Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development [IGADD] states that those foreign quarters did not want to see the establishment of peace, and were inciting the rebel movement in order that the negotiations fail. His excellency stated: We, as Sudanese, should advance the efforts of the IGADD in the negotiations, so that positive results are achieved. He confirmed that Sudan rejects foreign interference in the country's internal affairs.
