BFN [Text] Algiers, April 6 (AFP) -- A prominent Algerian businessman and opposition party leader, Mehdi Abbas Allalou, was badly injured in an attack in an Algiers suburb Wednesday, security officials said. Allalou, leader of the small Popular Association for Unity and Action (APUA) movement, was rushed to hospital after the attack in the southeastern El Harrach district. Officials gave no details of the circumstances. He was the third party leader to have been attacked since Moslem fundamentalists launched a bloody campaign which has claimed more than 3,000 lives since the authorities in January 1992 cancelled the second round of a general election the Islamic Salvation Front (FIS) was poised to win. The FIS was subsequently outlawed. The businessman, known for his close ties to the Gulf countries and Saudi Arabia, took a stance in favour of Kuwait when it was invaded by Iraq in 1991.
