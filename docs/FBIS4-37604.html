BFN [Report by Ibrahim Humaydi: "Damascus: Khaddam and al-Shar' Meet With Hamas Delegation"] [Text] Damascus -- Abu-Muhammad Mustafa, representative of the Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) in Damascus, has told AL-HAYAH that a Hamas leadership delegation headed by Dr. Musa Abu-Marzuq yesterday met with Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam and Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' separately. Hamas sources said that the meetings, which were also attended by Hamas spokesman Ibrahim Ghawshah, Political Bureau Member Muhammad Nazzal, and Abu-Muhammad Mustafa, "covered the latest developments relating to the Palestinian question as well as Arab developments." The sources said that both meetings were "warm and serious. We expressed our appreciation of the Syrian position." In a statement to AL-HAYAH, Abu-Muhammad Mustafa said the two meetings dealt with "the current situation and the role of the vital forces in facing what is going on. We heard their (Khaddam's and al-Shar''s) views and they heard ours. The views were responsive." He added that Hamas considered itself "to be in a confrontation trench as an organization and considered it (Syria) to be in the same trench as a state." He said that the delegation of the Movement, which is opposed to the Oslo agreement, and al-Shar' "made a political assessment of the current phase, future possibilities, and the position of Hamas inside [the occupied territory]." He added: "We explained the position of the Movement inside accurately." He said that the discussions also broached the meeting of the 10 [Palestinian opposition] factions held yesterday evening. "We acquainted the Syrian officials with the picture and with possible developments. There is a clear Syrian interest in the continuation of the Alliance of Palestinian Forces (opposed to the Oslo agreement) and the reactivation of its role." But the Hamas representative added that Khaddam and al-Shar' pointed out that "the matter is up to the Alliance." Meanwhile, sources in the Democratic Front for the Liberation of Palestine, led by Nayif Hawatimah, have told AL-HAYAH that the meeting of the Central Command of the Alliance held yesterday evening "was devoted to discussing the latest developments in the occupied territory in light of the various interactions taking place in Gaza and Jericho, and the role of the opposition forces there." They added that the conferees also discussed "the question of the municipal appointments in the occupied territory and practical moves to foil these appointments."
