940217 FT 17 FEB 94 / World Stock Markets: Latin America reflects flow of funds Latin America has been benefiting over recent weeks from a general movement of institutional money out of Asia. This is clearly indicated by a 20 per cent rise in dollar terms since the start of the year, compared to an 11 per cent fall in Asia. The European markets have been hit even harder, dropping by some 20 per cent since January, although here the story has been influenced by a steep decline in Turkey. On a summary inspection, Brazil stands out as the clear leader in Latin America this year. But, says Mr Roger Palmer of Kleinwort Benson in London, since last November all of the markets in the region have performed in a broadly similar manner, with rises of between 42 to 52 per cent in dollar terms. While investors have been encouraged by progress in balancing the Brazilian budget, it is still too early, says Mr Palmer, to view the country's economic difficulties as all but solved. A lot depends on whether the introduction of a new index designed to tackle inflation will make a significant impression. He takes the view that this is far from certain, especially because of the fluid political situation, with elections due later this year. Sri Lanka has been showing solid performance in south Asia, with investors attracted by its relative cheapness, compared with India.
