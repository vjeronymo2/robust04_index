BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Tbilisi, 12 Jun -- "For all friendly states the Day of Russia's Independence will be a symbol of the new Russia's democratic aspirations," Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze, the speaker of the Georgian parliament, has told ITAR-TASS. In the opinion of the Georgian leader, Russia is celebrating its Independence Day in new and different circumstances. Despite the fact that Russia is going through a difficult period of establishing itself economically and politically, a new social climate is forming in society. "It is vital for all world countries that this country, which plays the decisive role in whether the post-Soviet region will become a conflict-free zone, remains stable," he noted. "If one is to speak about Russia's new role in history, it is the mission of peacemaker and guarantor of stability."
