BFN [Text] The Indonesian Foreign Ministry's Public Affairs Director (Abid) said in an interview with ITAR-TASS on 9 June that, as the chair state of the nonaligned movement, Indonesia is making quiet diplomatic efforts to peacefully resolve the serious nuclear issue of the Korean peninsula. Indonesia is relying on the dispatch of special envoys to countries directly concerned of the dispute, including the DPRK, the United States, the PRC, and Japan, and the contact with the International Atomic Energy Agency in Vienna. These are aimed at peacefully solving all the problems, he said. The Foreign Ministry spokesman pointed out that the nuclear issue of the Korean peninsula had been discussed in depth during the meeting of the foreign ministers of nonaligned countries held in Cairo some time ago. The participants agreed that problems concerning the Korean peninsula should be solved through peaceful means and that contacts between all parties concerned should continue.
