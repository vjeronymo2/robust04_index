Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, March 1 (SNARK)--Aleksandr Kavsadze, chairman of the Human Rights' Protection commission of the Georgian Parliament expressed his bewilderment on the recent declarations of Armenian leaders. The other day Armenian Foreign Minister Vahan Papazyan declared that Georgia will have nothing to do but to accept Armenian provisions. Earlier Armenia suggested to exchange some amount of gas, bought by her from Turkmenistan, with free shipment through Georgian territory. "Such declarations won't promote our relations", Aleksandr Kavsadze told SNARK. In his opinion, "when the situation is difficult you should not to make such statements but to solve all the problems in consent. Moreover it is inadmissible to scare each other," said Georgian Deputy.
