Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Speaking at a news conference on 30 March, Governor's Office Director Takayama disclosed that Governor Ota plans to call on U.S. Ambassador to Japan Mondale, who is scheduled to visit Okinawa on 10 April, to settle three serious problems resulting from the existence of U.S. military bases on Okianawa which are: 1) return of Naha Port; 2) return of the Yomitan Auxiliary Airfield and the abolishment of parachute training; and 3) cancellation and abolishment of live-fire exercises across prefectural Highway 104. Ambassador Mondale is scheduled to pay a courtesy call on Governor Ota on 10 April. After receiving his courtesy visit, Governor Ota plans to petition him for the solution of those issues. Director Takayama said: "The Okinawa Prefectural Office heartily welcomes Ambassador Mondale's visit. In fact, we would like to arrange a meeting with him aside from his courtesy call. However, since his itinerary is crammed, we want to call on him to solve the issues immediately after receiving his courtesy visit."
