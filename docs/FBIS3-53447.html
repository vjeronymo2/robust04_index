Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by Sefi Handler from Labor Central Committee meeting in Tel Aviv -- live] [Excerpt] The prime minister has already left the Labor party Central Committee meeting. The political statement he had promised was not delivered for lack of time, but emerging from the meeting, the prime minister answered reporters' questions on the upcoming summit meeting between President Bill Clinton and Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad, slated to be held in Geneva on 16 January. The prime minister was not optimistic: [Begin Rabin recording] I do not know exactly what will take place there. Unfortunately, I do not anticipate Syria showing greater flexibility on the matter of the price it is demanding for peace with Israel. It may show greater flexibility on the matter of the nature of peace, but we will only know that after the encounter. [end recording] [passage omitted]
