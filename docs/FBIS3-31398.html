Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Feb. 15 -- Polish Foreign Minister Andrzej Olechowski and his Moldovan counterpart Ion Botnaru, now on an official visit to this country, initialled on Tuesday a treaty on friendship and cooperation between Poland and Moldova. The two ministers also signed a protocol on mutual consultations between their ministries. Olechowski said that the treaty would be signed in the first half of this year during a Polish visit by Moldovan President Mircea Snegur. The Polish foreign minister stressed that relations with Moldova -- like the ones with the neighbouring countries and kazakhstan -- were considered by Poland as top priority of the Polish foreign policy towards the states established after the break-up of the Soviet Union. Olechowski added Poland wanted to help Moldova consolidate its presence on the international arena as it was security of "such states as Moldova" in which Poland saw a significant condition of its own security and favourable development of European relations. The Polish foreign minister said Poland would open its embassy in Chisinau still this year. Next, Olechowski said that during the Polish-Moldovan talks the two sides discussed the necessity to improve living conditions of the Polish minority in Moldova, to provide possibilities of learning the Polish language and to ensure Poles access to national culture. The two sides also spoke about prospects of economic cooperation, especially between small and medium-sized enterprises. Botnaru said he hoped Moldova could make use of the Polish experience in introducing market reforms.
