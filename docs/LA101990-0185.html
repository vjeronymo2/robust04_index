October 19, 1990, Friday, P.M. Final

THE SIDELINES;

MURRAY BEATS WARD FOR TITLE

Charles Murray scored a unanimous and lopsided 12-round decision over Irish Micky Ward to become the United States Boxing Assn. junior welterweight champion.

The 22-year-old unbeaten Rochester native won the title Thursday night before a cheering crowd of more than 5,000 at the Rochester Community War Memorial.

Murray (18-0, 12 knockouts) had never gone more than eight rounds before. But he threw quick combinations to Ward's body and continually beat Ward, 25, of Lowell, Mass., to the punch.
