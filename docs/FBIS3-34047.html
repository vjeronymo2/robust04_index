Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Deputy Foreign Minister Yosi Beilin says Israel is counting on Egyptian authorities to deal with violence by militants in that country, and that Jerusalem is not recommending that Israelis stay out of Egypt. Egyptian militants have warned foreigners to leave the country for their own safety, saying the next attacks will be extremely ferocious and strong. Deputy Foreign Minister Beilin gave the Israeli assessment to reporter Jack Katzenell: [Begin recording] [Beilin] Well, we are of course following the situation, but we are sure that the Egyptian Government is able to handle this situation and to take care of those developments which are, of course, not very positive, but still are far from representing the general mood in Egypt. [Katzenell] Do you think that Israelis can still visit Egypt, or should still visit Egypt as tourists? [Beilin] We are not calling on Israelis not to visit Egypt. [end recording]
