BFN [Text] Cairo, 30 Apr (MENA)--President Husni Mubarak, in his capacity as OAU chairman, has asked the United Nations to maintain and bolster its role in Rwanda. A Foreign Ministry statement said tonight that President Mubarak is currently holding contacts with the UN secretary general on the deteriorating situation in Rwanda and the role of the international organization in this regard. The statement said contacts with OAU Secretary General Salim Ahmed Salim are now under way, with President Mubarak demanding that the central apparatus for resolving African conflicts play an effective role in stopping the bloody confrontation between the government and opposition in Rwanda. President Mubarak also requested that all measures be taken to stop the deterioration in Rwanda. The statement said summit-level contacts between the OAU chairman and a number of African presidents will be held as part of efforts to contain the deteriorating situation in Rwanda.
