BFN [Excerpts] The Libyan foreign minister visited Paris twice during this current month. [passage omitted]. A report by Laurent Bazin, Eric Bourbotte, and David Cosset: However official it might be, the visit smacks of heresy. Twice in less than a week, the Libyan foreign minister spent a few happy days in Paris, staying at the glamorous Hotel de Crillon. The first time [Libyan Foreign Minister] 'Umar al-Muntasir stopped in Paris was when he was on his way to South Africa. The second time, for a longer period, was on his return from South Africa. The long weekend was organized by his old friend, Adnan al-Khashoggi, a Saudi businessman who specializes in all kinds of transactions between the West and Arab countries. [passage omitted] No official contact with al-Muntasir was made, the French Foreign Ministry stressed, but there was no reason to ban the visit: [Begin Juppe recording] He is the foreign minister of a country with which France has not severed its diplomatic relations. When he asked us to travel via Paris, as was the case today, I don't see on what basis we could refuse the stopover. [end recording] After a rest, the Libyan minister returned to Tripoli via Tunis.
