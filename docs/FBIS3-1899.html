Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington, March 2 (XINHUA) -- Relations between the United States and China entered a new phase last September, according to U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher who said President Clinton had directed him to develop a broader set of relationships with China. The U.S. secretary of state made the remarks this afternoon in the U.S. State Department during an interview with Chinese, Japanese, Australian and South Korean reporters, on the eve of his departure for a 10-day trip to Australia, Japan and China which begins March 4. "That new relationship is symbolized most importantly by President Clinton's meeting with China's President Jiang Zemin in Seattle last November," Christopher stressed. He said that he met with China's foreign minister last September in New York following Clinton's directive and the two countries agreed to develop a broader set of relationships. That resulted in U.S. Secretary of Finance Lloyd Bentsen, U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor and U.S. Secretary of Agriculture Mike Espy meeting with or having contact with high-level officials from China. "At the same time we sought to deepen our relations as we are two prominent and great nations with tremendous resources," added Christopher. "It's important we have more communications so that we understand each other's viewpoints and problems." He said that he is looking forward to meeting with Chinese Foreign Minister Qian and discussing a full range of topics which would include security, political and international issues, trade, non-proliferation and human rights. At the end of his fourth 10-day Asian trip Christopher will proceed to Vladivostok to meet with Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev to discuss developments in the Balkans as well as the tension created over the arrest of a CIA official who allegedly spied for Russia. Christopher also answered reporters' questions concerning U.S.-Japan trade and the situation on the Korean peninsula.
