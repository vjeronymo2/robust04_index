BFN [Text] Dushanbe, May 31, IRNA -- A Tajik Foreign Ministry official here Tuesday said that the talks between Tajik Government and the opposition leaders, due to be held in Tehran on June 4, has been postponed. The official, wishing to remain anonymous, also said that no exact date has so far been fixed for the new round of talks between the two sides. However, it may be held in Tehran on June 17, he added. Both the Tajik authorities and the opposition leaders had agreed to begin talks on June 4. However, the date has changed after the recent visit to Tajikistan of a United Nations envoy. In another develpoment, Iran Red Crescent Society has sent some 11 tons of humanitarian aids including food, medicine and clothing to victims of war in Tajikistan.
