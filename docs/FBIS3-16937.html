Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Secretary of the Northern Fleet's Military Council Yuriy Polozkov confirmed that the Pomor-94 naval exercises did in fact aim primarily to insure the coordination of moves in the preparation of a multinational naval task force for the imposition of a naval blockade at "hot spots" around the world. Polozkov was interviewed by Interfax following a REUTERS report on the exercises. REUTERS quoted the Chief of the Russian Navy's Press Center, Aleksandr Veledeyev, as doubting whether the exercises aimed to insure coordinated performance by the Russian and Western navies. Norwegian, U.S., British, German, and Russian warships are to take part in the Pomor-94 exercises in the Sea of Norway on March 21. The First Deputy Commander of the Russian Navy, Admiral Igor Kasatonov, has told Interfax the Pomor-94 exercises are part of a series of moves to enhance coordination between the Russian and NATO fleets. Admiral Kasatonov will be monitoring the exercises on behalf of Russia.
