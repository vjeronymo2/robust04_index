BFN [Unattributed report: "Italy in Support of Macedonia"] [Text] "The Prime Minister of Italy Silvio Berlusconi feels that the Greek embargo against Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] is irrational and that, as a man who puts the economy in the forefront, he can simply not accept this as a form of communication with another country," Macedonia's Prime Minister Branko Crvenkovski emphasized at yesterday's press conference, following his visit to Italy. Crvenkovski emphasized that Berlusconi had promised that at the next EU meeting on Corfu, he would express the Italian stance in relation to the Greek embargo, in front of the Greek government representatives. The Macedonian Prime Minister expressed satisfaction with what Macedonia displayed on a cultural plan within the framework of the manifestation "Macedonia in the Honor of St. Cyril," but also on a political plan, after the realized contacts with the high-ranking officials of Italy and Vatican. Branko Crvenkovski underlined that a particular emphasis in the talks was placed on Italy's economic participation in Macedonian and the East-West project, which, together with its sub-projects, such as the gas line and the traffic route, offers possibilities for a joint appearance of construction workers from both countries on the Albanian market.
