BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Boris Krivoshey] [Text] Moscow June 7 TASS -- Russia canceled economic limits on Abkhazia on May 24, 1994, bearing in mind positive changes in the Sukhumi stand on Georgian-Abkhazian settlement and the grave economic position of the Abkhazian side resulting from the armed conflict, head of the Russian Foreign Ministry's Information and Press Department Grigoriy Karasin told a briefing here today. "Certainly, corresponding issues should be settled with due account of our essential stand on recognition of territorial integrity of the Georgian republic," said Karasin. In his words, the United Nations Security Council's secretariat held consultations on cooperation in peace-keeping in Abkhazia late in May in New York. The consultations were held in the presence of Russia. "Their main result is the end to watchfulness as regards the Commonwealth plans of a peace-keeping operation in Abkhazia, and understanding of the need for close interaction between our peace- makers and the U.N. mission in the conflict zone," said the Russian Foreign Ministry's representative.
