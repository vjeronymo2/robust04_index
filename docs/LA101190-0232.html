October 11, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

BRITISH FILM FESTIVAL BEGINS

For the next 11 days, classic British films will be shown at the Fullerton Museum Center. The festival begins tonight with "Blackmail" (1929), Alfred Hitchcock's -- and the British film industry's -- first talkie.

Charles Bennett, who wrote the screenplay, will attend the screening and will answer questions from the audience.

The series will continue with Paul Czinner's "As You Like It," a 1936 adaptation of the Shakespearean comedy that features the late Sir Laurence Olivier's first screen appearance (Friday); "Blithe Spirit" (Saturday); "The Winslow Boy" (Sunday); Olivier's "Hamlet" (Monday); "The Ladykillers," a comedy starring Alec Guinness and Peter Sellers (Tuesday); "A Taste of Honey" (Wednesday); "Georgy Girl" (Oct. 18); "The Go-Between" (Oct. 19); "The Hireling" (Oct. 20), and Ken Russell's "Tommy," based on the Who's rock opera (Oct. 21).

Screenings begin at 7:30 p.m. The museum center is at 301 N. Pomona Ave. in Fullerton. Admission is $3 ($2 for museum members). Information: (714) 738-6595. The series is being presented as part of the Orange County Festival of Britain, a combination arts festival-retail promotion. ERIK HAMILTON
