BFN [Text] High-ranking Croat and Muslim political representatives agreed yesterday in Sarajevo on the release of prisoners and on a number of political issues related to the functioning of the Bosnia-Herzegovina federation, the Herzeg-Bosna delegation said on their return from Sarajevo. Zdenko Vranjes reports from Mostar: [Vranjes] The negotiations in Sarajevo were held in a good atmosphere. It was agreed who can represent the Croat and Muslim people at the federation level until new elections are held. It was also agreed that legal preparations should commence to form a government of the federation. It has still not been agreed whether the person holding the post of president of the federation until the elections should be a Croat or a Bosniak, nor what nationality the deputy president and the prime minister should be. This decision is to be made by the constituent assembly, which is expected to hold a session at the end of this month. Before that high-ranking representatives of the two peoples will meet again and discuss the remaining issues.
