941123 FT 23 NOV 94 / Gas pay rises 'outstrip others' Staff in the gas industry have experienced pay increases above the average for the whole economy, the Chartered Institute of Public Finance and Accountancy says today, Motoko Rich writes. Following attacks on British Gas for giving Mr Cedric Brown, its chief executive, a 75 per cent pay rise, a study by the institute found that gas industry earnings have risen 42.8 per cent since 1990 as opposed to 23.8 per cent in the whole economy. The study also found that among all the privatised utilities - gas, telecommunications, electricity, and water - employees have received increases above the average rate in all industries. When looking at weekly earnings, the study also found that in the economy as a whole 30 per cent of full-time adult workers earn less than Pounds 220 per week.
