Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, January 12 (SNARK) -- One of the active figures of the party at power, that is Armenian National [Pannational] Movement, Bagrat Asaturyan was elected as a new Chairman of the Central Bank of Armenia. Earlier he headed a parliamentary commission on social aspects and health care. A 38-year-old economist, graduate of the Yerevan Institute of National Economy, worked at the Institute of Economy of the National Academy of Sciences for a long period of time, defended a thesis for a candidate decree. President Levon Ter-Petrosyan who submitted the candidature of a new head of the Central Bank informed at the parliament session that Asaturyan "took an active part in arrangements concerning money exchange in Nagorno-Karabakh at the end of the last year". Armen Darbinyan, the former Head of the ''Armenintorg'' enterprise, was elected as the first deputy of the Head of the Central Bank of Armenia.
