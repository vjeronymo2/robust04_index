930219 FT 19 FEB 93 / Parliament and Politics: Higher ceiling for questions THE GOVERNMENT yest-erday raised the advisory cost limit for answering parliamentary questions from Pounds 400 to Pounds 450 - an increase of 12.5 per cent, David Owen writes. In a written answer, Mr Stephen Dorrell, financial secretary to the Treasury, also advised that the average cost of preparing parliamentary answers had risen to Pounds 94 for a written question and Pounds 218 for an oral question. He said the advisory cost limit, which was last increased in 1992, was 'intended to act as a threshold for disproportionate cost.'
