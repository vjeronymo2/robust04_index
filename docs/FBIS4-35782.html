BFN [Text] The latest security reports from the south say that areas in al-Nabatiyah al-Fawqa, the eastern hills of Kafr Rumman village, the outskirts of the abandoned Lebanese Army barracks in al-Nabatiyah, al-Wadi al-Akhdar, 'Ayn Bu Siwar, and Jarju' villages are coming under violent Israeli artillery bombardment from the occupation troops' posts in al-Zaffat. Citizen 'Imad 'Abd-al-Hasan Hallal, who chanced to be driving by, was killed and two others riding in the same car were wounded. In the meantime, Israeli warplanes have conducted low altitude flights and carried out mock raids over al-Nabatiyah and Iqlim al-Tuffah areas. More in this dispatch from National News Agency's correspondent Muhammad Ruba'i: [Begin recording] Tension reigns in the south, particularly in areas adjacent to the occupied border belt, and the resistance fighters are on the alert. Security sources have told us that at 1515 a rocket fell on the Yarin position in the central sector, where the occupation troops and their agents are posted. A number of personnel manning the position were injured. For its part, the enemy has placed its troops on alert in anticipation of any attack by the resistance fighters in response to its air raids. We just received word that the enemy brought a number of vehicles and long-range guns into the positions and hills overlooking the central and Western sectors. Three 155-mm artillery shells have fallen on the outskirts of Kafra village, in the central sector, near the village school. Since 1630, enemy aircraft have been flying at low-altitude over the south. [end recording]
