Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Paris, Jan 13 (AFP) -- United Nations peacekeepers in Bosnia-Herzegovina will face greater "risks in the near future", French Defence Minister Francois Leotard said here on Thursday [13 January]. Leotard was referring to UN plans to reopen the airport at the Moslem enclave of Tuzla and to relieve Canadian troops blocked in the Srebrenica enclave. Leotard told journalists that if no action was taken, "the risks will be even greater if we refuse to carry out the essential task of our armies, which is protection of human dignity." At Tuzla, the minister said, Swedish peacekeepers "need a Danish unit with heavy Leopard tanks and communications systems" to allow the airport to be reopened. Effective air support from NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organisation) warplanes was also needed, he said. The 12 tanks are at present at Pancevo, near Belgrade. They will have to skirt Bosnia to the west and be shipped to Split before reaching Tuzla by road, an informed source said. Earlier Leotard told the National Assembly's defence committee that starting the Tuzla and Srebrenica operations depended on UN Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali who on Thursday was due to meet his representative for former Yugoslavia, Yasushi Akashi. A parliamentary sources said that plans were underway for a relief corridor between the Dalmatian coast and the capital, Sarajevo. Meanwhile in Belgrade, Bosnian Serb chief of staff General Manojlo Milovanovic warned that NATO air strikes against Bosnian Serb positions would directly threaten the UN Protection Force. In an interview in Thursday's POLITIKA paper, he said: "Let them bomb, but by doing so they put the UNPROFOR [United Nations Protection Forces] units directly in danger, to say nothing of the Croat and Moslem forces in direct contact with the Serb forces on all the fronts in Bosnia-Herzegovina."
