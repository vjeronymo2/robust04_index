BFN [Text] Skopje, 25 Jun (TANJUG) -- Macedonian President Kiro Gligorov received Lord David Owen and Thorvald Stoltenberg, cochairmen of the Geneva conference on former Yugoslavia, at their request in Skopje this morning, an announcement from the president's office says. The talks focused on several issues of relevance to the situation in the region of former Yugoslavia and the international mediators' mission. These issues were also discussed at the European Union summit in Corfu, from where Lord Owen and Stoltenberg have just arrived. TANJUG has learned that Gligorov and the two cochairmen also discussed the dire economic situation in The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM] caused by the economic sanctions against the FRY [Federal Republic of Yugoslavia], Greece's trade embargo, and the situation in the border area between the FYROM and the FRY.
