Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 15, IRNA -- Special envoy of Pakistan's prime minister, N.D. Khan, discussed the Kashmir issue with Vice-Chairman of the Majles Foreign Relations Committee Mohammad-Javad Larijani here Tuesday [15 February]. Khan in the meeting said that Pakistan intends to propose a resolution on the rights of the Kashmiri Muslims to the future United Nations meeting in Geneva. Expressing grave concern over the condition of Muslims in Kashmir, he called on the Islamic Republic of Iran to support the resolution. Larijani in reply said the Iranian Majles would decisively support any efforts aimed at the restoration of Muslims rights in Kashmir. Majles is ready to offer constructive views for the preparation of a comprehensive and practical resolution in this regard, he added. Khan who is visiting several member countries of the Organization of the Islamic Conference, also held talks on the same issue with chairman of the Majles Human Rights Committee Sa'id Raja'i Khorasani later in the day.
