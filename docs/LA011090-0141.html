January 10, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

FOR THE RECORD

Fire Loss -- Insurance covered the majority of costs resulting from a 1985 fire at the Huntington Art Gallery.

A Friday article on conservation of fire-damaged art erroneously reported that about half of the Huntington's $1-million bill for cleanup and renovation was paid by government grants and that private donations covered the rest. In fact, the Huntington received an emergency grant of $30,000 from the National Endowment for the Humanities and a grant of $17,500 from the National Endowment for the Arts. Individuals, foundations and corporations contributed an additional $72,000. Insurance covered damage to the building.
