Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Director-General of the GATT world trade body has called for the creation of a new global grouping to replace the Western-dominated Group of Seven industrialized nations. Peter Sutherland said the new body should be drawn from developing and former communist states as well as the 24 developed nations in the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development. He was speaking to leading economists from Europe and the United States who are attending the World Economic Forum in the Swiss resort of Davos. Mr Sutherland said it was not possible to continue with the majority of the world's people excluded from global economic management. Meanwhile economists have expressed differences over policies for reviving the struggling world economy
