930401 FT 01 APR 93 / People: Non-executive directors Argyll, the UK retailing group, has appointed two non-exec-utive directors following the retirement from the board of Sir Cranley Onslow MP, a former chairman of the 1922 committee, and a non-executive director since 1983. The appointments increase non-executive representation on the board from four to five. Ann Burdus (above left), 49, joins the board from today, and will join Argyll's audit and remuneration committees. A well-known personality in the advertising world, she joined Ogilvy, Benson & Mather in 1961, before moving to McCann & Co where she rose to be chairman 1979-81. She is a former chairman of the Advertising Association, and is a member of the Top Salaries Review Board. She is also a director of Dawson International. Neville Bain (right), 52, chief executive of Coats Viyella, joins on May 1. Before joining Coats in 1989 he worked for 29 years in the food industry with Cadbury Schweppes, where he was latterly finance director and deputy chief executive. He will chair Argyll's audit committee, and join the remuneration committee.
