921217 FT 17 DEC 92 / International Company News: General Mills sees record year GENERAL MILLS, reporting improved second-quarter returns, expects to achieve record results for all of fiscal 1993, Reuter reports from Minneapolis. In a statement, Mr Bruce Atwater, chairman, said he expected another year of record sales and earnings. Second-quarter earnings at the leading US consumer foods producer and restaurateur, rose to Dollars 138.1m, or Dollars 0.85 a share, from Dollars 128.3m, or Dollars 0.76, a year ago. The current-year results included a gain of Dollars 1.9m, or 1 cent a share, from the sale of the Pycasa frozen prepared meals business in Spain, and miscellaneous restructuring charges. The restructuring charges involved the Betty Crocker packaged mixes production and European food operations. Consumer Foods' operating profits include a net pre-tax gain from these items of Dollars 19.9m, and corporate expense includes a Dollars 5.7m charge for the debt call. Second-quarter sales rose to Dollars 2.1bn from Dollars 1.99bn. Excluding revenues of Pycasa and the operations that became part of the Snack Ventures Europe joint venture with PepsiCo Foods International in July, sales rose 10 per cent. First-half net earnings advanced to Dollars 297.7m, or Dollars 1.82 a share, from Dollars 270.4m, or Dollars 1.63, previously on revenues of Dollars 4.12bn, up from Dollars 3.91bn.
