June 18, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OUTTAKES: TEAM PLAYER

Sly Stallone has a prison pic, "Lock-Up," due in August from Tri-Star. But he's not exactly Rambo-ish about taking on the summer competition. "Have you ever seen so much hype? Definitely scary," Sly admits. "I figure I should stay low profile. I'm purposely not going to be doing any sky writing."

The $24-million film, directed by John Flynn ("Best Seller") and shot largely at New Jersey's Rahway State Prison, is now being rushed through postproduction. ("We cut the film in just 12 days.") He calls it "the closest thing I've done to 'Rocky,' in that it's really an ensemble piece. My character is reliant on a lot of other people -- a support system." Pat H. Broeske
