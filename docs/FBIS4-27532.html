BFN ["A Country Where Power Is in the Hands of Popular Masses" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, June 2 (KCNA)--Hardial Bainz, national leader of the Communist Party of Canada (Marxist-Leninist), in his memoirs on the looks of the re-built Korea, which was published in a recent issue of the Canadian paper TODAY AND TOMORROW, said during his stay in the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, he could see at first hand the Korean people shaping their own destinies by their devoted efforts, and realized that Korea is, indeed, a country where the power is entirely in the hands of the popular masses and which was built by the people for themselves. The memoir says the United States that provoked an aggressive war and destroyed everything in Korea in order to block the victorious advance of communism after the Second World War, blared that Korea would be unable to recover even in 100 years, but Korea completed the reconstruction in a short span of time and has now set an example of human efforts and creation. The author wrote that through the visit to Korea, he knew that Korea has a mysterious strength capable of smashing imperialism and that strength is the singlehearted unity of the Korean people who are struggling for the national reunification, firmly rallied behind the party and the leader. The Nepalese paper DRISTI quoted the former director of the Organizational Department of the C.C. [Central Committee], the Nepal Communist Party (United Marxism-Leninism), Jeev Raj Ashrit, as saying in his impressions of Korea that the entire Korean people are feeling satisfied with and proud of the socialist life they enjoy and striving to realize the national reunification independently and peacefully.
