Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kingston, Jamaica, 15 Jan, CANA -- Jamaica and Guyana are exploring ways to strengthen cooperation between the two countries in trade investment promotion. The state news agency Jampress said the effort, which will include joint projects between the two nations, was the focus of discussions between Jamaica's foreign affairs and foreign trade minister, Dr. Paul Robertson and Guyana's trade, investment, and tourism minister, Michael Shree Chan, this week. Robertson pledged Jamaica's support for Guyana's efforts to develop trade and investment promotion mechanisms and to cooperate in other areas of natural interest in the economic sphere. The two ministers discussed the long-standing relations between their two nations and economic measures being undertaken to liberalise their economics and gear them to meet the challenges of the new global economy.
