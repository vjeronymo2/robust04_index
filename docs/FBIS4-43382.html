BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Alain Juppe said this evening that if the massacres in Rwanda continue, France will send soldiers to Rwanda: [Begin recording] [Juppe] Either the cease-fire is respected, or else the massacres go on. If they go on, then we cannot continue to tolerate this situation, and France would in this case be prepared to mount -- with others, with its European partners or with African states -- an operation to save the people. [Reporter] How could such an operation be mounted? [Juppe] You must surely understand that I am not going to spell it out for you here. France decided this morning to say that it is prepared to take part in such an operation, and we are now studying the modalities of implementation. [end recording] Bernard Kouchner, former minister of humanitarian action, left for Rwanda yesterday evening. He wants to encourage dialogue between the different factions.
