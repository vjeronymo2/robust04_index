940304 FT 04 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Progress at AB Ports' scheme Sir Keith Stuart, chairman of Associated British Ports, at the company's most important property investment project, Capital Waterside at Cardiff, part of the redevelopment of Cardiff Bay. The chairman said the scheme was making excellent progress. In the autumn of 1993, the Welsh Health Common Services Authority took occupation of Crickhowell House, their new 150,000 sq ft offices, and work was also well under way on 119,000 sq ft offices for NCM Credit Insurance on an adjacent site. Sir Keith said all sectors of ABP's business had done well: 'The prospects are now set fair for us to perform well on a sustained basis in the years ahead.'
