940411 FT 11 APR 94 / Harsher crime penalties opposed Harsher prison sentences would not cut crime, Ms Anne Mace, chair of the Association of Chief Officers of Probation, said yesterday presenting the association's annual report. Consistent policies aimed at gaining positive behaviour changes had apparently been replaced, 'by a populist sound-bite campaign, characterised by the slogan 'prison works''. She said that harsher penalties were largely irrelevant to crime levels, and the US was a salutary reminder of the limited use of more prisons to tackle rising crime.
