BFN [From the "Union Radio News" Newscast] [Text] Portugal and Venezuela have broadened even more their bilateral ties with the signing of three agreements that will be used as a frame of reference for future negotiations between the two countries. At the meeting held at the Yellow House, Portuguese Prime Minister Anibal Cavaco Silva and Venezuelan President Rafael Caldera signed three agreements on reciprocal investment promotion and protection; cooperation; and prevention, control, supervision, and suppression of illegal consumption and trafficking of drugs and psychotropic substances. The Portuguese prime minister explained Portugal chose to broaden ties with Venezuela because Portugal believes Venezuela is one of the Latin American countries with the largest credibility.
