March 27, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

KRAUTHAMMER

I wish to respond to Charles Krauthammer's column (Op-Ed Page, March 11):

Mr. Krauthammer, what kind of dream world are you living in? Your figures, I presume, are supposed to make us "feel good," but they are framed only by percentages of success revealed by the GNP and the metamorphosis of communism to the so-called democratic electoral process which is bogged down in tremendous complexity before international benefits can result. Meanwhile, our deficit-laden economy sits heavily on the backs of the people of the United States.

Our health has declined as has our educational system. Union busting by the previous Administration and deregulation in every phase of business accompanied by the lack of medical insurance, the stress and strain of husband and wife employment necessitated by rising inflation, our roads and transportation deteriorated, violence caused by economic and housing failures, military wastefulness, questionable incursions into other countries, all place the United States and the world in jeopardy.

Mr. Krauthammer, your peripheral thinking reminds me of the saying that "liars figure but figures don't lie." It takes courage to see things as they really are instead of twisting figures to make us "feel good." You do not recognize the need to change our priorities so that you can get clear on the difference between honest figures and what is hogwash!

HARRIET SCOTT

Coronado
