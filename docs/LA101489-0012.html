October 14, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

COUP ATTEMPT AGAINST GEN. NORIEGA

In response to "Bush Takes Residence in an Indecisive House of Halfway Measures," by Charles Krauthammer, Op-Ed Page, Oct. 6:

This column claims that the United States government should have intervened in the failed Panamanian coup. It viciously slashed at President Bush's lack of action in what appeared to be an attempt to overthrow Noriega.

I would just like to point out that the rebels were not freedom-loving democratic white knights. They were a bunch of power-hungry Noriega wanna-bes who wished to " . . . help him retire quietly." This was not a coup. Rather, it was a feeble attempt to wrest power away from Noriega by his own followers. This is clearly illustrated by the fact that they neglected to eliminate Noriega when they had the chance. These rebels did not want to change Panamanian society for the better; nor did they want to right the social injustices and cruel inhumanities wrought by Noriega's regime. They wanted power for themselves. They were motivated purely by greed.

Now I ask Krauthammer: Why should the U.S. involve itself in the petty internal affairs of some Latin American nation, when the involvement could only hinder American foreign policy objectives in Central America? Is it worth it to get rid of one "two-bit drug-runner?"

CHRIS LOHR

San Clemente
