Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 15 (YONHAP) -- GEC Alsthom, the Anglo-French company chosen as the priority negotiator for the Seoul-Pusan high-speed railway project, announced Tuesday [15 March] that it will include Daewoo in the TGV (Train a Grand Vitesse) Korea consortium, currently comprising Hyundai, Hanjin, Goldstar and Samsung. Daewoo, which said it had been assured a partnership role before Alsthom won the priority negotiating rights, later took the matter to court when it was left out of the TGV consortium. GEC Alsthom promised that all five Korean companies will receive full technology transfers according to the Korean share of the project -- out of a total of 46, 34 TGV train sets will be produced in Korea. The trains will be assembled and tested at Hyundai facilities: The engines (motor cars) produced equally by Hyundai and Daewoo: and the motorized carriages by Hanjin. The technology and production of components such as traction motors, bogies and electrical equipment will be allocated to specialized companies, Alsthom said. Goldstar and Samsung will participate in the signalling and traffic control systems.
