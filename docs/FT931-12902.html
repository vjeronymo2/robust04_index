930127 FT 27 JAN 93 / The Base Rate Cut: Gilts marketmakers protest - Timing upsets some dealers LEADING gilt-edged market-makers clashed with the Bank of England over the timing of yesterday's cut in interest rates. They said it caused disorderly conditions in the market and cost them about Pounds 25m. The marketmakers are a group of about 20 big international investment groups. The four biggest are SG Warburg Securities, Greenwell Montagu, UBS Phillips & Drew and Barclay de Zoete Wedd. The groups are upset that the rate cut came just before today's auction by the Bank of Pounds 2.5bn of 8.5 per cent Treasury stock maturing in 2007. Many marketmakers had already sold large quantities of the stock at relatively low prices to clients such as pension funds. The investment groups were dismayed that yesterday's events, which triggered a 1 point jump in gilt prices, will leave them having to buy stock at significantly higher prices at the auction. There was particular concern in the market that the Bank's move in cutting rates, made in accordance with instructions from the Treasury, may have soured Threadneedle Street's relationship with the marketmakers ahead of a big programme of gilt issues. The Bank will probably need to issue Pounds 1bn of gilts a week in 1993-94 to pay for Britain's large public sector deficit. A Bank official said: 'There is no way we can guarantee not to cut interest rates the day before an auction.'
