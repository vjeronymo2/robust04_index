940214 FT 14 FEB 94 / Observer: A Syron voice at the Amex Richard Syron has his work cut out at the American Stock Exchange, where he took over as head at the end of last week. A distant third to the New York Stock Exchange and Nasdaq markets, the Amex looks increasingly superfluous in a world of automated trading. Syron, 50, admits the Amex has had an unhappy few years: 'I don't think we've done as well as we need to do.' The former head of the Federal Reserve in Boston has no detailed plans on how to reverse matters, only a belief that the sort of auction market run by the Amex serves investors better than the dealer markets which are increasingly prominent in the US. Unfortunately, the Securities and Exchange Commission has steadfastly refused to favour auction markets. In five years at the Boston Fed Syron built a solid reputation as a tough, astute operator. Fixing the Amex will need all his energy and political skills - and then some.
