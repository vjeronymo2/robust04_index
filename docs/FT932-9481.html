930513 FT 13 MAY 93 / International Company News: First-term improvement at Turner TV group TURNER Broadcasting System, the Atlanta-based cable television group controlled by Mr Ted Turner, yesterday reported higher revenue and income for the first quarter. Net income totalled Dollars 20.14m, against Dollars 731,000, although the 1992 figure reflected costs associated with Winter Olympics coverage. The income figure for the latest period was struck before a charge of Dollars 286m following the adoption of the FAS 109 accounting standard on income tax. Revenue rose 9 per cent to Dollars 398.4m. Mr Turner said: 'The overall tone of the advertising market has been healthier.' Operating profits for the entertainment division, which includes TBS SuperStation and Turner Network Television, rose to Dollars 46.5m from a depressed Dollars 16.1m. Revenue advanced 11 per cent to Dollars 189m, due to increased subscription and advertising revenue. In the news division, which includes CNN, operating profits rose 23 per cent to Dollars 52.15m on revenue 15 per cent higher at Dollars 144.2m. The syndication and licensing division suffered an operating loss of Dollars 5.95m, against a Dollars 2.74m profit, due to a shift in the revenue mix from higher to lower margin businesses. Revenue fell 8 per cent to Dollars 50.33m. Losses in the sports division rose from Dollars 3.98m to Dollars 4.87m, on revenue of Dollars 1.17m, against Dollars 1.14m. On Wall Street, Turner shares were unchanged at Dollars 20 1/8 .
