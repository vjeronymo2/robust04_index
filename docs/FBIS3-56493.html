Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Lvov -- Mykola Horyn, Lvov Oblast council chairman and a candidate for people's deputy of Ukraine, said in a conversation with a UNIAN correspondent on 14 January that his stance regarding nuclear weapons differs from the stance of his brothers -- Mykhaylo Horyn, chairman of the Ukrainian Republican Party, and Bohdan Horyn, people's deputy of Ukraine, who are in favor of keeping nuclear weapons on our territory. Mykola Horyn proposed an approach to this issue not from a political, but from a technical and economic aspect, for nuclear weapons have not only to be stored, but properly serviced too. "These weapons have to be tested. But will Lvov and other oblast councils give permission for underground nuclear explosion tests to be conducted on their territories?" Mykola Horyn posed the question.
