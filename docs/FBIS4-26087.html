BFN [Text] Washington, June 16 (XINHUA) -- U.S. President Bill Clinton said here today that the U.S. would be willing to reopen negotiations with the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) if the DPRK cooperates with the International Atomic Energy Agency. Clinton said he is interested in hearing more about the DPRK's offer to let international nuclear inspectors remain in the country. He described the latest developments from the DPRK as a promising one.
