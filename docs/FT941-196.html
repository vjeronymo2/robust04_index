940331 FT 31 MAR 94 / Hopes rise for agreeing nuclear test ban treaty A comprehensive nuclear test ban treaty (CTBT) could be completed within a year, Mr Miguel Marin-Bosch, chairman of the United Nations negotiating committee, said yesterday, writes Frances Williams in Geneva. He said the negotiations, which began in January, had made faster progress than expected and he hoped for an initial draft treaty text by late spring 1995. A treaty could be finished just before or just after the April 1995 conference on extension of the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty. A number of developing nations have made a test ban treaty a condition of their agreeing to an indefinite extension of the NPT. Mr Marin-Bosch said there seemed to be broad agreement for the treaty 'to ban all tests in all environments for all time'. This would include so-called 'peaceful' nuclear explosions as well as nuclear weapons tests, though the possibility of exceptions was being discussed. The treaty would contain verification measures and provisions for sanctions against countries violating the ban. China, the only one of the five declared nuclear powers still testing atomic weapons, says it will stop tests after 1995.
