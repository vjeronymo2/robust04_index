Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Russian government's spokesman told Interfax that Andrey Illarionov was removed from the post of head of the government's group for analysis and planning by Viktor Chernomyrdin on February 7 for violating labor discipline. The government's personnel department stated that from January 17 to 20 Illarionov did not report to work and that he refused to give any explanations. It turned out that during that time he was giving lectures in Britain without informing the government about this. Illarionov's statement that he resigned because he did not support the government's policies was an attempt to conceal the real cause of his dismissal, said the government's spokesman.
