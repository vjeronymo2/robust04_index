Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Police have arrested two Chinese men in Chiang Mai for allegedly trafficking in 52.7 kilos of heroin, Pol Gen Chaovalit Yodmanee told a press conference yesterday. The suspects from Yunnan, identified as Pakasit sae Yang, alias Kwo-ong, 35, and Sumeth Meengarm, alias Ar-fu, 32, were arrested on Monday at a Chiang Mai department store along with the 52.7 kilos of heroin. Police said the two admitted that they had been hired to transport the drug, which was concealed in candle wax, to a dealer in Hat Yai, Songkhla province. Sumeth allegedly confessed to police that he had successfully delivered heroin twice before and received Bt80,000 in return, while Pakasit allegedly said he had done the job four times previously. Police said the two refused to identify who was behind the operation, admitting only that there were a number of people involved.
