BFN [Text] Progress towards a negotiated peace in Bougainville is reported to have taken a step forward with representatives of the Papua New Guinea [PNG] government and the secessionists signing an agreement to hold further talks soon. The agreement was reached at talks in Honiara hosted by Solomon Islands Prime Minister Francis Billy Hilly. Among those attending the meeting were the secretary to PNG's prime minister, (Brown Bau), and the secessionist international representative, (Moses Havini). Sources in the Solomon Islands Government said the meeting paved the way for a summit conference of Bougainville leaders representing both the secessionists and the PNG Government in Honiara next week. Solomon Islands radio said the summit will be part of the consultative process to establish dialogue in the lead up to proposed Pan-Bougainville peace conference.
