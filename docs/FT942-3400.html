940615 FT 15 JUN 94 / House sales fall by 10% over year House sales last month fell by more than a tenth compared with May last year according to a survey of more than 4,000 estate agents' offices handling about a half of all sales. The survey by the Ombudsman for Corporate Estate Agents (OCEA), covering 26 of the largest estate agency chains, provides further evidence of a sharp setback in the housing recovery. Mr Peter Constable, OCEA's chairman, blamed tax increases and the introduction of higher interest rates for fixed-rate mortgages for a 12.4 per cent decline in net sales compared with May last year. Sales were also 4.9 per cent lower than April this year. A sale is recorded if finance has been arranged, solicitors have been instructed and no chain is involved, according to OCEA, which began compiling figures in 1991. Mr Constable said new instructions were 2.6 per cent lower than a year ago and 6.5 per cent lower than the previous month. He said: 'The earlier froth has now been eliminated from the market by tax increases and by the hardening of interest rates for fixed rate mortgages.'
