Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The ceremony opening the first period of the military training year was held today in Granma Province. To declare Granma a Defense-Ready, Phase II province in 1995, on the 100th anniversary of the fall of Jose Marti in combat in Dos Rios, was the commitment reaffirmed on 20 February in Bayamo. During the ceremony kicking off the first training period -- which was attended by Division General Ramon Espinosa Martin, chief of the Eastern Army --Alfredo Enriquez Pina, who is a member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of Cuba [PCC] and PCC first secretary in Granma Province, said that great efforts are being made to attain the status of Defense-Ready, Phase II: [Begin recording] It is really very difficult; but it depends, basically, on the efforts -- and the results of those efforts -- that we achieve in Bayamo Municipality. [end recording] Bayamo Municipality, which will be the site of the Eastern Army's Military Council in the next few months, delivered on its commitment to be defense ready in 1994; it received the status of complier in the preceding year. Brigade members and militiamen were decorated at this ceremony dedicated to the 99th anniversary of the resumption of the war of independence.
