December 2, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: UNITED WAY REACHES 65% OF FUND GOAL

With eight weeks remaining in the 1989 United Way of Orange County fund-raising campaign, an estimated 65% of the $20.5-million goal has been achieved, officials said.

Campaign pledges have risen steadily since the drive's midway point and United Way officials say they believe the goal can be met if the community responds generously in the weeks ahead.

Campaign officials had previously expressed pessimism at the rate of giving this year. But recent tabulations from companies with large employee groups has been encouraging, said Campaign Chairman Ron Merriman.

Several companies have recorded large increases in employee giving, including Tarbell Realtors, with a 500% increase over 1988 results; Ciba-Geigy, with a 256% increase over last year's totals and Neiman Marcus employees recorded a 100% increase over last year. County of Orange employees have already raised more than $45,000 over their campaign goal.

The United Way campaign began on Sept. 9 and will conclude on Jan. 24. CARLA RIVERA
