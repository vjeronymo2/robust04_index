940202 FT 02 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Sharp turnround at Research Machines Research Machines, a privately-owned computer manufacturer based in Oxfordshire, returned to profit following restructuring and a change of business strategy. The company had been a leading supplier of computers to the education sector since its inception, but had been trying to expand into the business market. Now it has decided to refocus on education. In the year to September 30 it made pre-tax profits of Pounds 2.53m, compared with a loss of Pounds 6.6m for the previous year when substantial restructuring costs were incurred. Turnover rose slightly from Pounds 65.1m to Pounds 66.2m. Basic earnings per share were 12.4p (32.2p losses), or 11.3p fully diluted. Operating costs were reduced by Pounds 3.8m during the year as operations were consolidated on a single site. The company had gross cash balances of Pounds 6.6m at the year-end.
