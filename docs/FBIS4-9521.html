BFN [Text] Podgorica, 27 Apr (TANJUG) -- The Federal Republic of Yugoslavia [FRY], created two years ago, is no new state; its creation merely confirmed the will of the Serbian and Montenegrin people to set up their own state in a new way, FRY President Zoran Lilic stated today in an interview to Radio Montenegro. He said the current Yugoslavia "emerged under domestic and international conditions of general chaos." The creation of the FRY was a rational response by the Montenegrin and Serbian people to this chaos, Lilic stressed on the occasion of two-year anniversary of formation of the common state of Serbia and Montenegro. According to him, the founding, preservation, and strengthening of the FRY are values of such importance that their full meaning will only be able to be assessed in retrospect. Lilic also said that it is very important to preserve continuity in relation to the former Yugoslavia "both because of specific international rights, which will have to be recognized, and for the sake of the interests of parts of the nation outside the FRY." Most political parties, according to Lilic, have understood that they cannot disregard the will of the people when it comes to the reasons for the founding and preservation of the FRY, and its prospects. The FRY president stated that "sporadic disagreements" between the two federal units are only a consequence of the difficult economic blockade and other unfavorable circumstances. According to him, "the Montenegrin people have stoically suffered many trials and tribulations" over the past two years. Lilic said the harsh blockade had interrupted many positive processes in the FRY. However, even in these conditions many changes for the better have been achieved. "During two years of condensed history the hardened forces have maintained themselves and will be ready for the time when the sanctions are lifted," Lilic stressed, expressing the hope that this would happen soon. The FRY president on this occasion also repeated his view that a peaceful resolution of all the conflicts on the territory of the former Yugoslavia remains the only constructive option and firm orientation.
