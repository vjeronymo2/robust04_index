BFN [Text] Mr. ['Ali Akbar] Velayati, minister of foreign affairs, and Mr. [Romero Periz] Ballon, envoy of the UN secretary general, replied to the questions raised by the journalists at a radio and televised news conference held at the end of the Tajik reconciliation talks. Referring to the Islamic Republic of Iran's readiness to continue its efforts until the realization of peace in Tajikistan, Mr. Velayati said: The signing of the communique in Tehran should lay the foundation for the national reconciliation which is the most important part of the talks. Our country's foreign minister expressed the hope that with the signing of this communique practical steps will be taken toward the return of a just peace in Tajikistan. Referring to the progress in talks during the past two years, Mr Ballon, the UN secretary general's envoy in the Tajik peace talks, described the signing of the Tehran communique as the most complicated and most important part of the talks. The UN secretary-general's envoy said: We hope Iran and Russia will play their basic role in achieving a permanent peace in Tajikistan by giving their continuing support for the talks.
