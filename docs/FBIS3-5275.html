Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vientiane, March 5 (KPL) -- German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel, on March 3, met in Bonn his Lao counterpart Somsavat Lengsavat and exchanged views with him mainly on bilateral economic relations, reports Xinhua. According to the Deutsche Presse-Agentur (DPA), during the talks, Kinkel spoke highly of the open economic policy of Laos, and said Germany will continue to offer aid to help Laos' development. As for investment, Kinkel stressed that Laos itself must create overall conditions for it. Germany has provided development aid totalling some 80 million deutsche marks (about 4.72 million U.S. Dollars) to Laos since 1990. Somsavat Lengsavat is the first Lao foreign minister to visit Germany since the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries in 1958.
