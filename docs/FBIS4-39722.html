BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Yerevan 10 June TASS -- Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev flies to Tbilisi this morning, where he will have a meeting with Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze. Later in the day, he plans to go to Gudauta, Abkhazia, where the Russian 345th airborne regiment is stationed. Turning to a dispatch of Russian peace keepers to Abkhazia, Grachev, now on a working tour of Transcaucasia, said in Yerevan that the military part of the problem on sending a Russian peace-keeping force to Abkhazia has been fully resolved. The Russian Defence Ministry prepared for this purpose three motorised battalions out of two peace-keeping divisions deployed in the Leningrad and Volga Military Districts. They will be ready for air-lifting within three days, the minister said. The 345th airborne regiment stationed in Gudauta is also ready for the peace-keeping mission. Besides, the minister gave orders to the commander of the Russian Army group in Transcaucasia to prepare another three battalions for peace-keeping functions. According to Grachev, the question of the dispatch of troops to Abkhazia can be resolved only at the political level. Troops are ready to fulfill the peace-keeping operation.
