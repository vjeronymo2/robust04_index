August 11, 1990, Saturday, San Diego County Edition

Correction Appended

CHARGERS NOW FIND OUT: SHOULD HE STAY, OR SHOULD HE GO?

The Chargers open their four-game exhibition season tonight against the Dallas Cowboys with a simple mandate: Find out who can play and who can't.

If the philosophy sounds basic, it is. But it isn't easy.

Talent evaluation is a tricky part of NFL life, and with 80 players still in camp, this is the time of year it can be most complicated.

And it could be particularly so for the Chargers, a team with more new faces than most. In addition to the usual collection of free agents, many of whom were originally brought in as little more than warm bodies to fill out training camp practices, there are quite a few more expensive newcomers.

Primary among these are the team's nine veteran Plan B free agents, plus 16 draft choices out of a class of 17, largest in the NFL.

These are the players to which the Chargers will be paying careful attention in the exhibition against the Cowboys beginning at 7 p.m. at San Diego Jack Murphy Stadium.

"We've determined that of the new players, there are some people we really feel have a chance to make this team and be productive for us," Coach Dan Henning said. "We're not going to evaluate the guys in this game on what they think they can do, or what we think they can do. We're going to evaluate them on what they do.

"You pick them for potential, but you play them by performance. That is the only way you can tell."

The best way is in a game. And after three weeks of practice, including controlled scrimmages against the Rams and Phoenix Cardinals, that is what the Chargers will do tonight against a team that is coming off a 1-15 season, the Cowboys' worst since they were 0-11-1 in their debut year of 1960.

That led to a talent turnover that rivaled any in the league. The Cowboys were the most active NFL team in the Plan B market, signing 15 players. They also tried to boost their offense in the draft, taking running back Emmitt Smith of Florida in the first round and wide receiver Alexander Wright of Auburn in the second. The only problem now is that both are unsigned and will not play.

The Cowboys do have quarterback Troy Aikman, back for his second high-paid season and saying he is more comfortable than last year.

"Last year, everyone was more curious about what was going to happen," Aikman said. "This year, I can just go out and be myself."

The Chargers are looking for much the same from Billy Joe Tolliver, their second-year quarterback.

Tolliver had a rough time last week in the scrimmage against the Cardinals, throwing two interceptions. This game will continue his NFL education. While Henning is using the game to evaluate several new players, he also is laying groundwork for the season opener against the Cowboys in Dallas Sept. 9. Maybe nowhere is this more important than at quarterback, where the Chargers consider themselves talented, but where they clearly are inexperienced.

Tolliver will be making only his seventh NFL start of any kind. But even that little bit of game action almost qualifies him as seasoned veteran compared to his backups.

Tolliver will be followed by Mark Vlasic, who played in only three games in his first three seasons and missed last year because of knee surgery, and John Friesz, a sixth-round draft choice from Idaho making his debut in live NFL contact situation.

Henning said Tolliver will take about half the snaps, with Vlasic and Friesz splitting the other half. That does not necessarily mean Tolliver will not play in the second half, Henning said.

"What if we get only five snaps in the second quarter?" he asked.

Presumably, Tolliver then would also play after halftime. Henning has left that option open. There are a number of other things he wants to see tonight as well.

It's that time of year.

Charger Notes

The game will shown in San Diego on a taped basis on Ch. 10 at 3 p.m. Sunday. The radio broadcast will be live on XTRA-AM (690). . . . Tight end Andy Parker has two loose teeth and 12 stitches in his lip as the result of an altercation in Thursday's practice with linebacker Cedric Figaro on a punt coverage drill and will not play, Coach Dan Henning said. Henning said he expected also to hold out tight end Arthur Cox (back) and that running back Ronnie Harmon (dislocated finger), H-back Joe Caravello (coming back from knee surgery), defensive end Burt Grossman (shoulder) and nose tackle Joe Phillips (knee) likely will play sparingly. Sure to be missing are linebackers Leslie O'Neal and Junior Seau, the team's two remaining contract holdouts. . . . Fuad Reveiz and John Carney will alternate kicking duties, and John Kidd and Keith English will trade off the punting, Henning said. . . . Henning on wide receiver Nate Lewis, a seventh-round draft choice from Oregon Tech: "Nate practiced better against Phoenix than he played in the scrimmage (last week). That has got to be reversed. Not to the point where I want him to practice poorly, but the object is to play well in the game." . . . The Cowboys will stay on in San Diego for joint practices with Chargers Monday and Tuesday at UC San Diego.
