BFN [By ITAR-TASS] [Text] Yerevan, 1 May--May Day was marked in Armenia for the first time in several years. The central rally was held in Yerevan under the flags of the USSR and the Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic and with the portraits of Lenin. According to various estimates, from 10,000 to 60,000 people took part in it. They gathered on the square in front of the opera theater under the slogans: "A New Union With Russia is the Only Salvation for Armenia" and "No to Capital". Sergey Badalyan, the leader of the Armenian Communist Party, called for early presidential and parliamentary elections in Armenia and for Armenia to join the ruble zone. He expressed solidarity with the Communist Party of the Russian Federation, having said that "Zyuganov's party fully controls the political situation in Russia today."
