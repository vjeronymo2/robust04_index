Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council, received a phone call this evening from his brother Colonel Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi, leader of the Libyan 1 September revolution. The two sides discussed several issues of interest to the two fraternal countries, as well as developments that interest our Arab nation. They also discussed the developments in our country in light of the signing of the pledge and accord document and the efforts exerted to end the political crisis and face the challenges that are imposed on the march of unity and democracy in our country. The brother president reassured his brother Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi on the situation in our country, explaining to him that progress is being made to contain the crisis and tackle its regrettable repercussions. He pointed out that all the good forces in the homeland are pursuing efforts to eliminate the hotbeds of tension, implement the accord document, and adopt peaceful dialogue as a means for settling all disagreements and differences. During the phone conversation, the brother president referred to the successes the joint military committee has scored on the level of checking the military repercussions and making the necessary preparations to implement the military part of the accord document. The brother president expressed thanks and appreciation to his brother President Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi and the brothers in Libya for their sincere brotherly stands, which embody an eagerness to safeguard the march of unity and democracy in our country. He also praised the pan-Arab role the brothers in Libya have carried out to support our country and all the unity efforts that brought about the establishment of the Republic of Yemen on 22 May 1990. For his part, brother Col. Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi, leader of the Libyan 1 September revolution, voiced Libya's strong and uninterrupted support for Yemeni unity, stressing that stands by the side of all the good efforts that seek to preserve the unity march, given that it is a pan-Arab objective and a beacon for all the Arab unionists. He reaffirmed that Libya cannot but be with the unity and against all those who seek secession, or to harm the republic.
