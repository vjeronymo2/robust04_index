Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Geneva, March 9 (BTA) -- President Zhelyu Zhelev of Bulgaria today visited the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) in Geneva. He arrived here from Paris on his way back to Bulgaria. Conferring with Prof. Llewellyn Smith, director general of CERN, Dr. Zhelev expressed the hope that cooperation between CERN and Bulgaria will help this country preserve its scientific potential in the transitional period, especially in the field of high-energy physics. President Zhelev confirmed Bulgaria's determination to gain full CERN membership which has already been granted to some Central European countries. The Deputy Minister of Science and Education Prof. Boris Gulubov, who is accompanying the President, handed the CERN managers a draft update of the effective agreement on cooperation and expressed the readiness of the Bulgarian Government to continue the negotiations on the terms and the character of future relations. The Bulgarian president was also accompanied by Bulgarian physicists of the Bulgarian Academy of Science and Sofia University.
