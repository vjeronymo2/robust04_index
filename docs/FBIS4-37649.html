BFN [Excerpts] President Rabbani has expressed his readiness to hand over power to a council of people's representatives. He refused to hand in his resignation in order to avoid a power vacuum in the country and said: Because a council formed of nine jehadi leaders is not sufficient to find the solution to country's problems, representatives of the people will also participate in the council. After failing to achieve a victory against the Islamic state and the failure of his coup d'etat, Golboddin persistently insists that Rabbani was appointed to the presidency under the Jalalabad agreement, and thus will relinquish power after his term expires. In response, the Afghan president has said that he does not insist on staying in power, and whenever a council is formed of the real representatives of the nation, he is ready to hand over power to them. [passage indistinct] On the one hand Golboddin alleges that no government exists and therefore is not in control anywhere. However, the people of Afghanistan, and especially the people of Kabul, recently saw the broad gathering of governors and jehadi personalities from different provinces. It is not clear why Golboddin cannot see this real and complete power. [passage omitted] He accuses the council, which is to be held in a few days, and continues his crimes as in the past. In order to satisfy himself even for a few moments and to forget the bitter memories of all his weaknesses and defeats, Golboddin bestows upon himself the presidency of the state of Afghanistan, and declares, completely shamelessly, that he is ready to relinquish the office of premiership. It is not known to which premiership he is referring. It is clear that Golboddin knows what he means more than anyone else, and knows how laughable his words are. Why has a person who kneels before the dirtiest figure in history [Dostam] and does not refrain from subservience to others for power, agreed to step down from the premiership? The best thing for Golboddin is to learn from the past. Because the removal of Ahmad Shah Masud from the Ministry of Defense did not help him, he will not benefit from handing over power to the representatives of the nation. The nation's representatives can very well distinguish a national personality from a foreigner's servant and can separate good and bad. Confidently, the upcoming choice of a national council will be nothing else.
