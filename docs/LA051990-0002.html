May 19, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

WALTON OBVIOUSLY HAS A LOT TO LEARN

How can Jerome Walton (Morning Briefing, May 13) compare a professional sport to teaching? Teaching involves management and appropriate response to more stimuli per minute than most other professions.

Baseball players have some time to rest on the bench, but teachers, even at their break times, are making phone calls to parents or preparing for their class.

The staff of Children's Choice/McDowell Elementary Campus in Laguna Niguel invite Mr. Walton to come and experience a day with teachers and students.

With a lot of rest the night before, he may survive.

SHEREE ANN SCHUMACKER, El Toro
