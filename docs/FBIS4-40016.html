BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature: Compiled by Nikolay Zherebtsov, Andrey Petrovskiy; edited by Vladimir Shishlin] [Text] President Nazarbayev meeting Friday [10 Jun] in Alma Ata [Almaty] with the visiting Estonian President Lennart Meri said that the creation of a Euro-Asian union (EAU) does not envisage the restoration of the former Soviet Union. He said there are such fears since some politicians support the idea in their statements. This would be a great tragedy and would bring about great troubles and more bloodshed. Nazarbayev said that the EAU provides for civilized relations with the non-members. The Union is open for membership of not solely members of the Commonwealth of Independent States. The president said that China showed a keen interests to the idea. For his part, Lennart Meri said that Estonia was interested in the fact that prevailing on the Euro-Asian mainland would be the principles of the democratic development and any threats to regional clashes be removed. Proceeding from these positions, the Estonian leader said that he warmly welcomes the idea by President Nazarbayev about the creation of a Euro-Asian Union. According to Meri, Estonia would not like to fence itself from Russia and was prepared to develop the relations. He noted there are also problems in relationships with the Russian Federation, to which the president attributed the presence of 2500 servicemen of the former Soviet Army on the Estonian territory. Meri hopes, however, that the problem would be solved. He said that 31 August when the former Soviet troops leave Estonia, Germany and other countries, would an [as received] important European event.
