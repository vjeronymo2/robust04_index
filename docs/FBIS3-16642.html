Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The officer on duty in the Primorskoye Territory Interior Department told Interfax that the media reports of riots provoked in Vladivostok by the removal of mayor Viktor Cherepkov for bribe taking were untrue. The officer also said that no gunmen had taken over the mayor's office Thursday. Cherepkov and his deputy had been evicted from the mayor's office Wednesday. The officer said that as few as twenty, mostly elderly, supporters of Cherepkov, had been outside the city administration office in the early hours of Thursday. Igor Lebedinets, deputy head of the territorial administration, told Interfax that the former mayor's supporters exaggerated the importance of the event. An officer on duty in the Federal Counterintelligence Service said that the police, and not his agency, were handling the case. The official representative of the territory in Moscow told Interfax that the situation in Vladivostok was quiet and Cherepkov had been removed from the mayor's office the day before. Cherepkov could not be reached by phone. The territory representative said that Cherepkov had changed his telephone number on several occasions during his tenure of office. Now he probably simply did not want to lift the receiver. The official said that the exact whereabouts of Cherepkov were unknown. He had not been arrested but had been told not to leave the city.
