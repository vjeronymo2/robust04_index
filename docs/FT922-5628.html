920529 FT 29 MAY 92 / Hungary wins back from Major HUNGARY yesterday won backing from Mr John Major, the UK prime minister, for its claim to be the first in the queue of former communist states waiting to join the European Community, writes Philip Stephens in Budapest. A joint declaration signed by Mr Major and Mr Jozsef Antall, his Hungarian counterpart, included an explicit recognition of Budapest's ambition to be part of the EC before the end of the century. Mr Major, who arrived in Hungary after a visit to Prague, was accused yesterday by Slovak nationalists of intervening in Czechoslovakia's general election. He had said in the Slovak capital, Bratislava, that Britain favoured maintaining the federation of the Czech lands and Slovakia. Mr Jozef Prokes, leader of the secessionist Slovak National Party, was quoted as saying: 'The visit's taking place before the election was no accident.'
