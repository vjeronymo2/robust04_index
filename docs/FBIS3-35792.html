Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Zakharchenko] [Text] Seoul February 1 TASS -- The development of Russo-South Korean relations is "extremely important for strengthening peace in the world and, especially, in northeastern Asia," South Korean President Kim Yong-sam said at a ceremony today when Georgiy Kunadze, extraordinary and plenipotentiary ambassador of the Russian Federation to Seoul, presented credentials to him. He said also that he follows with great interest the pace of reforms in Russia and wished Russian President Boris Yeltsin a success in puting them into life. Kim Yong-sam expressed an intention to pay an official visit to Russia during this year. "It will be five years this year since my first visit to your country and I would like to visit it again," he told the ambassador. Kim Yong-sam visited Moscow in 1989 as a leader of the opposition Democratic Party for Reunification. He met Boris Yeltsin in Seoul, being a presidential nominee from the Democratic Liberal Party, during the Russian president's visit to South Korea in November 1992.
