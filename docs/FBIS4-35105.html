BFN [Editorial Report] Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish on 15 June, on page 10, carries an unattributed 600-word article on Chilean exports to the Latin American Integration Association, Aladi, and to other continents. The article says that during the first quarter this year Chilean exports to Aladi increased 19.8 percent compared to the same period in 1993. Chilean exports to Aladi during the first quarter totaled $465.4 million. Overall, Chilean exports totaled $2.5101 billion for the same period. Argentina, Brazil, and Peru are main importers of Chilean products. Exports to these countries between January and March this year are up 51.8 percent and 52.3 percent, respectively, compared to the same quarter in 1993. The article says: "The Foreign Ministry strategy to achieve agreement with the Common Market of the South, Mercosur, does not surprise us because it is a way to access this market. Exports to this group totaled $258.7 million, that is, 55.5 percent of our exports within Aladi." Chilean exports to the United States represented 20.8 percent of overall exports during the first 90 days of the year while exports to Canada and Mexico represent 23.3 percent, some $586.4 million. The article adds that Chilean exports to Asia dropped 5.4 percent during the same period. South Korea, Taiwan, and Hong Kong are among the main importers of Chilean products. Chilean exports to Japan during the first quarter increased 2.3 percent, while exports to the European Community amounted to $586.3 million, a drop of 9.8 percent compared to the same period in 1993. Germany, the United Kingdom, and the Netherlands are the main purchasers of Chilean products.
