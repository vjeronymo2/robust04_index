BFN [Text] Two soldiers were killed this morning south of the Erez junction when shot by terrorists from a vehicle which left the Gaza Strip and returned there following the shooting. The fatalities were reservists. One soldier was named as Moshe Bukhra of Netanya, while the name of the other fatality has not yet been released. The soldiers were deployed with a third soldier at a temporary roadblock south of the Erez junction when a Peugeot car suddenly appeared from the south and opened fire at them. The two soldiers died on the spot while the third managed to return fire at the assailants. The vehicle returned to the Gaza Strip. No chase was given because there were no additional IDF [Israel Defense Forces] soldiers there. The representative of the Palestinian police force in the Gaza Strip told AP by telephone that the Palestinian police force did not open fire, and said that from the point of view of the Palestinian authorities in the Gaza Strip the incident is over. Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] and the Islamic Jihad claim responsibility for the attack. Hamas said in announcements made in Gaza mosques that the assailants belonged to 'Izz-al-Din al-Qassam. A different announcement made to the foreign press in the Gaza Strip said they belonged to the Islamic Jihad in the Gaza Strip.
