Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Feb 03, BNS -- A NATO delegation is coming to Riga Thursday [3 February] on a two-day trip to meet with the Latvian working group for the Partnership for Peace program. The groups will discuss Latvia's signature to the cooperation program and concrete fields of Partnership activities for Latvia, like common maneuvers, defense planning, democratic control over defense forces. The guests are scheduled to meet with Latvian Foreign Minister Georgs Andrejevs. The mission includes Danish Ambassador to NATO Ole Bierring, German Ambassador to NATO Hermann von Richthofen, General Klaus Steinkopff and Colonel Richard Cohen. It visited Vilnius and Tallinn this week to discuss similar issues as planned for the Latvian visit. NATO's gradual integration plan for former Cold War foes, Partnership for Peace, has already been accepted by Lithuania, Poland, Romania, the Czech Republic and Hungary.
