BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Evgeniy Belovitskiy] [Text] Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk June 15 TASS--The chief Russian counter-spy said his service is ready to cooperate with Japanese colleagues in fighting international crime and solving issues of mutual interest. "Regional bodies of the Federal Counterintelligence Service have been o'kayed to establish direct contacts with their colleagues from the land of the rising sun", Sergey Stepashin told TASS on Wednesday after visiting the Kurile islands in the Far East. Stepashin said it is necessary to join efforts of secret services of various countries to fight international crime. His service has concluded corresponding agreements with 38 partners in other states. Eight new agreements are being prepared, including with China, South Korea and Egypt, according to Stepashin.
