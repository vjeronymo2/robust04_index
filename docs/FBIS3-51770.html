Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Quito, 18 Jan (PL) -- President Sixto Duran-Ballen today canceled his visit to Peru scheduled later this week because of diplomatic difficulties that have arisen in recent days between the two countries. Sources close to the president told PRENSA LATINA that the president decided a few hours ago to cancel his trip to Lima, Peru, and that -- for the time being -- no date has been set for his meeting with Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori. Ecuadoran Foreign Minister Diego Paredes recently said that Duran-Ballen's trip to Lima was subject to some diplomatic details that were being worked out, inasmuch as this was a very complex trip because the topics to be discussed were very delicate. One week ago, Fujimori told Ecuadoran newsmen that he was waiting for Duran-Ballen with open arms, but only to discuss topics on integration and economic cooperation and that he would not talk about the so-called "border conflict." Diplomatic sources feel the Peruvian president's remarks and negative reaction from various public sectors prompted the Ecuadoran president to cancel his trip.
