940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Grosvenor Inns seeks Pounds 5.2m for purchases Grosvenor Inns, the public house operator, is raising a net Pounds 5.2m through a rights issue and is buying six pubs. The USM-traded group also announced pre-tax profits up from Pounds 319,000 to Pounds 358,000 in the six months to November 27. Four pubs in Essex and Hertfordshire are being purchased from CH Webster for Pounds 3.3m cash. In the year to September 30 they made a pre-tax profit of Pounds 71,000 (loss of Pounds 24,000) on turnover of Pounds 1.27m (Pounds 1.2m). Two Hedgehog pubs in Hove and Southampton are being bought from Mr David Bruce, a director of Grosvenor, for Pounds 400,000 in shares. In the 15 months to June 30 they incurred a pre-tax loss of Pounds 21,000, against Pounds 11,000 for the previous 12 months, on turnover of Pounds 898,000 (Pounds 749,000). Grosvenor is issuing 4.87m shares at 120p on a 3-for-5 basis. Part of the cash will be used to finance the purchase with the balance going towards further acquisitions. The shares gained 7p to 170p yesterday. Following the purchases, Grosvenor will operate 37 pubs, of which nine are freehold and 16 free of tie. The result was achieved on turnover of Pounds 4.51m (Pounds 3.52m). During the period it bought six pubs. The pre-tax figure was achieved after exceptional costs of Pounds 45,000 relating to compensation for loss of office of a former director. Earnings per share were 2.9p (2.8p). The interim dividend is raised to 2.25p (2p) and a final payment of 2.5p (2.25p) is forecast. During the period Mr Bruce, founder of Bruce's Brewery and the Firkin pub chain, joined the board to develop a new subsidiary called Belcher's Pubs.
