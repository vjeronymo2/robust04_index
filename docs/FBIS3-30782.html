Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 4 Feb (TANJUG)--Following the latest incidents on the border between Yugoslavia and Albania, the Federal Ministry of Foreign Affairs has issued the following statement: "As the Yugoslav public is aware, the number of incidents on the Yugoslav-Albanian border, in which several Albanian citizens regrettably lost their lives while attempting to illegally cross into Yugoslavia, has recently increased. "In order to discuss additional measures that could be taken, in the spirit of respect for the international and bilateral obligations that both countries have undertaken to prevent border incidents, the Yugoslav side has recently proposed to the relevant Albanian bodies that an extraordinary session of the main joint Yugoslav-Albanian commission for border incidents be held in Belgrade." The statement goes on to say: "The normalization of relations on the Yugoslav-Albanian border would improve relations between the two countries, which the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, adhering to the principles of good-neighborliness, has as its lasting goal."
