931230 FT 30 DEC 93 / International Capital Markets: Italy scraps duty for bond trades Italy has scrapped stamp duty on government bond trading on regulated markets in an effort to retain business and prevent trading from shifting abroad, the cabinet said, Reuter reports from Rome. Italian government bonds remain subject to the 12.5 per cent withholding tax on coupon payments. Italy has a large secondary government bond market which trades about L13,000bn worth of paper a day. However, increasing demand for Italian paper from foreign investors has caused a boom in centres such as London.
