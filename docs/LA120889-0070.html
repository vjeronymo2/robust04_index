December 8, 1989, Friday, Valley Edition

COLLEGE BASKETBALL ROUNDUP;

ANTELOPE VALLEY STREAKS PAST SAN DIEGO MESA

Antelope Valley College switched to a full-court press and an up-tempo offense late in the first half and ran past San Diego Mesa, 101-72, Thursday in the first round of the Golden West tournament.

Antelope Valley held a 30-29 lead when Mesa's Marvin Prather made a three-point basket with 5:55 left in the first half.

But the Marauders (8-2) outscored Mesa, 17-5, the rest of the half and led, 47-35, at the break.

Antelope Valley then outscored Mesa, 20-4, in the first 6 1/2 minutes of the second half to lead, 67-39.

Demetrius Phillips came off the bench to lead Antelope Valley with 16 points. Paul Ernst and Tony Madison each had 15 points, and Dedan Thomas had 11 points and a team-high 12 assists.

Antelope Valley will play Golden West in a championship semifinal game at 8 tonight. STEVE KRESALMID-STATE TOURNAMENT Moorpark 94, MiraCosta 84 -- Moorpark blew a 22-point lead in the second half but survived to win the first-round game at Cuesta College. Sophomore swingman Roger Thomas, who led Moorpark with 27 points, was 10 of 14 from the field and scored 11 points in the second half. Point guard Sam Crawford, who did not start for disciplinary reasons, scored 20 points and had 10 assists and three steals. Canyons 80, Palomar 72 -- Nick Sanderson scored a game-high 21 points as the Cougars built on a one-point halftime lead in a first-round game. Forwards Aaron Clark and Larry Allbritton scored 16 and 13 points and guard Steve Valenzuela scored 10 points and grabbed eight rebounds for Canyons (4-4).WOMEN Pierce 56, Chaffey 55 -- Sophomore forward Carol Schoenmann scored 17 of her game-high 28 points in the final 14 minutes to lead Pierce in a consolation semifinal game of the Pasadena tournament.
