Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Max van der Stoel, high commissioner for national minorities of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe (CSCE), on Monday had a meeting in Moscow with Georgiy Satarov, aide to the Russian president. A source in Satarov's office told Interfax the meeting had focused on the position of ethnic Russians in various former Soviet republics, such as the Baltic states, Ukraine and Kazakhstan. Satarov said that, despite some obstacles, conditions had taken shape for solving these problems, because "the potential of political stability in the life of society has increased and good mutual understanding has been established between the Federal Assembly and the president, including on that problem." During his three-day visit to Russia, van der Stoel, who arrived in Moscow on Monday, also plans to meet Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, Minister of the Affairs of Nationalities and Regional Policy Sergey Shakhray, and the chairmen of the parliamentary commissions on human rights and international affairs, Sergey Kovalev and Vladimir Lukin.
