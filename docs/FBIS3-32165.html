Declaration for a Democratic and Sovereign Peru With Peace Based on Social Justice
Those of us who sign this declaration have begun talks seeking to generate a current of action and thought that will permit us to converge for the creation of a broadly based Promotion Committee in order to unite the left and people's movement. This committee will be integrated with representatives of political parties and movements; regional leaders; labor, neighborhood, and youth leaders; and progressive leaders. They will combine the efforts of Peruvian social and political movements to propose political initiatives and actions for a democratic alternative to the country. They also will represent citizens' aspirations for peace with social justice in order to fight the neoliberal project and Alberto Fujimori's authoritarian government's attempt to stay in power. This is a starting point for a dialogue and a new attitude for the democratic and socialist left. From this platform we want to promote the broadest programmatic debate possible in light of the latest changes in Peru and the world and in order to generate a new practice with people's and social forces. It also is the beginning of an intense collective reflection on our role in Peruvian national life in the past decades, a reflection with deep self-criticism on past mistakes and the commitment to overcome them in order to assume the responsibilities the people and Peru demand of us. We wish to call on all those who individually or collectively -- and from a socialist, democratic, and deeply Peruvian perspective -- might want to join our efforts and commit themselves to fight for a modern and efficient economy implemented with solidarity; for the full implementation of social and political rights; for the country's moralization; and for respect for human rights. Those who join us will be involved in the construction of a National Project for Development, Modernization, Decentralization, with Sovereignty and Peace, and Based on Social Justice in order to make our country really viable. To this effect, we will promote numerous regional and local events nationwide to discuss in an ample and democratic manner your opinions regarding the unity of the left and the people's movement within the national political context and the responsibilities of the current national situation. These discussions will be conducted with the participation of political parties and movement leaders, different personalities, intellectuals, social and regional leaders; members of this vast progressive and people's sector. These opinions will be studied in a session at the national level. We propose to hold it as soon as possible so we will be allowed to establish the proposed Promotion Committee and thus undertake a joint Action and Working Plan. In line with this effort to achieve the unity of the left, we hereby express our willingness to hold a constructive dialogue with other political and social organizations that will help establish a vast national civic movement for Democracy and Peace based on Social Justice. Lima, January 1994
