BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tamara Zamyatina] [Text] Moscow April 5 TASS -- "Deputies of the State Duma are thoroughly analyzing the draft agreement on civic peace in Russia and think it can be concluded," Duma speaker Ivan Rybkin told today a visiting U.S. Congress delegation headed by Congress democratic majority leader Richard Gephardt. Despite press reports claiming the Duma's unwillingness to consider the "intermediate document", the upgraded draft was submitted to the Duma by presidential structures on April 4 and has been analyzed by committees, commissions, factions and deputy groups, head of the duma press service Viktor Cheremukhin told ITAR- TASS. In his words, the U.S. congressmen were inspired by Rybkin's words that the civic peace agreement will be concluded by representatives of the three power branches and party leaders and remain open to all political forces of society.
