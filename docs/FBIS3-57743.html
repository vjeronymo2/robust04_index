Language: English Article Type:BFN [By BELINFORM for TASS] [Text] Minsk January 28 TASS -- Three lawmakers remained in the list of candidates for the post of the Belarusian speaker after consultations among lawmakers, self-nominations and refusal of some candidates to join the race. First Deputy Speaker Vyacheslav Kuznetsov told the parliament on Friday [28 January] that the three are Mechislaw Hryb, chairman of the parliamentary commission on national security, defence and anti-crime fight, Mikhail Marynych, deputy chairman of the state committee on foreign economic relations, and lawmaker Viktar Hanchar. Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich refused to vie the post. Hryb and Marynych were nominated by the presidium of the parliament which held a meeting on Thursday and which was attended by Kebich and heads of republic's regions. The presidium also nominated three other candidates -- Nikolay Voytenkov, head of the Gomel region, Viktor Danilenko, the ambassador in Russia, and Vladimir Rusakevich, deputy head of the Brest region. However, the three immediately announced that they quit the race. Hanchar was nominated by Aleksandr Lukashenko who initiated the no-confidence vote to former speaker Stanislav Shushkevich. Other candidates nominated by lawmakers included Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich, Chairman of the Party of Popular Accord Gennadiy Karpenko and Ambassador in Latvia Valentin Velichko. However, they all withdrew themselves from the list.
