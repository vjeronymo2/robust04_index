930417 FT 17 APR 93 / World News In Brief: Leyland Daf wins order for return of unsold trucks Leyland Daf, the UK truck company in administrative receivership, won a High Court order for the return to Britain of 210 unsold trucks which were shipped to the Netherlands over the Easter holiday without its knowledge. Contractors Edcrest and its Dutch parent company GM de Rooy, which are in dispute with the failed manufacturer over unpaid bills, moved the trucks - worth about Pounds 4m - from a compound at Leyland's Lancashire plant to de Rooy's base near Eindhoven.
