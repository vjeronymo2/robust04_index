Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA Mar 3 -- Singaporean Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong who is here for an official friendship visit to Vietnam paid a courtesy visit to party General Secretary Do Muoi this afternoon. The prime minister affirmed the Singaporean Government's policy to continue accelerating the cooperation and exchange of experience with Vietnam, and to encourage Singaporean businessmen to further invest in Vietnam. General secretary Do Muoi highly valued the Singaporean Government's assistance to Vietnam in personnel training and expressed his wish that the two countries will further promote their multifaceted cooperation, especially in the field of urban planning and management, tourism development and building of industrial zone and ship building and repairing technology. Earlier in the day Deputy Prime Minister Phan Van Khai received Singaporean Minister of Trade and Industry Yeo Cheon Tong and minister in charge of finance and transport Teo Chea Hean. Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam, for his part, received his singaporean counterpart. The Singaporean delegation also met with Minister Do Quoc Sam, head of the State Planning Committee, Minister Dau Ngoc Xuan, chairman of the State Committee for Cooperation and Investment, and Minister of Trade Le Van Triet. The two sides exchanged views on important issues in order to strengthen the friendship and mutual understanding, thus contributing to the acceleration of cooperation investment, and economic-trade exchanges between the two countries in the coming time. The minister in charge of finance and transport witnessed the signing of documents on West Lake project of straits steamship land, an economic cooperation project between the two countries in Hanoi. Some members of the delegation called at the construction site of the West Lake regency project, a joint venture between Lian Court group of Singapore and the Housing Investment and Development Company of Vietnam. Also today, Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong and his wife paid a floral tribute to President Ho Chi Minh at his mausoleum.
