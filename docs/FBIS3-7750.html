Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Brussels, Feb 28 (AFP) -- Four Serbian Galeb aircraft were shot down by U.S. fighters near the northwestern town of town of Banja Luka early Monday [28 February], a NATO military spokesman confirmed here. Earlier Admiral Chistos Liberis, the chief of staff of the Greek armed forces, announced in Athens that the four Serbian aircraft were downed 55 miles (90 kilometers) west of Banja Luka between 0430 GMT and 0500 GMT. The NATO spokesman said the Serbian aircraft had ignored orders to land relayed to them by NATO aircraft enforcing a no-flight ban over Bosnia-Herzegovina as part of operation Deny Flight. In Athens, Admiral Liberis made the announcement to journalists who were awaiting the end of talks between Defense Minister Gerassimos Arsenis and visiting British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd. In Sarajevo, UN sources said a Serbian aircraft was shot down by NATO aircraft over the central town of Zenica early Monday morning. The Serbian plane had taken off from Banja Luka, they added. A spokesman for the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees said the humanitarian airlift into Sarajevo and all movements to Serb-controlled areas were being suspended.
