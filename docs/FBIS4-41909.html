BFN [Text] The status granted to Ukraine of associate council member of the Central European Initiative could create favorable conditions for Ukraine to acquire full membership of this authoritative organization as soon as possible. During a meeting with journalists, (Volodymyr Lapytskyy), Ukraine's coordinator in this organization, stressed that Ukraine confirmed its willingness to take part in the work of the Central European Initiative groups on transportation, energy, culture, ecology, science, technology, and information. Ukraine pays particular attention to cooperation in the sphere of transportation. It is already taking part in the construction of the Ukrainian part of the Trieste-Budapest-Kiev Trans-European highway. The European Bank of Reconstruction and Development is financing this project.
