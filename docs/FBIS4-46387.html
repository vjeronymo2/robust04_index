BFN [Report by Najwa 'Abd-al-'Aziz] [Text] Counselor 'Abd-al-Sami' Sharaf-al-Din, Public Attorney at the Higher State Security Prosecution Office, yesterday decided to remand in custody members of a Muslim Brotherhood [MB] organization for 15 days pending investigations. He ordered the items seized with the members to be sent for examination by forensic and laboratory experts. Security men have arrested 11 members of the organization in several governorates. They include doctors, education experts, furniture dealers, students, and civil servants. Investigations conducted by District Attorney Sharif 'Abd-al-Nabi and Hisham Badawi with the organization members have uncovered secret cells formed by the organization in four governorates. According to the investigations, the new organization is an extension of the "Salsabil" organization uncovered a few years ago. The prosecutors charged members of the new organization with forming a consultative council, planning the overthrow of the regime, and preparing antiregime organizational documents and leaflets. The members used computers to keep information, and computer discs have been seized. The defendants informed officials on the remaining members of the organization in the other governorates. The prosecutors charged them with joining a secret organization that seeks to overthrow the regime.
