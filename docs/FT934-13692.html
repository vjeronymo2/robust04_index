931020 FT 20 OCT 93 / World Stock Markets: Brazil ends 3.1 per cent firmer as Mexico retreats NEWS THAT Congress would go ahead with Brazil's recently started charter reform in spite of an inquiry into corruption allegations against dozens of political leaders spurred the Sao Paulo stock market ahead by 3.1 per cent, following a fall of some 9.5 per cent on Monday. The Bovespa index closed at 17,665, up 529 points, in heavy trade. Mexico retreated on profit-taking, having set record highs in three consecutive sessions. The IPC index finished 30.98, or 1.56 per cent, down at 1,960.63. Volume was a busy 101.75m shares. Stocks have risen steadily over the past two weeks as investors have been drawn to an undervalued equity market and as the Nafta no longer appeared to be a paralysing force.
