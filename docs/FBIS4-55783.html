BFN [Text] Bratislava May 18 (CTK) -- If parliamentary elections were held now, 27 percent of voters would support for the Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS), according to a poll conducted by the Slovak Statistical Office (SU SR) whose results were handed to CTK today. In April, 32 percent of those polled said they would vote for the HZDS. The HZDS-led government fell in March following a parliamentary no-confidence vote in former Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar. The Democratic Left Party (SDL) with 16 percent support fares three percent better than in April. The Christian Democratic Movement (KDH), led by ex-Premier Jan Carnogursky, with 11 percent support also gained three percent against April, followed by the coalition between Egyutteles and the Hungarian Christian Democratic Movement supported by seven percent of voters. In contrast, Premier Josef Moravcik's Democratic Union of Slovakia (DEUS) with current support of five percent was the only party in the current government coalition to receive less voter preference than in April (seven percent). The Slovak National Party (SNS) and the Greens Party would each obtain four percent of the vote, the Social Democratic Party in Slovakia (SDSS), the Slovak Workers' Party and the National Democratic Party -- New Alternative (NDS-NA) each three percent of the vote and the Democratic Party (DS) and the Party of Slovak Entrepreneurs and Tradespeople (SSPZ) each two percent of the vote. Eight percent of the respondents did not know for whom they would vote.
