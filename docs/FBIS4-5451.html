BFN [7 April PASASON editorial: "New Step for Lao-Australian Relations and Cooperation"] [Text] By an invitation from His Excellency [H.E.] Khamtai Siphandon, prime minister of the Lao People's Democratic Republic [LPDR], H.E. Paul Keating, prime minister of the Commonwealth of Australia, led a government delegation to pay an official, friendly visit to the LPDR from 5 to 6 April. During the visit, H.E. Australian Prime Minister Paul Keating and his delegation were accorded a warm welcome by H.E. Prime Minister Khamtai Siphandon and the multiethnic Lao people. H.E. Paul Keating and the delegation paid a courtesy call on H.E. Nouhak Phoumsavan, president of the LPDR, in an atmosphere of warm friendship. It was considered a very significant event in the history of good relations between Laos and Australia. An official consultative meeting to exchange views between H.E. Khamtai Siphandon and H.E. Paul Keating was held at the Prime Minister's Office in Vientiane in an atmosphere of profound friendship and mutual understanding. During the meeting, the two prime ministers informed each other about the domestic situation as well as lines and policies in their respective countries, the lines and policies which serve as a firm basis for effectively strengthening cooperation for the benefit of the peoples of Laos and Australia, thus contributing to the common trend of establishing and promoting peace, stability, friendship, and cooperation among various countries in the region and the rest of the world. Noteworthy is that following the consultative meeting, the two sides reached an agreement on investment promotion and protection between the two countries. This agreement is considered an important basis for improving cooperation between the Lao and Australian peoples to a new level -- a level which will bring about ever greater benefits for the two countries. Following the meeting, the Australian Government pledged to continue its support and assistance to Laos. Regarding this, beginning in 1994, the Australian Government will establish a 47.5 million Australian dollar four-year aid project for Laos. In addition, the Australian Government has decided to give, through the WHO, gratis aid in the form of food worth one million Australian dollars to Laos. It will also continue to support and encourage more and more Australian businessmen to invest in the LPDR. During the visit, H.E. Paul Keating also attended the hand over ceremony of the Border Control Station at the Lao-Thai Friendship Bridge, which was built with gratis financial aid from the Australian Government and people as a gift to the LPDR. The assistance given to the LPDR by the Australian Government and people in building the Border Control Station as well as the Friendship Bridge and other projects in the recent past has substantially reflected the long-standing relations of friendship between Laos and Australia, which have fruitfully developed with each passing day. The multiethnic Lao people highly assess the effective assistance in various fields given by Australia, and consider it an important contribution to the development of economic infrastructure for the LPDR. The multiethnic Lao people also would like to wholeheartedly hail the glorious success of H.E. Paul Keating and delegation on their visit to the LPDR, a visit which will contribute to further strengthening the already effective friendly relations and cooperation between Laos and Australia for the benefit of the Lao and Australian people as well as in the interests of peace, stability, friendship, and cooperation for the development in Asia-Pacific and the rest of the world.
