Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Caracas, 1 Mar (NOTIMEX) -- Today, President Rafael Caldera denied Venezuela supports the candidacy of Colombian President Cesar Gaviria Trujillo for OAS secretary general. According to sources at the government palace, Gaviria asked Caldera to support his candidacy, but the Venezuelan president said the country has its own candidate, whose qualities measure up to the needs of the hemisphere. For several months, Venezuela has promoted the candidacy of Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli Rivas to this post. A Foreign Ministry spokesman affirmed Burelli will not only maintain his candidacy, but will also "fight for it until the second or third round" of votes at OAS headquarters in Washington. The Venezuelan delegation to the OAS sent a statement to the evaluation committee noting Burelli Rivas as the "sole option to renewing the system." The communique added that with Venezuela, the inter-American system would have an OAS which is much more active and committed to the objectives and principles of its charter. Political analysts feel the Venezuelan foreign minister would withdraw his candidacy to avoid a resounding defeat by Gaviria or Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus, who seems to have received the support of Central America and the Caribbean.
