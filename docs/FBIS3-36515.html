Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By correspondent M. Vardzelashvili from Tbilisi] [Text] The reaction in political circles and among the population in Georgia to Boris Yeltsin's visit to the republic is not unequivocal. However, most politicians and inhabitants of Georgia assess positively the signing of the bilateral treaty with Russia. The Department for Sociological Studies at the Georgian Academy of Sciences and also leading republic's newspapers have published the results of the sociological poll of the population following the Russian president's visit to Georgia. According to the poll results, 67 percent of respondents assess positively the normalization of Russian-Georgian relations; 10 percent of those polled view the visit by the Russian delegation negatively. As for the rest, they find it difficult to define their position. At the same time, 90 percent of respondents believe the establishment of good-neighborly relations with Russia is in Georgia's state interests.
