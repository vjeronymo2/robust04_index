921029 FT 29 OCT 92 / World Trade News: Honda luxury car planned for Korea Daewoo Motor said yesterday it planned to produce a modified Korean version of Honda's luxury car Legend by the end of 1993, writes John Burton from Seoul. Mr Kim Woo-choong, Daewoo chairman, met Honda officials this week in Tokyo to discuss details about the possible production of the Legend for the Korean market. Daewoo said yesterday it could not confirm whether a licensing agreement had yet been signed. Daewoo, South Korea's third largest car maker, would become the third competitor in the country's luxury car market. Daewoo, an unlisted company, recorded an estimated loss of Won50bn (Pounds 37m) in the first half of this year and has an enormous debt/equity ratio of over 2,000 per cent.
