BFN [Text] My visit was a result of very important and specific changes in both our countries, Indonesian Foreign Minister Ali Alatas said at a news conference today prior to his departure. He pointed out that in addition to developing trade and economic relations, the two countries must seek together third markets in their regions, the Balkans and the Far East. Regarding the conflict in Bosnia-Herzegovina, the guest said that the ethnic basis of the conflict must not be ignored and that Indonesia supports dividing Bosnia-Herzegovina into three ethnic parts -- provided it is done in a just way and in accordance with the country's independence.
