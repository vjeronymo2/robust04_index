DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION Federal Aviation Administration 14 CFR Part 39 [Docket No. 94&hyph;CE&hyph;15&hyph;AD] Airworthiness Directives; American General Aircraft Corporation Models AA&hyph;5, AA&hyph;5A, AA&hyph;5B, and AG&hyph;5B Airplanes AGENCY: Federal Aviation Administration, DOT. ACTION: Notice of proposed rulemaking (NPRM).

SUMMARY: This document proposes to adopt a new airworthiness directive (AD) that would apply to American General Aircraft Corporation (American General) Models AA&hyph;5, AA&hyph;5A, AA&hyph;5B, and AG&hyph;5B airplanes. The proposed action would require inspecting (one-time) the wing attach shoulder bolts for fretting, scoring, wear, excessive clearance, or enlarged or elongated mounting holes (known as damage from hereon), replacing any damaged parts and repairing any damaged areas, and reporting to the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) the results of the inspection. Four reports of wing attach shoulder bolt failure prompted the proposed action. The actions specified by the proposed AD are intended to prevent wing attach shoulder bolt failure, which could lead to structural damage of the wing/fuselage to the point of failure. Discussion The FAA has received four reports (three in England and one in the United States) of wing attach shoulder bolt failure on American General Models AA&hyph;5, AA&hyph;5A, AA&hyph;5B, and AG&hyph;5B airplanes. Investigation reveals that excessive wing to center spar clearance could have contributed to the bolt failures; however, in each of the four instances, the bolts failed prior to reaching the service life of 7,250 hours time-in- service (TIS). The FAA has determined that, in order to assure the safety of these airplanes, the established service life of these bolts needs review.
