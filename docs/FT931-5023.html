930309 FT 09 MAR 93 / Howe urges a hard line on economic direction A STRONG plea for the government to re-establish a clear direction for its economic strategy with Budget tax increases and spending curbs is made today by Lord Howe. The former chancellor and foreign secretary warns in an article in the Financial Times that a 'Reaganomics-style' budget deficit threatens Britain with a return to the stop-go economic cycles of the 1960s and 1970s. Urging Mr Norman Lamont to eschew populist measures in favour of widening the tax base and curbing tax privileges, Lord Howe says: 'A hard-headed attempt to explain and address the real problems of our economy is more likely to reap political reward than playing to the gallery and hoping for the best.'
