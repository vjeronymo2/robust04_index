Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Kutakhov] [Text] Tokyo January 25 TASS -- Russia and Japan have reached agreement on the concrete dates for holding meetings of the working team, charged with the preparation of a peace treaty between the two states, and consultations at the level of deputy foreign ministers. ITAR-TASS has learned from sources within the Japanese Foreign Ministry here today that the meetings will take place in Moscow from February 21-23. The Russian delegation will be led by Deputy Foreign Minister Aleksandr Panov, and the Japanese -- by his counterpart Hiroshi Fukuda. The bilateral working team, charged with the elaboration of a peace treaty, which is also discussing the South Kurils territorial issue, is to meet for the first time after President Yeltsin's visit to Tokyo last October.
