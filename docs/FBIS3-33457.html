Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Chilean Foreign Minister Enrique Silva Cimma this afternoon disclosed Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada's official response to the invitation conveyed to him by Chilean President Patricio Aylwin to attend the Chilean presidential inauguration. Sanchez de Lozada says that it is not still advisable for a Bolivian president to attend an official ceremony. He added that his wife Ximena Iturralde de Sanchez de Lozada will represent him. Silva Cimma read to the press Sanchez de Lozada's reply: [Begin Silva Cimma recording] He says that for reasons that are not beyond President Aylwin's illustrious acknowledgement, a visit by a Bolivian president to Chile to attend an official ceremony is not yet advisable. He has, however, empowered his wife, Mrs. Ximena Iturralde de Sanchez de Lozada, to represent him as a proof of his consideration and personal esteem toward their excellencies President Aylwin and President Frei Ruiz Tagle. [end recording] Sanchez de Lozada also conveyed thanks for the invitation conveyed to him by his Chilean counterpart Patricio Aylwin, and wished the best success to future President Eduardo Frei Ruiz-Tagle. Silva Cimma said it is not suitable to speculate on this response which, according to him, does not affect bilateral relations. [Begin Silva Cimma recording] I hope this letter will not lead to speculations, considering that the reply to the invitation was made in a very delicate way and in terms that I think are self- explanatory. Therefore, I am simply announcing the official report, and that is it. [end recording] Diplomatic relations between Chile and Bolivia were disrupted after the Pacific War of the last century, when Bolivia lost its outlet to the Pacific Ocean.
