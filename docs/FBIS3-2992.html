Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, March 15 (XINHUA) -- The Eighth Sino-Japanese Economic and Trade Consultative Conference was held here today. The regular meeting -- the series was inaugurated in 1985 -- focuses on economic and trade relations between the two countries. A representative of China's State Planning Commission gave a brief report about the country's economic system reform and a representative from Japan's Ministry of International Trade and Industry reported on the Japanese economy. Wang Zukang, director of the Shanghai Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation Commission, gave a report on the city's foreign trade and the development of Pudong, one of China's largest economic development zones. During the meeting, the participants from the two countries also discussed cooperation in machinery, hardware, light industry, grain and animal products.
