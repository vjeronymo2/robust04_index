Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Refugee Affairs Minister Walid Junblatt, speaking to the QATARI NEWS AGENCY after a meeting with Qatari Amir Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani, announced that he is committed to the al-Ta'if Accord, wants to see it improved, and does not want a return to the pre-accord situation. [passage omitted] Asked to comment on a connection between the Syrian presence in Lebanon and the situation in the south, he said there is no such connection. He added: Since there is a clear international decision to implement UN Security Council Resolution 425, let the major powers pressure Israel to implement it. He said the Syrian presence in Lebanon is an issue that concerns only the Lebanese and Syrian Governments. Commenting on the peace process, Junblatt asserted that there can be no peace between the strong and the weak. He asked: Could 'Arafat regain Jerusalem, get rid of the settlements, and establish a state on his own? Junblatt said there are ambiguous points in Oslo's secret agreement and Washington's public agreement. [passage omitted]
