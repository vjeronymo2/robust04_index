BFN [Text] Budapest, 14 June (MTI) -- Hungarian Prime Minister Peter Boross sent a telegram to Austrian Chancellor Franz Vranitzky expressing his good wishes on the occasion that the vast majority of the Austrian people opted to join the European Union. Foreign Minister Geza Jeszenszky on Monday [13 June] congratulated by letter Austrian counterpart Alois Mock, on the referendum result. The letter, sent to MTI on Tuesday [14 June], says, "The outcome of the referendum is a clear signal to the whole of Europe. Both Austria and the Union stand to benefit from Austria's membership, which will also promote European unification. To Hungary, it is particularly important that neighboring Austria will be in the European Union." Jeszenszy believes Hungary will be able to count on Austria's support in the EU. Hungary, which has applied to join, is confident that both countries will jointly help build a single Europe.
