BFN [From the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] The executive branch has instructed Fogade [Deposit Guarantee and Banking Protection Fund] to immediately pay up to 4 million bolivares to depositors of the eight banks intervened by government decree, in accordance with Article 23 of the special law protecting depositors and regulating emergencies at banking institutions. The measure was adopted at a special Council of Ministers meeting at Miraflores Palace, where the issue was examined in barely 10 minutes. The ministers interpreted the text of the law, according to which it is not possible to wait until the banks in question are rehabilitated or their debts settled to return depositors' funds. The announcement was made by Finance Minister Julio Sosa Rodriguez, who said the 13-article decree explains the situation. Sosa has announced the General Bank Superintendency, Fogade, and the interveners of the eight banks, who were discussing payment schedules, have already been notified of this decision. [Begin Sosa recording] Payment of the 4 million bolivares will begin, to the extent possible, on 27 June. The depositors are entitled to 4 million bolivares whether a bank is liquidated or rehabilitated, and there is no reason to subject them to delays of up to 30 days, which is how long it takes to make that decision. There is no reason to subject depositors to that deadline. [end recording] Sosa has denied the wave of rumors concerning alleged crises at other banks. He said such remarks are unfounded and greatly harm the country. Sosa has urged the Venezuelan people not to spread such rumors because foreigners, willing to invest in the country, purchase Venezuelan Government and private institutions' assets when conditions are good and a good effort could be wasted.
