921219 FT 19 DEC 92 / International Company News: Alcoa to take Dollars 1.2bn charge ALUMINUM Company of America (Alcoa), the world's largest aluminium producer, yesterday said it would take after-tax charges of Dollars 1.286bn, or Dollars 15.06 a share, for the adoption of new accounting standards and for restructuring. Alcoa, in common with many other US companies, is adopting Financial Accounting Standard (FAS) 106 and FAS 109 ahead of the first-quarter 1993 deadline. Charges of about Dollars 1.183bn, or Dollars 13.85, for the adoption of FAS 106 will apply to the years before 1992. Alcoa also announced 1,100 redundancies as part of a redesign of some manufacturing and administrative processes.
