BFN [Text] In order to make it more convenient for Chinese in southwest China to visit Thailand and following the approval of the Chinese Foreign Ministry, the Thai Foreign Ministry decided to open a consulate general in Kunming City of Yunnan Province on 1 July. The consulate general will be responsible for issuing entry visas for Chinese living in Yunnan and nearby provinces so that those Chinese who want to travel to Thailand for sightseeing or visiting their relatives will not have to travel a long distance to apply for a visa from the Thai Embassy in Beijing. Silachai Sulai, director of the Chiang Mai Branch of the Tourism Authority of Thailand, says that the establishment of the Thai consulate general in Kunming will help promote the development of a quadrangular economic, cultural, and tourism cooperation among Thailand, Burma, Laos, and China.
