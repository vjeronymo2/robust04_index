Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 17 Feb--Addressing a meeting of the Senate Foreign Affairs Commission today, Andrzej Olechowski, minister of foreign affairs, presented the main assumptions of Poland's eastern policy. Olechowski said that the policy toward East Europe should focus on developing good relations with countries important from the point of view of Polish political and economic interests and the country's security. "Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, the Baltic states, Moldova, and Kazakhstan form the group of states that are of particular interest to us," Olechowski said. According to the minister, support for political and military stability in the region and the creation of new possibilities for beneficial economic cooperation are among the important goals of Poland's eastern policy.
