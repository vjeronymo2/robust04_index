Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The second power unit at the Ignalina power station was put back into operation last night. Genadiy Negrivoda, chief engineer at the nuclear power station, told the Baltic News Service correspondent that all the work was carried out smoothly and according to the planned schedule. Today the second power unit is operating with the capacity of 700 MW and the first unit at 1,000 MW. According to Mr. Negrivoda this amount of power is sufficient for the needs of consumers, therefore, neither of the units is opearting at the maximum capacity of 1,300 MW. [Passage omitted] Mr. Negrivoda also said that the first unit of the nuclear power station is to be shut down at the beginning of April for routine maintenance. This year as a result of minor malfunction the first and the second reactors at the Ignalina nuclear station were shut down on 18 and 29 of January.
