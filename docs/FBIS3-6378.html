Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 17 (YONHAP) -- The chairman of the ruling Democratic Liberal Party, Kim Chong-pil, will visit China in the second half of this year at the invitation of the Chinese Communist Party, preceded by DLP Secretary-general Mun Chong-su, who may travel to Beijing as early as April. DLP Deputy Secretary-general Cho Pu-yong met with the deputy head of the International Liaison Department of the Chinese Communist Party, Zhu Shanqing, on Thursday here to discuss expanding cooperation and exchanges. At the working-level meeting, the two sides agreed that the DLP will send a five- to seven-member mission led by Secretary-general Mun to China as early as April. But DLP Chairman Kim's visit to Beijing, which was scheduled for the first half of the year, has been postponed to the second half because President Kim Yong-sam will go to China soon. In return for Mun's visit to China, Beijing will send a high-level delegation to Seoul headed by a party Politburo member or cabinet minister.
