941203 FT 03 DEC 94 / Weekend Money (Budget Analysis): Your budget Income tax: No change in 20p, 25p or 40p rates. From next April, 20p lower band extended by Pounds 200 to Pounds 3,200. Personal allowance indexed (to Pounds 3,525); threshold for higher rate tax increases to Pounds 24,300. Age-related allowances increase above inflation. Personal allowance for those aged 65-74 will rise to Pounds 4,630, 75 and over to Pounds 4,800. Married couple's allowance*: Relief to be restricted to 15 per cent (from 20 per cent). But married couple's age allowance will rise for those aged 65-74 to Pounds 2,995 and for those aged 75 and over to Pounds 3,035. Income limit for age-related allowances will increase to Pounds 14,600; all from next year. VAT-on-fuel* compensation package: Help package to cuahion effects of increase of VAT on fuel - from 8 per cent to 17.5 per cent from April 1 - to be extended. Home Energy Efficiency Scheme basket of grants increases by Pounds 10m. Cold-weather payments rise to Pounds 8.50 per week from Pounds 6 two years ago. Single pensioners will receive Pounds 52 more in their retirement pensions while couples will receive Pounds 73 beginning 1995-96. From April 1996 there will be Pounds 68 extra on the single pensioner rate and Pounds 96 for pensioner couples. National insurance: Employers will earn an NI 'holiday' of up to a year by taking on people unemployed for two years or more, from April 1996. Capital gains tax: Exempt amount increased from Pounds 5,800 to Pounds 6,000 a year and inheritance tax threshold increased from Pounds 150,000 to Pounds 154,000, both from April 6 1995. Tobacco and alcohol: Tax on a packet of 20 cigarettes to be increased by 10p. No change in the level of duties on beer, table wine and spirits. Fuel and vehicle taxes: Petrol taxes up immediately by 2.5p a litre for leaded and unleaded petrol. Tax on diesel up 3p a litre. Vehicle excise duty on cars will rise by Pounds 5 to Pounds 135. Miras*: Tax relief on mortgage interest on first Pounds 30,000 of mortgage cut from 20 per cent to 15 per cent from next April. Housing benefit: Rules to be changed so cost of benefit will not be reimbursed to local authorities for rents that are significantly higher than local averages. Business taxation: No change to Advance Corporation Tax, Corporation Tax or Capital Allowances. Recovery of VAt to be allowed on business lease car purchases. Small business support: Threshold for business VAT registration to be increased to Pounds 46,000. Thirty per cent increase in threshold for monthly PAYE payments for businesses, taking in two-thirds of all businesses. Business rates: Rises as a result of revaluation limited to 10 per cent for large businesses/buildings and 7.5 per cent for small ones. Tax avoidance: Measures to tighten some loopholes, including avoidance of VAT through property transactions and share issues, or operations with discounted securities. * Scheduled in previous budget.
