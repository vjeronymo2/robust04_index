BFN [From the "Focus on Africa" program] [Text] Talks between Malawi's two main opposition parties seem to have come to fruition today. The former ruling Malawi Congress Party, MCP, which was ousted in last month's elections, used to be the bete noire of the Alliance for Democracy, Aford. Aford's leader, Chakufwa Chihana, was jailed for several years under Dr. Banda and was at the forefront of the campaign for a multiparty system, but both lost out to the UDF [United Democratic Front], who formed the new government. After weeks of talks, the MCP and Aford have signed a memorandum of understanding. It was announced at a press conference at a Lilongwe hotel this afternoon. Willie Zingane was there. On the line Chris Green asked him what the memorandum says. [Begin recording] [Zingane] It appears that the two parties in opposition would like to stick together in checking some of the issues that maybe the ruling United Democratic Front would be following, but the two leaders that signed the memorandum are Chakufwa Chihana, president of Aford, and vice president of the Malawi Congress Party, Gwanda Chakwamba, said that they saw the danger of tribalism in Malawi, and this could spark into violence. [Green] What was the atmosphere like in today's announcement? [Zinane] Well, from the people who have been at the conference, they belong to Malawi Congress Party and Aford, and so they were cheering the thing, but from outside, the United Democratic Front have also got their side of the story, and they feel that the two parties are just coming together to try and give them tough time in parliament. [Green] There must be people inside Aford who regard talking to the Malawi Congress Party as akin to talking to the devil. [Zingane] Yes, there are. There has been a rumor that some senior members of Aford didn't attend today's function because they are not happy, because this is the same party that they fought against. [Green] Are there specific people that you would have expected to be present today who are conspicuous by their absence? [Zingane] Well, the vice president of Aford, Reverend Peter Kaleso, was not there. The publicity secretary of Aford, Simon Pandule, who should have chaired the press conference is in Lilongwe, but did not come, and rumors are saying that they expressed reservation about this come together. [Green] What do Malawians who don't belong to one of these three parties think of the antics since the elections? [Zingane] The Malawians in general are a bit disturbed, because they are saying that they voted for change, and they have got it in the referendum against the one-party system and they won, and the next thing they voted in the general elections, and at least an opposition party won, and they are saying where is the change, if people are going to waste time arguing and quarreling about things they should solve even before parliament meets next week. [end recording]
