Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Beirut, 17 Jan (MENA)--Lebanese Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz has ruled out the possibility of holding a meeting of the cordon countries--Syria, Jordan, Lebanon, Egypt, and the PLO--ahead of the coming round of peace talks in Washington. Buwayz told MENA that no arrangements have been made for such a meeting because time is short. The Lebanese minister said that Arab delegations to the peace talks will begin heading for Washington in the coming few days to prepare for another round of talks. Buwayz said that the Washington talks will be decisive and important in light of the al-Asad-Clinton summit, where the two presidents agreed to resume these talks immediately in order to draw up a declarations of principles, particularly on the Syrian and Lebanese tracks. Minister Buwayz predicted that the coming talks in Washington will lead to positive results in light of the positive atmosphere created by the al-Asad-Clinton summit. Buwayz pointed out that the Arab negotiating delegations will meet in Washington for consultations and to coordinate their positions during the talks.
