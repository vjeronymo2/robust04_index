Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] New York (DPA) -- On Friday [25 March] UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali expressed the hope that Germany will make a greater contribution to the work of the United Nations. In a talk with Wolfgang Schaeuble, chairman of the Christian Democratic Union/Christian Social Union group in the German Bundestag, he was, however, very restrained regarding any specific demands: Germany itself must decide what the greater German involvement in the United Nations should be like. The United Nations has many and great needs, Butrus-Ghali said. Schaeuble pointed out that Germany has gone through considerable learning processes regarding its new international role since 1989 and has made considerable progress in this respect. Butrus-Ghali stressed that in the United Nations the issue is not at all just blue helmet soldiers and peacekeeping -- this amounts to only 20 to 30 percent. More than half of the UN activities and also of his own work refer to social problems in the world, from environmental protection to development aid to population issues, the secretary general stressed. The critical financial situation of the United Nations played a considerable role. Schaeuble stressed his readiness to advocate more intensively the interests of the international organization. However, as a parliamentarian and as the leader of the parliamentary majority, he can do this only if it is possible to define clearly the work of the United Nations and to make its success visible. The financial means that were available for the Cold War, Butrus-Ghali complained, are in no proportion to those that are used to eliminate the relics of this period. Schaeuble assured the secretary general that Germany will certainly not leave him alone with his important task. Before the talk with Butrus-Ghali Schaeuble had met with Russian UN Ambassador Yuliy Vorontsev. Both expressed the hope that the worst military conflicts in the former Yugoslavia are now over. However, Vorontsev expressed the fear that the negotiations about a peace settlement might take years. It has been shown that the United Nations cannot force anyone to accept a peaceful solution, Vorontsev said.
