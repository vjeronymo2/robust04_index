930513 FT 13 MAY 93 / International Company News: Teleglobe bounces back to the black TELEGLOBE, Canada's overseas telecommunications group, posted first-quarter net profit of CDollars 12.2m (USDollars 9.6m) or 20 cents a share, against a loss of CDollars 5.6m or 17 cents a year earlier, on operating revenues of CDollars 141m, up 26 per cent. All subsidiaries, including non-regulated units, did better and international traffic volumes were higher. A new subsidiary, Teleglobe Cable Systems, has been set up to develop the submarine cable operations. Teleglobe, in which BCE, the country's biggest telecom group, has a 23 per cent voting interest, is part of the Cantat-3 consortium building a CDollars 400m transatlantic cable due in service next year and doubling total traffic capacity. The group plans to tap emerging markets in eastern Europe and the CIS.
