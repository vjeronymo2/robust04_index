Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, Tues -- The National Automobile Manufacturing Industry Limited is confident the government's decision to deny new contracts to British firms will not influence its customers in the United Kingdom. Its public relations manager, Nik Kamariah Nik Abdul Rahman, said today Proton's [Malaysian car] involvement in the United Kingdom market was solely a business venture. The Proton cars have received strong response in the country because of their quality and value coupled with the aggressive marketing strategy adopted by the distributor, Proton Cars (UK) Limited. She said Proton would countinue to support its distributor's commitment on services and warranty. Proton has exported 70,491 cars to the United Kingdom since 1989. The company expects to export 17,580 units this year. It was reported today Proton cars (UK) had told its customers and buyers not to be unduly concerned over the Malaysian Government's decision to stop awarding new contracts to British companies.
