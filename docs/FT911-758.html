910510 FT 10 MAY 91 / Parliament and Politics: Heseltine's rehabilitation continues - Scottish Conservatives' Conference in Brief MR Michael Heseltine's political rehabilitation among Conservative party activists moved a step closer yesterday at the Scottish Conservative conference. The environment secretary, who has received a frosty reception from local parties since his challenge last year provoked Mr Margaret Thatcher's downfall, won a standing ovation from the Perth gathering. In a wide-ranging speech Mr Heseltine claimed that the Conservatives had proved themselves the only party which could effectively safeguard the environment. The enthusiastic, if less than warm, reception from the conferences, however, came after he delivered a sharp attack on Labour's policies and predicted that the government was firmly on course for a fourth election victory.
