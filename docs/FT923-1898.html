920921 FT 21 SEP 92 / Shelter sees rise in debt victims RECORD NUMBERS of homeowners are turning to the housing charity Shelter because of mortgage debt. The number of homeowners threatened with repossession seeking the charity's advice increased by half last year. Homeowners now accounted for one in seven of those wanting its help, Shelter said in its annual report. Ms Sheila McKechnie, a Shelter director, said: 'The changing face of our caseload reflects the continuing debt crisis that is still swamping new victims every day. 'Shelter has directly helped thousands of people stay in their homes in the short term . . . but there has never been more urgency in our national campaign for more affordable housing for rent.' The charity reported an increase of 24 per cent in income to more than Pounds 7m. The report said: 'In spite of the growth we were still hit by the recession, which required us to revise our target during the year. However, the fund-raising division did remarkably well in making up a decline in income in some areas by increases in others.'
