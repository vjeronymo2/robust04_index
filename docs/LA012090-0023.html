January 20, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

HOORAY FOR THOSE WHO AMBUSH DUCKS

Thank you for the front-page article, "South for the Winter," on Jan. 16.

Although I must admit that I have never experienced the thrilling sport of ambushing ducks from behind the bushes, I look up to macho-men like Mr. Missildine with sheer awe.

After all, it takes a special kind of person to blast more than 2,000 birds in two days, just as it takes a special kind of newspaper to feature these types of American heroes.

Maybe next week you can do an article on blinding deer with high-powered lights, then blasting them with semiautomatic rifles. How about chasing raccoons up trees with a pack of dogs and shooting them out of the branches? Whatever article it is, I can only hope it measures up to the standards established by your detailed profile of those who blow ducks out of the sky.

By the way, cancel my subscription.

MICHAEL VOGT, Valencia
