Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 9 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- It was officially reported today that at least 97 are dead, an undetermined number are wounded, and 107 armed men have been captured by the Army since the beginning of the conflict in the southern Mexican state of Chiapas. The National Defense Secretariat also stated in a communique that the armed group today, Sunday, fired for the eighth consecutive day against the headquarters of the 31st Military Zone, 10 km southeast of San Cristobal de Las Casas. This time the members of the self-styled Zapatista National Liberation Army [EZLN], which on 1 January occupied several towns in Chiapas State, also cut the electricity supply to the region, stated the source. According to the communique, 61 members of the armed group are dead and an undetermined number are wounded. As for the Army, three officers and six soldiers have died, and two officers and 27 soldiers have been wounded. It has also been officially reported that 22 policemen and five civilians died during the first days of the clashes. The military source reported that 107 members of the EZLN have been captured and that 145 firearms of different types and calibers, 17 grenades, 9,600 rounds of ammunition of different calibers, and 94 sticks of dynamite, among other materiel, have been seized from the EZLN. On the ninth consecutive day of the conflict, the municipal capitals of Ocosingo, Las Margaritas, Altamirano, and San Cristobal de Las Casas, as well as other towns such as Chanal and Morelia, are under military protection. The Army maintains checkpoints in the conflict areas to "protect the citizenry from the aggressors' attacks against vehicles" and to keep the armed group from stealing vehicles and using them in their activities. The communique indicated that in towns such as San Cristobal de Las Casas, Ocosingo, and Altamirano, the Army is offering medical attention, distributing food, and providing attention to [words indistinct].
