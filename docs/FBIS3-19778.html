Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Outstanding Bank Debts Rise Sharply"] [Text] Rome, 21 Mar (ANSA) -- The outstanding loans or bad debts held by Italian credit institutions continues to rise sharply and at the end of last year totaled in the neighborhood of 70 trillion lire, over 41.5 billion dollars, sector sources confirmed here Monday [21 March]. The outstanding debts included over 46 trillion lire in expired loans together with 23 trillion in bad debts held by special credit institutes. Outstanding medium-to-long-term loans held by special credit institutes jumped 13.9 percent in December, over the previous month, and 34.1 percent on a 12-month basis. Short-term bad debts, however, for the first time declined, dipping from 47.029 trillion lire in November to 46.335 trillion lire at the end of last year, for a reduction of 1.4 percent. Outstanding short-term debts, however, rose 23.7 percent in December compared to the same month in 1992.
