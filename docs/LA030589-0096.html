March 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: SUITE FOR CALLIOPE A NOVEL OF MUSIC AND THE CIRCUS BY ELLEN HUNNICUTT (LAUREL / DELL PUBLISHING: $7.95)

Ada Cunningham (stage name Norma) finds refuge in the unlikely company of a lioness, an aging trapeze artist, a popcorn magnate and the remnants of a circus troupe near retirement.

Her real family was no less eccentric than her current company. Her mother, Grace (who died when Ada was 8), was a newspaper columnist who fabricated some of her best stories, the most popular of which was about her prodigy daughter: Ada, she reported, could read and write both English and Latin by age 3 and play violin, piano and compose music.

In fact, Ada is partly handicapped.

Her father, Reynard, a violinist for the Indianapolis Symphony, is committed to a sanitarium by the time Ada is 16. But the early music training that he indeed gave her finds her a place in the circus: composing and playing music on the steam calliope.

Ada's memories of her past, as well as the secret that caused her to flee Richmount, Ind., are interwoven into the narrative of circus life. An entertaining first novel.
