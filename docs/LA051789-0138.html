May 17, 1989, Wednesday, P.M. Final

LOCAL;

DEALER FOUND DAZED, GEMS GONE

A New York diamond dealer, found semiconscious in a restroom at Los Angeles International Airport, said today that his briefcase containing unmounted diamonds worth $1.2 million was missing, police said.

Phillip Pilikyan, 38, was found about 8 p.m. Tuesday in a men's room in Terminal One, where he was having difficulty breathing and was barely conscious, said Lt. Ruben Martinez of the Department of Airports police bureau. Pilikyan was treated by paramedics and taken to Daniel Freeman Marina Hospital, Martinez said.

Pilikyan phoned police shortly after midnight to report that his briefcase, filled with 2,500 carats of loose diamonds was missing. There was no immediate evidence that Pilikyan had been the victim of a robbery, Martinez added, but detectives were investigating the incident.
