Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Shanayev] [Text] Vladikavkaz, 17 Jan -- Sergey Khetagurov, chairman of the Council of Ministers of the republic of North Ossetia, who stood for president of the republic and lost in yesterday's election to the parliamentary leader Akhsarbek Galazov, resigned today. He explained his decision saying that "in the republic of North Ossetia, the president is the head of executive power and there cannot be two top figures of executive authority. It is a matter of honor for the loser to give way to the winner". Sergey Khetagurov asked the members of the republic's Cabinet of Ministers to remain in their posts and carry out their duties until the new government is formed. It is not yet known whether Akhsarbek Galazov, elected on 16 January as the first president of the republic of North Ossetia, has accepted the resignation of Chairman of the Council of Ministers Sergey Khetagurov.
