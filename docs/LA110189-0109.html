November 1, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: EL TORO;

VASQUEZ TO SPEAK ON TRAFFIC, DEVELOPMENT

County Supervisor Gaddi H. Vasquez will discuss traffic, development issues and construction of a water pipeline through South Orange County at a homeowners' meeting tonight.

"Traffic is probably one of the No. 1 concerns of people in this area, and this will give them an opportunity to talk about it with Supervisor Vasquez," said Roger Thomas, a board member of the Lake Forest Drive Assn., which is sponsoring the meeting.

The association represents about 200 homes in Sterling Heights.

In addition, a pipeline that will route water into Mission Viejo will be up for discussion. Residents of the area are concerned that the pipeline, which will be built starting Jan. 9, could add to South County's congested traffic.

The meeting will begin at 7 p.m. and will be held in the multipurpose room of Serrano Intermediate School, 24642 Jeronimo Road, El Toro.

An open forum will follow Vasquez's remarks, Thomas said.
