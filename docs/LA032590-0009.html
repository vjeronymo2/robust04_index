March 25, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

O, HAPPY DAYS!

The "Mid-Century Memoirs," by Karen Lansky, about how wonderful it was growing up in Los Angeles in the '40s and '50s, were almost all sentimental and nostalgic -- with very little mention of the era's underbelly. One of my most vivid memories of the late '40s was of an apartment-building sign that I used to stare at every morning while waiting for a bus to work: "No dogs, no peddlers, no Jews." What hit me hardest was not the bigotry of the building's owner, but that such a sign was even permitted.

DAVID R. MOSS

Los Angeles
