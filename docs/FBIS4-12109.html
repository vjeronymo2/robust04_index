BFN [Text] The Jordanian side, led by His Majesty King Husayn, and the Palestinian side, led by President Yasir 'Arafat, held a meeting at the al-Hashimiyah Palace last night. His Highness Crown Prince Hasan and a number of officials from both sides attended. His Majesty King Husayn emphasized Jordan's position in support of the Palestinian people's struggle, and toward easing the suffering of the kinsfolk in the occupied homeland in preparation for restoring their rights on their national soil. For his part, President 'Arafat stressed the depth of relations between the two fraternal peoples, and expressed the Palestinian leadership and people's appreciation for Jordan's constant support for the Palestinian people's just cause and King Husayn's support for their struggle and national legitimate rights. The two sides emphasized the importance of joint action on all issues of mutual concern, especially the peace process and the difficulties facing it. They stressed the importance of consultation and coordination between the Jordanian and Palestinian sides on one hand, and the Israeli side on the other, concerning the issue of refugees. The two sides discussed activating previously formed Jordanian-Palestinian joint committees, which include the Jerusalem Committee, the Administrative Committee, and the Economic Committee. On the Jordanian side, the meeting was attended by Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali, prime minister; al-Sharif Zayd Bin-Shakir, Royal Hashemite Court chief; Marwan al-Qasim, political adviser to his majesty the king; Talal al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs. On the Palestinian side, the meeting was attended by Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh and Yasir 'Amr, PLO Executive Committee members; Salim al-Za'nun, acting Palestine National Council speaker; al-Tayyib 'Abd-al-Rahim, Palestinian ambassador in Jordan; and Faysal al-Husayni.
