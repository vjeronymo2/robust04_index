November 18, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

READER DOESN'T GO EASY ON RACE WRITER

Why can't Bill Christine accept reality? Apparently, he still believes that Easy Goer is the top 3-year-old, even though he has lost three of four to Sunday Silence.

His comment that Sunday Silence is better, "but not by much," sounds like a typical broken-down horseplayer explaining why he should have cashed his most recent bet.

Isn't it just possible that Whittingham's horse wins because he is superior, by quite a margin, and that the fluke in the series with Easy Goer was the Belmont, run in the latter's back yard?

MARK BOYKIN, Los Angeles
