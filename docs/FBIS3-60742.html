Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] It has been learned that the United States and North Korea on 20 December had an unofficial working-level contact in New York and continued to compromise concerning the resolution of the North Korean nuclear issue that is in stalemate. According to Washington sources, in the 20 December contact, the United States received the official North Korean response to the U.S. proposal of nuclear inspection presented on 10 December. It is also known from the sources that although North Korea showed some progress in its attitude, it will not accept the U.S. proposal 100 percent. In the unofficial working-level contact on 10 December in New York, the United States repeatedly urged North Korea to accept overall inspection [chonmyon sachal] on all of the seven declared nuclear facilities in Yongbyon and respond to the resumption of South-North dialogue, and delivered the U.S. position that it is willing to hold the third round of high-level U.S.-North Korea talks and extensively discuss the improvement of relations when the proposal is accepted. It has also been learned that the contact was attended by Thomas Hubbard, deputy assistant secretary of state for East Asian and Pacific affairs from the U.S. side, and Ho Chong, deputy permanent representative to the United Nations, from the North Korean side.
