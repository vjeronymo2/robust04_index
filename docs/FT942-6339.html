940531 FT 31 MAY 94 / Observer: War games So who did win the war? As the D-day celebrations get under way there seems to be considerable doubt, according to a poll commissioned by France's Le Monde newspaper and Ted Turner's CNN TV news network. Two-thirds of Americans and nearly half the French believe the US did most to beat the the Nazis. The Germans believe their defeat was jointly attributable to the then Soviet Union, the US and Britain. Only 18 per cent of Britons thought that the Americans were the most important factor, whilst 64 per cent of the French believed their Resistance movement was as important as the Allies in liberating France. Only thing that everyone agrees on is that the Germans lost.
