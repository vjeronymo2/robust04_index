940702 FT 02 JUL 94 / UK Company News: Donelon Tyson Donelon Tyson, the construction and civil engineering group, fell into the red during 1993 and, as intimated in a statement in May, passed its dividend. The results, delayed from May following slow settlement of Pounds 3.5m of contract claims, showed turnover of Pounds 72m (Pounds 66.9m restated) and losses before tax of Pounds 2.57m, against profits of Pounds 345,000. While the construction operations remained in the black, operating losses in the joinery side widened to Pounds 1.5m (Pounds 500,000); a rationalisation programme has involved the loss of some 70 jobs. There were also operating losses of Pounds 500,000 on the rundown of the industrial insulation business. Losses per share were 3.2p (earnings of 0.8p).
