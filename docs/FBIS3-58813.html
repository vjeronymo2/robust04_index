Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report signed "pur": "Vranitzky Strictly Against Regular Army"] [Excerpt] Vienna -- On 18 January Chancellor Franz Vranitzky sharply rejected the idea of a regular army, which was suggested by Austrian People's Party [OeVP] Chairman Erhard Busek. The chief of the Social Democratic Party of Austria [SPOe] said that all objective facts speak against such a form of national defense; moreover, Busek's move had come "absolutely at the wrong time." The restructuring of the Army, which was agreed upon in 1992, would only be implemented in two years and, therefore, one could not think about introducing something new now. In addition, a regular army would be much too expensive and had not proven successful in other countries. Defense Minister Werner Fasslabend (OeVP) said it should not be forbidden to discuss the idea of a regular army. However, in view of the current threat, the strength of the militia army could not be reduced in the next few years. [passage omitted]
