930427 FT 27 APR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Challenge renewed on social chapter EFFORTS to ensure that MPs can have an effective vote on the social chapter have already been renewed in advance of the next stage of the marathon debate on the Maastricht treaty. Both the Labour frontbench team and Mr Bill Cash, a leading Tory Euro-sceptic, have re-tabled the amendment excluding the social protocol from the bill ratifying the treaty. While Labour MPs are concentrating on the social chapter, almost 60 amendments have been tabled so far by various Euro-sceptics on both sides of the Commons. Their determination not to relax their efforts has been fuelled by anger over the comments of Mr Douglas Hurd, the foreign secretary, who signalled at the weekend that the UK intended to join other EC countries in closer economic and political co-operation even if Denmark rejected Maastricht in its referendum next month. Last night, former Tory party chairman and prominent Euro-sceptic Lord Tebbit said: 'If his (Mr Hurd's) words have any meaning they can mean only that the pledge he gave at Edinburgh - the pledges of the prime minister, too - were not matters of honour, just words to help get the Maastricht bill through parliament.'
