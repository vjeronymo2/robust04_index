BFN [Text] A provision on the confidence-building measures has been included in a modified draft UN Security Council resolution on the renewal of the UNFICYP [UN Forces in Cyprus] mandate in Cyprus. Specifically, the draft resolution stresses the urgent need to implement the confidence-building measures that are mentioned in UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali's report on the Cyprus issue dated 1 July 1993. The draft resolution, which suggests the renewal of the UNFICYP mandate for another six months, mentions that the UN Security Council will reconsider the situation in Cyprus, specifically the role of the United Nations, within the framework of the UN secretary general's report of 30 May 1994. The draft resolution calls on both sides to commit themselves to significantly reducing the number of foreign troops that are in the Republic of Cyprus and to reduce defense spending to help restore confidence, which is the first step toward the withdrawal of all non-Cypriot forces as provided in Butrus-Ghali's set of ideas. The Cyprus Broadcasting Corporation correspondent reported from New York that the draft resolution will be presented for approval to the Security Council either today or tomorrow.
