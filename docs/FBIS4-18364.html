BFN [Text] Ankara, April 1 (A.A) -- Turkey and Libya will sign a trade agreement on April 17, Libya's visiting Foreign Minister 'Umar al-Muntasir said on Friday [1 April]. Speaking at a press conference at the conclusion of his contacts in Ankara, he said that with the signature of this agreement a new phase will begin in trade relations between his country and Turkey. He said the agreement foresees financing by Eximbank in Turkey and of a local bank in Libya. Al-Muntasir said his contacts in Ankara were positive and constructive adding that new foundations were thus laid down for the strengthening of present relations, adding that besides bilateral relations, regional and international issues were also taken up with emphasis on developments in the Mediterranean region. Al-Muntasir further said during his talks with Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin and the latter assured him of Turkey's assistance for efforts to lift a UN imposed embargo towards his country. Commenting on the Cyprus issue, he said that his country favours a solution that would be peaceful and acceptable to both sides, and which would not favour any of the communities on the island, treating them as equals.
