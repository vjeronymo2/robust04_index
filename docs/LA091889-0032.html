September 18, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

COUNCIL FUNDS FOR SCHOOL PROGRAM

The Los Angeles City Council made a big mistake when it voted to give the Los Angeles Unified School District $4.5 million for an after-school recreation program for latch-key children.

Don't get me wrong; the program reportedly is a fine one. My concern is not about the program itself, but how it is being financed.

The City Council took action despite the fact that it has little knowledge about the school district's $3.8 billion budget. It's hard for me to believe that the district itself could not have spared the money for the program, an expense of less than one-tenth of 1% of its total budget. But, nobody took the time to find out.

Equally disturbing is the fact that the City Council repeatedly cries poor when it comes to funding the services for which it is responsible. We can't spare the money to shorten the six-year backlog on tree trimming. We can't spare the money to buy more no-parking signs so that street sweepers can do their job, and as a consequence, we continue to waste tax dollars sweeping ineffectively. Recently, the City Council decided it couldn't spare $3 million to fund a new 61-officer Police Department foot patrol in high-crime areas.

City residents and taxpayers are not well served when the mayor and the City Council can't find the money for critically needed improvements in city services, but blindly fund another jurisdiction's program with no questions asked.

ERNANI BERNARDI

Los Angeles Councilman

7th District
