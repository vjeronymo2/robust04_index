BFN [Unattributed report: "Solidarity as Political Support"] [Text] External political departments of Argentina, Denmark, Israel, China, and Japan have recently made official statements in support of Ukraine with regard to resolving the conflict situation in the Crimea. This became known at a briefing at Ukraine's Ministry of Foreign Affairs. In addition to official circles of the world's leading countries, international organizations, public associations, and the Ukrainian diaspora have also demonstrated their solidarity with our state. All of them are in favor of a peaceful settlement of the conflict situation in which the principles set by the CSCE -- inviolability of borders and maintenance of states' territorial integrity -- will be adhered to. It was also stated at the briefing that a Ukrainian delegation headed by minister of foreign affairs A. Zlenko will soon visit Istanbul where it will take part in the session of the Council of the North Atlantic Cooperation. The program "Partnership for Peace" will be the main topic of the discussion.
