931124 FT 24 NOV 93 / Serbs again halt aid convoys Bosnian Serbs yesterday blocked aid convoys trying to reach Moslem enclaves in central Bosnia in spite of pledges by Serb leaders to allow the relief effort to resume after a near one-month suspension, our Foreign Staff writes. Serb military leaders yesterday held up four convoys heading for the eastern towns of Tuzla, Srebrenica and Sarajevo, all designated UN 'safe areas'. Meanwhile, Mr Warren Christopher, US secretary of state, cautioned yesterday against any premature lifting of sanctions against Serbia, as proposed by European Union leaders on Monday, in return for more land for the Moslems. Mr Christopher said in a radio interview the Serbs had to first make firm commitments.
