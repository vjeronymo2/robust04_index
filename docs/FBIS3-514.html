Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The defense force and police force of Bophuthatswana have cordoned off residential areas in the capital, Mmabatho, and have prevented people from attending a gathering in the city. Meanwhile, public servants have gathered for an antigovernment rally at Lichtenburg on the South African side of the border. Traffic ground to a halt in Mafikeng after a meeting in (?Rooigrond). Teachers and health workers have also embarked on a strike and public servants have begun a consumer boycott against businesses owned by the government. In a separate development, ANC [African National Congress] leader Nelson Mandela has asked the South African government to recall officials seconded to Bophuthatswana and to maintain a defense force presence on the border to monitor the situation. However, the defense force had denied that it has been deployed to prevent the unrest in Bophuthatswana from spilling over into South Africa.
