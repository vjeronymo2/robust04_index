921015 FT 15 OCT 92 / UK Company News: UK Land 18-month loss at Pounds 17m UK Land, the property investor and developer, which is in talks with its banks about a restructuring, announced a pre-tax loss of Pounds 17.1m for the 18-months to March 31 after an exceptional Pounds 20m for estimated liabilities incurred in development programmes. In the year to September 30 1990 losses amounted to Pounds 14.8m. Below the line there was a Pounds 24.1m write-off in respect of the group's development companies which went into liquidation in the period. For the six months to September 30 a further loss of Pounds 454,000 was incurred (Pounds 966,000 for the six months to March 31 1991). Mr Colin Tett, the chairman, said the group had three assets remaining - the Elephant & Castle Shopping Centre and Camden Lock in London and La Madeleine in France. Turnover for the six months to September amounted to Pounds 1.4m (Pounds 10.5m) and losses per share to 11p (20p).
