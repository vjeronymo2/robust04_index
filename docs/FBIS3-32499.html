Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Bolivia and Paraguay have signed a letter of intent that can open prospects for selling gas to that country. This important document was signed during the visit Paraguayan Foreign Minister Luis Ramirez Boettner is paying to Bolivia. [passage omitted] The Bolivian foreign minister and his Paraguayan counterpart today signed a major document of understanding that determinedly supports the construction of a gas pipeline between Puerta Grande, Bolivia, and Asuncion, Paraguay, for supplying Bolivian natural and liquefied petroleum gas to that nation. [Begin Ramirez Boettner recording] I believe the work we did yesterday afternoon and today to prepare for the visit Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada will pay to Paraguay on 14 and 15 March has been very fruitful. Among the issues we addressed -- because we addressed several issues -- one of the more important is the one dealing with the energy problem, that is, the possible sale of Bolivian gas to Paraguay. [end recording]. In addition to the Bolivian president's visit to Paraguay, other major issues were considered like economic supplementation; a tripartite agreement on the Pilcomayo River by Argentina, Paraguay, and Bolivia; the Paraguay-Parana waterway; and the Trans-Chaco Highway. Concerning the sale of gas to Paraguay, the price and volume will be decided at a meeting of specialists from Bolivian Government Oil Deposits and a Paraguayan private enterprise. The construction of the gas pipeline will be totally financed with private capital. [passage omitted]
