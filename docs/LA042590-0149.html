April 25, 1990, Wednesday, Valley Edition

HIGH SCHOOL ROUNDUP;

FERNANDEZ THROWS NO-HITTER

Henry Fernandez, a senior left-hander, threw a six-inning no-hitter, allowing just two runners to reach first base, as Crescenta Valley High defeated host Pasadena, 8-0, in a Pacific League baseball game Tuesday.

Fernandez (4-0) struck out eight and walked none. Pasadena Coach Bob Berans was thrown out of the game for arguing with the home-plate umpire in the sixth. Pasadena ultimately was forced to forfeit the game after Berans refused to leave the premises.

"He walked outside his dugout but would not leave the area," base umpire Randy Hines said.

Eric Berger homered to pace Crescenta Valley (10-8, 7-3).

In other baseball games:

Hart 12, Burbank 2 -- Kevin Foderaro and Keith Halcovich combined on a five-hitter for host Hart in the Foothill League game.

The win was the 14th in a row for the Indians (17-1-2, 10-0).

Saugus 5, Canyon 4 -- J. B. Johnson, Chad Hankinson, and Jeff Albert homered for Saugus in a Golden League game at Canyon.

Montclair Prep 1, Western Christian 0 -- Dylan Jones' two-out single in the sixth scored Russell Ortiz as the Mounties (18-2, 6-0) won an Alpha League game.

Rio Mesa 15, Santa Barbara 2 -- Dmitri Young was three for five, including a home run, to lead host Rio Mesa (13-5-2, 6-1-2) in a Channel League game.

Crespi 9, Loyola 4 -- Mark Venturine (five for five) and Kevin Walsh (four for four, home run, six runs batted in) paced a 20-hit Crespi attack in a Del Rey League game at Loyola.

San Fernando 13, Cleveland 7 -- John Najar (four hits) led an 18-hit attack for host San Fernando in a North Valley League game.

Kennedy 10, Granada Hills 7 -- Jason Rosen was four for four to lead host Kennedy (13-7, 8-4) in a North Valley League game.

SOFTBALL

Thousand Oaks 3, Simi Valley 1 -- Thousand Oaks (16-6, 5-4) scored all of its runs in the fourth inning as a result of Pioneer physical and mental errors to upset the Marmonte League-leading Pioneers (17-8, 7-2).

Alicia Zinner (16-5) allowed only four hits. She was backed by sparkling plays from second baseman Suzie Klusyk, shortstop Phronsie Franco, left fielder Lisa Stoddard and first baseman Amy Chellevold.
