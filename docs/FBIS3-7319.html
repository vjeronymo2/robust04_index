Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 28 KYODO -- Japan's household spending in January stood at 328,431 yen, an inflation-adjusted increase of 2.7 percent from a year earlier, posting the fourth straight year-on-year rise, the government reported Monday [28 March]. In nominal terms, the yearly growth rate stood at 3.8 percent, the Management and Coordination Agency said. The agency said spending by salaried workers' households grew a net 0.6 percent for the first upturn in nine months. Spending by households of nonwage earners such as the self-employed surged 6.0 percent in real terms, the agency said. Overall, household expenditures on education and housing climbed 16.9 percent and 14.2 percent, respectively, over a year earlier while those on transportation and communication, furniture and household goods, and utility rates gained 7.7 percent, 5.8 percent and 5.3 percent, the agency said. Spending on medical care declined a net 3.0 percent, the agency said. It said expenditures on food accounted for 72,458 yen, up 1.6 percent in nominal terms but down 0.5 percent in real terms.
