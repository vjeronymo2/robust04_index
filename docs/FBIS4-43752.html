BFN [Text] Ankara, June 20 (A.A) -- Prime Minister Tansu Ciller on Monday [20 June] met with Turkmenistan President Saparmurad Niyazov, currently in Turkey on an official visit. She said during the meeting that Turkey and Turkmenistan share common cultural and historical ties and have good relations, adding that efforts should be made to take them even further. She said that Eximbank opened a credit line of 90 million dollars to the republic to develop trade between the two countries, and 10 million have not yet been used, adding further requests could be met with pleasure. She said that there are about three thousand Turkmen students studying in Turkey adding that both countries should develop relations and cooperation in all fields. Niyazov's talks in Turkey are expected to focus on possible joint projects to develop and operate the Central Asian Republic's major natural gas reserves and construction of a pipeline to export Turkmen gas to Europe across Turkey. Turkey's bilateral trade with the republic rose to 160 million dollars last year from 28 millions in 1992.
