Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Report by 'Adnan Husayn: "`Kurdish Government' Reshuffle Affecting Three of Talabani's Ministers"] [Excerpts] London -- AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT has learned that the "government of the region of Kurdistan" which runs the affairs of the Kurdish areas in northern Iraq has reshuffled its portfolios and that three ministries held by members of the leadership of the Patriotic Union of Kurdistan [PUK], led by Jalal Talabani, were affected. Iraqi Kurdish sources told AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT yesterday that Sa'di Ahmad Birah, the agriculture minister in the Kurdish Government, replaced Humanitarian Affairs "Minister" Muhammad Tawfiq Rahim, who left the government; that the agriculture "ministry" portfolio was given to PUK Executive Committee Member Hazim Yusufi; and that Jamal 'Abdul, former governor of al-Sulaymaniyah, replaced Shirku Biks, the former "minister" and well-known poet who resigned in December last year, as culture "minister." [passage omitted] PUK sources have confirmed the cabinet reshuffle but denied that it had any political significance. [passage omitted] Meanwhile, some Iraqi opposition figures and groups living in the British capital have completed preparations for a week of solidarity with the Iraqi people next week with the participation of influential figures in the fields of politics, culture, and art.
