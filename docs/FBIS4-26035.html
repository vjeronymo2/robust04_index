BFN [Text] Beijing, June 16 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Premier Li Peng said here today that Sino-French relations have improved greatly and turned in a new direction since the recent visit to China by the French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur. Li made the remarks while meeting this afternoon with Jean-Pierre Desgeorges, president of the French-China Committee, a French institution designated for promoting trade and economic cooperation with China. Desgeorges, once chairman of the French electric company Alsthom- Atlantic, has made great efforts to enhance power cooperation between China and France. Li expressed his appreciation over the efforts Desgeorges and other French friends made toward improving bilateral relations when the relations met difficulties. On Sino-French economic cooperation, Li said that the two countries may increase cooperation on projects with a smaller scale of investment besides joining efforts on large projects. Desgeorges said his committee will make greater efforts to enhance exchanges and cooperation in the fields of trade and economy between France and China.
