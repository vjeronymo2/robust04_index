920219 FT 19 FEB 92 / Crackdown after Abidjan rioting Ivory Coast's main opposition leader was arrested with hundreds of supporters yesterday when an anti-government march degenerated into the worst rioting seen in the west African country, Reuter reports from Abidjan. Mr Laurent Gbagbo, leader of the Popular Front, was detained at a military camp with other opposition leaders. Mr Alassane Ouattara, the prime minister, said that they were held because the opposition had failed to control the marchers, estimated at about 20,000.
