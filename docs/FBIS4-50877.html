BFN [Text] Hong Kong, May 21 (XINHUA) -- Former British Foreign Secretary Geoffery Howe has said that the Sino-British joint declaration would still play a vital role in the transfer of power of Hong Kong in 1997. Addressing an American Chamber of Commerce lunch here Friday [20 May] following his recent Beijing trip, Howe also said China's economic progress had given some assurance to the people of Hong Kong about their future. Meanwhile, he warned that a decision by the United States to revoke China's most-favored nation (MFN) trading status would hit Hong Kong much heavier. "Curtailment of MFN status would provoke China as much as a bee-sting, and damage Hong Kong as much as a sledgehammer," he said.
