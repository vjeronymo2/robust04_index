Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- The new Slovak minister of justice is Milan Hanzel, proposed by the Democratic Left Party (SDL). Hanzel is the former state secretary of the Justice Ministry. He was born on April 27, 1947 in Kosice, east Slovakia. In 1970 he graduated from the Law School of the Comenius University in Bratislava. In 1984 he gained an external doctorate from the Department of Criminal Law, and started teaching in the department as an external lecturer. After finishing his military service, he became a justice of a military court. From 1972-1989 he practiced in district military courts, in 1989 becoming judge and chairman of the Higher Military Court in Trencin. He worked on the Advisory Committee of the Slovak parliament's Constitutional Committee, and was a member of the parliamentary Committee for Petitions, Legal Protection and Security. In November 1992 he was appointed state secretary of the Justice Ministry.
