BFN [From the "Vesti" newscast] [Text] The management of the North Caucasus railway has suspended all train traffic through the territory of Chechnya because of a sharp increase in banditry on this stretch of the railway. Abramenko, our correspondent in Rostov-on-Don, reports that on 6 May, nine freight trains were looted in broad daylight. Damage caused is estimated at several billion rubles. All freight for Azerbaijan, Georgia, and Dagestan is now being stocked on safe stretches of the railway. Deputy Russian Prime Minister Sergey Shakhray and Chechen President Dzhokhar Dudayev have been informed about the problem and about the measures taken by the railway management.
