Language: Urdu Article Type:CSO [News Report: "Operation Against Illegal Arab Immigrants in Sarhad"] [Text] Peshawar (MASHRIQ Correspondent) -- According to informed sources, the minister of internal affairs has started action against the illegal aliens from Egypt, Algeria, Syria, and Tunisia who are living in Pakistan. The sources revealed that the hundreds of families from these four countries that are living in Peshawar have been instructed to leave by 26 November. If they remain, action will be taken against them. Most of these people came here in connection with the Afghanistan jihad. The sources revealed that the government has instructed them to obtain a visa in Pakistan before 26 November and go to any country they wish to. It is believed that, if these people are sent directly to their country of origin, they will be arrested on arrival there. After the Home Ministry's decision, the Arab residents of Peshawar were in a tumult because of their fear of being arrested. Meanwhile, the citizens of these four countries and their leaders met with the officials of the Home Ministry, and they were told that the citizens of these countries, who are legally working in government offices, are exempt from this operation.
