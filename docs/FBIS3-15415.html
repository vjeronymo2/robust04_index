Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] During the meeting of expert groups for the Russian-Latvian interstate talks, the delegations managed to agree on several issues in the draft treaty on the social protection of retired Russian military personnel, reported Aivars Vovers, deputy secretary of state for eastern affairs at the Latvian Foreign Ministry, at the end of the talks. He added that next week a scheduled meeting of experts is due in Moscow, where the experts in the two delegations will continue to deal with the other draft treaties included in the package of documents on the agreement for the withdrawal of Russian troops from Latvia. The leader of the Russian delegation, Sergey Zotov, was also positive in his view of the result of the experts' meeting. He agreed with President Guntis Ulmanis that the Latvian-Russian talks on the withdrawal of troops are approaching an end and expressed the hope that in the next stage of the talks, from 14 to 16 March, it will be possible to settle all outstanding questions
