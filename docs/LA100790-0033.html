October 7, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

PHILIPPINES;

BOMBS RAISE FEARS OF CONTINUED UNREST

A bomb exploded at Defense Department headquarters in Manila, where authorities were holding Col. Alexander Noble, the leader of last week's Mindanao Island rebellion.

Hours later, a second bomb exploded outside the Finance Department in Manila. The blasts raised fears of continued unrest after Noble's secessionist revolt ended on the Philippines' second-largest island. In the first explosion, one bystander was injured and two jeeps were destroyed. The second bombing hurt no one, but destroyed a concrete plant box.
