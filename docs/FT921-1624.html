920324 FT 24 MAR 92 / EC ready to recognise Georgia THE European Community said yesterday it would recognise Georgia, the former Soviet republic that has stayed out of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS), Reuter reports from Lisbon. Georgia has accepted conditions the 12 EC states laid down last December for recognising new republics in Eastern Europe and the former Soviet Union, an EC statement said. Georgia said in a letter to the EC it accepted the conditions for recognition, which included guaranteeing the rule of law, democracy and human rights. Mr Eduard Shevardnadze, Georgia's new leader who previously served as Soviet foreign minister, said in the letter that Georgia would hold fresh parliamentary elections by October.
