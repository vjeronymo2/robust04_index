BFN [Text] Rome, April, 10, IRNA -- Iran and Italy are now engaged in financial negotiations focusing on rescheduling Iran's debts to Italian firms, Iran's ambassador here, Majid Hedayat-Zadeh Razawi said Saturday [9 April]. Razawi told IRNA that Italy is Iran's third largest trade partner and Iran is Italy's third largest supplier of crude oil with trade between the two countries standing at drls. 3 billion annually. The issue can be settled if Italian creditor companies agree to reschedule the debts owed by Iran to them in a way that Iran can pay off its debts in a four year period starting two years after such an agreement is reached, he said. "As a matter of fact, no figure has been mentioned in the negotiations or in a letter of understanding inked by the two countries.... What is important is the topic of discussion and an overall agreement," he said.
