BFN [Text] The deputy foreign minister of Sudan met with his Iranian counterpart in Tehran yesterday. During this meeting, the two sides called for the expansion of bilateral relations. The Sudanese deputy foreign minister commented on the limitations by the Saudi Government on the quota of pilgrims to Mecca and on the need to examine problems faced by the world of Islam during the Hajj congress, and emphasized the significance of the religious and political aspect of the Hajj pilgrimage. The Sudanese deputy foreign minister stated: When the Saudis say that the quota for pilgrims from Islamic countries has been set by the Islamic Conference Organization, then this organization should also make all the decisions concerning the Hajj pilgrimage as well.
