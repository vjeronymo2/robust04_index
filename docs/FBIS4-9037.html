BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 19/4/1994 -- Romanian and Dutch Prime Ministers Nicolae Vacaroiu and Ruud Lubbers assessed the necessary framework existed for fertile bilateral cooperation. It would be helped also by the Agreement on Mutual Guaranteeing and Protection of Investments, signed by the two premiers on 19 April in Bucharest. Other bilateral agreements will follow, including two documents on road and water transports, said Premier Vacaroiu during the news conference that followed the talks between the two heads of government. Referring to Romania's current situation, the guest said that what was worst seemed to have been overcome. "We are interested in cooperation and expect many Dutch investors to come to Romania", Lubbers added. Premier Vacaroiu stressed that the visit demonstrated the upward course of the bilateral relations. Premier Lubbers told the same news conference about the Netherlands' initiative to back, through the PHARE [Economic Reconstruction Aid for Poland and Hungary] program, Romania's efforts to cut down power consumption and improve the technical performance in urban central heating.
