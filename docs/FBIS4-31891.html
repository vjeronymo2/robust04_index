BFN [Text] Poznan, June 15 -- Minister President of the German Eastern land Brandenburg Manfred Stolpe told a press conference in Poznan on Wednesday that his land is particularly interested in cooperation with Poland. "Poland is our third biggest trade partner, along with Russia and the Netherlands," he observed. According to Stolpe, the last three years were very good for cooperation between Poland and Brandenburg. Now the German land wants to increase the import of Polish fruit and vegetables. Stolpe also said that the developing suburbs of Berlin will be a good market for Polish construction materials for the next 20 years. Brandeburg also wants to increase imports of Polish products of the electronics, metal and electrical industries. Deputy Minister for Foreign Economic Relations Andrzej Byrt, also present at the conference, said that Germany is very interested in possibilities of building in Poland a network of highways and high speed railways leading through the Polish territory to Belarus, Lithuania, Ukraine and Russia.
