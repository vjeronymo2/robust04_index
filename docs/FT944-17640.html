941007 FT 07 OCT 94 / Road projects attract 70 companies More than 70 companies forming 17 consortia have applied to bid for contracts to build and manage new or improved roads, the Department of Transport said yesterday. A shortlist of those selected to make bids for the four road schemes already chosen by the government will be announced in about a month. Under the scheme contractors will be expected to design new roads or improvements to existing ones, finance their construction and manage and maintain them for between 15 and 25 years. They will be paid 'shadow tolls' by the government, reflecting traffic volumes on the roads and the quality of maintenance. Among the consortia which have declared an interest in the the programme are: Construction companies Costain and Bovis, the RAC and NatWest Markets. The RAC contribution would be its traffic management and information systems. Contractors John Laing and Tarmac with Welsh Water, Transroute, a French toll road operator, and BZW, a merchant bank. Construction companies Amec and Alfred McAlpine with a Spanish contractor, Dragados. Construction groups Taylor Woodrow, Sir Robert McAlpine and Amey with banker Morgan Stanley. Trafalgar House, Wimpey and Autostrada of Italy. Trafalgar House said it was interested in bidding for the biggest of the schemes proposed. Balfour Beatty, WS Atkins, Philipp Holzmann and Bank of America. Balfour Beatty said it would bid for all four schemes. Mowlem, Dumez GTM of France and Schroders merchant bank.
