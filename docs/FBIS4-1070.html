BFN [Text] In-depth talks between the Afrikaner Volksfront [AVF, Afrikaner National Front] and the Freedom Front could lead to both the Conservative Party [CP] and the volksfront taking part in the elections. This comes after Freedom Front Leader Constand Viljoen approached AVF president and CP leader Ferdie Hartzenberg with proposals on participation at provincial level. This morning, Dr. Hartzenberg said he would take part in the election only if certain conditions were included in the constitution. These include the guarantee of a volkstaat [homeland] if more than 800,000 voters cast their ballots in favor of as volkstaat at provincial level. The second condition is that the volkstaat be granted political independence but economic interdependence. If General Viljoen accepts Dr. Hartzenberg's proposals, he will approach the government and the ANC. Dr. Hartzenberg said Gen. Viljoen should withdraw from the election if the government and the ANC refuse to accept the proposals.
