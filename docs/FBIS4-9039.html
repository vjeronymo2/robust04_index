BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 18/4/1994 -- In the presence of Jean Paul-Carteron, president of the Forum Foundation, of the Romanian Government's secretary-general and secretary-general of the Crans Montana Conference in Bucharest Viorel Hrebenciuc and numerous mass media representatives the press centre was inaugurated on 18 April of the Crans Montana Forum due in Bucharest over 21-24 April. Foreign Ministry Spokeswoman Simona Miculescu will be spokeswoman also of the centre. The press centre will issue every morning a document in Romanian, English and French with details of the day's schedule. It will also issue daily press releases in Romanian, French and English referring to the debates in plenum and in the sections on themes. They will comprise syntheses of the participants' addresses. Journalists will also receive Romanian and foreign press reviews, home and foreign news supplied by the ROMPRES national news agency, the texts of the statements and addresses of the previous day's speakers, other publications. The spokesperson will meet the journalists daily at 1300 hours [1000 GMT]. Journalists that want to contact various personalities will apply directly to the office set up to this end under the conference's secretariat general. The press office will function daily between 0800 hours [0500 GMT] and 2000 [1700 GMT]. It will accommodate also offices of the national radio, television and of ROMPRES. The documents issued by the press centre will not be official. So far, the following heads of state have confirmed their participation in the Crans-Montana Forum: of Cyprus, Belarus, Estonia, Yugoslavia, Lithuania and the Palestine Liberation Organization. All in all there will be 1,100 participants and 700 journalists. Mme. Catherine Lalumiere will be present on the presidium at the inaugural session. Participating will also be nine prime ministers and deputy prime ministers, five parliament presidents and speakers, 12 foreign ministers and deputy foreign ministers, 20 ministers, 17 ambassadors, 12 secretaries-general and assistant secretaries-general of international organizations, 67 members of the diplomatic corps accredited in Bucharest, 129 other political personalities. The meeting of the Crans Montana Forum, a world-scale event, is going to take place over April 21-24 in Bucharest at the Parliament Palace, one of the world's largest buildings. With a surface of 508,000 square meters, Romania's Parliament Palace is mentioned by the Guiness book of records as the second largest administrative building in the world, next to the Pentagon in Washington with a total surface of 604,000 square meters. In point of the constructed volume, the palace is world's third with 2,550,000 square meters, next to a sky-rockets assembling building in Cape Canevral, Florida, with 3,666,000 square meters and Quetzalcoatl Pyramid at Cholula, Mexico, with 3,300,000 square meters, but ahead of the Keops pyramid ranked fourth with 2,500,000 square meters. The construction works started on June 25, 1984, and swallowed about 480,000 cubic meters of high-quality cement, 65,000 tonnes of steel for reinforced concrete, 42,000 tonnes of rolled steel. The palace is furnished with a 286,000 square meter green backyard. The building is the outcome of a great national effort, starting from its design and erection and ending with the materials suppliers, everything is Romanian. About 17,000 people have worked on the building site. Along with numerous main and secondary offices, reception rooms, meeting rooms, a plenary sitting room of the Chamber of Deputies, the Parliament's Palace has also a set of halls, rooms and lobbies impressing through astonishing elegance, size, furnishings and design, bearing names such as the main hall, the honorary gallery, the hall of the provinces, the union hall, the lobby of the union hall, the human rights hall.
