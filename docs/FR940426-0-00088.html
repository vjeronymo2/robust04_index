A school that does not meet the criteria to originate loans must use the services of an alternative originator. The Secretary may allow a school to originate loans at a level for which it would not otherwise be eligible if the Secretary determines that the school is fully capable of originating loans at that level. The Secretary may also require a school that meets the criteria for the second level of origination to use the services of an alternative originator if the extent to which the school fails to meet other criteria merits such an action. A consortium of schools may originate loans at a level of origination for which all members of the consortium are eligible.
