941025 FT 25 OCT 94 / International Company News: Watchdog 'looks at' Microsoft The US Justice Department's anti-trust division is 'looking at' Microsoft's planned Dollars 1.5bn acquisition of Intuit, a personal finance software publisher. The department routinely reviews takeover and merger plans to ensure they do not create anti-competitive monopolies. However, it declined to characterise its interest in the Microsoft-Intuit transaction, saying only that it had not undertaken 'a review'. Microsoft said the action was routine and not related to an anti-trust consent decree signed by the company earlier this year, or to Justice Department monitoring of the company's activities that stems from the consent decree. Microsoft critics have called for the department to undertake a broad investigation of the implications of the acquisition of Intuit. 'This deal concentrates too much power in one company,' said Mr David Coursey, editor of PC Letter, an industry newsletter. 'Microsoft already dominates the PC operating system market and the PC applications software market, now they want to control the on-line service that provides financial services. It is the aggregation of all these interests that should cause the Justice Department to take a second look at Microsoft.'
