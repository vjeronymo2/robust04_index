BFN [Text] [Iraqi] Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf met in Cairo yesterday with Algerian Minister of Foreign Affairs Mohamed Salah Dembri. The foreign minister briefed the Algerian minister on the positive atmosphere and stands that have emerged on the part of most of the members of the Security Council, thus confirming Iraq's sound position and its call on the council to fulfill its obligations in Paragraph 22 of Resolution 687, after Iraq has fulfilled all its international obligations toward it. They also discussed issues related to joint Arab action and Arab developments, in addition to relations between Iraq and Algeria. Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf also met with Qatari Foreign Minister Shaykh Jasim Bin-Hamd Al Thani. He briefed him on Iraqi ties with the United Nations and the UN Special Commission, and the fact that there is now a large circle calling for lifting the unjust blockade imposed on Iraq, represented in some Security Council members and the international community in general. During the meeting, they discussed brotherly relations between the two countries. During the two meetings, the Algerian and Qatari foreign ministers reiterated their countries' constant position calling for lifting the blockade on the Iraqi people, especially since Iraq has implemented its obligations per Security Council Resolution 687 and cooperated fully with the Security Council and the Special Commission. Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf is currently visiting Cairo to attend the meetings of the foreign ministers of the Non-Aligned Movement. The foreign minister also met in Cairo with the foreign minister of Rwanda who reviewed the motives for foreign -- especially U.S. -- intervention in his country. The Rwandan foreign minister said the United States seeks to impose its hegemony on Rwanda and appoint its agents in the region as part of a plan to strike at international liberation movements and use force to impose a fait accompli in contradiction to the aspirations of nations. The foreign minister briefed his Rwandan counterpart on Iraq's efforts at the Security Council to have the unjust blockade lifted and on the positive indications that started to emerge within the council. The Rwandan foreign minister praised Iraq's principled stand in the face of U.S. hegemony and its resistance to all kinds of aggression, as well as its people's steadfastness in the face of the unjust blockade. The Republic of Rwanda is a member of the Security Council in its current session. It had called for lifting the Iraq blockade at the council.
