Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, January 12 (BTA) -- "Bulgaria agrees with the U.S. Partnership for Peace initiative and will shortly accept the invitation to enter into a Partnership for Peace contract," the Bulgarian president's spokesman told reporters today after a working meeting between Dr. Zhelev and senior national security officials was held to coordinate Bulgaria's stand on cooperation with NATO. This stand will be set forth tomorrow by President Zhelev to Ambassador Madeleine Albright, special envoy of U.S. President Bill Clinton. At Dr. Zhelev's invitation, the meeting was attended by Prime Minister Lyuben Berov, Army General Lyuben Petrov, chief of General Staff of the Bulgarian Army, Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov, Defence Minister Valentin Aleksandrov, Interior Minister Viktor Mikhaylov, Nikolay Slatinski, chairman of the National Assembly Committee on National Security, and Yunal Lyutfi, who is chairing the national security on foreign policy [as received]. At the meeting it was decided that President Zhelev will sign the documents on behalf of Bulgaria, Prime Minister Berov told the press. When it meets on Monday [17 January], the cabinet will discuss this decision and will form a working group to draft a proposal for Bulgaria's participation in Partnership for Peace and the actual contractual arrangement. "We had no differences at all and concurred on the conclusion that Bulgaria meets the political requirements to join Partnership for Peace and must not lose momentum because this is a way to prove our earnest intentions of integrating into the Euro-Atlantic security architecture," Mr. Slatinski told the press. On December 21, 1993 the National Assembly voted a declaration on relations between Bulgaria and NATO. The MP's [members of parliament] declared themselves in favour of making use of the opportunities for cooperation with the North Atlantic Alliance. On January 11, the Bulgarian Foreign Ministry declared Bulgaria's intention of joining the Partnership for Peace Initiative. The government Press Office today denied an allegation of Mr. Asparukh Panov MP of the Union of Democratic Forces that the cabinet failed to take appropriate action for the circulation of the National Assembly declaration on the country's relations with NATO. "I was not exactly surprised to learn about yet another slanderous story in the Bulgarian press about our diplomatic activities in Brussels," Mrs. Lea Koen, Bulgaria's ambassador in the Belgian capital, said on Radio Free Europe today. She was reacting to allegations of the KONTINENT daily that she delayed the circulation of the National Assembly declaration on Bulgaria's relations with NATO by mailing it instead of handing it personally to the leaders of the Alliance. According to Mrs. Koen, "certain people in the Foreign Ministry have made a practice of laying the blame at the wrong door."
