BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 15 (KCNA) -- The Democratic People's Republic of Korea is preserving the revolutionary relics and remains as eternal wealth. Recently the technical agency for the preservation of revolutionary sites manufactured a new fireproof and waterproof cloth and a glass cylinder, making it possible to permanently preserve historical mementoes, slogan-bearing trees, relics and remains of the period of the anti-Japanese revolutionary struggle. There are many revolutionary mementoes and remains in all parts of the country, especially in the area of Mt Paektu. The dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il has long since made sure that the revolutionary relics and remains associated with the glorious revolutionary traditions of the Workers' Party of Korea were preserved well to be conveyed through generations. Saying the revolutionary slogan-bearing trees are wealth more precious than a thousand pieces of gold, he indicated the orientation and way of permanently preserving them in glass cylinders. The officials and working people in charge of the preservation of revolutionary sites are building up the revolutionary battle sites and historical sites and preserving them well while discovering and arranging revolutionary historical materials and revolutionary mementoes. Musums have been built in every place associated with the revolutionary traditions and relics and remains are preserved well in their original state. Secret bases, slogan-bearing trees, relics and remains of the period of the anti-Japanese armed struggle are being discovered in large numbers through a vigorous unearthing of revolutionary relics. Entering the 90s alone, more than 11,650 slogan-bearing trees including those inscribed with the slogans "Let us uphold general Kim Il-song, the hero of the nation and great man of all people", "Woman general of Mt. Paektu Kim Chong-suk, woman general of the anti-Japanese war produced by Korea" and "The Paektu lodestar rose up above Mt. Paektu. It shines over three thousand-ri land", and new secret bases and many revolutionary relics and remains have been founded in Musan and Yonsa Counties of North Hamgyong Province and other places.
