930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / Lawyers warn on shortage of judges A SHORTAGE of Commercial Court judges is threatening London's future as a centre for resolving international business disputes, the heads of the legal profession say. A letter to Lord Mackay, the lord chancellor, from Mr Mark Sheldon, president of the Law Society, and Mr John Rowe QC, chairman of the Bar, warns that at stake is the Pounds 450m contributed by English commercial law to invisible foreign earnings each year. The normal complement of Commercial Court judges is six. At present there are only five. The problem is made worse because a general shortage of High Court judges means that the five commercial judges are frequently required to sit in other divisions of the High Court.
