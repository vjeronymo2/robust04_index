September 25, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

PEOPLE'S PHARMACY: BAKING SODA, CORN STARCH TEAM TO CONQUER UNDERARM ODOR

Question: I'm having difficulty finding an underarm deodorant that will not cause irritation. Within a day of using any deodorant, my skin develops a deep red irritation. I have even tried natural products from health food stores, but with no success.

The only non-irritant I have found is baby powder and corn starch, but I have a stressful job and require more protection. Help!

Answer: Here are some hints to help prevent irritation from antiperspirants and underarm deodorants. Never apply them after showering or shaving under your arms. For greatest effectiveness, antiperspirants should be applied to dry skin in the evening before bed.

If that doesn't work, here is a recommendation you may find helpful. A reader of this column offers the following:

"After reading about underarm odor, I feel led to write you about a simple solution to this problem. My daughter has underarm odor. I used to nag her to wash herself better and use deodorant, but it never helped. One day I read about baking soda. After much arguing, my daughter agreed to give it a try. That was the end of her objectionable smell. But if she doesn't use it, I can still smell her from across the room. Baking soda really works, and it is very inexpensive."

This isn't the first time we have heard of this home remedy. A baking soda and corn starch combination, dusted on, can be effective for some people.

Q. When I read the letter from the woman whose husband caught genital herpes from her after 10 years of marriage, I wept. The pain she expressed reminded me of the anguish my husband and I went through ourselves.

Herpes is a cruel disease, but Zovirax has helped us cope with the physical symptoms. Our local support group has given us strength to deal with the emotional symptoms. I highly recommend the Herpes Resource Center, which can put people in touch with a support group in their area. The number is (919) 361-2120.

A. Thanks for the tip. People with herpes need all the help they can get. There is also a newsletter put out by the Herpes Resource Center with information on research and practical advice on dealing with this viral infection. Another source of information about herpes and other sexually transmitted diseases is the STD hotline: (800) 227-8922.
