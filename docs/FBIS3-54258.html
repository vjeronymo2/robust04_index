Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] New Delhi has sent six proposals to Pakistan to resolve bilateral issues. Quoting official sources, PTI says the proposals include steps to resolve the Siachen problem, demarcation of boundary in the Sir Creek area, and the Tulbul navigation project controversy. The agency said the proposals which were handed over by the foreign secretary, Mr. J.N. Dixit, to the Pakistan high commissioner, Mr. Riaz Khokhar, in New Delhi today also envisage confidence building measures on the nuclear issue and stabilizing the situation along the Line of Control in Jammu and Kashmir. The proposals are a follow up of the foreign secretary-level talks held in the first week of this month.
