BFN [Text] Dr. Yasin Sa'id Nu'man, the chairman of the board of the secretariat of the Central Committee of the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP], emphasized that those who ran away from implementing the pledge and accord document about which they used to talk a big mouth talk are the ones who plunged Yemen into civil infighting and who set plans for a brutal crime, the effect of which will not end for generations to come. He clarified in an interview with the Middle East satellite television channel, MBC, that the war imposed on the Yemeni people has levelled the gunpowder, the bullets, and the shells to the heart of the unity and that the unity has been stabbed in the back. Pointing to those who committed this brutal crime, he said that this brutal crime did not only lead to the killing of the citizens in the peaceful villages and cities but it also directed a stab against the Yemeni people and to its history and future. He said: In this critical situation I call upon ... if we want to preserve something for the future, it is in the interests of Yemen that people are pledged to a logic rather than the logic of war. He expressed satisfaction about the good intermediation efforts and said in this connection: Those who reject these intermediation under the illusion of achieving a military victory must wake up from their illusions, which I am afraid will hit them in their strongholds. [Al-Jundi] What is the purpose of your visit to Riyadh? [Al-Iryani] To discuss bilateral ties and explain the situation in Yemen. [Al-Jundi] The Presidential Council dismissed Prime Minister Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas and the defense minister, while the House of Representatives dismissed Presidential Council Vice Chairman 'Ali Salim al-Bid. [Al-Iryani] Concerning the prime minister, the defense minister, and the oil minister, they were dismissed on the basis of a clear constitutional provision, namely, Article 111. The House of Representatives did not withdraw confidence from 'Ali Salim al-Bid, but from those who support secession and work against Yemeni unity.
