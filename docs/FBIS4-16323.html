BFN [Text] Simferopol, 14 Apr -- Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov has issued a number of decrees appointing Anatoliy Franchuk, former first deputy chairman of the Crimean Cabinet of Ministers, as acting minister for the management of state enterprises in Crimea, and Lyudmila Zaytseva, as acting minister of finance. Andriy Chernyavskiy is appointed Crimea's acting minister of economy. Decrees have been issued on the dismissal of Militia Major General Oleksandr Plyuta, Crimea's internal affairs minister, and his replacement with Army Major General Valeriy Kuznetsov known for his dismissal from the post of Crimean Army Corps Commander in 1994 for his failure to obey a decree by the Ukrainian defense minister. Another President Meshkov decree dismisses General Ivan Kolomyytsev, head of Crimea's security service, and replaces him with Retired Major General Volodymyr Lepikhov from the Crimean presidential staff. Personnel of Crimea's Internal Affairs Ministry and Security Service have refused to comply with Meshkov's decrees. The premises of these departments are surrounded by unidentified armed individuals (according to some observers, the Crimean president's so-called personal bodyguards). The dismissed generals, Plyuta and Kolomyytsev, are guarded by their supporters. The Crimean president's decrees are subject to the Crimean Supreme Council's approval at its next session on 10 May. According to the law, the issued decrees contradict the Constitutions of both Ukraine and Crimea, as well as the law of Ukraine on the division of powers between state power bodies of Ukraine and Crimea, said Crimea's Supreme Council Chairman Mykola Bahrov.
