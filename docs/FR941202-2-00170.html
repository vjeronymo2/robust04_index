Option IV: The following scoring protocol applies to: All chemical analytes; bacteriology samples that require quantitation (total and fecal coliform in non-potable water); fibers in air determined by phase contrast microscopy; asbestos in friable solid material by polarized light microscopy; and asbestos in air and potable water by transmission electron microscopy. The true values may be established through robust statistical analysis of the results reported by all laboratories, in order to reject gross outliers and establish a mean result and standard deviation, or through results obtained by a panel of 12 reference laboratories (this is done for asbestos in friable material). A laboratory's result on a given sample is then assessed as: Good if it is within the 95% confidence interval about the mean, or reported as ``less than'' the method detection limit if the sample is a blank; Marginal if it is outside the 95% confidence interval, but within the 99% confidence interval about the mean, or reported as ``less than'' twice the method detection limit; or Unsatisfactory if it is any other result.
