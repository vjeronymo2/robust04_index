Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Zagreb, March 8 (AFP) -- An unidentified aircraft was shot at and hit over Croatia Tuesday [8 March], a UN Protection Force official said. The incident happened over Ogulin in northwestern Croatia, UNPROFOR spokesman Lieutenant-Colonel Jean Marcotte said, adding that no UN aircraft was flying in the area at the time and it was not known who shot the plane down. The incident happened at 12:35 p.m. (1135 GMT). UNPROFOR was informed by its local commander, Marcotte said. A Croatian Government official, who insisted on anonymity, said that the aircraft, a Cessna, had been hit by a missile. The official said there were several injured aboard the aircraft, which had landed on the island of Krk off the northern Croatian coast.
