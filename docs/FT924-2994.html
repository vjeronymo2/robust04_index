921210 FT 10 DEC 92 / Secrets charge in Singapore The Singapore authorities have brought charges against four people in connection with press reports earlier this year concerning the country's economic performance, Kieran Cooke writes. On June 29 Singapore's Business Times newspaper published preliminary estimates of second quarter growth figures, saying it would be about 4.6-4.8 per cent. The government said the report was based on leaked information and the internal security department launched an investigation. Mr Patrick Daniel, the Busines Times editor, is among those charged under the provisions of Singapore's official secrets act. Also charged are a senior official at the Monetary Authority of Singapore and two economists working for Crosby's Securities. The four were released on bail of SDollars 5,000 (Pounds 1,900) each.
