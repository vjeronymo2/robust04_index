BFN [Text] In Zagreb yesterday President Tudjman received the leaderships of the Croatian republic of Herceg-Bosna and the Croatian Democratic Union [HDZ] of Bosnia-Herzegovina, and Croat deputies from the Constitutional Assembly of the Federation of Bosnia-Herzegovina, stated the presidential office on Saturday evening. The Croatian president and his guests discussed the further implementation of the Washington agreements and confirmed that it was very important to make efforts for implementing the agreement regarding the organization of the B-H Federation and the creation of a confederation with the Republic of Croatia. Undertaking these efforts, the Croatian people in Bosnia-Herzegovina should remain united in order to achieve its political aims and the strategic interests of the Croatian state. On this occasion President Tudjman announced his forthcoming visit to Sarajevo in an effort to contribute to the implementation of the Washington agreement. Last night's meeting was also attended by Croatian Foreign Minister Mate Granic, Defense Minister Gojko Susak, Ivan Milas, president of the HDZ deputies' club in the assembly, Ivan Aralica, deputy spokesman of the Chamber of Counties, Ivic Pasalic, HDZ member and the president's internal affairs adviser, and Miomir Zuzul, Croatian Ambassador to the United Nations.
