BFN [Text] The Liberal Democratic Party of Russia and its faction in the parliament unequivocally oppose Russia signing NATO's Partnership for Peace [PFP] program, the party's leader Vladimir Zhirinovskiy declared during his meeting with the adviser of the Canadian Embassy to Moscow Ferry de Kerckhove van der Varent [name as received] on Friday [1 April], held at the request of the diplomat, reported the press service of the LDPR faction in the State Duma. The Canadian diplomat himself showed interest in Zhirinovskiy's attitude towards this problem and enumerated his own arguments in favor of signing the document, reports the press service. Zhirinovskiy expressed the opinion that joining the Partnership for Peace program would mean Russia's involvement in NATO in a concealed form, pursuing NATO's interests, and consequently the interests of the United States.
