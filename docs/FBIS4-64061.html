BFN [By CRIME-TASS correspondent Olga Semionova] [Text] Moscow May 27 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin told the collegium of the Federal Counterintelligence Service (FCIS) on Thursday that he had sent to the State Duma for approval the law on the Federal Counterintelligence Service after endorsing it, the FCIS chief, Sergey Stepashin announced at the continuing conference of the service leaders. The passing of the law will strengthen the legal framework which is now operating on the basis of the regulation signed by the president in January. While in the FCIS, Yeltsin proposed to restore the investigative department to the service, which had been taken away from it after the president had signed a decree in December ordering the reorganization of the security agencies of the Russian Federation. It has become clear since that time that the move complicated the activity of the FCIS, as well as of the prosecutor's office and the interior affairs agencies. They had to investigate cases for which they had no adequate experience and lacked appropriate support.
