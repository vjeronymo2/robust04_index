BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 31 KYODO -- Police Tuesday [31 May] started raiding a right-wing organization's office and some other locations in connection with Monday's gunshot fired near former Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, police said. Police are searching the Shinjuku office of the right-wing group "Shokonjuku" on suspicion of violating the law to control the possession of firearms and swords. Hosokawa escaped unhurt when Masakatsu Nozoe, a one-time Shokonjuku member, fired a gun toward the ceiling close to the former premier at the Keio Plaza Hotel in Tokyo's Shinjuku ward. Nozoe, 52, was arrested immediately after the attack.
