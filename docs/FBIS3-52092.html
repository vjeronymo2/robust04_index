Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, Dec 30 (AFP)--The PLO has sent its own modified version of a working paper aimed at ending the deadlock on the launch of self-rule in the occupied territories, a Palestinian source said Thursday [30 December]. The source, declining to be named, said the paper was the Palestinian version of a draft understanding, details of which were released earlier the same day in Israel. If Israel accepts the latest document, the negotiations could result in agreement within two or three weeks, he said. The PLO earlier denied Israeli claims that they had drawn up a draft agreement on launching Palestinian autonomy and said negotiators still had to hammer out an accord. "We are trying in good faith to reach an agreement but, unfortunately, we have not reached it," said PLO negotiator Nabil Sha'th. "We are going back to Taba in the old format of the negotiations," he told journalists, referring to the Egyptian Red Sea resort where the autonomy talks opened two months ago before moving to Cairo. Egypt wound down its mediation efforts after a three-day meeting here of a liaison committee headed by Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres and executive committee member Mahmud 'Abbas for the PLO. Sha'th refused to comment on a draft understanding and said: "Our agreement was not to publish anything and anybody who is publishing anything is violating the agreement." An Israeli withdrawal from Gaza and the West Bank town of Jericho, under the September 13 autonomy accord, was to have started on December 13 but the deadline passed because of three main stumbling blocks. The Palestine Liberation Organisation and Israel are at loggerheads over the size of Jericho, the control of international crossing points and security for Jewish settlers in the Gaza Strip. According to Israel's draft, the size of an autonomous Jericho will be doubled from the original offer and the border crossings into Jordan and Egypt will be jointly manned but Israel would have the final say. But PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat said Thursday in Amman that his organisation had rejected the Israeli draft.
