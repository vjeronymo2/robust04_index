BFN [Text] Mexico City, 14 Apr (NOTIMEX) -- The National Foreign Trade Bank (Bancomext) and the Chamber of Representatives of Foreign Enterprises (Crecex) have signed an agreement to exchange information on statistics and customs duties systems. Bancomext issued a communique stating that it will make arrangements for Crecex to access Bancomext information to locate foreign trade business opportunities through its Foreign Trade Service Center [Centro de Servicios de Comercio Exterior]. The Costa Rican organization will provide information on the demand for Mexican products in that Central American country and on biddings by public and private organizations, as well as everything regarding the customs duties system and foreign trade statistics. This first agreement was signed within the framework of NAFTA to strengthen and broaden economic relations between Mexico and Costa Rica and to contribute to regional development. Bancomext Assistant Director Humberto Molina Medina and Crecex President Gerardo Loria signed the agreement during a visit to this country by a group of Costa Rican businessmen. The objective of the visit is to contact Mexican suppliers of chemical, plastic, and pharmaceutical products, printing paper, and auto parts.
