Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In December of 1993 the average wages in Russia were eight times higher compared to December, 1992, while prices rose almost ten-fold. This is stated in a report on the situation in the social and labor area prepared by experts from Russia's Labor Ministry. The report was forwarded to Interfax. According to the report, in 1993 the gap between people's wages widened considerably. For example, the average salaries of the highest-paid officials (10% of the work force) were 27 times higher than the wages of low-paid workers who make up the same percentage. At a number of facilities directors' salaries were 12 to 15 times higher than the wages of their employees. According to the report, the growth of salaries lost any connection with the final results of production. "The dynamics of price increases are a decisive factor in this process," the document points out. The wages of about one half of government employees continue to remain below subsistence minimum, the report says.
