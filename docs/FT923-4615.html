920905 FT 05 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Sale Tilney cuts losses to Pounds 2.53m Progress has been made towards resolving many of Sale Tilney's problems according to Mr Andrew Coppel, chief executive. However trading in the six months to May 27 had been disappointing with net liabilities increasing to Pounds 11.6m. The company, with food, industrial, insurance and financial services interests, cut its pre-tax loss to Pounds 2.53m (Pounds 3.68m) on turnover of Pounds 44.2m (Pounds 69.3m) of which Pounds 11.2m (28m) related to discontinued businesses. Debt was cut from Pounds 26.3m to Pounds 20.7m over the six months. The net interest charge was lower at Pounds 1.62m (Pounds 2.31m). The outcome for the year was dependent on the progress of certain contracts in the industrial division. After a Pounds 1.12m extraordinary surplus, attributable losses came out at Pounds 1.51m (Pounds 4.57m). Losses per share were 9.1p (13.6p). Again there is no dividend.
