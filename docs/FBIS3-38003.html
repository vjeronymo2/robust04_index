Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By Shakhnoza Ganiyeva and Sadyr Ziyamukhamedov; from the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] Imamali Rakhmanov, chairman of the Tajik Supreme Soviet, arrived in Tashkent on a short visit today. Serious and painful problems of the Tajik economy were discussed at the talks with Uzbek President Islam Karimov at the palace of friendship among peoples. In the opinion of the two leaders, Tajikistan's economic problems can only be surmounted if the social and political situation is stable, A bilateral agreement on trade and economic cooperation between Uzbekistan and Tajikistan was signed as a result of the talks. The news conference held after the signing of the document, however, was dominated by political issues, among them the problem of settling military conflicts along the Tajik-Afghan border and the attitude towards the state of affairs in Afghanistan. Referring to these issues, the heads of the two states replied that they adhered to a policy of non-interference in Afghanistan's internal affairs and that stability in Afghanistan could only be guaranteed if Afghanistan chooses in favor of a democratic development. Islam Karimov said that the presence of Russia's peacekeeping forces here was essential to ensure calm in the whole Central Asian region.
