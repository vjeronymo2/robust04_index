930427 FT 27 APR 93 / South Africa talks delayed Prospects for rapid progress in South Africa's multi-party constitutional talks appeared dim yesterday after several parties, led by the mainly Zulu Inkatha Freedom Party, tried to delay the talks over procedural issues, writes Patti Waldmeir in Johannesburg. Yesterday's meeting ran into immediate problems as Inkatha and the right-wing Conservative Party sought an adjournment. Inkatha opposes the installation of a multi-party Transitional Executive Council by June, as agreed by the government and the African National Congress.
