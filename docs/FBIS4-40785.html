BFN [Report by David Makovsky] [Excerpts] Ukraine has offered to sell Israel missile- launchers for Israeli satellites, participants in a meeting between Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk and Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres said in Kiev yesterday. Peres said Israel would consider the offer. Amid a dispute over the Jewish Agency's aliya activities in Ukraine, Peres and Ukrainian leaders agreed yesterday to set up cultural centers in each other's countries. [passage omitted] Peres told reporters yesterday that while anti-Semitic incidents have occurred in Ukraine, he was convinced that Ukrainian leaders were genuinely trying to combat this phenomenon. In his talks in Kiev yesterday, Peres' Ukrainian counterpart also suggested that the two countries establish consulates in Haifa and Odessa. Ukrainian leaders also extended invitations both to President 'Ezer Weizman and Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin to visit Kiev. Bilateral agreements were signed in the areas of investment, taxation, police, science, and culture. [passage omitted] [A report by Hemi Shalev on page 12 of Tel Aviv MA'ARIV in Hebrew on 16 June adds: "Ukraine offered to try to use its good relations with Damascus to create yet another channel of communications between Israel and Syria. ["Ukrainian President Kravchuk told Peres that his country considers Israel `a strategic neighbor,' and that he hoped the agreements Peres will sign today would provide `a firm legal basis' to the relations between the two countries." [The report notes that Peres complained to his Ukrainian counterpart about Ukraine's votes in the United Nations and other international organizations, and that the Ukrainian foreign minister replied: "I never dreamed that I would be hosting in Kiev an Israeli foreign minister who would voice his complaints and would also manage to convince me. You must understand that getting used to our independence and getting over the past takes time."]
