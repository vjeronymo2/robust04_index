BFN [Text] His Majesty King Husayn has said that Jordan's democratic course is an irrevocable option and that Jordan, despite the facts in the current Arab situation, insists on continuing that course because it is linked with the Arab citizen's honor, freedom, and right to take part in building his present and future. His majesty also stressed the constant principles of Jordan's march, while it faces challenges and builds an exemplary Jordan through the democratic and social changes. His majesty's statements came when he met at the Royal Hashemite Court today with the House of Representatives speaker, the members of the permanent and temporary committees, the spokesmen for the parliamentary blocs, and the representatives of the House's Permanent Bureau. His majesty called for further distinguished performance by the deputies in the fields of control and legislation and for clarifying relations between the three authorities, stressing the democratic traditions and bases of a meaningful dialogue that enriches the march and enables it to achieve its noble goals. His majesty briefed the deputies on the latest developments in the peace process, as well as Arab relations, stressing that Jordan insists on achieving a comprehensive, just, and lasting peace in the region. House Speaker Tahir al-Masir and the chairmen and rapporteurs of the parliamentary committees expressed their thanks to his majesty for giving them the opportunity to meet him and listen to his instructions. They also stressed the importance of such meetings as they add momentum to the democratic march. His majesty also listened to the deputies' observations and opinions on various local issues and the democratic march. The meeting was attended by Prime Minister 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali, Royal Hashemite Court Chief His Excellency al-Sharif Zayd Bin-Shakir, Chief Chamberlain His Highness Ra'd Bin-Zayd, and his majesty's advisers.
