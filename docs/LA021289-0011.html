February 12, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CRITICS' CHOICE: EVA LUNA

by Isabel Allende; translated from the Spanish by Margaret Sayers Peden (Alfred A. Knopf: $18.95; 272 pp.)

" 'Eva Luna' is a picaresque tale of a South American child who likes to tell stories and grows up to write novels. An inventive political fable from one of South America's most interesting writers." Elena Brunet
