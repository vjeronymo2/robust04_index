Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] New York, 15 Feb (INA) -- Iraq has informed the general secretariat of the United Nations of further violations of its airspace by U.S. aircraft for purposes of surveillance and provocation and dropping thermal flares. Iraq's permanent UN delegate, in a letter to Secretary General Butrus-Ghali, said that from 1 to 10 February, 841 sorties were conducted over large areas of northern and southern Iraq. Two T.S.R.S1 [as received] American reconnaissance planes violated Iraq's airspace on 3 and 6 February, flew over al-Nasiriyah, al-'Amarah, al-Samawah, al-Jalibah, al-Ratawi, Qal'at Sukkar, and (?al-Busayyah), and then headed to Saudi Arabia. A hostile plane on 7 February dropped three thermal flares over the Ba'shiqah district in the Governorate of Ninawa.
