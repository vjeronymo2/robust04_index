Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Veronika Romanenkova] [Text] Moscow January 14 TASS -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher have signed here today, after the third round of the Russo-American summit talks, an agreement on cooperation in studying radiation after-effects with a view to minimise radioactive pollution hazards to human health and the environment. The purpose of this document is to lay the foundations for close and long-term cooperation in studying radiation hazards to human health and to the environment. Russia and the United States will jointly study the aftermaths of the exposure of humans to ionising radiations, will organise the collection and processing of data on radiation hazards, will study the environment, and will conduct corresponding scientific research. A joint coordination committee will be set up to study the after-effects of radiation. The document notes that the sides shall ensure an adequate and effective protection of intellectual property, created or handed over under the current agreement, and conclude corresponding accords on its implementation. The agreement will remain in force for five years, subject to repeated prolongation for the same term.
