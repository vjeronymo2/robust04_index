Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report under the "In Brief" rubric] [Text] Moscow -- According to information from competent sources, the chiefs of major commercial enterprises are worried over stubborn rumors about Russian Federation President Boris Yeltsin's being very seriously ill. According to information allegedly received from people around the president, Boris Yeltsin has a serious pneumonia, which called for hemodialysis [gemodializ], rather than a slight cold as the president's press service maintains. According to experts, these rumors were provoked by U.S. President Bill Clinton's statement about a "technical impossibility" of contacting the Russian Federation president.
