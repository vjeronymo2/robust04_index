BFN [Text] Bangkok, April 21 (XINHUA) -- The Petroleum Authority of Thailand (PTT) will undertake joint ventures with the Charoen Pokphand Group and petrochemical firms in China and Vietnam. The joint ventures involve a total investment of 6,420 million baht (256.8 million U.S. dollars). Of the amount, 635 million baht (25.4 million U.S. dollars) is invested by the PTT. The joint ventures will operate petroleum business in China, Vietnam and Thailand, according to official bulletin available here today. The business is in line with the policy and directions for state enterprises to move towards the international trade and strengthen relations between Thailand and China and Vietnam. It said the cabinet has given the green light to the plan. The joint venture in China involves the setting up of oil stations with China's Guangdong, Shanghai, Tianjin and Talian petrochemical firms, the bulletin.
