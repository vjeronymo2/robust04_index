Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] Anti-U.S. demonstrations were held in Moscow on 12 January, the day U.S. President Clinton started his three-day visit to Russia. Masses from all walks of life taking part in the demonstration carried red flags and slogans reading, "Deputies, do not yield to U.S. pressure!" "Bourgeois dictatorship will never get through!" and "We object to Clinton's visit!" Demonstrators who gathered in front of a building of the State Duma of the Russian Federation Federal Assembly held up their fists and shouted slogans such as "Yankee, go home!" and "Punish murderers!" and marched toward the U.S. Embassy in Moscow. After holding an anti-U.S. protest rally in front of the U.S. Embassy in Moscow, they headed to an office building of the Federation Council of the Russian Federation Federal Assembly, shouting slogans.
