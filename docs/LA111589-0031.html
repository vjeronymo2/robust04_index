November 15, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORE BAILOUT FUNDS FOR S&LS

"Alan, we hardly knew ye."

"Say it ain't so, John."

If Edwin Gray's (former chairman of the Federal Home Loan Bank Board) charges against Sens. Cranston, John Glenn (D-Ohio), John McCain (R-Ariz.) and Dennis DeConcini (D-Ariz.) prove to be accurate, all four should resign their Senate seats.

I am especially disillusioned at the possibility that Glenn and McCain are involved in Keating's sordid Lincoln S&L mess. Glenn has been one of our national role models. And McCain earned our admiration for his steadfast courage despite years of torture while a prisoner of the North Vietnamese. How could they?

If these senators lied recently in denying Gray's account of their closed-door meeting with him, they have sunk to a new low.

JERRY COWLE

Pacific Palisades
