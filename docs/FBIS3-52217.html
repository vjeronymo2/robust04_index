Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] The second round of talks between the foreign secretaries of Pakistan and India was held in Islamabad last night. Both sides held detailed talks on all aspects of the Jammu and Kashmir dispute. The Pakistani delegation is being led by Foreign Secretary Shaharyar M. Khan and the Indian side by his Indian counterpart, J.N. Dixit. The Indian foreign secretary will meet Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali today and the third round of talks will begin after the meeting. Describing the current rounds of talks between Pakistan and India as satisfactory, the Indian foreign secretary said that he has found an extremely reasonable and conducive atmosphere at every stage of the talks in Pakistan. Speaking to newsmen in Islamabad yesterday evening, he said the first round was held in a cordial atmosphere. During the first sitting, the two sides presented their views on Kashmir in a realistic manner. He said now it should be seen as to how can we make further progress in this direction. Pakistan Foreign Secretary Shaharyar M. Khan, who was present on the occasion, told newsmen that they held detailed and serious discussions on the core issue of the Kashmir problem, which was the central point of the first round of talks.
