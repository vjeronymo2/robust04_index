BFN [Excerpts] Tunisian-Namibian joint statement was issued following the official visit to Tunisia by President Sam Nujoma of the Republic of Namibia starting 16 June at the invitation of President Zine Al-Abidine Ben Ali. The statement said: On this occasion the two state presidents, in an amicable atmosphere, held a series of talks which allowed them to look into bilateral relations and means to strengthen them and to exchange views on current affairs on both the African and world arenas. They both expressed their satisfaction at the ideal ties that link their two brotherly countries and their strong commitment to African solidarity and human values and their firm determination to bolster democracy and human rights. [passage omitted] The two leaders reviewed current affairs at the regional and international levels; they recorded with satisfaction their agreement over the various issues discussed. In this context, they reiterated their commitment to the principles and goals of the United Nations and the Organization of African Unity [OAU] and decided to intensify consultations between officials from the two countries during bilateral meetings and international and regional conferences. [passage omitted] The two presidents stressed the importance of the resolutions issued by the OAU Tunisian summit and which are to bolster peace and cooperation in Africa and promote Africa's role on the world arena. Regarding the situation in Africa, presidents Ben Ali and Nujoma expressed their satisfaction at the efforts made towards a peaceful settlement of all the conflicts in the continent in order to create a suitable atmosphere for peace, security, and development. They also expressed their satisfaction at the success of the democratic process in South Africa, an event which would give the African continent a positive impetus at the political and economic levels. The two presidents discussed the Rwandan crisis and expressed satisfaction at the indications of detente, manifested in the agreement of the two parties to halt hostilities, and expressed their wish that dialogue would help achieve national reconciliation in this African country. Regarding the situation in the Middle East, the Namibian and Tunisian presidents hailed the brave struggle fought by the Palestinian people to establish its independent state on its land and expressed satisfaction at the positive and encouraging progress in the Middle East peace process according to UN resolutions.
