Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, March 27, IRNA -- Loss of floppy diskettes containing vital classified information has terrified the Iraqi regime, according to radio 'Voice of the Iraqi People', monitored here today. The radio said Iraq security forces have started inspecting houses of hundreds of persons suspected of removing the diskettes, and have arrested a number of employees of related departments. It has not yet been clarified what data the diskettes contained, but the way the Iraqi regime is treating the suspects, the radio said it is likely that they might contain vital security information. What's more concerning for the regime is that foreign embassies in Baghdad can get access to the diskettes. The radio added that broadcast of the classified information contained in the diskettes by internationl radio stations might also be critical for the Iraqi regime. Voice of the Iraqi People is an opposition radio operating from somewhere inside Iraq. It did not give further details.
