Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Elections will be held again in Oti constituency, the first constituency. The constitutional chamber of the Supreme Court of Togo, which met on 25 March, invalidated the election on 6 February of Bemba Bahissa, the candidate of the Togolese Union for Democracy. The reason given by the Supreme Court is that his alternate, Tiamoko Seydou, did not meet the conditions concerning residence in Togo as envisaged by the electoral code. The protest was filed by (Assabou Gyabite), candidate of the Rally of the Togolese People [RPT]. According to the electoral code, a new election must be held within one month at the latest. In the same judgement, the Supreme Court rejected other demands for annulment -- a total of 20 -- which were made by the RPT, the Union for Labor and Justice, the Union for Renewal and Development Party, and the Togolese Democratic Party following the first round of the legislative elections. The Supreme Court will also have to deliberate the RPT's contesting of the results of the second round of the legislative elections in seven constituencies. The probable deadline for the verdict is 1 April.
