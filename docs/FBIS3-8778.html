Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Text] A high-ranking NATO officer has confirmed that the AWACS planes patrolling in Hungary's airspace contributed to the shooting down two weeks ago of four Serb aircraft that had violated the UN air exclusion zone over Bosnia. The NATO European Command's deputy chief of staff responsible for operations said that NATO was not surprised when Budapest asked that no AWACS planes should be in Hungarian airspace at a time of a potential NATO air strikes in Bosnia. However, in order to have effective intelligence it was necessary for NATO to obtain data from the AWACS planes located over Hungary. The general also said that NATO was in constant radio contact with Hungarian Government organs and the Hungarian military air defense leadership.
