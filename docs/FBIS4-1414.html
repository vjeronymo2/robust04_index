BFN [Text] Pretoria April 28 SAPA -- The Army worked through the night printing extra ballot papers, then rushed them to Air Force planes before dawn for delivery to areas around the country where thousands were waiting to cast their votes. A spokesman for the SA [South African] National Defence Force said, after a request by the Independent Electoral Commission [IEC] on Wednesday afternoon [27 April] , the Army's 1 Military Printing Regiment had 375,000 ballot papers printed by 6AM on Thursday. It hoped to have another batch of 300,000 ready for dispatch by 9.30AM. The operation had started at 6PM on Wednesday, and by 3.30AM the first batch of 210,000 had been delivered to Waterkloof Air Force Base for dispatch to areas designated by the IEC. A second batch of 165,000 had been delivered by 6AM, and was sent by the IEC to Ulundi.
