BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 21 KYODO -- The largest opposition Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) agreed Tuesday night to take a formal decision Wednesday on the submission of a no-confidence motion against the coalition government of Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata, party officials said. Under the accord reached by LDP head Yohei Kono and other leaders, the party will hold a series of leadership meetings to authorize the motion, which it hopes will be joined by the second largest opposition Social Democratic Party (SDP). The accord came amid a flurry of strategic talks held separately by the SDP, which quit the governing alliance in April, and coalition parties seeking to lure the SDP back into the fold.
