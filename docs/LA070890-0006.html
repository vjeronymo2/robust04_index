July 8, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

LEGALIZING STREET VENDORS: MOVE TO AVOID

Iwould like to comment on "Vendors vs. the Law" (June 27).

I was born and raised in Venezuela where street vendors, although illegal, are as thick as flies and just as annoying.

In business districts, they take up most of the sidewalk, creating hazards for the pedestrians and large amounts of trash that wind up on the street. The vendors are unfair competition for merchants since they do not have to pay rent or taxes and have no upkeep. Peeled fruit and most foods tend to develop nasty bacteria that can make unsuspecting customers extremely ill.

If vending were legalized as LAPD Chief Daryl Gates longs for, we would have uncontrolled vendors all over the city and another incompetent government agency that will be overworked and understaffed. Councilman Michael Woo should take a little trip down to Latin America and experience vendors personally before creating another government monster. Believe me, they are not something we would like to add to our way of life in this city.

MERCEDES WEIL

Canoga Park
