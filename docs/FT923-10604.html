920729 FT 29 JUL 92 / World Stock Markets: Mexico Mexico's volatile stock market rallied 71 points, or 4.8 per cent, yesterday to close at 1,537.60 - its biggest single-day advance since January 1991 - after Telmex reported better than expected results, writes Damian Fraser. Trading was a busy 119.4m shares. Telmex L shares jumped 7.1 per cent on news of a 12.7 per cent rise in first-half operating income. Cemex B, down by more than 25 per cent since the company announced its Spanish cement acquisitions earlier in the month, rose 6.5 per cent in heavy trading. Televisa, reporting earnings up 62 per cent in the first quarter, gained 7.6 per cent.
