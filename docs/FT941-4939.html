940309 FT 09 MAR 94 / Light van sales higher in February Sales of light vans made their long-awaited upturn last month, broadening the commercial-vehicle market recovery to every sector except buses, coaches and utility four-wheel-drive vehicles. In some areas of the market the sales surge last month reached spectacular levels, with the important heavy trucks sector - over 15 tonnes - up 31.9 per cent compared with February last year. Statistics from the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders show that sales of light vans - mainly those derived from cars - rose 5.9 per cent, to 5,334 from 5,037, reversing a long declining trend. Sales of larger panel vans, typified by the Ford Transit, were 14.8 per cent higher last month than in the same period a year ago, at 7,040 from 6,133. Leyland Daf, recovering from the former Daf group's term in receivership early last year, said: 'The market has definitely turned. Dealers are reporting many more inquiries being turned into orders and we're now seeing fleet customers who we haven't talked to for a considerable time.' It is increasing weekly output from 200 vans to 250. The market for trucks - vehicles over 3.5 tonnes - was up 11.95 per cent last month, the statistics show. Last month's 8.13 per cent rise in total new commercial vehicle registrations, to 16,312 from 15,085 a year ago, raised the total for the first two months of the year to 33,954, a 4.46 per cent rise from 32,503 in the same period last year. Imports continue to take a larger share.
