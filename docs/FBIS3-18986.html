Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vienna, March 10 KYODO -- Inspections of North Korean nuclear facilities, suspected as part of a suspected nuclear arms development program, are not progressing according to schedule, sources at the International Atomic Energy Agency said Thursday [10 March]. The sources said the inspections to ensure that North Korea has not been diverting nuclear fuel to military purposes may not be completed by March 14 or 15 as originally scheduled. An IAEA official said the inspectors are not being denied access to North Korea's seven declared nuclear sites and that there is still time to complete the inspections, refusing to acknowledge a possible delay in their safeguards checks. The sources, however, said North Korean officials have reportedly told inspectors that it is "not necessary" to check a certain number of sites before the nuclear experts have had a chance to look for themselves. Diplomatic sources have suggested the lack of progress may have been influenced by the stalemate in inter-Korean working-level talks Wednesday on the exchange of presidential envoys. Washington has already tied a third round of high-level talks with Pyongyang in Geneva on March 21 to the completion of the IAEA inspections and the dialogue on envoys. The sources here suggested that if the inspections are delayed, the U.S.-North Korean talks will be placed in a delicate situation. Further, if IAEA inspectors cannot ensure the continuity of the nuclear safeguards, the issue will likely be referred to the U.N. Security Council, which could call for sanctions against Pyongyang. Meanwhile in Seoul, the Korean Broadcasting System (KBS) reported that the inspectors have found some of the seals on equipment to ensure the nuclear safeguards may have been damaged. The seals were among those in seven declared sites in 40 locations. However, there appeared to be nothing unusual on the seals on the 5,000-kilowatt reactor in the nuclear complex at Yongbyon, some 90 kilometers north of Pyongyang, KBS reported quoting IAEA sources.
