Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Maria de los Angeles Garcia; from "The City" newscast] [Text] State Governor Ausencio Chavez Hernandez has denied the existence of armed groups in Michoacan. He has described the attempts two days ago by unidentified individuals to down a Federal Electricity Commission high-tension electricity pylon, located 10 km from Periban, as an isolated incident. Chavez has met with reporters at the State Government House and said there is a climate of work, calm, and social peace in the state. The governor also denied that dynamite or blowtorches were used in the attempt to down the high-tension pylon, and he revealed that a fretsaw had been used to cut the base of the tower. Chavez added that the sabotage had been perpetuated by people who wanted to stand out by following examples set in other areas, referring to the events in Chiapas. Chavez reported that the high-tension pylon had not been damaged and that the electricity supply had not been cut off.
