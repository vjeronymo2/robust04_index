April 19, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

RICE NOT PERFECT FOOD

Question: I enjoy watching the programs on public television that deal with the way people live in other parts of the world. It seems to me that in many countries, children are brought up on rice, if they're lucky, and little else.

Is rice such a wonder food that it can provide everything a child needs in the way of a nutritious diet?

Answer: Children who live almost entirely on rice do so out of necessity, not out of choice. In many cases, the alternative is not to eat at all.

No single food can provide all of the child's nutritional needs, and rice is no exception. For example, rice provides almost no Vitamin A, which is essential for good vision. Children on all-rice diets who do not receive Vitamin A supplements are at risk of blindness.
