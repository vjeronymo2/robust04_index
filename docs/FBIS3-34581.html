Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum, 9 Feb (SUNA) -- The Transitional National Assembly, the parliament, has called on the European Parliament to stop interfering in the Sudan's internal affairs and to revise its attitude by obtaining firsthand information on the state of the country. The Sudanese parliament also called on the European Parliament to promote dialogue among the concerned parties in Sudan on the allegations made against Sudan by Western circles. In a communique issued in Khartoum on Wednesday, the Sudanese parliament cautioned against religious hatred, especially against Islam, calling on the European Parliament not to adopt politically motivated resolutions which could affect Sudan. The communique also condemned the plots against Sudan and the European Parliament's decision to suspend its assistance to Sudan under the Lome Convention.
