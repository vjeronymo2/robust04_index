940218 FT 18 FEB 94 / Observer: Taking the biscuit An explanation arises for the number of large unwieldy sub-committees and interminable meetings held at the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales. A 'biscuit memo' is doing the rounds at Moorgate Place, the institute's London bastion. It decrees no coffee for meetings that last less than two hours, and no biscuits at all unless outside volunteers are in attendance. Shaken rather than stirred, seems the bean-counters' response.
