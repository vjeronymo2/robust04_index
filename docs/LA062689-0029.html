June 26, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

SENIOR DRIVERS

Much publicity has been properly given to the valiant efforts of the Police Department to cut down on accidents resulting from drunk drivers. This is a major effort, which is rightfully applauded by all citizens.

I have a suggestion that I think will contribute a great deal to safety on the highways. I believe all citizens over the age of 75 (which includes me) should have their license renewed for only one year at a time and that an actual driving test be mandatory.

There are far too many senior citizens on the roads that, if subjected to a driving test, would fail to pass. And this group too constitutes a safety hazard.

ARMAND S. DEUTSCH

Los Angeles
