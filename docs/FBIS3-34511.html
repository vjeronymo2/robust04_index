Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Amman (J.T.)--Japan and Jordan Sunday [6 February] signed an agreement rescheduling over $14 million in Jordan's debts owed to Japan. The agreement, signed by Finance Minister Sami Qammuh and Japanese Ambassador Yuji Ikeda, followed a decision in June by the Paris Club of major government creditors to ease Jordan's foreign debts. Under the accord, Japan will reschedule installments on arrears from July 1993 until February 1994, the Jordan News Agency, Petra, said. The sum will be added to a previously rescheduled amount of $87.3 million dollars, it added. The deal provides for a grace period of nine years for government debts and seven years for government-guaranteed commercial loans.
