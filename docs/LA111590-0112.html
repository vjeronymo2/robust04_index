November 15, 1990, Thursday, Southland Edition

PRATT & WHITNEY GETS $220-MILLION ORDER

Pratt & Whitney, a unit of United Technologies Corp., said it has won a contract worth up to $220 million to provide jet engines for Korean Air Lines.

Deliveries are scheduled to start next September, Pratt & Whitneyt said.

The carrier placed firm orders for 16 engines, including spares, worth $135 million, Pratt & Whitney said. These engines are for use on seven A300-600R airliners KAL has ordered from Airbus Industries, the European consortium, according to Pratt & Whitney.

Also, KAL took an option on another 10 engines, worth for $85 million.
