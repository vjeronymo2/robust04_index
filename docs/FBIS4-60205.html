BFN [Excerpts] Kuwait, 23 May (KUNA) -- Radio Sanaa has reported that northern troops have managed to control Shabwah Governorate and its districts which are rich with oil and that they are advancing to the southern part, which declared secession from Yemen last Saturday. Radio Sanaa, which is received here, quoted a military source as saying that the northern troops in the eastern Shabwah axis have captured the military base and the central axis command headquarters in the city of 'Ataq, the capital of the province, with all its districts and all its military installations. These reports, which are considered an important development that would deprive the southern part of its important military and economic resources, have not been confirmed. [passage omitted] The military source said that northern troops have seized a large quantity of weapons, ammunition, and military depots in Shabwah Governorate. [passage omitted] A southern military spokesman today denied reports that 'Ataq military base, the capital of Shabwah Province, has fallen to northern forces. Aden radio, received here, quoted the spokesman as saying that northern troops are not in control of Shabwah Province, and that the news announced by Sanaa is totally unfounded. [passage omitted]
