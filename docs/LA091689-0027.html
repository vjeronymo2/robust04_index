September 16, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

EDUCATOR SAYS SAT DESIGNED TO BE FAIR

Sam Marchiano's excellent article on young athletes affected by the NCAA's Proposition 48 shows how important it is to help these students prepare for college earlier, and to help them realize what adequate preparation entails.

Should Marchiano's article give readers the impression that the SAT is biased against black and other minority students, let me state emphatically that this is not the case.

Before a question appears on an SAT, it is scrupulously reviewed and analyzed by a racially and ethnically diverse panel of experts, then pre-tested by thousands of students under real-test conditions to detect the slightest sign of "item bias." It is used in a real SAT only if it is fair for students of similar ability of any race, ethnic background or gender.

DONALD M. STEWART, The College Board, President, New York
