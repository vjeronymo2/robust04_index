941014 FT 14 OCT 94 / London Stock Exchange: Telecoms attract buyers The telecoms stocks, in the forefront of the market's upsurge of recent days, continued to make good progress yesterday with Cable and Wireless leading the pack higher again, and with buying stimulated by a 40-page buy note published by Hoare Govett. C & W rose 5 more to 426p on turnover of 10m. Hoare described C & W shares as having 'further to go' and highlighted its 500p price target for the shares. The broker said C & W's entire market capitalisation is now less than the value of its holdings in HK Telecom and in 23 cellular groups around the world, leaving Mercury and the rest in the price for free. Hoare said that if floated independently these operations would be worth Pounds 3.6bn, equivalent to the value of a middle-ranking FT-SE 100 company. The prospects of a potential bid for the group was also mentioned by Hoare. He said that eight of the world's top 10 telecoms companies are involved in important deals. The broker cited AT & T, US West, Pacific Telses and Hutchison as potential bidders/partners for C & W: 'It is even conceivable that BT could buy C & W for its presence in the far east and then sell off Mercury.'
