BFN [Text] Ankara, June 2 (A.A.) -- Cabinet on Thursday [2 June] approved of National Security Council's decision to extend emergency rule for another four months starting from July 19. `The government has decided to propose to parliament the extension of the state of emergency from July 19,' government spokesman Yildirim Aktuna said after a cabinet meeting chaired by Prime Minister Tansu Ciller. Aktuna who is also state minister said government has a successful struggle with terrorism and the separatist terrorist organization lost logistic support and power. The emergency rule is in practice in Batman, Bingol, Bitlis, Diyarbakir, Hakkari, Mardin, Siirt, Sirnak, Tunceli and Van Provinces. The state of emergency is imposed under an agreement which needs parliamentary approval every four months. It gives extraordinary powers to an emergency rule governor in Diyarbakir who coordinates between military and civilian authorities in the fight against terrorism. The powers include the right to impose curfews or block off entry and exits to villages if there has been an incident.
