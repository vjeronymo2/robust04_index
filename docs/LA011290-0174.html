HOW TO DETERMINE FRAME SIZE

To determine your frame size, extend your arm and bend the forearm up at a 90-degree angle. Keep fingers straight and turn the inside of your wrist toward your body. Place thumb and index finger of your other hand on the two prominent bones on each side of your elbow. Measure the space between your fingers against a ruler or tape measure. Compare it with the following guide for medium-framed men and women. If the measurement is less than that stated here, you have a small frame; if it is more, you have a large frame:

For men --

5 feet, 2 inches-5 feet, 4 inches 2 1/2 to 2 7/8 inches

5 feet, 5 inches-5 feet, 7 inches 2 5/8 to 2 7/8 inches

5 feet, 8 inches-5 feet, 11 inches 2 3/4 to 3 inches

6 feet-6 feet, 3 inches 2 3/4 to 3 1/8 inches

6 feet, 4 inches 2 7/8 to 3 1/4 inches

For women --

4 feet, 10 inches-5 feet, 3 inches 2 1/4 to 2 1/2 inches

5 feet, 4 inches-5 feet, 11 inches 2 3/8 to 2 5/8 inches

6 feet and taller 2 1/2 to 2 3/4 inches
