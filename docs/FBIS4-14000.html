BFN [Text] The government has prepaid $1.13 billion as part of the loan drawn from the International Monetary Fund. A statement of the Reserve Bank of India issued in Bombay yesterday said with this payment the level of the country's foreign exchange reserves stood at $14.50 billion. It may be recalled that the finance minister, Dr. Manmohan Singh, during his budget speech had committed to prepay a part of the IMF loan in view of comfortable foreign exchange reserves position.
