BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] The Army is competing with Santa Fe de Bogota radio stations that broadcast to young audiences. Radio station S5.9, created and run by soldiers of the Presidential Guard Battalion, is on the air beginning today. The Presidential Guard Battalion no longer has to wake up to the sounds of reveille only. The soldiers now wake up to music played on 88.3 MHz, a frequency that identifies S5.9, a military radio station. [Begin radio announcer Juan Carlos Ramirez recording] All of the equipment came from the Presidential Guard Battalion. In general, everyone ranging from colonels to captains contributed, including the soldiers. It all began when the soldiers started bringing their records. They brought their drawings representing the radio station. They brought microphones and equipment. The console came from the Presidential Guard Battalion. [end recording] The radio station will reach all residents of Santa Fe de Bogota with variety shows, such as the War Tank, and the reveille beginning today. They hope to get civilians to listen to the radio station as well; this does not mean the radio station will ease up on military discipline. Here is what the colonel had to say: [Begin unidentified speaker recording] Training is stronger now, but it is accompanied by psychology. These men are being trained so that when they finish their military career they can work in a radio or television station. [end recording]
