Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Canberra, March 1 (XINHUA) -- Australian Foreign Minister Gareth Evans today urged all parties in Bosnia-Herzegovina to "press ahead urgently with the peace process". The war-torn nation needs a negotiated settlement to end the tragic conflict, Evans said in a statement after U.S. planes shot down four military aircraft in Bosnia yesterday. He regarded the action as "in strict accordance with the provisions of the U.N.-imposed no fly zone in Bosnia". Meanwhile, he called upon all parties concerned in the country not to take any retaliatory action against the U.N. Forces or international relief efforts. "It is essential that the humanitarian effort be allowed to continue uninterrupted and that the current momentum for restoring peace in Bosnia-Herzegovina continue," Evans said.
