Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias described his tour of Middle East countries, which began this morning, as difficult but very significant. Tunis is the first stop on the tour. In a statement shortly before leaving for the Tunisian capital, the Greek foreign minister stressed that the European Union [EU] and the Greek Presidency's presence in the Middle East may contribute at this crucial time. He added: An effort will be made so as not to lose everything that has been achieved toward a permanent, peaceful solution to the Middle East problem. The foreign minister is visiting the Middle East in his capacity as the president of the EU Council of Foreign Ministers. EU Commissioner Hans Van Den Broek is accompanying him. Today, the foreign minister will meet the Tunisian president and foreign minister as well as PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat. Tomorrow, Papoulias will go to Jordan for talks with King Husayn. On Thursday [3 March], he will go to Israel for talks with Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, and Palestinian representatives. On Friday, the foreign minister will visit Egypt for talks with Egyptian President Husni Mubarak and the secretary general of the Arab League. In his talks with his counterparts and political leaders in each country, Papoulias will review bilateral relations.
