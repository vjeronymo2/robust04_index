BFN [Editorial by Hasan Sabra: "Butrus-Ghali, Listen to the Bosnians"] [Text] "If you have any honor, you should resign." This is what Bosnian President Alija Izetbegovic told UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali following the barbaric Serbian attack on Gorazde. We cannot but congratulate the Bosnian president for his words to the UN secretary general, although we are confident that the latter will not heed Izetbegovic's advice for obvious reasons of which the secretary general is aware. The secretary general is in the habit of denouncing any legitimate operation mounted by dispossessed Palestinians against the Jewish usurpers in the land of Palestine, equating such actions with the attacks the Zionists wage against Palestinians, Lebanese, and Arabs everywhere every day. Such a man, of course, will hardly find fault in the failure of the UN forces to perform their simplest duty in Bosnia-Herzegovina, that of stopping the massacres against the inhabitants of that country. We never thought the secretary general likely to deviate from the Western line of thinking and decision-making. Butrus-Ghali's elevation to his current job was, after all, a reward for certain policies of which he and the Egyptian regime are aware. The day Butrus-Ghali defies Western--more specifically American--wishes will certainly be his last on the job. Nor do we hope for a UN secretary general who is not of Butrus-Ghali's type in the foreseeable future. We do not think that Butrus-Ghali's being Orthodox makes him sympathetic to the Orthodox Serbians in their continuous and barbaric aggression against the Muslims of Bosnia-Herzegovina. We do not think that ethnic bias taints his position in the Muslim- Serbian strife. The secretary general, an Arab, will understand the meaning of the Arab saying: "Do not flaunt your sins." Is there a worse sin against ethics, values, and human rights than to remain silent when you monitor and know, even beforehand, the massacres the Serbs are perpetrating against Bosnians, whose only crime was to want a separate home of their own. We did not need to wait for the UN's silence about Serbian crimes to reach Izetbegovic's conclusion. We long ago made up our minds about the secretary general--an Arab, to our lasting shame. Is not Butrus-Ghali the man who fully backs Western and U.S. persecution of Iraq, its people, and its children through an embargo of which innocent Iraqis bear the brunt? The real criminals are still free in Iraq, and living very well at the expense of their downtrodden people. Is not Butrus-Ghali the man who condoned, and still condones, U.S.-Israeli pressure on a weak and defeatist Palestinian leadership? The latter is now set to cooperate in the liquidation of the Arab-Zionist conflict, an outcome that suits Western, particularly U.S., interests. Is not Butrus-Ghali the man who condones Zionist crimes and the way in which the United States backs these crimes at the UN Security Council? Would this secretary general ever tell us what he thinks when he sees UN resolutions trampled under the feet of the Zionists, the West, and the United States, particularly those resolutions concerning the Arab-Zionist conflict? These are resolutions that merely aim to deter Israel, psychologically at least, from attacking Arab children, women, and men. We did not await Butrus-Ghali's silence about the Serbian barbarism in Bosnia-Herzegovina to say to the man: Speak up and walk out. He made up his mind a long time ago. Since the moment he got on the payroll of the United Nations he become an employee of the U.S. Administration. He takes his orders from the United States, particularly in matters involving the flogging of his fellow Arabs and Third World wretches, doomed to live under the U.S. thumb. Butrus-Ghali came to office to follow orders. If he does not, he will lose his job. And he knows that. He would not want to trouble his remaining days with moral questions or ethical considerations that only bother prophets, and he is not one.
