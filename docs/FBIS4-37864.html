BFN [Text] A new meeting has brought together the government and the central trade union leadership. The meeting is continuing with negotiations attended by Prime Minister Mokdad Sifi and seven ministers concerned with social affairs, as well as Abdelhak Benhamouda, the secretary general of the trade union central leadership. A report from Reeza Guteri: [Guteri] The government's meeting with the trade union leadership this morning opened a new round of talks, and includes an agenda containing a number of demands. The trade union leadership presented a platform of demands that includes a wage increase in the public service and economic sectors and a reconsideration of the amounts allocated for the protection of low wage earners. This is in addition to other demands related to improving the social services designated for workers in the health, transportation, and social housing sectors. Other issues were put forward such as raising family allowances, the reorganization of the national economy, and the reform of public service laws. All of these points are under discussion today during the negotiations, which have been described as difficult, between the government and the Algerian General Workers Union [UGTA].
