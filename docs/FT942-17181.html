940406 FT 06 APR 94 / UK Company News: Stylo returns to black with Pounds 2.6m Stylo, which owns the Barratts chain of shoe shops, returned to the black in the year to January 29, exceeding City expectations with pre-tax profits of Pounds 2.55m. This compared with losses of Pounds 746,000 last time, struck after a Pounds 1.33m gain from sales of fixed assets. The latest figure included an exceptional gain of Pounds 220,000. Turnover increased to Pounds 116.5m (Pounds 105.5m). Operating profits doubled to Pounds 6.92m (Pounds 3.47m). Mr Michael Ziff, managing director, said the group was concentrating on better products, improved merchandising and tighter control of expenses in its core areas - Barratts, Instep sportswear outlets, and the golf and equestrian wholesale business. During the year the group opened 25 new branches and closed six. Net borrowings fell by Pounds 4m to Pounds 39.9m, and net interest payable dipped to Pounds 4.59m (Pounds 5.55m). Earnings were 12.1p (losses of 4.01p). The single dividend is lifted to 4p (3p). A 1-for-2 scrip issue is also proposed.
