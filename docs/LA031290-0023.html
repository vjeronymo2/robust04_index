March 12, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

BUCHANAN ON COLLABORATORS

So Buchanan is calling the collaborators to account? It seems to me that a world struggling to free itself from opportunistic and uncaring overlords is bound to spawn leaders like Lenin, Mao, Castro, Ho and Ortega, standard-bearers dreaming of national self-determination, affordable education and health care and guaranteed employment. It's ironic that when the political offspring of such revolutionaries begin to live like fat-cat Republicans, forcing the people to throw them out, Buchanan should be the one sounding the call for a domestic blood bath.

I ask him to look at how two terms of Reagan Administration mismanagement have devastated our nation's economic, educational and health-care systems. We'll be decades putting this country back together, spending billions to bailout banks, savings and loans, pension funds and insurance companies. The United States may never again be a world leader in public education and health care. We've been raped by petty opportunists and uncaring ideologues, and indeed the time has come to call the collaborators to account. Step forward please, Mr. Buchanan.

JON WILLIAMS

Reseda
