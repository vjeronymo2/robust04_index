BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA May 22 -- Foreign Minister of the Republic of Korea (ROK) Han Sung-chu concluded his four-day visit to Vietnam today. While in Vietnam, Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu was received by President Le Duc Anh and held talks with his Vietnamese counterpart, Nguyen Manh Cam during which the two sides informed each other of the situation of each country and exchanged views on regional and international issues of common concern and on the further development of cooperation between Vietnam and ROK in the fields of economy, science and technology, culture, education, healthcare and labour cooperation. On this occasion, Vietnamese Minister of Finance Ho Te and Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu signed an agreement on double taxation avoidance. On May 21, Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu visited Ho Chi Minh City, where he called on Mr Nguyen Van Linh, advisor to the Central Committee of the Communist Party of Vietnam and met with Mr Truong Tan Sang, mayor of Ho Chi Minh City. The ROK foreign minister also toured a number of economic, cultural and historic establishments in the city.
