May 30, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

NEW HOME SALES CONTINUE FALL

Sales of new single-family homes dropped 1.6% in April for the fifth straight monthly decline while prices shattered all previous records, a government report said today.

Private economists had expected a somewhat better sales showing.

But in the hard-hit Northeast, sales jumped by 18% in April, while dropping by 2.1% in the Midwest, 6% in the South and 6.4% in the West, the Commerce Department report said.

The median price for a new home rocketed to $135,900 in April, after dipping to $119,400 in March, and April's average price rose to $160,200, after falling to $144,100 in March, the report said.

A Commerce Department spokesman said the April prices were the highest on record, but the report did not indicate why there was such a huge increase in new home costs during the month.

But the level of new home sales -- 546,000 at an annual rate -- was the lowest since 521,000 in December, 1982, the spokesman said.
