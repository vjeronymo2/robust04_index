930908 FT 08 SEP 93 / Survey of Software at Work - Autumn 1993 (8): Snapshot - Stonesfield Telecottage Stonesfield Telecottage, Friends Close, High Street, Stonesfield, Oxfordshire OX7 2PX. Member of the Telecottage Association Nature of business: Providing facilities to local businesses. Founded in 1988 and owned by Stonesfield Community Projects, the active arm of Stonesfield Community Trust, whose main activity is the provision of affordable rented housing for local people. In the late 1980s, funding for the first project of six dwellings, Friends Close, came from the Quaker Housing Trust, Barclays Bank (a loan of Pounds 80,000), a prospective tenant and other private supporters. Second project under way. Turnover: Pounds 8,200 in 1992, with profit of Pounds 3,800 (about Pounds 320 per month). Income goes into the Trust to finance local housing schemes. Current campaign is to raise Pounds 200,000 for the conversion of buildings to create a larger business centre. System: Apple Mac IICx, with Microsoft Word and Aldus Pagemaker, and an Apple laser printer, total cost Pounds 6,500 including educational discount. Other equipment includes two phone lines, answering machine and fax, which doubles as photocopier (a second-hand photocopier is the next acquisition). Key Personnel: Kathy Coonerty, manager and director of projects; Tony Crofts, co-founder and director, also chairman of Stonesfield Community Trust. Other trustees are Nigel Braithwaite, Kate Winstanley and Elizabeth Hartwell, chairman of Stonesfield parish council. Contact: Tony Crofts or Kathy Coonerty Tel: 0993 891686
