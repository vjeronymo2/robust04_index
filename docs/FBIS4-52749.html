BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 13 KYODO -- Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa on Friday [13 May] sought the cooperation of his cabinet colleagues to meet strong U.S. and European Union demands for Japan's greater deregulation efforts in "visible" form, officials said. He made the request at the first meeting of ministers concerned with deregulation. The United States and EU are asking for the removal or relaxation of seven types of government regulations, including those concerning the Large-Scale Retail Stores Law and the management of public and private pension funds. Their demands also include more relaxed building standards and electrical goods standards to promote the international harmonization of standards. The ministers are expected to meet again early next week as some ministers such as construction minister were not present at the first meeting, the officials said.
