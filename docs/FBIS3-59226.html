Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] President Glavkos Kliridhis has reemphasized that he accepts the principles of the package of confidence-building measures proposed by UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali and is ready to begin talks on ways to implement the measures as soon as the Turkish Cypriot side states the same clear and unconditional acceptance. Since the Turkish side has not accepted the package and seven months have been lost, the president proposes that the secretary general have the two sides return to a discussion of the substance of the Cyprus issue, beginning with their concerns about security issues, until the Turkish side decides on the confidence-building measures. Kliridhis' positions are outlined in his letter to the secretary general the text of which was made public today in Nicosia. In his seven-page letter, the president reiterates his position that he accepts the package of confidence-building measures as proposed by Butrus-Ghali, that is, the whole fenced-off area of Famagusta coming under UN administration and the Nicosia International Airport reopening under United Nations' control. The president stresses that Turkish Cypriot leader Rauf Denktas has not replied in a straightforward way on whether he accepts the agreement proposed by Butrus-Ghali. After wasting seven months since June, when the talks were suspended because of his stance, the Turkish Cypriot leader's own letter to the secretary general raises several issues outside the framework of the principles set out by the UN secretary general. Kliridhis writes various notes on particular issues raised by Denktas and submits three questions, stressing that he expects clear answers to these questions: First, whether Denktas accepts, irrespective of his so-called concerns about security issues, that the whole fenced-off area of Famagusta be turned over to the United Nations to administer and police and that the Greek Cypriot residents will return to their homes and properties. Second, whether Denktas abandons any notion of the Greek Cypriot side recognizing, either directly or indirectly, through any practical measures, legal status for the occupied ports and the airport in the north. Third, whether Denktas accepts that any confidence-building measures agreed upon must be implemented without waiting to see if they are in line with an overall and complete Cyprus solution. The president continues: On Denktas' concerns about security issues raised in his letter to Your Excellency, both regarding the final solution and the confidence-building measures, I wish to highlight that the Greek Cypriot side also has serious concerns about security issues. Since both sides, the president adds in his letter, admit that they have serious concerns about security, obviously, we must discuss and resolve this issue before any other, instead of finding it in front of us every time we reach a step toward progress. If Denktas is ready to discuss both sides' concerns about security, I am ready to participate, the president concludes.
