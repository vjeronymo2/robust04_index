930724 FT 24 JUL 93 / CGT for June THE TABLE shows capital gains tax allowances for assets sold in June. Multiply the original cost of the asset for the figure shown for the month in which you bought it. Subtract the results from the proceeds of your sale and the balance will be your taxable gain or loss. Suppose that you bought shares for Pounds 5,000 in June 1985 and sold them in June 1993 for Pounds 13,000. Multiplying the original cost by the June 1985 figure of 1,478 gives a total of Pounds 7,390. Subtracting that from the Pound 13,000 gives a capital gain of Pounds 5,610, which is below the 1993-94 CGT allowance of Pounds 5,800. If you sell shares bought before April 6 1982, you should use the March 1982 figure.
