August 8, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: TRUMP'S 'CRYPTO-NAZI' CONNECTION STIRS GOSSIP

EVERYBODY'S TALKING about Marie Brenner's interview in Vanity Fair, especially that concerning Donald Trump's manner of having glided over his German ancestors. (It's true that reporter James Revson laid all this out in his Newsday column months ago, but Marie has revived it with an added dash of spice.) To wit, that Donald appears to take aspects of his German background seriously.

John Walter works for the Trump organization and when he visits Donald in his office, Ivana told a family friend, he clicks his heels and says, "Heil Hitler," possibly as a family joke.

Reporter Brenner lays out the details of a somewhat amazing story that has been making the rounds but, to my knowledge, was never nailed down before; a story to the effect that Trump reads from a book of Hitler's collected speeches, having kept the work "My New Order" in a cabinet by his bed. (A copy of this is also now in the office of Ivana's lawyer, Michael Kennedy, "as if it were a grenade.") Brenner notes that Hitler was a master propagandist and that Trump has followed in his footsteps, at least in this regard. She calls his habit of exaggeration and prevarication "the big lie," and notes that if one keeps reiterating something, then some people will finally come to believe it.

WHEN MARIE bearded Trump in his den on this issue, he said that it was his friend Marty Davis of Paramount Pictures who'd given him a copy of "Mein Kampf" and he's a Jew. Davis says he didn't give Trump "Mein Kampf," but the aforesaid "My New Order." "I thought he would find it interesting. I am his friend, but I'm not Jewish."

Trump told Brenner, "If I had these speeches, and I am not saying that I do, I would never read them." It is left for Brenner to ask if perhaps Ivana is hoping to convince her friends and her lawyers that her husband "is a crypto-Nazi."

Brenner describes Ivana as having become something like her husband -- "a double agent, able to project innocence and utter confidence." And she quotes Kennedy, describing his beautiful client as suffering from the "Stockholm syndrome" -- meaning that "she had the mentality of a captive. After a while she couldn't fight her captor anymore and began to identify with him. Ivana is deaf, dumb and blind when it comes to Donald."

ONE THING you'll be able to pick up from this magazine story is Trump's attitude today.

Brenner quotes a Trump attorney as saying: "Donald's very up. This is the kind of challenge Donald likes. It's weird. You would never know anything is wrong."
