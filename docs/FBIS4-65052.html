BFN [Text] Brussels, May 11 (AFP) -- Economic growth in the European Union [EU] should reach 1.6 percent this year, higher than the previously predicted figure of 1.25 percent, the European Commission said here Wednesday [11 May]. The EU's executive, issuing its latest economic forecasts, added that growth measured by gross domestic product (GDP) could reach 2.5 percent in 1995, up from the previous official prediction of 2.1 percent. Last year the EU's aggregate GDP shrank by 0.3 percent.
