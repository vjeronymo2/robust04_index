Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Premier Li Peng met with two out-going and six newly-appointed foreign ambassadors to China here this afternoon. The two out-going ones were: Italian Ambassador Oliviero Rossi and Chilean Ambassador Eduardo Bravo Woodhouse. The six newly-appointed ambassadors were: Mustafizur Rahman, of Bangladesh, Sawanit Khongsiri, of Thailand, Sverre Bergh Johansen, of Norway, Juwana, of Indonesia, Bassam Kubbah, of Iraq, and Victor J. Gauci, of Malta. During the meeting, the Chinese premier briefed the ambassadors on China's domestic situation, saying that to develop the national economy was the central job for China. The whole country, with one heart, was now concentrating on accomplishing the tasks set by the newly concluded second session of China's 8th National People's Congress. Premier Li said that China needs a peaceful international environment, and it is willing to develop cooperative relations of mutual benefit with all friendly nations. Li expressed his thanks to the two out-going ambassadors for their efforts in promoting China's relations with their respective countries during their terms, and he hoped after they return home, they would continue to contribute to the development of such relations. The Chinese premier said that he hoped that the new ambassadors will, in their terms, make efforts for the promotion of relations in various fields between China and their own nations, and that he is expecting to see a sustained development of such relations.
