March 9, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

EDBERG, GILBERT WIN; MUSTER ELIMINATED BY MANSDORF

Second-seeded Stefan Edberg of Sweden and sixth-seeded Brad Gilbert won their second-round matches in contrasting fashion Wednesday, while fourth-seeded Thomas Muster of Austria was eliminated by unseeded Amos Mansdorf in the $415,000 Eagle tennis tournament.

Mansdorf, of Israel, defeated Muster, 7-5, 6-2.

Edberg, the 1988 Wimbledon champion currently ranked fourth in the world, needed just 77 minutes to beat Austrian Horst Skoff, 7-5, 6-3.

Gilbert, who won this WCT event in 1987, when it was called the Scottsdale Open, struggled before turning back Scott Davis, 2-6, 6-3, 6-4.
