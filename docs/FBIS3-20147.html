Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Text] The vote today and tomorrow vote is considered to be of historic importance. For Italy it is a matter of turning a page in history, and it is the voters who are deciding the transition from the First to the Second Republic. Voting is taking place in a completely calm atmosphere. This morning the most important state figures cast their votes. Rocco Tolfa reports. [Tolfa] Quiet please, we are voting. Italians are going to the polls and the party leaders are keeping quiet after a fiery electoral campaign. However, the leading figures of the Italian state are saying that these elections truly represent a historic turning point. This was stressed by President Oscar Luigi Scalfaro, who voted in his hometown of Novara. The head of state said this is a decisive moment in the history of our country. Prime Minister Carlo Azeglio Ciampi, who cast his vote in Rome, stressed the importance of this vote when he spoke to our reporter, Anna La Rosa. [Begin Ciampi recording] I hope that the Saturday of silence helped the Italian people to meditate internally and to ask themselves questions about past experiences and about the true interests of the country in the future. [end recording] [Tolfa] Ciampi refused to say any more. He held up the law of electoral silence, which he said he did not intend to break so as not to disturb the Italian voters who are about to turn a new page.
