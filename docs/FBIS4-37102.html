BFN [Text] Baghdad, 16 Jun (TANJUG) -- Iraq and Yugoslavia today expressed their commitment -- during the hardships currently faced by both countries -- to finding mutually useful solutions for problems in their current cooperation and its development, when the conditions for it are ripe. This is a brief summary of the talks held in Baghdad this afternoon between Milorad Unkovic, minister of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia for economic relations with foreign countries and Iraqi Trade Minister Muhammad Mahdi Salih. This meeting opened a several-day-long session of a joint Yugoslav-Iraqi committee for economic and technical development. The aim of the session is to review the cooperation so far and establish the total outstanding claims of Yugoslav companies in Iraq. In addition to the federal Yugoslav minister, the talks are being attended by FRY Deputy Foreign Minister Radoslav Bulajic, and Jovan Kostic, the charge d'affaires of the FRY Embassy in Baghdad, as well as a group of directors of large Yugoslav companies whose firms have carried out work in Iraq. The Yugoslav delegation is expected to hold talks with senior Iraqi economic and state leaders over the next few days.
