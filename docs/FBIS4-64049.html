BFN [Text] Ukrainian Supreme Soviet Speaker Aleksandr Moroz told deputies today that Communist representatives would head the parliamentary commission on the fuel and energy complex, that on transport and communications and the commission on legislation and law. Moroz said that a RUKh [Ukrainian People's Movement for Perestroyka] representative would lead the commission on nuclear policy and nuclear safety. The Unity faction will lead the commission on juridical policy and state structure, that on local government and the commission on economic policy. Representatives of the Center group will head the commission on health care, protection of childhood and motherhood and that on basic industries and social-economic development of regions. Moroz commented on the balance of forces in the parliament. It is the following: the communist faction - 87 representatives; the Center deputy group - 43; the socialist faction - 25; the agrarian faction - 33; the Unity faction - 27; the Reforms deputy group - 26 and the Statehood faction 26 representatives. There are 41 independent deputies who have joined none of these groups or factions.
