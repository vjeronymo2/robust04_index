BFN [Text] Today participants in a meeting on Nagorno-Karabakh opened in Bishkek on May 4 at the initiative of the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly started to discuss a protocol on a cease-fire in the zone of the Armenian-Azeri conflict. As Interfax learned from well-informed sources in the Kyrgyz capital, the cease-fire is planned for May 9, 1994. The meeting is attended by Chairman of the Armenian parliament Babken Ararktsyan, Deputy Chairman of the Azeri Milli-Mejlis Afiyaddin Dzhalilov, Acting Chairman of the Karabakh parliament Karen Baburyan, and mediators Chairman of the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly and the Russian Council of Federation Vladimir Shumeyko and Chairman of the Kyrgyz parliament Medetkan Sherimkulov. As Sherimkulov told Interfax during a break that the talks were difficult and their results unknown. At the same time, he expressed hope that on Wednesday the sides will agree to sign the protocol. As a well-informed source in the Azeri presidential administration told Interfax, Chairman of the Azeri parliament Rasul Guliyev failed to arrive in Bishkek due to the aggravation of the situation in the zone of the Karabakh conflict. The source said that Guliyev has been in the front line zone in the past few days.
