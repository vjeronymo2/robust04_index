BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 25 KYODO -- Imports of crude oil in March rose 4.2 percent from the year before to 149.26 million barrels, posting the second straight month of year-to-year gains, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) said Monday [25 April]. As a result, crude oil imports for fiscal 1993 totaled 1,613.07 million barrels, up 0.3 percent from the preceding year and a gain for the seventh consecutive year. The United Arab Emirates was the biggest supplier of crude in the reporting month with 39.63 million barrels, followed by Saudi Arabia with 27.05 million barrels, Iran with 18.30 million barrels and Indonesia with 12.39 million barrels, MITI said. The amount of crude oil processed in March increased 4.0 percent to 136.24 million barrels, registering the third consecutive month of year-to-year gains. Processed crude for fiscal 1993 fell 1.7 percent to 1,473.37 million barrels, slipping from year-before levels for the sixth year in a row. Fuel oil output for March came to 131.34 million barrels, up 5.1 percent for the eighth consecutive year-to-year rise. The annual total was given as 1,376.31 million barrels, up 3.2 percent. Fuel oil imports for March rose 6.2 percent to 16.67 million barrels while fuel oil exports grew 4.0 percent to 10.13 million barrels. Domestic fuel oil sales for March were up the fourth month in a row, rising 3.1 percent to 142.66 million barrels, a record for any month of March. Those for fiscal 1993 edged down 0.4 percent to 1,428.52 million barrels.
