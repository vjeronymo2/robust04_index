Language: English Article Type:BFN [Quotation marks as received] [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 5/1/1994 -- "The Social Democracy Party of Romania [PDSR], that assumed the responsibility of ruling the country in these difficult moments of crisis, is keen on reaffirming on this occasion as well that Romania's integration in the Euro-Atlantic structures is a fundamental objective of our foreign policy," reads a communique released in Bucharest on 5 January. "From this position, PDSR fully backs the official efforts Romania is making to become a member of the North Atlantic Alliance. PDSR salutes the initiative of Romania's President Ion Iliescu to address on 24 December a letter to the heads of states or governments of the NATO member states, before the upcoming meeting of the alliance, reconfirming Romania's constant interest to become a full member of the NATO structures. PDSR took note with satisfaction of the recent declarations of high officials in the U.S. Administration, according to whom "the Partnership for Peace" is for the budding democracies in Europe, Romania included, a first step in the process of their integration in the NATO structures and a guarantee of security at the same time. We are sure that under the conditions of the political developments in our proximity, NATO will be in a position to offer security guarantees to Romania, necessary for preserving its national being and ensuring conditions for carrying the democratic transformations the Romanian people has committed to since the December 1989 revolution. On the eve of the Brussels meeting of the NATO countries, PDSR expresses hope that Romania's security interests will resonate with the leaders of the NATO member states, that no discriminatory practices will be accepted concerning the countries interested to enter the Euro-Atlantic structures. At the same time, we understand the concerns repeatedly expressed by the U.S. Administration and other NATO member countries for not isolating Russia and for taking into account its security interests, without prejudicing in this way Romania's security interests."
