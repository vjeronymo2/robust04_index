March 3, 1989, Friday, Southland Edition

HEREDITARY RULERS PICK MALAYSIAN MONARCH

Nine hereditary rulers Thursday elected Sultan Azlan Muhibuddin Shah, a 60-year-old former Supreme Court judge, as Malaysia's monarch for the next five years.

After two days of deliberations at the national palace, the sultans in a secret ballot selected Azlan, the present deputy king, to ascend to the throne. He will take office in April, replacing King Mahmood Iskandar, 56, the sultan of the southern state of Johore, who took office in 1983.
