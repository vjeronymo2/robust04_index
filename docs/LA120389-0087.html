December 3, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OTHER COMMENTARY / EXCERPTS;

IS WORLD READY FOR GERMANY?

The cautious chancellor of West Germany, Helmut Kohl, has just caught his countrymen by surprise with an uncharacteristically bold proposal: Reunification, by-the-numbers -- 10 points that will give us one Germany, one more time.

And now we know for sure that what just months ago was unthinkable is now unstoppable.

America's conservatives love it -- after all, anything that hurts Soviet communism has got to be good. Patrick Buchanan, my frequent sparring colleague on Cable News Network, champions reunification, asking: "What precisely are we afraid of? . . . Was not a free, united Germany what we fought for in World War II?"

Not the way I recall it. And not the way many sincere Germans recall it. They, too, understand the lessons of the history they've either lived or read. And they, too, seem concerned that a significant minority of their countrymen hasn't learned this lesson and perhaps never will. They were troubled by the results of a survey of 2,200 Germans published in Der Spiegel magazine last April. Asked to rate Adolf Hitler on a numerical scale (ranging from very negative to neutral to very positive), a frightening 26% replied that they were neutral or positive about Der Fuhrer. And 43% answered there was both "good and bad" to say about the Nazis.

No wonder that, in the same survey, 44% of Germans said they didn't favor reunification -- it would mean one nation of 78 million Germans with one army 650,000 strong. History, a discipline that may have eluded Pat Buchanan, is clearly on the minds of Germans these days.
