BFN [Text] Prague, April 28 (CTK) -- The activity of Austrian politicians concerning the completion of the Temelin nuclear power plant in south Bohemia should not be seen as a threat to or violation of the Czech Republic's sovereignty, Czech Premier Vaclav Klaus told Parliament today. He said steps taken on the issue by some Austrian politicians should not serve as a reason for the Czech diplomacy to resort to resolute actions the consequences of which might hamper the Czech Republic's relations with Austria. Czech-Austrian relations in the power industry are defined by a Czech-Austrian agreement on nuclear security and protection against radiation. According to the agreement, experts from both countries meet annually and Austrian representatives are informed on all Czech goals concerning the use of nuclear power.
