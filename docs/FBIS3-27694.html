Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, February 25 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice Premier Zhu Rongji said here today the ongoing reform in China will push forward bilateral cooperation between Tokyo and Beijing, Chinese Foreign Ministry officials said. Zhu said China and Japan have gained "a historical opportunity" to further develop ties as China is now striving for a shift to market economy from the traditional planed economy, the officials said. He made the remarks when he was lunching with Yoshio Sakurauchi, head of the Japanese Association for the Promotion of International Trade. The vice premier spoke highly of the association for having made "precious contributions" to the strengthening of Japan's trade and economic ties with China, they said. In a separate meeting, Zhu briefed a group of leading Japanese bankers on the current economic situation in China and its reforms on finance and taxation. In the evening, he dined with leaders from six organizations for Japan-China friendship and called on them to continue their efforts to help solidify bilateral relations, the officials said. Earlier in the day, he met Japan's International Trade and Industry Minister Hiroshi Kumagai, State Minister for Economic Planning Manae Kubota and former Foreign Minister Michio Watanabe. Zhu arrived here Wednesday [23 February] for a nine-day visit. He is leaving Tokyo Saturday for western and southwestern Japan to take a first-hand look at corporate activities there.
