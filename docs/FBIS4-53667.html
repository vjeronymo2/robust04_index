BFN [Text] Washington, May 21 KYODO -- Japanese and U.S. officials on Saturday [21 May] called off a third day of talks aimed at striking an elusive deal on jump-starting stalled trade talks on ways to increase foreign access to key Japanese markets, informed sources said. The two countries were earlier scheduled to resume the talks on Saturday. Senior U.S. and Japanese officials huddled for more than 10 hours in two days Thursday and Friday to "explore" a common basis to break a three-month deadlock in the so-called "framework" talks.
