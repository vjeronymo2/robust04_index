June 3, 1989, Saturday, Valley Edition

AGOURA TO FACE BIG BEAR FOR SOFTBALL TITLE

When Coach Linda Moule took over as Agoura High softball coach last year, she inherited a team that had won only one game in the two previous seasons.

Moule, who coached the Chargers' junior varsity in 1987, has since turned the program around.

Agoura tied Santa Paula for the Frontier League championship in 1988 and won the title outright this year with a 9-0 league record. The Chargers last won the league crown in 1978.

Now Agoura (22-2), which had never survived the first round of the Southern Section playoffs, will play for the 1-A Division championship. The Chargers, winners of 19 in a row after starting the season 3-2, will play second-seeded Big Bear at 2:30 p.m. Saturday at Mayfair Park in Lakewood.

Because they lack experience in a major tournament championship, the Chargers are slightly numb to the feeling.

"I know they're excited," Moule said. "But I don't think the real meaning of it has hit them yet."

The realization should have sunk in Tuesday when Agoura eliminated top-seeded Paraclete, 10-3, in a semifinal. Big Bear defeated St. Bonaventure, 5-1, in the other semifinal.

Big Bear (22-3) is no stranger to postseason play. The defending Small Schools Division champion is making its fifth consecutive playoff appearance.

Agoura (22-2), the fourth-seeded team, will rely on pitcher Melissa Thatcher -- the Frontier League's most valuable player -- in its attempt to dethrone Big Bear.

Thatcher (22-2), who has an earned-run average of 0.56 and averages six strikeouts a game, threw a four-hitter and struck out four against Paraclete. The sophomore right-hander is a low-ball pitcher who gets a lot of outs on ground balls.

Big Bear, the De Anza League champion, will counter with starter Marjie Sievers (20-3), who has struck out 199 of the 547 batters she has faced this season.

The Chargers are led by catcher Lisa Little, the only senior on the team and, according to her coach, a sound defensive player. "We haven't had very many people steal against us this year," Moule said, adding that at 6-foot-1 she blocks the plate well. "If she puts her shoulder down, you can forget it."

Little (.430 batting average) Jill Hartwig (.380) and Leslie North (.400) are the team's top batters. Wendy Baertschiger had three hits against Paraclete.

Big Bear's Chelcee Clifton, Coach Mike Clifton's daughter, is batting a team-high .635 and has five home runs. She is also the team leader in runs batted in (39) and runs scored (40).
