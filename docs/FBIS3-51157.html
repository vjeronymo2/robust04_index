Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Chile monitored through 13 January. According to the National Statistics Institute, INE, the accumulated inflation rate during 1993 was 12.2 percent, the lowest in the past 12 years. The Consumer Price Index, IPC, in December 1993 was 0.2 percent. The unemployment rate, according to the INE, during the last quarter of 1993 was 4.4 percent. This means 225,000 workers are unemployed. (Santiago Television Nacional de Chile Imagen Internacional in Spanish 0000 GMT 6 Jan 94) Foreign investment in the first 11 months of 1993 totalled $2.3 billion, an increase of 91.7 percent compared with the same period in 1992. Mining registered the highest investment with 47.1 percent, industry with 29.4 percent, services with 16.8 percent, transportation with 2.7 percent, and forestry with 1.5 percent. The major investors are the United States with 36.8 percent, Canada with 11.5 percent, Great Britain with 8.5 percent, Spain with 7.2 percent, Australia with 5.6 percent, and Japan with 4.3 percent. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 23 Dec 93 p B1) On 22 December 1993, the National Fisheries Service reported that fishing exports totalled 1.038 million metric tons from January to October 1993, a 16.4 percent drop compared with the same period in 1992. This yielded a $974 million profit, a 14.7 percent drop. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 23 Dec 93 p B10) Exports of textiles and clothing totalled $144 million in 1993, an increase ov 26.3 percent compared with the previous year's $114 million. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 23 Dec 93 p 22) The Chilean trade balance registered a $826 million deficit in the January-November 1993 period; in the same period in 1992 it registered a $716.5 million surplus. The November trade balance registered the highest deficit with $174 million because of a substantial decline in the copper price. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 24 Dec 93 pp A1, A20) Chilean Foreign Minister Enrique Silva Cimma and Japanese Ambassador Eto Yukinisa exchanged notes in which Japan donates $14 million in nonreimbursable cooperation to build the most modern fish market in Chile. It will be built in Lo Espejo District; its design is based on the most modern Japanese experience and technology. It will work as a center to promote the consumption of sea products and will be ready by 1995. Japan will construct the market, while Chile will provide the site and pay administrative and supervision costs. (Santiago Radio Cooperativa Network in Spanish 1000 GMT 13 Jan 94)
