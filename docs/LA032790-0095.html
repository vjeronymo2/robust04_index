CULTURAL IDENTITY CRISIS IN 'AMERICAN EYES'

Adolescence is a trying time under the best of circumstances, but 16-year-old John Henderson has added trials: he's an adopted Asian in an all-Caucasian family, in an all-Caucasian town.

How he comes through his identity crisis and comes to terms with his adopted family and his cultural heritage is the subject of today's feel-good-about-yourself "CBS Schoolbreak Special," "American Eyes" (3 p.m., Channels 2 and 8).

John (Jason Scott Lee) has his differences pointed out to him by a bigoted high school bully. He resents his adopted brother's (Brian Krause) protection and begins to question his place in the close-knit Henderson family.

David Ogden Stiers, as his concerned dad, urges him to go to a Korean cultural summer camp for teens to find his roots. Mom (Concetta Tomei) is worried about losing him.

Kelly Hu is the sympathetic and lovely girl John meets at camp who helps him break down his defensiveness and accept who he is with pride.

Herb Stein directed and co-wrote the script with Cynthia Cherbak and Elizabeth Hansen, and it is sensitive and well-acted. But how will John deal with the violent racial prejudice he was faced with in school? We'll never know. It doesn't fit into the quick-fix, happily-ever-after wrap-up. LYNNE HEFFLEY
