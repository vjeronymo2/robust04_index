Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Mar 25, BNS -- Cooperation between the Baltic states develops but "this does not mean that cooperation cannot be improved," Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas said on the eve of his meeting with Estonian and Latvian presidents. The Baltic leaders meet in the Lithuanian seaside resort of Palanga Friday [25 March]. In an interview with the Voice of America and the Latvian newspaper DIENA, Brazauskas said much attention during the summit will be paid to relations of the Baltic states with other countries, including Russia. The three leaders also intend to discuss steps that must be taken to approach the European Union. Well-informed sources said one of Lithuania's initiatives for the summit is a joint resolution on coordination of actions in international organizations. The Baltic presidents are also expected to discuss the work of embassies and possibilities to create joint Baltic consulates. Lithuania intends to propose a resolution on cooperation with countries of the Visegrad Treaty, the Czech Republic, Hungary, Poland and Slovakia. According to the summit's program, the presidents are scheduled to hold trilateral and bilateral meetings.
