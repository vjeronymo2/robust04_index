BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Andrey Palaria and Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow April 20 TASS -- The four-day official visit to Russia by Turkish Defence Minister Mehmet Golhan is expected to give a boost to Russian-Turkish military ties. The first tangible results of the visit that begun just today was the signing an agreement on military and technical cooperation between the two countries by Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev and Golhan in Moscow on Wednesday. During the talks, the two ministers discussed Russia's peace-keeping activity in the United Nations and the CIS. They also focused on the restriction of Russia's positions in the south, following the disintegration of the Soviet Union and the emergence of the Commonwealth of Independent States. The sides also touched upon the Karabakh conflict, the Bosnian crisis and the implementation of the CIS collective security agreement. Grachev will have a one-to-one meeting with his Turkish counterpart on Sunday to discuss prospects for military and technical cooperation between Russia and Turkey and other issues of mutual concern. On Thursday, Golhan will lay a wreath at the tomb of the unknown soldier in Moscow, visit the Mil design bureau and tour a motorised infantry brigade.
