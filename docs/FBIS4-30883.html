BFN [Interview with Defense Minister Valentin Aleksandrov by Georgi Kardanov in Sofia on 26 May: "Current Political Situation Should Not Affect Subordination in the Army"] [Text] Despite the heavy parliamentary program connected with debating the vote of confidence requested by Professor Berov and the tension surrounding some government ministers, before the secret ballot began, Defense Minister Valentin Aleksandrov found time to grant a brief interview to a BULGARSKA ARMIYA reporter. [Kardanov] Mr. Minister, if the government obtains a vote of confidence, what will be the first matter that Minister Valentin Aleksandrov will address? [Aleksandrov] Along with these demands for the government to resign and these numerous votes of confidence that have been moved, a considerable number of problems have accumulated that require solutions, so how can one decide which of them is the most pressing? I have the feeling that some officers seem to have forgotten that the most important thing in the army today is to obey the orders of one's superiors. Regardless of the current political situation, this subordination, as well as order and discipline, must be strictly observed in the army and the armed forces, otherwise we will have no army. During this severe political crisis, it is clear that an extreme psychological load is placed on the troops, which is especially harmful for subordination. [Kardanov] Certain parliamentary forces and political circles have supported the view that your demand for certain high-ranking officers to be retired has created tension in the army. What is your counterargument? [Aleksandrov] Look here, the General Staff has carefully drafted a plan that completely conforms with my views, so that the dismissal of the officers concerned is completely within the order of things, and it will take place in the fall. I do not see any grounds for tension as a result of this fact, or at least cannot not accept the force of these grounds, even in the event that the Defense and Armed Forces Act is not passed, and I personally cannot undertake to affirm that parliament will indeed pass it. [Kardanov] You mentioned the words "in the event that the Defense and the Armed Forces Act is not passed...." Prime Minister Lyuben Berov's short-term program envisages passing the law before the end of June, yet yesterday Ahmed Dogan, chairman of the parliamentary group of the Rights and Freedoms Movement, told a reporter that one should not be too hasty to pass the law, that it is a very extensive piece of legislation, so that there is hardly likely to be time to pass it. What is the true situation? [Aleksandrov] I cannot say which is best, but in any case the The Defense and the Armed Forces Act should be carefully examined from all angles. It is better for us to continue to work with the existing law to give time to precisely formulate the new legislative material. I do not want to commit myself to making a personal assessment of the bill that has been submitted, because I am the proposer. In any case, we cannot influence the politicians and legislators regarding their priorities. On the other hand, this issue has begun to be interpreted too much on a personal level, and for this reason I prefer to make no comment. [Kardanov] The National Security Commission is doing everything possible to speed up the ordering of all the amendments and proposals connected with the defense and armed forces bill, so they can be passed on to the Legislative Commission and reach the parliament voting stage more quickly. [Aleksandrov] Yes, it is true that the National Security Commission is well advanced with preparing all the passages of the bill, but nevertheless one should not be in too much of a hurry, so as to avoid having to pass a new law in a year or two. [Kardanov] Minister Aleksandrov, even if the defense and armed forces bill, which is supposed to provide the legislative basis for the democratic reform in the Armed Forces, is delayed, at least the Law on the Consultative Council on National Security was passed. Are you satisfied with the work of this supreme body, which is supposed to coordinate the efforts of all the country's institutions that are concerned with national security? [Aleksandrov] Yes, I have twice attended sessions of the council and I can say that both times they debated important issues of national security that had a positive effect. This gives me good reason to hope that it will also continue to do useful and constructive work in the future.
