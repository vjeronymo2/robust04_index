BFN [Text] Croatian President Dr. Franjo Tudjman received in the Presidential Palace this evening a delegation of countries whose diplomatic envoys make up the contact group for Bosnia-Herzegovina. French Ambassador Henri Marie-Chenu, British Ambassador Brian Sparrow, Belgian Ambassador Frans Hintjens, Aleksandr Grishchenko, deputy head of the Russian diplomatic mission, and German Charge d'Affaires Claude Robert Elner informed President Tudjman about conclusions reached at a meeting in Geneva yesterday by ministers of foreign affairs of the countries whose diplomatic envoys make up the contact group for Bosnia-Herzegovina. They handed President Tudjman a letter which the ministers of the contact group had sent to Alija Izetbegovic, president of the Bosnia-Herzegovina presidency, Kresimir Zubak, representative of the Bosnian Croats, and Bosnian Serb leader Radovan Karadzic, containing the principles that the contact group is going to follow to halt the hostilities in Bosnia-Herzegovina. President Tudjman said he agreed with these principles and promised to exert his influence so that the negotiating sides should accept these principles and new measures for the resolution of the Bosnia-Herzegovina crisis.
