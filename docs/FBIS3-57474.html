Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [ITAR-TASS special correspondents Grigoriy Arslanov, Vyacheslav Tomilin, Georgiy Shmelev] [Text] Beijing, 27 Jan -- Relations with China are a priority trend in Russia's foreign policy, as was stated during the visit of Russian President Boris Yeltsin to the PRC in December 1992, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev stressed. He was speaking before the start of the Russian-Chinese talks with a large number of people attending the Diaoyutai residence for guests of honor in Beijing. "We should like to raise Russian-Chinese relations to a qualitatively new level -- one of constructive partnership -- both in bilateral ties as well as from a regional and an international point of view," Andrey Kozyrev said. Greeting the Russian minister, Qian Qichen, deputy prime minister of the State Council, PRC minister of foreign affairs, noted "according to the Chinese proverb 'spring is a wise time' and starting something in the spring means it will be successful." He expressed the conviction that relations between China and Russia would continue to develop in the interests of the two countries in the new year. Qian Qichen congratulated Andrey Kozyrev on his being confirmed head of the Russian foreign political department.
