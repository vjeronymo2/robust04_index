940917 FT 17 SEP 94 / FT Guide for the Serious Investor (11): Some selected aphorisms of Warren Buffett 'The problem of over-diversifcation (is) if you have a harem of 40 women, you never get to know any of them very well.' 'Markets dominated by the erratic behaviour of the big boys are ideal for any investor . . . as long as he sticks to his investment knitting' 'Short-term forecasts of stock or bond markets are useless . . . They may tell you a lot about the forecaster; they tell you nothing about the future.' 'We try to avoid small commitments. If something's not worth doing at all, it's not worth doing well.' 'If something can't go on forever, it will end.' 'A hyperactive stock market is the pickpocket of enterprise.' 'As a group, lemmings may have a bad image, but no individual lemming ever received a bad press.'
