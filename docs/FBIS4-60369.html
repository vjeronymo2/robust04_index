BFN [Excerpts] Lebanon has complained to the Security Council about Israel's abduction of Mustafa al-Dirani. The complaint was submitted as Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres was meeting UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali in New York. Peres said Yasir 'Arafat has to make clear his commitment to all elements of the Declaration of Principles [DOP] and express opposition to the use of violence in relations with Israel. From New York, Judy Lessing reports: [Begin Lessing recording] Foreign Minister Peres told Mr. Butrus Butrus-Ghali about Israel's relations with Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, and the Palestinians, and he said a high priority is the building of the new Middle East. He said it is Israel which must make the tangible concessions, and Israelis need to know there is a new Middle East. The secretary general assured Mr. Peres the United Nations has a sincere interest in the peace process in the region, and he has offered UN help to promote it, though he did not give many specifics. He did talk about the possibility of setting up some kind of apparatus to oversee UN agencies, the World Bank, and the International Monetary Fund, as the funds promised for the Palestinian self-ruling authority are made available. Foreign Minister Peres says that Israel is pleased its relations with the United Nations are improving, but he said he would like to see more progress, and he told the secretary general Israel wants to become a normal, full participant in the United Nations. For example, he said, Israel is willing to contribute to peacekeeping operations, and though the government would have to approve any contribution of forces to say, Angola, this could be a possibility. At a time when the United Nations is having serious problems getting skilled troops for its operations, this should have been a welcome suggestion. It is almost certain the question of where Ron Arad is was discussed when Mr. Peres and Mr. Butrus-Ghali had a private meeting before their aides joined them. [passage omitted] [end recording] Addressing the Israel Policy Forum, Foreign Minister Peres said that with peace in the Middle East, the threat of nuclear weapons will be eradicated. [Begin Peres recording] We shall have a Middle East free of the danger of war. We shall also, I hope so, have a Middle East free of the danger of the nuclear threat. But it must be in this order. You cannot build a second floor before you have the first one. When the Egyptians in particular are demanding that Israel will sign the NPT [Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty] and so on, we are asking them privately: Gentlemen, are you going to guarantee that Iran is okay, Iraq is okay, Libya is okay? What are you talking about? You cannot have it done partially. Their nuclearization is invisible. Otherwise it does not exist. If we shall have a different Middle East, we shall have a different policy concerning the nonconventional arms. [end recording]
