Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Guatemalan economic developments monitored through 18 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. Willy Zapata, president of the Bank of Guatemala, BANGUAT, announced on 10 January that the Monetary Board has authorized BANGUAT to obtain loans for up to $260 million from foreign credit institutions in 1994. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 11 Jan 94 p 70) BANGUAT authorities have reported that the annual inflation rate as of 31 December 1993 was 11.64 percent, more than two points below the 1992 inflation rate of 14.22 percent. The same sources noted that the goal established by the Monetary Board was met, as inflation was kept below 12.5 percent. (Guatemala City SIGLO VEINTIUNO in Spanish 12 Jan 94 p 5) Finance Minister Richard Aitkenhead Castillo confirmed on 14 January that the World Bank has authorized the second disbursement of funds for the Economic Modernization Program it signed with Guatemala in 1992. Aitkenhead added that in the next few days Guatemala will receive $25 million to be invested in social projects. The total credit is for $120 million, of which $48 million have already been disbursed. (Guatemala City SIGLO VEINTIUNO in Spanish 15 Jan 94 p 6)
