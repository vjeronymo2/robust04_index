Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Yesterday, Government Minister Arnoldo Oriz Moscoso said he plans to counter drug trafficking in Central America in 1994 by reinforcing international agreements. He gave the information outside the National Palace after being asked what he knew about the arrest of Guatemalan citizen Velmar Espina Novo, who was involved in a cocaine shipment in Nicaragua. Ortiz said: "For the time being we have no official information only that a person was arrested. We will therefore solicit information on the alleged Guatemalan drug trafficker and the alleged member of the military, Byron Robeto Morales." He said there are signed agreements on antidrug trafficking, which will be put into effect with the United States, Colombia, Mexico, and the remaining isthmian countries. Ortiz admitted Guatemala is becoming more and more a bridge for drugs and a storage center as well. Regarding the death of inmates in penitentiaries, he said each case is being investigated, although most deaths seem to be prompted by fights or personal vengeance.
