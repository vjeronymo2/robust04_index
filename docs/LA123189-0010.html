December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

ABOUT THE TOP MOVIES

While including on their Top 10 lists for the 1980s voyeuristic sleaze ("Blue Velvet"), hip amateurism ("Do the Right Thing"), childish trivia ("Raiders of the Lost Ark") and pretentious pseudo art ("Raging Bull"), the Times' movie reviewers revealed the prerequisites for their job: short memories and bad taste.

Conspicuous by their absence were such clearly superior works as "Breaker Morant," "The Flight of the Eagle," "The Official Story," "When Father Was Away on Business," "A Cry in the Dark," "Melvin and Howard" and "Diner."

Even Peter Rainer included on his list "E.T." -- which may have been fun, but let's not raise calculating box-office movie candy to the level of films with genuine substance, originality and lasting merit.

DOUGLAS CARASSO

Whittier
