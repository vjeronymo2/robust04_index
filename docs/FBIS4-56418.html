BFN [Text] Havana, 27 Apr (PL) -- Zambian President Frederick Chiluba visited places of economic and social interest in Cuba today to strengthen bilateral cooperation, he said. The president, on an official and friendly visit to our nation upon invitation from his Cuban counterpart, Fidel Castro, visited an agricultural cooperative in western Havana Province and "Hermanos Ameijeiras" Hospital, one of the country's most important. Cuban Health Ministry sources said 22 Cuban specialists render their services in that southern Africa nation, and we hope to increase that number. The Zambian Government has stated its interest in reaching cooperation agreements in the sugar industry and its derivatives, the pharmaceutical industry, and the public health and sports sectors. Local Foreign Ministry sources have announced plans for the construction of a sugar refinery by Cuban technicians, and Lusaka will finance all the operations. The exchange of trainers in several sport disciplines, such as boxing, soccer, and baseball, is also expected. Chiluba was awarded the "Jose Marti" national order, the highest distinction granted by the Cuban Government, and he held official conversations with Fidel Castro, during which they discussed issues dealing with current international politics and the situation in Angola. The Zambian delegation will leave for Varadero Beach, approximately 100 km east of the capital, tomorrow and will travel next Friday to the eastern city of Santiago de Cuba, where many young people from this African nation study.
