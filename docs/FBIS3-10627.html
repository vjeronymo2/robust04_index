Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Cochabamba, 24 Feb -- President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada, commenting on the "rapprochement" with Chile, stated here today that "diplomatic relations are not resumed based on conditions; conditions are not established for this." He added: "It is when one sees that the Chilean people, the Bolivian people, have experienced a true change in mentality that there is true acceptance, that there is hope for negotiations. Many people in Bolivia maintain that we must not resume relations until we establish conditions, but this leads nowhere. What one has to see is the moment in which the Chilean people, the Bolivian people, are willing to start true talks, negotiations, and thus work toward a first meeting." Sanchez de Lozada said that "things are changing rapidly," as are the countries, and therefore this is "a subject that requires, as I have stated before, audacity and prudence." The chief of state insisted that the subject must be handled "seriously" because "domestic policy is one thing, and international policy another." "There was a feeling that if I attended this presidential inauguration [Chilean President-elect Eduardo Frei's inauguration on 11 March] it would be a sort of acknowledgement, a tacit resumption of diplomatic relations. I think there are chances that this will happen at some point, but I still do not have a deadline nor a date set for restoring relations. We will see what happens with time, but we thought this was not the appropriate moment. But neither was it a matter of turning the invitation down, so we thought the best solution would be to send someone close to me who at the same time did not belong to the government, and I think we have come up with an imaginative solution." However, consulted about the significance to Bolivia of the landlocked subject, he said: "Well, the sea problem has existed for more than 100 years, and we are not going to solve it with my wife's visit, but it represents a rapprochement. It is very hard to hold a dialogue with someone with whom you have no relations. You must talk to reach an understanding. We believe this was the adequate solution, and a step forward." Sanchez de Lozada referred to the invitation extended to him by Chilean President Patricio Aylwin, which he termed "important." The president ended by stating: "This is how one starts a rapprochement, trying to find the right moment for this rapprochement process so it can result in something concrete and to the benefit of the Bolivian people."
