Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Tsutomu Hata, deputy prime minister and foreign minister, gave a news conference in his capacity as head of Shinseito [Japan Renewal Party] at the Diet building on 29 January. Touching on the question of Japan's sending personnel to the former Yugoslavia to participate in UN Peacekeeping Operations (PKO), Hata said: "From an official standpoint, the overall situation has not met with the five principles on PKO participation (such as a cease-fire accord) yet. We will have to clarify this point." Thus, he indicated his view that the current situation in the former Yugoslavia does not fulfill the five principles.
