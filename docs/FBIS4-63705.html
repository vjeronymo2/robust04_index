BFN [Text] The brief meeting between Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk and British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd in Kiev on Tuesday [24 May] will focus on settlement of the Crimean issue. This was said by Hurd himself in an interview with INTERFAX in Murmansk. Hurd suggested that this issue must be considered within the scope of development of friendly relations between Great Britain and Ukraine. Hurd recommended that talks on Crimea be held in the presence of the Russian party which could contribute to better understanding between Kiev and Simferopol through its calm and constructive participation. "We hope that both parties are reasonable enough to solve the problem by peaceful methods," Hurd said.
