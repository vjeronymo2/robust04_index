940114 FT 14 JAN 94 / UK Company News: Harmony stake in Leisuretime Harmony Property, the former hotel and restaurants group, has harked back towards its old business by acquiring a stake in Leisuretime Inns, the unquoted pubs operator. The company, which last month changed its name from Harmony Leisure, said it was teaming up with Mr Barry Cox, a joint founder and former operator of the Hard Rock Cafe, to inject fresh finance into Leisuretime. Mr John Main, Harmony's chairman and chief executive, said the company had reached agreement with Mr Cox and Mr Bob Halsey, managing director at Leisuretime, to acquire a joint holding of 29.9 per cent in the group. Mr Cox, whose latest restaurant operation is called Fatboy's Diner, would offer extensive catering expertise to the company, Mr Main added.
