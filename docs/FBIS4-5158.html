BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 8 (KCNA) -- The South Korean Federation of General Student Councils (Hanchongnyon) declared Thursday afternoon that students of more than 140 universities and colleges across South Korea would go on a strike all at once from April 8 in an effort to check a "parliamentary ratification" of the farm market opening and as an expression of protest against signing of the "Uruguay Round Accord" due in Morocco this month, a radio report from Seoul said. Along with this, a more than 200-member "hunger-strike team of Hanchongnyon for national salvation" will launch into an indefinite hunger sit-down at the "National Assembly Hall" in Youido, Seoul, and fasting will be staged in different areas and at universities for an indefinite period, it announced. Hanchongnyon took a vote on the farm market opening and the signing of the "Uruguay Round Accord" among 350,000 students of 145 universities and colleges, and 90.5 percent of the voters expressed opposition to them.
