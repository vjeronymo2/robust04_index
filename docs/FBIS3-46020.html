Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, January 19 (XINHUA) -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev's remark Tuesday [17 January] on military existence in former Soviet republics was "tortured", a Foreign Ministry spokesman said here today. According to local press reports, Kozyrev told a Foreign Ministry meeting that Russia must maintain its military presence in the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) and the Baltic nations to protect Russia's interests. Russia's interests in these areas are "critically important," Kozyrev said, adding that "the major threats against Russia also come from there." His statement incited strong repercussions from the CIS and Baltic nations. Estonian Foreign Ministry has lodged a protest today. The Russian Foreign Ministry spokesman told the INTERFAX news agency that Kozyrev's words was tortured. Kozyrev today also summoned U.S. Ambassador to Moscow, saying that U.S. State Department spokesman's reaction was "difficult to understand."
