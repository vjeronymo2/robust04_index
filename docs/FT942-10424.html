940511 FT 11 MAY 94 / Zil re-opens Zil, Russia's ailing truck manufacturer, said yesterday it had reopened after a 10-day stoppage and much political furore over its troubles, writes Leyla Boulton. Executives at Zil, a Moscow-based enterprise with an old-style management and 85,000 employees, said they proposed to reverse the company's fortunes by focusing on recovering money from debtors in the former Soviet Union, and expanding its vehicle range. They also said Zil would take overdue steps to trim its work force. However, Professor Yevgeny Yasin, the new economic adviser to President Boris Yeltsin, said that Zil had managed to secure financial support from the Moscow mayor's office. Prof Yasin stressed that the Russian government could not afford to help Zil
