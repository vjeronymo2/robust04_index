BFN [Text] The 12th plenary session of the Cuban-Mexican Business Commission, seeking to increase trade relations between the two countries, began at noon today at Hotel Nacional in Havana. The meeting was chaired by Mexican ambassador to Cuba, Carlos Tello Macias. Emilio Carrero, current president of the Cuban business committee, welcomed the participants at the opening session. Tribute was also paid to the memory of Luis Miguel Porras, Mexican Embassy commercial attache to Cuba, who passed away a few days ago. Marcos Durante, chairman of the Mexican business committee, outlined the objectives of the meeting to facilitate new business opportunities through bilateral talks. Porfirio Mederos, vice president of the Cuban Chamber of Commerce, briefly reviewed the Cuban economic situation and stressed the desire to increase commercial relations.
