BFN [Excerpt] Quito, 15 Jun (EFE) -- The Ecuadoran Government today set 7 August as the date for the referendum which President Sixto Duran-Ballen announced on 10 June and which most of the opposition political forces in the country have described as illegal. During a radio and television network today, an announcer just announced the date for the referendum and read the seven-question questionnaire Duran-Ballen presented three days ago. The results of the referendum will decide whether another plebiscite will be held on 2 January 1995. This second plebiscite will be held to learn of the people's wishes regarding the summoning of a constituent assembly to make changes to the Constitution. "Our most heartfelt wish is to make actual changes to the social, political, economic, and moral plans," President Duran-Ballen said during last week's radio and television network. [passage omitted]
