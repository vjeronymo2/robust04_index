January 14, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

FEAR AND REALITY

Regarding "Fear and Reality in the Los Angeles Melting Pot," by Joel Kotkin (Nov. 5): The future will show that in Los Angeles, as with the United States, diversity of the people has strengthened us, not diminished us. My life has been enhanced by the variety of people that I have met of various races and cultures.

I am now able to solve problems in a variety of ways and look at them from different angles.

I work for a company that helped send people to the moon. We did not do this by homogeneity. We accomplished this by having everyone work together and giving a variety of inputs. In the future, there will be many more global projects like this, and we need the diversity of people to succeed. Business is now global in every aspect.

The world is changing quickly, too quickly for some people. Americans will have to get used to change if we want to compete globally. Los Angeles should feel lucky that these people chose us: They can help us move into the 21st Century.

PAUL R. MICKELSON

Los Angeles
