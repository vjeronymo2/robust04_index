BFN [Text] Asked to comment on a REUTER report last night that the governments of European Union states may ask the Twelve to take action if they believe the Turkish side is continuing to obstruct progress in the confidence-building measures, government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis said the Cypriot Government thinks that sanctions are one way to end the Turkish side's intransigence. Referring to the sanctions, the government spokesman said the most important measure is an embargo against the pseudostate's illegal exports, an issue on which a European Court decision is still pending.
