February 4, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

OUTTAKES: FELIX AND OSCAR II?

Jack Lemmon, Walter Matthau, writer Neil Simon and producer Howard W. Koch -- last seen together in 1968 working on the hit film "The Odd Couple" -- recently powwowed at the Beverly Hills Tennis Club. Subject of conversation: an "Odd Couple" sequel.

"This is not a sequel like 'Police Academy 5,' " Koch told us. "We would pick up their lives 20 years later. They're older people now, not old men, but older. They have families and children. The comedic values are there, plus the dramatic values are there."

Prior to the meeting, Matthau said of a possible sequel: "I'm sure (Koch) would prefer to call it 'The Odd Couple 2' to cash in on the words 'Odd Couple.' I was never for sequels, myself. Always avoided them. But I'm open to anything. I certainly wouldn't close my mind to fellows like Neil Simon, Jack Lemmon and Howard Koch."

Simon's "The Odd Couple" has resurfaced in several forms since premiering on Broadway with Matthau and Art Carney in 1965, including a hit TV series and a female stage version.

"We've always talked about doing a sequel through the years," Koch said. "It's been bothering me that we hadn't done it." So Koch asked for the meeting. "We came up with an idea for a story that we all agreed on. Neil said he would do it as soon as he can get some free time to write it, and then we'll go from there." Daniel Cerone
