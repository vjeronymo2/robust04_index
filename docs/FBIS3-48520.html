Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Ministry of Energy estimates that the production of natural gas will be increased to 80 million cubic feet [cu ft] per day from the present ceiling of 40 million. Myanmar Oil and Gas Enterprise, in this open season, has increased its natural gas production and test wells are being drilled in addition to the existing four. Production from Aphyauk Gas Well alone could reach the 80 million cubic feet per day very soon. The gas thus produced will be transported to Yangon through 14 in. pipes, and from here [Yangon] to Sittaung and Myainggale through a network of 10 in., eight in. and six in. pipes. The laying of pipelines in now under way. On completion, natural gas will be conveyed to the gas-driven electric power station in Ywama and the cement factory in Myainggale. A pipeline is also being laid across the Irrawaddy River at Prome-Myanaung crossing. This pipeline will carry gas to the cement plant at Kyangin, the methanol plant at Seiktha and the electric power station at Myanaung. At present, the 40 million cu ft per day production from four wells is being piped to industries in Yangon, Sittaung Paper Mill and Thaketa Power Station as well as to the power station at Shwedaung.
