920721 FT 21 JUL 92 / International Company News: Polish bank flotation well-received POLAND'S Export Development Bank (BRE) yesterday reported the first tranche of its public share offer was oversubscribed. An unnamed Austrian bank has bid to buy 2 per cent of the equity, and declared an interest in acquiring a further 20 to 25 per cent. The first tranche of 450,000 shares, or 22.5 per cent of the bank's equity, was for large investors. The shares were priced at 155,000 zloty each, valuing BRE at about Dollars 23m. The shares will be allocated by August 3. Meanwhile, the second tranche of 300,000 shares for small investors went on offer yesterday. BRE's employees have been sold a further 200,000 shares at half price. In all, 47.5 per cent of BRE's equity is being offered, with the rest held by several state-owned banks, the Foreign Trade Ministry, and around 440 state and private companies. The bank reported net profits of 56bn zloty (Dollars 4.2m) for 1991. Mr Krzysztof Szwarc, BRE's managing director, said net profits for the first half of this year would come out at about 79bn zloty. The sale is Poland's first bank privatisation. The BRE is being advised by N. M. Rothschild and audited by Moore Stephens.
