Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Jan 28, BNS -- At a session of the non-member states committee of the Council of Europe [CE] Parliamentary Assembly Thursday [27 January], Russian representative Yevgeni Ambartsumov claimed that the demand for the withdrawal of Russian troops from Estonia and Latvia is unfair, the Estonian parliament's press service reports. Ambartsumov, to support his claim, referred to the continued presence of Turkish troops in Cyprus. The session discussed the conditions for Russia's joining the Council of Europe. Swedish representative Daniel Tarschys resolutely stated no concessions will be made to Russia, the parliament's press service informs. Mati Hint, a member of the Estonian delegation, and Latvia's Andrejs Pantelejevs emphasized that their countries cannot agree to Russian senior officials' attempts to tie troop withdrawal to various conditions or call it in question. The CE Parliamentary Assembly session Thursday morning discussed the situation of the displaced Kurdish population of Iraq and heard an address by Luxembourg Prime Minister Jacques Santer. The afternoon session focused on European transportation problems, environment, and agriculture.
