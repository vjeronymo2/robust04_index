Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Zheng Guoxiong Pointed Out That Financial Arrangements Must Keep Expenditures Within the Limits of Revenue; Zhang Junsheng Doubted Whether Hong Kong Government Could Honor Its Repeated Promise of Keeping More Reserves For the Government of the Special Administrative Region, SAR"] [Text] Zheng Guoxiong, deputy director of the Hong Kong branch of the XINHUA NEWS AGENCY, said yesterday: We have just received the Hong Kong Government's financial budget proposal but have not yet been able to study it. However, he stated: Financial arrangements must uphold the principle of "keeping expenditures within the limits of revenue." He said: Financial arrangements should be given a long-term consideration and are not something that can be accomplished in a year. The Hong Kong Government must give consideration to its operations for the next several years. Should anything unexpected happen, it must have fully adequate financial reserves to cope with the situation. Moreover, the Hong Kong Government must also consider that, after the reversion of sovereignty, there must be a basic convergence financially as well as a sizable amount of reserves to enable the Hong Kong economy to continue to expand. Zhang Junsheng, deputy director of the Hong Kong Branch of the XINHUA NEWS AGENCY, said: Yesterday afternoon, we received the Hong Kong Government's financial budget proposal for the new financial year. This is represents a change the previous practice of giving the document to the agency only after the financial budget proposal was made public. He stated: If the Hong Kong Government has a financial surplus and is willing to leave it to the SAR Government, we will suggest that the government sign something to show its commitment. He said: Every year the Hong Kong Government remarks that it is leaving large quantities of reserves. He expressed the hope that the Hong Kong Government could effectively implement its statements. Moreover, he said: It is hoped that the Hong Kong Government will leave 25 billion [no currency specified] in financial reserves to the SAR Government in accordance with the Memorandum of Understanding on the new airport.
