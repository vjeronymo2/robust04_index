May 16, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

STATE OFFICIALS CONTINUE AERIAL SPRAYING AGAINST PESKY MEDFLY

Thoughts inspired by "Officials Abandon Spraying Deadline" (Part A, May 5):

Long before aerosol spray cans filled with exotic chemicals became the commonly-accepted weapon in the war on insects, around the house and garden, a labor-intensive, hand-pump spray gun was used on the home front, accompanied by the battle cry, "Quick, Henry, the flit!"

Today, given Gov. Deukmejian's stony support of the indiscriminate aerial dousing of residential areas with a pesticide in the futile war on the Medfly, the slogan is now, "Quick, Duke, the malathion!"

When China's teeming population was fast becoming outnumbered by flies, Mao had fly swatters issued to every household with orders for everyone to meet a daily fly-kill quota. Presto! Soon no more flies.

Deukmejian should take a page from Chairman Mao's book and order Southern Californian homeowners to take to their gardens armed with this fail-safe weapon. Medflies wouldn't stand a chance against such an invincible Swat Team.

KYLE T. SCHWIMMER

Los Angeles
