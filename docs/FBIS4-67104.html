BFN [Text] Pakistan and Iran have agreed to make joint efforts with technical and financial assistance from the United Nations to check drug trafficking along their borders. A memorandum of understanding to this effect was signed in Tehran by retired Major General Nasirullah Khan Babar, Pakistani interior minister; 'Ali Mohammad Besharati-Jahromi, Iranian interior minister; and Giorgio Giacomelli, executive director of the UN International Narcotics Control Program. Under the agreement, antinarcotics measures along the border between the two countries will be made more effective, communications facilities will be improved, and arrangements will be made for training professionals of law-enforcement agencies. About $30 million will be spent on the new antinarcotics measures, of which $21 million will be paid by Iran which will mostly be used for road construction to facilitate the speedy movement of security forces. Pakistan will provide $5.5 million, while the UN International Narcotics Control Program will pay $3.6 million.
