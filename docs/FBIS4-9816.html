BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 3 Apr (NOTIMEX) -- Delegates of the Socialist Renewal Movement (CRS) signed today the peace agreement achieved with the government of President Cesar Gaviria, which will lead to the demobilization of 300 rebels on 9 April. The rebels at the conference, which was held at the rebel camp in Flor del Monte, some 1,300 km north of Santa Fe de Bogota, stuck to their request for a seat in the Senate once the organization turns over its weapons. The CRS, a dissident faction of the National Liberation Army (ELN), signed a political agreement with the Colombian Government last week. The agreement signed between the guerrillas [words indistinct] and the Colombian Government includes the government's pledge to grant sufficient resources for the incorporation of the CRS into the country's democratic life and guaranteeing two seats in the Chamber of Deputies for the rebel group. The CRS summit, which began on 31 March and concluded today, welcomed the proposal of Jacinto Ruiz and Gabriel Borja -- its negotiators -- to vote blank on 29 May, the date of the presidential elections in Colombia. After rejecting the candidacy of Antonio Navarro Wolf as an alternative, the CRS plenum announced that it will promote a political movement "with a high degree of internal democracy that will set us apart from the bipartite tradition that has dominated this nation." According to a CRS spokesperson, participants in the summit included at least 140 CRS delegates as well as followers of the legally constituted left wing, leaders of unions, and peasant organizations that are interested in the new political project.
