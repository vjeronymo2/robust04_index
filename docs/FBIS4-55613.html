BFN [Text] Zvornik, 17 May (TANJUG) -- Radovan Karadzic, president of the Serb Republic, and David Owen and Thorvald Stoltenberg, cochairmen of the Conference on Former Yugoslavia, had talks in Zvornik this evening. Their talks focused on the Geneva meeting between the ministers of foreign affairs of the United States, Russia, and the European Union, and the work of the contact group for former Bosnia-Herzegovina. According to a report broadcast this evening on the Serb TV-Sarajevo Studio, the Serbian side presented to the cochairmen its views on the resolution of the Bosnia-Herzegovina war and told them that the Muslims were continuing their spring offensive. The talks, which were also attended by Momcilo Krajisnik, president of the people's assembly of the republic of Srpska and Nikola Koljevic, vice president of the republic of Srpska were assessed as being very important and useful for the continuation of the peace process in former Bosnia-Herzegovina.
