Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Former Soviet Defense Minister Dmitriy Yazov has denied accusations of his involvement in the death of three persons during the August 1991 putsch. At the court hearings in Moscow Wednesday he said that soldiers had to shoot only under extreme conditions. He said that in line with the military regulations servicemen have the right to use fire arms in case of an attack on a patrol. Yazov said that the eight armored personnel carriers that moved along the Garden Ring were patrol vehicles and soldiers inside them were forced to open fire when they faced danger to their lives. The ex-minister said that as a result of the investigation into the murder of three persons, experts found no corpus delicti in the actions of servicemen of the Taman Division who were involved in the incident. The court hearings will continue January 27 at 10 a.m.
