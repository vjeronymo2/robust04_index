921103 FT 03 NOV 92 / Parliament and Politics: EC Commissioner was Britain of losing inward investment BRITISH EC Commissioner Sir Leon Brittan warned yesterday that Britain was in danger of losing inward investment if it failed to ratify the Maastricht Treaty. 'A number of important business ventures are being delayed, are holding fire, because people in Japan or the United States are asking themselves the question: is Britain going to be really going ahead with its partners or is Britain going to be holding back?' If Britain failed to ratify the treaty, he said, 'the damaging effect would be on the whole country'. Sir Leon, above (right) with Tory MP Hugh Dykes, said this scenario had been the nightmare of all politicians for a generation.
