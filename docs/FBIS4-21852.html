BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian guerrilla activities monitored through 4 April. Two police officers were killed when a two-kg explosive device launched against the home of Fabio Ospina Giraldo, former secretary of the Administrative Services of the Valle Governorship, exploded in southern Cali. This is the second time that police officers assigned to protect Ospina have been killed. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 30 March 94 p 8a) The Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia's (FARC) 12th Front burned 27 new vehicles being transported by car carriers on the Barrancabermeja-Puerto Araujo highway. Subversive checkpoints have appeared throughout the department. Military units in armored vehicles initiated an intense sweep operation where daily the guerrillas' actions cause significant losses to the transportation sector. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1800 GMT 31 March 94) Army troops supported by helicopters attempted yesterday to encounter two guerrilla commandos that last Friday attacked two military patrols in Boyaca and Cesar, leaving eight dead soldiers and 11 wounded. Meanwhile, on 26 March ELN (Army of National Liberation) rebels dynamited once more the Cano Limon-Covenas oil pipeline at the Km 60 marker in Arauquita Municipality. As a result pumping was halted although it was resumed yesterday. In Bogota, the XIII Brigade uncovered a secret factory of guerrilla uniforms. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 27 March 94 p 19a) Pedro Jose Chavarria David, alias "Colacho" or "El Mono," third man of the dissolved Popular Liberation Army's (EPL) dissident movement, was captured together with two other persons in a Medellin neighborhood, official sources reported today. The sources said that Chavarria is in charge of the finances of the EPL's dissident movement, which is comprised of 150 men who operate in the Antioquia, Risaralda, and Caldas departments. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1637 GMT 1 April 94) The Colombian Government today blamed FARC for the murder of Prospero Merino, councilman and political leader of the Caribbean community in Cienaga, Magdalena Department, in an attempt to hamper the 29 May presidential elections. Merino was shot in a small town outside Cienaga by five men who also killed Luis Sanchez accompanying him while two others were wounded. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 1535 GMT 3 April 94) Army sources disclosed an alleged terrorist plan by the guerrilla against several government institutions at the Narino Department. Authorities confirmed that at the former Public Works Ministry's camp were seized 230 kg of dynamite, 1,250 meters of slow fuse, as well as maps of institutions to be allegedly attacked by the Farc's 29th Front. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 4 April 94) On 30 March, guerrillas set 27 new cars and the trailer in which they were being transported on the Pan-American Highway near Barrancabermeja. In addition, a bus -- which was carrying no passengers -- was also destroyed on the road between Bucaramanga and Maicao. It was also reported that during the past few hours army troops killed seven FARC and ELN guerrillas during clashes in Santander and Putumayo. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 2 April 94 pp 1A, 8A) Two presumed FARC guerrillas were killed on 1 April during clashes in the rural area of Sampues, Sucre Department. The Army confiscated a .38-caliber revolver, a .9-mm pistol, ammunition, and subversive propaganda. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 3 April 94 p 16A)
