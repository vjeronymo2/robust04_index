BFN [Text] Moscow May 21 TASS -- The Russian Government adopted on Saturday main guidelines of social policy in 1994 aimed at stabilisation and gradual improvement of living standards in Russia. The document signed by Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin obliges all federal executive bodies to draw up their programmes of targeted social assistance and wage increases on these guidelines. They call in particular for measures to stop further decreases of wages and salaries, balance economic interests and material possibilities of three major categories of the population -- employees, entrepreneurs and disabled people, reform the social sphere and increase the role of the state in employment and retraining of citizens. The document also envisages establishing efficient private ownership of housing and preservation of housing subsidies for lower income families.
