BFN [Text] None of the candidates in yesterday's presidential elections received the necessary number of votes -- 50 percent of all votes cast -- to achieve an outright victory and become president of Belarus. However, two candidates -- who will continue their battle for the presidential post in a second round of voting -- have been specified. They are People's Deputy Alyaksandr Lukashenka, former chairman of the legislative anticorruption commission, and Belarusian Prime Minister Vyachaslaw Kebich. Alyaksandr Lukashenka was the obvious leader in yesterday's presidential race -- he received some 2,659,409 votes -- 45.1 percent. Vyachaslaw Kebich received 17.4 percent of the vote (1,025,732 votes). Zyanon Paznyak, leader of the opposition Belarusian Popular Front faction in the legislature, received 12.9 percent (757,586 votes), former Supreme Soviet Chairman Stanislaw Shushkevich -- 9.9 percent (584,604 votes), Alyaksandr Dubko, chairman of the Belarusian Union of Kolkhozes -- 6 percent (356,222 votes), and [Vasyl Novikaw], cochairman of the Communist Party of Belarus -- 4.6 percent. BELAPAN draws attention to the fact that these are preliminary results, subject to change, however, not considerably. All in all, 5,870,055 voters participated in the elections. The total number of eligible voters in Belarus is 7,457,939. At a briefing held this morning, Alyaksandr Abramovich, chairman of the Central Commission for Presidential Elections, predicts the need to hold a second round of voting. According to him, the final results of the 23 June vote will likely be announced on 30 June. The second round of presidential elections will be held no later than 14 days after the results of the first round are officially announced.
