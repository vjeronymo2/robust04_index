Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Belgrade, 16 Jan (TANJUG) -- From 24 January Yugoslavs will have a new, convertible dinar, worth one German mark. According to the Decree on the New Dinar and the Decree on the Dinar Exchange Rate, adopted by the Federal Government this evening, the new dinar will be in circulation both in giro and in effective form [as received] and can be exchanged for any foreign currency. At a news conference after the federal government's session, Dragoslav Avramovic, the head of the team of experts which made the economic policy programme, and Ratko Banovic, vice governor of the National Bank of Yugoslavia, explained to reporters that the old, existing dinar will continue to be used in financial transactions in the initial period. The rate between the old and the new dinar will be defined daily, on the basis of the exchange rate between the old dinar and foreign currencies. A fund of $200 million from the National Bank of Yugoslavia has been prepared as a foreign exchange support for the first emission of the new dinar, scheduled to amount to $100 million.
