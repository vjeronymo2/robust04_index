910430 FT 30 APR 91 / International Company News: PepsiCo in 13% climb PEPSICO, the world's second biggest soft drinks company, yesterday turned in a 13 per cent rise in first-quarter net earnings with strong growth in its soft drinks and restaurants businesses offsetting lower results from snack foods operations. The figures were in line with expectations and shares in PepsiCo fell Dollars 1 7/8 to Dollars 30 1/4 at yesterday's close. For the three months to March 23, PepsiCo recorded net income of Dollars 205.4m or 26 cents a share, against Dollars 181.9m or 23 cents a year earlier. Sales advanced 12 per cent to Dollars 4.12bn. Pre-tax profits in the first three months of 1991 rose 15 per cent to Dollars 316m. PepsiCo's soft drinks business suffered from the loss of the Burger King account and volume fell 1 per cent. Sales , however, rose 7 per cent to Dollars 1.4bn and operating profits increased 8 per cent to Dollars 161.1m. Operating profits from PepsiCo's international soft drinks business surged 45 per cent to Dollars 17.7m on sales which climbed 35 per cent to Dollars 344.6m.
