BFN [Text] Kamchibek Shakirov has been relieved of his duties as finance minister for office abuse, sources close to the Kyrgyz leadership told INTERFAX. The decision on Shakirov's dismissal was made at a government presidium meeting yesterday night. At the last session of the Republic's parliament which ended on 16 April, Shakirov was confronted with the accusation of having granted credits under privileged conditions. The Kyrgyz mass media widely publicized the fact that Shakirov had granted a soft credit worth 2 million sums (Kyrgyz currency) to his son-in-law.
