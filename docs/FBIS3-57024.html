Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] [Announcer] Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze today ended his official visit to France and returned to Tbilisi. In his first interview after arrival, Eduard Shevardnadze told journalists that realistic ways for a peaceful settlement of the crisis had taken shape at the talks between Georgia and Abkhazia that are under way in Geneva. [Begin recording] [Shevardnadze] The main aim has been achieved, I mean the elaboration of a legal basis for cooperation and friendship between the two states [France and Georgia]. A large treaty was signed, and the prospects for economic relations look good. There was much talk, of course, about the state of things in Russia which is our concern and worry, too. The world community must support the democratic choice made by the whole of Russia and its president. [Correspondent] Touching upon the prospects for relations between Georgia, UNESCO, and the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development, Eduard Shevardnadze had the following to say: [Shevardnadze] This work has just started. The status...[pauses] I have visited the headquarters of this organization and it seems to me that the prospects here are also good. We have agreed that a special delegation will visit (?us) and we will sort things out on the spot. [end recording]
