920408 FT 08 APR 92 / Moves in dispute at Bank of Italy A long-running wages and conditions dispute involving the bulk of the 9,400 employees at the Bank of Italy has reached partial solution following agreement between the central bank and union representatives on a new package after three days' negotations, writes Haig Simonian in Milan. However, the deal has been rejected by independent groups outside the mainstream trade unions, meaning that selective strikes in key departments are set to continue. Among opponents of the settlement is Fabi, the bank's biggest union.
