Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 17 KYODO -- Japan and Europe remained split over quota of Japanese auto exports to the European Union (EU) for 1994 at the first day of two-day talks on Thursday [17 March], Japanese negotiators said. Japan sought an increase while the EU asserted that the quota should be unchanged from 1993. In 1993, both sides agreed to cut Japan's exports by 18.5 percent from the previous year to 980,000 vehicles in light of slumping demand in Europe. They will try again to reach an agreement Friday, the second day of the bilateral talks at the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI), according to the negotiators.
