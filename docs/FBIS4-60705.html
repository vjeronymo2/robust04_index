BFN [Text] President Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari said that Pakistan and India can live in an atmosphere of complete peace and harmony if the Kashmir issue is resolved. Addressing a dinner hosted by the Association of Pakistan Professionals in New York last night, he said the security situation in the South Asian region has further worsened due to the Indian plan to deploy Prithvi missiles. He said that the only target of these missiles is Pakistan and this has aggravated further the tension which has been caused by the Kashmir problem. President Leghari said that contrary to the India-U.S. secret talks in London, Pakistan's silent diplomacy with the United States is continuing and it is not secret. The diplomacy is aimed at resolving the issue of the Pressler Amendment in the context of the efforts to realize the goal of nuclear nonproliferation in South Asia. President Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari said that Pakistan welcomes all reasonable proposals. It, however, will not accept any unilateral restriction on its peaceful nuclear program. On narcotics control, the president said that the Government of Pakistan has recently taken speedy steps to eradicate the operations of the drug mafia and also enacted stringent laws to root out drug trafficking. On the privatization of the power production projects of [word indistinct], the president said that progress in this regard has been halted due to lack of appropriate legal regulations. He said that the whole process of privatization will be very transparent and in conformity with the law. In future, thermal power production will be entrusted only to the private sector, while the government will take care of hydroelectric power production.
