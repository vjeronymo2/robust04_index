BFN [Text] The cabinet of our country's president of the Presidency has released a statement explaining the position of the Federation and Republic state delegation at the negotiations. The delegation demands that the cease-fire throughout the territory of Bosnia-Herzegovina should be for a period of four weeks. Arms of 12.7-mm caliber and upwards must be concentrated far from the front lines, and have to be guarded and monitored by the UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR] and NATO. The monitoring of the cease-fire and communication between the opponents would be secured by UNPROFOR according to the procedure coordinated with the legitimate authorities in Bosnia-Herzegovina. The Bosnia-Herzegovina delegation also insists on guarantees for the unimpaired passage of humanitarian organizations throughout Bosnia-Herzegovina, the immediate release of all civilians held by the aggressor side, the unconditional and immediate opening of Tuzla airport for the delivery of goods for civilian purposes, the cessation of ethnic cleansing, and the effective implementation of all Security Council resolutions regarding the protection of security zones. The negotiations in Geneva are not being conducted directly with the representatives of the aggressor, but according to the principle of separate rooms. UN representatives are acting as mediators for the proposals of the parties involved, reads the statement from the cabinet of the president of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Presidency.
