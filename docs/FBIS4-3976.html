BFN [Text] Beijing, April 28 (XINHUA) -- China will continue to make efforts to promote Hong Kong's economic development and improve people's living there, Wu jianmin, Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman, said here today. He told a weekly news briefing here this afternoon that the two panels of the Preliminary Working Committee (PWC) for the Preparatory Committee of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region in charge of political and economic affairs will meet in Hong Kong from May 2-3 and from May 4-5, respectively. Lu Ping, PWC deputy director and secretary-general, will head mainland members of the two panels and other personnel of the PWC Secretariat to attend the meetings in Hong Kong, he said. While in Hong Kong, Lu will listen to advice and proposals from the Hong Kong people, he said. "The Chinese side has always been greatly concerned with the economic affairs and people's livelihood of Hong Kong," he noted. "We have already made efforts, and will continue to do so, in order to promote Hong Kong's economic prosperity and improve the people's livelihood there," he stated. He said that China hopes to maintain cooperative relations with the British side in this regard. Whether or not the cooperation will go smoothly depends entirely on the attitude and actions of the British side, Wu added.
