Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 10 Jan (TANJUG) -- A session of the Supreme Defense Council was held in Belgrade today. It was presided over by the president of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia. In addition to the members of the Supreme Defense Council -- Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic and Montenegrin President Momir Bulatovic -- the session was also attended by Federal Vice Premier Jovan Zebic, Federal Defense Minister Pavle Bulatovic, and Yugoslav Army Chief of Staff Lieutenant Colonel General Momcilo Perisic. The Supreme Defense Council examined the issue of material reserves, and developing and supplying the Yugoslav Army with equipment in light of implementing its constitutional duty to defend the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, the military office of the president of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia has announced.
