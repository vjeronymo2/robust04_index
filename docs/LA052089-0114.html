May 20, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Stock Exchange Charges Rival: The Philadelphia Stock Exchange filed a lawsuit accusing the American Stock Exchange of pirating its blueprint for a controversial new financial instrument that combines aspects of stocks and futures. The Philadelphia exchange demanded that the New York-based Amex stop trading the new financial product, known as stock baskets or index participations, under rules allegedly copied from Philadelphia. The Amex said there was no basis to the charge. Philadelphia and the Amex started trading the stock baskets a week ago after a stay was lifted by the Securities and Exchange Commission. The product is based on various stock indexes and pays quarterly dividends, although its holders do not own the underlying stock.
