Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Reports from Afghanistan speak of continued fighting among rival mojahedin groups for the third day today. The fighting is concentrated in the southeastern part of Kabul and various other areas in the northwest. Dostam militia is pitted against forces loyal to President Borhanoddin Rabbani. AFP report says forces loyal to Prime Minister Golboddibn Hekmatyar are also believed to be taking part in the fighting against President Rabbani's forces. Government troops have attacked Dostam militia posiitions in northern Faryab and Qonduz Provinces. Fierce fighting has been reported near the river port Sherkhan Bandar, Balkh, and important northern city of Mazar-e Sharif controlled by Dostam militia. The rival groups have made conflicting claims of victories. Government sources in Kabul claim that Mazar-e Sharif airport is under the government control and no Dostam plane can take off. Dostam has around 40 planes there. A statement issued by the Embassy of Afghanistan in Islamabad says that situation in Kabul is improving. Afghan Government spokesman Habib Shah Hamid told newsmen that the Mojahed Army of Afghanistan Government has captured the airport of Mazar-e Sharif. Harakat-e Enqelab-e Eslami [Islamic Revolution Movement] of Mowlawi Mohammad Nabi Mohammadi, Mahaz-e Meli-e Eslami [National Islamic Front] of Pir Sayyed Ahmad Gilani, Etehad-e Eslami [Islamic Union] of Professor Sayyaf, Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party] of Mowlawi Mohammad Yunes Khales and Jehad councils of Nangharhar and Konarha Provinces have declared their support for government of Afghanistan. All the personnel of Pakistan Embassy in Kabul are safe and well.
