Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Anatoliy Krasnov] [Text] Moscow February 21 TASS -- "The Japanese are ready for a dialogue on international and regional issues, including in the military sphere, and want the talks to be held at a higher level," a Russian top diplomat said after the first day of Russian-Japanese consultations in Moscow on Monday. The talks focused on the situation in the world, Russia's participation in the upcoming G-7 summit meeting in Naples and current processes in the Asian-Pacific region. "So far everything goes pretty smoothly," Nikolay Solovyov added. The Russian delegation to the talks is led by Deputy Foreign Minister Aleksandr Panov, his Japanese counterpart Hiroshi Fukuda heads the Japanese delegation. Fukuda said Tokyo is ready to continue support for Russia's economic reform and called for expanding trade and cooperation in the sphere of investments between the two countries, ITAR-TASS has learnt from diplomatic sources. The sides also agreed to seek to boost humanitarian exchanges, considering them as an integral part of Russian-Japanese relations.
