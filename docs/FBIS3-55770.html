Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [by ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Demidetskiy] [Text] Chisinau, 11 Jan -- The Supreme Soviet of the unrecognized Dniester republic has today adopted an appeal to deputies of the Russian Federal Assembly, asking for support for the Dniester region and hoping for understanding of its proposals on the creation of a confederation in Moldova. In their message the Dniester deputies accuse the leadership of Moldova of "persistently not wanting to tackle the Dniester problem and pursuing a policy of endless veering between threats, military conflict, and political blackmail." If Chisinau does not want to "find a mutually- acceptable settlement" of the conflict, Tiraspol asks the Russian parliamentarians "to recognize the Dniester Moldavian republic as an independent and sovereign state friendly to Russia". The document also contains a request not to withdraw the Russian 14th Army from the Dniester region. Moldavian Parliament Deputy Chairman Victor Puscas told the ITAR- TASS correspondent that the appeal from the Tiraspol authorities to the Russian parliamentarians bore witness to the fact that they were unwilling and unable to settle the Dniester region problem around the negotiating table. "There can be no question of any confederation," he said. "The Dniester region problem can and should be resolved within the framework of a united Moldova by means of granting the left-bank districts special juridical status."
