BFN ["News Release"] [Text] Go-between in controversial $60 million arms deal between Estonia and TAAS Israel Industries Ltd., Leonid Apananski, had in 1992 sponsored the election campaign of three prominent members of the ruling Fatherland Party, former Defence Minister Indrek Kannik told the Estonian Radio on Tuesday. Kannik said Apananski had sponsored present Foreign Minister Juri Luik, MP Mart Nutt and himself with 9,000 kroons ($692). "I am sorry that those documents from Fatherlandos election-time archive should have come out now," Kannik said while adding that the archives had stayed with Tiit Pruuli, the former adviser to Prime Minister Mart Laar. Kannik also said Laar and Pruuli had advised them to ask Apananski for campaign funds. Kannik's interview came a day after MP Aivar Kala had accused Kannik of accepting Apananski's funds.
