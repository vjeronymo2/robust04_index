Comments for inclusion in the Rules Docket must be received on or before July 5, 1994. 14 CFR Part 39 [Docket No. 94&hyph;ANE&hyph;12; Amendment 39&hyph;8895; AD 94&hyph;06&hyph;09] Airworthiness Directives; Teledyne Continental Motors (Formerly Bendix) Magnetos AGENCY: Federal Aviation Administration, DOT. ACTION: Final rule, request for comments.

SUMMARY: This document publishes in the Federal Register an amendment adopting airworthiness directive (AD) 94&hyph;06&hyph;09 that was sent previously to all known U.S. owners and operators of Teledyne Continental Motors (TCM) (formerly Bendix) SC&hyph;20, SC&hyph;200, and S&hyph;1200 series magnetos by individual letters. This AD requires inspection to determine if capacitors that may have an intermittent open circuit condition are installed in magnetos, and replacement, if necessary, with serviceable capacitors. This amendment is prompted by reports that TCM produced and delivered magneto capacitors that have the potential for an intermittent open circuit condition that may exist between the internal through-lead and the p-lead stud terminal. The actions specified by this AD are intended to prevent possible injury or death to ground personnel due to a non-grounded magneto.
