April 26, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: MUSIC

Tower First Woman to Win Honor: Composer Joan Tower is the 1990 recipient of the prestigious, $150,000 Grawemeyer Award from the University of Louisville. Tower, 52, a music professor at Bard College in Annandale-on-Hudson, N.Y., is the first woman and native-born American chosen for the 6-year-old prize. She was honored for her orchestral piece, "Silver Ladders," written for the St. Louis Symphony. BETH KLEID
