941129 FT 29 NOV 94 / Securities watchdog fines broking house Pounds 25,000 Durlacher & Co, the stockbroking house, has been fined Pounds 25,000 by the Securities and Futures Authority, for breaches of regulations. Mr Peter Durlacher, the chairman, has also been fined Pounds 5,000 by the regulator for securities and futures brokers. Mr David Tabizel and Mr Tom Scrase, who held senior positions with the house at various times, have also each been fined Pounds 5,000. Mr Douglas Braddock and Mr Craig Thomson, registered representatives of the firm, have been fined Pounds 10,000 and Pounds 5,000. Mr Braddock has been put on notice that if he is found guilty of misconduct in any further disciplinary proceedings his registration with the authority will be terminated. The men all admitted the SFA charges, which included failure to maintain sufficient financial resources to comply with SFA requirements and reporting inaccurate financial statements to the authority. The firm also admitted to failing to calulate correctly the margin requirement on a customer's option trading account and failing to review the portfolio regularly. The customer incurred a considerable loss from the trading, according to the SFA. Compensation has been agreed from the firm, Mr Braddock and Mr Thomson.
