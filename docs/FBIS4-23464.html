BFN [Text] A former presidential candidate, Chief Moshood Abiola, has appealed to his supporters to refrain from media attacks on the Hausa-Fulanis for his failure to become president of Nigeria. In an interview with newsmen at his Ikeja residence, Chief Abiola appealed to politicians, the media, and traditional rulers to prevail on their people to make more responsible utterances, in the interest of peace and national unity. He recalled that in the annulled presidential elections he won more support in Kano than Ogun, his home state, stressing that such people deserve better treatment than attacks on pages of newspapers.
