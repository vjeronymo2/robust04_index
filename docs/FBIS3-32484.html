Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Brussels, 28 Jan (PL) -- Ricardo Alarcon, president of the National Assembly of Cuba, stated today that he is satisfied with his current visit to Belgium, where he has met with numerous political personalities and European Parliament members . The Cuban leader asserted that such contacts contribute to a better knowledge and understanding of the island's realities, which is currently confronting the most complicated situation in the history of its revolutionary process. Alarcon's statements were made during a long news conference attended by a large and heterogeneous group of media representatives from Belgium and other countries. The news conference covered issues related to the Caribbean nation. Among the issues recovered were the elections held in Cuba in February 1993, the island's international relations, the U.S. blockade, the possible commercial liberalization, and the people's support for those measures. The Cuban Legislative Branch president heads a delegation that has been visiting Brussels since last Sunday. They have kept a tight program of interviews and meetings with leaders of the European Parliament, the Belgian Government, and parties and political organizations. At the news conference, the visitor considered that those contacts will help promote Cuba's ties with the European Union and its political and executive branches and with the Kingdom of Belgium. Today, Alarcon met with Leo Tindemans, head of the European People's Party faction in the European Parliament. Later, he attended a dinner in honor of the Cuban delegation which was hosted by the Belgian Coordinator, an organization that furthers the lifting of the U.S. blockade. This organization has more than 45 local political organizations that support Cuba and tomorrow, there will be an event in support of the Caribbean nation at the Free University of Brussels. There will also be conferences and Cuban singer Silvio Rodriguez will give a concert.
