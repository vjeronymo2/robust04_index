BFN [Text] PAC [Pan-Africanist Congress] leader Clarence Makwetu held talks with President F.W. de Klerk. Mr. Makwetu said afterwards that the PAC was considering the working structures of the TEC [Transitional Executive Council]. He said the PAC wanted to address the escalating violence in the country and to resolve the issue of indemnity for its returning exiles. [Begin recording] [Makwetu] They suggested that we look into the documentation of the TEC and see whether it will be possible for us to join the subcouncils of the TEC in order to solve such problems. [De Klerk] We suggested that the PAC should fully normalize its position by now signing the necessary documentation, abandoning violence, and not merely suspending it, and becoming part of the TEC and the transitional structures. [end recording]
