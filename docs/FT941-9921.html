940211 FT 11 FEB 94 / International Company News: Cost savings lift Petrobras Petrobras, Brazil's government-controlled oil company, reported net profits of Dollars 673m for 1993 compared with Dollars 53m for 1992. The company said the improvement was due to increased productivity and cost savings. Mr Marcos Menezes, head of Petrobras's accounting division, said the company increased production from an average 653,000 barrels per day in 1992 to 671,000 last year. He added that Petrobras saved Dollars 600m in operational costs in 1993, Dollars 400m of which was due to lower financial costs after a switch from expensive long-term financing in the local market and comparatively low interest rates in international markets. Net turnover for the period was Dollars 13.79bn.
