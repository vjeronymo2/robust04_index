941107 FT 07 NOV 94 / MP quits as Hurd aide 'to be free' Mr David Martin last night resigned as parliamentary private secretary to Mr Douglas Hurd, foreign secretary. He said he wanted to be free to take his own line on government policy. The MP for Portsmouth South had held the post for four years. He was previously PPS to Mr Alan Clark as defence minister. Mr Martin declined to comment on the reasons for his resignation, although he said that the matter would become clear in due course. Mr Hurd said: 'David Martin told me a little time ago that after four years as a PPS he would like to regain his freedom of action.'
