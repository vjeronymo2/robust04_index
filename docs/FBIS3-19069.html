Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Lefkosa, Mar. 10 (A.A.) -- Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus (TRNC) President Rauf Denktas criticized on Thursday [10 March] a report of U.N. Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali, on the indirect talks being held in Cyprus concerning the confidence-building measures. In a statement from the TRNC capital, Denktas also announced that he had postponed a meeting scheduled for this afternoon with U.N. envoy Gustave Feissel, to Friday. He said that he was to review the draft document given to him by the envoy, together with the prime minister and consultants. "An incomphrehensible haste prevails in U.N. circles," he said. "This is not an issue that could be resolved in such a haste. Even a single word needs emphasis." Denktas pointed out that the Butrus-Ghali report attributed a title to one of the sides, which is unacceptable to the other and that they would not accept Kliridhis being called "president," while he himself is referred to as "leader." "The course of events are directed to support the `Cyprus republic' and to just mend us to it. [sentence as received] Therefore we shall continue to review the document put before us, very minutely," he said.
