931027 FT 27 OCT 93 / Edmonds in plea for job security COMPANIES can combine job security for employees with the ability to adjust overall employee numbers as markets change, says one of Britain's leading trade unionists. Mr John Edmonds, general secretary of the GMB general union, cited Rover and National Power as companies which had non-compulsory redundancy agreements with their unions but had also shed large numbers of staff in the past two years. Mr Edmonds, in an interview with the Human Resource Management Journal, also said that he had no problems 'in principle' with performance-related pay. He said: 'It has to be fair, acceptable to those involved, and transparent. If it really means 'your manager will decide your pay', then that will be demotivating and will not work.' On employment security he said that unions should demand it as their share of the 'spoils' from more effective management. Mr Edmonds said: 'Employers want guarantees of all kinds from us. People's time, ingenuity and commitment will only be forthcoming if the giving of these is not going to put them out of work.' He added: 'Admittedly, the line between voluntary and compulsory can be thin. But management will have to come to accept that redundancy is a failure in the same way that passing the dividend is a failure.'
