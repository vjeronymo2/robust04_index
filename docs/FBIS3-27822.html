Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 29 (KCNA) -- Comrade Kim Chong-il, supreme commander of the Korean People's Army [KPA], sent a sixtieth birthday table to merited artiste Chong Tu-myong, a composer of the military band of the Supreme Command of the KPA. When Seoul was liberated by the People's Army in the period of the fatherland liberation war, Chong, a middle school student, joined the People's Army in September 1950 and fought bravely before being called to the military band of the Supreme Command. He became a composer after graduating from a university. Over the last 20 odd years he has composed the welcome music (a band music) for major political functions, and hundreds of pieces of band music, light music and instrumental concert. Upon receiving the sixtieth birthday table and order of the National Flag First Class amid the blessings of his comrades-in-arms Friday, Chong Tu-myong said: "I feel the urge to go to the South with this table and loudly tell my parents, brothers and sisters and South Korean people that this is a clear token of the noble love and politics of trust of our dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il for the revolutionary soldiers."
