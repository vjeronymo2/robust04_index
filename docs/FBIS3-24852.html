Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] Mohammad-Khan, minister for economic affairs and finance, in a meeting with a delegation from the World Bank, spoke about the significant and fundamental role of the Islamic Republic of Iran in the region. Mohammad-Khan said: The main indexes obtained during the first development plan indicate that the country's economy is moving toward an organized and programmed economy -- and taking into account the world economic recession, Iran enjoys a relatively good growth. Referring to the substitution of imports that will reduce future foreign currency requirements, he said: With the increase in domestic production, there will be a fall in the import of petrochemical goods and foods, as well as commodities needed by consumer and intermediate industries while the export of nonoil goods will increase. Mohammad-Khan also spoke on the policy of privatization and said the government intends to sell factories through auction or limited partnership with individuals interested in investment and management. The minister for economic affairs and finance also spoke on Iran's important role of creating positive prospects for neighboring countries' economies, as well as creating provisions for transport of goods from Iranian ports.
