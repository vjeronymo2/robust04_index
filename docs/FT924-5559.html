921126 FT 26 NOV 92 / Deficit in motor trade widens THE UK motor industry trade balance deteriorated sharply in the third quarter with the deficit increasing by 154 per cent to Pounds 1.2bn from Pounds 471m in the same period a year before. The deficit in cars, commercial vehicles and automotive parts and accessories in the first nine months rose by 131 per cent to Pounds 2.56bn, more than double the deficit for the whole of last year. The motor industry alone accounted for more than a third of the total UK visible trade deficit of Pounds 3.25bn in the third quarter. The performance has worsened in the face of faltering exports of automotive products and a rapid rise in the value of imports. The Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders warned that 'it may be some time yet' before the motor industry trade balance benefited from the devaluation of sterling. The value of automotive imports continued to rise with a jump of 22 per cent in the third quarter.
