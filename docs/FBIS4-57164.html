BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the elections scheduled for 21 August. During a telephone interview, Cuauhtemoc Cardenas, presidential candidate for the Party of the Democratic Revolution (PRD), admitted that the Mexico City branch of the PRD had engaged in irregularities in nominating its candidates to the Assembly of Representatives of the Federal District. He urged those involved to respect the party's mandates, and he said the mistakes could be corrected. (Mexico City UNOMASUNO in Spanish 5 May 94 pp 1, 6) Dr. Ernesto Zedillo Ponce de Leon, presidential candidate for the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI), visited Chiapas State for the second time today. Dr. Zedillo visited the town of Tapachula, where he told those present that he wants the peace agreements with the Zapatist National Liberation Army to be signed to enable them to be fulfilled as soon as possible. Zedillo said it is "unacceptable" to have so many poor people in such a rich state. "We have to achieve social justice here," according to Zedillo. (Mexico City Radio ACIR in Spanish 0000 GMT 6 May 94) PRI candidate Ernesto Zedillo has met with representatives of the National Network of Handicapped People in southern Mexico City. The representatives denounced the lack of opportunities for the handicapped in the areas of education and employment. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 11 May 94) The National Action Party (PAN) has asked the other eight political parties that will take part in the August elections to allow public audits of their campaign expenses, according to Felipe Calderon, secretary general of the National Executive Committee of the PAN. Calderon said the audits would serve to clear doubts over the campaign expenses of the PRI, which had reported expenditures of 24.515 million pesos as of 17 March. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 11 May 94)
