930324 FT 24 MAR 93 / Clarke hints at sentence reform MR KENNETH CLARKE, the home secretary, yesterday indicated that the government would review the rules on the sentencing of criminals, following criticism by the Lord Chief Justice. Lord Justice Taylor said the 1991 Criminal Justice Act had been founded on a 'misconceived notion' which restricted judges' discretion. A desire to reduce custodial sentencing had led to 'restrictive provisions forcing the judge into an ill-fitting straitjacket'. These included rules which stopped magistrates taking into account previous convictions. Mr Clarke told BBC Radio: 'Plainly, if the act is not working as it is intended we must revisit it and change it. 'I totally agree that somebody who comes before the court having committed many burglaries is a more serious criminal than a man who has committed burglary for the first time.'
