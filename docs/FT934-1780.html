931217 FT 17 DEC 93 / MMC attacked for 'perverse decision' The chairman of the Commons all-party Treasury select committee has tabled an early day motion criticising recent recommendations of the Monopolies and Mergers Commission for failing to uphold the public interest. Mr John Watts, the Conservative chairman, yesterday attacked what he called the MMC's recent 'perverse decision' that the perfume 'complex monopoly' was not against the public interest.
