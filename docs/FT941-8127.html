940222 FT 22 FEB 94 / UK Company News: New stores lift Essex Furniture Benefits of new store openings enabled Essex Furniture to raise pre-tax profits by 14 per cent, from Pounds 619,000 to Pounds 703,000, in the six months to December 31. Sales at the USM-quoted furniture retailer and manufacturer grew 44 per cent to Pounds 7.47m. Mr Michael Franks, chairman, said trading from Boxing Day was ahead of forecast with excellent input from new locations. Results for the full year were expected to be 'very satisfactory.' He reported that the interim performance had exceeded budgeted forecasts and the company had absorbed the opening costs of a new head office and distribution facility of five new showrooms in the period. An additional 60,000 sq ft had been added to selling space and the company said it intended to open a minimum of five showrooms in 1994. Earnings advanced from 3.73p to 4.06p and the interim dividend is 1.8p (1.5p).
