May 6, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

LINCOLN SAVINGS FAILURE WON'T COST UNCLE SAM

In "Lincoln S&L Posts Loss of $1 Billion for Year," (March 28) the last line reads "the failure of the thrift . . . could cost the government more than $2 billion."

This is wrong. It will not cost the "government" one cent because it has no money.

It will cost the taxpayers $2 billion -- or more.

IRENE CORSI

Hemet
