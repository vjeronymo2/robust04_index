Language: English Article Type:BFN [By CRIME-PRESS correspondent Sergey Oleynik -- tass] [Text] Moscow January 10 TASS -- The staff of the central Moscow headquarters of the Russian Counterintelligence Service will be reduced by more than a half and will go down from 3.500 to 1.500 officers, first deputy director of the service Sergey Stepashin told a press conference on Monday. Earlier spokesman of the service Aleksey Kondaurov told TASS that the total number of Russian counterintelligence officers will amount to 75.000 which is 46 per cent less than before the former Security Ministry was transformed into the Federal Counterintelligence Service. Stepashin said that besides counterintelligence tasks, his service will, jointly with the Interior Ministry, fight political terrorism. "The president, the government and the parliament have to know what's happening in the country. It is necessary to predict also situations connected with political terrorism, other actions aimed to overthrow the constitutional system", he said. This task will be partially solved by the Interior Ministry and partially by the counterintelligence service, he added. The main objective of the counterintelligence service, which directly reports to the president, is to cut short foreign spying and subversive activities against Russia, according to Stepashin. The service will have a department of counterintelligence operations, of counterintelligence provision for strategic objects, of military and economic counterintelligence. At the same time the former Security Ministry anti-organised crime department was transferred to the Interior Ministry, while the investigation department became a part of the office of the Russian prosecutor general. The former KGB investigation prison in Lefortovo was also trasferred to the Interior Ministry. The counterintelligence service, in its turn, nay get the "Alpha" and "Vympel" commando units.
