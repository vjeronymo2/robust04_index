Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] 205,000 cubic meters of natural gas arrived in Turkey today, one day after the natural gas delivery was completely cut when Ukraine diverted the Russian natural gas destined for Turkey. Veysel Atasoy, minister of energy and natural resources, went to Bulgaria to hold talks with a view to eliminating the shortage. The delegation headed by Atasoy will then to go to Ukraine to discuss the issue and tell Ukraine not to pump natural gas from the Turkey-Bulgaria-Romania pipeline. According to information received from Ministry officials, after the natural gas supply was interrupted yesterday, reductions were made in the electricity, fertilizer, and manufacturing sectors. The reductions continue, but no reductions are planned for homes at this stage. Of the natural gas consumed in Turkey, 11 percent is consumed in homes and 89 percent in the industrial sector.
