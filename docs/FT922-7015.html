920520 FT 20 MAY 92 / International Company News: Abitibi-Price sells units to Alco Standard for CDollars 340m ABITIBI-PRICE, the Canadian forest products company which is 82 per cent owned by property developer Olympia & York, has approved the sale of its printing and industrial paper products distribution businesses to Alco Standard, the US paper and office products distribution company, for CDollars 340m (Dollars 284m), Reuters reports. The four business units involved are Inter City Papers, Barber-Ellis Fine Papers and Price Daxion in Canada, and Whitaker Carpenter Paper in the US. Mr Ron Oberlander, president, told a news conference: 'There will be no special dividend issued from these proceeds. 'These proceeds will go entirely to Abitibi-Price. 'This is part of our announced strategy of selling assets at attractive prices to strengthen our balance sheet,' Mr Oberlander added. The deal is expected to be completed in mid to late July and is subject to the usual investigations by the purchaser and completion of documentation.
