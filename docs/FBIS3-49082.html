Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] According to the information of the republican Health Care Headquarters, 141,605 people have been killed and 160,000 wounded in Bosnia-Herzegovina since the beginning of the war. In Sarajevo alone, 9,662 have been killed, while over 56,000 were wounded. During the Christmas cease-fire, 106 people were killed and 470 wounded. Thirty-nine people were killed and 200 wounded in Sarajevo. This is stated in the statement of the republican Health Care Headquarters. Death from starvation is threatening displaced persons and the rest of the population in Tesanj, Maglaj, Olovo, Gorazde, Mostar, Tuzla, and other cities. Therefore, it is necessary to secure powdered milk and other dairy products for these areas, as well as fresh, dried, and canned fruits, powdered eggs, meat products, and fresh and canned vegetables.
