Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] President Carlos Salinas de Gortari received Roy MacLaren, Canadian international trade minister, during his visit to Mexico. During a news conference held after the meeting, the Canadian minister announced the Mexican, U.S., and Canadian trade ministers will meet on 14 January in Mexico City to analyze issues such as subsidies and anti-dumping and to establish a mechanism, before the end of 1994, so other countries may join the North American Free Trade Agreement. He said Chile will be the next country to join NAFTA, but he said it is open to all the countries in the world that fulfill its requirements.
