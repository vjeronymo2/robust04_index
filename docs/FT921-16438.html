920103 FT 03 JAN 92 / Demonstrators mark through Algiers AN estimated 200,000 demonstrators marched through Algiers yesterday after a call to save the country from a fundamental Islamic state, Francis Ghiles reports from Algiers. Mr Hocine Ait Ahmed, leader of the lay opposition party, the Socialist Forces Front (FFS), seen above waving his scarf to the marchers, had called on all Algerians to demonstrate peacefully yesterday to defend democracy. .The opposition Islamic Salvation Front (FIS), which won 188 seats outright in the first round of the general election last week. In doing so, it put itself far ahead of all other parties but the final composition of the national assembly will not be resolved until a second round on January 16. The march, during which riot police guarded main public buildings but generally maintained a discreet presence, was joined by many supporters of the National Liberation Front (FLN). It had held a monopoly of power until the riots of October 1988 but last week only picked up 15 seats. Many workers and supporters of smaller parties were also on the march.
