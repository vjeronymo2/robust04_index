921202 FT 02 DEC 92 / World Trade News: Mines cost Kuwait Dollars 730m KUWAIT has contracted to spend about Dollars 730m (Pounds 480m) on clearing mines left over from the Iraqi occupation, the defence minister said, Reuter reports from Kuwait City. In response to a parliamentary question, Sheikh Ali Sabah al-Salem al-Sabah gave details of all contracts signed with mine-clearing companies. The largest contract, with an affiliate of the Egyptian Ministry of Defence, was for Dollars 166m. CMSI (Conventional Munitions Systems International) of the US will be paid Dollars 134m and the French company Sofremi, owned by the French Interior Ministry, will receive Dollars 111m. Other contracts are with the Bangladeshi Defence Ministry (Dollars 103m), the Pakistani Defence Ministry (Dollars 95m), Royal Ordnance of Britain (Pounds 59m) and the Turkish company Ictas (Dollars 30m). ECC of the US has a Dollars 3.8m contract to oversee the work.
