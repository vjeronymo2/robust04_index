930427 FT 27 APR 93 / International Company News: Seibu turns in first deficit SEIBU, the Japanese department store group, announced yesterday that sales had fallen below those of Mitsukoshi, its main rival, for the first time in five years, writes Charles Leadbeater in Tokyo. Seibu, which is privately owned, took the unprecedented step of publishing a financial report. It announced it had made its first ever pre-tax unconsolidated loss in the year to end-February. It was the first loss at the group since its founding in 1940. The loss was mainly due to a scandal involving medical equipment, which forced Seibu into an extraordinary loss of Y19.7bn (Dollars 175m).
