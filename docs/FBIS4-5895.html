BFN [YONHAP from Washington] [Text] North Korea hopes to resume dialogue with the United States, CNN TV reported today in a Pyongyang-datelined story. In a live report via telephone from Pyongyang during "Larry King Live" program, Chinoy, a Beijing-based CNN TV correspondent who is now in Pyongyang, said that North Korea desperately hopes [kalmanghada] to have high-level talks with the United States and that President Kim Il-song seeks a U.S. figure who would listen to his views regarding the nuclear issue. Evangelist Billy Graham, who appeared in the program, said that he had a three-hour-long talk with President Kim during his recent visit to Pyongyang and, on this basis, expressed an upbeat view on the smooth solution of the nuclear problem. He confirmed that he had delivered President Kim's reply to the White House. However, he declined to give the specific contents of the reply.
