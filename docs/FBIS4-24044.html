BFN [Text] Three persons -- Chief Segun Osoba, Mr. Jonah David Jang, and retired Air Commodore Dan Suleiman -- yesterday appeared before the Federal High Court [FHC] in Lagos on charges of treason. The chief judge of the FHC, Mr. Justice Mohamed Belgore, who was presiding over the case, directed that the men, who are on bail should not be rearrested. The defense had complained that the accused persons were being harassed by the police and security agents. Mr. Justice Belgore ruled that the police or security agents should not interrogate, visit, or invite any of the three accused persons without specific order from the court, pending the determination of the case. He also directed that the bail of the accused persons should continue under the present condition till tomorrow when hearing of the case would continue. Also, yesterday, Mr. Justice Belgore granted bail to a Lagos- based medical practitioner, Dr. Beko Ransome-Kuti, standing trial for treasonable felony. Dr. Ransome-Kuti was however asked to sign an undertaking not to commit the same offense he was charged with in the [words indistinct] bail. He was banned from addressing any gathering, issuing press statements, or participating in any public gathering, and he must remain within his locality. In addition, he will produce two sureties and pay a sum of 10,000 naira which would be forfeited in the event of nonfulfillment of the conditions. Mr. Justice Belgore stressed that any breach of the conditions would invalidate the bail.
