941209 FT 09 DEC 94 / Economic Watch: EU unemployment falls slightly European Union unemployment fell fractionally to 10.7 per cent in October from a revised 10.8 per cent in September, the EU statistics office said. Unemployment fell in the UK to 8.9 per cent from 10.3 per cent, in Denmark to 9.5 per cent from 10.7 per cent, in Ireland to 17.6 per cent from 18.4 per cent and in Spain to 22.1 per cent from 22.5, Eurostat said. In contrast, more people were without work in Italy, where the rate rose to 12.1 per cent from 11.3 per cent and in Luxembourg, where it climbed to 3.5 per cent from 2.8 per cent. The EU's male unemployment rate fell to 9.3 per cent in October from 9.6 per cent a year earlier, while the female rate rose to 12.8 per cent from 12.6 per cent. French non-farm market sector payrolls rose by 59,100 (0.4 per cent) in the third quarter of 1994, after gains of 0.2 per cent and 0.6 per cent respectively in the first and second quarters. Industrial production in Denmark rose by 5 per cent in September compared with the same month last year.
