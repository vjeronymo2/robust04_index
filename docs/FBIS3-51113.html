Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Peruvian economic developments monitored through 11 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. The National Association of Exporters said today that in 1993, Peru's trade balance was negative for a third consecutive year. The export-import deficit reached $530 million. In 1993, Peru exported goods worth $3.467 billion and imported goods worth $3.997 billion. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1833 GMT 5 Jan 94) According to Petroperu, the state petroleum agency, crude oil production increased 9.2 percent in 1993 over 1992. Daily production is averaging 126,200 barrels. (Paris AFP in Spanish 2129 GMT 4 Jan 94) Canadian Foreign Relations Minister Cristine Stewart said on 8 January that $25 million will be given to Peru for assistance programs, specifically targeting social programs. Stewart also noted that Canadian businessmen are interested in investing in Peru and that she will study investment prospects in this country during her visit. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2315 GMT 8 Jan 94) The German and Peruvian Governments today agreed to consolidate the Peruvian debt. According to the agreement, due dates for Peruvian debt have been rescheduled for March 1996 in order to reduce pressure to make payments on $90 million. Economy Minister Jorge Camet said that the agreement has also postponed the cancellation of loans until 2003 and 2006. Camet also stressed that a recent $30-million loan by the German Government is part of its 1993 contribution to the Support Group for Peru. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 0142 GMT 5 Jan 94) Peru collected $4 billion in taxes in 1993. The National Superintendency of Tax Administration confirmed that as a result Peru has surpassed the goal set by the IMF, which said that taxes collected in 1993 must be equal to 10 percent of the gross national product. (Lima Global de Television Network in Spanish 0100 GMT 5 Jan 94) Modifications to the tax system designed to increase tax revenues will take effect on 1 January. People earning less than 900 soles a month will not pay any income tax, while those earning more than that figure will pay a 15-percent income tax. People earning 7,000 soles a month will pay 30 percent. (Lima Global de Television in Spanish 0100 GMT 4 Jan 94)
