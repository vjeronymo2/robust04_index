Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 27 Mar (INA)--Qatari Foreign Minister Hamad Bin-Jasim Al Thani has reiterated his country's firm stand on the need to lift the embargo imposed on the people of Iraq. Speaking to reporters in Cairo today on the sidelines of the Arab League Council meeting, the Qatari foreign minister said that relations with Iraq are going well and are continuously developing in the interest of the Iraqi and Qatari peoples and the Arab nation. Answering a question by an INA correspondent in Cairo on Qatar's stand vis-a-vis the attempts to prolong the embargo imposed on Iraq, the Qatari foreign minister said that Qatar is working toward ending the suffering of the Iraqi people as soon as possible because we cannot remain silent about the effects of the sanctions, adding that it is in the interest of the nation to end this situation. The Qatari minister said that he met with [Iraqi] Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf and discussed with him issues related to lifting the blockade imposed on the people of Iraq, noting that Iraqi-Qatari relations have never ceased. The Qatari minister said that Qatar is playing an Arab role to bring closer the viewpoints of the Arab parties and brothers everywhere in the interest of the nation's solidarity and strength.
