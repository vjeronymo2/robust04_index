940405 FT 05 APR 94 / Slovakia: A tumultuous three years Apr 1991: Vladimir Meciar, former state enterprise lawyer who became prime minister of Slovakia after Czechoslovakia's November 1989 'velvet revolution', forced out of office after parliamentary revolt. June 1992: Meciar sweeps back to power at head of Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS) and starts talks with Czech Premier Vaclav Klaus about greater autonomy just short of independence. Klaus rejects proposals and both sides begin divorce proceedings. Jan 1, 1993: Slovakia, under Hungarian rule until 1918 and a neo-Nazi puppet state during second world war, achieves independence. Michal Kovac becomes first president. Feb 1993: Slovak currency separates from Czech currency, followed by devaluation, a 10 per cent import surcharge and other measures to shore up economy afflicted by rising unemployment and inflation. Jan-Mar 1994: Progressive deterioration in relations between President Kovac and the Meciar government, which Kovac accused of divisiveness, abuse of power and corruption. Mar 11: Meciar resigns after 78-2 no-confidence vote with 70 abstentions. Mar 14: Jozef Moravcik becomes prime minister of interim coalition government until elections on September 30.
