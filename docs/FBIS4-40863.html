BFN [Untitled report under the "NEGA Reports" rubric] [Text] In the wake of last week's incident in parliament, the indignant refugees making their temporary home in the city of Zugdidi and Zugdidi Rayon have addressed to Eduard Shevardnadze a brief letter supporting the head of state's actions aimed at a peaceful settlement of the Abkhazian conflict. "Enough bloodshed, enough casualties, our material and moral resources have long been exhausted," the letter says. "The refugees have reached the point where they have begun begging." The documents [as published] are signed by several hundred people driven to desperation, they voice their indignation at the irresponsibility of opposition-minded deputies and their "jingoist speeches" in parliament. "We ask you to take all the necessary measures for a timely and unimpeded bringing in of peacekeeping forces and their deployment in Abkhazia," the authors of the letter insist.
