BFN [Text] The prime minister has asked the nonresident Indians, NRIs, to play their legitimate role in the speedy development of the country. Addressing a large gathering of NRIs in New York, Mr. Narasimha Rao asked them to evaluate for themselves the reform process without attaching any emotions. He emphatically stated that the economic changes underway in India are irreversible. The prime minister invited them to take advantage of the opportunities and invest in the country to help expand and strengthen the economic liberalization process. He said that structural hurdles posed at the initial stages have been overcome and there is a turnaround now. The prime minister appreciated the good work being carried out by the NRIs in various walks of life outside the country. Our correspondent Rama Rao, covering the prime minister's visit, reports from New York that Mr. Narasimha Rao will be meeting the captains of industry at Houston today in the second leg of his visit to the United States. He described the visit as a step towards a strong, friendly, and cordial cooperation between the two great democracies of the world. During his one-day stay in Houston, the prime minister will be meeting the chief executive officers and presidents of the major companies under the auspices of the Greater Houston Partnership. The partnership brings together over 2,000 companies based in the city. The mayor of Houston will be presenting the keys of the city to Mr. Narasimha Rao. The prime minister will attend an Indian community reception before leaving for Boston tomorrow. Our special correspondent Bhupal Inder Saini reports from Washington: [Begin Saini recording] One of the primary objectives of the prime minister's goodwill visit to the United States is to explore the potentials and opportunities of stepping up Indo-American cooperation in the economic sphere. The United States is already India's largest trading partner and the economic reforms undertaken by New Delhi under Mr. Narasimha Rao's leadership had created tremendous opportunities for further stepping up economic links and trade. Significantly, a large number of leading Indian businessmen are also in the United States to tap the potential for tie-ups with American companies. [end recording]
