BFN [Text] Taku Yamasaki, former director general of the Defense Agency, and Yoichi Tani, former director general of the Hokkaido and Okinawa Development Agency, both legislators affiliated with the opposition Liberal Democratic Party [LDP], will visit North Korea on 14 June. Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata met them on 10 June to discuss the issue of North Korea's suspected development of nuclear weapons. During the meeting, the prime minister told them, "I hope both of you will make North Korea understand that the international community is paying great attention to the North Korean nuclear issue and is deeply concerned over the issue." The prime minister also said, "I hope your forthcoming visit to North Korea will serve to help it open its door to and promote a candid dialogue with other nations," adding that he hoped they would convey that remark to North Korea as his verbal message to that country.
