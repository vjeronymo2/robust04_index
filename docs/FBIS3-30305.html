Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague Jan 31 (CTK) -- The Czech Republic's foreign trade turnover was 10.6 percent higher in 1993 than it was in 1992, according to the preliminary results of a Czech Statistics Office (CSU) study. Czech imports increased by 2.7 percent and exports by 20.0 percent last year. The only countries with whom Czech trade decreased were those of the former Soviet Union. Czech Republic exports and imports increased to all other countries. A total of 58.0 percent of Czech trade in 1993 was made with countries with mature market economies, while 8.7 percent was exchanged with former members of the Soviet Union and 6.1 percent with other Eastern European states in economic transformation. The Czech Republic's trade surplus was 5.9 billion crowns ($200 million) last year. The largest surplus was recorded with developing countries (13.6 billion crowns, $453 million), while the surplus with European Union states was 7.5 billion crowns ($250 million). The Czech Republic's largest trade deficit was with countries of the former Soviet Union ($19.8 billion, $660 million). Czech trade with Slovakia was at a surplus balance of 9.6 billion crowns ($320 million).
