November 26, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

KUSC'S REVISED FORMAT HAS GIVEN A REVISED FORM TO AT LEAST ONE LISTENER

Smith states that Mozart would love his new programming. Mozart, when playing, would not abide a cough from an audience member. What makes Mr. Smith think that he would appreciate hearing his works truncated or sandwiched between jazz?

If Smith believes he can sucker a jazz lover into listening to Mahler he is mistaken. His programming change can be likened to casting Disney characters in a play by Eugene O'Neill.

If playing the best serious music of the past 500 years is a "narrow focus," let me forever remain narrow-minded.

DAVID SPIEL

Santa Monica
