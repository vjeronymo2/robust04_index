Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, March 15 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The newspaper "ARDIYN ERH" informs that the Prime Minister P. Jasrat has visited departments of police of some districts of the Mongolian capital, get acquainted with work of policemen. As the newspaper notes, the prime minister has paid attention to the growth of cases of stealing and hard-drinking in the city. The police organs have noted that a striving for "an easy life" furthers the increase of cases of stealing. Cases of stealing are often committed by persons in a drunken state. Lately cases of cattle stealing have become very frequent. As the newspaper informs, the prime minister has decided to meet tomorrow with the personnel of the central police department to discuss problems of struggle with criminality. "ARDIYN ERH" publishes a number of materials about electric energy and heat supply. The directors of the thermo-electric power stations of Ulaanbaatar and the chief of the control service of the central electric energy system note that more than 50 percent of damages at the electric power stations have been in consequence of irresponsible work and bad discipline of workers, low qualification level of workers and engineering-technical personnel and weak control. The newspaper notes that to maintain normal work at the two biggest thermo-electric power stations of the city, a special strict regime is introduced there for the period of half a month. The newspaper informs about the joint meeting of the State Great Hural Standing Committees on Food and Agriculture, on Budget-Finance and Currency Credit Policy and on Policy of Development of Economy and Infrastructure. At the sitting, information of the Minister of Food and Agriculture T. Oold was heard about a conception of development of agriculture and preparation for the spring sowing. In the report it is noted that in the current year it is envisaged to sow more than 553 thousand hectares with food grains, 9, 6 thousand hectares with potatoes, 3, 4 thousand hectares with vegetables, about 40 thousand hectares with fodder crops. At the sitting it was pointed out to unsatisfactory preparation of agricultural machines for the spring sowing, and it was noted that the plan of repair of machines is fulfilled for the present only by 20 percent. At the sitting the President of Mongol Bank D. Molomjamts announced that the bank has granted additional credits for carrying out the spring field works, but the bank was not able to grant them on favourable terms. "ARDIYN ERH" publishes an article "The Last Night of Pompei" by the State Great Hural (SGH) member from the opposition T. Elbegdorj. He notes that a crisis is observed in the parliament of Mongolia in consequence of an unequal balance of different political forces in the State Great Hural. In the article it is noted that the government still fails to realize its program, has not met its engagements to meet the requirements of the population in food in 1993. Pointing out to insufficient steps in the sphere of democratization of the society and transition of economy to market relations, T. Elbegdorj considers that the present situation in the country reminds [is similar to] Pompei before the explosion of the volcano Vesuvius at the beginning of our era. The newspaper of the Mongolian capital "ULAANBAATAR" informs about the exhibitions under the motto "Each Family Enterprise, Each Citizen-Producer". Such exhibitions are organized lately regularly at the halls of the former museum of V. I. Lenin. The newspaper notes that many districts of the city, hundreds of citizens are successfully engaged in production activity. They are producing different goods and food-products. The newspaper notes that this kind of work is especially widely spreading in the Chingeltei District of Ulaanbaatar, number of horoos (horoo-territorial administrative primary unit) of which was granted a premium for their success in production activity. The head of the sector of the Epidemiological Center L. Togooch in an interview with the newspaper correspondent announces that among the city population, particularly among children cases of falling ill with infectious meningitis are still observed. L. Togooch notes that an external factor of spreading of meningitis is cold weather. As he says, in all probability the falling ill with meningitis will not stop till June. At present in the city two teams of physicians are working on liquidation of hotbeds of this disease. The newspaper publishes materials devoted to the international day of consumers, in particular, publishes the statement of the society of protection of consumer's interests of the capital, an information about the international union of consumers.
