BFN [Text] Johannesburg April 1 SAPA -- Two Eskom [Electricity Supply Commission] protective services employees suspended on Friday pending the Goldstone Commission investigation into the cancelled arms sale that could have seen 1,000 automatic weapons sold to kwaZulu, have been relieved of their duties for their "serious error of judgement". Eskom spokesman Kevin Morgan said on Friday night the service's departmental manager and the employee who negotiated the arms deal had, however, acted "above board". He would not identify the arms dealer other than saying he was from Johannesburg.
