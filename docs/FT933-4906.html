930907 FT 07 SEP 93 / Labour attacks premium rises A LABOUR party survey shows that the average family faces a 19 per cent increase in its household insurance bill this year, Richard Lapper writes. The survey of premiums set by nine companies in 11 areas also shows that the highest quote is likely to be 2 1/2 -times more expensive than the cheapest. Mr Robin Cook, shadow trade and industry secretary, said: 'Customers are getting a raw deal. I want to see these insurance companies make clear to their policyholders by how much premiums are being increased, and then to justify their behaviour. They should not get away with exploiting consumers in this way.'
