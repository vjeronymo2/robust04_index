BFN [Text] Tianjin, May 12 (XINHUA) -- The Walmart Company, a leading retailer of the United States, is studying the possibility of entering the Chinese retail market, according to the chairman of the board of directors of the company. Robson Walton, whose company's retail sales volume tops 55 billion U.S. dollars annually, has arrived in Tianjin to investigate market potential. He said he is very much interested in China's huge market potential and is looking for investment possibilities in the north China port city. The chairman has inspected several large commercial centers in the city and conducted in-depth discussions with local officials. Tianjin, one of the leading trade and commercial centers in northern China and one of the few cities that allow foreign investment in commerce and retail sales, has become the favorite of many foreign investors. The Isetan Company of Japan, which leased and opened a large shopping center in the city last year, is achieving a rising business volume. Another large store funded by a Hong Kong company is also being constructed. Other overseas businessmen eyeing Tianjin market potentials include the Chia Tai Group company of Thailand and the Nino Textile Company of Germany, local officials revealed.
