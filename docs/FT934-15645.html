931011 FT 11 OCT 93 / Newspaper executives condemn possibility of newspaper VAT TWO senior newspaper executives last night strongly condemned the possible imposition of VAT on newspapers. Sir David English, chairman of Associated Newspapers, said on BBC TV's The Money Programme that the move would be 'devastating'. He added: 'If the industry absorbs it, it will wipe out virtually all profits. If it does not absorb it, it will cut horrendously into circulation figures.' Mr Andrew Knight, News International chief executive, warned on the same programme that that the move could kill off a number of titles.
