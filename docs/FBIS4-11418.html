BFN [Text] In Colombia, a Latin American meeting on science and technology has approved five Cuban projects on development, with innovations applied to the economy. These projects will be carried out with Spanish collaboration. The approval was announced by Raul Taladrid, Cuban representative to the 10th meeting of the technical management council of the Latin American program of science and technology for development. Taladrid said the projects have to do with applying data processing to the sphere of medicine and with association by Cuban and Spanish businesses for mutual benefit through exports. The Cuban vice minister also pointed out that these development projects will make possible greater collaboration between scientific institutions of Spain and Cuba.
