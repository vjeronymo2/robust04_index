BFN [Text] Indonesia will explore the possibility of making contact with Amnesty International, an international nongovernmental organization, to discuss various matters, especially the East Timor issue, to enable them to understand the incorporation of the territory into Indonesia. Foreign Minister Ali Alatas said this in Jakarta today before leaving for a tripartite meeting with UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali and Portuguese Foreign Minister Durao Barroso in Geneva on 6 May. According to Ali Alatas, Indonesia's readiness to accept members of international nongovernmental organizations and foreign reporters is part of its effort to create mutual trust with Portugal. Regarding Vietnamese refugees on Galang Island, Riau, Ali Alatas confirmed that a refugee died from self-immolation when an interdepartmental team visited the area recently. Ali Alatas deeply regretted the inevitable incident.
