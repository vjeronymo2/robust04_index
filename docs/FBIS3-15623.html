Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Mar 08, BNS -- The Lithuanian-Romanian political treaty indicates the significance of the first visit to Lithuania by a Romanian leader after the restoration of Lithuanian independence, Romanian President Ion Iliescu said at a press conference after the accord was signed in Vilnius Tuesday [8 March]. The agreement on friendship and cooperation was signed by Iliescu and Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas. Agreements on investment protection and promotion, and commercial cooperation in 1994 were signed by Lithuanian Industry and Trade Minister Kazimieras Klimasauskas and Romanian Trade Minister Cristian Ionescu. "The signing of the friendship and cooperation treaty with Romania expands Lithuania's relations with Black sea countries," the Lithuanian president said. In his words, Lithuania uses every opportunity to improve its ties with both "next-door" and more distant neighbors. Lithuania and Romania should make use of their geographical location to strengthen economic and commercial relations, he said. Iliescu said Romania welcomes every initiative that enhances security and stability. The Romanian leader said a positive thing is that security zones are created around both the Baltic and the Black sea, but it is "hard to speak" about a union of these zones. Both presidents said their countries pay much attention to safeguarding security. Brazauskas pointed out that both countries signed up for NATO's Partnership for Peace program. The Lithuanian president expressed hope that Russia will join the program in the near future. "The problem of creating blocs in Europe would then disappear," he said. In Iliescu's words, Lithuania and Romania seek to join different European and international cooperation structures, and "this should not be hindered by any economic or political barriers." Commenting on the commercial cooperation agreement, Minister Klimasauskas said it is very democratic, allowing to choose goods and to set prices without any restrictions. The Romanian delegation completes its two-day visit to Lithuania Tuesday. Before departure, Iliescu will visit Vilnius University.
