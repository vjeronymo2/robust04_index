Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Paris, Feb 25 (TANJUG) -- Paris, Washington and Moscow have fully agreed that "diplomatic contacts be intensified" for the soonest possible negotiated settlement in the ex-Yugoslav Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina, the French Foreign Ministry said on Friday [25 February]. The three parties were also completely agreed as to the latest initiatives for the resolution of the Bosnian conflict, said ministry spokesman Richard Duque. He said this in announcing that Foreign Minister Alain Juppe on Thursday night talked on the telephone with U.S. State Secertary Warren Christopher and Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev. The spokesman, however, said nothing about France's proposal for the U.N. to carry a resolution to place Sarajevo under U.N. administration. French media assert this proposal will not easily pass the U.N. Security Council, knowing that Moscow apparently disfavours it while Washington has not yet made up its mind.
