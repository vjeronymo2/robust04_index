941019 FT 19 OCT 94 / Arts: Today's Television Having been knocked off my motorbike a few months ago, with consequent broken ribs and a damaged leg, I shall watch Dispatches with particular interest (9.00 C4). We are promised examples of the increasingly questionable methods used by insurance companies in their determination to avoid paying damages. It seems that private detectives posed as market researchers, for instance, and tried to get Lynn MacDonald to undertake cleaning work incompatible with her medical condition so that they could secretly film her doing it. Lovely people. Highlight of the evening is the return of Between The Lines, the most impressive police series to have appeared in the past 10 years (9.30 BBC1). Bravely the producers have decided to take a major sideways jump in the plot, moving Clark not only out of the internal investigation bureau but right out of the Met. He finds himself working undercover for the Sun. The opening programme in C4's Counter Culture (the title is a pun) was about a tea and coffee merchant. Today's shop is the one in Newcastle United's stadium (9.45).
