BFN [Text] Damascus, 24 May (SANA)--Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas, who is entrusted with forming a cabinet in the Democratic Republic of Yemen which was declared in Aden, has denied the downfall of any city in the south. He stressed that no party has achieved victory over the other in the raging war. In an interview with Radio L'Orient today, al-'Attas, who is now in London, said that he will leave for Aden soon to form a cabinet and to hold further consultations with the brothers in Aden. Concerning President 'Ali Salim al-Bid's presence in Hadhramaut, al-'Attas said that Mr. al-Bid is inspecting some troops there. Al-'Attas said: We should work to stop the shedding of more blood and to save what remains of the Yemeni national resources.
