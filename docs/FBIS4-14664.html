BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valentina Ivanova] [Text] Cheboksary, 1 Apr -- A communique was signed today in Cheboksary on the intention to strengthen and develop economic and cultural links between Chuvashia and Tatarstan. In the opinion of Nikolay Fedorov, the president of Chuvashia, and Vasiliy Likhachev, the vice president of Tatarstan, the document paves the way for the conclusion of a bilateral treaty of friendship and cooperation, which it is proposed to sign in the near future. In their discussion of the socio-political and economic situation in Russia the sides expressed support for the reforms based on the principles of democracy, justice, and law that are being implemented. The leaders of the two republics, which are parts of the Russian Federation, consider that all contentious issues within the territory of the Russian Federation should be tackled only on the basis of negotiations. The president of Chuvashia supported a proposal by the vice president of Tatarstan that a Euro-Asian international cooperation center be set up in Kazan. Both sides also put forward an initiative that an international project be developed in Cheboksary under the auspices of UNESCO for the purpose of preserving and developing the cultures and languages of the peoples of the Volga-Urals region.
