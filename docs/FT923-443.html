920929 FT 29 SEP 92 / The FT Review of Business Books (20): For rail buffs everywhere IN Russia, demand for inter-city travel has grown to the point where incoming trains have to be split into two before they enter Moscow terminals because they are too long to fit into the platforms. Paraguay's locomotive fleet still consists of 26 wood-burning steam locomotives, most of them British. In the UK, private sector trolley catering is now standard on all express trains operated by Regional Railways, British Rail's provincial sector. Fast-changing international events have caught Jane's World Railways 1991-92 (edited by Geoffrey Freeman Allen, Jane's Information Group, Pounds 125, 798 pages) on the hop in some areas, especially eastern Europe, but this remains the pre-eminent compendium of information - great and small - on the world's railway industry.
