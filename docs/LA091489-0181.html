September 14, 1989, Thursday, Orange County Edition

TELEMANN AND WAGNER OPEN CHAMBER BILLS

The Orange County Chamber Orchestra's 1989-90 season opens Nov. 5 with the Suite from Telemann's "Don Quichotte der Lowenritter" and Wagner's "Siegfried Idyll," conductor Micah Levy announced Wednesday.

Included in the concert will be Mozart's Sinfonia Concertante in E-flat, with violinist Diana Halprin and violist Geraldine Walther; Faure's Berceuse in D for Violin and Strings, and Hindemith's "Trauermusik" for Viola and Strings. The program will be repeated Nov. 6.

Sunday performances during the chamber's seventh season will be held at 4 p.m. at St. Joseph's Center at the Loyola Marymount University Orange Campus. Each program will be repeated the following Monday at 8 p.m. at South Coast Repertory in Costa Mesa.

The season will continue with the following programs:

-- Feb. 4 and 5, 1990: Boyce's Overture in D, No. 4; Haydn's Symphony No. 82 ("The Bear"); Robert Cummings' Oboe Concerto, with soloist Allan Vogel, principal oboist with the Los Angeles Chamber Orchestra. Cummings is a county resident.

-- March 18 and 19: Handel's Concerto a Dui Cori, No. 3; Leopold Mozart's Sinfonia da Caccia; Ernest Chausson's Piano Trio, with violinist Sydney Weiss and pianist Jeanne Weiss; work to be announced.

-- May 20 and 21: Suite No. 1 from Handel's "Water Music"; Mozart's Concerto No. 21, with pianist Gita Karasik; Peteris Vask's "Musica Dolorsa." Vask is a contemporary Latvian composer.
