Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow, 3 February--Boris Yeltsin has stated that he will sign the friendship, good-neighborliness and cooperation Treaty in Tbilisi today but its ratification in the parliament will be delayed until the conflicts in Abkhazia and South Ossetia are fully resolved. The President stated this at the Vnukovo-2 airport before his departure for an official visit to Georgia. The President admitted that situation in South Ossetia and Abkhazia "create problems which should be solved on the step-by-step basis." Answering the ITAR-TASS correspondent's question on the main aims of his visit, the President said: "First of all, we are trying to stop bloodshed in Georgia. Russia cannot wait indifferently for things to sort themselves out over there." The second aim of the visit, said Yeltsin, is joint defense of the borders and the third is joint use and protection of the railways. The President also noted the need for a full-scale treaty with Georgia as well as a number of military-technical agreements.
