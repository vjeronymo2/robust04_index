Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, March 21 (ATA) -- "Albanians in the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM] are not enjoying all their rights. There are signs that the government of this newly formed state is trying to change the situation in this multinational country," Mazowiecki, UN envoy for human rights in the FYROM, said in an interview with FLAKA E VLLAZERIMIT. Referring to this interview, the BBC points out that very few Albanians in this republic have been engaged in the state structures of this country, stressing that this this phenomenon in evident mostly in the government, army, police, administration and in different political and social institutions. Mr. Mazowiecki emphasized that the human rights situation is not good in the fields of information and education. "The newly formed Macedonian state, a new member of the UNO [UN Organization], should respect the rights and freedoms of the non Macedonian people, the more so because the perspective of this state depends on the observation of international norms and laws," Mr. Mazowiecki said in conclusion.
