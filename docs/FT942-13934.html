940422 FT 22 APR 94 / Observer: Sabres out Sir Percy Cradock, the foreign affairs adviser to two prime ministers who now spends his days advising merchant bankers Kleinwort Benson, has a nice line in one-liners. He particularly relishes exercising the talent at the expense of Chris Patten, Hong Kong's governor. In pursuing confrontation with China over Hong Kong's political development, Patten has 'taken Hong Kong over the edge', while the Legislative Council has embarked on a 'legislative Charge of the Light Brigade', in approving Patten's plans. When asked if the legislature might not have the right at least to give democracy a try, Cradock intoned: 'You assert the universal right of legislatures to commit suicide . . . an interesting proposition.'
