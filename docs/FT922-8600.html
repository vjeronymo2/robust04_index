920512 FT 12 MAY 92 / Observer: Royal return The more mischievous among Conservative MPs were speculating that the Queen may have decided to settle an old score with Margaret Thatcher when she speaks to the European Parliament in Strasbourg today. During her time in Downing Street, Thatcher consistently refused to sanction a royal visit to an institution whose federalist ideals she abhorred. Now the Queen is planning to tell the MEPs that her government sees Strasbourg winning an ever more important role in fulfilling the European ideal. Most wounding for the former prime minister, an original draft of the Queen's address - the subject of sharp differences at Westminster last night -argued that differences in national parliamentary tradition were insignificant against a European-wide commitment to democracy. At that, Thatcher, already planning to deliver another onslaught on Federalism in the Hague on Friday, was expected, in the words of one Tory MP, to 'go nuclear'.
