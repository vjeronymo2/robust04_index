BFN [Text] The head of the Russian Government Viktor Chernomyrdin, who arrived in Washington on a visit on Tuesday, held a meeting in his official residence with representatives of influential Jewish organizations. The leaders of the organizations stated their support for the process of Russian reforms and stressed that they would do everything possible to assist them. At the same time they voiced concern at rising crime in Russia and acts of anti-Semitism. Viktor Chernomyrdin stated that the country's leadership intends to firmly strive for the observance of human rights and to combat crime and manifestations of chauvinism. At the same time he noted that political stability can be based only on economic stability and economic and trade cooperation between Russia and the Unites States could play an immense role here. At the meeting a wide range of issues was discussed, in particular the need for the abolition of the Jackson-Vanick amendment as one of the barriers which are holding back the development of bilateral trade.
