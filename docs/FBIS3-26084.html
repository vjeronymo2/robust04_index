Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By reporter Zhang Rongdian (1728 2837 0368)] [Text] Beijing, 2 Feb (XINHUA) -- While meeting with Secretary General Enrique Correa Rios of the Chilean Government this afternoon at Diaoyutai State Guest House, Qian Qichen, State Council vice premier and foreign minister, expressed the belief that the traditional friendly relationship between China and Chile will continue to develop constantly. He also hoped that the two countries would carry out more extensive cooperation in the area of international affairs. Qian Qichen appreciated the Chilean Government's resolute pursuit of the "one China" and nonintervention in other countries' internal affairs policies. He recalled President Yang Shangkun's successful visit to Chile in 1990 and Chilean President Aylwin's successful visit to China in 1992. He said the two countries have made big strides in economic development. He said these have provided excellent prospects for both sides to further carry out mutually beneficial cooperation in diverse forms and through various channels. On behalf of the Chilean Government, Correa congratulated China for its diplomatic achievements in recent years. He said China has played an important role in promoting peace and development in Asia and in the world. China has consistently stood for adopting peaceful means to settle international disputes. Internationally, reform and opening up have won honor and prestige for China. Correa added that the Chilean Government would strive to further strengthen ties with China in various fields. Qian Qichen and Correa also exchanged views on the international situation. Correa arrived in Beijing for a goodwill visit on the evening of 31 January at the Chinese Government's invitation. Tomorrow, Correa and his party will leave Beijing and visit Shanghai and Shenzhen.
