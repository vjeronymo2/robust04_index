941222 FT 22 DEC 94 / Business rates blow for London Companies in London will pay more than expected in business rates next year, making them unwilling to accept higher rents and more likely to move to new premises, property experts said yesterday. While property values in central London have fallen by up to 60 per cent since business rates were last set in 1988, transitional arrangements outlined in last month's Budget limit cuts in rates bills to 5 per cent a year for large buildings. The average central London office occupier currently pays business rates of about Pounds 16 a sq ft. Based on property values that liability should have fallen to about Pounds 6.75 a sq ft. Under the transitional arrangements, however, occupiers will still be paying Pounds 15.55 a sq ft next year - a saving of only 45p. Property agents said higher than expected rates would make it more difficult for landlords to charge higher rents. Jones Lang Wootton, the surveyors, has reduced its rental growth forecasts for top City properties in the next two years, although it expects the transitional arrangements to be more generous to central London occupiers thereafter. Rates on new buildings - occupied for the first time after April 1995 - will be set immediately at the lower level, reflecting the current value of the premises. This will make new offices up to Pounds 10 a sq ft cheaper to occupy than older premises and could encourage developers to press ahead with new buildings. Yet agents said that business rates would not in themselves spark a development boom. Sir Brian Goswell, of Healy & Baker, the surveyors, said: 'What really matters to a developer is whether he can find a tenant for a new building. Most tenants are locked into old leases and do not have the option of moving.'
