920113 FT 13 JAN 92 / Construction Contracts: Chiswick sewer Recent contract awards totalling Pounds 8.7m have been won by civil engineering contractor REES HOUGH. The largest, valued at Pounds 3m, is a contract for the construction of a combined sewer in Chiswick for Thames Water Utilities. The two-year project requires the installation of a 2.5 kilometre pipeline using pipe jacking, tunnelling and open-cut techniques. Other pipeline installations awarded to the companyamount to nearly Pounds 2m and include a 200 metre surface water sewer in Barnet, north London, a 700 metre pipe jacked sewer in Corsham, Wiltshire, and a tunnelled sewer in Gloucester, which is being carried out partly through pre-treated ground.
