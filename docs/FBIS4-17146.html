BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Andrey Palaria and Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow April 20 TASS--Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev will visit Brussels on May 25-26 to inform NATO officials about Russia's position on the Partnership for Peace programme. Following recent NATO air strikes in Bosnia, the Russian Defence Ministry "has slightly reviewed its stand on the matter, taking into account the position Russia holds in Europe and in the rest of the world," Grachev said. Speaking at a meeting with visiting Turkish Defence Minister Mehmet Golhan in Moscow on Wednesday, Grachev said that during his visit to Brussels he will also brief the defence ministers of NATO and other countries on the key provisions of Russia's military doctrine.
