BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 12 (KCNA) -- The dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il received a rare camellia presented by a South Korean botanist on the occasion of his birthday. Camellia, an evergreen noted in Korea from olden times, is a plant which grows on mild seashores and foots of mountains and around houses in the central and southern parts of the country. It is also an ornamental plant. The gift camellia with 52 growth rings is 6 centimetres in diametre at the bottom of its stem, 60 centimetres in height and 75 centimetres between tips of spread branches. The botanist decided to present this plant to General Kim Chong-il, representing the desire of him and his colleagues to live like a camellia which stands green all the year round, letting its red flowers bloom, always revering the general. Handing camellia to a fellow countryman, he said: "This camellia carries the unanimous wishes not only of me and my friends but also of the South Korean people who hold General Kim Chong-il in high esteem. "We ask you to courteously convey this camellia of the South Sea coast to the general who put forward a reunification strategy of chuche and is devoting his all to the noble cause of reunification with matchless grit and iron will."
