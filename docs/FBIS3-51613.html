Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Namibian Prime Minister Hage Geingob met with Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina Gonzalez on 19 January in Windhoek, the capital of Namibia. Geingob thanked Robaina for Cuba's support in Namibia's struggle for liberation. The Namibian prime minister expressed satisfaction with the positive course of the agreements signed between the two nations and praised the work done in Namibia by 68 Cuban collaborators in the health field. Before concluding his first visit to Namibia on 21 January, Robaina will talk with President Sam Nujoma, to whom he will deliver a message from Cuban head of state Fidel Castro Ruz. As part of the work of the delegation headed by Foreign Minister Robaina, the two countries will sign a trade agreement in which each grants the other most-favored-nation status.
