July 24, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

TOP AIR POLLUTION PENALTIES

The South Coast Air Quality Management District has responsibility to control air pollution. It can seek court-imposed fines against polluters of from $25 to $25,000 a day based on such factors as the extent that emissions exceed legal limits, potential danger to the public, whether the violation was intentional, accidental or due to negligence and the company's history of violations. These are the 10 top penalties in May:

COMPANY

Somitex Prints of California Industry

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Polyester fabrics printer

PENALTY

$50,000

VIOLATION

Re-routing duct work without a permit; operating equipment contrary to permit conditions; excessive hydrocarbons from dyeing process; excessive visible emissions.

COMPANY

R.D. Builders Orange, and Groundwater Technology Torrance

TYPE OF BUSINESS

General contractor for excavating underground gasoline storage tanks; soil monitoring and sampling firm

PENALTY

$15,500

VIOLATION

Failure to notify the AQMD within 24 hours of detection of gasoline-contaminated soil at a Tarzana site; failure to: properly monitor excavated soil for gasoline contamination, cover contaminated soil, implement mitigation measures and comply with permit conditions.

COMPANY

ARCO Petroleum Products Co. Carson

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Oil refinery

PENALTY

$13,800

VIOLATION

Vapor recovery compressor breakdown; vapor leaks at butane loading stations; excessive hydrocarbons and visible emissions; operating a gasoline storage tank contrary to permit conditions.

COMPANY

Professional Refinishing Organization Los Angeles

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Metal coating firm

PENALTY

$8,000

VIOLATION

Excessive hydrocarbons from solvent usage.

COMPANY

Texaco Inc. Berth 84, Long Beach

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Oil tanker operator

PENALTY

$5,000

VIOLATION

Public nuisance violation due to excessive smoke and soot from ship's smokestack, including soot deposits on a nearby auto lot.

COMPANY

Coastcast Corp. Gardena

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Stainless steel parts manufacturer

PENALTY

$4,000

VIOLATION

Excessive smoke and odors from a steel melting furnace.

COMPANY

Avante Designs South Gate

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Wood furniture manufacturer and designer

$3,500

Operating a spray painting booth contrary to permit conditions.

COMPANY

Lapidus Popcorn Maywood

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Popcorn processor

PENALTY

$3,500

VIOLATION

Failure to obtain a permit to operate a diesel dispensing facility.

COMPANY

BFI Hospital Waste Systems of California Huntington Beach

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Waste treatment company

PENALTY

$3,000

VIOLATION

Public nuisance violation due to odors from a medical waste incinerator.

COMPANY

Ferguson Door Co. Gardena

TYPE OF BUSINESS

Airplane hanger door manufacturer

PENALTY

$3,000
