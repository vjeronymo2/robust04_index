BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow May 18 TASS -- Russian lawmakers have moved for inviting presidential parliament spokesman Aleksandr Yakovlev and Chairman of the Federation Council Vladimir Shumeyko to one of its forthcoming sessions to report on the situation inside the Russian procurator-general's office. They expressed concern about the fact that in accordance with a presidential decree, all of the Russian procurator-general deputies had been dismissed and wanted to know the reasoning behind the decision. Russian legislators also voted on Wednesday to invite head of presidential administration Sergey Filatov to a plenary session of the State Duma to answer to their inquiries related to President Yeltsin's letter in regard of the law on the status of a State Duma deputy, which he signed earlier. They were dissatisfied with explanations which were offered by the chief of the Main Legal Department under the Russian president Ruslan Orekhov at a plenary session of the Duma last Friday.
