BFN [Excerpts] President Ali Hassan Mwinyi will convene peace talks in Arusha to be attended by the Rwandan Government and rebels of the Rwanda Patriotic Front [RPF] on 23 April. A statement issued by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation said the meeting was called following a deterioration of the war situation between the Government of Rwanda and RPF fighters, and as part of UN efforts to bring about a ceasefire. Tanzania has been involved in reconciling the two sides for a long period, culminating in the signing of a peace accord last year. [passage omitted] The statement by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation said the government had received unconfirmed reports of the death of three Tanzanians. The statement also stated that the government was continuing with arrangements to repatriate Tanzanians currently in Rwanda [words indistinct] The government also called on the Government of Rwanda to immediately release 44 vehicles and the goods in them. The vehicles belong to Tanzanians and have been held at the border. In the meantime, the Kagera regional commissioner, Honorable Phillip Mangula, has said that six UN soldiers have entered the Mara Region from Rwanda and that they are being looked after by the UN High Commissioner on Refugees.
