930603 FT 03 JUN 93 / Call to cut European interest rates The US yesterday urged Japan and Germany to boost their economies as a report from the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development warned that unemployment in the industrial world was reaching 'alarming' levels. Mr Lloyd Bentsen, the US treasury secretary, called for Japan to increase its fiscal stimulus and for 'further substantial reductions' of European interest rates to promote faster growth. His thinly veiled call on the Bundesbank to cut its interest rates was backed by the European Commission. Mr Henning Christophersen, commissioner for economic affairs, said further reductions in short and long-term European rates were needed. All European Community states could do more, he added.
