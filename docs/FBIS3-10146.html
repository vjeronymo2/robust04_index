Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Paraguayan Foreign Minister Luis Maria Ramirez Boettner told ABC COLOR on 26 February that the decision of Brazilian customs officials to eliminate the 300 percent bond that Paraguayan imports had to pay upon arrival at the Brazilian port of Santos for further transfer to Ciudad del Este, was greatly influenced by the positive attitude of the Brazilian Foreign Ministry, which reacted favorably to a request from its Paraguayan counterpart and recommended that the issue be studied. Following two days of negotiations with Paraguayan Customs Director General Miguel Angel Britos, who had been sent to Brasilia to find a solution to the problem, top officials of the Brazilian Federal Revenue (the office in charge of collecting custom taxes) announced on 25 February that the 300-percent bond on all Paraguayan imports through the port of Santos has been eliminated.
