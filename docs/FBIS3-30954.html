Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] United Nations, Feb 11 (AFP) -- UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali has authorized his representative in Bosnia-Herzegovina to approve the use of air strikes to defend UN personnel anywhere in the former Yugoslav republic. The representative, Yasushi Akashi, already had the authority to call in air support to help operations aimed at reopening the airport at Tuzla and for rotating UN personnel in and out of the besieged areas of Srebenica and Zepa. Butrus-Ghali's authorization was notified Friday [11 February] in a letter to the UN Security Council and which was obtained by AFP.
