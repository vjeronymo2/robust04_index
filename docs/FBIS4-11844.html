BFN [Text] Cairo, 31 Mar (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has welcomed the agreement reached today between the Palestinians and the Israelis, which protects the Palestinians in Hebron in the occupied West Bank. In statements upon his arrival from New Delhi, where he took part in the summit meeting of the Group of 15 that convened in India recently, Musa said that Egypt approves of this agreement if the Palestinians approve of it. On developments in Burundi, Musa described the situation there as regrettable and said that Egypt tried to contain it. President Husni Mubarak sent an envoy there at the beginning of the crisis. The OAU secretary general also tried to send an African force to Burundi but officials there hesitated in accepting this force. 'Amr Musa noted that this evening he will contact the OAU secretary general to discuss steps that need to be taken to stop acts of violence in Burundi. He added that he will submit a report on the situation in Burundi to President Husni Mubarak in his capacity as current OAU chairman. Answering a question on the situation in South Africa, he said that recent developments there, such as clashes and bloodshed, are painful and will affect the elections. The objective of these elections is to establish democracy. 'Amr Musa said that Egypt is very upset by current developments in South Africa. He pointed out that a message will be sent from Egypt to Nelson Mandela, leader of the African National Congress, on this matter. He added that an understanding will be reached with the OAU secretary general in light of the resolutions that were taken at the recent summit in Harare to find out what can be done to stop the deteriorating situation in South Africa. On the meetings of the Group of 15, Musa said that Egypt submitted two projects to bolster cooperation among the countries of the group. He pointed out that the ministers of economy and trade will hold a meeting in Marrakech on 15 April before the GATT meeting to define the developing countries' stand on this agreement.
