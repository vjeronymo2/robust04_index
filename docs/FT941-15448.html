940114 FT 14 JAN 94 / World Commodities Prices: Wool After uncertain opening prices paid, wool became firmer and closing levels yesterday were described as the highest of the week. The market indicator at 475 cents managed a gain of 3 cents compared with the close before Christmas. Currency changes over the past month make wool dearer to buy for several important using countries but resistance has not been enough to undermine the market. Business in major consuming countries is still generally found to be slow and margins are under pressure. Prices for British wool at a Scottish sale in Bradford levelled out this week after recent sharp rise, which were due to an attempt to correct an oversold position by two or three individual firms
