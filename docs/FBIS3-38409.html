Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Egyptian President Husni Mubarak has arrived in Ankara on a one-day working visit. He was received at Ankara's Esenboga Airport by President Suleyman Demirel. Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin was also present. An official reception for President Mubarak was held at the Cankaya Mansion. After the national anthems of both countries were played, the two presidents inspected the honor guard. Present at the ceremony were Husamettin Cindoruk, speaker of the Turkish Grand National Assembly; General Dogan Gures, chief of the General Staff; Mesut Yilmaz, leader of the Motherland Party; and other officials. Speaking at the ceremony, Demirel said that this visit, which is taking place at a time of important developments in the region, will constitute an opportunity for a beneficial exchange of views between the two countries on regional and international issues of concern. He pointed out that there Turkey and Egypt have deep-rooted ties based on a common history and cultural heritage, adding: There are many similarities between Turkey and Egypt. They have coasts on the Mediterranean. They are trying to further their social and economic development on one hand and to establish peace and stability in their region on the other. Consequently, Turkey and Egypt constitute factors of balance and stability in the Middle East. Demirel stressed that Egypt plays an important and effective role in the efforts being made to establish peace and stability in the Middle East. We appreciate Egypt's efforts in this regard, he said, adding that there are many things Turkey and Egypt can do together in the political and economic fields in the Middle East, which is being reshaped. In turn, Mubarak said that both countries carry weight in the Islamic world and that they pursue a common policy on international issues. Both countries are determined to make every effort to help establish a just and lasting peace in the Middle East. The two presidents then conferred for some time, after which the talks between the delegations began. Accompanying President Mubarak are Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa; Electricity and Energy Minister Muhammad Abazah; Sulayman Tantawi, minister of defense and war production; Information Minister Muhammad Safwat al-Sharif; and Mahmud Muhammad Bayyumi, minister of economy and foreign trade. Demirel is now giving a luncheon in honor of the visiting president at the Cankaya Mansion. President Mubarak is expected to leave Turkey this evening.
