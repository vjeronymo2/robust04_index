Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Conakry, March 30, IRNA -- Foreign Minister of Iran 'Ali Akbar Velayati left here for Burkina Faso today at the end of his two-day visit and was seen off at the airport by his Guinean counterpart Ibrahima Sylla Wednesday [30 March]. During his stay here, Velayati conferred with Guinean President Lansana Conte, and conveyed to him a message from President 'Ali Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani. Velayati, who arrived here Tuesday after a stopover in Chad, also held talks with Sylla and Guinean Minister of Natural Resources Toumani Dakoum Sako. He also attended a large gathering of Guinean Muslims at the Jame' Mosque in Conakry where he called for expansion of bilateral ties in all grounds. Waving the flags of the Islamic Republic of Iran and carrying pictures of the father of the Islamic revolution, the late Imam Khomeyni, and the present leader of the Islamic Republic of Iran 'Ali Khamene'i, Guinean Muslims in Conakry warmly welcomed the Iranian delegation. Velayati is to visit Burkina Faso on the second leg of his six-nation tour of African states which will also take him to Zimbabwe, Uganda, Kenya and Namibia.
