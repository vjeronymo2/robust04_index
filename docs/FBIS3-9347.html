Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, March 22 -- Visiting Foreign Minister of Ukraine Anatoliy Zlenko said on Tuesday the strategic base of Polish-Ukraine partnership relations should be both countries' national security. "Ukraine and Poland are two big partners in our subregion, and the deepening of their mutual relations may have a significant meaning for the entire region, and may become an example of jointly naming and solving concrete problems," said Zlenko during a press conference on the second and last day of his visit to Poland. The Ukrainian guest said he is satisfied with the talks with Polish politicians. He said he discussed with Polish Foreign Minister Andrzej Olechowski Ukraine's possible access to the France-German-Poland axis. He said he also discussed the filling of the security vacuum in central and eastern Europe and the creation of a joint collective security system.
