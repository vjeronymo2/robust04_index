Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Karlsruhe (DPA)--The investigating magistrate of the Federal Court of Justice (BGH) has issued a custody order for Gerhard Fleischle, 55, the chief political editor of Deutschlandfunk radio (Cologne). The Federal Prosecutor's Office said today that the journalist is accused of having worked for the GDR Ministry for State Security's Intelligence Department since 1960. The man had been provisionally detained on Thursday [20 January]. According to the investigation so far, the editor primarily supplied information about his colleagues and about journalistic projects to his employers in East Berlin until 1989. He is also assumed to have passed on information about the EC and NATO when he was a correspondent in Brussels between 1979 and 1984.
