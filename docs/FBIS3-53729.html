Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council, received at the Presidential Palace in Ta'izz today Arthur Hughes, the U.S. ambassador to our country. During the meeting, they discussed bilateral relations as well as the issues and developments of interest to both friendly countries. They also reviewed developments in our country and the U.S. position on them in light of the U.S. support for our country's unity and democracy. The U.S. ambassador reaffirmed that the United States is interested in our country's circumvention of the current political crisis through peaceful dialogue and by renouncing violence in a way that will entrench the march of unity and democracy in Yemen. He pointed out that a unified Yemen is an important element in regional security and stability and in the world peace march. The brother chairman of the Presidential Council expressed his appreciation for the positive U.S. position on developments in our country. He lauded the level of Yemeni-U.S. relations, adding that they are relations of friendship established on a firm basis of cooperation and common interests. He voiced confidence that our country will get through the current circumstances and challenges by constructive and responsible dialogue in a way that will safeguard the march of unity, democracy, and peace in Yemen. Foreign Minister brother Muhammad Salim Basindwah attended the meeting.
