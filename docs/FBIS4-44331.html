BFN [Unattributed report: "PSOE Retorts That People's Party Believes That `Democracy Is Lottery'"] [Text] After learning of [People's Party (PP) Chairman] Aznar's statements demanding an early election, PSOE [Spanish Socialist Workers Party] and CiU [Convergence and Union] leaders stated yesterday that the two political parties are opposed because they deem more important the government's stability in order to combat the crisis. The PNV [Basque Nationalist Party] was also opposed, while ERC [Republican Left of Catalonia] reiterated its demand for Gonzalez' resignation. Juan Pedro Hernandez Molto, PSOE of Castilla-La Mancha secretary general and a prominent renewalist leader, said in the Congress of Deputies that Aznar's remarks are unfortunate statements for the country's general interests. "You cannot present a version of democracy," he added, "as if it were a lottery in which you gamble until you strike it lucky. The public knew that they were voting in elections to the European Parliament, and that was their intention and the interpretation that must be made."

Conditional Support
Democratic Union of Catalonia (UDC) Steering Committee Chairman Jose Antoni Duran Lleida stated that "we believe that the country needs not bringing the election forward but taking important decisions, for which we want a strong government." He added that as long as the government is able to "guarantee stability" and ensures the strengthening of the economic policy that is good for the country, UDC is opposed to bringing the election forward because "it means leaving the country open and without a government for six or seven months." Nevertheless, he warned that if the Socialists do not guarantee the necessary stability "or their own internal division renders unfeasible the continuation of the economic policy, governability would be cut short by the Socialist government, and our support would not make sense." For his part, CiU deputy Jordi Casas said that beginning an electoral period right now would mean several months with a government with little ability to act, and what is needed "is the contrary, in order to try to take advantage of the signs of economic recovery." He added that what could prove positive is a process of bringing closer together all the political forces' stances in order to improve the economic situation and create jobs. PNV spokesman in the Congress Inaki Anasagasti was opposed to calling an early election on the ground that it would "set back" the autonomous community elections that should be held in the Basque Country in the fall. Anasagasti stated that the possibility of bringing forward the election should be considered only if the government "did not have sufficient parliamentary support or did not have its own party's support." The Basque nationalist leader said that he "understands" the strategy adopted by the People's Party following the European elections, "because it should take advantage of the impetus achieved with the magnificent 12 June results," although he asserted that the elections to the European Parliament cannot be turned into a general election. Last, ERC deputy Pilar Rabola agreed with the PP chairman in asking Felipe Gonzalez to bring forward the election. However, Rabola specified that, "unlike Aznar, we are demanding that it be brought forward not in order to govern but because Felipe Gonzalez has lost his political credibility amid so many corruption scandals."
