931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / UK Company News: Changes to dividend calculations FROM TODAY, the London Share Service pages show gross dividend yields calculated in a slightly different way. The changes, which also affect the net dividend reported in each Monday edition, bring the figures into line with those used for the FT-Actuaries All-Share Index and other FT-SE Actuaries indices. Previously, the individual company share price listings on Mondays showed the total of each company's dividends for the last full financial year. This meant that the figures were only updated once a year, and did not reflect the impact of dividend changes. The new method is that the figure is updated after every dividend announcement, whether interim or final. For most companies, this means that the figure will be updated twice a year. At the six month stage, therefore, the total gross dividend per share will typically be the latest net interim dividend plus the net final dividend paid in the second half of the company's previous financial accounting year, plus the tax credit which currently stands at 20 per cent. The same procedure, suitably modified, will apply to those few companies that pay dividends more than twice a year. Dividends declared by a company up to 5pm each weekday will be reflected in its shares' gross dividend yield in the following day's tables. Dividends declared later in the day are held over for 24 hours. Where companies make explicit forecasts of end-year dividends, the yield figures reported are modified to reflect these projections.
