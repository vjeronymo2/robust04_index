Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Slovak Republic, February 16 (XINHUA) -- The prime minister of the Slovak Republic, Vladimir Meciar, said today that he is looking forward to setting up closer economic ties with China during his forthcoming visit to the country. In an interview with XINHUA, Meciar said Slovakia wishes to learn from China's experience in economic reforms and in establishing a market economy. He said his country admires China's success in transferring a central-controlled economy into a market-oriented one. Learning how to achieve this transfer is very important for Slovakia, he added. He also said that there are great potentials yet to be developed in economic relations between the two countries, and that he will bring new proposals to China. He said that Chinese entrepreneurs will have opportunities to invest and get profits in a Slovakia which is undergoing an economic privatization process. He also wished that financial and monetary exchanges and cooperation with China will attain a higher level.
