931129 FT 29 NOV 93 / Survey of Software at Work - Winter 1993 (9): Supplier/product profile Product: SIA's GIS dataMAP was launched in 1985. Version 2.1.1 released November 1993, and dataMAP for Windows is due in 1994. GIS dataMAP is a modular PC workstation-based GIS and has the power to handle large volumes of complex spatial data whilst fulfilling specific applications requirements. Supplier: founded in 1968, SIA specialises in logistical and management support systems, providing software products, consultancy and services. Employees: 50 in the UK. Turnover: Pounds 3.48m with Pounds 327,023 profit in 1992, compared with Pounds 2.99m with Pounds 250,778 profit in 1991. Number of users: 100+ dataMAP licences in UK. Entry price is Pounds 2,500 ranging to Pounds 15,000; customised application solutions have varied from Pounds 6,000 to Pounds 100,000. Hardware and operating systems: PC/workstation based under MSDOS, designed to be used over local area networks, can be linked to Unix workstations and file servers, also mini and mainframe file servers and databases. Sales and support/maintenance: 15 per cent of list price. Other services available: consultancy, training, applications development, data capture, data conversion, map digitising, computer hardware, peripheral equipment, scanners, plotters; also and satellite positioning systems for Automatic Vehicle Location. User views include 'low cost; flexibility; 4GL for applications development, simple to use . . . can use raster or vector maps: fast re-draw rate for vector maps. Auto-focus when zooming in to change scale.' Other observations: 'slow forward development of enhancements in line with new market developments; DOS memory limitation; training 'could be improved.''
