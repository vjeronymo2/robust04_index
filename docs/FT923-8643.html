920810 FT 10 AUG 92 / Observer: Bright light Did you know that smoking one pack a day of Brazilian cigarettes provides the equivalent radiation dose to two chest X-rays? The Physics Institute of Sao Paulo University has discovered that fertilisers used in the cultivation of Brazilian tobacco contain dangerously high levels of uranium. Some popular brands contain 11 times as much radioactivity as American cigarettes. It gives a whole new meaning to the term 'lighting up'.
