April 15, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

JAZZ SOCIETY SPECIALIZATION

Asking a Dixieland society to play modern jazz is like demanding that a Bach society play some Sousa marches. Feather should know that to impose such a switch on people who joined a society for a particular style would cause them to leave the concert hall or cancel their memberships. You must sell tickets and memberships to have a successful jazz society.

HAROLD GRAY

Past President

Potomac River Jazz Club

Washington, D.C.
