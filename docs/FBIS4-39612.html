BFN [Text] Today Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev left for the Caucasian republics on a working visit in order to discuss with the Armenian, Azerbaijani, and Georgian leaders issues on the establishment of Russian military bases on the territory of their countries, Grachev's aide Yelena Agapova told INTERFAX. Grachev will first stop in Yerevan and meet Armenian President Levon Ter-Petrosyan. Subsequently, Grachev will meet Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze in Tbilisi. The Azerbaijani leadership has asked Grachev to come to Baku in order to speak to Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev. Russia intends to have about thirty military bases and facilities in neighboring countries, namely, three in Georgia, two in Armenia and a station for warning against a military attack in Azerbaijan. During his meetings with the Caucasian leaders Grachev is expected to discuss peace-keeping operations in Nagorno-Karabakh and in the zone of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict. Well-informed sources in Tbilisi suggested in an interview with an INTERFAX correspondent that during Grachev's visit to Georgia the peace-keeping operation may begin in the zone of the Georgian- Abkhazian conflict. The same sources said that at the first stage the conflicting parties would be separated by the Russian units stationed in Caucasus.
