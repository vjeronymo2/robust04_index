Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] U.S. Ambassador to Port-au-Prince William Lacy Swing said he is sensitive to the concerns of President Aristide who has rejected the plan proposed by Haitian parliamentarians as a resolution to the crisis. Swing said that he is optimistic about the chances of finding a solution to the crisis. He mentioned four reasons that should favor a quick outcome to the crisis: the context of change of political systems in the hemisphere, the consensus of the four countries called Friends of Haiti, the availability of massive economic aid, and the Governors Island agreement.
