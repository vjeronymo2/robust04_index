November 16, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: CANADIAN BACON PROVES ONE WAY TO SLICE CALORIES FROM RICH PASTA DISH

DEAR JEANNE: Please send me a revision of Spaghetti a la Carbonara, enclosed.

-- R. M., Anacortes, Wash.

SPAGHETTI A LA CARBONARA

1/4 cup butter

2 eggs plus 2 egg yolks

1 cup grated Parmesan cheese

1/2 pound (8 slices) bacon, preferably pancetta, cut in small strips

2 tablespoons olive oil

1 teaspoon dried red pepper flakes

1 (16-ounce) package spaghetti

1/2 cup whipping cream

Warm serving bowl in 200 degree oven. Cream butter in small bowl and set aside. Beat together eggs and egg yolks in another bowl. Stir in 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese and set aside.

Fry bacon pieces over medium heat but do not allow to crisp. Reduce heat to low and pour off all but 1 tablespoon bacon fat. Add olive oil and red pepper flakes and keep warm over low heat.

Boil spaghetti in large kettle of boiling salted water. Do not overcook. While spaghetti is cooking, add whipping cream to pan with bacon. Bring cream to simmer.

Drain cooked spaghetti well and place in preheated bowl. Working quickly, immediately toss spaghetti with creamed butter. Stir in hot bacon-cream mixture, then add egg-cheese mixture, tossing until well-blended.

Divide between 8 heated bowls and top with remaining 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, 1 tablespoon per serving. Makes 8 (1-cup) servings.

DEAR R. M.: Bacon, which is practically all fat, is normally very difficult to eliminate without missing its flavor. However, by using Canadian bacon and reducing the amount by half I have come up with a revision that should please your palate as well as your waistline.

My recipe only makes six cups, but I think it can still serve eight people because it is so rich-tasting.

LIGHT SPAGHETTI A LA CARBONARA

1/4 pound Canadian bacon, chopped

2 tablespoons corn-oil margarine

1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

1/2 teaspoon dried red pepper flakes

1 egg plus 2 egg whites, lightly beaten

1/2 cup evaporated skim milk

1/3 cup plus 3 tablespoons freshly grated Parmesan cheese

12 ounces spaghetti

Warm serving bowl in 200 degree oven. Cook bacon in non-stick skillet until lightly browned. Remove bacon from skillet and set aside.

Heat margarine and olive oil over very low heat in same skillet. Add red pepper flakes and, stirring continuously with wire whisk, add beaten egg mixture.

As sauce begins to thicken, stir in milk and 1/3 cup cheese. Keep warm over very low heat, stirring frequently.

Boil spaghetti in large kettle of boiling water for about 7 minutes. Do not overcook. Drain well and place into preheated bowl.

Working quickly, toss spaghetti with sauce and bacon. Serve in heated bowls. Sprinkle each serving with 1 1/2 teaspoons Parmesan cheese. Makes 8 (3/4-cup) servings.
