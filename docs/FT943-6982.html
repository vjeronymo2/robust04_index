940825 FT 25 AUG 94 / World Stock Markets: Emerging Markets Recent weeks have been dominated by elections in the world's emerging markets, polling having taken place in both Mexico and Sri Lanka. Investors are now keeping a close watch on the performance of Brazil, which has presidential elections in October. The Sao Paulo stock market has been encouraged this week by new opinion polls which have suggested that the former economy minister, Mr Fernando Henrique Cardoso, has extended his lead over the other main candidate, Mr Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva. A period of profit-taking is expected in the short term, given the market's strength in recent sessions. However, over the longer term analysts remain positive on Brazilian stocks, helped by positive economic signals, particularly in the form of lower forecasts for inflation data covering August and
