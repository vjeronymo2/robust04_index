May 21, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

LIFE IN L.A.

Why does Los Angeles persist in self-flagellation? The single item that decreases my quality of life in L.A. is the whining of those who don't appreciate how fortunate they are. Those who moved here years ago from points east are the worst offenders. These non-travelers have the impression that everything "back there" has remained as they left it.

Most people (especially those you interviewed) are unaware that the logarithmic increase in world population has, and will increasingly have, a degenerative effect on the quality of all our lives. L.A. is fortunate that it has suffered the least from this.

F. H. HACK

Torrance
