BFN [Text] Guangzhou, April 21 (XINHUA) -- A contract for a new express highway to be jointly built by China and Malaysia, that will link Yangjiang with Zhanjiang in south China's Guangdong Province, was signed here today. Its cost is estimated at 6.68 billion yuan, which will be raised by two Malaysian companies, the United Engineers (Malaysia) Berbhad and the Ho Hup Construction Company Berbhad of Malaysia. The 188-kilometer express highway will have four lanes, designed for speed up to 120 km per hour. It involves the building of 148 bridges and 34 overpasses. The construction of the highway will be started late next year and is expected to be completed by 1998.
