Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] In response to the request from the United Nations to increase the number of soldiers in the conflict zone, Spain will send to Bosnia-Herzegovina about 200 more blue helmets, who will join the Spanish UN battalion next week. In all, the new Spanish contribution will comprise around 200 men, who will be joining the 1,200 Spanish blue helmets stationed in the Mostar area. It is expected that they will be able to travel to the former Yugoslavia during the course of next week. According to an official spokesman, a radar detection unit, which locates mortar and heavy weapon trajectories, will be sent. The unit will comprise six vehicles and 25 men, who will have the task of monitoring and consolidating the cease-fire in the city of Mostar, which is considered vital for the security of the population.
