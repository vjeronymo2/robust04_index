March 6, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: POST-TRAUMATIC STRESS

Question: I read the column in which you said that even people who have never been in combat can have post-traumatic stress disorder. But does there have to have been a physical injury before a person can say that he has such a problem? And is there medicine to treat the condition, or is this something for which you need a psychiatrist?

Answer: Physical injury is not a prerequisite for a diagnosis of post-traumatic stress disorder. For example, a person who witnesses an automobile accident in which a friend or relative is killed may develop the disorder.

Some antidepressants may be beneficial for some patients, and anti-anxiety therapy may be helpful for others.

Neil Solomon is author of "Sick & Tired of Being Sick & Tired," Wynwood Press. If you have a medical question, write to Dr. Solomon, P.O. Box 36184, Baltimore, Md. 21285-6184. Solomon cannot give personal replies but will answer as many questions as possible in his column.
