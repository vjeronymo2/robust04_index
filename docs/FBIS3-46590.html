Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Received via Nikkei Telecom Database] [Text] Commenting on reports that the United States declared in the Japan-U.S. comphrensive economic talks that it is not seeking to set numerical targets, a Foreign Ministry source on 22 December criticized the United States, saying "the United States is calling on Japan to raise foreign products' market shares in Japan to the level of their shares in the seven industrialized nations (G-7). That is as bad as setting numerical targets." The source added that "it was decided in July (at the time of Japan and the United States agreeing on frameworks) that no numerical targets should be set."
