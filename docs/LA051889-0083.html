May 18, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

UNIONS: CHARGE OF BRAINWASHING;

EASTERN UNIONS BRAINWASHING, LORENZO SAYS

Eastern Airlines boss Frank Lorenzo has accused the carrier's striking unions of brainwashing members and has asked psychiatrists to study the materials sent by strike leaders to the rank and file.

In a speech last Friday to students at Stanford University's business school, Lorenzo, chairman of Eastern's parent, Texas Air Corp., said the psychiatrists met in Washington to examine the union literature.

Texas Air spokesman Art Kent said the company had no immediate comment on Lorenzo's remarks. An account of Lorenzo's speech in Palo Alto was released by the business school.

"We have people here (in the unions) who have been totally, completely brainwashed," said Lorenzo, as quoted by a Stanford news release.

"We have, in fact, a meeting going on today in Washington, D.C., by a bunch of psychiatrists and psychoanalysts who are going through the propaganda materials that these people are being sent. And this is brainwashing in the style of the '40s.

"What is being put into their heads gets them excited about giving up their jobs and giving up their careers."

It was unclear from Lorenzo's remarks whether he had hired the psychiatrists and, if so, how much he was paying them. It also was unclear how he intended to use their analysis of the union materials.

Dan Ashby, a spokesman in New York for the Eastern pilots' union, called Lorenzo's remarks "a continuation of his desperation tactics."

"We would suggest that Mr. Lorenzo sit down with these people (the psychiatrists) and find out what his own problems are," he said. "We're very sane people; we're very serious about what we're doing."

Since March 4, pilots and flight attendants at Miami-based Eastern have supported a strike by the airline's machinists' union. Lorenzo put Eastern into Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection March 9.

Protesters threw eggs in Lorenzo's direction before he entered the auditorium to make his one-hour speech and called him "Hitler" and "greedy bastard" when he left, the business school's news release said. It also said the 200 or so protesters included pilots and machinists.
