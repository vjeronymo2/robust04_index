BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vilor Niyazmatov] [Text] Tashkent, 16 Jun -- Our national currency will still further strengthen Uzbekistan's independence, President Islam Karimov stated at today's sitting of the presidium of the Supreme Council and Cabinet of Ministers, which discussed the introduction into circulation from 1 July this year of Uzbekistan's own first ever national currency -- the sum. It is expected that the intermediate currency at present in circulation -- the sum coupons -- will be exchanged at the rate of 1,000 sum coupons to one sum. The national currency of the Republic of Uzbekistan -- the sum -- is being issued into cash and monetary circulation in the form of bank notes of the Central Bank of Uzbekistan in denominations of 1, 3, 5, 10, 25, 50, and 100 sums and metal coins with the value of 1, 3, 5, 10, 20, and 50 (?tiyins). Therefore, from 1 July this year the sum will be the sole legal means of payment in the territory of the Republic of Uzbekistan. Along with the national currency the sum, freely convertible currency is permitted to be used in the stipulated way as a means of payment in the territory of the Republic of Uzbekistan.
