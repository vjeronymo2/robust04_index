Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 17 Mar (MENA)--The Higher Military Court this morning passed the death sentence against nine defendants involved in an assassination attempt against Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi. Fifteen defendants, including six fugitives, were on trial in this case, which is better known as the "Vanguards of Conquest" case. Three defendants were given a 15-year prison term with hard labor, one defendant was given a five-year prison term with hard labor, one was sentenced to three years in prison and fined 500 Egyptian pounds, and another defendant was acquitted. The defendants sentenced to death are: Fugitives 'Adil al-Sayyid 'Abd-al-Quddus, Tharwat Salah Shihatah, Yasir 'Ali al-Sirri, and Ahmad Isma'il 'Uthman, Salah al-Sayyid Sulyman, 'Isam Muhammad 'Abd-al-Rahman, Nur-al-Din Sulayman Muhammad, Amin Isma'il Musaylihi, and Tariq 'Abd-al-Nabi al-Fahl. The defendants sentenced to 15 years in prison with hard labor are: Tuhami Ahmad 'Abdallah, Muhammad 'Ali 'Ali Khalil, and Hani 'Abdallah 'Abd-al-Ra'uf. Defendant 'Abd-al-Rahman Hamid was given a five-year sentence with hard labor. Defendant Wahid Sa'd Fikri al-Mikkawi, a fugitive, was sentenced to three years in prison and fined 500 Egyptian pounds. Defendant 'Amr Husayn, also a fugitive, was acquitted.
