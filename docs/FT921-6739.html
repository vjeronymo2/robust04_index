920226 FT 26 FEB 92 / Paternoster Square application called in MR MICHAEL HESELTINE, environment secretary, yesterday took charge of the planning application for Paternoster Square, putting another twist into one of the most controversial planning debates in London. His decision to call in the application is likely to mean the end of the neo-classical proposals put forward by Paternoster Associates to replace the post-war office blocks on the site north of St Paul's Cathedral. Mr Heseltine's intervention may be controversial in that the proposals were warmly received by the public. Many critics felt, however, the scheme was excessively bulky. In December the Corporation of London's planning committee sent the scheme back to the developers with 22 objections. Nuclear Electric's application to redevelop Sudbury House, adjacent to the square, has also been called in by Mr Heseltine. Mr Heseltine has called on the landowners and the corporation to prepare a master plan to guide development. 'In my view it is essential that this area is developed in a coherent way and to the highest possible standards, given the national importance of the site.' He added that he would consider the called-in planning applications on their merits. 'However, they will be judged against the high standards I consider to be appropriate for this site and the need for the area to be developed coherently.'
