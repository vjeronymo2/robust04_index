April 24, 1990, Tuesday, Southland Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

SUDAN;

JUNTA PUTS DOWN 2ND COUP ATTEMPT

For the second time in a month, Sudan's military government crushed a revolt by junior army officers. The country's military leader, Lt. Gen. Omar Hassan Bashir, said in a broadcast on government radio that loyalist forces arrested the rebels and maintained control of the East African nation. He did not give casualty figures or the number of arrests.
