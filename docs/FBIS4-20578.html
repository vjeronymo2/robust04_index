Boron 2p Electron Density of States of Various Boron Compounds and Their Comparison With K-V X-Ray Emission Spectra 43070061I Tokyo ISBB'93 in English Aug 93 p 48--FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY 43070061I Tokyo ISBB'93 English CSO [English abstract of article by Jun Kawai, Masayoshi Kobayashi, and lwami Higashi, Institute of Physical and Chemical Research (RIKEN); Yasuji Muramatsu, NTT Interdisciplinary Research Laboratories; and Hirohiko Adachi, Department of Metallurgy, Kyoto University] [Text] The K-V (K_a_) X-ray emission (fluorescence) spectra are emitted by the electric dipole transition of an electron from 2p to 1s orbital after the photoionization of one of the 1s electron (V denotes valence hole state). The 2p orbitals of boron, carbon, nitrogen, and fluorine form valence molecular orbitals (i.e., a valence band) in the compounds. Therefore the K-V X-ray line shapes represent the local (X-ray emitting atom) and partial (2p) electron density of states (DOS) of the compounds. The discrete-variational (DV) Hartree-Fock-Slater (X_a_) calculations of carbon 2p DOS of various carbon compounds have successfully reproduced the measured carbon K-V X-ray line shapes of benzene, C[.sub]60[/],[.sup]1[/] C[.sub]70[/],[.sup]2[/] graphite, and diamond.[.sup]3[/] We then extend these calculations to various boron compounds in the present paper. The boron 2p DOS of various boron compounds (B, B[.sub]4[/]C, BN, B[.sub]2[/]O[.sub]3[/], B[.sub]6[/]O, BF[.sub]3[/], ...) are calculated by the DV-X_a_ method with a cluster-molecule approximation, where, e.g., B[.sub]12[/] molecule is calculated in place of a solid boron. The calculated B 2p DOS is compared with the measured X-ray emission spectra in the literature, resulting in satisfactory agreement between the calculation and the experiment. References 1. J.
