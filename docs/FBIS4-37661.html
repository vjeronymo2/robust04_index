BFN [Text] The National Medicines Selection Board at the Ministry of Health has approved the dispensing of Izoline 50 mg [name as transliterated] to patients suffering from angina pectoris [a heart attack] instead of Angipist [name as transliterated]. A Health Ministry source said that this measure was taken due to the unavailability of Angipist due to the unfair embargo on medicines for the Iraqi people and the UN Sanctions Commission's disapproval of Iraq's request to obtain this medicine from the British Force Whitcome Company [name as transliterated], which supplies Iraq with this medicine.
