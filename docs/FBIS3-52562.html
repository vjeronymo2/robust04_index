Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Sudan did not receive any official note from the Eritrean Government on the statement attributed to Eritrean President Isayas Afewerki on the infiltration of hostile elements into his country across the Sudanese border, the foreign minister, Dr. Husayn Abu-Salih, said. Speaking at the Foreign Ministry's regular forum, Abu-Salih reiterated that the Sudan and Eritrea maintain strong and fraternal relations, pointing out that the two countries ties have been found [as heard] on strong principles. He said the Sudan had extended continuous support to the Eritrean liberation struggle till the country achieved independence, and that the Sudan was among the first countries who supported Eritrea as an independent state. The foreign minister went on say that the Sudan now hosts half a million Eritreans. Less than half of them are living in nine camps under the supervision of the Sudanese Refugees Administration. The refugees camps house refugees from all colors of the political spectrum, the foreign minister said, stressing that the refugees are not allowed to exercise any form of political activity. Abu-Salih indicated that the Sudan provides 75 percent of the needs of the Eritrean refugees while the international community extends only 25 percent of their requirements, calling for the conclusion of a tripartite agreement between the governments of the Sudan and Eritrea and the UNHCR to organize the voluntary repatriation of the Eritrean refugees.
