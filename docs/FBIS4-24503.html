BFN [By Liu Kun-yuan and Danielle Yang] [Text] Madrid, May 27 (CNA) -- Taiwan registered its first four-monthly deficit ever with the European Union through April, according to customs statistics. The trade deficit of US$40 million resulted from increasing imports from and decreasing exports to the EU, customs officials said. Taiwan registered a US$543 million trade surplus with the EU in the first four months of 1993. During the January-April period, Taiwan imported US$3.596 billion worth of products from the EU, a growth of 8.7 percent from the first four months of last year. Taiwan's exports to the area totaled only US$3.556 billion, a 7.7 percent decline, the statistics showed. The US$7.153 billion in two-way trade between the two was a 0.1 percent decline.
