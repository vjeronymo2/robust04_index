910508 FT 08 MAY 91 / Observer: Hit lucky The battle between wealthy investment tycoon George Soros and his ex-butler makes fascinating reading to those of us accustomed to life below stairs. Tales of handwashed socks, Pounds 500 per bottle cooking wine, and 18th century French silver which melted after being put in the dishwasher are a reminder to out-of-work investment bankers what life can still be like in the fast lane. It is a world where the grocery bills are so high that they are felt to be confidential. And while the butler has won his fight for unfair dismissal, Soros retains a lucky streak. His Jaguar V12 Sovereign was rammed from the rear, first by a company chairman's wife driving a Range Rover, then by a member of the Diplomatic Corps. But as each hit the same spot, only one repair was necessary.
