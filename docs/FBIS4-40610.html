BFN [Text] Berlin June 19 TASS -- Today, on the last day of an official visit to Germany, a State Duma delegation, led by its Chairman Ivan Rybkin, laid a wreath at the memorial to Russian liberator soldiers in the Treptow Park, Berlin. After two days of meetings and talks with leaders and prominent politicians in Bonn, Russian legislators arrived in the German capital on Saturday, where they familiarised themselves with the experience of privatization of state enterprises in the eastern part of the country, as well as the examples of the participation of foreign business people in that process. During their visit to the Kvo Kabel plant, which now fully belongs to a British firm and supplies half of its output to Russia, as well as during their conversation with Berlin Burgomaster Eberhard Dipgen members of the delegation got very interesting and useful information, Rybkin told ITAR-TASS. "There should be 101 ways of privatization of state-owned enterprises instead of just one," Rybkin pointed out. He said that at present there are some 1,500 industrial enterprises in Russia which may go bankrupt. In the opinion of Rybkin, their situation calls for a comprehensive approach. The Russian delegation will have a meeting today with heads of the Berlin chamber of deputies in the Palace of Charlottenburg. The guests will also visit the Reichstag building. In the afternoon of the last day of the visit they will have meetings with the command of the Western Army group and the personnel of the Berlin Brigade.
