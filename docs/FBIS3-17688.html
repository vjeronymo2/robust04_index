Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] As of date, Armenia gives roof to 305,000 refugees, 260,000 of which were deported from Azerbaijan and the rest -- from Abkhazia and the Shaumyanski District of Nagorno-Karabakh. According to chief Vladimir Movsisyan of the national Agency on the Affairs of Refugees, the deportees enjoy all the civic rights and freedoms but no special status. They are regarded as people without citizenship. The 16,000 people deported in 1988 from Sumgait, in Azerbaijan, have by now been provided with housing facilities or live in houses of their own. 28,000 deportees enjoy the right to permanent residency in Armenia and another 35,000 have been given plots of land.
