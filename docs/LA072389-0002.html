July 23, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

Shame on Channel 5 for running horribly "colorized" versions of classic films marred by Turner Program Services. I tried watching "The Big Sleep" and it looked ludicrous, a true desecration of a piece of art. Black and white was absolutely essential to the mood. Do the Spanish slop colored paint on Picasso's "Guernica"?

I turned down the color on my set, but found it too bright and in need of contrast adjusting. It still didn't look right. I've refused to watch colorization before, but I really wanted to see this movie.

As for the advertisers, I saw their commercials in black and white. Their impact was greatly diminished. I would have seen them as you'd intended if KTLA had shown the film as its artists had intended.

Thanks to Television Times for printing no checks in front of the film's title and noting (color) after it. I'll certainly avoid them in the future.

Bruce Kuehn, North Hollywood
