BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 5 May (NA) -- The PEN [National Executive Branch] has announced that the Constituent Convention will meet as of 0900 [1200 GMT] on 25 May. The executive branch asked the Entre Rios provincial government "to make all necessary arrangements for the meeting." The call comes in Decree 657, which was published yesterday in the Official Gazette. The decree calls on "Constituent Assembly members elected on 10 April to meet in Parana, Entre Rios Province, at 0900 on 25 May 1994." The decree is signed by President Carlos Menem and Interior Minister Carlos Ruckauf. It states that the call follows Law 24309, which establishes that the Convention must open within 60 days of the election. The decree adds that the law declaring the need for reforming the Constitution "establishes that the Chamber of Deputies' internal regulation will regulate the Convention's operation" and that "due to the importance of the event, the opening date -- 25 May -- grants the occasion the necessary solemnity and importance because it coincides with another anniversary of our independence."
