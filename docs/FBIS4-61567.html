BFN [Text] On Saturday Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Defense Minister Pavel Grachev sent congratulation telegrams due to the forthcoming Victory Day addressed to the chairman of the Sevastopol city council of people's deputies Viktor Semenov, residents of Sevastopol, and the Black Sea Fleet mariners. Having congratulated the Sevastopol residents and the Black Sea Fleet mariners with the Victory Day and with the 50th anniversary of the liberation of the city from German fascists, Yeltsin stressed in his telegram that Sevastopol, the major base of the Black Sea Fleet, "is one of the national sacred places for all Russians" which "strengthens the force and the spirit of the Russian people and gives them a hope for the best future." "Eternal glory to those heroes who defended the freedom and independence of Sevastopol," says the president's telegram. For his part, Pavel Grachev pointed out that despite "the difficult times" which Sevastopol is experiencing now "neither economic difficulties, nor political contradictions, nor painful problems related to the Black Sea Fleet division should darken this bright holiday." "During my recent visit to Sevastopol I was made sure of the strong will of the residents of this hero-city and saw how its residents trust that their city would be the major base of the well known Black Sea Fleet," Grachev underlined.
