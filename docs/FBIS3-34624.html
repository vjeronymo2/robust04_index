Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, Feb 9 (MENA) -- President Husni Mubarak tonight attended the signing ceremony of the Cairo Agreement on implementation of steps related to checkpoints in the occupied Arab territories and the size of the West Bank town of Jericho as well as maps supplemented to the accord. Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres initialled the treaty. 'Abu-'Ala' [Ahmad Quray'], head of the PLO economic department , and Uri Safir, Israeli Foreign Ministry director-general, also initialled the agreement's supplements and [word indistinct]. Information Minister Safwat al-Sharif along with members of the Egyptian, Palestinian and Israeli delegations and two representatives of the Norwegian Government have officiated the signing ceremony. Following the signing of the agreement, President Mubarak delivered a brief speech in which he alluded to intensive talks held here over three days. He asserted that this agreement will remove all obstacles blocking the final signing by 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin on an agreement on implementation of the declaration of principles they signed in Washington in September. The president expressed hopes that upcoming Palestinian-Israeli negotiations would achieve progress. He also congratulated both the Palestinian and Israeli peoples on the signing of this treaty, meant to put an end to bloody conflict and tension in the region. Following the signing of the accord, President 'Arafat hugged President Mubarak amidst applaud by delegation members. Peres also embraced President Mubarak on the occasion of this historic moment. The PLO chairman extended heart-felt thanks in the name of the Palestinian people and his name to President Mubarak for his efforts that helped produce such results. 'Arafat also thanked 'Amr Musa as well as Foreign Ministry experts for the help they gave in hammering out the treaty. [Word indistinct] consider what we have done over the past three days a vital step towards implementation of what we signed in Washington," added the PLO chairman. Shim'on Peres made a brief speech thanking Presidents Mubarak and (?'Arafat) for efforts aimed at achieving peace in the Middle East. He also extended thanks for Foreign Minister Musa. Peres said the talks tackled five or six points considered the most intricate. I want to tell the Palestinians that we are serious and looking forward to peace between Jews and the Arabs, Peres said. Attending were some 300 representatives of world media organs and news agencies.
