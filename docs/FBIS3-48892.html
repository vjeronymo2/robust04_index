Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, January 26 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The former chief of the counterintelligence Colonel L. Sanjaasuren has notified the chairman and the vice chairman of the parliament and the prime minister that in case these high officials not made clear conclusions he is ready to make public new facts telling about their involvement in corruption and contacts with secret services of some foreign states. In this way the former chief of the counterintelligence who has been removed recently from the office expressed his readiness to complete "the case." L. Sanjaasuren started from fact than at the end of the last year he has held a press conference at which he informed that there are materials documents in his department telling about possible contacts of the above mentioned high officials with foreign secret services. The parliament working group on investigation of this information has not revealed any signs telling about liquidation of these materials as L. Sanjaasuren supposed in this connection. Colonel L. Sanjaasuren had to announce that a special system of concealing improper deeds of state high officials is already formed in the country. As far as the parliament speaker N. Bagadandi, vice speaker J. Gombojav, and the Prime Minister P. Jasray are concerned they are resolutely rejecting the charges of L. Sanjaasuren and the press.
