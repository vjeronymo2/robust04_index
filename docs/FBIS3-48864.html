Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] A senior official in the U.S. Association of Automobile Industries has said that at Japan-U.S. economic framework talks, U.S. negotiators had asked their Japanese counterparts to go along with a U.S. demand for setting a target for selling 100,000 American-built cars three years later on the Japanese market. Commenting on the U.S. official's remark at a regular news conference on 27 January, Hideaki Kumano, administrative vice minister of the Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI], said: "The Japanese Government cannot commit itself to goods that Japanese consumers buy. The U.S. demand cited by the official is out of the question and an inconceivable concept."
