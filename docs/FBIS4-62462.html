BFN [Text] Moscow, 12 May (TURAN)--The meeting between President Aliyev and Armenia's President Ter-Petrosyan is due to take place in Moscow in the up-coming days, reports sources from the Russian Foreign Ministry. Russia is acting as initiator and organizer of the Armenian-Azerbaijani summit. "The presidents of both republics are expected to sign an agreement providing for a ceasefire, withdrawal of troops from captured territories, and a restoration of communications," reported the Russian Foreign Ministry. The Russian Foreign Ministry reports that the leadership of Nagorno-Karabakh will sign the agreement as well. The agreement is to be based on the agreement signed in February 1994 by Azerbaijan's and Armenia's Defense Ministers as well as representatives of Nagorno-Karabakh. The key point of the agreement is the introduction of peacemaking separating forces in the zone of the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict. "In line with the agreement, Moscow will be the guarantor of the Armenian-Azerbaijani truce. The peacemaking forces can be referred to as international element to the CIS peacemaking forces, but they will be headed by Russian armed forces," told a highranking diplomatic source to Moscow's TURAN's correspondent.
