Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Report by BELINFORM correspondent Alyaksandr Lyushkevich under the "Facts and Comments" rubric: "Our Customs Will Cooperate"] [Text] During Bill Clinton's visit to Minsk, the Government of the United States and the Republic of Belarus signed an agreement on cooperation and mutual assistance between their respective customs services. A BELINFORM correspondent has asked Hyenadz Shkurdz, chairman of the State Committee for Customs, to comment on this agreement. "This is the first governmental agreement on cooperation of our customs service with one of its foreign counterparts. It will become effective 90 days after the date of signing. "This document provides that the two sides will assist one another in preventing and investigating any violations of the law, which are within the competence of their customs services. According to the agreement, the sides will provide each other with all information concerning the imposition of custom duties and other taxes. It is also very important that the sides assist each other in providing customs services with modern equipment. This also includes personnel training and the exchange of information on the latest developments in 'customs' equipment. That is of the greatest importance for us right now. The agreement also provides for the exchange of experts."
