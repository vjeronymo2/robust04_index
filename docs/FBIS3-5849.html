Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 5 (MTI) -- Hungarian Minister of International Economic Relations Bela Kadar arrived in Japan today to attend a session of the Japanese-U.S. committee set up to promote the Central European countries' economic development on Monday and Tuesday. Subsequently, Kadar will hold talks in the Ministries of Foreign Affairs, International Trade and Industry. The discussions are expected to focus on Hungary's admission to the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development and the related Japanese support. Set up in 1989, the Japanese-U.S. committee regularly analyzes recent changes in the Visegrad countries (Czech Republic, Hungary, Poland, Slovakia) and reviews how Japan and the United States can help them catch up with the advanced industrial nations.
