BFN [Text] Vienna, June 29 (AFP) -- Ukraine has signed a draft agreement with the International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] to allow the UN atomic watchdog agency to inspect its nuclear installations, an IAEA statement said Wednesday [29 June]. Under the preliminary accord, "Ukraine undertakes to use the nuclear material and facilties under its jurisdiction or control exclusively for peaceful purposes." The draft safeguards agreement, as it is known, was reached in two days of talks with Kiev officials at IAEA headquarters here and must be approved by the agency's Board of Governors takes it up in September. It also provides for "the application of safeguards by the IAEA to all nuclear material in all peaceful nuclear activities of Ukraine." IAEA spokesman Hans Friedrich Meyer said the draft accord is a key first step towards Ukraine's joining the Nuclear Non-proliferation Treaty. Ukraine is the site of the world's worst civilian nuclear disaster, caused by a meltdown in the number four reactor at its Chernobyl nuclear plant on April 26, 1986. Western states have been applying pressure for the plant to be totally shut down but President Leonid Kravchuk has brushed the demands aside, saying his country needs to keep it going for at least several more years to cover Ukraine's energy shortage.
