May 22, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Baker Hughes Agrees to Sell Unit: Baker Hughes Inc. of Houston said it agreed to sell its diamond drill bit business to oil service company Dresser Industries of Dallas to comply with a Justice Department consent decree.

Terms were not disclosed. Baker Hughes agreed to the sale to meet conditions of a decree in connection with Baker Hughes' $550-million acquisition of Eastman Christensen Co. announced last month, said Baker Hughes vice president Isaac Kerridge. The diamond drill bit business had revenue of about $18 million in fiscal 1989.
