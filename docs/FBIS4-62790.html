BFN [INTERFAX report: "Prosecutor's Office To Check Legality of Opposition Leaders' Speeches"] [Text] The Russian General Prosecutor's Office has instructed Moscow Prosecutor Gennadiy Ponomarev to investigate illegal actions which took place in the capital 9 May 1994 during a rally in Lubyanka Square. INTERFAX was told at the General Prosecutor's Office that G. Ponomarev has been instructed to carry out an investigation and make the appropriate legal assessment of the case of the public burning of the Russian Federation state flag, of the calls from the rally speakers for a struggle to "destroy the existing regime," of the abusive attacks on top state officials, and of the anti-Semitic slogans and banners. The rally in Lubyanka Square was organized by a number of opposition parties and movements. In particular, it was addressed by former Russian Vice President Aleksandr Rutskoy and National Salvation Front leader Ilya Konstantinov, who both spent around six months in jail on the charge of organizing the mass riots in Moscow 3-4 October 1993. In his speech Rutskoy called for the 50th anniversary of the victory over fascism in 1995 to be marked by a "victory over this antipeople regime." A statement from the General Prosecutor's Office notes that the Prosecutor's Office is attentively following the development of the situation and warning that "full use will be made of the powers granted it under the law to put a stop to unconstitutional actions by both individuals and associations of citizens aimed at destabilizing the situation in society."
