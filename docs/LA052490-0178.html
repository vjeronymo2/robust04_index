May 24, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

BEST SANDWICHES

Question: My children take peanut butter sandwiches to school every day. They enjoy them, I've been told that peanut butter is nutritious, and the sandwiches take very little time to prepare.

But I've noticed that there's usually a little bit of oil at the top of the jar, and I'm becoming concerned that my children are getting too much cholesterol. Would you please let me know how much cholesterol there is in peanut butter, compared, for example, with a frankfurter? Which would be better for them to have?

Answer: One frankfurter provides about 34 milligrams of cholesterol. Peanut butter is not an animal product, and therefore does not contain any cholesterol. On all counts, a peanut butter sandwich, particularly if prepared with whole wheat bread, would be much more nutritious than a frankfurter.
