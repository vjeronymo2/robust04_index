BFN [Excerpts] As part of celebrations marking media day, President Husni Mubarak will inaugurate Channel Three of the central delta on Monday. The channel will begin experimental transmission for four hours per day, gradually increasing to eight hours per day in June, until it finally reaches full transmission in October. Information Minister Safwat al-Sharif has stated that Channel Seven will begin transmission in al-Minya in President Mubarak's presence. The president inaugurated the first stage of that project last October. As for the Egyptian international channel, Nile TV, the information minister said that it transmits two hours daily at present. This will be increased to six hours, using the European satellite [not further specified]. He added that preparations are underway to expand this channel to African areas via Arabsat by the end of 1994. [passage omitted] Al-Sharif added that a strategic project will be submitted to President Mubarak. This project is the Egyptian satellite, for which studies have been completed by French and U.S. firms. The satellite will have between 12 and 56 channels. Al-Sharif hopes that work on the project will start before the end of this year so that the satellite can be launched by the end of 1996. [passage omitted]
