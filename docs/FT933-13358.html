930720 FT 20 JUL 93 / Parliament and Politics: New row over Churchill speech CONSERVATIVE MP Mr Winston Churchill last night repeated his call for a halt to further immigration. 'If we are to curb the scourge of racism in our society we must first and foremost stop adding to the problem. 'The government must act - and urgently - if in the years ahead we are to avoid in our own cities the sort of ethnic violence we have witnessed in Germany, or worse,' he said. Mr Nigel Griffiths, Labour MP for Edinburgh South, called for Mr Churchill to be expelled from the Conservative party. 'There is no point in the prime minister saying he disagrees with Mr Churchill if he takes no action to distance the Tory party from his views. 'He should do this by withdrawing the whip and telling the country he may not stand again as a Tory candidate.'
