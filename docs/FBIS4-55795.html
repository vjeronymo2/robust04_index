BFN [Address to the nation by Bulgarian President Zhelyu Zhelev in Sofia on 20 May -- recorded] [Text] Dear compatriots: During the last few days a farce has developed in Bulgaria that poses a threat to the foundations of parliamentarianism. Guided by personal and party interests, many deputies have involved themselves in lobbying machinations and bargaining that are well on the way to finally destroying the people's trust in the institutions of state. I do not have the right to judge anyone, but I am convinced that democracy, apart from merely being a form of procedure, also means a sense of responsibility, which some people have forgotten. The deepening political crisis has led to a complete paralysis of state life. In my view, we can extract ourselves from this situation only with the determination of all the responsible political forces in the country. This conviction led me to invite the chairmen of the parliamentary groups to a meeting. Yesterday and today I held talks with National Assembly Chairman Aleksandur Yordanov; with Stefan Savov, chairman of the parliamentary group of the Union of Democratic Forces [SDS]; with representatives of the NSD [New Alliance for Democracy] and the Independence parliamentary group, Georgi Kostov, Nikolay Vasilev, and Ivan Pushkarov; and with Aleksandur Tomov, chairman of the GOR [Civic Alliance for the Republic]. For reasons outside my control, no meetings took place with Nora Ananieva, parliamentary group leader for the BSP [Bulgarian Socialist Party] and its coalition, or with Ahmed Dogan, leader of the DPS [Movement for Rights and Freedoms] parliamentary group. I will now propose to the nation what I proposed to the politicians: the reaching of an agreement between the parliamentary forces on early elections. The agreement should define the following: First, the exact date of the elections. Second, it should guarantee the passing of laws that will ensure the conclusion of the agreement on our foreign debt and create conditions for carrying through real reforms. Third, the agreement should lead to the formation of a government that will enjoy the confidence of all the parliamentary forces, guarantee responsible government in the pre-election period, guarantee the fairness of the elections, and continue the reforms that have begun. If such an agreement is concluded and such a government elected, I shall assume the obligation to entrust this government with the additional task of carrying out the functions of a caretaker government during the final two months preceding the elections. I hope that this will put an end to the dirty speculations concerning the role of a future caretaker cabinet and will enable us to emerge from the crisis, because what we are now putting at risk is not merely the instability of one government or another, of some parliament or other, but faith in parliamentarianism and democracy. Dear compatriots, I want to believe that the parliamentary forces will find the courage, responsibility, and goodwill to conclude such an agreement on early elections, an agreement for preserving democracy and parliamentarianism. However, at the ballot box the decision lies with you.
