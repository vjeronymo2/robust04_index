BFN [Excerpts] Kiev, [no dateline as received] -- At the Crimean Supreme Council's plenary sitting on 21 May, Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov congratulated the deputies on the adoption of the Crimean law "On restoring the constitutional basis of the Republic of Crimea's statehood" and declared that the constitution he had sworn allegiance to was that adopted on 6 May 1992. Yuriy Meshkov gave an account of his tour of Krasnodar on 20 May, where he took part in the work of the Association of Socio-Economic Cooperation between North Caucasian republics, krays, and oblasts. At the association's sitting in Krasnodar, Crimea was admitted to this North Caucasian organization as an associate member. In Yuriy Meshkov's words, preparations for Crimea's entry into the association had been under way for six months, especially since his election as president. During his stay in Krasnodar, the Crimean president also took part in the work of the Grand Assembly of Cossack Host Atamans and met representatives of the Krasnodar kray public. [passage omitted] Crimean Supreme Council Deputy Viktor Mezhak informed the deputies that, under the accord of 21 May with the head of the Supreme Council of Ukraine Secretariat, a Crimean Supreme Council delegation, to be led by Crimean parliamentary speaker Serhiy Tsekov, would meet a Ukrainian Supreme Council delegation, to be led by Borys Oliynyk, in Kiev next week.
