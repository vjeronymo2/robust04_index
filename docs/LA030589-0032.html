March 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OSCAR SCHOLARSHIP

The international uproar over the novel "The Satanic Verses" should be called "The Last Temptation of Christ -- Part II." Whether it's Islamic leaders threatening the life of an author or Christians burning theaters in France for showing Oscar nominee Scorsese's "Last Temptation," it gives religion a bad name.

These attempts at artistic suppression have served only to motivate people to (1) buy a book that doesn't exactly sound like zippy material and (2) see a film they wouldn't have wanted to see.

LINDA COWAN MOSE

Sherman Oaks
