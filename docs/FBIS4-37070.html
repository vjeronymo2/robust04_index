BFN [Text] Khartoum, 14 Jun (SUNA) -- Mrs. Melissa Wells, U.S. President Clinton's envoy, in a statement to SUNA in the context of the peace efforts and the resolution of the southern problem, has described her visit to Sudan as constructive. Mrs. Wells said after being received by President al-Bashir yesterday evening, that she would begin a tour today of some Sudanese states in line with her mission to Sudan. Dr. Ghazi Salah al-Din, the minister of state at the presidency of the Republic responsible for political affairs, had earlier told SUNA after President al-Bashir had received Mrs. Wells that she had brought a letter from U.S. President Clinton for President al-Bashir. He said Mrs. Wells came with the intention of expressing U.S. support for the Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development initiative. He added that Mrs. Wells had referred to the importance of taking humanitarian aspects into account in the provision of relief and to the need to negotiate in a spirit of seriousness so as to achieve the aspirations for peace.
