BFN [Text] Seoul, June 16 (YONHAP) -- The South Korean Government held an unofficial unification and security policy coordination meeting Wednesday and agreed to a five-item U.S. draft resolution on U.N. sanctions against North Korea. "South Korea and the United States have already completed their consultations on the draft resolution," said one senior government official Thursday. "There would not be any change in close coordination between Seoul and Washington in the process of proposing the draft resolution at the Security Council and its adoption," the official added. The U.S. Government sent a copy of the draft resolution to Seoul Tuesday afternoon to hear South Korea's views on it. The government's unofficial meeting Wednesday was presided over by Deputy Prime Minister Yi Hong-ku. Another unofficial meeting is planned for Thursday, sources said.
