BFN [News conference by Muhsin Dallul, defense and acting information minister, with unidentified correspondents in Beirut on 20 April--recorded] [Excerpts] [Dallul] The cabinet held its weekly session under His Excellency Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri at 1700 today in the Government House. The prime minister began the session by highlighting the government's insistence on handing over the two Iraqi defendants to the judicial authorities to continue the investigation. His Excellency Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz presented a detailed report on the issue and on the implementation of the cabinet decision to sever diplomatic ties and on conveying this decision to the Iraqi charge d'affaires. He also reviewed the reactions to the decision. The cabinet then discussed the items on its agenda. [passage omitted] [Correspondent] Did the cabinet discuss the workers' living conditions and demonstrations? [Dallul] We are always discussing this issue. The cabinet's decision is to ban demonstrations. This decision remains in effect and will not be rescinded, because it serves the interests of the people and Lebanon. We will confront whatever harms Lebanon's interests. We will not let such things pass, whatever the consequences.
