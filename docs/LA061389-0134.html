June 13, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ALMANAC / ORANGE COUNTY CROP REPORT: NURSERY STOCK, FLOWERS TOP LIST OF CROP VALUES

For the 20th straight year, nursery stock and cut flowers topped the list of Orange County crops ranked by dollar value, according to the 1988 Orange County Crop Report.

Nursery stock was valued at $123.9 million, a decline of 2% from 1987 but almost triple the value of the second-place crop, strawberries, and about six times the value of third-place Valencia oranges.

Gross dollar value as measured in the report includes cost of production, harvesting and preparation for market, and does not reflect net return to the producer.

Increases in the animal industry, orchard and truck crops helped push the gross value of all crops to $253.6 million, a 3% increase over 1987's total.

HOW AGRICULTURAL MIX HAS CHANGED

The value of Orange County's nursery, orchard and vegetable crops have increased substantially since 1968, while the animal industry's dollar value has declined.
