940520 FT 20 MAY 94 / International Company News: Job cuts and closures at P&G Procter & Gamble, the US consumer products group, yesterday said it was closing three plants and shutting production lines at four other plants over the next 30 months with the loss of 1,100 jobs. The closures are part of the restructuring announced in July last year under which Procter & Gamble is closing 30 plants and shedding 13,000 jobs, some 12 per cent of its workforce world-wide. Under the plans announced yesterday, the company will close the Manati pharmaceuticals plant in Puerto Rico in 1996; the Hatboro health and beauty care products plant in Pennsylvania by May 1996; and the Torshall throat drop products plant in Sweden by the end of next year. Production will be transferred to other sites. Procter & Gamble said employees would be offered early retirement, transfers or 'supportive' severence packages. Skin care production lines will close in Mississauga, Ontario; Hunt Valley, Maryland; and Naucalpan, Mexico.
