BFN [Text] India would have to reappraise the country's defense requirements and take adequate steps to safeguard its security in view of Pakistan's acquisition of sophisticated military equipment. The minister of state for external affairs, Mr. Salman Khurshid, told the Lok Sabha during question hour today that the government has conveyed that the U.S. proposal to transfer F-16 aircraft and other military equipment to Pakistan is a matter of deep concern for India. In a written reply, the minister said the U.S. Government has not pressed for nuclear capping by India as they have proposed to Pakistan as a condition to supplying F-16 fighters. In another written reply, Mr. Salman Khurshid informed the house that the government has consistently maintained that it will not sign the NPT [Nonproliferation Treaty] in its present form because of its discriminatory nature. Referring to the efforts by the U.S. administration to modify the Pressler Amendment, the minister said latest reports indicate that these efforts have not been successful.
