BFN [Text] Chisinau BASAPRESS, 26/4/1994 -- The military delegation of the Republic of Moldova, which had been invited to today's reunion of the heads of major general staffs of NATO countries and partner states, did not leave for Brussels, announced to BASA informed sources from the Minister of Defence (MD) from Chisinau. Anatol Cosciug, the chief of the military policy and treaties section of the MD of Moldova, said that the fact that the Moldovan delegation did not attend the reunion in Brussels is not connected with the refusal of Russia to sign the "Partnership for Peace" NATO program. Cosciug, excluding any political aspect, indicated that the Embassy of Belgium in Moscow didn't manage to issue the necessary visas. The Moldovan delegation was to be headed by Pavel Chirau, the chief of the General Staff of the Military Forces of Moldova.
