BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Yuriy Sizov] [Text] Moscow, 30 Apr -- The Russian Armed Forces are to be withdrawn from the territory of the Latvian Republic by 31 August 1994. This was reflected in the Treaty between Russia and Latvia signed by the presidents of Russia and Latvia in the Kremlin today. Replying to a question from an ITAR-TASS correspondent after the signing ceremony in the Vladimir Hall of the Kremlin, Yeltsin said that a package consisting of five documents had been signed. They had been prepared, he said, with full agreement by both sides. Noting that the documents mainly connected with the withdrawal of troops and the social protection of servicemen and retired servicemen were signed in the Kremlin today, Yeltsin said a package of bilateral economic agreements would be drawn up and signed during a visit to Latvia by the head of the Russian Government Viktor Chernomyrdin.
