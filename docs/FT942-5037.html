940607 FT 07 JUN 94 / Loans blow for recovery denied Lenders were keen yesterday to damp concerns that the latest monthly figures on lending from the CSO reflect a setback for the consumer-led recovery. 'The situation is still buoyant as far as we are concerned,' said Mr Gordon Rankin, deputy marketing director of Barclays Bank. 'Our finance and credit-card operations have not seen any drop off in business, with May and the first few days of June showing positive growth,' he said. Mr Dick Spelman, general manager of marketing for Halifax, said that Class 3 loans - unsecured personal loans and overdrafts - were buoyant 'across a wide range of customers. We have seen month on month improvements from March to April and then April to May.' The CSO figures show that total lending to consumers fell from Pounds 518m in March to Pounds 413m in April. This was mainly due to a sharp fall - Pounds 131m - in lending by finance houses which in turn was due mainly to falls in lending for vehicle purchases and store goods. But the finance houses do not appear concerned. Lombard, a NatWest subsidiary, says 'the fall in car lending is not significant because of the seasonal nature of this business'. Lombard said it was not adjusting its view that 'the market will be around 2m new car units in 1994 compared to 1.78m in 1993'. This view was reiterated by Ms Lesley Kay of the Finance and Leasing Association. 'It's not surprising to see that lending for car purchases has fallen,' she said. 'It is a reflection of the fact that consumers brought forward their car purchases to the first three months of this year in response to special deals from dealers.'
