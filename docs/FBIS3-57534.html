Language: English Article Type:BFN [By BELINFORM-TASS correspondent] [Text] Minsk January 27 TASS -- Vladimir Shumeyko, the speaker of the upper chamber of the Russian parliament, offered Stanislav Shushkevich, who was removed from the post of the chairman of the Belarusian parliament on Wednesday, to head the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly, it was learned here from an anonymous sourse close to Shushkevich. The offer was made on Thursday morning by telephone. This post was previous held by Ruslan Khasbulatov, who was jailed after the October opposition rebellion in Moscow. Shumeyko informed Shushkevich that the offer was agreed with the heads of CIS parliaments. It is worth recalling in this connection that Shushkevich was a co-founder of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) alongside with Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Ukrainian leader Leonid Kravchuk. BELINFORM reported also with reference to the press service of the Belarus Supreme Soviet that Shushkevich would not attend a meeting of the Belarus parliament today. He is expected to hold a press conference here on Monday.
