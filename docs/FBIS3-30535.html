Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Knin, 3 Feb (TANJUG) -- Military sources in Knin [Serb Krajina, Croatia] claim that four guards' brigades of the Croatian Army are engaged in fighting in former Bosnia-Herzegovina -- the First Zagreb Guards' Brigade, the Fourth Split Guards' Brigade, the Fifth Vinkovci Guards' Brigade, and the Ninth Rijeka Guards' Brigade. The same source quotes intelligence reports as saying that in addition to the Croatian Army's guards' brigades, Croatia has reserve units in the field -- the 114th Split Brigade, the 123d Slavonia-Pozega Brigade, and the 128th Rijeka Brigade, as well as units of the 101st and 102d Zagreb brigades, and the 112th Zadar Brigade, numerically equivalent to one battalion. These units, military sources in Knin claim, are mainly engaged against Muslim forces from Stolac [in Herzegovina, southeast of Mostar] to Gornji Vakuf [in central Bosnia].
