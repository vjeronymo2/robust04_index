930616 FT 16 JUN 93 / Observer: Visa problems It appears that Britain may have gained a slight diplomatic advantage over the French with the victory of Chief Moshood Abiola in Nigeria's presidential elections. Abiola, who made his fortune selling telecommunication systems to the Nigerian government in the 1970s, has close ties with Britain. He owns the London-based African Economic Digest and is a frequent visitor to the city. However, Observer hears that France's corps diplomatique may need to mend a few fences. The problem dates back to Abiola's application for a visa to visit France three years ago. After some delay, the chief's personal assistant went to the French embassy in Lagos to complain. Embassy staff, who had not heard of the great man, demanded Abiola provide a return ticket before issuing the visa. 'Chief Abiola does not need a ticket,' the assistant explained. 'He has his own plane.'
