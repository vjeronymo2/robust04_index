921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / The chancellor's loan rate challenge to banks The Bank of England will take 'another look' at interest rates charged by high street banks Norman Lamont, chancellor The latest base rate cut would remove Pounds 1bn from industry's costs 'provided it is passed on ..to small businesses as well as large'. John Major, prime minister 'The government should publish the rates charged by banks, and warn them that they could face an inquiry if these are not cut.' Gordon Brown, shadow chancellor 'There is great dissatisfaction. It is not always apparent how banks arrive at the additional charges over base rates they make.' CBI, headed by Howard Davies 'We may charge a higher margin over base rate for higher-risk businesses. But it certainly is not a blanket policy.' Midland Bank, headed by Brian Pearse 'NatWest has passed on to business customers the full benefits of rate cuts immediately.' Lord Alexander, chairman of NatWest Bank 'We need to maintain margins, our capital base and the best rates possible to savers and investors.'
