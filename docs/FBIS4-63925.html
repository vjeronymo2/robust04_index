BFN [Text] The Russian president's visit to Tatarstan scheduled for May 30-31 is a planned trip the head of state makes to various regions of Russia, Presidential Aide Anatoly Korabelschikov told Interfax. He pointed out that after Moscow and Kazan signed the Mutual Power Delegation Treaty, this visit had no political underlying. According to Korabelschikov, the president will pay special attention to bringing on-line the motor plant damaged by a fire. The plant belongs to the association KamAZ, one of the biggest truck-manufacturing associations in Russia. During his visit, Yeltsin plans to examine principally new engines designed there, in particular the engines of new city buses. The president is also interested in the creation of a financial and production group by KamAZ, Togliatti car-making plant and Avtovazbank. Such groups created in various regions of Russia are called to stimulate the development of top-priority branches of the economy, Korabelschikov said. In Kazan, Yeltsin plans to meet with the Tatar leadership and visit a number of agricultural enterprises.
