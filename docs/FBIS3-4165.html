Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 26 (XINHUA) -- South Korean President kim Yong-sam left here today for China following his three-day visit to Japan. During his scheduled five-day stay in Beijing, Kim is expected to discuss with Chinese leaders on bilateral relations and the situation on the Korean Peninsula. Kim and his wife Son Myong-sun concluded a three-day visit to Japan where he and Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa held talks, among others, on bilateral ties and the nuclear issue on the Korean Peninsula. The two leaders also stressed the importance of building a future-oriented relationship between Tokyo and Seoul.
