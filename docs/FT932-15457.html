930413 FT 13 APR 93 / International Company News: Alcoa suffers as sales and metal prices weaken ALUMINUM Company of America (Alcoa), the world's largest aluminium producer, was hit by low ingot prices and competition in the metal's sheet market in the first quarter of 1993. Net profits for the three months fell by 50 per cent to Dollars 27.6m, or 31 cents a share, down from Dollars 55.1m, or 64 cents, a year earlier. Sales fell 7 per cent to Dollars 2.1bn from Dollars 2.3bn. The net figure included Dollars 4.8m, or 6 cents a share, in foreign exchange profits following the devaluation of the Surinam guilder. The comparable figure included profits of Dollars 8m, or 10 cents a share, on the sale of investments in Venezuela.
