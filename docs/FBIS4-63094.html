BFN [Text] Yerevan ("AZG" daily), 05/19/94 -- "Armenia greets and appreciates the heavy burden of a disengager, which Russian Federation took," said acting spokesman of the President Levon Zurabyan regarding the recent meeting of Defense Ministers in Moscow. The Azeri delegation didn't sign the agreement for some reasons, and left for Baku to continue discussions. Nevertheless, the atmosphere of the Moscow meeting was such that the sides were close to a peaceful resolution of the conflict. "The fact is, there is no military euphoria neither in Mountainous Karabakh nor in Azerbaijan today," Zurabyan said. Presidential spokesman considered a success the fact that Mountainous Karabakh was recognized as an equal side for the first time, and fully participated in negotiations. Regarding the document, developed in Moscow on 17 May, Zurabyan said that its essence was in coordination of military-technical aspects of the cease-fire. It gives details about implementation of the cease-fire, the limits, to which the Azeri and RMK [Republic of Mountainous Karabakh] troops must be withdrawn, defines the order of stationing the observers, which will include military representatives of Russia, Armenia, Mountainous Karabakh, Azerbaijan, as well as CIS and CSCE member countries. "Armenia pays great importance to the CSCE participation in the observers mission and believes, this will significantly strengthen stability of the cease-fire," said Levon Zurabyan. Moscow meeting prepared a new agreement, called "An agreement on stopping the military conflict and eliminating its consequences," which will be signed on a highest level. The agreement stipulates withdrawal of armed forces from occupied territories, lifting the blockade and return of refugees. According to Zurabyan, this agreement will open the way for discussing the RMK status within the CSCE framework.
