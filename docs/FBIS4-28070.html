BFN [Text] On 6 June, the government decided on a package of measures to deal with the issue of North Korea's suspected development of nuclear weapons. Under the package of measures, 1) Japan will strongly urge North Korea to cooperate fully with nuclear inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) to clear up suspicions about its development of nuclear weapons; 2) Japan will study steps it must take in response to economic sanctions if the UN Security Council votes to impose them, while watching how the UN Security Council handles the DPRK nuclear issue; 3) If the UN Security Council decides to take appropriate measures including economic sanctions to force North Korea to change its behavior, Japan would support the measures and take the maximum steps possible within the framework of the Constitution; and 4) As a member of the international community, Japan will make maximum diplomatic efforts to urge China to exert influence on the DPRK while maintaining international cooperation. Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa will visit South Korea on 11 June to affirm with his South Korean counterpart that both Japan and South Korea will work together cooperatively to settle the DPRK nuclear issue. His visit to China on 12 June is being coordinated by the two countries, and he intends to seek the Chinese Government's understanding for the four-point measures if his visit to that country is realized.
