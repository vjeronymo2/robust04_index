Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Brussels, March 24 (AFP) -- The former Yugoslav republic of Slovenia will sign up to the Partnership for Peace programme of military cooperation with the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation Wednesday [30 March], a NATO official said Thursday. Slovenia will become the first part of the former Yugoslavia to sign up to the NATO programme. Bosnia-Hercegovina and Croatia have also expressed interest in joining the Partnership, although no decision has been taken on it, the official said. The Partnership for Peace programme allows for joint manoeuvres, military planning and peacekeeping operations with the existing NATO members. NATO has already decided that four other countries can join the programme if they wish -- Austria, Finland, Sweden and Switzerland. Russia is expected to sign up to the programme before the end of April. Georgia signed Wednesday, joining Albania, Bulgaria, the Czech Republic, Estonia, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Moldova, Poland, Romania, Slovakia and Ukraine. Among the former Soviet republics, only Tajikistan has said it is not interested in joining the programme, the official added.
