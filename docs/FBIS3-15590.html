Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] The series of official contacts of Romanian President Ion Iliescu occasioned by his state visit to Lithuania was inaugurated today at the headquarters of the parliament [in Vilnius], with Iliescu's meeting with Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas. The meeting was followed by talks among the Romanian and Lithuanian delegations, as well as between President Ion Iliescu and the speaker of the Lithuanian Parliament. In a statement made following the talks, Romania's president assessed that the visit is taking place in the best atmosphere and that during all the talks held so far the wish of the two countries has been expressed to further bilateral relations. For his part, Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas said that the meeting had been cordial and positive and expressed his belief that the current visit will result in important changes in the furthering of Romanian-Lithuanian relations. Among the issues discussed during today's meeting the Lithuanian president mentioned relations with neighboring countries, the internal political situation in each of the countries, the progress achieved in their economic reforms, and the furthering of economic and commercial ties between Romania and Lithuania. According to our reporter, an official dinner hosted by President Algirdas Brazauskas is now taking place. Today the ministers of foreign affairs of the two countries also held talks, after which [Romanian Foreign Affairs Minister] Teodor Melescanu told our reporters that during the talks both parties displayed interest with respect to the real security and cooperation perspectives in our area, more precisely the influence exerted upon security and stability by the evolutions in the Russian Federation and the entire region of the CIS. Hence Romania and Lithuania had a common interest in participating in the Partnership for Peace and in associating themselves with the European Union and other European structures, Melescanu stated.
