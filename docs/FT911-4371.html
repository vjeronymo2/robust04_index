910419 FT 19 APR 91 / World News in Brief: Chernobyl warning Dr Vladimir Chernousenko, scientific director of the 20-mile exclusion zone around Chernobyl, claimed up to 10,000 people died following the 1986 explosion - compared with the official Soviet figure of 31. Meanwhile the Atomic Energy Authority said up to 40,000 people worldwide may die from cancer over the next 70 years because of the disaster.
