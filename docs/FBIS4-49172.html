BFN [Text] Beijing, May 4 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-President Rong Yiren expressed the hope here today that Sino-U.S. ties would be further improved and developed. He told Frank Zarb, vice-chairman and executive officer of the Travelers Company of the United States, during their meeting that since the two countries play important roles in the world, the improvement and development of their bilateral ties would not only serve the interests of the two peoples, but also benefit peace and development in the world. He hoped that the existing differences should not damage normal political and economic exchanges between the two countries, and that the differences could be solved through discussions and exchanges. He said that the meeting between Chinese President Jiang Zemin and U.S. President Clinton in Seattle last year was beneficial to the improvement of bilateral relations, and he hoped that the favorable tendency of development could be maintained. Zarb agreed with Rong's views and said that in the United States many people now enthusiastically hope that U.S.-China relations will improve. Zarb and his party are here as guests of the China International Trust and Investment Company. The Travelers Company is one of the largest insurance companies in the United States, with total assets valued at over 101.3 billion U.S. dollars, and the turnover in 1993 stood at 6.8 billion U.S. dollars.
