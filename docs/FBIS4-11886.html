BFN [Text] A meeting among brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council; His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id of Oman; brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, vice chairman of the Presidential Council; and His Highness Tuwayni Bin- Shihab, the personal representative of His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin- Sa'id, was held at 2030 yesterday at al-Hisn Palace in Salalah in the Sultanate of Oman. During the meeting, the conferees discussed brotherly relations between the two fraternal countries, Yemen and Oman. The conferees also discussed efforts under way to contain the political crisis and enhance unity and stability in Yemen. An official round of Yemeni-Omani talks was held under brother Lt. Gen. 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id of Oman. The session was attended by members of the delegations of the two sides. Following the joint session, brother Lt. Gen. 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id held a closed session. The Yemeni News Agency, SABA', has learned that talks addressed the warm brotherly relations between the two fraternal countries and ways of enhancing them, as well as developments in Yemen in light of the efforts taking place to contain the political crisis, reactivate constitutional agencies, and implement the accord document. The purpose of this is to safeguard unity, democracy, and stability in Yemen and enhance security and stability in the region. In addition, several regional, Arab, and world issues and developments were discussed, primarily issues of interest to the two fraternal countries. His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id of Oman received in Salalah yesterday evening brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, vice chairman of the Presidential Council and secretary general of the Yemeni Socialist Party. Brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid arrived in the Sultanate of Oman yesterday from the United Arab Emirates. He was received at Salalah airport by His Highness Tuwayni Bin-Shihab Al Sa'id, the personal representative of His Majesty Sultan Qabus Bin-Sa'id, and several other Omani officials.
