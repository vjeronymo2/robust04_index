921104 FT 04 NOV 92 / Bottomley saves consultant's job A HOSPITAL consultant who claims she was made redundant after criticising patient care should continue in her job, Mrs Virginia Bottomley, the health secretary, ruled yesterday. Dr Helen Zeitlin, former consultant haematologist at a West Midlands hospital, was told last year by health officals she would no longer be needed because of a reduced workload. She claimed the real reason was that she criticised nursing shortages at the Alexandra Hospital in Redditch, Birmingham.
