BFN [Text] Moscow/Kiev 22 May TASS -- The situation on the Crimean Peninsula remains rather tense. According to a spokesman for the Crimean president, 29 Ukrainian armoured personnel vehicles entered the regional capital Simferopol on Sunday [22 May] night. With this in view, the Crimean Interior Ministry reinforced its units securing key government buildings, including the main broadcasting centre and parliament headquarters, the spokesman reported. According to reliable military sources, the armoured vehicles brought by railway are now deployed at the station of Simferopol. The Black Sea Fleet's combat units are staying at their usual locations, a spokesman for the fleet's headquarters told TASS. Because of the volatile situation in the Crimea, the fleet reinforced the guard of its installations, but there are no manoeuvres or redeployment. Meanwhile, in an interview with national television on Sunday night, Ukraine's First Deputy Interior Minister Valentin Nedrigailo denied reports about sending Ukrainian armoured personnel vehicles to Simferopol. According to him, the situation in the Crimea and Ukraine is quite calm.
