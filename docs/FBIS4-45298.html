BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on narcotics developments in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific monitored by Bangkok Bureau between 23 and 29 May 1994. Source information is given in parentheses after each item.

SOUTH ASIA
India
Officials Seize Heroin in Lucknow Raid
Officials of the Central Bureau of Narcotics seized 775 grams of heroin in a raid conducted on the Aliganj, Lucknow residence of two "notorious smugglers" identified as Ms. Afsha and Ms. Hazrabegum. (Delhi THE HINDUSTAN TIMES in English 21 May 94 p 9)
Pakistan
Nine Arrested in Lahore With Heroin, Opium
Nine people have been arrested by the Antinarcotics Task Force in Lahore after they were found with 45 kg of heroin, 100 liters of acetic anhydride, and 108 kg of opium. Eight of the suspects were identified as Ghafoor Niazi, Ijaz Ahmed Khalid, Shoukat Ali, Altaf, Rafique, Jamshed, Arshad, and Azhar Butt. (Karachi DAWN in English 21 May 94 p 6)
Frontier Corps Seizes Opium Bound for Iran
The Frontier Corps recovered 80 kg of fine opium in the Mand area as it was being smuggled to Iran by camel. There were no arrests. (Islamabad THE NATION in English 26 May 94 p 12)
SOUTHEAST ASIA
Burma
Authorities Kill Two, Arrest Six, Seize 23 Kg of Heroin
Acting on a tip, members of the Defense Services Intelligence Unit and the antiterrorist group of Mong Ko-Phaungsai Region stopped a group of people near Namtaung Village, Mu-se Township on 8 May. One of the suspects threw a grenade; the authorities returned small arms fire. Yi Sein, 20, and an unidentified suspect were killed in the exchange. Six others were seized along with 90 packets of heroin weighing 23.08 kg: Kyan Pi San, 18; Mo Tae Chan, 20; Kyaw Lwin, alias Wan Min Khwan, 20; Kyin San, 25; Mar Dut, 28; and Aik Gnaw, 26. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 14 May 94 p 7)
Authorities Seize Heroin, Arrest Three in Shwepyithar
Members of the Rangoon special antidrug squad and Shwepyithar police searched the house of U San Myint, 40, and Daw Cho Cho, 32, in Zigon Ward, Shwepyithar on 6 May. They found 27.155 grams of heroin in plastic containers hidden under a pile of humus in a hut. The authorities also arrested Daw Cho Cho's brother Kyi Win, 29. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 19 May 94 p 7)
Mandalay Railway Police Seize Opium
On 20 April, the Mandalay Railway Police seized 6.367 kg of opium from Myint Kyaw, 25, a passenger on the Mandalay-Myitkyina train. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 19 May 94 p 7)
Opium Seized in Mogaung Township; Two Arrested
Acting on a tip, a combined group from the Defense Services Intelligence Unit, Namti People's Police Force, and the ward law and order restoration council searched the house of Ma N Khun Taung, 35, in Namti Village, Mogaung Township on 3 May. They found 3.918 kg of opium. They also arrested Ma Rit Kaw Jah, 32, the owner of the drugs. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 24 May 94 p 7)
Laos
Provincial Court Imprisons Woman on Opium Charge
On 25 May, the Sayaboury Province People's Court sentenced Ms. Teng, 37, of Nakha village, Boten District to six months and 15 days in jail and fined her 70,000 kip. She was charged with possession of 43 kg of opium. The court proceedings, interrogation, and police investigation showed that the accused did not intend to violate state laws. The opium was subsequently destroyed by the authorities. (Vientiane Vitthayou Hengsat Radio Network in Lao 0000 GMT 27 May 94)
Philippines
Narcotics Agents Arrest 25, Seize Methamphetamine
Hydrochloride Narcotics Command agents have confiscated 20.484 kg of methamphetamine hydrochloride worth 51 million pesos from 25 drug dealers during a week-long operation. Among those arrested were two members of the Police Aviation Security Command assigned to Ninoy Aquino International Airport, a police officer, the son of a police officer, and two overseas Filipinos. (Quezon City GMA-7 Radio- Television Arts Network in Tagalog 0930 GMT 23 May 94)
Narcotics Regional Command Destroys Marijuana in Sulu
Elements of the 9th Narcotics Regional Command destroyed some 65,000 marijuana plants, 15,000 marijuana seedlings, and 20 kg of marijuana seeds following a raid on a four-hectare marijuana plantation in the mountains of Maimbung, Sulu. Maimbung is one of the many areas in Sulu considered to be a Moro National Liberation Front stronghold. (Manila PHILIPPINE DAILY INQUIRER in English 23 May 94 p 15)
Thailand
Two Arrested in Chiang Rai With 4.5 Kg of Heroin
Narcotics officers arrested Sawang Wongyai, 36, and Santi Kattiyasarn, 34, at the Chiang Rai bus terminal on 19 May. They had 12 packs of heroin weighing 4.5 kg hidden inside of a suitcase. (Bangkok BANGKOK POST in English 24 May 94 p 6)
