BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 4 May -- The Russian Federation's Foreign Ministry expressed its willingness to cooperate with the new democratic South Africa. A statement issued by the foreign policy department voiced the hope that the good relations that have taken shape between the two countries and their peoples "will, in the period that lies ahead, be further developed in the interests of world peace and prosperity." In the opinion of the Russian Foreign Ministry, history's first general election in the South Africa is an event "that has fundamentally altered the course of historical development of that country and of the region as a whole." "The international community, which has for decades sought persistently to bring about a restoration of racial and social justice in South Africa, notes today that that goal has been attained. For its part, Russia, which has invariably backed all constructive efforts by the government, parties, and public organizations in South Africa along the road of democratic transformation, shares with everyone a sense of profound satisfaction with the results of those efforts," the Foreign Ministry statement stresses.
