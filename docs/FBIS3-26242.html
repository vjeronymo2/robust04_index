Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "News" program] [Text] As ROC [Republic of China] Premier Lien Chan returned to Taiwan on Tuesday from his second overseas trip in a month, President Li Teng-hui's private (?visit) to Indonesia was still in limbo and Foreign Minister Frederick Chien was reported of planning to resign. Chien, currently in Boston, was reported as wanting to resign due to frustration of the impasse to broaden diplomatic relations and internal opposition to diplomatic policies from the Legislature, local papers reported. Presidential aide said Li has made no concrete plans to go abroad, denying a local newspaper report that the president will leave for Bali on February 9 and will meet with Indonesian President Suharto. Esther Kuo, a presidential spokeswoman, said that once the president's overseas travel plan matures, the office will announce that at the proper time. Kuo urged the media not to play up Li's possible trip, saying the (?extension) of the itinerary will create unnecessary trouble. She said that the pressure from Peking was the apparent obstacle, saying Li's travel plan over the past two years were aborted due to the similar obstacle.
