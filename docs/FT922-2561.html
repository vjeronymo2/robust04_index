920616 FT 16 JUN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Kinnock quits contest for European job MR NEIL KINNOCK, the Labour leader, last night withdrew from the contest for the presidency of the European Communities' Confederation of Socialist Parties because of a possible conflict with his party's stance on the Maastricht treaty. He withdrew after consultations in Lisbon with other socialists had pointed to difficulties he would face if the confederation backed the treaty but Labour chose to vote against it in the Commons. The decision is another personal setback for Mr Kinnock, who is soon to stand down as Labour leader. He is looking for a role in Labour politics after his general election defeat, but felt obliged to withdraw from the European confederation election even though there were no other candidates for the post. He urged postponing the contest until the autumn. Mr Kinnock said that, following the Danish referendum's rejection of the Maastricht treaty, it was 'reasonable' for the Labour party to try to change the treaty. 'This might involve the Labour party in delaying or opposing the Tory version of the Maastricht Treaty,' he said in Lisbon. 'The possibility that Labour might oppose the Tory version of the treaty could mean an inconsistency between my duties as president of the confederation and my duties as leader of the Labour party and subsequently as a Labour MP.'
