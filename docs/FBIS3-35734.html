Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chisinau, BASAPRESS, 28/1/1994 -- The extended session of the Moldovan Prosecutor's Office College took place today, with the participation of President Mircea Snegur and Speaker Petru Lucinschi. The report of the Prosecutor's Office activity in 1993 was read at the session, states the Presidential Press Service. Snegur declared that Moldova enjoys the support of international organizations and has the chance to successfully perform political reforms. However, concerning the domestic affairs, "difficulties are considerable and continue to aggravate." The economic crisis, the population's bad material condition, the new bureaucracy that blocks the reforms, "on the background of obvious cases of corruption of administrative apparatus and its junctions, sometimes, with Mafia structures, endangers the republic's independence and sovereignty themselves," said the president. Snegur asserted that law infringements are committed in privatization. "A massive control last year proved that from 600 enterprises, illicit privatization of fixed funds was detected in 200." Speaking about economic violations that brought big prejudices to the republic, Mircea Snegur mentioned the policy of unfounded credits, illegal issue of licenses for export of material resources, law violations while constituting the initial capital of non-state economic structures, created partially on state property, improper use of buildings, illicit distribution of lodging. Snegur mentioned among measures to improve the situation the discussion by the future Parliament of legislative acts, the display of the activity of justice bodies, including the Prosecutor's Office, the enhancement of methods of analyzing and quickly informing the Parliament and republic's leadership, states the communique.
