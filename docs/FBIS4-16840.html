BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Pavel Bogdanov] [Text] Moscow April 20 TASS -- During Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet's planned visit to Moscow in June, most probably a package of important intergovernmental agreements will be signed. The documents will regulate economic cooperation and political relations between the two countries, a high-ranking source in the Russian Foreign Ministry told ITAR-TASS. The head of the Vietnamese Government will pay his first visit to Moscow after the disintegration of the Soviet Union. It is considered an important landmark in bilateral relations in the Russian Foreign Ministry. According to the Foreign Ministry source, during talks with the Vietnamese prime minister discussions will be held on the prospects of broadening Russia's interaction with Vietnam, a traditional ally of Moscow in the Asia-Pacific region. The diplomat emphasised that the two countries are "successfully coordinating their political steps in the Asia-Pacific region. Useful exchange of views had taken place during meetings between heads of foreign affairs department of the two countries in lobbies of two ASEAN (Association of South East Asian Nations) summits. On a majority of issues positions of the two states coincide or are close, the diplomat added.
