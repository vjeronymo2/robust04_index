BFN [Text] On 8 June, a top official of the Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI] explained a view that in an attempt to end negotiations in priority areas prior to the Japan-U.S. comprehensive economic talks, it is likely that ministers, including Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa and MITI Minister Eijiro Hata, will visit the United States and hold ministerial meetings with U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor and other secretaries. Regarding goals of the negotiations, the same senior official said that "details of a report, which will be submitted to the Hata-Clinton talks, will be worked out in deputy ministerial talks before the [Naples] summit." In this way, he explained the idea of producing some results before the summit in the four priority areas -- government procurement, auto and auto parts, insurance, and export promotion and competitive power.
