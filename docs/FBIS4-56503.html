BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] Today, the Mexican president said his government is duty-bound to guarantee that the law prevails. Democracy and law go together and cannot be separated, President Carlos Salinas de Gortari said at Los Pinos presidential residence when he received the 1994 Norma Corona Sapiens Award, granted by the Association of Litigant Lawyers of Mexico for the Defense of Human Rights. Salinas was decorated with the national award named after Norma Corona Sapiens, a lawyer from Sinaloa who defended human rights and who was murdered during her struggle against drug traffickers associated with policemen. As a result of her murder, President Salinas created the National Human Rights Commission, which he later incorporated into the Constitution. [Begin Salinas recording] My government has assigned top priority to the defense of human rights under all circumstances and with all due authority. I have pledged to guarantee rule of law. [end recording] Even though he admitted there is much more to be done, the president said Mexico's legal system is at the vanguard of this century. [Begin Salinas recording] Fighting against criminal bands, particularly those of drug traffickers, is indispensable for guaranteeing the civilized coexistence of society. [end recording] Maximo Carvajal, dean of the School of Law of the Autonomous University of Mexico, said the award was given to President Salinas for his work in defending human rights in Mexico.
