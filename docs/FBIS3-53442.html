Language: Bengali Article Type:BFN [Text] The two-day Bangladesh-India border talks have ended in New Delhi with both sides agreeing to take steps to check border crime and smuggling. Major General Mohammad Anwar Hussain, director general of the Bangladesh Rifles, and Prakash Singh, director general of the Border Security Force of India, represented their respective delegations at the talks. The two sides also agreed to check drug trafficking across the border. On the corridor issue, both Bangladesh and India agreed to adhere to the 1974 agreement and the 1975 border guideline. Maj. Gen. Hussain also called on Indian Home Minister S.B. Chavan and the home secretary in New Delhi yesterday.
