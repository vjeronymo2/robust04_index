BFN [Unattributed report under the "Here Is the News" rubric: "Miners Striking Again"] [Text] Miners in the Baykal and Transbaykal areas stopped mining and dispatching fuel to consumers yesterday. With the exception of workers at two opencut mines, the 20,000-strong collective of the "Vostsibugol" joint-stock company is insisting on immediate payment of debts which now amount to approximately 170 billion rubles [R]. The principal debtors are power industry workers, specifically in Khabarovsk and Maritime Krays. However, the list of demands put forward by the miners is topped by an appeal to the Russian Federation Government, which they say should allocate funds to pay off debts of R69 billion run up by budget-financed and other organizations In accordance with a statement made by Vladimir Solomin, chairman of the joint-stock company strike committee, the strike action is scheduled to last for three days and also has a political angle: The East Siberian miners are demanding the resignation of the government and early presidential elections.
