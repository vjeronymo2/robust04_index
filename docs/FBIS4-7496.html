BFN [Text] Skopje, 31 March (TANJUG) -- General John Shalikashvili, chairman of the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff, said at a news conference in Skopje today that U.S. President Bill Clinton was ready to send fresh military units to The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM] as part of the UN mission. Shalikashvili said this after talks with Macedonian President Kiro Gligorov and Defense Minister Vlado Popovski. Popovski said that they did not discuss the issue of establishing an American military base on the Krivolak training grounds, adding that the issue "is not on the agenda now." Gen. Shalikashvili today visited American soldiers deployed in FYROM.
