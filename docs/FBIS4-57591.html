BFN [Text] At the conclusion of the vote count, the Central Electoral Commission of the Dominican Republic declared President Joaquin Balaguer the winner of the elections held on Monday [16 May.] In the final provisional bulletin, Balaguer maintained a one- point margin over Jose Francisco Pena Gomez, who demanded the annulment of the elections due to reported frauds. Today, THE NEW YORK TIMES commented that the international community should not recognize President Balaguer's victory if it is the result of electoral fraud, as credible sources have indicated. THE NEW YORK TIMES pointed out that Balaguer was also accused of committing fraud in 1990's general elections, which confirmed him as president of that nation for another term.
