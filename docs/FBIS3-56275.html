Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow January 14 TASS--President Bill Clinton early on Friday morning laid a wreath at the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier at the Kremlin wall as a token of the American people's respect for their World War Two allies. "Your Name is Unknown, Your Feat is Immortal" are the words inscribed on the red-and-black granite of the tomb. To the strains of sad music the president laid the wreath at the tomb and stood at attention as the ban played the national anthems of the United States and Russia. The U.S. President was accompanied by Russian Minister of Foreign Affairs Andrey Kozyrev, Russian Ambassador to the United States Vladimir Lukin and members of the Russian and American delegations.
