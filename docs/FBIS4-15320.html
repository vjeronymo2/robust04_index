BFN [Text] Tbilisi, 4 Apr -- The IBERIA agency reports that at about 1300 hours today Davit Zeikidze voluntarily left the building of the Tbilisi police directorate, while Demur Mikadze, deputy minister of internal affairs of the Republic of Georgia, officially began to perform the duties of acting chief of the police directorate. He has already met police directorate employees. [In an earlier report, Tbilisi IBERIA in Georgian at 1020 GMT on 4 April carried the following: "D. Zeikidze issued an ultimatum demanding that he be given permission to deliver a statement on television, that criminal proceedings against him be suspended, and that the charges of an attempted coup d'etat be withdrawn. The ultimatum has partly been met. Zeikidze's request to see his closest friend, the speaker of parliament, has also been honored."]
