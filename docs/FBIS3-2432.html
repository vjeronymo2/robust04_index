Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 9 (XINHUA) -- A preparatory meeting for the Second Session of the Eighth National People's Congress (NPC) elected here today a presidium consisting of 162 people and a secretary-general. Compared with the presidium of the first session last year, there are only minor changes in the makeup of the just-elected presidium. It consists of representatives of six social circles, including 27 party and state leaders. They include Jiang Zemin, Chinese president and general secretary of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China (CPC); Qiao Shi, chairman of the NPC Standing Committee; Li Ruihuan, chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference; Liu Huaqing, vice-chairman of the Central Military Commission; and Hu Jintao, member of the Standing Committee of the Political Bureau of the CPC Central Committee. Retired Chinese leader Deng Xiaoping has also been elected member of the presidium. In accordance with convention, members of the State Council, president of the Supreme People's Court and procurator-general of the Supreme People's Procuratorate are excluded from the presidium from the second session of each national people's congress. Therefore, Premier Li Peng, Vice Premier Zhu Rongji and other leaders of the State Council, who were members of the presidium of the first session, do not sit on the current presidium. Tian Jiyun, vice-chairman of the NPC Standing Committee, doubles as secretary-general. The presidium includes leaders of democratic parties, heads of All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, persons without party affiliation, leaders of people's organizations, representatives of special economic zones, Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan, representatives of ethnic minorities and local leaders.
