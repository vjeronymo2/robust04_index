Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report by Mariya Starozhitskaya of UNIAN under "From Our Correspondents and News Agencies" rubric: "Ukrainian Political Parties Commence Election Statements on Television and Radio"] [Text] Kiev -- As of 12 January each of the 23 parties registered with the Central Electoral Commission is being granted 10 minutes of airtime on weekdays. It has been decided to refrain from casting lots: The order of the statements has been decided by the order of registration. The second stage of the campaign will begin 1 February -- hour-long discussions between blocs or parties. Only commercial television companies will be able to grant airtime to individual aspirants: according to a minimum tariff within the limits of the candidate's fund. This fund stands at 10 times the minimum wage or 6 million karbovanets, while one minute of airtime on a nonstate channel, according to the lowest estimates, will cost approximately 2.5 million.
