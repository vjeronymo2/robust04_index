BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Mikhail Shevtsov and Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow, 6 May -- Peacekeeping functions in Georgia could be successfully carried out, alongside the Russians, by units and subunits of the armed forces of the CIS states, and especially the participants in the collective security treaty. This was stated at a news conference today by Russian Defense Minister Army General Pavel Grachev. Russia, the minister noted, would be grateful to the United Nations if, during the fulfillment of peacekeeping functions in the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict, representatives of this international organizations could participate as observers. The defense minister also said that during a conversation with UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali during his recent visit to Moscow, he obtained agreement in principle for Russian participation in peacekeeping activities in Georgia.
