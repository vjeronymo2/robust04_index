Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "United Nations: Andreatta on Peacekeeping Operations"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, March 22 -- The United Nations has been aiming too high with its peacemaking and peacekeeping operations which in 1993 ended up with partial successes and some failures, said Foreign Minister Beniamino Andreatta on Tuesday [22 March]. The United Nations "probably expected too much. It has tried to carry out operations without sufficient experience," Andreatta told a meeting of representatives from religious news agencies. "The 1993 results, the result of (UN Secretary General Butrus) Butrus-Ghali's pride in organising peace forces to rebuild public well-being and order, to rebuild countries, has ended in very partial successes. And in some cases in failure," the foreign minister was quoted as saying in a text released after his appearance. Andreatta accused the United Nations of attempting peacekeeping operations "without sufficient experience; it has sent troops with somewhat precarious chiefs of staff. But on the whole it seems to me that it meant well." As Italian troops concluded their pull-out from Somalia, which they are leaving with all the other Western contingents there, Andreatta painted a bleak picture for that East African nation, saying that without an organised state it is difficult to imagine justice, development or respect for human life. "You cannot expect to transfer the democratic state model of Western industrial powers with no perception of anthropological complications, as Americans of good will but little experience often do," said Andreatta. "This is the problem that the experiences of the Butrus-Ghali secretariat will leave to the United Nations," the foreign minister said. Despite setbacks for UN operations, he concluded, failure to intervene in crisis points would not put the situation to right. "It would seem to me to be a mistake," he concluded, "on the basis of these initial experiences, to suggest that the world community should not take an interest in those parts of the world where all legal power has disappeared, and where anarchy reigns supreme."
