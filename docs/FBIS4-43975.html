BFN [Text] Government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis announced today that Joe Clark, the UN secretary general's special representative on Cyprus, will arrive on the island at 1730 this afternoon and at 2000 will be received by President Glavkos Kliridhis. The spokesman also said that UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali had a telephone conversation with President Kliridhis last night and announced his intention to send Clark to Cyprus. Kliridhis, the spokesman added, asked Butrus-Ghali whether Clark would be carrying any letter. Butrus-Ghali said Clark would not. The government spokesman added that tonight's discussion at the presidential palace will concern the UN secretary general's report, which was submitted to the Security Council on 30 May, and particular emphasis will be placed upon Butrus-Ghali's proposals. The spokesman repeated that the Greek Cypriot side's position remains clear: Discussion of the confidence-building measures has ended and we remain committed to the 21 March document. Answering a question, the government spokesman stressed that our side will not yield to pressure from the United States and Britain. Finally, the spokesman said that President Kliridhis will leave for Corfu tomorrow morning on board a private plane instead of this afternoon as scheduled.
