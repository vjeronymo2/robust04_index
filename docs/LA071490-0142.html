July 14, 1990, Saturday, Orange County Edition

BASTILLE DAY: TIME TO STORM THOSE FRENCH EATERIES;

RESTAURANTS: CAN-CAN, MUSIC AND THAT WORLD-FAMOUS CUISINE WILL BE FEATURED TODAY THROUGHOUT ORANGE COUNTY AS THE SPIRIT OF LIBERTY, FRATERNITY AND GASTRONOMY OVERTAKE THE POPULACE.

Special menus, accordions, can-can dancers -- you can count on French restaurateurs to pull out all the stops for Bastille Day today. Here are a few options for your celebration of the 1789 attack and capture of the French prison fortress.

Bastille Day commemorates the triumph of the populace, the bourgeois, according to Marica Hefti of Le Medi in Newport Beach. So she and her chef-husband Walter Ruttiman will serve from big pots of moules mariniere, the traditional mussels dish, on the restaurant's patio at bourgeois prices -- from $1.75. Also available outdoors: seafood salad and strawberries with black pepper. Inside, diners can sit down to a dinner of duck Bigarade (with orange), French onion soup and chocolate mousse, all for $14.95. The same menu will be served at lunchtime, from 11:30 to 3 p.m.; and dinner, from 6 until "whatever," according to Marica. Reservations: (714) 675-4904.

Can-can dancers liven the festivities at several restaurants, including La Vie en Rose in Brea, which will also present a magician. A special multi-course dinner, priced at $50, will be served from 6 to 10 p.m. Reservations: (714) 529-8333.

The celebration began Friday at the Bouzy Rouge in Newport Beach with a French fashion show. Tonight's the night for the can-can dancers, an accordionist, door prize drawings and the opening of a methuselum (that's eight bottles' worth) of champagne. Service from the Parisian bistro menu begins at 6 p.m. Reservations: (714) 675-3440.

At Chanteclair in Irvine, chef Byron Gemmell, will present a nine-course menu de degustation complemented by four wines. It's $85 per person plus tax and gratuity. Reservations for tonight or Saturday: (714) 752-8001.

Accordionists set the mood for celebrating a la Francaise at several other restaurants, including Pascal in Newport Beach, during its festivities tonight. The Provencale country dinner includes four courses, all seasoned with Provencale herbs, of course, with a choice of such entrees as rabbit, duck and grilled fish. There will be wine on the tables, and the tab is $39 per person, exclusive of tax and tip. Reservations: (714) 752-0107.

An accordion player will also entertain at Cafe Francais in Orange. Owner Jacques Duc also promises a six-course dinner, highlighting feuillete of scallops with caramelized onions, confit of duck with cloves and fresh rosemary. The tab is $36.50. Reservations: (714) 998-6051.

In Huntington Beach, at his new Voila, the very French Yves Briee will add strictly French specials to his menu and pour a complimentary glass of champagne for each diner. Incidentally, the restaurant is now open for dinner only. Reservations: (714) 841-1166.

LaFayette in Buena Park celebrates with a five-course Bastille dinner. There will be appetizer and soup-salad choices; salmon in lobster sauce; a selection of meat courses and imported cheese and pastries. It's $40 a person. Reservations: (714) 537-5011.
