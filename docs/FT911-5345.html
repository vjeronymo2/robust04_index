910415 FT 15 APR 91 / UK News (Employment): Study shows mental health problems of City dealers CITY DEALERS working for US-owned financial institutions are likely to suffer from poorer mental health and find their jobs less satisfying than counterparts in more conservative UK companies, psychologists have found. Results of the survey, carried out in 12 institutions with 200 dealers, were presented to the annual conference of the British Psychological Society by Dr Howard Kahn of Heriot-Watt University. Female dealers were better able to cope with work-related stress, although they experienced less job satisfaction. In spite of better academic qualifications, women reported general discrimination and fewer promotion opportunities. More than 40 per cent of dealers questioned in an initial survey said they drank heavily, compared with 8 per cent of other professionals. Dr Kahn said: 'Though it appears that dealers are able to cope with the long hours and pressures associated with their work, their high intake of alcohol . . . the pattern of relaxation at and after work with colleagues, and the lack of support outside work gives rise to concern.'
