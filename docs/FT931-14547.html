930118 FT 18 JAN 93 / Construction Contracts: Pounds 19m workload for Ernest Ireland Nearly Pounds 19m worth of new work has been won in the south, west and south Wales by ERNEST IRELAND CONSTRUCTION of Bath, part of John Mowlem Construction. The largest contract, valued at Pounds 5.7m, is a 38,000 sq ft superstore for a major multiple retailer at Litchard Park, near Bridgend in south Wales. The store will be of steel framed construction with rubble-stone faced blockwork walls and a hung tile mansard roof. There will be a six-pump petrol station and parking for 470 cars. At Southampton Retail Park work is under way on a Pounds 2.6m project to build two retail warehouse units totalling 100,000 sq ft for City Estates, Southampton. B&Q will be taking the larger 75,000 sq ft unit which will become its flagship 'depot' style superstore. The steel-framed units have a striking high technology appearance combining brightly coloured external bracing, curtain waIling and flashings with silver composite wall cladding and contrasting low-level brickwork At Avon Rubber, Melksham, Wiltshire the company has been awarded a Pounds 1.4m contract to construct a plant to mix and blend rubber. At Brockworth in Gloucestershire, Ernest Ireland has won contracts totalling Pounds 2.2m for a textile development facility and customer centre for ICI Fibres and in Portsmouth Ernest Ireland is converting a building to form a bingo and social club under a Pounds 947,000 contract for Top Rank. Other awards include a weir at Tewksbury (Pounds 681,000) and tidal defence earthworks for a project in Gloucestershire (Pounds 620,000).
