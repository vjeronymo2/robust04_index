930203 FT 03 FEB 93 / International Company News: Reebok profits unchanged in fourth quarter REEBOK International, the US sports shoe maker, yesterday announced fourth-quarter profits from continuing operations of Dollars 49.8m, unchanged from a year earlier. However, the company took a previously-announced restructuring charge of Dollars 135m in the final quarter which, along with a one-off gain of Dollars 18m from the sale of stock in CML Group, left Reebok with a net loss of Dollars 67.6m for the last three months of 1992. For 1992, earnings before charges and special gains totalled Dollars 232.3m, down slightly from the Dollars 234.7m earned in 1991. Mr Paul Fireman, chairman and chief executive of Reebok, said he was pleased with the company's overall performance in the fourth quarter, citing increased sales of its Reebok brand both in the US and abroad, of its sports apparel, and of its Rockport products. However, sales of the Avia brand, fell 21 per cent in the quarter.
