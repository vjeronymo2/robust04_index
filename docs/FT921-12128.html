920128 FT 28 JAN 92 / International Company News: Total sees 41% climb in net earnings TOTAL, the French state-controlled oil group, yesterday estimated net earnings rose by 41 per cent last year to FFr5.8bn (Dollars 1.07bn). The increase, from FFr4.1bn in 1990, represents a slowdown on the growth published in the previous year. However, Total has since changed its accounting methods to reduce the wild swings in its results that used to come from oil price changes. It has switched from accounting for stocks on a first in, first out basis to accounting for oil stocks at replacement cost. Last year's growth reflects an upturn in activity and productivity gains across the group, said Total. Margins were lifted by the Gulf war, but then shrank with the subsequent fall in oil prices and the dollar's value.
