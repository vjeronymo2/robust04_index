930205 FT 05 FEB 93 / World News in Brief: Rebel Tories may settle for saving only 12 pits The government may be able to secure backbench support for an energy policy which saves well under half the threatened 31 coal pits. Telephone interviews with the most likely Tory backbench rebels indicated that a plan which spared between 12 and 14 pits would be likely to keep the government out of danger. But a hardline group remains which insists that up to 20 must be kept. Rebels want more saved, Page 6
