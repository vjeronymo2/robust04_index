BFN [Text] Cairo, 21 Jun (MENA) -- The Sudanese Ummah Party has condemned the regime's arrest of its leader al-Sadiq al-Mahdi and two of its prominent members, 'Abd-al-Rahman Farah 'Abd-al-Rahman and Professor Hammad Baqadi. In a statement today, the Ummah Party described the arrest as a "drama" to divert people's attention from the numerous internal problems, particularly the aggravating living conditions. The party said the arrest was part of the crackdown within the regime amid the extensive corruption ailing the state. The party held the Sudanese authorities and the National Islamic Front responsible for the safety of the detainees and appealed to the international community and human rights organizations to put pressure on the regime to safeguard the detainees' lives.
