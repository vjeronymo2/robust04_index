Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Turkish agents could be responsible for the bomb explosion last night at a mosque near Faneromeni Church in Nicosia. This is the conclusion reached by the investigative authorities. Correspondent Yeoryios Platonos has the following report: [Begin Platonos recording] The bomb that exploded last night on Lefkonos Street was made with a small quantity of high density explosive material and was placed on the outside wall of the mosque. The homemade device exploded at 2300. The Criminal Investigation Department [TAE] is evaluating information that Turkish agents are responsible for the explosion. It is believed that the Turks possibly came from the occupied areas to cause tension in view of the Cyprus issue talks. TAE is also studying the relation of this incident to the arrival of the Greek National Defense Minister Yerasimos Arsenis. Police last night visited and searched other mosques as a precautionary measure. A UN officer visited the area after the explosion, possibly after notification by the Turks. The explosion caused the shattering of windows of a nearby school and shops. [end recording]
