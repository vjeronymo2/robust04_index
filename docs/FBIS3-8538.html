Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 10 Mar--On 10 March, Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak received Marshal Jacques Lanxade, chief of staff of the French Army. The government press office reported that Alain Bry, French ambassador to Poland, and General Tadeusz Wilecki, chief of the general staff of the Polish Army, were also present. Poland and France signed an agreement on defense cooperation. Marshall Lanxade and Gen. Wilecki briefed the prime minister on the scope of the cooperation and stressed the importance of individual contacts between officers of both armies. Polish and French soldiers will have an opportunity to meet in Normandy in June during the 50th anniversary of the D-day landings on the continent. The government press office communique says that the marshal assured the prime minister that France would support Poland's aspiration for full integration with the Western security system.
