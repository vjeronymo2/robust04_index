940504 FT 04 MAY 94 / Boarding schools in funding talks Boarding schools, which suffered a 5.2 per cent drop in boarding-pupil numbers last year, are discussing possible extra state funds with education ministers. Mr Ian Small, chairman of the Boarding Schools Association, which represents both independent and state boarding schools, and headmaster of Bootham School in York, said: 'At the moment there are a lot of financial constraints on parents. He suggested that the government could contribute towards independent schools' salary bills or it could expand the assisted places scheme, which funds children from low-income families to attend independent schools.
