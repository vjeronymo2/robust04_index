Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 3/1/1994 -- The Romanian Government plans to withhold on a line of cautious fiscal, monetary, and budgetary policies in 1994 and 1995, says the letter of intent and the memorandum for the following two years agreed with the International Monetary Fund. The letter was forwarded by the prime minister [Nicolae Vacaroiu] to parliament for debate and approval. The said policies will be implemented through a package of measures of structural reform including an accelerated privatization and restructuring of the enterprises, deep reforms of the fiscal and financial sector, and the establishing of a inter-bank currency market. The memorandum of economic policy contains details of these policies aiming to achieve the economic goals of the government. The Romanian Government requests a 19-month stand-by agreement with the IMF to make available 131.95 million special drawing rights [SDR] plus two drawings totalling 377 SDR [figure as received], half of which within the structural transformation facility and following the approval of the memorandum, with the other half available by the date of the finalization of the first assessment by the IMF scheduled for 15 November 1994. The second assessment is scheduled for 15 May 1995. The first assessment is expected to outline the macro-economic, budgetary, and monetary objectives for 1995 and to reassess the estimations for external financing needs.
