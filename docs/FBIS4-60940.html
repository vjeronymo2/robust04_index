BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Anna Melnikova and Yuriy Sizov] [Text] Moscow, 30 Apr -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin has declared in his interview to an ITAR-TASS correspondent after the ceremony in which a package of bilateral documents were signed: "The Russian-Latvian documents signed today in the Kremlin have been elaborated with the full consent by the both sides." The head of the Russian state said that "as a whole, five documents have been signed: the Treaty and four agreements in which, in particular, the protection of servicemen was determined and the question of pensioners and former servicemen was solved." Boris Yeltsin paid special attention to the treaty signed by him and Guntis Ulmanis, which ensured the withdrawal of Russian troops from the territory of Latvia. He said that "the final date of the withdrawal was determined to be 31 August." In addition he said that "the Russian side will try to do this as soon as possible because we want children of the servicemen to go to school in Russia already this 1 September." The president also said that during the negotiations the delegations of the both countries "worked on about 10 economic documents as well." In this connection he underlined that "the presidents of the two countries have reached an agreement that the questions on free trade, the regime of the most favored nations on a mutual and equal basis, taxation, tariffs, and duties will be prepared by a special group and Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin will visit Riga where the signing of these documents will take place." Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis answered a question by the ITAR-TASS correspondent and said that in one of the documents signed today "we were talking about the regime of the most favored nations between the two countries." He believes that "this is a very valuable achievement." He said that entrepreneurs of the both countries can now breathe freely because, speaking frankly, the politics "minded its own business, and the economy suffered from that."
