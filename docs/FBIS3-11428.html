Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Chile monitored through 17 March. According to the Central Bank's preliminary reports, the Chilean trade balance registered a $33.1 million deficit in January, with exports at $765 million and imports at $798.1 million. This means the negative balance has been maintained for the ninth consecutive month. The country's purchase rate dropped 4.5 percent and the sales rate increased 14 percent compared with the same month last year, a fact that Finance Minister Foxley regards as "good news." (Santiago LA TERCERA DE LA HORA in Spanish 24 Feb 94 Economic Section p 3) Evaluating the economic performance in 1989-1993, Finance Minister Alejandro Foxley reported on 23 February that the economy grew 6 percent in 1993. He noted the GDP rose from $28.1 billion in 1989 to $43.8 billion in 1993. Investment rose from $6.5 billion to $11.3 billion. He said government investments also increased from $760 million to $1.425 billion. He explained that the government's savings rose from $850 million in 1989 to $2.2 billion in 1993. This generated a fiscal surplus that increased from $506 million in 1989 to $890 million last year. Foxley said these results led the Chilean economy to grow four times faster than in developed countries and three times more quickly when compared with Latin American countries. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 24 Feb 94 pp A1, A9) The National Fishing Association reported Chile exported 1.233 million metric tons of fishing products worth $1.172 billion in 1993, a drop of 10 percent and 9.5 percent, respectively, compared with the previous year, when exports were 1.370 million metric tons valued at $1.295 billion. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 28 Feb 94 pp B1, B2) The National Statistics Institute, INE, reported that the unemployment rate was 4.8 percent from November 1993 to January 1994, an increase of 0.3 percent compared with the previous quarter, and a 0.4 percent increase compared with the November 1992-January 1993 period. The labor force totalled 5,254,200 people, an increase of 4.4 percent compared with the same quarter last year. The number of employees was 5,580,000, an increase of 3.9 percent compared with the same quarter in 1993. This means that 187,290 new jobs were created. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 3 Mar 94 p B10) The INE reported on 2 March that the February consumer price index was 0.3 percent, and that the cumulative inflation rate for the first two months of the year was 1.3 percent, and 13.1 percent for the past 12 months. Housing increased 1.7 percent and transportation and communications 0.5 percent; food dropped 1.2 percent and clothing 1.7 percent. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 3 Mar 94 pp B1, B10)
