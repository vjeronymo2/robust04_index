BFN [Text] Ricardo Alarcon de Quesada, president of the National Assembly of the People's Government [ANPP], has visited the British Parliament at Westminster Palace. Alarcon's tour of the historic building was the first activity in the Cuban delegation's official visit at the invitation of the British Parliamentary Union group. The Cuban delegation received a detailed explanation of the Parliament's procedures, followed by a luncheon hosted by MP William Rogers, Labor Party spokesman for foreign affairs. The Cuban delegation also visited the famous Tate Gallery, where valuable works by artists of the UK are exhibited. Alarcon also met with Don Arnold, member of the Conservative Party and treasurer of the Cuban-British group of the House of Commons, and Michael Marshall, president of the British Interparliamentary Council. Alarcon attended a welcome dinner hosted by John Ward, president of the British group, U.P. [expansion unknown] According to sources from the Cuban Embassy in London, the ANPP delegation, on the first day of their visit, received expressions of satisfaction from the hosts and were pleased by the interest in learning the structure and function of the ANPP.
