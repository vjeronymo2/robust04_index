920515 FT 15 MAY 92 / Astra skill centre cuts attacked THE NUCPS Civil Service union reacted angrily yesterday to the news that Astra Training Services, which took over the bulk of the 60 skill centres privatised in 1990, had announced to staff a further three rounds of closures and redundancies. Rochdale, Somerset and Redmouth centres are to close, and other redundancies totalling 70 were announced. That follows the announcement four weeks ago of the closure of three centres and 162 redundancies. 'This tactic of announcing such closures is roundly condemned by the NUCPS,' said Tony Gallagher, national officer of the union, which is in annual conference at Blackpool. 'It's not the first time but the third consecutive mid-conference announcement when the union officials are away from the workplace.' He further condemned the government underfunding of employment training, which Astra considers the reason for such cuts. 'Privatisation has been disastrous for the providers and those in need of training. It's a national disgrace that should be condemned by all.'
