CSO [Text] Tehran, 3 Farvardin [19 April] KEYHAN News Service -- 'Abbas 'Abdi, editor in chief of the newspaper SALAM, who was arrested early in the month of Shahrivar [23 August-22 September 1993] and was sentenced on 1 Dey [22 December] at the revolutionary court to one year in prison and 40 lashes in suspension, was released on a bond payment of 10 million tumans. The newspaper SALAM made the point that 'Abdi has served 10 months of his sentence and only four months remain, and that taking a 10-million-tuman bond to free him is not appropriate for the remaining time. It regarded this as a violation of judicial procedure. During the month of Dey [22 December 1993-20 January 1994], concurrent with the issuance of the sentence punishing 'Abdi and the press discussions concerning the nature of his crime, the deputy minister of guidance for press affairs said: The charges against 'Abbas 'Abdi are not press related.
