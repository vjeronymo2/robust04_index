BFN [Excerpt] Ankara, June 16 (A.A.) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin said on Thursday [16 June] that the regulations on the straits were under the authorization of Turkey and it will be put into effect on July 1 adding that giving up Turkey's sovereign rights was out of question. Main opposition Motherland Party (ANAP) Deputy Bulent Akarcali, speaking in the parliament, claimed that decisions against Turkey's sovereign rights were made during a meeting of International Maritime Organization where Turkish delegation also attended in London last month. Akarcali said all Turkish sovereign rights on the Bosporus and Dardanelles were transferred to international organizations. He said Turkish delegation had no proposals while Greek proposals were approved of with the support of Russian, Greek and Greek- Cypriot representatives. Answering the ANAP deputy's claims, Cetin said the stories taking place in the press were not based on facts. Cetin said the straits regulations were not discussed during the so-called meeting adding that traffic schemes were brought to that meeting. [passage omitted]
