April 3, 1990, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

GIRLS' ATHLETE OF THE WEEK: NO-HITTERS ARE THE NORM, NOT EXCEPTION, FOR AKAU

Shannon Akau of Buena Park High School pitched two no-hitters last week, which surprised no one.

Akau, The Times' Athlete of the Week, is a threat to pitch a no-hitter every time out. Dating to March 23, she had pitched three consecutive no-hitters until giving up four hits in a 7-2 victory over Walnut Friday.

"I don't feel (the consecutive no-hitters are) surprising. It's not even a topic," Buena Park Coach Brenda Sapp said. "She's a power pitcher. I can tell you she's up there with (former Valencia pitcher Michele) Granger. I believe she can throw with any college pitcher."

Akau has pitched five no-hitters this season and has a 7-1 record, helping Buena Park to an 8-1-1 start. In 56 innings, Akau has 89 strikeouts and only two walks. Her earned-run average is 0.25.

Tuesday, Akau pitched her second consecutive no-hitter, striking out 15 and walking a batter in the fourth inning, in a 3-0 victory over Savanna.

The third one came Wednesday, when she had 11 strikeouts and a walk (in the first inning) in a 12-0 victory over Dominguez. That game was stopped after five innings because of the mercy rule.

"The pitching has 50% to 80% to do with winning," Sapp said, "but without the bats we couldn't win a game for Shannon."

In three games last week, Buena Park supported Akau with 21 runs. And Akau has been doing her part from the batter's box too.

She went three for three against Dominguez, including a three-run home run in the first inning.

"I consider myself a contact hitter," Akau said. "I don't think being a pitcher helps me anticipate what the other will throw."
