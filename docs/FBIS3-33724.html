Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Jan 30 (A.A) -- Iran on Sunday accepted Turkey's offer to set up a special committee to investigate allegations that civilian villages were hit in Iran's border regions during Turkey's air strike on Friday against terrorist bases in northern Iraq, near the Turkish border. AA was told by officials at the Iranian Foreign Ministry that Iran will not "at this point" ask for any compensation. Iran had alleged on Friday that nine civilians from its border villages were killed during raids by Turkish war planes aiming at the terrorist bases in northern Iraq. The Turkish Foreign Ministry for its part had stated that close inspection of the photographs taken of the area where the strike was held, showed that no such mistake was made.
