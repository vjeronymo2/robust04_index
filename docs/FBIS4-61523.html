BFN [Text] Today the new Uzbek parliament adopted a package of laws in order to improve the state system and to speed up the market reforms at its session opened in Tashkent. Deputies adopted a law on the Uzbek parliament (Oly Majlis) at first reading. The parliament will consist of 250 deputies elected from territorial districts on the multi-party basis for a five-year term. Laws on local elections and guarantees for suffrage were also passed. The Uzbek parliament adopted several economic laws, including a law on foreign investments and investors. The law provides for preferential treatment for foreign investors aimed at reducing the risk of loss of capital and to guarantee the property rights. Laws on bankruptcy and taxes on operations with securities were also passed. The Uzbek parliament will continue its work on 6 May.
