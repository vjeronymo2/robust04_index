BFN [Editorial report] The following is a compilation of reports on Mexican economic developments monitored through 21 June. The Finance and Public Credit Secretariat has authorized the Mexican Association of Banks to extend a line of credit of up to 2 billion pesos to conclude a restructuring program of expired loans. Most of these loans are for the agriculture sector. (Monterrey EL NORTE (Foreign Edition) in Spanish 16 June 94 p 21A) The agriculture and hydraulic resources (SARH) secretary has said the marketing system is the Achilles heel preventing Mexican producers access to international markets. The secretary has offered SARH support to Yucatan Peninsula producers of rice, chile peppers, sugarcane, and citrus fruits to cover not only local demands but also export goals. (Mexico City UNOMASUNO in Spanish 17 June 94 pp 1, 5) The Mexican Bank of Foreign Trade has granted $222.1 million to the southeastern part of the country from January to May, 40 percent more than during the same period in 1993, Alberto Rio, promotion manager for that area, has reported. The sectors that benefited in the five states -- Yucatan, Quintana Roo, Chiapas, Tabasco, and Campeche -- were primarily agriculture, tourism, fishing, and manufacturing. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1957 GMT 18 June 94) Approximately 50,000 producers owe $16 billion to the Mexican banking system, and given the impossibility of recovering the investment, the Mexican Banking Association has offered a renegotiation aimed at financing the development of the nation. The problems started in July 1993 due to the economic recession and uncontrolled growth in loans granted without guarantees that were aimed at reactivating economic growth in the past five years. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1408 GMT 18 June 94) According to the National Banking Commission, Mexican banking systems revenues dropped 14.1 percent during the first quarter compared to the same period in 1993, the result of a reduced volume of business, bad loans, a fall in the exchange rate, and an increase in interest rates. Nevertheless, it was stated that for the remainder of the year, this trend could be reversed due to the government's monetary policy. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1426 GMT 18 June 94) Yesterday, peasant organizations stated the failure of the agricultural policy during the current six-year period has resulted in a 19-percent decrease in basic grain production, the unemployment of 2 million peasants, and an annual loss of 13 billion pesos by producers. The organizations stated the compensatory measures the executive has announced will not make up for losses in past years registering in the millions (Monterrey EL NORTE (Foreign Edition) in Spanish 20 June 94 p 15A)
