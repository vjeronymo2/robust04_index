BFN [Text] The agreements on free trade between Latvia and the European Union (work on the text of this agreement was concluded on Wednesday in Brussels) is to go into effect January 1, 1995. Latvia's State Minister on Foreign Trade Olgerts Pavlovskis who reported this at a press conference in Riga Wednesday also pointed out that the republic has become the first Baltic state which is ready to initial the document. All Baltic states started the negotiations on the conclusion of this agreement late February. In Pavlovskis' words, the document can be initialled on June 20 and then it is to be ratified by the Latvian parliament. In accordance with the agreement, no customs tariffs will be imposed on Latvian goods imported to the Western European countries. As he said, this regime is valid only for 40% of Latvia's total foreign trade. However, it does not touch upon agricultural products and textile, Latvia's major export goods. Pavlovskis indicated that the agreement has both economic and political significance because it is a good gesture on the part of the Western European countries which view Latvia as an integral partner.
