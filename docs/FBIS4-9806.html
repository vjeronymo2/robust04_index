BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] Government Minister Alfredo Mendieta has said that recent kidnappings and the assassination of Javier Barahona, the deputy mayor of Wiwili, are desperate actions by criminal gangs trying to undermine peace. Mendieta added that the government is working to combat this situation. [Begin Mendieta recording] We are prepared to fully guarantee that the disarmament process of the 3-80 Northern Front will conclude satisfactorily on 8 April, as scheduled, and that the second phase of disarmament will begin tomorrow without any delay. [end recording] Mendieta said he would meet with high-ranking EPS [Sandinist People's Army] officers to counter the criminal gangs. [Begin recording of Colonel Oswaldo Lacayo, member of the EPS General Staff] We will conduct operations in full coordination with the group being disarmed in the areas surrounding the 3-80 Northern Front disarmament area to prevent any problems. [end recording] According to the EPS, gangs such as those under the command of Charro, Nortiel, and Omar are trying to hinder the disarmament process, but the International Commission of Support and Verification-OAS [CIAV-OAS] believes the peace process is well under way. [Begin recording of Sergio Carabana, CIAV-OAS representative] These incidents are isolated. I believe the government will take steps in this regard, and we will help conduct a thorough investigation to find the culprits. [end recording] The CIAV-OAS will propose that Barahona's case be discussed by the tripartite commission.
