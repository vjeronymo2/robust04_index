931208 FT 08 DEC 93 / Legal threats at Lloyd's If Names reject yesterday's offer, many of the agencies which have managed their affairs at the market will face a spate of legal actions over the next few years First big case will start in April next year, involving 3,000 Names who were members of Gooda Walker syndicates 164, 290, 298 and 299 between 1988 and 1990. Names allege negligent underwriting and seek damages of more than Pounds 550m from 71 Lloyd's members' agencies. In October Mr Justice Saville will hear a case brought by 1,650 Names who were members of Feltrim syndicates 540/542 and 847 between 1988 and 1989. Feltrim Names also suing for negligent underwriting, targeting 60 agencies - of which 59 are members' agencies - in effort to recover up to Pounds 600m in losses. Third large case could come to court early in 1995. Some 2,000 Names on Merrett syndicate 418 are taking legal action against 130 members' agents, the Merrett managing agency, the syndicate's auditors, Ernst & Whinney, and Mr Stephen Merrett, the syndicate's underwriter, over losses registered in the 1985 year. The claim is estimated at about Pounds 350m. Smaller case involving more than 320 Names on Pulbrook syndicate 334 in the 1985 year had been expected to come to court in December this year. The Names face losses of at least Pounds 80m.
