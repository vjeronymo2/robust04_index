BFN [Text] Peruvian Foreign Minister Efrain Goldenberg Schreiber and Argentine Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella on 5 April issued a joint communique at the end of Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella's official visit. In the communique, the foreign ministers reassert their countries' willingness to increase bilateral integration and cooperation at different levels. During his stay in Lima, Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella met President Alberto Fujimori, to whom he extended the greetings of Argentine President Carlos Menem. He also met Jaime Yoshiyama Tanaka, president of the Democratic Constituent Congress. The Peruvian and Argentine foreign ministers stressed the importance of the upcoming Argentine-Peruvian presidential summit scheduled to be held in three months. In the communique, the Argentine Government confirms its willingness to grant financial help to Peru for the construction of integration highways. The communique also expresses the desire of Argentine companies to increase their investments in Peru and to form joint ventures with Peruvian companies. In the communique, the Peruvian and Argentine Governments reassert their commitment to world peace and condemn the proliferation of weapons. Before departing for Argentina, Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella held a news conference in which he referred to the full enforcement of the Rio de Janeiro Protocol signed by Peru and Ecuador, of which Argentina is a guarantor. Di Tella stated that any negotiations undertaken should be conducted within the framework of this protocol. He said: "We believe that what has been signed must be enforced. We believe in this protocol and in the role we have assumed." Di Tella added that the Rio de Janeiro Protocol "is the most adequate mechanism."
