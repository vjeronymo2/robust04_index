Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Future Chilean Foreign Minister Carlos Figueroa on 24 February assessed the visit of U.S. Trade Secretary Mickey Kantor for the presidential inauguration as a U.S. Government sign that it favors a commercial relationship with Chile. Figueroa commented: "I consider him a very important (U.S.) Government official. I also believe his visit is a sign that the U.S. Government favors the search for a significant commercial relationship with Chile, either through a free trade agreement or Chile's acceptance as a NAFTA member, which are, precisely, the subjects we are going to discuss here with the secretary." Figueroa held a lengthy working meeting at the Diplomatic Academy with the outgoing and incoming Foreign Ministry director generals. [passage omitted] The meeting's purpose, according to Figueroa, was to review all the ministry's tasks, exchange opinions regarding the ministry's general guidelines, and to discuss specific subjects. Carlos Figueroa said some of the specific subjects raised during the meeting were Chile's bilateral policy and its relationship with neighboring countries and the European Union, as well as the rapprochement with Mercosur [Common Market of the South].
