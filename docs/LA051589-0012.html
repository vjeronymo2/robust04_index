May 15, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

GRAFFITI

Supervisor Pete Schabarum has sunk to a new low. His answer to recent graffiti denouncing Los Angeles County's inadequate AIDS policies is to take the money to clean it up out of the already pathetic county AIDS budget (Metro, May 10). How clever!

Along similar lines, a number of possibilities suggest themselves. Since, for example, much of the gang graffiti marring local buildings is the handiwork of people of low income and members of minorities, we could clean it up with funds from the programs that provide food, housing, etc. for the poor, or from job-training programs.

Better yet, take it out of Schabarum's salary. If he worried half as much about health care as he does about graffiti, there never would have been a problem in the first place.

BRUCE MIRKEN

Los Angeles
