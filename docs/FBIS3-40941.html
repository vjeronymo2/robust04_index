Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev, Jan 14 (AFP) -- Leading Ukrainian lawmakers sharply criticized the treaty on Ukraine's nuclear disarmament signed in Moscow Friday, with several calling it "treason" and demanding that President Leonid Kravchuk step down. "It is national treason," Vyacheslav Chornovyl, leader of the nationalist opposition movement Rukh, told AFP. "The United States and Russia have brought Ukraine to its knees." The treaty, under which Ukraine pledged to get rid of all the nuclear weapons on its territory, was signed at the Kremlin by Kravchuk, US President Bill Clinton and Russian President Boris Yeltsin.
