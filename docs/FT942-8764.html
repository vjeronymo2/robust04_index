940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / Education scheme draws objection The government is objecting to the European Commission's planned budget for the new Socrates educational exchange programme, due to be introduced under the Maastricht treaty on January 1. Mr Tim Boswell, the education minister responsible for international relations, yesterday told the Commons committee on European legislation that the commission aimed to spend Ecu1bn (Pounds 0.77bn) on the programme during the next five years. 'The aims proposed for Socrates are important ones, but they are also, quite rightly, modest and achievable ones,' he said. 'That is why the government believes that the commission's proposed funding requirement is too high.'
