930930 FT 30 SEP 93 / Warning on photocopier lease 'ruses' THERE IS evidence of 'unacceptable practices' in the photocopier leasing market, Sir Bryan Carsberg, director-general of Fair Trading, said yesterday. He called on the industry to reform itself, warning that he would take direct action otherwise. Sir Bryan's remarks follow widespread complaints in the past year about contracts issued by some photocopier dealers, which are alleged to involve customers in hidden costs and enormous cancellation fees. An Office of Fair Trading investigation into the office equipment leasing market was launched in February. Sir Bryan said the OFT report, which is expected late next month, 'will illustrate the seriousness and variety of the malpractices and excesses that have occurred'. He said some of the main sufferers had been small businesses, solicitors, accountants, schools and housing associations. 'Some of these have found themselves facing five or even six-figure bills to extricate themselves from contracts.' Sir Bryan said there were various steps he could take to stop abuses. These included a reference of the photocopier leasing industry to the Monopolies and Mergers Commission, and revocation of individual licences. However, he said, there were signs of change in the industry. 'I hope . . . that this will be sufficient to make the market work well without regulatory intervention.'
