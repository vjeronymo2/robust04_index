941129 FT 29 NOV 94 / OECD deal over chief in doubt A breakthrough in the deadlock over who should head the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development, the industrial world's economic think-tank, was in the balance yesterday after a meeting of OECD ambassadors was adjourned for 24 hours. Hopes for a new secretary-general were raised last week when Washington grudgingly agreed to a compromise deal. But diplomats said a Franco-Canadian consensus eluded the 25 nations at their two-hour meeting. They agreed to meet again today when they will have to come up with a solution because the mandate of a stand-in head expires the next day. Under the proposed compromise, Frenchman Jean-Claude Paye - whose second five-year term ended in September - would be reappointed for 18 months and Canada's Donald Johnston would then take a full five-year tenure. Several countries, particularly Japan, were said to be opposed to this.
