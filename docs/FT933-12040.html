930727 FT 27 JUL 93 / Company probes by DTI up 50% THE NUMBER OF confidential company investigations started and completed by the Department of Trade and Industry's investigation division rose by more than 50 per cent in the first quarter of this year, figures released yesterday show. Sixty-three investigations were started and 64 were completed, compared with 40 started and 35 completed in the previous quarter. The division looks into insider dealing and other offences under companies, financial services and insolvency legislation. Complaints and requests for company investigations to the DTI rose by more than 20 per cent, from 280 in the previous quarter to 338. Three people were convicted during the quarter following prosecutions resulting from investigations carried out in earlier periods. In one case two defendants pleaded guilty to false accounting offences and were sentenced to 15 months and nine months imprisonment respectively, both suspended for two years. A defendant found guilty of fraudulent trading, theft, and possession of forged share certificates was sentenced to 2 1/2 years imprisonment and disqualified from acting as a company director for five years.
