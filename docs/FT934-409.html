931224 FT 24 DEC 93 / Finance and the Family: Your CGT allowances The table shows capital gains tax indexation allowances for assets sold in November. Multiply the original cost of the asset by the figure for the month in which you bought it. Subtract the result from the proceeds of your sale, and the balance will be your taxable gain or loss*. Suppose that you bought shares for Pounds 6,000 in September 1985 and sold them in November 1993 for Pounds 13,000. Multiplying the original cost by the September 1985 figure of 1.484 gives a total of Pounds 8,904. Subtracting that from Pounds 13,000 gives a capital gain of Pounds 4,096, which is within the 1993-94 CGT allowance of Pounds 5,800. If selling shares bought before April 6 1982, you should use the March 1982 figure.
