921106 FT 06 NOV 92 / People: Non-executive directors International Media Communications, a USM-quoted group, last month announced its first full-year profits since 1985, helped by selling its Skyview in-flight video system to British Airways. It has now persuaded Sir David Nicolson (above), former chairman of BA, BTR and Rothmans International, to join its board. IMC, whose other interests include Alpine Soft Drinks and the marketing of video tapes, is looking for an acquisition in the consumer products field; David Cirucel, IMC chairman, says Sir David's background in the air travel and consumer products industries fit in well with IMC's aspirations. ***** John Richardson, joint deputy chairman of Sketchley, at EXCO INTERNATIONAL; he is a nominee of Ernst & Young, administrators to British & Commonwealth Holdings, Exco's parent until last June. ***** Gordon Waddell, chairman of Fairway and Gartmore Scotland Investment Trust, at SHANKS & McEWAN. ***** Arthur Morton, former chairman of The Burgess Group and chairman and founder shareholder of Norank Systems, at SILVERMINES. ***** Paul Horsley has resigned from GIBBS MEW. ***** Alan Flack has resigned from PRIMADONA. ***** Neil Shaw has resigned from SCOTTISH & NEWCASTLE. ***** Denis O'Brien has resigned from XTRA-VISION. ***** Pat Howe at AIRSPRUNG FURNITURE GROUP. ***** Sir Christopher Harding, chairman of BET and a director of Hanson and English China Clays, at SLOUGH ESTATES. ***** Michael Wright (below), professor of engineering and mathematics at Aston University and chairman of Aston Business School, as chairman of The 600 GROUP.
