930114 FT 14 JAN 93 / CPS considers corruption claims THE Crown Prosecution Service is considering a police report about alleged corruption involving some members of Peterborough council's planning committee. The allegations, including claims that large sums were offered in exchange for commercial and residential planning permissions, were referred to the police late last year by the council's chief executive, Mr Bill Samuel. His action followed reports to him by members of the planning committee concerned about offers of bribes made to them. No charges have yet been brought. In a statement, the council said yesterday: 'It is entirely a matter for the CPS as to whether any further action is taken.'
