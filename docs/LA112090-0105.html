November 20, 1990, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

HOLIDAY RUSH: Sheriff Brad Gates, trying to keep the jail population within court-ordered limits, has asked local judges to forsake a longstanding holiday tradition. . . . Reluctant to lock up less serious prisoners over the holidays, judges typically stay sentences until Jan. 2. The result: up to 1,000 people lining up for booking on the day after New Year's. . . . Gates is asking that the rush be spread over four to six weeks, and Superior Court Presiding Judge Leonard Goldstein says judges will try to cooperate. Jim Newton and Jerry Hicks
