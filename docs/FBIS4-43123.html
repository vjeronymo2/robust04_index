BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Iranian Interior Minister 'Ali Mohammad Besharati-Jahromi arrived in Ankara on an official visit today. He was received at Esenboga Airport by Interior Minister Nahit Mentese. In a statement at the airport, Mentese said that Turkey and Iran, which are linked by common values, unity of faith, and deep cultural ties, are two friendly, neighboring countries with special relations. Mentese stressed that the Turkish-Iranian border has been a line of peace for many centuries. [Begin Mentese recording] As two neighboring countries, Turkey and Iran have common fields of information on which they must exchange views, especially in the security area. It is also a known fact that certain developments which take place in one country affects the other as well. [end recording] In his statement, the Iranian interior minister declared that the security issue as well as military, political, and interparliamentary matters will be discussed during the talks in Turkey. Besharati noted: It is my impression that this is the first time that the friendly relations between Iran and Turkey have reached such a high level. The two ministers then left Esenboga for Izmir, where Besharati will be received by President Suleyman Demirel before returning to Ankara.
