Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 24 KYODO -- Japan's steel exports in 1993 rose 23.8 percent from 1992 to 23,506,000 tons, exceeding 20 million tons for the first time in four years, an industry group said Thursday [24 February]. The Japan Iron and Steel Federation [JISF] said the exports, on a shipment basis, also posted the third consecutive year-on-year rise. It attributed the sharp export growth to increased exports early in the year to China, whose economy is sharply expanding. The dollar value of the exports rose 9.3 percent, but the yen value dropped 4.1 percent, due to the yen's appreciation against the dollar, the federation said. The unit price of all exports dropped to 73,400 yen per ton, down 21,400 yen from the previous year. It was the lowest average unit price in six years. Despite an increase in export volume, export earnings showed little growth, industry officials said, predicting exports in 1994 will level off from 1993 both in volume and value. The federation also reported exports to China increased 2.9-fold to 6,910,000 tons. Shipments to Taiwan rose 19.0 percent to 2,910,000 tons and those to South Korea increased 1.5 percent to 1,670,000 tons. But exports to the United States plunged 30.6 percent to 1,820,000 tons. Steel imports in 1993 increased 3.6 percent to 9,185,000 tons, the federation said.
