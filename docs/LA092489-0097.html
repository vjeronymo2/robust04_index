September 24, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CLASSICAL PROBLEMS

Yolanda Krell's letter (Sept. 17) regarding the change at KFAC and Los Angeles' alleged lack of sophistication and culture was irritating in its simple-mindedness.

As a longtime resident and lover of Los Angeles, I'm tired of the L.A. bashers who complain about the dearth of culture in our city. Since when is classical music the only source of "culture"?

Los Angeles is a thriving city for the arts, and home to many respected artists, musicians, writers, film makers, etc.

Los Angeles has always been and continues to be a vital center for new music, design, theater and more, but I suppose Krell was too busy eating her "designer pizza" to notice.

AMY SRODON

Santa Monica
