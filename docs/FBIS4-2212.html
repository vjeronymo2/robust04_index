BFN [Text] Beijing, April 7 (XINHUA) -- French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur flew in here today to begin his four-day official visit to China. The visit, the first by a French head of government in the past 16 years, is seen here as an important political move which will produce fresh impetus to the Sino-French relations. Accompanying the prime minister on the visit were French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe, Industry, Posts and Foreign Trade Minister Gerard Longuet and other senior officials and parliamentarians. Greeting the prime minister and the delegation he is leading at the airport were Shi Dazhen, chairman of the Chinese Government Reception Committee and minister of power industry, and other senior Chinese officials. Balladur is scheduled to have formal talks with Chinese Premier Li Peng this afternoon.
