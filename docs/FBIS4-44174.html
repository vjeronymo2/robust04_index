BFN [Text] Here are the results of the run-offs in the June 1994 local elections. The first important result is the lowturn out. Only 53 percent of voters went to the polls, compared to 74 percent in the previous elections. In the mayoral elections, the progressives made a comeback: They won in 14 provincial capitals out of 21, including Asti, Rovigo, Pistoia, Matera, and L'Aquila. The Freedom Alliance won in Rieti, Como, Savona, Verona, and Gorizia. In the Sardinian regional election, the progressives won 42 percent of the vote and the Freedom Alliance won 36 percent. The Italian Popular Party [PPI] and the Pact for Italy won 20 percent of the vote. In the provincial elections, the Freedom Alliance won in Lucca, Reggio Calabria, Caltanissetta, and Ragusa. The progressives won in Agrigento and Trapani, the PPI won in Enna.
