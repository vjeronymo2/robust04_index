920108 FT 08 JAN 92 / Commodities and Agriculture: Pakistan cotton crop up sharply PAKISTAN'S cotton production totalled 8.2m bales (each of 375 lb) between the start of the 1991-92 season in September and January 1, up from 7m bales in the corresponding period of 1990, reports Reuter from Karachi. The 1991-92 harvest, which will run till April, was likely to reach 11m bales, up from the 1990-91 record of 9.7m, said Mr Khwaja Mohammad Ilyas, acting chairman of the Pakistan Cotton Ginners' Association. He said the state-run Cotton Export Corporation of Pakistan had purchased 741,021 bales, up from last year's 22,600 bales.
