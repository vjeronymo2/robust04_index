Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] The UN Security Council president, Kovanda Karel of the Czech Republic, has expressed serious concern about the human rights violations in the occupied Kashmir. He expressed this concern at a meeting at the UN with Attorney Sultan Mahmud, president of the Azad Jammu and Kashmir Liberation League. According to APP, Karel supported an investigation at the international level in the human rights situation in the occupied Kashmir. Attorney Sultan Mahmud also met with the UN undersecretary general, Mr. Marrack Goulding. He demanded that the United Nations send a delegation of the human rights commission to the occupied Kashmir to investigate the very grave situation there. The Kashmiri leader also met with many other members of the Security Council and presented a memorandum to them explaining the current situation in the occupied Kashmir.
