920127 FT 27 JAN 92 / Construction Contracts: National Exhibition Centre extension CROWN HOUSE ENGINEERING, part of Tarmac's construction division, has been awarded a Pounds 13.6m mechanical and electrical design and installation contract at Birmingham's National Exhibition Centre's new Hall 10 complex by Laing Midlands. Due for completion in mid-1993 and already fully booked for the first four months after opening, the Hall 10 complex will provide four exhibition halls, a total of 30,000 sq metres of display space, with two large restaurants and a multi-purpose suite. The existing atrium concourse will be extended, linking the new complex to the existing centre, which is already Britain's largest exhibition centre. Crown House Engineering has started work on the project, which has an overall construction programme of 84 weeks, with completion scheduled for June 1993. The contract will cover the design and installation of all HVAC and electrical services, fire protection systems, CCTV, public address and sound reinforcement systems, public health services, standby generators, exhibition services and a building management system.
