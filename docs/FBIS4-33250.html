BFN [Article by Marcio Aith] [Text] The PFL [Liberal Front Party] has not helped the PSDB [Brazilan Social Democracy Party]-- at least not in the states where the coalition between the two parties was made formal in the electoral colleges in which presidential candidate Fernando Henrique Cardoso and the PSDB were already strong. According to polls, gubernatorial candidates Mario Covas and Marcelo Alencar are the favorites in Sao Paulo and Rio de Janeiro, respectively. Both, who belong to the PSDB, have received weak PFL support. Although the PSDB coalition with the PFL has increased the former's electoral chances in Pernambuco, Miguel Arraes -- the PSB [Brazilian Socialist Party] gubernatorial candidate -- is the main favorite. Arraes supports Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva's presidential candidacy. In Parana, the PFL-PSDB coalition's strongest candidate is Jaime Lerner, of the PDT [Democratic Labor Party]. In Bahia, Santa Catarina, Ceara, Minas Gerais, and Para, the coalition was not made concrete because of lack of interest on the part of the two parties' regional leaders. In these states the PFL is strong, and will be an important PSDB rival. Presidential candidate Cardoso told reporter Ana Carolina Silveira in Campinas (Sao Paulo) yesterday: "The parties are establishing coalition in different ways. We thought that Brazil was uniform, but it is not. There can be no vertical coalition, and the parties are adopting different coalitions in the different states." The latest coalitions -- the PT has decided to support gubernatorial candidate Jutahy Magalhaes in Bahia, and the PMDB [Brazilian Democratic Movement Party] gave up the idea of presenting a candidate in Parana -- were decided upon over the last weekend and on 30 May.
