931118 FT 18 NOV 93 / World Trade News: Mexico waits for news from Capitol Hill Attention has remained firmly fixed throughout the week on the political machinations which have been taking place on Capitol Hill, Washington. However, while it was by no means clear earlier yesterday that President Bill Clinton and his administration had gathered enough votes to ensure that the North American Free Trade Agreement would win US congress approval, the Mexican equity market has behaved as though last night's vote was a formality. On five successive days Mexico's IPC index has reached record levels. But data supplied by the IFC tells a slightly different story: the country recorded a modest gain in dollar terms on the week, its improvement on the year to date standing at just above 16 per cent, similarly restrained in comparison to gains elsewhere in the region and the world. Pakistan, for instance, saw the week's best performance as investors responded to an improved political climate, while Thailand also moved on.
