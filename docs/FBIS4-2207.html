BFN [Text] Beijing, April 7 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier Qian Qichen said here today that China would welcome civil servants in Hong Kong to continue to serve the public after 1997 when China restores its sovereignty over Hong Kong. Qian, who is also foreign minister, made the remarks when meeting with a delegation from the Business and Professionals Federation of Hong Kong, headed by its chairman Vincent Lo Hong Sui. Qian said that civil servants in Hong Kong do not need to worry about their future after 1997. However, senior officials in the future Hong Kong Special Administrative Region should meet the relevant requirements spelled out in the Basic Law, Qian said. Only a few are involved in this regard, most civil servants are welcome to continue their service, he said. The vice-premier also attributed worries among some Hong Kong residents about the central government's policies towards Hong Kong after 1997 to the lack of knowledge of the Basic Law, in which, he said, most policies are clearly stated. Therefore, he agreed with guests' ideas on accelerating the pace of spreading the knowledge of the Basic Law and even providing more financial support for these efforts.
