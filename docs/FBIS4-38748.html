BFN [Excerpts] Kazakhstan and Turkey concluded an agreement in Ankara on cooperation in the field of recovery, refining, haulage and selling of Kazakh oil. [passage omitted] In line with the agreement, a joint Kazakh-Turkish company will begin work in August-September this year at a deposit in the west of the republic. [passage omitted] Speaking at the ceremony of signing the agreement, Syzdyk Abishev said that the government of Kazakhstan understands Ankara's anxiety regarding the transportation of oil via the Black Sea. He expressed confidence that this problem can be tackled if the interested parties are involved. Abishev stressed that Kazakhstan and Turkey have concluded about 60 agreements, and most of them are at the stage of implementation. Over 150 Turkish companies are working in the republic now, and several joint banks have been set up. Turkey granted Kazakhstan a loan worth $200 million. This money is used for construction of a five-star hotel, a textile factory, a tannery, and other projects.
