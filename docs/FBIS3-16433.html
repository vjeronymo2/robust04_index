Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Aleksandr Krylovich and Dmitriy Chubarov] [Text] Moscow March 15 TASS -- The Russian Foreign ministry expressed grave concern over the danger of fresh armed clashes in the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict zone. The Ministry's chief spokesman Grigoriy Karasin told a briefing today that "Georgian-Abkhazian relations have got exacerbated recently". According to the spokesman, at the latest round of Russian-brokered talks on political settlement in Abkhazia, which was held under the U.N. auspices in New York on March 7-9, the Georgian side threatened "to go back to war path" unless the independence-seeking Abkhazia recognized Georgia's territorial integrity and agreed to the return of all ethnic Georgian refugees. Karasin stressed that the Russian side was taking "a number of urgent steps to prevent fresh fighting in Abkhazia and to keep the negotiating process going". Russia will also press for a peacekeeping operation in Abkhazia to be carried out by the United Nations or with its approval and "resting, if need be, on a Russian military contingent", the Foreign Ministry spokesman emphasised.
