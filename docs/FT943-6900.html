940826 FT 26 AUG 94 / Pupil-teacher ratios worsen School class sizes in England and Wales rose last year. In all state-maintained schools there was an average of 18.1 pupils for each teacher at the beginning of this year, compared with 17.8 in January 1993. The reduction in teacher numbers has been concentrated on primary schools, where the average class size was 22.7, up from 22.1 in 1984.
