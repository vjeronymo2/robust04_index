Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] An experts panel which probed the failure of the maiden PSLV [polar satellite launch vehicle] launch last September says that a software error in the pitch-controlled loop of the guidance and control processor led to the setback. According to the chairman of the panel, Mr. N. Pant, the error occurred when the control command exceeded the maximum limiting value. The panel, however, confirmed that all the major systems, including the giant solid booster and the liquid propulsion systems of the PSLV worked as planned. There is no lacuna in the rocket design also. The finding of the panel was released by the Indian Space Research Organization in Bangalore today.
