Language: English Article Type:CSO [Text] Bombay, December 9--Customs officers of the marine and preventive wing yesterday arrested three Kenyan women at Sahar airport after nine kgs of heroin, worth about Rs 27 crore in international markets, was discovered in their baggage. The officials said the drug had been concealed in tins of milk powder, coffee, mashed potato, and orange juice in the luggage of Victoria Muthen Katnu. The names of her companions were given as Hadija Ndope Saidai and Naima Nyakinywa. All three were scheduled to fly to Nairobi by an early morning Kenyan Airways flight yesterday. Subsequently, the customs officers searched the Bandra residence of Dr Kalu Ojiewu, the alleged supplier of the heroin. Dr Ojiewu reportedly attempted to escape by leaping off the balcony of his first floor flat at plot number 51 on 29th road, but was overpowered and held. It is believed that the narcotics were procured by Dr Ojiewu from a source in Rajasthan.
