January 9, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

STATE OF THE EARTH

Regarding your editorial "Children of Calamity" (Dec. 22) and Lawrence E. Bruce Jr.'s article on Third World debt (Op-Ed Page, Dec. 22):

"We have met the enemy and he is us." While the UNICEF report makes for sad reading, it is not nearly as important as the worldwatch "State of the Earth" report.

UNICEF would have us stop sending axes to the Third World and start sending chain saws and bulldozers to cut the rain forests down faster. We send oral rehydration kits for 2 year olds, then we have to send boatloads of grain for 12 year olds, then the 18 year olds become either rebels or refugees. The leftists blow up the power lines while the rightists kill the teachers.

This planet will support 1 billion living like Americans, 2 billion living like Western Europeans, 3 billion living like Eastern Europeans, 5 billion living like Chinese or 10 billion living like Ethiopians. If we insist on breeding like rabbits, we will wind up living like rabbits; a handful of vegetation per day, a dirt house and no clean water.

Bruce says we must import more from the Third World, so should we turn Africa into a giant strip mine? He calls for "real aid" and "essential services." What is really needed all over the world is a bottle of the new French birth-control pills in every slum shanty and village hut. China is improving the quality of life of its citizens and what is its reward? We call its population control policies "draconian" and cut off funding. They have learned you can have quality of life or quantity of life, not both.

WOODROW J. HUGHES

Northridge
