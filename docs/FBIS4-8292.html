BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 9/4/1994 -- Romania's national defense doctrine and the training of the Romanian peace-keeping unit were among the issues approached at the Romanian Defense Ministry's first weekly press conference, on Friday [8 April] in Bucharest. Romania's national defense doctrine is based on the principle of defense sufficiency during peace-time, said the defense ministry spokeswoman, Major Elena Olariu, who is the first woman to hold that office in the history of the Romanian Armed Forces. The doctrine provides that Romania will never attack another state. For the situations when Romania could be the target of an aggression, the doctrine provides for reactions based on the principle of omnidirectional defense. The defense doctrine is going to be discussed on Monday [11 April] by Romania's Supreme Defense Council, together with all the other documents concerning the whole concept of national security. The Romanian peace-keeping unit, established on 15 July 1993, is now in an advanced stage of preparations, a fact appreciated also by General George Joulwan, the supreme commander of the Allied Forces in Europe. The unit comprises one staff company, three infantry companies, one millimeters-caliber thrower battery, one anti-tank artillery battery, one mixed anti-aircraft artillery battery, and a logistics sub-unit. Romania is currently contributing to the U.N. missions with the following military: Six watchers in Iraq-Kuwait, six watchers in Rwanda ("no bad news about them at the time being," Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu said on Friday after his meeting with Belgian Foreign Minister Willy Claes,) and 136 people with the Romanian military hospital in Somalia (where the tide of patients is rising and stronger security measures have been taken after the departure of the U.S., German, and Italian troops.) The defense ministry spokeswoman said also that the ministry had nine officers sent for studies in the U.S.A., six in the United Kingdom, six in France, and five in Germany.
