941217 FT 17 DEC 94 / Weekend Money (Companies): Directors' dealings Traditionally Christmas is a period of good cheer in the City when the market rallies, writes Vivien MacDonald of The Inside Track. The absence of such a rally this year may make some people despondent, but the number of directors beginning to buy shares again should offer a glimmer of good spirits. In the past, increasing numbers of directors buying has presaged a rise in the market. Better news is filtering in from the US where the bears are slightly in retreat and the bulls are coming back. This would be genuinely good news for the market though its impact may not come through till the new year. Filofax, the company that supplies specialised stationery, has featured several times in this column over the past year as directors have bought large quantities of stock. The sale by Robert Collischon, the chairman, of 20,008 shares at 223p still leave him with over 1.76m shares. The shares have performed well over the past year, recording an out-performance of some 43 per cent relative to the market. Williams Holdings is a group with diverse interests - ranging from homes and garden through to specialist engineering. The shares have been performing steadily.
