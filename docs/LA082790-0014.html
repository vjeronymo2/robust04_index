August 27, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: POP/ROCK

Concert Crashers: A group of about 40 youths scaled gates at the Shoreline Amphitheater in Mountain View, Calif., during a performance Friday by the rap group Public Enemy and randomly assaulted concertgoers and security guards.

Thirty people were treated for minor injuries at the venue's first-aid stations, said police Lt. Gary Smith. Victims told police that they believed the attackers were members of a gang, but Smith said no gang affiliation had been confirmed. The assaults, which took place in the plaza surrounding the amphitheater, apparently did not disrupt the concert attended by 8,000 people. BETH KLEID
