Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sarajevo, Jan 11 (AFP) -- The UNHCR humanitarian airlift to Sarajevo resumed on Tuesday morning after a break of almost a week because of shelling around the Bosnian capital's airport. The first plane to land, an American C-130, touched down at 0645 GMT, an official for the UN High Commissioner for Refugees, Kris Janowski, said. He said 21 flights were expected during the day. Usually between 25 and 27 planes daily bring in food, medicine and cold weather supplies, according to Captain Joel Fouquet, who is in charge of the 250 UN troops controlling the airport. The UN Protection Force would like to up that to 40 a day, but "the Serbs are opposed to it," Fouquet told a press conference. Nearly 90,000 tonnes of supplies have been unloaded from more than 8,000 flights into Sarajevo since the airlift started, he said.
