BFN [Report by Svetlana Belousova: "Bulgarian Socialist Party Initiates Petition To Topple Berov"] [Text] At the first sign of deterioration in the social situation in the country, Yuri Borisov will initiate a petition among the deputies of the Parliamentary Union for Social Democracy [PSSD] with the demand to propose a vote of no-confidence in Lyuben Berov's cabinet. The deputy informed journalists of his intention during the 29 May plenum of the Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP]. At their two-day national meeting in Karlovo the members of the Socialist Youth Union [SMS] decided that their organization should leave the PSSD if a petition aimed at prompting a no-confidence vote is not immediately initiated, SMS leader Lyubomir Nikolov announced on 29 May. The upcoming 41st BSP Congress should direct the party's activity toward holding early elections, while the PSSD should facilitate the adoption of the necessary laws by the end of the summer vacation, BSP leader Zhan Videnov insisted in the draft resolution he proposed at the plenum. Videnov also proposed to the PSSD deputies to reject changes in the cabinet which lead toward asserting a party character in this organ. In yet another proposal he suggested to the PSSD to deny support to the governmental program until September 1994, and seek possibilities of initiating joint activities with the trade unions vis-a-vis the cabinet and the social commitments it assumed. In his statement he clearly implied that in anything related to amendments in the Constitution and the Election Law negotiations must be conducted only with the Union of Democratic Forces [SDS]. At the plenum Ananieva explained the situation related to the confidence vote, requested by the prime minister on 26 May. According to Ananieva, Aleksandur Tomov [leader of the Civic Alliance for the Republic] was trying to organize the support of 30 PSSD deputies for the idea of forming a pro-president government. The alternative of forming a caretaker cabinet had also been discussed, Ananieva said. The young Socialist proposed a draft resolution calling on the PSSD deputies to carry out a vote of no-confidence in their chairman, but according to Videnov the proposal was not made in accordance to the correct procedure and was not discussed at the plenum because of its unserious character.
