930311 FT 11 MAR 93 / International Company News: Toys 'R' Us moves ahead 29% to Dollars 437m TOYS 'R' Us, the US-based specialty store chain, yesterday reported a 29 per cent increase in after-tax profits to Dollars 437.5m in the 12 months to end-January. The group said that it planned to add another 95 to 100 stores this year, the majority overseas. Toys 'R' Us scored the advance on sales of Dollars 7.17bn, up from Dollars 6.12bn, while earnings per share rose from Dollars 1.15 to Dollars 1.47. The company added 43 stores in the US in 1992, and 41 outlets overseas - taking the total to 540 US-based toy stores, 167 international outlets and 215 Kids 'R' Us children's clothing stores. The full-year figures came after a strong fourth quarter, when after-tax profits increased from Dollars 261.6m to Dollars 339.7m. Sales in the three-month period were up from Dollars 2.86bn to Dollars 3.4bn. Revco, the Ohio-based drugstore operator which emerged from bankruptcy protection last year, recorded an after-tax profit of Dollars 4.27m in the three months to February 6. Sales were Dollars 552.4m, compared with Dollars 522.9m a year ago, and operating profit increased from Dollars 18m to Dollars 24.6m. The company said that same-store sales in the quarter - the third of its financial year - rose by 4.6 per cent. For the nine-month period, Revco's after-tax profits stand at Dollars 2.2m and sales at Dollars 1.51bn.
