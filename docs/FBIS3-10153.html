Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 17 February. The auction of the Ilo copper processing plant was suspended on 2 February after the three companies that won the international bid declined to submit their offers in the face of the $70 million base price proposed by the government. They also were compelled to make a $20 million minimum investment. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 3 Feb 94 p 8) The Economy and Finance Ministry on 11 February assumed responsibility for the total foreign medium- and long-range debt of National Telecommunications Enterprise Peru, Entel Peru, as a step to its privatization, which will begin on 28 February. So far there are five consortiums interested in buying Entel and the Peruvian Telephone Company, CPT. The base price is $525 million, of which $190 million will be used to increase CPT's capital and the remainder will be divided between CPT and Entel. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 12 Feb 94 p A23)
