May 24, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

MARTINEZ'S 'LOVE IN THE LIGHT'

When Al Martinez came on the scene, I surmised another person who will give dialogue and anecdotes about "the good old days" of Los Angeles. While amusing and factual, The Times was not torn apart to get to Page 2 of the Metro section to read his column.

With reading his column "Love in the Light" (May 15), concerning Paul Monette and his book "Borrowed Time," this man became a talisman. The strength to open himself up and look around and ask, "This picture of life around me, is it what I want to see, if not, where change can be made?"

From my bed here in the AIDS ward of the Los Angeles County/USC Medical Center, I salute you, Mr. Martinez.

Hopefully, with the willingness, compassion and understanding of humans like you, the prejudice, misunderstanding and indifference toward persons with AIDS and gay people as a whole will be slackened enough so that there won't be any "gay brother" or "straight brother," just "brother."

JOHN BRINKMAN

Los Angeles
