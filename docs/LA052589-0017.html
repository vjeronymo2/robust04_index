May 25, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COMMENTARY / EXCERPTS;

A MIGHTY BLOW FOR FRIENDLY PERSUASION

Tian An Men Square is converted into the world's largest peace park. Images from China are telling the world that nonviolence is effective, both as a conviction of the heart and a tactic of the street. The eruption of nonviolent disobedience has brought the Chinese dictators to a state of powerless confusion that could never have happened as quickly or been as widespread had the students chosen guns or bombs.

Without exception, every show of force -- nonviolent or violent force -- has as its goal the changing of minds. George Bush sends 2,000 troops to Panama to persuade Manuel Noriega to change his mind about ruling his country. Bank robbers aim guns at tellers to change their minds about who should own the money in the drawer. Mahatma Gandhi fasted to change England's mind about who should rule India.

Every conflict or problem, whether among individuals or governments, has been, and will be, solved through the exertion of violent or nonviolent force. No third way exists. Choosing nonviolent noncooperation, the Chinese students are proving the truth of Gandhi's statement: "Government of the people is possible only so long as they consent either consciously or unconsciously to be governed."

Eleven Latin American dictators were overthrown in this century through nonviolent strikes. Armed Philippine guerrillas tried for years to drive out the Marcos regime. He was defeated by nonviolent resistance.

What has violence done? Compare El Salvador and Poland. At about the same time in the late 1970s, rebels in both countries wanted to change the mind of the government. In El Salvador, they attacked with guns. More than 70,000 have been killed on all sides. Governance has worsened. In Poland, they attacked with moral force. Few have died. Governance has improved.

A massacre of innocents -- the "crackdown" that pessimists say is inevitable -- is still possible in China. What isn't possible is that the bravery and wisdom of the past two weeks of massive nonviolence can be canceled. The story still to be written is how this group of Chinese students came to understand so well and so boldly that the strength of truth and justice is superior to the weakness of guns and tanks.
