BFN [Text] If NATO remains faithful to its original ideas in the Partnership for Peace program, it will not accede to Russian demands, said Jiri Payne, chairman of the parliament's foreign affairs committee, in reaction to Russian demands that the Partnership for Peace agreement be accompanied by a treaty guaranteeing a system of consultation between Russia and NATO. Payne described as rather shocking the notion that Russia should take part in decisions concerning the expansion of NATO to include Central and Eastern Europe.
