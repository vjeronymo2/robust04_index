Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [By Pak Ui-chun] [Text] Considering the operation of the North-South Joint Nuclear Control Committee [JNCC] a must to resolution of the North Korean nuclear issue by the North and South, the government has decided to officially propose resuming the committee's operation to North Korea when special envoys are exchanged between the North and South sometime this month. A government official on 2 March said: "It is very important to operate the JNCC at a present stage to independently resolve North Korea's nuclear issue and to realize the denuclearization on the Korean peninsula," and added "the government intends to suggest this matter to the North side when special envoys are exchanged." He also disclosed that: "As soon as a working-level contact for the exchange of special envoys is over, the steps for the resumption of the JNCC will be extensively discussed," and concluded "however, it is not clear whether North Korea will accept our proposal."
