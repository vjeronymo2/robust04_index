Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Manki Ponomarev report: "Preparations for Exercise Gaining Momentum"] [Text] William Perry, the new U.S. defense secretary, recently addressed the International Conference on Security Questions, confirming that a joint Russian-U.S. exercise is to be held in July as scheduled to rehearse collaboration in peacekeeping operations. Some of the forces of the Russian 27th Motorized Rifle Division in the Volga Military District and the U.S. 3d Mechanized Division stationed in Germany will be involved in the exercise. Preparations for this, the first joint exercise in the history of the two armies, are gaining momentum. The KRASNAYA ZVEZDA editorial office learned that General Leonard Holder, commander of the U.S. 3d Division, is arriving in Russia Sunday 13 February. After a brief stopover in Moscow he will head off to the Volga Military District, where the exercise will be held, and will meet there with Major General Anatoliy Sidyakin, commander of the Russian 27th Division. The coming exercise will be a command-staff exercise. It is thought that around 250 men on each side will be involved in it -- officers from both armies' divisions, regiments, and battalions.
