BFN [Text] On 18 April, within the scope of the parliamentary plan that has already received the support of the United States and the United Nations, Frantz Robert Monde, president of the Chamber of Deputies, began his consultations with employers in order to resolve the crisis. Both Monde and Max Villard, vice president of the Haitian Chamber of Commerce and Industry, deemed those talks very positive. According to the president of the chamber, the two officials tackled the core of the problem: the executive vacuum and how to fill it. Villard renewed the employers' support for the parliamentary plan for the resolution of the crisis. This plan, which provides for the installation of a new prime minister after the retirement of General Raoul Cedras, but does not set a date for the return of Jean-Bertrand Aristide, has been rejected by the president-in-exile. On 13 April, Monde criticized President Aristide for his stubborn refusal to appoint a new prime minister and announced that he wanted to initiate consultations on fill the executive vacuum. Deputy Monde labeled this meeting as fruitful, but refused to reveal the points discussed.
