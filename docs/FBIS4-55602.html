BFN [Article by Antonin Jilecek of the Czech Statistical Office: "The Economy of the Czech Republic Developed in the First Quarter According to Expectations"] [Text] Industrial output of the Czech Republic was 0.9 percent higher in March than in the same period last year. In the first quarter, industrial output fell 0.7 percent short of last year's level. A marked increase, by a quarter, was recorded by small organizations and individual businessmen. The volume of merchandise production in organizations with 25 or more employees was 3 percent lower than in the first quarter of last year. The production of goods increased in the paper and printing industries by 15.1 percent; in coking and refinery oil processing by 5.9 percent; in the food and condiments industry by 2.7 percent; in the chemical and pharmaceutical industry by 1.4 percent; in the glass, ceramics, china, and construction material industries and the industries that manufacture electrical and optical instruments by 0.5 percent. It declined in all other areas. The number of employees in larger industrial enterprises declined by 5.5 percent in the first quarter in comparison with the same period last year, to 1,353,000 people. Average monthly wages reached 6,102 korunas [Kc]--i.e., 13.8 percent (real wages 3.1 percent) more than in the first quarter of 1993. Construction output was 4.1 percent higher in March, 6.2 percent higher in the first quarter than in the same period last year. It was 8.2 percent higher in the first quarter in organizations with 25 or more employees. The number of employees in these organizations was 219,000 in January-March 1994--i.e., 1.2 percent more--and their average monthly wages reached Kc6,674--i.e., 14.4 percent (real wages 3.3 percent) more. The public freight forwarded 43.4 million tonnes of cargo--i.e., 7 percent less than in the first quarter of 1993. Sales of slaughter hogs were 0.9 percent higher in the first quarter, while sales of slaughter cattle declined by 8 percent, poultry by 28.4 percent, milk by 6.7 percent, and eggs by 4.7 percent. The cost of living in white-collar workers' households rose in March 2.6 percent from December 1993, in farmers' households by 2 percent, and in pensioners' households by 2.8 percent. Expenditure on services grew the fastest. The overall volume of retail sales in current prices was 16.4 percent higher in the first quarter and, in physical terms, rose by 7.4 percent in comparison with the same period last year. The number of job seekers seasonally declined in March to 184,500 and was 32,900 higher than a year ago.
