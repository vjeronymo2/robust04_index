Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Inchon, South Korea, March 17 (YONHAP) -- The Swedish mine-laying ship "Carlskrona" put in at Inchon port on Thursday, with 154 Swedish Naval Academy students and crew aboard. The 3,300-ton mine layer, which boasts the latest weapons, shooting control equipment and advanced navigation system, left Sweden on Jan. 4 for a round-the-world trip and has visited Morocco, Hawaii and Japan, crossing the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. Carlskrona, the first Swedish Navy vessel to stop in South Korea, is expected to help promote bilateral relations. The students and navy men plan to visit the truce village of Panmunjom, folk village in Yong-in and other major historical sites before sailing for Hong Kong. The ship will also visit Bangkok, Malaysia, India, Pakistan, Turkey and Spain before returning to Sweden in May.
