Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] President Aristide continues his tour of Canada. President Jean-Bertrand Aristide was due to meet with Canadian Prime Minister Jean Chretien on 25 January. Following an initial meeting with Canadian Minister of Foreign Affairs Andre Ouellet, Jean-Bertrand Aristide, on 25 January, also met with a Canadian delegation led by Mr. Ouellet. The two delegations, Haitian and Canadian, discussed a number of subjects, agreeing on the following points: The imposition of a total embargo to relieve the situation in Haiti as soon as possible. The training of a police force, outside of Haiti, with the collaboration of the United Nations or with the participation of several friendly countries. According to the agreement concluded by the two governments, this force will be trained not only in maintaining order and security, but also in respect for the laws and Constitution. [passage omitted] During the talks, the two delegations also reviewed several areas of activity in which Haitian-Canadian cooperation would be broadened, including education, literacy, cooperatives, communication, and energy, following the return of democracy to Haiti. It should also be noted that the possibility of a radio station broadcasting to Haiti has also been discussed.
