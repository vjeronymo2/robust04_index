Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 21 KYODO -- Toyota Motor Corp. expects its U.S. manufacturing arm to produce some 300,000 units this year, an increase of about 25 percent over the previous year, a company spokesman said Friday [21 January]. Toyota Motor Manufacturing U.S.A. Inc. (TMM), based in Georgetown, Kentucky, turned out 234,000 units last year, the spokesman said. TMM is scheduled to begin production at a second plant next to the current facility in late March. The new plant will assemble the Camry, which is now built at the current plant, and the Avalon, TMM's new flagship car to be introduced this autumn. Besides Camry sedans, the current Georgetown facility also produces and exports Camry station wagons and coupes to several foreign countries, including Japan.
