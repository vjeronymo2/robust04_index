BFN [Text] Two sports agreements were signed this afternoon with Ecuador and Spain respectively at the headquarters of the National Institute for Sports, Physical Education, and Recreation [INDER]. (Sabino Hernandez), president of the Ecuadoran Sports Committee, together with Conrado Martinez Corona and Manuel Gonzalez Guerra, signed the Cuba-Ecuador exchange document for the period of 1994-95. The agreement establishes increasing Cuban technicians in Ecuador in the upcoming months to reach a total of 70 as well as reciprocal participation of the two countries in events of diverse disciplines. On the other hand, (Francisco Guzon), deputy chief of the Spanish Superior Sports Council, and INDER Vice President Raul Villanueva signed a similar cooperation agreement for the current year, described by the visitor as one of the most important agreements signed by his country. The agreement includes 30 points of exchange in various spheres, including the participation of athletes in reciprocal tournaments.
