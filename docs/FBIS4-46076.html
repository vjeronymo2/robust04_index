BFN [Text] Vienna, May 25 (AFP) -- Inspectors from the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) have completed their inspection of a controversial experimental laboratory in North Korea and took samples from spent fuel rods, IAEA officials said in Vienna Wednesday. The samples should allow the IAEA to determine how long the fuel rods were in the reactor and thereby calculate whether plutonium could have been diverted, possibly for use in making nuclear bombs.
