Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Small-Medium Businessmen Leaning Right, Poll Shows"] [Text] Milan, 8 Mar (ANSA) -- Silvio Berlusconi and his Go Italy [Forza Italia] movement are favored by a relative majority of artisans and small- and medium-sized activity businessmen, followed by Mario Segni and his Pact for Italy, according to a poll to be published in the next edition of the "SRL" magazine, to be distributed with the economic daily ITALIA OGGI. The poll showed how Berlusconi won the support of 32 percent of those interviewed, followed by Segni with 24.5 percent and the People's Party [Italian Popular Party], the ex-Christian Democrats, with 12.9 percent and liberals with 8 percent, while the Northern League was favored by only 6 percent of those quizzed and the rightists of National Alliance, headed by the neo-fascist MSI, came in at 2.5 percent. The poll also asked sector organizations to list the priorities and coming in first was a rationalization of the taxation system for businesses, a general reduction in labor costs and greater flexibility for hiring and firing.
