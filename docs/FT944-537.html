941228 FT 28 DEC 94 / India's ex-President Singh dies Priests of several religions offered last prayers for former President Zail Singh, India's first Sikh head of state, as his body was cremated according to Sikh custom yesterday (pictured above), Reuter reports from New Delhi Hindu, Moslem, Sikh, Christian and Buddhist prayers were chanted on the banks of the Yamuna river in Delhi near the memorials to two of India's founding leaders, Jawaharlal Nehru and Mahatma Gandhi. Prime Minister P V Narasimha Rao laid a wreath on the body at the ceremony attended by ministers, opposition leaders and chiefs of the army, navy and air force. Singh, who was 78, died in the northern city of Chandigarh on Sunday after suffering multiple fractures when his car was hit by a truck on November 29. He was appointed by then-prime minister Indira Gandhi, and served as president from 1982 to 1987. He was called Giani, or scholar, for his mastery of Sikh scriptures.
