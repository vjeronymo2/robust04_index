CSO [Text] New Delhi, May 12 -- The 25-year-old dispute over the Yamuna waters was settled today with the five river basin States--Haryana, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh and Delhi--reaching an agreement on the distribution of water in the presence of the Union Water Resources Minister, Mr V. C. Shukla, report UNI and PTI. After signing the agreement along with the Chief Ministers of Haryana, Rajasthan and Delhi, Mr Shukla announced that the consent of the Chief Ministers of Uttar Pradesh and Himachal Pradesh had been obtained through fax and telephone. The agreement, which was signed by Mr Shukla, Mr Bhajan Lal (Haryana), Mr Bhairon Singh Shekhawat (Rajasthan) and Mr Madan Lal Khurana (Delhi), envisages the allocation of 5.730 billion cubic metres of water to Haryana, 4.032 bcm to Uttar Pradesh, 1.119 bcm to Rajasthan, 0.378 bcm to Himachal Pradesh and 0.724 bcm to Delhi.
