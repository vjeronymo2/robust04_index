Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 22 (CTK) -- The majority of Czech citizens (65 percent) firmly believe that no further property should be returned to the former aristocracy and 42 percent oppose the restitution of church property, according to a poll conducted in March by the Institute for Public Opinion Research (IVVM) among 835 people over age 15. Only two percent of those polled were definitely for the return of property to aristocrats and eight percent were definitely for the return of church property. A total of 58 percent of the respondents said they were against the return of property to people who emigrated from Czechoslovakia and did not come back while only three percent said they favored the return of their property. The restitution of church property and the return of property to emigrants was supported mostly by inhabitants of North Moravia. Views of potential voters for various political parties differed considerably. Among those who favor the restitution of church property, 28 percent would vote for the Civic Democratic Party (ODS), 11 percent the Civic Democratic Alliance (ODA), 11 percent for the Czech Social Democratic Party (CSSD), and four percent the Left Bloc (LB). Among those who support the return of property to aristocrats 19 percent would vote ODS, 8 percent ODA, 6 percent CSSD, and no one LB. The return of property to Jewish communities is supported by 54 percent of ODS voters, 50 percent CSSD, and 20 percent LB.
