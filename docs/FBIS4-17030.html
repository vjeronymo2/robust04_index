BFN [Text] Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev today received the U.S. ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to our Republic, Richard Kozlaric. Our correspondent Sakit Ochakhly reports: [Begin Ochakhly recording] New U.S. Ambassador Richard Kozlaric presented his credentials to Geydar Aliyev at the presidential residence today. The ambassador noted that as the official representative of the United States in Azerbaijan he will do everything to develop bilateral ties in a healthy spirit and broaden ties in the social, political, scientific, and cultural fields. Kozlaric also stated that in his new post he will use every means to objectively convey the developments in Azerbaijan to the international community, primarily to the country he represents, and to speedily end the conflict between Azerbaijan and Armenia. He also said that the agreement to be signed with Azerbaijan's oil company will open a new phase in economic cooperation. President Aliyev congratulated Kozlaric on his appointment as the U.S. ambassador to our Republic and wished him success in such a responsible task. The president noted that the development of diplomatic relations with a great country like the United States would help to establish closer ties between the two peoples. Aliyev briefed the new ambassador on the sociopolitical situation in Azerbaijan, Armenia's aggressive policy, and the results of the meeting of CIS heads of state in Moscow. He noted that everyone knows the problems faced by a country 20 percent of the land of which has been occupied and that has over 1 million refugees, yet the United States has imposed an embargo on humanitarian assistance to Azerbaijan. He hoped that the new ambassador would appraise developments and the situation realistically and inform his country that Armenia is an occupying state. Later a frank talk was held between Aliyev and Kozlaric. They exchanged views on issues of mutual interest. [end recording]
