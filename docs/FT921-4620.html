920309 FT 09 MAR 92 / Expectation of change in Scotland A SUBSTANTIAL minority of Scottish companies expect constitutional change in Scotland after the general election, even though a majority expect the election will be won by the Conservative party, which opposes constitutional change, James Buxton writes. Two-thirds of Scottish companies expect the Tories to win the election, according to a survey by BBC Scotland and the accountants Pannell Kerr Forster. While 48 per cent of those asked do not expect constitutional change, 42 per cent do expect change. The survey was based on 136 interviews among the top 200 Scottish companies in all sectors and a further 100 big privately-owned companies.
