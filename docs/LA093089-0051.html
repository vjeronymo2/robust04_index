September 30, 1989, Saturday, San Diego County Edition

RBV BEATS NEIGHBOR VISTA

Finally, after Rancho Buena Vista defeated Vista Friday night, 34-14, RBV Coach Craig Bell acknowledged what he had refused to admit all week.

"You say this is just another league game," he said. "Then there are balloons, presentations, floats, you name it . . . "

No. 2 RBV scored 20 second-half points to run away in the schools' first meeting. Markeith Ross scored on a 23-yard run, his second touchdown of the night, and James Lewis scored on runs of 28 and 14 yards.

There was as much activity off the field as there was on. There were 1,000 balloons released before the game, as well as a gift exchange between the student governments.

The game was dedicated to Sal Aunese, a former Vista quarterback who died of cancer last Saturday. "Sal" was painted onto the field across the 50-yard line facing bleachers on both sides of the field, and a large red helmet with "8" -- Aunese's number at Vista -- was painted in the middle. SCOTT MILLER
