BFN [Excerpts] Brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council and supreme commander of the Armed Forces, yesterday chaired a meeting of the Defense Ministry Command and the General Staff Command, in the presence of brother Yahya al-Mutawakkil, the minister of interior, and Staff Brigadier General, 'Abd-Rabbuh Mansur Hadi, the minister of defense. [passage omitted] Staff Brig. Gen. 'Abd-Rabbuh Mansur Hadi, the defense minister, yesterday took the constitutional oath before brother Lt. Gen. 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council and supreme commander of the Armed Forces, after he was appointed defense minister. Following is the biography of Defense Minister Staff Brig Gen. 'Abd-Rabbuh Mansur Hadi: He was born in 1944 in the Abyan Governorate, the al-Ghadir area. He received a military education in Egypt, Britain, and the former USSR and holds a master's degree from the Nasir Academy in Egypt and the [word indistinct] academy in the former USSR. He held several leading military posts, including the chief of staff of the Armored Corps, chief of staff of the War College, commander of operations sector, director of supplies and logistics, and deputy chief of staff for supply and logistics. After the reunification of the homeland on 22 May 1990, he was appointed adviser at the Presidential Council. He was one of the strugglers of the liberation war. He was awarded several medals. He is the father of five children.
