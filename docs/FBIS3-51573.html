Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Text] Concerning the disturbances that have been taking place in various educational institutions throughout the country, Interior Relations Minister Carlos Delgado Chapellin reiterated that these incidents are being caused by small groups of troublemakers. He added that these groups include suspected extremists who are being investigated. [Begin recording] [Chapellin] I believe that this would seem to be a political tactic that will lead nowhere. Some high school students are being manipulated. They are students who are being misled [words indistinct] by people who have nothing to do with the educational sector. These disturbances have no popular support, and the best thing the students can do is to go home and study and allow the elected president to be sworn-in peacefully. [Reporter] Mr. Minister, by whom are they being manipulated? [Chapellin] Manipulated by forces associated with the so-called extremists. We are currently investigating several extremists. There might be some surprises in terms of who is manipulating these squalid protest marches. Do not blow these disturbances out of proportion because they are worthless. [Reporter] Will the authorities ever uncover who... [Chapellin, interrupting] Of course we will. Just yesterday [words indistinct] four masked men. The university authorities were the first to [word indistinct]. Let us give... [Reporter, interrupting] Yes, but the uncertainty caused by these same four people... [Chapellin] Well, we will have to arrest those four people, even if they decide to hide on university grounds. [end recording]
