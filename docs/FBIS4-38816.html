BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow June 2 TASS -- "The time will come when our two countries will again return to the Euro-Asian union idea, first suggested by Kazakhstan President Nursultan Nazarbayev, notwithstanding the fact that some CIS members are not prepared so far to join such a union, State Duma Speaker Ivan Rybkin said here on Thursday during a meeting with Kazakhstan Foreign Minister Kanat Saudabayev. "However, it may so happen that by that time the legislations of our states will become so different from one another that a good deal of effort would have to be made to bring them closer together again," Rybkin said. In view of this, "it is essential to seek cooperation in the lawmaking process within the CIS framework so that the legislations of our states would be adequate to one another as much is possible", pointed out the speaker of the lower house of the Russian parliament.
