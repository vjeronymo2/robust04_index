BFN [Excerpts] Santo Domingo, 18 May (NOTIMEX) -- Today, Dominican President Joaquin Balaguer announced he would accept holding supplementary elections in the provinces where it can be proven dishonest actions took place. The 87-year-old chief of state pointed out "he would accept holding these special elections in those places where irregularities took place, but the Central Electoral Board (JCE) must organize them." Balaguer, the Social Christian Reformist Party (PRSC) presidential candidate, declared himself the winner of the 16 May elections, but the opposition claimed there were irregularities during the elections. Expecting to begin his seventh term at 87, on 17 May, the almost blind president denied there was fraud and maintained the elections were honest. [passage omitted] Today, the JCE suspended issuing the bulletin that would make public the name of the winner of the general elections in order to analyze the PRD [Dominican Revolutionary Party] allegations of fraud. A PRD delegation delivered the vote challenge to the JCE. According to a JCE spokesman, it will take "pertinent" time to give an opinion on the opposition's charge and petition, but will make a ruling on 19 May at the latest. [passage omitted]
