Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Three IDF [Israel Defense Forces] soldiers were slightly injured by a grenade thrown at them in Rafah this afternoon. Our army affairs correspondent Alon Ben-David reports that the three were evacuated to hospital, and IDF troops launched searches for the perpetrators. Two Arab residents were wounded by soldiers gunfire in Gaza this afternoon. Our correspondent Shlomi Eldar reports that as a soccer game between Gaza and Rafah teams was in progress, IDF soldiers entered the field to pursue an armed suspect. While in pursuit, they were pelted with stones by the spectators. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 1700 GMT on 29 January adds: "Last night in Rafah, IDF soldiers captured a Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] terrorist carrying a Kalashnikov and ammunition. ["A Molotov cocktail was thrown at a Border Police patrol near Abu-Dis tonight. The bottle shattered and ignited, but no one was hurt and no damage was caused."] Fatah figures have expressed satisfaction with the results of the elections to the Gaza Strip Engineers Association, in which Fatah representatives were elected to four out of the nine seats, with Islamic Jihad and Hamas, which ran on a joint list, gaining the other four seats. [The ninth seat is to be held by rotation.] The elected chairman is Sa'id Khurama from Fatah. Our correspondent in the south notes that because of Fatah's dire financial condition, by comparison to other organizations, Fatah had been unable to achieve better results in the election campaign, with many candidates defecting to Hamas as a result of Fatah's inability to pay salaries.
