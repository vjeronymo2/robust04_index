941018 FT 18 OCT 94 / World Trade News: Boost for Tunisian telecoms Tunisia is planning to spend an average of Dollars 200m a year on modernising its telecommunications system. Among projects under way or planned for the next few years are investments in telephone lines and exchanges, optic fibre links, a GSM (Groupe Special Mobile) standard digital mobile phone system and a rural phone system. Tunisia is already linked to the world's longest Optical Submarine communications system connecting south-east Asia, the Arabian peninsula and western Europe. It also invested Dollars 8m in a submarine optic fibre cable linking it to eastern Europe, the Americas and South Africa.
