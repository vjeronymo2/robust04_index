Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 9 Feb (PL) -- Cuban Vice President Carlos Lage will meet with Colombian President Cesar Gaviria today in the continuation of strengthening bilateral ties following the resumption of diplomatic relations a little more than three months ago. Lage, who is also secretary of the Council of Ministers Executive Committee, will have lunch with Gaviria shortly after arriving for an official visit to conclude a tour which included Brazil and Venezuela. He will meet with Foreign Trade Minister Juan Manuel Santos, who will travel to Havana in the next few days to effect a binational commission that was established in the agreements signed during Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina's visit here in December. He will also hold meetings with Agriculture Minister Jose Ocampo, Economic Development Minister Mauricio Cardenas, and Health Minister Juan Londono, who visited Havana in January and signed cooperation agreements in various aspects of his field as well as for the purchase of $7-million worth of vaccines. On the afternoon of 10 February, before leaving Colombia, the Cuban leader will meet with the board of directors of the Bank of the Republic, the central bank which regulates the financial and fiscal policy. He will also meet with Armando Montenegro, chief of the National Planning Institute, and he will hold contacts with various media. Invited by the powerful National Industrialists Association, he will hold a working breakfast tomorrow with representatives of the industrial sector, associations of exporters, financial institutions, and small- and medium-sized industries. The breakfast will also be attended by representatives of the federations of merchants, cattle ranchers, metal products industry, farmers, and the Santa Fe de Bogota Chamber of Commerce.
