Language: Tajik Article Type:BFN [Text] Sayed Abdullo Nuri, leader of the Islamic Rebirth Party of Tajikistan, has sent a letter addressed to Abdulmadjid Samadov, chairman of the Council of Ministers of Tajikistan, which says: In the name of God, the merciful, the compassionate: To esteemed Abdulmadjid Samadov, chairman of the Council of Ministers of Tajikistan, and esteemed Mahmudzoda, chairman of the executive committee of the Soviet of People's Deputies of Leninabad Oblast. Your Excellencies, you are among those persons who have been witnesses to all the [words indistinct] of the republic, which need no explanation. However, I am confident that our hearts are in pain at the suffering of the nation and at the pains of the freedom and independence of the homeland. Tajikistan is [words indistinct] and bleeding. He who sprinkles the salt of hatred and animosity on these wounds and he who is eager for bloodshed is not among the wise. [passage indistinct] One can conclude from the course of events in Tajikistan that certain forces are bent on (?spreading) the devastation of the civil war to the ancient Khojand [present-day Leninabad]. One should remember the tragic events in Afghanistan and should refrain from repeating them in Tajikistan. Now is the time to ponder--for the sake of national sanctity, the integrity of the homeland, and the destiny of the present and future generations--to (?remove) the communist government. Efforts should be made to bring about a government that is for justice and that cares for its citizens and one that will bring prosperity to all the people of Tajikistan. [passage indistinct] [Signed] Sayed Abdullo Nuri, leader of the Islamic Rebirth Party of Tajikistan
