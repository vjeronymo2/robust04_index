940212 FT 12 FEB 94 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Gentle Australian upturn as holidays quieten region Tokyo had its own holiday yesterday and this, with the Chinese new year celebrations, meant that most of the region's markets were closed. Among those which were open, activity was generally restrained. AUSTRALIA recovered from an unsteady start to close higher in spite of limited offshore buying and lower gold stocks. The All Ordinaries index added 7.1 to 2,241.4 in turnover of ADollars 659.6m, off 4 per cent on the week. News Corp came back from a session low of ADollars 9.80 to close 2 cents up at ADollars 10.16. BTR Nylex was up 13 cents at ADollars 3.26 in volume of 2.8m shares. BHP firmed 8 cents to ADollars 18.48, after falling to ADollars 18.28. Coca-Cola Amatil was one of the day's main movers, climbing 40 cents to ADollars 11.20 in volume of 1.4m shares after announcing a record annual profit of ADollars 95.4m. In golds the index shed 73.1 or 3 per cent to 2,352.3 on profit-taking. Placer Pacific fell 21 cents to ADollars 2.54, Normandy Poseidon 15 cents to ADollars 2.25 and Newcrest Mining 31 cents to ADollars 6.05. Among resource stocks, Western Mining was 10 cents down at ADollars 7.60 but off session lows after reporting a 38 per cent rise in interim net profit. NEW ZEALAND slipped back in thin turnover, with Telecom losing 7 cents to NZDollars 4.58. The NZSE-40 index lost 14.38 to 2,349.50, up 0.6 per cent over the week. Air New Zealand, which announced a profit of NZDollars 88m for the six months to December 31, ended down 3 cents at NZDollars 4.04. MANILA rose on strength in the banking sector and second-line stocks. The composite index gained 27.14 to 3,040.90, a week's improvement of nearly 1 per cent. Turnover rose to 1.2bn pesos from 973m pesos. Philippine National Bank led the gainers to close uo 4 per cent at 630 pesos. BOMBAY fell after the Calcutta stock exchange set carry forward costs at 4.0-4.5 per cent, compared with 2 per cent in Bombay. The BSE index lost 39.16 at 4,000.74 for a week's decline of 2 per cent.
