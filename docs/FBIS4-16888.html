BFN [Text] Islamabad -- Pakistan on Monday warned the Arab nationals, who fought in the Afghan war and are now living in NWFP [North-West Frontier Province] to immediately leave the country or face severe action. The government has sent a letter to all such organisations to make sure that all Arabs leave by April 18. Those staying here after April 18 May be arrested and the organisations still employing them may face punitive action. A govt official said about 120 such Arab nationals were still in NWFP, about 80 to 70 in Peshawar. Most of them are from Egypt, Algeria, Saudi Arabia and Tunisia. He said those with valid papers had already been allowed to stay, but other would have to go. "We can't allow them to use Pakistani territory for carrying out terrorist acts against other nations," the official said.
