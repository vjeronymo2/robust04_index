940222 FT 22 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Prior makes second cash call Hanson's Beazer Estates subsidiary is taking a 3.5 per cent stake in Prior, the property group and architectural ironmonger, as part consideration for the sale of two properties in Hull and Liverpool. Beazer, which has given Prior an option on a further property, has undertaken not to sell the shares for at least 12 months. Prior is also buying Bouverie House in London's Fleet Street for Pounds 11.5m from a company controlled by the family trust of Prior's chairman, Mr James Prior. It is seeking Pounds 4.55m net through the placing of 108.3m shares at 4 1/2 p. They are being made available to shareholders on 3-for-4 basis. The shares were suspended last week at 7 1/4 p. The company also announced pre-tax profits of Pounds 2.02m (losses Pounds 467,000) for the six months to September 30 as a result of the placing in May, which raised Pounds 2.37m net, allowing a release of bank indebtedness of Pounds 2.05m. Turnover was Pounds 609,000 (Pounds 596,000). Prior is buying the three properties for an aggregate Pounds 12.7m, satisfied by the issue of 32.2m shares, Pounds 2.28m cash and a loan of Pounds 9m from Century Life, which has also been granted an option for up to 4m shares at 4 1/2p.
