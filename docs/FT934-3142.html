931210 FT 10 DEC 93 / World Trade News: Australian telecoms deal Telecom Australia, the country's largest purchaser of electronic equipment, said yesterday it had placed orders worth up to ADollars 2.5bn (Dollars 1.63bn) with three European companies as part of a five-year programme to digitalise its network, Nikki Tait writes from Sydney. The beneficiaries are Alcatel of France, Germany's Siemens and Ericsson of Sweden. The French group will provide equipment worth up to ADollars 1.1bn, including local exchanges, access electronics, and a transit switching network. The Ericsson contract is worth around ADollars 850m, and will also be made up of digital switching equipment. Siemens will supply some ADollars 500m in transmission equipment. Telecom added that it is still negotiating with NorTel, the Canadian-owned manufacturer, for the supply of corporate customer switching services.
