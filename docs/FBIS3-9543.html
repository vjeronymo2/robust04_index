Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, March 24 (MTI) -- The insinuation heard recently on Hungarian Television that the Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP) would take over the media if it came to power after May"s parliamentary elections, is utterly ridiculous, MSZP cultural executive Gabor Kis Gellert said at the party"s press conference on Thursday. He said his party would appoint new heads to Hungarian Radio and Television, and would remedy legal offences like the recent dismissal of 129 Hungarian Radio staff. Gellert said television news programmes hardly ever carry reports about the MSZP. According to their survey, from March 16 to 22, Hungarian Democratic Forum [MDF] politicians appeared on 30 occasions, not counting parliamentary coverage. In this same period, one single report was carried about the MSZP, and that was about the jogging accident of MP Laszlo Bekesi, the Socialist's economic expert.
