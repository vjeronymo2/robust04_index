BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 21 April. The National Statistics Institute reported that the Bolivian trade balance registered a $445.2 million deficit in 1993 with exports at $804.1 million and imports at $1.2493 billion, a 10.5 percent increase compared with 1992. Exports of nontraditional products registered the highest increase with 27.6 percent. Hydrocarbon exports dropped 27 percent compared with 1992. (La Paz LA RAZON in Spanish 7 Apr 94 p B5) The Superintendency of Banks reported that banks' deposits totalled $2.0761 billion through 31 March, of which $1.9387 billion came from the private sector and $137.4 million from the public sector. (La Paz HOY in Spanish 7 Apr 94 Economic Section p 17) The Bolivian Central Bank reported that the fiscal deficit was $150 million in the year's first three months, a drop of 50 percent compared with the same period in 1992, when it was $305 million. International reserves went from $223.7 million in the first three months of 1993 to $368.6 million in the same period this year, an increase of 64.8 percent in 12 months. (La Paz LA RAZON in Spanish 8 Apr 94 Economic Section p B5)
