Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By Itar-Tass correspondent Sergey Startsev] [Text] Rome, 18 Feb, TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin on Thursday [17 February] sent a message to Italian Prime Minister Carlo Azeglio Ciampi, in which he told him about the efforts undertaken by Russian diplomats to unblock the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. The document confirms that Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin, special envoy of the Russian president at the talks on the settlement of the Yugoslav conflict, has managed to get the Bosnian Serbs' consent to withdraw their heavy weapons from Sarajevo. The message also states readiness to deploy a Russian UN contingent, currently in the conflict zone, in the Bosnian capital. According to sources close to the Italian Government, Rome expressed great interest in Boris Yeltsin's message. In particular, Russia's efforts aimed at achieving guarantees for the demilitarization of the zone around Sarajevo and putting it under international monitoring received high appreciation.
