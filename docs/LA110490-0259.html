November 4, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

ENTERTAINING;

CALIFORNIA PARTIES -- PAST AND FUTURE

People in California have always known how to throw a party. In this issue we celebrate the state's tradition of entertaining.

Our authors reminisce about the way things were. A granddaughter remembers the barbacoas on the last rancho in California. California's greatest cook recalls beach parties, abalone hunts and grunion runs in the '40s. In the '50s, a restaurant critic remembers waking up to find Ruby Keeler dancing in his living room. In the '60s, the best parties in town were Sunday-night suppers at the home of Roz and Gene Wyman. In the '80s, famous chefs threw potluck parties for themselves.

In the '90s, the tradition continues. Come join the party.
