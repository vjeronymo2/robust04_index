Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] President Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari and Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto have appealed to the Afghan president, Professor Borhanoddin Rabbani; the prime minister, Engineer Golboddin Hekmatyar; and all other political parties in Afghanistan to cease hostilities and observe a permanent cease-fire effective from 0600 on Friday [14 January] morning. The appeal has been made by the Pakistani leaders being encouraged by positive response to Pakistan's earlier request for a temporary cease-fire and keeping in view the brotherly ties existing between Pakistan and Afghanistan. It is also based on a (?view) to restoring peace and stability in Afghanistan. All Afghan parties have further been urged not to resume hostilities until the Islamic Conference Organization and friendly countries have had a full opportunity to effect conciliation and to resolve their differences. Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari and Ms. Benazir Bhutto have noted with alarm the situation in Afghanistan over the last 10 days and the resort to force by contending parties which have resulted in the tragic loss of life and large-scale destruction of property. In order to alleviate the sufferings of Afghan brothers and sisters affected by the recent hostilities, Pakistan has extended immediate humanitarian aid containing medicines and fully equipped field hospitals. These will be sent to Afghanistan as soon as the cease-fire is agreed. Pakistan is already actively participating in relief facilities for displaced Afghans who have left Kabul.
