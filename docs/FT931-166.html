930331 FT 31 MAR 93 / Observer: Object lesson For all Jacques Attali's vocal support for the Russians' reform programme, the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development's chairman gave signs of thinking they had gone a bit far yesterday in demonstrating their need for western aid. His visit to the country's provinces was cut short by a truck which ripped into his bullet-proof limousine. Although the crash was spectacular, the 50-year-old escaped with scratches to his face. But when his apologetic hosts blamed the accident on primitive Russian highways, he replied: 'In Stalin's time, you would no doubt have been sent to the camps for an accident involving a foreign guest. Today, all you are doing is asking an international institution for money to improve your roads.' Fortunately, by the time he dined with local officials and industrial managers in the historic town of Suzdal, he'd relented enough to promise he would return.
