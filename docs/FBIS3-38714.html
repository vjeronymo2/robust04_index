Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Coskun Kartal report from Munich over still of regional map] [Excerpt] [Passage omitted] In his short speech at the defense policies conference in Munich today, Mesut Yilmaz, leader of Turkey's main opposition Motherland Party, spoke mainly of Bosnia-Herzegovina. He compared the current NATO stand regarding Bosnia-Herzegovina to that of a charity organization such as the Red Cross. Yilmaz stressed that the Alliance must change its stand. This incident shows the extent to which the Alliance is passive, he stated. Yilmaz said: At minimum, the innocent people who are being attacked must be given the chance to procure arms to defend themselves.
