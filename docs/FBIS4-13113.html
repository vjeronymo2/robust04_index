BFN [Text] Cairo, 17 Apr (MENA) -- Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa today received a report from Assistant Foreign Minister Ambassador Sa'id Rif'at on the mission he undertook in Liberia as President Husni Mubarak's envoy. Ambassador Rif'at stated that President Mubarak entrusted him with a message to the Liberian State Council, reflecting his interest in the situation in Liberia after the various parties signed a peace agreement. The mission was within the context of Egyptian efforts to settle the Liberian problem and its backing for the State Council, the transitional government, and constitutional measures. Ambassador Rif'at added that during his visit to Liberia he met the State Council members and discussed developments in the Liberian problem and ways to settle it, with particular emphasis on disarming the factions and completing the formation of the transitional government. He pointed out that the Liberian parties reiterated their eagerness to find a settlement and form a government. The Liberian powers expressed their appreciation of the Egyptian role and reiterated their desire to see Egypt contributing to the country's reconstruction once the peace agreement is implemented, he said. Rif'at added that he assured Liberian officials of Egypt's preparedness to offer its expertise in development and to extend training assistance to Liberians in medicine, engineering, and nursing.
