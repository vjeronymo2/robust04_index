Language: 1128 Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] [No dateline as received] Measures are being taken by Uzbekistan for the stabilization of the credit-currency relations. The government of the Republic has instructed the Central Bank to strictly limit credit and cash flow. [passage omitted] The Finance Ministry and the Central Bank must, within a period of one month, formulate the mechanism for the formation and exploitation of the gold and currency reserves of the Central Bank to create a stabilizing fund and provide measures to support the course of the national currency. Enterprises with all forms of ownership, including those involved in trading operations with states of the former union, must give 15 percent of their earnings to the hard currency funds of the Republic. Specialists consider these measures adopted by the Government of the Republic of Uzbekistan to be an important stage in preparing for the introduction of the national currency that will replace the current transitional sum-coupon.
