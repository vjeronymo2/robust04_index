BFN [Excerpts] Here are the highlights of former President Jaime Paz Zamora's six-hour testimony before the Constituent Commission. [Begin recording] [Amalia Pando] In his testimony before the Constituent Commission, former President Jaime Paz Zamora declared he was both a victim of his own mistakes as well as those of the Special Antinarcotics Force [FELCN] and the Prosecutor's office. He referred to a U.S. plot that is allegedly focusing on him because his government defended coca farming at an international level and because he invited Fidel Castro to visit Bolivia. [Paz] I hope the commission agrees with me when I say that we run the risk of our ineffective struggle against drug trafficking being used within our country for political or other purposes. [passage omitted] Do you not think, when I saw the regrettable way in which the FELCN and the Prosecutor General acted, I did not wonder how they acted in previous cases and how many people may have been victims of this irresponsible, corrupt, and superficial management of our prisons throughout the country. [Pando] During his administration, Paz Zamora was asked to safeguard our sovereignty in light of the obvious intervention by the U.S. Embassy. At that time Paz Zamora talked about a sham [pacotilla] sovereignty. [Paz] We must not let the FELCN, in the name of the noble struggle against drug trafficking, become a nightmare for the citizenry as was the case of the Tonton Macoutes in Haiti. [Pando] The former president then made a series of charges which had been reported by the media throughout the four years of his administration. [Paz] Higher ranking FELCN officers, district prosecutors, and the special prosecutors for drug cases receive their salaries directly from the financial cooperation granted by the United States. This is not normal. I would say this is a typical case of cooperation -- which we need -- but which could turn into intervention. After all, you obey the one who pays you your salary. [Pando] He ended his attacks against the FELCN and the prosecutors in a categorical manner. [Paz] Nothing of what was said was true. [passage omitted] [end recording]
