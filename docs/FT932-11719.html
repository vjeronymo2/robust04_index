930501 FT 01 MAY 93 / UK Company News: Kingston Oil changes to recycling KINGSTON Oil & Gas is making a number of changes which will result it controlling a third of the waste fuel oil recycling market in the UK, DCC, a private Dublin-based company, becoming its largest single shareholder. It is also changing its name to Greenway Holdings. It is selling Kingston Oil Corporation, its US oil and gas interests for Dollars 3m (Pounds 1.9m) cash to its management and buying BCS from Emo Oil, a 75 per cent owned subsidiary of DCC, for Pounds 2.8m, satisfied by the issue of 5.6m shares representing 29.9 per cent of the enlarged capital. The company also announced 1992 pre-tax losses of Pounds 4.1m, compared with profits of Pounds 1.19m, after an exceptional charge this time of Pounds 4.49m relating to the write-down of the US interests less exchange gains on US intra-group indebtedness. Turnover was Pounds 6.09m (Pounds 6.63m). Losses per share were 32.44p (earnings 9.01p). A final dividend is not being paid, leaving the total for the year at 1p (2.5p). BCS, a waste oil reprocessor, will be merged with Orcol Fuels, Kingston's offshoot in the same business. The US write-off will result in a deficit on reserves and the company is seeking court approval to reduce the group's share capital.
