941003 FT 03 OCT 94 / Chancellor urged to simplify tax regime The Chartered Association of Certified Accountants, one of the profession's six leading bodies, today urges the chancellor to simplify the tax regime. 'The introduction of self-assessment makes it essential that the system should be as simple as possible. We urge the government to give urgent attention to this matter,' it says in its Budget submission. It also calls for the extension of tax relief on childcare costs to the self-employed as a temporary measure. This would help parents who are self-employed or without employer-provided facilities. The Institute of Chartered Accountants in Ireland has responded to the chartered association's proposal for a general accounting council for the UK and Ireland. 'The formation of another umbrella group for accountancy bodies is unlikely to be any more effective than those that already exist,' it said.
