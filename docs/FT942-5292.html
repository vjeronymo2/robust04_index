940606 FT 06 JUN 94 / Criticism of Mexican claim Mexico's main opposition parties have criticised the special prosecutor's claim that the assassin of Luis Donaldo Colosio, the ruling party's candidate for the presidential election in August, probably acted alone, writes Damian Fraser in Mexico City. Mr Miguel Montes, the government's special prosecutor, last week retreated from his contention that several people aided the assassin. Without calling for the release of three men arrested for helping, Mr Montes said: 'The hypothesis is strengthened that the murder was committed by one man alone - Mario Aburto Martnez.' Mr Diego Fernandez de Cevallos, presidential candidate of the centre-right opposition party, said: 'They should get to the bottom of this. What Montes has said so far does not satisfy me or my party.'
