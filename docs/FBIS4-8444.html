BFN [Text] Prague, April 12 (CTK) -- Deputy Foreign Minister Alexandr Vondra told the North Atlantic Assembly (NAA) representatives visiting the country that the Czech government is shortly to debate a document expounding its conception of cooperation with NATO in the framework of the Partnership for Peace. The Czech Republic joined the Partnership for Peace program in March this year. The document will cover both cooperation in peace-keeping activities, such as the present participation of a Czech contingent in the UNPROFOR [UN protection] force in ex-Yugoslavia, as well as questions of funding and legislation, he said. Vondra was speaking after his meeting with NAA Chairman Loic Bouvard and Secretary- General Peter Corterier. Parliamentary Chairman Milan Uhde told journalists it was a huge lesson in solidarity that the Czech Republic, although feeling threatened by no country, was intensively interested in collective European security.
