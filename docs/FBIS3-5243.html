Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Mar. 7 (YONHAP) -- South Korean farmers have grown less enthusiastic about their occupation following the conclusion of the Uruguay Round [UR] talks. An opinion poll taken by the National Agricultural Cooperative Federation toward 2,000 farmers at the end of last year showed on Monday that 35.1 percent of the respondents said they would reduce their farming scale to a point where they could obtain food grains of their own need only. On the other hand, 45.1 percent replied they would continue to engage in farming as now even after the Uruguay Round agreement. Those who said they would give up farming altogether accounted for 9.2 percent of the total and 10.5 percent said they would delegate their farmlands to some ones else. Those who said they would abandon farming were mostly those with tiny farmlands. Those with relatively large farmland areas said they would continue farming. Regarding the issue of whether to allow non-farmers to possess farmlands, 54.1 percent favored non-farmers' possession in the interests of the buildup of farming competitiveness. But, 30.7 percent of the questioned said they were against it because it runs counter to the traditional principle of "farmlands for farmers."
