921119 FT 19 NOV 92 / International Company News: Loss turned in by Brazilian clothing maker SAO PAULO Alpargatas, the largest Brazilian footwear and clothing manufacturer, suffered a loss of Dollars 64m for the first nine months this year. The company blamed the deficit mainly on lower sales volume and tight profit margins, both a result of the country's continued recession. Gross sales figures for the period were Dollars 361m, compared with Dollars 425m for the first nine months of last year. In 1991, the publicly traded company landed in the red for the first time in 56 years, showing a loss of Dollars 38m.
