Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian economic developments monitored by Panama Bureau through 17 January; source follows in parentheses after each item. The Treasury is easily losing nearly 1 trillion pesos in uncollected taxes per year due to contraband, which represents a little more than 25 percent of the country's total annual imports. Cigarettes are the leading contraband product. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL NUEVO SIGLO in Spanish 6 Jan 94 pp 1, 17) Colombian exports increased only by 2 percent from January to September 1993, totaling $5.269 billion. A huge reduction in the export of coffee, oil, fuels, and other traditional products are to blame for the poor showing. A 13.5-percent increase in nontraditional exports saved the situation, keeping the export percentages from falling into the negative side. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 7 Jan 94 p 2b) Colombia and the UK have made significant progress in negotiating an investment agreement. Foreign Minister Noemi Sanin has said she hopes the negotiations will conclude in two months; in addition, the Colombian foreign minister has signed a technical cooperation agreement to modernize the state. According to the agreement, the UK will contribute 130,000 pounds and Colombia will contribute 144 million pesos to a program that seeks to improve management in government institutions. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 12 Jan 94 p 7a) The manufacturing industry grew by 5.69 percent from January to October 1993, according to the National Administrative Department of Statistics. The production of transportation equipment and material increased by 49.5 percent. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 12 Jan 94 p 6b)
