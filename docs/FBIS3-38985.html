Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] In Geneva, the foreign ministers of the seven countries bordering the former Yugoslavia discussed for five hours the international consequences of the sanctions against Serbia and Montenegro and the most suitable way to prepare the rebuilding of the Balkans -- all this while the war is still raging. Some of those taking part spoke about the climate of the day's meeting. Foreign Minister Andreatta: [Begin Andreatta recording] We discussed the rebuilding of a Balkan order. Even though our overall reading of the Yugoslav affair was not in perfect alignment, our chief cause for concern, which we all shared, was the possible creation of a dominant military power.
