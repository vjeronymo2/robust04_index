930407 FT 07 APR 93 / International Company News: Dutch paper group down 75% KNP BT, the big Dutch paper and packaging group created out of a three-way merger last month, saw net profits plummet by nearly 75 per cent in 1992, underlying the difficult market conditions. Combined pro forma net profit for the three merger partners - the Dutch companies KNP, Buhrmann-Tetterode and VRG - fell to Fl 116m (Dollars 64.6m) from Fl 443m in 1991. If extraordinary items are excluded, net profit declined by 64 per cent to Fl 175m from Fl 489m a year earlier. Overall, group operating profit was down 42 per cent at Fl 538m. Sales increased by 2.2 per cent to Fl 11.76bn.
