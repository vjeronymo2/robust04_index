920219 FT 19 FEB 92 / International Capital Markets: SEC proposes easing rules for small companies THE Securities and Exchange Commission, the US watchdog, plans to unveil regulatory changes next month designed to make it easier for small companies to raise money in the US securities markets, writes Patrick Harverson. Mr Richard Breeden, chairman of the SEC, told the National Press Club in Washington DC yesterday, of some of the proposals to be unveiled in early March for a period of public comment. They include: Allowing small companies to issue up to Dollars 1m in unrestricted securities and to canvas the public for investor interest. Raising the ceiling for companies eligible to use a simplified disclosure document for share offerings. A new provision permitting small companies to file what Mr Breeden called 'junior' quarterly and year-end reports. Creating a new filing document for share offerings by companies below a specified size.
