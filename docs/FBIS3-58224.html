Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] [Announcer] We return to the center of attention in the world today: the NATO summit. [passage omitted] The Atlantic alliance's summit has already accepted the Partnership for Peace proposed by the U.S. -- a half-way formula to satisfy desires for security, although the East European countries consider it still not enough. The Balkans war is the other pressing question at this NATO summit and, in this regard, a toughening of the sanctions against the Serbs has already been agreed on, with the repeated threat of air strikes if the attacks do not cease. Meanwhile, Spain has succeeded in getting security in the Mediterranean on the agenda of the summit. Spanish Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez is in Brussels. Our correspondent Nacho Hernandez also reports from there. Good afternoon. [Hernandez] Good afternoon. In his speech Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez emphasized the points contained in this declaration: The importance of strengthening the transatlantic link and support for the initiative on Partnership for Peace. Spain thinks that this formula for the former members of the Warsaw Pact does not mean automatic application of Article V of NATO, which establishes immediate response by the Allies in the case of an attack. Foreign Minister Javier Solana: [Begin Solana recording] We are all fighting for greater stability in Europe and logically in face of potential focuses of instability we must act. [end recording] [Hernandez] In the [draft] final communique of this summit there is a reaffirmation of the decision adopted by NATO in August according to which allied aircraft would bomb military positions to end the siege of Sarajevo under the command of the UN Security Council. The sanctions will also be strengthened and there is talk of determination to put an end to the obstacles facing the UN Forces in Bosnia. It is not acceptable, the foreign minister said, for the blue helmets to have difficulties in carrying out their humanitarian aid missions. Of great interest to Spain is the declaration included on stability in the Mediterranean. We reiterate, it says, that security in Europe is affected by security in the Mediterranean. Dialogue with the countries of the region will be promoted. Efforts directed to strengthening security in this region must be increased, the final declaration adds.
