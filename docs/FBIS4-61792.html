BFN [Text] Riga, May 10, BNS -- Latvian Socialist party organized Monday [9 May] a rally to mark victory over Germany in World War II. Nearly 2,000 people took part at the meeting at the Victory monument. The participants of the meeting recalled battles, commemorated the victims of the war, and they invited war veterans to fight for their rights. They remembered the "good times" of the communist regime, regretted their having supported "perestroyka", and protested against "capitalism in Latvia." They claimed that presently there is ethnic inequality in Latvia, and they were dissatisfied that only the citizens of Latvia are allowed to take part a the local governments elections. The participants of the meeting said they hoped that the agreement between Latvia and Russia on retired Russian militaries' social guarantees, signed on April 30 in Moscow, would improve their life in Latvia.
