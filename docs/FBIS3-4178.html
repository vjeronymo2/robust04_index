Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 28 (AFP) -- China has refused to pressure North Korea into dropping its nuclear weapons program, saying it was against U.S.-South Korean military exercises and the deployment of Patriot missiles in the South. "China is not in favour of any action or measures detrimental to the maintenance of peace and relaxation on the Korean peninsula, whether it was military exercise or deployment of missiles," said a Chinese foreign ministry spokesman told journalists. [sentence as received] The spokesman, Shen Guofang, said China hoped the nuclear issue "shall be settled through patient and constructive talks between the parties directly concerned." He made the remarks following a crucial meeting between South Korean President Kim Yong-sam and Communist Party General Secretary Jiang Zemin here Monday.
