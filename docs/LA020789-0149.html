February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Late Final Edition

WORLD;

JAPAN PARTY CHIEF QUITS IN SCANDAL

Japan's stock-profiteering scandal claimed yet another prominent casualty today as the chief of an opposition party resigned over his lucrative dealings with the company at the heart of the affair.

Saburo Tsukamoto, 61, chairman of the Democratic Socialist Party, stepped down amid pressure from party members who want to distance the party from the scandal involving the Recruit group of companies. He said he was not guilty of any wrongdoing but was quitting for the party's sake. Three Cabinet members have resigned because of links to the affair, as have more than a dozen other government officials, business leaders and media executives.
