BFN [Text] Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov stated in Ankara that Bulgaria does not oppose the participation of Turkish troops in the UN peacekeeping forces in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Ending two days of talks with his host, Turkish Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin, Daskalov stressed that regional countries in principle should not send their troops on such delicate missions. Cetin and Daskalov expressed the concern of their governments over the continuation of the war in Bosnia, and called on the international community to put in an added effort in order to end the Bosnia-Herzegovina drama. The two sides also expressed their commitment to ending the embargo against Bosnia-Herzegovina. According to domestic media reports, Ministers Cetin and Daskalov said this should be done as soon as possible.
