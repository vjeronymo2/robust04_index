Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Lima, 23 Jan (EFE) -- The attitude of Alexander Watson -- U.S. under secretary of state for Inter-American Affairs -- during his recent visit to Peru was considered paternalistic in an editorial published today by EXPRESO, a Lima daily. In a critical tone, the editorial noted that the U.S. high- ranking government official who was in Lima this week on a three-day official visit "came and took notes -- as if he had a superior right -- as to whether we have `progressed' in democracy, justice administration, and human rights." The daily considers that Watson gave the impression of encouraging those who object to "the very existence of (President Alberto Fujimori's) government and the new Constitution," making reference to the meeting the under secretary of state held with opposition political sectors. "It is not a way to create a climate of democratic consensus that will close the gap opened on 5 April. It is not enough to call for a dialogue," EXPRESO comments in reference to the 5 April 1992 coup staged by Fujimori in which he dissolved Congress and stripped the Judicial Branch, while he assumed total power. EXPRESO -- which supports the government -- adds that Watson admitted that in Peru progress had been made with respect to human rights and democracy. Nevertheless, he had said "there is still much more to do."
