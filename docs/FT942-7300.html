940526 FT 26 MAY 94 / Infolink survey sees fall in consumer debt arrears Consumer debt arrears are falling, according to a survey published today by Infolink, the credit information group. The survey found 7.6 per cent of instalment credit accounts in arrears in the first quarter of 1994, compared with 10.1 per cent in the first quarter of 1993. The proportion of accounts significantly in arrears (three months or over) fell from 4 per cent to 3.6 per cent over the same period. Infolink said the regions worst hit by the recession, the south-east and the south-west, had shown particular improvement. Scotland had the greatest credit arrears. The region with the lowest level of repayment problems is Northern Ireland, where only 6.4 per cent of agreements are in arrears. Wales is the next best, at a level of 7.1 per cent. Dr Brian Bailey, Infolink's chairman, said: 'Falling unemployment levels and low interest rates appear to have boosted confidence and increased consumers' ability to meet financial commitments.'
