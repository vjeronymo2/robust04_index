931209 FT 09 DEC 93 / Poor interest on shares push The government's push for wider and deeper share ownership in the UK shows only modest success, according to a survey for the Treasury. The NOP survey shows that 10m people, or 22 per cent of adults, own shares, compared with 21.5 per cent in December last year. At the peak in 1991 there were 11m shareholders in the UK compared with only 3m in 1979, before the wave of privatisations started. The survey was based on interviews with 7,592 adults. Share Ownership 1993. Publications Unit, HM Treasury, Parliament St, London SW1P 3AG, Pounds 25.
