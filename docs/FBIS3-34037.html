Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, 3 Feb (INA)--A second round of talks lasting two and half hours between Iraq and the UN Special Commission held at the Foreign Ministry was concluded at noon today. In a statement to the IRAQI NEWS AGENCY after this round of talks, Rolf Ekeus said: "We are continuing our talks on future steps. I cannot elaborate any further on the issue, since the talks are continuing. We are holding working sessions." Ekeus spoke to reporters on his arrival at the al-Rashid Hotel, where he is staying, expressing hope that progress will be made. He added: I am convinced that we will make progress. We will continue our discussions tomorrow. It is premature to provide details about the talks. The first round of talks between Iraq and the Special Commission was held here last night.
