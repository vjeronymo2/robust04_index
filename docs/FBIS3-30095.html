Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hong Kong, Feb. 25 (YONHAP) -- China has invited North Korean President Kim Il-song to visit Beijing some time this year after March, a month when top South Korean and Japanese leaders would visit China, the SOUTH CHINA MORNING POST reported on Friday. The daily, quoting Chinese diplomatic sources, said Kim Chong-il, the heir to and son of Kim Il-song, would also visit China before summer this year. The sources observed it is almost certain that Kim Il-song would accept the Chinese invitation. The paper quoted the sources as saying that China is inviting the top leaders of South and North Korea and Japan in a move to demonstrate its influence in Northeast Asia. It seems Kim Il-song would ask for China's economic aid and cooperation and also its support for Kim Chong-il as the next ruler of North Korea, the POST said. Kim Il-song is also interested in meeting China's paramount leader Deng Xiaoping to affirm the "lips and teeth relations" between China and North Korea, said the paper adding that Deng would agree to meet Kim on a private basis.
