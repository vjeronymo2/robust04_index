Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, January 14 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The state security organs should not play a role of political police, announces the new chief of the State Security Central Department, Lieutenant Colonel D. Sandag in interview with correspondent of the newspaper ARDIYN ERH. D. Sandag notes that the functions of the department are intelligence and counterintelligence work, the state guarding. The department should not be engaged in other activity, except the mentioned functions, says D. Sandag. He considers expedient to revise the law on state security organs, which was adopted in 1992. This law, he notes, was based on the old structures, methods and forms. Of activity of the previous state security organs, D. Sandag considers as necessary promotion of young people, the raising of their qualification. Answering questions of the correspondent, D. Sandag stresses that those working at state security organs as military servicemen should observe a strict discipline, abstain from belonging to any party, as it is envisaged by the law. In this sense, he says, forces in opposition should not exist in state security organs. Concerning the statement by Colonel L. Sanjaasuren on intentions to liquidate the materials about abuse of power and bribery by some high officials, which are kept in the State Security Department, D. Sandag notes that, in his view, such serious violations do not exist, and that the accusations brought might be based on a striving for winning authority, by using the differences of political forces. D. Sandag notes that the issues, which were touched upon by Colonel L. Sanjaasuren, are being now checked up by a special commission of the parliament and the public will be get acquainted with results in its time [sentence as received]. In D. Sandag's view, the recent changes of the leadership of the State Security Central Department have been connected with shortcomings in their activity.
