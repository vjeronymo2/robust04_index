930929 FT 29 SEP 93 / Observer: Benn on Benson Labour MPs may whinge about their colleagues from the Conservative party having a monopoly on City sinecures, but one can perhaps see why. Ejected from Labour's national executive at the beginning of the week, Tony Benn was yesterday affecting a distinctly shaky grasp of demarcations in the British financial world. Deploring supposedly highfalutin media reporting of economic affairs and pointing out that pensioners are not in the habit of dumping their D-Marks after the TV evening news, Benn lamented how 'when there is a crisis, people always go and ask some chartered accountant at Kleinwort Benson'. Merchant bankers KB yesterday owned up to 51 accountants on the pay roll 'any of whom would be most flattered to be consulted in an economic crisis'.
