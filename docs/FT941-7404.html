940225 FT 25 FEB 94 / Retailers hit by cartel ruling The German cartel office ruled yesterday that Karstadt and Hertie, two of Germany's biggest retail groups, must prune their operations before merging, writes Ariane Genillard in Bonn. The two retailers must dispose of four operations in audio equipment to avoid 'excessive market positions' in Berlin, Hamburg, Munich and Schleswig-Holstein. The cartel office said market positions in home textiles, perfumes, cosmetics, audio equipment and toys would have to be decreased to 'a level not harmful to competition. Karstadt, Germany's biggest store chain with 1992 sales of DM20.6bn (Pounds 8bn), plans to buy Hertie Waren and Kaufhaus for DM2.7bn.
