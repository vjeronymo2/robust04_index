BFN [Text] The national chairman of ULIMO [United Liberation Movement for Democracy in Liberia], Alhaji Koromah, has ordered the release of the five ECOMOG [Economic Community of West African States Cease-Fire Monitoring Group] soldiers of the Nigerian contingent. The ULIMO chairman issued a release order yesterday to his field commander, General (Mohamed Dumbula), following discussion on the situation between him and ECOMOG Field Commander John Inienger and his deputy, Colonel Kasala Camara. A ULIMO press release issued last night says Gen. Koromah said that the decision to release the five ECOMOG soldiers was based on appreciation for Nigeria's sacrificial role in resolving the Liberian conflict. He said the ULIMO High Military Council of which he is the chairman endorsed the decision in a spirit of furthering the speedy implementation of the Liberian peace process under the Cotonou Accord.
