940416 FT 16 APR 94 / Theft of notes from Bank denied Mrs Christine Gibson, accused of being the prime mover in the theft of banknotes from the Bank of England's used notes destruction depot at Debden, Essex, yesterday denied in the High Court that she had smuggled out Pounds 20 and Pounds 50 notes in her clothing. The Bank is suing three couples for Pounds 600,000. Mrs Gibson is being sued with her husband Peter for more than Pounds 300,000. She was never prosecuted for theft. All six deny taking the money. The hearing was adjourned to Monday.
