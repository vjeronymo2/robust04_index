BFN [Text] Cairo, 4 Apr (MENA)--Official Palestinian sources have stated that the first group of political deportees, whose return Israel has approved, will leave Cairo for the Gaza Strip tomorrow, Tuesday. Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin announced earlier that Israel has consented to the return of 48 deportees. Their return is part of the confidence-building measures between the Palestinian and Israeli peoples after the signing of the Declaration of Principles in Washington on 13 December 1993. [In a related, Tunis-datelined report, Paris AFP in English at 1734 GMT on 4 April adds the following: "Seventeen Palestinian exiles left here Monday bound for their homes in the occupied Gaza Strip and West Bank. ["The 17 were part of a group of 46 Palestinians allowed to return under an Israeli-PLO security accord signed in Cairo last Thursday. They will travel via Jordan and Egypt. ["Six of the Palestinians flew in to Tunis earlier Monday. ["The 11 others had lived in Tunisia since being expelled for belonging to the mainstream Fatah faction led by Palestine Liberation Organisation Chairman Yasir 'Arafat and participating in the intifadah, or uprising, in the occupied territories. ["Five will return to the West Bank from Jordan via the Allenby bridge and the other 12 will enter Gaza from Egypt."]
