Among the 1,477 credit unions charging more than 15 percent for unsecured loans, there are 356 credit unions with 20 percent or more of their assets in this kind of loan. For these credit unions, lowering their rates would damage their liquidity, capital, earnings, and growth. Table 3 shows credit unions charging more than 15 percent that have more than 20 percent of their assets in these loans. In general the percent of assets in unsecured loans goes down as credit union size goes up.
