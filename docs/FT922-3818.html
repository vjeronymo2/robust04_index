920609 FT 09 JUN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Experts appointed to lead inquiry THE Department of Trade and Industry adopted its normal practice with investigations yesterday by appointing two experts to examine Mirror Group Newspapers - a lawyer and an accountant. Mr John Laugharne Thomas QC, aged 44, who is based at 4 Essex Court in the Temple, London, specialises in a wide range of commercial and international law in one of the leading commercial sets, or practices. Mr Thomas was called to the Bar in 1969. He was appointed a Queen's Counsel in 1984, and in 1987 became a recorder on the Wales and Chester circuit. He has been a visiting faculty fellow in maritime law at the University of Southampton for the past two academic years. Mr Raymond Turner, aged 56, a senior partner with Neville Russell, a firm of accountants with many insurance-sector clients, was previously part of the team investigating BOM Holdings Group, a DTI probe launched in February 1990 and which reported earlier this year. He has also previously worked on a number of confidential DTI investigations. His other appointments have included being liquidator to CR Driver, a small insurance underwriting firm closely linked with London United Investments, the insurance group that collapsed in May 1990.
