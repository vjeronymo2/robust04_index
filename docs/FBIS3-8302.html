Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Belgrade, March 7 (TANJUG)--Serbia's Deputy Commissioner for Refugees Slobodan Popovic said on Monday that Germany's decision to expell refugees who hold Yugoslav passports was uncalled for, in view of the fact that the refugee status is regulated under international law which protects refugees everywhere in the world. In a statement made to TANJUG, Popovic specified that, since the outbreak of war in former Yugoslavia, between 17,000 and 20,000 refugees from the breakaway Yugoslav Republics of Bosnia-Herzegovina and Croatia have transited through Yugoslavia (Serbia/Montenegro) on their way to Germany as well as to America, Asia and Australia. "German authorities appear to see the refugees in only one light--viz, as Yugoslavs," Popovic said. Germany has announced its intention to begin expelling the refugees from Serbia and Montenegro in three days' time. The local press estimates that the decision will affect about 200,000 refugees, mainly Bosnian Serbs and ethnic Albanians from the Serbian province of Kosovo-Metohija -- holders of Yugoslav passports. The Serbian commissioner for refugees believes that this unprecedented move on the part of Germany is meant to provoke a reaction from Yugoslavia which would then be taken as a pretext to continue to pursue the present anti-Yugoslav policy. The German weekly DER SPIEGEL estimates there are about 350,000 refugees from former Yugoslavia in Germany, 230,000 of whom have come from Serbia and Montenegro. This number obviously includes Serbs from Bosnia-Herzegovina and Croatia who have refused to renounce their Yugoslav citizenship at the time of the disintegration of the Balkan federation and take the citizenship of the newly-proclaimed states.
