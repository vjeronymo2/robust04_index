Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the 16 March Romanian press review] [Excerpt] Bucharest ROMPRES, 16/3/1994 -- "Irredentist Fangs in Bucharest and in Transylvania" is the headline in daily ADEVARUL of the set of reports on the observance of the anniversary of the 1848 revolution by the ethnic Hungarian in Romania. The matter is covered by all newspapers that emphasize the attempt of the Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania [UDMR] Deputy Imre Borbely to make an address in Hungarian in Romania's Parliament. "To speak Hungarian before the Chamber of Deputies means an official offense to the country and to the fellow parliamentarians," writes daily CRONICA ROMANA, noting that "this characteristic rudeness of Mr. Borbely, a UDMR hardliner, (...) serves only those accused of nationalism (...) and we must admit that in such situations they are right, because one needs not be a nationalist to feel offended in one's own home, in one's own country, and in one's own parliament." Daily ORA covers the visit paid to Transylvania by high state officials of Hungary in order to celebrate together with the Hungarian minority there the memory of the 1848 revolution. "The Hungarian Government's gesture to celebrate at high level the event that was a happy one for them and a tragical one for us, is impolite and this is an understatement (...). It is offending the Romanians' feelings" daily ORA writes. "The Hungarian revisionism is behaving as if the Hungarian empire existed, temporarily subjected to different, unjust laws, but still an empire, consequently the government has to participate in the festivities in its eternal provinces, irrespective of the present situation (...) The Hungarian revisionism tries in every possible way [to achieve] Romania's isolation from the community of European states. In an isolated Romania, subject to the quarantine of civilization, the difficult mission has been assumed by the UDMR. In Romania's Parliament there is a party hostile to Romania, that despises the Romanian nation." [passage omitted]
