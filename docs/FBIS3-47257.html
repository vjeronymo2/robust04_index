Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 7 (YONHAP) -- The National Defense Ministry reported on Friday it had established a defense system improvement committee to work out ways to improve military arms procurement system so as to prevent the recurrence of incidents similar to the recent arms procurement scam. Lt. Gen. Chang Song, superintendent of the military academy, was named to head the committee, a ministry official said. The committee, to go into operation on Jan. 10, will have two subcommittees -- one on the improvement of the Yulgok project and the other on the improvement of military supply procurement system. The former will be chaired by Maj. Gen. Kil Hyong-bo and the latter by Brig. Gen. Yu Song-u. Defense Minister Yi Byong-tae, naming Lt. Gen. Chang as committee chairman, told him to oversee committee business on a full-time basis, leaving military academy duties to the cadet corps commander, the official said. The committee will be operated through the end of February and its membership number 15 -- eight for the Yulgok project subcommittee and seven for the supply procurement one. Members have been picked up from among logistics experts at the ministry, Joint Chiefs of Staff, tri-service headquarters and defense institutes, the official added.
