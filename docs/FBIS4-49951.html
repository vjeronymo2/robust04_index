BFN [Text] Shandong Province has vigorously strengthened the building of infrastructure facilities by giving consideration to the interests of foreign traders, thus creating a fine environment for foreign countries to run plants and do business in the province. Over the past several years, thanks to the implementation of the strategy of promoting the development of the entire economy with an export-oriented economy, Shandong's opening up has extended from eastern coastal areas to western inland areas, and the new pattern of opening to all directions and levels has taken shape basically. Thus far, eight cities and prefectures and 56 counties, cities, and districts have been delimited as the coastal economic open region, with its area accounting for 40 percent of the province's total territory, its population accounting for 38 percent of the province's total, and its GNP accounting for 61.8 percent of the province's total. Now, this coastal economic open region has become one of the largest open regions in China's eastern coastal areas. Qingdao, Yantai, and Weihai economic and technological development zones have given rise to the new framework of modernized cities. Nine high and new technology industrial development zones in Jinan, Weihai, and some other localities have begun to take shape and become the areas where high science and technology industries develop in a concentrated manner. According to statistics, in 1993 the province set a record high in utilizing foreign funds, with the foreign funds utilized equal to the total in the previous 14 years. In 1993, the number of newly approved projects using foreign funds surpassed 8,000, the amount of contracted foreign funds reached $7.55 billion, and the foreign funds actually utilized reached $2.26 billion, respectively showing an increase of 73 percent, 81 percent, and 83 percent over 1992. Among the projects using foreign funds, the projects with optimized structure, high grades, large amount of investment, advanced technology, and close connections with industries increased obviously. Of them, the projects using more than $10 million each numbered 257. Thus far, 65 countries and regions in the world have invested in Shandong, and the foreign investment sphere has extended to commerce, retail sales, and finance. Yamaguchi Bank in Japan and (Huifeng) Bank in Hong Kong have opened their banking organs in Qingdao. (Xiri) Bank and (Rixin) Stock Company in Japan, (Baosheng) Corporation in Hong Kong, (Bada) Corporation in the Britain, and the Long-Term Credit Bank in the ROK have established representative offices in Shandong.
