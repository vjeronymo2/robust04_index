BFN [By BUSINESS-TASS] [Text] Moscow June 7 TASS -- Moscow Mayor Yuriy Luzhkov has canceled his instruction of April 1, 1994, which banned registration of joint stock societies in Moscow, the press service of the Russian State Committee for Property reports today. Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin sent a letter to Luzhkov on May 27 to demand cancellation of the illegal instruction within three days. The mayor told a news conference on June 3 he does not plan to change his instructions and "will gladly go to the procurator's office" to prove the correctness and lawfulness of "special privatization" in the Russian capital. "The cancellation proves that Yuriy Luzhkov has admitted gross violations of Russian legislation, presidential decrees and government resolutions on privatization. The Federal Committee for State Property Management is ready to help Luzhkov in restoration of lawful privatization in the city of Moscow," says a statement of the press service.
