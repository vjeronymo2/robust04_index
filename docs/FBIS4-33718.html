BFN [Article by Joao Baptista Natali] [Excerpts] The 170 or so radio stations linked to the Catholic Church will, next January, begin joint transmissions of part of their programs. They will transmit through the Igreja-Sat distribution system operated by Brasil-Sat (Brazilian Satellite Telecommunications). The system will use the satellite channel that was utilized by Radio Aparecida, from Apareida do Norte (Sao Paulo). The decision to install a Catholic radio broadcasting network was quietly made in April, during a general assembly of CNBB (National Conference of Brazilian Bishops). On 23 April, bishops and advisers of the Igreja-Sat Executive Group decided to create a foundation to manage the radio system [passage omitted] Father Cesar Moreira was designated president of the Igreja-Sat system. He also heads Unda (wave, in Latin), a church organization linked with electronic media. [passage omitted]
