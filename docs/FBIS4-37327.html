BFN [Text] Cairo, 19 Jun (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa said after his meeting with the Yemeni delegation that Lakhdar Brahimi, special representative of the UN secretary general, is currently making contacts with the parties involved in the Yemeni dispute and that these contacts will continue all day. He added that today's meeting was held to exchange views and listen to the Yemenis' viewpoint. He pointed out that the Egyptian contacts are independent of those being made by Brahimi and that they aim to spare Yemen the misfortunes of a continued war and help establish a cease-fire and begin a dialogue. Musa said that a north Yemeni delegation will arrive in Cairo today. Touching on his meeting with U.S. President Bill Clinton's envoy, he said that it concerned the envoy's special mission in Sudan. He added that views were exchanged on the situation there. Musa explained that Egypt's stance is clear and that it emphasizes the need to preserve Sudan's territorial integrity, despite our political differences with that country. However, preserving Sudan's territorial integrity is an important matter that is part of mutual respect and safeguarding the rights of all the Sudanese people, he added.
