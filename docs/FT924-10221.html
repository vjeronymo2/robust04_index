921102 FT 02 NOV 92 / International Company News: N of England Bldg Society lending up 26% In the six months ended June 30 1992 the North of England Building Society increased its gross mortgage lending to Pounds 172.4m, a rise of almost 26 per cent over the same period of 1991. But the society said there was strong competition for retail funds from National Savings, and net retail receipts fell substantially, from Pounds 24m to Pounds 13.5m. Provision for loan losses were Pounds 400,000, but remained significantly lower than many other competitors, the society claimed. At June 30 the assets stood at Pounds 1.36bn, an increase of 11per cent over the six months. Liquidity ratio dropped from 17.77 per cent to 15.8 per cent. Net profits for the period were Pounds 5.22m (Pounds 4.86m).
