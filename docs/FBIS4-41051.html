BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vadim Byrkin] [Text] Moscow, 22 Jun -- Having the tendency to further aggravation was the description of the situation on the Tajik-Afghan frontier given at the press bureau of the Russian border guards where the ITAR-TASS correspondent inquired today. According to the border guards' reports, the movement of groups of fighters from the provinces of Konduz and Takhor in Afghanistan toward Badakhshan continues, for the purpose of further dispatch to the territory of Tajikistan. By 22 June the number of fighters has reached some 900 men. The personnel of the Pyanj, Moscow, and Khorog frontier detachments have since 18 June, in five instances prevented illegal crossings of armed gangs of Tajikistan's Islamic Revival Movement into the territory of the Republic. Leaders of the irreconcilable Tajik opposition and field commanders have been given instructions to mount a series of armed and diversionary operations against the subunits of the Pyanj and Moscow frontier detachments, and also against the civilian population of the frontier regions of Tajikistan. At the press bureau of the Russian border guards it was reported that on the territory of Gorno-Badakhshan Autonomous Oblast, in the past few days narcotics traders have twice attempted to blockade the frontier detachment's base so as to provoke the Russian border guards into taking counter measures. The border guards are taking the necessary measures to prevent any provocations or cases of frontier violations and are prepared to act with utmost decisiveness.
