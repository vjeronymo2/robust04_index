BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 12 April. Data collected by the Foreign Investment Superintendency, Siex, and the National Committee for the Promotion of Foreign Investments indicate German companies made $43.6 million in direct investments in Venezuela in 1993. Sven Heldt, president of the Venezuelan-German Chamber of Commerce and Industry, has said most investments focused on the chemical and pharmaceutical areas. The main investors were Hoechst ($24.9 million), Bayer ($7.5 million), Siemens ($4.6 million), Schering ($3.5 million), Wella ($ .8 million), Fichtel and Sachs ($ .6 million), and Viex Anstalt ($ .6 million) (Caracas EL REPORTE DE LA ECONOMIA in Spanish 5 April 94 p 22) The most recent Siex figures indicate foreign investments reached $3.03 million in March 1994 compared to $30 million in March 1993, a 90-percent drop. The accumulated investment for 1994 is $21.867 million compared to $33 million for the first three months of 1993, a 30-percent drop. The major contributors of foreign capital are Denmark, the United States, the Netherlands, Switzerland, and Saint Martin. The manufacturing sector, with $13.5 million, was the main recipient of foreign capital in the first three months of 1994. Transportation, storage, and communications also received major investments. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 7 April 94 p E-8) The Venezuelan Petroleum Corporation on 5 April announced a price increase of its crudes and other products as of 6 April. Heavy crudes increased by 50 cents, light crudes by $1 per barrel. Other products increased by 2 to 3 cents per gallon. Gasoline increased by $2.50, jet fuel by 3 cents, and distilled products by 2 cents. The decision stems from an increase in international petroleum prices and an adjustment in the price of Venezuelan petroleum to the price established by the international market. This increase established the price of Venezuelan export petroleum at $12.05 per barrel. (Caracas EL DIARIO DE CARACAS in Spanish 7 April 94 p 15) The Central Bank has reported Venezuela paid $783.5 million of its foreign debt during the second half of 1993. Of that figure, 5.4 percent was used to pay capital, the rest to pay interest. Aggregate domestic demand dropped by 5.6 percent in real terms during the second semester of 1993 due to a 1.2-percent drop in private consumption and 4.1-percent drop in government consumption. Investment dropped by 3.3 percent in real terms due to a 12.5-percent drop in public investment. In contrast, private investment grew by 13.4 percent, fueled by demand from the construction sector. Hotels and restaurants, commerce, agriculture, construction, and manufacturing were the most affected areas. The Central Bank has reported the domestic public debt amounted to $223.641 million bolivares during the second half of 1993 in comparison to $156.546 million over the first half. Treasury bills totaled 30.7 million bolivares during the first half of 1993 compared to 66.08 million in 1993 over the second half. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 10 April 94 p E-8) Banco Latino spokesmen have reported this bank increased its cash savings and deposits by 6.19 percent between 4 to 7 April, the first week following its reopening. Deposits and savings amounted to 84.961 million bolivares as of 7 April. (Caracas REPORTE DE LA ECONOMIA in Spanish 11 April 94 p 3)
