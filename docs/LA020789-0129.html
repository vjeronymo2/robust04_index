February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

CAR CRASH KILLS VICTORVILLE MAYOR, INJURES WIFE

Victorville Mayor Jeff Goodwill was killed and his wife critically injured in a head-on collision with a driver attempting to pass a mini-van on a two-lane desert highway, authorities said Monday.

California Highway Patrol Officer Robert Huizinga said no one was charged in connection with the death Sunday of Goodwill, 41, but that an investigation was continuing because the third vehicle continued on after the crash.

Goodwill's wife, Deborah K. Nagle-Goodwill, 37, who was driving a 1982 Volkswagen that collided with a 1978 Dodge van about 20 miles east of Victorville, was flown by helicopter to San Bernardino County Medical Center where she was listed in critical condition. The driver of the van that was attempting to pass, Ben Owen, 66, of Lancaster, was listed in critical condition at Victor Valley Community Hospital.

Goodwill, a lifelong resident of Victorville and principal of High Desert High School, was elected mayor of the San Bernardino County community of 30,500 people last April.

"We are all in shock," said Victorville City Clerk Marian Nygaard. "All meetings of the City Council, Planning Commission and advisory committees have been canceled this week."

George Davis, superintendent of the Victor Valley Union High District, added: "We are flying flags at half mast at each school in the district. Jeff was a unique and deeply committed man who touched a lot of lives in this area."

The crash occurred at 4:30 p.m. Sunday on El Mirage Road when Owen's westbound van tried to pass a mini-van driven by Gregory Fox, 37, of Palmdale, Huizinga said.

"The Owen and Goodwill vehicles struck head-on," Huizinga said. "The vehicle that was being passed continued on."

Huizinga said Fox acknowledged that he was driving the mini-van when the accident occurred.

Attempts to reach Fox were unsuccessful.
