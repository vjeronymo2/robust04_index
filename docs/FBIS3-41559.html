Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Nov 19 KYODO -- Tokyo bay's water quality improved significantly in 1992, with the environmental quality standard increasing 10.5 percentage points over the previous year to 73.7 percent, Environment Agency officials said Friday. The officials cited improved sewage systems along side rivers flowing into the bay. They also said the sewage treatment rate improved to around 65 percent in fiscal 1990 compared with 43 percent in fiscal 1979. The agency surveyed chemical oxygen demand as the indicator of water pollution in the bay. The Tokyo Bay assessment was released Friday as part of an annual nationwide survey of water pollution in lakes, rivers and seas. The quality standard in western Japan's Seto Inland Sea stood at 78.2 percent in 1992, the same as the previous year, the survey said. The national average quality standard in sea areas hit 80.9 percent, a slight increase of 0.7 percentage point over the previous year, according to the survey. The average river water quality standard stood at the same level as the previous year, 75.4 percent, it said. The quality standard for lakes and marshes improved by 2.3 percentage points to 44.6 percent, the survey reported.
