BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 21 April. Bilateral trade between Chile and South Korea rose from $100 million in 1985 to more than $750 million in 1993. This is equivalent to 4 percent of Chilean foreign trade. The trade balance with this country registered a $70 million surplus with exports at $413 million and imports at $343 million. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 31 Mar 94 p 25) Industrial Development Association President Pedro Lizana on 30 March reported that industry grew 4.8 percent in the year's first two months compared with the same period in 1993, when it increased 9.2 percent. Leather products, except shoes, registered the highest increase with 34.6 percent: furniture and accessories, 28.7 percent; and nonelectrical machinery, 28.7 percent. (Santiago LA TERCERA in Spanish 31 Mar 94 p 3) Benedito Carlos de Padua, Real Bank Foreign Trade Department director, said Brazilian imports from Chile between 1991 and 1993 dropped from $528 million to $407 million, while imports from the Common Market of the South, Mercosur, member countries totalled $3.334 billion last year, an increase of 47 percent. De Padua said Mercosur became a very efficient channel for medium and small Brazilian enterprises. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 5 Apr 94 p B5) The Concon Oil Refinery reported that internal oil production dropped 40 percent from 1988 to 1993, and domestic consumption rose 45 percent in the same period. Domestic production accounts for only 9 percent. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 6 Apr Economic Section p 8) The National Statistics Institute on 6 April reported that the unemployment rate increased 5.1 percent from December 1993 to February 1994, 0.8 percent higher than the December 1992-February 1993 period. The labor force totalled 5,282,690, an increase of 4.5 percent compared with the same period last year. Employed people totalled 5,012,470, an increase of 3.5 percent. This means the number of jobs rose by 170,460. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 7 Apr 94 p B3) The Central Bank on 7 April reported that the trade balance registered a $24 million surplus in the first half of March, with exports at $486.4 million and imports at $462.4 million, a drop of 75.6 percent compared with the same period in 1993. The cumulative surplus in 1994 rose to $92.6 million, compared with the $141.2 million deficit registered last year. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 8 Apr 94 Economic Section p 4) Central Bank net reserves increased $149 million in March, of which $72 million came from the trade balance surplus. The cumulative reserves through 31 March totalled $10.431 billion. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 8 Apr 94 p 10) Economy Minister Alvaro Garcia reported that foreign investment totalled $804 million in the year's first three months, an increase of 19 percent compared with the same period in 1993. The major investment was in mining with $271 million. (Santiago Radio Cooperativa Network in Spanish 2300 GMT 20 Apr 94)
