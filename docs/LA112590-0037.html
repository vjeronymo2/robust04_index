November 25, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

BIRD OF PARADISE NOT JUST SHOW PIECE

The Bird of Paradise seems to scare off a good many gardeners who think of it as a show piece for botanical gardens and plant specialists.

Bird of Paradise is less tender than most people think, often standing several degrees of frost inland and thriving too in cool coastal gardens.

A wise gardener will give it some protection from frost in valley areas and plenty of sun near the coast. The reward will be bright blooms, almost startling in their beauty, from fall through spring, and often into summer.
