941015 FT 15 OCT 94 / Employees face insider charges The Japanese Securities and Exchange Surveillance Commission is to file insider trading charges against 32 people allegedly involved in dubious trading of shares in Nippon Shoji, an Osaka-based drug maker and distributor. Twenty seven of the accused are Nippon Shoji employees who allegedly sold Nippon Shoji shares last year after obtaining unpublished information that the company's shingles drug had caused deaths among cancer patients.
