Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Gaydar's resignation may alleviate the confrontation between those in the government who favor tough and fast reforms, and those who prefer gradual ones, the Deputy Chairman of Incombank's council of experts, Sergey Popov, told INTERFAX. According to Popov, the possibility that Fedorov may resign after Gaydar and Pamfilov did "will not lead to a crisis in the government." He views Yavlinskiy as the most acceptable candidate for the post of finance minister. The Director of the foreign relations department of Stolichny Bank, Aleksnder Skvortsov, said he hopes that Gaydar's resignation will not be accepted. According to him, only Gaydar can conclude the reforms launched in Russia. The Deputy Chairman of NGS Bank, Viktor Shabrov, said that "the policy of reforms is not carried out by one man; the question is who will replace him." A majority of spokesmen for commercial banks do not link the fall of the ruble with the government reshuffling, since Fedorov envisaged it at the end of last year. However, the rate of the fall heightened "because of instability in the highest echelons of power."
