BFN [Text] Pakistan has urged President Borhanoddin Rabbani, Prime Minister Golboddin Hekmatyar, and other leaders of Afghanistan to stop fighting and sit together to resolve their differences. The call was made by the foreign minister, Ali Sardar Asif Ahmad, while talking to newsmen in Islamabad today. He also called upon the Afghan leaders to accept any invitation to them by the ICO [Islamic Conference Organization] to discuss a draft peace plan. Meanwhile in the Afghan capital, Kabul, jet fighters of the forces opposed to President Borhanoddin Rabbani bombed the center of the city for the second day today, killing 12 people and injuring several others.
