BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the elections scheduled for 29 May 1994. A recent poll conducted in 25 cities nationwide between 22 and 26 April by the National Center shows Liberal Party presidential candidate Ernesto Samper leading Conservative Party candidate Andres Pastrana by 7 percent. In the case of a run-off election, Samper will reportedly beat out Pastrana by 53 percent for Samper to 44 percent for Pastrana. During the closing of the Governors' Summit, candidate Pastrana stressed that the state cannot forget its responsibilities for the health of its citizens, and added that the tax base and revenue sources for the various departments must be broadened. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 1 May 94 p 9A) Pastrana's campaign-coordinator Luis Moreno has indicated Pastrana has always been willing to hold debates. Conservative vice presidential candidate Luis Ramirez said the latest polls indicate Pastrana is on the rise, people are very enthusiastic about Pastrana's campaign. He adds that, regardless of polls, they have always welcomed debates. Antonio Navarro Wolff, candidate for the 19 April Movement, noted that Samper's indecision regarding his participation in debates is worrisome. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Canal A Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 10 May 94)
