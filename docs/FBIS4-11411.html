BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments through 19 April. Finance Minister Cesar Robalino announced on 5 April that the government signed a letter of intent with the IMF according to which Ecuador agreed to reduce inflation to 20 percent by the end of 1994 and cut the fiscal deficit to 0.5 percent of GNP. The agreements are directed at renegotiating the county's $12.6-billion foreign debt and seeking new loans. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1844 GMT 5 Apr 94) The Defense Ministry announced that routes and frequencies currently belonging to Ecuatoriana de Aviacion were ceded to several other companies on a temporary basis. Defense Minister Jose Gallardo said that "once the inactive airline resumes operations, the beneficiaries will relinquish the routes." (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 5 Apr 94) A recent government report states that foreign reserves increased 4.39 percent in March over the previous month. Reserves now total $1.331 billion. According to a Central Bank official, this represents an influx of $56 million. The official said that there is increased confidence in Ecuador's economic future following the recent implementation of government measures. (Quito Voz de los Andes in Spanish 2300 GMT 11 Apr 94) Ecuador's international monetary reserves set a new internal record in March by totaling $1.3 billion, the 15th best registration since 13 years ago when the economic indicator totaled $563 million.
