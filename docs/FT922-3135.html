920612 FT 12 JUN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Science to get 'higher profile' A WHITE PAPER setting out the government's proposals for giving a higher profile to science and technology in Britain will be published in the early part of next year, Mr William Waldegrave, minister for public service and science, told the Commons last night. In his first speech since being given responsibility for the newly created office of science and technology he acknowledged the need for an improved performance in translating invention into innovation where it really counted -in the market place. Mr Jeremy Bray, from Labour's front bench, urged him to ensure that the white paper dealt with policy aims as well as organisation. Ms Anne Campbell, Labour MP for Cambridge, highlighted the growing loss of scientists to Britain. She said the proportion of Fellows of the Royal Society living in the US had increased from 4 per cent in the early 1960s, to 13 per cent in 1984, 20 per cent in 1991 and an estimated 30 per cent in 1992. 'This shows how the scientific community is expressing its frustration with the funding mechanisms and the lack of resources here,' she said.
