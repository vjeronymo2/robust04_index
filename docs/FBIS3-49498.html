Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Romanian President's Letter to the Heads of State or Government of the NATO Member Countries and to the NATO General Secretariat" -- ROMPRES headline] [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 7/1/1994 -- "Before the high level meeting of the NATO countries scheduled for 10 January 1994, I should like to reassert Romania's position on the alliance and reconfirm the constant interest of my country to become a full member of the organization. I have expressed this interest on various occasions, including in the letter I addressed on 18 September 1993 to the NATO General Secretariat, to Mr. Manfred Woerner. "Romania considers the North Atlantic Alliance, that has proved its ability to adjust to the new realities emerged after the close of the Cold War, as being the only organization apt to ensure stability on the European continent and guarantee the values of democracy and the state of law. "I have received with special interest the `Partnership for Peace' proposal, perceived in Romania as being an important preliminary stage in the process of the country's full integration into the NATO structures. My country has shown willing to participate in this new form of cooperation, with respect of the requirements imposed by the status of member of the alliance. "I would like to use this occasion to reassert Romania's strong conviction that, as an European state, it has a rightful place into NATO. "In analyzing our request, please take into account Romania's vital interests of security, that have been given new dimensions following the recent political developments in the areas in the proximity of my country. "Moreover, Romania is resolute to get more actively involved in ensuring stability in the region and in Europe in general, it is willing to assume all responsibilities it would have as a member of the alliance. "I am sure that Romania's integration into NATO would offer us security guarantees absolutely necessary to create conditions adequate for translation into practice the democratic transformations and the economic reform Romania has firmly committed to. "In my outlook, to be a member of NATO is an essential constituent of the global process of integration of Romania in Euro-Atlantic structures (NATO, WEU [Western European Union], the European Union, and the Council of Europe), this is the essential objective of the Romanian foreign policy. "I hope that during the debates to be held at the high level meeting in Brussels you will consider the position of my country, on which all political parties in Romania's Parliament, the whole Romanian people, have agreed."
