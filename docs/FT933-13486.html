930719 FT 19 JUL 93 / Arts: Today's Television Best of the repeats: Waiting For God, outstanding comedy about two people in a retirement home, unfairly overshadowed (because of the popularity of the Victor Meldrew character) by 'One Foot In The Grave', Stephanie Cole and Graham Crowden form a magnificent miniature awkward squad (8.30 BBC1); KYTV with Angus Deayton as presenter for a naff television station; and Blott On The Landscape (10.10 BBC1) Malcolm Bradbury's nicely judged adaptation of Tom Sharpe's farce. World In Action investigates the endowment mortgage business. Seventy per cent of home owners insure their mortgages, but the programme asserts that there is growing fear that many will fail to pay off at a high enough rate to cover the debt (8.30 ITV). In Panorama Tom Mangold reports on the case of the Florida dentist held responsible for transmitting HIV to six of his patients (9.30 BBC1). Channel 4 screens a documentary, 'The Star And Its Shadow' in the Global Image slot, looking at the darker side of the Mercedes Benz history during the period of the Third Reich (10.55).
