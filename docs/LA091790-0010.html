September 17, 1990, Monday, Valley Edition

POLICE LEAVE BREAKFAST TO CHASE SUSPECT

That was to go, right?

Four uniformed Los Angeles police officers had just sat down to breakfast at a North Hollywood restaurant Sunday morning when a man allegedly grabbed an envelope full of cash from a drawer under the cash register and ran out the door.

Abandoning their coffee, the officers gave chase and apprehended 30-year-old Jerald Patrick McMurray of Studio City a few blocks from Sitton's Restaurant at 11329 Magnolia Blvd., Lt. Michael Ranshaw said.

Officers found $63 and McMurray was held in lieu of $5,000 bail on suspicion of burglary.

"All I can figure is he just didn't see them sitting there," Ranshaw said.

The officers returned to complete their meal. It was on the house.
