940113 FT 13 JAN 94 / Petrol retailing rules changed Oil companies will have greater power to restrict other products petrol retailers can sell, Mr Neil Hamilton, corporate affairs minister, said yesterday, David Lascelles writes. He is releasing oil companies Shell, BP, Esso, Mobil and Texaco from commitments they made in 1965 following a monopoly inquiry to refrain from limiting other brands of lubricants, paraffin, anti-freeze and other non-oil products. However that he expects to conduct a further formal review of competition in the market in 1997.
