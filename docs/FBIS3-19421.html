Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Verona, Italy, March 17 (AFP) -- Right-wing Italian leader Franco Freda and 69 others have been charged with trying to set up a fascist party in violation of anti-discrimination laws, judicial sources said here Thursday [17 March]. Freda, who heads the National Front party, was charged with spreading racist and fascist ideas following a nine-month investigation. The fascist party has been banned in Italy since the end of World War II. Freda had previously been charged for his alleged role in a terrorist attack in Milan on December 12, 1969, in which 16 people died and 107 were injured. He was acquitted of the charges by an appeals court in 1985.
