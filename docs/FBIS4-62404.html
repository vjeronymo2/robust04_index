BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Vadim Byrkin and Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan May 16 TASS -- Chairman of the CSCE Minsk Conference on Nagorno-Karabakh Jan Eliasson has left Yerevan for Baku. He will resume consultations with Azerbaijani leaders on ways of settling the conflict in Nagorno-Karabakh. The Swedish diplomat stopped consulting with leaders of Armenia and Nagorno-Karabakh on Sunday's evening. He expressed satisfaction with results of his meetings by saying that they made a serious contribution to the peace-making efforts in the region. Ambassador-at-Large Vladimir Kazimirov, plenipotentiary representative of the Russian president, who also participated in the Yerevan talks, has already returned to Moscow, where a meeting between military leaders of Russia, Azerbaijan, Armenia and Nagorno- Karabakh will take place shortly. Deputy Chairman of the CSCE Minsk Conference on Nagorno-Karabakh Mathias Mossberg and leaders of military planning system of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe are to participate in the Moscow meeting. The Russian and CSCE representatives expressed satisfaction with the fact that military activities in the conflict area were on the small side and proposed to legalize the virtual ceasefire in a relevant agreement. This agreement may provide for stationing international observers in the area, they said on Sunday.
