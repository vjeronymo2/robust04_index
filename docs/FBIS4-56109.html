BFN [Excerpts] National Assembly Chairman Aleksandur Yordanov is already at the National Assembly rostrum and Prime Minister Lyuben Berov is in the Assembly hall. Everything is ready for the official announcement. [Yordanov] I call upon Mr. Kiril Zhelev to announce the results of the voting on the confidence motion in the cabinet of Lyuben Berov. [Zhelev] A secret vote took place today at the request of the Council of Ministers to receive a vote of confidence. [passage omitted] Some 221 National Assembly deputies voted. Some 125 cast their ballots in support of the confidence motion, with 95 against. One National Assembly member cast an empty vote. There were no invalid ballots. On the basis of the results, the National Assembly of the Republic of Bulgaria, based on Article 112, Paragraph 1 of the Bulgarian Constitution and in connection with Article 83, Paragraph 4 of the Regulations on National Assembly Activity, has decided to grant confidence in the government in connection with its Program of Intent Until September 1994. [Yordanov] Mr. Prime Minister, you may take the floor. [Berov] Esteemed Mr. Chairman, ladies and gentlemen, and People's Representatives: It is usually customary on such occasions to express gratitude. However, I think that even those who, because of various reasons, oppose me would hardly envy me for the task that has been bestowed on me for the next three and a half months. This task is to steer the ship on which all of us are traveling through troubled voters, near dangerous rocks, and toward some safe port. This is a difficult task and this is a contradictory country. I promise to make every effort to implement my task and steer the ship toward a safe port. Thank you. [applause, jeers] [Yordanov] Thank you, Mr. Prime Minister. I now hand the floor over to Mr. Vasil Gotsev, who will speak on behalf of the Union of Democratic Forces [SDS] parliamentary faction. [Gotsev] Esteemed Mr. Chairman, esteemed ladies and gentlemen, People's Representatives, and Mr. Professor Berov, who, after everything that has happened, I cannot calmly call Mr. Prime Minister: We have witnessed another parliamentary farce, which took place in this hall last week. [passage omitted] After being supported by only 25 members of the National Assembly in last week's no-confidence motion proposed by the SDS, the prime minister failed to do what a prime minister should do in every democratic state where the parliament and the Constitution are respected -- resign. [passage omitted] Instead of doing this -- devoid of any political and human dignity, pardon the expression -- this prime minister forced us to vote confidence in connection with this government's plans until September 1994. [passage omitted] Only 25 deputies gave you their vote of confidence last week. This means you did not receive a vote of confidence for what you have done so far. Why then did you ask for a vote of confidence until September? What parliament prolonged the life of this disgraceful cabinet by giving it their confidence? Indeed, what actually took place today behind closed doors -- on the one hand, at the meeting of the Bulgarian Socialist Party faction, where long discussions were held and where prudent people certainly said that we must proceed toward early parliamentary elections and, on then other hand, in your room, Mr. Dogan? You implemented the instructions of the ambassador of a foreign state, the very same state that under the name of the USSR dictated everything that took place in our country. Today you obeyed the orders of [Russian] Ambassador Avdeyev. This shows the civilized world your true colors. Ladies and gentlemen and people's representatives, we are witnessing the agony of our state, not just the agony of this government. All of you who supported a prime minister that disregarded the Constitution, the principles of parliamentarianism, and the interests of the Bulgarian people contributed to this agony. However, despite your desire to rule until the end of your life at the early elections, the Bulgarian people will cast their votes for those who today did not support you. [applause] [Yordanov] Thank you, Mr. Gotsev. I close the session. The next session will take place tomorrow at 0900.
