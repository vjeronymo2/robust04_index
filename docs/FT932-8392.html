930519 FT 19 MAY 93 / Observer: Paying up Among the deft bits of prudent accounting which helped the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales to get out of the red in 1992, one item stands out. The institute's Pounds 38,000 surplus is more than accounted for by a doubling, to Pounds 109,000, in income from 'maintenance of professional and ethical standards' - in other words, a substantial increase in the number and level of fines against misbehaving members.
