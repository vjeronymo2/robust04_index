921007 FT 07 OCT 92 / Observer: Wrong angle Meanwhile, it was surprising that an old media hand like Sir Norman Fowler, the Tory party chairman, did not spot the problem in advance. Douglas Hurd's rousing conference speech on Europe was marred by much hissing from the floor. It seems that this had more to do with Sir Edward Heath's picture, which kept on flashing up on the big conference screens, than Hurd's delivery. Each time the conference cameras picked up Heath, the Euro-sceptics showed their disapproval.
