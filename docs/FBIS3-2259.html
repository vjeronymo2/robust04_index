Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] New Delhi, March 8 (XINHUA) -- India would get Russian help in modernizing its T-72 tanks and other Soviet-designed weapons, according to a report reaching here today. This was announced by Commander-in-Chief of the Russian land Forces Col-Gen Vladimir Semyonov after his final round of talks with Indian Army Chief General B.C. Joshi who is paying one-week official visit to the country. The Russian general also did not rule out leasing some types of weapons for two or three years to India, but he did not elaborate. However, observers noted here that India had taken a nuclear powered submarine from Russia on lease in the 80s. Report said during his visit to Russia, the Indian army chief expressed India's willingness to continue military-technical cooperation with Russia and said 70 percent of arms with Indian Army were Soviet-designed. He also showed interest in purchasing the latest T-72 C tanks, self-propelled artillery guns and military aircraft. Joshi was the first Indian Army chief and chairman of the Joint Committee of Chiefs of Staff to visit Russia in the last four years, according to local sources.
