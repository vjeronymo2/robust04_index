Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Colombian elections, scheduled for 8 May. Humberto de la Calle, Liberal Party presidential hopeful, has responded to former President Alfonso Lopez, who said the presidential hopefuls had not expressed their position on the exchange rate. De la Calle reminded Lopez that when he spoke before the National Federation of Businessmen, he said that "the next administration must balance its public finances, because it is the only way we can avoid continuous currency reevaluations and maintain low interest rates." (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 24 Jan 94 p 7A) Ernesto Samper Pizano, Liberal Party presidential hopeful, committed himself to promoting agricultural activities and the tourist free zone and to developing the Magdalena region. Samper seeks to "promote products abroad and reactivate the agrarian fund, the national rehabilitation plan, and the IDEMA [Agricultural-Livestock Marketing Institute], which are pillars of the country's agricultural sector." (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 24 Jan 94 p 7A) Carlos Lemos, former Colombian foreign minister and Liberal Party presidential hopeful, said on 25 January that if Colombia and Venezuela really want to resolve their border differences, they must "accept arbitration by a third party." A high-level bilateral commission appointed by the presidents has submitted a report to the two governments with its recommendations. (Paris AFP in Spanish 2106 GMT 28 Jan 94) Andres Pastrana Arango, leader of the New Democratic Force, has confirmed that he will launch his presidential campaign on 10 February. He will tour 192 municipalities of Antioquia only 87 days before the elections. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0130 GMT 31 Jan 94) Carlos Lemos, Liberal Party presidential hopeful, has stated that liberalism is being handled foolishly. In a statement made only two months before the elections, Lemos said the party must state its position regarding the concentration of wealth in Colombia. For his part, Ernesto Samper agrees that the party must be reformed but says "liberalism has shown its willingness to reform and modernize itself," unlike other parties. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0000 GMT 1 Feb 94)
