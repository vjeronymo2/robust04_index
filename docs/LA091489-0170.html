September 14, 1989, Thursday, Orange County Edition

THREE D TALKS MERGER WITH LINENS PLUS

Three D Departments Inc., a Costa Mesa-based operator of a bed-and-bath chain, is discussing a possible merger with Linens Plus Inc., Three D executives said Wednesday.

If the merger is completed, Linens Plus -- a retailer with eight linen specialty stores in Phoenix -- would be operated as a wholly owned subsidiary of Three D.

Three D operates 26 Three D Bed & Bath stores in California and Connecticut, nine of them in Orange County. The company is traded on the American Stock Exchange.

Under the possible merger announced Wednesday, Linens Plus principal shareholders Norman Goldman and Lynn Levin would continue to operate the Linens Plus stores, with buying conducted from Three D's Orange County headquarters. Linens Plus stores would keep their name.

Three D, which is run by a father-and-son executive team, moved from East Hartford, Conn., into Orange County headquarters in 1987.

The specialty retailer had a net loss of $71,304 in the third quarter ended April 29. For the first nine months of fiscal 1989, the company posted net income of $639,985, up very slightly from the comparable period last year. New and expanded Three D Bed & Bath stores helped sales for the nine months rise 14.8% to $29.5 million, the company said.

Three D said it hopes to complete the deal early next year.
