BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 6 (KCNA) -- The Measure Council of Surviving Comfort Women for the Army of South Korea consisting of women who were forced to serve as "comfort women" during the period of the Japanese imperialist colonial rule held a press conference on May 3 and demanded compensation from the Japanese Government, a radio report from Seoul said. The council declared that it would pay a protest visit to Japan on May 16 in case the Japanese Government failed to make an apology and compensation for the issue of the "comfort women for the army".
