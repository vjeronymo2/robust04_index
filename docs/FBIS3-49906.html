Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Geneva, Jan 18 (AFP) -- A senior UN representative has recommended against using allied warplanes in Bosnia-Herzegovina as proposed by NATO to relieve trapped UN soldiers and reopen a key airport, officials said here Tuesday [18 January]. UN officials confirmed the Bosnian Serbs had already agreed to the replacement of some 180 Canadian troops stranded for six months in the eastern enclave of Srebrenica, making military action unnecessary. The assessments were contained in a report handed Monday to UN secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali by Yasushi Akashi, his special representative for the former Yugoslavia, the officials said. [passage omitted] Marrak Goulding, deputy UN secretary general for political affairs, was dispatched to Brussels on Tuesday to brief Manfred Woerner, secretary general of the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation, on the UN plans. UN High Commissioner for Refugees Sadako Ogata welcomed Akashi's call to avoid air strikes on the Serbs. "It was not a solution," she told AFP. "It would have been an all-out war and what do we do after that?" [passage omitted]
