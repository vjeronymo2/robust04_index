April 23, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OTHER COMMENTARY / EXCERPTS;

GINGRICH'S CORRUPTION ARGUMENT IS SO MUCH MALARKEY

Jim Wright's downfall will be healthy if Congress's zeal for ethical witch hunts dies with his speakership. Yes, we should encourage high standards of personal ethics by those in government. But the way to get high standards is to get good people, and the present malicious mania for minutiae drives away from government the best of those we should strive to get into government. The best don't need a 6-foot shelf of law books to tell right from wrong. And their interest is in serious governance, not the politics of competitive sanctimony.
