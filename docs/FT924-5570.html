921126 FT 26 NOV 92 / Sunday trading TWO THIRDS of retail companies opposed deregulation of Sunday trading but the same proportion believes competitive pressure would force them to open if the Shops Act were repealed, according to research from Cambridge University. The research, released by the Keep Sunday Special lobby group, found that half the shops thought their profitability would decline as a result of Sunday trading and only 10 per cent thought it would increase.
