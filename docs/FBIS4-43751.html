BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Turkmen President Saparmurad Niyazov, who arrived in Turkey yesterday as the guest of President Suleyman Demirel, began his contacts today. The official talks between the two countries' delegations were also held in Ankara today. Demirel and Niyazov signed a memorandum of understanding incorporating the possibilities for cooperation between the two countries. This morning Niyazov first visited the Ataturk Mausoleum, where he laid a wreath, observed a moment of silence, and signed the guest book. Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin accompanied the visiting president. Niyazov then met with Demirel at the Cankaya Mansion. Also attending the meeting were Cetin and Turkmen Foreign Minister Khalykberdi Atayev. Later, talks were held between the two countries' delegations headed by their respective presidents. At the end of the talks, Demirel and Niyazov signed a memorandum of understanding, incorporating the possibilities for cooperation between the two countries and displaying their political will to develop cooperation. In a speech after the signing, Demirel declared that Turkmenistan, which gained its independence following the bloodless dissolution of the Soviet Union, has always entertained good relations with Turkey. Stressing that the peoples of the two countries are brothers, Demirel said: Turkmenistan is our motherland, and Turkey is the second homeland of the Turkmen people. Demirel explained that the most important project between the two countries is the natural gas pipeline and that during the talks the sides reached an agreement on the realization of the project as soon as possible. Niyazov, in turn, said that agreement was reached during the talks in Ankara on several issues. Underlining that he shares all of Demirel's views with regard to bilateral relations, Niyazov expressed gratitude for the support accorded to his country until now. The memorandum of understanding signed between the two countries envisages cooperation on infrastructure projects such as highways, railroads, and ports, as well as cooperation in the fields of industry, agriculture, and energy. The memorandum stresses their determination to realize the pipeline project for transporting Turkmen natural gas to Europe via Turkey. Under the memorandum, the two countries also grant most favored nation status to one another.
