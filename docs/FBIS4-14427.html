BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS] [Text] Moscow 30 Mar, TASS -- The lower house of the Russian Parliament is making efforts to reach stabilisation in society which meets the support of Moslem believers in Russia, said the speaker of the State Duma, Ivan Rybkin, at a meeting with Moslem leaders of religious denominations of the Russian Federation on Tuesday. The meeting was attended by the chairman of the Central Department of Moslem Communities of Russia, the European Part of the CIS and the Baltic States (MDEC), Talgat Tadzhuddin-Sheikh-ul-Islam, members of the MDEC presidium, Ravil Gainutdin and Dzhafar Bikmayev. During the talks the sides discussed problems of cooperation between the state and Moslem organisations in reaching civic peace and accord in Russia, problems of imposing fundamentalism and extremism on Russian Moslems by foreign Islamic organisations and the assistance rendered by the state in the restoration and construction of mosques and education of priests. During the talks religious leaders raised the question of setting up a special state department for religious affairs. They also touched upon unfair distribution of air time on television, which prefers foreign missionaries, infringing on the rights of traditional religious confessions in Russia.
