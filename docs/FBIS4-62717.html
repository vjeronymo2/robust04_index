BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladas Burbulis] [Text] Vilnius 16 May TASS -- The official delegations of Lithuania and the United States began talks in Vilnius on Monday [16 May] to discuss military cooperation. The American delegation is led by Ambassador at large Charles Freeman, assistant defence secretary for international security, and Alexander Vershbov, a high-ranking State Department official. The Lithuanian delegation is led by Security Minister Linas Linkevicius and representatives of the Lithuanian Interior Ministry. The sides will discuss Lithuania's participation in the Partnership for Peace programme, the concept of national security for Lithuania and its new military doctrine. Much attention will also be paid to the establishment of a Lithuanian peace-keeping battalion and American assistance in establishing it and the Lithuanian Army. The U.S. delegation, which has already visited Latvia will meet with members of the Lithuanian parliamentary committees for foreign affairs and national security and hold a news conference in the evening. On Tuesday it will leave for Estonia.
