Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow, 21 Feb -- Four hundred Russian airborne assault troops from the peacemaking battalion deployed in the Serbian Krajina (Croatia) have today started to carry out their duties in the buffer zone between the confronting sides near Sarajevo. The settlements where the Russian "Blue Helmets" are to be accommodated will also be named today. An ITAR-TASS correspondent was told this by the press service of the airborne assault troops. According to the same source the Russian subunits, which arrived on Sunday evening in the area set by the United Nations for separating the sides, were met by Vitaliy Churkin, the Russian president's special envoy, by Russian Airborne Forces commander Colonel General Yevgeniy Podkolzin, and by his peacekeeping force deputy, Major General Nikolay Staskov. In conjunction with the UN forces command they are to resolve the issue of the need to strengthen Russian peacekeeping forces in this region.
