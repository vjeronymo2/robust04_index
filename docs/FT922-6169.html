920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / Arts: Book prize The UK's biggest literary prize, worth a basic Pounds 30,000, was announced yesterday. To be known as the David Cohen British Literature Prize it will be awarded biennially, starting in 1993, to a living British writer in recognition of a lifetime's achievement, writes Antony Thorncroft. The Arts Council, which has been instrumental in setting up the Prize, will award an additional Pounds 10,000 to the winner who will use it to commission a new work from a younger writer.
