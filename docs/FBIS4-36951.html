BFN [Text] Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali left Amman [for Washington] this afternoon to join his Majesty King Husayn. In a press statement at Queen Alia Airport prior to his departure, Dr. al-Majali said that the summit due to be held between His Majesty King Husayn and U.S. President Bill Clinton is important. He added that during this summit, the two leaders will discuss the means of promoting and enhancing Jordanian-U.S. ties, regional developments, and international issues. Dr. al-Majali went on to say: They will also discuss the most recent developments in the peace process, particularly those relating to the Jordanian track of negotiations, as well as the negotiations on the other tracks, especially the Syrian and Lebanese tracks of negotiations, because we believe that the negotiations on these tracks should see progress. Jordan's firm position underscores the need for a comprehensive solution. He noted that the progress made on one track is also good for the other tracks of negotiations. On inter-Arab coordination at this phase, the prime minister said: The concept of coordination is not being seriously observed. The level of inter-Arab coordination has not risen to the desired levels. He indicated that coordination among the Arab sides engaged in the peace process has continued to take place whenever there is an opportunity to do so. He added that whenever new developments occur, the other parties are informed of this. He noted that this is done constantly. With regard to the issue of al-'Aqabah, Dr. al-Majali said that the Jordanian Government is about to sign a deal in this regard with Lloyds Register. He noted that there is a minor problem hampering this, and that this minor problem is about to be resolved. The prime minister was seen off at the airport by Deputy Prime Minister Dhuqan al-Hindawi, several cabinet ministers, the chief of Public Security, and several other officials.
