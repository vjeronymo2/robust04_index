March 19, 1989, Sunday, Valley Edition

DIGEST: LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF;

DRIVE-BY SHOOTING KILLS YOUTH

Los Angeles homicide detectives are investigating a drive-by shooting at a Van Nuys street corner Saturday morning that left one teen-ager dead and two others wounded.

Police said a two-door Toyota Corolla carrying five male teen-agers had stopped at a traffic light at Sepulveda Boulevard and Sherman Way when another car drove up and its occupants began shooting. The car then sped away.

The shots killed Roberto Sanchez, 18, of Van Nuys and wounded two others. The driver of the Toyota drove to nearby Valley Presbyterian Hospital, where Sanchez was declared dead.

A hospital spokeswoman said Lorenzo Perez, 17, was operated on for multiple gunshot wounds. He remained in intensive care Saturday.

Another victim, Walter Quintanilla, 17, was transferred to Los Angeles County-USC Medical Center. A hospital spokesman said he was treated for minor gunshot wounds and released.

The other two passengers were not hurt. Police said they have no suspects in the shooting.
