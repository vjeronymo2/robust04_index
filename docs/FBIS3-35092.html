Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] The prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, has sent a letter to the U.S. President, Mr. Bill Clinton. The letter relates to the efforts being made by Pakistan to prevent human rights violations in the occupied Kashmir by presenting a resolution at the UN Human Rights Commission meeting being held in Geneva. This was stated by Pakistani Foreign Secretary Shaharyar Khan in Washington yesterday evening. He said that he has personally delivered the letter to senior State Department officials. Shaharyar Khan said that he also held talks on matters of mutual interest to Pakistan and the United States during his meetings with U.S. officials. Answering a question, the foreign secretary said that it has been unanimously decided in Islamabad to present a resolution on human rights violations in Kashmir during the current session of the commission. He said that the situation this time is different from the time when Pakistan, on the suggestion of some friends and Islamic countries, had agreed to defer a similar resolution. They wanted to allow an opportunity for talks so as to peacefully resolve the dispute. He said that the situation in the Indian-occupied Kashmir remains unchanged and there has been no respite in Indian oppressive activities. The foreign secretary expressed dissatisfaction over the outcome of the recent Pakistan-India talks. He said that contrary to what Pakistan expected, Indian atrocities in Kashmir recently increased, which clearly indicates how sincere India is about holding talks with Pakistan. In reply to another question, Shaharyar Khan said that it has always been Pakistan's conviction that the Kashmir issue should be resolved in accordance with the aspirations of the people of Jammu and Kashmir. The foreign secretary has now gone to Bonn on the next leg of his trip to secure support for the Pakistani stand on Kashmir.
