Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Vladimir Akimov and Georgiy Shmelev] [Text] Almaty February 9 TASS -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev began his official visit to Kazakhstan with a meeting with President Nursultan Nazarbayev today. The Russian minister had arrived in this republic on Tuesday evening. Kozyrev will also hold talks in the republic's Foreign Ministry, visit the Russian Embassy and meet the press. Kozyrev's itinerary also includes visit to the Baykonur space complex and meetings with military and civil personnel there. According to the foreign minister, this is a regular visit. He thinks that the more frequent and regular are such contacts between leaders of the two states, the lesser problems will be in relations between the states. Andrey Kozyrev said that during his meeting with the Kazakh president he will hand over him a personal message from Russian President Boris Yeltsin. The range of questions for discussions in Kazakhstan, according to the minister, is quite wide. First of all it includes military-technical cooperation, the implementation of agreements of the START-1 Treaty and destruction of nuclear weapons. The two sides will also consider problems of giving a specific content to the economic union, discussions on such bilateral humanitarian problems as problems of citizenship, emigration and consular relations. The Russian side is in favour of opening additional consulates in Kazakhstan and sign a consular convention in accordance with the treaty between the two countries. The sides will also consider specific agreements on humanitarian problems.
