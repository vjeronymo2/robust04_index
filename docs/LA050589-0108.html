May 5, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

67 ORANGE COUNTY TRANSIT BUSES ARE SIDELINED

Orange County officials pulled 67 buses from service Thursday as a precautionary measure after mechanics found cracks that caused the rear suspension on two buses to collapse last week, dropping coaches onto the rear axles.

The removal, the largest in the history of the Orange County Transit District, will lead to some delays in service, officials said. The buses withdrawn from service represent more than 15% of the fleet.

Each of the suspect buses was equipped with a hydraulic lift to accommodate wheelchair-bound patrons. Transit officials said lift-equipped vans will be dispatched if regular buses cannot accommodate those seeking service.

No one was injured in either incident in which suspensions collapsed, officials said.
