920910 FT 10 SEP 92 / Arts: Today's Television Two notable series return at 8.30. The familiar and often excellent current affairs programme This Week begins its last series on ITV. After 36 years it will disappear at the end of this run, victim of ITV's Thatcherisation. Today's programme investigates the growth of vigilante groups up and down the country. Waiting For God (8.30 BBC1) is among the best contemporary comedy series. Graham Crowden and Stephanie Cole star as a couple of shrewd, bolshie inmates of an old people's home. This, with its very high standard of acting and direction, is what we shall extol in 20 years time as one of the marvels of the good old days. C4's documentary series about Nomads (9.00) began exceedingly well last week with an extraordinary blend of anthropology and soap opera in Mauritania. Today's subject is the Mongols and particularly the Xilinhot Nadam where 10,000 gather to watch archery, horse racing and wrestling. ITV screens the last in the politically oh-so-correct American legal soap opera LA Law (9.00) and BBC2 shows the first of nine 60-minute documentaries about how the recession is affecting people, Present Imperfect (9.30).
