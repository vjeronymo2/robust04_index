Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Dhaka, February 26 (XINHUA) -- China is to provide Bangladesh with a 8.5 million U.S. dollars interest-free loan in 1994 as a governmental financial assistance to this South Asian nation. This was confirmed by an agreement signed on Saturday [26 February] by visiting Chinese Vice-Premier Qian Qichen and Bangladesh Finance Minister M. Saifur Rahman. The usage of the loan is to be decided between the two sides later this year, according to Chinese sources. Since the diplomatic relation between the two countries was established in 1975, remarkable progress has been made in trade and economy as well as many other fields. The two sides have expressed their hopes to expand the friendly relations, especially in trade and economic sector. Qian, who is on an official goodwill visit to Bangladesh, is scheduled to leave for Sri Lanka on Sunday. Bangladesh is the second leg of his five-nation trip which also includes Pakistan, Maldives and Iran.
