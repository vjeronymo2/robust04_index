Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over regional map] [Text] It was announced that there was loss of life and property damage as a result of bomb fragments, dropped by Turkish Air Force planes during the Zhalah, falling on antiaircraft positions along the Iranian border. Turkey expressed great regret about the incident and stated that it will do everything in its power to compensate for the damages. According to the Foreign Ministry statement, the joint Iranian-Turkish delegation which conducted investigations in the region on 1 February prepared a joint report following its study tour. The Foreign Ministry statement summarizes the report based on the conclusions of the investigating delegation as follows: It was determined that there was loss of life and damage to property as a result of bomb fragments ricocheting toward Iran after hitting the ground and following the explosion of several fragmentation bombs over antiaircraft positions situated along the hills bordering Iran. The statement further states that the close friendly relations and cooperation between the two countries, which are also neighbors, is of great importance to both Iran and Turkey. Turkey expresses great regret over this unfortunate incident and over the loss of life and damage to property incurred on the Iranian citizens. The statement notes: We offer our condolences to the families of our Iranian brothers who have lost their lives and a swift recovery to the wounded. Turkey will do everything in its power to heal the wounds incurred in this unfortunate incident.
