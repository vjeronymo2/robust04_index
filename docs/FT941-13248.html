940126 FT 26 JAN 94 / International Company News: Amcor cuts jobs, invests ADollars 51m Amcor, the Australian paper group, is to cut 530 jobs and invest an immediate ADollars 51m (USDollars 36m) in mills in Tasmania and New South Wales, in its efforts to create a viable Australian white paper industry. Amcor acquired Associated Pulp and Paper Mills from its domestic rival, North Broken Hill Peko, for ADollars 415m in September. The deal left Amcor as the sole Australian manufacturer of white paper. Rationalisation was expected, as Amcor attempted to combat strong import competition. Amcor said that the job cuts would occur in Victoria, New South Wales and Tasmania. Its five-year plan also envisages enterprise bargaining agreements for individual mills. The company also said that it was evaluating Dollars 65m-worth of investment in projects to upgrade facilities and service.
