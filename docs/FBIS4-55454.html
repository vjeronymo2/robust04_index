BFN [Telephone interview with Hungarian Socialist Party Chairman Gyula Horn by Szendrei in Miskolc on 11 May: "The Country Has To Be Put In Order"] [Excerpts] [passage omitted] [Szendrei] Would you now have the courage to predict the final results for the Hungarian Socialist Party [MSZP] at the elections? [Horn] Naturally, it is difficult to enter into such predictions. I would very much like our individual candidates to gain as many mandates as possible. There is a chance for the majority to win the elections. It is no accident that they had such good results in the first round. I continue to have faith in the sober judgment of the voters. [Szendrei] Do you think it possible for the MSZP to gain an absolute majority in parliament? [Horn] I think this is also possible. The MSZP was the party that offered the real political change, therefore, it is not out of the question for us to reach more than 66 percent. [Szendrei] Does this mean that Hungary might end up with a one-party government? [Horn] I continue to hope for the government to have as many political forces as possible, or for it to have their support. This would be good for the country and for handling the very serious heritage we are facing. We will have to begin putting this in order immediately after the round on 29 May. The country has to be put in order. It cannot carry on the way it is. [Szendrei] I will ask again: Will your party be willing to govern on its own? [Horn] If the voters wish, and if our support is so big that we can undertake this on our own, then we will. We have never yet run away from any tasks. [Szendrei] After the first round, have you discussed a possible coalition with the Free Democrat leaders? [Horn] No. I know that, after my accident, the Free Democrat leaders immediately spoke to [Deputy Chairman] Imre Szekeres and wished me a speedy recovery through him, but political talks did not take place between the two parties at all. [passage omitted] [Szendrei] In the interview you gave to the Television's News program, you categorically refuted that you might be supporting Gabor Kuncze [prime ministerial candidate of the Alliance of Free Democrats] as prime ministerial candidate. Does this mean that you will apply for the prime ministerial position? [Horn] As it has already been announced, the MSZP will hold an extraordinary congress and the party's prime ministerial candidate will be selected there. [Szendrei] How will the MSZP begin to govern? [Horn] First of all, it is most important to get a realistic view of the country's situation. We have only partial information on the past four years, we do not have a full view of the situation. I am afraid that, when it comes to defining the practical tasks of governing, we will find far graver details than those we are aware of at present. [passage omitted]
