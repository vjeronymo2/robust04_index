931013 FT 13 OCT 93 / International Company News: Girobank to sell 51% of its shares GIROBANK, the state-owned bank, has confirmed that 51 per cent of its shares are will be sold through an issue to the public before the year-end. Terms were not disclosed. The flotation will be the first important privatisation sale in Denmark for several years. A minority stake in TeleDanmark, the monopoly telecommunications group, will be sold within the next year. Girobank was formed in 1991 from the post office giro operation. It ranks as the country's fifth-largest bank with assets at the end of last year of DKr44.7bn (Dollars 6.9bn). The shares to be issued will have a face value of DKr255m.
