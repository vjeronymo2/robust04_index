Language: Thai Article Type:BFN [Text] An air service agreement was signed between the Thai and Mongolian Governments at Government House toady. The Thai Government was represented by Foreign Minister Squadron Leader Prasong Sunsiri, while the Mongolian trade and industry minister signed on behalf of his government. Prime Minister Chuan Likphai and Mongolian President Punsalmaagiyn Ochirbat witnessed the signing. A Mongolian delegation visited Thailand from 15-16 September to negotiate an air service agreement, and a draft agreement was initialed on 16 September. The Mongolian president's 25-28 February visit to Thailand presented a good opportunity to sign the initialed air service agreement. Flights by the two countries' airlines will commence when each is ready. The agreement will facilitate visits by people from the two countries and promote tourism, trade, and investment as well as cultural and art exchanges between the two nations. Thailand currently has air service agreements with 72 countries; 69 airlines from 55 countries fly to Thailand on regular basis, averaging 532.5 flights per week.
