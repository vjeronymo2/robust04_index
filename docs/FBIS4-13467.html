BFN [Text] President Lieutenant General 'Umar Hasan Ahmad al-Bashir this afternoon received Hamid Yusuf Hammadi, personal envoy of His Excellency President Saddam Husayn and culture and information minister, who relayed to him a personal message from the leader president containing the Iraqi leadership's views regarding the situation at the UN Security Council, and the possible activation of this situation to lift the unfair embargo clamped on the Iraqi people. The message also underscores the importance of coordination among fraternal and friendly states at international forums to bring about this eventuality. During the meeting, Sudanese President 'Umar Hasan Ahmad al-Bashir reiterated Sudan's firm pan-Arab support for and solidarity with Iraq, which he described as the nation's bulwark in its jihad against exploitative enemies, and also in its quest for development and progress. Lt. Gen. al-Bashir noted that Sudan has always been, and will always remain supportive of Iraq in its resistance to colonialist powers. He expressed his admiration and appreciation of the accomplishments made by the Iraqi mujahidin under leader President Saddam Husayn in the course of rebuilding what the aggressive evildoers destroyed, drawing on their intrinsic resources and national expertise. The personal envoy of the leader president also discussed with Lt. Gen. 'Umar Hasan Ahmad al-Bashir the means to promote and enhance the fraternal bilateral ties in the interest of the two peoples and the Arab nation. The meeting was attended by Sudanese Minister of Culture and Information 'Abd-al-Basit Sabdarat; Sudanese Minister of Presidential Affairs Dr. 'Awad Ahmad al-Jaz; Brigadier General 'Abd-al-Rahim Muhammad Husayn, secretary general of the Presidency of the Republic and minister of interior; and 'Abd-al-Samad Sumayd 'Ali, Iraqi ambassador to Sudan. Culture and Information Minister Hamid Yusuf Hammadi arrived in Khartoum this morning.
