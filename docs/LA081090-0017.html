August 10, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

CUTTING WATER USE AND DWP LEAKS

I would like to register my outrage at the way the new storm drain levy schedule was calculated by the Los Angeles City Council (Aug. 1). Once again, people who reside on large lot residential properties have been unfairly singled out.

Isn't the sewer fee devised by the DWP bad enough?

The additional water used on our property goes into the ground, not into the storm drains and certainly not into the sewers. Our trees, roses and other shrubs (no pool) help the environment of this increasingly crowded, asphalted and polluted city.

Indeed, what makes this entire situation so infuriating is that this same group of geniuses (otherwise known as the City Council) just approved the paving of the hills of Porter Ranch with 4,000 new homes and office buildings. No doubt Councilman Hal Bernson will probably tell us that this grotesque project will have little impact on the storm drains just like it isn't going to impact the sewers, freeways, air and landfills. Sure, Hal.

LOREN ZELDIN

Reseda
