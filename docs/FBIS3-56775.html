Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] INTERFAX has learned that a session of the RF [Russian Federation] Security Council to be chaired by Russian President Boris Yeltsin is to be held on Wednesday, January 19. Well-informed sources assert that the agenda will include several issues related to the development of Russia's military doctrine confirmed by the Security Council last November. In particular, it is to consider several military-technical aspects of the doctrine. This document contains a model of the defensive-industrial complex activity under the system of market relations. Competitive development of arms proclaimed by the doctrine must rule out harmful parallelism in creation and development of various kinds of armaments and to provide for rational spending of allocated funds. High quality of the Russian army's military equipment should enjoy priority. The document stresses that military equipment of the Russian army should obligatorily correspond to world standards. The session to be held behind closed doors is to consider several issues regarding recent reshuffles in the supreme echelons of power.
