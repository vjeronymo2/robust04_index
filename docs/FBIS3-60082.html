Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 18 KYODO -- Giorgio Giacomelli, secretary general of the United Nations Drugs Control Plan (UNDCP), urged Japan on Tuesday [18 January] to give more financial aid to the fight against the global narcotics problem, Foreign Ministry officials said. Giacomelli made the request when he visited Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata at the ministry. Hata told the UNDCP chief that Japan will cooperate with the U.N. program as far as possible, the officials said. Emphasizing that Japan donated 4.5 million dollars to the UNDCP in 1993, Hata said Japan recognizes the drug problem as an important global issue, the officials said.
