940318 FT 18 MAR 94 / Paul Touvier Nazi collaborator Paul Touvier, the first Frenchman to be tried for crimes against humanity, sits in a bulletproof box at the start of his trial in Versailles yesterday. Touvier, 78, who was intelligence chief of the French militia in occupied Lyon, faces life imprisonment if convicted of overseeing the execution of seven Jews in June, 1944.
