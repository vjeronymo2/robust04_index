BFN [Text] Mexico City, 5 Apr (NOTIMEX) -- It was reported today that Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari designated Santiago Onate Laborde as chief of the Office of the Presidency of the republic. A communique from the Presidency states that Onate replaces Jose Cordoba Montoya, recently appointed Mexican representative to the Inter-American Development Bank. Onate Laborde has been Mexico's permanent representative to the OAS (1991-92), federal attorney for protection of the environment (1992-93), and federal deputy (1985-88). Before his appointment, Onate was secretary of international affairs of the National Executive Committee of the ruling Institutional Revolutionary Party.
