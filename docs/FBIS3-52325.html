Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The Jordanian side, led by Talal al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs, and the Qatari side, led by His Excellency Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Bin-Jabr Al Thani, Qatari foreign minister, held a round of talks this evening. They reviewed the fraternal and distinguished bilateral relations between the two fraternal countries and peoples, established by His Majesty King Husayn and his brother His Highness Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani, amir of the State of Qatar. The two sides emphasized the importance of developing and fostering bilateral cooperation in all fields, and agreed to form a joint committee headed by minister of state for foreign affairs and the Qatari foreign minister to activate and follow up the development of bilateral relations in various fields in light of His Highness Crown Prince Hasan's visit to Qatar last August, and the subsequent exchange of visits by ranking officials of both countries. The joint committee will meet alternately in the capitals of the two countries. It was agreed that the first meeting will be held in the Qatari capital in the first quarter of this year. The meeting reviewed details of various bilateral agreements and executive programs in the fields of cultural cooperation, manpower, vocational training, exchange of expertise, health, environment, press exchange as well as exchange of programs and news dispatches, transportation and airfreight, and gas. Special emphasis was placed on the exchange of visits between the chambers of commerce of the two countries and the private sector, facilitating the travel of businessmen to the two countries, and encouraging investment and cooperation in the banking field. Reviewing Arab developments, the two sides emphasized the importance of bolstering solidarity and the unity of Arab ranks in a manner that preserves the Arab nation's interests and realizes its objectives. Al-Hasan explained the developments of the peace process, and emphasized Jordan's firm commitment to this process, both in its bilateral and multilateral talks, in accordance with the constants and principles on which the peace process was established. These constants and principles are the implementation of Security Council Resolutions 242 and 338, the exchange of land for peace, the Palestinian legitimate rights on the Palestinian national soil, restoration of Arab rights in land and water, a solution to the problems of the refugees and displaced persons in accordance with the resolutions of international legitimacy, and full Israeli withdrawal from the occupied Arab territories, including holy Jerusalem. He emphasized that Jordan is committed to a comprehensive solution on all tracks with the aim of reaching a just, comprehensive, and lasting peace in the region. The minister summed up the economic difficulties and challenges Jordan is facing, and referred to the blockade imposed on al-'Aqabah port and the subsequent losses to the Jordanian economy. He also explained the democratic march in Jordan and the recent parliamentary elections. The guest minister expressed gratitude and appreciation for the warm reception he and his delegation were accorded, and emphasized the depth of fraternal bilateral relations, and the eagerness of the State of Qatar to bolster and develop these relations within the framework of bolstering Arab solidarity. The minister hailed Jordan's role in the peace process and emphasized his country's support for the peace process in a manner that secures restoration of Arab rights and the legitimate rights of the Palestinian people on their national soil, in accordance with the resolutions of international legitimacy and the principles on which the peace process was set up. The two sides reviewed a number of Arab, regional, and international issues of mutual concern, and exchanged views on them. The meeting was attended by the delegation accompanying the guest minister, secretaries general of the Foreign Ministry and Industry and Commerce Ministry, the Jordanian ambassador to Qatar, Qatari ambassador in Amman, and a number of Foreign Ministry officials.
