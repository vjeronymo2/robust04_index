940223 FT 23 FEB 94 / Leaked drug report sparks row The government's policy on drug misuse was under attack yesterday after leaked papers showed it had altered a report calling for a coherent approach to the problem. Two versions of a report by the Department of Health show the original paper containing criticism of the government's failure to provide a co-ordinated drugs policy. In the final version the title has been changed, important passages calling for a new partnership to tackle the problem in local communities have been watered down and some chapters omitted. Mr Tony Blair, shadow home secretary, said: 'These two versions are drastically different with every sign that hard-hitting recommendations have been deliberately watered down.'
