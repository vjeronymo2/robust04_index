Language: Bulgarian Article Type:BFN [Text] On 6 January President Zhelyu Zhelev met representatives of the Bulgarian Social Democratic and Green Parties. The economic situation in the country, problems with structural and agrarian reforms, and the alarming growth in unemployment were discussed. Also discussed were issues of international importance, including the situation in Yugoslavia in particular and in the Balkans in general. After the meeting Stefan Radoslavov, chief secretary of the Bulgarian Social Democratic Party, expressed satisfaction with the rather similar positions on various issues that were noted. On behalf of the Green Party, Vladimir Sotirov conveyed to the president a charter on national unity worked out by Green Party experts. President Zhelev also met representatives of the Union of Thracian Associations, Internal Macedonian Revolutionary Organization-Union of Macedonian Associations, the Initiative Committee on Protecting the Rights of Bulgarian Refugees, and the Green Party. They demanded that the president utilize his influence on the parliament and the cabinet and facilitate the solution of the problems of the refugees of the Aegean and Thracian Macedonia and the issue of compensating those refugees. A petition on this matter was submitted by the four organizations on 6 November 1993 to the parliament, and they expect a more rapid solution of the issue.
