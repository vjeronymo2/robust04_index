BFN [Text] For the first time since the Yemeni battles started, representatives of the conflicting sides met in New York yesterday. AFP reports that the two sides could not set a date for a cease-fire. The talks, which were held in the presence of Lakhdar Brahimi, the UN secretary general's special envoy for Yemeni affairs, were attended by 'Abd al-Karim al-Iryani, Yemen's planning and development minister, representing the northern forces, and Haydar Abu Bakr al-'Attas, representing the southern forces. The official Yemeni news agency has reported that 'Ali Salim al-Bid, who was living in al-Mukalla in east Aden, was wounded and has gone to an Arab country for treatment. The news agency has not revealed the name of the Arab country. Another report citing the official Yemeni news agency states that Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih has accused the southerners of trying to internationalize the Yemeni war. Speaking with Egyptian President Husni Mubarak on the phone yesterday, he said the southerners are thus trying to achieve their expansionist objectives.
