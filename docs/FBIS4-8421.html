BFN [By Lindita Karadaku] [Text] Tirana, April 12 (ATA) -- Since April 11, the border crossing point of Tushemisht in the border between Albania and Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] has begun a 24-hour service for the crossing of nationals from both sides and of small vehicles, such as cars and other means of transportation. Whereas the Qafe-Thane border point, known for the long line of vehicles and people waiting to cross the border, will continue to work for 24 hours like before, but only for trucks. This change intends to facilitate the crossing of the border by nationals and vehicles, according to a source close to the Albanian Ministry of Public Order. According to a note published today by the newspaper RILINDJA, the Macedonian Ministry of Interior has confirmed this change in a communique, as well.
