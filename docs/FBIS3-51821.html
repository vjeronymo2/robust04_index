Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Salvadoran economic developments monitored through 25 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. Central Reserve Bank President Jose Roberto Orellana Milla reported that El Salvador is experiencing sustained, accelerated economic growth. Urban unemployment dropped from 8.4 in 1989 to 8.1 percent in 1993, and 149,950 new jobs were created between 1989 and 1994. Orellana said the minimum urban wage has increased to 935 colones a month and that public workers' salaries increased more than 22 percent in 1993. (San Salvador LA PRENSA GRAFICA in Spanish 19 Jan 94 p 15) The Inter-American Development Bank representative reported that in 1993 El Salvador received $109.2 million, which is equivalent to 948.5 million colones, for development projects. The funds financed 32 projects covered by 16 loans, five concession loans, and 11 donations of technical cooperation. The IDB approved three credit operations worth $85 million and four donations of technical cooperation worth $2.86 million for El Salvador in 1993. (San Salvador LA PRENSA GRAFICA in Spanish 2 Jan 94 p 13)
