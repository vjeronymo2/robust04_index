Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 9 Mar (MENA)--Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih has expressed his country's appreciation to Egypt for its support of and assistance to the Yemeni revolution and for its contribution to the development of his country. In a statement made after he arrived in Cairo today, he praised President Husni Mubarak's efforts, eagerness to stand by the Yemeni people, and sincere work to contain the crisis and preserve Yemeni unity and democracy in his country. He said his visit to Cairo is an opportunity to review bilateral relations and explore more aspects of cooperation between Egypt and Yemen. He added that he will brief President Mubarak on the latest developments in his country in light of the efforts to implement the Pledge and Accord Document, expressing optimism on this matter. He pointed out that dealing with terrorist operations and security disturbances in Egypt and Yemen will be discussed during his visit to coordinate the stances of the two countries on the issue. He said Egypt's security is the security of Yemen and the entire Arab nation. He noted Egyptian and Yemeni efforts to achieve Arab reconciliation and restore Arab solidarity. The Yemeni president expressed the hope that these efforts will be consolidated to achieve the Arab nation's aspirations to overcome the current problems and open a new chapter in inter-Arab relations.
