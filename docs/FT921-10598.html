920205 FT 05 FEB 92 / Travel agents report optimism on outlook MOST travel agents are optimistic about business prospects this year, according to a survey for Hertz, the car rental company. The survey of 200 travel agents last month confirms reports from package tour operators that the holiday market appears to be unaffected by the recession. The survey found that 72 per cent of travel agent chains were more optimistic about business compared with three months previously. Nineteen per cent were less optimistic and 9 per cent reported no change. Eighty per cent of independent travel agents were more optimistic, with 10 per cent less optimistic and 10 per cent saying they saw no change. The travel agents said they thought business would improve further over the next three months. Eighty-one per cent of the chains and 82 per cent of the independents said they expected foreign travel bookings to increase. Only 26 per cent of the chains and 29 per cent of the independents expected to increase staff levels over the next three months.
