Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 10 (YONHAP) -- Trade, Industry and Energy Minister Kim Chol-su and his Romanian counterpart Cristian Ionescu agreed Thursday to hold the first meeting of the Korea-Romania Joint Economic Committee as soon as possible. In their meeting, Kim proposed that Romanian industries set up subsidiaries and stage goods fairs here while Ionescu, who is visiting with Romanian President Ion Iliescu, suggested that the two countries exchange trade delegations and private-sector visits in a bid to narrow the current trade imbalance. Seoul sold goods worth 80 million U.S. dollars to Bucharest and imported products worth 6 million dollars to record a 74-million-dollar surplus last year.
