930922 FT 22 SEP 93 / UK Company News: Lower interest helps Mayborn advance 14% MAYBORN GROUP, the fabric dyes, baby products and florists' sundries concern, made further steady progress in the first half of 1993 and helped by a sharp fall in interest charges, pre-tax profits rose by 14 per cent to Pounds 1.9m, against Pounds 1.66m. Turnover of the USM-quoted group edged up 3 per cent to Pounds 17.5m. However, on a more comparable basis - after adjusting for the effect of recent transfers of Dylon operations (fabric dyes) in France and the US to independent distributors - the increase was 5 per cent. At the operating level, profits grew 4 per cent to Pounds 1.93m. before interest charges of Pounds 34,000 (Pounds 195,000). Earnings per share rose to 6.4p (5.8p) and an increased interim dividend of 1.8p (1.6p) has been declared. Mr Ian Peacock, chairman, said trading in the second half was expected to show more satisfactory growth. The baby products side in particular, would benefit from significant Christmas gift orders already received and confirmed. Rainbow Acrylics - an importer and distributor of melamine and acrylic children's tableware products acquired in July - was unlikely to have a material impact on this year's results. Net borrowings at the half year were Pounds 841,000 (Pounds 2.84m) giving gearing of 9 per cent (36 per cent). This was higher than the year end figure of Pounds 194,000, but Mr Peacock said it represented more normal seasonal trading fluctuations and 'we have every reason at present to expect another healthy cash position at the end of December 1993.'
