Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] A ship arriving in Haiti under a Honduran flag was inspected by the U.S. Coast Guard, which discovered about 100 kg of cocaine on board. They arrested the 15 members of the crew. The drug was discovered inside a container during the preliminary search at the U.S. Naval Base at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba. The ship was due for a more thorough search on 6 March upon its landing in the Florida Everglades. Of the 15 people arrested, 12 are of Colombian nationality and the three others are from Peru. Their identities were not revealed. Twelve kg of cocaine concealed in plastic containers was intercepted on 5 March at the international airport. The national anti-drug brigade proceeded to arrest the two smugglers, a U.S. citizen of Haitian origin and a Haitian woman named Joanne Francois. The drug seized at the airport is assessed at 12 kg. Furthermore, a small boat carrying 50 bags of marijuana wrecked in Torbeck in the Port-Salut region and was discovered by the anti-drug brigade. However, the smuggler was concealed by the population that had seized the boat. Two men from Colombia, who were still aboard the boat, were arrested.
