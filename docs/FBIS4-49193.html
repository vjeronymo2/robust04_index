BFN [Text] Beijing, May 4 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen met with the World Travel and Tourism Council (WTTC) delegation, headed by Jonathan S. Linen, vice-chairman of American Express, here today. Despite the tremendous achievements over the past 15 years of reform and opening up, Qian said, China's tourist industry is still short of experience and needs to learn from countries with advanced tourist industries. China is willing to cooperate closely with WTTC, Qian told the visitors. WTTC is a non-governmental group, composed of about 70 executives from aviation, hotel and railway industries, aimed at drawing government support for the tourist industry, sources here said. The delegation came here to attend a tourism symposium at the invitation of China's National Tourism Administration (NTA). Briefing Qian on the symposium, Linen said the delegation is impressed on China's tourist industry development and the world-class tourist facilities built in a relatively short period of time. WTTC Chairman Robert Burns voiced his satisfaction with the cooperation between WTTC and NTA. NTA Director Liu Yi attended today's meeting.
