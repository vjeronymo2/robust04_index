Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] President Hashemi-Rafsanjani has stressed the need for exchange of views and all-embracing cooperation between Iran and Pakistan to help toward solving the region's difficulties including Afghanistan and Kashmir. Speaking at a meeting this morning with General Abdul Wahid Kaker, Pakistani Army chief of staff, the president referred to the joint cultural, religious, and historic heritage of the two nations of Iran and Pakistan. He said: In view of the talks held regularly between the two countries' high-ranking delegations in Islamabad and Tehran and the important agreements reached as a result, it is expected that we will in future witness practical progress in political, economic, and cultural cooperation between Iran and Pakistan. The president also stressed the need for greater cooperation between the two countries' border forces to combat the merchants of death [drug traffickers]. According to a report of the Central News Unit, in the course of this meeting, Pakistan's chief of staff referred to the significance of strengthening friendship and cooperation between the Islamic Republic of Iran and Pakistan. He said: Decisive action against drug traffickers and maintaining security of the two countries' joint borders are priorities in the cooperation between Pakistan and the Islamic Republic of Iran.
