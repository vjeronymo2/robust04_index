Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Naryshkin] [Text] Zagreb, 14 Mar -- Vitaliy Churkin, the Russian president's special envoy, who returned from Belgrade to Zagreb this evening, has met Croatian leader Franjo Tudjman for the second time in the last 48 hours. Churkin also held talks with Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and foreign minister. The high-ranking Russian diplomat told an ITAR-TASS correspondent that during the talks they discussed ways to organize further negotiations on Bosnia and possible options for a state system in the Bosnian federation, as well as Croat-Serb relations. Tomorrow the Russian deputy foreign minister will hold a number of working meetings in the Croatian capital.
