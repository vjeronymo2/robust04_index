Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Dhaka, February 26 (XINHUA) -- Prime Minister Khaleda Zia said here today that China's economy has been rapidly developing in recent years due to its reform and open-door policy and Bangladesh hopes to learn from China's experience for its own economic development. Talking to visiting Chinese Deputy Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen, who called on her at her office this afternoon, Khaleda Zia said that the present Government of Bangladesh attached great importance to the development of friendly cooperation between Bangladesh and China and hopes to further strengthen their bilateral cooperation. Qian Qichen expressed his satisfaction with the development of the traditional friendship between the two countries in recent years, saying that the Chinese Government highly valued the friendly cooperation between the two countries. Khaleda Zia briefed the Chinese foreign minister on the cooperation among member states of the South Asian Alliance [as received] for Regional Cooperation (SAARC) founded on the initiative of the late President Ziaur Rahman, and sought China's support for the SAARC. Qian Qichen said China hopes that SAARC would play an active role in economic developments of SAARC member states as well as in resolving the existing disputes among the SAARC states. Qian Qichen today also called on President Abdur Rahman Biswas at the president's office. Abdur Rahman Biswas spoke highly of China's rapid economic development and expressed the hope that the economic cooperation between China and Bangladesh will be further strengthened. Qian Qichen said both China and Bangladesh are now concentrating their efforts on economic development for raising the living standard of their people. The two countries should support each other and strengthen their cooperation, he added. He said the Chinese Government will encourage Chinese companies and enterprises to invest in Bangladesh.
