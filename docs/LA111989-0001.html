November 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

The Godfather (Channel 13 Sunday at 1 p.m.): Francis Coppola's modern gangster classic, starring Marlon Brando. (4:00)

The Lonely Passion of Judith Hearne (Bravo Sunday at 5 p.m.): Maggie Smith is brilliant and heart-breaking as a desperate Irish spinster thrown into spiritual crisis. (2:00)

Passport to Pimlico (Channel 28 Wednesday at 1 p.m.): Classic British comedy in which the discovery of an ancient treaty leads a small group of people to set up their own country in the heart of London. Stanley Holloway and Margaret Rutherford star. (1:30)

Scarface (AMC Saturday at noon): The raw, brutal Howard Hawks 1932 original, in which Paul Muni plays an Al Capone carbon. (1:30)

The Last Waltz (Channel 7 Saturday at 1 a.m.): Martin Scorsese brought an unprecedented intimacy and power to the concert film in this 26-song look at the last appearance of The Band, at the time (1978), America's most distinguished rock group. (2:00)
