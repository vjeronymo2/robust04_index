931118 FT 18 NOV 93 / Christopher threatens N Korea with UN action THE United States warned yesterday that it would recommend United Nations action against North Korea if Pyongyang refused to allow its nuclear facilities to be inspected and to resume dialogue on nuclear issues with South Korea. Mr Warren Christopher, secretary of state, said in a speech at the University of Washington in Seattle: 'We are prepared to recommend that the UN security council consider options other than negotiation.' He urged North Korea to 'join the community of responsible nations'. Describing North Korea as 'caught in a time warp', he said: 'It is the most isolated country in the world, unmoved by the winds of change that have swept across the region.' However, he said the US remained committed to a diplomatic solution and that UN action could be avoided if Pyongyang fulfilled its commitments under the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty and under its denuclearisation agreement with Seoul. Mr Christopher, who is host at the ministerial meeting of the Asia Pacific Economic Co-operation grouping which got under way last night, highlighted North Korea as the most serious security risk in Asia. However, he noted that 'while the tensions of the cold war have subsided, many Asian nations harbour apprehensions about their closest neighbours'. The US was committed to remaining engaged in Asia in order to limit such concerns, he said. 'It remains in our unambiguous national interest to deter regional aggression and to sustain Asia-Pacific economic growth.' The US, he said, would 'maintain our forward military presence throughout the Pacific.'
