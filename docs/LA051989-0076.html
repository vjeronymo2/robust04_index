May 19, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Macy Says Units Not for Sale: The chairman of R. H. Macy & Co. said the retailer -- which owns the Bullocks and I. Magnin chains -- feels its financial footing is secure since it went private in 1986 and it doesn't intend to divest any department store divisions. Edward S. Finkelstein said there have been persistent rumors that Macy's might be planning to sell off some assets to reduce its debt. There were also reports that Limited Inc., the nation's leading specialty apparel retailer, had preliminary talks about an investment in Macy.
