Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The visit by (Michael Cozac), the assistant to President Clinton's special representative in the Haitian crisis, has come to an end. (Cozac) had arrived in Port-au-Prince over the weekend for the purpose of holding conversations with personalities involved in finding a solution to the crisis. Mr. (Cozac) termed the situation in Haiti encouraging. He made the Haitian military responsible for the reimposition of the sanctions. He explained that if the military respected their commitments within the scope of the Governors Island agreement, the sanctions would be lifted. He further said that the U.S. Government wants the Haitian Army to be professional and apolitical. Furthermore, Mr. (Cozac) said that the Haitian parliamentarians' plan is a good and serious effort to disentangle the democratic process. In his opinion, the plan must not replace the Governors Island agreement, and the United States does not wish to impose a plan on President Aristide or to require from him actions not provided for by the 3 July agreement. Finally, Mr. (Cozac) gave the following warning, quote: The U.S. Government will not have relations with any de facto governments in Haiti, unquote.
