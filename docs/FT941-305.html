940330 FT 30 MAR 94 / International Company News: La Rinascente seeks to increase capital La Rinascente, Italy's largest retailing group, plans a substantial capital increase. The move has prompted speculation in Milan that La Rinascente, which is controlled by the Agnelli family's Ifil holding company, is planning to put in a bid for the Gs supermarkets and Autogrill restaurants, which are being sold as part of the government's privatisation of the remains of its SME foods group. La Rinascente is to raies a total of L48.7bn (Dollars 29.8m) in new capital through the issue of ordinary shares. The company also plans a L390bn debenture loan to be co-ordinated by Mediobanca. La Rinascente, together with the Centromarca foods consortium and FinComit, the finance arm of Banca Commerciale Italiana, has expressed interest in the SME concerns. La Rinascente said group stores increased sales by 10.7 per cent, to L5,579.4bn, in 1993. Net profits were up from L102.2bn to L105.3bn. It is lifting its dividend from L190 to L200. Belleli, the Mantua-based engineering group with a strong presence in the Middle East, increased group sales to L1,380bn in 1993, compared with L1,295bn the previous year. Snia Fibre, the fibres arm of the Fiat group, has won shareholder approval for a L152.5bn capital increase to tackle heavy restructuring costs. Snia Fibre incurred a net loss of L49.6bn in 1993, against a loss of L66bn in 1992.
