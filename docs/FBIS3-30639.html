Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, February 4 (BTA) -- International mediators Lord David Owen and Thorvald Stoltenberg left Bulgaria en route to Tirana this morning. Yesterday they had talks with President Zhelyu Zhelev and Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov. The mediators are seeking support from the countries in the region for a peaceful settlement of the Yugoslav conflict. Invited to comment on Alija Izetbegovic's statement that the forthcoming talks in Geneva would be a waste of time, Lord Owen, admitting that negotiations had been going on for long, said that if any of the parties thought it a waste of time, there were lots of other things to do. Mr. Thorvald Stoltenberg added that efforts would continue both before and after the meeting. Asked what gives him hope about the peace process, Lord Owen said it was the general belief/human nature [as received] that some day human beings stop fighting and prefer to act and live together. People in Bulgaria are suffering because of sanctions, this is a fact: You have lost many dollars, as well as your neighbours, he went on to say. And the question which the region has to say is how much longer they all are going to go on making sacrifices to try and bring a settlement, Lord Owen said. He described Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, Macedonia and Greece as severely affected by the sanctions against ex-Yugoslavia.
