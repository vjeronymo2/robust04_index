BFN [Text] The Pacific Fleet has changed the composition of ships taking part in the Putina-94 operation aimed at combating illegal sea fishing in the Far East. Throughout several weeks of the first stage, from April 20 to May 10, two trawlers controlled the catch and the use of fish resources of Russia in the Aniv Bay. They are to be replaced by two sea trawlers which will patrol in the Sea of Japan, Interfax was told by a spokesman for the Pacific Fleet. During the first stage of the operation, crews of over 20 Russian and four foreign vessels were brought to justice, Interfax was told by the commander of the Pacific Border Guard District, Major General Vitaliy Sedykh. He said that about 700 vessels, including 200 foreign ones were spotted in Russia's economic zone. Of this number, over 100 Russian and over 20 foreign vessels have been checked. Besides the Pacific Fleet, taking part in the operation were vessels of the Pacific Border Guard District, vessels of fish protection organizations Primorryba and Sakhrybvoda, staff members of the federal counterintelligence service and of the Far Eastern customs department.
