BFN [Text] Deputy Commerce Minister Chulin Laksanawisit said there should be no effect from the United States keeping Thailand on its priority watch list for trade retaliation. He said the United States will not be able to take retaliatory trade measures now that international trade will be overseen by the World Trade Organization set up under the GATT agreement. Besides, in the announcement, the United States said it would like to see Thailand's copyright act passed by the Thai parliament first before removing Thailand from the list. Chulin said the copyright law is now being scrutinized by a special committee of the House of Representatives pending the second and third readings of the bill, which are expected to take place in May or June.
