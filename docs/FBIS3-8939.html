Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 11/3/1994 -- The visit of the Romanian premier, Nicolae Vacaroiu, in the counties of Mures, Harghita and Covasna is welcome, this being an opportunity for him to know first hand the problems of the inhabitants of these counties, Greater Romania Party's (PRM) leader Corneliu Vadim Tudor told the press at his weekly news conference. Tudor said that in certain places in Transylvania leaflets printed in Hungary are reported to be circulating, threatening PRM leaders and the Hungarians who live in harmony with the Romanians. In his opinion, once the conflicts in former Yugoslavia are over, "international professionals" are trying to shift the testing arena to Romania. PRM president voiced his satisfaction for the recent government reshuffle, which he termed as a response full of "responsibility" to the alarm signals given by his party. Without assuming the "merit" for the replacement of the former Minister of Defence Nicolae Spiroiu, Tudor qualified his removal as "a consequence of his own mistakes," and the reaction of the "monarchist" opposition as a proof that Nicolae Spiroiu "was an important part in the political machinations" that wish the coming of former King Mihai the First to Romania.
