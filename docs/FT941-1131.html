940326 FT 26 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Beazer and Maid sink below their flotation prices Two newly-floated groups saw their shares fall to a discount in first day's trading yesterday. Maid, the business information supplier, closed at 102p, an 8p discount to the offer price. Beazer Homes, the housebuilder sold by Hanson, closed at 162p, 3p short of its price. Maid, set-up nine years ago, came to market earlier this month via a placing of 11.5m shares representing 14 per cent of the share capital. Beazer saw 53.7m shares traded. Since the issue was priced the FT-SE 100 index has fallen 3.6 per cent. A total of 280.8m shares were sold, of which a quarter were offered to the public. This part of the issue was only 1.3 times subscribed.
