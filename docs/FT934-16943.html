931004 FT 04 OCT 93 / Jaguar to enter Chinese market JAGUAR, the luxury carmaker, is to enter the Chinese market with the aim of establishing a network of distribution and service centres in cities throughout the country, Kevin Done writes. The company , a subsidiary of Ford of the US, is appointing Inchcape, the UK-based international services and marketing group, as its exclusive importer/distributor for China. Inchape is the Jaguar importer/distributor for Hong Kong, the carmaker's fastest-growing market, and for Macao. Jaguar China is to be set up as a division of Inchcape Pacific with the aim of starting Jaguar sales in China before the end of the year. Mr Nick Scheele, Jaguar chairman and chief executive, said that China was set to become one of the company's most important development markets. Jaguar said that it expected to sell 40 cars in China this year rising to 100 in 1994.
