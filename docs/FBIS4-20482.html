CSO [Article by Ji Yonghua [0679 3057 5478], Lu Lifang [7120 7787 5364], Xu Ke [1776 4430], Shanghai Institute of Physiology, Chinese Academy of Sciences; Hiroyuki Hatori, Suzumu Terakawa, Okazaki National Institute of Physiology, Okazaki, Japan] [Abstract] Researchers recently purified and sequenced three toxin components (AgTx-1, AgTx-2 and AgTx-3) from Agkistrodon blomhoffii brevicaudus (also called Agkistrodon halys Pallas) venom. All of them were toxoproteins being composed of 122 amino acid residues. The three toxoproteins' LD[.sub]50[/] to mice were 0.075, 0.51 and 6.6 mg/kg respectively. Toxicological analysis conducted on chicken neck muscle indicated that AgTx-1 and AgTx-2 were presynaptic neurotoxins which only blocked transmission between neurosynapsis, and both AgTx-1 and AgTx-2 had the same N-terminal 32-peptide sequence. The AgTx-3 was identified as a newly discovered cytotoxin that paralyzed the muscle cells only.
