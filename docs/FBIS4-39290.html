BFN [Text] Moscow, June 3 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and Russia on Friday [3 June] afternoon agreed to expedite the transfer of Russia's high-tech expertise to South Korea in the areas of aeronautical materials, airplane engine and energy. At the fourth joint Korea-Russia science and technology committee meeting, the two countries decided to establish research centers of the three areas in Moscow with the participation of research institutes and industries of the two countries. South Korea was represented at the meeting by Science and Technology Minister Kim Si-chung who was accompanying President Kim Yong-sam to the Moscow visit, and Russia by Science and Technology Policy Minister Boris Saltykov. The joint institutes the two ministers agreed on are a Korea-Russia aeronautical materials research center, a Korea-Russia airplane engine research center and a Korea-Russia energy research center. The idea of the two ministers is that the two countries jointly develop new technology in Moscow through these research centers and that Russia would accelerate the transfer of high-tech expertise it possesses to South Korea.
