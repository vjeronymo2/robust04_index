BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Andrey Serov] [Text] Moscow April 8 TASS -- Russia is getting ready to sign a framework document on its participation in NATO's Partnership for Peace [PFP] program after April 20, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev told member of the EC Commission Hans Van der Broek here today. The documents are being finalized with due account of Russian peculiarities as a nuclear power. The sides focused today on preparation of an agreement between Russia and the European Union. There are two problems to be solved. The first one, banks, has been settled at the experts' level. The other is more complicated as it concerns sales of nuclear fuel. Moscow is not satisfied with the quotas envisaged by the draft agreement. The sides basically agree that it is time to finish preparations and sign the agreement.
