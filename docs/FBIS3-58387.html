Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Butrus-Ghali, the UN secretary general who is visiting France, said he was in favor of air strikes in Bosnia if the men on the ground requested it. Butrus-Ghali was interviewed by Luc Lemonnier after having been heard by the Senate and before being received by Balladur in his office: [Begin recording] [Butrus-Ghali] I think that the resolutions that have been adopted will allow the use of air strikes [in Bosnia]. We have no difficulty. Once those who are responsible for military operations, humanitarian operations, and political operations have asked or decided to use air strikes, we will immediately ask NATO to use air strikes. I must stress that, so far, I have never received any request for the use of air strikes. The day I receive such a request -- and I have the support of the military in charge of humanitarian operations and in charge of negotiations, considering the use of air strike as urgent -- I will be the first to support the use of air strikes. [end recording]
