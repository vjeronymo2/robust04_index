BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Anna Melnikova, Yuriy Sizov] [Text] Moscow, 30 Apr -- Russia is not pursuing any military aims or interests in Estonia, Russian President Boris Yeltsin said in the Kremlin today while answering journalists' questions after the signing of a package of Russian-Latvian agreements. Russia intends to withdraw all troops from Estonia, but we want this to be done on a legal basis, as is already taking place in Russia's mutual relations with Lithuania and Latvia, Yeltsin stressed. In connection with this, he recalled that Russia was strictly observing the accords reached in Germany, and that Russian soldiers would leave this country by the end of the year. If the Estonian side shows goodwill, it will have full consent from ours, Yeltsin said in conclusion.
