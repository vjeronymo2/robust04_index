Language: English Article Type:BFN [From 12 March RAHVA HAAL, PAEVALEHT, p 1; "News Bulletin" No. 49/94] [Text] Monday, March 14 -- President Lennart Meri, Mrs. Meri, and a delegation including Foreign Minister Juri Luik, State Secretary Ulo Kaevats, Riigikogu [parliament] defence commission chairman Rein Helme and Foreign and Economy Ministries and the Presidential Chancellery representatives, as well as a number of business figures, left for a state visit to Israel and Ukraine on Sunday [13 March]. The delegation was received in Jerusalem by Israeli President Ezer Weizman, Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin, parliament chairman Shevah Weiss, Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, and Israeli Ambassador to Estonia Tova Herzl. President Weizman compared the way of Israel and Estonia towards independence. President Meri stressed the importance of peaceful settlement of problems. President Meri met Prime Minister Rabin, Foreign Minister Peres and chairman of the "Likud" opposition party Benjamin Netanyahu. The discussions concerned the dynamic development of Estonian-Israeli relations after the concluding of diplomatic relations on January 9, 1992. An agreement on cultural, scientific and educational cooperation has been signed already and the agreement on promotion and protection of investments is to be signed during the current visit. Estonian-Russian relations and the troops withdrawal problems were also discussed. The tour of the Estonian delegation is to continue in Ukraine. The delegation is to return late on Thursday.
