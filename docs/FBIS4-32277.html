BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 21/6/1994 -- The third plenary session of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Black Sea Economic Cooperation Zone opened on Tuesday, 21 June, morning in Bucharest with a ceremony attended by Romanian President Ion Iliescu. In his address President Iliescu said that just three years after launching, the Black Sea economic cooperation was on a developing way to become a genuine subregional organization. "Promoting actively the principles of the Conference for Security and Cooperation in Europe [CSCE] and of the United Nations Charter, it is contributing to the creation of new opportunities for integration in the Euro-Atlantic space. I hope that the meeting in Bucharest will represent an important step in finding and employing the community of interests favored by the vicinity of the Black Sea," said the Romanian president. President Iliescu expressed his confidence that once the problems generated by the transition in the new states of the zone are surpassed, and the problems caused by the lack of infrastructure, institutions, and managers at micro-economic level are solved, then the economic cooperation of the Black Sea would manage to succeed in building a "unity in diversity" as a basis for future economic projects able to bring a contribution to the political and economic stability in the area, and to ensure a climate of peace and understanding. "This is, ultimately, our common interest in whose realization we have not only the right, but also the duty to engage ourselves with all our forces, with all the intellectual ability of each of our countries," said President Iliescu. The Romanian head of state said that in the achievement of this goal an important role is to be plaid by diplomacy, which gains multiple dimensions, including an important, necessary, and useful parliamentary component. The fact that the Parliamentary Assembly of the Black Sea Economic Cooperation Zone has succeeded in such a short period of time in creating its own structures within the economic zone represents a contribution to the promotion of a climate of trust, favorable to cooperation, and to the stimulation of the governments in proving flexibility and realism, said the Romanian president. "In spite of the cultural and economic diversity, which sometimes bars collaboration, the Black Sea states have solely the chance of cooperation, are destined, in a positive manner, of course, to develop tight cooperation. At the same time, Europe cannot be conceived without this area, the west European countries cannot ignore this region of big economic interest, which is representing at the same time a very important potential of collaboration in all fields and a bridge to other geographic zones of high interest," said President Ion Iliescu. "Romania has supported from the very beginning and in the most active manner the cooperation idea between the countries situated on the shores of the Black Sea and continues to assign to it increased importance as a connecting factor in the promotion of the general European cooperation, in extending the North-South cooperation, the cooperation between the Western world and the Middle East and, last but not least, between Europe and Asia," said President Iliescu. "I hope that the current meeting will represent, along with a valuable contribution to the consolidation of the parliamentary component of the economic cooperation of the Black Sea, also an important step in the promotion of a climate of understanding and tolerance, in the achievement of a strengthened regional stability, in preventing the conflict situations, for the development of the region, and, last but not least, for closer relations among our countries," said Romanian President Ion Iliescu.
