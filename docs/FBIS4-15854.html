BFN [Excerpts] The news conference in the Moncloa palace given by Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez and Russian leader Boris Yeltsin, has just ended. [passage omitted] The situation in Bosnia and the allied air strikes were obvious subjects dealt with by Felipe Gonzalez and Boris Yeltsin. Angel Garcia reports from the Moncloa Palace: [Garcia] Boris Yeltsin made clear reference to the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovia. Yeltsin expressed his discontent and disagreement to U.S. President Bill Clinton and he also said that only a negotiated solution could be considered and he ruled out the use of force in Bosnia. At the same time he said that if the conflict escalated, responsibility would fall on those who have carried out these attacks. However, according to Yeltsin, the most important factor in the situation in Yugoslavia is to keep the Serbs away from the military confrontation. [Begin Yeltsin recording in Russian with superimposed Spanish translation by Garcia] Our envoy Vitaliy Churkin is already there, Yeltsin says, and perhaps it will be necessary to send Foreign Minister Kozyrev there the day after tomorrow to ensure that the Serbs stay away from the military confrontation. [end recording] [Garcia] Yeltsin also referred to NATO's peace association and he said that they were in no hurry to sign that document. [passage omitted]
