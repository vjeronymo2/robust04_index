Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow January 14 TASS -- The Russian and US Presidents urged to observe human rights and denounced aggressive nationalism and political extremism in a joint statement adopted on Friday. The Presidents "share the view that full guarantees of respect for human rights and fundamental freedoms of all persons are indispensable for the maintenance of good relations between countries and the strengthening of stability and security in the world", according to the statement on human rights. Boris Yeltsin and Bill Clinton agreed that "aggressive nationalism and political extremism are the main threat to peace and democracy today" and reaffirmed their resolve to eliminate discrimination, intolerance, racial and national prejudices, xenophobia and anti-Semitism. The presidents said they would take coordinated steps to boost activities of international organizations in order to improve human rights practices in the world. Bill Clinton reaffirmed the support of his country for democratic reforms in Russia and the two presidents agreed that democratic transformations in Russia are of great importance for promoting democracy and human rights in the world, as well as for maintaining international stability and security.
