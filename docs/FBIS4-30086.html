BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 23 KYODO -- Japan's automobile production in May dropped 15.3 percent from a year earlier to 734,671, marking the 20th straight year-on-year decline, an industry association said Thursday [23 June]. The Japan Automobile Manufacturers Association said passenger car output totaled 543,691, down 16.6 percent from a year earlier, posting the 14th consecutive year-on-year drop. Production of medium-sized cars with engine displacement of more than 2000 cc was down 1.5 percent at 169,383. Small cars with engines of 661 to 2000 cc were down 24.2 percent at 319,074 and minicars with 600 cc or less were down 7.1 percent at 55,234. Truck production marked the 34th consecutive month of decline, falling 10.9 percent to 187,535. Bus production plunged 28.9 percent to 3,445 for the 11th consecutive year-on-year decline, the association said. The May production of four-wheeled vehicles brought the cumulative production for the first five months of this year to 4,283,705, down 12.4 percent from a year earlier. Motorcycle production in May dropped 17.1 percent from a year earlier to 205,451, extending its string of declines to 14 months.
