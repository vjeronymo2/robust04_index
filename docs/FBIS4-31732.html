BFN [Text] Representatives of the parliamentary parties of the Democratic Convention of Romania [CDR] and the Democratic Party [PD] held a meeting at the Nastase Club this morning. Following the meeting they issued a communique which says that the signatory parties -- the PNTCD [National Peasant Christian-Democratic Party], the PAC [Civic Alliance Party], the PSDR [Romanian Socialist Democratic Party], the PER [Romanian Ecology Party], the PL-93 [Liberal Party-1993], the UDMR [Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania], and the PD -- representing the entire opposition in parliament, after having discussed the current political situation, including certain statements and actions by Romanian President Ion Iliescu that are unworthy of his presidential office and contradict the spirit of Constitution, have agreed to invite President Ion Iliescu to give the necessary explanations in front of a joint session of the chambers of Parliament before the end of the current session. The communique also says that, depending on the president's answer to the invitation, the signatory parties reserve their right to further act according to stipulations of the Constitution. The communique also says that the signatory parties reached agreement regarding certain joint political actions they will carry out over the coming period of time.
