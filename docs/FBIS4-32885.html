BFN [Text] Caracas, 27 May (EFE) -- Enzo del Bufalo, minister of the Central Office of Coordination and Planning, today resigned in what appears to the beginning of a crisis in Rafael Caldera's government. Del Bufalo has become the first minister of Caldera's government to resign from his post in the 114 days of the current administration. Nevertheless, it has been pointed out that in the coming hours other officials will leave the cabinet team. Christian Democrat Caldera is preparing to make important changes in his government team, which in the eyes of his political adversaries, has offered an image of contradictions with regard to his policies, particularly in the economic field. The changes, it appears, are an attempt to revitalize his government, which appears trapped between the severity of the crisis and social demands. Today, the Venezuelan press points out that all the ministers will place their posts at the president's disposal, thus making it easier for Caldera, who assumed power on 2 February, to make the changes he may deem necessary. The opposition has accused Caldera of offering a contradictory image with regard to the policies that he insists on applying, and his ministers have found themselves in seriously contradictory positions. Nevertheless, it is a tradition of the old-time politician, who already governed Venezuela between 1969-74, to make his decisions under total secrecy until he decides to inform the nation, such as when he designated his cabinet a few hours before assuming the presidency. The departure of Transport and Communication Minister Cesar Quintini has been reported by the Venezuelan media as a fact, although he has denied his resignation. Quintini is accused of maintaining a tolerant attitude with regard to corruption in his ministry. Rafael Irribarren, deputy minister of transport and communication, resigned one month ago from his post after accusing Quintini of obstructing his efforts to dismantle the complex network of corruption that functions in that ministry. Irribarren charged that in his ministry, licenses, permits, and other official documents are sold and that commission fees are charged for granting concessions. Local political sources claim that Quintini's continued presence in his post has created serious doubts about Caldera's will to struggle against corruption, one of the electoral banners that took him to the presidency. Other ministers are unhappy because they believe they have not received the support they need for a successful start to their duties. In almost four months of government, Caldera has had to face a grave economic crisis, with a fiscal deficit of more than $4.5 billion. The devaluation of the bolivar has become a pressing problem for Caldera. The dollar, which at the beginning of the month had been valued at 127.15 bolivares, this past Wednesday was valued at 175. It appears that Development Minister Luis Carlos Palacios and Agriculture Minister Ciro Fonseca Anez could also leave the government.
