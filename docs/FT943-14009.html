940716 FT 16 JUL 94 / International Company News: Strong sales lift Texas Instruments to Dollars 2.5bn Texas Instruments, the US semiconductor and electronics manufacturer, reported record second-quarter results, driven by strong sales of semiconductor products. Earnings were above Wall Street expectations, but TI's prediction that world semiconductor market growth will now 'moderate somewhat' hit the shares which closed down Dollars 6 1/8 at Dollars 80 1/4 . Net revenues for the quarter were Dollars 2.5bn, up 19 per cent from last year's Dollars 2.1bn. Profits from operations were Dollars 292m, up from Dollars 173m, while net income was Dollars 184m, or Dollars 1.93 a share, against Dollars 112m, or Dollars 1.18. Semiconductor revenues rose by 29 per cent, with strong growth in memory chip revenues. 'The Asia-Pacific region continues to be the fastest growing semiconductor market in the world,' TI said. Half-year revenues were Dollars 4.96bn, up 24 per cent from Dollars 3.99bn a year earlier. Net income was Dollars 318m, or Dollars 3.35 a share, against Dollars 192m, or Dollars 2.04. The latest results include a Dollars 132m pre-tax charge for European restructuring and a Dollars 69m gain from royalty revenues.
