BFN [Text] According to a military source, the U.S. imperialists staged a nighttime airborne refueling exercise for a northward invasion on 18 and 19 April. For this war exercise, two KC-130 refueling planes from an overseas base were deployed to a U.S. air base in South Korea in the afternoon of 18 April. These refueling planes, which were mobilized on the skies over the sea off Kunsan around 2100 hours [1200 GMT], that day refueled some 50 various types of fighters-bomber and assault fighters, flying between Oeyon island and Taehuksan island until dawn of the next day. This war exercise, which was frantically staged under the cover of fighters from U.S. air bases in Japan, was aimed at improving the capability of nighttime airborne refueling to airplanes mobilized from overseas air bases and long-range airplanes belonging to the U.S. Air Forces unit in South Korea and the puppet Air Force for a northward invasion.
