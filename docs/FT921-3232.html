920316 FT 16 MAR 92 / Slaughters tops league table of profitability SLAUGHTER and May, the City solicitors, has emerged as the UK's most profitable law firm last year according to a league table of earnings published by the magazine Legal Business. Slaughter and May had average net profits per partner of Pounds 377,000 on a turnover of Pounds 113m and profits per fee earner (lawyer) of Pounds 65,000. The firm has 542 fee earners. Its nearest rival was Linklaters & Paines with profits per partner of Pounds 349,000 on a turnover of Pounds 144m and profits per fee earner of Pounds 69,000. Linklaters has 692 lawyers. The UK's largest law firm, Clifford Chance, not surprisingly had the biggest turnover of Pounds 232m. With more than 200 partners and 1,122 fee earners its profits per partner were Pounds 278,000. Most of the top 10 most profitable firms had gross fee revenues of about Pounds 1m per partner, according to the legal business statistics. The magazine says it has had the co-operation of partners in the vast majority of firms in compiling its figures, although there was a 'handful' of firms for which it made 'informed guesses'. None of the firms included in the league table would admit yesterday to having co-operated with the magazine. Categorical denials of co-operation were given by Slaughter and May, Freshfields, Lovell White Durrant, Allen & Overy, Linklaters & Paines and Clifford Chance. No firm was prepared to comment on the accuracy of the figures, however. The magazine stresses that profits per partner did not represent take-home pay. There are a few senior partners in London firms who earn more than Pounds 1m a year but in general earnings for partners in the top firms range from about Pounds 200,000 a year for junior partners to Pounds 500,000 for senior partners, it says. Most large law firms operate a 'lockstep system' which means their earnings are capped after a certain number of years in the partnership.
