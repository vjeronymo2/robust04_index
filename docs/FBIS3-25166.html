Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Inkatha Freedom Party [IFP] has called for the scrapping of Section 29 of the Internal Security Act, which allows for detention without trial, and castigated the Transitional Executive Council for not abolishing the legislation. This followed the TEC's decision to retain provisionally Section 29 of the Internal Security Act. IFP spokesman Ed Tillet said in a statement in Durban that the TEC's decision made a mockery of the lengthy campaign fought by the ANC [African National Congress] and its allies to have the legislation removed from the statute books.
