June 28, 1990, Thursday, Southland Edition

NATION IN BRIEF;

WASHINGTON, D.C.;

HOUSING BILL CLEARS SENATE ON 96-1 VOTE

The Senate approved a sweeping overhaul of the nation's housing policies. The 96-1 vote would make available $17.9 billion next year -- an increase of $3.1 billion from the current level -- for housing for low- and moderate-income families. The Bush Administration supports the Senate bill but opposes a measure awaiting House action that would increase federal housing programs by far more.
