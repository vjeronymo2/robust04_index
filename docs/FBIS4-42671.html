BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Thai Foreign Minister Prasong Sunsiri has concluded his Ankara contacts and left for Istanbul. He was seen off at Esenboga Airport by Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin. In an airport statement, Cetin said that hopeful decisions on promoting bilateral relations were made during Sunsiri's visit. Recalling that the Turkish Airlines has three weekly flights to Bangkok, Cetin added that they have agreed to increase the number of flights. He noted that decisions have also been reached to bring together the private sectors, especially the contracting sectors, of the two countries and to continue high-level visits within this framework. Cetin said: [Begin Cetin recording] We believe that if Turkish manpower, development potential, and experience are combined with the technology, the rapid development rate, and market quests of Asian countries, both sides can benefit from the results. No doubt, within this framework, Thailand is an important country in its region. Our cooperation with Thailand will greatly contribute to Turkey's cooperation with that region. [end recording] Sunsiri said his visit has been beneficial for bilateral relations. He added that during his contacts an agreement was also reached to hold the Turkish-Thai joint economic committee meeting in Turkey this year. Sunsiri will leave Turkey this evening.
