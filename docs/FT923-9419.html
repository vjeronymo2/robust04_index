920805 FT 05 AUG 92 / Threat of Labour discord on Maastricht THE THREAT of a schism in the senior ranks of the Labour party over the Maastricht Treaty on European union loomed last night when Mr Bryan Gould spoke out against the accord, Ivo Dawnay writes. In an interview with Sky television, the sole challenger to Mr John Smith in the party's recent leadership election, made it clear that he vigorously opposed the terms of the pact. Mr Smith, who won the leadership with a crushing 91 per cent of the vote, has made known his firm support for the Maastricht Treaty. Mr Gould, Labour's new national heritage spokesman, is reported to have said that many of his colleagues at Westminster had only a scant knowledge of what the treaty terms entailed.
