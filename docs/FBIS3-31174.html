Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Feb 9 (CTK) -- More than one-third of ethnic Slovaks think that the activities of ethnic Hungarian political parties in Slovakia should be banned, while 27 percent of Hungarians think that Prime Minister Vladimir Meciar's Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS) should be banned, the independent daily SME writes today. Twenty-one percent of Hungarians favor a ban on the coalition Slovak National Party (SNS), the paper writes. The public opinion research was carried out by Glasgow University in cooperation with the Gallup polling firm between December 2 and 12, 1993. According to the poll of 667 Slovak citizens, HZDS supporters are more likely to favor a ban on ethnic Hungarian parties. Fifty-two percent of HZDS voters polled expressed this view. Forty-three percent of SNS supporters, 36 percent of Democratic Left Party (SDL) supporters, and 18 percent of Christian Democratic Movement (KDH) supporters shared this view.
