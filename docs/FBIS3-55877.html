Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Yermakova] [Text] Moscow January 11 TASS -- Four out of 17 candidates will run on Wednesday for the post of the speaker of the upper chamber of the Russian parliament, the Federation Council, and First Deputy Prime Minister Vladimir Shumeyko is clearly in the lead. On Tuesday 78 deputies nominated Shumeyko for the post and he needs 86 votes to become the speaker, a half plus one vote of 171 registered members of the upper chamber. The other three candidates are far behind Shumeyko. Petr Romanov, director of a plant in the Siberian city of Krasnoyarsk, was nominated by 13 lawmakers, Eduard Rossel, former head of the Sverdlovsk region and a staunch advocate of the Ural republic idea, got 10 votes, while former Industry Minister Aleksandr Titkin - 6. Shumeyko told TASS that he would not quit the race even if lawmakers insist on that. "I do not intend to withdraw my candidature even if I am offered to", he said.
