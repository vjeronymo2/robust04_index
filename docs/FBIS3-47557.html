Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, January 13 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The Constitutional Court of Mongolia has established the breach of the constitutional provision committed by a member of the Mongolian Parliament T. Turmandah. According to the Constitutional Court, the member of the Great State Hural has violated Article 29 of the Constitution of Mongolia which prohibits members of the Parliament to be engaged in activities which are not referring to those prescribed by the law. By taking this decision the court has met the complaints of a number of Mongolian citizen, all the delegates of the opposition in the parliament concerning the actions of T. Turmandah being engaged in commercial business as a director of the independent television broadcasting company MCHT-1. T. Turmandah became the first member of a new parliament of Mongolia to have committed the breach of the legislation of the country for a period of year and a half of its activities.
