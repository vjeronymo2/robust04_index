Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier Zou Jiahua met with Craig Barrett, chief operating officer and vice-president of the U.S.-based Intel Corporation, and his party here this evening. During the meeting, Zou said that China and Intel have already had 11 years of cooperation, and that he hoped such cooperation will be further developed. Barrett expressed his satisfaction at the cooperation between his company and the Chinese side, hoping that the two will have yet more cooperation in computers and communications. After the meeting, the Chinese vice-premier attended a signing ceremony for a technology cooperation agreement between Intel and the China Electronics Corporation. The Intel Corporation, founded in 1968, is the biggest manufacturer of integrated circuits, and a main maker of personal computers, networks and communications.
