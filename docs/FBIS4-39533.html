BFN [Text] The Moldovan Vice-Premier Ion Gutu told a news conference in Kishinev [Chisinau] on Wednesday [8 June] that in 1994 through 1997 the priorities of the new cabinet will be the strengthening of the economy, reforms, social security and the expansion of foreign trade. He also said that the government program of action will be discussed by parliamentary committees and then by a plenary session of parliament. Gutu said the country has made certain financial achievements: in May the inflation rate was only 4 percent. Falling production remains the main problem. In the first five months industrial production amounted to 71 percent of last year's level. The number of employed in industry dropped by a third. Many factories are sending their workers on unpaid leave. Gutu said the government wants to suggest to parliament making urban land an article of trade, which in his opinion could attract foreign investments in the Moldovan economy.
