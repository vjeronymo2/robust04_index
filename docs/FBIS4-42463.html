BFN [Unattributed article: "Italy, Germany To Serve Two Years on Security Council"] [Text] (ANSA) New York, June 1 -- Italy and Germany have been put forward by the Western nations to take over, January 1, from New Zealand and Spain for a two-year stint among the UN Security Council's 10 nonpermanent members, an Italian spokesman confirmed here this morning. The candidacy was made after the withdrawal of Ireland, Greece, and Sweden, which said it will be seeking the job for 1997-98. The appointment is expected to be approved by the UN General Assembly in October. The Security Council has five permanent members -- the United States, Russia, China, Britain and France -- and 10 nonpermanent members rotating every two years. Italy and Germany are among the countries which have repeatedly requested an enlargement of the Council.
