930422 FT 22 APR 93 / International Company News: Improvements at two Canadian newsprint groups TWO large eastern Canada newsprint companies posted improved first-quarter results, signalling an end to the three-year slump in the industry. Donohue, controlled by the Quebecor publishing and printing group, reported profits of CDollars 6.3m (USDollars 5m), or 18 cents a share, against a loss of CDollars 8.3m, or 26 cents, a year earlier, on sales ahead at CDollars 135m, against CDollars 114m. Quno cut its loss to CDollars 4.2m, or 30 cents, from CDollars 15.7m a year earlier, on sales of CDollars 127m, against CDollars 106m. The Chicago Tribune remains Quno's biggest single shareholder and customer.
