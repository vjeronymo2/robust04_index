BFN [Summary] Simferopol -- On 20 June presidential candidate Volodymyr Lanovyy visited Crimea where he met Viktor Mezhak, the deputy chairman of the Crimean Supreme Council. He also took part in a meeting of the higher economic council, which was was formed on 19 June under the Crimean Supreme Council presidium. In his speeches Volodymyr Lanovyy supported the idea of democracy and economic regionalization. In his view, it is necessary to give the regions the right of autonomy in budgetary and tax affairs. He supported the idea of private ownership of land, but in the form of shares in collective or cooperative farms. Speaking about his economic program, Volodymyr Lanovyy said that the duties on exports and imports are very high and that he would abolish export duties. As regards import duties, he considered that it should be no higher than 5 percent. He also believes there should be no restrictions on trade with the exception of those on strategic goods.
