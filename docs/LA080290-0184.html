August 2, 1990, Thursday, Southland Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

BULGARIA;

OPPOSITION LEADER CHOSEN AS PRESIDENT

Bulgarian lawmakers elected opposition leader Zhelyu Zhelev as the nation's first non-Communist president in more than four decades.

Thousands poured into the streets of Sofia, the capital, in a spontaneous demonstration. The election of Zhelev, 55, ended a four-week deadlock since the resignation of Petar Mladenov, a member of the ruling Communist Party, now renamed the Socialist Party. Zhelev's elevation, with Socialist support, was seen as a commitment to pluralism by the government.
