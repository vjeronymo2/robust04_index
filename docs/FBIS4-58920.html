BFN [Text] Gaza -- Attorney Khalid al-Qidrah, head of the Cairo talks Legal Committee, has said that it has been agreed that all Palestinians who were registered in the Gaza Strip, the Jericho area, and the West Bank up to 4 June 1967 will be repatriated to their homeland and families. Al-Qidrah said that the agreement does not include only those who resided in the West Bank and the Gaza Strip after the occupation and emigrated, but also those who lived there before the occupation. Agreement has been reached on the repatriation of these Palestinians as well. Al-Qidrah noted that if a resident of the Gaza Strip or Jericho was traveling, studying, or employed abroad when the occupation took place, he has the right to return in accordance with the Declaration of Principles accord signed on 13 September. Al-Qidrah said that talks over the timetable of this repatriation will be held within the coming days.
