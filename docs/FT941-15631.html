940114 FT 14 JAN 94 / Colombian surrender hits snags Negotiations on a possible mass surrender by members of the world's biggest cocaine cartel have hit snags, Colombian government officials said yesterday, Reuter reports from Bogota. Lawyers acting for members of the Cali cartel this week reiterated their bosses' willingness to leave trafficking and surrender, but said they wanted to serve their sentences under house arrest. Justice Minister Andres Gonzalez said: 'Policy was set on the subject of surrendering; it is very clear. People who wish to take advantage of the law must surrender, must serve their sentences and must serve them in jails.'
