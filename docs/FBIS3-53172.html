Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Amman, PETRA--Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali has met with foreign journalists at the Prime Ministry and answered questions about the latest regional and local political developments. [passage omitted] Concerning reports on a joint Moroccan-Bahraini initiative to achieve reconciliation between Jordan and Saudi Arabia, the prime minister said that such an initiative constitutes a good opportunity which Jordan welcomes, especially since it is in harmony with the policy of reconciliation, particularly with the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, with which we have joint neighborly relations and interests, and which plays an effective role in the Gulf region. He added that the relations with Kuwait might need a longer time. About Jordan's relations with Iraq, the prime minister said: The fraternal Iraqi people have suffered a great deal and are still suffering. We should all, in the Arab homeland, seek to alleviate their suffering. He stressed that our relations with all the Arab states are based on mutual respect and on noninterference in the others' affairs. [passage omitted]
