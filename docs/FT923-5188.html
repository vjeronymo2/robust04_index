920902 FT 02 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Cost control gives lift to Macfarlane STRICT control over costs helped Macfarlane Group (Clansman) lift profits 13 per cent in the first half of 1992. On static turnover of Pounds 42.4m (Pounds 42.2m), pre-tax profits were Pounds 4.55m (Pounds 4.02m). Earnings per share rose to 6.06p (5.33p) and the interim dividend is increased from 1.75p to 1.88p. Lord Macfarlane, chairman of the Glasgow-based packaging company, said that strict cost controls would ensure the improvements would continue despite 'the recession lasting longer than the economists, and others who should have known better, predicted.' At the end of the period the company had no borrowings and cash in hand of Pounds 6m.
