Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on regional guerrilla activities and clashes with government military units monitored through 3 February; source follows in parentheses after each item.

Colombia
Venezuelan authorities reported on 26 January the capture of "Commander Danilo," one of the top commanders of the FARC (Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia), at the Colombian-Venezuelan border near the town of Guasdualito, Apure State. Commander Danilo was arrested while in the company of three other individuals who are accused of the murder of several Venezuelan soldiers, as well as dynamite attacks, ambushes, and kidnappings. On their part, Colombian authorities have reported eight additional rebels from the FARC and the ELN (Army of National Liberation) were arrested in Barrancabermeja in possession of 560 rounds of 9-mm ammunition, 104 rounds of 12-gauge ammunition, and 12 electric fuses. Colonel Rodrigo Rojas Guerra has reported approximately 150 rebels of the National Guerrilla Coordinating Board (CNG) have deserted from the ELN over the last 14 months. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 27 Jan 94, pp 1, 6) The Army has reported a commander of the dissident wing of the EPL (Popular Liberation Army) known as "Moises" was killed in the Uraba region and that seven additional rebels were arrested. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL NUEVO SIGLO in Spanish 28 Jan 94, p 25) Military sources have reported that on 29 January FARC rebels wounded a noncommissioned officer and two soldiers in Aquitania jurisdiction, Boyaca Department. The sources added a helicopter belonging to Ecopetrol (Colombian Petroleum Enterprise) was attacked with rifle fire by FARC rebels and forced to make an emergency landing near Toquilla, Casanare Department. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 30 Jan 94, p 6-A) The Venezuelan news agency VENPRES reported on 3 February that stock breeder Danilo Arismendi, who was kidnapped in October by ELN rebels near the Colombian border, was set free after his family paid a ransom of approximately $47,000. Arismendi arrived home in El Guayabo on 2 February. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1521 GMT 3 Feb 94) Authorities in Cucuta have dealt a harsh blow to the ELN by seizing a large number of weapons and arresting four rebels. More than 23,000 rounds of Venezuelan-made ammunition, FAL assault rifles, 9-mm pistols, explosives, a computer, and a book entitled "State Terrorism," which denounces alleged human rights violations by the certain officers, were seized by Army intelligence personnel during the search of a house in Bogota. According to the Army, the weaponry belongs to the Northeastern Commando of the ELN, which operates in the Arauca, Santander, and Norte de Santander Departments. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0000 GMT 4 Feb 94)
El Salvador
International Danger & Disaster Assistance, a Belgian firm, "officially" declared on 28 January El Salvador clear of the mines placed by the Army and guerrillas during the 12-year war that ended in January 1992; nevertheless, company representative Michel Jean-Pierre warned the Salvadoran people there may still be some devices, and he recommended children be educated in that regard. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2143 GMT 28 Jan 94)
Guatemala
Authorities in Coban, Alta Verapaz Department, reported on 27 January that unidentified individuals blew up a stretch of the oil pipeline between Rubelsanto and Santo Tomas de Castilla, Izabal, causing thousands of quetzals in losses. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 28 Jan 94 p 2) URNG guerrillas issued a communique on 1 February reporting they had inflicted 17 casualties on Army troops in clashes in Quiche Department. The URNG said 14 of the casualties were inflicted during an attack against the military barracks in Mayaland, Quiche Department, where they seized four M-16 rifles. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1814 GMT 1 Feb 94) Israel Chavez Perez, alias Commander Ivan, a member of the Javier Tambriz Front of the guerrilla group Revolutionary Organization of the People in Arms, has turned himself in to the human rights attorney general in Retalhuleu and has requested amnesty. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 3 Feb 94 p 2)
Peru
Twenty-five Shining Path rebels were killed in two clashes with Army troops, and 243 rebels, who had surrendered, were presented to the media today in the northern jungles of Peru. Army chief Nicolas Hermoza announced the clashes took place in Saposoa and San Jose de Sisa, San Martin Department. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1910 GMT 26 Jan 94)
