Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Stepanakert, January 29 (SNARK) -- Today morning Vladimir Kazimirov, the Russian President's Special Ambassador, completed his negotiations with the leadership of the Republic of Nagorno-Karabakh. The negotiations lasted two days. The Karabakh Department of Press and Information (DPI) told SNARK the sides continued discussing the Russian peaceful initiative on the Karabakh conflict settlement. Meanwhile, during the Kazimirov's staying in Stepanakert the Azerbaijani aircraft three times invaded the Karabakh airspace. About half past three the Azerbaijani jets bombarded the suburbs of Stepanakert. "I consider wrong and unacceptable these actions of the Azerbaijani aircraft", Kazimirov commented on the last air-raid. DPI himself estimated these actions of Azerbaijan as an obvious provocation aiming to fail the intermediary efforts of Russia. Today morning Kazimirov has left Stepanakert. Stepanakert, January 29 (SNARK) -- Vladimir Kazimirov, the Russian President's Special Ambassador, has arrived today morning in Yerevan from Stepanakert. The Karabakh Ministry of Foreign Affairs told SNARK on January 28 in the evening the Ambassador discussed with the Karabakh leaders the problem of truce conclusion. Earlier the Karabakh authorities more than once declared that they are in favour of any neutral intermediary initiatives on peaceful settlement of the Karabakh conflict. After the negotiations in Yerevan Vladimir Kazimirov is departing to Baku.
