BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow April 15 TASS -- Moldova has joined the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) and became its full member, the ITAR-TASS correspondent has learned at the close of a session of the CIS Council of the Heads of State in the Kremlin today. On the strength of the session results, the leaders of CIS countries are to sign about 12 documents and other accords. According to President Leonid Kravchuk of Ukraine, "the CIS countries found common points of view on the matters discussed". "The most important thing at today's meeting is that good mutual understanding has been reached on all problems discussed," Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze told ITAR-TASS. Shevardnadze said CIS countries' leaders discussed mainly economic problems, free trade principles, economic cooperation committees, and some other issues connected with conflict-ridden areas. The Georgian leader told journalists that participants in the meeting "did not discuss the question of accession of a number of CIS countries to NATO's partnership-for-peace programme."
