BFN [Text] This afternoon at the Prime Minister's Office in Vientiane, His Excellency [H.E.] Khamtai Siphandon, and H.E. Kang Song-san, premier of the Administrative Council of the DPRK, held official talks. Attending the talks on the Lao side were Khamphoui Keoboualapha, deputy prime minister and chairman of the Committee for Planning and Cooperation; Somsavat Lengsavat, foreign affairs minister; Bouason Boupphavan, deputy minister attached to the Prime Minister's Office; Thongvan Phanlatsavong, deputy minister of trade; Ponmek Dalaloi, Lao ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to the DPRK; and a number of technical officials. Attending the talks on the DPRK side were Kong Chin-tae, vice premier; Yi Song-tae, chairman of the State External Economic Affairs Committee; Jo Kyu-il, deputy foreign affairs minister; Chong Song-nam and Choe Bong-su, vice chairmen of the State External Economic Affairs Committee; and Chang Yong-chun, DPRK ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to Laos. At the talks, which lasted nearly two hours, the two sides informed each other on their respective internal situations and the confusing changes in the international situation. They reviewed the bilateral relations of the past and during the period of renovation. The two sides pledged to increase cooperation for mutual benefit. The talks were permeated with an atmosphere of intimacy and mutual understanding. At 1700 Kang Song-san and his entourage paid a courtesy call on President Nouhak Phoumsavan at the Hokham Presidential Palace. At 1900 today at the National Assembly hall, the prime minister will host a banquet in honor of the DPRK delegation.
