940124 FT 24 JAN 94 / Observer: Poisoned chalice Who will get the job of selling Europe to the Brits? The search for a candidate to run the European Commission's London office is looking increasingly jinxed; at least one prospective applicant has declined the offer. The recruitment has stalled thanks to an internal EC argument over whether to pick an in-house candidate or gamble on an outsider. Sir Leon Brittan, the EU's chief trade negotiator and senior UK commissioner, believes that spreading the net will ensure the best quality. Other bureaucrats, notably David Williamson, secretary general, are pushing for an insider to run the 25-strong London office. So Geoff Martin, the temporary stand-in, must rate his chances. However, the post has been unfilled since last May which raises the question of whether it is being kept open for an unlucky Tory MEP who loses a seat in the forthcoming European elections. Surely not.
