930209 FT 09 FEB 93 / Bank of France lowers rate of 24-hour emergency lending facility THE BANK of France yesterday lowered its 24-hour emergency lending facility rate by one eighth of a percentage point to 11.5 per cent, writes Alice Rawsthorn in Paris. It was the third cut in a week and was part of its attempts to alleviate pressure on the banking system by bringing down French money market rates, The French authorities were forced to raise short-term interest rates early last month to protect the franc. However, the currency's recent stability, combined with last week's cut in German interest rates, has enabled the central bank to take a more relaxed stance.
