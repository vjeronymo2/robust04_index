BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Demidetskiy] [Excerpts] Kishinev [Chisinau], 25 Jun -- In the course of his official visit to Moldavia [Moldova], Sergey Stepashin, director of the Russian Federal Counterintelligence Service (FCS), today visited the Dniester region. He went there together with Vasile Calmoi, national security minister. During the trip the head of the FCS had a meeting in Tiraspol with Aleksandr Lebed, commander of the 14th Russia Army, which is stationed there, and was acquainted with the operation of its special section. The ITAR-TASS correspondent has learned from Tiraspol that the meeting with Gen. Lebed was held in camera. Oleg Gudyma, deputy security minister of the as yet unrecognized Dniester Republic, who arrived at the Army headquarters, was not allowed to attend the meeting. Speaking to journalists in Tiraspol, Sergey Stepashin said he was satisfied with the agreement on cooperation between the Russian FCS and the Moldavian National Security Ministry, which was signed in Kishinev 24. [passage omitted] On the problem of the Dniester region, representatives of the Russian FCS said: "It should be settled only peacefully, and Russian special services will assist this process using all legitimate means."
