Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The new secretary general of the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation [SAARC] has said that tension between member nations will not affect the group's progress. Yadav Kant Silwal, the forum's new secretary general, said at a briefing at the Overseas Correspondents Association in Bangladesh that regional tensions have not affected the other groupings and there is no reason why it would affect SAARC. He said that he saw no reason why contentious bilateral issues should affect SAARC and added that it was prospering despite these (?problems). He had talks with Bangladeshi Prime Minister Khaleda Zia, who is current chairperson of SAARC, on Tuesday [18 January].
