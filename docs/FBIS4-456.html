BFN [Text] Samo Gudo, Mozambique United Front-Democratic Convergence Party [Fumo-PCDRN] secretary general, has expressed support for holding Mozambique's first multiparty elections on the dates set by the president of the Republic, following a proposal by the National Elections Commissions. In an interview he granted this morning, the Fumo-PCDRN secretary general said that election dates are not the greatest source of concern to his party. He added that the most important thing is that they be held in October, as scheduled. Samo Gudo also noted that the setting of an election date will encourage the international community to make funds available to the opposition parties. (David Amuzanda), of the Patriotic Action Front, FAP, also expressed agreement with the election dates. He conveyed, however, concern about delays in the implementation of parts of the peace process.
