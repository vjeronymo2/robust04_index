931209 FT 09 DEC 93 / November truck sales 26% higher UK sales of new trucks were 26.5 per cent higher last month than in November last year as the recovery in demand for heavy commercial vehicles gathered pace. Registrations of new commercial vehicles rose 6.3 per cent in November year-on-year, the third consecutive monthly increase. Commercial vehicle sales remained depressed in the first half of the year. Yet in the past two months demand from the small-business and local-distribution sector has increased, with rising registrations of medium and heavy vans. The first signs of recovery came from the heavy-truck market late last year, as hauliers started to replace ageing fleets. Registrations of heavy trucks (above 15 tonnes) last month were 37 per cent higher than a year before. The UK is virtually the only truck market in west Europe in which demand is growing. UK truck registrations (above 3.5 tonnes gross vehicle weight) have risen by 15.5 per cent in the first 11 months. Truck sales across west Europe are forecast to plunge more than 20 per cent in the full year, the fourth successive annual decline. European truckmakers are concentrating intense marketing efforts on the UK, as they are hit by declining demand elsewhere. Both MAN of Germany and Scania of Sweden have increased their truck sales in the UK by more than 50 per cent this year.
