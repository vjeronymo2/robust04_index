BFN [Text] Italy is the European country that has said most clearly that it is ready to intervene in Rwanda, but on condition that the intervention is not a unilateral or multilateral one, but it takes place under an international umbrella. Here is Defense Minister Cesare Previti: [Begin Previti recording] We can take part in international humanitarian and peace operations. If we reach agreement to do something, we are ready and we have already said so. Reaching the agreement is the problem. We have tried to speed things up through the WEU [Western European Union]; there will be another meeting on Tuesday [21 June]. The French are insisting on a unilateral operation in which we have no intention of taking part, I believe. [end recording]
