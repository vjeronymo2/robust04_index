Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, March 10 (BTA) -- Turkish drug trafficker Ibrahim Pinar, who got a six-and-a-half-year sentence last year for smuggling large quantities of heroin, will be brought to a second trial. This was ruled by the Supreme Court following a supervisory review of the already enforced sentence, pronounced by the court in Ruse (northeastern Bulgaria) back on July 13, 1993. The drug trafficker was found guilty of smuggling on two occasions a total of 13.5 kg of heroin of 60 percent purity, worth 3,432,000 leva, across the border checkpoints at Kapitan Andreevo and Ruse. The judges of the court in Ruse qualified the case as a grave crime, but pronounced a sentence of six and a half years, although for such cases the law provides between 5 and 15 years. At the same time the court found the defendant innocent of the charges that he carried in his luggage half the amount of heroin when going from one border checkpoint to the other. This sentence was contested by the prosecutor and as a second instance on October 14 the Ruse Regional Court repealed the sentence in its acquitting part and returned the case for trial by another panel of the court. This ruling was criticized by the prosecutor general of the Republic who suggested that the Supreme Court repeals it. In this way, after the Supreme Court judges examined the sentence under the supervisory review, they repealed the amended part and returned the case for reconsideration from the stage of hearing.
