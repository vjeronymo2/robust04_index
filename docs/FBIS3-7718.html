Language: Burmese Article Type:BFN [Text] The National Convention Plenary Session continued at 0900 this morning in the Central Meeting Hall of the President's Residence compound on Ahlone Road, Yangon [Rangoon]. The plenary session was attended by Lieutenant General Myo Nyunt, chairman of the National Convention Convening Commission, Vice Chairmen Lt. Gen. Maung Thint and Brigadier General Myo Thant, and commission members; U Aung Toe, chairman of the National Convention Convening Work Committee [NCCWC], Vice Chairman U Tha Tun, and work committee members; Brig. Gen. Tin Aye, chairman of the National Convention Convening Management Committee, and committee members; delegates; and local and foreign correspondents. U Tin Maung of the delegate group representing technocrats and the intelligentsia acted as alternate chairperson of the plenary session. Dr. Than Nyunt, Daw May Than Lwin, U San Tha Aung, U Mahn Ohn Maung, U Kan Nyunt, U Soe Maung, U Khin Maung Htoo, and U Sai Aung Htun acted as members of the Panel of Chairmen. U Khin Maung Myint, director general of the NCCWC Office and the official in charge of meetings, acted as secretary of the meeting. First, the secretary of the meeting announced the session was valid because 654 of the 694 delegates were in attendance. According to the agenda, delegates from the delegate group representing elected representatives read proposals on suggestions to prescribe fundamental principles related to the state, state structure, and head of state chapters that will be included in drawing up the constitution. At the plenary session, Dr. Thein Lwin; U Tun Aung, alias U Tun Tun Hein; U Saw Oo Reh; and U Hla Min -- representing the National League for Democracy in the elected representatives delegate group -- read proposal reports. The plenary session recessed at 1120. The afternoon session of the National Convention Plenary Session is in progress at the Central Meeting Hall of the President's Residence compound on Ahlone Road, Yangon.
