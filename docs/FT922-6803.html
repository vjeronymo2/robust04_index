920521 FT 21 MAY 92 / International Company News: American Airlines denies holding talks with KLM MR Robert Crandall, chairman of American Airlines, yesterday quashed rumours that the US carrier had been in talks with KLM Royal Dutch Airlines over some form of partnership, possibly involving the purchase of a share stake. Breaking from American's normal policy of not commenting on merger rumours, Mr Crandall said: 'There is no KLM deal - it's absolutely made up.' Earlier this year, merger talks between KLM, the Dutch flagship carrier, and British Airways broke down. Recent newspaper reports have suggested that KLM might have found an alternative suitor in American. Back on the domestic front, Mr Crandall acknowledged the serious competitive problem posed by Southwest Airlines, a small low-cost carrier which competes in a number of short-haul markets. He said American was looking at various options to deal with the situation. These included the possible withdrawal from some markets it serves or 'introducing a re-defined product . . . where our full feature service is more than our customers want or need.'
