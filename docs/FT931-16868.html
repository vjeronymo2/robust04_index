930104 FT 04 JAN 93 / Asean cable to link Thailand, Malaysia TELEKOM Malaysia and the Communications Authority of Thailand (CAT) have signed a Dollars 77m (Pounds 51m) contract with the Mitsui group of Japan to install an optical fibre submarine cable linking Malaysia and Thailand. The cable will be more than 1,300km long and have a capacity of 560 Mbps, equivalent to 7,560 telephone circuits. Called the M-T cable project, the link is part of an optical fibre submarine cable network linking the countries of the Association of South-East Asian Nations (Asean). The M-T project is due for completion in mid-1994 and will be the last part of the Asean network. Links between Malaysia and Singapore and Singapore and Indonesia are due to be completed in the middle of this year. Links between Malaysia, Brunei and the Philippines and between Brunei and Singapore are already in operation.
