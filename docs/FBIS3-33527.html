Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Havana, 17 Feb (PL) -- Cuban President Fidel Castro has met with a visiting delegation of the French National Assembly headed by Andre Santini, president of the French-Cuban Friendship Parliamentary Group and the Production and Exchange Commission. The newspaper GRANMA reports today that the delegation, on invitation from the Cuban parliament, will visit the country from 13 to 19 February. According to the newspaper, the meeting with the Cuban president was marked by a brotherly and friendly atmosphere and a broad dialogue on current topics with the participants commenting on reports about the two countries. His Excellency Mr. Jean Raphael Dufour, French ambassador to Cuba; Carlos Lage Davila, member of the Politburo of the Communist Party of Cuba; and Deputy Alfredo Guevara, president of the Cuban-French Friendship Parliamentary Group, also attended the meeting. According to the newspaper, Deputy Eric Raoult, vice president of the National Assembly, and Deputy Pierre Mazeaud, president of the Commission of Constitutional Laws, Legislation, and General Administration of the Republic, are part of the French delegation, which also includes legislators Muguette Jacquaint, Dominique Busserau, and Gerard Tremege and Parliament and Foreign Ministry officials Claude Lebedel and Marie-Claire Gerardin.
