Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Today, Tuesday [4 January], Spanish Foreign Minister Javier Solana returned to Madrid following his official visit to Tunisia. Solana described the visit as very useful from all points of view because it permitted progress to be made in the various fields of cooperation. Before concluding his visit, Javier Solana was received today by Tunisian President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali, with whom he discussed matters such as bilateral relations, the situaton in the Maghreb countries, and the common desire to proceed toward formulas which would permit greater security in the Mediterranean. The Spanish foreign minister said that another component of this visit to Tunisia was related to the peace process in the Middle East, for which reason he had a meeting with Palestinian leader Yasir 'Arafat.
