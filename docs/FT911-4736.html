910418 FT 18 APR 91 / Arts: Today's Television By the age of 13 most of us had forgotten those joke questions like 'Please sir, do you mean the natives were revolting?' But they were all remembered by Talbot Rothwell who went on to write the scripts for most of the Carry On films. If you are one of those rare people who have never seen one, you can judge the general standard by watching Carry On Up The Khyber (6.00 BBC2), remembering that this is among the better ones. Sadly it looks as though television's tendency to turn moderately personable presenters into hysterical show-offs is beginning to affect Keith Floyd. He used to be a natural enthusiast, but in his new series about cookery in Australia, Floyd On Oz (8.00 BBC2), he looks as though he has overdosed on uppers. Revelations about Chernobyl have been occurring virtually since the explosion. The latest come from This Week (8.30 ITV) on how radioactive debris was removed. Channel 4 repeats the 'controversial' documentary about the killing of unarmed IRA terrorists in Gibraltar, Death On The Rock (9.30).
