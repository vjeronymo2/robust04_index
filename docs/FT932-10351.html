930508 FT 08 MAY 93 / International Company News: French bank buys new HQ CIC Paris, France's fifth largest commercial bank, has bought a new headquarters building in Paris for FFr2.3bn (Dollars 426m) in one of the city's largest property deals for several years. It has bought a 290,000 sq ft building in the heart of the financial district at the junction of Rue de la Victoire and Rue Taitbout. It was built by a partnership of Banque Paribas, Fradim, Cogedim and other financial institutions. CIC will move to the building in the second half of 1994. As part of the deal, the developing partners are buying the old headquarters building of CIC Paris for FFr1.6bn.
