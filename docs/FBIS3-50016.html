Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Bucharest ROMPRES, 19/1/1994 -- The spokesman for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Romania, Mircea Geoana, read to the press at the 19 January news briefing a statement by which the Romanian Foreign Ministry takes note with interest and satisfaction of the signing in Moscow by the presidents of the United States, the Russian Federation and the Ukraine of the documents by which the Ukraine renounces the nuclear strategic missiles deployed in its territory. The Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs reasserts Romania's firm attachment to the aims and principles of non-proliferation of nuclear arms and welcomes this important decision made by the Ukraine, a decision apt to contribute to strengthening security, stability and confidence in our geographical zone, in Europe and the whole world. Romania, while duly appreciating the signing of the Moscow documents as an important example of nuclear disarmament, expresses its hope that other countries holding nuclear armament in their possession will take new steps towards liquidating their arsenals, the statement concludes. Other subjects mentioned at the news briefing: Scheduled for 27-28 January 1994 in Rabat, the proceedings of the eighth session of the Joint Romanian-Moroccan Intergovernmental Commitee for Economic and Technical Cooperation will be chaired by the two countries' foreign ministers. This will occasion Teodor Melescanu an official visit to Morocco at the invitation of state minister in charge with the external affairs and cooperation Abdellatif Filali. The two sides will tackle and establish concrete measures and actions apt to lead to the relaunch of the bilateral political dialogue, to boost commercial relations and favour the furtherance of economic cooperation in fields of joint interest, such as port facilities, road building, drilling, technical assistance, professional training. The sides also propose to negotiate and sign an agreement to guarantee and promote investment, as well as a cooperation protocol between the two foreign ministries and the programme reflecting the 1994-1996 cultural agreement. Friday, 21 January, Foreign Minister Melescanu will have an interview with Joseph Kruzel, U.S. deputy assistant secretary of defence for European and NATO policies, who is heading the U.S. delegation to the meeting of the U.S.-Romania bilateral working group on matters regarding political-military cooperation relations. Spokesman for the Foreign Ministry Mircea Geoana also told the 19 January briefing that the message of Mrs. Madeleine Albright, U.S. ambassador to the United Nations, special envoy of American President Bill Clinton, who paid a visit in Romania Saturday, January 15, was considered, alongside recent statements by President Bill Clinton and Vice President Al Gore, to substantially back Romania's efforts to reinforce its stability and security in a troubled regional framework and in a larger context, in order to consolidate the Romanian democratic society.
