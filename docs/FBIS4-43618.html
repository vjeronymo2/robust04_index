BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Nevsehir deputy Esat Kiratlioglu and Adana deputy Selahattin Kilic returned to Turkey today after attending the 40th session of the Western European Union [WEU] Assembly in Paris. At a news conference at Istanbul Ataturk Airport on their return, the two deputies pointed out that two separate reports were drawn up to make three NATO member countries, including Turkey, full members of the WEU. Kiratlioglu pointed out that one of these reports was accepted. [Begin Kiratlioglu recording] Turkey has taken a major step toward becoming a full member of the WEU. We believe that our association membership will be discussed and a conclusion will be reached in November. In the following stage, Turkey will be accepted as a full member of the WEU Assembly. [end recording]
