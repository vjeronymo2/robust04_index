BFN [Text] Palestinian leaders are continuing widespread political activities in East Jerusalem, which are coordinated with the Palestinian Authority in the Gaza Strip and Jericho. The Palestinians are now saying that they are prepared to renew the negotiations with Israel on the transfer of authorities in the other West Bank towns. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports: [Begin recording] [Amir] The furor surrounding the letter written by Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres has led to a confidence crisis among Palestinian leaders. The affair has been exploited by Palestinian supporters of the peace process who oppose the Gaza-Jericho First agreement and believe that it is detrimental to the Palestinian cause. Faysal al-Husayni, as if to highlight the political activities at Orient House, received a delegation of foreign consuls in his office and Palestinian leaders continued yesterday to hold discussions on Jerusalem's current situation as well as its future. A Palestinian source pointed out that although the famous letter sent to the Norwegian foreign minister was not addressed to the PLO, the letter's contents had undoubtedly been coordinated with the PLO. To prove his point, the source noted that he had a copy of the letter before it was signed by Foreign Minister Peres, which was dated 9 September. Jamil al-Tarifi, a member of the higher liaison committee and one of 'Arafat's closest aides, yesterday emphasized that at the time, the letter of commitment guaranteed that the Palestinian delegation could carry out the political activities in East Jerusalem that were necessary in order to conduct negotiations with Israel. Al-Tarifi asserted that it was impossible to separate the political activities of PLO institutions in East Jerusalem and the activities of the autonomy institutions, which will indeed be located in the Gaza Strip and Jericho, as stipulated by the agreement. [Al-Tarifi, in English] Everybody knows that we have many institutions in Jerusalem. It will be as it is, and of course it's a Palestinian institute and we will not change anything on it. So, nobody can cut the relations between the Palestinians and their leadership, which is PLO and one of its organizations PPA [sentence as heard]. [Amir translates above remarks into Hebrew and continues] Al-Tarifi expressed the assessment that now, following the establishment of the Palestinian Authority, the negotiations with Israel on the transfer of authorities could be resumed. Al-Tarifi said that the Palestinians would demand authority over more than the five spheres of health, education and culture, tourism, social welfare, and direct taxation stipulated by the Gaza-Jericho First agreement. [Tel Aviv IDF Radio in Hebrew at 0500 GMT on 8 June adds that "the PLO has rejected an Israeli proposal to release hundreds of Palestinian prisoners tomorrow after Israel demanded that the prisoners remain in the Gaza Strip before returning to their homes until their original prison terms were over. This was stated by Jamil al-Tarifi, a member of the Palestinian Authority. Al-Tarifi said that Yasir 'Arafat had instructed him over the telephone yesterday to reject the release proposal and said that the Israeli offer contravened the Cairo agreement. He expressed the hope that this was merely a misunderstanding and that an agreement would be reached on the release of the Palestinian prisoners."]
