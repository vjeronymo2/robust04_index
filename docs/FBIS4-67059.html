for U.S. PA0505175594 Madrid EFE in Spanish 0001 GMT 5 May 94 PA0505175594 Madrid EFE Spanish BFN [Text] Mexico, Federal District, 4 May (EFE) -- The Mexican Judicial Police have seized 5.8 tons of marijuana worth $36 million, a spokesman for the Attorney General's Office (PGR) reported on Wednesday. The operation was conducted in La Paz, capital of Baja California Sur State, some 1,250 kms northeast of Mexico City. According to the available information, the seizure was made at a La Paz loading terminal during the inspection of a trailer that had come from the port of Mazatlan, Sinaloa State, one of the states with the highest incidence of narcotrafficking. According to the driver, the truck was bound for the California border, where the drug would have a commercial value of $36 million. The PGR is holding the truckdriver pending a decision on any penal action against him for crimes against the public health. It has also begun investigating the plan and possible accomplices of the prisoner, as well as the place of origin and the destination of the drugs.
