931018 FT 18 OCT 93 / Latin American leaders in trade plea to Clinton LATIN AMERICAN leaders are to write to President Bill Clinton urging the US Congress to approve the North American Free Trade Agreement. Failure to do so, they will say, would be a bleak omen for US policy towards the continent. The decision to send the letter, to be drafted by Chile's President Patricio Aylwin, was taken in Santiago at the weekend during a two-day summit of the Rio Group of Latin American and Caribbean states. Leaders see Nafta as a vital first stage in building a regional trading bloc, regarded as an insurance policy against possible failure of the Gatt global trade talks. 'Nafta is a first step towards creating a huge free trade zone spanning the continent,' said President Cesar Gaviria of Colombia.
