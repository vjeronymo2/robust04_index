BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Chernov] [Text] Panama May 27 TASS -- Relations between Panama and Russia must be intensified and expanded, according to Panamanian Foreign Minister Gabriel Galindo. He said in an exclusive interview with ITAR-TASS that "we are aware of the fact that there is much to be done to take relations between Panama and one of the world's great powers, Russia, to a level which both countries find satisfactory. Nonetheless we can state with satisfaction now that a groundwork has been laid for the development of mutually beneficial cooperation. The Panamanian foreign ministries of Panama and Russia [as received] has made steps lately which led to an exchange of consulates general. We regard this as a good beginning". According to the Panamanian minister, Russia can use Panama as a "gateway for penetrating Latin America". These gateways can provide an opportunity for the supply of Russian technologies to the countries on that continent and exports of manufactured goods, equipment and raw materials. "We would like Russia to be a partner capable of making a better use of our services, which are undoubtedly the most effective in the region". "The liberalization of the Russian economy enables Panamanian investors to consider seriously prospects for the participation in joint ventures on Russian territory. Our diplomats in Moscow will be entrusted with bringing home to the Russians what advantages cooperation with us can give your entrepreneurs. A case in point is the opening of a Russian bank in our international financial centre," the minister said. The Union of Banana-Exporting Countries with its headquarters in Panama, is now studying the possibility of exports to Russia and Eastern Europe as a whole, an area which has a huge and promising market, the minister said. Gabriel Galindo is a member of the government which is being formed by president Ernesto Perez Balladares who won the country's general elections on May 8. The new government will take office on September 1.
