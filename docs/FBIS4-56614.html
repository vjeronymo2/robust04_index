BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 5 May. Central Bank President Roque Fernandez admitted today that local banks lost 5 percent of their capital because the prices of government securities on the international markets plummeted. Fernandez confirmed that Central Bank reserves total about $16 billion. (Buenos Aires NOTICIAS ARGENTINAS in Spanish 2208 GMT 21 Apr 94) The Economy Ministry has reported that the government's primary surplus totaled 707.7 million pesos in the first quarter of the year. This sum is 217.3 million short of the targets established by the IMF for that quarter. (Buenos Aires NOTICIAS ARGENTINAS in Spanish 2128 GMT 29 Apr 94) A Latin American Economic Research Foundation report notes that during the first quarter the industrial production rate grew 6.4 percent. (Buenos Aires LA PRENSA in Spanish 30 Apr 94 Section 1 p 6) Inflation for the year ended April 30 will be "slightly above 4 percent," Economy Minister Domingo Cavallo said yesterday. As to the retail price index in April, Cavallo's statement indicates that consumer prices last month went up around 0.3 percent. (Buenos Aires BUENOS AIRES HERALD in English 4 May 94 p 5) In a communique, the state-owned Electricite de France (EDF) company announced that it had won a bid on the Rio Diamante hydroelectric complex in Argentina. This bid is part of Argentina's privatization drive in the electric production field. EDF joined an Argentine bank, the Bank of Galicia, for this bid. EDF's bid totaled $32 million. EDF will hold 51 percent of the shares of the concessionary company. (Buenos Aires NOTICIAS ARGENTINAS in Spanish 1459 GMT 5 Apr 94)
