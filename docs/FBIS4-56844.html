BFN [Text] Guatemala City, 9 May (NOTIMEX) -- A former officer of the defunct Panama Defense Forces (FDP) said here today that Panamanian military officers exiled in Guatemala could return to Panama following the election victory of opposition presidential candidate Ernesto Perez Balladares. "We will return when the victory of Perez Balladares in the general elections held this Sunday is confirmed," former FDP Lieutenant Carlos Alberto Rumbo told NOTIMEX. The military officer said the young officers of the defunct Panamanian Army "will return to give back to Panama its dignity, which General Manuel Noriega, President Guillermo Endara, and the Americans trampled on." At least 10 Panamanian military officers sought exile in various Latin American countries following the U.S. invasion in December 1989, which overthrew Noriega. Noriega was later sentenced to 40-years of incarcaration in the United States for drug trafficking activities. Rumbo said he fled Panama after participating in a post-invasion, uprising by National Police members against President Endara; an uprising which Endara described as a "coup attempt." Perez Balladares, from the United People coalition headed by the Democratic Revolutionary Party, became the virtual winner of the Panamanian presidential elections. Perez Balladares, popularly known as "El Toro", has remained in first place with approximately 33 percent of the vote since the Electoral Tribunal (TE) started providing election results yesterday. After 89 percent of the votes were counted, the TE placed Mireya Moscoso de Gruber from the ruling Arnulfist Party in second place with 28 percent of the votes. She was followed by Ruben Blades of the Papa Egoro Movement, who gained 17 percent of the vote. Regarding these figures, Guatemalan analyst Walter Robles said "Toro's" victory was the result of his ability to renew Panamanians' affinity for the policies of the PRD founder, the late General Omar Torrijos. During the election campaign, PRD leaders were able to remove the party from the shadow of deposed General Manuel Noriega, who became Panama's "strongman" following the death of Torrijos in 1981, according to Robles. Panamanian writer Rafael Ruiloba said the new government must find the best way to lead the country down a nationalist path and the best way to recover the dignity lost following the humiliating U.S. invasion. In San Salvador, the country's media reported on the virtual victory of the PRD candidate in Sunday's elections, in which deputies and mayors were also elected. Conservative newspaper LA PRENSA GRAFICA printed on its front page: "Perez Balladares Virtual Winner of Elections in Panama," and right-wing newspaper EL DIARIO DE HOY published: "Balladares Wins Elections in Panama."
