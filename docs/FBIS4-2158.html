BFN [Text] Beijing, April 7 (XINHUA) -- All parties concerned should, in the current situation, avoid taking actions that will sharpen the contradictions in the Korean Peninsula, a Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman said here today. Spokesman Wu Jianmin told the weekly press conference that the maintenance of relaxation and stability of the Korean Peninsula conforms to the common interests of both the Korean people and all other parties concerned. Wu said that China opposes any action harmful to the relaxation and stability in the Korean Peninsula, be it military exercises or deployment of missiles. Wu said that all parties concerned should refrain from taking actions that would sharpen the contradictions in the Korean Peninsula, especially under the current circumstances. He made the statement when asked to comment on the fact that the United States has begun shipping "Patriot" missiles to the Republic of Korea (ROK) and that the United States and the republic of Korea are planning to resume their Team-Spirit joint military exercises. Wu also reiterated China's stand that the nuclear inspection issue should be settled through dialogue.
