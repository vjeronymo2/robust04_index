Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, January 14 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- "According to the prognosis of the Russian specialists our country has uranic ore reserves worth of 1.5 million tons. This is not a small figure and Mongolia needs to appear on the world market. Jointly with Russia and the U.S.A. we will exploit the Haraat deposit in Dundgobi Aymag. Prospecting works will start from this year," said the general director of the Geological Research Department of the Ministry of Geology and Mineral Resources of Mongolia G. Jamsrandorj [spelling as received]. He refuted the rumours about taking on a long-term lease by Russia of the Mardai [spelling as received] uranic ore deposit as a compensation of the external debt of Mongolia and said that the issues are being discussed about changing the terms of the earlier signed agreements and that the sides agreed in principle of foundation of a joint enterprise. According G. Jamsrandorj, prices of uranic ore on the world market will start increasing in 5-7 years, as in the 21st century atomic energy will become a basic source of electric energy in the world. Taking this into consideration, the ministry has founded an independent company "Uranium" for establishment of contacts with the World Nuclear Energy Agency [title as received], well-known companies, exploration of opportunities to appear on the world uranic ore market. In general, the Mongols should dispose themselves their uranium [sentence as received]. At the recent sitting of the National Security Council of Mongolia considered was a report of the Ministry of Geology on the state of geological prospecting and exploitation of the uranic ore deposits, perspectives for this sphere, foundation of joint enterprises, opportunities for appearing on the world market, and ecological issues.
