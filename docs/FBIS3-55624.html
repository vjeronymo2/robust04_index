Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Report by BELINFORM: "Europarliament Delegation in Belarus"] [Text] A Europarliament delegation for cooperation with CIS states has arrived in Minsk. Magdalene Phoff who headed this delegation said that the main aim of the visit is to get acquainted with the political and economic situation in the Republic and to widen the existing contacts between the Europarliament and Belarus. On 4 January, Vyacheslav Kuznetsov, first deputy chairman of the Belarusian Supreme Soviet, received the members of the Europarliament delegation. During the talks, Magdalene Phoff said that the Europarliament will provide Belarus with specific aid. The Europarliament has already drafted a program for cooperation in the sphere of technology, and currently the implementation of this program is being studied. Vyacheslav Kuznetsov, in his turn, spoke about the situation in the Republic. He said that 1994 will become a year of political activity for Belarusian citizens and a turning point for the development of the Belarusian economy. He stressed that the Supreme Soviet elections and the elections of a leader of Belarus will give an impulse to this process. The Supreme Soviet deputies are convinced that presidential elections should be carried out through nationwide elections. Further, Vyacheslav Kuznetsov, emphasized: "It is very important for us to hold presidential elections in the spring of 1994. A new president will form a new government; later, we could elect a new legislature." The first deputy chairman of the Belarusian Supreme Soviet spoke about the moves of the Belarusian Cabinet of Ministers and the Supreme Soviet aimed at the creation of an economic union and a ruble zone of a new type. This will enable us to upgrade cooperation among the CIS states and, particularly, to bolster economic cooperation. Speaking on the historic relations with Russia, Vyacheslav Kuznetsov emphasized that in the new political and economic situation the Supreme Soviet is striving to integrate the Belarusian economy into the world economic community. In connection with this, the lawmakers have adopted a range of laws regarding the foreign economic relations which, in the experts' opinion, correspond to existing international norms. On the same day, Belarusian Foreign Minister Petr Kravchanka and CIS Executive Secretary Ivan Korotchenya received the Europarliament delegation.
