November 24, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

CLOTHESLINES

Question: My husband works in Manhattan and is due for a new coat. He wants to buy a Chesterfield, and I feel that style is too dressy for a lawyer with a lot of conservative clients. Please comment. -- R.R.O.

Answer: Chesterfields are the comeback coat of the season in menswear. Perhaps your husband will find this advice from the coat's namesake, 19th-Century Earl of Chesterfield, as valid today as when it was written in 1840: "Take great care always to be dressed like the reasonable people of your own age; in the place where you are; whose dress is never spoken of one way or another as either too negligent or too much studied."

The Chesterfield with its slight shaping and velvet collar is available in traditional gray and navy herringbones as well as such unexpected colors as taupe and olive.
