BFN [Text] All last night, Ukrainian national television was broadcasting Easter services from churches of various religious confessions, while this morning the Socialist Party called on people to attend an officially sanctioned May Day rally and march to the Lenin monument to lay flowers. At this moment there are three demonstrations in progress in Moscow. They are being organized by the Federation of Trade Unions, the Moscow Labor [Trudovaya Moskva] Movement, the Liberal Patriotic Party of Rebirth, and a bloc of communist parties. Today, for the first of time in recent years, International Labor Day is being celebrated in Yerevan. The workforces of dozens of Yerevan's enterprises and institutions will turn out for a rally in the city. An unsanctioned May Day rally is planned to be held on the central square of Bishkek by the Civic Council [Grazhdanskiy sovet]. This Kyrgyz public organization was set up two months ago under the aegis of the republic's legal rights movement to defend the rights of pensioners and the needy.
