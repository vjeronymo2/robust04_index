BFN [Text] Frankfurt/Main (DPA) -- The Bundesbank has again loosened its interest reins very slightly: In the latest security transaction under repurchase agreement, it allotted short-term money to the banks at the marginal interest rate of 5.73 percent -- 0.03 percentage points under the previous level; 80.4 billion German marks [DM] were allotted, relevant transactions for DM82.1 billion had become due. The great majority of the allotments ranged from 5.73 to 5.75 percent. The rate for security transactions under repurchase agreement is regarded as the central bank's third key rate. Because of the volume, it is far more important for the money market than the classic discount and Lombard key rates, which are currently 5.25 and 6.75 percent respectively.
