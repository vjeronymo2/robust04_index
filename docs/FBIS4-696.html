BFN [Text] Pretoria April 13 SAPA -- A halt on sales of state land was on Wednesday [13 April] called for by the Transitional Executive Council's [TEC] sub-council on regional/local government and traditional authorities. In a statement, the sub-council said it had decided to ask the government to stop the sale of state land without first consulting the sub-council. On the sale of state land in kwaNdebele, the sub-council decided that no land be disposed of unless in exceptional circumstances and specially not through the process being planned by the homeland government; that sales, when necessary, be transparent and based on consensus; that the process of township development proceed on the basis that no decision be taken on entitlement of occupation or ownership; and that the process of establishing the identities of people living on the land in kwaNdebele proceed on the basis that it not involve decisions on who will be entitled to occupation or ownership.
