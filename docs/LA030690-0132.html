March 6, 1990, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: FULLERTON;

HEARING TONIGHT ON COYOTE HILLS PROJECT

The City Council will hold a public hearing at 7:30 tonight to consider allowing 220 homes to be built in the East Coyote Hills.

Newport Beach developer J.M. Peters Co. Inc. will appeal a Planning Commission vote in February to reject the company's plans for 110 houses and 110 condominium units to be built at State College Boulevard and Bastanchury Road.

Some of the commissioners said plans for the gated housing community pack too many units into the hilly area and require too much of the hill to be flattened. The master plan for the area requires buildings to follow the contour of the hill as much as possible.

The plans call for condominiums to be built on graded land near the intersection. The houses would wrap around north, east and south of the condominiums on a lower portion of the land bordering Craig Regional Park. TOM McQUEENEY
