Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] President Glavkos Kliridhis said the Turkish side has given a reply on the confidence-building measures that he described as negative. He added that the five UN Security Council permanent members have asked the UN secretary general to brief them on each side's positions. Kliridhis made this statement while replying to reporters' questions at a luncheon for Greek Independence Day hosted by Greek Ambassador to Nicosia Alexandros Sandhis. Asked whether there is any possibility that the proximity talks will continue until 29 March, Kliridhis said there is very little leeway. He added that he believes the UN secretary general should proceed in preparing his report on the conclusion of the proximity talks and submit it to the Security Council. After today's parade marking the 25 March holiday, Kliridhis was asked to comment on the latest developments in the proximity talks. He said that both the Greek Cypriot side and the five UN Security Council permanent members have asked for an objective report that describes each side's stance.
