930714 FT 14 JUL 93 / UK Company News: Increased investment helps Savills to Pounds 1.4m AN INCREASE of one third in property investment by institutions and cost-cutting helped Savills, the surveyor and estate agent, return pre-tax profits of Pounds 1.44m for the 12 months to April 30, against losses of Pounds 2.86m last time. A final dividend of 1p is proposed; directors omitted the interim having made no payment in the year to April 1992. Earnings per share were 2.2p compared with losses of 6.5p. Group turnover rose to Pounds 25.3m (Pounds 23.7m). Mr George Inge, chairman, said: 'Generally, the mood is improved on a year ago although economic uncertainty continues to dampen market confidence.' The commercial consultancy division, which includes investing for institutions, improved turnover to Pounds 10.7m (Pounds 9.68m) reporting operating profits of Pounds 819,000 (Pounds 1.25m losses). Mr Inge said: 'Our investment activity was up by about a third. Consultancy work on behalf of banks and other lending institutions continued to prosper during the year.' Turnover from the agricultural and residential business was virtually flat at Pounds 14.5m (Pounds 14m) with operating profits of Pounds 458,000 against losses of Pounds 1.66m. Net interest received was Pounds 162,000 (Pounds 48,000). The group finished the year with Pounds 5m (Pounds 1.95m) cash and no borrowings.
