Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] A group of legislators are proposing a decree that would grant President Aristide 48 hours in which to appoint a new prime minister. According to a document circulating behind the scenes in the parliament, a copy of which was sent to Radio Metropole, some deputies [word indistinct] propose the ratification of a decree advocating the implementation of Article 149 of the Constitution in the event that the president of the Republic does not appoint a new prime minister within 48 hours. These deputies also indicate that this prime minister should be appointed in compliance with the spirit of Article 148 of the Constitution, an article that gives full power to the prime minister and his cabinet in case the president of the Republic is indisposed. In a letter addressed to the president of the Chamber, these deputies from the southern region request an urgent vote on their proposal.
