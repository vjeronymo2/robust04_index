BFN [Text] Baku, May 9 (TURAN)--Milli Mejlis' speaker, Rasul Guliyev, on behalf of Azerbaijan signed the Bishkek Protocol according to Vladimir Kazimirov, the special representative to the Russia president on Nagorno-Karabakh. Vladimir Kazimirov arrive to Baku and held talks with Azerbaijan's leadership. He noted that the Protocol calls for an appeal for a ceasefire. [words indistinct] responsibility to observe the ceasefire. However, from the information of May 9 it became known the ceasefire was violated by both sides. He also said that "the Bishkek Protocol must be signed alongside another document that is signed by the leaderships of Armenia, Azerbaijan, Nagorno- Karabakh and Russia". He stated, "The Bishkek Protocol should be treated as an invitation to the fighting parties to begin implementation of Russia's settlement plan". The plan consists of three stages. The first stage involves a ceasefire and to take measures to ensure that it is not broken. A six-day term has been given to implement this stage. The second stage must include the freeing of the captured territories of the six regions outside of Nagorno-Karabakh within thirty-four days, the exchange of prisoners of war, hostages, corpses and free exchange of information about the abovementioned categories of persons, the resumption of transportation lines and energy lines, the return of refugees and displaced persons to their homes. The third stage is to begin the negotiation process including the issue concerning the status of Nagorno-Karabakh. According to Kazimirov, the status of Shusha and Lachin will be resolved during the final stage along with the final status of Nagorno-Karabakh. "Shusha is like Jerusalem. Azerbaijanis say it is theirs, and Armenians say it is theirs. This is not a simple question and this question must be negotiated...it is more useful for Azerbaijan to accept the Russian proposal in order to free 6 regions according to this plan: Agdamskiy, Fizulinskiy, Dzhebrailskiy, Zangelanskiy, Kubatlinskiy, and Kelbadzharskiy. Only after this, can the fate of Shusha and Lachin be discussed", said Kazimirov. He noted that "There are three parties to the conflict: Azerbaijan, Nagorno-Karabakh and Armenia...this is Russia's position and it reflects the existing realities and on realistic steps can settle this problem more effectively."
