Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 24 KYODO -- South Korean President Kim Yong-sam on Thursday [24 March] officially invited Emperor Akihito and Empress Michiko to visit South Korea to forge a new future-oriented relationship, the Imperial Household Agency said. "I would like to promote relations between South Korea and Japan from a forward-looking, future-oriented view," a senior Imperial Palace official quoted Kim as saying. Bilateral relations are still overshadowed by Japan's colonial rule of the Korean peninsula from 1910 to 1945, and anti-Japanese sentiment continues to run high. Kim and his wife, Son Myong-sun, had a 25-minute audience with the imperial couple in an "overall pleasant atmosphere," Kiyoshi Sumiya, grand master of ceremonies, said. "I think it would benefit our friendly neighborly relations if we could welcome the emperor and the empress in South Korea. I would like to seize this good opportunity to offer an invitation," Kim told the imperial couple. The emperor replied it is the Japanese Government's decision on whether he goes abroad. In an interview ahead of the visit, Kim, who was elected last year to a single five-year term, said he believes he will be able to receive the emperor in Seoul during his term in office. The emperor told Kim it is important that both nations deepen their exchange while reflecting on history and respecting each other's cultures. During colonization, Koreans were forbidden from speaking Korean in public and had to adopt Japanese names. In addition, Japan's forced recruitment of tens of thousands of Korean women for wartime military brothels has refueled bitter memories.
