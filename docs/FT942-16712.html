940408 FT 08 APR 94 / UK Company News: Goldsmiths boosted across the board Goldsmiths Group, the retail jeweller which runs 111 branches throughout the UK, returned pre-tax profits of Pounds 1.55m for the 11 months to January 29. That compared with just Pounds 48,000 for the 12 months to February 27, 1993. The figures were achieved on the back of a rise in turnover from Pounds 42.9m to Pounds 44.6m. On a comparable basis, the sales rise was 11 per cent. Mr Jurek Piasecki, chairman, said trading was 'consistently good' across all product groups and through the country as a whole. Exceptional growth was experienced in the south of England. He added that significant increases in sales were recorded each month, culminating with an 'excellent' Christmas period, when sales on a like-for-like basis were 13 per cent up on the previous year The upward trend had continued into the current year with sales in February and March some 15 per cent ahead of 1993's figures. Earnings per share emerged at 5.11p (0.12p) and the single dividend is stepped up from 0.3p to 1p. By the year-end, borrowings had been reduced.
