BFN [Text] In his interview with Interfax Levon Zubaryan, aide of the Armenian president, reconfirmed the information about the preparation for a meeting between Armenian and Azerbaijani presidents on the settlement of the Karabakh conflict scheduled for the near term in Moscow. At the same time he reported that the precise date is as yet unknown. According to Zubaryan, "preparatory diplomatic work is being carried out with the mediation of the Russian side." In Zubaryan's words, this meeting has been foreseen by the protocol signed on the results of the meeting between representatives of the parliaments of Armenia, Azerbaijan, and Nagorno-Karabakh under the mediation of the CIS Inter-Parliamentary Assembly on May 5 in Bishkek. One of the items of the protocol says that the appeals to cease fire in the conflict zone should be fixed "in the near term by signing a reliable and legal agreement which would stipulate a mechanism preventing resumption of any military and hostile actions, withdrawal of forces from the occupied territories, restoration of communications, return of refugees, and continuation of the negotiation process."
