Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Our correspondent Ivan Samel reports from Bratislava Castle on the official appointment of the new ministers, including the formal swearing-in ceremony. [Samel] [passage omitted] Jan Findra, head of the presidential office, read out a statement in which the president appointed Jozef Moravcik as the prime minister of the Slovak Republic and chairman of the State Defense Council. He also appointed Brigita Schmognerova as deputy prime minister responsible for economic and political transformation, Roman Kovac as deputy prime minister responsible for noneconomic activities, and Ivan Simko as deputy prime minister responsible for legislation. Michal Kovac further appointed Rudolf Filkus minister of finance; Peter Magvasi minister of the economy; Mikulas Dzurinda minister of transport; Pavel Koncos minister of agriculture; Milan Janicina minister for privatization and administration of national property; Ladislav Pittner minister of the interior; Pavel Kanis minister of defense; Eduard Kukan minister of foreign affairs; Milan Hanzel minister of justice; Lubomir Harach minister of education; Lubo Roman minister of culture; Tibor Sagat minister of health care; Julius Brocka minister of labor, social affairs, and family; and Juraj Hrasko minister of the environment. After the government was appointed, the new ministers were sworn in by the president of the Republic and confirmed their oath with their signatures.
