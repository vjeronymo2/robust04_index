BFN [Text] Canberra, May 11 (XINHUA) -- Tian Jiyun, vice chairman of the Chinese National People's Congress (NPC), left Brisbane this morning for home after winding up his week-long visit to Australia. Australia was the last leg of Tian's four-nation tour in the South Pacific, which had taken him to New Zealand, Western Samoa and Papua New Guinea. During his week-long stay in Australia, Tian and his entourage visited Canberra, Melbourne, Sydney and Brisbane. In Canberra, he had meetings with Governor-General Bill Hayden, Prime Minister Paul Keating and other senior government officials on increasing relations between the two countries as well as issues of common interest. He also had extensive contacts with Aussie parliamentarians with a view to enhancing links between the two parliaments and exchanged views with local businessmen on expanding economic and trade cooperation. Tian, who started his four-nation South Pacific trip on April 18, described it as "the successful, satisfactory and fruitful one." He said that his visit to the four nations has achieved goals of "increasing understanding, getting acquainted with friends and promoting cooperation." Tian's South Pacific nation trip was seen as China's attaching to the importance of parliamentary diplomacy, which Tian said "can play a major role in increasing friendship and cooperation between China and the countries in the world."
