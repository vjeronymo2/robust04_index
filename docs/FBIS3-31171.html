Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Casta Papiernicka, west Slovakia, Feb 10 (CTK) -- Vladimir Bajan and Marian Kelemen, the deputy chairmen of the ruling Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS), resigned from their positions at the meeting of the HZDS Club of Deputies, a spokeswomen for the party told journalists today. The meeting, which started on Wednesday (9 February), was originally expected to deal with the creation of a parliamentary majority, but it is, in fact, busy with maintaining the unity of the HZDS. The meeting was attended by Foreign Minister Jozef Moravcik, Transport Minister Roman Hofbauer, Agriculture Minister Jozef Baco and Deputy Prime Minister Roman Kovac today. In addition, representatives of the Slovak National Party (SNS), the minor coalition party, were present but when the internal HZDS affairs were discussed, they left the room. According to the independent daily NARODNA OBRODA, the "alternative faction" in the HZDS led by Moravcik and Kovac came into being on Wednesday. No one has, however, confirmed the information. The paper claimed that the 16-deputies-strong faction advocated the idea of creating a broad government coalition without participation of party leaders. They consider the solution to be temporary as they assume early elections called for early 1995.
