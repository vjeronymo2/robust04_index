BFN [Text] Riga, Jun 01, BNS -- As many as 101,298 people, or 6.6 percent of the economically active Latvian residents, were looking for jobs on May 31, according to figures from the State Statistics Committee. From those, 87,792 have registered themselves as unemployed; 34,520 receive allowances; 32,731 are long-term unemployed. As reported, the number of job seakers was 102,200 on May 3. State employment service spokeswoman Ilga Upeniece told BNS that the decreasing number of unemployed could be explained with the beginning of summer. She prognosticated that the figure would be up again in autumn.
