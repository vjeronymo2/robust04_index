BFN [Text] Robert Lamb, the U.S. State Department special coordinator on Cyprus, arrived in Cyprus this afternoon to brief President Glavkos Kliridhis and Turkish Cypriot leader Rauf Denktas on his contacts in Athens and Ankara. Speaking at Larnaca Airport, Lamb said he is trying to make each side understand clearly and precisely the positions and concerns of the other. He also said he would try to achieve progress in the implementation of the package of confidence-building measures. Replying to a question, the U.S. official pointed out that the issue of access to Famagusta is one of the most serious problems but he said he did not want to go into details. Lamb claimed that Ankara is looking for a way out of the situation.
