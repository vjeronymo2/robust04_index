BFN [Text] At a news conference in Nicosia today, Israeli Ambassador in Nicosia Shemi Tzur said Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres will soon visit Cyprus for talks on the peace process for the Middle East. Tzur said Cyprus is becoming more involved in the peace process for the Middle East. He added that President Glavkos Kliridhis will go to Israel on 8 July in conjunction with a sailing competition between Cyprus and Israel and he will meet the Israeli president, prime minister, and foreign minister. The Israeli ambassador reiterated that his country recognizes only one government in Cyprus, the legal government of the Republic of Cyprus. He also denied reports that Israeli planes fly to the occupied areas and described them as exaggerated and wrong. He said: There has been no official Israeli activity in occupied Cyprus. He admitted, however, that a small travel agency, called (?Ricky Tours Inhetera), is cooperating with a Turkish agency and takes tourists to the occupied areas. Tzur said the Israeli Tourism Ministry has sent a letter to every travel agency in the country stressing that the northern part of Cyprus is under occupation and that the Israeli Government cannot offer any assistance to its nationals who visit occupied Cyprus.
