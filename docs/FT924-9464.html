921105 FT 05 NOV 92 / Managers buy out Celmac CELMAC, ONE of Britain's leading manufacturers of toilet seats was yesterday bought out by its management from Burmah Castrol for Pounds 3m. The company, which employs 135 in Manchester and 45 in Wicklow, Ireland, was part of the Foseco group, which Burmah Castrol bought last year. The lavatory seat company was losing money at the time and was being restructured by Mr David Coveney, managing director and leader of the buy-out team. The buy-out was advised by Coopers & Lybrand, the accountants, and Eversheds Alexander Tatham, the solicitors. 3i, the investment capital provider, has taken Pounds 1m of equity, with County NatWest Ventures and Barclays Development Capital taking Pounds 500,000 each. Debt finance of Pounds 1.3m has come from the Manchester office of the Bank of Scotland.
