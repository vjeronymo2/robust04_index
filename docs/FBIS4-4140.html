BFN [Text] United Nations, April 26 (XINHUA) -- Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali today reiterated U.N.'s determination to use NATO air strikes against Bosnian Serb Forces if they do not withdraw from Gorazde by midnight. "I am hoping that the Serbs will comply with the different resolutions which have been adopted by the United Nations (and NATO) and that they will withdraw from Gorazde to a perimeter of 20 kilometers, and that we will not be compelled to use air strikes tonight at 8 o'clock New York time," said the secretary-general in a press conference in Bridgetown, Barbados today. "But if they will not withdraw... [ellipses as received]," said the secretary-general, "Then we will use air strikes against them without any hesitation," according to a U.N. news release today. The NATO April-22 decision said all Bosnian Serb heavy weapons must be withdrawn by 0001 GMT on April 27 (0800 New York time). Otherwise, "These weapons and other Bosnian Serb military assets, as well as their direct and essential military support facilities, including but not limited to fuel installations and munitions sites, will be subject to NATO air strikes."
