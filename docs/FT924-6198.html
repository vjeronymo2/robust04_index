921123 FT 23 NOV 92 / Retailers may branch out RETAILERS are increasingly likely to penetrate each other's markets and move into non-traditional areas in the competitive era after the recession, a report by Manchester Business School and Cambridge Econometrics says. The report, on prospects for UK retailing until 2005, says sales growth in the sector has failed to keep pace with consumer spending. Discretionary spending has been directed at leisure activities and at goods and services not traditionally supplied through retail outlets.
