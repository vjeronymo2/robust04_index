930510 FT 10 MAY 93 / Construction Contracts: Evered Bardon awarded Pounds 17m for US projects EVERED BARDON, the international quarry and aggregates group, has won a series of contracts in the US with a combined value of Dollars 27m (Pounds 17m). In Massachusetts, Bardon Trimount will be the main contractor on four highway resurfacing contracts valued at more than Dollars 16m. Over 300,000 tons of coated stone will be supplied to these contracts, which include resurfacing a tolled section of Interstate 90 between Grafton and Framingham for the Massachusetts Turnpike Authority. The company has also won contracts to supply materials to two major projects in downtown Boston. 17,000 cubic yards of readymix concrete will be supplied to Turner Construction, which has won the contract to build the New England Medical Centre, and more than 400,000 tons of aggregates are to be supplied to Spectacle Island, part of the Dollars 6bn Boston Harbour clean-up scheme. In Washington DC and Maryland, Evered Bardon's Mid-Atlantic division has won contracts to supply more than Dollars 6m of materials. Over 300,000 tons of aggregates will be supplied to the Government Federal Triangle building in Washington DC and a similar quantity is to be railed and barged to the Patuxent Naval Air Station in southern Maryland for a major resurfacing contract. The company has also been awarded the contract to supply 20,000 cubic yards of readymix concrete to the Baltimore City Jail Annexe. Construction begins this month. Aggregates for the concrete on this project will be supplied via Evered Bardon's second aggregates rail terminal in the region, which opened late last month at Canton in Baltimore City.
