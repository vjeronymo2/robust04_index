Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 21 Mar (MTI) -- Prime Minister Peter Boross has sent a telegram of greetings to newly appointed Slovak Prime Minister Jozef Moravcik, wishing him success and expressing his satisfaction with Moravcik's statement, which took a stand for settling Hungarian-Slovak relations as soon as possible. "I believe good intentions on both sides will help us solve current problems and that the Hungarian and Slovak peoples will join their creative powers to tackle their common historical tasks," Boross wrote. Foreign Minister Geza Jeszenszky sent his congratulations to his Slovak counterpart Eduard Kukan. Jeszenszky wrote he believed that improving Hungarian-Slovak links was considered to be a priority by the Slovak foreign minister, too.
