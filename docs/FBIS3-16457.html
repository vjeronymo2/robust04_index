Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Rustam Mirzoyev, the Tajik minister of economy and forecasting received Artis Zeglitis, deputy secretary of state at the Latvian Ministry of Economy. Issues relating to trade and economic relations between the two nations were examined during the course of the meeting. We are happy that we are resuming close contacts in the spheres of trade and economic cooperation with our friendly Latvian Republic, which were disrupted as a result of the known events, said Mirzoyev. Tajikistan always supports the further strengthening of cooperation and relations. The Latvian guests showed an interest in the export of [words indistinct], aluminum, and other products. The meeting was held in an atmosphere of friendship and mutual understanding. The visit by the Latvian trade and economic delegation to Tajikistan resulted in the signing of a protocol of an agreement on trade and economic cooperation between the two nations. In accordance with this, our Republic will supply Latvia with cotton and aluminum. In its turn, Tajikistan will receive food, technological lines, equipment, and radio technical outputs. The protocol was signed by Artis Zeglitis, deputy state secretary at the Latvian Ministry of Economy on behalf of the Latvian Government. On behalf of the Tajik side, the protocol was signed by Hamid Usmonov, first deputy at the Ministry of Economy and Forecasting of Tajikistan.
