Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Bags of cocaine that washed up on the coasts of Villa Clara, Ciego de Avila, Camaguey, and Guantanamo were incinerated by Ministry of the Interior workers and troops in the ovens of large industries of the country during 1993. We are referring to drugs that international traffickers lost during operations close to the territorial waters of Cuba. As on other occasions, members of the National Antidrug Commission, the Ministry of the Interior, and a representative of the Public Prosecutor's Office performed the analysis and certified the incineration of the 2.5 tons of cocaine that washed up on our coasts. This act affirms that Cuba's policy of confronting the actions of international drug trafficking remains unchanged.
