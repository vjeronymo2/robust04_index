BFN [Text] Geneva, 6 Jun (TANJUG) -- The talks on an absolute cease-fire in Bosnia, which have been postponed since last Thursday, should begin in the Palais des Nations in Geneva soon. Yasushi Akashi's spokesman Michael Williams has officially confirmed that "all Serb elements have left the 3-km exclusion zone around Gorazde, as required by the UN resolution." A joint statement confirming this has been signed in Gorazde by representatives of UNPROFOR [UN Protection Force] and the Muslim side, Williams said. A meeting between Yasushi Akashi, the UN secretary general's special envoy, and the vice president of the Croat-Muslim Federation will be held in the Palais des Nations first. The talks between the warring sides should follow.
