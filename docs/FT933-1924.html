930922 FT 22 SEP 93 / Countdown to Yeltsin's decision June 1991: Yeltsin elected president of the Russian republic Aug. 21: Yeltsin helps thwart coup against Gorbachev. Oct. 18: Gorbachev and presidents of eight Soviet republics create economic union. Dec. 1991: The Soviet Union ceases to exist; Gorbachev leaves office. Feb. 1992: Demonstrations in Moscow and other cities as living standards fall. March 1993: Yeltsin declares 'special rule' pending April 25 referendum, results of which show Russians in favour of Yeltsin's reforms. Aug. 27: Parliament overturns Yeltsin's veto of its inflationary budget. Sept. 3: Parliament rejects Yeltsin decree suspending Rutskoi as vice president. Sept. 10: Yeltsin acts against constitution in refusing to sign budget law.
