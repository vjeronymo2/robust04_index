March 12, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

IN BRIEF: SCIENCE / MEDICINE;

KANGAROO GETS PACEMAKER

Surgeons have implanted a heart pacemaker into an ailing kangaroo at the San Francisco Zoo.

Middle-aged, 80-pound Skippy is believed to be the first zoo animal to receive a pacemaker, zoo officials said.

The surgery was performed last week by three heart surgeons and four anesthesiologists.

A zoo spokeswoman said Wednesday that Skippy, 7 years old, was in guarded condition.

Mike Sulak, the zoo's curator of mammals, said the kangaroo had a heartbeat of only 18 to 40 beats per minute before surgery, far slower than the normal 125 to 150 range.
