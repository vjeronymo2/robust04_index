Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kampala, 5 Mar (PANA) -- Sudanese Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih, said in Kampala on Saturday [5 March] that bombing raids being carried out by the Sudanese Airforce on position held by the rebel Sudanese Peoples Liberation Army (SPLA), is aimed at forcing them to the negotiating table. "Much as the Sudan favours a cease-fire and a complete cessation of hostilities, previous experiences have shown that treating the issue of the cease-fire separately is a futile exercise", the foreign minister told a press conference in Kampala. Abu-Salih who arrived in Uganda from Kenya on Friday, with a message from President 'Umar al-Bashir to Preesident Yoweri Museveni said, Khartoum believes that fighting is not a solution to war. "We want a just and genuine peace in the Sudan", he said. He claimed that much of the fighting in the southern Sudan is betwen the rebel factions themselves and tribes hostile to the rebels. The foreign minister who returns back to Kenya Saturday said Sudan fully supports peace initiatives by the four heads of state in the Inter-Governmental Authority on Desertification and Development (IGADD), the objectives of which is to reach a negotiated peaceful settlement of the civil war in the southern Sudan. "These countries sharing the same borders with the Sudan, being fully knowladgable of the root causes of the problem, are better placed to help bring about the desired peaceful settlement", he said. He added however, that proper negotiations should be consucted by his government and the rebels to reach a peaceful settlement. The Khartoum [as received] in 1992 launched the latest massive military operation against the southern rebels led by the SPLA which accused the central governmment of trying to Islamise the entire country.
