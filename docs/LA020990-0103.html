February 9, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

TOP POLLUTERS

The South Coast Air Quality Management District this week released its annual list of companies emitting the largest volume of two pollutants -- reactive hydrocarbons and oxides of nitrogen. This list covers emissions during 1988.

REACTIVE HYDROCARBONS

These are produced by incomplete combustion of gasoline, and from fumes from fuels, paints, and industrial and dry-cleaning solvents. They are a prime ingredient in formation of ozone, the invisible, lung-irritating gas that is smog's main component.

OXIDES OF NITROGEN

These are toxic gases formed by fuel combustion in motor vehicles, power plants and industrial boilers. They combine with hydrocarbons to form ozone, the most intractable pollutant in the Los Angeles basin.
