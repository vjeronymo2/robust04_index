Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Banja Luka, 30 Jan (TANJUG) -- Humanitarian aid convoys organized by the Banja Luka delegation of the ICRC [International Committee of the Red Cross], which have been suspended since 17 January this year, will start running again today, a TANJUG correspondent has learned. A convoy for the local population will arrive in [Serb-held] Banja Luka today, as well as another one destined for [Muslim-held] Zenica. Both have been inspected at the border crossing [between Croatia and Bosnia] in [former Bosanska] Gradiska on the river Sava. As has been reported, the Banja Luka delegation of the ICRC temporarily suspended aid deliveries following the incident when an explosive device was planted under an ICRC vehicle [in Banja Luka] on 17 January this year.
