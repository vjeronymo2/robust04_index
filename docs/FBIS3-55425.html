Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Sevastopol, 8 Jan -- Speaking on a Crimean radio program, Ivan Yermakov, head of the Sevastopol state administration, sharply criticized the leadership of Ukraine, saying that the Ukrainian Government's policy was leading to the impoverishment of the population, first of all, in Sevastopol. At the same time, Ivan Yermakov spoke emphatically against the transfer of the Crimea to Russia: "One should not rush events and promise the unfeasible. This would lead to critical developments," Yermakov said. He spoke in favor of Ukraine, Russia, and Belarus joining in a political and economic union.
