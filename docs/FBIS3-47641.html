Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Wakayama, Jan. 13 KYODO -- The Wakayama District Court on Thursday [13 January] sentenced 26 Chinese accused of illegally entering Japan to eight months in prison with the sentences suspended for three years. The 26 Chinese were the first group to be sentenced among 78 people from China's coastal province of Fujian who were charged with illegally entering Japan. Presiding Judge Yoshimichi Takigawa handed down suspended sentences, saying there was room for sympathy although they entered Japan for the purpose of working illegally in the country. Prosecutors had demanded prison terms of eight months. The 78 Chinese were among a group of 104 who arrived in Shirahama, Wakayama Prefecture, on November 22 aboard a Taiwanese-registered fishing vessel. Prosecutors did not charge the seven crew members and 19 other Chinese were not indicted because they are minors. The group left China on November 8 after paying about 30,000 renminbi (about 400,000 yen) to Chinese brokers.
