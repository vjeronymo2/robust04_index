BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, April 19 (CTK) -- Czech Premier Vaclav Klaus arrived for an official visit to Argentina last night. His Argentinian visit is the first on his ten-day trip around South America, which is to lead him also to Chile and Brazil. At a briefing held after his arrival, Klaus told journalists that his trip was aimed to establish the best possible mutual contacts and find methods of the extension of mutual trade. The Czech Republic is open to the world and, like Argentina, fighting against all kinds of protectionism, Klaus said. Argentinian Economic Minister Domingo Cavallo, at present also in charge of foreign affairs, who met Klaus at the airport, showed interest in the privatization process in the Czech Republic. Later in the night, Klaus was to visit the prestigious Argentinian Committee for Foreign Relations, where he was to acquaint leading Argentinian politicians with the transformation process now underway in the Czech Republic, and with his country's integration into European political, security and economic structures. During his three-day stay in Buenos Aires, Klaus is to hold talks with Cavallo and meet with Argentinian President Carlos Menem. He is to lecture at Buenos Aires' Belgrano University and meet with representatives of Argentinian financial, business and industrial circles.
