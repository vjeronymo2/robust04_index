BFN [By Danielle Yang] [Text] Taipei, May 4 (CNA) -- President Li Teng-hui left for Nicaragua Wednesday [4 May] morning on the first day of his 13-day, four-country overseas tour. During the next two weeks, Li will pay official visits to Nicaragua, Costa Rica, South Africa, and Swaziland. All four countries maintain close official relations with the Republic of China [ROC]. Traveling with Li are Foreign Affairs Minister Chien Fu, Governor of the Central Bank of China Samuel Shieh, Chairman of the Council for Economic Planning and Development Hsiao Wan-chang, Executive Yuan Advisor Jeffrey Koo, Presidential Vice Secretary-General Raymond Tai, and the directors of the Foreign Ministry's Central and South American Affairs Department Yen Ping-fan and African Affairs Department Du Ling. Li will make a brief stopover in Hawaii to refuel before flying to Managua, Nicaragua. Chairman Natale Bellocchi of the American Institute in Taiwan will meet Li in Hawaii during the stopover. Li is scheduled to arrive in Managua Wednesday afternoon. He will spend two days there. Li will then fly to Costa Rica to attend the Sunday inauguration of President-Elect Jose Maria Figueres, and later fly to South Africa, where on May 10 he will attend the inauguration of newly-elected South African President Nelson Mandela. He will travel to Swaziland on May 11. During his tour, Li will sign joint communiques with his counterparts in Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Swaziland to strengthen bilateral ties. This will be Li's first official overseas tour since he assumed the presidency in 1988. He paid unofficial visits to Singapore in 1989 and to the Philippines, Indonesia, and Thailand in February this year. All four Southeast Asian countries recognize Beijing while maintaining close economic ties with Taiwan. Li is scheduled to return to Taipei May 16.
