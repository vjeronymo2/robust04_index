920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / Commodities and Agriculture: CBoT wins approval for scrap steel futures trading THE COMMODITY Futures Trading Commission has approved a scrap steel futures contract for the Chicago Board of Trade. Scrap steel is not traded on any other futures exchange. The CBoT plans to make the scrap contract the first on its new electronic order-matching system called Project A. The screen trading system, now under development, will be a local area network for launching new futures and future-options or listing low-volume products. Mr William O'Connor, the exchange's chairman, said: 'Ferrous scrap futures are just the sort of market Project A is meant for'. The ferrous futures contract will for 100 tonnes of either shredded or bundled scrap and will have the trade months of March June, September and December. The minimum price movement will be 25 cents a tonne or Dollars 25 a contract. The daily limit on price movements will be set at Dollars 10 a tonne. Delivery against the contract will be made on the basis of tendering shipping certificates issued by scrap processors and approved by the exchange.
