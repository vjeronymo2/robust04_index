BFN [Text] Cairo, 2 Jun (MENA)--Two Egyptian experts returned to Cairo from Khartoum today after the Sudanese authorities refused, without giving a reason, to let them enter Sudan. Engineer Muhammad Amin Muhammadayn, former Irrigation Ministry under secretary and technical adviser to the Nile Water Authority; and Dr. Muhammad Jasir, also a former Irrigation Ministry under secretary and currently a professor at the Institute of River Silt and Hydrology, left for Khartoum aboard an Egyptian plane this morning at the Sudanese Government's invitation to discuss several joint cooperation projects. Upon his return to Cairo, engineer Muhammadayn said that the Sudanese authorities refused to give any reason for banning him and his colleague from entering the country. He said the Sudanese told him they will explain the reasons to Egyptian Public Works and Water Resources Minister 'Abd-al-Hadi Radi.
