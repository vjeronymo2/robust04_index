BFN [Text] The Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI] and the French Ministry of Industry, Post, Telecommunications, and Foreign Trade will hold a two-day joint conference of government and private sector representatives on information electronics and space technology in Paris from 19 April. Japan and France plan to exchange information on development projects for multimedia equipment, artificial satellites, and rockets. MITI aims to promote joint ventures between Japanese and French companies in multimedia development and in the space industry through this conference. Among Japanese companies which will join the conference are: Toshiba Corporation, Mitsubishi Electric Corporation, Kawasaki Heavy Industries Ltd., Hitachi, Ltd., Sony Corporation, and Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corporation (NTT). They are engaged in information, communications, and space-related industries. Japanese participants will be divided into two groups, one on the information and communications sector, and the other on the space industry, and exchange views on technology and future tasks with their French counterparts, such as Thomson Corporation, Alcatel Corporation, Aerospatiale Inc., and other French companies.
