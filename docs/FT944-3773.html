941212 FT 12 DEC 94 / Dollars 2bn aid pledge for Zambia Donor nations and agencies have pledged a total of Dollars 2bn (Pounds 1.2bn) in financing assistance to Zambia for 1995. The biggest chunk of the money, about Dollars 1.2bn, will go toward retiring arrears on International Monetary Fund loans granted in the 1980s to the struggling African country. About Dollars 347m will be provided for project and commodity assistance and Dollars 441m more will be given in balance-of-payments aid. Separately, a further Dollars 154m has been set aside for debt relief, the bank said after a meeting in Paris of the consultative group of donor nations, the IMF and the World Bank. The donors said support would depend on Zambia maintaining the momentum of its recovery programme.
