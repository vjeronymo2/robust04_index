BFN [Text] Today Eduard Shevardnadze, the Georgian head of state and chairman of parliament, received Russian Ambassador to Georgia Vladimir Zemskiy. The ambassador presented to the head of state a letter from Boris Yeltsin in which the Russian president thanks Eduard Shevardnadze for his message of greetings on Russian Independence Day, and expresses confidence that friendship and cooperation between Russia and Georgia will continue for the benefit of the two countries' peoples. The two discussed today's decision by Russia's Federation Council, authorizing the deployment of Russian peacekeeping forces in the conflict zone in Abkhazia. The head of state said that the decision is very important. He stressed that this international initiative by Russia is of historic importance not only for Georgia, and that its success would be a prerequisite for stability in the Transcaucasus. In the opinion of Eduard Shevardnadze, the success of the operation will boost Russia's international reputation, and would be a positive example to other countries.
