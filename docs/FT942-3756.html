940614 FT 14 JUN 94 / World News in Brief: North Korea quits nuclear agency in inspections row North Korea said it was withdrawing from the International Atomic Energy Agency, but stopped short of pulling out of the nuclear non-proliferation treaty. Pyongyang said it would no longer permit IAEA inspectors into the country, indicating that it would eject two inspectors who are monitoring the storage of spent fuel rods at the North's nuclear facilities at Yongbyon.
