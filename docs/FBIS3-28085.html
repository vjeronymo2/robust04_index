Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 3 (YONHAP) -- Prime Minister Yi Hoe-chang, deploring the violent farmer-student demonstration against rice market opening in Seoul on Tuesday afternoon, has ordered that all of those who broke the law be punished. Presiding over a cabinet meeting on Thursday morning, Yi said street violence should not be allowed to occur again, noting some 300 people were injured in Tuesday's demonstration, his Press Secretary Kang Hyong-sok said. He also instructed that strict controls be imposed on elections for farm, livestock and fisheries cooperatives, saying they have already shown signs of corruption and rigging. Yi told Economic Planning Minister Chong Chae-sok to pay special attention to price movements so that prices do not rise before Lunar New Year next week, while instructing Labor Minister Nam Chae-hui to see that workers' wages are not held in arrears during the holiday. He also stressed the need to ensure safe land and coastal transportation during the holiday.
