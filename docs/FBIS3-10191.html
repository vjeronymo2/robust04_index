Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Elisa Betias Otero; from the "ACIR Radio News" newscast] [Excerpt] I would like to report that President Carlos Salinas de Gortari indicated this afternoon that his administration is unflinchingly taking on the commitments agreed during the process for peace and justice taking place in Chiapas. He also announced that the necessary decisions to guarantee the execution of these commitments will be made in the next few days. The president expressed his optimism with the conclusion of the peace and reconciliation talks in Chiapas and he indicated that the dialogue has already provided evidence of positive results. In this respect, he called for the support of society in order to reach to the signing of a peace agreement. The president's statements were made public through a communique issued by the Presidency of the Republic. The communique stresses that the list of demands presented to the government through Peace Commissioner Manuel Camacho was acknowledged with the true disposition of listening and solving the Indians' demands for justice, welfare, and dignity. With regard to the letter the communique points out that the government has decided to seriously honor its commitments and that the necessary decisions to ensure the execution of said commitments will be made in the next few days. We expect society's support on the points agreed upon and that by backing these points we will all be looking out to ensure that all details are honored which will allow us to reach to the signing of a final peace agreement.
