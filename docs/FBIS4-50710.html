BFN [Text] Shenzhen, May 19 (CNS) -- The electronic industry in Shenzhen, the Special Economic Zone city in Guangdong Province, takes a lead both in its production value and export value among big cities including Beijing and Shanghai in the country. According to the Shenzhen Electronic Industry Association, the city saw its electronic industrial output value of RMB [renminbi] 18 billion last year, making up 12.9 percent of the total value earned from this sector in the country. The export value of this industry was nearly RMB 10.8 billion, accounting for 15.3 percent of the total value of this kind of products in the country. There are to date over 1,500 enterprises specializing in this industry in the city with employment of 160,000 people. The city has seen its dozen of products including colour TV sets, video cassette recorders, mini computers, program-controlled switchboards, telephones, colour tubes, discs, liquid crystal indicators and software ranking first in the country in terms of their production volume, and some of them have entered the international market. China's annual capacity of production of discs has to date reached 1.5 billion, about one fourth of the total turned out in the world. 800 million of them were produced in the city. 39 out of 55 new- and high-tech enterprises confirmed in the city are electronic ones and a number of high-tech items including production lines of computers and laser devices, super large-scale integrated circuits, mobile phones and magnetic heads for video cassette recorders have been put into operation or under construction. They will greatly promote the development of the electronic industry in the city.
