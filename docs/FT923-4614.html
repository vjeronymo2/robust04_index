920905 FT 05 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Cost cuts boost Gibbs & Dandy An attack on costs enabled Gibbs & Dandy, the builders' merchant, to cut its pre-tax loss from Pounds 405,000 to Pounds 92,000 in the opening half of 1992. Turnover fell 2.5 per cent to Pounds 11.3m because of the recession in the construction industry, with the consequent reduction in volume sales. However, operating profit moved ahead from Pounds 65,000 to Pounds 121,000 as staff levels were cut by a further 5 per cent, bad debts were reduced through stricter control, and operating costs were tightened. On top of that, interest charges were reduced by Pounds 85,000 to Pounds 213,000. Losses per share worked through at 1.2p (5.1p).
