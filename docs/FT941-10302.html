940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / Trading standards move opposed Welsh district councils are resisting attempts to set up large trading standards authorities once local government in Wales is reorganised. Trading standards officers believe businesses would face fragmented and inconsistent regulation if the service were devolved to the 21 councils that will replace the current eight Welsh councils. An amendment to create three new trading standards authorities for Wales has been tabled in the Lords by the Lord Elton (Conservative). But the Council of Welsh Districts said yesterday: 'Such a move would mean another blow to local democracy. The trading standards service is no more complex and demanding than environmental health, a function which district councils have carried out without complaint for many years.'
