940824 FT 24 AUG 94 / Employee fights 'racism' charge A member of the far-right British National party lost his job as a council caretaker after being shown on TV 'inciting racial hatred', an industrial tribunal heard yesterday. Greenwich Borough Council in south-east London said that the trust and confidence it needed to have in an employee, who by the terms of his contract of employment had to deal with members of a vulnerable minority, could not exist where someone was an 'avowed and active racist'. The Croydon tribunal hearing, which is expected to end today, heard that Mr Ian Dell was suspended last year after he was seen on television holding a copy of the BNP newspaper and chanting 'rights for whites'. Mr Philip Engelman, for the council, said Mr Dell's action had been tantamount to an 'incitement to racial hatred' and a fundamental breach of trust and confidence in his ability to do his job. Mr Dell said that what he did in his own time was his business. He had never discriminated against anybody while carrying out his job. Of the slogan chanted at the demonstration, he said: 'I was a member of a perfectly lawful organisation, carrying out lawful campaigning, putting a lawful message across, and that was not aimed at Greenwich council.'
