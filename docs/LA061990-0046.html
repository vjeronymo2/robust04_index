June 19, 1990, Tuesday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: VENTURA;

BEACH RESTROOMS TO CLOSE TO SAVE WATER

A majority of the restrooms and some showers at San Buenaventura State Beach will be closed weekdays under a plan to conserve water during Ventura's drought emergency.

Park officials hope to save up to 4,000 gallons of water a day by closing the facilities, a step they say is necessary to meet Ventura's mandatory 20% reduction in water use.

Beginning this week, three of the five restrooms at the popular state beach will be closed during off-peak hours, said Walt Deleu, chief maintenance supervisor for the Channel Coast District. Four of the beach's eight outdoor cold showers will also be turned off to conserve water, he said.

Chemical toilets will be installed outside the closed facilities and signs will be posted nearby directing beach-goers to the nearest open restrooms and showers, Deleu said.

The bathrooms and showers will be reopened on weekends and on days when there are many visitors to the beach, Deleu said.

"We're going to be very flexible with this," Deleu said.

Similar restrictions are being considered for Emma Wood State Beach, where a 15% reduction in water use has been achieved by limiting landscape watering, Deleu said.

The full contingent of bathroom and hot-shower facilities will remain open at Carpinteria and McGrath state beaches, where the installation of a pay shower system has led to a 20% reduction in water use. CAROLYN KUSKY
