BFN [Text] There are no problems in Russian-Kazakh relations which could hamper their integration, Kazakh Foreign Minister Kanat Saudabayev said in an interview with Interfax. Saudabayev is now in Moscow on a non-official visit. For instance, he said that there were no problems concerning the Russian-speaking population in Kazakhstan since this amounted to 95 pct of the Kazakh population. Saudabayev stressed that there was no alternative to the process of economic, political and cultural integration between Russia and Kazakhstan. Today Saudabayev discussed increased cooperation between the two countries with Deputy Chairman of the Federation Council Ramazan Abdulatipov. Saudabayev announced that they had agreed that the legal basis for the integration had already been established or was being established. The goal now is to make it concrete.
