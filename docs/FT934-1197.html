931221 FT 21 DEC 93 / Redwood cracks down on NHS posts Mr John Redwood, the Welsh secretary, yesterday an-nounced a moratorium on the recruitment of National Health Service managers in Wales and axed two top jobs that were being advertised, Christine Buckley writes. Mr Redwood, who has clashed with Mrs Virginia Bottomley, the health secretary, over NHS bureaucracy, said no managers were to be recruited without his permission. He eliminated the posts of a health economist and capital planner for the Welsh Health Common Services Authority.
