BFN [Text] Lhasa, June 15 (XINHUA) -- The Tibet Meteorological Bureau stresses cultivation of Tibetan professionals for the weather forecast sector. Since 1981, the bureau has selected 520 Tibetans from the region and sent them to study in specialized schools and universities in inland provinces. Now the proportion of Tibetan weather men to the total has increased from 18.6 percent to 70.3 percent. Of all technological personnel in the Tibet meteorological sector, the Tibetan technicians account for more than 50 percent. Purpu Cering is the first Tibetan to graduate from Nanjing Meteorology Institute, China's most prestigious in the field, with a master's degree. Last year, he went to the United States to participate in a modern meteorology program with the recommendation of the National Meteorological Bureau. The 25-year-old engineer completed several research projects and returned with high praise from Chinese and foreign experts. This year, the Tibet Meteorological Bureau selected six young Tibetans to enter Nanjing Meteorology Institute's master's program.
