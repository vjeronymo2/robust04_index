BFN [Text] Kiev, 24 Jun -- The international conference "Partnership for Peace: objectives and prospects," organized by the National Institute of Strategic Research of Ukraine, is under way. John Barrett, chairman of NATO's Political Affairs Division, Ukrainian Deputy Foreign Minister Borys Tarasyuk, and Vadym Hrechaninov, assistant to the defense minister for issues of military policy, took part in the conference among others. The conference participants discussed NATO's attitude toward the "Partnership for Peace" program and analyzed the state's military planning and armament policy in connection with the "Partnership for Peace" initiative. The issue of Ukrainian-Russian relations within NATO's "Partnership for Peace" initiative did not remain beyond the attention of the conference participants. Today, the conference participants discussed prospects for Ukraine's defense policy and the Ukrainian issue within NATO's plans. NATO's representative John Barrett delivered a report on the geopolitical location of Ukraine and its influence on NATO's plans for the implementation of the "Partnership for Peace" program. The participants in the conference analyzed the political, economic, and military aspects of this issue.
