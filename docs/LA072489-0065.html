July 24, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

NATIONAL LEAGUE ROUNDUP;

SLUMP CONTINUES FOR THE REDS, 12-4

This may be the first time in major league history that a court order has enabled a manager to keep his job.

Under normal circumstances, the collapse of the Cincinnati Reds might have brought about the firing of Manager Pete Rose. Even before Rose went to court to battle Commissioner Bart Giamatti, there were reports that Red owner Marge Schott was ready to make a change.

But no matter how long the Reds' current slump continues, baseball cannot take Rose's job away at least until the court makes a decision on the Rose case.

And, make no mistake about it, the Reds have collapsed. It started with a series of injuries, but now they can't make routine plays.

Mike Fitzgerald hit two home runs and drove in five runs Sunday at Montreal to lead the streaking Expos to a 12-4 victory over the Reds. It was the eighth consecutive defeat for the Reds and their 12th in the last 13 games. From a team challenging for the pennant in the National League West a short time ago, they have become a club diving toward the cellar.

The losing streak is their longest since they lost nine early in the 1986 season.

Against the Expos, the Reds took a 2-0 lead on a home run by Ken Griffey in the first inning, but Danny Jackson (6-11), a 23-game winner last year, couldn't make it hold up through the first inning.

Dennis Martinez was not especially impressive, but he lasted five innings and won his 10th in a row. He is 11-1 for the season.

In an understatement, Rose told the Associated Press he had a confused team.

"Because of all these injuries," he said, "the players' roles have changed drastically. I'm the only one whose role hasn't changed. What do you do about it? Maybe you guys can tell me."

Rose neglected to say that usually in such confused situations, it is the manager who departs.

The four-game sweep enabled the Expos to keep ahead of their challengers in the East. Although the New York Mets won their sixth in a row, the Expos have a three-game lead.

Chicago 9, San Francisco 5 -- Now that the Cubs are learning how to win at Wrigley Field, they not only have remained in the race in the East, they have tightened up the race in the West.

Mark Grace and Andre Dawson each drove in three runs at Chicago, the Cubs improved their record at home to 26-23 with their third win in their last four games, and the Giants' lead over Houston dropped to 1 1/2 games. The Cubs are only 3 1/2 games behind the Expos.

Grace, who extended his hitting streak to 13 games, had a double and a single. Dawson, who returned Friday after missing five games with knee problems, hit a three-run home run to break it open in the sixth inning. In the last two games, he has a triple and two home runs.

"We didn't hit well, we didn't pitch well and we didn't catch well in the series," Giant Manager Roger Craig said.

The Giants' 34-15 record is the best of any major league team at home, but they are 24-25 on the road.

Houston 3, Philadelphia 2 -- The Astros' four-game sweep over the Phillies at Houston was tempered by the loss of hitting star Glenn Davis.

Davis bruised his foot on a foul tip earlier and limped off in the seventh inning after drawing a walk.

The bright spots were another strong five innings from Bob Forsch (3-3) and a circus catch by left fielder Billy Hatcher to end the game.

The Phillies had two on and two out in the ninth when Len Dykstra hit a fly ball to short left off Larry Andersen. Hatcher dived, rolled over and came up holding the ball. Despite the protest of the Phillies, rookie umpire Charlie Rehliford ruled it was a game-ending catch.

New York 7, Atlanta 2 -- The Mets' sixth consecutive victory matched their longest winning streak of the season.

David Cone (8-5) had an easy time after the Mets scored six times in the first inning. Cone lasted 6 2/3 innings to improve his record at home to 6-0. He is unbeaten in 10 starts since June 5.

"It's tough when you look up and see Montreal winning," Cone said.

St. Louis 3, San Diego 2 -- Jose Oquendo extended his hitting streak to 21 games with a fifth-inning double, but it was Willie McGee and Milt Thompson who keyed the Cardinals' attack.

McGee drove in two runs and Thompson had three hits to help the Cardinals sweep the four-game series at San Diego. DAN HAFNER
