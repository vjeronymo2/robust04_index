CSO [Article by Rafael Medina Cruz] [Excerpt] Over eight tons of packed marijuana, distributed in a railroad car, a tank truck, buckets, drums, and nine vehicles equipped with special compartments, were seized yesterday by Federal Judicial Police [PJF] personnel detailed to Sinaloa, Veracruz, Oaxaca, San Luis Potosi, Coahuila, and Tamaulipas States, where 15 presumed public poisoners were arrested. It is estimated that the drugs, intended to be sent to the United States for sale and consumption, were worth approximately 150 million new pesos. According to a report submitted to the assistant attorney for local offices and inspection, Alfonso Cabrera Morales, prominent among the seizures made by the federal agents is the one that occurred at the railroad terminal in the port of Mazatlan, Sinaloa. There, three tons of pressed marijuana were confiscated. It was noted that the PGR [Attorney General's Office] representatives, in cooperation with the officials at that station, made a routine inspection on the train that runs from the city of Guadalajara, Jalisco, to Mexicali, Baja California (with a stop in Mazatlan). Upon discovering that the seals of the freight car on which the drugs were being carried had been tampered with, the police agents intensified the search. They found the three tons of cannabis, bound for Baja California, contained in hundreds of packages. At the order of the PGR representative in that state, Luis Antonio Ibanez Cornejo, PJF deputy commissioner Eduardo Salazar Carillo continued the investigation at the aforementioned railroad terminal. The local PGR offices in Jalisco and Baja California were also advised to continue the probe, capture the drug owners, determine whether any National Railroads employee was implicated in the incident, and take the appropriate action. Furthermore, as was reported to the PJF's general director, Adrian Carrera Fuentes, Jose Braulio Salvador Campos Beltran, Salvador Campos Beltran, Juan Manuel Espino Trejo, Juan Sergio Espino Chan, and Jose Salvador Francisco Campos Beltran were arrested in the municipality of Acayucan, Veracruz. They were carrying approximately two tons of cannabis indica on a tank truck, also bound for the country's northern border. It was explained that the drugs in question had been packed in 280 plastic buckets which, in turn, were distributed in the tank of the vehicle. The latter is presumed to be owned by the Felem Company, which is being investigated by agents under orders from Deputy Commissioner Fernando Asiain Barriga. [passage omitted]
