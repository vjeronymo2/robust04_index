Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin has instructed the Finance Ministry to raise funds to help phase out the state TV companies' debt of 80 Bn rubles to the Communications Ministry. His decision came after an emergency meeting with Communications Minister Vladimir Bulgak and Chairman of the Ostankino TV Company Aleksandr Yakovlev on Wednesday. The meeting follows a threat by the communications workers trade unions to cut down TV broadcasts to morning and evening newscasts beginning Thursday unless the TV companies service their debt. The prime minister expressed concern over the TV companies' arrears over 1993 and this year, his press office told Interfax. He instructed the Finance Ministry to start covering the debt by paying out wages to the communications workers, now several months overdue.
