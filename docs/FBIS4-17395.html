BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Glazovskaya] [Text] Ashkhabad [Ashgabat], 22 Apr -- The Turkmen-Chinese summit talks have ended with the signing of three intergovernmental and five interdepartmental agreements in Ashkhabad. Agreements have been reached on the provision by China of a state loan of 50 million yuan to Turkmenistan, on cooperation between the Foreign Ministries of the two countries, and also on the study by inhabitants of the two countries of the Chinese and Turkmen languages. Letters of intents have been signed: - Between the Ministry of Oil and Gas of Turkmenistan and the Chinese national oil and gas company on joint cooperation in the development of the two countries' oil and gas sectors; - Between the Turkmen "Kuvvat" state power technology corporation and a Chinese trade and industry group of companies, in which the Chinese side has expressed willingness to build an iron and steel works to process scrap metal and an aluminum production works; - On long-term cooperation between the Ministry of Construction and Architecture of Turkmenistan and the all-China engineering and construction corporation, which expresses willingess to cooperate on the setting up of joint enterprises to produce welding electrodes, porcelain goods, ceramic tiles, earthenware, and furniture. - A letter of intent between the Ministry of Trade of Turkmenistan and a Chinese electronics import and export company envisages reciprocal commercial activity; in particular, deliveries by the Chinese company of domestic electronic equipment and electronic goods. - A letter of intent between the Ministry of Agriculture of Turkmenistan and the "Chinatex" textile import and export company of China reflects a willingness by the Chinese side to purchase Turkmen cotton fiber.
