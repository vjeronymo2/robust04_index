BFN [Text] Ankara, June 23 (A.A.) -- President Suleyman Demirel on Thursday [23 June] said that relations between Turkey and Uzbekistan are developing in line with both countries' interests. Demirel was speaking at the welcoming ceremony for Uzbek President Islam Karimov who arrived in Turkey for a two-day official visit. `These relations are carried out based on the principles of equality, non-interference in domestic affairs, and mutual interest, love and respect,' he said. `Cooperation between the two countries will contribute to the development and welfare of the region of which Turkey and Uzbekistan are two important countries.' He said the foreign trade volume between the two countries in 1992 was 75 million U.S. dollars and this increased to 245 million dollars in 1993. He said the financial portrait of the Turkish firms investing and doing business in Uzbekistan amounts to 446 million dollars, with Turkish Eximbank opening credits of 250 million dollars, of which half has already been used.
