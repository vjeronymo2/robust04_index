941114 FT 14 NOV 94 / Iran takes Azeri oil stake Azeri and Iranian officials said over the weekend that Azerbaijan had agreed to give Iran a 5 per cent stake in an international consortium for developing Azeri oilfields in the Caspian Sea. The agreement, reached in Baku, the Azeri capital will give Iran a quarter of Azerbaijan's 20 per cent stake in the Dollars 7bn deal in exchange for financial and technical assistance. Speaking on Radio Tehran, monitored by the BBC, Mr Gholamreza Aghazadeh, Iran's oil minister, said he expected Iran to invest between Dollars 300m and Dollars 350m in the project. Azeri officials told the Russian news agency Interfax that Iran had been brought into the deal because of Azerbaijan's inability to contribute its share of the financing.
