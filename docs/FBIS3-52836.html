Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kabul, Jan. 9, IRNA -- The truce in force between the warring Afghan factions until 6 a.m. local time Sunday was extended to 6 a.m. Monday, it was announced here Sunday. A 24-hour ceasefire was agreed by the warring factions on Saturday as a result of appeals by the Iranian Foreign Ministry in order to evacuate those injured in six days of deadly fighting between the forces of President Borhanoddin Rabbani and Abdul-Rashid Dostam. During the past 24 hours residential areas and the district around Kabul airport were evacuated. Corpses of innocent people killed in the crossfire between the two sides still litter the streets. All government offices and radio and t.v. stations have suspended their regular services. Foreign embassies have been closed and diplomats are leaving the capital before the ceasefire ends. Convoys of foreign diplomats leaving the war-torn city has made people worried of the prospect of possible renewed outbreak of civil war. IRNA reporter who saw the area said that the ruins and debris cover a vast area and would take decades to rebuilt.
