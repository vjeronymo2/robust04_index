940715 FT 15 JUL 94 / UK Company News: Marks and Spencer sales receive overseas spur First-quarter sales for Marks and Spencer, the retailer, in north America, continental Europe and east Asia were up on last year and particularly good in Hong Kong and the International Franchise Group, Sir Richard Greenbury, chairman, told the annual meeting. He added that sales in the UK were 'up to expectations'. The group is to open two new stores, in Paris and Valencia, in the autumn and is investigating the possibility of moving into Japan and China. The pace of expansion in the UK was 'accelerating', said Sir Richard, with plans for capital expenditure of more than Pounds 1bn over the next three years. In the past year capital expenditure reached 'a new high of Pounds 350m.' the group had 'the biggest footage expansion programme in (its) history'. M&S shares rose 8p to 414p.
