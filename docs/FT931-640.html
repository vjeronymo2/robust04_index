930329 FT 29 MAR 93 / Managers' pay rises slow but beat inflation MANAGERS' basic pay comfortably outstripped inflation in the 12 months to February but the trend of the settlements fell in the course of the year, a survey of more than 1,000 companies has found. The survey by pay research group Reward found that basic pay rose by 4.4 per cent in the year, compared with a 1.8 per cent rise in the retail prices index, but that six months ago pay was rising at an annual rate of more than 5.5 per cent. Mr Steve Flather, Reward managing director, said that pay rises were responding to the drop in inflation. 'But these results also suggest that those organisations which are surviving the tough times of the recession are giving above-inflation increases to ensure that they retain their key staff,' he said. Few managers have swapped their company cars for cash, Reward found. Among senior managers, 81.6 per cent now receive cars, compared with 82.3 per cent in March 1992. The figure for middle managers has risen from 37.9 per cent in 1992 to 39.6 per cent. Among junior managers, 20.2 per cent have cars, compared with 19.8 per cent last year. Last year's graduate recruits earn a little more than the current batch of entrants to the jobs market, according to Reward. The average graduate starting salary is Pounds 12,750, with a range of Pounds 11,720 to Pounds 14,000. Last year's graduates have an average salary of Pounds 12,800, with a range of Pounds 11,750 to Pounds 14,000. Reward management survey, Diamond Way, Stone Business Park, Stone, Staffordshire ST15 OSD. Pounds 200.
