921030 FT 30 OCT 92 / Prison conditions are attacked THE CONTINUED failure to improve conditions in jails was condemned by Judge Stephen Tumim, the chief inspector of prisons, in his annual report published yesterday. He highlighted overcrowding, lack of training for prison officers, lack of investment, the concentration of prisoners' meals and recreation within the short working day, the failure to end the requirement on prisoners to slop out, and delays in dealing with inmates' complaints.
