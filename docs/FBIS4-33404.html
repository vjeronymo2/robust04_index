BFN [Text] Caracas, 3 Jun (EFE) -- Venezuelan Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli said here today that Venezuela has agreed to receive Haitian refugees on the condition that they comprise only a small number of refugees who would remain in Venezuela temporarily, until the crisis in the Antillean nation is resolved. The Venezuelan Government's decision was announced after an official U.S. delegation headed by Under Secretary of State Strobe Talbott asked the government of Venezuelan President Rafael Caldera to accept Haitian refugees. The U.S. official called the Venezuelan offer "generous," and he recalled that Caracas had received the very first Haitian refugee--President Jean-Bertrand Aristide who was received by Venezuela after he was overthrown in September 1991. Talbott reiterated that his government wants a "peaceful and multilateral" solution to the Haitian crisis. Burelli told newsmen that Venezuela "has informed the United States that it will not close its doors and that it will receive a group (of Haitians), provided they are temporary refugees who will remain until the precarious situation in Haiti is resolved." The foreign minister explained that the Venezuelan Embassy in Washington will list the number of Haitians eligible to travel to Venezuela as an "undetermined number," although "this cannot be a large number."
