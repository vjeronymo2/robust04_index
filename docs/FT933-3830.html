930911 FT 11 SEP 93 / UK Company News: Bletchley Motor rises to Pounds 785,000 WITH ALL divisions, particul-arly contract hire, performing well, Bletchley Motor lifted first half 1993 pre-tax profit from Pounds 604,000 to Pounds 785,000. Mr David Dunn, chairman, said contract hire produced Pounds 473,000 (Pounds 324,000) as it continued to benefit from giving 'high quality service'. Self-drive was at last emerging from the recession, and reduced costs and increased demand pushed profits up to Pounds 73,000 (Pounds 11,000). In car sales new vehicle registrations were ahead 9 per cent but at a small cost to the used car business. The chairman said that after-sales activities remained strong, despite extended service intervals and improved vehicle reliability. Turnover came to Pounds 39.6m (Pounds 35m) realising an operating surplus of Pounds 1.28m (Pounds 1.26m). Interest costs were cut to Pounds 492,000 (Pounds 657,000). Earnings per share worked through at 10.9p (11.4p) and the interim dividend is raised to 4.75p (4.4p)
