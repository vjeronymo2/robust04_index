BFN [Report by Vicente Briceno from Miraflores Palace; from the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] As we have reported, everything seems to indicate that President Rafael Caldera was prompted to replace Venezuelan Investment Fund [FIV] Minister Abdon Vivas Teran for failing to sell Aeropostal through the bidding process. Fernando Egana, deputy minister of the Secretariat of the Presidency, has admitted that the chief of state has made this decision, because he wants to accelerate the process of privatization. He has added the former minister has established the bases for this policy and the government will develop it: [Begin recording] [Egana] He has established solid and important bases, and the government will continue his efforts on such bases. [Unidentified reporter] Is there anything concrete in this regard? [Egana] A plan of privatization is already under way. [Reporter] What is in store for Vivas? [Egana] The president of the Republic will decide. [Reporter] It was said the first attempt at privatization has failed. Will changes be made? Will a new element be introduced to make it more effective? [Egana] Using the bases already established, the government will try to make greater efforts to attract foreign investments that are needed at this time. [end recording] When asked whether the MAS [Movement Toward Socialism] will have a greater role in the government in view of the changes, Egana has said the country has achieved greater participation during the administration of President Caldera.
