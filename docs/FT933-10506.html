930804 FT 04 AUG 93 / World Trade News: Boost for Yemen oil exports The first cargo will be shipped today from a new oil field expected to boost Yemen's exports by two-thirds, oil industry sources said yesterday, Reuter reports from Aden. They said the 550,000 barrel shipment from the Masila field in the eastern Hadramaut region was being transferred to the refinery at the al-Shihr terminal on the Gulf of Aden. The Masila project is a joint venture between Canadian Occidental Petroleum, which holds 52 per cent, Pecten Yemen, a US subsidiary of Royal Dutch/Shell, with 20 per cent, Occidental of the US with 18 per cent and Lebanese Contractors International, 10 per cent.
