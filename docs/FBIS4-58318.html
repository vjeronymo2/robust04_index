BFN [Text] Tehran, May 2, IRNA -- [Words indistinct] formed a "defence committee" aimed at foiling attacks by the Baghdad-based terrorist Mujahedeen Khalq Organization (MKO), a weekly disclosed here Monday. The bi-lingual weekly 'KAYHAN HAVA'I' in its latest issue further wrote that the committee, in its first reaction to MKO attacks on foreigners in Iraq, has requested the Iraqi government to provide more security to foreign diplomats based in that country. "Over the past few months, foreign diplomatic missions in Iraq, including the Iranian Embassy, have complained about increasing terrorist attacks and the plunder of their property by the MKO," according to the weekly. It referred to March 22 incident in which four shots were fired by MKO assailants at Djibouti's charge d'affaires in Iraq, stealing of an Iranian embassy vehicle a few days after the incident and December assassination of Caglar Yucel, the administrative attache of the Turkish Embassy saounding [as received] of a second man as examples of MKO attacks on foreign diplomatic missions in Iraq.
