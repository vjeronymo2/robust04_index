October 31, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

LONG BEACH FREEWAY

The Times' editorial "A Gap That, Sadly, Must Be Filled" (Oct. 18) sounds like crocodile tears for the residents of South Pasadena and El Sereno, shed by the people living to the north of them. Why are people in inner areas always asked to sacrifice for the convenience of people in the outer areas?

It is not that the residents of the impacted communities are unwilling to have people from other areas passing through -- just that they do it in a more civilized manner. The residents have made it clear that they would support rail freight instead of trucks, and light rail for commuters instead of highway lanes for cars.

The editorial builds the best possible case for the link, but the case is inherently weak. How could this link not generate additional traffic? Whether or not more growth will occur around the gap is irrelevant. The trips originate in the hinterlands where growth is occurring; the freeway link would stimulate further growth to generate the trips to more than fill the new freeway link.

Even the idea that free-flowing traffic generates less pollution is fanciful.

Inclusion of mass transit in plans for the freeway should be recognized for what it is: a sugar coating for a bitter pill. A real mass transit solution would omit the freeway.

The Times has an excellent record of support for endangered species, but the freeway is a dinosaur that has caused enough problems already. Extinction is a fitting end.

ED DUNCAN

Los Angeles
