Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Moscow February 11 TASS -- Four-day consultations of experts from Georgia, Abkhazia, Russia, the United Nations Organization and CSCE have failed to coordinate stands of the conflicting sides on the Abkhazian political status. Any center and region are out of the question, since Georgia and Abkhazia will be equal partners, head of the Abkhazian delegation Andrey Dzhirgenia told ITAR-TASS. The Abkhazian side insists on greater autonomy in many aspects of state life. It has offered to create a special body in charge of joint competence in foreign policy, trade, customs and border services, power engineering, transport and credit-financial policy. In the opinion of the Abkhazian side, the rest should be in the exclusive competence of Georgian and Abkhazian power structures. The Abkhazian delegation does not agree to make its republic a Georgian land, whose powers will not exceed social issues, culture and fishing. Sources at the Abkhazian Supreme Soviet's press service told ITAR-TASS, the sides are to submit more detailed proposals by the next round of Geneva negotiations scheduled for February 23.
