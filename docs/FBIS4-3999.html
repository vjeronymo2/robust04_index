BFN [Text] London, April 26 (XINHUA) -- U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher said today that Bosnian Serbs must do more to avert the threat of NATO air strikes. Speaking to reporters after talks with British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd, Christopher said the Serbs had not yet done enough to avoid the threat of being bombed. "The information I have this morning is that the Serbs have moved a number of heavy weapons outside the 20 km zone, but there are a number still there," he said. "It is clear that today will be an important day to see if they really will comply with requirements," he stressed. Hurd also told reporters that he hoped the Serbs would pull back completely. "If they don't then they know there will be air strikes against them," he warned. Christopher and Hurd conferred as the "contact group", made up of high-level officials of the United States, Russia, the European Union and the United Nations, its first meeting in London [as received] to coordinate efforts to bring peace to Bosnia. NATO has given the Serbs until 0001 GMT on Wednesday [27 April] to pull back all heavy weapons to at least 20 km from the U.N. safe haven of Gorazde.
