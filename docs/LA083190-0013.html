August 31, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

TUNNEL FIRE

On behalf of Caltrans, District 7 -- and especially the bridge, construction, maintenance and traffic forces -- I want to express our thanks to the motoring public who helped us deal with the closing of the downtown Hollywood Freeway (101) due to last month's fire, which destroyed a portion of the RTD Metro Rail tunnel.

I realize the inconvenience to commuters was horrendous.

However, California drivers have proven once again that when we ask for their help, they are more than willing to respond.

While the various freeway closures were in place, Caltrans' maintenance forces removed graffiti from signs and walls, replaced some signs and washed the rest. They also replaced 4,200 pavement markers; stenciled pavement markings on all closed ramps; repaired guardrails and one bridge joint; swept shoulder areas, as well as maintained closures and barricades at all approaches to the downtown 101 slot.

When Caltrans and the California Highway Patrol began reopening freeway sections and ramps, it was very gratifying to see motorists smile and give us the thumbs-up sign as they drove by. I also want to thank the Los Angeles City Department of Transportation and County Health; RTD/Los Angeles County Transportation Commission/Metro Rail; the fire companies from throughout the Southland; the Los Angeles police and CHP, Cal/OSHA; Tutor-Salib-Perini, Metro Rail contractor and the Red Cross.

JERRY B. BAXTER

District 7 Director, Caltrans
