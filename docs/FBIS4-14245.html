BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 Apr (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has stated that there has been progress in the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations, contacts were made today, and others will be made when President Husni Mubarak receives U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher, Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, and Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres. Speaking at a joint news conference with Peres following their talks here today, Musa said: Egypt is willing to host the signing of the agreement any time, and the date of its signing will be set very soon. Shim'on Peres said: It will be possible to identify the remaining problems at the end of today's talks. We are still working to resolve the main points of dispute. We will set the date for signing the agreement when these issues are resolve. He added: If we reach an accord, then this agreement could be signed in an hour. But if there is no agreement, it might take a year. Peres stressed that Egypt is playing a responsible and very wise role in the negotiating process and that there is no doubt the Middle East region is fortunate to have Egypt playing a role, one that we fully appreciate.
