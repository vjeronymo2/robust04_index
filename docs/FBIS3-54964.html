Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS string correspondent Semyon Ivanov] [Text] Moscow, January 5 (TASS) -- Forty eight carrier rockets blasted off the launching pads of Baykonur (Kazakhstan) and Plesetsk (Russia) during 1993. The rockets launched 59 different space apparatuses into orbit. The share of Plesetsk northern "space harbour" -- amounted to 26 carrier rockets and 36 space apparatuses. A two-thirds of the space apparatus launchings were carried out for the Russian Defence Ministry, and the rest were for other ministries and departments. The most often used rockets were the R-7a -- three-stage "Soyuz" carrier rockets (18 launchings) and the "Molnia" four-stage -- (8). Sixty-five per cent of all space apparatuses were put into orbit during the first half of 1993. The second quarter of 1993 turned out to be the busiest period, with 24 Russian satellite launchings. Such a tight launching schedule of launchings became possible only thanks to the hard work put in by the personnel of space military forces of Russia, three of whom, officers Aleksey Metelkin, Andrey Nesytov and Vladimir Dubovik (all from Plesetsk) were decorated with military awards on orders of the president of the Russian Federation for displaying courage in conditions which endangered their lives. But the number of launches in 1993 was less than that carried out in 1992, when 55 carrier rockets put 75 space apparatuses into orbit. This was, firstly, due to the introduction of a new generation of space systems with long-term functioning periods in space. For example, the global navigational satellite system was inducted in 1993. Secondly, due to inadequate state financing, 1993 was the first year not a single carrier rocket complex for scientific purposes was launched. Besides this, the "Proton" carrier rocket disaster of May 27, 1993 delayed carrier rocket launchings for four months. As a result, only three "Proton" launchings could take place by the end of 1993. Nevertheless, the new year promises to be sufficiently busy. This year's programme will begin with the launching of the "Soyuz TM-18" manned space ship which is scheduled to be put into orbit on January 8.
