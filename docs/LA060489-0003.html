June 4, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

Laura (AMC Sunday at 9:45 p.m.): The cop who falls in love with the socialite murder victim. A great film noir, directed by Otto Preminger. (1:45)

El Sur (Bravo Monday at 8 p.m.): A beautiful 1983 psychological study of an abandoned girl in the provinces, by the superb contemporary Spanish director, Victor Erice. (2:00)

Seven Women (Channel 13 Tuesday at 1:30 a.m.): John Ford's last film, with Anne Bancroft and Margaret Leighton among a septet of women besieged at a Chinese mission. One of his masterpieces. (2:00)

Four by Godard (Bravo Wednesday at 6:30, 8:30, 10 & 11:30 p.m.): An evening with the great, iconoclastic French film maker, Jean-Luc Godard. Four films from his '60s heyday: "Alphaville," "Les Carabiniers," "Le Petit Soldat" and "Breathless."

Heart Like a Wheel (Channel 11 Friday at 8 p.m.): Bonnie Bedelia takes home the trophies (or should have) in Jonathan Kaplan's feisty bio of drag-race champ Shirley Muldowney. (2:00)

Once Upon a Time in the West (Channel 11 Saturday at midnight): In memoriam: Sergio Leone (1929-1989). (3:00)
