921127 FT 27 NOV 92 / More workers are charged on safety WORKERS are four times more likely to be prosecuted for health and safety offences than employers, the government said in a written Commons answer yesterday. In 1990-91 six directors were prosecuted by the Health and Safety Executive and four were convicted. The average fine was Pounds 250. In the same period 26 prosecutions were brought against employees, with 19 convictions carrying an average fine of Pounds 291.
