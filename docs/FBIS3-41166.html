Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on narcotics developments in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific monitored by Bangkok Bureau between 31 January and 6 February 1994. Source information is given in parentheses after each item.

SOUTH ASIA
Pakistan
Customs Seizes 920 Kg of Hashish; Inspector Wounded
On 27 January, Customs officials stopped a truck for a routine check at the Khurkera checkpoint, 50 miles from Karachi. The truck sped away as one of the officers began to search it. After a brief chase, two suspects abandoned the truck and fled into the mountains. One of the suspects opened fire on the officers, critically wounding a Customs inspector. The suspects later escaped under cover of darkness. A search of the abandoned truck uncovered 920 kg of "B quality" hashish hidden in special cavities. Officials believe the hashish was en route to Karachi for local consumption. (Quetta BALOCHISTAN TIMES in English 30 Jan 94 p 7)

Customs Arrests 14 Nigerian Heroin Smugglers at Karachi Airport

Customs officials arrested 14 Nigerian nationals at Karachi's Quald-e-Azam International Airport on 24 January as they were about to board a Pakistan International Airways flight to Lagos. Over the next five days, officials recovered 13.8 kg of heroin powder from 925 capsules in the suspects' stomachs. (Islamabad THE MUSLIM in English 31 Jan 94 p 12)
Poppy Crops Destroyed in Bajaur Agency
So far this season, 2,090 kanals of land under poppy cultivation have been cleared in the Bajaur Agency. (Islamabad THE MUSLIM in English 1 Feb 94 p 4)
Peshawar Airport Security Seizes 4 Kg of Heroin, Arrests 19
On 31 January, Peshawar's Airport Security Force seized 4 kg of heroin from 18 passengers as they were about to board an Aero-Asia flight to Jeddah, Saudi Arabia via Karachi. The heroin was concealed in oval-shaped "floating balls" used in fishing. The passengers were expecting jobs in the Saudi fishing industry. Their tickets had been arranged by an agent, Mohammed Rahman, who gave them three bags each to carry. The passengers pleaded ignorance of the drugs. Assistant Police Subinspector Humayun Khan, who came to the airport and "tried to arrange their smooth passage" past inspection points, was also arrested. (Islamabad THE NEWS in English 1 Feb 94 p 12)

Three Africans Arrested Near Iranian Border With 2 Kg of Heroin

On 30 January, a special narcotics squad arrested Tanzanian nationals Jury Abdullah and Sood Mohammad and Mauritian national Saeed Amari as they were about to cross the border into Iran. The suspects were brought to a hospital, where x-rays revealed "a large number of mysterious things" in their stomachs. When the suspects' stomachs were pumped, officers found a total of 2 kg of heroin concealed inside of 200 rubber capsules. (Islamabad THE MUSLIM in English 2 Feb 94 p 5)
Peshawar Customs Seizes 120 Kg of Opium
Peshawar Customs intelligence officers seized 120 kg of opium and impounded a vehicle in Swabi on 4 February. The suspect escaped after an exchange of gunfire. (Islamabad THE NEWS in English 5 Feb 94 p 12)
SOUTHEAST ASIA
Burma
Authorities Seize 26 Kg of Opium in Pangtara Township
Acting on a tip, members of the Defense Services Intelligence Unit and antidrug squad searched a house in Yephyu Ashe Village, ShwePahto Village-tract, Pangtara Township, on 6 January in the presence of the ward law and order restoration council chairman and other witnesses. They found a total of 12 packets of raw opium weighing 26.046 kg. The opium belonged to Zaw Min, 35; and Khaung Hse, 25. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 13 Jan 94 p 7)
Mandalay Drug Squad Seizes 3 Kg of Opium
On 12 January, members of the Mandalay antidrug squad stopped and searched three people. Officers found two packets of raw opium weighing 3.02 kg in a black leather bag the suspects were carrying. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 21 Jan 94 p 7)
Officials Seize 30 Kg of Opium
Officials, together with members of the Pyinmana People's Police Force, arrested Maung Myint, 22, in a paddy field between Yekyo and Kan Oo villages, Pyinmana Township, on 19 January. Three people subsequently arrived on the scene. When they were searched, officers found 17 packets of raw opium weighing 29.729 kg in a knapsack. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 21 Jan 94 p 7)
Laos

Savannakhet Provincial Authorities Destroy Confiscated Marijuana

Authorities in Savannakhet Province destroyed 2,500 kg of compressed marijuana in a 31 January ceremony. The drugs were seized by local authorities in December. (Vientiane Vitthayou Hengsat Radio Network in Lao 0815 GMT 3 Feb 94)
Philippines
Narcotics Agents Seize, Burn 500 Kg of Marijuana
The 1st Narcotics Regional Field Unit seized 500 kg of dried marijuana leaves and marijuana seedlings in a raid on a plantation in the town of Santol, La Union. Officials burned the confiscated marijuana. (Quezon City PTV-4 Television Network in Tagalog 0500 GMT 31 Jan 94)
Police Arrest "Top Drug Pusher" in Bulacan
Police intelligence operatives have arrested Simeon Bania de la Cruz, "the top drug pusher in Bulacan." They also seized a plastic pouch containing 100 grams of methaphetamine hydrochloride and a car. (Manila PHILIPPINE DAILY INQUIRER in English 2 Feb 94 p 12)
Singapore
Malaysian Hanged for Drug Trafficking
Malaysian national Gan Kok Cheng, 33, was hanged at Changi Prison on 4 February. He had been convicted in October 1992 of trafficking in 106.8 grams of heroin in 1989. (Hong Kong AFP in English 0710 GMT 4 Feb 94)
