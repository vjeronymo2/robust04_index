931014 FT 14 OCT 93 / UK Company News: NatWest to set up new Lloyd's trust NATWEST Markets Corporate Finance yesterday announced the formation of a new Lloyd's investment trust. The trust will be designed to raise as much as Pounds 75m in equity capital to support the Lloyd's of London insurance market. The Angerstein Underwriting Trust will be listed on the stock exchange and is expected to invest in a range of Lloyd's syndicates. NatWest has been working closely with Coutts, the private bank, and aims to market the new trust to institutional and private investors. Stace Barr Underwriting Agencies, an independent members' agent, will advise Angerstein. NatWest Wood Mackenzie will be broker and NatWest Investment Management will be investment manager. Mr Andrew Smithers, chairman of Angerstein, said: 'The proposed introduction of corporate capital not only provides an opening to invest in Lloyd's, without the risks of unlimited liability, but it comes at a time that appears favourable for investors.'
