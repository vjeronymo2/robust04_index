941206 FT 06 DEC 94 / Directors worried about losing staff More directors are concerned about losing staff than about the skills shortage, according to a survey of job trends published today. In a poll of more than 400 companies across the UK, one in four senior directors said retaining employees was the most important issue in human resources. A similar poll in April revealed that more directors were concerned about the lack of skills among employees. The skills gap now ranks second in the list of personnel priorities, with 20 per cent of directors citing it as their most pressing problem. Mr Alec Reed, chairman of Reed Personnel Services, which carried out the telephone poll at the end of last month, said: 'While companies are confident that salary rises will be held at an average of 3.2 per cent, they are already feeling the effects of a tighter labour market, with its attendant pressures.'
