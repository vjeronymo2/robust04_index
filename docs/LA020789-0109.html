February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST: LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF;

LOW TURNOUT IN 50TH DISTRICT EXPECTED

Candidates are working to get out the votes for today's special election for a vacant seat in the 50th Assembly District. Turnout is expected to be low.

Campaign consultants said turnout could be as low as 15% for the rare February special election in the district encompassing Inglewood, El Segundo, Westchester, Lennox and parts of South-Central Los Angeles.

The election pits Curtis Tucker Jr., son of the late Assemblyman Curtis R. Tucker (D-Inglewood), against Democrats Lois Hill-Hale and Carl McGill and Republican Mike Davis.

Tucker is regarded as the favorite because of name recognition, a large fund-raising lead and backing by state Democratic leaders. The special election was called when the elder Tucker won a posthumous reelection victory in November, a month after his death from cancer.
