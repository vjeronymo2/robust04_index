Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 25 KYODO -- The Food Agency has asked an electric appliance discount store operator to scrap its plan to sell rice, the company reported Friday [25 March]. Jonan Denki Co., which operates a discount store chain in the Tokyo area, said it plans to sell 29 tons of a popular domestic rice brand harvested in Akita Prefecture, starting on Sunday. The company said the agency asked it to call off the plan, warning that selling rice without a government license will constitute a violation of the Domestic Food Control Law. Toshio Miyaji, president of the company, told reporters he has no intention of changing the plan at present.
