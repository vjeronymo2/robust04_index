BFN [Unattributed report: "Is Ukraine Flexing Its Muscles?"] [Text] On 5 April, a group of officers under the leadership of Hennadiy Hurin, first deputy chief of Ukraine's Ministry of Defense General Staff, visited the Crimea. The aim of the visit was to check, as scheduled, the state of affairs and to study problems in Ukraine's military units. On that day, Hennadiy Hurin visited the headquarters of Ukraine's Naval Forces in Sevastopol and ships of the Ukrainian Fleet. The group of officers also included Ukrainian presidential adviser for military issues Major General Volodymyr Petepko, who visited commander of the Black Sea Fleet Admiral E. Baltin in a naval hospital. On the same day, Ukraine's first deputy minister of internal affairs Colonel General Valentyn Nedryhaylo also visited the Crimea. He headed a group of the ministry's officials that arrived in order to render assistance in the operational work on combating crime. Both the Ministry of Defense and Ministry of Internal Affairs of Ukraine rejected the assumption by a number of mass media on Ukraine's alleged flexing its muscles in the absence of President Yu. Meshkov [who was on a visit to Cyprus at the time].
