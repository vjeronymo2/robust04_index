February 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

Greed (Z Monday at 1 a.m.): Even though this famous 1924 Erich Von Stroheim film was cut drastically by Irving Thalberg, it remains a cinema landmark. Gibson Gowland stars as a simple San Francisco dentist undone by his crazed wife (Zasu Pitts). (2:15)

Three Bad Men (Z Monday at 3:15 a.m.): A 1926 John Ford silent about three noble outlaws in a tale of self-sacrifice which includes a spectacular re-creation of the Oklahoma Land Rush. (2:03)

The Fallen Idol (Channel 7 Thursday at 1 a.m.): The Carol Reed-Graham Greene classic about a small boy who unwittingly incriminates his best friend, the family butler, in murder and scandal. (2:00)

The Postman Always Rings Twice (Channel 13 Friday at 1:30 a.m.): Tay Garnett's memorable 1946 version of the James M. Cain tale of sex, murder and guilt starring Lana Turner (at her sultriest) and John Garfield. (2:00)
