March 6, 1990, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

PREP BASEBALL ROUNDUP;

SANTA ANA VALLEY'S CROSBY SHUTS DOWN CORONA DEL MAR, 9-1

Scott Crosby didn't allow a hit for four innings as Santa Ana Valley High School defeated Corona del Mar, 9-1, in the first round of the Newport Elks' baseball tournament Monday.

Crosby (1-0) struck out three and did not walk a batter. He was relieved in the fifth by Jose Arroyo, who gave up a run. Santa Ana Valley coaches pulled Crosby, who was six pitches shy of his early-season limit of 50.

Crosby had two hits and a run batted in for Santa Ana Valley (1-0). Mike Murtaugh had two hits and two RBIs and Juan Sanchez had a hit and two RBIs for the Falcons.

In other Newport tournament games:

Foothill 11, Capistrano Valley 6 -- Kent Donnelly had a grand slam and pitched five innings, getting five strikeouts for host Foothill (1-0).

Santa Ana 12, Mater Dei JV 5 -- Victor Franco hit a two-run home run and John Morales and Santiago Lopez each had three hits and scored three runs for host Santa Ana (1-0).

Saddleback 10, Orange 7 -- Tommy Smith had a two-run single and a sacrifice fly and Ralph Cedillo had two hits and two RBIs for Saddleback (3-0). Ruben Hernandez hit a three-run homer for Orange (0-3).

Mission Viejo 2, Estancia 1 -- Greg Brannis of Estancia pitched a one-hitter with 11 strikeouts, but Mission Viejo scored two unearned runs. Steve Evans (1-0) allowed three hits for visiting Mission Viejo.

Mission Viejo's only hit was a two-out double in the fifth by Mark Newman. Pinch-runner Alex Diaz scored on a throwing error.

In the championship bracket of the Loara tournament:

Fountain Valley 11, Diamond Bar 6 -- Derek Brown had two hits, including a three-run homer, and four RBIs for host Fountain Valley (2-0).

El Dorado 4, Riverside North 1 -- Pitcher Andy Brazeel allowed three hits and struck out six for host El Dorado (2-0).

Lakewood 5, Laguna Hills 0 -- Laguna Hills' Kevin Lovengier had a two-out single in the seventh inning to break up a no-hitter by Lakewood's Dave Reeser.

Ocean View 13, Kennedy 1 -- Dan Hernandez had three hits and scored four runs, and sophomore Micah Edwardson had an RBI triple and three-run home run for host Ocean View (2-0).

Sunny Hills 14, Los Alamitos 6 -- Brian Veronda had three hits, scored two runs and had two RBIs for host Sunny Hills (2-2).

Katella 11, Magnolia 4 -- Eric Boyd had three hits and three RBIs and Dennis Briggs hit a solo home run for Katella (2-0).

Savanna 6, University 3 -- Doug Sutton's two RBI keyed a four-run fifth inning for Savanna (2-0). University dropped to 1-1.

In the consolation bracket:

Pacifica 7, Western 6 -- Chris Castellano doubled in two runs to give Pacifica the lead in the sixth.

Newbury Park 6, El Toro 3 -- Dave Owens had two hits and two RBIs for El Toro (0-2).

La Quinta 8, Irvine 7 -- Anthony Beltran had a bases-loaded single in the seventh to drive in Matt Logan with the game-winning run. Logan had started the inning with a double. Mike Seal had three hits with two RBIs for Irvine (0-2).

Villa Park 3, Los Amigos 2 -- Danny Strapp walked, stole second, went to third on a throwing error and scored on sacrifice fly by James Hong in the seventh for host Villa Park (1-1).

Huntington Beach 4, Loara 0 -- Kenny Mays pitched six-plus innings, allowing six hits and striking out six for the victory at Boysen Park in Anaheim.

San Clemente 7, Downey 2 -- Doug DeLaGarza hit a two-run home run, and had an RBI double to lead host San Clemente (1-1). He also threw a three-hitter with five strikeouts. Ken Dolan hit a solo home run for San Clemente.

Cypress 4, Marina 2 -- Catcher David Dzurilla threw out three runners trying to steal, had three hits, including two doubles, and two RBIs for Cypress (1-1). Marina is 0-2.

Santiago 5, Anaheim 3 -- Kip Cruz had a triple and two RBIs to lead Santiago (3-1) in the consolation round.
