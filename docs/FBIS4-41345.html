BFN [From the "Pacific Ocean" Program] [Text] The international community focused its attention on problems concerning the utilization of liquid radioactive waste in the Far East yet again. An international commission comprised of leading Japanese and British experts arrived in the Maritime Kray capital today. The representative delegation is headed by Jun Yamada, deputy chief of Japanese Foreign Ministry Department of Nuclear Energy. For three days, experts will hold talks with the kray administration, specialists of the State Committee for the Supervision of Nuclear and Radiation Safety, and the command of the Pacific Fleet. They will also visit the Zvezda nuclear submarine repair plant in the city of Bolshoy Kamen. The commission intends to apply conclusions drawn from the trip and propose to local authorities and fleet command methods of utilization of liquid radioactive waste.
