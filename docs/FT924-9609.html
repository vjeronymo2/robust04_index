921105 FT 05 NOV 92 / The Maastricht Vote: Government a mere two votes away from defeat THE narrowness of the government victory last night is highlighted by a breakdown of the results. If an extra two Tories had switched sides and voted against the government on its motion, or a handful more had abstained, Mr Major would be contemplating defeat this morning. Both the Labour and Conservative parties achieved a full turnout of members -267 and 332 MPs respectively. In the first vote - on Labour's amendment to the government motion - there were 10 abstentions by Tory MPs and 23 who voted against the government. But in the second vote, on the government's motion, three more Conservative MPs - Mr David Porter (Waveney), Mr Michael Lord (Suffolk Central) and Mr John Carlisle (Luton North) - joined the Conservative rebellion. In addition there were seven abstentions, cutting the government majority to just three. Four speakers and deputy speakers, plus the two tellers for each side, do not count in the final voting figures. A total of 319 members voted for the government motion to proceed with ratification. They included 299 Conservatives, 19 Liberal Democrats and one Ulster Populist Unionist MP. A total of 316 members voted against the government motion, consisting of 267 Labour, 26 Conservatives, one Liberal Democrat, four SDLP, three Scottish Nationalists, four Welsh Nationalists, eight Ulster Unionists and three Democratic Unionists.
