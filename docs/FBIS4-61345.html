BFN ["News Release"] [Text] Estonian Prime Minister Mart Laar, currently on a visit to France, was received, on Wednesday [4 May], by French President Francois Mitterand, Estonian Foreign Ministry press service told ETA. At the meeting, primarily Estonian-Russian relations and issues connected to Russian troop withdrawal from Estonia were discussed. "Be patient, but remain firm of your positions," Mitterand said. A festive dinner was given in honour of Laar at the French Foreign Ministry, where French state minister Pierre Mehaignerie held a speech, in which he pointed at the success of Estonian economic reforms and confirmed that France supported Estonia's moving towards European Union. "European Union has to open its doors to all those that are a part of European history and culture by the end of the century," Mehaignerie stressed. Mart Laar was also received by French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur and Foreign Minister Alain Juppe. Laar is to return to Estonia on Thursday.
