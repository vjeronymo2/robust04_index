BFN [Excerpt] Nabil Sha'th, who arrived in Gaza last week, will meet tomorrow in East Jerusalem with members of the Palestine National Authority [PNA] from the West Bank. Today he will meet with Major General Amnon Shahaq to discuss issues left unresolved in the Israel-PLO peace treaty. Mark Weiss reports: [Begin Weiss recording] All the members of the PNA from the West Bank have been invited to tomorrow's meeting at the Orient House in East Jerusalem. These include Bethlehem Mayor Ilyas Frayj, Sa'ib 'Urayqat, and Faysal al-Husayni. Other leading figures from the West Bank could also take part, such as Radwan Abu-'Ayyash, the head of the Palestine Broadcasting Corporation, and Hanan 'Ashrawi. Opposition figures have also been invited to the meeting. Sha'th, who holds the diplomatic relations portfolio in the PNA also reportedly intends to visit the al-Aqsa Mosque on the Temple Mount and churches in the Old City during his visit to Jerusalem. [passage omitted] [end recording] [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 0500 GMT on 19 June adds: "Tzomet has called on the prime minister not to allow Nabil Sha'th to hold a meeting with the Palestine Authority members in Orient House in Jerusalem. Knesset Member Moshe Peled told our correspondent Hayim Hecht that the ability to prevent this meeting will be the government's first test of the seriousness of its declarations concerning Jerusalem."]
