BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Sergey Bychkov] [Text] Moscow June 16 TASS -- Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet remains optimistic about prospects of signing a bilateral treaty with Russia. "Talks with the Russian side are going on productively. The new interstate treaty has been in general agreed upon which is very important", he told TASS on Thursday and added that the document would be signed after certain improvements. The treaty was scheduled to be signed today. However, the delegations failed to eliminate various disagreements, TASS learned from sources close to the Russian Government. Sources from the Vietnamese delegation explained to TASS that the disputed issues include prospecting and production of oil and natural gas by the joint "Vietsovpetro" venture, the problems of the Vietnamese community in Russia, as well as the Russian naval base in Cam Ranh. Vietnam wants to revise its leasing terms. Besides, agreement is yet to be reached on the role of the United Nations in the world and the Vietnamese debt to Russia.
