930730 FT 30 JUL 93 / International Company News: Microsoft sees lower sales SALES at Microsoft, the US computer software group, for the fiscal year just started will not reach the record levels of the year to June, Mr Michael Brown, treasurer, told analysts, writes Richard Waters. New products will provide 45 to 50 per cent of sales, against the 35 to 40 per cent predicted for the last fiscal year. Income from applications software should rise from 58 to 63 per cent of sales, systems revenues fall from 34 to 31 per cent and hardware income slip from 6 to 5 per cent.
