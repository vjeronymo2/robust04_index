BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 9 KYODO -- Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata spoke to his cabinet ministers Monday [9 May] about the need to express to future generations the importance of Japan's remorse for its former wartime aggression, government officials said. Hata outlined his basic position on Japan's behavior in World War II after former Justice Minister Shigeto Nagano created an uproar last week with his denial of historical facts on Japanese aggression, the officials said. "As the head of Shinseito during last year's general elections, I expressed (in speeches) my deep remorse and convictions over the Japanese military's aggressive behavior and colonial rule (in Asia)," Hata told the cabinet meeting. "Looking ahead to the 50th anniversary of the end of the war next year, we must express to future generations (our remorse)," Hata was quoted as saying. On his decision to appoint Nagano as justice minister, Hata said that he had asked about his views of the war and got the reply that "war must not happen again." When Nagano offered his resignation over his controversial remarks, including the charge that the Chinese account of the 1937 Nanjing massacre is a "fabrication," Hata said he accepted the resignation without any special effort to dissuade Nagano. At Monday's cabinet meeting, newly appointed Justice Minister Hiroshi Nakai greeted the ministers, but did not go on to offer his views of the war, the officials said. During an extended Japanese holiday period last week while Hata was on a four-nation tour of Europe, the prime minister outlined his position on the war and Japanese colonial rule in a letter to the cabinet ministers. Borrowing from former Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, Hata wrote "In the past, the aggressive behavior and colonial rule of our nation inflicted unbearable suffering and sorrow on many people. I want to express anew my feelings of deep remorse and apology." Chief Cabinet Secretary Hiroshi Kumagai said he thought the cabinet ministers fully understood Hata's cautionary letter regarding Nagano's controversial remarks. Meanwhile, Hata spoke at a ceremony at the Foreign Ministry to officially hand over the post of foreign minister to Koji Kakizawa, and said he is well aware that Japan's colonial policy and actions during World War II inflicted unbearable suffering upon neighboring and other Asian nations. The prime minister said he wants to do his best to bring peace to the world based on remorse. Hata called Nagano's remarks on Japan's wartime aggression very "regrettable" and that it was an "unexpected happening" since he personally had trust in Nagano. Nagano resigned over the weekend due to international outrage over his remarks that the 1937 Nanjing massacre never happened and that Japan's invasion of China and other Asian nations was not an act of aggression but aimed at liberating these countries from colonialism. Hata said that as a Japanese, he feels proud to apologize and show remorse about the fact that Japan caused pain to other nations. He urged Kakizawa to carry out diplomacy aimed at bringing peace to the world. Kakizawa told the same ceremony he will carry forward Hata's diplomatic policy and work to create a Japan which can be trusted and loved by other nations.
