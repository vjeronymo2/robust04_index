930816 FT 16 AUG 93 / Mail union mix-up delays decision on peace deal A BALLOT which might resolve the 14-day-old Royal Mail dispute in Cardiff has been delayed after confusion in the Union of Communication Workers. Hopes of a settlement had been high after the UCW's national executive agreed on Thursday to recommend a return to work on the basis of a framework agreement negotiated between national officials and Royal Mail. Mr Derek Hodgson, deputy general secretary of the UCW, said yesterday that the intention had been for national officers, accompanied by local branch representatives, to hold a meeting in Cardiff on Saturday to explain to the 900 workers the progress made in the talks and to decide whether to proceed with a ballot. He said that a substantial number of people did not attend, however, because of confusion between the head office and the branch about whether the meeting was going ahead. A meeting is now planned for today. At issue has been the introduction of new fixed shift patterns for workers at two Royal Mail offices to replace the roster system, a change that has taken place already in a large part of Royal Mail. The strike has paralysed delivery of more than one million letters and hundreds of companies in south Wales have had to make alternative arrangements, such as posting letters outside Cardiff and investing in fax equipment. Mr Graham Hall, head of Cardiff Chamber of Commerce, said that a number of companies were having financial problems. 'They cannot pay their suppliers as they are not receiving payment from their customers.'
