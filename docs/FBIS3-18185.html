Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Istanbul, Feb 24 (A.A) -- Premier Tansu Ciller said on Wednesday [25 February] that there was no need to invest in foreign currency. Ciller assured businessmen during a meeting held in Istanbul that they need not to invest on foreign currency "what we need is solidarity and confidence" she added. She said "Turkey could only overcome its economic problems by being shoulder to shoulder" and called for support from the businessmen. Ciller said there was no increase in inflation at the moment, "the only thing we should do is to increase the entrance of foreign exchange." The Central Bank effectively devaluated the Turkish lira by nearly 12 percent against the dollar on Wednesday. Turkish newspapers, comparing banknote rates, calculated the change at 13.6 percent. The Turkish lira ended on Thursday at 17,600-700 to the dollar after Central Bank intervention above 17,500 restored some calm to markets. The Turkish lira was devaluated to 17,250 on Wednesday night. Exporters, whose goods will become more competitive abroad, said short-term cost inflation arising from devaluation could nevertheless disrupt industrial production.
