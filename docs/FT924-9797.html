921104 FT 04 NOV 92 / Hurd in talks with Danes Britain last night reaffirmed its desire to avoid upsetting the Maastricht ratification process, in spite of difficulties posed by Denmark's proposals for changes in the treaty, writes David Marsh, European Editor. In talks between Mr Douglas Hurd, the UK foreign secretary, and Mr Uffe Ellemann-Jensen, his Danish opposite number, in London the British government said it wanted to work towards an accord with Denmark without 'reopening' the treaty to fresh negotiation.
