BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents] [Text] Bonn May 11 TASS -- "We have agreed to see off the Russian troops (from Germany) in a worthy manner and add festive character to the event", Russian President Boris Yeltsin told a press conference on Wednesday adding that he and Chancellor Helmut Kohl had discussed the issue in detail. Kohl earlier said the farewell ceremony would take place in Berlin where the Second World War ended. The ceremony should signal an end to a certain historical phase, according to Yeltsin. Germany, Russia and the whole of Europe are entering a new era of relations, an era of reconciliation, he said and added that the ceremony would be a prologue for the celebrations of the 50th anniversary of the end of World War Two to be marked in 1995. Yeltsin stressed that Kohl had confirmed the German position to politicallly include Russia into the G-7, to accept it into the Council of Europe and to promote an early partnership and cooperation agreement between Russia and the EU [European Union].
