931222 FT 22 DEC 93 / People: Plaistowe takes on APB Ian Plaistowe, a senior partner with accountants Arthur Andersen, is to become chairman of the Auditing Practices Board, the standards-setting body for auditors. He will take over in April next year from Bill Morrison, who retired recently as deputy chairman of KPMG Peat Marwick. Michael Boyd, a partner with Ernst & Young, is also resigning as vice chairman of the APB at the same time, and will be replaced by Ian Percy, a partner with Grant Thornton. Michael Chamberlain, chairman of the Consultative Committee of Accountancy Bodies, which funds the APB, says he sees two roles for Plaistowe: 'The first is to continue its programme of revising and tightening up existing auditing standards and guidelines; the second is to look forward and deal with the expectations and issues of the future.'
