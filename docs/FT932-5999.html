930601 FT 01 JUN 93 / Deputy chairman of GA dies ROBERT ADAM, deputy chairman of General Accident, died last week aged 70. After training as a chartered accountant he joined British Petroleum in 1950. Subsequently he became one of the leading figures in the oil industry. After retiring from BP in 1983 he became a director of MEPC, the property company, which he served as chairman between 1984 and 1988. He joined the board of GA in 1980 and became deputy chairman in 1987.
