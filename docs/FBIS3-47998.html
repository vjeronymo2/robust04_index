Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 21 (YONHAP) -- Russia has made official its offer to repay a South Korean loan with military hardware and technology, Foreign Ministry officials said Friday. The Ministry had received a list, very close to one sent last month by a private defense institute, of arms and equipment that it was willing to include in such a deal on Jan. 19, they said. To make the offer formal, Deputy Premier Aleksandr Shokhin sent a signed letter with it, they said. Talks are expected among Foreign, Defense and Finance Ministry officials to decide whether Russian arms are compatible with Korea's current weapons and to weigh the pluses of Moscow's proposal. Seoul agreed on 3 billion U.S. dollars in aid to Moscow but delivered only 1.47 billion dollars and suspended the rest after the collapse of the Soviet Union. When Shokhin was visiting the Taejon Expo last year, he suggested that his country pay back the loan in military goods.
