BFN [Report by Yuriy Kukanov: "Results of St. Petersburg Ballot Will Be Counted by...the Courts"] [Text] St. Petersburg -- Elections in the city on the Neva could be ruled valid in only half of the 50 districts. And even there, whether they are legally valid or not, will have to be decided by the city court, since city Mayor Anatoliy Sobchak has rejected a protest from the prosecutor's office over the extension of the voting hours in the ballot, which took place 20 March. The fate of a further 25 seats will be decided in the fall. However, the new organ of representative power could prove noncompetent -- its legal authority is determined by the presence of two-thirds of elected deputies.
