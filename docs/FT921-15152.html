920111 FT 11 JAN 92 / 750 jobs to be cut at GEC companies GPT, Britain's biggest telecommunications manufacturer, yesterday announced it is to shed 600 jobs in April at its Coventry plant. The company, which is 60 per cent owned by General Electric Company, makes telephone equipment, digital exchanges and fibre optics. It employs 18,000. The recession and changes in technology and market requirements were blamed for the job losses. GEC-Marconi Communications said that up to 150 jobs are to go at Chelmsford as part of a streamlining move.
