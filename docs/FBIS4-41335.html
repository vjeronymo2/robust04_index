BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Pavel Vanichkin, Oleg Velichko] [Text] Washington June 23 TASS -- The World Bank will provide Russia four credits totalling 820 million U.S. dollars. This is the main outcome of the talks held between Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and World Bank President Lewis Preston on Wednesday evening. The agreements on providing these credits were signed by Russian Vice-Premier Aleksandr Shokhin and World Bank Vice-President Wilfried Thalwitz. Two of these credits are meant for assisting the development of Russia's agriculture sector. These are credits to support reforms in agriculture (240 million dollars) and to support land reform (80 million dollars). The third credit will be provided by the bank for restoring roads (300 million dollars), and the fourth for the development of financial institutions (200 million dollars). After the signing ceremony of the agreements, Viktor Chernomyrdin said that Russia plans to create a special federal organization which will work with international financial institutions. "We requested the World Bank to put pressure on countries who are Russia's debtors," he said. "Finally, one more important result of the talks was that the World Bank president will pay a visit to Moscow," the Russian prime minister said.
