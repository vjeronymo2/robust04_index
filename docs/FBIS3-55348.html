Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Jan 06, BNS -- Estonian Prime Minister Mart Laar at 1 p.m. local time [1100 GMT] today formally presented to the country's president the proposals concerning the replacement of Foreign Minister Trivimi Velliste, Finance Minister Madis Uurike, Economics Minister Toomas Sildmae and Defense Minister Juri Luik, officials at the presidential chancellery and the government press office said. The new appointments are Juri Luik as the foreign minister, Heiki Kranich as the finance minister, Toivo Jurgenson as the economics minister and Indrek Kannik as the defense minister. The changes are aimed at "bringing new blood into the government and improving cooperation between the parliament factions and the government," government spokesman Ain Saarna said. Today's formal presentation of the appointments was preceded by the president's and the prime minister's consultations with the heads of the ruling coalition factions in the parliament. The minister nominees have also presented their plans of action before the council of the ruling coalition and the Cabinet. All of the minister candidates received the support of the ruling coalition parties. The changes in the government must be approved by President Lennart Meri, who under the government law has got three days to make his decision.
