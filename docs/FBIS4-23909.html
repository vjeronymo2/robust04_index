BFN [Text] The newspaper, CITY PRESS, reports that the first group of APLA [Azanian People's Liberation Army] candidates for the South African National Defense Force has arrived in South Africa for screening at De Brug outside Bloemfontein. The group of 200 arrived by air from Tanzania along with several Umkhonto we Sizwe [MK, Spear of the Nation -- ANC Military Wing] cadres. The paper quotes the chief of army staff logistics, Major General Pieter Coetsee, as saying that another 1,200 APLA members are expected in the country this week. General Coetsee also announced that between 500 and 600 MK soldiers, who had been part of the National Peacekeeping Force, would be integrated into the new army.
