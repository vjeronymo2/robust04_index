BFN [Text] Weihai, May 17 (XINHUA) -- East China's Shandong Province has recently signed a contract with Singapore on a 200 million U.S. dollar project for an "international city." Located in Yantai city and covering an area of 658,000 square meters, it is the biggest project wholly financed by Singapore. According to the plan, the "city" will have facilities such as meeting center, exhibition center, education center, entertainment center, office buildings and luxury apartments. Figures given at a press conference showed that Singapore injected over 100 million U.S. dollars into the province from January to March this year, jumping to second largest investor after Hong Kong. Last year, the province absorbed a promised investment of 390 million U.S. dollars from Singapore, quadruple that of 1992.
