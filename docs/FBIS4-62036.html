BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Nikolay Geronin] [Text] Moscow May 11 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin will hold talks with South Korean President Kim Yong-sam during his visit to Moscow on June 1-4. The two leaders will meet one-to-one and then hold talks in delegations. They will also have an informal meeting, Valentin Moyseyev, an Asia department official of the Russian Foreign Ministry's department, said on Wednesday. Moyseyev said that during the visit the sides will sign a political document determining the main directions of future cooperation between Russia and South Korea. They are also expected to sign a package of agreements on various aspects of cooperation, including on environmental protection and prevention of incidents at sea and outside territorial waters of the two countries. The Russian and South Korean foreign ministries have prepared and finalised a protocol on mutual exchanges. The sides may also sign an agreement on the protection of intellectual property. Kim Yong-sam's visit will be his first trip to Russia, and the fifth Russian-South Korean summit meeting. After Moscow, the South Korean leader will visit Tashkent for talks with Uzbek leaders on bilateral relations. On the way back home, Kim Yong-sam plans to make a one-day stopover in Vladivostok to meet with local officials and discuss prospects for cooperation between South Korea and the Russian Far East.
