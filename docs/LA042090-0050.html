April 20, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON;

CHOLESTEROL AND FAT

Question: I've followed your advice about reading labels on the food I buy, and it's really been a valuable piece of advice.

But sometimes I'm not clear about what the label is telling me. For example, if a label says the food does not have cholesterol, does that mean it doesn't have much saturated fat?

Answer: A cholesterol-free food can be either high or low in saturated fat.
