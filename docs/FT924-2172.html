921215 FT 15 DEC 92 / International Capital Markets: KPMG global fee income rises 2.3% over year KPMG PEAT MARWICK, the worldwide accounting network, reported global fee income up 2.3 per cent to Dollars 6.15bn for the year to September 30, writes Andrew Jack. Audit fees grew 5 per cent to Dollars 3.2bn and tax advice by 7 per cent to Dollars 1.3bn. The recession affected growth in Australia and New Zealand, and also North America, where income fell 0.8 per cent to Dollars 2.23bn. In common with other firms, KPMG does not provide any details of its profits or assets. It reported strong growth in eastern Europe, other parts of Asia Pacific and Latin America, where income was up 14 per cent. The firm had 6,004 partners and some 73,000 staff in 124 countries worldwide.
