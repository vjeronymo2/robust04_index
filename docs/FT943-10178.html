940806 FT 06 AUG 94 / Economic Diary: Forward events TOMORROW: International Moslem conference in London. 10th international Aids conference in Yokohama (until August 12). MONDAY: Central Statistical Office announces producer price index numbers (July) and figures for credit business (June). Jordanian and Israeli negotiators resume bilateral peace talks at the Dead Sea. Interim figures from British Airways. TUESDAY: Central Statistical Office publishes figures for the balance of visible trade (May). US wholesale trade (June). Mr Bill Clinton, US president, meets Mr Levon Ter-Petrosyan, president of Armenia, at the White House. World Gold Council issues statement on gold demand trends. Interim results from Barclays and General Accident. WEDNESDAY: Analysis of bank lending to UK residents (second quarter) from the Bank of England and the index of production for Scotland (first quarter) from the Scottish Office. Confederation of British Industry/Business Strategies regional industrial trends survey. Mr Mumtaz Soysal, foreign minister of Turkey, expected in Damascus to attend tripartite talks with Syria and Iran to review developments in Iraq. Interim results published by Standard Chartered, Commercial Union and GKN. THURSDAY: Bank of England gives figures for capital issues and redemptions (July). US producer price index (July); retail sales (July). Interim figures from BOC Group, Royal Insurance and Shell Transport. FRIDAY: BISPA issues production figures for usable steel (July). US consumer price index (July); business inventories (June); real earnings (July). Non-Aligned Movement ministerial meeting on foreign debt in Jakarta (until August 15). EPA releases monthly economic report in Tokyo. RMT plan further 24-hour strike in continuing dispute with Railtrack. Interim results from Unilever. Glorious Twelfth - grouse shooting season begins.
