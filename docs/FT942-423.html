940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / Letters to the Editor: Veto power Sir, Earlier this month the 12 oil ministers of Opec member countries met in Vienna to elect their secretary general. Eleven agreed on Dr Alirio Parra of Venezuela, but Iran vetoed the appointment. There was much criticism of the Iranian position on the bounds that 'civilised' behaviour in international relations is to acquiesce in matters of appointment to the unanimous views of colleagues. Last Saturday in Corfu. . . Are there other leaves for John Major to take from Tehran's book? Robert Mabro, fellow, St Antony's College, Oxford Institute for Energy Studies, 57 Woodstock Road, Oxford OX2 6FA
