921210 FT 10 DEC 92 / Assault on red tape is launched THE government will examine more than 6,000 pieces of legislation and 2,500 forms as part of its review of red tape affecting businesses, Mr Neil Hamilton, corporate affairs minister, said yesterday, Charles Batchelor writes. At the same time, the Institute of Directors launched a campaign to raise awareness of the dangers of over-regulation and to promote measures to reduce burdens on business. Mr Peter Morgan, institute director-general, said that small companies in particular faced a 'systems overload' when dealing with regulations. Mr Hamilton told an IoD seminar that government departments should complete their reviews of business legislation within a few weeks. They would then decide how to reduce the burden.
