December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

NEVER GIVE UP

Regarding Joselito Paule's Dec. 17 letter, I must say that if the writer gives up his dream of becoming a film maker based on one sentence by Steven Spielberg, then he never believed in it to begin with.

Mr. Paule states that he "can't afford (the) risk" of trying to make films of "personal satisfaction" in a competitive industry. But I ask Joselito Paule if he can afford the risk of knowing that he sold out on his dream, that he didn't even try. This is the stuff of mid-life crises.

I share the same dream, an undying passion to tell a good story, and no band of cutthroat businessmen is going to keep me from it.

I am a steadfast idealist who believes that the best life a person can live is the one closest to his or her dreams. And I believe Steven Spielberg would agree with this.

PERRY J. KING

Los Angeles
