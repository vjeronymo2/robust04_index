February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

CHILDREN RETURNED TO U.S.;

SANTA ANA POLICEMAN ACCUSED IN ABDUCTION

A 30-year-old Santa Ana police officer, accused of abducting his two children from his estranged wife's home after visiting them in northeastern England on Thursday, is back in the United States, authorities said Monday.

Malcolm Lowery has contacted British police and told them that he and his children, Sarah Jane, 8, and David Anthony, 7, are back in America and in good health, according to Inspector Edward Hill of the Newcastle upon Tyne Police Department.

The children were allegedly taken from the home of their mother, Barbara Lowery, 30, at about 8:30 p.m. Thursday, Hill said. "She left the room, went to the bathroom, and while she was there he took the children (away) in a car."

Officials on both sides of the Atlantic do not know how Lowery evaded an all-points bulletin.

Neither Lowery nor his estranged wife could be reached for comment.

Santa Ana Police Department spokeswoman Maureen Thomas said Lowery telephoned the department Monday. "He's obtained legal counsel, and he's going to be in contact with British authorities to resolve the custody situation," she said.

Lowery, who was hired in October, 1987, has been on vacation and is scheduled to return to patrol duty next week, Thomas said.

"Upon his return, his supervisors will discuss the issue and evaluate his conduct," she said.

"Basically, the only thing we can say is that British authorities have contacted us and said they do not intend to file any criminal charges against Officer Lowery," she said. "They feel it's a domestic issue and not a criminal issue."

Hill said Barbara Lowery "intends to take legal action to have the children returned."

The Lowerys came to the United States from Britain in 1985, he said, adding that Barbara Lowery and the children returned home in 1987 because "she didn't like America."
