Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Lebanese Forces Commander Dr. Samir Ja'ja' said in a telephone interview with the London-based AL-HAYAH newspaper that he does not know the reasons behind the tightened measures still being taken by units of the Lebanese Army in Ghidras. Ja'ja' expressed his willingness to hand over any wanted person if investigations prove he had a connection with recent explosions. Stating that the state, with all its apparatuses, has not requested he hand over anyone, Ja'ja' said: We were surprised by these measures, for which I can find no explanation. Ja'ja' revealed that 20 people have been arrested and that a 100 members of the Lebanese Forces are being treated as suspects and are being harassed.
