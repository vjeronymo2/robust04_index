Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] Norway and Nicaragua have signed a five-year technical advisory assistance agreement in different areas. Twenty-one Norwegian technicians are working in various regions of the country. Milviana Jerez has filed a report: [Begin recording] [Jerez] Tom Tyrihjell, resident representative of the Norwegian Development Authority and Sergio Mario Blandon, acting foreign cooperation minister, signed the five-year agreement. The average cost for each technician who will provide technical assistance is $80,000. [Tyrihjell, in progress] ...that is very important, because it represents a relationship more than technical cooperation. It is a relationship at the human level by Norwegian technicians who want to aid a developing country and return to their country with new experience. [Jerez] The Norwegian technicians are lending their technical assistance in the First, Fourth, Fifth, and Rio San Juan Regions. [Blandon, in progress] ...is really praiseworthy, because while we have difficult campaigns that hinder the cooperation process in Nicaragua, we are receiving encouragement from countries, such as yours, that are willing to help this country. [end recording]
