BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 21 KYODO -- The House of Councilors' ad hoc committee on political reform on Tuesday [21 June] passed bills to modify the constituencies for upper house elections. The bills, submitted jointly by the Liberal Democratic Party, the Social Democratic Party and three other groups, are designed to achieve a better balance in representation by adding one seat each in four constituencies, while slashing four seats from others. There will be no change in the size of the second chamber at 252 seats. The bills are scheduled to be sent to the House of Representatives after their expected passage Wednesday by the full upper house.
