Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The president, Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari, returned to Islamabad this evening after a four-day visit to Saudi Arabia. Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto received the president at the airport. Mr. Asif Ali Zardari and principal adviser to the Punjab chief minister Syed Faisal Saleh Hayat were also present. During his stay in Saudi Arabia, the president performed umrah [minor pilgrimage] and held talks with King Fahd of Saudi Arabia on a wide range of issues. In an airport statement before his departure from al-Madina al-Munawarrah, President Farooq Leghari said that during the talks with King Fahd the most important issues which came under discussion were recent developments in Indian-occupied Kashmir, situation in Afghanistan, and the plight of the Bosnian Muslims. He said he apprised King Fahd about the latest developments in South Asia and Pakistan's efforts to contain the explosive situation in Jammu and Kashmir in the face of brutal repression in the valley. He said he also informed King Fahd that Pakistan had accepted the Indian offer of talks at the foreign secretary's level in good faith and hopes that India would engage in a meaningful dialogue for resolving Jammu and Kashmir dispute in accordance with the UN Security Council resolutions and the Simla Agreement. However, he added, for further dialogue it is essential for India to create the right conditions and to desist from the policy of repression in the Indian-held Jammu and Kashmir. He said he also informed King Fahd that Pakistan had accepted the Indian offer of talks at the foreign secretary's level in good faith and hoped that India would engage in a meaningful dialogue for resolving Kashmir issue in accordance with UN Security Council resolutions.
