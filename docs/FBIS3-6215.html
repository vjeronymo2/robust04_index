Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 14 (YONHAP) -- South Korea's technology exports are on the rise, business sources said Monday [14 March]. Samsung Electronics Co. has exported 300 million U.S. dollars worth of technology and plants since 1990 and plans to export more than 20 kinds of technology to developing and developed countries this year. Samsung exported "synchronous D-ram" technology necessary to make 16 mega D-rams to Japan's Oki Electric Ind. Co. in February last year, as well as a 95-million-dollar refrigerator plant to Kazakhstan along with 6 million dollars in technology. Goldstar Co. has exported 820,000 dollars worth of technology in the color television field since 1990 and expects to earn another 700,000 dollars from color TV technology this year. Daewoo Electronics Co. exported a color television plant with an annual production capacity of 100,000 units and related technology to Iran Electric Industry (IEI) last year for 5 million dollars. In addition, SKC Ltd. exported 750,000 dollars in technology for assembling video cassette parts and for video cassette plant control to China last year. Hyundai Motor Co. signed a Complete-Knock-Down (CKD) contract with a Malaysian Company to assemble and produce 1.25-ton trucks in January this year. Hyundai will receive royalties for each truck produced in Malaysia from the company. Meanwhile, Dongbu Steel Co. Ltd. contracted with Germany's Wacker-Chemitronic Co. to export new processing technology for high-purity poly-christaline silicone last Tuesday. Dongbu will receive a 3.84-million-dollar technology fee and 1.0-2.5 percent of Wacker's total sales of the silicone products.
