Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan March 21 TASS -- 34 bodies of people killed in the crash of the Iranian C-130 plane in Nagorno-Karabakh, including nine children, will be sent home on Monday in presence of Iranian diplomats, relatives and experts who arrived in Yerevan. The Iranian experts intend to fly to Stepanakert to participate in the investigation of the aircrash on the spot. It became known from reliable sources that the recording of the talk between traffic controllers of the Yerevan airport and the crew of the Iranian plane was sealed and would be studied by experts. The C-130 plane had to fly through the Armenian airspace bound for Tehran, but deviated 100 kilometres from the route for unknown reasons. It thus got into the airspace of Azerbaijan and crashed three kilometres off Stepanakert, the capital of Nagorno-Karabakh.
