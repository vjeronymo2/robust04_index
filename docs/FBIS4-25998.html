BFN [Text] Dhaka, June 15 (XINHUA) -- "Bangladesh has never seen China as a military power or felt threat from it," said Mr. M. Morshed Khan, special envoy of Bangladesh Prime Minister Khaleda Zia. He made the remarks here on Tuesday at a news briefing held for the Japanese investment study delegation that left for Myanmar the same day. The official said that Bangladesh and China have historic ties based on culture as well as trade. China has been helping Bangladesh in its agricultural development, small engineering, garments as well as textile and other sectors, he added. "We are impressed by China's phenomenal economic successes and we are acquainted with its economic advancement," the special envoy said. He stressed that the word "confrontation" should be wiped off the Asian dictionary and economic cooperation ought be the word for Asia. Bangladesh stresses on harmonious development of investment and trade relations in its immediate neighborhood, in Asia and beyond, he said.
