Language: Dutch Article Type:BFN [Article signed KvdB: "PS Plummeting, VLD Losing Much Ground; Agalev and Flemish Bloc Only Flemish Winners in February Poll"] [Text] Brussels -- The PS [Walloon Socialist Party] is suffering very adverse effects from the continuous scandals which have been ravaging the government in recent months. The February poll, conducted by Dimarso on behalf of DE MORGEN, reveals that the Walloon Socialists only represent 30.1 percent of the French-speaking electorate, representing a 6-percent drop since December. In Flanders, the VLD's [Flemish Liberals and Democrats] progress seems to have come to a standstill. Party leader Verhofstadt and his supporters lost 1.5 percent (down to 28.5 percent), and are now at their lowest level since July 1993. Dimarso's monthly poll was conducted between 7 and 16 February among 1,000 Flemish, 1,000 Walloons, and 400 people from Brussels. There was a "no response" from 18 percent of those questioned. In Flanders, the majority parties are not benefiting from the VLD's downturn. The CVP [Christian People's Party] remains practically at the same level (21.8 percent), while the SP [Flemish Socialist Party] has dropped from 18.3 to 17.6 percent. Even the Volksunie [People's Union] has fallen from 6.6 to 6.3 percent, despite the strong European list it disclosed in February. The winners are Agalev [Live Differently] (up 0.7 percent to 11.3 percent), the Vlaams Blok [Flemish Bloc] (from 9.7 percent to 11.5 percent), and Rossem (from 2 to 2.4 percent).

Walloon Region
In the Walloon region, the PSC [Social Christian Party] is the biggest winner. Deprez' party registered a 1.5 percent increase to 20.1 percent; the PRL [Liberal Reformation Party] climbed slightly to 23 percent (up 0.7 percent); and the Ecolo [Ecologists] jumped to 16.3 percent (up 0.9 percent). The category "others," which in addition to the FDF [French-Speaking Front] and some minor leftwing parties includes ultraright-wing parties, lost some ground (from 11.2 to 10.5 percent).
