May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

BRIEFCASE: TRANSPORTATION;

TRANSCON FOCUSES ON SILVER LINING IN REPORT ON ITS LARGEST LOSS EVER

Transcon Inc., a large Orange-based trucking firm, lost $23.7 million last year. But you have to turn to Page 17 of the company's annual report to find that figure.

In an upbeat message to shareholders and employees, Chairman Orin S. Neiman starts by stating that Transcon closed the year with "a small, but meaningful profit" of $142,000 for the fourth quarter.

He goes on to mention that $23.9 million was lost in the previous three quarters, but the net loss for the full 12 months is not reported for another 15 pages. In between are six, full-page color photographs of Transcon customers giving testimonials and details of operating improvements made by the company over the past year.

The tone of the 1988 report is much different from a year earlier. When the company lost $19.9 million in 1987, the loss was listed on Page 1 of that year's report, and Neiman's message was blunt. It started: "Transcon suffered badly in 1987 . . . our biggest loss ever."
