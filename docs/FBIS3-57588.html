Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jan 26, BNS -- The Lithuanian president has appointed career serviceman Stanislovas Stancikas as the head of the Lithuanian State Border Defense Service. Colonel Stancikas, 47, held the post in 1991-1992, but was dismissed by then Prime Minister Gediminas Vagnorius. The new chief border guard received his military education in Russia. The president's entourage characterizes him as "an initiative worker with a strong feeling of responsibility." Stancikas replaced Petras Dragunaitis who served as the temporary head of the service.
