BFN [From the "Brief Local News" column] [Text] Designated Foreign Minister Gabriel Lewis Galindo, who leaves the country on a 15-day vacation today, revealed yesterday that Spanish Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez will meet with Panamanian President-Elect Ernesto Perez Balladares in Madrid, Spain, on 10 July, when the latter starts an official four-day visit in that country. The possibility of Spanish investments in Panama, support for the tourism program to be implemented by the new Panamanian Government, and other economic and social issues are part of the agenda to be discussed by Perez Balladares and Gonzalez, Lewis Galindo reported. He also said the banana issue will not be absent even though it is well-known that this is a very complex problem and could never be resolved during this trip. "The main purpose of this visit to Spain is to look for a rapprochement with Panama's traditional friends, such as the Spaniards and especially their president, Felipe Gonzalez," Lewis Galindo stated. The designated foreign minister, however, did not reject the possibility that the Democratic Revolutionary Party will make efforts to be reinstated into the Socialist International [SI], an organization of political parties with a great deal of prestige. Felipe Gonzalez is one of the SI's most important leaders. He added that President Perez Balladares will be accompanied by designated Planning Minister Guillermo Chapman, designated Presidency Minister Raul Arango, designated State Security Minister Jose Luis Sosa, former President Aristides Royo, and Mario Velasquez, the president-elect's spokesman.
