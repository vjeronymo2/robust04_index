BFN [Text] The minister for foreign affairs, Alhaji Babagana Kingibe, says the Federal Government is still committed to its decision that the controversy over the ownership of the Bakassi Peninsula can be resolved in accordance with the Charter of the Organization of African Unity, OAU. The minister was speaking today in Abuja when he received the OAU fact-finding team on the Nigeria-Cameroon border dispute. He pointed out that Cameroon has been unwilling to (?resolve) her contention with Nigeria in accordance with the provisions of the OAU and UN Charters. The minister expressed the hope that the visit would strengthen the spirit for a peaceful resolution to the Bakassi dispute. In his response, the leader of the team and assistant secretary general of the OAU, Ambassador (M.T. Matunga), said the team would not only look for facts but also explore ideas from both countries on a peaceful resolution of the dispute.
