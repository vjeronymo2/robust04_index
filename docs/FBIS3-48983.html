Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, January 2 (TANJUG) -- Heavy fighting continued in the former Yugoslav republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina over the new year holidays, despite an agreement reached by the three warring sides to halt hostilities from December 23 until January 15. Moslem forces continued attacks on Serb positions and villages on fronts in the wider region of Doboj in northern Bosnia-Herzegovina, the Doboj operative group of the Bosnian Serb army said on Sunday [2 January]. Moslem forces were the most active in the eastern part of the Ozren front, the source said. The villages of Milino Selo and Vozuca took the brunt of the attack. Moslem forces attacked Serb defence positions in the region of Foca and Cajnice in southeastern Bosnia-Herzegovina from the direction of Gorazde on Saturday night, the Herzegovina Corps in Bileca said on Sunday morning. Moslem-Croat conflicts also continued last night and this morning in southern Bosnia-Herzegovina and in its central part. Croatian radio reported on Sunday morning that Moslem forces last night attacked Bosnian Croat positions in the region of Mostar with infantry and artillery weaponry. Moslem Radio Sarajevo said that Croatian forces fired more than a hundred shells on Blagaj in southern Bosnia-Herzegovina on new year's eve and on Saturday.
