BFN [Text] Moscow May 9 TASS -- The second stage of the evacuation of Russian citizens from Yemen is scheduled for today. An official of the Russian Ministry for Emergency Situations told ITAR-TASS today in the morning that, according to the existing information, two Tu-154 planes will arrive in Sanaa, capital of north Yemen, in about two hours. One more plane is expected to fly to Djibouti, in north-eastern Africa. Marina Ryklina, head of the press centre of the Ministry for Emergency Situations, specified on Sunday that 152 Russian citizens were planned to be evacuated during the second stage, including staff members of the Russian Embassy. Aside from it, 78 Russians, as well as several Americans and eight French citizens are now in the port of Al Hudaydah. U.S. and French representatives officially appealed to the Russian Ministry for Emergency Situations to help evacuate their people to Djibouti and from there to Europe by a Russian plane. The Russian dry-cargo ship Khudozhnik Kustodiyev is now on the roadstead of Djibouti. It is ready to evacuate all those people. A task group of the Ministry for Emergency Situations, led by Valeriy Shuykov, is also staying in Djibouti. It came there several days ago before the first plane of the Ministry for Emergency Situations.
