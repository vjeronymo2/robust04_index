January 5, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: SAN CLEMENTE;

FIREFIGHTERS OK PACT RAISING SALARIES 5%

Ending six months of negotiations, city firefighters have agreed to a new four-year contract that includes a 5% annual pay increase, city officials announced Thursday.

The new labor agreement is retroactive to July 1 and covers 23 non-management fire personnel, Assistant City Manager Gregory L. Hulsizer said.

Besides the pay increase, the contract allows firefighters to retire at age 50 instead of 55, beginning in 1993. The contract expires June 30, 1993.

General city employees agreed to a new two-year contract in November, Hulsizer said. That pact, which affects 160 non-management employees, provides a 5% salary increase for fiscal year 1989-90 and a 4 1/2% increase for fiscal year 1990-91.

Negotiations are continuing with the city's 43-member police force. The city's contract with the San Clemente Peace Officers Assn. expired Sunday. The police union has complained that, according to a recent union survey, its members are the lowest-paid police officers in Orange County.
