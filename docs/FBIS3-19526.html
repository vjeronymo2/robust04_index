Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Canada's defense minister says the break up of the Soviet Union and the end of the Cold War doesn't mean an end to all tensions in Europe, and he says Canada must take that fact into consideration as it reviews the role of the Canadian military in the coming years. David Collenette points to last December's election victory by extreme nationalists in Russia as a potential threat to peace in the future. Canada's Liberal government has promised deep cuts in military spending, but Mr. Collenette says those promises were based on the belief that the world had entered a more peaceful era and that belief may no longer be valid.
