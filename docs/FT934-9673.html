931109 FT 09 NOV 93 / US attack on Tokyo over trade THE US increased pressure on Japan yesterday to bring down its trade surplus, urging Tokyo to cut income taxes substantially so that consumers have more money to spend on imports, Reuter reports from Washington. Administration officials repeatedly hammered Japan for failing to do more to spur growth and open its markets to imports. Nearly half of the annual US trade deficit is with Japan. 'I am concerned that Japanese growth appears to be slowing and Japanese demand for foreign products is increasing less rapidly than had been hoped,' Treasury Secretary Lloyd Bensten said in a statement. US officials pin hopes on reform of Japan's tax system. A Japanese advisory panel is expected to issue a report on tax reform in mid-November. Deputy Treasury Secretary Roger Altman told a Senate finance sub-committee: 'Strong fiscal action through a stimulative tax programme could be the key to reviving growth.'
