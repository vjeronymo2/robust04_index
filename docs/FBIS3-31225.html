Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Today, on the second day of his visit to Romania, Croatian President Franjo Tudjman and the Croatian delegation visited the Romanian parliament. They met the president of the House of Deputies, Adrian Nastase, and President of the Senate Oliviou Gherman. President Tudjman visited the House of Representatives, which was in session. He was welcomed with rapturous applause. Nastase said that all matters of political importance were discussed in the parliament comprising 14 parties and 460 representatives. He said that the main issues being discussed by the representatives were the democratic development of Romania and economic reforms. [words indistinct] the right way, he stressed, and added that all political disputes are resolved by political means in parliament and that there is a general consensus among the parties on foreign policy. In his temporary residence in Bucharest, President Tudjman received Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu. Discussing the crisis in the former Yugoslavia, they agreed that the international community had reacted too late and that there is disunity regarding solutions between the United Nations, European union, and the United States. The Croatian president and the Romanian prime minister also discussed economic relations between the two countries. Tonight the Croatian president will meet representatives of the Croatian community in Romania at the Croatian embassy in Bucharest.
