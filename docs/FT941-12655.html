940128 FT 28 JAN 94 / FT Quarterly Review of Personal Finance (8): The main points: a reminder MEASURES ALREADY IMPLEMENTED Indexation can no longer be used to create a loss for capital gains tax purposes All chargeable gains (for CGT purposes) made by individuals can be deferred, where the gain is reinvested in shares of a qualifying unquoted trading company. Retirement relief from CGT (for entrepreneurs selling a business) is extended. Full relief is available on the first Pounds 250,000 (previously Pounds 150,000) and half relief is available on the next Pounds 750,000 up to Pounds 1m (previously Pounds 450,000 up to Pounds 600,000). Vehicle excise duty up by Pounds 5 to Pounds 130. Rise in excise duties puts 11p on pack of 20 cigarettes, petrol up 3p a litre. Beer and spirits unchanged. Excise duties put 2p a bottle on wine New fixed interest bond for pensioners introduced. A new Enterprise Investment Scheme will give income tax relief, at a 20 per cent rate, to those investing up to Pounds 100,000 a year in a qualifying unquoted trading company. MEASURES THAT WILL APPLY FROM APRIL Personal allowance frozen at Pounds 3,445 for those under 65, Pounds 4,200 for those aged 65-74 and Pounds 4,370 for people 75 and over. Relief on married couple's allowance restricted to 20 per cent.* The allowance is frozen at Pounds 1,720 but increased from Pounds 2,465 to Pounds 2,665 for those aged 65-74 and from Pounds 2,505 to Pounds 2,705 for those 75 and over. Blind person's allowance increased from Pounds 1,080 to Pounds 1,200. 20 per cent band increased from Pounds 2,500 to Pounds 3,000.* Higher rate threshold frozen at Pounds 23,700. Employees' National Insurance contributions increased from 9 to 10 per cent*. This will apply to weekly earnings of Pounds 57-Pounds 430 (compared with Pounds 56-Pounds 420 previously). Rate of Mortgage Interest Relief restricted to 20 per cent.* Capital gains tax and inheritance tax thresholds maintained at Pounds 5,800 and Pounds 150,000 respectively. Extra 50p a week for single pensioners and 70p a week for pensioner couples as compensation for VAT on fuel. Pensions scheme earnings cap up from Pounds 75,000 to Pounds 76,800. Permanent health insurance benefits will only be tax-free for the first 12 months, where entitlement starts after April 5. Tax relief on private medical policies for those aged 60 and over will be restricted to 25 per cent. MEASURES THAT APPLY FROM OCTOBER New 3 per cent tax on general insurance premiums. New departure tax comes into force. Pounds 5 per passenger on flights to the UK and European Union; Pounds 10 for flights elsewhere. New childcare allowance of up to Pounds 28 a week available to help those on family credit, who wish to find jobs. Cold weather payments up from Pounds 6 to Pounds 7 a week (in winter). MEASURES THAT APPLY FROM APRIL 1995 Rate of Mortgage Interest Relief restricted to 15 per cent. Relief on Married Couple's Allowance restricted to 15 per cent. Married couple's allowance for those aged between 65 and 74 increased from Pounds 2,665 to Pounds 2,995. The allowance for those aged 75 and over increased from Pounds 2,705 to Pounds 3,045. A single pensioner will receive a further 50p a week to help cope with VAT on fuel. Pensioner couples will get an extra 70p a week. Those on income benefit will get extra help. MEASURES THAT WILL APPLY FROM APRIL 1996 Unemployment benefit will be replaced by the Jobseeker's Allowance, which will last for six months rather than 12. A further 30p a week help for single pensioners with VAT on fuel. A pensioner couple gets 45p a week. Further rises also apply to those on income support MEASURES THAT APPLY FROM 2010 State retirement age for women up to 65, phased in over 10 years. *Announced in the March Budget by Norman Lamont.
