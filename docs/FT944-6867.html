941128 FT 28 NOV 94 / Lib Dems identify 'Pounds 2bn tax waste' The Liberal Democrats yesterday challenged Mr Kenneth Clarke, the chancellor, to recoup Pounds 2bn over two years by tackling waste and inefficiency. Mr Malcolm Bruce, the party's Treasury spokesman, said the savings would allow Mr Clarke to cancel the proposed rise in value added tax on domestic fuel to 17.5 per cent. Instead he could cut it from 8 per cent to 5 per cent. Mr Bruce identified 10 areas which could bring savings of Pounds 820m in the first year and Pounds 1.3bn in the second year.
