930407 FT 07 APR 93 / World News in Brief: Radioactive waste blast at Siberia arms plant A tank of radioactive waste exploded at a weapons plant in Siberia, contaminating an area of about 2,500 acres and exposing firefighters to dangerous levels of radiation. The accident, reported by Interfax news agency, may be the worst in the former Soviet Union since Chernobyl in 1986. Interfax said the wind was carrying radiation toward unpopulated areas, but it was not clear how many people might be affected.
