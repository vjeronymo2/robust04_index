BFN [Text] Dr. Jonas Malheiro Savimbi, chairman of the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA], reiterated in the city of Huambo yesterday that his party is committed to searching for a longlasting peace in Angola. The UNITA chairman met with UN Special Representative Alioune Blondin Beye. However, the contents of the meeting are not known. At the end of the meeting, which lasted more than four hours, the UN official addressed the Huambo residents who welcomed him joyously. Our correspondent Lourenco Bento reports from Huambo: His excellency Dr. Jonas Malheiro Savimbi, chairman of UNITA and supreme commander of the Armed Forces for the Liberation of Angola, has reiterated his party's wish to continue negotiating for peace. Addressing thousands of Huambo residents on the afternoon of 18 June at the end of his meeting with the special representative of the UN secretary general, Dr. Jonas Malheiro Savimbi said a true peace representing freedom and dignity was necessary. In an allusion to what is delaying the advent of peace and reconciliation of Angolans, the leader of the largest democratic opposition party in Angola stated that whenever UNITA attempted to achieve peace, the Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola [MPLA] responded with war. After noting the need for unity and trust among the people, Dr. Savimbi addressed a message of faith and hope to Angolans, quote, the difficult days can never be eternal, there will be a way out, the day when we shall be happy will come, the time will come when the MPLA will accept that it cannot govern this country without UNITA, unquote. Meanwhile, the senior UNITA leader stressed the need for cooperation between his party and the MPLA for peace to prevail in Angola. Dr. Savimbi stressed that the MPLA must understand that for peace to prevail in the country, for us all to leave as free citizens, it is necessary that MPLA cooperate with UNITA, and no one can ignore this. In turn, the UN secretary general's envoy, who held a meeting with the UNITA leadership for more than four hours, addressed the Huambo residents after the meeting.
