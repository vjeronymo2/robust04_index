Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Feb 21 (AA) -- Serbian Deputy Foreign Minister Zivadin Jovanovic arrived in Ankara on Monday [21 February] to hold talks on the recent developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Jovanovic told at Ankara's Esenboga Airport that he will pave official contacts to increase the dialogues on the problem in Bosnia, adding he will also discuss peace and stability in the former Yugoslavia and the Balkans. [sentence as received] Jovanovic recalled that the two country foreign ministers earlier met in New York. Asked if his visit would contribute to the problem while Turkey's policy was supporting the Muslims, Jovanovic said `Turkey, being a Balkan and a European country, contributed a lot to the efforts.' Jovanovic said Serbian foreign minister sent a message to Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin last week.
