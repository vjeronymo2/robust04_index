Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 28 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and China on Monday agreed to make joint studies for environmental protection and undertake other schemes to beef up bilateral technical cooperation. An official at the Ministry of Science and Technology said the agreement was made at a meeting in Beijing between South Korean Science and Technology Minister Kim Si-chung and his Chinese counterpart, Song Jian, minister of the State Science and Technology Commission. Minister Kim was in Beijing as a member of the official suite of President Kim Yong-sam presently on a state visit to China. As an initial project of joint environmental programs, the two countries would soon inaugurate a joint Yellow Sea study team to work out ways to control Yellow Sea contamination. In the aeronautical area, South Korea and China would jointly develop knowhow on airplane design with a view to producing mid-sized airplanes in a joint scheme. Also at Monday's meeting, the two ministers decided to exchange 30 technicians between their countries this year instead of 15 and step up cooperation in the area of elementary science. They further agreed to conclude a Korea-China atomic energy agreement and an atomic energy safeguards memorandum at the second Korea-China joint Science and Technology Committee meeting slated for October this year in Seoul, the official said.
