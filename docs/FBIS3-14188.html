Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] A minister has revealed to SHIHAN that the defendants arrested after placing weapons and explosives in al-Qasr, al-Karak, have strong ties with one of the leaders of the Afghan revolution. The minister said security quarters found messages exchanged between the defendants and Golboddin Hekmatyar, leader of the militias opposing the government of [Afghan President Borhanoddin] Rabbani. In another development, confessions by those accused of the recent explosions in Amman, the Jordanian Afghans, have led to the arrest of elements affiliated with the Muslim Brotherhood, Suwaylih branch. It was discovered that one of the group leaders is affiliated with the Muslim Brotherhood. This leader confessed that one of the Muslim Brotherhood officials in charge in al-Baq'ah and Suwaylih was involved in the explosions. Confessions show this took place without the knowledge of the Muslim Brotherhood leadership, and that Saudi funds and Islamic relief organizations finance the extremist organizations, not only in Jordan, but also in Egypt and Algeria.
