March 15, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: BRYANT GUMBEL TALKS HIS HEAD OFF TO WALTERS

"I WOULD say that Bryant Gumbel is an emotional guy, and where possible we should capitalize on it, and where it hurts us, we should try to minimize it . . . and he's not the one who should be the heavyweight, and so we should make the heavyweight decisions, and make sure that Bryant stays in the background," says Bryant himself to Barbara Walters in an evaluation of -- Bryant Gumbel!

You can see this exchange and a lot of other stuff this Friday night on ABC's "20/20" when Gumbel talks his head off to Walters. They address the infamous "Today" show memo, the one that caused Willard Scott's toupee to stand up all by itself.

They ramble over Bryant's alleged "arrogance" and how he gets along with his mom and dad and what his future hopes are and, of course, the Jane Pauley-Deborah Norville question. (A question, let's not forget, that the NBC peacock caused to be raised all by itself and created into a tangled web.)

BARBARA ASKS Bryant: "Did you want Jane Pauley to stay?" He says, "I wanted Jane Pauley to be happy in what she did." (I guess we might say that Bryant doesn't exactly answer Barbara's question.)

Barbara leads hims, saying maybe Jane would have been happy staying at the main desk and letting Deborah do the news. She remarks rather aggressively to Gumbel: "You could have made it happen." He demurs: "I'm not sure I could have made it happen, Barbara, I mean, you know, hindsight's perfect." Gumbel asserts that if he'd interfered, it might have been interpeted as trying to stunt Norville's career. Then he says "No" three separate times to the question of whether there is any truth to the rumor that NBC is thinking of putting Jane back on the "Today" show.

Barbara asks Bryant: "If they could bring Jane Pauley back, if she would come back . . . would you like that? Would it help?" Gumbel again steps over the query and counters with another one: "To take my place or Deborah's?" They spar a moment, then he says: "Oh, I don't know. I think it's always difficult to rewrite history, Barbara. I think the idea of bringing Jane back right now, while certainly a pleasant one for a lot of people to consider, would be fraught with so many difficulties involved, and so many hurt feelings, and so much discomfort for all included, including Jane, that I think it's kind of an unrealistic thing to even consider."

You'll have to tune in for the rest. As it is, I more or less "stole" this portion and am breaking a release date.

PEOPLE ARE talking about Jane Pauley's new persona these days. This girl is literally walking on air. Nobody has ever seen her quite like this before. Nobody has ever known a Jane Pauley who went about popping out her chest and busting her buttons with pride and joy. So if you are a Jane Pauley fan, that should make you feel good for Jane.

And maybe our darling old peacock, NBC, will decide that they've made an inadvertent star out of Jane and give her all those night-time slots I told you the other day might not happen. It would be nice to be wrong here.

BOB HOPE is rounding up the Sports Illustrated swimsuit lassies for his NBC special on April 9. And he'll feature a special bathing beauty -- Phyllis Diller. Someone asked Bob if he likes bathing beauties? Hope said, "I don't know. I've never bathed one."

BABY LOVE: Muscleman Lou Ferrigno, who IS "The Incredible Hulk," and his wife, Carla, named their new son, Brent. He's their third child. . . . Lawrence Olivier's son, Richard, not only is producing "Abra-Cadaver" at the Wyndham Theater in London, but is handing out cigars for his and wife Shelley's new daughter.
