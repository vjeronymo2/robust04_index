941031 FT 31 OCT 94 / Business Travel: Calls from the air KLM, the Dutch airline, is installing telephones in every business-class seat of its long-haul Boeing 747s, and on wall-mounted units in tourist class. The airline will use the Inmarsat satellite network. This permits almost unrestricted calls, as opposed to other carriers which use aircraft-to-ground systems, where calls are possible only within range of a ground station. Calls will cost Dollars 9 a minute, with a connection fee of Dollars 5.
