Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Shanayev] [Text] Vladikazkaz, 12 Jan -- "The Federation Council, consisting mainly of the leaders of executive organs of power in Russia's republics, krays, and oblasts, must learn from the sad experience of the working of the former Russian Congress of People's Deputies and prevent confrontation with the president's staff and the government," an ITAR-TASS correspondent was told today by Akhsarbek Galazov, head of the Republic of North Ossetia, on his return from the first sitting in Moscow of Russia's new parliament. Galazov thinks that the Federation Council will accomplish its historic role if it resolves the artificially created problems associated with the confrontation of the regions. "It is the republics that form the basis for Russian federalism and reducing them to the level of the other components of Russia may lead to an intensification of separatist sentiment and the disintegration of the Russian Federation," the head of North Ossetia declared.
