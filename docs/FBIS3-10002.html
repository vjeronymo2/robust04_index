Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Four Serbian aircraft have been shot down by U.S. F-16 fighters today near Banja Luka in northwestern Bosnia, in keeping with a UN Security Council resolution that forbids any and all flights in the skies over Bosnia, according to a NATO military spokesman. This is the first time NATO planes have opened fire in Bosnia, after the Serb planes ignored the F-16s' orders to land. NATO planes have been patrolling Bosnia daily since April 1993, with a view to enforcing respect of UN Security Council Resolution No. 816, which forbids any and all flights in Bosnian airspace, according to the spokesman. Meanwhile, a spokesman for the UN High Commissioner for Refugees [word indistinct] said all humanitarian flights to Sarajevo have been suspended, as has all movement toward areas controlled by the Serbs.
