920410 FT 10 APR 92 / Banks to shed over 4,000 jobs THE TWO largest high street banks, Barclays and National Westminster, yesterday an-nounced large job losses but denied they had selected election day to make the news public to keep exposure to a minimum. National Westminster, the second-largest UK bank with 102,000 employees, is to shed 4,000 jobs this year, including 1,900 in its UK branch staff. All staff received letters telling them of the cuts. However, the bank has not yet decided who will lose their jobs. NatWest said actual redundancies might be well below the number of job losses. 'We shall be using natural wastage through resignations and retirements to keep the numbers down as far as possible,' the bank said. In a separate move Barclays, the largest UK bank, is to cut 450 jobs over the next two years because of the installation of new technology at Barclays Bank Trust, its subsidiary which handles taxation and investment advice, and executor and trustee work. Bifu, the bank employees' union, said the banks timed the announcement to coincide with election day, but both last night strongly denied the claim, saying the timing was purely coincidental. NatWest, which shed 6,400 jobs last year, said it had not planned to make the announcement public yesterday. 'We wrote a private letter to our staff telling them of job cuts in their region, following our original announcement at our annual results on February 25 that more job losses were on the way,' NatWest said. 'We chose April 9 a long time before John Major did,' said Barclays. 'We wanted our staff to have the chance of a full two years to cope with the reorganisation.'
