930601 FT 01 JUN 93 / Observer: Diplomatic push Yasushi Akashi, the Japanese diplomat in charge of UN operations in Cambodia, has been offered a neat solution to two of his problems, namely the reluctance of Japanese UN policemen to stay at their posts in the face of danger and the obsessive interest of the Japanese media in the country's first ground operations abroad since the second world war. One of Akashi's aides suggested the journalists should become policemen and the policemen journalists. 'Then,' said the aide to a polite laugh from Mr Akashi, 'we'd have a timid Japanese press corps and an aggressive police force.'
