920629 FT 29 JUN 92 / Afghan president named Professor Burhanuddin Rabbani, a 50-year-old Islamic scholar and guerrilla leader, was yesterday named president of Afghanistan, the fourth man to hold the post in 10 weeks, Reuter reports from Kabul. Mr Rabbani is the second president of the Islamic government of Afghanistan, established by rebel forces in April after the capture of Kabul. He took over from Sibghatullah Mojaddedi, who served two months under an agreement signed by resistance leaders in the Pakistani city of Peshawar before Kabul fell.
