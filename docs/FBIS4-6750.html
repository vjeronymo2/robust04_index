BFN [Text] Petaling Jaya, April 22 (OANA/BERNAMA) -- Malaysia Friday called on United Nations Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali to resign for doing nothing to resolve the conflict in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Prime Minister Dr. Mahathir Mohamed said he was disappointed because Butrus-Ghali did not take a firm stand in dealing with the Serb aggression in that strife-torn country. The UN should elect a secretary-general who is really an activist and brave, who could resolve the problems in Bosnia-Herzegovina, he told reporters in Subang, here. Mahathir said he believed the resignation of Butrus-Ghali would bring change towards settlement of the problem in the former Yugoslav republic. What is certain is he did not do anything to settle the conflict in Bosnia, he added. Dr. Mahathir expressed regret that Butrus-Ghali took the conflict in Bosnia lightly when it erupted more than two years ago. At first, Butrus-Ghali said the occurrence in Bosnia could be found anywhere... He said it was not the only one but there were 10 more in other places, he said. Butrus-Ghali's statement appeared to encourage the Serbs to continue their aggression against the Bosnian Muslims on the grounds that it was not something extraordinary as the same thing was happening elsewhere. Asked if Malaysia would play a more important role in the election of the UN secretary-general in future, Dr. Mahathir said Malaysia's influence was limited in that matter. In fact, he said, many countries were pressured by the big powers to elect their candidate to the post. Malaysia did not support Butrus-Ghali's election to the post in 1991, he added. Dr. Mahathir reiterated that the Bosnian Muslims should be provided with arms to defend themselves from Serb attacks. He believed the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina would be different if the reverse occurred where the people who are murdered are non-Muslims and the murderers were Muslims. If this is the situation, I believe action will be taken speedily, he said. Meanwhile Gerakan, a component party of the ruling National Front, expressed support for the call for Butrus-Ghali's resignation. He was biased and racialist in making important decisions, party president Dr. Lim Keng Yaik said. Dr. Lim, who is Minister of Primary Industries, said the UN was influenced by big powers like the United States and European countries and it was uninterested in settling problems in a country that did not share the same religious belief or in which they had no special interest.
