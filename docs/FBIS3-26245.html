Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 3 (XINHUA) -- The Association of Relations Across the Taiwan Straits (ARATS) of the Chinese Mainland this morning suggested continuing discussions with the Taiwan-based Straits Exchange Foundation (SEF) on the issue of Taiwan investment on the mainland. ARATS also expressed hope that there will be mutual visits between industrial and commercial circles and scientific and technological personnel on the two sides across the Taiwan Straits. Zhao Zhengyu and Li Yafei, deputy secretary-general of ARATS, held a group meeting with Shih Chi-ping, deputy secretary-general of SEF, this morning, a preparation for a meeting between major officials of the two organizations tomorrow. ARATS suggested continuing to discuss the issue of Taiwan businessmen's rights and interests and other related issues in the second quarter of this year, Li Yafei said at a press conference after the meeting. At the same time, the issue of unifying the scientific and technological terms and standardizing scales of products to improve exchanges regarding computers and other industries will be discussed, Li said. The venue will be Beijing or Taipei. ARATS also suggested that the two bodies start to discuss issues of strengthening co-development of and exchanges in energy and other resources in 1994. Both sides agreed that economic and scientific and technological exchanges should be advanced on the principle of an agreement reached between ARATS President Wang Daohan and SEF chairman Ku Chen-fu last year. Shih Chi-ping expressed full understanding of ARATS's suggestions, saying that SEF would respond to them soon. Two other deputy secretaries-general of ARATS and SEF also held a group meeting on issues concerning routine affairs this morning. Both sides reckoned that they should consolidate the achievements that have been made.
