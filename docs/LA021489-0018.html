February 14, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

POLYUNSATURATED: A WELCOME WORD ON LABELS

Question: I would appreciate it if you would clear up something that has been confusing me. If I read a label on a food package and it says that it contains no cholesterol, is that the same as saying that it's low in saturated fat?

Answer: A food item may not contain any cholesterol and still be high in saturated fat. For example, baked goods that list either coconut or palm oil, or a hydrogenated vegetable oil, may be high in saturated fat.

When you check food labels, try to select items that contain more polyunsaturated than saturated fat, because polyunsaturated fat lowers blood cholesterol. Vegetable oils high in polyunsaturated fat include corn, soybean, safflower and sunflower oil.

Q: I have seen situations where a person is awakened at night to take some medicine, and I could never understand this. I know that when I'm not feeling well, the most important thing I want to do is to sleep, which I think does a person more good than any medicine. Can you explain why someone should be waked to take medicine or is it just foolishness?

A: The reason medicine is taken at specific intervals is so that the proper amount can be kept in the bloodstream at all times. If there is too much, it may have an adverse effect on the patient; if there is too little, the medication may not do the job for which it was intended. Sometimes the exact intervals are not very important; when they are, however, the patient may have to be awakened.

Obviously, a little common sense never hurt anyone. I would not wake a patient to give him a sleeping pill so that he will be able to go back to sleep.

Q: I would like some information about the drugs used to treat depression, especially their side effects. If these drugs work the way they're supposed to, won't the person want to take them all the time? To me, this means addiction.

A: There are a number of medications that may be prescribed for depression and, compared with other drugs, they are rather safe. Moreover, these drugs are not addictive even if taken for prolonged periods of time, which may be necessary in some patients.

Antidepressants, like other drugs, do have side effects but these are rarely serious. For example, the medication may result in drowsiness or cause the patient's mouth to be dry. One way to avoid these effects is to take the medication close to bedtime. This way, most of the side effects, if any, will occur while the patient is in bed.

Q: I know that people can be allergic to drugs, but the only drug I ever hear mentioned as causing an allergy is penicillin. Why is penicillin singled out so much for causing allergies?

A: The reason you so often hear of penicillin being implicated in drug allergy reactions is that it accounts for 90% of all such reactions.
