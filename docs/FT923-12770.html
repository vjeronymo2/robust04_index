920717 FT 17 JUL 92 / Parliament and Politics: Scots MPs in protest over water A GROUP of Scottish Labour MPs staged a protest in the Commons yesterday over what they claimed were government plans to privatise the Scottish water industry. Led by Mr George Galloway (Glasgow Hillhead), the group blocked the removal of the Commons mace as the House was adjourned at 3pm for the three-month summer recess. Attempts were made by officials to order the press out of the gallery, but journalists refused to leave. The 18-minute demonstration ended after an assurance that Mr Tony Newton, Leader of the House, would meet the group to discuss their grievances. The other MPs involved were: Mr John McAllion (Dundee East), Mrs Irene Adams (Paisley North), Mr Mike Watson (Glasgow Central) and Mr Dennis Canavan (Falkirk W) - all members of the 'Scotland United' group which campaigns for devolution. The Serjeant-at-Arms, Sir Alan Urwick, hurried to the chamber and after lengthy negotiations the MPs left, promising the campaign for a Scottish parliament would continue.
