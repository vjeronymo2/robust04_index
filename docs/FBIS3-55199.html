Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow, January 5 (TASS) -- A group of indepedent members of the newly-elected Russian State Duma announced on Wednesday that they plan to form an inter-regional association of independent deputies. The group held a meeting in the parliamentary centre, where they approved a declaration about the association. The declaration says, "the association can and must became a true centre for the consolidation of all the State Duma's constructive forces". In the future the association intends to lobby for parliamentary faction status. The declaration also states that the independent deputies, "who were elected in one-seat constituencies and are not obligated to any of the political parties or movements, are personally responsible before their voters, the constitution, and Russia as a whole". The association promises to base its activity on "the principles of parliamentary freedom and responsibility, common sense, political realism and pragmatism, and respect for the views of other groups and factions".
