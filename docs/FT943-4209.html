940909 FT 09 SEP 94 / World Commodities Prices: Wool Fine Merino wool sold at prices rising 30 to 40 Australian cents a kg. each day auctions were held this week. This sector is favoured by fashion and very little stock remains in Australia to supplement new production. Recovery in most wool consuming countries is placing pressure on available supply for this and other high fashion and highly priced natural fibres and prices can already be two to three times their lowest point in the recession which can now be seen to have ended twelve to eighteen months ago. Other wools are selling at rising prices with this week's rise a particularly strong one. Other wools are selling at rising prices, with this week's rise a particularly strong one. The Australian market indicator (Eastern states) finished at 780cents a kg., from 728 a week ago. British wool also sold at prices 5% higher than at the previous sale.
