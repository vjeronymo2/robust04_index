Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, February 4 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- At the sitting of the General Council of the biggest opposition party in Mongolia the Mongolian National Democratic Party (MNDP) discussed were the issues of coordination of activity of the party central organizations, a report of the Control Commission of the MNDP. At the sitting the party leader D. Ganbold made a speech. At the party sitting made were some organizational changes in the MNDP steering council. Instead of J. Narmandaa, who was released from the duties of the member of the MNDP General Council, elected was T. Enhtubshin, chairman of the MNDP organization of Ulaanbaatar, informs the representative of the MNDP to the MONTSAME correspondent. The country's public was expecting that the MNDP would express its position on the case about involvement of some leaders of the parliament and the government in corruption, but this did not happen.
