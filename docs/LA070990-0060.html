July 9, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

WIFE TELLS OF APARTHEID FOE'S AFFAIR

The wife of mixed-race anti-apartheid cleric Allan Boesak said their marriage had ended because he was having an affair with a white journalist, newspapers reported Sunday.

"This time he has gone too far. . . . Our marriage is over," Dorothy Boesak told the Sunday Times.

Allan Boesak, 44, a friend of Nobel Prize winner Archbishop Desmond Tutu and the president of the Geneva-based World Alliance of Reformed Churches, has admitted to having an affair with television journalist Elna Botha, niece of former Home Affairs Minister Stoffel Botha, newspapers said Sunday.

The papers said Boesak and Botha, who is married to a television news anchor, were seen at a Cape Town hotel where they had spent four days together.
