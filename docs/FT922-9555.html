920507 FT 07 MAY 92 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Nikkei advances 3.3% on arbitrage buying Tokyo ARBITRAGE-related buying lifted the Nikkei average by 3.3 per cent, but volume remained subdued after the recent holidays, writes Emiko Terazono in Tokyo. The 225-issue average gained 575.27 to 17,878.66. The index opened at the day's low of 17,325.77, and hit the day's high of 17,929.65 in the afternoon. Volume picked up modestly to 280m shares from Friday's thin 230m. The market was closed on Monday and Tuesday. Trading was dominated by arbitrage-related activity ahead of today's option exercise day. The yen's firmness against the dollar and a higher bond market provided support. Light bargain hunting by foreigners and investment trusts was noted. Advances outscored declines by 893 to 125, with 107 issues unchanged. The Topix index of all first section stocks moved up 26.02 to 1,339.43. In London the ISE/Nikkei 50 index put on 2.11 to 1,086.31. Speculative issues were in the limelight. Teac, the electronics company, was the most active issue of the day, gaining Y10 to Y1,090. Some high-technology exporters have been promoted by brokers as beneficiaries of the US economic recovery. Canon, the precision instruments maker, advanced Y40 to Y1,440. Short-term buying also lifted capital goods manufacturers on reports of an increase in plant construction projects in south-east Asia. Chiyoda added Y40 at Y1,610 and JGC rose Y40 to Y1,970. Biotechnology issues were firm, with Morinaga Milk up Y8 to Y792 and Okamoto climbing Y30 to Y1,240. Toyo Trust & Banking was an exception in a generally firm banking sector, losing Y10 to Y890 on reports that it will cut dividend payments for the last fiscal year ended March due to an increase in bad loans to real estate concerns and non-bank financial companies. In Osaka, the OSE average improved 429.05 to 20,275.69 in volume of 18.8m shares. Roundup PACIFIC RIM markets were generally weaker, with Bangkok registering its steepest loss in a single day since the military coup in February 1991. HONG KONG declined on profit-taking. The Hang Seng index retreated 50.01 to 5,581.20 in turnover of HKDollars 3.8bn. Cheung Kong fell 50 cents to HKDollars 24.20 and Hutchison Whampoa slipped 30 cents to HKDollars 18.10. HSBC Holdings was HKDollars 1.25 firmer at HKDollars 43.50. BANGKOK fell sharply as the political crisis increased. Yesterday anti-government demonstrators surrounded the parliament building and called for the resignation of Prime Minister Suchinda Kraprayoon. The SET index dropped 43.36, or 5.7 per cent, to 717.62 in turnover of Bt3.34bn. Bangkok Land receded Bt14 to Bt140. SEOUL weakened, with most activity coming in small-capitalisation issues. The composite index fell 8.48 to 611.85 in turnover of Won351.8bn. TAIWAN lost ground after the central bank announced that it was tightening money supply. The weighted index relinquished 54.28 to 4,482.25 in TDollars 17.67bn turnover (TDollars 16.8bn). MANILA's composite index slipped 35.78 to 1,209.74 in combined turnover of 240m pesos. AUSTRALIA gave an unenthusiastic response to the one percentage point cut in interest rates. The All Ordinaries index edged forward 3.3 to 1,668.9 in turnover of ADollars 261.18m. NEW ZEALAND's NZSE-40 index put on 2.97 to 1,473.09 in turnover of NZDollars 15m. Telecom gained 3 cents to NZDollars 2.01. BOMBAY fell on fears that a central bank enquiry would lead to tightening of credit for brokers. The BSE index lost 327, or 8.42 per cent, to 3,560.72. KUALA LUMPUR advanced on selective buying of major stocks. The composite index gained 1.44 to 604.46.
