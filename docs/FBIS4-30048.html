BFN [Text] Seoul, June 23 (YONHAP) -- U.S. President Bill Clinton phoned South Korean President Kim Yong-sam Thursday morning and discussed concerted measures on the North Korean nuclear dispute. Clinton called Kim at 7:57 AM and explained the background behind the U.S. decision to agree to resume a third round of high-level talks with North Korea. Kim, for his part, explained the progress of Seoul's preparations for the proposed inter-Korean summit meeting. The two leaders exchanged views on steps to take with regard to the North Korean nuclear issue.
