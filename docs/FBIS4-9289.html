BFN [Text] Belgrade, 22 Apr (TANJUG) -- The president of the Bosnian Serb republic, Radovan Karadzic, and the UN special envoy, Yasushi Akashi, and his aides reached an agreement late this evening, after almost 10 hours of talks, on an absolute cease-fire in the Gorazde area, which is to come into force at 1000 GMT tomorrow. It was also agreed that the Serbian forces would withdraw heavy weapons from Gorazde to a distance from which, as Yasushi Akashi said this evening, it would not be possible to hit the town. An agreement on the completely free movement of humanitarian convoys and the evacuation of the wounded from Gorazde was also reached. It has been announced that the talks would resume at 0700 GMT tomorrow, with the same people attending.
