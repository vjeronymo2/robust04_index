BFN [Text] Cairo, 9 Apr (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa said today that he had telephoned OAU Secretary General Dr. Salim Ahmed Salim. Musa added that he had informed the OAU secretary general of the views of President Husni Mubarak, the OAU chairman, on the current tragedy in Rwanda and what the OAU ought to do to contain this tragedy. The foreign minister said agreement was reached with the OAU secretary general to stay in touch while following the developments in Rwanda in order to put an end to the conflict. He added that Salim Ahmed Salim told him that immediately after he returns from Tanzania the [OAU] body in charge of resolving African disputes will hold an urgent meeting at the ambassadorial level at OAU headquarters in Addis Ababa to follow up the situation in Rwanda. On the situation in Yemen, Musa said that Information Minister Safwat al-Sharif left for Yemen today as the envoy of President Husni Mubarak, along with Rashid Bin-'Abdallah al-Na'imi, the United Arab Emirates foreign minister, as envoy of UAE President Shaykh Zayid Bin-Sultan, on a mission to follow up on the situation there and meet President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, his deputy 'Ali Salim al-Bid, and other leaders in an effort to take the necessary measures to preserve Yemeni stability and unity. Musa added that he had held talks with the UAE foreign minister yesterday evening, that President Mubarak received the UAE minister today, and that all aspects of the issue and possible measures were discussed. On his meeting today with the British Ambassador in Cairo, Christopher Long, Musa said several issues were discussed. He added that the ambassador conveyed a message from British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd and an official invitation for him to visit Britain. The foreign minister said they also discussed the Middle East peace process and developments in the Libyan-Western crisis.
