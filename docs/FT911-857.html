910509 FT 09 MAY 91 / Observer: Swaps knock Michael Heseltine's decision to sit on his hands over the local authority swaps affair is yet another disaster for the UK banking lobby. The trade groups representing UK banking interests already had an impressive record of failure in their Whitehall battles. The latest setback - which will cost the banks Pounds 550m - came despite two official visits to the Treasury by Sir Jeremy Morse, chairman of the British Bankers Association, and a lobbying campaign by Sir Martin Jacomb, chairman of the British Merchant Bank and Securities Houses Association. Even Robin Leigh-Pemberton, governor of the Bank of England, weighed in. All argued that the affair was harming the City and needed to be righted by retrospective legislation. But to no avail. It puts a heavy onus on Sir Nicholas Goodison, the new BBA chairman, to strengthen the bank lobby's firepower. Early signs suggest he's wasting no time in rallying his forces and will play a more up-front role. The Bank of England also might come to regret the way its ineffectiveness in the affair has damaged its image among its flock, particularly the foreign bankers who turned to it for help. As one said: 'The BBA described this as regrettable. Personally, I'd have used the word disgusting.'
