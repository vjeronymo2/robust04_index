BFN [Text] On 5 April, Acting Prime Minister Yukhym Zvyahilskyy received U.S. Ambassador to Ukraine William Miller. In the course of a very frank conversation, the ambassador spoke about his impressions after visiting the Crimea and meeting with Crimean leaders. He pointed out that the United States regards the Crimea as an integral part of Ukraine and sees the resolution of the peninsula's problems in the economic sphere. The ambassador underlined that assistance granted by the United States to Ukraine for the implementation of joint projects, is also meant for businesses in the Crimea. The sides also discussed the issue of assisting Ukraine in resettling Crimean Tatar emigrants and resolving problems related to the Chernobyl catastrophe. A wide range of issues concerning agricultural cooperation were also tackled. The meeting was attended by Yuriy Karasyk, Ukrainian minister of agriculture and food.
