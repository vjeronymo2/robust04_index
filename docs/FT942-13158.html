940426 FT 26 APR 94 / World Stock Markets (Mexico): US provides clues for Latin America Mexican shares closed lower on reports that another businessman had been kidnapped. The IPC index finished down 18.23 points at 2,191.08. Earlier the market had risen to 2,289.60 after Friday's strength, buouyed by the peso's firmness and expectations of easier short-term interest rate and good first quarter results from Telefonos de Mexico. Mexican stocks registered their biggest advance in more than three years last Friday after the Banco de Mexico moved decisively to protect the peso and quell speculation. On Friday, the 36-share IPC closed 133.99, or 6.5 per cent higher at 2,209.31, its biggest single-day advance in percentage terms since 17 January 1991.
