Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 15 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and the United States on Tuesday reaffirmed that they will hold the joint military exercise "Team Spirit" this year if IAEA inspections of North Korean nuclear facilities are deemed unsatisfactory and an inter-Korean envoy exchange fails to take place. The reaffirmation came at a meeting between South Korean Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae and U.S. Deputy Secretary of Defense Frank Wisner in Seoul on Tuesday. The two officials also discussed security on the Korean peninsula and strengthening bilateral security cooperation, officials said. Wisner told Yi that the Korean peninsula is vital to U.S. strategy while the two officials agreed that the security arrangement between the United States and South Korea remains firm, the officials said. The meeting was attended by other military officials from both sides. Wisner, visiting South Korea for the first time, is scheduled to visit the U.S. Second Infantry Division, an underground tunnel dug by North Koreans in the Demilitarized Zone, South Korea's First Infantry Divison and the Joint Security Area of the truce village of Panmunjom before departing on Thursday.
