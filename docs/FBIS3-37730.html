Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Anatoliy Zlenko, Ukraine's minister of foreign affairs, arrived in Bratislava on an official visit today. A special correspondent reports that in the course of the day the head of our state's foreign policy will hold talks with Slovakia's leaders. He is to meet Michal Kovac, president of the Slovak Republic, Ivan Gasparovic, chairman of the National Council, and Jozef Moravcik, minister of foreign affairs. It is envisaged that the sides will exchange opinions with regard to the situation in Yugoslavia, analyze the state of economic and border cooperation between the two states, and express their positions concerning Ukraine's entry into European structures and its role in implementing the partnership for peace program. Anatoliy Zlenko will also take part in the official opening ceremony of Ukraine's embassy in Bratislava.
