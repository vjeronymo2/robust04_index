920822 FT 22 AUG 92 / Repossession order led to arson A MAN set fire to his home the day before it was to be repossessed by Citibank, Oxford Crown Court heard yesterday. Mr Darren Kelly and his two brothers wanted to sell the house for Pounds 63,000 to clear their debts and had found a buyer, the court was told. But it was claimed that Citibank refused to sanction the deal because it was still owed another Pounds 3,000. Judge Patrick Medd told Mr Kelly, of Littlemore, Oxford, who admitted arson: 'The position you were put in when Citibank refused to accept Pounds 63,000 I can understand. It must have been frustrating and infuriating to a degree.' Mr Kelly was given a 12-month prison sentence suspended for two years. Citibank, which was not called to give evidence, said later: 'We feel we should clarify that we have no record of any formal offer or interest from potential purchasers in Mr Kerry's property.' It added: 'We believe we acted responsibly and the customer was fully informed of our intended actions at every stage.'
