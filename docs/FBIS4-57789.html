BFN [Editorial Report] Managua BARRICADA in Spanish published a report on 17 May 94 pp 1, 7 that says the economic cabinet on 16 May formally asked the diplomatic representatives of donor countries for $180 million in additional aid for the next three years. Presidency Minister Antonio Lacayo said the additional funds would result in higher economic growth rates. In the meeting, the government presented to the community of donors a report on progress made toward the democratization and macroeconomic stabilization process. It highlighted the demobilization of the 3-80 Northern Front, the constitutional reform process, and the conclusion of the Enhanced Structural Adjustment Facility, ESAF, negotiations. Lacayo said Nicaragua is requesting additional resources, not in donations but in credit lines in the medium and long term to help the country's financial system.
