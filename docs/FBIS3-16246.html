Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, March 15 -- Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak said starting his visit to Moscow on Monday that it was high time that Poland and Russia gave up mutual declarations and stopped talking about assistance but proceeded to concrete steps beneficial to both countries. Pawlak said he would like to translate the language of treaties and accords into practical actions. He said he meant primarily long-term agreements on strategic raw materials and steps to build a gas pipeline from Russia to Western Europe, a railway line and a highway linking Berlin, Warsaw and Moscow.
