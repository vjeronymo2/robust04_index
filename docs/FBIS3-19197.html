Language: German Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Austrian state television reported the following results for the three provincial diet elections held on Sunday, 13 March 1994. In Carinthia, the Social Democratic Party of Austria (SPOe) led with 14 seats based on 37.4 percent of the vote, down 8.7 percent from the previous election. The Freedom Party of Austria (FPOe) won 13 seats with 33.4 percent of the vote, a gain of just under 5 percent. The Austrian People's Party (OeVP) obtained nine seats with 23.7 percent of the vote, a gain of 2.5 percent. The Greens and the Liberal Forum finished with 1.6 and 2.5 percent of the vote respectively but did not win any mandates. In Salzburg, the OeVP captured 13 seats based on 38.6 percent of the vote received, a decline of 5.4 percent from the previous election. The SPOe won 11 seats with 27.1 percent of the vote, down 4.1 percent. The FPOe finished with eight seats based on 19.5 percent of the vote, up 3.1 percent. The Citizen's List won three seats with 7.2 percent of the vote, a gain of 1.1 percent. The Liberal Forum received 5.7 percent of the vote, but has no seats in the diet. The OeVP led again in Tyrol with 19 seats based on 47.3 percent of the vote, a decline of 1.4 percent from the previous election. The SPOe finished with seven seats based on 19.9 percent of the vote, a decline of 2.9 percent. The FPOe won 6 seats with 16.2 percent of the vote, a gain of .6 percent. The Greens won four seats with 10.6 percent of the vote, a gain of 2.3 percent. The Liberal Forum obtained 3.3 percent of the vote and did not win any seats.
