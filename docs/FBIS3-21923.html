Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Monica Martin] [Excerpts] Almost all the heroin produced illicitly in Mexico is smuggled into the United States. During 1992, the Mexican alkaloid accounted for approximately 23 percent of the drugs circulating on the U.S. black market: a proportion lower than 33 percent, the number reported in 1988. This announcement was made yesterday by Manuel Quijano, vice chairman of the UN's Internal Narcotics Inspection Board, upon submitting that agency's annual report. At a press conference, he added that, during the past three years, a control program in Mexico also has reduced the illegal opium poppy crops to their lowest index in 10 years. During 1992, the Mexican authorities reported the eradication of 11,583 hectares of opium poppy plants. According to reports, opium production declined from seven tons in 1989 to four tons in 1992. [passage omitted] The Mexican authorities seized 38.8 tons of cocaine in 1992. It is estimated that 60 percent of the alkaloid entering the United States from Colombia passes through Mexico.
