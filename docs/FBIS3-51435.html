Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Article by special correspondents] [Text] Colonia, Uruguay -- The four presidents of the Mercosur [Common Market of the South] countries have reaffirmed the principles, goals, and timetables established in the Asuncion Treaty to move ahead in a coordinated, balanced, and sustained manner toward the establishment of a customs union and setting the basis for structuring Mercosur in a joint communique released at the conclusion of the presidential summit. They also noted that the coordination of macroeconomic policies is an essential condition for the sucess of the integration process. They pointed out that the fulfillment of the timetable established in Las Lenas [Argentina], as well as the instrumentation of the decision of the Common Market Council on the common external tariff, measures, and instruments for structuring Mercosur, are a firm commitment of the four countries. They also agreed that the progress on the different priority areas must be made symmetrically and simultaneously. Recognizing that the main challenge is to promote the prosperity of their people and to reach the highest standard of living, the presidents expressed their special satisfaction in face of the substantial trade increase within Mercosur which went from $3.6 billion in 1990 -- before the Asuncion Treaty was signed -- to more than $8 billion in 1993. This accelerated trade growth is the result, among other reasons, of the fulfillment of the commercial liberation program which currently has a tariff preference of 82 percent between the states. They also expressed their satisfaction over the technical progress reached on the common external tariff, and asserted their determination to conclude the definition of the common external tariff on 30 June 1994 as the basic instrument for structuring the customs union which is a significant stage for establishing the common market. They also emphasized the importance of the special meeting to be held this year to establish the institutional structure of Mercosur. The meeting will serve to create the permanent institutions that will start operating on 1 January 1995. They also recognized the importance of the current and future works of infrastructure which are the basis of physical integration that also includes the water way. In the joint communique the four presidents also recognized the importance of strengthening the Latin American Integration Association. They pointed out that it is essential to maintain the integrity of the 1980 Montevideo Treaty which made important projects possible. They also expressed their safisfaction over the presence of Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada and reiterated their pleasure at the Bolivian Government's willingness to strengthen its links with Mercosur. They also noted the institutional cooperation provided by the EC with Mercosur as well as the cooperation given by the Inter-American Development Bank.
