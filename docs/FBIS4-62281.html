BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature: compiled by Nikolay Zherebtsov, Andrey Petrovskiy, edited by Vladimir Shishlin] [Text] The conciliatory commission for implementation of the civil accord treaty will possibly be chaired by First Vice-Premier of the Russian Federation Oleg Soskovets, head of the Russian presidential administration Sergey Filatov told IF's and RIA's correspondents on Thursday. Filatov believes that Soskovets is a preferable candidate since this post requires a personality [word indistinct] the situation in the country well, especially in the economics. Filatov thinks that it is so important, because the working groups implementing the civil accord treaty will focus in the first place on economic and social problems. Filatov said that several "round-table" meetings will be organized on the issues embraced by the treaty: political stability, the overcoming of the socio-economic crisis, the federative structure, national security, science and culture. These round-table meetings, Filatov said, will become kind of working centers and the first stage of conciliatory procedures, in the course of which concrete documents will be prepared. "The conciliatory commission is designed to control the activities of "the round-tables" and to solve controversial issues which failed to be resolved at a lower level," Filatov said. He noted that direct connection will be established between the commission and the president who "will take final decisions and give orders to specific organizations." Filatov said: "There is a plan of legislative works which will be published soon. A group is already working in the administration: it prepares draft agendas for the round-tables, in particular, a document is under preparation regarding the concept of a new tax policy." Filatov suggested that the conciliatory commission which will number 27 members will start working at the end of May.
