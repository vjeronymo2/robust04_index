BFN [Text] Guatemala City, 31 May (NOTIMEX) -- Unidentified individuals tonight detonated two bombs in different places in Guatemala City, according to the volunteer fire department. The fire department chief, Emilio Najera, told NOTIMEX that one of the bombs exploded in front of the home of the brother of Monsignor Rodolfo Quezada Toruno, bishop of Zacapa and the former mediator in negotiations between the government and the guerrillas. The second bomb went off at Climat, a store located in the exclusive Zone 9 of this capital. Firemen did not specify the type of explosives used in the attacks, both of which caused considerable damage but no casualties. The attacks were carried out on the Day of Constitutionality, just 24 hours before the peace talks between the government and the Guatemalan National Revolutionary Unity (URNG) are due to resume in Mexico.
