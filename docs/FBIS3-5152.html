Language: Burmese Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The Central Bank of Myanmar [Burma] in Yangon [Rangoon] today announced the issuance of new bank notes bearing the king lion motif. The text of the announcement reads: To assist the economic reforms being undertaken in accordance with the state's market economic system and to facilitate legal tender for the public, the Central Bank of Burma is to issue a highly secured and international standard new series of Kyat 500, 200, 50, 20 bank notes and 50 Pyas bank note, bearing the king lion motif, on 27 March 1994. [passage omitted on description of notes] [Hong Kong AFP in English at 1245 GMT on 3 Mar, in a similar report adds: "The currency announcement came amid widespread rumours that the 100 kyat bank-note, introduced in 1990 with the 90 kyat note, were to be replaced. Introduction of the new bank-notes will have no direct impact on inflation as they would replace old notes as they were destroyed, a senior bank official said. "The official put the volume of currency in circulation at about 52 billion kyats as of March 31, 1993, with an inflation rate of 31.6 percent. "In the meantime the volume of foreign exchange certificates (FEC) introduced a year ago to help promote tourism, had reached 15 million dollars, with an estimated 30 percent in the hands of the public, Win Tin said. While declining to give specific figures of Burma's present foreign exchange reserves, he said it was `very healthy' and that there was no cause for concern."]
