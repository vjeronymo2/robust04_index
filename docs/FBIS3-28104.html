Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 3 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa on Thursday [3 February] announced a 6 trillion yen tax reduction plan that includes 5.3 trillion yen in income and resident tax cuts. Hosokawa, announcing the decision at a hastily arranged news conference, said the coalition government will propose abolishing the 3 percent consumption tax but will instead call for imposing a 7 percent national welfare tax. The prime minister said the government plans to make the tax cuts retroactive to January 1, 1994, and to impose the new national welfare tax April 1, 1997. Hosokawa said he has gained agreement at a meeting of top officials of the government and ruling parties.
