920404 FT 04 APR 92 / Standard on currency criticised SUPPORT is widespread for the overhaul of accounting standard SSAP20 dealing with foreign currency translation, according to a survey conducted by the Chartered Association of Certified Accountants. In a study of 32 companies, institutions, accounting firms and individuals conducted for the Accounting Standards Board, the association found that most organisations wanted disclosure in the accounts of any limitations on the extent to which overseas assets can be remitted to the UK. That has been prompted by the experience of Polly Peck International. Many of those surveyed wanted clarification of reserve accounting, the treatment of complex capital instruments, and hedging transactions.
