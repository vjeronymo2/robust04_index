BFN [Text] Lebanese Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz arrived in Amman yesterday evening to take part in the 17th ministerial session of the ESCWA [Economic and Social Commission for Western Asia], which opened its meetings in Amman yesterday. Upon arrival, the Lebanese minister expressed happiness over his visit to the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan. He said the Lebanese people cherish and appreciate the Jordanian people and that His Majesty King Husayn occupies a special place in the hearts of all the Lebanese. He added that during his visit he will take part in the ESCWA's ministerial session, especially since Lebanon is one of the countries nominated to serve as the headquarters for this commission. On the peace process, the Lebanese minister said that after more than three years since the launching of the peace process and the Madrid conference, this process has not achieved the hopes that had been pinned on it. He asserted that the two cosponsors of the Madrid conference should continue to play an active role in line with the requirements of the Madrid process through the resolutions of international legitimacy and to play the role of a fair broker. The Lebanese minister noted that he will meet with several Jordanian officials to discuss Arab developments of interest to both countries.
