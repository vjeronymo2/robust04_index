Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Paris, Jan. 19, IRNA -- Iran's Embassy here Tuesday denied allegations on having sent an explosive letter to an Iranian residing in Sweden and termed it "banal propaganda". In a statement the embassy said the allegations are mere lies fabricated by counterrevolutionary terrorist groups. The embassy also said that futility of the allegations will become further clear to all when it is asked how it could be possible that the sender of such a letter writes down his address on the back of the envelope? The statement stressed that it was time for the French public opinion to deliberate on the presence of terrorist groups who have repeatedly committed terrorist acts in France and abroad. AGENCE FRANCE PRESSE (AFP) in a report from Stockholm Monday quoted the city police sources that a 45-year-old Swedish of Iranian origin was injured the same day due to the explosion of a letter sent to his wife. According to the AFP report police sources declined to identify the injured, introduced as an opponent of the Islamic Republic of Iran. It also added that they did not have any idea about the point of departure and exact type of the explosive. However the same sources claimed the letter it was sent from the Iranian Embassy in Paris.
