930810 FT 10 AUG 93 / Arts: Today's Television The four-part series on Genghis Khan and the Mongol empire, Storm From The East which begins today (9.45) was made after Japan's main broadcaster, NHK, approached the BBC. The Japanese and the British, we are told, found that there were 'significant differences in emphasis' in what they wanted to say. BBC producer Robert Marshall claims 'The Japanese find it very difficult to be critical about anyone, even Genghis Khan'. So the two scripts differed: 'The BBC's goes into detail on the conquests and massive slaughter involved while the Japanese concentrate more on anthropology and the countryside'. The mind boggles. The shift towards real-life crime as entertainment continues rapidly. Inside Crime is yet another series which promises to combine analysis with 'reconst ructions'. Today's opening episode (7.30 ITV) will reconstruct the mugging of Frederick Carter who died a week after being attacked in a lift. Won't that be nice. In Far Flung Floyd the leader of the gastronauts moves on to West Malaysia (8.30 BBC2).
