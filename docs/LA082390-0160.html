August 23, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

MALCOLM X DEATH SITE TO BECOME A MUSEUM

The site of the assassination of Malcolm X will be preserved as a museum and memorial to the black leader. Under an agreement announced Wednesday, Columbia University will also use half of the building as a biotechnology center.

The university had wanted to tear down the Audubon Ballroom in Washington Heights, where Malcolm X was slain 25 years ago, but agreed to the compromise under pressure from a citywide coalition of black activists.
