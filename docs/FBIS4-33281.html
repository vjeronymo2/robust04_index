BFN [Text] Ricardo Alarcon de Quesada, president of the National Assembly of the People's Government [ANPP], is attending the fourth special session of the provincial People's Government in the City of Bayamo. The main goal of the meeting is to discuss and approve the progress report which Granma Province will render to the ANPP session in July. It was ratified at the meeting that the document will be enriched by the people in meetings to be held in every district in the province. Granma's progress report includes a broad overview of the work conducted between 1985 and 1989, as well as the intense and productive effort of the entire population between 1990 and 1994, years within the special period marked by a great spirit of sacrifice and commitment to solve the problems created by acute material shortages. Granma's delegates and deputies stressed that the food program is in need of joint efforts, to increase the seed banks, the organic planters, and to use the scientific potential to quickly satisfy the people's food needs in a controlled and intelligent manner. The fourth special session of Granma's government is also being attended by Commander of the Revolution Guillermo Garcia Frias; Adolfo Diaz, vice president of the Council of Ministers; and Alfredo Enriquez Pina, first secretary of the Communist Party of Cuba [PCC] in Granma. The session also discussed the proposed shield of the province in which the principal and most significant elements of the glorious historic and revolutionary endeavors of the province are represented.
