Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Yermakova] [Text] Moscow January 4 TASS -- The Central Electoral Commission is presently making arrangements for the upcoming move to the White House government building, the former parliamentary residence. Although the election campaign is over, the Central Electoral Commission is going ahead with its work, election officials stressed at their meeting on Tuesday. The Commission is now preparing a report on the election financing and the use of funds by candidates and election blocs. Arrangements are being made for elections in Russia's six constituencies where the December 12 poll was declared invalid. But the main task to be tackled by the Central Electoral Commission is related to the presidential decree on measures to improve Russia's electoral system, Commission Chairman Nikolay Ryabov stressed in an interview with TASS. This document providing for transformation of the commission for the 1993 elections to the Federal Assembly into a standing Central Electoral Commission of the Russian Federation "does not allow us to relax", Ryabov noted. While drafting a law on elections in coordination with the Justice Ministry, the Central Electoral Commission will take into consideration all remarks made by individual candidates and parties. It will also head a system of commissions to be set up for holding elections tol power bodies of all levels in Russia's regions.
