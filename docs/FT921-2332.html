920319 FT 19 MAR 92 / Observer: Peer pressure Yet again, the recent exchange of letters in The Times has proved that when it comes to fielding heavy- weight teams of sympathetic businessmen, the Tories win hands down. In big-business experience, Labour peer Lord Hollick's team was no match for Sir Allen Sheppard's Conservative cheerleaders. Whereas FT-SE 100 company bigwigs (a Bank of England director among them) dominated the signatories of Sir Allen's letter, Hollick's list did not include the chief executive of a single Footsie company. Apart from the odd entrepreneur such as publisher Paul Hamlyn, seasoned industrialists seemed sparse by comparison with media and property types. The City was under-represented - although Charles Cavanagh is a senior Kleinwort Benson fund manager as well as a clergyman.
