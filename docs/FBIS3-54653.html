Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tajikistan's Parliament has lifted the curfew in Dushanbe, Kurgan-Tyube, Kulyab, and Kalininabad as of midnight January 1 at citizens request because the situation in the cities has returned to normal. The curfew and state of emergency were declared in the capital on January 6, 1993 and were imposed on Kurgan-tyube, Kulyab, and Kalininabad on March 30, 1993 to restore law and order, guarantee the citizens safety, confiscate weapons from illegal armed groups, curb pillage and other heinous crimes. The curfew was in effect from midnight till dawn. The state of emergency is still in force. As Nikolay Majar, a senior interior ministry official, has told Interfax, not later than January 6, 1994, the state of emergency must be either lifted or prolonged since the decree on it is valid only for up to one year.
