940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / Arts: Washington Opera appoints Domingo Placido Domingo has been appointed as the new artistic director of Washington Opera. Announcing the decision at a press conference yesterday, he said he planned to continue singing and conducting in the major opera houses of the world but at the same time maintain 'full control of all musical and artistic matters,' in Washington. He also plans to perform in one Washington production each year. In 14 years under the outgoing director Martin Feinstein, Washington Opera has grown from an interesting provincial company with a Dollars 2m annual budget to one of international stature with an Dollars 11.5m, selling more than 100,000 tickets each year. Mobil Corporation has agreed to sponsor the opera house for the next three years.
