BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the elections scheduled for 21 August. Ernesto Zedillo, presidential candidate for the Institutional Revolutionary Party, PRI, presents a 10-point program to combat poverty, pledges to continue to implement and decentralize the solidarity program, and calls upon all Mexicans to join in the fight against poverty. Cuauthemoc Cardenas, presidential candidate for the Party of the Democratic Revolution, PRD, finishes a three-day visit to Chihuahua State and is criticized by the National Action Party, PAN, for his negative comments on PAN Governor Francisco Barrios. PAN publicly accuses Cardenas of diverting funds while he was governor of Michoacan. National Opposition Union, UNO, candidate Pablo Emilio Maderos visits the National Autonomous University of Mexico where he pledges to fight unemployment and poverty. Jorge Gonzalez Torres, presidential candidate for the Mexican Green Ecologist Party, PVEM, visits Oaxaca State, where he says the 21 August elections will suffer because of the high level of poverty in Mexico. He calls for a peaceful solution to the Chiapas conflict because, he says, an armed conflict benefits no one. [Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 16 Jun 94] In Veracruz, Zedillo speaks to thousands of PRI members, saying the country's institutional life cannot be negotiated or transformed into the political gain of uncompromising minorities. He calls upon Mexicans to respect social peace, sovereignty, national unity, independence, and freedom. [Mexico City UNOMASUNO in Spanish 17 Jun 94 pp 1-5] Rafael Aguilar Talamantes, presidential candidate for the Cardenist Front for National Reconstruction, ends his visit to several Jalisco municipalities. Labor Party candidate Cecilia Soto holds a news conference in Reynosa, Tamaulipas State, during which she says that the United States should provide Mexico and Colombia with evidence of its will to reduce drug use, combat money laundering and arms smuggling, and prosecute drug lords in the United States. [Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 17 Jun 94] Cardenist Front candidate Aguilar Talamantes says Peace Commissioner Manuel Camacho Solis' resignation is good for the country and that his party and the Zapatist National Liberation Army, EZLN, are both fighting for the same improvements. [Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 20 Jun 94] During an interview, PRD candidate Cardenas pledges to improve Mexico's education system and says there is still time to make electoral reforms. [Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 21 Jun 94] PRI candidate Zedillo admits that problems in the electoral process have made would-be voters distrustful. He invites PRI businessmen and intellectuals to attend meetings on his behalf because such meetings would lend legitimacy to his probable victory. [Monterrey EL NORTE (Foreign Edition) in Spanish 21 Jun 94 p 1A] Zedillo campaigns in Villa Alvaro Obregon, a Mexico City low-income neighborhood where he reiterates his pledge to combat excessive poverty. In Mexico City, Labor Party candidate Soto calls for reforming the power of the president. [Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0140 GMT 22 Jun 94] A new group called the Citizen's Network has been created to promote peace during the elections. The PRD calls for an amendment to the Electoral Law, but the PRI disagrees. Zedillo says the people, not the government, own the solidarity program because the people pay for it. PAN presidential candidate Diego Fernandez de Cevallos denies that his campaign has slowed down, saying that he stopped visiting small communities only because such visits make him lose sight of the larger picture. Labor Party candidate Soto proposes a women's secretariat and claims that she is the victim of slander on the part of her opponents. [Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 22 Jun 94] The Federal Electoral Institute, IFE, reports that foreign visitors will be allowed to observe the coming August elections as a means of eliminating suspicions of fraud but the visitors will be banned from participating in political activities. IFE reports that the 48 million ballots required for the coming elections have been sent to the states. Zedillo holds a meeting at the Change 21 Foundation, where he says it is necessary to create a new federalism. In Toluca, Labor Party candidate Soto opposes the PRD's proposal to institute the death sentence. [Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 23 Jun 94]
