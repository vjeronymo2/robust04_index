Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The new Sabah chief minister to replace Datuk Joseph Pairin Kitingan is scheduled to be sworn in this afternoon. The national news agency BERNAMA said in Kota Kinabalu that it has received a fax from the Information Department inviting newsmen to cover the ceremony at 3.30 pm [0730 GMT] at the palace in the state capital. The department's press (?advisory) confirmed the widespread talks that Datuk Pairin would step down this afternoon. Sabah National Front chief Tan Sri Sakaran Dandai is set to become chief minister. Yesterday, the National Front, BN, staked its claim to form the new Sabah Government by petitioning the governor, Tun Mohamed Said Keruak, that it now commanded the majority in the State Legislative Assembly. There are altogether 30 state assemblymen, including those from the Sabah United Party, PBS, who defected to the National Front, filed the petition to those who respected the governor to ask Datuk Pairin to step down and offer the post to Tan Sri Sakaran.
