Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Focus on Africa" program] [Text] Malawi's preparations for the multiparty elections in May have got under way today with the beginning of voter registration and the start of three days of debate on the Constitution. For the last few months, the National Consultative Council, which has representatives from both the ruling Congress Party and the opposition, has been working on changes to the old one-party system and now a constitutional conference is being held in Blantyre, from where Willie Zinkhane reports: [Begin Zinkhane recording] The constitutional conference opened on a hot note today in Blantyre, where 250 delegates from all walks have the difficult task of (?commenting) on the draft Constitution drawn up by the National Consultative Council. In the afternoon, the presidents of seven contesting parties had 10-minute speeches to (?share) their expectations of the new Constitution. President Hastings Banda was represented by his deputy, Gwanda Chakuamba Phiri, who said the new Constitution should be a model that will work. From the speeches delivered this afternoon by the political party presidents, several critical points came out clearly: the new Constitution should reduce [word indistinct] the powers of the president, respect for human rights, independence of the judiciary, press freedom, land distribution, and the role of women and of the disabled. Traditional chiefs, who did not have the floor today, looked excited about the developments. One traditional chief said: we are happy that we [words indistinct] in the new Constitution, unlike during the one-party system, when everything was dictated to us. Meanwhile, the registration of voters has started in all the (?24) districts of Malawi. Reports from both rural and urban areas indicate that the turnout was good, but was mainly dominated by the youth, who rushed for the registration points. With the voting age coming down from 21 to 18, many boys and girls look at the whole exercise with special interest. [end recording]
