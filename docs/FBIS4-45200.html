CSO [Article by Rafael Medina Cruz] [Text] Upon arresting six presumed drug traffickers at the international airport in Guadalajara, Jalisco, and at the Oaxaca State capital airport, Federal Judicial Police [PJF] agents confiscated over 5 million pesos' worth of ephedrine, psychotropic pills, and white heroin. The latter was nearly 100 percent pure, and sufficient to prepare 18,000 doses. The representative of the Attorney General's Office [PGR] in Oaxaca, Manuel Figueroa Lange, announced that PJF personnel under orders from the deputy commissioner, Juan Jose Tafoya Gonzalez, arrested Erasmo Jesus Cruz Esperon, Jorge Efrain Garcia Cardoso, and Jose Antonio Varela Fuentes in the residence at Independencia and Garcia Vigil Streets, in the city of Oaxaca's first block. They had in their possession 260 grams of white or Thai heroin, considered by experts as one of the most toxic drugs on the narcotics market. According to the report submitted to the PJF's general director, Adrian Carrera Fuentes, the accused were closing a deal for the purchase and sale of the drug, which, based on laboratory tests, was estimated as sufficient for processing 18,000 doses. The authorities noted that the drug, of unknown origin, causes immediate addiction among those consuming it: hence the significance of the confiscation. It was specified that the heroin was distributed in five packets, to be sold both in our country and the United States. The investigations to determine the origin of the narcotic are being continued. For its part, the PGR branch office in Jalisco, headed by Raul Garza Serna, reported that Gabriel Rodriguez Cervantes and Jesus Herrera Lopez were captured at the Guadalajara international airport. They were carrying, in four suitcases, over 50.5 kg of ephedrine and several dozen psychotropic pills. The drugs were bound for Tijuana, Baja California. It was added that the aforementioned persons were captured after the investigative agents had discovered on the inspection conveyor belts that the four bags contained a white, crystalline powder resembling cocaine. It proved to be ephedrine, a chemical precursor used for processing amphetamines and cocaine base. At the same airport, during another search, Alejandro Ortiz Torres was arrested. He was carrying over 17 kg of compressed cocaine in a traveling bag, and was also bound for Tijuana.
