Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 20, IRNA -- Commander of the Iranian law enforcement forces Major General Reza Seifollahi here Sunday said that 20 drug traffickers were killed and 11 others were injured when a convoy of illicit drugs was smashed in Sistan-Baluchestan border province, in southeast of Iran, two days ago. The commander added that 103 drug traffickers were arrested and a total of 3,287 kg of opium was seized from them during the operation. He noted that the amount of narcotics seized in the current Iranian month of Bahman (Jan. 21-Feb. 19) showed an increase of 45 percent compared to the same period last year. Pointing to the severe clashes the law enforcement forces had with traffickers in border areas in the past eight months, the commander added that the forces had also seized 80,066 kg of narcotics in current year showing an increase of 15 tons as compared to the previous year. He further said that 14,612 traffickers and 43,464 addicts have also been arrested this year. Seifollahi pointed out that the law enforcement experts had made a device to be employed in eight provinces to identify and register the number of those addicted to hashish in the country. As for government policy to severe struggle against smugglers, General Seifollahi said that to this end the law enforcement forces had carried out several operations in Sistan-Baluchestan and the Persian Gulf coastal provinces of Bushehr and Hormozgan.
