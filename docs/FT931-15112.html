930114 FT 14 JAN 93 / International Company News: International Paper slips into the red after charge INTERNATIONAL Paper, the US paper group which has been expanding fast in Europe, reported a fourth-quarter net loss of Dollars 176m, after taking a Dollars 263m after-tax charge for a productivity improvement programme. The company said the results worked through at a loss of Dollars 1.46 a share and were struck on sales of Dollars 3.4bn. Without the charges, earnings totalled Dollars 87m, or 71 cents a share. In the fourth quarter of 1991, the company earned Dollars 107m, or 96 cents a share, before special charges and Dollars 66m, or 59 cents a share, after charges. Mr John Georges, chairman, said the company had achieved fourth-quarter sales comparable to the second and third quarters, in spite of difficult conditions in the US and European economies. Pulp and paper sales rose to Dollars 995m, from Dollars 970m, while paperboard and packaging improved to Dollars 895m from Dollars 870m and distribution to Dollars 735m from Dollars 685m. Specialty products were up to Dollars 545m from Dollars 485m, while timber and wood products were unchanged at Dollars 350m. For the full year, International Paper reported net earnings of Dollars 86m, or 71 cents a share, after special charges and Dollars 405m, or Dollars 3.34 before charges. Sales totalled Dollars 13.6bn, up from Dollars 12.7bn in 1991. Net earnings in 1991 totalled Dollars 184m, or Dollars 1.66 after special charges and Dollars 452m, or Dollars 4.09 before charges.
