March 10, 1989, Friday, P.M. Final

NATION;

SULLIVAN SWORN AS HEALTH SECRETARY

President Bush installed Louis W. Sullivan as secretary of health and human services today, describing his only black Cabinet member as "a man of energy, enthusiasm and intellect" with a "sense of mission."

Bush, accompanied by his wife, Barbara, led a swearing-in ceremony at the department that began with the Sullivans' youngest son, Halsted, a senior at the University of Virginia, singing "The Star-Spangled Banner."
