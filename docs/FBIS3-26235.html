Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 3 (XINHUA) -- Jing Shuping, chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, pledged today to bring into full play the role of non-governmental chambers of commerce in promoting the growth of the non-state-owned economic sector. The Federation, also known as the National Non-Governmental Chamber of Commerce, now has 670,000 members throughout the country and 2,299 local organizations at and above the county level. Speaking at a spring festival reception attended by Chinese and foreign guests, Jing Shuping said that his federation will try to promote economic, technological and trade cooperation between China and other countries. The Federation will also help expand cooperation between mainland dealers and manufacturers and their colleagues in Taiwan, Hong Kong, Macao and in foreign countries, he added. "In particular, we will help businesses within the non-state-owned sector make great progress in economic, technological and trade cooperation with their overseas counterparts," he told the reception. To date, the Federation has established cooperative ties with nearly 100 chambers of commerce overseas, he said. Jing extended thanks to foreign embassies in Beijing and commercial offices for their support of the Federation.
