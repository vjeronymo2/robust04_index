March 18, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

STATISTICALLY SPEAKING, BOGGS IS BETTER OFF SINGLE

If Wade Boggs, who has the fourth-highest batting average in major league baseball history at .354, hit .221 when his wife was with him and .341 when Margo Adams was around, he'd better stay away from both of them.

They're killing his lifetime batting average.

TOM BOKARAE

Huntington Beach
