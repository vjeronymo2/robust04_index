Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "EC: Commission Considers Extending `Europe Against AIDS' Programme Until 1994"; as released by Brussels Textline database] [Text] Brussels, 1 Oct 1993 (AGENCE EUROPE) - On the initiative of the Commissioner responsible for Social Affairs, Padraig Flynn, the European Commission is suggesting extending the Community "Europe Against AIDS" Programme until end-1994. Ecu 9 million has been included in the draft budget for 1994 approved by the Commission. Mr. Flynn stressed that despite the huge efforts being made in Europe to combat AIDS, the epidemic was continuing to grow: by March 1993, 84,000 AIDS cases had been reported in the Community and it is estimated that about 500,000 people are infected by HIV. The new measures being proposed take account of the changes in the pattern of the epidemic and also the results from an interim evaluation of the present 1992-1993 programme. The new proposals place particular emphasis on tackling several issues of growing concern in the Member States, including: - AIDS and drug abuse; - HIV transmission in prison; - HIV transmission linked to travel and tourism; - the increasing number of women and children with the disease; - fighting discrimination against those affected. The "Europe Against AIDS" Programme is implemented mainly by supporting projects in Member States in the various areas of activity identified in the programme. Projects submitted for funding are assessed by an Advisory Committee on the basis of criteria such as their relevance for public health and whether or not there is a "Community added value". About 80 projects had been assisted by end-1992. The Commission also intends drawing up long-term proposals for the fight against AIDS; which will form part of the strategy on public health being developed on the basis of the Treaty on European Union once it is ratified.
