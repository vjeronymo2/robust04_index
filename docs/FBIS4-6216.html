BFN [Text] A meeting on Thai-Cambodian border problems was held at the Foreign Ministry today. The attendants were Foreign Minister Squadron Leader Prasong Sunsiri; Interior Minister General Chawalit Yongchaiyut; Gen. Charan Kunlawanit, National Security Council secretary general; Navy Commander Admiral Prachet Siridet; and other officials concerned. Reporting afterwards, the foreign minister said the meeting discussed problems along the Thai-Cambodian border and retaliatory measures against Cambodia. The Foreign Ministry will today send a letter to Cambodian Prime Ministers Prince Norodom Ranariddh and Hun Sen regarding the Cambodian allegations about Thai support for the Khmer Rouge. The letter will clarify what has occurred and point out that Thailand is basically complying with the Paris Agreement. It will confirm that Thailand has not supported any particular Cambodian group, as well as not having supplied weapons to the Khmer Rouge. The foreign minister said Thailand will not accept further Cambodian refugees. The Foreign Ministry will encourage Cambodia to promptly establish within one year a safety zone for refugees fleeing the fighting. The foreign minister said the Thai military will take drastic action to deal with shelling of Thai territory by the Khmer Rouge or Cambodian Government forces.
