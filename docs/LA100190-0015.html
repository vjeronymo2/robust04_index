October 1, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

COUNTERPUNCH LETTERS;

THE MIRROR OF TELEVISION IN AMERICA

An Oscar, an Emmy, a Tony, an Obie, a Clio and a Grammy to Allen. Let's get back to basics! If the current crop of writers were properly educated, they wouldn't have to resort to shock material, which is nothing more than working the L out of shlock.

Let's get back to regulation! There was a time in the not-too-distant past when regulations and the National Assn. of Broadcasters code wouldn't allow such sex, violence or language on television.

E. PAUL WILEY

Los Angeles
