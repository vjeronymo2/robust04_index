Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Urgent" item; by ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Tbilisi February 1 TASS -- Georgian Defence Minister Georgiy Karkarashvili offered to resign on Tuesday, two days before Russian President Boris Yeltsin is to visit Georgia and sign a bilateral military-political treaty. The resignation offer published by the Tbilisi-based "IBERIA-EXPRESS" newspaper today was confirmed to TASS by officials in the Defence Ministry who failed to provide explanations on the reasons of the resignation. The press service of Eduard Shevardnadze also refused to give any comments. Karkarashvili, 28, is the symbol of the national-liberation movement. A former Soviet Army captain, he fought in Afghanistan and was one of the founders of the Georgian Armed Forces. He participated in the ouster of late President Zviad Gamsakhurdia, fought in rebel Georgian regions of South Ossetia and Abkhazia. Karkarashvili replaced Tengiz Kitovani as defence minister after a major military defeat near Gagra in Abkhazia. He was the defence minister when Georgia lost the war to Abkhazia.
