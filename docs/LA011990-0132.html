January 19, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

FREEWAY LEAGUE ROUNDUP;

LA HABRA CONTAINS FULLERTON

La Habra High School's "freak" defense in the second half and Leslie Ferguson's 27 points and 14 rebounds led the Highlanders over Fullerton, 58-40, in a Freeway League girls' basketball game Thursday night at Fullerton High.

Ferguson scored 12 of La Habra's 20 points in the third quarter and its defense controlled the boards, forcing Fullerton (11-5 overall, 4-1 in league play) into poor shots.

"We put our freak defense, designed to take two shooters (Fullerton's Summer McLane and Jennifer Comstock) out of the game while increasing our intensity . . . in the second quarter," La Habra Coach John Koehler said.

"As a result, our defense forced the ball into the hands of Fullerton's nonshooters and contained them to only one shot, and our rebounders were in position to sweep the boards."

La Habra led only 22-18 at halftime, but Fullerton lost momentum when Lynn Gilbert, its inside shooter, injured her right ankle with 7:43 to play in half. Gilbert injured the same ankle last year and was out for three weeks.

La Habra (13-2, 4-0) had 47 rebounds. Zrinka Kristich had 15 rebounds and 12 points and Ana Kristrich had 12 rebounds and 13 points.

Fullerton Coach Vernie Ford called her team's poor shooting (15 of 54) "discouraging, an age-old problem for us."

"We played hard and made good adjustments, but our shooting just wasn't on target," Ford said.

McLane hit six of 15 shots from the field (15 points), and Comstock hit six of 17, one a three-pointer (13 points).

Koehler said the score is not indicative of the game. "We made mistakes in the first half and Fullerton got after us," he said. BOB BORSUM Sonora 54, Troy 35 -- Chris Kiel had 15 points, Casie Lozano 13 and Denise Donadio 12 to lead host Sonora (4-10, 1-3). Brandy Richardson had 22 points for Troy. Sonora trailed, 15-3, in the first quarter, but came back with a press and took the lead, 33-31, in the fourth quarter. Sonora made 14 of 18 free throws in the fourth quarter and outscored Troy, 40-14, in the second half. Buena Park 55, Sunny Hills 30 -- Three girls scored in double figures to lead host Buena Park. Melissa Massinsin had 13 points for the winners. Gina Bongiovanni had 11 and Daniele Fagins added 10.
