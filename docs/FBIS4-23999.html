BFN [Text] A memorandum of understanding was signed today in Lilongwe between the Alliance for Democracy, Aford, and Malawi Congress Party, MCP, under which the two parties will be working closer together to defend and preserve what they termed the endangered national unity and security of the country. Speaking at the signing ceremony, the vice president of the MCP, Mr. Gwanda Chakuamba, urged people of this country to support the MPC-Aford national security initiative so as to achieve the desired result. Mr. Chakuamba gave credit to the Aford leadership for what he called their vision that the two parties should work together for the sake of national unity. And in his remark at the same ceremony, the president of the Aford Party, Mr. Chakufwa Chihana, pointed out that Aford and MCP had no intention of derailing the democratic change now in Malawi, and called on all the people to work together in creating what he called a common security for every Malawian. The Aford president said he was mindful that democracy is a delicate thing and that his conviction was that this country should not be divided just because someone wants to be a leader.
