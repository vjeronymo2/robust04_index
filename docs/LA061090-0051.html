June 10, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

ANDREW DICE CLAY

Regarding: "To Brooklyn in Search of the Real Andrew Dice Clay" (by Patrick Goldstein, June 3):

A very pretty portrait for your June 2 cover -- misogynistic bigot with fiancee. And such a sensitive young thing he turns out to be, only awful, really, when the eyes of the world are on him, and only then when he needs the money for his family. Heartwarming.

Now that you are shamelessly in bed with Andrew Silverstein, I await your apologia for 2 Live Crew, Chuck D and N.W.A. Once you offer the poor-misunderstood-performer excuse for Silverstein, it seems that you're equally committed to shifting the focus of your rap lyric coverage from First Amendment rights to human interest. Or don't you bend over backwards to pander to hatred unless it's got the full force of a major studio publicity department behind it?

I've got a suggestion for another cover story -- those shiny metal silhouettes of nude women that are so popular on the mud flaps of trucks. Better yet, how about a feature about mud flaps, or, even more to the point, about mud?

SHAWN A. LEVY

Irvine
