Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Hector Ramos Aguilar; from "The City" newscast] [Text] After stating his disagreement with the measures cited by Federal District Mayor Manuel Aguilera Gomez to protect Mexico City residents, Mexico City PRD [Party of the Democratic Revolution] leader (Rene Bejarano), who believes that the measures will restrict the people's freedom of movement, announced plans for a large peace demonstration. [Begin (Bejarano) recording] We will march from the Revolution Monument to the Zocalo [Mexico City main square] on 12, 13, or 14 January, and we will hold a large demonstration, which will probably be attended by our PRD presidential candidate, to demand peace in the country. [end recording] (Bejarano) called the federal district mayor and political leaders to an urgent meeting to establish citizen protection measures that will not include the use of Army personnel to patrol the city. He said that far from calming the people, Army patrols will only alarm the public. [Begin (Bejarano) recording] We believe the mayor should accept a meeting with political party presidents, social organizations, and city government personnel to reach an agreement that will impede [words indistinct] further in the political climate. [end recording]
