930902 FT 02 SEP 93 / UK Company News: Calderburn falls as forecast but expects upturn Calderburn, the office furniture group, reported pre-tax profits of Pounds 1.41m for the first half of 1993, a 36 per cent fall on the comparable Pounds 2.21m. The company said the fall had been expected. Mr James Blyth Currie, chairman, said that the market had continued to decline in the first half but there were now signs of a recovery. Turnover fell 13 per cent to Pounds 12.9m (Pounds 14.9m). Earnings per share came out at 3.6p (5.7p) but the interim dividend is maintained at 2.8p. The company is spending up to Pounds 4m on the rationalisation of production facilities and the concentration of all furniture output.
