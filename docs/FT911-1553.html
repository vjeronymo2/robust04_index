910504 FT 04 MAY 91 / Economic Diary TODAY: African, Caribbean and Pacific countries hold ministerial meeting in Brussels to prepare for ministerial talks with European Community (May 6-7). TOMORROW: US Democratic Party leadership conference in Cleveland, Ohio. MONDAY: World Health Organisation's annual two-week assembly begins in Geneva. Annual meeting of African Development Bank (until May 9). Mr Hans van den Broek, Dutch foreign minister, will travel to Jerusalem for two days of talks with Israeli officials on Mideast issues; he will meet Mr Yitzhak Shamir, the prime minister, Mr David Levy, foreign minister, and Mr Moshe Arens, defence minister. TUESDAY: Central Statistical Office publishes figures for finished steel consumption and stock changes (fourth quarter). The Department of Environment gives statistics for housing starts and completions in March. Mr Felipe Gonzalez, Spanish Prime Minister, visits UK for talks with Mr John Major, Prime Minister, and Mr Douglas Hurd, Foreign Secretary (until May 8). Trafalgar House interim statement. Mr Michael Howard, Employment Secretary, launches report on small companies. Mr Michael Heseltine, Environment Secretary, gives Shell Lecture, London. Conference on the future of the TUC at Congress House, London. Scottish Conservatives annual conference opens in Perth (until May 3). Scottish electricity companies share offer launched. WEDNESDAY: The Department of Employment issues details of employment, unemployment, earnings, prices and other indices. Cannes film festival opens (until May 20). THURSDAY: The Department of Transport publishes the annual vehicle census for 1990. 24-hour Underground train strike. Mr Roger Levitt appears at Bow Street Magistrates Court. FRIDAY: British Steel/BISPA publishes figures for usable steel production in April. CSO issues figures for overseas travel and tourism (January/February). Start of two-day informal meeting of the economic and financial council of the European Community in Luxembourg.
