Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- The series of German-Finnish consultations on Helsinki's entry into the European Union (EU) has continued, with the foreign ministers meeting in Bonn on Wednesday [16 Februaqry]. The exchange of opinions between FRG Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel and his counterpart, Heikki Haavisto, focused on Finland's concerns about structural adjustments and the special agricultural problems of the Nordic winter economy. For Finnish politicians, the negotiations for membership have entered the "decisive stage." They are banking on German support in Brussels. In January, Foreign Trade Minister Perti Salolainen was in Bonn and Parliamentary President Ilkka Suominen followed a few days later.
