October 11, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

WORLD;

POLICE FIRE ON RETURNING SOVIETS

Soviet police fired machine-guns at three families in four cars that failed to stop at a road checkpoint returning from a trip to the West, the Communist Party daily Pravda said today.

A 17-year-old boy was injured and the cars were riddled with bullets in the nighttime incident near the Ural Mountains city of Chelyabinsk. The families, driving foreign-made cars from Germany to Kazakhstan, said they ignored an order to stop because they mistook the police for roadside bandits.
