December 12, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

PHILIPPINE COUP ATTEMPT

Let's see if I have this right now. The Bush Administration says that its military support of the Philippine government to suppress the rebellion was legal because international law permits governments under attack to seek outside help. But the Nicaraguan government, under attack for eight years, was not allowed to seek help from the Soviets or anyone else.

And the United States is allowed to take sides in the El Salvador civil war, but no one else is allowed to support the other side.

And the Soviet Union should not interfere in the Western Hemisphere, but the United States can support the rebels in Afghanistan.

And the government and society of Iran are highly repressive, but the Afghan rebels, who will institute a very similar system if they take power, are freedom fighters.

And it's a marvelous thing that the Soviet Union has allowed Eastern Europe to freely pursue its own path, but the U.S. should not do the same for Central America.

And union militancy, a multiparty electoral system and social reform are wonderful to see in Eastern Europe, but they have no place in the United States.

WILLIAM BLUM

Los Angeles
