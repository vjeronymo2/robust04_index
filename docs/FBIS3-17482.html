Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Mar 26, BNS -- Estonian officials in Paldiski had by Friday [25 March] night registered 3,554 residents of the town, special representative of the government in Paldiski Juri Tikk told BNS. The number allows to assume that most of Paldiski's civilian residents will turn up for registration. Registration of the population began March 22 and is scheduled to be completed at 4 p.m. today. Tikk didn't rule out that the registration stations may stay open longer. At present about 250 Estonian citizens and 4,000 civilian non-citizens live in Paldiski. Besides, an estimated 800 to 1,000 Russian military serve there. Russian military command presented the day before yesterday a list of officers and soldiers in Paldiski, which included 891 names. Some 600 of them are permanently stationed in the town and are expected to leave by Aug. 31, the rest are specialists and technical personnel brought in to dismantle the two nuclear reactors at the Russian naval training base. The majority of the Russian military living in Paldiski serve in the navy, but some officers of the Tallinn-based 144th motorized rifle division also reside in the town. Russian military authorities promised to present their list on March 29, Tikk told BNS.
