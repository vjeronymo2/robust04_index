BFN [Text] Damascus, 1 Apr (SANA)--Igor Ivanov, Russian deputy foreign minister and Russian President Boris Yeltsin's envoy to the Middle East, and his delegation arrived in Damascus this afternoon. Their visit to the Syrian Arab Republic will last for several days. Ivanov, was received at the Damascus International Airport by Raslan 'Allush, assistant to the foreign minister, director of Eastern Europe Desk at the Foreign Ministry, the Russian ambassador in Damascus, and embassy staff. In a statement to the Syrian Arab News Agency's correspondent, Ivanov said: I am carrying a personal message to President Hafiz al-Asad from Russian President Boris Yeltsin. President al-Asad's ideas are appreciated by the Russian leadership, and his excellency enjoys high reputation and esteem in Russia. He said this visit was mandated by the Russian president within the framework of efforts the Russian leadership is carrying out to overcome the situation in the region caused by the Hebron massacre. Ivanov emphasized that Russia wants the peace process to continue and wants a strong and comprehensive peace to be realized in the Middle East. He added that Russia, in its capacity as a superpower and one of the sponsors of the peace process, has made and will continue to make all efforts toward reaching this objective. He emphasized that Moscow attaches great importance to cooperation between Syria and Russia. Ivanov said he will discuss in Damascus issues relating to the Middle East peace process in addition to bilateral relations. Concluding his statement, he emphasized that the latest Russian initiative is valid and long term, and that its implementation depends on the positions of all concerned parties.
