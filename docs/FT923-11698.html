920723 FT 23 JUL 92 / Mafia car bomb costs police chief his job THE police chief of the Sicilian capital Palermo was sacked yesterday in the wake of the car bomb murder of the anti-Mafia judge, Mr Paolo Borsellino and five bodyguards, Reuter reports from Palermo. Embittered Sicilians demanded that heads roll after the authorities failed to prevent Sunday's killings in Palermo, despite warnings of an imminent murder attempt from a Mafia informer and from Mr Borsellino himself. The Interior Ministry said police commissioner Vito Plantone had been dismissed 'with immediate effect' and would take up a desk job at the ministry in Rome.
