BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yuriy Kozlov] [Text] Moscow June 23 TASS -- UNESCO Secretary General Federico Mayor has ended his visit to Russia today. On the final day of the visit the UNESCO director general attended the opening ceremony of a UNESCO bureau in Moscow. Mayor pointed out that the opening of the UNESCO bureau became possible due to the desire expressed by the Russian Government and the UNESCO Secretariat to deepen mutually advantageous cooperation. Mayor highly assessed talks he had in Moscow with the Russian President Boris Yeltsin, Chairman of the Russian Federation Council Vladimir Shumeyko and Patriarch Aleksiy II, heads of the Russian Ministry for Science and Technical Politics, Culture and Education. In an interview with ITAR-TASS Mayor said that during the talks the sides summed up the results of activities aimed at the realization of concrete projects included in a memorandum on cooperation between Russia and UNESCO. These projects envisage support to the Bolshoi Theatre, the Russian State Library and the Hermitage. The sides also discussed prospects for the development of cooperation in the field of communications, the organisation of international conferences, the reform of secondary education in Russia and the use of intellectual potential of Russian scientists. According to Mayor, UNESCO has allocated half a million dollars to support various projects in Russia, predominantly connected with the support to research centers, special schools and a programme of studying the Russian language. As a result of the talks agreements were reached to hold a second world congress on information science and education and establish an international department for human rights at the Moscow State Institute of International Relations. During his visit Mayor met with representatives of intelligentsia, Russian mass media chiefs, the company of the Bolshoi Theater and students of the Moscow State University. The UNESCO secretary general also took part in the opening ceremony of an exhibition devoted to the famous French poet Arthur Rimbaud. The UNESCO secretary general was awarded with an Order of People's Friendship for his outstanding contribution to the development of international cooperation. The award was conferred upon him by the Russian President Boris Yeltsin in the Kremlin.
