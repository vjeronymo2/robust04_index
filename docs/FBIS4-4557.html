BFN [From MAINICHI SHIMBUN 1 April Evening Edition] [Text] Hiroshi Kumagai, minister of international trade and industry, told a news conference on 1 April after a cabinet meeting that "if an annual report on foreign trade barriers recently compiled by the United States is linked to sanctions to be imposed in accordance with the Super 301 provision, Japan cannot watch idly with indifference." Commenting on the inclusion of three priority sectors discussed at the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks in the annual report, he stated, "If the Super 301 provision is applied to the three sectors, the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks would collapse. Both Japan and the United States need to work to avert such a situation. They should resume the framework talks to discuss bilateral economic issues in a calm atmosphere and work for a conclusion of the framework talks. This is the way to expand and balance Japan-U.S. trade." In this way, Mr. Kumagai urged the United States not to abuse the annual report and stressed the need for resuming the framework talks.
