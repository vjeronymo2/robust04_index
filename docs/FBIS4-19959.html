BFN [Excerpt] The Greek Government is satisfied with Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou's meeting with U.S. President Bill Clinton at the White House. The prime minister described the talks as especially useful. Tilemakhos Khitiris, the deputy minister to the prime minister, said the visit was very successful. Athens News Agency reported today from Washington that according to U.S. evaluations, the lengthy meeting of the U.S. President with the prime minister gave the opportunity to the two leaders to reconfirm the excellent bilateral relations and analyze further the prospects of cooperation in light of the new international conditions, particularly in the Balkans. Washington is convinced--the U.S. President has made it clear to the prime minister--that the White House considers Greece the most stable country in the region that can play an important and leading role in the Balkans.
