910503 FT 03 MAY 91 / Appointments: Barclays recruits Japanese and Dutch directors BARCLAYS has appointed its first two non-British directors. They are Mr Shijuro Ogata of Japan and Mr Jan Peelen of the Netherlands, writes David Lascelles, Banking Editor. Mr Ogata was deputy governor for international relations at the Bank of Japan and subsequently deputy governor of the Japan Development Bank, from which he retired earlier this year. Mr Peelen is a director of Unilever with responsibility for the East Asian and Pacific region. Sir John Quinton, Barclays' chairman, said the appointments would be of value to Barclays as an international corporate and investment bank, and he hoped eventually to appoint a director from the US as well. Mr Ogata said he hoped his appointment would lead to greater international understanding on banking issues at a time when global banking markets were becoming increasingly integrated.
