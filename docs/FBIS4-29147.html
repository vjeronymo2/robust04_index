BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Kim Boldokhonov] [Text] Ulaanbaatar, 6 Jun -- The aim of the newly created Mongolian Democratic Rebirth Party is the renewal of the country on the basis of democratic principles and taking into account the specific conditions and tasks of our society at the present stage, as well as the principles of human civilization as a whole. Dashiyn Byambasuren said this today in an interview with an ITAR-TASS correspondent. He opened the founding forum of this new political force today with brief introductory remarks. The former head of the first multiparty coalition government in Mongolia's history set up an organizing committee with a group of like-minded people, which submitted a report "On the sociopolitical situation in the country and the tasks of the party" and the draft rules and program of the Mongolian Democratic Rebirth Party for debate by delegates. We favor a state with strong democratic power, a society of civic accord and genuine market relations in the economy. Our party regards strong presidential power and a small, highly professional parliament to be most acceptable for Mongolian conditions, D. Byambasuren noted. On the matter of collaboration with other political forces, D. Byambasuren said: "We favor close cooperation with all parties if our views are common or similar. The Mongolian Democratic Rebirth Party favors the formation of a general democratic front." Moscow ITAR-TASS World Service in Russian at 1117 GMT on 6 June notes the following: "Congress delegates adopted the party's rules and program and elected its ruling bodies. Dashiyn Byambasuren becomes chairman of the party, and the publicist Luvsangiyn Purevdorj its general secretary. With this the congress concluded its work."
