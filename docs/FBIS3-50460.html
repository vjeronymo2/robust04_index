Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Rayma Rondon; from "The News" newscast] [Text] Defense Minister Vice Admiral Radamez Munoz Leon gave assurances that relations between Venezuela and Colombia have never been better. He also assured that the relations between the Armed Forces of both countries are excellent and that the measures Colombia is taking to reinforce Venezuelan border surveillance are the best possible. [Begin Munoz recording] Never have relations with Colombia been as good as they are now. The relationship between both Armed Forces go further than the personal friendship between the heads of the Colombian and Venezuelan Armed Forces and this also holds true for the defense ministers -- relations are excellent. I believe the measures Colombia is taking to support our domestic measures is a great demonstration of a broad support based on the various international agreements exercised through both defense ministers and the various military commanders, both Colombian and Venezuelan. The agreements we have are being honored. [end recording] Likewise, the defense minister reported that starting January greater border security efficiency is to be expected. Regarding the possibility that civilian and military personnel in the Amparo Case [alleged border massacre] may be pardoned, Munoz said that the president is studying the case together with other cases.
