930727 FT 27 JUL 93 / Arts: Today's Television Thin pickings. BBC1 looks very sad and sorry for itself, like a network that has lost Pounds 35m and found a bag of used cassettes. Between 8.00 and 10.30 there is nothing but repeats, and even the American programme beginning at 10.30, Vigilante Cop, may well be another repeat. By billing this television drama as a 'Film' the BBC, by its own weird rules, avoids the need to come clean and say whether it really is a repeat or not. So it probably is. Ironically one of the network's few original programmes, Bobby Davro: Rock With Laughter, looks like something dredged up from the 1950s. Davro himself is a professional enough variety artist, capable of delivering a song and dance act with impressions thrown in, but the formula (and even the guests: tonight Neil Sedaka) look as though they have been trapped in a time warp (7.00). Nature (8.00 BBC2) investigates British seaside holidays and names the 10 cleanest and 10 dirtiest beaches. In Far Flung Floyd Keith Floyd reports from Thailand (8.30 BBC2).
