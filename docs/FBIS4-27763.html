BFN [Text] The National Assembly Office issued Communique No. 8 as follows: Today, the National Assembly held a plenary meeting in the conference hall. In the morning, under the direction of National Assembly Chairman Nong Duc Manh, the National Assembly continued to debate the revised draft law on the organization of the people's councils and the people's committees and the revised draft law on the elections of delegates to the People's Councils. Nine deputies, out of 41 deputies registered to address the meeting, expressed their views. They were Le Ngoc Sanh of Dac Lac, Tran Thi Le Thu of Binh Dinh, Ca Kha Sam of Son La, Lam Phuc Co of Yen Bai, Nong Thai Nghiep of Bac Thai, H' Nghia of Gia Lai, Pham Thi Son of Ba Ria-Vung Tau, Phan Tan Tai of Vinh Long, and Dieu Bao of Dong Nai. Their opinions concentrated on the following issues: the number of annual meetings of the people's council; its supervisory role; the content of its supervisory role; the relationship between the people's council with the standing committee of the people's council and the people's committee; issues relating to organs responsible for the organization of elections; the application, selection, and introduction of candidates to the people's council; the election procedures; and the supplement of delegates to the people's council. In the afternoon, under the direction of National Assembly Vice Chairman Nguyen Ha Phan, the National Assembly listened to a report on the draft law on the promotion of domestic investment presented by Minister Do Quoc Sam, chairman of the State Planning Committee. Then Nguyen Thanh Phong, vice chairman of the National Assembly's Economic and State Budget Committee, read an investigative report on the draft law on the promotion of domestic investment. National Defense Minister Doan Khue read a report on the revised draft law to amend and supplement some articles of the Law on Military Obligations. Then Pham Loi, vice chairman of the National Assembly's National Defense and Security Committee, read an investigative report on the revised draft law to amend and supplement some articles of the Law on Military Obligations. Tomorrow, 4 June, in the morning, the National Assembly will meet in the conference hall and debate the revised draft law to amend and supplement some articles of the Law on Military Obligations. In the afternoon, the National Assembly deputies will exchange views in their delegations on the draft law on the promotion of domestic investment.
