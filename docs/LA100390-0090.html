October 3, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

OFFICIALS SEEK CAUSE OF CONVENTION ILLNESSES

City Health Department officials were attempting Tuesday to determine the cause of the apparent food poisoning of more than 150 people attending a convention.

The officials said the apparent cause was food served Sunday at a hotel, but they added that it could be several days or even weeks before a determination is made. A survey of area hospitals showed that at least 155 people were treated for symptoms of food poisoning.

The meal at the Hyatt Regency Hotel included a green salad, chicken stuffed with wild rice and bread pudding with cream sauce.
