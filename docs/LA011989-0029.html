January 19, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: LESS IS MORE FOR TASTIER TREATS

DEAR JEANNE: Please determine whether this recipe can be adjusted to lower the fat and cholesterol levels. It's delicious but dangerous.

-- VIRGINIA, Kenmore, N.Y.

SPINACH SQUARES

1 cup flour

1 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon baking powder

1 cup milk

1/2 cup margarine, melted

2 eggs, beaten

1 cup minced onion

10 ounces Cheddar cheese, shredded

10 ounces cooked spinach, drained thoroughly

Combine flour, salt, baking powder, milk, margarine, eggs, onion, Cheddar cheese and spinach. Place in greased 9-inch square pan. Bake at 350 degrees 30 minutes. Cut into squares. Makes 8 servings.

Note: May be baked 1 day ahead and reheated.

DEAR VIRGINIA: This is a perfect example of when less is more. By simply reducing the problem ingredients in the recipe, you end up with a finished product that is more enjoyable from both a health and taste standpoint.

The margarine was easily reduced by half and egg substitute worked beautifully in place of the eggs, so the fat and cholesterol are greatly reduced. I have found that low-fat Cheddar cheese is always appropriate as a substitute for the traditional high-fat variety, and I was able to reduce the amount of cheese called for without missing it.

I think you will agree that this recipe gives more of everything you want from food by using less of the things you don't really need.

-- JEANNE

REVISED SPINACH SQUARES

1 cup unbleached flour

1/2 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon baking powder

1 cup nonfat milk

2 tablespoons corn-oil margarine, melted

1/2 cup liquid egg substitute

1 cup minced onion

6 ounces 20 % fat-reduced Cheddar cheese, shredded

1 (10-ounce) package frozen spinach, cooked and well drained or 3/4 pound fresh spinach, chopped, steamed 2 minutes and well drained

Combine flour, salt, baking powder, milk, margarine, egg substitute, onion, Cheddar cheese and spinach. Place in 9-inch square pan sprayed with non-stick vegetable coating. Bake at 350 degrees 30 minutes. Cut into squares. Makes 8 servings.

Note: May be baked 1 day ahead and reheated.

Each serving contains approximately:
