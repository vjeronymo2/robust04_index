BFN [Interview with National Assembly member Mehmed Hodzha by Miroslava Badzheva; place and date not given: "Mehmed Hodzha: The Movement for Rights and Freedoms Will Get No More Than 1.5 Percent in Elections"] [Excerpts] [Badzheva] Mr. Hodzha, now that you have been expelled from the Movement for Rights and Freedoms [DPS], will you form an alternative to the movement? [Hodzha] We left the DPS parliamentary faction with the clear understanding that the DPS cannot survive with this leadership. Therefore, we demanded an extraordinary conference. Indeed, not only we demanded such a conference, but also the representatives of local branches in various regions. However, obviously this will not happen. I am convinced that there will be no new national conference. This means that we are left with the second variant -- to offer our former and potential voters something different than the present DPS. Both in the DPS parliamentary faction and Central Council there are honest people, but this in itself is not enough. An alternative must be proposed. I can tell you that this idea exists already several months. It has already matured, now we must only put it into effect. We will convene a new meeting to which we will naturally invite people from all regions in the country who adhere to positions similar to ours. We want to attract the dissatisfied people who reject the policy of the DPS leadership, and most of all, those who are dissatisfied with the cooperation with the Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP]. All Europe, not only Bulgaria, understood that this cooperation is already a fact. This leadership is simply genetically committed to cooperating with the BSP. The same phenomenon will also exist in the next parliament. [Badzheva] What will be your new party's name? [Hodzha] This is not important. The basic principle around which we will rally, will be that this is an anticommunist party that is not based on an ethnic foundation. Many Bulgarians already demonstrate interest in the new party. [Badzheva] Who are the people that will form the core of this formation? [Hodzha] I will not divulge any names, everything is still within the framework of ideas. Let us wait a bit longer. [Badzheva] When will you announce officially its establishment? [Hodzha] In the next few weeks. [Badzheva] When will you hold the planned meeting? [Hodzha] Again, in the next few weeks. [Badzheva] Is the Union of Democratic Forces [SDS] helping you in forming the "alternative DPS"? [Hodzha] We do not need any helpers. We are sufficiently prudent and able people, and can manage alone. [Badzheva] However, nevertheless, you plan to cooperate with the SDS? [Hodzha] We would like to work together with the SDS, but this is not an unconditional desire. [Badzheva] What conditions will you pose? [Hodzha] The blue [SDS] coalition must share our intentions and ideas. We will stress the democratic principles, human rights, and resolving social and economic problems -- all this that existed at the beginning, when we were together with the SDS. [Badzheva] Would you participate in the next elections on the same ticket with the SDS? [Hodzha] I cannot answer this question. Let the SDS assess whether it needs me or not. [passage omitted] [Badzheva] What do you think, if elections were held now what percentage of the vote will the DPS get? [Hodzha] No more than 1.5 percent. I think that this is the last participation in the parliamentary elections with this leadership. [Badzheva] You claim that the new formation will not be based on an ethnic foundation. Will its program contain the demand on proclaiming the ethnic Turks in Bulgaria a national minority -- a thesis you defended when you were still in the DPS? [Hodzha] Personally, I support collective rights. Now the Council of Europe is preparing precisely such a document regarding the collective rights of the minorities. [passage omitted] [Badzheva] Do you still claim that the higher DPS circles are corrupt? [Hodzha] Since I am magnanimous, I will take cigarettes to some of the DPS leadership members when they are in prison. [Badzheva] Will you support your claims with documents? [Hodzha] Yes, when the time comes. [passage omitted]
