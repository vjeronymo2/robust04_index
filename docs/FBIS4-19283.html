BFN [Text] Ukrainian Internal Affairs Minister Andriy Vasylyshyn, who arrived on an official visit in Ankara today, has begun his contacts. He first called on Interior Minister Nahit Mentese and conferred with him for some time. Later the official talks between the delegations headed by the two ministers began. Mentese, in a statement prior to the talks, said that Turkey and Ukraine are two friendly, stable, and strong countries in the region. He added that a comprehensive and effective cooperation between the two countries on international drug smuggling, terrorism, and other organized crimes will greatly contribute to regional peace. Mentese noted that a crime prevention agreement will be signed at the end of the talks. Vasylyshyn said that bilateral cooperation on [words indistinct], police training, and scientific research will be beneficial for both countries.
