BFN [Text] Kiev -- Ukrainian Foreign Affairs Minister Anatoliy Zlenko on 31 March met Canadian Foreign Affairs Minister Andre Ouellet who is on an official visit to Ukraine. After the negotiations ended, the sides signed "The Agreement Between the Government of Ukraine and the Government of Canada on Trade and Commerce Relations," "The Declaration on Special Partnership Between Ukraine and Canada," "The Declaration on Cooperation Between the Government of ukraine and the Government of Canada on Implementing the Program of Organizing Environmental Management in the River Dnieper's Basin," and "The Agreement on Cooperation Between the Canadian Ukrainian Chamber of Commerce and the Ukraine's Chamber of Commerce and Industry." Andre Ouellet commented on the event: "The documents signed are a significant step along the path of consolidating relations between Ukraine and Canada. We attach especial significance to economic agreements that have demonstrated that Canadian business will be represented on the Ukrainian market. The Government of Canada is also ready to assist Ukraine in converting its defense enterprises and retraining its military personnel." Anatoliy Zlenko stressed that relations between Ukraine and Canada were developing consistently, stably, and dynamically. The documents signed, he said, "are the beginning of a special partnership between the countries, which is the world's new phenomenon, and put Ukraine in a position of privilege as well as creating conditions for joint actions on the international arena."
