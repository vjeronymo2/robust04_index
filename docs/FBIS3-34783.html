Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Lebanese officials are watching with keen interest this week's developments. After the resumption today of the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations in Taba to work out the final details of the Israeli military withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho and the transfer of civilian power, and on the eve of the bilateral negotiations in Washington, U.S. Ambassador to Lebanon Mark Hambley delivered to Foreign Minister Faris Buwayz a written message from U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. The ambassador would not reveal the contents of the message, saying only that it deals with the bilateral peace talks that will resume in Washington tomorrow. Hambley expressed optimism about the results of the talks. He said the deliberate tension and violence in southern Lebanon is aimed at undermining the talks. He stressed that his country is eager to maintain the peacekeeping forces in the south as a factor of stability in Lebanon. [passage omitted]
