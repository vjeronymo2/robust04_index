910510 FT 10 MAY 91 / World News in Brief: Rushdie criticism Author Salman Rushdie criticised two Islamic leaders in Britain who have ruled that his conversion to the faith cannot be accepted because he has not withdrawn his controversial novel The Satanic Verses. The author has been in hiding since February 1989, when Iran's late leader, Ayatollah Khomeini, ordered his execution for alleged blasphemy.
