Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Taba, 25 Jan (MENA)--MENA has learned that the Civilian Affairs Committee, tasked with transferring civilian powers from the Israelis to the Palestinians in accordance with the Gaza-Jericho agreement, has postponed its session scheduled for today until next week. Radwan Abu-'Ayyash, head of the Palestinian radio and television corporation, which is still under formation, has told MENA that in its two sessions yesterday the committee discussed only the question of communications, particularly the establishment of a Palestinian radio and television in the Palestinian self-rule region. He noted that the French Government has earmarked $30 million to set up this important facility. The one-day session in Taba of the Civilian Affairs Committee, under Lawyer Jamil al-Tarifi on the Palestinian side and Gadi Zohar, head of the Israeli Civilian Administration in the West Bank, is the fastest round of talks yet held between the two sides. The Security and Civilian Affairs Committees are scheduled to resume their sessions in Taba next Monday [31 January], by which time the diplomatic maneuvers that Egypt is carrying out with Israel to overcome the obstacles to the implementation of the Gaza-Jericho agreement will have been clarified. MENA has learned that the Palestinian delegation, led by Dr. Nabil Sha'th and comprised of Brigadier General 'Abd-al-Razzaq al-Yahya, commander of the Palestine Liberation Army; Hasan 'Asfur; and Dr. Camille Mansur met today in Cairo to discuss the Israeli reply brought back by Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa, in preparation for a meeting with Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat. 'Arafat is expected to arrive in Cairo today from Riyadh.
