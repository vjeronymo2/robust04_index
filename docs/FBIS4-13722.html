BFN [Text] India and Nepal have decided to accord priority to the completion of several road projects. The meeting of the India-Nepalese high level task force in New Delhi today also decided to expand the Bir Hospital in Nepal. The projects to be completed on a priority bases are 17 bridges on the Kolhapur-Badrapur Road and the Chatara-Birpur Road. The task force stressed the early completion of project reports on the Burhi Gandak and the Panchesh projects. The progress in these sectors will be reviewed by a secretary-level meeting next month. The task force also considered several new project proposals including the India-Nepal broad-gauge railway link from Raxaul to a point in Nepal. It expressed satisfaction over the progress of Indian-aided projects in Nepal. The high-level task force is headed by the principal secretary to the prime minister, Mr. A.N. Varma, and the chief secretary to the Government of Nepal, Mr. M.P. Gorkhali. The Nepalese delegation today called on the external affairs minister, Mr. Dinesh Singh, and the minister of state, Mr. R.L. Bhatia. Mr. Dinesh Singh said there is significant potential for bilateral cooperation in the development of water resources. He lauded the work of the task force in ensuring successful completion of Indian-aided projects in Nepal. The prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, has said India and Nepal should expedite all the agreed projects which symbolize the excellent cooperation between the two countries. Talking to members of the Nepalese delegation on the India-Nepal high-level task force, who called on him in New Delhi today, Mr. Rao described the relations between the two countries as close and friendly.
