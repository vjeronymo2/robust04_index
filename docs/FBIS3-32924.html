Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Mexican economic developments monitored through 15 February. Luis German Carcoba Garcia, the president of the Business Coordinating Council, has noted that "excessive surveillance along the border by U.S. immigration officials is a bothersome and delicate issue that violates human rights." He added, however, that the situation will not affect trade relations between the two countries. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 9 Feb 94) In an address to the Business Opportunities Seminar in Guadalajara, Gilberto Jimenez Garcia de Alba, the president of the National Council for Foreign Trade, said that exports of Mexico's nonpetroleum products increased 17 percent during 1993. He added that higher nonpetroleum exports, $15 billion in foreign investments, and overall price stability "confirm that we are on the right track." (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0112 GMT 11 Feb 94) In a speech to the Business Opportunities Seminar in Guadalajara, Eugenio Carrion, the under secretary of commerce and industrial development, said Mexico has one of the most attractive markets in the world. He said proof of this was the record growth of many companies on the Mexican Stock Market. Companies such as Cifra, Gigante, and Comercial Mexicana registered growth of 14-24 percent. Mexico offers a stable economy, low inflation, and virtually no foreign debt, all of which attract investment. Carrion also said "Mexico can supply the U.S. Hispanic market," which spends about $230 billion a year, which is equivalent to 75 percent of Mexico's overall GNP. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2231 GMT 9 Feb 94) Jaime Serra Puche, the secretary of commerce and industrial development, has said foreign investment surpassed $40 billion over the past five years. He met in Guadalajara with local and foreign business leaders and announced that investments meant an influx of capital totaling $15.6 billion during 1993 -- an 87 percent increase over 1992. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 9 Feb 94) Mexican crude oil prices increased in January, according to a report issued be Mexican Petroleum (Pemex). The report quotes Isthmian Light Crude at $12.95 a barrel, Maya Heavy at $10.15, and Olmeca Extra Light at $14.20. These three types showed export levels of 213,300, 807,600, and 313,800 barrels per day, respectively. Exports went mostly to the United States, while 14.1 percent went to Spain and 4.4 percent went to the Middle East. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0026 GMT 10 Feb 94) Pedro Aspe Armella, the secretary of finance and public credit, says interest rates are continuing to fall. He called this a "memorable event, because rates on the money market have reached a single digit figure -- 9.75 percent." Meanwhile, consumer prices rose 0.8 percent in January. This is the smallest increase in 22 years. Officials pointed out that this is the first time the inflation rate fell below 1 percent for this time of year. The annual projection for consumer prices is set at 7.5 percent. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 9 Feb 94)
