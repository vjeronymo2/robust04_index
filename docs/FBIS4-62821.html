BFN [Text] Azeri, Armenian, and Karabakh defense chiefs will not meet again with their Russian counterpart as the mediator, Colonel General Georgiy Kondratyev, Russian deputy defense minister, told Interfax Wednesday. He said that while the Armenian, Karabakh and Russian ministers had signed the protocol on stage-wise end of the Karabakh war, the Azeri defense chief had not. Kondratyev said that Russia would give up its mediation efforts if Baku did not sign. Kondratyev, who represented Russia in the talks on Nagorno-Karabakh, said that Azeri President Geydar Aliyev was discussing with his top aides whether he should sign the protocol. Kondratyev also said that Vladimir Kazimirov, the Russian president's special envoy, also stayed in the Azeri capital. According to Kondratyev, the Azeri Defense Minister Mamedrafi Mamedov was not received by Aliyev on May 17. Kondratyev expressed the hope that Azerbaijan would join the protocol.
