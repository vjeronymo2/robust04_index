September 2, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

'HEROIC' VOYAGER, ITS HUMAN GUIDES

The most prominent feature in Voyager's photographs of Neptune is a huge oblong blob that mars the planet's serene blue cloud surface. This has been officially dubbed the Great Dark Spot.

Privately, Voyager project scientists have a better name for it.

They call it "Exxon."

CHARLES BELL

North Hollywood
