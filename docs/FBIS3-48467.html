Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 24 (KCNA) -- A new botanical specimen gallery has been built in the Central Botanical Garden which is situated at the foot of scenic Mt. Taesong in Pyongyang. The total floor space of the gallery is 2,000 square metres. The gallery will permanently preserve hundreds of thousands of specimens including those taken from more than 2,800 plants presented by foreign heads of state and public figures to the great leader President Kim Il-song and the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il. The gallery consists of a gift plant specimen room, a Korean plant specimen room, a world plant specimen room, a trunk piece and seed specimen room, a provincial plant specimen room, a dendrolite specimen room and an immersion specimen room. It also has an automation gauge room for scientific and technical preservation of specimens and specimen lending and study rooms and research rooms. At the gallery scientists and researchers will develop new varieties by comparatory analysis of plants of foreign countries and trace the course of the evolution of plants through fossil plants. Rich materials for writing books will be available at the gallery. The gallery will exchange plant specimens with many countries. The botanical specimen gallery which will make a big contribution to the development of the country's botany will be opened soon.
