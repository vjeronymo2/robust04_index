Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Feb. 14 -- According to chairman of the NATO Military Committee, Marshal Richard Vincent, all NATO commanders are strong supporters of Poland's admission to the organization, spokesman for the College of Polish Armed Forces Commanders, Major Mariusz Jedrzejko, said on Monday [14 February] after the meeting between Vincent and the chief of the General Staff, General Tadeusz Wilecki. Marshal Vincent, who is paying a two-day visit to Poland, was received by Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak and met with chairman of the Sejm Defence Committee Jerzy Szmajdzinski on Monday. His talks with Szmajdzinski focused primarily on "Partnership for Peace." Szmajdzinski said after the meeting that NATO wants serious attention to be paid to this initiative and proposes working out a new and detailed plan for further mutual contacts. On Tuesday [15 February] Richard Vincent will meet with ministers of defence Piotr Kolodziejczyk and foreign affairs Andrzej Olechowski.
