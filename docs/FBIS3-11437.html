Language: Creole Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Within the scope of world efforts to resolve the Haitian crisis, union representatives are meeting with President Jean-Bertrand Aristide. A delegation from the unions left Haiti last week. The members of the crisis committee of the Haitian union organization met with Aristide on 19 March to seek a solution to the critical situation in Haiti. A member of this delegation labeled their first meeting with Aristide as very promising. Representatives of six Haitian union organizations -- including the General Independent Organization, Federation of Unionized Workers, Federation of Haitian Blue Collar Workers, and the two branches of the Autonomous Organization of Haitian Workers [CATH], including Mezyeux's CATH and Manuci Pierre's CATH -- were present. Jean-Claude Lebrun, secretary general of CTH, the Federation of Haitian Workers, said that during this meeting discussions turned on how to create the conditions necessary to the lifting of the embargo. [passage omitted] A second meeting between the union committee and President Aristide is scheduled for 21 March. The delegation that is to meet with President Aristide is now in Miami.
