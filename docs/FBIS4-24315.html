BFN [Text] Ongoing talks among intellectuals and elders of Mogadishu indicate that they do not wish to see a resumption of fighting among the brotherly Somali people. This was stated by Ali Mohamed Ugas Ali, member of the United Somali Congress [USC] Central Committee. Ugas Ali, who was speaking to reporters from Radio Mogadishu, the voice of the Somali people, said there are a few self-seekers who are bent on creating trouble among the Somali people but that they now understand the value of peace and are tired of the agony of civil war. Ugas Ali added that the intellectuals and elders are working to unite against all the causes of insecurity. Concluding, Ali Mohamed Ugas Ali stressed that Somalis in general, and those in Mogadishu in particular, should be extra alert to the traps being laid by those wishing to start senseless fighting. He added that efforts should be made towards healing the wounds of the past.
