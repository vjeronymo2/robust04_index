Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 13 Jan (MENA)--During talks held today chaired by Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa and French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe, Egypt and France reached agreement to convene a ministerial meeting of foreign ministers of Mediterranean countries next summer. In the joint news conference held with Alain Juppe after the talks between them, 'Amr Musa stated that the objective of such a meeting is to strengthen relations of cooperation in the Mediterranean Sea region and specify the fields of action in this direction. He clarified that this meeting will be preceded by a preparatory meeting attended by intellectuals and diplomats. The Egyptian foreign minister said his talks with his French counterpart touched on current international issues, topped by the peace process in the Middle East, Bosnia-Herzegovina, the Mediterranean Sea area, and bilateral relations between Egypt and France. During the news conference, Juppe affirmed that they discussed problems of a regional and international nature, concentrating on the peace process, which is backed by Egypt and France, as well as cooperation within the framework of the Mediterranean basin. He said France asserted its interest in cooperation among the Mediterranean Sea countries, adding that it is one of these and is interested in every aspect of cooperation among them, politically, economically, and socially. It is also interested in establishing peace in the region. Juppe praised Egypt's role in pushing the peace process forward as decisive and active. France is also interested in pushing this march forward, since it has hosted the Palestinian and Israeli negotiators.
