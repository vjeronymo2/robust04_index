BFN [Text] Albanian Foreign Minister Alfred Serreqi made some provocative remarks from the floor of the Conference on Stability in Europe, being held in Paris. Serreqi spoke about the extreme nationalist stance taken by Greece, which is affecting the Greek minority in Albania and described the guidance, as he put it, given the North Ipirus citizens as unacceptable. Deputy Foreign Minister Yeoryios Papandreou, who met with Serreqi on the sidelines of the conference, reacted strongly. Papandeou protested the Albanian foreign minister's remarks and against the arrests of North Ipirotans. Serreqi denied that any arrests had taken place. Alternate Foreign Minister Theodhoros Pangalos and Papandreou described Albania's stance as paranoid. The Athens Television Network correspondent in Gjirokaster reports that more North Ipirotans were arrested last night and today, and were taken to court.
