BFN [Text] All Muslim-Bosniak prisoners in Mostar's Heliodrom prison, except two, today ended their hunger strike. At his first news conference today Vlado Sojic, new defense minister of the Croat republic of Herceg-Bosna, said, I quote, that he feels like a minister of peace rather than war as he was appointed at the time of the Washington agreement. Asked whether the war in Bosnia-Herzegovina was over he nevertheless said: [Begin Sojic recording] Unfortunately, the war is not over. [end recording] Of course, Sojic said, the Croat side will continue to do everything, just as it has been doing hitherto, to achieve peace through negotiations. He then called on his fighters to be patient enough until a final peace, given the fact that they have managed to save Croat territories and the Croat population. Replying to a question as to who started the war and whether the Croats are the losers, Sojic says: [Begin Sojic recording] The Croats did not start the war, nor are they the losers. As to what extent they are the winners, one can speculate in various ways. [end recording] Minister Sojic also added that the Croats had so far always accepted every peace agreement, ranging from the Cutillero agreement to this latest Redman one. Asked whether the Croats and Muslim can live together after so much bloodshed, Sojic says: [Begin Sojic recording] The, shall I say, memories of the war with the Muslims are still fresh, so it is hard to talk about coexistence, particularly so where there has been fighting, but I nevertheless believe that Croats and Muslims can live in a common state if it is organized in a democratic way and secures the national rights of all peoples. [passage omitted] [end recording]
