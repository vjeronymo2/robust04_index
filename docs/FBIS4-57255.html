BFN [Text] The president has cancelled a trip that he was to have made to the PRC beginning on 23 May. In a message to the PRC president, Franco said that he laments the cancellation, and reiterates his willingness to support cooperation projects between the two countries. Senator Jarbas Passarinho, Senator Jose Sarney, and Brazilian Ambassador to Portugal Jose Aparecido de Oliveira had advised Franco to avoid being absent from the country during the implementation of the real and while there are ongoing strikes in the public sector, according to presidential advisers. In addition, a presidential absence would be negative for the electoral process.
