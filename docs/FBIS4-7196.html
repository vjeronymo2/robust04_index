BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 28 KYODO -- Newly named Transport Minister Nobuaki Futami is a distinguished debater and member of the Buddhist-backed Komeito [Clean Government Party], succeeding Shigeru Ito of the Social Democratic Party (SDP) [of Japan -- SDPJ]. He rose swiftly through the ranks of Komeito, serving as political editor and an editor-in-chief of the party's newspaper KOMEI SHIMBUN before being elected to the House of Representatives from a constituency in Ibaraki Prefecture. The 59-year-old legislator is well-versed in a wide range of policy affairs. He chaired the party's Policy Affairs Council for three years. Futami has also been active in addressing environmental issues, particularly water pollution. He is popular among legislators, including members of the main opposition Liberal Democratic Party. He has a reputation for being lighthearted and sensitive. Born in Tokyo, Futami completed graduate school at the prestigious Waseda University. He is currently serving as vice chairman of Komeito. He has been elected to the powerful lower house for seven times.
