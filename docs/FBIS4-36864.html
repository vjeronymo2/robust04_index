BFN [Report by Sophia 'Amarah in Cairo] [Text] Cairo today bid farewell to a member of the Palestinian National Authority [PNA] formed to administer the Palestinian autonomous areas. Dr. Nabil Sha'th, who will be accompanied by Monte Carlo radio in the car that will take him from the Egyptian capital to his homeland -- where he will settle in Gaza, held a news conference in the Cairo this morning prior to his departure. [Begin recording] ['Amarah] Dr. Nabil Sha'th: Let us brush aside the issue of your return home. What are the major difficulties that you will confront at the start of your new mission in Gaza? [Sha'th] Our major difficulty will be with Israel. It lies in the implementation of the agreement signed with the Israelis, and the continuation of the negotiations, as provided for in the Declaration of Principles. There are still many issues that we and the Israelis need to work out and agree upon, including the release of all prisoners, the opening of a safe corridor between Gaza and Jericho, bringing the West Bank under the PNA's authority, and the repatriation of the persons displaced in 1967. We must immediately begin efforts to settle these issues, which were obscured by the Israeli [words indistinct]. Moreover, we must confront any problems the Israelis might create over Jerusalem. ['Amarah] Dr. Nabil Sha'th has also announced that Palestinian border guards will soon be deployed on the Allenby Bridge and the Rafah Border Post. He added that the Palestinians will shortly be allowed to move freely from the Gaza Strip to the Jericho area. With regard to the Islamic Resistance Movement, Hamas, Sha'th had the following to say: [Sha'th] We will not allow people bearing arms to move freely on the street. However, we will not storm houses to seize weapons. As we once fought the Israelis, we will not now allow the Israelis to move freely with their weapons on streets and roads, and to use them to open fire on the Palestinians. In the same vein, we will not allow the Palestinians to open fire on the Israelis. We will not allow any of us to turn into a judge, indictor, prosecutor, and executioner. We will not allow this to happen. There might be some collaborators with the occupation authorities. Those who have any complaints in this regard must head for the Palestinian police, provide evidence, and demand a trial of those suspected of collaboration. If everybody acts conscientiously and out of a sense of responsibility; that is, if everybody believes in Islam, in their leaders, in a constitution, and a host of principles, then he or she must grant officials and leaders the opportunity to continue the struggle in its various forms in light of what is deemed appropriate by the current phase, taking into account what the Muslim community deems best. I believe that the Palestinian people now have the opportunity to achieve their goals. If we were to fail in this, then nobody would blame us if we return to armed struggle. ['Amarah] On the other hand, Sha'th announced that Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat will arrive in the autonomous areas next week to reside in Jericho. In a related development, the Palestinian official affirmed that the PNA, which pledged to donor nations to achieve self-reliance within a year's time, is awaiting international aid modelled on the Marshal plan launched by the United States to aid the collapsing West European states in the wake of World War II, rather than a redemption operation similar to that of redeeming ruptured [word indistinct], as he put it. We will update you on relevant developments once we arrive in Gaza Strip. [end recording]
