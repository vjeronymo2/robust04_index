930216 FT 16 FEB 93 / UK Company News: Lincoln House in the black LINCOLN House, the USM-traded home furnishing group, returned to the black in 1992 with a pre-tax profit of Pounds 193,000 compared with the previous year's loss of Pounds 286,000 and a deficit of Pounds 2.35m in 1990. Operating profits amounted to Pounds 424,000 on sales of Pounds 7.3m; against losses of Pounds 40,000 on sales of Pounds 7.7m last time. Operating profits for discontinued businesses totalled Pounds 63,000 (Pounds 86,000) while losses of Pounds 55,000 were attributed to head office. Mr Ian Topping, chief executive, said the sale of Impala Displays and Mayers and Shaw to their managements reduced borrowings by some Pounds 800,000. Interest charges fell to Pounds 239,000 (Pounds 332,000). Earnings per share emerged at 1.2p against losses of 1.8p.
