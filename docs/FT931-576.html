930329 FT 29 MAR 93 / 'Crucial' talks today in Tyneside shipyard dispute MANAGEMENT and unions at a Tyneside ship repair company where 350 workers have been given redundancy notices after rejecting a cost-cutting pay deal meet today in an attempt to find common ground. The talks, described by management as 'crucial' and by the unions as 'make or break', are being held as the workers enter the sixth week of their 12-week notice period. They have been told that if they want to work for A & P Appledore Tyne beyond mid-May they must accept new terms of employment. The workers, who earlier this month staged a one-day unofficial strike, have since voted in a ballot by four to one for industrial action. The company, part of A & P Appledore Holdings, says it must cut costs at its Tyneside repair yards in North Shields and Wallsend because of the world recession and increasing competition. It says the yards made a loss last year. 'The very existence of the company is on the line,' it said. The package rejected by the hourly paid employees includes a pay freeze and reductions in other payments. The unions are especially opposed to proposals that temporary workers should be paid lower rates than permanent employees, and that entitlement to idle-time payments when lack of work results in layoffs should be reduced from the present maximum of 60 days a year. Mr David Blincow, managing director of A & P Appledore Tyne, said: 'We have to get about 10 per cent of costs out of the business.'
