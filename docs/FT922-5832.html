920528 FT 28 MAY 92 / The Cadbury Report: Men behind the decisions THE Committee on the Financial Aspects of Corporate Governance was officially formed a year ago at the instigation of the accountancy bodies and the Stock Exchange. Mr Michael Lickiss, a partner at Grant Thornton and then chairman of the Institute of Chartered Accountants, Sir Ron Dearing, chairman of the Financial Reporting Council, and Sir Andrew Hugh Smith, Stock Exchange chairman, were its founding members. After discussions with the government, a committee with representatives from industry, shareholder groups and the accountancy profession was formed under the chairmanship of Sir Adrian Cadbury. Other members were: Mr Ian Butler, a member of the CBI Council. Mr Jim Butler, senior partner, KPMG Peat Marwick. Mr Jonathan Charkham, adviser to the Governor of the Bank of England. Mr Hugh Collum, chairman, 100 Group of Finance Directors, and finance director at SmithKline Beecham. Mr Andrew Likierman, Professor of Accounting and Financial Control, London Business School. Mr Nigel Macdonald, vice-president, Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland. Mr Mike Sandland, chairman, Institutional Shareholders Committee. Mr Mark Sheldon, vice-president, Law Society. Sir Dermot de Trafford, IoD chairman. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- MAIN POINTS OF CADBURY REPORT ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Companies must disclose extent of compliance with Cadbury code to maintain a stock exchange listing ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Board authority divided between chairman and chief executive. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Pay packages of chairman and highest-paid UK director should be disclosed in annual accounts split into salary and performance-related pay ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Each board should have an adult committee 'wholly or mainly' composed of non-executives ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Interim financial statements should be discussed with auditors although they need not be formally audited. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Audit committees should meet at least once a year with external auditors and without the presence of executive directors. -----------------------------------------------------------------------
