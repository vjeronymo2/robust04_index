BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian guerrilla activities monitored through 25 May. On 11 May, 40 members of the Jaime Bateman Cayon column, a dissident group of the 19 April Movement, occupied for three and one-half hours a stretch of the Pan-American Highway between Ibague and Armenia, 10 km from La Linea. The guerrillas blew up the toll booth for the sixth time, stole 1.6 million pesos, and burned two vehicles in an action that left four people wounded. On 12 May, one guerrilla was killed and six arrested in Antioquia, Santander Department. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 13 May 94 p 8A) On 14 May, Army of National Liberation (ELN) members belonging to the Camilo Torres Front killed Oswaldo Arturo Pajaro Garcia, president of the Municipal Council of Aguachica, Cesar Department, and kidnapped Alderman Elibardo Galvis Barrera. In another incident, guerrillas suspected of belonging to the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia burned a Colombian Air Force plane which had made an emergency landing in Caqueta Department. The plane was attacked while the crew members were seeking assistance. Meanwhile, 40 ELN guerrillas kidnapped Sucre Mayor Juan Carlos Cure Ramirez, releasing him three hours later after having forced him to give them a financial report. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 18 May 94 p 8A) On 20 May, Colombian police rescued industrialist Joaquin Prada San Miguel, who was kidnapped 10 days earlier by ELN guerrillas in Santander Department. Five rebels were killed and several weapons were confiscasted during the operation. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2042 GMT 20 May 94)
