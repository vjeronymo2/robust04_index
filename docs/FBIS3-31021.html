Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 10 Feb (TANJUG) -- Vojislav Seselj, president of the Serbian Radical Party [SRS], said today that the Srpska republic should totally reject yesterday's NATO ultimatum. At a news conference Seselj stressed that the radicals advocate negotiations and a truce with the Muslim side on a reciprocal basis but NATO should not interfere. "If the Srpska republic and the Muslim Jamahiriyah [Bosnia-Herzegovina] agree on a cease-fire and the withdrawal of heavy weaponry, such an agreement should be respected, but NATO has no right to interfere," the SRS leader says. He welcomed Radovan Karadzic's statement that everyone who talks to Serbs in the language of ultimatums should consider the Austro-Hungarian empire's experience. Seselj said that the massacre in Sarajevo's marketplace was staged by the Muslim regime and that it evidently demonstrated that Muslim leader Alija Izetbegovic is a "religious fanatic" who does not think about human lives, as he wants to trigger foreign military intervention. In the event of an attack on Serb positions in Bosnia, the SRS leader said, every Serb's patriotic duty will be to resist this aggression. "We would regard such an attack in the same way we would regard aggression against Serbia, Montenegro, and the Serb republic of Krajina; in such a case the SRS would rally all volunteers from the SRS and Serbian chetnik movement and dispatch them to the territory of the Srpska republic." He noted that in this event all interparty squabbling should be relegated to the background. Asked about his personal view, Seselj said that in the event of an attack "one should pound all NATO's possible targets -- bases in Italy and other countries from which aircraft would be operating -- as well as UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] troops." "Air raids can achieve nothing. I believe this is how the Serbs will react, whereas we radicals will take part in this reaction," the SRS leader stressed.
