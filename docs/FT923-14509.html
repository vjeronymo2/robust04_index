920707 FT 07 JUL 92 / Observer: Bitter harvest Non-alcoholic fumes could soon be wafting Japanese businessmen into the land of nod. Suntory, the country's leading drinks producer, seeking a solution to the problem of sleeplessness caused by stress, has stumbled on a new nightcap. Scientists at its Institute for Fundamental Research in Osaka put a number of human guinea-pigs under pressure by giving them a maths test and keeping them 'in a state of arousal'. Their rooms were then filled with six different smells to see which most quickly soothed them to sleep. Although a slug of Suntory Royal whisky ought to have done the trick, a sniff of bitter orange proved most effective.
