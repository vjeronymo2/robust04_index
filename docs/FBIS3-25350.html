Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Government troops in Angola are said to have won back three towns in the country's northern province of Bengo. However the government newspaper, JORNAL DE ANGOLA, says government troops are under heavy guerrilla pressure in Caxito, some 50 km from the capital, Luanda. The paper said today that the northerly towns of Nnambuangongo, Ucua, and Ambriz fell to the army after fierce battles with UNITA [National Union for the Total Independence of Angola] rebels which began in mid-January, but it gave no casualty figures. Independent sources in Luanda said there was still heavy fighting in the border regions between Bengo, more than half of which is in government hands, and Uige Province, wholly held by UNITA. In the southernmost province of Huila, where army-rebel clashes have broken out, a military spokesman in the chief town, Lubango, said troops had recaptured the iron mining town of Jamba, after a five-day fight to dislodge UNITA units, which overran Jamba on 3 February.
