931206 FT 06 DEC 93 / 26% rise seen in consumer credit Consumer credit in the three months to the end of October was 26 per cent higher than in the same period last year, the Finance and Leasing Association says. While the figure for October at Pounds 1.084bn was 12 per cent down on September it was 21 per cent higher than in October 1992. The association's figures show that personal loans in October were 32 per cent higher than a year earlier. Infolink, the independent credit information organisation, says it found a 12.6 per cent year-on-year increase in loans for new cars in October.
