Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Gridneva] [Text] Dushanbe February 25 TASS -- The Tajik parliament ruled on Friday to prolong until July 1 the state of emergency in areas bordering on Afghanistan. The state of emergency was initially introduced in January 1993 and covers, in particular, the Shuroabad, Pyandzh, Shaartuz regions and the city of Khorog, the administrative centre of the Gorno-Badakhshan region. According to the parliamentary decree, the state of emergency was prolonged for the sake of "stabilisation of the political situation in the above-mentioned areas, safety of residents living there, as well as in connection with incessant armed provocations on the Tajik-Afghan border".
