Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The minister of petroleum resources, Chief Don Etiebet, has assured Nigerians that the decision of OPEC not to cut oil production would not adversely affect this year's budget. He gave the assurance in Geneva, Switzerland, yesterday while answering questions from newsmen shortly after the 12th meeting of the Ministerial Monitoring Committee of the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries. From Geneva, energy correspondent Alex Udoh reports on the outcome of the meeting: [Begin recording] [Udoh] In a communique issued at the end of their meeting, the ministers decided to maintain the present overall production ceiling of 24.52 million barrels of crude oil per day for the remaining part of the year 1994. In doing so, they pledged strict adherence to their individual allocated production levels of last year. The OPEC ministers said they would not tolerate any violation of the agreement, pointing out that necessary sanctions would be imposed on defaulting member countries. Briefing newsmen after the meeting, the minister of petroleum and mineral resources, Chief Don Etiebet, said Nigeria was in support of the decision reached at the meeting. Chief Etiebet said although Nigeria favored a cut in production as a means to firm up oil prices, that could not be borne in isolation. Chief Etiebet, however, gave detailed explanations on why the decision will not affect Nigeria's present budget.
