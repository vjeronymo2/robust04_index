BFN [Text] Tallinn, May 04, BNS -- A Council of Europe Parliamentary Assembly subcommittee delegation had meetings in Tallinn today with a deputy speaker of the Estonian parliament, members of the parliament's social affairs commission, Social Affairs Ministry officials. The delegation of the subcommittee for social rights under the Social, Health and Family Affairs Committee of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe includes representatives from the Netherlands, Germany, Britain, France, Italy and Ireland. Members of the Estonian parliament's social affairs commission, Social Security Department and Social Affairs Ministry officials at their meeting with the delegation spoke about the country's steps in working out a new pensions system to take into account also the wage level of each employee. The first step in the creation of the new system is a national employers registry, work on which was finished Tuesday [3 May], said Georg Mannik, the head of the Social Security Department. A transition to the new system is planned for the next year, he said. Toomas Vilosius, adviser at the Social Affairs Ministry, spoke about the Estonian health insurance system. Political situation in Estonia and the legislative activity were the main topics discussed at a meeting of the delegation with Estonian parliament's Deputy Speaker Tunne Kelam earlier today. Kelam spoke about Estonia's plans for further integration with Europe. The delegation members asked questions about various aspects of the legislation, the country's economic development and the problem of the retired Russian military, the parliament press office reports. A separate meeting with the chair of the parliamentary social affairs commission, Siiri Oviir, and visits to social welfare sector establishments in Tallinn were scheduled for the afternoon. The delegation will visit Narva for meetings with representatives of the northeastern city's Estonian and Russian communities, as well as visits to social security institutions Thursday.
