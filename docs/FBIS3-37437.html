Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Azerbaijan wants numerous provisions of the CIS Collective Security Treaty revised and updated, especially those covering threats to security of its signatories from within, as well as outside, the CIS. Azeri President Geydar Aliyev and Foreign Minister Hasan Hasanov made this known when they met with Aleksandre Chikvaidze, Georgian Foreign Minister, who had a stopover in Baku on his way to Iran. Internal conflicts, the Azeri leaders think, are likely to make the CIS an unviable organizations. Informed sources in the Azeri leader's headquarters told Interfax that economic cooperation between Georgia and Azerbaijan and the situation of Azeris in Georgia as well as cooperation between the two countries in the CIS framework had been discussed. An agreement was reached on opening a Georgian embassy in Baku and an Azeri embassy in Tbilisi.
