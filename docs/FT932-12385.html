930428 FT 28 APR 93 / World Stock Markets (America): Dow rallies as recent selling spree abates Wall Street THE RECENT selling spree on Wall Street ended yesterday afternoon in spite of continuing concern about the economic outlook, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial average closed 17.56 higher at 3,415.93 after posting losses through much of the day. The Standard & Poor's 500 was up 4.32 at 437.86, while the Nasdaq composite finished 6.65 ahead at 652.52. Trading volume on the New York SE amounted to 284m shares and rises narrowly outscored declines by 950 to 934. Stocks withstood pressure from continuing declines in US bond prices, with the treasury's bellwether 30-year bond sliding 29/32 to 102 27/32 , pushing its yield up to 6.895 per cent. Owens-Corning Fiberglass advanced Dollars 1 5/8 to Dollars 39 1/8 after saying it had cancelled a proposed public offering of 4.5m shares of common stock. The company blamed market conditions for its decision but said its plan 'to pursue its growth agenda remains unchanged'. Xerox tumbled Dollars 2 3/4 to Dollars 75 after announcing that US sales were hit in the first quarter by a reorganisation of the sales force. In the drugs sector, share prices benefited from reports that the Clinton administration may enter voluntary cost- control agreements with the industry, instead of imposing price controls without industry consent. Merck added Dollars 1 at Dollars 37 1/8 , Pfizer climbed Dollars 3 7/8 to Dollars 67 3/4 , Bristol-Myers Squibb appreciated Dollars 2 3/4 to Dollars 61 7/8 and Glaxo Holdings firmed Dollars 1/2 to Dollars 18 5/8 . Biotechnology issues also advanced in Nasdaq trading, Chiron adding Dollars 4 1/2 at Dollars 54 1/2 and Amgen Dollars 2 7/8 at Dollars 41. Bank shares recovered some of the ground lost on Monday when investors moved away after a strong run this year. Citicorp was up Dollars 1 1/8 at Dollars 26 7/8 , BankAmerica gained Dollars 1 1/8 at Dollars 45 3/8 , Banc One improved Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 53 3/4 and Chase Manhattan put on Dollars 1/2 at Dollars 30 5/8 . The transport segment also recouped some of its losses, with the Dow Jones Transportation average recovering 18.93 to 1,599.48 after dropping 37.47 on Monday. AMR, parent of American Airlines, ended Dollars 2 1/4 better at Dollars 69 1/4 , UAL, parent of United Airlines, climbed Dollars 7 1/8 to Dollars 146 1/2 and Delta Air Lines was ahead Dollars 2 3/8 at Dollars 58 3/8 . In Nasdaq trading, Intel regained Dollars 4 5/8 at Dollars 92 after falling Dollars 5 1/4 on Monday. Apple Computer moved forward Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 50 1/4 and Lotus Development rose Dollars 1 to Dollars 25. Canada THE TORONTO market ended mixed after active trading, having shown little reaction to the federal budget, released after Monday's close. The budget was criticised by business leaders for failing to include major spending cuts. The TSE 300 index was up a net 7.4 at 3,691.2 after spending the bulk of the day in negative territory, but falls led rises by 381 to 306. Volume fell to 63.3m shares from 81.5m. Nine of the 14 stock groups ended higher, but the energy and gold shares sectors relinquished some ground. Northern Telecom rose CDollars 1 1/4 to CDollars 44 3/8 after the telecommunication group posted earnings that were not as bleak as the market had feared.
