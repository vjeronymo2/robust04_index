930427 FT 27 APR 93 / Payout starts for Isle of Man bank THE FIRST batch of ex-gratia payments from the Isle of Man government was sent yesterday to those who lost money in the 1982 crash of the Savings and Investment Bank with debts of Pounds 42m. Payments were sent to 576 of the bank's 2,000 depositors. The Pounds 4.5m scheme, designed to help smaller depositors, will give those making satisfactory claims 50 per cent of their money, up to a maximum payment of Pounds 5,000. The cost of yesterday's payments was Pounds 1.4m and the total cost is expected to be Pounds 4.5m.
