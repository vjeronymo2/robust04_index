Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 20 Jan (MENA)--After President Mubarak received Guinean Foreign Minister Ibrahima Sylla today, Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa stated today that Mubarak handed Sylla a message answering the Guinean president. The foreign minister said bilateral relations between the two countries will continue, especially in light of recent developments in Guinea after presidential and legislative elections. Guinean Foreign Minister Ibrahima Sylla stated that President Mubarak, in his capacity as current president of the OAU, is very concerned about all conflicts in the region. He added that Guinean President Lansana Conte asked him to inform President Mubarak on the conflicts in that part of Africa, especially the problem in Liberia, which has started moving toward a solution. The Guinean foreign minister praised Egypt's extensive diplomatic activity on Middle Eastern and African problems. He noted that he exchanged opinions with 'Amr Musa on the situation in the Middle East and Africa and other issues in the international arena.
