Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan March 7 TASS -- The Karabakh conflict can be settled only by joint efforts of the international community which combine the peace initiative of the Conference for Security and Cooperation in Europe and Russia's mediatory initiatives, according to the concerted view expressed by Armenian officials and chairman of the CSCE's Minsk Group Jan Eliasson on the second, closing day of the visit by the Swedish diplomat and his colleagues to Armenia. The visiting group members are scheduled to meet Armenian President Levon Ter-Petrosyan and parliament members, as well as to visit Catholicos Vazgen II, the head of the Armenian Apostolic Church. Before leaving Yerevan for Vienna, CSCE delegation members are due to meet reporter at a press conference devoted to the results of their visit. On the first day of the visit, the CSCE group met Armenia's Minister of Foreign Affairs Vahan Papazyan. During the meeting Jan Eliasson told the Armenian minister about his impressions from a trip to Baku and Stepanakert and talks with the leaders of the warring sides -- Azerbaijan and Nagorno-Karabakh. According to the Swedish diplomat, people in the region are firmly convinced that the Karabakh conflict can not be settled by forcible methods. This view is shared by Baku and Stepanakert, the CSCE official said. Eliasson said the Minsk Group maintains contacts with the Russian president's personal envoy Vladimir Kazimirov. "The leaders of the CSCE's Minsk Group hope to find the key to the peaceful settlement of the conflict in close interaction with all parties concerned, including Russia," the diplomat said. For his part, the Armenian minister expressed the hope that the new leadership of the Minsk Group would work more effectively to identify the starting positions for setting in motion the process of preparations for the Minsk conference on Karabakh. Jan Eliasson also met Armenia's Defence Minister Serzhik Sarkisyan.
