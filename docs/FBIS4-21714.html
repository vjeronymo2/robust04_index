BFN [Text] Lima, 27 Mar (EFE) -- The Peruvian media reported today that the Mexican Government has asked Peru to help their anti-terrorist struggle. The Lima daily EXPRESO says that within the next few days two National Police Counterterrorism Directorate (Dincote) experts will travel to the Mexican capital with President Alberto Fujimori's authorization. EXPRESO cites high-ranking police sources who state that the officers are Colonel Juan Gonzalez Sandoval and Lieutenant Colonel Alberto Sarmiento Gutierrez. It adds that Gonzalez and Sarmiento will be in Mexico for at least 10 days. Mexican officials want to learn the Peruvian way of facing a possible upsurge of the terrorist phenomenon following the Chiapas rebellion and the death of presidential candidate Luis Donaldo Colosio.
