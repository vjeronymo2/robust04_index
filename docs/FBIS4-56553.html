BFN [From "Caracol's 0600 to 0900" newscast] [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota -- Cuban President Fidel Castro has reiterated his government's respect for Colombia's self-determination, affirming that he does not interfere in Colombian internal affairs and saying that he does not support any guerrilla activities. Early this morning, President Castro spoke for four hours with Foreign Minister Noemi Sanin de Rubio who arrived in Cuba on an official visit. Castro said that trade relations with Colombia are at their best level, adding: Our foreign minister reiterated that President Cesar Gaviria's government is willing to sell Cuba the oil it needs, as long as negotiations meet the demands of Colombian oil authorities. Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina, who accompanied Colombian Foreign Minister Sanin de Rubio, emphasized the changes made in Cuba's economy and said that the government will encourage the necessary changes, as long as they respect its autonomy, which, he said, is not negotiable.
