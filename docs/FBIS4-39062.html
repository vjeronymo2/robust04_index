BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Zlobin] [Text] Petrozavodsk, 4 Jun -- The head of the Karelian government, Viktor Stepanov, has issued an instruction halting the privatization in Karelia of buildings and installations that are the republic's state property. Leasing them out has also been banned. These measures are temporary, the document says. They have been taken to bring order to the questions of privatization. A special commission has been set up to check the use of buildings that are state property. The commission has been given until 15 July to present proposals on correcting the privatization process in Karelia.
