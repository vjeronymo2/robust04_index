Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, Jan 17 (AFP) -- Communists and reformers of the Yabloko coalition will dominate key Russian parliamentary committees after a vote in the Duma on Monday [17 January] sidelined ultra-nationalists into less important technical groups. Right-wing firebrand Vladimir Zhirinovsky was kept off the list of chairmen while one of his supporters was elected to head a specially established commission on geopolitical affairs. The deputies approved by 290 to 63 votes with eight abstentions a package of positions that gave the key legislative, security, social and religious commissions to communists. Reformists were elected to the vital budget and foreign affairs commissions while Zhirinovsky's Liberal Democratic Party took the commission for industry, transport energy and public works and a group that will look into natural resources.
