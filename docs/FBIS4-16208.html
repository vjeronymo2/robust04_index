BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow, 13 Apr -- A federal law "On the status of a Federation Council deputy and the status of a State Duma deputy of the Federation Council of the Russian Federation" was adopted today as a whole by the State Duma. This law defines the rights, obligations, and responsibility of Federation Council and State Duma deputies of the first convocation, and envisages the basic legal and social guarantees of the work of elected deputies. The law notes, among other things, that Federation Council and State Duma deputies enjoy immunity in the course of the whole of their term of office. They cannot be subjected to criminal or administrative responsibility submitted in legal form, detained, arrested, or subjected to search or interrogation without the consent of the appropriate Federal Assembly chamber -- apart from instances of their detention at the scene of a crime -- or subjected to personal inspection, with the exception of instances where this is provided for by a federal law to ensure the security of other persons. A deputy's immunity extends to his place of domicile and work, his luggage, his private and company transport, his correspondence, the means of communications for his personal use, and also documents belonging to him. A Russian Federation Federal Assembly deputy may not be subjected to criminal and administrative proceedings for a view or a position expressed during voting, including at the time of the expiry of his term of office. This situation does not extend to instances where a deputy has made public insults or committed slander and other violations, the responsibility for which is covered by federal legislation.
