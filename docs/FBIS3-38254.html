Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- In January the number of resettlers who came to Germany declined strongly compared with the previous month. According to an announcement by the Federal Interior Ministry on Tuesday [1 February], 18,827 persons -- including 18,349 Russian Germans -- moved to the FRG. In December, the overall number was 27,043. According to the Ministry, the number of applications for a notification of admission continues to decline: In January, 22,517 ethnic Germans applied for resettlement; in the same month last year there were 28,731 applications. Horst Waffenschmidt (Christian Democratic Union), parliamentary state secretary in the Interior Ministry, spoke of a "calming down": Obviously, the Germans now also see prospects for the future in the settlement areas in the Russian Federation. The FRG Government will continue to make efforts for improving living conditions there. However, the success primarily depends on the course of the restructuring process in the CIS states.
