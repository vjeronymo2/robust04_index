BFN [By BUSINESS-TASS for TASS] [Text] Moscow June 8 TASS -- The production of oil with gas condensate in Russia during the first five months of 1994 has come to 124.4 million tonnes, a drop of 15.2 percent in comparison with the same period of 1993, Ministry of Fuel and Energy told BUSINESS- TASS. The largest quantity of oil -- 77.6 million tonnes -- was extracted by the state enterprise "Rosnephth" during January-May, 1994. The oil company "Lukoil" produced 17.5 million tonnes, the "Surgutnephtegaz" 14.4 million tonnes and the "Yukos" 11.3 million tonnes. The gas production came to 266.5 billion cubic metres, a fall of 2.1 percent. The "Gazprom" extracted 250.6 billion cubic metres of gas, and oil industry enterprises a total of 12.8 billion cubic metres. The production of coal during this period was 117.6 million tonnes, a drop of 10.5 percent. Oil supplies to former Soviet republics accounted for 36.8 million tonnes, a rise of 10.5 percent. Gas exports came to 44.8 billion cubic metres, increasing by 3.6 percent.
