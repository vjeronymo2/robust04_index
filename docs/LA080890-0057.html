August 8, 1990, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

STAGE REVIEW;

ANAHEIM TROUPE CLIMBS EVERY MOUNTAIN OF A CLASSIC

There's a production of "The Sound of Music" here acted entirely by people 5 to 18 years old. The auditorium is stuffy, and you sit on a folding chair. How good can it be?

Pretty darned good.

Director Dan Halkyard has created a show with spontaneity and an admirable absence of chaos, considering the two large alternating casts (80 children in all) and the jitters of a second consecutive opening night.

Halkyard uses the small stage smoothly, relying on a red velvet curtain and the talents of his young players to evoke the sweep and romance of this epic tale of the singing Von Trapps, who escaped the Third Reich, on foot, over the Alps.

His players are up to the challenge. Jennifer Schlotterbeck, a high school freshman, is the right age to play the flibbertigibbit postulate Maria. Because her singing is lovely and confident, her acting warm and earnest, she is a winning leading lady. In Patrick Johnson as Capt. Von Trapp, she has a strong and handsome leading man.

Scott Staudt as the luxury-loving Max Detweiler and Laurie O'Remus as the disapproving Sister Berthe both capture moments of real humor. Jannene Lowry does very well as the ever-wise Mother Abbess, bringing gorgeous pitch and plenty of power to "Climb Every Mountain," a big, daunting anthem.

In fact, all the young people playing adults, including Dyan Hobday as heiress Elsa Schraeder, realize their roles admirably. They're a bit stiff as grown-ups, but then being grown-up is a tough act at any age.

The kids who play kids do well too. The Von Trapp children, all seven, are charming. And the marionettes who show up in "The Lonely Goatherd" add a hilarious touch of improvisation, because you know something's always going happen any time you put 19 children and 40 strings together in a small space. The other cast looks promising just from the photos in the program.

There are some glitches in the sound system (the show is miked with recorded orchestral accompaniment). But who can resist a dozen serious-faced, miniature nuns, even if they are only singing along with the record?

'THE SOUND OF MUSIC'

An Anaheim Cultural Arts Center Center Stage production of Rodgers and Hammerstein's musical, directed by Dan Halkyard, with Jennifer Schlotterbeck, Jannene Lowry, Patrick Johnson, Susanna Johnson, Dyan Hobday, Scott Staudt, Rachel Pfeifer, Stephanie Haymond, William Ticknor, Glory Gray, Claudine Francois and Jared Pfeifer. Vocal Director: Ann Graber. Costumes: Laurie Holden. Scenic Artist: Patrina Pfeifer. Tickets: $8 for adults, $6 for seniors and students. Continues Thursday through Aug. 12 at 7:30 p.m. at 931 N. Harbor Blvd., Anaheim. (714) 491-7144.
