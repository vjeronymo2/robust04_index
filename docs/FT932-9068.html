930515 FT 15 MAY 93 / UK Company News: Sleepy Kids seeks Pounds 1.2m as losses rise Sleepy Kids, the USM-quoted creator and developer of animated cartoons, yesterday reported increased losses for 1991-92 and at the same time called on shareholders for Pounds 1.2m. The deficit for the 12 months to end-October widened from Pounds 155,927 to Pounds 356,243 at the pre-tax level after taking account of exceptional provisions of Pounds 170,251 (Pounds 58,079). The fully underwritten rights issue is of 7.49m new ordinary shares at 18p. The shares closed 1p lower at 26p. Some Pounds 900,000 of the net proceeds of the issue will be used to fund the group's participation in the production of the animated series of Budgie the Little Helicopter. The balance will provide additional working capital. The directors expect the Budgie series, created from stories by the Duchess of York, to be available by April 1994. Negotiations for a production agreement have reached an advanced stage for the animation of the series with Fred Wolf Films. It is proposed that Sleepy Kids provide up to maximum Dollars 1.3m (Pounds 840,000) towards the overall cost of production.
