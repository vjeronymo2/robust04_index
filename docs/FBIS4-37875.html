BFN [Text] Sudanese Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih has declared that the government supports Yemeni unity, and calls for an end to fighting between brothers to preserve the gains and resources of the brotherly Yemeni people. He pointed out the need for dialogue to preserve Yemeni unity. In an interview with AL-SUDAN AL-HADITH, published today, Dr. Abu-Salih added that Sudan is endeavoring to achieve Arab and Islamic solidarity and transcend differences, while directing its attention against designs aimed at destroying Arab unity. He explained that Sudan had taken some earnest steps in this respect, through dialogue and adherence to initiatives directed at improving relations among Arab states, the Gulf Cooperation Council and the Arab Maghreb states. The foreign minister declared that a number of western states have begun to make contacts with Sudan to restore their previous relations. He pointed to the change in relations with the U.S. Government after the visit of Madeline Albright. He added that those changes came as a result of the realization by those states of Sudan's firm stands toward respecting the sovereignty of states, and noninterference in their internal affairs.
