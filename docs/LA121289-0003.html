December 12, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

PHILIPPINE COUP ATTEMPT

As one of the many Filipino immigrants living here in Los Angeles, I am deeply saddened by the recent military attempt in the Philippines.

However, I feel that the Filipino people and the world should not totally discredit Aquino. People must understand that the country's economy was at its lowest possible point when Aquino took over. The economy has improved by 4% since she became president. It took 20 long years for the Filipino people to react to all of Marcos' political wrongdoings and corruption. The least they can do now to contribute to the country's stability is to give Aquino a break and a little more time.

A coup attempt like this only puts the economy further down and discourages foreign investors from doing business in the Philippines. What Filipinos should work on is the establishment of discipline and unity as a people. There can be no growth and progress without these vital factors.

SOCKIE CARRIEDO

Torrance
