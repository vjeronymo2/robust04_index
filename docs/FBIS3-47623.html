Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 14 (YONHAP) -- The Korea Maritime Institute (KMI) said Friday it has accepted a proposal by the Chinese State Planning Commission's Institute of Comprehensive Transportation for a joint study of traffic and transportation in Northeast Asia. Experts from the two institutes will discuss details of the study of transportation of cargo between South Korea and China and developing transportation networks in inland China in Beijing this month. Effective transportation by air, sea and land between the two countries, and the development of a better transportation system in China are the aims. China will hand necessary materials to KMI, which will share the experience that Korea has with economic development since the 1960s. Seoul has a deep interests in expanding the shipment of cargo to Europe via the trans-China railroad and in building a rail link between Korea and China after unification of the Korean peninsula. Korean and Chinese experts agree that a satisfactory traffic system does not exist in Northeast Asia, unlike Europe, so a joint study is necessary, KMI researcher Chong Pil-su said, and the joint study on traffic between Korea and China should be expanded to all Northeast Asia. The Institute of Comprehensive Transportation, built in 1959, is the largest transportation research institute in China and has nearly 100 researchers.
