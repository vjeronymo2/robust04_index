Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on narcotics developments in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific monitored by Bangkok Bureau between 14 and 20 March 1994. Source information is given in parentheses after each item.

SOUTH ASIA
Pakistan
Customs Seizes Hashish Near Peshawar
Customs officials seized 7,380 kg of hashish near Peshawar on 20 March. The drugs were found on board a truck travelling from the tribal areas near the Afghan border to Karachi. The driver and two accomplices were arrested. (Hong Kong AFP in English 1406 GMT 20 Mar 94)
SOUTHEAST ASIA
Philippines
"Shabu" Dealer Arrested in Rizal
Philippine National Police in Rizal arrested Arcadio Tolentino, 47, "one of the most wanted shabu dealers in Metro Manila and Rizal," during an 11 March sting operation. Tolentino was reportedly distributing drugs to different schools in Rizal and Metro Manila. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 12 Mar 94 p 12)
Narcotics Command Arrests Drug Trafficker in Manila
The Philippine National Police Narcotics Command has arrested a drug trafficker operating in Metro Manila. The suspect was identified as Alberto Palorina of Kapitolyo, Pasig. He is the brother-in-law of Ric Katahan, a drug lord killed by government authorities last year. Officials confiscated 20 grams of methaphetamine hydrochloride and a .22 caliber pistol during the raid on his house. (Quezon City PTV-4 Television Network in Tagalog 0500 GMT 14 Mar 94)
Authorities Conduct Antidrug Operation in Cagayan
Police and intelligence operatives in Tuguegarao, Cagayan have conducted an antidrug operation called "Operation Thunderbolt." They arrested a government employee caught in the act of selling 10,000 Philippine pesos worth of methaphetamine hydrochloride, and also burned about 15,000 fully-grown marijuana plants at a plantation in Kalinga-Apayao. (Manila MANILA BULLETIN in English 15 Mar 94 p B-21)
Thailand
Singaporean Arrested at Bangkok Airport for Heroin Possession
Singaporean national Tan Wun Hong Chu-eng, 25, was arrested with 3.700 kg of heroin at Bangkok's international airport on 12 March. He was boarding an Ethiopian Airlines flight bound for Ethiopia when a team of Customs and Narcotics Suppression Police searched him and found the heroin hidden in his travel bag. (Bangkok SIAM RAT in Thai 13 Mar 94 p 3)
Two Italians Arrested With Heroin, Syringes
Italian siblings Gianni Labella, 49, and Mariafia Labella, 47, were arrested in their room in Bangkok's Metro 21 Hotel on 11 March. Officials found 80 tubes of heroin and 23 syringes hidden in a clothes bag. The suspects said they bought the drugs in Bangkok for $3,000. (Bangkok BANGKOK POST in English 15 Mar 94 p 3; Hong Kong AFP in English 0348 GMT 15 Mar 94)
