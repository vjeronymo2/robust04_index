930618 FT 18 JUN 93 / International Capital Markets: Mexico to sell 70% of state insurance group THE Mexican government is to sell 70 per cent of Aseguradora Mexicana (Asemex), the state insurance group, Reuter reports from Mexico City. An initial package of 2.55m 'A' shares, 51 per cent of the insurer's equity, is to be sold into the private sector. A further 19 per cent is to be offered as an option to whoever wins the first tranche of shares. The finance ministry said the government would retain a minority 30 per cent stake in the company. Parties interested in buying Asemex would be required to deposit 75m new pesos in Mexican treasury bills, known as Cetes. Bids have to be in by September 10 and the result of the auction will be announced by September 27. Foreign investors can not hold more than 30 per cent of Asemex.
