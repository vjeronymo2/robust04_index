BFN [Unattributed report: "Neither Military Bases Nor Accession"] [Text] "Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] wishes to join the program `Partnership for Peace,' since under the conditions of historical passion and grand appetites that have been ruling the Balkans, and Macedonia being a small country, it sees its security in this NATO program," said President Gligorov in his interview for Belgrade weekly EKONOMSKA POLITIKA. In his interview, Mr. Gligorov speaks extensively also of the Greek embargo, pointing out that its aim is for Macedonia to accept the conditions given by Athens, which leads to identity loss of a nation and to destabilization of the region. He rejects the thesis that the Americans want Macedonian military bases to be given up to them in compensation for $1 million. He also decidedly rejects the thesis that Washington requests Macedonia to join any of the state creation that are being formed on the territory of former Yugoslavia.
