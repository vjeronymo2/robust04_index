921201 FT 01 DEC 92 / Presidential reform urged President Francois Mitterrand last night proposed a slight reduction in the term of French presidents from seven to 'at least 'six years, as part of a constitutional reform that would also give more power to prime ministers and more scope for decision by referendum, writes David Buchan in Paris. But in a letter to France's two parliamentary chambers and constitutional court, Mr Mitterrand proposed no timetable for the changes and gave no indication that he intended to apply any reduction in presidential tenure to himself.
