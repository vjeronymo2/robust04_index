Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The president, Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari, has said the Kashmir resolution at the UN Human Rights Commission has greatly helped in internationalizing the issue and in molding the international opinion in support of the Kashmiris' cause. Talking to newsmen in Islamabad today, he said the resolution on violations of human rights in occupied Kashmir was moved by Pakistan at an international forum after a long time and it helped in highlighting the Kashmir issue. He was sure that its results would be positive in future. The president said the Kashmir freedom struggle has been going on since long, which the people of the occupied state are waging against Indian oppression and occupation [sentence as heard]. He said Pakistan is extending all the political, moral, and diplomatic support to them. In reply to a question, Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari said it will be his effort and endeavor that on all matters which pertain to Pakistan's security interests, national interests, and other vital interests, including Kashmir, there must be a bipartisan approach. He said in the interest of the Kashmiri struggle, there should be a consensus on the Kashmir policy. He said that he has tried in the past and will do so in future to create better coordination between the government and the opposition. To a question, he said as the president of Pakistan, all political parties are to equal to him.
