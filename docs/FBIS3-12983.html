Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Interview with Deputy Foreign Minister Yosi Beilin by Umberto De Giovannangeli in Jerusalem; date not given: "A Rapid Israeli Withdrawal -- Only in This Way Can We Save Peace" -- first paragraph is L'UNITA introduction] [Text] Jerusalem -- "Despite all the current difficulties, I believe that the handshake between Rabin and 'Arafat represents a point of no return. We must get back to the negotiations table as soon as possible: because I am convinced that this time we will succeed in resolving all the issues that still remain open." Israeli Deputy Foreign Minister Yosi Beilin is certain of this. The hopes of peace in the Middle East have not faded. [De Giovannangeli] In your opinion do the margins for a resumption in negotiations between Israel and the PLO exist for the immediate future as well as for an acceleration in the withdrawal of the Israeli Army from Gaza and Jericho? [Beilin] In the last sessions of negotiations with the PLO we dealt with practically all the fundamental issues relating to the implementation of the declaration of principles signed in Washington. We were on the point of closing this phase of talks. Then the Hebron massacre took place. Nevertheless, I am convinced that if we return to negotiate quickly, which I think is possible, we will succeed in speeding up the implementation of autonomy in the Gaza Strip and Jericho. [De Giovannangeli] At what point are diplomatic discussions on the possible deployment of international observers or a multinational military contingent in the occupied territories? [Beilin] The problem has already been raised in Oslo and an accord with the PLO has been reached on an international presence in Gaza and Jericho during the transitory phase of autonomy. The Palestinians should understand the novelty and the importance of this "yes" by Israel, a fact that is without precedent in our history. [De Giovannangeli] A civilian presence or even a military presence? [Beilin] Civilian, only civilian. [De Giovannangeli] The position of Syria is crucial in achieving overall peace in the Middle East. Can you tell us if something is moving in negotiations with Damascus, even if officially interrupted. [Beilin] There is no doubt that Syria today still maintains a central role in the Middle East scenario: Peace with President al-Asad would mean really turning over a new leaf in this tormented region. But peace as defined by Syria is still a limited "armored" peace that is dictated more by necessity than by conviction. [De Giovannangeli] Instead what kind of peace does Israel propose? [Beilin] Total peace, without any restrictions on the economic, political, and diplomatic relations front. If Syria is willing to talk about peace in these terms, on our side we could easily open discussions on the schedule and the extent of our withdrawal from the Golan Heights. [De Giovannangeli] In these days, the usual proclamations of war seem to be accompanied by some signs of flexibility from the Palestinian refusal front, and even from the Hamas integralists. Do you think that these signs are credible? [Beilin] Absolutely not. Hamas has gained consensus not because of the strength of his ideas but because of the poverty and the profound hardship that marks the life of Palestinians today, particularly in the Gaza Strip. For this I consider it crucial to accelerate the process of autonomy. I am convinced that when the economy of the territories becomes buoyant and when the Palestinian police force is formed, many will pass over to the side of the PLO. I believe that the effort of 'Arafat, also in view of the elections in Gaza and Jericho, will be to form a coalition among the moderate elements. At least this is what I hope. [De Giovannangeli] Are you optimistic, pessimistic, or "uncertain" over the likelihood of a resumption in peace negotiations? [Beilin] Despite everything, I am confident about the future of the negotiations. For a very simple reason: because I do not see any other path to follow. There is no alternative to dialogue. The choice of peaceful cohabitation, which lies at the basis of the Washington accords and which unites the majority of the Israelis and Palestinians remains irreversible, and no terrorist act can place this under discussion again. [De Giovannangeli] The Jewish settlements in the territories, which the Palestinians are asking to be dismantled even in light of the Hebron massacre, are an obstacle on the road to peace today. [Beilin] The Oslo accords postponed discussions on the settlements to the final phase of negotiations. And for us this still remains valid. It does not mean, however, that we will not act to stop the colonization and above all to suppress the terrorist acts of extremist groups. [De Giovannangeli] But disarming the colonists and dismantling, even if this is gradual, the settlements are requests that have also been advanced by the Meretz ministers, to whom Rabin seems to want to respond by opening to the Tsomet of the right. [Beilin] Personally, I hope that the Tsomet will not enter the government. However, even if this were to happen, the peace process will not be interrupted and neither will the decision to negotiate with the PLO under the terms sanctioned by the accord on Gaza and Jericho be questioned again.
