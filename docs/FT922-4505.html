920605 FT 05 JUN 92 / Observer: Swiss courage Switzerland's reputation for security and stability has taken knocks lately. But Zurich Insurance, one of the biggest of its kind in Europe, wants to leave no doubt about its continuing strength. Asked how it would shape up to the most damaging sequence of disasters imaginable, chief executive Rolf Huppi was pat with the answer. The worst he could think of, he replied, was the California coast sliding into the ocean followed by a massive earthquake in Tokyo, a hurricane sweeping up the US eastern seaboard, and torrential storms swamping western Europe. 'Then we would flinch,' he said . . .but hastily added: 'I don't think it would kill us."
