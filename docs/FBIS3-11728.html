Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Ivonne Romero; from the "Upon Awakening" newscast] [Text] Canadian Prime Minister Jean Chretien said today Canada will continue to negotiate and invest in Mexico despite the tragic death of Luis Donaldo Colosio Murrieta, presidential candidate of the Institutional Revolutionary Party, on 23 March. [Begin Chretien recording, in English] We must not allow violence to prevail. We must not give in to democracy's enemies. We must not allow this tragic event to turn back the clock of democracy and economic progress in Mexico. [end recording] During a news conference in Mexico City, the Canadian prime minister explained he met with President Carlos Salinas de Gortari at Los Pinos on Wednesday evening. He said they both agreed the development of democracy and economic opportunities must continue. Chretien also issued an invitation to Mexican and foreign businessmen. [Begin Chretien recording, in English] You must not let last night's tragedy change your plans to do business in Mexico. Keep investing in this country, keep building the Mexican economy, keep working toward a more open and democratic society. [end recording]
