October 15, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

Radio Days (Channel 13 Sunday at 8 p.m.): Growing up in Brooklyn, dreaming of Manhattan, heroes and heroines of the radio. Another wonderful Woody Allen fantasy-reminiscence. (2:00)

Blowup (TMC Sunday at 2:45 a.m.; Wednesday at 6 p.m.): Michelangelo Antonioni's contemporary classic: murder, malaise and photography in swinging London. (1:51)

Mephisto (A&E Tuesday at 6 & 10 p.m.): Istvan Szabo's 1981 Oscar-winner: a vibrant portrait of an opportunist actor (Klaus Maria Brandauer) in Nazi Germany, based on Klaus Mann's novel. (2:15)

Victor/Victoria (Channel 5 Wednesday at 7:30 p.m.): Julie Andrews as a chanteuse in a chaos of cross-dressing in 1930s Paris: prime Blake Edwards' comedy from a 1933 German original. (2:30)

Hour of the Star (Channel 28 Saturday at 10 p.m.): The story of a plain Sao Paolo typist who dreams of glamour, made into a powerful portrait of the urban underclass by Suzana Amaral. (2:00)
