Language: German Article Type:BFN [Herbert Kremp commentary: "Europe Is Expanding"] [Text] The inclusion of three Scandinavian states and Austria is changing the political and intellectual-cultural prospects of the European Union [EU]. The imbalance toward the south is disappearing and, with the accession of the north and the east, the touch of Carolingian stuffiness is waning. Since countries with tested national sovereignty are involved in the further decisions, the form of a federation of states, of cooperation of national states and their parliaments, is beginning to dominate. The old-established members of the EC scene in Brussels have long predicted this development with regret, and they are not wrong: With the geographic and mental expansion, the political "deepening" of the Union becomes a rhetorical postulate without any prospect for realization. At least the Scandinavians do not have any inclination toward integration in the form of a federal state. We are facing the end of the late romantic period. With Finland, the Union gets 1,300 km of Russian border and the proximity of the Baltic states -- with all the flair and all the risk. In the case of Sweden, Norway, and Austria, the single market, that is, the real economic and trade policy advantage of the community, has the greatest attractiveness. However, particularly Finland and Sweden hope for an increase in security, at least in political support. With Austria, the German language section of the EU is growing. However, more important is its function as a bridge to Hungary, Slovenia, and Croatia. Here, too, the Union, which has so far been oriented toward West Europe, is going to receive a fruitful closeness to the East. Until the date of accession at the upcoming turn of the year, several obstacles -- both in substance and in terms of referendums -- have to be overcome. These apply mainly to Norway and Austria. In both countries the result of the referendums is uncertain; in Finland and Sweden it is considered relatively certain. Germany has pushed the membership negotiations forward with great energy. As a result, It is natural and unavoidable that it has not gained just friends in the old European Community.
