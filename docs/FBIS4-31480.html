BFN [Text] Warsaw, June 9 -- Romanian Defence Minister Gheorghe Tinca proposed three groups of issues on which Polish and Romanian military should work in further contacts at a meeting with his Polish counterpart Piotr Kolodziejczyk on Thursday, the office of the spokesman for the National Defence Ministry said Thursday. Kolodziejczyk and Tinca, who started his three-day official visit to Poland on Thursday, stressed great political importance they attached to the meeting. The first group of issues covers cooperation as part of Partnership for Peace, with a special regard to the presence of observers and sub-units at exercises. Minister Tinca proposed participation of the platoon of the Polish alpine troops in joint exercises in Romania. The second group would embrace cooperation in armament and between arms industries of the two countries. As for this area, the Romanian minister proposed to set up a mixed military committee to precisely specify Polish-Romanian needs in these areas. The third group includes mutual consultations on security, developments in Europe, the exchange of training cadres and joint seminars on political questions. Minister Kolodziejczyk said that all proposals made by the Romanian minister corresponded very well to the Polish understanding of the policy of security and cooperation. He added that Poland would be very open in all these areas. On Friday, the Romanian defence minister is to be received by the Polish president, the Sejm speaker and the prime minister.
