March 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

LOOK FOR THE SPRING ISSUE OF TRAVELING IN STYLE TODAY

Don't miss the spring issue of the Traveling in Style magazine in this week's Los Angeles Times. Keeping readers on the edge of their seats, journalist Jimmy Breslin recalls a spur-of-the-moment trip to Central America with an expired passport. Best-selling mystery author Mary Higgins Clark shares the joys of her hideaway in Cape Cod. Times Travel Editor Jerry Hulse, with intimate, on-site reporting, introduces us to Britain's most celebrated stately home now operated by the National Trust as a magnificent hotel for connoisseurs looking for the finest in accommodations and service.

Other contributors are astrologer Sydney Omarr, who predicts what the stars say about your traveling plans this year. Dr. Harvey Ruben gives pros and cons about couples taking separate vacations, and Jennifer Merin takes us on a shopping spree to some of the most splendid emporiums in the world. Through the camera's eye of Times photographer Iris Schneider, travel aboard the Trans-Siberian Express on a three-day journey across the Soviet Union. And there's much more.

Look for the Traveling in Style magazine in this Sunday's Los Angeles Times. BETTY HUGHES
