920410 FT 10 APR 92 / Election 1992: Election night countdown 10.00: BBC Election '92 predicts hung parliament, with the Conservatives 10 short of an overall majority. 10.05: ITN/Harris poll shows Labour and the Conservatives much closer in marginal constituencies. 10.29: Mrs Margaret Thatcher, questioned by the BBC on her return from US. says: 'It looks hopeful. I am quite optimistic.' 10.38: Responding to the BBC's exit poll, shadow environment secretary Mr Bryan Gould said the result showed the biggest swing to Labour since 1945. 10.53: Stockbroking service ShareLink reported brisk business following the exit polls, with investors buying across the board. 11.07: First results: Labour holds Sunderland South (2 per cent swing to Labour as Chris Mullin is re-elected). Rupert Allason holds Torbay for Conservatives. 11.23: Tory David Amess holds marginal Basildon with an increased majority. Swing to Labour is 1.3 per cent. 11.37: First recount - at Portsmouth South. The Tories later held the seat by 242 votes. 00.03: Wrexham result shows Labour holds seat with increased majority and 2.4 per cent swing from Conservative to Labour. 00.14: Liberal Democrats capture Cheltenham from Conservatives. Mr Nigel Jones, Lib Dem victor, denies the race issue had helped to defeat Mr John Taylor, black Conservative candidate. 00.27: Labour captures Nuneaton from the Conservatives with a 7 per cent swing. 00.34: Mr David Mellor, chief secretary to the Treasury, first member of the cabinet to be re-elected, with an increased majority at Putney, London. 00.45: Mr Dave Nellist, (pictured) who was expelled from Labour party for his alleged links with Militant, narrowly defeated by official Labour candidate in Coventry South East. 00.54: Scottish National party suffers stinging reverse as Mr Jim Sillars, deputy leader, loses his Govan seat - recaptured by Labour. 01.09: First ministerial casualty. Mr Michael Fallon, junior education minister, defeated in Darlington. Five per cent swing from Conservative to Labour. 01.20: Mr Edward Heath, former prime minister, re-elected as Conservative MP for Old Bexley and Sidcup and becomes father of the House of Commons. 01.26: Michael Forsyth, controversial Scottish health minister, holds Stirling. 01.38: Mr Chris Patten, Tory party chairman, loses Bath - the third Liberal Democrat gain of the night. 01.49: Overseas development minister Lynda Chalker loses her Wallasey seat on Merseyside to Labour, but a Conservative majority of five seats is predicted. 01.51: Plaid Cymru wins Ceredigion and Pembroke North by unseating Liberal Democrat Mr Geraint Howells. 01.55: Labour gains Ellesmere Port and Neston and Barrow in Furness
