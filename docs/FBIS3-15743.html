Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 05, BNS -- The European Union [EU] in a statement welcomed progress in the talks between Latvia and Russia on the Skrunda radar base, the DIENA daily newspaper reported with reference to the Latvian Embassy in Brussels. The agreement foresees the radar base at Skrunda, Latvia, to operate under Russian command during the next 4 years and to be then dismantled within 18 months. The EU in the statement says that the temporary agreement on the Skrunda base will contribute to progress in Latvian-Russian talks on the withdrawal of Russian troops. The EU regards the agreement as containing no threat to Latvia's sovereignty, and calls for approval of the document. The good will demonstrated during the talks gives hope that good- neighborly relations will be established between Latvia and Russia, the EU statement says.
