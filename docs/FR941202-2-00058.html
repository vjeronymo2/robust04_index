Background The National Housing Act (12 U.S.C. 1701&hyph;1749aaa&hyph;5) (the Act) authorizes and governs the Department's mortgage insurance program. Section 221 of the Act permits certain HUD-insured mortgagees to assign the original credit instrument and the mortgage securing the credit instrument to the Secretary of HUD. Section 221(g)(4)(A) sets forth the two requirements for mortgage assignment. The commitment to insure must have been entered into prior to November 30, 1983. Secondly, the mortgage must not be ``in default at the expiration of twenty years from the date the mortgage was endorsed for insurance.'' Section 221(g)(4)(C), which was added by section 336 of the National Affordable Housing Act of 1990 (Pub. L. 101&hyph;625, approved November 28, 1990), authorizes the Secretary to conduct a multifamily project mortgage auction in lieu of accepting the mortgage assignments. Section 221(g)(4)(C)(ii)(IV) of the Act states that the Secretary ``[s]hall accept the lowest interest rate bid for purchase that the Secretary deems acceptable.'' Section 221(g)(4)(C)(ii)(IV) also mandates that ``[t]he Secretary shall cause the accepted bid to be published in the Federal Register .'' On October 20, 1994, the Department conducted the fourth multifamily project mortgage auction. All 111 projects mortgages in the auction were sold to one bidder. In accordance with section 221(g)(4)(C)(ii)(IV) of the Act, the Department is publishing details concerning the accepted bid, as follows: Winning Bidder: CS First Boston Mortgage Capital Corporation Winning Bid: 78.98 basis points Dated: November 28, 1994.
