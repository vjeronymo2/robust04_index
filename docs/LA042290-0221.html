April 22, 1990, Sunday, Orange County Edition

ALSO WORTH WATCHING

The two-hour pilot for "Spenser: For Hire" (Monday at 4 p.m. on Lifetime) finds the Boston private investigator (Robert Urich) hired to find the runaway wife of a successful land developer.

Imogene Coca guest stars on "Moonlighting" (Monday at 8 p.m. on Lifetime) as Agnes Dipesto's crazy mother, who is unaware that she's actually being used as a courier by a gang of international smugglers.

Richard Kimble (David Janssen) stops running long enough to be drawn into the illegal schemes of a a comic-turned-bookmaker (Mickey Rooney) on "The Fugitive" (Tuesday at 7 a.m. on A&E).

James Stacy received an Emmy nomination for his performance on "Cagney & Lacey" (Tuesday at 10 a.m. on Lifetime) as a handicapped man who becomes romantically involved with Christine (Sharon Gless) while she's investigating a series of muggings of disabled people.

David (Bruce Willis) is shocked when he discovers that the pregnant Maddie (Cybill Shepherd) has married a man she met on the train from Chicago on "Moonlighting" (Thursday at 8 p.m. on Lifetime).

Irene Ryan and Raymond Bailey-Granny and Milburn Drysdale of "The Beverly Hillbillies" -- visit "Mr. Ed" (Thursday at midnight on Nickelodeon).

A pre-"China Beach" Dana Delany guests on "Moonlighting" (Saturday at 9 p.m. on Lifetime) as David's ex-girlfriend who seeks his help to find some missing jewelry but really has murder-David's-on her mind.

Jose, the elevator operator (Bill Dana), and Danny (Danny Thomas) become dog walkers on "Make Room for Daddy" (Saturday at 12:30 a.m. on Nickelodeon).
