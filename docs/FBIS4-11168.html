BFN [Text] Santo Domingo, 24 Apr (AFP) -- UNHCR representative Juan Manuel Castro, a Peruvian national, has said talks with the group of 102 Haitians who are occupying the offices of the UN High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) in the Dominican Republic have been unsuccessful up to now. The Haitians, 50 men, 20 women, and 32 children, occupied the UNHCR offices in downtown Santo Domingo on Friday to demand that they be granted refugee status. Castro told the media that the talks "have been unsuccessful, and we do not know what their intentions are. F. Castro visited the Haitians who are occupying the UNHCR offices today and ordered they be given more food and medicine. The Dominican police have had the block where the UNHCR offices are located surrounded since Friday, but there has been no official statement from the government of President Joaquin Balaguer.
