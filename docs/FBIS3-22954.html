Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Reports by Faysal Makram in Sanaa and Iqbal 'Ali 'Abdallah in Aden: "Aden: Socialist Party Talks About Foiling Attempt To Shoot Down al-Bid's Plane"] [Excerpt] Military sources in Aden have confirmed that the city's military intelligence (which is controlled by the Yemeni Socialist Party) [YSP] recently "uncovered a plot to shoot down the civilian aircraft scheduled to take Presidential Council Vice Chairman and YSP Secretary General 'Ali Salim al-Bid and his party's delegation from Aden to Amman in the next few days to sign the Pledge and Accord Document." They accused some central security elements attached to the northern governorates and working at the Radfan Camp overlooking Aden airport of preparing to carry out the mission of attacking al-Bid's plane with "shoulder-fired RPG shells" when it takes off from the airport. They said that "Brigadier General Salih Munsir al-Suyayli, governor of Aden and member of the YSP Political Bureau, ordered the expulsion of the northern members of central security from Aden to the north and the arrest of those who were planning to carry out the attack on al-Bid's aircraft." Furthermore, military sources in Aden say that "the forces deployed in al-'Anad camp (80 km north of Aden) arrested two Iraqis in the evening of the day before yesterday as they tried to infiltrate the camp, which has a military airfield and some installations." They added that "the Iraqis, who came from the northern governorates, confessed that their mission was to damage a number of military aircraft belonging to the army in the south and to hijack two planes to Iraq."
