April 19, 1989, Wednesday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

TUNA BOAT OBSERVER FUNDS ARE LOW

The government is running out of money to pay observers on U.S. tuna vessels to make sure dolphins are not needlessly injured or killed, a Justice Department lawyer said today.

Charles W. Brooks said unless Congress comes up with an additional $1 million, the government after May 30 may not be able to comply with a court order and staff all tuna boats with monitors.
