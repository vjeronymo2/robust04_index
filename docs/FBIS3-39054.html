Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Turkey, Romania, and Bulgaria have decided to establish a permanent working group to following the developments in the field of transportation. An agreement on the issue was signed in Ankara by Transportation Minister Mehmet Kostepen, Romanian Transportation Minister Paul Teororu, and Bulgarian Transportation Minister Kiril Ermenkov. In a statement at the signing ceremony, Kostepen said that the developments in the Balkans have increased the importance of the cooperation among the three countries. He added that with this agreement various measures have been adopted in a bid to make the transportation among the three countries safer and faster. Kostepen said that the three countries have also decided to take steps to once more regulate Danube transportation and delineate the Black Sea air space and FIR [Flight Information Region] line.
