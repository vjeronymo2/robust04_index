921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / Arts: Today's Television Some of the best arts programmes of recent years have come out of BBC2's Bookmark (8.10). The series returns today with an account of the English Shakespeare Company's tour of Malawi with 'Macbeth'. Producer John Purdie says 'Despite the language problems, Malawian audiences are weaned on an oral story-telling tradition and live in a world where witchcraft and power politics are daily realities. They quickly identify the plot of the Scottish tragedy and relish the obvious parallels between Shakespeare's characters and their own political leaders'. Dispatches sounds like yet another of those occasions when programme makers climb on their high horse and flay the powers-that-be for spending too little on hospital care. What broadcasters never do is state where they would draw the line, given that there would be no great difficulty in the modern world of expensive high-tech medicine in spending the entire GNP on health care (9.00 C4). BBC2 begins Nice Town, a three-part drama in which Paul McGann and Philip Davis play brothers one of whom decides to become a secret sperm donor for the other's wife (9.25).
