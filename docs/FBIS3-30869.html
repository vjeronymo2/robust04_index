Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 7/2/1994 -- Dumitru Paslaru, spokesman of the ruling party of Social Democracy of Romania (PDSR), said developments in the Republic of Moldova, the reconsideration of the stands of important political personalities there and their statements rendered delicate the continuation of the political relations and the rapprochement between Romania and that state. In an interview with ROMPRES, Paslaru added that the PDSR considered the statements made by Snegur [president of the Republic of Moldova] and Motpan [president of the Agrarian Democratic Party of Moldova] to be inciting, offending the Romanians everywhere, an attempt at the country's national integrity, an obvious disconsideration of public opinion of the Romanian majority in the present Republic of Moldova. "We however hope these statements are just a temporary element in the political climate of the Republic of Moldova. These statements should better not turn into ruling theses and the optimistic tone of our relations of so far should be resumed," the PDSR spokesman also said. The Romanian National Unity Party [PUNR] Vice-President Cornel Brahas assesses as a mistake the fact that the Romanian authorities recognized officially "this shadow state in Romania's historical territory. It is an open attempt at our integrity and a tragical betrayal of the Romanian nation. No matter what eastern or western chancellories encouraged Snegur to assume this terrible burden, we must not give in to defeatism," Cornel Brahas said. "I have not been taken by surprise by this statement made by Mr. Mircea Snegur, a good Romanian speaker but not an equally good Romanian. It was prepared by other political actions which we ignored when we did not encourage," said Professor Ion Coja, an outstanding member of the Agrarian Democratic Party of Romania (PDAR). "What's even worse is that, in a concerted manner, some sort of declaration of annexation of Romania's territory to the former USSR is also launched. It is high time that the president of Romania, the parliament and government took at long last a stand that should not be criticized by the future generations of Romanians on either bank of the Prut," Professor Ion Coja said. "The National Council of Reintegration and the National Party of Reintegration have decided to `back' Mircea Snegur, organizing the participation in the referendum of the more than one million refugees or their descendants, either Romanians, Jews, Germans, Poles," said Mircea Druc, former prime minister of the Republic of Moldova, now president of the National Party of Reintegration. "A referendum of all those who were forced to leave their native land following the Soviet invasion. In a near future, we will convene also a world congress of all refugees because we too, want to be a part of `Snegur's people' summoned to the referendum," Mircea Druc said. "Mircea Snegur has proved he has remained a cent [as received] percent communist and no longer thinks, even for a moment, that he is Romanian," said Ion Diaconescu, vice-president of the Christian Democratic National Peasant Party. "For him, the notion of Romanian is completely subordinated to the political creed for which he has campaigned for a lifetime. His theses are communist, they were Stalin's as well and he keeps them because he grew up with them. "Nobody imposes Romania's union with Bessarabia by force, but to go as far as declaring that Romanians and Moldovans are different peoples is a nonsense launched by communists which Mircea Snegur, as a truthful disciple, has adopted."
