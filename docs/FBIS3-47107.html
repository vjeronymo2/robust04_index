Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] On the afternoon of 5 January at the office of the Council of Ministers, a meeting was held to discuss the organization and development of tourism in the Kingdom of Cambodia [KOC] in 1994 under the lofty chairmanship of Prince Krompreah Norodom Ranariddh, first prime minister; and His Excellency [H.E.] Hun Sen, KOC second prime minister. Addressing the meeting H.E. Veng Sereivut, minister of the Council of Ministers and state secretary of the Tourism Ministry, informed the meeting of the plans, organization, and development of tourism in 1994, during which the Tourism Ministry will give priority to three zones: Siem Reap-Angkor, Sihanoukville, and Phnom Penh City. He said that, according to the plans, the three airports in these zones will be expanded and improved, and routes will be opened and security provided along all roads throughout the KOC, particularly the routes along Angkor Thom to Banteay Srei temples. Electricity will be provided in Phnom Penh City and the other two cities. Guards will be posted at temples and guides and interpretors provided at each temple. Felling trees in the Angkor area will immediately be stopped. The Siem Reap River will be dredged and hotels will be given a Cambodian character. Visa formalities will be made easier for tourists and guests. Attention will be paid to improving Kep resort. [passage omitted]
