BFN [By David Daniel Victoria] [Text] The Colombian-Panamanian Neighborhood Commission agreed yesterday to send a note to Enrique Iglesias, president of the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB), creating a permanent organization to safeguard the progress of land communication between the two countries, an official source reported yesterday. According to the source, the IDB committed itself to granting a nonreimbursable credit for $1.5 million for a study on the environmental impact of the construction of a highway linking Colombia and Panama. During yesterday's meeting, it was agreed to create a banking and finance subcommission, and procedures were outlined to establish border markings. Also, an immigration agreement will be reached to provide documents for 15,000 Colombian immigrants who are in Panama illegally. The Neighborhood Commission met for two days on Contadora Island for the last talks by Colombian delegates under the current government. It was learned that the permanent commission for land communication, which will be made up of authorities from both countries, will define reference terms for the environmental impact study and explore possible sources of financing for the highway between Panama and Colombia.
