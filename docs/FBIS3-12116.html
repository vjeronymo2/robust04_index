Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Today, the newspaper EL TIEMPO in Bogota, Colombia, published a letter from Cuban President Fidel Castro who acknowledges the paper's apologies for publishing reports on a supposed guerrilla meeting in Cuba. In his letter, which is addressed to the newspaper's editor and which was published on the front page of the largest Colombian newspaper, the head of the Cuban Revolution states that the issue is important and serious. The letter adds that the incident could have had serious and undesirable repercussions both for the credibility of many people and for [words indistinct]. In his letter to the Colombian newspaper EL TIEMPO, Fidel Castro added that he considers it his duty to express his most sincere appreciation for the step taken to deny reports published by the same newspaper that were not in keeping with the truth, and for the apology to Monsignor Nel Beltran and the Cuban Government. Fidel Castro notes that in explaining the incident and apologizing to those affected, the newspaper EL TIEMPO has shown courage and honesty. We admit this, Fidel Castro said in his letter. He added: We accept your apologies and congratulate you, the newspaper's board, and the reporters and workers for an attitude that exalts us. Fidel Castro concludes his letter to the newspaper EL TIEMPO in Bogota, Colombia, with an expression of hope and expectation that we will all learn a useful lesson from the unpleasant incident and that mutual respect and relations between the peoples of Cuba and Colombia will be strengthened.
