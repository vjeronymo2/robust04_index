BFN [Text] Tirana, May 26 (ATA) -- On May 22-23, 1994, the financial police in cooperation with the Ministry of Public Order and the Attorney General's Office exercised control in the border areas along the Buna River and the Lake of Shkodra to intercept Montenegro bound smugglers of fuel. Three tankers, an IFA [expansion unknown] vehicle, and a van which had transported fuel as well as three persons filling barrels with fuel to be moved across the border were intercepted in the area of Kamice village. Four motor boats coming from Montenegro, five Montenegrin citizens and an Albanian, who had come to get fuel from barrels along the Lake of Shkodra, were intercepted in the area of the Kasan village. Another 14 Albanian citizens, who were making ready to ship the fuel by motor boat were intercepted as well. Among them was a person armed with a hunting rifle. Barrels of fuel and three Albanian citizens, smuggling fuels, were intercepted in the Samrish village along the Buna River. Also, Montenegrin motor boats which has crossed the Albanian water frontier for fuel contraband were intercepted. In the area of the Murriqan village, again along the Buna River, three Albanians were intercepted while smuggling fuel across the frontier in collaboration with Montenegrins, who fled immediately on a motor-boat as the Albanian order forces intervened. In the end of the control on contraband and violation of the embargo imposed by the United Nations, acts which run counter to the laws of the Albanian state, 24 Albanian and five Montenegrin citizens were denounced for criminal prosecution, and five vehicles, about 160 barrels of oil as well as large quantities of empty barrels were intercepted. It is not the first time that the financial police bodies and those of the public order have exercised unexpected controls to prevent smuggling of fuel into Montenegro. Over the recent months, pipes were discovered in the Lake of Shkodra, which served the transportation of these fuels into the Montenegrin territory. Several tankers have also been intercepted recently in Malesia e Madhe, which had transported fuel out of the Albanian frontiers. For this reason, customs officials as well as public order employees of the border crossing point were detained.
