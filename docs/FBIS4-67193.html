Chuan Denies Ploy To Discredit Opposition

BK1205050494 Bangkok BANGKOK POST in English 12 May 94 p 1 BK1205050494 Bangkok BANGKOK POST English BFN [Text] Prime Minister Chuan Likphai yesterday denied that drug allegations against Chat Thai MP for Nakhon Phanom Thanong Siriprichaphong were an attempt by the Government to discredit the Opposition following a recent threat from the Opposition to censure the Government. Mr Chuan dismissed reports that a large number of politicians were known to be involved in the narcotics trade but conceded that some of the drug suspects were politicians. The Prime Minister said legal action would be taken against any individual suspected of being involved in narcotics trafficking regardless of who he or she is if enough evidence could be found to substantiate the charges. However, he said the Government could not reveal any U.S.-supplied information on drug traffickers to the media in Thailand to avoid defamation charges. PM's Office Minister Thoetphong Chaiyanan, who is also chairman of the Government Parties Coordination Committee, said the allegations against Mr Thanong must be thoroughly checked before the House of Representatives can make further moves on Mr Thanong's case.
