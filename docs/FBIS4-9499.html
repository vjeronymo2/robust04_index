BFN [Text] Two agreements were signed during the first day of the official visit of Albanian President Sali Berisha to Bulgaria. The first was an agreement on trade and economic cooperation and the second was an agreement on the mutual protection of investments. The agreements were signed by Selim Pelurtaja, Albanian minister of economy and trade, and Bulgarian Trade Minister Valentin Karabashev. In brief statements after the official ceremony to sign the agreements, Bulgarian President Zhelyu Zhelev and Albanian President Sali Berisha expressed their satisfaction with the development of relations between the two countries. They pointed out that this tendency was confirmed by the two documents, which regulate bilateral cooperation between Bulgaria and Albania. Presidents Zhelev and Berisha had a 45-minute face-to-face talk, but refused any statements before the joint news conference scheduled to take place on 29 April.
