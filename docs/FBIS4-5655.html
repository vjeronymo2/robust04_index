BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 8 KYODO -- Japan's Overseas Economic Cooperation Fund (OECF) said Friday [8 April] it has signed an accord with Peru to furnish up to 2.24 billion yen in loans to the Latin American republic. The untied loans will finance a project to reinforce Peru's health services, the OECF said. The credit, carrying an interest rate of 3.0 percent per annum, will be redeemed in 30 years, including a grace period of 10 years. The OECF's cumulative loans to Peru totaled 96,055 million yen.
