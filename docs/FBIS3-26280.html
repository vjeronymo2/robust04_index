Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hong Kong, Feb. 4 KYODO -- China on Friday [4 February] gave a cautious welcome to Hong Kong's latest funding proposal for the new multibillion dollar airport at Chek Lap Kok. The Hong Kong Government has sent China a new airport financing package, offering to pump about 7.76 billion U.S. dollars into the project and reduce the overall debt burden. A Hong Kong-based spokesman for the Sino-British Joint Liaison Group (JLG) told KYODO news service by telephone that the latest offer is "a distinct improvement" on three previous ones which all sought a greater level of debt. "We still need time to study the proposals fully but it's fair to say that we are more optimistic than before that an agreement can be reached," the spokesman said. "I can't tell you exactly when we will respond to the proposals, especially with the Chinese New Year holiday coming up, but it should not be too long." The holiday is February 9 and 10. The spokesman declined to speculate when China might be prepared to attend a JLG airport committee meeting to hammer out a final funding agreement, but said the new proposals have "improved the prospects" for such a meeting. Under the latest plan, the Hong Kong Government would dip into its huge fiscal reserves to help pay for airport projects and reduce the amount of debt payable after China resumes sovereignty over the territory in July 1997.
