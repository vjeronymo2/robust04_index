November 19, 1990, Monday, P.M. Final

THE SIDELINES;

INJURED ILL. TACKLE TO MISS FINALE

Illinois All-American nose tackle Moe Gardner broke his right forearm and will miss the season finale against Northwestern, an Illinois trainer said.

The senior -- one of four finalists for the Lombardi Trophy, given annually to the best defensive lineman in the country -- broke the arm in Saturday's 24-10 victory over Indiana.

"I don't know exactly when it happened, but it was late in the game," Gardner said. "It was bothering me then, but I wasn't quite sure whether or not it was broken. But when I woke up (Sunday), it hurt pretty bad. I knew something was wrong."

Gardner will be in a cast for three weeks. Trainer Al Martindale said Gardner could be ready for the Hall of Fame Bowl on Jan. 1.
