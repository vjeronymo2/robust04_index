April 18, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

PEOPLE'S PHARMACY: DRY SKIN REMEDY IS AS CLOSE AS NEIGHBORHOOD FARM

Question: Recently, I read in your column about a product called Bag Balm that helps dry skin. I've never seen it in my drugstore. The pharmacist looked at me as if I were a nut case when I asked about Bag Balm.

Where can I get it? I tried all the advertised products for this skin condition, with no results.

Answer: You and dozens of other readers have written to us to help you find Bag Balm. What you need is an old-time pharmacy or a farm supply store.

Bag Balm is a veterinary product, designed to keep cows' udders from chapping and drying out. Old dairy farmers learned that it worked as well for their hands as for their cows' udders.

It contains petroleum jelly, lanolin and a disinfectant. You could probably substitute a product with similar ingredients, such as A&D Ointment, Cutemol or Lanoline.

If you want to try the original Bag Balm (which hasn't changed its ingredients or green tin can in decades), you can contact the manufacturer, Dairy Assn. Co. of Lyndonville, Vt., 05851.

Q. I'm recuperating from a reaction to Feldene. My doctor prescribed it a month ago for sore shoulders and mentioned it might cause indigestion.

I liked the way it eased the pain in my shoulders, but after 30 days my stomach suddenly started bleeding. I lost three units of blood. My blood pressure dropped so low I couldn't stand up.

Clearly in my case the cure was worse than the ailment. How often does this happen to Feldene users?

A. You are not the first, and assuredly won't be the last to develop a bleeding ulcer while taking Feldene (piroxicam). Almost any of the anti-inflammatory agents such as Motrin (ibuprofen), Naprosyn (naproxen), Indocin (indomethacin) or Voltaren (diclofenac) can cause stomach irritation, ulcers or bleeding. As you found out, the bleeding may occur without warning.

Studies show that about one patient in 100 will develop an ulcer or serious intestinal bleeding when taking one of these arthritis drugs for three to six months.

In patients treated for a year, 2% to 4% experience such problems, which appear to be more serious, if not more common, among older people.
