BFN [By P.C. Wu and Debbie Kuo] [Text] Bali, Indonesia, May 19 (CNA) -- Republic of China [ROC]'s Economic Affairs Minister P.K. Chiang has been invited to take part in a ministerial meeting of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) forum scheduled for Oct. 6 in Jakarta. According to APEC sources, Indonesian Trade Minister Satrio Joedono sent invitations Thursday to trade and economics ministers of all 17 APEC member nations, including the Republic of China. The time and place of the October APEC ministerial meeting was decided Thursday during an APEC senior officials meeting, the second of its kind, which opened Wednesday here with more than 100 delegates from 17 nations and regions taking part. Other decisions made by the senior officials Thursday included that the third and fourth APEC senior officials meetings will be held Sept. 10-14 in Yogjakarta in central Java and Nov. 6-10 in Jakarta, respectively. Indonesia is the chair country for 1994 APEC meetings and activities. The APEC senior officials also decided that APEC ministers will meet in Jakarta for another round of talks Nov. 11-12 after the October meeting. The APEC leadership meeting is scheduled for Nov. 15 in Bogor in western Java, the senior officials decided. The APEC senior officials Thursday focused their discussions on the development of manpower resources, promotion of small and medium-sized enterprises and infrastructure development. The three-day meeting will also discuss follow-up plans for last year's APEC leadership meeting in Seattle. Taipei is represented by an 18-member delegation led by Shen Kuo-hsiung, director of the Foreign Affairs Ministry's Department of International Organizations. APEC groups 17 nations, and Chile is expected to become the 18th member by the end of this year. An additional 14 nations have expressed interest in joining the club.
