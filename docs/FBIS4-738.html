BFN [Text] The prime minister, the right honorable Dr. Ntsu Mokhehle, yesterday released a statement on the current military crisis in the country. In his statement Dr. Mokhehle indicated that a contingent of the military attacked the home of the honorable deputy prime minister, Mr. Selometsi Baholo, during which attack Mr. Baholo was fatally wounded. The prime minister further said that four government ministers were subsequently abducted by this section of the army and these are the Honorable Monyane Moleleki, the Honorable Shakhane Mokhehle, the Honorable Pakalitha Mosisili, and the Honorable Kelebone Malope. Dr. Mokhehle further indicated that he reported the matter to His Majesty King Letsie III and requested his majesty's intervention. He said that after consultations a delegation was sent to both military barracks with the purpose of safeguarding the lives of the kidnapped ministers and to try to find a solution to this new wave of unrest in the country. Dr. Mokhehle said that the Council of State under the presidency of his majesty, King Letsie III, also held a meeting yesterday afternoon to resolve this new disturbing situation. While the matter was being resolved, the prime minister said the government received the assurance of the delegation which had been sent to both Makoanyane and Ha Ratjomose barracks that the lives of the four ministers were safe and that they had been released to their families and were receiving all the protection they required. Dr. Mokhehle conveyed his most sincere condolences to the widow and family of the deceased and to his majesty, King Letsie III, and the government and people of Lesotho for this great loss. The prime minister concluded by appealing to the Basotho nation to remain calm to enable a speedy solution to the crisis.
