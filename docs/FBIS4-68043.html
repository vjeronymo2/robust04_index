BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombian guerrilla activities monitored through 4 May. As the losses from two guerrilla attacks against two Banadex company piers were being estimated, six military agents, wounded during an ambush in northern Uraba, were recuperating. In addition, two guerrillas died, and five others were captured in northern Antioquia Department. Meanwhile, the 48th Front of the Colombian Revolutionary Armed Forces (FARC) detonated a bomb as an Army patrol passed by in Santa Catalina, San Pedro de Uraba, wounding six military personnel. Also, a counterguerrilla patrol and some National Liberation Army (ELN) members clashed in Botero, where two unidentified men died. Finally, a police patrol captured five ELN members in Argelia, southeastern Antioquia. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 25 Apr 94 p 18A) The Army on 24 April officially reported that one of the eight guerrillas captured 75 days earlier in Mesitas, Cundinamarca Department, is third in command of the ELN. His name is Luis Fernando Orozco Ortega, aka Ramiro, who was captured by the 13th Brigade. He is being held at the Modelo National Prison and has been charged with complicity in a terrorist wave of attacks against multinational oil companies. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 25 Apr 94 pp 1A, 18A) Some 25 members of the ELN's Carlos Alirio Buitrago Front have forced four riders off their motorcycles and shot them dead on the Medellin-Santa Fe de Bogota highway, where they also set two trailers and 14 new cars on fire. Meanwhile, two minors and a peasant died when a guerrilla bomb went off in Caqueta. In a separate incident, the Army killed a guerrilla and captured another. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 27 Apr 94 pp 1A, 8A) Narino State military spokesmen report that Colombian authorities on 27 April seized 300 kg of dynamite near the border with Ecuador and arrested three people, who claimed the dynamite came from Ecuador and was going to be used by the ELN. (Paris AFP in Spanish 2151 GMT 27 Apr 94) FARC forces attacked Giraldo Municipality, causing 50-million pesos worth of damage. They also stole 15 rifles and wounded four soldiers. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0000 GMT 5 May 94)
