BFN [Text] On 3 May Islam Karimov, president of the Republic of Uzbekistan, received Ukeru Magasaki, ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary of Japan to our country. Noting the prestigious position that Japan occupies in the world community, Islam Karimov stated that firm mutual economic, trade, and cultural ties have been established and contacts in all other fields are developing successfully. Japan is a distant and very much a legendary country for us; it has already stepped into the 21st century and is a leading power, the leader of Uzbekistan told the ambassador. Japan is supplying companies worldwide with modern technology and if mutual relations with Uzbekistan, where there are stable conditions, a good climate, and rich natural resources, become closer, then the development of such economic cooperation will become even more beneficial for both countries. Ukeru Magasaki thanked the president for the warm reception and stressed that the newly formed Government of Japan will strive further for the productive development of mutually beneficial ties with Uzbekistan. Saidmukhtar Saidkasymov, minister for foreign affairs of Uzbekistan, was present at the meeting.
