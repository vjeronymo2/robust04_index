930512 FT 12 MAY 93 / International Company News: Sakura merger with US clearer gains approval SAKURA, the Japanese bank, and Dellsher Investment, the Chicago futures and options clearing firm, have received US Federal Reserve approval to merge. Sakura, which was formed in 1990 with the merger between Mitsui Bank and the Taiyo Kobe Bank, offered to become Dellsher's majority shareholder in 1991, but the deal was delayed by US regulatory reviews. The new firm, Sakura Dellsher, will be a clearing member of the CME and the Chicago Board of Trade. Sakura's consolidated assets of Dollars 449bn will give strong backing to Dellsher in the derivatives business, which demands large amounts of capital. Dellsher, owned by Mr Leo Melamed, one of the founding fathers of Chicago futures, is a small firm with an estimated Dollars 4m to Dollars 5m in assets. Mr Kenichi Suematsu, president of Sakura, said: 'The competence of Dellsher, coupled with the unique qualifications of Mr Melamed, will afford our clients with outstanding access to global financial futures and options markets.'
