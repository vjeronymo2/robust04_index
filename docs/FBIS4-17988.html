BFN [Text] Chisinau BASAPRESS, 26/4/1994 -- The Sudan ambassador to Moldova, Faruq Mustafa `Ali, with permanent residence in Bucharest presented his credentials to President Mircea Snegur, states the presidential press service communique. The Sudan diplomat stated during the meeting that the people he represents respect the Moldovan people's choice expressed on February 27th. He called this "a choice to erect a democratic, independent and sovereign state." Snegur pointed out that Moldova has overcome a series of difficulties inherent in the transition to a market economy, mentioning the stability of the national currency and reduction of inflation. According to Snegur, Moldova's foreign policy will be founded on certain laws that will be examined and adopted in the near future. He said that "the present Parliament that has a majority faction displays constructivity in its activity. It is very likely to eliminate the conflicts in Eastern and Southern Moldova," said Snegur. Both Snegur and Faruq Mustafa `Ali pointed out that the economic relations between the two states can be developed, particularly in agriculture.
