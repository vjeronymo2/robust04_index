940518 FT 18 MAY 94 / World Stock Markets: Brazil The strong rally on Wall Street and in US bond prices sparked a sharp buying spree in Brazilian shares, boosting equities in Sao Paulo by 5.5 per cent in what brokers called a 'selective return' of foreign investors. The Bovespa index ended 858 points up at 16,484. Turnover was Cr286.3bn (Dollars 180.4m). Investors were also focusing, domestically, on an expected congressional vote on a key presidential decree. Approval of the temporary presidential decree, which created Brazil's single price index on March 1, is regarded as a key element for the continuation of the government's anti-inflation policy.
