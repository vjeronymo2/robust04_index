BFN [Announcer-read report over still of Prime Minister Tansu Ciller] [Text] It was reported that Prime Minister Tansu Ciller and U.S. President Bill Clinton have definitely not discussed the Cyprus issue at a tete-a-tete meeting they had in Washington last week. According to a statement issued by the Press Center of the President's Office, it was recalled that certain statements regarding the Cyprus issue were attributed to Ciller in today's issue of a newspaper [not further identified]. The statement notes that at the meeting between the delegations held in Washington, Clinton stated that there was a deadlock regarding the confidence-building measures [CBM's]. Ciller, in turn, said the following at the meeting: The original CBM's package was, in principle, approved by the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus and was also endorsed by Turkey. The package was subsequently changed. It will be appropriate to discuss the issue once more before the end of April and achieve a rapprochement between the views of the two sides. The statement further notes that the minutes of the meeting were dispatched to the Foreign Ministry the day of the meeting.
