Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] A FARC [Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia] front blamed for killing 35 people in Apartado over the weekend has denied its participation in the massacre by claiming the culprits are the same people responsible for the killings at Honduras, La Negra, and Pueblo Bello haciendas. Today, the Hope, Peace, and Liberty Movement said it will not withdraw from the Uraba political pact. The movement's topmost leader, Senator Anibal Palacios, however, has said that FARC's Bolivarian Militias are the ones that massacred 35 people in La Chinita, Apartado, neighborhood. This is Palacios' reaction to a letter FARC addressed to Antioquia Governor Juan Gomez Martinez denying FARC participation in the massacre. [Begin Gomez Martinez recording] We did not say that Patriotic Unity is responsible, but rather that the Guerrilla Coordinating Board and the Bolivarian Militias had a hand in the La Chinita massacre. [end recording] This afternoon, the movement leaders met with Government Minister Fabio Villegas Ramirez, whom they have asked to be an international observer for Uraba. [Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish on 26 January publishes a 400-word article on page 6-A reporting: "The Hope, Peace, and Liberty Movement's decision to withdraw from the follow-up table [mesa de seguimiento], which guarantees the existence of the political pact signed in Uraba on 23 November, produced an avalanche of reactions." The article also reports: "Government Minister Fabio Villegas said the commitment has not been broken" and he adds that the Hope, Peace, and Liberty Movement still recognizes the validity of the political pact that was signed."]
