930405 FT 05 APR 93 / International Company News: Saudi British Bank up 51% SAUDI British Bank lifted net profit for the first quarter of 1993 by 51 per cent to a record SR88.5m (Dollars 23.6m), Reuter reports from Manama. SABB, 40 per cent owned by HSBC Holdings, the parent of Hongkong and Shanghai Banking Corp, in February offered 2m new shares worth SR1.14bn. The flotation by the bank, the fourth-biggest joint venture in Saudi Arabia, raised its share capital by 150 per cent to SR1bn. Total assets climbed 38 per cent to SR21.8bn at end-March 1993. Customer deposits in the first three months rose by 32.5 per cent to SR16.3bn.
