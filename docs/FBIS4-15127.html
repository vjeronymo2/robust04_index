CSO [ARMENPRESS report: "Iran, Turkey, and Russia Share Common Interests"] [Text] Morteza Bank, a high-ranking representative of the Iranian Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Ankara, expressed support for intensified efforts by the countries of the region aimed at settling the Karabakh conflict. "Security in the Transcaucasus and Central Asia is profiting from the expansion of bilateral and multilateral contacts among states of the region," he stated. Iran, Turkey, and Russia have their own points of view regarding resolution of the Karabakh problem, but their common interests demand a convergence of these views, Bank noted.
