Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 18 March (MTI) -- A Romanian river freighter was held up at Mohacs on the south Hungarian stretch of the Danube due to its violation of UN sanctions against rump Yugoslavia, local customs authorities told MTI. The ship, Slobozia Mare, arrived at Mohacs on Monday [14 March] with Budapest as its destination. When checked thoroughly for compliance of embargo regulations, it was found to be carrying only 18 tonnes of oil instead of the 52 tonnes indicated in its official documents, with water replacing the missing quantity. According to unconfirmed information, the Romanian captain and the crew of two Serbs and six Ukrainians admitted to having sold the missing oil, worth 2.7 million forints ($1 equals 103 forints), in Serbia. Customs investigations are continuing.
