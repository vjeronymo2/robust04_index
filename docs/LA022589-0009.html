February 25, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

IACOCCA ON THE INFLUENCE OF FOREIGN LOBBIES IN WASHINGTON

"From Toshiba to Trucks, We're Being Run Over by Foreign Lobbies," screams the headline on Lee Iacocca's column (Op-Ed Page, Feb. 15). Iacocca complains that "every time foreign lobbyists in Washington jerk a string . . . the American government jumps." Iacocca contends that his complaint is not a tariff issue or a trade issue, but "who's got the power to change the" law of the United States if they don't happen to like it."

Iacocca argues that the people we elect to make our laws, instead of being bought by foreign lobbies, should be bought by American lobbies -- that American interests should jerk the strings that make government jump. To this I say "the pox on Iacocca and his special-interest groups!"

What we need instead is a separation of state and economy. Let there be no laws in the United States that interfere with free market exchanges between willing buyers and willing sellers -- regardless of race, sex or nationality. Without such laws there will be nothing to sell to lobbyists. Corruption in Washington will be vastly diminished. Our welfare will be vastly improved.

ERNST F. GHERMANN

Winnetka
