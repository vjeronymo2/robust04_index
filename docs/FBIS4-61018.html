BFN [Excerpt] Riga, May 02, BNS -- Latvian and Russian state delegations signed a protocol on the most favored nation status in economy, Latvian Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs told reporters Sunday [1 May] on return from Moscow. Under the protocol, the favored nation relations take effect starting 1 June. The respective agreement is scheduled for the second half of May when Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin tours Riga. Birkavs draw a link between the achieved compromise on the amount of rent for the Skrunda radar and the agreement on the most favored nation status in economy. As reported, Russia initially offered Latvia 2 million U.S. dollars per year while Latvia demanded 400 million U.S. dollars per year. The 30 April troop withdrawal treaty provides for a $5-million yearly rent. Birkavs pledged to channel the funding for the needs of Skrunda locals who will have to put up with the radar for four more years. Latvia needs long-term goodneighborly relations with Russia, said Birkavs. He claimed that Latvia and Russia faced a new historic epoch in this aspect. "The new, democratic Russia understands Latvia's problems and the signature of the agreements will consolidate democracy also in Russia itself," noted Birkavs. [passage omitted]
