910502 FT 02 MAY 91 / Kurds claim Iraq agrees to elections A Kurdish leader claimed that Iraqi officials had agreed to abolish the ruling Revolutionary Command Council and hold multi-party elections within six months. In a BBC television interview, Jalal Talabani, a leader of the Iraqi Kurdistan Front, said from his base in northern Iraq that 'a freely-elected parliament will function instead of all other bodies'. There was no confirmation of the report from Baghdad. Page 4 In Turkey, 30 British troops accused of harassing a Turkish official at a Kurdish refugee camp last night left the country. Turkey said it had demanded their expulsion but the Ministry of Defence in London said their departure was coincidence.
