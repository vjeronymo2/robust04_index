930513 FT 13 MAY 93 / Chambers add to late-pay pressure BRITAIN'S Chambers of Commerce yesterday added their voice to the lobby calling for the government to give businesses a statutory right to interest on unpaid bills, Charles Batchelor writes. This formed part of a package of measures intended to change the payment culture of British business announced by the Association of British Chambers of Commerce at its annual conference in Leeds. Mr Richard Brown, the chambers' policy director, said: 'Every effort to tackle the problem must focus on changing the payment culture. Consultation within the chamber of commerce movement has revealed that the voluntary process of exhortation is not working.'
