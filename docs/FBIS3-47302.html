Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 11 (YONHAP) -- President Kim Yong-sam stressed Tuesday [11 January] closest South Korea-U.S. cooperation and understanding now on the North Korean nuclear problem while repeating that the problem has to be solved for Korean and regional peace. Kim met with visiting American senators Sam Nunn and Richard Lugar over breakfast and requested continued support from the U.S. Congress for such cooperation, Spokesman Chu Ton-sik said. Nunn, chairman of the Senate Armed Services Committee, and his entourage came to Seoul after visiting Russia on what officials here called a field trip. The spokesman said there was no discussion of sanctions on North Korea in case diplomatic dialogue fails but described the senators visit as intended to gather necessary information on various possibilities if sanctions become inevitable. Nunn reaffirmed the U.S. security commitment to Seoul, recalling President Bill Clinton's words that an attack on South Korea is an attack on the United States. This, he said, was the position of the United States. He called South Korea-U.S. cooperation an invaluable prerequisite to stability on the Korean peninsula and added that American troops need to stay in South Korea, the spokesman said. The senators return to Washington later Tuesday.
