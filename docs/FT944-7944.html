941122 FT 22 NOV 94 / International Company News: Rockwell wins battle for Reliance Electric The bid battle for Reliance Electric, the US electronics company, has been won with an increased offer from Rockwell International of Dollars 31 a share, valuing Reliance at Dollars 1.6bn. The offer, raised from an initial Dollars 30, capped an agreed bid from rival manufacturer General Signal. Reliance must now pay General Signal a break-up fee of Dollars 50m, plus Dollars 5.15m in expenses. In recent weeks, Rockwell had attempted various legal approaches to have the fee waived, but without success. The bid from General Signal, worth Dollars 1.3bn, had been expected to fail. General Signal is a much smaller company than Rockwell, and its offer was in paper rather than cash. Reliance's shares closed 50 cents higher yesterday at Dollars 30.75. Rockwell plans to merge Reliance with its Allen-Bradley division, creating a worldwide industrial automation business with sales of about Dollars 3.5bn. Rockwell plans to sell Reliance's telecommunications business, which made up about a third of Reliance group sales last year. Rockwell said yesterday the acquisition would bring its turnover to Dollars 12.6bn, of which over 70 per cent would come in commercial markets. As a leading manufacturer of rockets and missiles, Rockwell has been hit by the downturn in US defence spending and has been seeking to build up its non-defence business. Mr John Morley, Reliance's president, said: 'We believe that Dollars 31 per share is a fair price and in the best interests of our shareholders.'
