BFN [Text] Belgrade, 5 Apr (TANJUG) -- Around 1115 hours yesterday morning Yugoslav Army border guards arrested three persons belonging to the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] civilian police in the village of Slavujevac, three kilometers away from the border between the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia [FRY] and The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM], because they had crossed the border without authorization, the information service of the Yugoslav Army General Staff says in a statement issued today. Two policemen and a female interpreter who illegally crossed the state border in a motorized vehicle were kept in the Slavujevac watchtower where they were questioned about their motives and reasons for crossing into FRY territory. Around 1430 hours three UNPROFOR armored personnel carriers entered 300 meters into FRY territory. Following an energetic action by Yugoslav Army border guards, the armored personnel carriers were returned to their base on FYROM territory, the statement says.
