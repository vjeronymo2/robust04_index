930623 FT 23 JUN 93 / Observer: Small world Meanwhile London Business School's long hunt for its first professor and assistant professor in business ethics has at last been resolved, but only after some embarrassment for the selectors. The problem seems to have been a dearth of ethical academics. In its eagerness to draw on the best expertise in compiling the shortlist, the committee originally recruited the highly-respected Jesuit Jack Mahoney as external adviser. It then realised that the vital missing name was Mahoney himself, who - whilst not involved in his own selection in any way - yesterday became the anointed one for the professorship.
