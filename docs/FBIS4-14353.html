BFN [Excerpt] The cabinet held a session on Wednesday [27 April] night. At the end of the session the first vice-president and the government spokesman, Mr. Habibi, announced: According to operational regulations adopted by the cabinet all government vehicles will be released from government control and sold off to natural and corporate persons on an instalment basis. He added: Those public servants who wish to take early retirement or form a cooperative will be able to purchase government vehicles. He said: 20 percent of the vehicles belonging to the government must be sold off by the end of the year [ending 20 March 1995] according to a decision by the Islamic Consultative Majlis.
