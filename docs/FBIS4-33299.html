BFN [Text] Caracas, 5 Jun (AFP) -- Starting this week, Haitians fleeing their country will be taken to three U.S. ships [not further identified] anchored off Jamaica, and those who can demonstrate refugee status will travel to any member nation of the group "Friends of Haiti," it was reported today. Guy Prim, representative for Venezuela and the Caribbean of the UN Office of the High Commisssioner on Human Rights [UNHCR], said this international organization will pay for the operation, which is expected to amount to about $1.5 million. In statements to the Caracas media, Prim said the difficult situation facing Haiti makes its 6.9 million inhabitants "potential asylum seekers." He announced that starting this week, Haitians who flee their country will be housed aboard three U.S. ships anchored off the Port of Kingston, Jamaica. One of the ships will serve as a hospital. He added that two commissions will be tasked with classifying the guests. These commissions will include an UNHCR commission consisting of 10 jurists and 10 translators and a commission of the U.S. Immigration and Naturalization Service consisting of 50 officials. Prim said those who manage to demonstrate refugee status during an interview will be informed about the possibility of going to any member country of the group "Friends of Haiti." This group is composed of Canada, United States, France, Argentina, and Venezuela. Prim, however, voiced his concern that Haitians might not want to go to other countries because they are obsessed with traveling to the United States. He bases his concern on a recent experience when a majority of a group of Haitians who failed to reach U.S. coasts returned to their native island. In Venezuela, Prim added, the Social Pastoral Office of the Bishop's Conference reached an agreement with the UNHCR to offer lodging to Haitians welcomed by the administration of President Rafael Caldera. Yesterday, Venezuelan Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli Rivas said his country will welcome refugees for as long as the difficult situation on the island persists.
