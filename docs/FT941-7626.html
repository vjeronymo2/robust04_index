940224 FT 24 FEB 94 / Code to be issued on profit forecasts A committee sponsored by the Stock Exchange will today issue a code of conduct instructing directors that they should no longer privately correct individual analysts' profits forecasts but should inform the entire market, Norma Cohen and Robert Peston write. Institutional investors and stockbrokers which receive unpublished price sensitive information will also be advised to encourage companies to publish the information widely. The new code has been drawn up so that companies, shareholders and stockbrokers can be confident they can communicate with each other without infringing the provision of the Criminal Justice Act on insider trading, which comes into force on Tuesday. It has been drafted by representatives of the 100 Group of Finance Directors, the Institutional Fund Managers Association and the Association of British Insurers. One member said the committee was particularly concerned about earnings forecasts from analysts employed by a company's own stockbroker which 'have been surprisingly accurate'.
