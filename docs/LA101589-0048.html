October 15, 1989, Sunday, Valley Edition

WOMAN, 31, COAXED FROM FREEWAY OVERPASS

Police officers and firefighters coaxed a 31-year-old North Hollywood woman from the edge of an overpass about 60 feet above the Hollywood Freeway in North Hollywood on Saturday night.

It was the second such incident in the San Fernando Valley in two days.

The woman inched along the edge of the Whitsett Avenue overpass to its west side while police held on to her clothes through a fence.

"She was not irrational and not combative," said Los Angeles Fire Capt. Frank David. Police took the uninjured woman to Olive View Medical Center for psychiatric evaluation, said Los Angeles Police Sgt. Christopher Vasquez.

Firefighters and California Highway Patrol officers blocked traffic on the freeway until the woman was safely off the bridge about 6:20 p.m. Officers and firefighters had reached the scene about 10 minutes earlier.

On Friday, a Burbank police detective saved a 17-year-old Burbank girl by grabbing her arm as she stepped off an overpass above the Ventura Freeway about 5 miles from the site of Saturday's incident.
