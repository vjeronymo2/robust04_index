October 10, 1990, Wednesday, Southland Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

FRANCE;

YOUTHS RIOT FOR 4TH STRAIGHT NIGHT

Rampaging youths torched a tobacco shop and a store in the shopping center of a low-income suburb of Lyon, France, in the fourth straight night of unrest.

The violence was far less severe than on the three previous nights, however, when hundreds of stone-throwing youths set fire to 20 buildings and burned two dozen cars. The rioting was triggered by the weekend death of Thomas Claudio, 21, a passenger on a motorcycle that collided with a police patrol car. The rioters blame police for the death.
