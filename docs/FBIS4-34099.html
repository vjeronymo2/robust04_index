BFN [Text] Caracas, 14 Jun (AFP) -- According to similar reports issued in Caracas today, seven Venezuelan commercial banks closed their doors today "until further notice" following a decision by the Central Bank of Venezuela to exclude them from clearinghouse activities. Although the source did not deny nor confirm the news to AFP, its reporters were able to confirm that the Construccion, Barinas, Maracaibo, La Guaira, Metropolitano, Bancor, Hipotecario de Occidente banks and the Sociedad Financiera Fiveca remained closed today. Despite the fact that the banks were closed, their employees were working while hundreds of customers protested at the doors. These seven banks and Latino Bank -- the country's second largest bank, in which the government intervened in January and which reopened in April with losses of $5 billion -- have received financial assistance amounting to $4.300 billion since February to prevent their collapse.
