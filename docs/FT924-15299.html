921005 FT 05 OCT 92 / Construction Contracts: Pounds 34m workload for Lovell companies LOVELL GROUP has been awarded work valued at over Pounds 34m, the construction division contributing Pounds 20.7m and Partnerships Pounds 13.6m. Walter Lilly has obtained Pounds 4.7m of orders in the London area comprising office projects for Paribas in central London worth Pounds 1m for the Wellcome Foundation at Beckenham for Pounds 1.7m, for SCOR (UK) Reinsurance at Pounds 1.37m and for bomb damage at the Baltic Exchange for Pounds 700,000. Bullock Construction has won orders worth more than Pounds 9.2m including school contracts at Warrington and Chesterfield for Pounds 3.6m and local authority housing upgrading at Leicester, Winsford, Coventry, Derby, Huyton and Birmingham totalling Pounds 5.6m. Lovell Construction has been awarded work worth Pounds 6.8m, comprising local authority housing at Crawley for Pounds 3.4m, a bus garage at Sutton for Pounds 1.2m, upgrading student accommodation at the Sunningdale Civil Service College for Pounds 1.2m, underground work on the Central Line for Pounds 600,000 and bomb damage reinstatement in Leadenhall Street for Pounds 400,000. Lovell Partnerships has recently secured Pounds 13.6m of new business in seven partnership housing developments around the country, representing in excess of 400 new homes. In the west, a partnership with Thamesdown Borough Council and Knightstone Housing Association will provide 109 homes in Swindon and a further scheme with Ogvale Housing Co-operative will result in 115 homes. In the north west, two projects have been received: the first, at Salford, in partnership with Irwell Valley Housing Association, will be a development of 33 homes; the second, at Hyde, Cheshire is for a mixed tenure scheme of 69 homes for sale and rent through a partnership with Tameside Borough Council and Portico Housing Association. In Leeds the company has two projects, which will provide a total of 78 homes.
