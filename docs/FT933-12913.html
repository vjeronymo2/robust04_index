930722 FT 22 JUL 93 / Chinese slowdown to hit HK growth MOVES to damp economic activity in China will lop about 1 percentage point off Hong Kong's economic growth next year, Mr Hamish Macleod, the colony's financial secretary, said yesterday, Simon Holberton reports from Hong Kong. He said he accepted predictions by private economic forecasters that the colony's growth would be affected by the measures announced in China to deal with overheating. However, he stood by his March budget forecast of 5.5 per cent growth in gross domestic product for 1993. He would not make a 1994 prediction but most forecasters expect about 4.5 per cent. He said he believed the Chinese authorities would be successful in cooling economic growth on the mainland. 'When the centre (Beijing) makes it plain that it has a policy priority, sends teams to the provinces, and shifts people from their jobs I think it will be taken seriously.'
