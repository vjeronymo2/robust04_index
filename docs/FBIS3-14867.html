Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Boris Krivoshey and Aleksandr Krylovich] [Text] Moscow February 28 TASS--There is much to be clarified about the incident with four planes which bombed the town of Travnik in central Bosnia and were brought down by NATO air forces, which exercised control over the no-fly zone in the air space of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Vitaliy Churkin, deputy Russian foreign minister, said on Monday evening. According to him, "It is not clear so far, what sort of planes were shot down". As soon as the first report about the incident was received, the Russian Foreign Ministry got in touch with the Serbian leadership, he told a press conference held in connection with a visit of a delegation of NATO officials to Moscow. The Serbian leaders were "utterly bewildered" as they could not understand, why the Serbian planes should bomb Travnik, the site of Croat-Moslem clashes, Churkin said. "Whatever party should have made the military air raid in Bosnia in violation of the relevant resolutions of the UN Security Council on the no-fly zone, it bears the full measure of responsibility for what has happened," he said. Military planes of the belligerents must not fly over Bosnia, he emphasized. "If all the reported circumstances should be corroborated, it will be evident that the regime of the no-fly zone established by a resolution of the UN Security Council was violated--with all the resultant consequences.
