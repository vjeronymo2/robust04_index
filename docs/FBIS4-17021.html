BFN [Text] The new government of the Ukrainian autonomous Republic of Crimea on Wednesday took office in Simferopol. Its nucleus is the "Moscow team" of Yevgeniy Saburov, a Russian economist invited by Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov to hold the post of deputy prime minister. The government includes Economics Minister Andrey Chernyavskiy, Finance Minister Lyudmila Zaitseva, Agriculture and Food Minister Sergey Nikolskiy, head of the Service of State Affairs Viktor Minin, Minister for the Management of State Enterprises Anatoliy Franchuk (deputy premier in the previous Crimean government), Security Service chief Valeriy Lepikhov, Interior Minister Valeriy Kuznetsov, and Justice Minister Lyubov Yeliseyeva-Bora. Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk earlier canceled Lepikhov's appointment as head of security. All the appointments are subject to confirmation by the Crimean parliament. Before that, their titles will be prefixed by "acting."
