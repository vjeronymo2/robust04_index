920113 FT 13 JAN 92 / Observer: Woolly thinking Why should the Falkland Islands' Agriculture department have just imported 650 Aussie sheep to establish a national stud flock, at an initial cost of Pounds 260,000, when they already have some 300,000 of the blighters? The officially-expressed reason is 'to improve the conformity of the wool .. by supplying farms with rams of superior merit'. Slicing through the jargon, it would seem that the local flock is rather suffering from generations of inbreeding. Yes . . . .
