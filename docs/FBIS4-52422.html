BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 9 (KCNA) -- Talks were held here Sunday between delegations of the Workers' Party of Korea [WPK] and the Communist Party of India (Marxist). Present there on the Korean side were secretary Hwang Chang-yop and vice department director Kim Yang-kon of the WPK Central Committee and other officials concerned, and on the opposite side the delegation led by Jyoti Basu, Politburo member of the party Central Committee and prime minister of the west Bengal State. At the talks the sides informed each other of their party activities and exchanged views on a series of matters of common concern. Both sides declared that socialism is the future of mankind, stressing the need to develop the friendly relations between the two parties and strengthen solidarity with the socialist forces of the world. The talks proceeded in a friendly atmosphere.
