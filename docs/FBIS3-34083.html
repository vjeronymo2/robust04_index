Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 2, IRNA -- Iran, Pakistan, Afghanistan opened a five-day trilateral meeting in Islamabad on Tuesday to consolidate cooperation on border security, the anti-drug headquarters at the president's office announced here Wednesday. It said the ongoing meeting is being held at the proposal of the United Nations. An Iranian delegation is in Islamabad to attend the meeting. The participants are studying the plan for strengthening border security cooperation. After the meeting the delegates are to visit the joint border areas and will later hold talks with Iran's Minister of the Interior 'Ali Mohammad Besharati in Tehran, it added.
