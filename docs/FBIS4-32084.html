BFN [Interview with Zhan Videnov, chairman of the Bulgarian Socialist Party Supreme Council, by Teodora Encheva; place and date not given] [Excerpts] [Encheva] Mr. Videnov, do you want to be prime minister? [Videnov] I want Bulgaria to have a lasting, efficient, and leftist-centrist government. I think that the voters will soon resolve this issue. I have often said that Bulgaria will hardly be an exception to the other East European countries, where the composition of leftist-centrist cabinets is formed after complex coalition negotiations that often have an unexpected or unconventional result. [Encheva] I meant your personal ambition. [Videnov] I have neither sick ambitions nor an inferiority complex. Both the Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP] and I could cope with such a challenge. [passage omitted] [Encheva] Why did you sacrifice Georgi Pirinski as a member of the BSP Executive Bureau? You know that he will not work together with Doncho Konakchiev and Nikola Koychev. He repeatedly stated that their economic policy concepts are incompatible with his. Could you comment on him withdrawing his candidacy and resigning as deputy chairman of the BSP parliamentary faction? [Videnov] Georgi Pirinski did not know the entire membership of the Executive Council, but he was informed about Doncho Konakchiev and Nikola Koychev. I am sure that this is not the reason he withdrew his candidacy. I rather think that his personal decision was influenced by the plenum's discussion, which showed that the new membership is firmly determined to implement the congress decisions regarding early elections in the fall. [Encheva] Do you mean that Georgi Pirinski does not want elections? [Videnov] Only he could answer this question. I think that his decision was influenced by certain economic measures that the cabinet and parliament are adopting now. They could produce a positive effect in the fall, if we have political stability. However, it would be an illusion to hope that things will proceed at a good pace in the legislative sphere. I would not dramatize his resignation from the post deputy chairman of the parliamentary faction, because he said that he wants to work in the Supreme Council organs, and as far as I am concerned, I will help him. [passage omitted] [Encheva] Could you say when there will be elections? [Videnov] The elections issue must be resolved this year. Theoretically, this proposal was already discussed by the BSP leadership last fall. Now we are certain that early elections will take place. [Encheva] Prior to the congress you were more categorical regarding having the elections this fall.... [Videnov] Technically, the end of October-beginning of November is the best time for elections. Afterward it will be too late to develop an election campaign, form a new cabinet, prepare the new budget, and so forth. Naturally, not all political forces take those criteria into consideration and surprises of all kinds are possible. Therefore, the degree to which one could be categorical is limited. [passage omitted] [Encheva] Do you think that the influence of Aleksandur Lilov and Andrey Lukanov over you has decreased after the 41st BSP Congress? [Videnov] The influence of noted BSP figures over party policy has always been implemented through their participation in the party's leading organs and not through the party chairman. [Encheva] Do you think that Aleksandur Marinov will form an ideological current within the BSP, as he said in his STANDART NEWS interview? [Videnov] No, I do not think that our ideological differences are that great. The BSP does not need a new ideological current. The time when inner party and personal problems were resolved in such a manner is over, therefore, I think that such a step has a very low probability. I think that this is rather intensified activity on the eve of the referendum on the party program. I assume that Mr. Marinov will be among the participants in the discussion related to the party program. [passage omitted]
