Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vientiane, March 9 (KPL) -- The Ministry of Justice of Lao PDR [People's Democratic Republic] and the Australian Attorney General's Department signed on 8 March 1994 a memorandum of understanding on cooperation in legal affairs, according to a press release by the Australian embassy here. The document was signed by Somlit Chanthachak, deputy minister of justice, and Alan Rose, secretary of the Australian Attorney General's Department. It spelled out both sides' intention to consolidate their bilateral relations and cooperation. Under the agreement Australia will provide assistance to the school of laws of the Ministry of Justice. On the same occasion, Alan Rose presented law texts on different subjects, including environmental protection, business, commerce and others to the Lao Ministry of Justice. Justice Minister Kham-ouan Boupha and Australian Ambassador to Laos Michael Mann witnessed the signing of this document. While in Laos, Alan Rose held talks with a number of Lao ministers, with an emphasis on possibilities of cooperation in legal affairs between the two countries. He left here yesterday ending his five-day working visit to Laos. He also spent a short visit to Luang Prabang Province.
