930125 FT 25 JAN 93 / Public sector pay curbs attacked PAY CURBS for public-sector employees are guaranteed to provoke a hostile reaction from unions, Ms Rhiannon Chapman, director of the Industrial Society, the independent advisory and training organisation, said yesterday. She said ministers might have underestimated opposition to the 1.5 per cent wage limit, and warned of the dangers of the government's plan to abolish wages councils, which set minimum rates for low-paid workers. 'It is not obvious how the economy will be helped by licensing poverty-pay cowboys to undercut reputable companies providing a respectable service,' she wrote in the society's newsletter. 'It is possible that ministers calculate that employment legislation has emasculated unions to the point where opposition to these steps is unsustainable. They should think again.'
