Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bridgetown, Barbados, 7 Jan, CANA -- Barbados unemployment rate during the third quarter of 1993 was pegged at 25.5 percent, approximately 2.2 percentage points above the rate for the corresponding period in 1992, according to the Ministry of Economic Affairs. "The adult population was estimated at 190,200 persons, of whom 126,000 comprised the labour force," the ministry reported in a statement. "The labour force for the period July to September, 1992 was 123,700 persons. The number of persons employed during the review period was estimated at 94,100 persons, some 1,000 persons fewer than in the corresponding period in 1992." Of the total labour force, approximately 31,900 persons were unemployed during the third quarter of 1993, compared with 28,600 persons during the corresponding period in 1992. Males unemployed increased from 20.0 percent during July to September 1992 to 22.4 percent during the review period. Unemployment among females rose by 1.9 percentage points to reach 28.4 percent, compared with 26.5 percent in the July to September quarter of 1992. The under-forty age group accounted for 24,700 or 77.4 per cent of the unemployed. The government services employed 19,100 persons; general services approximately 18,100 persons; the wholesale and retail trade 15,000; tourism 10,100; manufacturing 8,000; construction and quarrying 5,000; financial, insurance and business services 6,000; and agriculture and fisheries 4,600, while another 6,500 found employment in other areas of activity. "The rate of participation during the review period was 66.3 percent compared with 65.6 percent for the corresponding period in 1992," the ministry reported. "The rate for females rose by 1.4 percentage points to reach 59.8 percent during the review period while that for males remained vitually unchanged at 73.8 percent. "The number of persons not in the labour force was 64,100 persons, some 900 fewer than during the same period in 1992. Approximately 25,900 were retired, 15,300 kept house, some 12,100 were at school while 8,500 were either incapacitated or voluntarily idle."
