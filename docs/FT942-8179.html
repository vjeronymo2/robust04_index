940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / British Gas may float business and home supply arm British Gas is considering floating its trading business, responsible for supplying gas to domestic and business customers, in an apparent U-turn on evidence it gave to last year's Monopolies and Mergers Commission inquiry into the gas industry. Both the Department of Trade and Industry and Ofgas, the industry regulator, have been informed of British Gas's plans, which would take more than a year to complete. The company is likely to give current British Gas shareholders shares in the new separate trading company rather than sell it off. The move comes as British Gas prepares to face competition in the domestic gas market. From April 1996 the entire non-household market will be opened up. At the same time 5 per cent of the household market will be opened to new suppliers, followed 12 months later by another 5 per cent tranche. Volume limits will be removed entirely in April 1998 if Ofgas is satisfied with progress. The MMC's report on the gas industry, published last August, recommended the enforced breaking off of British Gas's trading arm from its transportation assets, against the company's wishes. Mr Michael Heseltine, trade and industry secretary, rejected the MMC's proposal, recommending instead full internal separation of the trading arm from transportation. British Gas's trading arm was recently split into two parts - public gas supply and contract trading - with combined annual turnover of more than Pounds 8bn. British Gas now appears to have altered its view, believing that flotation sooner rather than later would maximise shareholder value. Mr Peter Spring, an analyst with brokers Henderson Crosthwaite, said: 'If you are a monopolist you only have one way to go when competition is introduced. Splitting off trading may help shake the operation up and prepare it for the onslaught from independent gas companies.' The company said: 'The restructuring of the company into five units is proceeding so that they have their own profit centres and accounts.'
