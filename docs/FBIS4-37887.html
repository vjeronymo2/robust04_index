BFN [Text] Bushehr, June 16, IRNA -- Preparations have been made to complete Iran's nuclear power plant in Bushehr in cooperation with Russian experts, deputy chief of the plant Mr. Khabir has said. He said that Russian experts were finishing their technical studies on the power plant and it was predicted to become operational by the end of the second five-year development plan. Russian experts will also set up a gas power station adjacent to the nuclear power plant in Bushehr, he said, but did not mention the capacity of the proposed plant or when the project would begin. Khabir said that once operational, the nuclear power plant of Bushehr would provide the country with one seventh of its needed electricity. Construction work on Bushehr's nuclear power plant started in 1974 under a German mark 7.8 billion contract with two German companies. By the end of 1978, 80 percent of the first reactor and 60 percent of the second reactor of the plant were built. During the 1980-88 Iraqi imposed war, Iraqi planes bombed the power plant 10 times, killing 12 staff members of the plant and inflicting heavy losses on it.
