930610 FT 10 JUN 93 / Inspection plan for ships attacked GOVERNMENT plans to privatise most of the ship inspection work carried out by the Department of Transport were criticised yesterday by marine insurers. Mr Roger Nixon, chairman of the Lloyds' Underwriters Association's joint hull committee, gave evidence to the Donaldson inquiry into last January's Braer oil-spill off the Shetland Islands. He warned that contracting-out ship safety checks would weaken Britain's ability to carry out its international obligations. The hearing continues.
