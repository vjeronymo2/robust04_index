920127 FT 27 JAN 92 / Car tax urged as pollution penalty CAR TAX should be adjusted in proportion to the pollution a vehicle causes, according to the Adam Smith Institute, the rightwing think tank. The institute says in a report published today: 'This would put systematic financial pressure on motorists to buy cleaner vehicles and keep them tuned to peak efficiency.' The report, by Mr Robert Taylor of the University of Chicago, advocates unorthodox solutions to save species, including private ownership of the elephant and the whale. The Market In Environment. Adam Smith Institute, 23 Great Smith St, London SW1P 3BL. Pounds 36.
