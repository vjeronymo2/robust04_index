BFN [Text] Moscow, May 19, RIA -- Russian troops will start receiving a new submachine gun already this year, said Chairman of the State Committee for Defence Industries Viktor Glukhikh. The new gun developed by designer Ivanov is called Abakan and is said to surpass the Ak-74 currently used in the Russian Army. Glukhikh noted that Abakan has already been field tested and turned out to be a high-precision weapon. Its calibre is traditional for the Russian Army (7.62) and magazine contains 30 cartridges. Glukhikh said it was too early to say when the Army would adopt new submachine guns and speak about their exports.
