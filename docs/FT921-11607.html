920130 FT 30 JAN 92 / International Company News: CCE in Dollars 1.5bn corporate bond offering COCA-COLA Enterprises (CCE), the leading US bottler of Coca-Cola which is 44 per cent owned by the parent company, yesterday sold Dollars 1.5bn of bonds, one of the largest US corporate bond issues of the past 12 months, writes Alan Friedman in New York. Salomon Brothers, the sole manager of the bond offer, said Dollars 550m of the proceeds will be used in a tender offer to buy back senior and subordinated notes issued by Johnston Coca-Cola Bottling, a unit of CCE. The remaining Dollars 950m will be used to redeem other CCE debt. The CCE offer, priced yesterday, was supposed to consist of Dollars 1bn of 10 and 30-year notes. Strong demand resulted in the issue being expanded to include Dollars 500m of 10-year notes, Dollars 250m of 20-year paper and Dollars 750m of 30-year bonds. The 10-year paper, with a 7.875 per cent coupon, was priced at par, as was the 20-year issue, with a coupon of 8.50 per cent. The 30-year bonds, was priced at 98.83 to yield 8.61 per cent. These pricings represent, respectively, spreads of 68, 78 and 89 basis points above US Treasury bonds of comparable maturities.
