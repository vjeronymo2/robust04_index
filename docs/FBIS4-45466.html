BFN [Text] Sofia, June 16 (BTA) -- The National Council for Combatting Drug Abuse and Trafficking today approved a report of the Organized Crime Control Service and the National Narcotics Service (with the Ministry of Health Care) on an inquiry conducted in the region of Sandanski, Petrich and Melnik (southwestern Bulgaria) into illegal cultivation of opium poppy, BTA learnt from the Press Office of the Council of Ministers. The Ministry of Agriculture has been assigned to draft a programme for restricting opium plantations by the end of September 1994. The council appointed a working group for updating the legislative basis on control over narcotic substances production, circulation and use, including on tightening control over poppy cultures. Mr. Pavlin Nedelchev, deputy justice minister, was appointed head of the working group. The National Council decided to update the prices of narcotic substances subject to illegal trafficking to be used for the needs of the investigation, the court and the prosecution.
