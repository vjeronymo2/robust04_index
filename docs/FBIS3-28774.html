Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington, Feb. 9 KYODO -- Japanese Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata arrived in Washington on Wednesday [9 February] night to try to break a deadlock in the stalled framework trade talks between Japan and the United States. U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor earlier in the day announced the suspension of working-level negotiations until Hata's arrival in the hope that a Japanese political decision could lead to a breakthrough. The talks, which had resumed in Washington early this week, stalled due to a squabble over how to measure Japan's efforts to open its auto and other markets. Hata is to join Friday's summit meeting between Prime minister Morihiro Hosokawa and President Bill Clinton.
