Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 25/1/1994 -- In an interview granted to the daily CRONICA ROMANA, President Ion Iliescu shows his opinions regarding the memorandum concluded between Romania and IMF and Romania's participation in the Partnership for Peace NATO programme. "The memorandum is not a document imposed on the Romanian Government by the IMF. It reflects in fact the government's economic principles negotiated and approved by the IMF, for the principles in the memorandum are part of the Romanian Government's economic reform strategy. That is why I view as false the references regarding the fact that the IMF is dictating to us, that the memorandum could have had negative effects. In fact these are issues specific to the process of transition and economic reform in Romania, directly linked to the essence of the government's economic policy. The truth is that we are facing a difficult situation which implies also specific restrictive measures that we are to self-impose on us, precisely to relaunch and bring to normality the economic life in our country." Related to Romania's participation in the NATO programme of Partnership for Peace, President Ion Iliescu stated the following: "the taken decision is a solution for which we have strived a lot. It was obvious that there were several efforts in the past years to impose a discriminatory treatment in the ties with the countries in this area... It seems to me that once with the American Administration's initiative that has carried into effect that partnership, the NATO leadership has answered Romania's fundamental concerns regarding non-discriminatory elements among the countries in this part of Europe. That is why the adopted solution seems to be the most correct one corresponding to the current period". Ion Iliescu also showed criticism over certain positions expressed in Romania against the country's participation in the Partnership for Peace programme. "To follow this line would mean nothing else but to give satisfaction precisely to those who are seeking to isolate Romania and put our country at threat as regards its security and stability".
