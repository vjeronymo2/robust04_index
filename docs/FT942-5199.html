940606 FT 06 JUN 94 / Observer: Gaza stripped A group of young Arabs is engaged in heated discussion in a West Bank cafe. As the chat takes a political turn, an older man taps one of the group on the shoulder, beckoning him aside. 'You are an Israeli soldier in disguise,' says the village elder. 'Unless you admit it and make yourself scarce I shall expose you to the rest of the gathering.' The younger man is visibly distressed. 'All right, I admit it,' he replies. 'But please, how did you recognise me? I've always been assured my Arabic accent is perfect.' 'Quite right,' says the elder. 'It is perfect. But you are the only man in that group who has said nothing critical about Yassir Arafat.'
