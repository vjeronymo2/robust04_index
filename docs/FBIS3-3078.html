Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Lilian Wu] [Text] Taipei, March 15 (CNA) -- Chang Feng-hsu, president of the Chinese Taipei Olympic Committee, will lobby for Taiwan's bid to host the 2002 Asian Games while at the executive board meeting of the Olympic Council of Asia. The meeting, to be held March 26 and 27 in Bangkok, Thailand, will focus on reviewing some of the progress the city has made since it was chosen to host 1998 Asian Games. The site of the 2002 Asian Games is expected to be decided during the 1994 Asian Games in Hiroshima, Japan this October. Meanwhile, the Chinese Taipei Olympic Committee has selected the southern port city of Kaohsiung to represent Taiwan in its bid to host the 2002 Asian Games. Kaohsiung beat out Taipei 12 to 7 with one absentation during voting Monday [14 March]. The Olympic Committee' decision will be sent to the Ministry of Education and the Executive Yuan to await final approval.
