921130 FT 30 NOV 92 / InterCity's passenger's Charter fails to meet required levels BRITISH RAIL'S InterCity sector is set to end the first year of the Passenger's Charter with all seven of its 'routes' (groups of lines) failing to meet the required levels of punctuality, reliability or both. Three of its routes - Anglia, West Coast and Cross Country - are falling so far short of their punctuality targets that they look likely to trigger 5 per cent discounts for passengers renewing season tickets. Two more routes - Midland Main Line and Gatwick Express - are performing so poorly on reliability that these, too, look likely to trigger 5 per cent discounts. The Great Western and East Coast routes are also failing to meet one or both targets. In neither case, however, does the margin reach the level necessary to trigger discounts. InterCity says the targets it has been set are unrealistic because they require all routes to perform to the standards of punctuality and reliability regardless of whether they have been modernised. It wants separate targets for each route.
