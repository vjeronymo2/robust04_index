BFN [Excerpts] On the sidelines of the proceedings of the Arab Maghreb Union [AMU] summit, an official ceremony was held yesterday at the Foreign Ministry during which 11 Maghrebian agreements on cooperation were signed. These agreements pertain to a number of fields, mainly the economic, cultural and trade sectors. The most important of these agreements is the decision to set up a free trade zone among the five Maghrebian countries. These agreements were signed on the Tunisian side by Foreign Minister Habib Ben Yahya; for Algeria by Foreign Minister Mohamed Salah Dembri; for Morocco by Foreign Minister Abdellatif Filali; for Mauritania by Foreign Minister Mohamed Salem Ould Lekhel; for Libya by Mr. Jum'ha al-Fazzani, General People's Committee secretary for unity. [passage omitted: officials welcome signing of these agreements] The Tunisian secretary of state for foreign affairs said it has been agreed to start the practical implementation of the free trade zone agreement immediately, and that a working group will begin preparing articles regarding this agreement as from the current month of April.
