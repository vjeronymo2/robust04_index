BFN [Report by Foreign Ministry spokesman Radko Vlaykov] [Text] London, May 19 -- Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov started his working visit to Britain today. Foreign Minister Daskalov's visit is a continuation of Bulgaria's efforts to maintain regular dialogue with the EU [European Union] member states. The most important of Mr. Daskalov's meetings here was his working lunch and meeting with British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd and with other senior Foreign Office representatives. The two sides discussed a wide range of issues of mutual interest and Balkan security issues. They exchanged opinions on the situation in the former Yugoslavia and in Bosnia-Herzegovina in particular. The Bulgarian foreign minister recalled Bulgaria's stand against Balkan countries' participation in the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] in Bosnia-Herzegovina. The two sides also discussed issues of bilateral relations. Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov also visited the British Parliament where he met Michael Marshall, chairman of the Interparliamentary Group and chairman of the Group for British-Bulgarian Parliamentary Cooperation.
