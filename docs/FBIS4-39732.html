BFN [Text] Baku, 9 June, IRNA -- Iranian ambassador to Baku 'Ali Asghar Nahavandiyan and Azeri Minister of National Security Nariman Imranof on Wednesday explored ways of confronting certain elements suspected of disturbing Iranian people residing in Azerbaijan. Nahavandian complained about the troubles created for Iranian Government employees in Baku, businessmen and passengers and called on the Azeri Government to prosecute such "irresponsible" elements causign troubles for Iranian nationals. Imranof, referring to the "friendly" relations between Tehran and Baku, reiterated that his ministry was responsible for protecting the life and properties of Iranians residing in Azerbaijan. He invited his Iranian counterpart 'Ali Fallahiyan to pay an official visit to his country and expressed hope that in the upcoming visit to Tehran of Azeri President Heydar Aliyev agreements on border and security cooperation would be signed by the two countries.
