930413 FT 13 APR 93 / Observer: Tongue tied Is this another Silicon Valley in the making? The favoured spot for Britain's new breed of telephone bankers is. . . . West Yorkshire. Firstdirect, the Midland Bank pioneer, is based just outside Leeds and now it has been joined in Bradford by PrimeLine, National Westminster's experimental competitor. Market researchers say that the spot was picked not for its water, cheapness of labour, or its natives' ability to count money, but because of the soothing West Yorkshire accent. Firstdirect spent a lot of money finding out which regional accent the British most liked. Bottom of the table came Liverpool scouse because most people found it too harsh. Scottish and cockney were also rejected. This left West Yorkshire, which sounded warm and friendly. Both banks now claim that they have access to staff whom customers like the sound of.
