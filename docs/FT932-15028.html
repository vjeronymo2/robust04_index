930415 FT 15 APR 93 / International Company News: Salomon debt downgraded by Standard & Poor's SALOMON, the US securities house, has had about Dollars 8bn of outstanding debt downgraded by Standard & Poor's, the US credit rating agency. The downgrade, which could push up the cost of Salomon's borrowings, reflected the firm's reliance on proprietary trading activity 'which has increased its vulnerability to quarterly earnings volatility,' S&P said. Salomon's longer-term senior debt rating was cut to single A minus from single A. Its subordinated debt rating fell to triple B plus from single A minus. Preferred stock received a rating of triple B from triple B plus, and the commercial paper rating was cut to A-2 from A-1. Mr Donald Howard, chief financial officer said: 'Some rating agencies focus too heavily on the short-term volatility of Salomon's earnings and give inadequate weight to our results measured over longer time-frames and to the exceptional quality and liquidity of our assets.'
