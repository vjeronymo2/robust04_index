BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Viktor Demidenko, Mikhail Melnik, and Galina Nekrasova] [Excerpts] Lvov, 12 Jun -- Today, for the first time, Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk unveiled his program for the 26 June presidential elections during a working trip to Lvov Oblast. At a meeting at Lvov Polytechnic State University with representatives of the authorities of the western region of Ukraine, businessmen, the intelligentsia, and clergy, Leonid Kravchuk spoke of the main tenets of his program. Its main thesis is "to strengthen the state and speed up the reforms through civic accord." He sees his main task as head of government as ensuring that "the economic reforms get going and that people begin to live better lives," that Ukraine's state construction moves ahead. Speaking of the Crimean problem, Leonid Kravchuk stressed that "no matter how events develop, Ukraine will not allow the territorial integrity of the state, its unity, to be violated. Regardless of what the Supreme Council, the government, the president of the Crimean autonomous entity do, everything will be done to ensure that events on the peninsula do not spill over the bounds of the Constitution of Ukraine. There can be no alternative here." [passage omitted] During his trip today Leonid Kravchuk unveiled a monument in Lvov's central boulevard to Mikhail Grushevskiy who, in 1917-18, was the first president of the Ukrainian People's Republic. The president of Ukraine left for Kiev in the evening.
