The 570 million U.S. dollar resettlement project should be held up as an example to follow in future resettlement, said a World Bank official, after an on-the-spot investigation of the resettlement area. The first group of resettlers have moved into 12 new villages where water and electricity supply is available. "We used to live in cave dwellings," a local peasant Tu told the World Bank official. "Now the conditions are much better. [no closing quotation mark as received] Persisting in the principle of resettlement through development, the local government has built houses totaling 220,000 square meters and created a dozen hectares of new land. The 12 new villages are accessible by roads. In addition, more than 800 local peasants have been given jobs in more than 30 old and new industrial enterprises. The 300 households from the Xiaolangdi village of Mengjin County, Henan Province, have moved into new detachment houses [as received]. The village has also built a primary school and a kindergarten. Last year the village invested three million yuan in a new steel rolling mill which may produce 40-million yuan worth of products a year. Located 40 km north of Luoyang city, in central China's Henan Province, the Xiaolangdi water control project, China's second largest after the Three Gorges Dam on the middle reaches of the Chang Jiang, will play an important role in diverting the Yellow River, handling floods, generating power and controlling sediment, according to experts. The project will displace 180,000 rural people of 190 villages in eight counties of Henan and Shanxi Provinces. In addition, five million sq. m. [square meters] of houses and cave dwellings, and seats of 11 township governments, 250 factories and enterprises will have to be moved. The officials in charge of resettlement of Xiaolangdi water control project said the relocation of residents from the construction area completed so far was only a very small part of the entire resettlement work for Xiaolangdi Dam, and greater efforts should be made to relocate the remaining 170,000 rural population from the dam area. According to the resettlement plan, 154,000 more rural residents will have to be relocated by the turn of this century, while the remaining 17,000 will be relocated during the second phase during 2010-2011 period. The local officials added that supervised management system will be introduced in resettlement work of Xiaolangdi water control dam, which means that specially-assigned engineers will carry out an overall supervised management on relocation of resettlers, and social and economic appraisal organizations are also supposed to give an objective and fair assessment of the resettlement, in a bid to protect the legitimate rights of resettlers.
