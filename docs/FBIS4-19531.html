BFN [Text] Talks between the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, and the German chancellor, Mr. Helmut Kohl, were held in Bonn today. The two leaders are understood to have discussed bilateral relations as well as regional and international issues. The talks were marked by extreme warmth and cordiality which are the hallmark of the relations between Pakistan and Germany. During the talks, the prime minister is understood to have put forward a number of proposals for expanding economic cooperation between the two countries. She also briefed the chancellor about the latest situation in occupied Kashmir and reiterated Pakistan's firm belief that the Kashmir dispute could be resolved only by the grant of right of self-determination to the Kashmiris. She said Pakistan was ready to enter into a dialogue with India provided it lifts the siege of Hazratbal Shrine, puts an end to the ongoing repression, and considerably reduces the presence of its troops in occupied Kashmir. The talks continued during the luncheon hosted by the German chancellor in honor of the prime minister.
