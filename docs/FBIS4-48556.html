BFN [Text] His Excellency President Bakili Muluzi has appealed to all political parties and people of this country to forget the past and work together constructively toward the development of Malawi. President Muluzi was speaking at the Kamuzu Stadium in Blantyre today after being sworn in as the first president of a multiparty Malawi. He said the democracy which Malawi has now embraced should be used constructively for the welfare of every Malawian. President Muluzi also stressed the need to coexist peacefully and to tolerate people of different political views. The president pledged that his government would endeavor to develop the available resources in the country for the betterment of every citizen. He said his government's priorities are poverty alleviation and food security. In his inaugural speech President Muluzi also declared that he would not condone corruption and mismanagement of the country's resources because the country is poor. He said he will ensure that measures are put in place to check corruption. The president then promised to improve the efficiency of the civil service to make it more effective in discharging government services and enhancing the country's economic development. He explained that he was aware that the morale in the present civil service was low and promised to look into the issue soon. Commenting on the democratization process in Malawi, President Muluzi said he would make sure that democracy is never curtailed in the country. In order to sustain this process and to improve human rights in the country, the president ordered the closure of (Mukuyu), (Nzaleka), and Nsanje prisons which, he said, were used to abuse human rights. He also ordered the release of all political prisoners and commuted death sentences to life imprisonment. President Muluzi also allowed human rights organizations to visit this country's prisons without any hindrance. In his speech, the president declared the new state house in Lilongwe as the new parliament and also announced that a committee would be set up to dispose the country's many state presidencies. President Muluzi then thanked the international community for supporting Malawi's democratization process and hoped for continued assistance. He also expressed gratitude to churches in Malawi for the leading role they played in changing the country's political system and urged them to continue being the society's watchdogs. Turning to outgoing President Ngwazi [Paramount] Dr. Kamuzu Banda, President Muluzi said he deserves respect and he will be remembered for the role he played in this country. At the ceremony which was attended by thousands upon thousands of people, Mr. Justin Malewezi was also sworn in as vice president.
