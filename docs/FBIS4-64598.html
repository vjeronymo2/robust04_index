BFN [Text] The government of Finland has welcomed the outcome of the vote of the European Parliament on Finland's EU [European Union] membership. Prime Minister Esko Aho described the outcome of the vote as a positive piece of news, and Foreign Trade Minister Pertti Salolainen as a dramatically good result. The government was delighted that a larger majority than expected of European deputies voted for Finland's membership. The government was following the voting by the European Parliament at its evening meeting at the Prime Minister's residence in Kesaeranta. The government agreed at its meeting that the date for the EU national referendum and the size of national subsidies will be decided by the 26 May.
