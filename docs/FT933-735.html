930928 FT 28 SEP 93 / Bottomley warns on Pounds 13bn sick bill INDUSTRY cannot afford the Pounds 13bn cost of sick days off work, Mrs Virginia Bottomley, health secretary, warned yesterday. Alcohol, smoking-related diseases and mental illness were mainly responsible, she told a Confederation of British Industry conference. Mrs Bottomley said: 'Avoidable ill-health is a burden the country cannot afford. 'In 1979 strikes cost our country 29m lost working days. Today we lose twice that through alcohol and tobacco-related illness every year.'
