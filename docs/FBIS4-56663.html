BFN [Passages within quotation marks in Creole] [Excerpts] The National Reconstruction Movement [MRN] of Rene Theodore has demanded the resignation of General Raoul Cedras as a way out of the deadlock. MRN leader Rene Theodore, former prospective prime minister, explained in a statement on Signal FM that the departure of Gen. Raoul Cedras is necessary to avoid a military intervention in Port-au-Prince: [Begin Theodore recording] "Instead of speaking of military intervention, since the Haitian military institution is not hostile toward anyone, the foreign powers should carry out their commitment to bring military cooperation to Haiti to help discipline the Haitian Army, professionalize the officers and noncommissioned officers of the Haitian Army, and establish the police force provided for by the Haitian Constitution. They signed documents to this effect and they must fulfill their obligation." [end recording] Theodore also calls on the sectors involved in the crisis to work toward a respect for legality and legitimacy. MRN demands the return of President Jean-Bertrand Aristide to avoid the reinforcement of sanctions against the country. Theodore reminds parliamentarians of the difficult crossroads the country is facing: [Begin Theodore recording] "[Passage omitted] It is very clear to everyone that the resolution of the crisis will begin with the resignation of Gen. Cedras. MRN demands the resignation of Gen. Cedras as the first step toward cancellation of the international community's actions and claims." [end recording] This was Rene Theodore who has ended his silence, his long silence, to take a stand in favor of the return of President Jean-Bertrand Aristide.
