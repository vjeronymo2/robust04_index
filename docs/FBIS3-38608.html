Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Andjelko Perincic report from the Vatican] [Text] Pope John Paul II today received Mate Granic, the Croatian deputy prime minister and foreign minister, for a private audience. The meeting, held in the Pope's private library, lasted almost half an hour. At the end of the meeting the pope also received other members of the Croatian delegation, including Smiljana Sinac, assistant foreign minister, and Ivo Livljanic, ambassador of the Republic of Croatia to the Vatican, as well as a group of Croatian journalists who are covering minister Granic's visit to Rome. It was learned from well-informed sources that during the meeting between the pope and Granic discussed the situation in our region, especially that of the Croats in the crisis areas, and a possible visit to Croatia by the pope. After the meeting Croatian Foreign Minister Mate Granic was received in the Vatican by Vatican secretary of state Cardinal Angelo Sodano.
