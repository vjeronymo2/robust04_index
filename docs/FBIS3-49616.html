Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 11/01/94 -- Gorj, a southern county of Romania, reported the lowest unemployment rate as at the end of December 1993: 3.7 percent, the National Statistics Committee informs. Following Gorj were Bucharest City with 5.5 percent, Brasov County with 6.7 and Timis with 6.9 percent. Other 23 counties reported rates below the all-nation average of 9.9 percent. At the other end of the scale, Vaslui recorded the highest rate 22.1 percent, followed by Bistrita-Nasaud with 20.3 percent, Tulcea 18.2, and Neamt 17.1 percent, all in the East of Romania. The all-Romania registered jobless were 1.1 million persons, of whom 525,000 were on the unemployment benefit scheme, over 509,000 were recipients of subsistence allowances, and another 97,000 were on record as unemployed whose benefit rights were overdue.
