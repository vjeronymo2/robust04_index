BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 16 KYODO -- Public works projects started in fiscal 1993 totaled a record 19,758.5 billion yen, up 7.8 percent from the previous year, the Construction Ministry reported Monday [16 May]. The figure, which marks the ninth straight yearly increase, reflects a series of government moves to bolster public works as part of its economic stimulus packages. The central government, public corporations and other state agencies placed orders for 4,860.7 billion yen worth of the total, up 8.1 percent. Projects financed by autonomies and other local agencies accounted for the remaining 14,897.8 billion yen worth, a 7.7 percent increase. Both figures are the highest on record. The ministry also said public works investments during March soared 67.8 percent from a year earlier to 2,353.5 billion yen.
