BFN [Excerpt] Cairo, 5 May (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has stated the importance of the signing of the Israeli-Palestinian agreement to implement self-rule in Gaza and Jericho, which took place in Cairo yesterday. In remarks he made today, Musa described the agreement as "good," noting that it is the first step toward reaching a comprehensive solution for the Palestinian issue. Musa pointed out that the implementation of the Israeli-Palestinian self-rule agreement in Gaza and Jericho will begin in a week or two, with the return of Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat and senior PLO officials. He noted that the first step will be implemented in Gaza and Jericho, to be followed by extending the Palestinian authority over the remaining Palestinian territory in the West Bank. He added: Other steps have been agreed upon and we hope the pace of the peace process will accelerate to reach the final stage as soon as possible and within two years of the implementation of the agreement. Musa emphasized that any step toward achieving peace and stability in the region will have a positive impact on all the region's countries. He noted that Egypt is interested in establishing peace and stability in the region. He pointed out that if the Arab-Israeli conflict is resolved in accordance with the resolutions of the international community and through negotiations and agreements among the parties concerned, the region will undoubtedly enter a better stage. It will be a stage of cooperation and development in which the region's peoples can enjoy peace. On U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher's recent talks in Damascus, Musa noted that such meetings show Syria's interest in the U.S. role regarding the peace process. [passage omitted]
