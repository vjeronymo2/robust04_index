BFN [XINHUA "Roundup" by Xie Dongfeng: "Israel, PLO Are Turning Words Into Deeds"] [Text] Jerusalem, May 13 (XINHUA) -- It is the ninth day after the signing of the Gaza-Jericho accord. Israel and the Palestinians are turning words into deeds smoothly on the whole, though there have been some delays in the entry of Palestinian policemen into the autonomous areas. In Jericho, Israeli and Palestinian officials will hold a brief ceremony this morning during which the authority of the city will be transferred to Palestinians, who will then take control of the civil administration building. The last Israeli troops will leave the area this afternoon, ending almost 27 years of Israeli rule. On Thursday [12 May], an advance team of 20 Palestinian officers crossed the Allenby Bridge and toured the Jericho area. Later today, they will be joined by another 40 police officers who will take control of the sites to be taken over by Palestinians. The two major military installations to be taken over are the Israeli Army base in the south and the Israeli police office in the city center. Only a handful of Israeli troops still remain there and everything is ready for a hand-over. The Palestinian police will be also stationed at the site of the ancient synagogue, although under an agreement, Israelis are guaranteed access to the synagogue. If the hand-over goes on smoothly, the area will be taken control by 426 Palestinian policemen from the Palestine Liberation Army from the Iraq-based Aqsa brigade who will arrive later this afternoon through the Allenby Bridge from Jordan. Palestinian Police Chief Mudar Yusuf said Thursday in Jericho that the first priority will be to ensure law and order and the plans are for a self-rule area open to all. So far, Jericho is still a closed military zone. An Israeli Army commander, Ilan Biran, said he issued such an order for fear of terrorist attacks both by extremist Palestinians and Jewish settlers. In Gaza, though the first entry of Palestinian policemen was much delayed until the early hours of Wednesday, the Israeli troops completed the withdrawal from Southern Gaza Thursday night. The soldiers evacuated all the outposts in Rafah although Palestinians have yet to take control of them. Earlier this morning, three positions in Khan Yunis were handed over to the Palestinian policemen. It is reported that the Gaza City, Jabalya Refugee Camp and the rest of the northern strip of Gaza are expected to be handed over to Palestinians by next Wednesday. In the coming days, contingents of PLA and Palestinian policemen will arrive in Gaza and Jericho from Arab states. Meanwhile, the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO) published a list of 15 members of the 24-member governing council for the Gaza-Jericho areas. Israel had demanded a full list before handing over the reigns of authority. Under the Gaza-Jericho accord signed in Cairo last week, the transfer of power from Israelis to Palestinians will be concluded within three weeks, that is, by May 25. Israeli Deputy Chief of General Staff Amnon Shahaq said Wednesday that the Israeli withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho could be fulfilled by the end of next week. Now it seems that the Israeli troops are speeding up their pace of withdrawal to keep its promise that it had no intention to drag on in the future autonomous areas. While the Palestinians are celebrating and preparing for their long-awaited autonomy, the Israeli Cabinet and Parliament have given green light to the Gaza-Jericho agreement to ensure support for the implementation of the accord. The opposition forces are strong, but nobody can turn the page upside down. Accompanying the implementation of the accord is a peaceful scene the two areas have never seen in these years. Both Israeli soldiers and Palestinians have shown their restraint, except for the clashes between Israeli soldiers and the anxious and impatient Palestinians awaiting the arrival of their own police Sunday. On the other hand, the Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) has stopped its terrorist actions recently, after it reached an agreement with the Fatah that it would suspend attacks on Palestinian collaborators for a month once the autonomy accord was signed. However, there were still violent elements emerging these days. Israeli security forces Thursday detained two Palestinians suspected of firing at an army post in Jabalya. Israeli border policemen arrested four men near Rafah Thursday, who were suspected of shooting and wounding an Israeli truck driver in Gush Katif in the afternoon. On Tuesday, three Israelis were wounded by gunshots fired at egged bus No. 474 in Beitel of the West Bank, but it is still not clear who the attackers were.
