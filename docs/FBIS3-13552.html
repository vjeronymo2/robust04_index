Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Fayiz Abu-al-'Aynayn, director general of the Bank of Jordan, left Amman today for the occupied Arab territories to supervise the opening of the bank's first branch in Ramallah. Muhammad 'Ali Ibrahim, the bank's assistant director general, said that work in this bank, which has been properly equipped and staffed with employees, will begin next week. He noted that the branch will open after it receives the approval of Jordan Central Bank. He added that the bank will open other branches in Nabulus, Hebron, Janin, Tulkarm, Jericho, Bethlehem, and Gaza after they are proven economically feasible and are approved by Jordan Central Bank.
