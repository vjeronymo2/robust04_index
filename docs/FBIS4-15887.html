BFN [Summary] A news conference was given by President Nursultan Nazarbayev at the national press-center in Tokyo. He said he is satisfied with the results of his first official visit to Japan. He emphasized the importance of the joint Japan-Kazakhstan committee for cooperation that unites 32 leading Japanese companies. Missions of 16 Japanese companies have opened in Kazakhstan. President Nazarbayev expressed hope that their number will increase after his visit to Japan. Nazarbayev spoke about prospects for future cooperation with Japan. According to him, the most promising areas for cooperation are the oil and railway industries, mainly in the Asiatic region. An unexpected resignation by Japanese Prime Minister Hosokawa will not affect the implementation of agreements and treaties that were signed between Kazakhstan and Japan, said Nazarabyev. The leader of Kazakhstan was not taken by suprise at Hosokawa's decision to resign. "Whenever you have a coalition with a great number of participants it is always accompanied with complications."
