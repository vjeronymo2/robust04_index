Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Tunis, 6 Jan (MENA)--Egypt and Tunisia today signed an agreement for security cooperation between the two countries. The agreement provides for security coordination; the exchange of information, expertise, and visits; and cooperation in the fields of combating terrorism, violence, and other crimes. Under the agreement, Egypt will benefit from the Tunisian experience in combating terrorism. Egyptian Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi and his Tunisian counterpart Abdallah Kallel signed the agreement at the Tunisian Interior Ministry. The Egyptian-Tunisian joint security committee concluded its meetings, which were held on the sidelines of the Arab interior ministers' meeting, last night. The new agreement seeks to upgrade the security agreement concluded last year and promote bilateral coordination in security matters, particularly efforts to combat terrorism, violence, and religious extremism.
