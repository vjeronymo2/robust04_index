940602 FT 02 JUN 94 / Hatton accused of fraud on company Mr Derek Hatton, former Labour deputy leader of Liverpool City Council, was charged in the city last night with conspiring to defraud an insurance company and the theft of a motorised horsebox. He went to a police station voluntarily with a solicitor and was arrested on his arrival. Two other men had been arrested earlier in Crumpsall, Manchester, and brought to Liverpool for questioning. Police said Mr Hatton had been charged with conspiracy to defraud Norwich Union of Pounds 20,324.08 and conspiracy to obtain Pounds 38,000 by deception from the company. He was also charged with the theft of a Pounds 38,000 motorised horsebox and attempting to obtain Pounds 38,000 by deception from Norwich Union.
