Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] EU [European Union] Commissioner on Foreign Affairs Hans Van Den Broek said that during his visit to Cyprus he reconfirmed his impression of the last few months about the Cyprus Government's firm belief in the effort to achieve a peaceful solution to the Cyprus issue, as well as its effort to harmonize, in any way, its economic and trade policy with the European Union's policy. Speaking at Larnaca Airport shortly before his departure for Athens, Van Den Broek again expressed the European Union's multiple support for the confidence-building measures, which he said are an important step toward the achievement of an overall solution to the Cyprus issue. The EU commissioner added that he has assured both President Glavkos Kliridhis and Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis that in case the confidence-building measures are implemented, the European Union is ready to offer any help and the EU commission will proceed in that direction with the presentation of proposals to the EU Council of Ministers. Foreign Minister Mikhailidhis said that Cyprus' movement toward Europe has began and will end with its accession to the European Union. Speaking at Larnaca Airport shortly before Van Den Broek's departure, the foreign minister stressed that Cyprus is working hard for its accession and has already proceeded with a series of decisions in this direction. Mikhailidhis said that the harmonization of Cypriot legislation with that of the European Union continues at a quick pace. He added that Cyprus' foreign policy will also begin to be in line with that of the European Union. Referring to the talks he had with EU Commissioner Van Den Broek, the Cypriot foreign minister described them as constructive and comprehensive.
