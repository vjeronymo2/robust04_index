BFN [Text] Fuzhou, May 12 (XINHUA) -- A foundation-stone-laying ceremony was held Tuesday for the Dongshan Economic and Technological Development Zone in Dongshan county, Fujian Province. The zone is separated by the sea from Taiwan and lies between the Xiamen Special Economic Zone (SEZ) in Fujian and the Shantou SEZ in neighboring Guangdong Province. Approved by the State Council, the 10-sq-km zone will focus on the growth of such industries as food processing, electronics, tourism souvenirs and new building materials, according to an official of the zone administration commission. Chinese and overseas investors will also be encouraged to expand the financial, commercial and real estate sectors in the zone, the official said. He said that the Dongshan county government is doing everything within its power to create a fine investment environment in the zone. To date, the zone has invested 40 million yuan in building three km of roads and a substation, and installing a program-controlled switchboard with 6,000 lines, he said. A port in the zone has opened to foreign ships, he said. With two berths for ships of 5,000 dwt, it is capable of handling 9,000 standard containers annually. Regular container ship services between Dongshan and Hong Kong began May 1 this year.
