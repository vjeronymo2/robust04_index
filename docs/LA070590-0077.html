July 5, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

IT'S SURPRISE, SURPRISE FOR PARENTS OF TWIN BOYS;

BIRTHS: RITA AND BILL WILLIAMSON EXPECTED BIG BABIES, BUT ONE WEIGHED MORE THAN 11 POUNDS, THE OTHER MORE THAN 9 POUNDS.

Big babies run in the Williamson family. But nobody was prepared for twin boys -- one 11 pounds, 8 ounces, the other, 9 pounds, 10 ounces -- least of all mom Rita Williamson.

"I was all baby," said Rita Williamson, 27, who was resting Wednesday at the Los Alamitos Hospital birthing center. "I'm kind of tired now, so I'm glad it's over."

Craig Allen and Thomas Arthur were born Monday by Cesarean section. Craig, the biggest, was first at 8:21 a.m. A minute later, little brother Thomas followed. Both measured more than 21 inches.

"They're the largest twins the hospital has ever had," nursing supervisor Josephine Vantrees said. "They were pretty enormous."

Twins usually average about six pounds. But the Williamson twins are bigger than most single-birth babies, which average around seven pounds. A single birth baby weighing more than nine pounds is considered large, Vantrees said.

The twins and mom will join their sister, Tanya, 2, and dad, Bill, at home in Cerritos once Williamson recovers, which is expected to be within a few days.

Rita Williamson said she tried to stay cool during the past two months, despite having to carry around 56 extra pounds. "I'm a pretty big woman to start with," she said, "but the babies added more around the tummy."

She said the hardest part of her pregnancy came during the wave of triple-digit temperatures last month. She coped by wearing loose clothing, staying in air-conditioned rooms and doing as little as possible. Even just walking seemed like strenuous exercise.

The couple discovered that two very big boys were on the way Friday, when Rita had an ultrasound test.

"We expected them to be little guys 'cause, you know, they're twins," said Bill Williamson, a computer programmer. "But I guess we were more than lucky."

By delivery time, the Williamsons were "geared for big babies." Rita had had some experience with an unusual birth -- Tanya had been 9 pounds, 12 ounces and in the breech position.

"The two of them have already beaten me in a way," says Bill, who was 8 pounds, 7 ounces at birth. "They got me by a couple of ounces.

Bill already foresees a great future in football for his sons. Craig, the bigger one, is going to be a linebacker, and Thomas, no small potato himself, is going to be a lineman, he predicts.

"We're waiting for USC to call," Williamson quipped. "I bet they can take crawlers for the next season."
