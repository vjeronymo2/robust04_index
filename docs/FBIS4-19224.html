BFN [Unattributed report: "Thaw in Trade With Iran"] [Text] On Tuesday [12 April], during Iranian Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati's visit to Austria, the interest of the media focused on human rights and the Rushdie case. However, during Velayati's talk with Economics Minister Schuessel, the focus was on the revival of Austrian-Iranian trade: In 1993 Austria's exports to Iran decreased by 20 percent and imports even by 29 percent. The reason was Iranian payment difficulties and, as a result, nonliability by the Austrian Control Bank. In mid-March at least the obstacle of financing and liability was overcome: In bilateral negotiations in mid-March, Austria rescheduled Iranian debts amounting to 5 million schillings.
