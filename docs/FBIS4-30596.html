BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 28 KYODO -- The government will continue to make efforts to open Japan's markets following the release Tuesday [28 June] of a package of deregulatory measures aiming at expanding domestic demand and boosting imports, Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa said. Kakizawa told a press conference the Foreign Ministry will explain the package to the United States, the European Union and other nations. The package, a detailed version of portions of a market-opening program released in March, contains 279 deregulatory steps prepared by the government's administrative reform promotion headquarters. Kakizawa said the latest package meets some of the demands made by other countries. Japan pledged to come up with deregulatory measures before the Naples summit of the Group of Seven industrialized nations in July.
