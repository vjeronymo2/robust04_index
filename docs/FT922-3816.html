920609 FT 09 JUN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Professor Roy Goode QC appointed to head independent review of occupational pension schemes PROFESSOR Roy Goode QC, (left) the Professor of English Law at Oxford University appointed yesterday to head an independent review of occupational pension schemes, is one the UK's foremost academic commercial lawyers, Robert Rice writes. Professor Goode, who is 59, was a member of the Monopolies and Mergers Commission from 1981 to 1986 and is currently a member of the Department of Trade and Industry's committee on arbitration law and of the Council of the Banking Ombudsman. He has also been a member of the United Nations committee on international trade law. In 1988 he was called to the Bar by Inner Temple and was made a QC in 1990.
