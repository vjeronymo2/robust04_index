December 28, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

U.S. RULING ON SCHOLARSHIPS

In his article on minority scholarships (Commentary, Dec. 18) Clarence Page quotes Booker T. Washington: "No greater injury can be done to any youth than to let him feel that because he belongs to this or that race he will be advanced regardless of his own merit or efforts."

Certainly there are many minority youths who would be willing to suffer these same injuries so long endured by white, Protestant males.

CAROL BARKIN

Studio City
