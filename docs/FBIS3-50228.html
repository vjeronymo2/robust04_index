Language: Bulgarian Article Type:BFN ["Recorded comments" by parliamentary deputies and party leaders on Prime Minister Berov's statement on national radio on 25 January -- recorded] [Text] How do the Bulgarian Socialists assess Prime Minister Lyuben Berov's statement on national radio today. Here is a comment by Nora Ananieva, chairman of the parliamentary group of the BSP [Bulgarian Socialist Party] and its coalition: [Ananieva] The prime minister's viewpoint was realistic, in the sense that stability, our authority, continuing the reform without interruption, and developing our international contacts all depend on the budget being passed. [Announcer] Professor Lyuben Berov's address provoked a quick reaction from the SDS [Union of Democratic Forces] coalition. Here is what Filip Dimitrov, chairman of the SDS National Coordinating Council, had to say: [Dimitrov] We have long considered that the only reasonable alternative to the chaos that reigns in the country's economy and the drama that is developing on the streets of Bulgarian towns is to hold new elections. Accordingly, in making his statement, the prime minister hardly discovered America. As regards the issue of the budget, in his statement the prime minister made a fetish, as it were, of the act of adopting a law, but disregarded its contents. [Announcer] The parliamentary group of the Rights and Freedoms Movement [DPS] will support the initiative calling for a special plenary session of parliament on the state budget bill, but the DPS deputies do not consider that 16 February is a realistic deadline for adopting the budget. Here is a comment from DPS parliamentary representative Osman Oktay on the prime minister's statement: [Oktay] I would describe the prime minister's statement on the budget as somewhat overhasty. We have assured him -- we have given an assurance in the proper place -- that we will do everything possible to speed up the submission of the budget to a plenary session of the National Assembly. [Announcer] DPS deputies expressed their disquiet over the delays in the examination of the budget by certain parliamentary commissions. According to them, the early submission of the budget bill to the full parliament is of importance primarily in connection with our commitments under the agreement with the international financial institutions. According to Deputy Oktay, the president will probably meet with the DPS leader [Ahmed Dogan] next week. According to DPS Deputy Serife Mustafa, this meeting may take place tomorrow.
