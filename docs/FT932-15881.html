930408 FT 08 APR 93 / UK Company News: Pelican seeks Pounds 7.4m to fund purchase THE PELICAN Group is paying Mr Robert Earl, who made his fortune creating the Hard Rock Cafe chain, Pounds 6m for four UK restaurants. The purchase will be funded by a Pounds 7.4m placing and open offer. The Mamma Amalfi restaurants and Rock Island Diner had warranted pre-tax profits of Pounds 800,000 in the year ended March 31 against Pounds 767,000 a year earlier. Pelican will pay the vendor, Worldwide Leisure Holdings, controlled by a trust which benefits Mr Earl's family, Pounds 3m in Pelican shares and Pounds 3m in cash. The open offer to shareholders is on a 7-for-10 basis at 50p per share. After Pounds 400,000 of expenses, Pelican will retain Pounds 4m cash from the fund raising. The shares closed yesterday up 8p at 58p. At the same time Mr Richard Myers, Pelican chairman, is placing 800,000 shares, at 50p apiece, to repay a personal bank loan. Pelican will also receive Pounds 75,000 a year for three years from licence agreements.
