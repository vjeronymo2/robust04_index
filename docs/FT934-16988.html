931004 FT 04 OCT 93 / The Russian Crisis: Leaders' plea for backing 'I BELIEVE in our common sense and our strength,' declared President Boris Yeltsin as he mustered support to put down yesterday's turmoil. 'We have a great deal more of these than those adventurers who are shooting at Muscovites,' he said in a statement read out for him on television. He said he had ordered the dismissal of Vice-President Alexander Rutskoi, who had spurred the revolt on. He had also dismissed the Afghan war hero from the armed forces. 'Today the fate of Russia and the fate of our children is being decided. The forces of civil war will not succeed,' he said, adding: 'We will triumph.' In another address to Muscovites, First Deputy Prime Minister Yegor Gaidar warned that a 'bloody, brown curtain' of fascism would fall on Russia if the revolt succeeded. Much depended on the support of the population, he said. 'If today we let into power those who are grasping for it, if we place the keys of government in their hands, they are capable of bringing a bloody brown curtain down over the country for decades.'
