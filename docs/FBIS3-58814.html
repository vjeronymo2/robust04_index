Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] On the first anniversary of the "Sea of Lights," a torchlight procession from Vienna's Ballhaus Square to St. Stephen's Square was organized by the "SOS Fellow Man" group to protest against isolating foreigners, underprivileged, and poor people ("Human beings come first"). A total of 5,000 people participated in the procession. [passage omitted] In St. Stephen's Square, the number of participants increased to 10,000. During the final rally, writer Johannes Mario Simmel warned against newly developing right-wing extremism. He said: "The Nazis are on the advance like never before since 1945." He said the letter-bomb attacks in Austria are only a "preliminary culmination point." [passage omitted].
