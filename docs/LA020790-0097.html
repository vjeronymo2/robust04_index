February 7, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

CHINA CURB ON STUDY ABROAD TOLD

The government has decided to sharply restrict overseas study by requiring Chinese university graduates to work for five years before they become eligible to study abroad, officials said Tuesday.

Western educational specialists said the government wants to use the five-year work rule to halt a "hemorrhaging" flow of tens of thousands of talented young people who have left to study in the West but often fail to return to China.

The decision to impose the new rule follows President Bush's recent promise to extend the visas of Chinese students in the United States.
