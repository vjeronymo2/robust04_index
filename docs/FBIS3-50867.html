Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Excerpt] The attorney general of the Republic and the finance minister have commented on the ruling issued by the 3d Court for Tax Issues and agreed it does not invalidate the collection of the value-added tax [IVA]. Instead, it urges the Executive Branch to better publicize the law. [Begin recording] [Attorney General Ricardo Ernst Contreras] The law is still in effect. A court ruling in connection with a request submitted by anyone does not derogate a law. Laws are only derogated by other laws. Laws are also derogated when someone submits an appeal for annulment because the law violates the constitution -- and the appeal has sufficient grounds. This can involve the whole law or some of its provisions. Also, a law can be rescinded in connection with a given process because one of the interested parties claims the law violates the Constitution. However, the ruling issued by such a court in connection with such an appeal obviously cannot derogate a law. [passage omitted] [end recording]
