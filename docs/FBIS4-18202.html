BFN [Unattributed article: "Finland Becomes 14th Member of European Space Agency"; as released by EUROPEAN REPORT] [Text] Finland has just joined the ranks of the European Space Agency (ESA). The Secretary-General of Finland's Ministry for Trade and Industry Matti Vuoria, and ESA Director-General Jean Marie Lutton signed an Agreement which admits Finland as the 14th member of the Agency. Finland will officially receive the official status of full participating member on January 1, 1995, once its Parliament has ratified the Agreement, and will then join its two Scandinavian neighbours, Norway and Sweden, which are already ESA members. Since 1987, Finland had held the status of associate member, and is already involved in a number of ESA programmes. The new member intends to become more involved in space science programmes, as well as earth observation and satellite telecommunications. ESA member countries are: Austria, Belgium, Denmark, France, Germany, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland and the UK.
