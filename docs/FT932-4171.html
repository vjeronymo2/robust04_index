930610 FT 10 JUN 93 / UK Company News: Revamped Albrighton cuts losses ALBRIGHTON, the heavily-restructured quarrying group, reported a sharply reduced deficit over the 12 months to March 31. On turnover of Pounds 2.45m (Pounds 2.53m from continuing operations) and after reduced interest charges of Pounds 277,000 (Pounds 684,000), losses before tax were reduced to Pounds 358,000 (Pounds 14.1m restated for FRS 3). The USM-quoted group has made a number of disposals over the past two years to reduce debt. Following the Pounds 3.2m acquisition of five quarries and two masonry processing plants late last year from Tarmac - the acquisition was 'substantially completed and going well' - it now focused on natural dimensional stone products. Mr Humphrey Wood, chairman, said the outcome was affected by rationalisation and redundancy costs; the group still had a bad debt problem and trading activity was at a low level, although the current year had got off to a 'promising' start. Losses per share were 0.6p (225.1p).
