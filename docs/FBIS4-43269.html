BFN [Text] Government Spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis described President Glavkos Kliridhis' decision to resign if the UN Security Council continues discussion of the confidence-building measures, as a strictly personal choice. President Kliridhis, the spokesman said, weighed the developments very seriously before reaching this decision and decided if resigning from the presidency would make the future of Cypriot Hellenism easier, he would not hesitate to do so. Responding to a question, the government spokesman said that if the president resigns, he would not seek the presidency again. Kasoulidhis also said that if President Kliridhis resigns and calls for new elections, the elections would be a popular mandate on a specific issue. Kasoulidhis also said that there has been no reaction from the United States or Britain to the letter that President Kliridhis sent to President Bill Clinton and Prime Minister John Major. The spokesman refused to reveal the contents of the letter, saying the letter was confidential.
