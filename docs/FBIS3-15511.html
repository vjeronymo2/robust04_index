Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Congress of Peoples of Ingushetia, a republic in Russia's North Caucasus, on Sunday qualified the performance of the republic's government as unsatisfactory. The government, dismissed by decree of President Ruslan Aushev as of March 6, will remain in office until parliament takes a final decision. The Congress passed a resolution which says recent re-election of Aushev as president of Ingushetia showed that the people approved of his internal and foreign policy. Aushev himself, in a message to the congress, described the presidential and parliamentary elections and referendum on the republic's constitution, all held on February 27, as an historic step towards Ingushetia's revival as a state.
