Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Belinform correspondent Larisa Klychnikova for TASS] [Text] Minsk February 2 TASS -- A meeting of the parliamentary assembly of the CIS will be held in the Tavria Palace in St. Petersburg on February 8-9. The delegations will be represented by heads of parliament of CIS countries. The assembly will concentrate on debating the candidature of chairman of the interparliamentary assembly, Ivan Korotchenya, executive secretary of the CIS, told BELINFORM. Korotchenya does not rule out the possibility of Ivan Rybkin or of Vladimir Shumeyko, Russian representatives, being appointed chairman of the parliamentary assembly. The 15 items planned to be debated include legal regulation of relations between the CIS countries in the framework of the economic union -- coordination and unification of the economic policy and bringing closer legislations of states of the commonwealth. The meeting will also discuss a long-range plan and results of the interparliamentary group's activity on peace settlement in Nagorno-Karabakh.
