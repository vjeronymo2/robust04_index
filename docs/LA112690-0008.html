November 26, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

RABBI KAHANE'S CONTRIBUTIONS

It is symptomatic of the leftist detractors of the Jewish people, many of whose vociferous exponents are unfortunately themselves Jewish, that they misunderstand the phenomenon of Rabbi Kahane.

Sprinzak's assessment of Kahane's popular support is absolutely wrong.

We know that this is true due to the fear of the so-called "right-wing" party and its collaboration with the leftists to attempt to liquidate Rabbi Kahane through parliamentary maneuvering. It is this "right-wing" that had the most to fear of the rabbi: a substantial loss of financial support and portfolios in government. His prophetic prediction of an emasculated Likud government, financially subservient to the largess of U.S., has been proven correct. This poses as a real danger to Israel.

The truth that the leftists and their Arab supporters fail to see is that there cannot, nor will there be, any peaceful coexistence between Arabs and Jews in our land.

The leftists and Jewish apologists refuse and abhor the concept of the revelation of Mt. Sinai, decreeing the exclusivity of the land of Israel to the Jewish people. What they mistakenly mistake for "neo-religious ideas about redemption" and the "indivisibility of the land of Israel" as undesirable are nothing more than a genuine, healthy Jewish reawakening of their identity as a people, a reacceptance of the yoke of G-d's kingdom.

For those of us whose tears are not yet dried, the painful loss of Rabbi Kahane will only mean a redoubling of our efforts in the causes he dedicated his life for.

RABBI NACHUM SHIFREN, Director, Center for Jewish Activism, Santa Monica
