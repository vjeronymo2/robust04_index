921106 FT 06 NOV 92 / International Company News: Brazilian pulp company moves into the black ARACRUZ Celulose, the world's largest eucalyptus pulp company, posted net profits of Dollars 9.1m for the first three nine months, against Dollars 90m losses a year ago. Mr Mauro Molchansky, the company's chief financial officer, attributed the improved performance to a recuperation in world pulp prices, a reduction of local sales tax, an increase in production and economy of scale due to the coming on stream of a Dollars 1.3bn expansion project. Gross operating revenues for nine months were Dollars 345m, up 44 per cent and production was 33 per cent up at 760,000 tonnes. 'We are now running at full capacity and expect to hit 1m tonnes this year,' Mr Molchansky said. Exports took more than 87 per cent of sales.
