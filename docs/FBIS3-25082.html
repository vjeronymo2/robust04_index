Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Jonas Savimbi [leader of National Union for the Total Independence of Angola - UNITA] has explained his absence from Houphouet-Boigny's funeral in Cote d'Ivoire by referring to the situation in Cuito. Despite that, Portuguese Secretary of State for Cooperation Briosa e Gala will take the opportunity to make some contacts concerning the war in Angola. More details from Cote d'Ivoire from Fatima Torres: [Torres] [passage omitted] All the journalists have been talking about Angola, but with the absence of Portuguese President Mario Soares and UNITA leader Jonas Savimbi, the expectation of some impressions being exchanged on the Angolan issue have vanished. Eugenio Manuvakola confirmed that Savimbi is being represented here by his sister and the UNITA secretary general, due to the escalation of the fighting in Angola. Everybody was expecting Jonas Savimbi to come here because he had a strong friendship and polical links with the deceased President Houphouet-Boigny. On the other hand, the Angolan Government sent a strong representation headed by the parliament speaker and the foreign minister. [passage omitted]
