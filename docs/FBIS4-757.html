BFN [Text] Lagos, 14 Apr (PANA) -- France denied Thursday [14 April] in Lagos that the recent joint military exercise it held with Benin, Nigeria's western neighbour, was aimed at intimidating Nigeria in the event of an out-break of hostilities with Cameroon over the disputed Bakassi Peninsula. French defence attache in Nigeria, Col. John Eglinger, said that the exercise was planned "about a year ago," long before the current disagreement between Nigeria and Cameroon. Col. Eglinger said that Nigeria had been duly informed about the plans for holding the joint exercise. Concerning France's position in the Bakassi dispute between Nigeria and its former colony of Cameroon, englinger said that France was committed to ""an amicable resolution"" of the conflict. He reiterated France's preparedness to assist mediate in the dispute which, he said, France believed could be resolved through "bilateral discussion." France held its military exercise with Benin in March, an operation interpreted by military sources in the Nigerian capital as an effort to intimidate the Nigerian Army should the dispute over the oil-rich Bakassi Peninsula escalate into a military confrontation between Nigeria and Cameroon. France had allegedly used an unusually "large" arsenal including war planes and war ships in the exercise which lasted for several days.
