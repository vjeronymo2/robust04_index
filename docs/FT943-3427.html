940914 FT 14 SEP 94 / Observer: Splinter group Forget the high-tech surveillance gadgetry; clearly, what national leaders need to equip themselves with are solidly-rooted trees. The magnolia that blocked the kamikaze aircraft attack on the White House is not the first example of handy arborial defences. John Major and his cabinet perhaps owe their lives to the cherry-tree that deflected an IRA mortar aimed at 10 Downing Street three years ago. What more proof do we need that George Washington did himself a considerable disservice?
