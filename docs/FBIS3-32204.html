Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Relay from Los Pilajones, Camaguey Province, by Adolfo Silva] [Text] Pedro Ross, secretary general of the Cuban Workers Federation and a Politburo member, has stated that during the workers' parliaments in the country, the workers convey their political perception and patriotic spirit in facing current problems. In remarks to the press in Camaguey, Ross praised the positive development of these parliaments, during which the workers express ideas and propose solutions. Ross today chaired the first of the three parliaments at Camaguey University scheduled to discuss the economic and financial situation in the country and at the university. Among the suggestions proposed by the workers, to be implemented at the university, are the better use of the budget without affecting the educational process, increasing the [word indistinct] of outstanding students, solutions to the food self-supply difficulties, and the control of resources. The list of proposals also includes ceiling prices for products sold by self-employed vendors, paying for tickets to sports activities, collecting fees from foreign students who can afford to pay them, tax exemption for salaries, and increasing economic activity at the regional level.
