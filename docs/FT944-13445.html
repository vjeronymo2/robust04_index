941027 FT 27 OCT 94 / International Company News: Data General loss bigger than expected Data General reported wider than expected fourth-quarter losses quarter, but revenues rose as the minicomputer manufacturer continued its difficult transition from proprietary computers to 'open' systems based on industry standards, writes Louise Kehoe in San Francisco. Revenues rose 5 per cent to Dollars 292.6m from Dollars 278.4m a year earlier. Net losses for the quarter were Dollars 6.2m, or 17 cents a share, against a loss of Dollars 37.2m, or Dollars 1.06 last year when a restructuring charge of Dollars 25m was taken. The company said it had received a Dollars 53m payment from Northrop Grumman in settlement of a copyright infringement suit. The gain will be recorded in the current quarter. Full-year revenues were Dollars 1.12bn, against Dollars 1.08bn in fiscal 1993. Net loss for the year was Dollars 87.7m, or Dollars 2.45 a share, including a restructuring charge of Dollars 35m in the second quarter. In fiscal 1993, the company reported a net loss of Dollars 60.5m , or Dollars 1.73 a share, which included a Dollars 25m restructuring charge.
