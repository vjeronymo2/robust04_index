Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on regional guerrilla activities and clashes with government military units monitored through 7 March; source follows in parentheses after each item.

Colombia
Seven Caqueta and Cauca government officials kidnapped by FARC (Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia) guerrillas were released unharmed on 1 March after being held captive for nearly a week. Simultaneously, military forces arrested 10 alleged guerrillas in Bolivar. In another incident that has occurred in Bolivar, a UP (Patriotic Union) guerrilla was killed during a clash with a military patrol. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 2 Mar 94 p 8a) At least seven guerrillas have been killed and four arrested by Army units in several clashes that have occurred in Sucre, Santander, Boyaca, Norte de Boyaca, Cauca and Casanare; in another incident, the ELN (Army of National Liberation) has kidnapped Argelia Mayor Carlos Ivan Agrada. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1730 GMT 1 Mar 94) As the election draws nearer, extremist forces are continuing their attacks against candidates for public office. Unidentified individuals have killed Evaristo Amaya Morales, La Uribe mayoral candidate, while he was campaigning in Meta Department. In addition, a terrorist group detonated an explosive device while congressional candidate Telesforo Pedraza was campaigning in Huila Department. The government has announced it will not reschedule elections due to political violence. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 26 Feb 94 pp. 1a, 6a)
Guatemala
Official sources have reported a Guatemalan National Revolutionary Unity (URNG) column has attacked a military base in the northern Department of Quiche. A UN delegation was visiting the base at the moment of the attack. The Army Press and Information Department (Dide) has said a UN Human Rights delegation was at the base at the time and was able to witness the attack. Dide has also said the attack, which occurred on 2 March, is clear evidence of the URNG's lack of willingness to negotiate peace. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1701 GMT 5 Mar 94) Members of the National Police Department deactivated two explosive devices on 27 February. Both bombs carried subversive propaganda and were found in Guatemala City, one behind the municipal building, the other hidden inside a bench at the central park. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 2 Mar 94 p. 18)
