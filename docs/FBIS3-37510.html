Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Vladimir Malyshev and Georgiy Shmelev] [Text] Athens February 17 TASS -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev arrived on Thursday night [17 February] to Athens, the first point of his eight-day tour. Upon arrival, he immediately met his German counterpart Klaus Kinkel to discuss preparations for the Russian president's visit to Germany scheduled for this coming spring, as well as a settlement of the Bosnian conflict. The Bosnian settlement and relations with the European Union (EU) are to be in the focus of the upcoming political consultations between Kozyrev and the so-called EC "Troika" consisting of the foreign ministers of Belgium, Greece and Germany -- the former, present and future EC chairs. The Athens meeting is initiated by Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias. The programme of Kozyrev's visit also provides for a meeting with Greek Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou, talks on bilateral Russian- Greek relations and a meeting with the Cyprian foreign minister. After visiting Greece, the Russian foreign minister is to make a tour of East European states entering the so-called Visegrad group. The first country to be visited is the Czech Republic where Kozyrev is expected to continue a diologue on various aspects of Russia's relations with these countries and to look into the progress of implementation of the earlier signed agreements. The sides will also exchange opinions on the current situation in Europe, specifically on problems of conflict settlements, above all in Bosnia-Herzegovina, and on a new structure of European security. While in Krakow, Poland, Kozyrev is to attend a Russian-Polish conference on cooperation, "Partnership Under New Conditions".
