940905 FT 05 SEP 94 / Record complaints about doctors Official complaints about doctors are at record levels and the number of doctors struck off has jumped sharply, the General Medical Council's annual report shows. There were 1,615 complaints in 1992-93, a rise of 24 per cent, and 286 were acted on compared with 216 in the previous year. A total of 162 cases were referred to the council's professional conduct committee, which rules on the most serious cases, and 55 doctors appeared before the committee, up from 43. Thirty doctors were struck off compared with four in the previous year. The British Medical Association, which represents doctors, said the rise did not necessarily mean standards were falling. It said: 'Since the publication of the Patient's Charter and various government targets there's no doubt patient expectations have increased enormously. 'Patient awareness of complaints procedures has also increased.'
