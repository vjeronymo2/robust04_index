BFN [TA SR report: "Do We Have a Firm Grip on It?"] [Excerpts] Bratislava--In an interview for Slovak Radio, Prime Minister Jozef Moravcik described the early elections in the Slovak Republic as extremely necessary. As far as the crisis in parliament is concerned, he is of the opinion that the Movement for a Democratic Slovakia is to blame for the situation. [passage omitted] In the interview, Moravcik also responded to a REUTER report on the problems within the Slovak banking system. According to him, the Slovak Government will take measures to ensure that the situation depicted in the agency's inaccurate report does not arise. He described the banking system as the economy's nervous system. The Slovak Government intends to keep it in order. "Everything indicates that we have a firm grip on the situation and that there is no danger," he said.
