BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 21 April Morning Edition] [Text] Gaishi Hiraiwa, chairman of Keidanren [Japan Federation of Economic Organizations], commented on Japanese-U.S. relations, which have been troubled by Japan's trade surplus, and other issues at a news conference on 20 April. He said, "The two countries should compromise whenever possible, assert their views when necessary, and build a good relationship." He also stated, "We should build a sound bilateral relationship which is also acceptable from the European point of view." He asked the government not to give priority to the United States alone.
