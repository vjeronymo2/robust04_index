BFN [Text] Seoul, June 28 (YONHAP) -- The government has appointed four bureau director-general-level officials as aides to the three-member delegation to the inter-Korean preliminary contact, it was learned Tuesday. The four are Ku Pon-tae, chief policy-maker of the unification board; Chang Chae-yong, chief of the American affairs bureau of the Foreign Ministry; Om Ik-chun, assistant to the prime minister; and Yi Chae-ho, a member of the Inter-Korean Dialogue Secretariat. They will assist chief delegate Yi Hong-ku (deputy premier and unification minister) and delegates Chong Chong-uk (senior presidential secretary for foreign affairs and national security) and Yun Yo-chun (special assistant to the prime minister) during the preliminary meeting which opened at the truce village of Panmunjom Tuesday morning. A government official said the decision had been made Sunday to select the aides from among bureau director-general-level officials.
