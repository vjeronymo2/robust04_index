BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] Diego Fernandez de Cevallos, National Action Party [PAN] candidate for president, has ruled out the possibility that an alliance of all the opposition forces in Mexico can defeat the Institutional Revolutionary Party [PRI] in the coming August elections. Nevertheless, he said alliances could work under certain circumstances to advance democracy and defend the vote. [Begin Fernandez recording] We can and should work with any political party to advance democracy. [end recording] During a news conference held at the foreign correspondents section, Fernandez said there are two basic requirements for holding pure, credible elections. First, the PRI should lower its publicity expenses to keep them within legal limitations. Second, the media should allocate time to candidates equitably and serve the truth honestly.
