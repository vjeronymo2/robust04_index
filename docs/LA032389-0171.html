March 23, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

CULINARY SOS: SAY CHEESE WHEN YOU THINK OF MAKING TOAST

DEAR SOS: Would it be possible to get the recipe for the garlic-cheese toast served at the Covina Bowl Coffee Shop in Covina? I'm not able to bowl anymore and would like to make the bread at home.

-- MILDRED

DEAR MILDRED: It's a strike, all right. The cheese toast would be great to serve with Easter dinner or soup and salad suppers later on.

COVINA BOWL

CHEESE TOAST

1/2 cup butter or margarine

2 tablespoons shredded sharp Cheddar cheese

1 teaspoon granulated garlic

1 teaspoon paprika

2 1/2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese

1 loaf sourdough bread

Whip butter thoroughly, then add Cheddar cheese, garlic and paprika. Mix lightly. Fold in Parmesan cheese.

Spread cheese mixture over sourdough bread slices. Place under broiler until toasted and bubbly. Makes 8 servings.

DEAR SOS: Lately we have enjoyed Lemon Chicken at several restaurants. I've tried a few times to come up with similar recipes. Can you send me a recipe that comes close?

-- MRS. R.W.

DER MRS. R.W.: Let's give this recipe a try.

LEMON CHICKEN

2 to 3 pounds chicken, cut into serving pieces

Garlic salt

1/4 pound butter

1 1/2 to 2 cups lemon juice

2 teaspoons dry mustard

Rinse chicken and pat dry. Sprinkle on all sides with garlic salt. Place in shallow baking dish.

Melt butter in saucepan. Mix lemon juice and dry mustard into melted butter. Pour mixture over chicken and bake at 400 degrees 1 hour, basting often. Makes 4 servings.

DEAR SOS: I have lost my recipe for black bottom pie and can't find a replacement. This is a delicious and special dessert and I hope you can help me obtain a recipe for it.

-- NADINE

DEAR NADINE: As a special treat, how about this one shared by the famous Brown Derby in Hollywood? It's one of our favorites. Keep it in mind for Easter, too.

BROWN DERBY'S BLACK BOTTOM PIE

1 envelope unflavored gelatin

2 tablespoons cold water

1/2 cup milk

2 tablespoons sugar

Dash salt

1 teaspoon vanilla

1 egg yolk

3 ounces sweet or semisweet chocolate

2 cups whipping cream

1 (9-inch) baked pie shell

Soften gelatin in cold water. Bring milk to boil in top of double boiler. Beat together sugar, salt, 1/2 teaspoon vanilla and egg yolk until light and creamy. Add small amount of milk to egg mixture, blending well.

Return to remaining milk in pan. Cook, stirring, over hot water until mixture barely comes to boil. Remove from heat and stir in gelatin until dissolved.

Grate 2 ounces chocolate. Add to custard and stir over hot water until chocolate is melted. Remove from heat and beat with rotary beater until custard is smooth.

Chill until custard reaches cream-like consistency. Whip 1 cup cream until stiff and fold into custard with remaining 1/2 teaspoon vanilla. Turn into baked pie shell and chill until firm.

At serving time, whip remaining 1 cup cream until stiff and spread over filling. Shave remaining 1 ounce chocolate and sprinkle over top of pie. Makes 1 pie.
