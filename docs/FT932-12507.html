930428 FT 28 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television I have never talked to a lifeboat man who did not admit to being frightened of the sea; stupid to be otherwise, you may say. But that does not prevent them repeatedly braving the worst conditions around our coasts; among the worst in the world. ITV begins a six-part documentary series, Lifeboat, illustrating a year in the life of Salcombe's RNLI crew (7.00). Continuing on The Great Picture Trail (7.30 BBC2) Neil MacGregor, director of the National Gallery, considers Titian's 'Bacchus and Ariadne' and Mondrian's 'Composition With Grey, Red, Yellow And Blue', very suitable for printing on tea towels. QED records how a specialist from Great Ormond Street Hospital attempted to bring to his own patients the benefits of a Chinese herbalist who works from a Soho basement. Six days a week Dr Luo treats the first 50 patients who write their names on a list taped to her door. Most suffer from eczema (9.30 BBC1). It is almost embarrassing to find how little difference the absence of Dorothy makes to the Golden Girls who are now appearing as a trio in The Golden Palace (10.00 C4).
