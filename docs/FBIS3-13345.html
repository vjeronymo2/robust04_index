Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Algiers, March 12 (AFP) -- Security forces were engaged in a massive manhunt Saturday for about 1,000 prisoners, including 100 Moslem fundamentalists on death row, who escaped when Islamic guerrillas stormed their prison, reliable sources said. Police, gendarmes and troops launched a joint security sweep using helicopters to search the mountainous area near Tazoult, 400 kilometres (240 miles) southeast of the capital, following Thursday's mass breakout. Their search is being hampered by the difficult terrain which nationalist fighters used to hole up during the 1954-62 war of independence from France. The Algerian authorities has so far refused to officially confirm the escape, the most spectacular since the start of fundamentalist violence in Algeria two years ago. The Algerian authorities have rounded up thousands in a crackdown on Islamic fundamentalists who launched a bloody guerrilla war against the secular administration following the January 1992 cancellation of a general election the now outlawed Islamic Salvation Front (FIS) was poised to win. According to the sources around 150 Islamic guerrillas attacked the prison around 7:00 p.m. (1800 GMT) on Thursday during the Ramadan meal break when security was lax, killing four prison warders, the sources added. It was not clear whether the operation had been launched by the Islamic Group (GIA) or its rival Armed Islamic Movement (MIA), the sources said. Some 400 fundamentalists serving life sentences also escaped in the jail break, as well as 200 criminals with common law convictions. Tazoult prison accommodates 3,000 detainees, many of them considered particularly dangerous. [passage omitted]
