BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Honduran economic developments reported through 12 April. Tegucigalpa Telesistema Hondureno de Television in Spanish 0100 GMT 6 Apr 94 reports the government confirmed on 6 April that fuel prices will be adjusted every 15 days. A government official said that fuel prices will be based on an average of international prices and that the government will prevent profiteering. It was officially announced on 6 April that electricity rates will be increased for those who consume 300 kw of electricity or more. Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2141 GMT 11 Apr 94 reports that according to a study conducted by the Honduran Economists Association, food prices increased 21 percent in the first three months of Carlos Reina's administration. Reina's economic cabinet eliminated $8 million worth of fuel subsidies and increased the dollar exchange rate from 6.20 to 7.27 lempiras in March. The Honduran Central Bank revealed that the consumer price index increased 1 percent, compared with 0.2-percent increase in March 1993. Martin Barahona, vice president of the Economists Association, said that 1.5 million people are able to work but 25 percent of them are unemployed or underemployed. Barahona added that eight out of 10 Hondurans live below the poverty level.
