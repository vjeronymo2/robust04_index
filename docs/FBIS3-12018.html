Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Lima, 28 Mar (DPA) -- In statements given to a Peruvian television station and reported by Lima newspapers, a youth leader of the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI) said that Luis Donaldo Colosio, the assassinated governmental presidential candidate, would have fallen "victim to a conspiracy that the highest power spheres plotted." Ramiro de la Rosa, the PRI representative who heads the Youth Secretariat of the Latin American Parliament, blamed some government officials, whom he refused to name, for the "climate of conspiracy that surrounded the assassination." He told "Panamericana Television Network" that the Mexican media has virtually ignored him, adding that President Carlos Salinas de Gortari will exploit Colosio's death to "promote a candidate that lacks the support of the PRI's rank and file and that fails to meet the expectations of the Mexican people." He said: "We and the Mexican people are demanding that President Salinas not be involved in the selection of the presidential candidate for the upcoming elections." The youth leader also accused the president of "having endorsed and encouraged the ambitions of the PRI's dubious leader Manuel Camacho Solis." De la Rosa added that the government has used Camacho to create specific political situations. The Lima newspaper LA REPUBLICA published de la Rosa's statements today by running a front-page headline that reads: "It Was a Plot." This report differs from that of assassin Mario Aburto, which also received ample coverage. Aburto maintained that he worked alone and followed no one's orders. In his interview with the "Panorama" talk show, however, de la Rosa expressed optimism for the PRI's future: "There is a large sector that maintains a position of democracy, openness, and renewal. I am not alone; there is a growing democratizing current."
