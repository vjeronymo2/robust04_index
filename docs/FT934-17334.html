931001 FT 01 OCT 93 / Observer: Virtual reality It makes a refreshing change these days to learn that Treasury ministers are not above poking fun at their own department, especially in the pre-Budget period when they have traditionally been duty-bound by an official edict to keep silent. Thus Stephen Dorrell, Treasury financial secretary, took full advantage this week of the change in rules which has eliminated 'purdah' ahead of the November 30 Budget. Dorrell, who joined the Treasury only 17 months ago after a two year stint at the Department of Health with responsibilities including mental health, told a London conference that he had been deemed well-suited to take the job on the grounds that the Treasury contained the world's largest collection of clever people doing stupid things.
