Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Moscow deal on the withdrawal of nuclear weapons from Ukrainian soil is part of "President Kravchuk's defeatist policy," member of the parliamentary commission for defence and security matters Igor Derkach told Interfax on Saturday. He says the agreement is not likely to be implemented given further economic recession in Ukraine. Derkach pointed to a lack of convincing arguments in statements by the American and Russian leaders about security guarantees and technical assistance to Ukraine. "The $175 Mn aid to Ukraine the Americans promised a long time ago will be used to pay U.S. experts," he said. The dismantling and transfer of long-range missiles from Ukraine to Russia will cost $3 Bn, according to the estimates local experts made prior to the ratification of the Start I agreement by Ukrainian deputies.
