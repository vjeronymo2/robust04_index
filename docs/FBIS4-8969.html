BFN [Text] Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs, had a phone conversation with Bosnia-Herzegovina Prime Minister Haris Silajdzic today. Granic and Silajdzic discussed the situation in Gorazde and the coordination of the activities of the two states in the international arena -- particularly in the United Nations and the EU -- and in political and humanitarian fields. They expressed pleasure with the results achieved so far in the implementation of the Washington agreement and agreed that both sides should persist with it so that the international community might halt the Serb aggression against Gorazde and so that conditions be created for the continuation of the initiated peace process. Minister Granic informed Prime Minister Silajdzic that Slobodan Lang, Croatian ambassador at the Croatian Ministry of Foreign Affairs in charge of humanitarian affairs, is in Sarajevo to coordinate humanitarian activities. They also discussed preparations for the announced visit by Croatian President Franjo Tudjman to Sarajevo and agreed that the visit should go ahead as soon as security conditions allow.
