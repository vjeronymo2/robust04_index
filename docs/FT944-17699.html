941006 FT 06 OCT 94 / IMF/World Bank: Low labour mobility hits Russia Regional unemployment levels in Russia, from 1 to 11 per cent of the workforce, are likely to become entrenched because of low labour mobility, a World Bank report says, David Goodhart writes. The national unemployment rate is 5-6 per cent, as opposed to the official rate of 2 per cent, but the duration of unemployment is found to be short. In 1993, 60-80 per cent of those leaving unemployment did so within four months.
