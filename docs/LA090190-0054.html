September 1, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

NOT BEWITCHED

I was horrified, but not surprised, at Michael Wilmington's review of "The Witches" (Aug. 24). The movie revolves around a plot by an organization of witches to turn the children of the world into mice.

All the witches in the film are portrayed as ugly and evil old women.

Actually, there are witches, and we celebrate and worship the Goddess (otherwise known as Mother Earth). The golden rule of witchcraft is "An it harm none, love, and do as you will." Witches believe that whatever you do, comes back to you threefold. Therefore we don't use magic at the expense of others. We have been slandered, tortured and burned down the centuries because we do not believe in the same God as the church (though we recognize other people's right to believe in whatever they like).

"The Witches" is intended to be lighthearted fantasy. But what would Wilmington's reaction have been to a lighthearted fantasy called "The Jews" in which the Jews of the world were revealed to be ugly, evil people who plot "the destruction of every last child in England?"

TALIESIN

Santa Monica
