Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Afonso Dhlakama, Mozambique National Resistance [Renamo] leader, has reiterated that his party wants the country's multiparty elections to be held in October of this year. In an interview he granted Portugal's LUSA news agency, the Renamo leader said that there were people interested in postponing elections but he noted that was not Renamo's position. The Renamo leader noted that the international community has repeatedly warned that it will withdraw its UN-supervised peacekeeping mission in Mozambique, should the holding of elections be moved to a later stage.
