Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] There has been another reshuffle at the highest level of executive power in Uzbekistan. By decrees of President Karimov, Tulkin Miryakubov has been released from his post of deputy prime minister, and Shuhrat Gafurov from his as chairman of the State Property Committee. At the same time, Viktor Chzhen has been appointed deputy prime minister and chairman of the State Property Committee. He worked for a long time as head of local industry in the republic. Shamil Gataulin has become chairman of the recently set up Taxation Committee, which also took in the customs service and a considerable part of the service, dissolved in the MVD [Ministry of Internal Affairs], of combatting economic crime. Up until now he has been working as chief of the main taxation directorate under the Cabinet of Ministers of Uzbekistan. This is already the second major reshuffle in the government over the past week, and, according to authoritative sources, will not be the last in the near future. Clearly such measures were conditioned by the resolve of the president to activate the process of market reforms in the republic, and to bring more energetic people into the government. He has spoken several times of the need for such actions in his speeches at the session of the council of international [words indistinct]
