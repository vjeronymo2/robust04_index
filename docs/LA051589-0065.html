May 15, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

DREXEL APPOINTS EIGHT NEW VICE PRESIDENTS

Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. named eight new executive vice presidents as part of a continuing shake-up following the firm's agreement April 13 to settle Securities and Exchange Commission charges of securities fraud.

Peter Ackerman, 42, at one time thought to be a possible successor to Michael Milken as head of the firm's junk bond department, was moved out of the department and named head of Drexel's newly created International Capital Markets Services Group. Drexel has said the new separate department was established as a way of restricting the flow of confidential information between the firm's corporate finance department and securities traders. As executive vice president, Ackerman will continue to perform much the same duties as before, designing the securities to be used in corporate restructurings or leveraged buyouts, Drexel said.

James M. Davin, 43, who joined Drexel last year after 19 years at First Boston Corp., was named executive vice president and designated as the senior trading official responsible for ensuring that the firm's trading activities comply with federal laws, Drexel said. The SEC settlement requires Drexel to appoint such an official.

Stephen L. Joseph, 46, the brother of Drexel Chief Executive Fred Joseph, was also named executive vice president and given responsibility for domestic fixed income trading and sales, but not including high yield bonds or government securities. He had established and been head of Drexel's mortgage-backed securities department, which is being folded into a newly created "structured asset" department.

Arthur Bilger, 36, Leon Black, 37, and John Sorte, 41, also were named executive vice presidents. As previously reported, the three earlier this year were named to jointly head the firm's corporate finance department.

The other new executive vice presidents are Michael J. Kugler, 50, in charge of government securities trading and money market operations, and Walter J. Sieczkowski, 44, who joined Drexel in 1987.

The eight new executive vice presidents join two Drexel officials who already hold that title. They are John H. Kissick, currently serving as head of the high-yield bond department in Beverly Hills, and Burton Siegel, director of domestic and international institutional equities.
