Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Text] (Sonia Ciambatti), former magistrate of the Supreme Court of Justice, said that the Executive Branch, through the Central Bank of Venezuela, will begin operating a data base that will allow the detection and neutralization of money transfers linked to drug-trafficking activities that are coming into the country from overseas. (Ciambatti) made the announcement within the framework of a conference in Caracas on U.S. experiences in fighting money laundering in the United States. If money laundering is not eradicated, it is capable of spilling over and harming the financial system of a nation, because people involved in money laundering take advantage of the system for their own benefit. According to (Ciambatti), Venezuela has acquired international tools, such as the Vienna Convention and the standard regulations on the legitimization of capital, that enable a country to fight the crime. Establishing the proposed data base, therefore, will be a smooth process. [Begin (Ciambatti) recording, in progress] ... so there is a check to allow only banking transfers and economic situations that are entirely legal and which benefit the country and to keep Venezuela from becoming a center of operations for international drug-trafficking activities. [end recording]
