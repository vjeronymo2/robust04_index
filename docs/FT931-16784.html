930105 FT 05 JAN 93 / Prison Jobclubs suffer dual burden POOR ORGANISATION and lack of resources are hampering prison Jobclubs, according to a study by the Department of Employment. Even so, they are achieving results comparable with mainstream Jobclubs, the study by the occupational psychology branch of the Employment Service concludes. The study focused on two prison Jobclubs, which aim to help offenders find work during the final weeks of their sentences. The study cites as difficulties factors including lack of telephones and inadequate communication between the Employment Service, the Prison Service and agencies that run Jobclubs. Ms Jo Rick, research director, said yesterday: 'With a new project of this nature, there are bound to be a lot of teething troubles, but this research has shown us how we can develop the programme to run more effectively in future.'
