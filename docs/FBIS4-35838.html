BFN [Text] Zahedan, Sistan va Baluchistan Prov., IRNA -- One million Afghan refugees have so far returned to their country, the Interior Ministry's director general in charge of foreign national affairs 'Ali Husseini [name as received] said here Thursday. The return of the refugees was arranged by a tripartite commission comprising the governments of Iran and Afghanistan and the U.N. High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR). Preparations have been made for return home of some 300,000 more Afghan refugees this year, Husseini told IRNA. Husseini said that according to the policies of the Islamic Republic, those Afghan refugees who have received identification cards may stay in Iran as long as they wish. The commission has given those Afghans in Iran illegally until July 1 to leave the country, he said.
