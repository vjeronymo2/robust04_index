Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Brussels ROMPRES, 27/1/1994 -- After an interview with NATO Deputy General-Secretary Amedeo de Franchis on 26 January, Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu stated to a ROMPRES correspondent that a general exchange of opinions was made on the occasion regarding "security in southeast Europe, the prospects opened by the Partnership for Peace (PFP), as well as its contribution brought to stability in the mentioned area and to Romania's stability." "We also approached the issue regarding the concurrent support that we wish to gain from the alliance for the elaboration of the necessary documents regarding our offer of participation in the Partnership for Peace and I may say that the answer was extremely positive and determined," Melescanu said. The Romanian top diplomat also added that consultations were already held regarding "technicalities and administrative issues" related to the future appointment (under the Partnership's provisions) of liaison active officers with the alliance headquarters and with "the gray cell" of the Partnership for Peace headquartered at Mons.
