BFN [Text] Belgrade, April 19 (TANJUG) -- The government of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia on Tuesday [19 April] approved a request of the U.N. Protection Force [UNPROFOR] for transit through Yugoslav territory for the Ukrainian and Russian batallions which are to join UNPROFOR in Bosnia-Herzegovina, the Federal Information Ministry said today in Belgrade. There are already 550 Ukrainian troops on the UNPROFOR in Bosnia-Herzegovina, and 160 more left Ukraine for that former Yugoslav Republic on Monday. The first 100 soldiers of the additional Russian UNPROFOR contingent left Russia in late March and are already in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Most of them have been deployed in the area of Sarajevo.
