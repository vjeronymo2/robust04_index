Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Dec 30 (CTK) -- Miroslav Sladek, chairman of the extreme right Assembly For the Republic-Czechoslovak Republican Party (SPR-RSC), is the least trusted Czech politician, a poll conducted by the Institute for Public Opinion Research (IVVM) in early December revealed. Sladek, mistrusted by 76 percent of the polled, was followed by Chairman of the Christian Democratic Union-Czechoslovak People's Party (KDU-CSL) Josef Lux and Interior Minister Jan Ruml (Civic Democratic Party, ODS) with 57 percent of mistrust for each. The least known politician (62 percent of Czechs do not know him) is Jan Krycer, chairman of the HSDMS (Self-Governing Democracy Movement For Moravia and Silesia, 14 deputies in a 200-seat parliament). He is followed by communist politicians Miroslav Grebenicek and Jaroslav Ortman with 58 and 57 per cent, respectively. Five and seven percent of the polled do not know President Vaclav Havel and Premier Vaclav Klaus, while Sladek is not known by 9 percent.
