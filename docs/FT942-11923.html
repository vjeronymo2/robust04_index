940503 FT 03 MAY 94 / World News in Brief: Grand prix deaths prompt emergency probe into safety An emergency meeting to consider Formula One safety following the deaths of grand prix drivers Ayrton Senna and Roland Ratzenberger has been called by the world governing body of motor sport, the Federation Internationale de l'Automobile. The move to ascertain the precise causes of the double tragedy came as tributes were paid around the world to Senna, the 34-year-old triple world champion from Sao Paulo. Brazil's president Itamar Franco declared three days of national mourning for Senna, who died of head injuries when his Renault-Williams car left the track at 180mph during Sunday's San Marino Grand Prix at Imola.
