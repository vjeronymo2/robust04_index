BFN [Text] President Eduardo Frei departed for a three-day visit to the United States at 2045 on 26 June. During his brief trip to the United States, Frei is scheduled to hold meetings with top-ranking U.S. political and economic officials, the most important of which will be his meeting with U.S. President Bill Clinton. It is expected that the two presidents will surely discuss the Chilean-U.S. free trade agreement during this meeting. Before his departure, the president headed the traditional transfer-of-power ceremony. During this ceremony, President Eduardo Frei transferred the presidency to Interior Minister German Correa in compliance with a decree read previously by Interior Under Secretary Belisario Velasco. German Correa assumed the presidency in his capacity as Chilean vice president. Before boarding the plane that would take him to the United States, President Frei stated that his meeting with Bill Clinton does not seek solely commercial objectives: [Begin Frei recording] This visit has much broader objectives. The free trade treaty is, of course, among the issues that will be discussed. This is not, however, the only issue to be discussed. There are many other issues. We are most interested in strengthening our political ties, on having fluent bilateral ties with the United States in vast areas. [end recording]
