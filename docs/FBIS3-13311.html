Language: Persian Article Type:CSO [Text] With the approval of the total figure for government revenues, the first consultation of the 1373 [21 March 1994-20 March 1995] budget bill has been approved by the deputies. The Central News Unit reports that Article 1 of next year's budget bill has been approved in its first consultation and at yesterday's open Majles session. In terms of revenues and other credit sources and in terms of expenses and other payments, the 1373 [21 March 1994-20 March 1995] national budget is more than 69,772,752,987,000 rials[R]. In terms of revenues and expenses the government's general budget is more than R32,330,350,987,000. General revenues and other sources of credit approved and set by the Majles amount to R30,480,946,179,000, revenues allocated to the ministries and government institutions are R2,254,404,808,000, and the budget for the government companies, banks and profit-making institutions affiliated with the government is more than R45,453,090,000,000 in terms of revenues and other sources for credits. Based on Article 1 as approved, foreign exchange revenues from the sale of crude oil in excess of $10,154,300,000 up to $11.48 billion will be used to repay the nation's foreign exchange commitments, and anything in excess of that will be used to carry out the projects given in this law and to make up the credit shortages for allocated development projects. This report states that the expert from the Plan and Budget Commission explained the changes made in the revenues section of the budget. He said: The national budget has decreased about $10,000 billion [as published] and the general budget has also decreased about R3,700 billion compared to the bill proposed by the government, and the net change that will have to be made in next year's budget for the second consultation is about R2,220 billion.
