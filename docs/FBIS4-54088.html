BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 24 (KCNA) -- The Pakistan committee for support to the repatriation of unconverted long-term prisoners in South Korea to the North was inaugurated in Karachi on May 15. At the inaugural meeting, chairman of the Pakistan committee for supporting the proposal to found the democratic confederal republic of Koryo B.M. Kutti was elected chairman of the new-born committee and secretary of the Karachi branch of the Pakistan-Korea Friendship Association Abdul Malik secretary of the committee. At the first session of the committee, the participants noted that there are many unconverted long-term prisoners in South Korea who were taken prisoner during the Korean war and that they have been kept under detention for more than 40 years for their refusal to be converted. They urged the South Korean authorities to immediately return to the DPRK Kim In-so and Ham Se-hwan who spent long years in prison, refusing ideological conversion, and called on the peoples of Pakistan and the world and international human rights organisations to wage an international solidarity movement for their repatriation.
