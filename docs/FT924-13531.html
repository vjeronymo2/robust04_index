921015 FT 15 OCT 92 / Whalen calls for cut in interest rates MR Geoffrey Whalen, chief executive of Peugeot-Talbot, the UK subsidiary of the French carmaker, last night called for a cut of 1 percentage point in interest rates 'as soon as possible', Kevin Done writes. He said that the government should not embark on a 'mad dash for growth' and added that any reduction should 'not be of such a size as to threaten the control of inflation'. He said that an early return to the European exchange rate mechanism should be ruled out, as that would prevent the cut in interest rates that the economy desperately needed. He said that industry needed 'to be persuaded that when the government says that it cares about the survival and future well-being of British manufacturing industry, it really means it. 'As a first and basic step, all cabinet ministers should be told that in all that they and their departments do, manufacturing industry's needs ought to be considered with the greatest care.'
