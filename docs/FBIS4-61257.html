BFN [Text] Brussels (4 May) TURAN -- This morning in Brussels in the governmental residence, a meeting between Azerbaijan's President, Geydar Aliyev and Belgium's Prime Minister Jean Luc Dehaene took place. During their meeting they discussed issues of bilateral cooperation and regional security. Aliyev told the Prime Minister about the developments in the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict. Both sides were unanimous in the opinion that the conflict should be solved by peaceful means. Azerbaijan's joining the preliminary document of the "Partnership for the Sake of Peace" programme will promote the development of Azeri-Belgian relations. "We support the attempts to restore peace and stability in the Caucasus and support the negotiation process within the CSCE Minsk Group framework," said Dehaene. After the talks, Aliyev said that Belgium will review the draft for an agreement on friendship and cooperation plus a number of other agreements put forth by Azerbaijan.
