May 20, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

5 STATE OFFICES REGULATE VARIOUS ESCROW AGENTS

California escrow providers are regulated by five state agencies -- the departments of Corporations, Insurance, Real Estate, Savings and Loans and Banking.

The agency regulating escrow companies in California varies with the escrow firm's organizational structure and the type of escrow the company is permitted to handle.

The state's 1,212 independent escrow companies are regulated by the Department of Corporations, with the Federal Trade Commission also handling consumer complaints.

Escrow departments of real estate brokers are under the jurisdiction of the Department of Real Estate.

Savings and loan associations holding transactions in escrow are under the jurisdiction of the Department of Savings and Loan, Office of Thrift Supervision.

A California licensed bank acting as an escrow agent is regulated by the Department of Banking, while consumer complaints regarding escrows handled by a federally licensed bank can be directed to the Comptroller of the Currency.

Escrow transactions handled by title companies are regulated by the Department of Insurance.
