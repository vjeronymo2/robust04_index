BFN [Text] In the wake of the implementation of the autonomy agreement, Israel will stop financing busywork jobs for Palestinians in the Gaza Strip. Security sources noted that in May Israel employed 18,000 Gazans in busywork jobs at a cost of 20 million shekels. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports that Faysal al-Husayni announced that the Palestinians would shortly have enough money to finance the expenses stemming from the implementation of the Gaza- Jericho agreement. The Agriculture Ministry will immediately proceed to set up a special unit to oversee the passage of produce from the autonomy to Israel. The unit will tighten controls on shipments of produce from the territories to the ultra-Orthodox community. Our correspondent Shulamit Schmerling reports that the decision to establish the unit already at this point was made at a special meeting with the agriculture minister in a bid to curb the smuggling of produce by taking advantage of the permits given to the ultra-Orthodox community which is currently observing a ritual fallow year. Originally, the unit was scheduled to be established only after the opening of the borders with the autonomy to the passage of produce.
