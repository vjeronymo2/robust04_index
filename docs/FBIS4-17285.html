BFN [Text] On Thursday Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin reported at the sitting of the Cabinet's presidium that the government had received a letter from the president which contains direct instructions regarding the situation in the state economy and in the country as a whole. Chernomyrdin noted that "the president's concern is not ungrounded" and "his attitude towards the instructions of the head of the Russian state is always a very serious one." In Chernomyrdin's words, the document received from Yeltsin was already considered on April 19 and the issues defined in it would be solved in the near term. Chernomyrdin reported that Vice Premier and Economics Minister Aleksandr Shokhin had been commissioned to fulfill the president's instructions. The sitting of the government's presidium approved the principal trends of the Cabinet's social policy for 1994. The social policy gives priorities to the following: prevention of any lowering of the population's level of incomes, preserving of the balance between economic interests and material opportunities of three major population groups: hired workers, entrepreneurs, and disabled citizens. To this effect Chernomyrdin indicated that the tax reform to which he pays the greatest significance is to be the key element of the social policy. In his words, "taxes should not bring damage to people." The government's presidium returned for revision the draft of the federal program on assistance in the population's employment in 1994 prepared by the Federal Employment Service. As Chernomyrdin said, the document does not properly point out measures for helping in the employment of those who graduate from educational institutions, does not take into consideration investment programs the implementation of which the unemployed can be involved. A governmental commission headed by Vice Premier Yuriy Yarov has been formed to revise the federal program.
