Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Ivan Ivanov and Vladimir Mostovets] [Text] Helsinki, 11 Mar -- As a result of the intergovernmental talks held today in the Finnish capital and attended by Prime Ministers Viktor Chernomyrdin and Esko Aho, a hefty package of documents has been signed between the governments of the two countries. These mainly relate to economic cooperation. This package includes intergovernmental agreements on Russo-Finnish state border checkpoints, on cooperation and mutual assistance in customs affairs, in the area of fishing, on cooperation in the area of postal and electronic communications, on the cancellation of the Russian Federation's debt to the Finnish Republic through deliveries of special stock, and a memorandum on cooperation between the customs services of the two countries in the fight against the smuggling of radioactive materials. Also, as a result of the talks, a number of secret documents specifying the volumes and delivery mechanism for Russian arms as part of the cancellation of Russia's debt have been approved. Andrey Vavilov, first deputy finance minister of the Russian Federation told an ITAR-TASS correspondent that Russia would deliver modern weapons systems worth $80 million toward the cancellation of its debt. Among these would be anti-aircraft and artillery systems.
