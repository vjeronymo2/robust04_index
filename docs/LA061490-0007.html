June 14, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

VANDALISM AND HATE CRIMES

Your article on the vandalism of St. Francis of Assisi Church misses the point ("Attacks on Church Part of Rise in Hate Crimes," Metro, May 27). These attacks are incorrectly characterized by many, including the conservative majority of the Los Angeles County Commission on Human Relations, as hate crimes.

It is clear that whoever these vandals are, they are not protesting the church's wafer and wine, statuary, prayer beads or incense. Any attack against the use of these implements and their associated rites are and should be considered hate crimes. They are not the issues here.

The vandalism, though regrettable, is clearly in protest of the church's anti-life AIDS policies, and anti-choice abortion policy. If churches are going to encourage their parishioners to engage in political activity, if church officials are going to take controversial political stands and threaten political candidates with ecclesiastical retaliation unless they apply church teachings to their secular responsibilities, they cannot expect to be exempt from protest.

They cannot cry foul when confronted with the anger of those who work to protect the rights and health of citizens.

MICHAEL SHERE

Pasadena
