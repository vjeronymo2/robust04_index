Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The prime minister has invited German businessmen to invest in India. Addressing the Indo-German Economic Conference in Bonn today, Mr. Narasimha Rao described the industrial climate in the country as extremely good and favorable for investment. Making it clear that his government has absolutely no hesitation in going ahead with the economic liberalization process, Mr. Rao asserted that India will step up the reform process. He assured the German industry that there will be no constraints coming in the way. He added that constraints if any will be resolved by him and the German chancellor, Dr. Helmut Kohl. Opening the conference, the chancellor, Dr. Kohl, assured India of all possible assistance for the development of the industrial and technological cooperation. He said the economic policies of India are on the right path. He told the conference that India with its reform-oriented economic policy is one of Germany's most important partners on the Asian continent. Dr. Kohl announced that both the countries have agreed to establish a high-level India-German commission for science and technology. The president of the Confederation of Indian Industry, Mr. N. Godrej, asked Germany to remove the constraints and controls on technology transfer. Our correspondent, Sudarshanam, reports from Bonn that German industry has drawn up certain proposals for deregulating and liberalizing India's economy. These proposals have been forwarded to the Indian Government. The Indian Government after a study has sent certain recommendations. These are being examined to strengthen the economic and trade cooperation. Our correspondent understands that a German festival in India will be held as soon as possible. Mr. Rao was given a formal ceremonial welcome in the forecourt of the Federal Chancellor's Office at Bonn. Mr. Rao inspected a guard of honor by the German Air Force. Here is a report from our special correspondent A. Karuppaswamy: [Begin Karuppaswamy recording] The opening of the first India-German economic conference in Bonn by the German chancellor and Mr. Narasimha Rao is another successful step towards the India-German cooperation. The event follows the successful dialogue between Indian and German industrialists since the prime minister's last visit in 1991. The exchange of view and suggestions between the two delegates at the conference largely reflects the compelling necessity of both the nations to come closer to strengthen their bilateral ties in global interests. Modern India is viewed by Germans as a very valuable strategic partner in economic and scientific development because of India's ongoing reform-oriented economic policies aimed at liberalization offering one of the biggest markets in Asia. [end recording] That was a report from our special correspondent in Bonn. Earlier, Mr. Narasimha Rao held talks with the German minister for economic cooperation and development, Mr. Carl-Dieter Sprangar, and reaffirmed India's commitment to closer cooperation with Germany. Mr. Rao also told German leaders that New Delhi is keen on enhanced trade and investment flow from Germany. Germany has assured India that it will continue to take a neutral stance on the Kashmir issue. Kashmir figured prominently in the talks between the prime minister and the German leaders in Bonn. Mr. Narasimha Rao explained the problems faced by India because of Pakistan's abetment of terrorism in Jammu and Kashmir and Punjab. He said, Pakistan is deliberately creating conditions to obstruct a meaningful dialogue.
