BFN [Text] Talks with the economic delegation from the United States began in Kiev today. As agreed by the sides, they are being conducted in four joint working groups. Their main task is to make more specific the directions and substance of Ukraine's cooperation with the United States and international institutions on the matter of developing a market infrastructure in our country. Other issues are being discussed too, in particular special-purpose financial assistance to us on the part of the United States and the investment of U.S. private capital in the Ukrainian economy. It was confirmed at today's plenary sitting that the United States is allocating $150 million for three years to support private entrepreneurship in Ukraine. A proposal to set up a permanent bilateral consultative group was also expressed. Roman Shpek, minister of the economy, heads the Ukrainian delegation at the talks.
