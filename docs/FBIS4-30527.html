BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 28 (KCNA) -- The South Korean opposition Democratic Party at a general meeting of assemblymen on June 25 contended that the strike of the railway and subway workers was attributable to the anachronistic labour policy of the authorities and their ruthless invocation of "government" power, a Seoul-based radio report said. In a resolution it urged an immediate release of the workers whisked away and a halt to the invocation of "government" power against the strike. The resolution said the authorities must solve the problem through dialogue with railway and subway trade unions.
