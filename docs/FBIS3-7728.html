Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report by Veronika Seyr from Skopje] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] The Greek punitive blockade is not only causing serious economic damage in Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM], a country that has been strangled by Yugoslavia's disintegration, the wars in Croatia and Bosnia, and the UN embargo against Serbia. The economic situation is desperate and the political consequences are unpredictable, says Petrus Stefanov, economics minister in Skopje. [Begin Stefanov recording, in Macedonian with superimposed translation into German] The blockade is seriously endangering our economy, but it also affects the overall political situation. It is a direct attack on the stability of the whole region. [end recording] The nationalist opposition in Macedonia sees all its fears confirmed. Ljupco Georgijevksi from the Internal Macedonian Revolutionary Organization says: [Begin Georgijevski recording, in Macedonian with superimposed translation into German] The decision of the Greek Government has not surprised me very much. It is the logical consequence of the Greek policies of the past three years. I would not be surprised either if Athens were to resort to military means. This is a cold war against Macedonia, a cold war which is aggravating the conflicts in the whole of the Balkans and also has consequences for Europe. Accordingly, we deplore that the European Community is not doing enough. [end recording] [passage omitted]
