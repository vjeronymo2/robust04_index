940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / World Stock Markets (America): US shares slip back on easier dollar Wall Street US blue chips finished modestly higher even though a soft dollar and rising commodity prices sent the bond market into retreat, writes Frank McGurty in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average improved 7.37 to 3,766.35 on program-guided buying which erased a 15-point deficit in the final half-hour of the session. But the more broadly based Standard & Poor's 500 was 1.56 lower at 454.92, as declines on the Big Board led advances, 1,131 to 997. The monthly 'double witching' expiration of stock options and future contracts, which was responsible for the Dow's late surge, lifted trading volume to 295m shares. In the secondary markets, the American SE composite pushed in positive territory, closing 1.16 better at 438.20. But the Nasdaq composite, down 0.61 to 726.70, tilted in the opposite direction. Generally stocks traced movements in the bond market, where prices turned lower after six straight days of improvement. Later the benchmark 30-year government bonds were showing a solid loss as traders considered an array of unfavourable developments. Rising commodity prices were troubling, and the market was concerned about an influx of next week's supply influx. Most vexing, however, was the persistent instability of the dollar, which weakened further against the D-mark and failed to make any progress against the yen. But even though the long bond was down, the damage to equities was modest. Stocks were helped by the perception that the pull-back in Treasuries mostly reflected a technical adjustment after the surge that followed Tuesday's move by the Federal Reserve to lift short-term interest rates.
