Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The foreign ministers of the Baltic states have met in the Latvian capital, Riga, to discuss joint foreign policies. During their Friday meeting they talked about the Baltic states' relations with Russia and the Nordic countries. The discussion also covered participation in the NATO Partnership For Peace program and the European Stability Pact. The foreign officers of the three states coordinated their future meeting with their German counterpart, Klaus Kinkel. The agenda of the meeting also included discussions on the establishment of the Baltic states Council of Ministers and the opening of an information bureau in Moscow. The foreign minsters of Lithuania, Latvia and Estonia spoke out for the adoption of jointly coordinated laws and for a united stand in regulating their relations with other countries.
