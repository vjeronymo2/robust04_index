BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yekaterina Zhilyakova] [Text] Moscow April 14 TASS -- The Party of the Majority set up two months ago will not sign the draft memorandum on civic peace and accord, party leader Vyacheslav Grechaninov told a news conference in Moscow on Thursday. Grechaninov said that the authors of the document place it even "higher than the constitution." Additionally, it restricts the rights of parties, public associations and subjects of the federation. He strongly opposed the provisions rejecting political campaigning for elections to federal bodies of power and banning trade unions from organising strikes. The draft agreement also obliges the subjects of the federation to pledge not to change their status for two years. Anatoliy Panfilov, leader of the Constructive Ecological Movement of Russia (KEDR), who also attended the news conference, said that his movement supports the position of the Party of the Majority and has no intention of signing the agreement. The Party of the Majority is currently negotiating with Panfilov's movement the possibility of joining forces to set up a united bloc for the next elections. At the same time, Grechaninov said that his party is ready to take part in the preparation of a "more serious" memorandum of accord.
