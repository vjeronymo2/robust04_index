930104 FT 04 JAN 93 / Singapore has 5.6% growth Singapore's economy grew by 5.6 per cent last year and growth of between 5.5 and 6.5 per cent is expected in 1993 according to Mr Goh Chok Tong, the island republic's prime minister, Kieran Cooke reports. 'Given the slowdown in developed countries and a more mature Singapore economy, this is a good performance,' said Mr Goh in a new year message. He said that a big push by Singapore companies overseas, particularly into China and other south-east Asia countries, should provide the basis for further economic growth. Mr Goh said his government planned to reduce personal and corporate taxes in order to attract more investments while a goods and services tax will be introduced to make up for any revenue shortfalls. Investment commitments in 1992 were a record SDollars 3.5bn (Pounds 1.4bn), he said.
