BFN [Text] At an extraordinary closed meeting today, the government adopted the draft framework agreement with the parliament that was proposed by Prime Minister Berov. It will be in effect until new parliamentary and municipal elections are held in the fall. The Executive Bureau of the Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP] and the leadership of the BSP parliamentary faction discussed the framework agreement. It was agreed to support the segment that deals with setting a term for early parliamentary and municipal elections in the fall. The Socialists will also support the commitments of the Council of Ministers in the social sphere and its efforts to successfully end the negotiations on the country's foreign debt. They think the amendments to the Privatization Bill and a program on social and mass privatization should be adopted in June, while the Law on Defense and Armed Forces, amendments to the Election Bill, the Municipal Administration Bill, the Judicial Act, and the Constitution should be adopted in July. In the decision that was adopted the Socialists committed themselves to supporting the personnel changes envisaged in the framework agreement and have proposed a candidate for the post of deputy prime minister.
