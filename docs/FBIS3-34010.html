Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 2 Feb (MENA)--Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi announced today that the terrorists killed in a hideout in northern Cairo's al-Zawiyah al-Hamra' neighborhood yesterday were prominent and dangerous terrorists known for their recent violent acts in Upper Egypt. He added that the group included several suspects who had been on the run and others who had been convicted in connection with numerous terrorist cases in Upper Egypt. He said that in yesterday's incident no escapes occurred. The interior minister explained that information was received that a group of fugitive terrorists were staying in a hideout in al- Zawiyah al-Hamra' and that after obtaining permission from the Prosecutor's Office, the hideout was stormed. He added that the terrorists opened fire on the security forces, who returned the fire. He said that a detailed statement will be issued later today naming the suspects and the details of the incident. Al-Alfi continued that a quantity of bombs, ammunition, explosives, and weapons that the terrorists had been using in their operations were discovered in the hideout. He pointed out that the security forces found large sums of money with the seven terrorists who were killed, proving that they were hired agents paid for their acts, which he termed cowardly and vile. The interior minister said that the police will not rest as long as one terrorist remains at large in Egypt but will pursue and arrest him. The interior minister reaffirmed that the police have the initiative now and are conducting campaigns of arrests and surveillance against terrorists and all those linked to violent and terrorist crimes. He explained that the police are using scientific methods and careful planning that are based on full information. Asked if Egypt has signed a security cooperation agreement with Pakistan to extradite fugitive terrorists, the interior minister said that there are agreements and contacts with various countries to exchange data and extradite criminals. Hasan al-Alfi, who was speaking after inspecting an exhibition of stolen goods organized by the Cairo Security Directorate, concluded his remarks by saying that public security is one integral whole and that it operates concurrently on security, economic, criminal, and social levels. He explained that the police are making great efforts in all these fields based on the concept that their work is a vocation and not a mere job.
