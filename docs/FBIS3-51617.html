Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Cuban Vice Foreign Minister Isabel Allende today ended her two-week working visit to Spain. The visit has been described as positive. Allende began her working visit to Spain in Madrid on 11 January. She also visited Barcelona, Seville, and Las Palmas, where she met with local authorities and friendship with Cuba associations. Allende told the press that in Madrid, she met with high-ranking Foreign Ministry officials with whom she discussed matters of bilateral. Allende added that the meetings were held within a sincere and constructive atmosphere. At the meetings the Cuban and Spanish officials reviewed the work being done by the Spanish-Cuban cooperation in the areas of science, culture, industry, and technology. They also discussed Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina's scheduled visit to Spain.
