940607 FT 07 JUN 94 / World Trade News: BAe wins Colombia jet order British Aerospace yesterday won a Dollars 230m (Pounds 153.3m) order for regional jets from Colombia and confirmed it was discussing a possible regional aircraft link-up with Fokker, the Dutch aircraft group controlled by Deutsche Aerospace. The Colombian deal involves an order for BAe's regional jet division, Avro International, for eight RJ100 Avroliners from Colombia's Sociedad Aeronautica de Medelln Consolidada. Confirmation of talks between BAe and Deutsche Aerospace over a possible link with Fokker reflects the efforts of the European industry to rationalise regional jet business. BAe said no decision had been made on a possible link-up with Fokker. The British company is also discussing an alliance in the turbopropeller regional aircraft sector with Aerospatiale of France.
