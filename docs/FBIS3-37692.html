Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Zhirnikhin] [Text] Budapest, 21 Feb -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev is to begin a working visit to Hungary today [21 February]. He will visit Budapest during his tour of the countries of the Visegrad group which include Hungary, Poland, and the Czech and Slovak Republics. The head of the Russian foreign policy department will hold talks with his Hungarian counterpart, Geza Jeszenszky, and will meet Hungarian President Arpad Goencz and Prime Minister Peter Boross. In the opinion of the Hungarian side, apart from the development of bilateral ties, the talks will discuss, above all, relations between Hungary and NATO, the participation of the countries of Eastern Europe in the Partnership for Peace program, and ways of resolving the Bosnian crisis.
