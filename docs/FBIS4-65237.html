BFN [Text] Ankara, May 11 (A.A) -- Turkey's current transactions deficit for January and February increased by 57 percent compared to the same period last year, it was announced on Thursday [12 May]. According to the `balance sheet' released by the Central Bank, the deficit for the year's first two months amounted to 701 million U.S. dollars. This amount was 446 million dollars in 1993 for the same period. However the foreign trade deficit has declined by 75 million dollars compared to the same period last year from 1.35 billion dollars to 1.275 billion dollars.
