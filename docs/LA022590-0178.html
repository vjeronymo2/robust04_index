February 25, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

NEW MEXICO STATE ROLLS OVER SAN JOSE FOR 16-0 HOME MARK

Keith Hill scored 22 points and Randy Brown had 21, including eight in a row late in the first half, as No. 24 New Mexico State beat San Jose State, 101-70, Saturday night and finished the year 16-0 at home.

The Aggies (23-3, 14-2 in the Big West) have two road games and the conference tournament left.

Brown's eight points sparked a 14-3 run. He scored on a three-point shot, a three-point play and then stole the ball and scored on a dunk to give the Aggies a 37-27 lead.

San Jose State (7-18, 4-11), which rallied from deficits of 10-0 and 21-13, never recovered. New Mexico State led at halftime, 47-35.

Michael New and Reggie Jordan added 18 points apiece for New Mexico State, which broke the 100-point mark for the second game in a row and for the fourth time this season. Kenne Young led the Spartans with 16.
