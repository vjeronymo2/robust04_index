921214 FT 14 DEC 92 / Construction Contracts: A16 Spalding bypass development BIRSE CONSTRUCTION has won several building and civil engineering projects which include the A16 Spalding bypass, worth Pounds 22.6m, for the Department of Transport. The contract is for improving 18.5km of the A16 between Spalding and Sutterton in Lincolnshire. Work starts early in 1993 with a programme period of two and a half years. The company has also been awarded the A568 Widnes eastern bypass, worth Pounds 14.9m, also from the Department of Transport. The 6km dual carriageway will be started immediately and will be completed in two years. In Scotland the company has received its first large award from Aberdeen Harbour Board for a multi-berth facility, value Pounds 8.5m over an 11-month contract period. The project is for forming berthing facilities by piling inland from existing quay faces, major dredging and excavation plus ancillary works including concrete paving, transit shed and offices. A building contract for the Ministry of Defence has also been won for an RAF Hospital at Lakenheath in Lincolnshire, worth Pounds 10.9m, for completion in 112 weeks with a start in January. The contract covers an operating hospital with full support services and includes refurbishing existing facilities. Other awards include an extension to a school in Leeds for the city council, value Pounds 4m, a bridge over the River Soar and roadworks on the A563 for Leicestershire County Council, worth Pounds 2.1m.
