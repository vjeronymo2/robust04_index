BFN [Text] Beijing, April 20 (XINHUA) -- An international syndicate consisting of six foreign banks and a Chinese bank will provide a loan of 36 million U.S. dollars for a project to manufacture polypropylene in China. The loan is being made by the China Dalian West Pacific Petrochemical Company (WEPEC), a joint-venture of China, France and Hong Kong. The agreement was signed at the Great Hall of the People here today. WEPEC, the largest Sino-foreign joint venture in China's petrochemical field, will use the loan to bring in Italian equipment and technology for the manufacture of polypropylene. WEPEC, established in 1990 with a total investment of 800 million U.S. dollars, is one of the key projects for China's eighth five-year plan period (1990-1995). The loan was arranged and lead-managed by the Shanghai branch of the ABN Amro Bank of the Netherlands. Other participants include banks from Australia, Germany, Singapore, Italy, the Middle East and the Shanghai Pudong Development Bank of China. Among those present at today's signing ceremony were Gu Xiulian, China's minister of chemical industry, D.J. Van Houten, Dutch ambassador to China, and Igno van Waesberghe, regional manager for North East Asia of ABN Amro Bank.
