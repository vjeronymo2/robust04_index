BFN [Report by Carlos Castanaza Rosales] [Excerpt] Guatemalan Foreign Minister Maritza Ruiz de Vielman said yesterday that the Guatemalan Government is trying to persuade the U.S. Government to eliminate the travel warning that has decreased the flow of tourists to Guatemala. She denied that the Guatemalan Government is not doing anything about the situation and said that efforts have been made both in Washington and at the U.S. Embassy in Guatemala. The minister said the interested parties should be aware that this warning is not aimed at Guatemala alone, but that such directives generally include several countries. That is why the Guatemalan Government has presented the necessary social and economic arguments for a favorable resolution, although this warning is a unilateral U.S. decision. She said the hunger strike declared by a U.S. citizen in front of the U.S. Embassy in Guatemala City is something that Washington should take into consideration when a new decision is made.
