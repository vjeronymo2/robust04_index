Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku, Feb. 8, IRNA -- The visiting Iranian deputy foreign minister Mahmud Va'ezi met here Monday night [7 February] with Azeri President Geydar Aliyev who called for the speedy implementation of the agreements already reached between the two countries. The Azeri president termed the last October visit to Baku of his Iranian counterpart Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani as a "turning point" in bilateral relations and congratulated the 15th anniversary of the victory of the Islamic revolution in Iran. Aliyev also expressed hope that in his next month visit to Tehran he would discuss new ways to promote bilateral relations with the Iranian official. Outlining latest developments in Karabakh conflict, the Azeri president reiterated Baku's willingness to find a peaceful solution to the crisis. He called as "unacceptable" the existing conditions offered by Armenia for the establishment of peace in Karabakh and stressed that Azerbaijan was fully ready to defend its territorial integrity. Va'ezi, in charge of Euro-American affairs of Iran's Foreign Ministry, emphasized that he would try to remove the existing obstacles on the way of the implementation of Tehran-Baku agreements. The Iranian official arrived here yesterday on a three- day visit during which he will inaugurate Iran's new consulate here. He held talks with Azeri Foreign Minister Gasan Gasanov upon arrival.
