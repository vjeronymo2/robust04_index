Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Report by Valeriy Gromak under "News" rubric: "If We Had the Money, Would We Buy Ourselves a Defense Capability?"] [Text] Vilnius -- Budget appropriations for Lithuanian territorial defense needs in 1994 will remain proportionally unchanged compared to last year, comprising 4.6 percent of the budget. However, the size of these appropriations has increased 60 percent and stands at 130 million litai (approximately $33 million). This was announced by Prime Minister Adolfas Slezevicius. National Defense Minister Linas Linkevicius is sure that, despite these meager budget appropriations, the country will be able to establish a defense system that befits our membership of international security structures.
