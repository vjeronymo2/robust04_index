BFN [Text] Guangzhou, June 23 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen stressed here that the country's coastal areas, particularly the Special Economic Zones [SEZs], should push their reform and opening up drive to an even higher level. Qian, who is a member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China (CPC), made these remarks when he was touring south China's Guangdong Province from June 17 to 22. He said during his inspection that the coastal areas, particularly the special economic zones, which have been the country's pilot areas to carry out the policy of reform and opening to the outside world, have strong economic and technological forces and have accumulated rich experience in international trade, should now concentrate their efforts on developing high-tech industries and adopting high-level management. Vice-Premier Qian visited Shenzhen and Zhuhai Special Economic Zones as well as Zhongshan and Foshan cities. He also inspected some ports, airports and tourist resorts. After inspecting several ports linking the mainland of China with Hong Kong and Macao, Qian said that customs should be strict in making checks and pay attention to providing good facilities for trade and exchanges. With the economic development, it was also necessary to promote ideological and ethical progress. Vice-Premier Qian also inspected some dikes where local people and soldiers were fighting the floods. He conveyed regards to the flood fighters on behalf of the CPC Central Committee and the State Council.
