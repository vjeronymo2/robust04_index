Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Cecilia Pachano from "The News" newscast] [Text] Today, Defense Minister Rafael Angel Montero Revette met with the president and submitted a report on the officers currently being tried in connection with the 4 February and 27 November events so the president can study the information and make his final decision concerning a possible stay of proceedings. General Montero Revette confirmed he met with the prisoners yesterday at the San Carlos Barracks. He said the goal was to explain why they are not compelled to request their release, adding that this decision could be adopted at will. He also said the government has pledged to give them scholarships and employment so they may quickly become part of society. The scholarships and employment will be given based on their rank and work areas. The defense minister confirmed that Yare Prison's military annex was definitely closed down; he added that the officers being tried in connection with the 27 November and 4 February events will not be transferred to Ramo Verde.
