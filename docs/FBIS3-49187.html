Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ljubljana, Jan 5 (AFP) -- Slovenia suffered a trade deficit of 304 million dollars for the first 11 months of 1993 due to a sharp drop in exports and a small rise in imports, the Economics Ministry announced on Wednesday. Exports amounted to 5.5 billion dollars for the period, off 10.9 percent from the same period in 1992, while imports were set at 5.8 billion dollars, up 3.9 percent. Economic Relations Minister Davorin Kracun said that despite the trade deficit, Slovenia's foreign exchange reserves had increased by around 300 million dollars over the period, to almost 1.5 billion dollars at present.
