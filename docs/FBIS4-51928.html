BFN [By Toru Kunimatsu] [Text] Brussels, 27 Apr -- A diplomatic source disclosed on 27 April that the EC Commission has proposd that Japan create an arena where experts from Japan and the European Union (EU) can hold discussions on deregulation on a regular basis. Earlier, the commission gave Japan a 31-point list requesting improvements in its regulation system, including liberalization of financial and other markets, investment stimulation, and import expansion. These matters were supposed to be discussed at the Japan-EU ministerial talks starting on 22 April, but the talks have been postponed due to the political chaos in Japan. Japan included deregulation in a guideline for external economic reform adopted last month, and plans to work out concrete measures for it by the end of June. By securing a channel of dialogue on deregulation, the EU appears to want to "monitor" Japan in making certain that its deregulation program is put into practice.
