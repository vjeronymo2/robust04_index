940512 FT 12 MAY 94 / Major locks horns with Smith over Scott affair The prime minister and the Labour leader were last night locked in a furious row over standards of conduct in government after a minister who admitted misleading parliament was reprieved by the Speaker of the Commons. Mr John Major gave full backing to Mr Nicholas Scott, the social security minister, after the Speaker declined to refer him for disciplinary action over a false statement that he had made in a debate on rights for the disabled. Mr John Smith pressed home calls for the minister's resignation, claiming that the government had 'disgracefully deceived' disabled people by plotting to wreck legislation that would have given them new rights. Miss Betty Boothroyd, the Speaker, said she would not ask the Commons committee on privileges to rule whether Mr Scott was guilty of 'contempt' of the House. Her decision caused consternation among Labour MPs, some of whom asked her to reconsider the ruling. But ministers privately admitted that the tactics which the government had used to defeat the bill had done serious damage to the Tories' credibility before the European elections. Mr Scott apologised to the Commons on Tuesday, withdrawing a statement that his department had had 'no part' in helping Tory backbenchers draw up the amendments that scuppered the bill. Mr Major said in a letter to the Labour leader yesterday that he regarded the apology as an end to the matter. Downing Street officials also stressed that Mr Scott had 'inadvertently' misled parliament, and had not been in full possession of facts relating to the amendments during last Friday's debate. But Mr Smith retorted that the government's tactics in defeating the private member's bill - and its unwillingness to sack Mr Scott - were 'a squalid affair'. As ministerial colleagues rallied to his defence, Mr Scott's daughter Victoria, who works for the Royal Association for Disability and Rehabilitation, accused the government of 'lying' over the bill. She told BBC Radio 4's Today programme: 'I have spoken to him (Mr Scott) and I hope to talk about it again.'
