920603 FT 03 JUN 92 / World Trade News: Greek phone tenders out SIX consortia have been asked to submit final bids for setting up Greece's first two mobile phone networks, Kerin Hope reports from Athens. Operating licences will be awarded to two in August, covering networks for Greater Athens and the cities of Salonica and Patras. Both networks will use the GSM cellular system. Each operator is expected to invest Dollars 250m (Pounds 138.8m) in the networks, to start operating in mid-1993. They will mark the end of the state phone monopoly. Bidders include: Southwestern Bell and Pacific Telesis of the US; Hutchison Telecommunications of the UK with AT&T; Motorola with Bell Canada; Stet of Italy; Panafon (including Vodafone, France Telecom and Intracom of Greece).
