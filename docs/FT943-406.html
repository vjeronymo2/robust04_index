940929 FT 29 SEP 94 / UK Company News: TI pays up to Pounds 19.4m for German acquisition TI Group, the specialist engineering company, has agreed to pay up to DM47.4m (Pounds 19.4m) for Technoflow, which makes flexible fuel carrying systems for European car manufacturers at plants in Germany and Spain. Technoflow is being sold by Technoform, a private German engineering group based in Kassel, to Bundy, the sector of TI which specialises in fluid carrying systems. Last year Technoflow made a pre-tax profit of Pounds 2m on sales of Pounds 17m. It had net assets of Pounds 6m at the year-end. TI is paying Pounds 5m for an initial 25 per cent stake and will acquire the remaining share capital in two equal parts in 1995 and 1996. Mr Karl Caprano, the chief executive of Technoflow, and the management have agreed to stay with the company.
