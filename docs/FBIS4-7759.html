BFN [Text] Warsaw, 6 Apr--President Lech Walesa, while receiving Heikki Haavisto, Finland's foreign minister, in the Belweder on 6 April, expressed Poland's will to expand contacts with Finland. Earlier, Minister Haavisto was received by Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak. According to the Polish president, Polish-Finnish relations are "not bad," but they do not make full use of all of the capabilities of either of the two countries. In his opinion, Poland and Finland, as medium-size countries, could play a greater role in international organizations. Lech Walesa said that Poland and Finland want to join European organizations that "have proved themselves so far." Finland has already succeeded in this field. Poland still has a long way to go, the president said. The subject of talks with Waldemar Pawlak was economic cooperation between the two states and trade exchange. The prime minister conveyed to the Finnish minister an invitation for the head of the Finnish Government to pay a visit to Poland.
