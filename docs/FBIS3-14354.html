Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, March 26 (AFP) -- Arab countries oppose efforts by Oman and Qatar to end a boycott on firms doing business with Israel, a diplomat close to the Arab League said Saturday [26 March]. The two Gulf oil states recently began "intensive efforts" to ease the embargo, but "this campaign did not win the approval" of other Arab countries because of the February 25 Hebron massacre, the diplomat told AFP. "All the other Arab countries want to maintain the indirect boycott" affecting companies dealing with Israel, the diplomat said on condition his name not be used. Diplomats, who had expected the Arab League to agree to lift the indirect boycott, said Saturday the "climate was not favorable" because of the massacre of 30 Palestinians by a Jewish settler in the West Bank town of Hebron. The United States had called on Arab states to ease their 46-year-old trade boycott of Israel in recognition of the accord on Palestinian self-rule signed in September. But Syrian Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' said on arrival in Cairo for the Arab League meeting: "The boycott of Israel was decided because of its occupation of Arab lands. "It cannot be lifted before Israel withdraws totally from occupied Arab territories and the Palestinians create their own state," al-Shar' said. Syria made no distinction between the direct boycott of Israel and the indirect boycott, al-Shar' said. Apart from "one or two countries," other Arab states had the same view, he added. Two-thirds of the Arab League's 22 members would have to support such a move if it came to a vote. Since the Israeli-PLO accord was signed on September 13, Washington has called for the indirect boycott to be lifted in exchange for US participation in developing the Gaza Strip and West Bank town of Jericho where autonomy is to be launched. Arab countries have boycotted Israel since it was created in 1948. Three years later, they set up a body in charge of drawing up a "black list" of international firms with interests in Israel. Apart from Egypt, which made peace with Israel in 1979, only Kuwait has lifted the indirect boycott, last year. Qatari Foreign Minister Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Al Thani met in London in January with Israeli counterpart Shim'on Peres and called for a lifting of the boycott. Oman has invited Israel to attend a multilateral meeting on Middle East water resources on April 17 -- the first such move by a Gulf country.
