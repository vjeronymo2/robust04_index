Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Interior Minister Salamah Hammad has stated that the criminals who planted explosives at the Cinema Rivoli in Amman, Cinema Salwa in al-Zarqa', and the al-'Akrush Supermarket in the town of Safut have been apprehended. The explosive charge planted in Safut was discovered and defused, he noted. In a statement to the Jordanian News Agency, PETRA, the interior minister added: Muhammad 'Ali Shihadah, alias Abu-'Abbad, a construction worker aged 26 years, and 'Id Salih al-Jahalin, a plumber aged 31 years, have admitted that they committed these crimes. They are part of a group that will be uncovered later. Hammad went on to say that the apprehension of these two criminals was the result of remarkable and persistent efforts by the Public Security organs since these crimes occurred. He said that investigations are continuing to unearth the details of plans to undermine the homeland's security and stability. Hammad said that these two criminals who perpetrated these crimes, which aimed to undermine the citizens' security, have connections with some persons who have returned from Afghanistan. Concluding his statement, Hammad said: We take pride in our inspired leadership, which has always rendered us loyal soldiers of the homeland, eager to perform our duties. We pledge to this leadership to remain, as King Husayn has always known us, people who have faith in the message, and who will keep the trust.
