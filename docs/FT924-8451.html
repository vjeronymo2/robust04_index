921111 FT 11 NOV 92 / Checkland to leave BBC early SIR Michael Checkland, director-general of the BBC, announced yesterday that he is to hand over to Mr John Birt, his designated successor, at Christmas, more than two months earlier than expected. It had been believed that Sir Michael, angered by being given an extension of only one year to his five-year contract by the BBC board of governors, would stay until the end of his term. His contract was due to finish on February 28. Sir Michael said in a statement that he had suggested and agreed the arrangement with Mr Marmaduke Hussey, the BBC chairman, yesterday. The news of Sir Michael's early departure comes three weeks after he made extraordinary remarks about the BBC chairman and the board of governors at a Royal Television Society symposium. The BBC director-general accused the board of governors of getting too involved in the day-to-day running of the corporation. He also called for a wider range of governors and ages. The real controversy was caused when he said it was absurd for the BBC chairman, who was given a second five-year term by the government, to be leading the corporation into the next century at the age of 73. Mr Hussey is now 69. Sir Michael apologised later in the day. Mr Hussey said the BBC owed Sir Michael a great debt for his dedication to the corporation's interests. 'In his six years he has worked with courage, determination and enthusiasm to ensure that the BBC understands the challenges it faces.'
