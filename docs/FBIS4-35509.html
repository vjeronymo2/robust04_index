BFN [Text] Nicosia, May 30 (CNA) -- Cyprus and Syria have signed a cultural cooperation programme for a three-year period. The programme was signed in Damascus by Cyprus Education and Culture Minister Klairi Angelidhou and her Syrian counterpart Muhammad Ghassan al-Halabi, during Angelidhou's official visit to Syria. During her stay in Syria, Angelidhou had separate meetings with the Syrian ministers of culture and higher education, Najah al-'Attar and Salilah Sunqur. Angelidhou was also received by Syrian Prime Minister Mahmud al-Zu'bi, whom she briefed on the latest developments in the Cyprus problem. She thanked al-Zu'bi for the consistent position of the Syrian Government and of president Hafiz al-Asad on the Cyprus problem. Al-Zub'i reiterated his country's support for a just solution of the Cyprus issue on the basis of U.N. Security Council resolutions and stressed that Syria rejected any kind of occupation or intervention of one country in the affairs of another. The Northern part of the Republic of Cyprus has been under Turkish military occupation since 1974. The Cypriot minister had also a meeting with the Greek Orthodox patriarch of Antioch Ignatios.
