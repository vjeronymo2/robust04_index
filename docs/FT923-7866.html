920814 FT 14 AUG 92 / Secretarial rises 'above average' SECRETARIAL PAY, which has risen an average of 6.5 per cent over the past year, is outstripping inflation and average earnings, according to Reward Group, David Goodhart writes. Chief executives' secretaries pay rose 7.3 per cent, while shorthand/audio typists' increases averaged 5.6 per cent. Average earnings as a whole are rising at 6 per cent, with clerical staff receiving 4.5 per cent, according to Reward. The study shows that the average pay of chief executives' secretaries in central London has risen from Pounds 11,229 in July 1987, 25 per cent above the national average, to Pounds 18,624 in July 1992, 40 per cent above the national average. In central London, where the cost of living has moved closer to the national average, the average pay for a manager's secretary is Pounds 14,914.
