Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] During a session that lasted until the early hours of this morning, the People's Assembly passed the Unified Tax Bill in its final reading. Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi said the law, as issued, is well-balanced considering the amendments that have been made to it. He explained that the government was responsive to all the views offered by the deputies. He added that a high-level committee chaired by the justice minister will amend all relevant legislation to bring it into line with current circumstances. People's Assembly Speaker Dr. Ahmad Fathi Surur asserted that the new law, which was discussed at length, contains no contradictions of Islamic shari'ah. After passing the new bill, the People's Assembly adjourned until 8 January 1994. The Assembly approved articles 85, 87, and 88 pertaining to tax exemptions for families. It also approved Article 90, which specifies the tax rates as follows: Section 1: 20 percent on income of up to 2,000 pounds. Section 2: 27 percent on income greater than 2,500 pounds and up to 7,000 pounds. Section 3: 35 percent on income greater than 7,000 pounds and up to 16,000 pounds. Section 4: 40 percent on income greater than 16,000 pounds and up to 27,000 pounds. Section 5: 45 percent on income greater than 27,000 pounds and up to 68,000 pounds. Section 6: 48 percent on income greater than 68,000 pounds. Regarding income from salaries and similar and other revenue [as heard], the tax rate on them will be the same as that in section 1 -- 20 percent.
