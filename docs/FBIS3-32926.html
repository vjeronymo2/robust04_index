Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Cordoba, 14 Feb (NA) -- Cordoba Governor Eduardo Angeloz today confirmed that he wants to become a presidential candidate for the Radical Civic Union [UCR] in 1995 "if the party accepts it and if all the instrumental steps that I want to impose are accepted by the party." Angeloz denied press reports in which he allegedly conveyed to President Carlos Menem his decision to give up his presidential candidacy. Angeloz explained: "This confusion is not good for our country or anyone. I am the one who, depending on my mood, will have to decide in due time if I feel like becoming a candidate." Concerning the possibility that Senator Fernando de la Rua might actually become the UCR candidate, the governor said: "In due time we will see who is in a better position to represent the UCR." Angeloz later stressed: "There will not be any internal rift within the UCR," although he admitted "the existence of acute, hard, and hostile disagreements. The party will remain unchanged."
