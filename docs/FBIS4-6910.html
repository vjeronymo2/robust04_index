BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA April 22 -- The third conference on sub- regional economic cooperation was opened here this morning in the Presence of P.M. Vo Van Kiet and Asian Development Bank [ADB] President Mitsuo Sato. The conference are attended by high-level delegations of the Governments of Thailand, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar [Burma] and the People's Republic of China and Vietnam. ADB senior officials and ambassadors and charge d'affaires of the participant countries are also on hand. After the opening speech delivered by Deputy Prime Minister Tran Duc Luong, head of the Vietnamese delegation to the conference, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet delivered a speech in which he said the high-level conference on sub-regional economic cooperation initiated by ADB was highly valued and actively responded by the countries concerned including Vietnam. He said the Vietnamese Government pledged to extended support and cooperation in sub-regional projects, and called for financial assistance from the international community to carry out these projects. The Vietnamese leader expressed his hope that this conference would mark an important step in the process of cooperation in the sub-region and wished it a success. Many papers on projects of cooperation in various fields including energy, transport, communications, tourism and trade were also delivered at the conference.
