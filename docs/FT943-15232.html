940711 FT 11 JUL 94 / Insurer paying Pounds 3,000 a recruit Allied Dunbar, the insurance company, is paying its managers up to Pounds 3,000 for every new recruit who meets specific standards in an effort to increase the quality of its sales force. The scheme fits in with efforts in the life industry to take on more experienced recruits who are deemed more likely to offer 'best advice' to clients and so meet more exacting regulatory requirements. In a scheme introduced this year believed to break new ground in the life industry, Allied Dunbar is paying its biggest bonuses to managers who bring into the company sales agents who have previous experience with life products. Smaller sums are paid for taking on people regarded as quality recruits by dint of their age, background and previous salaries in other jobs. Last year, in an experimental version of the scheme, about 45 of the company's 350 managers qualified for recruitment bonuses, earning between Pounds 3,000 and about Pounds 21,000. While Allied Dunbar believes paying bonuses for recruits is an innovative way to tackle some of the problems facing life insurance, some industry critics believe that offering cash incentives to recruit sales people can lead to managers bringing in large numbers of staff who may in some cases be given inappropriate guidance. Eight former sales agents and two present agents, all from Dunbar's office in Chelmsford, Essex, have said they were given inflated promises from company representatives about how much they could earn at Allied Dunbar and then were given little management support to help them meet sales targets. Allied Dunbar denies that any inflated promises were made, although it added: 'We acknowledge that there was poor management support at Chelmsford, and we have taken steps to change the management.'
