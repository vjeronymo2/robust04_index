940820 FT 20 AUG 94 / Finance and the Family C & G action groups grow - At a Glance Action groups are growing among Cheltenham & Gloucester building society members unhappy with the board's plans for accepting Lloyds Bank Pounds 1.8bn cash offer for the society. First there was the C & G alternatives group, headed by Paul Rivlin and Peter Nicholson, which plans to call a special meeting to discuss the society's future. Now James Edwards, of Cobham, in Kent has written to the Times proposing to set up a C & G's borrowers' action group. Borrowers have been excluded from cash payments under the recent high court ruling.
