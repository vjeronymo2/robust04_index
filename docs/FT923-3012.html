920915 FT 15 SEP 92 / Accountants are reprimanded THREE FIRMS of chartered accountants have been reprimanded by their disciplinary body for breaches of investment business regulations. RA McLeod of Windsor, was fined Pounds 500 and ordered to pay Pounds 250 in costs by the disciplinary committee of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales following hearings last month. The firm failed to give adequate warnings of the risks involved to a client relating to certain investments, failed to send engagement letters and failed to keep proper records in accordance with regulations. Sowerbutts & Co, based in Camberley, Surrey, was fined Pounds 500 and ordered to pay Pounds 250 costs for a series of breaches of regulations including failing to keep adequate records. Bygott Burn of Grimsby, Humberside, was reprimanded and ordered to pay Pounds 250 costs for handling a money from an investment business client when not authorised to do so. In addition, Mr Peter Downey of Horner, Downey & Co in Bromborough, Merseyside, was reprimanded, fined Pounds 1,000 and ordered to pay Pounds 500 costs for 13 issues surrounding the audit of a limited company since 1986. He failed to prepare the accounts in accordance with the format laid down in the Companies Acts, statements of standard accounting practice and auditing standards.
