940805 FT 05 AUG 94 / Labour mounts pollution offensive Mr Chris Smith, Labour's spokesman on environmental protection, yesterday launched a drive to combat city pollution and increase active public interest in the issue, David Lascelles and James Blitz write. Mr Smith, launching Labour's Airwatch campaign, urged the government and local authorities to take steps to improve the monitoring of pollution levels. He called for a statutory obligation on local authorities to collect information on regional air quality and inform schools and other local bodies of the results. He encouraged individuals to test the amount of nitrogen dioxide, a toxic pollutant, in the air, using a simple diffusion tube.
