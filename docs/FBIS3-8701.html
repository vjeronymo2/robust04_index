Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Dorina Baesu report: "The Atmosphere in the Army Is Tense"] [Text] Sources within the armed forces have told us that the atmosphere is currently very tense among the officers, and even among the lower ranks, owing to the appointment of the new [national defense] minister, Gheorghe Tinca. First, because for the first time a civilian -- who is not familiar with military issues -- is leading the armed forces, and second, because many of the members of the Army Intelligence Direction claim that Tinca used to work for the Securitate, more precisely for SIE [Foreign Intelligence Service]. During the reign of the deceased [former dictator Nicolae Ceausescu] it was known that the Army and the Securitate used to loath each other. The memory of those times still persists. Had it not been for the precedent of the CADA [Action Committee for the Democratization of the Army, an organization of young officers immediately after December 1989], when the attempt by certain officers to revolt against the army leadership was suppressed, many officers would now react more harshly, the sources said.
