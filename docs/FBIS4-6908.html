BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA April 22 -- Speaker of the Indian House of Commons [as received] Shivraj Patil [speaker of the Indian Lok Sabha] received in New Delhi yesterday afternoon Vietnam's National Assembly Chairman Nong Duc Manh and his party now on an official visit to India. Speaking during the reception, Chairman Nong Duc Manh said he treasured the close friendship and solidarity between Vietnam and India which had been fostered by President Ho Chi Minh and Prime Minister J. Nehru. He stressed that the renovation process and expansion of the cooperation ties with other countries over the world was a good opportunity for Vietnam to learn experience from foreign countries including India. He invited Mr Shivraj Patil to visit Vietnam, and expressed his wish for further cooperation between the two legislatures. Mr. Patil accepted Mr. Manh's invitation with pleasure. The former spoke highly of the achievements recorded by the Vietnamese people, and he wished that the all-sided cooperation between Vietnam and India would continue to develop to a higher level. Later the Vietnamese delegation met with 30 Indian parliamentarians of various parties from different states. The same day speaker Shivraj Patil gave a banquet in honour of the Vietnamese National Assembly Chairman and his party.
