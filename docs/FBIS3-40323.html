Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:CSO [Article by A.S.: "Russians Bring Aid"] [Text] A plane from Russia with 22 metric tons of humanitarian aid for distressed children and humanitarian organizations in Yugoslavia and eastern Bosnia arrived at Surcin Airport yesterday afternoon. The shipment contains 20 tons of food for babies and small children and two tons of indispensable medications such as penicillin, other antibiotics, and diarrhea remedies. Two-thirds of the shipment will stay in Yugoslavia; one-third will be distributed in eastern Bosnia, according to Lindel Sachs, spokesman for the UN High Commissioner for Refugees in Belgrade. According to Mrs. Sachs, this is the first humanitarian shipment of food from Russia. Prior to this, only teams of drivers used to come from Russia to drive trucks through the war zones. "Our office was accorded the honor of organizing the distribution of the shipment, which is always welcome in these regions," said Lindel Sachs. The donor of this humanitarian aid is the State Committee of the Russian Federation for Elimination of Consequences of Natural Disasters and for Civilian Aid; the intermediary is the Russian Embassy in Belgrade; while the distributor is the UN High Commissioner for Refugees in Belgrade.
