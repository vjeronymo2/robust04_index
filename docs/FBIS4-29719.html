BFN [Text] Thailand yesterday suggested the Cambodian government and the Khmer Rouge try to agree on the agenda before starting peace negotiations to ensure progress. "It is better than talking about the issues which each side cannot accept. "Otherwise, there would be no breakthrough," Foreign Minister Prasong Sunsiri said when asked to comment on the failure of the round-table discussions in Phnom Penh on Thursday. The second round of talks, which resumed on Wednesday following the one hosted by King Norodom Sihanouk in Pyongyang last month, was a deadlock as both sides could not agree on the agendas. Sqn Ldr [Squadron Leader] Prasong reiterated that the peace negotiation could not be successfully concluded in just one meeting and encouraged the Cambodian leaders and the Khmer Rouge representatives to find opportunities for more talks.
