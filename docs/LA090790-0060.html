September 7, 1990, Friday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: MOORPARK;

COUNCIL, MERCHANTS SEEK REVITALIZATION

Outdoor dining, entertainment and produce displays will be allowed in Moorpark in an effort to revitalize the downtown area, the City Council has decided.

The council voted 5 to 0 Wednesday night to issue temporary permits for businesses wishing to participate. The permits, which merchants can apply for at a cost of $28.80, will be good for six months, said Pat Richards, the city's community development director.

Richards said once the trial period is over, the program will be re-evaluated by the council.

The older downtown area is centered at Moorpark Avenue and High Street. CARLOS V. LOZANO
