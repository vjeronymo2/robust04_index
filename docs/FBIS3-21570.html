Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The first train carrying 60 nuclear warheads, removed from the Ukrainian strategic missiles, left the railway station of Pervomaisk at 19:42 on March 4 and headed to Russia, an informed source in the Russian Defense Ministry told Interfax. He said that at 12:00, Saturday, the train was near the city of Poltava in Ukraine. The same source said that the nuclear warheads would be taken to the Sverdlovsk Region, where they would be disassembled. He also pointed out that a second train would be sent to Russia from the city of Khmelnitskiy in the middle of March. There are a total of about 2,000 nuclear warheads in Ukraine's 46th missile division (Pervomaisk) and the 43rd missile brigade (Khmelnitskiy).
