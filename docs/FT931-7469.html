930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / UK Company News: Chas Church to get more bank support A number of banks, which 18 months ago launched a Pounds 53m rescue of Charles Church, the Surrey-based housebuilder, have agreed to put more money into the business. A group of ten leading shareholders led by Royal Bank of Scotland are understood to have provided about Pounds 5m of additional working capital to the company. Mr Stewart Baseley, chief executive, said the money would be used to buy land. He said that house sales by the group were currently almost double that of the corresponding period last year. In 1991, some 16 banks agreed to swap Pounds 53m of borrowings for an 81 per cent stake in the group. Royal Bank of Scotland said yesterday: 'We have been extremely pleased with the company and its management who substantially have met the objectives set down two years ago. We now perceive that the time is right to be investing in the company to provide for future growth.'
