Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] The leaders of the opposition factions have requested to meet with Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin. They wish to discuss jointly agreed-upon steps to guarantee the safety and well-being of the Jewish settlers in Hebron in an attempt to prevent a rift among the Israeli public. Likud Chairman Binyamin Netanyahu even proposed that Rabin join the opposition factions in agreeing on a practical plan to protect the residents of Judaea, Samaria, and the Gaza Strip.
