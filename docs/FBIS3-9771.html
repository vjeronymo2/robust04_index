Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, March 30 (BTA) -- In the course of his formal visit to Albania completed yesterday, Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov and his Albanian partners expressed a wish to promote bilateral relations to build up trust in the Balkans. Foreign Ministry spokesman Radko Vlaykov today met the press to elaborate on the visit. Asked if Bulgaria and Albania differed on the UN Security Council decision to send Turkish peacekeepers to former Yugoslavia, Vlaykov said this issue was only considered in the course of the general review of the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Daskalov reiterated the Bulgarian call on neighbouring countries to refrain from participation in the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces]. Vlaykov said during a joint press conference of the Bulgarian and Albanian foreign ministers in Tirana yesterday, Daskalov was asked about the Bulgarian position on Kosovo. He said Bulgaria opposed any interference by foreign forces in seeking a solution to this internal problem.
