BFN [Text] Budapest, May 18 (MTI) -- "We believe that those who voted for the Socialist Party in the first round of parliamentary elections on May 8 wanted a change and considered us to be a predictable, open and expert party," Hungarian Socialist Party (HSP) [MSZP] deputy chairman Imre Szekeres told a press conference in Tiszaujvaros (E Hungary) on Wednesday. The HSP would like to see Hungary ruled by a coalition government pursuing three major foreign-policy aims: rapid integration with European structures, including NATO; normalized relations with neighbouring countries; and ensuring autonomy for cross-border Hungarian minorities. "It would be good if the country had a fully-formed new government by late June or early July. The HSP is ready to conduct expeditious coalition talks in public," Szekeres said.
