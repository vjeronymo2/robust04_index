BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Mainayev] [Text] Bishkek 9 June TASS -- Indian Minister of State for External Affairs Salman Khurshid expressed satisfaction with the results of his working visit to Kirghizia [Kyrgyzstan]. The minister said that all agreements, which are to be signed during Indian Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao's forthcoming official visit to Kirghizia, are almost ready or in the final stage of completion. The Indian premier is scheduled to visit the Republic in the first half of July. The agreements will cover a wide range of bilateral relations between the two countries, from trade, economic, cultural ties to working out fast coordinated actions in fight against international terrorism and drug trafficking. A group of Indian businessmen, who are accompanying the minister, finalized their priorities in cooperation with their Kirghiz counterparts. The Indians are interested in the possibility of setting up joint enterprises to produce medicine, process agricultural produce, dress leather, manufacture paper, extract minerals and create infra-structure and telecommunications networks. Salman Khurshid said that the two sides have almost agreed to allot a supply quota of mercury, antimony, rare-earth metals and dressed leather and pelts to India. According to the minister, India intends to review the agreement's clauses about making investments in Kirghizia and crediting specific branches of its economy. This is being done to find a compromise and satisfy both parties, he said.
