Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, Feb. 2, IRNA -- Iran's Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati wound up a two-day visit to Bulgaria Wednesday. A three-year trade agreement was signed at the end of Velayati's visit. According to the agreement, signed by Iran's Deputy Commerce Minister Hoseyn Noqrehkar Shirazi and Bulgarian Commerce Minister Valentin Karabashev, the two countries are to boost volume of bilateral trade. Furthermore the agreement calls for creating a balance in the Tehran-Sofia trade. It also calls for setting up of a trade commission to oversee implementation of the terms of the agreement, propose proper solutions for the problems and find ways of boosing bilateral trade. During his two-day visit Velayati held talks with Bulgarian President Zhelyu Zhelev and Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov.
