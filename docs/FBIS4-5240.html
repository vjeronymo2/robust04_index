BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA April 7 -- Swedish Prime Minister Carl Bildt arrived here this morning for an official friendship visit to Vietnam as guest of Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. He was welcomed in a state ceremony at the Presidential Palace by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet, Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam, Mayor of Hanoi Le At Hoi and other Vietnamese ranking officials. Swedish Ambassador to vietnam Mats Aberg and members of the diplomatic corps and representatives of international organizations in Hanoi were also present. Speaking at the ceremony Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet thanked the Swedish Government and people for their great support and assistance to the Vietnamese people. For his part Prime Minister Carl Bildt stressed that his visit was a landmark of a new era in the relationship between the two countries. He reaffirmed that the Swedish Government and people would continue their aid to Vietnam, thus to contribute to its economic renovation. Later P.M. Carl Bildt and his party attended the inaugural ceremony of household electric appliances centre in Hanoi, a joint venture between Vietnam and Electrolux of Sweden. In the afternoon, a number of ranking officials in the Swedish delegation met with representatives of the State Planning Committee, the State Committee for Cooperation and Investment, and the Ministry of Trade. During these meetings, the two sides exchanged views on possibilities of broadening the cooperation and investment and economic exchanges between Vietnam and Sweden in conformity with the new situation. Yesterday, the Swedish prime minister and his party arrived in Ho Chi Minh City, beginning a three-day official friendship visit to Vietnam. The same day, mayor of the city Truong Tan Sang paid a courtesy visit to Mr. Carl Bildt, during which he informed the Swedish guests of the socio-economic situation, especially the achievements recorded by the people of the city in the current renovation process. In the evening, mayor Truong Tan Sang gave a banquet in honour of Swedish Prime Minister Carl Bildt and his party. Earlier, the Swedish prime minister attended a ceremony to inaugurate a mobile telephone system which started in early 1994. The system were installed with equipment made by Ericsson of Sweden at a cost of more than USD 6 million.
