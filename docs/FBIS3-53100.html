Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Faysal Makram and Iqbal 'Ali 'Abdallah report: "24 Jordanian Officers in Sanaa To Supervise Implementation of Dialogue Committee Decisions"] [Text] An atmosphere of caution prevailed in Aden yesterday, and shops in the city closed following the refusal of 'Ali Salim al-Bid, deputy chairman of the Yemeni Presidential Council, to go to al-Jand Mosque in Ta'izz to meet with President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih. It seems that the Yemeni crisis has entered a new phase of complications as a result of the failure to hold the meeting for which the ulema called. [passage omitted] Military sources have revealed to AL-HAYAH the presence of 24 Jordanian officers in Sanaa at a request from the national dialogue committee concerned with the Yemeni crisis to supervise its decisions concerning the military aspect, redeployment of the Armed Forces, and efforts to end military tension. However, these officers have not been able to move into Aden as a result of Socialist Party leaders' objection to their presence and to entrusting them with certain tasks, despite their earlier approval.
