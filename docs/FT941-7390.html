940225 FT 25 FEB 94 / Stock prices fall sharply after governor's speech Hong Kong stock prices fell sharply after Governor Chris Patten told legislators yesterday he would press forward with electoral reform against China's opposition, Reuter reports. The Hang Seng index tumbled 331.21 points, or 3.08 per cent in thin trading yesterday to close at 10,432.02 and this morning again declined sharply, dropping through the 10,000 point level. After just six minutes of trading the index lost 489.21 points or 4.69 per cent to 9,942.81. Mr Patten said he would publish the final and most controversial part of his reform package today. Investors have followed every twist in Sino-British relations.
