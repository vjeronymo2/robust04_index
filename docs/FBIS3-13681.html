Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Report by Anis Abu-al-'Ila in Bonn] [Excerpt] In the first official reaction to statements made yesterday by U.S. President Bill Clinton and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin on the developments of peace in the Middle East, Syrian Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' said in an exclusive statement to the MBC: [Begin al-Shar' recording] The requirements of peace are well known: Full [Israeli] withdrawal and implementation of Security Council Resolutions 242, 338, and 425. For security and stability to be achieved in the region, the peace process must be comprehensive. Otherwise, it remains merely talk that will not produce concrete results; on the contrary, using hollow expressions about peace without real results will lead to terrorism and violence, as happened in the massacre at the holy al-Ibrahimi Mosque. [end recording] The statement was given during al-Shar's current three-day visit to Bonn. [passage omitted]
