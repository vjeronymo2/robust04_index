BFN [Passages within quotation marks in Creole] [Excerpts] On 21 April in Port-au-Prince, Haitian Senate President Firmin Jean-Louis presented a bill granting amnesty to the authors and accomplices of the coup d'etat that overthrew President Jean-Bertrand Aristide on 30 September 1991. Jean-Louis' presentation, accompanied by about 10 other senators, took place in a hotel of the capital in the presence of representatives of the UN secretary general's Friendly Four countries. The following is a report by Pierre Jerome Richard: [Begin recording] [Richard] "The senators who are part of the normal Senate met the morning of 21 April at the Christopher Hotel to propose an amnesty law for the benefit of those who participated in the 30 September [1991] coup d'etat, according to the senators' report to the press. Several foreign diplomats, including Eric Falt, UN spokesman for Haiti; (Mrs. Elza Ocho Campe), Venezuelan charge d'affaires; (Francis Fillol), Canadian ambassador; and [U.S. Ambassador] William Lacy Swing were present at the ceremony, which began at about 1130. Senate President Firmin Jean-Louis opened the ceremony. He thanked all the diplomats and members of the press who were present. In the opinion of Senator Jean-Louis, this proposal is the key to unblocking the country's crisis. Sen. Martinez describes the contents of this proposal:" [Martinez] Considering that the 30 September 1991 coup d'etat, which involved the forced departure of the president of the Republic, is an act provided for and punishable by law, and this event gave rise to many violations that necessarily took on a political character. Considering that the legislative power lays down laws on all subjects of public interest, the Senate has voted the following law: Article 1. The authors and accomplices of the 29-30 September 1991 coup, which entailed the departure of the president of the Republic, are granted amnesty. Article. 2. This amnesty law applies to all violations ensuing from the coup d'etat, ranging from 29-30 September 1991 to the date of promulgation of this amnesty law. Article. 3. Regarding the rights of third parties, the victims of the acts excused by this law will have recourse to a special compensation fund that will be created for that purpose. Article. 4. This law will be published and executed at the behest of the ministers of justice and the interior in the areas that respectively concern them. [Richard] "Sen. Jean-Louis hopes the international community will continue helping the Haitian people in their struggle for democracy." [passage omitted] "This proposal, which was supported by 13 out of 17 senators, was adopted by senators from many blocs within the Senate. Among those who voted for this law are Julio Larosiliere, Herard Pauyo, Turneb Delpe, and Clark Parent. At the conclusion of the meeting, we noted the presence of [former] Sen. Reynold Georges and Sen. Jacques Rony Mondestin, who were going to bring their support to the plan. ``During the ceremony, Sen. Jean-Louis asked everyone present to sing the country's national anthemn. This is how the ceremony ended, with Sen. Jean-Louis, as master of ceremonies, inviting the guests to partake of refreshments. Many of the participants took advantage of the refreshments to give their opinions. Among them was foreign diplomat Eric Falt:'' [Falt] We have been in a deadlock for several months. It is a proposal aimed at getting out of the current crisis. We can congratulate ourselves on that. It is a step in the right direction. Regarding the rest, it is first and foremost a Haitian issue. [words indistinct] but as a general rule, we think it is a step in the right direction that could unblock the crisis. [Richard] "The host, Sen. Firmin Jean-Louis, asked the press to help him popularize the bill. The senator also said he would take steps within the Chamber of Deputies to see if it would subscribe to this proposal in the future. Pierre Jerome Richard for Signal FM." [end recording]
