BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali has said Pakistan is pursuing a foreign policy based on national interests, which has the definite direction and the thrust to achieve major objectives. In an interview in Islamabad, he said the Kashmir issue has been internationalized in a successful manner and that initiatives have been taken for peace in Afghanistan and Tajikistan. Such initiatives have also been taken regarding Somalia and Bosnia. The foreign minister said Pakistan has been successful in securing U.S. agreement not to press for a rollback of its nuclear program. Reviewing the achievements of the present government in the foreign affairs, Asif Ali expressed the confidence that Pakistan will be able to acquire the F-16 aircraft without compromising its fundamental stand on nuclear nonproliferation. He said Pakistan has refused to accept any unilateral and discriminatory conditions on the nuclear issue.
