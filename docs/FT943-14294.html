940715 FT 15 JUL 94 / Dentists face health reforms Ways of extending the purchaser-provider structure of the 1991 health reforms to the dental service are being considered by the government, Alan Pike writes. A green paper published yesterday envisages introducing a system under which health authorities would purchase dental services at local level. They would agree contracts with dentists to obtain services reflecting specific local conditions and the state of oral health in the area. Dr Brian Mawhinney, health minister, said: 'The government believes that there would be clear advantages if the system of payment for dentists could be made more sensitive to local needs and priorities for dental care. That was the thrust of our changes to the wider NHS, and the benefits there are now tangible.'
