March 17, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

'THE HANDMAID'S TALE' AS A COMMENTARY ON ABORTION

In his on-the-mark review of "The Handmaid's Tale" (March 7), Peter Rainer refers to abortion only once, somewhat obliquely.

Did he miss the fact that the movie was an attempt to be a commentary on abortion? Or was he just confused about what the message was and therefore left the subject untouched? If so, I don't blame him.

The only way I knew the film was a pro-abortion effort was because the director, Volker Schlondorff, spoke at the Los Angeles premiere and made his intent clear.

The movie backfired on him, though. The dismal future world it portrays is one that is the logical extension of unfettered abortion. If every woman is entitled to abortion, then all women are entitled to abortion, and we have delegated to pregnant women the right to extinguish the species. In the film, only 1 in 100 women can have children; it's a short step from that to "1 in 100 will have a child."

KEVIN R. DAVIS, Beverly Hills
