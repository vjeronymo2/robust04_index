March 26, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: OSCAR QUOTES

"I hate getting dressed up. Granny Clampett's clothes are much more comfortable." -- Irene Ryan, 1966

"This is not an Academy Awards, ladies and gentlemen, it's a freak-out."

-- Bob Hope, 1970

"Shut up, Bob Hope." -- Shirley MacLaine, 1970

"I'll never let myself in for another emotional experience like that. Ye Gods, I almost had a heart attack." -- Shelley Winters, 1952

"The academy is only two or three years behind the critics."

-- Gregory Peck, 1971

"(Oscar night) is just another excuse to get drunk or something."

-- Gary Busey, 1978

"Unless someone happens to be looking over my shoulder, I'll be voting for myself." -- David Niven, 1959 (in a cablegram to Tony Curtis)

"This guy Charleton Heston is a nice fellow, but what a hamola."

-- Aldo Ray, 1960

SOURCE: "Inside Oscar," by Mason Wiley & Damien Bona
