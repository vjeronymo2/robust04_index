BFN [Text] Baku, June 13 (TURAN): "The leadership of the republic sees the results of the meetings and talks with Russian Defense Minister, Pavel Grachev, as fruitful and useful", the State Advisor, Gabil Guseyinli, told TURAN. The same source reports that on June 11 during the talks, the Azeri President and Russian Defense Minister discussed military cooperation within the framework of the Collective Security Pact. Pavel Grachev spoke about the need for cooperation between military personnel of both countries and promised to contribute to the creation of the Azeri army. Discussing the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict, Pavel Grachev said that he recognizes the territorial integrity of Azerbaijan and considers Nagorno-Karabakh a part of it. During the talks, the Grachev wished that Baku take into account the strategic and military intersts of Russia in the region. The Azeri President said that Russia's military leadership should use its opportunities and authority to reach a peaceful and diplomatic settlement of the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict and the liberation of all occupied- territories by the Armenians. Sources from the Presidential staff of Azerbaijan report that the introduction of the Russian peacemaking forces in the conflict region was not a major part of the discussion. However, Grachev and people close to him, repeatedly stated the necessity to introduce Russian peacemaking forces in the conflict region.
