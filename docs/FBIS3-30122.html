Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 24 KYODO -- Japan's imports of crude oil in January fell 3.7 percent to 151.71 million barrels for the sixth consecutive month of decline from year-earlier levels, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said Thursday [24 February]. The United Arab Emirates was the biggest supplier of crude oil to Japan with 38.43 million barrels, followed by Saudi Arabia with 30.95 million barrels, Iran with 15.91 million barrels and Indonesia with 11.95 million barrels. The amount of crude oil processed in January came to 140.14 million barrels, up 2.3 percent from a year earlier. Production of fuel oil increased 4.2 percent to 127.50 million barrels, rising for the sixth straight month. Domestic sales of fuel oil edged up 0.7 percent to 132.97 million barrels, gaining for the second consecutive month. Fuel oil imports dropped 4.1 percent to 17.30 million barrels, affected by sluggish imports of C-type fuel oil. Fuel oil exports jumped 53.2 percent to 11.45 million barrels.
