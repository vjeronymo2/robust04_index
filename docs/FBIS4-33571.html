BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Nicaraguan economic developments monitored through 7 June. The value of the country's exports in the first half of the year grew 8 percent when compared to export revenues obtained during the same period last year, according to Nicaraguan economic authorities. (Paris AFP in Spanish 0001 GMT 3 Jun 94) The Nicaraguan Central Bank (BCN) has announced that in line with the BCN's policy of monthly 1 percent devaluations, the corboba has lost 5 percent of its value in relation to the U.S. dollar over the past five months. The official exchange rate is now 6.65 cordobas to the dollar, and, if the rate of devaluation continues unchanged, the exchange rate will be 7.12 cordobas to the dollar by the end of the year. Up until April, the overall rate of inflation was 2.41 percent. The authorities forecast the inflation rate will increase to 10 percent by the end of the year. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1903 GMT 1 Jun 94) The BCN has announced that in the first five months of 1994, the overall rate of inflation was at 3.38 percent. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2156 GMT 6 Jun 94) The BCN Board of Directors decided on 3 June to lower the discount rate from 11.75 percent to 11.10 percent, BCN President Taboada told BARRICADA. He added that this reduction will lower by 1 percent those loans used to finance most of the nation's production activities. (Managua BARRICADA in Spanish 3 Jun 94 pp 1, 5) BCN President Jose Evenor Taboada has announced that coins for five, 10, 25, and 50 centavos will begin to circulate in the country on Friday, 3 June. He noted that the coins, which are worth a total of $10 million, were minted in Mexico. (Managua Radio Nicaragua Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 3 Jun 94) Over the past four years, the World Food Program has donated $10 million to domestic food programs, such as the School Food Program, the Autonomous Atlantic Region Socioeconomic Rehabilitation Program, and the Milk Development Program. (Managua EL NUEVO DIARIO 4 Jun 94 p 6)
