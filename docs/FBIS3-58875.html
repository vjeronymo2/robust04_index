Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] The contents of a letter President Rauf Denktas sent to UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali informing him that the Turkish Cypriot side is ready to negotiate on the confidence-building measures have been revealed. The letter was given to the press yesterday but was embargoed until 1200 today. In the letter, Denktas said that the Turkish Cypriot side is ready to cooperate with the UN secretary general in reaching an agreement on the confidence-building measures, which it accepts in principle. Denktas pointed out that in order to achieve this aim, he is ready to start negotiations with Greek Cypriot leader Kliridhis. We think it will be beneficial to hold the negotiations in Lefkosa [Nicosia], Denktas said. The president told the UN secretary general that the government program which was approved by the Turkish Cypriot Republican Assembly anticipated that he, Denktas, be the negotiator, and that he has accepted this task. We are pleased, Denktas says, that the UN secretary general shares the understanding of the Turkish Cypriot side regarding the confidence-building measures. We have observed, he adds, that the various understandings and concepts which we meticulously emphasize are also included in the reports the secretary general submits to the Security Council. President Denktas stressed that the measures to be implemented should not eliminate the limited means of the Turkish Cypriot side, that the arrangements to be made in connection with Maras [Varosha] and the Lefkosa [Nicosia] International Airport should help lift the embargoes against the Turkish Cypriots, and that sanctions should be imposed on the side that undermines the interests created by the implementation of the measures. Denktas said in his letter that the fenced area of Maras which are to be given over to UN rule should be areas of contact and cooperation fitting the two communities' common interests. If the Turkish Cypriot side is prevented from benefiting from Lefkosa International Airport, Denktas said, then this region should be returned to Turkish Cypriot rule. The president pointed out that one of the basic principles on which both sides agreed during the negotiating process was that a comprehensive solution should be sought by discussing the issue with all its aspects as a whole. It is obvious, Denktas said, that the approach to implement measures that will build confidence between the two sides will not change this basic principle. He added that the United Nations also admits that the confidence-building measures aim to reach a comprehensive solution easier. Denktas said: I would like to stress that we have noted the guarantees in your reports saying that partial agreements will not be posited in the future, during our search for a comprehensive solution. Denktas recalled that as cited in the 13th paragraph of the secretary general's report of 1 July 1993, the aim of the confidence-building measures is not to grant political advantages to the sides and that within that framework, the two sides will not be forced to make political concessions. Also, Denktas said, the two sides will not be forced to change their positions regarding each other's political status. The two sides reserve their positions regarding a comprehensive solution. Denktas went on to say: On the other hand, I would like to stress that in our assessment of these measures, we took into consideration that one of the results of implementing the measures will be the elimination of the obstacles before the Turkish Cypriots in the commercial and economic fields, and in foreign travel. Denktas stressed that he attaches great importance to the reassurances the UN secretary general made that the advantages to the two sides will be important, equal, fitting, and guaranteed in practical terms. Denktas said: The Turkish Cypriot side's security considerations within the framework of the solution of the Cyprus problem is a vital issue that the United Nations takes into consideration as well. Whether in connection with measures that aim to increase mutual confidence, or within the framework of the search for a comprehensive solution, security is a priority issue for the Turkish Cypriot side. This security factor is as valid in connection with the confidence-building measures on which we now work. The importance we attach to this issue, particularly in connection with the Maras issue, was conveyed to the UN secretariat general in a list of questions we asked on 23 October 1993. Considering the history of the Cyprus problem, I am convinced that you will attach the necessary importance to this factor which is of vital importance to the Turkish Cypriots. Denktas concluded his letter by saying that he would be pleased to confer with the secretary general by accepting the invitation he will extend to him at an appropriate time.
