Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 29 (CTK) -- The system of 81 one-mandate constituencies for Senate elections on which the Civic Democratic Party (ODS) insists could bring regional deviations during the elections and destabilize the political spectrum, the Christian Democratic Party (KDS) says in a statement given to CTK today. The statement has come in reaction to today's meeting of the leadership of the ODS, the strongest party in the government coalition, which confirmed ODS's intention to push for Senate elections in 81 one-mandate constituencies, although other coalition parties, including the KDS, prefer a system of 27 three-mandate constituencies. The KDS is disappointed that the ODS is not willing to discuss other variants for Senate elections, the statement says. It adds, however, that regardless its reservations towards the ODS's stand the "KDS respects the Czech Constitution" and considers it more important that the Constitution be observed than that its own model of Senate elections be implemented. The Senate, an upper house of the Czech Parliament, is defined in the Constitution but has not yet been filled.
