920521 FT 21 MAY 92 / UK Company News: Willis Corroon hit by lower interest rates Lower interest rates and continued flatness in US insurance rates were the main factors pegging back profit growth at Willis Corroon, the insurance broker, where pre-tax profits dipped by 4 per cent to Pounds 42.1m in the first quarter. Brokerage and fee income rose to Pounds 165.6m (Pounds 146.5m) in the three months to March 31, with an underlying growth of 3 per cent when the effects of acquisitions and currency movements are stripped out), contributing to higher turnover of Pounds 175.1m (Pounds 163.7m). However interest and investment income fell to Pounds 13.1m (Pounds 18.4m) and expenses rose to Pounds 142m (Pounds 126.5m). Underwriting claims fell to Pounds 6.3m (Pounds 12.6m), leaving operating profit at Pounds 39.9m (Pounds 43m). The share of associates' profits rose to Pounds 3.4m (Pounds 2.5m) and interest payable fell to Pounds 1.2m (Pounds 1.5m). Mr Roger Elliott, executive chairman, said: 'Conditions for our UK operations have improved . . . (but) there is no evidence of such an improvement emerging in the USA.'
