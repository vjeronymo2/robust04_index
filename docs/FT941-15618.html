940114 FT 14 JAN 94 / World Trade News: Malaysian deal Asea Brown Boveri, the Swiss-Swedish engineering group, has won a Dollars 430m (Pounds 286m) contract to build a turnkey 720MW combined-cycle power plant at Kuala Langat near Kuala Lumpur, writes Andrew Baxter. The contract was placed by Genting Sanyen Power, a Malaysian independent power producer, and underlines the increasing importance of IPPs in the rapidly growing Asian power market. ABB has also signed an operation and maintenance contract with Genting.
