BFN [Text] Cairo, 2 Jun (INA) -- [Iraqi] Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf met here today with Lal Bhatia, the Indian minister of state for external affairs. The foreign minister briefed his Indian counterpart on the positive stands that prevailed at the recent UN Security Council discussions concerning the lifting of the embargo clamped on Iraq, and on the majority that called for taking measures to lift the embargo as long as Iraq has implemented Resolution 687. The talks dealt with relations between Iraq and India, means of promoting them, and coordination between them. The two ministers stressed the importance of reactivating the Nonaligned Movement's role in international relations, of maintaining the spirit and essence of the movement's principles, and of reinforcing economic relations among its member states. Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf also met with Cypriot Foreign Minister Alexandros Mikhailidhis. The foreign minister explained the positive stands at the recent UN Security Council discussions, which have clearly shown that Iraq has implemented all the UN Security Council resolutions, and that what remains is a resolution to lift the embargo imposed on Iraq without delay. An INA correspondent in Cairo reported that the foreign minister explained to the Indian and Cypriot ministers that the Iraqi people, under the leadership of President Saddam Husayn, are determined to foil the embargo and the chapters of aggression. He also briefed them on the Iraqi people's efforts to reconstruct what was destroyed by the aggressors. Dr. Nabil Najm, Iraq's permanent representative to the Arab League, attended the two meetings.
