910415 FT 15 APR 91 / Construction Contracts: Contracts worth Pounds 3.7m won by Don Reynolds DON REYNOLDS, the curtain walling subsidiary of Henry Barrett Group, has been awarded contracts worth about Pounds 3.7m. The four contracts, two in Scotland, one in Northern Ireland and one in England, will use Series 200, 400 and 500 of the curtain waIling systems. A four-storey extension plus refurbishment to the Amerada Hess building in Aberdeen will be carried out by Don Reynolds. It will be the Hess Corporation's new European headquarters. The project is worth about Pounds 1.2m and the main contractor is Miller Construction, also from Edinburgh. The curtain wallers will build a four-storey office block for Scottish Nuclear Fuels' headquarters in East Kilbride. The main contractor is the GA Group and the project value is about Pounds 1.1m. After the IRA firebombing on January 5 of Sprucefields Shopping Centre in Lisburn, Northern Ireland, Don Reynolds will rebuild the single-storey units. The main contractors are McLaughlin and Harvey, Belfast, and the contract value is about Pounds 950,000. Glenlivet Properties has awarded a contract for a three-storey office development at Carbrook Hall, Sheffield. The main contractors are Bowmer and Kirkland and the contract is worth about Pounds 450,000.
