920807 FT 07 AUG 92 / International Company News: Sales improve at top US stores MANY leading US retailing groups yesterday reported improved sales for July after a generally disappointing June. The Gap, a specialty clothing chain, posted a 7 per cent gain in comparable store sales, while overall sales in the four weeks to August 1 climbed 18 per cent to Dollars 181m. Rival specialty retailer The Limited, however, saw same-store sales fall 6 per cent in the month, and a more modest 4 per cent improvement in total sales to Dollars 415m. Wal-Mart, the fast-growing discount chain and the biggest US retailer by sales, reported a 13 per cent improvement for July. Total sales advanced 28 per cent to Dollars 4.32bn. K-mart, another big discount retailer, posted its best comparable store sales gain this year. They rose 7.5 per cent, while total sales were up 13.3 per cent to Dollars 2.69bn. Sears, the Chicago-based stores and catalogue group, said comparable US store sales slipped 2.2 per cent in July. JC Penney, a department store and catalogue group, saw same-store sales increase 9.7 per cent, while total sales rose 10.4 per cent to Dollars 870m. Among other big US retailers, Woolworth saw US comparable store sales rise 2 per cent while total domestic sales slipped 0.2 per cent to Dollars 395m. Dayton Hudson's comparable store sales were 3.2 per cent higher, while its net retail sales advanced 11.8 per cent to Dollars 1.18bn. May Department Stores had comparable store sales that were 3.9 per cent higher while total sales rose 8.9 per cent to Dollars 667.4m
