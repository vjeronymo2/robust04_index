Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Djibouti, Jan 30 (AFP)--Security was stepped up here Sunday [30 January] for the first court appearance of four opposition leaders accused of inciting an armed uprising. Police surrounded the court and held supporters of the outlawed United Front for Djibouti Opposition (FUOD) as the four party leaders were questioned by a judge. The four, Mohamed Ahmed Issa, Galal Abdourahman, Kamil Ali Mohamed, and Mahdi Ibrahim Goud, were detained in custody ahead of a decision on their trial. Legal proceedings were launched against the four after a petition calling for the armed fight in Djibouti to be stepped up was released during the second FUOD conference in Addis Ababa on January 23.
