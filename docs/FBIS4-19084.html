BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Mate Granic, Croatian foreign affairs and deputy prime minister, has arrived in Ankara on an official visit. Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin received the guest minister at Ankara's Esenboga Airport. In statement he issued at the airport, Cetin said Turkey and Croatia play a very important role in solving the conflict in former Yugoslavia. Indicating that Granic personally contributed to the agreement signed between the Croats and the Bosnians, Cetin said that the agreement is a significant step toward peace. Cetin stated that Turkey is among the countries which requested to participate in the peace forces in Bosnia-Herzegovina and added that this issue will be discussed during his visit. Cetin announced that an aviation agreement will be signed with Croatia. Granic, in turn, stressed Turkey is an important country regarding the protection of peace and stability in the region and in the Balkans. Granic noted that future work regarding the agreement signed between the Bosnians and the Croats will be assessed during this visit.
