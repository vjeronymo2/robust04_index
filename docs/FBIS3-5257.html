Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The leader of the Fretilin independence movement in the Indonesian province of East Timor has challenged Jakarta to allow a referendum on self-determination in the former Portuguese colony. Mr. (Niko Koni Santana) issued the challenge in a videotape which had been smuggled out of East Timor to the nearby Australian city of Darwin. Loraine Davies reports: [Begin Davies recording] (Koni Santana) says that if, as Jakarta asserts, the people of East Timor have already freely determined their desire for integration, why is it so afraid of a referendum. He defies Jakarta to hold the referendum under UN supervision to establish the true wish of the East Timorese. He states the resistance undertakes to accept the outcome, whatever it may be, and also to give up their arms and themselves to the Indonesian authorities if the East Timorese vote for integration. [end recording]
