930421 FT 21 APR 93 / UK Company News: Freeman shares rise on recovery to Pounds 0.87m FREEMAN GROUP, the USM-quoted building products company, achieved a measure of recovery in 1992 with pre-tax profits of Pounds 874,000 compared with Pounds 342,000. The shares rose 17p to 139p. Turnover was Pounds 61m, compared with Pounds 74m, which included Pounds 18m from discontinued activities. The contracting division was sold in February 1992. Operating profits were Pounds 1.56m, against Pounds 966,000 after accounting for losses of Pounds 1.12m on discontinued activities. Overseas, where Freeman now has a total of 32 trading locations in four European countries, France set the pace. The Spanish subsidiary, acquired in August 1992, incurred a pre-tax loss of Pounds 102,000 and a positive contribution was not expected in 1993. There was a transfer from reserves of Pounds 131,000 (Pounds 3.3m) after a smaller extraordinary charge of Pounds 310,000 (Pounds 3.06m) relating to a provision against the possible costs of liti-gation on a contract for work for Taylor Woodrow at EuroDisney. Earnings worked through at 7.8p, compared with losses of 0.6p. The directors are recommending the restoration of the final dividend with 3p to make a total of 5p (3p).
