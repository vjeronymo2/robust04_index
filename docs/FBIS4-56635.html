BFN [Excerpt] General Ramon Vega Hidalgo, commander in chief of the Chilean Air Force [FACh], has said the institution is analyzing Russian aerospace technology and will possibly introduce it into the systems it currently possesses. He pointed out that "together with the Russians, we are evaluating the possibility of introducing their technology into our national systems, and then establishing programs with them." He explained that it is a slow process. Gen. Vega reported that for many years the FACh has been working on concrete technological programs with U.S. academies and universities, and training engineers in electronics, communications, and computing, as well as creating new systems for the institution. At noon yesterday, the FACh commander in chief granted an interview to the media at the end of a ceremony celebrating the 57th anniversary of the Air War Academy. [passage omitted]
