Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, 20 Feb (XINHUA) -- Recently, the Central Literature Publishing House and the Social Sciences Literature Publishing House, respectively, published Deng Xiaoping Thought on and the Practice of Building Special Economic Zones [SEZ] [deng xiao ping te qu jian she si xiang yu shi jian -- 6772 1420 1627 3676 0575 1696 6080 1835 1927 5280 1395 6432] and A Study of Deng Xiaoping Thought on Building Special Economic Zones [deng xiao ping te qu jian she si xiang yan jiu -- 6772 1420 1627 3676 0575 1696 6080 1835 1927 4282 4496]. Deng Xiaoping Thought on and the Practice of Building Special Economic Zones is a collection of materials. The book collects Comrade Deng Xiaoping's important propositions since 1978 on opening up to the outside world and on building SEZ's; propositions on building SEZ's in important party and government documents; and economic policies and statutes on developing SEZ's promulgated by the National People's Congress, the State Council, the governments of coastal provinces and cities, and the governments of SEZ's. The book also illustrates the profound changes and tremendous achievements of Shenzhen, Zhuhai, Shantou, Xiamen, and Hainan since they were built into SEZ's. A Study of Deng Xiaoping Thought on Building Special Economic Zones is a collection of treatises. It contains the latest research findings by workers in theoretical circles, as well as those engaged in practical work, over the past two years regarding Deng Xiaoping thought on building SEZ's. Specifically, the papers compiled by leading cadres, specialists, and academics who spent many years assisting in the construction of SEZ's illustrate the profound content and theoretical significance of Deng Xiaoping thought on building SEZ's, expound the great practical effects of the thought, sum up the tremendous achievements and valuable experiences in building SEZ's over the past 14 years, and outline problems arising from the development of SEZ's during the nineties and the measures for solving these, all from the perspectives of different disciplines -- philosophy, economics, politics, and sociology -- and by integrating the past and the present, as well as theory and practice.
