BFN [Text] The Revolution Command Council has decided to grant the wife of a man who is officially declared missing, the right to separate from her husband after four years. This came in a law the Council issued amending Personal Status Law No. 188 of 1959. The following is text of the law: In the name of God, the merciful, the compassionate. Republic of Iraq, In the name of the people, Revolution Command Council Decision No. 62, dated 26 Dhu al- Hijjah 1414 Hegira corresponding to 6 June 1994: In accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 1 of Article 42 of the Constitution, the Revolution Command Council decided to issue the following law: Law No. (8) of 1994: Law Amending Personal Status Law No. 188 of 1959. Article 1: The following paragraph shall be added to Article 43 of Personal Status Law No. 188 of 1959 so that it shall become the fourth paragraph of that article: Fourth: 1. The wife of a man who is officially proven to be missing may apply to the court for a separation from her husband after the lapse of four years since he was discovered to be missing. The court shall ascertain that the husband has continued to be missing in the same way in which he was proven missing and then issue its verdict on separation. 2. Following the issuance of the separation verdict, the wife of the missing husband shall observe 'iddah [the legal period of waiting before a divorced or widowed Muslim woman can remarry under Islamic law] for four months and 10 days. 3. If the missing husband returns, the marriage shall continue unless the wife has already remarried. In this case, the second husband shall be considered as having entered into marriage without true knowledge that the first husband is still living. Article 2: This law shall be implemented as of the date of its issuance in the OFFICIAL GAZETTE. [Signed] Saddam Husayn, chairman of the Revolution Command Council.
