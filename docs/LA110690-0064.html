November 6, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

PRO FOOTBALL REPORT;

WEEKDAY UPDATE;

RAMS;

SUTTON NEEDS SURGERY, OUT A MONTH

Ram cornerback Mickey Sutton, scheduled to undergo arthroscopic surgery on his left knee today, is expected to be sidelined for at least a month.

Complicating the picture in the secondary, the status of injured cornerback Jerry Gray remains a question.

"We're not looking for any miracles right now," Coach John Robinson said of Gray, who did not play Sunday because of a sore left knee. "You keep hoping that all of a sudden the Pro Bowl cornerback would come back. But that just hasn't happened. Until he can come back in good fashion, we just can't press him. We will just have to take that day by day."

The Rams hope to shore up their secondary losses with Darryl Henley, who on Sunday made a successful comeback from a hip injury. Also, Clifford Hicks, who has been the physically unable to perform list all season with a knee injury, might be available.

Robinson continued to be vague about when he plans to activate running back Marcus Dupree from the injured-reserve list. "At some point," Robinson said. With Sutton out, there will be an open roster spot for Dupree this week, if Robinson chooses to use it that way. TIM KAWAKAMI
