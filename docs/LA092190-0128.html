September 21, 1990, Friday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: WESTMINSTER SCHOOL DISTRICT

Here are the candidates running for three seats on the Westminster School District Board of Trustees in the Nov. 6 election.

Cindi Arthur

Age: 35

Occupation: Incumbent/ community volunteer

Background: Has served as a member of the Westminster School District Board of Trustees for the past five years.

Now serves as president but has held positions as clerk and vice president.

Issues: Wants to address school maintenance, child care, increasing enrollment and the needs of bilingual students.

Joseph G. Davis

Age: 38

Occupation: Attorney

Background: Served as chairman of the Community Services Commission, president of the Outdoor Science Foundation and a member of the District Advisory Council.

Issues: Wants to achieve the fundamental goals of education while understanding and appreciating the ethnic and cultural differences of the community.

Sheryl Neugebauer

Age: 38

Occupation: Flight attendant/ parent

Background: Served on DARE, disaster preparedness, school site and district advisory committees, PTA and participated in youth sports programs.

Issues: Believes in a strong anti-drug and anti-gang program, open enrollment and more funds for building maintenance.

Margie L. Rice

Age: 62

Occupation: Incumbent

Background: School district trustee for 13 years.

Issues: Believes in the neighborhood school concept and opening more schools to handle enrollment increases.

Ron Morgan

Age: 38

Occupation: Incumbent/key account executive, Pacific Bell Directories

Background: Appointed to the board in 1988 to fill a vacancy. Has served as a member and representative of political action to Orange County school boards. Previously served on the district's budget and open enrollment committees. Served 10 years as a Boy Scout adviser and volunteer.

Issues: Believes in anti-gang and drug programs.
