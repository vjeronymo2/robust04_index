930114 FT 14 JAN 93 / International Capital Markets: Warburg unit to deal in China POTTER Warburg Securities, the Australian stockbroking firm, has been awarded a licence to deal on the Shenzhen stock exchange, the larger of China's two fledgling stock markets. Potter Warburg, a subsidiary of S. G. Warburg of the UK, is the first Australian broker to be awarded a licence since foreign firms were invited to apply in September by the People's Bank of China. The licence will allow the firm to underwrite and distribute Chinese issues to foreign investors. Foreign brokers licensed to operate at Shenzhen include Credit Lyonnais, Yamaichi International and Swiss Bank Finance Asia.
