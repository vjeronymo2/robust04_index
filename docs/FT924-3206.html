921209 FT 09 DEC 92 / Arts: Today's Television The growing use of guns in British crime is widely acknowledged, but were you aware of 'an alarming increase in drugs-related gun violence among Britain's African-Caribbean community' ? It is the sort of matter which is nervously skirted by politically correct current affairs people for fear of being seen as racist. Good old Open Space, however, BBC2's access slot, has an account by Sheldon 'Curfew' Thomas, member of a group of rap and reggae artists, who calls for action to curb this black-on-black violence (7.40). Today's Bookmark sounds like a collector's item. Poets read their work on the subject of love and reflect upon the phenomenon. It seems that Hugo Williams' wife finds his poems 'ludicrous ..but she's French', and Wendy Cope does not think she will ever stop falling in love, often with other poets (8.10 BBC2). Dispatches is very excited about the degree of access it achieved in making today's programme about Britain's presidency of the EC, seen partly from the inside of the Foreign Office and even from within a cabinet committee. It promises to be a timely report given that it is now only two days before the Edinburgh summit (9.00 C4).
