Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Qingdao, March 17 (XINHUA) -- The transportation sector, which was once controlled completely by the government, is now opening to domestic and overseas investors in east China's Shandong Province. Some 49 percent of the share ownership of the 3.1-billion-yuan Jinan-Qingdao Expressway -- one of the top-class expressways in China -- will be sold to investors from both at home and abroad, sources at the ongoing `94 Qingdao Trade Fair said today. Meanwhile, a host of transportation development projects, including construction of an expressway, four highways and a river navigation lane, as well as a harbor, costing some 15 billion yuan, are also on offer for investment at the fair. Officials of the provincial communications department noted that the winning bidders will enjoy a series of preferential policies granted by the government. For instance, they will be given priority in developing real estate and other related sectors. Zhu Zhenchang, head of the department, told a press conference that besides that batch of projects, a succession of other transportation projects will be opened to outside investors in the future.
