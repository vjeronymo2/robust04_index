November 5, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

MOVIES TO TAPE

Babette's Feast (Bravo Sunday at 5 p.m.): Subtle, amusing Isak Dinesen tale starring Stephane Audran as a selfless servant who offers an astonishing gift of gratitude to her employers, a pair of elderly sisters. (1:40)

Modern Romance (Channel 13 Tuesday at noon): Albert Brooks' 1981 romantic comedy, in which he casts himself as a Hollywood film editor so totally enamored of an attractive bank official (Kathryn Harrold) that he makes their lives miserable with his possessiveness and jealousy. A fresh and acute vision of life and of self from a distinctive talent. (2:00)

Footlight Parade (Channel 5 Thursday at 3:15 a.m.): Zesty classic Warners musical starring James Cagney and featuring many Busby Berkeley numbers. (2:15)

Studs Lonigan (A&E Friday at 1 a.m.): Ambitious 1960 attempt to film the James T. Farrell novel of the 1920s, directed by Irving Lerner and adapted and produced by Philip Yordan. Christopher Knight stars; Jack Nicholson has a small role. (2:00)

Monterey Pop (Channel 28 Saturday at 12:30 a.m.): The first important rock concert film is still one of the best, a record of the historic 1967 gathering featuring Jimi Hendrix, Janis Joplin and many others. (1:30)
