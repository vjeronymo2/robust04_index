910420 FT 20 APR 91 / Finance & The Family: Investors need a PEP talk A Gallup survey conducted for Gartmore, the fund management group, last week showed that ignorance about personal finance is widespread, despite recent proliferation of products. It found strong support for more education on personal finance, preferably in schools or higher education colleges. More than half of the 507 people polled admitted to having a poor understanding of bonds, investment and unit trusts, and Personal Equity Plans, although Tessas appear to have caught on quickly with the public. Only 34 per cent of respondents had a poor understanding of Tessas, and only 11 per cent were not aware of the scheme, which is only four months old.
