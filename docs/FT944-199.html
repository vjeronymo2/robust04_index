941230 FT 30 DEC 94 / International Company News: Telefonos de Mexico sets record NYSE trade One of Mexico's biggest companies this week became the first in the history of the New York Stock Exchange to trade 1bn shares in a year, writes Lisa Bransten in New York. In consolidated trading, 1.02bn shares of Telefonos de Mexico (Telmex), the telephone monopoly, changed hands as of December 27. Throughout 1994 Telmex, which is generally considered Mexico's benchmark security, was consistently among the most actively traded shares on the NYSE, and Mexico's latest financial crisis created enough volatility to send volumes into record territory. Mr Gregg Schoenleber, a market analyst at the equity research firm Birinyi Associates, attributed the high volume in the shares to volatility in Mexico and the popularity of the company among holders of Mexican equities. 'If you own (stock in) Mexico, you own Telmex,' he said.
