May 10, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

TOWER POWER: Building a tunnel beneath the Strait of Gibraltar (D5) is not Charles E. Kaempen's first dream. . . . In 1975, he suggested building a hollow plastic tower, 6,500 feet high and 325 feet wide. The idea: to disperse urban pollution in the upper atmosphere while producing water and generating electricity. Some experts said the concept was worth exploring, but it never got off the drawing board. Kristina Lindgren and David Reyes
