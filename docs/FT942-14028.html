940422 FT 22 APR 94 / India may sell train inspection Indian railways, one of the largest rail networks in the world, is considering handing over its massive ticket control system to private agencies, according to Mr MK Rao, the Railway Board chairman, Reuter reports from New Delhi. He said the move would help cut losses incurred as a result of people travelling without tickets. Private agencies would be expected to be more diligent in examining tickets, he said. Officials were 'seriously thinking of handing over ticket examination work to private agencies to increase income'.
