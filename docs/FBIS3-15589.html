Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The president of Romania and the delegation accompanying him have arrived at the president's office and the Seimas. Talks between Algirdas Brazauskas, president of Lithuania, and Ion Iliescu, president of Romania, began at 1400. The closed meeting of the two presidents lasted half an hour. Following this a meeting of the state delegations headed by the heads of the states was held. [passage omitted] In a short impromptu interview given to Lithuanian and Romanian journalists, Algirdas Brazauskas said that issues related to the foreign and internal policies of both countries were discussed by the two presidents. He stressed that their two countries have many similarities -- both Lithuania and Romania are currently solving problems of economic reform and privatization. Algirdas Brazauskas also mentioned that there were very many similar trends in the foreign policy of both states. For example both states give priority to good relations with neighboring states. Prospects for integration by both the states into the European Union, and the implementation of the NATO Partnership for Peace program were discussed by the presidents. Nerijus Maliukevicius, the president's spokesman for the press, told ELTA that Algirdas Brazauskas acquainted the Romanian president and the delegation with the social and economic situation in Lithuania. [passage omitted] Ion Ilescu stressed during the talks that Lithuania and Romania are united by the wish to arrange relations with their neighbors in such a way that these will not become hotbeds of tension either now or in future.
