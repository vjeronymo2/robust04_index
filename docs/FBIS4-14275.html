BFN [Text] Here are the details of the agreement achieved by Israel and the PLO. Israel has consented to the Palestinian administration's issuing its own laissez-passers [travel document] featuring the term passport. The Palestinians will be able to print stamps under the heading -- Palestinian Authority. The autonomy will have its own international telephone area code. Israel has agreed to a temporary international presence in Gaza and Jericho composed of representatives of countries donating to the autonomy. Possibilities of expanding the Palestinian powers on the Gaza coast and the al-Mawasi strip abutting on the Qatif Bloc will be explored. No agreement has been achieved on the Palestinian territorial waters. Israel refused to permit the appointment of a Palestinian overseer at the Rafah terminal. Our correspondent adds that 'Arafat has pledged to propose annulling the Palestinian Covenant at the next Palestine National Council session. 'Arafat has further pledged to enter the territories as PLO chairman and not to claim any other title. He has not reiterated his demand to pray in Jerusalem. 'Arafat has again promised to make an effort to forward to Israel additional information about its POW's and MIA's. Tonight the parties will probably conclude the issues of prisoner release and pardon for fugitives. The committee planning the 4 May ceremony has already launched preparations. [A related report on Jerusalem New Channel 2 Television Network in Hebrew at 1700 GMT on 28 April adds that "Israeli withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho will begin on 5 May, the day after the agreement is signed in Cairo."]
