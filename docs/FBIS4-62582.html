BFN [By BUSINESS-TASS correspondent Robert Serebrennikov] [Text] Moscow May 17 TASS -- According to Vladimir Shumeyko, chairman of the Federation Council (Russian parliament's upper house), "most serious" sentiments prevail inside South-African business community, which favour further development of trade and economic relations with Russia. He came to the conclusion after a meeting with leading South-African businessmen in Cape Town. The two parties exchanged views on prospects for building up mutually beneficial bilateral relations. Shumeyko led a Russian delegation invited to attend the inauguration of the new South-African President Nelson Mandela in Pretoria. South-African businessmen displayed interest in broadening trade and economic relations with Russia, Shumeyko said in an interview with ITAR-TASS. There is a lot of fields for developing bilateral cooperation. Let us take, for instance, mining industry: Both Russia and South Africa extract gold, diamonds and rare-earth metals. South Africa has a well-developed processing and packing industries. Bilateral cooperation in military field has good prospects, too, Shumeyko emphasized.
