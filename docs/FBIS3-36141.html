Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Our correspondent reports that the presidents of Russia and Georgia have signed a treaty on friendship, goodneighborliness and cooperation between the two states. The treaty comprises 35 articles in which the contracting parties pledge to cooperate in the political, military and economic spheres. Experts are focusing most attention on those articles which acknowledge the inalienable rights of Russia and Georgia to adopt the necessary measures to protect their state borders. The sides will independently confront the questions of ensuring national security and defense construction, and in this regard will conduct close cooperation and practical interaction. The sides will adopt measures to ensure the effective implementation of the agreement on the temporary sojourn of Russian troop formations on the territory of the Georgian Republic. According to Article four, Russia and Georgia pledge not to take part in any alliances or blocs aimed against each other, nor will they render assistance to third states in the event of an outbreak of armed conflicts between these states and another party. The current treaty covers a period of 10 years.
