BFN [Text] Sarajevo, April 10 (AFP) -- Bosnian Serb forces took control Sunday of the riverbank where the Moslem town of Gorazde is located, and Serb tanks rolled into the southern part of town, the United Nations said. UN spokesman Dacre Halloway said the Serb forces took over the bank after crossing the Drina river, which had been the frontline between the two warring parties. He said there was sniper fire outside the office of the UN High Commissioner for Refugees. Serb forces have been attacking the Gorazde enclave for the past two weeks.
