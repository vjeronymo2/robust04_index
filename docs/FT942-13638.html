940423 FT 23 APR 94 / Finance and the Family: Your CGT allowances The table shows CGT indexation allowances for March. Multiply the original cost of the asset by the figure for the month you bought it. Subtract the result from the proceeds of your sale; the balance is your taxable gain or loss. Say you bought shares for Pounds 6,000 in September 1985 and sold them in March 1994 for Pounds 13,000. Multiplying the original cost by the September 1985 figure of 1.493 gives a total of Pounds 8,958. Subtracting that from the Pounds 13,000 gives a capital gain of Pounds 4,042, which is within the 1993-94 CGT allowanace of Pounds 5,800. If selling shares bought before April 6 1982, use the March 1982 figures.
