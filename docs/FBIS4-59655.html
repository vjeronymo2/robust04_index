BFN [Text] Cairo, 16 May (MENA) -- Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi has ordered the release of a large number of detainees who, according to the State Security Intelligence services, have renounced extremism, departure from the right religion, and terrorist practices. The Interior Ministry issued a statement saying these releases demonstrate that various police agencies are fully aware of movements of terrorist groups at home and abroad. It also proves that these agencies firmly adhere to law and legitimacy, honestly shoulder their national responsibility, and highly appreciate the public's support for them in their decisive confrontation with bloody terrorism and its elements, which target the nation and its interests. An official security source at the Interior Ministry stressed that the release reflects a firm and credible policy by the Ministry regarding the emergency law; namely, that it should not be applied except against the elements that pose a threat to society. The source said that these release orders will be implemented within hours, and will be followed by releases of other detainees whose files and positions on terrorism and extremism are now being reviewed by the State Security Intelligence agencies.
