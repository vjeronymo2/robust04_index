May 19, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

GERRYMANDERING IN CALIFORNIA AND MERITS OF PROPS. 118, 119

Your editorial opposing Proposition 118 was right on target in pointing out that the measure really changes nothing in the ways incumbent politicians draw lines to ensure their own political survival ("Prop. 118: Chaos in Sacramento," May 12).

Rather than being a reform, Proposition 118 is really the "incumbent protection plan."

Under the two-thirds scheme, Democrat and Republican leaders would assure the preservation of incumbents and go further to reward and punish party members to maintain power for the leadership.

Remember also, that the current districts were approved by a two-thirds vote of the Legislature and could have been vetoed by the governor -- so what's new?

It is time to put the public back in charge. Only the nonpartisan, independent commission approach in Proposition 119 puts citizens in a position to establish district lines for the politicians.

Proposition 119 is the best chance for fair redistricting for the 1990s. With the League of Women Voter's support, the voters may actually get the chance to pick their politicians rather than politicians picking their voters.

ROY ASHBURN

Kern County Supervisor

Bakersfield
