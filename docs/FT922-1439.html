920623 FT 23 JUN 92 / Two held in Bombay scandal Mr Harshad Mehta, the stockbroker at the centre of India's worst securities scandal, and Mr R Sitaman, an officer of the government-owned State Bank of India, have been remanded in custody until July 3, writes R C Murthy in Bombay. But Mr Justice S N Variava, the special judge appointed to speed up the trial, yesterday ordered the release on bail nine others, including two State Bank officers and two brothers of Mr Mehta. The Reserve Bank of India, the central bank, has meanwhile issued new guidelines for interbank transactions of securities, banning what are called 'ready forward' deals.
