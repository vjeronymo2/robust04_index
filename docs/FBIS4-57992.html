BFN [Text] The Barbados Labor Party says it does not intend to take the proposed 8 percent pay cut issue lightly. Opposition leader (Owen Arthur) said the laws that allow government to interfere with the salaries of public workers as it sees fit are bad and offensive. According to the opposition, these laws should be removed from the statute books. Mr. (Arthur) says he is getting legal advice on the matter, which he intends to put before parliament. [Begin (Arthur) recording] Clause 26, which was (read) as a basis upon which the government has acted in this instance, was recommended as one of those aspects of the Constitution that should have been changed. I deeply regret the (?Balisur) Party government did not change it then. I deeply regret this was not tested for fear... [pauses] only because we fear that because the government then did not have a two-thirds majority, or because we did not have... [pauses] [words indistinct] it was not changed, but it is such an offensive law that it ought to be off the statute books of this country.
