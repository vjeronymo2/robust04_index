BFN [Text] Jeddah, June 27, SPA -- The delegation of the Organization of the Islamic Conference (OIC) [ICO] started intensive talks with the representatives of the Afghan leaders in Islamabad on the on-going crisis in the country and the escalated situation and the difficult position being faced by the government which was formed on bases of the Afghan consensus following the Islamabad and Makkah accords and subsequent agreements. A statement issued by the OIC said the delegation has started talks with the Afghan political leaders and representatives of the Government of Pakistan including Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, the foreign minister. The statement said the secretary general of the OIC is planning to visit the region early next month to meet with the leaders of various factions and try to find an agreement for ceasefire and refreshment of Afghan political, economic and social structures. The secretary general sent separate messages to various factions, calling on them to back the efforts being made by the OIC to establish peace and stability in Afghanistan.
