Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Havana, 20 Feb (PL) -- Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina has announced that as of next Saturday he will tour six Asian countries, and beginning on 20 March he will spend seven days in Spain. Robaina also announced that during the first half of April he will tour several Latin American nations, thus responding to invitations from several regional foreign ministries. During an impromptu news conference following a three-day tour of Central Cuba with members of the diplomatic corps, Robaina said last night that the first nations he will visit on the Asian continent are Vietnam, Laos, and Myanmar. He added that at present certain aspects of his visits to India, Malaysia, and Indonesia are being arranged. This tour of Asia is very important to us because of what these countries represent and because several of their foreign ministers have paid official visits to Cuba, Robaina said. Robaina also pointed out that acting on an invitation from the Spanish Foreign Ministry, he will make a week-long trip to that country. Regarding his Latin American tour, Robaina refused to disclose any details, saying only that he has received invitations from several foreign ministries. He mentioned the first half of April as a tentative date for this tour.
