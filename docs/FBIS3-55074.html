Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev -- A UNIAN correspondent was informed at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Ukraine on 4 January that Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko, has sent a personal message to Qian Qichen, vice premier of the State Council and minister of foreign affairs of the People's Republic of China, in connection with the second anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic links between Ukraine and China. In particular the letter notes that "China became one of the first states in the world to recognize Ukraine, establish diplomatic relations with it, and open its embassy in Kiev. China's attitude to the development of relations with Ukraine is highly valued in our young country. The visits to China by Leonid Kravchuk, president of Ukraine, and Ivan Plyushch, chairman of the Supreme Council, in this short period of time have demonstrated this." It was reported at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs that the legal and contractual basis of inter-state relations between both states has been elaborated much better than that with many other states which had stronger links with Ukraine prior to the break-up of the USSR. Twelve documents were signed during the visit to China by Leonid Kravchuk in 1992 alone, some of which have already been ratified by both parliaments. According to information of the Ministry of Foreign Economic Relations of Ukraine, according to the results for 1993, China is the leader in the East Asian region in volume of exports from Ukraine. The People's Republic of China purchases road technology, electrical equipment, timber, cement, metal, pipes, fittings, and other goods from us. The structure of products imported from China is distributed almost evenly between products of light and textile industries, products of the conversion of the Chinese military-industrial complex, and agricultural produce. A reception on the occasion of the second anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two states took place at the embassy of the People's Republic of China in Ukraine on 4 January. Yuriy Rylach, deputy minister of foreign affairs of Ukraine, who was present at the reception, expressed the hope for further fruitful development of interstate relations. Ivan Drach, chairman of the "Ukraine" society; Petro Myroshnykov, chairman of the Ukrainian-Chinese friendship society; and Valentyn Lipatov, deputy minister of foreign affairs of Ukraine, were also present at the reception. Zhang Zhen, ambassador of the People's Republic of China in Ukraine, thanked the guests from ministries and departments of Ukraine, the active members of the Ukrainian-Chinese friendship society, and representatives from business circles of the capital and from the diplomatic corps for their attention and cooperation.
