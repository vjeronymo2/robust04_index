September 12, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Mosbacher Criticizes Japan: U.S. Commerce Secretary Robert A. Mosbacher slammed Japan for closing its markets to American goods and services but praised South Korea for opening its markets wider. Mosbacher told South Korean business leaders that Washington applauded Seoul's spirit of cooperation in response to trade pressure, imposed in particular through the 1988 U.S. Trade Act. But he was critical of Japan's efforts and promised to voice his criticism when he visits Tokyo today after his visit to Seoul. "We opened our markets. And we worked to create a trading system that would allow for everyone to prosper. Now it's Japan's turn to show the same kind of commitment to the world trading system," Mosbacher said.
