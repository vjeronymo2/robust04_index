April 4, 1989, Tuesday, Southland Edition

THE NATION;

CIVILIAN SECRETARIES FOR MILITARY CHOSEN

The Bush Administration has chosen its three civilian service secretaries -- Assistant Secretary of Defense Richard L. Armitage for the Army, Undersecretary H. Lawrence Garrett III for the Navy and Donald B. Rice of the Rand Corp. for the Air Force -- defense officials said. Armitage and Garrett are veteran government bureaucrats while Rice is a former government official who has stayed in contact with the bureaucracy as a defense specialist at RAND, the Washington Post reported. The three must still be formally nominated, then confirmed by the Senate, before assuming their duties.
