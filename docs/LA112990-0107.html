November 29, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

KASPAROV, KARPOV PLAY TO ANOTHER DRAW

Gary Kasparov and Anatoly Karpov drew for the eighth straight game Wednesday in the World Chess Championship.

The two Soviet rivals are now tied at 7 1/2 points after 15 games.

Wednesday's 15th game began similarly to the 13th game on Saturday, with Karpov and Kasparov playing a Grunfeld opening with an exchange variation. The position was the same after 10 moves as the two previous games, but Karpov varied his game when he went to a bishop move, not found in any previous game using the Grunfeld opening.

At the 33rd move, Kasparov proposed the draw, which Karpov accepted.

The next game is Saturday.
