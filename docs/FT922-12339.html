920418 FT 18 APR 92 / International Company News: Inland Steel trims deficit to Dollars 27.4m INLAND Steel, the struggling Chicago steel maker, decreased its net loss in the first quarter of 1992 compared with last year and, after a long gloomy period, expressed cautious optimism about the future, writes Barbara Durr in Chicago. It posted a net loss in the first quarter of Dollars 27.4m, or Dollars 1.14 per share, compared with a loss of Dollars 40m, or Dollars 1.50 last year. Sales rose 8 per cent to Dollars 895.9m from Dollars 832.7m a year ago. Mr Frank Luerssen, Inland's chairman, said the improved performance 'reflects a better mix of products as well as our efforts to reduce costs'. Time Warner, the US media group, is undertaking a private placement of Dollars 1bn of long-term debt with institutional investors, AP-DJ reports. Proceeds will be used to reduce the company's commercial bank debt.
