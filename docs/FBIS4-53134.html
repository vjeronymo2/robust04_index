BFN [Text] The air transport tax exemption agreement between Thailand and Saudi Arabia will foster relations and cooperation in tourism, trade, investment and exports between the two countries, Deputy Foreign Minister Surin Phitsuwan said yesterday. The signing ceremony for the Agreement for Reciprocal Exemption of Taxes on the Activities of Air Transport Enterprises took place last Tuesday in Jeddah by Mr Surin and Saudi Deputy Minister of Finance and Economics Dr Abdulaziz al Nasrullah. "According to the Revenue and Aviation departments, the agreement would help reduce people's travelling expenses by 10 per cent and promote air transport and travel," Mr Surin said upon his return from Jeddah. The deputy foreign minister left Bangkok on May 9 for Jeddah as chief of Thai pilgrims to Mecca for the annual Haj rites. He is scheduled to leave for Saudi Arabia again on Wednesday. The agreement was the first between the governments of Thailand and Saudi Arabia in the last five years, he said.
