Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Gaishi Hiraiwa, chairman of Keidanren [Japan Federation of Economic Organizations], at a news conference in Nagoya city on 3 March, commented on the issue of setting sector-by-sector numerical targets over which Japan and the United States failed to reach agreement at the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks. Hiraiwa said this issue should be settled by the industries of the two nations, insisting: "The problem should be resolved not by governments setting numbers, but by the private sectors talking to each other." He added: "Negotiations are continuing and we will try to find a compromise through these negotiations."
