Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Montevideo -- The case of the 1-million-ton shipment of Canadian wheat that will enter Brazil from Canada at a very low price is closed. Argentina has asked Brazil for "compensation" to its growers to counterbalance the "unfair competition" from the Canadians. The Itamar Franco government has, however, already made its decision: "The state does not intervene in the grain market, which is absolutely free." In a conversation with CLARIN, Foreign Trade Secretary Renato Marques said: "As of 1991, wheat purchases are no longer handled by the state. Today private parties freely import wheat. Brazil no longer exercises foreign trade control." [CLARIN] There was the situation when Brazil granted Argentina compensation after confirming that U.S. wheat sold to Brazil was subsidized... [Marques] That was an entirely different case. The United States was exporting wheat under the Export Enhancement Program, and this involved oficial recognition that wheat was being subsidized.
