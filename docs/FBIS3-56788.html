Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Comments on the tripartite negotiations by the Ukrainian, American, and Russian presidents that took place on 18 January in Moscow were presented at a briefing held at the Ukrainian Foreign Ministry. In particular, it was noted that the tripartite declaration adopted at the negotiations is a document containing political obligations regarding moving toward nuclear disarmament. It is of crucial importance for Ukraine because since the entire rocket-launching infrastructure is stationed outside of Ukraine, nuclear weapons are not a deterring factor for our state, as well as because the rockets, whose storage expiration terms are already ending, pose a serious environmental threat to Ukraine itself. So the juggling of some Ukrainian political forces with people's patriotic feelings inflicts only damages. Responding to a question about compensating Ukraine for the value of the enriched uranium contained in warheads that are located on its territory, the briefing organizers said that it will be provided in the form of heat-emitting elements for (?Alpha). At the negotiations, Bill Clinton reiterated the U.S. pledge to allocate about $800 million for Russia, Ukraine, Kazakhstan, and Belarus to heighten safety [words indistinct] nuclear forces and storage of decomposable materials. At the initial stage, Ukraine will receive $175 million.
