Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Panamanian economic developments monitored through 15 February. Panama Stock Market Manager Roberto Brenes has disclosed to LA PRENSA that transactions on the domestic stock market amounted to approximately $203 million in 1993. Brenes explained that this figure surpassed expectations by more than $50 million. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 9 Feb 94 p 11A) In its preliminary assessment of regional economies, the Economic Commission for Latin America (ECLA) reported that during 1993, Panama maintained its trend toward growth (5.5 percent) but at a slower pace than in the previous two years. According to the report, Panama's economic expansion was attributed to active investments and, to a lesser extent, on consumption which showed a moderate increase. The construction sector registered a growth of nearly 40 percent. Farm production dropped because of a reduction in the harvest of plantains, sugar, and tobacco. The ECLA report added that unemployment dropped from 13.6 percent in 1992 to 12.4 in 1993. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 9 Feb 94 p 11A) Agricultural Development Minister Cesar Pereira Burgos has reiterated that the closing of silos to the Melo, Fidanque, and Toledano poultry firms for an indefinite period of time is due to their alleged illegal import of 50,000 quintals of corn from the United States. Last Monday, President Guillermo Endara expressed disagreement with this corn import, ordered the closing of the silos, and banned the unloading of soy beans from a ship that docked at Balboa port. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 10 Feb 94 p 2A) A Canadian-Panamanian mining consortium will invest more than $300 million in the exploitation of a "polymetallic" mine whose reserves are estimated at $20 billion. Such a project will generate more than 3,200 direct jobs during its full period of activity. The Cerro Petaquillaa project, located in Colon Province, is in the exploration process and is being carried out by Minamerica Corp. jointly with Adrian Resources Ltd. of Ontario, Canada. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1548 GMT 13 Feb 94)
