BFN [Text] Moscow June 21 ITAR-TASS -- More than one-third of Russians polled in a recent survey by the Public Opinion Fund said it is in the interests of the country to reunify Russia with other former Soviet republics in one state. But one-fourth of the respondents in the poll said they were against any reunification with any country. Asked what countries should be given preference in the unification process, three percent favored Azerbaijan, four percent Armenia, 24 percent Belarus, four percent Georgia, 13 percent Kazakhstan, three percent Kirgizia [Kyrgyzstan], four percent Latvia, three percent Lithuania, eight percent Moldavia, four percent Tajikistan, three percent Turkmenia, four percent Uzbekistan, 24 per cent Ukraine and three percent Estonia. Thirty-four percent called for reunification with all former Soviet republics. Only 13 percent of those polled were uncertain. The poll of 1200 respondents was conducted in June, and its results were published in the BUSINESS WORLD WEEKLY on Tuesday.
