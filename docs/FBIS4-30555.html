BFN [Text] On the afternoon of 26 June 1994, Foreign Affairs Minister Somsavat Lengsavat left Vientiane for a one-week official visit to the SRV. The visit, scheduled from 26 June to 3 July, is aimed at holding consultations with the Vietnamese side on issues of common interest. It is also intended to strengthen the traditional solidarity and friendship between the foreign affairs ministries of Laos and Vietnam as well as between the parties, states, and peoples of the two countries. After the one-week visit to Vietnam, Somsavat Lengsavat will proceed to the Kingdom of Cambodia for an official visit. Scheduled from 4 to 7 July, this visit is in response to an invitation of Prince Kromkhun Norodom Sirivut, deputy prime minister, minister of foreign affairs, and chairman of the Joint Cambodian-Lao Cooperation Committee. Somsavat Lengsavat will visit Cambodia on this occasion as chairman of the Joint Lao-Cambodian Cooperation Committee. It will reciprocate the visit to Laos by Prince Kromnkhun Norodom Sirivut in February 1994. During the visit, Somsavat Lengsavat will discuss with the Cambodian side bilateral cooperation in various fields. The visit is also aimed at further strengthening the long-standing solidarity and friendship between the two countries. Somsavat Lengsavat, minister of foreign affairs and chairman of the Joint Lao-Cambodian Cooperation Committee, was seen off at the airport by deputy ministers of foreign affairs. Bui Van Thanh and Khek Lerang, respectively ambassadors of Vietnam and the Kingdom of Cambodia to Laos, along with a number of high-ranking officials of the Foreign Affairs Ministry, were also on hand to see the foreign affairs minister off at the airport.
