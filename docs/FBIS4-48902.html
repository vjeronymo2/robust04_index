BFN [Text] New York, April 29 (XINHUA) -- Chen Muhua, vice-chairman of China's National People's Congress, arrived here today to receive a reward by the United Nation's Children's Fund (UNICEF). UNICEF will present its Maurice Pate Award, which includes a 25,000 U.S. dollars stipend, on May 2, to the All-China Women's Federation, whose president now is Chen Muhua. The All-China Women's Federation is selected for its exemplary leadership in promoting the well-being of children and women on a nationwide scale. The award, named in honour of the first UNICEF executive director, is given annually for extraodinary and exemplary leadership in, and contribution to, advancement of the survival, protection and development of children. It is the first time the reward has been given to China.
