BFN [Text] Seoul, May 3 (YONHAP) -- The South Korean Government welcomed the conclusion of free elections in South Africa [RSA] Tuesday and looked forward to stronger bilateral relations after the end of apartheid. South Korea "commends the firm determination and efforts of South African leaders, both black and white, who have wisely overcome the long disharmony between the races and have made this election a reality," said Foreign Ministry spokesman Chang Ki-ho. "Furthermore, the Government of the Republic of Korea is of the view that the present elections provide historic momentum for bringing about an end to the 342-year legacy of minority white rule over the majority black population, and the regime of apartheid," he said. South Korea "looks forward to further strengthening the already existing friendly relations between our two countries through the momentum created by the free elections in South Africa," Chang said. Seoul and Pretoria established diplomatic ties in December 1992.
