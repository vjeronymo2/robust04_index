BFN [Text] Croatia is satisfied with the development of relations with the Russian Federation and wants to have friendly relations in all areas, President of the Republic of Croatia Franjo Tudjman said in an interview with reporters of leading Russian media in Zagreb today. Answering questions by reporters from Russian state television, ITAR-TASS, and the newspaper MOSKOVSKIYE NOVOSTI, President Tudjman stressed that relations which existed before the formation of the sovereign, independent, and democratic Croatia only continued following the establishment of diplomatic ties between the two countries. He said that Russia had so far played a positive role in the search for a political solution to the crisis in the region. He particularly stressed the role of Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and the Russian president's special envoy, Vitaliy Churkin, at the Geneva conference. They tried to exert influence on Serbia and the Serbs in Croatia to honor Croatia's internationally recognized borders. President Tudjman also spoke in favorable terms about the economic relations between the two countries and said that great successes had been made in that sphere.
