September 1, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

FEINSTEIN, WILSON CAMPAIGNS FOR GOVERNOR

Why don't our two gubernatorial candidates realize that the one who will get the winning vote will be the one who expresses concern for California and outlines plans and goals to make our state a better place.

For far too long now, both parties have been concentrating their efforts on digging up skeletons from their opponent's closet, rather than preparing a campaign that will educate the California public on their priorities for the state.

I think I can speak for the majority of Californians when I say that I am not interested in what candidates do with their personal lives and finances, as opposed to what they plan to do with mine.

I urge our two well-qualified candidates to stop running a race on what their opponent has done wrong, and to start running the race on what they've done right.

ANGELO TURNER

San Diego
