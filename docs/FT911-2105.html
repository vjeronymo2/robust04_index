910501 FT 01 MAY 91 / UK Company News: Amstrad's UK chief dismissed AMSTRAD, the electronics company, has responded to difficult trading conditions by dismissing the managing director of its UK operation. Mr Barry Young was one of 37 employees to be made redundant. The redundancies include 31 staff at Amstrad's headquarters in Brentwood, Essex. The remainder are warehouse and clerical employees at other sites. Mr Alan Sugar, chairman, said yesterday that he and the company's board would take direct control of UK operations. Amstrad's UK sales, which last year accounted for 44 per cent of turnover, have been badly affected by the recession and the downturn in consumer spending. Mr Young was appointed to head the UK company in 1989, allowing the board to concentrate on running overseas subsidiaries and on cutting costs and inventories. Mr Sugar said: 'When times are difficult, it is important for us to bring back the first eleven and to cut back on our overheads. 'The overall management structure we put in place some time ago to run our international and UK businesses has worked well and remains firmly in place.'
