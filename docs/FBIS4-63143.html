BFN [From the "Pacific Ocean" Program] [Text] The first stage of Operation Putina has been completed. The aim of the operation was to protect Russian economic interests in its territorial waters. In other words, to combat illegal fishing. Border troops; officials from the Ministry of Internal Affairs, the Customs, the Taxation Police, Primorrybvod [Maritime Kray Fishery Department], Sakhalinrybvod [Sakhalin Fishery Department]; and naval personnel took part in the operation. Captain First Rank Aleksey Velikanov, deputy chief of the Pacific Border District Maritime Department, tells us about the results of the first stage of Operation Putina. [Begin Velikanov recording] The first stage of Operation Putina '94 ended on 10 May. The results are as follows: During the operation, more than 800 vessels, of which nearly 300 were foreign, were found in Russian territorial waters and exclusive economic zone. One hundred and fifteen [Russian] and 24 foreign vessels were inspected. Twenty [Russian] and four foreign vessels were cited for breach of fishing regulations. The offending [Russian] vessels were fined more than 200 million rubles. The Japanese fishing vessel was fined $15,385 and fishing permits of the three remaining foreign vessels were revoked. Losses estimated at approximately 20 billion rubles were averted through the resources of those who took part in the operation. Various resources, namely the Pacific Border District, the Pacific Fleet, Primorrybvod, Sakhalinrybvod, the Environment Protection Committee, Spetsmorinspektsiya [Special Maritime Inspection], as well as the Pacific Fleet and Border District aviation took part in the operation. The next stage of the operation is planned for the latter part of June. However, this does not mean that in the interim the security of the exclusive economic zone and the state border will be relaxed. Already, the customs and border troops in Korsakov, Sakhalin Oblast, early this morning detained two vessels engaged in the illegal transfer of crabs. They are both currently under investigation. [end recording]
