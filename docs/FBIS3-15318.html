Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Mainayev] [Text] Bishkek March 4 TASS -- Two draft military cooperation treaties between Russia and Kyrgyzstan were intitialled in Bishkek today. The first draft treaty relates to military service of Russian citizens in the Armed Forces of Kyrgyzstan. The treaty will be effective until December 31, 1999. In compliance with this treaty, Russian citizens, serving in the army of Kyrgyzstan, will retain all priviliges envisaged by the Russian law. After a Russian officer retires, the Ministry of Defence of the Republic of Kyrgyzstan will be obliged to purchase him with living accommodation in Russia (not including Moscow and St. Petrersburg). The second draft treaty is about military service by officers, warrant officers and extended-service men. Armed forces personnel belonging to these categories will be allowed to serve until December 31, 1994, provided they sign a contract about their unhindered transfer from one army to the other. The draft documents were initialled by head of the Russian Defence Ministry's department Colonel-General Stanislav Petrov and Kyrgyzstan's Minister of Defence Lt. General Myrzakan Subanov.
