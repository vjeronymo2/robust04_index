BFN [Text] President Ramiro de Leon Carpio yesterday acknowledged that there are Guatemalan settlers residing in Belizean territory; however, he explained that no pressure has ever been exerted on the peasants by Belize to get them to leave the area. Land is being sought to transfer them into those protected areas, he added. [sentence as published] De Leon Carpio explained that his government's plan is to have the families living in the Chiquibul region leave the area, because it is a nature reserve. He added that studies are being conducted with the authorities of the National Institute of Agrarian Reform (INTA) to transfer these people to an area that meets their basic living requirements. Nevertheless, he reiterated that Belizean troops are not driving these people out, as had previously been reported. He also said there is no final deadline for the withdrawal of the settlers. "The government is taking all necessary measures for those peasants to settle in lands that will enable them to raise their crops. It is necessary for them to withdraw from Chiquibul, because this is a national park. But certainly, it is not because Belize is pressuring them to leave," De Leon Carpio noted. In light of the situation, Foreign Ministry spokesman Antonio Monzon assured that the Guatemalans will be transferred in September to the Dolores municipality in Peten, located several kilometers from where they are currently settled. He explained that the trip will be sponsored by INTA, the Peten Land Committee, and the National Fund for Peace. This issue caused several reactions last week when Carlos Asturias, former governor of the northern department of Peten, disclosed that Belizean troops had set a 25 June deadline for the settlers to withdraw from the area. Asturias recently said the report had been received by a parish priest in the region, explaining that a group of soldiers who patrol the border area "are threatening them constantly."
