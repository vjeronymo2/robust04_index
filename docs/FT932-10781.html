930507 FT 07 MAY 93 / Insolvency profession found wanting MOST PROPERTY lenders do not believe that the insolvency profession provides value for money, a survey by Chesterton, a firm of chartered surveyors, shows Almost two thirds of the 60 financial institutions questioned said they had not received good value. Some lenders were also dissatisfied with the service provided by receivers and administrators. Just over one in five respondents described the service from insolvency practitioners as poor, while 44 per cent described it as fair and 30 per cent as good. Mr Mark Homan, president of the Society of Practitioners of Insolvency, said in response to the findings that in some straightforward cases insolvency practitioners might not bring a great deal of added value. Property insolvencies often involved extremely complex situations, however, where there was a clear need for expertise.
