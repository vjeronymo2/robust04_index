920730 FT 30 JUL 92 / London Stock Exchange: Equity futures and options trading BUYERS returned to the stock index futures as both technical and market factors combined to cause a surge in a busy session, writes Christopher Price. The September contract on the FT-SE opened weakly, at 2,399, as traders were lulled into believing that Tuesday's bounce in the market was a technical one and that yesterday promised a corresponding downturn. But the strong equities market was quickly underpinned by a big order from BZW, which became the signal for other buyers to take advantage of what had been a sellers' market. September began to race ahead, tranforming a 10-point fair value discount to a similar premium within half-an-hour. Many of the small independent traders were said to be caught short by the buying binge, as were some larger houses. The contract continued to be squeezed up into the afternoon session and a strong Wall Street helped sentiment. September closed at 2,445, up 58 from its previous finish and around a 10-point premium to its fair value of around 12. In traded options, turnover was a brisk 28,240 contracts, a third up from Tuesday's total. British Airways led the stock options on 1,243 contracts dealt, with the October 300 series featuring.
