BFN [Text] Zambia has mobilized 800 troops to help the United Nations in Rwanda, where more than 500,000 civilians have been slaughtered in a two-month civil war there. Defense Secretary Brigadier General Jack Mwaanga said in an interview with ZNBC [Zambia National Broadcasting Corporation] today that the 800 soldiers are ready to leave for Rwanda any time. He said the contingent is on standby on the Copperbelt but cannot leave without special equipment which the Zambia Army has requested from other countries. Gen. Mwaanga said because of the difficult nature of the operation in Rwanda, the Zambian troops will need special equipment.
