940812 FT 12 AUG 94 / World Trade News: Hanoi warns over abuse of licences Vietnam has said it will fine the growing number of foreign companies that are abusing representative office licences issued by the government. Representative offices are often an investor's first step into Vietnam but their licence only allows marketing and liaison. They are not allowed to receive income in Vietnam. However, an increasing number of foreign investors are using them for trading and carrying out business transactions. They say this is because the rules applying to representative offices are not in keeping with the present investment climate. 'They were essential in the early years (of foreign investment) when people came to do feasibility studies,' said one US consultant based in Hanoi. 'But now people are actually trying to do business.' Foreign representative offices found violating their charter will be fined Dollars 50,000 and will be subjected to surprise inspections by the ministry of trade, which grants the licences, the government said. A further fine of Dollars 20,000 would be imposed for offices operating beyond expiry of their licence. Vietnam's chamber of commerce and industry says there are 964 representative offices with 629 in the southern industrial hub of Ho Chi Minh City and the rest in the capital, Hanoi. Most of those breaking the law are understood to be small Asian companies.
