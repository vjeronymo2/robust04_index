930701 FT 01 JUL 93 / NUM to ballot on longer shifts DELEGATES at the NUM miners union conference in Scarborough yesterday voted to hold a ballot on industrial action if longer shifts and a seven-day week were introduced by British Coal to improve productivity. British Coal's productivity has risen 23 per cent over the last year to 8.5 tonnes a man-shift. Mr Neil Clarke, chairman, said improved efficiency was the best way of ensuring a future for threatened pits.
