Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Today, people opposing the U.S. blockade against Cuba are in Washington supporting a bill calling for the reestablishment of ties between the two countries. The support was organized by religious groups, scholars, and politicians to mark the beginning of a campaign against the aggressive U.S. policy toward Cuba. The bill is entitled, Act for Free Trade With Cuba, and is sponsored by Democratic Representative Charles Rangel, who describes Washington's policy as hypocrital, given the current international conditions. However, in political circles in the United States, the climate is that of prudence in forecasting a victory in Congress because a change in policy toward Cuba depends to a degree on the Cuban-American community and Wall Street businesses.
