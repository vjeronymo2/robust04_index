Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 13 February (MTI) -- "What the Hungarian nation wants to have after the May 8 elections is better, more, fairer, and more efficient than it has today. It is uncertain, though, that it wants something fundamentally different," Laszlo Surjan, chairman of the Christian Democratic People's Party (KDNP) and welfare minister, told a rally marking the beginning of his party's electoral campaign today. The chairman of the smallest party of the governing coalition said the Christian Democrats rejected Prime Minister Peter Boross's statement that the Hungarian Democratic Forum (MDF) is the only force able to govern the country. "We consider ourselves fit for the task," Surjan said. He added, however, that the Christian Democrats stand the closest to the MDF. Outlining the priorities of the KDNP's electoral manifesto, Surjan said that the Christian Democrats pressed for a calculable legislature, and wished to guarantee a safe and calculable future for the people. Making a critical approach to the past four years, the KDNP chairman said the state had got rid of its oversized tasks too quickly and left society unprepared to face a multitude of problems. The Christian Democrats wish that the state should assume responsibility for, rather than intervene in, the economy. "We want to have a strong rule of law and stable public security," Surjan said.
