BFN [Text] Formal talks between the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, and the Nepalese prime minister, Mr. Girija Prasad Koirla, were held at the Singhe Darbar in Kathmandu today. The talks, which lasted over 90 minutes, were held in an atmosphere of cordiality, reminiscent of constructive and meaningful cooperation existing between Pakistan and Nepal. Pakistan Television's representative, Abdul Wahid, reports from Kathmandu that regional and international issues, including Kashmir, figured in the talks. The two leaders also discussed bilateral relations with particular emphasis on economic cooperation. They agreed on steps to enhance cooperation in several fields, including trade and culture. Ms. Benazir Bhutto was assisted by the foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali; the minister of state for finance, Maqdum Shahabuddin; Special assistant to the prime minister on social sector, Begum Shahnaz Wazir Ali; the foreign secretary, Mr. Najmuddin Sheikh; and Pakistan's ambassador to Nepal, Mr. Mohammad Nasir Mian. The Nepalese prime minister, Mr. Girija Prasad Koirala, was assisted by a high-level team which included some cabinet ministers and economic experts. Talking to newsmen after the talks, Ms. Benazir Bhutto described her discussions with the Nepalese prime minister as useful. [Begin Bhutto recording] We discussed regional and international issues. And we also discussed how to improve cooperation. We must be holding the joint commission on an annual basis and we are also going to be holding an industrial exhibition here. So, Nepali businessmen can see what goods we have in Pakistan and we are going to have a cultural exchange office for (?our two nations). [end recording] Speaking at a civic reception hosted by the citizens of Kathmandu this afternoon, the prime minister stressed the need of devotion and hard work for the economic uplift in the developing country. A very warm and colorful reception was extended to the prime minister and members of her entourage when they arrived at the Municipal Hall. The mayor of Kathmandu, Mr. P.L. Singh, greeted the distinguished guests. The prime minister was profusely garlanded. The prime minister was enthusiastically greeted as she entered the hall. The mayor of Kathmandu welcomed the prime minister on her historic visit. The mayor then presented the key of Kathmandu city to the prime minister. Speaking on the occasion, Ms. Benazir Bhutto said the people of Pakistan and Nepal who are fellow members of SAARC [South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation] countries have common struggle for parliamentary democracy and democratic institutions. She reminded that the population living in the developing countries have to strive for achieving the cherished goals of economic betterment of their masses. She thanked the people of Kathmandu for extending a rousing reception to her and the members of her delegation and said this reflects the sentiments of Nepalese people for their Pakistani brethren. The president of the Nepalese Congress, Mr. Krishnanath Prasad Bhattarai, called on the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, in Kathmandu yesterday. During the meeting, the prime minister briefed the president of the Nepalese Congress about the democratic process in Pakistan. The prime minister also reiterated the unflinching commitment of her government for democracy and the growth of democratic institutions. She said her government is also undertaking steps to bring positive changes in the socioeconomic structure of her country. Mr. Bhattarai paid tributes to the prime minister for her struggle for democracy and the rule of law. The chairman of the Communist Party of Nepal and the leader of the opposition in the Nepalese parliament, Mr. Manmohan Adhikari, also called on the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, in Kathmandu. The leader of the Nepalese opposition lauded the prime minister of Pakistan for her deep commitment toward democracy and human rights. The prime minister, while reciprocating the gesture, informed the Nepalese leader that Pakistan wants to see democracy in South Asia and the growth of human rights in the region.
