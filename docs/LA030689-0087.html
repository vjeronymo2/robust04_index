March 6, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Purchasing Managers See Slowdown: Economic growth slowed in February, easing inflation pressures that have pushed short-term interest rates to the highest level in four years, the National Assn. of Purchasing Management said. In its monthly report based on a survey of purchasing executives at 250 companies, the trade group said the U.S. economy expanded but at an even slower rate than the sluggish growth seen in January. The group's leading purchasing management index was 53% in February, nearly unchanged from 53.2% in January. The previous low for the index was 52.6% in February, 1987. The index is a composite of new orders, production, deliveries, inventories and employment.
