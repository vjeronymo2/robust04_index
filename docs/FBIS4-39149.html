BFN [Text] Max van der Stoel, CSCE high commissioner for minorities, has been visiting Ukraine since 31 May until 4 June. The aim of his visit was to study the situation in the Republic of Crimea, which took shape after its Supreme Council adopted a decision on restoring the 6 May 1992 wording of the Crimean constitution and also to work out recommendations concerning the participation of the CSCE in its settlement. During the visit, Mr. Max van der Stoel had meetings with President Leonid Kravchuk, high-level officials of Ukraine and Crimea, and representatives of public organizations. The high commissioner highly assessed the balanced stand of Ukraine's leadership, based exceptionally on the provisions of the Constitution of Ukraine in its approaches to the solution of the Crimean issue. Max van der Stoel emphasized the necessity of abiding by the fundamental principles of the CSCE, especially such principles as ensuring territorial integrity and inviolability of borders and also the necessity of the soonest possible settlement of the situation on legal principles. The issue on the situation that took shape as a result of the actions by the Crimean leadership and the possible promotion of its solution on the part of CSCE is to be considered at a sitting of the CSCE committee of Senior officials, which will take place in Prague on 13-16 June.
