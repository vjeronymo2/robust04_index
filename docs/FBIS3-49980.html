Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] According to a government decision, Hungary will sign the Partnership for Peace documents, which are available next week in the Brussels NATO headquarters. It was also said at the government spokeswoman's briefing that the cabinet, led by Peter Boross, will remain in office after the expiry of its mandate, that is after 2 May, as an executive government, until a new government is elected. Gabor Bankuti reports: [Bankuti] The government has authorized Minister of Foreign Affairs Geza Jeszenszky to sign NATO's Partnership for Peace documents. They can be signed in Brussels as of next Monday. [passage omitted]
