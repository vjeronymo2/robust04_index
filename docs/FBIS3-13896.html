Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India today regretted Pakistan's decision to close its consulate office in Bombay. Responding to a question at the daily briefing in New Delhi today, the spokesman of the External Affairs Ministry said Islamabad's action falls into the continuing and regrettable pattern of taking negative actions which are not conducive to good neighborly relations. He pointed out that India has always tried to assist in creating a climate in which the Pakistani Consulate can perform its functions. Regarding accommodation, the spokesman said Pakistan was not interested in certain locations offered by the Maharashtra Government as was clarified by the chief minister himself. He said Pakistanis themselves know the accommodation problem in metropolitan cities and pointed out that India is paying markets rates for its properties in Islamabad and Karachi.
