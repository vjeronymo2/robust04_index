910508 FT 08 MAY 91 / UK Company News: Ambassador Sec helped by purchases FUELLED BY acquisitions pre-tax profits at Ambassador Security Group improved 64 per cent, from Pounds 625,000 to Pounds 1.02m, in 1990. Fully diluted earnings per share showed a more modest increase, from 4.08p to 4.26p. The basic figure declined to 4.33p (4.48p). During the year the Kent-based company made a number of purchases including Alarm Manufacturing and Electronic Holdings for Pounds 2.5m when it raised about Pounds 4m from a placing and open offer. At the same time it moved up from the USM to the main market. For the future Mr Paul Skuse, chairman, said in the recession the company was striving for greater efficiency and productivity. The first three months of 1991 were up to expectations. Turnover was more than doubled at Pounds 4.69m (Pounds 2.1m). A final dividend of 0.25p is proposed for a total of 0.6p (0.5p).
