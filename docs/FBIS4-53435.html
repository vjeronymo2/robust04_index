BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 19 KYODO -- Nippon Steel Corp. expressed regret Thursday [19 May] at a U.S. International Trade Commission ruling that Japanese steelmakers are selling certain steel products in the U.S. market at unfairly low prices. "It's regrettable that our argument was not understood, but this is the ITC's final ruling, so we'll consider what to do in the future by considering the implications of it," a Nippon Steel official said. At stake in the ruling are two types of special steel -- grain- oriented steel and stainless angle used in heavy electrical machinery and the construction industry.
