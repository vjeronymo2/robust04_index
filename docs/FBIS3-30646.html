Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Today" -- BTA lead] [Excerpts] Sofia, February 4 (BTA) -- [passage omitted] "What we know is that the trains carried 7,000 tonnes of cleared fuel for Serbia. UN monitors have issued official permits for this," Prime Minister Lyuben Berov said today, answering a question of the parliamentary faction of the Union of the Democratic Forces related to a reported embargo-busting passage of four tank wagon trains across the border with Serbia. [passage omitted] This evening Prime Minister Lyuben Berov met the leaders of the Civil Alliance for the Republic (GOR). Speaking on the behalf of the GOR, set up recently by defectors of the Bulgarian Socialist Party, Aleksandur Tomov told the prime minister the group will support the cabinet at the no-confidence vote. The GOR representatives voiced their concern over the fact that the government is subject to political pressure aimed at personnel changes in the Interior Ministry before the investigation has concluded.
