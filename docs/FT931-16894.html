930104 FT 04 JAN 93 / Financial Times A hundred years ago, the Financial Times appeared for the first time on pink newsprint. The colour has become an integral part of our character, instantly recognised and regularly copied on newsstands around the world. To mark the anniversary, the newspaper appears today dressed nearly all in white. A special third section includes a replica of the first pink FT alongside a little local history, a snapshot of life in the City in the early 1890s. Today also marks the first full working day of the European single market. In addition to our usual coverage, this issue examines how business and economies are adapting to the changes, and how the EC as a whole may develop in the years ahead. Tomorrow, and for the next 100 years, we are back in the pink.
