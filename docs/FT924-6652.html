921120 FT 20 NOV 92 / Former Bishop of London plans move to Roman Catholic church in protest at vote for women priests GRAHAM LEONARD, former bishop of London, is planning to join the Roman Catholic Church in protest at last week's vote in favour of the ordination of women priests. The bishop, third most senior in the Anglican hierarchy until he retired last year, suggested in an article in the Catholic Herald that a breakaway church might be formed under the authority of the Pope. The archbishop of Canterbury, Dr George Carey, yesterday called for 'ill-considered judgments' to be avoided.
