Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The Higher Islamic Shiite Council held a meeting at its temporary headquarters in Tallat al-Khayyat this morning and elected Shaykh Muhammad Mahdi Shams-al-Din council chairman, succeeding Shaykh Musa al-Sar, the council's founder [who disappeared during a visit to Libya in August 1978]. Shaykh Shams-al-Din has been deputy chairman of the council since 1974. The council members also elected Ja'farite Mufti 'Abd-al-Amir Qabalan as first deputy chairman and Dr. 'Adnan Haydar as second deputy chairman. Some 46 of the council's 48 members attended the session. [passage omitted]
