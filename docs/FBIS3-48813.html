Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, January 25 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The outcome of checking the activities of the general board on ensuring the state security and the central police department by the working group of the parliament. The representatives of the opposition group in the parliament noted that the chairman and deputy chairman and the prime minister as well have expressed their request to check the above mentioned accusations of their so called abusing the official powers. They refused to give any commentaries on this point pleading the necessity to adhere to the parliamentary ethics. The capital city newspaper "ULAANBAATAR" published an article by scholar S. Ichinnorov on the creation of the present Mongolian capital. The article says that the city was founded as far back as 1639 and had been transferred from time to time until its stationing on its present site. Those movings of the capital historian is explaining in connection with the traditions of the people and necessity of nature protection. Besides this he considers that among the other reasons there were aggressions of the neighbouring western tribes and the needs for maintaining commercial and economic relations. Journalist O. Lhagvadorj in his article in the newspaper doubts whether the granting of the building of a club having been named after Lenin on lease to Russian citizen S. P. Kokarev was legitimate. The above mentioned citizen in his turn began letting lease of the building to various organizations which was recognized by the chief of the Consular Department of the Russian Embassy to Mongolia as being a violation of the decree of the Mongolian Government, the newspaper informs.
