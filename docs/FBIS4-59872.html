BFN [Text] Khartoum, May 18, IRNA -- The Sudani deputy foreign minister, 'Umar Yusif Baridu, Tuesday called for strengthening of ties with Iran and said Khartoum is proud of having relations with Tehran. In a meeting with Iranian ambassador to Khartoum Majid Kamal, Baridu voiced his country's readiness to promote bilateral ties with Iran. He termed his recent trip to Iran and his negotiations with Iranian officials as fruitful. Meanwhile, the Sudanese official expressed regret over fierce fighting in Yemen and Afghanistan. Referring to the conspiracies masterminded by the world arrogance against Muslims, Baridu urged for an end to the ongoing genocide of Muslims and called for the settlement of their disputes through peaceful means. At the end of the meeting he welcomed the imminent trip of his Iranian counterpart 'Abbas Maleki to Sudan.
