Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN [Communique of the office of the government spokesman -- read by announcer] [Text] A joint ministerial meeting was held at 0900 on the morning of 23 March at the Cabinet's office chaired by Samdech Hun Sen, second prime minister of the Royal Government of Cambodia [RGC]. The joint ministerial meeting focused on strengthening the victory won by the Khmer Royal Armed Forces [KRAF] in seizing control of Pailin at 1800 on 19 March. The meeting also outlined a number of urgent principles, targets, and measures to control Pailin, which had been under the Khmer Rouge since the end of 1989. The Khmer Rouge had used this area as their autonomous zone and center for carrying out illegal economic exploitation to continue the war, carrying on the massacre, and creating insecurity for the Cambodian people throughout the country. In this connection: 1. The meeting discussed, examined, and determing a number of measures to cope with the resettlement of the people who will settle in the Pailin area and provide them with security; 2. The meeting reiterated the RGC's determination to rearrange the Pailin area by sending the people to settle there. The government also plans to develop this area. 3. The meeting decided in principle to set up measures to bring back the Cambodian people who had fled the Kingdom of Thailand when the KRAF launched its attack to drive the Khmer Rouge out of Pailin. 4. The meeting believed that the various foreign businessmen and companies which formerly dealt in gems and logs with the Khmer Rouge would definitely stop having contacts with the Khmer Rouge. At the same time, the RGC solemnly declared its recognition of all the agreements. [sentence as heard] The meeting ended at 1030 in a spirit of unity and (?responsibility). [Dated] Phnom Penh, 23 March 1994
