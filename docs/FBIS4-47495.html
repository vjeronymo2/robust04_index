Vavilov; UDC 535.317.1] [Abstract] Inspection of multimodular optical telescopes with a synthesized aperture for proper phasing by means of an achromatized diffractional hologram interferometer is proposed, the aim being to maximize the resolving power of such telescopes in terrestrial astronomical observatories. The scheme is demonstrated on a bimodular telescope with four smaller mirrors including the main one in each module and one common larger spherical concave mirror which synthesizes the exit pupil while forming the image of a star in its focal plane. The holographic system consists of a spherical concave collimating mirror and a hologram. It can operate with an either internal or external light source. In the former case the light source, a point source, is placed in the focal plane of a booster lens and the hologram is placed in front of the collimating mirror. The holographic system forms the inspecting wave, which is to ``simulate'' the radiation entering both telescope modules upon arrival from a star within the middle of the field of view. The hologram is a plane mirror with a reflective diffraction grating, an equidistant array of lines, deposited on its active surface. Its spatial frequency has been selected so as to ensure that radiation in +1 and -1 diffraction orders will impinge on the main mirror of module ``+1'' and module ``-1'' respectively. Each of these mirrors is covered by a projecting reflective hologram with zero-order optical power and the same spatial carrier frequency. The collimating mirror and the holographic mirror can be combined a single reflective or transmissive hologram. A theoretical analysis of the inspection process, taking into the geometry of this scheme, indicates that decreasing the spatial frequency of the holograms will allow widening the tolerances on the telescope parameters. For verification, an experiment was performed using an incandescent lamp as the light source and diffraction gratings with 200 lines/mm each. The hologram before the collimator was used as a measuring bridge and a Fourier objective with its optical axis passing through the center of that hologram was simulating a phased telescope with a synthesized aperture. Following appropriate adjustment of the three holograms in the focal plane of this objective, a high-contrast achromatic interference pattern was seen which remained stationary while the lamp was being moved in the focal plane of its booster lens.
