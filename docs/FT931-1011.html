930326 FT 26 MAR 93 / Finance summaries 'difficult to read' SUMMARY financial statements are often more difficult to read than a company's full annual report, a study sponsored by the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales says. A desire to save costs is the main reason why companies offer shareholders summary statements, says the report. However, it says, the profit and loss account and balance sheets statements are normally identical to those provided in the full accounts, but come without the notes. Summary statements were introduced in the 1989 Companies Act. Companies can send them to shareholders in place of the full accounts. The survey, based on the experiences of 300 industrial and financial companies, found that producing summary statements rarely saved money. Summary Financial Statements.
