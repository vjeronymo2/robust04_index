Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Border Partially Deblocked"] [Text] Seven foreign vehicles that had been waiting for 2 weeks to leave Greece were allowed to enter Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] two days ago: Two Holland lorries carrying textile, two Italian trucks with raw materials for the leather industry, and three Greek trucks carrying oranges and olives for Macedonia. Four fuel-carrying vehicles of the firm Mlaz from Bogdanci were also permitted to leave Greece. Two additional freight-cars loaded with phosphates for metallurgic industry in Titov Veles also arrived in Macedonia from the Greek railway station Idomeni yesterday. The remaining 18,000 tons of phosphates left from Salonika for Burgas, Bulgaria.
