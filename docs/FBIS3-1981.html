Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Male, March 3 (XINHUA) -- Visiting Chinese Vice-premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen called on Maldivian President Maumoon Abdul Gayoom here today. During their meeting, Qian conveyed an invitation of Chinese President Jiang Zemin to Gayoom to visit China at a convenient time. He said Gayoom's visit to China in 1984 has promoted the development of cooperative relations between the two countries. Qian noted that "China and the Maldives have shared common views on many international issues and the two countries have been active in developing their bilateral relations". He believed that with joint efforts, cooperations will further develop in various fields of the two countries. Gayoom said, Qian's current visit "will be another milestone in the development of the friendly relations between the two countries". He also spoke highly of the fast economic development in China in the past decade. Gayoom asked Qian to convey his invitation to Chinese President Jiang Zemin to visit the Maldives and expressed the hope that he will pay another visit to China in the near future.
