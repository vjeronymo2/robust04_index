Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By reporter Hu Suojin (5170 6956 6930)] [Text] Hanoi, 22 Feb (XINHUA) -- Do Muoi, general secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam [CPV], SRV President Le Duc Anh, SRV Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet, and CPV Central Committee adviser Pham Van Dong met separately with the visiting Chen Yuan, vice governor of the People's Bank of China, and his party on 21 and 22 February. During the meeting, Do Muoi stressed that it is very necessary for Vietnam and China to expand and strengthen their cooperation in all fields of endeavor. Do Muoi said: Science and technology have enjoyed rapid development in China in recent years. China's machinery equipment is very suitable for Vietnam. At present, economic construction is the central task of various work in Vietnam. Vietnam and China should seize the opportunity and should successfully conduct trade and economic cooperation as quickly as possible. It is beneficial for both countries. Do Muoi also spoke highly of Comrade Deng Xiaoping's theory on building socialism with Chinese characteristics as well as of the successes achieved by China in economic reform. During the meeting, Le Duc Anh stated that developing friendly Vietnamese-Chinese relations is in keeping with the historical trend and is a necessity as a result of the development of the situation. He expressed the hope that banks of the two countries will strengthen cooperation, and he expressed his support for the two countries' further developing trade and economic relations. During the meeting, Chen Yuan said: China and Vietnam have tremendous potential for trade and economic cooperation. Chinese and Vietnamese banks exchanged views during the delegation's visit on the reform of the banking system and unanimously agreed to strengthen cooperation between themselves. The People's Bank of China delegation led by Chen Yuan arrived in Hanoi on 19 February for a week-long visit at the invitation of the State Bank of Vietnam. The delegation is scheduled to leave Hanoi this afternoon for Ho Chi Minh City to continue its visit.
