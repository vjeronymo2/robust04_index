Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tamara Zamyatina] [Text] Moscow March 2 TASS -- The "laurels" of Sergey Shakhray, leader of the Party of Russian Unity and Accord, whose faction initiated the adoption of the resolution on political amnesty has obviously touched Russian nationalist Vladimir Zhirinovskiy on the raw. In a statement issued on Wednesday, the faction of his Liberal Democratic Party of Russia claimed the right to Shakhray's laurels. "The party has promised its voters to ensure the adoption of this act and it has kept its promise," the statement said. However, in his statement on February 23 Konstantin Zatulin, secretary of the faction of Shakhray's party in the parliament, said that the initiative to adopt the memorandum of accord together with the resolutions on amnesty belonged to the Party of Russian Unity and Accord. Meanwhile, Shakhray and Zhirinovskiy have been trying to distance themselves from the political consequences of amnesty. Shakhray said at a session of the State Duma, the lower house, that despite his will, "the consistency of the plan of national reconciliation was broken." As a result, those pardoned will "again dive for political fighting," he said [as received]. Zhirinovskiy noted that his "party is in no way connected with the people who have been pardoned under amnesty."
