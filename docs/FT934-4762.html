931202 FT 02 DEC 93 / Observer: Feudal justice Rarely does Britain's Department of Trade and Industry show imagination. But in its investigation into the affairs of hotel chain Queens Moat Houses - which recently notched up the second biggest loss in UK corporate history - a bright spark has been at work. For the DTI has selected Patrick Phillips QC as one of its inspectors on the case - and Phillips knows about moat houses. Each summer he organises a historical recreation of Tudor domestic life at his restored stately home, Kentwell Hall, complete with moat and 200 suitably dressed Elizabethans. Queens Moat executives may have cause to fear that Phillips' love of the 16th century encompasses another favourite Tudor practice: head-chopping.
