BFN [Text] At the invitation of State President Guntis Ulmanis, Aleksandr Rannikh, Russian ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to Latvia, today met the Latvian president. The ambassador orally conveyed Russian President Yeltsin's invitation for the Latvian president to pay a working visit to Moscow on 21 April and to sign earlier initialled agreements. Our president expressed his thanks for the invitation, explaining that he considers such a visit to be useful because there are many issues concerning Latvian-Russian relations which need to be clarified, especially those related to the order of 5 April. Talks with Boris Yeltsin at which an exchange of opinions and coordination of views concerning the initialled agreements and the prospects for future relations between Latvia and Russia would have great significance on the international scale. The Latvian president recalled that the Saeima will discuss the possibility of signing the agreements at tomorrow's extraordinary plenary session. The situation in the countries of the former USSR and the latest events on the territory of former Yugoslavia may also be discussed at the talks with the Russian president. A reply from the Latvian state president on his possible visit to Moscow will be given to the Russian ambassador later today.
