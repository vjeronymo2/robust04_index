BFN [Text] Hong Kong, 17 Jun (XINHUA) -- The Defense and Public Security Experts subgroup of the Sino-British Joint Liaison Group ended its 16th meeting in Hong Kong yesterday. The parties did not reach a consensus in all aspects on the transfer of military-use land in Hong Kong, but made progress on some issues. Both parties agreed to hold an informal meeting in the next few days to continue their consultations. In his interview with reporters after the meeting, Chen Zuoer, head of the Chinese delegation, said: During these four days, both parties have further narrowed their discrepancies and made some progress. But there are some issues which need further discussions. The Chinese side will try its best to continue to seek a resolution to some pending problems in an informal and flexible manner so as to reach an agreement at the earliest possible date.
