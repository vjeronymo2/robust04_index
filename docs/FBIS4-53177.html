BFN [Text] It was disclosed on 17 May that Democratic Socialist Party Chairman Keigo Ouchi said at a meeting held with senior members of a religious group in Tokyo in the evening of 16 May that "we would like to form a nonpartisan caretaker government after approval of the fiscal 1994 budget and resignation of the cabinet en bloc." He made this remark as a countermeasure to deal with the situation if no-confidence motion is submitted against the Hata cabinet. According to a source concerned, Ouchi took into consideration the possibility of including the Liberal Democratic Party in forming the next coalition government. Ouchi reportedly said: "We need to immediately approve the bill for redrawing electoral districts for the House of Representatives. After that, we can dissolve the Diet and hold a general election in either November or December."
