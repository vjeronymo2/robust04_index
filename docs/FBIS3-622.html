Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Lagos, 9 March (AFP) -- A war between Nigerian and Cameroon over ownership of the Bakassi peninsula would not be only a diversion but also "a misdirected and ill-advised aggression", a Lagos-based human rights group said here Wednesday [9 March]. The Committee for the Defence of Human Rights (CDHR) said in a statement that leaders of the two neighbouring nations "can and should seek" an amicable solution to the conflict in order to avoid imminent "war that now stares both countries in the face" "We cannot at this time afford to go on a war whose disastrous end no one can predict", the statement said. At a time when Nigerians and Cameroonians were "wallowing in abject poverty" and yearning for democracy, it was unfortunate that leaders of the two nations are "warming up for war", it continued. [passage omitted] Wole Soyinka, the 1986 Nobel literature laureate, warned earlier in the week that it would be senseless for the two West African countries to go to war over a border dispute.
