BFN [Text] Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou gave a television interview to Antenna [private radio and television network] yesterday and referred to the improvement in the climate of Greek-Turkish and Greek-Albanian relations following the recent contacts by Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias. The prime minister said that the government will insist on the preliminary conditions it has set for lifting the embargo against Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM] but he did not rule out the possibility of the issue being resolved before this autumn. Referring to the economy, he ruled out the implementation of new economic measures until the end of the year and expressed his optimism in terms of a recovery of the economy. Finally, Mr. Papandreou said that a government reshuffle will take place after the end of the Greek presidency of the European Union but did not want to talk about his possible move to assume the presidency of the country.
