BFN [Text] Seoul, May 7 (YONHAP) -- Japanese Ambassador Toshio Goto told Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu Saturday morning that the Hata cabinet's view of history mirrors that of the previous Hosokawa cabinet. Conveying the Japanese response to Seoul's official protest against remarks made by Justice Minister Shigeto Nagano in defense of Tokyo's role in World War II, Ambassador Goto said he had been instructed to clarify that the Hata cabinet attaches importance to promoting a future-oriented bilateral relationship and an honest view of history, Foreign Ministry spokesman Chang Ki-ho said. Goto told Han he had conveyed Seoul's concern to his home government, adding that Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata would take appropriate action on Nagano's remarks upon returning from Europe Sunday. Tokyo will notify Seoul of any action, he added. Minister Han told the ambassador that the justice minister's remarks had hurt and angered Koreans and that the Japanese Government should move to heal this hurt and prevent the recurrence of such remarks, Chang said.
