910430 FT 30 APR 91 / Parliament and Politics: Labour plan for change in 'macho' management - City Policy A LABOUR government would seek to change Britain's management culture to make company boards more accountable to employees and curb directors' pay rises, Ms Marjorie Mowlam, the party's City spokesman said last night, Ralph Atkins writes. Company directors have become like 'medieval barons', willingly reducing their accountability, Ms Mowlam said. Britain was far more restrictive that other European Community countries. 'The macho management culture of the 1980s, characterised by toughness and autocratic attitudes, is all too common. It manifests itself in the relative loss of power by employees and the generally inadequate response to training and R&D' She used a speech to the London Bullion Markets Association to attack recent large pay rises for directors. Few boards would consider taking a pay cut to reflect declining returns to shareholders. Directors pay rises have outstripped those of other employees for at least the last five years and were often 'disproportionate' to the actual benefits which shareholders received, Ms Mowlam said. 'Few shareholders bothered to question such handsome rewards.' Ms Mowlam said cuts in the top rate of income tax to 40p were intended to improve incentives. 'However many directors took it upon themselves to cream off even more of the profits with record pay awards in 1989, the first year of the lower tax rates. This was a blatant abuse of the original intention.'
