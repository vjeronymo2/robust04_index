Claircom Communications Group, L.P., GTE and In-Flight Phone Corporation request clarification of the definition of ``subscriber'' in section 9(g) when applied to the payment of regulatory fees for the Air-Ground Telephone Service. Unlike conventional telephone service, subscribers to that service, usually operators of commercial aircraft, lease their service for the purpose of making it available to their own customers. There is no contractual relationship between the air-ground service operator and the end user of its service. Consequently, as suggested by the parties, we will treat the operator of an aircraft in which its service is installed as the subscriber to the service and charge the fee based upon the number of transceivers leased by the operator. Similarly, licensees in the air-ground service should include in their total fee a payment on a transceiver basis for service they provide to users other than commercial aircraft, such as private aircraft. 3.
