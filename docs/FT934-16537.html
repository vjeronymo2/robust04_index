931006 FT 06 OCT 93 / The Conservative Party Conference: Lamont's credibility was undermined, says Clarke MR KENNETH CLARKE yesterday fuelled tension between himself and Mr Norman Lamont by asserting bluntly that his predecessor as chancellor had lost all credibility on economic policy before his removal earlier this year. Mr Clarke said Mr Lamont had 'got himself into a dreadful situation where nobody took the slightest notice of anything he said on economic policy.' 'He was subjected to a campaign of continual harassment about his credit cards and all kinds of irrelevancies by people who presumably thought they were entitled to do this because they believed he should have resigned after Black Wednesday.' Mr Clarke's remarks in the Bow Group magazine Crossbow came just two days before both men are due to speak at the conference - Mr Clarke in the main hall and Mr Lamont on the fringe. Party managers are worried that Mr Lamont may be provoked into launching a fresh attack on the government's policies after his recent endorsement of rightwing opposition to tax increases. Arguing that Mr Lamont was 'entitled to take the credit for the fact that Britain is now recovering when the rest of Europe is not,' Mr Clarke said the Pounds 50bn public sector borrowing requirement his predecessor had bequeathed was a 'big problem.'
