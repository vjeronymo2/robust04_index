BFN [Text] Tehran, April 24, IRNA -- Majles Speaker Hojjat ol-Eslam 'Ali Akbar Nateq-Nuri here Sunday [24 April] called on Islamic countries to lift arms embargo against Muslims in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Speaking at today's Majles open session, the speaker referred to the support of the international bodies, including the U.N. and hegemonist powers, for the Serbian crimes and said, "Islamic countries, given the unreliability of U.N. Security Council resolutions, should lift the arms embargo against Bosnia to enable the Muslims in that country to defend themselves." He expressed hope that Islamic countries would not limit themselves to uttering words but would also enter the scene of action. The speaker referred to his recent talks with Malaysian and Pakistani officials and said authorities in the two countries held identical views in connection with the double standard of the internatiional community on Bosnia and its inaction in standing against the Serbs aggressions. He also condemned the failed attempt against the life of Hojjat ol-Eslam Abaie, susbstitute Friday prayer leader of Mashhad, by terrorist groups and called on the Information Ministry to take a serious stance towards the issue. "Those lackey munafiq (hypocrite) elements should know that by resorting to such assassination attempts and explosions they could not disappoint the ulema and the people to abandon their struggle and could not create a state of terror in the society," remarked the speaker.
