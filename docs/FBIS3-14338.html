Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 25 Mar (MENA)--An official Palestinian source has stated that the Palestinian and Israeli delegations reached a formula for an agreement at dawn today, during meetings held in Cairo. The formula explains the task of the Palestinians police force and the international observers in the city of Hebron. In a statement to MENA today, the Palestinian source pointed out that differences persist on certain sensitive points related to the security authority in Hebron and the role of international observers in establishing and maintaining security in the city. The source added that these disputed points, the number of Palestinian policemen and international observers, have been referred to the Palestinian and Israeli leadership for consideration and consultation. The Palestinian source noted that if an agreement is reached on these disputed issues, negotiations will resume in Cairo on Tuesday. The Palestinian side is requesting that the Palestinian police force and international observers include 1,000 men each. The Israeli side is absolutely opposed to this number.
