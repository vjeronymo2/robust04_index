BFN [Text] Warsaw, 16 May (PAP)--An intergovernmental agreement on "cooperation and exchange of information on some issues concerning defense" was signed today by Swedish Defense Minister Anders Bjorck and Poland's National Defense Minister Piotr Kolodziejczyk. Poland is the first country of the East bloc with which Sweden has signed such an agreement. Minister Bjorck pointed out that this transpires from the fact that Poland stands "in the forefront of the states of this part of Europe that have carried out democratic changes." So far, Poland has signed 21 agreements on military cooperation, among others, with all of its neighbors.
