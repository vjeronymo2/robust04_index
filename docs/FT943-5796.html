940902 FT 02 SEP 94 / First Chinese private bank approved China has approved its first private bank with the shareholders coming from domestic private companies, according to the official Xinhua news agency, Tony Walker writes. The People's Bank, China's central bank, gave its approval to the new institution as part of continuing reforms of the banking sector. China has been licensing some new banks, but they have been connected with public sector companies or municipalities. Mr Jing Shuping, chairman of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, said the new bank would not accept foreign shareholders, but it would act for foreign clients. Little detail of the new bank was provided in a brief Xinhua despatch, but Mr Jing said the bank's initial capital would be Yn2bn (Pounds 151.5m). The establishment of the privately owned bank was described as a 'significant experiment' in China's banking reforms.
