Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [From the "National Hookup" program] [Text] Liu Huaqing, member of the Standing Committee of the CPC Central Committee Political Bureau and vice chairman of the Central Military Commission, pointed out today: The Army must adhere to high standards in combating corruption and promoting ethical integrity. Leading cadres, particularly high-ranking cadres, should strictly implement the systems and rules and regulations of the CPC Central Committee and the Central Military Commission concerning the party style and the promotion of ethical integrity. They should set an example of serving the people wholeheartedly and abiding by discipline. Liu Huaqing made these remarks at the all-Army discipline inspection work conference. After expounding on the achievements made by the Army in promoting the party style and ethical integrity in 1993, he pointed out: We should grasp four links in this year's work. First, we should further enhance our ideological understanding and fully understand that the task of combating corruption and promoting ethical integrity has a bearing on the survival of the party and the country, and that it is a long-term task for the entire stage of socialism. Second, it is necessary to made a new breakthrough in investigating and handling major cases. Third, more efforts should be devoted to dealing with key issues. Fourth, new steps should be taken to improve relevant rules and regulations and to supervise and inspect their implementation. Liu Huaqing said: The party committees and leading cadres at all levels of the Army should set the example in promoting the party style and ethical integrity and should resolutely implement the decisions of the party Central Committee and the Central Military Commission. While we must seriously implement rules and regulations, we absolutely must not disregard orders and bans. We must truly identify ourselves with the party Central Committee politically and ideologically. In promoting the party style and ethical integrity, leading cadres should display a revolutionary devotion and sense of responsibility and set an example of performing more actual deeds than talking and of being as good as their word. Yu Yongbo, member of the Central Military Commission and director of the General Political Department, presided over the meeting today. Present at the meeting were Zhang Zhen, vice chairman of the Central Military Commission; Chi Haotian, Zhang Wannian and Fu Quanyou, members of the Central Military Commission; and leading comrades of the three general departments of the People's Liberation Army [PLA], all major PLA units in Beijing, and the Armed Police Force. The all-Army discipline inspection work conference opened on 25 February. Zhou Ziyu, deputy director of the General Political Department and secretary of the Central Military Commission Discipline Inspection Committee, delivered a report to the conference. Those in attendance included members of the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection serving in the PLA, and members of the Central Military Commission Discipline Inspection Committee, as well as secretaries of discipline inspection committees and directors of discipline inspection departments of major PLA units and the Armed Police Force headquarters.
