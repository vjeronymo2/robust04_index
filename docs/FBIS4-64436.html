BFN [Unattributed article: "G-7, Aid to Russia at Hata-Ciampi Talks"] [Text] Rome, May 3 (ANSA) -- The July summit of the seven major industrialized countries and economic aid to the former Soviet republics, and to Russia in particular, were the two main points discussed at a meeting here Tuesday [3 May] between Japanese Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata and caretaker Prime Minister Carlo Azeglio Ciampi, a government communique said. Hata, the communique said, confirmed Japan's full support of the approach given by the Italian chairmanship of the G-7 summit, to take place in Naples July 8-10, which will issue two separate economic and political statements as well as a "declaration" on security summing up talks held at the summit with Russian President Boris Yeltsin. Ciampi today reiterated his suggestion that the International Monetary Fund issue special drawing rights to support economic reforms in the former Soviet Union. The two government heads also discussed the situation in China, the communique said. After his meeting with Ciampi, the Japanese premier attended a working lunch with industrial leaders including Fiat Chairman Gianni Agnelli, Alitalia Chairman Renato Riverso and ENI [National Hydrocarbons Agency] Managing Director Franco Bernabe.
