BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- The Federal Government on Thursday [14 April] welcomed the reduction of the prime rate by the Bundesbank. The decision of the Central Bank Council to reduce the discount rate from 5.25 to 5 percent and the Lombard rate from 6.75 to 6.5 percent supports the Federal Government in its efforts for a further strengthening of the forces of growth, FRG Finance Minister Theo Waigel stated. Economics Minister Guenter Rexrodt called on all people involved in economic circles to further orient their decisions toward the goal of stability. The inflation rate in both parts of Germany, which has been constantly declining since the beginning of 1993, as well as the Federal Government's consistent adherence to its policy of consolidation and the course of this years wage negotiations, so far have made the current reduction of interest rates easier. The interest rate reduction will support the forces of upswing in the economy.
