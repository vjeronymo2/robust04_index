May 3, 1990, Thursday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: OAK PARK;

SCHOOLS WEIGH Y'S CHILD-CARE PROPOSAL

The Oak Park Unified School District is studying a proposal from the Triunfo YMCA to provide preschool and after-school child care for students in the Agoura area.

Officials from the YMCA, which covers Westlake Village, Agoura and Oak Park, presented the child-care proposal to the school district's Board of Trustees Tuesday night.

YMCA Executive Director Pete Regli said his organization would put a portable building behind the school district office on Conifer Street and would offer various programs at a cost comparable to other preschool and child-care programs. Regli said the program would offer sliding-scale payments to low-income parents.

The YMCA has not figured out how to come up with the initial financing for the preschool, Regli said.

"There is an up-front cost of about $40,000 that we can't afford right now," he said. "But the ongoing costs would be paid by the people who use the center."

The preliminary plan is to open a preschool, then expand to after-school child care, Regli said.

"We do get a lot of calls from people asking for preschool and child-care programs," he said. "We have a problem referring them because there just aren't that many preschools."

The proposal was generally well-received by the trustees, who asked Regli to return when he has firmer cost estimates and a more detailed plan for programs that would be offer.

The school district has 1,580 students at two elementary schools, a middle school and a high school. KAREN McKEAN
