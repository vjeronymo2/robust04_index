September 15, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

VOTERS FACE 28 INITIATIVES IN NOVEMBER

Gov. George Deukmejian is running around the state pitching his pet project of putting prison inmates to work in outside industry. He says it's a great way to make prisoners productive and also would help in their rehabilitation.

Don't be fooled.

What the Deukmejian proposal (Prop. 139) would really do is put felons in direct, unfair competition with the regular work force. Employers would be favorably disposed to hire them because of the tax incentives included in this measure, not to mention the advantages of having a cheap, submissive labor pool.

There's no evidence that this program would save taxpayers a dime. In fact, administration and enforcement of the law could be very costly, because each of California's 58 counties would be free to devise its own set of rules for using and supervising inmate labor. Prisoners would be exploited and workers who haven't committed a crime would be severely penalized if this amendment were adopted.

THOMAS J. VANDEVELD, President

United Food & Commercial Workers

Union, Local 135, San Diego
