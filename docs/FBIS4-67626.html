BFN [Article signed NS/WI: "1993 Swiss AIDS Statistics. 684 Persons Have Contracted the Virus, 2461 Have Died Since 1983."; as released by Bern ATSA/SDAA database] [Text] Bern, 31 Jan (ATS) -- A total of 684 new AIDS cases have been registered in Switzerland in 1993, compared to 650 the previous year. Of the 3,561 AIDS cases that have been recorded since 1983, 2,461 have died, the Federal Office of Public Health announced in its bulletin on Monday. 531 of the [1993] registered cases are men, while 153 are women. The main victims of the disease are drug addicts (293 cases), followed by gay and bisexual men (240). In addition, last year, 63 men and 49 women have been infected through heterosexual contacts. More than one third of all cases registered since 1983 are from the canton of Zurich (1287). Next are Geneva (531), Vaud (425), Bern (330), and Basel (277). Considering the total population figure, Geneva is the hardest hit canton with 124.5 cases per 100,000 inhabitants. For the entire country, 18,949 positive tests have been registered through the end of 1993. The strongest proportion of positive tests has been recorded in the canton of Zurich (32.8 percent of the total number).
