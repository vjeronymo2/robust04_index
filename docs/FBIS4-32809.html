BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the elections scheduled for 21 August. In Mexico City, officials from eight of the nine political parties have signed an Agreement for Electoral Transparency and Fairness. In the agreement, the parties committed to taking necessary steps to ensure that all stages of the electoral process are carried out within a framework of harmony, mutual respect, transparency, security, trust, and credibility. The director of the National Action Party (PAN) in Mexico City said: "Public funds should not be channeled to any political party." (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 20 May 94) Federal District Mayor Manuel Aguilera Gomez has told the leaders of the eight political parties who signed the Agreement for Electoral Transparency and Fairness that Mexico City will not be held "hostage" to conflict caused by political differences among the various parties. Aguilera Gomez said: "If we want to live in peace, we must adopt a policy of inclusion, not exclusion." (Mexico City UNOMASUNO in Spanish 10 May 94 pp 1, 11) The Chamber of Deputies has elected six new advisers who will form part of the Federal Electoral Institute's General Council in the upcoming 21 August elections. All of the advisers meet the legal requirement of not having belonged to or led any political party over the past three years. The main duty of these advisers will be to oversee the election's transparency and honesty. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 25 May 94) PAN candidate Diego Fernandez de Cevallos claims that ever since the 11 May debate, attempts to tarnish his image have increased. "I have been accused of being an attorney for drug traffickers, a member of Opus Dei, and a member of secret organizations," Fernandez said. He said there is an attempt to undermine his platform in which he places his trust for democracy's victory in Mexico. (Mexico City Canal 13 Television Azteca Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 25 May 94) PAN candidate Diego Fernandez de Cevallos has spoken out in favor of modifying the Federal Labor Law in an effort to modernize it without taking back gains made in the area of workers rights. He also said there is an urgent need to democratize labor unions. He said: "For the sake of protecting the workers, we must seek measures that protect jobs and create structures that do not hinder the hiring of workers. We know that many times false protectionism only leads to unemployment." (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 26 May 94) After a four-hour meeting, members of the Democratic Revolutionary Party's (PRD) National Executive Commission decided to accept a debate with Carlos Almaga, director of the National Voter Registration Office. The PRD will be represented by Samuel del Villar, and it was decided that the debate would last 53 minutes instead of 45, as had originally been proposed. The debate will take place at Federal Electoral Institute Headquarters. PRD leader Porfirio Munoz Ledo said: "Del Villar has a knowledge of the issues -- legal as well as technical knowledge -- and, therefore, we hope he can demonstrate what is evident: the fact that enormous errors exist in the electoral register." (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 26 May 94)
