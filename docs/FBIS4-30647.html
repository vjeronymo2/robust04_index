BFN [Excerpts] Jakarta, Jun 28 (OANA-ANTARA) -- President Suharto and visiting Australian Prime Minister Paul Keating agreed here Tuesday to work together to reduce Indonesia's trade deficit with Australia. Both leaders shared the view that bilateral trade ties between the two countries need to be intensified to cut the deficit, Minister/State Secretary Murdiono said when announcing the results of a two-hour talk between President Suharto and Prime Minister Paul Keating. Two-way trade between Australia and Indonesia is worth US $2.15 billion a year. In the meeting, which lasted 30 minutes longer than scheduled, the two heads of state also discussed the upcoming informal summit of APEC (Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation) to be held in Bogor, about 50 km south of Jakarta, on 15 November this year. The second APEC meeting will be attended by representatives from 18 countries, namely the ASEAN member states (Indonesia, Brunei Darussalam, Malaysia, Singapore, the Philippines and Thailand), the United States, New Zealand, Mexico, Papua New Guinea, Chile, Australia, China, Hong Kong, South Korea, Canada, Taiwan and Japan. Prime Minister Keating expressed his readiness to help Indonesia make the APEC meeting a success, Minister Murdiono said. [passage omitted -- covered in referent item] Minister Murdiono also confirmed that President Suharto explained about the recent revocation of the licences of TEMPO weekly magazine, EDITOR magazine and DETIK tabloid to his guest. Keating arrived here on Monday for a three-day visit to hold talks with President Suharto and other Indonesian officials. He is also scheduled to open a major Australian trade exhibition on Wednesday. The trade exhibition is a part of "Australia Today Indonesia '94", the largest trade and cultural promotion program Australia has ever undertaken in the region. It is scheduled to last until 3 July 1994. This is the third visit Keating has made to Indonesia since he became prime minister.
