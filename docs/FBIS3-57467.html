Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev, 26 Jan -- The foreign ministries of central and eastern European countries are holding multilateral working consultations on issues of regional security in Ukraine for the first time ever. They are discussing general issues of security in central and eastern Europe, in particular, Ukraine's initiative on setting up a zone of stable security in the region. During a conversation with a UNIAR correspondent, Ihor Kharchenko, chief of the Ukrainian Foreign Ministry's department of political analysis and planning, underscored that participants in the consultations support the signing of the trilateral declaration in Moscow and expressed hope that such consultations will be held in the future. It is worth mentioning that representatives of all the countries of eastern Europe are taking part in the consultations. The Czech Republic is taking part as an observer. Ukrainian Deputy Foreign Minister Borys Tarasyuk and Dmytro Pavlychko, chairman of the [Ukrainian parliament] foreign affairs commission, addressed participants in the working consultations and explained Ukraine's stand on issues of consolidating security in central and eastern Europe.
