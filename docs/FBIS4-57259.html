BFN [Text] Army troops have withdrawn from Federal Police [PF] buildings in Porto Alegre and Foz do Iguacu, but they are still occupying PF facilities in Sao Paulo. The order to withdraw from Foz do Iguacu came late in the afternoon. The 180 Army soldiers who have been guarding the Brazilian border with Paraguay for the past four days quickly boarded their trucks and returned to barracks. Army troops are still occupying the three PF buildings in Sao Paulo. Traffic remains closed and pedestrians have only limited access to the area. Army sentries continue to monitor the area from the roof of the main building. Although Planalto Palace authorized the withdrawal of troops, the Army will not withdraw in Sao Paulo until it becomes certain that the PF strikers will resume basic services. The Army southeast regional commander will decide when to withdraw the troops. Complying with orders from President Itamar Franco, Justice Minister Alexandre Dupeyrat will meet again tomorrow morning with members of the Chamber of Deputies National Defense Committee in an attempt to resume negotiations and end the PF strike.
