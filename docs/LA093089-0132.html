September 30, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY DIGEST: MISSION VIEJO;

POLICE CAPTURE MAN, 22, SUSPECTED IN BURGLARIES

Orange County sheriff's investigators said they arrested a 22-year-old janitor early Friday in connection with a string of burglaries in Mission Viejo that began Aug. 15.

Sheriff's spokesman Lt. Richard J. Olson said 11 deputies had put the home of Donald Alan Driver under surveillance Thursday night. Driver was captured shortly after midnight in the 27000 block of El Moro as he was allegedly rifling through a purse that later was determined to have been stolen from a nearby house earlier that night.

Driver was being held on suspicion of residential burglary, first-degree burglary and resisting arrest. Sheriff's officials said there have been nine thefts by a cat burglar in the Mission Viejo area between Aug. 15 and Sept. 21.

Olson said credit for the investigation goes to Deputy Neil Hansell, who is assigned to the Mission Viejo area. Hansell developed the lead that resulted in the department's staking out Driver's home, Olson said.
