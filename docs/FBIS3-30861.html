Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 9 February (MTI) -- Hungarian Foreign Minister Geza Jeszenszky on Wednesday [9 February] held talks with Ukrainian Minister of Defense Vitaliy Radetskyy in Budapest. Following the meeting, General Radetskyy told journalists that, among other things, they had discussed the NATO peace-partnership program. Hungary and Ukraine jointly signed the cooperation framework agreement documents on Tuesday [8 February]. Radetskyy said Hungary and Ukraine share the same evaluation of the essence of the peace-program, and its main elements. In answer to a question on Ukrainian-Russian relations, Radetskyy denied an increase in tension between Kiev and Moscow.
