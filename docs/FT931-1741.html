930324 FT 24 MAR 93 / Arts: Today's Television The popular perception of William Cody stems almost entirely from his famous Wild West Show which, for 30 years around the turn of the century, brought cowboys and indians to the cities of Europe. Timewatch (8.10, BBC2) digs out rare film footage and talks to some who knew Cody to re-evaluate the story of the man who was better known as Buffalo Bill. Joan Shenton who, via her Channel 4 programmes, has done more than anyone to undermine the dogma of the Aids fanatics, continues her work today in Dispatches (9.00). Goggle Eyes, described as a 4-part comedy drama, is adapted from Anne Fine's award winning novel about a one-parent family. Mother Rosie falls for an older 'rather conventional' man with glasses and the resultant mayhem is seen through the eyes of 12-year-old Kitty, played by the wonderfully named Honeysuckle Weeks (9.25 BBC2). With the incidence of skin cancer rising rapidly, QED investigates the causes: sunbathing and the thinning of the ozone layer (9.30 BBC1). BBC2 begins a new comedy written by the creator of Rab Nesbitt: I, Lovett. The central character is an eccentric inventor (10.05).
