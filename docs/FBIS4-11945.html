BFN [Text] An official spokesman in New Delhi today denied reports appearing in a section of the press that the Indian security agencies have sought an assurance from the LTTE [Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam] regarding the prime minister's security during his recent visit to Britain. He termed the reports as mischievous and baseless. The spokesman said there is absolutely no basis for reports of an alleged deal between the LTTE and the government in this regard. He said there is no question of lifting the ban on the LTTE imposed two years ago under the Unlawful Activities Prevention Act.
