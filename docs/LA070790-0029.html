July 7, 1990, Saturday, Valley Edition

PRESERVING FRYMAN CANYON

The Sierra Club has suggested that Fryman Canyon be a monument in order to preserve this unique piece of the Santa Monica Mountains' natural history, which contributed to the specific cultural history of the area in which this canyon exists.

The people of our city have a right to access our cultural history in monuments, as well as books, and the city recognized this almost 20 years ago, for example, when Stoney Point Outcroppings in north Chatsworth was designated due to its being "one of the most picturesque areas in Los Angeles."

This site is analogous to a giant redwood grove. Has familiarity blinded us? Is this one-of-a-kind remnant of our mountains' past natural state not worthy of our concern and protection? Or shall we guide it to extinction?

SUSAN GENELIN

Sherman Oaks
