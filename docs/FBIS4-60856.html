BFN [Text] Yerevan, April 29 (SNARK) -- The Yerevan center of the Armenian National Network of Data Transmission will allow Armenia to establish communication with 100 National centers in 42 countries of the world, members of the Association of International Communications. The Center, which will be opened by the end of May, will allow the 5 largest Armenian cities to receive and send information by fax, telex, teletype and E-mail by means of satellite communication. Afterwards, other settlements of Armenia will join the network. The press-service of the Armenian government told SNARK the Prime Minister Grant Bagratyan visited the Center today. Among other questions there was discussed a possibility of joining the network by the state banks, which will allow to create a united center of bank information.
