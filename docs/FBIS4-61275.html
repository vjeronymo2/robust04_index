BFN [Text] Moscow and Washington for the first time showed complete agreement on the necessity for an urgent international meeting on a settlement in Bosnia. This was announced by Kozyrev's Advisor Galina Sidorova when commenting on the meeting between Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S. secretary of State Warren Christopher in Cairo today. Sidorova said that they had discussed a possible meeting between the foreign ministers of the states involved in the settlement of the Bosnian crisis which might take place in Geneva in the near future. Sidorova said the terms were still being worked upon. For instance, Russia, the USA, three EU members -- France, Germany and Great Britain and international mediators Torvald Stoltenberg and Lord Owen are expected to participate. The final date will be defined during consultations between the future participants in the meeting. This meeting will open the way for a summit on a settlement in Bosnia with the same countries participating. Kozyrev and Christopher were deeply concerned over the situation around the Bosnian city of Brcko. The situation in Brcko calls for an agreement in order to put an end to all military actions on Bosnian territory, Sidorova said.
