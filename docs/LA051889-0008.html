May 18, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

THE BUTCHER: SPANISH CASSEROLE IS FAMILY FARE OR ELEGANT ENTREE

Bonifacio Guenechea is a very generous man and has some very special cooking ware. I first met Boni only a few months back when I borrowed three of his pans to cook at a benefit beach party for cystic fibrosis at my brother's home in the Florida Keys. Boni's pans (he calls them magic pans) are giant paella pans that come with their own butane burner and tripod, and are perfect for cooking for a crowd. With three of them, I cooked beef, chicken and Florida lobster for 250 people in just over an hour.

My brother called again last month, "Hey how about coming down and cooking for the Florida Keys Fishing Guides Assn.? They do a lot of conservation work and they are gonna be doin' a fund-raiser and thought maybe you'd help out. Whadda ya say! Maybe we could use Boni's pans again."

I called Boni.

"Sure," he replied, "you can use 'em. How many will ya be cookin' for?"

"I'm not sure," I said, "maybe 200, maybe 400."

"Tell ya what," he said, "I'll come down and help ya. I'll bring my big pan. We can do paella."

Paella's Spanish History

Boni's big pan is almost 4 feet in diameter and 6 inches deep -- big enough to cook paella for about 150 people at a time. Two batches was all it took. As we stood around his pan stirring, Boni told me a bit about the world-famous dish from his native Spain.

Paella gets its name from the pan it is cooked in. The pan is the paella. The dish can be made from a variety of different ingredients, meat, fish, poultry or a combination. The only ingredients that are traditionally used are olive oil, rice and saffron. Boni said he sometimes uses yellow food coloring in place of the very expensive saffron, "but it's not nearly as good." He also said that you don't have to have the traditional paella, you can make it in any casserole dish, "but it's not quite the same."

Paella can be simple, inexpensive family fare with chicken and pork, or elegant festive fare with lobster and stone crab. Either way, it's fun food. Boni sent me the following recipes to document paella's versatility.

PAELLA A LA AMERICANA

1/4 cup olive oil

1 green pepper, chopped

1 small onion, chopped

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 chicken, cut into several pieces

1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce

1 (8-ounce) can corn

1/4 teaspoon ground cumin

5 cups water

2 teaspoons salt

1 tablespoon yellow food coloring

2 cups rice

1 lemon

Pimientos

Preheat oil in paella pan over medium heat. Stir-fry green pepper, onion and garlic 2 minutes. Add chicken and brown evenly on both sides.

Add tomato sauce, corn and cumin. Stir 1 minute. Combine water, salt and food coloring and pour into pan over high heat. Once water begins to boil, add rice evenly to pan. Let boil 15 minutes.

Lower heat when rice begins to rise and cook 5 more minutes, then cover with foil 5 minutes before serving. Squeeze lemon on rice right before serving and garnish with pimientos. Makes 4 to 6 servings.

PAELLA MAGIC-PAN

1/2 teaspoon yellow food coloring

1 teaspoon paprika

1 teaspoon dried rosemary

1 teaspoon salt

2 small lobsters, cut in half

1 pound mussels

1 dozen clams

1 pound monk fish, cut in 2-inch chunks

1 pound shrimp, peeled

4 pieces stone crab claws

1 pound squid, cleaned and cut in strips

1/4 cup olive oil

1 medium onion, chopped

2 ripe tomatoes, chopped

1 garlic clove, minced

1 green pepper, chopped

2 cups rice

Pimientos

Fresh parsley

1 lemon

Bring 3 quarts water to boil in large pot. Add yellow food coloring, paprika, rosemary and salt. When boiling, add lobsters, mussels and clams. Boil 10 minutes. Remove and set aside.

Add fish, shrimp, stone crab claws and squid to water and bring water to boil again. Remove ingredients as soon as water boils. Set water aside for later use.

Preheat oil in pan over medium heat. Add onion, tomatoes, garlic and green pepper to pan. Stir-fry 5 minutes or until tender. Add rice and stir with vegetables 2 minutes.

Add 5 cups reserved water and cook over high heat 10 minutes. Add fish and seafood (except lobster and shrimp), and cook on high 10 more minutes.

Lower heat and place lobster on rice (shell side up) and decorate with shrimp, pimientos and parsley. Cook 5 minutes. Cover with foil and let stand 5 minutes before serving. Serve each portion with lemon slice. Makes 4 to 6 servings.
