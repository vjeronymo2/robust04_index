940304 FT 04 MAR 94 / Disqualification for 17 directors A total of 17 company directors were disqualified for up to 12 years during the final quarter of 1993, Department of Trade and Industry figures showed yesterday. Eight disqualifications arose from civil proceedings after confidential company investigations, and nine resulted from criminal proceedings following reports from Official Receivers. The DTI's investigations division dealt with 260 requests for investigations in the quarter, bringing the number for the year ending December 31 1993 to 1,086, an increase of 12 per cent on the previous year's 967. Of 260 requests, the division accepted 98 for confidential investigations.
