Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] At the request of the Prosecutor General's Office, the Russian Federal Counterintelligence Service (FCS) revealed the persons who had been spreading rumors about another coup in Russia, FCS Director Sergey Stepashin told a news conference in Moscow Friday. He declined to reveal their names saying "we will tell you more about this as soon as possible." In an answer to an Interfax question, Stepashin only indicated that four rumor-spreaders were identified. Their fate and location are now in the hands of the Prosecutor General's Office. Stepashin appreciated the calm reaction by the president, the prime minister and other people involved in the alleged coup and called on journalists to "approach fakes of all types in a more balanced manner." Among other reasons for the appearance of such rumors, Stepashin pointed to the intricacy of the economic situation, the amnesty and its political effects. He added that the authors of the scheme also wanted to see the reaction by the president, the power structures and persons mentioned in their speculations and set them against each other. They failed, Stepashin said.
