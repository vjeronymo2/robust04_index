Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Jaime Lopez Castaneda] [Excerpts] San Cristobal de las Casas, 15 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- The possibility of a dialogue to end the conflict seemed to be drawing closer on Saturday, 15 January, in the wake of the government offer to give "immediate attention" to any genuine proposal by the armed group operating in southeastern Mexico. Fifteen days into the conflict, this region of Los Altos de Chiapas -- approximately 200 km southeast of Mexico City -- was calm following a brief violation of the unilateral truce the government ordered 72 hours ago. [passage omitted] Following a three-hour meeting with President Carlos Salinas de Gortari in Mexico City last night, Manuel Camacho Solis, special commissioner for peace and reconciliation in Chiapas, said that negotiations in Chiapas are advancing although the problem is very complex. Without going into details, Camacho Solis said: "We are working well in a serious manner. It is a complex problem, but the approach is appropriate." On Friday a group of armed men broke the truce established by a presidential order to suspend military operations in Los Altos de Chiapas by attacking an army unit on a stretch of the highway to Oconsingo. There were no casualties. [passage omitted] Last night Camacho Solis announced that Bishop Samuel Ruiz of San Cristobal de las Casas will be the practical "liaison" to establish contact with the armed group. The clergyman, known as a strong defender of Indians in Chiapas, made an appeal "to seek alternative means" to solve the major, profound problems affecting the region. Camacho Solis offered to give his immediate attention to any genuine proposal made by the armed group through Bishop Ruiz, who last week agreed to a petition by the rebels to serve as mediator in the dialogue with the government. "Peace will be the result of a solution to the military and political problems, as well as of an orderly and institutional handling of the changes to be made in the region," the peace commissioner noted. "If Chiapas achieves reconciliation by taking steps forward, instead of being a problem for the country, it will provide a major contribution to the nation as a whole," Camacho Solis added. Los Altos de Chiapas is a region of 100 square km with a largely Indian population which, the government acknowledges, lives in extreme poverty. A communique by the Bishops Committee on Peace [Comision Episcopal para la Paz] announced that a mission of bishops will visit the conflict area to convince the armed militants to agree to a dialogue after laying down their weapons. [passage omitted on background material] Former U.S. Attorney General Ramsey Clark has also joined the repeated appeals for talks. He is a delegate of a human rights organization based in Southern California. [passage omitted on background material] Clark's visit coincided with a report by the National Human Rights Committee, headed by ombudsman Jorge Madrazo, that 60 percent of the cases reported as missing during the conflict have been resolved. Madrazo noted that out of 231 persons reported missing in the 10 days of fighting and attacks, 163 have been found.
