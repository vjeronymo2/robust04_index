Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of fileworthy reports on antinarcotics operations in countries covered by FBIS London Bureau from 4-9 February. Sources are given at the end of each report.

Germany
On 26 January, customs officers found 90 kilos of heroin, worth 9 million German marks, in a Turkish truck crossing the German-Czech border at Furth im Wald. According to a press release from the Higher Tax Office in Nuremberg, the drugs were in transit to the Netherlands. (Berlin DDP/ADN in German 1307 GMT 8 Feb 94)
Italy
Fifteen people were arrested in Milan as part of an anti-drug operation. Officers seized 16.5 kg of heroin, 2 kg of cocaine, and 400 million lire in cash. The operation, code-named "Felix" after the leader of the gang, was carried out by officers from the Porta Genova police station with the help of Criminalpol, the flying squad, and the Central Anti-Drugs Directorate. (Rome RAI Televideo Teletext in Italian 0852 GMT 8 Feb 94) Seven people, almost of all them foreign nationals living in Perugia, were arrested during an anti-drugs operation in which police seized seven kilograms of cocaine. The operation was coordinated by the Interior Ministry. (Rome RAI Televideo Teletext in Italian 0910 GMT 9 Feb 94)
United Kingdom
Customs officers seized 1.3 tonnes of herbal cannabis with a street value of 3 million pounds from a Dutch truck arriving at Dover. The driver ran off while the truck was being searched. (London PRESS ASSOCIATION in English 1914 GMT 7 Feb 94)
