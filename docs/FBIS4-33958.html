BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 9 June.

PRODUCTION
The Agriculture Ministry reported that agriculture and livestock production increased by 8.9 percent in March as compared with the same month last year. A total of 1,252,006 hectares were earmarked nationwide for the agricultural campaign from August 1993 to March 1994 -- 247,943 hectares more than in the 1992-1993 campaign. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 21 May 94 p B8) The National Industries Association reported that industrial production grew 14.6 percent in the first four months of the year as compared with the same period in 1993. Manufacturing industry increased 12.94 percent, and the primary sector, which includes mining, fishing, and construction, rose 17.96 percent. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p B3) The National Statistics Institute reported that the GDP rose by 13.8 percent in April and 10.7 percent for the January-April period, as compared with the same periods last year. The wholesale price index rose 0.3 percent. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 1)
INVESTMENT
The National Commission for Foreign Investments and Technology reported that foreign investments amounted to $1.484 billion through 15 May, and to $3.486 billion from December 1990 to 25 May 1994. Approximately $952.32 million were invested in the stock market. The mining and industrial sectors accounted for 34.2 percent and 32.7 percent of investments respectively. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 26 May 94 p B4)
FOREIGN CREDIT
Peru obtained today in Washington a credit line of $150 million from the World Bank to reorganize the transportation system. The loan agreement was signed by Peruvian Ambassador Ricardo Luna and by the vice president of the World Bank. The loan represents 62 percent of the project, which is estimated at $242 million and will also be financed by the Peruvian and German Governments. (Lima Radio Programas del Peru Network in Spanish 1800 GMT 3 Jun 94)
REVENUES
The National Tax Administration Superintendency reported that tax revenues rose by 4.228 billion new soles in the first four months of the year, an increase of 23.8 percent compared with the same period last year. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 27 May 94 p B9)
INFLATION
The Cuanto consulting firm reported that the May inflation rate was 0.7 percent, and the cumulative inflation rate 27.5 percent for the past 12 months and 8.24 percent so far this year. The Apoyo consulting firm reported that the inflation rate was 0.8 percent in May, 24.1 percent in the past 12 months, and 8.2 percent so far this year. House rental and fuels registered the highest increases, with 2.9 percent; clothing and shoes 2.8 percent; health 2.2 percent; and restaurants and other goods and services 2.0 percent. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 1 Jun 94 p 1)
PRECIOUS METALS
Approximately 8 metric tons of gold valued at $89 million are being smuggled into Brazil and Bolivia per year. The metal is sent to the city of Rio Branco in Brazil and to Puerto Pardo in Bolivia from Madre de Dios Department. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 2 Jun 94 p 1)
