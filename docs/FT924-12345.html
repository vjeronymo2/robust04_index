921021 FT 21 OCT 92 / Importer challenges GM over franchise IM GROUP, the privately owned UK importer of Isuzu vehicles, has launched a legal action in the US against General Motors, which holds a substantial minority stake in the Japanese vehicle maker, in an attempt to prevent GM taking away its UK franchise. It is understood that IM Group's Isuzu franchise agreement is due to expire at the end of 1993. In most other European countries GM, which holds a stake of more than 30 per cent in Isuzu, has already taken over its Japanese affiliate's import operations. GM has rebadged the Isuzu Trooper as the Opel Monterrey in most of Europe and would like to sell the vehicle under the Vauxhall badge in the UK. The IM Group is suing GM for wrongful interference in its UK Isuzu business. Mr Bob Edmiston, IM Group chairman, said: 'GM would like the franchise. We do not wish them to have it. There are legal proceedings in hand at the moment.'
