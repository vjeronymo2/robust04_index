Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku, December 27 (TURAN)--At the recent founders' conference of the Boz Gurd Party, Bahdiar Ahmedov, a professor of history from the Baku University, was elected chairman of the party yesterday. He was born in 1936 in the village of Govlar in the Tauzskiy region in the western part of the country. He graduated from the faculty of history of the Azerbaijan State University. He is a doctor of history, is married and has four children. I joined the democratic movement five years ago at its very inception, Ahmedov said. Many who have been taught the Turkic traditions by myself are among the members of the party. The professor believes that Abulfaz Elchibey and Iskander Hamidov are the most respectable politicians of Azerbaijan. After Iskander Hamidov's resignation from the post of minister of interior, I became his close confidant and supporter in the Boz Gurd movement. The theme of his masters' dissertation was Azeri-British relations in the 16th century.
