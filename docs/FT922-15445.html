920401 FT 01 APR 92 / Firms settle S&L suits ERNST & YOUNG, the second biggest US accounting firm, and Jones, Day, Reavis & Pogue, a Cleveland law firm, have become the latest in a series of US accountants and lawyers to agree multi-million dollar settlements of lawsuits from investors claiming their negligence contributed to the 1989 collapse of Mr Charles Keating's Lincoln Savings and Loan, Alan Friedman writes from New York. Ernst & Young is to pay Dollars 63m (Pounds 36.4m) and Jones Day Dollars 24m in the face of investor claims that their faulty professional services contributed to the Lincoln collapse, which cost US taxpayers an estimated Dollars 1.6bn. The firms settled out of court because a guilty verdict in a trial could result in higher damages.
