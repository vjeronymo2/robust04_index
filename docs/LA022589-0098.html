February 25, 1989, Saturday, Southland Edition

POLAND RIOT POLICE BREAK UP PROTEST

Riot police backed by water cannon, batons and tear gas grenades broke up a demonstration Friday by thousands of students protesting police brutality in the southern city of Krakow, witnesses said.

One student source said at least two people were hit with concussion grenades and hospitalized and about 10 were detained.

Student activist Jacek Plaza said about 4,000 people, most of them students, gathered in the market square of Krakow for a march to municipal headquarters with a petition demanding the punishment of police for beating demonstrators Feb. 17.

"Police barred the way and dispersed the crowd with water cannon, tear gas grenades and batons," he said. Other witnesses said the demonstrators had hurled stones at the police.
