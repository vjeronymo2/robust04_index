April 7, 1989, Friday, Orange County Edition

SADDLEBACK GAME SUSPENDED AFTER BRAWL

Saddleback College's baseball game against Riverside was suspended in the ninth inning Thursday after a bench-clearing brawl that lasted approximately five minutes, according to Saddleback assistant coach Charley Phillips.

The fight started with two outs in the top of the ninth inning and Saddleback leading, 8-3, at Riverside.

The brawl began after Saddleback outfielder Justin Pysar was pushed to the ground after being tagged out in a rundown between first and second, Saddleback Coach Rich Rommel said.

Both benches cleared and Saddleback's Larry Maddox punched Jackie Jempson, Phillips said.

Maddox, Jempson and a second Riverside player were ejected and the umpires suspended the game. All three players will be suspended for one game, Rommel said.
