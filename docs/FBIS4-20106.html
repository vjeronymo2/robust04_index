BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Excerpts] General Dogan Gures, chief of the General Staff, has proposed that the ground forces serving in Bosnia-Herzegovina under the United Nations be placed under NATO's command. The NATO Military Committee meetings, where the developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina were mainly discussed, have ended in Brussels. Gen. Dogan Gures, who attended the meetings, held a news conference in Brussels this morning. He pointed out that NATO is troubled about Bosnia-Herzegovina, and that the debates at the Military Committee focused on the respectability and prestige of the alliance. ANATOLIA reports that at his news conference, Gen. Gures pointed out that NATO is decisive about Bosnia-Herzegovina but that the political decision must be made by the United Nations. He also said that to establish peace in Bosnia-Herzegovina, the air force and navy are not enough, and that the ground forces in that country must be placed under NATO's command even if they remain under UN control. [passage omitted] Gen. Gures then left Brussels for Paris as the guest of his French counterpart.
