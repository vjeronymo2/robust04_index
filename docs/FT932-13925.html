930421 FT 21 APR 93 / UK Company News: Sir Owen Green receives 0.37% rise BTR has maintained its relatively austere attitude to executive remuneration by giving Sir Owen Green, who is about to retire as chairman of the acquisitive conglomerate, a pay rise of Pounds 804, writes Andrew Bolger. That 0.37 per cent increase brings his salary to Pounds 217,616 for 1992, a year in which pre-tax profits increased by 18 per cent to breach the Pounds 1bn barrier for the first time. Mr Alan Jackson, the chief executive who successfully integrated Hawker Siddeley, received a more substantial increase of Pounds 18,504 to Pounds 272,590, a rise of 7.2 per cent. Sir Owen, 68, uses his last report to shareholders to get in another dig at the Cadbury report on corporate governance, which he has dismissed as 'wishy-washy'. He has been sceptical of Cadbury's emphasis on the importance of non-executive directors and failure to outlaw practices such as combining the role of chairman and chief executive. The annual report states: 'The introduction of a code bearing on this one facet of the corporate crystal, however well-intentioned, cannot ensure integrity or competence - the twin towers of an enduring business structure. Caveat investor.'
