Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih has stated that Sudan's foreign relations with neighboring countries rely on firm principles, which are based on respect for existing regimes and borders and noninterference in internal affairs. He pointed out that there were some powers which did not approve of the stability of Sudan and which strove to disrupt its relations with its neighbors. He said this yesterday when he met a Chinese media delegation. The foreign minister pointed out the presence of foreign interference, which sought to disrupt good relations with fraternal Eritrea. He stressed that there was a need to be wary of such plots. During the meeting, Dr. Abu-Salih reviewed Sudan's efforts to achieve an Arab accord. His excellency appealed to African countries to rely on themselves to develop their own capabilities. He pointed out that the stands of western countries against Sudan came about because of Sudan's independent decision-making. He confirmed that Sudan would be turning toward the east and China to achieve its independence.
