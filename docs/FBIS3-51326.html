Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Vector 21" newscast] [Excerpts] The U.S. trade representative [Mickey Kantor] and the Canadian minister of international trade [Roy MacLaren] agreed at a news conference that the Mexican Government alone must resolve the armed conflict in Chiapas State by acting wisely to restore social peace. MacLaren said the Mexican Government must be cautious to achieve a peaceful solution. [Begin MacLaren recording] I hope the Mexican Government's good sense and wisdom will lead to the prompt resolution of the conflict in Chiapas. [end recording] Mickey Kantor said his government has no reason to meddle in the internal affairs of other nations, but nothing can prevent the United States from observing the events that take place in other nations. [passage omitted] Jaime Serra Puche, Mexican Secretary of Commerce and Industrial Development, was also present at the news conference and said that a society can progress only if there is social peace. [Begin Serra Puche recording] Every sector of Mexican society should try to create a harmonious atmosphere that will allow our society and economy to operate in peace and stability and sufficiently free from concern so we can enjoy continued progress and growth and improve the distribution of our country's wealth. Every Mexican must make this effort. [end recording] Aside from political issues, the commerce secretaries announced that this was the first meeting held since NAFTA went into effect and that it marked the inauguration of the Free Trade Agreement Commission, which will be headed by the Mexican commerce secretary, the U.S. trade representative, and the Canadian international trade minister. The commission will be based in Mexico. Plans for opening other commissions were also set into motion. Serra, Kantor, and MacLaren agreed to meet on 15 April in Morocco within the framework of the conclusion of the GATT Uruguay Round. The officials also discussed the possible participation of other nations in NAFTA, but no conclusions were reached.
