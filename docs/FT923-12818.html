920716 FT 16 JUL 92 / International Company News: SEC adopts new powers THE Securities and Exchange Commission, the US financial markets watchdog, yesterday took on new powers that would make it easier for the agency to discover whether US securities firms are in financial trouble, writes Patrick Harverson. The new rule gives the SEC the right to demand from holding companies information about their securities subsidiaries, even if the parent is not regulated by the agency. The SEC hopes the rule will allow it to discover if a parent company is having financial problems that could affect a securities firm. The agency was granted authority to institute the new rule in a reform bill prompted by the collapse of Drexel Burnham Lambert. Under the rule, parent companies of securities firms must file quarterly financial statements giving information on its, and subsidiaries', exposure to a variety of assets, including securities and commodities, real estate holdings, and off-balance sheet derivatives such as interest-rate swaps.
