Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Prague, January 10 (CTK) -- The United States would welcome a common standpoint from the Visegrad countries (the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Poland, and Hungary) on the Brussels NATO summit, U.S. Ambassador to the UN Madeleine Albright said today after meeting Czech Foreign Minister Josef Zieleniec. Also present was John Shalikashvili, chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff of the U.S. Armed Forces. [passage omitted] Albright and Shalikashvili informed Czech representatives on the political principles and individual aspects of the U.S. Partnership for Peace initiative, Czech Foreign Minister Josef Zieleniec said after a meeting with them today. He stressed that the Czech Republic considered Partnership for Peace as a project which could bring new impulses to the creation of European security framework. The Czech Republic intends to show its partners that it is not striving just to acquire unilateral security guarantees, but that it is able to assume the relevant share of responsibility, Zieleniec said. Zieleniec called the American initiative an important step which opened up the way for the Czech Republic to NATO. [passage omitted]
