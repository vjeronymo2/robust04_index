920624 FT 24 JUN 92 / Shift in education funding signalled THE government intends to link some state funding of further education colleges directly to an expansion of student numbers when it takes over funding of the sector next year, a CBI conference on education was told yesterday, Andrew Adonis writes. Sir John Caines, permanent secretary at the education department, told delegates that governing councils of the colleges would assume control of their management - but core funding would come via an quango. He drew a parallel with polytechnics, whose rapid expansion in the past two years was stimulated by funding incentives. The 418 further education colleges and 115 sixth-form colleges in England and Wales are to be transferred from local authority administration next spring. 'As independent institutions they (colleges) should be able to respond more quickly and flexibly than before,' said Sir John. 'Colleges will have a direct financial incentive to expand participation. Funding will include an element directly related to student numbers.'
