941124 FT 24 NOV 94 / Mexico's deputy attorney general resigns Mr Mario Ruiz Massieu yesterday resigned as Mexico's deputy attorney general after accusing leaders of the ruling Institutional Revolutionary party of a cover-up in investigating the murder of his brother Francisco, writes Ted Bardacke in Mexico City. The IPC stock market immediately fell 3.3 per cent before recovering to close 1.4 per cent down. Mr Ruiz Massieu also quit as lead investigator in the murder of his brother, the PRI's secretary general. Mr Massieu claimed he had proof Mr Humberto Benitez, attorney general, Mr Ignacio Pichardo, PRI president, and Ms Maria de los Angeles Moreno, ex-majority leader in Congress, had conspired to cover up participation of PRI members.
