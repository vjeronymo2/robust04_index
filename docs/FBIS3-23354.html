Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Hague, 22 Mar (EFE) -- Surinamese President Ronald Venetiaan expressed his willingness to hold a dialogue with the rebels who seized a hydroelectric dam and are holding 30 workers hostage, the Dutch agency ANP reported from Paramaribo today. On 21 March, 100 guerrillas of the self-styled Liberation Front of Suriname seized the Afobaka Dam, located 100 km south of Paramaribo, and mined its main access roads. The rebel group is holding 30 workers of the Suralco mining company hostage and threatening to blow up the dam at 1400 today Dutch time (1300 GMT), leaving Paramaribo without electricity, if the government does not resign. In addition to President Venetiaan's resignation, the Front demands a broad program to improve "the critical economic situation" as well as health and education services for the population. The group is also asking for the withdrawal of a Canadian company that extracts gold in various concessions in the interior of the country and that the mining rights be granted to Surinamers. In an appeal to the population, Venetiaan asked for a dialogue with the rebels to reach a peaceful solution to the conflict, ANP revealed from the former Dutch colony in northern South America. The president added, however, that violence does not solve anything and that "terrorism will never force me to resign."
