Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] The prime ministers of the Republics of Croatia and Slovenia, Nikica Valentic and Janez Drnovsek respectively, signed in Zagreb six agreements on cooperation in various fields. Intensive talks over the past few weeks resulted in the regulation of future relations between the two countries in the sphere of education, culture, employement, and judiciary, cooperation in sea fishing, and economic and trade relations. Ater the talks between the two prime ministers and their associates and after the agreements were signed in Banski Dvori in Zagreb, both prime ministers expressed satisfaction with the progress made in relations between the two countries. According to them, today's talks are the result of several months of hard work and meetings between experts and state officials. At the same time, the talks are an added impetus for solving the remaining problems. Both also said that there are no disputed issues or unsolvable problems beween the two countries. The Croatian prime minister also said that it was understandable that both countries were interested in cooperation because we are encouraged to cooperate by tradition and other interests. Slovene Prime Minister Janez Drnovsek added that both countries would benefit from more intense cooperation, especially in the field of economy.
