May 7, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

AUSTRALIA;

NEW TRAIN COLLIDES WITH OLD, KILLING 6

A modern, double-decker passenger train slammed into the back of an old-fashioned steam train that apparently stalled going up a steep hill.

At least six people were reported killed and 90 injured. The crash occurred in a remote area about 25 miles north of Sydney. The trains were carrying about 430 people.
