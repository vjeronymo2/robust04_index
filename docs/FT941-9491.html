940215 FT 15 FEB 94 / Patten in plea for human rights Hong Kong's Governor Chris Patten yesterday made an impassioned plea for human rights to remain the major issue as Asian countries meet the 'political challenges of economic success,' AP reports from Sydney. He found the argument that human rights were relative and some could be suspended and make way for others unacceptable. 'Are some Asian leaders really prepared to argue that respect for human dignity is an exclusively European or North American or Australian concept?' he asked. 'I don't believe for a moment that human rights stop at the borders of Asia; nor, do I believe, do most Asians.'
