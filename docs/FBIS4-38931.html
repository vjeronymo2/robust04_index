BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anatoliy Yurkin] [Text] Moscow June 2 TASS -- Abkhazia is prepared for stationing peacemaking forces, press service of Abkhazian Parliament said on Thursday. In accordance with ceasefire agreement signed on May 14, the two belligerents are now busy withdrawing their forces from the Ingur river banks which form the stand-off line and will soon be turned into a buffer zone, where some three thousand peacemakers from the CIS member states will be stationed. Visiting Lieutenant Colonel Aleksandr Sokolov, Russian deputy commander-in-chief of overland troops, met with Abkhazian Parliament Speaker Vladislav Ardzinba, the press service said. He visited the Gal district, where CIS peacemaking forces will be deployed on June 15. Abkhazia considers the peacemakers to be guarantors of peace and stability in the region.
