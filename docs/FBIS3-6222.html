Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 14 KYODO -- The administrative reform headquarters of Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa on Monday [14 March] held its first meeting to discuss how to relax government regulations in information and telecommunications fields. The premier urged an affiliated working group on information and telecommunications to propose ways to ease regulations in the two fields, Chief Cabinet Secretary Masayoshi Takemura told reporters. The panel was asked to report as soon as possible. The group will meet once or twice a month and is expected to make its proposals by the end of June. The next meeting is scheduled for early April. The group will weigh the possibility of developing an optical fiber-based communications network modeled after the national information infrastructure program of the U.S. Government, said Takeshi Fujii, chief of the Cabinet Councillor's Office on Internal Affairs.
