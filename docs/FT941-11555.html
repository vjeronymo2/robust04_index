940203 FT 03 FEB 94 / International Company News: Sears Canada swings back to the black Canada's biggest department store retailer, Sears Canada, turned round in the final quarter of 1993, putting the chain into the black for the first time in two years. The company, which operates 110 stores and about 1,500 catalogue businesses, has undergone a substantial restructuring. Net profit in the December quarter was CDollars 50.6m (USDollars 38.1m), or 53 cents a share, against a loss of CDollars 3.7m, or 4 cents, a year earlier, on revenues little changed at CDollars 1.3bn. Profit for all 1993 was CDollars 4.4m, or 5 cents, against a loss of CDollars 90.9m, or CDollars 1.44, on revenues of almost CDollars 4bn, little changed.
