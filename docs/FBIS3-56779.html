Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Republic of Georgia Cabinet of Ministers has adopted a decree on the supply of fuel and electric power to economic complexes and the population during the first three months of 1994. According to this decree, a reduction in the supply of thermal power and the economical use of existing fuel and power resources are viewed as a task of particular importance. The appropriate institutions and departments are entrusted with reducing street lighting and all kinds of neon advertising by 50 percent and regulating independent sources of electric power. According to the decree, schedules for the supply, production, use, and allocation of gas and electric power have to be established. The 5,000 tonnes of (?kerosene) that has arrived in the form of humanitarian aid from Japan is to be taken into consideration. It is stated in the decree that, in addition to local administrative bodies, the Republic of Georgia Ministry of Internal Affairs and the Ministry of Defense should ensure the protection of electric power installations in Georgia.
