BFN [Text] Jakarta, June 28 (OANA-ANTARA) -- The government has set up an inter-ministerial commission to deal with Acquired Immunity Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS), the deadly disease which is also on the rise in Indonesia, it was announced here Tuesday. Secretary of the Office of the Coordinating Minister of People's Welfare Suyono Yahya said the commission -- created by a presidential decree, bore the task of fighting the spread and "not the victims" of the incurable disease. The commission would launch programmes to fight AIDS not only at national and provincial levels but at district level as well, Yahya said. He said the commission's programmes would emphasize efforts at preventing people from contracting HIV, the virus that leads to AIDS. The commission would also disseminate information on AIDS designed to let the public have the right knowledge and understanding of the disease. This was necessary to prevent the public from unduly becoming restive when encountering or having to live with actual AIDS cases. Coordinating Minister for People's Welfare Azwar Anas is chairman of the commission while four other cabinet ministers, namely Health Minister Suyudi, Religious Affairs Minister Tarmizi Taher, Social Affairs Minister Inten Suweno and State Minister for Population Affairs Haryono Suyono will be members. The following cabinet ministers are also involved in the commission: Information Minister Harmoko, Tourism, Post and Telecommunications Minister Joop Ave, Home Affairs Minister Yogi S. Memed and Youth and Sports Minister Hayono Isman.
