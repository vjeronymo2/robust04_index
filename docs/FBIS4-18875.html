BFN [Text] Bonn -- Defense Minister Volker Ruehe (Christian Democratic Union) welcomed his Ukrainian counterpart Vitaliy Radetsky at the Defense Ministry with military honors this morning. Radetsky has started a three-day visit of the Bundeswehr in Germany. He wants to continue the dialogue on security policy that Ruehe initiated with a visit to Ukraine last year. At the time, the two ministers signed an agreement on military cooperation between the Ukrainian and German Armed Forces. Immediately after the welcoming ceremony, the ministers began their exchange of views on the continuation of the contacts. They also discussed the development of a European security structure and cooperation between Kiev and NATO. Ukraine belongs to the 14 East European countries that have voted in favor of close military and political cooperation with the Western alliance as a result of the NATO initiative Partnership for Peace. In the next few days, Radetsky will also visit Bundeswehr units in Nirvenich and Hemer.
