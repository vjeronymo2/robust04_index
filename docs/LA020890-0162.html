February 8, 1990, Thursday, Southland Edition

NATION IN BRIEF;

MASSACHUSETTS;

SEIZURE DRUG FOUND TO LOWER YOUTHS' IQS

A drug widely used to treat babies who have convulsions during high fevers can at least temporarily lower their IQs and should not be routinely used, researchers have concluded in a study published in the New England Journal of Medicine. The medicine, phenobarbital, is the drug of choice for infants with so-called febrile seizures. The researchers found a small but measurable decline in the IQs of youngsters who took the medicine for two years.
