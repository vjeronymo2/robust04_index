BFN [Text] Aden, Yemen, June 23 (AFP) -- The bombardment of Aden by northern forces resumed at dawn Thursday, with shells and missiles wounding 11 civilians, the city's main hospital said. As artillery duels between north and south continued near Aden, three southern soldiers were reported killed and 28 wounded. Nine residents of the northern district of Shaykh 'Uthman were wounded by shells falling on the city itself at the rate of two or three a minute, mainly landing near the airport, the al-Jumhuriyah hospital said. A southern military spokesman said three missiles were fired at Aden, two of which were brought down by anti-aircraft fire. The third exploded on the hills overlooking the town, near the al-Tawahi district. The hospital said two women were wounded in the blast, which badly damaged a number of wooden homes in the district.
