May 18, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

THE NATION

A Senate panel approved a uniform poll-closing time of 10 p.m. EST for presidential elections in an effort to end network projections of results before all voters have cast their ballots. "I believe early projections before the polls close threaten the right of some voters to go to the polls and feel their votes count," said Sen. Brock Adams (D-Wash.) after the 9-2 vote by the Senate Rules and Administration Committee. The poll-closing bill was approved as part of a package of unrelated measures, including a blueprint for having eight regional presidential primaries around the country with the Federal Election Commission determining the order of the events by lot.
