Language: Vietnamese Article Type:BFN [Text] The Criminal Court of the Supreme People's Court entered its third day of work in Hanoi today. The case in this trial, which is preliminary as well as final, involves roundabout dealings with 4,000 tons of steel designed for the construction of the north-south 500-kilovolt powerline. These dealings caused 3.1 billion dong in losses to the state budget. This afternoon -- after two and a half days of interrogating the suspects, witnesses, and some relevant public organs -- Mr. Nguyen Tien Dam, a senior officer who represented the Supreme People's Organ of Control at the trial, presented a conviction report. Afterward, lawyers continued their defense of the suspects in this case. The court will continue its session on Monday, 21 February.
