Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Morning Information Review" newscast] [Text] Carlos Lage, vice president of the Cuban Council of State [as heard], arrived today in Rio de Janeiro as part of the last stage of his official visit to Brazil. Shortly after arriving, Lage met with Rio de Janeiro business representatives. During a news conference that took place shortly before leaving Brasilia, the Cuban vice president described talks with Brazilian President Itamar Franco, Foreign Minister Celso Amorim, Finance Minister Fernando Henrique Cardoso, and Health Minister Henrique Santillo as positive. Lage remarked that during the interview with President Franco they discussed issues related to enhancing political and economic relations between Cuba and Brazil. He highlighted that the Brazilian president reiterated his criticisms of the U.S. blockade against Cuba and the so-called Torricelli Bill just as Foreign Minister Amorim had done. The Cuban vice president explained that his country offers opportunities for foreign investments especially in the tourism sector. He also pointed out that businessmen who decide to invest in Cuba will be able to recover it in a short period of time.
