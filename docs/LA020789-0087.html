February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

U.S. PROBES FASTENER MAKER IN CHATSWORTH;

FBI LOOKING INTO TEST DATA SUPPLIED BY VSI

Federal agents spent nearly 48 hours this weekend searching VSI Aerospace Products in Chatsworth, a unit of Fairchild Industries, in connection with a criminal investigation into improper testing of nuts, bolts and rivets that the company produces for aircraft.

An agent at the Federal Bureau of Investigation in Seattle, where the investigation is headquartered, said the search of VSI was seeking evidence that the company supplied "false test data" for fasteners.

The fasteners, which are sold under the original company name Voi-Shan, are used various military and commercial passenger aircraft. Some of the products include high-strength cadmium-plated fasteners that require sophisticated tests to ensure that they meet detailed technical requirements for aerospace products.

Declined Comment

Allegations against Voi-Shan include a report that the company supplied test data for its products that indicated they were examined by "inspector No. 11." An FBI agent said, "It is alleged that there is no inspector No. 11."

A spokesman for Fairchild, which is based in Chantilly, Va., declined to comment. Local company executives declined comment as well.

Federal agents arrived at the company's Chatsworth facility at 4:45 p.m. Friday and did not leave until 3:30 p.m. Sunday. They reportedly brought along computer equipment to log documents that they were taking.

The task force of federal agents also included representatives of the Naval Investigative Service, the Air Force's Office of Special Investigations, the Defense Criminal Investigative Service and the Internal Revenue Service.

The involvement of IRS agents suggests that financial irregularities may also be under investigation, but the FBI declined to comment on the scope of the investigation. An FBI agent declined to identify the person or persons making the allegations.
