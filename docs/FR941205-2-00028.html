Recombinant Vaccinia Virus Encoding Cytochromes P&hyph;450 Gelboin, H.V., Battula, N., Gonzalez, F.J., Moss, B. (NCI, NIAID) Serial No. 07/787,777 filed 6 Nov 91 U.S. Patent 5,164,313 issued 17 Nov 92 Serial No. 08/166,287 Filed 13 Dec 93 [FWC of 07/930,781 (Aban), which is DIV of 07/787,777] This invention describes the construction and uses of recombinant vaccinia viruses containing DNA sequences that express enzymatically active cytochromes P1&hyph;450 and P3&hyph;450 in mammalian cells without requiring the extraneous addition of NADPH cytochrome P450 reductase or cell fractions for catalytic activity. This novel recombinant virus can be used to evaluate the cytochrome P&hyph;450-mediated metabolism and mutagenicity of xenobiotic or endobiotic compounds and chemical agents. The invention represents the first expression of cytochrome P&hyph;450 in a variety of mammalian systems using infectious viruses.
