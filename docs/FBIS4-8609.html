BFN [Text] Doboj, April 14 (TANJUG)--Bosnian Moslem forces on Thursday twice shelled the Serb-controlled town of Doboj in north-eastern Bosnia-Herzegovina. About twenty shells hit the town in this former Yugoslav republic but there have been no reports about possible casualties. In the meantime, Moslem-controlled radio of the autonomous province of western Bosnia reported that eight soldiers of the Fifth Corps loyal to the Sarajevo authorities had been killed and 15 wounded during heavy inter-Moslem clashes in the Cazinska Krajina region in western Bosnia on Thursday morning. The source also said that three soldiers of the autonomous province had been killed. Fierce attacks, supported by artillery fire, launched against positions of units loyal to Moslem leader Alija Izetbegovic continued in the area of Velika Kladusa and Cazin on Thursday afternoon, Moslem Radio-Sarajevo reported. Two people were killed and 25 wounded in the attacks according to the same source. Autonomous province of western Bosnia forces are destroying everything in their way with multi-barrelled missile launchers Osa and Zolja, Radio Sarajevo said. The autonomous province was proclaimed last year by Moslems--followers of Fikret Abdic, who oppose Izetbegovic's war-mongering policy. The command in Sarajevo admitted that about 500 troops had been killed and more than 2,000 wounded during the eight months of inter- Moslem clashes. The provincial army announces a counter-offensive to liberate Cazin, which is held by Izetbegovic's loyalist.
