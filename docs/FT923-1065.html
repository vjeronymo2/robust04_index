920925 FT 25 SEP 92 / World Commodities Prices: Market report ZINC moved into backwardation again on the LME in an otherwise routine trading day for the base metals. Dealers said cash zinc closed Dollars 9.50 ahead of three-month metal, compared with a discount of Dollars 5 on Wednesday. Although fears of an October squeeze have faded, the market will remain susceptible to pockets of technical tightness. COPPER closed steady. Chinese buying in the morning aided sentiment, and dips towards Dollars 2,380 for three-month metal are likely to encounter support. After the close, news emerged that Peru's Tintaya copper mine unions have voted to strike by 1 October. Three-month TIN prices continued to drift downwards towards the Dollars 6,500 a tonne chart objective. London COCOA remained under pressure from talk of Ivorian selling. 'With the calmer tone in the currency markets we're finding the cocoa fundamentals are playing their part again,' one dealer said. New York COTTON futures were sharply lower at midday. One analyst said the bears continued to be in charge. 'The fact that there is excess cotton in mainland China, and the uncertainty as to what they are going to do with it, is hanging heavily over the world market.'
