October 19, 1989, Thursday, South Bay Edition

SATURDAY'S HEROES

Following are last week's top collegiate football performances by South Bay high school graduates:

Leroy Hall, USC, senior: The former Banning fullback rushed for a career-high 160 yards on 21 carries and scored on a 55-yard run in a 31-15 win over UC Berkeley.

Dan Speltz, Cal State Fullerton, senior: The former Rolling Hills quarterback completed 17 of 22 passes for 228 yards and one touchdown in a 35-26 victory over Pacific. He ranks fourth in the nation in passing, averaging 152.5 yards a game.

Ken Sale, Texas-El Paso, senior: The former Bishop Montgomery linebacker has a team-high 11 tackles in 26-7 win over New Mexico.

Dominic Cefalone, Texas-El Paso, junior: The former West Torrance kicker had field goals of 45, 30 and 35 yards in the Miner's win.

Errol Sapp, Arizona, freshman: The former Carson running back gained 77 yards on 12 carries and scored the Wildcats' final touchdown on a 14-yard run in a 42-7 win over UCLA.

Brian Treggs, UC Berkeley, sophomore: The former Carson wide receiver caught three passes for 129 yards, including touchdowns of 75 and 14 yards, in a loss to USC.

Pee Wee Smith, Miami, senior: The former Gardena wide receiver had a 63-yard punt return in a 48-16 win over San Jose State.
