940129 FT 29 JAN 94 / Finance and the Family: Caution reigns - Directors' transactions As the market climbed to new heights, directors remained on the sidelines, particularly at the beginning of the week. Activity was marked by a number of deals in companies with large market capitalisations. Sir Allen Sheppard, chairman of Grand Metropolitan, sold 80,000 ordinary shares. There have been several option-related transactions recently but this was the first deal in ordinaries. The sales accounted for a quarter of Sir Allen's holding. Osprey Communications was noted earlier this year when Ronald Sidaway bought 125,000 shares at 41p. He has continued to buy at prices as high as 49p; the shares are trading now at about 53p. ASTEC (BSR), now based in Hong Kong, is involved in designing and making power conversion products and other electronic equipment. Technical director Neal Stewart sold his entire holding at 85.5p, although he retains some incentive shares. Correction: Last week, we reported a sale of 35,000 shares by three directors of Blacks Leisure.
