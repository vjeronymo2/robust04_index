BFN [Text] Beijing, May 18 (XINHUA) -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin said here today that closer cooperation in the field of petroleum between China and Saudi Arabia, two major oil producers, will benefit both of them. Jiang said this when meeting with Hisham Nazir, minister of petroleum and mineral resources of Saudi Arabia, here this morning. On bilateral relations, Jiang said that the two countries have been enjoying very sound relations, and marked progress has been made in the cooperation and exchanges in various fields since 1990, when diplomatic ties were established between them. China and Saudi Arabia share many common interests in safeguarding world peace and promoting world development, Jiang said. A sustained friendship and cooperation based on the five principles of peaceful co-existence are not only in line with common wishes of the two peoples, but beneficial to world peace and development, he said. During the meeting, Nazir said that the Saudi Arabian Government is willing to strengthen its cooperation with China in various fields, and especially supports Saudi Arabian oil enterprises' cooperation with Chinese partners. The minister also passed on to the Chinese president a signed letter from King Fahd of Saudi Arabia. Extending his thanks for the letter, Jiang asked the minister to convey his regards to the Saudi Arabian king and invited the king to visit China at a proper time. In the afternoon, Chinese Vice-Premier Zou Jiahua met with the minister and his party.
