Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, January 11 (SNARK) -- Today the private exchange offices stopped the operations of exchanging of Russian rubles for Georgian currency because coupons dropped down. The high-price goods disappeared from the shops. At the last auction of Interbanking Exchange of National Bank of Georgia there was the rate -- 100 Georgian coupons for 1 Russian ruble. Comparing with the previous auction the rate is dropped down 20 points. Specialists connect the collapsing dropping down of coupons with the recent statement of Vakhtang Goguadze, the speaker of Georgian Parliament, about necessity of Georgia's entering to the ruble zone.
