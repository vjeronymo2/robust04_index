Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Ignacio Gutierrez A.] [Excerpt] Another sizable drug shipment, this time totaling three tons and 876 kg, was confiscated yesterday by federal agents assigned to the Office of the Attorney General of the Republic [PGR] and detailed to Nuevo Leon State. With other seizures made recently, this brings the figure for the week to 10 tons. The total worth of these drugs is approximately 250 million new pesos. On this occasion, the Federal Judicial Police agents captured 10 presumed drug dealers who were about to move the drugs to the United States. Of the five vehicles seized from them, four were escorting a trailer truck carrying the marijuana under a shipment of fertilizer. The PGR's branch office in that state reported that, after a series of investigations, it was learned that a group of drug traffickers intended to make the move in the vicinity of the highway leading from Monterrey to Reynosa, Tamaulipas. Arrested on that highway, near the La Tortola ranch, were Fernando Ruiz Salgado, Gumaro Garza de la Garza, Juan Ariel Lopez Moreno, and Jorge Antonio Gomez Garza. They were riding in a Grand Marquis vehicle without license plates, forming the surveillance convoy for the large marijuana shipment. Also arrested were Jaime Ortiz Garcia, Edgar Moreno Silva, and Maximo Avila Murillo, riding in a Cutlass sedan, and Edmundo Canales Rodriguez, all of whom were engaged in surveillance. Two Nissan compact cars were seized as well. The last one to be arrested, Jaime Ramirez Gonzalez, was driving the red tractor trailer truck with treads. Realizing that he had been detected, he attempted to escape with the truck; however, after a chase along the highway, he was forced to stop. When the vehicle was searched, hundreds of packages wrapped in self-stick plastic, with a gross weight of three tons and 876 kg, were discovered. The Federal Public Prosecutor's Office indicated that, within the next few hours, the decision would be made for the remand of these individuals to the pertinent district court, based on their presumed guilt of committing various crimes against health. An investigation is also being made to determine the possible participation of Jose Luis Antu Martinez, who attempted to obstruct the federal agents' work. [passage omitted]
