Language: Bulgarian Article Type:BFN [Text] On the basis of its verdict pronounced on 8 February, a five-member panel of the Supreme Court, chaired by Court Deputy Chairman Melkon Melkonyan, has suspended the resolution adopted by the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Bulgaria on 16 and 20 December 1993 on dismissing the following members of the Privatization Agency Supervisory Board: Nikola Katsarski, Yonko Kalpazanov, Dimitur Saraivanov, Todor Borilkov, and Lazar Buchvarov. The Supreme Court established that the Council of Ministers has once more acted illegally by committing a breach of the Constitution and of legal acts. The Council of Ministers has abused its power, which is an inadmissible offense in a rule-of-law state, especially if committed by an organ that is responsible for the comprehensive administration of the country, the Supreme Court's announcement reads.
