940817 FT 17 AUG 94 / TUC queries falls in jobless total Recent falls in the unemployment total have more to do with men between 35 and 60 giving up the search for work than with real reductions in unemployment, says a report published by the TUC yesterday, in advance of today's unemployment figures. The TUC says its analysis of the government's Labour Force Survey showed that 110,000 men between 35 and 60 withdrew from the labour market in 1993, dropping out of the government's unemployment statistics. A 30 per cent reduction in places on the government's special employment and training programmes since 1990 has also cut opportunities for the long-term unemployed to retrain and make themselves more employable. Mr John Monks, TUC general secretary, accused the government of creating 'a lost generation of middle-aged men who have given up hope of work. There is little prospect of many of them getting back into the labour market unless the government steps up special measures to help the long-term unemployed and acts to reinforce Britain's patchy recovery.'
