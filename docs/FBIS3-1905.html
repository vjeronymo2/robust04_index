Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Male, March 3 (XINHUA) -- Chinese vice premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen left here today for Iran en route from Karachi, Pakistan, after his two-day official goodwill visit to the Maldives. Qian was seen off by Maldivian Foreign Minister Fathulla Jameel and other Maldivian Government officials and Chinese Ambassador to the Maldives Zhang Chengli at the Male International Airport. During the visit, Qian exchanged views with Jameel on bilateral relations and regional and international issues of common interest. The two sides also signed an aviation agreement and another agreement on economic and technical cooperation. The Maldives is the fourth country of Qian's current five-nation tour. Qian visited Pakistan, Bangladesh and Sri Lanka before coming to the Maldives.
