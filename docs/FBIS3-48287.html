Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From Tokyo MAINICHI SHIMBUN in Japanese 22 Jan 94 Morning Edition] [Text] On the evening of 21 January, State Minister (in charge of political reform) Sadao Yamahana and Minister of Home Affairs Kanju Sato held a joint news conference at the Ministry of Home Affairs. When asked if they were ready to resign as a result of the upper house's disapproval of the political reform bills, State Minister Yamahana denied his immediate resignation, stating: "I think I should carefully consider if it is appropriate to resign right now. I have to consult with the ruling parties about this. In fact, I am not allowed to determine my course of action at my own discretion." Minister of Home Affairs Sato said: "I have the primary responsibility for the enactment of the reform bills. I should fulfill my duties by trying my best as a cabinet member at least until 29 January (when the extraordinary Diet session closes)."
