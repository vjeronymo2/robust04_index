940921 FT 21 SEP 94 / Arts: Today's Television Q: How do you justify writing 'New Series' on top of a collection of repeats? A: You imitate the record industry, and package a lot of old material under a title such as 'The Best Of'. Today BBC2 launches its 'New Series', A Feast Of Floyd, containing programmes as much as 10 years old, beginning with the episode in Provence where Floyd cooks fish soup (8.00). That is followed at 8.30 by another 'New Series': University Challenge, which is a mere 32 years old. Granada are still the producers, even though it now appears on BBC2, but Bamber Gascoigne's place is taken by Jeremy Paxman. In Trial And Error David Jessel produces new evidence in the case of Raymond Gilmour, a man who, in 1981, was convicted in a Scottish court on a majority verdict after confessing to police to the murder of a 16-year-old girl. There was no forensic evidence, no evidence to corroborate his confession, and the detective superintendent in charge originally rejected the confession as false. Then a Serious Crime Squad was brought in, and Gilmour confessed again (9.00 C4).
