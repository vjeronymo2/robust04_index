Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Georgiy Shmelev] [Text] Moscow, 2 March -- Questions about the situation in the Bosnia region following Russian President Boris Yeltsin's initiative for peaceful progress in a settlement were discussed today at the Russian Foreign Ministry during consultations on Russian-French cooperation in settling the Bosnian crisis. An exchange of opinions took place on draft documents proposed for examination by the UN Security Council. Taking part in the consultations from the Russian side was Vitaliy Churkin, deputy foreign minister and special representative of the Russian president at the talks on the former Yugoslavia, and from the French side: Alain Dejammet, deputy general secretary of the French Foreign Ministry, and Buno Racine, director of the Center for Analysis and Forecasting.
