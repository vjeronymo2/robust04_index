940128 FT 28 JAN 94 / UK Company News: Louis Newmark cuts loss Pre-tax losses at Louis Newmark for the half year ended October 2 were cut from Pounds 888,000 to Pounds 638,000. With the second half usually the stronger, losses are expected to fall further. Turnover for the company, which has interests in the manufacture of electronic instruments, machining and watch importing, advanced to Pounds 12.2m, against Pounds 10.7m which included Pounds 665,000 from discontinued activities. Sales from continuing operations rose 22 per cent with a sharp recovery in the capital goods businesses. The company said that the pension fund had moved into deficit and that its annual provision would have to be increased by Pounds 160,000. This was reflected pro rata in the interim results. Losses per share came out at 15.2p (30p).
