Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 17 (CTK) -- The Czech and Ukrainian Foreign Ministers Josef Zieleniec and Anatoliy Zlenko initialed today a Treaty on Friendship and Cooperation between their countries. Zlenko ends today a two-day official visit to the Czech Republic. At a press conference the two politicians appreciated the importance of the document which is yet to be affirmed on a higher level. The Treaty, as well as a Czech-Russian agreement on trade and economic relations and an agreement on scientific cooperation will be an inspiration for mutual relations. Zlenko said that Czech-Ukrainian economic relations should be developed, as there existed still unexploited possibilities in this sphere. Ukraine will gladly draw from Czech experience from the transformation process, Zlenko said. Zieleniec informed Zlenko that Ukraine was one of the Czech Republic's significant priorities. Stable Ukraine is also an important factor of stabilization in Central and Eastern Europe, he added. Zieleniec said he was pleased by Zlenko's confirmation of continuing supplies of oil and gas via the Ukrainian territory, saying he was glad that the Ukrainian Government saw this question as an important part of Czech-Ukrainian relations. The two ministers also discussed sub-regional and regional cooperation and ways of ensuring security in Europe. Zlenko also met with Czech Premier Vaclav Klaus. After the meeting, Klaus said that the Czech Republic was ready to send its economic experts to Ukraine if asked to. Zlenko said that Klaus had expressed his support for Ukrainian foreign political activities.
