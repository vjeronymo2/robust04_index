BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 26 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The Vice Minister of Infrastructure Development T. Damiran said in his interview to the governmental newspaper "DZASGIYN GADZRYN MEUEE" that in the current year it is scheduled to allot some 6 billion tugrik for capital investment in the sphere of construction at the expense of the state centralized budget. This sum is exceeding by 2 billion tugrik the last year's ones. T. Damiran noted that including the means of aimags' budgets, enterprises and economic entities privately-owned the total volume of capital investment in the construction constitutes this year some 16 billion tugrik. At the same time the vice minister underscored that the growing amount of capital investment should be understood in connection with the inflation that is to say the actual volume of construction is not to exceed the last year's taking into consideration all the means allotted it is expected that more than 500 objects are to be made available in the current year. As was informed by the vice minister, at present in the sphere of construction there are working some 20 thousand qualified construction workers and additionally some 15 thousand seasonal ones. In accordance with the contracts concluded there are about one thousand Russian workers engaged in the construction sphere as well. According to the latest statistics, there were about 60 thousand qualified workers engaged in the construction industry of Mongolia up to 1990.
