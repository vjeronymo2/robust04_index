November 17, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

CS LONG BEACH TO PLAY HOST TO UNLV

Cal State Long Beach, trying for its first winning football season since 1986, will play Nevada Las Vegas in a Big West Conference game today at 1 p.m. in Veterans Stadium.

The 49ers are 5-5 overall and 3-3 in the Big West.

Las Vegas is 4-6 and 3-3.

Coach George Allen said a victory is important for the future of the Long Beach program, adding: "We have to get better student support."

Although they are 5-0 at home, the 49ers are averaging only 4,800 fans a game.

Long Beach quarterback Todd Studer, who has passed for 17 touchdowns, is 11th in the country in total offense with 2,388 yards.

Las Vegas uses two quarterbacks -- Derek Stott, who has thrown for 1,706 yards, and Hunkie Cooper, who is sixth nationally with 1,622 all-purpose running yards. Cooper also plays flanker and returns kicks.

The Rebels have won the last four games in the series. DICK WAGNER
