940310 FT 10 MAR 94 / Survey of Software at Work, Spring 1994 - Office Automation (18): Lotus notes This product is a work group or groupware system launched in 1990 and now on version 3.0, released in 1993. The system is based on a document database which allows team members to share documents and add comments. Other facilities, some of which are illustrated in the Lawson Mardon and Unipart case studies, range from work flow automation to setting reminder alarms for forthcoming tasks or events. Some companies have used Notes in unexpected ways to develop business systems such as accounting. Other software companies are developing products to offer add-on facilities. Supplier: Lotus Development, formed in the US in 1982 and in the UK in 1983. The company leapt to stardom with the 1-2-3 spreadsheet package. Turnover in 1993 was up nine per cent to Dollars 981.2m. Net profit was Dollars 75.4m, compared with Dollars 57.2m in 1992, when there were acquisition and restructuring costs. Lotus has 4,200 staff world-wide. Number of users: Notes is used by more than 750,000 people in 3,200 companies world-wide and is clear leader in an ill-defined market. Price: Pounds 375 for one user, Pounds 3,715 for 10 users, then discounts increase. Sales and support: direct or through dealers or systems companies (value-added resellers). Hardware and operating systems: IBM-type personal computers running Windows or OS/2, plus Apple Macintosh computers.
