Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] The Federal Electoral Tribunal has unanimously annulled the resolution approved by the General Council of the Federal Electoral Institute [IFE] on 23 December 1993. This resolution approved the procedure to determine the ceiling for presidential campaign spending. The IFE General Council had appointed three of its magistrates to establish the amount that would constitute the ceiling for presidential campaign spending. The Federal Electoral Tribunal, however, decided that this procedure transfers the IFE General Council's decisionmaking power to the magistrates. Therefore, the Federal Electoral Tribunal, as explained by its president Jose Luis de la Peza, decided that the IFE General Council should issue a new resolution before 31 January. [Begin de la Peza recording, in progress] ...by which it can determine the ceiling for the amount of money parties can spend on their campaigns for the presidential elections. [end recording] In turn, the Federal Electoral Tribunal issued a resolution that took into account a suit filed against the IFE by the Party of the Democratic Revolution.
