940607 FT 07 JUN 94 / UK Company News: Restructure surplus lifts UK Land The inclusion of a Pounds 43.7m surplus this time arising on restructuring meant UK Land, the property investment and dealing group, turned in a pre-tax profit of Pounds 45.02m for the year ended March 31. This compared with a Pounds 14.9m loss. The pre-tax figure also included a Pounds 961,000 profit (Pounds 13.7m loss) on termination of operations. Profit before exceptional items came to Pounds 414,000 (Pounds 1.19m loss), and earnings per share were Pounds 10.13p (Pounds 58.99p losses). Net assets of the company, which owns the Elephant & Castle shopping and office complex in London, came to 15p (Pounds 225.05p deficit).
