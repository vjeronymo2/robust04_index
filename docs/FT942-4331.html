940610 FT 10 JUN 94 / Exporters upbeat on order books Nearly 80 per cent of UK exporters consider their order book to be good or very good, according to a survey conducted by Trade Indemnity, the credit insurance group. About 89 per cent of those surveyed said the number of their export customers increased over the last quarter. Asia and North America were considered the most buoyant markets by exporters. Only 22 per cent of those polled considered European Union markets to be buoyant, and Italy was the country that was perceived as being the greatest risk over the next 12 months.
