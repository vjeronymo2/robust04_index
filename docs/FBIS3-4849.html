Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Nagoya, March 2 KYODO -- The Bank of Nagoya, a medium-sized Japanese regional bank, said Wednesday [2 March] it has signed agreements with two Chinese municipal authorities on business cooperation. The bank said the agreements, concluded with Changzhou and Konshan in the coastal province of Jiangsu, call for exchanges of financial information and cooperation in promoting bilateral trade and joint ventures. It now has collaboration agreements with six Chinese municipalities, the only Japanese commercial bank to do so. The bank said it has so far helped 24 customers to launch joint ventures and 60 firms to conclude deals with Chinese partners.
