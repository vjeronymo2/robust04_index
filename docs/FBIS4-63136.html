BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yevgeniy Belovitskiy] [Text] Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk, 20 May -- Viktor Yerin, Russian Federation minister of interior, ended his visit to Sakhalin. At a news conference today on the results of the trip, the minister stressed he came to Sakhalin to acquaint himself with the work of the Interior Ministry bodies in the island oblast and to resolve the question of giving aid to the leadership of the oblast's directorate of internal affairs. The minister announced that in accordance with the results of a check that was carried out prior to his visit by an Interior Ministry commission, a number of responsible officials of the oblast directorate had been punished and measures were worked out to strengthen law and order on Sakhalin and in the Kurils. In connection with the Putina-94 operation that is being carried out in the Russian Far East, Viktor Yerin described it as very expedient in the struggle against organized international crime. The minister believes concrete contacts between the island's Interior Ministry bodies and the police of Japan's most northerly island, Hokkaido, could be very beneficial.
