Language: Persian Article Type:CSO [Text] Economics Division. In the year 1371 [21 March 1992-20 March 1993] 14,442 domestic companies with capital of about 342 billion rials[R] in 82 of the nation's cities were registered, representing increases of 2.8 percent in terms of numbers of companies and 44.3 percent in terms of capital over the year 1370 [21 March 1991-20 March 1992]. The Central Bank's Office of Economic Statistics reports that in 1371 [21 March 1992-20 March 1993] 1,706 domestic companies with capital in excess of R20.4 billion were officially dissolved, representing increases of 1.4 percent in terms of numbers and 157.6 percent in terms of capital compared to 1370 [21 March 1991-20 March 1992]. Based on this report, last year 36 foreign companies with capital of more than R1.626.1 trillion were registered, a 2.17-percent decrease in terms on numbers compared to 1370 [21 March 1991-20 March 1992]. Of the total number of registered companies, 50.1 percent are corporations, 41.5 percent are limited partnerships, and 7.7 percent are cooperatives. This report adds that of the total number of companies registered in 1371 [21 March 1992-20 March 1993], about 28.1 percent was in the construction sector, 32.4 percent was in the industrial sector, 19.4 percent was in the commercial sector, 13.6 percent was in the credit institution, insurance, brokerage and commercial services sector, and the remaining 10.8 percent was in other economic activities. Likewise, the percentage of registered companies whose dissolution was announced in 1370 [21 March 1991-20 March 1992] was 12 percent in terms of numbers and 3.3 percent in terms of capital. This report adds that after Tehran the cities ranking highest in numbers of registered companies were Esfahan, Ahvaz, Mashhad, Shiraz, Tabriz, Urmia, Bandar 'Abbas, Kerman, and Kermanshah, and that after Tehran the cities ranking highest in terms of the total capital of their registered companies were Bandar 'Abbas, Mashhad, Esfahan, Kashan, Shiraz, Yazd, Tabriz, Ahvaz, and Urmia.
