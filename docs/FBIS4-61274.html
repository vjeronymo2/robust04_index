BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS special correspondent] [Text] Cairo, 4 May -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher expressed serious concern over the unstable situation that still pertains in Bosnia-Herzegovina and which contains the threat of a flare-up of new dangerous hot-beds of conflict. During a meeting that took place today in the Egyptian capital, the heads of the foreign policy departments of the two countries called on the warring sides immediately and totally to implement a withdrawal of forces from the established zones around Gorazde and to undertake immediate steps to reduce tension and to prevent offensive military actions in the region of Brcko. Kozyrev and Christopher also called for the signing, as soon as possible, of an agreement on a total cessation of hostilities over the whole territory of Bosnia-Herzegovina that would envisage the disengagement of military formations, the withdrawal of heavy weapons, and the stationing of UN forces between them. They called for the immediate renewal of talks without preconditions in order to work out a comprehensive settlement in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Russia's foreign minister and the U.S. secretary of state are convinced of the necessity for providing a powerful political impulse to the process of finding a Bosnian settlement. They call for the holding of a meeting at ministerial level with the participation of the member countries of the contact group. Such a meeting could take place in Geneva on 13 May.
