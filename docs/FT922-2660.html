920616 FT 16 JUN 92 / International Company News: Siemens jobs cuts seen by next chairman SIEMENS, the German electronics group, would need to cut jobs in some sectors to improve its cost position, the future chairman, Mr Heinrich von Pierer, said yesterday, Reuter reports from Bonn. Mr Von Pierer, due to take over in October after his appointment has been ratified next month, told a German newspaper he had no plans to restructure the company's top management after assuming the chairmanship. But he said some areas, such as loss-making computer and electronic equipment producer Siemens-Nixdorf Informationssysteme (SNI) and Siemens' semiconductor unit, would see cutbacks.
