BFN [Text] Moscow, 16 Apr, JIJI -- Russian Vice Foreign Minister Panov (in charge of Asian Affairs) gave an interview to the JIJI NEWS AGENCY on 16 April. Touching on the military alliance included in the treaty of goodwill and mutual assistance which the former Soviet Union had signed with the DPRK (North Korea), Panov stated: "It will be reviewed taking various factors into consideration." He also disclosed that the treaty will be revised before its expiration in 1996. In the interview, Vice Minister Panov stated: "North Korea does not have the technology for developing nuclear weapons." Panov stressed that North Korea's nuclear arms development issue should be resolved not by compulsory measures such as economic sanctions, but by peaceful measures such as the convocation of an international conference advocated by Russia.
