921001 FT 01 OCT 92 / Examiners reject GCSE criticism ENGLAND'S examining boards yesterday rejected criticism from the schools inspectorate that standards slipped in this year's GCSE examination. The Joint Council for the GCSE accused the inspectorate of making sweeping critical generalisations on the basis of 'very flimsy evidence'. Mr Dennis Hatfield, chairman of the council, said that tightening up since the introduction of GCSE in 1988 meant this year's procedures were more secure than ever before.
