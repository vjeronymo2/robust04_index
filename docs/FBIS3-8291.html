Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Professor of Economics Dariusz Rosati is the official candidate of the Democratic Left Alliance [SLD] to the post of deputy prime minister and finance minister. Journalists were informed about this decision by the Alliance's press spokesman, Zbigniew Siemiatkowski. [Reporter] The SLD leadership and that of the Polish Peasant Party, at a joint session on Saturday, gave their support to the candidature of Prof. Rosati, Zbigniew Siemiatkowski said. The deciding factors were the qualifications of the candidate, his wide experience, and the fact that he is well known in international economic circles. On Saturday afternoon, the Alliance leader, Aleksander Kwasniewski, sent a letter regarding this matter to Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak. The prime minister is in touch with our candidate, Siemiatkowski said. Prof. Rosati has spent the past few days in Warsaw. He is at present in Geneva, where, for the past three years, he has been working as an expert for the United Nations at the European Economic Commission. Prof. Rosati is to return to Warsaw at the end of the week, Zbigniew Siemiatkowski said. Now a few words about the candidate: He is in his forties, is not a member of any party, and had been a member of the Polish United Workers Party until its dissolution. He comes from Radom. He is married, with two children, and has outstanding qualifications and abilities for these posts, Zbigniew Siemiatkowski stressed.
