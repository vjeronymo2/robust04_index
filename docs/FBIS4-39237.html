BFN [Text] The draft law on Russia's 1994 budget will definitely be adopted in June, State Duma Speaker Ivan Rybkin told Interfax on Monday. As we reported earlier, the discussion of the draft budget had been scheduled for June 8. To quote Rybkin, allocations will be increased by a total of 8 trillion rubles on research, military-conversion projects, science, education, culture and social welfare. Jointly with the government, the State Duma is looking for additional sources of revenues, including arms exports. Commenting on the Federation Council's recommendations to increase military spending from 37 trillion to 55 trillion rubles, Rybkin said: "Dreaming is no sin." Asked about the reason behind his meeting with Yeltsin scheduled for Tuesday, he said that it would be a routine meeting held monthly on the first Tuesday to discuss the most urgent issues.
