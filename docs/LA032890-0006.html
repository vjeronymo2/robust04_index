March 28, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

RECHY ON ROONEY

Novelist John Rechy discovered that Andy Rooney, after making his apologies on CBS' "60 Minutes" for some loony remarks about gays and blacks, had sustained more popularity than President Bush (Op-Ed Page, March 9). So, the USC lecturer then set out to prove that Rooney was really a bigot after all, albeit a nice one.

The current trend with such matters is that unless you hold solely to a politically correct view on race, homosexuality, women's rights, etc., you become open to yet another powerful victims lobby and in Rooney's case, considerable persecution and nearly the loss of his job. It's thought control and repression of the 1950s revisited, only this time perpetrated by many college professors and universities. Rooney found out that the only way subjects of race or homosexuality can be discussed is through bland inane pieties that do little to advance knowledge or understanding.

Rechy makes some missteps of his own. For his purposes, he prefers that the option, if you will, of deciding whether you are "straight" or homosexual is much like deciding whether you're going to be a Republican or a Democrat. Then he corrupts Rooney's positive affirmation of his culture and his life's preferences that flow from that as a statement of bigotry with moral equivalents about Nazis and white supremacists. How thoughtful! Rooney like Rechy added little to the dialogue, but in this instance Rechy made victims of us all.

BURTON FREEMAN

Los Angeles
