931109 FT 09 NOV 93 / Arts: Today's Television Tuesday is proving to be one of television's best nights this season. From its launch BBC2's foreign affairs series Assignment has been one of the better Birtist additions to the corporation's current affairs empire. Today (7.45), South African expatriate Donald Woods considers what role television might play in his homeland's future. Six programmes on the subject of talking might seem daunting, but since The Talking Show (C4, 8.00) is presented by comedian Sandi Toksvig, womankind's answer to the pocket battleship, they should at least be worth sampling. The series begins with consideration of the voice and regional accents C4's excellent culture series Without Walls features art critic Brian Sewell in its 'J'accuse' slot (9.30), putting the boot into Leonardo da Vinci. In the best police series for years, Between The Lines (BBC1, 9.30), the police hierarchy closes ranks to protect a chief constable. Omnibus (BBC1, 10.25) considers a Russian 'sex boom' following the disintegration of the Soviet Union.
