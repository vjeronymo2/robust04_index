BFN [Text] Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou returned satisfied from his trip to the United States. In a statement shortly before leaving New York for Athens, Papandreou said that his trip had specific goals that have been partially achieved. He added that some issues will be evaluated later. Asked whether his expectations from the visit were fulfilled, Papandreou said: I cannot say that the flow of history has changed. In any case, we managed to make our positions understood and interlocutors were responsive. Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias described as completely unfounded rumors that he had met with Stevo Crvenkovski, his counterpart from Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM], adding that he is ready to return to New York if talks on Skopje require it.
