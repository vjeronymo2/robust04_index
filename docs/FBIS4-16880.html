BFN [Text] Riga, Apr 18, BNS -- Latvian Saeima (parliament) Deputy Speaker Andrejs Krastins met on Monday [18 April] with U.S. State Department's political experts Julie Cohen, Martin Schwartz and U.S. Embassy in Latvia Secretary Scott Dean. The group discussed relations between Latvia and Russia and problems related to Latvia's draft citizenship law. Krastins also pointed to shortcomings in the initialed Latvian- Russian agreements on troop withdrawal. The package fails to demand compensation for Russian damages to Latvia, allows Russian forces to retain ownership rights on real estate and tolerates foundation of Latvian-Russian joint ventures on the basis of former military installations, said Krastins. Krastins reiterated that Latvia is interested to receive international guarantees for the process of the signature of the troop withdrawal package and the control of its implementation. Dean voiced his country's support for the conclusion of an interstate agreement that would fully resolve the issue of Russian troop withdrawal from Latvia.
