BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA June 3 -- A delegation from Australia's largest state New South Wales, led by its Premier John Fahey, has visited Vietnam since May 31 aimed at promoting the multi-faceted relations between the two sides. While staying in Vietnam, the New South Wales' delegation, which was accompanied by several Australian major companies and businessmen, was received today by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. It had working sessions with Minister of Trade Le Van Triet on steps to expand the commercial and economic ties between Vietnam and Australia in general and New South Wales in particular. It also had working sessions with the ministries of science, technology and environment, public health, energy, the State Committee for Cooperation and Investment and other institutions concerned, and the People's Committee of Quang Nam-Danang Province. The delegation called at several economic establishments, including the State Electric Control Centre and the National Optical Centre. At these meetings, both sides compared notes on how to further promote their bilateral relations. It is scheduled to leave Vietnam on June 4.
