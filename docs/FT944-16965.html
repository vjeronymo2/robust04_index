941011 FT 11 OCT 94 / Arts: Today's Television Since other book prizes involve larger sums of money, and since The Booker Prize is so flawed by the determination to choose the politically correct rather than the best, it is hard to see why BBC2 continues to play along by providing the hype so deeply desired by the organisers. But they do. In addition to live coverage of the event at 8.00 they also lay on an interview with the winner in The Late Show at 11.15. BBC1's new series Natural Neighbours sounds like another of those 'just fancy that' shows which are replacing journalism on television. Today's opening edition rounds up lots of stories about brave and intelligent pigs (8.30). Wasted Windfall is the second in three programmes about North Sea oil. Do you really need to watch once you have read the title? (9.00 C4). Talking of titles, there is surely a hostage to fortune in Alan Bleasdale Presents. There would be weight in 'Alan Bleasdale Writes' or even 'Produces', but 'Presents' just sounds like a way of working in his name. Tonight's drama is said to be a black comedy (10.00 C4). Network First is about Britain's chemical weapons programme (10.40 ITV).
