July 5, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

FLAWED MIRROR HAMPERS HUBBLE SPACE TELESCOPE

Discovery of an apparent flaw in the Hubble Telescope has been accompanied by a flurry of statements and speculations relating to the management and technical execution of the program. Since the Hughes name has been associated with the technical difficulties, I feel it is important to clarify the Hughes relationship.

The Hubble mirrors were manufactured almost 10 years ago by a division of the Perkin-Elmer Corp. This organization was acquired late last year by the Hughes Aircraft Co. and renamed Hughes Danbury Optical Systems. Hughes is fully supporting the technical teams both at NASA and at Danbury in analyzing and correcting the problem as soon as possible. The full range of Hughes technologies are being made available for this effort.

I am obviously concerned about the Hughes name and our technology -- neither of which are in question here.

MALCOLM R. CURRIE, Chairman

Hughes, Los Angeles
