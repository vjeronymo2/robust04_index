Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 4 February (MTI) -- Hungarian Foreign Minister Geza Jeszenszky held talks with NATO Deputy Secretary-General Gebhardt von Moltke in Budapest today [4 February]. Von Moltke later told reporters he had come to Budapest to discuss implementation of the peace-partnership plan and help draw up a document in which Hungary would outline its cooperation concepts to Brussels. His talks with Jeszenszky concerned the delegation of a Hungarian liaison officer to NATO's Brussels headquarters, and military-planning cooperation between NATO and Hungary. Von Moltke described the peace-partnership plan as a process permitting cooperation on an extensive scale. He said preparations would soon begin for the first joint military exercise, to be held before the year is out.
