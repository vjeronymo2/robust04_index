September 11, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Chip Industry Barometer Falls: The semiconductor industry's benchmark indicator dipped in August to its lowest level since November.

The San Jose-based Semiconductor Industry Assn. said the book-to-bill ratio, which compares orders with shipments, fell to 0.99 in August from 1 in July. A book-to-bill ratio of 0.99 means that for every $100 of semiconductors shipped to the U.S. market in the three months ended in August, chip manufacturers received new orders worth $99. A ratio of less than 1 is considered unhealthy for the industry.
