Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] A Lebanese Foreign Ministry delegation, led by Zafir al-Hasan, the Ministry's secretary general, arrived in Amman this morning to offer condolences on behalf of the Lebanese Government and people on the martyrdom of the late Na'ib 'Umran al-Ma'ayitah, the counselor at the Jordanian Embassy in Beirut, who was assassinated by criminal terrorists in Beirut last Saturday. On arrival at Queen Alia International Airport, al-Hasan said: The crime that was committed was an ugly crime, and we strongly condemn it. We feel great sorrow over the martyrdom of the Jordanian diplomat because the victim was one of our Jordanian brothers. He added: We bow with appreciation to martyr al-Ma'ayitah because he offered much during his mission. We in Lebanon, and on all levels, are doing all we can to identify and punish the criminals. He emphasized Lebanon's readiness to cooperate with Jordan in order to secure their arrest. He voiced sincerest condolences in the name of Lebanon--president, government, and people--on the martyrdom of the Jordanian diplomat, and said: We offer our heartfelt and sincerest condolences to Jordan--king, government, and people--and to the martyr's family.
