Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev does not intend to take a "pro-Serbian" position in the settlement of the Bosnian conflict despite the threats from LDP [Liberal Democratic Party] leader Vladimir Zhirinovskiy. Kozyrev announced this to an Interfax correspondent before leaving Moscow for Kyrgyzstan and China on Monday. Zhirinovskiy stated last week that he gave Kozyrev a "week for thought," and if the Russian foreign minister did not correct his approach Zhirinovskiy would seek his resignation. The foreign minister pointed out that the LDP leader's position was an "overt attempt to push Russia into a world war." He warned against engaging Russia in the conflict on the Balkan peninsula, where the First World War had started and led to the seizure in Russia of power by an irresponsible clique causing monstrous damage to the country for 70 years. According to Kozyrev, "some political forces" may try to get Russia involved in a "global catastrophe," to make a career of this and to seize power. "I am not satisfied with this scenario either in foreign or in domestic policy and I will never be satisfied with it. If after this Zhirinovskiy demands my resignation I will consider this as the best recognition of the correctness of my policy," the minister pointed out.
