Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 19 Jan (TANJUG) -- Slobodan Jarcevic, minister for foreign affairs of the republic of Serb Krajina, said today that the joint statement signed today in Geneva by the ministers for foreign affairs of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia and the Republic of Croatia "does not infringe on the integrity and sovereignty of the republic of Serb Krajina." A public statement signed by Jarcevic says that this joint statement, signed by Vladislav Jovanovic and Mate Granic, envisages mutual recognition, normalization of relations and the introduction of representative offices in Belgrade and Zagreb. "The Republic of Croatia can sign international treaties and make agreements only for the territories under its control and those that are not disputable," the statement says. Invoking CSCE documents, as stated, cannot imply the borders desired by the government of the Republic of Croatia, but the real ones. Jarcevic stresses that the joint statement by Yugoslavia and Croatia in its reference to the UN Charter "relates to those provisions that regulate peaceful solutions to disputes and a people's right to self-determination," something "that implies the will of the people of the republic of Serb Krajina articulated clearly at their referendum, the statement emphasizes." The government of the republic of Serb Krajina, as stated, welcomes the peace process in the area of the former Yugoslavia and the normalization of relations between the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia and the Republic of Croatia, and believes that "this will contribute to the restoration of peace along the borders between the republic of Serb Krajina and the Republic of Croatia.
