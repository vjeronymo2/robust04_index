BFN [Text] The Agency for National Security Planning [NSP] revealed on 7 May that three North Koreans -- Kim Tae-ho, 35, work team leader of the Namchon chemical complex of the Ministry of Nuclear Energy; and brothers Hwang Kwang-chol and Kwang-il, 20 and 18, pitmen in a mine -- escaped North Korea and have defected to the ROK. The NSP said these three people will hold a news conference on their defection on 9 May at 1400 [0500 GMT] at the Press Center in Taepyongno, Seoul. The NSP revealed that Kim Tae-ho was born in Jilin Province, China, and that he was the work team leader of the waste disposal sector of the Namchon chemical complex of the Ministry of Nuclear Energy, which is a uranium refinery plant. It also revealed that Hwang Kwang-chol worked as a pitman of Kungsing mine in Hoeryong city.
