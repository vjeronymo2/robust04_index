BFN [Text] The Lithuanian Government has adopted a decision to join the agreement on patent cooperation signed on 19 June 1970 in Washington. As of 11 March this year, there were 67 states that are members of this agreement. By joining this agreement, Lithuania will have to upgrade its rules of patent preparation and its system of classification and appraisal. From now on, information about patents issued by the Lithuanian State Patent Bureau will be provided to the headquarters of the patent cooperation agreement in Geneva. The State Patent Bureau will work mostly with foreigners who seek protection for their technological innovations on Lithuania's territory.
