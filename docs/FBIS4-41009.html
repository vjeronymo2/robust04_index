BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Lev Rumyantsev] [Text] St. Petersburg June 21 TASS -- Russian governors will "insist on a soonest meeting with President Boris Yeltsin" to tell him about their opinion of ways out of the economic crisis, Leningrad Regional Governor Aleksandr Belyakov told ITAR-TASS today upon the completion of a governors' meeting in Novgorod. "The president meets anyone but us, and we know the Russian life from inside," said Belyakov. In his words, the governors have elaborated 23 measures which, they believe, will help to overcome the economic crisis and they want to tell the president about that immediately. The governors give a priority to the 10,000,000,000,000 ruble emission to make the ruble/dollar correlation real and settle the non-payment problem a reason for which is the enterprises' lack of proceeds they have earned. Anti-crisis proposals also include the introduction of a modern bill mechanism, state insurance of loans to the population, state control over the increased cost of energy carriers and cargo transportation, subordination of all federal structures in regions to the municipal authorities and the introduction of the governor status in Russia.
