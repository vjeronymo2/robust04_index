Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu said the government should keep its composure because North Korea has nothing to gain by delaying the resolution of the nuclear issue. At the conference of heads of ROK overseas missions on 2 February, Minister Han Sung-chu said it is his view that North Korea's attempt to delay, whether for one or two months or for a half a year, will not help to promote its nuclear development capability and nuclear development activities. Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu's remarks urging composure are noteworthy because it is made at a time when the possibility of referring the North Korean nuclear issue to the United Nations is increasing.
