Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] Myechyslaw Hryb, chairman of the Belarusian Supreme Soviet, gave a news conference today at which he made a statement expressing sincere gratitude to the citizens of the republic and to the leaders of the CIS countries who congratulated him on his election to the post of chairman of the Supreme Soviet. The statement also notes that the change of leader of the Belarusian parliament does not mean a change in political and economic course. No, good-neighborly relations with all states, all peoples, recognition of the borders within which we exist -- those are the main foreign policy priorities set out by the Supreme Soviet of the Republic of Belarus. In the Supreme Soviet chairman's statement, he says he will support the course of nonnuclear status and neutrality proclaimed by parliament. Myecheslaw Hryb expressed hope that the Belarusian people will demonstrate a sense of organization and discipline for the speediest way out of the difficult situation in which Belarus finds itself. In the near future, he said, the Supreme Soviet Presidium, together with the government, intends to (?present) urgent measures to provide for the spring sowing and to take the necessary measures to activate the activity of our industrial enterprises. The Supreme Soviet chairman replied to numerous questions from the journalists.
