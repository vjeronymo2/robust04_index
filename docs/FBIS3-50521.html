Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Peruvian economic developments monitored through 3 January 1994. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. On 28 December the Economy and Finance Ministry approved an agreement to reduce the foreign debt and create a fund. In accordance with the agreement, which was signed on 17 December by the Peruvian and Swiss Governments, Switzerland will pardon the debt -- worth over 196 million Swiss francs -- so part of the money can be used for aid and development programs. (Lima Global de Television Network in Spanish 0100 GMT 29 Dec 93) The Peruvian Energy and Mines Ministry reported on 30 December that in 1994 the proven hydrocarbon reserves will increase by 10 percent, thus reaching 420 million barrels, in accordance with the results of drilling done in 1993. The data indicate that production increased by 9 percent compared to average production for 1992. The report also states that mining increased by 4.5 percent compared to 1992, and that the production of all important metals increased, except silver production, which dropped by 2.3 percent. The report adds that gold production increased by 3.9 percent in August and 10.8 percent in November. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 1648 GMT 30 Dec 93)
