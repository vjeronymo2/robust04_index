Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Kuchina] [Text] Riga March 2 TASS -- Energy supplies to the Russian radar station in Skrunda, Latvia, resumed on Wednesday, two days after an explosion damaged one of the supports of the power line feeding the station with electricity. Police have found the remains of the explosive which have been sent for examination. This proves preliminary conclusions of the investigating commission that the action had been planned in advance. Police are still questioning local residents. The power line security has been tightened. The explosion occurred on the day when Russian and Latvian delegations were supposed to resume their talks. Observers believe that this is not an incidental coincidence.
