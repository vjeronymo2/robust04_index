BFN [From BTA's 19 May "Press Review"] [Excerpt] Sofia, May 19 (BTA) -- "Prime Minister Stuns Parliament" (the trade union daily TRUD), "Prime Minister Gets Mixed Up Kicking His Own Chair" (ZEMEDELSKO ZNAME, the daily of the Bulgarian Agrarian Party), "MP's to Boycott Berov's Cabinet Overhaul" (the private daily STANDART NEWS), "Berov May Fall Today" (the private daily 24 CHASA) -- this is a sample of newspapers front-page headlines on Prime Minister Lyuben Berov's proposals for structural and personnel changes to the cabinet which he unveiled in parliament yesterday. Prof. Berov read his proposals at the beginning of debates on the sixth vote of no confidence in his cabinet the parliamentary Union of Democratic Forces (SDS) moved for last week. The no confidence motion is to be put to the vote after 18:00 hrs today. The private daily OTECHESTVEN VESTNIK stresses that "the New Union for Democracy (NSD, set up by breakaways and expelees from the parliamentary SDS), and the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS, on whose mandate the incumbent cabinet was formed at the end of 1992), have withdrawn their political and parliamentary support for Prime Minister Berov." "The problem is whether the NSD and DPS joint declaration is mere words or actual intention," a signed commentary in the same daily says. Citing Socialists, the SDS daily DEMOKRATSIYA writes that the Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP) is expected to adopt a decision on toppling Prof. Berov and his cabinet at a plenum today. "It is worth talking about a new cabinet if some six million Bulgarians have their say on it first", the socialist daily DUMA writes referring to a statement by BSP leader Zhan Videnov. Commenting on the NSD-DPS joint declaration, Mr. Videnov said that "the two parliamentary groups refuse to give credit to Lyuben Berov after he fulfilled all their wishes". TRUD predicts that Prof. Berov's Cabinet will be succeeded by a government formed by centrist forces -- the DPS, the NSD, the independent MP's and the Civil Alliance for the Republic (GOR, part of whose members broke away from the BSP). Prof. Todor Vulchev, governor of the Bulgarian National Bank, is expected to head the new cabinet, the paper writes. The MP's' unwillingness to dissolve themselves before the expiry of their term is a guarantee for the appointment of a new cabinet, the paper says adding that the centrists and the moderate in the SDS and the BSP are expected to conclude an agreement. "The SDS and the BSP would better admit that the bogging down of reform and the disintegration of the state is their fault, and not the fault of that cabinet of figure-heads," a signed commentary in 24 CHASA says. According to the publication, "more than four years have passed but the SDS, the BSP and the president still do not dare publicly name the price of the transition to a market economy which Bulgarians have been paying through the constant drop in their incomes, living standards and social status". "They say nothing about the price because they do not dare take the responsibility and govern the country in an open and honest way," the author points out. "75 percent of Bulgarians disapprove of Parliament, 65 percent are against Berov", 24 CHASA reports publishing the results of a representative opinion poll conducted by the BBSS Gallup International in May.
