Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 30 (XINHUA) -- South Korean President Kim Yong-sam said here today that he had fruitful discussions with Japanese and Chinese leaders on ways to ensure stability and prosperity in Northeast Asia. And he said that he believes the tour has provided the momentum to unfold a new age for the Asia-Pacific region with South Korea, Japan and China walking hand in hand. Kim explained that in discussions with Japan's Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa he had explored ways to further promote mutual cooperation, and in China he had shared with President Jiang Zemin the view that peace and stability on the Korean peninsula are essential to maintain that state for the whole of Northeast Asia. He added that South Korea and China have an identical view on the realization of peace and stability on the Korean peninsula and that the two countries will cooperate closely in resolving the nuclear dispute over site inspections with the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK). He said he believed that his visit to China has laid a solid foundation for strengthening economic cooperation between the two countries. Kim paid a state visit to China from March 26 to 30 after his three-day visit to Japan, his first visit to China since he assumed office in February last year.
