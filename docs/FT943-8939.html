940815 FT 15 AUG 94 / Kohl 'would serve full term' German Chancellor Helmut Kohl will stay in office for all four years of the next parliament if he wins October's general election, he said yesterday. Mr Kohl, 64, denied accusations by the opposition Social Democrats that he would deceive voters by stepping down in mid-term if he won, making way for his hardline right-wing lieutenant Mr Wolfgang Schauble. 'I have made it quite clear to my party and in public before the election that I want to run again,' Mr Kohl, who has been chancellor since 1982, told German ZDF television in his Austrian holiday retreat of St Gilgen. 'I want to know (if the voters still back me), and I will naturally run in order to make full use of the time given to me by the voters.'
