BFN [Text] Beijing, May 11 (XINHUA) -- China today expressed its firm determination to take part in the world multi-lateral trade system. At the international symposium on China's strategy for foreign economy and trade in the 1990s opened [as received] here today, Chinese Vice-Premier Li Lanqing said that China is expecting to resume its status as a signatory country of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) at an early date and become a founding member of the World Trade Organization (WTO). In his speech, Li noted that China, along with other countries in the world, will continue to make efforts to strengthen a multi-lateral trade system in the world and maintain an open world trade environment. Li expressed his hope that on the basis of the multi-lateral trade system, China will further its economic and trade relations with other countries so as to make its due contributions to the world economy. In her report, Wu Yi, minister of Chinese foreign trade and economic cooperation, said that China resolutely advocates the multi-lateral trade system represented by GATT and is speeding up negotiations on its re-entry into GATT, adding that China's determination to re-enter GATT is firm and China has made great efforts to this end. In accordance with international trade norms and practices, she said that China has conducted a series of reforms of its foreign trade system such as the improvement of conditions on market access and foreign trade transparency. Wu noted that China's more open market plays an active role in the world economy and trade, and through reforms this year, China's economy and trade will be further brought in line with international rules. Wu expressed her thanks to most contracting parties who support China for its re-entry into GATT. Meanwhile, she hoped that some contracting parties can realize their commitments and take action to support China in the bid.
