BFN [Text] The provincial plenum of the Communist Party of Cuba in Matanzas analyzed, as a special point on the agenda, the current situation in the province regarding implementation of the measures recently approved by the Council of State as well as Decree-Law 186, which regulates self-employment violations. Osvaldo Umpierrez, president of the Administration Council in the province, advised that there are currently 10,227 people self-employed, mostly as hairdressers, manicurists, and transport cart drivers. Since February when Decree-Law 186 was approved, a contingent of 231 tax inspectors have imposed over 400 fines, of which more than half are against violators from other provinces. Also under review is the situation of over 500 automobiles that are not in the possession of their owners, 52 of which have been confiscated. Additionally, 1,300 motorcycles have been inventoried. Currently, in the province, 47 cases of money hoarding are being worked on, six of which are now being analyzed by the commission created for this purpose.
