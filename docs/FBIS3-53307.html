Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] ISLAMABAD, Jan 13 (AFP) -- The Pakistani government has closed its main crossing point on the border with Afghanistan, in a bid to halt a flood of Afghan refugees fleeing the fighting in Kabul, officials said Thursday. Thousands of Afghans were blocked after the border crossing at Torkham, in the northwest of Pakistan, was ordered closed Wednesday, witnesses said. Habibullah Khan, the government's representative for the KHYBER AGENCY, said only those bearing a passport and a valid visa for Pakistan would be admitted into the country. A government official, speaking on condition of anonymity, said the Pakistani authorities were willing to help the refugees, but this had to be done in Afghanistan. Tens of thousands of civilians have fled Kabul since fighting erupted between rival factions there on January 1. Pakistan housed three million Afghan refugees during the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan. Several hundred thousand returned home after the communist regime fell in April 1992.
