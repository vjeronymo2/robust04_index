940725 FT 25 JUL 94 / Portillo cool on full employment Mr Michael Portillo yesterday signalled a change in the tone of the government's approach to employment issues with a distinctly lukewarm endorsement of the goal of full employment. Speaking days after replacing Mr David Hunt as employment secretary, Mr Portillo dismissed suggestions that he would refuse to continue the dialogue with trade unions begun by his predecessor. He said on BBC Television: 'I am not refusing to work with these people but I will point out very strongly that their words and their actions point in opposite directions.' He was similarly unenthusiastic about Mr Hunt's recent public avowal of the government's commitment to full employment. 'It is a great aspiration, but unless you are careful it becomes merely a slogan. You have to take action to help people get into jobs.'
