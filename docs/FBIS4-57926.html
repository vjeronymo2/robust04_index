BFN [Text] Lima, 26 May (EFE) -- Today, the presidents of Bolivia, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and Venezuela reasserted their commitment to the integration process of the Andean Group (GRAN) or Cartagena Agreement on the occasion of its 25th anniversary. The five presidents expressed their support for the Andean integration process in separate communiques issued here today by the Cartagena Agreement Board (JUNAC), the GRAN's technical and executive body. Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada stated the common problems affecting Andean countries are the basic factor in their unity. He added: "More than ever before, the challenges and hopes the Andean Group represents still prevail." Sanchez de Lozada stated: "With our creativity and effort, we still must strive to achieve a better life for our countries." His Colombian colleague, Cesar Gaviria, stated on the occasion of GRAN's 25th anniversary, "we have to rejoice over the implementation of this idea that has made a great contribution to the unity of our countries." He added: "Today, trade is the great force behind economic development, integration, and the well-being of nations." Gaviria asserted that in these two and a half decades, GRAN faced difficulties "because the prevailing protectionist practices undermined commercial and integration processes." He added: "Today we hope the Andean Group will develop. We also hope to establish a common export tariff, although we are aware this does not constitute a goal in itself but a means for achieving a free trade zone throughout the entire continent of the Americas." Ecuadoran President Sixto Duran-Ballen stressed the "integrating tradition" of GRAN members and reasserted his country's support for "the Andean integration process." Duran-Ballen stated the Andean Group faces the immediate challenge of establishing an Andean free trade zone and helping Andean countries achieve "a level of modernization and proficiency that will allow them to achieve a definitive and advantageous presence in the international market." Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori stated that his country shares with other Andean countries "a profound integrationist spirit." Fujimori explained that Peru temporarily withdrew from GRAN's economic process from August 1992 to April 1994 because of the serious domestic crisis his country was experiencing. He added that today, Peru has been able to achieve economic stability and has reassumed "with great enthusiasm, the noble task we assumed with our Andean brothers a quarter of a century ago." After noting that businessmen were skeptical about the Andean Group's success when it was implemented, Rafael Caldera, the president of Venezuela, which joined the Andean Group 23 years ago, praised the fact that private business has become the most important integration force. Caldera added: "The celebration of the anniversary of one of the most effective and auspicious Latin American integrationist movements constitutes an auspicious event not only for its members but for all developing countries."
