BFN [Text] Bordeaux, France, April 11 (AFP) -- A bomb alert that turned out to be a false alarm was reported Monday [11 April] on a commercial jet carrying French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe, airline officials said. The French domestic airline Air Inter said the Airbus 300 carrying 306 people was halfway between Paris and Bordeaux when an anonymous caller to Paris' Orly airport said there was a bomb on board. The pilot decided to continue the flight, and all the passengers were evacuated as soon as the jet landed in Bordeaux in southwest France. A search of the aircraft turned up no bomb. Juppe was traveling to Bordeaux to inaugurate an organization designed to boost trade between regional companies and international firms.
