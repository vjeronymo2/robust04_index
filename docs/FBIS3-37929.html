Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow February 24 TASS--The Russian Navy denied reports that a nuclear submarine from the Kamchatka flotilla of the Pacific Fleet had been netted by a Russian seiner 10 miles off the coast of the peninsula on Sunday. "Submarines of the Pacific Fleet have nothing to do with the incident near the coast of the Kamchatka Peninsula", the press service of the Russian Navy told TASS on Thursday. According to the military, only one nuclear submarine of the Kamchatka flotilla was at sea during the time. After its return to the base representatives of the company which owes the fishing vessel checked the U-boat and did not find either the net or any traces of damage. Besides, a detailed investigation which also studied the submarine's register and other documents confirmed that the U-boat was not involved in the incident. The Kamchatka-based VESTI newspaper reported on Wednesday that the Russian "Kamsudo-3" seiner had netted a nuclear submarine which safely escaped together with the net worth 20 million roubles. The ship mistook the submarine for a big shoal of fish. The submarine turned out to be right under the fishing vessel and had it surfaced there would have been a shipwreck. Astonished fishermen watched the surfaced conning tower of the U-boat pass along their vessel and disappear together with the net, the newspaper said. It added that this was the second incident of the type. The first one was registered in 1976. Now it remains to clear up who the submarine belonged to.
