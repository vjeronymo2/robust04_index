Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Tunis, 21 Mar (MENA)--Dennis Ross, U.S. coordinator on the peace process in the Middle East, described his talks with Palestinian leader Yasir 'Arafat today as "good and useful." In a statement following his meeting with 'Arafat, which lasted about two hours this afternoon, Ross said that he will continue the talks with 'Arafat and his aides this evening. Meanwhile, PLO Executive Committee member Yasir 'Abd-Rabbuh said that today's talks with Ross did not yield tangible results. 'Abd-Rabbuh, who took part in the talks, said that the issues that were discussed with Ross included providing international protection for the Palestinian people in the occupied territories, the form of the international presence approved by the Security Council in these territories, and the status of the Israeli settlements.
