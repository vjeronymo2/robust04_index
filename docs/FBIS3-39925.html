Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel has promised Ukraine further German support to help develop the country's economy. Kinkel met Anatoliy Zlenko, his Ukrainian counterpart, in Bonn today. Oleh Slepichev, Ukrainian minister of external economic relations, and Ukrainian Health Minister Yuriy Spizhenko also attended the meeting. Following talks that lasted more than two hours, Kinkel told journalists that the country, with its 53 million inhabitants, has considerable economic problems. Germany also wants to help within the scope of the European Union. Kinkel also stressed that Ukraine must be included in the new European security structure. Zlenko acknowledged Germany's willingness to support the process of economic reform in Ukraine. The country remains on the economic reform course. In order to continue these reforms, it needs the support of partners, he said. Earlier, he handed a letter from Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk to Chancellor Helmut Kohl. The letter contained specific requests for help, Zlenko added. He announced that Kravchuk will visit Germany soon.
