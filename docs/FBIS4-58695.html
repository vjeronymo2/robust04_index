BFN [Report by Ibrahim Humaydi: "'Ali Nasir Tells AL-HAYAH: Two 'Ali's Should Go If They Fail To Solve Problems"] [Text] Damascus -- Former Yemeni President 'Ali Nasir Muhammad has urged President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid to "leave" power "if they fail to solve the problems." He pointed out that the solution to the problem lies in the implementation of the Pledge and Accord Document signed in Amman, which "stipulated the establishment of a federation between the two parts of Yemen." He also warned of the possible partition of Yemen into "regions, not just two parts." In a statement to AL-HAYAH 'Ali Nasir said that yesterday he phoned a number of Arab leaders and Yemenis "to contain the crisis and halt the political and military collapse taking place, and to return to the negotiating table and dialogue, because the alternative to dialogue is collapse and ruin." He denied he had made direct contacts with foreign leaders. "The contacts were made via Arab channels." However, he did not name the Arab or foreign leaders with whom he made contacts. He said: "I cannot say that the fighting will lead to the collapse of unity. It is a wound in the body of national and Yemeni unity. It will take a long time for this wound to heal." He added: "If current developments turn into civil war, Yemen could turn into more than one state or an entity divided into regions." He stressed that he personally is "against infighting and separation. There is no future for Yemen except under unity. The leadership is the problem. Everyone is blaming unity for the problems, when unity has nothing to do with them. They talk in the name of the (Pledge and Accord) Document, unity, and the revolution, but their practices have nothing to do with these things or with democracy. Their actions are based on their narrow personal interests." Concerning a way out of the current crisis, the former South Yemen president said: "I stress the need to conduct a dialogue and merge institutions. If they (Yemeni leaders) fail to solve the problem and extricate Yemen from the current crisis, it is better for them to depart and safeguard their history and the achievement they have made." He noted that he contacted President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih the day before yesterday evening, but he could not speak to 'Ali Salim al-Bid because telephone lines to Aden are cut off. He also contacted Yasin Nu'man, chairman of the Socialist Party secretariat, and Muhammad 'Ali Ahmad, Abyan governor and member of the Socialist Party Political Bureau, two days ago. 'Ali Nasir commended the U.S. position on the current crisis, saying the Americans "are for safeguarding unity and democracy. They urge modernizing the building of the state and stability in Yemen and the region, and I support this. If unity is in their interests, it is in our interests too." He stressed the need to implement the Pledge and Accord Document. He said that "many of its stipulations still have not been implemented, even though it enjoyed a national, Arab, popular, and international consensus." On his opinion of the federation question as a solution and a way out of the current crisis, he said that the document stipulates "the establishment of a federation. But regrettably, it has not been implemented. Nor has the Constitution been applied or the document implemented. Its essence should be implemented. What I am interested in is to safeguard unity; the other things are details." On the possibility of his return to Yemen he said that, "if this question is raised, I am willing to play any role to contribute to finding a way out of the crisis, but not for the sake of power or a post." He avoided "holding anyone responsible. Everyone is responsible. The issue is not who started it all, but, rather, the devastating effects we are experiencing."
