941222 FT 22 DEC 94 / Pressure on Spanish markets A knock-on effect from Italy, plus addition to home-grown political unease, put the Spanish markets under pressure yesterday for the second consecutive day. Investor nervousness has been triggered brought on by separate legal probes that centring on the collapse a year ago of Banesto, Spain's fourth-largest bank at the time, and on an alleged government cover-up in an undercover war directed in the 1980s against Basque separatists which has led to the imprisonment this week of three former senior interior ministry officials. The peseta remained very weak, trading at Pta84.48 against the D-Mark, the yield on 10-year bonds kept hovering at a high level near of around 11.50 per cent, and Spain's stock market general index fell further to 291.59. Fuelling the selling pressure, the Treasury raised six-month bill yields in order to bring them closer to money market rates. Traders said the domestic market faced the additional burden of being bracketed with Italy - 'we're trapped in the garlic belt,' said one - although Spain had acceptable economic fundamentals and a far more stable political environment.
