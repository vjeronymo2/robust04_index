Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Naha, Jan. 10 KYODO -- A small wooden boat carrying 21 people believed to be Vietnamese was spotted Sunday [9 December] afternoon by a Japanese fishing vessel some 84 kilometers southwest of Cape Kiyan in Itoman on Okinawa Island, local maritime officials said. A Maritime Safety Agency patrol boat confirmed the same day that the five-ton craft, adrift in international waters in the East China Sea, is carrying 21 people, including women. A patrol boat will inspect the boat when it enters Japanese territorial waters, the officials said. It was the first time this year a boat reportedly carrying refugees was spotted in the vicinity of Okinawa, they said.
