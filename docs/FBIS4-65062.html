BFN [Unattributed article: "Bosnia/Italy: Giant Humanitarian Convoy Sets Out for Tuzla"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, May 10 -- After a flurry of intense coordination efforts between Rome and Sarajevo, authorities the largest humanitarian convoy yet organized by the Italian Government, in cooperation with its Bosnian counterpart, set out today for Tuzla, an Italian Foreign Ministry communique announced here. The convoy is made up of 35 local trucks with 17 belonging to the Italian Team, along with nine escort vehicles, and is carrying 450 [metric] tons of food (including 300 tons of rice) for the Bosnian city and environs. The foodstuffs come from the warehouse in the Croatian city of Split where the Italian Solidarity Consortium and Italian cooperation officials have supplies stocked. From the start of the year, Italian Cooperation has organized 27 humanitarian convoys from the base in Split, distributing some 600 tons of food and other basic necessities to various localities in Bosnia, the Farnesina communique added. Furthermore, another 16 tons of supplies were furnished to Bosnian refugees in Dalmatia over the same period, the ministry said.
