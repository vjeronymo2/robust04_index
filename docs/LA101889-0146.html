October 18, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: INTERNATIONAL KIDNAPING TRIAL OF COVINA MAN OPENS

A well-to-do Covina chiropractor was described by a prosecutor at his international kidnaping trial as a man with a violent temper who threatened to kill a former girlfriend and her daughter to recover more than $100,000 in money and property he said he was owed.

Rolando Peralta, 51, is on trial in Los Angeles federal court, accused of masterminding the kidnaping for ransom last June of a dental student in Mexico City in order to force her mother, Indiana Robleto, to pay Peralta the money he said he was owed.

Peralta, his secretary, Socorro Contreras, and her boyfriend, Rigoberto Herrera, are charged with international hostage taking, conspiracy and extortion in the abduction of Maria Robleto, 22, who was released after a $40,000 ransom was paid. Robleto, a Maywood dentist, paid the ransom but it was unclear where the funds came from.

Although the abduction occurred in Mexico, the case is being tried in federal court here because U.S. laws take precedence and because Peralta was arrested here.

In opening statements to the jury, Assistant U.S. Atty. Carol Gillam said Peralta was a "vile-tempered" man who threatened to kill Indiana Robleto in telephone conversations with her that were secretly recorded by the FBI.

But defense attorney Anthony Brooklier told jurors that Peralta was "set up" by Robleto because she hated him because of their failed love affair. He also argued that the abduction never took place, contending that Maria Robleto cooperated with her alleged kidnapers.
