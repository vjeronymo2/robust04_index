Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Mexico City, 29 Mar (EFE) -- Today, the Attorney General's Office (PRG) accused former policeman Tranquilino Sanchez Venegas of complicity in the murder of Luis Donaldo Colosio, PRI [Institutional Revolutionary Party] presidential candidate. The murder occurred on 23 March in Tijuana. Sanchez Venegas was arrested on Saturday. He then gave his deposition to the Attorney General's Office. Today, in federal court, he was accused of alleged complicity. The suspicion that he participated in the murder is based on an analysis of the videotape broadcast on television and on graphic material in the police's possession where it appears that Sanchez Venegas, while watching, opened a path through the crowd for the murderer, without acting to prevent it, as confessed murderer Mario Aburto Martinez shoots Colosio in the right temple. Various PRG officials, who asked to remain anonymous, indicated that the arrest of the former policeman was made after a lady friend of Aburto Martinez declared that on 11 March the two individuals met in a Tijuana park. According to official reports, the former policeman was employed by the Tijuana PRI for "crowd control" at the event were Colosio was murdered. Colosio was murdered on 23 March in Tijuana as he was concluding an event in his electoral campaign. The candidate was shot in the stomach and in the head. This resulted in instant brain death. Three hours later he died. The alleged murderer is being held at the Almoloya jail in Juarez, Mexico's maxiumum security jail. He is expected to be tried in the coming weeks once the investigation concludes. The PRG has indicated that for now, no one else has been arrested as an accomplice in the murder, although several individuals are being investigated.
