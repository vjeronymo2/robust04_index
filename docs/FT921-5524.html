920304 FT 04 MAR 92 / Baltic states seek renewal of trade and cultural ties AFTER a break of nearly 70 years, foreign ministers from the 10 countries surrounding the Baltic Sea will renew historic trading and cultural ties at a conference in Copenhagen this week, writes Xueling Lin in Copenhagen. The conference, which opens tomorrow, is expected to set up a regional organisation, the Council of the Baltic Sea States, to promote co-operation in a wide range of fields, including political and economic affairs, trade, the environment, energy and transport. 'The purpose of the Council is to create a political forum which will be the driving force behind future political development in the region,' said Mr Uffe Ellemann-Jensen, Danish foreign minister. The conference will be attended by foreign ministers from Denmark, Germany, Finland, Russia, Sweden, Poland, Norway, Estonia, Lithuania and Latvia.
