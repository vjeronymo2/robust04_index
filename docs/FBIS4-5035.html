BFN [Text] A formal bilateral trade commission is to be set up between Thailand and Australia. Evan Williams reports from Bangkok that the body will boost relations by providing a formal network for trade and foreign ministries: [Begin Williams recording] Launching the second leg of his Asian tour, Paul Keating meets his Thai counterpart Chuan Likphai today before addressing a business lunch and having an audience with the king. A new joint economic commission will be set up, and Thailand wants Australia to play a more active role in coordinating the future of APEC [Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation]. Tomorrow, Mr. Keating will help open the Australian-funded bridge to Laos in a ceremony chaired by the Thai king. The bridge is seen as an important sign of Australia's commitment here and a symbol of the region's new economic potential after years of war. [end recording]
