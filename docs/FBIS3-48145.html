Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 24 KYODO -- Finance Minister Hirohisa Fujii said Monday [24 January] the government will simply "wait and see" the stock market situation despite the tumble in morning trading over political turmoil. Fujii attributed the sharp decline to the muddy outlook in the political scene ahead. But "(stock price fluctuations) are a common phenomenon," he said. "We will wait and see for the time being," he said. The key Nikkei stock average of 225 selected issues plunged more than 4 percent during the two-hour session in a negative reaction to the defeat on Friday of the government's political reform bills in the House of Councillors.
