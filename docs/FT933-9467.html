930810 FT 10 AUG 93 / Lib Dem victor warns on VAT THE Liberal Democrat victor in last month's Christchurch by-election claimed yesterday that the government's plans to impose value added tax on domestic fuel bills could threaten Mr John Major's hold on Downing Street. In a letter to the prime minister Mrs Diana Maddock - who overturned a Conservative majority of 23,000 - said the by-election result showed that the imposition of VAT would be as damaging to the government as the move by former prime minister Baroness Thatcher to replace rates with a poll tax.
