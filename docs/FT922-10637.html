920430 FT 30 APR 92 / UK Company News: Exceptionals behind rise at Eadie EXCEPTIONAL profits, arising from the disposal of subsidiaries, pushed Eadie Holdings, the USM-quoted Glasgow-based specialist engineer, back into the black in the 1991 year. The sale of its Alloy Wire and Astraflex offshoots produced an exceptional Pounds 1.3m bringing the pre-tax line to Pounds 1.24m, against losses of Pounds 503,000. Operating profits rose from Pounds 566,000 to Pounds 720,000 with all the improvement occurring in the second half, directors said. Turnover dipped 14 per cent to Pounds 20.1m. If the results of the two subsidiaries are stripped out, the fall was 4 per cent. Interest charges fell to Pounds 784,000 (Pounds 1.16m); gearing at the year-end was 48 per cent, against 120 per cent 12 months earlier. Earnings worked through at 4.32p (losses of 1.1p).
