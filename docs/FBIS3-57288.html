Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Jan 21, BNS -- Mercenaries from Estonia and Lithuania fight as part of the Armenian troops in the conflict over the Nagorno-Karabakh region, said a Russian soldier taken prisoner by Azerbaijani forces in December. A BNS correspondent has seen videotapes of the interrogation of the Russian soldier, Vasily Lugovoy, by Azerbaijani Interior Minister Vagif Novruzov. Lugovoy at interrogations in the Azerbaijani capital Baku on Jan. 15 said he had met three female snipers near the Juhhar-Bahmal village in the conflict region on Dec. 28 and 29. The three woman soldiers had claimed they were from the Baltics. Lugovoy says the women had talked with what he called a Baltic accent and were either Lithuanians or Estonians. Lugovoy, a former Soviet military, served as mercenary in a pioneer unit of the Armenian troops. He said he had also previously met both male and female wage soldiers from Estonia and Lithuania. He said the Armenians valued highly the skills of "mercenaries from the Baltics" and paid them in U.S. dollars. Lugovoy said Russian soldiers earned 35,000 rubles a day in the Karabakh fighting in late 1993. The Russian soldier also said in the interrogation he had been told that "two Baltic mercenaries" were granted the rank of senior lieutenant of the Armenian Armed Forces and that they had received a personal gun as gift from Armenia. Lugovoy, born in 1960, formerly served in the Soviet troops in Germany. A BNS correspondent earlier has met with an artillery officer fighting on the Armenian side in Nagorno-Karabakh, who said he came from Estonia. The man, however, was not ethnic Estonian.
