August 12, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

PART-TIME COLLEGE TEACHERS

Why didn't Gordon mention one of the main reasons that this unfair system has been allowed to exist, at least in the Los Angeles Community Colleges? It is a verifiable fact that department chairs get a considerable stipend for every part-timer who teaches, presumably because of the paper work involved in scheduling classes.

Therefore, when a large department has three times as many part-timers as full-time teachers, the chair of that department is making (not earning) a nice addition to the base salary.

As always, these inequitable matters seem to come down to a matter of economics.

MARGIE REMAR

Van Nuys
