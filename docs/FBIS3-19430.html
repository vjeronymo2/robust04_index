Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vienna, March 16, IRNA -- Iran's ambassador to Finland, Sayyed 'Ali Mahmoudi, discussed in Helsinki Wednesday [16 March] Iran-Finnish parliamentary relations as well as international affairs with the new Finish parliament president Riitta Uosukainen. During the talks, Uosukainen referring to the importance of the Tehran-Helsinki ties stressed the need for a further development of bilateral relations, exchange of parlimentary delegations, and also parliamentary cooperations. Mahmoudi, expressing his concern with human rights abuses in the occupied Palestine and Bosnia-Herzegovina, called on Finland to actively support an end to the atrocities. The Finnish politician said: "Five years ago nobody could imagine that the world would witness new wars," she also informed Mahmoudi on the presence of Finish soldiers in Bosnia. In a separate meeting, also on Wednesday, Mahmoudi held talks with Perti Pasio, president of Finland's parliamentary Foreign Relations Committee, on the establishment of a parliamentary friendship group, and exchange of parliamentary delegations. A high ranking Iranian Parliamentary delegation, headed by Majlis Vice Speaker Hojatoleslam Dr. Hassan Rowghani, is expected to visit Finland in the near future.
