November 4, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

IT'S HUNTING SEASON IN WESTWOOD AS FANS GO AFTER THE BRUINS

Once upon a time, UCLA had a good football team. They won three of four Rose Bowls, and seven straight bowl games.

And with that success, Terry Donahue had the golden opportunity to recruit the nation's top players for future Bruin greatness. Today, those recruits are starting. I don't think Donahue knows what he's doing. He recruited five of the nation's top running backs. Please tell me what you need with five running backs? How about recruiting just one or two, and maybe some linemen to block for them!

Call me crazy, but I thought only one player at a time could carry the football.

GARY FENTON, Beverly Hills
