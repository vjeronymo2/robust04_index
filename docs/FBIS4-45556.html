BFN [Text] Defunct U.S. radar installations have permitted Colombian and Peruvian drug traffickers to once again collect drugs in the Upper Huallaga valley, from where they "moved" some years ago when the FAP [Peruvian Air Force] began operations there. Proof of this is a seizure by Dinandro [Antidrug Division] of 537 kg of cocaine base, weapons, and communications equipment on 5 June in Biabo district, Bellavista province, San Martin department. Biabo, which is on the left bank of the Huallaga River, six km from Bellavista, had been free of traffickers. The traffickers are said to have returned to the area. An individual known as "Lucho Tulon" might be one of the big shots. The drugs, weapons, and communications equipment were buried in three "cellars"--a type of silo--some 500 meters from a house that had been raided. This is one of the most important events, following the seizure of 3,500 kg of cocaine base in Boca Manu, El Diamante, Madre de Dios, and in Inapari. Actions by the Army and the Antidrug Division (Dinandro) of the PNP [Peruvian National Police] in coca producing areas in Apurimac, Ayacucho, Pasco, and Madre de Dios has helped to drive traffickers to Upper Huallaga. As is known, when FAP Tucano planes began operations in 1992, the interception of Colombian traffickers' planes was intensified. Some 200 small planes and civil planes are known to have been intercepted in coca growing areas since the start of FAP operations. FAP Tucano planes operated from illegal airstrips in the jungle, helped by the U.S. radar installations in Yurimaguas, and the Orion and AWACS planes. As of May, however, the United States suspended radar operations leaving FAP Tucano planes without classified information to help intercept traffickers' planes. The traffickers took advantage of this and have resumed their illegal work in the Huallaga region.
