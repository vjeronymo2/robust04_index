930916 FT 16 SEP 93 / Study attacks Sheehy research THE controversial Sheehy inquiry proposals for reform of police pay are based on 'flawed' and 'meaningless' research, according to a study for the Police Federation. The Sheehy inquiry looked at research into market rates in the public sector before recommending performance-related pay for the police and a drop in starting salaries. But the federation has argued that there are no comparable public-sector jobs. Professor Robert Elliott, of Aberdeen University, dismissed the inquiry's research as 'fundamentally flawed and an inappropriate basis from which to proceed'.
