BFN [Text] Santo Domingo, 14 Jun (EFE) -- The total embargo along the Dominican border with Haiti will be impossible to maintain for much longer, said Dominican Foreign Minister Juan Aristides Taveras Guzman. He has added that neither the OAS nor the United Nations have made any move to send resources to change that. Taveras has affirmed that the possibility of these international organizations helping the Dominican Republic to defray the cost of keeping 15,000 troops deployed along the Haitian border has not been discussed. Dominican authorities hope the Haitian situation will be resolved soon because it is almost impossible for the country to withstand the cost of the military deployment for much longer. The troops have been in the area since 26 May. The foreign minister added that his government has requested technical assistance to help supervise what he called the "good job" his country is doing to prevent violations of the trade embargo, which the international community has imposed on Haiti. He cited the recent OAS General Assembly, saying: "We are the last ones to want an embargo violation, and we have supported all of the resolutions issued to that effect. Furthermore, we just made a firm and definitive statement at the recent foreign ministers meeting in Brazil asking for everyone to stop scrutinizing and criticizing us." Directly addressing the Haitian problem, he said the situation has affected the Dominican Republic more that any other member of the international community. The Dominicans have had to pay a high price for the Haitian problem and, therefore, believe the criticism against them should cease. Taveras said his country is still waiting for the list of products that will be allowed to cross the Haitian border. Dante Caputo, OAS and UN representative, has promised to provide the list of items on humanitarian aid. The foreign minister has discarded the possibility of military intervention in Haiti "for the time being." Such an operation would seek to dislodge the military leaders there and restore constitutional President Jean Bertrand Aristide to power. Taveras said the international organizations are determined to use every possible peaceful, diplomatic, and political means to resolve the Haitian crisis. He affirmed that if such a military operation were to occur, it would be a multilateral effort. The United Nations, the OAS, and several countries have criticized the violations that have occurred before 26 May along the Haitian border. The embargo became totally effective 24 hours after a meeting between Dominican President Joaquin Balaguer and U.S. and OAS representatives.
