Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Interview with NATO Secretary General Manfred Woerner by Francesco Mattioli; place and date not given -- both Woerner and Mattioli speak in English with superimposed Italian translation--recorded] [Text] Following the punishment inflicted on the Serbs we have seen an increase in NATO's credibility: This is the view of NATO Secretary General Manfred Woerner, who was interviewed by RAIDUE news correspondent, Francesco Mattioli. [Mattioli] A clear-skies policy over Bosnia has been in force for months and it has been violated hundreds of times, why has NATO decided to react right now? [Woerner] It has not been violated -- as you say -- hundreds of times. This is the first violation by jets. We tolerated violations of the policy by helicopters because from a military point of view they were not significant. These were jet-fighters and furthermore our pilots saw them bomb targets on the ground. [Mattioli] This was the first military engagement for a military alliance that has existed for 45 years, how do you feel? [Woerner] Of course it is always preferable to avoid using force. In general we want to achieve our objectives without using force. However, we cannot tolerate violations of UN decisions using force, and so we went from words to actions. We are determined and I believe we have given a basic lesson that means that now everyone must take NATO seriously. [Mattioli] Do you not fear that the war will be extended? [Woerner] No, not at all, in fact it is more likely that the opposite will happen. I believe that in the future the no-fly zone will be respected.
