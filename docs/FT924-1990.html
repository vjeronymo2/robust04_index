921215 FT 15 DEC 92 / Pakistan curbs bank liquidity The state Bank of Pakistan, the country's central bank, has tightened liquidity requirements for the country's commercial banks after an 'unprecedented' monetary expansion during the past five months, writes Farhan Bokhari in Islamabad. Mr I A Hanafi, central bank governor, said yesterday the banks had been ordered to raise their liquidity ratio to 45 per cent from 40 per cent. The decision takes effect from December 19.
