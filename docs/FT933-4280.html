930909 FT 09 SEP 93 / UK Company News: Tullow Oil back in the black at halfway STRONG GROWTH in oil and gas sales helped Tullow Oil, the Irish exploration and development company, to turn in an operating profit of IPounds 271,958 (Pounds 254,000) for the first half of 1993, against a Pounds 10,343 loss last time. Sales grew by over 72 per cent to IPounds 938,479, but the directors explained that that included only one month's contribution from the Senegal well. And the sales were affected by disruption to UK production caused by testing programmes. Sales in the second half were expected to include full contributions from production in both Senegal and the UK as well as first production from the Czech licence, the, directors stated. At the attributable level, profit came out at IPounds 290,159, against IPounds 2.16m last time which included IPounds 2.19m profit on the sale of a licence interest. Earnings per share at six months fell from 1.48p to 0.2p.
