BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 25 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- As was informed by the special issue of the newspaper "ERH CHOLOO" the Mongolian Prime Minister P. Jasray has refused to sign an agreement with the participants of the hunger strike going on their action demanding the resignation of the government. He gave such reason for his refusal as that neither in the Constitution of the country nor in the law on the government there is proper provision providing for a preliminary arriving at an agreement on resignation of the government with individuals or organizations. During the meeting with the representatives of the hunger strikers on 24 April the prime minister also called upon the strikers to discontinue the hunger strike as a sign of respect of the stand by the president, the recommendations of the Council for Ensuring the National Security and the decisions by the parliament. In the agreement on concord there is provided for as a condition for ending the hunger strike the resignation of the government in case their failure to fulfill their programme of action for the year 1994. In association with the fact that the head of the government has denied this proposal it has been decided to continue the hunger strike of members and supporters of the Mongolian Democratic Association, Union of Mongolian Students and Union for New Progress demanding the resignation of the government. The hunger strike is being continued for 15 days. The participants of the counterhunger strike in support of the government have stopped their action on 23 April.
