931206 FT 06 DEC 93 / Study shows power of stock-pickers A small group of leading fund managers account for half the 'stock-picking' activity in the UK equity market, contributing to the volatility of individual share prices, according to Citywatch, a service that monitors institutional shareholdings. In contrast, many fund managers do very little real stock-picking, a Citywatch study concludes. Instead, their choices closely mimic the relative value of shares within the FT-A All-Share Index. Mr Mark O'Hare, Citywatch's chief executive, says the findings carry implications for companies' investor relations. Arguably, directors should improve communication with stock-pickers who will sell or buy shares depending on their perception of the company. Details, Page 16
