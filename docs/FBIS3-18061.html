Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Italy a Leader in Joint Ventures With Ex-USSR"] [Text] Rome, 22 Feb (ANSA) -- Some 13,000 joint ventures have so far been registered in the Soviet Union, and 8,200 of these are presently active, according to a survey made last October by the Italian publication SISTEMA ITALIA, the weekly of the Italian Trade Board and the Milan Chamber of Commerce. The value of these joint ventures has been estimated at 3 percent of the total industrial production of the Commonwealth of Independent States, while the value of joint venture exports for the first nine months of 1993 reached 3.2 billion dollars. The main markets are Japan, Germany, Italy and the United States. According to SISTEMA ITALIA, foreign investments in Russia and other ex-Soviet republics equaled 7 billion dollars last fall. Sixty percent of the investors are Russian companies, followed by 20 percent who are Italians, and a smaller percentage of Austrians, Dutch and Belgians.
