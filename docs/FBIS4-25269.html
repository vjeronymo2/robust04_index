BFN [Text] Beijing, 8 Jun (XINHUA) -- Jiang Zemin, CPC Central Committee general secretary; Li Peng, State Council premier; and Qiao Shi, National People's Congress [NPC] Standing Committee chairman, recently gave important written instructions on comprehensive management of public security in rural areas and for a national meeting in Suzhou on the comprehensive management of public security in rural areas. Jiang Zemin's written instructions were: Public security in rural areas has an extremely important effect on their stability and develolpment, and even on the whole country's stability and development. The current meeting held by the Central Committee for Comprehensive Management of Public Security [CCCMPS] is very important. Party and government leaders at all levels should earnestly carry out all measures laid down by the meeting, address themselves to practicing comprehensive management of public security in rural areas, and strive to noticeably improve public security situations in rural areas within a relatively short period. Li Peng's written instructions were: Ensuring good comprehensive management of public security in rural areas is an unshirkable responsibility of governments at all levels, particularly governments at the county and at the township and town levels. Governments at all levels should strengthen leadership and endeavor to carry out the CCCMPS's struggle aimed at improving public security in rural areas; they must make unremitting efforts to achieve practical results. All government departments concerned should actively participate in the work and provide necessary manpower, financial, and material support, to create necessary conditions for rural economic development and for the people to live and work in peace and contentment. Qiao Shi's written instructions were: To ensure good public security in rural areas, we must adhere to the principle of comprehensive management. In particular, we should rally the enthusiasm of party members, cadres, and peasants in rural areas and step up our efforts to promote democracy and the rule of law in rural areas. I hope the CCCMPS will make the current meeting successful and ensure that all decided measures are truly carried out.
