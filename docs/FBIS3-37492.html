Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow February 17 TASS -- On Thursday, the interior ministers of the member countries of the Commonwealth of Independent States concluded their two-day meeting by signing a series of agreements on cooperation. The meeting was attended by delegations from all the CIS countries, as well as an observer delegation from Latvia. The Russian delegation was headed by Interior Minister Viktor Yerin, ITAR-TASS learned in the Russian Interior Ministry's public communications centre. During their meetings, the ministers examined the effectiveness of cooperation between their respective interior ministries. In particular, they looked to strengthen their joint measures in the fight against organized crime. Decisions were adopted concerning the bureau for the coordination of the fight against organized crime and other forms of dangerous crimes committed on CIS territory. This bureau was formed in accordance with the decision of the council of CIS heads of state from September 24, 1993, and will be located in Moscow. The interior ministers confirmed S. Rukhlyadev, an official from the Belarusian Interior Ministry, as director of the bureau. His deputy, from the Armenian Interior Ministry, will be R. Kara-Pogosyan. In addition, official representatives signed an agreement on cooperation between internal ministries in the fight against organized crime. Other agreements were signed on regulations for the extradition of suspected criminals and on police escorts for especially valuable or strategic cargoes being transported from one country to another. According to the Russian Internal Ministry, it is planned to hold the next meeting in Uzbekistan.
