BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Dmitriy Gorokhov and Sergey Feoktistov] [Text] Istanbul June 10 TASS -- Russia considers the Partnership for Peace program to be an area of European cooperation, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev said here today upon the completion of his meeting with U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher. Kozyrev said he will soon visit Brussels to give a boost to the Partnership for Peace framework document and the program of Russian- NATO interaction. He said this program should not include mutual vetoes and "surprises". The Russian minister voiced the need for the soonest holding of the second conference on Bosnia with participation of ministers from Russia, the United States and five nations of the European Union but stressed the conference should be well prepared. He called on the conflicting sides for political settlement of their differences and termination of combat operations in Bosnia. In his turn, U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher said that his negotiations with Andrey Kozyrev discussed cooperation in provision of stability and security around the globe. The sides exchanged opinions of bilateral relations and called for the development of a constructive dialogue to help settlement of regional conflicts and differences.
