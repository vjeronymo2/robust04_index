BFN [Text] Yesterday, the health workers and the government reached an agreement by which the workers received a 12 percent salary increase. Therefore, after solving their problem, the health workers, about 600 of them, decided to lift the hunger strike and left for home, happy after several days of fasting. This agreement could create problems for the COB [Bolivian Labor Federation] in view of the possibility that other sectors may do the same, thus breaking up the united struggle. The COB said, however, that this agreement will not weaken the workers' struggle for their demands, which will again be negotiated. Yesterday the government asked the COB to lift the coercive measures. Today, after a COB assembly, a decision was made to maintain the hunger strike and the indefinite strike of some sectors, and to suspend the street demonstrations over the entire country, starting this afternoon, until next Sunday when the march celebrating Labor Day will be held. [Begin COB Executive Secretary Oscar Salas recording] This demonstration of flexibility, of political will shown by the COB and the workers, should be responded to by the government through new proposals which would serve as a basis for discussion to reach a solution to the conflict. [end recording] On 1 May, workers will concentrate in the Plaza San Francisco to start the march to celebrate Labor Day. And on 3 May, the COB will hold a national assembly. The government has, however, a different opinion of the decision made by the COB. The government believes that all coercive measures should be lifted to continue with the negotiations tonight. [Begin Communications Minister Ernesto Machicao recording] The information I have about the COB's decision is still unofficial in reference to the efforts made over the last few days and weeks to have the coercive measures lifted. [end recording] We should remember that the COB said it would only stop the street demonstrations, not the hungers strikes and the indefinite strike. But despite this decision, the step taken by the health workers is an important factor in the COB-government negotiations. [Begin Labor Minister Reynaldo Peters recording] The salary increase granted to the health workers and the increase we are studying for the teachers will be implemented thanks to the savings made in each of these sectors. The salaries paid to people who did not work but got paid as a result of corruption will now be used for the salary increases in those sectors. This will be one of the main proposals we will take today to the COB. [end recording] The COB, however, has not lost its hope of getting a higher salary increase percentage.
