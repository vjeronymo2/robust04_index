930924 FT 24 SEP 93 / US health insurance industry takes conciliatory approach The US health insurance industry, which would bear the brunt of price controls under the healthcare plan, took a conciliatory approach yesterday, applauding the aim of reducing costs and extending coverage, Richard Waters writes from New York. However, leading companies continued to oppose the ceiling on insurance premiums proposed in earlier drafts. Met Life, one of the big five insurers, said his plan 'contains extensive price controls, payroll-based financing and regulatory health alliances which would make managed competition unworkable.'
