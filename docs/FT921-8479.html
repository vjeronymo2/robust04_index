920217 FT 17 FEB 92 / Observer: Fast food 'Once we're geared up,' said a Yeltsin aide enthusing about his country's potential, 'within 10 years 80 per cent of Muscovites could have their own private jet.' 'But what would they want one for?' asked a western listener. 'Well,' came the reply. 'Supposing they heard there were potatoes in St Petersburg, they'd be able to get there quick.'
