920730 FT 30 JUL 92 / Tourists' outlay in England drops 4% SPENDING by tourists in England fell by 4 per cent to Pounds 14.3bn last year, the English Tourist Board said yesterday, Michael Skapinker writes. Expenditure by UK tourists staying at their destination for a night or more held up reasonably well, the board said, with revenues down 2 per cent to Pounds 7.9bn. Spending by overseas visitors fell 9 per cent to Pounds 6.3bn. Mr William Davis, board chairman, called on the government to take steps to brighten up the English weekend in an attempt to attract more visitors. He said: 'We support the idea of Sunday shopping but we also want to see more attractions, theatres and restaurants open for business on Sunday and we would like to see more sporting events, including racing.'
