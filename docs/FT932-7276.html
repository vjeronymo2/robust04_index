930525 FT 25 MAY 93 / Parliament and Politics: More franchises for BR sell-off THE government yesterday signalled its determination to proceed with the privatisation of British Rail by setting out its detailed plans for transferring the whole of the passenger network to the private sector. Mr John MacGregor, transport secretary, announced that BR's passenger services would be divided into 25 geographically based business units which would ultimately be taken over by private sector franchisees. Mr MacGregor predicted that the first franchises would be handed over to the private sector in the second half of next year and that a 'substantial proportion' of passenger services would be in private hands within the lifetime of the current parliament. However, he said it was impossible to predict how long the process would take to complete. Mr MacGregor said he placed particular importance on the fact that franchising would be an 'evolutionary' process. 'There won't be sudden dramatic difficulties in terms of big closures or anything like that,' he said. 'As people see their services improving, as people start to see the individual franchises working, they will start to see the whole point of it.' The 25 franchise areas - which include seven announced in February - will be operated by BR as 'shadow' franchises until they pass to the private sector. The aim is that they build up a trading record that will be known when the bidding begins. Mr MacGregor said the precise boundaries of the franchise areas could change if that helped the pro-cess. The Department of Transport has also yet to settle on a final scheme for franchising Regional Railways' sprawling central division, the Regional Railways long-distance services and InterCity's cross-country operations.
