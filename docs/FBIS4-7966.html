BFN [Excerpts] Belgrade, 5 April (TANJUG) -- According to a statement by the Federal Ministry of Information, at its session, chaired by Prime Minister Radoje Kontic, today the Federal Government examined and adopted the draft law on a foreign currency loan to be raised by the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia [FRY] both at home and abroad to the tune of $300 million to finance its program of transforming monetary system and the strategy of the country's economic recovery. [passage omitted] Under the draft law, the loan would be paid off over two-year period with a one-year grace period and at the interest rate and other terms as customary on the financial market. The loan will be repaid in 12 equal monthly installments and the loan repayment fund -- to equal the amount used up -- will be secured by the FRY, or rather the republics. [passage omitted] At this evening's session the Federal Government considered and approved a document entitled: "FRY Government stances on the initiatives of other states to conclude agreements on friendship and cooperation." Bearing in mind the changes which have taken place in Europe, globally and in the Balkans, as well as changes in the international position of the FRY, the Federal Government assesses that it is necessary to accept the initiatives of friendly, neighboring countries to conclude general agreements on friendship, good-neighborliness and cooperation in order to strengthen the country's international position and stimulate bilateral and multilateral cooperation. [passage omitted] The government at the same time approved the Yugoslav draft text of an agreement on friendship and cooperation between the FRY and Romania, the Federal Information Ministry's statement says at the end.
