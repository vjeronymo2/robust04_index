930427 FT 27 APR 93 / Weather hits US home sales SALES of previously owned US homes dropped 2.9 per cent in March, the third straight month of declines caused by severe weather, a real estate trade group said yesterday, AP reports from Washington. Sales fell in every region except the Midwest. The National Association of Realtors said sales of previously owned homes totalled 3.36m at a seasonally adjusted annual rate in March, down from 3.46m a month earlier. The revised 8.5 per cent drop in sales in February was even steeper than the 6.1 per cent initial estimate. Sales had fallen 6.4 per cent in January after rising 4.7 per cent in the final month of 1992. Mr William S. Chee, president of the association, said this year's big blizzard brought housing activity to a virtual standstill for several days in March in the south and north-east. But he said anecdotal evidence suggested sales had resumed: 'Buyers are continuing to take advantage of low mortgage rates.'
