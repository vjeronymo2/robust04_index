Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Sources from the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP] say that the party's secretary general and Yemeni Vice President, 'Ali Salim al-Bid, has refused to leave for Ta'izz to meet with President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih. Salih arrived in Ta'izz last night for a meeting with al-Bid which he described as a meeting of reconciliation. The sources, who preferred anonymity, said that al-Bid's attendance will depend on the implementation of the 18 points that the YSP presented as solutions and practical ways out of the current crisis and which the Salih-led General People's Congress party accepted unreservedly. The Ta'izz meeting between the two men had been prepared by the Yemeni Ulema Association. Several hours before it was scheduled to begin today a machine-gun attack was mounted on al-Bid's house in Aden. The Yemeni police who caught the assailant are interrogating him to reveal the motives for his attack. It is not known if al-Bid was at home when the attack occurred. It is worth mentioning that al-Bids has refused to leave Aden for the past five months and is demanding deep-rooted reforms in Yemen as a condition for traveling to Sanaa.
