Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan has urged the UN Human Rights Commission to send a special committee to occupied Kashmir to assess the human rights violations committed by Indian troops there. This was stated by Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahamd Ali during his talks with his French counterpart, Alain Juppe, in Paris. The foreign minister handed over a message from Pakistan's prime minister to Alain Juppe, appealing for French assistance toward setting up a special committee. Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali explained Pakistan's efforts for establishing peace in South Asia, especially toward resolving the Kashmir issue. He also informed the French foreign minister of human rights violations and atrocities committed by Indian troops in occupied Kashmir.
