BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Motherland Party leader Mesut Yilmaz left for the United States today to attend the World Forum to be held in Denver. In a predeparture statement at Istanbul's Ataturk Airport, Yilmaz said many distinguished statesmen will attend the meeting, to be held under the chairmanship of former U.S. President Gerald Ford. International security policies and topics concerning the world economy will be discussed at the meeting, Yilmaz reported. He added that possible global developments in the 2000's will also be on the agenda. Yilmaz explained that he will then proceed to Washington, where he will meet with U.S. Administration and Congressional officials. He stated: I will have the opportunity to convey our views on various issues to U.S. officials, particularly at this time when we are experiencing some tension in Turkish-U.S. relations. In reply to a question on whether he will discuss the extension of the Poised Hammer mandate with U.S. officials, Yilmaz said: [Begin Yilmaz recording] The question of whether the Poised Hammer mandate will be extended depends on the decision of the Turkish Grand National Assembly. The importance the U.S. Administration attaches to this issue is evident, and if U.S. officials raise the subject, I will convey our views to them. [end recording] Yilmaz remarked that on 20 June, he will hold a news conference on his contacts in the United States, and he will return to Turkey the following day.
