BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Nicaraguan economic developments monitored through 28 June. Presidency Minister Antonio Lacayo on 21 June reported that the government, during a meeting with the World Bank Consultative Group in Paris, obtained a package of $750 million for programs that will be implemented in the next three years, and not just in 1994 as was initially reported. He added that Nicaragua requested another $180 million but the Consultative Group will discuss the request at upcoming meetings. (Managua BARRICADA in Spanish 22 Jun 94 p 5) Central Bank President Jose Evenor Taboada announced on 27 June that the World Bank the previous week granted a $60 million credit to support the economic recovery program. He confirmed that the IMF on 25 June approved a $173 million loan to help the country ease the effects of the structural adjustment program that the Chamorro administration has implemented since March 1992. Taboada added that World Bank, IMF, and international community assistance will help the country to stabilize its economy. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 0052 GMT 28 Jun 94)
