920422 FT 22 APR 92 / Royal Bank of Scotland suspends staff in dispute MORE than 40 Royal Bank of Scotland staff in Glasgow have been suspended as a dispute between Bifu, the banking union, and Royal Bank intensified yesterday. The dispute, originally about pay, now centres on whether Bifu members are entitled to withdraw from overtime they normally work. A total of 41 Bifu members have been suspended for refusing to work 'contractual overtime'. The bank said: 'Staff who refused to work normal hours have been sent home. They are free to resume work as soon as they agree to restrict any industrial action to legitimate voluntary or abnormal overtime.' The bank said action over the Easter weekend had had an 'insignificant effect' on services. It anticipated minimal disruption at affected branches, all of which are in the centre of Glasgow. The union launched an overtime ban and work-to-contract action just before the Easter weekend as part of its campaign of industrial action over pay. It has rejected an offer of between 2.5 per cent and 3.5 per cent for clerical grades. It has also rejected a performance-related pay package for managers, which it says fails to guarantee a rise. Bifu has asked Mrs Gillian Shephard, the employment secretary, to intervene in the dispute. It alleges that the bank's management is intimidating Bifu members engaged in legal industrial action. Mr Leif Mills, Bifu general secretary, is due to address a meeting of Royal Bank of Scotland staff, including those who are suspended, in Glasgow this evening.
