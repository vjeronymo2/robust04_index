Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Lyudmila Aleksandrova and Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow February 2 TASS -- The Federation Council adopted today on the whole the procedural rules of the upper chamber, which consist of 13 chapters, as well as a resolution on the procedural rules of the first Federation Council. The legislators discussed amendments to two out of the three parts of the rules, which deal with general procedural regulations of holding sessions of the upper chamber, as well as the procedure of the discussion of laws adopted by the State Duma. The Editing Commission will submit its remarks concerning the third part of the procedural rules before March 1. This part deals with the discussion of issues, which, in accordance with Article 102 of the Constitution, are within the authority of the federation Council. These include the approval of the changing of borders between subjects of the Russian federation, of presidential decrees on imposing martial law, the taking of a decision on whether or not Russian troops should be used outside the Russian territory, the naming of the date of presidential elections, the impeachment of the president and so on. Members of the Federation Council assigned the Committee for the Affairs of the Federation, the Federal Treaty and Regional Policy to take part in the forthcoming talks in Moscow between Russian and Tatarstan on concluding a federal treaty. They decided as well to assign the committee to analyse the problem of starting negotiations with the Chechen Republic and to report the results to the upper chamber. A break until Thursday was announced at the session of the Federation Council. The agenda of the Thursday meeting includes a government report about its intentions in the sphere of the financial, crediting, tax, agrarian and industrial policy. Initially it was planned that the report would be made by head of the government Viktor Chernomyrdin. Today, however, Chairman of the Federation Council Vladimir Shumeyko did not specify today who would make the report.
