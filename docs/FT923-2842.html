920916 FT 16 SEP 92 / Actuaries agree heavy cuts in policy bonuses SUBSTANTIALLY smaller bonuses will be paid in future on millions of with-profits life assurance and pension policies, actuaries meeting at Harrogate agreed yesterday. Many life companies are anxious about the impact of lower bonuses - caused by poor investment results in the past five years - on the public and on intermediaries such as brokers and salesmen. Some cuts were made last year but many offices maintained their bonuses in the vain hope that investment conditions would improve. Yesterday Mr John Martin, president of the Institute of Actuaries, chaired an unprecedented meeting following the profession's annual conference. Speakers stressed the fundamental structural changes on the investment markets following the entry of the UK into the European Exchange Rate Mechanism. It was not a question of once-and-for-all cuts, speakers stressed. Reductions would continue for some years unless conditions were to change. The meeting was concerned about how to present the message to the public, and some actuaries said they feared a struggle with life company boards over setting a responsible bonus strategy. One speaker called on appointed actuaries - the company officials who have a statutory responsibility to safeguard the solvency of their life offices in spite of competitive pressures to pay high bonuses - to 'stand up and make sure that their views are heard. Marketing considerations must take second place.' Appointed actuaries also have a duty to consider policyholders' 'reasonable expectations'. Speakers said those expectations had to be reduced. 'We have to get out and explain that something fundamental is happening here,' one actuary said. Support was offered for company actuaries whose views were ignored. 'Anyone who is getting into a difficult situation and would welcome confidential discussions with senior members of the profession is very much encouraged to get in touch,' said a senior actuary. Mr Martin said: 'This is a very important actuarial matter indeed. Bonuses should be coming down. The debate is about when and how.'
