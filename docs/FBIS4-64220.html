BFN [Text] Koenigswinter (DPA) -- The Dalai Lama has encouraged the FRG to take over responsibility in international politics. He hopes that, as a result of its special historical development, Germany will play an important role in establishing world peace, the secular and spiritual head of Tibet said in Koenigswinter near Bonn on Saturday [30 April]. The Dalai Lama, who has lived in exile in India for 35 years, pointed to the "particularly friendly relations" between Germany and China. However, this should not lead to a strong tolerance toward the Chinese Government. Germany has the opportunity to influence Chinese policy. The 58-year-old man stressed that awareness of the special situation in his homeland is widespread among the German public. The Nobel Peace Prize laureate affirmed his path of nonviolence. "I am, on principle, against the use of weapons." This is also the basis of the support granted to the Tibetan people by the world public. The Dalai Lama is paying a three-day visit to Germany on the invitation of the Friedrich-Naumann Foundation, which is affiliated with the Free Democratic Party of Germany.
