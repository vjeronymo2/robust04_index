BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Nataliya Cherepanova] [Text] St. Petersburg May 14 TASS -- "Our main objective is to pool the efforts of Christian Democrats of the world in combatting the nuclear danger," Vice President of the International Christian Democrats Andre Louis said in his message of greetings to participants in the fourth international congress "Christian Democracy Today" which opened here today. According to the vice president, North Korea's reluctance to join the nonproliferation movement is one such danger. The Christian Democratic International has always aimed and continues to aim to make the treaty on the renunciation of possession, testing and use of nuclear weapons in definite and encourage as many countries as possible to join it. The congress, which is being attended by delegates from 18 countries of Europe, Asia and Africa, will discuss, during its three days of work, problems of dialogue between different denominations of Christianity, Buddhism and Islam, plans for the expansion of activity of Christian Democrats in African and Commonwealth countries and specify the conceptual framework for the development of the organisation after the disintegration of the socialist camp in Eastern Europe.
