941027 FT 27 OCT 94 / Flights to India resume Eleven airlines yesterday announced resumed flights to India after the World Health Organisation declared most of the country plague-free. Air India, the international carrier, has been allowed to resume its services to Dubai and Doha, while Emirates (Dubai's airline), Gulf Air and Kuwait Airways have also resumed their Indian services after nearly a month. But Abu Dhabi, Sharjah and other members of the United Arab Emirates, as well as Pakistan, Bangladesh and some Russian and central Asian countries, have yet to lift the ban on flights.
