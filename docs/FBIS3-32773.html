Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 10 Feb (DPA) -- Several gunmen on a highway leading to the Colombian city of Ibague murdered Colombian Ambassador to Honduras Lucelly Garcia today. Garcia, 60, had presented her credentials to the Honduran Government 14 days ago and had returned to Colombia to present a book entitled "Petition for Protection, Opening to Democracy." The police reported the diplomat was traveling from the coffee-producing area of Calarca, her hometown, to Santa Fe de Bogota in the company of her sister Ruby Garcia, the Colombian consul to Mexico, who was injured. Ruby Garcia said a four-wheel drive vehicle blocked the path of the car in which they were traveling, and two men came out of it, and one of them shot the ambassador, who was taken to a clinic in Ibague where she died. The circumstances surrounding the attack on Ambassador Garcia are unclear. The police said robbery could have been the motive, but murder was not ruled out. Garcia, a Liberal, was governor of the western Department of Quindio, where Calarca is located. She was also a member of the Lower Chamber of the Colombian Congress. She lost her seat over a scandal about alleged irregular activities favoring her hometown's Culture House. For more than 30 years the diplomat's political life was characterized by her combativeness, which was reflected in strong speeches, vigorous decisions, and constant clashes with her adversaries. Her body will reportedly be taken to Santa Fe de Bogota in the next hours for an homage at the Congress of the Republic, political friends of the murdered ambassador said.
