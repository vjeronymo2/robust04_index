931021 FT 21 OCT 93 / UK Company News: UDO shows decline to Pounds 3.73m and looks for acquisitions UDO HOLDINGS, which supplies drawing office equipment and reprographic services, saw pre-tax profits fall from Pounds 5.71m to Pounds 3.73m over the year to July 31 on reduced sales of Pounds 48.5m, against Pounds 51.7m. Earnings per share fell to 8.99p (13.66p); the recommended final dividend, however, goes up to 5.13p giving a total of 7.2p (6.7p). 'The dividend remains covered and the company has substantial cash balances,' said Mr Graham Ede, finance director. Mr Mike Wright, chairman, said the company had tried to offset declining demand for diazo equipment - used to copy large format drawings using light sensitive paper or film - by focusing on higher technology, higher margin products as part of a continuing programme of measures, intended to counter recession in its main markets. Operating profits, including a small gain on the disposal of fixed assets, reached Pounds 2.74m (Pounds 4.34m). UDO generated Pounds 1.5m in cash, bringing cash balances to Pounds 14.8m (Pounds 13.3m) at the year end, about 50 per cent of net assets, with which it plans to hit the acquisition trail. Interest receivable came to Pounds 992,000 (Pounds 1.37m). Mr Wright said: 'Although no acquisitions were made during the year the group is actively looking at potential candidates in the UK and Europe.'
