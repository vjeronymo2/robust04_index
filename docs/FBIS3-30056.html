Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, February 22 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The central Mongolian newspapers are paying a great attention to the official visit of the Mongolian President P. Ochirbat to India, Thailand, Laos, Vietnam, which began yesterday. As the newspaper ARDIYN ERH informs, the President P. Ochirbat sent telegrams of greeting to the chairman of the PRC Jiang Zemin and the Chairman of the State Council on Restoration of Law and Order of the Federal State Myanmar [Burma] Than Shwe. The newspaper informs that before leaving on a visit to the countries of Asia the President P. Ochirbat has received the representatives of Bayanhongor Aymag at their request. They have acquainted the president with their life and activity, in particular, with the course of realization of a number of production programmes. The newspaper notes that the President P. Ochirbat during the meeting with the representatives of Bayanhongor Aymag has pointed out to a special importance of development of infrastructure. As the newspaper informs, the representatives of Bayanhongor Aymag have been received by the Prime Minister P. Jasray. The newspaper publishes an information about the government sitting. At the sitting the basic attention has been paid to training and retraining of lawyers. The newspaper of the Mongolian capital ULAANBAATAR publishes an article devoted to problems of debt of Mongolia to Russia. In the article it is noted that, according to the calculations, a debt worth of 850 grams of gold to Russia falls on each citizen of Mongolia. In other words, Mongolia is in the debt to Russia worth of 1700 tonnes of gold. The newspaper notes that gold at present is still in popular demand at the world market. The newspaper publishes an article about the wellknown figure Dambiijantsan, about whom the wellknown Mongolists A. Pozdneyev, O. Lattimore and other scientists have been writing. Dambiijantsan, who is from western Mongolia, was a prominent figure of liberation movement. The newspaper publishes an article devoted to creative activity of markes [as received], problems of modern literature.
