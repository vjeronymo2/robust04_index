941128 FT 28 NOV 94 / Business Travel: Jamaica safer than US Crime-weary Jamaica is touting the results of a new study which found that visitors are at higher risk in various US cities than in Jamaica. A study by Richard Bennett of American University, Washington, found that visitors are three times more likely to be killed in the US capital than in Jamaica. Using 1992 statistics, the study compared Washington, Orlando, Miami and New York with the Jamaican resorts of Ocho Rios, Montego Bay, Negril and Port Antonio. It was found that Jamaica's resorts have a combined murder rate of 22.58 per 100,000, compared with 75.21 per 100,000 for Washington and 37.89 per 100,000 for Miami.
