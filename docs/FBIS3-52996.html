Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Khartoum 9 Jan (SUNA) -- The total amount of relief supplies transported to affected areas in the southern states by various means have now reached 62,000 t [tonnes]. This was said to SUNA by Mr. Awad Khalifah, commissioner general of the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission, who stressed that relief supplies had been taken to the affected areas without any discrimination -- including areas penetrated by the rebel movement. He also said that the third fleet of river steamers carrying relief supplies from Kosti to Juba in Equatoria State had begun to move. He pointed out that preparations for the movement of the third train from Babanusah to Wau in Bahr al-Ghazal State was complete. He went on to say that this year would witness a process of concentration on reconstruction, in addition to the expansion of overland transport. He also said that work was now going on to rehabilitate the Malakal Hospital in Upper Nile State, within the framework of Sudanese-French cooperation. He said that the hospital would provide medical services for the displaced persons and the returnees.
