941213 FT 13 DEC 94 / 'Bazaar' plan angers ex-GLC boss A former Tory leader of the Greater London Council protested yesterday against new plans for its former headquarters, County Hall, saying the building was in danger of being turned into 'an eastern bazaar'. Lord Plummer urged ministers at Lords question time to ensure that the redevelopment was carried out in accordance with its planning permission. The original proposals from the building's owners, the Japanese property company Shirayama, included provision - recently abandoned - for a 600-bedroom hotel. It is now planned to turn the building into a Pacific Asia centre. Lord Plummer, GLC leader from 1967-73, told peers: 'County Hall occupies a prime location in the heart of the capital and should not be allowed to lapse into an eastern bazaar.' Lord Inglewood, for the government, said it was for Lambeth council, not ministers, to ensure the planning rules were observed. But he added that English Heritage was satisfied that the works accorded with the planning consents. Tory Lord Beloff said it would have been far better if the building had been taken over by the London School of Economics as a 'major educational edifice'. Baroness Hollis of Heigham, for Labour, said two commercial schemes for the building had collapsed, the Shirayama deal had been a 'shambles' and possible future use by the public sector should be considered. But Lord Inglewood said: 'Anyone who owns a building and has planning permission for it is entitled to implement that permission.'
