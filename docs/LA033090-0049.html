March 30, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

JUDGMENTS ON THE KCBS 'OFFICE ROMANCE'

When Bree Walker says "I don't think it has anything to do with my news-gathering abilities . . ." in defense of her apparent adulterous actions, it reminds me of Gary Hart.

He used the same words in a vain attempt to defend his ability to run the country after being accused by the press and finally admitting the adultery during a presidential campaign.

It's time the Fourth Estate stopped having it both ways. If you are going to call politicians to judgment on moral grounds, you must open yourselves to moral scrutiny.

If I have a right to know the integrity of my political representatives as defined by their personal lives, I have a right to know the same of my journalistic representatives.

After all, the fact is that journalists have as much influence as politicians over the life of the average citizen.

BARRY COLMAN, Long Beach
