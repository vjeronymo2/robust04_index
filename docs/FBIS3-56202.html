Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow January 13 TASS -- A hard-line lawmaker from the Agrarian Party, who was a leader of the Communist faction of the disbanded Russian Supreme Soviet, is most likely to become the speaker of the state Duma, the lower house of the Russian parliament. Ivan Rybkin got 233 votes during a so-called "rating vote" on Thursday which is far above the majority of the Duma. Yuriy Vlasov, another conservative lawmaker, was second with 200 votes. Rybkin's success should be attributed also to a representative of the communist party, Valentin Kovalev, who withdrew his candidacy in favour of Rybkin just before the vote. The winner will be determined on Friday morning when the deputies would elect one of the two lawmakers.
