May 29, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: MOVIES

A Blow to the Stomach?: Heavyweight boxer Evander Holyfield is offering fellow pugilists James Buster Douglas and Mike Tyson easy money; easy, that is, if they can sit through a horror movie. "Blood Salvage," which Holyfield helped finance and in which he makes an appearance, tells the story of a south Georgia auto mechanic. The grease monkey also moonlights as a dealer in transplanted organs, which he extracts from, shall we say, unwilling donors. In newspaper ads for the movie, which opened Friday in Atlanta, Holyfield says: "This movie is so scary that I'm willing to donate $10,000 to the charity of their choice if Mike Tyson and Buster Douglas have the guts to sit through an entire screening on their own! Are you man enough?" GREG BRAXTON
