Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Feb.25 (A.A) -- Deputy spokesman of parliament Vefa Tanir rejected a motion given by 12 deputies which demanded the restoring of the honor of a group of anti-Kemalists who were tried and executed 70 years ago as their plot to assassinate Turkey's leader Mustafa Kemal Ataturk has been brought into light. Tanir sent a letter to fundamentalist Prosperity Party member Hasan Mezarci, who led 11 more deputies in the initiative, saying that, "your motion which attempts to degrade a certain period of the Turkish Grand National Assembly and Mustafa Kemal Ataturk, our great leader who has founded the Turkish state and republic and which, hurts the feelings of the public opinion, has been rejected, under article 68 of the parliamentary regulations." Anti-Kemalist politician Hasan Mezarci, who announces in every occasion that he is against the secularist and democratic principles of Mustafa Kemal Ataturk, led eleven more deputies from various parties, in his initiative. As the only True Path Party member in the motion, Melik Firat had been subjected to disciplinary action by his party just after the motion was handed to parliament, another signatory, Okkes Sendiller from Great Unity Party (UBP) decided to withdraw his name from the motion.
