BFN [Text] Bishkek, 16 Jun -- "The som has strengthened its position," said Prime Minister Abbas Dzhumagulov in an interview with a KYRGYZKABAR correspondent. "Judge for yourself. The inflation rate in February this year was 16.9 percent and in June, 3.5 percent. The national currency has also stabilized. One dollar now costs not 12.35 soms as in February, but 11.4. In March-April, production became more brisk and the tendency could have been maintained had inflation slowed down, but [word indistinct] on all kinds of industrial goods over a month has decreased by 28 percent. All of the national economy's indicators have deteriorated. To improve the situation, we need investors from outside, and we are looking for them. I recently met Salem Ehasesom [name as received], the representative of the World Bank. The topic under discussion was attracting investments to the mining industry. We have been told that the World Bank is looking for the funds to carry out a tender so as to finance geological surveys in this area. The problems are expected to be settled during a meeting in June in the U.S. city of Houston. We know that the donor countries, such as Norway, will render us consultative services not only for surveying oil fields, but also in elaborating the law on the use of nature. "The Government of Kyrgyzstan is eager to render any kind of support to foreign entrepreneurs in surveying and developing gas and oil fields. "The issue of investments will be the main topic on the agenda of the second meeting of the Japan-Kyrgyzstan economic commission, which is due to take place in November. I have the honor of being the chairman of this commission. We have prepared a number of projects for joint ventures with Japan in the food industry, for example, in the production of rice, baby food, silk, leather, and also in the mining industry. We should invigorate prospecting for oil and gas. Kyrgyzstan will give an advantage to those companies who have experience in developing oil and gas fields. He also said [as received] that in the near future we intend to open a free economic zone centered in Tokmak, near the capital. "We can only add here that over one month, the money income of the population has decreased by 3 percent and spendings and savings have increased by 12.9 percent. In reality, accumulations have been minimal. In January-May, the savings bank of the Republic has attracted 5-percent less in savings than during the same period last year. "There has also been a slowdown in business activities. For example, a number of commodity and raw materials exchanges has decreased twofold. More than 60 percent of their turnover comes from the machine-building industry, diesel fuel, and timber. "In a nutshell, inflation has been brought to a minimum, but we have not yet started the structural transformations in industry. The Republic is awaiting a large Western credit, but it has not created conditions for invigorating investment activity."
