BFN [Excerpt] Buenos Aires, 10 May (NA) -- Finance Secretary Ricardo Gutierrez today denied reports indicating that the IMF would ask for a 10 percent cutback in the 1994 budget to be implemented. He emphasized that the fiscal surplus targets agreed upon for the first quarter "were surpassed." Gutierrez noted that the surplus agreed upon with the IMF "totaled $640 million, and this target was surpassed by $60 million as a $700 million surplus was achieved." The finance secretary also believes that the IMF delegation will issue a favorable report on Argentine fiscal accounts. Gutierrez added: "What we have done so far will not represent any problem or be deserving of any remarks from the IMF." The finance secretary described as "entirely inaccurate" the reports on IMF concerns over the first quarter's fiscal results. He confirmed that this "is entirely mistaken information." Gutierrez emphasized that "the first quarter's targets were surpassed." The finance secretary said the IMF delegation would meet on 12 or 13 May with the financial team. In addition to Gutierrez, the financial team also includes Economic Programming Secretary Juan Llach and Public Revenues Secretary Carlos Tacchi. Gutierrez made these remarks after an economic team meeting at the Economy Ministry today. [passage omitted]
