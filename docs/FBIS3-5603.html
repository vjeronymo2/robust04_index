Language: English Article Type:BFN [Station commentary] [Text] Dutch Foreign Minister Peter Kooijmans and his wife will soon visit Vietnam at the invitation of the Vietnamese Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam. We bring you a review of recent development in relations between Vietnam and Holland. It is the first visit to Vietnam by the Dutch foreign minister. His visit will contribute to strengthening cooperative relations between the two countries in all areas. The leaders can consider future cooperation. Vietnam and Holland have had established diplomatic relations since 1973, but with some interruptions. However, by late of 1993, Holland decided to reopen its embassy in Vietnam. Many delegations from the two countries have exchanged visits over the past few years. Holland has developed many goodwill attitude to Vietnam [as heard]. At the Paris Conference, Holland was one of the countries which agreed to prolong the time for Vietnam to pay its debt over a 30 year period. The two countries have initiated an agreement on investment encouragement and protection. Vietnam and Holland have also concluded agreements on air transport and on river and sea dredging. Holland has a number of projects in Vietnam involving cooperation and investment. They have effectively cooperated in the field of health care. Now, they are hoping to cooperate in infrastructure building. Although Holland ranks the ninth among the investors in Vietnam, by now it has the 10 investment projects in Vietnam capitalized at $218 million, including the joint venture in oil and gas exploration, and another joint venture in producing Heineken Beer. With existing relations, the visit to Vietnam this time of the Dutch foreign minister will surely be a landmark in relations between the two countries that help bring bilateral relations to a new level. The Vietnamese people want to welcome this visit to Vietnam of the Dutch foreign minister and his wife, and wish it a success.
