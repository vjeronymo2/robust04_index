Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] Socorro Diaz, deputy secretary for civilian protection at the Government Secretariat, read today a 28-page document with important information on the armed groups that have provoked violence in Chiapas State. She said the so-called Zapatist National Liberation Army [EZLN] is an extremist organization. [Begin Diaz recording, in progress] ...violent, professional, and well trained. Its profile is highly ideological and uses a language that is characteristic of extremist guidelines. [end recorded] The armed group includes the participation of ideologists and clergy; there are medics and transmission technicians, and it is comprised of Mexicans and foreigners. Its operation strategy for 1993 consisted of advocating the invasion of lands and provoking an overall violence. In certain cases it kidnaps children to force their parents to join the armed group. Referring to the group's weapons, Diaz said it has machine guns, shotguns, revolvers, and sophisticated weapons that are suited for guerrilla warfare. The weapons were manufactured mainly in the United States, Russia, and the UK. [Begin Diaz recording] The so-called EZLN appeared in 1993 in five municipalities of Chiapas State--Ocosingo, Las Margaritas, Comitan, Altamirano, and Chanal. It has an organized network of activists cells that are in permanent contact with civilian band radios. It has 15 training centers in various municipalities; six of them are located in Las Margaritas, five in Ocosingo, two in Sabanillas, one in Altamirano, and one in Chanal. [end recording] The report read by Mrs. Socorro Diaz is based on information that was provided by the National Defense Secretariat and the Attorney General's Office. She said the group has martial arts instructors and health promoters. Each squad of activists is comprised of one sergeant, one corporal, one radio operator, one health promoter, and 20 activists. The group has 172 civilian band radio stations in its camps, and authorities have found propaganda of the Revolutionary Workers Clandestine People's Union Party [Procup], which the group supports by distributing its publications. The aggressor group is comprised of and supported by various extremist organizations. They have safe houses and assembly centers where they show political-revolutionary movies to the people. Diaz said many of its leaders have already been identified.
