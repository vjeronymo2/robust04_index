Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] San Jose, 7 Mar (ACAN-EFE) -- Costa Rican President Rafael Angel Calderon said today that he will talk to Nicaraguan President Violeta Chamorro about the dispute over the San Juan River, which delimits the border between the two countries, during her 27 March visit. San Jose Municipality invited Chamorro to visit Costa Rica to open the Nicaragua Park in this capital. She will seize the opportunity to meet with Calderon. The dispute emerged over an alleged five-dollar charge, which is considered illegal by Costa Rican authorities, collected from tourists navigating along the San Juan River which delimits almost 200 of the 320 km that the two countries share. Last weekend the Costa Rican president hinted that the next administration, which will be inaugurated on 8 May, could take this dispute to the International Court of Justice, headquartered at The Hague (the Netherlands), in case it cannot be solved through other means. Calderon today stressed the cordial relationship between Costa Rica and Nicaragua and said that he will talk to Chamorro to find a solution to the said charge which Nicaraguan authorities consider legal. Nicaragua has sovereignty over the San Juan River, which drains Lake Nicaragua, but according to border treaties signed last century, Costa Rica also has the right to navigate it freely. Costa Rican Foreign Minister Bernd Niehaus today told ACAN-EFE that the matter could be taken to the International Court of Justice at The Hague, but he is hopeful it can be solved through talks between the two nations. He said that he hoped to talk today to his Nicaraguan counterpart, Ernesto Leal, to find a solution to this problem, which arose due to Costa Rican tourist enterprises.
