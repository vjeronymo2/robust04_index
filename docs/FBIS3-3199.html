Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 17 (XINHUA) -- The World Health Organization (WHO) has recently designated the Dongcheng District Health Bureau of Beijing as the WHO collaborative center for urban health development. S. T. Han, a regional director of WHO, unveiled a plaque for the center here today. According to an official from the center, this collaborative center is aimed at enhancing the development of urban health care. It will study and report health problems caused by rapid urbanization and improve health care services, lifestyles and public policies for urban health development. The center will also provide technical information and training for national and foreign professionals and support WHO's project activities in urban health development, said the official.
