Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Antonio Barragan; from the "Vector 21" newscast] [Text] Cuauhtemoc Cardenas Solorzano, presidential candidate of the Party of the Democratic Revolution [PRD], met during the morning hours with students of the Acatlan Professional Studies Collegiate of the National Autonomous University of Mexico [UNAM]. Cardenas met with students from the Science and Humanities College during the afternoon hours. During the morning meeting with the students, he said it is not only necessary to remove those Chiapas State high-level officials such as the governor or [words indistinct] but also to remove those state municipal presidents who it was proven have contrived with land owners and local bosses [caciques] to humiliate the Indian groups. The PRD presidential candidate addressed the students of the Eastern Science and Humanities College during the afternoon. On this occasion, he said the Mexican Government had coincided for the first time with the PRD and its way of thinking concerning the arrangement of a cease-fire in Chiapas State, followed by an amnesty, and afterward by dealing with this issue politically. He said: This is the first time the government and I have agreed on something. In expanding on this issue while addressing the students at a PRD organized meeting, Cuauhtemoc Cardenas stated: [Begin Cardenas recording] If a political solution is not found and the state continues to believe the solution lies in military measures, we will be going through a long-term and prolonged conflict that will surely affect the country's public life. [end recording] The PRD candidate was asked later if the presidential candidates should intervene in meetings planned to learn of the EZLN [Zapatist National Liberation Army] problems. In this respect he answered that as far as he is concerned, he is not going to go to where he has not been invited. He added that that issue is the responsibility of the coordinator appointed by the president of the republic to try and solve the problems of Chiapas.
