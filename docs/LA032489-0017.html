March 24, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

MENTAL HEALTH SYSTEM CRISIS

I refer to your editorial "Answering a Distress Call," March 2.

I go to a county mental health clinic in San Diego County. I see a psychiatrist for 15 minutes every six weeks. I take medicine. I believe that the homeless mentally ill should be placed in state hospitals until they can be placed on SSI and in a board-and-care home, or by themselves, or with their families.

As a former mentally ill person, I was glad to get the shelter, food and medicine at a state hospital. It costs about $4,000 a month per person to be in a state hospital. It costs about $1,000 a month per person to be on SSI, Medi-Cal and live in the community.

I am a pen pal with mental patients all over the country. Most of them are doing well. Many of them work full-time. I'm sure the police do take many mentally ill people to local hospitals which then ship them to the state hospitals until they can be placed. This happened to me in 1984. Please lobby for the hospitalization of the homeless mentally ill. The state should build new state hospitals.

TERRY CORDOBA

San Marcos
