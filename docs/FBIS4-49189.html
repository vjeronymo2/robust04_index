BFN [Text] Helsinki, May 4 (XINHUA) -- Li Ruihuan, chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC), and Finnish Foreign Minister Heikki Haavisto held talks here today on bilateral and international issues of mutual concern. During the talks, Li said the two countries have made great achievements in exchanges and cooperation in the economic, scientific and other fields and China is satisfied with the smooth development of Sino-Finnish relations. On China's foreign policy, Li said China pursues a policy characterized by independence and self-reliance with peace and development as its goals. This is the first visit to Europe ever paid by China's CPPCC chairman and its purpose is to deepen mutual understanding, expand exchanges and enhance cooperation. Haavisto said China's great success in economic construction has won respect and admiration from the people of the world. "Finland hopes to further cooperate with China in various fields and take an active part in China's economic construction," the foreign minister said. He added that Li's visit will deepen China's understanding of Finland, promote the friendship between the two peoples and boost economic and trade relations between the two countries. The two leaders also exchanged views over international issues of mutual concern. Li arrived here Monday [2 May] and has met with Finnish President Martti Ahtisaari and parliament Speaker Riitta Uosukainen. He is on an official goodwill visit to five European countries -- Finland, Sweden, Norway, Denmark and Belgium.
