Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 10 KYODO -- Broader exemptions to the land-holding tax are being considered as a way to stimulate the stagnant economy, government sources said Monday [10 January]. The tax, introduced January 1, 1992, is aimed at curbing Japan's astronomically high land prices. The business community has called for the tax to be scrapped or its rate of 0.3 percent to be lowered. While refusing to accept these calls, the Finance Ministry has decided to increase the exemptions from the tax. Points being considered are a cut in the tax burdens on holders of land which is open to the public, public parking lots or sites for urban development, the sources said. They said the plan will substantially ease the tax burden on retailers, financial institutions, realtors and the leisure industry, which pay hefty taxes at present because much of their land is located in big cities.
