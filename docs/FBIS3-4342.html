Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Benjamin Yeh] [Text] Taipei, March 28 (CNA) -- The Republic of China [ROC] and the Central African Republic on Monday [28 March] signed a joint communique pledging to step up bilateral friendly relations. Foreign Affairs Minister Chien Fu and his Central African Republic Counterpart Simon Bedava-Ngaro signed the communique on behalf of their countries. The deal came at the close of the first meeting of the mixed commission comprising officials from both countries. Topics discussed during the six days of talks were economic and technical cooperation, transportation issues facing the African country, cooperation in rural development, medical and educational cooperation, and mining cooperation. Chien told the press that Bedaya-Ngaro had reiterated his country's full support of the ROC's bid to join the United Nations. The commission will meet in the Central African Republic next year. While here, Bedaya-Ngaro called on President Li Teng-hui and Premier Lien Chan.
