BFN [Text] Russian President Boris Yeltsin addressed the nation on the occasion of the eighth anniversary of the Chernobyl disaster. He said that April 26 was a day of mourning to those who died in radiation emergencies and disasters. He paid homage to those who were exposed to radiation while fighting the disaster. He said that the country would not forget those who were still living in areas contaminated by nuclear radiation. In an address released by the presidential press service Yeltsin said April 26 was a day when the danger of nuclear disasters was especially obvious and that everything had to be done to make the world a safer place. "On the day when Russia bows its head in memory of those who died in radiation disasters I express my deep condolences to the Russians and citizens of other countries whose relatives and friends fell in a fight with a deadly force and shielded us from disaster," the address says.
