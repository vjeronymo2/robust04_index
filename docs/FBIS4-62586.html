BFN [Text] In the near future the research and production association Hartron (Kharkov) will sign a contract with the American Westinghouse company for the production of modernized nuclear reactor protection systems for Ukrainian nuclear power stations. This was disclosed to INTERFAX-Ukraine by the marketing director of Hartron Alexander Bek. On 17-19 May, American representatives are to visit Hartron to work out the details of a business plan. During the visit to the U.S.A. of a Ukrainian Government delegation led by Vice-Premier Valeriy Shmarov an agreement was signed on the American government offering five million dollars for the project. It was the first bilateral agreement promoting the conversion of the Ukrainian defence industry. Hartron was named by Ukraine as a leading organization for reconstructing control systems for nuclear power stations. It is known in Ukraine as an experimental producer of control systems used in missile and space programs. According to Bek, the money offered by the American Government should be spent on the purchase and installment of new equipment, the organization of testing and the training of personnel. Bek said that according to unwritten rules the contribution of Westinghouse is going to be four times greater than that of the American Government. As its own contribution to the project Hartron is offering its production capacities, premises, staff and intellectual potential. In Bek's opinion, the contract with Westinghouse should be long-term and not limited to this project.
