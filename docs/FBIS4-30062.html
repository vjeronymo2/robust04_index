BFN [From the "NHK News 7" program] [Text] The ruling coalition parties and the opposition Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] held talks at 1930 [1030 GMT] this evening to discuss ways to form a new coalition government. NHK reporter Kusada will report on their talks from the Diet. [Begin Kusada recording] The third round of talks between the ruling coalition parties and the SDPJ ended just a while ago. Prime Minister Hata and SDPJ Chairman Murayama are set to hold a top-level meeting at 2100 this evening. The ruling coalition parties and the SDPJ are likely to resume their talks at 1100 tomorrow morning after seeing the results of their top-level meeting. The ruling coalition parties and the SDPJ held three rounds of talks this morning, this afternoon, and this evening. During the talks, they came closer to agreeing that an extraordinary Diet session must be called as soon as possible to pass a bill on demarcating the single-seat constituencies, but they failed to reach an agreement on the question of handling the tax system reform. (end recording)
