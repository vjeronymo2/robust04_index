Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Knin, 18 Mar (TANJUG) -- The Executive Committee of the Serb Democratic Party of Krajina [SDSK], chaired by former Krajina President Milan Babic, today rejected a proposal put forward by Milan Martic, president of the republic of Serb Krajina, to appoint Borislav Mikelic as prime minister of the new Krajina government, this party announced today. The announcement says that the appointment of Borislav Mikelic as the new RSK government's prime minister-designate represents an act of ignoring the will of the parliamentary majority, which is a constitutional obligation and common practice in parliamentary democracies throughout the world. It has also been said that this party was in favor of a national unity government, "remaining open to negotiations on the composition of the government with all political factors, which is in the national interest."
