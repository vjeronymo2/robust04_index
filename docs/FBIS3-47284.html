Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 8 (KCNA) -- Indonesian President Suharto urged all parties concerned to sincerely contribute to creating the needed circumstances for talks on the solution of the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. In a statement on the nuclear issue of the Korean peninsula he issued on January 5 in the name of the chairman of the Non-Aligned Movement [NAM], he recalled that heads of state and government of all member nations of the NAM, at the 10th summit conference of the non-aligned nations, had discussed the affirmative development for the peaceful reunification of Korea and realization of the joint declaration on the denuclearisation of the Korean peninsula and expressed support for them. The president said that Indonesia, as the chairman-state of the NAM, had got a series of measures taken to create a climate favorable to a peaceful solution of the nuclear issue. Indonesia is firmly convinced that the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, a member of the NAM, is intending to solve the nuclear problem through negotiations, he said, and added: It welcomes the will of all parties concerned to solve the nuclear problem through negotiations.
