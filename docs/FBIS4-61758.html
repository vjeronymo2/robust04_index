CSO [Reports by Nargiz Sadykhly and Azertadzh: "Azerbaijani Members of Parliament in Bishkek"] [Text] A parliamentary delegation of the Azerbaijan Republic left on 3 May for Bishkek for a meeting of the Interparliamentary Assembly of members of the CIS. As Vice Speaker Afiyaddin Dzhalilov, leader of the delegation, declared at a briefing prior to departure, B. Ararktsyan, chairman of the Supreme Council of the Republic of Armenia, Armenian deputies, representatives of the Azerbaijani and Armenian communities of Nagornyy Karabakh, political scientists, experts, leaders of the Aland Peace Institute (Finland), and representatives of the Aland and Finnish parliaments would be taking part in the meeting convened on the initiative of M. Sherimkulov, chairman of the Supreme Council of Kyrgyzstan, and V. Shumeyko, chairman of the Interparliamentary Assembly, together with the Azerbaijani members of parliament (G. Guliyev, M. Efendiyev, Sh. Aliyev, S. Mamedov, and M. Zabelin). The question of a peaceful settlement of the Karabakh confrontation will be discussed. The main issues will be a cease-fire the length of the front, the withdrawal of troops from the occupied territories, and the return of refugees to their permanent places of residence. The Azerbaijani delegation will also seek a political assessment of the aggressive actions of Armenia, which has occupied 20 percent of the territory of Azerbaijan. Concerning the question of the possible deployment of a CIS peacekeeping force on the territory of Nagornyy Karabakh, A. Dzhalilov emphasized that the Azerbaijani side would accept only political and diplomatic mediation, and nothing else. "The cause of the Azerbaijani people is just. We will uphold the interests of Azerbaijan in every possible way," the vice speaker concluded. *** The first positive signs emerged at a meeting in the capital of Kyrgyzstan of working groups of the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly on Karabakh. Following three hours of negotiations behind closed doors, journalists' questions were answered by Afiyaddin Dzhalilov, deputy chairman of the Azerbaijan National Assembly. "It is hard to say whether some document will be signed at the meeting," the head of the Azerbaijani delegation declared. "Discussions are taking place, primarily concerning the withdrawal of the Armenian armed formations from the territory of Azerbaijan, the return of the refugees, and the establishment of a lasting cease-fire." A. Dzhalilov observed that the question of observers would be decided today. To the question: "How does the Azerbaijani delegation view this request," A. Dzhalilov said: "We proceed from the fact that the conflict has grown into a war and that Armenia and Azerbaijan should resolve this by peaceful means. It is today a question of not burying the problem in the `sand,' a war is under way on the territory of Azerbaijan. One million refugees are living under tent-city conditions. Because of this, reason must prevail."
