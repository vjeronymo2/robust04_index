Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] A large consignment of highly enriched uranium extracted from Iraq's research reactors was this morning delivered to Chelyabinsk-65. This is reported by Interfax, citing competent sources. The nuclear material, in thick-walled containers, arrived by air from Baghdad at Koltsovo Airport near Yekaterinburg. From there it is being sent by road to the Mayak radio chemical combine, where it is intended to reprocess the material. The operation of bringing the nuclear material out of Iraq has been carried out on the basis of a UN resolution under the supervision ofthe International Atomic Energy Agency.
