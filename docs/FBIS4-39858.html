BFN [Excerpts] On 10 June, Uzbek President Islam Karimov, on the occasion of the return of the pilgrims from the holy pilgrimage of hajj, received Abdulghani Abdullayev, chairman of the Committee for Religious Affairs of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic and Mufti Mukhtarkhan Abdullayev, head of the spiritual board of the Muslims of Transoxania [place name as heard]. Karimov congratulated the pilgrims on their successful hajj. [passage omitted] There is no point in hiding that there are forces who speak badly of our independence. It is from here that we must make the people understand the successes of independence. [words indistinct] The participants of the meeting expressed their gratitude to the president on behalf of all the pilgrims for the financial assistance and the special flights made available for them. [passage omitted] I can recall very well, said Karimov, that during the Communist Party's rule it was difficult for the people even to visit the (mosque) and for women to use headscarves when [words indistinct]. Thanks to independence all [words indistinct] but still unfortunately there are those who do not appreciate these opportunities [words indistinct].
