940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / Surplus expected from Olympic bid Manchester expects to show a small surplus on the Pounds 5m-plus spent on its failed bid for the 2000 Olympic Games, with insignificant costs to the city council. The money was raised from the private sector and government agencies. Mr Graham Stringer, leader of the Labour-controlled city council, said yesterday that benefits of the bid included Pounds 100m of public investment and a similar sum from the private sector. Mr Stringer said about 5,000 'person-years' of employment had been created so far, with a similar amount still to come. The city also planned to build the new Millennium Stadium, creating another 3,000 jobs. The stadium forms the centrepiece of Manchester's bid for the 2002 Commonwealth Games. 'Last year the city council gave Pounds 750,000 in grants to small businesses to help create or safeguard 622 jobs,' Mr Stringer said. 'Compare that with the infinitely greater benefits of our Olympic bid.'
