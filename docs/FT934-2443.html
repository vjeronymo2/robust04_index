931214 FT 14 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television In the last of the current run of BBC2's often excellent foreign affairs series, Assignment, (7.45) Vincent Hanna investigates the way in which Germany and other EU countries have, as he puts it, 'quietly agreed to amend their immigration and asylum laws to make it administratively impossible for people to seek political asylum in the Community unless they are rich or well connected'. Even worse refugee stories featured in the first episode of Robert Fisk's series about the development of Moslem fundamentalism, From Beirut To Bosnia. Today Fisk visits Gaza to talk to members of the 'Hamas' group and to Palestinians and Israelis. Fisk's main motivation seems to be the (probably correct) belief that practically no one in the UK cares about the feelings of fundamentalists in the Middle East (9.00 C4). Ironic that the BBC should announce now that they are scrapping 40 Minutes, just when it has returned to form. Today's programme is about a group of London entrepreneurs who went to Moscow to open up the market (9.40 BBC2).
