910509 FT 09 MAY 91 / Bonn threat to block Soviet housing finance THE BONN government yesterday threatened to block its financing of a housing programme for returning Soviet troops after Moscow's decision to award the first of the lucrative contracts to Turkish and Finnish companies. Mr Klaus Beckmann, state secretary in the Economics Ministry, said Bonn would withhold the earmarked funds unless Moscow agreed to review its decision, announced on Tuesday. The row over allocating some of the DM7.8bn (Pounds 2.6bn) Germany has pledged for rehousing troops threatens to disrupt plans for the Soviet Union to withdraw 350,000 soldiers from east Germany by the end of 1994. The withdrawal plan, centre-piece of the accord under which Chancellor Helmut Kohl won Moscow approval for German unity, is being financed through DM12bn of Bonn government grants and a DM3bn interest-free loan. Officials close to President Mikhail Gorbachev have hinted, in recent contacts with politicians in Bonn, that the Soviet Union could be persuaded to speed up the timetable by a year provided Germany agreed to put up more than the DM12bn. The DM7.8bn promised by the German government covers construction of 36,000 flats for soldiers returning from east Germany. Although tenders for the projects were put for international bidding, the Bonn government expected that German building concerns - including hard-pressed companies in east Germany - would win the lion's share of the orders. The first batch of contracts was to build 4,000 flats, including 3,000 this year. Soviet officials declined to name the companies that had won the orders, saying the choice had not yet been cleared with Bonn. But they argued that the Soviet Defence Ministry, which selected the companies after an evaluation of the offers by German and Soviet consultants, was entitled to the final say as the contracting party. 'If the German offers had been competitive economically, then there would have been room for some favouritism,' said one official at Soyuzvneshstroimport, the state organisation responsible for foreign construction work. 'But the Turkish and Finnish firms know our market well and they offered more favourable conditions. 'Since this is an international tender, we have to follow the rules of international trade.'
