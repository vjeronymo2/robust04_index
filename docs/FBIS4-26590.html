BFN [Text] Beijing, June 23 (XINHUA) -- China is happy to see the positive developments on the Korean nuclear issue and appreciates the wise attitude adopted by the parties concerned for a peaceful settlement of the issue, Foreign Ministry Spokesman Wu Jianmin said here today. U.S. President Clinton announced today that the U.S. would hold the third round of talks with DPRK in Geneva in early July to seek an overall improvement of relations between the two countries, Wu noted at a press conference here this afternoon. He continued that according to Clinton, the U.S. would also stop pushing for sanctions against DPRK in the UN Security Council. Meanwhile, DPRK and ROK have reached an agreement on holding preliminary talks for a summit meeting between them. Wu said, China is happy to see these positive developments on the Korean nuclear issue and appreciates the wise attitude adopted and positive efforts made by the parties concerned for a peaceful settlement of this issue. The spokesman stressed that dialogue, instead of exerting pressure, is the basic way for a peaceful and proper settlement of the Korean nuclear issue. "This conforms to the interests of all parties and serves peace and stability on the peninsula." China sincerely hopes that all parties concerned will treasure and seize upon this important turning point, persist in consultation and dialogue with an equal, pragmatic and sincere approach and make constructive efforts to bring about a denuclearized, peaceful and stable Korean peninsula, he said.
