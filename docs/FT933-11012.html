930731 FT 31 JUL 93 / UK Company News: Cheltenham & Gloucester advances 71% Cheltenham & Gloucester, the sixth largest building society, disclosed a 71 per cent rise in pre-tax profits from Pounds 44.1m to Pounds 75.3m in the six months to June 30 after provisions for bad and doubtful debts eased. Provisions fell to Pounds 75.3m (Pounds 98.5m) as the society did not have to repeat provisions made last year to cover commercial lending and inherited loans from its merger with the former Portsmouth Building Society. The society's assets rose by 2.5 per cent to Pounds 16.5bn (Pounds 15.3bn) as mortgage lending rose 4.6 per cent to Pounds 1.1bn helped by fixed rate products. However, the society predicted that lending for the year would be down on 1992. Mr Andrew Longhurst, chief executive, said it was 'a solid performance in a sombre market'. The society discloses half-year results because it has issued permanent interest-bearing shares (Pibs).
