BFN [Text] A total of 100 servicemen from the Ukrainian military contingency of the UN peace-keeping battalion have left Kiev for ex-Yugoslavia. The Ukrainian Defense Ministry told INTERFAX-Ukraine that several diplomats, including the Ukrainian Foreign Ministry's Envoy, Andrey Ozadovskiy, had left for the conflict zone by the same plane. The Ukrainian Foreign Ministry's press service said the Ukrainian diplomatic mission had left for ex-Yugoslavia in order to examine the situation in the region where the Ukrainian battalion was stationed and to conduct consultations on the issue. The 550 men of the new contingency will be stationed near Gorazde. The Ukrainian servicemen were trained in the Prikarpatsky Military District. The Ukrainian military contingency will be transported to ex-Yugoslavia by four flights. This will take three days. Ukraine is expected to have a 1,200-man military contingency in the region. The latest figure for the Ukrainian battalion in Bosnia and Herzegovina was 500 servicemen. For the period of the Ukrainian battalion's presence in Sarajevo six servicemen have been killed and about 30 wounded.
