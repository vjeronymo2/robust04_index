BFN [Excerpt] Aden, Yemen, May 12 (AFP) -- Southern forces in Yemen's civil war shot down three northern warplanes and sank a warship west of the vital port city of Aden, a southern commander announced Thursday. Colonel 'Ali Qasim Talib, the sacked Yemeni navy chief, told the Aden daily 14 UKTUBAR the northern targets were hit late Wednesday in the Haraz region, near the strategic Bab el-Mandeb Straits on the Red Sea. Earlier the northern forces loyal to President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih claimed they had captured a southern mechanised brigade based at Haraz. A northern military communique quoted by the official news agency SABA' said northern forces had captured all the tanks and artillery of the southern 25th mechanised brigade, and many of the brigade's 1,200 soldiers had surrendered.
