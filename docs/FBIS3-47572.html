Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 15 KYODO -- The Japanese Government on Friday [14 January] offered its condolences to Norway over the sudden death of Foreign Minister Johan Holst on Thursday and said it will send telegrams expressing its sympathy. The death of Holst, who played a pivotal role in the Middle East peace process, was "a great shock," said Foreign Ministry spokesman Sadaaki Numata, adding it is a loss not only for Norway but the whole world. Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata and other Japanese Government officials will send telegrams of condolence later in the day, the spokesman said. Holst, 56, who was instrumental in brokering the historic agreement between Israel and the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO) last Septmeber for limited Palestinian self-rule in the occupied territories, died after reportedly suffering a stroke. He visited Japan last October and held talks with Hosokawa and Hata.
