Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Election Campaign Officially Starts, New Rules"] [Text] Rome, 25 Feb (ANSA) -- The March 27-28 election campaign on Friday [25 February] officially took off, with a host of new election rules going into effect for the next 30 days. These are some of the most important among the new rules: As of today, mass media will not be allowed to publish or broadcast advertising for parties or candidates but will be allowed to announce, broadcast and report on debates, round tables, press conferences and speeches, as well as the programs of parties and candidates. On Saturday, March 12, (15 days before the polls open), a ban on the publication or release of any opinion polls or popularity ratings will go into effect. An individual campaign spending ceiling of 92 million lire (54,700 dollars) has been set for Lower House candidates; the spending ceiling for Senate candidates is 115 million lire (68,400 dollars). Candidates found to have spent more than twice the allotted spending ceiling for their campaigns will face removal from office.
