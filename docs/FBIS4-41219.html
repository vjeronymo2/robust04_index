BFN [Text] The All-Russia Public Opinion Center held an opinion poll among 1594 respondents on the eve of June 22 which marks the 53rd anniversary of fascist Germany's attack on the Soviet Union. Forty-seven percent of Russian citizens believe that the rise of fascism is quite possible in Russia, while 35 percent have the opposite opinion. Eighteen percent of the respondents were undecided. Seventy-five percent of those polled said that fascist organizations must be banned and 8 percent that such organizations have the right to exist. Five percent of the respondents think there are no and will be no such organizations in this country. Seven percent of the respondents were undecided and 5 percent said that this problem did not interest them. Eighty-one percent of those poled believe that June 22 must be declared a day of national mourning and memory for the victims of the War. Seven percent were against this idea. Seven percent were undecided and 5 percent were not interested in the problem.
