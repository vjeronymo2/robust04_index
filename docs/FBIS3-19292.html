Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Madrid, March 14, IRNA -- Iran's deputy foreign minister for Euro-American affairs, Mahmud Va'ezi, arrived here for a three-day official visit here Monday [14 March] and was received by the director general of the Spanish Foreign Ministry for the Middle East and Northern African Affairs Moratinod [name as received]. Va'ezi said his trip is intended to promote Iran-Spain relations in economic and cultural areas as well as on the international scene. He said his talks with Spanish officials would encompass topics as wide as those of Russia, Central Asia, Karabakh, Tajikistan, Iraq, the Persian Gulf, the European Union and current developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Commenting on an Iranian loan outstanding to Spain, Va'ezi said the loan is not reallly substantial and commented that in just the same way as agreements had been reached with Germany on outstanding loans, so too, a similar agreement would be arrived at with Spain. He said Spain is a major buyer of Iranian oil, and that in addition there is very good prospects for export of Iran's non-oil products to that country. He said the two countries can also exchange students and artists and also establish departments of Persian and Spanish at their respective universities. The Iranian official said since Spain is due to become chairman of the EU [European Union] next year, he would also discuss cooperation with Spanish officials in this field.
