BFN [Text] Cairo, 2 Jun (INA) -- [Iraqi] Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf, head of the Iraqi delegation to the nonaligned conference in Cairo, met in Cairo today with Pakistani Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali. During the meeting, Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf briefed his Pakistani counterpart on the developments in the relationship between Iraq and the UN Special Commission following the recent review of the Security Council on lifting the blockade on Iraq. The review showed clearly Iraq's full cooperation with the United Nations and its Special Commission and the need to lift the Iraq blockade in accordance with Paragraph 22 of Resolution 687. The Pakistani foreign minister stressed his country's position toward lifting the Iraq blockade, which is in harmony with the progress that Iraq has made and Iraq's positive response to the efforts of the United Nations and the Special Commission. The two sides assessed the performance of the nonaligned conference over the past two days and the dialogues that called for reviving the role of the Nonaligned Movement in light of the current world developments. The two sides had identical views.
