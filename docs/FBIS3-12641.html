Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, has said that Pakistan will continue to assist the Afghan brothers despite some unpleasant events. Talking to representatives of nongovernmental organizations and international aid agencies at [name indistinct] Afghan refugee camp near Peshawar this afternoon, she said Pakistan wants the establishment of peace in Afghanistan at the earliest possible time. This was necessary for speedy reconstruction of Afghanistan. She said the representatives of relief agencies to continue assisting the Afghan people [sentence as heard]. The prime minister pointed out that Pakistan, Iran, Saudi Arabia, the members of the Islamic Conference Organization and other countries have appealed to Afghan leaders to stop armed confrontation and establish peace in the country. She said Pakistan has not closed its border with Afghanistan and all Afghans having valid documents are allowed to travel into Pakistan.
