940216 FT 16 FEB 94 / Arts: Today's Television In the last episode of Middlemarch on BBC2 the air is full of the sound of chickens coming home to roost. Raffles the blackmailer returns and falls ill at Bulstrode's. Dr Lydgate attends for medical reasons but is also looking to Bulstrode for money to avoid bankruptcy. Rosamond is determined to leave rather than suffer the stigma of poverty, but is found by the saintly Dorothea in compromising circumstances with heart throb Ladislaw. Vintage Palmolive (9.30). That is preceded by the outstanding spoof The Day Today which ridicules the pretensions and mannerisms of news and current affairs television by imitation, exaggeration, and the unexpected (9.00). BBC1 begins its six part series on 'professional' crime in Britain since 1945, The Underworld (9.30). Today's programme features 'Mad' Frankie Fraser who has spent 40 years in prison, Eddie Chapman, said to be the first man in Britain to blow safes with gelignite, and burglar George 'Taters' Chatham who stole the Duke of Wellington's swords from the V & A Museum .
