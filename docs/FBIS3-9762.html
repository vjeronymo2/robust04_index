Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 27 (CTK) -- The somewhat pointed exchange on the issue of Senate elections between the Civic Democratic Alliance (ODA) and the Civic Democratic Party (ODS), the main coalition party, was due to the lack of coalition agreement on the matter, Czech Deputy Premier and ODA Chairman Jan Kalvoda said on television today. Kalvoda appeared in a Nova television station discussion programme "7 or Seven Days." Lack of "coalition communication" is a serious matter, Kalvoda said, adding that the fact that several government proposals have been turned down by parliament proved his argument. "It is possible to ponder over the weight of this or that party in the light of election results but we can only wield parliamentary majority if we stay together," Kalvoda said, adding that a working coalition communication would prevent media disclosures of clashes during debates. Foreign Minister Josef Zieleniec (ODS) said in the programme that often what happens in cases like that is that "small coalition parties try to assert themselves even at the cost of breaking the coalition." Zieleniec said that he considered it important to "lead public discussion." The government-proposed Senate elections bill has not been drafted by the ODS but by the Interior Ministry, Zieleniec said. A week ago, ODA deputy chairman Daniel Kroupa said in an interview with Nova that the parliamentary and Senate elections bill had been worked out by the ODS with an eye to gaining the ODS a position of advantage.
