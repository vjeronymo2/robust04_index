Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Paris, Jan. 25, IRNA -- The Iranian Embassy here on Tuesday [25 January] categorically denied Iran's involvement in the 1971 assassination in Paris of Shapur Bakhtiar, the last prime minister in the former regime of Iran. In a statement released here the Iranian Embassy also condemned any act of terrorism. The statement was a response to allegations recently raised by French papers that Iran had been involved in Bakhtiar's assassination. It underlined the need for the French judicial system to maintain its independence and keep aloof from any hue and cry while investigating the case. The AGENCE FRANCE PRESSE in a news dispatch on Monday accused Iran of providing logistical assistance in the assassination of Bakhtiar. The Iranian Embassy advised that no prejudgement should be made on the issue.
