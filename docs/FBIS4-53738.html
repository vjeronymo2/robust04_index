BFN [Station commentary] [Text] The South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu will visit Vietnam this week. Our radio editor reviews Vietnam-South Korea relations. The visit to Vietnam by South Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu will further promote cooperative ties between Vietnam and the Republic of Korea. The event takes place nearly a year after the official visit to Seoul by Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. It further proves that since the establishment of diplomatic relations in December 1992, ties between the two countries have significantly developed. Last year, the two-way trade turnover between Vietnam and South Korea was $800 million, an increase of 60 percent over 1992. Vietnam has exported to South Korea coal, farm produce, textile, and sea foods; and imported from Korea electronic products, transport equipment and machinery. The two countries last year signed important agreements on investment encouragement and protection and trade, and aviation cooperation. They also initialed an agreement on avoidance of double taxation. South Korea's investment in Vietnam has reached $530 million with 53 projects, an increase of 200 percent over 1992. Many large South Korean corporations such as Daewoo, Samsung, and Lucky Goldstar are undertaking major projects in Vietnam. South Korean investment in Vietnam diversified in area and territorial locations and in forms. South Korea has also granted Vietnam $50 million in development aid to help build infrastructures. It has also given grant of two million U.S. dollars to Vietnam. South Korea is considered the country with dynamic economic development in Asia and the Pacific and Vietnam is also quickly developing. Therefore, both countries are becoming important partners in the Asia-Pacific era. The coming visit by the South Korean foreign minister will contribute to taking the bilateral relations to a higher level.
