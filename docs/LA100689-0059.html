October 6, 1989, Friday, Southland Edition

THE WORLD

Greek Prime Minister Tzannis Tzannetakis, saying he has fulfilled his mandate to clean up national politics, announced that his government will resign within four days, opening the way for a caretaker government to oversee new elections Nov. 5. He said a caretaker government should be formed within a week of his resignation. The prime minister, who heads a coalition of the conservative New Democracy Party and the Communist-led Leftist Alliance, said his government has achieved what it pledged to do when it took office -- clean up Greek political life from the scandals that brought down the last administration.
