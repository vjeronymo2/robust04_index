BFN [Text] Moscow, 21 Jun (ITAR-TASS) -- A party with an unusual name -- "Lefsha" [left-hander] -- has been registered at Russia's Justice Ministry. Dmitriy Semenov, the president of the new political association, told ITAR-TASS that: The party intends to defend the interests of qualified wage labor workers. It consists of qualified industrial and agricultural workers, scientific-technical and cultural intelligentsia, students, and professional military. We are for forming a middle class in Russia, which, as is known, consists not only of medium and small owners but what is more important, of qualified and highly-paid workers, the party's president said. In keeping with its program, the party will seek to gain power constitutionally, in order to carry out a realistic reform in the interests of wage labor workers. The aim of the party is a legislative reform of social and labor relations. The party has started preparations for its nationwide congress, which is to take place at the end of September-beginning of October 1994.
