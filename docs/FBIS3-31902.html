Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Afrticle by Jaroslav Kmenta: "Iran Wants Tamara, but the Manufacturer Will Not Sell"] [Text] Prague--The Islamic Republic of Iran has several times shown an interest in the Tamara passive radar system, the Czech state-of-the-art air defense product that, according to the experts, is even capable of detecting the "invisible" U.S. Stealth aircraft. This information has been confirmed by Ludek Stodulka, vice president of the HTT Group, which manufactures Tamara. He emphasized, however, that HTT will not sell it to this country. Although Iran is not one of the countries to which arms exports are banned, many foreign politicians nevertheless consider it to be a very-high-risk region. "We respect the state's interest in not supporting the arming of this region," added Stodulka. According to him, countries such as India, China, South Korea, Taiwan, and Thailand could purchase Tamara. France is also interested in it. Tamara and its predecessor, Ramona, have only been exported to some of the countries of the former East bloc. At the moment, Tamara is also owned by the German Bundeswehr, which acquired it from the former GDR National People's Army, and the Americans. Apparently, they got it for trials from the Polish Army's weaponry. The former Czechoslovakia also sold Tamara to Syria. The HTT Group has also developed a civilian version of Tamara, said Stodulka. He added that the company could supply Tamara, for example, to the police force or the taxi service. The radio impulses that the equipment receives from the atmosphere could determine precisely the location of vehicles in a city. According to Stodulka, Tamara could even be used in the hunt for those who kidnap children. If a child were kidnapped along with its baby carriage, the radar system could monitor the culprit's movements. Stations that could receive and analyze the appropriate signals would have to be set up in a city for these purposes.
