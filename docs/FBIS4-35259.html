BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 May (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has stated that Egypt and a number of Gulf states have submitted a request to the UN Security Council to discuss the situation in Yemen and the ongoing fighting there. He pointed out that a cease-fire is important to stop the deteriorating situation in that country. He noted that all parties, including Arab and international ones, are interested in halting the continuing bloodshed in Yemen without prejudice to the political situation in that country. Musa emphasized that what is important now is to reach a cease-fire in Yemen because this is in the interest of both the Arabs and the Yemenis. A cease-fire, he added, would allow for a return to negotiations and safeguard Yemeni unity. He noted that the continued bloodshed will certainly hurt Yemen's unity and future.
