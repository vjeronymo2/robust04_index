January 9, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: ART

Baccarat Table to Be Installed at LACMA: A dazzling crystal table, made in the mid-19th Century at the Baccarat French crystal house, is being installed today in the Los Angeles County Museum of Art's decorative arts galleries. The 5x3-foot table is a longterm loan from Los Angeles collectors Arthur and Rosalinde Gilbert.

The crystal table formerly belonged to Liberace. Asprey galleries of London bought it for $44,000 in Los Angeles at an April, 1988, auction of the late singer's collection. The Gilberts subsequently purchased the table from Asprey for an undisclosed price. Reproductions of baccarat tables are occasionally offered for sale but, according to the museum, the only other known original is in the collection of the Corning Museum of Glass in Corning, N.Y. SHAUNA SNOW
