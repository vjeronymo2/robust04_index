BFN [Editorial Report] PRC media monitored by Okinawa Bureau carried the following reports on PRC leadership activities from 1 through 7 April. The source is noted in parentheses after each report. Chen Xitong Attends Donation Ceremony -- "The Jiahua Far East Company, Ltd. of Hong Kong recently donated 2 million yuan to Beijing municipality. The donated money was shared by Beijing's Greening Committee and the development foundation of the Beijing People's Art Theater. Chen Xitong, secretary of the Beijing Municipal CPC Committee, and Beijing mayor Li Qiyan attended the donation ceremony." (Beijing XINHUA Domestic Service in Chinese 0429 GMT 4 Apr 94) Li Ruihuan, Li Tieying, Li Lanqing Work as Advisers to Contest -- The first national "Golden Fan Award" new comic dialogue contest will be held soon. Li Ruihuan, Li Tieying, and Li Lanqing will serve as advisers to the contest. (Beijing XINHUA Domestic Service in Chinese 0823 GMT 4 Apr 94) Chen Xitong Attends Signing Ceremony -- The Beijing Municipal People's Government and Aviation Industries of China recently signed an agreement on jointly running the Beijing Aeronautics and Astronautics University which was run solely by Aviation Industries of China before. Chen Xitong, Li Qiyan, and Zhu Yuli attended the signing ceremony. (Beijing XINHUA Domestic Service in Chinese 0448 GMT 4 Apr 94) Qian Qichen, Chen Xitong, Peng Peiyun on World Women's Congress -- Xu Zhijian, deputy secretary general of the State Council and vice chairman of the Chinese Organizing Committee for the Fourth World Women's Congress, told reporters: "The Party Central Committee and the State Council attach great importance to the preparatory work of the Fourth World Women's Congress and constantly inquire about it and give instructions. On 29 January, Hu Jintao, Chen Xitong, Qian Qichen, Chen Muhua, Peng Peiyun, and other leading comrades heard a briefing again on the work of the Chinese Organizing Committee." Hu Jintao, member of the Standing Committee of the CPC Central Committee Political Bureau and member of the Central Secretariat, said after hearing the briefing: "China's hosting of the World Women's Congress shows that this country is becoming more and more influential in the world and enjoying higher and higher international prestige. We have had more and more friends in the world. We should be determined to work with one heart and one mind to make the women's congress a success." Vice Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen said: "We should, through the World Women's Congress, establish China's image of reform, opening up, and social prosperity in the international community." Chen Xitong, secretary of the Beijing Municipal CPC Committee, said: "Beijing is determined to do a good job in preparatory work to ensure a smooth convocation of the world women's congress. We must make the congress a success and take advantage of it to show off Beijing's new appearance as a cosmopolis." (Beijing XINHUA Domestic Service in Chinese 0158 GMT 4 Apr 94) Wu Bangguo Greets Establishment of Award Fund -- The Shanghai Petrochemical Spiritual Civilization Building Award Fund was set up on 26 March. Chen Zhili, deputy secretary of the Shanghai municipal party committee, addressed the inauguration ceremony and conveyed to the meeting the greetings and thanks extended by Wu Bangguo, member of the CPC Central Committee Political Bureau and secretary of the Shanghai Municipal CPC Committee, to Shanghai Petroleum Company Ltd. and Shanghai Jinshan Industrial Company for their donation of 5 million yuan, and establishment of the fund. (Shanghai JIEFANG RIBAO in Chinese 27 Mar 94 P 1) Jiang Zemin Praises Shanghai Concert -- During two months of performance, the 1994 Shanghai Folk Music Festival yielded good social effects. General Secretary Jiang Zemin recently said in praise of the concert: "When Comrade Li Guifen was playing erhu [a two-stringed musical instrument], she was accompanied by 200 children playing pipa [a plucked string musical instrument]. It was very touching. In this way, children's interest in music has been aroused." (Beijing Central Television Program One Network in Mandarin 1100 GMT 25 Mar 94) Jiang Zemin, Others Mourn Deceased Comrade -- Zhang Zhenhuan, former chairman of the Science and Technology Committee of the Commission of Science, Technology and Industry for National Defense, died of illness on 23 March in Beijing at the age of 79. At the funeral there were wreaths from Jiang Zemin, Rong Yiren, and Song Jian. Liu Huaqing, Zou Jiahua, and Zhang Zhen paid their last respects to Zhang Zhenhuan's remains at the Babaoshan Cemetery for Revolutionaries in the morning of 2 April. (Beijing Central Television Program One Network in Mandarin 1100 GMT 2 Apr 94) Li Peng Writes Inscription for Canal Renovation Project -- The renovation of the Hongqi Canal in Henan Province was completed on 2 April. Premier Li Peng wrote an inscription for the renovation project -- "The profound friendship existing between the armymen and the people is as long as the stream in the Hongqi Canal." (Beijing Central Television Program One Network in Mandarin 1100 GMT 3 Apr 94) Liu Huaqing, Zhang Zhen Call on Veterans of No. 1 Field Army -- Liu Huaqing, member of the CPC Central Committee Political Bureau Standing Committee and vice chairman of the Central Military Commission; and Zhang Zhen, vice chairman of the Central Military Commission, went to the Jingxi Guesthouse on the morning of 5 April to visit veteran comrades of the editorial committee for the compilation of the battle history of the No. 1 Field Army. Liu Huaqing and Zhang Zhen joined the veteran comrades in recalling the glorious battles of the People's Liberation Army [PLA] during the liberation war. They said writing the PLA's battle history to recreate the magnificent scenes of the liberation war has great significance for preserving the Red Army's true qualities, for preserving the PLA's honorable traditions and "five revolutionary spirits," and for promoting socialist spiritual civilization. (Beijing XINHUA Domestic Service in Chinese 0952 GMT 5 Apr 94) Li Peng, Others Write Inscriptions on Anniversary of Ancient Irrigation Project -- Sichuan's Dujiangyan, the world's oldest irrigation project, today marked its 2,250th birthday. Premier Li Peng wrote an inscription to mark the occasion. The inscription reads: "Sichuan has benefited from the great project of thousands of years." Tian Jiyun, vice chairman of the National People's Congress [NPC] Standing Committee; Yang Rudai and Qian Zhengying, both vice chairmen of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [CPPCC]; and state councilor Chen Junsheng also wrote inscriptions to mark the occasion. (Beijing XINHUA Domestic Service in Chinese 1335 GMT 5 Apr 94) Jiang Zemin, Qiao Shi Write Inscriptions for Memorial Hall -- A ceremony was held in Henan's Linzhou city on 5 April to mark the completion of the memorial hall dedicated to martyr Sun Zhanyuan, a great international communist fighter, a first-class combat hero of the Chinese People's Volunteers Army, and a hero of the DPRK. Jiang Zemin, general secretary of the CPC Central Committee, president of China, and chairman of the Central Military Commission, wrote an inscription for the hall. His inscription reads: "Martyr Sun Zhanyuan is immortal." An inscription written by Qiao Shi, member of the CPC Central Committee Standing Committee and chairman of the National People's Congress [NPC] Standing Committee, reads: "The heroic and noble spirit runs through the universe; the martyr spirit will carry on for thousands of years." Also writing inscriptions to mark the occasion were Qin Jiwei, vice chairman of the NPC Standing Committee, who was formerly commander of the 15th Army of the Chinese Volunteers Army to which martyr Sun Zhanyuan belonged; and Li Desheng, member of the former Central Advisory Commission Standing Committee. (Beijing XINHUA Domestic Service in Chinese 1403 GMT 5 Apr 94) Li Peng, Zou Jiahua, Li Lanqing Write Inscriptions for Entrepreneurs Activities -- China's first national entrepreneurs activities day was held in Fuzhou recently. Premier Li Peng and vice premiers Zou Jiahua and Li Lanqing wrote inscriptions to encourage entrepreneurs to be bold in seeking new ways to build a modern enterprise system, and to better display their pioneering spirit in the country's reform and opening up. (Beijing Central People's Radio Network in Mandarin 1030 GMT 5 Apr 94) Wu Bangguo, Others Lay Wreaths at Mausoleums -- To mark the 45th anniversary of the liberation of Shanghai, party, government, and military leaders of Shanghai, and representatives from all sectors of the city went to the Shanghai Municipal Martyrs Mausoleum, the Soong Ching-ling Mausoleum, the Gaoqiao Martyrs Mausoleum, and the Baoshan Martyrs Mausoleum to sweep tombs, on the morning of 5 April. At 0900, on behalf of the Shanghai Municipal CPC Committee, the municipal people's congress, the municipal government, and the municipal committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [CPPCC], Wu Bangguo, member of the CPC Central Committee Political Bureau and secretary of the Shanghai Municipal CPC Committee; and Ye Gongqi, chairman of the Shanghai Municipal People's Congress Standing Committee, laid wreaths at the Shanghai Municipal Martyrs Mausoleum. Later, Wu Bangguo and other comrades laid wreaths at the Soong Ching-ling Mausoleum. (Shanghai People's Radio Network in Mandarin 2300 GMT 5 Apr 94) Li Peng Writes Inscription for Agricultural Report -- According to a report from the fourth issue of the ZIGUANGGE magazine, the Ministry of Agriculture has organized 323 people to separately go to 100 counties in 25 provinces and autonomous regions to study agricultural problems. Premier Li Peng wrote an inscription for the Agriculture Ministry's 1993 report on the study of agricultural problems. The inscription reads: Go deep to grass-roots units to serve peasants; make scientific decisions to develop rural economy. (Beijing Central Television Program One Network in Mandarin 1100 GMT 6 Apr 94) Li Tieying Urges Learning From Light Industrial Company -- At a meeting on 6 April to exchange the Beijing No. 1 Light Industrial Company's experience in taking comprehensive and interrelated measures to reform the enterprise, Li Tieying, state councilor and minister in charge of the State Economic Restructuring Commission, said: Beijing No. 1 Light Industrial Company not only has enlivened itself, but also has enlivened the country's light industry. Only by taking comprehensive and interrelated reform measures can we enable state-owned enterprises to free themselves from the current predicament. He stressed: In learning from the Beijing No. 1 Light Industrial Company, we should take local reality into consideration, focus on important issues, and strive to achieve practical results. We should particularly pay attention to properly handling state assets, and to preventing the loss of state assets. We should strive to preserve and increase the value of state assets by readjusting the makeup of resources and other essentials, and by transforming enterprise operating mechanisms. (Beijing Central Television Program One Network in Mandarin 1100 GMT 6 Apr 94)
