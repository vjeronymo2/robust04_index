BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yuriy Shcherban] [Text] Moscow June 21 TASS -- At the trial of Valentin Varennikov, former commander-in-chief of Soviet Land Forces and deputy minister of defence, the military panel of the Supreme Court on Tuesday endorsed the defendant's application requesting that additional witnesses be summoned. Former KGB chief Vladimir Kryuchkov and former prime minister Valentin Pavlov have been invited as witnesses for the study of circumstances of the meeting at ABC facility on the eve of the formation of the self-proclaimed emergency committee. Yuriy Plekhanov and Vyacheslav Generalov will be called to the court as witnesses to clarify the circumstances of the disconnection of government communications in the Crimea and the isolation of Soviet President Mikhail Gorbachev. The two already appeared as witnesses at the coup plotters' trial. Other witnesses will also be invited to the court, including those suggested by Arkadiy Denisov, senior military prosecutor at the main department for the exercise of supervision in the armed forces, who represents the prosecution. The hearing of the case goes on.
