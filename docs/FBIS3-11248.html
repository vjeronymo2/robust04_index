Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] David Heathcoat-Amory, UK deputy secretary of state for foreign and commonwealth affairs, met with Bernardo Arevalo, then-acting deputy foreign minister, on 15 March. Heathcoat-Amory asked the Guatemalan official about the recent letter of reservation sent to the UN secretary general, within the framework of national reclamations over Belize. Arevalo said the nonspecific portion of the letter was addressed, adding the UK official requested an expansion of the existing information on the official reservation the country has made before the important organization. "The requested information was explained to them, and the issue was not further elaborated," the official explained. Arevalo also reported the points of the bilateral agenda discussed with Heathcoat-Amory during the meeting. He said among the topics of discussion was the so-called San Jose X Meeting between Central America and the European Union, which will be held in Athens. Arevalo remarked Foreign Minister Marithza Ruiz de Vielman will participate in the meeting, because it will take place within the framework of the foreign ministers meeting of both blocs to discuss economic and cooperation matters. In addition, during the meeting Heathcoat-Amory and Arevalo exchanged letters establishing the elimination of visa requirements for tourists traveling to and from the United Kingdom to Guatemala. The UK official's activities also included the inauguration of the Anglo-Guatemalan Institute, as well as visits to the Defense Ministry chief of staff, the human rights ombudsman, and the head of the Government Peace Commission.
