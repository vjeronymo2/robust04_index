Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The motion to extend the state of emergency was passed in parliament by a majority of 82 votes yesterday. One hundred MP's voted for the bill, while 22 [as heard] voted against it. Winding up the debate, Prime Minister Ranil Wickremasinghe said that the objective of the government is to conduct a free and fair provincial poll in the south. All candidates have been given full police protection, thereby ensuring the right of voters to exercise their franchise freely. The government is taking measures to strengthen democracy in the country. The prime minister thanked the voters and those who were instrumental in the successful conclusion of the local elections in the east and Vavuniya. He claimed that the result of the local elections in the east was the clear indication that terrorism had been eliminated. The prime minister said that the government has taken measures to develop these areas by strengthening the administration in the east. Referring to the security forces' operations in the north and east, the prime minister said that they had achieved several victories against the LTTE [Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam] terrorists. He claimed that the Tamils in the north and east and others were praising the activities of the security forces.
