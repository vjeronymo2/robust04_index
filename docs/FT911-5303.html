910415 FT 15 APR 91 / International Company News: Alcoa posts fall in first-quarter earnings of 34% ALUMINUM Company of America (Alcoa), the world's biggest producer of aluminium whose performance is a gauge of America's industrial economy, has posted a 34.7 per cent drop in first quarter earnings on a 9 per cent decline in revenues, writes Karen Zagor. Net income for the first three months of 1991 was Dollars 97m on revenues of Dollars 2.41bn, against earnings of Dollars 148.6m on revenues of Dollars 2.64bn a year ago. Earnings per share slid 32.5 per cent to Dollars 1.14 from Dollars 1.69. The 1991 earnings included favourable translation and exchange adjustments of Dollars 10.2m or 12 cents a share. In the 1990 first quarter, Alcoa's earnings included adjustments of 5 cents a share.
