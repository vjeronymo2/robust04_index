BFN [INTERFAX report under the "In Brief" rubric] [Text] Moscow -- A conflict continues in the ROSSIYSKAYA GAZETA editorial office between the administration and the journalist collective caused by changes in the publication's charter and the list of members of its staff. According to a staffer who asked not to be identified, "in violation of the law on the mass media, the new charter of the publication had not even been discussed at a meeting of the journalist collective." Besides, the proposed reorganization slates nearly 70 percent of journalists for dismissal. They are being offered work at 10 new agencies to be organized on a commercial basis. As the journalist put it, "the charter, the list of staff members, and the accord with the founder -- which is the Russian Federation Government -- had been concealed from the collective as a "commercial secret" by the newspaper chiefs with its editor, Natalya Polezhayeva, at the head.
