Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Hamburg (DPA) -- According to WESTDEUTSCHE ALLGEMEINE ZEITUNG (WAZ, Essen), the Federal Prosecutor's Office has brought charges against the alleged attackers of Solingen. [On 26th May 1993 two Turkish women and three children died when their house was set on fire.] They would be tried for five counts of murder and 14 counts of attempted murder. The young men are suspected of having set fire to a house occupied by Turks accepting that the inhabitants might die as a consequence. According to WAZ, the Federal Prosecutor's Office will hold the trial at Duesseldorf Higher Regional Court. Recently there had been doubts about whether the four detained suspects were guilty. However, following further checks, the Federal Court of Justice had upheld the detention order. A spokesman for the Federal Prosecutor's Office would not confirm or deny the information. "No comment" was the reply to an enquiry in Karlsruhe. Generally officials would comment only after the suspects had been informed of the charges.
