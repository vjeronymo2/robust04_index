BFN [Text] The Democratic Liberal Party [DLP] urged the government to reconsider [chaegomto] the Joint Declaration on the Denuclearization of the Korean peninsula if non-implementation continues. Some 250 lawmakers of the DLP and of district party chapters held a series of meetings today in presence of Foreign Minister Han Sung- chu and Defense Minister Yi Pyong-tae to seek measures to actively cope with [nungdongjoguro taechohanun panganul mosaek] the North Korean nuclear issue that is becoming increasingly strained with each day [nallo kinbakhaejyo kago innun]. While reading a report entitled "Our Party's Basic Position on the North Korean Nuclear Issue," the DLP lawmakers and chairmen of party chapters said that the North Korea's ambition [yayok] to develop nuclear weapons is a serious challenge [chungdaehan tojon] that threatens the peace and security of the Korean peninsula, and stressed that it can never be allowed.
