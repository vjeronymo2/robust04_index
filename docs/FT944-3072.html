941215 FT 15 DEC 94 / DIY chain faces kitchen refunds The collapse of an insurance company has left Texas Homecare, the DIY retailer owned by Ladbroke group, facing the possibility of having to hand money back to customers who bought kitchens from it in 1992. To boost the sale of kitchens during the recession, Texas launched a promotion promising customers their money back in 10 years' time if they bought Wrighton kitchens worth up to Pounds 1,500. A percentage of the retail price was invested in a trust fund in Jersey to provide funds for customers who remembered to claim their money back in 2002. However, Texas had insured itself against the possibility of claims exceeding the proceeds of the trust fund with MGI, an insurance company which recently failed. Texas is now taking legal action against the trust fund - run by the Royal Bank of Canada group - and brokers who advised it on which company to insure with. It wants them to shoulder the cost of any excess claims at the end of the 10-year period. Texas said yesterday: 'The trust fund was based on a calculation by actuaries of the number of people likely to come back in 2002 and ask for their money back. But the insurance against excess claims turns out to have been placed with a lesser-rated insurance company.' The DIY retailer could not provide figures for the number of customers who had taken up the special offer - although the then chairman Mr Ron Trenter said at the time that kitchen sales had tripled. Texas said yesterday the amount of money involved was 'not material as far as the company is concerned'. It said the offer, a promotional method which originated in the US, had run for only a few months in 1992, and this was the only time Texas had used this method. It added: 'This was effectively just another deep discount offer.'
