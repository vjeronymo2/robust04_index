930722 FT 22 JUL 93 / Murdoch eyes TV channel Mr Rupert Murdoch's News Corporation is likely to be a leading applicant to run Poland's first commercial national television network, writes Christopher Bobinski in Warsaw. The company is considering applying jointly with the Polish Investment Consortium, set up this week, which comprises local banks, privatised companies and wealthy businessmen. Under Polish legislation foreigners are limited to a 33 per cent share in broadcasting companies. The Bertelsman Corporation from Germany as well as Mr Silvio Berlusconi from Italy have also shown interest. At the moment state-run television has two channels. A recently established independent Radio and TV Council will consider applications in the autumn for the licence to run the third private channel.
