BFN [Unscheduled announcement of "State of Emergency" decree, issued by the Presidency in Sanaa on 5 May] [Text] Republican decree No. 20 of 1994 declaring a state of emergency: The chairman of the Presidential Council, taking cognizance of the Constitution of the Republic of Yemen and the law on the state of emergency and Law No. 1962 of 1991 on the establishment of the National Defense Council; in view of the eruption of internal sedition in the country due to the rebellion of secessionist elements in the leadership of the Yemeni Socialist Party against the Constitutional legitimacy, and subsequent military actions and the direct threat to the unity of the homeland and the security and stability of the citizens; according to the Council of Minister's presentation and in accordance with the assessment of the National Defense Council on the need to declare a state of emergency; in accordance with the requirements of the supreme national interest; and following the approval of the Presidential Council, decides: Article 1: A state of emergency shall be declared as of 0800 today, Thursday, 25 Dhi al-Qi'dah, 1414 Hegira, corresponding to 5 May 1994, in all parts of the Republic for 30 days. Article 2: Based on Article 97 of the Constitution of the Republic of Yemen, this decree shall be presented to the House of Representatives within a period of time not exceeding seven days as of its date. Article 3: This decree shall be enforced as of the time and date of its issuance. It shall be published in the official gazette. Issued at the Republic's presidency in Sanaa on 25 Dhi al-Qi'dah, 1414 Hegira, corresponding to 5 May 1994. [Signed] Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council; Muhammad Sa'id al-'Attar, deputy prime minister
