August 17, 1989, Thursday, Southland Edition

KAIFU WINS 46.5% IN JAPAN OPINION POLL

Japan's new prime minister, Toshiki Kaifu, won 46.5% support in an opinion poll, one of the lowest ratings for an incoming premier but far above that for his ill-fated predecessor, Sosuke Uno, the newspaper Tokyo Shimbun reported Wednesday.

Uno started his brief term with only 35.5% support in the polls and resigned 68 days later, taking responsibility for the Liberal Democratic Party's humiliating defeat in July upper house elections and discredited by allegations that he paid a geisha for sex.
