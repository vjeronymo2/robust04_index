921224 FT 24 DEC 92 / UK Company News: Caspen to capitalise on Villiers name Caspen Oil is to change its name to Villiers Group to reflect the shift in balance of the business towards engineering, and to capitalise 'on a well-known and respected name in the engineering world.' The company moved into engineering in 1991 through the acquisition of Machine Tool Services, and in August bought the specialist engineering business and trade names of Villiers. Meanwhile, results for the year ended July 31 1992 showed a pre-tax loss of Pounds 166,000, after crediting exceptionals of Pounds 169,000, compared with a profit of Pounds 60,000 after gains of Pounds 164,000. Oil and gas turnover came to Pounds 891,000 (Pounds 1.46m) and engineering sales to Pounds 499,000 (nil). Operating profit fell to Pounds 97,000 (Pounds 304,000) while interest charges were at Pounds 225,000 (Pounds 195,000) and administration expenses at Pounds 249,000 (Pounds 261,000). Losses per share were 0.32p (earnings 0.47p).
