BFN [Text] Singapore, 30 May (ITAR-TASS) -- Turkmenistan and Malaysia have today signed five agreements on trade and economic cooperation, scientific and technical cooperation, visa arrangements for travel, and mutual debt payment. Turkmenistan's President Saparmurad Niyazov, who is paying an official visit to Kuala Lumpur, and the Malaysian Prime minister Mahathir bin Mohamad, were present at the signing ceremony. According to an official spokesman of the Malaysian Foreign Ministry, Mahathir bin Mohamad promised his Turkmen guest to share experience in the sphere of privatizing state-owned enterprises and implementing economic reforms. Turkmenistani nationals will study banking in Malaysia. The visitors also said they would be interested to learn from Malaysians how to set up free economic zones. Giving a speech today at the National Chamber of Trade and Commerce, Niyazov called upon the Malaysians to step up their investment in the branches of industry that are actively developing in Turkmenistan, such as the oil and gas industry, tourism, and others.
