Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] On 1 February, Cuba and the Argentine province of Misiones reached agreement on a compensated exchange of products and established formulas for putting this mutually beneficial collaboration into practice. Misiones Province Governor Federico Ramon Puerta and Acting Foreign Trade Minister German Amado Blanco signed the document, which will strengthen economic ties between the two nations. The agreement proposes that Misiones Province send our island mainly lumber and paper, while Cuba would supply certain medicines, medical advice [asesoria], and specialized health services. Morever, according to what was learned at the signing of the cooperation agreement in Havana, a group of Cuban physicians will provide technical assistance in developing a primary health care program in Misiones Province. The agreement calls for a team of four experts in general medicine, a nurse, and a dentist to go to Misiones within the next few months to act as consultants in applying their Family Doctor Program experience to local conditions in Misiones.
