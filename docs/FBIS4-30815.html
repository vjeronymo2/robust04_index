BFN [Article by N. Perkovic: "Under the Sea Into the World"] [Text] Podgorica, 16 May 1994 -- Owing to a project made possible by the OTE, the Greek Telecommunications Organization, Montenegro and Yugoslavia will not remain a desert island in the "sea" of international telecommunications. A part of the $3.5 million equipment for digital radio-relay connections, which had been bought by Montenegro from the Italian company "Alcatel-Teletra" before the sanctions were introduced, will be delivered to Montenegro via Greece. Beginning from September 1996, Montenegro should have an underwater connection from Bar, through Durres and Corfu, to the whole world. This is the result of the agreement achieved in Salonika and Athens between the Yugoslav and Montenegrin PTT and the representatives of the Greek telecommunication service. In Salonika, Vucic Cagorovic, the Yugoslav PTT general manager, Milorad Jaksic, the Serbian PTT general manager, and Jusuf Fetahovic, the Montenegrin PTT general manager, concluded the work of expert groups from both countries. There followed a meeting of expert groups in Athens, led by Jusuf Fetahovic and Petros Labron, the OTE general manager, in which the agreement was reached that Montenegro should be included in every project for optic cables planned for the Adriatic. Experts consider that the $30 million investment in the radio-relay and optic cables project will be the most profitable investment in Montenegro. Therefore it is considered that this historic endeavor will be fervently supported by the Montenegrin Government, especially if we know that both Montenegro and the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia would profit from it because this would enable them to become linked to the international communication network. Otherwise they would not only be cut off from the world and deprived of the possibility of following scientific achievements in this field, but they would also lose an important income in hard currency. Taking into consideration all parameters, this will be the most profitable ecological and tourist undertaking in the ecological state of Montenegro [as published].
