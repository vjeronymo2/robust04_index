BFN [Text] Moscow June 22 TASS - Russian President Boris Yeltsin signed a decree on Wednesday to enforce human rights guarantees in Russia, the presidential press service said. For the purpose of ensuring the rights and freedoms of citizens provided for by the Russian Federation laws "on rehabilitation of the victims of political repressions" and "on pensions of citizens leaving for permanent residence outside the Russian Federation" the president has instructed the government to approve within the month the following documents: On return of property illegally confiscated during repressions and payment of compensations, on the procedure for paying compensations to the victims of political repressions, and on the procedure for paying pensions to citizens leaving for permanent residence outside the Russian Federation. Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin has been authorised to oversee the implementation of the decree.
