Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 7 KYODO -- Japan's current account surplus soared to a new record last year, with the surplus in December rising 9.3 percent, the Finance Ministry reported Monday [7 February]. The surplus in the current account, the broadest measure of trade in goods and services plus some unilateral transfers, totaled 131.35 billion dollars before adjustment for seasonal factors in 1993, up 11.7 percent from 1992, according to the ministry's preliminary report. Within the current account, the merchandise trade surplus rose 6.9 percent to 141.43 billion dollars last year. In December alone, the current account surplus rose 9.3 percent to 12.57 billion dollars, resuming an upward trend that halted in November. The December trade surplus rose 8.7 percent to 14.15 billion dollars.
