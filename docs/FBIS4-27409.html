BFN [Text] The Bureau of Immigration and Deportation [BID] today cancelled the visas of 10 foreign nationals currently attending the Asia-Pacific Conference on East Timor [APCET]. Immigration Associate Commissioner Manuel Roxas reiterated that their action is in compliance with the government's position to bar foreigners attending the conference. Those foreigners who are now attending the APCET are believed to have gained entry into the country before their ban took effect. In this connection, immigration agents headed by Tom Natividad this morning served summons on 10 foreigners attending the conference at the Malcolm Hall of the University of the Philippines. Natividad said the foreigners should personally go to the immigration office before 0800. He clarified that they went to Malcolm Hall just to serve the subpoenas to the foreigners and not to arrest them.
