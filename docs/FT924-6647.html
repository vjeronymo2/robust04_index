921120 FT 20 NOV 92 / TGWU official on board at Rover MR JACK ADAMS, deputy general secretary of the TGWU general union, is to join the board of a Rover training subsidiary as part of a union-management initiative to bring in a working practices deal agreed in April, David Goodhart writes. The subsidiary, Rover Learning Business, does not directly make policy but its managing director sits on the main Rover board. The union said the appointment was the first time a union official had been asked to join a policymaking board with a car company. Next Monday 450 Rover shop stewards will attend a special seminar at Longbridge to be addressed by Mr Bill Morris, leader of the TGWU, and Mr John Towers, managing director of Rover. Mr Harry Dunlevy, personnel operations director at Rover, said yesterday: 'The seminar is a very positive way forward and it is a milestone along the way in what has already been a success story.'
