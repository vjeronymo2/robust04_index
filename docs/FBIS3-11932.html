Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 24 March. The Brazilian Petroleum Corporation broke a production record in February with average production of 702,833 barrels of oil per day. The company attributed the increase to operational efficiency improvements in Rio de Janeiro's ocean oil platform. (Brasilia Voz do Brazil Network in Portuguese 2200 GMT 18 Mar 94) Last year Brazil sold iron ore to 35 countries, of which Japan, Germany, South Korea, France, Italy, and the PRC purchased 65.4 percent of the 111.861 million metric tons produced. Japan was the leading purchaser with 28.329 million metric tons, 1.681 million more than in 1992. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 18 Mar 94 p 15) According to Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics, IBGE, estimates released on 18 March, the 1993 GDP totaled 44.87 trillion real cruzeiros in current value. The economy grew 4.96 percent. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 19 Mar 94 Section 1 p 10) The IBGE and the Getulio Vargas Foundation projected a 42.24 percent inflation rate for March. The consumer price index, IPC, was 40.9 percent on 17 March. The IPC for those who earn up to eight minimum salaries in Sao Paulo was 45.08 percent, 2.45 percentage points higher in just one week. The IPC for Rio de Janeiro was 43.68 percent, 2.07 percentage points higher. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 19 Mar 94 Section 2 p 1) The Brazilian Iron and Steel Institute reported that Brazilian steel production was 1.964 million metric tons in February, a daily average of 70,000 metric tons more than in January. Production increased 4.7 percent compared with February 1992. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 19 Mar 94 p 10) Brazil's trade exchange with Common Market of the South, Mercosur, member countries totaled $8.73 billion in 1993, an increase of 37.56 percent compared with 1992's $6.35 billion. Brazilian exports to Argentina, Paraguay, and Uruguay were $5.4 billion; imports were $3.3 billion. The trade balance with Mercosur was 11.64 percent more than in 1992. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 21 Mar 94 p 5) The Brazilian trade surplus in February was $726 million: Exports totaled $2.778 billion and imports $2.052 billion. The Industry, Commerce and Tourism Ministry reported that this is a record surplus for February. (Brasilia Voz do Brasil Network in Portuguese 2200 GMT 22 Mar 94) Industrial production grew 9.8 percent in January compared with the same month last year. It registered the highest production level since February 1987, barely 5.2 percent lower than the record rate registered in that month. Cumulative industrial production grew 14.8 percent from September to January. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 22 Mar 94 p B10) Stock markets dropped because of the crisis between the executive, legislative, and judicial branches and high interest rates. They decreased 6.19 percent in Sao Paulo and 4.56 percent in Rio de Janeiro. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 22 Mar 94 p B1)
