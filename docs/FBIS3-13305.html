Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] During his visit to the tomb of his great grandfather Prophet Muhammad, God's peace and blessings be upon him, in Medina, and during his circumambulation in Mecca, His Majesty King Husayn reminded the Islamic nation on the night of al-Qadr [the night the koran was revealed] of the importance of Islamic and Arab solidarity against the ferocious onslaught against Muslims and Arabs. King Husayn's gesture is not new; it stems from the continuous role the Hashemites have played in unifying the Arabs and Muslims throughout the ages. It also indicates that this blessed month is the month of communication, amity, and love among the Muslims in all their countries. In addition to performing the minor pilgrimage, the king's visit to Mecca and Medina sought to intensify Islamic and Arab efforts, which stem from the Hashemites' struggle in national, pan-Arab, and humanitarian Jordan, to unite in rank, word, and position. Jordan rose above the pan-Arab wounds to heal the rift which afflicted the Arab homeland after the Gulf war for the sake of restoring Arab solidarity and reactivating joint Arab action that can confront the serious challenges threatening the Arab and Islamic nation.
