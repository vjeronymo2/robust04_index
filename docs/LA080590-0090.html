August 5, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

FOOTNOTE FOOTNOTE

In his perceptive April 8 review of "The Cinema of Adventure, Romance and Terror," David Colker mentions that "some of the behind-the-scenes anecdotes . . . are suspect because the book is not footnoted and the sources . . . go mostly unnamed."

Most of the material in my book is from primary sources, such as author conversations and interviews over a period of many years with personnel involved in production of the films and from information in studio files. . . .

For example, our information on "The Most Dangerous Game" was obtained from the producer, the director, the music supervisor, four of the cameramen, two effects technicians, the first matte artist, a stuntman, two lead players and the RKO production files.

We tried to avoid published sources and whatever secondary materials were used are attributed in the text. Further blame may be attached to the editor's college-days hatred of reading footnotes.

GEORGE E. TURNER

HOLLYWOOD
