Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and China will soon start talks on reducing troop levels on both sides of the Line of Actual Control. This is in pursuance of an agreement signed during the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao's visit to Beijing during September last year. The Chinese ambassador, Mr. Cheng Ruisheng, said in New Delhi that an expert group of diplomats and military personnel set up as part of the agreement will hold its talks in the capital next month. He said both the countries want to divert their resources from military to economic development. He also said that a high-level delegation of the Communist Party of China will be on a week-long visit to India from seventh of this month at the invitation of the Congress-I. A festival of India will be held in China in May this year, he added.
