BFN [Text] Dunajska Streda, West Slovakia April 18 (CTK) -- "Relations between the Slovak Republic and Hungary must be deideologised and relieved of the unnecessary historic burden," Peter Weiss, chairman of the coalition Democratic Left Party (SDL), said today after his talks with Gyula Horn, chairman of the Hungarian Socialist Party. Weiss said both countries accepted the idea of concluding a general agreement which would also enshrine the unchangeability of the border. Horn said that before the agreement is signed, Slovakia must comply with the recommendations from the Council of Europe (CE) and solve the problems of bilingual names of communities and the way of writing Hungarian names. But he added that "the status of ethnic minorities in Slovakia is still better than in neighbour countries." Turning to the controversial Gabcikovo-Nagymaros hydroproject on the Danube, Weiss said he supported the completion of Nagymaros. He voiced the hope that a technical solution beneficial to both countries would be found. Weiss said he was convinced that the problem would be solved so that neither side "lose face." Horn said that the problem of Gabcikovo, the Slovak part of the hydroproject completed in 1993, must be depoliticised and that a commission composed of independent experts from both countries should be set up to propose a solution. Hungary has been strongly criticising Gabcikovo, complaining that it has diverted water from its territory.
