BFN [Unattributed report: "President Rugova Meets Foreign Minister Godal" -- issued by the Democratic League of Kosova] [Text] The president of the Republic of Kosova, Dr. Ibrahim Rugova, was received today in Oslo by the Norwegian foreign minister, Bjorn Tore Godal. In a long and cordial meeting, Dr. Rugova briefed the Norwegian foreign minister on the situation in Kosova, describing it as very tense and dangerous due to total discrimination and unabated violence exercised by the Serbian regime since the military and police occupation in 1989. The Kosova problem requires an urgent solution and concrete and comprehensive involvement of the international community, President Rugova said. He also said that the solution should be sought in the direction of implementation of the right to self-determination of the Kosova people. The people of Kosova has declared itself for an independent and [words indistinct] and repeated his call for a temporary international civil protectorate in Kosova. He called on the Norwegian Government to help in reaching a solution to the Kosova issue within a global approach for the solution of the former Yugoslav crisis. Without a just solution of the Kosova question, no lasting peace in the Balkans is possible, Dr. Rugova said. The Norwegian foreign minister, Godal, said he was honoured to meet Dr. Rugova and that his country admired the peaceful policy of the Kosova people and that it had understanding for their demands. Mr. Godal praised the readiness of the Albanian side to solve the problem through dialogue. We are concerned for the situation in Kosova and we would like to see a solution as soon as possible, he added.
