Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] As Caracol network reported one hour ago, a Russian-made helicopter crashed shortly after taking off from the Tarqui Battalion headquarters in the town of Sogamoso. According to the information confirmed by authorities, the Tarqui Artillery Battalion commander, a counterinsurgency commander with the rank of major, a judge, her secretary, and a noncommissioned officer died in the accident. Several people were injured, three of whom were taken to San Jose Hospital in Sogamoso. The authorities also reported that the three helicopter pilots were Russian nationals. One pilot is injured. The information Caracol obtained indicates that three of the people taken to San Jose Hospital have been released. One of them will continue to receive medical attention but is out of danger. Here is German Garcia from Sogamoso with more details on the incident. [Garcia] Sogamoso medical officials reported that three people were injured in the accident that took place at 1250 today. Three persons are at Sogamoso Hospital and the Social Security's Julio Sandoval Medina Clinic. We have invited Dr. Fernando Ortiz, who is the emergency ward chief, to tell us about the victims' conditions. [Ortiz] We treated two injured persons. One was a civilian. The other patient was wearing civilian clothes, but I understand that he is a serviceman. [Garcia] Are other injured people being cared for in other local clinics? [Ortiz] I have learned that another injured person was taken to the Social Security clinic. [Garcia] What are their names? [Ortiz] Excuse me, the Prosecutor's Office asked me not to divulge that information. As you can understand, I cannot provide those names. [Garcia] Do you know if Tarqui Battalion Commander Ramiro Guevara was among those wounded? [Ortiz] I do not think so. The colonel was not one of the two patients that were brought to this hospital. I do not know if he was the injured person taken to the Social Security clinic. [Garcia] Reports indicate at least nine persons were aboard the helicopter. The three injured persons were able to jump from the helicopter 200 meters before it crashed. Luckily, nobody was in that area. Our sources told Caracol that Artillery Tarqui Battalion Commander Ramiro Guevara, two sergeants, two captains, one judge, her secretary, and one civilian who has not been fully identified were aboard that helicopter. Dr. Fernando Ortiz insisted that the Prosecutor's Office is not authorizing him to reveal the names of the people who were injured in this accident.
