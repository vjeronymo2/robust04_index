940528 FT 28 MAY 94 / International Company News: Amalgamated Banks SA has weak result Amalgamated Banks of South Africa, South Africa's largest banking group, has had another disappointing year, with attributable income before extraordinary items falling 2.5 per cent to R666.4m (Dollars 182.4m). Interest on advances dropped marginally to R10.17bn from R10.97bn as did investment income, declining to R940.4m from R1.44bn. Operating income improved to R4.82bn from R4.73bn. Mr David Brink, chairman, said the results were disappointing but that the overall quality of ABSA's book had improved and some market share in the mortgage industry had been recovered.
