Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Lukavica, 18 Feb (TANJUG) -- Radovan Karadzic, president of the Srpska republic, and Yasushi Akashi, the UN secretary general's special envoy for former Yugoslavia, met in Lukavica today to discuss issues concerning the implementation the Russian initiative, the control and the withdrawal of Serb artillery, and the observance of the Sarajevo cease-fire. Speaking at a news conference, Colonel Richard Pernod, head of the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] Sarajevo sector press center, could not confirm that the Serb artillery had already begun withdrawing. Asked what was happening on the territory of Sarajevo, Pernod explained that weaponry was being assembled, and expressed the hope that the Serb and the Muslim sides would begin withdrawing within the envisaged period. He added that "convoys were being formed on the Serb side" in five locations: Lukavica, Mokro, Vela, Blagovat, and Saracevac. As regards the Muslim army, these locations are Malo Polje and Marshal Tito barracks. Asked whether the Serb convoys would move in the next 24 hours, Pernod replied: "Naturally, the withdrawal should begin today, but so far I have no information." TANJUG has learned from Serb sources that more than 100 Serb artillery pieces on the territory of Sarajevo have already been put under control [as received], and that the handover of heavy artillery will be completed within the deadline agreed on with UN representatives.
