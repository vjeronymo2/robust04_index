Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] Today, First Deputy Prime Minister Mikolay Myasnikovich held a news conference on the results of talks with a Russian delegation on the merging of the Belarusian and Russian monetary systems and the creation of a single ruble zone. According to Mikolay Myasnikovich, both parties have stated that obligatory conditions for the functioning of a single money system are as follows: 1. The setting up of equal conditions for mutual trade between economic entities in Russia and Belarus; 2. The creation of a single mechanism for price formulation. 3. The use by Belarusian economic entities of Russian prices for energy and other resources; 4. The setting up of single customs duties [as heard]. Mikolay Myasnikovich said that the parties have agreed on the methods for establishing the exchange rate of Belarusian "hares" and Russian rubles. According to these calculations, as of 1 January 1994, the exchange rate of one Belarusian "hare" to one Russian ruble is 2.19:1. The exchange rate as of 1 March 1994 will likely be 2.5:1.
