Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [By Kim Kwang-hyon] [Text] The Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Energy [MTIE] on 4 March issued a statement reporting: "If the United States seeks to impose Super 301 trade-sanctions on the ROK, the ROK intends to refer the issue to the World Trade Organization [WTO], which is slated to begin in 1995 for the settlement of disputes." In the statement entitled "the MTIE's position on the revival of Super 301 authority on trade-sanctions," Kim Chol-su, minister of Trade, Industry, and Energy, said: "The MTIE calls on the United States to restrain from invoking the authority arbitrarily [chauijogin]." The minister also warned that: "The reinstatement of the Super 301 provision runs counter to the spirit of Uruguay Round, which concluded at the end of last year. We express concern that this may hinder the world's free trade order at a time when the WTO is about to begin."
