Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Accra, 11 Jan (PL) -- Today, Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina concluded his official, two-day visit to Ghana after meeting with President Jerry Rawlings, with whom he discussed the situation in Cuba and the latest African events. Robaina presented a message from President Fidel Castro to the Ghanaian president during this meeting. During his two-day stay in Accra, the visiting minister also met with Ghanaian Foreign Minister Obed Asamoah, with whom he studied the international situation and, specifically, the problems of their Third World countries. Both officials agreed on the need to maintain the Nonaligned Movement as an independent force to confront the neocolonialism that governs the world's unipolarity. During his talks with the Ghananian hosts, Captain Kojo Tsikata, member of the state council in Ghana, asserted that they greatly valued Cuba's efforts to maintain its dignity and to make its own decisions. Tsikata also emphasized Ghana is willing to continue making its modest contributions to helping Cuba do away with its current difficulties. Roberto Robaina explained the measures adopted by his country's authorities following the collapse of the socialist bloc and the disintegration of the Soviet Union to the Ghanaian officials. Cuban delegation sources have reported the Cuban foreign minister will now travel to Angola, a country with which the Cuban Government and people maintain historic ties of friendship. Angolan Foreign Minister Venacio da Moura asserted today, in exclusive statements to PRENSA LATINA, that those ties continue unaltered despite the ongoing changes and the war in Angola. Da Moura highlighted Havana's cooperation with Luanda and emphasized that the blood shed by their Cuban brothers to defend the territorial integrity of this southern country when it was invaded by South African forces in 1975. Robaina is conducting his first tour to this continent, which will also include Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Namibia.
