Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Deputy Prime Minister Suphachai Phanitchaphak has concluded an official visit to Finland successfully. During the visit, during 17 to 20 March 1994, the deputy prime minister paid a courtesy call on Prime Minister Esko Aho and had consultations with Deputy Prime Minister [title as heard] (Kari Hopanatti) and Finnish business leaders as well as members of Finland-ASEAN Society. On trade cooperation, the two sides expressed satisfaction with rapid increase in trade between the two countries. Thailand asked Finland to increase imports from Thailand and give Thailand generalized system of preferences, or GSP. Both countries emphasized a new role of trading gateways to Indochina, Commonwealth of Independent States, and Russia. During the visit, the two countries signed an agreement for investment promotion and protection. Thailand wants Finland to make direct investment in Thailand without going through a third country. Finland agreed to send experts to help Thailand on science and technology. It also asked to increase flight frequency to Bangkok to cope with tourist demand.
