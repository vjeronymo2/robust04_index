Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Madrid, March 9, IRNA -- Iranian ambassador to Madrid, Bahram Qasemi, and Spanish minister of industry and energy, Juan Manuel Eguiagaray, here today discussed ways of expanding Tehran-Madrid bilateral cooperation. Qasemi briefed Eguiagaray on the present process of bilateral cooperation in the areas of energy and industry and voiced Islamic Republic of Iran's readiness to boost cooperation in the areas of vehicle manufacturing, ship building, aviation and power plants. Eguiagaray in return referred to the importance of Iran and the "fruitful" bilateral cooperation in the past and said his country was for promotion of mutual cooperation. He said Spain is to send an expert delegation to survey ways of improving bilateral industrial cooperation, Madrid's contribution to implementation of industrial projects and assisting Iran with its reconstruction projects during the second five-year economic developement plan. The two sides also held talks on joint investment, and conducting of research by the two sides' experts in the field of industry, especially on car manufacturing, ship building and aviation.
