BFN [Text] Today coal miners belonging to the KNSB [Confederation of Independent Trade Unions in Bulgaria] and Podkrepa held a protest rally in Aleksandur Nevski Square in Sofia. Representatives of working collectives from the pits in Maritsa- Iztok, Troyanovo-1, -2, and -3, the briquette factory in Gulubovo, and mines in Pernik, Bobovdol, and elsewhere. The miners put forward demands for protecting the national coal-mining industry and the drafting of a clear concept for its future development. They also demanded the immediate restarting of the briquette factory in Gulubovo, which provides fuel for more than 2,000 families, and the exemption of its production from value added tax, which makes it output unsalable. According to a press release issued by the Council of Ministers Press Center, Prime Minister Lyuben Berov and Nikita Shervashidze, chairman of the Committee on Power Supply, met with representatives of the striking coal miners and power industry workers. On behalf of the strikers, the representatives handed Prof. Berov a declaration containing their demands. The strikers' representatives were briefed on the measures adopted today by the government to ensure the financial stabilization of the energy industry until the introduction of a mechanism for periodically updating the prices of energy products.
