Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 26 Feb (MENA)--Counternarcotics squads from the Cairo Security Directorate seized 1 kg of crude heroin, with an estimated value of 1 million pounds, from two newcomers to the drug-trafficking scene who wished to distribute the heroin to small traffickers. Brigadier General 'Abdallah al-Watidi, director of the Counternarcotics Department at the Cairo Security Directorate, said that investigations had revealed that Yasir Muhammad Fathi, 28, a cook at the Taba Hilton; and 'Abd-al-Wahhab Hasan Rashwan, 40, an employee of the Red Sea Bus Company, possessed a large quantity of heroin powder that they planned to sell. They were arrested in a hideout in the Cairo suburb of Madinat al-Nasr. The heroin was found with them. The two suspects made a confession to Major General Mahmud Wajdi, director of the Detectives Department. They said that they had planned to sell the drug. They were referred to the Prosecutor's Office for further interrogation.
