February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Late Final Edition

LOCAL;

L.A. OVERRIDES GUN LOBBY, ADOPTS BAN ON SEMI-AUTOMATIC WEAPONS

Over the loud objections of gun owners, the City of Los Angeles enacted a sweeping ban on the sale and possession of semi-automatic weapons that have become increasingly popular with gang members.

Mayor Tom Bradley signed the ordinance about an hour after a unanimous City Council voted for the two-pronged assault on the military-style weapons. Under the ordinance owners of the rapid-firing rifles will have 15 days to render them inoperable or turn them over to police for their destruction. Sales of the weapons are outlawed immediately.

Gun shop owners and members of the National Rifle Assn., who booed the council during its debate, vowed to fight the new ordinance in the courts. They said that criminals will not turn in the assault guns, leaving law-abiding citizens vulnerable to attack.
