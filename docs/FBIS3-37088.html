Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS-KAZTAG correspondents Amangeldy Akhmetalimov and Igor Barsukov] [Text] Washington, 14 Feb--Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev arrived here on Sunday afternoon on an official visit at the invitation of U.S. President Bill Clinton. A meeting between the presidents of the United States and Kazakhstan is to take place in the White House today. Nazarbayev will also meet U.S. Vice President Albert Gore, the defense and trade secretaries, and representatives of the U.S. business community. Documents on cooperation between the two countries are to be signed. On Tuesday, the Kazakh president is scheduled to have discussions with the heads of the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund. After his stay in the U.S. capital, Nazarbayev is to leave for New York, where he is to have a meeting with UN Secretary General Butrus-Butrus Ghali.
