BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA May 18 -- Prime Minister [P.M.] Vo Van Kiet met with New Zealand Prime Minister, J. Bolger in Singapore today during his participation in an international conference on opportunities for world trade. Speaking on the occasion, P.M. Vo Van Kiet highly appreciated the New Zealand Government's decision to send a delegation of experts to Vietnam to study its animal breeding and forestry. The two sides stressed the importance of peace stability and cooperation in the interests of the developmnt of the Asian-Pacific region, and they agreed that Vietnam and New Zealand would work together for that notable target. For his part, P.M. Bolger supported Vietnam to join ASEAN and participate in all Pacific systems. He also affirmed New Zealand's desire to have close relationship with all Asian-Pacific countries, including Vietnam.
