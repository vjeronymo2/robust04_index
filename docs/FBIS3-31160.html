Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Feb. 10 -- Solidarity leader Marian Krzaklewski on Thursday declared that his trade union was "willing to talk" with the government. Krzaklewski sent a letter to Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak in which he said that in connection with "tense relations" between the government and Solidarity he declared the willingness of his trade union to talk and to exchange clear positions of both sides, as well as to find solutions to present and potential conflicts. Krzaklewski pointed out that the present serious situation requires urgent negotiations.
