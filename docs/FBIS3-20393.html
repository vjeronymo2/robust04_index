Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Ioannina (DPA) -- On Saturday [26 March], at their meeting in Ioannina in northwestern Greece, the foreign ministers of the European Union agreed on assigning Hans Koschnick with heading the reconstruction in Mostar, the capital of Herzegovina. The former Social Democratic mayor of Bremen and internal affairs senator had been suggested by the Federal Government as head of the European Union administration of the town, which has been tortured by the war. This was stated by FRG Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel on the sidelines of the deliberations in Ioannina. FRG Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel has already phoned Koschnick. The former Bremen mayor has accepted the offer. Details must still be clarified, Kinkel said. The ministers' decision must now be formally confirmed at the next meeting of the chief diplomats in Luxembourg on 18 April. [passage omitted]
