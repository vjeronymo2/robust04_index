December 10, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

THE PROTEST AGAINST 'THE NORMAL HEART'

Like so many AIDS pieces in The Times, Koehler's article is blatant homosexual propaganda.

I believe that the producers of "The Normal Heart" had every right to present the play in Springfield, Mo. I also believe that local citizens had every right to organize against it.

When have you seen a play produced for the truly innocent victims of AIDS, such as those who received tainted blood?

It is fitting that the article's final word is "homophobia." After all, isn't this the standard epithet used against those who dissent from the AIDS "party line"?

RALPH W. BALLMER, Escondido
