February 25, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

CRUISE AND QUAYLE

I was enormously insulted by Greg Morrison's suggestion that Tom Cruise obviously accounts for the popularity of "Rain Man" with female audience members. ("What's Driving 'Rain Man?' Women," by Nina Easton, Calendar, Feb. 10).

Isn't this reminiscent of some members of the Republican Party suggesting a cute vice presidential candidate like Dan Quayle would attract women voters and close up the gender gap? I don't mean to suggest Tom Cruise in any way resembles our vacuous VP, but I went to "Rain Man" because of the subject matter and Dustin Hoffman's brilliance as an actor.

SUSAN MASON

Santa Monica
