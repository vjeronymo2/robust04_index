920423 FT 23 APR 92 / International Company News: Inco hit by sharp fall in earnings INCO, the western world's biggest nickel producer, barely broke even in the first quarter, as sagging metal prices, lower shipments and higher unit costs slashed earnings from its mining operations. Net earnings slid to USDollars 2.2m, or one cent a share, from Dollars 53.6m, or 50 cents. Sales fell almost a fifth to Dollars 680m, while operating earnings in the primary metal division slumped to Dollars 42m from Dollars 136m. The alloys and engineered products division suffered a small operating loss. First-quarter shipments of almost all metals were lower. Nickel deliveries slipped to 121m lb from 136m lb. Copper shipments fell to 59m lb from 75m lb, and platinum-group metals fell to 69,000oz from 86,000oz. Finished nickel inventories rose to 60m lb on March 31, from 45m lb three months earlier. The average nickel price received by the company fell to Dollars 3.59 per lb from Dollars 3.94 a year earlier. Refined copper prices slipped to 99 cents from Dollars 1.10 per lb. Inco does not disclose unit production costs, but these are understood to be around Dollars 2.50 per lb of nickel and Dollars 1.60 per lb of copper.
