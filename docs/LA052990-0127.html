May 29, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

LOCAL;

REAGANS, GORBACHEVS TO MEET

Former President Ronald Reagan, who once called the Soviet Union an evil empire and challenged President Mikhail S. Gorbachev three years ago to tear down the Berlin Wall, will meet the Soviet leader in San Francisco next Monday, Reagan's office said today.

Reagan's wife, Nancy, and Raisa Gorbachev, wife of Gorbachev, will also attend the breakfast meeting, to be held at the home of the Soviet consul general in San Francisco, Reagan's office said.

The meeting between the Reagans and the Gorbachevs was at first expected to take place in the Soviet Consulate itself.

Soviet officials visited Reagan aides in Los Angeles two weeks ago and suggested the site for a meeting. But Reagan aides were said to have regarded the Consulate, technically Soviet territory, as not an ideal meeting place.

The meeting at the residence is apparently a compromise, local diplomatic sources said.
