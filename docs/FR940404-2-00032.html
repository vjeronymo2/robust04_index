[Docket Nos. RP94&hyph;85&hyph;001 and RP94&hyph;130&hyph;001] Colorado Interstate Gas Co.; Proposed Changes in FERC Gas Tariff March 29, 1994. Take notice that on March 24, 1994, Colorado Interstate Gas Company (CIG), tendered for filing as part of its FERC Gas Tariff, First Revised Volume No. 1, the following revise tariff sheet: First Substitute Original Sheet No. 369A CIG states that it has submitted First Substitute Original Sheet No. 369A to comply with Ordering Paragraph (B) of the March 9, 1994 Commission order issued in Docket Nos. RP94&hyph;85&hyph;000 and RP94&hyph;130&hyph;000. To comply with the March 9, 1994 order, CIG states that it has reduced the total Buyout-Buydown costs sought to be recovered in Docket Nos. RP94&hyph;85&hyph;000 and RP94&hyph;130&hyph;000 by $39,537, brining those total costs to $5,843,834. The jurisdictional portion of the corrected costs is $5,818,869. Consistent with CIG's proposal to absorb 50 percent of the jurisdictional portion of these costs, CIG now proposes to recover $2,909,435 from its affected jurisdictional firm sales customers. CIG has requested that the Commission accept this filing, to become effective January 20, 1994, the date the Commission accepted the original tariff sheets in Docket Nos. RP94&hyph;85&hyph;000 and RP94&hyph;130&hyph;000. CIG states that copies of the filing were served upon all persons listed on the Commission's service lists for Docket Nos. RP94&hyph;85&hyph;000 and RP94&hyph;103&hyph;000. Any person desiring to protest said filing should file a protest with the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, 825 North Capitol Street, NE., Washington, DC 20426, in accordance with §385.211 of the Commission's Rules and Regulations. All such protests should be filed on or before April 5, 1994. Protests will be considered by the Commission in determining the appropriate action to be taken but will not serve to make protestants parties to the proceeding. Copies of this filing are on file with the Commission and are available for public inspection in the public reference room. Lois D. Cashell, Secretary.
