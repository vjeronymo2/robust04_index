BFN [Text] Manila, June 16 (XINHUA) -- The Philippines' exports to China is projected to increase by 70 percent this year following the signing of a protocol on the importation and exportation of commodities between the two countries, an industry leader said today. Philippine Chamber of Commerce and Industry President Herman Montenegro, who recently visited China together with a Philippine trade mission, said there is vast opportunities for Philippine products in China. Montenegro said Philippine construction materials like saniware products are hot and fast selling items in China as the country is currently experiencing a wave of construction activity. In Beijing alone, he said, some 10 million square meters is being constructed annually. The bilateral trade between the Philippines and China amounted to 480 million U.S. dollars and is expected to increase by 35 percent this year, according to the Department of Trade and Industry. The trade mission, Montenegro said, was able to establish a trade desk in Guangdong Province which would help establish contact between Chinese and Filipino businessmen. Another mission is being planned for October this year with the objective of organizing focus groups in Beijing, Shanghai, Xiamen and Guandong, Montenegro said.
