Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Jakarta, Feb 3 (ANTARA) -- Representatives of APEC (Asia Pacific Economic Cooperation) member countries at a meeting here Thursday agreed to elect the United States as chairman of the forum's Budget and Administration Committee. The meeting, officially called APEC Senior Officials Meeting (APEC-SOM), is the first of a series of four scheduled to prepare an APEC ministerial conference in November. With the agreement reached at Thursday's meeting, the United States would be the first country to lead the forum's Budget and Administration Committee. Saodah Syahruddin, an Indonesian delegate to the APEC-SOM, said the United States was one of three nominees for the position. The meeting eventually chose the United States in view of its position as former chairman of APEC's first summit meeting which took place in Seattle in November last year. Mrs. Syahruddin, who is director of developing countries economic cooperation at Indonesia's Foreign Ministry, explained that the meeting also agreed to make it a convention that the chairman of the last APEC summit meeting automatically becomes chairman of APEC's Budget and Administration Committee for the subsequent term. This meant that after Indonesia acts as chairman of the second APEC summit meeting scheduled for later this year, it would thereafter also serve as the forum's Budget and Administration Committee leader, she said. Mrs. Syahruddin said delegates at Thursday's meeting earlier considered two other ways of determining who would lead the Budget and Administration Committee. One proposal would have the chairman of a coming APEC summit become the committee leader. The other would require the chairman of the committee to be appointed based on an election held annually. "But finally, all participants agreed that the chairman of the last APEC summit meeting should serve as chairman of the committee. This will be a permanent procedure," she said. Mrs. Syahruddin also said APEC will not admit new members during the next three years. The question of admitting new members would be studied first, she added. Currently APEC has 17 members, namely the six countries grouped in ASEAN -- Brunei Darussalam, Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, plus Australia, Canada, New Zealand, China, Taiwan, Korea, Hongkong, Papua New Guinea, Japan, the United States, and Mexico. At the Seattle summit, it was agreed that Chile would be admitted as a member at the summit due to be convened in Indonesia next November. The APEC-SOM meeting opened last Wednesday and will end on Friday. It is being attended by officials of its member countries and three observers.
