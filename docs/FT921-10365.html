920206 FT 06 FEB 92 / Businessman remanded A BUSINESSMAN charged over the collapsed Bank of Credit and Commerce International was refused bail when he appeared before City of London magistrates yesterday. Mr Mohammed Abdul Baqi, 66, of Kings Langley, Hertfordshire, is accused of conspiring to falsely inform accountants that a company owed BCCI more than Dollars 76m. He was remanded in custody until February 11. Mr Baqi is a former managing director of Attock Oil, an oilproducing and trading group based in London. He is the first person to be arrested in Britain in connection with the investigation. It is alleged that he conspired with Mr Basheer Chowdry and other members of the BCCI senior management to supply audit confirmations to Price Waterhouse, external auditors of BCCI (Overseas) Ltd, showing that Attock Oil International owed BCCI sums totalling Dollars 76,517,952 - to his knowledge misleading, false or deceptive in that the debts were not in fact owed.
