Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 17 (CTK) -- Ukrainian Minister for External Economic Relations Oleh Slepichev and Czech Trade and Industry Minister Vladimir Dlouhy today signed an agreement on economic cooperation between their two countries. He also signed an agreement on the support and mutual protection of investments with Czech Finance Minister Ivan Kocarnik. Dlouhy told reporters he considered important that the Czech Republic should sign accords adjusting mutual commercial relations with all successor states to the Soviet Union corresponding to the regulations of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade. A Finance Ministry spokeswoman told CTK the Czech Republic was bound by 16 accords signed before the Czechoslovak Federation split (on 1 January 1993) and it automatically assumed all the responsibilities resulting from them. Since the split, the spokeswoman said, the Czech Republic has signed a further ten accords, which are not yet in force. The countries concerned are Hungary, Poland, Romania, Slovenia, Ukraine, Tajikistan, Thailand, Australia, Portugal, and Egypt. Slepichev is a member of the Ukrainian delegation led by Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko and scheduled to leave the Czech Republic this afternoon.
