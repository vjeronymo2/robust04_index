Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Ivantsov] [Text] Moscow February 8 TASS -- The Russian Government is determined to cut the inflation rate to 7-9 percent and the state budget deficit to 7-7.5 percent of the Gross Domestic Product by the end of this year, according to Economics Minister Aleksandr Shokhin. He was speaking at the congress "reality and prospects for the development of the economy in Russia, which is being held in Moscow within the framework of the international forum "wold experience and the Russian economy". Shokhin said the government's priorities this year include financial stabilization, combatting inflation and toughening the tax and financial discipline. With this in mind the government plans to cut budget spending and encourage effective use of budgetary resources. According to Aleksandr Shokhin, any projects that require funding from the budget, will be first examined by experts to see if they meet stringent international standards. Any benefits provided by the state must be fully and comprehensively accounted for by the recipient enterprises in reports on their activity and financial incomes of their leaders. Shokhin said this approach will increase the revenue. Last year's revenue reached 65-70 percent of the plan. This year's target is 80 percent.
