BFN [Excerpt] New Army units are expected to be armed soon, as a result of an agreement reached between the government and the Mozambique National Resistance [Renamo] today, regarding the selection of weapons for the future army. A meeting held between the government, Renamo, and the United Nations at the Military Club in Maputo this afternoon decided to send a tripartite team to Chimoio on 8 April to select the weapons to be used by the new army [words indistinct] to the Joint Commission for the Formation of the Mozambique Defense Armed Forces for the subsequent distribution to the new army units. Another issue discussed today was the issue of the 16,000 troops demobilized by the government in 1992 before the signing of the General Peace Accord. A source from the United Nations presiding over the meeting told Radio Mozambique that the parties came to the conclusion that there is no technical reason preventing these soldiers from receiving a demobilization subsidy.
