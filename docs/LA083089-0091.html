August 30, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

THE WORLD

The World Tourism Organization voted to shelve the issue of Palestinian membership until 1991. Delegates to the organization's general assembly in Paris approved by a 40-34 vote a U.S. motion to postpone any decision on the issue. Other members of the 107-member organization were either absent or abstained. There was no immediate reaction from Palestine Liberation Organization officials. Since the PLO declared a state of Palestine last November, publicly renouncing terrorism and recognizing Israel, it has tried to gain entry to two other U.N. agencies -- the World Health Organization and the Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization. Both agencies also voted to shelve the requests.
