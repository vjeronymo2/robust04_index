941004 FT 04 OCT 94 / Companies House plans attacked London users of Companies House said yesterday that plans to contract out its services to the private sector threatened the future availability of corporate information in the capital. About 50 users, specialising in company formations and searches, met in London to question SRU, the private-sector consultants seeking views on contracting out. Companies House, the official repository of corporate information in England, Scotland and Wales, is based in Cardiff and has five regional offices. It holds the details of nearly 1m companies and last year handled the incorporation of nearly 100,000 new ones. A campaign led by the Civil and Public Services Association aims to kill off the plan announced by Mr Michael Heseltine, trade and industry secretary, in July this year. Mr Kevin Brown, managing director of Marriots Company Services, said: 'SRU did not appear to have any idea about the structure of the market. They simply have not thought the process through.' He said his company paid Pounds 60,000 a year in fees to Companies House, and warned that contracting out threatened much higher access fees and the future of the London retail outlet. 'I think this is simply an ideological move,' he said. Mr Graham Cowan, managing director of Access Company Information, said London users feared that information might only be available in an expensive wholesale form in future. He said many small businesses were worried that carrying out daily company searches would be impossible and that in the end the London service would be scrapped. Business information companies feared that data might be concentrated in the hands of between two and four large operators spending Pounds 100,000 a year for access. Mr Peter Scott of SRU said it planned to meet many more customers and hear their views, and that no final decisions had been taken about the future of Companies House. A final report to Mr Heseltine is due in late October.
