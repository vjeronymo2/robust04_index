BFN [By reporter Ji Xinlong (1213 2450 7893)] [Text] Pyongyang, 20 May (XINHUA) -- Kye Ung-tae, member of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea's [DPRK] Korean Workers Party Central Committee Political Bureau and secretary of the party Central Committee, met here today with a Chinese Supreme People's Procuratorate delegation, led by Deputy Procurator General Liang Guoqing. Kye Ung-tae said: Facilitating contacts and enhancing exchanges and cooperation between DPRK and PRC procuratorial organs are conducive to deepening the friendship of the two countries and to improving their respective tasks. China and the DPRK are neighboring countries that are separated by a river. The Chinese- DPRK friendship was jointly established by Chairman Mao Zedong and President Kim Il-song. At present, the friendly relations have made good progress. He said: The DPRK values such relations and will make efforts to further develop the relations to a higher level. Kye Ung-tae also briefed the Chinese guests on relevant situation of the DPRK nuclear issues and the DPRK's new economic strategies. Liang Guoqing explained to Kye Ung-tae China's domestic situation and its procuratorial work. The Chinese Supreme People's Procuratorate delegation arrived here on 13 May.
