BFN ["Pool" item] [Text] Belgrade, May 19 (TANJUG) -- Moslem forces on Thursday morning followed strong artillery fire with major infantry attacks on Serb positions west of Brcko (northeast Bosnia), but were repelled by the Bosnian Serb Army, the Bosnian Serb Army Press Centre in Brcko said on Thursday. On Wednesday evening the Moslems launched another offensive in Brcko by opening mortar fire on Serb positions five km to the west of this town. Intensive Moslem massing of forces has also been reported in the Sava River valley where the Moslems are bringing reinforcements from the region of Tuzla, Moslem-controlled town south of Brcko. According to Bosnian Serb sources, two brigades of the First Moslem Army Corps have been transferred from the Sarajevo area. The Moslems continue violating the ceasefire agreement in the region of Sarajevo by opening strong sniper fire on the Serb settlements of Ilidza and Grbavica and the Local Rehabilitation Institute, the Bosnian Serb Army said. Along the entire front line close to Orasje, Bosnian Croat forces (HVO) on Wednesday opened infantry and artillery fire on Serb positions. Troop movements towards Serb positions were also recorded on the left bank of the Sava in the territory of Croatia, as reported by U.N. monitors stationed in Brcko, the Bosnian Serb Army sources said. A general artillery and infantry attack launched by the Moslems on Wednesday along the Serb defence lines on Mt. Vlasic (central Bosnia) continued on Thursday, TANJUG reported from Zvornik citing Bosnian Serb Army sources.
