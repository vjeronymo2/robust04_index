921008 FT 08 OCT 92 / UK Company News: Allied Leisure falls by 29% ALLIED LEISURE, the nightclub and ten-pin bowling operator, yesterday reported a 29 per cent fall in pre-tax profits to Pounds 2.21m in the year ended July as leisure spending continued to show no signs of recovering. The company has yet to sell any of the loss making theme bars which it said earlier this year it was writing off its books with an extraordinary charge of Pounds 3.23m. Since the company's most recent rights issue 18 months ago, the share price has dropped from about 100p to 36p. Pre-tax profits in the year to July fell from Pounds 3.1m to Pounds 2.21m on sales up 25 per cent at Pounds 27.42m. Earnings per share fell from 12.2p to 5.26p but the group will pay a final dividend of 3.25p, making a total for the year of 4.75p. After Pounds 13m of capital expenditure on its Megabowl, including two new sites at Preston and Dundee, net debt has risen to Pounds 14.3m. As a result gearing has risen to 51 per cent from 24 per cent last year. Mr Duncan Moss, finance director said the company aimed to maintain gearing below 55 per cent. Then group has made a Pounds 304,000 provision covering future costs relating to the stand-alone theme bars which are to be sold and have been written off in the accounts.
