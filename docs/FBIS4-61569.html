BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Denis Perkin] [Text] Moscow, 6 May -- Russia is ready to continue to contribute to the settlement of the regional conflict in Karabakh and is counting on the support of CSCE, UN, and other organizations, Andrey Kozyrev said today at a meeting with the Swedish diplomat Jan Eliasson who heads the CSCE peace conference on Nagornyy Karabakh [Nagorno-Karabakh]. According to Russia's foreign minister, "the cooperation between the Russian intermediary and CSCE is improving" in the sphere of unblocking the situation and their efforts are mutually complementary. Andrey Kozyrev stressed Russia's special interest in putting an end to the Karabakh conflict because its links with the region have deep historical roots. In his words, "we cannot remain indifferent." The leading Russian diplomat said that other countries were taking an active part in discussing the issue. In particular, the situation in Nagornyy Karabakh was touched upon during his working meeting with Karl Bildt, the Swedish head of government, in St. Petersburg on Thursday [5 May]. Andrey Kozyrev said to Jan Eliasson: "Although you are representing the CSCE here rather than Sweden, nevertheless, we are pleased to say that we have reached an understanding on this matter on the bilateral level as well."
