930901 FT 01 SEP 93 / Link of jobs to growth queried POLICIES DESIGNED to increase the rate of economic growth in Europe will do very little to cut unemployment, Mr Paul Ormerod, a former economics director of the Henley Centre for Forecasting, says. The problem of unemployment is a question 'not of economics, but of social values and social relationships', he writes in a paper prepared for a seminar on full employment in Europe to be held this week in Sussex. Societies with low unemployment are characterised by high levels of 'social cohesion'.
