BFN [Text] Egypt and Pakistan signed a judicial cooperation agreement today. The agreement provides for extraditing criminals and terrorists and individuals implicated in illegal dealing in weapons and explosives. Justice Minister Counselor Faruq Sayf-al-Nasr and his Pakistani counterpart Iqbal Haidar signed the agreement. Sayf-al-Nasr affirmed that Pakistan, like all other Islamic states, condemns terrorism in every form. He noted that the agreement they initialed aims to fight crime and guarantee respect for human rights. The final signing will take place in Islamabad soon. The Pakistani justice minister commended the Justice Ministry's efforts to conclude the agreement. He noted that the Pakistani Government is willing to enforce its articles as of today if the Egyptian Government can specify the names of terrorists who have escaped to Pakistan so that they can be handed over like any other criminal.
