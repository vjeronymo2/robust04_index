BFN [Text] Havana, 26 May (PL) -- The Cuban weekly GRANMA INTERNATIONAL will be published in German for distribution in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and The Netherlands to inform tourists from those countries who visit the island, the newspaper's directors announced here today. GRANMA INTERNATIONAL editorial director Gabriel Molina said, at a ceremony during which Edition Zero in German was presented, that the event was a day for celebration because a dream has come true after four years of hard work. GRANMA's first issue of its monthly edition to German-speaking European countries is dedicated to Cuba's struggle to maintain its sovereignty and the achievements of its revolution despite the disappearance of the Socialist Bloc and the intensification of Washington's blockade. Molina noted that the German edition of GRANMA INTERNATIONAL, the first Latin American publication in that language, will at first be issued monthly. Once it is consolidated, it will become a weekly publication as are the Spanish, English, French, and Portuguese editions. GRANMA was initially published in three foreign languages on 15 February 1966, later in Portuguese, and now in German. It now it circulates in 76 countries. For the past two years it has been printed in Argentina, Mexico, and Brazil. The German edition will be printed in Luxembourg so that it will reach readers faster. Including the copies printed in various countries, GRANMA's publishes over 70,000 copies.
