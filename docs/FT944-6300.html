941130 FT 30 NOV 94 / Budget 94 (News and Analysis): Making the economy work better - At a glance guide FISCAL STANCE The Budget is designed 'to ensure that the improvements in the public finances announced in 1993 are delivered and to introduce further structural reforms to make the economy work better'. Spending squeezed Government spending plans in 1995-96 to be reduced by Pounds 7.2bn, with further reductions of Pounds 9bn in 1996-97, and Pounds 9.8bn in 1997-98. This reflects a fall in the projected levels of inflation and government savings. The control total has been reduced to Pounds 249.6bn in 1994-1995, Pounds 1.3bn below the level announced last November in current prices. The control total is forecast to rise to Pounds 255.7bn in 1995-1996, Pounds 6.9bn lower at current prices than last year's projections, and Pounds 0.5bn lower in 1993-1994 prices. As a share of national output total government spending is projected to fall from 44 per cent to 41 per cent over the three years. Page 20 Limited tax changes Tax changes in the Budget are relatively limited. Changes in income tax reliefs and VAT announced last year and scheduled to come into place in April next year will raise taxes by a further Pounds 2.75bn in 1995-96 and Pounds 0.75bn in the following year. This will be partly offset by measures to help businesses and the unemployed, and ease the impact of the imposition of value added tax on fuel. These will cost Pounds 1bn in 1995-96. As a result, the total level of taxes received by the government is projected to be Pounds 252.5bn in 1994-95, and rise to Pounds 278.9bn in the following year. Taxes and social security revenues as a proportion of GDP will rise from 33.75 per cent in 1993-1994 to 35.25 per cent in 1994-1995. Page 23 PSBR cut further Public borrowing, including privatisation proceeds, is forecast to fall from Pounds 34.4bn in 1994-95 to Pounds 21.5bn in 1995-96. The public sector borrowing requirement is then projected to fall further, to Pounds 13bn in 1996-97, Pounds 5bn in 1997-98, and then return to balance at the end of the decade. Consequently, the PSBR as a proportion of gross domestic product is forecast to fall to 5 per cent in 1994-95, and then 3 per cent in 1995-96, and then 1.75 per cent in the following year. Page 20 SPENDING Public sector pay and personnel Tight controls to be maintained on public sector pay and government departments to offset any increase in public sector pay next year by increased efficiency. Total civil service numbers to fall to below 500,000 over the next four years. Central government Total central government costs to remain constant in cash terms for the next four years, representing a 10 per cent fall, in real terms, in the cost of government. Local authorities The 1995-96 total standard spending in the UK to be 2.2 per cent higher than this year. Page 27 Social security The rules covering housing benefit to be changed so that the cost of the benefit will not be reimbursed to local authorities for rents that are significantly higher than local averages. The level of state support for mortgage payments in cases of sickness and unemployment to be reduced. Further initiatives against social security fraud, including the introduction of electronic benefit payment systems in Post Offices. Page 27 Education Education spending to be increased by almost 1 per cent in real terms next year. Student grants and loans to increase in line with inflation. Page 27 Home Office Spending on police to rise 3 per cent in 1995-96. Chief constables to be given more control over their budgets. Further initiatives to attract private investment into prisons. Page 26 Health Health budget to increase by Pounds 1.3bn, or 1 per cent next year. This follows an increase of 3.75 per cent in real terms this year as a result of falling inflation. Further initiatives to attract private finance in health construction pro-jects. Transport Public spending on motorways and trunk roads to be reduced significantly. However efforts will be made to encourage further private-sector investment. London Underground is expected to attract considerable private finance, as well as Pounds 1bn of public investment in the next financial year. Page 27 Urban regeneration project An extra Pounds 800m will be made available for new projects in deprived urban areas in 1996-97. Page 26 MEASURES TO REDUCE UNEMPLOYMENT Benefit system changes Family Credit to be increased for people in full-time work. The payment of housing benefit and Family Credit to be speeded up for people taking jobs. Job-finding schemes such as Workwise to be extended. Experimental schemes paying family credit to childless people to be introduced. Page 17 Employment tax changes Employers who hire people who have been unemployed for more than two years will be be exempt from National Insurance contributions for a year. Employer National Insurance contributions for the low-paid to be reduced 0.6 per cent a year from April 1995. Page 17 TAXES Business taxation No change to Advance Corporation Tax, Corporation Tax or Capital Allowances. Recovery of VAT to be allowed on business lease car purchases. Consultation paper announced on taxation of waste disposal in landfill, with the aim of reducing employer National Insurance contributions. Further measures to reduce tax-avoidance schemes on the issue of securities and the buying of companies to utilise management expenses. Small business support Threshold for business VAT registration to be increased to Pounds 46,000. Thirty per cent increase in threshold for monthly PAYE payments for businesses, taking in two-thirds of all businesses. Business rates Rises as a result of revaluation to be limited to a real 10 per cent for large businesses/buildings and 7.5 per cent for small ones. Page 24 Export cover ECGD credit insurance premiums to be reduced by 10 per cent. The limit for cover for key developing markets to be raised by Pounds 300m in 1997-98. Personal taxes No change in 20 pence, 25 pence or 40 pence rates of income tax. Full indexation of personal allowances and the threshold for higher rate tax. Personal allowances for over 65s up Pounds 430 - Pounds 330 more than indexation. The 20 pence lower band to be extended by further Pounds 200 to Pounds 3,200. The capital gains tax exempt amount is increased from Pounds 5800 to Pounds 6000 a year. The inheritance tax threshold is increased from Pounds 150,000 to Pounds 154,000. Venture capital Tax relief at 20 per cent on first Pounds 100,000 invested in venture capital trusts, and tax-free dividends. Amusement arcades Amusement machines to be taxed in line with gaming machines. Page 22 Tax exempt special savings accounts (Tessas) Tessas to be extended beyond their first expiry date of January 1996. Investors who have held a Tessa for five years able to renew their account, with tax-free interest on sums up to Pounds 9,000. Page 29 Personal equity plans (Peps) Peps to be extended to cover not only investment in equity, but also specified corporate bonds. Page 29 Taxes on spending: No change in value-added tax. VAT-on-fuel compensation package Package of help cushioning effects of VAT on fuel to be extended. The Home Energy Efficiency Scheme basket of grants increases by Pounds 10m. Cold-weather payments rise to Pounds 8.50 per week from Pounds 6 two years ago. Single pensioners will receive Pounds 52 more in their retirement pensions as compensation for VAT on fuel while couples will receive Pounds 73 beginning 1995-96. From April 1996 there will be Pounds 68 extra on the single pensioner rate and Pounds 96 for pensioner couples. People of working age cannot expect tax cuts or a reverse of taxes already announced. Page 17 Fuel and vehicle taxes Road fuel duties to increase by a real 5 per cent a year in future Budgets, as announced last year. Petrol taxes to go up immediately by 2.5 pence a litre for leaded and unleaded petrol. Tax on diesel to rise 3 pence a litre. Vehicle excise duty on cars will rise by Pounds 5 to Pounds 135. Page 23 Tobacco and alcohol Tobacco duties will continue to be increased by on average a real 3 per cent a year, as announced last year. Tax on a packet of 20 cigarettes to be increased by 10 pence. No change in the level of duties on beer, table wine and spirits. Page 23 Tax avoidance Measures to tighten some tax loopholes, including measures against the avoidance of VAT through property transactions and share issues, or operations with discounted securities. THE FORECAST Low inflation: Underlying inflation expected to remain low. Target range remains 1-4 per cent and is expected to fall to the lower half of this during the present parliament. Underlying inflation is expected to reach a plateau at 2.5 per cent next year, due to higher commodity prices and increasing profits, after which it is expected to fall. Page 20 Stronger growth GDP during 1994 will rise by over 4 per cent while the 1995 forecast increases to 3.25 per cent from summer forecasts of 2.75 per cent for 1994 and 1995. The Treasury forecasts that the current account will improve Pounds 6bn this year. OPEN REPO MARKET The Bank of England is to publish a consultative paper, on development of open gilt repo market.
