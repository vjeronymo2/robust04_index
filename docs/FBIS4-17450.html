BFN [Text] At a meeting between the parliamentary leadership and law-enforcement officials in Tbilisi Friday, Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze announced the beginning of a crackdown on terrorism and corruption. "Neither I nor the security service will rest until we catch the murderer of Deputy Interior Minister Giya Gulua," he was quoted as saying. Shevardnadze called corruption "the biggest danger facing the country." He added that "it will devour all Georgia, and dreams of democracy will remain a fairy tale." Shevardnadze instructed the Interior Ministry, security service, prosecutor's and judicial bodies and the whole government to develop a draft decree on tighter corruption controls, including specific tasks, within ten days.
