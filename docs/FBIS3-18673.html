Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "GATT: Social Clause Requested by European Parliament"] [Text] Strasbourg, 7 Mar (ANSA) -- The European Parliament [EP] has requested the inclusion of a "social clause" in the new General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) to be signed in Marrakesh this April, it was learned here today. The clause's objective would be to lay down stipulations prohibiting forced labour and the employment of minors to be contained in the international regulations that govern world trade. "In a world in which commercial exchange takes on a global dimension and markets no longer have frontiers, commercial activity could prove a forceful means of promoting social innovation and improving conditions for the weaker sections of society in the poorer countries," the European Parliament declared in defence of its request.
