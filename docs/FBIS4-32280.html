BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 20/6/1994 -- Oliviu Gherman, the president of the Romanian Senate met Monday, 20 June, with the president of the Parliament of the Republic of Moldova, Petru Lucinschi. The guest presented the situation in the Republic of Moldova at the moment, and expressed the view that in the question of his country's relations with Romania one should be able to overcome the emotional approach and replace it with a pragmatic attitude. The sides agreed on the necessity of developing closer parliamentary ties forged through direct contacts between the standing committees of the two legislative bodies. The president of the Romanian Senate, Oliviu Gherman, also met Monday, 20 June, with the president of the Grand National Assembly of Turkey, Husamettin Cindoruk. Messrs. Gherman and Cindoruk approached matters regarding the bilateral ties and the activities of the economic cooperation of the Black Sea, with a stress on the prospects the parliamentary assembly of this organization offers to the member states. The sides agreed that if willing to develop long-lasting economic relations, the partners must base their actions on a firm political will for cooperation and understanding. The political stability of the member countries, the guest underlined, will ensure the economic stability as a natural consequence. Mr. Cindoruk emphasized that everything must be done to stop the conflict between Armenia and Azerbaijan, while ensuring the unity of Georgia.
