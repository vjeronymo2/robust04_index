BFN [Text] Amman--Well-informed Jordanian sources say that the Jordanian Deputy Prime Minister Sa'id al-Tall's recent visit to Baghdad dealt with the issue of Jordanian nationals being held in the Baghdad regime's jails. Official Jordanian circles estimate the number of these convicted detainees, who were charged with offenses ranging from illegal currency exchanges and smuggling to espionage for a foreign state, at 200. The Jordanian Government is trying to solve this problem by reaching an understanding with the Iraqi authorities. The sources also say that during his visit to Baghdad, al-Tall reached an agreement with the Iraqi officials on organizing a meeting between the interior ministers of the two countries in order to tackle unsolved issues facing the Jordanian nationals in Iraqi jails.
