930717 FT 17 JUL 93 / Finance and The Family: Where to find a PHI policy THE FOLLOWING is a small selection of companies providing PHI, but many others offer similar policies. An independent financial adviser can help you to find the best one for your circumstances. Unless otherwise stated, the premiums quoted are for level cover for a male white collar worker who is 35 next birthday, earns around Pounds 40,000 and wants the maximum sickness benefit possible, retiring at 65 with a 13-week deferred period; and for a woman with the same details except for a retirement age of 60. The premiums quoted are for illustration and are not strictly comparable. Friends Provident. Guaranteed premiums. Maximum benefit Pounds 1,000 a week. For benefit of Pounds 480 a week, a man's premium would be Pounds 43.24; a woman's Pounds 58.12. Norwich Union. Reviewable premiums. Maximum benefit Pounds 78,000 a year. For a benefit of around Pounds 2,083 a month, the premium would be Pounds 48.20 for a man, Pounds 37.16 for a woman. Permanent Insurance, a subsidiary of the Medical Sickness Society, which insures doctors and dentists. Reviewable premiums. Maximum benefit Pounds 1,000 a week. For benefit of Pounds 521 a week, a man's premium would be Pounds 44.35 and a woman's Pounds 68.97. Prime Health, a specialist health insurer. Reviewable premiums for benefit of Pounds 2,000 a month, increasing by 5 per cent, would cost a man Pounds 33.60 a month and a woman Pounds 43. Standard Life. Reviewable premiums for benefit of Pounds 2,257 a month, assuming a retirement age of 65 for both with cover increasing by 5 per cent, would be Pounds 56.02 for a man and Pounds 82.97 for a woman. Sun Alliance. Guaranteed premiums. Maximum salary coverable Pounds 120,000 a year. For benefit of Pounds 480 a week, the man's premium would be Pounds 45.99 and the woman's Pounds 67.74, with non-smoker discount.
