930909 FT 09 SEP 93 / UK Company News: Vardon Pounds 0.5m in the black VARDON, the leisure company which owns Sea Life Centres and The London Dungeon and came to the market in October 1992, made pre-tax profits of Pounds 540,000 in the six months to June 30, against restated losses of Pounds 282,000. The interim dividend is raised from 0.25p to 0.3p. Turnover at the visitor attractions division grew to Pounds 5.44m (Pounds 3.15m), giving operating profits of Pounds 539,000 (Pounds 302,000). Three new attractions were added during the period - the Seal Sanctuary in Cornwall was bought in February and Sea Life Centres in Southend, Essex, and Scheveningen, the Netherlands, were opened in May. The London Dungeon was refurbished and now includes the Jack the Ripper Experience. Capital expenditure totalled Pounds 3.9m in the period. The group's 10 bingo clubs, acquired in May, traded for seven weeks within the group and contributed turnover of Pounds 1.02m and operating profits of Pounds 99,000. Group turnover was Pounds 6.45m (Pounds 3.75m). Earnings worked through at 0.7p (losses 0.9p).
