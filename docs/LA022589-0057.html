February 25, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

SOUTHERN BAPTISTS PLANNING DOOR-TO-DOOR DRIVE IN VEGAS

Southern Baptists plan a door-to-door evangelism campaign this summer when they hold their convention in Las Vegas -- the city of slot machines and legalized gambling.

More than 1,160 lay people and pastors from several states already have signed up to knock on doors before the denomination's annual meeting in the Nevada city June 13-15.

"This is an organized witnessing effort to share Christ with every home in the city," said Larry J. Lewis, president of the Southern Baptist Convention Home Mission Board.

The Home Mission Board also has distributed pages of the Las Vegas telephone directory to more than 6,000 "prayer volunteers," who have pledged to pray for residents of the city.

The volunteers will get pep talks from television minister Jerry Falwell and Los Angeles Baptist Pastor E.V. Hill -- neither of whom are Southern Baptists -- and Arthur Blessitt, a Hollywood evangelist who has dragged a cross through much of the world, and Florida evangelist Rick Stanley, stepbrother of the late Elvis Presley.

They will distribute about 10,000 New Testaments and 10,000 gospel tracts in the city.

Las Vegas Chamber of Commerce President Mark Smith, a 30-year resident of the gambling capital, said he has "never heard of anything like this happening in Las Vegas before. But it may be better received than a lot of people might think."

Simultaneous revivals are set for eight Las Vegas area churches during the week before the annual meeting, which is expected to attract about 20,000 Southern Baptists.
