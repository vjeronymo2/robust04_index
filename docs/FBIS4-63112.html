BFN [Text] Beijing, May 15, RIA -- Satisfied with the talks with Chinese colleagues, Ivan Rybkin, chairman of the State Duma of the Federal Assembly of Russia, pointed to the importance of restoring Russo-Chinese parliamentary contacts at a briefing at the end of his delegation's second day in China. "The talks are conducted in a businesslike and serious atmosphere," he said. The Russian delegation "is accumulating information vital for cooperation." The sides expressed interest in developing contacts between the north-eastern part of China and Siberia, exchanged opinion on international problems and discussed the situation in the planet's hot spots. Rybkin said that good relations developed between the State Duma and the Russian Government in the past four months. He also said about potential candidates to the post of the Russian president: "We will continue to be short of leaders until we establish elections of administration heads." When asked if he would run for the post, Rybkin replied that many people don't suspect the burden of responsibilities of the president.
