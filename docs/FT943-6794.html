940826 FT 26 AUG 94 / World Commodities Prices: Wool Wool sold at higher prices this week wherever auctions were held. In Australia the Eastern States market indicator closed 22 cents higher than a week ago at 728 cents a kg. Prices paid for finer merinos wore again rising more quickly than other categories but the rise was nevertheless general and definite. South African wool at the first auction of the season there sold at prices 6% tp 12% higher than at the last sale in June, and there was a complete clearance. New Zealand crossbred wool was dearer on the week in the 2.5% to 5% range, and British wool at Wednesday's Bradford auction met with strong demand at prices 7.5% to 12.5% higher than at the comparable sale on July 27. Rising prices in recent weeks have brought out more business and some of it is well forward.
