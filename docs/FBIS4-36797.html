BFN [Text] Baghdad, June 5, INA--Baghdad's Chamber of Commerce has recently received commercial offers from a number of Arab and foreign companies for economic and commercial cooperation with Iraq. In a statement to a local daily, chairman of the Baghdad's Chamber of Commerce Zuhayr 'Abd-al-Ghafur said the chamber has received an offer from a British firm specialised in food processing and another offer from an Australian firm specialized in exporting wheat, rice, sugar, milk and meat. Moreover, the chamber has also received an offer from the Egyptian al-Zahra' Company for Construction Materials, Steel and Glass, said Mr. 'Abd-al-Ghafur, adding that seven other companies from Spain, Pakistan, India, Turkey, Jordan, Singapore and Sri Lanka have expressed their desire and readiness to resume and establish commercial ties with Iraqi traders.
