920619 FT 19 JUN 92 / International Company News: Dow Corning to take Dollars 45m implant charge DOW CORNING, the 50-50 joint venture between Dow Chemical and Corning, yesterday said it would take a Dollars 45m second quarter pre-tax charge for costs related to its discontinued silicone breast implants. In the same period last year, Dow Corning had net earnings of Dollars 40.5m on sales of Dollars 462.1m. The charge comes on the heels of Dollars 24m in implant-related expenses in the first quarter and a Dollars 25m pre-tax charge in the fourth quarter. Mr Keith McKennon, chairman and chief executive, said: 'We believe this second quarter pre-tax charge, combined with previous charges, is sufficient to cover reasonably anticipated costs associated with breast implants.'
