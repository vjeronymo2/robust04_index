Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 29 KYODO -- Sales at Japan's large retailers decreased 2.8 percent in February from a year earlier to 1,452.3 billion yen, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) said Tuesday [29 March]. It was the 21st straight month of falling sales at department stores and supermarket chains, the ministry said in a preliminary report. The stagnant performance reflected continuing price consciousness among Japanese consumers, the ministry said. Sales at 419 department stores across the nation dropped 4.4 percent to 723.0 billion yen, for the 24th running month of declines from year-earlier levels, it said. Sales at 2,059 supermarket chains edged down 1.0 percent to 729.4 billion yen, marking the 19th straight month of drops. The 2.8 percent sales fall in February compares with a 2.5 percent drop registered in January.
