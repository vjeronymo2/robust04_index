Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 22 KYODO -- An Anglo-Japanese consortium has signed a contract to conduct a feasibility study for oil development in the Tarim Basin in far western China, a Japanese partner reported Tuesday [22 March]. Nippon Oil Co., Japan's leading distributor of oil products, said the agreement was signed in Beijing Tuesday with China's state-run National Petroleum and Natural Gas Corp. The group consists of BP Exploration Operating Co., an oil development arm of British Petroleum, and four Japanese oil and trading firms, including an affiliate of Nippon Oil. Under the three-year contract, the group will engage in a feasibility study on the development of oil deposits in the southeastern region of the basin, the company said. The company said the group has an option to negotiate with the Chinese corporation for oil production in the area. It said the study will begin in April.
