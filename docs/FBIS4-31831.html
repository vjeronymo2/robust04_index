BFN [Text] Warsaw, 15 June -- The Armed Forces have been a part of the state defense policy, but they should preserve their apolitical nature, Jerzy Milewski, secretary of state in the Ministry of National Defense said today, while summing up the proceedings of an international seminar, "Civilian and Democratic Control of the Armed Forces," organized in Warsaw at Poland's initiative and with cooperation from NATO Headquarters. The seminar was attended by representatives of 38 states of the former Warsaw Pact and the North Atlantic Alliance. Milewski emphasized that the most important decisions on defense policy should be made by the authorities coming from "free and general elections." Civilian control over the Army should not be identified with restrictions, but public acceptance is necessary for the principles of the defense policy, the functioning of the armed forces, and defense spending.
