Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Estonian government's press service announced that during the night of January 10, Estonian President Mart Laar held telephone discussions with members of the Latvian government following a provocation in Riga against Russian servicemen and diplomats, and the placing of Russian troops in Latvia in a state of increased combat readiness. The Latvian side informed Laar that the situation was getting back to normal. Both sides compared the events with the situation in Latvia in 1940 which led to those countries' occupation by the Soviet Union, and emphasized the importance of preserving peace and stability, and of efforts to ensure their countries' security. An agreement was reached on maintaining permanent contacts between the Latvian and Estonian governments until the crisis is resolved.
