BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- A group of experts led by Walter Hohlefelder, chief of the Department on Reactor Safety in the Federal Environment Ministry, left for Ukraine on 30 May to prepare steps for closing down the Chernobyl nuclear reactor. Two reactor blocs of the power plant are currently still in operation, while the third one was closed down following a fire. The experts are primarily developing a concept for gradually closing down the whole reactor, setting up a second "sarcophagus" for the defective reactor, and ensuring an adequate power supply by completing reactor blocs under construction at three other sites in Ukraine.
