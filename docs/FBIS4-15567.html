BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Velichko] [Text] Moscow April 8 -- "We have reached a basic agreement on joint construction of an underground nuclear power plant in the Far East with the Japanese side," Russian Minister for Nuclear Power Engineering Viktor Mikhailov told ITAR-TASS here today. In his words, the plant will be built in the area of Komsomolsk-on-Amur. It will be a reactor with a relatively low electric capacity of 200-300 megawatts. The minister said the agreement is estimated at 600-700 million U.S. dollars.
