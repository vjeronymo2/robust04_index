January 9, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

BRITISH VIEW

I am an Englishman who has spent the past two weeks on vacation with relatives in Los Angeles.

I am writing to comment on the subject of proportional representation, as raised by Matthew Shugart (letter, Jan. 1). He claims that Britain's electoral system "works," although the majority party almost never has a majority of votes. It does not work.

We desperately need proportional representation and the coalitions that are likely to result from it. Only these can rescue us from the extremes of pendulum politics in which each government tends to undo the work of its predecessor. (This tendency is much more marked than in America, with its checks and balances). It has also intensified the divisions between the prosperous south and the economically disadvantaged north of England, Scotland and Wales.

This apart, what kind of democracy can we really claim to be when, in the 1987 election, 43% of the votes cast for Conservatives gained 60% of the seats in Parliament, but over 20% who voted for the center alliance gained 3% of the seats?

TONY NOAKES

Middlesex, England
