December 23, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

CALIFORNIA IN BRIEF;

SONORA;

HISTORIC CAVE ADDED TO NATIONAL PARK

Stanislaus National Forest has completed acquisition of Bower Cave, which historians say was known to Indians hundreds of years ago.

The deal involved a land exchange with Fibreboard Corp. under which the Park Service obtained the 855-acre Linkletter Ranch, including the historic cave. Miwok Indians called the Mariposa County cave Oo-tin, meaning Home of the early Star and in the 1870s gold miners used the cave as a dance hall. Forest Service officials said access to the cave will be restricted because of potential hazards.
