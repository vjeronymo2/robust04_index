941001 FT 01 OCT 94 / Finance and the Family: Gilt auction success - At a glance Prices of UK government stock - gilt-edged - continued generally weak, partly because of the fall in US Treasury bonds on Thursday. But earlier in the week the government's Pounds 2bn gilt auction had drawn an unexpectedly strong response. Demand was much as predicted. But an unusually large proportion of the stock on offer went to long term investors - life insurance companies and pension funds. And some market-makers had to scrabble round buying stock after the auction. In the end the Bank of England had to sell them some.
