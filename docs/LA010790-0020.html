January 7, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

A FAMILY AFFAIR

Whenever people ask me about the situation in Nicaragua, I tell them it's a civil war. Now Art Seidenbaum has reviewed a book that points out how and why it is a civil war ("Memoirs of a Counter-Revolutionary" by Arturo Cruz Jr., Book Review, Nov. 19).

Families are so interrelated that when I lived in Nicaragua, we used to joke that one should never make disparaging remarks about anybody, because there was bound to be a relative of that person sitting next to you.

If the South, more than 100 years after our Civil War, is still hoping to "rise again," it should be obvious to our government that it is futile to expect the U.S. to ever resolve the differences in Nicaragua by intervening in their civil war.

Thanks for an excellent review.

ANGELA MCEWAN

WHITTIER
