930614 FT 14 JUN 93 / Nadir fraud inquiry 'IT IS quite clear that there is far more to this Nadir story than people had hitherto thought,' Mr Alistair Darling, chief Labour parliamentary spokesman on the City, said yesterday. He said he would ask Mr John Major, the prime minister, today to state 'how many ministers made representations or approaches to the attorney-general on behalf of Nadir once he came under the suspicion of the Serious Fraud Office'.
