Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 3 March. The Central Bank has reported that the Uruguayan public sector's gross foreign debt increased $33 million, or 0.8 percent, and that the net foreign debt dropped 4.1 percent in the first 11 months of 1993. The gross foreign debt was $4.1688 billion late in November, $35 million less than late in October, because of a drop in the public financial sector's debt. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2010 GMT 24 Feb 94) The National Statistics Institute on 2 March reported that the consumer price index rose 1.43 percent in February, the lowest monthly rate since December 1987. The cumulative inflation rate for the last 12 months was 48.96 percent, the best rate since April 1984. Health care registered the highest increase, with 3.93 percent; housing 2.19 percent, and recreation 1.77 percent. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 2112 GMT 2 Mar 94)
