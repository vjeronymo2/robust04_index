940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / World Stock Markets: Brazil A mild inflow of foreign money to the market due to expectations of stable US interest rates left share prices 8.6 per cent stronger on the Sao Paulo stock exchange. The Bovespa index ended 1,425 points higher at 17,909. Volume came to Cr296.5bn (Dollars 183.8m). Analysts said that lower inflation forecasts, which have dragged down Brazilian nominal long-term interest rates over the last few days, also helped the stock market's performance. Brazil June inflation forecasts were revised down by a full percentage point to 45.5 per cent.
