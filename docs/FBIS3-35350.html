Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih has confirmed that the tour by Donald Petterson, the U.S ambassador in Khartoum, of some areas in the south of the country took place with the knowledge of the Foreign Ministry. He added that the American ambassador had briefed him during a meeting with him today about his tour of some areas on the southern border. Abu-Salih said that the government approved the food drops for camps of displaced people in the Mundri and (Parajok) areas in cooperation with the United Nations. Dr. Abu-Salih added that he had assured the American ambassador that the government was trying to end to the ongoing war in the south, stressing that Sudan was very dependent on the initiative of the Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development states. The foreign minister affirmed that a cease-fire in the south should be part of a comprehensive solution to the problem of the south.
