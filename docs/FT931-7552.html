930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / Long-term unemployed exceeds 1m classified as long-term - has exceeded 1m for the first time in five years, David Goodhart writes. The figure rose by 75,000 in the quarter to January to 1,030,000, an increase of 38 per cent since January last year, with the largest rise in the south-east. The figure is still almost 327,000 below the peak of April 1986, and the proportion of the unemployed categorised as long-term is 34 per cent, below the peak of 43 per cent of October 1987.
