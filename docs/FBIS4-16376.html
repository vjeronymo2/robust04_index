BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents Andrey Krylovich and Anatoliy Krasnov] [Text] Moscow, 14 Apr -- South Korea views as "constructive" Russia's proposal to convene a multipartite conference on questions of the security and nuclear-free status of the Korean peninsula. This was stated today by Han Sung-chu, the foreign minister of the Republic of Korea at a meeting with Ivan Rybkin, chairman of the State Duma of the Russian Federation Federal Assembly. The South Korean minister is in Moscow on an official visit. The Russian initiative provides for the participation in such a conference by both Korean states, Russia, the United States, China, Japan and also the United Nations and the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA). Together with all the interested parties, the minister said, we will continue to analyze and study this proposal in an active way. Relations with the Republic of Korea, I. Rybkin noted, are a priority in Russian foreign policy. There is no doubt whatsoever that we are worried by the situation which has developed on the Korean peninsula. The chairman of the State Duma told the South Korean minister about the work of the Russian Parliament, stressing the importance of interparliamentary ties. Han Sung-chu conveyed the wish of the Republic of Korea President Kim Yong-sam to address the Russian parliamentarians during the course of his coming visit to Moscow. He invited Ivan Rybkin to pay a visit to South Korea.
