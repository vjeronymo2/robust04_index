940409 FT 09 APR 94 / Norton future looks uncertain The future of Norton, a nostalgic name in British motor-cycling, looks increasingly uncertain amid claims from the company's workers that the manufacturer's Canadian owners have failed to support its relaunch. Norton employees have called for a meeting to discuss the future of the company with directors of Norton Motors (1993), the Canadian-backed group which last year paid Pounds 500,000 for the Midlands plant. In a letter to Ms Rozanda Skalbania, one of Norton's new directors, they write: 'Since the takeover in August 1993, and now nearly six months later, we have not seen anything positive happening.' In reply, Ms Skalbania said last night: 'The comments in the document are misinformed and inaccurate.'
