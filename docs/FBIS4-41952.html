BFN [Text] The additional powers granted to the law-enforcement bodies by the president shall only be used in relation to individuals suspected of banditry or membership in a group of criminals who have committed serious crimes, says the joint directive on the procedure of implementing the decree, signed by acting Prosecutor-General Aleksey Ilyushenko, Interior Minister Viktor Yerin and Director of the Federal Counterintelligence Service Sergey Stepashin. The document says that the term "gang" shall be applied to two or more armed individuals who have formed a group for carrying out one or more attacks on organizations, enterprises or individual citizens. Pending the initiation of legal action, checks on suspects' financial and economic activity, and property and financial status, shall be made only if reliable evidence is available pointing to the individual's or organization's involvement in criminal activity. Preliminary checks shall be made following the issue of a permit by inquiry bodies, approved by the prosecutor. Prosecutors, investigators and specialists involved in the implementation of the decree shall be warned about their responsibility for disclosing or mishandling state and commercial secrets. Banks and commercial organizations shall present documents on their financial and economic activity, and information about the deposits and operations of juridical entities and individuals involved in serious organized crimes, following the receipt of a motivated inquiry from the prosecutor, investigator or head of an inquiry body, based on a suit or results of a check. An individual suspected of committing a crime and detained for more than three days shall have full access to the rights envisaged in the constitution and the Criminal Code, including the right to have a lawyer. If there is sufficient evidence, charges shall be brought against the detainee, after which he shall be put in custody for a term of up to 30 days. The directive instructs the prosecutors to take proper measures to reveal and exclude instances of illegal detention of citizens without prior checks on their financial and economic activity, and property and financial status, particularly the suspects' and defendants' relatives, physical entities and public associations. They shall also exclude actions which can inflict damage on citizens' property or reputation, promptly respond to complaints, take urgent measures to restore violated rights and initiate legal action against criminals.
