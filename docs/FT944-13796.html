941026 FT 26 OCT 94 / Observer: Dayly grind Lord Howe may be the last of the Thatcher government's troika to publish his memoirs, but he could be the first to stage a political comeback - if you listen to Sir Robin Day, the veteran TV interviewer. Sir Robin, attending the launch of Lord Howe's book, confidently predicted that Howe would soon be back in government as Lord Chancellor, giving it some 'much-needed bottom'. Then again, Sir Robin might be a bit biased. The two of them go back a long way. Both were called to the Bar on the same evening in 1952 and both stood for Parliament in 1959. Sir Robin and Geoffrey Howe failed, but it was at that election that Maggie Thatcher first became an MP. 'I disappeared into the obscurity of the TV studios . . . such is the way the destiny of great nations is decided,' joked Sir Robin.
