Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 18 (XINHUA) -- The Japanese Government today provided China with a loan totaling 40 billion yen as a compensation for its tremendous trade surplus over China. The contract was signed in Tokyo today by the Bank of China (BOC) and the Import and Export Bank of Japan. According to BOC officials, this is the second Japanese Government loan to China made specially to make up for the trade imbalance between the two countries. The first, made in 1987, totaled 30 billion Japanese yen. BOC officials said that the loan would be used to accelerate the construction of industrial projects in south China's Hainan Province and in Qingdao of east China's Shandong Province. Japan has been at the top of the list of China's trade partners. In 1993, its trade surplus over China was close to 7.5 billion U.S. dollars.
