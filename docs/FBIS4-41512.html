BFN [Text] In an interview with ZVYAZDA on 25 June, Uladzimir Zamyatalin, secretary to Belarusian Prime Minister Vyachaslaw Kebich, commented on the results of the first round of presidential elections. In particular, he said: "The prime minister realizes that there have been no elections since 1990. He understands the primary motives which guided people during the presidential elections. The point is that the people voted not against Kebich but against the current developments in the Republic." Uladzimir Zamyatalin voiced doubts over fears that when Lukashenka comes to power he will begin with repressions. The press secretary refuted rumors that the Kebich team will attempt to disrupt the second round of elections. He stated that Kebich and his team have not yet discussed his withdrawal from the elections.
