BFN [Text] Cairo, 29 Jun (MENA) -- Nine Yemeni leaders called today for an immediate cease-fire in Yemen and a quick return to negotiations in order to allow all efforts to bear fruit, stop the shedding of the Yemeni people's blood, protect Yemen's unity and territorial integrity, and halt the bleeding of human resources. In a faxed statement received by MENA's main office, the nine leaders called on the two sides to resort to reason so as to end the suffering of the Yemenis, to stop the destruction of the homeland's resources and capabilities, and to allow peace to prevail all over Yemen's territories and thus thwart all the attempts to deepen the tragedy. The nine leaders are: Ahmad Muhammad Nu'man, Jarallah 'Umar, 'Ali Nasir Muhammad, Muhammad 'Abd-al-Rahman al-Ruba'i, Sinan Abu- Lahm, Muhammad 'Abd-al-Quddus [word indistinct], Mujahid [word indistinct], Muhammad 'Abd-al-Malik al-Mutawakkil, and Ahmad 'Ali al-Salami.
