BFN [Text] Congressmen of the ruling [as heard] Socialist Party of Chile have asked Chilean President Eduardo Frei to reestablish diplomatic relations with Cuba, which have been severed for more than 20 years. Deputy Anibal Perez [as heard], who spoke on behalf of those Chilean congressmen, referred to the level of development achieved between the two countries following the resumption of consular and commercial ties in 1991. According to the socialist congressmen, over the past three years and within a climate of rapprochement, Chilean businessmen have invested more than $20 million in Cuba, while 11,000 Chilean tourists traveled to Cuba last year. The Socialist Party of Chile is the main ally of President Frei's ruling Christian Democratic Party.
