910429 FT 29 APR 91 / UK Company News: British Nuclear Fuels improves 17% to Pounds 145m BRITISH Nuclear Fuels returned profits of Pounds 145m pre-tax for the 1989-90 year, an improvement of 17 per cent over the previous year's Pounds 124m. Turnover pushed ahead by 14 per cent to Pounds 1.04bn. Mr Christopher Harding, chairman, said the publication of the company's results had been delayed by the continuing negotiations with its two main UK customers, Nuclear Electric and Scottish Nuclear, over new fixed price contracts for fuel services. These discussions had been held up by the government's decision to keep the nuclear industry out of the electricity privatisation programme. Mr Harding said BNFL's prospects for expansion were mainly in the export business. He pointed to orders worth some Pounds 800m from German utilities for reprocessing fuel at BNFL's thermal oxide reprocessing plant. He said the company was now looking for new business opportunities in eastern and central Europe. 'There is great scope for the application of advanced radioactive waste management and decommissioning techniques.'
