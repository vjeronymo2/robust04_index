Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 24 Mar (MENA)--Dennis Ross, U.S. coordinator for the Middle East peace process, stated upon arrival in Cairo that the conferees meeting to discuss financial aid for the Palestinian police force will discuss all issues relating to this force. He expressed his belief that the talks will proceed very well. He has come to Cairo to join talks, which started here today, on financial aid to be allocated for the Palestinian police force Mr. Ross was late for the meeting because the plane he was flying in from Amman airport was delayed due to technical reasons. In another development, Dr. Usamah al-Baz, first Foreign Ministry under secretary and director of the President's Office for Political Affairs, stated that the agenda of today's talks includes financial aid to be allocated to the Palestinian police force and that the amount of aid will be set according to the scale of the armaments and the size of the force. It is known that 25 countries are taking part in these talks, in addition to the United Nations and the World Bank.
