Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The city of Taejon will establish a sisterhood relationship with Nanjing in southern China, a spokesman for the Taejon City Government said yesterday. A five-member delegation, headed by Wang Haoliang, secretary- general of the Nanjing Municipal People's Government, will arrive in the Korean central city Jan. 17 for a four-day visit at the invitation of Taejon Mayor Yom Hong-chol, he said. During their stay in Taejon, the Chinese delegates will discuss preparations to set up a sisterhood relationship between the two cities and exchange bilateral friendship memorandum. They will also discuss steps to increase personnel exchanges and economic cooperation. They will also visit the city's Taedok & Ginseng Corp. in Taejon, the spokesman said. Nanjing, with a population of 2.47 million, is China's center stage for commercial, financial and trade activities in the southern region, he said. The spokesman said Taejon, in accordance with the nation's recent emphasis on internationalization, is also seeking to set up sisterhood ties with other foreign cities, including Japan's Oda, Russia's Petersburg and Hungary's Budapest.
