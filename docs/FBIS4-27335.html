BFN [Text] Keigo Ouchi, chairman of the Democratic Socialist Party [DSP], delivered a speech in Morioka City on 29 May, in which he commented on the "Kaishin" parliamentary group in the House of Representatives, the formation of which triggered the Social Democratic Party of Japan's secession from the coalition government. He said, "While this group is being criticized, from a long-term point of view, it will prove to be useful (for the realignment of the political world)." He thus stressed the significance of the group's formation. Ouchi held an informal meeting with reporters after the speech. Asked about demands inside the DSP and from labor unions supporting DSP for his resignation as chairman, Ouchi said, "I will have to reach a conclusion before the party convention (to be held on 7 and 8 June)."
