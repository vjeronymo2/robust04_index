921107 FT 07 NOV 92 / London ambulance inquiry ordered AN INQUIRY into manage-ent, computer operations and industrial relations at the London Ambulance Service was ordered yesterday by Mrs Virginia Bottomley, health secretary. It will last three months and be led by Mr Don Page, chief executive of the South Yorkshire Ambulance Service. The inquiry, the report from which will be published, is intended to restore public confidence following the breakdown of the computerised emergency 999 call system. It has been claimed that recent computer failures led to the deaths of some patients. Mrs Bottomley said in the Commons there was a need to 'identify the lessons to be learned'.
