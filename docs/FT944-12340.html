941102 FT 02 NOV 94 / Lottery adverts anger charities Charity fundraisers yesterday lodged a complaint with the Advertising Standards Authority, the advertising watchdog, about advertisements promoting the National Lottery in last Sunday's newspapers. The advertisements say: 'Every time you play the National Lottery, someone else gets a better chance.' They add: 'For every pound that you spend playing the National Lottery, at least a quarter will go to hundreds of worthy causes throughout the UK.' The Institute of Charity Fundraising Managers, the fundraisers' professional body, said it was concerned the advertisements might encourage people to stop donating to charities and switch to buying lottery tickets, which go on sale later this month. Mr Stephen Lee, institute director, said charity fundraisers had long supported the lottery but this support had always been based on the principle that income from the National Lottery would be truly additional to existing levels of support to our sector. Research commissioned by the National Council for Voluntary Organisations had estimated that the lottery could result in an annual loss of charitable income of between Pounds 190m and Pounds 270m. Mr Lee added: 'We were led to believe that the lottery will be promoted primarily as a fun activity, not as a means of supporting charities and voluntary organisations.'
