Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Cartagena de Indias (Colombia), 13 Jan (EFE) -- Police report that some unidentified gunmen shot Colombian Liberal politician Jorge Luis Horta, who is running for the Senate, in Cartagena de Indias on 13 January. Colonel Hector Mario Castro, police commander in Bolivar Department, whose capital is Cartagena, said that the attack took place some time after 1200 in the neighborhood of San Felipe Castle on Pedro de Heredia Avenue. According to Col. Castro, the attackers, who were on a motorcycle, intercepted Horta's vehicle and shot him. However, at that moment, a police patrol came along and prevented the terrorists from accomplishing their objective, which was seemingly to assassinate him. Following a confrontation between the criminals and policemen, the criminals fled while the patrol took Horta to the Cartagena de Indias University Hospital. Horta was shot in the chin, neck, and a hand. Col. Castro stated that the reasons for the attack are unknown, and that although Horta remained conscious while he was being taken to the hospital, his health condition is critical.
