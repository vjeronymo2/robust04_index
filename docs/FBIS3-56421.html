Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Boris Fedorov has put the following conditions to his joining the government as the minister of finance: Aleksandr Zaveryukha should not be the deputy prime minister and the president should not confirm Viktor Geraschenko as the head of the Central Bank. In a brief interview to the journalists on Monday evening [17 January] in the lobbies of the State Duma B. Fedorov said that he had written a letter asking to join the membership of the Banking Subcommittee of the Plans and Budget Committee of the State Duma. According to B. Fedorov he also made a decision to join the Union of December 12 group of deputies headed by the deputy premier of the Moscow government Aleksandr Braginsky.
