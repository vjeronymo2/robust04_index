910502 FT 02 MAY 91 / UK Company News: Starmin makes first profits since 1984 Starmin, the quarry products company formerly known as SI Group, has achieved its first profits since 1984 and has rejoined the dividend list in the year to end-December. The company, run since October 1989 by brothers Osman and Raschid Abdullah, reported taxable profits of Pounds 764,000 for 1990, against a Pounds 1.22m loss in the comparable period on sales up 72 per cent to Pounds 8.8m (Pounds 5.12m). A dividend of 0.1p is recommended for the year on earnings per share of 0.5p (losses of 3.4p). At the year-end shareholders' funds stood at Pounds 13.7m (Pounds 1.72m) and borrowings at Pounds 1.47m represented gearing of 10.7 per cent. The company has focused operations on Scotland and the A1 corridor. The acquisition near the year-end of quarries in Scotland and an option on a Derbyshire site involved the issue of 6.67m shares plus Pounds 250,000 cash.
