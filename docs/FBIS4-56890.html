BFN [Text] The Chilean Foreign Ministry has announced that soon Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina will receive an official invitation to visit Chile in the near future. Jose Miguel Insulsa, deputy secretary of Foreign Relations, made the announcement in Santiago de Chile. Insulsa stated it will be beneficial to conduct an exchange of opinions on international politics and the current situation in Cuba. Insulsa pointed out that the visit's agenda has yet to be finalized, but mentioned that important issues would include the ongoing U.S. economic blockade of Cuba and Cuba's ties with the hemisphere. Insula pointed out that differences in political systems is no obstacle to the existence of diplomatic relations between Cuba and Chile.
