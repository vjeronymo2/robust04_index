941019 FT 19 OCT 94 / Albert Emerick: FT executive - Obituary Albert Emerick, who retired as director of administration of the Financial Times in 1974 after 46 years' service with the newspaper, has died suddenly aged 80. He joined the FT as a messenger in 1928. In 1940 he went into the army, winning the BEM (Military Division) for gallantry and distinguished service as a sergeant with the South Wales Borderers in Burma. Emerick rejoined the FT in 1946 in the accounts department. In 1961, after two years as assistant secretary, he was appointed company secretary. He joined the FT board and became joint general manager in 1966. In 1971 he was appointed director of administration. He was also a director of St Clements Press, the newspaper printing subsidiary of the Financial Times, of Throgmorton Publications, then publisher of the Investors Chronicle and Stock Exchange Gazette, and of Apollo magazine. In his younger days he was a keen cricketer and a stalwart of Sutton Common cricket club - for which he scored a century at the age of 60. He also enjoyed playing cards. He is survived by his wife, May, their three daughters, eight grandchildren, and three great grandchildren.
