BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] The arrest of two suspects in a plot against the Honduran president, the arrest of Commander Saul Alvarez' murderer in Miami, and the drug problem in the country were topics addressed by Commander Fernando Caldera, director of the National Police. Police arrested two individuals suspected of participating in the plot to assassinate Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina. Santiago Rivas Betancourt was arrested in Esteli on 5 April, and Luis Rene Quintero was arrested in Managua on 6 April. [Begin Caldera recording] Because it is a sign of a serious act, we are proceeding to arrest them. The law empowers us to hold them for 72 hours; during that time, we are going to try to determine the facts. [end recording] Another Nicaraguan suspect in the case, which was made public in Honduras on 5 April, remains at large. Honduran authorities have identified him as Roger Arguello. Caldera made these statements when he announced Commander Saul Alvarez' murderer was arrested in Miami last week. Javier or Manuel Velasquez murdered Commander Alvarez on 21 September 1992 during a transportation strike. The murderer illegally entered the United States by going from Managua to Guatemala and Mexico, then to the United States. The police worked in coordination with the FBI and Dade County police to capture Velasquez. [Begin Caldera recording] He is now under the jurisdiction of immigration authorities. The Nicaraguan Government -- it is no longer up to the police -- will make every effort to bring him to Nicaragua so he can be tried in Managua. [end recording] Velasquez left the country 10 days after the shoot-out on the northern highway. On another topic, Commander Caldera said he is unaware of the State Department report which indicated Nicaragua has reportedly not achieved progress with its strategy against drug trafficking. [Being Caldera recording] The Nicaraguan state has been advancing in the antidrug struggle starting from zero. Drug trafficking in Nicaragua is a relatively new crime. [end recording] He described the draft law against drugs as a modern law that will fight crime.
