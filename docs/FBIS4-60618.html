BFN [Text] In the context of expanding political-economic cooperation between the Islamic Republic of Iran and South Africa, the first South African political-economic delegation headed by their Foreign Ministry director general arrived in Tehran for talks on defining ways of cooperation between the two countries. Members of the 13-man delegation, which includes a number of government and private sector officials, this morning attended a seminar on the expansion of economic relations at Iran's Chambers of Trade, Industry, and Mines and announced that they were prepared to expand trade and economic relations with Iran. According to our correspondent: Shayk-ol-elslam [name as heard], our country's deputy foreign minister in charge of Afro-Arab affairs told the meeting the fact that the Islamic Republic of Iran was the first country to reopen its embassy in South Africa after the end of apartheid indicated Iranian interest in cooperation with South Africa in all fields. Referring to oil, industry, and trade as fields for the expansion of economic relations between the two countries, he called on industrialists and businessmen to take into account the common views of the political and economic officials in the two countries and start efforts toward the expansion of cooperation. During their stay in Iran, the South African delegation will meet economic representatives of the government and the private sector as well as industrialists.
