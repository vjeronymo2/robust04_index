BFN [Text] Cairo, May 17 (XINHUA) -- U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher today pledged international support for the fledgling Palestinian self-rule in the West Bank area of Jericho and the Gaza Strip while visiting the self-rule area, reports reaching here said. Christopher, the first foreign VIP to tour the self-rule area since Palestinian police took over control, said that the U.S. had pledged 500 million dollars in aid to the autonomy government over the next five years, and had started to equip Palestinian police with 200 U.S. Army vehicles apart from helping rehabilitate hospitals and schools there. "Much remains to be done and one of the reasons that I came here today is to make it clear to our Palestinian friends that they have supporters," he said amid tight security. "I must say that it is a tremendous thrill to see the Palestinian police patrolling, to see the joint patrols, to see the administrators begin to take over," the U.S. official told reporters at the Jericho Police Station, which the Palestinian police took over Friday [13 May]. Faisal Husseini [name as received], one of the members of the Palestinian governing authority, expressed welcome to Christopher's visit to Jericho, saying it is the first step toward building a new Palestine. Also today, Israel formally handed over the government of the Gaza Strip to the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO), although Israeli soldiers still remain in parts of the strip to be turned over to the Palestinians. Zakaria al-Agha [name as received], a member of the Palestinian authority that will govern the strip and Jericho, and Israeli Major General Dov Gazit signed a two-page document to accomplish the transfer of power. No ceremony was held for the takeover. A similar handover of power in Jericho took place Friday. Under the accord signed in Cairo two weeks ago, Israel is to pull out of Jericho and Gaza, handing the areas over to Palestinian self-rule. Christopher, who is shuttling between Israel and Syria in an effort to promote a peace treaty, met later today with Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin and Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres on his talks with Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad.
