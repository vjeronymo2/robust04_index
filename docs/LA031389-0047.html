March 13, 1989, Monday, San Diego County Edition

SAN DIEGO SPORTS ET CETERA: AZTECS, LEFT OUT OF NCAA EVENT, HEAD FOR TEXAS

Not selected as one of the 48 teams in the NCAA tournament, the San Diego State women's basketball team was chosen Sunday as the No. 1-seeded team in the 21st annual eight-team women's National Invitational Tournament in Amarillo, Tex., March 23-25.

"We thought perhaps that we should have been one of the teams in the NCAA, but the committee didn't feel that way," SDSU Coach Earnest Riggins said.

"We feel that we're a better team than we showed in the last six or seven ballgames. Certainly, our overall record (23-8) was good enough but losing five of our last seven hurt us."

The Aztecs will take on eighth-seeded Radford (23-6) at 1 p.m. March 21 in the first round of the single-elimination tournament. The top-seeded team has been defeated in the first round three consecutive years.

DePaul (22-8), the fourth-seeded team, is the first defending champion to play in the tournament in 16 years. JIM LINDGREN The San Diego County boys' and girls' high school basketball all-star games will be played at Serra High April 8. The boys' game at 7:30 p.m. will feature the Class 3-A, coached by Sweetwater's Dave Ybarra, against the 2-A, to be coached by Kearny's Bill Petersen. The county girls' team, coached by Vista's Joe DeMaria, will take on the city girls' team with Mission Bay's Larry Willis as coach. Teams will be selected later this week.BASEBALL San Diego State managed just two ninth-inning runs as the Aztecs lost, 7-2, to Pepperdine in a nonconference game at Smith Field. Danny Brown went two for three and Anthony Johnson two for five with an RBI for SDSU (11-8), which had only six hits. Pepperdine (12-8-1) jumped to a 4-0 lead in the first inning and added a run in the fourth and two in the seventh off six Aztec pitchers.SOFTBALL U.S. International University split 1-0 games in a home doubleheader with UC Santa Barbara. The Gauchos (9-9) scored in the first inning of the first game. USIU (12-7-1) scored in the first inning in the second. The Gulls' Nora Flores (5-3) pitched her third shutout of the season in the second game.MEN'S TENNIS San Diego State (5-10 overall, 2-1 in the Western Athletic Conference) swept the singles and won two of three doubles matches in defeating New Mexico, 8-1, at SDSU. In No. 1 and No. 2 singles, Tole Marincovic (6-4, 1-6, 7-5) and Joe McDonough (1-6, 6-3, 7-6) won in close matches.WOMEN'S TENNIS San Diego State defeated Princeton, 9-0, in a nonconference match at SDSU. Sondra Mitchell, Kristin Hill and Dorey Brandt won their matches. No. 18 SDSU improved to 6-5. Toreras Jennifer Larking, Aby Brayton, Tonya Fuller, Sakolwan Kacharoen, Christine Schmeidel and Christy Drage won their singles matches. USD is 11-4. MEN'S CREW A good showing against UC Santa Barbara on Mission Bay gives the young UC San Diego team confidence going into the San Diego Crew Classic April 1-2. UCSD defeated the Gauchos in novice lightweight and novice B boat. The Tritons also defeated Santa Barbara Community College in men's varsity fours.
