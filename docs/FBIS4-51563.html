BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 28 KYODO -- The government's new chief economic planner said Thursday [28 April] business is still generally in a bad state despite some bright spots. Yoshio Terasawa, director general of the Economic Planning Agency, made the comment at a news conference after the first meeting of the new cabinet led by Tsutomu Hata. On a proposed tax reform, he indicated the need for further administrative streamlining and further deregulation prior to any raise in the consumption tax.
