Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Andreatta Meets Mahmun al-Qurdi"] [Text] Rome, 25 Jan (ANSA) -- Foreign Minister Beniamino Andreatta today received the Saudi Foreign Affairs under secretary Mahmun al-Qurdi, who is in Rome to take part in a meeting of the joint Italo-Saudi Commission. The commission's previous meeting (the fourth in the series) took place in Riyadh from January 30 to February 1 of last year. The two men today first reviewed economic matters of common interest. Andreatta and al-Qurdi agreed on the advisability of stepping up initiatives aimed a bolstering trade and industrial cooperation between the two countries. Recalling the Italian government privatization plan outlined to the Saudis when Treasury Minister Piero Barucci travelled to Saudi Arabia in January of 1993, Andreatta plumped for Saudi investments in share companies being formed from the former public ones. The two men also discussed international matters and especially the situation in the Mediterranean and in Bosnia. The previous political contact between the two countries took place in November of last year when Saudi Foreign Minister Sa'ud al-Faysal al Sa'ud came to Rome, the Farnesina [Foreign Ministry] noted.
