BFN [Text] As reported to ETA by the Estonian Justice Ministry, a 12-strong delegation of the Swedish Justice Ministry is visiting Tallinn today. According to Imbi Markus, the head of the ministry's foreign relations department, the delegation consists mainly of (?clerical) staff and financial and administrative managers. According to Markus, this visit is linked to preparations for a meeting to map out an extensive cooperation project between the two ministries. Today, a basic agreement on cooperation between Estonian and Swedish postal services was signed. This will be followed by the conclusion of a string of agreements on specific areas. There are plans to reform the Estonian system of postal codes. Cooperation will continue with the issue of postage stamps and the training of staff.
