921216 FT 16 DEC 92 / Observer: Currency quiz A cry for help from Goldman Sachs no less. David Morrison, the company's chief international economist, is worried that no one is going to be able to solve his Christmas quiz and thereby win two jeroboams of champagne. These are the questions: Which currency would President-elect Clinton be least likely to draw on if reminiscing on his days at Oxford? Which eastern currency detached itself in early November and would prove useful in attempting to win the football pools but, ironically, not in France? In response to which event this year did a European foreign minister declare: 'If you can't join them, beat them?' In the event of a tie, the overall winner will be the producer of the best completed limerick, the first two lines of which are as follows: 'There once was an old central banker, whose currency filled him with rancour. . . ' Answers to Morrison by tomorrow night. Telephone 071-774 1000; fax 774 1181.
