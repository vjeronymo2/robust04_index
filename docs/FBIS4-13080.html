BFN [Text] The signing of the GATT by India at Marrakesh has evoked mixed reaction. The former prime minister and the Samajwadi Janata Party leader, Mr. Chandra Shekhar, has criticized the signing of the GATT adopting Dunkel proposals by India. Addressing public meetings at Chandigarh, Ludhiana and Amritsar yesterday, Mr. Chandra Shekhar said these proposals will badly affect and erode the Indian values swadeshi [indigenousness] and self-sufficiency as propounded by Mahatama Gandhi and other leaders. Reacting to the signing of GATT by the government, the Uttar Pradesh chief minister, Mulayam Singh Yadav, said the people should have been taken into confidence before signing it. He said the chief ministers should have been consulted before signing the agreement which would affect the national interests. The Bihar chief minister, Mr. Lalu Prasad Yadav, addressing a public meeting at Sopol, charged the Congress-I with selling out the country's sovereignty by signing the agreement. The BJP [Bharatiya Janata Party] yesterday observed a protest day in Madhya Pradesh against the signing of GATT, while the Communist Party of India-Marxist observed the black day in the state. Janata Dal workers held demonstrations in some towns of Uttar Pradesh. However, the Congress-I has welcomed the signing of agreement by India and charged the Opposition with indulging in a disinformation campaign on the issue. Speaking to reporters in New Delhi yesterday, the party spokesman, Mr. V.N. Gadgil, said the agreement will boost India's agricultural and textile exports.
