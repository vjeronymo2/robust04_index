940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / Arts: Today's Television In Nature Detectives professional camera crews visit some of Britain's natural history enthusiasts. Today they include David Mossman and Christine Wagner who swim with grey seals in the North Sea; Derek and Pauline Harrison who attempt at their Lake District hotel to feed red squirrels and not grey; and businessman Mike Warburg who breeds great crested newts in Chorleywood (7.00 BBC1). BBC2 investigates the Indian passion for cricket in A Whole Different Ball Game and shows that the game affects and is affected by all aspects of life: political, religious and social (7.40 BBC2). Subject of the last in BBC2's Heretic series, looking at men whose radical ideas have led to their being ostracised, is Eric Laithwaite, former Professor of Electrical Engineering at Imperial College. His heresy is a belief in gyroscopic propulsion which appears to challenge Newton's laws. Last month he patented a new device which he believes will make his name (10.00). Billy Connolly's World Tour Of Scotland splendidly combines stage act and fond travelogue (10.25 BBC1).
