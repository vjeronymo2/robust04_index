BFN [Text] An agreement on the Kazakh-Chinese border, stretching 1700 kilometers, is the main result of the Kazakh-Chinese talks, Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev told a press conference on Tuesday. He said that none of the former Soviet republics bordering on China had concluded such an agreement. He also believes that this document reflects Kazakhstan's and China's determination to maintain stable, friendly relations. Nazarbayev pointed out that the positions of the two states' leaders on the political situation in Asia were the same. Both parties stated that the agreement between the Kazakh Transportation Ministry and the Chinese Railways Ministry lifted all limitations from the transit of cargo and passengers between China and Kazakhstan. Nazarbayev announced that the agreement on the extension of a 50 million yuan credit will be used to broaden bilateral and invigorate border trade, to reconstruct the Karaganda steel mill and build enterprises putting out consumer goods.
