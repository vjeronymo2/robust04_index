BFN [Text] Cairo, 3 May (MENA)--President Husni Mubarak received a message from Russian President Boris Yeltsin. Following his audience with Mubarak today, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev also said that the message invites President Mubarak to visit Moscow soon. He added that he believes the visit will be made soon. Kozyrev said that President Mubarak has a broad and unique view of the region and its problems. He praised the excellent relations between Presidents Mubarak and Yeltsin and added that he congratulated President Mubarak on his birthday tomorrow, which coincides with a historic event, namely, the signing of the Palestinian-Israeli Agreement. He said Russia is looking forward to this day and this celebration, which will crown the strenuous efforts exerted to solve this difficult problem. He went on to say that President Mubarak has declared Egypt's commitment to continue its peace process efforts. He added that Russia too is committed as a cosponsor of the Middle East peace efforts on the other tracks. He stressed in this regard that Russia is committed to every track and that it maintains constant contact with Damascus. He said that Russia is trying to bring the Syrian and Israeli viewpoints closer and their outcome will appear later. The Russian foreign minister stressed that his country supports the Palestinian issue politically and diplomatically. He noted that Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat was in Moscow two weeks ago and held detailed talks with the Russian leadership. He said: We will help the Palestinians with some technical means to enable them to deal with whatever problems they might face in the stage after next, such as problems relating to borders.
