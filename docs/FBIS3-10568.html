Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] DPRK Vice President Kim Pyong Sik, who is the chairman of the North Korean Social Democratic Party's Central Committee, is paying an unofficial visit to Peru. He yesterday attended a ceremony marking the 94th anniversary of the birth of Dr. Luciano Castillo Colonna, the founder of the Peruvian Socialist Party. The ceremony was attended by DPRK Ambassador to Peru Kim Gyong Jo, by Dr. Maria Cabredo de Castillo, and by other Socialist Party members. The DPRK diplomat said Kim Pyong has been in Peru for some days to exchange views with representatives of various political parties, among them are the New Majority-Change 90, the Popular Action, and the APRA [American Popular Revolutionary Alliance]. He said that during his stay in Lima, the visitor met with First Vice President of Congress Carlos Torres y Torres Lara. The DPRK vice president will leave for Ecuador and other South American countries.
