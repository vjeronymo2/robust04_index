920513 FT 13 MAY 92 / Parliament and Politics: Caution by PM on Rio summit MR JOHN MAJOR, the prime minister, yesterday sought to play down expectations of what might be achieved at the forthcoming Earth Summit in Brazil next month, saying that there was 'bound to be a limited amount of success' at the conference. 'What is agreed at that conference is only the beginning of the work that needs to be done,' he said in reply to a question from Mr Simon Hughes, the Liberal Democrat environment spokesman. 'There will be a good deal to be done subsequently both in this country, the EC and beyond.' Mr Major will attend the meeting with other leaders including President George Bush of the US, who had previously said he had not decided whether he would attend. Mr Hughes said earlier that proposals to be put at the EC Commission today were 'a watering down of tough energy-saving commitments.'
