941007 FT 07 OCT 94 / Union resolves dispute with BT British Telecommunications and its main union, the NCU communications union, have finally reached agreement on a more flexible system of working time, David Goodhart writes. The system will mean more BT employees are available in the evenings and at weekends. It has been the subject of tense negotiations especially over whether employees would be compelled to work new shifts. Union members have now voted heavily in favour of the changes, having rejected them equally heavily a few weeks ago. About 14,000 union members responded to the ballot out of a possible 26,900. Just over 10,000 voted in favour and just over 4,000 voted against. In a joint statement last night BT and the NCU said: 'We are now committed jointly to ensuring the agreement is implemented in a quality way, and is honoured in both letter and intent.'
