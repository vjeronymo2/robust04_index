930407 FT 07 APR 93 / China 'trying to rein in growth' CHINA'S leaders are trying to slow the breakneck pace of their economy but it is unclear whether they can keep it from running out of control, the president of the World Bank said yesterday, Reuter reports from Beijing. 'Certainly they are very concerned about the risks of boom and bust,' Mr Lewis Preston told the press. 'We suspect, and the leadership we have spoken to confirms, that the economy is growing at a rate that at least equals the figures last year of 12.8 (per cent).' 'But I think,' he continued, 'there is an indication that through various macroeconomic policies, (China's leadership) would like to see that growth slow down to something closer to 8 per cent and thereby avoid the disruptions of stop-and-go.' Asked if he thought China could break out of a pattern that has seen the government clamp down on runaway inflation triggered by periods of high growth, Mr Preston said: 'We'll see.' He said that he thought that China's economy could sustain a growth rate of between 7.5 and 8 per cent. 'The need to sustain growth within the bounds of what the physical and institutional structures can handle is paramount in their minds,' Mr Preston said after meeting top officials, including China's president, premier and central bank governor. 'The need to do so without creating a stop-go cycle and retain the confidence of domestic and foreign investors is of considerable concern to them.'
