BFN [Text] Agriculture Minister Ya'aqov Tzur has decided to delay the opening of Israel's borders for free passage of agricultural produce from the autonomy until satisfactory compensation is provided to Israeli farmers. According to the agreement, the borders were to open this week for free passage of fruit, vegetables, cattle, and flocks -- apart from poultry -- eggs, tomatoes, cucumbers, and potatoes, which will gradually be allowed in under quotas. The Ministry of Agriculture is to approve the import of vegetables from the Gaza Strip into Israel in a few days time. Tests show there is no danger in the vegetables and the farmers there do not use pesticides. Agriculture Minister Ya'aqov Tzur is to instruct the ministry to publish all information concerning laboratory tests on agricultural produce as of tomorrow. Recently, tests of fruit and vegetables showed the remnants of dangerous pesticides have been found in several Israeli agricultural products. The contaminated produce was destroyed and the farmers were fined.
