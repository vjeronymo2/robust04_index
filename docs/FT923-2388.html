920918 FT 18 SEP 92 / World News In Brief: Mauroy to head Socialist International Former French prime minister Pierre Mauroy (above) became president of the Socialist International in succession to Willy Brandt who held the job for 16 years. Mauroy, 64, was the only candidate for the post which was decided at a Berlin conference of the organisation. Brandt, 79, a former West German chancellor, is seriously ill and could not attend the meeting.
