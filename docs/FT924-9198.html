921106 FT 06 NOV 92 / International Company News: Echo Bay shows loss of Dollars 4.3m ECHO BAY MINES, faced with lower gold prices, suffered a third-quarter loss of USDollars 4.3m, or 4 cents a share, against a profit of USDollars 2.3m, or 2 cents, a year earlier. Revenues were Dollars 75.2m, against Dollars 79.1m. Average realised price for the quarter was Dollars 361 per oz, down Dollars 30 from a year earlier. Echo Bay, based in Edmonton, operates four gold mines in Canada and the US. Production in the latest quarter was 183,320 oz, against 178,385 oz, and cash cost was Dollars 241 per oz, against Dollars 243 per oz. Debt was reduced by Dollars 136m through the sale of a new convertible preferred issue. Production was higher at the McCoy/Cove mine in Nevada, but lower at the Lupin mine in the Northwest Territories. The Round Mountain, in Nevada, produced 8 per cent more gold. Output at Kettle River, in Washington State, was stable. Nine months' loss was USDollars 6.5m, or 6 cents a share, against a profit of USDollars 11.4m, or 11 cents. Revenues were Dollars 232m, compared with Dollars 241m.
