BFN [Text] Mashad, Khorasan Prov., May 1, IRNA -- Some 300,000 Afghan refugees residing in Iran will return home this year under a program worked out by a commission consisting of representatives of Iran, Afghanistan and the U.N. High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR). According to the director general in charge of foreign nationals and immigration affairs at the Interior Ministry Ahmad Husseini, the repatriation of the refugees is to start from June 22. Husseini recently visited the Afghan province of Harat along with other members of the commission to inspect the status of Afghan refugees who have returned home. He told reporters here Saturday that the outcome of a plan concerning the repatriation of 850,000 refugees last year and their integration into Afghan society was satisfactory. He emphasized that the Afghan refugees who have returned home have played a key role in the reconstruction of their country mainly due to their theoretical and practical skills. In response to the request of the Islamic Government of Afghanistan, Iran has issued six month long residence permits for some 540,000 refugees who are living in Iran without having identification cards, Husseini added. He stressed that during his stay in Harat he announced the Interior Ministry's readiness to set up camps inside Afghan territory for accomodation of refugees in emergency cases.
