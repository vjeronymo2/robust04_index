September 26, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

THE SIDELINES;

NO MORE RIZZUTO? HOLY COW!

"Like General MacArthur, I shall return," New York Yankees broadcaster Phil Rizzuto said.

But it was unclear after Tuesday's game between the Yankees and the Baltimore Orioles whether Rizzuto, who often shouts "Holy Cow" at crucial moments of games, would be back at the microphone.

Rizzuto, who celebrated his 73rd birthday Tuesday, has been in the broadcast booth for 34 years, but that may end after this season if WPIX-TV is unable to purchase the rights to broadcast any of the Yankees' 1991 games from the Madison Square Garden cable network.

"I have a terrible feeling right now," said Rizzuto, known as "Scooter," in a story in today's editions of the New York Post. "I'm not an emotional guy, but today, thinking about all of this, I had tears in my eyes.

"Fifty years have gone by and I can't believe there's a chance I won't be coming here any more," said Rizzuto, who joined the Yankees as a shortstop in 1941. "It's kind of frightening."
