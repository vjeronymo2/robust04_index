920428 FT 28 APR 92 / Happier staff with profits STAFF at the most profitable UK companies are significantly more satisfied with their jobs than staff at less profitable companies. The finding, part of a survey of 140,000 staff, conducted by International Survey Research (ISR) at 55 companies, used 10 standard measures of satisfaction. Staff at the most profitable companies were more satisfied on every count. ISR believes staff satisfaction is necessary for profitability. Mrs Rhiannon Chapman, director of the Industrial Society, the independent body that promotes harmony at work, said: 'Staff feel more directly involved in the business and therefore more able to contribute when they know how their area of work affects profits. It may be that the most profitable companies are more willing to give out that kind of information.' Dr Adrian Furnham, a reader in psychology at London University, said the survey did not show that staff satisfaction caused profits. 'It may be, however, that the most profitable companies also have the best selection procedures and select out the neurotics. There is a clear connection between neurosis and work dissatisfaction.'
