Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Jaroslav Kmenta report: "Iran Has Apparently Manifested an Interest in Products From Czech Arms Factories"] [Text] Prague -- Representatives from Skoda Plzen, which is part of the RDP Group arms consortium, held talks during their visit to Iran in autumn with officials from the Iranian Army at the country's Defense Ministry. A diplomatic source informed MLADA FRONTA DNES of this yesterday. He added that this was supposed to have been an informative meeting concerning possible cooperation in the arms industry. Skoda Director General Lubomir Soudek has not denied this report; at the same time, however, he has not confirmed it. "I am not saying yes or no. I do not think it is anyone's business what the talks were about, how extensive they were, or with whom they were held. When the talks assume a specific form, we will proceed in accordance with the laws," he said. The Iranian regime is described by some countries -- for example, by Israel -- as a terrorist regime. Lubomir Soudek denied contacting the Iranians to offer them surface-to-air missiles or the Tamara passive radar system. According to the most recent issue of the weekly RESPEKT, there exists an internal memorandum by Lubomir Soudek from the trip to Iran in which he writes about a meeting at the Defense Ministry and about talks with the army. According to this document, the Skoda director was, for example, supposed to have briefed Iranian Deputy Defense Minister Vaheed on the RDP Group, the Czech company involved in the production and sale of weapons. The Iranians were supposed to have shown an interest in the new generation of surface-to-air missiles, the Tamara radar system, and modernized tanks. Soudek has confirmed that a memorandum from his visit to Iran exists; nevertheless, he said that it is not available to reporters. The Skoda director told us the following in connection with the talks on the Russian T-72 tank, in whose modernization the plant will participate: "So far, we have not modernized a single tank; we have nothing to offer. However, if Iran is on the list of countries to which the export of special materials is prohibited, of course, we will not consider exporting there." The Foreign Ministry, according to our source, will ask Lubomir Soudek for an official report on his company's activities. Czech diplomats do not know anything about representatives from Skoda Plzen holding talks with army officials in Iran. According to our source, the ambassador of the Czech Republic to Iran was only informed of the Skoda delegation's visit to the Agriculture and Energy Ministries. "If Skoda Plzen negotiated in Iran as it was alleged to have done, then after 1 March -- when the law on arms trading should come into force -- it would be possible to regard such activity as an offense," said our diplomatic source, without giving any details. Lubomir Soudek has emphasized that he held talks in Iran on -- for example -- plans for a joint enterprise in the sugar refining industry and on the founding of a company to manufacture Skoda trolley buses for the Teheran public transportation system. "If some people want to turn the business trip into a scandal, then they should not be surprised if the number of unemployed increases," declared Soudek.
