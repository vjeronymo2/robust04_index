BFN [Text] Beijing, June 13 (XINHUA) -- Liu Huaqing, vice-chairman of China's Central Military Commission, said here today that the parties concerned should try every means to resort to dialogue for settling the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. During a meeting with Choe Kwang, chief of the General Staff of the Korean People's Army, and his party this morning at the Diaoyutai State Guesthouse, Liu said that on the Korean peninsula nuclear issue, it is China's basic goal to promote denuclearization and maintain peace and stability in the region. The people and armies of China and the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) enjoyed profound friendship, Liu said, and such valuable friendship should be passed on from generation to generation. He said he believed that the delegation's current visit to China would be helpful to promoting mutual understanding and friendship. Choe, expressing his happiness to meet Liu again five years after their first meeting during Liu's DPRK visit, said he appreciated China's effort in developing friendship between DPRK and China. During the meeting, both army leaders expressed the hope that there would be more military exchanges between the two armies. Besides Beijing, the delegation, which arrived here on June 6 at the invitation of Zhang Wannian, chief of the General Staff of the Chinese People's Liberation Army, has visited Shanghai and Hangzhou.
