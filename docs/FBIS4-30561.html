BFN [Text] Foreign Affairs Minister Gareth Evans says it would be irresponsible if Australia rushed into making a decision on whether to send troops to Rwanda as part of a United Nations peacekeeping mission. The government has deferred a decision and instead has sent a five-person reconnaissance team to assess the situation in the strife-torn central African nation. Senator Evans says this is not an unreasonable response to what could be a very risky endeavor. [Begin Evans recording] The situation is simply so complex, so messy, so uncertain, that I think we would be irresponsible if we made any firmer decision than that before knowing how it's going to pan out. [end recording]
