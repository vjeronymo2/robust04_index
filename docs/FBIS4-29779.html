BFN [Text] The president and Mrs. Suharto this morning welcomed the Cambodian state guests, namely First Prime Minister Norodom Ranariddh and Second Prime Minister Hun Sen, at a military ceremony with a 19-gun salute and the national anthems of the two countries. After the official ceremony held at the Merdeka Palace grounds, President Suharto introduced the two Cambodian leaders to the Sixth Development Cabinet ministers and other high-ranking Indonesian officials. Shortly after seeing the state guests off to the State Guest House, Minister-State Secretary Murdiono briefed reporters on the significance of the Cambodian guests' visit to Indonesia. [Begin Murdiono recording] Cambodian First Prime Minister Prince Ranariddh, who spoke more than the other guests, expressed his gratitude for the invitation to visit Indonesia and also for Indonesia's role in supporting Cambodia's peace process, which led to the establishment of a Cambodia based on the new Constitution. Prince Ranariddh also said Indonesia was a true friend of the Cambodian people and expressed hope that the current visit would further strengthen existing traditional ties between the two countries. [end recording] Also present at the ceremony this morning were the vice president and Mrs. Sutrisno as well as members of the diplomatic corps. The Cambodian state guests are scheduled to lay a wreath at Jakarta's Kalibata heroes cemetery this afternoon.
