930724 FT 24 JUL 93 / Economic Diary TOMORROW: Lord Owen and Mr Thorvald Stoltenberg expected to attend talks in Geneva about the future of Bosnia. MONDAY: Judicial review of the government's planned ratification of the Maastricht Treaty. Mr Francois Mitterrand, French president, and Mr Edouard Balladur, French prime minister, attend annual Franco-British summit meeting in London. Annual Asean post-ministerial conference opens in Singapore (until Wednesday.) TUESDAY: Confederation of British Industry publishes industrial trends survey (third quarter). US consumer confidence (July). Interim figures from Reuters Holdings. House of Commons rises for summer recess (until Monday October 18). WEDNESDAY: Mortgage possession statistics (second quarter) issued by the Lord Chancellor's Department. Mortgage arrears and possessions (January-June) from the Council of Mortgage Lenders and bricks and cement production and deliveries (second quarter) from the Department of the Environment. US durable goods (June). Euromonitor launches market report on the UK books industry. Interim figures from BT and BAT Industries. THURSDAY: By-election in the Conservative seat of Christchurch after the death of MP Robert Adley on May 13. Digest of UK energy statistics 1993 from the Department of Trade and Industry; new vehicle registrations from the Department of Transport and energy trends (April) from the Department of Trade and Industry. US jobless claims; gross domestic product (second quarter 1993). Bundesbank central council meets. Launch of English Tourist Board annual report. Interim statements from ICI, Zeneca and Harrisons & Crosfield. FRIDAY: Central Statistical Office gives figures for economic trends (July) and monthly digest of statistics (July). US personal income (June); new home sales (June). Interim results from Lloyds Bank and First National Finance.
