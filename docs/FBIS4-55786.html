BFN [Text] Tirana May 20 (ATA) -- The parliament of The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia [FYROM], approved the Law on the Census of Population, after a long debate and also in the presence of the experts of the Council of Europe, reports the Albanian TV correspondent, from Skopje. The census will be conducted from June 21 to July 1st of this year. The commission of the Council of Europe that is staying in Skopje, met with the Albanian representatives and the VMRO [Internal Macedonian Revolutionary Organization] group, regarding the draft law on population census, before the holding the parliament's session. The content of the talks is not yet known but the chairman of the experts' team, Vener Hog [name as received], declared that they support the draft law and their is no room for any change in it. The law, said he, is a compromising setting, where each side wins something, but none of them all. The Albanians declared that they support the general census of population only under the international supervision.
