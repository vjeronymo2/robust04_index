Language: Slovene Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Foreign Minister Lojze Peterle at today's news conference spoke about some most important events of the last few days and about the meeting of the foreign ministers of the Central European Initiative, which will start in Trieste today. Igor Juric reports: [Begin Juric recording] Minister Peterle first expressed satisfaction that NATO had accepted the Slovene request for integration into the Partnership for Peace. Slovenia will sign the agreement as the first among the countries that did not have the right to automatic membership in the agreement. Minister Peterle and Prime Minister Drnovsek are expected to sign a document on cooperation between Slovenia and NATO at NATO Headquarters on 14 or 15 March. In the continuation of the conference, Minister Peterle hailed the signing of the agreement between the Croats and the Muslims in Washington. He said that he had received a letter from U.S. Secretary of State Christopher yesterday, who thanked our country for assistance in the resolution of the crisis in the Balkans and informed us about the end of the negotiations. Minister Peterle sent a reply to him today. With the Hungarian ambassador, Lojze Peterle exchanged a ratifying charter on friendly cooperation between the two countries.
