BFN [Text] Caracas, 27 Jun (EFE) -- Venezuela will not accept impositions from the United States regarding Haitian refugees, Foreign Minister Miguel Angel Burelli Rivas stated in remarks reported today. The foreign minister stressed that reports that the U.S. Government is forcing Venezuela to accept Haitian refugees are false. "Venezuela is a sovereign country and does not accept impositions from anybody," Burelli Rivas noted, although he admitted his government may accept some Haitians for a brief period. He added that the Rafael Caldera government shares the concern regarding Haiti but added Venezuela has a foreign immigrants problem, and for that reason cannot accept any more. Burelli Rivas added, however, that since Haiti is involved and that Venezuela is committed to searching for solutions so that democracy can return to that country, some Haitians may be allowed in for a brief period. "That was the response I gave to the U.S. Department of State," he indicated. The top official said no Haitians have come to the Venezuelan Embassy in Port-au-Prince and added that the United States has two ships off the Jamaican coast to pick up Haitians and that only six out of 35 who requested asylum were accepted.
