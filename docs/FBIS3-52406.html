Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuwait, 4 Jan 1993, (KUNA) -- A responsible source at the public relations department of the Interior Ministry denied today the statements made by a local newspaper, according to which the ministry would not allow nationals of the adversary countries to enter the country, even if relations with these countries were resumed. In a statement, a copy of which was obtained by KUNA, the source added that the Interior Ministry granted residence permits to a large number of Arab nationals including subjects from the adversary states. It also added that the statements made by that paper were absolutely untrue. The source also denied that any official had made a statement in this regard and pointed out that those nationals enjoyed all the rights and privileges that other people coming to Kuwait have in accordance with the laws and regulations in force in the country. The source concluded its statement by stressing that despite the continuation of severance of relations with those states, the concerned bodies at the Interior Ministry were carrying out all dealings concerning those nationals without any exceptional measures. Last Sunday, a local paper published a statement it attributed to "a responsible source at the Interior Ministry" according to which the ministry would not allow the nationals of adversary countries to enter the country even if diplomatic relations were resumed with those countries.
