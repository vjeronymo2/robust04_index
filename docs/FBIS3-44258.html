Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The PAC [Pan-Africanist Congress] and the IFP [Inkatha Freedom Party] had the media guessing today when they called an unexpected joint media conference at the PAC offices in Johannesburg. But according to the leaders of the two organizations, today's meeting was in line with ongoing talks between the two groups. [Begin recording] [Reporter Nan Roux] The ongoing violence and the rejection of a one-ballot system for the coming elections. These were the issues discussed at a meeting between IFP leader Mangosuthu Buthelezi and PAC President Clarence Makwetu. The two leaders said such a system was undemocratic and would rob voters of their freedom to choose parties at both the provincial and national level. [Makwetu] If we talk of democracy, if I vote on a provincial level, and I want to vote differently on a national level I should have liberty to do so. It should not be decided by the bureaucrat where my vote should lie. [Roux] Mr. Makwetu said the PAC and the IFP had already contacted the Democratic Party, the Dikwankwetla Party, and others who favor the two-ballot system, in order to work out a joint strategy. On the issues of violence, both leaders committed themselves to addressing the situation as soon as possible for fear that it might jeopardize the entire election process. [Makwetu] I support a move by the people directly involved in the violence sitting together, trying to resolve their differences. [Roux] Mr. Makwetu said joint activity between the PAC, the IFP, and the ANC, was the only solution. [end recording]
