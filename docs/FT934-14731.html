931015 FT 15 OCT 93 / Ex-MoD Tory calls for Ulster troops cut A FORMER Tory minister called last night for a cut in the number of British troops in Northern Ireland. His comments threatened to weaken the Conservative party's recently cemented understanding with the Ulster unionists, David Owen writes. Mr Archie Hamilton, the former armed forces minister, said British forces in the province could be cut by a battalion - equivalent to about 600 men - every year. Maintaining the UK's commitment at present levels was limiting the government's ability to deploy troops elsewhere, he told a Defence Institute meeting in London. His remarks were opposed by Ulster unionist MPs, with Mr Ken Maginnis, Official Ulster Unionist party security spokesman, saying they were an admission of Mr Hamilton's 'incompetence'.
