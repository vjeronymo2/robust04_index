October 14, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

BOYARSKY ON L.A. COUNTY

Having known Bill Boyarsky for a good many years, I was disappointed in his first effort as columnist in the Metro section ("Government by Five Soloists," Sept. 29). His column read as though he has been in hibernation for perhaps 8 or 9 years.

The balance of power on the Board of Supervisors has moved among the members according to each particular issue. For example, the debate over contracting out county services was settle in 1978 when nearly two-thirds of the voters of Los Angeles County voted in support of this kind of government economy and efficiency. Supervisors Deane Dana, Pete Schabarum and I supported the voters' decision. It was opposed by Supervisors Kenny Hahn and Ed Edelman.

However, it was with the support of Dana and Edelman that I was able to push forward with the construction of the new Olive View Medical Center. Schabarum opposed this critically needed facility.

Dana, Schabarum and I opposed the distribution of bleach kits and so-called clean needles in the fight against AIDS because we believe money is better spent on drug education for our young people and in helping get addicts off of drugs.

Regarding my interests in promoting healthy diets, the board acted unanimously in 1981 with an order that improved the type of foods served in our probation facilities.

The board has seen various alliances form and dissolve from time to time, and this accrues to the benefit of all our citizens. Admittedly, we are five very different individuals. At the same time, each of us was elected by a different constituency and each of us has particular obligations to our district.

But the record would bear out the fact that we are extremely successful in attaining a high degree of teamwork. For your information (and Boyarsky's), the board is unanimous on approximately 95% of each weekly calendar.

MICHAEL D. ANTONOVICH

Supervisor, Fifth District
