Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] More than $1.4 billion in foreign funds came into the country as portfolio investments in 1993. Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas [Central Bank of the Philippines] (BSP) statistics showed that bulk of the foreign capital was lodged in the equities markets. This capital placements fuelled the bull run in the domestic stock market in 1993, particularly during the later months. Out of the $1 429 billion, about $808.632 million or 56.59 percent, was invested in stocks, mostly of commercial and industrial listed companies. The balance was invested in banks, which included registered bank deposits of non-residents, government securities paper, utility and investment and finance firms. Of the commercial and industrial classifications, a sizable amount, around $468.795 million, went to buy shares of listed telecommunications companies. This amount push the shares of Philippines Long Distance Co. last year as the highest gainer in the stock market. [passage omitted] BSP said that these foreign funds lodged in the local bourses put the country in the international map as among the emerging stock markets in the region. As has been reported, the country's stock market registered the best ever performance in the recent years with a record high of 150 percent growth in 1993. Trading in both bourses were heavy particularly during the last days of December, posting at one time the highest turnover of P4.26 billion [Philippine pesos].
