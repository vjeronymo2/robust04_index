BFN [Text] This weekend, generator unit no. 3 of the Carlos Manuel de Cespedes Electric Plant will go on a 35-day maintenance program in order to reestablish its full capability of 158 megawatts per hour. Electric maintenance and Construction Ministry workers have the necessary resources to conduct the maintenance with the required quality and complete it ahead of schedule. The plant workers are also working to reactivate the 30 megawatts per hour generator unit no. 1, which has been inoperative for three years. Their goal is to make it operational as soon as possible. On 27 June, workers will also begin an 80-day repair program on the five megawatts per hour unit at the Dionisio San Roman plant, Cienfuegos, which has also been out of service.
