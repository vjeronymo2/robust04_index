940426 FT 26 APR 94 / World Commodities Prices: Tea The Tea broker's Association reports, good general demand. Best liquoring East Africans were fully firm, but better mediums and plainer Africans were irregular and sometimes 2 to 3p easier. Good ceylons sold well at firm to dearer rates, but lesser sorts tended easier. Ceylons met widespread competition and prices were mainly firm to dearer. Central Africans were barely steady. Price indications for the week: best available 230p/kg nom, nom good 150p/kg, good medium 138p/kg, medium 120p/kg, low medium 86p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 276p for a Rwanda pf. 1
