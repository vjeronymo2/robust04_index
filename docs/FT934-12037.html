931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / International Company News: Rise to IPounds 1.67m at Fitzwilton PROFITS AT Fitzwilton, the Irish industrial holding company headed by Mr Tony O'Reilly, advanced from IPounds 298,000 to IPounds 1.67m (Pounds 1.59m) pre-tax for the half year ended June 30. The outcome benefited from improved results from associates FA Wellworth, a food retailer, and Waterford Wedgwood. In addition, interest charges were lower following the disposal of the Keep Trust motor division. Turnover fell from IPounds 233.7m to IPounds 180.3m of which discontinued operations accounted for IPounds 49.8m (IPounds 105.8m). Operating profits of continuing activities, including associates' contributions, surged from IPounds 1m to IPounds 3.36m. Interest accounted for IPounds 2.62m (IPounds 3.19m). Losses per share emerged at 0.34p (earnings 0.08p) after extraordinary costs of IPounds 646,000 relating to the Keep sale. The interim dividend is maintained at 1.5p. At the period end net debt stood at IPounds 32.1m (IPounds 53.7m at December 30 1992).
