BFN [Text] Seoul, April 21 (YONHAP) -- Prime minister Yi Hoe-chang instructed relevant government offices on Thursday [21 April] to obtain his approval of all unification-or national security-related policies referred to the Policy Coordination Council before they are put into practice. "Since the Unification and National Security Policy Coordination Council is designed to review and adopt draft government policies, all policy matters referred to and decided by the council should now on be approved by the premier prior to their implementation," the premier said. Kang Hyong-sok, the prime minister's spokesman, said Yi gave the instructions at his senior officials' meeting earlier in the day while stressing that all government policies should be drafted and determined through cabinet debate. The premier stressed that it was unthinkable that, for instance, the policy of allowing North Korean loggers' resettlement in South Korea was released to the press before it was adopted at the cabinet level. "Prime Minister Yi asked his secretariat to caution relevant government offices in this connection," the spokesman said. Prime Minister Yi also told his staff to see to it that any expansion of mountain roads should be made in a way that does not damage environment, the spokesman said.
