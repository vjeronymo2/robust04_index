Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] Turkey will purchase 19 MI-17 helicopters from Tatarstan. The helicopters will be assigned to the General Command of the Gendarmerie Forces. It has been ascertained that the talks on the purchase of the helicopters have entered their final stage. In a statement to INTERFAX, Aleksandr Lavrentyev, director of Russia's most important helicopter manufacturing installations in Tatarstan, has said that Turkey decided to purchase 19 MI-8 and MI-17 helicopters for its gendarmerie forces in 1993. Stressing that another Turkish delegation visited Kazan early in January, Lavrentyev said that it held talks for the purchase of several more helicopters in addition to those that Turkey planned to buy for $65,000,000. The helicopters Turkey will buy for its gendarmerie forces are capable of flying 225 km per hour. They can fly and land on any terrain 4,000 meters above sea level.
