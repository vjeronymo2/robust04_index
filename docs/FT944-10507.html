941110 FT 10 NOV 94 / Arts: Today's Television The opening edition of Channel 4's great egg race, which is called Beat That Einstein, did not resist the temptation to talk down to us. Simple scientific ideas were explained as to a four-year-old, and the presenter proved willing to do anything - lie in the bath, fall in the sea - to appear matey. So here is one viewer who will not be watching when today's guinea pigs attempt to build a tree house and a flying machine (8.00). Last week Ludovic Kennedy argued a revisionist version of the Lucky Lucan story, this week it is ITV's turn. The Trial Of Lord Lucan brings the evidence to a courtroom setting and a 'real' jury tries him (8.30). Dawn French takes the title role in BBC1's new comedy series The Vicar Of Dibley, thus adding a new dimension to the 'All Gas And Gaiters' school of comedy which has, so far, been all-male (8.30). The X-Files carries on a tradition of fantasy drama for grown up audiences which has always been stronger in North America than in Britain. Today agents Mulder and Scully investigate scientists who have been driven to suicide in Alaska (9.00 BBC2).
