Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 3 -- General Secretary Do Muoi received here this afternoon a visiting delegation of the Cambodian National Assembly [NA] led by Chairman Samdech Chea Sim. Speaking to his guests, General Secretary Do Muoi considered the visit as a significant event in the building and development of the neighbourly and friendly relations and the multifaceted cooperation between Vietnam and Cambodia in the interests of the two peoples, for peace, stability, friendship development and cooperation in the region and the world as a whole. General Secretary Do Muoi affirmed that now as before Vietnam has always done their best to build and consolidate the traditional friendly and neighborly relations between the two peoples and to support the Cambodian people's efforts to build a peaceful, independent, non-aligned and friendly country with all countries, especially neighbouring. On this occasion General Secretary Do Muoi and the Cambodian NA delegation to convey his regards to Cambodian head of state Samdech Norodom Sihanouk. [sentence as received] Chairman Samdech Chea Sim, for his part, informed General Secretary Do Muoi of the results of the talks held between the two legislative bodies. He expressed his deep gratitude to Vietnam's positive contributions to the process of peace and reconciliation in Cambodia. Also this afternoon, Samdech Chea Sim was received by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. The NA chairman of Cambodia informed Mr. Kiet of the present situation in Cambodia and brought out the Cambodian people's determination to overcome all difficulties and obstacles, thus continuing to take the revolutionary cause to stable and developed status. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet welcomed the delegation visit as a significant event, and manifestation of the friendship, solidarity and cooperation between Vietnam and Cambodia. He affirmed that the Government and people of Vietnam have always supported the efforts of the National Assembly, Government and people of Cambodia in their national construction.
