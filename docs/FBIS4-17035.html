BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Tbilisi April 20 TASS -- Georgian Deputy Interior Minister Georgi Gulua, 38, who headed Georgian Transport Police, fell another victim of terrorist acts this morning. He and his two subordinates were riddled with bullets when they were driving to his office. Terrorists escaped from the place of crime unidentified. This is the eighth terrorist act in Georgia against a high-ranking government official over the past few months. Law enforcement bodies could not identify executors of assassinations even once. Head of the State Security Service Igor Georgadze told journalists at a recent news conference that Georgia is living through a period of fierce struggle between mafia clans, and that terrorist acts are inevitable.
