October 8, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: EVERYBODY LOVES YOU BY ETHAN MORDDEN (ST. MARTIN'S PRESS: $8.95)

Ethan Mordden is one of the few authors writing about gay life in America to reach a general audience. His sure, straightforward prose has such a natural tone that he seems just to have jotted down the words and misadventures of his friends. Although "I Read My Nephew Stories" was cited in the 1989 National Magazine Awards, the strongest stories in this collection are "The Boffer," about a man who learns to accept his changed identity when he attends a college reunion, and "The Dinner Party," in which one of the best and brightest members of the gay community is forced to confront the terrifying reality of AIDS and his own mortality. In the final story, the narrator declares that this third volume concludes his "tales of gay Manhattan" -- a regrettable decision, as these stories comprise some of the best work of this prolific writer of both fiction and nonfiction.
