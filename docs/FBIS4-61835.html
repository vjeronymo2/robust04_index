BFN [Text] Our correspondent reports that the first session of the new Crimean parliament opened in Simferopol today. A total of 94 deputies are attending the sitting, 55 of them from the "Rossiya" presidential bloc. At the beginning of the session Aleksandr Kruhlow, deputy of the Crimean Supreme Soviet, who is chairing the session, addressed the deputies. He stated that in the present circumstances Crimea can no longer accept being separated from Russia, its historical Motherland. Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov spoke at the session. He wished the deputies success in their work and condemned the actions of the Ukrainian naval command with regard to the Black Sea Fleet units. The deputies were also addressed by Konstantin Zatulin, a representative of the Russian State Duma. He appealed to the Crimean parliamentarians to make well-considered decisions so as not to exacerbate the situation in the Crimea. The session will continue its work in the afternoon, after a break. Issues concerning the election of the Speaker of the Crimean Supreme Soviet, the restoration of the 1992 Crimean Constitution, changing the management of the Krym TV and Radio company, and several other questions are expected to be on the agenda.
