Language: Dutch Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Parliament Awards Fokker Defense Order Worth 300 Million"] [Excerpt] Fokker has won a defense order worth nearly 300 million guilders. The Second Chamber voted on Thursday [10 February] to purchase four F-60's, the military version of the Fokker-50. Defense Secretary Frinking and Fokker's new top man, R. van Duinen, are to finalize the contract this afternoon. The fact that Fokker was in the running in the first place for the supply of these four aircraft is thanks to Parliament. The Air Force, with the strong backing of Frinking's predecessor Baron Van Voorst of Voorst, the present governor of Limburg, did not originally believe Fokker capable of producing a medium-sized transport plane able to satisfy all modern military requirements. The Air Force preferred Italian or Spanish aircraft. But the Second Chamber and the Ministry of Economic Affairs succeeded -- heedful of the dire straits of the Dutch plane manufacturer -- in allaying the doubts of the Ministry of Defense. The former is to contribute 30 million guilders to developing the aircraft, which is to be equipped with an extended fuselage, reinforced loading platform, and cargo door. The aircraft are to be used for transporting men and wounded and dropping parachutists. [passage omitted]
