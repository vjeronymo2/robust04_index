931216 FT 16 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television After Life In The Freezer what will Attenborough and his team do for an encore? It will have to be 'Life On Mars' since there cannot, surely, be anywhere on earth more extreme than Antarctica. In today's programme winter has arrived with temperatures falling to minus 70 and winds rising to 100 mph. All animals bar one try to move away from these, the worst conditions on earth. The exception is the emperor penguin which hunkers down under the southern lights with an egg between its feet (this is the male of the species, of course) as the storms rage (8.00 BBC1). In Goodnight Sweetheart television repairman Gary finds a time warp in an alleyway and regularly visits a pub during the London Blitz. This time travel element is making for an unusually habit forming comedy. Today, we are told, Gary is hit by an ambulance in wartime London and ends up in hospital (8.30 BBC1). C4 screens the last in the series about Aids, The Plague (9.00) and BBC2 goes to Northern Ireland for the last of Michael Ignatieff's series about nationalism, Blood And Belonging (9.30).
