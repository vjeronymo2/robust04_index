Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Feb 23 (CTK) -- Director of the Russian Counterintelligence Service Yevgeniy Primakov, who arrived for a two-day visit to the Slovak Republic on Tuesday [22 February], told journalists after meeting Slovak President Michal Kovac today that no underhanded motives lay behind the timing of his visit. Primakov's visit was motivated by the need to create a basis for cooperation between the intelligence services of the two countries in the fight against international crime, terrorism, the drug and arms trades, and the situations in unstable regions of Europe, Presidential Spokesman Anton Bodis told journalists. "The cooperation will be not directed against any third country," Bodis said, adding that the intelligence services of the two countries agreed to exchange representatives in their headquarters in order to establish connection. Primakov's Spokeswoman Tatyana Samolis told CTK that the talks with the Slovak Intelligence Service would continue tonight and tomorrow morning. "The issue was not raised," she replied to a question about the activities of Russian agents in Slovakia.
