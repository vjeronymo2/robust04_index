BFN [Text] San Miguel de Tucuman, 11 Jun (NA) -- It has been officially reported in this city that an alleged member of the Peruvian Maoist organization Shining Path was arrested during a joint Federal and Tucuman Police operation. The alleged terrorist, identified as Jorge Diaz Reategui, was captured at a hotel in the poor district of El Bajo, near the city bus terminal. At the time of his capture, Reategui was with other alleged guerrillas, who managed to escape. Judge Ricardo Maturana is in charge of Federal Court No. 2 of this city. On 10 June he questioned Reategui, but the details of the questioning have not been released. Police sources have said Reategui had been under very strict surveillance by the Federal Police. It has been suggested that this province is a refugee camp for Shining Path members who clandestinely cross the border and train Peruvian and Argentine militants who support their ideology.
