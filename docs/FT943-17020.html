940701 FT 01 JUL 94 / Scottish steel sale causes confusion Confusion over the possible sale of British Steel's mothballed Ravenscraig plant in Lanarkshire intensified yesterday after a Taiwanese company said a deal had been agreed in principle but British Steel said it had not yet received an offer, Laura Tyson and Andrew Baxter write. An Feng, the Taiwan steel group, said it was in the final stages of a deal with Grupo F Jacinto of the Philippines to buy jointly the main equipment at the plant, closed two years ago. A finance manager at An Feng, Taiwan's biggest private-sector steel company, yesterday said a deal had been agreed in principle but the price was under discussion. British Steel confirmed the plant was for sale but said: 'We cannot agree that we have 'agreed in principle'.'
