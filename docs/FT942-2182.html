940621 FT 21 JUN 94 / World Cup '94: Germany look for win and second round If they beat Spain today at Soldier Field, Chicago, defending champions Germany could become the first of the 24 World Cup teams to advance to the second round. 'We don't have a team that knows how to play for a draw,' said coach Berti Vogts. 'We are an offensive team and that's how we'll play.' Vogts gave no clue whether that meant a recall for veteran striker Rudi Voller. Germany beat Bolivia 1-0 in the Group C opener last Friday, while Spain wasted a 2-0 lead against South Korea, drawing 2-2. Worse for Spain was the sending-off of captain Miguel Angel Nadal for an untimely tackle. Nadal was later given a two-match suspension. Spanish coach Javier Clemente has criticized Fifa, soccer's governing body, for penalising Nadal more harshly than the usual one-match suspension. 'I think it's totally unfair and out of proportion,' he said. 'I don't understand it.'
