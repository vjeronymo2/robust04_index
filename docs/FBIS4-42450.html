BFN [Report by Yeoryios Kallinikou] [Excerpts] Nicosia and Athens yesterday expressed satisfaction with the contents of the UN secretary general's report to the Security Council, whereas [Turkish Cypriot Leader] Rauf Denktas launched an attack against Butrus Butrus-Ghali. Most Cypriot political parties also expressed satisfaction with the fact that the report very clearly blames the impasse on the Turkish side and that, for the first time, the Turkish side's lack of political will has been recorded. However, AKEL [Restorative Party of the Working People] expressed concern and the Free Democrats Movement [former President Yeoryios Vasiliou's party] expressed the view that...the Turkish side has been reinforced. [passage omitted] AKEL General Secretary Dhimitrios Khristofias said that he does not believe that we should be happy with the report because the secretary general admits that the package of confidence-building measures is clearly in favor of the Turkish Cypriot side and that among the alternative actions more weight is given to the option of continuing the discussion of the confidence-building measures, even though the report refers to taking strong measures against Denktas. Khristofias said: "We would combine the discussion of the substance of the Cyprus issue with the assumption of practical and strong measures, which would force Denktas and Turkey to discuss the substance of the issue on the basis of UN resolutions." He also added that no one has the right to celebrate about anything. DIKO [Democratic Party] Chairman Spiros Kiprianou said: "At first glance, it is evident that the UN secretary general's report clearly blames the lack of agreement on the confidence-building measures on the Turkish Cypriot side. He says that there is no political will, but he does not mention Turkey clearly. He should have had the courage to name Turkey. He also refers to others in the region who do not have the political will. I want to believe that he implies Turkey, and not anyone else." Kiprianou also stressed the need to remind the international community that the Cyprus issue is a problem of invasion, occupation, settlement, and violation of human rights. EDEK [United Democratic Union of Cyprus] Vice Chairman Takis Khatzidhimitriou stressed that the report does include, at least theoretically, the possibility for measures to be taken against Denktas. Khatzidhimitriou said that in his report the secretary general appears, even if only a little, to imply that Turkey does not have the political will for a solution to the Cyprus problem. For the first time, he said, there is also a reference to measures, something we are not used to hearing from the secretary general. He added that the report is lenient but the reference to possibilities that could be used in the future gives our side the opportunity to assume initiatives. Liberal Party Chairman Nikolaos Rolandhis described the UN secretary general's report as exquisite. He said: "In his report, the secretary general stressed his clear concern for Cyprus and the lack of progress on the problem. The worst alternative for our side is the withdrawal of the United Nations from Cyprus as a peacekeeping force and as a political presence; the second worst alternative is to return to Butrus-Ghali's set of concepts; the third worst is to continue with the confidence-building measures and some other plan, including an idea to take measures against the Turkish Cypriots. It is clear that if President Glavkos Kliridhis had not followed the policy he did our position now would have been difficult, and I think that the only alternative now would have been for the United Nations to withdraw from Cyprus." Free Democrats Chairman Yeoryios Vasiliou described the report as objective. However, he added that the report shows how big a mistake it was to get involved in the dialogue on the confidence- building measures. Vasiliou said: "The report is objective, but it does not give anyone the right to celebrate. On the contrary, it shows how big a mistake it was to begin these negotiations on the confidence-building measures and how, as a result of these negotiations, the Turkish Cypriot side has been reinforced and our side has become weaker." Finally, ADISOK [Democratic Socialist Renewal Movement] Chairman Mikhail Papapetrou expressed satisfaction with the report because it clearly blames the Turkish side for the impasse and states that the lack of political will by the Turkish side systematically blocks all the efforts for a solution to the Cyprus problem. In addition, he said, for the first time the report mentions measures to pressure the Turkish side. Furthermore, Papapetrou said that the UN secretary general's report creates future prospects for the issue and also confirms the correctness of the political forces that have supported the confidence-building measures.
