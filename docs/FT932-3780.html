930612 FT 12 JUN 93 / CSO warns first quarter figures more likely to revision than normal The Central Statistical Office warned yesterday that the first-quarter trade figures were more than usually liable to revision as a new system for measuring EC trade flows settled down, Emma Tucker writes. The figures showed little change in the UK's trade deficit with the European Community. Following the opening of the single market at the end of last year, Customs declarations - the old source of trade statistics - are no longer required for movements of goods within the community. Intrastat, a system for measuring intra-EC trade statistics, has therefore been introduced. It takes its information from a combination of surveys and value added tax returns. The CSO said Customs had received returns from traders representing about 86 per cent of the value of exports and 78 per cent of imports in the first quarter.
