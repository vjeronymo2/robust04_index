March 17, 1990, Saturday, Valley Edition

DEVELOPING VENTURA BOULEVARD

The Los Angeles City Planning Commission held a hearing on the Ventura Boulevard Specific Plan on Feb. 22. I was there, and I didn't like what I heard.

After more than three years, the Citizens Advisory Committee for the plan made its recommendation, which proved to be compromise for all sides -- residents, businesses and developers.

However, after the committee submitted its plan, and before the hearing, major changes were made that deserved further consideration by the committee. From what I could tell, not everyone at the meeting was happy to hear that the Planning Commission would like to see six-story buildings, fewer mom-and-pop businesses on Ventura Boulevard, and excessive trip fees for new development.

I left the hearing feeling "What are they going to do now?" There is just no time for the committee to give the changes careful thought. It appears that the version of the plan found to be satisfactory by those most affected will now give way to political pressure and special interests.

The plan must call for reasonable fees from the developers for new projects, local shopping services for residents, which will promote economic growth and jobs, and a two-story height restriction on the boulevard.

As a Sherman Oaks homeowner who wants business growth, yet at the same time knows the importance of preserving the residential neighborhood of our community, it angers me to think developers who live somewhere else can come to my neck of the woods to pressure city planners into a decision that will turn our residential streets into freeways and parking lots, shadowed by towering buildings offering few local services.

We need a strong neighborhood-based plan approved quickly by the City Council, otherwise a healthly vision for the future of the Valley will become a pipe dream of the Citizens Committee.

ELLEN VUKOVICH

Sherman Oaks
