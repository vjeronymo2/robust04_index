Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Bolivia monitored through 17 March. The annual balance of the mining and metal working industries issued by the Mining Secretariat shows that mining exports dropped 12 percent in 1993 compared with the previous year. Sales abroad totaled $366 million. (La Paz LA RAZON in Spanish 1 Mar 94 p B4) The revenues of the six state-run companies that will be privatized amounted to $1.060 billion in 1993. Bolivian Government Oil Deposits accounts for 59 percent of this total; Lloyd Boliviano Airlines 10 percent; and the remaining 30 percent is distributed among the National Railway Enterprise, the National Telecommunications Enterprise, the National Electricity Enterprise, and the National Smelting Enterprise. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 1 Mar 94 p 3) The Minera del Sur Inc. Company, Comsur, this year will invest $30 million, including a $12.3 million credit recently granted by the World Bank, to promote mining operations in many parts of the country. Comsur, whose main shareholder is President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada, is planning to invest $26 million in the Bolivar mine in Oruro. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 1 Mar 94 p 1) The Andean Development Corporation approved two loans for $98 million for Bolivia. A $38 million loan was approved for the improvement of highways. Another $60 million loan was approved for financing investment projects and operating capital to foster productive activities in the farming, livestock, agro-industrial, industrial, mining, tourism, and services fields. (La Paz Radio Fides Network in Spanish 1100 GMT 17 Mar 94) Miguel Cirbian, Bolivian Government Oil Deposits exploration manager, reported that the new Los Kusis oil field discovered in Santa Cruz can initially produce 150 barrels per day. The field also can produce natural gas. (La Paz LA RAZON in Spanish 2 Mar 94 p B2)
