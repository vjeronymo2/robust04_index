940118 FT 18 JAN 94 / Arts: Today's Television There is endless bluster in the argument between Britain and China over the hand-over of Hong Kong, but it is difficult to understand what sort of constraint the blusterers imagine Britain might employ if we don't get our way. Should we send a gunboat? Threaten economic sanctions against the 1.2bn Chinese? Perhaps Brian Barron's report in Assignment will make everything clear (7.45 BBC2). The opening programme in C4's series Undercover Britain was a brave but not wildly dramatic expose of the exploitation of some domestic servants: employers who don't always say 'Please' may be unpleasant but it is hardly the end of the world. This week's exercise reports on badger baiting (9.00). Today's 40 Minutes sounds like one of the less solemn editions of this series. It is concerned with human hair: those who have it, those who don't and some who seem to but are borrowing other people's (9.50 BBC2). In Network First the Duchess of York joins a party of mentally disabled climbers in the Himalayas (10.40 ITV).
