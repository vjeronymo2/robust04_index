Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Report from the press service of the Republic of Georgia Procurator's Office: "Georgia-Armenia: A Treaty Has Been Drawn Up"] [Text] A treaty on mutual legal assistance has been signed in Tbilisi and Yerevan between the procurator's offices of the Republics of Georgia and Armenia. "This treaty has particular importance for both sovereign countries," stated Jamlet Babilashvili, Republic of Georgia acting procurator general. "It will promote the joint efforts of the law enforcement bodies of the neighboring states in the struggle against organized crime, in particular terrorism, the narcotics business, and so forth; it will contribute to the successful fulfillment of supervisory functions connected with investigations, the arrest and transfer of criminals, and the resolution of other important questions." Armenia is the seventh country with whose procurator's office Georgia's Procurator's Office has drawn up a treaty. Such treaties have been concluded with the Russian Federation, Ukraine, Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, and other republics. Treaties are being prepared with the procurator's offices of Turkey, Bulgaria, Azerbaijan, Iran, and Afghanistan and they will soon become official. In short, Georgia's Procurator's Office is gaining international recognition on a broad scale.
