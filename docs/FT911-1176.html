910508 FT 08 MAY 91 / UK Company News: Upton & Southern falls deeper into the red LOSSES ON its property side left Upton & Southern Holdings, the Middlesbrough-based retailing and property company, deeper in the red in the year to January 26. On turnover of Pounds 11.49m (Pounds 12.5m) pre-tax losses increased from Pounds 800,000 to Pounds 1.43m. The property market continued to be depressed and further amounts were written off to bring development values in line with the market. Retailing was profitable overall and the department stores continued to show steady progress. The auditors again qualified the accounts as to the provisions and work in progress. They said because of uncertainty in the market they were unable the state whether the provisions were sufficient or to value the work in progress. Interest charges increased to Pounds 665,000 (Pounds 352,000) and there was a tax credit of Pounds 3,700 (Pounds 254,000). Losses per share came out at 15.76p (6.05p).
