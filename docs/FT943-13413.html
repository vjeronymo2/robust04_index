940720 FT 20 JUL 94 / German pay experiment Germany is to experiment with performance-related pay and bonuses for civil servants to make its bureaucracy more efficient. Bonn, which has often complained that cumbersome bureaucracy hampers economic growth, approved a small shake-up in the cash-strapped public sector, provided there is no increase in overall costs. 'Existing pay structures in the public service apparently do not always make it sufficiently clear that performance is the key criterion for paying public service staff,' said an interior ministry report approved by cabinet. 'For work which is considerably above average, public servants could receive a performance bonus as a once-off payment or for a limited period.' Meanwhile, Volkswagen, the German carmaker, said it had agreed a pay deal giving workers a 1 per cent rise plus extra payments totalling DM900 (Pounds 370).
