940507 FT 07 MAY 94 / Finance and the Family: Making the most - Annuities If a couple retires, each spouse having accumulated their own pension funds, the question always arises: should they buy a single or joint life annuity? Take a couple with Pounds 100,000 each in their pension funds. If the husband, aged 65, uses his Pounds 100,000 and buys a joint life annuity from Canada Life (see table), his gross annual annuity will be Pounds 9,234.48. If the wife, aged 63, uses her Pounds 100,000 to do likewise, hers will be Pounds 9,162.96. Should both buy single annuities with the same benefits, however, the husband would receive a gross annual income of Pounds 11,643.12 and the wife Pounds 9,951.48. So, the total gross annual income for the couple with a joint life scheme would be Pounds 18,397.44. And should one die before the other, the income would remain at this level until the survivor's death. But if, on the other hand, each bought a single life annuity, they would get an initial gross income of Pounds 21,594.60. Of course, the death of either partner would mean their portion of the income died with them. This means that if the husband died first, for example, she would be left with an income of Pounds 9,951.48 for the rest of her life.
