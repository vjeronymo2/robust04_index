BFN [Text] The Georgian head of state's press secretariat reports that Eduard Shevardnadze, chairman of the Georgian parliament and head of state, met representatives of the Georgian-English cultural and business center from Great Britain -- Trevor Jones, head of the Islington borough council foreign relations department, and Anthony Wokes, an economic adviser. The conversation dealt with the activities of the center and its future prospects. It has been pointed out that the aims of the Georgian-English cultural and business center are to enhance interest toward Georgia in Britain, extend friendly relations, arrange joint creative work, and reveal and develop mutual interests in business. The center has been an initiator of an agreement between the Islington municipality of London and Tbilisi mayoralty. New business relations will be initiated soon between London, Moscow, and Tbilisi. The head of state promised the visitors assistance and support.
