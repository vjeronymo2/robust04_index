920225 FT 25 FEB 92 / UK Company News: Tuskar Resources At the same time as reporting a fall back into losses in the six months to September 30, Tuskar Resources, the Dublin-based oil exploration group, announced that it was experiencing a funding crisis. Mr Neil O'Donoghue, chairman and chief executive, said that the company's advisers considered that the amount of money required could not be raised now. The company has no other significant assets and no cashflow from other sources: Mr O'Donoghue said that there was a consequent possibility that Tuskar would not be able to carry out the work programme required by Ecopetrol, the Colombian state oil group. First-half losses amounted to IPounds 1.01m (Pounds 934,000) pre-tax, against profits last time of IPounds 765,000. In the year to March 31 1991 pre-tax losses were reduced to IPounds 8.26m (IPounds 10m). Losses per share were 0.37p (earnings 0.44p).
