BFN ["hal" report: "Orders Received Increasing Strongly"] [Text] Bonn, 4 May -- In March, orders received in the processing industry in western Germany rose 3 percent -- seasonally adjusted for price changes -- compared with the previous month. In February, they also went up by 3 percent. In particular, foreign demand continued to rise clearly, namely by 6 percent. Orders by domestic customers, on the other hand, rose only by 1 percent, the FRG Economics Ministry in Bonn reports. The greatest increase was reported by the investment goods sector with a total of +\5 percent. The basic materials and producer goods industry registered 3 percent more orders. In contrast, business deals for the producers of durable consumer goods went down by 4 percent. The more informative two-month comparison (February/March compared with December/January) showed an average increase in orders by 5 percent for the processing industry. In these two months foreign business expanded by 7.5 percent, while domestic demand went up by 4 percent. The basic materials and producer goods sector registered the highest increase with 6.5 percent. In the investment goods sector and the durable consumer goods industry orders went up 5.5 percent and 2 percent respectively. All together, in February and March 6.4 percent more orders were placed than during the same months last year.
