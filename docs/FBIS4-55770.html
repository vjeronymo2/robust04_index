BFN [Text] Budapest, May 18 (MTI) -- "The new parliament will need a strong opposition so as to be able to the preserve democratic achievements of the last four years," Prime Minister Peter Boross, who is heading the Hungarian Democratic Forum"s national election list, told an election forum in the west Hungarian village of Gyor on Wednesday. He warned that if the Socialists gain a supreme government position in the parliamentary elections, living conditions will not improve, against all promises. Boross said that those who voted "left" in hope of a better life are set to face a bitter surprise. In the years to come, less will be spent on research, culture, sport and urban development, he presumed.
