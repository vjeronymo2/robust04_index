BFN [Report by BELINFORM-TASS] [Text] Minsk 19 April, TASS -- An IMF delegation arrived in Minsk on Tuesday to discuss articles of the IMF Charter and hold talks on granting Belarus the second part of the credit from the fund of system transformations. "The Belarussian [Belarusian] Government has taken all the necessary measures to obtain the second half of the credit worth 98 million U.S. dollars," said Natalya Prokopyeva, chief expert of the department of international financial organisations of the Belarussian Council of Ministers. The first part of the credit was granted to Belarus in July, 1993. The credit was intended "for leveling the balance of payments" and using the money mainly to repay Belarussian debt to Russia. The credit was granted by the IMF for a term of ten years, allowing a delay of 4.5 years. The interest rate is 5.67 per cent a year.
