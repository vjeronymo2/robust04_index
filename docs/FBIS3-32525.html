Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Panamanian economic developments monitored through 8 February. Ricardo Duran, Panama Chamber of Commerce president, has rejected a survey published by THE ECONOMIST last week which included Panama among countries in which foreign investors are afraid to invest. Duran said he does not know what the publication is based on because Panama's annual economic growth rate of approximately 9 percent shows investors have confidence in the country. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 4 Feb 94 p 9A) Commerce and Industries Minister Ricardo Fabrega has reported that the Foreign Ministry sent a note to Mexico and Venezuela calling for a meeting to be held as soon as possible to discuss a proposal for paying the petroleum debt to those countries. He said interest on the petroleum debt has raised the debt to approximately $220 million. (Panama City EL PANAMA AMERICA in Spanish 4 Feb 94 p 2A) The Cabinet Council has authorized the signing of agreements with various foreign banks and financial organizations to eliminate the risk of some public enterprises or autonomous institutions becoming delinquent in the payment of their obligations or credit lines, which could lead to legal proceedings against the country and the elimination of credit lines. A cabinet decree indicated that the measure is part of the policy to normalize the state's credit situation with its international creditors. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 6 Feb 94 p 4A)
