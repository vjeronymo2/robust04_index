Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tirana, March 28 (BTA) -- The formal visit to Albania by Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov will give an impetus to bilateral relations. This observation was made at the talks between Daskalov and his Albanian counterpart Alfred Serreqi. Daskalov was also received by parliament leader Petar Arbnori and Prime Minister Aleksander Meksi. He held talks with the leader of the foreign policy committee of the Albanian Parliament. No disputes exist between Bulgaria and Albania. The two countries value their poltical dialogue and wish to complete building the legal basis of their relations which would further promote trade and economic cooperation. Bulgaria and Albania intend to take part in building an East-West transport corridor between the Adriatic and the Black Sea. The sides agreed the promotion of bilateral relations has a positive impact on strengthening trust in the Balkans. Bulgaria and Albania are interested in a political settlement to the crisis in Bosnia and the whole of former Yugoslavia. They support the international effort to restore peace and stability in the Balkans.
