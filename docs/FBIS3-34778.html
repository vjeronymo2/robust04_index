Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 15, IRNA -- President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani in a letter to Majles speaker here Tuesday introduced Mostafa Mirsalim as the new minister of culture and islamic guidance. The proposal is in compliance with Article 133 of the constitution of the Islamic Republic of Iran. Mirsalim replaces 'Ali Larijani who last Sunday was appointed by leader of the islamic revolution as the new head of the Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting (IRIB). Born in 1947, Mirsalim received his post-graduate degree in mechanics in Paris in 1972. He was an active member of the Islamic association of Iranian students both at home and abroad for which he was arrested and imprisoned several times by security forces. Mirsalim has previously served in various state posts such as first deputy interior minister, supreme presidential advisor, member of the high council of cultural revolution as well as the council's secretary. He is married and has three children.
