Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russia's Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev has declared that the Russo-Georgian Treaty on Friendship, Good Neighborliness, and Cooperation, signed in Tbilisi on February 3, "demonstrates the vital interests both states have in each other." In an interview with Interfax, he said that the treaty and the entire visit should be regarded "as a strong impetus to political efforts to return peace to Abkhazia and South Ossetia rather than a substitute for such efforts." Kozyrev stressed that in defining the future status of the regions it was necessary to renounce force and seek "a realistic compromise."
