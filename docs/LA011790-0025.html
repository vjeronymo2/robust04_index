January 17, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

BRAZIL;

OIL WORKERS' STRIKE MAY AFFECT U.S.

The country's 10 state-run oil refineries were expected to grind to a halt within 24 hours as union workers went on strike.

Oil unions called the strike in support of a claim for a 64.8% pay raise to make up for inflation. The state-run oil company, Petrobras, offered 21.47%. The strike is likely to disrupt gasoline exports to the United States. Brazil is America's second-biggest foreign gasoline supplier.
