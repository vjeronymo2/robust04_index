940920 FT 20 SEP 94 / Accountants warned on skills Employers increasingly want qualified accountants to have computing, leadership and communication skills rather than the traditional abilities of a 'number-cruncher', says a survey published yesterday. Reed Accountancy, the specialist recruitment agency, asked nearly 100 employers in the industrial and commercial sectors what they were looking for in job applicants. They concluded that there was a growing demand for 'thinking accountants'. Asked to rank the importance of various qualities, nearly a quarter of respondents said 'number-crunching' was less valued. In contrast 55 per cent were looking more often than in the past for industry-specific experience, 80 per cent for computing skills and 68 per cent for leadership skills. Some 44 per cent of those asked said their criteria had changed in the past five years. Mr Richard Close, chairman of the Board for Chartered Accountants in Business, and managing director finance for the Post Office, said that pure 'number-crunching' was a 'less and less valued trait' and that 'analytical skills and objectivity' were being more highly prized. Mr John Feear, head of the career service at the Institute of Chartered Accountants, said accountants had to contribute more broadly to a business. 'It is not just a case of numbers for numbers' sake. There is a lot more emphasis on commercial skills.'
