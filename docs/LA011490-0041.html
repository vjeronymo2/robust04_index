January 14, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENTS: HI-TECH;

EVERYTHING BUT THE KITCHEN CALCULATOR

ANYONE WHO HAS EVER forgotten in mid-recipe how many tablespoons make a quarter of a cup will appreciate the Kitchen Whiz recipe-conversion calculator by Seiko Instruments.

It also enlarges or reduces recipes and converts European metric recipes to U.S. weights and measures.

Based on a Massachusetts Institute of Technology patent, the Kitchen Whiz computes cooking times for roasts and acts as a food-preparation timer, temperature converter, clock, calendar and calculator. Powered with two AA batteries, it comes with a full one-year warranty and sells for $59.95. Available at major department stores. BARBARA THORNBURG
