BFN [Following is a fuller version of an item published in the 17 May China DAILY REPORT, page 1, column 1, headlined "Jiang Urges Tokyo, Beijing To Put Nagano Remarks in Past"] txt}[Text] Beijing, May 17 KYODO -- Chinese President Jiang Zemin on Tuesday [17 May] urged Japan and China to put the remarks of former Justice Minister Shigeto Nagano behind them and move forward with the development of friendly bilateral relations for the future, Japanese officials said. "Let's put the Nagano remarks in the past and move ahead thinking of the future," Jiang told Wataru Kubo, secretary general of Japan's No. 2 opposition party, the Social Democratic Party [of Japan] (SDP) [SDPJ]. Kubo on Monday delivered a letter to Jiang from Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata expressing regret over Nagano's claim that the 1937 Nanjing massacre was a fabrication. Jiang told Kubo that he appreciated the apology from Japan. Regarding concerns that North Korea may be trying to build a nuclear arsenal, Kubo said he hopes the issue could be resolved through dialogue and he had expectations for China's role in unlocking the stalemate. Jiang also hoped to see the issue resolved through negotiations but cautioned the international community should not overestimate China's influence on North Korea. "Looking forward to a resolution through dialogue, China hopes to play a positive role," Jiang said but added, "it is unrealistic to overestimate China's role." Jiang told Kubo that China, North Korea's only major ally in the world, wants to see a stable Korean peninsula free of nuclear arms. Kubo is currently in China on an eight-day visit through Sunday as head of an SDPJ delegation marking the 10th anniversary of the party's exchanges with China's Communist Party. He told Chinese Vice Foreign Minister Tang Jiaxuan in a meeting later Tuesday that China should join working-level negotiations among North and South Korea, the United States and the International Atomic Energy Agency on concerns over North Korea's alleged nuclear weapons program. Tang replied that talks between the United States and North Korea will be effective in resolving the issue, SDP officials said. Tang also said that China is making an effort to bring about a third round of high-level talks between the U.S. and North Korea, and that he wants the SDP to work on the U.S. through the Japanese Government. Kubo is expected to meet with Hata after returning to Tokyo, according to government sources in Tokyo.
