Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Colombia's economic developments monitored through 1 March 1994. Revenues from income taxes, the VAT [Value-Added Tax], and foreign trade increased by 44 percent in 1993 following the implementation of the tax reform law passed in 1992. The Treasury collected 400 billion pesos during the first half of February and expects to collect 200 million pesos in the third week. The treasury placed a sizable portion of the collected funds in securities to prevent instability in the financial system. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 16 Feb 94) Colombia and Romania have signed a commercial cooperation agreement whereby beginning in September Romanian campers will be assembled in Colombia. During a recent visit to Colombia, Romanian Trade Minister Cristian Ionescu told his Colombian counterpart that his government would like to import Colombian coal for energy production and coke [coal residue], coffee, and bananas in exchange for Romanian-made mining equipment. Ionescu also proposed his country as a distributor of Colombian coffee and expressed interest in an aerial route between Romania and South America. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1811 GMT 22 Feb 94)
