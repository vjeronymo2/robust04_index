BFN [Text] Ankara, June 25 (A.A) -- Technical specialists from Turkey, Russia, the United States, and Kazakhstan concluded their meetings in Ankara, officials said on Saturday [25 June]. BOTAS [Pipeline and Petroleum Transport Corporation] Director General Hayrettin Uzun told AA reporter that as the Azeri officials could not attend the meeting no concrete developments had been possible but a sub-committee would be set up to make further technical researches. The "the pipe-line working group" whose meeting lasted two days was to discuss the route of a proposed Azeri, Russian, and Kazakhstan oil pipeline that will reach Europe. The United States which was represented by Chevron company which will produce and market the oil participated as third country, while Turkish and Russian delegations constituted the main sides. Officials from Foreign Ministry, Foreign Trade Under Secretariat, energy and natural resources, and BOTAS company also took part in the Turkish delegation.
