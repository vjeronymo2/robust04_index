940531 FT 31 MAY 94 / UK Company News: Seafield makes Pounds 348,000 so far Seafield, the troubled transport and distribution company, announced first quarter 1994 profits of Pounds 348,000 which compared with Pounds 804,000 for the whole of 1993. Mr Brian Chilver, chairman, said that with Charterhall Properties now sold - an acquisition he described as a disaster and which cost Seafield Pounds 21.7m in losses - management had been free to concentrate on the core business. The first quarter had gone well, he said.
