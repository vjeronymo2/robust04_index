921217 FT 17 DEC 92 / UN plan for Mozambique THE UN Security Council last night approved unanimously a big peacekeeping and election-monitoring operation for Mozambique in support of the October ceasefire agreement which ended a ruinous 14-year civil war, writes Michael Littlejohns in New York. Mr Boutros Boutros Ghali, the secretary-general, proposed sending more than 7,500 troops and civilian staff at an estimated cost of more than Dollars 330m. But the council decided to seek economies through a phased deployment of forces, as well as strict reporting on progress made.
