Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 8 March (MTI) -- The Hungarian representative in the Danube Commission recently forwarded a letter to representatives of the other member states, Hungarian Foreign Affairs Spokesman Janos Herman announced on Tuesday [8 March]. In the letter, the Hungarian Government, citing the February tragedy of the Ukrainian tugboat, reminded the member states of the Danube Commission that the Slovak Government, in serious violation of its international contractual obligations, rechanneled the Danube river flow in a 40-kilometer section from the river's natural bed, previously used for international shipping, and has thus prevented navigation on this section. In order to fully enforce the safety and freedom of Danube shipping, the Hungarian Government considers it necessary for the Danube Commission to call on the Government of the Slovak Republic to continuously provide the necessary water amount in the natural bed of the Danube in order to restore the original conditions of navigation, as was previously demanded by the Hungarian side. The Hungarian Government also deems it necessary for the Danube Commission to call on Bratislava to restore the official announcement system which has not been in force since the Gabcikovo facility was unilaterally placed into operation. Under the terms of the system, the Danube Commission, and through it the governments of the countries concerned, must immediately be informed of all circumstantial events -- for instance, the serious accident in February. However, the Danube Commission has still not received any such notification concerning the Ukrainian boat accident. The Hungarian Government has requested that the Commission place these issues on the discussion agenda for the session next month.
