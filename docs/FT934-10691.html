931104 FT 04 NOV 93 / Attack on 'appalling' life policy advice LIFE assurance sales repre-sentatives are accused of giving appalling, inappropriate and misleading advice in a new report, Bethan Hutton writes. The research for Which?, the Consumers' Association magazine, was carried out on visits to 45 advisers - a mixture of independents, tied agents and sales representatives employed by insurance companies. Researchers asked for guidance on the cheapest way to protect their families in the event of their deaths. According to Which? the most appropriate solution was term assurance, which pays out if the person insured dies within a fixed period but has no other cash value. Most advisers recommended this, but a significant minority suggested endowments or whole-of-life policies, which are generally more expensive and pay more commission. Two researchers could have bought pension-linked term assurance, but 13 of 28 advisers failed to mention this. Lautro, the industry regulator, said yesterday: 'Obviously we will never eradicate all the bad apples from the marketplace, but we do our best to minimise the risks.'
