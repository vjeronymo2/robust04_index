920124 FT 24 JAN 92 / Greek tycoon gets 5 years FALLEN tycoon George Koskotas, central figure in a Dollars 200m bank embezzlement scandal which dragged down Greece's Socialist government in 1989, was convicted of forgery yesterday and sentenced to five years in prison, Reuter reports from Athens. He was found guilty of forging a document he said proved socialist ministers had asked him to deposit large sums of money in New York. A week ago, socialist former premier Andreas Papandreou was acquitted on charges of complicity in the scandal.
