BFN [Text] Cartagena -- Salvadoran President Armando Calderon Sol on 14 June in Cartagena urged his colleagues meeting in the Fourth Ibero-American Summit of Heads of State and Government to form a common front to defend the legitimate right to development of the region's peoples. He said that strengthening representative democracy, promoting human rights, and achieving economic and social development should be the great goals of the 21 Ibero-American leaders. President Calderon, who is attending for the first time the meeting of Ibero-American leaders, a group that has already met on three occasions, added that now that nearly all peoples of the region are enjoying democracy, it is crucial that we direct our efforts toward improving the living conditions of our people. He called for an expanded discussion among the presidents on the integration issue, not just on trade liberalization. Calderon pointed out that our Central American countries are making enormous efforts toward integration.
