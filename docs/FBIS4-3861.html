BFN [Text] Tirana, April 23 (XINHUA) -- The Albanian parliament today ratified a document on Partnership for Peace with NATO as a step toward better relations with West European countries. In February, Albanian President Sali Berisha signed the document with NATO Secretary General Manfred Worner in Brussels and later submitted it to the People's Assembly for approval. Before the parliament convened over the weekend, the president told the lawmakers that ratification of the agreement was an important step toward new cooperation with NATO. He stressed that it was also part of Albania's efforts to obtain full membership in NATO. The document provides that Albania and NATO will cooperate in peacekeeping missions, rescue operations and military exercises. No further details were available.
