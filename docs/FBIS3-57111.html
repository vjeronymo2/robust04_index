Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow January 19 TASS -- The Liberal Democratic Party of Ultra-Nationalist Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, which won elections to the new Russian legislature on a party list, failed to enjoy the same success in one-seat constituencies and thus became the third strongest faction in the lower chamber, according to information made public in the Duma on Wednesday. It registered 63 lawmakers and Sergey Churkin is the secretary of the faction. The Russia's Choice and the faction of independent lawmakers overtook Zhirinovskiy. The Russia's Choice has registered 76 lawmakers headed by Sergey Yushchenkov, while the independents united in the "New Regional Policy" faction with 65 seats and Gennadiy Kalistratov as the coordinator. The Agrarian Party is the fourth in the list with 57 lawmakers headed by Secretary Nikolay Kharitonov. The Communists come fifth with 45 seats, the Party of Russian Unity and Accord has 30 lawmakers, the Yabloko Bloc of Grigory Yavlinsky - 27, the "Women of Russia" - 23 and the last is the Democratic Party with 15 seats.
