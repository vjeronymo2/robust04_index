BFN [Selin Caglayan report: "Ankara Warns European Union on Cyprus"] [Text] Ankara--While Greece maintains its pressure on the European Union [EU] to have south Cyprus admitted into that organization as a member, Ankara has warned the EU countries that they "will have to accept the consequences of south Cyprus becoming an EU member and that such a development will mark the end of the negotiating process in Cyprus." It has been reported that the messages Ankara has conveyed to the EU countries at various levels can be taken to mean that the TRNC [Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus] will unite with Turkey if the Greek Cypriot side is admitted into the EU as a full member. Turkey defends the view that the Greek Cypriot side becoming an EU member will mean the de facto annexation of south Cyprus to Greece. Meanwhile, a number of officials in Turkey have maintained that several points in UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali's report on Cyprus, which has elicited strong reaction in Turkey, can be justified. A Turkish Foreign Ministry official has said that the fact that Butrus Butrus-Ghali has agreed that the Cyprus problem has continued for 30 years is "a very positive development." The Greek Cypriots have maintained that the Cyprus problem was created as a result of Turkey's intervention in the island in 1974. Furthermore, Ankara has regarded Butrus-Ghali's view that "the limited UN resources may be used to resolve other disputes" as a positive suggestion.
