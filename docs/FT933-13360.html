930720 FT 20 JUL 93 / Parliament and Politics: Smith wins vote backing at NEC LABOUR'S ruling national executive committee last night endorsed controversial proposals by party leader Mr John Smith for one-member-one-vote in the selection of parliamentary candidates. The NEC voted by 20 votes to seven in favour of the 'consensus' Mr Smith said was achieved at a meeting between Labour MPs and union representatives last week. But the decision makes it more likely the leadership will face a battle at the party's annual conference in September, since some of Britain's largest unions including the GMB and the TGWU have made clear they are still opposed to parts of the plan. The trade union reform group's report would form the basis of rule changes to be put to the conference. Mr Smith said at the end of the meeting: 'I recommend (the proposals) to the party and believe (they) will greatly strengthen both Labour party democracy and the links between the Labour party and the trade unions and their members.' But NEC member Mr Dan Duffy, chairman of the TGWU general union, said: 'As far as we're concerned the present proposals will reduce the links and will emasculate the trade union movement.' He added: 'If the proposals are still the same as they are at present, we will be arguing against them.'
