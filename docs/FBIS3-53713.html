Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Kuwait, 18 Jan (KUNA) -- The second deputy prime minister and minister of finance and planning Nasir al-Rawdan said today that oil revenues between 1 July 1993 and the end of last year reached 1.235 billion Kuwaiti dinars whereas nonoil revenues were only 166 million dinars. This came in a financial report, a copy of which was obtained by KUNA, which was presented by al-Rawdan to the National Assembly on the economic situation in Kuwait, on problems and challenges facing it, and recommondations that might boost the economy. The report predicted, however, that Kuwait's oil revenues could be as much as 944 million dinars during the next six months presuming output would remain at two million barrels per day and at an average of $10.50 per barrel.
