BFN [Text] Tirana, June 3 (ATA) -- Prime Minister Meksi met on June 3 with the speaker of Macedonian Parliament, Mr. Andov. They discussed the possibilities for the development of the relations between the two countries in various fields of mutual interest. "We are neighbours and have had good relations which we should further develop," Mr. Meksi said, adding that "observation of the rights of all the nationalities living in Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] and of the Albanians in particular, of their status, are an expression of the application and observation of the European documents and norms, guaranteed human rights." Focusing on the possibilities existing for an economic cooperation, Mr. Meksi mentioned such fields as transport, electric power, fuel, border trade, facilitation of conditions for private business, culture, education, and science. Mr. Andov expressed Macedonia's commitment to respect all the rights of the Albanians, adjustment of their status, a real census of the population and the readiness for a broader cooperation in the economic field. Mr. Andov appreciated the readiness of Albania to help Macedonia to overcome the Greek embargo and Albania's stand about events in the Balkans. Both sides stressed the need of increasing bilateral contacts and materialization of bilateral initiative in the fields of mutual interest and to the benefit of the two peoples.
