BFN [Text] Today Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich departed on a working visit to Moscow. The purpose of the prime minister's visit is to finally agree upon the documents on the unification of monetary systems of Belarus and Russia. The Belarusian delegation to the talks with Russian leadership includes Deputy Supreme Soviet Chairman Bambiza, First Deputy Prime Minister Myasnikovich, Deputy Prime Minister Kostsikaw, Deputy Prime Minister Zalamay, National Bank Chairman Bahdankevich, Finance Minister Yanchuk, Defense Minister Kazlowski, Shurkdz, head of the State Committee for Customs, and other officials. Just now, we have received the following information from Moscow. Prime Ministers Viktor Chernomyrdin and Vyachaslaw Kebich signed an agreement on the unification of monetary systems of Russia and Belarus. The essence of the achieved agreements is that the process of unification will take place in two stages. In the first stage, beginning from 1 May 1994, custom duties in trade will be abated, fees for transit of Russian cargoes on Belarusian territory will be abolished, and Russia will be given the right to no-charge lease of strategic facilities of the Belarusian Armed Forces. The second stage -- the direct exchange of Belarusian cash currency for Russian rubles on a one-to-one exchange rate -- should be preceded by the political expression of will of Belarusian people on account of the unification of [Belarusian] monetary system with Russia. The Russian-Belarusian intergovernmental talks have also resulted in the signing of seven bilateral documents, which are an indispensable part of the agreement on the unification of monetary systems.
