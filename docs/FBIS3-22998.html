Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The existence of a terrorism academy in Burj al-Barajinah [south Beirut] was revealed yesterday. Judge Nasri Lahhud, the government's representative at the Military Tribunal, has indicted the Palestinian Ghalib Husayn al-Jamal, currently in custody, on the charge of training individuals to carry out terrorist operations and manufacture the necessary explosives for such activity. Al-Jamal opened a school in Burj al-Barajinah, calling it the Academy of Terrorist Operations. He charged $50 for each lesson. Judge Lahhud also indicted al-Jamal's brother-in-law, Mahmud al-Zu'bi, currently at large, for supplying al-Jamal with terrorist trainees over a period of four months. He referred the case to the military prosecutor.
