Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 03, BNS -- World Bank (WB) plans to lend Latvian Government 35 million U.S. dollars for the revival of Latvian Universal Bank and Latvian Savings Bank, Valdis Freidenfelds, Finance ministry press secretary, informed BNS. Latvian Finance Minister Uldis Osis met with WB mission, led by Alex Fleming, to discuss the banks' revival project. The project is done by WB and the Bank of Latvia experts and it provides for the invest of part of 35 million loan in Latvian Universal Bank to take out of circulation those credits which cannot be presently paid off. The Universal Bank has such credits for 25 million lats and Savings Bank for 7 million lats. Freidenfelds noted that WB loan to the Latvian banks will increase the extent of financial resources for granting of credits. Part of the invested sum will be used for technical aid to those enterprises which cannot pay back credits and to save them from possible bankruptcy. WB mission will stay in Latvia for two more weeks and meet with Economic Ministry and the Bank of Latvia's experts.
