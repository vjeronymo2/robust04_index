Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] New Delhi, February 4 (XINHUA) -- India has successfully test-fired a multi-target surface to air missile Akash (sky) at Chandipur in the eastern Indian state of Orissa on Thursday, according to a Defense Ministry announcement available here today. The announcement described the test-firing of a 25 kilometer long range missile as a technological breakthrough. The announcement said that the missile used the integrated two-stage rocket ramjet propulsion. This was the first successful test-firing of the 650 kilogram missile. The missile had multiple warheads which could target four to five enemy aircraft at long range.
