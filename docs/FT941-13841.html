940124 FT 24 JAN 94 / Jobs gain forecast for 1995 German unemployment will increase this year but the trend will reverse in 1995, when restructuring measures adopted by companies would be advanced or finished, Mr Gunter Rexrodt, economics minister, was quoted as saying yesterday, Reuter reports from Frankfurt. According to the text of an interview to be broadcast on German television, Mr Rexrodt said: 'The year 1994 will be a hard year with higher unemployment. . . I assume this trend can be expected to change in 1995.' The east German job market, which had already stabilised, would see more of an upturn, Mr Rexrodt said. West German unemployment rose to 2.49m in December, or 8.1 per cent. In east Germany, the jobless total rose to 1.18m (15.4 per cent).
