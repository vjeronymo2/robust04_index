Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Talks between the government delegations of Ukraine and Russia started in Kiev Tuesday. The talks will last for two days and are being held behind closed doors. The Russian delegation is led by special envoy Yuriy Dubinin, the Ukrainian delegation -- by Deputy Prime Minister Valeriy Shmarov. About 6 agreements are expected to be signed following the talks. The spokesman for the Russian Foreign Ministry, Yuriy Sergeyev, told Interfax-Ukraine that his delegation has a mandate to hold talks on nuclear weapons, the Black Sea Fleet, and mutual debts. Commenting on the statement made by Dubinin that there are unclear points in Ukraine's stand on nuclear weapons, Sergeyev said that by its decision the Ukrainian parliament says "yes" to nuclear-free status.
