BFN [Text] Sevastopol, 28 Jun -- Interviewed by the KRYMSKIYE IZVESTIYA [CRIMEAN NEWS] newspaper, Serhiy Nikulin, leader of the Russia grouping at the Supreme Council of Crimea and chairman of its nation-building, legislation, legality, law and order standing committee, accused Yuriy Meshkov and Yevhen Saburov, the autonomy's president and deputy prime minister respectively, of "attempts to gain absolute power" in Crimea and "place themselves outside any control by parliament, as happened in Russia after the shooting at the Supreme Soviet." Serhiy Nikulin says this began with "the Crimean presidential administration and government moving away from the political forces that ensured Yuriy Meshkov's victory in the presidential election." He doubts whether the president intends to carry out his pre-election program. The Russia bloc, Serhiy Nikulin underlines, "opposes this Crimean government's unlimited power only in order to be able on behalf of the people to supervise its actions."
