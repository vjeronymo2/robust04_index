Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vienna, February 26 (XINHUA) -- Inspectors of the International Atomic Energy Agency were granted today visas to visit the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK), according to a news bulletin released by the agency. The bulletin said the group of inspectors were scheduled to arrive in Pyongyang on March 1, and then visit seven nuclear facilities in the country. DPRK agreed on February 15 to open the seven nuclear facilities, but said two other facilities were not included. The U.N. agency urged DPRK on February 23 to grant visas to the inspectors as soon as possible and set a one-week deadline. In a related development, the United States agreed to cancel joint military exercises this year with South Korea and to begin the third round of DPRK-U.S. talks on March 21 in Geneva. The two sides also agreed that the working-level contacts between the North and South should resume in Panmunjom.
