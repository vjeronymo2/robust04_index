January 14, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

NATION IN BRIEF;

OHIO;

FIRM PLANS BIGGEST CIVILIAN JET ENGINE

General Electric Co. will detail next week in Cincinnati plans to develop what is billed as the world's biggest and most powerful civilian jet engine, designed to power the new generation of jumbo jets in the next century, a company spokesman said. The engine will permit the proposed Boeing 777 super jumbo jet and other planes to handle increased weight with only two engines rather than the three or four on today's jets, saving fuel costs and providing greater operating efficiency, the spokesman said. Sources said the impetus for the new engine was the recent announcement by Boeing Co. that it would go ahead with plans to build its 777.
