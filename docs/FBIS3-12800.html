Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] In a statement distributed on 14 February, the Supreme Islamic Committee [SIC] in Jerusalem rejected Israeli proposals to discuss the future supervision of the Islamic holy shrines in the occupied territories, such as the al-Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron, al-Nabi Yusuf Tomb in Nabulus, al-Nabi Samuil Mosque in Jerusalem, and Rachel's Tomb in Bethlehem, on the basis that these holy places are sacred to both Muslims and Jews. The statement said: The Islamic mosques should not be a place of worship for non-Muslims. The mosques to which the proposals refer and that are mentioned in the statement are Islamic mosques built by Muslims. The statement added: The current practices in the mosques are based on the force of the occupation. From the beginning, the SIC has objected to these practices, stressing its adherence to the Muslim holy shrines and mosques.
