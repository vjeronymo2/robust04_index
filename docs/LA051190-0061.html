May 11, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

CYCLING TOUR DE TRUMP;

DUTCHMAN WINS 10TH STAGE; SOVIET STILL LEADS

Michael Zanoli of the Netherlands won the 10th stage of the Tour de Trump on Thursday and Soviet amateur Vladislav Bobrik retained his overall lead for the fifth consecutive day.

Three riders were injured in a four-bike crash about 50 miles into Thursday's 120.5-mile Stroudsburg to New Paltz Road Race, none seriously.

Zanoli, 22, who resides in Boulder, Colo., came out of a group of four riders about 30 miles from the finish and outsprinted Ron Kiefel, also of Boulder, Colo., by about two feet for the stage victory. Kiefel's finish moved him into fourth place overall.

Bobrik, 19, has worn the leader's jersey since last Saturday after winning the third stage, the Fredericksburg to Richmond Road Race.

After finishing 11th Thursday in 5:21:19, Bobrik leads Mexico's Raul Alcala by 1:27 and Atle Kvalsvoll of Norway by 2:10 with three stages in the 1,107-mile race remaining.
