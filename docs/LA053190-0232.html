May 31, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

WORLD;

RYZHKOV SLIGHTLY HURT IN CRASH

Soviet Premier Nikolai I. Ryzhkov was slightly injured in a collision between his limousine and a military bus on the outskirts of Moscow, Tass news agency said today.

Soviet Foreign Ministry spokesman Vadim Perfiliev, asked at a regular news briefing about the accident Wednesday morning, said the premier received some bumps but "feels just fine."

Just before the accident, the bus was approaching Ryzhkov's limousine on a highway on the western edge of Moscow.

Tass said a security car in Ryzhkov's motorcade alerted the bus by flashing its lights, and the bus pulled aside.

"The bus's wheels struck the curb, the bus spun and blocked the road, prompting the collision," an official told Tass.
