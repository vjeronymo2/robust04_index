BFN [Text] Palestinian leaders in the territories are focusing efforts on East Jerusalem, mainly in a bid to lift the closure which has separated the city from the rest of the territories. Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] has distributed a leaflet in the territories calling for an exacerbation of the battle until Jerusalem is liberated. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports: [Begin Amir recording] A senior Palestinian source stressed yesterday that PLO supporters will focus their political activity on the issue of East Jerusalem. The source stressed that the Palestinians will not remain silent in the face of demonstrations by the Israeli right wing, such as that organized by the Likud yesterday outside the Old City's walls. Faysal al-Husayni will hold a discussion on Jerusalem this afternoon at the Orient House building and report on the latest steps taken by the Israeli Government against Palestinian institutions in the eastern part of the city. Hamas has also exhorted its followers to put an emphasis on the struggle over Jerusalem. In a special leaflet distributed in the territories yesterday, 'Izz-al-Din al-Qassam members were called upon to step up activities against Israel until what the leaflet described as the liberation of all of the homeland, including Jerusalem. The Palestinian source explained that the PLO will now concentrate its efforts on lifting the closure on East Jerusalem, which has severed the city from the territories. In discussions held yesterday by Palestinian self-government officials in the Gaza Strip and Jericho, the police commanders in both areas expressed praise for the rapid organization of the Palestinian forces and noted that they believe the police are operating smoothly and satisfactorily despite all the difficulties. General 'Abd-al-Razzaq al-Yahya, who was head of the Palestinian delegation on security affairs in the peace negotiations, crossed through the Rafah border terminal into the Gaza Strip yesterday. Gen. al-Yahya will be in charge of public security in the autonomous areas, together with Police Commander Nasr Yusuf. An advance force of 36 Palestinian intelligence officers has arrived in Jericho. The men received training in intelligence work in Egypt, Algeria, and Tunis. They will mainly be in charge of the PLO's military intelligence. [end recording]
