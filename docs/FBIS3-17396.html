Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tamara Zamyatina] [Text] Moscow, 24 Mar -- At a meeting between Sergey Filatov, head of the Russian president's administration, and Aslambek Akbulatov, Chechen secretary of state, the Chechen side broke the preliminary agreement to the effect that recognizing the republic as a constituent part of the Russian Federation would be a condition for the talks, Filatov told an ITAR-TASS correspondent today. In addition, according to the head of the presidential administration, recognizing the bilateral accord between the federal authorities and Tatarstan as a general basis was yet another condition for starting talks between Russia and Chechnya. Instead, the Chechen side put forward its own version of the treaty, Filatov stated. At that point the meeting was suspended. The talks can be resumed only if the above conditions are accepted, the head of presidential administration stressed. Also, Filatov noted that the Chechen side had proposed to hold a meeting between Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Chechen leader Dzhokhar Dudayev. The meeting would be premature until fundamental issues are solved, he believes.
