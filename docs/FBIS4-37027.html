BFN [Text] Iraq has protested to the Arab League against the continued violation of its airspace by the U.S., British, and French aircraft flying from bases in Saudi Arabia and Kuwait, noting that this poses a serious threat to Iraq's security, sovereignty, and national unity. In a letter addressed to Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid, Arab League secretary general, Iraq's permanent representative to the Arab League said these aircraft continued their violation of Iraqi airspace during the period 1-8 June, carrying out 462 aggressive sorties. Iraq's representative asked that the letter, along with the list detailing these violations, be circulated to the Arab member states.
