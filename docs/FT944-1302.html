941221 FT 21 DEC 94 / BBC in US deal for news service The BBC plans to launch its 24-hour satellite news service, BBC World, in the US in February in a deal with International News Network (INN) of New York. The launch, much earlier than expected and less than a month after BBC World goes on air in Europe, is designed to take advantage of changes in US cable regulations. The Federal Communications Commission recently decided that cable operators could charge more for their basic cable packages if new channels were offered. Mr Reese Schonfeld, the former president and chief executive of the CNN network, who is chairman of INN, said yesterday that he expected a high proportion of big cable operators to at least test BBC World in their markets. He conceded, however, that competition would be intense with more than 60 channels competing for about six additional slots. Mr Schonfeld said CNN provided the who, what, where and when of television news. 'BBC World will add the how and the why with the help of pieces from Newsnight and Panorama,' he added.
