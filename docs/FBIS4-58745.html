BFN [Text] Cairo, 6 May (MENA)-- Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has announced that Egypt will submit a number of proposals to the emergency meeting of the Arab League ministerial council to be held on Saturday [7 May]. They will include the possibility of the League's intervention to end the fighting and monitor the situation in Yemen. In statements today, Musa said Egypt is contemplating submitting a proposal to send Arab troops to Yemen upon the League's decision, and after obtaining Yemen's approval. Musa said he has contacted the foreign ministers of Saudi Arabia, the Sultanate of Oman, and the United Arab Emirates on developments in Yemen and the ministerial council meeting. After contacting President Mubarak and a number of Arab leaders, Musa urged Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid to call an emergency meeting.
