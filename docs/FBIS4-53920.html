BFN [Text] China intends to purchase more goods from Malaysia, especially commodities such as processed palm oil and crude petroleum as well as food products. China's State Planning Commission Vice Chairman, Encik [Mr.] Gan Ziyu said China would like to improve its imports from Malaysia because the momentum for the economic growth of 9 percent in China is still high. He told newsmen this after calling on Deputy Minister of International Trade and Industry, Encik Chua Jui Meng in Kuala Lumpur. China is also interested to import the commodities and food products through more direct trade with Malaysia. At the same time, it plans to improve its exports of edible oils, textile machinery, and fabrics to Malaysia. Gan said China's exports to Malaysia in 1993 increased by 21 percent over the previous year while at the same time its imports rose by 30 percent. Bilateral trade between Malaysia and China grew by 500 percent between 1985 and 1993. Malaysia's exports of commodities such as processed palm oil, crude petroleum, natural rubber and cacao made up 78 percent of total exports to China.
