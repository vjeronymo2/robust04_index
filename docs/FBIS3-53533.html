Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Maharashtra, stray incidents of stone pelting and arson at various places in Marathwada Division were reported after the announcement of the renaming of the Marathwada University after Dr. B.R. Ambedkar. The police fired in the air at (Shaksarpimpri) in Bidh District where an irate mob set fire to eight hutments. In Jalna District, the mob tried to set fire the Sarwari Railway Station. Curfew was imposed in Manwat, Sailu, and Pakri towns in Parbhani District as a precautionary measure. The situation is reported to be under control. In Andhra Pradesh, Sarvari station of (Manwar-Purna) section of Hyderabad Division of South Central Railway was burnt last night by agitators suspected to be connected with the renaming of Marathwada University. In the same section between (Manmand-Palegaon), concrete slippers were kept on the track by miscreants blocking rail traffic. Pangra bridge between Balda and Seri station of (Akola-Purna) sections was set on fire by agitators. As a precautionary measure, trains have been regulated and diverted on this section. Railway Protection Force is protecting the disturbed area. Meanwhile, renaming of Marathwada Biddyapit [University] in Maharashtra as Doctor Baba Saheb Amedkar Biddyapit has been widely welcomed. The BJP [Bharatiya Janata Party] has congratulated the Maharashtra government for the decision and said the renaming will solve all disputes in Marathwada and elsewhere. Various groups of the Republican Party also hailed the decision and described it as a progressive step. The CPM [Communist Party of India-Marxist], the CPI [Communist Party of India] and the Janata Dal have also welcomed the decision.
