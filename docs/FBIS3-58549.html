Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara -- In his assessment of Greek oil prospecting around Thasos Island, President Suleyman Demirel warned: "Problems will arise if Greece acts like `this is my sea, my lake' with regard to the Aegean." Indicating that for Greece to try to do something in a sea in which Turkey has interests would lead to problems, President Demirel said: "When I say these things, they immediately get offended. I am not the one creating tension. I merely want all the problems to be resolved peacefully." Indicating that Greece has nothing to be gained in the present situation by fomenting and escalating a crisis with Turkey, the president said: "You are not always at fault in a traffic accident. You might be a very good driver, but someone might come along and collide with you." When reminded that he brought Prime Minister Ciller into politics, Demirel said he cannot comment on the prime minister's statements and views and added: "Whether I defend or criticize them, it will still open the way to various speculations. I am not an inspector of the prime minister; I too am a mere voting citizen."
