BFN [Text] Cairo, 1 Jun (MENA) -- Croatian Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs Mate Granic has declared that his country strongly supports a cease-fire in Bosnia-Hercegovina and a federation between Muslims and Croats. At a press conference that he held today on the sidelines of the Non-Aligned Movement meeting in Cairo, the Croatian foreign minister said that Muslims and Croats have several things in common and that both are interested in establishing contacts and relations. He affirmed that no problems exist between the two sides and that they have an understanding and a type of cooperation between them. Granic added that Croatia, one of the newest world states, is interested in building strong political and economic ties with different states. He noted that he seized the opportunity of his participation in the non-aligned meeting to hold over 200 meetings with the foreign ministers at the conference to enhance bilateral ties and expand the scope of cooperation. He noted that his country seeks to join the European Union [EU] and expects to sign an economic agreement with the EU by the end of the year.
