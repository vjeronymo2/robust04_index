BFN [From the "NTC News" newscast] [Text] Prosecutor General Gustavo de Greiff today met with Medellin Cartel member John Jairo Velasquez, alias Popeye, in his office. The meeting was part of a process that includes negotiations on Popeye's sentence. Velasquez Vasquez was taken to the Prosecutor General's Office to enable him to take advantage of the surrender to justice program. Popeye, Ottoniel de Jesus Gonzalez, alias Otto, and Roberto Escobar, alias El Osito, surrendered to authorities 18 months ago. Velasquez Vasquez met today with Prosecutor General Gustavo de Greiff, Deputy Prosecutor Francisco Sintura, and Ana Montes, the director of the Prosecutor's Office. This is the first time since his surrender that Popeye has agreed to negotiate with authorities. The meeting lasted 90 minutes. The charges against him include drug trafficking, conspiracy, illegal enrichment, escaping from prison, and illegally possessing weapons. Amid tight security, Popeye was returned to Modelo Prison at around noon today. At the Prosecutor General's Office, Velasquez Vasquez expressed his wish to cooperate with justice and to confess to the crimes in which he has participated.
