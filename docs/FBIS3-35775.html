Language: Azeri Article Type:BFN [Text] The Azerbaijani Foreign Ministry Press Center has revealed that the fourth ECO [Economic Cooperation Organization] Ministerial Council meeting in Tehran has ended. A delegation headed by Foreign Minister Gasan Gasanov represented Azerbaijan at the meeting, which was also attended by the foreign ministers of Afghanistan, Iran, Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, Pakistan, Turkmenistan, Turkey, and Uzbekistan. The ECO secretary general, leading officials of several UN committees and organizations, the president of the Islamic Development Bank, and WHO representatives were invited to attend as guests. President Hashemi-Rafsanjani delivered the opening speech, stressing the need to improve cooperation in the region and expressing concern over the continuing dispute between Azerbaijan and Armenia. Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati, Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ali, and Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin also spoke. They strongly condemned Armenia's aggression against Azerbaijan and called for the withdrawal of the occupying forces as soon as possible. Foreign Minister Gasan Gasanov addressed the meeting on behalf of the Azerbaijani delegation. He briefed delegates on developments in Azerbaijan since the last ECO Ministerial Council meeting and the time when Azerbaijan became an ECO member. Pointing to Armenia's aggression against Azerbaijan, Gasanov urged the ECO members to support Azerbaijan's people, take concrete steps, and adopt economic and political sanctions against the aggressor. Regarding issues relating to economic cooperation, Gasanov noted the importance of improving cooperation in transport and communication in the region. Gasanov's concrete proposals were included in the ECO's program of work for 1994. The meeting approved the ECO plan for 1994. It calls for holding the next meetings of the ECO Red Crescent Organizations and the ECO committees on power generation, transport, and communication in Baku. Gasanov held bilateral talks with the foreign ministers of the ECO members and leading officials of international organizations. Gasanov conveyed President Geydar Aliyev's letters to the presidents of the republics in Central Asia when he met his counterparts from Kyrgyzstan, Turkmenistan, Kazakhstan, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. Gasanov invited Foreign Ministers [name indistinct], Suleymenov, and (?Asymov) to stop over in Baku after leaving Tehran. They held talks with President Aliyev on the phone in Baku. Aliyev said he believed that the republics in Central Asia would support the justified struggle waged by Azerbaijan's people to restore their country's sovereignty and territorial integrity and ward off Armenia's aggression. He also stressed the need to strengthen mutual cooperation between Azerbaijan and the republics of Central Asia and wished peace and security for these countries' peoples.
