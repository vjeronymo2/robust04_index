March 16, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

MCorp Estimates Big Loss: MCorp, the second-largest bank company in Texas and the latest to receive a federal bailout, estimated that it lost $903 million last year. The Dallas-based firm, which operates 26 banks throughout Texas, lost $213 million in the fourth quarter -- more than its $174-million loss for all of 1987, according to quarterly documents filed with the Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. But MCorp has been fighting to resurrect itself. In October, its management asked FDIC for $1 billion to supplement $400 million in private capital it lined up for a restructuring. The company has also raised $630 million by shedding assets.
