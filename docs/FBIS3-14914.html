Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Radio Slavyanka" program of the Russsian Defense Ministry] [Text] Radio Slavyanka has reported, quoting the VECHERNIY BISHKEK newspaper, that Kyrgyzstan's leadership is holding talks with NATO on providing the bloc with military bases on the territory of the republic. The newspaper wrote that when its correspondent approached the Kyrgyzstan Defense Ministry for an explanation, it was unable to either confirm or deny this report. During his official visit to Moscow, the republican President Askar Akayev clarified the situation in an interview with our correspondent. [Begin Akayev recording] This will never happen, because we were one of the first co-founders of the collective security council. We were one of the first five countries in Tashkent to sign the collective security treaty and the decision to set up joint CIS Armed Forces. So of course we will rely on the joint armed forces in the business of defending our security and ensuring peace in Kyrgyzstan, and indeed in all the countries of the CIS, on the territory of the whole of the former USSR. [end recording]
