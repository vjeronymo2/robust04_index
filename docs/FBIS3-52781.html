Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo 6 Jan (SUNA) -- Brigadier al-Tijani Khalifah, director of air operations at the Civil Aviation Authority, is currently on a visit to Cairo. Yesterday he stated that the Russian Antonov aircraft, which is on hire to certain quarters in Sudan, and whose Russian crew fled with it last week and landed at Luxor Airport, had been carrying foodstuffs belonging to the World Food Program from al-Ubayyid to Bahr al-Ghazal. In a news conference he held in Cairo, he said that the aircraft arrived in Sudan in accordance with a commercial contract with a Sudanese commercial aviation company, and that the Sudanese Government was not a party to the contract. Brig. al-Tijani Khalifah denied the claims of the aircraft crew, published by the Egyptian press, that Sudanese authorities had been holding the aircraft. He said: We in Sudan did not imprison any crew in Sudan. He pointed out that there were six sets of foreign crews for air transport in the country.
