920327 FT 27 MAR 92 / UK Company News: Lower oil prices hit Monument Oil Monument Oil and Gas, the independent UK oil and gas exploration company, announced a drop in net profits to Pounds 6.5m for the year to December 31 against Pounds 8.8m in 1990. Earnings per share slipped from 1.52p to 0.99p. Lower oil prices had affected the result, which at the pre-tax level fell to Pounds 6.24m (Pounds 8.2m). Mr Tony Craven-Walker, chief executive, said that the company had enjoyed considerable exploration success, increasing oil reserves last year by 50 per cent to some 120m barrels. The company said it would continue its policy of not paying a dividend in order to conserve capital to invest in its substantial oil and gas fields. Turnover was Pounds 38.6m (Pounds 33.4m).
