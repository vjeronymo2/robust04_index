BFN [Edict from Head of State Eduard Shevardnadze "on the State Coordination Commission for Promoting the Attraction and Realization of Foreign Investment and Credits"] [Text] The Georgian head of state has issued a edict to implement in an effective way priority programs and projects aimed at reviving the Republic of Georgia's economy by means of foreign investment and credits. The State Coordination Commission for Promoting the Attraction and Realization of Foreign Investment and Credits will continue its work with a new staff; its chairman will be Head of State Eduard Shevardnadze and its deputy chairman will be Otar Patsatsia, Republic of Georgia prime minister. The commission's senior secretary is Tengiz Geleishvili, the Georgian head of state's adviser on questions concerning foreign economic relations. The following export groups have been created within the State Coordination Commission for Promoting the Attraction and Realization of Foreign Investment and Credits: fuel and power, industry, agroindustrial, metallurgy, telecommunication systems, construction, health protection, science and technology, environmental protection complexes, and in the private sector sphere. It is pointed out in the edict that the Republic of Georgia Ministry of Economy along with the Committee for Foreign Economic Relations will examine measures stipulated in the anticrisis program and, proceeding from the interests of the republic's economy, will submit top-priority programs to the commission within two weeks; it is expedient to implement them with foreign investment and credits. To prepare a general plan of work, the heads of the export groups will elaborate within two weeks a calendar plan for examining investment projects in accordance with top-priority programs and will prepare detailed proposals concerning individual projects. [Signed] E. Shevardnadze.
