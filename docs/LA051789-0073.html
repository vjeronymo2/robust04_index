May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY DIGEST: COUNTYWIDE;

SUPERVISORS OPPOSE HEALTH SERVICES CUTS

In adopting a series of resolutions Tuesday, the Board of Supervisors formally opposed Gov. George Deukmejian's proposal to cut more than $485 million from health and welfare services in the state's 1989-90 budget.

Supervisors unanimously approved the resolutions calling for the restoration of funding that affects health-care programs for thousands of poor, disabled and elderly Orange County residents.

Hardest hit by the governor's blueprint are county programs serving low-income families and the medically indigent.

In recommending passage of the resolutions, Supervisor Harriett M. Wieder said the county must "stand firm and fight for these programs or many county residents will suffer."
