931029 FT 29 OCT 93 / Plans to deregister architects scrapped ARCHITECTS were claiming victory last night after the government climbed down over plans to end more than 60 years of statutory registration for the profession. The plan to deregister architects had caused deep resentment. Ending registration would have allowed anyone in the European Community to use the title. But following a campaign by the Royal Institute of British Architects, supported by the Royal Society and the National Consumers Council, the government has decided not to repeal the Architects (Registration) Act. The move was to be part of the government's deregulation bill. Baroness Denton, junior environment minister, said in a parliamentary answer that the government understood the strength of feeling against the removal of statutory protection of title. It now plans to examine measures which could simplify the profession's system of self-regulation. Dr Francis Duffy, president of the Royal Institute of British Architects, said: 'It is now important that we seize the opportunity to reshape the profession in a form that is more able to deal with current and future challenges.'
