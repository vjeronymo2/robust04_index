Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tamara Zamyatina] [Text] Moscow January 17 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin's top adviser academician Andranik Migranyan hailed the success of the U.S.-Russian summit meeting that was held in Moscow last week. Commenting on the achievements of the summit meeting Migranyan said that "the president of Russia admitted the need to seek a new architecture of European security, saying that the partnership for peace concept born within the NATO is not the only possibility." By this he indirectly admitted the need to establish such a system of European security in which all countries of eastern Europe would have equal rights in adopting decisions to maintain peace and stability, the academician said. Secondly, the U.S. side confirmed "Russia's special role in maintaining stability on the territory of the post-Soviet space," Migranyan said, referring to the U.S.-Russian-Ukrainian agreement to eliminate Ukrainian nuclear weapons. Additionally, "the U.S. President went further than making declarations and or giving advice, he concluded concrete agreements with Russia to open the American market to Russian goods and include russia into the GATT as an equal member," as well as integrate it into other international financial and economic structures. The academician also welcomed Clinton's decision to help defend the rights of Russian minorities in the Baltic states.
