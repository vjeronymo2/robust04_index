931217 FT 17 DEC 93 / MP raps MMC on 'perverse' rulings The chairman of the Commons all-party Treasury committee has tabled an early-day motion criticising recent recommendations of the Monopolies and Mergers Commission for failing to uphold the public interest, Roland Rudd writes. Mr John Watts, the Conservative chairman, yesterday attacked what he called the commission's recent 'perverse' ruling that the so-called 'complex monopoly' created by the perfume industry was not against the public interest. His motion, which has been signed by members of all three main parties, calls on the commission 'to be fearless in eliminating anti-competitive behaviour in the distribution of consumer products'. Mr Watts said he had in mind the commission's investigation into competition in the Pounds 800m-a-year ice-cream market. The commission is examining the widespread practice among manufacturers of supplying freezer cabinets free of charge to small retail outlets, on condition that the cabinets are not used to stock competitors' products. Mr Watts singled out Walls, part of the Anglo-Dutch Unilever group, as an example of an ice-cream maker which was using its muscle as a freezer supplier to discourage small retailers from supplying other ice creams. Mars, the US-owned food manufacturer, has repeatedly challenged Unilever's domination of the European ice-cream market in Brussels and in national courts. It started making ice-cream versions of its chocolate bars in 1989 and now has about 10 per cent of UK impulse sales, which are the fastest-growing part of the market. The all-party early-day motion on the commission says it 'regrets that a number of recent MMC recommendations have favoured current market structure at the expense of the consumer interest and therefore the public interest'. Mr Malcolm Bruce, Liberal Democrat industry spokesman, who signed the motion, said: 'This is a direct message to the MMC that they are too cautious and too conservative.'
