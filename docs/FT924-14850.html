921008 FT 08 OCT 92 / Gorbachev foundation thrown out of HQ in Moscow THE former Soviet president, Mr Mikhail Gorbachev, was yesterday stripped of the spacious Moscow-based complex which houses his research foundation, writes Chrystia Freeland in Moscow. The decree was issued by Russia's President Boris Yeltsin less than 10 hours after a leading Russian newspaper published a scathing interview with Mr Gorbachev in which he said his successor 'is clearly not coping with his duties'. Mr Yeltsin transferred the buildings to the Russian government's new Financial Academy, an institution charged with teaching banking and trading. The presidential decree directs the academy to allow Mr Gorbachev and the dozens of employees of his Gorbachev Foundation to use one of the buildings on the property if they can pay the rent. In yesterday's interview Mr Gorbachev described himself as a 'refusenik', presumably in reference to his refusal to testify to Russia's constitutional court on the issue of the illegality of the communist party and the government's subsequent ban on him travelling abroad.
