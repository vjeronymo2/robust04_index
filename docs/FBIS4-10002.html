BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 5 April. Venezuelan inflation reached 2.8 percent during March, according to a report issued by the Venezuelan Central Bank. The source has said the main contributor was household goods, followed by food, liquor, and tobacco. In the first three months of 1994, accumulated inflation was 9.3 percent, a slightly higher figure that that registered for the same period last year. (Hamburg DPA in Spanish 1442 GMT 2 April 94) Venezuelan oil officials have said oil production levels will remain unchanged. Following two days of discussions in Geneva, Erwin Arrieta, mines and energy minister, announced OPEC expects to counter falling prices. He also announced that Venezuela has nominated Alirio Parra as a candidate for secretary general and that the election will be held at the next OPEC conference. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 27 March 94 p-E2)
