Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The ruling body of the Crimean Communist Party made public a statement Friday saying that the assassination attempt against party leader Leonid Hrach was designed "to frighten the presidential candidate" and foil the presidential elections. Shots were fired on January 12 at the windows of Hrach's office. One of the party officials was sitting in Hrach's armchair at that time. By the latest reports, the bullets pierced the table but did not hit the official. The statement expressed its willingness to set up workers' self-defense units which would "within the framework of the law fight organized crime and stop the Mafia." The Crimean Interior Ministry's press service says, meanwhile, that the incident was an act of hooliganism rather than an assassination attempt.
