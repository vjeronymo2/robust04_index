931025 FT 25 OCT 93 / IRA blast hits main London-West railway line Up to 40,000 commuters faced disruption today after an IRA bomb at Reading damaged the British Rail main line between the West and London last night, police said. No casualties were reported. The blast, which followed a coded warning, came hours after the army disabled bombs at Reading and Basingstoke railway stations. BR laid on contingency plans for commuters if the route remained closed this morning. Guildford station was also shut after a warning, and a controlled explosion was carried out at Waterloo, but police said the item was not a bomb.
