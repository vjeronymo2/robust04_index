BFN [Text] Mr. Alpha Oumar Konare, president of Mali, arrived in Tehran a few moments ago and Mr. Habibi, first vice president, welcomed him. Here is our correspondent with a report: [Correspondent] Mr. Alpha Oumar Konare, Mali president, arrived in Tehran a few moments ago at the head of a delegation and was officially welcomed by First Vice President Mr. Habibi. On arrival in Tehran, the Mali president said he considers the expansion of friendly and fraternal relations with the Iranian nation and government to be very important and seeks to hold talks with Iranian officials on expansion of ties, as well as on regional and world issues. He added: The cooperation and mutual understanding between the two Muslim countries is most crucial under present world conditions and considering the new world order. Mr. Habibi, our country's first vice president, referring to the priority of ties with Muslim and African states in the Islamic Republic of Iran's [IRI] foreign policy said: Taking into consideration the ancient history of the state of Mali and the influence of Islam in that country, Iran wishes to expand its cooperation with that country and during the visit the expansion of economic and cultural ties will be discussed. The first round of talks between the two presidents of IRI and Mali will be held in Tehran tomorrow morning.
