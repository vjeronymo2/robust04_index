BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow June 10 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin will submit 17 most important bills to the State Duma (lower chamber of the Russian Parliament) in June. They are intended to help implement the social concord agreement. Their list was today handed out to Duma members. The package includes bills "On the Government of the Russian Federation", "On Combatting Organised Crime", "The Criminal Code of the Russian Federation", "On General Principles of State Power in the Russian Federation", "On Federal Executive Bodies", and also on the flag and coat-of-arms of the Russian Federation. The bill on the "Criminal-Procedural Code of the Russian Federation" will be the last to be submitted to the State Duma -- on June 30. The first ones to be discussed will be two bills: "On the Procedure for Adopting and Putting Into Force the Federal Constitutional Laws and Federal Laws" and "On Amendments to the Law on the State Border". The documents also include a bill "On the Right to Information". Yeltsin has instructed his plenipotentiary representative in the Federal Assembly to agree with the State Duma and the government on their joint work to finalise the bills, which the lower chamber of the parliament is to discuss. He also instructed the head of the presidential administration to appoint the people who will be personally responsible for drawing up the bills in conformity with the fixed plan.
