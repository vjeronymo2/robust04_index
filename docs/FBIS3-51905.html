Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] The Health Ministry and the PRC Pharmaceutical State Administration yesterday signed a protocol of intention with a view to promoting technical exchange in the pharmaceutical area. The Brazil news agency has reported that the document provides for, among other things, exchange of information in the areas of of raw material purchasing, final products, and the provision of technology. Health Minister Henrique Santillo said that the government has great expectations over the expansion of bilateral cooperation with the PRC. The protocol provides for training and consultation services by professionals of the two countries, and for the possibility of creating joint medical and pharmaceutical facilities in Brazil and in the PRC. The PRC mission, which is headed by PRC Pharmaceutical State Administration Director Huang Jive -- who signed the protocol on behalf of his country -- will stay in Brazil until tomorrow. A Brazilian mission made up of state laboratory directors and technicians from the National Health Control Secretariat will visit the PRC in April to observe the facilities of the PRC state company and to discuss the strengthening of the protocol terms.
