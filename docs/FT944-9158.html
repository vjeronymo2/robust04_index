941116 FT 16 NOV 94 / International Company News: Procter cuts disposable nappy prices Procter & Gamble, the US consumer products group, yesterday said it was raising the prices of some of its tissue products in the US by up to 8 per cent because of higher pulp prices, but simultaneously announced an 11 per cent cut in the price of its Luvs disposable nappies. The company also said it was turning Luvs into a unisex product instead of having different types of nappy for each sex. It said the resulting cost savings, combined with higher volumes flowing from the price cut, should have a positive effect on profits. Analysts say Luvs has about 13 per cent of the US disposable nappy market. Pampers, another Procter & Gamble product, has about 26 per cent, Kimberly-Clark's Huggies about 30 per cent, and the rest is taken by private label products. While Pampers and Huggies vie for the top end of the market, Procter & Gamble has been repositioning Luvs as a direct competitor for private label products by lowering its price. Yesterday's move was the latest in a series of price reductions. Procter & Gamble also said it was cutting the price of Pampers by about 2 per cent. Analysts said this was to prevent the differential between Pampers and Luvs becoming too wide. Kimberly-Clark is expected to follow suit because Huggies and Pampers have for several years sold at virtually the same price.
