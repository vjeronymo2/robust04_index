Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] The first round of official talks between the presidential delegations from Croatia and Romania led by Presidents Franjo Tudjman and Ion Iliescu took place at the Cotroceni Palace this afternoon. The major part of the discussions was devoted to an exchange of opinions about the current situation in former Yugoslavia and to ways and means to overcome the Bosnian crisis, bearing in mind the recent NATO decision and ways to encourage the achievement of a peace accord, which is the priority at this stage, Presidency Spokesman Ambassador Traian Chebeleu told the media. In this context, President Ion Iliescu greeted the first step toward the normalization of relations between Serbia and Croatia as an important element in seeking a peaceful solution on the territory of former Yugoslavia and expressed his readiness to support the peace process in general, both within the Geneva conference and through bilateral contacts with the other states created on the territory of former Yugoslavia. During the talks, the two sides also discussed bilateral relations and the determination of the two states to develop them. The exchange of opinion between the two presidents will also continue during the official dinner that President Ion Iliescu is to give in honor of the Croatian President Franjo Tudjman.
