BFN [Text] Cairo, 11 Apr (MENA)--Israeli Ambassador David Sultan announced today that the Palestinian and Israeli delegations are now discussing the remaining unresolved points pertaining to the implementation of the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles and the deployment of Palestinian police in Gaza and Jericho. He told reporters that the talks will deal with several security, civil, and legal issues pertaining to the Declaration of Principles. Sultan said that the Israeli delegation will return to Israel tomorrow, Tuesday, and if by that time no agreement is reached, then the talks will be resumed next week. He added that if the two delegations reach an agreement, it will be signed under the patronage of President Husni Mubarak. He explained that PLO Chairman Yasir 'Arafat and the Israeli prime minister will sign the agreement. An official source reported meanwhile that Rabin's visit to Cairo is due to take place after President Husni Mubarak returns from his tour of Japan and China but before the end of April at any rate.
