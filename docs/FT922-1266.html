920623 FT 23 JUN 92 / International Capital Markets: Fox and BMG form record company TWENTIETH Century Fox, the US entertainment group, and Bertelsmann Music Group (BMG), the US-based recorded music arm of Bertelsmann, the German media and entertainment conglomerate, are forming a joint record company, writes Alan Friedman. Financial terms related to the start-up of the new company, to be called Fox Records, were not disclosed. The venture calls for Fox to supply music from its films and television programming, while BMG will manufacture and distribute the records. ***** Charterhouse International Group, a New York-based investment operation owned by institutional investors in the UK and continental Europe, said yesterday that it was forming a new vehicle to invest in 'US-based companies that specialise in industrial waste management and environmental control', writes Nikki Tait. Charterhouse - which is owned Electra Investment Trust, the British Coal Pension Funds, Slough Estates, Charterhouse Finance Corporation and Merifin Charter Venture Partnership - said it would be the principal investor in the new venture, called Environmental Capital Group. ***** Abitibi-Price, the Canadian paper maker controlled by ailing property developer Olympia & York, is selling its US building products business as part of a strategy of focusing on newsprint and groundwood papers, writes Bernard Simon. The total purchase price is USDollars 100m, but the four divisions of the building products unit are being split between two sets of buyers. One group, led by Kohlberg & Co, the New York merchant bank, will buy the hardboard panelling, plastic moulding and hardboard siding operations. The millwork division is being sold to a New Jersey-based investment group led by Mr Alan Sitkin. ***** Mr Leon Charney, the property developer who is seeking to buy United Press International, the troubled news agency, said he would present an offer package amounting to Dollars 3.8m, writes Alan Friedman. The formal offer for UPI will be submitted to a bankruptcy court today. ***** News Corp's Fox Broadcasting Corp will not renew its contract with Turner Broadcasting System's Cable News Network, when it runs out at the end of this year, Mr Rupert Murdoch, the Fox chairman said, Reuter reports. ***** American Express, the US financial services group, yesterday dismissed Mr Richard Smith, a senior vice-president, in connection with an investigation into his outside activities during this time at the company, Reuter reports.
