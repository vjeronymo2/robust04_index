Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Georgiy Shmelev] [Text] Moscow, 6 Jan -- The Russo-U.S. consultations held in the U.S. capital were very successful; an agreement was reached on all of the main points of the program for U.S. President Bill Clinton's state visit and of the agenda for the summit meeting in Moscow, Georgiy Mamedov, the Russian Federation deputy foreign minister, believes. He has just returned here from Washington, where he discussed the program for the visit with Strobe Talbott, coordinator of U.S. policy toward Russia and the CIS countries. In Georgiy Mamedov's view, the Moscow forum will devote its main attention to problems of economic interaction between the countries, and examination of a set of questions linked with the implementation of the policy of reform in Russian society. On the international level, the sides will consider the strengthening of security and the settlement of regional conflicts, particularly in Bosnia and Nagorno-Karabakh, and also problems of nuclear nonproliferation and cooperation in arms control.
