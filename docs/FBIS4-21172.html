BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on regional narcotics developments monitored through 15 April. Colombia On 7 April, police seized 12 kg of coca paste near the border between Caldas and Risaralda Departments. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 8 Apr 94 p 16A) The Colombian Army captured 120 drug traffickers and confiscated more than 10 tons of cocaine from January to April, according to a Defense Ministry communique. The Army also seized 14,952 gallons of acetone, 11 tons of caustic soda, and three tons of cement found in clandestine laboratories. In addition, four tons of marijuana were seized and 15 hectares planted with it were razed. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2141 GMT 13 Apr 94) Narcotics police have cratered three clandestine landing strips in Caqueta Department. The airstrips were being used by Colombian, Bolivian, and Peruvian drug traffickers. (Madrid EFE in Spanish 1928 GMT 13 Apr 94) Costa Rica Police have recently disabled three domestic cocaine distribution networks, captured 10 drug traffickers, and confiscated more than 2 kg of cocaine and crack. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2015 GMT 13 Apr 94) Guatemala Counternarcotics agents have confiscated 20 kg of high-grade cocaine, valued at 2 million quetzales, and have arrested two Guatemalans and one Colombian in Guatemala City. (Guatemala City LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 13 Apr 94 p 8) Treasury Police have captured two people and located and destroyed a field planted with marijuana in Olopa, Chiquimula Department. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 11 Apr 94 p 24) Panama The Technical Judicial Police's Drug Secretariat has burned 795 kg of confiscated cocaine. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 15 Apr 94) The Customs Directorate has arrested U.S. citizen Norma Gibson and daughter Olivia Tarona Mahon after they were found to be in possession of 2 kg of cocaine worth $70,000. The directorate has seized more than 300 kg of cocaine so far this year. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 2247 GMT 11 Apr 94) Cristobal Fundora, director of the National Air Service, has reported that fields of marijuana have been spotted from the air in the areas of Bocas del Toro, Punta Burica, and the Perlas Archipelago. He added that all of these areas will be sprayed to wipe out the fields. (Panama City EL SIGLO in Spanish 14 Apr 94 p 12) Inspector Ramiro Jarvis, head of the Technical Judicial Police's Narcotics Division, has reported that 400 kg of cocaine have been seized so far this year. In 1993, the office seized approximately 5.45 metric tons. More than 14 metric tons of narcotics have been confiscated over the past two years. (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 14 Apr 94 p C6) Venezuela Street sales of narcotics have earned drug traffickers $520 million in the first quarter of this year. During the same period, police seized 2.5 metric tons of cocaine. Deputy Luis Emilio Rondon, head of the Counternarcotics Commission in the Chamber of Deputies, says this figure is alarming because in the same period in 1993, drug mafias only netted $140 million. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0008 GMT 14 Apr 94) Deputy Rondon has reported that more than 2.5 metric tons of cocaine and 765 kg of marijuana were confiscated in the first quarter of 1994. Rondon opened a debate on drugs and drug trafficking in Venezuela by drawing attention to the threatening magnitude of drug trafficking in the country. (Caracas EL UNIVERSAL in Spanish 13 Apr 94 p 1-13) Colombian citizen Mauricio Uribe has been arrested in Tachira department with 2 kg of cocaine found hidden in a suitcase. (Caracas Venezolana de Television Network in Spanish 1550 GMT 13 Apr 94)
