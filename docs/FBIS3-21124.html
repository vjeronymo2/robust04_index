Language: Japanese Article Type:CSO [Text] On 7 December JAERI disclosed the manufacturing status of the intermediate heat exchanger to be installed on the high temperature engineering test reactor (HTTR), which is a high temperature gas reactor research facility that JAERI is building at its O-arai Laboratory in Ibaraki Prefecture. At present the heat exchanger is about 55% completed and is scheduled to be transported to the construction site in September 1994. The HTTR heat exchanger is being built at Yokohama Plant No. 1 (Yokohama City) of Ishikawajima-Harima Heavy Industries. The device will transfer the heat from high temperature helium gas coming out of the reactor at 850-950C to a secondary helium gas system. The secondary helium gas will reach a temperature of 900C and become a heat source for thermal systems other than those used for power generation. These will be the highest temperatures in the world for a heat exchanger installed in a reactor to operate under. To assure durability under these high temperatures, the body of the heat exchanger has a two-layered structure, and the space between the inner and outer casings will be cooled by secondary helium gas flowing through pipes at about 400C. This design will help maintain the pressure-resistant outer casing at a lower temperature. The high temperature gas reactor uses [omission] as fuel, graphite as the core structural material, and helium gas for cooling. It offers advantages because high temperature heat can be obtained for a variety of uses in addition to power generation. Construction began on the HTTR at the research facility in March 1991, and work is proceeding with a target of attaining initial criticality in 1998.
