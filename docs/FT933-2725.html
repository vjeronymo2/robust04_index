930917 FT 17 SEP 93 / Sheehy defends police reforms SIR PATRICK SHEEHY today launches an outspoken attack on critics of his report on police reforms. In Police Review magazine he accuses Mr Paul Condon, Metropolitan Police commissioner, of raising the spectre of bureaucracy to oppose attempts to link reward with effort. The Police Federation, he says, is displaying a 'special kind of prejudice' in arguing that the recommendations would centralise power.
