BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 22 (KCNA) -- Yi Ki-taek, president of South Korea's largest opposition Democratic Party, at a press conference in Washington Wednesday said the position of his party was to oppose the imposition of "sanctions" on the North for nuclear inspection and find a peaceful solution to the nuclear issue, a foreign news report said. He said diplomatic means was the only way to ease the tensions on the Korean peninsula. He hoped the nuclear issue could be settled peacefully through DPRK-USA talks. He declared that the Democratic Party did not welcome the deployment of Patriot missiles in South Korea.
