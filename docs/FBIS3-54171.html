Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] A mammoth funeral was held in al-Qardahah today for President Hafiz al-Asad's son, Staff Major General Basil al-Asad, who died yesterday in a heartbreaking accident. The funeral procession was led by President Hafiz al-Asad. Attending the funeral were Egyptian President Muhammad Husni Mubarak, who arrived in al-Qardahah today, and his delegation comprising Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa, Defense Minister Muhammad Husayn Tantawi, and Usamah al-Baz, the director of the Egyptian president's office for political affairs. Also attending the funeral were Lebanese President Ilyas al-Hirawi, who led a high-level delegation comprising senior Lebanese officials, ministers, and religious figures; Nabih Birri, speaker of the Lebanese Chamber of Deputies, who led a large delegation of Lebanese parliamentarians; Lebanese Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri, who led a delegation of Lebanese ministers; Prince Hasan, Jordan's regent and crown prince, who arrived in al-Qardahah leading a delegation comprising former Prime Minister Zayd al-Rifa'i and Sa'id al-Tall, deputy prime minister and education minister. Other officials attending the funeral included: Prince Mut'ib Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz, Saudi public works and housing minister representing His Majesty King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz; 'Abd-al-'Aziz al-Dakhil, the Kuwaiti minister of state for cabinet affairs, representing Shaykh Jabir al-Ahmad al-Sabah, the amir of Kuwait; Habib Ahmad Qasim, Bahraini trade and agriculture minister, representing Shaykh 'Isa Bin-Salman Al Khalifah, the amir of Bahrain; Shaykh Muhammad Bin-Hamad Al Thani, brother of Qatari amir Shaykh Khalifah Bin-Hamad Al Thani, representing the Qatari amir; a delegation representing the Iranian Presidency and Foreign Ministry; the Arab agricultural ministers and heads of delegations attending the meetings of the ministerial council of the Arab Organization for Agricultural Development; and the Arab and foreign ambassadors accredited in Damascus. The funeral was also attended by senior party and state officials and tens of thousands of citizens who encircled the mosque in which the prayer for the dead was conducted.
