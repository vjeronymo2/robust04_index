December 4, 1989, Monday, P.M. Final

PEOPLE'S PHARMACY: FDA'S FOOT-DRAGGING ON ALZHEIMER'S DRUG IS PUZZLING

Question: My dad, who lives with me, has Alzheimer's. He had reached the point where he was disoriented most of the time, especially at night. He would wander off, and sometimes we'd find him miles from home. But he couldn't find the bathroom in the house and talked nonsense most of the time.

I managed to get some THA on the "black market" and since starting on it, he has improved so much that we are in shock! He is never disoriented now and his sense of humor and joy in family and life are back. It is truly an amazing difference.

Papa's doctor is monitoring him for elevated liver enzymes, but no problems have come up. I am angry that this medication isn't readily available, and that it costs so much the way we have to smuggle it in. When will the FDA allow us easier access to this lifeline?

Answer: That is a question we too have asked, but the Food and Drug Administration has failed to answer. Although most patients with Alzheimer's disease probably won't respond as dramatically to THA as your father has, there have been enough reports of improvement to warrant speedier action.

Three years have passed since Dr. William Summers reported his landmark study on THA for Alzheimer's patients. Since then millions of people have continued to suffer and deteriorate like your father did.

The FDA has a mechanism for allowing access to unapproved medications that seem promising for life-threatening conditions like AIDS or cancer. We don't understand why the agency has turned a deaf ear to the pleas of Alzheimer's victims and their families.

Q. My husband is a pediatrician and washes his hands between every patient. His hands get very dry and rough this time of year. Last winter he started using Lac-Hydrin lotion at the office, and that helped a lot.

He recently asked me to replenish his supply. To my astonishment, I discovered it is available by prescription only. Why is a moisturizer a prescription product?

A. Lac-Hydrin is also our favorite lotion for dry, cracked skin. The manufacturer has apparently positioned it as prescription only, even though the active ingredient (alpha-hydroxy acid) is also available at lower strength in over-the-counter products like LactiCare and Aqua Glycolic Lotion.
