Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuwait City, Feb 13 (AFP) -- The Kuwaiti cabinet will resign in mid-March and a new government will be formed, the Monday edition of AL-SIYASAH daily reported, adding that the country's leaders wanted to strengthen the executive. The paper, citing informed sources, said the resignation would occur after 'Id al-Fitr, the feast marking the end of the Moslem holy fasting month of Ramadan which began February 11 in Kuwait. Senior Kuwaiti officials want to "bolster the executive branch by unifying positions and speeding up the decision-making process," the paper said. AL-SIYASAH said the current government had accomplished its mission by reviving the 1962 constitution and parliamentary life. The outgoing 15-member cabinet was formed in October 1992 and is led by the crown prince, Shaykh Sa'd al-'Abdallah al-Sabah. Six ministers are independent MPs or opposition members. The key portfolios -- foreign affairs, interior, defense and information -- are held by members of the ruling Al-Sabah family. The opposition swept the legislative elections of October 5, 1992 leading the previous cabinet to resign two days later.
