940329 FT 29 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Roxboro beats flotation forecast with rise to Pounds 6.4m Roxboro, the Newmarket-based manufacturer of specialist electronic and electrical products, yesterday reported 1993 pre-tax profits of Pounds 6.4m, comfortably ahead of the Pounds 5.7m forecast when the group came to market in November. The pre-tax figure represented a 66 per cent increase over the Pounds 3.9m recorded in 1992. Turnover from continuing operations increased by 28 per cent to Pounds 41.9m with exchange rate movements contributing Pounds 3m. Export sales, mostly to original equipment manufacturers, grew by over 60 per cent and now account for 25 per cent of total sales. After plant closure costs of Pounds 500,000, offset by a Pounds 450,000 release from other provisions, operating profits worked through at Pounds 6.63m (Pounds 3.64m), including Pounds 445,000 (Pounds 65,000) from discontinued operations. BLP Components, the electromagnetic product business, boosted operating profits from Pounds 273,000 to Pounds 1.27m, on sales Pounds 2m ahead at Pounds 16.7m. The growth in the main came from export markets where sales increased by 47 per cent to Pounds 6.3m. Dialight, the US-based electro-optical business, raised operating profits from Dollars 6.8m to Dollars 9.8m (Pounds 6.7m) on sales which grew by 19 per cent led by continued growth in the Local Area Network market. The group ended the year with a Pounds 9.7m (Pounds 7.8m) order book which Mr Harry Tee, chief executive, said had increased further to Pounds 10.8m at the end of February. As a result of the flotation the balance sheet was considerably strengthened and the group ened the year with net cash balances of Pounds 4.7m after capital expenditure last year of Pounds 1.9m. Earnings per share amounted to 14.3p (13.2p). As forecast, there is no divi-dend. The shares, which were issued at 230p, closed 15p higher at 248p.
