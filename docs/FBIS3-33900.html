Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Rafiq Khalil al-Ma'luf report: "Qatar Officially Confirms Plan To Supply Israel With Gas"] [Text] Washington--Qatari Foreign Minister Shaykh Hamad Bin-Jasim Al Than announced yesterday that the development of Qatari-Israeli relations is linked with the scale of progress in the negotiations on the Syrian and Lebanese tracks. He denied that Qatar's policy toward Israel is aimed against any of its neighbors. However, he said that this policy is independent and that there are "differences between us and the other Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] states in this connection, but they are not major differences." The Qatari minister confirmed that he met with Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres last Sunday. He also confirmed that Qatar is making contacts with Israel regarding a natural gas deal, but it will not begin implementation before progress is achieved in the peace process with Syria and Lebanon. He indicated that Qatar may independently decide to abolish the second- and third-degree boycott of Israel if progress occurs in the negotiations. However, he associated the abolition of the main boycott with a decision from the Arab League. In a news conference he held yesterday after his talks with U.S. officials, especially with Secretary of State Warren Christopher, Shaykh Hamad said that Qatar has special interests in developing the peace process and making it a success. He expressed his hope that progress would be made on the Syrian and Lebanese tracks in 1994. He said: "We hope that the peace process will lead to the establishment of a new order and a new Middle East free from bloodshed...." He expressed satisfaction with the fact that the Palestinians and Israelis have sat together to resolve their differences directly and with support from the United States and Russia. He said that at the end of April Qatar will host a round of the multilateral negotiations and will receive Israeli diplomats like other diplomats. "And we feel that our people will welcome that, because the aim is peace and an end to bloodshed." Asked whether he had managed to conclude a gas deal with the Israelis in London, he said: "True, a meeting was held in London, but so far we have not concluded a gas deal...." He pointed out that the Israelis know well "Qatar's position and its terms, as we clearly said that we want progress to be made in the peace process on the Syrian and Lebanese tracks..." He added, "We are thinking about a deal, but nothing has been achieved." Asked whether his country "is playing with fire" with its neighbors in terms of its policy with Israel, the Qatari minister said that "it is exciting sometimes to play with fire. But this should be done safely. However, we do not believe that our relations with Israel are aimed against any of our neighbors. Qatar has good relations with Iraq and Iran and also with the other GCC states. Of course we have some problems with our neighbors, but it does not mean that we are doing things deliberately...." Asked whether Qatar consulted or coordinated with its GCC neighbors over its new policy toward Israel, he said: "It is our policy and I can only speak about Qatar...." He said that "we have differences with the GCC states, but they are not major differences, and as I said, it is our policy." Shaykh Hamad disclosed that he had discussed with Christopher the issue of Qatar's relations with Iran and Iraq and that he had told the U.S. secretary that "Iran is one of Qatar's neighbors. We clearly told the secretary of state that we wish to establish good relations with Iran...so that the opposite will emerge."
