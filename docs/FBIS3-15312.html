Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Report by Mikhail Rudkowski] [Text] Today at the Supreme Soviet session, as usual, people's deputies, devoted half an hour to addresses on miscellaneous topics on the agenda. Next, a discussion on the new draft constitution was resumed. In particular, the discussion focused on the status of the official language in the Republic of Belarus. The deputies adopted an article of the draft constitution, which provides Belarusian with the status of the official language, while Russian is confirmed as the language of interethnic intercourse [mizhnatsyyanalnyya znosiny]. Following the discussion, the deputies adopted Article 40 of the draft constitution, which provides the citizens of the Republic of Belarus with a guarantee of the right to work -- the most dignified way of man's self-realization -- that is, the right to choose a profession, job, and employment in accordance with personal ability, education, and vocational training as well as with due regard to social needs; it also includes the right to healthy and secure working conditions. The deputies also adopted Article 66 of the draft constitution, which stipulates the election procedure in our state, as well as Article 73 concerning a referendum in the Republic. A referendum, reads this article, shall be organized by the Supreme Soviet of the Republic of Belarus following a request by the Republic's president or by a body of no less than 450,000 citizens eligible to vote. Today's session proceedings positively resolved the question of changes and amendments to the basic law of our state. One more deputy chairman of the Supreme Soviet was also elected today. This honor was extended to People's Deputy Uladzimir Vasilyevich Rusakevich, deputy head of the executive committee of the Brest council of people's deputies. The session will be resumed at 1600 [1400 GMT].
