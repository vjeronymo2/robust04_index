BFN [Report by Rebeca Sanchez from the "Early Morning" newscast] [Text] In the past 17 months, 2,040 metropolitan policemen have been dismissed on charges of insolence, abuse of authority, physical aggression, intimidation, blackmail, and for soliciting bribes, reported General Rene Monterrubio, roads and safety secretary, when he appeared before the Federal District's Assembly of Representatives. [Begin Monterrubio recording] As a result of procedures for police justice during the past 17 months, police authorities approved 2,040 discharge or dismissal resolutions; 212 admonishment resolutions; 212 [word indistinct] charges; 1,209 arrests; and 105 admonishments [as heard]. [end recording] Commenting on his organization's achievements, Monterrubio cited this past April's creation of the Honor and Justice Council to fight impunity and improve discipline of the police corps. He said there have been 18,312 preventable crimes in the Federal District so far this year. He said his work as head of the Metropolitan Police is aimed at changing its image, performing a professional job, and consolidating an administrative structure capable of offering a better service to the community. [Begin Monterrubio recording] The police must represent a guarantee for the people's security and peace, not be the cause of insecurity and unrest. [end recording]
