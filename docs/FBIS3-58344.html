Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Frankfurt/Main (DDP/ADN) -- After seasonal adjustment, the money supply, M3, rose in November by 7.2 percent in comparison with the fourth quarter of 1992. This was announced by the Bundesbank in Frankfurt am Main on Friday [7 January]. In October the rate was 6.9 percent. Frankfurt's money traders reacted calmly to the announcement. The Bundesbank will adhere to its policy of relaxation of interest rates, it is said in banking circles. An economist with the Commerzbank said: "Hopes remain for an early reduction in base rates." The Bundesbank stressed that it is seeking growth in the money supply of between 4 and 6 percent in 1994. [passage omitted]
