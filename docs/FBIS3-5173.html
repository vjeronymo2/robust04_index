Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Prasong Sunsiri yesterday left for Manila, confirming that he would seek the Philippine government's view on Burma's attendance of an ASEAN meeting and also ways of enhancing trade between Thailand and the Philippines. Speaking to reporters shortly before his departure, Squadron Leader Prasong expressed confidence that the Philippines would have no objections to the idea of inviting Burma to the meeting of ASEAN foreign ministers in Bangkok in July. Singapore, Indonesia and Malaysia have affirmed that they would not object to Thailand extending an informal invitation in its capacity as host of the meeting. Deputy Foreign Minister Surin Phitsuwan is due to visit Brunei soon to sound out the opinion of the remaining ASEAN member state. But ASEAN officials have made clear that a formal invitation would require more progress in Burma and more discussion among ASEAN members. Foreign Minister Prasong said he would also seek Manila's views on what will be the first meeting of the ASEAN Regional Forum -- planned for the six ASEAN states, their seven dialogue partners and five others, including China, Russia, Vietnam, Laos and Papua New Guinea. Bilaterally, Sqn Ldr Prasong said he would discuss ways of enhancing economic relations during talks with Philippine President Fidel Ramos. Sqn Ldr Prasong noted that the current volume of trade was relatively small -- about 8,000 million baht. He suggested that counter-trade might be one way of stepping up this activity. Sqn Ldr Prasong yesterday cochaired the first meeting of the Joint Commission for bilateral cooperation in trade, investment, agriculture, science and technology and tourism. He is scheduled to return from Manila tonight.
