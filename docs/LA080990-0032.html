August 9, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

CHANGING THE ENDANGERED SPECIES ACT

In reading the remarks of Fein and Meese I did not know whether to laugh or cry. My God, they're serious!

If it prevents us from making money, drive it into extinction. If we do not immediately recognize some pragmatic benefit to ourselves, let it slip into oblivion. Put it in a zoo! We've got lumber to cut!

By the beginning of their second paragraph they reveal why they hold the environment and all "lesser species" in such disdain. They equate "economic growth with human well-being." Can't breathe? Stay indoors and turn on a fan. Can't drink the water? Have a beer. Fein and Meese ask who needs spotted owls, and what is so important about red squirrels? What anemic thinking! What a display of impoverishment of the soul.

Since 1968 I have been a Republican. In June I registered as a Democrat. As environmental spokesmen for the Bush Administration, Meese and Fein offer two of the clearest reasons why I have done so.

RICHARD DUNKERLY

Whittier
