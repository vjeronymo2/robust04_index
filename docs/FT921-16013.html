920107 FT 07 JAN 92 / World Commodities Prices: Tea Only fair demand at generally lower rates, reports the Tea Brokers' Association. Landed Assams came to a selective market and prices for medium kinds declined by 10p to 15p. A few of the brightest east Africans sold at firm rates but brighter mediums lost 3p to 8p with the exception of pekoe dusts which were an improved feature and remained firm. Offshore, good demand. East African teas tended easier but central African description sold readily at fully firm levels. The highest price realised this week was 168p for a Rwanda pd. Quality: 150p/kg, nom good medium 115p/kg, medium 96p/kg, low medium 78p.
