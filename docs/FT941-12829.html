940128 FT 28 JAN 94 / Warning made on anti-semitism A group of leading religious figures yesterday said that anti-semitism was 'alive and literally kicking' in Britain, and called for a change in the law to protect religious minorities. The call came in a report for the Runnymede Trust, a body that monitors trends in social affairs, by a commission that included Rabbi Julia Neuberger and the Rt Rev Richard Harries, bishop of Oxford. The report says that between 1984 and 1992 there was an 85 per cent increase in anti-semitic incidents reported to the synagogues' Board of Deputies.
