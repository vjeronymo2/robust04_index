Italy, Luxembourg, Portugal, UK. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------- Leading cross-border retailers operating in Europe ----------------------------------------------------------------------- ALDI Europe's largest discount grocery retailer. More than 3,800 stores in Germany, Austria, Netherlands, Belgium, Denmark, US, France, UK, Poland. Turnover 1992: DM 22.6bn (estimated), more than 40 per cent outside Germany. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- CARREFOUR France's largest quoted food retailer. 11.1 per cent of the national market. More than 500 stores in France, Spain, Brazil, Argentina, Taiwan, Portugal, US. Turnover 1992: FFr 117.1bn, more than 30 per cent outside France. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- AHOLD The Netherlands' largest retailer (36 per cent of Dutch grocery market), and one of the top ten largest food retailers in the US through ownership of four chains. 600 supermarkets in the Netherlands, and more than 500 stores in the US. Turnover 1992: DFl 22.32bn, more than 50 per cent from abroad. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- PROMODES France's second-largest quoted food retailer, 10.2 per cent of French market. More than 4,000 hypermarkets and supermarkets in France, Spain, Germany, Italy, Portugal, Greece, US, Taiwan. Turnover 1992: FFr 84.2bn, almost 40 per cent from abroad. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- TOYS R US The biggest toy retailer in the US, with more than 20 per cent of US market. Operates 581 stores in the US and 234 overseas, including Canada, UK, France, Austria, Germany, Spain, Japan. Turnover 1992: Dollars 7.2bn. ----------------------------------------------------------------------- MCDONALDS World's biggest fast-food restaurant chain, with more than 16 per cent market share in the US. More than 14,000 restaurants in 70 countries - especially Canada, US, UK, France, Germany, Australia, Japan - nearly 80 per cent owned by franchisees. Stores outside US have doubled to 4,700 in six years. Turnover 1993: Dollars 24bn (1992 Dollars 21.8bn). ----------------------------------------------------------------------- IKEA World's widest-spread international retailer of furniture and furnishings. Operates more than 120 stores in 24 countries. Turnover, year to August 1993: DFl 7.8bn. 75 per cent outside Sweden -----------------------------------------------------------------------
