920801 FT 01 AUG 92 / Inquiry into BNL officials dropped A Rome magistrate has dropped an investigation of two former senior officials of BNL, the Italian bank whose Atlanta branch granted Dollars 2.8bn (Pounds 1.46bn) of unauthorised loans to Iraq, AP reports from Rome. Judge Vittorio De Cesare ruled that no irregularities had been committed by the two former officials as well as four other employees of Banca Nazionale del Lavoro in connection with the case, the ANSA news agency said. BNL chairman Merio Nesi and director general Giacomo Pedde resigned in August 1989 after US authorities discovered the loans. The US congress charged that the money helped finance Iraqi arms purchases. BNL maintained the loans were for food and industrial machinery.
