BFN [Text] Before he met Radovan Karadzic in Pale, Yasushi Akashi met Alija Izetbegovic, leader of the Bosnian Muslims, in Sarajevo. Through Eurovision exchange, we received brief statements by Akashi and the leader of the Bosnian Muslims after their meeting in Sarajevo in the morning. [Begin recording] [Akashi in English with superimposed translation in Serbo-Croatian] The attack stopped as of 1800 yesterday. However, we have to work first on a greater UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] presence. A larger number of armored vehicles should be sent and General Rose will go there very soon -- tomorrow or the day after tomorrow. At the same time, we are determined to work on a general, global cease-fire, not only the one between Muslim and Croatian sides, but also including the Serbian side, so as to establish basis for a real, lasting, and global peace. [Izetbegovic] As regards a general cease-fire, we are of the opinion that one should place the surrender of heavy weaponry in the center of the question. In our opinion, there will be no peace without that. [end recording]
