BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 31 May. Approximately 25,000 enterprises in Zulia State have been thrown into a recession because of the latest fluctuations of the bolivar. One business sector representative explained that the dollar auction mechanism is hurting Venezuelan businessmen. He said inflation and the lack of imported supplies are two short-term consequences of the instability in the exchange rate. He predicted there may be long-term mass layoffs and company closures as a result of the economic decline. (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 0200 GMT 22 May 94) Energy and Mines Minister Erwin Jose Arrieta has announced Venezuela will obtain an additional $120 million as a result of higher prices on the international oil market. He explained that higher oil prices resulted in $70 million in additional revenues last month. Arrieta attributed the increase in oil prices to temporary conditions, such as repairs in North Sea oil facilities, Nigeria's domestic situation, the conflict in Yemen, and increased consumption by industrialized nations. (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 26 May 94) Venezuelan exports to Andean Group countries increased by 40.6 percent in the first quarter of the year, going from $195 million in the first quarter of 1993 to $275 million in 1994, according to estimates by the Cartagena Agreement Board. According to the figures, Venezuela replaced Colombia as the largest exporter in interregional trade. The estimated value of interregional trade amounted to $698 million. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 27 May 94 p E-7)
