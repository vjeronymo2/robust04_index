BFN [Text] On 25 April, the government held a meeting of the "Council for Promotion of Processing Market Opening Complaints" (generally known as the Ombudsman Council and led by Chairman Yoshio Ogawara), which is composed of civilians who are non-government officials, to discuss how to deal with 26 cases of complaints connected with the standards and approval system. At the meeting, participants confirmed that actions had been decided on 24 cases. Among actions to be taken are: Deregulation on activities of lawyers specialized in foreign legal affairs; changes in food product labels to have the expiration date indicated; and improvement of customs procedures for handling imported cargoes after business hours. Regarding safety standards of helicopters and specifications of water supply equipment, it was decided that from the standpoint of promoting imports, the council will continue to study the possibility of easing standards.
