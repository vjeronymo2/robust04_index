Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Jose Marcano from "The News" newscast] [Text] In accordance with a Democratic Action [AD] National Executive Committee decision made during a Monday morning meeting, more than 2,000 members will be expelled from the party. The expulsion will include members who supported other political organizations during the recent campaign and those who were candidates of political parties with which AD had no alliances. The National Executive Committee also appointed a commission to conduct a political analysis of the departments to prepare for the national convention. As for the new government, AD Organization Secretary Lewis Perez reiterated that his party will support all things deemed positive for the country, even though National Convergence and Movement Toward Socialism voted against AD leader Carmelo Lauria in the Chamber of Deputies. [Begin Perez recording] The AD will support any action or decision made by President Rafael Caldera that will benefit the country. We are going to remain critical of and follow closely the implementation of the government program proposed by Caldera during the recent campaign. [end recording] It was also revealed that the National Executive Committee will hold a special meeting this week for members to review the country's fiscal and economic situation and establish a solid position on the measures to be implemented by Dr. Rafael Caldera.
