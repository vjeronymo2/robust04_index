BFN [Text] Prague April 20 (CTK) -- The Czech Government today approved a supplement to the payments agreement between the Czech Republic and Slovakia eliminating the old account, Deputy Premier Josef Lux announced. Payments on the old account of the trade payments clearing system served for settling transactions concluded between the two countries before the currency division on February 8, 1993 and were effected at an exchange rate of one Czech to one Slovak crown. Lux added that the government at the same time agreed to discuss a new agreement between both republics, under which non-government liabilities arisen before the currency division would be transferred to the current clearing account, used for the payment of liabilities arisen after the currency division at the rate valid on February 8, 1993 -- 34.5 crowns (1.15 USD) to one ECU. State payments from the old account would continue to be paid at the one to one exchange rate. According to data provided by the Czech Republic, Czech companies owed 2 to 3 billion crowns (70-100 million USD) at the end of the first quarter and Slovak companies 12 to 14 billion crowns (400-460 million USD).
