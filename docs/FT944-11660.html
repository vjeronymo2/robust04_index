941104 FT 04 NOV 94 / International Company News: Cigna buys back former S Africa subsidiary Cigna became the first big US insurer to re-open for business in South Africa with the announcement yesterday that it had bought back its former subsidiary in the country, writes Richard Waters in New York. The subsidiary was sold to its employees in 1987 following the imposition of sanctions against the country by the US government, and by state and local authorities. The subsidiary, which was renamed Concord Insurance Company of South Africa in 1987, will take back the Cigna name.
