Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "NTC News" newscast] [Text] Two guerrilla attacks hit the outskirts of Bogota. In Pasca in the southeast and in Guasca in the northeast the Simon Bolivar National Guerrilla Coordinating Board [CNGSB] attacked toll booths, communications antennas, and police stations. Just hours ago rebels from the 22d Front of the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia [FARC] launched simultaneous attacks against police stations in Aguaditas and Pasca and a toll booth in Alto de San Miguel, Cundinamarca. At 2130 [0330 GMT] 40 subversives from the Juan de La Cruz Front took positions in houses. They shot rockets and hurled grenades at the Aguaditas police station. For two hours four policemen faced the attack. [Begin unidentified policeman recording] They arrived in a pickup truck and began shooting. Fortunately, my comrades and I did not panic and were able to face the situation. As you can see, we came through it without injuries. [end recording] Meanwhile, another small group of the 22d FARC Front dynamited the Alto de San Miguel toll booth. [Begin unidentified employee recording] They arrived at about 2100 and made us move our personnel away from the building, where they were collecting the toll payments. Then they blew up the installations. [end recording] The authorities also reported that 60 rebels destroyed the Pasca police station. Seven policemen held off the attackers for eight hours.
