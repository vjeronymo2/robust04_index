Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, March 2 (BTA) -- Bulgarian Minister of Justice Petur Kornazhev expressed his satisfaction with his three-day formal visit to Greece paid at the invitation of Greek Minister of Justice Yeoryios Skouvelakis. PASOK's [Panhellenic Socialist Movement] Cabinet stands firmly in favour of closer contacts with Bulgaria, Mr. Kornazhev told a BTA reporter upon his return to Bulgaria. He also said that Greece is expecting a visit by Bulgarian Prime Minister Lyuben Berov in a two week's time. Mr. Kornazhev told this reporter that he and his host identified a common understanding on the judicial problems of the European Union. The two discussed a variety of issues, including the conditions in Bulgarian and Greek jails, problems pertaining to the execution of death penalty and the specificity of the Balkans. Mr. Kornazhev and Mr. Skouvelakis reportedly considered a bilateral agreement on transfer of convicts which has to be further deliberated before being signed. Speaking with his Greek hosts, Mr. Kornazhev stressed that Bulgaria is supportive of a policy of active neutrality in the Balkans and that it sees no controversial issues concerning its borders with Greece. The Bulgarian minister of justice said he also met Greek Interior Minister Akis Tsokhatzopoulos.
