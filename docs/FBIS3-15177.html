Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Georgiy Shmelev] [Text] Moscow, 2 Mar -- Vitaliy Churkin, the special representative of the Russian president and deputy Russian foreign minister today met Radovan Karadzic, the leader of the Bosnian Serbs, who arrived in Moscow on Monday [28 February] at the Russian side's initiative. During the course of talks on problems of a Bosnian settlement at the Russian Foreign Ministry, Karadzic spoke about the Bosnian Serbs' consent to the opening in the near future of the airport at Tuzla under the control of and with the participation of international observers in order to supply humanitarian cargoes to the town. At the same time, Karadzic noted, the Bosnian Serbs proceed from the fact that Russian observers will be taking part in controlling the non-military use of the airport. Also examined in Moscow were questions of the implementation of the Russian president's initiative on holding a summit meeting in the Russian capital with the purpose of arriving as quickly as possible at an agreement on peace, an all-embracing settlement to the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina, and the lifting of sanctions against the FRY. At a regular working meeting between the Russian president's special representative and Karadzic, specific measures for implementing the accords and proposals of other countries for discussion at a session of the UN Security Council were examined.
