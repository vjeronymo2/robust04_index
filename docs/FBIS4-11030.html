BFN [Excerpt] Buenos Aires, 19 Apr (NA) -- Central Bank President Roque Fernandez today said the interest rate increase in the United States is going to cause an increase in the local interest rates on deposits, and he believes that during the rest of the year there will be "ups and downs in the markets as a result of the U.S. Federal Reserve's policy." The banker indicated that the increase in the U.S. interest rate is going to cause an increase in the interest rates on deposits, and "this is an incentive to save, something that is not bad." "It is bad when the active rates on loans increase," he said, and explained that monetary authorities are studying "how this incentive on the passive rate will favor savings." The measure the Central Bank has under study is a decrease in the spread [preceding word in English], in other words, banking costs, "so the active interest rate will remain where it is."
