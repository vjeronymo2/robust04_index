BFN [Text] Defense and Security Minister Edi Sudrajat considers the purchase of 39 former East German warships at $1.1 billion expensive because the cost could be reduced to $300 million. The Indonesian Navy's estimate of $300 million for the warships covers the cost of the warships, which also includes repairs, voyage expenses, and expenses for port facilities. During his briefing in Jakarta today, Minister Edi Sudrajat urged the finance minister to reconsider the current $1.1 billion allocation because previous finance department allocations for the warships had been based on the Indonesian Navy's estimates of $300 million. Minister Edi Sudrajat said the Navy had taken into consideration the age of the warships, which were constructed for war purposes.
