July 3, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

U.S. ADMINISTRATION SUSPENDS DIALOGUE WITH ARAFAT'S PLO

Bush says it's time for peace in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict; Arafat agrees, Abul Abbas disagrees, Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir disagrees.

Bush says Israel shouldn't raise tensions by allowing Soviet Jews to live in the occupied territories; Arafat agrees, Shamir disagrees.

Abul Abbas makes news by staging and attack on Israeli soil; Bush is angry, Arafat is angry, Shamir is angry.

Bush cuts off talks with Arafat; Shamir is happy, Abul Abbas is happy.

The winners: the real terrorists. The losers: all Palestinians and Israelis who want peace.

MICHAEL EMERY

Santa Monica
