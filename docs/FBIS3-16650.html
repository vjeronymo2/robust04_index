Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Yelena Tregubova report under the "Project" rubric: "State Duma Deputies Intend To Legitimize `Russia's Peacemaker Activities'"] [Text] Yesterday, the State Duma passed in the first reading a draft federal law "On the Procedure for the Provision of Russian Federation Military and Civilian Personnel for the Participation in Operations to Maintain or Restore International Peace and Security, and in Other Types of Peacekeeping Activities." According to Defense Committee figures, at the moment, one Russian battalion of 1,200 men is on the territory of Yugoslavia, four battalions numbering 1,778 men are in Moldova, one regiment of 503 men is in Georgia, and one division of 5,982 men is in Tajikistan. In addition to these military formations, according to committee chairman Sergey Yushenkov, 87 Russian military observers are taking part in UN peacekeeping operations. Georgiy Kondratyev, Russian Federation deputy defense minister, told parliamentarians yesterday, however, that 16,500 Russian soldiers are present in the so-called "hot spots" today. Interestingly, General Kondratyev urged that on no account "a limit be set" on the permitted participation of Russian Federation servicemen in such actions. The presenters of official reports agreed that "peacekeeping forces" should be withdrawn from the jurisdiction of Russia's Armed Forces and subordinated to the Ministry for Emergency Situations. It was decided to forward the draft federal law to the president, the deputy factions, the groups and commissions "for the preparation of proposals and remarks."
