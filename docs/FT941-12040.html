940201 FT 01 FEB 94 / International Company News: Canadian pulp group cuts loss to CDollars 143m Canadian Pacific Forest Products, the pulp and paper group, reduced its operating loss to CDollars 143m (USDollars 109m) in 1993 from CDollars 190m in 1992. After special items the loss widened to CDollars 286m or CDollars 4.88 a share, compared with a deficit of CDollars 248m or CDollars 4.82 in 1992. Sales were little changed at CDollars 1.8bn. The fourth-quarter loss was CDollars 160.5m after special items, against a loss of CDollars 59.5m a year earlier on sales little changed at CDollars 466m. The group is spinning off its paperboard business into a new company, St Laurent Paperboard, with annual sales of more than CDollars 300m. CPFP will get CDollars 240m from a recent financing in February. Dominion Textiles, a Canadian-based international textile producer, turned in net profit of CDollars 7.8m or 13 cents a share after special items in the six months ended December 31. This compared with CDollars 10m or 21 cents a year earlier, on sales of CDollars 624m, against CDollars 631m.
