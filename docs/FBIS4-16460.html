BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Igor Ignatyev and Sergey Oleynik] [Text] St. Petersburg April 18 TASS -- Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin pledged adherence to market reforms and predicted that inflation may be curtailed to 7-8 per cent by the end of the year if the budget drafted for 1994 is approved by the State Duma. "The course towards reforms, financial stabilisation and economic restructuring remains the backbone of the Russian economic policy", Chernomyrdin told a meeting of the governors of the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development which opened in the second biggest Russian city on Monday. He recalled that in the first quarter of 1994 inflation was below the 10 per cent level and predicted it would be further curtailed to monthly 7-8 per cent by the end of the year "if the planned parametres of the budget are preserved". The Russian State Duma gave an outline approval to the draft budget, but demanded that the government introduces certain corrections to it.
