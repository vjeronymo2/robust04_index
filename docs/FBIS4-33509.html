BFN [Report by Juan Pritsiolas] [Text] President Guillermo Endara is planning to announce this week an amnesty that will benefit 578 former officials of the former regime, according to official sources. The list includes 75 former members, 510 former corregimiento representatives, former President Eric Arturo Delvalle; Juan B. Sosa, former ambassador to the United States; and Vice President-elect Gabriel Altamirano Duque [figures as published]. The case of the 510 representatives exists because they appointed General Manuel Antonio Noriega as chief of government. The dossiers against Delvalle and Sosa are related to the investigations the second circuit prosecutor is conducting on alleged irregularities in the handling of $10.8 million from the funds the United States froze on Panama. The case against Tomas Gabriel Altamirano Duque concerns charges made by Omayra Correa, Panama City mayor-elect. Concurrently, the assembly is debating an amnesty for political crimes. Legislator Raul Ossa has said the individuals in line to receive these benefits should only be those accused of violations against state security; otherwise, this amnesty would leave a door open to benefit those being investigated for common crimes. Meanwhile, Miguel Bush, legislator from the Democratic Revolutionary Party, has wanted to add an article to the draft amnesty so that it would exclude those charged with homicide and embezzlement. A judiciary source has said the dossier against the former members of the General State Council includes: Manuel Solis Palma; Manuel Antonio Noriega; Francisco Rodriguez; Rodolfo Chiari De Leon; Jorge Ritter; Orville Goodin; Rene Bultron; Juan Bosco Bernal; Elmo Martinez Blanco; Darien Ayala; Cesar Martans; Jose Renan Esquivel; and Ricardo Bermudez. It also includes Nander Pitty; Celso Carrizo; Carlos Villalaz; Olmedo Sanjur; Pascual Gonzalez; Virgilio Mirones; Rafael Arosemena; Azael Purcait; Jaime Simons; Francisco Sanchez Cardenas; Alejandro Vernaza; Aida de Gartner; Alberto Luis Tunon; Dario Fernandez; Dario Gonzalez; Carlos Orillac; Benjamin Colamarco; Tomas Noriega; Rafael Castrellon; and Gilberto Tunon. Other former members of the General State Council are: Angel Jaen; Mario Luis Pitti; Luis Blanco; Cesar Montenegro; Bernardo Dominguez; Leovigildo Herrera; Euclides Tejada; Elvia de Ortiz; Dagoberto Lara; Ana Villa de Flores; Carlos Estrada; Veyra Remon; Victor Levy; Victoria de Robolt; Marcos Hughes; Jose Arauz; Jose Rodriguez; Marcos Mariscal; Alberto Arjona; Fermin Herrera; and Eufemia Laguna. Also included in the dossiers are Julio Shaik; Jorge Bristan; Mateo Castillero; Arturo Diez; Augusto Valderrama; Aminta de Guillen; Nilson Espino; Hidalgo Fung; Abdiel Juliao; Eduardo Marin; Rafael Moreno; Paulino Romero; Jorge Tason; Aurea Torrijos; and Ligia Castro.
