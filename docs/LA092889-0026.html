September 28, 1989, Thursday, Valley Edition

CHOPPING A BOTHERSOME, BEAUTIFUL TREE

I am writing this letter to the sound of a chain saw hacking away at a beautiful 35-foot palm tree that only moments ago was chopped and then fell to the ground with a deadening bang.

You see, the lady next door was "bothered" by this 40-something-year-old beauty in her front yard. It had, in fact, bothered her for a long time, and so she finally ordered it to be chopped down, hacked up and removed. She had this done, by the way, at 9 on Sunday morning -- a day when most neighbors enjoy sleeping in, a day free from work, free from the noise of traffic and construction. . . . But today, many of us were awakened in horror and watched in amazement as this tree was destroyed.

I wonder why it's so easy for a person to "take a contract out" on a tree, simply because it bothered them? This tree represented no danger to any person, home or telephone line. Does my neighbor not realize that trees allow us to breathe purer air, a point of concern of any Los Angeles residents?

When a person orders a tree to be cut down, isn't there an organization that should intervene and determine if there is indeed a sound reason for that removal, and if not, prevent this from happening? We expect large corporations to replant the trees they take for industry. Should this not be enforced on a individual scale as well?

Yes, the tree was on her property, but it seems to me that this planet is all of our "property" and we should be held responsible for its health, yes, even in the case of one tree on Tujunga.

I am a reasonably concerned environmentalist, not a fanatic. But this morning as I witnessed a defenseless, majestic tree lying in the middle of the road while its trunk and limbs were being severed, I felt pained and hurt. Thanks to my neighbor, I am becoming a fanatic.

SUSAN ANGELO

Studio City
