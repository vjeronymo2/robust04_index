BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on insurgent activities in Peru compiled from Peruvian media monitored through 26 May. On 18 May, Army personnel killed Augusto Enrique Gutierrez, a Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement, MRTA, member as he tried to escape. He and four other "suspicious persons" were arrested in the Centro Gavilan zone, Perene District, Chanchamayo Province, Junin Department. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 20 May 94 p A13) Col. Eduardo Mendoza Rodriguez, chief of the Cajamarca Police Subregional Directorate, reported that on 19 May 15 repentant "terrorists" showed up at the Cajamarca Police headquarters. They operated in Cajabamba, Cajamarca, and San Marcos Provinces. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 21 May 94 p A12) An Army infantry battalion has captured 23 Shining Path, SL, members of the so-called "Red Sea" column, including top leaders, who were trying to avoid capture in Upper Huallaga. The troops also arrested MRTA leader Hermenegildo Murillo Alejos, aka Comrade Alejos, who perpetrated many attacks in the Upper Huallaga and the Maranon Valley. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 22 May 94 p 54) On 23 May SL "terrorists" kidnapped and tortured an Army soldier during a clash with a military patrol in the Pasto Grande village, Monzon District, Leoncio Prado Province, Huanuco Department. His remains were found 12 hours later. The 30 SL "terrorists" who were captured on 5 April are being held at Los Laureles Army Unit. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 25 May 94 p A13) Counterterrorism Directorate personnel arrested two SL "terrorists" identified as Diodoro Romero Quispe, 39, aka Gringo, and Marcelina Coro Silva, 37, aka Marcia. Their daughter confirmed that SL special command members met at their house to arrange for planting car bomb outside the Lima Prefecture and the U.S. Embassy. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 25 May 94 p A14) The SL "Red Sea" battalion ambushed a military patrol in Puerto Maria, 3 km from Cachicoto, Huamalies Province, Huanuco Department. A civilian guide was killed and two soldiers were seriously wounded. The Huallaga countersubversive front commander reported that the "subversive" battalion was reorganized and currently comprises 60 members. They are trying to recover the so-called Bolson Cuchara, the last SL stronghold in this part of the central jungle. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 26 May 94 p 31) The police arrested Clemencia Ramos, aka Comrade Maria, a member of the South-Medium SL regional committee, and two other alleged SL "subversives" in a downtown street of Huamanga, Ayacucho Department. She came to this city to reorganize local operations and carry out a wave of attacks on 18-19 June. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 26 May 94 p A11) The Departmental Antiterrorism Division has arrested seven "terrorists" during operations in many shantytowns of El Callao. They perpetrated dynamite attacks against government institutions in Junin and Ayacucho Departments and in the central mountain range. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 26 May 94 p A11)
