921126 FT 26 NOV 92 / Parliament and Politics: Treasury hints at economic decline THE TREASURY is near to admitting that Britain's long-term potential to produce goods and services has been damaged by the lingering recession, Treasury officials indicated last night, Peter Marsh writes. Giving evidence to the Commons Treasury and Civil Service committee, Professor Alan Budd, the Treasury's chief economic adviser, also suggested that unemployment was likely to continue rising through next year. It would be 'many years' before the government's finances returned to surplus. The new assessment by Treasury officials of the longer-term effects of the recession is the gloomiest indication that the Treasury has given so far into the economic decline that Britain faces deep-seated economic problems. Mr Chris Riley, the Treasury official in charge of medium-term policy analysis, said it was 'quite possible' that in the budget next March the Treasury would revise down its long-term projections about the potential of the economy.
