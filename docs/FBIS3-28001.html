Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 2 KYODO -- Some senior officials of the Social Democratic Party (SDP) [of Japan] [SDPJ] on Wednesday [2 February] urged that the party quit the seven-party coalition government of Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, SDP sources said. At a meeting of the party's policy-making Central Executive Committee, the officials expressed strong opposition to a government proposal to rename the consumption tax to "welfare-purpose" tax and raise the rate from the present 3 percent to 7 percent, the sources said. Among the opponents was Shun Oide, a party vice chairman, the sources said. The Hosokawa cabinet, launched last August, includes six ministers from the SDP, the largest of the seven ruling parties. They are Transport Minister Shigeru Ito, Construction Minister Kozo Igarashi, Home Affairs Minister Kanju Sato, National Land Agency Director General Kosuke Uehara, Economic Planning Agency Director General Manae Kubo and Sadao Yamahana, minister in charge of political reform.
