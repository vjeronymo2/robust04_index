Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava Jan 7 (CTK) -- Slovak President Michal Kovac, in his statement on the problem of ethnic minorities in Slovakia published in the independent daily SME today, stressed that it was in the interest of Slovaks, ethnic Hungarians and other nationalities and ethnic groups in Slovakia to seek to prevent irresponsible politicians from southern Slovakia from artificially creating a conflict situation. Kovac's statement calls for dialogue and rejects the expressions of nationalism that appeared in a Hungarian-language daily on the eve of a meeting of representatives of the Hungarian minority in Komarno convened by the Association of Towns and Villages of Zitny Island. Although Kovac resolutely rejected as unconstitutional a proposal to create a special province in southern Slovakia, at the same time he supported as justified demand that the new territorial administrative division of Slovakia not discriminate against the Hungarian minority.
