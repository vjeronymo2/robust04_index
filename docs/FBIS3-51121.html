Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Accra, 10 Jan (PL) -- Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina arrived today for an official visit to Ghana during his first tour to five African countries since his appointment in April last year. The Caribbean island's foreign minister was welcomed at Kotoka International Airport by his Ghanaian counterpart, Obed Asamoah, and Juan Carretero, Havana's ambassador in Accra. Interviewed by local media, Robaina said one of the goals of the trip is to explain the domestic situation in Cuba and changes being implemented in the economic sector. He said his country's government is interested in knowing about the situation in Ghana, a country with which it maintains a fluid trade which stands out within the context of the so-called Black Continent. Referring to the Latin American country's planned foreign policy for 1994, the head of Cuban diplomacy said it must be different, because the situation so demands. The key is to have the ability to exist in a constantly changing world and to ensure that our views are known at the international level, he said. The foreign minister said that despite predictions by the international media in 1993, his country is living, working, and has opted to counter the adverse situation it is facing with many efforts, without sacrificing sacred achievements, such as in health and education. Referring to this, he said 1993 should go down in history as the most difficult year for the revolution, which was born in 1959, but 1994 is the beginning of an upward trend. During his 48-hour stay in Accra, Robaina will meet with Ghanaian President Jerry Rawlins; Justice Daniel Annan, parliament leader; the foreign minister; and Richard Kwame Peprah, energy and mines minister and chairman of the Cuban-Ghanaian Mixed Commission. He will also meet with members of various friendship associations and the Cuban ambassadors to Cape Verde, Guinea-Bissau, Nigeria, Benin, and Burkina Faso. The foreign minister and his delegation will leave on Tuesday for Angola, the second phase of his two-week tour, which also includes Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Namibia.
