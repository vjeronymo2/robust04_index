941012 FT 12 OCT 94 / East Timor talks offer Guerrillas opposing Indonesian rule of East Timor have declared a unilateral ceasefire in an effort to persuade the Jakarta government to hold face-to-face talks on the future of the former Portuguese colony, Peter Wise reports from Lisbon. In a recorded message broadcast by Portuguese radio, Mr Konis Santana, commander of the small East Timorese guerrilla force, said the ceasefire began on October 1 and would be made permanent if Indonesia agreed to negotiate and to release the resistance fighters' jailed leader, Mr Xanana Gusmao.
