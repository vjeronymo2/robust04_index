941019 FT 19 OCT 94 / International Company News: Far Eastern Textile sharply higher Rising prices for synthetic fibres and share sales sent profits sharply higher at Far Eastern Textile, Taiwan's largest textile manufacturer, during the first nine months of the year, writes Laura Tyson. The company reported pre-tax profits of TDollars 1.949bn (USDollars 75m) for the year to September 30, against TDollars 608m during the same period in 1993. Turnover surged to TDollars 17.757bn from TDollars 14.834bn. The company said income was boosted by TDollars 700m in sales of shares in other companies and higher prices for Far Eastern's products. Prices for man-made fibres had climbed 20 per cent in the last year.
