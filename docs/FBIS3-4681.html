Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Sozaburo Okamatsu, deputy minister of the Ministry of International Trade and Industry [MITI], held a news conference at the Japan Press Club in Uchisaiwai-cho, Tokyo, on the afternoon of 28 February. Touching on the Japan-U.S. economic friction at the news conference, Mr. Okamatsu said: "For Japan, the biggest issue is to achieve the economic growth driven by domestic demand." He also indicated that Japan needs to adopt a new macro policy to cut its current account surplus in addition to the four-point measure including deregulation under consideration by the government to open the Japanese market. Referring to the automobile and auto parts sector on which both Japan and the United States have negotiated under the Japan-U.S. framework talks, the MITI deputy minister said: "The time has come for Japanese enterprises to make their own judgment to address trade imbalance between Japan and the United States." In this way, he urged the Japanese enterprises to work on their own to boost imports.
