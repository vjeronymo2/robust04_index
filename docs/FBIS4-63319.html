BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Viktor Zhilyakov] [Text] Krasnodar May 20 TASS -- Regional heads of North Caucasian republics attending a conference in Baku have appealed to the Russian president, asking him to lift sanctions against Abkhazia. Participants in the conference believe that lifting economic sanctions will step up the process of the settlement of the conflict in the region. Head of the republics of Adygeya, Dagestan, Ingushetia, Kabardino-Balkaria, North Ossetia, Karachayevo-Cherkessia, the Krasnodar, Stavropol, Rostov regions and South Ossetia took into account that after signing agreements with the participation of the Russian Federation conditions for normal and stable life are being created in the region. A tendency for the restoration of cultural and economic ties has been growing. This question was not included in the agenda of the conference and was raised in the course of discussions, journalists were told after the conference since they were not admitted. The conference was attended by Russian Minister for Affairs of Nationalities and Regional Policy, Nikolay Yegorov, who answered journalists' questions. Participants adopted an address to President Yeltsin in connection with his message to the people of the Northern Caucasus on the occasion of the 130th anniversary since the end of the Caucasian war. The address says that the people of the Northern Caucasus accepted Yeltsin's message with gratitude, believing that by the mercy of fate the Russian president put the last stop to the Caucasian war.
