BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 19 May. On 28 April, Industrial Development Association President Pedro Lizana reported that industrial production grew 9.2 percent in March as compared with the previous month. Cumulative production rose 6.4 percent in the year's first quarter; the beverage industry registered the highest increase, with 26.5 percent, and chocolate and candy production rose 11.8 percent. (Santiago LA TERCERA DE LA HORA in Spanish 29 Apr 94 Economic Section p 3) On 3 May, the National Statistics Institute, INE, reported that the April inflation rate was 0.5 percent. The cumulative inflation rate has been 3 percent so far this year, and 12.7 percent in the past 12 months. Clothing increased 4.4 percent; housing 0.7 percent; transport 0.2 percent; while food dropped 0.5 percent. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 4 May 94 Economic Section p 7) The INE reported that the unemployment rate was 5.2 percent from January to March, an increase of 0.1 percent as compared with the same quarter last year. Nationwide, the labor force totalled 5,257,050 people; and the number employed was 4,985,220. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 4 May 94 Economic Section p 7) The Central Bank reported that the trade balance registered a $160.9 million surplus in the first half of April, with exports at $522.7 million and imports at $361.8 million, raising the cumulative surplus to $295.6 million. (Santiago EL MERCURIO in Spanish 10 May 94 pp B1-B5) On 9 March, the Central Bank reported that the foreign debt was $19.799 billion through 31 March, an increase of $619 million compared with the cumulative figure registered in December. (Santiago ESTRATEGIA in Spanish 10 May 94 p 9)
