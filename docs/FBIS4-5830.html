BFN [Text] The Governments of Indonesia and Bosnia-Herzegovina have officially established diplomatic relations at the ambassadorial level, but the appointment of ambassadors will be decided later. The communique to establish diplomatic ties was signed by Nugroho Wisnuwurti, Indonesian permanent representative to the United Nations, and Bosnian Vice President Ejup Ganic at UN headquarters in New York on Monday [11 April] afternoon. The communique said the establishment of diplomatic ties was in response to the two countries' desire to strengthen friendly ties between the two governments and peoples based on equality and mutual respect for each country's sovereignty, integrity, and independence.
