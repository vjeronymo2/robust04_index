BFN [Text] A communist movement in the Philippines, the National Democratic Front [NDF] celebrated its 21st anniversary, Sunday, but reports saying the rift within the communist movement is expanding as more statements were issued by factions in observance of the group's anniversary. A spokesperson for two breakaway communist factions in Mindanao in southern Philippines said groups who oppose the leadership of exiled communist leader Jose Maria Sison have been branded by forces loyal to him as counter-revolutionaries. Ike de los Reyes, speaking for NDF Central Mindanao and the Moro Revolutionary Army said, members of the breakaway groups now fear liquidation not only by the military but also by former comrades who still acknowledge Sison's leadership. Europe based critics of Sison also said a statement declaring they now represent the real NDF indicating that the rift have extended to the continent. Sison is himself exiled in the Utrecth, Netherlands.
