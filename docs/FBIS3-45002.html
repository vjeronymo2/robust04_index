Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 12 (XINHUA) -- Authorities in Harbin have burned some 500 kg of fake tiger bones, Deputy Forestry Minister Shen Maocheng said here today. He said the fakes had been confiscated by 30 local law-enforcement officers during a week-long crackdown on the illegal trade in tiger bones and rhinoceros horns last September. China has strictly banned transaction, transport and use of rhinoceros horns and tiger bones, which were used in making traditional Chinese medicine.
