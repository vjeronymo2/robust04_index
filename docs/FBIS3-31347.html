Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [News conference by Vitaliy Churkin, Russian deputy foreign minister, after his talks with Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic in Belgrade on 16 February; all questions and answers are in English with superimposed Serbo-Croatian translation -- recorded] [Text] [Churkin] President Milosevic and I spoke mostly about Sarajevo and other issues concerning a political solution for Bosnia-Herzegovina. I expect to see him again later today or tomorrow. [Unidentified reporter] Are you being successful in your diplomacy? [Churkin] I think that I am, but, to be more serious, it is too early to speak about success. We are looking at all the aspects of the situation around Sarajevo. Having been to Sarajevo yesterday, yes, I am satisfied. The situation on the ground is much better. There is peace in the city. It is fragile but is nevertheless peace. We are doing everything that we can to make it a firmer and more lasting peace, not only in Sarajevo, but throughout Bosnia.
