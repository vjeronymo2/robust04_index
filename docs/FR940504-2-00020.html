Federal Energy Regulatory Commission [Docket No. ER94&hyph;1174&hyph;000] Florida Power Corp.; Filing April 28, 1994. Take notice that on April 20, 1994, Florida Power Corporation (FPC) tendered for filing an agreement between itself and Oglethorpe Power Corporation pursuant to which FPC will sell to Oglethorpe 50 MW of peaking capacity and energy in the months of June through September 1997 and 275 MW of peaking capacity and energy in the months of June through September 1998. FPC proposes to make the Agreement effective 60 days after filing. FPC states that copies of its filing have been served upon Oglethorpe and the Public Service Commissions of Georgia and Florida. Any person desiring to be heard or to protest said filing should file a motion to intervene or protest with the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, 825 North Capitol Street, NE., Washington, DC 20426, in accordance with Rules 211 and 214 of the Commission's Rules of Practice and Procedure (18 CFR 385.211 and 18 CFR 385.214). All such motions or protests should be filed on or before May 12, 1994. Protests will be considered by the Commission in determining the appropriate action to be taken, but will not serve to make protestants parties to the proceeding. Any person wishing to become a party must file a motion to intervene. Copies of this filing are on file with the Commission and are available for public inspection. Lois D. Cashell, Secretary.
