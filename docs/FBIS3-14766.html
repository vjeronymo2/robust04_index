Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] [Announcer] On Friday, at the Latvian Foreign Ministry, there was a press briefing about Latvia's developing relations with Israel and the Middle East, as well as Latvia's attitude to Ukraine's concepts of foreign policy. On 14 January this year in Moscow, the presidents of Ukraine, the United States, and Russia issued a statement that the problem of nuclear weapons located in Ukraine is for the whole world to solve. But on 3 February, Ukraine's Supreme Council adopted a resolution to accede to the Lisbon Protocol. The press secretary of the Latvian Foreign Ministry, Ivars (?Embrekts), pointed out: [Begin Embrekts recording] The Foreign Ministry of the Republic of Latvia believes that such action by the president and Supreme Council of Ukraine demonstrates to the world community Ukraine's goodwill and serious intention to promote the disarmament process, and that it is a significant investment in ensuring peace and world security. We hold in high esteem Ukraine's stance in the sphere of nuclear disarmament, and (?in wishing to regard) Ukraine as a (?powerful) and friendly country, Latvia supports the view of the Ukrainian president, that in our modern world a country's security is dependent not only, and not even so much, on its military potential, as on the successful development of its economy. Latvia (?believes in) Ukraine's state consolidation and economic prosperity. The Foreign Ministry has the conviction that the position of the Ukrainian president and Supreme Council on the issue of nuclear disarmament will not only strengthen peace and international security, but will also assist Ukraine to overcome its economic and internal policy difficulties. [end recording]
