930225 FT 25 FEB 93 / Observer: Loadsa dough A hundred paper millionaires at a Taiwanese flour-mill are wishing they'd been faster on the draw. Last pay day, their wages were multiplied by 10,000 by the computer crediting their bank accounts, upping their half-month's earnings to nearly Pounds 3m apiece. Before most of them got their hands on it, however, the Bank of Taiwan spotted and corrected the computer's mistake. And even though a dozen managed to draw out some of the unexpected riches, it was apparently not enough for them to retire on. The bank says it is confident of recovering the erroneous payments because it has arranged for the mill to fire the recipients if they don't repay the cash.
