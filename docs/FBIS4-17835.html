BFN [Text] Speaking at a news conference held in Lvov on 25 April, People's Deputy Yaroslav Kendzior said that after holding talks with a number of Ukrainian lawmakers with different political views, Supreme Council Chairman Ivan Plyushch, and President Leonid Kravchuk, he came to the conclusion that "presidential elections will, most probably, take place." According to Kendzior, Plyushch categorically insists that the elections be held in June, while Kravchuk "still cherishes hope that the elections will be annulled or postponed." Kendzior also pointed out that since President Kravchuk's recent visit to the United States, "certain circles in that state have had an interest in him and will help him become president of Ukraine."
