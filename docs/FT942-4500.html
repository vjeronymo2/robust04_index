940609 FT 09 JUN 94 / UK Company News: Chemring at Pounds 2.85m as overheads rise Chemring, the maker of anti-missile chaff, distress rockets and waterproof clothes, yesterday reported reduced profits in the six months to April 1, writes Caroline Southey. The pre-tax figure fell by 2.3 per cent to Pounds 2.85m (Pounds 2.92m) on increased turnover of Pounds 24m (Pounds 21.3m). Operating profits rose by 5.3 per cent from Pounds 2.96m to Pounds 3.12m after a Pounds 173,000 charge this time for 70 redundancies, mainly at Pains-Wessex, the distress signals maker. There was also a 16 per cent rise in other overheads to Pounds 4.49m (Pounds 3.87m) following the acquisition of Alloy Surfaces in the US. The pre-tax figure was struck after losses at associated undertakings of Pounds 100,000 (Pounds 57,000) and net interest charges of Pounds 161,000. The interim has been lifted by 8 per cent to 3.24p, compared with 3p, from earnings per share which fell from 9.69p to 9.35p basic.
