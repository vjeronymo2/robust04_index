BFN [Unattributed report under the "In Brief" rubric: "Energy Debt Mounting"] [Text] A situation close to disastrous has taken shape in Kazakhstan's fuel and energy complex. Its imported fuel debts have topped $117 million. Reporting this, the Republic's business newspaper PANORAMA gives the list of Kazakhstan's energy creditors, which are Kirgizia [Kyrgyzstan], Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, and Russia. The lion's share of the debts falls on the Uzbek side: Kazakhstan owes it more than $100 million for the use of natural gas and electricity.
