August 7, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

HELMET BILL VETO

I applaud Gov. Deukmejian's veto of the mandatory motorcycle helmet bill. The proposed helmet law embraces the philosophy that it is better to force the judgment of the majority on the individual than to grant that person the freedom, dignity, and occasional mistakes that all come as a package deal with being truly human.

Furthermore, as long as the state reserves the right, at its own discretion, to draft a man and send him off to end up face down in a rice paddy somewhere with his brains blown out, it has no business telling him he must wear a helmet when he rides because otherwise he might get hurt.

JOEL VOELZKE

Tujunga
