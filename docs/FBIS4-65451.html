BFN [Excerpt] Madrid--Deputy Foreign Minister Yeoryios Papandreou was interviewed on Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM] by the Spanish newspaper ABC. According to the MACEDONIAN NEWS AGENCY, Papandreou said that "he is not very sure of the outcome of the economic measures against Skopje," but added that there is new movement surrounding these negotiations. He expressed the hope that this could result in a solution to the problem. According to the MACEDONIAN NEWS AGENCY, the deputy foreign minister expressed the hope "that a way to break the impasse of the talks will be found." He added: "For now, we are proposing a package of measures to strengthen mutual trust and to reexamine the name issue." Another point, Papandreou said, is that Greece is not afraid that it may be led to a military clash with Skopje. He stressed, however, that a clash with any side could cause war to spread.
