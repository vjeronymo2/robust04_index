Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Gennadiy Kulagin, Nikolay Pakhomov and Yuriy Sidorov] [Text] London March 21 TASS -- A meeting between President of Kazakhstan Nursultan Nazarbayev, who arrived here on an official visit, and British Prime Minister John Major ended after signing a number of important bilateral agreements in London today. The documents included a joint declaration on friendship and mutual understanding, an agreement on cooperation in the spheres of education, science and culture, a joint declaration on economic cooperation, a convention on avoiding double taxation, an agreement on air transport and a declaration on cooperation between foreign political departments. In honour of the esteemed guest a guard-of-honour was presented and Kazakhstan's national anthem was played in the British Foreign Office yard. Summing up the results of the talks, John Major said that discussions were held on questions of investment in Kazakhstan's economy, and also on steps to increase turnover. The head of the British Government noted that the development of cooperation in these spheres will help good relations established between Great Britain and Kazakhstan. He emphasised that his government will explore opportunities for the implementation of the understandings already reached. The British prime minister accepted Nursultan Nazarbayev's invitation to visit Kazakhstan. He also highly appreciated Nazarbayev's initiative to call a conference on steps to increase trust and security in Asia. This question was comprehensively discussed at the meeting, and the foreign ministers of the two countries are continuing consultations on this topic, John Major added.
