BFN [Text] Beijing, May 12 (XINHUA) -- A Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman said here today that China appreciates Japanese Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata's attitude toward the history of Japan's aggression against China, saying that his attitude is sensible and is in accordance with the trend of history. Commenting on the letter from Japanese Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata to Chinese leaders here this afternoon at the weekly press conference, the spokesman, Wu Jianmin, said that on May 10, Japanese Ambassador to China Kunihiro Michihiko conveyed to Chinese Vice-Foreign Minister Tang Jiaxuan a message from Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata to Chinese Premier Li Peng on the remarks made by former Japanese minister of justice, Shigeto Nagano, in which Nagano distorted the history of Japan's aggression against China. "We have taken note of the profound introspection expressed by Prime Minister Hata that Japan's aggression and colonial rule in the past have inflicted unbearable suffering on many peoples," Wu said. In the letter, the Japanese prime minister promised that history shall be treated correctly and the young generation shall be educated with the correct historical views and determined to prevent the past unfortunate history form being repeated. "This attitude of Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata is sensible and is in accordance with the trend of history. We appreciate that," said the spokesman.
