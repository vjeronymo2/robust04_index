BFN [Text] Cairo, 18 Jun (MENA) -- Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa has denied that a meeting between the Yemeni parties will be held in Cairo tomorrow, Sunday. In a statement he made today, he said that UN envoy to Yemen Lakhdar Ibrahimi will leave Cairo for New York tomorrow and that there are no agreement on any specific proposals or steps. Musa added that what Egypt is demanding and insisting on is a cease-fire and the disengagement of forces in Yemen. He stressed that the opportunities for protecting Yemen's unity will decrease the longer the fighting continues. Regarding the situation in Rwanda, Musa said that the United Nations has decided to send troops there, and noted that France has also taken an initiative on this issue. He added that Egypt is bound by whatever the African countries decide.
