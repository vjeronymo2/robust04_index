Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, December 31 (XINHUA) -- The international bidding for exploitation of the East China Sea oilfield has ended, with nine large foreign companies selected, according to an official from China National Offshore Oil Corporation (CNOOC). He said that these companies, including Texaco, Chevron, Exxon, Shell and Japan Petroleum Exploration Co., Ltd. (Japex), have signed 18 contracts and agreements, involving a total investment of 300 million U.S. dollars. On June 30, 1992 CNOOC called for tenders to explore a part of the East China Sea covering 72,800 sq km. Some 70 oil firms from 19 countries entered the bidding. The official said that 18 out of 20 zones have been sold, with an area of 64,246 sq km. Experts forecast a total of up to four billion tons of oil and natural gas in the area.
