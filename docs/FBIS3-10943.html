Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] The State Council has touched on the most sensitive issue for multinational oil companies in Colombia by ruling that those companies must leave when their contracts expire. Guido Nule Amin, energy and mines minister, fears that the decision will scare away foreign capital. The State Council's decision to eliminate the possibility of automatically renewing grant contracts signed with Colombia fell on the foreign oil companies like a bucket of cold water. [Begin Shell official Luis Enrique Conde recording] Shell has asked the Colombian Government to extend its contract for 10 years beginning in 1994. Considering the points we have mentioned, Shell expects the government to grant the extension. [end recording] Guido Nule Amin, mines and energy minister, believes the ruling jeopardizes foreign investment in Colombia. [Begin Nule Amin recording] The disadvantage that the ministry and the government see in this ruling is that it weakens the relationship with and the expectations of foreign investor companies. [end recording] Five contracts signed between Ecopetrol and the multinational corporations Esso, Texpet, Jocol, and Petronor, which extract crude oil in Lower, Middle, and Upper Magdalena and in the Catatumbo Basin, will expire this year. Ecopetrol will take over all installations and oil fields now in the hands of those companies.
