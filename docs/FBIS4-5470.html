BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA April 11 -- British Minister of State Alastair Goodlad left Vietnam today concluding his five-day visit here as guest of Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam. He was accompanied by representatives of a number of UK corporations working in banking, finance, petroleum, construction, electricity and air service. While here, the British delegation was received by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet, Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam and Vice Chairman of the People's Committee of Ho Chi Minh City Nguyen Van Huan. It also held talks with Deputy Foreign Minister Nguyen Dy Nien and had working sessions with Minister of Finance Ho Te, Minister of Transport and Communications Bui Danh Luu, Minister of Energy Thai Phung Ne, Vice Chairman of the State Planning Committee Tran Xuan Gia and Deputy Governor of the State Bank Chu Van Nguyen. The two sides in those meetings hold that Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet's visit to the United Kingdom in last June has ushered in a new chapter of cooperation between the two countries. The Vietnamese side highly appreciated the British Government's efforts to promote its cooperation with and investment in Vietnam, and expressed its readiness to discuss with the British side measures to develop the bilateral ties to a new level. The British side highly valued the achievements recorded by the Vietnamese people in their renovation as well as in their implementation of the open foregn policy which aims to diversify and multilateralize Vietnam's relations with the international community. It expressed its wish to cooperate with Vietnam in the fields of banking, finance, construction of infrastructure, and so on. The two sides agreed that they would exchange views and discuss more about projects in the above-said fields. An agreement on avoidance of double taxation and a letter of exchange on research strategy and maintenance of the system of national highways and provincial roads in Vietnam were signed on this occasion. During its stay here, the British delegation also visited the Hoa Binh hydroelectric power plant.
