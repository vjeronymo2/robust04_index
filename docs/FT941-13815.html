940124 FT 24 JAN 94 / Midlands business warns on recovery Business leaders in the Midlands have warned Mr Kenneth Clarke, the chancellor, that the tax increases taking effect in April could choke economic recovery unless they are balanced by a cut in interest rates. Mr Bryan Townsend, chairman of the West Midlands regional council of the Confederation of British Industry, said: 'The anticipated cut in interest rates is necessary to make sure that the tax increases don't dampen down consumer confidence.' Mr Geoff Nicholls, chairman of the East Midlands CBI, said that if the new taxation had the effect of cutting the public's confidence then it would halt recovery. Although the business chiefs agree that there is steady growth in the economy, in the east Midlands there is concern about the rise of sterling to around DM2.60. This provides, in Mr Nicholl's view, further justification for a cut in interest rates. 'Our worry is primarily that, if we keep them high, the pound will stay high.'
