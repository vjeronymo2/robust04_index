Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Simferopol, 16 Feb -- On 16 February, the KRYMSKAYA PRAVDA [Crimean Truth] newspaper published an interview with President of Crimea Yuriy Meshkov. In particular, he noted that confidential meetings with Russian government officials had taken place during the Crimean delegation's visit to Moscow. According to Meshkov, issues were discussed that are to be resolved in the near future: The supply of additional gas to the peninsula and the allocation of grain and agricultural produce. The Crimean president also stressed that the issue of opening an official mission of the Russian government in the Republic of Crimea had been examined. Meshkov confirmed that Yevgeniy Saburov, the well-known Russian economist, had agreed to occupy the post of deputy prime minister of the autonomous republic. All of Russia is behind Saburov. All Russian Federation officials have approved his decision, stated the Crimean president. He also reported that the deputy prime minister would be forming the Cabinet of Ministers of the autonomy.
