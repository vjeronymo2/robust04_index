BFN [Text] The Council of Ministers resumed its extraordinary meeting this afternoon under Acting Prime Minister Dr. Muhammad Sa'id al-'Attar to examine UN Security Council [UNSC] Resolution 924 adopted in its session No. 3,386 held on 1 June. After an exchange of views, the Council expressed its appreciation for the resolution, which was based on the provisions enshrined in the UN Charter, particularly the Republic of Yemen's sovereignty, independence, and territorial integrity. The following is the text of the resolution: In the name of God, the merciful, the compassionate. Text of UNSC Resolution No. 924 of 1994, unanimously adopted by the UNSC at its session No. 3,386 held on 1 June 1994. Having examined the situation in the Republic of Yemen; Taking into account the purposes and principles of the UN Charter; Extremely concerned over the tragic death of innocent civilians; Appreciative of the efforts under way by the Arab League, the Gulf Cooperation Council, the Islamic Conference Organization, neighboring countries, and other concerned countries to contribute to a solution to the conflict through peaceful means and ensure the achievement of peace and stability in the Republic of Yemen; Realizing that continuation of the situation there can jeopardize peace and security in the region, the UNSC: 1. Calls for an immediate cease-fire. 2. Urges an immediate halt to the supply of arms and other equipment that could contribute to the prolongation of the conflict. 3. Reminds all those concerned that political disputes cannot be resolved by force, and urges them to immediately return to the negotiating table so that they may resolve their disputes by peaceful means and reestablish peace and stability. 4. Calls upon the UN secretary general to dispatch a fact-finding mission to the region at the earliest possible opportunity to assess the prospects for renewed dialogue among all concerned parties so that they may make additional efforts to resolve their disputes. 5. Urges the UN secretary general to submit a report on the situation at the appropriate time, but within a period not exceeding one week as of the completion of the fact-finding mission. 6. Decides to keep the issue under practical consideration.
