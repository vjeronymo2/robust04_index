Language: Malay Article Type:BFN [Text] Datuk Sri Anwar Ibrahim, deputy prime minister, today pledged to the people of Sabah that the Barisan Nasional, BN, Government will deliver on the promises it made during state elections campaigns. He said that this would include the introduction of a rotation system for the appointment of any leader to the state chief minister's post, and to set up a university in Sabah. Datuk Sri Anwar, who is now in Honolulu to attend the meeting of Asia Pacific Economic Cooperation, APEC, finance ministers, has extended his congratulations to Tan Sri Sakaran Dandai, chairman of the Sabah BN, on his appointment as new chief minister of Sabah. The deputy prime minister also expressed the hope that the new state government in Sabah would not fail to achieve its goal to set up a clean and just administration.
