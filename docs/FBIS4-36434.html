BFN [Text] Tehran, June 8, IRNA -- Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias said here Wednesday [8 June] that talks between Iranian and European officials would help remove any obstacle in the way of relations between Iran and the European Union (EU). Greece is now trying to improve Iran-EU relations and help them have "good" ties, the Greek foreign minister said in a joint press conference with his Iranian counterpart 'Ali Akbar Velayati. He departed Tehran after the conference. The minister expressed hope that the present impediments to the relations between some European countries and Iran would be removed. Referring to the Bosnian issue, Papoulias said despite problems facing Europe, it is now trying to solve the issue. However, he admitted that the question has remained unsolved for europe. Papoulias, terming the Bosnian issue as a European problem, maintained that the issue could be solved through peaceful means and that there would be no military solution. EU member states and the permanent members of the United Nations Security Council in the latest meeting on the Bosnian crisis reached the conclusion that the Bosnian issue could be resolved solely through negotiations between the warring sides. Velayati termed as "positive" Papolias talks with Iranian officials which he said focused on international issues, especially relations between EU and the Islamic Republic of Iran and on growing ties between Tehran and Athens . On the issue of human rights, Velayati said that Iranian officials, based on their Islamic beliefs and ideology, believed that human rights should be respected by all countries. He added that the recent meeting of the foreign ministers of the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) in Cairo approved that human rights should be observed by the third world countries with regard to their beliefs and ideologies. Papoulias, who arrived here on Tuesday for a two-day official stay at the head of a high ranking delegation, conferred with president Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani and his iranian counterpart 'Ali Akbar Velayati. Papoulias and Velayati in two rounds of talks discussed expansion of Tehran's relations with the EU and Greece, the crisis in the Balkans and issues relevant to Central Asian and Caucasus republics.
