Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] No radical government reform is planned, First Vice Premier Oleg Soskovets said in a statement in Moscow on Tuesday following "huge media interest" in the subject. He said any government reshuffle was a routine process which should not been seen as a change in the reform policy. Soskovets said the functions of his commission for routine economic management were widened. It will now monitor the rational use of raw materials, transport safety and prices and tariffs for electricity, fuel, oil products and railway freight. The commission is also responsible for issuing export quotas for vital raw materials and products.
