BFN [Excerpts] Strobe Talbott, the U.S. deputy secretary of state, began a one-day visit to Slovakia this morning. [passage omitted] This morning, Talbott met President Michal Kovac. Agatha Stanekova reports. [passage omitted] [Begin Stanekova recording] The first point on the agenda of Talbott's visit to Slovakia was the reception by Slovak President Kovac at Primacialny Palace in Bratislava. President Kovac highlighted the importance of Central European countries for the security of the whole of Europe. He reiterated the invitation to President Clinton to attend the celebrations of the 50th anniversary of Slovak national uprising [end of August] and briefed the guest about the internal political situation in Slovakia. Talbott handed personal greetings from Bill Clinton to President Kovac. President Clinton considers that a prosperous and democratic Slovakia is beneficial for the United States. The U.S. deputy secretary of state praised the interest of our state in the Partnership for Peace program and Slovakia's integration efforts, which enjoy the full support of the United States. [end recording] [passage omitted]
