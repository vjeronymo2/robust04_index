BFN [Excerpt] Cairo, 23 Apr (MENA) -- Informed Palestinian sources in Cairo expect a tripartite meeting to be held in Cairo on 28 April with Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher to agree on a date to implement the Gaza-Jericho agreement and eliminate the obstacles hindering Palestinian-Israeli talks. The sources told MENA that if the remaining points of difference are resolved during the coming round of talks that will begin in Cairo tomorrow, Wednesday [as received], a summit meeting is expected to be held between 'Arafat and Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin in Cairo on 2 May. That summit will discuss an agreement on the area of Jericho, which was left to the two leaders, and the issuance of passports for Palestinians in Gaza and Jericho. According to the same sources, the final signing of the agreement will be held under the sponsorship of President Husni Mubarak on 4 May. The signing will be attended by U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, and a representative of the Government of Norway which sponsored the secret negotiations, in addition to representatives from donor states. Peres is expected to arrive in Cairo on Tuesday evening to follow up the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations closely, together with PLO Executive Committee.
