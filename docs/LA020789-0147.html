February 7, 1989, Tuesday, Late Final Edition

FAIRFAX STORES EMPTIED IN METHANE LEAK

About 50 commercial buildings in the Fairfax district were evacuated early today after potentially explosive levels of methane gas were detected on a sidewalk and in the basements of two stores, one of which was shattered by a gas explosion in 1985.

Authorities expected the evacuation to last all day. To prevent a dangerous buildup of gas, firefighters planned to pump ground water from beneath the area into the sewer system. Removing the water, officials said, would relieve pressure on the gas, which is naturally produced by rotting organic material in old oil fields underlying the area.

Workers at a K Mart and Ross Dress for Less Store in a Third Street shopping center across from the Farmers Market reported the gas seepage at 6:12 this morning. Dangerous levels of methane also were found on the sidewalk in front of the Gilmore Bank, as well as in the shopping center.

Several blocks of Third Street between Fairfax Avenue and Gardner Street were immediately blocked off.

Authorities attributed the gas threat to the same forces that caused the 1985 explosion, which injured 21 people. Subterranean methane gas still seeps to the surface, where it is trapped by buildings and streets.

"The gas is always looking for a way out of the ground," said Assistant Fire Chief Jim Young. The recent rain has compounded the problem by saturating the ground and leaving less space for the gas.

"The percolation is quite forceful," said Vince Marzo, another Fire Department spokesman. "We'd probably be looking at angels with harps" if a match were struck near the area, Marzo said.

After the 1985 blast in the Ross Dress for Less Store in the Parklabrea Shopping Center, gas vents were installed beneath the parking lot and in Ross's basement. The city has also since required the installation of gas detection devices in residential basements and directed commercial building owners to meet new ventilation standards in their basements or to install gas detection equipment.
