Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Report by correspondent Nikolaos Bellos from Brussels] [Excerpt] [Passage omitted] According to reliable NATO sources, Greece and Canada were expressing opposition to air raids until the last minute. The decision was unanimous, but the Greek permanent representative, Ambassador Vasilios Zafiropoulos, submitted the following statement to the Council's minutes: The Greek presidency stresses that in view of the latest developments--the decision of the Serbs and Muslims to withdraw their heavy weapons--and based on information supplied by international mediators Lord Owen and Thorvald Stoltenberg on Sarajevo's demilitarization, it believes that pursuing the same goal through violent methods is not only illogical but also unavoidably creates the danger of a negative outcome. Therefore, in addition to expressing general reservations about the purpose of air raids, the Greek Presidency separates [dhiakhorizi] its position.
