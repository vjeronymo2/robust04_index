National Park Service Keeper of the National Register of Historic Places September 23, 1994. The title of Keeper of the National Register of Historic Places is hereby assigned to the Chief of Registration, National Register of Historic Places effective as of September 23, 1994. Under 36 CFR 60 and 63 the Keeper is the individual who has been delegated the authority by the National Park Service to list properties and determine their eligibility for the National Register. The Keeper may further delegate this authority as he or she deems appropriate. Under 36 CFR 60 and 63 the decision of the Keeper is the final administrative action on listings and removals from the National Register and on determinations of eligibility. The authority to resolve appeals of decisions in which the Chief of Registration has already participated will remain with the Associate Director, Cultural Resources. ␣␣Sincerely, Jerry L. Rogers, Associate Director, Cultural Resources and Keeper of the National Register of Historic Places, National Park Service.
