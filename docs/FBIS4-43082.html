BFN [Excerpts] Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis, who left Athens this morning for UN Headquarters, will begin consultations in New York on the Cyprus issue. Mr. Mikhailidhis will have contacts with UN Security Council members and will meet with UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali on Thursday [16 June]. Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias will also be visiting New York and Mr. Mikhailidhis will have a meeting with him to coordinate actions. Mr. Mikhailidhis will also have contacts in Washington with leading U.S. State Department officials and with members of Congress. [passage omitted] Government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis has stressed that the role of the five Security Council permanent members is to strengthen the UN secretary general's efforts and not to be a substitute for him. Responding to a correspondent's question on Rauf Denktas' demand that the Robert Lamb ideas be incorporated into the 21 March document, Mr. Kasoulidhis said that there cannot be any Lamb ideas and pointed out that, according to the secretary general's mandate of good offices, those who can submit ideas and discuss ideas at the negotiating table are the secretary general and his representatives.
