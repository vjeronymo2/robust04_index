Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Hamburg (DDP/ADN) -- According to a report in the news magazine DER SPIEGEL, the German Government is planning to send soldiers and civilian aid workers to war-torn Bosnia as soon as a peace settlement for the country is negotiated at a summit meeting. The magazine quotes planning papers by the German Chancellery, according to which a German administrator commissioned by the European Union is to rebuild the administration in the Croat-besieged Muslim stronghold of Mostar. German policemen and administrative officials could help the "emergency mayor." Under Bonn's plans, Bundeswehr medical officers are to operate the city's hospital and Bundeswehr engineers are to repair bridges and roads.
