930324 FT 24 MAR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Why is the government losing committee-stage votes? - As the bill to ratify the Maastricht treaty returns to the House of Commons, FT writers look at the remaining obstacles to its passage MOST MPs support the Maastricht treaty. And yet the government has already lost one committee-stage vote and is in danger of losing more. Nor can it command the votes necessary to speed its progress by forcing MPs to debate it beyond 10pm. Why? The short explanation is that the two main opposition parties - though pledged not to wreck the treaty - have not ruled out seeking to 'improve' it. Nor does Labour in particular feel any compunction about prolonging the committee stage as much as it can. This delays the consideration of other bills and complicates life for those charged with managing the government's legislative programme. Combine these votes with those of the hard-core Tory Euro-sceptics intent on sinking the bill, and the government's overall majority of 20 becomes vulnerable on all but amendments that would change the text of the treaty and therefore wreck it.
