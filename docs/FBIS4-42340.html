BFN [Text] Russian President Boris Yeltsin will visit Corfu during the European Union [EU] summit and will sign the EU-Russia cooperation agreement. Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou is expected to pay an official visit to Russia next fall. This became known after a meeting between Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and his Greek counterpart, Karolos Papoulias, which focused on problems in the Balkans, Cyprus, Turkish provocations in Istanbul, and Russia's relations with Greece and the EU. The two sides reconfirmed their excellent political relations and their identical views on the issues of the Balkans and Cyprus. Both ministers insisted that it is necessary to maintain the rule of the Montreux Treaty on the passage of ships in the Black Sea Straits. Kozyrev said there are some difficulties in the development of bilateral economic relations but stressed there is political will to improve this sector. He added that the establishment of an association of [words indistinct] businessmen is being promoted within this framework and Papoulias will have a meeting with them. Kozyrev said he is grateful for the work that the Greek Government has done so cooperation between EU and Russia will proceed. He added that significant events may happen in this sector through the end of the Greek presidency. Papoulias described bilateral cooperation on issues of the Balkans as close and said the two countries have identical views on ways to resolve the Yugoslav crisis. The foreign minister will meet the Russian patriarch tonight and will discuss his initiatives for peace in the Balkans.
