Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 17 KYODO -- A top-ranking Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) official on Thursday [17 March] called for a slowdown in research and development [R & D] on plutonium recycling. This will be necessary if Japan is to allay possible suspicions that it may possess nuclear weapons in the future, said the official who wants not to be identified. Japan is known to be ahead of other countries in R & D related to plutonium recycling.
