BFN [Text] Prague, April 15 (CTK) -- If parliamentary elections were to take place now, they would be won by the Civic Democratic Party (ODS) with 30.7 percent of the vote, according to a poll of 1,546 Czechs taken on April 1-10 by the Center for Empirical Research (STEM). The second strongest party would be the Czech Social Democratic Party (CSSD) with 15.7 percent, while third would be the coalition Civic Democratic Alliance (ODA) with 12.7 percent. The former Left Bloc coalition would receive 9.1 percent, and 6.9 percent would vote for the Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia (KSCM). With 5.3 percent, the government coalition Christian Democratic Union-Czechoslovak People's Party (KDU-CSL) would narrowly receive more than the five percent of votes needed for entry to parliament. The Christian Democratic Party (KDS), also a coalition party, would receive only 0.6 percent. Parties on the rise include the Assembly for the Republic (SPR- RSC) with 3.8 percent and the Free Democrats (SD, formerly OH) with 3.4 percent of votes. The Green Party would receive 2.2 percent and the Liberal and Social Union 1.2 percent. Seventy seven percent of respondents would take part in elections, a figure which rises to 92 percent among ODS supporters and 83 percent of ODA supporters. At the other end of the scale, 62 percent of Green Party supporters and 61 percent of SPR-RSC supporters would take part.
