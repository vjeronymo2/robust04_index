BFN [Excerpt] Mate Granic, deputy prime minister and foreign minister, and U.S. Ambassador to Croatia Peter Galbraith have signed an agreement at the Foreign Ministry between the governments of the Republic of Croatia and the United States on economic, technical, and related aid. Tihomir Vinkovic has this report. [Vinkovic] Prior to signing the agreement Minister Granic said that this agreement is of major political and economic significance for the Republic of Croatia. This is an all-encompassing agreement and serves as a basis for all other specific agreements, Minister Granic said, adding that this is the cornerstone of future cooperation between the United States and the Republic of Croatia. Minister Granic stressed that such agreements are signed only with countries that have developed friendly relations with the United States and which the United States wishes to help. In any case, following the signing of the Washington agreement and the meeting between Presidents Clinton and Tudjman, this represents the most important instance in the development of friendly relations between the United States and the Republic of Croatia, Minister Granic said. He expressed his hope that talks on concrete projects will continue and that specific agreements on cooperation between the United States and the Republic of Croatia will be signed over the next couple of months. Minister Granic thanked the U.S. Government, which reacted swiftly after the signing of the Washington agreement and signed today's agreement. U.S. Ambassador to Croatia Peter Galbraith expressed his satisfaction with his signing of today's agreement on behalf of the United States. This general agreement provides a framework for extending U.S. aid to the whole world, including Croatia, Galbraith said. He added that bilateral relations between the United States and Croatia will develop on the basis of genuine partnership and consultation. This agreement will place U.S. know-how and experience at the disposal of Croatian public and nongovernmental organizations, Galbraith said. [passage omitted]
