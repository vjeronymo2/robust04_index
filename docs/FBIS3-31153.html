Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Feb 10 (CTK) -- Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus said today the government and the Civic Democratic Party (ODS), of which Klaus is chairman, believe the civil war in Bosnia should not be resolved by external intervention but by internal settlement. In his personal view the air strikes which the NATO Council threatened Bosnian Serbs with unless they withdraw heavy artillery from surrounding Sarajevo are "unfair". "Personally I see it as a little unfair to say: Ground troops no but from the air yes. That seems too easy to me and I don't know if it is fair," Klaus said. Given the non-existence of a front and the unclear positions of the armies, the government considers external intervention could lead to "far greater massacres and catastrophes," Klaus said. On Wednesday [9 February] the NATO Council gave the Bosnian Serbs 10 days from February 10 to withdraw all heavy artillery including tanks and mortars to a distance of 20 km from the center of the besieged Bosnian city of Sarajevo and deliver them into the control of UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] troops, NATO will conduct air strikes against their positions.
