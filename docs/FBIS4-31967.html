BFN [Excerpts] Podgorica, 16 Jun (TANJUG) -- Industrial output in Montenegro dropped by almost 21 percent in May compared with April, the republic's statistics institute has disclosed. [passage omitted] According to the figures released, prices in Montenegro were lower in May than in April, although prices for agricultural products have gone up by 34 percent, while the prices for fresh fruit have even registered an 83-percent increase. Coffee prices have gone up by 100 percent. The average Montenegrin salary was 98 dinars in May; 90 dinars in the public sector, and 120 dinars in the private sector, the republic's statistics institute has announced.
