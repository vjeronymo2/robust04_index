Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "European Union: Italy Critical of Its Japanese Car Import Quota"] [Text] Rome, 19 Mar (ANSA) -- The 47,000 unit quota for Japanese automobile imports to Italy is too high, given the overall drop in the demand for new vehicles and the imports of the so-called transplants, Japanese cars made in Europe, according to a statement issued here Saturday by the Ministry for Foreign Trade. According to the ministry, an in-depth review must be made of the situation which takes into account the various aspects of the question. "We are, in fact, even with the individual limits for the so-called protected markets, in a situation of a single market which allows free circulation among all European countries." "Furthermore" the note continued, "the agreement to limit `yellow' car imports does not take into account the sale of transplants, which account for a significant share of the market held by Japanese makes and represents another element of serious concern for Italy." The effective performance of the new car market, the ministry argued, is "in the end influenced by the competitiveness of prices, which determined, in 1993, only a partial use of the Japanese quota." The statement from the Ministry for Foreign Trade went on to underscore how "it is still not clear whether, as in 1993, the possibility exists to verify and re-examine, in the course of the year, that adjustments which the evolution of the market would dictate."
