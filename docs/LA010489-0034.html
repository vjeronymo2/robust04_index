January 4, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

CHILD ABUSE

In response to Donna Caffrey's letter ("Addicted Baby," Dec. 28):

Yes, it is very sad that Michael Hudson was born addicted to cocaine. What is even sadder is Caffrey's statement that "giving birth to a sick baby and having him taken away from her" is punishment enough for Toni Hudson, the infant's mother.

This woman knew as do all potential drug addicts that cocaine is addictive. Willingly abusing drugs is not a disease, it is a choice. Toni Hudson of her own free will decided to take cocaine. There was no disease to impair her judgment or cause her to take it against her will. The only disease she is suffering from is stupidity.

She is guilty of child abuse from the moment she decided not to take or use birth control. There is enough information available to her and other potential drug addicts to warn them against the dangers of drug abuse, and the harmful effects to pregnant women and their unborn children. Again, it was her choice not to use birth control and her choice to use cocaine.

Isn't being born to a mother who totally disregarded his health and welfare and being placed in a hospital to suffer during his young life punishment enough for Michael?

SANDRA ORTIZ-LOPEMAN

Downey
