940707 FT 07 JUL 94 / World Stock Markets: F&C targets emerging markets Foreign & Colonial believes the time is right to be moving back into the emerging markets, including some - like Hong Kong, Malaysia and Thailand - which have been hardest hit in the recent downturn. Mr Arnab Banerji, F & C's chief investment officer, told an investment gathering last night that over the last 20 years there were four occasions when emerging markets fell by the magnitude seen in recent months. During the early 1980s the markets were stagnant for two years, but this coincided with the global recession and the Latin American debt crisis. On other occasions, the downturn lasted for no more than eight months. Each downturn had followed a rise in US interest rates, along the lines of that seen in late February. All were terminated by a recovery, driven by strong OECD growth. With the US economy strong, the UK recovering, Germany recording expansion and Japan at or near the bottom of its business cycle, Mr Banerji sees the right conditions to start buying again.
