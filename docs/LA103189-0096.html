October 31, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Construction Rebounds in September: Contracts for new construction rebounded in September from an August slump, making the performance for the third quarter equal to the second-quarter rate, F. W. Dodge Group reported. Commercial and industrial building joined forces with public works construction to lead September's 8% gain in newly started projects to an annualized rate of $274.2 billion, the division of McGraw-Hill Information Services Co. said. The Dodge index (1980 equals 100) climbed to its high for the year of 175 in September.
