October 21, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

MASSIVE EARTHQUAKE JOLTS BAY AREA

In between emergency responses from our fire station, I watched the heroic efforts of our brother and sister firefighters in San Francisco as they attempted to suppress the fires and rescue people trapped in structures. The mayor had put out a call for all off-duty fire fighting personnel to immediately report to their fire stations.

While watching the coverage, my mind kept drifting back to an obscure article I had read earlier in the day. This article described how San Francisco Mayor Art Agnos had reduced the San Francisco Fire Department staffing by 76 positions four months earlier. I kept waiting for someone to ask Agnos about these reductions, but no questions were forthcoming.

There is no doubt that the remaining personnel of the San Francisco Fire Department would give their all during this major disaster, but when the dust is settled, I am sure they will ask Agnos if he still feels the same way about his cuts in the fire department -- and I can't wait to hear his answer!

DALLAS JONES

President

Los Angeles County

Fire Fighters Local 1014
