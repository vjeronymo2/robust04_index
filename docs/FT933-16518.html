930703 FT 03 JUL 93 / Government poll concludes European opinion increasing against Japanese The number of European opinion leaders who think that Japan cannot be trusted is increasing, according to a government poll published on Friday, Reuter reports from Tokyo. A total of 3,690 politicians, government officials, labour union leaders, scholars and journalists in Britain, Germany, France, Italy, Belgium, the Netherlands and Spain replied to the survey, carried out by Japan's foreign ministry. In Britain and France, about 40 per cent said they could not trust Japan or were not inclined, almost double that in the previous poll in 1990.
