Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 10 KYODO -- The Food Agency said Thursday [10 March] an additional 30,000 tons of rice, harvested in Japan, will be supplied to the market between this month and June as an emergency measure to meet a shortage. The 30,000 tons, equivalent to two days' national consumption, are part of the agency's prefectural branch stocks, agency officials said. However, it is up to prefectural governments to determine when to put the extra rice on the market, the officials said. The shortage is caused partly by delayed imports of foreign rice, they said.
