BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondent Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] St. Petersburg April 17 TASS -- Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin met here on Sunday with delegations of the Group-of-Seven highly industrialized countries and international financial organizations. Participants in the closed-door meeting discussed coordination of international assistance to Russian reforms, specifically the possibility of creating a multilateral agency to insure foreign investments against non-commercial risks. Chernomyrdin told the gathering that the Russian leadership was firmly committed to the course of reforms. "We have advanced considerably in market-oriented transformations and can now speak about certain progress on this way", the Russian prime minister emphasised. Among the most significant achievements, he listed the bringing of inflation down to 8-9 per cent a month. Chernomyrdin thanked Western partners for their participation in the meeting. Russia was represented at the meeting by Deputy Prime Minister Aleksandr Shokhin, Central Bank Chief Viktor Gerashchenko, Acting Finance Minister Sergey Dubinin and other officials.
