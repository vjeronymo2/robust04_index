Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Sao Paulo -- The night before he was murdered, Oswaldo Cruz Junior, president of the ABC [Sao Paulo's industrial belt] Highways Driver Union, granted an interview to the NEWSPAPER OF GREATER ABC in which he accuses Luis Inacio Lula da Silva, the virtual PT [Workers Party] presidential candidate, of heading a scheme to transfer union funds to the party. He told the newspaper: "Sometimes Lula personally asked us, the labor leaders, to collect money from members." Oswaldo Cruz Junior was murdered by Jose Benedito de Souza on 6 January. Souza was a supporter of his uncle, union secretary general Cicero Bezerra da Silva, who recently accused Cruz of illegal enrichment. From the moment he returned to his post, Cruz [who returned as part of a deal to leave his post to Bezerra and run for president at the drivers federation] reported the irregular transfer of union funds to the PT. During the interview, Oswaldo Cruz Junior revealed that the transfer of funds to the PT was systematic and that the operation was carried out in three different ways. One way was to label the transfer of funds as special union expenses. "The drivers union spend 15 million cruzeiros in special union expenses during 1993 alone," Oswaldo Cruz Junior said. Cruz reported that the second way to transfer funds to the PT was to increase union contributions, authorized by ghost assemblies. "Sometimes we published edicts convening assemblies to authorize discounts of up to 6 percent. The trick was that the edicts were not distributed, no one came to the assembly, and the discounts were approved," Cruz said. Oswaldo Cruz Junior's interview in the NEWSPAPER OF GREATER ABC states that the third mechanism for transferring union funds to the PT was the manipulation of deposits held for social security contributions and monthly contributions by companies, which were directly sent to the unions as of 1988. "If you were to audit the ABC Metallurgical Union you would see that the union almost went bankrupt in 1989," Cruz stressed. Oswaldo Cruz said that the union contracted Cotrigal Graphics of San Andres to print Lula's presidential campaign material in 1989, but the material was printed in the workers' newspaper CHAPEU DE BICO which increased its number of issues from 10,000 to 300,000 in a single month. "He will not be able to deny it after I show you what was published," the union leader said in a challenging manner. Cruz added that in February 1992 5 million cruzeiros was transferred to the Santo Andre branch of the PT. The union money was to have been used to purchase of the party headquarters. Cruz said that the PT Municipal Building was to be purchased by the drivers, the chemical, and the metallurgical unions. During the interview Cruz said that he received several calls from Deputy Arlindo Chinaglia, the PT state president, proposing a deal to stop denouncing Lula's party. Cruz said he had proof, adding that he had a meeting scheduled with Senators Jarbas Passarinho (PPR-PA) [Progressive Renewal Party-Para] and Esperidian Amim (PPR-SC) [PPR-Santa Catarina] for 11 January to talk about the CPI [Congressional Investigating Committee] on the CUT [Sole Workers Center]. During the interview with NEWSPAPER OF GREATER ABC, the labor leader produced a box of video tapes which, he said, contained proof of th? transfer of union funds to the PT. Antonio Carlos Monico, former Santo Andre PT president, yesterday denied allegations that ABC unions had ever transferred money to the PT for the electoral campaign of party candidates. Monico was named by union leader Oswaldo Cruz Junior, who was murdered on 6 January, as a witness of the PT use of union funds in electoral campaigns.
