Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- In the view of Bundeswehr Inspector General Klaus Naumann, NATO will need to institute a new command and control structure in order to carry out peacemaking interventions in international conflicts. In an interview with Berlin "Deutschlandradio," Naumann said on Friday [4 March] that the alliance must also ask itself the fundamental question of whether it should assume a mandate in which it simply carries out orders on behalf of the United Nations or the CSCE, without being permitted to make its own decisions; or whether NATO should make decisions on a United Nations or CSCE mandate. The question is also whether NATO will say of its own volition: Here is a security problem that we want to solve in the sphere of our interests in consensus between the 16 nations, and this is where we will prepare for the necessary action. Naumann emphasized that this issue requires a political answer. The inspector general reiterated his view that, in future, German units should also participate in such NATO operations.
