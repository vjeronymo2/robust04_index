October 15, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

BROTHERLY HELP

In Patrick Goldstein's Oct. 8 article, actor Beau Bridges is quoted as saying that years ago he was afraid his younger brother Jeff "was a pansy," so he took him out to the park and taught him to throw a curve ball.

Bridges' comment prompts the following open letter:

Beau, my nephew wanted to go see you and Jeff in your new movie, "The Fabulous Baker Boys." You'll be proud of the fact that I told him:

"No way -- you get out there and play some baseball or I'll think you're a pansy."

Thanks, Beau. You've saved your brother from a fate worse than death, and now my nephew is saved too!

By the way, do all of the young people in your performing-arts group have to prove they're straight to work with you?

Or do you just call them "pansy" or "dyke" if they're not?

Do you send them out to toss a few balls around if you're suspicious?

Patrick Goldstein should not have included your remark in his story and The Times should not have printed it.

I don't think they did you a favor, and it shows an editorial lack of sensitivity.

FRANC CAGGIANO

Malibu
