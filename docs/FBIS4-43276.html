BFN [Text] During a meeting in Athens today between the defense ministers of Greece and Cyprus, Yerasimos Arsenis and Konstandinos Iliadhistoward, significant decisions were made toward a joint defense doctrine between Greece and Cyprus. Details regarding the joint defense doctrine were announced after the two defense ministers met in Nicosia last March. Today's talks between Arsenis and Iliadhis lasted for three hours. They agreed to hold joint military exercises between the Greek Armed Forces and the Cypriot National Guard, and construct infrastructure projects to promote cooperation between the airforce and navy of Greece and Cyprus.
