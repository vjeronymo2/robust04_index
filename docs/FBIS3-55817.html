Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondents] [Text] Moscow January 11 TASS -- U.S. President Bill Clinton and the accompanying officials will arrive in the Russian capital late at night on January 12 on board of the Air Force-1 plane. At the Vnukovo-2 airport they will be met by Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and head for the place of their accommodation. The working part of the visit will start on Thursday, January 13. An official meeting ceremony between the U.S. President and his Russian counterpart Boris Yeltsin will be held in the morning in Kremlin's St. George Hall. After greetings the two leaders will have a conversation to be followed by another round of the summit negotiations. There will be a short break between the meetings to enable their brief sight-seeing tour on Kremlin grounds. In the afternoon Bill Clinton will visit the St. Daniel's Monastery and meet Patriarch of Moscow and All Russia Aleksey II. At night there will be a reception for representatives of the Russian political, business, scientific and public communities at the Moscow residence of the U.S. ambassador. The program will end with an unofficial dinner in a narrow circle at the Russian president's countryside residence. Early in the morning on Friday, January 14, Bill Clinton will attend a ceremony of huge symbolic meaning for the Russian and U.S. peoples - he will visit the Unknown Soldier's tomb near the Kremlin wall to lay a wreath. After the ceremony to commemorate the Russian-U.S. combat union in the Second World War, the U.S. delegation will come to the Yekaterina Hall in Kremlin to attend a tripartite meeting of the Russian, U.S. and Ukrainian delegations and sign documents. The Russian-U.S. negotiations will continue in an expanded composition to result in signing of joint documents in Vladimir's Hall of the Grand Kremlin Palace. A news conference of the two presidents will mark the negotiations completion. The U.S. leader will visit the Ostankino TV center on Friday afternoon to address the Russian TV viewers and people invited to the studio and answer questions of Russians. Bill Clinton's wife will arrive in Moscow on Friday to have a separate program of stay, including a visit to a children's hospital. The same day will witness a state reception given by Boris Yeltsin in honor of Bill Clinton and his wife in the Kremlin Faceted Chamber. The two leaders will exchange toasts. The U.S. President will end his Moscow visit on Saturday, January 15. After visiting the U.S. embassy for an unofficial meeting with embassy staffers and members of their families, Bill Clinton will go to Kremlin to attend an official farewell ceremony in St. George's Hall. The president and the accompanying officials will leave Moscow for Belarus on a visit.
