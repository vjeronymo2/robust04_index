BFN [Text] Tirana, April 21 (ATA) -- In the course of his visit to Albania, Norman Anderson, the chairman of CSCE mission in Skopje, met the Albanian President Sali Berisha, Deputy Foreign Minister Arjan Starova, public order and transport ministers, Mr. Musaraj and Mr. Bitincka respectively, on April 20. In these meetings Mr. Anderson was interested in knowing Albanian views in reference to the Albanian-Macedonian relationship in general. They focused mainly in the situation of Albanians in the Macedonian Republic [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM]. They pointed out in the meetings that Albania will continue to demand the respectability of the rights of Albanian people in Macedonia and wherever they are, which constitutes one of the priorities of Albanian foreign policy. The respectability of the Albanian rights constitutes an important element of stability in the whole region. Firstly, in this framework Albania is interested in achievement in conformity to the international laws as soon as possible of the population census and the demands of the Albanians should be taken seriously by the census law. At the same time was stressed the good will of the Albanian side to offer its territorial advantages for the passing of goods, because Macedonia suffers from Greek embargo. Not only for this reason but also foreseeing the future development of economical and commercial relationship with Macedonia, Albanian authorities pointed out the readiness for the further intensification of the relations, making concrete proposals to specific fields.
