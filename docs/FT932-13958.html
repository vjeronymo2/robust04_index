930421 FT 21 APR 93 / Observer: Stone's throw So what did jovial Wim Duisenberg, outgoing chairman of the European central bank governors' committee, say when asked in Basle about the likely home of the new European Monetary Institute, due to be set up next year? First, that he, the Dutch central bank governor, knew of a most suitable building in Amsterdam. Second, that it 'does not contain any marble'.
