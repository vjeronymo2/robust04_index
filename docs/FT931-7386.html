930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / International Company News: Nobel Industries to sell defence unit to Celsius SWEDEN'S Nobel Industries is selling its defence electronics activities and related real estate to state-owned Celsius Industries in a SKr1.55bn (Dollars 204m) deal which starts the process of broadening the ownership structure in Celsius. The move came as Nobel's shares were suspended on the Stockholm and Copenhagen bourses pending a separate announcement today. Nobel will receive SKr325m in shares in Celsius for an approximate stake of 10 per cent, which values the whole of the defence group - 100 per cent state-owned - at SKr3.25bn. Two weeks ago, the government lined Celsius up as its next privatisation candidate by saying it wanted to broaden the group's ownership structure and obtain a share listing before the summer. Celsius is buying NobelTech Systems and NobelTech electronics for SKr755m, and real estate amounting to SKr800m. The purchase will give the companyan estimated 50 per cent share of the country's defence industry. Mr Olle Lund, Celsius chairman and chief executive, said: 'The acquisition of NobelTech further enhances our position as one of Europe's leading defence industry groups, especially important with respect to export markets.'
