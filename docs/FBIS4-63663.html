BFN [Text] The situation in Crimea is largely calm, RIA was told by (Petr Chernichevich), an advisor to the Crimean president on military issues. However, he said that some tension could nevertheless be sensed, caused by the unpredictability of the Ukrainian authorities' next moves. Reinforcements are constantly arriving at the Ukrainian National Guard units in Simferopol and Sevastopol. The number of guards has already reached 2,000, consisting mainly of men from western Ukraine who arrived in Simferopol on Sunday. They have also received 29 armored personnel carriers, according to Chernichevich. All important sites in the Crimean capital remain under the strict guard of interior troop units loyal to the Crimean Government, and the city's streets are being patrolled by Crimean Cossacks.
