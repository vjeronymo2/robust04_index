BFN [Text] "The agreement on civic peace in Russia will be prepared but it will change nothing in the political situation in the country." This is the view of 71.5 percent of respondents who participated in the opinion poll conducted by the independent service Vox Populi headed by Professor Boris Grushin, member of the Presidential council, in late March - early April. As many as 400 public figures, deputies of the State Duma, leaders of executive power bodies, leaders of political parties, prominent entrepreneurs, military commanders, media representatives, and scientific and cultural workers participated in the poll. According to 15 percent of the opinion poll's participants, "the agreement will be prepared and will reach its goal," 6.5 percent said the "the agreement will not be prepared at all." Military commanders and media leaders, 88 percent and 80 percent respectively, are especially pessimistic with respect to peace and accord. There were more optimists among the executive leaders (34 percent) and directors of state enterprises (18 percent).
