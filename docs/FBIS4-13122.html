BFN [Text] Cairo, 16 Apr (MENA)--The security service in western Cairo has launched a campaign in shanty towns and remote areas in the past two days to capture fugitive criminals and escaped convicts. Major General Mahmud Wajdi, director of the Cairo General Detective Department, has stated that the campaigns resulted in the seizure of 66 firearms, persons involved in 26 drug cases, 296 suspects in political cases, and 12 fugitive convicts. He said that 410 cases of individual gangs and another three-member gang specializing in car robbery have been uncovered. As for the political suspects, Wajdi said the arrested 296 persons are being checked to determine whether they belong to extremist groups. Maj. Gen. Wajdi added that campaigns that covered northern Cairo have unveiled 29 cases of narcotics and 11 criminal cases and have seized five firearms. During these last campaigns, nine persons suspected of illegal political activity and 125 fugitive convicts were captured. Maj. Gen. Wajdi pointed out that the campaigns started two days ago in various sectors in Cairo at the instructions of Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi.
