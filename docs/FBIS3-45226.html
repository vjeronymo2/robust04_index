Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, January 14 (XINHUA) -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin today described as successful U.S. President Bill Clinton's first official visit to Russia. At a 50-minute joint news conference held in the press center of the Foreign Ministry, the Russian president stressed the visit was important for the United States as well as for Russia, saying it encouraged the "search for new components in the relations between the two countries." Yeltsin said that he and Clinton effectively put an end to the Cold War in Vancouver and the two countries were now moving to "cooperation and partnership." Touching upon the domestic situation in Russia, Yeltsin stressed that the adoption of a new constitution and the December 12 parliamentary elections were initial signs of success of the democratic process of Russia. On December 12, the president said, the Russian people voted for "a better life." Yeltsin also told reporters that Russia would keep reform going further and strengthen its social stability and peace.
