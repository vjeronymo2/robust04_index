Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Egypt today strongly condemned the incident committed by an Israeli settler in Hebron in the occupied West Bank, which left scores of Palestinians dead. In a statement commenting on the incident, Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa said: Egypt affirms the need to confront the extremism that has hit numerous societies of different religions, politics, and traditions. The foreign minister said that the incident confirms the importance of preserving Palestinian security and not just Israeli security. Therefore, calls for security are not restricted to Israel alone. He said that in light of the dangerous nature of and the signs given by the incident, Egypt will take part in any meetings the Security Council decides to hold in response to a request from Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat. The foreign minister said the incident affirms the importance of the subject of Jerusalem and the need to put it on the agenda of the negotiations. The fate of Jerusalem cannot be abandoned to the behavior of extremists, criminals, or mad men. Elsewhere, the PLO blamed the Israeli Government for the massacre committed by a Jewish settler against Palestinian citizens praying at the Hebron mosque this morning. The death toll from the massacre has risen to 45 martyrs, while four Palestinians were killed by the bullets of the Israeli occupation Army in Hebron during the violent clashes that erupted after the massacre.
