Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tallinn, Feb 23, BNS -- Estonian security police and armed forces in a joint operation on Feb. 21 discovered more than 500 TT pistols near Luhamaa at the country's southeastern border with Russia. The guns were seized and handed over to the defense forces, said Juri Pihl, director general of the Defense Police. Pihl said nobody was arrested so far, but his department has initiated a criminal case on arms smuggling and misprision charges. The criminal code foresees a jail sentence of 3 to 8 years for such crimes. Russian customs in their checkpoint of Petseri, opposite Luhamaa, on Feb. 21 uncovered five guns and lots of cartridges in a car headed from Estonia to Russia. Those who were in the car have been arrested.
