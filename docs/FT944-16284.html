941014 FT 14 OCT 94 / World Trade News: US car demand irks Japanese - Contracts & Ventures A Japanese official yesterday denounced remarks by a US official calling for resumption of talks about vehicle trade, in the first flare-up in US-Japan trade relations since the two sides reached a partial trade agreement on October 1. That agreement did not include cars and car parts, which Tokyo had proposed be left to the private sector. But Mr Jeffrey Garten, US undersecretary of commerce, said on Wednesday there would be 'no let-up' in US pressure on Japan for car concessions. He called for new talks in cars and parts. A Japanese official said cabinet members and the prime minister were considering whether Japan should file a formal protest over the remarks.
