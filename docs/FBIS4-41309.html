BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Igor Borisenko, Igor Barsukov, and Pavel Vanichkin] [Text] Washington June 24 TASS -- On Thursday, U.S. President Bill Clinton received Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin who is at the head of the Russian delegation at a session of the joint Russo-American commission on economic and technological cooperation. The meeting took place in the White House behind closed doors. According to White House press secretary Dee Dee Myers, the two parties paid most attention to the results of the third session of the so-called "Gore-Chernomyrdin" commission and to the agreements which were worked out during the session. President Clinton expressed satisfaction with the way Russia's economic reforms develop, and the United States were concerned about their fate in the end of 1993 and in the beginning of 1994, she said. Asked whether Clinton and Chernomyrdin discussed a definite date for the forthcoming Russo-American summit in Washington which has been provisionally scheduled for next September, the press secretary replied in the negative.
