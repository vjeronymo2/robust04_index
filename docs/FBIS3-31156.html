Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, Feb 9 (CTK) -- The Czech Government is opposed to the creation of a special law on ethnic minorities or of an institution to deal with related questions, Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus said today. The government reached the conclusion after studying material on ethnic minorities submitted to it by Minister Igor Nemec, chairman of the Government Ethnic Minorities Council, Klaus said. He stressed that the government unequivocally supported "a policy of ethnic minorities' non-discrimination." According to the 1991 census, the Czech Republic had 10.3 million citizens. The strongest ethnic minorities were Slovaks (314,877), Poles (59,383), and Germans (48,566).
