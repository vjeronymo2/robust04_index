BFN [Text] Shanghai, May 28 (XINHUA) -- A Sino-Japanese supermarket was launched here today, the first Sino-foreign joint venture of its kind set up in Shanghai. The store, a joint venture between Japan's noted retail company Yaohan, and the Shanghai Liannong Inc., cost 300,000 U.S. dollars and sells foodstuffs, clothes and other daily consumer goods, some being imported from Japan, company officials said. Feng Guoqin, vice-secretary-general of the Shanghai Municipal Government, said the supermarket demonstrated that overseas businesses will be allowed a share in developing local chain supermarkets. An official from Yaohan said today that the company has set up a China department to supervise its development in China. The Yaohan-Lian Co. plans to set up five more chain supermarkets in Shanghai in two years, sources said.
