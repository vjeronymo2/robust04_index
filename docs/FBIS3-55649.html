Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Viktor Zamyatin, Sergey Tsekhmistrenko report: "Personnel Shakeup in Russian Foreign Ministry. Minister Will Have New Deputies"] [Text] The just completed New Year and Christmas season has brought about more changes in Russian diplomacy. According to information available to KOMMERSANT-DAILY, the question of appointing Aleksandr Panov, the current ambassador to Seoul, as deputy foreign minister, has for all intents and purposes been resolved. It has come to light simultaneously that Igor Ivanov, ambassador to Madrid, will probably become yet another deputy for Andrey Kozyrev. Georgiy Kunadze, whose place will probably be taken by Aleksandr Panov, was in charge of Foreign Ministry personnel policy and relations with the countries of Asia and the Pacific region. Kunadze was not quite successful in the personnel job, though, some high-ranking diplomats note. One of the probable reasons is the fact that he has not been a career diplomat (before joining the Ministry of Foreign Affairs he was in academic science), which is why they argue on Smolenskaya Ploshchad that he could not entirely get rid of his "outsider" image. Last October, personnel work was entrusted to an "insider" -- Sergey Krylov -- and some time later, the question of Kunadze's appointment as ambassador to Seoul was resolved in principle. This leaves the Asia and Pacific region "untended," and now Aleksandr Panov is to step into this breach. As for Igor Ivanov, according to some information, he is tipped to take the place of current First Deputy Minister Anatoliy Adamishin, who has become a member of parliament. It is still not clear, though, which Foreign Ministry areas he will be in charge of if one thinks that changes in the ministry's structure (and the redistribution of duties among top diplomats it entails) have not been yet completed. [article contains following boxed passage] Profiles of the Russian Foreign Ministry Head's New Deputies Igor Sergeyevich Ivanov, born in 1945. Diplomatic rank -- extraordinary and plenipotentiary ambassador. Graduated from the Moscow Maurice Torez State Pedagogical Institute of Foreign Languages, has been working in the Foreign Ministry system since 1973. Was counselor-envoy of the embassy in Spain, a Foreign Ministry expert, minister's aide, division head and chief of the Foreign Ministry General Secretariat, member of the Ministry Collegium (1989-1001). In 1991, he was appointed extraordinary and plenipotentiary ambassador of the USSR (of Russia as of 1992) to Spain. Aleksandr Nikolayevich Panov, born in 1944. Diplomatic rank -- extraordinary and plenipotentiary ambassador. Graduated from the Moscow State Institute of International Relations, candidate of historical sciences. He has been working with the Foreign Ministry system since 1968: first at the embassy in Japan, from 1997; at the USSR permanent mission with the United Nations, from 1983; and at the embassy in Tokyo again. In 1988-1990, he was a Foreign Ministry apparatus staffer, and as of 1992 the extraordinary and plenipotentiary ambassador of Russia to the Republic of Korea.
