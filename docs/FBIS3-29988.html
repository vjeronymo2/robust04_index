Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 24 (YONHAP) -- Dacom Corp. will become the first Korean concern to participate in the local telecommunications business in Russia, Dacom officials said on Thursday. Dacom said that its subsidiary, Dacom International Corp., and Goldstar Information and Communications Ltd. will sign a contract with Russian firms to invest in "Nakhodka Free Economic Zone Telecommunications Co." on Friday in Seoul. Nakhodka City in Primoskiy Kray (maritime province) of Russia recently established the company to provide telecommunications service within the city. Six Russian organizations including the Administrative Committee of Nakhodka Free Economic Zone, Nakhodka City and telecommunications and information agency of Primoskiy Kray will invest 2 million U.S. dollars while Dacom International and Goldstar plan to invest another 2 million dollars in the local telecommunications business. Dacom will pour about 1.8 million dollars and Goldstar 200,000 dollars into the joint venture. Nakhodka Free Economic Zone Telecommunications Co. will begin full operations in April and eventually expand its service to all areas of Primoskiy Kray, according to Dacom.
