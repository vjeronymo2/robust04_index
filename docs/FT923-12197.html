920721 FT 21 JUL 92 / Medical charity quits camps Medecins Sans Frontieres, the medical charity group, withdrew its medical staff from Kenya's Somali refugee camps yesterday in protest against violent attacks and banditry, Reuter reports from Nairobi. 'MSF has decided to withdraw all its medical staff of 24 from the camps until further security measures are taken,' the group said in a statement. 'In the last few weeks relief workers from MSF and other organisations have fallen victim to an increasing number of armed attacks and in some cases to physical violence. 'Food stocks of children's feeding centres have been looted and roads between refugee camps have become dangerous for relief workers and refugees alike.'
