BFN [Text] Seoul, April 30 (YONHAP) -- Daewoo Shipbuilding and Heavy Machinery, Ltd. delivered the nation's first submarine "Ichon" to the Navy in a ceremony held at the company's Okpo Shipyard near Pusan over the weekend, with chief of naval operations Adm. Kim Hong-yol, Daewoo Vice Chairman Yun Yong-sok and many other Navy and company officials attending, the company announced. The diesel engine-powered T-209-class submarine is armed with torpedoes and depth charges and can conduct independent operations for two months with a crew of 30. It can cruise at a maximum speed of 22 knots (40 kilometers per hour), compared with North Korea's Whiskey- and Romeo-class submarines cruising at 12 to 14 knots, the announcement said. Daewoo started construction of the warship in November 1989, using designs and technology of Germany's HDW, and completed it in October 1992. The ship had been successfully test-operated before the delivery to the Navy.
