BFN [Text] Beijing, June 24 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier Li Lanqing had a meeting here today with the full board of directors of the recently-created Import and Export Bank of China (CEIB). He stressed the need to give the utmost help to promote China's exports, particularly of machinery, electronics and other equipment. CEIB is China's second policy bank after the People's Bank of China, the central bank. Established in Beijing on April 27 this year, the bank is responsible for providing import and export credits for capital goods, including large machinery and electronic equipment. It also handles credits as well as government and commercial loans provided by foreign countries for their import of Chinese capital goods. Vice-Premier Li recalled that the bank was set up to meet the needs of establishing a socialist market economic system, speed up the reform of the financial system, separate the policy banking business from commercial banking business, enable the country's foreign trade to develop in a sustained, steady and healthy way, improve the structure, grade and quality of export commodities, and increase exports. Vice-Premier Li urged the CEIB to formulate strict rules and regulations and establish a strict internal management system.
