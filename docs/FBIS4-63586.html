BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Yezhov] [Text] Moscow May 23 TASS -- The delegations of Russia and Ukraine are now meeting in Moscow to discuss the situation in the Crimea and the disputed Black Sea Fleet. Opening the talks, Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin said that "the problems which have brought us here are very difficult." He asked his Ukrainian counterpart Yukhym Zvyahilskyy to outline the problems he wants to discuss at the talks. Zvyahilskyy named the Black Sea Fleet and the location of the Russian part of the fleet as two top priority issues. Before the talks Chernomyrdin and Zvyahilskyy had a one-to-one meeting. The talks are also being attended by Ukrainian Vice-Premier Valeriy Shmarov, Deputy Foreign Minister Borys Tarasyuk, Deputy Defence Minister Ivan Bizhan [name and title as received] and Navy commander Vladimir Beskorovainy. The Russian delegation includes Deputy Foreign Minister Igor Ivanov, First Deputy Defence Minister Boris Gromov [name and title as received] and Navy Commander Feliks Gromov. Also taking part are the Ukrainian ambassador to Russia and the Russian ambassador to Ukraine.
