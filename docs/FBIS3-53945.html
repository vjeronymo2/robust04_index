Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Ministry Secretary General Zafir al-Hasan received from U.S. Ambassador Mark Hambley today an invitation for Lebanon to attend the bilateral peace negotiations that will be resumed in Washington next week. After the meeting, Hambley announced that the heads of the Arab and Israeli delegations will meet on 24 January to make preparations for a new round of talks. No specific date for the beginning of the talks has yet been set. Hambley noted the positive atmosphere created by the U.S.-Syrian summit and said he hopes that progress will be made on the Syrian and Lebanese negotiating tracks. In answer to a question, the U.S. ambassador said the withdrawal of foreign forces from Lebanese territory is one of the most important tenets of U.S. policy in Lebanon, which endorses the substance of UN Security Council Resolution 425.
