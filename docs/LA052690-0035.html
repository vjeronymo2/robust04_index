May 26, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

WHAT'S WRONG WITH THE LAKERS? IT HAS BEEN ASKED BEFORE

A veteran championship team hits an unfortunate slump in the early rounds of the playoffs and fails to make the finals for the first time in years. Fans wail, gnash their teeth and call for the coach's blood, ignoring an otherwise excellent season.

Sportswriters insist major changes must be made, the team is clearly over the hill, and the young, victorious team is anointed as the new power in the conference.

The Lakers of 1990, you say? Well, yes, but also the Lakers of 1986, after which the same over-the-hill veterans won back-to-back championships and would have had a third but for ill-timed injuries. Let's not panic, but thank the Lakers for a fine regular season, and in Dodger tradition, vow to "wait till next year."

DARREL INGERS, Irvine
