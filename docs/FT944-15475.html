941018 FT 18 OCT 94 / International Company News: Scott jumps by 75% as restructure continues Scott Paper, the international tissue paper manufacturer that has been undergoing a big restructuring, yesterday reported a near 75 per cent jump in third-quarter net income to Dollars 60.6m, or 80 cents a share. This compares with Dollars 35.1m, or 48 cents, in the comparable quarter last year. Scott last week announced the sale of its SD Warren printing paper subsidiary to the South African company Sappi. The deal was part of its strategy to focus on tissue-based consumer products, The profits advance was made in spite of flat sales, at Dollars 1.1bn. Mr Albert Dunlap, the new chairman brought in to oversee Scott's restructuring, said: 'Our third-quarter results represent the best quarterly performance in four years.' During the third quarter, SD Warren's sales increased modestly to Dollars 294.7m, from last year's Dollars 290.8m, while its operating income rose to Dollars 26.3m, from Dollars 26m. For the first nine months, Scott recorded income of Dollars 126.0m, or Dollars 1.68, against last year's Dollars 93.3m, or Dollars 1.26.
