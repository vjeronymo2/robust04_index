Language: Malay Article Type:BFN [Text] Senator Mustapha Mohamed was sworn in as the new deputy finance minister before Paramount Ruler Sultan Azlan Shah at the National Palace in Kuala Lumpur today. Present at the ceremony were Datuk Sri Dr. Mahathir Mohamed, prime minister; Tun Hamid Omar, lord president of the federal courts of Malaysia; Attorney General Datuk Mokhtar Abdullah; Rahim Mohamed Noor, deputy inspector general of police; Tan Sri Ahmad Sarji Abdul Hamid, chief secretary to the government. The appointment of Senator Mustapha, 44, was announced by Dr. Mahathir after the later chaired the UMNO [United Malays national Organization] Supreme Council meeting last Saturday. Senator Mustapha is replacing Mr. Abdul Ghani Othman, who has now been appointed youth and sports minister. Mustapha was appointed senator in 1991, which made him eligible to be appointed the parliamentary secretary of the Finance Ministry.
