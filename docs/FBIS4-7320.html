BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Kim Boldokhonov] [Text] Ulaanbaatar, 21 Apr (ITAR-TASS) -- Mongolia's leading political parties today signed a conciliation document aimed at overcoming the acute political crisis in the country which is in its second week. In the presence of President Punsalmaagiyn Ochirbat, leaders of the three parliamentary parties -- the Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party, the Mongolian National Democratic Party, and the Mongolian Social Democratic Party -- signed the document. The document, called `Political Consensus', stipulates in particular that at its spring and fall sessions parliament should adopt laws on public rallies and demonstrations, on free speech, freedom of the media, on the moral code of officials, and on fighting corruption. The present crisis broke out 13 April after the Mongolian Democratic Union and a number of other opposition parties started mass actions in support of their demands for the government's resignation. They insisted that its socioeconomic program has failed and that it is unable to lead the country out of the crisis. They also called on the law enforcement agencies to urgently investigate all the accusations of corruption and abuse of power made against top officials. Representatives of the opposition continue their hunger strike on the capital's central square until their demands are met. Supporters of the present government have also staged their own hunger strike. They have already refused food for six days now and state that they will end their hunger strike only after the opposition does the same. At the same time, the conflicting sides continue their mass rallies. `Free microphones' are installed in the public places for all those wishing to express their opinions on the issues they are concerned about.
