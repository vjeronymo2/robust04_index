BFN [Text] Algiers, 27 Apr, KUNA--Algerian Foreign Affairs Minister Mohamed Salah Dembri today expressed his "astonishment that there was some confusion" over his country's position on the crisis resulting from the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait. He said that the position adopted from the beginning "was clear and demanded the immediate withdrawal of Iraqi troops from Kuwaiti territory." He added, in an interview with the Kuwaiti news agency, that the visit of Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad, first deputy prime minister and foreign minister, to Algeria "was an opportunity to remove the misunderstanding resulting from the crisis caused by the Iraqi invasion of Kuwait." The Algerian foreign minister also called for the rebuilding of Arab solidarity in accordance with principles that take into account the national character of every people of the Arab nation and that respect the sovereignty of states and their existing borders. Dembri also said: "The Arab world needs solidarity to confront external challenges." He indicated that this did not mean ignoring the foundations and principles on which such a solidarity had to be built. He added, in this respect, that his talks with Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad, Kuwaiti first deputy prime minister and foreign minister, dealt with ways to achieve an effective Arab solidarity that would be beneficial to the Arab nation. Regarding the future of Kuwaiti-Algerian relations in the light of Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad's visit, Dembri said that the talks between the two sides "will give a big boost to bilateral relations." He added that the relations between the two countries "are solid and good but they need to be somewhat revived in a number of areas."
