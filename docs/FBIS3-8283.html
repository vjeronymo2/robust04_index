Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] A whole system of submersible walls has had to be built against oil leakages at River Olse in the border area from Jablunkov in the direction of Bohumin. More from our reporter Frantisek Tvrz, who is in the affected area: [Tvrz] At the moment, investigators and ecology inspectors are investigating how many tonnes of the crude oil product leaked from the fuel area of the Czech railways at Jablunkov yesterday. According to this morning's statement by the breakdown commission, the nonspecialist estimates suggest a leakage of approximately six to seven tonnes. The whole system of submersible walls was built immediately below Jablunkov but also at the brook at Navsi and then lower down, toward the confluence of the Olse border river to the Odra River, via Trinec, Cesky Tesin up to Bohumin-Vernovice.
