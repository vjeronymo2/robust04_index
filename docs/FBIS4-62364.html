BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Vadim Byrkin and Tigran Liloyan] [Text] Yerevan, 15 May -- During consultations with the leadership of Armenia, Azerbaijan, and also Nagornyy Karabakh [Nagorno-Karabakh], representatives of Russia and the CSCE proposed that the cease-fire agreement that was reached should be strengthened by giving it an appropriate formula which, in particular, provided for the stationing of international observers, according to a report for the press that was communicated to the ITAR-TASS correspondents here. Ambassador Vladimir Kazimirov, plenipotentiary representative of the president of Russia in Nagornyy Karabakh, and Jan Eliasson, spokesman for the CSCE Minsk conference, who are touring the region of the conflict, also expressed satisfaction that the cease-fire agreement that was reached on 12 May between the defense ministers of Azerbaijan, Armenia, and the commander of the Army of Nagornyy Karabakh, with the mediation of Russia and the CSCE, had led to a decline in combat activity.
