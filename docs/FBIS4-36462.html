BFN [Text] Cairo, 8 Jun (MENA)--A police sweep was carried out in southern Cairo's slum neighborhoods early this morning to apprehend fugitive terrorists, outlaws, and ordinary criminals. Assistant Interior Minister Mansur 'Isawi said the sweep in which the various Interior Ministry departments took part resulted in the arrest of 12 men from the fundamentalist Islamic groups. He said that the sweep, which concentrated on the 'Arab Ghunaym area in Hulwan, also resulted in the arrest of 32 men from the Muslim Brotherhood and al-Salafiyun. Moreover, 20 political suspects were arrested and referred to the State Intelligence Department. Some 16 ordinary criminals and outlaws were also arrested. Five pieces of various firearms were seized.
