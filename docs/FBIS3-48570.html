Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The recently formed breakaway groups in the major leftist legal organizations are being funded by the government, according to exiled communist leader Jose Ma. Sison. The Netherlands-based Sison, in his renewed attacks against his critics, also accused columnist and University of the Philippines professor Alex Magno as being hired by the government to write anti-communist propaganda. In a statement faxed to the INQUIRER yesterday, Sison said groups like the Bukluran ng Manggagawang Pilipino, Demokratikong Kilusang Magbubukid ng Pilipinas, and the Kapatirang Anak Pawis [Union of Filipino Workers, Democratic Peasants Movement of the Philippines, and the Brotherhood of Laborers] are being used by the government for propaganda against the Communist Party of the Philippines [CPP] and the National Democratic Front. Magno laughed off the accusation, saying Sison should learn to be more tolerant of outside comments. "What does he want, no one comments on the CPP internal debate?" he asked, adding the debate has become a public issue.
