BFN [Text] Budapest, 9 Jun (MTI) -- "The election victory of the Socialist Party does not mean a leftist turn in Hungary, as the votes reflect, by and large, equal support for the three political trends: the left, the conservatives, and the liberals," President Arpad Goncz and Christian Democratic People's Party (KDNP) Chairman Laszlo Surjan agreed in talks at Budapest's parliament on Thursday. The consultation was part of Goncz's recent series of political discussions with parliamentary party leaders. Surjan said he had agreed with the president on the key importance of a smooth transfer of governmental affairs. They both supposed that a coalition government would be formed. Regarding the KDNP's future, Surjan said the party should break out from the category of a religious party, and emerge in political life as a socially-committed party with its own system of values and its own message for the whole of society.
