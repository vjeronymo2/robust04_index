BFN [Text] The first stage of talks between Chairman of the Chinese State Council Li Peng and Turkmenian President Saparmurad Niyazov ended in Ashkhabad [Ashgabat] on Thursday. The two sides discussed the possibilities of economic cooperation between the two countries. Niyazov and Li Peng expressed interest in developing contacts in trade, light, fuel and energy and chemical industries, Head of the Information and Press Department of the Chinese Foreign Ministry Shen Guofang told a briefing of journalists in Ashkhabad on Thursday. In his words, "the signing of four-five intergovernmental agreements is possible" on 22 April, but "their texts are yet to be coordinated." As INTERFAX learned from well-informed sources, the Turkmenian leadership is considering the proposal by the Chinese side to grant a credit worth 50 million yuan ($6 million). The guests also suggested discussing the joint construction of two plants in Turkmenistan for the production of bricks and metallic items for seismo-proof building. The two sides also discussed the possibility of building a gas pipeline Turkmenistan-China, but a representative of the Chinese Foreign Ministry said that this issue "was not discussed in detail, because the project demands specific preparation and technical and economic foundation." The talks will continue on 22 April.
