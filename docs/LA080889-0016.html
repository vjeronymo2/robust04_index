August 8, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

POLITICAL LABELS

Philip Dunne's excellent column "Pinning Down Political Labels" (Editorial Page, July 29) pointed out the semantic absurdities going about these days. Preposterous people, such as James Watt, are being described by equally preposterous -- and tortured -- phrases, "radical conservative," for one.

A solution to this, it seems to me, would be the revival of a word which seems to have vanished, without a trace, from the American political vocabulary, namely: reactionary. It is not an epithet. It is a simple, direct, description of a political viewpoint. Its return to our language would spare us the absurdities and euphemisms which now irritate us.

Calling James Watt, Jesse Helms, and others too numerous to mention, "conservatives" (no matter how you qualify the word) is about as accurate as calling Hitler a troublemaker.

ELLIOTT REID

Los Angeles
