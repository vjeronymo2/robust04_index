BFN [`DW' report: "CDU Remains Strongest Force in Saxony"] [Excerpt] Although the Christian Democratic Union [CDU] was able to defend its position at the Saxony communal elections as the leading political force in Saxony, it received fewer votes at the municipal and district elections, as well as at the district and town council elections of cities that do not form part of districts, than in 1990. According to the preliminary result, the CDU received 38.1 percent of the votes, compared to 44.6 percent in 1990. The Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD], Party of Democratic Socialism [PDS], and Alliance 90/Greens registered a clear increase in votes. The SPD received 21.6 percent of the vote (14.7 percent in 1990), the PDS 16.3 percent (11.6 percent in 1990), and Alliance 90/Greens 7.7 percent (4.5 percent in 1990). The Free Democratic Party received 7.5 percent of the votes, compared to 6.3 percent in 1990, With 0.1 percent of the votes nationwide, the Republikaner suffered a clear political rebuff.
