940406 FT 06 APR 94 / International Company News: IBM plans sale of financing arm IBM is to sell its fledgling investment management business to Fleet Financial in a deal which could bring the Rhodes Island-based banking group around Dollars 1bn in assets. IBM Credit, the computer company's financing arm, began a mutual funds busi-ness in 1990 and currently manages Dollars 642m held in 68,000 accounts. It also handles Dollars 349m in 34,000 money market accounts. The sale, expected to be completed by the end of June, continues the move by US banks into the retail investment management business. Fleet already manages some Dollars 4bn in a range of mutual funds. Fleet will take over the management and admin-istration of the mutual funds, and will offer money market account holders similar Fleet products.
