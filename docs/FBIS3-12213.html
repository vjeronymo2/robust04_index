Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Hisham Milhim dispatch from Washington] [Excerpt] The Governments of Syria, Lebanon, and Jordan have decided to suspend bilateral peace negotiations with Israel in Washington before the end of the last week of talks on Wednesday. This decision is to put on record their vehement denunciation of the Ibrahimi Mosque massacre and to express their solidarity with the Palestinian people. Syrian delegation head Muwaffaq al-'Allaf communicated this united Arab stand to peace negotiations coordinator Dennis Ross on Sunday afternoon. Al-'Allaf told Radio Monte Carlo: In light of the circumstances resulting from the massacre, we found that it is not logical or suitable to pursue the negotiations. We also want to express our solidarity with the Palestinian people after this barbaric attack. The suspension of negotiations takes place three days before they were scheduled to end.
