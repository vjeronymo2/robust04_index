May 31, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

MISANDROSY AND MILLS COLLEGE STUDENTS' BATTLE TO BAR MEN

Arnold gives misogyny a bad name. His raving attack on the Mills students -- and all women -- is a good example of why Mills students want to keep their school the way it is -- distinct and independent.

It is obvious that what they want at Mills is "their own space." It has nothing to do with man-hating or hating of anyone else. And if it did, that's their business!

When I attended Cal Berkeley and visited friends at Mills, there never was any hatred evident. And Arnold complains of the notion that men are perceived to be "good (only) for making money." But Michael Milken, Ivan Boesky, Ronald Reagan and Charles Keating didn't come from Mills, did they, Father?

ANDREW LIBERMAN

Los Angeles
