BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 10 May. The Venezuelan Central Bank issued a communique the night of 2 May indicating that the Consumer Price Index registered a 3.3 percent increase in April. This increase, added to the figures accumulated through March, reveals a 12.6 percent inflation rate for the first quarter of 1994. (Caracas EL GLOBO in Spanish 3 May 94 p 11) Today, Venezuelan Central Bank sources revealed that the drop in international reserves for April was estimated at $900 million. According to these sources, the drop in reserves during the first quarter of 1994 was due to falling oil revenues, mistrust unleashed by the Banco Latino scandal, and huge foreign debt payments. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1604 GMT 5 May 94) During the first quarter of 1994, Citgo Petroleum Corporation, a subsidiary of Venezuelan Petroleum in the United States, registered net profits of $43.300 million, which represents a 133 percent increase over profits registered during the same period in 1993. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 5 May 94 p E-7) Berend Roosen, president of the National Council of Commerce and Services, pointed out that his organization is not responsible for the recent outbreak of price increases resulting from the devaluation of the bolivar. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 5 May 94 p E-8) In light of the fiscal crisis, the government is considering selling between 10 and 20 percent of the shares of the National Telephone Company of Venezuela for the second half of 1994 as a last resort. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 5 May 94 p E-8) It has been unofficially reported that international reserves dropped $900 million in April. The sources added that the largest drop was reported between 25 and 29 April when it was announced that the president of the Central Bank of Venezuela would be resigning and an interest rate drop was possible. Central Bank of Venezuela authorities said the bank will not release official figures for the month of April. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 5 May 94 p E-1) Financial sources have reported that between January and April, the eight banks for whom the state has intervened have had to pay $550 billion bolivars. The run on the banks forced the institutions to turn to the government. According to recent studies conducted by the Banks Superintendency, overall losses amount to approximately 359 billion bolivars. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 7 May 94 p E- 1) Between 1 January and 6 May, the bolivar has been devaluated 22.8 percent. A marketing research firm reported that this will represent reduced purchasing power and greater unemployment. (Caracas EL UNIVERSAL in Spanish 8 May 94 p 2-1) The Foreign Trade Institute has reported that Colombia has replaced the United States as the main market for Venezuelan nonpetroleum products. In 1993, Venezuela exported $808.237 million in nonpetroleum products to Colombia. (Paris AFP in Spanish 2114 GMT 9 May 94)
