Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Moscow Mayor's Office and its government refuted the allegations that a decision had been made to rebury the body of Vladimir Lenin. The Moscow government transferred a statement to Interfax saying that the Moscow authorities had made no decisions on reburial of Lenin nor about his mausoleum. In accordance with the Russian law on the status of Moscow, all buildings and facilities on the Moscow territory on which the legislative, executive and judicial bodies are situated belong to the Russian Federation. The statement underlined that the Moscow City's Executive Committee had mentioned the Lenin Mausoleum among architectural monuments in March, 1987. "This is to say that only the federal bodies can decide on the future of the mausoleum. However, the position of the Moscow government which defends the historical monument, must be taken into consideration," the statement says.
