BFN [Text] This year's annual "May 15 Peace Parade" (sponsored by the Okinawa Peace Movement Center) kicked off at 0930 (local time) on 13 May at three different locations in Okinawa, including the Peace Prayer Park in Itoman city and the Nago city office. The parade has traditionally been held to appeal for the establishment of peace in Okinawa through the removal of U.S. military bases. Participants in the parade will continue marching until they reach their destination, which is a beachside park in Kadena Township where a general rally is to be held on 15 May. The event has been held 16 times in the past. A total of about 2,000 participants, including about 800 from other prefectures, are expected to participate in the parade. About 5,000 participants are expected to gather at the general rally. A ceremony was held at the Peace Prayer Park to mark the beginning of the parade for the group of marchers who plan to take the southern route starting in Itoman city. There were about 400 participants at the ceremony. In a speech greeting the participants, Zenshun Arakaki, chairman of the Peace Movement Center, said: "The existence of a `secret deal between the Japanese and U.S. Governments for bringing nuclear weapons to Okinawa' has been revealed. We have learned that the three nonnuclear principles were only a hoax. Moreover, the ruling coalition government is planning to enact emergency legislation by taking advantage of the issue of the DPRK's (North Korea) suspected nuclear weapons development program. What we really need is a nuclear inspection of U.S. military bases on Okinawa." Arakaki encouraged the marchers by saying, "Let us complete our parade." In their speeches, local representatives of various parties one after the other stressed the seriousness of the issue of the secret deal regarding bringing nuclear weapons to Okinawa. Representatives from other prefectures also said: "For Okinawa, the postwar period is not yet over. The reversion of Okinawa to Japan was a fraud."
