Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- Pavol Kanis, a member of the Communist Party for many years and its leading representative since the November 1989 "velvet revolution," was today appointed defense minister in the new Slovak Government. Kanis, doctor of philosophy, was born on August 27, 1948, in Prague. After leaving secondary school, he was employed as a worker in the Povazske Strojarne Engineering Company in central Slovakia. In 1972 he graduated from the Philosophical Faculty of Bratislava's Comenius University and worked as a scientist in the Bratislava- based Institute of Marxism and Leninism. In the first half of 1990 he was a deputy to the House of the People of the then Federal Assembly. In June 1990-June 1992 electoral term he was a deputy to the House of Nations of the Assembly and a member of the Assembly's Presidium. He joined the Communist Party of Czechoslovakia (KSC) in 1981. Shortly after the November 1989 revolution, which toppled the communist regime in the country, he became a member and later a secretary of the Executive Committee of the Communist Party of Slovakia (KSS). From November 1990 to August 1991, he headed the KSC Federal Council, and later, until November 1991, the Federal Council of the Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia (KSCM) and the Democratic Left Party (SDL) -- the two successor parties to the KSC in the Czech Lands and Slovakia. Since December 1991, Kanis has been SDL deputy chairman. In the June 1992 elections he was elected a SDL deputy to the Slovak Parliament.
