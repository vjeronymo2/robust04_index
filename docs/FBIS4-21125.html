Narcotics Control Strategy Report 6 April 1994 LATIN AMERICA COLOMBIA

Congressmen React Negatively to Report

Congressmen React Negatively to Report PA0604214294 Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1730 GMT 6 Apr 94 PA0604214294 Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 Spanish BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Excerpts] Today the "National Newscast" learned about the U.S. State Department's annual report on the struggle against drug trafficking. The report places the country in the dock and notes that corruption is widespread in Colombia, particularly in the judicial branch. [passage omitted describing contents of report] The policy of surrendering to justice which the State Department criticizes has also been a controversial issue in Colombia. We consulted congressmen who approved the Penal Procedure Code: [Begin recording] [Senator Hernan Echeverri] We politely reject this insinuation and this sort of U.S. intervention in our domestic affairs. [Representative Ramiro Lucio] The Colombian Congress, autonomously--without consulting the U.S. State Department, the Belgian Justice Ministry, or any other authority anywhere in the world--decided to approve the Penal Procedure Code basically including the antidrug policy that the national government presented to us. [Senator Alberto Santofimio Botero] What is happening is that we are increasingly losing our sovereignty. We are going to wind up asking the gringos what we are to do in order to issue laws in Colombia. [Representative Edmundo Guevara] It is easier to see the mote in your brother's eye than to see the beam in your own. It is in the United States where sentences are negotiated. We copied from them. [end recording]
