Language: Vietnamese Article Type:BFN [Report by Dinh Khai on the ceremony held in Hanoi on the morning of 16 March to welcome Thai Prime Minister Chuan Likphai -- recorded] [Summary] At 1130 sharp, the motorcade escorting Prime Minister Chuan Likphai and other distinguished Thai guests arrived at the Presidential Palace. On hand to welcome Prime Minister Chuan and his entourage were Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet; Deputy Prime Minister Tran Duc Luong; Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam; Le Xuan Trinh, minister and director of the Government Office; Dau Ngoc Xuan, minister and director of the State Committee for Cooperation and Investment; Vietnamese Ambassador to Thailand Le Cong Phung; Le At Hoi, chairman of the Hanoi City People's Committee; and many leading officials of various ministries, departments, sectors, and mass organizations at the central level and in Hanoi. Also present at the welcoming ceremony were members of the diplomatic corps, representatives of international organizations in Hanoi, and many Vietnamese and foreign journalists. "As the motorcade stopped, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet walked to the door of the limousine carrying Prime Minister Chuan Likphai to greet him. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet then invited Chuan to join him on the honorary stand while the military band played the national anthems of Thailand and Vietnam." Next, Prime Minister Chuan Likphai reviewed the guard of honor before being introduced to Vietnamese officials, members of the diplomatic corps, and representatives of international organizations in Hanoi. "Immediately following the official welcoming ceremony, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet cordially received Prime Minister Chuan Likphai at the Presidential Palace. On behalf of the Vietnamese Government, Vo Van Kiet welcomed Chuan and his entourage on their official visit to Vietnam. He highly valued the visit and expressed his belief that this event will contribute to promoting cooperative ties between the two countries in the days ahead. "In his reply speech, Chuan thanked Vo Van Kiet and the Vietnamese Government and people for according his delegation a very warm welcome. He said: We have waited a long time for this opportunity to visit Vietnam and we are convinced that this event will further strengthen the good relations between the two countries. "The talks between the Vietnamese Government delegation headed by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet and the Thai Government delegation headed by Prime Minister Chuan Likphai took place at the Presidential Palace this afternoon. The two sides informed each other about the situation in each country and exchanged views on issues of common concern in a bid to further develop the friendly and cooperative ties between the two countries. "A ceremony was held immediately following the talks to sign agreements on tourist cooperation and cooperation between the Vietnam Chamber of Commerce and Industry and the Federation of Thai Industries."
