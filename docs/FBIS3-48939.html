Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Belgrade, 31 Dec (TANJUG) -- Due to the shortage of electric power, Belgrade is now divided into only two blocs, instead of the previous four -- those having electricity and those not. It is not known for how long this situation will last; it depends on the resumption of normal coal production in the Kolubara complex, Miodrag Simic, the technical operations manager of the Belgrade Electricity board said at a news conference today. [passage omitted] According to information available to the Belgrade Electricity Board it is not certain that the miners will go back to work and resume coal deliveries. According to Simic, if the power plant in Obrenovac does not resume work, half the Belgrade population will have only candles for light during New Year's Eve. [passage omitted]
