BFN [Text] Washington, April 25 (XINHUA) -- The United States today sent best wishes to the new Japanese prime minister, describing Tsutomu Hata as "a well-known friend of the United States." Hata "shared his predecessor's desire to implement reforms in Japan. We look forward to working with him on a wide range of issues," White House spokeswoman Dee Dee Myers said. "He was just elected this morning, so we'll have to wait to see how that situation shapes up domestically," she told a White House press briefing. "We will work with Prime Minister Hata's new government," Myers said. Hata, 58, was former foreign minister of Japan. He replaced Morihiro Hosokawa, who resigned amid allegations of financial wrongdoing.
