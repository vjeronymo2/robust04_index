Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] Tomiichi Murayama, chairman of the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ], in an interview with YOMIURI SHIMBUN on 27 December, said it was highly possible that if the political reform bills failed to pass the current special Diet session, either the lower house would be dissolved or the cabinet would resign en masse. He added emphatically: "Judging from the way the economy looks now, unemployment will peak around February or March. We must not create a governmental vacuum now by dissolving the Diet. We must think of a way to avoid such a situation." "That is one of the reasons why I say the bill to prevent corruption must be acted on first," he said. Murayama's remarks made it clear that he wants the ruling and opposition parties to reach agreement.
