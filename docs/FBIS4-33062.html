BFN [Text] Mexico City, 31 May (NOTIMEX) -- A police source has reported that one person was arrested and an unknown number were injured today during a clash between riot police and demonstrators demanding housing in Iztapalapa, in eastern Mexico City. According to the Road and Safety Secretariat [SGPyV], the incident occurred early this morning when policemen dispersed members of the so-called "Francisco Villa People's Front" [Frente Popular Francisco Villa] who were blocking an avenue with two buses. The SGPyV said nine policemen were injured in the clash; however, the source failed to mention the number of wounded demonstrators. The source also failed to mention how many riot policemen participated in the operation. Eyewitnesses calculated that there were approximately 500 riot policemen in the area. The source added that when the riot police intervened, the demonstrators withdrew to a nearby school from where they hurled bottles, sticks, stones, and "Molotov" cocktails at the police, damaging several privately owned vehicles. After the clash, the demonstrators occupied and destroyed the offices of two district attorneys and hurled stones at the policemen while demanding that their arrested leader be released. The arrested leader was released but another demonstrator was arrested, the police source added. The demonstrators failed in their attempt to [words indistinct] and broke windows. Among the injured were three riot policemen, who were intentionally hit by vehicles. The others were injured by "a rain of projectiles thrown at them," the source added.
