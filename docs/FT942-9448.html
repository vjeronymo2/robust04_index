940516 FT 16 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television Benny Hill was one of the earliest British casualties of political correctness. Though his popularity with the general public never waned, he was banished from British television by the feminists. They sought brownie points at smart London parties by having fits of the vapours over Hill's 'sexism'. The fact that his comedy was in direct line of descent from Chaucer and Shakespeare, and that 90 per cent of the audience loved it, was ignored. Yet ironically he became, simultaneously, a huge success in the US despite the greater prevalence of feminism there. Benny Hill - Unseen comprises material produced abroad, late in his life, and not previously screened here (8.00 ITV). Horizon tells how Barry Marshall happened upon the real cause of most stomach ulcers but has had an uphill job persuading the stick-in-the-muds of medicine to abandon their 'stress' diagnosis (8.00 BBC2). The BBC says that today's Panorama will be the one about shenanigans on Westminster council which was supposed to have been shown just before the recent local elections (9.30 BBC1).
