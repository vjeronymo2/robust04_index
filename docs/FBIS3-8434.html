Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Text] The Hungarian parliament has passed the agent bill, which stipulates vetting the past of important officials. According to the law -- which was passed with 177 votes for, 12 against, and 50 abstentions -- parliamentary deputies, government members, the heads of the National Bank of Hungary, and the mayor of Budapest will have to be vetted in future. The past of ambassadors, military and police heads, the top officials and editors of Hungarian Radio, Hungarian Television, MTI, and newspapers with a circulation of more than 30,000 will also have to be checked.
