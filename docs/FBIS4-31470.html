BFN [Text] Prague June 7 (CTK) -- Czech trade during the first quarter was at a deficit of 2.1 billion crowns [Kc], and after four months the deficit diminished to 1.5 billion crowns, according to a government budget report that was given to parliament recently. Czech export to advanced market economies increased by nine percent in the first quarter over the same period last year. Czech exports to Slovakia decreased by 22 percent to 15.5 percent. Exports to former Soviet countries dropped from six to five percent, and to countries with transitional economies, from 6.3 to 5.9 percent. The share of machines and consumer goods was nearly 40 percent, and the export of raw materials increased the most rapidly (from 6.6 to 9.8 percent). Overall, exports decreased by 5.6 percent (5.2 billion crowns) in the first quarter against the first quarter last year, and by 6.5 percent (8.2 billion crowns) during the first four months of 1994 in the same year-on-year comparison. Imports in the first three months of 1994 increased by 18 percent (almost 14 billion crowns), and by 11 percent in the first four months (11.6 billion crowns). Imports from advanced economies increased (by four percent) as well as from countries with transforming economies and from former Soviet states. Slovakia's share in Czech imports decreased from 21 to 15 percent. Export prices grew by 2.5 percent, and import prices by only half a percent. The surplus on the current account was at the same level as in the first quarter of 1993, and the surplus on the capital account of the payments balance was $350 million larger. Overall hard currency reserves were $700 million higher than in the first quarter last year.
