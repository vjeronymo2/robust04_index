BFN [Excerpts] Khartoum, 22 May (SUNA) -- The government delegation to peace talks in Nairobi with the rebel factions arrived in Khartoum on Sunday [22 May] morning. Mr. Muhammad al-Amin Khalifah, speaker of the Transitional National Assembly [TNA] and head of the delegation to the peace talks, confirmed that the government is seriously committed to achieving a just and long-lasting peace with the rebel movement at the next round of talks, scheduled to be held on 18 July. Mr. Khalifah told reporters upon arrival at Khartoum airport that the current round of talks was designed to discuss basic issues of the conflict, but the delay by three days of the arrival of the rebel factions prevented a detailed examination of these issues. [passage omitted] Mr. Khalifah stressed the need for contacts to continue between the government and the rebel movement, as that is the way to consolidate confidence between the two sides.
