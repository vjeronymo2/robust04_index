Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Johannesburg Jan 25 SAPA -- Suspended Pan Africanist Congress [PAC] National Publicity Director Waters Toboti on Tuesday said he had not threatened to unleash chaos if his suspension went ahead. Mr Toboti said in a statement to SAPA his comments on Radio Transkei earlier on Tuesday were in Xhosa and had not been properly translated in subsequent reports. "What I said was people who suspend members of the organisation on false premises are causing chaos and division in the party." Mr Toboti said he still had not been told of his suspension. Earlier, Jaki Seroke of the PAC's publicity and information office told SAPA they were having difficulty contacting Mr Toboti to tell him of his suspension. On Saturday the PAC announced the suspension of Mr Toboti, Central Transkei Regional Secretary Mfanelo Skwatsha and Azanian National Youth Unity member Chargin Mabaso with immediate effect for criticising the PAC's suspension of its armed struggle. Mr Seroke added the PAC had no interest in getting involved in a "slanging match" with Mr Toboti. A decision had been taken, he said.
