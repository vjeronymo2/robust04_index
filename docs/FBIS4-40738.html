BFN [Text] The Public Chamber under President Yeltsin upheld Sergey Filatov's proposal for focusing on the realization of the civic accord agreement on June 23. Filatov is the chief of staff under Boris Yeltsin. The Public Chamber is to have commissions on political stability, social and economic problems, administrative makeup and interethnic relations, security, and science research, education and culture. The five commissions will be expected to supervise the realization of the civic accord agreement and offer advice to the conciliatory commission. In Filatov's view, they must have a membership of twenty nine and bring together representatives of political movements and parties, parliamentary factions, public organizations, labor unions, religious communities, etc. Arkadiy Volskiy, Mark Masarskiy, Anatoliy Dolganov, Viktor Andreyev and Vladimir Kudriavtsev were presented as choices for the commission's chairmen. According to Filatov, they represent "a wide spectrum of the Russian public" because their political affiliations as well as jobs they hold are very different.
