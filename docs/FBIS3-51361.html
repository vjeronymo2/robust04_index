Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Mario Jovel from the "Up to Date" newscast] [Text] Augusto Ramirez Ocampo, chief of the UN Observers for El Salvador [Onusal], has clarified that his mission will leave the country at the conclusion of the peace agreement verification process and not immediately after the elections, as one local newspaper has reported. According to the diplomat, the Onusal mandate will continue past the March elections. [Begin Ramirez Ocampo recording] Onusal's obligation is to verify the peace agreements, and therefore Onusal's duration is determined by compliance with the agreements. [end recording] Ramirez Ocampo said the concern some political sectors have over the limited number of party representatives on department boards has been resolved by a bill that the Supreme Electoral Tribunal presented Monday to the Legislative Assembly. According to the Onusal spokesman, the bill states that all the parties, including the coalitions, can have a party representative in both the department and the municipal boards. [Begin Ramirez Ocampo recording] It is obvious that no party can be deprived of representation, but it is also obvious, I believe, that there cannot be more than one representative. This means that it is important that all contending political parties are represented equally at the electoral department boards. [end recording] As Ocampo sees it, it must be guaranteed that the electoral department and municipal boards function legally.
