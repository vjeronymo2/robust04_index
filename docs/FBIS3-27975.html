Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, February 1 (KCNA) -- The Royal Tomb of Wang Kon, the founder of Koryo, has been rebuilt. Koryo was the first unified state of Korea which existed for 475 years from 918. The tomb situated at the foot of Mt. Songak in Kaesong consists of a grand domed grave on three stages of foundation stones, a portrait of the king, stone sculptures portraying celebrated civil servants and warriors who displayed their wisdom and courage at the time of the unification of three states, paintings on the fight of the Koryo people and the tomb gate and a shrine which represent the then architecture. In front of the tomb gate there stands "monument to the reconstruction of the royal tomb of Wang Kon, founder of Koryo" handwritten by the great leader President Kim Il-song. The visitors can go into the grave and see the interior and mural paintings. A wide range of park lies before the tomb. The rebuilt tomb was opened to the general public with a due ceremony Monday. In his address at the ceremony, Yim Su-man, chief secretary of the Kaesong municipal committee of the Workers' Party of Korea and chairman of the Kaesong municipal people's committee, said the Royal Tomb of Wang Kon, the founder of Koryo, was rebuilt under the wise guidance and care of the great leader Comrade Kim Il-song and the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il. "This means that we have restored another valuable cultural heritage in order to hand down to the posterity the traditions of national history ranging from Kojoson (ancient Korea) founded by Tangun, which marked the entry of the Korean nation into an era of state, an era of civilization, down to Koryo, the first unified state succeeding the powerful state in the east Koguryo and Palhae," he said.
