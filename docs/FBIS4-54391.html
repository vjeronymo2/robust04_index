BFN [Text] Sofia, April 26 (BTA) -- The leaderships of the two major political powers, the Union of Democratic Forces (SDS) and the Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP), today discussed the future of the cabinet, their representatives said today at their regular weekly news briefings. "The time of waiting and half-way measures is running out," said BSP deputy floor leader Georgi Pirinski, voicing his party's stand. BSP expects that the prime minister's ideas about cabinet changes would become clear by the end of the week. "The cabinet should go instead of reshuffling," said SDS leader Filip Dimitrov. According to BSP analyses, confrontation in society has been further exacerbated, yet Bulgaria has the real chances of stabilizing its law and order and its foreign economic relations. The Socialists have not changed their demands to the Cabinet; yet they have never raised the issue of the restoration of ministries or reinstatement of persons. "Structural and personnel changes lie within Prime Minister Berov's own discretion," Mr. Pirinski said. In his view, Mr. Berov has not shown he is decisive enough to "make a U-turn in the cabinet's performance." BSP set several demands to the government: resumption of political dialogue with the trade unions, stabilization of the national budget, setting in motion structural adjustment, unblocking privatization, etc. "Unless Mr. Berov makes his ideas clear by the end of the week, BSP will reconsider its stand," the Socialists said at their news conference. Later in the day, Socialists' leaders held an unscheduled meeting with Prime Minister Berov, national television reported in its main newscast. "The prime minister alone can say how far things have been specified with him. I can't say that because what is going on now is a change in his notions, frame of mind and intentions. There will be continuous changes in the coming days and weeks, they will be exceedingly vigorous," BSP leader Zhan Videnov told a TV interviewer. The government Press Office specified that Prime Minister Lyuben Berov will address parliament tomorrow [27 April]. SDS leader Filip Dimitrov described the intended cabinet changes as "the latest trick of anti-national behaviour on the part of the communist parliamentary majority." At a regular meeting today, the SDS top leadership considered its reaction which will depend on whether the cabinet changes will be introduced before parliament on Wednesday or not. Mr. Dimitrov, however, did not specify the strategies adopted. The parliamentary New Union for Democracy (SDS expellees and defectors) and the MP's of the ethnic Turks' Movement for Rights and Freedoms (DPS) today met to discuss the intended policy which Prime Minister Berov is expected to set forth before parliament tomorrow, as well as his concept of structural cabinet changes. The two factions agreed that no structural and personnel changes are necessary until parliament's Easter recess, said the spokesman for the parliamentary DPS Yunal Lyutfi. During and after the recess, the two factions will continue their consultations with Prime Minister Berov on the government's programme and the need of personnel and structural cabinet changes. The forthcoming structural changes in the cabinet were discussed today by Prime Minister Berov with Mr. Aleksandur Tomov, leader of the Civil Alliance for the Republic (a radical movement which broke away from the BSP), national radio reported. Mr. Tomov said that certain political forces should not take advantage of the grave economic situation to put pressure on the government. According to him, in order to avoid a power vacuum and an economic collapse, political forces must reach a consensus on the changes needed by Prof. Berov's cabinet.
