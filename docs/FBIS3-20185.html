Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Erlangen, Germany, March 29 (AFP) -- German industrial group Siemens has set up a joint venture with Chinese firm Machine Tool Electric Appliance Factory (SMTEAF) in Suzhou, eastern China, the German group announced on Tuesday. It said the joint venture, to employ 1,400 people in an initial phase, would make low-voltage electricity distribution equipment. Investments will amount to 100 million marks over five years, the announcement said. Siemens will hold a majority stake in the company, to be called Siemens Electrical Apparatus Ltd. Siemens senior executive Carl-Heiner Thomas said the purpose of the joint venture is to commercialize Siemens technology on the Chinese market and acquire a larger presence on Southern Asian markets.
