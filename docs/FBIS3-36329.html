Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] At its evening session today, the Federation Council discussed the status of its deputies and issues related to the maintenance of their activities. The deputies decided to return to the consideration of their status later, since the State Duma had not yet passed the law "On the status of the deputy of the Federal Assembly." The session approved the structure of the chamber's staff and set their salaries equal to those of government support staff. Each deputy will have an unlimited number of assistants and manage a fund equal to 80 minimum salaries plus guaranteed bonuses and compensations to pay their salaries out of. The Federation Council scheduled its next session for February 18. The draft agenda of the next meeting includes discussing the findings of the council's commission in the area of the Ossetian-Ingush conflict, approving the composition of parliamentary committees, looking into the results of the activities by the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly and passing a plan of legislative activities. The Federation Council also plans to invite Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin on the same day. As was announced before, Russian President Boris Yeltsin is expected to make his annual address to the Federal Assembly on social and economic policies on February 18.
