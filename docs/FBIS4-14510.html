BFN [Text] On 31 March the Moldovan parliament has accepted the resignation of the government. Within the next few days a new prime minister and a new Cabinet will be appointed. On Thursday, the parliament elected a permanent bureau of the legislative body of the Republic to replace the presidium of the former parliament. The regulation stipulates that the bureau consists of nine members. Apart from the chairman of parliament and his deputies, the bureau includes six representatives from parliamentary factions. Chairmen of 10 permanent commission were also elected. Representatives of the ruling Agrarian Democratic Party of Moldova were appointed as heads of eight commissions. Two commissions are led by representatives of the bloc of the Socialist Party and the Unity movement which gathered 22 percent of votes in parliamentary elections.
