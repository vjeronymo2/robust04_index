Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Federation Council deputies today drew up a ballot paper for the election of the chairman of the upper chamber of parliament. [passage omitted] In order to be elected speaker of the Federation Council, it is necessary to receive 86 votes, INTERFAX points out. In an interview with that agency, Vladimir Shumeyko declared that, if elected, he will leave the post of first vice premier of the Russian Government. According to Shumeyko, a decision will soon be adopted in the Federation Council according to which the leadership of the chamber, unlke ordinary deputies, will operate on a permanent basis.
