Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 7 Feb (MTI) -- The Hungarian Army and the Ministry of the Interior's Fire and Civil Defence National Command have set up an Accident Information Centre in Budapest, in order to avert nuclear accidents and catastrophes. The centre, to work as part of the operation staff of the nuclear accident prevention government committee, is to coordinate the national radiation monitoring, signalling and control systems of the two organizations. The network is to provide continuous information from 60 points throughout the country on the radioactive radiation level. The measuring stations are to measure the gamma doses in the air, which is termed harmful to health if measuring 500 nanosievert per hour. (measurements between 1000 and 5000 nsv were registered in the vicinity of the Chernobyl nuclear accident.) With the decline in the danger of nuclear explosions and nuclear attacks, the accident information centre will mainly focus on nuclear power plant accidents, the delivery of nuclear materials, and possible meteorite impacts. The majority of the measuring stations will be operated near barracks, as they are affiliated to the army, however, the Central Physics Research Institute of Budapest and the Paks Nuclear Power Plant (southern Hungary) will also carry out measurements. Data will be continuously evaluated and stored in the centre, and if higher than average figures are reported, immediate measures can be taken by involving the civil defence and army units.
