Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Brussels, Feb 14 (AFP) -- The North Atlantic Treaty Organisation is largely satisfied with a four-day-old ceasefire around the Bosnia-Herzegovina capital of Sarajevo, a senior NATO official said here Monday [14 February]. NATO deputy secretary-general Sergio Silvio Balanzino told journalists: "For the time being we notice with satisfaction that the truce, the ceasefire, is being by and large respected." The ceasefire began on Thursday one day after NATO warned that it would launch air strikes against Bosnian Serb artillery encircling Sarajevo unless it was withdrawn or placed under United Nations control by midnight next Sunday. But Balanzino brushed aside a question about demands from the Bosnian Serbs that they should be allowed to retain the artillery in their own quarters, subject to UN surveillance, instead of physically handing the weapons over. "The goal is to have the guns and heavy weapons put under control. Now there could be a variety of controls. As to techniques, these are steps to be worked out," Balanzino said, refusing to elaborate any further. [passage omitted] A senior UN officer warned in Sarajevo that air strikes were inevitable if no substantial withdrawal of the Serb guns had taken place by Friday.
