921208 FT 08 DEC 92 / UN Mozambique peace force plan Mr Boutros Boutros Ghali, the United Nations secretary-general, proposed yesterday the establishment of a UN peace-keeping operation, involving about 7,500 troops, police and civilians, to help monitor an agreement ending a devastating civil war in Mozambique, Reuter reports from the UN in New York. The UN has already launched peace-keeping missions this year in Yugoslavia, Cambodia and Somalia, in addition to maintaining nine existing operations in such places as Lebanon, Cyprus, El Salvador and on the Iraq-Kuwait border.
