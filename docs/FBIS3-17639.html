Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yevgeniy Belovitskiy and others] [Text] Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk March 28 TASS -- Elections to the Sakhalin regional duma (parliament) were recognised as valid, TASS learned from the vote-counting commission on Monday. 12 out of 16 duma members have been elected. The polls failed in the southern Sakhalin constituency where four duma mandates were competed for. Only 18.3 per cent of the electorate came to the polls there. However, the local duma with its 12 members has a quorum which enables it to work. Irkutsk -- 45 per cent of the electorate came to the polls in the Irkutsk region in Siberia to elect the governor, local parliament, heads of administrations, cities and towns, urban and rural councils. A vote on the main provisions of the charter of the region also took place. Current Governor Yuriy Nozhikov retained his post. Elections to local parliament were valid in 42 out of 45 constituencies, while the polls to local self-government bodies were valid in all constituencies. Novgorod -- Elections to local duma were declared valid in 19 out of 27 constituencies, according to Igor Zlatin, a member of the vote-counting commission. An average of 37 per cent of voters came to the polls. However, elections failed in the regional centre where turnout was insufficient in all of its eight constituencies. Eight out of 19 elected members of the new local parliament are heads of city and district administrations.
