BFN [Editorial report] Kiev Radio Ukraine World Service in Ukrainian at 1000 GMT on 27 June broadcasts a live relay from the Central Electoral Commission, which includes a 12-minute live interview with Viktor Pohorilko, deputy chairman of the Central Electoral Commission. Pohorilko gives the following preliminary information about the presidential election results: - Poltava Oblast: Leonid Kravchuk 29.5 percent, Leonid Kuchma 28.4 percent, Oleksandr Moroz 18.2 percent, Volodymyr Lanovyy 14 percent. - Kirovograd Oblast: Leonid Kravchuk 31 percent, Leonid Kuchma 20 percent, Oleksandr Moroz 22 percent, Volodymyr Lanovyy 17 percent. - Rovno Oblast: Leonid Kravchuk 70.7 percent. - Lvov Oblast: Leonid Kravchuk approximately 70 percent. - Kherson Oblast: Leonid Kravchuk 25 percent, Leonid Kuchma 34 percent, Volodymyr Lanovyy 9.9 percent, and Oleksandr Moroz 19.5 percent. - Republic of Crimea: Leonid Kuchma 82.5 percent, Leonid Kravchuk 7 percent. Petro Talanchuk and Ivan Plyushch got 2-3 percent. - Sevastopol: Leonid Kuchma 82 percent. Petro Talanchuk and Ivan Plyushch got 2-3 percent, and Volodymyr Lanovyy and Oleksandr Moroz got slightly more than Talanchuk and Plyushch. In conclusion, Pohorilko emphasizes that these are only preliminary results, not the final figures. In general, the candidates winning the most votes are Leonid Kravchuk and Leonid Kuchma; the candidates with the next largest number of votes are Oleksandr Moroz and Volodymyr Lanovyy.
