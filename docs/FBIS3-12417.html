Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin says he hopes and knows that the peace negotiations will be resumed. Speaking to the Conference of Presidents of Major American Jewish Organizations, he said the massacre at the mosque in Hebron would be remembered as the Black Friday of the Jewish people. Rabin was asked if he would accept the PLO's demand for an international force in the territories to protect the Palestinians: [Begin Rabin recording] In the DOP [Declaration of Principles], it is written international presence but not international forces presence, and we offer that in the context of the Gaza-Jericho First, we will not oppose representatives or international presence of certain individuals in Gaza and Jericho -- not a force, not armed people. This is part of the DOP. Whatever we sign, we'll stick to it. All the other demands I believe that you mentioned -- dismantling of settlements, no; disarm of all arms from settlers, no. [end recording]
