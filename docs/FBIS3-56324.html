Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Boris Krivoshey] [Text] Moscow January 13 TASS -- The joint working group for concluding a peace agreement between Russia and Japan, within the framework of which talks are being conducted over the disputed Kuril Islands, is expected to meet in the second half of February, a high-ranking official of the Russian Foreign Ministry told TASS on Thursday. He denied the reports by the foreign mass media alleging that Russia had applied to Japan with a request to postpone indefinitely the meeting of the Russo-Japanese working group earlier scheduled for late January. At present, the Russian diplomat noted, officials of the Russian and Japanese foreign ministries are agreeing upon the concrete date of the meeting.
