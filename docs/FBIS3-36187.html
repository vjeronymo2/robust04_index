Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] Early this morning the presidential airplane took off for Tbilisi. The program for Yeltsin's one-day trip to the Georgian capital is very busy. [Begin Yeltsin recording] We will sign the treaty today, after all, but we will wait with ratification until the conflict is completely settled, and this will suit both our Caucasian republics and, I think, Georgia and Russia as a whole. Together with the CSCE and EEC, Russia is mediating in order to somehow halt the bloodshed there; second, to protect the border jointly; third, jointly to exploit railways and protect them against attacks, accordingly; fourth, a full-scale treaty to be signed with Georgia as well as military-technical agreements, an accord.
