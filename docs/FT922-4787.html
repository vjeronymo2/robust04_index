920603 FT 03 JUN 92 / International Company News: Volkswagen to invest Dollars 2.3bn in Brazil plants VOLKSWAGEN chairman Mr Carl Hahn said that the carmaker planned to invest Dollars 2.3bn until 1995 to modernise the company's plants in Brazil and expand business in the country, according to presidential spokesman Mr Pedro Luiz Rodriguez, Reuter reports from Rio de Janeiro. Mr Rodriguez said that Mr Hahn made the announcement when he met President Fernando Collor de Mello in Rio. Mr Hahn is in Brazil to take part in the United Nations Earth Summit. Volkswagen is Brazil's largest car manufacturer, and in 1990 sold over 320,000 units in the country.
