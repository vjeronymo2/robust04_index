920511 FT 11 MAY 92 / Arts: Today's Television The makers of Channel 4's six-part series Female Parts (9.00) are clearly hoping to hook a huge audience by starting with the most provocative of the series: 'Rude Women'. This, we are assured, is 'a full frontal assault on the myth that women don't like sex, don't talk about it and never make the first move', which sounds like a trio of straw, er, women which should not take too much knocking down. The Pussy Posse hold a safe sex party, the editor of one of the new spate of women's sex magazines explains just what you cannot show, and Susanna, who has a drawer full of chains, explains how she has never found any man tough enough to tie her up. Horizon looks into the question of who really discovered the virus that causes Aids. In 1984 American Robert Gallo claimed the credit, but today most of those involved accept that the discovery rightfully belongs to Dr Luc Montagnier of the Pasteur Institute in Paris (8.10 BBC2). In Panorama the FT's John Plender tries to establish whether, in future, owning a house in Britain will still be a sure way of doubling your money (9.30 BBC1).
