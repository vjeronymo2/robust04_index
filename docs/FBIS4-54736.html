BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 4/5/1994 -- Radu Campeanu has been acknowledged as legitimate president of the National Liberal Party (PNL) by virtue of a court sentence passed yesterday, 3 May, by the Bucharest Municipal Court, which ruled against a similar claim by the group led by Mircea Ionescu-Quintus. The conflict between the two factions of the PNL, generated by the wish to reign supreme in the liberal movement, opposes Radu Campeanu, liberal member in the 1940's, repatriated in 1990 after years of residence in France, who held the PNL chairmanship over 1990-1993, and ran for the presidency of Romania in 1990, to Mircea Ionescu-Quintus, lawyer and writer, liberal minister of justice in Mr. Theodor Stolojan's cabinet, 1991-1992. The conflict and tensions between these two factions and between them and other liberal splinter groups have inflicted, commentators observe, a negative image on Romania's main liberal party, which the 1990 elections had earned an important number of seats in the Parliament that functioned until the next elections of 1992 when it failed to gain the minimal share of votes for parliamentary representation. At that time, liberal leaders blamed the failure on Radu Campeanu's strong ego, who on the one hand decided to withdraw from the principal opposition bloc known as the Democratic Convention of Romania (CDR) and on the other blundered tragically proposing the former sovereign of Romania, Mihai I, who abdicated in 1947, as candidate for presidency, both moves meeting popular disagreement. Starting from February 1994, PNL has had a bi-cephalous leadership, one being Mircea Ionescu-Quintus, elected PNL president in 1993, and the other Radu Campeanu, whom the "Campeanu" grouping designated as president of the National Liberal Party at an extraordinary congress. After this the story started of what the press called "the fight for the party's stamp" and the fight for the Bucharest central seat of the party, until the sides turned to the law for a verdict. The resolution made yesterday by the Bucharest Municipal Court is practically the rejection of a contention lodged by the Quintus wing against a previous resolution. Mr. Quintus's group announced last evening that they would not abandon legal actions until the situation of the PNL was clarified and intimated that many PNL branches had extended them their support.
