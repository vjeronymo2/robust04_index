BFN [Text] Budapest, 6 June (MTI) -- "I sincerely hope that Hungary's new government will not forget its obligations towards ethnic Hungarians beyond the border, that it will use all possible means -- including international forums -- to further their interests, and that it will hold preliminary discussions on all related political moves with legitimate leaders of cross-border Hungarian organizations beforehand." ``I also hope that the new government will require that policy on ethnic Hungarians abroad be invariably based on a consensus between parliamentary parties. "I shall be in a position to warn my successor in the post of prime minister of this obligation," says a letter from Peter Boross to Oradea Bishop Laszlo Tokes, which was released by the Government Spokesperson's Office to MTI on Monday [6 June]. Boross was replying to Tokes's open letter, published in the Budapest daily MAGYAR NEMZET on April 11, asking for Hungary's political support for Transylvanian Hungarians, urging the conclusion of a bilateral cooperation accord between the Hungarian Government and the Hungarian Democratic Federation of Romania, and calling for political and economic assistance to curb the emigration of Hungarians from Transylvania.
