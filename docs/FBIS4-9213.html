BFN [Excerpt] Belgrade, 22 Apr (TANJUG) -- Yasushi Akashi, UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali's special envoy for the former Yugoslavia, arrived in Belgrade this morning for a meeting with Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic and Radovan Karadzic, president of the republic of Srpska, immediately upon his arrival, we have learned unofficially. The meeting of the three in Belgrade is attracting a great deal of attention, particularly because of the Gorazde drama and the latest NATO threats to the Serbs in Bosnia. Only a few days ago Akashi told Butrus-Ghali that he would not talk to Slobodan Milosevic and Radovan Karadzic unless, as he said, the attacks on Gorazde were to cease and UNPROFOR [UN Protection Force] members were to be released. Akashi also made his meeting in Belgrade conditional upon a total cease-fire in and around Gorazde. At the moment the only thing that is certain is the fact that the Serb Republic has declared a unilateral cease-fire, so the question now arises as to whether this move was sufficient for Akashi to change his mind.
