930130 FT 30 JAN 93 / Smoking report's research attacked THE Advertising Association and the Tobacco Advisory Council, which represents the main tobacco manufacturers, yesterday attacked a government report that says tobacco advertising plays a significant role in encouraging people to smoke. They claimed the report was fallacious and inadequately researched. In its response to the report the association said the report had used invalid evidence and had ignored international research that contradicted the report's conclusions. It quoted a 1984 World Health Organisation study of smoking behaviour among schoolchildren in Norway and Finland, where tobacco advertising was banned, and Austria and the UK, where it was not. It concluded: 'A difference (in smoking habits) might have been expected. No such systematic differences are found.'
