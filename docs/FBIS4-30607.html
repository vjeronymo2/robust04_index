BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 28 KYODO -- Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata on Tuesday welcomed the agreement between North and South Korea to hold their first summit meeting next month, saying it is "good news." North and South Korean officials agreed earlier in the day to hold a meeting of their leaders in Pyongyang on July 25-27, followed by a second meeting at a later date, with the place still to be decided. The summit would also have a good effect on the relations between Japan and North Korea, Hata told reporters. On the prospects for Japanese economic and technological assistance to North Korea, Hata predicted smooth progress if the nuclear issue is settled in forthcoming high-level talks between the United States and North Korea.
