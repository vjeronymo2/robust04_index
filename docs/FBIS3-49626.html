Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Text] Talks with the Slovak President Michal Kovac concluded the series of bilateral meetings between U.S. President Bill Clinton and the highest representatives of Visegrad Group countries that were held in the course of this morning. Talks with Hungarian President Arpad Goncz, Polish President Lech Walesa, and Slovak President Michal Kovac took place in the U.S. Embassy in Prague. These talks were a followup of Bill Clinton's talks with President Vaclav Havel and other Czech Republic representatives yesterday. During the talks with the delegations of the four Visegrad countries, President Clinton emphasized the importance of the Partnerhip for Peace plan as a path designed to promote these countries' integration into Western structures and prepare them for admission into NATO later. Expansion of technical assistance and Western investments into these states was another important topic of the talks. The bilateral talks will be followed by a working lunch given by President Clinton for the presidents, premiers, and foreign ministers of Visegrad countries. A short statement for the press is expected after the meeting. Before he departs from Prague, the U.S. President will take part in a discussion about privatization and the prospects of Czech-U.S. trade in the department store K-Mart.
