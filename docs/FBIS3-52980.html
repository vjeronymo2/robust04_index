Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, Jan 10 (MENA)--The Supreme State Security Court today listened to the defence counsel in the case in which 'Umar 'Abd-al-Rahman (now in the United States) and 47 others are accused of inciting crowds outside a mosque in al-Fayyum. The court will continue to hear the counsel for the defence tomorrow morning. The argument of the defence counsel was that differences between religious groups and the systems of government have existed for a long time since the French expedition came to Egypt. Representative of the state prosecution said one of the accused who had been at large has been apprehended and was behind bars at today's hearing.
