February 16, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

ETHIOPIA;

RELIEF FOOD STALLED BY NEW FIGHTING

No famine relief food has been trucked from Ethiopia's northern port of Massawa for a week because of fierce fighting between government and rebel forces along the Red Sea coast, church groups reported.

The U.N.'s Food and Agriculture Organization repeated a warning that 1.1 million tons of grain will be needed to ward off starvation threatening 4 million people in Ethiopia. House-to-house fighting was reported between Eritrean rebels and government troops battling for control of Massawa. Heavy casualties on both sides were reported.
