Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Text] Vladimir Dlouhy, [Czech] minister of industry and trade, is on a one-day visit to Austria today. Marie Budhansova reports from Vienna: [Begin Budhansova recording] Vladimir Dlouhy, minister of industry and trade of the Czech Republic, is in Vienna today at the invitation of the Austrian Government. In the morning, Dlouhy met with Victor Klima, minister of transport and public works. According to Mr. Fojtik, the commercial counsellor of the Czech Embassy, the Austrian side was interested in questions connected with supplies of oil and gas to the Czech Republic, progress and details of privatization, and problems of the Czech metallurgical industry where Austrian firms are involved. Minister Dlouhy was briefed by his hosts on the privatization of Austrian state industry -- above all the OeMV company, which is entering the Czech market -- and ways to achieve guarantees with regard to industrial investments and Austrian exports. At a joint discussion, he exchanged views and experiences with Austrian state sector businessmen. Minister Dlouhy also met today with Vice Chancellor Busek and Economic Affairs Minister Schuessel. The ambassadors of the two countries were present. Dloughy is to end his one-day visit to Vienna by paying a courtesy call on Chancellor Vranitzky. [end recording]
