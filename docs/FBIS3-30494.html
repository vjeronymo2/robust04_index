Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Sarajevo Radio Bosnia-Herzegovina Network in Serbo-Croatian at 1100 GMT 4 February within its regular 17-minute newscast carries the following reports on the situation in Sarajevo. According to a communique from the Information Service of the 1st Corps of the Army of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina read by an announcer, "a calm morning in the Sarajevo region was interrupted by enemy artillery shells that ended their lethal flight in the region of Dobrinja. The aggressor fired them from the region of Vranjes. They were 82-mm mortar bombs. There were three bombs and Sarajevo has thereby added one more massacre to its list. The victims of this one are innocent civilians." Jasna Niksic then reports live from Dobrinja that "so far seven people have been killed and 17 injured, of whom four are extremely seriously wounded and have been taken to the City Hospital. This is the balance of the morning Chetnik shelling in the Sarajevo Dobrinja district. Namely, the morning was extremely calm, but this calm was interrupted by two Chetnik shells that suddenly fell on Dobrinja One as civilians gathered to divide humanitarian aid. This is all the information we have now. We shall report with more details in our next newscasts."
