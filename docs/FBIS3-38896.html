Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report from Brussels by correspondent Nacho Hernandez] [Excerpt] [Hernandez] For the Spanish authorities there must be a warning to the Serbs so that they will lift the siege of Sarajevo. For Foreign Minister Javier Solana the solution to the conflict in Bosnia is not military, although he has faith in the deterrent effects of a decision within NATO. [Begin Solana recording] So that those responsible for the massacres of the last few days will receive an unequivocal signal from the Atlantic alliance of its readiness to raise the siege of Sarajevo. And I believe I can tell you that I believe there will be a sufficient consensus today within the Atlantic alliance and within the European Union for a clear, firm decision to be taken with regard to Sarajevo. [end recording] [Hernandez] A formula could be sought for Sarajevo to be defined as a UN-protected zone. The foreign minister confirmed that there are measures to protect the troops, relocating them according to the proximity of the danger -- we mean the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] forces which are now carrying out humanitarian aid work in Yugoslavia. We must cooperate, the foreign minister said, to end the siege of Sarajevo. In his opinion there is a sufficient consensus to make a decision and give a forceful response. [passage omitted]
