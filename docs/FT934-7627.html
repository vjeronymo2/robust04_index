931119 FT 19 NOV 93 / Brussels opens consumer debate The European Commission yesterday launched a pan-European debate on how to settle serious consumer complaints about goods and services bought from other EU member states, writes Andrew Hill from Brussels. Mrs Christiane Scrivener, consumer affairs commissioner, said legal and judicial barriers impeding the growth of cross-border shopping had to be broken down. 'The confidence of consumers is an essential condition for the success of the single market,' she said, launching a discussion document on improving consumer 'access to justice' in the single market.
