Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Now to the Labor primaries for the party nominee for the post of Histadrut [trade union federation] secretary general. Incumbent Hayim Haberfeld is well ahead with some 70 percent of the votes counted. Over to Dani Gur-Arye in Tel Aviv: [Begin Gur-Arye recording] With 70 percent of the votes counted, Histadrut General Secretary Haberfeld has received two thirds of the votes, Knesset Member 'Amir Peretz received one third, and the third candidate, Jacques Amir, received just three percent of the votes. [passage omitted] The results deal a tough blow not only to 'Amir Peretz, but to the whole group of young reform-minded Labor Knesset members, including Hayim Ramon, Yosi Beilin, Avraham Burg, Ya'el Dayan, Hagay Merom, and others. The group, especially Hayim Ramon, garnered all its influence to back Peretz and lost. Already late yesterday Labor members were talking about how a Peretz defeat would effect other major decisions in the party, including the one on Ramon's health reform bill. [end recording] [passage omitted]
