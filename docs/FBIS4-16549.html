BFN [Text] As Interfax has learned from Russia's Foreign Ministry, the Russian President's Special Envoy, Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin arrived in Sarajevo on Saturday to help end hostilities and reconcile Serbs and Moslems in Bosnia-Herzegovina. In the words of a senior Russian diplomat, the negotiations have been quite tough, although the sides have revealed some encouraging signs. Nonetheless, in his opinion, the achievement of an agreement within the next two-three days is hardly possible for political, military, and technical reasons. An accord must fix a disengagement line between the Serbs and the Moslems, a line which in their view, could be a frontier for a long time. The negotiators have been bargaining for virtually each mile of the mountain terrain. This does not portend a quick completion of the negotiating process, the diplomat said. However, he did not rule out that an accord could be signed already next week.
