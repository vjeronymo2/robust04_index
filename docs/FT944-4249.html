941209 FT 09 DEC 94 / Ex-Nissan UK head loses case The House of Lords yesterday dismissed the application from Mr Michael Hunt, the former managing director of Nissan UK, for leave to appeal against his conviction for involvement in a Pounds 55m corporate tax fraud. Mr Hunt was jailed for eight years in 1993 after being found guilty of the biggest corporation tax fraud in UK history. He lost his appeal against both conviction and sentence before the Court of Appeal in May, but in July the Court of Appeal certified that the case raised a point of law of general public importance enabling a petition to be made to the law lords for leave to appeal. The point of law concerned whether cheating the Inland Revenue was an offence under common law.
