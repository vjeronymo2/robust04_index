930121 FT 21 JAN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Labour leadership assured of non-compliance with Tory Euro-sceptics LABOUR'S pro-Maastricht faction yesterday won assurances from the leadership that nothing would be done during the debate on ratification that could be interpreted as helping the Tory Euro-sceptics, Ivo Dawnay writes. The pledge came from Mr Jack Cunningham at the weekly meeting of the parliamentary Labour party where those seeking outright opposition to all closure motions cutting short debate on amendments to the treaty clashed with others committed to qualified support. In an attempt to calm the debate on tactics, the shadow foreign secretary said Labour's business managers intended to continue to keep their cards close to their chests. The main goal was to stress Labour's objections to aspects of the bill, primarily the lack of UK adherence to the social chapter on workplace rights. But Mr Cunningham assured the pro-Europeans that Labour was not in the business of 'wrecking' the bill.
