BFN ["rds"-signed report: "SPD Divided Over Austerity Course"] [Excerpt] Bonn, 17 May -- An open conflict has broken out within the Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] over the party's course on financial and social policies. The chairman of the SPD's employees' association, Rudolf Dressler, contradicted the Deputy SPD chairman and "shadow finance minister" in Rudolf Scharping's election team, Oskar Lafontaine, who advocated a tough austerity course that might also affect social benefits. Such announcements are "not legitimate at all," Dressler stated in Bonn yesterday. Neither the section of the draft of the SPD government program on economic policy nor the part on social policy include intentions to cut social benefits, Dressler stressed. [passage omitted]
