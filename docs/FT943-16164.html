940706 FT 06 JUL 94 / Swan Hunter employees agree to reduced pay-offs The employees of Swan Hunter, the Tyneside shipbuilder in receivership, agreed at a mass meeting yesterday to abandon the company severance scheme after March 1995 if Constructions Mecaniques de Normandie, the only potential bidder for the company, buys the yard. The decision means any employees made redundant after that date would receive a pay-off from CMN based on the state minimum, about 50 per cent less than the present company severance scheme offers. Mr Eddie Darke, one of Swan Hunter's union leaders and co-ordinator of the Save Our Swans campaign, said: 'People realised the main priority was keeping Swan Hunter open. This is the price which has to be paid.' Mr Fred Henderson, leader of CMN's bid team for Swan Hunter and prospective managing director of the company, welcomed the decision. Mr Henderson and Mr Iskandar Safa, whose Paris-based company Soffia bought Cherbourg-based CMN in 1992, lobbied MPs at Westminster yesterday in support of Swans' bid for the refit of the landing ship Sir Bedivere. CMN's bid is conditional on Swan Hunter winning this order from the Ministry of Defence. An announcement is due this month. 'The Sir Bedivere contract will ensure the survival of Swan Hunter,' said Mr Safa. 'But more than that, it will also lead British shipbuilding into a new era of international co-operation and the potential for the joint development of a new generation of surface warships for European navies.'
