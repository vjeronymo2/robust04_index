BFN [Text] Kiev [no dateline as received] -- Today, experts from the European Union's [EU] Technical Aid Program to CIS Countries (TACIS) held a news conference on assistance to Ukraine in agricultural production and food distribution. Under the TACIS program, a total of 150 million ECU [European currency unit] has been provided for Ukraine since 1991. It was agriculture, however, that Luis Moreno, EU ambassador to Ukraine, described as a priority aspect of the program that mostly deals with consultative and technical assistance. Around 100 million ECU worth of food will be given to Ukraine. Luis Moreno expressed the hope that the volume of agricultural production in Ukraine will, in 10 years, be the second largest in Europe. EU experts Stuart Lisor and Patrik Pyusemye spoke about the implementation in various areas of Ukraine of aid programs to modernize the infrastructure of baby food manufacture and production of meat, milk, vegetables, fruit, animal feed, the improvement of wholesale and retail food distribution, and the privatization of food stores.
