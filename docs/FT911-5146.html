910416 FT 16 APR 91 / UK Company News: French Connection returns to the black French Connection, the USM-quoted fashion manufacturer and retailer, returned to pre-tax profit in the second half of 1990-91, making Pounds 277,000, and expects further improvement in the current year. Together with the first half loss, the group is left with a profit of Pounds 57,000 for the year ended January 31, against the previous deficit of Pounds 4.67m. Net interest charges were cut by Pounds 1m to Pounds 1.24m as bank borrowings fell from Pounds 6.1m to Pounds 4.6m by the year-end. Earnings were 1.4p per share (losses 29.7p). The dividend is again 2.7p with a recommended final of 1.8p. The cost is Pounds 95,000 (Pounds 96,000) after directors' waivers totalling Pounds 334,000. Turnover for the year rose to Pounds 56.68m (Pounds 53.31m). That generated an operating profit of Pounds 1.3m (loss Pounds 4.19m), struck after losses of Pounds 410,000 (Pounds 1.16m) in the US and Pounds 710,000 (Pounds 50,000) in France.
