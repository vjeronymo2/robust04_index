BFN [Text] Yerevan, June 2 (SNARK)--Armenia is content with the idea of the CIS peacekeeping troops' introduction into the zone of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict, said to the journalists Levon Zurabyan, the acting press secretary of the Armenian President. In his words, the position Armenia takes and expressed at the recent forum of the CIS leaders was confirmed at the yesterday's meeting of the Armenian President Levon Ter-Petrosyan with the CIS Executive Secretary Ivan Korotchenya. Mr. Zurabyan refused to inform journalists if Armenia is on the desire to send Armenian troops within the peacekeeping forces to the region. As for the desire expressed by the Azerbaijani authorities to send 400 militaries to the zone of the conflict, Levon Zurabyan said that the Armenian authorities consider Azerbaijan doesn't bear a relation to it.
