BFN [Text] Ingushetia has again accused North Ossetia of boycotting the process of normalizing the situation in the area. A letter to the chairman of the Council of the Federation Vladimir Shumeyko says the tactics of North Ossetia during the past year and a half has been procrastinating a settlement by senseless talks, the failure to carry out approved protocols and agreements. The first round of the talks which were supposed to work out documents for the 2 April meeting of the presidents of North Ossetia and Ingushetia should have taken place in Terek, Kabardino-Balkaria on Tuesday. However, according to Interfax reporters the Ossetian side was late an hour and a half for the negotiations. The Ingush side regarded this as a demonstration of disrespect and left for home. The Ossetian side was late for the meeting because of a car crash in which two persons were injured and taken to hospital. The Ingush side waved off the explanation as groundless.
