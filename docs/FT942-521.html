940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / World Cup '94: Big Apple sports fans turn to soccer and suds Nearly two weeks into the tournament, Manhattan's sports bars may be catching World Cup fever at last, reports Patrick Harverson. After a slow start, when the New York Knicks' challenge for the national basketball championship kept the World Cup off screens at almost every sports bar in town, many establishments have decided to brave the ire of baseball aficianados and allow soccer fans to watch their heroes while supping a few beers. A straw poll of five top NY sports bars this week found that all were showing World Cup games, often to packed and enthusiastic houses. 'It's been very good for business, especially the mid-afternoon games when we're usually quiet,' said Paula Baldwin, a manager at Mickey Mantle's, the city's best-known sports bar. Downtown, near the financial district, the Sporting Club says it has been busier than usual thanks to the World Cup, especially when Ireland or Italy were playing. Games involving the US team are a big draw at the Polo Grounds on the upper east side, where manager Tony Spallone says discounted beer prices for soccer fans have won many new customers. But there have been problems at the nearby Entourage sports bar, particularly when the soccer has clashed with local baseball games. Baseball, as a matter of bar policy, always wins out, although not without complaint from the World Cup watchers. 'You never win with sports fans,' says the Entourage's manager, resignedly. There have also been complaints at Lee Mazzilli's Sports Bar and Cafe. Here, however, there has been a revolution of sorts. Peter Kramer, the bar's manager, says: 'We had the Yankee game on our 52in screen, but people complained. They wanted to watch the soccer - so we turned it over.'
