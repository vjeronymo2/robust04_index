January 13, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

EXPANDING L.A. COUNTY BOARD

I must respectfully take issue with some of the points raised in your editorial.

The Times has long supported expansion of the five-member Board of Supervisors and election of a county executive; so have Supervisor Kenneth Hahn and I, and we signed the ballot arguments for proposed county charter amendments to accomplish that in 1976 and 1978.

The Times has called upon the board to settle the case out of court and spare county taxpayers the time and expense of a protracted trial; so have Supervisor Hahn and myself.

We have made every effort to persuade a majority of our colleagues to this position. In this light, I believe it is both unfair and inaccurate to ignore a significant difference of opinion on policy, strategy and tactics among the board's membership and lump us all together.

EDMUND D. EDELMAN

Supervisor, Third District

Los Angeles County
