February 10, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

PENTAGON SPENDING AND CLOSING BASES

If the shipyard at Long Beach were to be closed, it would be a strategic error. It will be the Navy and the Defense Department, not the many people who work there, that will suffer a tremendous loss.

The Navy has not lost money, but rather saved funds that would otherwise have been spent unnecessarily. Being the only naval shipyard on the West Coast, the Long Beach shipyard is of key importance to the Navy. If the shipyard were to be closed, the Navy would be forced to send its ships to civilian yards, thus adding to the cost of the upkeep of our Navy.

Before conducting such rash actions as the shipyard closure, Secretary Cheney and Congress must carefully analyze the probable consequences that may result.

HELEN KNIPE

Torrance
