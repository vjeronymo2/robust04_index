Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (XINHUA) -- Kim Yong-sam, president of the Republic of Korea (ROK), said here today his country shares the view with China that neither the north nor the south in the Korean peninsula should possess nuclear weapons. At a press conference here this afternoon, Kim Yong-sam stressed that the ROK Government hopes that the North Korea nuclear issue could be settled through dialogue and peaceful means. He said he also expressed this view at his meeting with Chinese President Jiang Zemin. The ROK president also said that his current visit to China is of great significance for strengthening the substantial cooperation between the two countries. Kim expressed his admiration for China's rapid economic growth, adding that in recent years the economic and trade cooperation and the exchange of experience between the two countries had benefited both sides. He noted that the agreement on avoidance of double income taxation and prevention of tax evasion, signed between the two countries yesterday, would help promote Sino-ROK economic exchanges. Kim said that during his visit, he had exchanged views with Jiang Zemin on expanding bilateral economic ties, and economic cooperation in the Asia and Pacific region. The ROK president reaffirmed that his country would actively support China's re-entry to the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT).
