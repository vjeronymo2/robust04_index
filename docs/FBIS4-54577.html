BFN [Text] Tehran, May 1, IRNA -- Iran's Minister of Heavy Industries Mohammad Hadi Nejad-Hoseynian and Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu exchanged views Saturday in their meeting in Bucharest. Vacaroiu praised Iran as his country's greatest partner in the Middle East and called for further expansion of ties between the two countries. Vacaroiu said that for expanding bilateral trade-exchange, vouchers of investment and waiving of taxes should be signed first between the two sides. He also said that establishment of Tehran-Bucharest joint venture can help further expansion of ties to make it possible for expanding the work in a third country. Describing the broadening relations between Iran and Romania, Nejad-Husseinian referred to existance of over 900 Iranian active companies in Romania and asked for further facilities to be granted to them by the Romanian officials to pave the way for their further activities.
