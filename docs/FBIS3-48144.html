Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 24 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokwawa seemed unconcerned about the stock market tumble Monday [24 January] morning, saying the prices will go up again. Hosokawa said the effect on the stock market of the government's defeat on political reform was not a major problem. "(The stock prices) will move up again," he said. Hosokawa noted that the Nikkei index recently fell to around 16,000. The Nikkei stock average -- a key market indicator -- plunged 836.18 points to close the morning session at 18,471.25 in response to the failure Friday of the coalition government's political reform package in the House of Councillors.
