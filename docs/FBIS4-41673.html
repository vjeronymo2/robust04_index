BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow June 27 TASS -- Russia's signing of the Partnership for Peace programme and the protocol on cooperation with NATO opens up wide possibilities for the development of military and technical ties, a Russian top legislator said on Monday. The announcement was made by Sergey Yushenkov, chairman of the State Duma Committee for Defence, during his meeting with Supreme Allied Commander Europe George Joulwan who is in Moscow on a working visit. Yushenkov and Joulwan discussed relations between NATO and Russia and European security. Earlier today, Joulwan met with Russian Chief of the Army General Staff Mikhail Kolesnikov to discuss prospects for cooperation between the Russian army general staff and the NATO Supreme Command Europe, interaction in peace-keeping operations and other issues of mutual concern, the Russian Defence Ministry press centre said.
