January 14, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

'VINELAND' AND VINEGAR

In his review of "Vineland," Frank McConnell alternates between a gee-whiz talking down to us and an Agnew-like linguisitic shell game.

He actually assumes his readers won't understand the meaning of Vineland and 1984, and throws in gratuitous references to "Astaire" and Thelonious Monk.

. . . Plangent is a weak, ugly and obscure word for plaintive, and why call politics sclerotic? Sick, maybe, but is sclerosis more political than other diseases?

Such is the currency of the pseudo-intellectuals -- not real words but marks of mutual recognition. . . .

DANIEL O'HEARN, GLENDALE
