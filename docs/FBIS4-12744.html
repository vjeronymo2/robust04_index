BFN [Text] Cairo, 12 Apr (MENA)--Dr. Nabil Sha'th, head of the Palestinian delegation to the peace negotiations, declared that the Palestinian and Israeli sides have reached agreement on the size of the Palestinian police force in the Gaza Strip and Jericho area during the round of negotiations that ended in Cairo today. Speaking at a news conference today at the conclusion of the negotiations, Sha'th said that the police force agreed upon will comprise 9,000 personnel; 2,000 from inside [the occupied territories] and 7,000 from outside. He added that the agreement stipulates that 6,000 Palestinian policemen will enter immediately after the signing of the executive agreement of the Gaza-Jericho First agreement and then another 1,000 will enter within three months of the signing of the agreement. [passage indistinct] Sha'th went on to say: As to the weapons of the 9,000 Palestinian policemen, they will be modern automatic rifles for policemen, and officers will also be issued pistols. He said that the Palestinian police will be supplied with a number of machine guns and some armored personnel carriers. Regarding the issues on which no agreement has been reached so far, Sha'th said that these are: roads; the crossing points between Gaza, Jericho and Israel; joint patrols; the security measures on the roads; how to deal with any military operation; wrapping up the water issue. He added that these issues will be referred to the round that will be held in Cairo on 17 April. On what Amnon Shahaq, head of the Israeli delegation, said in his news conference today, that Palestinian detainees who are members of Hamas will not be released, Sha'th said that the Palestinian side in the negotiations represents all the Palestinian people and it is its responsibility to negotiate to release all the detainees and not only the Fatah ones. He added: I know that there is some sensitivity on the Israeli side, especially after the recent 'Afula incident in northern Israel. However, agreement was reached today to release 5,000 detainees and negotiations will be conducted to release the others. He stressed that the negotiations are being held in the name of the entire Palestinian people. He went on to say: We are not negotiating for some detainees and not others. The peace we want to conclude is a peace between the entire Palestinian people and the entire Israeli people and not a peace between a Palestinian organization and an Israeli one. He said: We must shoulder the responsibility for an agreement on a general amnesty that will lead to the release of all the detainees. Sha'th underlined the Palestinian side's determination to continue the negotiations to release all the detainees so that a real peace can be achieved, one which halts the conflict that has lasted for a long time and of which the detainees constitute a part.
