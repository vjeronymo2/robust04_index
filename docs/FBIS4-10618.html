BFN [Interview with Foreign Minister Ernesto Paz Aguilar by Reporter Roberto Reyes Pineda at Toncintin Airport on 11 April; from the "This Very Day" newscast -- recorded] [Text] [Paz] The Honduran Government is very pleased with the working visit by the president [title as heard] of Costa Rica, by President Figueres. There is no doubt that we have made progress on the road to integration, on the road to rapprochement with the Central American peoples. [Reyes] Is the position Costa Rica has maintained in the past few years going to change with the arrival of Jose Maria Figueres? [Paz] Yes, we believe that the National Liberation Party's leading of the Costa Rican Government, will ease the Central American integration process. It will ease the rapprochement Costa Rica should undertake with the rest of the Central America. [Reyes] Was an agreement reached with President-elect Figueres? [Paz] No joint communique was signed, given that he will not take office until 8 May. Nevertheless, the doors have been opened for a dialogue, the channels of communication have been opened between both governments. [Reyes] Is there anything specific with regard to trade relations, the increase that could take place in this sector? [Paz] Basically we talked about the impact the signing of NAFTA has had and its repercussions on Central America; what position should we Central Americans take on this free trade agreement which is a challenge to our nations. We also talked about structuring a common strategy to face problems such as the marketing of coffee, the price of bananas, and so forth. [Reyes] Was Mr. Figueres asked about Costa Rica's experience as a member of GATT, given that Honduras is on the verge of becoming a member? [Paz] If I recall correctly, at the Latin American level there are just two nations that are not members of the GATT, that is Panama and another South American nation. These, however, will join in the coming months. Costa Rica does have a long history and vast successful experience in the integration of the Costa Rican economy in the new international business structure. [Reyes] How are the good relations between Honduras and Costa Rica seen in the coming years? [Paz] I can say we have very good relations, and we expect to develop and enhance them even further.
