Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The News" newscast] [Excerpts] The bill on a special law protecting bank depositors and regulating emergencies in financial institutions was approved today during a second debate at the Chamber of Deputies. The second debates on the special law lasted approximately 12 hours; it was finally approved at 1230 today, after an agreement was reached on the final procedures. The law, which has 70 articles, stipulates the titles of Banco Latino depositors will be recognized regardless of the legal body that issued it, as long as the transaction was made at Banco Latino. The law also permits the flexibility of financial aid [auxilio financiero] and expands control of banks receiving financial aid to the point the board of directors can be replaced while assistance is being received. Fogade [Deposit Guarantee Fund] will be responsible for 4 million bolivares. [passage omitted] The bill approved by the Chamber of Deputies was sent this afternoon to the Senate, where, as the Congress president said, it will be given constitutional urgency; therefore, the Senate debates will begin 3 March.
