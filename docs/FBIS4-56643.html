BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 5 May. The construction industry continued to enjoy a boom during the first quarter of the year, as shown by increased sales of Portland cement for public and private construction, which grew by 80 and 11 percent respectively. Sales of gas oil and fuel oil dropped by 24 and 56 percent respectively in the first quarter. This is an indication of the recession that has been affecting the Uruguayan industry for over a year. A 4.3 percent drop in the volume of milk processed at pasteurizing plants was also registered in the said quarter. There has been an upturn in livestock activities during this quarter, as wool exports grew by 27 percent and the number of cattle slaughtered increased by 13.4 percent. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR in Spanish 21 Apr 94 p 9) The executive branch decreed an average 9 percent increase in gasoline prices. The measure entered into effect at 0000 on 27 April. (Montevideo EL PAIS in Spanish 27 Apr 94 p 1) According to Uruguay's Merchant Products Chamber, the country exported 32.5 percent more wool during the period October 1993-March 1994 than during a similar period last year. The country exported a total of 64,139 tonnes of wool, expressed in terms of dirty wool. Of this total the equivalent of 10,863 tonnes was washed wool while 39,482 tonnes was industrialized wool. The main importers of dirty wool were the United Kingdom, the PRC, the Netherlands, Colombia, and the United States. The main purchasers of washed wool were Germany, Italy, the PRC, and the United States, and the main purchasers of industrialized wool were the PRC, Italy, Germany, and Hong Kong. (Montevideo EL PAIS 26 Apr 94 p 1) There are two indicators of the good liquidity in the financial system at present. One is the considerable amount of overnight money deposited at the Central Bank on 25 April--337.5479 million pesos, at an annual rate of 29 percent. The second is the great influx of money in the biddings for Treasury bills in pesos with a 63-day maturity, which exceeds 100 million pesos every week. Interbank call money rates have also suffered the consequences of good liquidity, dropping to 31 percent annually. (Montevideo EL PAIS in Spanish 26 Apr 94 Market Indicators Section p 1) The National Statistics Institute has reported that the unemployment rate in Montevideo dropped by nearly 1 percent in the first quarter of the year as compared with the corresponding period last year. It stood at 8.1 percent. This figure, however, does not represent a decrease in the number of people unemployed, but rather a decline in the working population. The retail sector is the most severely hit by unemployment, with 7.8 percent, followed by industry with a 7.5 percent unemployment rate. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 27 Apr 94 p 7) Construction costs were up by 7.1 percent in March, thus registering a 9.5 percent increase so far this year. Over the last 12 months costs have edged up by 51.6 percent. (Montevideo EL PAIS in Spanish 27 Apr 94 Market Indicators Section p 1)
