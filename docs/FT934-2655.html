931213 FT 13 DEC 93 / The Business Traveller: Other news - Travel Update Concorde flops: A special 32 hour Concorde flight around the world to celebrate the New Year was cancelled after only 10 people expressed an interest in the tickets selling at Dollars 25,400 each. Air deal: Britain and Latvia signed an air treaty last week which allows scheduled flights between the two countries for the first time. Test case: A High Court case by a schoolgirl and her teacher against the Association of British Travel Agents over a Pounds 10 holiday insurance premium that was not repaid when one of its members went out of business ended last week. The judge has not said when he will make a ruling. Mr Robert Webb QC, counsel for the pair said there were about 25,000 cases, with a value of Pounds 250,000, resting on the outcome which had wide implications for the industry. Smoke Free by 1995: Cathay Pacific has taken a step closer to becoming a 'smoke-free' airline. From March 1994, all Cathay Pacific flights between Hong Kong and Europe will be no-smoking. This will bring the total no-smoking flights operated by the airline to 70%.
