930914 FT 14 SEP 93 / Airtours directors sell Pounds 20m of shares SEVEN directors of Airtours, the travel group, yesterday sold Pounds 20.8m of shares in the company but said this year's performance would be better than last. Airtours said the sale had been prompted by the need to broaden the shareholder base and increase the liquidity of the group's shares. The 5.2m ordinary shares were sold to institutional clients of Hoare Govett Securities for 403p a share. Hoare Govett were appointed Airtours' brokers last week, replacing Barclays de Zoete Wedd. Airtours' shares fell 6p to close at 409p. Airtours said that even after the sale, directors continued to own 42.4 per cent of the shares. It said bookings for summer 1993 had been 20 per cent ahead of last year. Hoare Govett said it was upgrading its forecast of 1992-93 full-year pre-tax profits from Pounds 41m to Pounds 44m, after Pounds 9m costs incurred in the bid, which failed earlier this year, for rival Owners Abroad. Pre-tax profits last year were Pounds 36.5m. The largest seller of shares was Mr David Crossland, chairman, who disposed of 2.6m shares with a total value of Pounds 10.6m. He retains 30m shares. The other directors who sold shares were Mr Tom Trickett, product review director, Mr Hugh Collinson, managing director, Mr Harry Coe, finance director, Mr Ton Waslander, Mr George Marcall and Mr Michael Lee. Mr Trickett will retire in January 1994. Mr Coe, Mr Waslander and Mr Marcall yesterday sold shares bought through options in the past few days. The exercise price ranged from 31.75p to 36p.
