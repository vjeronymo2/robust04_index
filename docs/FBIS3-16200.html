Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Baku (March 14) TURAN--"The Labour Party is very anxious about the search that was conducted in the offices of `Azadlyg' newspaper. These events are taking place in the background of the overall political censorship in the republic", says the statement from Mr. Balaevl, one of the founding committee members of the Azerbaijani Labour Party. The statement also notes that the present government's attitude to towards its critics is intolerant and the government "is striving towards limiting freedom of speech and democratic freedom, in general". The statement further notes that the Labour Party of Azerbaijan opposes the all forms of radicalism in policy making and criticize this violations. The Labour Party of Azerbaijan has not been officially registered. The conference of the party which took place on January 2, 1994 was disrupted by the Baku's Yasamal district police department in the presence of deputies, journalists and foreign diplomats.
