BFN [Text] On Monday President Boris Yeltsin signed the federal budget law for the second quarter of 1994 which binds the government to finance spending in the first half of the year based on the guidelines for 1994. The Central Bank was ordered to offer the government a credit of 10.6 trillion rubles to cover the budget deficit. The credit is given for 10 years at 10 percent annual interest with repayment in equal installments as of 1998. To restrict spending on federal government bodies the following ceilings have been set on current expenditures: 82 Bn rubles to the presidential administration, 11.303 Bn to the Council of the Federation of the Federal Assembly, 27.754 Bn to the State Duma, 15.189 Bn to the government (except ministries and departments). The federal budget expenditures approved by parliament last week are set at 46 trillion rubles (25.2 percent of the annual figure), revenue - 28.6 trillion (23.7 percent of the annual sum) with a deficit of l7.4 trillion. The budget law came into force immediately after being signed by the president.
