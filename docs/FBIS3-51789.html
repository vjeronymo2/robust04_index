Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Francisco Trejo; from the "Good Morning From Mexico" newscast] [Text] President Salinas created during the weekend of 21 January the Amnesty and Reconciliation Commission, which will benefit those involved in the violent actions in Chiapas. Here is Francisco Trejo with a report: [Begin Trejo recording] The federal government issued on 22 January the Amnesty Law, which will benefit all those involved in the violent actions in Chiapas from 1 January until 1500 20 January 1994. The law, which was published in the Official Gazette, became effective as of 22 January, and it has four permanent articles and two transitory ones. Article One of the text contains the Amnesty Law, which favors all those against whom any legal action were or could be taken before the federal courts due to crimes associated with the violent actions on 1 January 1994. The executive established a commission that will coordinate implementation of the law. Article Two establishes that those who are either inside or outside of the country due to crimes indicated in Article One will be able to benefit from the amnesty as long as they release hostages and turn in their weapons. In Article Three the amnesty permits the application of the law and civil responsibility [responsabilidad civil] of those who request it. Those who benefit from the law cannot be in the future questioned investigated, arrested, apprehended, or tried in accordance with the terms of the current amnesty. The Amnesty Law will be published throughout the conflict area in both Spanish and the native dialects. [end recording]
