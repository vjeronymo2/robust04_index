Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 5 (CNS)--China's new regulations for border trade formulated by the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation [MOFTEC] mainly include the following: Companies engaging in small-volume border trade are subject to approval by the ministry regarding their setting up and trading sites set up by residents of border areas at border trade fairs will be organized in a unified way and managed by provincial governments. Before the end of 1995, those companies approved by the ministry will have their import tariff and product tax reduced by 50 percent on some of their traded commodities (except for electro-mechanical products, cigarettes, wines and cosmetics). Products worth less than RMB 300 [renminbi] will have taxes imposed on them according to regulations while those products such as cigarettes, wines and cosmetics will all have taxes levied on them. Border trade companies are allowed to export a small amount of commodities listed as Category Two and Category Three within the total quotas granted by the ministry. Items for cooperation between the border areas and their corresponding areas in foreign countries are subject to approval by the department in charge of foreign trade and economy under the provincial government and commodities (except for electro-mechanical products, cigarettes, wines and cosmetics) imported through such cooperation and used for the payment to laborers on the Chinese side will not have any import tariff and product tax levied on them while those other than for this purpose will have their taxes reduced by 50 percent.
