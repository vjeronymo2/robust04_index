BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Feoktistov] [Text] Ankara, 1 April -- The talks between Russian and Turkish Interior Ministers Viktor Yerin and Nahit Mentese yesterday ended in Ankara by signing a joint protocol. The sides confirmed their agreement and resoluteness to develop cooperation between the two countries in the fight against international terrorism and drug smuggling. During the talks the decision was taken to hold a meeting of the joint commission in the very near future with the aim of stepping up mutual actions in the security field. Speaking at the protocol signing ceremony, Mentese said strengthening cooperation between Turkey and Russia on the basis of friendship and unity of interests would be for the benefit of both states. The Russian and Turkish Interior Ministries, Yerin said in his turn, agreed to coordinate actions in the fight against organized crime. According to the Russian minister, special attention was given to drugs and arms smuggling. It is expected that the Russian delegation will leave Turkey today.
