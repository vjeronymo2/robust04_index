941103 FT 03 NOV 94 / Adverts called for on BBC2 BBC2 should be allowed to accept advertising and sponsorship, the Incorporated Society of British Advertisers said yesterday in response to the government's white paper on the future of the BBC. A mixture of funding from the licence fee and advertisers would enable the corporation to compete effectively with growing satellite and multimedia rivals, said the society, which represents many of the UK's largest advertisers. Mr Kenneth Miles, the society's director-general, said all other 'leading countries in Europe now have mixed funding for state channels, with advertising as a major source of income'. The society wants to see BBC1 continue to be funded through the licence fee, while BBC2 would operate with advertising and sponsorship. The white paper, published in July, reinforced the current universal licence fee arrangements until at least 2001. Separately, the Institute of Practitioners in Advertising, accused the BBC of mirroring the commercial sector in its programming. It added: 'Why should licence payers be obliged to fund programmes that would, in the absence of the BBC, be provided by commercial broadcasters?'
