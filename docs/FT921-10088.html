920207 FT 07 FEB 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Dow little changed ahead of employment figures Wall Street The secondary market rally ran out of steam yesterday afternoon and primary stocks dithered in a narrowly mixed range as investors waited for today's release of January's employment figures, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average was 2.01 lower at 3,255.59 in moderate volume of 238.2m shares. Big board advancing issues led declines by 894 to 773. The Nasdaq composite, gave up its highs on the day but consolidated its record closes on Wednesday and Tuesday by adding 0.70 to 637.67 in late trading. On Wednesday, the Dow closed at 3,257.60, down 15.21. Among featured issues, Owens-Corning surged Dollars 7 5/8 to Dollars 36 5/8 after the newly-appointed chairman of the glass fibre group said that the company had taken a non-cash charge of Dollars 800m against fourth quarter earnings to cover future asbestos claims. The prospect of fewer problem loans at Continental Bank helped the company's shares climb Dollars 1 to Dollars 15. Other bank stock's continued to rally including Citicorp, up Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 16 7/8 and Chemical Bank, Dollars 1 higher at Dollars 30 5/8 . Strong chain store sales figures for January, albeit on the back of sharp price-cutting, helped a number of retailers post gains, as the market responded to reports of lower inventories. Wal-Mart ended the day down Dollars 5/8 at Dollars 54 3/4 . But Sears closed Dollars 1 1/2 higher at Dollars 43 7/8 , JC Penney rose Dollars 2 1/2 to Dollars 59 7/8 and May Department Stores was up Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 58 3/8 . In the oil services sector, Dresser Industries rose Dollars 1 3/8 to Dollars 20 3/4 in active trading after it announced plans to spin-off its industrial and mining equipment divisions to shareholders. Transportation stocks continued to firm, with the Dow Jones Transportation Index adding 8.78 to 1,392.19 after a gain of 15.14 on Wednesday. Goodyear Tire & Rubber, the last of the big US-owned tyre makers, edged Dollars 1 1/4 lower to Dollars 62 1/8 . Eastman Kodak, which led the Dow lower on Wednesday when it dropped Dollars 4 1/4 on the back of its fourth quarter loss, added Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 46 3/4 yesterday in active trading. Canada TORONTO share prices strayed little from the previous closing level, but trading remained strong. The composite index edged 0.85 points upwards or 0.02 per cent to 3,606.70, as advancing issues topped decliners 310 to 286. Volume was 30.3m against 32.1m the day before, with trading value CDollars 383.8m against CDollars 323.7m.
