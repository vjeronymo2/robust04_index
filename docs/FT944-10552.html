941110 FT 10 NOV 94 / Bidders named in road management deals Eleven consortia have been invited to bid for four contracts valued at Pounds 380m to build and manage new or improved roads, Dr Brian Mawhinney, transport secretary, announced yesterday. The projects are the start of the government's 'design, build, finance and operate' initiative to increase private-sector involvement in roads. The bidders for the Pounds 190m scheme to link the M1 and the A1 south of Leeds are UK Highways, comprising John Laing, Tarmac, Transroute of France and Welsh Water; ExpressRoute comprising John Mowlem and GTM-Entrepose of France; Trafalgar House-Wimpey-Autostrade (of Italy); and Hochtief of Germany. Bidders for the Pounds 145m widening of the A1 are Autolink Concessionaires, consisting of Amey Holdings, Sir Robert McAlpine and Taylor Woodrow; Road Management Group, comprising AMEC, Brown & Root, Dragados and Alfred McAlpine; Connect, consisting of WS Atkins, Bank of America, BICC and Philipp Holzmann of Germany; and the Modern Highways Group consisting of SGE of France, Hollandsche Beton of the Netherlands and Kier. Bidders for the Pounds 35m widening of the A419/A417 are National Road Operators, consisting of Bovis and Costain; UK Highways, Connect and Road Management Group. The consortia selected for the Pounds 10m A69 project are Road Link, consisting of Henry Boot, Christiani & Nielson, Cogefarimpresit of Italy, Morrison Construction, Pell Frischmann and ASTM-SINA; Graham Network Operators comprising John Graham (Dromore) and Graham Consulting Group; Autolink Concessionaires and the Modern Highways Group.
