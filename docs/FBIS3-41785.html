Language: English Article Type:BFN [Following item transmitted via KYODO] [Text] Almaty, Kazakhstan, has not declared a state of emergency following the appearance of cholera cases in the city. All market places were opened on Tuesday [14 September]. Only the street trade in pies and beverages is banned. However, the quarantine introduced on Monday has not been lifted. Lessons in schools have been interrupted until Friday, but day care centers continue to function. According to the civil aviation department, charter flights have been stopped to Pakistan, Afghanistan, Iran, Turkey, India, and the United Arab Emirates, as well as to Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, Kyrgyzstan, and South Kazakhstan. There have been no restrictions in other directions. The acting chief of the city epidemiological service, Aleksandr Kim, announced that the 152 passengers who arrived by plane from Karachi, Pakistan last Thursday were isolated as suspected cholera patients. They are in satisfactory condition. The chief of the sanitation service of the city, Anuar Talkimbayev, reports there are 16 cholera patients in Kazakhstan, 8 of them in the capital. Treatment is active and there is no reason to fear for the lives of the patients. However, experts from the Kazakh anti-plague center claim that the type of cholera brought from Pakistan differs from the classical form and is difficult to treat by conventional means.
