Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Dr. Hamid al-Ghabid, secretary general of the Islamic Conference Organization [ICO], urged India to revise its inhuman policy on occupied Kashmir and adopt a path for the peaceful resolution of the Jammu and Kashmir dispute in accordance with the UN resolutions. In a statement on arrival in Islamabad today on a two-day visit to Pakistan, he said the legitimate aspirations of the Kashmiri people cannot be crushed by force. The ICO secretary general said the Indian atrocities have crossed the limits on which the ICO is deeply concerned. The large-scale massacres in Sopor and Kupwara, in which hundreds of people were martyred, have dealt a deep blow to the Islamic world. He said the ICO will move a resolution at the UN Human Rights Commission meeting currently being held in Geneva to halt the human rights violations in occupied Kashmir. On Bosnia-Herzegovina, the secretary general said our efforts are to get the use of force against Serbs authorized by the UN Security Council and also get the unjust and illegitimate arms embargo on Bosnia-Herzegovina Government lifted.
