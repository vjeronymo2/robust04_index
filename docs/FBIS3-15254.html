Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow, March 2 (TASS) -- Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and visiting Uzbek President Islam Karimov met here on Wednesday for intergovernmental talks. The talks ended in signing an ample package of Russo-Uzbek documents. The documents include intergovernmental agreements on principles of mutual technical and material supply of armed forces, on cooperation in air transport, on avoiding double taxation. Russian and Uzbek plenipotentiary envoys signed a consular convention, a programme for cooperation between the foreign ministries for 1994, agreements on cooperation and mutual assistance in customs affairs, on cooperation between customs offices in seizing and returning illegally exported and imported cultural treasures, as well as in customs inspection of citizens' personal belongings. When asked questions from journalists after the signing ceremony, Chernomyrdin said today's talks had helped resolve many problems hampering the development of Russo-Uzbek relations. "I am very happy about the talks outcome", he stressed. In his turn, the Uzbek president noted that "the documents signed pave the way for normalizing economic ties abd relations between the two peoples".
