BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 21 KYODO -- A senior Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) official said Tuesday he got the impression that North Korea has nuclear weapons during his recent trip to Pyongyang. "From the series of talks (with North Korean officials), I got an impression that the country possesses nuclear weapons," Yoichi Tani told a press conference. Tani, along with former Defense Agency head Taku Yamasaki, visited North Korea last week and held talks with North Korean officials, including Korean Workers' Party Secretaries Kim Yong-son and Hwang Chang-yop. Tani criticized recent remarks by Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata, who said he did not believe North Korea has atomic bombs. "I don't have the slightest idea why he said such a thing," Tani said. Yamasaki, also at the press conference, said North Korea is now considering opening the door to its market after solving the nuclear issue diplomatically.
