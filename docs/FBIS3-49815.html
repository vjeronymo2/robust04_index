Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Zagreb, Jan 15 (TANJUG) -- Bosnian Croat commander General Ante Roso said on Saturday [15 January] he had ordered his troops to bomb, if neccesary, the explosives factory in Vitez which has been the target of fierce Moslem attacks in the pasts few days. Vowing not to allow the enemy to take control of the factory, General Roso said in a statement broadcast on Zagreb television that the Moslems were fiercely attacking Vitez and Novi Travnik, but that the local Croat troops were successfully yielding [as received] to the pressure. Commenting on the situation in central Bosnia, he said the fiercest Croat-Moslem fighting had been reported in the Lasva river valley. The Bosnian Croat commander also said he had not accepted or signed any ceasefire at the latest round of talks with Bosnian Moslem commander Rasim Delic, noting that comprehensive political documents were needed for such an agreement.
