Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Spanish Foreign Ministry's directorate general of foreign policy for Africa and the Middle East has been assessing since this morning the speech by King Hassan II of Morocco in which he said that the solution to the dispute concerning the Spanish cities of Ceuta and Melilla in North Africa cannot wait any longer. Meanwhile Javier Ruperez, the foreign affairs spokesman of the conservative Popular Party, today called on the Spanish Government to be very firm in its response to the veiled threats made by King Hassan II of Morocco yesterday on the Ceuta and Melilla dispute. The spokesman for the Spanish conservative opposition calls on the government to remind the king of Morocco once again of the constitutional terms in which the territorial integrity of Spain is understood.
