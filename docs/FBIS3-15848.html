Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Audrius Braukyla reports from Sweden] [Text] Sweden will support the efforts of Lithuania and of the other Baltic states to have observer status in the Nordic Council. This was stated to Lithuanian National Defense Minister Linas Linkevicius by Swedish Prime Minister Carl Bildt, who received this Lithuanian representative in Stockholm this afternoon. The wish by the head of the Swedish Government to meet the Lithuanian representative means Sweden's emphatic attention to bilateral relations between Lithuania and Sweden, Radio Vilnius was told by Linas Linkevicius. He noted that during the meeting the sides decided to develop cooperation in the sphere of maintaining peace. According to the Lithuanian national defense minister, Lithuanian experts will be trained in Sweden's special centers for maintaining peace. Linas Linkevicius also drew attention to the efforts by Carl Bildt to strengthen by every means the cooperation between the Nordic countries and the Baltic states, according to the formula five plus three. Somewhat earlier, meeting journalists, the Swedish prime minister was for the continuous expansion of the European Union, including the Baltic states.
