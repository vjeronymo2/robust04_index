BFN [Text] The UN Security Council has decided to extend the mandate of the UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR] in former Yugoslavia for another six months and strengthen the force with 3,500 servicemen. Speaking at the session, Vasiliy Sidorov, the deputy of the permanent representative of Russia at the United Nations, pointed out that Russia has decided to send 300 servicemen to the force, and that 100 of them have already arrived in Sarajevo to reinforce the Russian contingent deployed there. Sidorov noted that Russia is also prepared to send civil observers to ensure the operation of Tuzla Airport.
