Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Report by TANJUG correspondent Savo Jankovic from Knin] [Text] The first plenary session of the first regular meeting of the assembly of the republic of Serbian Krajina [RSK] concluded this afternoon by electing members of seven parliamentary committees. The unanimous election of the commission members was preceded by the consultations held by the heads of caucuses, where a complete coordination was reached about the (?composition) of the committees, something that Assembly Chairman Branko (Vodnjica) stated with pleasure at the beginning of the afternoon part of the session. Immediately after the assembly committees were constituted, the first constitutive sessions of some of them were held while the remaining committees will do that by tomorrow morning. The second plenary session is expected in the afternoon, which will most probably discuss the Krajina negotiatory platform in the peace process, amendments to which were initiated by President Milan Martic. By the way, at the morning part of the session today, Martic presented a report on the current political and security situation in Krajina. One may expect the plenary session [tomorrow] to discuss Martic's speech and there is also the possibility of the government submitting its report on the same topic. Moreover, the government is expected to submit a proposal on legal documents or ordinances regarding Krajina joining the united balance of payments with the FRY [Federal Republic of Yugoslavia], monetary reconstruction, and economic recovery.
