Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Gridneva] [Text] Dushanbe March 2 TASS -- Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Anatoliy Adamishin paid a short visit to Dushanbe on Tuesday, where he discussed with Tajik leaders a political situation in Tajikistan and ways to stabilise it. The Russian diplomat had a meeting with chairman of the Tajik parliament Imamali Rakhmanov. Problems, discussed by them, included the speeding up of a dialogue with political opposition. Adamishin praised the efforts of the Tajik leaders, aimed at establishing peace in Tajikistan. He said that Dushanbe had displayed goodwill, and now it is the turn of the opposition forces to take reciprocal steps. According to Adamishin, the Russian Federation agrees to organise a meeting, probably in Moscow, between representatives of the political opposition and the Tajik leadership under the aegis of the U.N. "Now it is the turn of the opposition to reciprocate, and it should show by its deeds what it really wants," Adamishin stressed. ITAR-TASS learned, however, that Khodzhiakbar Turadzhon-Zoda, former spiritual leader of Tajik Moslems and now deputy chairman of the Islamic Movement of Tajikistan, who is now in emigration, said in an interview with the Tehran-based newspaper KAYHAN that he does not recognise the present government of Tajikistan and will never stop fighting it. At the same time, the former spiritual leader of Tajikistan does not reject a possibility of a political dialogue with the republican leaders, but he believes the meeting should be held in Tehran. in his opinion, Iran and Afghanistan should be intermediaries. Khodzhiakbar Turadzhon-Zoda is one of influential leaders of the irreconcilable opposition.
