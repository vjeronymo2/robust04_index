940325 FT 25 MAR 94 / International Company News: J P Morgan head paid Dollars 6.6m in 1993 Mr Dennis Weatherstone, chairman of JP Morgan, the US bank, was paid Dollars 6.6m last year, a rise of 45 per cent on 1992 and an echo of other big pay rises in the US financial sector announced recently. Morgan said Mr Weatherstone's remuneration, in the form of Dollars 3.1m cash and Dollars 3.5m of restricted shares, reflected the bank's 'very strong earnings' during 1993. The New York-based institution, one of the most profitable commercial banks in the US, made after-tax earnings of Dollars 1.73bn, up from Dollars 1.13bn the year before. The British-born Mr Weatherstone, who has headed the bank since 1990, was paid a base salary of Dollars 700,000, with the rest of his income linked to performance. Among other high-paid bank chairmen, Mr John Reed of Citicorp received Dollars 6.2m, while Mr Dan Tully, chairman of Merrill Lynch, received Dollars 9.6m, a rise of 30 per cent.
