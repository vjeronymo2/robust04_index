BFN [YONHAP report from UN Headquarters] [Text] On 17 June, James Rubin, a spokesman for the U.S. mission to the United Nations stated that consultations at the UN Security Council are proceeding without interruptions on the sanction resolution against North Korea. Mr. Rubin was instructed by Madeline Albright, U.S. ambassador to the United Nations, to make this point clear. This notwithstanding, Pak Kil-yon, North Korean ambassador to the United Nations, stated to reporters that the North Korean Government will not allow special inspections [tukbyol sachal] for two facilities suspected to be nuclear waste processing facilities. On 16 June, U.S. Assistant Secretary of State Gallucci said: As a way to a general solution of the situation, North Korea expressed its willingness to completely accept safeguards regulations of the Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty and the International Atomic Energy Agency, including special inspections. North Korean Ambassador Pak Kil-yon spurned Assistant Secretary of State Gallucci's remarks, stating that this was either Mr. Gallucci's personal interpretation or his personal pronouncement.
