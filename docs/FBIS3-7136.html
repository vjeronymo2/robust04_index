Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, March 25 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- The five British soldiers who went missing on Mount Kinabalu, Southeast Asia's highest peak in the east Malaysian Sabah State, are alive and were spotted Friday by a Royal Malaysian Air Force helicopter combing the area. The group, missing since March 8, was sighted at 10.10 am about 15 km from Nalangkap Nahandang Village, Captain Mohamed Affendie Mohamed Hanafi of the Defence Ministry's Public Relations Unit said. Another helicopter with a rescue squad, including several members of a British search and rescue team was immediately sent to the area.
