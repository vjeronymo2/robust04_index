921014 FT 14 OCT 92 / International Company News: Worldwide demand gives Motorola 38.7% advance MOTOROLA, the US elec-tronics manufacturer, reported a 38.7 per cent jump in third-quarter earnings, boosted by strong demand across several product segments in international markets. Net earnings for the period rose to Dollars 129m, or 97 cents a share, from Dollars 93m, or 70 cents, in the same quarter last year. Revenues rose 24 per cent to Dollars 3.4bn from Dollars 2.75bn. Motorola said sales of its semiconductor products advanced 24 per cent to Dollars 1.5bn in the quarter. Orders increased in the Asia-Pacific region, Europe and the Americas, but were flat in Japan. In the communications segment, sales were up 20 per cent to Dollars 1.04bn, while computer systems sales jumped 42 per cent to Dollars 932m. The company said demand for its digital wireless communications products rose in Asia and the Pacific regions as well as Europe. 'The order growth in most of our businesses makes us optimistic about the future,' said Mr George Fisher, chairman. 'About half of our sales are in international markets, and we are seeing especially robust results in the Asia-Pacific region outside of Japan.' 'While worldwide macroeconomic signals remain mixed, the outlook for growth in the product markets we serve is brighter.' The company said domestic orders rose in all major markets except the government, where electronics group's sales declined 5 per cent. For the first nine months, earnings increased to Dollars 402m, or Dollars 3.02 a share, from Dollars 328m, or Dollars 2.48 cents in the corresponding period last year. Sales were Dollars 9.59bn, against Dollars 8.3bn.
