940526 FT 26 MAY 94 / Labour poll likely to cost Pounds 1.5m: Leadership contest The Labour leadership election is likely to cost about Pounds 1.5m, of which only Pounds 100,000 will be borne directly by the party, it emerged yesterday. Most of the cost will fall on affiliated unions which will have to organise postal ballots of more than 4m members who pay the political levy. The TGWU and the GMB, the two biggest general unions, said the gross cost of balloting their 1.8m members would be about Pounds 300,000 each. Some unions, such as the AEEU craft union, will reduce costs by sending ballot papers with routine literature such as union journals. Labour officials said that up to a third of the cost might be recouped by combining the ballot with a membership drive and delivery of marketing and advertising material. Mr David Blunkett, Labour chairman, said: 'Provided it is done with sensitivity and with an eye to the political nuances, we can ensure that we raise money and don't just spend it.' The unions' leadership election costs will be deducted from the movement's financial contribution to the Labour party. Union funds cover about half of Labour's annual costs and provide most of the election fund, which is expected to reach about Pounds 20m by 1996. 'For the first time . . . almost everyone . . . is deciding who to back on the basis of who can win the election, not on the basis of ideology,' said one official, adding: 'It is an extraordinary cultural change.'
