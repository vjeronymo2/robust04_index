October 15, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

ADMINISTRATION, CONGRESS WRANGLE OVER THE BUDGET

How's this for a solution to our budget crisis? Design budgets with two-year life spans; have the people vote on them at congressional elections in November, and give them three alternative budgets to choose from: one proposed by Congress, one proposed by the White House and one proposed by non-elected officials from the Treasury and OMB. What a great way to force a deadline on our representatives and make them accountable!

PHIL RINEHART, Los Angeles
