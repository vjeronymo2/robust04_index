Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Denis Perkin] [Text] Moscow February 22 TASS -- The Russian Foreign Ministry praised a special protocol signed by the Azerbaijani and Armenian defence ministers and a Nagorno-Karabakh military representative, a Foreign Ministry spokesman said on Tuesday [22 February]. The protocol was signed on February 18 as a result of talks mediated by Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev and attended by the Russian president's special envoy Vladimir Kazimirov. The document provides for a complete cease-fire and termination of combat operations, as well as for troops disengagement. At the moment. A joint group of experts is working out a procedure for putting this protocol into practice. The Russian Foreign Ministry called upon all warring sides to strictly observe the reached agreements and to go ahead on their basis with concluding a full-scale political accord. "The Russian Foreign Ministry welcomes the aspirations of the warring sides to put an end to bloodshed, to help fulfil resolutions by the U.N. Security Council to this effect and to promote an all-round settlement of the conflict within the framework of the CSCE peace conference on Nagorno-Karabakh," the ministry said in the statement.
