Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] An intervention force is to be deployed in districts of Brazzaville where there have been socio-politicial disturbances. This measure, which forms a part of efforts to restore peace to our country, was initiated by the Parliamentary Standing Committee for Dialogue Between Pool and Niari Legislators. In a statement issued yesterday in Brazzaville, the committee warned all delinquents against attempting to disrupt the implementation of this measure. Let us listen to the statement: [read by announcer] The executive of the Parliamentary Standing Committee for Dialogue Between Pool and Niari Legislators wishes to inform the Congolese people, especially those armed persons involved in recent clashes, that a cease-fire was decreed at midnight on 30 January. This cease-fire is accompanied by a series of concrete measures that will contribute to restoring life to normalcy. Primary among these measures are: Fighters are to stop any further advancements; they are to withdraw with their arms; they are to lay down their arms and ammunition; and an intervention force is to be established that will be deployed on 14 February. In this regard, any armed person caught in the act of disrupting peace and order shall be punished in accordance with the law.
