920323 FT 23 MAR 92 / Construction Contracts: Improving access to Newmarket The LILLEY GROUP has been awarded Pounds 38m of contracts during February and March. The largest order, worth Pounds 9.3m, is the A11 Four Wentways to Newmarket contract, won by Lilley Construction. The contract involves the upgrading of the existing single carriageway to two lanes and the construction of new and realigned side roads and four bridges. Work is due to commence on March 30 and will run for 75 weeks. Lilley Building has won a contract worth Pounds 5m to build a Post House hotel for Forte (UK) in Colchester. Work is due to start at the end of March and is scheduled for completion early in 1993. Henry Jones Construction has been awarded a Pounds 5.3m design and build award for the construction of 124 dwellings and some infrastructure work at the Preston Barracks site in Brighton. The project will run for 72 weeks. The remaing Pounds 18.5m of contracts were won throughout the group; the Scottish companies, Lilley Construction Scotland and MDW (Pounds 2.7m); the north of England companies, Eden Construction and Robison & Davidson (Pounds 9.8m); the Midlands operations, Lilley Construction Midlands, the Standen Group and Piper Buildings (Pounds 2.2m); and the southern-based companies, Henry Jones and Lilley Construction Southern (Pounds 3.8m).
