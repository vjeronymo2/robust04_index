941026 FT 26 OCT 94 / International Company News: American Express may sell life assurance arm American Express, the US financial services and travel group, said it had retained investment bankers to explore a possible sale of its Amex Life Assurance unit, which has been approached by several insurers interested in acquiring the business, Reuter reports. American Express said it had recently decided that the Amex Life long-term care insurance business was 'not a critical element in the company's long-term financial services strategy'. Amex Life, with about 400 staff, had Dollars 2bn in assets at the end of 1993. 'The interest we are seeing from potential buyers comes at a good time,' said Mr Thomas Skiff, Amex Life president. 'These insurers see the potential of this business and the opportunity to capitalise on our position as the industry leader.'
