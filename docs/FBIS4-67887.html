BFN [Text] Australia has become the sixth country to ratify the Chemical Weapons Convention in a document lodged at the United Nations headquarters in New York. Since the convention was opened in January last year, 157 countries have signed. The convention banning chemical weapons will come into force after it is ratified by 65 countries. Australia's Foreign Minister Gareth Evans said Australia's ratification ensured it would continue to play a leading role in efforts to get a global ban on chemical weapons. Senator Evans said it was vital that other countries that had signed the treaty more quickly to meet the requirements for ratification. [sentence as heard]
