Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, the Liberal Democratic Party of Russia (LDPR) leader, told Interfax that the Russian president had been right in refusing to meet former U.S. President Richard Nixon. In Zhirinovskiy's view, by meeting opposition leaders and former vice-president Aleksandr Rutskoy, "who did not represent anybody," before sitting with the president, Nixon was "discourteous to the head of state." Meeting Yeltsin was expected to be the highlight of Nixon's visit. Zhirinovskiy was not sure whether he would meet with Nixon. He did not have an official word on this but knew that such a meeting was on Nixon's schedule. Zhirinovskiy made it clear that his country "never assumed a posture" or refused contacts. He said that his party had been invited to a reception in the U.S. Embassy Thursday night on the occasion of Nixon's stay in Russia. Zhirinovskiy's deputy in the LDPR and Deputy Chairman of the State Duma Aleksandr Vengerovskiy would attend the reception.
