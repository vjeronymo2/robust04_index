940527 FT 27 MAY 94 / International Company News: Big jump in volume of Korean futures Trading volume in financial derivatives by banks in South Korea more than doubled in the first quarter of 1994, Reuter reports from Seoul. The Bank of Korea (BoK) said derivatives traded by banks dealing in foreign exchange totalled Dollars 107.1bn from January to March, up 163.1 per cent from Dollars 40.7bn a year earlier. Derivatives are financial contracts designed to hedge the risk to underlying assets from fluctuations in interest rates, currencies and commodity prices.
