940204 FT 04 FEB 94 / Observer: Commissar Chris The office of Chris Meyer, the no-nonsense press secretary charged with polishing John Major's image, offers an insight - maybe a warning? - as to how he plans to run the show. Meyer has adorned his office with framed copies of Pravda, the once all-powerful organ of the Soviet Communist party. Close inspection reveals that the issues date not from post-democracy Russia, but instead from the good old days, when anti-regime criticisms could merit a spell in the gulag. Pravda's most important function then was to kid its readers that all was well and the political leadership the best imaginable. Meyer's nostalgia may be deeper than he thinks . . .
