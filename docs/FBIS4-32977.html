BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 31 May. Preliminary forecasts by the National Foreign Trade Council show that Mexican exports to the United States reached $4.5 billion in April, a 6-percent increase over exports in March. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 24 May 94 p 22) The Finance and Public Credit Secretariat has reported that from 1990 to date, Mexico's public foreign debt has negatively affected the international exchange of the dollar as compared with other currencies. The report adds that in the first three months of 1994 the balance of Mexico's foreign debt rose to $1.116 billion. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 25 May 94 pp 1, 4, 3A) Francisco Rojas, director of Mexican Petroleum (PEMEX), has announced that a huge oil deposit has been discovered in the Jujo-Tecominoacan field, Tabasco State, with reserves estimated at 431 million barrels of crude oil. Rojas also reported that PEMEX will launch the Olmeca Project which is designed to increase the utilization of light crude reserves from the Mezozoico Chiapas- Tabasco deposits. He added that the development of the Caan, Ek-Balam, and Taruntunich fields will produce revenues of $1.3 billion, which will allow PEMEX to fully recover all of its 1993 and 1994 investments. (Mexico City LA JORNADA in Spanish 25 May 94 pp 1, 52) EL FINANCIERO has conducted some economic research that shows Mexico does not have the capacity to retain foreign reinvestments aimed at increasing productivity because profits sent abroad amount to $1.41 billion, which is 25 percent higher than last year's figure. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 25 May 94 pp 1, 4, 3A) Finance and Public Credit Minister Pedro Aspe Armella has reported that although jobs have increased at assembly plants and commercial establishments, the number of workers registered with the Mexican Social Security Institute has decreased 1.3 percent over the first three months of 1994. Aspe added that the unemployment rate during the same period is the highest in the past six years. He explained that the unemployment rate was 3.8 percent in January, 3.7 percent in February, and 3.6 percent in March. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 27 May 94 p 25) Victor Manuel Terrones Lopez, president of National Association of Processing Industry, has stated that the Mexican private sector will not give into pressure from U.S. industrialists and farmers who want Mexico to expedite the lifting of tariffs on various products under NAFTA. Terrones Lopez pointed out that the U.S. industrialists and farmers have prepared a list that would place Mexico at a disadvantage. The list contains food, chemical, plastic, and manufactured products. (Mexico City Radio ACIR Network in Spanish 0000 GMT 27 May 94) The World Bank has granted loans for $80 million to support educational programs in Mexico over the next five years, according to a spokesman for the World Bank. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 2155 GMT 27 May 94) On 27 May, PEMEX reported that Mexico had exported 1,338,900 barrels of crude oil per day during the first three months of the year. The oil went to Europe, the Far East, and the United States. (Paris AFP in Spanish 0029 GMT 28 May 94) The Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) has granted Mexico a $150 million credit for development projects connected with Cancun's recreational and tourism facilities. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0259 GMT 28 May 94) A study prepared by the National Bank of Mexico (BANAMEX) points out that the private sector's level of indebtedness with the banking system has become an obstacle for overall economic growth. The report describes statements by some experts who predict there will be economic growth of more than 3 percent in 1994 as "exaggerated optimism." The BANAMEX study adds that 1994's economic growth will be approximately 2 percent. (Mexico City LA JORNADA in Spanish 30 May 94 p 53)
