BFN [Unattributed article: "United States/Italy: Maroni Meets With Ambassador Bartholomew"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, May 19 -- Italian Interior Minister Roberto Maroni met there Thursday [19 May] with United States Ambassador Reginald Bartholomew, also in view of President Bill Clinton's upcoming visit to Italy, and reiterated how the fight against organized crime was a top priority of the new government, headed by Premier Silvio Berlusconi. A statement from the Interior Ministry added how Maroni had stressed "the validity and appreciation of the Italian government for the high level cooperation achieved in the fight against organized crime and drug trafficking." "He then underlined" the Ministry note continued, "how one of the government's top priorities, also to enhance the confidence of citizens in the institutions of the democratic state, remained the offensive against the mafia, especially in the harder hit regions, which will now involve a more incisive role by local governments." "Ambassador Bartholomew" the statement added, "renewed the high esteem of the FBI and DEA for the significant level of professionalism and specialization of Italian law enforcement agencies." During today's meeting, the Ministry said, the minister and ambassador confirmed the September meeting in Washington of the Italian-American committee for collaboration against the mafia, terrorism and drug trafficking.
