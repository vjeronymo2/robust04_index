Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali has reaffirmed Pakistan's principled stand that the Kashmir issue should be resolved on the basis of the UN resolutions, and India should end its brutalities in occupied Kashmir. Addressing a joint news conference with his Kazakh counterpart in Almaty, he said both India and Pakistan are committed within the framework of the UN resolutions and the Simla Agreement to settle the Kashmir issue peacefully. He said Kashmiris have been subjected to the worst kind of genocide in the occupied territory by Indian forces, and India has also blocked all access to the Kashmir valley to the outside world, including humanitarian organizations. Explaining Pakistan's peaceful nuclear program, the foreign minister said Pakistan has made a political decision not to make nuclear weapons.
