Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [ITIM report] [Text] Portuguese Foreign Minister Jose Durao Barroso arrived in Israel yesterday afternoon for a two-day visit, his first to this country. Upon his arrival, Barroso said he is closely involved in the Middle East peace process and noted that he had conferred with Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres during the latter's visit to Portugal and with Yasir 'Arafat. According to Barroso, the purpose of his visit is to deepen relations between Israel and Portugal in political, economic, trade, and tourism spheres. Upon his arrival yesterday, Barroso stated that Portugal and Israel will also deepen their cultural ties. Yehuda Milo, assistant director general of the Foreign Ministry, who greeted Barroso at the airport, said that there is fruitful cooperation between the defense industries of Israel and Portugal and that the two countries are now working on a cooperation agreement in the area of science and development. The agreement may be ready to be signed when Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin visits Lisbon next week. Barroso was scheduled to confer with President 'Ezer Weizman last night and later to dine with Minister Peres. Today the Portuguese foreign minister will hold a working session with his Israeli counterpart, confer with the prime minister, and tour the Yad Vashem Holocaust Memorial Museum. He will also be briefed on the region by a senior Army officer.
