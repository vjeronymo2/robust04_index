This FMN reports the names of one newly appointed ambassador, the recipients of five diplomatic title awards, three ministerial and six judicial appointees and the new State Security Administration press representative. President Punsalmaagiyn Ochirbat appointed Dariyn Puntsag Mongolian ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to the Republic of Cuba. (Ulaanbaatar ARDYN ERH in Mongolian 15 Jan 94 p The president also awarded the diplomatic title of ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to O. Ochirbal, director of the European and American Desk of Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and the diplomatic titles of envoy extraordinary and plenipotentiary to the following: L. Dabaagib, Mongolian ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to the U.S.; A. Tsolmon, Mongolian ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary to Germany; D. Chuluundorj, director of the Press and Information Department of the Ministry of Foreign Relations; and L. Erdenechuluun, permanent Mongolian representative to the United Nations. (ARDYN ERH 19 Jan 94 p 1) At the conclusion of the 14 January 1994 meeting of the State Great Hural, the following changes in senior assignments were made as a result of restructuring the Mongolian government and merging ministries: R. Sandalhan was relieved of his duties as minister of highways, transportation and communications and appointed minister of infrastructure development; and B. Jigjid was relieved of his former duties as minister of fuel and power and appointed minister of power, geology and mining. With the abolishment of two ministries the following were relieved of their assignments: T. Damiran as minister of construction and town planning; and D. Tsogbaatar as minister of geology and mineral resources. (ARDYN ERH 18 Jan 94 p 1) The Government of Mongolia appointed Dagbadorjiyn Tserendorj deputy minister of culture. (ARDYN ERH 22 Feb 94 p 2) Based on the recommendation of the General Council of the Court, B. Batsangee was appointed judge for a Darhan City District People's Court and B. Haldzad judge for a Dundgobi Aymag District People's Court, and N. Dulamsuren was released from his duties as a member of the Suhbaatar Aymag Court. At the recommendation of the General Council of the Court based on paragraph 2 of Article 51 of the Constitution the following appointments were made: J. Hashdorj and P. Tsengee as judges of the Supreme Court; and N. Dulamsuren as Suhbaatar Aymag Appeals Court judge. (ARDYN ERH 15 Jan 94 p 1) In accordance with an order of the State Security Administration, N. Tomorhuyag was appointed the press and information representative of the administration. (ARDYN ERH 12 Jan 94 p 1) (AUTHOR: MIAB/CISD.
