BFN [Text] Vidin, April 12 (BTA) -- The representatives of the Bulgarian state institutions controlling the implementation of sanctions along the Danube and the officials of the Western European Union [WEU] ended their formal meeting. The participants focussed on the measures that would ensure efficient control over the application of sanctions along the Danube and specified the actions which the teams of the WEU and the Bulgarian institutions should take in case of embargo busting, BTA was told by Vasko Vasilev, deputy head of the Vidin Customs. The representatives of Bulgaria and the WEU shared the opinion that each specific conflict should be resolved by peaceful means in order to protect the population and the population centres and prevent any environmental disaster along the river. It was agreed that a similar meeting should be held in Bucharest, where the WEU representatives would talk with Romania's governmental commission to specify the actions to be taken in a case of embargo busting and their coordination by the Bulgarian, Romanian and WEU teams.
