930804 FT 04 AUG 93 / Tory MPs warn against British involvement in Bosnia air strikes The reaction of some Tory MPs suggested that British involvement in air strikes would risk opening fresh divisions in the party, David Owen writes. Mr Nicholas Budgen, MP for Wolverhampton South-West, said: 'We should declare that there is no British national interest at stake here . . ., that the best way we can contribute to future peace is to withdraw our forces and emphasise to the contestants that their future lies in their own hands.' Mr John Wilkinson, MP for Ruislip Northwood, said he was 'sceptical' about the efficacy of air strikes. 'I think it would heighten Serb intransigence and probably lead to retaliation against our troops.' But Mr Patrick Cormack, MP for Staffordshire South, said: 'I believe we should have taken firm action a long, long time ago.'
