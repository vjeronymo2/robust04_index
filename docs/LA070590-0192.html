July 5, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

JAPANESE RESTAURANT CHAIN BUYS BEST WESTERN FOODS

Restaurant chain operators Kyotaru Co. said today that it has purchased Best Western Food Inc., a meat-processing company based in Los Angeles, for $41.26 million to supply beef for the Japanese market.

Japan, under pressure from the United States, agreed in 1988 to drop quotas on imported beef products by April 1, 1991.

In response, Japanese food companies have been actively buying ranches overseas to continue to supply beef to their customers through Japanese sources.

Best Western will supply beef to the Kyotaru restaurant chain, according to reports in the Nihon Keizai Shimbun and the Kyodo News Service.

Kyotaru spokesman Masakazu Yoda said the company has not decided if it will import beef for other distribution purposes through Best Western.

Yoda said that Robert Wiviodt, Best Western's president, has been replaced but that other management-level employees will remain with the company.

Best Western had sales of $66.13 million in 1989, according to Kyotaru.
