BFN [Text] Budapest, 10 May (MTI) -- The Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP) considers winning over 30 percent of votes in the first round of the elections as a major achievement, which has increased the Socialists' sense of responsiblity, Acting Deputy Chairman Imre Szekeres told MTI after the MSZP Presidium's session today. No matter what the second round brings, the MSZP has become a determining factor in Hungary's political life, he said. The Presidium confirmed its previous position that coalition talks should only start after the second round. The Socialists underlined that such talks should be based on the compatibility of party manifestos.
