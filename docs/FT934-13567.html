931021 FT 21 OCT 93 / Hong Kong plea is rejected THE GOVERNMENT said yesterday that it would not relent over its refusal to grant British citizenship to some 7,000 people from non-Chinese ethnic minorities in Hong Kong in spite of the Lords defeat it suffered on the issue in July. Earl Ferrers, a Home Office minister, told peers the government had given 'very careful consideration' to the matter, but had no plans to introduce fresh legislation. Lord Bonham-Carter, the Liberal Democrat peer who spearheaded the earlier defeat, said that he thought the government's response was 'deeply unsatisfactory'.
