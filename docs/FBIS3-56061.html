Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report under the "NEGA Reports" rubric] [Text] Regarding the meeting in Kiev between the presidents of the United States and Ukraine scheduled for today, Yuriy Sergeyev, head of the information service, stated at a news briefing at Ukraine's Foreign Ministry that it will be devoted to discussing the final text of a document on the future of Ukrainian nuclear weapons. This document is expected to be signed in Moscow on 14 January by the Presidents of the United States, Ukraine, and Russia. According to Sergeyev, there is still no final text of the document, and it is still unknown whether it is going to be an agreement, treaty, statement, communique, or declaration of three sides. As is known, a treaty and an agreement are subject to ratification by the Ukrainian parliament, whereas a communique, statement, or a declaration do not need this. Yuriy Sergeyev also stated that should the text of the document not be agreed on in Moscow, the tripartite meeting will be of a consultative nature, the same as recent similar talks in Kiev, Moscow, and Washington.
