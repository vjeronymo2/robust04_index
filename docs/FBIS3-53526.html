Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] A highly placed source has said that an explosion occurred in the Border Guard Corps headquarters, which is adjacent to [words indistinct] building in Baghdad, on 11 January 1994. The source reported that the evacuation of those who were killed or wounded in the blast to the hospitals lasted one and a half hours. The source added that a military building used by the Special Forces in Baghdad was the target of an attack with automatic weapons on 1 January. Captain Sabri Wahid Duri and three soldiers were killed in the assault. Commenting on the deteriorating health conditions in Baghdad and the other Iraqi governorates under the regime's control, the source said that AIDS has spread in Baghdad and other cities to the point where hospitals are unable to offer treatment to all the patients or control this disease. In Baghdad 222 AIDS cases have been reported with 23 other cases in al-Basrah, 11 in Karkuk, and 86 in al-Ramadi. Additionally, the Bubonic Plague has spread in al-Basrah, al-'Amarah, and Baghdad with 26 cases of this disease reported by Baghdad hospitals alone.
