BFN [Text] Chairman of the State Duma Committee for international affairs Vladimir Lukin considers "improbable" that the lower chamber of parliament will hold a closed meeting to discuss the situation in former Yugoslavia Wednesday, April 20. Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin earlier reported to Interfax about the possibility of holding such a meeting after meeting with speaker of the State Duma Ivan Rybkin. According to Lukin, the Council of the State Duma on Tuesday decided to put on the agenda of the plenary meeting on Wednesday only the issue of sending a parliamentary delegation to the republics of former Yugoslavia. He pointed out that the delegation will include representatives of all factions and intends to get acquainted with the positions of all sides in the conflict.
