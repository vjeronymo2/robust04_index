BFN [Text] Moscow June 8 ITAR-TASS -- A monthly poll conducted by the Association of Political and Economic Experts on order from "THE NEW DAILY" has revealed that President Boris Yeltsin, Moscow Mayor Yuriy Luzhkov, and First Vice-Premier Oleg Soskovets are at top of the list of the most influential politicians in Russia in May. These leaders scored 54.5, 52.625, and 50.25 points respectively. They are closely followed by Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin (49.75), Central Bank Governor Viktor Gerashchenko (48.125), Yeltsin's body-guard chief Aleksandr Korzhakov (47.75), State Duma Chairman Ivan Rybkin (47.625), National Security Council Secretary Oleg Lobov (46.75), Federation Council Chairman Vladimir Shumeyko (44.5), and the president's first aide Viktor Ilyushin (44.125). The list published in today's issue of THE NEW DAILY comprises 50 names. The newspaper explains that they are the names of the leading politicians, including the leaders of parties and political movements, executive and legislative bodies of power, information and commercial structures in command of considerable resources and opportunities for making political and state decisions and ensuring their realization. According to experts, regional leaders and officials of the military-industrial complex "are now on the offensive". "The modest and quiet Rybkin is today's real alternative to the president," they say. "Unless, of course, the left-wingers and the patriots agree to support a common candidate, as proposed by the Communist Party leader Gennadiy Zyuganov. The largest faction in the State Duma is controlled not by the Liberal-Democratic Party of Russia (50 deputies), and not by Russia's Choice (70), but by the coalition of Communists and Agrarians (100 deputies). "Sergey Shakhray's rating has not declined as expected" after his dismissal from the post of the minister of nationalities and regional policy, the newspaper says. "Shakhray is an independent figure and his time is not past," the newspaper concludes.
