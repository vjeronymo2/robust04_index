930324 FT 24 MAR 93 / People: Mercury Asset Management One member of the government's National Advisory Council for Education and Training Targets, a high-powered committee unveiled last week, is Carol Galley (right), one of the City's most successful female fund managers. Galley, deputy chairman of Mercury Asset Management, points out that, given her close involvement in investment decisions, it would be inappropriate for her to sit on the board of a company. But she says she has agreed to work for Nacett not only because she believes generally in the importance of vocational training to the success of British industry but because of her faith in the abilities of Peter Davis, chief executive of Reed Elsevier and of Post Office chairman Michael Heron, the council's chairman and vice chairman respectively. 'I have observed them in the corporate sector. If anyone has a chance of making something like this work, it is these people,' she reckons. She joined Warburg Investment Management as a librarian in 1971 straight from university.
