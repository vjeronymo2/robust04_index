920404 FT 04 APR 92 / Election 1992: Labour plans early Budget in first parliamentary term LABOUR yesterday set out its legislative timetable for the first session of parliament, saying it hoped to present its Budget before the end of May. Mr Jack Cunningham, campaign co-ordinator, said bills would be introduced during the first session to establish a Scottish parliament, abolish the poll tax and ensure freedom of information. These bills would all have their second reading before the summer recess. 'Victory is certainly within our grasp,' Mr Cunningham said. But he conceded that no election was over until the votes were cast and promised strong campaigning right up to polling day. The party leader, Mr Neil Kinnock, said: 'We have constructive and realistic policies to deal with the serious problems that the UK faces and will get on with serving our country from the first moment of election to office.' Mr Kinnock said that Labour's programme for immediate action included a Pounds 1.1bn economic recovery programme to end the recession and to create sustained economic growth. He detailed increases to old age pensions and child benefit, and improvements to the National Health Service, halting its 'privatisation' and an immediate start to use the extra Pounds 1bn allocated to the health service. There would also be improvements to education, with investment of an extra Pounds 600m over the next 22 months, which would include the provision of a new books grant of Pounds 10 for every pupil. Mr Kinnock said: 'The National Institute for Economic and Social Research has studied our proposals and confirmed that our recovery programme will lead to higher growth and cut unemployment by 300,000 as compared with the effects of the Tories' Budget.' Mr Kinnock also pledged to extend Britain's influence within the European Community, ensuring that Britain was in the 'European first division'. Asked about the campaign, Mr Kinnock said later in Southampton: 'We are going steady and victorious but not triumphalist.'
