Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, Jan 13 (TANJUG) -- Representatives of the UN High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) in Sarajevo reported Thursday [13 January] that forces of the Bosnian Croat army HVO, have for the past two days been holding up a humanitarian convoy bringing aid to Bosnian Moslems in Maglaj, northern Bosnia. They reported, according to AFP, that the column of nine trucks with food and medicine is blocked in Novi Seher, 10 kilometers from Maglaj, and that the UN representative Larry Hollingworth is negotiating its passage with the local HVO commanders. They also said that the Bosnian Serb forces allowed the convoy to pass through their territory without any problems. UNHCR representatives in Sarajevo also warned about the extremely difficult position of the 40,000 civilians in Maglaj since the last convoy arrived there on October 25 last year.
