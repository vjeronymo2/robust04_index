Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [YONHAP report from Tokyo] [Text] Japan's KYODO news agency reports from Beijing on 9 March that in the upcoming Chinese People's National Congress meeting, the government action report that Premier Li Peng will present is supposed to mention China's friendly relations with North Korea first in its foreign policy division, thereby stressing the complete restoration of North Korea-China relations. KYODO reported this citing Chinese sources, and said that while China's top priority last year was on the development of Sino- Japanese relations, the order of priority has changed this year. KYODO said that China will list North Korea-China friendly relations first on China's priority list in Premier Li Peng's foreign policy action report apparently to proclaim the complete restoration of Sino-North Korean relations which have cooled off since the establishment of Sino-ROK relations in August 1992.
