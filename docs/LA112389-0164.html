November 23, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

SPILLING THE BEANS

Question: Only a short while ago I never would have believed some of the foods that are being recommended today as part of a healthy diet. I always used to think that pasta and potatoes had to be avoided, but now I can understand that these are nutritious foods. Now I see that beans have become a kind of glamour food. Is this just a fad, or do beans belong in the average diet?

Answer: Beans are a nutritious food that have the added advantage of providing a great deal of fiber. They also lend themselves to being served in combination with a variety of animal foods, such as meat, fish, poultry, eggs, milk and cheese, as well as with non-animal foods, such as peas and lentils. These combinations provide a complete protein.

Still another advantage is that beans are an economical food, a consideration that cannot be ignored. Furthermore, they do not contain unwanted fat or salt.

One of the criticisms often heard about beans is that they cause gas. While this is true, this effect can be reduced by adding beans to the diet gradually, as well as by the manner in which they are prepared.
