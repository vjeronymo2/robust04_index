Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Sitnikov] [Text] United Nations Organisation March 3 TASS -- The planes, brought down in Bosnia, took off from the Croatian territory, Croatian Foreign Minister Mate Granic admitted on Wednesday [2 March] at the U.N. headquarters. Responding to the question of journalists, whether the planes, that were brought down, were Serb or Croatian, Granic said: "The planes flew from the zone, protected by the U.N., from the Nudvina airport. A permanent representative of Croatia at the U.N. has already informed of it the chairman of the Security Council. We suggested that the U.N. put that airport under control."
