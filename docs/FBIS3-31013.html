Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Pool" item; by Dejan Lukic] [Text] Washington, February 13 (TANJUG) -- Since the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation (NATO) sent an ultimatum to Bosnian Serbs that they would be bombed unless they withdrew their heavy weapons to a distance of 20 km from the center of Sarajevo by February 21, media in the United States have been showing their own and the world public every possible way in which every possible piece of ammunition will inevitably drop on the heads of Bosnian Serbs. For a full three years since the Gulf war, American media, spearheaded by the CNN, have never had a better chance to promote in such an aggressive manner the power of American weaponry and such accompanying arrogance that can hardly be attributed to a civilized country. According to American media, the planes of Admiral Burda, who is in charge of the operation in Bosnia, revved up their engines already on Friday evening because some shells exploded in Sarajevo. It was later established, however, that there were about fifty of them and that most of them were Moslem. The American public was immediately informed from NATO's southern command in Naples that a special group in charge of picking potential Serb targets had specified several hundred of them, from individual artillery pieces to warehouses of fuel and munitions. In the war of nerves which was launched by the U.S. Administration and taken up with clockwork precision by the American media, the information was released immediately that the turning on of the engines of Admiral Burda was, unfortunately, a false alarm. The action was nevertheless welcome, the media emphasized, because it had enabled all services and commands to be put into operation as in an actual situation. Admiral Burda has 146 planes at his disposal. In the escalation of military combinations and war cries, he immediately asked for an additional eight "super" planes, cheered on by CNN and company, which would be able to strike by day or by night, in rain or fog, from high or low altitudes. Eight F-15s were immediately flown from bases in Great Britain to airports in Italy. CNN hurried to show until now unusual "secret" CIA shots showing, as the reporter explained, the helpless appearance of Bosnian Serb positions around Sarajevo, filmed with optical instruments of U.S. planes. Everything has been filmed, pinpointed, nothing can be hidden, in the daytime or at night, and now all that is expected is the signal to begin. One of the "commanders" of the CNN is already in the Adriatic on the aircraft carrier The Saratoga. Everything is in readiness here, engines are roaring, missiles have been attached under the wings of the planes, only Butrus Butrus-Ghali seems to be hesitating still, and the Russians are ignored anyway.
