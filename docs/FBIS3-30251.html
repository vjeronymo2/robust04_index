Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Prices Went up in December Also"] [Text] Bratislava--According to an analysis by the Slovak Statistical Office, consumer prices rose by 0.6 percent in December. Over the 12 months of 1993, however, in comparison with December of 1992, the prices grew by 16.7 percent, and in comparison with all of 1992 by 23.2 percent. Inflation increased in December last year by 0.6 percent in comparison with November and by 23.2 percent in comparison with the average of 1992. The increase in the price of eggs and egg products (4.1 percent) is most responsible for the overall increase in the price of food products (0.3 percent). In the nonfood group, prices rose by 0.2 percent in most of the categories surveyed, and by 0.5 percent in transportation and sporting accessories.
