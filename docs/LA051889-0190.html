May 18, 1989, Thursday, Orange County Edition

A LOW-KEY ARMED FORCES DAY

To honor all branches of the U.S. military service, Armed Forces Day has been celebrated the third Saturday in May since 1950. This year, local military bases' plans are very low key:

Armed Forces Reserve Center, Los Alamitos

Members of the Army's 40th Infantry Division march annually in the Torrance Armed Forces Day parade. No public observances are planned for the post itself.

Marine Corps Air Station, El Toro, and Marine Corps Helicopter Air Station, Tustin

Orange County Marines will not have public observances of the celebration. The El Toro air show April 29-30 and the Tustin open house earlier in the month were held in lieu of Armed Forces Day celebrations.

Naval Weapons Station, Seal Beach

The Seal Beach Station will share color guard honors with the Navy's Long Beach base in the annual Armed Forces Day parade in Torrance. As it did last year, the Navy will mount a photo display at Del Amo Fashion Center May 18-22. The exhibit features the Seal Beach site and its annexes in Corona, Fallbrook and Pomona.

Marine Corps Base, Camp Pendleton

The First Marine Division band and between 100 and 150 troops will march in the Torrance Armed Forces Day parade. Military equipment including armored trucks and a howitzer also will be paraded. Maj. Gen. John Monahan, commanding officer of the First Marine Division, will be in the reviewing stand.

Source: Individual bases
