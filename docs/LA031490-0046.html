March 14, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: MILK ALLERGIES

Question: When I try to drink milk by itself, I usually have a reaction to it.

I think I may be allergic to it. But when I have milk with chocolate syrup, it doesn't seem to bother me. Can chocolate keep someone from being allergic to milk?

Answer: People who are lactose-intolerant may experience gas and bloating when they try to drink milk. As I've explained previously, these people lack a sufficient amount of the intestinal enzyme lactase, which digests lactose. Lactose, in turn, is the sugar found in milk.

Investigators have reported that when chocolate flavoring is added to milk, the symptoms of gas and bloating are not as prominent. Exactly why this occurs is not known.
