Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 22 (XINHUA) -- Chinese State Councillor Luo Gan met with and hosted a banquet for Lawrence Eagleburger, former U.S. secretary of state, here yesterday evening. Eagleburger is here as a guest of the Chinese Foreign Ministry, leading a U.S. "China investment and trade" delegation. Luo, who is also secretary-general of the Chinese State Council, told Eagleburger and his party that the importance of Sino-U.S. relations has not been changed by the profound changes that have taken place in the world. He said that China and the U.S., two nations of major influence in the world, have extensive common interests in safeguarding world peace and stability and promoting international economic and trade cooperation. Luo said that China and the U.S. are complementary in many ways, including economy and trade, and the potential for developing bilateral cooperation of mutual benefit is great. The State Councillor noted that maintaining normal and good relations between the two countries is not only in the fundamental interest of the two peoples, but also accords with the will of the people of the whole world. Luo expressed the hope that the two sides would grasp the current favorable momentum in bilateral relations and make joint efforts based on concrete reality to put bilateral ties on the correct path as soon as possible. Eagleburger agreed with Luo, saying that the friendly relations between the U.S. and China are very important for the next century, and that he hoped to see such relations further improved and developed as soon as possible. Chinese Deputy Foreign Minister Liu Huaqiu and Eagleburger's entourage, including former Republican leader of the U.S. Senate Howard Baker and former U.S. Secretary of Education Lamar Alexander, took part in the meeting and banquet. Eagleburger and his party arrived here yesterday evening. In addition to Beijing, they are scheduled to visit Shanghai, Guangzhou and Shenzhen.
