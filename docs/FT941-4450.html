940311 FT 11 MAR 94 / World Trade News: EU rejects US allegations of hypocrisy over US-Japan trade dispute The European Commission yesterday rejected US allegations of hypocrisy over the US-Japan trade dispute, and denied it had erected barriers against US products, writes Gillian Tett in Brussels. It said although the EU did not endorse US strong-arm tactics in the trade row, it was 'just as concerned' about the deficits the EU and US had with Japan. The commission was responding to an attack on Wednesday night, by Mr Micky Kantor, US trade representative, who accused Europe of standing on the sidelines in the US-Japan dispute while continuing to impose barriers against some US goods. Sir Leon Brittan, EU trade commissioner, accepted that some problems remained between the EU and US over trade barriers, but insisted progress was being made to remove them. 'The EU also has a very long list of US trade barriers, some of which have been in existence for decades,' said Sir Leon. Examples included US tax rules on certain cars and regulations which stipulated levels of American tobacco in cigarettes, a spokesman added.
