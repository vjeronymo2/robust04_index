Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In Brussels Ukraine and the Commission of the European Union have initialled a treaty of friendship and cooperation. At a news conference after the ceremony the Ukrainian Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko called it the fist step to carry out the Rome agreement on the possible admission of new countries to the European community. In his opinion, in the near future the agreement will "guarantee Ukraine political support and economic assistance in its efforts to expand democracy and reform the economy on free market principles." Ukraine was the first CIS country to initial such a treaty. Earlier the three Baltic states became associated members of the European Union. Under the initialled document the two sides are to carry out a set of measures to integrate Ukraine in the community. Talks should begin in 1998 on Ukraine's membership in a free trade zone. In the near future agreements will be worked out on Ukrainian metal and coal exports to EU countries.
