Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Lord Jeffrey Archer, envoy of British Prime Minister John Major, delivered a speech at a meeting held by the Kurdistan parliament yesterday. The meeting was attended by Parliamentary Speaker Jawhar Namiq Salim; Deputy Speaker Najat Ahmad 'Aziz Zadah; Kurdistan Prime Minister Kusret Rasul; Muhammad Tawfiq Rahim, minister of humanitarian affairs and cooperation; and Khurshar Zibari, member of the Political Bureau of the Unified Kurdistan Democratic Party. The session was opened by the parliamentary speaker, who delivered a speech in which he welcomed Mr. Archer and spoke about his role in supporting the cause of the Kurdish people. He also noted the long relationship between the Kurdish people and Mr. Archer and his important role in supporting our people. The parliamentary speaker also praised the British Government's positive role in supporting democracy and human rights in the world in general, and in Iraq in particular, and its role in the implementation of UN Security Council Resolution 688. Mr. Archer then delivered a speech which he began by reading a letter that British Prime Minister John Major addressed to the parliament and the regional government in Kurdistan.
