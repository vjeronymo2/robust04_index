930728 FT 28 JUL 93 / Tourism red tape identified THE GOVERNMENT has identified 'more than 80 examples of expensive and time-wasting red tape hampering the British tourism industry', Mr Iain Sproat, junior national heritage minister, said yesterday. The areas receiving most complaints were: food safety and hygiene, fire safety, package travel regulations, electricity at work regulations, public entertainment licensing, tourism signposting, and price display regulations.
