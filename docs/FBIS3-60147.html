Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Rafael Medina Cruz] [Excerpts] Over 50 million new pesos worth of heroin, cocaine, ephedrine, maritol, and packed marijuana were confiscated during the past 48 hours by Federal Judicial Police forces in counternarcotics operations conducted in 15 federative states. The traffickers presumably intended to smuggle the drugs into the United States for sale and consumption. It was stressed that no incidents nor confrontations between authorities and criminals were reported during the activities. The operations were carried out in Sonora, Baja California, Michoacan, Tabasco, Tamaulipas, Sinaloa, Durango, Nayarit, Coahuila, Oaxaca, Guerrero, Nuevo Leon, Chihuahua, Jalisco, and Morelos. They also enabled the Office of the Attorney General of the Republic [PGR] to arrest 52 presumed drug dealers, and to seize 13 vehicles, a boat with an outboard motor, five firearms, and scores of useful rounds, which are at the disposal of the Federal Public Prosecutor's Offices. It was specified that, at the highway checkpoint established on the outskirts of the town of Hermosillo, Sonora, federal agents intercepted a tank truck purported to be carrying liquid petroleum gas. However, over 4.5 tons of cannabis indica were found in its tank, outfitted with special compartments. [passage omitted] As for Baja California State, the PGR reported that, in operations conducted in the towns of Tijuana and Mexicali, Humberto Fernandez Vazquez, Aurelia Peralta, Martha Bernal Silva, Margarita Montano Avila, Manuel Gracia Ramirez, and Juan Luevano Luna were arrested. They had in their possession 80 kg of ephedrine, a chemical precursor for refining cocaine and processing amphetamines. They intended to smuggle the ephedrine north into neighboring California. Concurrently, at the ferry terminal in Pichilingue, Baja California Sur, the federal agents seized approximately two tons of compressed marijuana being transported in special compartments on a trailer body and under mudguards of a passenger bus. Two persons were placed at the disposal of the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office in connection with this incident. [passage omitted]
