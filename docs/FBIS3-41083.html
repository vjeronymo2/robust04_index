Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Ignacio Gutierrez A.] [Text] Yesterday, the attorney general of the republic, Diego Valades Rios, made the first appointments of his administration, confirming Humberto Benitez Trevino as assistant attorney for preliminary investigations. He also designated Marcos Castillejos Escobar as assistant attorney for process control; Ricardo Franco Guzman, as assistant juridical attorney; and Alfonso Cabrera Morales, as assistant attorney for local offices and inspections. Javier Duenas Garcia was named chief administrative officer. Other appointees were Raul Campos Rabago, as commissioner of the National Institute To Combat Drugs, and Antonio Rodriguez Patino, as head of the Planning Center for Drug Control. As of yesterday, Fernando Aguilar became the new director of news media for the federal institution of justice, and Javier Casillas Zabalza, the private secretary to the head of the PGR [Office of the Attorney General of the Republic]. The new officials assumed their posts immediately, and were called upon by Diego Valades to "expend all their professional capacity and dedication to service on the critical tasks of the prosecution of justice, with strict adherence to the law and deep respect for human rights."
