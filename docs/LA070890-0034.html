July 8, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

RECORD RACK: *** CHRIS THOMAS, "CRY OF THE PROPHETS," SIRE/HIGHTONE: ALBUMS ARE RATED ON A SCALE OF ONE STAR (POOR) TO FIVE (A CLASSIC).

On his major-label debut, the son of minor-league blues man Tabby Thomas concocts a strange brew of deep soul, country blues, modern funk, Hendrix and Marley that falls somewhere between a more rockin' Robert Cray and a Lenny Kravitz without the Lennonisms. Despite a pro forma approach to songwriting and a somewhat limited vocal palette, young Thomas gets by on the depth of his emotional expression and a stinging, rootsy guitar style. An audience for this amalgam awaits. DON WALLER
