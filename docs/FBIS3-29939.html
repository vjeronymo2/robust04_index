Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, February 24 (KCNA) -- Party and state leaders of various countries voiced support to the just cause of the Korean people. Ugandan President Yoweri Kaguta Museveni, when he received a delegation of the Workers' Party of Korea (WPK) headed by Yi Chi-su, president of the Chuche Idea Academy, expressed full support to the WPK's stand toward the reunification question and the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. He expressed the hope that the relations between the Uganda National Resistance Movement and the WPK would be consolidated and developed further still. Inaan Raad, chairman of the Nationalist Socialist Party of Syria in Lebanon, when he received the DPRK ambassador to Lebanon, expressed firm support and solidarity for the Workers' Party and people of Korea in their struggle to defend the sovereignty of the country and dignity of the nation. The just struggle of the Korean people is a struggle to defend the interests of the peaceloving peoples of the world including the Arab people, he added. Pointing out that the "nuclear issue" of Korea is a product of the anti-DPRK moves of the United States, he stressed: "The U.S. must stop the nuclear threat as agreed upon in the DPRK-U.S. joint statement and take a sincere stand to solve the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula by means of negotiation, not by pressure." Mohamedoun Dicko, secretary general of the Executive Committee of the African Party for the Solidarity and Justice of Mali, when he received the DPRK ambassador, said that his party's support to the lines and policies of the Workers' Party of Korea would be invariable in the future. He expressed the conviction that Korea would certainly be reunified in the form of confederation clarified by the great leader his excellency President Kim Il-song. "It is the wisest way to solve the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula through the DPRK-U.S. talks," he said.
