Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow January 8 TASS -- Ad hoc committees and parliamentary groups set up to handle the preparation of the first session of the State Duma, the lower house of Russia's new parliament, continued their work on Saturday. Most heated debates took place at a meeting of the inter-regional association of independent deputies which was also attended by deputies who represent some Russian constituent republics and autonomous entities. The speakers stressed that independent deputies elected in individual constituencies who are not bound by any pledges to political parties, blocs and movements and who realise their responsibility before their voters, the constitution and Russia believe that "integration and coordination of efforts are necessary for real work of the State Duma." The participants in the meeting discussed ways to reach the goals set by their joint declaration adopted on January 5. In the declaration they expressed their determination to build their activities in the State Duma on the basis of "parliamentary freedom and responsibility, common sense, political realism, pragmatism, and respect for opinions and positions of other factions and groups." They plan to nominate their own candidate to the post of the chairman of the State Duma as their first step in this direction. The deputies called on their colleagues elected in individual constituencies to join their efforts for the benefit of all peoples of Russia. The commission for the procedural regulations of the State Duma and the deputy status discussed key questions the solution of which should ensure the effective work of the parliament.
