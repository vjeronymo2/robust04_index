Language: English Article Type:BFN [From YONHAP] [Text] Rev. Billy Graham of the United States left Beijing for Pyongyang on Thursday carrying with him a verbal message from U.S. President Bill Clinton to Kim Il-song, a government source said. "We understand Clinton's message calls on North Korea to resolve the nuclear question at an early date," he said. The source added that Rev. Graham will drop into Hong Kong after his visit to North Korea, where he will have a press conference on the results of his visit to Pyongyang. During his stay in Pyongyang, Graham is expected to meet President Kim Il-song and officiate service at the Pongsu church in Pyongyang. This is Graham's second visit to North Korea. During his first visit in April 1992, he conferred with Kim Il-song and gave a lecture at Kim Il-song University.
