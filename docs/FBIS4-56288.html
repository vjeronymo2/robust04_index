BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 28 April: The Central Bank of Uruguay reported on 15 April that the GDP grew 1.5 percent in 1993. The manufacturing industry's activities declined by 9 percent; the electricity, gas, and water supply companies' activities declined by 4.5 percent; agricultural and livestock activities barely grew 0.3 percent; fishing activities grew 0.8 percent; construction activities grew 13 percent; trade, restaurant, and hotel activities grew 6.7 percent; and transport and communications activities grew 9.2 percent. (Montevideo EL PAIS in Spanish 18 Apr 94 Market Indicators Section, p 1) Daniel Berti, a National Association of Dairy Farmers board member, has announced that milk production increased 11 percent since early January. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 22 Apr 94 Agriculture and Livestock Section, p 1) The Central Bank of Uruguay reported on 21 April that the government accumulated a $74-million deficit during the first two months of this year. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 22 Apr 94 p 10)
