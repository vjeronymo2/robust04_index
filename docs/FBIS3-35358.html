Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Israel thinks it possible for a comprehensive agreement to be signed with the Arab states by the end of the year: This was stated this evening by Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin at a dinner with [Portuguese Prime Minister] Cavaco Silva and other members of the government, during which Rabin offered to extend the hand of peace. [Begin recording of Rabin, in English] As for our other negotiating partners, my message is this: I am extending my hand to you as a sign of peace. We feel no hatred, no grudge or bigotry. Let us all make a pledge that we will do our utmost to achieve peace during this coming year so as to usher in a new era in a new Middle East, an era in which the integrity, the security and the sovereignty of all nations, all states, are respected in that Middle East of our wishes, where cooperation with dignity will prevail. [end recording] A statement to go down in history, marking the high point of this first day of the Israeli Prime Minister's visit to Portugal.
