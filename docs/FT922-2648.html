920616 FT 16 JUN 92 / International Company News: NZ white goods group shows sharp growth FISHER and Paykel, the New Zealand white goods manufacturer, has reported an 88.4 per cent rise in net profits to NZDollars 8m (USDollars 4.3m) from NZDollars 4.2m a year earlier, helped by strong growth in exports. The company reported a 50 per cent advance in sales in Australia following the opening of a factory in Queensland. This gives it 10 per cent of the Australian market. Continuing slack demand in New Zealand saw an 8 per cent drop in sales there to NZDollars 331.1m. Total export earnings, to Australia and the US, rose 40 per cent to NZDollars 144.3m. Operating revenue was virtually unchanged at NZDollars 504.83m. Directors said demand would remain low for the rest of the year. Tax was NZDollars 3.76m (NZDollars 2.2m). Bottom-line profit was NZDollars 6.9m against NZDollars 4.4m) last year. Shareholders' funds were NZDollars 225.9m compared with NZDollars 224.9m a year earlier. A total dividend of 8 cents a share is being recommended, up from 10 cents.
