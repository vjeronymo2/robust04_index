Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing March 24 (XINHUA) -- The upcoming visit to China by President Kim Young-sam of the Republic of Korea (ROK), will further promote the two countries' relations, especially in the economic field, said the ROK's ambassador to China, Hwang Pyong-tae, in a recent interview here with XINHUA. Hwang noted that although it is only a year and a half since the two countries set up diplomatic ties, China and ROK have greatly increased political, economic and cultural contacts. He said that the trade volume between the two countries last year reached 9.1 billion U.S. dollars, an increase of 50 percent over 1992. He noted that China has become ROK's third largest trade partner and ROK is now the sixth largest trade partner of China, adding that by the end of last year ROK's total investment in China exceeded one billion U.S. dollars. Hwang predicted that the annual trade volume between China and ROK will increase greatly by the year 2000 if the two sides keep up the current momentum of economic and trade cooperation. Exchanges in the cultural and other fields between the two countries are increasing as well, the ambassador said, adding some of the well-known universities, such as Beijing University and Fudan University (in Shanghai), now have research institutes on ROK studies. More than 150,000 people from the ROK toured China last year, according to Hwang, and is estimated to grow to about 500,000 this year. On the prospect of Sino-ROK ties in the 21st century, Hwang said that the biggest problem of the coming century would be the economy. He stressed that China and ROK, bringing their respective economic advantages into play, should make joint efforts to strengthen their economic cooperation.
