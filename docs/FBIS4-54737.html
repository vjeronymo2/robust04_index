BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 3/5/1994 -- "We believe that some positive results have been obtained and Romania's relation with Germany is on the right track," said Chamber of Deputies Speaker Adrian Nastase at a press conference on Tuesday, 3 May, while assessing the results of a five-day visit in Germany made by a Romanian parliamentary delegation. The members of the Romanian delegation consider they delivered to the German party a clear message stressing the interests Germany could have in this zone. In reply, the German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel said his next visit abroad would be in Romania. According to the delegation members, the German minister said Germany did not make discriminations between Romanian and the member countries of the Visegrad Group. The members of the Romanian parliamentary delegation opined that Romania's relations with Germany must develop not only at federal level but also at the level of the lander, especially the Eastern ones.
