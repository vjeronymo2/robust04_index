BFN [Text] Libyan revolution leader Colonel Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi hosted a banquet last night in honor of President Husni Mubarak and his delegation. It was attended by the Libyan prime minister, senior Libyan officials, secretaries of the General People's Congresses, Egypt's ambassador to the Libyan Jamahiriyah, and the Libyan ambassador in Egypt. Talks between President Husni Mubarak and Libyan revolution leader Colonel Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi opened in the Libyan city of Surt last night. President Mubarak and Colonel al-Qadhdhafi held a bilateral session of talks followed by an expanded session attended by members of the delegation accompanying President Mubarak, the secretary of the Libyan General People's Committee, ministers, Egypt's ambassador to the Jamahiriyah, and Libya's ambassador to Egypt. Information Minister Safwat al-Sharif said the meeting between President Mubarak and Colonel Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi dealt with several Arab, regional, and world issues. The delegates exchanged views on the Yemeni crisis, developments in Somalia, and the Middle East issue. The meeting also dealt with the general world situation and its political and economic impact on Arab and regional issues. Al-Sharif added that the two leaders' talks covered the outcome of the African summit held in the Tunisian capital recently and the meetings and discussion held on the sidelines of the summit. The information minister said the delegates reviewed bilateral relations and assessed the implementation of previous resolutions passed by the Egyptian-Libyan Higher Joint Committee. Within this context the delegates discussed implementing a project to build 170 km of railroad between Musa'id and Tobruk supervised by a joint working group from the Egyptian railroad and Libyan technicians using both countries' local resources. Also discussed was a project to lay an oil pipeline between Libya and Egypt and an agreement to conduct a joint study of the project and set up an Egyptian-Libyan company to execute it. The information minister added that the delegates reviewed the electrical grid project between the two countries and measures taken in this regard as well as bilateral cooperation in various sectors, including cultural and media cooperation. President Mubarak and his delegation arrived in the Libyan city of Surt last night. They were received by Colonel Mu'ammar al- Qadhdhafi and top Libyan officials.
