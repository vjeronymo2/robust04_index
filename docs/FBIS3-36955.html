Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] The National Space Agency of Ukraine has reached an agreement with the well-known European company Matra-Marconi Space on the latter's participation in the satellite program Lebid. The European Community has allocated $400,000 for its development. Such well-known Ukrainian enterprises and institutes as Dnipropetrovsk's Pivdenmash, Kiev's Kvant and Saturn, and Symferopol's Musson have also been included in the program. It is expected that, through the cooperation of the French, British, and Ukrainian firms, a communications satellite will be sent into geostationary orbit in 1997, most probably from the Baykonur launch facility. This was reported today by the national Space Agency of Ukraine.
