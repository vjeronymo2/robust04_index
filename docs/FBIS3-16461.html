Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Liberal Democratic Party's (LDP) leader, Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, asked the U.S. "not to support those who had lost the parliamentary elections on December 12, that is to say Gaydar, Yavlinskiy, Shakhray and others who consider themselves democrats. This was announced by Zhirinovskiy after a meeting with former U.S. President Richard Nixon which lasted for about an hour and a half. According to Zhirinovskiy, the former U.S. President "has the right understanding of the situation in the world and of our problems." "The main point that I wanted to make known to U.S. President Bill Clinton is that he must not be afraid of me," Zhirinovskiy emphasized. He said Russia was moving towards a military regime and the danger stemmed from those who had lost the elections, from the so-called radical democrats. Commenting on the chances of candidates running for presidential elections "which may take place earlier than within two years," Zhirinovskiy said his only real contender was former Russian vice president Aleksandr Rutskoy. According to the LDP leader, neither Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin, nor Moscow Mayor Yuriy Luzhkov, neither Gaydar, Yavlinskiy and Shakhray, nor candidates from "the Communist, pro-communist or patriotic forces" could be his contenders. Richard Nixon's advisor, Dmitri Simes, told the news conference that Zhirinovskiy had told Nixon that Yeltsin was "losing his influence." According to Symes, Nixon gave neither personal comments nor assessments during their conversation. Asked whether Nixon changed his opinion of Zhirinovskiy after their meeting, Simes said that Nixon had been "informed that Mr. Zhirinovskiy could perform in different genres and in different situations. I am confident that the way Mr. Zhirinovskiy spoke today made due impression on President Nixon and he could place it in a certain context, since he knew both the speeches and political history of Mr. Zhirinovskiy." Shortly after his meeting with Zhirinovskiy, Nixon received the leader of the Democratic Party of Russia faction in the State Duma, Nikolay Travkin. Their meeting lasted for 1 hour 20 minutes. In an interview with Interfax, Travkin said the conversation mainly focused on economic issues and prospects for equal partnership between Russia and the U.S. According to Travkin, he told Nixon that Russia would "not fall into coups and civil wars," because Russia had everything needed to avoid it. The only thing "lacking is political will, that of the president in the first place. You seem the only optimist here," concluded Nixon.
