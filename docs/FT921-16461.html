920103 FT 03 JAN 92 / Major urges UN Security Council summit Prime minister John Major is proposing a United Nations Security Council summit so that the council's five permanent members can discuss the security implications of the break-up of the Soviet Union. The initiative, disclosed as Britain takes over the presidency of the Security Council, would allow council members to discuss the issue of control over the former Soviet Union's nuclear arsenal.
