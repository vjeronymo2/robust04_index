920214 FT 14 FEB 92 / International Capital Markets: Toronto exchange to automate MEMBERS of the Toronto Stock Exchange voted to end floor trading in equities in the first quarter of 1993 and to switch entirely to automated trading, Reuter reports from Toronto. Mr Pearce Bunting, the stock market's president and chief executive, said the exchange would redesign and fully test its equity trading network and substantially increase its capacity. There will continue to be registered traders but they will operate from members' offices. Floor trading will be retained for derivative instruments.
