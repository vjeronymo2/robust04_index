August 13, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

FOR SOME, 'PARENTHOOD' IS NO LAUGHING MATTER

Some comments on Nina J. Easton's July 30 article about the Ron Howard's film "Parenthood," which, according to the headline, "reflects the movies' latest discovery: kids and families."

Brian Grazer, Howard's partner in Imagine Films, is quoted as saying: "Now being hip is being a responsible dad. . . . It's hip to have substance."

Hip? He makes child rearing sound like the latest dance step.

'Hip to have substance" now? Does that mean that a short time ago it was "hip to be superficial"?

I hope that reading Grazer's remarks doesn't provoke a bunch of folks to rush out and become parents so they too can share in the "hip" experience. Just as bell-bottom pants and alligator-emblem shirts went speedily out, so might this latest craze.

And when a child no longer serves a desire for hipness, what do you say to little Mary or Johnny? "Sorry, kid, but you're out of style"?

LAUREL DEWEY

Glendale
