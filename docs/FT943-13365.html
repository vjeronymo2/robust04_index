940720 FT 20 JUL 94 / Code on property leases proposed The government has decided not to legislate against commercial-leasing practices after a one-year consultation that followed widespread criticism by tenants, Simon Davies writes. However it is urging the property industry to set up its own code of practice covering the controversial areas of upward-only rent reviews, confidentiality clauses and dispute resolution procedures. Mr Tony Baldry, environment minister, said: 'It is evident that the market is now providing a greater variety of lease terms'. The proposed code would encourage greater flexibility. He was replying to a written question from Mrs Angela Browning, Tory MP for Tiverton, on the conclusions of the consultation paper. Concerns had been expressed in parliament that the practice of upward-only rent reviews was a significant contributor to inflation. Mr Baldry said: 'Whilst the relative bargaining strengths of the parties will depend on the state of the market, it is not clear that occupiers fully appreciate the strength of their own bargaining positions.'
