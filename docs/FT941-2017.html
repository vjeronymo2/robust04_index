940323 FT 23 MAR 94 / Payouts challenge allowed The TUC and 10 unions yesterday won the right to bring a legal challenge to government plans to reform the criminal injuries compensation system. It intends to replace individual assessments with a fixed tariff from April 1. Mr Justice Owen granted leave in the High Court for the unions to bring judicial review proceedings and said the hearing should take place as soon as possible. Mr Guy Sankey, QC for Mr Michael Howard, home secretary, said the application was misconceived. But he did not oppose the application for leave as he accepted there was an arguable issue of public interest. He gave an assurance that no final award would be made to any claimant until the courts had decided the matter. Mr Andrew Dismore, solicitor for the TUC and the unions, said: 'The unions are very concerned about the effect on their members who face daily risks of being attacked in their jobs.'
