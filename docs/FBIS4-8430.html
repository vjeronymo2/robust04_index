BFN [Excerpts] Sarajevo, April 12 (AFP) -- Russian envoy Vitaly Churkin and Bosnian Serb leader Radovan Karadzic headed to the besieged Moslem enclave of Gorazde Tuesday [12 April] as Serb forces pursued their advance on the town in defiance of NATO warnings. [passage omitted] General Bertrand de Lapresle, commander of all UN forces in former Yugoslavia, said the air strikes should not be blown up in importance and should not "put in question the well advanced peace process." [passage omitted] Major Rob Annink, a spokesman for UNPROFOR in Sarajevo, said Serb forces had "closed up to the river Drina" all along the southwest of the Gorazde enclave on Monday and were trying to push southward from the north, coming close to Bosnian army positions.
