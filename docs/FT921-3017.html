920317 FT 17 MAR 92 / Arts: Today's Television In contrast to the usual tales of gloom and destruction shown in so many wildlife programmes, today's Survival promises a story of success and hope. Conservationists were worried about the development of Stansted airport but today, a year after the new airport opened, orchids thrive at the end of the runway, rabbits play, owls hunt for voles and mice and there are still kestrels in residence (7.30 ITV). Beginning in the desert cities of Samarkand and Bokhara on the Old Silk Road, Assignment reports from central Asia following the disintegration of the Soviet Union. The newly independent states of Uzbekistan and Tadjikistan are bankrupt and Moslem fundamentalism is resurgent. The programme asks whether any political party can control the social forces unleashed by the end of Soviet rule (7.45 BBC2). At 8.00, Channel 4 has a profile of former war photographer Don McCullin who now lives in Somerset and concentrates on landscape and still-life photography. ITV's downbeat series about real life detection, Murder Squad ends with the views of two men now serving life sentences as a result of investigations featured earlier in the series (9.00).
