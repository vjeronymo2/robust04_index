BFN [Text] Johannesburg April 29 SAPA -- Voting in KwaZulu, Gazankulu, Lebowa and Venda started off slowly on Friday [29 April] morning with queues shorter than the last two days, reports SABC [South African Broadcasting Corporation] radio news. Among them are people also queuing for temporary votercards. Voting has been extended until 7PM on Friday in the four regions, along with Ciskei. In Durban, Independent Electoral Commission liaison officer Mr Glen Mcdougall, said that no problems were expected at KwaZulu polling stations. He added that the number of voters in Natal-KwaZulu could be more than the 4.5 million estimated earlier.
