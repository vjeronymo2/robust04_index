Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 10 Mar--The Sejm, which is deliberating today, has rejected a motion by the KPN [Confederation for an Independent Poland] parliamentary group for adding to the agenda a report by the foreign minister on actions taken by the government to prevent any public insults of the Polish Republic and its bodies by foreigners. Justifying the motion, the KPN recalled that Vladimir Zhirinovskiy is to come to Poland soon and asked whether the government had taken any actions to stop the visit. The motion was supported by 118 deputies; 133 voted against and 69 abstained. Next, the Sejm approved a motion by Deputy Bogdan Pek to discuss the government's report on the privatization of Bank Slaski as the first item on the agenda.
