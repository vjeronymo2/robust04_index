940302 FT 02 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Three buys for IMC as profits fall IMC Industries, the USM-quoted soft drinks, video tapes and in-flight entertainments company, reported pre-tax profits down from Pounds 202,000 to Pounds 165,000 in the six months to October 31. The company also announced the purchase of Eurotrade (UK), Creation Entertainments and 50 per cent of Wideworld Trading for Pounds 143,000 cash and 25 per cent of the enlarged share capital of IMC Video. All are involved in marketing videos. IMC has the option to acquire the 25 per cent at a profit-related price not exceeding Pounds 450,000. Eurotrade and Creation have net assets of about Pounds 22.5m. Wideworld has only recently begun trading. All three companies are controlled by Mr Brian Phipps, a director of IMC. The company, of which the ultimate holding company is Cursitor (Fifty Two), said demand for its two main products, soft drinks and video tapes, had remained weak. Further rationalisation had taken place at Alpine Soft Drinks and new titles introduced by IMC Video. Turnover was Pounds 2.73m (Pounds 2.97m). Earnings per share came out at 0.07p (0.09p).
