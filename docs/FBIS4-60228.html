BFN [Excerpt] The Foreign Ministry issued a statement noting that yesterday at noon, it received a report from the Egyptian embassy in Khartoum saying that Sudanese security forces had expropriated three houses owned by Egyptian military attaches. One was occupied by the assistant air force attache, and the other two by embassy staff. According to the report, the Sudanese security forces seized the residence of the Egyptian chairman of the joint irrigation authority of the Nile River water, in addition to 30 other residences owned by the Egyptian Irrigation Authority that were occupied by large numbers of Egyptian irrigation mission personnel and their families. The Sudanese security forces prevented these employees and their families from entering their homes. At the same time, the Foreign Ministry statement adds, the Sudanese security forces expropriated the building of the Egyptian sports club in Khartoum. In view of these measures, the Foreign Ministry immediately summoned the Sudanese charge d'affairs in Cairo to strongly protest the unacceptable and unjustified conduct which primarily targets Egyptian individuals and families residing in Sudan.
