Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kingston, Jamaica, 27 Jan, CANA -- The Jamaica Government has tabled supplementary estimates in parliament seeking an additional three billion dollars to finance its 1993/94 budget. The revised estimates which are likely to be approved early next month will increase the budget for the financial year to 43.392 billion dollars (one Jamaican dollar just over three US cents) from the original 40.371 billion dollars. The additional money will go to recurrent or housekeeping expenses, which move up from 28.146 billion dollars to 31.758 billion dollars while the capital side of the budget has been slashed by 591 million dollars from 12.225 billion dollars to 11.634 billion dollars. The greater portion of the increase will cover public debt payments and public sector salaries.
