BFN [Text] Aden, Yemen, June 21 (AFP) -- Fighting between Yemen's rival armies left 16 southern soldiers dead and 71 wounded on Tuesday [21 June], while shelling of this port city killed three civilians and wounded 20, hospital officials said. More than 1,100 northern soldiers and officers were killed or wounded as they tried to advance toward the southern stronghold on Sunday and Monday, a military spokesman said in remarks published here. Heavy artillery battles continued to rage Tuesday on the front around Aden and the roar of multiple rocket launchers could be heard in the city. Northern forces launched offensives on three fronts around Aden late Monday -- at Abyan, 25 kilometres (16 miles) to the east, at Sabir, 20 kilometres (13 miles) and Bir Ahmad, 20 kilometres to the northeast. They also hit Aden with shells and rockets, killing three civilians and wounding 19 in the northern suburbs near the frontline, hospital officials said. Another person was wounded by a shell which landed in the city's central Crater district. Shelling of Aden has killed 82 people and wounded 268 since Thursday, according the latest hospital toll.
