Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] According to the vice president of the Chamber of Deputies, Deputy Yves Pericles Beauge, the Senate of the Republic is a serious obstacle to any resolution of the crisis. In an interview with Signal FM, Beauge acknowledged that the Senate will need to play a dominant role in achieving a way out of the crisis. He says a modus operandi must be developed to keep the nation from bearing the brunt of divergences among senators. [Begin recording, in Creole] If the situation in the Senate remains as it is -- already a great handicap -- it will continue to prevent a resolution because any and all decisions, even if President Aristide consents to the plan, require that the Senate take certain steps that fall within the scope of its responsibilities.
