February 9, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

CATALINA GOATS

The Catalina Conservancy should be commended for its recent efforts to reduce the feral goat population on Santa Catalina Island. Despite the fact that this program is long overdue, it is no less welcome.

After decades of overpopulation, feral mammals, including pigs, bison, deer, and the goats, have turned most of Santa Catalina into a lunar landscape. It is obvious to anyone flying over the Channel Islands that Santa Catalina is easily the most ecologically degraded of the entire group. The inevitable result of passive management of such mammal populations in the absence of natural predators is the extinction of the native species of plants and animals and, ultimately, the agonizing starvation of the feral mammals themselves.

I am hopeful that the Conservancy's recent goat removal program is the precursor of organized efforts to eliminate all exotic mammals from Santa Catalina. Perhaps it finally signals recognition by the Conservancy of its moral obligation to restore the ecological integrity of this special island.

LLOYD KIFF

Director, Western Foundation

of Vertebrate Zoology

Los Angeles
