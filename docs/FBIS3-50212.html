Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 20/1/1994 -- President Ion Iliescu stressed in an interview with the government daily VOCEA ROMANIEI the necessity of continuing the dialogue with the representatives of the political parties as well as of talks to be started among parties, between parties and trade unions, and between the government and the trade unions, so as to single out a platform of national interest, a program for Romania's development for the beginning of the 21st century. The president termed his meetings with political leaders as "a first touch (...) that will have to be furthered. Likewise, the direct inter-party dialogue should continue. Which is what I have suggested. And so said also the representatives of the Democratic Convention of Romania" (the group of opposition parties.) "To the extent to which a political pact or at least an understanding among parties will or will not come to the fore," President Iliescu went on, "it seems to me that the next step to be taken is to extend the dialogue at a parties--trade unions and government-trade unions level. I have been always ready for direct talks with the representatives of the big trade union federations, in order to secure a framework of partnership for the dialogue among these political forces, trade unions, government, administration, and employers." Of the major targets of the envisaged wide-scale dialogue, Ion Iliescu mentioned the establishment of the contact points for the establishment of a platform of national interest and the sketching of a perspective program, of a social-economic development strategy for Romania with the beginning of the 21st century in sight. "We have to look ahead, so as to open the prospect for Romania's integration in the processes characteristic of the world economic development," President Ion Iliescu stressed. "Or, here, I think, all forces, political included, should be interested. And all our valuable people -- and we do have such people -- should be attracted to this work of national interest."
