Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] President Alberto Fujimori is the favorite candidate for the 1995 elections according to a poll conducted by the Datum enterprise. The poll reveals that between Fujimori and Javier Perez de Cuellar, 35 percent prefer Fujimori and 30 percent Perez de Cuellar. The survey also reveals something new: If Fujimori competed against an unidentified candidate of the opposition, the latter would obtain 36 percent of the votes and Fujimori 39 percent. Even like this, Fujimori would still be 11 points below the figure required to, hypothetically, win the first round of elections and thus eliminate a second round. The results of the poll, which included a universe of 400 people of different social levels in Lima and Callao between 26 February and 1 March, were disclosed yesterday during the television program "Contrapunto."
