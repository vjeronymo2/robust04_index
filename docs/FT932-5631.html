930603 FT 03 JUN 93 / Absenteeism costs Ireland dear Absenteeism, mostly due to certified illness, is costing Irish business an estimated IPounds 600m (Pounds 580m) a year, with some 12m working days being lost in 1992, according to a report by the Irish Business and Employers' Federation, writes Tim Coone in Dublin. That was 65 times higher than losses through strikes. The report said 4.5 per cent of available working time was being lost in this way. This compares with 3.5 per cent in the UK.
