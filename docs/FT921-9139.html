920213 FT 13 FEB 92 / Truck and van sales fall 17% NEW COMMERCIAL vehicle sales fell 17.2 per cent to 16,677 last month, against 20,131 in January last year. According to figures released by the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders, the recession in the UK new-vehicle market shows no sign of easing, with commercial-vehicle registrations in January 50 per cent down on the same month three years ago. Sir Hal Miller, SMMT chief executive, said sales last month were the lowest January total since 1981 and reflected 'the continuing serious state of the commercial-vehicle industry'. Sales of light and medium trucks and medium vans were particularly hard hit. Registrations of new trucks (above 3.5 tonnes gross vehicle weight) have fallen by 63.9 per cent in the past three years from 6,442 in January 1989 to 2,325 last month. Rover again lost ground in the small-van market, while sales of Mercedes-Benz and Nissan medium vans fell sharply. Volkswagen improved in this sector, helped by the progressive launch last year of its Transporter range.
