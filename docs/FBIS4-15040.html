BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Vladimir Beskromnyy] [Text] Moscow, 5 Apr -- An outline agreement on Russia's accession to the Partnership for Peace program will possibly be signed on 21 April. This was announced today by Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin at a briefing at the Foreign Ministry's press center. He noted that the forthcoming visit to Brussels by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev would be crucial in determining Russia's stand on this issue; Kozyrev will not only sign the outline agreement, but also hand to NATO leaders a presentation document defining specific conditions on which Russia intends to cooperate within the framework of the Partnership for Peace program.
