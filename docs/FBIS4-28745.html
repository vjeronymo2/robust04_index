BFN [Text] 1. In response to an invitation from His Excellency [H.E.] Nouhak Phoumsavan, president of the Lao People's Democratic Republic [LPDR], H.E. General Than Shwe, chairman of the State Law and Order Restoration Council [SLORC] of the Union of Myanmar [Burma] paid an official friendship visit to the LPDR from 9 to 13 June 1994. 2. Accompanying H.E. Gen. Than Shwe, chairman of SLORC, are Mrs. Than Shwe; H.E. Lieutenant General Khin Nyunt, first secretary of the SLORC, and Mrs. Khin Nyunt; H.E. Lt. Gen. Myint Aung, minister of agriculture; H.E. Brigadier General D.O. Abel, minister of planning and economic development; H.E. U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs, and Mrs. Ohn Gyaw; and H.E. Brig. Gen. Myo Thant, minister of information; along with a number of high-ranking officials of the Myanmar Government. 3. H.E. Gen. Than Shwe, chairman of the SLORC of the Union of Myanmar, and his delegation were accorded a warm welcome, which reflected the long-standing close relations of friendship between the peoples of the two neighboring countries. 4. During the visit to the LPDR, H.E. Gen. Than Shwe, chairman of the SLORC of the Union of Myanmar, and H.E. Khamtai Siphandon, chairman of the Lao People's Revolutionary Party Central Committee, met and talked in an atmosphere of close friendship. 5. During his stay in the LPDR, H.E. Gen. Than Shwe, chairman of the SLORC of the Union of Myanmar, called on and held official talks with H.E. Nouhak Phoumsavan, president of the LPDR. The two leaders fruitfully discussed bilateral relations and international issues of common interest. The meeting and talks proceeded in an atmosphere of close friendship and mutual understanding. 6. Attending the meeting and talks on the Myanmar side were H.E. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, first secretary of the SLORC; H.E. Lt. Gen. Myint Aung, minister of agriculture; H.E. Brig. Gen. D.O. Abel, minister of planning and economic development; H.E. U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs; H.E. Brig. Gen. Myo Thant, minister of information; and H.E. U Maung Maung Lay, ambassador of the Union of Myanmar to the LPDR. Attending on the Lao side were H.E. Choummali Sai-gnason, minister of national defense; H.E. Somsavat Lengsavat, minister of foreign affairs; H.E. Sisavat Keobounphan, minister of agriculture and forestry; H.E. Thongdam Chanthaphon, head of the Presidential Office; H.E. Bounteng Vongsai, acting minister of information and culture; H.E. Somphong Mongkhonvilai, deputy chairman of the Planning and Cooperation Committee; H.E. Thongvan Phanlatsavong, deputy minister of trade; and H.E. Kideng Thammavong, ambassador of the LPDR to the Union of Myanmar. 7. The Myanmar leader informed the Lao leader of the extensive changes in the constitutional, political, economic, and social fields in the country and efforts to achieve national concord. The Myanmar side reiterated its positive implementation of the foreign policy of independence on the basis of the five principles of peaceful coexistence. It also reiterated its active contribution to the nonaligned movement. 8. The Lao leader informed the Myanmar leader of the success in implementing the comprehensive and principled restructuring policy in the LPDR in recent years and the socioeconomic development plan from now to the year 2000. The Lao leader also reiterated the consistent implementation of the foreign policy of peace, independence, friendship, and cooperation with all countries, first and foremost with neighboring countries. 9. H.E. Gen. Than Shwe and his delegation visited a number of economic establishments, historic and cultural places, and temples in Vientiane capital, Vientiane Province, and Luang Prabang Province. 10. The two sides highly assessed the success of the visit and considered it as a firm basis for the enhancement of the relations of neighborly friendship and cooperation in the future and as a contribution to the promotion and establishment of peace, stability, and cooperation for development in the region. 11. H.E. Gen. Than Shwe, chairman of the SLORC of the Union of Myanmar, expressed sincere thanks to H.E. President Nouhak Phoumsavan, the government, and people of the LPDR for the warm welcome accorded to him and his delegation throughout the official visit on this occasion. 12. During the visit, consultative meetings were respectively held between H.E. Saman Vi-gnaket, chairman of the National Assembly of the LPDR, and H.E. Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, first secretary of the SLORC of the Union of Myanmar; between H.E. Khamphoui Keoboualapha, deputy prime minister and chairman of the Planning and Cooperation Committee of the LPDR, and H.E. Birg. Gen. D.O. Abel, minister of planning and economic development of the Union of Myanmar; between H.E. Somsavat Lengsavat, minister of foreign affairs of the LPDR, and H.E. U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs of the Union of Myanmar; between H.E. Sisavat Keobounphan, minister of agriculture and forestry of the LPDR, and H.E. Lt. Gen. Myint Aung, minister of agriculture of the Union of Myanmar; and between H.E. Bounteng Vongsai, acting minister of information and culture of the LPDR, and H.E. Brig. Gen. Myo Thant, minister of information of the Union of Myanmar. 13. During the visit, the two sides jointly signed the following agreements: the agreement between government of the LPDR and government of the Union of Myanmar on the permanent international boundary between the two countries along the Mekong River, the agreement on the establishment of a joint commission for bilateral cooperation between government of the LPDR and government of the Union of Myanmar, and the agreement on tourism cooperation between government of the LPDR and government of the Union of Myanmar. 14. H.E. Gen Than Shwe, chairman of the SLORC of the Union of Myanmanr, has invited H.E. Nouhak Phoumsavan, president of the LPDR, and wife to pay an official visit to the Union of Myanmar at an appropriate time for both sides. H.E. President Nouhak Phoumsavan has accepted the invitation with pleasure. [Dated] Vientiane, 13 June 1994
