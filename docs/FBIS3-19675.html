Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report signed "wip": "Austrian Rescheduling Agreement With Iran Signed"] [Excerpt] Vienna -- Iran has now successfully concluded its negotiations with Austria on rescheduling credits with Austrian state guarantees. The new agreement will settle the repayment of short-term loans of 5.6 billion schillings, including the interest accumulated. Austria has granted Iran a suspension of payments for outstanding capital until 1 July 1996. At that point, Iran will pay back its debts in 16 quarterly installments. As far as the accumulated debt is concerned, repayment is to be in 16 monthly installments to start on 30 June 1994. On the basis of the agreement on Iran's debts, Austrian exporters can now hope for new Control Bank guarantees. [passage omitted]
