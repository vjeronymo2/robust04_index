BFN [Interview with Yi To-sop, North Korean ambassador to Thailand, by NHK on 15 June; place not given -- recorded; from the "NHK News 6" program] [Text] In an interview with NHK today, Yi To-sop, North Korean ambassador to Thailand, said a visit by former President Carter to North Korea would provide a good opportunity for North Korea to manifest its enthusiasm for a settlement of the nuclear issue. [Begin Yi recording in English] I think that his visit will provide a good opportunity to manifest our willingness to solve this nuclear issue or break the nuclear impasse with the United States through dialogue, peacefully. [end recording] During the interview, the North Korean ambassador stated whether North Korea will withdraw from the NPT, the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty, or not hinges on how the United States will deal with the nuclear issue and stressed anew that only dialogue between North Korea and the United States could lead to a settlement of the nuclear issue. Commenting on IAEA inspectors who are still staying in North Korea after North Korea announced its withdrawal from the IAEA, the North Korean ambassador said North Korea would not forcibly expel them.
