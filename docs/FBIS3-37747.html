Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In an interview with Interfax the Deputy Commander of Nagorno-Karabakh's defence forces, General Khristofor Ivanian, described the attempt to drive a wedge into Karabakh defences in Kelbajar District as "a gross blunder on the part of the Azeri command." According to him, the Karabakh units holding most of the heights there did not find it hard 5 days ago to surround and neutralize a large Azeri army group. "The adventure in Kelbajar, which pursued largely political goals, turned for Azerbaijan into pointless losses; over 500 troops got frozen to death high in the mountains," Ivanian said. In the meantime the Karabakh army's arsenal was supplemented with trophies: 12 armored vehicles, including five tanks, two 152-mm self-propelled artillery installations, a jet rocket system, heavy guns and other military equipment. According to the general, about 20 Azeri servicemen surrendered. The Karabakh side lost 3 men; 15 troops were wounded. The general believes that, having failed to score genuine success in its two-months-long offensive, Azerbaijan ought to get convinced once again that "to use the language of force toward the people of Karabakh" is useless.
