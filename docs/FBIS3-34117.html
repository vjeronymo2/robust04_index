Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 3 Feb (MENA)--The Working Group on Arms Control and Regional Security emanating from the multilateral Middle East peace talks concluded its meetings in Cairo this evening. During its five days of meetings, the committee discussed the two issues of arms control and regional security arrangements. It also discussed working papers and ideas submitted by Egypt and Jordan and a joint Russian-American working paper, a compilation of what was discussed during the committee's previous meetings. The group also discussed a paper submitted by the International Atomic Energy Agency on measures to inspect nuclear weapons. During the meetings, members also formulated a joint paper to be presented to Mideast capitals to form an official opinion and prepare for the paper's presentation during the meetings of the General Committee slated to start in Doha at the end of April. MENA has learned that the coming meeting of the second working group will be held in Turkey during March to prepare for the meetings of the General Committee. Some 19 countries, including the United States and Russia, will participate in the meetings of the Arms Control Committee.
