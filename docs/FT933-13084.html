930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / Arts: Today's Television Having suffered periodic back pain for 20 years I shall watch Doc Martin's Casebook (7.30 BBC1) with fascination and scepticism. Doctors stand in relation to back pain as clergymen do to god: it is all faith and mumbo jumbo. They know nothing for sure, work entirely in the dark, and success (rare) is a matter of pure chance. If we cannot have another British police series as good as Between The Lines, and the Americans are producing nothing to compare with Kojak or Hill Street Blues, I suppose third best - an Australian series - will just have to do. Presumably the broadcasters feel that in Police Rescue they do at least speak English, or a close approximation. In today's episode there is derring-do on a skyscraper and a crane. The last of Channel 4's tribute to the late Juris Podnieks, Camera Of Courage, deals with events in Latvia and Armenia just prior to the collapse of the Soviet Union (9.00). BBC2's season of repeats of The Wednesday Play from the 1960s offers an early Dennis Potter: 'Where The Buffalo Roam', starring Hywel Bennett as a retarded Welsh teenager (9.00).
