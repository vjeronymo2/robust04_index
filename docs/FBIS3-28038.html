Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, February 3 (KCNA) -- Keiko Tsuwa, chairperson of the Japan Women's Council, in a letter of request to the prime minister on January 25, denounced it is an anachronistic act that the Japanese authorities charged a trade company with "suspected violation of Cocom [Coordinating Committee for Multilateral Export Controls]" and linked it with the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. She expressed concern over the anti-DPRK, anti-Chongnyon false propaganda of the Japanese Government and press and demanded that the government make apology and compensation for colonial domination and strive to improve Japan-Korea relations.
