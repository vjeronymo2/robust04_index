BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Zakharchenko] [Text] Seoul May 20 -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin on Friday sent a personal message to President Kim Yong-sam of the Republic of Korea about a postponement of the first session of the Russian-South Korean Intergovernmental Commission for Economic Cooperation, according to diplomatic sources in Seoul. The sources said the letter explains that the commission's session, which was previously scheduled to be held on Seoul on May 20, has been postponed because Aleksandr Shokhin, vice-premier and minister of economics, cannot arrive in South Korea "due to purely domestic problems". Yeltsin suggested that the commission hold a session in Moscow before Kim's visit to Russia. The visit is scheduled for June 1-4.
