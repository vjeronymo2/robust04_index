BFN [Text] On 3 May, during an unusual news conference that linked him with reporters in several parts of the world, U.S. President Bill Clinton upheld his position of not varying on the U.S. policy toward Cuba. In response to a question by a Cuban television journalist, Clinton reiterated his support of the Torricelli Law and stressed that he does not expect any change in that policy for now. President Clinton, who has been labeled as indecisive, at another point in the news conference, acknowledged that the United States is incapable of solving the problems of the world and [words indistinct] only when U.S. vital interests are endangered. Regarding Haiti, Clinton warned that he has not dismissed the use of force and insisted that the DPRK must allow full inspection of its nuclear program. However, he was less aggressive regarding Bosnia when he affirmed that Washington's goal continues to be a negotiated peace.
