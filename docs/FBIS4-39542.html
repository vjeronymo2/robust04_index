BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Tigran Lyloyan] [Text] Yerevan June 8 -- Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev arrived today in the Armenian capital. "It is a scheduled visit connected with a summer training season that has started in Russian troops deployed in Armenia," the minister told ITAR-TASS Upon his arrival. According to Grachev, during his visit the question of Russia's military bases on the Armenian territory should be settled together with that of the deployment of Russian peace-keepers in the Karabakh conflict area. Grachev told TASS that, although his visit to Azerbaijan was not scheduled, he will go there at the request of Azeri President Gaydar Aliyev. Russian President Boris Yeltsin has okayed his several hour stopover in Baku, the minister added. Grachev said that he will insist that the agreements drafted by defense chiefs of Russia, Armenia, Azerbaijan and Nagorno-Karabakh "should be signed by all means." "No one will decide for us," the minister stressed.
