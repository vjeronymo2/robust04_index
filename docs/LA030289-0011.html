March 2, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

L.A. TEACHER SALARY DISPUTE

In response to Frank del Olmo's column "Who Gains if UTLA Alienates the Parents?" (Op-Ed Page, Feb. 15), I say, what a shallow analysis, premised on a false assumption!

The current labor dispute between United Teachers-Los Angeles and the Los Angeles Unified School District isn't interfering with the education inner-city parents want their children to get. Education is proceeding as if the teachers had a contract.

So far only the families of teachers observing the boycott of certain "non-academic" duties have suffered financially, because of docked paychecks.

I can't see how teachers can be painted as selfish and shortsighted when we put our livelihood on the line every year to reach a fair settlement -- not just for ourselves, but also for the students.

The public should keep in mind that poor working conditions for teachers mean poor learning conditions for students, and that low pay equals low morale and mediocre performance.

The battle for good education is led by the teachers. We pay the price every year through stressful and bitter negotiations, only to have oversimplistic analyses like Del Olmo's paint us as cynics.

When teachers across this country are paid their comparable worth, boycotting, slow-downs and strikes will be a thing of the past.

If we treasure our kids, we should pay teachers commensurate to the social value they represent. Short of that, I'll stand by the union.

STERLING DELONE

Los Angeles
