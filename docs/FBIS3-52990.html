Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali met today with Prime Minister Hamed Karoui and Abdallah Kallel, minister of state and interior minister, at Carthage Palace. During the meeting the head of state signed three decrees relating to the upcoming presidential and parliamentary elections. The first decree invites voters to elect the president of the republic and parliamentary deputies on 20 March 1994. The decree also sets the deadline for candidacies, the last day of the election campaign, and the amount of allowances offered to candidates for their election campaign. This is in accordance with the terms of the electoral code. The second decree fixes the total number of parliamentary seats, the constituencies and the number of seats for each of these constituencies. The third decree deals with the polling stations to be set up abroad for the presidential elections and also with the conditions surrounding the holding of these elections. The head of state also recommended that practical measures be taken immediately to ensure that the upcoming elections are held in the best possible conditions.
