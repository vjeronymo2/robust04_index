BFN [Text] According to a poll, Armenia's ex-Foreign Minister Raffi Ovannisyan is the most popular politician in that republic in April. He was favoured by 73.2 percent of the 1200 Armenians, polled by the Center for Sociological Studies at the Republican Academy of Sciences. President Levon Ter-Petrosyan proved popular with only 22.9 percent of the interviewed, 57.1 percent voiced dislike for him. Nor does the Prime Minister Hrant Bagratyan enjoy popularity. He was spoken of favorably only 11.6 percent of the respondents, 73.2 percent said he was no good. The most popular party, according to the poll, is Dashnaktsutyun, an opposition revolutionary federation. Going next is the Armenian Communist Party. The ruling Armenian National Movement comes only third in the list. Of foreign politicians, the U.S. President Bill Clinton and Russia's President Boris Yeltsin enjoy the greatest popularity in Armenia.
