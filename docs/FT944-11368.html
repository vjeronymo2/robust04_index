941107 FT 07 NOV 94 / Fears raised on school pay levels Local management of schools has led to the appointment of less experienced teachers, the Audit Commission says in a report published today. The commission examined appointments in 74 schools and found that the average salary scale point of newly appointed teachers had dropped in each of the past three years from slightly over scale 7 to just over scale 5. Mr John Vaughan-Jones, the commission's assistant director of local government studies, said head teachers and governors tended to argue that they were appointing younger staff to introduce more up-to-date teaching methods. If that were the case the trend might now have levelled off. Instead, it was continuing.
