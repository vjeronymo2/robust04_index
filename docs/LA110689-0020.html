November 6, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

EXERCISE THE MIND

Question: People who have exercised all of their lives and who continue to exercise into their 60s and later are in much better physical condition than the kinds of people who fall into the couch-potato category. This is so obvious that no one should be surprised by it. But what about the mind? Does using the brain help a person stay sharp as he gets older? If so, what kind of mental exercise would be helpful?

Answer: The admonition "use it or lose it" applies to the mind as well as to the body. A person who wants to remain mentally sharp requires mental stimulation, and if the person no longer works, the stimulation, obviously, must come from somewhere other than a job.

Finding sources of mental stimulation is not a difficult task. Libraries contain interesting and challenging material, and so do newspapers and periodicals. An individual seeking food for the mind does not have to look very far.

Being with people can also be mentally stimulating. Many communities offer lectures and other programs that meet both a social and intellectual need. The theater, concerts, even athletic events serve the same purpose. And just because you may never have been interested in such programs is no reason not to participate in them now. It's never too late to develop new interests.
