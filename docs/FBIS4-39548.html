BFN [Text] Tbilisi, June 8 (SNARK)--The peace-keeping forces' deployment in the zone of the Georgia-Abkhazian conflict will be not only a guarantee of stability but also will promote building of the Georgian Army. Vardiko Nadibaidze, Georgian Defence Minister, declared of it at a press-conference in Tbilisi. He also reported that the peace-keeping forces will be composed of 3 battalions from the Urals, St. Petersburg and Siberian military districts. The united headquarters of the peace-keeping forces will be placed in Sukhumi and the local ones in Gali and Zugdidi. The security zone, where the refuges' return is planned, will stretch till the town of Ochamchira. Mentioning the special role of Russia in the cause of Georgian Army building, the Minister said the later should be united, scanty-10-15 thousand people and mobile". Nadibaidze also said he hoped Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev would arrive in Georgia after finishing his visit to Armenia.
