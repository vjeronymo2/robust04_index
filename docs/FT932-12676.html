930427 FT 27 APR 93 / International Company News: Steel recovery lifts USX for first period USX Corporation, the US steel and energy group, yesterday reported net income of Dollars 50m on sales of Dollars 4.28bn for the first three months of 1993, compared with profits of Dollars 4m on sales of Dollars 4.11bn a year earlier, excluding the effect of accounting changes. Operating income rose to Dollars 163m in the latest quarter from Dollars 137m. USX-US Steel, the Pittsburgh-based steel company, had net income of Dollars 13m, or 18 cents a share, in the 1993 quarter against an underlying net loss of Dollars 20m, or 41 cents, a year earlier, excluding the effect of accounting changes. Sales were flat at Dollars 1.2bn. The group had operating income of Dollars 40m in the 1993 quarter against restated operating income of Dollars 13m the previous year. In the latest quarter, operating income benefited from a Dollars 28m gain from the use of funds from a previously established insurance reserve to pay for some employee insurance benefits. Looking to the second quarter, the company expects operating income to include a gain of Dollars 8m as these reserves are depleted. In the latest quarter, the company had a pre-tax gain of Dollars 38m from the disposal of assets. USX-Marathon Group, the oil business, posted first-quarter net income of Dollars 31m, or 10 cents, on sales of Dollars 3bn against underlying income of Dollars 24m, or 8 cents on sales of Dollars 2.9bn a year ago, excluding accounting changes in 1992. Operating income for the group fell to Dollars 106m from a restated Dollars 124m last year.
