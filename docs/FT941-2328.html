940322 FT 22 MAR 94 / Brazil politician quits after corruption probe One of the most senior politicians named in an inquiry into corruption in Brazil's Congress yesterday announced his resignation as a congressman. Mr Genebaldo Correia, former leader of the Party of the Brazilian Democratic Movement (PMDB), the country's largest, was one of 18 politicians named in the investigation's final report, released in January. It alleged a widespread network of corruption by a government budget committee, which involved politicians earning fees in return for approving building contracts. Further investigations are under way and Congress is expected to vote whether to expel the 18 in the next few weeks.
