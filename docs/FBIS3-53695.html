Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Dr. Husayn Sulayman Abu-Salih, the foreign minister, has affirmed the importance of the visit of the Chinese delegation which visited the country recently led by the deputy chairman of the State Council and foreign minister. He noted that during the visit ways of strengthening bilateral relations and fields of bilateral and regional cooperation were discussed. Dr. Abu-Salih explained, at the Foreign Ministry forum today, that China pursues a policy of economic openness and it can find many opportunities for investment in Sudan in various fields. He explained that it contributes to many important projects in the country, like the Hamdab Dam project and the elevation of Al-Rusayris Dam and energy and irrigation projects. Dr. Abu-Salih touched on the visit of the Iranian delegation to the country, representing the Ministry of Construction Jihad. He pointed out the firm cooperation between Sudan and Iran. Ambassador (Mityang Malwal Mabor), director of the Peace Department at the Foreign Ministry and member of the government's delegation to the negotiations of the Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development [IGADD] states, which were recently held in Nairobi, also spoke at the information forum of the Foreign Ministry. He said that ministers of the four IGADD states, who had been entrusted with establishing peace in Sudan, had submitted their report in this respect to Kenyan President Daniel arap Moi. He explained that the Kenyan president, chairman of the IGADD committee, will hold consultations with the presidents of Uganda, Ethiopia, Eritrea and Lieutenant General 'Umar al-Bashir, president of the Republic, to discuss ways of establishing peace in the south of the country.
