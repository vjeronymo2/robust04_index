930128 FT 28 JAN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Stronger councils watchdog is urged THERE were bitter clashes in the Commons yesterday over allegations of corruption in Lambeth borough council, Alison Smith writes. Some agreement between Labour and Tory MPs that there might be a need for stronger powers for district auditors and for the Audit Commission, the independent local government watchdog, was lost in angry exchanges, as Mr Jack Straw, the shadow environment secretary, faced a concerted attack from Tories claiming Lambeth exemplified Labour in local government. While Mr Michael Howard, the environment secretary, told MPs the council was being asked about 'breaches of competition legislation', Mr Straw urged him to 'condemn the freeloaders, former members of your cabinet, who have had their hands in the till and their snouts in the trough of privatised industries'. Mr Howard told Mr Straw his remarks were 'quite appalling'. Earlier, Labour had published a dossier of 'mismanagement and fraud' in central government and Tory local councils.
