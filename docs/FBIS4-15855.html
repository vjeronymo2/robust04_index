BFN ["Urgent" -- by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Gondusov] [Text] Moscow, 11 Apr -- Speaking over the phone to U.S. Defense Secretary William Perry, Russian Defense Minister Pavel Grachev expressed his serious concern at the NATO air strike against Serb positions near the Bosnian town of Gorazde. The hot-line conversation took place at 1530, at the request of the U.S. side. Yelena Agapova, aide to the defense minister, told ITAR-TASS that [words indistinct] the use of military force, especially air force, will not yield positive results in resolving the conflict in this region. The Russian minister spoke in favor of bringing to the conflict zone additional ground troops for peacekeeping. Pavel Grachev noted at the same time that the conflicting sides should retreat to their previous positions, so that a demilitarized zone could be created.
