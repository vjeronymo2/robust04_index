Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Karlsruhe (DDP/ADN) -- The former Berlin SPD [Social Democratic Party of Germany] deputy Bodo Thomas and his wife, Helga, who has been an SPD member at Berlin's house of deputies since 1989, have been charged with spying for the former GDR. The federal prosecutor's office said in Karlsruhe today that the 62-year-old Bodo Thomas, who was a member of the house of deputies between 1971 and 1989, supplied the Ministry for State Security (MfS) with information from the Berlin parliament and SPD sources. Thomas was an SPD specialist in matters concerning Germany and Berlin. His wife, whose immunity as a deputy was lifted last year, is alleged to have helped Thomas and covered for him. Both accused, who deny the allegations against them, are at liberty, the office said.
