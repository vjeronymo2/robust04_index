940208 FT 08 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Consortium buys Delco Chassis arm from GM A consortium of European investors led by Mr Dennis Keech, the founder of Camford Engineering, has taken over the automotive electrical products operations of the Delco Chassis division of General Motors in the UK. The operation, based in Dunstable, Bedfordshire, has a workforce of 850 and an annual turnover of around Pounds 53m. The main products of the new company, to be called Automotive Components Dunstable, are windscreen wiper motor assemblies. Its main customer is GM Europe (Opel/Vauxhall), which accounts for around 56 per cent of its turnover, but it also supplies Rover, Toyota, Nissan and Honda car plants in the UK as well as several truck producers in Europe. GM has disposed of the operations as part of the restructuring of its Automotive Components Group Worldwide. Delco Chassis is concentrating its activities on suspension and brake systems. Mr Keech founded the Camford Engineering automotive components group in 1964.
