930729 FT 29 JUL 93 / Arts: Today's Television ITV's imported National Geographic series showing macho feats of endurance, Voyager, today tells the story of a journey by eight young men in an open boat, a replica of a 12th century Celtic galley. The plan was to retrace a journey made 700 years previously, from the west coast of Scotland to the Faroes (7.30). Is BS 5750 a means of turning British industry into a world beater, or a bit of bureaucratic nonsense? In Business Matters Nick Clarke looks at the effects of the international standard for quality introduced by the British Standards Institute (8.00 BBC2). There have been few original British programmes among the welter of repeats this summer, and most have been mediocre, so you wonder whether inclusion in the summer schedule is an admission of low quality. However, Thicker Than Water, a mini-series on BBC1, has been adapted by Trevor Preston and stars Theresa Russell and Jonathan Pryce, so it should be worth a look. BBC1 covers the declaration at Christchurch in By-election Special (12.15).
