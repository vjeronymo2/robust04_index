Language: Lithuanian Article Type:BFN [Text] President Algirdas Brazauskas and diplomats from the states of the European Union accredited in Vilnius held an informal discussion concerning Lithuania and the European Union [words indistinct]. During a working lunch at the Draugyste Hotel, preparation of the draft treaty on free trade between Lithuania and the European Union and the possibility of accelerating the signing of the treaty on Lithuania's associate membership -- the European Treaty -- was discussed. The president again stressed Lithuania's aspiration to integrate into the European security, economic and political structures. Petras Vaitiekunas, the president's adviser on foreign policy, who was present at the meeting, said that the ambassadors noted that Lithuania is in possession of the key out of Europe. According to the adviser the diplomats from the European states stressed that a civilized regulation of relations between Lithuania and Poland will be regarded by Europe very positively, and that Europe would welcome the signing of the interstate treaty.
