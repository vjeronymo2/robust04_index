940122 FT 22 JAN 94 / Finance and the Family: Directors' transactions The market soars to unexploded heights and we wait to see what directors will do. Some do nothing much, some sell a little to take profits, others continue to deal for personal financial planning reasons. Nobo Group produces office equipment. In the second week of January, the company announced interim results strongly ahead of the same period last year. The share price has moved up on the back of it so the sale of 200,000 shares each by Peter Kent, the non-executive chairman, and Reginald Barr, the chief executive, is not surprising. Each director retains considerable quantities of stock. Sales should aid market liquidity. Thomas French & Sons, the home decoration company, appears in both the sale and buy columns. Final results were announced at the beginning of January and M Snapes was appointed as a new chief executive. He then bought 100,000 shares. The chairman and erstwhile managing director, Thomas French, sold 100,000 shares to Snapes and sold the other 250,000 shares for a beneficial trust. A Beckman Plc is involved in both textile converting and merchanting and property investment.
