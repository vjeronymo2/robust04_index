May 18, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Air Route Talks Fail: The bid to expand air links between the United States and Japan failed after Tokyo halved a proposal that Washington had found inadequate five months ago, a U.S. negotiator said. In December, Washington proposed 16 new services for Japanese carriers and 13 new services for U.S. carriers, while Japan countered with 10 new lines for Japanese carriers and six for U.S. carriers. The United States had hoped the two countries would reach a compromise, the official said. But the new Japanese proposal was for five new Japanese services and three new U.S. services.
