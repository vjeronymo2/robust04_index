BFN [Text] Members of the Russian Federation Council decided to appeal to the Constitutional Court with a request to examine the anti-crime decree issued by President Boris Yeltsin. A total of 64 deputies (36 percent of the total number) voted for this decision. In accordance with provision 125 of the Constitution, at least one fifth of the Federation Council deputies must back an appeal to the Constitutional Court. The appeal was initiated by deputies Aleksandr Belyayev, Yuriy Boldyrev and Aleksey Manannikov. Belyayev said in an interview with INTERFAX that the Constitutional Court could consider the appeal this autumn after the State Duma adopted the law on Constitutional Court at second reading.
