931204 FT 04 DEC 93 / Turkish soft drinks company bids for Meyna A soft-drinks company based in Izmir, Turkey, which holds Coca Cola's local bottling franchise, is bidding to buy Meyna, the fruit and packaging business once thought to be the main contributor to the Pounds 107m reported profit of Polly Peck International's Middle East businesses, John Murray Brown writes. Mr Cemal Ozgorkey, head of the privately owned Ozgorkey Group, confirmed that he was in talks with the administrators to PPI. Meyna made net losses in 1990, 1991 and the first nine months of last year, according to internal management figures in a sales prospectus sent by the administrators to potential buyers.
