November 27, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

BISHOP BARS RITE FOR KILLEA

I am appalled at the decision of Maher barring Killea from receiving her church's sacrament. As a former Catholic, Maher validates my decision to leave the church.

The Catholic Church has a right to oppose abortion, however, this country has a deep-seated belief in separation of church and state. Trying to coerce an elected official by means of religious blackmail is inexcusable. Does Bishop Maher not believe that Killea first and foremost is answerable to the constituents who elected her? After all, if we do not like the way she does her job, we will not reelect her. What if 75% of the voters agree with her. Does she ignore them because of the church's beliefs?

Religious intimidation has no place in the government of a free people. Decisions of old men who are so far outside the mainstream of lay life styles do not reflect the complex problems in this century. What they really want is long gone -- women in the kitchen and pregnant.

I would rank Maher's actions with the church's attitude on condoms to combat AIDs. Their apparent lack of care for the individual is abhorrent to any thinking person.

JEANNE RAY

Fallbrook
