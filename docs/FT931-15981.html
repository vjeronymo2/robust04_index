930109 FT 09 JAN 93 / World Commodities Prices: Sugar German statistician FO Licht has cuts its estimate for world sugar production in 1992-93 to 114.88m tonnes from a previous forecast of 116.42m tonnes, reports Reuter. Output in 1991-92 was revised up to 116.17m tonnes. All estimates are based on national crop years. The statistician reduced forecasts for India and China, which it described as 'two countries whose stocks figure importantly in the total supply overhanging the market.' But production in both would drop in 1992-93, Licht said, 'which will presumably be reflected in the tonnages each brings to the market.'
