Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 24 KYODO -- Six cabinet ministers shared the view Monday [24 January] that political reform is a key vehicle to jacking up the economy, a cabinet spokesman said Monday. "They agree that a major premise for making decisions on important economy-related policies hinges on achieving political reform," Deputy Chief Cabinet Secretary Nobuo Ishihara told a news conference. "They confirmed that seeing through the political reform package is one of the best economy-spurring measures," he said. The six cabinet ministers made their views known at a meeting held at Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa's official residence. They are all government members of an economic panel that advises the seven parties in the ruling coalition. The six are Foreign Minister Tsutomu Hata, Chief Cabinet Secretary Masayoshi Takemura, Finance Minister Hirohisa Fujii, International Trade and Industry Minister Hiroshi Kumagai, Economic Planning Agency Director General Manae Kubota and Home Affairs Minister Kanju Sato. During the meeting, the ministers agreed that the next 15 months are vital to getting the economy back on its feet in 1994 and ensuring stable growth in 1995 and beyond, Ishihara said. The six did not discuss when additional pump-priming measures will be worked out and the national budget for fiscal 1994, starting April 1, will be formulated, he said.
