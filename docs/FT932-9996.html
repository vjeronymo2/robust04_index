930511 FT 11 MAY 93 / International Company News: Apple uses chip developed with IBM, Motorola APPLE Computer is to use a microprocessor chip, co-developed with International Business Machines and Motorola, in a new generation of Macintosh personal computers. Apple plans to incorporate the powerful Risc-based microprocessor into its line of PCs beginning in the first half of 1994. At a software developers' conference yesterday, Apple demonstrated a prototype high-performance Macintosh computer incorporating the new microprocessor, called PowerPC, running standard Macintosh applications software without modification. Apple announced the support of 11 software developers which are writing new versions of their applications for the PowerPC Macintosh. Apple formed an alliance with IBM and Motorola to develop the PowerPC chip in October 1991.
