Language: Hungarian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The institutionalized protection of minority rights was discussed at the Budapest consultation of youth organizations of the European Democratic Union. [passage omitted] Attila Kert reports: [Kert] Foreign Minister Geza Jeszenszky has spoken, among other things, about collective rights. Referring to his recent visit to Belgrade, he spoke with cautious optimism about the cultural autonomy efforts of the Hungarians in Vojvodina [Vajdasag]. He said that negotiations with President Milosevic can give rise to some optimism. At the same time, the foreign minister said it was regrettable that the draft law on ensuring parliamentary seats for the representatives of the ethnic minorities in Hungary was not adopted in the Hungarian National Assembly by the required two-thirds majority. He stressed, however, that even now the Hungarian National Assembly had currently as many as 30 members from various nationalities. [passage omitted]
