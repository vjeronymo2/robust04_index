930814 FT 14 AUG 93 / Briefcase Q&A: Afloat in an old pool I have a holding of shares in Redland plc, all of which are in a pre-April 1982 pool. I have now received the additional shares in respect of the enhanced scrip dividend and I am considering selling them. When these new shares are sold, do they constitute a new pool or are they to be added to the base cost of the pre-April 1982 pool and the CGT calculated accordingly? Like any other bonus (or rights) issue, scrip issue in lieu of a dividend falls into the same pool as the holding which produced it. In your case, therefore, the scrip falls into the old pool of Redland shares acquired before April 6 1982. The free Inland Revenue pamphlet CGT13 should clarify the rules for you.
