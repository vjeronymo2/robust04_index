BFN [By reporter Wang Hui (3769 1920)] [Text] Macao, 7 Jun (ZHONGGUO XINWEN SHE)--Chen Ziying, deputy director of the State Council's Hong Kong and Macao Affairs Office, said here today that the British side is not cooperating in the political area but China will still cooperate as much as possible with the British side in other areas. The matter hinges on the British side. Concerning the talks on Hong Kong's new airport, he hopes that progress can be made and an important breakthrough achieved. Chen Ziying said that regarding the Hong Kong question, China has always advocated Sino-British cooperation because it is conducive to Hong Kong's smooth transition. Although the British side is not cooperating in the political area he hopes that, in other areas, it will not repeat the methods used in the political area. In other areas, we will try our best to cooperate with the British side. China's attitude has always been clear and we have made efforts in this regard and the matter rests with the other side. Talking about the issue of Hong Kong's new airport, Chen Ziying said that along with Hong Kong's economic development, a new airport should be built and everyone had identical views on this. He said: Concerning the issue of Hong Kong's new airport, we think that things should be done according to the regulations stipulated by the original Memorandum of Understanding on the Airport and no extra financial burdens should be created for the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region [SAR]. In order to protect the interests of Hong Kong residents, and in order not to create extra financial burdens for the Hong Kong SAR, we have negotiated with the British side. Concerning this question, the Chinese side has never raised any extra demands; we have only one thing to say: Act according to the Memorandum of Understanding on the Airport. Chen Ziying said that China is positive and enthusiastic about the airport talks and hopes that the talks on the new airport make progress and an important breakthrough is achieved as soon as possible.
