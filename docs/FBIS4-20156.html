BFN [Report by Guido Dell'Anquila] [Excerpts] It seemed as though it had all been sorted out: President Scalfaro was going to be a super-guarantor and was ready to appoint Berlusconi premier. Then problems started and Berlusconi has still not been asked to form a government. [passage omitted] [Dell'Aquila] It is not Scalfaro who is hesitating--Berlusconi has asked for more time. He wants all the pieces of the jigsaw in place before he takes up the mantle, in order to avoid surprises. There are at least two problems which still have to be solved. On the one hand there is the problem of guarantees--Berlusconi's dual role as premier and businessman. Yesterday, Scalfaro came forward to play the role of super-guarantor, but today the Northern League has applied the brakes. The League's Francesco Speroni has said: Berlusconi will be watched by us and by parliament. The other problem still waiting to be solved concerns the much criticized plan to divide the functions of the Interior Ministry. However, the Northern League is sticking to its guns. [Passage omitted]
