BFN [From the "Evening Information Review" newscast] [Text] Cuban authorities have taken measures against 10 persons in Pinar del Rio and Sancti Spiriti, by virtue of the application of decree-law No. 149 that establishes the confiscation of property and assets of all those who have illegally acquired wealth. An official of the Attorney General's Office explained that [words indistinct] and that such measures do not imply anyone's arrest. The same source added that decree-law No. 149 is established in chapters 60 and 61 of the Cuban Constitution. As of this moment, those persons accused of illegally gaining wealth have 10 days to demonstrate with authentic documents that the property in custody of the authorities was obtained legally. If this cannot be done, the Attorney General's Office official added, [words indistinct] if it is proven that the persons acquired their wealth illegally, the property will be transferred to the Cuban Government.
