BFN [Text] Tirana, May 4 (ATA) -- At the request of the Union of the Independent Trade Unions of Albania (UITUA), a meeting was held May 3 between the UITUA representatives and the government, headed by Premier Aleksander Meksi; the minister of labour, emigration and social assistance, Dashamir Shehu; the minister of mining and energy resources, Abdyl Xhaja; and other government members. The participants in the meeting agreed that the issuing of a law or agreement on indexation of inflation be sanctioned until the end of May also setting the deadline it will come to force. As regards privatization, by the end of May, the government promised to issue a decision which will also include the demands of the independent trade unions. They also agreed that the trade unions be familiarized with the procedures of preparing the vouchers and that legislation regarding this question be completed within the first six months to be submitted to the parliament for approval. Mr. Meksi said that the price rise in the electric energy is an indispensability of the reform, but, taking into consideration the demands of the Independent Trade Unions for compensation for the electric energy, he will once again study the financial possibilities especially in the northeastern zone.
