Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 17 February. Collection of the IPMF, provisional tax on financial operations, during its first month was $336.1 million (about 131.3 billion cruzeiros), which is below the estimated $400 million per month. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 8 Feb 94 Business and Finance Section p 3) The Brazilian Petroleum Corporation, Petrobras, showed a $666 million net profit in 1993, an increase of 1,089 percent compared with 1992. The government's debt with the company dropped $366 million. The total debt now amounts to $3.358 billion. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 10 Feb 94 Business and Finance Section p 5) The Planning Secretariat reported that at least 40 of the 108 state-owned companies, including Petrobras, in 1993 surpassed the budget limitations set for them by Congress. Expenses surpassed the investment ceiling by 13.9 percent. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 10 Feb 94 Business and Finance Section p 4) The government is spending $1.2 billion per year to maintain 15 unfinished hydroelectric and thermoelectric plants. A study by the Andersen Consulting firm shows that each 1,000 megawatts of unfinished plant costs $100 million. Besides this, $12.3 billion already has been invested in these plants. The Angra-2 nuclear plant's expenses alone already have hit $2.6 billion. (Rio de Janeiro JORNAL DO BRASIL in Portuguese 13 Feb 94 Business and Finance Section p 15)
