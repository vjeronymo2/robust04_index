Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Yelistratov] [Text] Cairo January 4 TASS -- The first joint exercise of Kuwaiti and Russian naval forces ended in the Persian Gulf on Tuesday. The exercise lasting 12 days involved several Kuwaiti warships as well as a large anti-submarine ship, the Admiral Vinogradov, a large landing ship, the Nikolay Vilkov, and an auxiliary vessel of the Russian Navy. The exercise was carried out in conformity with a treaty on joint defence between the two countries which was concluded late last November. According to military observers, it was conducted at a high professional level. All tasks set forth before the crews were successfully fulfilled. Referring to a statement by the spokesman of the Kuwaiti Defence Ministry, the Sawt al-Arab radio station reported on Tuesday that preparations are now in progress to conduct another Kuwaiti-Russian exercise which will involve all services -- the navies, air forces and land troops.
