Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by BELINFORM for TASS] [Text] Minsk 29 Mar, TASS -- A law on elections of the Belarusian president was approved today in the second reading by 215 parliament deputies to be introduced on Wednesday, 30 March. Under the law, a citizen aging over 35, enjoying the electoral right and Belarusian resident for no less than ten years can be elected president for five years and no more than two terms. The presidential elections shall be universal, equal and direct. The president shall be elected by Belarusian citizens aging over 18. The right to nominate a presidential candidate will be enjoyed by those who manage to collect signatures of no less than 70 parliament deputies or no less than 100,000 signatures of Belarusian citizens. The nomination of presidential candidates shall start 75 days before the elections and end 40 days before the voting. Under the law, candidates for Belarusian president shall equally participate in the election campaign since the time of their registration by the central commission for presidential elections and enjoy equal rights to the use of mass media. All the registered presidential candidates shall have equal possibilities of material-technical and financial provision of their election campaign. During the election campaign the candidates can be freed from their service duties and preserve an average salary. If the ballot sheet includes no less than two presidential candidates and neither of them is elected, there will be repeated elections with repeated nomination of candidates. The Belarusian president shall be considered elected if his candidature was supported by over 50 per cent of participants in the elections.
