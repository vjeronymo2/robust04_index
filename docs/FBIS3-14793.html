Language: English Article Type:BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] St. Petersburg February 26 TASS -- St. Petersburg newspapers reported today at a Friday [25 February] briefing at the Federal Counter-intelligence Service's department despite all the refutations. The briefing told correspondents about details of the detention of five terrorists on the eve of 2 February near the Kazan cathedral. In the words of head of the service department's division against terrorism Aleksandr Kuznetsov, the detained, some of whom participated in the October 1993 events in Moscow, prepared an armed attack at a Petersburg police station. The attack was expected to seize arms to be later used in an attempt upon the life of Petersburg Mayor Anatoliy Sobchak and an attack at the Moscow government house. The information is taken from materials of the Petersburg counter-intelligence service. According to mass media reports, the individuals were detained right after their attack at the sentry-box of the Zarya scientific-production amalgamation, where they seized five revolvers and 2,000 cartridges. Kuznetsov did not comment on political hidden motives of the terrorists' actions in the interests of the investigation. However, he said intentions of the group, whose youngest member is 39 years old and the oldest is 47, were serious.
