Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Belgrade, 28 February (TANJUG) -- The General Staff of the Yugoslav Army categorically rejects the possibility that the four planes that were shot down this morning by NATO fighters belonged to the Yugoslav Air Force, a TANJUG journalist was told today by the Yugoslav Army General Staff. Yugoslav Army planes, respecting the UN Security Council resolution banning flights over the former Bosnia-Herzegovina, did not fly around or overfly this zone, the Yugoslav Army General Staff stresses. Since the establishment of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, Yugoslav Army planes have not flown outside FRY air space, they stress at the General Staff. [passage omitted]
