Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Varlamov] [Text] Tokyo February 3 TASS--Two-day Russo-Japanese consultations on foreign policy planning at foreign department official level began in Tokyo today. In an interview with ITAR-TASS head of the Russian delegation, chief of the Russian Foreign Ministry Analysis and Prognosis Department Ednan Agayev said that the range of problems, which is being discussed at the meeting, embraces not only general tendencies of the foreign policy situation in the world, but also problems, concerning the development of Asia-Pacific region, in particular, the development of integration processes. According to him, the evolution of Russian and Japanese approaches towards ensuring regional security will also be an important topic for exchange of views. Annual consultations on foreign policy planning between foreign ministries of the two country are being held since 1992 and have become a form of regular contacts between the two foreign departments. Consultations are held alternately in Moscow and Tokyo. They are serving as mutual exchange of information about questions, concerning the formation and development of foreign policies of the countries and also about the international situation.
