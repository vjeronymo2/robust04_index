Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- Pavel Koncos was appointed new Slovak agriculture minister today, at the proposal of the Democratic Left Party (SDL). Koncos, chairman of an agricultural cooperative in Klenovec, central Slovakia, started to appear on the Slovak political scene in 1990. He was elected a parliamentary deputy in the June 1992 elections on the SDL slate. Koncos was born on September 28, 1947, in Rimavska Pila, central Slovakia. In 1966-1972 he studied at the Agricultural School in Nitra, central Slovakia, and then worked as a chief economist in a breeding farm in Rimavska Sobota and in 1979 became chairman of the agricultural cooperative in Klenovec. From 1990 Koncos worked as deputy chairman of the Slovak Union of Agricultural Cooperatives. After the establishment of the Slovak Agricultural and Food Union in 1991 he was elected its chairman. Koncos is actively dealing with agricultural problems in the Slovak parliament. In June 1992 he was elected chairman of the Parliamentary Committee for Agrarian, Forestry and Water Management.
