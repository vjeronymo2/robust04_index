Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] The Israeli Arab delegation today conferred with Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad in Damascus. At the end of the meeting, the spokesman for the president's bureau said that al-Asad thanked the members of the delegation for sharing his grief and for their sincere condolences. A short time ago, our correspondent 'Ofer Vardi spoke with Knesset Member 'Abd-al-Wahhab Darawshah, the head of the delegation: [Begin Darawshah recording] It was a good, interesting, and lengthy meeting which lasted over two hours. The president greeted us with manifest warmth and modesty. Despite the grief over the death of his son, he gave us an extraordinary reception. He stood up and kissed each one of us, and upon our departure he walked me out of the hall and bid farewell to each of us. In the meeting, we extended our condolences and discussed the peace process. He lectured us at length about the Syrian position on the peace process. He also welcomed our initiative to come to Syria to extend our condolences, and said he regarded us as part of the Arab world. At the same time, he emphasized that Syria's strategy is one of peace. He said: We are prepared to embrace the formula of full peace in exchange for full withdrawal, and we already know what kind of peace we want. We want full peace in all its senses, but the Israeli Government has not yet defined the kind of withdrawal it has in mind. The prime minister speaks about a withdrawal on the Golan, rather than from the entire Golan. [end recording]
