BFN [Excerpt] The National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] and the Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola-Labor Party [MPLA-PT] continue to discuss peace in Lusaka under UN mediation. Reports from the Zambian capital yesterday said the two sides have finally agreed to review the Electoral Law. Independent sources have described that move as a qualitative step forward in the thorny Lusaka talks marathon. Meanwhile, UN sources have said that there are not too many differences concerning the points under discussion and that a plenary meeting should be called soon. The UNITA and MPLA-PT delegations have been discussing since 22 April the specific principles and modalities that will govern the implementation of the electoral process. [passage omitted]
