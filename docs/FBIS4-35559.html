BFN [Text] Cairo -- On the sidelines of the meetings of the 11th Non-Aligned foreign ministers' conference, [Syrian] Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' today met with Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, Jordanian minister of state for foreign affairs. The talks dealt with the Middle East peace process and its developments. The talks also dealt with the issues listed on the agenda of the Non-Aligned Conference. Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' also met with Kim Yong-nam, vice premier and foreign minister of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. During the meeting, the two sides exchanged views on issues listed on the agenda of the conference. The talks also dealt with relations between the two friendly countries. Faruq al-Shar' also met with South African Foreign Minister Alfred Nzo. Al-Shar' congratulated the South African foreign minister on his country's joining of the Non-Aligned Movement. He said that the lifting of apartheid is a victory for the movement and mankind in general. During the meeting, the two sides expressed their concern for establishing firm relations between the two countries in all fields. The South African foreign minister highly appreciated Syria's support for the just struggle of the African National Congress and its historic success in dismantling the apartheid system and establishing a nonracial democratic republic. The foreign minister also met with Bosnian Foreign Minister Dr. Irfan Ljubijankic. The talks dealt with the developments in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Al-Shar' met with Professor Guido De Marco, Maltese deputy prime minister and foreign minister. During the meeting, the talks dealt with the situation in the region, and latest developments in the Middle East peace process.
