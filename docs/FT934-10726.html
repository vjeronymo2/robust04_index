931104 FT 04 NOV 93 / Call to promote television expertise SIR Christopher Lewinton, chairman and chief executive of TI, the specialist engineering group, yesterday told Mr Michael Heseltine, trade and industry secretary, that it was time to start treating British broadcasters as 'winners' rather than a problem, Raymond Snoddy writes. He was one of the few non-broadcasters at a meeting with Mr Heseltine and Mr Peter Brooke, national heritage secretary, called to look at ways of maximising UK programme production and programme exports. Others at the meeting included Mr Bob Phillis, deputy director-general of the BBC, Mr Michael Grade, chief executive of Channel 4, Mr Leslie Hill, chairman of Central and the new chairman of the ITV Association, and Mr Michael Green, chairman of Carlton Communications. A main theme of the meeting was the need for further deregulation if Britain's broadcasters are to compete in a world of media giants.
