BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Tokyo 6 April, TASS -- President of Kazakhstan Nursultan Nazarbayev arrived on his first official visit to Japan on Wednesday. He is to have an audience with Emperor Akihito and meet Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, parliamentarians and businessmen. A joint declaration and a number of bilateral documents are to be signed. Talks will focus on economic cooperation and Kazakhstan will request Japanese aid to help overcome the aftermath of nuclear tests at the Semipalatinsk range and to conduct irrigation work to save the disappearing Aral Sea, TASS learned from a spokesman of Nazarbayev. International issues, including the granting to Japan of the status of a permanent member of the UN Security Council, will also be discussed.
