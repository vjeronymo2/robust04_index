December 16, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

FANS CAN'T TAKE MUCH MAS OF THIS

Why does Jim Murray even bother writing boxing articles? They are so Sugar Ray Leonard biased, it's nauseating.

He recently had the audacity to claim that Leonard has ruled boxing much in the same way that Sugar Ray Robinson did decades ago.

Robinson never avoided fighters in their prime (as Leonard did with Marvin Hagler). If a fighter ever gave Robinson anything even resembling a good fight, he gave them an instant rematch. He didn't wait for the pug to turn into a battle-scarred cadaver (as Leonard did with Hearns, and then almost lost anyway).

Leonard is a feather-fisted slapper. He couldn't mark up the cut-prone Hagler, he couldn't floor the battle-worn Hearns with his best punches, and couldn't knock out Roberto Duran, an old, puffed-up lightweight.

DOUG CAVANAUGH, Canoga Park
