941228 FT 28 DEC 94 / World News in Brief: Cricketer Peter May dies Peter May, widely-regarded as England's finest post-war batsman, died after a short illness. He was 64. He played 66 Tests for England from 1951 to 1961, scoring 4,537 runs, and was captain a record 41 times. He was chairman of the England selectors in the 1980s.
