940610 FT 10 JUN 94 / China exports up 24% China's exports grew faster than imports in the first five months of the year, raising prospects of balanced trade this year after last year's Dollars 12bn (Pounds 8bn) deficit, Tony Walker writes from Beijing. Exports grew 24.1 per cent to May against 18.1 per cent import growth. Exports reached Dollars 37.49bn and imports Dollars 39.28bn, but customs reported faster export growth in May. China's foreign trade deficit reached Dollars 1.79bn against Dollars 3bn in the same 1993 period.
