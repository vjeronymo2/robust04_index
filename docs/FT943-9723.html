940809 FT 09 AUG 94 / Murdoch warms to Blair Mr Rupert Murdoch, chairman and chief executive of News Corporation, said last night he 'could imagine' supporting Mr Tony Blair, the Labour leader. Mr Murdoch said in an interview with the German magazine Der Spiegel that although he was a conservative, his newspapers had supported Lord Wilson, the former Labour prime minister, and many Labor politicians in Australia. 'I could even imagine supporting the British Labour leader Tony Blair,' he said. Mr Murdoch's company owns five UK national newspapers and a majority stake in BSkyB, the satellite TV network in which Pearson, owner of the Financial Times has a stake. Mr David Blunkett, Labour party chairman, said last night: 'I think there has been a very clear change in the attitude of the Murdoch press anyway over the last two years, with their virulent attacks on John Major.' But Mr John Maples, deputy Tory party chairman, said: 'He only said he could imagine doing it. He did not say he was going to do it, and it would be very odd if he did.'
