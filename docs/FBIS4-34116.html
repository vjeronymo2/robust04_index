BFN [Text] Havana, 10 Jun [date as received] (PL) -- High level delegations from Cuba and Mexico began talks here today to review their multifaceted bilateral relations and to seek new formulas to expand and diversify their relations. Cuban President Fidel Castro and Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari are holding their first round of private talks in a protocol residence in the outskirts of the capital. Both presidents will focus their dialogue on strategic aspects of a political and economic nature, while working groups from the two countries will simultaneously examine a long list of aspects of common interest in economic and scientific-technical cooperation. The Mexican delegation consists of Foreign Secretary Manuel Tello Macias; Jaime Serra Puche, Commerce and Industrial Development Secretary; and Guillermo Jimenez Morales, Fisheries Secretary. The talks are also being attended by Herminio Blanco, commerce under secretary; Santiago Onate, who is head of the presidential advisers; and Carlos Tello Macias, Mexican ambassador to Cuba. The Cuban delegation consists of Carlos Lage, vice president of the Council of State; Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina; and Jose Ramon Fernandez, vice president of the Council of Ministers. The delegation includes Fish Industry Minister Jose Fernandez Cuervo; Foreign Trade Minister Ricardo Cabrisas; Ernesto Melendez, minister president of the State Committee for Economic Cooperation; Hector Rodriguez Llompart, president of the Banco Nacional de Cuba; and Marcos Javier Portal, Basic Industry minister. Cuba and Mexico have joint businesses in tourism, cement industry, and in glass manufacturing. They also have conducted negotiations on other business ventures in the fields of telecommunications, energy, and textiles. During the current visit, it is expected that such projects will be discussed or will be prepared for signature, according to sources close to the delegations of both countries. Later, President Carlos Salinas will hold a news conference, and he will meet with members of the Mexican community in Cuba, before continuing en route to Cartagena de Indias, Colombia, where he will participate in the Fourth Ibero-American Summit.
