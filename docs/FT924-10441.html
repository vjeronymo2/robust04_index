921031 FT 31 OCT 92 / International Company News: Coopers to pay Dollars 95m settlement COOPERS & Lybrand, the accounting firm, has agreed to pay Dollars 95m to settle claims over its work for former client MiniScribe, a computer disk drive manufacturer which went bankrupt amid allegations of financial fraud, writes Martin Dickson. The settlement, approved by a Denver court, is with shareholders of MiniScribe and the company's bankruptcy estate. The settlement is understood to bring to at least Dollars 140m Coopers' payments for MiniScribe related claims, since it has already agreed to pay Dollars 40m - Dollars 50m in a suit brought by bondholders in Texas. Coopers neither admitted nor denied wrong-doing in the Denver settlement.
