Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] Hekmatyar's Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party of Afghanistan] has rejected a plan proposed by Ahmad Shah Mas'ud, that suggested a 15-member council should mediate between the warring factions in Afghanistan. According to an IRNA report, the Hezb-e Eslami spokesman said: Hekmatyar's Hezb-e Eslami considers this proposal as an effort to create a rift between Afghanistan's Jihadi groups. Furthermore, the Hezb-e Wahdat-e Eslami-e [Islamic Unity Party of Afghanistan] spokesman in Peshawar told IRNA: The resolution of the Afghan problem can only be accomplished by agreement among the four major parties in that country; that is Hezb-e Eslami, Hezb-e Wahdat, Jonbesh-e Shomal [the Northern Movement] and Rabbani's Jami'at-e Eslami [Islamic Society of Afghanistan].
