BFN [Excerpts] [passage omitted] The Milan Public Prosecutor's office has sensationally asked for 37 politicians and businessmen involved in the Enimont affair to stand trial. This was the same affair for which financier Sergio Cusani was tried and found guilty. Dario Carella reports from the Milan courthouse. [Carella] [passage omitted] Chief Public Prosecutor Borrelli said that Deputy Prosecutor Antonio Di Pietro has requested to Examining Judge Italo Ghitti that 37 people, mostly politicians, stand trial on various charges connected with the Enimont investigation. Among the 37 are Northern League leader Umberto Bossi, former Northern League treasurer Alessandro Patelli, [former Prime Minister] Bettino Craxi, [Italian Republican Party leader] Giorgio La Malfa, [former Justice Minister] Claudio Martelli, [former Italian Liberal Party leader] Renato Altissimo, Pino Berlini, Luigi Bisignani, Montedison's Carlo Sama, ENI's Alberto Grotti, [former Foreign Minister] Gianni De Michelis, [former Christian Democratic Party Treasurer] Severino Citaristi, [former Prime Minister] Arnaldo Forlani, [former Italian Liberal Party Minister] Egidio Sterpa, [former Milan Mayor] Paolo Pillitteri, and [former Budget Minister] Paolo Cirino Pomicino. Turning to the one billion lire that was allegedly paid to the Italian Communist Party -- the Milan Criminal Court's Second Section also found Cusani guilty in relation to this. Borrelli said that as yet it is not known to whom the one billion lire was physically paid, and no decision has been made for the time being. Borrelli stressed `for the time being,' so the investigations are continuing.
