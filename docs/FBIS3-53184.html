Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, 12 Jan (INA) -- Vice President Taha Yasin Ramadan today received a written message from Jordanian Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali. The message states Jordan's desire to develop relations with Iraq in the interest of both countries and to strengthen the fraternal and historical ties between the two countries. Jordanian Minister of Energy and Mineral Wealth Walid 'Asfur delivered the message when Taha Yasin Ramadan received him here today. During the meeting, Taha Yasin Ramadan said the future will see the implementation of strategic development projects in Iraq. The projects the Iraqis have accomplished and their advanced experience in the oil, industry, and reconstruction fields are in the service of the Arab nation, Ramadan said. Taha Yasin Ramadan also expressed Iraq's concern to develop and enhance its relations with fraternal Jordan. The Jordanian minister of energy and mineral wealth praised Iraq's reconstruction drive despite the embargo, saying this reflects genuine self-reliance in reconstruction and progress and not depending on external capabilities. The meeting was attended by Oil Minister Safa' Hadi Jawad and the Jordanian ambassador in Baghdad.
