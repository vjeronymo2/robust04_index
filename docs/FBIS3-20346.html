Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Rome, March 29 (AFP) -- President Oscar Luigi Scalfaro said Tuesday [29 March] that he hoped Italy's next parliament would "guarantee stablility for policies and stability for the Italian people." Scalfaro, who will designate the next head of government following the landslide victory by a right-wing bloc headed by media magnate Silvio Berlusconi, said in a statement to the press that the new administration "was entitled to work in peace." He hailed the climate of "tranquility and normality" in which the election had been conducted despite a complex new electoral law. Scalfaro is to convene a meeting on April 15 of the new chambers of Italy's 12th legislature since World War II.
