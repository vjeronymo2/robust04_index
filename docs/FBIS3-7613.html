Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, March 30 (KCNA) -- One million students under the South Korean Federation of General Student Councils (Hanchongnyon) have risen up in a new struggle to check the "parliamentary ratification" of the "implementation schedule of the Uruguay Round accord" including rice market opening, according to a radio report from Seoul. Students of 130 universities and colleges affiliated with Hanchongnyon decided to launch into various forms of struggle such as rallies, a signature campaign in streets and distribution of literatures in order to check the railroading of the "motion" on market opening at the "National Assembly" session which will begin on April 12. It decided to hold a meeting of representatives at Pusan University in early April with the attendance of more than 15,000 student representatives from all parts of South Korea and then go on a strike against the "parliamentary ratification" of the schedule. It also decided to send students to the countryside to wage a joint struggle with farmers and expand the struggle in solidarity with trade unions in May.
