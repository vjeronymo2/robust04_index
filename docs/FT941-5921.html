940304 FT 04 MAR 94 / India's exports surge 21% India's exports surged 21.41 per cent to Dollars 17.8bn (Pounds 9.8bn) in the 10 months from April 1993 to January this year. Preliminary figures yesterday showed that while exports are continuing to grow strongly, imports in the period increased only 0.68 per cent to Dollars 18.38bn against last year's Dollars 18.26bn. The trade deficit fell from Dollars 3.6bn in the 10 months to Dollars 581.68m. Main commodities that have led to the growth in exports are gems and jewellery (18 per cent); ready-made garments (12.3 per cent); engineering goods (9 per cent); textiles (6.8 per cent), and leather goods (3.9 per cent). Imports continued to be dominated by petroleum and crude oil. The Asian Development Bank yesterday approved a Dollars 600,000 grant for emergency rehabilitation management in Maharashtra, where 10,000 people were killed in an earthquake last September.
