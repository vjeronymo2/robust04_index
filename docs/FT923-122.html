920930 FT 30 SEP 92 / Arts: Today's Television It's an odd thing but during all the discussion of joy-riding over the past year and more, never once have I heard any mention of young people elsewhere in Europe engaging in joy-riding. So maybe it is a particularly British form of flamboyant sexual display. Whatever the truth may be, joy-riding is a favourite pastime in Belfast which provides the setting for tonight's Screenplay on BBC2, You, Me and Marley (9.00). The play won a small stack of awards for Belfast playwright Graham Reid at this year's Edinburgh International Film Festival, so it promises to be well worth watching. BBC 1's Inside Story, The Assassin (9.30) describes the unusual career of Michael Townley, a normal American guy until he married a quite exceptionally forceful Chilean political activist. As a reward for their anti-communist fervour, the couple were invited to join President Pinochet's ruthless secret police, called DINA. Together they went on long foreign holidays, taking with them a perfume-flask full of nerve-gas with which to eliminate enemies of the state.
