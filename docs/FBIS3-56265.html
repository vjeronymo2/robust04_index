Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow January 13 TASS -- Bill Clinton's visit to Moscow is "evidence of respect of the United States for our great country," Yevgeniy Ambartsumov, deputy to the State Duma, told ITAR-TASS. He was for a long time head of the Committee for International Affairs at the previous Russian Parliament. "I hope the U.S. President will take advantage of the visit to familiarise himself even more deeply with Russian problems. I think the U.S. should proceed from the assumption that it is necessary to promote the development of Russian democracy, from the understanding that any sort of encirclement is not for Russia." In the opinion of Ambartsumov, the creation of another sanitary cordon around Russia should be out of the question. "We are now living through a difficult period of our history. However, Russia has seen even worse times, but inevitably was brought back to life, like Phoenix from ashes. I hope the U.S. Administration thinks the same way," he said.
