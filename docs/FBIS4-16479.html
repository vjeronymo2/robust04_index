BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Zlobin] [Text] Petrozavodsk April 17 TASS -- Viktor Stepanov, chairman of the local parliament in Russia's northwestern Republic of Karelia, was elected on Sunday head of the republic's executive branch, according to preliminary results of elections to Karelia's new power structures. Local observers believe that the post of head of government to be occupied by Stepanov is very similar to that of president as for its powers. Elections were also held to a new two-chamber local parliament, the Legislative Assembly. The parliament is to consist of 61 lawmakers. Apart from this, residents in Karelia's cities, towns and districts voted for candidates to local governing bodies. At the moment, election officials are counting votes at polling stations. According to preliminary information, the average turnout across the republic made up slightly over 40 per cent, while only 35 per cent are required for elections to be valid. Foreign observers of Vermont, as well as representatives of various parties and movements said the polls were well-organized. No irregularities were reported.
