BFN [Text] Riyad, 25 Jun -- A meeting of representatives of foreign ministers of the Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] in Riyadh today to prepare for the next meeting of the international steering committee set up to work in tandem with the Israeli-Arab peace talks. The committee includes members of a joint Palestinian-Jordanian delegation; and representatives of Israel, Saudi Arabia -- representing the GCC states --Tunisia, Egypt, Canada, Japan, and the European Union, in addition to representatives of Russia and the United States as co-sponsors of the peace process. Diplomatic sources in Riyadh told KUNA that the Gulf officials will discuss the statement of principles to be submitted to the steering committee, which defines the agenda of talks on arms control in the Middle East and regional security in the future. The sources added that the committee, which oversees the work of other committees emanates from the multilateral talks, will also discuss the resolutions issued by the arms control committee at the end of its meeting held in Qatar last April. At its last meeting, the international steering committee discussed the results of meetings of the working groups set up in Moscow in January 1993. The groups dealt with issues of arms control, refugees, economic cooperation, water resources, and environment.
