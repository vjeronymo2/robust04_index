BFN [Text] Simferopol, June 3, RIA -- Another round of negotiations conducted by the joint working group including representatives of the Ukrainian and Crimean Parliaments ended in Simferopol today. The group discussed the entire range of legal problems arising from relations between the two parliaments. The chief negotiators, Aleksey Melnikov, deputy chairman of the Crimean Parliament, and Vladimir Butkevich, head of the Ukrainian Parliament's Human Rights Commission, told RIA that the negotiations had been conducted in a friendly and constructive atmosphere. It has been decided to resume the activities of the working group shortly and invite experts on legal and economic issues to take part in the discussion of specific problems. The deadline for the next meeting will be fixed out of session.
