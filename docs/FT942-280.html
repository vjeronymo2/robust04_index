940629 FT 29 JUN 94 / Savings blow for Australian banks Australian banks yesterday lost another round in their battle to carve out a role in the nation's rapidly expanding superannuation industry, when Mr Ralph Willis, the federal treasurer, ruled out special tax or regulatory regimes would have allowed them to offer 'super' investment accounts. The treasurer failed to use a statement yesterday on superannuation policy - much of which dealt with the mechanics of how small savers and businesses could make cost-effective contributions, and avoid unwieldy fees - to introduce proposals which would have allowed banks to set up their own superannuation-style accounts. The omission was quickly attacked by the nation's big banking groups. The banks claim that their branch networks make them well placed to collect such savings and warn there could be pressure on rates charged to borrowers if they remained locked out of the superannuation market. 'Government regulation has meant that superannuation is becoming the nation's preferred savings vehicle,' said Mr Don Mercer, head of ANZ. 'As more of the nation's savings are held by life offices and the tax office, banks will be forced to gather an increasing proportion of their deposits via the bond and money markets.'
