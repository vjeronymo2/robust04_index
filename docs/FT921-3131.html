920316 FT 16 MAR 92 / Election 92: Lamont loses out to his shadow Assessment of the relative qualities of the two party leaders is just how overwhelming is the backing for Mr John Major. The prime minister is preferred by 93 per cent against the 1 per cent backing Mr Neil Kinnock. But there is an unwelcome message in the survey for Mr Norman Lamont. Only 39 per cent believe he is the best choice for Number 11 Downing Street against the 46 per cent prepared to forgive Mr John Smith his party affiliation and say that he would make the better chancellor. Mr Smith is particularly popular among executives of companies with a turnover of between Pounds 500m and Pounds 2bn, where his rating jumps to 56 per cent, while the very biggest companies are kinder towards Mr Lamont. Mr Michael Howard scores for his implacable opposition to the minimum wage and to any increase in the powers of unions by winning the preference of 69 per cent for the job of employment secretary. Mr Tony Blair, the young star of the Labour front bench, gets the vote of only 14 per cent.
