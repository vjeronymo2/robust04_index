Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Article by Jaroslav Kmenta: "The Tamara Radar Could Monitor the Situation in the Middle East"] [Text] Prague--The Czech radar, Tamara, which, according to the experts, can even detect the "invisible" U.S. Stealth aircraft, could participate in monitoring the situation in the Middle East. The manufacturer of this state-of-the-art air defense system offered it to the United Nations at the beginning of the year. According to preliminary conjectures, the Tamara radar could be deployed in Cyprus. From this island, which is some 150 kilometers from the coast of Lebanon, Israel, and other Middle Eastern states, the radar could monitor the airspace above this region. It could also partially monitor the transfer of military hardware on land and sea. Tamara has a range of up to 450 kilometers. Tamara can detect aircraft against which normal radar systems are ineffective. It is similar to the U.S. AWACS [Airborne Warning and Control System] early warning system. However, as opposed to active radar systems, which emit radio waves into the atmosphere and assess their reflections, Tamara only receives radio impulses from the atmosphere. Ludek Stodulka, vice president of the HTT Group, which produces the radar, told us that Tamara can detect air-defense systems and the precise coordinates of missiles, airfields, and warning systems. It can differentiate between the false and real locations of military hardware. It can distinguish types of aircraft, missiles, ships, and radars at great distances. According to Stodulka, the Czech Foreign Ministry has described the company's initiative as encouraging. However, the appropriate UN offices have not yet responded to the HTT Group's offer. In the opinion of Army experts, the deployment of the Tamara system would have forestalled such tragedies as the shooting down of flight KAL-007 in 1983 by a former Soviet fighter bomber or the shooting down of the A-300 passenger aircraft by a U.S. Navy missile over the Persian Gulf. Stodulka said that the HTT Group would like to apply to the Association for Czech Republic Quality Awards to grant the system a Czech Made trademark.
