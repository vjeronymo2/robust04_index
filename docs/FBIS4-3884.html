BFN [Text] Amman, April 24 (XINHUA) -- A 10-member delegation of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) arrived here today on a four-day visit to the Kingdom to promote bilateral relations. The delegation is headed by Wang Zhaoguo, vice chairman of the CPPCC. Soon after his arrival, Wang Zhaoguo headed for the Jordanian parliament to meet with Senate Speaker Ahmed al-Lawzi. In his speech at the meeting, al-Lawzi said that the sphere of cooperation between Jordan and China is vast, including domains of politics, economy, culture, trade, youth and sports. He lauded China's stand on supporting the just causes in the Inzmnal arena. Saying that China is of significance in the on going Middle East peace process and a foundation on which Jordan relies. He noted that it is important for Jordan that China is one of the five permanent U.N. Security Council members. He called for Chinese action to help implement relevant U.N. resolutions on the Arab-Israeli conflict and on the Middle East peace issue. Al-Lawzi also asked the Chinese side to import phosphate, potash and chemical fertilizers from Jordan to address the current trade imbalance between the two countries. In his speech, Wang Zhaoguo introduced to his Jordanian hosts the achievements China has made during the past 15 years of reform and economic construction. Wang spoke highly of Jordan's peaceful, neutral and non-aligned foreign policy, saying that the Kingdom has made unswerving efforts for the Arab-Israeli peace talks and the Middle East peace process. He said that China is devoted to developing friendly ties with all countries, especially the developing countries no matter how the international situation changes, Wang said, China's principled stand of peaceful co-existence and the policy of developing friendly and cooperative relations with developing countries will not change. Wang said China has consistently supported the just struggle of the Palestinian people, advocating a political solution to the Middle East problem on the basis of relevant U.N. resolutions, especially on U.N. Security Council Resolution MWRW [as received] and 338. On behalf of the CPPCC, Wang extended an invitation to al-Lawzi for a visit to China next year, and al-Lawzi accepted the invitation. He is expected to meet with Prime Minister 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali later today.
