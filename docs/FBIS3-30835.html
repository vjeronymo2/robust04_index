Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Belgrade, 8 Feb (TANJUG) -- Yugoslav Prime Minister Radoje Kontic today held a meeting in the Palace of the Federation with the prime ministers of the republics of Serbia and Montenegro, Nikola Sainovic and Milo Djukanovic, the Federal Ministry of Information has announced. The meeting was attended by Dragoslav Avramovic; Bozidar Gazidova, deputy governor of the Yugoslav National Bank; Zarija Martinovic, managing director of the payment transactions service, and a number of federal ministers. They examined the implementation of this year's economic policy and the program of reconstruction of the monetary system and Yugoslavia's economic recovery. It was concluded that significant results beyond expectations have been achieved in the implementation of the anti-inflation program, primarily as regards the purchase of foreign currency, market supply, and the reduction of hyperinflation. The Yugoslav National Bank has up to today purchased foreign currency to the tune of 54 million new dinars, and at the same time has sold only 3 million German marks, which shows that the foreign currency reserves of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia have increased by 51 million German marks in 17 days of implementing the economic program. In addition, the level of prices over the past seven days dropped by 0.5 percent compared with the previous week. Some 150 million new dinars and 5 million German marks have been deposited in business bank accounts. The Yugoslav National Bank has printed notes: 227 million new dinars have been forwarded to the payment transaction service, and 271 million new dinars to business banks. [passage omitted]
