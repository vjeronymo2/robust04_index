930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / World Stock Markets (America): Cyclical spree sends US prices into spiral Wall Street AN AFTERNNON spree in cyclical issues sent stock prices spiralling in spite of a recovery in the healthcare sector, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 19.73 to 3,323.26 after adding more than 5 points in morning trading. The more broadly based Standard & Poor's 500 was 0.45 lower at 434.79, while the Amex composite added 0.67 to 399.96 and the Nasdaq composite eased 0.86 to 651.56. Trading volume on the New York SE was heavy with more than 323m shares changing hands on the day, and rises outnumbered declines by 1053 to 909. On Monday, the Dow climbed 20.81 to 3,342.99. The stock market lost ground on a day when the treasury's bellwether 30-year bond soared 1 3/8 for a yield of 6.824 per cent. Eroding prices for cyclical stocks contributed to Wall Street's afternoon retreat. In the auto sector, Chrysler dropped Dollars 2- 1/8 to Dollars 35- 7/8 , Ford lost Dollars 1- 7/8 at Dollars 44- 7/8 and General Motors slumped Dollars 1- 3/4 to Dollars 36- 7/8 . Pharmaceutical stocks, which have slumped on fears that President Clinton's healthcare reforms will cut into profit growth, regained some of their recent losses yesterday. Merck was Dollars 1 higher at Dollars 37- 5/8 , Bristol-Myers Squibb rose Dollars 2- 3/4 to Dollars 56 5/8 , Pfizer grew Dollars 3 to Dollars 57 and Johnson & Johnson firmed Dollars 1 to Dollars 40. Mylan Laboratories, which plunged Dollars 4- 5/8 on Monday, was quoted Dollars 3- 3/4 higher at Dollars 29- 1/8 . The pharmaceutical products company said it expects to turn in record revenues and earnings for its fiscal year ending in March. Among featured issues, Dean Witter Discover was quoted at Dollars 31 in heavy trading. The initial public offering of 29.5m shares in the financial services company was priced at Dollars 27 late Monday. Sears, parent of Dean Witter, Discover had expected a price of Dollars 23 to Dollars 25. Shares in tobacco giant Philip Morris continued to sink, reflecting investor worries that the Clinton administration might impose an excise tax on cigarettes to help fund reform of the healthcare system. The stock fell Dollars -7/8 to Dollars 63-5/8. An analyst at Smith Barney, Harris Upham was reported to have removed Philip Morris from his recommended list. Other tobacco stocks also lost ground including RJR Nabisco, off Dollars 1/4 at Dollars 7-3/4 and UST, down Dollars -3/8 to Dollars 25-7/8. Oil stocks, which led Monday's rally, were mixed yesterday in spite of further gains in crude oil futures. Chevron slid Dollars 1-1/8 to Dollars 78, Mobil added Dollars -1/2 to Dollars 67-3/8, Texaco lost Dollars 1-1/8 to Dollars 62-1/8 and British Petroleum's ADRs edged Dollars -1/8 lower to Dollars 48-3/8. Canada TORONTO share prices closed slightly higher in active trading, boosted by a strong increase in gold stocks. According to preliminary data, the 300 composite index gained 5.11 points, or 0.15 per cent, to 3,452.78. Advances led declines 351 to 312. Volume rose to 40.64m shares from 39.80m shares on Monday, and trading value was CDollars 409.4m, up from CDollars 397m. The banking sector was up 0.50 per cent as Salomon Brothers Inc. raised its investment ratings on Toronto-Dominion Bank, Bank of Nova Scotia and Canadian Imperial Bank to 'buy' from 'hold.' Air Canada said it will ask all employees to accept a 5 per cent salary cut as of April 1.
