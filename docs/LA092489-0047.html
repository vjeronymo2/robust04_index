September 24, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

HOME PROTECTION

It was fine that Stephen J. Frazier of San Diego wrote to correct an error on the river that flows through his home state of Vermont (Letters, Sept. 10), but why did he have to add the last sentence: "I only hope you didn't encourage too many of them to visit"?

If he is so protective of his home state, why isn't he back there protecting it instead of being in San Diego taking advantage of its opportunities? Did California discourage him from visiting or moving in? Isn't there enough room in the United States for each of us to find our own chosen visiting or living space?

VICKIE L. SPRATT

Pomona
