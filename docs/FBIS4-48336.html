BFN [Text] Durban May 16 SAPA -- The Human Rights Commission [HRC] on Monday said 337 people had been killed in KwaZulu/Natal violence in April, more than a third of them in the greater Durban area. The April toll was noticeably higher than March's figure of 311. The HRC said in a statement 80 per cent of the killings occurred before the April 19 announcement of the Inkatha Freedom Party's participation in the elections. The HRC said 143 people had been killed in the first week of April, 75 in the second, 77 in the third and 42 in the last. The toll had been 117 in the greater Durban area, 41 in Northern Natal, 96 on the North Coast, 58 on the South Coast and 25 in the Midlands.
