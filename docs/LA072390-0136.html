July 23, 1990, Monday, P.M. Final

PEOPLE'S PHARMACY: ONIONS, IF NOT TOBACCO, CAN RELIEVE PAIN OF BEE STINGS

Question: At our family reunion last week my little niece was stung by a yellow jacket. My dad said his father always swore by tobacco juice, but no one at the gathering was chewing tobacco. Someone tried soaking a cigarette and squeezing the juice on the sting, but that didn't work.

Isn't there some other home remedy that would work to take away the pain of a sting?

Answer: In our part of the country tobacco juice is an old home remedy for bee stings too, but we've never seen any scientific data to show if this works.

But one of our readers came up with a home remedy for stings that really took our breath away. He credits his German neighbor lady with suggesting that a cut onion be applied to the sting for at least 10 minutes.

When we checked with the world-renowned onion researcher, Dr. Eric Block of the State University of New York at Albany, he confirmed that a fresh-cut onion may minimize the pain. Onions contain an ingredient that can break down the chemicals responsible for inflammation and discomfort.

Q: I will be attending my 25th class reunion next month and I am feeling embarrassed about my weight. I weigh 12 pounds more now than I did at the last reunion, and my classmates made fun of me then.

I came across a diet pill that promised I could lose up to five pounds a week. The ingredient is guar gum. What is it and how does it work?

A: Guar gum is a soluble fiber from guar seeds. Like the fiber in oat bran and pectin, guar gum has been shown to lower cholesterol and perhaps even improve blood sugar levels in diabetics.

There is controversy, however, about whether guar gum actually promotes weight loss. Although you might have a feeling of fullness after consuming this fiber, you should not expect to lose a lot of weight in a short period of time.
