930825 FT 25 AUG 93 / The Lex Column: UK regulation Once again, a prosecution brought by the Serious Fraud Office has collapsed. In the latest instance, the judge was even moved to question whether the whole matter might not have been more effectively examined by the regulatory authorities than the criminal system. That strikes at the heart of the current problem. The SFO has failed to manage the investigation of complex fraud in an effective way. Yet even if some allegations were better pursued through civil actions and the threat of exclusion from professional bodies, the current self-regulatory organisations are ill-placed to handle the task. Since the Financial Services Act has not so far proved equal to the task, the case for wholesale reform grows stronger.
