CSO [Text] A total of 564 tons of nonoil export goods valued at 736,929,000 rials [R] went through Gonaveh customs bound for the Arab nations of the Persian Gulf during the month of Farvardin [21 March-20 April 1994]. The chief of Gonaveh Customs said: This amount represents a 37-percent increase in terms of weight over the same period last year and a decrease of about 9 percent compared to the same period last year in terms of value. Zabihollah Jamali added: Likewise, during this period 93 tons of goods valued at R116,442,000 was imported into the country through this customs office by various trade and peddler cooperatives. He said the main items exported through this customs office were plastic and melamine containers and ceramics and that tea, automobile tires, and sewing thread were the main import items.
