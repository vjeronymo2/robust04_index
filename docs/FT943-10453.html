940804 FT 04 AUG 94 / S African shop strike settled A bitter 24-day strike over wages by workers at South Africa's Pick'n Pay supermarket chain ended yesterday but strikes in other sectors of the economy continued to hit production, Reuter reports from Johannesburg. Walkouts in the motor, metal, paper and oil industries and the public sector hit the country's fragile economy amid government fears that labour unrest could frighten off investors. Pick'n Pay, with 350 stores across the country, signed an agreement with the South African Commercial Catering and Allied Workers' Union giving employees a 180 rand (Dollars 40) increase and a 1,110 (Dollars 308) minimum wage. But there was no sign of progress in negotiations over a strike by 25,000 car workers that has brought seven big plants to a halt. 'There was no substantial progress in our negotiations and therefore we have nothing to say at this stage,' an official of the National Union of Metalworkers of South Africa said.
