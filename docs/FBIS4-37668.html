BFN [Text] Algerian Prime Minister Mokdad Sifi is in Paris today, where he has met French President Francois Mitterrand and Prime Minister Edouard Balladur. He has made a statement to the international media. Rachida Hamadi reports from Brussels [as heard]: At this very moment [as heard], immediately after his arrival from Belgium to France, the head of government, Mokdad Sifi, is meeting French President Francois Mitterrand. [Begin recording] [Unidentified correspondent addressing Sifi] Prime Minister, have you found a favorable response from the French side regarding Algeria's demands or needs for assistance? [Sifi] I have not come here to ask for anything. I have come to hold talks with French and European officials within the framework of cooperation in all spheres -- political and economic spheres. My meeting with President Mitterrand allows me to say that he supports the Algerian Government's and head of state's endeavors. [end recording] After his meeting with President Mitterrand, the prime minister went to French government headquarters, where he had a meeting with French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur. The meeting lasted about an hour.
