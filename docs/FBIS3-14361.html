Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 27 Mar (MENA)--Arab foreign ministers resumed meetings at the Arab League's headquarters today. They are scheduled to discuss the remaining items on the agenda of the 101st regular session of the Arab League Council. Yemeni Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah is chairing the session. The ministers are holding a closed session to discuss Arab-European dialogue, Arab-African dialogue, and Arab ties with the countries of the Commonwealth of Independent States, as well as the political and legal aspects of the issue of Arab and international water resources. The ministers are also seeking to form a coordinated Arab policy vis-a-vis weapons of mass destruction and to revive efforts to create an area free of these weapons in the Middle East. They are also due to discuss the issue of Bosnia-Herzegovina and the drafting of an Arab charter for human rights. Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid is expected to hold a news conference following the conclusion of today's session to announce its outcome and final recommendations.
