BFN [Text] Beijing, April 7 (XINHUA) -- "China always attaches great importance to its relations with France," Chinese Premier Li Peng said in talks with his visiting French counterpart Edouard Balladur here this afternoon. Balladur said his current China visit is aimed at improving French-Chinese relations. According to a spokesman of the Chinese Foreign Ministry, the talks between the Chinese and French prime ministers proceeded in a sincere and friendly atmosphere. During the talks Chinese Premier Li said: "Through joint efforts, China and France reached understanding in resuming traditional friendly relations of cooperation in January this year. With the publication of the joint communique between the two governments, Sino-French relations returned to the normal track." Li pointed out that although the world has seen tremendous changes in recent years, the importance of the Sino-French cooperation has not been reduced, but increased. The two countries, he continued, have many points in common in safeguarding world peace and promoting economic development. Economically, Li said, the two countries are mutually complementary, and the potential for the development of economic cooperation is great. He stressed that China would pursue long-term, stable and friendly ties of cooperation with France according to the principle of seeking common ground while reserving differences, mutual respect, mutual non-interference in each other's internal affairs and equality and mutual benefit. Li appreciated the positive efforts Prime Minister Balladur and the French Government had made to restore and improve the bilateral ties. "We should have an eye on the 21st century, and handle the bilateral ties strategically to make the Sino-French relations enjoy healthy development," Li said. The premier said that China and France have bright prospects in developing their economic and trade cooperation since the economy of China has entered a sustained, rapid and healthy development track, thus making it possible for China to expand its economic and trade cooperation with every countries in the world including France. Secondly, he said, France has advantages in the fields of transportation, energy and telecommunications and these sectors are just China's priority for development. China welcomes more French enterprises to participate in competition in China on the basis of equality. Thirdly, Li said, China always take a positive attitude towards the development of its trade and economic cooperation with France. He hoped that French enterprises would strengthen their competitiveness in the Chinese market so as to expand Sino-French trade and economic cooperation. Balladur also believed there are great potentials for France- China cooperation. He said the future visits of Chinese and French ministers will promote bilateral trade and economic cooperation. Li and Balladur also discussed the possibilities of cooperation on important projects. They agreed that the two sides, keeping the momentum of high-level contacts, will strengthen political consultations and confirm the memorandum of consultations between foreign ministers and deputy foreign ministers, which was reached this January when Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen visited France. Touching on issues of China's re-entry in GATT, Balladur said that France supports China to resume its founding party position in GATT, and thus become a founding nation in the world trade organization. Li expressed his thanks for this. The Chinese premier briefed Balladur on U.S. Secretary of State Christopher's China visit, and explained China's principled stand on human rights issues. Balladur briefed Li on France's views over the rights issue, saying that France, having no intention to interfere in other nation's internal affairs, is willing to discuss the issue with other countries on an equal footing. Premier Li, at the guests' request, also briefed them on China's independent policy of foreign affairs and its relations with its neighbors. The prime ministers exchanged views on international and regional issues of common concern. They noted that they share views on many issues. The two sides agreed that both China and France are influential nations in international affairs, and that the maintenance of good relations between them accords with the fundamental interests of the two sides and the world peace and stability as well. Among those present at the talks were Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen, Chairman of the Chinese Government Reception Committee and Minister of Power Industry Shi Dazhen, French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe, and Minister of Industry, Posts, Telecommunications and Foreign Trade Gerard Longuet. Prior to the talks, Premier Li presided over a welcoming ceremony for Prime Minister Balladur. In the evening, Li hosted a banquet in honor of Balladur and his party. This afternoon, Zhou Jue, vice-chairman of the Foreign Affairs Committee of the National People's Congress of China, held working talks with Vice-Presidents Gilles de Robien and Pierre-Andre Wiltzer of the National Assembly of France. Balladur arrived here this morning as Premier Li's guest on a four-day official visit to China.
