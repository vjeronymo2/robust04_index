930609 FT 09 JUN 93 / People: Daiwa replaces European chief Japan's second largest securities house Daiwa is bringing Ryonosuke Miyoshi, deputy head of the capital markets division in Tokyo, to London as the next chairman and chief executive of Daiwa Europe. Miyoshi, who celebrated his 49th birthday yesterday, was in London between 1983 and 1988 as head of the fixed income, money markets and derivatives division. He replaces Katsuhiko Fujimoto, who has been in London since May 1991. Fujimoto, 56, returns to set up the new trust banking operation, a service that the securities houses are now able to offer as a consequence of the continuing capital markets deregulation. Like Miyoshi, he is on the main board of Daiwa Securities. In common with other Japanese securities houses, Daiwa Europe has found its business from Japan tailing off. Pre-tax profits at Daiwa Europe and its subsidiaries were Pounds 4.2m for the year to March 1993 compared with Pounds 18.9m in the previous year, Daiwa revealed yesterday. However, it has sought to build up its European activities to compensate; the company points out a number of Euromarket successes including recently becoming the first Japanese house to lead manage a Eurosterling issue (for Eurofima).
