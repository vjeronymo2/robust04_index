Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Jean Chretien and other Canadian cabinet members say they want time to weigh options before deciding whether to support (?a) call for air strikes against Bosnian Serbs. The call for the NATO-led strike was made by the UN secretary general, Butrus Butrus-Ghali, after 68 civilians were killed and almost 200 others were wounded in a mortar attack on a Sarajevo market place on Saturday [5 February]. Mr. Chretien says Ottawa wants to consult its NATO allies and to obtain a report from Canadian peacekeepers in Bosnia, before reaching its decision. A primary consideration for Ottawa is the safety of Canada's 2,400 peacekeepers in the former Yugoslavia, but Mr. Chretien told the House of Commons that the situation in Bosnia is very, very bad, and that a decision may be taken in the next few days. The United States and the European Union support the call by Mr. Butrus-Ghali, but Russia has signalled strong opposition to any attack.
