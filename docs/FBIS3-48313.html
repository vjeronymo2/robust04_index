Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kathmandu, Jan. 20 KYODO -- Japanese Parliamentary Vice Minister for Foreign Affairs Shozo Azuma arrived in Kathmandu Thursday [20 January] on a two-day official visit to Nepal. Azuma said on arrival during his trip to the three-countries of South Asia that he will renew Japan's pledge to support the process of democratization and efforts dedicated to economic development through the process in these countries. He is scheduled to meet King Birendra and Prime Minister Girija Prasad Koirala on Thursday. He will also sign and exchange notes on two development projects to be carried out with Japanese assistance in Nepal. Azuma's current trip to the region will also take him to Pakistan and India. Earlier he paid visits to five African countries, including South Africa, Mozambique and Namibia, the vice minister said.
