May 15, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

TWA Reports Loss: Trans World Airlines lost $83.1 million on revenues of $989.7 million in the first quarter, swelling by 58% the shortfall it suffered a year earlier. Sales were 7.5% higher in the three months ended March 31 than a year ago. The nation's ninth-largest airline said its operating expenses increased for the quarter largely because of higher employee costs. TWA, which was taken private by Carl C. Icahn last year, also said it had $16.3 million in charges against earnings that resulted primarily from that transaction.
