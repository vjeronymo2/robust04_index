BFN [Text] Islamic Majles Speaker 'Ali Akbar Nateq-Nuri met Pakistani President Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari in Islamabad yesterday. The two sides called for greater effort toward implementing joint projects in the construction of railroads, a refinery in Pakistan, and the sale and transport of gas to Pakistan. Our Islamabad correspondent reports: [Begin unidentified correspondent recording] Nateq-Nuri stressed the need to set up a common market among the Economic Cooperation Organization [ECO] member countries. The Pakistani president also stated that the ECO would provide appropriate scope for using the capabilities of the member countries and Islamic countries. During the meeting the two sides reached agreement on establishing a committee to explore ways and means of setting up an Islamic interparliamentary group. Similarly, the two countries' officials welcomed a special session of women members of Islamic countries' parliaments and called for broadening cooperation between the Iranian and Pakistani Parliaments. Speaker Nateq-Nuri is also expected to visit the cities of Lahore and Karachi, the two most important cultural and economic centers of Pakistan, during his stay in Pakistan. Voice and Vision of the Islamic Republic of Iran, Islamabad. [end recording]
