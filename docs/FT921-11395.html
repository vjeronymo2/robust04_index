920131 FT 31 JAN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Rightwing Tories urge Major to reform - PM's image Conservative MPs closely associated with Mrs Margaret Thatcher last night called on Mr John Major to abandon his 'safety-first' image in the final weeks before the general election. In a thinly veiled warning to the prime minister not to depart from basic Thatcherite principles, the Conservative Way Forward said it was time for him to demonstrate his radical credentials. The group, which has Mrs Thatcher as its president and counts former cabinet ministers Mr Cecil Parkinson and Mr Norman Tebbit among supporters, congratulated Mr Major on the achievements of his first year in office. It said that the public had come to know him as 'pleasant, hardworking and trustworthy, with steady judgment and the will to fight hard for Britain'. But in a judgment that appears to reflect some irritation on the right of the party with the way Mr Major has distanced himself from his predecessor's radical approach, it said: 'The safety-first image may have served him well but a bolder image is needed now.' It added that the voters needed to see the prime minister pushing forward with the reforms of the 1980s. That meant he should associate himself more closely with Conservative programmes such as the overhaul of the health services; that he should be seen to be heading the challenge to the educational establishment; and that he should be pushing for tax reform as well as tax cuts. Backing Mr Major's pledge to reduce the burden of inheritance tax, the group said he should go further and make all family bequests of less than Pounds 500,000 tax-exempt. Concluding that the loyalty of Conservative voters had been tested by the recession, the group said the party's supporters throughout the country were now ready to respond to bold leads. 'So step forward John Major Mark II - the reforming prime minister.'
