Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN [Text] Over 200 armed inhabitants at Don Taeuy village in Kampot District of Kampot Province cooperated with the National Army of Democratic Kampuchea [NADK] in attacking and destroying a battalion of the invading forces of the Vietnamese communist puppets and two-headed government, which intended to plunder the local residents' rice and cattle and fight off the national resistance forces, in an area south of the village at 0600 on 19 February. After 30 minutes of fighting, the invading troops were dispersed and forced to retreat. We killed five enemy soldiers and wounded 18 others; and we seized 15 AK rifles, three B-40 rocket launchers, a B-41 rocket launcher, a 60-mm mortar, a field radio, and some ammunition. On the same day, the people and NADK attacked and routed Vietnamese communist puppet soldiers at the checkpoints on the stretch of Route 3 between L'ang and Phnum Thvea in Chhuk District. We destroyed two military tank trucks and killed two enemy soldiers and wounded five others. The Vietnamese communist puppets and two-headed government on 11 February mustered and sent 300 soldiers from the 9th and 6th Regiments of the Province and District of Battambang and the 5th Military Region to plunder property of the residents living in a Democratic Kampuchea-controlled area east of Battambang. They attacked us from two Directions: The first was from Pralai Thmei to Ta Hen along the Stoeng Chas River and the second was from Khvang to (Rohai Chhak Tea). The people and NADK, however, ambushed, routed, and forced the invading forces to retreat. We killed five enemy soldiers and wounded eight others.
