January 21, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

PRESIDENT REAGAN'S FAREWELL ADDRESS

In his farewell address, President Reagan's closing comments concern the view from his White House window. He sees a "shining city on the hill . . . more prosperous, more secure and happier than it was eight years ago."

In easy sight of the White House are people living in the park; Washington has achieved the "honor" of being No. 1 in violent deaths, due, no doubt, to the huge increase of drug use in that city.

I'd like to have the name of the stuff they use to clean the White House windows.

WILFRED COUZIN

Laguna Niguel
