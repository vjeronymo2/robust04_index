BFN [Summary] Ukraine became a member of the Commonwealth of Danube Lands [Spivdruzhnist Dunayskykh Zemel] on 23 June. A session of the Commonwealth took place near Budapest on 23 June, where Ukraine was represented by Odessa Oblast. The issues of losses caused by UN sanctions against former Yugoslavia, the economy, the environment, and joint action by Danube lands in the areas of sport and tourism were examined at the session. Delegations from Germany, Austria, the Czech and Slovak Republics, Romania, and Bulgaria took part. Ruslan Bodelan, people's deputy of Ukraine and chairman of Odessa Oblast Council, who headed the Ukrainian delegation, said at Budapest airport: "This is another step toward our state's claim to the possibility of resolving issues jointly with our counterparts, with other countries. This especially concerns use of the Danube artery as a river, along which cargoes can and should be transported with the help of our Danube Shipping Company. It is about resolving problems of the ecological state of the Danube, preserving our reservations and national parks, and developing national culture and national traditions."
