931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / Technology: Sheffield City Council - Software at Work COMPANY SNAPSHOT Nature of business: Sheffield City Council provides services to 501,000 people in an area of 36,000 hectares. It is run by 87 councillors (known as members). The city hosts two universities, which increase the population during term-time by 25,000. The care of 250 schools are included in its educational duties, and its 76,000 council houses make it one of the biggest property owners in the country. Employees: 26,000. Turnover: The council has a revenue budget of Pounds 700,000 per annum to pay for the provision of services, and a capital budget of Pounds 50m to pay for the improvements of housing stock, schools, etc, on an asset-base of Pounds 10bn Key personnel: Pamela Gordon, chief executive; Malcolm Newman, city treasurer and deputy ceo; Wendy Miller, head of information technology; Alan Platt, GIS project manager. Property services manager Jill Bungay is programme director for the first two phases of the GIS. TECHNOLOGY FILE Software: Three bespoke geographical applications were developed by Unisys for Sheffield, using the the ARGIS fourth-generation language: Terrier, the land register, Highways (live September 93), and Cartography (live in May 93). Sheffield IT services has developed the Land Charges application in-house. The database used to store references is Oracle. Hardware: A pair of Unix-based Solborne 2000 servers, supporting seven PCs and seven 17-inch Sun and Solborne workstations, connected on a fibre-optic network. Supplier: Unisys specified the hardware and provided consultancy and development. Data capture included text and graphics, provisional quotes, and specifications, and was subcontracted to Cork-based agency QC. Other systems: Existing operational systems use a mixture of hardware, largely ICL, including an SX 580-20 mainframe and 30 Unix-based minis. Operational applications on housing, rents, etc, will be accessible via the GIS. Cost: Hardware is leased over a five-year period at Pounds 160,000. Development and consultancy cost Pounds 140,000. Data capture, budgeted at Pounds 300,000, now on a fixed price contract of Pounds 500,000. Estimated savings - on map-handling and salaries over a five-year period - are estimated at Pounds 335,000.
