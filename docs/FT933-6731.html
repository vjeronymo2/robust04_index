930826 FT 26 AUG 93 / Fresh call to reject Sheehy A CHIEF constable yesterday called on Mr Michael Howard, the home secretary, to reject the recommendations of the Sheehy report on police reforms. Mr John Over, chief constable of Gwent, urged Mr Howard instead to set up a royal commission on the role of police into the next century with a detailed focus on the whole criminal justice system. 'It will take a courageous home secretary but, if all that is done, then perhaps, as we all wish, we can have a well-trained, well-motivated, proud police service which will stand the tide of criminality,' he said. Mr Over was speaking at a Police Federation rally in Newport, south Wales, attended by officers from throughout Wales and the west country to protest at the Sheehy report and the white paper on the police.
