BFN [Unattributed report: "DOXA: 66 Percent of Italians Trust the Head of Government"] [Text] Rome -- Sixty-six percent of the Italians trust Silvio Berlusconi as prime minister, according to a poll taken by DOXA prior to the European elections and published only yesterday. Those interviewed said that they had "a lot" (27 percent) or "sufficient" (39 percent) trust in the present head of the government, who in this way achieved an approval rating of 66 percent. Eleven percent of those interviewed had no trust in him and 18 percent "little," a dissapproval rating of 29 percent. This makes Berlusconi the prime minister who, since 1992, has inspired most trust in Italians. Previously Carlo Azeglio Ciampi achieved a 60 percent approval rating in May 1993, which fell however to 51 percent in September and 49 percent in January of this year.
