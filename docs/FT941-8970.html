940217 FT 17 FEB 94 / Barclays rapped over man's suicide A widow said yesterday that she was considering suing Barclays Bank after an inquest returned a verdict of suicide on her husband. Stephen Langley killed himself after Barclays Bank demanded the return of his cheque book and card because making a loan repayment would have made his account overdrawn by Pounds 72, Birmingham coroners court heard. The inquest hear that his monthly wage of Pounds 943 had not been credited to his Coventry account because of a delay in the clearing system. 'Mr Langley was harried in a way quite unsuitable in the circumstances for a comparatively small amount of money,' said Mr Richard Whittington, the coroner. He had been depressed before and the bank's letters may have tipped the balance. The inquest heard that Mr Langley had struggled to repay several previous instalments of the Pounds 1,200 loan and had pledged to remain in credit after bank counselling. Barclays said: 'Knowing what we know, we believe we acted as sympathetically as possible in the circumstances and given the same situation again we would do the same.'
