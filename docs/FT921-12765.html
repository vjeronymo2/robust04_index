920124 FT 24 JAN 92 / Lloyd's bar to BCCI deal refused A NEW YORK judge yesterday rejected a request from Lloyd's of London to halt a recent Dollars 550m settlement of federal fraud and racketeering charges against the Bank of Credit and Commerce International (BCCI), Alan Friedman reports from New York. The London-based insurance institution, with other BCCI creditors, had tried this month to block the settlement on the grounds that it would not leave BCCI sufficient funds to pay creditors' claims. Lloyd's had filed its own lawsuit, under US racketeering statutes, alleging that it suffered losses on coffee transactions due to fraudulent behaviour by BCCI. Judge John Keenan ruled yesterday that the plea accord was 'a precisely written, scrupulously constructed and rigorously negotiated document.'
