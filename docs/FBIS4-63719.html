BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Viktor Demidenko and Mikhail Melnik] [Text] Kiev 24 May TASS -- "The negotiations with Crimean parliament deputies were constructive, and I am grateful to both delegations which have managed to reach the best possible way out," head of the Ukrainian parliamentary delegation Boris Oleynik told ITAR-TASS here today. The sides agreed to recommend the Ukrainian and Crimean parliaments to form a bilateral working group to discuss the negotiations' proposals. The Ukrainian parliamentary delegation called on the Crimean deputies to consider a suspended validity of the decisions, which restored the 1992 Constitution loosening the Crimea's links with Ukraine, for three days. Crimean Parliament Speaker Sergey Tsekov told a news conference upon the negotiations completion that the "Republic of the Crimea does not plan to separate from Ukraine. This can be said only by those who know nothing about the content of the Crimean legislative acts". In his opinion, a single legislative act should be adopted to achieve eternal stability in Ukrainian-Crimean relations. This may be an agreement between Ukraine and the Crimea, and the working group should discuss this problem.
