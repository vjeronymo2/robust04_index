931027 FT 27 OCT 93 / Coup falters in Burundi A military coup in Burundi appeared to be faltering yesterday after army generals asked the prime minister to come out of hiding and form a new civilian government, writes Leslie Crawford from Nairobi. But there were doubts about the army's true intentions and surviving members of the former government did not abandon their refuge in the French embassy in Bujumbura. Speaking from the embassy, Ms Sylvie Kinigi, the prime minister, called on Burundi's military to return to their barracks. Her address, broadcast on state radio, announced an end to the dusk-to-dawn curfew. She said the borders and international airport would re-open soon. Army generals on Monday asked her to form a new government, blaming the coup and the assassination of President Melchior Ndadaye on a handful of mutinous soldiers. But diplomats abroad said they believed that those now disowning the coup had been its architects.
