Language: English Article Type:BFN [From BTA press review] [Text] Sofia, March 16 (BTA) -- The political problems resulting from Prime Minister Lyuben Berov's illness are in the highlights of today's press. KONTINENT writes that Mr. Aleksandur Pramatarski, vice president of the Democratic Party in the Union of Democratic Forces (SDS), told its National Coordinating Council yesterday that a cabinet crisis is to be expected on March 21. The commentary goes on to say that the problem with the cabinet will most probably be solved before the national conference of the SDS in May and the new government is likely to be headed by Georgi Markov of the Democratic Party, who recently left the parliamentary SDS to become an independent MP. This situation is another godsend for the SDS, reads the commentary in KONTINENT. Yesterday President Zhelev and SDS leader Filip Dimitrov had lunch together but refused to comment on their meeting, writes 24 CHASA, quoting the Balkan News Agency. Actually, the SDS is not seeking to provoke a cabinet crisis, or is at least trying to postpone it because it is not ready for elections, reads an article in STANDART NEWS. "A diseased government cannot run a diseased state," writes the Bulgarian Agrarian Party's daily ZEMEDELSKO ZNAME. Now there is no one responsible for this country's government, says a commentary in the SDS daily DEMOKRATSIYA headlined "BSP Alone Is Blind to the Crisis". EKSPRES writes that the prime minister's wife told BSP leader Zhan Videnov over the phone that Professor Berov hopes to return to his office soon. The Socialist DUMA daily writes that the prime minister's illness has made the SDS put off its national conference until May. The same daily quotes the deputy chairman of the National Coordinating Council of the SDS, Mr. Stefan Sofiyanski, as saying that Deputy Prime Minister Valentin Karabashev and the acting Prime Minister Evgeni Matinchev are squabbling while this country is suffocating in a tightening economic knot. [Sofia Khorizont Radio Network in Bulgarian at 1100 GMT on 16 March reports two statements by a spokesperson of the SDS National Coordinating Council and by presidential spokesman Valentin Stoyanov, both denying that President Zhelyu Zhelev and SDS leader Filip Dimitrov held any meeting in Sofia on 15 May. Stoyanov is reported to have said that "the Balkan News Agency report on any meeting between President Zhelev and Filip Dimitrov is incorrect from beginning to end."]
