BFN [Report by Marta Marina Gonzalez and Arquimedes Gonzalez] [Text] Santiago Rivas Betancourt is 27 years old and allegedly one of the terrorists who were supposed to kill Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina for $400,000. He told BARRICADA: "I have never had anything to do with such a plan. I went to Honduras to buy spare parts. We are clean. We did not plan any murder. I am incapable of becoming involved in such a plan," he has said, expressing his willingness to appear before police authorities to clarify the entire situation, because, he said "I am innocent." Betancourt said he did in fact travel to Honduras with Luis Rene Quintero Gonzalez and Jose Arguello Hernandez at the beginning of March. They went to buy spare automotive parts. When they arrived in Honduras, he added, they were robbed at the San Miguel market in Comayaguela and "left penniless." They decided to go to the Nicaraguan Consulate. The ambassador gave them 100 lempiras so they could return to Nicaragua. He recalled they had requested a 90-day multiple entry permit. After the robbery, he said, "I had to ask an uncle, who lives in Honduras, for thirty lempiras to complete the return fare and then travel back to Nicaragua alone." He admitted meeting Luis Rene Quintero Gonzalez, the alleged informant, by pure chance. He met Jose Arguello Hernandez, another alleged plotter, when they were cell mates serving time at Esteli Penitentiary. Santiago Rivas Betancourt said he was arrested last year for insulting a law officer. Unofficially it is reported, however, he served time for illegal possession of weapons and drugs. He also said he was a former member of the Sandinist People's Army, where he served as a battalion chief in the Army's 5th Infantry Company, in the country's first region. Betancourt lost his left hand in a mine explosion, just before being discharged. This happened in 1990, when he switched to civilian life. Unofficial reports state Luis Rene Quintero Gonzalez, the alleged informant of the "plot," has an automotive body shop near Bello Horizonte Rotonda. None of the mechanics working in that area was able to give any information about Quintero. They did say, however, the police had been there at 1830 looking for the body shop and its alleged owner. A reliable government source has confirmed to BARRICADA the police were on Quintero Gonzalez' trail. They had not yet arrested him when we went to press. Unofficial reports state Quintero lived in the neighborhood of Fundeci, Managua. It was impossible to contact him there either.
