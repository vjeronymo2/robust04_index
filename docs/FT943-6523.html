940829 FT 29 AUG 94 / Arts: Today's Television If you are not keen on either cosy middlebrow nostalgia or the stuff endlessly extruded these days by Hollywood, then you might be best advised to ignore television today and opt instead for a restaurant or the cinema. If cash flow is sticky you could try a nice long bath and lots of radio. Those with 3 1/2 hours to spare can hear the Glyndebourne 'Don Giovanni' in BBC Proms 1994 (7.00 Radio 3). Or at 7.45 Radio 4 offers Lillian Hellman's 1935 play The Children's Hour, about accusations of lesbianism in an American girls' school. If you are keen on cosy middlebrow nostalgia then you are in clover. Following on from Saturday's tribute to Lew Grade, the two BBC channels offer a host of old ATV programmes beginning at 5.00 with episodes from four series: Sir Francis Drake (1962); The Buccaneers (1956) with Robert Shaw as the reformed pirate Dan Tempest; The Adventures Of William Tell (1958); and, most successful of them all, The Adventures of Robin Hood with Richard Greene in the title role. At 10.30 BBC1 screens an episode of The Saint starring Roger Moore, followed by Jason King, the Peter Wyngarde series (11.20).
