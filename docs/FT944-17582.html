941007 FT 07 OCT 94 / Arts: Today's Television The relationship between Frasier Crane and his hapless brother Nyles, laden with psycho-drama even in its most banal exchanges, also happens to provide the funniest half-hour on television. This week's Frasier (Channel 4, 10.00) is the last in the current series: the two siblings meet over a coffee and wonder if they are really happy. Why is it that only American sitcoms manage to tap the comedy inherent in all great philosophical questions? Sue Cook explores the world of the paranormal in Out of This World (BBC1, 9.30), a collection of reconstructed case histories considered by a studio audience. Among the strange-but-perhaps-true tales is the story of the Hull housewife who foresaw the Marchioness riverboat disaster, an assassination attempt on the Pope and a woman who recalls the details of a 16th century murder. Chlorine capers aplenty when Burt Lancaster plays a middle-aged man who suddenly appears beside a swimming pool, and decides to swim home to Connecticut via the pools of his friends in Frank Perry's diverting 1968 cult movie The Swimmer (Channel 4, 11.10).
