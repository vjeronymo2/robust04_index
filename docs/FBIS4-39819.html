BFN [YONHAP report from Kiev] [Text] North Korean Foreign Minister Kim Yong-nam said on 8 June that if the United States agrees to resume the third round of DPRK-U.S. talks, North Korea will allow the International Atomic Energy Agency to inspect its nuclear facilities in Yongbyon. In a news conference after winding up his Ukraine visit, Kim Yong-nam said that if the DPRK-U.S. talks are resumed, North Korea will guarantee the inspection of the nuclear facilities, including the test, measurement, and preservation of the fuel rods. Kim Yong-nam also said that the United States is rejecting the resumption of the third round of the DPRK-U.S. talks by carrying out stalling tactics. He added that since the United States and North Korea are the parties directly involved in this matter, the only way to resolve this matter is through the United States and North. He pointed out that the nuclear issue was caused by the United States and that the threat against the DPRK stems from the United States.
