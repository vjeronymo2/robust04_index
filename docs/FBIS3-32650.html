Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Port-au-Prince, 13 Jan [date as received] (AFP) -- Last night in Port-au-Prince a powerful explosion destroyed several clandestine fuel warehouses. An AFP correspondent reported on Sunday that the explosion claimed no victims. The explosions and resulting fire were caused by an irresponsible smoker. Dozens of buildings were damaged, including a small 20-room hotel. The fire had not yet been controlled this morning, six hours after the initial blast, which occurred when a tanker truck exploded. At the site, it was confirmed that firefighters have limited resources with which to work. The petroleum products stored at the warehouses on Cesar Street, near the cathedral in the Haitian capital, had been brought in from the Dominican Republic. Gasoline sells for 22 Haitian dollars ($7.5) [unit not given] on the black market. The UN ordered an oil embargo against Haiti last October to pressure the military government which seized power in 1991 by overthrowing Jean Bertrand Aristide, the democratically elected president of Haiti.
