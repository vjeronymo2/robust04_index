930929 FT 29 SEP 93 / Letters to the Editor: Ethics code needed for politicians With reference to your story 'Interest in ethics codes increases' (September 25), similar codes are likely to be introduced for National Health Service board members in the next few months. A recent Gallup poll showed that voters believe their political leaders to be dishonest, too predictable in what they say, and lacking in all humility. The German election results show far right parties recording the strongest election showing in years. The Poles have voted for a return of communism. The British electorate is losing faith in politicians. Voters are alienated from existing parties and are relieving their frustrations by backing extremist organisations such as the BNP. The Nazis came to power in Germany by a democratic vote of a similar proportion to that of the BNP in Tower Hamlets. The situation is serious, and urgent measures are required. I would propose that an all-party commission should examine the adoption of a comprehensive code of ethics for anyone who seeks election to office. The present squabbling between Labour and the Liberal Democrats over the Tower Hamlets election result might have been avoided, had there been such a code. It would prevent a situation where the prime minister, drumming up business abroad, is distracted by sniping at home. Sigmund Sternberg, member of the council, Institute of Business Ethics, Star House, Grafton Road, London NW 5 4BD
