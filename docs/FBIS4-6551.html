BFN [Station commentary] [Text] Ukrainian Foreign Minister Anatoliy Maksymovych arrived in Hanoi on Thursday for an official visit to Vietnam at the invitation of Vietnamese Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam. Radio Voice of Vietnam reviews relations between Vietnam and Ukraine over the past years. The visit to Vietnam by the Ukrainian foreign minister is of great significance to both countries as this is the first visit to Vietnam of a foreign minister from Ukraine, a republic of the former Soviet Union. It is a start in relations between the two countries. Following a foreign policy to be friends with all nations in the world, Vietnam has established diplomatic relations with all the member countries of the Commonwealth of Independent States after the collapse of the former Soviet Union. Diplomatic relations with Ukraine was established in January 1992. Today, the bilateral relations between Vietnam and Ukraine has developed in many fields. The number of agreements to be signed by the two countries with a view to exploiting each other's potential will help the economic development of each country. They include the agreement on economic relations; trade, military, technical, and aviation cooperation; a protocol on foreigners; and an agreement on the travelling of citizens in each country. Vietnam and Ukraine have also signed other documents such as the agreement on the encouragement and protection of investment; the agreement on the avoidance of double taxation; a consular agreement; and an agreement on cooperation in culture, science, and education. Both nations are now promoting the trading ties. In 1992, the two-way trade turnover was only $15 million. In 1993, the turnover was also lightly increased. Vietnam mainly imported dairy products, steel, and construction materials, while Ukraine imported agriculture products and consumers goods. Ukraine is assisting Vietnam in building the Yali Hydroelectric Power Plant and another project with a total capital of $18 million. This visit by the Ukrainian foreign minister is a good opportunity for the two countries to join efforts and find measures to further strengthen the cooperative economic relations. The visit is also a chance for the two countries to exchange experiences in economic development of each country. The Vietnamese people as well as the Ukrainian people hope that this visit to Vietnam by Minister Anatoliy Maksymovych will make contribution to the development of relations between the two countries.
