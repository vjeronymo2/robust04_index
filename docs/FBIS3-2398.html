Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Changsha, March 6 (XINHUA) -- Central China's Hunan Province, a major agricultural province of the country, has given priority to construction of infrastructure projects, aiming to improve its investment environment. The province invested 86.2 percent of its funds in energy, transport, telecommunications, water conservancy and raw material industries in 1993. By the end of last year, the province invested 5.762 billion yuan (about 700 million U.S. dollars) in key infrastructure projects, 2.3 times more than in 1992. Construction of some projects has been completed and they have gone into operation, including Dayong Airport, three telecommunication projects and a bridge. This year, the province plans to have 24 new projects, nine in transport, four in energy industry and two in telecommunications, with total investment of 14.5 billion yuan (about 1.6 billion U.S. dollars). The province has made efforts to attract and raise funds for the work. It has issued bonds and stocks and imported foreign funds.
