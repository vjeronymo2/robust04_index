BFN [Excerpts] President Mario Soares is going to try to talk to Jonas Savimbi at the request of Angolan President Jose Eduardo dos Santos. Soares met Eduardo dos Santos after Nelson Mandela's swearing-in ceremony, and he has given details of the conversation. [Begin recording] [Soares] [passage omitted] I had a very positive conversation with Eduardo dos Santos. I can say that it was probably the most useful conversation I have had with him. First, I found him very perceptive, I found him very conscious of the situation, and very willing to move toward a cease-fire. I also think this is the time for a cease-fire in Angola. I also spoke to Butrus-Ghali. President Eduardo dos Santos asked me to speak to Savimbi, which I will try and do. [Unidentified correspondent] Are you going to intercede? [Soares] No, I am not going to intercede, I am going to talk. I am going to try to talk -- it is not easy to talk to Savimbi, I have no direct channel of communication. He is the one who sometimes telephone me. I will see if I can ask him to telephone me. I would like to talk to him because this is definitely the time for peace. [end recording]
