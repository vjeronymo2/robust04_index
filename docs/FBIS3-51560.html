Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Article by Juan Manuel Diaz C.] [Text] The Molirena [Nationalist Liberal Republican Movement] party political committee decided unanimously to support [former Comptroller General] Ruben Dario Carles' presidential candidacy after negotiations to bring about an alliance with the Arnulfist Party [PA] failed. Molirena President Alfredo Ramirez indicated that after several hours of deliberations the party's political committee decided to select Carles as the presidential candidate because he represented the best option. Ramirez stated that, unfortunately, conversations with the PA had not progressed. For several months now Molirena has been stating its intention to participate in the May 1994 elections with its own candidate. At the same time, Ramirez pointed out that Molirena also seeks to occupy the first vice presidency of any ticket that supports Carles' candidacy. In order to accomplish this, it has designated a commission to prepare a list of nominees from which Carles can chose the person most suited to occupy the position of first vice president. A Molirena source reported that possible first vice president nominees include former Labor Minister Jorge Ruben Rosas, former Housing Minister Guillermo Quijano, and National Assembly President Arturo Vallarino. According to the same source, Carles could opt for Guillermo Quijano as first vice president and Tomas Herrera, member [and president] of the Civic Renewal Party as second vice president. As of today, Ruben Dario Carles' candidacy will be supported by three parties: National Renewal, Civic Renewal, and Molirena. Meanwhile, First Vice President Guillermo Ford indicated that he will continue to carry out his duties as a member of President Endara's cabinet. At the same time he accepted the Molirena political committee's decision and reiterated that he plans to continue being a member of said party. Ford said that he will continue serving democracy until 1 September 1994 when the new government is expected to be inaugurated. The vice president stipulated that together with President Endara he expects to guarantee clean and fair elections as an aftermath of the administration elected in 1989. On the other hand, it was disclosed that those cabinet ministers who are Molirena members have no plans to resign their current positions and indicated that they will step down if it is specifically requested by President Endara. Molirena is expected to hold its national convention today at the ATLAPA Convention Center and to ratify the decision made by the party's political committee. The party is expected to hold internal elections on 30 January to select candidates for legislators, corregimiento representatives, mayors, and council representatives.
