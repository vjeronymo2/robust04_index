BFN [Text] Vilnius, 13 May, BNS -- Lithuanian Prime Minister Adolfas Slezevicius on Thursday [12 May] named five candidates for ministers of economics, forestry, environment protection, culture, and education. The candidacies were reportedly submitted to President Algirdas Brazauskas for consideration. The premier proposed the social and economic policy adviser to the president, Aleksandras Vasiliauskas, to fill the vacant post of economics minister, and nominated the director of the Dubrava Forestry Research Institute, Albertas Vasiliauskas, for the post of forestry minister. Three other nominations are related to the law on government, which is likely to be adopted next week. The government law provides for 18 ministries instead of the present 16. Slezevicius proposed the deputy director general of the Environment Protection Department, Bronius Bradauskas, to head the new Environment Protection Ministry. According to the law on government, the Ministry of Education and Culture would be divided into two separate ministries. Dainius Trinkunas, who now heads the ministry, announced his intention to resign. The director general of the Vilnius Philharmonic, Gintautas Kevisas, was nominated for minister of culture. The post of education minister has been offered to the head of the Pedagogics and Psychology Department of the Kaunas Physical Culture Institute, Kestutis Miskinis.
