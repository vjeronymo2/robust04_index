930114 FT 14 JAN 93 / Attack on Iraq: Saddam calls for holy war against the west IRAQI President Saddam Hussein today called for a holy war against western aggressors and urged his air defences to strike back after US-led raids against southern Iraq, James Whittington and Reuter report from Baghdad. Addressing the nation on state television early this morning after the air attacks, he said any US or western aircraft in Iraqi airspace was a target. The Iraqi leader made no mention of casualties in the raids, but later the Iraqi news agency said one soldier and three civilians had been killed and four wounded in the province of Basra. 'Fight on against the aggressors. . . so that Iraqi airspace from the north to the south, from the east to the west, is turned into fiery lava,' he said. As the president spoke there appeared to be few signs in Baghdad that the capital was preparing for attack. In a five star hotel a newly wed couple danced the night away apparently convinced that there was little or no chance of a renewed outbreak of full-scale war. Dressed in a field marshal's uniform, Mr Saddam said: 'They want, through their vicious aggression, to impose the will of colonialism and rape the will of nations. They have come to say to Iraqis: You have no choice but to give in to evil.' The opposition Iraqi National Congress (INC), made up of liberal Arabs and Kurds, last night expressed support for allied military action against Iraq, writes Gareth Smyth in London. 'Any military strike that weakens Saddam Hussein will be welcomed by the Iraqi people,' said spokesman Ahmed Chalabi. 'The noose must be tightened around him.'
