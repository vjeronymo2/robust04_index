November 28, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

READING

I glanced at test scores of schoolchildren in Los Angeles County and felt great pain in my eyeballs. These youngsters represent the future of America. The prospect fills me with despair.

Let me recommend my solution -- a revolutionary reading program for the first 12 grades of school. Every student would be required to read at least 10 books during the summer months in order to advance to the next grade level. Validation of book reports would involve teacher and librarian cooperation.

Reading is the key to lifelong education. Benjamin Franklin and Abe Lincoln had a total of four years of schooling between them, but they became readers, and that made the difference.

In 12 summers each student will have read at least 120 books. Result: vastly improved ratings in writing, spelling and other areas. In three years, Los Angeles students will zoom up to mediocrity; in eight years they will breathe the rarefied air of top quartile in nationwide tests.

If this program sounds too radical for the school system, then let one innovative school principal try to experiment and lead the way.

GEORGE W. FEINSTEIN

Altadena
