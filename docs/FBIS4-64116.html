BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Albert Kochetkov] [Text] Tbilisi May 26 TASS -- Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze said: "Since Georgia normalised relations with Russia, and President Boris Yeltsin and myself announced to the world that our countries are friends and good neighbours, it has become possible to build the republic's strong armed forces as guarantors of Georgia's independence and territorial integrity." The statement, made at the close of the military parade in Tbilisi's Central Square to mark independence day, was born out by the march-past of infantry, naval and paratrooper units sporting new uniforms and armed with the latest Kalashnikov automatic guns, the roll-past of armoured vehicles, artillery guns and anti-aircraft missiles. A squad of the Su-25 attack planes assembled at the Tbilisi plant performed aerobatics in the sky above the Central Square during the parade.
