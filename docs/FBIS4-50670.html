BFN [Text] Brussels, May 19 (XINHUA) -- A senior Chinese leader stressed here today that China is willing to make joint efforts with Belgium to push forward trade and economic cooperation between the two countries. Li Ruihuan, chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, made these remarks while meeting with Belgian Minister of External Trade Robert Urbain. Li said that Sino-Belgian trade and economic relations have been developed smoothly in recent years, and that the economies of the two countries are mutually supplementary, thus offering great potential and a bright future for trade and economic cooperation. Li expressed China's willingness to make joint efforts with Belgium to push bilateral cooperation to a new stage, Urbain said he has been deeply impressed by the remarkable economic achievements in China. He also expressed his appreciation for the realistic and prudent policies of the Chinese government in developing the country's economy. Belgium puts great emphasis on its economic relationship with China and is willing to enhance friendly cooperation, Urbain said, adding that he believes Li's visit will lead to the further development of trade and economic cooperation. In the evening, the Chinese leader attended a welcoming banquet held jointly by the Federation of Belgian Enterprises and the Belgium-China Economic and Trade Confederation. Li told the guests that Belgium has become China's sixth largest trading partner, and that the joint-ventures with Belgian investment have introduced many advanced technologies into China. China welcome more investments from Belgium, he added. Also speaking at the banquet, Georges Jacobs, president of the Federation of Belgian Enterprises, said China is now at the world's center stage in development, and China's rapid growth has paved the way for the further development of bilateral cooperation. With growing interest in China, Belgium now wishes to enhance economic ties, Jacobs added. Li Ruihuan is on the last leg of a five-nation European tour, which has already taken him to Finland, Sweden, Norway and Denmark.
