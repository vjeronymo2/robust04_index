BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 28 KYODO -- The new farm minister Thursday [28 April] called for a cut in the price of Japanese rice. Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Minister Mutsuki Kato made the call in his first news conference after assuming his new post earlier in the day. He said that in determining the government's purchase price of rice, harvested in 1994, the difference between domestic and overseas prices should be narrowed.
