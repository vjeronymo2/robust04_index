November 23, 1990, Friday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: BETWEEN WORKOUTS, TYSON LIKES TO PARTY LIKE A CHAMP

MIKE TYSON is training at Atlantic City's Trump Plaza. The former heavyweight champ fights Alex Stewart on Dec. 9. Tyson, however, does believe that all work and no play makes Mike a dull boy.

Tyson recently caught a performance by the O'Jays at Trop World. The group spotted the big guy in the audience and pulled him up onstage, introducing him as "the newest member of our group." The champ then joined them in a song.

Before this, Tyson turned up at the Trump Plaza showcase for the former Miss America, Suzette Charles. Suzette, who has never wanted to reign alone like Garbo, told her audience that she and the former champ were "more than just good friends." That may indeed be true, but it wasn't Suzette who then accompanied Mike on an upscale boutique shopping spree on the Boardwalk wherein he spent $40,000 on clothes for himself and an unidentified lady friend. Tyson has also been seen cruising Atlantic City in his bright red Ferrari, a tall and gorgeous blond beside him.

I love to see young people having a good time, don't you?

LYRICIST FRED Ebb and composer John Kander ("Cabaret," "Chicago," "Flora the Red Menace") are rewriting their 1977 Broadway musical "The Act" for the talented Dorothy Loudon. Liza Minnelli originated the role of a famous singer making a Las Vegas comeback, involved with an older man. She won a Tony award for her tour de force performance.

Among the revisions for the new star is a change to show an older woman's infatuation for a younger man.

THE CARNEGIE DELI in Beverly Hills is booked solid for a private party to be held the night of Jan. 20, 1996. That's the day George Burns will turn 100.
