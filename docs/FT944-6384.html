941130 FT 30 NOV 94 / Hoechst signs contract to set up joint venture in Shanghai Germany's Hoechst chemicals group has signed a contract to set up a Dollars 29m joint venture in Shanghai to make polyester products for the building industry, reports Christopher Parkes from Frankfurt. The group's local holding subsidiary, Hoechst China Investment, based in Beijing, will take a 60 per cent stake in the new company. China Worldbest Corp of Shanghai will hold 25 per cent and the remaining 15 per cent will be owned a Beijing company, China New Building Materials. A new factory, due to open in 1996, will produce 4,500 tonnes a year of Trevira Spunbond, a material used mainly as a base for bitumenised roofing and flooring products.
