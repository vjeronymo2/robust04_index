BFN [Text] Lagos, June 17 (AFP) -- A court here Friday [17 June] rejected a charge of treason against Beko Ransome-Kuti, national president of the Campaign for Democracy, but he was refused immediate bail. Ransome-Kuti, who was arrested on June 9, heads an umbrella group for about 40 pro-democracy and human rights organisations. The Federal High Court rejected on technical grounds a charge of treasonable felony and intent to remove the head of state by unconstitutional means. Consideration of a bail application was postponed until Tuesday. Ransome-Kuti has been spearheading a campaign to remove the military junta from power.
