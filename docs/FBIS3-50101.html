Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] London, January 21 (AFP) -- In The Hague late Friday [21 January] UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali said he hoped London and Paris would not withdraw their troops. "If they will withdraw their troops, I will have to find other member states who will be ready to replace them," he said. "The role of the UN is to maintain peace, is to contain a dispute, to try to find a peaceful settlement to the dispute. If you just say they can continue to kill each other, I will say that this is an abdication," Butrus-Ghali said. He also expressed confidence in EC mediator Lord Owen, whose replacement was demanded Thursday by the European parliament, and UN representative Stoltenberg. "I have no criticism, on the contrary. I want to thank them for the wonderful work they're doing. It needs a lot of patience, of perseverance. I support them and express them my gratitude," he said. [passage omitted]
