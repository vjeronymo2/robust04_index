940405 FT 05 APR 94 / Arts: Today's Television A promising, and packed, evening. In the Young Musician Of The Year, the string players compete for a place in the concerto final (7.40 BBC2). Channel 4's series The Brief is becoming compulsory viewing: last week's programme about the Serious Fraud Office was eye-opening (8.30). That is followed by an 85-minute interview with Dennis Potter in Without Walls. In 30 years Potter has created a uniquely impressive body of work including 'The Singing Detective' (9.00). In the first of three programmes Network First tells how the US slowly came to terms with 'the enemy within', the Mafia. The series focuses on the Gambino family, said to be the richest of New York's crime families, headed by Carlo, model for 'The Godfather' (10.40 ITV). Omnibus looks at the work of American choreographer Bill T. Jones whose 'Last Supper At Uncle Tom's Cabin' was concerned with racism, sexism, religion and Aids. Roger Vadim's 1956 film And God Created Woman, in which he introduced Brigitte Bardot to the world, is now more significant as a social document than as a movie (midnight BBC2).
