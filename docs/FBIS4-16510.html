BFN [Text] Prime Minister Sergey Tereshchenko held a news conference in Almaty today. Attending were his first deputy Akezhan Kazhegeldin, Economy Minister Mars Urkumbayev, Finance Minister Yerkeshbay Derbisov, and National Bank Chairman Daulet Sembayev. He stressed that rumors about the lack of a constructive governmental program of action were groundless and there was no necessity to adopt a new program. It was said that the program exists, is viable, and is working already. The president has endorsed the anticrisis measures program for 1993-95. At the parliament session the government will report about the progress of implementation of this program. The Cabinet of Ministers is working in conformity with the adopted plan. Its work is described in the statement on the economic policy for 1994 which was published. Besides, in conformity with the president's instruction, a group led by the first deputy prime minister was set up which is examining the alternative programs of economic development and reform in Kazakhstan, submitted by the opposition parties. Members of the government, answering questions, touched upon a number of topical problems in the implementation of these reforms. In particular, the measures to enhance the national currency were discussed, as well as state loans and credits, foreign financial aid for entrepreneurship, and the introduction of the new taxation system. Also discussed were enforceable enactments adopted of late on abolishing natural [as heard] allowances for some categories of the population, increase of minimum wages and pensions, and further state policy regarding the public welfare of citizens with low income.
