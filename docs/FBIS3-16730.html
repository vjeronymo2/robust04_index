Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" feature: By diplomatic correspondents Dmitriy Voskoboinikov, Igor Porshnev, Vitaliy Trubetskoy and others] [Text] The president's press service had a set of letters of support for Islam Karimov published in local media. Tashkent residents wrote that whatever Uzbekistan had accomplished was the result of the president's resolute activities "for the great cause of making the republic one of the most advanced countries of the world." The authors of the letters "as Uzbek subjects" wanted to stand by the Uzbek leader to help him. They emphasized that Karimov "worked self-sacrificingly for peace and quiet in the Republic." The letters expressed indignation over "the activities of a bunch of slanderers who fled Uzbekistan and smuggled into it the publications that libel the Motherland." The authors of the letters called on the president to put an end to the activities of those who smuggled "slanderous pieces" into the Republic and "could not reconcile to Uzbekistan's future as a great state."
