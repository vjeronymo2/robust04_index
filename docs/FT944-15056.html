941020 FT 20 OCT 94 / Home Office sued on computer deal The Home Office last night confirmed that it is being sued for about Pounds 500,000 in compensation by two software houses which bid unsuccessfully for a Pounds 14m project to transfer 3m records of custodial convictions to the police national computer. According to Computing newspaper, Saztec Europe claims it was not told the duration of the project had been extended. Phoenix Interface Technology says its bid was not considered properly and fairly.
