Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Carlos Lage, vice president of the Council of State, will meet today in Brasilia with President Itamar Franco as part of his official visit to Brazil. The trip will conclude on 7 February. On 3 February, Lage meet with Foreign Minister Celso Amorim and other personalities of the Brazilian Government. Lage's visit is receiving extensive media coverage, particularly by Rio's O GLOBO which featured an interview, with written responses to questions submitted before the trip, in which Lage discusses various issues, including Cuba's economic situation. It was also announced that in an interview by AGENCIA BRASIL Lage pointed out that the U.S. blockade against Cuba has caused many difficulties and added that it is essential that Latin America reject this form of aggression. A PRENSA LATINA dispatch adds that during his meeting with Lage, the Brazilian foreign minister criticized the U.S. blockade against Cuba by rejecting unilateral measures by certain countries in violation of international law.
