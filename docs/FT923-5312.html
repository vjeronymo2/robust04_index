920902 FT 02 SEP 92 / Manila hopes rain will aid economy The Philippines economy was almost flat in the first half of the year because of electricity shortages and a drought in the south, Reuter reports from Manila. Officials said the economy may improve in the second half as the onset of rains had broken a drought covering southern Mindanao island and helped arrest the power crisis. Real gross national product grew by a slight 0.48 per cent, according to the government.
