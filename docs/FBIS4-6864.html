BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 22 April Evening Edition] [Text] The DPRK (North Korea) has softened its hardline stand on the nuclear issue by notifying the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) that it will allow IAEA inspectors to observe the replacement of fuel rods in an experimental reactor. Commenting on the North Korean proposal, Tsutomu Hata, deputy prime minister and foreign minister, said at a news conference on 22 April after a cabinet meeting that "this is something we have to welcome." At the news conference, the foreign minister also reiterated, "It is important for North Korea to explain things candidly [about its suspected nuclear weapons program] to the international community."
