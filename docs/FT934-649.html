931223 FT 23 DEC 93 / UK Company News: BAA chief makes Pounds 0.63m on sale of share options Sir John Egan, chief executive of BAA, yesterday made Pounds 628,000 on the exercise of share options in the airports operator. The company told the Stock Exchange that Sir John, whose remuneration last year totalled Pounds 768,000, had bought 261,500 ordinary shares at 384p each under the BAA senior employee share option scheme. After exercising his option, he subsequently sold 99,368 shares at Pounds 10.16 per share. The former chairman of Jaguar, who took over at BAA in 1991, still retains 167,946 ordinary shares in the company. Sir John also disclosed that he had a further 84,290 shares under option. Shares in the company - which operates Heathrow, Gatwick and Stansted airports - rose 11p to close at Pounds 10.24.
