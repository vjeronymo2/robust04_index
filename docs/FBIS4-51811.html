BFN [Text] Pusan, April 28 (YONHAP) -- The labor union of the Hanjin Heavy Industry Co. on Thursday seceded from the Federation of Korean Trade Unions (FKTU) in protest against the recommended wage increase of 5 percent for this year earlier agreed on between the FKTU and the Korea Employers Federation. It is the third major trade union to leave the FKTU. The two others are the Association of Hyundai Group Unions and the Council of Daewoo Group Unions. The Hanjin union decided on the secession in a vote participated by 46 union delegates. Of them, 41 voted for the withdrawal and two against. Three others abstained. "The wage increase recommended by the FKTU and Employers Federation is something that undermines the development of trade unions. The cessation reflects the anger of all our unionists at the recommended wage hike," a union official said.
