Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA March 27 -- General Secretary Do Muoi and his party arrived in Ho Chi Minh City this afternoon, concluding a 4-day official visit to Malaysia from March 24-27. On March 26, General Secretary Do Muoi and his party toured the state Melaka. In Melaka, the Vietnamese delegation call at the oil refinery Petronas Melaka of the Petronas Company. On this occasion, the leadership of Petronas Company gave a banquet in honour of General Secretary Do Muoi and his party. In the afternoon, the Vietnamese delegation called on the Teac-Malaysia company, and the cultural tourist attraction of village Zir Keroh in the suburbs of Melaka. Later, the delegation paid a visit to the Independence Memorial Museum and a number of historical relics in Melaka City. Also on March 26, Governor of Melaka State Ahmad Mahmud Shahabudin and Prime Minister Rahim Thamby Chik paid a courtesy visit to General Secretary Do Muoi. On this occasion, the Melaka Government gave a banquet in honour of the Vietnamese guests. Present at the banquet were Governor Ahmad Shhabudin, Prime Minister Rahim Thamby Chik, and a number of government's officials and businessmen. On his last day in Malaysia, General Secretary Do Muoi visited Kelang Port. Before leaving Kuala Lumpur, the Vietnamese were given a luncheon by Renong Group, whose companies are implementing investment or joint venture projects in Vietnam. Speaking to the businessmen, Mr. Do Muoi welcomed the group's investment projects in Vietnam and wished them success, thus contributing to the promotion of the friendly and cooperative relations between the two countries. Upon leaving Malaysia for home, General Secretary Do Muoi sent a message of thanks to Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad. The message says in part: `The fine results of the visit and the useful and constructive exchanges of views that we had with Your Excellency and the other excellencies have further affirmed our determination to promote the relations of friendship and multi-faceted cooperation between Vietnam and Malaysia in the interests of our two peoples, and of peace and development in the region and the world as a whole'.
