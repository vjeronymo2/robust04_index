Language: Finnish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Education Minister Riitta Uosukainen has been elected speaker of the parliament. In an election at a plenum in the parliament today, she received 107 votes, while Saara-Maria Paakkinen of the Social Democratic Party attracted 82 votes. In addition, Pentti Maeki-Hakola of the Coalition Party got four votes, and Mikko Pesaelae of the Center got one vote. Uosukainen became the first ever female chief speaker of the parliament. Paakkinen and Pesaelae will continue as deputy speakers. With the election of Uosukainen, the Coalition Party will carry out a reshuffle within its group of ministers. Under a proposal by the parliament group and the party leadership, the current Housing Minister Pirjo Rusanen should take over Uosukainen's post as education minister. Parliament deputy Ritva Laurila, chairman of Helsinki City Council, will become new housing minister. The ministerial reshuffle will be discussed on 10 February at meetings of the Coalition Party Board and parliament group. [passage omitted] President Mauno Koivisto will open the parliamentary session of 1994 tomorrow.
