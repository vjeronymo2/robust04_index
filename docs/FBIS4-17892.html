BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Dmitriy Zelenin and Oleg Velichko] [Text] Damascus, 27 Apr -- Oleg Soskovets, Russian first deputy prime minister, who is here on an official visit, and Syrian Defense Minister Mustafa Talas, today signed an agreement on military-technical cooperation. After the signing of the document, the Russian first deputy prime minister said that the Russian president considers it essential to resume cooperation between the two states that has been partially lost of late. The signing of today's agreement, Oleg Soskovets noted, shows the desire of Moscow and Syria to engage in systematic cooperation in the military-technical sphere. Mustafa Talas appraised the agreement as "a first step in the right direction." He expressed the need for a restoration of the former relationship with Russia. In this respect, he particularly emphasized that Damascus "has strong friends in Moscow." "The agreement signed today is evidence of this," he said. As for the essence of the document, an ITAR-TASS correspondent was told by a well-informed source in the Russian delegation that it is evidence of the beginning of a new relationship. The two sides agreed to form a working commission, which will set about its work in a few weeks' time. Today, too, Oleg Soskovets had a three-hour meeting with Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad. According to the deputy prime minister, the Syrian head of state supported the main positions in the agreement on military-technical cooperation. After the signing of the agreement, Oleg Soskovets left by air for Moscow.
