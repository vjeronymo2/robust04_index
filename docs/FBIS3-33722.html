Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The prime minister left New Delhi this afternoon on a six-day visit to Switzerland and Germany. He was seen off at the airport by cabinet colleagues and senior officials. Mr. Narasimha Rao has been invited by the World Economic Forum to address the closing plenary of the symposium at Davos tomorrow. The prime minister is expected to apprise the world business community of the liberalization process in India. The theme for this year's Davos meet is redefining the basic assumptions of the world economy. The meeting of the forum, which began on Tuesday, is being attended by more than 20 heads of government and state. In all 800 company chief executives and 150 government representatives are attending the deliberations. From Davos, Mr. Rao will go on to Bonn on Wednesday on the second leg of his visit. He will hold talks with the German chancellor, Dr. Helmut Kohl. Both the leaders will address the Indo-German Business Conference. The prime minister will later go to Berlin where he will meet the German president.
