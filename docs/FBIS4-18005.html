BFN ["Ukrainian Presidential Directive on Urgent Measures for Eliminating the Consequences of the Chernobyl Catastrophe in Kiev Oblast's Polissya Rayon" issued in Kiev on 22 Apr] [Text] 1. Ukraine's Cabinet of Ministers must see to it that, until 1 September 1995, residents be evacuated from the populated areas of Kiev Oblast's Polissya Rayon that are categorized as zones of unquestionable (mandatory) evacuation. To do so, it is necessary, by 1 May 1994, to find a possibility for allocating additional capital amounting to 1 trillion karbovantsi from the Fund For Carrying Out Measures on Eliminating the Consequences of the Chernobyl Catastrophe and Social Protection of the Population in 1994 to the Kiev Oblast State Administration for building housing; --with the participation of the Kiev Oblast State Administration, to take urgent measures for a radical improvement of the material and technological provision of medical and educational institutions and for establishing telephone communications and vehicle traffic between the populated areas of Polissya Rayon and Kiev; --to elaborate and adopt, by 1 July 1994, a separate program for eliminating the consequences of the Chernobyl catastrophe in the populated areas of Polissya Rayon from which residents are not evacuated; --to submit, by 1 July 1994, for the consideration of Ukraine's Supreme Council, proposals on the creation of a single body for managing exclusion zones and for unquestionable (mandatory) evacuation, as well as on placing those citizens who had, on the day of the accident, permanently resided and continue to reside and work in populated areas situated in the zone of unquestionable (mandatory) evacuation, into category two of the participants in the liquidation of the accident at the Chernobyl Nuclear Electric Power Station [AES]. 2. Ukraine's Ministry for Issues of Protection of the Population from the Consequences of the Accident at the Chernobyl AES, with the participation of Ukraine's Academy of Sciences, Ukraine's Ministry of Agriculture and Foodstuffs, and the Kiev Oblast State Administration, must submit, by 1 July 1994, for the consideration of Ukraine's Cabinet of Ministers the following: --proposals on the procedure of using land in Polissya Rayon, proceeding from the intensity of radioactive contamination, the fertility of the soils, the demographic situation, and the production costs and demand for the rayon's produce; --on the expedience of preserving and maintaining buildings and structures, as well as on the further utilization of land in the exclusion zone. 3. To draw the attention of Ukraine's Minister of Agriculture and Foodstuffs Yu. Karasyk to the unsatisfactory fulfillment of Article 6 of Ukrainian Presidential Edict No. 348 of 21 August 1993 "On Additional Measures Regarding the Elimination of the Consequences of the Chernobyl Catastrophe" in what concerns the organization and control over the supply of clean foodstuffs to those zones that have been affected by the catastrophe at the Chernobyl AES and to oblige him to eradicate the existing shortcomings as soon as possible. 4. The State Control and Auditing Service at Ukraine's Ministry of Finance and Ukraine's Ministry of Internal Affairs must, by 15 June 1994, carry out an inspection of the utilization in 1992-94 of the funds that were allocated for the liquidation of the consequences of the Chernobyl catastrophe and submit the materials of the inspection to the Ukrainian Presidential Administration. Ukrainian President L. Kravchuk Kiev, 22 April 1994
