930707 FT 07 JUL 93 / Anger at remarks over hospital sites A FRESH controversy about the closure of some of London's hospitals has broken out after a leading firm of property advisers described them as 'the London development sites of the 90s'. Save, a conservation group, described comments by Knight Frank & Rutley, chartered surveyors, about development opportunities presented by hospital closures as 'outrageous' and showing 'complete insensitivity to the importance of the architecture of London hospitals'. It particularly criticised a description of the site occupied by St Thomas's opposite the Houses of Parliament as 'a prestigious residential development site with its unique picture postcard view'. Mr Stephan Miles Brown, head of residential developments at Knight Frank & Rutley, said the scope for redeveloping sites of hospitals being considered for closure was relevant if their sale was expected to provide some of the funding for remaining centres.
