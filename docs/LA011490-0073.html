January 14, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

AGAINST 'B-1 BOB,' FORGET THE DIGNITY;

POLITICS: VETERAN RON KOVIC ASKS FOR A 'CAMPAIGN OF STATESMANSHIP' IF HE RUNS AGAINST REP. ROBERT DORNAN IN ORANGE COUNTY. HE WILL HAVE TO SETTLE FOR BRICKBATS.

Ron Kovic, the paralyzed war hero of "Born on the Fourth of July," is readying for a new firefight. This time, the terrain will not be the jungles of Vietnam but the John Wayne politics of Orange County. This time, his rival will not be the Viet Cong but Robert "B-1 Bob" Dornan, the most hawkish member of the U.S. Congress.

While the 44-year-old combat veteran has yet to formally declare a challenge to the reelection of the archconservative Dornan, shrapnel has begun to fly.

Kovic, an out-and-out critic of U.S. policy in Vietnam, says he is "deeply concerned" by the President's decision to invade Panama. "Are we going into Nicaragua next? Into El Salvador?" He attacks what he calls the "wall of fear" put up by the Administration and Dornan toward other countries in this hemisphere.

"It takes more courage to make friends than it does to pull a trigger," he says. "As a congressman, I would build bridges of friendship with our neighbors instead of this policy of fear and hostility."

Kovic hopes that his campaign for Congress will "remind people about what really happened in Vietnam so it won't happen again." Admitting that he is not a "professional" in the political world, he hopes his challenge to Dornan will be a "campaign of statesmanship," conducted on a "high plane of dignity."

Not likely. If Kovic wanted a high-toned debate over America's proper role in the world, he picked the wrong opponent.

Bob Dornan, the man whose congressional seat Kovic has targeted, despises such second-guessing of American policy. He has particular disdain for those like Kovic and filmmaker Oliver Stone who portray Americans as "scum" in the world's eyes.

"If (Kovic) hadn't been shot," says the man with a 100% conservative, pro-military voting record, "he'd have been strutting about his two tours in Vietnam."

It gets worse. The 56-year-old Dornan says that the only reason Kovic is talking about challenging him is to sell more tickets to "Born on the Fourth of July," the film Kovic co-authored and which is now the country's No. 1 box-office hit.

"He's going to milk this thing for two months. This guy has no idea whatever of spending the next two years going back and forth to Washington, getting on and off airplanes. He's just out to hype the movie."

Dornan mocks the Democrats' decision to recruit Kovic for the race against him. "They saw a wheelchair and said this guy's perfect for Dornan."

He says the Democrats deserve an " 'F' for candidate research." He accuses them of finding the wrong man to run in the wrong district.

On this, the peripatetic ex-Los Angeles talk-show host may have a point. Orange County is one of the most conservative areas in the country. Many of its middle-class voters honor the myth of an unbeatable, untarnished America, a nation that can do no wrong.

What will Ron Kovic say to such people? How will a man who knows the crippling defeat and moral destructiveness of America's worst war campaign among her most ardent patriots?

"I will talk about the building of a new American house," Kovic says without hesitation, "with a foundation that is based on truth, what we have gone through as a country and where we are going. I believe people have the strength of character to face up to this, to turn our wounds into wisdom. They are ready to be challenged again, just as they were by John F. Kennedy on that cold January day back in 1960."

Does someone who speaks in such grand phrases know the hell he faces at the hands of Bob Dornan?

"If I run, it will be an incredible battle, the most difficult challenge of my life."

For a Marine sergeant who endured the horror of Vietnam and the inhumanity of a V.A. hospital and who has spent almost three decades in a wheelchair, this may indeed be an overstatement. Anyone who has been through what Kovic has can survive whatever brickbats "B-1 Bob" may throw at him.
