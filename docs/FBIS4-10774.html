BFN [Text] Brasilia, 13 Apr (EFE) -- The Planalto Palace Press Office reported that Brazilian President Itamar Franco today appointed journalist Fernando Jose da Costa as the new presidential spokesman, replacing Francisco Baker. Da Costa is a 58-year-old employee of the state-owned radio and television network Radiobras [Brazilian Radio Broadcasting Company]. He has served as deputy to Baker for the past 18 months since Franco's inauguration. Baker resigned as Franco's spokesman last month to take a top position in communications at the IMF in Washington.
