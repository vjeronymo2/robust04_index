Language: English Article Type:BFN ["News Release"] [Text] Tallinn, March 14 -- Twenty seven MPs on Monday [14 March] presented the parliament a bill on amendments to Estonia's Constitution to change the current election law under which the president is elected by the parliament if none of the candidates receives the majority of votes at presidential elections. Under the bill, the two candidates who have gathered most votes would compete for the position in the second round of elections two weeks after the first round.
