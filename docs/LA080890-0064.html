HEEEEEERES JOHNNY, THE 'MAYOR OF HOLLYWOOD'

This man strikes you as something like Leonard Zelig (see Woody Allen's docucomedy "Zelig"), who is always seen hanging with the near great and plenty great, standing about with Joe Stalin's entourage or hitting line drives with Lou Gehrig in spring training.

Johnny Grant is always seen hanging around Hollywood stars, wherever they gather, a circular little man with an ingratiating nature, a human Smiley Face.

Now 67, the effervescing announcer-intreviewer-producer-host-star-gazer-hanger-on is feted in a two-hour special on KTLA Channel 5 tonight at 8: "The Mayor of Hollywood: Johnny Grant's 50th Anniversary in Show Business," commencing from his kid days in Goldsboro, N.C., when he walked around town with a sandwich advertising board.

Grant is vice president of public affairs for KTLA (for which he produces the Hollywood Christmas Parade), the "mayor of Hollywood" -- a goodwill assignment that sometimes takes him to distant lands -- and chairman of the Walk of Fame Committee, hosting the laying of new sidewalk stars around Hollywood Boulevard.

There's a lot of cornball here but it's a kick to stroll a little down Memory Lane in the golden-glamour days. Although the Walk of Fame often reflects Hollywood at its silliest, Johnny Grant represents the glitziest of the image, without any of the warts. IRV LETOFSKY
