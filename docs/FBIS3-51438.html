Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "National" newscast] [Text] Protests over the presence of U.S. troops at Malaga Bay continue. Today, several members of the M-19 [19 April Movement] reacted by seizing the cathedral in Ibague, the capital of Tolima Department. Approximately, 20 M-19 members are inside the Ibague cathedral right now protesting the presence of U.S. troops in Colombia and the incidents with the Venezuelan National Guard. They announced the action at 0700 this morning when they began a hunger strike for the dignity of Colombia. [Begin recording] [M-19 member Carlos Herazo] We want the president to explain to the country the agreement that the Colombian Government signed with the U.S. Government for these engineering maneuvers. [Unidentified Reporter] What do you want? [Herazo] We also want to criticize the Venezuelan Armed Forces' constant violations of our sovereignty. [end recording] The protest by this group of Colombians here at the Ibague cathedral will last until tomorrow.
