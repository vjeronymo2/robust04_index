940215 FT 15 FEB 94 / Public libraries may move to private sector KPMG Peat Marwick, the management consultancy, has been appointed to assist government plans for contracting out public library services to the private sector. Five pilot schemes have been started to look at franchising libraries, or operating the entire service by contract, and KPMG will publish an initial assessment of the way forward in the autumn. Mr Peter Brooke, the national heritage secretary, said that local authority responsibility for providing libraries was not at issue but that the consultants would examine the scope for contracting out the delivery of all or some elements of the ser-vice. He added: 'My intention is to set out a range of competitive options for library authorities to choose between, according to their local circumstances.'
