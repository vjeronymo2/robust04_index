BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Carlos Figueroa said today that Chile is putting the finishing touches on an agreement to link up with the Common Market of the South, Mercosur. Figueroa explained that Mercosur is seen by the great powers and by international investors as one of the most interesting centers for investment. Mercosur includes the two largest South American countries, Argentina and Brazil, which are Chile's main trade partners. [Begin Figueroa recording] In all of the bilateral meetings between the Mercosur country presidents and then President Patricio Aylwin, and now with President Eduardo Frei, the Mercosur countries' interest in having Chile become a member has been very clear. [end recording] Figueroa said that the decision regarding Mercosur cannot be delayed. He explained, however, that Chile cannot join Mercosur as a member due to the high tariff rate of that common market. This is why, he said, the Foreign Ministry is preparing a formula to reach an agreement with Mercosur as a whole.
