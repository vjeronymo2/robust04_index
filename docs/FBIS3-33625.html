Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Five O'Clock Sharp" newscast] [Text] The Nicaraguan Government and the leaders of the recontras today began their first direct meeting to discuss the disarmament of the rebels and peace in the country's north, where there has been violence the past few years. Sources within the Government Ministry, the Army, the OAS, and the Catholic Church confirmed the start of the talks in village of Caulatu near the Honduran border. The OAS and the church are acting as mediators. The delegation of recontras from the so-called 3-80 Northern Front is led by its leader, Jose Angel Talavera, alias El Chacal. The government is represented by Government Minister Alfredo Mendieta and Colonel Javier Carrion, the Army's deputy chief of staff. The mediation commission comprises members of the OAS and the Catholic Church who will verify fulfillment of any agreements reached within the next few days concerning the demobilization and disarmament of the recontras.
