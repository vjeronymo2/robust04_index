BFN [Report by Munsif al-Sulaymi: "Salim Salih Tells AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT: Hassan II's Reception Pleased Us, We Believe Recognition Exists Tacitly"] [Excerpts] Rabat -- Salim Salih Muhammad, member of the south Yemen Presidential Council and assistant secretary general of the Socialist Party, has described Moroccan King Hassan II's reception of the South Yemen delegation the evening of the day before yesterday as "very useful, and it pleased us." [passage omitted] On the possibility of Morocco's recognizing the proclaimed Democratic Republic of Yemen, especially as official Moroccan media services have said that the Moroccan monarch received the delegation of the "Democratic Republic of Yemen" [DRY], Salim Salih said that "the question of Morocco's recognition [of the DRY] tacitly exists, proceeding from our status prior to four years of unity which has proved difficult to achieve. Proof of this is the existence of two armies, two establishments, two currencies, and two regimes." [passage omitted] On his assessment of the causes of the failure of the endeavors made by the international envoy, Lakhdar Brahimi, he said that "we have always responded to all the initiatives, most importantly the U.S. and European initiatives. We sought to interact with the proposals presented. But most regrettably, the other party is focusing on one option -- war, which has entered its eighth week and yet has not achieved what the Sanaa regime wanted to achieve." [passage omitted] On the prospects for the dialogue with Sanaa regarding a mechanism to halt the fighting and how this mechanism can be agreed, as the idea was initially endorsed at the Cairo meetings headed by Brahimi, he said: I believe that "the other side does not at all want to reach clear and specific results regarding a cease-fire and choosing the right path. All it is doing is getting around the resolutions by giving others the impression that it has accepted the resolutions." [passage omitted] Salim Salih Muhammad said that Sanaa has offered no sign or hope of withdrawing its forces besieging Aden. He expected the war to continue for a long time. He expressed his hope that sanctions would be imposed on the side not complying with the resolutions of the international will and pushing the region into the furnaces of real dangers that threaten security and stability.
