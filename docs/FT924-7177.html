921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / Monetary and credit policy in Russia Mr Viktor Gerashchenko, central bank chairman, said yesterday that President Boris Yeltsin's decision to include him in the government would not mean greater government control over monetary and credit policy, writes Leyla Boulton. He said he remained answerable first of all to parliament, even though he welcomed moves to strengthen co-operation with the government. It also emerged yesterday that Russia has decided to withdraw its first Dollars 1bn credit tranche from the IMF after months of hesitation, expressed primarily by the central bank.
