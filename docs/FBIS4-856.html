BFN [Text] Djibouti President Hassan Gouled received this morning Hamid Yusuf Hammadi, minister of culture and information and President Saddam Husayn's envoy, who conveyed to him a personal message from the leader president. The message spells out Iraq's view of the position of the Security Council and the importance of activating this position to lift the unjust Iraq embargo, as well as the need to coordinate between friendly and brotherly countries toward this end. The president of Djibouti asked the personal envoy to convey his brotherly greetings to leader President Saddam Husayn, as well as Djibouti's solidarity with Iraq and its constant call for lifting the unjust Iraq embargo. Gouled stressed that Djibouti's position at the Security Council will be in favor of Iraq, especially since Iraq has fulfilled its obligations under Section C of Resolution 687. The Iraqi envoy discussed with President Gouled ways to enhance brotherly relations between the two countries, including activating their joint committee. President Gouled said he had issued instructions in this regard. Hamid Yusuf Hammadi met earlier with Mohamed Bolok Abdou, the foreign minister of Djibouti. During the meeting, they discussed the Security Council position. The Iraqi envoy met also with Barkat Gourad Hamadou, the prime minister of Djibouti. They discussed ways to enhance relations between the two brotherly countries. The Iraqi envoy then met with (Ismail Omar Ghila), chief of the Djibouti Presidential Court. They discussed the position of other nations toward lifting the unjust Iraq embargo, as well as bilateral relations.
