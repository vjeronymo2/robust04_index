930224 FT 24 FEB 93 / UK Company News: Baldwin's Pounds 2.7m cafe purchase BALDWIN, the holidays, printing and properties group yesterday announced it would pay Taylor Walker, a subsidiary of Allied-Lyons, Pounds 2.65m cash for the Muswell's cafe-bar-restaurant chain. Baldwin this week announced an 8.2 per cent increase in profits for the year to end-October. The company said it would pay for the outlets from its own resources. The cost of the 27 outlets, based mainly in the south and south-east of England, represents the value of the assets acquired, the directors said. The acquisition is part of the company's attempt to expand its interests in the restaurant, catering and leisure business. In October 1992, it acquired Simpsons of Cornhill, which owns and manages six restaurants in London, including the famous City chop house, as well as a restaurant in Oxford. The company also owns Keycamp, a tour operator. Mr Sandy Singh, chief executive, said the acquisition showed 'Baldwin's determination to be a market leader in what it believes to be the growth sector of the industry.'
