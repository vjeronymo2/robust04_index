BFN [Text] Seoul, April 27 (YONHAP) -- The foreign ministers of South Korea and Cote d'Ivoire met Wednesday for talks on promoting bilateral relations and cooperation in the international arena. Han Sung-chu greeted his counterpart Amara Essy at the Foreign Ministry and exchanged opinions on recent developments on the Korean peninsula and in Africa, officials said. The two foreign ministers shared the view that the North Korean nuclear problem should be solved through peaceful means, they said. Essy is the west African country's first foreign minister to visit South Korea. The two nations established diplomatic ties in 1961. Essy was to meet South Korean President Kim Yong-sam in the afternoon. He will tour major industrial plants and meet with business leaders before leaving on Saturday.
