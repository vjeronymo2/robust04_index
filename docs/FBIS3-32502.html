Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Costa Rican economic developments monitored through 8 February. Today, Central Bank President Jorge Corrales said the Bank's foreign currency increased by more than $90 million in January. Corrales added that the Bank's reserves, estimated at $800 million, are equivalent to the financing of almost five months of imports. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 1835 GMT 3 Feb 94) Central Bank President Corrales said GNP growth registered in 1992 and 1993 was the highest rate for a two-year period in the past nine years. According to the Economic Commission for Latin America, in 1993, Costa Rica registered the highest economic growth in Latin America, 6.5 percent. (San Jose LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 26 Jan 94 p 12A) During a 21 January meeting of coffee-producers in Guatemala, Costa Rica set the minimum price for its coffee exports at $80 per quintal. (San Jose LA NACION in Spanish 25 Jan 94 p 24A) On 28 January, the Costa Rican Government and the Central Bank signed a payment agreement for the fiscal debt of 2.122 billion colones. (San Jose LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 29 Jan 94 p 13A)
