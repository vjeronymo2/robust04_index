Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, March 10 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- In March 9 the representatives of the opposition in the State Great Hural (SGH) of Mongolia had a meeting with the president of the country P. Ochirbat to discuss the present situation in the country and possible crisis in the parliament, but they did not come to any decision and mutual consent. But the opposition the Mongolian National Democratic Party (MNDP) and the Mongolian Social Democratic Party (MSDP) are not going to abandon their decision to recall their representatives from the parliament of the country, informs the MONTSAME correspondent, referring to the official sources. The opposition has been waiting for the end of the official visit of President P. Ochirbat to the four countries of Asia to discuss with him the present situation in the country and the issues of recalling its representatives from the State Great Hural. P. Ochirbat has been elected to the post of president from the bloc of the MNDP and the MSDP. If, in the end, the opposition recalls its representatives, 70 out of 76 members of the parliament will represent only one party of the ruling Mongolian People's Revolutionary Party (MPRP). This responsible decision has been made recently by the opposition, with taking into consideration the present situation in the country, considering that the opposition can not work in the parliament later on preserving the party representative.
