940804 FT 04 AUG 94 / World Trade News: Appeals panel gives Canadians timber victory A US-Canadian appeals panel yesterday gave Canada an important victory, upholding an earlier finding that Canada's timber pricing and log export policies were not being subsidised, writes Nancy Dunne in Washington. Therefore, the US will have to return Dollars 800m in duties collected from its lumber exporters to the US. On Tuesday, the US and Canada had The panel, convened under the US-Canada free trade agreement, should have ended a battle over Canada's lumber pricing methods which dates back to 1986. Since then, the US commerce department has repeatedly found Canada's methods constitute a subsidy. Last December, a binational review panel reversed the department's findings. The three Canadian panellists ruled in favour of Canada while the two US members dissented. The US then appealed to an extraordinary challenge panel of judges, claiming that two of the Canadian panellists had failed to disclose conflicts of interest. The ruling yesterday was issued by two Canadian members and one US member. The dissenting American on the extraordinary challenge panel said that the majority had violated 'more principles of appellate review of agency action than any opinion by a reviewing body which I have ever read.' Mr Tom Buell, chairman of the Canadian Forest Industries Council said: 'Our governments and industries have expended enormous amounts of time, effort and money on a case that should never have been brought.'
