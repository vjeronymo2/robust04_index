BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladas Burbulis] [Text] Vilnius 2 May TASS -- Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas praised Russian-Latvian agreements reached in Moscow. "The negotiation process with Russia which has caused many disputes in Latvia has ended very effectively, namely with the signing of very important documents," Brazauskas said in an interview with the national radio on Monday. He said that on Tuesday the leaders of the three Baltic states -- Latvia, Lithuania and Estonia -- will meet in Riga, adding that "we have what to discuss." Brazauskas recalled that during Polish President Lech Walesa's recent visit to Lithuania they signed a treaty of friendship and cooperation between the two countries. "I think it will be interesting for my Latvian and Estonian colleagues to hear our opinion about that document. For my part, I would like to learn about the results of Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis's visit to Moscow and his talks with Russian leaders," he said.
