BFN [Text] Belarusian Premier Vyacheslav Kebich reacted with irony to the statements by Belarusian ex-speaker Stanislav Shuskevich and leader of the Belarusian Popular Front Zenon Poznyak who called the signing of Russian-Belarusian documents on the unification of monetary systems "a crime," press-secretary of the Belarusian prime minister Vladimir Zametalin told INTERFAX. He said that "Kebich considers such accusations another provocation by national-reformists, and the treaty with Russia, in the opinion of the prime minister, is the only way to preserve Belarus' sovereignty." Zametalin also announced the prime minister's plans to hold an open meeting of the presidium of the Council of Ministers in the near future with unlimited participation of all interested parties and mass media in order to stop speculations around "so-called corruption." (He referred to accusations against some high-ranking officials made by chairman of the recently disbanded parliamentary commission against corruption Aleksandr Lukashenko) Zametalin also said that the government meeting will be attended by top officials of the Republic's General Prosecutor's Office.
