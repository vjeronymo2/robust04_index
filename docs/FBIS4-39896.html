BFN [Interview with DPRK Ambassador Son Song-pil by Sergey Turchenko, date and place not given: "Threat of Blockade: Son Song-pil, DPRK ambassador to Russia, Talks About the Provocative U.S. Policy on the Korean Peninsula"] [Text] The situation on the Korean peninsula has flared up again sharply recently. The reason for this is apparently an internal matter relating to the DPRK's domestic economic activity: The latter has decided to replace some fuel rods at an experimental nuclear reactor. IAEA experts were invited in good time to monitor this work. Hardly had they had time to begin work than people in the United States kicked up what is clearly a propaganda fuss about the impossibility of the technical inspection of the fuel rods and began voicing a priori suspicions that the DPRK is producing plutonium for nuclear weapons. And it is on these grounds that the "world policeman" is demanding an economic blockade of North Korea and other sanctions. What is behind this campaign? We put that question to Comrade Son Song-pil, DPRK Ambassador to Russia. [Son Song-pil] I want to stress that we immediately gave the IAEA experts all the documentation relating to the method for the technical inspection of the fuel rods. The same documentation was also sent to the U.S. side. They promised us that they would study it. We expressed a readiness for any compromise if our method failed to satisfy the specialists in any way. However, a calm solution of the question was clearly not in the U.S. side's plans. Without waiting for the specialists' assessment the politicians mounted a noisy propaganda campaign aimed at inclining the United Nations toward sanctions against our country. But the main task of the United States remains unchanged -- it is to stifle a socialist state in Northeast Asia. In this connection we state that if sanctions are imposed on us we will take appropriate measures. At the same time we state that we will regard the imposition of sanctions as a declaration of war against us. At the same time we stress that we do not want to aggravate the situation and we believe that the nuclear problem can be resolved only on the basis of a simultaneous and comprehensive solution of all the questions connected with it. [Turchenko] Can you tell us in greater detail what kind of documents were handed over to the U.S. side, because for some reason the press is saying nothing about them? [Son Song-pil] They contain a technical description of our methods of measuring fuel rods. These methods have been approved and fully correspond to our technical capabilities. In this connection I repeat that we have informed the United States of our readiness for compromise options. But we have not received any suggestions from the United States. Apart from a completely unjustified demand for the inspection of two military installations which have nothing to do with the reactor. And that, after all, is a flagrant violation of the country's sovereignty. We will not agree to such a step. [Turchenko] Can you explain, please, what is meant by the simultaneous and comprehensive solution of the problem? [Son Song-pil] The United States is demanding steps only from our side right now. We, however, propose that everything be resolved as a package. For our part we are ready to ensure the possibility of regular and random inspections under [po] nuclear guarantees. We are also ready to return to the framework of the nuclear weapon nonproliferation treaty, which the DPRK suspended in response to the high-handedness of the United States toward North Korea. This, so to speak, is our contribution to the settlement of the problem. But at the same time the Americans must renounce the threat to use nuclear weapons against our country. In this context the U.S. nuclear weapons and nuclear bases must be evacuated from South Korea. The "Team Spirit" military maneuvers directed against us must also be terminated. In addition to all this we demand the renunciation of the provocative policy toward the DPRK and the normalization of relations with our country. That is the U.S. contribution to a solution. However, the Americans are demanding that we unilaterally accept their jurisdiction and then they will see whether it is worth fulfilling our demands. The problem cannot be resolved on that basis, which is, to put it mildly, unjust. [Turchenko] You spoke of appropriate measures in response to the sanctions. What do you mean? [Son Song-pil] Concrete sanctions will lead to concrete measures. It is difficult to make predictions just now. But we have stated that if any country takes part in sanctions we will view that as a declaration of war with all the consequences that such an action entails. [Turchenko] How do you view Russia's proposal to convene an international conference on the solution of the Korean problem? [Son Song-pil] A conference is a step toward the solution of the problem by peaceful means. Of course, we give the due assessment to this proposal. It has special significance also because the Americans have stated that it is impossible to continue the Korean-U.S. talks. If an international conference is held on a fair and equal basis we will support it. [Turchenko] Apart form the general proposal, has the Russian side provided any materials which give concrete substance to the form and content of the conference? [Son Song-pil] Unfortunately our side has not received any such materials to date. I recently had a conversation with Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Panov. The ideas suggested so far relate purely to the framework of this conference. It is to be hoped that this question will receive a positive development.
