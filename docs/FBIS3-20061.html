Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Erding (DPA) -- One day after the arson attack on the synagogue in Luebeck, Republikaner Chairman Franz Schoenhuber once again accused Ignatz Bubis, chairman of the Central Council of Jews in Germany, of "instigating people." He will not deviate "one centimeter" from this accusation, Schoenhuber said at a news conference at the Bavarian land congress of the right-wing extremist Republikaner in Erding near Munich on Saturday [26 March]. "The one who causes anti-Semitism in Germany is Mr. Bubis." The delegates' meeting, from which the press was excluded, was accompanied by protests. "Right-wing extremists are once again waging war against a hesitating democracy," said Fritz Schoesser, Bavarian land chairman of the German Trade Union Federation, with a view to the latest attacks. At a peaceful memorial rally he stated that the 70,000 registered right-wing extremists are a "warning signal for democracy in this country." According to the police, about 600 people participated in the demonstration against right-wing extremism.
