Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The ECOMOG [Economic Community of West African States Cease-Fire Monitoring Group] high command has warned the leadership of ULIMO [United Liberation Movement for Democracy in Liberia] not to allow their infighting in Tubmanburg to spill over into Monrovia. ECOMOG field commander, Major General John Inienger, gave the warning yesterday when he summoned the dismissed leader of ULIMO, Alhaji Koromah, for consultations. A serious split developed within the ranks of ULIMO between Krahn elements loyal to ULIMO military chief, Roosevelt Johnson, and Mandingo elements of Alhaji Koromah following the failure of Dr. Mohamed Sheriff to obtain a position on the State Council. Dr. Sheriff was being supported by Alhaji Koromah, but lost the vice chairmanship of the council to Mr. Thomas Ziah of the same organization. Ziah is being supported by General Roosvelt Johnson and other Krahn elements within ULIMO. Following news of Mr. Ziah's election over Dr. Sheriff, ULIMO leader Koromah has announced the sacking of Ziah and replaced him (?by Dr. Sheriff). Reports say the infighting in ULIMO has led to serious gun battle among its soldiers at the Po River in Tubmanburg ULIMO headquarters. The fighting was sparked off after Alhaji Koromah ordered the disarming of Krahn elements of ULIMO loyal to Johnson. Johnson forces are reported to have resisted the order, which resulted in a serious gun battle. Alhaji Koromah is said to be confused as the situation is now reported to be beyond his control.
