BFN [Teymuraz Mamaladze report: "Black Sea Fleet Incident Could Escalate Into Armed Conflict"] [Text] The much-loved naval game of tug-of-war has currently taken on threatening overtones in Crimea and the Black Sea Fleet. The style of the reporting on the attempted detention of the hydrographic vessel Cheleken would have been reminiscent of a pirate novel -- had not means been used which were not even dreamed of in the days of the "Jolly Roger." The missile-armed Su-15 ground-attack aircraft scrambled by the Ukrainian Air Defense forces and the high-speed cutters of the Ukrainian Border Guards which tracked the ship belong more to a modern armed conflict -- which is all the more likely because a Black Sea Fleet battle group came out to escort the Cheleken. This time, it seems, God was merciful, or some naval official had second thoughts. Talks began in Kiev. It blew over. Will it blow over the next time, when it is not a taut rope but someone's nerves that crack? This protracted Black Sea tug-of-war contest, in which the "teams" are battling for the prize of the property and status of the Black Sea Fleet units, is accompanied by an exhausting war of nerves, largely provoked by the sides' press centers and the mass media. Those who claim that unchecked and often concocted information (disinformation) works like a slow-burning fuse are absolutely right. As Emil Pain, member of the Presidential Council, stated in an interview with an ITAR-TASS correspondent, the appearance in the mass media of Black Sea Fleet situation reports that often fail to accord with reality excessively aggravates the situation. The demand that any report from the region be carefully checked out is undoubtedly correct. The trouble, though, is that the speed at which "disinformation" can be verified may not coincide with the firing speed of multiple rocket launchers. The sides are disseminating mutually exclusive versions of events. It seems as though these versions, numbered with different figures and painted in different national colors, are becoming a kind of infection typical of this time of conflict. The news fabric is being woven out of scraps, each of which has its own message at odds with the others. Public opinion is disoriented. These clashing versions are striking the spark that may cause the explosion, after which people will no longer ask who gave the order. Before that happens, we should ask who is fabricating these explosive reports and to what end. The incident of the Cheleken and the 318th Division of ships of the Black Sea Fleet reserve prompts us for the umpteenth time to consider the existence of a shadowy influence on big-time politics. After all, it is quite obvious that this latest "contest" was organized in circumvention and behind the backs of the top "referees" -- the Russian and Ukrainian presidents, and their agreement to refrain from unilateral action over the Black Sea Fleet. People have tried to cut down with the ax of shadowy schemes what was written with the pen in Yalta and Massandra. As one learned expert on the question has written, there still exists -- aside from the patent, obvious, and even specially presented picture of political life -- another, hidden intrigue of events that is inaccessible to the public gaze and controls. Which in this instance is deadly dangerous. In this connection, here is another statement from a man to whom the dangerous games over the Black Sea Fleet are of interest since they pose a most serious threat to general peace: "A conflict between Russia and Ukraine could make events in Bosnia look like a Sunday-school picnic." (Richard Nixon).
