940714 FT 14 JUL 94 / International Company News: Advertising sales boost Gannett Improved newspaper advertising revenues and steady operating costs helped Gannett, the US publishing and broadcasting group, report record after-tax profits of Dollars 131.8m, or 90 cents a share, in the second quarter. A year before, net income was Dollars 113.6m, or 78 cents. Gannett, whose publications includes USA Today, earned advertising revenue of Dollars 540m in the period, up from Dollars 513m, while income from newspaper circulation edged up Dollars 3m to Dollars 213m. Other sources of revenue were broadly in line with the year before, while costs, excluding interest payments, were flat at Dollars 736m. The company, which publishes 83 newspapers across the US, attributed the advance to gains in 'help-wanted' advertising. The 13 per cent increase in classified advertising sales contributed to a 5 per cent overall gain in newspaper advertising revenues, it said. Broadcasting revenues fell slightly, to Dollars 107m, reflecting the sale of three radio stations. However, profits climbed 29 per cent to Dollars 39m. Profits from outdoor advertising, by contrast, slid 8 per cent to Dollars 8.2m, as revenues fell 1 per cent to Dollars 63m For the first half, net income of Dollars 210m, or Dollars 1.43 a share, was up from Dollars 180m, or Dollars 1.23, a year before. Higher revenues in its information services business were behind a 16 per cent rise in net income at Dow Jones in the second quarter. The company, whose interests include the Wall Street Journal, said revenues from information services such as the Dow Jones/Telerate services, rose 12 per cent to Dollars 239m. Net income was Dollars 46m, or 46 cents a share, for the period, and Dollars 86m, or 86 cents, for the first half of 1994. These compare with Dollars 40m, or 40 cents, and Dollars 71m or 71 cents last time.
