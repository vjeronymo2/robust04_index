930127 FT 27 JAN 93 / The Base Rate Cut: Base rate cut could lead to extra car sales this year YESTERDAY's base rate cut should lead to 50,000 extra car sales this year provided the chancellor does not introduce a tax-raising Budget in March, the Retail Motor Industry Federation said last night, John Griffiths writes. The rate cut came less than 24 hours after the federation, which represents 12,000 retail motor trade businesses, unveiled a pre-Budget submission warning that without lower interest rates a forecast 5 per cent recovery in new car sales to 1.67m this year could peter out. The reduction was also given a cautious welcome by the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders whose chief executive, former Tory MP Sir Hal Miller, said that 'through his actions, the chancellor seems to be on our side'.
