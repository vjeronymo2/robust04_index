940802 FT 02 AUG 94 / Court allows wider use of fixed charge A Court of Appeal ruling in the case of New Bullas Trading, a small consulting company which went into receivership in 1991, has said for the first time that banks other than clearing banks may take a fixed charge on uncollected book debts. The decision could encourage foreign and smaller banks to lend more, particularly to service companies which have few fixed assets such as premises and equipment.
