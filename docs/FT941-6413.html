940302 FT 02 MAR 94 / Auditor's inquiry at Westminster council may be widened The residents of Westminster who first called on the district auditor to investigate the council's alleged 'gerrymandering' policy have met him again to discuss how separate allegations not included in his original public report should be handled, John Authers writes. The auditor has already provisionally recommended that five Conservative councillors and four council officers should be surcharged Pounds 21.5m following their involvement in the council's policy of 'designated sales' of council housing in politically marginal wards. Mr Neale Coleman, a former Labour member of the council and co-ordinator of the objectors to the council's policy, said last night that the scandal went 'far beyond homes for votes'. He said: 'When we got the auditor's report, we the complainants also received more than 6,000 pages of documents. These reveal on inspection that the gerrymandering went far beyond homes for votes.' The further allegations cover land disposals, disposals of property and planning decisions according to Mr Coleman. He added: 'We have asked the district auditor for assurances that he will be addressing these issues.'
