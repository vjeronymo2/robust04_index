BFN [Text] Croatian President Dr. Franjo Tudjman received Peter Galbraith, U.S. ambassador to Croatia, in the Presidential Palace today. President Tudjman told Ambassador Galbraith that the examination of proposals and negotiations on signing an agreement between the Croatian Government and the U.S. Government on economic and technical aid, to be granted to Croatia via the U.S. Agency for International Development, which is a constituent part of the State Department, should immediately be embarked upon. The agreement is expected to be signed at the beginning of May. Ambassador Galbraith informed President Tudjman of his country's intention to begin negotiations on certain concrete projects immediately after the agreement has been signed. He also told him that an agreement on agricultural cooperation to the value of $10 million will be signed at the Croatian Ministry of Finance. President Tudjman expressed his pleasure at the progress achieved in talks between the National Bank of Croatia and the Croatian Finance Ministry on the one hand, and the World Bank and the IMF on the other, that have been held in Washington over the past few days. President Tudjman and Ambassador Galbraith also discussed the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina and the UN protected areas [in Croatia], a statement from the president's office says.
