Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Cape Town Feb 8 SAPA -- The wave of condemnation against the Azanian People's Organisation [Azapo] for supporting its student wing's campaign to expel white teachers from the townships has not abated, with the South African Democratic Teachers Union [SADTU] entering the fray, SABC [South African Broadcasting Corporation] radio news reported on Tuesday. SADTU President Shepherd Mdladlana said in an interview that his organisation took the threat seriously because many white teachers in the townships were SADTU members. He said that if the Azanian Students Movement (Azasm) carried out it's threat there would be confrontation, because SADTU members would feel compelled to defend their colleagues.
