May 20, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

SOUTHERN SECTION BASEBALL PLAYOFFS;

4-A ROUNDUP;

DONNELLY GETS FOOTHILL PAST CHAFFEY

Kent Donnelly pitched a five-hitter as Foothill High School defeated Chaffey, 3-1, Friday in the first round of the Southern Section 4-A baseball playoffs at Foothill.

Donnelly (9-3) struck out 13 and walked only two.

Scott Barton broke a scoreless tie in the fourth with a two-run homer. With two outs, David Downs walked and Barton homered to left-center.

Greg Scott, who had two hits, had a run-scoring single in the fifth for the Knights (19-7).

Chaffey finished the season with a 13-11 record.

Mission Viejo 11, Hemet 4 -- Jeff Hawkins hit a two-run homer in the bottom of the first to give the Diablos a 3-2 lead and Mission Viejo never trailed again. Troy Kopp (8-4) pitched five innings in relief for the victory. Kopp, Lance Sabella and Damon Hubbard each drove in two runs for the Diablos (19-6). Curt Callicott and Geoff Clark had solo home runs in the first inning for Hemet (19-7).

South Hills 9, Sunny Hills 1 -- South Hills' Aaron Small hit a grand slam to take host Sunny Hills (18-9) out of the game early. Small also pitched a complete game for the victory. South Hills improved to 17-7.

Santa Ana 13, Downey 6 -- Catcher Julio Vargas went three for three, hit two doubles, had six runs batted in and threw out two baserunners to lead visiting Santa Ana (17-9). Santa Ana had four players get two hits, including winning pitcher Jess Rosas (6-3), who was two for four with a run batted in.

Western 5, Upland 4 -- Troy Babbitt doubled to right to lead off the bottom of the seventh and Doug McConathy singled him home to win the game for Western (16-10) at Cypress.

San Gabriel 6, La Habra 5 -- Ricardo Varela singled in Gilbert Quioan in the seventh inning to win it for host San Gabriel (14-8). Rob Szente hit a two-run home run in the second inning for La Habra (14-9).

J.W. North 2, Troy 1 (9 innings) -- Andy Martinez singled home Randy Hernandez with one out in the ninth for North (20-7) at Riverside City College. Tom Wilson (8-2) went the distance for Troy, striking out 11. He also had two hits.
