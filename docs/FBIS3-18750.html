Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Robert Lamb, U.S. special coordinator on the Cyprus issue, said that he has positive expectations for the talks and does not expect to face any problems. Speaking upon his arrival in Cyprus this afternoon, Mr. Lamb added that Washington believes the proximity talks on the confidence-building measures [CBM's] are proceeding well. He noted: There are difficulties and serious questions that, however, are addressed constructively and with prudence. The U.S. coordinator also said that he comes to Cyprus to be informed of the precise positions of the two sides, and that the United States believes this is a very critical time for the Cyprus issue and the future of Cyprus. Mr. Lamb added: What is going on now could open opportunities for the solution of problems that have been causing hardship for the island for a long time. Mr. Lamb further noted that the United States is prepared to offer any help it can. Asked about his recent visit to Moscow, Mr. Lamb said that a series of problems, including the Cyprus issue, were discussed during his visit.
