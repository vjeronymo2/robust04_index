920717 FT 17 JUL 92 / Insolvency service publishes targets THE government's insolvency service expects to deal with about 36,000 new cases this year and to have completed the administration of 24,000 cases. Mr Neil Hamilton, corporate affairs minister, yesterday said the service aimed to issue reports on the circumstances of companies becoming insolvent within 10 months where crime was suspected. It also aimed to reduce the average costs of the initial stage of bankruptcy and liquidation proceedings by 45 per cent compared with two years ago. Mr Hamilton said that the service planned to submit disqualification reports on directors within 15 months in 80 per cent of cases, and to hold an initial meeting of creditors within 12 weeks.
