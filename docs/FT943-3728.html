940913 FT 13 SEP 94 / Abstention likely on Bosnia arms Britain is likely to abstain in a United Nations vote on lifting the arms embargo on the Bosnian government, Downing Street said yesterday. It would mark a significant difference of opinion with the US administration, which is expected to press for a lifting of the embargo in the UN Security Council. Ministers are not considering using their veto, however. Mr Malcolm Rifkind, defence secretary, said: 'If the UN was to decide to start supplying arms to one of the combatants in this war, there is no way in which UN forces could remain in Bosnia on a bipartisan basis.' Mr Rifkind said it was not certain the US would respond to growing domestic pressure to lift the embargo by proposing a security council resolution - or that it would win the necessary backing. Asked if British troops would have to come out, he said: 'I have no doubt that is what will happen. There is no question about it if the arms embargo is lifted.' Lifting the embargo would simply reunite the Serbs and invite Russian hostility, he warned on BBC Radio. Although Tory MPs hope a public dispute between the US and the UK can be avoided after a period in which the international community is seen to close ranks against the Bosnian Serbs, Downing Street yesterday said: 'The US administration has known for many months that we believe the lifting of the arms embargo would make the situation worse.'
