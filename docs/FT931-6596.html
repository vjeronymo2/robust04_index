930301 FT 01 MAR 93 / Dublin woos Ulster Unionists MR DICK SPRING, foreign minister of the Irish Republic, sought yesterday to woo Northern Ireland's Unionist leaders into fresh talks on the province by softening slightly his stance on the republic's claim to the territory of the north. He said that constitutional change 'will be, if necessary, implemented' and that the republic should be prepared to 'change and look forward'. Speaking on BBC television, Mr Spring said articles 2 and 3 of the constitution, which refer to Northern Ireland, 'are not set in bronze'.
