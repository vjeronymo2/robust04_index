June 17, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

ALBUM REVIEW;

**** BOBBY WATSON AND HORIZON "THE INVENTOR" BLUE NOTE: ALBUMS ARE RATED FROM FIVE STARS (A CLASSIC) TO ONE STAR (POOR).

A veteran of Art Blakey's Jazz Messengers and the respected 29th St. Saxophone Quartet, alto saxophonist Bobby Watson shows all the right influences -- Johnny Hodges, Sonny Stitt and, of course, Charlie Parker -- but doesn't rely on imitation to get his point across. Tunes such as his sophisticated ballad "Dreams So Real," the smoothly-paced "Homemade Blues" and "Heckle and Jeckle," on which Watson trades bird calls with trumpeter Melton Mustafa, put a contemporary shine on the hard bop tradition and provide a framework for some strong emotional expression. Drummer Victor Lewis also contributes two numbers, including "The Shaw of Newark," a spirited tribute to the late trumpeter Woody Shaw. BILL KOHLHAASE
