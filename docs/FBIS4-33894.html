BFN [From the "Evening Information Review" newscast] [Text] Mexican President Carlos Salinas de Gortari, during a news conference in Havana, has reaffirmed his opposition to the U.S. blockade against Cuba, a country toward which he will always promote solidarity and respect. Shortly before concluding his brief visit to the Cuban capital, Salinas de Gortari told the local and foreign media that, in his talks with his counterpart Fidel Castro, he discussed political and economic issues, including trade and investments by private Mexican citizens in the island. In this regard, he confirmed an important investment by a Mexican company in the Cuban telephone industry as well as the preference that will be granted to the import of Cuban products such as textile products, tiles, and prefabricated houses. As for the Fourth Ibero-American summit that will start on Tuesday, the Mexican president said he will attend it convinced that specific results will be achieved in the areas of trade and integration. He added that summits have shown their value as formidable political forums to collectively discuss issues that are common to all peoples of the region.
