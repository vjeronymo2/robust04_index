940627 FT 27 JUN 94 / World Cup '94: Police facing crutch dilemma Security precautions have been intense at each of the nine World Cup venues. There have been no reports of hooliganism - feared by officials - but a ban on taking flag-poles and other sharp objects into the stadiums has not proved successful. New Jersey police have noticed a rise in leg injuries among spectators. 'People are coming through the gates on crutches, then pulling their flags from their underwear and flying them from a crutch,' said police spokesman John Hagerty. 'And we can't do anything,' he said. 'You can't take a guy's crutches away from him.
