BFN [Aydyn Mekhtiyev report under the "Conflict" rubric: "Another Karabakh Settlement Attempt Fails. Baku Believes That Middlemen Have Taken Armenian Side"] [Excerpts] Negotiations on a settlement of the Armenian-Azerbaijani conflict, which took place on 4 and 5 May in Bishkek under the auspices of negotiators of a CIS Interparliamentary Assembly peacemaking group and the Russian Federation Government, did not, as many observers predicted, contribute to the solution of the sharp contradictions between official Baku and the Armenian side. [passage omitted] The call on the conflicting sides to cease fire on the night of 9 May sounds at least strange given that by refusing to sign the protocol Baku has effectively assumed no cease-fire obligations. An official spokesman for the Azerbaijani Foreign Ministry, who asked not to be identified, told your NEZAVISIMAYA GAZETA correspondent that Russia and chiefs of the CIS Interparliamentary Assembly, who formally acted as middlemen at the Bishkek meeting, have in many instances effectively backed the Armenian side's demands, a stance that has become a factor of pressure of sorts on the Azerbaijani delegation, which nevertheless did not sign the final protocol. On the day when consultations on the Karabakh settlement started in Bishkek, the Azerbaijani leader left for Brussels, where a document on Azerbaijan's joining the NATO "Partnership for Peace" program was signed. According to available reports, NATO chiefs have stated their organization's readiness to take necessary steps to establish security and stability in the region. The fact that on his way from Brussels to Baku Geydar Aliyev made a brief stopover in the Turkish capital, Ankara, underlines the importance attached by the Baku authorities to Turkey's support in the current difficult situation.
