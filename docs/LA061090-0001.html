June 10, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

EARTH FIRST!

Sometimes our government seems to be unable to recognize the moral side of an argument even when it is delivered from a burning bush. The FBI has been on the wrong side of too many major American causes -- from the labor movement to protecting the country's environment.

The agency is too often an overpaid goon squad for big business and corrupt government. A primary FBI responsibility seems to be the protection of the financial interests of the nation's powerful and rich. It has a long history of wasting its resources on attacks on social dissidents such as Carl Sandburg, John Steinbeck, Vietnam War protesters, Lenny Bruce, civil rights workers, Martin Luther King, Amnesty International, Paul Newman, South American refugee shelters, and environmental groups and representatives, including Dave Foreman. This kind of activity is an embarrassment and an insult to the nation and to the competent and dedicated FBI employees who risk their lives fighting organized crime, terrorism, sophisticated white-collar crime and government corruption. I wish a few of those heroes were in the FBI's upper management.

THOMAS W. DAY

Huntington Beach
