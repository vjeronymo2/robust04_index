September 26, 1990, Wednesday, Valley Edition

DIGEST: BRADLEY, BARRETT NAMED TOP WSC PLAYERS ON OFFENSE

Freddie Bradley of Moorpark College and Jeff Barrett of Santa Monica have been named the Western State Conference's co-offensive players of the week.

Bradley rushed for 205 yards in 19 carries and scored three touchdowns in a 41-10 victory over Valley on Saturday night.

Barrett, a sophomore from Burroughs High, completed 16 of 21 passes for 302 yards and five touchdowns in a 53-26 victory over Compton. Barrett was named the conference's offensive player of the week the previous week.

Cal State Northridge women's volleyball team will take on U.S. International in San Diego tonight at 6. The Matadors (10-5) are coming off a three-game sweep of the University of San Diego in which Marianne Dixon had 17 kills and six blocks. USIU is 4-9.

Right-side hitter Shelly Swalec is doubtful for tonight's match because of a strained muscle.

"We feel like we're ready, tactically," CSUN Coach Walt Ker said. "The key for us tonight is to execute. Our outside hitting has been a bright spot, but it's also been inconsistent."

Greg Seiler has resigned as Cal Lutheran sports information director to take a similar position at Mars Hill College in Mars Hill, N. C.
