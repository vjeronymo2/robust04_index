BFN [From the "Business SNARK" file: "Transcaucasia Economic News: Energy"] [Text] Yerevan -- The energy system of Armenia, in its time one of the best in the former USSR, now is on the decline, declared Miron Shishmanyan, Armenian Minister of Energy and Fuel, at the International Energy Conference, held in Yerevan. In his opinion, the main ways of overcoming the crisis are re-opening of the Armenian Nuclear Station, optimum usage of the acting thermal plants and hydro-stations, building of the gas pipeline and high-voltage line from Iran to Armenia. It's also very important to stimulate fuel import to Armenia and to build here an oil-refining plant. Mr. Shishmanyan also mentioned the importance of using nontraditional sources of energy. He reported that the European Bank has already given 57 million dollars for the reconstruction and expansion of the Hrazdan Thermal Plant. As to the Iran-Armenia gas pipeline, the sides are preparing to sign the final agreement on its building.
