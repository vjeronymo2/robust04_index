September 10, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

'WAR IN EUROPE: 50-YEAR LEGACY'

In the overview "War Turned Impoverished U.S. Into a Superpower" (Aug. 31), Stanley Meisler notes: "Some Americans feared that unemployment and depression would return after demobilization of the armed forces."

I think at this point he should have mentioned that it was just this thinking that transformed the War Department into the Defense Department and put us on a permanent wartime economy.

RICK SANDFORD

Los Angeles
