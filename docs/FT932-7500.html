930522 FT 22 MAY 93 / Kyrgyzstan President promises to disband armed forces President Askar Akayev of Kyrgyzstan promised yesterday to disband his country's armed forces entirely in the next three to five years, writes George Graham. Mr Akayev, in Washington to sign loan agreements with the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund, said Kyrgyzstan had already halved its armed forces and planned to replace them altogether with a national guard. Mr Akayev said he had secured an Dollars 85m standby credit from the IMF, Dollars 62m from the World Bank, and Dollars 170m aid from the US, Japan and Switzerland.
