BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Igor Borisenko, Pavel Vanichkin, and Oleg Velichko] [Text] Washington, 23 June -- U.S. Vice President Albert Gore described the results of the session of the intergovernmental Russian-American commission on economic and technological cooperation as "an outstanding success." In two days the commisssion, whose co-chairman is Viktor Chernomyrdin, chairman of the Russian Federation Government, drew up a number of agreements between the two countries. The ceremony at which the documents were signed took place on Thursday in the conference hall of the government complex attached to the White House. Viktor Chernomyrdin and Albert Gore signed the Russian-American agreement on ecology, the statement on the principles of information exchange, the agreement on the shutting down of plutonium-making reactors, as well as the joint statement on cooperation in setting up an orbital station. Albert Gore stressed that 22 documents were signed that are opening up prospects for cooperation between the two countries in different areas. Assessing the results of the third session of the Russian-American commission highly, the U.S. vice president stressed: "As your partners and as your friends, we are acting together with you for the sake of furthering progress." Viktor Chernomyrdin noted that the documents signed are "landmarks in the relations" between the two countries, and we are now faced with the task of filling the agreements reached with specific content.
