920108 FT 08 JAN 92 / Thomson cuts prices on 300,000 holidays THOMSON, Britain's biggest holiday company, yesterday announced discounts totalling Pounds 15m, Michael Skapinker writes. Its decision to cut the prices of more than 300,000 holidays by an average of Pounds 50 was a response to aggressive pricing by Airtours and Owners Abroad, which are competing for second place in the holiday market. Owners Abroad announced price cuts last month and said it would offer free children's places on some holidays. Mr Charles Newbold, Thomson managing director, said the cuts applied to one in eight of the company's holidays. He added: 'The other two didn't allow for the possibility that their little scrap would draw Thomson in, but I'm not going to stand aside and let our business be carved up by them.' The move was not yet the beginning of an all-out price war, but he warned that if Owners Abroad and Airtours responded with further reductions his company would react in similar fashion. Mr George Marcall, Airtours sales director, said the Thomson move could be good for the industry. 'I believe it will stimulate the public to go out and buy holidays.'
