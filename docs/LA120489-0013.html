December 4, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

U.S.-ARAB RELATIONS

Does Saudi Arabia have a "consistent record of commitment to economic and political stability in the Middle East," as Shehab claims? If so, this commitment is at the expense and to the detriment of Third World and Western nations.

During the economically turbulent 1970s and early 1980s, oil price gouging by Saudi Arabia and OPEC plunged the world's economies into crisis. Remember $40 barrels of oil and the long lines at gasoline stations?

Adding to Saudi Arabia's lack of concern for the U.S. economy is its voting record at the United Nations. Saudi Arabia voted with the United States only 8% of the time, while voting in agreement with the Soviet Union 100% of the time. Thus, politically and economically, Saudi Arabia has, more often than not, maintained a record of opposition to U.S. interests.

Shehab's claim that this Saudi "friendship" should be rewarded in the form of U.S. tank sales is outrageous and insulting. Saudi Arabia has demonstrated again and again its preoccupation with its own self-interest and an unwillingness to aid America's interests.

DAVID A. LEHRER

Regional Director Anti-Defamation League

Los Angeles
