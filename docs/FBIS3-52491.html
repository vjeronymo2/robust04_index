Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kerman, Jan. 3, IRNA -- Iran produced 10.8 million tons of wheat this year, an increase of 3.8 million tons compared to 1988, Deputy Agriculture Minister Jalal Rasulof said Monday [3 January]. He said despite the fact that Iran's population has increased by six million over the past six years, imports of wheat has dropped form five million tons a year to 2.5 million tons a year. In the course of the first five-year economic development plan, launched in 1988, imports of rice, sugar and barley fell by 300,000, 400,000 and 500,000 tons respectively, he said. The deputy agriculture minister said a total of 18 million tons of various crops were produced in the country between 1988 to 1992. The Agriculture Ministry conducted 6,500 research projects and provided training courses for six million farmers during the first economic development plan, he said.
