BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 8 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The representatives of the Mongolian Democratic Association (MDA) have demanded a resignation of the government at the meeting organized by the MDA at one of the central squares of the capital. Those speaking at the meeting including the leaders of the MDA and people delegating the Social Democratic Party, the Students Union as well as the unemployed and pensioners have reminded the government all its promises concerning the stabilization of the national economy within a two-year period and criticized the policies being pursued by the government in the spheres of foreign trade, social welfare of the population, production and finance. It was said that the abuses of official position are acquiring a boundless character at any level of management. In this connection some speakers stressed the significance of the hearing of the issue on possible involvement of some leaders of the parliament and government in corruption and abuses of official position to be carried out as sooner as possible. The chairman of the parliament and the prime minister refused to take part in the meeting though they had been invited to it by the organizers of the meeting. The leaders of the MDA explained their suggestion as striving to provide the leaders of the country with possibilities to express their opinions and to be associated with the common people. When the meeting was over, its participants passed to the main square of the capital named after D. Suhbaatar where those who wanted could express their views from the "free rostrum" arranged by the MDA. The meeting was attended by not less than five-six [as received] thousand people.
