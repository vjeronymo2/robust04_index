Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, March 22 (CTK) -- The investigation underway into the shipping accidents that have blocked two locks at the Gabcikovo-Nagymaros hydroelectric power project on the Danube has so far not revealed any evidence of external interference, according to a Slovak Government statement which CTK has obtained. "Only the findings of the government commission working at the Gabcikovo water project will be authoritative," the government statement reads. Economics Minister Peter Magvasi refused to speculate about the possibility that the accidents were caused by sabotage, saying the commission had to continue and complete their investigation. The Slovak Government expressed regret today at its session that international passage on the Danube remained impossible because the right-hand lock at Gabcikovo was still blocked by the Ukrainian tugboat Zernograd which sank on February 11. The government announced it hoped river traffic could be resumed within a few days. A section of the bottom gate of the left lock was damaged -- it is not known how -- on Sunday [20 March] night and the resultant wave under the dam damaged mechanisms on the bottom gate of the right lock. Subsequently international ship transport along this section of the Danube has been interrupted. The Hungarian Foreign Ministry earlier issued a statement asserting that the Slovak Government bore full responsibility for the blockage and asked countries supporting free passage on the Danube to call on Slovakia to re-open the river.
