Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Brunei has confirmed that it is in favour of inviting Burma to an annual meeting of foreign ministers of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations in Bangkok in July, Foreign Minister Prasong Sunsiri said yesterday. Brunei believes the time is "appropriate" to invite Burma, Squadron Leader Prasong quoted Brunei Foreign Permanent Secretary Lim Jok Seng as saying. Dato Lim conveyed the message from his foreign minister, Prince Mohamed Bolkiah, to Sqn Ldr [Squadron Leader] Prasong during a, 30-minute meeting at the Foreign Ministry yesterday Dato Lim is in Bangkok to attend a meeting of ASEAN senior officials, who on Monday agreed in principle to invite Burma to their foreign ministers' annual conference. With Brunei's endorsement, Thailand's attempt to bring Burma out of isolation has won the support of all ASEAN partners, officials noted. But Sqn Ldr Prasong acknowledged that ASEAN member states remain undecided as to whether to invite Rangoon as guest of the grouping, or to let Thailand issue the invitation as host country. A Foreign Ministry official pointed out that Burma should be invited as Thailand's guest with the endorsement of other ASEAN members. The Foreign Minister reiterated that the invitation would strengthen security in the region and encourage Burma to bring about political and economic reforms. Meanwhile, a senior member of the military junta in Rangoon has met opposition leader Aung San Suu Kyi Voice of America reported yesterday. THE NEW YORK TIMES quoted Intelligence chief Lt-Gen Khin Nyunt of the State Law and Order Restoration Council as saying that the meeting "produced nothing useful," VOA said in a report monitored.
