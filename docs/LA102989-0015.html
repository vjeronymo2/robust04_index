October 29, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CRITICS' CHOICE: LION AT THE DOOR BY DAVID ATTOE (LITTLE, BROWN: $17.95; 232 PP.)

"Attoe has a muscular prose style in which nouns often double as verbs, and he's able to shift narrative voices -- male and female -- with idiosyncratic realism. Perfectly tuned, exquisitely told, 'Lion at the Door' is thorny beauty. Margot Mifflin
