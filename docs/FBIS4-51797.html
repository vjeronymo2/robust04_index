BFN [Text] Seoul, April 27 (YONHAP) -- President Kim Yong-sam met visiting Cote d'Ivoire Foreign Minister Amara Essy at Chongwadae [presidential office] Wednesday and exchanged opinions on ways to further promote friendly relations between the two countries. During the meeting, President Kim, explaining that South Korea plans to seek to obtain a non-permanent seat at the U.N. Security Council in 1996, asked for the African country's cooperation in this regard. Kim also expressed the hope that durable peace will settle in Africa and the election in South Africa will be held smoothly, adding that his government will send a deputy prime minister to the inauguration of a new South African president. In response, Minister Amara Essy said his country will support South Korea's advancement to the U.N. Security Council and will urge other African countries to extend similar support to Korea.
