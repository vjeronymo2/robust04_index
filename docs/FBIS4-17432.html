BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Sevindzh Abdullayev and Viktor Shulman] [Text] Baku, 23 Apr -- Azerbaijani President Geydar Aliyev and Sergey Baburin, the head of a Russian State Duma delegation and coordinator of the deputy's group Russian Path, who met today in Baku, spoke out in favor of restoring, developing, and strengthening ties between Azerbaijan and Russia. On the issue of the situation of Russians in Azerbaijan, the president stressed that they are and always have been an integral part of society, citizens of the republic with full rights. He said that "Azerbaijan's multi-ethnicity, its different languages, and use of Russian in many spheres have played an important role in the development and making of the culture of the people of Azerbaijan." Geydar Aliyev familiarized the Russian parliamentarians with the current situation in the conflict zone between Armenia and Azerbaijan. He said that Azerbaijan was making efforts toward a peaceful settlement of the conflict, but that the republic's interests have to be taken into account. Russia, in whom Azerbaijan holds great hopes, has not yet adopted a position in principle on this issue, he believes. The leader of Azerbaijan believes that some progress in the peacekeeping process could be made if Russian President Boris Yeltsin were to visit the region. He said that this was proposed to the Russian leader and that Azerbaijan are ready to provide for his security during his stay here.
