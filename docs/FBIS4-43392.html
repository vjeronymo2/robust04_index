BFN [Excerpts] There was a huge anti-Mafia operation in Milan this morning. Hundreds of people were arrested by the police. The northern gangs of the Calabrese Mafia have been broken up. They managed a huge drugs trade. Details of the operation were given a short while ago in Milan by Milan's Chief Prosecutor Francesco Saverio Borrelli, Chief of Police Vincenzo Parisi, and Interior Minister Roberto Maroni. Paola Maria Anelli reports: [Anelli] [Passage omitted] Dozens of people belonging to the gang of Giuseppe Mazzaferro, a very important member of the Calabrian 'Ndrangheta, were the target of the operation. For many years the gang had deep roots in Lombardy, in Como, Varese, Milan, and Brescia. [passage omitted] A total of 370 people -- 100 of whom were in jail -- were served with preventive detention warrants in Lombardy, Apulia, and Calabria. They were charged with holdups, extortion, drug and arms trafficking. [passage omitted]
