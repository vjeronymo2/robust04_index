Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 8 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese Government today expressed concern over the situation in Afghanistan, urging the conflicting parties there to achieve a permanent ceasefire as soon as possible. A Foreign Ministry spokesman voiced the concern when asked to comment on the current situation in Afghanistan. "Since the beginning of the new year, the civil war in Afghanistan has flared up again," the spokesman noted. "The Chinese Government is deeply concerned with the development of the situation in Afghanistan and hopes that the conflicting parties there will set store by the interests of their country and people and work to realize a permanent ceasefire as soon as possible, so as to avoid bloodshed and settle their disputes through peaceful negotiations, hence achieving at an early date a restoration of domestic peace, national reconciliation and the rebuilding of the country," the spokesman said.
