May 17, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

IMPROVING POLITICAL CAMPAIGNS

If both parties want to reform campaign financing and coverage (front page, May 7), the solution is simple. Since almost every dollar is spent on advertising, prohibit political advertising.

It's not needed and, if the goal is to turn out the voters, it's a miserable failure. The higher the spending, the lower the voter turnout.

Gorbachev has never advertised in the U.S. but his name, face and ideas are universally known.

That may be the secret -- ideas. If candidates stop selling themselves like soap and breakfast cereal, they would have to command attention with substance instead of production values.

If their ideas are compelling and plentiful enough, their day-to-day coverage would increase in ratio as would their TV guest appearances, debate invitations, and commentary page articles. Their personal exposure to the voters would come with innovative barnstorming enhanced -- if their ideas are newsworthy enough -- by regional exposure on television and newspapers.

Campaigns could be run at a fraction of today's costs. All it takes is a ban on political advertising and insistence by the media for legitimate news instead of "photo ops." Who knows, voters might start voting again.

One problem: How do you get the media to back an advertising ban?

PHIL WILLON

Palos Verdes Peninsula
