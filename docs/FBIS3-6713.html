Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 17 KYODO -- Japan's industrial production in January grew 1.0 percent from the preceding month, an upward revision from a 0.9 percent rise reported earlier, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry said Thursday [17 March]. The seasonally adjusted production index for the mining and manufacturing sectors stood at 88.7 against a base of 100 for 1990. The shipment index, given as 90.0, was revised up to a 0.7 percent growth from a 0.6 percent increase in the preliminary report released in late February. The inventory index, at 107.9, fell 0.4 percent from December, unchanged from the earlier report. The index of the inventory-to-sales ratio, at 122.3, dropped 2.9 percent, a revision from a 3.2 percent decrease in the preliminary report. Before adjustments, January industrial production registered a revised 3.0 percent decline from a year earlier. Both unadjusted shipments and inventories decreased a revised 3.1 percent in January from a year ago, MITI said.
