BFN [Text] Khartoum, 8 June (SUNA) -- Al-Sayyid Jalal Ali Lutfi, the chief justice, and those accompanying his excellency, returned to the country yesterday after visiting the Islamic Republic of Iran, where he held discussions with senior Iranian officials on joint cooperation in different judicial fields. 'Abd-al-Rahman Sharafi, a member from the delegation, told SUNA that the visit achieved its goal. He said that the chief justices of the two countries signed a memorandum of understanding on cooperation and coordination in the judiciary field and in international and regional forums, in addition to agreeing on other bilateral relations. He said that the Iranian Republic will provide the Sudanese judiciary with modern equipment and sufficient reference materials on Islamic jurisprudence. He added that an agreement was concluded on exchanging scholarships and training courses, in addition to (?providing) the Sudanese judiciary with [word indistinct] agricultural equipment and medicine necessary for the judiciary's service projects.
