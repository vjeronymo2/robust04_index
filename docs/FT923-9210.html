920806 FT 06 AUG 92 / More Swedes out of work Swedish unemployment soared in July to 307,000 or 6.5 per cent of the workforce, the highest figure in half a century, writes Robert Taylor in Stockholm. Mr Gote Bernhardsson, head of AMS, the labour market board, warned that Sweden's internationally famous labour market system with its emphasis on training and work for the unemployed was under threat as a result of the rapid escalation in the jobless total. The figure has more than doubled in a year.
