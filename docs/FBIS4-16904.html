BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondents Leonid Kotov, Vladimir Shvedov] [Text] New Delhi 19 April, TASS -- Ukraine and India are prepared to contribute to the strengthening of peace and cooperation in South Asia, jointly seek universal and complete nuclear disarmament and to eliminate existing hot beds of tension through political means, this was underscored during talks today between Prime Minister of India P.V. Narasimha Rao and Ukraine's Foreign Minister Anatoliy Zlenko who is here on an offical visit. The head of the Ukrainian foreign affairs department expressed support for India's fight for strengthening its national sovereignty and territorial integrity. Zlenko emphasised that Indian leadership's stand for the destruction of all nuclear arsenals in conformation with the plan of action for the creation of a nuclear- free world, which was worked out by former Indian Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi, is regarded with understanding in Kiev. P.V. Narasimha Rao expressed support for practical steps taken by Ukraine to achieve non-nuclear status. The head of the Indian Government highly appreciated Kiev's contribution to strengthen stability in the world and the development of equal and partner-like relations between all members of the world community. The two sides reaffirmed their interest in the continuation of consultations between the two countries on questions of bilateral and international issues on a permanent basis. The same day Zlenko held talks with Indian Foreign Minister Dinesh Singh. It was stressed during the meeting that India attaches great importance to relations with Ukraine which bear a progressive character, especially in the sphere of economics. Dinesh Singh, in particular, expressed India's readiness to provide necessary assistance to Ukraine in the implementation of its wide- ranging economic reform programmes and transition towards a market economy. India and Ukraine signed today agreements on setting up a Ukrainian-Indian intergovernmental commission for trade, economic, scientific, technical and cultural cooperation, a protocol on consultations between Foreign Ministries of the two countries and other documents.
