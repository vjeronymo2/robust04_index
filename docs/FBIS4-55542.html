BFN [Summary] According to an opinion poll carried out by Slovak Radio in May to find out current voting intentions, and based on the answers from 1,065 respondents, the Movement for a Democratic Slovakia [HZDS] polled 21.1 percent, the Party of the Democratic Left [SDL] polled 8.7 percent, and the Christian Democratic Movement [KDH] polled 7.9 percent of votes. The other parties failed to reach 5 percent of votes and would not therefore be represented in parliament. The respondents also answered the question about how would they vote if parties and movements were grouped in coalitions. The coalition of national oriented parties--the HZDS, the Slovak National Party, and the Christian Social Union--would win 1999 percent of votes. The coalition of left-wing parties--the SDL, the Social Democratic Party, the Green Party, and the Farmers' Movement--would win 15.1 percent of votes. And the coalition of right-wing and liberal parties--the KDH, the Democratic Party, the National Democratic Party, the Democratic Union, and the Party of Entrepreneurs and Small Businessmen--would win 13 percent of votes. The coalition of Hungarian parties received the support of 7.5 percent of respondents.
