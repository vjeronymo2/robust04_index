BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 28 KYODO -- The Construction Ministry has set up special monitoring committees for securing fairness in biddings for public works projects in all of its eight regional construction bureaus, ministry officials said Thursday [28 April]. The committees will keep watch on the procedures for biddings and receive complaints from companies that fail to get contracts, the officials said. The move came in response to a series of major corruption scandals, which exposed rampant, illegal collusion between politicians and big builders to fix public works contracts and deflect official investigation into bid-rigging. The committees, which comprise five experts, will advise the ministry's local branch heads. They will monitor and assess fairness of the qualifications for bidders for the public works contracts given by the local construction bureaus.
