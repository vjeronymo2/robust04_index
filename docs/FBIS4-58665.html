BFN [Text] The foreign ministers of the Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] and their counterparts in the European Union will arrive in Riyadh tomorrow to take part in their fifth joint meeting due to start on Sunday [8 May]. The GCC foreign ministers are expected to hold a [separate] meeting after their arrival to discuss items on the agenda of the [8 May] meeting. GCC foreign ministry undersecretaries had met early last week to examine the draft joint statement which will be issued at the end of the one-day meeting. Diplomatic sources have reported that the statement is expected to include several topics relating to energy, the environment and the developmnent of economic relations between the two communities, in addition to other issues of mutual interest, foremost the security situation in the Gulf region in the light of the Iraqi regime's refusal to abide by the resolutions of international legitimacy. The peace process in the Middle East and the situation in Bosnia-Herzegivina will also be mentioned in the joint statement.
