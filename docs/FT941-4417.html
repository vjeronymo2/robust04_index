940311 FT 11 MAR 94 / Insurers to crack down on bad debts Insurers are to assess the creditworthiness of their customers in a move to tackle a growing problem of bad debts. About a dozen companies are supporting the development of a credit database which will be used to assess the ability of an individual or commercial buyer to maintain instalment payments on policies. As many as one-in-five customers pays for insurance by monthly or quarterly instalments and bad debts have risen, partly as a result of steep increases in premiums. It is also believed that some of these defaults involve fraud. Default rates on instalment policies are running at nearly double the rate for other forms of credit offered by banks and building societies which already use credit information groups. 'The problem is all too apparent,' said Mr George Stratford, chief accountant at Cornhill Insurance. 'The high payout on claims . . . has long been exacerbated by a high level of late payments on personal policies.' Cornhill, which has used credit information for the past six years and is one of the companies which launched the initiative, noticed a trend 2 1/2 years ago for motor policyholders to pay their initial instalment and then default, said Mr Stratford. 'With a large proportion of personal lines (home and motor) business moving from insurer to insurer in search of best rates, discussions with fellow insurance companies revealed that we were not alone with this problem,' he said. Infolink, part of the UK's largest independent credit information organisation, is providing technical services for the scheme. Mr Kevin Still, head of insurance services at Infolink, said: 'There is also an element of fraudulent activity whereby individuals obtain cover and pay the initial payment, but have no intention to maintain their repayments.'
