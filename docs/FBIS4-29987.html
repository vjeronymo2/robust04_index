BFN [Text] The Central Executive Council members of the Indonesian Journalists Association [PWI] said they fully understand the government's reasons for revoking the publication permits of two magazines, namely, TEMPO, EDITOR and a tabloid paper DETIK. In a statement released in Jakarta today, the PWI Central Executive Council noted the several warnings issued earlier by the government to these three publications. The statement was signed by Tony Hadi and Tribuana Said, PWI's secretary general and chairman respectively. The PWI Central Executive Council is confident that this incident will not cause the active participation of the press in national development to slacken and it will not dampen the spirit of openness between government officials and the people.
