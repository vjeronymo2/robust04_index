BFN [Text] Officials in Aden called on the United Nations to impose sanctions on the government of Sanaa to lift the siege it was imposing on Aden. Anis Yahiya, head of the south Yemeni delegation touring Western Europe and the United States, said in a news conference he held in London that the threat of imposing sanctions was the only means to stop the northern bombardment of Aden. He added that the sanctions would force Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdullah Salih to comply with UN Security Council Resolution No. 924, which called for a cease-fire in Yemen. That came at a time when the intensity of the continued northern shelling of Aden and its suburbs had increased. A southern communique affirmed that the northern shelling had caused the death and wounding of 800 people since 17 June instant. [passage omitted] An official in Aden said that the main plant for generating electricity was subjected to an aerial and artillery bombardment by the northern forces, a matter which led to a power supply cut in Aden and caused heavy losses to the plant.
