920611 FT 11 JUN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Ulster complaints body to be set up NORTHERN Ireland secretary Sir Patrick Mayhew said last night that an independent assessor of military complaints procedures is to be introduced in the province. He also announced a scheme aimed at reducing the time defendants spend in custody before being tried, with a target of 12 months from first remand to arraignment. Public complaints about the Royal Ulster Constabulary rose by 25 per cent to 2,527 last year, according to a report by the Independent Commission for Police Complaints, published yesterday.
