BFN [From the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] Russia redeployed 153 paratroopers to Belgrade today to conduct rotation among the personnel of the UN collective peacekeeping forces. From there, the Russian soldiers are to be transported to the site of the 1st Russian Battalion, deployed in the town of Klisa in Serb Krajina. INTERFAX has learned this from Yevgeniy Podkolzin, commander of the Russian Air Force [title as heard] Yevgeniy Podkolzin also said that on 24 and 29 April two more aircraft with Russian paratroopers and one with a space communication station will be sent to former Yugoslavia. In all, about 600 members of the Russian contingent in the UN peacekeeping forces are to be replaced by the end of the month.
