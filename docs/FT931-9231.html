930215 FT 15 FEB 93 / Afghan parties agree ceasefire A CEASEFIRE was agreed yesterday to halt artillery battles between the Afghan government and dissident guerrillas that have killed and injured thousands of civilians, Reuter reports from Kabul. The truce between the government and the radical Hezb-i-Islami was arranged by retired Pakistani lieutenant-general Hamid Gul. President Burhanuddin Rabbani said he was ready for talks with any party. The president described the nearly four-week bombardment around the capital, Kabul, as necessary to defend residents.
