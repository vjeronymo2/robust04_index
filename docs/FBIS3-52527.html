Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The need to reorganize the command and control structure of the Army and providing thorough training to troops have been emphasized by the new army commander, Lieutenant General Gerry de Silva, when he assumed command of the Army yesterday. At the ceremonial taking over of command held at the Army Headquarters, Lieutenant General Gerry de Silva, told newsmen that manpower resources is the main obstacle that the Army have to face. He noted that welfare measures of the troops should be improved to a greater extent. The training of troops and reorganizing the army commanding control are now being reviewed. Adequate war materials including modern vehicles with sophisticated equipment are essential factors to win a battle. This is also vital for a greater mobility to improve the ground forces. The army commander assured every possible action to remedy the past setbacks and weaknesses to gain victory in the northeast battlefront in the future. He expressed hope that with the cooperation of all and concerted efforts the armed forces will be able to achieve success to restore peace and stability to the country.
