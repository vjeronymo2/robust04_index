Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] A meeting was held today to discuss the various aspects of the final agreement reached on the Uruguay talks. The meeting was chaired by Mr. Mourad Cherif, minister of foreign trade, foreign investments, and handicrafts. [passage omitted] After the meeting, Mr. Mourad Cherif, minister of foreign trade, foreign investments, and handicrafts, made the following statement: [Begin Cherif recording] Since 1986, the member states of GATT have been holding the Uruguay talks. These negotiations ended on 15 December last year with an agreement which will be signed on 15 April this year. It honors me to announce that the choice of the participating parties has fallen on the city of Marrakech to host the trade ministers' meeting during April this year. That was in response to a proposal by His Majesty King Hassan II, may God make him victorious. The final agreement of the Uruguay rounds will be signed during this meeting and the birth of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade will be announced. This choice is proof of confidence in and excellence of our country on the international sphere, and puts the seal on our encouraging and recorded economic achievements during a decade of structural reform. [passage omitted] The success of the Uruguay rounds, God willing, will add (?100) billion dollars to world revenue until the year 2000, according to a study carried out by the World Bank and the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development. The reduction in tariffs which the participating parties will give will enable Moroccan goods to enter foreign markets. The signing of the agreement in the city of Marrakech will give positive and important results to the Moroccan economy in general and our exports in particular. [end recording]
