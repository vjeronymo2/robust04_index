Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Yesterday was another [word indistinct] day in the senseless war in Bosnia-Herzegovina, as 60 persons died following a mortar explosion in Sarajevo's central market. Another 150 persons were injured, many of whom are in critical condition. Bosnia-Herzegovina President Alia Izetbegovic accused the Serbs, whereas Radovan Karadzic, leader of the Bosnian Serbs attributed the attack to a Muslim provocation in order to establish a negative climate against the Serbs. This murderous attack against noncombatants has been strongly condemned by the United States and other countries. In a written statement yesterday, U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher notes that his country will press for the United Nations to specify soon who is responsible for the deadly attack in Sarajevo. He also warned that air strikes by NATO should not be ruled out once it is determined who is responsible. He announced that he had a telephone conversation with his British counterpart on the issue and will hold more deliberations with other allied countries. The French Foreign Ministry condemned yesterday's massacre in Sarajevo, saying that France feels horror and disgust. The Italian and Belgian foreign ministers asked for specific measures in reaction to the attack. In the meantime, talks on ending the hostilities in Bosnia-Herzegovina will resume again. The Muslims will finally participate in the talks, despite expressing reservations yesterday. Lord Owen and Thorvald Stoltenberg, international mediators on the Yugoslav dispute announced yesterday that the foreign ministers of Greece, Turkey, Albania, Bulgaria, Romania, Hungary, Italy, and Austria will meet Wednesday [9 February] in Geneva to enforce the peace efforts in Bosnia-Herzegovina. In a statement regarding the Geneva meeting, the Greek Foreign Ministry notes that the Greek presidency of the European Union [EU] intends to examine along with its partners at the EU Council of General Affairs meeting, which will take place Monday, the reason and usefulness of this meeting at this time. According to the statement, Greece will insist on the principle that only countries which have borders with the former Yugoslavia should participate.
