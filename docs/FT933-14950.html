930712 FT 12 JUL 93 / Strategy urged for south-east engineering LOCAL and regional agencies need to work together to maintainengineering skills in the south-east, a study sponsored by local authorities and Training and Enterprise Councils has found, Andrew Baxter writes. The study recommends a regional policy forum to develop a strategy for the sector. It argues that the role of local and regional public agencies in economic and industrial policy development in the UK - and particularly in the south-east - is relatively undeveloped compared with other parts of Europe. The study was produced by the Local Government Centre at Warwick Business School and Warwick University's Institute for Employment Research. Towards a Regional Strategy.
