940707 FT 07 JUL 94 / Welsh fears on mines sell-off Owners of Welsh private coal mines are threatening to complain to the European Commission in Brussels about the proposed terms for the sale of British Coal. The owners are worried that the government will sell British Coal cheaply, in effect subsidising new competitors. Mr Rhys Jeffrey, chairman of the South Wales Small Mines Association, a trade group representing 60 companies, said his members calculated that it cost Pounds 15 a tonne to gain access to coal. But one-off pit-leasing deals implied a cost of only Pounds 5 a tonne. The Department of Trade and Industry stressed that the sales process was competitive. It said: 'The market will determine the true value of the business to be sold.'
