930831 FT 31 AUG 93 / Campaign to fight counterfeit Pounds 1bn sales THE LABOUR party yesterday set up shop in London's Spitalfields market to launch a campaign against counterfeit goods, John Griffiths writes. It says they cost 100,000 UK jobs and Pounds 1bn in lost sales to British companies each year. Mr Robin Cook, Labour's trade and industry spokesman, said some of the goods were dangerous and could even kill. Markets are a main channel for selling such goods. The party displayed recently seized counterfeit goods, including designer-label clothes, computer games, music cassettes, videos and replacement car parts. Mr Nigel Griffiths, Labour consumer affairs spokesman, said legislation left a loophole which acted as a 'counterfeiter's charter'.
