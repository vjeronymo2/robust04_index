930601 FT 01 JUN 93 / Union plea on nursery education SCHOOL STANDARDS will decline unless young children are given a head start through nursery education, says the National Association of Schoolmasters/Union of Women Teachers, the second-largest teachers' union. The union yesterday urged MPs to support nursery education for all three and four-year-olds in the same way as do other European Community countries, the US, Canada, Australia and New Zealand. Mr Nigel de Gruchy, NASUWT general secretary, said: 'The way to improve school standards is to teach very young children. It is not good enough just to mind them.'
