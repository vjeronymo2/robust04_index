BFN [Sergey Suslikov report: "Rocket Seeks Customer"] [Text] Omsk -- The "Polet" Aerospace Association, which in the recent past manufactured the launch rockets for the "Buran" shuttle in Omsk, has obtained a license from the Russian agency granting it the right to launch Russian and foreign satellites. "Polet" relies mainly on the "Cosmos" rocket, which is capable of lifting large numbers of satellites into orbit for a variety of purposes. Evidence from independent European experts testifies to the rocket's high class: Only 19 failures in 700 launches is one of the world's best records. The Russian rocket has a further advantage -- it is cheaper than foreign counterparts. All the same, it will not be easy to find buyers. The West dictates its own policy in the space commercial services market.
