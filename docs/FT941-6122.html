940303 FT 03 MAR 94 / Public optimism about economic outlook slumps Public optimism about the economic outlook has slumped to its lowest level for a year, according to a MORI opinion poll in today's Times. The newspaper's economic optimism index, measuring the proportion of the public who think that the general economic condition of the country will improve rather than get worse over the next 12 months, has fallen to -14, the lowest level since the -21 recorded in February last year. Separately, Mr Kenneth Clarke used an interview with Today newspaper to attack high executive salaries. He said he did not think the government had the power to intervene. 'But I do think that with the rates of taxation having been reduced, people should think twice before allowing their boards to award them excessively high salaries.'
