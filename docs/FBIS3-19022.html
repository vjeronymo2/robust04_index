Language: French Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Bell Gets Big Contract -- Alcatel Bell Wins Orders for Half a Billion Belgian Francs"] [Text] Alcatel Bell, the Belgian subsidiary of the Alcatel-Alsthom group, said on Wednesday [9 March] that it had won two contracts in the modernization of the public telephone exchange network in the region of Kaliningrad, a port on the Baltic. The total value of the projects is $15 million, or 532.5 million Belgian francs. These contracts were signed by LenBell, a Russian company in which Alcatel Bell is the majority shareholder. They concern the delivery of 50,000 telephone lines of the Alcatel 1000 System 12 type to a mixed Franco-Russian company. The order will be spread over four years and involves the delivery of a combined international-interurban-local exchange and several local exchanges, distant subscriber units, analog-numerical conversion equipment and material for transmission by fiber optics and electromagnetic waves. The first combined exchange will be put into service next September. Last year Alcatel Bell and LenBell had already signed contracts for the delivery and installation of 84,000 phone lines in the cities of Novosibirsk, Kostroma, Dubno, Ufa, Moscow, Voronezh, and Pakov [as published]. Most of the exchanges will be delivered before the end of 1994.

And in Korea too
On Wednesday Alcatel Bell also announced winning a 140 million Belgian franc contract in South Korea. Signed with Dacom Corporation, the contract also concerns the delivery of an Alcatel 1000 System 12 program. "This contract is the result of an international public call for tenders," a company press release stressed. The company headquarters are in Antwerp.
