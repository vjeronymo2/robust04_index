920323 FT 23 MAR 92 / City firms 'unprepared for fire and bomb alerts' AN ESTIMATE of the time lost to financial institutions in the City through such interruptions as security alerts, fires, floods and power failure has been issued by Telehouse, managers of a building in Docklands designed to withstand failures of facilities, Andrew Jack writes. It shows that more than a quarter of the 71 institutions questioned had suffered interruptions to dealing lasting at least an hour in the past year. At least 15 per cent of those questioned said they had no contingency plans to deal with interruptions caused by alerts, and a further 49 per cent taking part in the survey appeared to have no other adequate mechanisms to deal with a dealing-room failure. Mr Nick Simms, marketing manager of Telehouse, said a further informal study of companies in the Broadgate area of the City showed that at least 7 banks had lost three hours of trading time following a bomb scare earlier this month.
