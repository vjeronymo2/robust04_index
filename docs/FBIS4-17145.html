BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Andrey Palaria and Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow April 20 TASS--Russia will set up military bases in CIS states with their consent, Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev said on Wednesday. The announcement was made after the signing of an inter-governmental agreement on military and technical cooperation between Russia and Turkey in Moscow today. Grachev said Russia will have three military bases in Georgia and two in Armenia. In Azerbaijan, Russia plans to establish a military facility which is vital not only for Russia, but also for other CIS states. He said it will be a missile warning post in Gaballa.
