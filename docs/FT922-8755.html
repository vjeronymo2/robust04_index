920512 FT 12 MAY 92 / World Trade News: Malaysia in UK arms talks MALAYSIA IS discussing buying more arms from the UK in addition to over Pounds 1bn-worth ordered under a 1988 agreement, David White reports. The new requirements include armoured vehicles, extra aircraft and naval vessels. Mr Najib Razak, Malaysian defence minister, is in the UK this week for talks. British Aerospace wants Malaysia to take a squadron of Tornado bombers under a leasing deal through the RAF. Contenders for a sale are the Russian MiG-29, General Dynamics' F-16, McDonnell Douglas' F/A-18 and Dassault's Mirage 2000.
