January 14, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

PASADENA TOURNAMENT OF ROSES MARKS ITS CENTENNIAL FESTIVAL

Your series emphasized my biggest complaint with television coverage of the Rose Parade -- the cutting away from bands to provide commercials.

Young people who are part of a school band are hard-working dedicated students who put in many hundreds of hours practicing their music, and marching routines.

Receiving an invitation to march in the Rose Parade must be among the top recognitions those kids receive. Acceptance of such an invitation then means extensive fund-raising to allow their participation.

Let America see these shining examples of dedicated and deservingly proud youngsters. Let the "folks back home" enjoy that surge of pride when their hometown band marches across the screen.

Since sponsor fees are what provide the presentation to home viewers, the commercials are necessary. Certainly, creative people could devise a way to announce their sponsorship without cutting away from the parade.

MARILYN CALHOUN

Montclair
