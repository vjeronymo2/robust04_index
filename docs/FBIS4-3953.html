BFN [By Danielle Yang] [Text] Taipei, April 26 (CNA) -- German Economic Affairs Minister Guenter Rexrodt said Monday [25 April] that he supports Taiwan's accession to the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT). Speaking upon arrival at Chiang Kai-shek International Airport, Rexrodt also said he is visiting Taiwan to help strengthen bilateral economic and trade relations. Rexrodt arrived in Taiwan late Monday night at the the head of an eight-member delegation. He was met at the airport by Economic Affairs Minister P.K. Chiang. Meanwhile, Rexrodt met with Minister of Transportation and Communications Liu Chao-shiu on Tuesday to lobby for German companies seeking contracts to build Taiwan's proposed high-speed railway project. During a meeting with Vice Economic Affairs Minister Hsu Kai- sheng later in the day, Rexrodt and Hsu discussed the signing of a bilateral agreement to avoid double taxation. Hsu said he hopes germany can remove Taiwan from the "high tension area" list which Germany uses to prohibit arms sales to designated countries. Rexrodt said Germany welcomes Taiwan investment in eastern Germany and the establishment of a Taiwan Trade Center in Frankfurt to promote bilateral ties. Two-way trade between Taiwan and Germany totaled U.S.$7.73 billion in 1993 with Germany enjoying a trade surplus of U.S.$715 million. Germany is Taiwan's largest trade partner in Europe. Rexrodt is scheduled to leave Taipei Tuesday night.
