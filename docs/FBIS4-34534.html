BFN [Report by Julio de la Rue from the "TV Today" newscast] [Text] The Attorney General's Office has reported a slight increase in the number of reported election related crimes throughout the country compared to those filed during the first round. The report states there were claims of attempts to buy votes in five departments. Twenty people are under arrest, and they reportedly received gifts or favors to change their votes, according to the Attorney General's Office. [Begin Attorney General Carlos Gustavo Arrieta recording] We received reports in certain instances that gifts such as lunch and tickets were given to people who voted. [end recording] There was a positive assessment of the participation of state officials in the political campaign. [Begin Arrieta recording] Quite the contrary, concerning officials' participation in the electoral process, there was a significant decrease. [end recording] The Attorney General's Office will present to the judicial authorities the results of this report to allow them to conduct the appropriate investigations.
