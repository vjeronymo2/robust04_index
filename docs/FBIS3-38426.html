Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Members of the Chamber of Deputies' Foreign Relations Committee have expressed their concern that the war in the former Yugoslavia could spread south. The deputies participated in a briefing on the agenda of the Greek presidency of the European Union. Alternate Foreign Minister Theodhoros Pangalos, who briefed the members of the Foreign Relations Committee, expressed the view that the possible spread of the war to the south will depend on the stance of the Kosovar Albanians. [Begin Pangalos recording] We have every reason to believe, and we hope for it and try to use our influence toward it, that the Albanian side will show moderation. It is not logical to assume that the Serbs would open a new front at a time when they are engaged in other disputes, such as with Croatia, which is not an active dispute right now, and Bosnia, where they are forced to take the steps that [words indistinct]. Let us hope that the Albanians, who have legitimate demands for autonomy and education, will not raise the issue of independence, something that will create a conflict with consequences that can not be estimated. [end recording]
