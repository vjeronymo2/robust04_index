Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES 22/2/1994 -- "Together, we can surpass many problems," said Romanian Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu during his interview on Tuesday with Poland's Defense Minister Piotr Kolodziejczyk. The Polish dignitary is visiting Bucharest in order to sign a military cooperation agreement expected to induce tighter bilateral relations in all fields. The collocutors expressed their desire to develop the bilateral economic relations, according to the real potential of the two economies. The cooperation between traditional friends, such as Romania and Poland, based on updated, flexible and pragmatical agreements, are contributing to the building up of a stable structure on the continent, was a conclusion of the interview, attended also by Romanian Defense Minister Lieutenant-General Niculae Spiroiu and by Polish ambassador in Bucharest. Premier Vacaroiu requested that the desire for intensified bilateral contacts be conveyed to his Polish counterpart. The talks occasioned also an exchange of opinions on the "Partnership for Peace" recently joined in both by Romania and Poland.
