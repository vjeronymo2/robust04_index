910501 FT 01 MAY 91 / International Company News: Supermarket chain issues profits warning GREAT Atlantic & Pacific Tea Company, the US supermarket chain, has warned that if current trends continue, the group could see a sharp profits slump in the three months to mid-June. Mr Jim Wood, the company chairman, said results from A&P's Canadian operations had declined further in the first quarter of the group's financial year, although the US interests were showing an improvement. 'A continuation of the present trend in the current quarter which ends June 15, could result in a decline in overall profits by as much as 30 per cent below the same of last year,' he said. In the first quarter of its 1990-91 calender year, A&P said that it had earnings per share of Dollars 1.33 and net income of Dollars 51m.
