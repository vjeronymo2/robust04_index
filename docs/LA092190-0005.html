September 21, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

CONGRESSIONAL BUDGET DEBATE

It seems that the Congress is having the usual hard time putting together a budget. I suggest that our representatives just let Gramm-Rudman kick in and then resign.

These "honorable gentlemen" have not been able to get it together, ever. I work 3 1/2 months a year for the government already, and I know I don't get my money's worth. Banking, business and health care are crumbling, and all they want to do is raise taxes and cut social programs more.

Why can't they take responsibility for their past actions? Instead of Japan bashing, take a quick lesson. Admit that you have made mistakes and destroyed this great nation. Then get out of the way!

DENNIS E. HAMRICK

Fullerton
