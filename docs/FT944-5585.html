941202 FT 02 DEC 94 / International Company News: PepsiCola bottler targets Brazil Argentinian PepsiCola bottler Baesa yesterday launched a Dollars 400m foray into the Brazilian market. The company, which took over the Pepsi franchise from Brazil's soft drinks company Brahma, has built bottling plants in Sao Paulo, Rio de Janeiro and Porto Alegre in the state of Rio Grande do Sul. Baesa's franchise covers Brazil's most lucrative markets of Sao Paulo and Rio de Janeiro states, and the south-eastern states of Rio Grande do Sul, Parana and Santa Catarina. Mr Leon Timothy, a senior vice-president, said that by the end of next year Dollars 300m will have been invested in the new plants and a further Dollars 100m in advertising and promotion. Pepsi has 7 per cent of the Brazilian soft drinks market, compared with about 60 per cent for Coca-Cola, he said. The Brazilian operations will employ about 2,000 people and will be run by a wholly-owned Baesa subsidiary.
