Detailed 94WB0040C Mexico City EXCELSIOR in Spanish 10 May 94 pp 6-A-3, 7-A,3 94WB0040C Mexico City EXCELSIOR Spanish CSO [Article by Ignacio Gutierrez A.] [Excerpt] Another severe blow was dealt to the drug traffic yesterday by the Attorney General's Office [PGR], when it seized five tons, 347 kg, and 800 grams of dried packed marijuana, worth over 90 million new pesos. In operations conducted in eight states, the Federal Judicial Police arrested 24 presumed drug dealers, also confiscating eight automotive vehicles used to transport drugs. Among those arrested as persons presumed guilty of crimes against health were Urbano Barragan Ruiz, Javier Saldana Salazar, Lorena Salazar, Jose Israel Rueda Coria, Enrique Calderon Duarte, Jose Francisco Herrera, Jose Martin Ontiveros Galindo, Guadalupe Ontiveros Galindo, Jose Angel Limon Juarez, Jose Nieblas Chavez, Rafael Alcaraz Lopez, and Ignacio Torres Gil. One of the largest seizures took place in Nuevo Leon, according to the report submitted by Roberto Soltero Acuna, the PGR's state representative, and Horacio Brun Acosta, deputy director of the Federal Judicial Police, on the confiscation of one ton and 512 kg of cannabis indica, which had arrived concealed in a tank truck. Near km 145 of the Monterrey-Reynosa highway, Urbano Barragan Ruiz, driver of a 1986 Kenworth tractor-trailer truck with Federal Public Service license plates 256-AG9, was captured. The drugs were concealed in the "belly" of the tank, distributed in 72 packages. The vehicle was being guarded by Javier Saldana Salazar and Lorena Galvan, who were following the trailer in a Cheyenne pickup truck with Tamaulipas State registration VU-47085. In their statements to the public ministry, the accused claimed that the drugs had been packed in Uruapan, Michoacan, and were bound for McAllen, Texas. Another ton and 17 kg was seized in Sinaloa, specifically, in the municipality of Villa Union. Luis Antonio Cornejo, the PGR's state representative, reported that the drugs were being transported under a shipment of coconuts. At a checkpoint (Precos) set up on that site, the blue flatbed truck with plates 350-AG3 was subjected to a search, and the illicit load was discovered. It had arrived from the municipality of Arteaga, Michoacan, with the town of Tijuana, Baja California, as its final destination. [passage omitted]
