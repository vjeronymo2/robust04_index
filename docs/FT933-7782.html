930819 FT 19 AUG 93 / UK Company News: Rea Bros surges to Pounds 802,000 WITH ALL businesses contributing and offshore operations producing record figures, Rea Brothers, the private banking group, lifted pre-tax profit from Pounds 284,000 to Pounds 802,000 in the first half of 1993. Principal activities include banking, investment management, corporate finance and trust and company administration. The effect of low short term interest rates on earnings from capital had been offset by increased banking and investment activity. Fund management had a 'particularly good' six months. Benefit came from a reduction in doubtful debts from Pounds 90,000 to Pounds 21,000. But Pounds 175,000 was provided in anticipation of certain costs relating to the return to Alderman's House following damage from the Bishopsgate bomb in April. Earnings rose to 1.42p (0.26p) and the interim dividend rises to 0.3p (0.25p).
