BFN [Excerpts] Silvio Berlusconi has almost reached his goal. A final agreement on the new government was reached last night. [passage omitted] Mauro Mazza reports: [Mazza] Berlusconi's dream may become reality. There has been speculation since last night that Antonio Di Pietro will become interior minister. Maybe it is only a coincidence, but the prosecutor was in Rome this morning to meet with his colleague Saviotti. There are rumors that Di Pietro and Berlusconi met before the prime minister-designate went to the Quirinale [office of the president] at 1130. [passage omitted] Meanwhile, other problems were apparently solved at last night's summit: [Roberto] Maroni [Nothern League] and Giuseppe Tatarella [Italian Social Movement--MSI] will be deputy prime ministers. Maroni will also be justice minister, and Tatarella will also be minister of posts and telecommunications. Antonio Martino [Forza Italia] will be foreign minister. Lamberto Dini [no party affiliation] will be given the Treasury. [Giulio] Tremonti, former member of the Pact for Italy, will be finance minister. [Ombretta] Fumagalli Carulli [Christian Democratic Center-CCD] will be minister of education. [Giancarlo] Pagliarini [Northern League] will be budget minister. Leoni will be given the Ministry of Public Works. [Publio] Fiori [MSI] will be transport minister. [Domenico] Fisichella [no party affiliation] will be minister for cultural heritage. [Gianni] Letta [a journalist] will be undersecretary to the presidency of the council. [passage omitted]
