BFN [Text] Yerevan ("LRAGIR", "AZG" Dailies, Past), 06/07/94 -- Special Envoy of the Russian President Vladimir Kazimirov has visited Baku, Yerevan and Stepanakert, negotiating with the sides regarding the new Russian peace project. He reported to a "TURAN" correspondent, they are trying to find formulations and topics based on mutual concessions. According to Kazimirov, maximalist aspirations of both sides are not supportive to the progress, since the other side would clearly not accept them. "Unfortunately, the sides are not used to trade yet, but are fixed on their demands", said Russian President's Envoy. Kazimirov noted the haste of the Azeri side, which is not supportive for successful solutions. "In order to start withdrawal of troops from the occupied territories, we should not waste days and weeks but we should reach a Great political agreement", noted Kazimirov. He also referred to stationing peace-making forces and said they would be something inbetween peace-making troops and observers. "This forces wouldn't have disengagement functions", he noted, "these are observers with corresponding control and security bodies." According to Kazimirov, their structure is not defined yet, but financing will be shared equally by the three sides. Kazimirov said that the Azeri side made a proposal on liberating Shushi and Lachin, but this problems should be discussed only in two weeks after the current agreement is signed. Russian President's Special Envoy criticized Karabakh's demand of "status against territories", saying this is an attempt of resolving the problem by force. Having finished the negotiations in Baku, Kazimirov arrived to Yerevan late in the evening on June 4, and met his Armenian counterpart Davit Shakhnazaryan. The sides have been negotiating on several topics of the Russian project on peaceful resolution of the Karabakh problem. RA [Republic of Armenia] President Levon Ter-Petrosyan met Kazimirov on June 5. On the same day Russian President's Envoy left for Stepanakert. Kazimirov met Chairman of the RMK [Republic of Mountainous Karabakh] State Department of Defense Robert Kocharyan and the RMK Foreign Minister Arkadiy Gukasyan. The sides discussed problems relating to peaceful resolution of the Karabakh conflict and the recent Russian project. The RMK leaders expressed their worries about the non-constructive stance of Azerbaijan, which rejects the peace projects one after another. Kazimirov left Stepanakert on June 6.
