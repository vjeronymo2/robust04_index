920414 FT 14 APR 92 / Call for change in education policy AN END to the 'vivisection approach' to education policy, and a greater willingness by parents to pay for school improvements, were yesterday urged by the leader of a teaching union. Mrs Heather Ryan, president of Amma - the third-largest teachers' union - told its annual conference in Birmingham that teachers had 'been forced to breaking point' by the government's 'muddled planning and impossible targets, rigidly imposed without thought'. She added: 'Politicians and Civil Service advisers have approached education like vivisectionists, using schools as laboratories for doctrinaire curriculum, assessment and funding techniques, squeezing the life and creativity out of the education service.'
