June 27, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: TV & VIDEO

A spoonful of TV makes the medicine go down. A new 24-hour a day, in-house children's television channel will go on the air today at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. The commercial-free, nonprofit channel is being donated by DIC Enterprises, and will showcase such DIC-produced kidvid favorites as "The Real Ghostbusters," "ALF," "ALF Tales," "The Adventures of Teddy Ruxpin," "The New Archies," "Inspector Gadget" and "Zoobilee Zoo." "We felt we should do what we could to prove the adage that laughter is the best medicine," says Andy Heyward, president, DIC Enterprises Inc. For his part, Dr. David Rimoin, director of pediatrics at the hospital, says a patient's outlook is crucial to the healing process and the new children's channel is "just what the doctor ordered." ALEENE MacMINN
