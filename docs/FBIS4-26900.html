BFN [Text] Lhasa, June 26 (XINHUA) -- One drug trafficker in the Tibet Autonomous Region was publicly sentenced to death and three others were sentenced to imprisonment today by the Lhasa City Intermediate People's Court. Qamjig, the principal criminal, was sentenced to death for having purchased more than 1,000 grams of narcotics in Yunnan Province and resold them in Tibet at high profits. After the judgement was announced, Qamjig lodged an appeal. The Tibet Autonomous Regional Supreme People's Court reviewed the case, rejected Qamjig's appeal and reported to the Supreme People's Court of the People's Republic of China (PRC). According to the order of the president of the Supreme People's Court of the PRC, Qamjig was executed. Another principal criminal in the case was Dojie, who was sentenced to life imprisonment. The two accessories in the case were Huang Tinghong and Losang Soinam were sentenced to 15 years imprisonment. [sentence as received]
