Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on narcotics developments in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific monitored by Bangkok Bureau between 27 December 1993 and 2 January 1994. Source information is given in parentheses after each item.

Pakistan
Nigerian Arrested on Heroin Charges at Karachi Airport
The Customs Drug Enforcement Cell arrested Nigerian national Saliu Lasisi at Karachi's Quaid-e-Azam International Airport on 25 December. The suspect was aboard an Ethiopian Airlines flight bound for Lagos via Addis Ababa. Officers found 4.2 kg of heroin powder concealed in cavities inside of 30 wooden hangers in his luggage. (Islamabad THE NATION in English 27 Dec 93 p 12)
Peshawar Customs Seizes 8.25 Kg of Heroin
Acting on a tip about a drug shipment, Peshawar Customs established a checkpoint near Hayatabad Chowk. When a vehicle attempted to avoid the checkpoint, the officers gave chase. The driver later abandoned the vehicle. Officers found 8.250 kg of heroin. (Peshawar THE FRONTIER POST in English 31 Dec 93 p 3)
Burma
Officials Seize 10.319 Kg of Opium From Passenger Bus
Officials from Defense Services Intelligence and the special antidrug squad stopped and searched a passenger bus at Lashio checkpoint on 10 November. They seized 10.319 kg of raw opium from two passengers. (Rangoon THE NEW LIGHT OF BURMA in English 19 Nov 93 p 7)
Indonesia
Police Seize 120 Kg of Marijuana, Arrest Two
Police antinarcotics officers from Medan Precinct uncovered a drug syndicate on 30 December, seizing 12 bales of marijuana weighing 120 kg. Officers arrested a 43-year-old woman identified as S. BR. P., and a 35-year-old identified as Wah. They were caught while negotiating over the price of the marijuana, a police spokesman said. (Jakarta ANTARA in English 1321 GMT 31 Dec 93)
