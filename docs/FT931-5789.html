930304 FT 04 MAR 93 / Arts: Today's Television It will be useful at 9.30 to be the owner of three video recorders. Then, assuming you are in the middle of the final episode of Turning The Screws, Roger Graef's documentary series about Wandsworth Prison (9.00 C4) you will be able to record Chef] on BBC1, second episode in the new foodie comedy starring Lenny Henry (9.30 BBC1); the second episode in A Labour Of Love (9.30 BBC2) about child care in the first half of the 20th century which looks as though it may be nearly as good as 'A Secret World Of Sex"; and the second part of Disguises in which Adam Holloway finds out what it is like to be a schizophrenic on the streets. Earlier, in that fascinating series The Secret Life Of .. Tim Hunkin explains the word processor (8.30 C4). Later in Harry Enfield's Guide To Opera (10.30 C4) the comedian explains how he became interested in the subject. This sounds like a series aimed less at regular readers of the FT arts page than at those who liked the signature tune for the last World Cup. Thursday is a good night since we also get two of the best current comedies: French And Saunders (9.00 BBC2) and Drop The Dead Donkey (10.00 C4).
