Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran -- Talks on a rescheduling scheme for Iranian debts with German companies have been concluded. Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Mahmud Va'ezi told journalists in Tehran today that his country originally should have paid 3 billion German marks [DM] by 21 March. During his visit to Bonn this week, agreement was reached to repay a total of DM4.5 billion by the year 2000, beginning 1996. "This is the most comprehensive agreement Iran has ever concluded for rescheduling its foreign debts," Va'ezi stated. The deputy foreign minister headed an Iranian delegation that paid a three-day visit to Germany. He met with Chancellor Helmut Kohl and Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel during the visit. According to information from Bonn, Iran is to be granted a DM2.5-billion loan by German banks on the basis of commercial conditions, which is to be covered by Hermes guarantees. The Finance Ministry pointed out Iran's "good values" as far as the payment of debts is concerned. The country is currently facing liquidity problems because of lower oil prices.
