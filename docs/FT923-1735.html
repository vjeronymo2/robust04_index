920922 FT 22 SEP 92 / Solicitors reject latest plan for legal aid fees ICITORS' leaders yester-day rejected the government's latest proposals for fixed legal aid fees and pledged to fight their planned introduction. They said the plan merely repeated earlier unsatisfactory proposals. The Lord Chancellor, Lord Mackay, announced levels of standard or fixed fees for legally aided magistrates' court work, based on recommendations by Price Waterhouse, the management consultancy. Basic standard fees, payable under a two-tier structure, would range from Pounds 127 per case (Pounds 143 in London) to Pounds 497 (Pounds 631 in London) depending on the type and complexity of hearing, plus payments for travelling and waiting time. Some cases, however, judged the most complex or time-consuming, would be assessed for payment by the Legal Aid Board outside the standard fee system. Lord Mackay intends to introduce standard fees by the end of the year. The Law Society, which represents solicitors in England and Wales, said the Price Waterhouse recommendations simply repeated earlier 'flawed and discredited' proposals. 'The consultants' report has much to say about cost control, but virtually nothing about the risk that these proposals will make it more difficult for solicitors to provide a good service to their clients,' it said. 'The society will oppose the proposals with the utmost vigour.'
