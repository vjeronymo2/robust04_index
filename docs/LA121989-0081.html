December 19, 1989, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

STILL DRY: As most of the nation shivers (A35), the problem here is rain -- or the lack of same. . . . WeatherData, a private weather service, says no immediate end is in sight for Southern California's prolonged drought. "Right now, we're starting to get nervous," says meteorologist Rick Dittmann. "This high pressure has been here, it's been persistent for two months now, and although we have some of the rainier months ahead, we do need to start getting some precipitation.". . . The Santa Ana reporting station (B9) shows 1.04 inches of precipitation for the season; normal for this date is 3.01 inches. Dana Parsons and Mark I. Pinsky
