920114 FT 14 JAN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Supporters remain optimistic - National lottery particularly the football pools companies - to recognise that they were backing a losing cause and to concentrate instead on ensuring they benefited from its introduction, David Owen writes. They admitted, however, that a bill to establish a lottery may not complete its passage during the present session. Mr Ivan Lawrence (C Burton), the bill's sponsor, said: 'Even if a general election were to stop the bill's passage through Parliament, if public support is shown to be strong enough the next government will almost have to establish a national lottery.' The proposed legislation, expected to receive support of MPs in both major parties, will be debated in the Commons at the head of the list of private members' bills on Friday. Mr Lawrence yesterday projected that a lottery would raise about Pounds 3bn a year, of which Pounds 1bn would be paid out in prizes and a further Pounds 1bn allocated to 'good causes.'
