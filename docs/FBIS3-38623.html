Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and Germany have agreed to take all necessary steps to boost India-German cooperation in industrial and technical fields. The two countries have agreed to set up an India-Germany joint mechanism to monitor progress in scientific cooperation. An official spokesman summing up the prime minister's visit to Bonn said both the countries have also agreed to review the avoidance of double taxation treaty and pursue efforts to sign bilateral investment guarantees. Our correspondent Sudarshnam reports from Bonn: [Begin Sudarshnam recording] The Federation of German Industry and India-German Chamber of Commerce have sent 54 proposals to intensify the economic relationship between the two countries. These relate to general and financial issues, pharmaceuticals and chemical industries, private sector and air traffic. As many as 40 ministries and departments in India examined these proposals. Several of these have been implemented. Decision in respect of some are awaited. [end recording] In another development, leading political parties of Germany have assured India that the New Asia concept initiated by Chancellor Helmut Kohl making India as a target country for intensified bilateral cooperation will be honored whichever party comes to power in this year's German elections. The assurance was conveyed to the prime minister, Mr. P.V. Narasimha Rao, when important German political leaders called on him in Bonn yesterday. An official spokesman said that there is a multiparty support to Chancellor Kohl's New Asia concept, as Germany has willingly recognized that Japan, China and India in Asia will be important political and economic powers in the years to come. The chief of the German Agency for Aid has shown his willingness to open a second window for soft credit to finance infrastructure projects in India. The projects include power and telecommunications sector. Meanwhile, the minister of state for external affairs, Mr. Salman Khurshid, who is accompanying the prime minister, has reiterated India's willingness to improve bilateral relations with Pakistan. He made it clear that Islamabad has no locus standi in Kashmir, and it is not in a position to advise India in this matter. The minister said India has no problems regarding protection of human rights, and it is always ready to be open with international human rights organizations. As reported, the prime minister is leaving for Berlin today. He will meet the German president and address the Humboldt University. Mr. Narasimha Rao is also scheduled to have meeting with German business leaders and inaugurate the Indian Cultural Center in Berlin. On Sunday [6 February], Mr. Narasimha Rao is returning home. Our correspondent Karuppaswamy reports that his meeting with German indologists and business leaders will be continuation of his earlier exprience in Bonn to reassure Germans that the reform process in India is irreversible. The visit of the prime minister is the first official visit from India to the reunited Berlin -- the upcoming capital of the united Germany.
