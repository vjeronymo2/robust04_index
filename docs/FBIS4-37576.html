BFN [Text] (Robert Jejacques), member of the French Senate and deputy chairman of the French-Yemeni Friendship Society, left Sanaa this afternoon after a two-day visit to our country. During the visit, he met with several House of Representatives and government officials for talks on relations between our country and the Republic of France, as well as developments in the Republic of Yemen. In a statement to the Yemeni News Agency, SABA', the French senator clarified that he had met with the brother House of Representatives speaker and the acting prime minister in order to assess France's position toward developments in the Republic of Yemen and to stress the need not to harm Yemen's unity in light of Security Council Resolution No. 924. He said [words indistinct] the government in France of the Yemeni Government's position [words indistinct). The French official added that his country continues to support unity, legitimacy, and democracy in Yemen as part of the position the French president declared when he visited the Republic of Yemen, adding that this position will continue even under the worst circumstances, as is the case these days. (Robert Jejacques) also expressed his admiration for democracy in our country, which was adopted as a result of the elections on 27 April 1993. He also voiced his admiration for the Yemeni people's adherence to their unity, and said that the dialogues he held with Yemeni officials and citizens show great confidence, popular will, and support for the legitimate government, which expresses the citizens' feelings. He added that this will help in developing Yemen and that because of its unionist and democratic trend, Yemen will occupy a leading position in the Arab homeland. The French senator added that he met with the health minister and reviewed with him spheres of cooperation between Yemen and France in the health arena, particularly the lists of medicines presented by France. He also praised efforts [word indistinct] process to receive and distribute these medicines in record time--efforts that will encourage the dispatch of more medicines. He was seen off by 'Abd-al-Hadi al-Hamdani, deputy chairman of the Political and Foreign Relations Committee of the General People's Congress; 'Abd-al-Wahhab al-Rawhani, member of the House of Representatives; and Muhammad (?al-Qasus), member of the Board of Directors of the Yemeni-French Friendship Society.
