BFN [Report by C. Govender] [Text] Durban April 5 SAPA -- The African National Congress and the Inkatha Freedom Party [IFP] jointly announced on Tuesday [5 April] that international mediation scheduled for Thursday has been postponed to next week. The move is to clear the way for a summit between ANC President Nelson Mandela, IFP leader Mangosuthu Buthelezi, Zulu King Goodwill Zwelithini and State President F W de Klerk. A joint statement said progress with the international mediation was "assessed in the context of current developments". "In the light of the pending summit of leaders on Friday, the meeting decided to delay the start of the mediation until next week. "It is hoped the leaders' summit will help build consensus on the purpose and terms of reference of the international mediation." Mediators were being informed of the decision, the statement concluded. Senior ANC and IFP officials, meeting at the World Trade Centre in Kempton Park [words indistinct] mediation.
