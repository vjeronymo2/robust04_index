BFN [Text] Salvador, Brazil, 11 May (PL) -- Cuban President Fidel Castro arrived today in this northeastern Brazilian city during a service stopover on his return flight from South Africa to Cuba. The Cuban president visited Pretoria, the South African capital, where he participated in the inauguration of President Nelson Mandela. The Cubana de Aviacion flight landed at the Dos de Julio Air Base in the capital of Bahia state, where Fidel Castro and his entourage were greeted by current Governor Antonio Imbassahy, former governor Carlos Magalhaes, Ramon Sanchez Parodi, the Cuban ambassador to Brazil, and other local personalities. This is the second time the Cuban president has visited Salvador and the fourth time he has visited Brazil. Fidel visited Brasilia in early 1990 for the presidential inauguration that year. In June 1992 he attended the world summit on the environment in Rio de Janeiro, and in July 1993 he visited Salvador Bahia to participate in the third Ibero-American conference. According to official sources cited by the local press, the Cuban president will remain approximately 12 hours in Brazilian territory, although no official agenda was announced for his visit. Organizations of friendship and solidarity with Cuba as well as other associations in Bahia said they wanted to give Fidel Castro a great reception.
