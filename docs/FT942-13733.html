940423 FT 23 APR 94 / Ex-defence chief jailed for year A former senior defence official who took thousands of pounds in 'backhanders' over armaments contracts was jailed for a year yesterday at Southwark Crown Court. Mr Trevelyan, of Rowley Regis, West Midlands - who headed the Ministry of Defence's light armoured engineering systems - was convicted of four counts of corruption involving a total of Pounds 8,652 and one charge of attempted corruption, which could have netted him a further Pounds 28,580. Mr David Oliver, of Camberley, Surrey, managing director of defence agents Imvec, was fined Pounds 4,000 after being found guilty of corruptly paying Mr Trevelyan, a close friend, Pounds 2,000 for a report on armoured vehicles.
