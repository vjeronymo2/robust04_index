Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] The governments of Cuba and Namibia today in Windhoek signed an important commercial agreement granting each other favored-nation treatment. The three-year agreement was signed by Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina and Namibian Trade and Industry Minister Hidipo Hamutenya. The agreement states that the signing countries will make all payments in freely convertible currency and in accordance with the laws of the two countries. Robaina and Hamutenya agreed that the bilateral agreement will open the way for identifying other areas of cooperation. The Cuban foreign minister today concludes his three-day official visit to Namibia, the last leg of his two-week tour of Africa, during which he visited Ghana, Angola, Zimbabwe, and Zambia. Before leaving Namibia, Foreign Minister Robaina will meet with Namibian President Sam Nujoma.
