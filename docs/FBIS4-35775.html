BFN [Editorial Report] Beirut AL-SAFIR in Arabic in its 21 May issue features a straightforward 250-word report on page 1 citing REUTER, AFP, AP, and UPI on the resignation of U.S. Ambassador to Israel Edward Djerejian. The report says that Djerejian decided to resign because he was excluded by the State Department from the decisionmaking process in the Syrian-Israeli track of the peace negotiations. The report cites a university source in Washington as saying that Djerejian will resign to assume a new position at Rice University in Houston as director of the James A. Baker Institute for Public Policy. The report notes that a spokesman at the U.S. Embassy in Tel Aviv said that Djerejian is resigning for personal and family reasons. Beirut AL-DIYAR in Arabic in its 23 May issue carries a 300-word straightforward report on page 14 citing AFP. The report notes that the State Department has denied the Israeli newspapers' claims that Djerejian resigned because he was "excluded" from the peace process. Beirut AL-NAHAR in Arabic in its 20 May issue carries a 150-word straightforward report on page 1 citing REUTER as saying that Djerejian decided to leave his post after only six months to assume a post at Rice University. The report states that Djerejian may work as a political adviser to James Baker should Baker decide to run for president.
