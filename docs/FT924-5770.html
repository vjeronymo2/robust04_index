921125 FT 25 NOV 92 / Arts: Today's Television Thriller writer PD James is the subject of Bookmark (8.10 BBC2). After a less than happy childhood she missed university, became a housewife, went out to work when her husband's schizophrenia worsened, and rose from hospital administrator to the Home Office. Only at the age of 40 did she create Det. Chief Insp. Adam Dalgliesh who subsequently became a television favourite. For years there have been hints in Private Eye of the possible involvement of Margaret Thatcher's son Mark in armament deals. Today Dispatches looks at what part he 'may' have played in the sale of equipment to Iraq (9.00 C4). There were lots of sniffy reactions to Episode 1 of Guy Hibbert's serial Nice Town because it's all about sex. It opened with a wet nurse being relieved of her burden by a keenly co-operative chap on a train, and proceeded via sperm donation, teenage sex in a garden shed, and abortion, to a black suspender seduction scene in an 'Elizabethan' hotel where the staff 'thee' and 'thou' you. Perhaps it was the lack of reassuringly familiar violence that offended people. It is edgy but often funny (9.25 BBC2).
