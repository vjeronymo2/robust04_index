May 26, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

NCCJ WILL ASK SUPREME COURT TO RECONSIDER PEYOTE RULING

The National Conference of Christians and Jews has publicly aligned itself with an appeal in the U.S. judicial system for the first time in its 62-year history.

The organization has announced that it will support an effort asking the U.S. Supreme Court to reconsider its April 17 decision in a dispute over whether the First Amendment protects the religious use of the drug peyote.

On May 10, a diverse coalition of religious and civil liberties organizations, joined by 55 constitutional law scholars, had filed a petition urging the court to re-examine its ruling, which limits the application of a "compelling state interest" test in free exercise of religion cases.

In a statement, national conference board Chairman Ira A. Lipman and President Jacqueline G. Wexler said they would back the petition because they "are not convinced the court has given sufficient consideration to the ways in which the decision may limit valid expressions of religious liberty."

They said they "are not questioning the power of states to regulate the use of peyote but are distressed by the constitutional argument put forth by the court majority in the case. . . ."
