BFN [Text] Schwerin (DDP/ADN) -- In the view of Chancellor Helmut Kohl, "stability criteria are more important than the calendar" for European currency union. Kohl said in Schwerin today, during German-Spanish government consultations, that the national economies must "reach maturity" as a precondition for the planned currency union of the European states. The "calendar" for currency union could be postponed, but cannot be abandoned, Kohl stressed. Kohl and Spanish Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez are to talk further today about the topic of the European currency union. Gonzalez pointed out that his two days of talks in Schwerin are his first visit to one of the new federal German states.
