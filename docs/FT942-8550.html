940520 FT 20 MAY 94 / Economic Watch: Swiss trade slides into deficit Switzerland's trade balance in April recorded a deficit of SFr6.8m (Pounds 3.17m) following a surplus of SFr125.3m in March, the government customs office announced yesterday. In April, both imports and exports declined substantially from March and from April 1993. Imports totalled SFr7.55bn, down from SFr8.92bn in March and from SFr8.0bn in April 1993. Exports amounted to SFr7.54bn, down from SFr9.05bn in March and down from SFr8.01bn in April 1993. For the period January to April, the surplus was SFr1.55bn against SFr1.185bn in 1993. Exports rose slightly to SFr31.55bn, while imports were little changed at SFr30bn. Spain's finance ministry confirmed that the central government budget deficit on a cash basis shrank 20 per cent to an accumulated Pta598bn (Pounds 2.89bn) in the first four months of 1994 compared with Pta744bn in the same period of 1993. The Bank of Portugal yesterday lowered its key injection rate to 10.375 per cent from 10.75 per cent and its key absorption rate to 9.375 per cent from 9.75 per cent. The Danish finance ministry reduced its forecast of the 1994 state budget deficit to DKr51bn (Pounds 5.2bn), down from DKr54.4bn in its last forecast in December.
