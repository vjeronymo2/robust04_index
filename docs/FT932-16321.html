930406 FT 06 APR 93 / International Company News: Ontario sells 14% stake in Suncor for CDollars 190m THE PROVINCE of Ontario has ended a controversial and costly 12-year foray into the energy industry by unloading its remaining 14 per cent stake in Suncor, a large Canadian oil and gas producer. Ontario Energy Corporation, a provincial agency, yesterday sold its 7.6m Suncor shares to two securities firms, RBC Dominion Securities and ScotiaMcLeod, at CDollars 25.25 per share. Total proceeds are about CDollars 190m (USDollars 150.7m). Suncor's controlling shareholder is Sun Co, the Pennsylvania-based energy producer, with a 67.6 per cent stake. Ontario's involvement with Suncor dates back to the energy crisis of the late 1970s and early 1980s, when the province paid about CDollars 650m for a 25 per cent stake. Suncor suffered a CDollars 228m loss last year, largely as the result of a CDollars 360m restructuring charge. Ontario last year cut its stake in Suncor from 25 per cent to 14 per cent as part of the wider effort to contain the budget shortfall by selling assets.
