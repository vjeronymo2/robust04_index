Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN ["Bring the Role of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC) Into Full Play, Discuss General Strategy for Zhejiang's Reform and Construction; the Second Session of the Seventh Provincial CPPCC Committee Opens" -- ZHEJIANG RIBAO headline] [Excerpts] The second session of the Seventh Zhejiang Provincial Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference ceremoniously opened at Hangzhou's Great Hall of the People yesterday morning. [passage omitted] Provincial CPPCC Committee Chairman Liu Feng; Vice Chairmen Sun Jiaxian, Chen Fawen, Wu Renyuan, Jan Shaowen, Ding Deyun, Xue Yanzhang, Que Duanlin, Geng Dianhua, Zhang Kejian, and Wang Xixuan; Secretary General Li Qing; and several Standing Committee members were seated on the rostrum. Also seated on the rostrum were Li Zemin, Wan Xueyuan, Cai Songyue, Lu Zhangong, Liu Xirong, Liang Pingbo, Si Daxiao, Xu Xingguan, Wang Qidong, Mao Zhaoxi, Long Anding, Zhang Qimei, Xu Zhichun, Yang Shijie, and Shen Lei, provincial party, government, and military leaders; Xia Zhonglie, president of the provincial higher people's court; and Ge Shengping, chief procurator of the provincial people's procuratorate, who went to the meeting to extend their greetings. [passage omitted] Chairman Liu Feng chaired the meeting and gave an opening speech. He said: The primary mission of this session is to use Comrade Deng Xiaoping's theory of building socialism with Chinese characteristics and the party's basic line as the guide, deepen implementation of the guidelines of the Third Plenary Session of the 14th CPC Central Committee and the ninth provincial party congress, further emancipate our minds, achieve unity in understanding, review past year's work, set forth this year's tasks, make full use of the CPPCC's strong points, perform its functions in political consultation and democratic supervision, and discuss the general strategy for Zhejiang's reform and construction. [passage omitted] Vice Chairman Sun Jiaxian gave a report on the work of the standing committee. [passage omitted]
