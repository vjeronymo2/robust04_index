Language: Mandarin Article Type:BFN [Text] The year-long lawsuit of Hong Kong businessman Tsui Bao-jen charging the Lanzhou Qilihe District People's Government with violating his rights as an agent was ruled on by the provincial higher people's court; and the case was closed with the accusant's charge rejected, and Tsui Bao-jen was to pay the cost of some 68,000 yuan involved in the trial. A year before, Hong Kong businessman Tsui Bao-jen brought a case to the provincial higher people's court on the grounds that Qilihe District Government had violated his rights as an agent in marketing China-grown lily bulbs in Hong Kong because he had discovered an advertisement of the Hong Kong Maiya Trade Company claiming to be an agent in marketing lily bulbs, thus he demanded Qilihe District Government to acertain his general agent rights in Hong Kong, while claiming a 12.4 million yuan indemnity. Through hearing of the provincial higher people's court, it was held that the contract signed between the accusant Tsui Bao-jen and the relavant department in Qilihe district explicitly stipulated that the term of Tsui Bao-jen acting as agent was five years. When the term expired, the contract was not renewed; consequently, Tsui's status of the agent ended automatically.
