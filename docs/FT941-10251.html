940210 FT 10 FEB 94 / Observer: Front-bench etc Ever since George Robertson was promoted from Labour spokesman on Europe to shadow secretary of state for Scotland, he's never seemed very happy with the promotion. Yesterday he returned to his old stamping-ground at the Royal Institute of International Affairs, where he spoke on 'The decentralisation tide: the role of the nations and regions in Europe'. Recalling his most famous hour, supervising Labour's contribution to the Maastricht debates, Robertson lamented that all he gets to deal with now is riveting stuff like the Local Government Etc (Scotland) Bill. 'It's like Maastricht without the laughs,' he glumly acknowledged.
