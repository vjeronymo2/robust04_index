930216 FT 16 FEB 93 / World Trade News: Japan and China agree to bilateral negotiations Japan and China have agreed to start bilateral negotiations on tariffs on Japanese goods, to help Beijing's re-entry into the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (Gatt), a Foreign Ministry official said, Reuter reports from Tokyo. This is the first time China will have such negotiations with a G7 country, the official said. Date for the talks is not yet fixed. Officials from the two governments met in Beijing over the weekend for a regular two-day meeting to exchange views on bilateral trade issues.
