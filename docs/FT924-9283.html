921106 FT 06 NOV 92 / Councils warn of rise in tax LOCAL authorities claim government plans to tighten the collection of business rates could lead to unexpected increases in next year's council tax bills. The Local Government Chronicle says today that the plans have 'angered council treasurers', who fear that council tax bills will be significantly higher than original government estimates of between Pounds 267 and Pounds 800. The business rate is set by the government, but councils collect it. Under proposals set out in a consultation document, councils deemed by the Department of the Environment and the district auditor not to be diligent in collecting rates will be liable to make good the shortfall. Councils pay their business rate revenue into the national non-domestic rates pool, which bears all the costs of non-collection. The consultation document proposes that councils should no longer be able to adjust their payments to the fund for non-collection 'where collection of debts has been jeopardised by poor collection and enforcement performance'. The Department of the Environment said the change was necessary because claims by councils for non-collection would no longer be audited before being submitted. 'We have no evidence that local authorities are making unduly high provision for bad debts,' it added. Last year 95 per cent of the assessed business rate was collected nationwide. Mr Ewan Rayner, president of the Association of District Council Treasurers, said the proposed rule was 'extremely unfair' because it could place additional burdens on council tax payers 'who can have no responsibility whatsoever for business rates non-payment'. The head of Britain's leading association for local tax experts yesterday warned that many people in local government had a 'death wish' over the introduction of the council tax. Mr Colin Farrington, director of the Institute of Revenues, Rating and Valuation said: 'Whatever short-term difficulties there may be, it is time for us to reiterate that the new property tax for local government is basically sound.'
