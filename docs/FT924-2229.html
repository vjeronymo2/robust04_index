921214 FT 14 DEC 92 / Observer: Damage control The Chicago Board of Trade couldn't have asked for better weather for the launch of its new catastrophe insurance futures contracts. As trading began on Friday, gale force winds, rain, and flooding brought New York city to a halt and battered the East coast. Unfortunately, the first day's trading volume was virtually non-existent because the New Jersey-based property claims reporting service, that the CBOT relies upon to keep traders informed of estimated damages, was put out of action by mother nature.
