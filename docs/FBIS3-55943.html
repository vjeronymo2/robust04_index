Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Stepan Valentinnikov report under the "Around Lefortovo" rubric: "Bonus With a Protest"] [Text] An extremely curious document and a short commentary on it have been published in the first issue of the newspaper MEGAPOLIS-KONTINENT.... Directive No. 2105-r of the Russian Federation Council of Ministers-Government dated Moscow, 23 November 1993. 1. The Russian Federation General Prosecutor's Office shall be permitted to provide workers of investigative and operational brigades investigating crimes committed 3-4 October 1993 with material assistance equivalent to three months' salary. 2. The Russian Ministry of Finance shall to this end allocate 116.7 million rubles from the Russian Federation republic's budget. [Signed] O. Soskovets, first deputy chairman of the Russian Federation Council of Ministers-Government. We would like to believe that the government directive we have reproduced here is a joke. But the source, which passed the document to the editorial office, is entirely trustworthy. Of course, these two short paragraphs of an unclassified and seemingly ordinary government decision raise a number of questions. For example, why should the government permit the General Prosecutor's Office -- an independent state organ -- to do something when the actions of government officials are subject to scrutiny by this very same prosecutor's office? And why should workers at the prosecutor's office investigating October's bloodshed suddenly be allocated up to three (!) times their normal salary? Have they become destitute? Or are they no longer working for money? Or is it perhaps just compensation for the moral damage that this sort of investigation inflicts on the souls of the sensitive prosecutors? However, let them have it, these 116.7 million rubles from a budget that is already showing a deficit. The simple fact is that, judging from this document, yet another myth has been exploded -- the myth of the independence of the General Prosecutor's Office. How sad it is to part with our illusions about the rule-of-law state....
