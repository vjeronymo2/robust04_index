December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

ABOUT THE TOP MOVIES

If your selection of the best movies is any indication, the 1980s was a depressing decade indeed.

The best films in the 1980s emphasized brutal, cold-blooded violence and sexual perversion ("Blue Velvet," "Once Upon a Time in America," "Prizzi's Honor"), marital turmoil ("War of the Roses," "Shoot the Moon"), drug abuse ("Drugstore Cowboy"), child degeneration ("Pixote" -- about a 10-year-old pimp, drug user and murderer) and even offered a bleak look at the future ("Brazil," "The Road Warrior" and "Blade Runner").

Hopefully, movies in the 1990s will be a little more uplifting.

KENNETH L. ZIMMERMAN

Cypress
