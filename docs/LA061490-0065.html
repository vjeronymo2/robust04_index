June 14, 1990, Thursday, Valley Edition

AMERICAN LEGION BASEBALL ROUNDUP;

WILBUR'S 4 HITS SPARK WOODLAND HILLS EAST

Woodland Hills East third baseman Carey Wilbur was enjoying a four-for-four performance Wednesday when something terrible happened. His team kept scoring runs and he had to make another trip to the plate.

Wilbur made an out in his final at-bat, ruining a perfect day, but his team knocked off Granada Hills, 10-5, in an American Legion District 20 game at Granada Hills High.

East trailed, 3-2, in the fifth when Glen Nahmias tripled home three runs to key a six-run rally.

Right-hander Eric Rothman (3-0) scattered nine hits, walked two and struck out four. Shortstop Benji Belfield was two for four with a double for East (6-1).

Gabe Miller hit a solo homer in the seventh for Granada Hills (2-5).

In other District 20 games:

Las Virgenes 4, Chatsworth 3 (eight innings) -- Bret Resnick hit for the cycle and tripled in the winning run with two out in the eighth to lead Las Virgenes at Chatsworth.

Resnick finished with three runs batted in and teammates Paul Schaffer and Tom Hoover each had two hits for Las Virgenes (4-4).

Harold Turk, Dusty Wood and Paul Johnson combined on a six-hitter. Johnson, who pitched three scoreless innings of relief, earned the win. Rodney Bloom and Wes Doll each had two hits for Chatsworth.

Valley Encino-Tarzana 14, North Hollywood 5 -- Catcher Seth Rosenzweig hit a two-run home run in the third inning, then hit a bases-loaded triple in an 11-run fourth inning to finish with five RBIs and pace Valley ET (3-2) at Birmingham.

Mike Belasco, Noah Kirshbaum, Mike Malkin and winning pitcher Jason Mansfield (1-1) each had two hits for Valley ET.

Woodland Hills West 4, Encino-Crespi 1 -- Paul Geller and Pat Treend combined on a five-hitter to break a three-game losing streak for West (2-3).

Geller (1-0) pitched four innings, allowed five hits, struck out four and walked none. In three innings of relief, Treend struck out eight and walked none.

Chris Teich and Geller each had two hits for West, which has recorded both of its victories at the expense of Encino-Crespi.

Burbank 7, Van Nuys-Notre Dame 3 -- Burbank took a 3-0 lead in the first off spot starter Vinnie Orlando, who walked five batters in the inning at Notre Dame.

Bobby Medina was three for three with two RBIs and Bobby Sherwood was two for two with two RBIs for Burbank.

Jason McConaghy was two for two for Van Nuys-Notre Dame (5-1), which had four players on vacation.

Reseda 19, Agoura 6 -- Ari Jacobs and Joe Arnold each went three for five to pace Reseda (3-4) at Agoura.

Jacobs drove in three runs to support winner Robert Reynoso (1-0). Geoff Taylor went two for two for Reseda.
