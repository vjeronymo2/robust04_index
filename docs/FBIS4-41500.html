BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Sergey Borovkov, Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, June 26 (TASS) -- A State Duma delegation, led by its speaker Ivan Rybkin, which is continuing its working trip to Siberia and the Far East, arrived in Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky late on Saturday. In Kamchatka the legislators will meet representatives of the local government bodies, naval officers and sailors, fishermen and workers of fish-processing enterprises. Rybkin will visit the famous Geyser valley and Paramushir, one of the Kurile islands. Previously the delegation visited Irkutsk, where they took part in the work of the Siberian agreement council. It includes 19 republics, autonomous and national districts, territories and regions of Siberia. During the work of the Siberian agreement council Rybkin and heads of the main State Duma committees told in detail representatives of the executive and legislative authorities of Siberian regions about the law-making work of the lower chamber of the federal assembly and its prospects. Rybkin told journalists at the airport that the legislators "wished to see for themselves the life in those cities, which are so far away but are still our, Russian cities, to talk with people, to hear the opinion of heads of local administrations. We want advice on the best way to divide the pie which is called the budget."
