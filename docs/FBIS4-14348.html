BFN [Text] Tehran, April 26, IRNA -- The Philippine ambassador here, Oscar Valenzuela and First Deputy Foreign Minister Mohammad Hashemi, on Tuesday discussed the holding of the Tehran-Manila joint economic commission. The two sides agreed that the commission was an ideal forum to improve bilateral relations and explore avenues of cooperation. Valenzuela called for further expansion of bilateral relations and cooperation in light of the economic potential of the two countries. Hashemi suggested that cooperation between regional organizations such as the Economic Cooperation Organization (ECO) and the Association of South East Asian Nations (ASEAN), would prove useful to both sides. Philippine is a member of the ASEAN which also includes Brunei, Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore and Thailand, while Iran is a founding member of the 10-nation ECO, whose other members are Pakistan, Turkey, Afghanistan, Tajikistan, Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan and Turkmenistan.
