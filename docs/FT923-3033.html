920915 FT 15 SEP 92 / Irish to launch new phone tap investigation A police investigation is to be launched into Ireland's latest political scandal - the tapping of the telephone of Mr John Bruton, the leader of the main opposition party, Fine Gael, writes Tim Coone in Dublin. Mr Patrick Culligan, the Irish police commissioner, ordered the inquiry yesterday after a Sunday newspaper published transcripts of three alleged conversations between Mr Bruton and senior party colleagues that took place last January. The conversations related to revelations by a former justice minister about telephone tapping that occurred 10 years earlier. The January incident led to the resignation of Mr Charles Haughey, the prime minister, who was replaced by Mr Albert Reynolds. During the leadership contest, Mr Reynolds complained that he suspected he was under surveillance. Mr Padraig Flynn, appointed justice minister by Mr Reynolds, said yesterday: 'As far as my information is concerned, certainly there was no authorisation by the minister insofar as any illegal tapping of this kind.'
