Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Hague (AA) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin on Thursday [24 February] said that the presence of any foreign organization's observers during the coming local elections in Turkey is unnacceptable, adding Turkey is a democratic country and anyone can come and observe as an individual. He was speaking at a luncheon he hosted in honour of his counterpart, Peter Kooijmans, at the Turkish Embassy in The Hague. After the luncheon, Cetin met with members of the Foreign Ministry commission of the Netherlands' parliament. Speaking to reporters after the meeting, Cetin said his contacts in The Hague have been very constructive, adding talks focused on terrorism, the sitaution in Bosnia-Herzegovina and economic relations. On terrorism Cetin said that it is the international responsability of all countries not to support terrorist organizations, adding the NATO and UN resolutions necessitate this. Cetin also said that the Turkish citizens living in Turkey [as received] will be able to vote during the local elections.
