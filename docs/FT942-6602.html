940530 FT 30 MAY 94 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System The order of the EMS grid is unchanged. The Irish punt remains the strongest currency, but the Dutch guilder and Belgian franc made up ground last week. The escudo remains bottom, with traders predicting that it might come under attack this week. Currencies, Page 27 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the system. Most of the currencies are permitted to fluctuate within 15 per cent of agreed central rates against the other members of the mechanism. The exceptions are the D-Mark and the guilder which move in a 2.25 per cent band.
