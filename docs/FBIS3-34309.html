Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, 6 Feb (INA)--Rolf Ekeus, chairman of the UN Special Commission, has described as positive his talks in Baghdad, which he concluded yesterday. In a joint news conference with Riyad al-Qaysi, under secretary of the Foreign Ministry, here today, Ekeus said that the results of these talks have augmented the progress that the two sides have made during the past months. Ekeus stressed that these talks have bolstered the mutual trust between Iraq and the UN Special Commission, thus providing a better atmosphere for both sides to implement relevant UN resolutions and for lifting the embargo on Iraqi exports. He said that the two sides have agreed that the Commission and the International Atomic Energy Agency will submit as soon as possible their report to the UN Security Council on Iraq's adoption of the required measures in accordance with Paragraph 22 of UN Security Council Resolution 687. He stressed that in their talks, both sides dealt with political as well as technical issues related to the UN resolutions. For his part, Dr. Riyad al-Qaysi stressed that progress has been made toward the continuation of the two sides' early implementation of Paragraph 22 of Resolution 687. He added: From our point of view, Iraq has implemented all the measures pertaining to that paragraph. Dr. al-Qaysi indicated that both sides have agreed to continue cooperation, aiming for a speedy implementation of Paragraph 22 of the aforementioned resolution.
