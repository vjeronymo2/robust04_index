Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Rooigrond, Bophuthatswana March 10 SAPA -- A convoy of 20 vehicles carrying heavily armed khaki-clad rightwingers heading for Bophuthatswana's capital Mmabatho was diverted at a roadblock at Rooigrond, near the SA [South Africa] border, at about 11.30 PM on Thursday [10 March]. The convoy of cars and bakkies [pickup trucks] were each occupied by at least four or five khaki-clad men. According to SA Police manning the roadblock at Rooigrond, about 20km from Mmabatho, the convoy was re-routed to Buhrmons Drift. According to police, the road through Buhmorns Drift did offer access to Mmbatho. As no law had been broken, the convoy was allowed to proceed. [Johannesburg SAPA in English on 11 March at 0745 GMT reports that "the Afrikaner Weerstandbeweging [Resistance Movement -- AWB] has between 4,000 and 5,000 men in Mmabatho, AWB Gen Steven Meninger told journalists on Friday morning. The men had made the Bophuthatswana Air Force base their headquarters and they were deployed all over town, he said. AWB leader Eugene Terreblanche is also said to be in Mmabatho to take charge of his men."]
