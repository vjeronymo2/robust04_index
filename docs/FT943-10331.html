940805 FT 05 AUG 94 / UK Company News: BTP in agreed Pounds 12.6m German acquisition BTP, the speciality chemicals group, has conditionally agreed to acquire AGS Industrieklebstoffe, a German adhesives business, for DM30.6m (Pounds 12.6m) cash. Mydrin, BTP's subsidiary, has entered into the agreement with Scheidemandel, a subsidiary of Deutsche Gelatine. Some DM15.5m of the purchase price is to be paid on completion, which is expected to be on August 31. The remainder is due in four instalments between April 1 1995 and August 31 1996. AGS has net assets of DM24.7m and no borrowings. In 1993 sales amounted to DM36.6m and the loss before tax and interest totalled DM3.3m. In the year to March 31, BTP beat City expectations with a 48 per cent jump in annual pre-tax profits, from Pounds 20.5m to Pounds 30.3m. BTP's share price yesterday added 7p to 324p.
