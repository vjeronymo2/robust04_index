BFN [Text] Seoul, May 10 (YONHAP) -- The Vietnamese Government has informed South Korean lawmakers of its official position that it opposes North Korea's nuclear development program, the chairman of the National Assembly Foreign Affairs and National Unification Committee said Tuesday. Chairman Chong Chae-mun and five other committee members visited Vietnam from May 2-7. Chong, speaking to reporters about his tour of the Southeast Asian country, said, "the Vietnamese leaders made clear that nuclear weapons should not exist on the Korean peninsula and even though Vietnam has a communist system like North Korea, it does not support North Korea's lines." On the unification issue, Chong quoted Vietnam's leaders as saying that South and North Korea will eventually accomplish reunification through peaceful dialogue. During their trip the Korean lawmakers met with senior Vietnamese leaders, including former Chief Secretary of the Communist Party Nguyen Van Linh, House Speaker Nong Duc Manh and First Deputy Prime Minister Pham Van Khai, and discussed expanding economic cooperation, supporting children fathered by Koreans, locating Korean soldiers lost during the Vietnam war and returning the remains of Korean soldiers. Also visiting Hanoi were Reps. Pak Chong-su and Kim Tong-kun of the ruling Democratic Liberal Party, Pak Sil and Chin Nam-kung of the opposition Democratic Party and independent Yi Chong-chan.
