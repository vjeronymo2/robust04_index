BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- Two days after the Saxony Anhalt Landtag election, leading Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] officials spoke out in favor of electing Reinhard Hoeppner, the SPD candidate, as new minister president. Meanwhile, the Christian Democratic Union/Christian Social Union [CDU/CSU] on Tuesday [28 June] warned Hoeppner and the SPD against a "popular front alliance" with the Party of Democratic Socialism [PDS]. In an interview with STERN magazine, Lower Saxon Minister President Gerhard Schroeder (SPD) said that, in the sphere of social policy, the PDS was certainly closer to the SPD than the CDU on some points. On north German radio, Schroeder said that questions of possible forms of cooperation between the SPD and the PDS would have to be answered in Saxony Anhalt. In an interview for STERN magazine, Deputy SPD Chairman Oskar Lafontaine described the CDU warnings against the PDS as "impertinent." "After all, it was the CDU that took over the East CDU, an affiliate of the Socialist Unity Party of Germany, and all its assets. Kohl is not only -- what irony of history -- Adenauer's grandson but also Honecker's heir," Lafontaine said in the interview, parts of which were made available to DPA. In Lafontaine's view, there can only be one consequence after the election in Saxony Anhalt: "Hoeppner must become minister president." For Juergen Ruettgers, the parliamentary manager of the CDU/CSU Bundestag group, the voters of Saxony Anhalt decided that the CDU should form a government. In his view, the Saxony Anhalt SPD group is under pressure from the federal party. "The SPD has broken Hoeppner's backbone," Ruettgers said. The discussion on the PDS tolerating an SPD minority government only makes sense when "popular front alliances" can no longer be ruled out. Similar arguments were brought forth by Johannes Gerster, deputy chairman of the CDU/CSU Bundestag group, and Michael Glos, head of the CSU group in the Bundestag. If the SPD were to accept the support of the PDS in the election of minister president, then Willy Brandt's words that those things that belong together will grow together would become reality in the socialist sector, Glos said. Gerster told NEUE OSNABRUECKER ZEITUNG that the SPD-Greens minority government, which the SPD was seeking, would "directly lead to a popular front alliance." SPD Chairman Rudolf Scharping and Hoeppner seem to have become megalomaniac to believe that the CDU would tolerate a minority government between the SPD and Alliance 90/Greens, Gerster said.
