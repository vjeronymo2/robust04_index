940407 FT 07 APR 94 / International Company News: Pakistan plans to offer 25% bank stake The Pakistan government is planning to offer 25 per cent of its shareholding in the Allied Bank of Pakistan for public subscription within a few weeks, the bank said, Reuter reports from Karachi. Allied, with an authorised capital of Rs500m (Dollars 16.5m) and paid-up capital of Rs272m, is Pakistan's only bank owned and managed by its employees. It was privatised in September 1991. The employees' allied management group owns 51 per cent of shares and the government 49 per cent, said Mr Rasheed Chaudhry, a bank director. Mr Chaudhry said the shares would be offered at a price of Rs70 per share. He declined to give further details.
