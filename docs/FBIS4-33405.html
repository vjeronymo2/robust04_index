BFN [Text] Belem do Para (Brazil), 6 Jun (EFE) -- Today, Juan Aristides Tavares, Dominican foreign relations secretary of state, said the Government of the Dominican Republic guarantees it will maintain its border with Haiti, which was closed in compliance with the total embargo imposed by the United Nations on this Caribbean nation. Tavares indicated to EFE that there is a disposition within the 24th OAS General Assembly, which was inaugurated today in the Brazilian city of Belem do Para, to ensure the total effectiveness of the embargo imposed on Haiti in May by the UN Security Council. Tavares pointed out that the Dominican Republic is the nation most harmed by the Haitian political crisis because both nations share the island of Hispaniola and also because of the pressures it has been exposed to in order to implement the embargo. "No country has had as many problems as we have had. So much has been demanded of us; it is as if we were the only ones" responsible for contributing to the crisis, the foreign relations secretary of state indicated. He added that his government has supported all resolutions related to issues approved by the OAS and the UN Security Council. "Although the embargo has not been completely effective, we have not violated it intentionally, and, moreover, at this time, our government has ordered the closing of the border," the Dominican Republic foreign secretary said. The Haitian political crisis is the main issue dominating the 24th OAS General Assembly sessions which will be extended until next Friday and where 70 proposals related to democracy, human rights, security, technical cooperation, environment, counternarcotics, and the economic situation throughout the American continent will be discussed. Full compliance with the total embargo, especially the guarantee that the Dominican Republic will not violate the 350-km-wide border with Haiti, and the rejection of armed intervention to depose the military coupists are the two main proposals to be discussed during the Belem do Para meeting. Haitian President Jean-Bertrand Aristide, deposed by the military in September 1991 and still considered the legitimate president of Haiti by the international community, participated today in the Assembly inauguration. Aristide has met with Brazilian President Itamar Franco. Secretary of State Tavares pointed out that the OAS and UN technical observers, who recently visited his country, were able to ascertain that the total embargo is being implemented "firmly along the Dominican-Haitian border."
