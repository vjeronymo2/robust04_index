Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 1 KYODO -- Japan will extend 10 million dollars to cooperate in a United Nations program to reconstruct Somalia's police and judicial system, the Foreign Ministry said Tuesday [1 February]. Japan pledged the amount at a U.N.-sponsored meeting in Nairobi on the establishment of an organization to coordinate assistance to Somalia, the ministry said. The government will provide the assistance considering the urgent need to reconstruct Somalia's police and judicial system and establish a system to maintain public order and restore peace by the Somali people themselves, it said. The U.N. Operation in Somalia (UNOSOM II), currently deployed to maintain the public order and secure safety of humanitarian assistance activities, announced last October a program to reconstruct the local police and judicial system. The U.N. is calling on member countries to cooperate in the program, the ministry said.
