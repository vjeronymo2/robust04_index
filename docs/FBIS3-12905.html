Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The radio's central news unit correspondent has learned that Jacques Neriya, Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin's political adviser, met with Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat in Cairo today. The Israeli envoy presented the Palestinian president specific Israeli ideas on overcoming the crisis resulting from the attack on worshipers in the al-Ibrahimi Mosque and on creating a general climate that will allow the PLO to resume the negotiations. President 'Arafat discussed with the Israeli prime minister's adviser the measures that have to be taken to protect the Palestinians in the Arab territories. These measures include the protection of the Palestinians, provision of security for them, and a nonrepetition of the Hebron incident. The radio correspondent also learned that the Palestinian side stressed the importance of disarming the settlers, restricting or dissolving the extremist organizations, removing the settlers from Hebron and densely populated Palestinian areas, and providing the necessary protection for the Palestinians. President 'Arafat met with Neriya before the former's departure from Cairo this afternoon.
