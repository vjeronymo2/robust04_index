940121 FT 21 JAN 94 / Campaign against copier contracts Dissatisfied customers of Southern Business Group yesterday stepped up pressure on the office equipment supplier by launching a support network to campaign on their behalf. Backed by Mr Nigel Griffiths, Labour's consumer affairs spokesman, the customers are seeking the appointment of an independent arbiter in disputes over the terms of controversial 'cost per copy' photocopier contracts previously used by the company. The contracts combine the costs of leasing and service over a certain period, during which charges may be raised. Southern Business Group, which last year sacked 12 employees following the discovery of 'improprieties' in dealings with some customers, has agreed to support the appointment of an ombudsman for the photocopier industry. However, funding is uncertain.
