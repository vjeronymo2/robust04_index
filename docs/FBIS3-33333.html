Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Up to Date" newscast] [Text] Minister of the Presidency Oscar Alfredo Santamaria today denied that the government received any official document sent by the UN secretary general on nonfulfillment of the peace agreements. During this past weekend, FMLN [Farabundo Marti National Liberation Front] coordinator Shafik Handal reported that Butrus Butrus-Ghali told a delegation of former guerrillas who were visiting the United Nations that he had sent a letter to President Alfredo Cristiani in which he asked him to accelerate the application of some agreements, such as the deployment of the PNC [Civilian National Police], the former fighters' rejoining civilian life, and the transfer of land. A UN spokesman recently notified an FMLN delegation that traveled to the United Nations that Butrus Butrus-Ghali had sent a letter to President Cristiani requesting that he make use of his power with the same interest so that some pending agreements related to the PNC and the transfer of land be implemented. However, the minister of the presidency denied this report. [Begin Santamaria recording] We have not received any notification from the UN secretary general. [end recording] Santamaria explained that the government, the FMLN, and Onusal [UN Observers for El Salvador] are working on a new schedule with regard to the deployment of the PNC. The official added that nonetheless, the PNC issue is moving ahead well with regard to deployment. He also said that it is false that the government is not interested in fulfilling its commitments. [Begin Santamaria recording] The United Nations has made no such statement. All I can say is that at the end of its transition period, this government is committed to having concluded and implemented all of its agreements. Naturally, some of them will continue, because some agreements are scheduled to be concluded after the transition period. [end recording] Nonetheless, Dr. Santamaria admitted there are some delays in the programs to have the former fighters rejoining civilian life related to the transfer of land. According to him, this is due to the lack of financial support which the international community had promised to solve.
