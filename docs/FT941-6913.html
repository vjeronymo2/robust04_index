940228 FT 28 FEB 94 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System The order of currencies in the EMS grid remained unchanged at the end of a week which saw Spain, France and Belgium trim interest rates. The Spanish peseta, the weakest currency in the grid, made up ground against all the others. The Portuguese escudo ended the week barely stronger than the peseta. Currencies, Page 37 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the system. Most of the currencies can fluctuate within 15 per cent of agreed central rates against the other members of the mechanism. The exceptions are the D-Mark and the guilder which move in a narrow 2.25 per cent band.
