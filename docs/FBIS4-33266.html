BFN [From the "Morning Information Review" newscast] [Text] Cuban Foreign Relations Minister Roberto Robaina has arrived in Qatar for an official three-day visit by invitation of that country's foreign affairs ministry. He was greeted at the capital city airport by Ahmad bin Muhammad Ali al-Subayi, the minister of electricity and water resources. In reply to questions by the local media, Robaina said Cuba is interested in developing ties with Qatar and other gulf states. In this regard, he recalled the recent establishment of diplomatic ties with Oman and added that there is political will to do the same with other countries of the region. In less than 48 hours, the Cuban foreign minister will complete a busy schedule that includes meetings with Chief of State Amir Khalifa bin Hamad Al Thani; the crown prince; and the ministers of foreign affairs, health, and economy and finance. Robaina traveled to Qatar from Cairo where he attended the Nonaligned countries' ministerial meeting.
