BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar yesterday announced that President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada will travel to Colombia on 14 June to attend the Fourth Ibero-American Summit, which is to be held in Cartagena. This will be the first time Sanchez de Lozada will attend this forum, at which 23 presidents and chiefs of state from the continent and Spain meet every year. The summit will last two days. This year the main subject of the summit will be regional trade and democracy. The first meeting was held in Guadalajara, Mexico. Four years ago Bolivia was represented by Jaime Paz Zamora, who was president at that time. Aranibar also announced that Bolivia will send a representative to an international meeting to be held next week within the framework of the OAS annual forum, where countries of the continent will assess the progress made in the struggle against drug trafficking and the cooperation between countries to face this crime.
