BFN [Excerpt] Cairo, 8 May (MENA)--The People's Assembly held a meeting today chaired by Speaker Dr. Ahmad Fathi Surur. It approved the president of the Republic's decree extending Law No. 29 of 1972 empowering the president of the Republic to issue decrees having the strength of law. The law is extended for three years and expires at the end of fiscal year 1994-1996. The bill was opposed by 11 members of the opposition and independents. Commenting on opposition to the bill by deputy Lutfi Wakid of the National Progressive Unionist Grouping Party, Kamal al-Shadhili, minister of state for the People's Assembly and Consultative Council affairs, said that secrecy and speed in approving arms deals are necessary. Thus, the president of the Republic was authorized to issue such decrees, noting that submitting the bill to the People's Assembly in accordance with the law takes place after the president authorizes it and while the authorization is still valid. He stressed that the circumstances the Arab nation has been passing through require that Egypt have a strong army. [passage omitted]
