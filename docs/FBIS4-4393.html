BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 31 KYODO -- Japan loaned more than 1 trillion yen to 27 countries in fiscal 1993, a senior Foreign Ministry official said Thursday [31 March]. It also provided a record high of 999 million yen in small-scale aid grants to 54 nations and one region, the official said. Japan extended a total of 1.034 trillion yen in loans, down 13 percent from the previous fiscal year, the official said. Of the loans that covered 111 projects in 27 countries, 812 billion yen, or 80 percent, went to Asia, the official said. Japan extended loans totaling 6.5 billion yen to Kyrgyzstan, making it the first Central Asian nation to receive Japanese yen loans. Japan also extended a 52.3 billion yen loan to Vietnam for the first time. Yen loans for environmental projects reached 148.7 billion yen, the official said. Japan's small-scale grassroots assistance to recipients such as nongovernmental organizations, medical and educational institutions was up 42.8 percent from the previous year. The aid covered 258 projects in the educational, medical and environmental fields, the official said.
