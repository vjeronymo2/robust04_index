Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Paris, March 16 (AFP) -- Caen Mayor Jean-Marie Girault on Wednesday [16 March] backed down on a plan to invite German Chancellor Helmut Kohl to attend a ceremony there to mark the 50th anniversary of D-Day in Normandy on June 6. Girault, who visited the German embassy here, did not hand over an invitation to Kohl, but one for ambassador Juergen Sudhoff after the two held a meeting, an embassy spokesman said. [passage omitted] Speaking on RTL radio later, Girault said he could not officially invite Kohl, but still wanted the chancellor to attend. He said Sudhoff had accepted the invitation, on condition the Bonn government approved it. [passage omitted] An opinion poll in the French Christian weekly LA VIE said 57 percent of the French favoured a German Government presence at the D-Day ceremonies.
