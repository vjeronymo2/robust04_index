941119 FT 19 NOV 94 / Right to silence move draws fire Prosecutors in Scotland would be allowed to comment on an accused person's silence under government proposals in the Criminal Justice (Scotland) bill, foreshadowed in the Queen's Speech and published yesterday. Labour last night attacked the proposal, saying it was contrary to the views of the Scottish Office's advisers and had not been foreshadowed in the white paper. Mr John McFall, a Labour spokesman, said yesterday: 'The whole basis of the criminal justice system in Scotland rests on the assumption that a person is innocent until proven guilty. This is a fundamental attack on that assumption.'
