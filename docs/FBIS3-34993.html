Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Baghdad, 16 Feb (INA)--Sa'd Qasim Hammudi, secretary general of the Arab Popular Forces Conference, has met with Hugh Stevens, member of the British international war crimes commission. During the meeting, Hammudi asserted that the continued enforcement of the unfair embargo clamped on the Iraqi people is a crime against a people who opposed aggression, injustice, and slavery. For his part, Stevens condemned the unfair embargo imposed on Iraq, because it is a violation of all international conventions and norms, particularly since Iraq has honored all its commitments as enshrined in the relevant international resolutions. He noted the unethical conduct practiced against Iraq during the war of aggression.
