930929 FT 29 SEP 93 / Observer: Coffee break Visitors to the ludicrously cheap canteen enjoyed by officials at the International Monetary Fund's Washington headquarters cannot fail to appreciate the joke of Fund economists who are wont to lecture member countries across the globe on the infinite virtues of eliminating market-distorting subsidies. While the meals remain inexpensive, delegates to this year's annual meeting detect a welcome move in the direction of pricing transparency. The coin-operated espresso machine at last reveals the price of a cup - 55 cents; visitors no longer need to contribute to the subsidies by guessing the wrong amount.
