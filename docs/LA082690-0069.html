August 26, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

USING THE U.N. TO SETTLE MIDEAST CRISIS

Few men boasted as impressive a resume upon running for the presidency as President Bush. Nevertheless, this consummate politician may have misjudged how his playing golf and fishing will be perceived by the American people while thousands of our military prepare for conflict in the Middle East.

President Carter was seen as a virtual prisoner during the hostage taking late in his Administration. President Bush, apprehensive of a similar fate as that of Presient Carter, overreacted by appearing nonchalantly in front of the media on the first tee, determined not to be imprisoned in the Oval Office.

However, by this misplaced conduct the President opened himself to criticism from two camps -- either as a dedicated sportsman, to the detriment of his constitutional duties, or a politician too concerned with his image and the upcoming 1992 election season.

JEFFERY LA GOY

La Crescenta
