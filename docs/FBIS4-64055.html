BFN [Text] Kiev, 26 May -- "There is no disruption in the staffing of troop units today," said Major-General Valeriy Venher, head of the main organization and mobilization directorate of the general Headquarters of Ukraine's Armed Forces, at an armed forces briefing on the problems of staffing and conscription. With regard to the "Crimean problem", Valeriy Venher said that conscription in Crimea is going according to plan, and that the military commissar in the autonomy is obeying only the orders of Ukraine's commander in chief and minister of defense. Valeriy Venher refused to quote any figures concerning the draft or discharge, saying "they are not to be made public." He did say that 800 people did alternative service last year. On the whole, the head of the organization and mobilization main directorate believes, this type of service found little support among conscripts.
