930313 FT 13 MAR 93 / Finance and the Family: Gilt-edged Wednesday for investors - At a Glance Black Wednesday looks as though it should be re-christened White Wednesday as far as the long-dated gilts market is concerned. Initial reaction to the UK's departure from the European exchange rate mechanism stoked fears of higher inflation in future, and long-dated gilt prices fell. But once base interest rates began to fall, the market took off. As the graph shows, anyone with the courage to buy gilts at their lowest point should be sitting on a rise, once income is included, of 17 per cent in less than six months.
