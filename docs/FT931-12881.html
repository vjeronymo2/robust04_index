930127 FT 27 JAN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Smoking bans may be toughened THE Government will consider legislation to outlaw smoking in public places and at work if voluntary means fail, Dr Brian Mawhinney, the health minister, warned last night. The Health of the Nation white paper set targets of no-smoking policies in 80 per cent of public places by 1994 and for most employees by 1995. In a Commons written reply, Dr Mawhinney said: 'Good progress is being made by voluntary means, but if this does not continue we will consider taking statutory powers.'
