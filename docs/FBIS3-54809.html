Language: English Article Type:BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow January 4 TASS -- President Boris Yeltsin enjoyed 12 per cent popularity and was the first in the "man of 1993" opinion poll conducted before new year by the Institute of Parliamentary Sociology among 1,000 Muscovites. Yegor Gaydar, the architect of Russian reform, received only four per cent, while nationalist leader Vladimir Zhirinovskiy came third with three per cent popularity, according to the poll published by the "MOSKOVSKIY KOMSOMOLETS" daily on Tuesday. U.S. President Bill Clinton and noted Russian economist Grigory Yavlinskiy enjoyed two per cent of popularity. Former British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, famous cellist Mstislav Rostropovich, Deputy Prime Minister Sergey Shakhray and former Vice-President Aleksandr Rutskoy received close to one per cent. Two women listed their husbands as the man of the year. The poll showed there are fewer people dissatisfied with their life in Moscow than across Russia. In the Russian capital the figure was 59 per cent, while in the whole of Russia it is 75 per cent. 55 per cent of Muscovites said 1993 was unsuccessful for them, while 42 said it was successful.
