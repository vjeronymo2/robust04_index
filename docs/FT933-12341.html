930724 FT 24 JUL 93 / London Stock Exchange: Equity futures and options trading EARLY morning jitters in stock index futures gave way to more positive trade as optimism grew that the government would survive the Commons vote of confidence, Christine Buckley writes. The nervousness which had held back the September contract on the FT-SE 100 began to subside as hopes firmed that the spectre of Maastricht would soon be exorcised. Investors returned and significant buying was seen. The contract traded in one of its widest ranges of recent sessions and rose well above low levels prevalent over the past few days. It opened at 2,812 and dipped to 2,809 before embarking on an upward course. It hit its high of the day of 2,846 at about 3pm when profit-taking set in to take off the shine. The closing rate was 2,834 and volume 9,108 contracts. September finished the week 2 points ahead of its fair value premium to cash. In traded options the most activity in stocks was seen in Prudential as sellers acted on fresh regulatory plans. It traded 2,985 lots.
