BFN [Text] The Foreign Ministry's press service informed Estonian Radio that Prime Minister Mart Laar, who is currently in France on a working visit, met with President Francois Mitterrand today. Their meeting, which proceeded in a friendly atmosphere, dealt mostly with Estonian-Russian relations and issues to do with the Russian troop withdrawal. The following recommendation by Francois Mitterrand made its mark: Be patient at the negotiations while sticking firmly to your positions. The French Foreign Ministry gave a dinner in honor of Mart Laar at which Minister of State Pierre Mehaignerie made a speech. He highlighted the speed and fruitfulness of Estonian economic reforms and stated that France will in every way support Estonia's advances toward the European Union. The minister emphasized that the European Union must be open by the end of the century to all those who form a part of European history and culture.
