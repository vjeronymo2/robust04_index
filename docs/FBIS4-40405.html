BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Dmitriy Gorokhov] [Text] Moscow, 16 Jun (TASS) -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev today confirmed that he was prepared to meet U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher in the near future to agree on a common approach to a settlement of the situation on the Korean peninsula. An accord on such a meeting of the heads of the foreign policy departments of the two countries was reached in the course of the telephone conversation the other day between Boris Yeltsin and Bill Clinton. Speaking in a talk with journalists about the date for this dialogue, Andrey Kozyrev said, among other things: "As for the meeting of ministers of which the presidents spoke, I proposed to my friend and colleague Warren Christopher specific dates for this meeting, and I am awaiting a reply from him on specific dates. It goes without saying that I am prepared to meet in the nearest future and at a time convenient for both sides, in order to continue the work as the presidents agreed, that is, to work out a joint approach to this extraordinarily complicated question."
