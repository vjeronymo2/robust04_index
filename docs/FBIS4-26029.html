BFN [Text] New Delhi, January 1 (XINHUA) -- Indian Prime Minister Narasimha Rao said today that he would like to see a high-level India-China economic and trade cooperation. During his half-hour meeting with visiting Chinese Minister of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation Wu Yi here, Rao said bilateral trade between India and China is encouraging and that there exists the great potential for further development of India-China trade. He said that there is a long way to go in fully tapping the trade potential, hoping that both sides will find the ways and means to tap it as the time goes. India and China, the two developing countries sharing many commonalities, could explore some specific areas for setting up joint ventures, Rao suggested. He expressed the hope that there will be more and more India- China joint ventures in both countries and more and more areas will be covered in bilateral cooperation between the two nations. The Chinese minister said that the Governments of China and India should encourage direct trade between the two countries to push the trade volumes up. She expressed her gratitude to India for India's support to China in join the GATT and reiterate that China will continue to support India joining the Asia and Pacific Economic Cooperation forum.
