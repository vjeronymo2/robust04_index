Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Jan 13 (CTK) -- Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar said today he considered the Czech Republic's individual efforts to gain membership in the North Atlantic Alliance as soon as possible "a natural element of the interests of each country." "If the Czech Republic succeeds in gaining it (the membership) earlier, it would in no respect threaten us, on the contrary," Meciar told journalists. At the same time he stressed that Bratislava would seek coordination of actions and support mutual assistance among the remaining Visegrad Group states (Slovakia, the Czech Republic, Hungary, Poland) so that all the countries are admitted to NATO. The Czech Republic's interest in integration (with the West) -- if integration in the Central European region fails -- should not be considered straying away from the Visegrad Four.
