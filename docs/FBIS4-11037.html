BFN [Excerpt] [Presenter] Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus will today end his visit to Argentina and leave for Chile, the next stopover on his 10-day tour round Latin American countries. Our special correspondent in the prime minister's entourage, Miroslav Konvalina, reports from Buenos Aires. [Konvalina] An agreement on cooperation between the ministries of foreign affairs of the Czech Republic and Argentina was signed in Buenos Aires on 19 April. Czech Prime Minister Vaclav Klaus and Domingo Cavallo, Argentinian minister of economy and public works and services, also signed a memorandum on the imminent conclusion of an agreement on a free-trade zone between our countries or even between the whole regions, including CEFTA [Central European Free Trade Association] in Central Europe and MERCOSUR [Common Market of the South] in Latin America. The memorandum obliges the two countries to finalize as soon as possible the agreements on the protection of investments and on the prevention of double taxation. [passage omitted]
