BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Mehmet Gulcegun, deputy leader of the Social Democratic Populist Party [SDPP], has said that the Habur border gate with Iraq will regain its old vitality in the coming period. At a news conference in Ankara, Gulcegun said this step will partly alleviate the load placed on the Turkish economy by the embargo imposed on Iraq. Furthermore, he said, reopening the Habur border gate will end the crisis in the transportation sector, which is an important source of income in southeast Anatolia. Gulcegun pointed out that Murat Karayalcin, SDPP leader and deputy prime minister, will go to Baghdad next month. During the visit, the SDPP deputy leader said, work will be conducted to develop cooperation possibilities. [Begin Gulcegun recording] We are trying to solve the Habur problem, because it is the source of other negative developments in the region. Loyal to the unity of the country and to the state, the industrious regional people will make use of this opportunity in the best possible way. They will ensure their income while contributing to the economy of our country. [end recording]
