Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The organizing committee of State Duma sessions discussed the agenda for the first session of the new Russian parliament. Officials in the president's press service told Interfax that it will start with a speech by the oldest deputy, Georgiy Lukava, of the Liberal Democratic Party. After that, deputies will hear a report of the Central Election Commission on the elections to the State Duma, elect a commission to supervise the operation of the electronic voting system, as well as elect a counting commission and a provisional secretariat of the State Duma, and discuss the procedural rules on parliamentary groups. Following that, the organizing committee wants to have a break for the formation of a parliamentary group. The Duma must approve the minimal size of the group to be registered. Figures such as 14, 20, and 50 deputies have been suggested.
