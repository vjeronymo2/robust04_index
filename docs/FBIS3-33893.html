Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The former chief minister of Jammu and Kashmir, Dr. Farooq Abdullah, has dismissed Pakistan's accusations of widespread human rights violations in India. Exercising India's right to reply to the Pakistan prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto's, statement on Kashmir, Dr. Abdullah reminded Islamabad of the plight of the Ahmediyas, the Shi'ites and other minority communities in Pakistan. Addressing the opening session of the Human Rights Commission in Geneva, he observed that India always stood by its pledge and commitment of giving equal rights to all citizens. Dr. Abdullah asserted that Kashmir was and will remain a part of India. In a BBC interview later, he accused Pakistan of fomenting insurgency in Kashmir. He said India wants peace, but Pakistan is not responding to the offer.
