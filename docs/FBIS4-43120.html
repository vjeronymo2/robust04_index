BFN [Text] REUTER reported tonight that President Glavkos Kliridhis has said he will resign and proclaim presidential elections at end of October if the UN Security Council decides to continue talks on the confidence-building measures [CBM's]. REUTER added that President Kliridhis outlines this position in letters to U.S. President Bill Clinton and British Prime Minister John Major. According to the letters, copies of which REUTER claims to have obtained, President Kliridhis notes that the new president, who would be free from the committments Mr. Kliridhis has undertaken, would have to decide on the CBM's issue. Government Spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis announced today that President Kliridhis sent letters to President Clinton and Prime Minister John Major. Mr. Kasoulidhis said that the letters aim at promoting our side's positions and pointed out that the United States and Britain are the two powers who recently moved in the direction of continuing the CBM's process. In a statement to CyBC [Cyprus Broadcasting Corporation] tonight, the government spokesman reiterated his statement from yesterday saying he does not know of President Kliridhis' intention to resign and added that he is not aware of the content of the letters.
