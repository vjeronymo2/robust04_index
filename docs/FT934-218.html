931230 FT 30 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television One for sorrow, two for joy, three for a girl and four for a boy . . . why do magpies command such superstition? It is not limited to England: in Sweden magpies are connected with witchcraft and in Scotland if they fly near your window it heralds a death. Magpie In The Dock is presented as a courtroom drama with Geoffrey Palmer defending and Nicholas Rowe prosecuting (BBC1, 8.00). To male viewers over 45 the title Health And Efficiency means only one thing: weird photographs of naked women which used to appear in the naturist magazine of the 1950s and 60s. Arthur Marshall, author of If You See God Tell Him, who has chosen the title for his new comedy series (8.30, BBC1) must presumably know this, yet his series is set in a hospital. Julie Christie and Donald Sutherland, who enacted one of the greatest love scenes in Don't Look Now, star again in BBC2's 'Screen Two' drama The Railway Station Man. The script is by Shelagh Delaney from a novel by Ulster writer Jennifer Johnston, about a woman who flees to Donnegal with her young son after the killing of her husband (9.00).
