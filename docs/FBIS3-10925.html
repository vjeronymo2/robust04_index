Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on insurgent activities in Peru compiled from Peruvian papers monitored through 3 March. On 24 February the National Police presented to the press 23 Shining Path "terrorists" who operated in several provinces of Ancash Department. They had perpetrated nearly 50 attacks and selective crimes against civilian authorities over the past few months. They were arrested after a thorough investigation conducted over six months by authorities in the many shantytowns of Callejon de Huaylas and Conchucos, and in the Lima provinces of Huaura and Barranca. The forces seized a large amount of explosives, weapons, subversive propaganda, and maps of various public buildings used in attacking civilian authorities in Chavin Region. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 25 Feb 94 p A14) Lambayeque Superior Court ``faceless'' judges cleared physician Victor Echeandia Arellano, lawyer Victor Ciguena Campos, and six teachers of the charge of terrorism after they had served 14 months in prison. They had been "erroneously accused of involvement in terrorist activities." (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 25 Feb 94 p A14) Army personnel have arrested four Tupac Amaru Revolutionary Movement members operating in Paucartambo valley and Chanchamayo Province, Junin Department. They were identified as Juan Quispe Rojas, 28; Celestino Llantoy Duran, 27; Cesar Boza Ccoya, 24; and Victor Pardo Vivanco, 24. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 26 Feb 94 p A14) On 2 March 30 Shining Path "terrorists" surrendered to the military authorities at the Tingo Maria countersubversive base. Commander Juan Rivas said that 60 "terrorists" had surrendered under the repentance law over the past 72 hours, and that 1,100 "terrorists," most of them members of the Shining Path organization, had surrendered in the first quarter of 1994. Among the repentants had been "top leaders" in the subversive organization in Upper Huallaga, according to Rivas. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 3 Mar 94 pp 16-17)
