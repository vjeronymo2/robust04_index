930630 FT 30 JUN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Ministers to discuss fears over court cases PUBLIC CONCERN at the number of cases considered by the Crown prosecution service which do not lead to court proceedings was acknowledged by Mr David Maclean, home office minister, last night, Ivor Owen writes. Before the Criminal Justice Bill received its third Commons reading, he said he would discuss the matter with ministerial colleagues, adding: 'This is something we will want to discuss with the Crown prosecution service.' A Labour clause seeking to introduce a code of practice on liaison between the courts, the police and local authorities was defeated by a government majority of 47 (278-231).
