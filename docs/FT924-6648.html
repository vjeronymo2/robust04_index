921120 FT 20 NOV 92 / Investment by manufacturing industry in capital goods falls by 2 per cent INVESTMENT by manufacturing industry in capital goods fell by 2 per cent in the third quarter compared with a year ago, but was virtually unchanged on the previous quarter, Emma Tucker writes. The third-quarter seasonally adjusted figure at 1985 prices was Pounds 2.57bn, compared with Pounds 2.576bn in the second quarter, according to the Central Statistical Office. Spending by mineral extraction and chemicals industries dropped from Pounds 642m to Pounds 634m between the two quarters and spending by the engineering and car industries fell to Pounds 908m from Pounds 935m. Capital spending by the 'other manufacturing' sector, which includes printing and publishing, rose from Pounds 999m to Pounds 1.028bn in the third quarter. That was the highest level of spending in the category since the second quarter of 1990. Manufacturing industry spent less on new building in the third quarter but more on vehicles and plant and machinery.
