Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By unidentified ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 6 Mar -- Documents covering an investigation by the Riga garrison military procurator's office, which have been sent to the Russian chief military procurator's office, confirm the information provided on 25 February by ITAR-TASS correspondent Roman Zadunayskiy that soldiers belonging to the North Western Group of Forces [NWGF] had that day been admitted with wounds to Riga military hospital. According to E. Kuzmin, the military procurator's office junior sergeant and Privates S. Fedorov, A. Dumilin and A. Ivanov, soldiers belonging to the 651st Independent Company for escorting NWGF freight and stationed at military unit No. 52241 in Riga, were on the night of 25 February admitted to the 289th district military hospital in Riga with various wounds. (?Contact Service) Private Sergey Glinka of Military Unit No. 52241 diagnosed as suffering from a knife wound had already been admitted to the same hospital on the previous evening. All five stated on their admission to hospital that they had received their injuries and wounds when they were attacked by unknown civilians. All this investigation information was reported on the night of 25 February to the Russian chief military procurator's office by the Riga garrison military procurator's office duty officer. These facts need to be set out in such detail, since on 2 March the NWGF press center issued information that no servicemen suffering from injuries and wounds inflicted by unknown assailants had recently been admitted to Riga military hospital. The military procurator's office is continuing to investigate the facts.
