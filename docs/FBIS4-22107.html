BFN [Statement issued by Appeal Attorney General Judge Munif 'Uwaydat on 20 April -- read by announcer] [Text] Appeal Attorney General Judge Munif 'Uwaydat went to the Defense Ministry this afternoon where the investigating judge, Joseph Furayhah, is investigating the crime of the blowing up of the Sayyidat al-Najat Church in Zuq Mikha'il. After studying the file, it became evident that the investigation into the church bombing has solved the following crimes: 1. The crime of the assassination of Engineer Dany Sham'un [leader of the National Liberals Party] and his family in B'abda on 21 October 1990. 2. The crime of blowing up the car of Minister Elie Hubayqah and the killing of his companion in al-Ashrafiyah in 1991. 3. The crime of the assassination of Dr. Ilyas al-Zayik in al-Ashrafiyah in 1989. This is in addition to other crimes, which, together with the church bombing, remain under investigation. The case of the assassination of Dany Sham'un and his family was immediately referred to the judicial investigator, Judge Munir Hunayn, together with the Appeal Prosecution Office's charge of attacks on the state's internal security, which resulted in the death of Engineer Sham'un, his wife, and two children. Orders for the arrest of the following were issued: Hanna al-'Atiq, Robert Abu-Sa'b, Kamil Karam, Milhim Haddad, Marun Ghanim, Tony Musa, Walid Ja'ja', Rafiq Sa'adah, Fadi Sa'b, Simon Khiryati, Khalil Wakim, and Ilya Irz, who have been referred by Judge Furayhah. All of them belong to the Lebanese Forces' intelligence service. The judge also ruled that orders for the arrest in absentia of those who were proven to be involved by the investigation carried out by Judge Furayhah be issued. They are: Ghassan Tuma, Tony 'Ubayd, 'Atif al-Habr, Jean Shahin, Jean Samiyah, Farid Sa'adah, Elie 'Awwad alias Juliano, Tony Sim'an, Naja al-Qaddum, George al- Fighali, Ilya 'Aqiqi, and Francis 'Aqiqi. All of the above belong to the Lebanese Forces' intelligence service. Judge 'Uwaydat also issued charges against anyone shown by the investigations to be an active player, instigator, or participant in these crimes. He also asked that arrest warrants be issued for each one of the above wanted by the investigation as well as written subpoenas.
