921224 FT 24 DEC 92 / UK Company News: Kelsey falls Pounds 307,000 into red KELSEY INDUSTRIES finished the year ended September 30 1992 with a loss - the first one in its history - and is halving its dividend to 13p. Mr Brian Arbib, chairman, said in the worst trading conditions - with the exception of the far East - he could remember in 38 years, the group sustained a pre-tax loss of Pounds 307,000, compared with a profit of Pounds 1.44m which took in exceptional gains of Pounds 350,000. Turnover fell from Pounds 43.4m to Pounds 41.4m. Losses per share came to 16.2p (earnings 20p) and the final dividend is cut from 18p to 5p. The group's activities cover roofing, soldering products, audio/video products and engineering.
