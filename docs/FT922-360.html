920629 FT 29 JUN 92 / World News In Brief: Americans held in Soviet clinics General Dmitri Volkogonov, head of a joint Russian-US commission searching for missing US prisoners of war in the former Soviet Union, said that four Americans were held at a Soviet psychiatric hospital in 1953. At least one American prisoner of war was buried near the central Russian city of Tambov, south-east of Moscow. The report appeared to be the first tangible result of recent intensified efforts to trace 2,500 missing POWs.
