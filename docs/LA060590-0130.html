June 5, 1990, Tuesday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY NEWSWATCH

TURNOUT: The county is "hoping" for a 50% turnout of registered voters today in a non-presidential primary. . . . Not bad? Actually, that would amount to only 31% of county residents old enough to vote. . . . By contrast, turnout in Nicaragua's election last February was about 90%.

O.C. TURNOUT

1988 General Election

Percentage of total population who are adults*: 74.8%

Percentage of adult population who are registered voters: 70.1%

Percentage of registered voters who actually cast their vote: 76.6%

Total O.C. Population: 2,257,200

* at least 18 years old.

Sources: California Department of Finance and O.C. registrar of voters. Mark I. Pinsky and Steve Emmons
