921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / World News In Brief: Convicted doctor allowed to practise Consultant rheumatologist Dr Nigel Cox, 47, convicted of attempting to murder a terminally ill patient at the Royal Hampshire County Hospital in Winchester and sentenced to 12 months' imprisonment suspended for a year, is to be allowed to continue to practise. The General Medical Council's professional conduct committee ruled that his case should be concluded with no further action.
