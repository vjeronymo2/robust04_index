BFN [Article by Juraj Alner: "Colorful Mixture for Preelection Stew; There Are 38 Parties and 19 Movements in Slovakia"] [Text] The preelection period starts with the question of which parties (movements) are registered in Slovakia, which of them will participate in the elections, and on what basis some of them will be allowed to participate and others not. We therefore publish an updated list of parties and movements that are registered as of today with the Ministry of Interior of the Slovak Republic. The parties and movements are listed in chronological order, according to the registered date of their founding. The Democratic Party is the oldest party. Although the Freedom Part is also old, it had suspended its activity and resumed it only recently. A party (movement) can be founded under conditions set out in National Council of the Slovak Republic [NRSR] Law No. 47/1993, which changes and amends Law No. 424/1991 on association in political parties and political movements. Hence, it still holds true that, to set up a party (movement), it is necessary to submit a petition signed by at least 1,000 citizens who, apart from their signatures, list their first names, surnames, birth registration numbers, and complete addresses. Of the 38 parties and 19 movements (or any other parties and movements that will be registered in time), only those will be able to participate in the elections that will meet the special regulations set out in Slovak National Council Law No. 80/1990 on elections to the Slovak National Council, as amended by the law of 26 February 1992. Essentially, the same criteria will apply as during preceding elections. The slates of candidates must be submitted 60 days before the election date--that is, 60 days before 30 September. The condition for a party (movement) to run is to have at least 10,000 individual members. If it has fewer members, it must submit a petition signed by as many citizens as will supplement the missing number so as to reach 10,000. Again, the petition must contain all of the data necessary to identify the person in question. This condition need not be met by parties that were represented in the NRSR 60 days before the elections were called--that is, 60 days before 25 April, when the NRSR chairman promulgated the election date. By the same token, this condition need not be met by parties that gained at least 10,000 votes in the preceding elections, even if they did not make it into parliament. You will find the list of parties from which we will be able to choose, as well as their programs and intentions, in our newspaper in due course.
