BFN [ADS report under the "Events of the Week" rubric: "`Sakhalin-Japan' Entrepreneurs' Association Established" -- from the FINANSOVYYE IZVESTIYA section] [Text] On the Russian side, the association's founders include the International Center for Business Cooperation, "Sakhalinmoreneftegaz," the Sakhalin Shipping Line, "Sakhalinugol," and more than 30 other enterprises from Sakhalin; on the Japanese side the founders include a number of major companies such as Mitsui and the Bank of Tokyo. In accordance with its charter, the association intends to "formulate a set of investment projects and seeks to secure their implementation not for the sake of profit, but with a view to achieving all-around, mutually advantageous economic cooperation." The projects will be subsidized from the association's assets, which have been formed by contributions from its members. Mikhail Ryabtsev, director of the International Center for Business Cooperation (Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk), has been elected president.
