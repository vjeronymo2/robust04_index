BFN [Text] Baghdad, 26 May (INA) -- National Assembly Speaker Sa'di Mahdi Salih today received Masatu Okita, the higher adviser of the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ], and the accompanying delegation [name and title as received]. During the meeting, Salih stressed Iraq's desire to establish strong friendship and cooperation relations with Japan. He said the return of Iraqi-Japanese relations to normal is necessary in order to achieve the interests of both friendly peoples in a way that benefits both countries. Salih indicated the importance of promoting Iraqi-Japanese relations in the current and coming phases. He said Japan can play a greater role in this regard by making efforts to lift the blockade imposed on the Iraqi people, especially since Japan enjoys the international status entitling it to play such a role. Okita emphasized the SDPJ's desire to promote Japanese-Iraqi relations on the official and party levels in a way that would achieve the interests of both friendly peoples. He said: We are working to establish a just, comprehensive, and lasting peace in the Arab region, avoiding the use of force. Okita appreciated Iraq's peaceful positions and its call for the establishment of such a peace, so general welfare can be achieved for all regional states and also for friendly states. The Japanese delegation arrived in Iraq yesterday on a visit lasting several days.
