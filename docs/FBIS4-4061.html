BFN [Text] The seven-day Second Session of the Seventh Fujian People's Congress ended in Fuzhou this morning after fulfilling various items listed on the agenda. The session called on people throughout the province to unite more closely around the CPC Central Committee with Comrade Jiang Zemin as the core. Under the guidance of Comrade Deng Xiaoping's theory of building socialism with Chinese characteristics and of the party's basic line, and under the leadership of the provincial party committee, they must be of one heart and one mind, and must make persistent efforts to carry out reform, rely on their own efforts, and work hard to promote their undertakings. They must strive for a new victory in our province's reform, opening up, and construction. The executive chairmen of the session--Jia Qinglin, Yuan Qitong, Guo Ruiren, Huang Changxi, Su Changpei, Liu Yongye, Zhang Mingjun, Hong Huasheng, and Song Jun--were seated in the front rows of the rostrum. Presided over by Su Changpei, the session adopted a resolution approving the work report of the provincial people's government. The resolution held that the review of the report on last year's work conformed with reality, and that the tasks and arrangements proposed by the report for the coming year were practical and feasible. The session also adopted the following resolutions: The resolution on the implementation of the plan for the national economic and social development in our province last year, and plan for this year; the resolution on our province's final accounts last year, and this year's budget; the resolution on the work report of the provincial people's congress standing committee; the resolution on the work report of the provincial higher people's court; and the resolution on the work report of the provincial people's procuratorate. After approving these resolutions, Jia Qinglin, newly elected chairman of the provincial people's congress standing committee, and Chen Mingyi, newly elected governor of the provincial people's government, made their respective speeches.
