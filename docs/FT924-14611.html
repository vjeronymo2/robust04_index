921009 FT 09 OCT 92 / World News in Brief: Two car bombs explode in central London Two car bombs exploded in central London last night, causing transport chaos. A police officer was slightly injured. The blasts came only hours after the IRA admitted responsibility for two explosions in the capital on Wednesday. The first blast last night, outside the London Dungeon tourist attraction near London Bridge station, came at 9.30, 20 minutes after a warning. An hour later a second blast hit Melcombe Street in the West End near Marylebone station. Police later carried out a controlled explosion on a suspect car in St Pancras Way near St Pancras and King's Cross stations. Public transport services were severely disrupted but British Rail and London Transport hoped to run normal services this morning. The IRA statement, timed to coincide with the Conservative party conference in Brighton, had said the bombs demonstrated 'that there will be no respite' in their campaign. Last night, Mr George Churchill-Coleman, head of the Anti-Terrorist Squad, said at the scene of the Melcombe Street blast: 'These things are found from time to time. It does not mean that the terrorists control London at all. We have control.'
