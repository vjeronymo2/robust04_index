January 20, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

'DRUG WARS': MEXICO, U.S. GOVERNMENT AND THE NEWS MEDIA

What could have been an affecting story based on the life of Camarena turned out to be a caricature of what is supposed to be life in Mexico under the influence of drug lords and corrupted officials.

The series was full of the usual stereotypical and one-dimensional characters we've come to know in American television and movies.

The Latin characters are all buffoons, crooks or both. Throughout the movie I kept waiting for someone to blurt the line: "Badges, we don't need any steenken badges!"

Even Brokaw twice asked the question: "Do you think the President should have sent the Marines into Mexico?" Is he aware of the repercussions of such an action? Relations between our countries would be strained for generations!

ROBERT LOLA, Los Angeles
