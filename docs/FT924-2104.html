921215 FT 15 DEC 92 / Parliament and Politics: Former ministers raise rail concerns THE government's likely difficulties in parliament over the legislation to privatise British Rail were underlined yesterday by two former Tory cabinet ministers. On BBC television's Panorama, Lord Ridley, a former transport secretary, said he never believed it really possible to do very much, if anything, with the railways because they were basically loss-making. Lord Whitelaw, a former deputy prime minister, said that there were considerable risks involved, and he was not sure it would be a success.
