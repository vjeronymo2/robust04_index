November 27, 1989, Monday, Valley Edition

PIERCE FARM'S FUTURE

Ruth Loring's thoughtful, imaginative letter on the Pierce College Farm (Nov. 12) echoes my sentiments perfectly. I will go a giant step further than her suggestions and ask why not use the land for a Valley arboretum?

Los Angeles County has arboretums in the San Gabriel Valley and South Coast. Doesn't the Valley need an arboretum more than additional religious sites or high-rises?

Having lived 61 of my 62 years in Westlake Village, this summer I discovered Pierce College Farm.

With hardly any student traffic afoot or in cars, it was easy to explore the back country of the campus: the country store, the acres of orchards, the cornfields, the rangeland, the nature trail and horticulture buildings.

What I found appalled me. In this natural setting of potential great beauty and tranquillity, I found gross neglect and shabbiness.

Hopefully this open space will be preserved for the community college to develop (or other governmental agencies if necessary) to its highest potential as a place for all the people to use.

JENNY ERICKSON

Westlake Village
