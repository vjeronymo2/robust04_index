Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Stockholm, Feb 22 (AFP) -- Swedish Foreign Minister Margaretha af Ugglas will make official visits to Bosnia-Herzegovina, Croatia and Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] from March 7 to 11, a statement issued by the Foreign Ministry here said Tuesday [22 February]. Af Ugglas is to meet with United Nations Protection Force (UNPROFOR) officials and Swedish troops stationed in the three countries after security improved over the past days, the ministry said, cautioning however that the visits may be put off if the situation got worse. She is to visit Zagreb, Sarajevo and the besieged city of Tuzla in Bosnia as well as the Macedonian capital Skopje. Sweden has 15 military observers, 100 troops, six military police and 25 civilian police in Croatia. In Bosnia, 880 Swedes are part of a battalion from Scandinavian countries, with 170 stationed in Sarajevo, and in Macedonia 250 Swedish troops back up a 700-men battalion from northern Europe, according to military officials here.
