If the confirmation is used less frequently as an invoice, should some of the confirmation's content be shifted to periodic account statements? 7 6 Some commentators suggested that substantive changes to Rule 10b-10 will be necessary as a result of the adoption of Rule 15c6&hyph;1 of the Exchange Act, 17 CFR 240.15c6&hyph;1, which implemented T+3. Because Rule 10b-10 requires a broker-dealer to send a customer written confirmation ``at or before completion of the transaction,'' some have argued that a T+3 settlement period will make compliance with Rule 10b-10 impossible. The Commission notes, however, that broker-dealers can comply with Rule 10b-10 even under a T+3 settlement period. Rule 10b-10 requires only that the broker-dealer send written confirmation by settlement date, not that the customer actually receive it by settlement. In the current five days settlement period, broker-dealers typically send customer confirmations the day after trade date, which will satisfy Rule 10b-10 even under a T+3 settlement period. 7 Six of the exchanges and the National Association of Securities Dealers, Inc.
