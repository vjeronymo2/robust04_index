December 10, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

GERARD SCHWARZ: CLASS BENEATH THE BATON

Re Martin Bernheimer's "Confessions of a Talented Workaholic," Nov. 26:

A few years ago, Gerard Schwarz led the Los Angeles Chamber Orchestra in a program of Strauss and Haydn at UCLA. Claudio Arrau was the soloist, performing works both immediately before and after the intermission.

After completing the Strauss "Burleske," Schwarz followed Arrau onto the stage for their bows. Schwarz immediately noticed that the volume of applause rose perceptibly as he appeared. He stopped in his tracks and started to applaud Arrau -- focusing the attention of the audience upon the soloist.

After the intermission, Arrau returned to the piano, to the applause of the audience. Schwarz? He deftly weaved his way from the back of the orchestra and took his position on the podium.

Class. Real class.

MICHAEL T. GEORGE, Santa Barbara
