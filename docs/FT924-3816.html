921205 FT 05 DEC 92 / Brazil sticks to date for steel sale BRAZIL'S President Itamar Franco said yesterday he would go ahead as planned with the privatisation of the National Steel Company (CSN) on December 22 - to the relief of investors. With a minimum price of Dollars 1.6bn (Pounds 1.05bn), the CSN sale will be Brazil's largest privatisation so far. Mr Franco, who has frequently criticised the privatisation process, had been expected to delay the sale, but relented after meetings with ministers and the president of CSN. Cancellation of the sale would have increased fears about the new president's commitment to modernisation. He has, however, ordered a new evaluation of the price and warned yesterday that if he is not satisfied by its findings he will halt the sale. Mr Franco denied that he was planning to sack his economy and planning ministers. Rumours of the imminent dismissal of the two men have rocked financial markets all week after a comment by the president that 'ministers can leave the government at any time' and his refusal to approve their plans on future economic policy. In a change of strategy, Mr Franco said the ministers would now be free to adjust public sector tariffs and government controlled prices, but added: 'I would just like to know first.'
