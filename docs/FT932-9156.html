930514 FT 14 MAY 93 / Helsinki defiant on pay bill FINLAND'S government is to present a bill allowing employers to pay 30 per cent below minimum wage if they hire jobless youths, AP reports from Helsinki. 'We are not engaging in slave labour; we are simply giving work to the young,' said Mr Esko Aho, prime minister. Unions have threatened strikes if the bill goes ahead. Unemployment is at a post-war record rate of 17.8 per cent.
