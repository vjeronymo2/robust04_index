BFN [Text] Government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis said the Cypriot Government believes that the procedure followed so far by the United Nations to promote a Cyprus solution has failed because of the lack of political will by the Turkish side. It also believes that the time has come for the UN Security Council to consider alternative ways to promote the problem. The spokesman clarified that when he refers to the procedure he means the one on the confidence-building measures [CBM's] and all the others followed in the past to promote the sets of ideas from former UN Secretary General Javier Perez de Cuellar and UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali. Kasoulidhis added that the president has some thoughts on ways to promote the Cyprus issue and will submit them to the National Council after the UN secretary general submits his report. Answering a question, the spokesman said the government does not believe that a silent deadline has been given to the entire procedure but that it is merely a delay. He added that the Cypriot Government has not asked for new meetings with Joe Clark, the UN secretary general's special representative in Cyprus, or Gustave Feissel, the UN secretary general's alternate special representative in Cyprus, during this period. Kasoulidhis said: The government does understand what can be said in such meetings, unless Turkish Cypriot leader Rauf Denktas changes his position and that side accepts the UN document of 21 March. Our side has no margins for further negotiations on this document. Asked about information that Clark will resign, that a replacement will be appointed, or that observers will be appointed to the Cyprus issue the spokesman said the government has no information.
