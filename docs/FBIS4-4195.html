BFN [By reporters Bata Ercang (1572 1044 1422 0221) and Chang Wanlong (1603 8001 7127)] [Text] Ulaanbaatar, 29 Apr (XINHUA) -- Premier Li Peng met with chairman (Speaker) Natsagiyn Bagabandi of Mongolia's Great Hural and had a friendly conversation with him this morning. Li Peng first conveyed the regards of Qiao Shi, National People's Congress [NPC] Standing Committee chairman, to Bagabandi and then the NPC's hope for strengthened cooperation with Mongolia's parliament. Bagabandi expressed his thanks for this hope. He said: Relations between the two countries are developing smoothly thanks to the concerted efforts by both sides. We are satisfied with the progress. Premier Li Peng's official visit to Mongolia is a historic visit and is of great significance for pushing bilateral ties to a new stage. The Mongolian parliament approves of the two governments' policies of developing bilateral friendly cooperation. He spoke highly of the Mongolian-Chinese treaty of friendly relations and cooperation to be signed soon, and he said that the signing of this treaty will lay a good legal foundation for the further expansion of bilateral ties. Bagabandi said: The exchanges between the two parliaments play an important role in developing relations between the two countries. At present, cooperation between the two parliaments has been fully normalized, and exchanges have been increasing year after year. A delegation of the Mongolian Great Hural will pay an official visit to China in August in the hope of learning about China's reform and opening, as well as about the work of the NPC. Li Peng said he was very much satisfied with the fruitful results of his visit to Mongolia. The signing of the bilateral treaty of friendly relations and cooperation will turn a new page in bilateral ties and will promote the advancement of bilateral ties in all spheres. The Chinese Government will continue working toward that goal. Li Peng said: China's NPC and Mongolia's Great Hural shoulder an important responsibility for furthering friendship between the peoples of the two countries and for expanding bilateral friendly cooperation. We are happy to see that relations between the two parliaments have been developing well in recent years. Premier Li Peng also briefed the other side on China's domestic situation, reform, and opening to the outside world, as well as on its efforts to improve its legal system.
