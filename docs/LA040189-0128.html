April 1, 1989, Saturday, Orange County Edition

PREP ROUNDUP;

AKAU PITCHES ONE-HITTER AS BUENA PARK DEFEATS LOS AMIGOS IN SOFTBALL

Shannon Akau pitched a one-hitter as Buena Park High School defeated Los Amigos, 10-1, Friday in a 4-A division consolation pool game of the Canyon softball tournament at Peralta Park in Anaheim.

Akau (3-1) allowed an unearned run in the second inning. Kari Escobedo paced the Coyotes' with two triples and three RBIs.

In other 4-A division consolation pool games:

Millikan 4, El Modena 1 -- Michelle Shanks (1-0) pitched a three-hitter, striking out 14 for Millikan at Peralta Park.

Garden Grove 2, Woodbridge 1 -- Michelle Cash singled home Rachael Cooper in the top of the seventh for Garden Grove (9-5) at Peralta Park. Stephanie Smith pitched a five-hitter, striking out six, for the victory.

Buena Park 3, Villa Park 2 (9 innings) -- Villa Park's Robyn Kerfoot hit a solo home run in the bottom of the seventh to send the game to extra innings.

Buena Park (6-1) scored twice in the top of the ninth playing with an international tiebreaker rule, where each team starts with a runner on second base. The winning run was scored by pitcher Christina Urban on a single by Kari Escobedo. Villa Park is 6-10.

In 5-A division consolation pool games:

Mater Dei 1, St. Paul 0 -- Terri Kobata (4-1) pitched a four-hitter, striking out nine for Mater Dei (7-2-1) at Peralta Park.

Los Alamitos 3, Westminster 2 -- Shannon Skoglund (5-5) allowed eight hits and struck out four for Los Alamitos (5-5) at Peralta Park.

Los Alamitos 4, Loara 2 -- Skoglund pitched a four-hitter.

In Sea View league softball:

Newport Harbor 6, Estancia 3 -- Jenny Ryan had one hit, one RBI and scored three runs for Newport Harbor (3-3, 2-1) at Estancia. Ryan (1-0) also was the winning pitcher with two strikeouts in seven innings.

Tustin 3, University 1 -- Shannon Ellis (2-5) pitched her first no-hitter this season with 14 strikeouts for Tustin (3-0). University is 1-2.

In nonleague softball:

Mission Viejo 3, Huntington Beach 2 -- Center fielder Amy Bond threw out the tying run at the plate in the bottom of the seventh to preserve the victory for Mission Viejo (8-5).

In Sunset League badminton:

Marina 10, Ocean View 7 -- No. 1 boys singles Nappy Lam of Marina (1-1) got his second league victory in as many outings.

In Orange league golf:

Valencia 200, Brea-Olinda 208 -- The Tigers (5-0, 1-0) won their 42nd consecutive dual match with the victory at Alta Vista Country Club.

In nonleague golf:

Corona del Mar 194, Huntington Beach 202 -- John Walker shot par 36 for Corona del Mar (7-0) at SeaCliff Country Club.

In nonleague girls' swimming:
