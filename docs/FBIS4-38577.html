BFN [Video report from Tbilisi by correspondent D. Keliselidze] [Text] [Announcer] The introduction of CIS peacekeeping forces to the area of the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict may begin on 10 June. This has just been announced after a meeting between Eduard Shevardnadze and CIS Acting Secretary Korotchenya. [Keliselidze] Today Eduard Shevardnadze had a half-hour meeting at Tbilisi airport with Ivan Korotchenya, chairman of the CIS acting secretariat. The fact that the meeting took place at the airport and that there was no time for the traditional Georgian hospitality showed that this visit was urgent and concerned important questions. And that's how it was. Russian President Boris Yeltsin, head of the council of the CIS states, had appealed to the leaders of CIS countries to examine the question of introducing peacekeeping troops into the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict zone, as he was asked to by Georgian Head of State Eduard Shevardnadze and Abkhazian Supreme Soviet Chairman Vladislav Ardzinba. In order to resolve this question then according to procedure, it was necessary to get the agreement of all countries in the CIS. Ivan Korotchenya, before his arrival in Tbilisi, had already visited the central Asian states, and intends in the next few days to visit Baku and Yerevan, and then go to Moldova and Ukraine. Concerning the question of a possible date for the introduction of peacekeeping forces into the conflict zone, Ivan Korotchenya said: [Begin Korotchenya recording] By 10 June. Well, 10 to 15 June. On this there is probably a need for a final decision which will be taken by the Russian president. [end recording] [Keliselidze] Eduard Shevardnadze in his turn thanked the CIS leaders who responded positively to Yeltsin's appeal for the introduction of peacekeeping forces into the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict zone.
