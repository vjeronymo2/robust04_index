Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Jan. 25 -- President of the Slovak Republic Michal Kovac on Tuesday afternoon received Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak. "We have discussed economic issues and the results of these talks are very significant," Michal Kovac told reporters after the meeting. "We have agreed that Prime Minister Pawlak would come to Bratislava and that our governments will meet in their full composition," Kovac said. During the "Visegrad group summit" in Prague Waldemar Pawlak and Prime Minister Vaclav Meciar agreed that prior to a meeting of both cabinets, economic ministers of both countries will meet to assess the progress in preparation of agreements between both republics. Commenting upon the decision to postpone the signing of agreements between Poland and Slovakia, the prime minister said that "it is worth to devote more time to these agreements to prepare them better." Pawlak stressed that it would be good for the cooperation between Poland and Slovakia to be more concrete. "The problem boils down not to documents and agreements but above all to direct contacts of many people," the prime minister said.
