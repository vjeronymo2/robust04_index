Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Sitnikov] [Text] United Nations February 15 TASS -- A Russian-Uzbek statement adopted on February 8 by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and his Uzbek counterpart Saidmukhtar Saidkasymov was circulated here today as a document of the United Nations Security Council. "The foreign ministers of the Russian Federation and the Republic of Uzbekistan confirm the principled stand of their states on non- interference in internal affairs of Afghanistan and express profound concern with a new outburst of armed confrontation there... "They call for termination of fratricidal war in Afghanistan and insistently urge all Afghan military-political forces to abandon the stake on force methods and begin a joint search for political compromises in the name of national consolidation and reconciliation. "The sides believe that the conflict in Afghanistan is a consequence of domestic contradictions and it threatens to security and stability in the region. They are extremely alarmed with increased tension near southern borders of the Commonwealth of Independent States... The sides intend to keep to strict neutrality and the principle of non-interference in internal affairs of Afghanistan." Russia and Uzbekistan expressed their readiness for maximum assistance to the settlement of Afghan differences and the search for way of further Afghan development "within the framework of international efforts, on coordination with the United Nations Organization and all interested states".
