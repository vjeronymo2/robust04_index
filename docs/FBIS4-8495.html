BFN [Text] Belgrade, 13 Apr (TANJUG) -- Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic this evening again received Vitaliy Churkin, Russian President Boris Yeltsin's special envoy, who arrived in Belgrade following talks in Sarajevo and Pale. As the Serbian president's office has announced, the talks focused on the current situation, while particular attention was paid to the initiative for reaching a general agreement on the cessation of all hostilities on the territory of former Bosnia-Herzegovina. The Serbian president stressed in talks that abandoning the practice of discriminating between the warring sides in former Bosnia-Herzegovina represents an unavoidable precondition for reaching a final peace.
