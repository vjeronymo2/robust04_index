920629 FT 29 JUN 92 / Mongolians go to polls By camel, tractor and foot, Mongolia's voters went to the polls yesterday to decide if the former Communist party which ruled their country for more than seven decades will keep its grip on power, Reuter reports from Ulan Bator. Officials reported high early turnouts in grassland voting stations and in the capital as 293 candidates vied for 76 seats in Mongolia's new single-chamber parliament, the first elected under a new constitution which enshrines the concepts of human rights and private ownership of property.
