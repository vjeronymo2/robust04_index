BFN [Text] Seoul, April 19 (YONHAP) -- The Kimpo customs office on Tuesday sought arrest warrants against a Korean and an American man on charges of violating the customs law after they tried to smuggle in 400 million won worth of computer chips through the U.S. Army Post Office [APO]. The duo were identified as Yi Ho-song, 27, of Togok-tong, Kangnam-ku, Seoul: and David Miller, 31, an unemployed, of Chori-myon, Paju-kun, Kyonggi Province. The customs office apprehended an accomplice, U.S. military civilian John G. Booth, 42, and turned him over to U.S. military investigation authorities under the Status of Forces Agreement. A customs official said that Yi, in collaboration with Miller, brought in 1,250 486-type computer chips through the APO under the name of Mr. Booth on April 17 and 18. The computer chips were purchased from the Micro-Tech Co. located in California, U.S., the official said.
