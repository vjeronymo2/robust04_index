Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Croat and Muslim forces in Bosnia-Herzegovina have agreed to allow Unprofor forces to begin patrolling demarcation lines as of tomorrow, Commander Rob Anning, UNPROFOR [UN Protection Force] spokesman in Sarajevo said today. The agreement was reached in Gornji Vakuf today between representatives of the Bosnia-Herzegovina army led by General Muslimovic, and the Croat Defense Council led by General Vrbant, Anning said. Talks on the opening of six roads crossing the front lines are due to start at the same time. These measures should enable the implementation of the general cease-fire agreement and the collection of weapons, which should come into effect at 1200 on 7 March. Weapons are to be collected in five or six locations under UN supervision.
