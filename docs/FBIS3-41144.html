Language: English Article Type:CSO [Text] Police in Grenada last weekend made their biggest ever cocaine bust. Some 60 kilos of the illegal drug were seized on board the vessel, "Let Me Live" on Sunday inside Grenada's territorial waters. The illegal drug has a street value of around 33.3 million E.C. dollars. "Let Me Live" was on its way from neighbouring Trinidad and Tobago to Union Island which is one of the Grenadine Islands controlled by St. Vincent when it was intercepted by the Grenada Coast Guard. It was brought to the Coast Guard base at True Blue where the search was carried out by a joint Police and Customs party. After the drug discovery was made, the six occupants of the boat, five Vincentian nationals, and one Trinidadian were arrested and subsequently charged for drug possession. The Vincentians are Ishmel Hal (51), Constantine Alexander (25), Winston Nanton (25), Clement McIntosh (22), and Lawrence Alexander (48), while the Trinidadian is Sylvan Liverpool (50). With the exception of Liverpool, the other five are crew members of "Let Me Live." Liverpool has been quoted as saying that he was taking a ride on the boat in order to meet another vessel in St. Vincent on which he is employed as a seaman. When the six accused were brought before Magistrate Patricia Mark on Tuesday, she remanded them to Richmond Hill prisons until December 1. Barrister-of-law Neil Noel has been retained to represent four of the accused--Hall, Nanton, McIntosh and Liverpool. The two Alexander's who are close relatives will be represented by lawyer Anslem Clouden. Authoritative sources told GRENADA TODAY that the six suspects were arrested due to a tip-off received by Customs Officials. A source said that when the boat was intercepted by the Grenada Coast Guard the search party was directed straight to the section where the furniture was located and told to search there for the illegal drugs. The cocaine was discovered hidden among a number of parcels of black pepper among the furniture. Police suspect that the drugs were placed there in an effort to prevent snifferdogs from detecting them in case of a search. There are reports that the cocaine was intended for a national of Union Island. Another source told this newspaper that it appears that only two of the six suspects are really connected to the drugs. He also said that there is a "definite link" between the cocaine discovered and five Trinidadians freed in a Magistrate's court on the island recently. The source stressed that all of them belong to the same drug trafficking ring which operates out of Port-of-Spain. Prior to Sunday's haul, the biggest ever discovery of cocaine on the island was in June 1991, when 45 kilos carrying a street value of 25 million E.C. dollars were seized by police at a hotel in the south of the island. Nationals Diego Peters and Spencer Drakes of LaPotrie, St. Andrew's were arrested and charged for possession of the drugs.
