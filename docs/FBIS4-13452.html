BFN [Text] Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin today told the Knesset Foreign Affairs and Defense Committee that he is ready for a greater withdrawal on the Golan than that recommended by the IDF's [Israel Defense Forces] General Staff. The prime minister's statement was made in reply to a question from Knesset Member [MK] Beni Begin. Our correspondent Hayim Hecht reports that the prime minister stressed that the working paper he received from Major General Ilan Biran did not refer to a total IDF withdrawal from the Golan. Also at today's Knesset Foreign Affairs and Defense Committee session, Yitzhaq Rabin, prime minister and defense minister, said the regular troops are in a difficult situation, because all the men are tied up in operational activity in Judaea, Samaria, and the Gaza Strip. Rabin noted that instead of the planned six months of training, the regular troops were barely receiving three months, and if the situation continues, the level of preparedness will be affected. The prime minister added that there are many friction-prone sites in Hebron, which are liable to lead to a conflagration. For that reason, the best units are tied up in that location.
