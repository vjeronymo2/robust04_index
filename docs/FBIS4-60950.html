BFN [Text] Tiraspol BASAPRESS, 30/4/1994 -- The Dniester Republic Women's Union (TWU), which is considered an extreme branch of the separatist movement in eastern Moldova, sent a message to Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Defense Minister Pavel Grachev accusing General Aleksandr Lebed [commander of the 14th Russian Army stationed in Dniester] of attempting to liquidate the self-proclaimed Dniester Republic. The message says that if Lebed ignores the Dniester legislation on Tiraspol's right to possess the 14th Army's property, the women will enchain themselves arm in arm with their children to prevent the evacuation of the Russian military troops and equipment from the Dniester region. The TWU asks Yeltsin to dismiss General Lebed as the 14th Army's commander. It also demands Russia's recognition of the self-proclaimed Dniester Republic. The TWU warns Yeltsin not to use the Dniester region "as a medium of exchange in political games."
