July 24, 1990, Tuesday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

BIG THREE SALES SLUMP 15.9%

The nation's Big Three auto makers today said sales of their North American-made cars and light trucks tumbled 15.9% in mid-July compared with last year, when the sales rate zoomed upward.

Auto makers, analysts and dealers had been watching the numbers closely to see if a generally upward trend in the sales rate during the last two reporting periods would continue.

But General Motors Corp., Ford Motor Co. and Chrysler Corp. said car sales fell 17.8% and truck sales dropped 13%.
