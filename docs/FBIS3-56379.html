Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Chairman of Russia's Independent Union of Miners, Aleksandr Sergeyev, told Interfax on Friday that "he is satisfied with the discussion that a group of union leaders conducted with President Bill Clinton at a formal reception in the U.S. ambassador's residence in Moscow on Thursday." In his words, at a brief meeting the sides touched upon the problems of the workers' movement in Russia at a time of economic reform, international cooperation between free unions, and prospects of its development. At the end of the meeting, according to Sergeyev, Clinton instructed one of his aides to prepare a detailed report on the world trade union movement.
