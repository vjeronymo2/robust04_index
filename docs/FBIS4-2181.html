BFN [Text] Xian, April 6 (XINHUA) -- Li Ruihuan, chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, recently urged local officials here to better protect the Huangdi Mausoleum. Li Ruihuan is a member of the Standing Committee of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and honorary chairman of the China Huangdi Foundation. Huangdi (Yellow Emperor) is a legendary ancestor of the Chinese nation and yesterday was a traditional festival on which Chinese commemorate their ancestors. The Huangdi Mausoleum was of great significance for fostering Chinese culture, stimulating patriotism, strengthening national integration and advancing the modernization drive, Li said. In the course of the Chinese modernization drive, how to unite overseas compatriots to the maximum extent and give full play to their potential and intelligence has always been a major topic, Li said. He pointed out that Huangdi is a highly representative figure in the history of the Chinese nation. He called on the local government to give strong support to the repair and renovation of the mausoleum and to collect funds through public donations. He noted that there must be a long-term plan for the repair and protection of the mausoleum. Li Ruihuan conducted an investigation in Shaanxi Province on April 3-6. He inspected the No. 2 pit of the terracotta warriors being excavated and attended the public rites in honor of Huangdi yesterday.
