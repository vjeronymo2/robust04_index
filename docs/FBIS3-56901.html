Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev, 18 Jan -- In his speech at the conference of representatives of local councils of people's deputies of Ukraine, President Kravchuk noted that the problem of state building facing us, which had two aspects: internal political (to lay the basics of a balanced interaction between the branches of power and the creation of preconditions for their renewal) and external political (Ukraine has the image of a state that carries out a balanced policy). The first step along this path are the elections on 27 March. Leonid Kravchuk noted that it is inexpedient at this time to drag out the constitutional process because it leads to chaos and anarchy. He proposed the following mechanism: To support the basic conceptual provisions on the organization of state power and self-government today and then this would be examined by the Supreme Council and later submitted to a nationwide discussion. In the president's opinion, it would be inexpedient to include the issue of state symbols and language in the constitution. An amendment submitted by President Kravchuk was adopted regarding the Crimea: bodies of state power of the Crimea are formed on the basis of the constitution and laws of Ukraine; the authority of the Supreme Council of the Crimea may be suspended by the Supreme Council of Ukraine. Ivan Plyushch, chairman of the Supreme Council, said that an amendment to Article 114 of the Constitution, namely that the president may cancel acts of the state power of the Crimean Republic which contradict Ukraine's current legislation, will be discussed at a meeting of the Supreme Council on 20 January.
