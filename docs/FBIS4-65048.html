BFN [Text] Paris, May 11 (AFP) -- French Defence Minister Francois Leotard and his counterparts from the three Baltic states Wednesday [11 May] signed the first military cooperation accord among their countries in Paris. Leotard said the agreements "open up new perspectives in relations between France and each of the three (Baltic) states" allowing bilateral exercises as well as joint manoeuvres within the framework of the NATO Partnership for Peace, a military coooperation agreement recently signed by several East European states. Estonian Defence Minister Indrek Kannik stressed that his country and Latvia and Lithuania attached great importance to participation in all international forums. Leotard said Wednesday's accords would provide for young soldiers to attend training courses in French military schools and for wide-ranging exchanges of military personnel.
