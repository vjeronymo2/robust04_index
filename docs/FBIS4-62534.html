BFN [Text] At an extraordinary Latvian Government session today, Dainis Turlais, commander of the Latvian Defense Forces, was appointed Latvian plenipotentiary for settling issues related to the withdrawal of the Russian armed forces. According to the government press center, Turlais will also be a Latvian representative in the Latvian-Russian commission, which will tackle problems arising in the course of the implementation of the interstate agreement as well as controversial issues related to handing over, taking over, and marketing real estate and movable assets belonging to the Russian armed forces. The government appointed Latvian Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs to head the Latvian Government commission for control over the implementation of the above-mentioned agreement. Aivars Vovers, deputy secretary of state at the Latvian Foreign Ministry for eastern affairs, deputy head of the Latvian delegation for talks with Russia, was appointed Latvian plenipotentiary at the Skrunda radar station.
