BFN [Text] Havana, 3 Jun (NOTIMEX) -- Diplomatic sources have disclosed that two more Cubans entered the occupied residence of the Belgian ambassador in Havana at dawn today. With the new arrivals, the number of occupiers has risen to 124; they have been at the embassy since 28 May demanding permits to leave the country. The sources refrained from specifying how the two new arrivals entered the residence, which is surrounded by dozens of policemen from the National Revolutionary Police (PNR) and state security agents dressed in civilian attire. Today, Willy Verriest, the Belgian Government envoy, Ambassador Paul Vermiersch, and Foreign Ministry officials examined the "technical aspects of the measures that will be adopted to facilitate a final solution" to the problem, according to the state-owned news agency PRENSA LATINA.
