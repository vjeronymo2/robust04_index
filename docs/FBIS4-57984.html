BFN [From the "Economic Outlook" column by Miriam Leitao: "Displeasure"] [Text] Lawrence Summers, U.S. Treasury under secretary for international affairs, went right to the point in his talks with Workers Party [PT] economists. After announcing that the U.S. Government will respect the outcome of the Brazilian elections irrespective of who the winner is, Summers conveyed all his displeasure. He said: "I have talked with some 15 bankers and they have all expressed concern" about what they heard during PT presidential candidate Luis Inacio Lula da Silva's visit to the United States. "It was a tough conversation," economist Jorge Matoso said, "or frank, as Itamaraty would say."
