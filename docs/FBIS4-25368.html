BFN [By Flor Wang] [Text] Taipei, June 7 (CNA) -- Four representatives of the families of the 24 Taiwan tourists killed in the March 31 Qiandao Lake incident said Tuesday [7 June] that they will go to Hangzhou in southeastern Mainland China to witness the trial of the accused murderers, which begin this Friday. [Words indistinct] said at a press conference at the Legislative Yuan they hope to see a fast conclusion to the incident [words indistinct] mainland authorities, whom they condemned as not having shown respect for the memories of the victims or concern for human rights. Legislator Annette Lu, who accompanied family members of the 24 victims at the conference, called on the entire nation to support them. She said she also had contacted a score of international human-rights organizations for support, and had demanded that mainland judicial authorities postpone the trial of the three men until facts surrounding the incident are made publicly known. Meanwhile, H.Y. Hsu, deputy secretary-general of the Straits Exchange Foundation (SEF), the semi-official organization that handles cross-strait affairs with Mainland China in the absence of formal ties, called on mainland authorities not to ignore the rights of Taiwan citizens who lost family members in the incident and not to shrug off the compensation issue. As facts about the Qiandao Lake incident are not entirely clear, he said, mainland authorities should also continue investigating the incident and give reasonable explanations and answers to questions and doubts raised about it in a report released by SEF and the Mainland Affairs Council.
