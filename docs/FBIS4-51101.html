BFN [Text] Shenyang, May 24 (XINHUA) -- Overall labor reform has been completed by 9,200 enterprises in the northeast China province of Liaoning. A total of 2.11 million workers in state-run enterprises have been put under a new contract labor system, ending years of "jobs for life". This provincial capital cut 90,000 surplus workers from state-run enterprises last year. Meanwhile, the port city of Dalian has streamlined its 800 state enterprises to the tune of 32 percent, including 4,000 officials. All these changes have been taking place smoothly. In accordance with the labor system reform, a distribution system reform is also under way. First-line workers and technical specialists now receive relatively high wages. To safeguard the reform, the province has taken a big step forward in building a social security system, which covers most of the province's enterprises and workers.
