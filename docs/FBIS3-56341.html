Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Jan 13, BNS -- The Latvian Foreign Ministry plans to set up a working party to support Latvia's participation in NATO's Partnership for Peace program. The ministry will invite representatives of the Cabinet and officials of the Foreign Ministry and the Defense Ministry to join the group, which has not got a list of concrete members yet. The group's foundation may be confirmed in the next Cabinet meeting, spokespeople said. As reported, the Baltic presidents endorsed NATO's initiative in their Jan. 11 statement. The program offers the East European countries and the Baltic states a chance to cooperate with the alliance with a view to eventual membership.
