BFN [Text] The Salvadoran Government has offered the United Nations part of its Army for a future peacekeeping force, President Armando Calderon Sol has said. The Salvadoran president has said "El Salvador is unable to receive Haitian refugees," but he said "what he can offer the world is a professional, technical, and ethical armed force that can be ready for any UN mission if it needs brave men for a peacekeeping force." He has said "the United Nations knows it has a very professional armed force in El Salvador," adding the Salvadoran Government has already expressed the Army's willingness to assist. The government did not reveal the number of troops offered to the United Nations, but he said El Salvador has helicopter pilots with advanced training and other military specialists who can contribute to the international community. He has reiterated the United Nations can make the request, "which would be considered with great appreciation, because the Salvadoran Army is a professional and very technical Army." Moreover, Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina has rejected an eventual U.S. invasion of Haiti. "We reject invasions of any country," he has said.
