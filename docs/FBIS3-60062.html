Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on drug seizures and arrests related to drug trafficking monitored through 6 January.

Argentina
The Drug Addiction and Traffic Division, Ditonar, on 20 December 1993 seized 4.1 kg of cocaine and arrested a woman at the Cabo Vallejo police checkpoint on a bus linking Jujuy and the federal capital. Ditonar seized approximately 65 kg of cocaine hydrochloride and 150 kg of coca in 1993. (San Miguel de Tucuman LA GACETA in Spanish 21 Dec 93 p 20)
Peru
Colonel Hector Idiarte Rojas, chief of the San Martin-Huanuco Front Command, reported that 80 National Police members, including 13 officers from San Martin, have been charged with being involved in corruption, indiscipline, abuse, and drug trafficking. Idiarte said four police officers already have been dismissed. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 19 Dec 93 p A19) On 18 December 1993, the Peruvian Air Force, FAP, intercepted a small Colombian plane owned by drug traffickers when it was about to take off from a clandestine landing strip with 10 bags of drugs. The crew escaped. The incident occurred five miles south of the township of Palmapampa in Apurimac Valley. The FAP has seized 14 small airplanes owned by drug traffickers so far this year. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 19 Dec 93 p A19) Peruvian Navy personnel arrested 11 "narcoterrorists" and seized 20.4 kg of cocaine paste, 16 gallons of ammonium, 6.5 gallons of sulfuric acid, one scale, and "terrorist" propaganda during an operation in Huipoca township in Ucayali in the past few days. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 28 Dec 93 p A14)
