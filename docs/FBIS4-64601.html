BFN [Text] Oslo, May 4 (AFP) -- The European Parliament's decision to approve Norway's European Union (EU) membership agreement demonstrates that Norway is welcome in the EU, Norwegian Prime Minister Gro Harlem Brundtland said Wednesday [4 May]. "The decision also shows that the parliamentarians support the negotiation results, which ensure special Norwegian needs such as Norwegian agriculture, an active regional policy and new possibilities for support for fisheries," she said. The European Parliament accepted Norway's agreement Wednesday with 374 votes out of 456. Meanwhile, Norwegian Trade Minister Grete Knudsen, who was responsible for negotiating the EU agreement in Brussels, said Wednesday that a date for the Norwegian referendum would be decided upon by May 27. Swedes will vote November 13, Austria June 12, while Finland has yet to set a date.
