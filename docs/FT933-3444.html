930914 FT 14 SEP 93 / Arts: Today's Television A full and promising evening. The Oxford Dictionary Of Opera says that in Act 4 of Prokofiev's work The Fiery Angel 'Rupprecht watches Mephistopheles angrily devour a slow-moving serving boy and then resurrect him from a nearby rubbish dump. He joins the party'. Who wouldn't. BBC2 relays the opera from the Maryinsky Theatre, St Petersburg. Written in the Twenties, set against a background of religious hysteria in 16th century Germany, and banned by the Soviet regime, it was finally staged in 1955 (7.00). BBC1 screens the second documentary in The Skipper, about Cornishman Roger Nowell, 'fisherman and modern day buccaneer' (8.00). Channel 4 shows the second of its series DW Griffith: Father Of Film, about the man who made 'Intolerance' (9.00). Both began well. Today's film in BBC2's 10 x 10 showcase is 'The Sixth Continent' about the Channel Tunnel and Romney Marsh (10.20). Alan Whicker has always had an eye for attractive women, money, and vulgarity. Whicker's Miss World in South Africa seems to have the lot (10.40 ITV).
