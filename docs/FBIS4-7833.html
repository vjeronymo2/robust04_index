BFN [Text] Belgrade, 3 Apr (TANJUG) -- President of the Srpska republic [RS] Radovan Karadzic today issued an order to all bodies of the Ministry of Internal Affairs, military security bodies, and control points in the RS to urgently investigate the crimes in Prijedor and to secure full safety and freedom of movement of personnel of the International Committee of the Red Cross [ICRC]. The order, forwarded to TANJUG, says: In keeping with our obligations to international humanitarian law I order the following: To take all necessary measures to investigate crimes committed in the Srpska republic, particularly in Prijedor; To take all other actions needed to suppress extremism and secure the safety of members of national minorities who wish to stay in our republic; To remove all administrative obstacles to free movement of civilians who wish to leave in an operation organized by the ICRC; To secure police escort for all ICRC convoys, especially in the Prijedor area; All our bodies have the duty to provide full safety for ICRC personnel and civilians who decide to leave the RS under ICRC protection. Any objections to the conduct of the ICRC personnel must not be used as a pretext to threaten their safety. The office of the president of the republic is to be informed about any objections to the ICRC's conduct; To secure free passage across the Srpska republic borders to all ICRC convoys announced in advance and to exercise routine control; Failure to comply or fully to comply with the order entails the highest degree of accountability. A copy of the order will be forwarded to the ICRC to be used at checkpoints to avoid any possible misunderstandings.
