Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The press center of the Georgian Defense Ministry told Interfax that another explosion took place today at about 13:00 in the apartment of Georgian Deputy Defense Minister Niki Kekelidze, killed by the first one last night. The second explosion was much less powerful, but caused a slight concussion to Georgian Defense Minister Georgiy Karkarashvili, who came to see the site of the incident. The press center said that no one except the minister was hurt and suggested that a booby trap had been installed in Kekelidze's apartment. Meanwhile, the press center of the Georgian Interior Ministry failed to confirm the information on the second explosion.
