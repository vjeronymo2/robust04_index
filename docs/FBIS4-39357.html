BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Zakharchenko for KAZTAG] [Text] Almaty, 7 June -- "The main goal of my visit is to speed up the implementation of the accords between the two states which were attained earlier," Salman Khurshid, India's minister of state for external affairs, said at a press conference here today after completing his visit to Kazakhstan. "We have seen once again," the minister said, "that Kazakhstan leadership adheres to the strengthening of ties with India and the development of mutually beneficial cooperation of businessmen from both countries." He noted with satisfaction that a business council to coordinate joint activity had been set up. A solemn ceremony to inaugurate an Indian cultural center will be held in Almaty soon, and an Indian restaurant will open. By late June, regular Almaty- Delhi flights will start, something to boost bilateral relations even further. "During the visit," Salman Khurshid stressed, "mutual understanding of the need to sign accords on avoiding double taxation of exported goods and providing firm guarantees for investments took shape." He expressed the confidence that Kazakhstan and India would actively cooperate in many international organizations to secure peace and stability on the Asian continent. The Indian delegation left Almaty for Bishkek today.
