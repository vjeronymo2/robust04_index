Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Edward Chen] [Text] Buenos Aires, Feb. 14 (CNA) -- Two-way trade between Taiwan and Chile reached US$720 million in 1993, according to statistics compiled by the Economic Section of the Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in Chile. Chile exported US$484.7 million worth of goods to Taiwan last year, while imports totaled US$225.6 million, leaving a trade surplus of US$179.1 million in Chile's favor, the statistics showed. Copper, seafood, fish powders, lumber, pulp and fruits were Chile's main exports to Taiwan, while imports consisted of textiles, electric equipment, machinery, computers, bicycles, auto and bicycle parts, and fishing nets. Taiwan was Chile's number two trade partner in Asia during the period, and Chile was Taiwan's number two trade partner in South America, the statistics revealed.
