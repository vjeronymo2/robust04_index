Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] A press statement has been released on the meeting held yesterday at the headquarters of the General Secretariat of the Gulf Cooperation Council [GCC] between the GCC and the United States. The GCC side was chaired by Sulayman al-Sulayyim, Saudi minister of commerce and current chairman of the GCC Committee for Trade Cooperation. The U.S. side was chaired by His Excellency Secretary of Commerce Ronald Brown. According to the press statement, the meeting was attended by 'Abdullah al-Quwayz, the GCC assistant secretary general for economic affairs, Ambassador Ma'mun Kurdi, the general coordinator of economic negotiations in the GCC, a number of officials representing specialized sectors in the GCC member states, and U.S. officials. The statement welcomed the continuation of the economic dialogue between the GCC member states and the United States, and stated that this dialogue helped strengthen economic cooperation between the two sides in line with the strategic political ties existing between them. The statement also stressed the need to expand and diversify economic relations and improve the movement of goods between markets on both sides. The statement pointed out that the two sides had agreed to cooperate in the field of specifications and standards and to remove trade barriers. The statement referred to wider participation and a contribution in the development of investment opportunities, as reviewed by the conference of businessmen from GCC member states and the United States held in Washington 20-21 April 1992. The statement added that this conference was a good opportunity for both sides to exchange information on the perspectives of investment, particularly with regard to the new companies in the markets. The statement supported the idea of holding similar conferences once every two years and it welcomed Bahrain's offer to host the next conference in 1995. The statement further said that the two sides had agreed to set up two working groups, one to look into the possibilities of practical and technical cooperation and the other to examine and discuss the opportunities and the obligations resulting from the GATT system and the Uruguay agreements, facilitate the entry of the GCC member states to the GATT agreement, and find the necessary means to expand the perspectives of trade between the GCC member states and the United States. The two sides also agreed to start negotiations with a view to drafting a memorandum of understanding in the field of specifications. The statement added that the two sides agreed on the need and importance of setting up a free and open world trade system.
