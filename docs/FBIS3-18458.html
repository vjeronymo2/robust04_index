Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Mar. 2 (A.A.) -- Foreign Ministry officials said on Wednesday [2 March] that they have not received any information from Russia concerning a meeting in Moscow of the supporters of PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan], the separatist terrorist organization. Foreign Ministry Deputy Spokesman Ferhat Ataman told a weekly news conference that the Ministry asked for information on the issue. "However, Russia has yet to inform us on the meeting," Ataman said. Ataman said Foreign Ministry official Bilgin Unan, who is currently in Russia for official talks, could bring the issue up. A conference was held in Moscow last month by a number of organizations which are believed to have ties with the separatist terrorist organization. Russian Minister Valerie Samsurov [name and title as received] was in attendance at the Moscow meeting of the PKK supporters. Russian Ambassador to Turkey Albert Chernyshev was summoned to the Foreign Ministry and was asked to inquire about the meeting. He assured Foreign Ministry officials that there has been no change in his country's policy. Ataman said a delegation from the Foreign Ministry arrived in Moscow on Tuesday to discuss relations between Turkey and Russia.
