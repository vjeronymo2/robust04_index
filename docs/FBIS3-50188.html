Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Much has been said this week about the suspension of Russian oil suppplies to our country. According to the latest reports from Moscow, everything is all right now. Ambassador to Russia Roman Paldan, with whom our permanent correspondent to Moscow talked this morning, has more details. [Begin recording] [Correspondent Smihula] I know that you held intensive talks this morning with the senior staff at relevant ministries and offices. A while ago you spoke on the telephone to (Valeriy Chernayev), president of the Russian company Neftetrans. Ambassador, what is the latest development? [Paldan] The end result is that oil supplies to Slovakia will be resumed at 1200 hrs today. [Smihula] Do you mean our time or Moscow time? [Paldan] Moscow time. [Smihula] This means then that Russian oil should have begun traveling toward our country when the lunchtime edition of Radiozurnal is on the air. [Paldan] It is expected that the throughput of 27,000 tonnes in 24 hours will be achieved shortly. This is close to the normal throughput, I would say. Available at the present time are 150,000 tonnes of oil, for which documentation has been issued. Work on further quantities is going on in order to ensure a continuous flow and subsequently also continuous production in Slovnaft [Bratislava-based refinery working on Russian oil].
