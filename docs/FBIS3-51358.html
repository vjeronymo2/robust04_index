Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] San Jose, 15 Jan (NOTIMEX) -- President Rafael Angel Calderon said today Costa Rica will continue its struggle to get better conditions in European markets for all banana producing nations. Calderon regretted that Ecuador, Panama, Guatemala, and Honduras did not attend the 13 January banana producers' meeting in Costa Rica "to help find a joint position" against the commercial restrictions of the European Union (EU). Last December the EU offered to increase the Latin American producers' annual banana import quota by 10 percent. The quotas were set last July at 2 million tons with a high tariff on the surplus. The EU also proposed to distribute 70 percent of its import licenses among these nations by establishing fixed quotas for each country. Through this mechanism Europe seeks the withdrawal of charges filed by the area's producers before a GATT panel. Costa Rica summoned the banana producers to a meeting but Ecuador, Guatemala, Honduras, and Panama did not attend because they consider that any regional proposal should follow the GATT decision on the dispute. Calderon told NOTIMEX: "Costa Rica will continue its struggle to get better conditions in the European market to benefit not only the Latin American nations but also the Caribbean producers." The president said that Colombia, Costa Rica, Nicaragua, and Venezuela will seek median formulas to guarantee a satisfactory solution for everyone. Foreign Trade Minister Alberto Rojas said the problem was serious because "a GATT panel resolution did not obligate Europe and its enforcement could take years, with irreparable damage to the producers."
