Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] In an exclusive interview with our correspondent today, Mr. Haddadi, the Iranian ambassador in Kabul, described Iran's efforts to establish peace and stability in Afghanistan and entente cordiale between the warring groups in Kabul. Describing the situation in Kabul as deplorable he said: Most foreign embassies have ceased their diplomatic activities in Kabul and government offices have stopped working. He added: Hospitals in Kabul are full of the injured from recent clashes and treatment centers are facing shortages of medicines and medical facilities. Referring to the activities of our country's embassy in Kabul to establish peace in Afghanistan he said: So far direct talks have been held with the president, prime minister, and other leaders of Afghan mojahedin groups, on several occasions. The 24-hour cease-fire in Kabul, and the evacuation of diplomats and people from the city are the results of those efforts. Declaring Iran's preparedness to continue peace-seeking efforts in Kabul he said: A committee, under the auspices of the Iranian embassy in Kabul, is to review the existing disputes between the warring groups. So far the Afghan president has declared his readiness in that respect. Talks are to be held with the prime minister and other Afghan leaders.
