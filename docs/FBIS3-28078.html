Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 3 KYODO -- North Korean heir apparent Kim Chong-il's half-brother, Kim Pyong-il, has been named ambassador to Finland and is awaiting agreement from the Finnish Government, a western diplomatic in Seoul said Thursday [3 February]. Kim Pyong-il, long considered a political rival of Kim Chong-il, was removed as envoy to Bulgaria in early December last year amid signs that his return may indicate that Kim Chong-il has resolved the leadership succession issue. He had been appointed to take up the post of North Korea's ambassador to Hungary in August 1988 but was reassigned to Bulgaria in December that year. Meanwhile, the appointment of Kim Kwang-sop, husband of Kim Pyong-il's elder sister, Kim Kyong-chin, as North Korea's ambassador to Austria indicates that family members of Kim Pyong-il failed to occupy key posts in North Korea's power hierarchy. Speculation was abuzz on whether Kim Chong-il's succession to power has come under challenge after Kim Pyong-il returned from an overseas mission and Kim Yong-chu, younger brother of North Korean President Kim Il-song, was named to the Politburo of the North Korean Workers' Party. The appointment of Kim Pyong-il to an overseas diplomatic mission, however, showed that Kim Chong-il is still wary of his half-brother in securing his grip on power in the country, analysts said.
