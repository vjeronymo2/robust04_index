BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondent Vadim Byrkin] [Text] Simferopol, 11 Apr (ITAR-TASS) -- The Russia Bloc, which supports the strengthening of the peninsula's links with the Russian Federation, has won an overwhelming victory in the elections to the Crimean Supreme Soviet. The second round of voting was held on Sunday. Viktor Alsufyev, chairman of the republic's Central Electoral Commission, said at a news conference in Simferopol this morning that 54 out of the 94 seats in the parliament went to the Russia Bloc. Four seats remain vacant. Yuriy Meshkov, president of the Republic of Crimea, said in an interview with an ITAR-TASS correspondent that the Russia Bloc's victory is confirmation of support for the aims and tasks declared by the electoral bloc. The first session of the new Crimean parliament will be held May 10. It was also announced at a news conference that the inhabitants of Crimea have taken part in elections to the Ukrainian Supreme Soviet. Eleven deputies from Crimea out of 23 were elected.
