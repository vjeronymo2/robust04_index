BFN [By Crime Press correspondent Larisa Kislinskaya] [Text] Moscow, 28 Apr--The forces maintaining public order over the Easter and 1 May holidays will be increased considerably, Militia Lieutenant General Vyacheslav Ogorodnikov, head of the chief directorate for the maintenance of public order of Russia's Ministry of Internal Affairs, told a Crime Press correspondent. Reinforced detachments will be posted around cemeteries and churches where services and religious processions are to be held. Measures for maintaining public order will be in operation outside churches until six a.m., the finishing time for the religious processions. Reinforced posts will be mounted on 1 May at sites of mass public demonstrations, rallies, and marches. According to our information, these include Triumphal Square, Tverskaya Street, Manezhnaya and Kaluzhkaya Squares, and the University Metro station area. As Ogorodnikov emphasized, the maintenance of public order will be implemented entirely by Moscow militia forces. Ministry of Internal Affairs troops are only being kept in reserve should unforeseen circumstances arise.
