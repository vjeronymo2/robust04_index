BFN [Text] The Fifth Session of the Ninth National Assembly issues Communique No. 9 as follows: On the morning of 4 June, the National Assembly debated in the conference hall the draft law on the amendment of and supplement to a number of articles on the law on military service under the direction of National Assembly Vice Chairman Dang Quan Thuy. Twenty-one deputies, out of the 41 registered to address the meeting, expressed their views. They were Ho Duc Duyet of Song Be, Tang Nghia of Ha Tinh, Tran Van Lien of Kien Giang, Vu Thi Ngoc Dau of Thai Binh, Nguyen Khoa Kim Boi of Thua Thien-Hue, Nguyen Van An of Ho Chi Minh City, Nguyen Dinh Hong of An Giang, Lo Van Inh of Lai Chau, K'Tuat of Lam Dong, Nguyen Quoc Thuoc of Nghe An, Dinh Van The of Vinh Phu, Lo Thi Luyen of Son La, Truong Cong Pho of Tien Giang, Duong Cong Suu of Lang Son, Tran Thi Bach Tuyet of Dong Nai, Tran Duc Tam of Ha Bac, Ha Quang Du of Tuyen Quang, Nguyen Don of Quang Nam-Da Nang, Chu Duy Kinh of Hanoi, Nguyen Viet Hong of Minh Hai, and Dam Van Nguy of Cao Bang. The deputies held that the draft law has been carefully compiled and revised. It has received considerable input from people of all strata, National Assembly deputies, and different echelons and levels in the entire country. The deputies concentrated their discussion on the following issues: the obligation and responsibility of mass organizations and economic organizations in the implementation of the law on military service; members and the duties of the military service council; postponement and exemption of military service in peacetime; the service terms in peacetime for non-commissioned officers and soldiers; rewards, punishments, and social justice in the execution of the law on military service; and regulations and policies regarding soldiers who have completed their military service. In the afternoon, the deputies exchanged views in their groups about the draft law on the promotion of domestic investment in order to prepare for their debate on this draft law in the conference hall on 6 June.
