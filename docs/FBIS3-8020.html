Language: Slovene Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Following Slovenia's diplomatic actions over the past few weeks, our country was officially offered membership in the Partnership for Peace program in Brussels. Slovenia will join this program as the first country that is not a member of the NATO Cooperation Council. Edo Zitnik has the details: [Begin Zitnik recording] The NATO pact has now also officially offered cooperation in the Partnership for Peace to Slovenia as the 11th East European country. [passage omitted] Because each country signs the Partnership for Peace with NATO individually, its concrete contents will depend greatly on the Slovene offers for cooperation. We must keep in mind that Slovenia is not among the first in the Partnership and that other countries, because of their greater strategic importance, have already taken the best that NATO has offered.
