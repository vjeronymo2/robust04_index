Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Sitnikov] [Text] United Nations, 1 Mar -- Bosnian Muslims are against the deployment of Russian troops in Sarajevo and are appealing to the UN Security Council to "postpone" the deployment of an additional Russian contingent in Bosnia. This is stated in a letter from Alia Izetbegovic, the Bosnian president, which was forwarded to the current president of the UN Security Council on Monday [28 February]. This document, which has not been officially issued yet, was obtained by an Itar-Tass correspondent. "The behavior of the Russian soldiers after their arrival in Sarajevo has annoyed UN [words indistinct] in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Since their arrival they have displayed their support for the aggressor. Attitudes which remind us of certain episodes in the past have been strongly demonstrated. Our people regard this as a provocation which could cause an incident," Izetbegovic pointed out. "We do not endorse the arrival of additional Russian forces in Bosnia-Herzegovina," he stressed. "We believe that any such decision should be put off."
