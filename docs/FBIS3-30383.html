Language: Albanian Article Type:BFN [Text] A high-ranking NATO delegation has ended its visit in our country. Here is a report: [Correspondent] Continuing last evening's talks with the high-ranking NATO delegation and after stressing that Albania actively supports the Partnership for Peace [PFP] plan, Defense Minister Safet Zhulali referred to the letter by President Sali Berisha to NATO Secretary General Manfred Woerner in which he officially expressed Albania's desire to participate in the PFP and develop extensive cooperation within NATO. The letter also reads: We are ready to work with the political and military structures of the alliance, participate in joint military maneuvers and consultations on defense and security, and take advantage of other possibilities offered by the PFP. We are willing to offer a company, as well as our naval and air bases for the PFP purposes. Albania is the first ex-communist country to have requested NATO membership. Albania's goal continues to be membership in the North Atlantic Alliance. The talks concluded that Albania will be prepared to draft the position paper and sign the PFP agreement in Brussels. The meeting also defined those areas in which Albania commits itself to participate, such as joint exercises, peacekeeping and humanitarian operations, and the creation of air and port facilities. They also agreed that Albania will send its own permanent representatives to the relevant NATO institutions. NATO Assistant Secretary General Gebhardt von Moltke stated that he was impressed by Albania's performance and its readiness to participate in the PFP. We will help to complete the position paper and everything else so as to ensure Albania's active participation in the PFP plan. He asked our defense minister to convey greetings to President Sali Berisha from NATO Secretary General Manfred Woerner, as well as his high regard and best wishes on the cordial message and Albania's position considering PFP participation. The high-ranking NATO delegation left our country this morning. Defense Minister Safet Zhulali, Foreign Ministry representatives, and high-ranking Defense Ministry cadres were at Rinas Airport to see the delegation off. Greek Ambassador in Tirana Khristodhoulos Tsalikis was also present.
