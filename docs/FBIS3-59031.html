Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Giessen (DPA) -- Some 250 people joined a protest rally against the federal party congress of the National Democratic Party of Germany (NPD) in Ehringshausen in Hesse today. On banners, on leaflets, and in chants the demonstrators condemned right-wing extremism and xenophobia. According to police information, the demonstration passed peacefully and without incident. It ended at midday. The party congress was protected by a large number of police. [passage omitted] The NPD party congress, which was attended by about 200 delegates, is due to end this evening. The police had also set up cordons in the surrounding area. According to the federal chairman of the NPD, Guenter Deckert, the party congress was to nominate among others candidates for European elections. According to estimates by the Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution, which says the NPD shows unconstitutional tendencies, the party has about 5,000 members throughout Germany.
