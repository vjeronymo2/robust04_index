BFN [Text] Vilnius, 2 May, BNS -- A high-ranking Russian Foreign Ministry official should come to Lithuania to discuss relations between the two countries, Lithuanian Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys said Monday [2 May] after his meeting with President Algirdas Brazauskas. Gylys told reporters that he and Brazauskas spoke about the exchange of letters between Lithuanian and Russian presidents, as well as the problems discussed in them. "There are things to negotiate about," Gylys said, but noted that preparations for further talks only started. Brazauskas last Friday received Russian President Boris Yeltsin's letter implying that the implementation of bilateral economic treaties is bound up with the problem of Russian transit to the Kaliningrad region through Lithuanian territory. Minister Gylys emphasized that Russian transit through Lithuania is currently carried out according to Lithuanian rules. The minister said he intends to discuss the strategy of transit talks with the head of the working group for talks with the CIS, Virgilijus Bulovas. Gylys said he and Brazauskas also discussed the results of the minister's recent visits to the United States and several Latin American countries. The minister said they analyzed proposals for political, economic, diplomatic and cultural cooperation brought from Venezuela, Brazil, Uruguay, and Argentina.
