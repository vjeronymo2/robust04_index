BFN [Text] President Rauf Denktas has sent a letter to UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali explaining that he cannot possibly accept the amended confidence-building measures [CBM] package or have it approved by the people of the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus [TRNC]. Denktas sent a letter to the UN secretary general and reiterated the views of the Turkish Cypriot side regarding the CBM package. Recalling that the Turkish Cypriot side is committed to the CBM package as it appeared in Butrus-Ghali's report of 1 July 1993, Denktas expressed his hope that the balance between the risks and benefits that was upset in the package will be rectified through indirect talks. Stating that he is ready to continue with the negotiations and support Butrus-Ghali's goodwill mission, Denktas pointed out that having the road leading to Maras [Varosha] under UN control was not in the initial CBM package and is contrary to the assurances given to the Turkish Cypriot side by Butrus-Ghali and his representatives. Denktas said that the Turkish Cypriot side is ready to provide the Greek Cypriots safe entry to Maras. Reiterating that the lack of trust between the two sides is the real reason for the Cyprus problem, Denktas added that the Turkish Cypriots would like the embargo imposed on the TRNC eased and the citizens of the TRNC given freedom of travel in exchange for the opening of the fenced area in Maras. Explaining that he had the initial package approved by the Assembly after receiving assurances from the UN representatives that there will not be any amendments or additions, Denktas added that promises were made regarding the Greek and Turkish Cypriots' benefiting simultaneously from the implementation of the package. Stating that they were surprised when they were informed that items in the 27 March 1994 nondocument that did not exist in the original CBM package could not be changed later, Denktas stressed that items in Butrus-Ghali's report of 4 April 1994 differed from those in the already accepted 1 July 1993 document. In the letter he sent to Butrus-Ghali, Denktas reiterated that the Turkish Cypriots are determined regarding the following four points: simultaneous benefits to both sides from the opening of the Maras and Lefkosa [Nicosia] Airports, air traffic arrangements for airplanes using Lefkosa Airport, the issue of customs duty at the Maras and Lefkosa Airports, and free access to the area between the UN-controlled region and the fenced area in Maras. Denktas pointed out that the 11 March 1994 map, which shows the area that will be opened in Maras, differs from the 19 May 1993 map that had been agreed on. Denktas stressed that in the wake of all these developments, it will be very difficult for him to accept the new package, which was changed by the United Nations, and have it approved by the people of the TRNC.
