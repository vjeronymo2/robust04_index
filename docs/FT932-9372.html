930514 FT 14 MAY 93 / S African economy grows The South African economy has grown for the first time in 18 months and economists said they were cautiously optimistic that the country's longest recession might be coming to an end, Reuter reports from Johannesburg. The Central Statistical Service released figures yesterday showing that gross domestic product, seasonally adjusted, had risen at the equivalent of 0.8 per cent a year in the first quarter of 1993. It was the first growth since the third quarter of 1991, when the economy grew 0.7 per cent.
