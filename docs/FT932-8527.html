930519 FT 19 MAY 93 / Sterling rises on hopes of calmer ERM SEVERAL European currencies, including sterling and the Italian lira, rose strongly against the D-Mark in the build-up to yesterday's Yes vote in Denmark, writes James Blitz. Investors sold D-Marks after taking the view that a clear result in favour of the Maastricht treaty would ease tensions in Europe's exchange rate mechanism. In recent weeks investors, fearing a No vote, had been seeking a safe haven against currency turmoil and a renewed threat to economic and monetary union by buying D-Marks and Swiss francs. German government bond prices also fell yesterday, to the benefit of Italian and French bonds. Sterling gained a pfennig to close at DM2.4875 after the final Danish opinion polls showed a clear majority for the treaty. The Italian lira was also a strong performer, closing at L909.2 against the D-Mark from a previous close of L914.9. The dollar gained significantly from the D-Mark's weakness, rising more than a pfennig in Europe to finish at DM1.6272, a 7-week high. It closed in London at DM1.624 and at DM1.62465 in New York.
