Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Shanayev] [Text] Vladikavkaz January 17 TASS -- "I am grateful to the multinational people of North Ossetia for supporting my candidature during the election campaign and for voting for me at the January 16 elections," North Ossetian President-Elect Akhsarbek Galazov said in an interview with ITAR-TASS today. "The winners are above all, the sound forces who cherish the interests of peace and concord", he said. "The elections of the president of the republic must consolidate the republic's multinational society, they must provide a positive impulse for the active implementation of reforms in the economy, social sphere and political life. Much work lies ahead, primarily associated with reforming state structures and local government bodies, the adoption of a new constitution of the Republic of North Ossetia. Everything must be done for the benefit of the people". The Central Electoral Commission is now summarising the final results of the vote. According to preliminary data, about 160,000 voters, or 66.3 per cent cast their ballots in favour of Galazov, while than 81,000 people (33.7 per cent) voted for his rival, government leader Sergey Khetagurov. According to local observers, Akhsarbek Galazov was mainly supported by the masses of working people, veterans, teachers, scientists and cultural personalities. Sergey Khetagurov was mainly backed by industrialists, entrepreneurs and commercial structures. Akhsarbek Galazov was born in 1929 in an old Ossetian village of Khumalag in the Pravoberezhniy district of North Ossetia. After graduating from a teacher-training institute, he taught Russian to schoolchildren. He then worked as an education ministry inspector, head of the teacher-retraining institute, the minister of education of North Ossetia and the chairman of the council of ministers. From 1976 Akhsarbek Galazov worked as the rector of the North Ossetian State University. He is a professor who has written scientific works, manuals and monographs devoted to teaching and linguistics. From 1990 to 1991 Akhsarbek Galazov was the first secretary of the North Ossetian regional committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union. From 1990 he was chairman of the Supreme Soviet of the North Ossetian Soviet Socialist Republic (now the Republic of North Ossetia). Akhsarbek Galazov has consistently advocated the concord and consolidation of all branches of power and a peaceful settlement of relations between North Ossetia and Ingushetia. Last december he was elected to the Federation Council, the upper chamber of Russia's new legislature, the Federal Assembly.
