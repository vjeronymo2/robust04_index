Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, March 8, IRNA -- First Deputy Foreign Minister Mohammad Hashemi said at the gathering of African diplomats in Tehran that the Islamic Republic wishes to broaden relations with African countries. Hashemi who was addressing a reception at the embassy of Sudan said since the triumph of the Islamic revolution in 1979, Iran has been a friendly country to all African states, and supports the struggle of African peoples against racial discrimination. He said the Africans have added a golden leaf to the history of the struggles of world nations, and today certain sources that claim to be concerned about the status of human rights accuse certain African states of human rights violations while they continue to plunder the resources of the very same African countries. He said the Islamic Republic looks forward to broader cooperation with the African nations in the area of politics, economy and culture.
