August 10, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

CUTTING WATER USE AND DWP LEAKS

I read with interest the article on DWP leaks (July 26). I discovered a similar leak in my neighborhood a few days later.

I made at least four calls to the DWP "Trouble Board" to report this leak; I never got through to anyone who could or would help. For all I know the leak is still not fixed.

I am a water pipeline contractor and this type of incident always attracts my attention. The answer to this problem is to have these breaks repaired by private contractors.

The Los Angeles Department of Public Works has a list of contractors for emergency repair work of sewer problems. I am on this list and I know it works. We respond quickly to these problems and they are repaired for much less than the city could afford to do it.

I strongly suggest that DWP officials explore the use of private contractors to repair their system and also work on new installations. The work would get done quicker and private contractors would cost the public much less money.

JOHN T. MALLOY

Los Angeles
