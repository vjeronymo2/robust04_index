940919 FT 19 SEP 94 / Rudeness 'common' in jobs market Rudeness and discourtesy towards job applicants is widespread, the Industrial Society says in a report published today, Richard Donkin writes. Ms Julie Amber, the society's recruitment specialist, said she had found a surprising number of personnel managers who treated job applicants with complete disregard and rudeness. She found that many never replied to applicants who failed to be shortlisted, that treating people discourteously at interview was commonplace and that candidates were often not informed quickly enough about interview results. She said: 'Organisations will spend thousands of pounds on public relations and yet many of them fail to understand that people who seek jobs with them will tell their family and friends if they have been poorly treated.'
