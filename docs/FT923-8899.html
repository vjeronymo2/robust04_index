920808 FT 08 AUG 92 / International Company News: Canadian retail chain slumps in second period THE RECESSION and special charges reduced George Weston's second-quarter profit by about 90 per cent. The Weston family's main Canadian holding company earned CDollars 3.8m (USDollars 3.22m) or 3 cents a share, down from CDollars 51.3m or CDollars 1.06 a share a year earlier, on sales of CDollars 2.68bn against CDollars 2.6bn. Both periods included special items. On an operating basis profit fell 43 per cent to CDollars 38.8m. Canadian Tire, a national retailer, also felt the impact of the recession, earning CDollars 43.1m in the second half, against CDollars 68.5m a year earlier.
