Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (AFP)--A group of dissidents in Shanghai have sent a petition to parliament, signed by 54 people, demanding freedom of the press, party pluralism and free trade unions, one of the signatories, Bao Ge, said Tuesday. The dissident said in a phone conversation from Shanghai that the 19-point petition had been sent to the National People's Congress (NPC) last Friday. Entitled "Blueprint for the political democratisation of the state and society in 1994," the petition was signed by 54 people "from all walks of life in Shanghai -- intellectuals, academics, artists, peasants, workers and private businessmen," Bao said. The names includes those of Yang Zhou, Wang Fucheng and Li Guotao, prominent Shanghai dissidents, he said. "It is the first signature campaign, and we are going to extend the movement nationwide," he said.
