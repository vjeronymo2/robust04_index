BFN [INTERFAX report: "Estonia Equips Border With Modern Technology"] [Text] Construction of modern technical control facilities, including radar, television cameras, and night vision instruments, will begin on the Estonian border this summer. This was announced in an interview for the paper PAEVALEHT by Tarmo Kyuts, general director of the Estonian border protection department. According to him, at the moment most border guards are equipped only with binoculars and automatic weapons. Kyuts announced that the most difficult section of the border is the "control line with Russia." One of the most profitable criminal occupations there is the trade in "live goods." Border guards are offerd $50 each to help ferry one Kurd across the Russian-Estonian border. Kyuts noted that the organizers of the illegal ferrying of people to the northern countries in Russia and their assistants in Estonia are making millions.
