940517 FT 17 MAY 94 / World News in Brief: Blair seeks support from left in bid for Labour leadership Support for shadow chancellor Gordon Brown was ebbing rapidly among Labour MPs as shadow home secretary Tony Blair put out feelers to the left in his bid to succeed the late John Smith as party leader. Mr Blair is believed to be seeking the endorsement of Robin Cook, a potential leftwing candidate. A deal could open the way for a straight fight between Mr Blair and John Prescott, the centre-left shadow employment secretary. Page 20; Labour MPs hold advantage in poll, Page 11; Joe Rogaly, Page 18
