920810 FT 10 AUG 92 / The Week Ahead Results Due THREE of the biggest UK insurance companies will still be in the red when they report pre-tax losses for the first half of 1992 this week. But with rate increases in the home, motor and commercial insurance markets taking hold and companies avoiding the weather losses of recent years, there are some glints of light on the horizon. General Accident, which reports tomorrow, is widely expected to do best with pre-tax losses cut to between Pounds 20m and Pounds 30m compared to a deficit of Pounds 105.2m at the same stage in 1991. GA may even have made a small profit in the second quarter, according to some analysts. Commercial Union, the most successful and aggressive UK insurer, is on Wednesday expected to report losses of around Pounds 30m, a deterioration on last year's Pounds 26.3m. Royal Insurance, whose balance sheet is weakest, is on Thursday expected to register losses of between Pounds 70m and Pounds 100m, compared to a deficit of Pounds 97m last year. Hanson, the Anglo-American conglomerate, is expected to report a significant fall in pre-tax profits for the nine months to June when it announces its results on Thursday. Pre-tax profits are likely to have fallen from Pounds 967m to around Pounds 760m. However, last year's results were boosted by acquisition benefits. Net debt, which is US dollar denominated, is expected to have fallen to Pounds 1.1bn because of the weakness of the dollar. British Airways' first quarter figures tomorrow should reveal pre-tax profits of around Pounds 90m compared Pounds 9m a year earlier. The market will also be looking for news of progress on cost cutting, the recent deal to take a Dollars 750m stake in USAir and clues over the impact of price wars in the airline industry.
