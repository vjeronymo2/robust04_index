BFN [Text] A peaceful demonstration was held in Bujumbura this afternoon by Burundi University students. [Name indistinct] reports. [Correspondent] The students believed to be [words indistinct] thousands, gathered in front of the Burundi University dining hall. then they went to the French Embassy in Burundi and then to the Novotel Hotel. They passed through Unity Boulevard, in front of the [word indistinct] building [words indistinct] and ended their march at the Prince Louis Rwagasore stadium. Many of the slogans on their placards (?were against) Mitterrand and France's policy in Rwanda and Burundi, and read as follows: Mitterrand Sacrificed Rwandans; He Is Going To Sacrifice Burundians; [words indistinct] of the Subregion: Unite; the French African Policy is Meant To Cause Harm; France Should Answer for Its Crimes Committed in Africa; [words indistinct] Should Remember Vietnam. Other placards called on the Burundi authorities not to accept the transit of French troops through Burundi. The rejection of French troops was the motive for organizing the peaceful demonstration. At the end of their march at Prince Louis Rwagasore stadium, the students dispersed after deciding to meet tomorrow for a similar march, organized this time by the coalition of opposition political parties. In their own words [words indistinct] it is a question of combat and, currently, demonstrations are the best way to win.
