BFN [Text] Official Ukrainian-American negotiations on economic cooperation are underway in Kiev. Ukraine's Economics Minister Roman Shpek and special adviser of the U.S. President Nicholas Burns head the delegations. In the course of these talks the parties are discussing problems related to rendering assistance to Ukraine in the sphere of privatization, trade, investments, and allocation of funds to procure medicines and food products. The American party has again reaffirmed its readiness to support concrete and intensive reforms in the economy and to push for assistance to Ukraine from the G-7, International Monetary Fund, World Bank, and European Bank for Reconstruction and Development. According to Roman Shpek, "speeding up of the opportunity to receive financial, technical, and credit assistance for Ukrainian economic subjects of all forms of ownership" is one of the major tasks of these negotiations. In the course of the meetings the parties exchanged their opinions regarding the work of the American-Ukrainian Fund in support of private enterprises. The U.S. Government has already allocated $150 million for the three-year program of this fund.
