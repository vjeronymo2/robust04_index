940120 FT 20 JAN 94 / International Company News: Weyerhaeuser ahead despite pulp losses Weyerhaeuser, the US forest products company reported earnings of Dollars 101.7m, or 50 cents a share, in the fourth quarter, in spite of a Dollars 21m loss for the quarter in its pulp and paper division. Last year, fourth-quarter earnings were Dollars 85.3m, or 42 cents. Sales for the quarter were Dollars 2.59bn, a 10 per cent rise. The company said prices for logs and lumber exceeded year-ago levels, lifting operating earnings from its timberlands and wood products segment to Dollars 258m, from Dollars 175m in the fourth quarter of 1992. For the year, Weyerhaeuser recorded earnings of Dollars 579.3m, or Dollars 2.83, on sales of Dollars 9.54bn, compared with 1992 earnings of Dollars 372m, or Dollars 1.83, on sales of Dollars 9.27bn. Boise Cascade, the Idaho-based US forest products company, said its losses in the fourth quarter nearly matched a year ago, but that higher wood prices had increased revenues for the year. Losses continued at its pulp and paper operations. Boise Cascade posted a net loss of Dollars 23.7m, or 98 cents, for the quarter, compared with a loss of Dollars 29.6m, or 97 cents, a year ago. Sales in the quarter were Dollars 997m, against Dollars 906m in last year's fourth quarter. For the year, the company lost Dollars 77m, or Dollars 3.17, on sales of Dollars 4bn, compared with 1992 losses of Dollars 227.5m, or Dollars 6.73, on sales of Dollars 3.7bn.
