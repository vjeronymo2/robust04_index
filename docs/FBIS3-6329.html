Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 17 KYODO -- Japan's benchmark money supply grew 1.6 percent in February on a year-to-year basis, the Bank of Japan said in a preliminary report Thursday [17 March]. The M2 and certificates of deposit (CDs) gained a revised 1.6 percent in January, marking the first growth pace pickup in four months. M2 is cash in circulation plus demand and time deposits. The broader measure of liquidity, including postal savings, government debts and investment trusts, expanded 3.0 percent in February over a year before, compared with a 3.0 percent rise in January.
