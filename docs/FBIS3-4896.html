Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 2 (YONHAP) -- The South Korean Foreign Ministry denied Wednesday that the United States has formally raised the issue of Seoul abolishing its National Security Law, but was looking into the exact nature of comments made by a U.S. official last week. "The issue was never officially raised," said one ministry official, reacting to remarks attributed to Tom Hubbard, U.S. deputy assistant secretary of State, who said Washington wants to see democracy take firm root in South Korea and the National Security Law repealed. The remarks drew a sensitive reaction because Hubbard is the top U.S. working-level representative at nuclear talks with North Korea. South Korea's National Security Law prohibits any unauthorized contact with North Korea. "We don't believe that Hubbard's statement reflects the formal U.S. position, but we are looking into what the exact quotes were," said the ministry official.
