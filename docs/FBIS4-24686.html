BFN [Text] Tianjin, June 1 (XINHUA) -- Foreign-funded enterprises are taking an increasingly important role in the economy of this north China port city, according to the foreign-funded enterprises list for 1993 released by the Tianjin Statistics Bureau Tuesday [31 May]. According to the list, there were 56 foreign-funded enterprises last year with sales value of over 50 million yuan (about 5.7 million U.S. dollars) each. Their total sales value reached 11 billion yuan (about 1.26 billion U.S. dollars). A Sino-American joint venture, the Tianjin Otis Elevator Co. Ltd, is at the top of the list, with over one billion U.S. dollars-worth of annual sales. Many of the listed enterprises are world-famous transnational companies like America's Motorola, Philip Morris and Coca-Cola, Japan's NEC, Honda and Yamaha, France's Hennessy and Italy's Zanussi. The products of these foreign-funded enterprises vary from food, machinery and medicines to clothes, with most of them becoming hot sellers in Mainland China. In the first four months of this year foreign-funded enterprises in Tianjin continued to boom, with sales income increasing 1.17 times over the same period of last year. So far, the number of contracted foreign-funded enterprises has reached 7,000.
