Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 9 Mar (MENA)--Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat has emphasized that he refuses to meet with any Israeli official under the present circumstances and until the consequences of the massacre at the al-Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron in the occupied West Bank are removed. In a statement published in today's issue of the opposition weekly magazine AL-AHALI, 'Arafat said several attempts have been made to hold a meeting between him and Israeli officials, a lot of pressure has been applied, and international forces have interfered in arranging such a meeting, but he refuses until Israel takes specific and practical measures that show a real desire to safeguard the security of the Palestinian people, who are under occupation. 'Arafat stressed the PLO's adherence to a peaceful solution and its commitment to the agreements reached so far. He also referred to the continuing coordination and consultations with Egypt on the peace process in the Middle East.
