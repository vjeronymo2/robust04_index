BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature: by correspondents Dmitriy Voskoboinikov, Igor Porshnev, and Vitaliy Trubetskoy] [Text] Meditkan Sherinkulov, head of the CIS Karabakh peace group and chairman of the Kyrgyz Supreme Soviet who had met with Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev Wednesday, told Interfax that the next meeting of parliamentarians representing the warring parties would be held in Bishkek. He also said that he would go on a tour of Baku, Yerevan and Stepanakert Thursday to secure an agreement on the date to hold the meeting. Sherinkulov expressed the hope that a candid exchange of views would take place in Bishkek. "People are tired of death, of continuous flows of refugees and parliamentarians look for points of contact," he said. The Russian Foreign Ministry supports the desire of the CIS inter-parliamentary group to contribute to peace in Karabakh. Galina Sidorova, a political adviser of Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, who had met with head of the CIS Karabakh peace group and chairman of the Kyrgyz Supreme Soviet, told Interfax that the ministry maintained close contacts with the group and was willing to help it with expert advice. "We do our best as an executive body. But parliaments are in a position to play quite a different role in resolving the conflict by creating an appropriate environment for acceptance of what the executive branch has prepared," she said.
