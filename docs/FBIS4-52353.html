BFN [Text] Australia's foreign minister, Senator Gareth Evans, has called on Asia Pacific region countries to put pressure on North Korea to stop its nuclear weapons program. Speaking at the ninth Pacific Basin Nuclear Conference in Sydney, Senator Evans said the situation in North Korea was worrying, but not at crisis point. He said a nuclear-armed North Korea would undermine confidence in the Nuclear Nonproliferation Treaty [NPT] and present a serious security threat to the region. [Begin Evans recording] For the North Koreans, the choice is really a pretty straightforward and simple one -- to remain in the NPT and accept the disciplines of the treaty, including full IAEA [International Atomic Energy Agency] safeguards, or, alternatively, it can continue with its nuclear activities in violation of its treaty obligations and face not only almost universal international censure, but, in time, a package of severe and damaging sanctions. [end recording] The five-day Sydney conference will discuss the latest developments in nuclear technology and the region's demand for nuclear energy over the next 50 years.
