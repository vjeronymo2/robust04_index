Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The First Congress of Russia's Unity and Concord Party [PRES] opened in Moscow on Saturday. Taking part are 220 delegates from 66 regions of Russia. They intend to discuss the draft of a party program and the results of the elections to the Federal Assembly, amend the rules and elect the steering bodies, and work out the party's strategy and tactics for local elections. In his speech on the party program, its leader Sergey Shakhray declared that his party is "a national conservative organization, respecting such traditional values as state, family, property, and morality." In his words, the party aims at keeping Russia as a state by recognizing the economic, political, and cultural diversity of its regions. Shakhray proposed endorsing only "the framework of the program" at this stage and to finalize it at the 2d Congress, planned for the spring this year. Before that the program could be explained in the party's regional branches. As a delegate has revealed to Interfax, the money for the forum have come "from the party fund and from the commercial structures, sharing the party's goals and tasks."
