Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Akimov] [Text] Almaty, 17 March TASS -- Kazakhstan has summed up final results of its parliamentary elections held on March [as received]. The central electoral commission has announced that 73.52 [as received] of all eligible voters took part in the polls, electing 176 people (154 men and 22 women) to sit in the parliament. As concerns ethnic backgrounds, the new parliamentarians consist of 105 Kazakhs, 49 Russians, 10 Ukrainians, three Germans, three Jews, one Uzbek, one Tatar, one Korean, one Pole, and one Uigur. Forty-two people were elected from the state list, 75 were nominated by public associations and 59 were self-nominated. According to independent analysts, at least 60 per cent of the newly-elected parliamentarians are reliable supporters of Nursultan Nazarbayev, and they will therefore be able to control the majority in line with Nazarbyaev's wishes.
