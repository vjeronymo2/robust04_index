920421 FT 21 APR 92 / International Company News: Two US banks advance CHASE Manhattan, the large New York banking group, and NationsBank, the southern-based regional giant, yesterday underscored the improving health of the US banking sector with sharp improvements in first-quarter net income. Chase recorded a 21 per cent increase, despite higher provisions for credit losses, while NationsBank, recently formed from a merger between North Carolina-based NCNB and C&S/Sovran, reported a near doubling of income. NationsBank, reporting figures for the merged bank for the first time, produced net income of Dollars 310m, or Dollars 1.28 a share, compared with Dollars 159m, or 70 cents, a year ago. Chase reported net income of Dollars 141m, or 81 cents a share, compared with Dollars 117m, or 73 cents a share, in the same period last year. Chase's capital ratios also improved, with the Tier One figure rising to 5.7 per cent from 4.5 per cent a year ago and the total capital ratio rising to 10.2 per cent from 8.7 per cent.
