Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Nairobi, 23 March (SUNA) -- The government delegation and the two rebel factions participating in the Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development [IGADD] committee's negotiations on the Sudanese dispute have signed a document concerning their agenda. Mr. Muhammad al-Amin Khalifah, the speaker for the Transitional National Assembly and the leader of the government delegation, signed on behalf of the government; Salva Kiir [mayar dit] signed on behalf of the main faction -- Garang's Group; and Richard (Mola) signed on behalf of the unified group in the presence of the foreign ministers of Kenya, Ethiopia, and Eritrea and a representative of the Ugandan foreign minister. A report by SUNA from Nairobi said the agenda of the negotiations comprised the opening guidelines as read out by Kenyan President Daniel arap Moi in the first procedural session of the talks. It also comprised the principles that constitute the basis for solving the dispute in Sudan. The agenda also contained transitional procedures and steps to be taken to apply the principles agreed upon for solving the dispute in the south.
