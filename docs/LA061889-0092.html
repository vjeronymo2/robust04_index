June 18, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

TRAVEL PROGRAMS

Australia, New Zealand and Hawaii will be the focus of a three-part travelogue to be presented by E. Ross Harrington on consecutive Wednesday evenings starting this week. Sponsored by the Torrance Adult School, the lecture and slide presentation lasts from 7 to 9 p.m. on Wednesday, then continues June 28 and July 5. It will be given in Room 7 of the Jefferson Adult Center, 21717 Talisman St., Torrance. Admission is $8.

-- -- --

Alaska is the subject of travel photographer Mort Loveman's free lecture Wednesday at 1 p.m. at the Roxbury Park club house, 471 S. Roxbury Drive, Beverly Hills. For more information, call (213) 550-4761.

-- -- --

Guatemala and Mexico is Charles W. Noah's topic Thursday at 1 p.m. when he presents his weekly travel lecture at the Burton Chace Park community building on Mindanao Way in Marina del Rey. Admission is free. For more information, call (213) 306-8111 after 1 p.m.

-- -- --

Australia is the subject of a film being sponsored by the Travel Companion Network on Saturday at 7 p.m. at Tiny Naylor's Restaurant, 4670 Lincoln Blvd., Marina del Rey. Cost of the show is $6 for TCN members and $10 for nonmembers. For more information, contact Patti Zee at (213) 641-0218.
