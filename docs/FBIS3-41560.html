Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Fukuoka, Nov 23 KYODO -- Representatives from 16 prefectures facing the Sea of Japan proposed Tuesday [23 November] holding a governors' summit in 1994 to discuss preservation of the environment and establishment of a social infrastructure in the region. The proposal was made in a statement issued Tuesday at the end of a forum sponsored by the Social Democratic Party (SDP), the largest of seven parties in the ruling coalition. The statement also called on the government, local autonomous bodies and universities to undertake studies to safeguard the environment and promote management of fishery resources. The SDP hopes to convene the sea of Japan governors' summit around next June, a party official said. The party has already gained basic endorsement for the summit plan, the official said. The governors of Fukuoka and Hokkaido, the largest of the 16 prefectures facing the Sea of Japan, have both won elections under the SDP's backing. During the two-day forum attended by some 220 people, academics, prefectural and municipal officials, international exchange experts, and fishery representatives discussed a wide range of themes concerning regional exchanges and development. The Sea of Japan rim prefectures range from Hokkaido in the north to Fukuoka, Saga and Nagasaki Prefectures in the south. Development in those prefectures, particularly on Honshu island, is far behind that of prefectures facing the Pacific which include Japan's three biggest business centers of Tokyo, Osaka and Nagoya.
