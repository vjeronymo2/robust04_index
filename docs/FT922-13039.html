920414 FT 14 APR 92 / The City Bombing: Capel ready for service by 7am - James Capel builds dealing room in 48 hours STAFF at James Capel, the securities house, worked through the weekend to construct a replacement dealing room after surveying damage to its building from the IRA's bomb, Andrew Jack writes. Telephones, computers and furniture were installed in spare premises in Devonshire Square to provide a full dealing service by 7am yesterday. James Capel said: 'It was a Herculean task but we built a whole separate dealing room on the chance we would not be allowed back into our premises.' Twenty staff assessed damage at the Bevis Marks site on Friday evening, and up to 100 people were involved around the clock at the weekend. The Stock Exchange loaned 35 Topic screens carrying its share-dealing information service, while Reuters provided trading screens and Midland Bank offered office equipment, including fax machines, desks and chairs. Capel directors met at Sunday lunchtime to decide which departments should be reduced in number and which staff would be essential to permit trading at normal levels. A team of staff called all Capel's 1,000 employees on Sunday night with instructions on where to attend in the morning. About 200 were told to remain at home. The settlement department moved to the Devonshire Square site, but many staff were allowed to go back to work in Bevis Marks. Mr Peter Letley, finance director, said: 'Both buildings are running smoothly.'
