Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucaramanga (Colombia), 7 Feb (EFE) -- Three people were injured today in one of four bomb attacks against four political party offices in cities in Colombia's Santander Department (in the northeast part of the country), including the department's capital of Bucaramanga, according to authorities. [see Selected Video Reports section for TV footage of incidents] Police report the explosive devices were activated by urban commando units of communist guerrillas from the National Liberation Army (ELN), which operates in Bucaramanga and the nearby towns of Giron, Floridablanca, and Piedecuesta. According to one source, three people in Giron were treated at a hospital for minor injuries they incurred in one of the blasts. The offices that were attacked belonged to Aquiles Tores Breton, candidate for mayor of Bucaramanga; conservative candidate Jorge Humberto Mantilla; a civic movement in Giron; and Consuelo Duran de Mustafa in Piedecuesta. In December, other Santander political party offices belonging to candidates running in the 13 March congressional and municipal elections were also targets of similar bomb attacks in actions attributed to the ELN guerrillas.
