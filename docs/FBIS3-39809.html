Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan has said at the Geneva-based UN Human Rights Commission that the international human rights groups have condemned India for systematic and blatant human rights violations in occupied Kashmir. Pakistan's delegate Shafqat Kakakhel told the Commission that human rights groups and agencies have charged the Indian occupation forces with cold blooded murders, unjustified arrests, killing in custody, molestation of Kashmiri women, and acts of arson. In response to a statement by the Indian delegate, he said the Commission should send a fact-finding team to occupied Jammu and Kashmir to study the exact state of human rights violations there. He said this is the only way the international community can know and try to end atrocities being committed by the Indian forces in the Indian-held valley.
