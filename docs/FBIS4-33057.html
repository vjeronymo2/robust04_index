BFN [Text] Brussels, 31 May (NOTIMEX) -- Cuba has sent to the Belgian authorities a written commitment to respect the human rights of the 113 Cubans who invaded that country's diplomatic headquarters in Havana. The two parties agreed that there should be a peaceful evacuation. The Belgian Foreign Ministry, through its spokesman, told NOTIMEX that it had received a message from Cuban authorities committing themselves to not persecuting asylum seekers after they leave the embassy. Belgium made it clear it had no intention of receiving [recibir] the Cubans and called for a "swift and humane" solution to the problem that would restore tranquillity to the diplomatic headquarters in Havana, which is made up of five officials. One of the 114 refugees voluntarily left the Belgian diplomatic headquarters on Sunday evening and the police did not ask him to produce identification, the Spanish newspaper EL PAIS reported today. The spokesman said Belgium will continue giving humanitarian aid to the asylum seekers, including 34 women and 24 children. Belgian authorities sent additional drinks and food to the Cuban refugees in the embassy.
