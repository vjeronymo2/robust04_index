920130 FT 30 JAN 92 / Observer: In the pink From meat-purveyor to meet-promoter. Having retired from running the Dewhurst butchers' shops in the much indebted Vestey empire, Colin Cullimore has saddled himself with the contentious role of director of the newly formed Campaign for Hunting. One of his schemes to whip up support is to show that the sport is not just an upper-class preserve. Research has found that toffs make up only 17 per cent of hunt-followers, he says.
