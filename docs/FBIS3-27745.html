Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA Jan. 31 -- A delegation of the General Electric (GE) group of the United States led by its President Mr. John Trani in charge of medical equipment, and Vice President Donald M. Kusza, in charge of energy system, today called on Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. The Vietnamese leader welcomed the initial results of cooperation between Vietnam and GE in the field of healthcare. He informed the US businessmen of the development strategy in health-care and energy in the coming years and expressed his hope that GE, with its strength and as one of the leading US groups in cooperation with Vietnam, will expand its cooperation with and assistance to Vietnam, first of all in health care and energy. GE President John Trani, for his part, introduced the prime minister the group's plan on cooperation in Vietnam, and expressed his hope that in 5 to 10 years to come Vietnam will be one of the big markets of GE. The president affirmed that the US Government's embargo against Vietnam does not effect the group's assistance to Vietnam in healthcare field because it is a humanitarian issue. In the immediate time, GE will help Vietnam work out a number of healthcare development programmes, and build two major healthcare centres in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. As for energy field, Mr. Donald M. Kusza stated that GE will supply Vietnam with high-technology generators, and consultant service in electric energy and modernization of electric distribution system throughout the country. GE was first presented in Vietnam in 1992 with two representative offices in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.
