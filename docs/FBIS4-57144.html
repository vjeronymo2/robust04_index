BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities monitored through 12 May: The National Statistics Institute, INE, has reported that the average income of Montevideo households increased 11 percent in real terms in the first quarter of 1994 compared with the corresponding period last year. It totals 5,215 pesos ($1,144) free of rent. The ceiling for the lower income bracket was 2,713 pesos ($595) while the floor for the higher income bracket was 8,515 ($1,868). The INE further reported that salaries in the private sector grew 1.8 percent in real terms in March while those in the public sector declined 1.6 percent in the same period. The average wage index grew 9.9 percent in the first quarter, representing a 1.9 percent increase in real terms. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 28 Apr 94 p 10) The Central Bank disclosed on 29 April that wholesale prices rose 1 percent in April, which represents a 32.1 percent increase over the last 12 months. The upward trend of the wholesale price index continued in the first quarter of the year, increasing 8.2 percent compared with 7.4 percent in the corresponding period last year. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 2 May 94 p 9)
