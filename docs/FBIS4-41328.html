BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Boris Krivoshey] [Text] Moscow June 23 TASS -- The despatch of a multi-national force under French command does not fully conform to the "classic" criterion of U.N. peacemaking, but under the conditions of continued tragedy there it is a necessary step for stabilizing the humanitarian situation in that country, Grigoriy Karasin, director of the Information and Press Department in the Russian Foreign Ministry, said at a briefing today. "Russia had supported this U.N. Security Council resolution, regarding that such a decision is an emergency, but a necessary measure under the present circumstances. We proceeded from the assumption that the unprecedented human tragedy in Rwanda, and the continued massacre of the civilian population in that long- suffering country dictates the need to take swift and adequate measures capable of stopping the further bloodshed in Rwanda. Besides this, we took into consideration the condition that sufficiently long period of time will be required for a full deployment of any additional U.N. force," Karasin said.
