BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 6 KYODO -- Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata pledged Monday [6 Jun] to root out corruption related to public construction works by replacing the traditional designated bidding system with an open bidding system. "Public construction works must have trustworthiness and transparency," said Hata, answering questions at the House of Representatives Budget Committee. "Successive scandals were regrettable." "We are taking measures such as a full-fledged introduction of the competitive bidding system. We will constantly carry out the measures in order to get rid of bid-rigging and other unjust practices," he said. In April, prosecutors announced the end of 13 months of investigations into a series of bribery scandals involving politicians and major construction firms. A total of 32 people -- including a Diet legislator, two governors, two mayors and officials of eight major construction companies and a large paper manufacturer -- were indicted as a result of the investigation.
