921130 FT 30 NOV 92 / The Kuwait Investment Office: Spanish steps November 1984: KIO buys stake in packing company Inpacsa for Dollars 30m. August 1986: KIO takes control of the paper maker Torras Hostench, which buys Inpacsa. June 1987: Torras Hostench makes Spain's biggest rights issue - Dollars 500m. March through June 1987: KIO buys stakes in the chemicals group Explosivos Rio Tinto (ERT, later to become Ercros), Banco Vizcaya and the foods group Ebro. Stakes are bought by external accounts and are transferred, at profit, to Torras. Late 1987: KIO/Torras launch hostile bid for ERT and win. March 1988: KIO/Torras launch hostile bid for Ebro and win. June 1988: Torras Hostench becomes Grupo Torras, a quoted holding company. It acquires bank debt in order to pay for acquisitions. December 1988: Another capital increase - Dollars 400m - to help finance debt, is not successful. Torras accumulates its own stock financed with bank loan. December 1989: KIO decides to take Grupo Torras private. Torras shareholders are offered a mix of cash and Prima shares. New Torras loans taken out to buy up Prima stock that flows back to the market. These shares parked with associates. Early 1990: Efforts begin to try to sell assets. August 1990: Iraqi annexation of Kuwait. The value of collateral of Torras loans falls and the banks call in debt. Torras asks the KIO for Dollars 400m and, later, another Dollars 1.3bn. June 1991: These transfers converted into equity. Banks begin demanding KIO guarantees for credit to Torras. December 1991: Peat Marwick values Torras assets between Dollars 2.9bn and Dollars 4.4bn. February 1992: KIO chairman retires, replaced by al-Bader. May 1992: De la Rosa leaves Torras, and is replaced by new Kuwaiti team. June to November 1992: New KIO/Torras team attacks former KIO leaders and De la Rosa stewardship of Spanish assets. Ercros goes into receivership. Prima funding stopped. Torras says debts are Dollars 3bn.
