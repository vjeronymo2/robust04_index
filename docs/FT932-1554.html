930623 FT 23 JUN 93 / Observer: Poetic licence Odd how the owners of some of the country's ugliest buildings see themselves as stewards of the nation's artistic heritage. For one, the UK's biggest industrial landlord, Slough Estates, is running a poetry competition about the aesthetic merits of Slough to redress the injury inflicted on the town by Betjeman's 'disgraceful' poem. For another, British Land - owner of a motley collection of supermarkets, offices, and trading estates - bedecks its latest annual report with Ben Nicholson pictures and purple prose. Drawing a parallel between Nicholson's avant-garde work and the company's portfolio, the document intones: 'through the work that illustrates this report we sense his concern for observation and discernment, his feeling of balance and proportion - qualities that British Land regards highly in its own portfolio.'
