BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondents Lyudmila Aleksandrova, Nikita Yermakov, Yuriy Sizov, and Vladimir Shekhovtsov] [Text] Madrid, 12 Apr (ITAR-TASS) -- The situation in Bosnia is now slightly quieter, according to Russian President Boris Yeltsin. He spoke briefly to journalists at Madrid city hall today, when asked to give his assessment of the situation following the NATO air strikes against Bosnian Serb positions. "The shooting has stopped, so let us hope that this will not, after all, lead to greater confrontation and greater bloodshed," the president continued. "This is our hope. Russia will act accordingly." He disclosed that consultations will be held with UN Secretary- General Butrus Butrus-Ghali and with U.S. President Bill Clinton, "so that this does not happen again."
