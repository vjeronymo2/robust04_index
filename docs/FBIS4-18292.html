BFN [Article signed JWI: "European Union Calls On Turkey To Respect Human Rights"] [Text] Brussels, 31 Mar (BELGA) -- Following the arrest last week in Turkey of six Turkish deputies of Kurdish origin and the lifting of their immunity, the European Union (EU) states, in a press release published in Brussels, "that the right to freely express your ideas is a fundamental liberty for all, a fortiori for members of Parliament." The EU points out that "this right also forms the basis for parliamentary immnunity of representatives elected in democratic states." More generally, the EU expresses in this text its "concern" at the worsening human rights situation in Turkey and states that "the fight against terrorism must be conducted with full respect for the law and human rights." The EU wants to renew its "appeal in favor of a political solution to the problems posed in south-east Turkey" and is earnestly requesting the Turkish Government "to honor its commitments regarding constitutional reforms in the field of human rights."
