910426 FT 26 APR 91 / Korean minister goes over pollution PRESIDENT Roh Tae Woo of South Korea yesterday sacked his environment minister in response to a scandal concerning the widespread pollution of tap water. Mr Huh Nam Hoon was replaced as minister by Mr Kwon Hwi Hyuk. The scandal centres on contamination of the water supply in Taegu, an industrial city in south-east Korea. More than 300 tons of phenol, a toxic acid, were allegedly dumped into a river by Doosan Electro Materials, part of the Doosan Group, a large conglomerates.
