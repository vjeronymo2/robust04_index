BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 14 (KCNA) -- Papers here today voice support for the convocation of a national conference to seek a way for the great unity of the whole nation on the occasion of the forthcoming August 15 liberation day, which was proposed by a joint meeting of the DPRK Government, political parties and public organisations of April 11. They praise it as a patriotic initiative to promote the independent and peaceful reunification of the country. In a commentary titled "Patriotic Initiative for Great National Unity" NODONG SINMUN declares it is an urgent matter to achieve great national unity through the implementation of the ten-point programme of the great unity of the whole nation in view of the grave situation caused by outside forces and their zealous followers, the flunkeyist traitors. The news analyst says: If our nation fails to achieve harmony and unity under the acute and strained situation, it will unavoidably suffer the disaster of a nuclear war and fall a victim to outside forces. When a historic national conference is convened as proposed by the North, the convocation itself will mean a big stride toward reconciliation and unity and it will be possible to attain national unity and find a way of achieving peace in the country and reunifying the country in the 1990s through unconventional and open- hearted exchange of views. If the conference is to be convened, it is important to create a climate and conditions for it. The South Korean authorities must renounce the policy of depending on outside forces, take the position of national independence and stop all sorts of confrontation and war moves aimed at hurting and attacking fellow countrymen with the help of outside forces. They must scrap the "National Security Law" and other fascist laws and give up such things as "unified channel of dialogue" intended to monopolize dialogue. What position one takes toward the national conference, which would be an epoch-making event in the history of national reunification, is a criterion distinguishing whether one wants national unity and reunification, or not. An analyst of MINJU CHOSON emphasizes that if the South Korean authorities want the independent and peaceful reunification of the country, they must positively respond to the proposal for the convocation of a national conference.
