941018 FT 18 OCT 94 / Arts: Today's Television Does it matter that Takin' Over The Asylum tacitly conveys the message that most of the people in mental hospitals are rather charming eccentrics who are kept inside largely because other people are intolerant of their quirky little habits? Given the misery caused in some homes by the presence of those with mental illnesses it seems unfortunate to say the least, yet last week's episode was undeniably an entertaining piece of drama (9.00 BBC2). This week's drama in the C4 series Alan Bleasdale Presents is Jim Morris's play 'Blood On The Dole' which was first performed at the Liverpool Playhouse in 1981: 'Four Merseyside teenagers struggle to forge their own identities in an unsympathetic world blighted by unemployment and social deprivation' (10.00). In the week when the first volume of Doris Lessing's autobiography is published, she talks on Omnibus about her childhood in Rhodesia and her move to England (BBC 1). We have heard a lot recently about the assassination of Nicholas and Alexandra and their family in Russia in 1918 and we are not finished yet: Network First (10.40 ITV).
