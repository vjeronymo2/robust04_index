BFN [Excerpts] Some 6,000 Palestinian prisoners serving sentences in Israeli prisons and IDF [Israel Defense Forces] lock-up facilities have declared a hunger strike starting tomorrow and until further notice to protest the fact that they are not being released from jail. The leaders of the prisoners called on the inhabitants of the territories to boycott the celebrations for Yasir 'Arafat's arrival and to hoist black flags on the day of his visit in solidarity with the prisoners. Our correspondent Moti Amir reports: [Begin Amir recording] The Palestinian prisoners decided to overtly fight the Israeli decision not to release them from jail and to protest the way Palestinian leaders have been dealing with their case. The leaders of the prisoners announced through the Palestinian Prisoner Club in Bethlehem that all the prisoners will go on a hunger strike tomorrow and called on the inhabitants of the territories to help them in their struggle. 'Isa Karaki, head of the club, said the relatives of the prisoners will stage a demonstration outside the gates of the al-Aqsa camp in Jericho this coming Friday in solidarity with the prisoners. He added that the prisoners, including Fatah members, called on the inhabitants of the territories not to take part in the celebrations the day of 'Arafat's visit to Jericho, which may take place on 2 July. [passage omitted] It should be noted that at least 1,300 Palestinian prisoners were given the choice to get out of prison by virtue of the Gaza-Jericho agreement, but they refused to sign a declaration that they recognize the agreement and undertake not to use violence against the peace process. [end recording]
