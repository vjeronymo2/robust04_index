BFN [Text] Warsaw, April 11 -- Presidential press spokesman Andrzej Drzycimski said Monday that President Lech Walesa would be willing to meet representatives of the government coalition if a new candidate for the post of finance minister was put forward. He added that the coalition should look at matters anew and consider a new approach to the problem of who is to replace Marek Borowski as finance minister. President Walesa has already refused to accept the candidacy of Dariusz Rosati from Premier Waldemar Pawlak. Asked whether Walesa had finally decided whether or not to sign the budget law, Drzycimski replied, "The president still has time for this under the Constitution, he is considering the matter." According to the "small" interim Constitution the president signs a law within 30 days after it has been presented to him having been passed by both houses of parliament. According to Andrzej Gliniecki from the legal office at the president's chancellery, the budget law was presented to Walesa on March 28.
