August 21, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

DR. NEIL SOLOMON: SEX AND AGING

Question: I am a newly single woman who is getting very close to her 60th birthday. At a recent party that I attended, I was handed a line that I had not heard since I was in college many years ago.

During a conversation with one of the men there, he remarked that having sexual relations is good for a woman's health. When I told him that he reminded me of a teen-ager I once knew, he insisted he was serious. Is there anything to this?

A: Having sexual relations can help a woman maintain her genital health. It has been reported that among women 60 to 70 years of age, those who engage in sexual relations experience less vaginal atrophy and have better lubrication. It should be apparent, however, that engaging in indiscriminate sexual relationships is not a prescription for good health, genital or otherwise, for either men or women.
