BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 23 April Morning Edition] [Text] Ichiro Ozawa, Shinseito [Japan Renewal Party] secretary general, commented on his cooperation with Liberal Democratic Party [LDP] member Michio Watanabe, former deputy prime minister and foreign minister, in a Fuji Television program on 22 April. Ozawa indicated his intention to continue to seek a cooperative relationship with Watanabe in the future on the condition that Watanabe leaves the LDP. He said: "Allying with the LDP runs counter to our spirit (and our ideas upon forming Shinseito). However, I still hope to act with him if he secedes from the LDP." In criticizing the idea of "forming a coalition between the LDP and the Social Democratic Party of Japan" while excluding Shinseito, which is an idea that once cropped up in the process of establishing the next administration, Ozawa stated, "This concept lacks the right recognition of the times." In warning these parties, Ozawa said: "This is nothing other than a suicidal act. Why do they want to do this?"
