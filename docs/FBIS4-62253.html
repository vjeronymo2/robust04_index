BFN [Text] Creation of the standing legislative Commission for Military Issues, Security, and Protection of Servicemen's Rights became a sensation at yesterday's session of the Crimean Supreme Soviet. A report delivered by Mykhaylo Kukushkin, head of the commission and commander of the Black Sea Fleet Air Force Administration, shows that the commission will be seeking substantial powers. In his words, the deputies should watch the movements of all troops on Crimean territory. All military exercises in the Crimea and visits by military ships to local ports can only be conducted when permitted by the Crimean president. The new commission intends to assume control over the activities of the Crimean Security Service.
