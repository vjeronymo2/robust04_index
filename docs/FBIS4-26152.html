BFN [Text] Hangzhou, June 19 (XINHUA) -- Three men guilty of robbery and murder in the March 31 Qiandao Lake tragedy in Zhejiang Province, east China, were executed here today after the provincial higher court approved the death sentence. More than 1,200 people gathered at the Hangzhou theater this morning to hear the judgement announced by the Intermediate People's Court of Hangzhou. The verdict was read by the intermediate people's court, saying that Wu Lihong, Hu Zhihan and Yu Aijun on March 31 this year robbed and murdered 24 Taiwan tourists and eight mainland crew members on a pleasure boat on Qingdao Lake, a major tourist attraction in Zhejiang Province. It said that the three men robbed the tourists of large quantities of their property by threatening them with violence and then murdered the victims by blowing up the boat and setting fire to it. The three men accused of robbery and murder were sentenced to death and deprived of political rights for life on June 12. They did not appeal to a higher court against the sentence within the legal period. Chinese law grants a three-day period for appeals.
