BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature by correspondents Igor Porshnev, Vitaliy Trubetskoy and others] [All quotation marks as received] [Text] Moscow hopes that the United States and Russia's other western partners will exert their influence on the Bosnian Moslems and Croats in order to reach a ceasefire agreement in Bosnia and Herzegovina, a top-ranking Russian diplomat in charge for the settlement in the former Yugoslavia said in his interview with Interfax. In his words, the Geneva meeting between the special envoy of the UN General Secretary Yasusi Akasi and representatives of the confronting Bosnian parties was fruitless. "Moslems and Croats agree only to a quadripartite ceasefire with its possible prolongation for four weeks. The Bosnian Serbs are ready to cease fire at least for four months," the diplomat indicated. As he said, "fears that the Serbs would try to preserve the status-quo and to fix themselves on 70 percent of Bosnia and Herzegovina stand behind the obstinacy of the Moslems and Croats." "One cannot say that these fears are groundless. The threat of "an ethnic cleansing" in Bosnia and Herzegovina is still preserved and all confronting parties are the source of this threat, noted Interfax interlocutor.
