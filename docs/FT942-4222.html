940610 FT 10 JUN 94 / International Company News: French groups join global satellite telephone system The French telecommunications groups Alcatel Alsthom and France Telecom will join Globalstar, a global satellite-based mobile telephone system formed by US groups Loral and Qualcomm, AP-DJ reports from Paris. Loral said France Telecom would own a 51 per cent interest in a joint venture with Alcatel, which will hold 49 per cent. Total investment in the joint venture, called Tesam, is Dollars 37.5m, and represents about 8.3 per cent of the ownership of Globalstar. Tesam will be an owner and service provider of Globalstar in France and 'a number of other territories', Loral said. Loral said Globalstar would begin service in 1998, and is expected to have revenues of Dollars 1.6bn and 2.7m subscribers by the year 2002.
