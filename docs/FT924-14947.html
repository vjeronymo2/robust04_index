921007 FT 07 OCT 92 / Fall in new car sales continues with 4% drop THE UK car industry plunged deeper into gloom yesterday as statistics for September showed sales of new cars continuing to slide. Official figures from the Society of Motor Manufacturers and Traders showed sales down 4 per cent last month compared with a year ago to 121,823 from 126,906. But many yesterday acknowledged that the underlying situation was considerably worse. The market was down by nearly 20 per cent about two thirds of the way through the month, and the last-minute upsurge is believed to represent dealer registrations by manufacturers seeking to boost market share. Total registrations for the first three quarters are 1,297,423 - 2.62 per cent below last year. The resumed fall has left manufacturers seeking further ways of tempting buyers. Ford this week began a price-cutting campaign on 1992 models. Manufacturers and dealers are wondering whether even market forecasts of 1.55m units this year - down from forecasts of 1.7m or higher made in January - might not prove optimistic. In spite of economic uncertainty and political turmoil, some industry leaders were putting on a brave face. Mr John Towers, managing director of Rover Group, said at the Paris motor show that sales could recover to 1.65m or even 1.7m units next year. He said: 'Once we break through the consumer confidence barrier, then quite clearly there could be a lot of movement in the market.' Rover was one of the winners last month. It secured a 15.13 per cent market share, up 4 percentage points on last year, and for the first time in many months narrowly beat Vauxhall into second place behind Ford.
