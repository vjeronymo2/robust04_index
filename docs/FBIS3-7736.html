Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Slovak Foreign Minister Jozef Moravcik gave a news conference late this morning, probably his last as foreign minister. Journalists' questions concentrated mostly on the link between the internal political situation in Slovakia and Slovakia's international standing. [passage omitted] [Begin Moravcik recording] Foreign countries have been monitoring the situation in our country very carefully. They have drawn one conclusion from our internal situation: that there is a trend here to implement certain elements of an authoritarian regime. This is one negative factor. The second factor is the fact that statements by some politicians--above all, by the prime minister--are frequently ambiguous. This makes our internal politics rather incomprehensible, and this, of course, influences the conclusions drawn by foreign countries as regards their attitude toward our country. [end recording] [passage omitted]
