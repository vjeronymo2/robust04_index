BFN [Text] More than 5,000 men and women are reported to have taken part in a memorial rally that was held on Saturday [2 April] some 150 meters off the building that used to house Russia's dissolved parliament and is currently housing the Russian Government, in Moscow. A number of opposition parties and movements, including the Russian Communist Workers' party, the Working Russia movement, the Army Officers' Union and the Women for the USSR movement, held the rally to pay tribute to the memory of those who were killed on October 3 and 4 in armed clashes in Moscow. The rally declared the October butchers should not expect forgiveness. A member of the standing presidency of the Congress of Soviet People's Deputies Sazhi Umalatova, Komsomol leader Igor Maliarov and the leader of the Liberal-Socialist party Valeriy Skurlatov attended the event. A list of the fallen was read out loud and wreaths were laid to a symbolic grave at Moscow's White House. INTERFAX reporters heard no speaker make anti-semitic slurs although many of those who attended the rally talked about the need to purge the country of free masons and Jews". Graffiti on a wall near the White House said Jews were a criminal organization, and not an ethnic minority.
