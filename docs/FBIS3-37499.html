Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 17 Feb (ITAR-TASS) -- Russia's President B.N. Yeltsin today sent a message to S. Milosevic, president of the Republic of Serbia, and which is also addressed to R. Karadzic, other Serbian leaders, and the Serbian people. The message contains Russia's request to the Serbs to withdraw their heavy weapons from Sarajevo in as short a time as possible in the interests of stopping the bloodshed, and expressing Russia's readiness to deploy its contingent in Sarajevo within the framework of the UN peacekeeping operation. Yeltsin's message was handed over to S. Milosevic in Belgrade and to the Bosnian Serbs' leaders in Pale by Vitaliy Churkin, the Russian president's special envoy and Russian deputy foreign minister. The Serbian leaders have accepted the Russian Federation's initiative with gratitude. R. Karadzic stated at a news press conference in Pale that the Bosnian Serbs will withdraw their heavy weapons within the next few days. At the same time, they count on the Muslim troops also halting all military actions in Sarajevo, and that the UNPROFOR [UN Protection Force] will also keep their side of the bargain. The Russian and Serbian sides expressed mutual hope that by solving the Sarajevo crisis they will help to end the war and to achieve a political settlement in Bosnia-Herzegovina.
