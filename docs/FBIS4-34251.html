BFN [Text] The first group of Haitians to be processed off the coast of Jamaica arrived in Jamaican waters this morning. The Haitian boat people were picked up at sea by a U.S. Coast Guard vessel. It is expected that these Haitians will be taken on board the processing vessel the Comfort off the coast of Port Royal soon. The opposition Jamaica Labor Party [JLP] yesterday asked a number of questions about the memorandum of understanding between the Government of Jamaica and the United States Government establishing a facility to process Haitian refugees within the Jamaican territorial waters. JLP chairman (Booth Golding) asked whether Haitian nationals found at sea outside of the United States waters will be protected by the U.S. authorities after ascertaining whether they are in Haitian refugee status. He also asked what international law or convention governs the [words indistinct]. In a message to the Minister of Foreign Affairs and Trade Dr. Paul Robertson, Mr. (Golding) raises 13 qusetions on the memorandum of understanding. He asks what is the legal basis for the compulsory repatriation of Haitian nationals by United States authorities when the port of exit Jamaica. The JLP chairman also asked what role the Jamaican authorities have in the event of death or injury to any Haitian on board a processing facility. On the question of funding, Mr. (Golding) asked what recourse is available to the Jamaican Government in the event that the United States Government is not able to reimburse the Jamaican Government for the directly attributable costs incurred in this operation.
