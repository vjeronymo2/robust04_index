921113 FT 13 NOV 92 / Observer: Set point A German diplomat describes how, in a conversation with one of his French opposite numbers, he recently queried France's tactics over the Gatt negotiations - where Gallic intransigence has been stiffened by the Socialist government's worries about the March general elections. 'Why are you so worried about the elections?' the German asked. 'You've lost them anyway.' To which came the reply, with appropriate hauteur: 'France never loses elections.'
