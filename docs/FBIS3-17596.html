Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Gennadiy Zyuganov, leader of the Communist Party of the Russian Federation, speaking on Sunday, denied rumors that he had received an offer to become a member of the Presidential Council. INTERFAX recalls that reports to this effect have appeared in several Russian mass media. They claimed, in particular, that there are plans to reform the Presidential Council in the near future by also including in it Gennadiy Burbulis, Yegor Gaydar, Ivan Rybkin, Mikhail Lapshin, and Valeriy Zorkin. Interviewed by the agency, Gennadiy Zyuganov described these rumors as an attempt to draw into talks those who oppose the elimination of normal systems of government and the introduction of a dictatorship. In his view, its introduction is being prepared in the country.
