920717 FT 17 JUL 92 / International Company News: Colgate profits surge 33% to Dollars 124m COLGATE-Palmolive, the US consumer products group, yesterday unveiled a 33 per cent improvement in second-quarter net earnings, on sales which rose 19 per cent. Net income was Dollars 124.4m in the three months to June 30, on sales of Dollars 1.8bn, compared with earnings of Dollars 93.3m on sales of Dollars 3.4bn the previous year. Earnings per share rose 14 per cent to 75 cents from 66 cents. There were more shares outstanding in the latest quarter. Earnings before interest and taxes advanced 25 per cent in the quarter to Dollars 204.5m. The results were in line with expectations, and shares in Colgate-Palmolive held steady on Wall Street at Dollars 53 1/8 at midday. For the first half of 1992, net income grew 29 per cent to Dollars 238.2m on sales up 14 per cent to Dollars 3.4bn. Earnings per share stood at Dollars 1.49 compared with Dollars 1.31. Earnings before interest and taxes grew 22 per cent to Dollars 396.4m. Mr Reuben Mark, chairman and chief executive, said the company's gross profit margin improved to 47.1 per cent from 45.5 per cent in the 1991 second quarter. He attributed the improvement to a growing proportion of high-margin personal care business and production efficiencies.
