931109 FT 09 NOV 93 / Private flows to Third World Private investment flows to developing countries jumped by a half last year while the rich world's governments reduced financial support for the Third World, according to a report from the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development, Reuter reports from Paris. The net flow of resources to developing countries from the OECD's 22 wealthy member states rose 23 per cent last year to Dollars 165.5bn. Private inflows at Dollars 94bn, 51 per cent more than the year before, outstripped official finance for the first time. Official finance fell to Dollars 68bn from Dollars 70bn in 1991, with aid falling to Dollars 55bn from Dollars 57bn.
