930326 FT 26 MAR 93 / Observer: Cartoon capers A year after Punch folded, The Cartoonist comes into being. Although it is being launched on April 1, the fortnightly broadsheet newspaper is no joke. Backed by the upmarket Coutts, with a handful of small shareholders, it will have an initial print-run of 100,000 and a cover price of Pounds 1.20. But says its publisher, John Sorrell: 'If we achieve a settle-down figure of 40,000, we'll be very happy'. He adds: 'We think it is a perfect time to be launching. In a recession people are desperate for things to smile about.' But what happens when the recession ends?
