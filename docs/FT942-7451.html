940525 FT 25 MAY 94 / International Company News: Earnings forecast from Argus Newspapers Argus Newspapers, the South African press group in which Mr Tony O'Reilly's Independent Newspapers has bought a controlling shareholding, will today release a pre-listing statement showing unaudited group turnover of R706.7m (Dollars 193m) for the year to March and earnings of R29m. Earnings have been adjusted to take account of discontinued operations, such as the Sunday Star, and the sale of a majority interest in The Sowetan, South Africa's biggest selling daily, to a consortium of black investors. The company is forecasting turnover of R795m and earnings of R32.6m for the current year. In a calculation based on 45.1m shares in issue, audited pro forma earnings for the year to March were 64 cents a share. Net asset value was 384 cents a share. Argus Holdings and JCI directors will retire from the Argus Newspapers board prior to the listing and Mr Liam Healy and Mr James Parkinson, managing director and financial director of Independent, will join the board.
