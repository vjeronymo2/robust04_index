BFN [Station commentary] [Text] Vietnam, an active participant in the world trend for peace and cooperation, has attached great importance to friendship and cooperation with neighboring countries and nations in the Asia Pacific. This was stated by Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet at the international conference held in Singapore on opportunities for business in China, Vietnam, and ASEAN. Here is a review of relations between Vietnam and ASEAN. As reported earlier, at the invitation of Singapore Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong, Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet visited Singapore and a trade forum. Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet affirmed that Vietnam was actively preparing to become a member of ASEAN soon. Cooperation between Vietnam and ASEAN member countries has been developing in all fields especially trade. Two-way trade between Vietnam and ASEAN countries has increased annually. At the end of 1993, ASEAN invested in about 100 projects in Vietnam with the capital of hundreds of million of U.S. dollar, 10 times bigger than that of 1990, and accounted for 10 percent of the total capital invested in Vietnam. So far, nearly 30 agreements on cooperation in economy, trade, auditing, credit, aviation, and navigation have been signed between the two sides. Vietnam has also signed a large contract with (Primo) Company of Brunei. Many joint ventures are operating positively. Political ties between Vietnam and ASEAN have also developed. Vietnamese Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet has visited all ASEAN member countries, and ASEAN leaders have visited Vietnam. ASEAN leaders all affirmed their readiness to support and share experience with Vietnam, to help Vietnam develop rapidly.
