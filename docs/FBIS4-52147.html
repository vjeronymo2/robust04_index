BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 9 KYODO -- The Foreign Ministry said Monday [9 May] that it has advised 92 Japanese in Yemen to leave immediately as hostilities continued to grow between rival northern and southern army units. Japan is trying to have the Japanese evacuated on the seagoing vessels or emergency aircraft of other nations, government sources said. On Saturday, the Japanese Embassy in Sanna urged Japanese residents and tourists to evacuate the Yemeni capital. A total of 84 Japanese, including embassy related personnel, are reportedly in Sanna with five Japanese in the old northern city of Mafraq and another three in the old southern capital of Aden.
