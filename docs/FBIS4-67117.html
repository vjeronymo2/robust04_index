BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of fileworthy reports from 28 April-5 May on antinarcotics operations in countries monitored by FBIS London Bureau. Sources are given in parentheses at the end of each report.

France
French custom officers seized 15 kilos of heroin at Roissy airport in two separate seizures on 19 and 26 April. On both occasions the drug was hidden in boxes containing scientific equipment on its way to Africa. (Paris France-Inter Radio Network in French 1200 GMT 3 May 94)
Portugal
Police in Portimao arrested seven people and seized about 270 doses of heroin, a car, household electric appliances, cameras, car radios, hi-fi equipment, watches, money, and gold. The operations followed antinarcotics investigations in Western Algarve last week. The police believe they have broken up two drug trafficking gangs that operated in the region. (Lisbon Radio Renascenca in Portuguese to Europe 1400 GMT 4 May 94)
Sweden
On 2 May, customs officers in Ystad foiled a smuggling attempt by an Iranian arriving from Swinoujscie, Poland carrying 2 kg of pure opium worth some 3 to 4 million Swedish crowns--approximately $370,000 to $500,000. Two days earlier a Polish citizen was arrested in Ystad after customs officers discovered 10 kg of amphetamines hidden in his car. Both smugglers face sentences of at least five years in prison. (Warsaw PAP in English 1909 GMT 2 May 94)
