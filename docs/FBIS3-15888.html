Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] As many as six people from a military group of the Tajik opposition and Afghan Mojahedins were killed during the attempt to break through the Tajik-Afghan border near the Eleventh control post of the Moscow Border Guards' detachment on Thursday. Well informed sources in Dushanbe told Interfax that a military group of about sixty people armed with mortars, hand-held infantry anti-tank rocket launchers and automatic guns, had tried to cross the Pyanj River. A unit of the Tajik Security Ministry resisted the attack. One serviceman of the government's forces was wounded. This was the biggest Islamic military group which tried to break through the Tajik-Afghan border since the beginning of the year. According to military sources, groups of ten to fifteen people try to break through to Tajikistan every day. The situation may significantly deteriorate in April.
