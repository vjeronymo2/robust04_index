Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 13 Feb -- The well-known Russian economist Yevgeniy Saburov has accepted Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov's offer to head the new government of the republic. This was revealed today by the "Itogi" program on NTV [Russian TV channel]. In the recent past Yevgeniy Saburov held the post of economics minister in one of the Russian governments, namely in Ivan Silayev's government. Explaining his decision in an interview on "Itogi", Yevgeniy Saburov said he was born in the Crimea and his mother and his friends still live there. "The Crimea is my fatherland and my pain," he said. Therefore, in the present situation in the Crimea Saburov believes he must help and hopes he can do something. He also noted that he had agreed to cooperate with the new Crimean president, by whom he said he was very much impressed.
