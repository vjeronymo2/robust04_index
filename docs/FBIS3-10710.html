Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil monitored through 10 March. The Management and Economics Science Institute of St. Ursula University reported that Brazil's volume of exports increased 16.2 percent, whereas the value dropped 8.15 percent from 1991 to 1993 compared with the 1988-1990 period. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 3 Mar 94 p 3) The government increased the electricity rate 43.24 percent as of 8 March, the same day the real value unit was implemented. It did not explain, however, the reason for the increase despite having guaranteed that public utilities prices would not be readjusted higher than the inflation rate. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 3 Mar 94 Economic and Business Section p 1) The inflation rate measured by the Economic Research Institute Foundation, FIPE, was 46.25 percent in the past 30 days that ended 4 March. This is the highest rate registered by the FIPE. The Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics reported that the consumer price index rose 40.27 percent between 29 January and 1 March. (Sao Paulo O ESTADO DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 5 Mar 94 p B3) Car manufacturers' production in February totaled 114,700 units. This is 34 percent more than in February 1993 and the largest number ever produced in a single month in the country. (Rio de Janeiro Rede Globo Television in Portuguese 2300 GMT 7 Mar 94) Iron ore exports were 111.9 million metric tons in 1993, an increase of 5.5 percent compared with 1992's 106 million metric tons. Brazil was the third-largest iron ore producer after China and Russia. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 7 March 94 p 16)
