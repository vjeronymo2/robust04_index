Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin, who is on an official visit in New Zealand, has concluded his contacts in Wellington. Yasar Kefeli from the Turkish Radio and Television team in Wellington reports the following: [Begin Kefeli recording] The significant role New Zealand, a UN Security Council member, can play in the solution of regional issues such as Bosnia-Herzegovina and Karabakh was discussed at the talks Cetin held in Wellington. The restructuring of the UN Security Council and how it can become more effective were also discussed at the talks. Cetin was also received by New Zealand Prime Minister Jim Bolger. He also met with Donald Charles McKinnon, New Zealand deputy prime minister and minister of external relations and trade. Later official talks were held between the two delegations headed Cetin and McKinnon. The two ministers held a joint news conference after the talks. Cetin said that bilateral and regional relations were discussed at the talks and that the situation in Middle East, Iraq, the Balkans and the Caucasus were discussed within this framework. Cetin stated that if the tragedy in Bosnia-Herzegovina does not end then similar incidents might occur in other regions in the world as well, adding that the Armenian occupation of part of Azerbaijani territory constitutes such an example. [end recording]
