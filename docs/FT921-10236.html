920207 FT 07 FEB 92 / UK Company News: AmBrit Intl suffers 26% downturn to Pounds 127,000 PRE-TAX profits at AmBrit International, the USM-quoted independent oil and gas company which has recommended a Pounds 7.3m offer from United Energy, fell 26 per cent to Pounds 127,000 in the year to December 31. The company said that the figure last time had benefited from higher oil prices at the time of the Gulf war. However, the company fell into losses of Pounds 293,000 (profits Pounds 164,000) for the year after extraordinary charges of Pounds 420,000 which related to the costs of the defence against Pittencrieff's hostile bid and of the offer from United. Turnover rose to Pounds 2.64m (Pounds 2.53m) and operating profits fell to Pounds 187,000 (Pounds 237,000). Earnings emerged at 0.19p (0.25p) per share. Cashflow from operations increased to Pounds 1.24m (Pounds 1.05m). Gearing fell from 36 per cent to 23 per cent.
