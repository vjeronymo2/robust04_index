BFN [Text] On 19 May, the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] and Shinto Sakigake [New Party Harbinger] decided to form a policy consultation council to discuss their views on tax reform and to hold the council's first meeting on 23 May. The two parties also agreed that tax reform should come as part of drastic administrative reform programs. The parties were in general agreement that they should formulate their own administrative reform plan and that they should not cooperate with the ruling coalition in tax reform in case it is not cooperative enough on their administrative reform plan.
