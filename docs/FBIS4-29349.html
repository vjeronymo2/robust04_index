BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 16 KYODO -- The United Nations will not be able to revise its charter before 1996 to allow Japan to become a permanent member of its security council, former Japanese ambassador to the UN Yoshio Hatano predicted Thursday [16 June]. Hatano also said in a speech that a midterm report on UN reform by a working committee, due this fall, will not detail an increase in the number of UN Security Council members. He said it would be impossible for the 184-nation working panel to produce an agreement.
