930205 FT 05 FEB 93 / What bakers say about Major: How a sector of small business is surviving MR John Major conjured with the image of a 'homely' local bakery to evoke a benign image of his free-market philosophy when he made his Carlton Club speech on Wednesday. But how do bakers see the impact of the prime minister's policies? Mr David Harbourne, director of the National Association of Master Bakers, took Mr Major's comments as a pat on the back for his members' efforts to improve their service. 'We do have the friendly face behind the counter which Mr Major touched on, but we are not doing anywhere near as well as we might,' he says. He pointed out that small bakers have been particularly badly hit by the recession, as well as by Sunday opening by supermarket chains and uniform business rate increases. Mr Patrick Cross, owner of the Holborn Bakery in central London, has been affected by high interest rates and says rent for his premises has doubled in six years while nearby shops stand empty. 'We are in the middle of London and a lot of our customers are office workers,' he explains. 'Many firms have moved out of this area in recent years because of rent and rates and a lot of people have been made redundant, so our customer base has dropped quite drastically.' But for one baker in Bristol, Mr Major's 'workfare' proposal is the real cause for alarm. His independent bakery in the Montpelier area has a high proportion of residents claiming social security benefits. 'As long as they keep on paying so many people for nothing, we will be all right.'
