941212 FT 12 DEC 94 / Observer: Tour guide The suggestion by Valentin Sergeyev, Russia's government spokesman, that the Russian troops advancing into Chechnya were carrying not just guns but butter, pasta and other foodstuffs for poverty-stricken Chechnyans, surely wins 1994's public relations Stakhanovite star for spin-doctoring beyond the call of credibility. It also disinters a cold war joke we imagined buried forever. Poster in a Moscow travel office: 'Visit Russia - before she visits you.'
