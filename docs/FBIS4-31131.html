BFN [Text] Although Yasushi Akashi, (?special) envoy to the UN secretary general, said that Serbian soldiers had withdrawn from their positions around Gorazde, the Geneva negotiations on general cease-fire on the territory of Bosnia-Herzegovina have not resumed. The spokesman of the Zagreb UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] Command has (?however) stated that UNPROFOR representatives registered the withdrawal of Serb aggressor's forces from the western bank of the Drina near Gorazde, but that there are still no reliable data on whether the withdrawal (?has been completed). According to him, a final report should have arrived in the Command at about 1100. It has just been reported at the Sarajevo Peace Forces Command that the commander of the units of the Army of Bosnia-Herzegovina in Gorazde and the local UNPROFOR commander in the town had signed a joint document this morning which stresses that all remaining aggressor's policemen and armed civilians had left the 3-km zone around Gorazde. This announcement was immediately sent to Geneva where political negotiations on Bosnia-Herzegovina are expected to resume. We have not received any new reports from Geneva during [words indistinct]. (Peric Buljubasic), commander of the East-Bosnian Operations Group of the Army of the Republic of Bosnia-Herzegovina, together with the UNPROFOR commander in Gorazde, visited the border lines in the exclusion zone and established that the chetniks had withdrawn from (?Sjenokosi) and [word indistinct] toward [word indistinct]. According to a report by our correspondent in Gorazde, only a few ethnic Serb civilians have been left in the exclusion zone.
