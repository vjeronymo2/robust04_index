BFN [Text] Schwerin (DDP/ADN) -- Germany and Spain have agreed on several concrete steps for expanding bilateral relations and European cooperation. Government spokesman Dieter Vogel said this on Monday [6 June] evening in Schwerin, commenting on the results of German-Spanish consultations. It was agreed at the talks between the two states' heads of government, Helmut Kohl and Felipe Gonzalez, that there will be careful preparation for and bilateral coordination of the meeting of the European Union [EU] council in Corfu in June. There should also be closer cooperation among France, Germany, and Spain regarding the EU presidency. The representatives of the two countries' Foreign Ministries agreed to press for the accession of four states of Central and East Europe to the EU. The representatives of the Interior Ministries agreed, within the framework of the first government consultations to take place in eastern Germany, to develop and pursue a joint asylum and refugee policy within the EU. The work of the European headquarters for criminal investigation (Europol) should also be "pushed forward" and its activities extended to terrorism. Apart from relations between Russia and NATO, the talks between Defence Minister Ruehe and his Spanish counterpart were dominated by Spain's entry into the Eurocorps and the development of a joint European military transport aircraft, the government spokesman said.
