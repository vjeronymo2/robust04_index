BFN [Text] Pakistan and the United Arab Emirates [UAE] have agreed on a wider basis for close cooperation in joint ventures, investment, trade, and the industrial sector. The agreement was reached at the sixth session of the Pakistan-UAE Joint Ministerial Commission, which ended in Abu Dhabi yesterday. The joint commission agreed that steps should be taken to boost cooperation in water, power, agriculture, telecommunications, education, and narcotics control. Pakistani Foreign Minister Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali led the Pakistani delegation at the commission meeting, while the Emirian delegation was led by Shaykh Hamdan Bin-Zayid Al Nuhayyan, minister of state for foreign affairs. The Pakistani foreign minister later had a meeting with Emirian President Zayid Bin-Sultan Al Nuhayyan and exchanged views on bilateral matters and regional issues of mutual interest. The foreign minister briefed the Emirian president on the latest situation in occupied Kashmir.
