941128 FT 28 NOV 94 / Customs 'may lose 4,000 officers' More than 4,000 Customs and Excise jobs will be lost as a result of tomorrow's Budget, the CPSA civil service union said yesterday, Robert Taylor writes. In particular, it predicted that public spending cuts would lead to a loss of up to 600 jobs among Customs officers patrolling the coastline, ports and airports. Mr Geoff Lewtas, the union's national officer for Customs, said that such cuts would make it more difficult to stop the import of drugs in bulk. He said: 'These job cuts will do serious damage.'
