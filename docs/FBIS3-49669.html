Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Yasushi Akashi, the UN Secretary General Butrus Ghali's special envoy and UN Protection Forces [UNPROFOR] chief coordinator, stressed in his talks with the local press that at the moment, UNPROFOR's main problem in Croatia was the stabilization of the cease-fire, following which one can embark on the demilitarization of UN Protected Area zones and the implementation of other tasks within the protection force's mandate. The problem of the return of refugees, in his view, must wait for the materialization of a peace agreement. Akashi stressed that the challenges facing UNPROFOR in Croatia were greater than those in Cambodia where he had worked with a UN peace mission. For, he said, the protection force in Croatia us working both on achieving and keeping peace. UNPROFOR can do more than it has done so far, Akashi said and added that Chapter Six of the UN Charter, under which his mandate in Croatia has taken place, provides sufficient potential, which have not been harnessed yet. In this context, Akashi expects firm cooperation with the Croatian authorities and hopes that some past problems in their relations will be ironed out. Today, as he announced, Akashi will talk to Ambassador Gerd Ahrens, a mediator between the Croatian authorities and UN Protected Areas. The implementation in practice of the economic and communications reintegration of these areas in Croatia as a whole will be the main topic of their talks.
