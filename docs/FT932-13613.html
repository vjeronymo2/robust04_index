930422 FT 22 APR 93 / International Company News: Smith Corona reports sharp fall SMITH Corona, the US maker of portable typewriters which is 48 per cent owned by Britain's Hanson group, yesterday unveiled third-quarter net earnings of Dollars 800,000, or 3 cents a share, down from Dollars 4.9m, or 16 cents, a year ago. In the 1993 quarter, Smith Corona had restructuring charges of Dollars 800,000. Sales in the three months fell to Dollars 76.8m from Dollars 86.5m. Mr Lee Thompson, chairman and chief executive, said total unit sales fell in the third quarter in spite of increased shipments of personal word processors. For the first nine months, Smith Corona recorded a net loss of Dollars 300,000, or 1 cent a share, against net earnings of Dollars 16m, or 53 cents, the previous year. Sales slid to Dollars 244.8m from Dollars 285.4m. The company had restructuring charges of Dollars 10.3m in the 1993 period. The company said it was in discussions on a new revolving credit facility which it expected to be in place before the current agreement expires in October. 'Our balance sheet remains strong and out new agreement will provide us with the requisite working capital financing for the foreseeable future,' Mr Thompson said.
