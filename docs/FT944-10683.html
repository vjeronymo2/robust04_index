941109 FT 09 NOV 94 / World Stock Markets: Canada Toronto bounced back in moderate trading from five straight losing sessions. The TSE 300 index finished 21.12 up at 4,209.09, although advances led declines by just 359 to 357 after volume of 71.6m shares valued at CDollars 71.5m. Eight of the 14 sub-indices gained ground, led by gold, financial services, transportation and pipeline stocks. The heaviest losses came in real estate and paper issues. The paper group dropped 1.68 per cent as its most heavily weighted stock, Macmillan Bloedel, fell CDollars 7/8 to CDollars 17 3/4 in heavy trading. Gold shares were bid up on rising bullion prices as the New York spot price for gold climbed USDollars 1.70 to USDollars 383.15 an ounce. The group advanced 1.89 per cent, helped by gains for American Barrick, adding CDollars 3/4 at CDollars 32 3/4 , and Placer Dome, up CDollars 1/2 at CDollars 28 3/4.
