920121 FT 21 JAN 92 / Zimbabwe riot police called out Zimbabwean riot police were called out yesterday to control hundreds of anxious job seekers hoping to fill positions that opened up when 4,000 striking rail workers were dismissed, AP reports from Harare. There were no reports of injuries. Some 6,000 semi-skilled workers began striking last Tuesday, a work stoppage that has cost the indebted company an estimated Dollars 1m in losses. Only 2,000 strikers heeded an order to return to work by Friday, and Mr Alvord Mabhena, the railway chief, sacked the remaining 4,000. The strikers have demanded increases of up to 13 per cent on monthly salaries and allowances.
