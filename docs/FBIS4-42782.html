BFN [Excerpt] The government and the management of SEAT said today that the Spanish car make will not disappear. According to Juan Manuel Eguiagaray Ucelay, the minister of industry and energy, the Spanish Government is willing to help SEAT, but without disregarding the European Union [EU] rules set down for competition and as long as the work force reduction of 4,500 jobs wanted by Volkswagen is carried out in a nontraumatic way. [Begin Eguigaray recording] The Spanish Government will of course have to contribute what it can; I underline, it will be done according to the rules. The cut in the work force will cost all Spaniards -- through the state's budget -- some 30 billion pesetas, and if we have to add subsidies for technological equipment -- and we are quite willing to do so -- it will cost us more money. [end recording] The government's subsidies are to be conditional on Volkswagen -- the parent company to which SEAT belongs -- fulfilling its promises regarding research, development, and the survival of the make and its distribution and repairs network.
