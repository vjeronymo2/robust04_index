BFN [Text] Kyrgyz Prime Minister Abbas Dzhumagulov called on Japanese businessmen to play a part in reforming his country's economy. When he met with them in Bishkek Monday [20 June], he called on Japanese businessmen to invest in the reconstruction of defense, engineering and food processing industries. Dzhumagulov believed that as little as 7 to 8 pct of the country's rushing water is used to generate electricity and that Japanese participation in construction of small, medium, and large hydro power stations would be profitable to both countries. He also invited them to invest in the construction of a factory manufacturing semi-conductors in the south of Kyrgyzstan. Dzhumagulov emphasized that he pinned great hopes on the Kyrgyz-Japanese Economic Cooperation Commission, which he heads. He will attend its session in Tokyo next November. Dzhumagulov said that the government had set up a reconstruction and development bank which reflects the Japanese way of doing things. The bank will mobilize state resources to finance and credit priority projects and support small and medium business in priority fields.
