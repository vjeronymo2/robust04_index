Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] Reports from New York note that a new team of UN experts will come to Cyprus next week to contribute to the process of the proximity talks. According to our correspondent in New York, Gustave Feissel, the UN secretary general's special representative in Cyprus, has asked for the team. The team which will depart Monday [28 February], comprises one legal adviser and one official on issues of public administration. A third expert from the International Civil Aviation Organization will come to Cyprus later. Our correspondent reports that the duties of the three experts will be to assist Feissel with the technical details of the various aspects of the confidence-building measures. The legal adviser will basically assist with the terminology of the text of agreement. The work of the official in charge of public administration issues is related to the planning of the organizational structure of the UN administration of the fenced area of Famagusta and Nicosia Airport. The third expert's duties are related with the operation of Nicosia International Airport, flight control and other related issues. Our correspondent reports that the experts will not prepare new reports, nor does their presence mean there will be an effort to change the reports prepared by the two teams of experts for Famagusta and Nicosia Airport. Our correspondent also reports that another UN expert, Peter Taylor, will leave for Cyprus Monday.
