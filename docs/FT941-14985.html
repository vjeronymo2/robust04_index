940118 FT 18 JAN 94 / BBC Panorama libel case opens The High Court yesterday began hearing libel claims by US drugs company Upjohn over allegations made in a 1991 BBC Panorama programme on the marketing and side-effects of its sleeping drug Halcion. Upjohn and its UK subsidiary are also claiming damages against Professor Ian Oswald, former professor of psychiatry at Edinburgh University, over an article published in the New York Times. Prof Oswald is counter-claiming libel damages against Upjohn over what it has said in its defence.
