Language: Finnish Article Type:CSO [Article by Unto Hamalainen: "North Korea Appointed Kim Il-song's Son for Finland"] [Text] North Korea is sending Kim Pyong-il, the second son of leader Kim Il-song, to be ambassador to Finland. Kim Pyong-il was appointed ambassador already in the middle of last year and should have arrived in Helsinki by early March, but according to the Protocol Section of the Finnish Foreign Ministry, the new ambassador has not yet come. In a statement from the North Korean Embassy, the envoy will be in charge of conducting affairs. The previous ambassador left Helsinki in December. He was expected to handle affairs for only six months. Illness was given as the reason for his early departure. Kim Pyong-il has previously served as ambassador in Sofia. Kim Pyong-il is not as visible as his older brother Kim Chong-il, who is expected to succeed his father as North Korea's leader.
