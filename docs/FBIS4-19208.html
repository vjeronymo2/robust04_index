BFN [Text] Frankfurt/Main (DPA) -- At its session in Frankfurt on Thursday [14 April], the Central Bank Council of the German Bundesbank cut the prime rate by 0.25 percentage points. As of Friday, the discount rate will be reduced from 5.25 percent to 5.0 percent, and the Lombard rate from 6.75 to 6.5 percent. With its decision, the Central Bank Councils has "responded to the fact that the prospects for another decrease in the rate of price increases have improved." In addition, the Bundesbank assumes that the growth in the money supply will continue to slow down following the distortions at the turn of the year that were the result of special factors. On Thursday, the Bundesbank also announced that in the 1993 fiscal year, the Bundesbank achieved 18.83 billion German marks [DM] in net earnings, of which DM18.6 billion were transferred to the accounts of the Federal Government.
