Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Kiev--The Supreme Council of Ukraine recently ratified the Consular Convention between Ukraine and the Russian Federation that was signed on 15 January 1993 during President of Ukraine Leonid Kravchuk's visit to Moscow, said Natalya Zarudna, the head of department of the Information Directorate at Ukraine's Foreign Affairs Ministry, at a briefing on 22 February. The Russian Federal Assembly has not ratified the document yet. The consular convention is based on the principles of the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations of 24 April 1963. It is a bilateral agreement that supplements the principles of this multilateral international legal act. The convention determines the procedure for the establishment of consular offices and appointment of consular officials; consular functions, benefits for consular officials and their rights and duties as to the protection of their citizens' interests. According to Natalya Zarudna, the ratification of the convention will promote the establishment and development of Ukrainian-Russian consular relations, as well as the protection of rights and interests of Ukrainian and Russian citizens. Ukraine has already signed such conventions with China, Romania, Hungary, Poland, and Kazakhstan.
