Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Demidetskiy] [Text] Kishinev 29 Mar, TASS -- Petr Luchinskiy, a leader of the Agrarian Democratic Party that won the preterm parliamentary elections in February, was elected speaker of the Moldovan parliament on Tuesday. He was elected by a secret ballot, earning 57 votes. There were 43 votes against, and two ballots were found invalid. Luchinsky is 54 years old. In perestroika years he was first secretary of the Central Committee of the Moldovan Communist Party, secretary of the Central Committee of the Soviet Communist Party, CPSU. He was elected speaker of the previous Moldovan parliament after an acute parliamentary crisis last year. The parliament, torn by contradictions, had to be dissolved. It is the first time that a Moldovan parliament has been formed on a multiparty basis. It will include 104 deputies. The Agrarian Democratic Party will have parliamentary majority, 56 deputies. Agrarians believe that the legislative body should above all adopt a new constitution, create a legislative base for economic reform, and resolve the Dniester and Gagauz problems through granting special legal status to these territories.
