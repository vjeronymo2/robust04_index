BFN [From "News Bulletin" No. 502] [Text] Vilnius, May 18, ELTA -- Swedish Foreign Minister Margaretha af Ugglas arrived on a one day visit to Vilnius on Wednesday [18 May]. After the meeting with President Algirdas Brazauskas she told a news conference that they discussed the future development of the whole Baltic region. According to Margaretha af Ugglas, Sweden supports the opinion that Russian Army should leave the Baltic states by August 31, 1994. The minister also stressed that Sweden like Lithuania was interested in the demilitarization of Kaliningrad enclave. Sweden is ready to help Kaliningrad to develop its economics and to attract foreign investments. These questions will be discussed during the visit of Ugglas to Kaliningrad on Thursday. The minister answered the question about Sweden's attitude to the Russian military transit through Lithuania. She said that this problem of the two independent states should be settled in agreement with the international requirements. Wednesday afternoon the Swedish Foreign Minister visited Ignalina nuclear power station. Margaretha af Ugglas also met Lithuanian Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys and the leader of the opposition Vytautas Landsbergis.
