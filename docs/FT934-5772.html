931127 FT 27 NOV 93 / Finance and the Family: Nationwide's new bond - At a Glance Nationwide's new escalating three-year Fixed Growth Bond will pay 5.5 per cent gross in the first six months, rising to 7.5 per cent gross in the last six months. Those closing the account in years 2 and 3 will lose 180 days' interest. Britannia's Guaranteed Reversal bond pays a variable rate which changes by half a percentage point with every half point chnage in bank base rates. Base rates at 5.5 per cent attract interest at 6 per cent gross. The maximum interest rate is 7.5 per cent gross (base rates at 4 per cent) and the minimum is 5.5 per cent (base rates at 6.5 per cent). The bond matures on October 1 1994. The minimum investment in both building society bonds is Pounds 5,000. Portman is paying a fixed rate of 6.5 per cent gross for six months (or 6 per cent for one year) on a minimum deposit of Pounds 500. It will withdraw the bond on Tuesday.
