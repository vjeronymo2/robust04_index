940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / Remaining miners meet deadline for flexible hours A majority of British Coal's remaining 9,300 miners yesterday met a deadline that required them to accept more flexible working hours as part of a package which also entitles them to a Pounds 6,000 lump sum. Miners signed the deal after British Coal said no applications would be accepted after midnight. The two largest mining unions opposed the deal, which also provides maximum redundancy payments of Pounds 27,000 - less than miners have previously received but more than the statutory minimum. By late yesterday 83 per cent of Midlands miners and 40 per cent of those in the Yorkshire region had signed up. More were expected to follow
