Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] U.N. Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali met with Kazuo Aichi, director general of the Defense Agency, and Yohei Kono, president of the Liberal Democratic Party [LDP], separately in Tokyo on 21 December. Speaking about U.N. peacekeeping operations (PKO) in the meeting, Aichi expressed willingness to cooperate in the PKO efforts, saying: "The Defense Agency wants to participate in the United Nations' activities as much as possible." Secretary General Butrus-Ghali expressed his hope for Japan's contributions, saying: "Japan's participation is desirable, if only in symbolic forms, to help the United Nations strengthen itself in the long term." In the meeting with LDP President Kono, Butru-Ghali spoke about the issue of nuclear inspection of North Korea. He said he is willing to play the role of an intermediary between the United States and North Korea, saying: "If North Korea wants to hold talks with the United States, I am willing to convey its intention to the United States."
