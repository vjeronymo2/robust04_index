Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 24 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and China have completely agreed on the conclusion of a bilateral double taxation prevention agreement, which was initialed in Beijing on Thursday. A Finance Ministry official said the agreement will be formally signed during President Kim Yong-sam's official visit to China toward the close of next month. The agreement, consisting of 29 articles and a memorandum, will, among other things, allow South Korean industries active in China at least the same treatment as accorded to Chinese industries in terms of taxation. The agreement is expected to go into force late this year after being ratified by the National Assembly. The accord was initialed by Om Nak-yong, a Finance Ministry director, and Zhang Xanghai, deputy director of China's State Administration of Taxation, following their final-round negotiations since Feb. 20, the official said.
