Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 19 (YONHAP) -- South Korea and the United States agreed to push forward additional International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) inspections and inter-Korean exchange of special envoys, but refer the nuclear issue to the United Nations Security Council and hold the 1994 bilateral military exercise, "Team Spirit", if North Korea refuses to meet any of the two prerequisites, ruling Democratic Party (DP) chief policymaker Yi Se-ki said Saturday. Yi said he was told by Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu over the telephone that Han and his U.S. counterpart Warren Christopher coordinated their position over the phone a day earlier. If Pyongyang refuses any either of additional IAEA inspection or inter-Korean envoy exchange, the third-round high-level talks with Washington cannot take place and Team Spirit will be held this year, Han was quoted as saying. Under such circumstance, North Korean nuclear problem will be inevitably referred to the Security Council, which likely will create tension in the Korean peninsula, Han told Yi. But Seoul and Washington will make efforts to persuade Pyongyang in agreeing to extra IAEA inspections and continue inter-Korean dialogue to realize envoy exchange, Han said. "It now looks like the (North Korean nuclear) problem has returned to the starting point and all channels blocked, but in the long run, there is room for hope if we continue dialogue," Han was quoted as saying. Through dialogue efforts in the past year, North Korea has lost sympathy from the international community and Seoul and Washington have obtained sufficient grounds to persuade China to follow their steps in regards to possible decision by the Security Council, he said. The government and DLP [Democratic Liberal Party] will meet next week to discuss the North Korean nuclear problem.
