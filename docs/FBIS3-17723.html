Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chief Sergey Yudakov of the regional Administration's department on work with the bodies of municipal power told INTERFAX Sunday that in spite of the low voter turnout, municipal elections in the Moscow region would possibly be regarded as valid. According to Yudakov, 18 percent of the voters had come to cast ballots by 1500. By 2200, which is the polling stations' closing time, another 7 percent or more would have taken part in the elections. Under the existing regulations, at least 25 percent of the voters must come to the polling stations if elections are to be regarded as valid. On 27 March the Moscow and 30 other regions of the Russian Federation are holding municipal elections. Voter turnout is low everywhere. The central electoral commissions of the Kaliningrad and Kaluga regions say that slightly more than 21 percent of the voters cast ballots by 1500.
