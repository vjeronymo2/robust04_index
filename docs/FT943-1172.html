940926 FT 26 SEP 94 / Road users fearful over report Road users' organisations are fearful that a Royal Commission report on transport issues expected in October will come down heavily in favour of increased curbs on car use and road journeys. They are concerned that the weight given to the report will increase pressure on the government to limit roadbuilding and to increase the cost of journeys. The report is seen as giving a seal of approval to ideas expressed by the traditional critics of government transport policy. The British Road Federation said: 'The report will have a lot of status but we fear it will not contain a lot of sense. The commission must understand that roads and road transport are essential to our economy and to people's quality of life.' The Royal Commission on Environmental Pollution has been reviewing transport policy for the past 18 months. Its report is expected to contain more than 100 recommendations, including shifting the balance of spending towards public transport, raising fuel taxes and charging cars to enter congested city centres. The Automobile Association hopes the report will not be unfair to the motorist. It said: 'The car is necessary for people's freedom and mobility.' Environmental groups said they would welcome a report which called for a reduction in car use. The Council for the Protection of Rural England said: 'We anticipate the report will be reasonably strong. In our evidence to the commission we called for a proper, integrated transport policy.' Friends of the Earth said: 'The problem is getting the government to do something. We don't want a temporary alleviation of the problem. We want a sea change in government thinking.'
