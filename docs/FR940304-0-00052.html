Regulatory Flexibility Act The Regulatory Flexibility Act (Pub. L. 96&hyph;354) requires the Federal Government to anticipate and reduce the impact of regulations and paperwork requirements on small businesses. The primary impact of this final rule is on State governments and individuals. Therefore, we certify that this rule will not have a significant economic impact on a substantial number of small entities because it affects benefits to individuals and payments to States. Thus, a regulatory flexibility analysis is not required.
