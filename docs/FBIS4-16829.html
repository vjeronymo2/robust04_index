BFN [Text] Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis has received an invitation from Russian president Yeltsin to arrive in Moscow on April 21 for a working visit. He said that President Yeltsin had expressed a desire that the two sides sign a package of agreements on conditions for the Russian troop pull-out, which was initialed on March 15. Interfax was told by a spokesman for the Latvian president that Ulmanis promised to give a reply on Tuesday night. The spokesman said that speaking with an ambassador Ulmanis had said that on April 20 the Latvian parliament will take a decision on the possibility of the signing of the package of agreements. He said, however, that despite parliament's decision the meeting between the Russian and Latvian presidents would be expedient. President Ulmanis said that there are problems in the Russian-Latvian relations which should be clarified, including in relation to the Russian president's decision of April 5 which provides for the creation of a military base in Latvia. The emergence of the document caused a wave of criticisms in the Baltic countries and the Ulmanis said that his visit to Moscow, earlier planned for April 20, would be postponed for an indefinite period. prepared by the president's administration, if that is indeed the case."
