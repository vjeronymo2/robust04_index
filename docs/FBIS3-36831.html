Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow February 9 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin asked the State Duma, the standing lower chamber of the parliament, to pass a resolution on amnesty in the wake of the adoption of Russia's new constitution. The house will consider the president's legislative initiative on Friday, February 11, according to Duma Chairman Ivan Rybkin. The Duma will also have to finalise debates about its chief of staff, after failing to choose one out of seven candidates for the post at its meetings on February 7 and 8. The Duma chairman told today's session that some house committees have been instructed to draft a bill on combatting crime. The Duma as also expected to hold hearings about local self-government and preparations for the elections to the local bodies of power in the component parts of the Russian Federation. According to some sources, communists and other right-wingers are set to struggle for a majority in many regions.
