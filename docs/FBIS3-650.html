Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Mmabatho March 11 SAPA -- There might be a change in the pattern of shooting in Mmabatho on Friday [11 March] if rightwingers clashed with the reported 30 or 40 Umkhonto we Sizwe [Spear of the Nation -- MK] members who had been despatched to the area, Afrikaner Volksfront [National Front] General Constand Viljoen warned on Friday. Speaking on SABC [South African Broadcasting Corporation] television, he said: "We had a specific request not to deploy the Afrikaner Weerstandsbeweging (AWB) in the area." Troops from the farming community were therefore sent into Mmabatho. Gen Viljoen said he was aware the AWB had arrived in Bophuthatswana but after discussions with the Bophuthatswana government he had, at 4.45 AM Friday, asked the AWB to withdraw. He was aware that a political, and not military, solution was needed in Bophuthatswana but the territory needed stability for that to take place. The Bophuthatswana government should be allowed to make a decision about the territory's continued independence without being intimidated, he said.
