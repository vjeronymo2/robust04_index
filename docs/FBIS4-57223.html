BFN [Article by James Aparicio] [Text] As announced by LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA on Sunday, industrialist Gabriel Lewis Galindo, Christian Democratic Legislator Jose Antonio Sossa, and cattle rancher Carlos Sousa Lennox have accepted cabinet positions in the administration of President-elect Ernesto Perez Balladares. This has been confirmed by the nominees themselves. Lewis Galindo, who will be the foreign minister as of 1 September, was Panama's ambassador to the United States in 1977 when the Torrijos-Carter Canal Treaties were signed. He opposed the regime of General Manuel Antonio Noriega from 1987 to 1989. The new foreign minister is also the brother of Samuel Lewis Galindo, presidential candidate for Solidarity Party who came in next to last in Sunday's election. Sossa, who accepted the post of attorney general, is a founding member of the Christian Democratic Party (PDC), a traditional and staunch adversary of the PRD [Democratic Revolutionary Party]. Sousa Lennox, linked to the ruling Arnulfist Party and former president of the National Association of Cattlemen (Anagan), will be the minister of agricultural development (Mida). Research by LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA disclosed on Sunday as an exclusive report said that Perez Balladares has offered the Public Health Ministry to Carlos Garcia de Paredes, Raul (Baby) Arango Gasteazoro will hold the Ministry of the Presidency, Mitchel Doens will be the Ministry of Government and Justice, and Felix Gonzalez Santizo will be the minister of commerce and industries. The Education Ministry has been offered to Stanley Muschett, rector of the Santa Maria La Antigua University (USMA) and chairman of the Justice and Peace Commission. According to Perez Balladares, the other ministries will be held by PRD members, while Comptroller General Jose Chen Barria--also from the PDC--might be ratified in his post.
