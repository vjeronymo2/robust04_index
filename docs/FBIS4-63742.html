BFN [From the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] Addressing a conference entitled "New Aspects of Reforming Russian Economy" in Moscow, Aleksandr Khandruyev, deputy chairman of the [Russian] Central Bank, vehemently opposed the transformation of the Russian Central Bank into a U.S.-style federal reserve system. Such a system cannot be imposed on to Russian realities in its existing form, and neither can any other model, he stressed.
