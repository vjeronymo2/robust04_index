BFN [Text] Cairo, 15 Apr (MENA)--Finance Minister Dr. Muhammad Ahmad al-Razzaz has said that 30 agreements have been signed with other countries to avoid dual taxation. In a statement to MENA, he said discussion is under way to sign agreements between Egypt and Indonesia and Singapore on avoiding dual taxation to consolidate relations and economic ties. He added that the agreement concluded on Tuesday with the United Arab Emirates [UAE] and signed by the UAE minister of state for financial and industrial affairs, Ahmad Bin-Humayyid al-Tayir, is one of the most modern examples of agreements on avoiding dual taxation and dealing with every means of handling taxes on sources of income. Al-Razzaz said the Finance Ministry will submit the draft state budget for 1994-95 to the People's Assembly at the end of April to be debated by the Assembly in May, adding that it will come into force on 1 July. He said the preliminary estimates for the budget this year amount to 83 billion pounds and that the value of wages is approximately 12.8 billion pounds, a 2.2 billion-pound-increase on the current fiscal year, which ends at the end of June. The target budget deficit is 4.2 percent. Inflation is 2.8 percent and state revenues amount to 40.3 billion pounds, a 15-percent-increase on the previous year.
