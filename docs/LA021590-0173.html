February 15, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

ORANGE COUNTY FOCUS: COUNTYWIDE;

WIEDER, ROTH GET CAMPAIGN PAPERS

Orange County Supervisors Harriett M. Wieder and Don R. Roth have made their reelection bids official. Both have obtained election papers from the registrar of voters, and must complete and return them by March 9.

Supervisor Thomas F. Riley, who also faces reelection in June, had not taken out papers as of late Wednesday, a deputy registrar said.

Roth, who represents the 4th District and chairs the board, is seeking his second term as a supervisor. He faces no opposition.

Wieder, who is seeking her fourth term as a supervisor in the 2nd District, faces only nominal opposition.

Marie Alexis Antos, 45, a homemaker from Seal Beach, has also taken out papers to run for the 2nd District seat, but said she has not yet raised any money for the race. Anos said that she would concentrate on balancing the budget, reducing traffic congestion and seeking a solution to the landfill problem with a countywide recycling program.

Each of the incumbents facing reelection this year has raised well over $100,000.

Neither Supervisors Roger R. Stanton nor Gaddi H. Vasquez are up for reelection this year.
