May 5, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

MEDIA TREATMENT OF THE 'HILARY IN HIDING' CASE

Re "Trail of Abuse in the Hilary Custody Case," by Howard Rosenberg (April 24), about the dispute between Dr. Elizabeth Morgan and her former husband, Dr. Eric Foretich:

Rosenberg claims this "may be the most stunning case of abuse by the media. . . ." He himself has made a contribution to that abuse.

Nowhere does he address the central issue -- the (alleged) sexual molestation of 7-year-old Hilary.

Morgan was forced to take her case to the media.

Rosenberg inadvertently demonstrates why. While pooh-poohing the claims of sexual abuse by saying no court has upheld the allegation, he ignores that a court barred Foretich from seeing another daughter (from a previous marriage) after accusations of sexual molestation arose and he ignores other court findings in this area.

I know of no woman who would have acted differently from Morgan in going public.

MEGAN CALLAWAY, Los Angeles
