Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Cuban Vice President Carlos Lage Davila described the conversations held with Brazilian President Itamar Franco and three ministers of his cabinet as very positive. On the third day of his official visit to Brazil, Lage held a press conference with more than 20 local and foreign reporters at the residence of the Cuban ambassador to Brazil. Of his meeting with Itamar Franco, Lage said they discussed many issues related to the development of political and economic relations between the two states. Furthermore, he stated that, even though no agreements would be signed during this visit to Brazil, doors have been opened for the development of bilateral ties. Tomorrow morning, Lage Davila and the delegation that accompanies him will travel to Rio de Janeiro, where they will hold meetings with local businessmen and political personalities. When questioned on the lifting of the U.S. embargo on Vietnam, the Cuban leader answered that it is the result of the Asian country's perseverance in keeping alive their ideals and social system.
