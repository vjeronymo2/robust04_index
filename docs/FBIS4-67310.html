Conference AB1805221094 Lagos NTA Television Network in English 2000 GMT 18 May 94 AB1805221094 Lagos NTA Television Network English BFN [Text] The chairman of the National Drug Law Enforcement Agency, NDLEA, Brigadier General Musa Bamayi, has said that the agency will not spare anybody in redeeming the nation's image abroad, even if it means getting rid of staff or disbanding the agency. Brig. Gen. Musa Bamayi stated this at a press conference held at the Kano state office of the agency. He brought along with him 82 retrenchment letters and three of dismissal for the staff of the Kano agency, and he supervised the distribution of the letters at the news briefing. He explained that findings in the last three months in the Kano office of the NDLEA have shown that staff of the agency have tremendously betrayed the confidence reposed on them by Nigerians. NTA Kano correspondent Hamisu Rogo reports that Brig. Gen. Bamayi revealed that some claims by the international community that some officials of the NDLEA cooperate with drug barons are genuine, and that those retrenched have been found to be involved in some way or another with drug barons at the airport. The three dismissed officials were found to have been involved in acts capable of perverting the course of justice. The chairman said the NDLEA is ready to [words indistinct].
