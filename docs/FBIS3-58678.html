Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Anatoliy Tikhonov, Russian deputy minister for fuel and energy, has said Russia can supply Turkey's natural gas needs up to the year 2050. Tikhonov made a statement at Istanbul Ataturk Airport before departing at the end of his visit to Turkey. He said Russia annually supplies Turkey with 5.5 billion cubic meters of natural gas, adding that this amount will be increased by 2.5 billion cubic meters beginning in 1996. Pointing out that his visit has been very beneficial, Tikhonov said a protocol has been signed on cooperation in the fields of oil, natural gas, and coal. He concluded by saying that the conditions are conducive for further cooperation between the Turkish and Russian energy complexes.
