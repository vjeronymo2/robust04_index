BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Glazovskaya] [Text] Moscow, 15 Apr -- Saparmurat Niyazov and Leonid Kravchuk have expressed their mutual aspirations to overcome the difficulties that have arisen by taking both parties' interests and mutual advantage into account. The presidents of Turkmenistan and Ukraine met in Moscow today at the meeting of CIS heads of state. Agreement was reached that Turkmenistan would resume the supply of natural gas to Ukraine from 15 April this year, since the Ukrainian side has found an acceptable solution for paying off its debts. The presidents of the two countries approved an interstate agreement which sets out a clear schedule for the settlement of payments and reciprocal supplies from Ukraine. Thus, the first payments amounting to $10 million have already been made and the supply of goods and various products worth $60 million has begun. According to the schedule, Ukraine will make payments of $78 million and supply goods and various other products worth $200 million every quarter. During their conversation, the presidents spoke in favor of actively continuing their dialogue and developing cooperation, both on a bilateral basis and within the framework of the CIS.
