BFN [Text] Changsha, June 22 (XINHUA) -- The floods which besieged this capital of Hunan Province since June 16 were finally receded below the warning water level 18:00 hours today. Some 560,000 Armymen and residents have worked days and nights to combat the floods for almost a week. The Changsha city government and the city committee of the Communist Party of China have now begun to organize manpower to help resume production, while continuing operations to fight any further possible floods. During the past flood, the highest water level recorded at the Changsha monitoring station was 38.93 m [meters], some 3.93 m above danger level. One hundred and eighteen towns of eight prefectures and cities along the Xiangjiang River were flooded. Flood water also entered some 20 streets and lanes of Changsha, more than 300,000 city dwellers were affected. Over 400 factories and 5,000 shops had to stop production or suspend business.
