BFN [By ITAR-TASS] [Text] Moscow June 7 TASS -- A Cosmos-2281 artificial earth satellite was launched on June 7, 1994, by the Soyuz rocket-booster from the Russian Plesetsk launching grounds in the interests of the Russian Defence Ministry. Sources at the ground mission control center report that the satellite has been put on the orbit with the following parameters: - the initial period of rotation - 88.7 minutes; - the maximum distance from the earth surface (in the apogee) - 265 kilometers; - the minimum distance from the earth surface (in the perigee) - 192 kilometers; - the inclination of the orbit - 82.6 degrees. The on-board equipment is functioning normally.
