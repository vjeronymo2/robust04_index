August 5, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

ANATOMY LESSON

In Steve Weinstein's July 29 article on Mark Frost, co-creator of television's "Twin Peaks," Frost is quoted as describing a character as a "walking cerebellum," when he should have called him a "walking cerebrum."

The cerebrum, location of the cerebral cortex, is the major part of the brain and controls thought and other intellectual processes. The cerebellum, in contrast, is a smaller section that helps maintain coordination and facilitates movement.

In Charles Champlin's "Spike Lee Gets Personal," Lee mentioned the athlete who tears a rotator "cup" when he meant to say the rotator "cuff," tissue surrounding the shoulder joint consisting of muscle and tendons.

Proper labeling of body parts makes for better filmmaking!

DAVID E. AFTERGOOD, MD

Los Angeles
