EFFECTIVE DATE: June 18, 1994. [OR&hyph;943&hyph;4210&hyph;06; GP4&hyph;160; OR&hyph;48432(WASH)] Termination of Segregation of Proposed Withdrawal; Washington AGENCY: Bureau of Land Management, Interior. ACTION: Notice.

SUMMARY: The temporary 2-year segregation of a proposed withdrawal of 9,730.82 acres of public lands for the U.S. Department of the Army, Corps of Engineers expansion of the Yakima Firing Center terminates on June 17, 1994. The lands are included in another existing proposed withdrawal and will remain closed to surface entry and mining. The lands have been and remain open to mineral leasing. EFFECTIVE DATE: June 18, 1994. FOR FURTHER INFORMATION CONTACT: Donna Kauffman, BLM Oregon/Washington State Office, P.O. Box 2965, Portland, Oregon 97208&hyph;2965, 503&hyph;280&hyph;7162. SUPPLEMENTARY INFORMATION: A Notice of Proposed Withdrawal was published in the Federal Register , 57 FR 27267, June 18, 1992, as amended by 57 FR 61095, December 23, 1992, and by 58 FR 6413, January 28, 1993, which segregated the lands described therein for up to 2 years from settlement, sale, location, or entry under the general land laws, including the mining laws, but not the mineral leasing laws, subject to valid existing rights. The 2-year segregation expires June 17, 1994.
