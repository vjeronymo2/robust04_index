BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Viktor Demidenko and Mikhail Melnik] [Text] Kiev, 19 May -- By today's decrees Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk has reorganized the power ministries [defense, interior, and internal affairs] of the Republic of Crimea, despite the fact that he does not have a constitutional right to do so. Main directorates will be set up on the basis of the Ukrainian Security Service and the Interior Ministry. They will be subordinated directly to the Ukrainian Security Service chairman and the Ukrainian Interior Minister. Administrative and service premises, armaments, means of transport, and property which in the past were used by the Security Service and the Interior Ministry of the Republic of Crimea are being handed over to them. Major General Ivan Kolomitsev has been appointed by Leonid Kravchuk as chief of the main directorate of the Ukrainian security service in Crimea. ITAR-TASS has learned that by decision of the Cabinet of Ministers, an additional post of deputy minister and chief of the Main Directorate of Internal Affairs in the Crimea has been introduced onto the staff of the central apparatus of the Ukrainian Interior Ministry. Militia Colonel Valeriy Chernyshov has been appointed to this post. By decree of the Ukrainian president, the Ministry of Justice of the Republic of Crimea has also been reorganized into the main directorate of justice in the Crimea. From now on it will be subordinate to the Ukrainian Ministry of Justice.
