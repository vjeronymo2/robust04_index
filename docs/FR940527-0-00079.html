Alascom claims that the Board has ``elevated the policy goals of efficiency and competition,'' above other Board objectives. 41 There is no merit to Alascom's claim. First, we do not agree that efficiency and competition are lesser objectives than the other Board objectives. As stated in the Board's Supplemental Order tentatively adopting these objectives, ``a proper resolution of the issues before us must represent a balanced effort to achieve each of these goals. 42 Second, we find that the Board has balanced the five Board objectives in a manner that best serves the public interest. The Board has addressed the efficiency and competition objectives by recommending the termination of the JSA. It has addressed the objectives of universal service and revenue requirement neutrality by recommending several transition mechanisms including the continuation of the JSA for the first phase of the transition period, the AT&T payment of $150 million to Alascom, and the AT&T second phase must-purchase requirement.
