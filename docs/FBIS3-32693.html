Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil through 10 February: Congress on 3 February approved provisional measure No. 49 establishing ceiling salaries for federal public servants, including those in state-owned companies. Executive branch employees will be allowed to earn up to 90 percent of a state minister's salary. The other branches' employees can not earn more than members of Congress, state ministers, and Federal Supreme Court members. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 4 Feb 94 Section 1 p 5) Tax revenues totaled $4.8 billion in January, $1.4 billion more than the same month in 1993, and $300 million more than in December. Federal Revenue Secretary Osiris Lopes Filho said this is the result of the fight against tax evasion, the increase in Social Security contributions, and the collection of the provisional tax on financial operations. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 4 Feb 94 Section 1 p 10) Fuel prices were readjusted 16.5 percent on 8 February, the third price readjustment this year authorized by the Transportation Ministry. (Brasilia Radio Nacional da Amazonia in Portuguese 0900 GMT 8 Feb 94) According to the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics, the January inflation rate in Rio de Janeiro and Sao Paulo was 41.14 percent for consumers who earn up to 40 minimum salaries and 40.6 percent for those who earn up to eight minimum salaries. The inflation rate for the lower income group increased 4.08 percent compared with last December. In Rio prices increased 42.81 percent for the higher income group and in Sao Paulo by 40.54 percent. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 5 Feb 94 Section 2 p 3)
