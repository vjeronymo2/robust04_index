940718 FT 18 JUL 94 / Adams attacks peace moves Mr Gerry Adams, Sinn Fein president, yesterday attacked the latest London and Dublin peace moves and reiterated his party's position on the basis for a settlement. Last week the UK government said it was willing to consider amending the 1920 Government of Ireland Act. Mr Adams said: 'Cosmetic or semantic change in either the . . . act or the Irish constitution will serve only to invite reaction, without bringing about the new beginning that is so obviously required.' He added: 'Sinn Fein has consistently argued that the consent and the allegiance of unionists is needed to secure a peace settlement. 'However, the unionists cannot have a veto over British policy and the British government must stop hiding behind the pretence that they do.'
