930527 FT 27 MAY 93 / Parliament and Politics: Egg scare payouts 'cheated farmers' POULTRY farmers were cheated out of thousands of pounds in compensation for chickens they were forced to slaughter during the 1980s salmonella scare, a committee of MPs said yesterday. They accused the Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food of deliberately short-changing the farmers in an act of 'maladministration that should never be repeated'. The select committee for the parliamentary ombudsman said ministers had set compensation 'at the lowest levels legally defensible'.
