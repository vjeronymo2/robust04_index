Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Aleksandr Krylovich] [Text] Moscow January 31 TASS -- Russia is interested in political and economic stability in the Crimea, a Russian Foreign Ministry official said. In an interview with ITAR-TASS on Monday, the official said that "Russia is interested in political and economic stability in the Crimea and will continue to build its relations with Ukraine on the basis of the existing agreements, including those concerning regional cooperation." "Experience accumulated in relations between the two countries shows that in the current situation the development of versatile ties between various regions of Russia and Ukraine may help improve the economic situation and living standards and reduce social tension," the official said. Commenting on the results of the presidential elections in the Crimea on Sunday, the official stressed that "an assessment of the results of the elections in the Crimea should be made by Ukraine itself in the first place. It is its internal business." "The victory of the leader of the political bloc Russia, Yuriy Meshkov, signifies that his position on most urgent issues was closer to and more understandable for the residents of the Crimea. The complex economic situation in Ukraine and in this region in particular played not the last role in the outcome of the voting," he added.
