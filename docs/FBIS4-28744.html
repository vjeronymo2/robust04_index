BFN [Joint Communique issued on the visit of Burma's Senior General Than Shwe, chairman of the State Law and Order Restoration Council, to the Lao People's Democratic Republic -- place and date not given] [Text] Senior General Than Shwe, chairman of the SLORC [State Law and Restoration Council] of the Union of Myanmar [Burma], paid a goodwill visit to the Lao People's Democratic Republic [LPDR] from 9 to 13 June at the invitation of Laotian President Mr. Nouhak Phoumsavan. Sr. Gen. Than Shwe and wife Daw Kyaing Kyaing, were accompanied by Lieutenant General Khin Nyunt, SLORC secretary-1, and wife Dr. Khin Win Shwe; Lt. Gen. Myint Aung, minister of agriculture; Brigadier General D.O. Abel, minister of national planning and economic development; U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs, and wife; Brig. Gen. Myo Thant, minister of information; and senior responsible personnel. The SLORC chairman and his delegation were warmly welcomed by the host country, reflecting the existing friendly relations between the neighbors. During his visit, Sr. Gen. Than Shwe called on Mr. Khamtai Siphandon, chairman of Lao People's Revolutionary Party Central Committee, and held friendly and cordial talks. Sr. Gen. Than Shwe also met and held talks with Laotian President Mr. Nouhak Phoumsavan. At the meeting, the leaders held friendly, cordial, and constructive talks on matters relating to bilateral relations and international matters of common interest. The talks were conducted with full understanding. Also present at the meeting were Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, SLORC secretary-1; Lt. Gen. Myint Aung, minister of agriculture; Brig. Gen. D.O. Abel, minister of national planning and economic development; U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs; Brig. Gen. Myo Thant, minister of information; and U Maung Maung Lay, Myanmar ambassador to Laos. Present on the Laotian side were Lt. Gen. Choummali Sai-gnason, minister of national defense; Mr. Somsavat Lengsavat, minister of foreign affairs; Mr. Sisavat Keobounphan, minister of agriculture and forestry; Mr. Thongdam Chanthaphon, minister and head of the Presidential Office; Mr. Bounteng Vongsai, acting minister of information and culture; Mr. Somphong Mongkhonvilai, deputy chairman of the Planning and Cooperation Committee; Mr. Thongvan Phanlatsavong, deputy minister of trade; and Mr. Kideng Thammavong, Lao ambassador to Myanmar. The leader of the Myanmar delegation clarified matters relating to developments in drawing up a constitution, as well as political, economic, and social affairs and efforts for national unity. Moreover, the Myanmar delegation reaffirmed its independent and active foreign policy based on the five principles of peaceful coexistence and its continuing active role in the Nonaligned Movement. The Lao leader clarified the implementation of a reform policy in the LPDR in recent years and plans for public economic development until the year 2000. Furthermore, the Lao side also reaffirmed its consistently followed foreign policy based on peace, freedom, goodwill, and cordial relations with other countries, especially with neighboring countries. Sr. Gen. Than Shwe and the Myanmar delegation visited and toured economic production establishments, cultural, and religious sites in Vientiane, Vientiane Province, and Luang Prabang Province. Both sides firmly agree that this successful trip will further strengthen the foundation of the existing good neighborly relations and bilateral cooperation and will also enhance promotion of regional development, peace, stability, and cooperation. SLORC Chairman Sr. Gen. Than Shwe expressed his warm thanks to Laotian President Mr. Nouhak Phoumsavan, the Laotian Government, and the Laotian people for their kind hospitality during the Myanmar delegation's goodwill visit. During the visit, Lt. Gen. Khin Nyunt, SLORC secretary-1, called on Mr. Saman Vi-gnaket, chairman of the Lao National Assembly; Brig. Gen. D.O. Abel, minister of national planning and economic development, called on Mr. Khamphoui Keoboualapha, Lao deputy prime minister and chairman of the Planning and Cooperation Committee; U Ohn Gyaw, minister of foreign affairs, called on Mr. Somsavat Lengsavat, Lao minister of foreign affairs; Lt. Gen. Myint Aung, minister of agriculture, called on Mr. Sisavat Keobounphan, Lao minister of agriculture and forestry; and Brig. Gen. Myo Thant, minister of information, called on Mr. Bounteng Vongsai, acting Lao minister of information and culture. The following agreements were signed during the visit: 1. An agreement on the fixed international boundary of the Mekong River. 2. An agreement on the establishment of a joint commission for bilateral cooperation. 3. An agreement on tourism cooperation. Sr. Gen. Than Shwe, chairman of SLORC, extended an invitation to Laotian President Mr. Nouhak Phoumsavan and his wife to visit the Union of Myanmar during this present stable period and the Laotian president warmly accepted.
