BFN [Text] Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou and U.S. President Bill Clinton had a telephone conversation yesterday during which they agreed on the need for a speedy solution of the Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM] issue. During the telephone conversation, the U.S. President also expressed his support for the confidence-building measures for the Cyprus issue which, he said, are the best hope to achieve progress. Government spokesman Evangelos Venizelos described the U.S. interest in a solution to the Greek-Skopje dispute as obvious. Venizelos added that the Ubited States understands the Greek positions and wants a swift solution of the issue, something which we want as well. Next week there will be a meeting in New York between Ambassador Khristos Zakharakis, Foreign Ministry director general and UN Mediator Cyrus Vance.
