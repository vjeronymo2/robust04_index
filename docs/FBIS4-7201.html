BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 28 KYODO -- Newly appointed Home Affairs Minister Hajime Ishii played a major role in enacting political reform bills as the head of the House of Representatives Special Committee on Political Reform. Ishii, 59, succeeded Kanju Sato of the Social Democratic Party (SDP) [of Japan -- SDPJ]. A senior member of the coalition's core party Shinseito (Japan Renewal Party), Ishii served as director general of the National Land Agency under an administration of the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP). He joined Shinseito as new Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata and Ichiro Ozawa, the coalition's chief strategist, launched the party last year after bolting from the LDP, then the ruling party. As an LDP legislator, Ishii held the posts of parliamentary vice labor minister and transport minister. In 1977, he led a government mission to Dhaka, Bangladesh, where he negotiated with the hijackers of a Japan Airlines plane. Currently, Ishii is chairman of a Friendship Association made up of parliamentarians of Japan and North Korea. Ishii, who has studied in the United States, has a good working command of English. Hailing from Hyogo Prefecture in western Japan, Ishii has been elected to the powerful House of Representatives eight times.
