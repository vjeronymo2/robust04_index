BFN [Text] Sofia, May 3 (BTA) -- The Bulgarian Socialist Party (BSP), supporting the government, insists that parliament discuss the cabinet's economic and social policy together with the report of the National Bank, said BSP Executive Committee member Doncho Konakchiev at a press conference today. According to him, within the next few days Prime Minister Lyuben Berov will propose concrete personnel and structural changes in the cabinet. The premier announced his intentions to make changes in the cabinet last Wednesday [27 April] in a declaration before parliament. We are in touch with Prime Minister Berov and we hope he will keep his promises -- to restore dialogue with the trade unions, to make cost-of-living adjustments, to consolidate the foreign exchange and financial market, and strengthen national security, Mr. Konakchiev said. The opposition Union of Democratic Forces (SDS) will consider its next steps at the end of parliamentary Easter recess, when the future of Lyuben Berov's Cabinet will become clearer, the top leadership of the coalition decided at its regular session today. Within a week the parties in the SDS will make proposals how to provoke the resignation of the cabinet and early parliamentary elections. The government has come under serious trade union pressure. If Prime Minister Berov starts structural reforms without parliament's support, the Confederation of Independent Trade Unions in Bulgaria (KNSB) will block them, KNSB leader Prof. Krustyu Petkov said at a briefing today. The KNSB has called a warning general strike for tomorrow as part of the preparations for its general strike scheduled to start on May 17. The local structures of the other major trade union amalgamation -- Podkrepa -- will also take part in protest actions. The trade unions demand cost-of-living adjustment of the incomes of the population after prices doubled in two months with the steep slide of the lev against the U.S. dollar and the introduction of value-added tax on April 1. Prof. Krustyu Petkov said today that he expects 934 enterprises with over 200,000 workers to take part in the warning strike. The KNSB also demands the coordination and adoption of a state policy for stabilizing the foreign exchange market, clarity on the protection of Bulgarian producers, and transparency regarding the obligations undertaken by Bulgaria to the international institutions. In a letter to Prime Minister Lyuben Berov European Confederation of Trade Unions Secretary General Emilio Gabaglo expressed support for the KNSB. Mr. Gabaglo has sent his personal adviser Peter Seideneck to observe the observation of the rights of the strikers during the warning strike. This morning Prime Minister Lyuben Berov assured Prof. Petkov over the phone of his readiness to start negotiations on May 11 and to personally head the delegation of the government (on which the KNSB insisted). This was announced today by the leader of the confederation. "However, the beginning of negotiations does not mean stopping strike actions," Prof. Petkov said. In an interview for today's issue of the PARI financial news daily, Prof. Petkov says that Prime Minister Berov is ready to make maximum compromises in order to reach an agreement. However, according to the KNSB leader, the stands of the Prime Minister and the KNSB on protecting the savings of the population and local production are diametrically opposed.
