BFN [Text] South Korea will set up a consulate general in the Chinese port city of Qingdao by the end of September this year at the latest, a Foreign Ministry official said yesterday. He said two officials at the ministry will be sent to Qingdao in mid-May for preparatory works, which will take three of four months. In addition to dealing with usual consular affairs, the new consulate general will support Korean companies engaged in business in Shandong Province surrounding Qingdao, said the official. He added more Korean firms have advanced into Shandong than any other Chinese province. Seoul, which formalized diplomatic ties with Beijing in August 1991, now has one consulate general in Shanghai. The official said the government will push to set up several more consulates general in other key cities in China.
