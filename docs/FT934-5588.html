931129 FT 29 NOV 93 / Survey of Software at Work - Winter 1993 (15): Supplier/product profile Product: Intergraph's two core GIS-related systems are MGE, the Modular GIS Environment, which is the foundation for a suite of GIS and mapping applications, and FRAMME, which is designed for utilities' assets management applications. There are over 400 individual packages in Intergraph's software portfolio. Supplier: Intergraph (UK) is the UK subsidiary of Intergraph Corp of the US which was founded in 1969 in Huntsville, Alabama. The UK company was founded in London in 1980, with offices in London, Swindon and Leeds. Turnover: Dollars 1.2bn worldwide in 1992. Pounds 50m in UK 1992. Employees: 11,000 people worldwide, 300 in UK. Users: supplier claims 25 per cent of worldwide GIS market. Entry prices of Pounds 2,675 for MGE, Pounds 15,000 for Framme (software only). Hardware and operating systems: DOS/Windows, Windows NT, and Unix for MGE, Unix, DOS, Windows for Framme. Sales and support/maintenance: direct and through third parties. Other services available: project planning and implementation. User views include 'broad capabilities, flexibility, long-term future development assured.' Also: 'need for proprietary hardware, under-estimation of disk space, hardware under-sized.' Contact: Neil McLeod, Intergraph, Delta Business Park, Great Western Way, Swindon, SN5 7XP; tel 0793 618508.
