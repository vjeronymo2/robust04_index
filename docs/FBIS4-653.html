BFN [Text] The foreign minister, who was contacted this morning, has called on all Burundians in Kigali to gather if possible at the Burundian Embassy to await their imminent repatriation. According to a high-ranking official of the same ministry, negotiations on the repatriation of our compatriots from the Rwandan capital began last weekend. Those who were in Gisenyi have left Rwandan territory. There are around 40 people left. Two planes will leave Bujumbura soon for Goma, eastern Zaire, to where these people were evacuated. A special flight is planned in the next few hours for those who are in Kigali and who are able to reach the Burundian Embassy. The high-ranking Foreign Ministry official told us that at the Burundian Embassy in Kigali, 30 Burundians are awaiting repatriation, and will receive French and Belgian military assistance. Those who cannot easily reach the embassy are asked to join any Belgian or French citizens residing nearby to be evacuated with other expatriates. In case of problems, they can call the Burundian Embassy in Kigali on the following number: 75512.
