Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Munich (DDP/ADN) -- Hans-Peter Stihl, president of the German Association of Chambers of Industry and Commerce, has reaffirmed German industry's hope for a continuation of the liberal-conservative coalition between the Christian Democratic Union, Christian Social Union, and the Free Democrats. A red-green government following the Bundestag election in October is "not a desirable alternative," Stihl told journalists in Munich today. Stihl told the DDP/ADN news agency that industry also rejects the idea of a social democratic-liberal coalition involving the Social Democratic Party [SPD] and the Free Democratic Party, as the left wing of the SPD is pursuing a policy hostile toward industry and which threatens jobs. His skepticism toward a change of government was shared by Klaus Murmann, president of the Federal Confederation of German Employers' Associations, Tyll Necker of the Federal Association of German Industry, as well as Herbert Spaeth of the National Association of German Skilled Crafts. The industrialists met with Chancellor Helmut Kohl at the "top-level Munich talks of German industry."
