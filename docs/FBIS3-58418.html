Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn, Jan 13, IRNA -- A senior Iranian parliamentarian here Wednesday [12 January] termed as fruitful his visit to Germany and talks with German officials. Especial envoy of the Majles presiding board and head of Iran- Germany parliamentary friendship group Mohammad Javad Larijani told IRNA here that the Iranian parliamentary delegation visits Germany at the invitation of German parliament. He said his visit aimed at clarifying Iran's stands on important regional issues, removing obstacles on mutual ties and identifying new grounds for future cooperation in the region. Larijani added that the Iranian delegation and German authorities exchanged views on such topics as Central Asia, Iran-Germany cooperation in the region, Middle East issues and Israel-PLO deal. The Iranian official further noted that the apostate Salman Rushdie's blasphemous book had harmed peace, security and understanding among followers of different religions because of insulting millions of Muslims. He remarked that Iran's delayed debts to Germany was not considerable compared to the amount of Germany's exports to Iran during the past years and believed German Government could find reasonable settlements to the issue. Larijani and his accompying delegation arrived here last Monday and will visit other German cities and hold talks with local officials.
