BFN [Excerpt] Buenos Aires, 1 Jun (NA) -- Tucuman Governor Ramon "Palito" Ortega admitted today that his province has a monthly deficit of 6.5 million pesos. He blames this situation on the signing of the Fiscal Pact with the federal government and on the transfer of federal schools to the provincial government. Ortega indicated that his province has a "deficit" because of "the non-collection of revenues following the signing of the Fiscal Pact, which caused a social security deficit of 3.5 million pesos." The governor also blamed the situation on the transfer of federal schools to the provincial government and on salary increases for teachers. He emphasized that the latter measures increased the monthly deficit to 6.5 million pesos. [passage omitted]
