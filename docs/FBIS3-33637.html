Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Peter Mackler] [Text] Davos, Switzerland, Jan 30 (AFP) -- Israel and the PLO appeared moving toward a breakthrough Sunday [30 January] in talks on Palestinian autonomy with the PLO accepting a compromise proposal hammered out by negotiators overnight. Working groups laboured through the night and morning hours to nail down details of the Israeli withdrawal from the West Bank town of Jericho and Gaza Strip under an accord signed in September. 'Abd-Rabbuh, a spokesman for the Palestine Liberation Organisation, told reporters in this Alpine resort town that there was now a compromise on the table and "we accept it." He would not give details of the proposal or say explicitly whether the Israelis accepted it. But he added, "I can't say that there is any problem holding up an agreement." Israeli officials were not immediately available for comment. Israeli sources said the proposal was being transmitted to Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin. Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres, who had lengthy talks with PLO chief Yasir 'Arafat late Saturday and was to meet with him again outside the World Economic Forum here, was asked earlier if they were close to a breakthrough. "Closer than yesterday," he replied. He told reporters the two hours of discussions he had with 'Arafat at their hotel here Saturday were constructive but he refused to say whether they would produce an accord. 'Arafat declined to comment on progress, telling reporters at the hotel where he and Peres were staying: "You will have to wait." "Yesterday it was going well and we hope today we continue on the same lines," 'Arafat said. Israeli Environment Minister Yosi Sarid said the working groups of negotiators met until 3 a.m. (0200 GMT) Sunday and resumed at about 7 a.m (0600 GMT). He said they would continue their work and then 'Arafat and Peres and full delegations would meet again. Asked about progress he said: "As long as documents are not being signed, I don't want to speculate about progress." Peres is due to fly out Sunday night for Washington. But when asked if he was leaving Sunday night, he only said: "Maybe." 'Arafat had said at the start of his discussions with Peres, their first since a meeting in Oslo a week ago, that they hoped to get an accord here or at least "a main platform for an agreement." Peres had added: "There are serious differences between the parties, but we are here with serious intentions. We will do all that is possible to answer hopes for peace." Under an agreement signed on the White House lawn on September 13, the Israelis were to have started a four-month withdrawal of their troops from Jericho and the Gaza Strip on December 13. But Israel and the PLO have been wrangling over who should control border crossings with neighbouring Jordan and Egypt, the size of the Jericho autonomous area and security for Jewish settlements. Sarid had said earlier that if there was an agreement later Sunday it would focus on the size of the Jericho and Gaza autonomous areas as well as on the border crossings but leave many other issues still to be resolved, such as the status of Palestinian authority and administrative arrangements in the two areas. "There are many legal questions which we have not even discussed," Sarid said. 'Arafat had arrived from Tunisia on Saturday after appealing to US President Bill Clinton for help in ending the impasse which he blamed on the Israelis. He made the same request of British Prime Minister John Major on Friday.
