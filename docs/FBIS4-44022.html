BFN [Excerpts] Paris, June 23 (AFP) -- Israeli Deputy Foreign Minister Yossi Beilin on Thursday [23 June] commended France for what he called its "courageous" decision to launch a humanitarian military intervention in Rwanda. After a meeting here with French Minister Delegate for European Affairs Alain Lamassoure, Beilin added that he hoped that once Israel had sorted out its own regional problems, it could help other countries to deal with theirs. [passage omitted] Beilin, on a working visit to Paris, also discussed with Lamassoure the renegotiation of a trade agreement between Israel and the European Union, which was first signed in 1975 and reviewed in the early 1980s, he said. They also talked about cooperation in scientific research. Beilin was later due to meet Foreign Minister Alain Juppe and President Francois Mitterrand's chief of presidential staff, Hubert Vedrine.
