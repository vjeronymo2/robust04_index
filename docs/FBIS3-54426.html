Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] At his office in the Presidential Palace today, brother Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, chairman of the Presidential Council, received visiting Indonesian Foreign Minister Ali Alatas. Ali Alatas conveyed a message to the brother president from Indonesian President Muhammad Suharto. The message deals with bilateral relations, cooperation, and issues of interest to our Islamic nation. During the meeting, there was a review of bilateral relations and cooperation and ways of enhancing them in the interest of both countries, which share strong historical ties. The Indonesian foreign minister expressed his appreciation for the warm welcome he has received and admiration of our country's political achievements and development process, primarily unity and democracy. He also expressed Indonesia's desire to promote its relations with Yemen and to expand trade, technical, and industrial cooperation with our country. For his part, the chairman of the Presidential Council praised Yemeni-Indonesian relations, pointing out that they are special and historical relations based on Islamic brotherhood, a cultural dimension, and trade between the two countries. He also affirmed our country's eagerness to promote these ties to achieve the hopes of the two peoples. He noted that there are many opportunities to develop cooperation, especially in investment. In this regard, he said our country's investment law provides a good climate and gives guarantees and good opportunities for investors in all fields. The brother president also asked the Indonesian foreign minister to convey his greetings to President Suharto and his best wishes for the Indonesian people for more progress and prosperity. The audience was attended by Foreign Minister Muhammad Salim Basindwah, the members of the delegation accompanying the Indonesian foreign minister, and the Indonesian ambassador to our country.
