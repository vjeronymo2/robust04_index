Language: English Article Type:BFN [by ITAR-TASS special correspondent] [Text] St. Petersburg February 8 TASS -- A meeting of the Commonwealth Inter-Parliamentary Assembly's Council ended here today after electing Head of the Russian Upper Chamber Vladimir Shumeyko its chairman. The meeting welcomed delegations of Azeri and Georgian parliaments who have become members of the Commonwealth Inter-Parliamentary Assembly. The meeting focused on bringing national legislation in correspondence with the treaty on economic union and other commonwealth agreements and approved an initiative on Karabakh settlement put forward by the commonwealth interparliamentary group headed by Kyrgyz Parliament Chairman Meditkhan Sherimkulov. A regular meeting of the assembly's working group on the issue is scheduled for next spring in Bishkek. "The council stated deepened relations between the Commonwealth Interparliamentary Assembly and interparliamentary organizations of the world. A decision of the Interparliamentary Union to give the assembly the status of permanent observer at the Interparliamentary Union was given special attention. The assembly's council is fruitfully cooperating with the Nordic Council, the Finnish Parliament, the International Red Cross Committee and the Aland Peace Institute in joint peace-making operations," says the meeting communique. The meeting also decided to announce a contest for best designs of the assembly flag and emblem. The fourth plenary meeting is scheduled for March 17-18, 1994, in St. Petersburg.
