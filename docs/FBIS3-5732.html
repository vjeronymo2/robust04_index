Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 10 KYODO -- Pioneer Electronic Corp. said Thursday [10 March] it has agreed with Cable Television Laboratories Inc. (Cablelabs) of the United States to develop digital signal transmission technology for North American cable television systems. The agreement calls for the two parties to join hands in developing and standardizing a technique called 16VSB -- vestigal sideband -- which is suitable for high volume data transmission, the leading audiovisual equipment maker said in a press release. Digital signal transmission technology is viewed as a key element that will play a part in the next-generation digital CATV network, it said. Under the agreement, Pioneer will be responsible for development of transmission technology and special semiconductors, and Cablelabs will provide technical facilities and testing assistance, the company said. Pioneer said it expects results by the spring of 1995. Cablelabs is a research and development consortium of Cable Television System Operators representing more than 85 percent of cable subscribers in the U.S. The U.S. cable television industry is moving forward in building a next-generation CATV system for the so-called "Information Superhighway" championed by the administration of President Bill Clinton.
