BFN [Text] Several Western embassies have decided to evacuate their nationals from Serbia and Montenegro (Federal Republic of Yugoslavia) as a precaution against Serb positions in Gorazde being bombed in the next few hours and the possibility of reprisals. The United States, Great Britain, and France, whose aircraft could take part directly in the bombing, have already started the evacuation of Belgrade. As for the families of the diplomatic staff and employees of the Spanish mission in Belgrade -- a total of about 16 people -- only four of them have decided to leave the country, following the advice of the Spanish Government. As for the Spanish population of Serbia and Montenegro -- according to diplomatic sources, about 50 people, mostly the wives or children of Serbs -- they have said that they do not feel threatened at present and are not considering evacuation.
