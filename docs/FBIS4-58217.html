BFN [Text] Cairo, 2 May (MENA)--Dr. Nabil Sha'th, head of the Palestinian delegation to the Palestinian-Israeli talks aimed at reaching agreement on self-rule in Gaza and Jericho, has said the two delegations have almost completed drafting the major part of the agreement. He added that the Israeli withdrawal from self-rule areas in Gaza and Jericho will actually begin the day of the signing, Wednesday [4 May]. In press statements today, he said the remaining issues concern some security arrangements in Gaza, the maritime borders of the self-rule authority in Gaza, and the map of Gaza. He noted that discussion of these issues will be completed this evening and that the discussion on legal jurisdiction is almost over. Sha'th said the foreign ministers of Egypt, the United States, and Russia will sign the final agreement in addition to Palestinian president Yasir 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin. He affirmed that the agreement stipulates that the Israeli withdrawal will be completed within three weeks following the signing and he noted that the Israeli side has pledged to complete the withdrawal in two weeks. He said some Israeli camps will be evacuated Thursday evening or Friday morning after the signing and that the withdrawal will begin the day of the signing. He said a unit made up of 1,000 Palestinian policemen will enter Gaza and Jericho Thursday morning and that the remainder of the force will arrive within three weeks. This force is comprised of 9,000 personnel, 7,000 of whom come from outside the occupied territories and 2,000 from inside. Sha'th said that a Palestinian security delegation led by Ziyad al-Atrash will arrive in Cairo today. He noted that the Palestinian police are ready to enter Gaza and Jericho immediately after the Israeli withdrawal and denied Israeli claims to the contrary. As for the Palestinian detainees, the Palestinian official said that all points concerning the timetable of their release have been agreed upon. He said that every Palestinian, whether an individual or member of a Palestinian organization, should be bound by the agreements and pledges to which the PLO has committed itself. Commenting on the meeting of the committee in charge of detainees, Sha'th said that a meeting was held yesterday evening with Israeli General Yantof Sameh to draw up the timetable for the detainees' release. In another development, sources close to negotiations have told MENA that the points still under discussion are related to territorial waters. The Palestinians demand 12 nautical miles, while the Israelis insist on giving them only three nautical miles. As for mapping lands around roads, the Israelis want to set aside 55 meters on either side of each road that is not to be disposed of or built on. The Palestinians want this area to be 35 meters on each side. In another development, it has been learned that some 40-to-50 Palestinian deportees will return to Gaza and Jericho the day after tomorrow, Wednesday, and that 1,000 detainees will be released on Thursday, the day after the autonomy agreement is signed. It has also been learned that Israel has agreed to give the Palestinians 20 nautical miles for fishing purposes. The issue of joint patrols along parallel roads has not been settled yet.
