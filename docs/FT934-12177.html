931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / Moscow urges peacekeeping help Mr Andrei Kozyrev, the Russian foreign minister, yesterday called on the west to help Russia sustain difficult and expensive peacekeeping operations to prevent the former Soviet Union drifting into what he called a 'Yugoslav scenario', writes Anthony Robinson. Instead of criticising Russia's actions as neo-imperialist, 'we consider it high time to seriously consider the possibility of sharing this peacekeeping burden with Russia,' he said in a speech at London's Royal Institute of International Affairs.
