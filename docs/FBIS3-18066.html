Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Majority Want Mammi' Law Changed, Poll Says"] [Text] Milan, 22 Feb (ANSA) -- A majority of Italians want to repeal a controversial 1988 broadcasting law which took the name of then posts minister Oscar Mammi', according to a Directa telephone poll of over 1,000 people taken on February 19-21 in 76 small, middling and large Italian towns. Posed the question "if there was a referendum to abrogate the Mammi' law which assigns three TV networks to Silvio Berlusconi's group, would you vote to abrogate it or not to abrogate it?," 56.4 percent of the sample said they were pro-repeal and 43.6 percent anti-repeal. 6.4 percent expressed no preference. Directa said the most pro-repeal were males in general (57.3 percent), 35-to-54-year-olds, and inhabitants of northern and central Italy (58.3 percent). It added that the sample is representative of the adult population in terms of geographical area, sex, age, and socio- professional class. The maximum statistical margin of error was estimated at 4 percent.
