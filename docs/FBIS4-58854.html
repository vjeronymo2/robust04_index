BFN [Text] Rival Yemeni armies battled for a sixth day today in a bloody confrontation shredding the volatile state's three-year-old unity. A Yemeni military spokesman in Sanaa has said that fierce fighting was under way in the southern provinces of Aden and Lahij. The spokesman said that the four brigades of the northern army were advancing on Aden and predicted the city would fall today. But in a radio broadcast, the south denied that northern forces had reached the outskirts of Aden where a general mobilization had been ordered to defend the city. Meanwhile, the English language YEMEN TIMES, published in Sanaa, reported that 12,000 people had been already killed or wounded in the fighting, but it gave no source. On the other hand, Western embassies in Sanaa said that they were planning further evacuations today. About 230 British and Commonwealth nationals were expected to leave. The United States, Germany, Italy, and French Embassies had similar plans, but gave no numbers.
