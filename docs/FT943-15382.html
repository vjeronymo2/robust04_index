940709 FT 09 JUL 94 / Economic Diary: Forward Events TODAY: European Union social affairs ministers meet in Dortmund. American Festival Japan '94 begins in Tokyo (till Aug 31). Li Peng, Chinese prime minister, visits Rom-ania. Nordic prime ministers' meeting in Kerimaki, Finland. TOMORROW: Final day of G-7 summit. Bill Clinton, US president, visits Bonn. Run-off round of Ukrainian presidential election. MONDAY: EU economic and finance ministers meet in Brussels. Final day for acceptance of nominations in Colombo for elections on Aug 16. Nigeria's constitutional conference re-convenes in Abuja. UK producer price index numbers (June). TUESDAY: Seven Central American foreign ministers conference in Taipei. Germany's supreme court to rule on whether Bonn can take on global military role for United Nations missions. Seminar opens in Kuala Lumpur on Malaysian laws and economic development. Middle East peace negotiations multilateral steering group meets in Tabarka, Tunisia. Tim Wirth, US undersecretary of State, speaks at Washington National Press Club. UK construction: new orders (May). WEDNESDAY: Symposium in Tokyo on Japan and US: Challenges for the 21st century. UK retail prices index (June). UK labour market statistics (May-Jun/prov). Bank of England capital issues and redemptions (June). THURSDAY: Boutros Boutros-Ghali, UN secretary-general, visits Sweden. One-day national strike by employees of India's public sector. Eurocorps, including German troops, parade on Champs-Elysees on French national day. Service of thanksgiving at Westminster Abbey for late Labour leader John Smith. Marriage in London of Lady Sarah Armstrong, the Queen's niece, and Daniel Chatto. UK machine tools (May). FRIDAY: EU summit in Brussels to discuss new president of European Commission. Meetings of EU environment ministers in Dresden, and culture ministers in Wuerzburg, Germany. UK usable steel production (June).
