940108 FT 08 JAN 94 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Plunge in Bangkok leads region's losses Last minute arbitrage buying pushed up share prices, and the Nikkei closed above the 18,000 level for the first time since December 17, writes Emiko Terazono in Tokyo. The 225-issue index rose 242.02 to 18,124.01 after a low of 17,787.48 and a high of 18,131.41. The Topix index of all first section stocks rose 11.75 to 1,481.02. Trading was dull, with financial institutions and corporate investors taking small lot profits. However, a rise in the futures market in the afternoon triggered a late flurry of buying. US mutual and pension funds were placing buying orders in laggard stocks. Volume totalled 300m shares against 362m. Advances outpaced declines by 640 to 381, with 148 unchanged. In London, the ISE/Nikkei 50 index rose 6.90 to 1,237.12. Afternoon reports that the government was considering reducing the securities trading tax as part of the emergency economic package also generated buying. Telecommunications related stocks continued to rise on the government's plans to refurbish the country's telecom system to allow multi-media access, revealed on Thursday. NEC, the most active issue of the day, rose Y56 to Y962 and NTT by Y8,000 to Y787,000. Drug and bio-technology issues were higher on reports that the government will provide financial aid to developing countries facing the spread of the Aids virus. Ajinomoto, a foods manufacturer developing an anti-Aids drug, rose Y30 to Y1,360. Some export oriented stocks, which have been supported by foreign buying lost ground on profit taking. Sony fell Y40 to Y5,750. In Osaka, the OSE average rose 95.95 to 19,851.16 in volume of 31.3m shares. Roundup Aggressive profit-taking led to sharp falls across the Pacific Rim as Kleinwort Benson added its voice to those strategists advising clients to shift their investments. BANGKOK saw heavy selling by institutions and individuals and the SET index plunged 92.51 or 5.5 per cent to 1,575.43 in hefty turnover of Bt33.74bn. KUALA LUMPUR gave in to profit-taking for the second straight day and the composite index fell 4.6 per cent, taking the cumulative two day fall to 10.1 per cent. The composite index gave up 57.96 to 1,190.73 for a 6.6 per cent fall over the week. MANILA shed 4.1 per cent as profit-taking took hold after PDLT's Dollars 3 fall to Dollars 76 1/2 on Wall Street. The composite index fell 133.72 to 3,159.56. TAIWAN plunged 3.9 per cent on heavy profit-taking in a correction from a bull run which saw the index soar 23 per cent in the previous eight sessions. The index opened slightly higher but soon fell and ended 253.93 down at 6,200.59, still 2.1 per cent up on the week. HONG KONG suffered a 3.3 per cent fall as local and overseas investors took profits, for a cumulative two day decline on 9.6 per cent. The Hang Seng index fell 373.02 to 11,001.48, after a day's low of 10,611.73, in record turnover of a provisional HKDollars 15.23bn. The market shed 9.2 per cent in the week. The futures market also was down sharply with the January contract down 260 at 10,880. AUSTRALIA share market fell for the first time in more than two weeks, as investors took profits in blue chip issues. The All Ordinaries index closed 14.3 down at 2,186.0, 0.6 per cent higher over the week. SINGAPORE closed lower for the third straight day, and the Straits Times Industrials index fell 21.86 to 2,366.40, 2.4 per cent lower on the week, but well above the intra-day low of 2,304.62. JAKARTA closed lower in slow late trading, with profit-taking dampening sentiment. The market index fell 1.63 to 607.69 but was still 3.2 per cent higher this week. BOMBAY surged as foreign investors and domestic institutions bought a wide spectrum of shares after the exchange fixed carry forward costs at a less than expected 2.0-2.25 percent. The BSE-30 index rose 152.73 or 4.3 per cent to 3,662.20. SEOUL moved higher for the third consecutive session in spite of selling pressure from the Korea Stock Market Stabilisation Fund which eclipsed an early advance. The composite stock index added 2.08 to 895.25. NEW ZEALAND closed at a 51-month high, the NZSE-40 capital index rising 25.34 to 2,224.16, up 1.6 per cent on the week.
