BFN [Text] Cairo, 7 Apr (MENA)--The Sudanese Ummah Party has announced that the Sudanese security agencies rearrested al-Sadiq al-Mahdi, leader of the party and former prime minister, 24 hours after releasing him, without giving any reasons. The party said in a statement in Cairo today that the day before yesterday, security authorities asked al-Mahdi about the party's activities at home and abroad, his 'Id al-Fitr sermon, and his continuing refusal to reconcile with the ruling regime. The statement said: "The arrest reaffirms the confusion and tension experienced by the regime after losing every justification for survival." The statement appealed to the world community, human rights organizations, and Arab and non-Arab media to pressure the Sudanese regime to release al-Mahdi and all Sudanese prisoners immediately.
