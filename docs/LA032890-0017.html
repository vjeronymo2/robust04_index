March 28, 1990, Wednesday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: OXNARD;

THIEVES STAB MAN, TAKE HIS GROCERIES

An Oxnard man who was stabbed in front of a market by two men who took his two bags of groceries was in fair condition Tuesday at St. John's Regional Medical Center, a hospital spokeswoman said.

Rafael Martinez Archila, 22, was leaving a grocery store in the 2800 block of Saviers Road when two Latino men approached him, Oxnard police Lt. Stan Myers said.

One of the men demanded money. When Archila told him that he did not have any, the man grabbed him, a fight ensued and Archila was stabbed in the abdomen, Myers said.

The men took Archila's bags of groceries, worth about $25, and drove away in a brown Chevrolet Impala, Myers said.

Police described one assailant as a 30-year-old man with a heavy build and about 5 feet, 9 inches tall. The second suspect was described as about 22 years old and 5 feet, 9 inches tall, Myers said. CAROL WATSON
