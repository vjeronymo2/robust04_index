BFN [Text] Felipe Gonzalez has ruled out early general elections, and he does not think it is necessary to reshuffle his cabinet. He said this in Cartagena de Indias, Colombia, where the fourth Latin America summit closed last night. The prime minister also said that he does not think there are enough reasons at present to submit the goverment to a confidence vote in the Cortes although he would be prepared to do so were it necessary: [Begin Gonzalez recording] I do not rule it out, but I am not giving an immediate response to that problem because it is not an immediate problem. [end recording] In statements made before starting his journey back to Madrid, Gonzalez described a possible alliance with the United Left as difficult, but said he is open to dialogue with the Popular Party although with the following warning: [Begin Gonzalez recording] Responsibility for government will belong to the government's under all circumstances. If the government cannot discharge this responsibility then it will have to make other decisions but what it will not to do is abdicate its responsibility. [end recording]
