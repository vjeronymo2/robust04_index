March 21, 1989, Tuesday, Valley Edition

DIGEST: LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF;

YOUTH SENT TO PRISON FOR 17 YEARS IN SLAYING

A reputed gang member has been sentenced to 17 years in prison for the shooting death of a 10-year-old boy near Pacoima Park.

Joaquin Garcia, 18, was also sentenced Friday to an additional three years in prison for possession of homemade knives found in his jail cell.

A San Fernando Superior Court jury convicted Garcia in July in the fatal shooting of Alejandro Salazar. The child died after a ricocheting bullet struck him in the back of the head as he played with a group of children near Pacoima Park on July 19, 1987.

Garcia testified that he had fired his .25-caliber automatic pistol to scare away two rival gang members who he believed were beating up a 14-year-old friend.

According to police, Garcia fired several shots in the direction of the gang members, one of which ricocheted off the street and struck Salazar.
