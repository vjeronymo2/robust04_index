BFN [Excerpt] Indonesia has asked the UN Security Council to take decisive action to save Gorazde, one of the UN-designated safe havens in Bosnia, from the Serbian attacks. Speaking at a formal meeting of the UN Security Council in New York, Nugroho Wisnumurti, Indonesian permanent representative to the United Nations, said the status of Gorazde and other safe havens must be upheld and the arms embargo against Bosnia-Herzegovina lifted. Nugroho said a new diplomatic initiative aimed at reaching a cease-fire in Gorazde and the entire territory of Bosnia-Herzegovina must continue so as to find a political settlement to the conflict. He also stressed the importance of ensuring the safety and independence of the UN personnel serving in Bosnia. [passage omitted]
