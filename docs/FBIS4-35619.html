BFN [Excerpts] Kuwait, 27 May -- China has called on rival parties in Yemen to halt the fighting and resort to dialogue in order to solve the crisis there through peaceful means. In a statement to KUNA on his arrival in Kuwait today at the beginning of a tour of several Arab countries, Tian Zengpei, the Chinese vice foreign minister, said that China was following with interest the development of the situation in Yemen and wished for a halt of the hostilities there. The Chinese minister, whose country is a permanent member of the UN Security Council, said that the Yemeni crisis should be solved through peaceful means in order to preserve the interests of the Yemeni people. The Chinese call comes at a time the UN Security Council members are trying to discuss the Yemeni crisis that entered its fourth week after fighting broke out between northern and southern troops on 4 May. [passage omitted] The Chinese official said that he was carrying a written message from Chinese President Jiang Zemin to the Amir of Kuwait. He added that, during his three-day visit to Kuwait, he will meet a number of Kuwaiti officials at the Foreign Affairs Ministry to discuss bilateral relations and issues of common interests.
