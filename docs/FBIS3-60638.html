Language: English Article Type:BFN ["We Have No Idea of Having Dialogue Under Pressure, DPRK FM Spokesman" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, November 29 (KCNA) -- A spokesman of the Foreign Ministry of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea reiterated that the [words indistinct] negotiated solution of the nuclear issue, "but," he stressed, "if the United States intends to nullify the principles of the DPRK-U.S. joint statement and discontinue the talks, there will be no need for us to suspend any longer the effectuation of our withdrawal from the Non-proliferation Treaty uncomfortably". The spokesman issued a statement today in connection with ill- boding moves in the United States which lay grave hurdles to the solution of the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. The statement stresses: "If the United States acts to stifle the DPRK, pressuring the dialogue partner to accept its unreasonable demands, it will produce a very dangerous consequence. "If the U.S. thinks pressure can work on the DPRK, it is a miscalculation. "The United States seems not to know yet its dialogue partner. "Our people and People's Army will never yield to anybody's pressure. "When we declared our decision to withdraw from the Nuclear Non- proliferation Treaty, we had taken into account all possible consequences, and we are fully prepared to safeguard the sovereignty of the country even if the worst event such as "sanctions" or war is imposed on us. "If the United States truly wants the solution of the nuclear issue, it must sincerely accede to the talks rather than resort to pressure such as "sanctions" and the like out of the orbit of the dialogue. "The South Korean authorities had better seek a [words indistinct] mindful that their obstructions to the progress of the DPRK-U.S. talks would precipitate their own destruction. "Dialogue is not a gift of one party to the other, and we have no idea of having dialogue under pressure. "Success or failure in the peaceful solution of the nuclear issue entirely depends on whether the United States has the willingness to renounce the policy of stifling the DPRK, or not." The spokesman recalled that the DPRK proposed a formula of package solution of the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. "If the United States accepts the DPRK-proposed formula of package solution, all problems related to the nuclear issue including the compliance with the safeguards agreement will be solved and it will not take much time," said the spokesman.
