930715 FT 15 JUL 93 / Arts: Today's Television Lee Kuan Yew, former leader of Singapore, talks to David Lomax in Business Matters which also reports on the island's business prospects. In 30 years it has developed from a ramshackle dockyard into one of the most influential business centres in the world, but the programme asks whether Lee Kuan Yew's autocratic habits will be appropriate for a new generation (7.40 BBC2). Crime Limited would be a healthier programme if the makers would stop pretending that it was performing some sort of high minded social service and admitted that it is doing just what all the other ambulance-chasing series are doing: chasing the ratings. It would be more honest to promote it in the style of the old evening paper fudge boards: 'Horrible murder: full details' (8.30 BBC1). Given that the US did not make war on Iraq because of threats to American oil supplies (the suggestion was vehemently rejected) but because a sovereign state had been invaded, how should we explain America's failure for 40 years to fight a war on behalf of Tibet? Under The Sun reports on the Chinese occupation (9.30 BBC2).
