920123 FT 23 JAN 92 / World Stock Markets (America): Late programme trading gives Dow sharp boost Wall Street US STOCK prices wavered in a narrowly mixed range for most of yesterday, as the market digested a surfeit of corporate results, but a late round of programme trading pushed the market sharply higher, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The Dow Jones Industrial Average, which retreated 30.64 on Tuesday, closed 32.42 ahead at 3,255.81 after a fairly heavy New York SE volume of 228m shares. Advances outpaced declines by 1,132 to 618. The Standard & Poor's 500 rose 5.49 to 418.13. Shares started to move up in the afternoon following a successful two-year note auction, but it was the computer-driven programmes that triggered the late rally. Baxter International lost Dollars 3/8 to Dollars 37 in heavy trading on news that the company's supply contract with Hospital Corp, valued at Dollars 250m a year, might be in jeopardy. Hospital Corp is allowing its units to accept bids for some supplies. Monsanto tumbled Dollars 2 1/8 to Dollars 67 after the big US chemical company unveiled 1991 earnings of Dollars 296m, down 45 per cent from the previous year's figure. Banc One, the strong mid-western commercial banking group, added Dollars 1 1/2 at Dollars 49 5/8 on the back of a 25 per cent rise in 1991 net profits to Dollars 529.5m. Minnesota Mining and Manufacturing receded Dollars 1 to Dollars 96 1/8 on reporting disappointing fourth-quarter earnings of Dollars 1.18 a share, against Dollars 1.34 a year earlier. The company also warned that it expects a difficult start to 1992. Browning Ferris edged Dollars 1/8 lower to Dollars 22 5/8 after posting first-quarter earnings of 26 cents a share, compared with 40 cents a year ago. Campbell Soup advanced Dollars 2 1/4 to Dollars 39 after Goldman Sachs added the stock to its recommended list. News that Conseco had agreed to buy Bankers Life & Casualty for Dollars 600m from ICH Corp lifted Conseco's stock Dollars 5 to Dollars 69 3/4 . The issue has traded in a range of Dollars 13 3/4 to Dollars 70 in the last year. On the American Stock Exchange, ICH put on Dollars 1/4 to Dollars 4 3/8 . A strong buy rating by Merrill Lynch on Delta Air Lines' stock boosted the issue Dollars 2 3/8 to Dollars 74 1/4 . Among other airlines, AMR rose Dollars 1 1/4 to Dollars 74 1/8 and UAL gained Dollars 1 3/8 to Dollars 150 7/8 . USAir firmed Dollars 7/8 to Dollars 16 3/8 . Its results were released after the market closed. Computer Associates jumped Dollars 3 1/4 to Dollars 15 1/8 after the company turned in third-quarter earnings of 43 cents a share against 35 cents a year earlier. The secondary market posted stronger gains than the primary market, with the Nasdaq composite ending 15.81 up at 620.68. Henley Group advanced Dollars 3 to Dollars 28 3/4 . The manufacturer said it was creating a board committee to consider a recapitalisation plan that would distribute Dollars 250m to shareholders. Canada TORONTO stocks rebounded from morning weakness to end firmer on balance. The composite index was finally 18.3 ahead at 3,638.8, after a day's low of 3,607.6. Rises led declines by 327 to 253 following volume of 27.9m shares, against 31.7m. Dofasco gained CDollars 1/2 to CDollars 18 1/2 . The company's Algoma Steel unit announced a major restructuring which, among other things, would give Algoma's employees 60 per cent ownership of the unit and would turn the other 40 per cent over to Algoma's creditors and preferred shareholders. Deprenyl Research was up CDollars 1/2 at CDollars 22 3/8 . The company said its board recommended a two-for-one stock split.
