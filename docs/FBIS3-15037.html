Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow March 2 ITAR-TASS -- Former Vice-President Aleksandr Rutskoy, proclaimed Russia's president last autumn by Ruslan Khasbulatov's parliament already proclaimed dismissed by Yeltsin, said a couple of days after his release from jail last week that he accepts the legitimacy of the legislature Yeltsin created in the former parliament's place. This new legislature ruled on an amnesty which freed Rutskoy, Khasbulatov and other organisers of armed resistance to Yeltsin's move to crush the old Soviet-era parliament last October. Rutskoy called for a spirit of reconciliation in Russia in a statement faxed to Russian and foreign news agencies on Wednesday. In the same statement he defended the armed resistance to Yeltsin's move last autumn.
