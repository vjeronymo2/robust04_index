BFN [Text] Shenzhen, May 22 (XINHUA) -- The Shenzhen Special Economic Zone (SEZ) in south China has overtaken Shanghai, the traditional electronics producer, and Beijing to become the leading producer and exporter, according to Wang Dianfu, chairman of the Shenzhen Electronics Trade Association. Wang, who used to head China's National Electronics Corporation before the present Ministry of Electronics was reestablished in 1993, said that 60 percent of Shenzhen's electronics products are sold outside China, leading the rest of the country, He said there are 1,500 electronics enterprises in the SEZ, producing 18 billion yuan (about two billion U.S. dollars) in annual output value. With 160,000 employees, the electronics industry in the city made 10.8 billion yuan (1.2 billion U.S. dollars) in output value, he said. Most of Shenzhen's electronics enterprises are trans-provincial enterprise groups such as Konka, Huaqiang-Sony, Hitachi, heading China in the production of such products as colour TVs, tape recorders, microcomputers, telephone switchboards and software. Among Shenzhen's 55 hi-tech enterprises, 39 are in the electronics industry, the expert said. A number of hi-tech electronics enterprises are being constructed in the city, with more shares in components and appliance related products.
