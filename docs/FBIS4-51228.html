BFN [By Y.C. Tsai] [Text] Taipei, May 25 (CNA) -- The policy-making Central Standing Committee of the ruling Kuomintang Wednesday [25 May] approved a proposal to name Liang Kuo-shu, chairman of the state-run Chiao Tung Bank, as governor of the Central Bank of China (CBC). The appointment will become final upon approval by the cabinet. Liang, 64, will succeed Samuel Shieh, whose five-year tenure expires next week. He is expected to be sworn in June 1. He previously served as chairman of Chang Hwa Commercial Bank, chairman of the First Commercial Bank, deputy governor of the Central Bank, and economics professor of National Taiwan University. The senior banker graduated from National Taiwan University in 1953 and took his master's degree from the university in 1959. He later pursued advanced studies in the United States, where he took both his master's and Ph.D. Degrees from Vanderbilt University in Nashville in 1964 and 1970 respectively. Asked to comment on his appointment, Liang pledged that he would do his best in his new job and seek transparency in the central bank's policy making process. Liang is a well known advocate of Taiwan's financial liberalization and internationalization strategy. Market forces should be key to the development of financial markets, he said.
