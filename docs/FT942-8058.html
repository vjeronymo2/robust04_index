940523 FT 23 MAY 94 / Task for Ramaphosa President Nelson Mandela's African National Congress yesterday chose its secretary-general, Mr Cyril Ramaphosa, as chairman of an assembly that will draft a permanent non-racial constitution for South Africa, Reuter reports from Cape Town. Legislator and party spokesman Arnold Stofile told reporters that Mr Ramaphosa, 41, was unanimously elected to head the 490-member Constitutional Assembly, which will sit for the first time tomorrow. The Constitutional Assembly will comprise members of the National Assembly elected in the country's first all-race polls last month and senators appointed by nine post-apartheid provincial parliaments last week.
