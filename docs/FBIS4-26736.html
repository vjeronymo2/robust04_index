BFN [Text] Shanghai, June 24 (XINHUA) -- A spokesman for the Shanghai Securities Exchange announced here today that the securities exchange has succeeded in doing on-line business linked with 12 other securities exchange centers throughout China. At a news briefing, the spokesman said that this has not only provided investors in various parts of the country with easy ways of selling and buying the securities listed at the Shanghai Securities Exchange, but also greatly raised the efficiency of the securities market and indicated that a unified securities market has taken shape in China. The spokesman said that after succeeding in doing on-line business with securities exchange centers in Fujian, Shenyang, Sichuan, Hainan, Qingdao, Hunan and Guangzhou, the Shanghai Securities Exchange has recently gone on-line with securities exchange centers in Anhui, Harbin and Beijing. It has also been one-way linked with the staq [as received] legal-person stock exchange system. It is planned that representatives of the Shanghai Securities Exchange will attend a meeting in Hangzhou on June 28 and 29 together with representatives from other exchange centers throughout China to discuss how to further promote the on-line work and develop a unified securities market.
