BFN [Text] Caracas, 6 Apr (AFP) -- Claudette Werleigh, foreign minister of Haiti's constitutional government, will explain her country's political situation to representatives of international organizations and ambassadors to the Latin American Economic System (SELA), it was reported Wednesday. SELA headquarters are in Caracas. SELA spokesmen said that representatives of the World Bank, the Inter-American Development Bank, the OAS, UNESCO, the International Organization for Immigrants, and the UNHCR will attend the meeting. The ambassadors from Barbados, Grenada, Guyana, Suriname, Trinidad and Tobago, and Jamaica will also attend. During the last SELA Latin American Council held in Caracas in October 1993, representatives of the 27 member states signed a solidarity declaration with the Haitian people expressing their "deep concern for the lack of constitutional order in that country." The Council recommended that the SELA Permanent Secretariat "follow up on the situation in Haiti and remain alert so upon restoration of constitutional rule," a cooperation program can be immediately implemented.
