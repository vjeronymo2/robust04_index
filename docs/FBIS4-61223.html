BFN [Text] Representatives of the defense-related plants which are on a standby for a strike after two days of picketing of the Government House in Moscow at the end of April are pressing for a meeting with First Vice Premier Oleg Soskovets, Aleksandr Ilyin of the industrial trade unions told Interfax. In his words, First Deputy Defense Minister Andrey Kokoshin in a telephone conversation with union leader Sergey Shuklin had said that a solution to the industry's problems rested with the Prime Minister or his First Deputy. Defense industry workers are pressing the government for clearing its debt of more than 170 billion rubles and resolving the problem of non-payments as well as pressing for tax cuts. Ilyin said that most of the industry's workforce had to go on forced leave from May 1. He spoke of stoppages being announced until May 11 at nine of ten plants in the Vladimir Region and seven out of eight plants in Nizhnyy Novgorod Region. Similar plants in Chapayevsk (Samara region) will not resume work until May 15.
