Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [By Rey Rodriguez] [Text] Rangoon, 12 Mar (PL) -- In official talks after his arrival today in this capital, Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina expressed to Ohn Gyaw, his Myanmar (former Burma) counterpart, Cuba's desire to explore new venues of bilateral cooperation. Robaina arrived in Rangoon today, the third leg of his tour of seven Asian nations that he began in Indonensia then continued to Laos. After a two-day visit in Myanmar, he will travel to Cambodia, India, Malaysia, and Vietnam. During the meeting, both sides reviewed relations, which were described as good, between the two states and projects of common interest, which they agreed will require further study. Ohn Gyaw expressed interest in the possibility of hiring Cuban health specialists, as well as in a project to develop tourism in this country. Ohn Gyaw also voiced his admiration for Cuba, which he visited last year to explore areas of cooperation, praising the social achievements of the past three decades. Robaina's visit is viewed as a reaffirmation of the friendly relations between Havana and Rangoon, despite the distance separating both countries. Robaina's agenda includes several meetings with top government leaders. Prior to his departure to Cambodia on 14 March, he is scheduled to meet Vice Admiral Maung Maung Khin, prime minister of Myanmar [title as received]. Before traveling to this capital, Robaina met in Vientiane with Laotian President Nouhak Phoumsavan, Foreign Minister Somsavat Lengsavat, the ministers of trade, nutrition, finance, and other personalities. Following a very tight agenda, Robaina has tried to accomplish the objective of his tour to southeast Asia: to promote closer relations with the region and make known the Cuban reality.
