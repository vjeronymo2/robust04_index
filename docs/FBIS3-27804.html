Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 29 (KCNA) -- Visiting U.S. religious leader Rev. Billy Graham and his party Friday visited Mangyongdae, the home village of the great leader President Kim Il-song. The guests saw with keen interest mementos hearing immortal stories about the historic old home. Concluding the visit, the pastor said President Kim Il-song was born into a poor family and grew up, cultivating his strength and courage. The guests posed for a souvenir picture in front of the old home. They also visited Kim Il-song University Friday. While going round the revolutionary museum of the university, they were told that the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il strengthened the university into a chuche-based centre of native cadre training with his outstanding idea, theory and activity. Then, the guests had a talk with teachers and students of the university. Rev. Billy Graham told them that the visit deeply impressed him and that the university is a good place. The guests visited the Three Revolution Exhibition Saturday. Earlier, they met with officials of the C.C. [Central Committee], the Korean Christians Federation.
