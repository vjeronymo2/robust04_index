BFN [Excerpts] [Announcer] A trade agreement was concluded between Slovenia and Hungary. Kata Peli Nagy reports from Ljubljana: [Peli Nagy] In six years time, Hungary and Slovenia will trade industrial products without customs duty and other trade obstacles. Although the agreement will come to force on 1 June, the process of dismantling customs levies will take place gradually. [passage omitted] Through this agreement, which was signed about an hour ago by Bela Kadar, minister of international economic relations, and Slovenian Deputy Prime Minister Davorin Kracun in the government office in Ljubljana, an unhampered economic cooperation was established between the two countries. [passage omitted] During the two years of its independence, Slovenia has increased its trade with Hungary by 20 percent. [passage omitted] The value of Slovenian exports to Hungary last year was $62 million and Hungary's exports to Slovenia was $139 million. [passage omitted]
