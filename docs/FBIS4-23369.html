BFN [Report by Alberto de Sousa in Harare] [Text] The Frontline States met in Harare yesterday and noted that explosive situations still prevail in southern Africa. As a solution, the meeting called for continued consultations and joint efforts by the countries in the region. So, a decision has been made on the future role of the Frontline grouping. President Jose Eduardo dos Santos briefed the Harare summit on Angola's political and military situation and, particularly, the Lusaka peace talks. The summit assessed UN efforts to end the Angolan dispute. The summit was pleased with the Angolan Government's decision to accept unconditionally the proposals made by the UN mediator regarding national reconciliation. The summit urged the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola to adhere to the Bicesse Accord and abide by all UN Security Council resolutions. The summit urged the international community to increase relief assistance to the Angolan people. The Frontline heads of state have also assessed the situation in Mozambique and the tragic situation in Rwanda. In addition to Angola, special attention was also given to South Africa. The summit was pleased with the successful outcome of the multiracial South African elections, which culminated in the ANC victory.
