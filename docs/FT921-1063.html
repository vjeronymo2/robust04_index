920326 FT 26 MAR 92 / Letter: London saved Sir, Vanessa Houlder fails to give credit to a justifiable case of post hoc ergo propter hoc when she writes that 'the cost savings of moving to Canary Wharf have narrowed over the past two years, as rents in some parts of central London have halved' (Property Market, March 20). Indeed, they have - thanks almost entirely to the creation of new office capacity in Docklands. In the process, London has very possibly been saved from pricing itself out of the European office rentals markets. To quote the conclusion of a recent survey: 'London must find a way to improve its perception of itself and by others more fairly to reflect its relative strengths.' Osman Streeter, Savile Club, 69 Brook Street, London W1
