930724 FT 24 JUL 93 / Briefcase, Q&A: Complicated tax rules I WAS ABROAD when the 1993/94 Budget was submitted to parliament. In due course, I saw that the tax dividend credit on shares had been reduced to 20 per cent from the previous 25. I am still not clear as to the effect this will have on my income tax. In April 1993, I had no further tax liability (as a standard 25 per cent taxpayer) on dividends received. Does the new provision mean that I will have to pay the additional 5 per cent when my annual tax assessment is raised? If your income does not exceed the basic-rate limit, you will be liable only to tax at 20 per cent on your dividends (not 25 per cent). On the other hand, higher rate-payers have to pay 40 per cent on their dividends. The effect of these new tax rules for dividends is very complicated for capital gains tax-payers (and, indeed, the rules had to be re-written at the committee stage of the Finance (No 2) Bill). They are difficult to explain in words, but we shall be happy to give you a specimen calculation if you care to suggest some likely figures of (a) UK dividends, (b) foreign dividends, (c) other income, and (d) net chargeable gains.
