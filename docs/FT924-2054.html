921215 FT 15 DEC 92 / Letter: Monarchy - political dimensions of a show that will run and run Sir, David Cannadine's article about the Royal dilemma rightly stressed constitutional implications, but was incomplete. Every country, including Britain, needs a head of state. Does anyone seriously expect a Conservative prime minister to recommend the replacement of the monarchy by a president? Or can anyone imagine John Smith as prime minister putting such a proposal to the Commons? Notwithstanding all the family problems, this show will run and run. Richard Rose, director, Centre for Study of Public Policy, University of Strathclyde, Livingstone Tower, 26 Richmond Street, Glasgow G1 1XH
