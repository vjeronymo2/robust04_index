Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The leader of the public relations center of the office of Russia's prosecutor general, Aleksandr Zvyagintsev, said that the rumors circulated lately by some news media to the effect that the Prosecutor General, Aleksey Kazannik, is to be transferred to the Constitutional Court, are "absolutely unfounded." According to him, Kazannik is engaged in routine work; he holds conferences on pressing problems every day; under his initiative his office is working on ways to improve its performance. The plans of Russia's prosecutor general "extend far into the future," Zvyagintsev stressed in an interview with INTERFAX. According to news media allegations, the man who is likely to replace Kazannik is Aleksey Ilyushenko, chief of the control department in the office of Russia's president. Members of his department have said that "these reports are nothing but unconfirmed rumors." Ilyushenko himself is currently on vacation.
