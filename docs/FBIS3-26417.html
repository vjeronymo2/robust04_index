Language: English Article Type:BFN ["U.S. Uses Submarines for Deep-Sea Spying"] [Text] New York, February (XINHUA) -- The United States Navy has long had secret submarines for deep-sea spying, it was reported here today. For decades, the U.S. Navy has operated fleet of specially equipped submarines whose secret work is to comb deep waters for military intelligence virtually unobtainable by any other means, THE NEW YORK TIMES said today. These spy submarines, as experts in naval warfare said, are the Navy's counterpart to reconnaissance satellites, but better in some respects. They said such submarines can not only examine distant objects on the ocean floor, but also in some cases retrieve or manipulate them. The objects of interest include lost ships, submarines, planes, weapons, rockets, spacecraft and nuclear warheads, as well as functioning equipment, like other countries' undersea cables and devices, THE TIMES reported. "A vivid example of the spying technique was recently given to Congress by a former senior Navy official who disclosed an early mission of one of these submarines, the Halibut, to examine a sunken Soviet submarine in the Pacific in 1968," the newspaper noted. The naval experts, who spoke on condition of anonymity, said the Halibut was the first in a series of these submarines, which actually constitute a new class of American submarine designed to lower gear-laden cables for deep reconnaissance, recovery and manipulation.
