Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Our correspondent Yevgeniy Zherebenkov reports on proceedings in the State Duma: [Zherebenkov] The first session of the lower house of parliament, the State Duma, was opened by Lukava, senior deputy and representative of the Liberal Democratic Party. Then, at the proposal of the faction of the Communist Party of Russia, the deputies stood in memory of the victims of the October 1993 events. [passage omitted: Chernomyrdin conveyed Yeltsin's greetings to the Duma and spoke on government's plans] The deputies also listened to a report by Aleksandr Ivanchenko, deputy chairman of the Central Electoral Commission, on the results of the 12 December elections, and they have now started work on the formation of temporary working bodies, a temporary secretariat, a tellers' commission, and a commission for monitoring electronic voting.
