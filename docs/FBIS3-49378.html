Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava Jan 5 (CTK) -- The strategy of the leaders of Hungarian minority parties in Slovakia is unambiguous and will create an atmosphere of mistrust, intolerance and hatred among citizens, Slovak Parliament Deputy Chairman Augustin Marian Huska said at today's meeting of the coalition Movement For a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS) and Slovak National Party (SNS). "Dreams of a greater, or even of a `middle-sized' Hungary cannot be fulfilled," Huska said, adding that it was not possible for the government not to have contingency plans in case the state should be threatened. He expressed the conviction that Slovakia would be able to preserve its territorial integrity. Slovak Minister of Culture Dusan Slobodnik said that "there is no government more tolerant than the current Slovak one." Slovak Deputy Premier Sergej Kozlik called on Slovaks to express their dissatisfaction over attempts by the Hungarian minority to achieve autonomy in southern Slovakia. "It is time for parliament to consider passing a law on the protection of the Republic," Slobodnik said, adding that among the Hungarian minority there were still "many decent people who want to live peacefully in Slovakia." The worsening of the situation in Slovakia is tied to a demand made by the Association of Towns and Villages of Zitny Island, which is calling for autonomy for southern Slovakia from Bratislava to the Ukrainian border. An assembly to discuss the demand is to be held on January 8 in Komarno, southern Slovakia.
