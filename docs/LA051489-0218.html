May 14, 1989, Sunday, Orange County Edition

TEACHERS DESCRIBE A YOUNG MAN WHO ALWAYS KNEW WHO HE WAS

As former teachers of Julian Cummings, we read with great pleasure and pride your account (May 3) of his achievements at UC Berkeley. Even as a junior high school student, Julian knew who he was and what he wanted in a remarkably mature sense, so all that he has accomplished in the years after he left us comes as little surprise.

Ironically, the wording of the article's title, "Honors Graduate Had an Uphill Fight," revived one of our fondest memories of Julian. As an eighth-grader participating in a study trip to Yosemite, Julian wanted to have the same experiences as anyone else visiting the park. To realize this desire, he selected many activities that would challenge his physical abilities, one of which was a hike to the top of Yosemite Falls. Although we all admired his determination, we doubted such an arduous undertaking fell within the range of his ability.

As usual, however, Julian knew himself better than we did. When he and his father finally returned from their successful climb -- long after everyone else had come back to camp and well past the time we had begun to become concerned -- we were privileged to share a triumph with him and his parents that is unique in all our years of Yosemite experiences.

As a result, we've known all along that "uphill battles" are challenges that Julian meets with purpose and effectiveness. We congratulate him on his outstanding achievements and wish him every happiness and success in the future. Julian Cummings will always have a special place in our teaching memories.

JOY DE GRUCCIO

JAY DU VAL

ROY COLLIER
