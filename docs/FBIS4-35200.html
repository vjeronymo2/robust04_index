BFN [Text] Gaza, Jerusalem Press -- For the first time since its inception outside the occupied territories on 1 January 1965, Fatah -- the Palestinian National Liberation Movement -- has publicly opened the door of membership to its ranks. In a statement, Fatah said the need to rebuild its structures is the main reason why it is inviting the Palestinians in the Gaza Strip to join up. Fatah operated as a secret organization until the signing of the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles on 13 September 1993. The statement, which was signed by the Fatah Mobilization and Reorganization Department, announced the opening of several offices in the Gaza Strip to enable Palestinians to join the movement.
