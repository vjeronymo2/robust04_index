Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, March 22, IRNA -- Iran's Foreign Ministry today denied allegations that the Iranian C-130 plane which crashed near Karabakh region was on a spying mission. ITAR-TASS news agency had earlier indicated that there was a possibility that C-130 was involved in a spying mission. Mahmud Mohammadi, a spokesman for Iran's Foreign Ministry, told IRNA today that his ministry categorically rejected such allegations. "It is a pity that at a time when the people of Iran are mourning the death of the victims of this crash, such baseless and false reports are broadcast by ITAR-TASS news agency," Mohammadi said. The C-130 plane en route from Moscow to Tehran was carrying the family members of the Iranian foreign service associated with the Iranian diplomatic mission in Moscow.
