Karuppaswamy] [Text] The Indo-German relations and cooperation in industry and scientific development has a new vigor with the visit of the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, to Germany. The Germans also take cognizance of the importance of such a visit when Germany is renewing its outside endeavor and enagement after two and a half years of internal problem of unification. Businessmen and industrialists along with the government were very busy during the last two years. Inactivities concerned with creating parity between the two Germanys radically opposed with each in everything except the geographical contiguity artificially separated by a manmade wall [sentence as heard]. Germany is now looking for new grounds for investing and adequate return for their investment. The economic reforms toward globalization of market in India has made Germany immensely interested in collaboration. Mr. Narasimha Rao's assurance to the German industrialists that India is pressing ahead with economic reforms is a reassurance to many of them for removing their hesitation to open new units or financing Indian projects. The creation of an Asian-Pacific Committee of German Industry is a major step toward consolidating Indo-German cooperation. The low labor cost and skill of workers and the fairly unexploited market in India is the most potential market for German industrialists. The meeting between the prime minister and the federal minister for economic development and the German cochairman of the Indo-German Consultative Group in Bonn has paved the way for narrowing down some of the constraints. India's largest trading partner and market for Indian export in Europe will further invite Indian exports of silk, textiles, leather, basic chemicals, precious stones and engineering item. On their side, investment in lignite-based power plants, joint ventures like power equipment and scientific and institutional establishments are some of the added German ventures in India contemplated immediately. The German Investment Development Company, which has an office in Bombay, is accelerating its involvement in speeding up implementing the 2,500 approved projects for collaboration. In all, Germany is expected to exploit the scheme with the potentials of 75,000 rupees. Politically, the Indo-German relations are always cordial and consistent with adequate understanding in international fora. Germans are well aware of the fact that India was one among the first to establish diplomatic relations with Germany after the withdrawal of allied forces. The unequivocal stand of Germany on developments in Kashmir and its neutrality speaks of its noninterference in other country's affairs. Such friendly attitude and closer economic ties are bound to reflect in Indo-German growth as viable economic powers.
