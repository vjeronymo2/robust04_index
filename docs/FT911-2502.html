910430 FT 30 APR 91 / UK Company News: Corporate side buoys Wensum THE RESILIENCE of its corporate clothing business helped Wensum, the clothing company, report a 17 per centrise, from Pounds 635,000 to Pounds 746,000, in annual pre-tax profits. The improvement for the year to January 26 1991 came on turnover of Pounds 8.86m (Pounds 7.55m) which included a 33 per cent sales increase in its corporate wear division. This side of the business was not subject to the same degree of slowdown seen on the retail side, said Mr Andrew Hughes, chairman. Operating profit in thecorporate clothing business was up to Pounds 472,000 (Pounds 311,000). Although deemed satisfactory, results for the clothing side were not as buoyant. Turnover was up by 9 per cent to Pounds 5.33m and operating profit by 7 per cent to Pounds 490,000 (Pounds 458,000). Gearing was reduced from 36 per cent to 13 per cent. Earningsper share rose 13 per cent to 6.62p (5.85p) and a final dividend of 2.35p is recommended, making a total of 3.5p. The company was floated in July 1989.
