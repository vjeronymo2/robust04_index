910430 FT 30 APR 91 / Poland cuts base rate POLAND'S base interest rate will be cut tomorrow to 59 per cent from 72 per cent. It was increased from 55 per cent in January as part of the government's tight-money policy to combat annual inflation of 250 per cent at the end of 1990. The government is now expecting 55 per cent inflation this year against an earlier forecast of 36 per cent. The privatisation minister, Mr Janusz Lewandowski, said yesterday that the country's next public share offer would open around May 20 with the sale of the Swarzedz furniture factory near Poznan. Five per cent of the shares are to be given to three former owners of workshops taken over after the war to set up the factory. The ministry is also expected to sign an agreement at the same time selling 51 per cent of the Polkolor television works to Thomson of France for Dollars 35m.
