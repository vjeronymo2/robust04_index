940527 FT 27 MAY 94 / International Company News: Burma fund planned by HK group Kerry Securities, the Hong Kong-based securities group, is to launch an investment fund focusing on Burma, Reuter reports from Hong Kong. It hopes to raise USDollars 50m to Dollars 100m through Myanmar (Burma) Fund, a closed-end, direct investment fund, according to Mr Richard Neville, director of corporate finance. A total of 20m shares, targeted at institutional investors, will be issued initially at Dollars 10 each. The minimum subscription is Dollars 100,000. The fund will be launched in the first week of July and remain open throughout the month. It will be domiciled in the Channel Islands and a listing will be sought in Dublin. Kerry Investment Management will manage the fund. Irrawaddy Advisors, a private non-governmental agency, will be investment adviser. The fund will investing initially in tourism-related projects, infrastructure and industrial parks in Burma. Its key strategy would be co-investments with domestic entrepreneurs or overseas Myanmar or Chinese business groups. Kerry Financial Services is controlled by Malaysian tycoon Mr Robert Kuok and Malayan United Industries.
