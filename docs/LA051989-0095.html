May 19, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

Correction Appended

MORNING REPORT: STAGE

Judy Davis, playing the title role in "Hapgood" at the Ahmanson Theatre, was forced to leave at the end of the second act Wednesday night because of voice problems. She replaced by understudy Babbie Green. Davis earlier had received medical treatment for a throat ailment, and thought she was well enough to perform, said theatre spokesman Tony Sherwood. "She went on, thought she could get through it and lost her voice," he said. She missed Thursday's matinee performance, but was expected to be back on stage Thursday night, Sherwood said. Davis was the star of the Australian film, "My Brilliant Career."
