Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Paraguay monitored through 6 January. On 30 December 1993, Central Bank President Jacinto Estigarribia Mallada reported that the Consumer Price Index increased 1.2 percent in December and the total inflation rate for 1993 was 20.4 percent. In 1992 and 1991 it was 17.8 percent and 11.8 percent, respectively. He also said the GDP registered a real increase of 3.7 percent and the trade balance deficit was $606 million. He added that net international reserves total $700 million, 15-percent higher than December 1992. He said the net balance of the foreign public debt was approximately $1.227 billion, a drop of 1.8 percent compared with 1992. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 31 Dec 93 p 21) On 4 January, the government announced the readjustment of public services rates. Electricity increased 16.6 percent; water, 40 percent; telephone bills, 44 percent; and the minimum salary, 15 percent. The official price of fuel was increased as follows: regular gasoline is now 760 guaranies per liter, a 14-percent increase; premium gasoline is now 840 guaranies per liter, a 10.5-percent increase; and gas oil is now 550 guaranies per liter, a 14.5-percent increase. (Asuncion ULTIMA HORA in Spanish 4 Jan 94 p 1)
