930507 FT 07 MAY 93 / London Stock Exchange: BP basks in market approval MUCH BETTER first quarter figures than expected from British Petroleum vindicated the aggressively bullish stance on the shares adopted by most US and some UK brokers in the wake of the UK oil company decision last year to chop its dividend and jettison its chairman. BP shares were the second best individual performer in the FT-SE 100 index, rising around 3.4 per cent. Historic cost net income of Pounds 325m, well above a general consensus of forecasts around the Pounds 200m mark, and replacement cost net income of Pounds 231m, against forecasts of around Pounds 220m, prompted a surge of buying interest from UK and overseas brokers. At the close BP shares were 10 higher at 306p, with turnover reaching a healthy 9.1m shares. Long term bears of the stock, however, while acknowledging the good results, continued to point to the group's fundamental problems. Mr John Toalster at SGST said he would upgrade his current year forecasts for the company but maintained that the group was 'still shrinking, offered a mediocre return on capital employed and remained highly geared; switch into Shell.' Other analysts, although impressed by the latest profits, pointed to the company's cash flow position. The first quarter saw cash inflow of Pounds 600m but only after the sale of Pounds 675m worth of assets; 'the group is still leaking cash, and the figures were embellished by a write back of petroleum revenue tax.'
