930406 FT 06 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television There are two good movies tonight, though naturally they clash, so if you want to catch both you will have to tape one. Witness, (9.30 BBC1) a 1985 thriller, takes an American Amish community as its background; an idea which might have seemed contrived but works well. Harrison Ford gives probably his best performance as the detective trying to protect one of the community's women, and her son who witnesses a murder. Paris Texas, directed by Wim Wenders is more difficult to describe: it is about a man who loses himself, metaphorically and literally, and - perhaps - finds himself again. He also finds his wife, played by Nastassja Kinski (yet another role which scarcely begins to stretch her) working in a Texas peep show. Sam Shepard's script writing clearly worked well for Wenders (10.00 C4). Both films are concerned with the difficulty of sustaining individuality in modern American society. Howard Jacobson, flavour of the month thanks to 'Roots Schmoots', argues in Without Walls that Judas Iscariot was misjudged (9.00 C4).
