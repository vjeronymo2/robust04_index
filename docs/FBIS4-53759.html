BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, May 20 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- The Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) approved 240 projects involving an investment of RM [Ringgit Malaysia] 5,838.8 million (US$1: RM2.70) in the first four months of this year. Minister Rafidah Aziz said Friday that the figure was an improvement on the 231 projects involving RM2,930.3 million for the same period last year. Speaking at the launching of Teac's one millionth floppy disc in Batu Berendam, Melaka, 140 km to the south of here, she said, 11 projects had investments in excess of RM100 million while 229 had less than the amount. She said 62 companies were owned by foreigners, 66 by locals and 109 were owned jointly, adding that by sector, electronics had the largest number of investors both local and foreign with 63 projects involving RM690.08 million. Rafidah also said that there were five companies nationwide producing floppy discs with a total annual output of 19 million units. From this figure, 80 per cent was exported to the United States, Japan and Korea while the remainder was for local use.
