920318 FT 18 MAR 92 / Unions seek end to Civil Service pay gap THE GAP between the pay of senior civil servants and comparable white-collar workers in the private sector is no longer justifiable, according to unions basing their claims on a report on job security by Coopers & Lybrand Deloitte, the consultancy firm. Senior civil servants have traditionally been paid less than their private-sector counterparts, in part because they are less likely to lose their jobs and face long periods of un-employment. Their remuneration is now the subject of the most comprehensive review since the mid 1980s. It is expected that the Top Salaries Review Body will recommend significant increases when it reports later in the year. According to Coopers, in a report commissioned by the FDA, NUCPS and IPMS unions, there is ample evidence of a higher degree of turbulence and job-changing in the private sector, and the recession is sharpening the difference. The report says: 'However, it is important to recognise that not all turbulence is synonymous with insecurity, and not all insecurity is synonymous with hardship.' Job changes, the report says, reflect a desire by private-sector managers for wide experience and head-hunting by employers. 'Even in the midst of recession the length of time on average between jobs does not seem to outweigh the length of time over which financial support is available.'
