BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- On Wednesday evening [13 April], Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel received the leader of the Iraqi Kurds, Jalal Talabani, for talks during which the situation of the Kurds in this Arab country and their struggle for autonomy were focused on. Talabani, who is the secretary general of the "Patriotic Union of Kurdistan" (PUK), which is opposed by Baghdad, is currently trying to get more support by traveling through severla countries. Since the Gulf War in 1991 Germany has provided the Kurds in Iraq mainly with humanitarian aid, worth 400 million German marks. German state bodies and private organizations are operating a common coordination office for German aid in the town of Irbil. Recently, German journalist Lissy Schmidt was killed in this region; media have cited the Iraqi Government as the one that "ordered" the killing. On Tuesday, Talabani confirmed in Bonn that his PUK has apprehended two of the suspected killers.
