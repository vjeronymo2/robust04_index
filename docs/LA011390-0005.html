January 13, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

REBUILDING THE COLISEUM FOR THE L.A. RAIDERS

The mayor of Los Angeles has formed a committee of businessmen and others to keep professional football in the city.

The other California cities have gotten the bidding for a sports franchise into the $200-million range, and the end is apparently not in sight. The enlightened civil leaders seem hellbent on providing an area for a handful of athletes while muddling about on what steps to take to provide adequate housing for the working poor. Let the games begin.

RUSS MARLETT

San Diego
