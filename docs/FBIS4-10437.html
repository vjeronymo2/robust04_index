BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina, who is visiting several South American nations, will meet on 13 April with President Carlos Saul Menem at the beginning of a two-day official trip to Argentina. Robaina stopped briefly in Argentina over the weekend and met with Argentine Foreign Minister Guido di Tella to discuss international and bilateral issues. Robaina already visited Uruguay and Peru. According to the official agenda, Robaina will meet on 13 April with Menem following a meeting with the plenum of the Argentine Senate, headed by Eduardo Menem. Robaina also will conduct a courtesy visit to interim Foreign Minister Fernando Petrella since Di Tella is abroad, and will lay a wreath at the monument of national liberator Jose de San Martin. Also on 13 April, the Buenos Aires City Council will confer on Robaina the title of Illustrious Visitor. On 14 April, Robaina will give an address on investment and trade with Cuba to members of Argentina's Chamber of Commerce and Industry. Robaina will also meet with Deputy Economy Minister Carlos Sanchez and will conduct a second work meeting at the Argentine Foreign Ministry. Robaina's trip to Argentina will conclude with a public event organized by friendship and solidarity groups, which demand respect for Cuba's self-determination and lifting of the U.S. blockade of Cuba.
