930330 FT 30 MAR 93 / Companies to promote London created LONDON FORUM, the private-sector body set up to promote London as a business location and tourist centre, is to create two organisations to attract inward investment and expand tourism, Sir Allen Sheppard, Forum chairman and chairman of Grand Metropolitan, announced yesterday. London Inward will encourage business investment with emphasis on persuading international businesses to locate their headquarters in the capital. Led by Sir Colin Marshall, British Airways chairman, it plans to take over the First Stop Shop created by the City Corporation, Westminster City Council and London Docklands Development Corporation to provide information for inward investors. London Visitors will try to raise income from tourism by developing existing markets and targeting new markets in Japan, the European Community and the US. It hopes to merge with the London Tourist Board. Mr Michael Howard, environment secretary, said London needed to fight harder in world markets to maintain and raise its share of future opportunities. He added: 'We must all make the most of every opportunity to tell the world what London has to offer.' The deputy chairmen of the London Forum board are: Sir Hugh Bidwell, chairman London Tourist Board; Sir Colin Marshall, chairman British Airways; Sir Brian Wolfson, chairman Wembley. The other members of the London Forum board are: Sir Christopher Bland, chairman LWT; Mr Rocco Forte, chairman Forte; Mr Nigel Fox Bassett, senior partner Clifford Chance; Mr Harvey Goldsmith, director Allied Entertainments; Mr David Goldstone, chairman Regalian Properties; Ms Jan Hall, chief executive of Coley Porter Bell; Mr Anthony Hilton, managing director, Evening Standard; Sir Brian Jenkins, senior partner, Coopers & Lybrand and former Lord Mayor of London; Mr Keith Oates, managing director, Marks & Spencer; Mr Brian Pearce, chairman Pearce Signs; Sir Idris Pearce, consultant Richard Ellis; Mr Ian Reeves, chairman Highpoint; Mr Maurice Saatchi, chairman Saatchi & Saatchi; Sir David Scholey, chairman SG Warburg; Mr Clyde Williams, chief executive WWA.
