Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Groznyy January 9 TASS -- Russia and the Chechen Republic agreed on Sunday to have a joint defence system. A draft agreement to this effect was signed by the Russian and Chechen Defence Ministries, the CHECHENPRESS news agency said. Colonel Musa Merzhuyev, Chechen President Dzhokhar Dudayev's representative in the republic's Armed Forces main staff, said that under the agreement Russia will provide the Chechen Army with arms, equipment and ammunition, ensure their servicing and maintenance at its plants and train officers. It has also pledged not to use its Armed Forces against the Chechen Republic. In turn, the Chechen Republic will participate in the joint defence system to repel possible aggressions and provide its air bases to Russian Air Force units in wartime. Additionally, it will take part in joint military manoeuvres and war games and will not join any military alliances or blocs aimed against Russia. The draft agreement also stipulates that in peacetime the Chechen Republic will have an army not bigger than 1.5 per cent of its population.
