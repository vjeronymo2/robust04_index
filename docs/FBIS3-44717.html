Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, January 7 (XINHUA) -- Shanghai city in east China has formulated a plan for accelerating development on coastal waters. The plan notes that Shanghai now has a land area of 6,340.5 square kilometers, but its coastal waters cover 7,226 square kilometers. "The Shanghai Marine Development Plan" constitutes a major part of Shanghai's current urban planning, it says. By the year 2000, the proportion of the gross output value of the marine economy in Shanghai in the total output value in the city will rise by 0.5 to 1 percent [figures as received]. The focus of the development plan is on the coastal belts and nearby waters, involving opening up of oil and gas projects, manufacturing of oceanic medicines, underwater projects and surveying for mineral resources on the sea floor.
