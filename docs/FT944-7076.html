941126 FT 26 NOV 94 / Company failures continue to fall Company failures continued to fall in the third quarter, figures from KPMG Peat Marwick, the accountancy firm, show. A total of 6,140 bankruptcies was recorded in the quarter, a fall of 14.4 per cent compared with the same period last year. Liquidations fell 15.3 per cent to 3,623. The recovery recorded by KPMG varies across the country. The north-east saw a fall of nearly 20 per cent in liquidations between the second and third quarter while the Midlands and north-west saw increases. Mr Tim Hayward, head of corporate recovery at KPMG, said: 'A year ago the recovery was at a very fragile point. There is a more robust feeling in the economy now and companies, with the support of their banks, are gearing up for an improving economy.'
