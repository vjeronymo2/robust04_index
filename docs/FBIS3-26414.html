Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 7 (XINHUA) -- The General Motors Corporation (GM) and its two Chinese counterparts plan to set up a joint venture producing a new kind of motor engine controller. The venture will produce 300,000 such controllers a year and the output could hit 600,000 when the market demand increases. The new engine controlling system cuts fuel consumption by 5 to 10 percent while decreasing waste gas by 60 to 90 percent, compared with the old system. China has listed the system as a key national project in the Eighth Five-Year Plan period and the Ninth Five-Year Plan. The two Chinese corporations are the Beijing Wanyuan Industrial Corporation and the Beijing Economic Investment and Development Corporation. The new venture, Beijing Wanyuan-General Motor Electronic-Control Co. Ltd., will start with a 30 million U.S. dollar investment, to be followed by another 120 million U.S. dollars. The new venture will use the technology of the American vehicle manufacturing giant.
