April 12, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

BUSH TO UNDERGO ROUTINE PHYSICAL

President Bush, described as in excellent health, will undergo a routine annual physical examination today at Bethesda Naval Hospital, the White House announced Wednesday.

Bush, who jogs regularly and plays tennis, continues to suffer from a mild case of arthritis in his hips, a slight hearing loss sustained during his flight days in World War II and allergies related to bee stings and seasonal changes. The exam will conducted under the direction of the White House physician, Dr. Burton Lee.
