BFN [Text] Athens, May 3, IRNA -- The German Interior Ministry has threatend to expel the official spokesman of Algeria's Islamic Salvation Front (FIS) in Europe, Rabeh Kabir, from the country if he issues any press statements or even gives telephone interviews, a source close to Kabir told IRNA Tuesday. Kabir was given political asylum in Germany in February by the asylum authorities but the Interior Ministry has challenged the decision and the matter is in court now. "Rabeh Kabir has to renew his stay permit every week or two. We fear that he may be expelled from Germany," said the source speaking to IRNA by phone from Germany. "We are the representavites of the Algerian people but we are not allowed to speak. Where is the freedom of press and freeedom of opinion which they proclaim in Europe?" he asked. The source requested anonymity. German authorities had held Kabir in custody for three months last year, but he was set free after a court rejected an extradition request by the Algerian regime.
