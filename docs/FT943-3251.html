940915 FT 15 SEP 94 / World Trade News: India to liberalise consumer imports The Indian government said yesterday it plans to liberalise import of consumer goods, and warned industries to prepare for stiff foreign competition, AP reports from New Delhi. High import tariffs on consumer items such as television sets, refrigerators, music equipment, soaps and cosmetics have shielded Indian producers over the years. 'The time has come to tell industry that the government cannot afford to provide a high level of protection to Indian industry, particularly in the consumer goods sector, for an indefinite period,' the finance minister, Mr Manmohan Singh, told businessmen.
