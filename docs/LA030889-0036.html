March 8, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

SUPREME COURT ON CHILD ABUSE

Bravo! Rehnquist has finally said it! No hedging, no beating around the Bush. The Supreme Court rules that "Nothing in the language of the due process clause itself requires the state to protect the life, liberty and property of its citizens against invasion." Yes, ladies and gentlemen, it's loud and clear: The Administration is washing its hands.

If a policeman walks by while you are clobbered on the street, and he doesn't move a finger to protect you, under the new ruling you can't sue him or anybody else. If the possibly exonerating evidence in your trial is "lost" by the officials (biased or not), you have only yourself to blame. Yes, you are on your own.

Naturally, you still have to pay your taxes; the Constitution does not protect you from the IRS, but whether or not the services in return will be forthcoming is left to the benevolence of the state agencies. See if they feel like it. If, in the midst of a disintegrating welfare system, skyrocketing medical and insurance costs, inflation, bailouts, homelessness, drugs and cultural deprivation, you still had any doubts as to whose interest this Administration is representing, set them aside now. Just look up and count your thousand points of light!

ENDRE HULES

Van Nuys
