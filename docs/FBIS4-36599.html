BFN [Text] The United Nations has announced the postponement of the voter identification operation with regard to the self-determination referendum in Western Sahara. Our correspondent has more details: [Unidentified correspondent] Voter identification for those who are to take part in the self-determination referendum in Western Sahara, which was supposed to begin today in Laayoune, has been postponed until next week for logistical reasons. The United Nations announced the news from Rabat today, confirming that the transportation of Moroccan and Saharan notables to registration locations raised some technical problems. According to a UN-Moroccan joint statement signed at the end of May in Rabat by the Moroccan interior minister and Erik Jenssen, acting special envoy of the UN secretary general, all Saharans concerned with the referendum had been informed that voter identification would begin today in Laayoune.
