Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Test] Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs, said that the meetings of the Arab League Council, which ended in Cairo yesterday, tackled the issues of Arab solidarity and reconciliation within the issue of pan-Arab security. This came in statements to our political affairs correspondent Shakir Maharimah upon the minister's return from Cairo this evening. [Begin recording] [Al-Hasan] Foremost of the many issues discussed by the Political Committee were the Arab-Israeli conflict, the issue of Jerusalem, and the intifadah. Jordan made a lengthy comment on this issue. We reviewed the peace process and the Jordanian stand that condemned the massacre perpetrated in the holy Ibrahimi Mosque as well as our stand on UN Security Council Resolution 904. We also reviewed His Majesty King Husayn's efforts toward this issue and the Jordanian measures aimed at expressing condemnation of the horrific massacre and at showing concern and pressuring the international community to take measures to protect the Palestinian people. [Maharimah] Your excellency, What about your bilateral contacts during the current session of the Arab League Council? [Al-Hasan] During my visit to Cairo, I held a series of bilateral contacts. I met with a number of brother Arab foreign ministers. I met with the foreign ministers of Egypt, Syria, Oman, and Yemen. I also met with the foreign minister of the Federal Islamic Republic of Comoros, the new 22d member in the Arab League Council. I also had the honor of meeting with Egyptian Prime Minister 'Atif Sidqi. We discussed bilateral issues of interest to both countries as well as a number of Arab, regional, and international issues. [end recording]
