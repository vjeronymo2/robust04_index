931220 FT 20 DEC 93 / Economist urges exports push Businesses should target high-growth markets outside Europe to help close Britain's trade gap, according to Mr David Kern, chief economist at National Westminster Bank. Mr Kern said that focusing on Asia, Latin America and North America, was vital over the next few years if Britain was to win a larger share of world exports. In NatWest's latest Economic and Financial Outlook he said that the newly industrialised economies in Asia accounted for 11.4 per cent of world imports but took just 4.7 per cent of UK exports.
