941231 FT 31 DEC 94 / Weekend Money (Companies): Directors' dealings Fenner is involved in a wide range of different businesses, writes Vivien MacDonald, of the Inside Track. Over the past 18 months, the rise in the price has been spectacular, although it has slowed recently. Since the announcement of final results at the begining of November, three directors have bought stock on the market. The non-executive chairman, Colin Cooke, has now increased his holding by a sizeable amount. The year-end report was good and three directors dealing together is usually a vote of confidence. Asprey has been in the press a fair amount over the past year, and it has not done the company much good. After weak interim results, the share price fell almost 350p to 135p. At this lower level, directors have started to pick up more stock again. JR Asprey has added further to his already considerable family trust while Naim Attallah, who wisely sold when the stock stood at 310p, has also been back to the market and has more than returned to his previous level of holding. Largest sale of the week was at Abbott Mead Vickers, the advertising group. David Abbott, chairman, and Peter Mead, chief executive, sold a total of 440,000 shares at 337p each but retain sizeable holdings.
