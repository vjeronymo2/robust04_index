BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 28 KYODO -- Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata said Tuesday [28 June] the deregulation package unveiled by the government the same day will change the nature of Japan's economy and show the direction in which the country should be heading. Speaking at the administrative reform promotion headquarters, Hata said the package will be approved by the succeeding cabinet as soon as possible. The current political scene remains mired in confusion in the wake of the Hata cabinet's resignation announcement on Saturday. The scheme, compiled by the headquarters, contains 279 deregulatory measures in the fields of real estate, financial services, information and telecommunications. The plan was prepared in line with the government's market-access program announced in March to boost imports and spur domestic demand.
