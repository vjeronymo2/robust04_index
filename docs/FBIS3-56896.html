Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Chisinau, BASAPRESS, 19/1/1994 -- President Mircea Snegur met today with inhabitants of Slobozia District and Tiraspol who plead for Moldova's territorial integrity and independence, at their demand, states the presidential press service. The participants have asked the president to permit anticipate parliamentary elections in East Moldovan district, a week or two before February 27th. They said the population could be intimidated by authorities of the so-called Dniester Republic. Referring to the situation in Dniester, Mircea Snegur said the new Parliament will have the right to grant special juridical statute to east regions and localities mostly inhabited with Gagauzians. Snegur asserted that the declarations of Russian Minister of Foreign Affairs Kozyrev on the heed to defend Russians' right in ex-Soviet republics no not concern Moldova, where Russians have same rights as the local population. This fact was confirmed by representatives of various international organizations that examined the republic's legislation, states the presidential release.
