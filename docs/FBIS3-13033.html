Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by Uri Binder, Shefi Gabay, Hemi Shalev, and Avino'am Bar-Yosef] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] Israeli political sources yesterday said that Israel will not make any more gestures toward the Palestinians unless there is an explicit PLO promise that such gestures will lead to a resumption of the talks. It has been learned that in such a case, Israel will agree to push forward the implementation of some parts of the Gaza-Jericho first agreement. Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin yesterday said that the Gaza-Jericho first agreement can be implemented in "half the time" stipulated in the Declaration of Principles. Meanwhile, political sources yesterday denied reports on contacts aimed at organizing a Rabin-'Arafat summit in the near future.
