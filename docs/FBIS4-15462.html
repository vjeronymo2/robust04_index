BFN [Text] Moscow April 7 TASS -- ITAR-TASS received the following statement released by the Russian Defence Ministry on Thursday: "In connection with the publication in the mass media of Order no.174-RP of April 5, 1994 of the president of the Russian Federation on Russian military bases on the territory of former Soviet republics, the Ministry of Defence of the Russian Federation considers it necessary to make the following statement. "The Ministry of Defence of the Russian Federation has never planned and is not planning to establish any military bases on the territory of Latvia, nor test there new weapons and military hardware. The document prepared by the Ministry of Defence together with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs mentioned the establishment, on the basis of military units of the Russian Armed Forces stationed on the territory of former Soviet republics, of Russian military bases and facilities in the interests of ensuring the security of the Russian Federation and those states. It meant not the establishment of a military base, but the missile attack warning station in Skrunda, the negotiations on which are still underway with the Latvian side. The presidential order instructs the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Defence Ministry of Russia and other concerned ministries and departments to continue the negotiation process with the CIS member-states and Latvia on this matter."
