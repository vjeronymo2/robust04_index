BFN [Text] A National Council for Television and Radio has been set up in Ukraine in accordance with a decree by Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk. The presidential and government press office told INTERFAX-Ukraine that the council had the right to suspend the activities of television and radio broadcasting stations which violate Ukraine's legislation and annul their broadcasting licences. The council will be subordinate directly to the Ukrainian Supreme Soviet. Among other functions entrusted to the council, there are granting licences for using broadcasting channels and control over the use of the private band. According to the decree, the council will pursue state policy in the field of television and radio broadcasting regardless of the stations' form of property and supervise the implementation of the law on mass media.
