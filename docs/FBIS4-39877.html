BFN [Text] As reported earlier, Georgian Head of State Eduard Shevardnadze and Russian President Boris Yeltsin had a telephone conversation yesterday. Mr. Eduard Shevardnadze expressed satisfaction with decrees issued by the Russian president recently concerning the introduction of peacekeeping forces to Abkhazia. The head of state gave a positive assessment of Russian Defense Minister Grachev's visit, who came to Georgia on Yeltsin's instructions. For his part, the Russian president expressed satisfaction with improved relations between our countries, which have already produced positive results in Abkhazia. He also stressed the need for a speedy settlement of the conflict and a timely return of refugees to Abkhazia. The leaders of the two countries also discussed pressing issues relating to peace and stability in the Caucasus.
