Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin has declared that the Cyprus problem cannot be resolved through Turkey's support alone. The same support, understanding, and courage must be displayed by Greece as well. Cetin met with Joe Clark, the UN secretary general's special envoy for Cyprus, in Istanbul this morning. In a statement after the one-hour meeting, Cetin reported that the recent developments in Cyprus were discussed with Clark. Cetin recalled that in his letter to the UN secretary general, Rauf Denktas, president of the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus, announced that he accepts the confidence-building measures in principle. Cetin added: [Begin Cetin recording] Kliridhis must do the same. The two leaders will meet and discuss the confidence-building measures. We want a viable and just solution for Cyprus. As in the past, the Turkish Government will continue to contribute to efforts made by Mr. Denktas on this subject. [end recording] In turn, Clark remarked that the Denktas announcement that he accepts the confidence-building measures is an encouraging step. Clark noted that he does not doubt that Turkey and the United Nations share the same views on the Cyprus question. After his meeting with Clark, Cetin left for the World Economic Forum meetings in Davos, Switzerland.
