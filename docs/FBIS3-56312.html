Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] In the course of the meeting between Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Belarusian Supreme Soviet Chairman Stanislav Shushkevich in Moscow on 12 January, both sides conducted an in-depth analysis of the state of bilateral relations, placing emphasis on economic cooperation. The sides came to the conclusion that a new Belarusian-Russian treaty on friendship and cooperation is necessary, corresponding to these states' aspiration to build democracies with socially-oriented market economies and the special status of economic, political, and human relations that have developed between the two countries in the course of history. The sides exchanged opinions with regard to the upcoming visit of U.S. President Bill Clinton to Moscow and Minsk. It was pointed out that in the process of building economic relations with the United States it is necessary to proceed from programs of assistance to programs of promoting trade and investment, being primary prerequisites for the development and integration of CIS economies in the world economy.
