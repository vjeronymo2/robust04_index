930713 FT 13 JUL 93 / UK Company News: Dunedin launches Japan trust Dunedin Fund Managers yesterday launched, via a placing and offer for subscription, a new closed-end Japanese investment trust which hopes to raise up to Pounds 40m. Dunedin Japan Investment Trust, which will invest for long term capital growth in Japanese companies, is issuing up to 8m units. Each comprises 5 shares with one warrant attached at 500p per unit. Each warrant will carry the right to subscribe for one share in any September from 1995 to 1999 at 100p per share. The sponsors, UBS, has arranged a placing of 2.86m units; the remaining units are available for public subscription. It had succeeded in placing Pounds 14.3m with institutions. For the first five years of the trust's life, Dunedin will donate 35 per cent of its 1 per cent annual management fee to Barnardos, the charity. Minimum subscription for the offer which closes on July 23 is Pounds 250.
