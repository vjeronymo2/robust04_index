920128 FT 28 JAN 92 / Cameroon's debts rescheduled Creditor nations working through the Paris Club rescheduled the official foreign debt of Cameroon over 15 to 20 years yesterday, AP reports from Paris. The creditors announced that they advised stretching debt service payments to 20 years for public development assistance loans, and 15 years for other types of guaranteed loans. They did not say what amount of debt was covered by the package, but urged other creditors to arrange debt conversions for Cameroon. Those participating in the Cameroon talks included Austria, Belgium, Britain, Canada, Denmark, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, the Netherlands, Sweden, Switzerland, and the US.
