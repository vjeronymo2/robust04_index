BFN [Excerpts] Finnish President Martti Ahtisaari is very pleased with his visit to Russia, which ended today. [passage omitted] President Martti Ahtisaari, unlike previous presidents, carried out his first official visit to Russia according to a short schedule. During his visit, which only lasted 25 hours, Ahtisaari had time to meet President Boris Yeltsin and Premier Viktor Chernomyrdin several times. This morning he met the leaders of both chambers of Parliament. On returning to Finland, Ahtisaari reiterated that there are no political problems between the two countries. According to the president, future relations will be handled more and more between the parliaments. He cited as an example the exchange of visits by the Foreign Affairs Committees. [passage omitted] According to the president, when Finland becomes a member of the European Union [EU], trade between Finland and Russia will be guided primarily by agreements between Russia and the EU. The industrial delegation that accompanied Ahtisaari on his visit held talks with the Russians that concentrated on how the financing of individual deals could be guaranteed. There is currently no comprehensive system in Russia which would guarantee repayment of loans. No individual large deals were signed during the trip. At the end of the presidential news conference given at Helsinki-Vantaa Airport, the president explained when asked what he did late last night. According to Ahtisaari, the atmosphere at the dinner was enlivened mainly by good orchestral music. After the dinner, which ended at about 2100, he had discussions with the Finnish industrial delegation. The president's night ended with a football match between AC Milan and Barcelona.
