BFN [Text] Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin, who left Tallinn at lunchtime today, stated at a briefing he gave at the airport that, if the will is there, 31 August is not at all an unrealistic deadline for the withdrawal of Russian troops. According to Churkin, a firm basis by way of a firm agreement is required, however, to (?resolve) issues in such an energetic manner. According to Churkin, a realistic agreement on Russian military pensioners must be concluded so as to eliminate tensions in Estonian-Russian relations. During his two-day visit to Tallinn the Russian deputy foreign minister continued talks begun in Geneva with Raul Malk, the deputy chancellor of the Estonian Foreign Ministry, on Saturday [4 June]. Churkin also met with Estonian Prime Minister Mart Laar, Foreign Minister Juri Luik, and President Lennart Meri.
