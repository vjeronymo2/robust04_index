Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Khartoum [place-name as received], 27 March (SUNA) -- The Arab League is absolutely engaged in what is occurring in south Sudan, said Dr. Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid, the secretary-general of the league Saturday [26 March] in his opening session [as received]. [passage omitted] He added that south Sudan problem doesn't only concern the Sudanese people, but the whole national security of the Arabs. [passage omitted] Meanwhile, Foreign Minister Husayn Abu-Salih met his Egyptian counterpart, 'Amr Musa. The meeting tackled issues of mutual concern, said Musa, stressing that there will be meeting between him and abu salih in khartoum in the near future. Musa affirmed Egypt's firm stand against the separation of Sudan. Dr. Abu-Salih hoped that official dialogue between Sudan and Egypt would go ahead to solve issues of differences, hoping that Arab solidarity would be realized. Arab solidarity, he said, is the way that leads to the solution of the political and economic problems. On Nairobi peace talks, Abu-Salih said that Sudan has put a comprehensive plan for the solution of south Sudan issue, urging the Arab states and international community to support [the] peace initiative sponsored by [the] IGADD [Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development] states.
