BFN [Text] On 8 April, at the Council of Ministers, a working meeting took place between governmental delegations of the Republic of Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] and the Republic of Bulgaria, led by Deputy Prime Ministers Jovan Andonov and Valentin Karabashev, the Government Press Center reports. Implementing the 1993 agreements between the two countries and the updated protocol signed on 15 March in Sofia was discussed. The delegations also discussed certain problems regarding the transit of goods through our country, improving the handling capacity of the border checkpoints, constructing a railroad between Gyueshevo and Kumanovo, and improving cooperation between the oil refineries of the two republics. A readiness exists to sign soon in Skopje agreements on avoiding double taxation and mutual protection and encouragement of investments.
