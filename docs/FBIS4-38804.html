BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Valeriy Demidetskiy] [Text] Kishinev [Chisinau] June 1 TASS -- "By prolonging the state of emergency in the Dniester area, the Tiraspol leaders have grossly violated the agreement on the peaceful settlement of the Dniester conflict, signed by the presidents of Russia and Moldavia [Moldova]," co-chairman of the Joint Control Commission for promoting stability in the conflict area, First Deputy Interior Minister of Moldavia Viktor Katane said here today. As earlier reported, president of the unrecognised Dniester republic Igor Smirnov has decreed to prolong the state of emergency on the left-hand bank of the Dniester beginning from June 1. The state of emergency was announced on January 18 to prevent the holding of pre-term elections to the Moldavian parliament in the Dniester area. According to Katane, the state of emergency envisages the toughening of the border regime by the self-styled Dniester republic and deployment of additional paramilitary units within the safety zone. He described as far-fetched the arguments of the Tiraspol authorities that the state of emergency was necessary to combat crime in the region.
