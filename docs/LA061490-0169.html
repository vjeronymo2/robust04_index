June 14, 1990, Thursday, Orange County Edition

AMATEUR BASEBALL DRAFT;

ANGELS MAKE 53 SELECTIONS DURING RECORD 101 ROUNDS

The California Angels drafted 53 players, including four from Orange County, in last week's free agent amateur baseball draft.

The Angels selected center fielder Garret Anderson of Kennedy High School, right-handed pitcher Todd Blyleven of Villa Park, catcher Eric Buechele from Fullerton who played for Fresno State and right-handed pitcher Jay Hassel of Rancho Santiago College.

A total of 1,487 players were selected in a record 101 rounds.

The Houston Astros led all teams with 100 selections, surpassing their own record of 88, set last year. Seattle was second with 75 selections, followed by the New York Yankees (74), Montreal (71) and Atlanta (67).

There were 587 players selected from four-year colleges, 577 from high schools, 317 from junior colleges and six listed as being chosen from other categories.

Right-handed pitchers made up the largest single group of draftees (464), followed by outfielders (315) and infielders, excluding first basemen (289).

Those attending schools in California made up the largest group (349), followed by 142 from Florida and 116 in Texas. Five states -- Alaska, Delaware, Montana, South Dakota and Vermont -- had no players taken.

In 1989, 1,488 players were drafted in 88 rounds, both records at the time.
