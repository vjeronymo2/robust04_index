Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Santa Fe de Bogota, 22 Feb (EFE) -- The Colombian Foreign Ministry today confirmed Maria del Rosario Casas Ames, a U.S. citizen of "Colombian origin" who the U.S. Government says is allegedly involved in espionage, worked for the Foreign Ministry between April 1982 and December 1983. A Colombian Foreign Ministry press communique stated Mrs. Casas, "prior to her marriage, worked for the Foreign Ministry in the post of civilian attache 1 EX in the Colombian Embassy in Mexico." It was reported in Washington today Casas worked as a "paid CIA agent in Mexico, where she worked as a cultural attache in a foreign embassy." The U.S. Justice Department announced on 21 February the arrest of Casas and her husband, Aldrick Ames, a high-ranking CIA official, for allegedly spying for the dissolved Soviet Union and Russia. It is said they were "double agents," because they also "sold information" to the Russian KGB since 1985 for $1.5 million. The Foreign Ministry added in the communique issued in Santa Fe de Bogota that "the Colombian Government is waiting for official confirmation about Mrs. Casas' alleged links to the U.S. CIA during the time when she worked as an official of the Colombian Embassy in Mexico."
