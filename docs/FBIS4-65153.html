BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias today met U.S. Ambassador Thomas Niles and discussed all national and Balkan issues. Papoulias also met Van Der Stoel, the CSCE high commissioner in charge of minorities. Correspondent Foivos Karzis reports: [Begin recording] Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias and the U.S. Ambassador in Athens Thomas Niles this morning discussed Greek-Albanian relations, the issue of Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM] in light of the upcoming talks with UN mediator Cyrus Vance, and the Yugoslav issue in view of the Geneva meeting on Bosnia-Herzegovina. Foreign Ministry Secretary General Ambassador Khristos Zakharakis attended the meeting. Zakharakis will go to New York tomorrow to meet Vance on Friday [12 May] within the framework of the new round of talks on Skopje. He may also meet Matthew Nimetz, the U.S. presidential envoy on Skopje. Papoulias made a detailed reference to the Greek-Albanian relations in light of his recent meeting in Zurich with his Albanian counterpart, Alfred Serreqi. He emphasized the rights of the Greek minority, the violation of religious rights, education issues, and the detention of five Omonoia members. The foreign minister explained the same issues to Van der Stoel, the CSCE high commissioner in charge of minorities, who is visiting Athens. Van der Stoel is expected to submit a report. Papoulias and Niles also discussed the Yugoslav issue. The Greek foreign minister will represent the European Union, as the head of the communal troika, at the meeting of the so-called contact group that has been formed. U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev, and a representative of the UN secretary general will participate in the Geneva meeting. The main goal of the meeting would be a resumption of the talks between the three warring factions in Bosnia-Herzegovina through the return of the Bosnian Serbs to the negotiating table. According to reports, the Greek side will again make the suggestion that negotiations be linked with developments and the gradual lifting of the UN embargo against new Yugoslavia. [end recording]
