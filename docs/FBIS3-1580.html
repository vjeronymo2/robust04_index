Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 27 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese Association of Science and Technology (CAST) must work hard to promote the application of scientific results to production and the improvement of the country's science and technology, both a senior Chinese leader and a top CAST head said today. Alternate member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China Wen Jiabao and CAST Chairman Zhu Guangya voiced the requirement at a national conference of CAST, which began here today. The tasks set for the non-governmental scientific organization this year include: improving the production technologies and management of large and medium-sized state-owned enterprises, developing new products and new technologies, enhancing agricultural technologies, and doing research work on major regional and inter-regional development projects. According to an investigation conducted in 17 provinces, autonomous regions and cities, 1.1 million scientists and technical experts last year participated in 9,000 projects in transforming scientific results into productivity. "The economic turnover amounted to 3.23 billion yuan (about 660 million U.S. dollars then)," CAST's Zhu Guangya said. Zhu said his association, a link between the government and the nation's scientists and technicians, will also provide the government with advice and opinions on national policies and development plans. He said that CAST will strengthen its efforts in running scientific institutions and enterprises to better equip itself to fulfill its social responsibilities. Wen Jiabao asked CAST to promote the establishment of China's new scientific and technological framework. The new system should be suited to the development of a socialist market economy and the law on scientific and technological development, and characterized by a close union between the economy and science and technology, Wen said. Wen said the Communist Party of China and the government have always been ready to listen to the opinions and requests of intellectuals, to bring their initiative and creativity into full play. CAST, a national scientific association for the public good established in 1958, now has 4 million members across the country.
