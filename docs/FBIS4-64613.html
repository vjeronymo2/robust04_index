BFN [Text] Hans Stercken, chairman of the German Bundestag Foreign Affairs Committee, has announced that his country has lifted the ban on arms deliveries to Turkey. Stercken, who is conducting various contacts in Ankara, met with Interior Minister Nahit Mentese today. In a statement during the meeting, Stercken said that the arms deliveries are not undertaken only in accordance with the agreements between Turkey and Germany but also within the framework of NATO agreements. In turn, Mentese noted that the arms deliveries were suspended as a result of pressure from German public opinion, adding that the lifting of the ban is a welcome development. Stating that German weapons are not being used in the struggle against the terrorist PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] organization in south and southeast Anatolia, Mentese remarked that there are no restrictions on where and when the weapons should be used.
