BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Boris Krivoshey] [Text] Moscow, 15 Jun--Russian Deputy Foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin is flying to London today to discuss the whole range of problems connected with ways to settle the conflict in former Yugoslavia. He is expected to hold talks with representatives of the member states of the so-called "contact group." This was announced at a briefing today by Mikhail Demurin, deputy head of the Information and Press Department of the Russian Federation Foreign Ministry. "The visit is to be viewed in the context of the overall efforts of the Russian Federation aimed at finding ways to a peaceful settlement in the region and at preparing a second meeting on Bosnia between the foreign ministers of Russia, the United States, and five countries of the European Union. The Russian side put forward the initiative to hold such a meeting some time ago," Demurin said.
