Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] The United Nations, 16 Mar (INA)--This afternoon, Baghdad time, Deputy Prime Minister Tariq 'Aziz will begin contacts with officials of the United Nations and the UN Security Council. The deputy prime minister has arrived in New York on a visit to the United Nations for talks on the need for the UN Security Council to honor its commitments as enshrined in Paragraph 22 of Resolution 687, which provide for lifting the sanctions clamped on Iraq. Tariq 'Aziz will hold his first meeting with Jean-Bernard Merimee, the French ambassador to the United Nations and president of the UN Security Council, this afternoon. He will also meet with the representatives of several permanent and nonpermanent members of the UN Security Council. During his visit to the United Nations, the deputy prime minister will hold talks with UNSC members and with UN Secretary General Dr. Butrus Ghali. Tariq 'Aziz is accompanied on the visit by an Iraqi delegation comprised of Lieutenant General Engineer 'Amir Muhammad Rashid, director of the Military Industrialization Organization; Dr. Riyad al-Qaysi, under secretary of the Foreign Ministry; and several Iraqi experts.
