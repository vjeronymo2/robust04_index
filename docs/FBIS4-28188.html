BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 5 (KCNA) -- About 2,000 workers affiliated with the subway trade union council held a rally in the Chongmyo Park in Seoul on June 2 and vowed to win in the wage-hike struggle for 1994, a Seoul-based radio reported. Pointing to the instability of working conditions, the workers at the rally called for the enforcement of an 8-hour workday. They rejected the wage hike draft agreed upon between the subsidized South Korean General Federation of Trade Unions (Nochong) and the general federation of managers councils and demanded a wage hike up to a level sufficient to ensure living.
