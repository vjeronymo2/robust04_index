BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 12 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- In an interview to the "ARDYN ERH" correspondent the Ambassador of China to Mongolia Pei Jiayi has expressed his confidence in further development of goodneighbouring friendly relations between the two countries on the basis of efforts by both sides and the five principles of peaceful coexistence. He noted that for the development of Chinese-Mongolian relations in future of great significance would be the forthcoming visit by the premier of the State Council of the People's Republic of China (PRC) Li Peng to Mongolia at the invitation of the Prime Minister P. Jasray by the end of April in the current year. Having expressed his satisfaction with the successful development of bilateral relations in all spheres, the ambassador stressed that there are not any substantial obstacles in this respect and as for the particular problems to be arising they would be solved gradually step by step. The ambassador said that the Chinese Government is taking strict measures for ill quality goods not to be penetrating into the international market. He also expressed a hope for gradual regulation of some certain problems concerning the barter trade connected in particular with the shortcomings of infrastructure and activities of the transport.
