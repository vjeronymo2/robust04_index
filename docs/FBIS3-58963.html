Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Madrid, Jan 21 (AFP) -- Britain, Germany, Italy and Spain agreed here Friday [21 January] to cut the costs of their jointly-developed Eurofighter project by 30 percent, the Spanish defence ministry said. The heads of the respective air forces which signed the accord at the Toreejon air force base near here confirmed their support for talks currently under way with the manufacturers to reduce the price of each aircraft. The Eurofighter project, a lighter version of the original European Combat Aircraft, was launched in December 1992 in the wake of defence budget cuts and changing requirements after the end of the Cold War. The aircraft is being funded 37.5 percent by Britain, 3O percent by Germany, 19.5 percent by Italy and 13 percent by Spain.
