Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and foreign minister, today received Yasushi Akashi, the UN secretary general's newly appointed special envoy for former Yugoslavia. Minister Granic informed his guest about the process and results of the talks he had yesterday with Bosnia-Herzegovina Prime Minister Haris Silajdzic in Vienna. He laid particular stress on the importance of an urgent cessation of hostilities between the Croats and Muslims in central Bosnia, an issue on which Presidents Franjo Tudjman and Alija Izetbegovic will have talks in Bonn on Saturday. After informing his guest about the situation in the occupied areas of Croatia, Minister Granic said Croatia is resolved to achieve a solution for these areas in a peaceful way through negotiations in accordance with relevant UN Security Council resolutions. But he also mentioned the decisive responsibility borne by Serbia and Slobodan Milosevic for the present situation in these areas, and stated the readiness of local power-holders to negotiate and reach a peaceful solution to all problems. Yasushi Akashi pointed out he will do everything possible to implement all relevant UN Security Council resolutions and that he expects close cooperation with and support from the Croatian authorities in implementing the complex task of the United Nations, and particularly the UN Protection Force, in restoring peace and stability in this area.
