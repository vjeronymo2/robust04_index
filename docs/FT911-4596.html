910418 FT 18 APR 91 / Green issues sway investors INSTITUTIONS consider a company's environmental record before deciding whether to buy its shares, according to a survey released yesterday by Dewe Rogerson, the public-relations and marketing group. Dewe-Rogerson said that, of 80 senior fund managers interviewed, 40 per cent said the question was an important factor in their decisions. A further 58 per cent said a coherent and effective environmental strategy enhanced their view of a company, Dewe-Rogerson continued. It said 67 per cent believed environmental factors had a significant effect on a company's business. Only 11 per cent believed they were unimportant, said Dewe-Rogerson.
