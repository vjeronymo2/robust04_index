BFN [Text] Havana, 16 Jun (PL) -- Mozambican National Assembly President Marcelino Dos Santos expressed here his government's and people's gratitude to Cuba for its contributions to his country's independence struggle. During a meeting with Cuban internationalists who fought in Mozambique and on the occasion of the 19th anniversary of its independence from Portugal (25 June) Dos Santos recognized the help they received from Cuba during and after the armed struggle to consolidate and stabilize that nation. Dos Santos, who is also president of the Mozambican-Cuban Friendship Committee, referred to the peace agreements signed in Rome in 1992 with the opposition movement Renamo [Mozambique National Resistance] and the difficulties in fully implementing those agreements due to maneuvers by that armed organization. Dos Santos is currently on a visit to Cuba which will end on 30 June. During his stay he will meet with several leaders and will tour centers of economic, historical, scientific, educational, and social interest.
