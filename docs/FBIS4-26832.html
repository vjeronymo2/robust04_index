BFN [Text] Hong Kong, June 24 (XINHUA) -- The airport committee of the Sino-British Joint Liaison Group (JLG) ended its meeting here this afternoon with both sides having claimed that further progress has been made over the airport issue. "We have discussed the issue of the financial arrangements in today's airport committee meeting and have made further progress over the issue," said Guo Fengmin, senior representative of the Chinese team of the Sino-British Joint Liaison Group. "We have decided to entrust the experts to draw up a document of the meeting, which will be signed by the senior representatives of the two sides as an agreement of both sides," he said. "Of course, we still need to study some of the details and wording in the document, we hope to complete the drafting of the document as soon as possible," he said, adding that the progress achieved between China and Britain is the result of the common efforts of both sides. Hugh Davies, senior representative to the British side of the JLG confirmed the progress as guo claimed in a press briefing given after the meeting. "We are still discussing some of the details," he said, adding that he was confident that an agreement over the airport issue would be reached on the basis of the progress that the two sides had made.
