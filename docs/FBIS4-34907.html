BFN [Text] The Uruguayan, Paraguayan, and Bolivian foreign ministers will relaunch Urupabol [Permanent Joint Commission of Uruguay, Paraguay, and Bolivia]. This organization will attempt to improve communications and commodity outlets through regional rivers and highways. Uruguayan Foreign Minister Sergio Abreu Bonilla has arrived in Paraguay. He will participate in a ceremony to relaunch Urupabol, an organization made up of Uruguay, Paraguay, and Bolivia that was dissolved in 1982 because it was described as being unnecessary. [Begin Abreu recording, in progress] ...a cooperation agreement that existed a few years ago has been revamped in the area, and is being implemented under Paraguay's pro tempore secretariat...[end recording, in progress] An effort now will be made for the three countries to cooperate in the improvement of communications and transportation of commodities through the Paraguay-Parana waterway and the Transchaco Highway. Uruguay will offer its port services. The Bolivian foreign minister will arrive at 1430 today. Urupabol will serve as a Common Market of the South, Mercosur, bridge for Bolivia, which is an Andean Pact member. The three countries' foreign ministers will meet at 1630 today and will sign an agreement at noon on 25 June. During the Uruguayan foreign minister's visit to our country, an agreement on returning stolen motor vehicles will be signed. Paraguay already has signed this type of agreement with other South American countries.
