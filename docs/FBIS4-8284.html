BFN [Text] Warsaw, April 11 -- Feasible cooperation between the Polish defence system and NATO is the most important issue. If that occures Poland's membership of NATO will be a formal confirmation of the existing state of affairs, Foreign Minister Andrzej Olechowski told journalists following Monday's meeting with U.S. Deputy Secretary of State Strobe Talbott. According to the Polish minister, the visit of Mr. Talbott in Poland has rather not changed anything in Polish-American relations. "I do not see substantial changes in relation to what I heard three months ago," Olechowski said. He stressed that Polish presentation document stipulating proposals and possibilities for Poland's cooperation within the framework of the "Partnership for Peace" programme, to be presented soon, is being welcomed warmly. The minister said that Poland has various proposals of cooperation but declined to disclose details. He added that President Bill Clinton's July visit to Warsaw was also discussed at the Foreign Ministry. Strobe Talbott, asked by journalists when Poland is to become NATO member, replied that the Partnership for Peace programme is a path leading to NATO and stressed the determination of the Polish side to actively participate in the programme.
