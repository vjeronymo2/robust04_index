Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Dan Preda report from Strasbourg] [Excerpts] Currently the plenary session of the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe is debating the situation in former Yugoslavia, more precisely, the effects of the embargo instated against former Yugoslavia [by the UN Security Council] on the economy of the neighboring countries. The afternoon session began with the speech of Thorvald Stoltenberg. [passage omitted] The floor was also taken by Dumitru Buzatu, Romanian Chamber of Deputies member [and Vaslui deputy on behalf of the Social Democracy Party of Romania], who pointed out that, currently, further losses are registered in Romania's economy, which so far amount to over $7 billion. Senator Adrian Paunescu also took the floor to discuss that issue. He asserted that the embargo should be lifted and the principle of dialogue should be introduced. Romania is suffering, the PSM [Socialist Party of Labor] senator said, merely because of the wish to make Serbia suffer. [passage omitted]
