BFN [Text] As it was four years ago, Hungary will again have a parliament with six parties, and it will include the same parties as in 1990. However, their positions of power will be considerably different. According to the unofficial final results of the first round in the parliamentary elections, the Hungarian Socialist Party is in first place with 32.4 percent of the votes; the Alliance of Free Democrats is second with 19.4 percent; the Hungarian Democratic Forum is in third place with 12 percent of the votes, followed by the Independent Smallholders Party with 8.5 percent. The Federation of Young Democrats ended up in fifth place with 7.4 percent. The Christian Democratic People's Party obtained (?7.3) percent of the votes. The other nine parties fielding national lists did not achieve the 5 percent necessary to enter parliament.
