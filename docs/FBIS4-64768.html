BFN [Text] Brussels, May 6 (AFP) -- Turkmenistan is to join NATO's Partnership for Peace cooperation programme on Tuesday [10 May], the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation announced Friday. Deputy Prime Minister Boris Shikhmuradov will sign the framework agreement at NATO's Brussels headquarters, joining 17 other countries including Sweden and Finland, who are due to sign on Monday. The Partnership, offered to former East Bloc countries in January as an alternative to enlarging the Western security alliance, provides for joint manoevres, military planning and peacekeeping operations.
