BFN [Text] The Socialist Party [PS] has won the elections for the European Parliament in Portugal. The RTP/Catholic University forecast says that the Socialist Party won between 35 and 39 percent of the vote, the Social Democratic Party [PSD] has won between 28 and 32 percent, the Social Democratic Center Party [CDS] has won between 12 and 14 percent of the vote, and Communist-Greens coalition [CDU] has between 9.5 and 11 percent of the vote. This means that the PS has 11 MEP's [Member of European Parliament], PSD has eight MEP's, CDU has three MEP's, and the CDS has three MEP's. We will now compare these figures with those of the last European elections in 1989: The PS had 28.5 percent, the PSD had 32.7 percent, the CDS had 14.2 percent, and the CDU had 14.4 percent.
