920123 FT 23 JAN 92 / Arts: Today's Television The Queen has too few black people and women on her staff, according to This Week (8.30 ITV). It claims that there are 891 royal employees, only nine of whom come from 'ethnic minorities', which is presumably code for being dark skinned. It seems unlikely that the race industry would be satisfied with a royal household full of white Australians or New Zealanders. One critic asks 'How many people from working class backgrounds are in the royal court? It's not just the ethnic minorities and the feminist lobby that has a case, it is 95 per cent of the country'. How long before there is a move for positive discrimination in favour of innumerates on the staff of the Bank Of England? Do you know how to hit the ground running as you jump off the rear platform of a moving bus, without falling flat on your nose? You do? Odds on you're a Londoner. Of course you cannot do it with the accursed modern double-decker which has doors and no rear platform: you need a Routemaster. Designed in the 40s and built in the 50s with a theoretical life of 17 years, these splendid old liners of the London streets are now due to drive on past the year 2000 as Perpetual Motion explains (8.30 BBC2).
