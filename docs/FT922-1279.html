920623 FT 23 JUN 92 / UK Company News: Waterhouse back in the black Waterhouse Group, the USM-quoted business interiors specialist, returned to the black over the six months ended March 31 following action taken by the enlarged board to reduce costs and improve gross margins. At the pre-tax level profits of Pounds 55,417 compared with previous losses of Pounds 107,856. Turnover amounted to Pounds 1.17m (Pounds 2.07m) and gross profit as a percentage of turnover was 32.3 (24) per cent. Selling and administration expenses were cut to Pounds 320,988 (Pounds 575,111) and interest charges to Pounds 1,609 (Pounds 29,011). Earnings of 2.2p compared with previous losses of 4.3p - the last dividend, an interim of 1.7p, was paid in August 1990. Mr Alan Redfern, previously managing director of Malbourn, a subsidiary of Hanson, has been appointed chief executive of Waterhouse, which changed its name from the Sandell Group earlier this month.
