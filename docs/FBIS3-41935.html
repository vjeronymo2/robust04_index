Language: Thai Article Type:CSO [Excerpts] On 30 September, Mr. Sakthip Krairuk, director-general of the Information Department, Ministry of Foreign Affairs, said that the UN Security Council may impose an embargo on Libya. [passage omitted] Mr. Sakthip said that besides this, the government has taken steps to have the Labor Skills Development Department issue stipulations prohibiting Thai workers from engaging in activities abroad having to do with the production, stockpiling, and use of chemical weapons. "The Thai government supports the attempt by the people of the world to limit the spread of chemical weapons. On 13 March, the government implemented a royal decree on controlling shipments out of the country in order to prevent the shipment of chemicals and implements that could be used to produce chemical weapons," said Mr. Sakthip.
