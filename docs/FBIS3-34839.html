Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The external affairs minister, Mr. Dinesh Singh, has underlined the need for India and the United States to cooperate against terrorism for promoting peace. Mr. Dinesh Singh told the U.S. senator, Mr. Moynihan, who called on him in New Delhi today, that it is also necessary for Pakistan to desist from supporting terrorism if it is serious about normalizing relations. Both also discussed Indo-U.S. relations and felt that the economic relationship is picking up day by day. Mr. Moynihan said substantial increase in the U.S. economic engagement in India augurs well for the future. The Janata Dal has strongly criticized the U.S. interference in India's internal affairs. The national executive of the party, in a resolution adopted at its meeting in New Delhi today, said that the U.S. attempt to use the Kashmir issue to violate Indian sovereignty and Washington's comments on Punjab amount to rank interference.
