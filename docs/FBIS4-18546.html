BFN [Text] Basel, Switzerland, April 5 (AFP) -- The European Monetary Institute (EMI) has found a building in Frankfurt, Germany, for its headquarters, the Institute's Press Department announced in Basel on Tuesday [5 April]. The EMI, the forerunner of the European Union's [EU] central bank, has been theoretically headquartered in the German city since January 1, but it had not found premises. The announcement said the Institute would be moving into its premises next November 1, in the Eurotower Building in the city center, facing the Frankfurt Opera. It formerly belonged to BfG bank. EMI President Alexandre Lamfallusy of Belgium signed a lease to the building on Tuesday. The Institute has been temporary housed in Basel at the headquarters of the Bank for International Settlements -- which Lamfalussy headed until last January 1. The Institute's status as the forerunner of a European central bank and in preparation for the EU common currency was laid down in the Community's Maastricht Treaty.
