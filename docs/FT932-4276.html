930610 FT 10 JUN 93 / Parliament and Politics: Statement from Bank of England on political independence The Bank of England yesterday said: 'We believe that the key objective of monetary policy is to safeguard the value of money, but that for so long as policies are in place which secure that, the precise constitutional status of the central bank is less important. That is the position now, in that the government is committed to an inflation target and the Bank of England has a role in monitoring its achievement. Nevertheless as the governor has said on many occasions, there are clear advantages in making the central bank directly responsible for achieving price stability, if only because this may enhance the credibility of policy.'
