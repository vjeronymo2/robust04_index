March 12, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

SITTING FOR INGE MORATH BY HONOR MOORE

Her camera -- then a focused face

maneuvers to seek with a blue eye

what in my face she would embrace.

"I have an idea," her smile a trace

to reassure -- and posing, I comply.

Her camera, then a focused face

catch a countenance I'd efface.

She's triumphant, almost wry.

What in my face she would embrace

I can't predict. We talk of place,

become friends, light moves with each try:

Her camera, then a focused face

(together a sort of minotaur) race

shadow and my poise to prophesy

what in my face she would embrace

in darkroom darkness, how I'll grace

paper -- first a fray of shadow filched by

her camera, then a focused face:

what in my face she would embrace.

From "Memoir: Poems by Honor Moore" (Chicory Blue Press, 795 E. Street North, Goshen, CT 06756: $11.95; 80 pp.). Moore's work has often been anthologized, but this is her first book-length collection. She is also the author of a verse play, "Mourning Picture," produced on Broadway in 1974. The photo of her, above, is by Inge Morath. Honor Moore, 1988. Reprinted by permission of Chicory Blue Press.
