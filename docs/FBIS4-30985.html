BFN [Text] Alija Izetbegovic, president of the Presidency of Bosnia-Herzegovina, today received Vitaliy Churkin, special envoy to the Russian president for Bosnia-Herzegovina. The talks were attended by Prime Minister Haris Silajdzic. Churkin expressed interest in Bosnia-Herzegovina's stands regarding the cease-fire with the Serbian aggressor and the possible participation of a Bosnia-Herzegovina delegation at the negotiations on the political solution to the crisis. Izetbegovic informed Churkin of the positions he had also presented to the Assembly of Bosnia-Herzegovina. The Russian envoy said that he expected Serbs to withdraw from the Gorazde protected zone, something that Izetbegovic had set as a condition for negotiations to continue. The Bosnian side has no objections to the Serbian population remaining in the Gorazde region, but only if the people involved are those who had lived on the said territory before the war. It is known that the Serbian aggressor forces are trying to colonize the Gorazde region, which was also pointed out to [words indistinct].
