BFN [Interview with Sulejman Ugljanin, chairman of the Muslim National Council for Sandzak, by Amir Hodzic; place and date not given -- recorded] [Text] [Hodzic] Mr. Ugljanin, due to the communications blockade and other blockades in Bosnia-Herzegovina, we are not in a position to found out more about the real state of affairs in Sandzak. This is a chance to find out from you about what is really going on there. [Ugljanin] The situation in Sandzak is identical to that in Bosnia-Herzegovina before the war. There are mass arrests of Muslims, Muslim houses are searched, criminal charges are brought against them. Generally, this is a form of a refined state terror. It is nothing new, since with the start of the war, that is, the Serbian-Montenegrin aggression on Bosnia-Herzegovina, a campaign of terror was launched simultaneously against the Muslims in Sandzak. You know that since the start of the aggression on Bosnia-Herzegovina some 74,000 Muslims, women and children, adults who were expelled from Bosnia have passed through Sandzak. Some stayed a month, some two months, some stayed a year, and there are still some 16,000 of them there in Sandzak in our homes, who live with us. Apart from everything that happened to them [changes thought] their fears are doubled because they have experienced something terrible and now they are expecting even worse to come. Serbia and Montenegro, you know, planned the ethnic cleansing of Muslim and Croatian people of Bosnia-Herzegovina, the ethnic cleansing of the Muslim people of Sandzak, and the ethnic cleansing of the Albanian people of Kosovo. They are doing just that now. It is obvious. Now it is up to us to prevent that and have our status justly resolved. You might have heard, the whole world knows, that we as the national council called on the people to boycott the elections because of the Serbian-Montenegrin aggression on Bosnia- Herzegovina, and not one of our people voted in the Serbian and Montenegrin elections on 31 May 1992 or 20 December 1992, and the third time [words indistinct] that we do not have to participate in elections in the country that has attacked our mother state Bosnia-Herzegovina. The people completely boycotted the elections for the third time on 19 December 1993. After that, when Serbian-Montenegrin authorities realized that the people are united and determined, that they are nationally aware -- the people stuck by the Party of Democratic Action [SDA] -- then the police was sent by the politically defeated Serbia and Montenegro, and -- you must have heard -- the entire political leadership of the SDA was arrested. Hamo Hadzic, SDA chairman for Montenegro, was arrested, so was Rasim [words indistinct]; the entire leadership was arrested between 25 to 31 January this year. We have been publishing the statements of the lawyers who defend them -- a group of Montenegrin lawyers is defending them -- and the statements issued by the lawyers, the statements about the arrests given by our people in those prisons: that is a tragedy -- one can barely comprehend how a man can be such an animal toward another man. In this case, it has gone even beyond inhumanity. The attitude of the Serbian and Montenegrin authorities toward the Muslims in Sandzak is identical to the attitude in Bosnia-Herzegovina. The only difference is that we do not have the shelling.
