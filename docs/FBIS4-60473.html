BFN [Text] President Hashemi-Rafsanjani today received Mr. Erwin Arrieta, the Venezuelan energy and mines minister. At the meeting, the Venezuelan energy and mines minister described the role of Iran's history and civilization in the civilization of the world as very valuable. He said: The expansion of warm cooperation with Iran based on mutual interests is very important for us. The grounds for expanding bilateral cooperation are plentiful. He described one of the aims of his visit to Iran, besides assessing the oil issue in the oil-rich countries of Asia, as making use of Iran's valuable experience in the sphere of mines and metals. According to the Central News Unit, Mr. Hashemi-Rafsanjani, for his part, spoke of the activation of the mining sector during the first five-year plan, especially relating to iron ore, coal, refractory materials, copper, and aluminum mines. He said: Side by side with the mining sector, and in light of the high cost of transporting raw materials, the growth of the metallurgy sector is of special importance. Iran is ready to provide Venezuela with its expertise and experience in the fields of oil, gas, mining, the petrochemical industry, and metallurgy.
