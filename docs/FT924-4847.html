921201 FT 01 DEC 92 / Observer: Case study The tribal customs of Britain's main public sector trade unions have long been of interest to employers. They are now to be the subject of a formal study by an anthropologist charged with helping smooth the merger of Nalgo, Nupe and Cohse, into the public sector 'super-union' to be called Unison. Dr Alexandra Ourousoff, who has been given the job, says: 'It is important to understand the cultures of the organisations because it is so easy for differences in cultural values to impede communication'. Perhaps the unions are leading where companies should follow.
