Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, December 30 (XINHUA) -- China plans to increase irrigated farmland by 400,000 hectares next year and to control soil erosion in an area of two million hectares. Niu Maosheng, minister of water resources, said here recently that reforms will be continued in the water resource organizations and the infrastructure construction of water resources will be strengthened, to support agricultural development. Some flood control works will be built and some rivers will be dredged next year. Some 43 reservoirs in danger will be solidified and 38 will be checked and redesigned. The minister said that efforts should also be made to solve the problem of drinking water shortages in urban and rural areas. He also revealed that some hydropower stations with a total generating capacity of 1.1 million kw will be built next year. To meet the target, Niu said, reforms should be carried out in the aspects of investment, real estate management, price administration, water resources laws and regulations, and service.
