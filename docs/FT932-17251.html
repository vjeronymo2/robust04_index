930401 FT 01 APR 93 / International Company News: MGM cuts losses to Dollars 45.6m in term METRO-Goldwyn-Mayer, the Hollywood studio taken over by Credit Lyonnais, its French bank creditor, yesterday reported a fourth-quarter net loss of Dollars 45.6m, compared with Dollars 114.2m in the same period of 1991, and said the bank had agreed to support its business plan, which calls for substantial new film production, writes Martin Dickson in New York. MGM had an operating loss for the period of Dollars 8.5m, down from Dollars 65.3m in 1991. Revenues totalled Dollars 214.4m, compared with Dollars 218.7m a year earlier. Mr Alan Ladd and Mr Dennis Stanfill, co-chairmen, said the 'continuing and now formalised support of Credit Lyonnais is vitally important to our objective of building this organisation, maintaining its improved performance and renewing MGM's stature in our industry.' For the full year, MGM reported a net loss before extraordinary items and accounting changes of Dollars 236m, down from Dollars 347m, on revenues of Dollars 936.7m, against Dollars 921.7m.
