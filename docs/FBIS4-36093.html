BFN [Text] Cairo, 3 Jun (MENA) -- The Nonaligned Movement's [NAM] foreign ministers conference issued a resolution today ending the work of the NAM committee on South Africa and submitting a recommendation to this effect to the movement's countries and governments leaders for ratification. The conference said in its final session that this resolution is based on the start of a new stage in the history of South Africa following the holding of the first democratic elections there and the formation of a nonracial government. It noted that the resolution is in line with the report presented by Indonesia's special envoy Nanasutrisna [name as received], who chaired the NAM session and who called for ending the work of the committee in light of these developments.
