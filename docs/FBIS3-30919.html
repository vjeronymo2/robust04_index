Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 9 Feb -- Asked by a PAP journalist to comment on views that while signing Borowski's resignation, the president exerted influence on reaching a quick agreement within the coalition, Andrzej Zakrzewski, head of the office for political services to the president, said that Lech Walesa "does not interfere with the coalition, but simply learns what the coalition and the government are `producing.'" Zakrzewski added that the president signed Borowski's resignation "in accordance with the procedures." "According to the president, what happened is the internal matter of the coalition and the government; the matter of observing the agreements and following the procedure of changes in the cabinet. The president has nothing to do with it, and he can only accept the changes, and this is what he did," Zakrzewski said. Asked whether he does not think that the president accepted Borowski's resignation unexpectedly quickly, Zakrzewski replied: "The president is always quick."
