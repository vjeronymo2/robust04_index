930618 FT 18 JUN 93 / Diners 'liable for tax on tips' EVEN DINERS who tip waiters will in theory be required to withhold the tax and pay it to the Inland Revenue, under a controversial judgment from the Lords yesterday, Andrew Jack writes. Anyone who tips, or provides payments when there is no contractual relationship, will become an unofficial tax collector, according to accountants' interpretations of the law lords' ruling. The case - Inland Revenue Commissioners vs Herd - concerned a man who had sold shares in his employers' company back to the company. The Revenue argued the employer should have deducted tax before making a payment to him. But the ruling - which found against Mr Herd - suggests a very wide-ranging definition of what constitutes an employer required to comply with the pay as you earn tax system. It implies that anyone who pays emoluments to another person is an employer where the transaction results in pure profit. Those most directly affected will include employers paying termination money or those offering introductory payments to new employees. Ms Leslie Ferrar, a partner at accountants KPMG Peat Marwick, called the judgment 'a commercial nonsense'. She said it demonstrated the need for a fundamental overhaul of existing PAYE regulations - only a few weeks after the Revenue issued a consolidated version of the existing rules. The Revenue said last night: 'We would need to consider the implications of the judgment.'
