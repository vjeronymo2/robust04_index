920429 FT 29 APR 92 / DPP chief suggests safeguards for companies COMPANIES should separate the functions of chairman and chief executive as a safeguard against malpractice, Mrs Barbara Mills, the new director of public prosecutions, said yesterday. She suggested that companies appoint audit, remuneration and nomination committees, ensure no individual is sole signatory to a company bank account, and introduce effective non-executive directors. Mrs Mills said: 'All of these measures are safeguards which can protect an organisation against the over-powerful individual who may ruin a company, but they will not inhibit the talents, drive and energy of successful directors.' Mrs Mills, until recently director of the Serious Fraud Office, said that in common with best practice in the private sector, the public sector needed a clear corporate philosophy to improve its performance. Low ethical standards would bring individuals, organisations and entire markets into disrepute. Those who used undesirable business practices might believe they were gaining an advantage over more honest competitors, but this was bound to be short-term. 'There is no better a recommendation than a reputation for honesty and integrity,' she said. 'Low ethics will infect an entire market or profession, and it is just a short step from that to committing criminal offences.'
