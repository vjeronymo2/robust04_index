BFN [Text] Kiev, April 7 -- Polish Defence Minister Piotr Kolodziejczyk was received by President of Ukraine Leonid Kravchuk on Thursday, the second day of his visit to Kiev. During the meeting, the two discussed political problems in Europe. Both sides stressed the importance of friendly contacts between Poland and Ukraine. Earlier in the day, Minister Kolodziejczyk and Deputy Prime Minister Valeriy Shmarov agreed that the setting up of a joint Polish-Ukrainian bank would make cooperation between the two Defence Ministries easier. The two also discussed U.N. peacekeeping missions and the "Partnership for Peace" initiative. The Polish side was informed that Ukraine is to send more of its soldiers in blue helmets to former Yugoslavia in the near future. Ukraine, it was stressed during the meeting, does not intend to join any military bloc and thus treats "Partnership for Peace" initiative as a factor increasing security in Europe allowing for broader cooperation outside military alliances. A group of Polish experts meeting with Ukrainian counterparts worked on details of technical cooperation. The Polish side is interested in missiles while the Ukrainians in small arms. They also discussed the possibility of establishing communication links between Polish and Ukrainian General Staffs.
