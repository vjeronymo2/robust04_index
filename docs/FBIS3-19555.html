Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, March 17 (AFP) -- French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe said France would help Turkey affirm its "European identity," in talks here Thursday [17 March] with Turkish officials. Juppe spoke with reporters after discussions with Prime Minister Tansu Ciller and a private meeting with Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin. Turkish Government sources said they discussed the Kurdish separatist rebellion in Turkey and the case of seven Kurdish deputies who were detained on charges of "separatist" activity last week after their parliamentary immunity was lifted. The incident drew concern from several European countries, including France, who publicly urged the Turkish Government to respect legal guidelines in any action against the deputies. Juppe told reporters that Turkey and France were guided by the "same choices," which he listed as "democracy, justice and respect for human rights," and stressed that he "hoped that the two countries can continue to share the same principles." He made no public reference to the Kurdish deputies. The French foreign minister also called Turkey, which is a member of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, an "element of stability in a troubled region." Turkish diplomatic sources said Juppe's discussions here would deal essentially with international subjects of common interest. Juppe, who arrived Wednesday night on a two-day official visit, placed a wreath at the tomb of Mustafa Kemal Ataturk, a national hero and the founder of modern Turkey. He also met with Turkish President Suleyman Demirel and was to head to Istanbul later Thursday for meeting with Turkish and French businessmen.
