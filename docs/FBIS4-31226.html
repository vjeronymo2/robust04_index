BFN [Text] Some 45 foreign and 255 Bulgarian journalists covered the 41st Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP] Congress, which opened today. Our reporter Lilyana Yancheva, who will summarize the first day of the congress, was among them. [Yancheva] I would like to say that the report that party leader Zhan Videnov read today was well-received by the delegates, despite the fact that statements made during the afternoon debate were rather critical. This is precisely what I want to ask Mr. Videnov, who is sitting next to me: What do you think were the most essential and critical elements in the statements of the delegates who came to the congress from all parts of the country. [Videnov] I think that it is too early to say precisely what the focus of the discussion was, what the basic conflict it engenders is, and what direction the possible resolution will take. I think that the congress will determine the country's political calendar for the current year, and our resolution will contain the opinion of the Bulgarian Socialists regarding the tests that lie ahead. The fact that the congress agenda was amended today proves this. Now it contains the BSP election platform. A dispute broke out regarding the question of whether the democratic character of the transition will be guaranteed if a governmental and parliamentary crisis develops; guarantees were sought for a future leftist or leftist-centrist government, including listening to the opinions of our present and potential coalition partners. I think that this subject will unfold tomorrow, during the second day of the congress. [Yancheva] There were some very interesting statements on the part of Roma delegates to the congress; they voiced interesting claims. [Videnov] The Bulgarian Romas have the right to present claims to all political forces, state institutions, and public organizations. They have an exceptionally unequal social status, and they are also subject to serious discrimination, against which Bulgarian society as a whole and its institutions do not fight. Thus, I think that each party should think about this, and our party will certainly do so.
