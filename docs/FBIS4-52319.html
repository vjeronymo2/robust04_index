BFN [Text] Since the beginning of this year, Dong Nai Province has spent nearly 12 billion dong on three 15-kilovolt electricity projects to provide electricity service to isolated villages. The first project, at a cost of 4 billion dong, consists of a 15-kilovolt medium voltage line more than 15 km long, connecting Tan Phu District to the boundary of Lam Dong Province and a 0.4 kilovolt electricity line. The other two projects include a power line to Xuan Binh village, a heroic village in Nam Khanh District, with a total cost of more than 5 billion dong, and a low voltage line, at a cost of more than 2 billion dong, to Bao Han Mot village, a former revolution base in Thong Nhat District. With local funding and public contributions, Dong Nai is trying to provide electricity to all villages in the province by 1995.
