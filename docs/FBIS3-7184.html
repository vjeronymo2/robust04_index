Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 25 KYODO -- Taxes collected by local governments in fiscal 1992 shrank by 1.4 percent from the previous year to 34,568.3 billion yen due to the country's prolonged economic slump, according to a government report released Friday [25 March]. The report submitted by Home Affairs Minister Kanju Sato to the day's regular cabinet meeting said local tax revenues for the year that ended March 31, 1993 were down for the first time since fiscal 1975. The report, however, said local governments' total revenues, including local taxes and subsidies from the central government, came to 91,423.8 billion yen in fiscal 1992, up 6.7 percent from the previous year. Expenditures by local governments increased 6.9 percent to 89,559.7 billion yen, according to the report. The amount of bonds issued by local governments reached 10,099.7 billion yen, representing an increase of 40.5 percent from a year before. The figure brought the debt of local governments to some 79 trillion yen by the end of the fiscal 1992, the report said.
