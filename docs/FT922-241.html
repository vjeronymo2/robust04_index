920629 FT 29 JUN 92 / Observer: Home truths Since Alan Budd became chief economic adviser to the Treasury, he has studiously avoided making any personal forecasts about the economy. But he likes to tell how, when at London Business School, he sorely tried his family's patience by poring over reams of computer printouts at weekends. Finally, his son asked in exasperation: 'Daddy, why do you spend all your time trying to forecast things? Why don't you just wait and see what happens?' Treasury economists, take note.
