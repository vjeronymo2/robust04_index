Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] A delegation from President Paul Biya of Cameroon today delivered a fresh message to the head of state, General Sani Abacha, in Abuja. The delegation is led by the Cameroonian vice prime minister, Mr. Hamadou Moustapha. The vice prime minister expressed President Biya's concern at the turn of events in the past few days. He stressed that the problem could be better resolved through dialogue. Mr. Moustapha also stated President Biya's willingness to visit Nigeria to talk on the border problem. Responding, Gen. Abacha said President Biya's intention to visit Nigeria was a welcome development. The head of state expressed the Federal Government's desire to cooperate in finding a lasting solution to the differences between the two countries.
