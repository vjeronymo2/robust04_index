930907 FT 07 SEP 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: China expects cereal crop to be 10m tonnes lower CHINA'S 1993 grain crop will fall nearly 10m tonnes from 1992's total of 442.58m tonnes, according to the agriculture ministry, reports Reuter from Beijing. The Economic Information Daily quoted the ministry as saying that grain acreage this year had fallen by 20m mu (3m hectares or 7.4m acres) from the 1992 level. The country's summer grain crop reached a record 108.067m tonnes this year, up by 3.4m from the 1992 level, the ministry said. The official press has been full of reports on the low rewards of growing grain as against other, more profitable farm activities, and the rising prices of the raw materials farmers need.
