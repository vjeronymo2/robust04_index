930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / International Company News: United Technologies falls 12% UNITED Technologies, the US high-technology group, yesterday reported a 12 per cent drop in second-quarter net income as sharply lower profits at its Pratt & Whitney aero-engine business offset improved performances in most other parts of the group. Net income was Dollars 130m, or 89 cents a share, compared with Dollars 148m, or Dollars 1.03, for the same period of last year. Revenues were Dollars 5.6bn, down from Dollars 5.7bn. Pratt, which has suffered from the downturn in the airline industry, reported operating profits of Dollars 6m, down from Dollars 122m a year ago. Revenues dropped from Dollars 1.8bn to Dollars 1.7bn. Mr Robert Daniell, UTC chairman, said Pratt was showing an improvement from the first quarter, with an increase in commercial engine parts orders in the first six months. UTC's flight division, which includes Sikorsky, the helicopter manufacturer, produced operating profits of Dollars 96m, up from Dollars 63m, on revenues of Dollars 898m, down from Dollars 983m. Otis, the elevator group, made Dollars 98m (Dollars 84m) on revenues of Dollars 1.14bn (Dollars 1.12bn). Carrier, the air conditioner company, made Dollars 85m (Dollars 93m), on revenues of Dollars 1.24bn (Dollars 1.27bn). Automotive made Dollars 53m (Dollars 42m), on revenues of Dollars 640m (Dollars 633m). For the six months UTC had net income of Dollars 194m, or Dollars 1.31 a share, compared with Dollars 242m, or Dollars 1.79 last year before accounting changes.
