BFN [Text] Washington, June 18 (XINHUA) -- Most Americans support the U.S. Administration to resume talks with the Democratic Peoples' Republic of Korea (DPRK), a NEWSWEEK magazine poll said today. The poll showed that nearly 68 percent of 499 Americans interviewed by telephone believed the United States should resume talks with the DPRK. Twenty-five percent were against resuming talks before inspections on the DPRK's nuclear facilities, and seven percent said they didn't know, the poll said. U.S. President Bill Clinton said Thursday [19 Jun] that the U.S. would be willing to resume high-level talks with the DPRK if the DPRK is ready to freeze its nuclear program while talks go on.
