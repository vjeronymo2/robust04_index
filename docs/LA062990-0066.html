June 29, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

MAJOR SOUTHLAND FIRES

The status of major fires in Southern California:

1) SANTA BARBARA (INCLUDING GOLETA AND MONTECITO): Santa Barbara County

Status: Began Wednesday afternoon, still burning.

Damage: $190 million

Acres burned: 4,000

Homes destroyed: 567

Injuries: 40

Firefighters and equipment deployed: 800 firefighters; 62 engines, two bulldozers, one helicopter and three air tankers.

Cause: Arson suspected.

2) GLENDALE: Los Angeles County

Status: Began Wednesday afternoon, contained by 8 p.m. Wednesday.

Damage: $50 million

Acres burned: 350

Homes destroyed: At least 46

Homes damaged: 20

Injuries: Eight minor ones

Firefighters deployed: About 300 firefighters; three water-dropping helicopters, two observation helicopters.

Cause: Arson suspected, two people being sought.

3) CLAREMONT: Los Angeles County

Status: Began Thursday afternoon, contained but still burning by evening.

Damage: No estimate.

Acres burned: 400 acres.

Structures destroyed: None yet.

Injuries: None reported.

Firefighters and equipment deployed: More than 250 firefighters; 40 engines, 5 helicopters.

Cause: Arson suspected.

4) CHINO HILLS: Los Angeles, Orange and San Bernardino counties

Status: Began Wednesday, contained by nightfall Thursday.

Damage: No estimate

Acres burned: 6,250

Homes destroyed: 12

Injuries: Minor ones to eight firefighters.

Firefighters and equipment deployed: More than 600 firefighters; 103 engines, 12 bulldozers, four helicopters, six air tankers.

Cause: Arson suspected, one man arrested.

5) CORONA: Bedford Canyon, Riverside County

Status: Began Wednesday, still burning.

Damage: No estimate

Acres burned: 2,200 acres

Structures destroyed: 12

Injuries: Nine firefighters suffering effects of extreme heat and smoke inhalation.

Firefighters and equipment deployed: About 400 firefighters; nine engines, three helicopters.

Cause: A controlled burn that jumped fire lines of California Department of Forestry.

6) HEMET: Riverside County

Status: Fully controlled Thursday

Damage: No estimate

Acres burned: An undeveloped parcel of about 125 acres, southwest of Hemet.

Injuries: Seventeen firefighters burned, one severely.

Cause: Under investigation.

7) RINCON: San Diego County

Status: Began Tuesday night, controlled by 6 a.m. Thursday.

Damage: No estimate.

Acres burned: 300

Homes destroyed: None

Injuries: None

Firefighters deployed: 110

Cause: Arson suspected.
