BFN [Text] Cyprus wants to contribute as much as possible to improving security and stability in Europe and looks forward to a further strengthening of its relations with Europe, and particularly with the European Union. This was stressed today by Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis during his speech at a conference on stability in Europe that is being held in Paris. Referring to the Cyprus issue, the foreign minister stressed that for 20 years Cyprus has lived the tragic results of the lack of strong mechanisms to prevent expansionism and punish violations of international law. He also said that Cyprus is an example of a violation of borders and incitement of minority problems to facilitate expansionist designs.
