BFN [Announcer-read report over video; from the "National News Hookup" program] [Text] On the morning of 10 May local time, Swedish Prime Minister Carl Bildt met with Li Ruihuan, chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [CPPCC], in his office. Li Ruihuan said: China and Sweden are far away from each other and have no conflict of basic interests. On the contrary, there are broad prospects for economic cooperation because the economies of the two countries are mutually complementary. China is carrying out large-scale economic development, and there is much in Sweden's advanced technology and management experience that we can make use of. During my visit to your country, I have personally witnessed Swedish entrepreneurs' desire to strengthen cooperation with China. Our two countries should exert joint efforts to push bilateral cooperation to a new stage, which corresponds with the common interests and aspirations of the two peoples. The Swedish prime minister said that bilateral relations between China and Sweden have developed rapidly in recent years, and he expressed his hope that they will develop further. He also expressed Sweden's support for China's reentry into the GATT. [video shows Li and Bildt walking out of a room, then stop and stand side by side as photographers take pictures of them] Around noon, Troedsson, speaker of the parliament of the Kingdom of Sweden, met with Chairman Li Ruihuan in the parliament building. Li Ruihuan said: While developing the economy, China pays great attention to promoting socialist democracy and legality. In this regard, the CPPCC is playing an increasingly important role. Speaker Troedsson said that experience has shown that Swedish- Chinese cooperation and exchanges are in accordance with the fundamental interest of the Swedish and Chinese peoples. [video shows Li and Troedsson standing together, with glasses in hand, and speaking through an interpreter] In the afternoon, Chairman Li Ruihuan met with Swedish Minister of Foreign Trade Dinkelspiel. After successfully concluding his visit to Sweden, Chairman Li Ruihuan left Stockholm by a special plane and, less than an hour later, arrived at (Kathmond) Airport in Oslo. Upon his arrival, Chairman Li Ruihuan said in a written statement that China attaches importance to developing exchanges and cooperation with Norway in all fields. China wishes to develop a long-term, stable friendship with Norway. Bjorn Tore Godal, Norwegian acting prime minister and foreign minister, greeted Chairman Li Ruihuan and his entourage in the hall of the hotel where Chairman Li is staying. Chairman Li Ruihuan is making an official goodwill visit to the Kingdom of Norway at the invitation of the Norwegian Government.
