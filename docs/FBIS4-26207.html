BFN [By Ma Zhiping: "Boom Time for Free Markets"] [Text] The business volume in China's teeming free markets jumped 66 percent in the first quarter of this year, according to official sources. From January to March, business transactions hit 181.34 billion yuan ($20.84 billion), a 66.6 percent increase over the same period last year. Sales volume of free markets in urban areas reached 89.84 billion yuan (about $10 billion), an increase of 77 percent, while that in rural areas amounted to 91.5 billion yuan ($10.6 billion), up 57.6 percent. Five regions including Shanghai, Zhejiang, Tianjin, Jiangsu and the Tibet Autonomous Region gained a more than 100 percent increase in business volume, according to the latest statistics provided by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce, which oversees the country's market development. Free market business transections in Zhejiang Province, which has been leading the nation's free market development, reached a record 24.97 billion yuan ($2.87 billion).
