920721 FT 21 JUL 92 / International Capital Markets: Slowdown in German M&A activity MERGERS and acquisitions business in Germany slowed sharply in the first half of 1992, but purchases of companies in the former east Germany prevented an even steeper slide, according to latest figures from M & A International, a consultancy based near Frankfurt. The total number of deals involving German companies fell by 9 per cent from 953 in the same period of last year to 843. This compared with around 700 each in the first six months of 1989 and 1990. Because of slow economic growth among Germany's main trading partners, the total transactions in Germany by foreign companies fell by 14 per cent to 212, while deals by German companies abroad fell by 16 per cent to 98. The US, with 35 deals, remained the largest buyer of German companies. This was followed by Switzerland (with 43 deals), France (32), and the UK (28).
