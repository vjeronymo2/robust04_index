Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 11 (YONHAP) -- Korea's 10 largest business groups had exports of 47.32 billion U.S. dollars last year, or 57.4 percent of total exports, the Trade, Industry and Energy Ministry said Tuesday. Samsung, Hyundai, Lucky-Goldstar and Daewoo accounted for 40.2 percent of Korea's total exports of 82.44 billion dollars. Samsung ranked first with 12.7 percent of exports, up from 11.2 percent a year earlier. Hyundai ranked second with 8.3 billion dollars, off 370 million dollars because of labor disputes at 10 percent, down from 11.3 percent. Hyundai led Samsung last year at 8.67 billion dollars to 8.6 billion dollars. Lucky-Goldstar was stable in third place at 7.7 billion dollars, or 9.3 percent of exports compared with 9.2 percent in 1992. Daewoo followed at 6.8 billion dollars (8.2 percent), Hanjin at 4.81 billion dollars (5.8 percent), Sunkyong at 4.48 billion dollars (5.4 percent), Ssangyong at 2.6 billion dollars (3.1 percent) and Kia at 1.1 billion dollars (1.3 percent). The Hyosung Group had 2.64 billion dollars in exports, or 3.2 percent, for sixth place, but is not among the 10 biggest groups. Hanwha and Lotte ranked ninth and tenth at 0.7 percent and 0.4 percent, respectively. Ssangyong slipped from 3.2 percent to 3.1 percent, but the rest improved their figures.
