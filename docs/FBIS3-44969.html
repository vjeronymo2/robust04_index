Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 11 (XINHUA) -- The Sixth Far East and South Pacific Games for the Disabled will be held in Beijing from September 4 to October 10 this year. At Tuesday's [11 January] preparatory meeting of the organizing committee of the Far East and South Pacific [FESP] Games for the Disabled, it was disclosed that more than 2,000 athletes have applied to compete in the upcoming games. Deng Pufang, chairman of the Chinese Federation for the Disabled, presided over Wednesday's meeting during which Mayor of Beijing Li Qiyan and former Mayor of Beijing Chen Xitong both made speeches. Chen Xitong said that the FESP games for the disabled are one of the major events of 1994 and are a hit event for the disabled people of the municipality. Chen said that it is another opportunity to show the world China's openness to the outside world. He stressed, "Beijing will try its very best to guarantee the games a great success." Li Qiyan said: "The Beijing municipal government will endorse the games in the aspects of organization, equipment and finance. It is another opportunity to present Beijing to the entire world." Vice-Mayor of Beijing Zhang Baifa also attended the meeting.
