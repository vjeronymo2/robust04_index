920630 FT 30 JUN 92 / Sir Leon Brittan commends investment rules SIR Leon Brittan, EC financial services commissioner, yesterday promised that the agreement reached on the Investment Services Directive (ISD) would be 'good news for the City of London' as the capital requirements and trading rules were not designed to damage any market. He was speaking after EC finance ministers reached an agreement on the ISD last night. His statement will be greeted with relief by the financial services houses in London, many of which were concerned that the ISD might force them to publish information on security prices and volumes, and so put them at a disadvantage. The main issue that concerned the City was transparency, which would require securities houses to publish price and volume information on a screen-based system. Some of the EC member countries were pushing for immediate publication of such information. Many of the UK-based securities houses were anxious, however, that the immediate release of such information in the market might work against their interests. Market makers in securities argued that if they were forced to buy a large block of shares from an institution and the information became available to the market immediately, their competitors would be able to mark the price down, possibly forcing them to sell the shares at a loss. The solution agreed yesterday was broadly what had been expected by several large financial service houses in the UK. The question of freedom of access - banks will automatically be allowed to join as members of European stock exchanges - is seen as significant for the German commercial banks. . Until now they have not been able to enjoy full membership. A senior adviser at a US securities house said: 'Progress on the ISD is important as it means that further liberalisation of the European financial markets will be possible.'
