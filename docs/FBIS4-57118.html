BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Bolivia monitored through 12 May. The National Statistics Institute reported that mining production improved in the year's first quarter as a result of gold exploitation that increased 113 percent. It rose 9.4 percent in March, an increase of four points compared with the previous two months. Mining exports increased by 17.7 percent. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 1 May 94 National Section p 2) National Industry Chamber President Gary Lacunza expressed his concern to President Sanchez de Lozada over the drop in the GDP growth rate between 1991 and 1993. He reported that from a growth rate of 7.2 percent in 1991, it dropped to 3.37 percent in 1992 and reached no more than a 1.08 percent increase in GDP in 1993. (Santa Cruz EL MUNDO in Spanish 1 May 94 National Section p 1) During his recent visit to Germany, Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar Quiroga obtained a $30 million credit that will be used to implement several projects in Santa Cruz, Oruro, and Beni Departments. The Bolivian Foreign Ministry reported that German cooperation totalled $380 million in the 1989-1993 period. (Cochabamba LOS TIEMPOS in Spanish 3 May 94 p A6) The Spanish Government granted an $85 million loan to Bolivia during a visit to Spain by Economic Development Minister Fernando Cossio and Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar. (La Paz PRESENCIA in Spanish 4 May 94 p 12) The INE reported that the April inflation rate was 0.49 percent and that the cumulative inflation rate was 1.79 percent in the year's first four months. (La Paz HOY in Spanish 4 May 94 Economic Section p 17) The foreign debt totalled $3.788 billion through 31 December 1993, of which $2.1 billion are owed to multinational organizations and $1.62 billion to bilateral institutions. The Inter-American Development Bank is the main multilateral creditor, with $1.223 billion, while Japan is its main bilateral creditor with $507 million. (La Paz HOY in Spanish 5 May 94 Economic Section p 20)
