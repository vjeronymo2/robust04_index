930909 FT 09 SEP 93 / Cut-price Times holds sales rise THE TIMES newspaper appears to be maintaining an increase in sales of about 30 per cent three days into its cut-price experiment, according to research at 600 newsagents. The independent research suggests that overall broadsheet sales have increased by nearly 3 per cent since The Times cut its cover price by 15p to 30p during the week and to 40p on Saturdays. According to the research the Financial Times has been unaffected, while both The Guardian and The Independent have increased their sales. The Daily Telegraph has lost an estimated 4 per cent. In Scotland, sales of The Herald and The Scotsman have been hit. Mid-market papers such as The Daily Mail and the Daily Express do not appear to have been noticeably affected.
