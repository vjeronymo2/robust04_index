Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Benjamin Yeh] [Text] Taipei, Jun. 14 (CNA) -- The Mainland Affairs Council (MAC) on Friday [14 January] agreed to a request by a local airline [words indistinct] demanded that their passenger airplanes bound for [words indistinct] permitted to make emergency landing in mainland China. MAC Vice Chairman Kao Kung-lien told the press that his council has no intention to intervene in emergency landings. "The request is merely for safety considerations...it has nothing to do with the establishment of direct air links with the mainland," Kao stressed. Formosa Airlines will begin flight service from Taipei to Matsu on Jan. 17. Matsu is about 10 nautical miles off the coast of Mainland China. The airline asked the Ministry of Transportation and Communications to make the emergency landing request to the mainland authority. A ministry official, however, asserted that the likelihood of landing on the mainland is small as long as pilots fly planes in accordance with standard procedures required by the Civil Aeronautics Administration.
