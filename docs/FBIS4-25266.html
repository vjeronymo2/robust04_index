BFN [Text] Beijing, June 7 (XINHUA) -- Renowned Chinese atomic physicist Zhu Guangya was elected today as president of the newly-founded Chinese Academy of Engineering (CAE), one of China's two top scientific bodies, in a secret ballot at a general meeting of the CAE academicians. The 69-year-old Zhu, also an academician of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, is a research fellow with the Commission of Science, Technology and Industry for National Defence. He is also chairman of the Chinese Association for Science and Technology, a member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and vice-chairman of National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference. Also at the meeting, Zhu Gaofeng, an expert in posts and telecommunications, Shi Changxu, a metal expert, Pan Jiazheng, a hydro-engineering expert, and Lu Liangshu, an agronomist, were elected vice-presidents. They are all academicians of the CAE.
