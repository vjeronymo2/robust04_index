920611 FT 11 JUN 92 / Bank of Scotland bows out of refinancing package SENIOR executives of Mirror Group Newspapers have been told that Bank of Scotland has decided not to go ahead with providing Pounds 30m towards a refinancing package. The Bank of Scotland has been banker to the Glasgow-based Scottish Daily Record and Sunday Mail newspapers for nearly a century. It is understood that the Daily Record may now consider moving its account to another bank. Bank of Scotland is one of a consortium of banks negotiating a medium-term package to provide MGN with greater stability while decisions over its future are taken. The package is believed to involve a facility of Pounds 250m for two years. It is particularly important for the Record because new colour presses are already in Glasgow, but there is insufficient money to install them. MGN was told that the reason for Bank of Scotland's decision was 'financial prudence'. Bank of Scotland said last night: 'The bank has not withdrawn from the consortium, nor has it reduced the limits (of the Scottish Daily Record).' National Westminster, the senior consortium bank, has been reallocating the Pounds 30m among other member banks and it is believed that most of it has been placed. The refinancing of MGN is not thought to have been jeopardised. The company is expected to go ahead with the publication of its accounts later this month. That will be followed soon after by a relisting of its shares. Observer, Page 21
