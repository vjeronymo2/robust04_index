Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The foreign minister, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali, has arrived in Tashkent on the first leg of his week-long visit to the Central Asia states of Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, and Kazakhstan. He was received at the airport by his Uzbek counterpart Mr. Saidmukhtar Saidkasymov. Talking to newsmen at Tashkent airport, he said the Government of Pakistan attaches great importance to improving bilateral relations with the Central Asian countries, especially in trade and commerce. He said he is carrying a message of goodwill from the prime minister, Ms. Benazir Bhutto, for the Uzbek president, Mr. Islam Karimov. Earlier on his departure for Tashkent from Islamabad airport today, Sardar Asif Ahmad Ali said he will exchange views with the leadership of the Central Asian countries on regional situation, including latest developments in Kashmir, Afghanistan, Tajikistan, Nagorno-Karabakh, and ECO [Economic Cooperation Organization] affairs. Talking to PTV representative Abdul Khaliq Sarghana, he said he will review the progress on agreements signed between Pakistan and these states. [Begin Ali recording] The Central Asian republics are practically our neighbors and we have good relations with them; and since this government has just come in, we have to reinforce these relations and try to improve the relations in the regional context. [end recording]
