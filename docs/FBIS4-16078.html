BFN [Text] INTERFAX learned that the composition of the Moldovan delegation which will fly to Moscow on 14 April for taking part in the CIS summit was approved in Kishinev [Chisinau] on Wednesday. It is led by President Mircea Snegur. For the first time the delegation includes representatives of power structures of the self-proclaimed Transdniestrian Republic of Moldova. Sources in Kishinev say that Moldova plans to sign all documents submitted for discussion in Moscow, except those regarding military-political issues. Among them "On the Council of Defense Ministers of the CIS member-states," "On additional measures to stabilize the situation on the Tajik-Afghan border." Snegur will also sign in Moscow the document on Moldova adhering the decision of the Council of the CIS States to sign the CIS Charter except articles concerning military-political issues.
