BFN [Text] Cairo, 26 Jun (MENA)--President Muhammad Husni Mubarak left Cairo for the Libyan Jamahiriyah this afternoon. He will meet with brother Colonel Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi. The two leaders will discuss the developments of the Libyan-Western crisis, "the Lockerbie crisis," in light of Libya's recent offer to put the two Libyan suspects on trial before a tribunal consisting entirely of Scottish judges that would meet at the International Court of Justice. President Mubarak and Col. al-Qadhdhafi will review the developments of the Middle East peace process and current consultations over ways of boosting the negotiations on the other Arab-Israeli negotiating tracks. They will also discuss inter-African conflicts and some important international issues that have implications for the Arab region. President Mubarak is accompanied on this two-day visit by Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa; Information Minister Safwat al-Sharif; Engineer Sulayman Mutawalli, minister of transport and communications; Dr. 'Atif 'Ubayd, public business sector minister and minister of state for administrative development and environmental affairs; Dr. Zakariya 'Azmi, director of the presidential office; and Dr. Usamah al-Baz, foreign ministry first under secretary and director of the president's office for political affairs.
