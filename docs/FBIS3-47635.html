Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 13 (YONHAP) -- South Korea exported 280 strategic commodities for 102,285,000 U.S. dollars in the final quarter of 1993, the Trade, Industry and Energy Ministry said Thursday. Korea joined the Coordinating Committee for Multilateral Export Controls (COCOM) in October after establishing a system according to a memorandum of understanding on exports of strategic commodities and technical data signed in September 1987. The exports comprised 100,912,000 dollars of telecommunications equipment, including computers, 963,000 dollars of machinery, including navigation systems, and 410,000 dollars of materials, including carbon fiber. Exports to former or current communist countries, including China, Poland, Russia, Vietnam and Hungary, were less than 19.8 million dollars, or 19.4 percent of the total. The remaining exports of strategic goods were to Singapore, Germany, Malaysia, Hong Kong, the United States and Taiwan. Import certificates issued by the government reached 1,962 million dollars. The certificates are promises by a company not to export strategic items that it has purchased, and must be sent to the seller before he can ship the goods.
