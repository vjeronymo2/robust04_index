Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Islamabad, Jan 5 (AFP) -- Afghan Prime Minister Golboddin Hekmatyar would welcome any visit from Pakistan aimed at restoring peace in Kabul, his official representative here said Wednesday. "Hekmatyar has always welcomed visitors from Pakistan," Mangal Hoseyn said in a statement on the Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party of Afghanistan] leader's response to a planned mediation trip to Kabul by Pakistani Foreign Minister Asif Ahmad Ali. The foreign minister had to put off a scheduled visit after fighting broke out in Kabul on New Year's Day. "We respect Pakistan's concern for peace and stability in Afghanistan and are always ready to receive any delegation from the brotherly country," Hoseyn said.
