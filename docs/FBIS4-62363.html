BFN [Text] Well-informed sources in Yerevan told INTERFAX that the Armenian and Azeri Defense Ministers and the commander of the Karabakh defense forces would be meeting in Moscow under the mediation of the Russian Defense Minister on 16 May. Serge Sarakissyan, Mamedrafi Mamedov, Samvel Babayan and Pavel Grachev will be expected to confirm agreements negotiated on 18 February in Moscow and on 4 and 5 May, under the mediation of the Commonwealth's Interparliamentary Assembly, in Bishkek. Armenian Deputy Defense Minister Asvatsatur Petrossyan told newsmen in Yerevan on 14 May that within three days a peace agreement would be signed to put an end, as early as this year, to the war in Karabakh. According to Petrossyan, the Moscow meeting of the defense ministers and the commander of the Karabakh defense forces would concentrate on the scheduled introduction and deployment of a peacekeeping contingent in the area of the Armenian-Azeri conflict. Petrossyan said Armenia would welcome the introduction of Russian peacekeepers into the disputed area. He said that in the past five months 50 Azeri POW's and hostages had been exchanged for 25 Armenians. He said as many as 180 Armenians were listed as missing. Some 10 to 15 percent of these, he said, were civilians taken by Azeris off passenger trains. Petrossyan denied rumors on the existence of POW concentration camps in Armenia.
