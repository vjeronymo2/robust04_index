931104 FT 04 NOV 93 / World Trade News: US tobacco ruling attacked US legislation requiring the country's cigarette manufacturers to use at least 75 per cent US-grown tobacco attacked yesterday by Mr Martin Broughton, chief executive of BAT Industries, the UK tobacco and financial services group, Philip Rawstorne writes in London. It was ' a flagrant breach of Gatt rules' he said. Because of the move, BAT had closed its tobacco leaf operations in Panama, which exported to the US, at a cost of Pounds 7m in lost profits.
