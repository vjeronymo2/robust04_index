BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of fileworthy reports from 28 May-1 June on counternarcotics operations in countries monitored by FBIS London Bureau. Sources are given in parentheses at the end of each report.

Algeria
Customs officiers at Algiers port confiscated 70 kg of hemp on 1 June from an immigrant here on his one-month holiday who wanted to smuggle the hemp from Algeria to Marseille. (Algiers ENTV Television Network in Arabic 1900 GMT 1 Jun 94)
Former Yugoslav Republic Of Macedonia
Police in Skopje discovered and confiscated 32 kilograms of heroin from a Volkswagen van belonging to Arif Ismaili of Skopje on 26 May. The raid took place near the Bellevue hotel on the outskirts of the city. According to a police report, the heroin was transported from Istanbul via the so-called new Balkan east-west land corridor and was supposed to be smuggled to Scandinavia through Bulgaria, Macedonia, Albania and Italy. (Belgrade TANJUG Domestic Service in Serbo-Croatian 0838 GMT 28 May 94)
Kyrgyzstan
A large batch of opium has been confiscated by Kyrgyz security agents on the Kyrgyz-Tajik border. Information about the planned drug-trafficking operation was provided to the Kyrgyz security services by a confidential source. (Moscow ITAR-TASS in English 0917 GMT 1 Jun 94)
Tajikistan
In the first 4 months of this year Tajikistan's law enforcement agencies destroyed opium poppy on about 164 hectares, largely in Leninabad region in the country's north. According to data available to the law enforcers, the transit of narcotics to CIS countries and Europe through Tajikistan has considerably increased of late. Narcotics are smuggled largely across Tajikistan's border with Afghanistan that lies through Gorny Badakhshan, where the terrain is arduous and there are no technical installations along the border. (Moscow INTERFAX in English 1518 GMT 28 May 94)
