Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Osaka, Jan. 11 KYODO -- Six Japanese makers of mini vehicles have reached a basic accord to standardize parts for their products, one of them firms said Tuesday. Takashi Toyozumi, president of Daihatsu Motor Co., announced the agreement at a news conference. The six makers are Daihatsu, Honda Motor Co., Mitsubishi Motors Corp., Suzuki Motor Corp., Fuji Heavy Industries Ltd. and Mazda Motor Corp. Toyozumi said the agreement is aimed at slashing production costs to overcome the long-drawn-out recession.
