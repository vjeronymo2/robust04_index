Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan and Saudi Arabia emphasized the need for an unconditional cease-fire and cessation of all hostilities in Afghanistan. In a joint communique issued in Riyadh and Islamabad simultaneously, King Fahd Bin-'Abd-al-'Aziz and Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari urged the Afghan leaders to sincerely implement the accord reached in Islamabad, which was authenticated at holy Mecca, as well as the Jalalabad agreement. They urged all the Afghan leaders to cooperate with the Islamic Conference Organization and its secretary general for ensuring and monitoring a permanent cease-fire as envisaged in the Mecca Accord. Expressing grave concern at the happenings in Afghanistan, King Fahd and President Leghari said the situation has become all the more tragic and a cause of concern as the Afghan leaders had expressed their firm determination to work for peace.
