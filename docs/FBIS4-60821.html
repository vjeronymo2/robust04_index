BFN [Text] Moscow 30 April TASS -- Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis ended his visit to Moscow and left for Riga late on Saturday. During the visit Ulmanis and President Boris Yeltsin signed an agreement to withdraw Russian troops from Latvia by 31 August 1994 and a number of agreements concerning the status of Russian servicemen in Latvia, members of their families and military pensioners. In an interview with ITAR-TASS after the talks Yeltsin said that "Russian-Latvian documents signed today in the Kremlin were drawn up with full consent of both sides." Ulmanis said the reached agreements aim at normalising relations between the two countries. He stressed he and Yeltsin reached "full understanding" on all issues included in the agenda of the talks. Latvian Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs said that the agreement on economic cooperation and trade between Russia and Latvia is also practically ready and will facilitate the soonest possible implementation of the reached accords. Birkavs expressed the confidence that the agreement can be signed during Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin's upcoming visit to Latvia due to take place in the second half of May.
