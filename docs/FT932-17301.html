930401 FT 01 APR 93 / UK Company News: NatWest sued in US over dealings NATIONAL Westminster Bank is being sued in US federal courts over stock dealings on behalf of Phar-Mor, a drugstore chain which went into Chapter 11 bankruptcy in August, it was disclosed in the bank's annual report. It is being sued for allegedly placing Dollars 112m (Pounds 79m) of common stock on behalf of Phar-Mor which should have been registered. NatWest is also said to have made misrepresentations in connection with the sale of Dollars 24m of stock. The bank said it was contesting the allegations and did not believe they would have a material effect on net assets. It also disclosed that it had received claims in connection with the collapse of Maxwell Communication Corporation. The highest paid UK director was Mr Richard Goeltz, the chief financial officer, who received a total of Pounds 383,900, including Pounds 99,074 of expenses incurred by the bank in his move from New York to London.
