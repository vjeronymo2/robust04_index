July 6, 1990, Friday, P.M. Final

P. M. BRIEFING;

NORWAY SHUTS OIL SITES IN STRIKE

Norway's state-owned Statoil oil company today shut down production on four of its seven platforms in the North Sea because of wildcat strikes that so far have cost the country about $95 million.

Statoil chief Harald Norvik used loudspeakers aboard the offshore platforms today to announce his company's intention to fight the strikers, but the workers apparently paid little attention to his speech.
