BFN [Text] According to information passed to KONTAKT news agency by the press service of the Georgian Defense Ministry, Georgian Defense Minister Vardiko Nadibaidze met Teodor Starcevic, head of the UN mission in Georgia, and Brigadier General Hvidegaard, head of the UN observers in Georgia, who had been in Abkhazia since the beginning of the month [as heard]. The meeting was attended by Mr Sandro Kavsadze, chairman of the committee for human rights and ethnic relations, and Nodar Tatarashvili, chief of general staff and first deputy defense minister. Mr Hvidegaard expressed an interest in problems pertaining to the deployment of peacekeeping forces in Abkhazia, their number, and the location of their stationing. In addition, he acquainted the minister with the mandate of the UN mission about which he had talked with Mr Sandro Kavsadze. The presented draft mandate stipulates the control over the activities of peacekeeping forces and the cases of violation of the agreement as well as close relations with units of the peacekeeping troops. However, according to Mr Hvidegaard, the UN mission and the CIS peacekeeping forces will not interfere in each other's functions. The minister thanked the guests for the visit and emphasized the urgency of the arrival of peacekeeping forces in the conflict zone in order to secure the establishment of peace in the region. As for the number and location of the peacekeeping forces, this issue is being discussed in Moscow, where the deputy minister, Guram Nikolaishvili is at the moment. Speaking about the mandate, the minister said most of its paragraphs were acceptable for him. He also emphasized that cooperation between the UN observers and the international peacekeeping forces played a decisive role in the settlement of the conflict. He promised the visitors actual assistance in handling their problems.
