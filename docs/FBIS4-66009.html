BFN [Text] Brussels, May 25 (AFP) -- A high-level European Union [EU] team will travel to Algiers on May 30 to call for respect for human rights and urge "national dialogue" between the government and Islamic fundamentalists, a Belgian Foreign Ministry spokesman said Wednesday [25 May]. Greek Deputy Foreign Minister George Papandreou, whose country currently chairs the 12-member EU, Belgian Foreign Minister Willy Claes, the former chairman and a senior German official, representing the next country to chair the organisation, were mandated to make the trip by the EU at a meeting earlier this month. The representatives will convey a "clear message calling for respect for human rights" addressed to all factions in the country, the Belgian spokesman said. The EU will also express its satisfaction with a recent agreement concluded by Algeria and the International Monetary Fund on economic reforms. The EU recently released a second 150 million ECUs (172 million dollars) of an aid programme frozen since 1991.
