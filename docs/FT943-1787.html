940922 FT 22 SEP 94 / UK Company News: SBH acquires Kelvin Central in Pounds 11.1m deal Kelvin Central, a Scottish bus company sold by Scottish Transport Group to a management buy-out for Pounds 1 in 1990, is to be acquired by SBH, the parent company of Strathclyde Buses Group, in a deal which values Kelvin at Pounds 11.1m. For each of their shares, SBH is offering Kelvin shareholders Pounds 10 in cash or Pounds 10 nominal of loan notes or 10 new SBH shares. Over 53 per cent of Kelvin's shareholders have agreed to accept the offer. The management buy-out deal in 1990 included a Pounds 1m deferred consideration, which Kelvin Central will now repay. SBH reported pre-tax profits of Pounds 2.1m and turnover of Pounds 58.2m in the year to March 31. At that date it had net assets of Pounds 21.5m. Kelvin Central reported losses of Pounds 1.5m on turnover of Pounds 27.3m in the year to December 31. At that date it had net assets of Pounds 6.2m.
