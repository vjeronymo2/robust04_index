910510 FT 10 MAY 91 / UK Company News: Decline goes on at MMT Computing A FURTHER downturn at MMT Computing in the six months to February 28 saw taxable profits decline 38 per cent from Pounds 1.01m to Pounds 626,000. This followed the previous full-year result of Pounds 1.87m, a fall of 13 per cent, writes Katrina Lowe. The USM-quoted computer systems consultancy described the period as the most difficult it had ever encountered. It had been forced to make redundancies but the figure was less than 10. It was still burdened with two leases on unused space at its previous premises. The link with French computer group Axime, holder of a 20 per cent stake in MMT, had produced no work. That situation had not changed in the current half. Of the two young subsidiaries, MMT Computing (AS) made its first contribution 'at a very respectable level' but MMT Computing (Training) incurred a loss; it looked likely, however, to report a profit for the second half. On prospects for the full year, Mr Mike Tilbrook, chairman, said: 'Analysts have forecast profits of about Pounds 1.2m and the company has a history of beating these forecasts.' The interim dividend is maintained at 1p on earnings per share of 3.4p (5.7p).
