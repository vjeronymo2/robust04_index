BFN [Report by Tleuzhan Yesilbayev: "Kazakhstan: Opposition Has Come to Life"] [Text] Alma-Ata [Almaty] -- Parties, public movements, and associations in opposition to the policies of the president and government of Kazakhstan have formed a coordinating council in Alma-Ata. It includes representatives of the "People's Congress of Kazakhstan" party, the Socialist Party, the Communist Party, the Democratic Committee on Human Rights, the workers movement, and other organizations. The coordinating council is headed by S. Abdildin, former chairman of the Republic's Supreme Council. According to what he said at a news conference, the constructive opposition will promote the building of a democratic society through its deputies' faction in Kazakhstan's new parliament.
