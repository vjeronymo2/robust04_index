August 19, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

SILENCE ON BIGOTRY

John Boudreau's Aug. 12 article on "God's Country," a play about neo-Nazism, reflects the consequences of change in an America that has been too white, too greedy and too blind to its own weaknesses -- one of which is the belief that being white, and American, is somehow "special."

The play will make a difference for a good minute! Most of the audiences will continue to be part of one of the country's biggest weaknesses -- that group known as the Silent Majority.

ROSEMARY C. WATSON

Los Angeles
