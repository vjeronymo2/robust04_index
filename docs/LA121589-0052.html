December 15, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

REASONS FOR GLUCOSE TEST

Question: An uncle of mine was referred for a glucose test because his doctor thought that he had diabetes. It turned out that his glucose level was not normal, but that he doesn't have diabetes. I thought that diabetes meant that you did not have a normal glucose level. What else could it be?

Answer: Diabetes is usually the cause of an abnormal blood glucose measurement. However, blood glucose also may be affected by severe stress, an overactive thyroid, pancreatic tumors and use of diuretics.
