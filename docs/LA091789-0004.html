September 17, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

After recently watching America's most popular television show, "Roseanne," I sincerely believe that the viewing public wants good old family programming. What a great half hour! I learned so much to pass on to our younger generation. Don't Roseanne and her Neanderthal husband set a shining example of how to act drunk in a bar and minutes later arrive home, as if they did not drive even the least bit intoxicated? Hey, and that barroom fight. There is nothing like punching out a vulnerable drunken man, a good lesson for the impressionable youth of our country. Boy, I can still feel the testosterone racing violently through my veins at the sound of hand meeting face. Is this how family programming should make me feel?

Michael S. Hull, Playa del Rey
