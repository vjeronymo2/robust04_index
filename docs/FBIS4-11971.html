BFN [Text] Lebanese Prime Minister Rafiq al-Hariri received in Beirut today, Majid 'Izzu Ruhaybani, the Syrian minister of reconstruction and building, and his delegation who arrived in Beirut this morning. Talks centered on bilateral relations, historical links, and existing ties between the two countries and ways to bolster them to realize joint aims and interests. In a statement to reporters, the minister emphasized continued Syrian support of Lebanon's march to entrench its national unity, rebuild it, and support its struggle to liberate its occupied territory. The minister said: At President Hafiz al-Asad's instructions, Syria is always prepared--prompted by its brotherly feelings--to implement any projects by Syrian general construction companies if Lebanon needs them. Ruhaybani alluded to schools constructed in southern Lebanon by Syrian companies in cooperation with the higher committee in support of the south. He said: We are prepared to contribute to reconstructing Lebanon within the framework of the close brotherly cooperation between the two countries. This afternoon, Ruhaybani and his delegation held meetings with Dr. Hasan Yusuf, head of the council of the south, and the council's members. They discussed joint work to implement services projects being implemented in southern Lebanon by joint financing of the Syrian committee of solidarity with the people of the south and by the council of the south. Discussion also dealt with aspects of future cooperation and ways to support the people of southern Lebanon and western al-Biqa' to confront the repeated and bestial Israeli attacks. Dr. Hasan Yusuf, chairman of the council of the south, praised Syria's continuous assistance to the people of the south, under President Hafiz al-Asad, to ensure the means of their steadfastness in the face of the Israeli occupation.
