BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis said that a picture has begun taking shape as to what the next steps by the United Nations on the Cyprus issue should be. In a statement after his meeting with UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali this evening, Mr. Mikhailidhis added that the picture will be formulated as soon as Gustave Feissels' report on his latest meeting with Rauf Denktas arrives at the UN Headquarters. Asked to comment on reports that Denktas has accepted the confidence-building measures, the foreign minister said that the important thing is what Gustave Feissel will say to the secretary general and not what Denktas says. He added: The UN Secretariat is ready, as soon as it receives the Feissel report, to finalize the plan of action and suggestions with which it will apply to the Security Council. The foreign minister further noted that he has the impression that the suggestions he made to the secretary general will be jointly assessed in formulating the positions of the UN Secretariat. A press spokesman for the UN secretary general said that at the meeting they discussed all the aspects of the secretary general's good offices mission including the confidence-building measures and other questions of substance. The spokesman said that the United Nations continues to feel that the set of measures proposed is balanced with advantages for both sides. He was not in a position to comment on the latest Denktas statement and made it clear that he is waiting for Feissel's report. In an interview with Voice of America, the foreign minister described the results of his contacts in Washington and New York as very satisfactory. He added: The suggestions by the Cypriot side for getting out of the current situation concern the need for a new approach to the Cyprus issue for the Security Council's involvement which would mean upgrading the dialogue and strengthening the whole effort as well as creating a new agenda. The foreign minister concluded: The Cypriot side's suggestions were listened to with care and interest and will be evaluated properly.
