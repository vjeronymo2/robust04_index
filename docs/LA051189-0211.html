May 11, 1989, Thursday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

AMWAY WOULD SELL PERFUME UNIT

Amway Corp. said today it would sell Avon Products Inc.'s prestige fragrance business if its attempt to buy Avon for more than $2 billion succeeds.

The business includes such higher-priced perfumes as Giorgio Beverly Hills, Oscar de la Renta, Cher, Valentino, Perry Ellis and Deneuve, which are sold in stores instead of by Avon's person-to-person sales force.

Avon said the business is expected to run up sales of more than $300 million this year. Spokesman John Cox said the company had no comment on Amway's stated plan, which was in a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission.
