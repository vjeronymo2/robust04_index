June 6, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

THE DRUG CLINIC: ALCOHOLISM AND 'WILLFUL MISCONDUCT' CONTROVERSY

Question: Has anything been done about the recent Supreme Court decision that said that the disabling effects of chronic alcoholism are considered to be the result of willful misconduct?

Answer: The Supreme Court decision that you are referring to was made in April, 1988, in the case of Traynor and McKelvey vs. the Veterans Administration. It said, in effect, that these two veterans, who are recovering alcoholics, were ineligible to get an extension to utilize GI educational benefits. The decision sparked a lot of indignation -- both pro and con. It also marshaled an organized effort by the National Council on Alcoholism Inc. to change the Supreme Court decision.

Congress enacted legislation on Oct. 20, 1988, that said, "The disabling effects of chronic alcoholism shall not be considered to be the result of willful misconduct." The President signed this legislation Nov. 18, 1988. Since then the Veterans Administration has instructed all of its affiliates that the new legislation is in effect. Anyone who may have regained eligibility for educational benefits through the change should contact his or her local VA office.
