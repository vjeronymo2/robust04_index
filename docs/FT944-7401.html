941124 FT 24 NOV 94 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Region tumbles in response to US worries Wall Street's overnight fall took a hefty toll of a region lacking the influence of Tokyo, which was closed for the Japanese Thanksgiving holiday. HONG KONG tumbled a further 4.2 per cent in reaction to US worries, the Hang Seng index finishing 372.40 down at 8,576.03 after plunging to the day's low of 8,379.88 in the opening minutes of trade. Among the index heavyweights, HSBC fell HKDollars 2.75 or 3.1 per cent to HKDollars 85.50 and Henderson Land shed HKDollars 2.30 or 5 per cent to HKDollars 43.50. Analysts noted that an auction of two plots of government land, which produced results close to those predicted, failed to give the market significant support. Turnover amounted to a provisional HKDollars 5.9bn, compared with HKDollars 4.8bn on Tuesday. The H-share index of Chinese stocks listed in Hong Kong fell 19.48 or 1.6 per cent to 1,186.75. SHANGHAI's B share index was down 4.3 per cent by the close, led by a newcomer, Shanghai Lujiazui Finance and Trade Zone Development, and in line with the sharp fall in Hong Kong. The index lost 3.04 at 67.63 in heavy volume of 21.1m shares. Shanghai Lujiazui, which made a strong debut on Tuesday, encountered heavy overseas selling and weakened Dollars 0.072 or 8.8 per cent to Dollars 0.750 in volume of 17.3m shares. BANGKOK's SET index hit a low of 1,303 before finishing 69.96 or 5 per cent off at 1,332.85 in turnover of Bt13.7bn. The biggest falls were in banking, down 5.5 per cent in Bt3.9bn of turnover, and finance, 6.8 per cent lower with shares worth Bt2.7bn exchanged. The mild recovery in the afternoon appeared to be encouraged by foreign investors picking up cheap stocks in energy and petrochemicals, among other sectors. JAKARTA retreated 3.3 per cent as selective bargain hunting failed to halt a broad sell-off, and the official index dropped 16.66 to 482.01. Among the losers were Indosat and Gajah Tunggal, which fell almost 4 per cent to Rp7,825 and 6 per cent to Rp3,100 respectively. SINGAPORE picked up from its lows as some bargain hunting appeared among the blue chips, but the Straits Times Industrial index still finished 52.24 or 2.3 per cent down at 2,217.81. OCBC foreign dipped 90 cents to SDollars 14.60, Cycle and Carriage also 90 cents to SDollars 12.60 and Jurong Engineering SDollars 1.05 to SDollars 8.45. KUALA LUMPUR followed a similar course, improving from early lows with the emergence of bargain hunters. The composite index ended 24.38 off at 1,001.80 after a morning's low of 990.09. Volume increased to an estimated 170.9m shares from Tuesday's 105.4m. SEOUL saw institutional selling of blue chips for the fifth straight day and the composite index slipped 10.73 to 1,095.97. Brokers said tight liquidity also hurt the market as banks were squeezed to meet their reserve requirements from the central bank. Samsung Electronics went limit down by Won3,000 to Won119,500, while Korea Telecom lost Won12,000 to close limit down at Won559,000. SYDNEY was well off its lows after bargain hunters gave shares a late lift. The All Ordinaries index ended 20.6 or 1.1 per cent down at 1,857.1; but this was 2.3 per cent better than its intraday low of 1,814.5 - the lowest level for the All Ordinaries since July 28, 1993. Dealers said the market was banking on a recovery on Wall Street. Banks were the target of bargain hunters after ANZ reported a stronger than expected final profit. ANZ gained a net 7 cents at ADollars 3.74 after an early fall of 12 cents. Base metal issues also made a spectacular recovery, with the diversified mining groups CRA and Western Mining turning round early falls to close modestly ahead. However, golds were the weakest stocks on the day in spite of a higher gold price in New York. TAIPEI combined textile industry fundamentals, the ruling party's 100th anniversary today and speculation that the government might support equities to achieve a good recovery. The weighted index, which hit a low of 6,241.66 at one stage, closed 26.21 up at 6,371.48. Turnover expanded from TDollars 30.89bn to TDollars 39.4bn. The textiles sector index ended 3 per cent stronger on renewed expectations of an industry recovery. Hualon, the most active stock in the sector, put on TDollars 1.40 at TDollars 22.70, followed by Shinkong Synthetic Fibres, which jumped by the daily 7 per cent limit to TDollars 32.20, and Far Eastern Textile, TDollars 1.70 ahead at TDollars 39.30.
