BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 Apr (MENA)--Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres and his accompanying delegation, including Environment Minister Yosi Sarid, Foreign Ministry Director General Uri Savir, and the deputy chief of staff, General Amnon Shahaq, arrived in Cairo this morning. In an arrival statement Peres expressed the hope that the Israeli-Palestinians talks would succeed. He said he would discuss the issues of security in the Gaza Strip and Jericho and the control of the crossing points. He added that it is difficult to say that this will be the last round of talks and noted that no progress has been made on the Syrian track.
