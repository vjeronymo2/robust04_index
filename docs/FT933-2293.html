930920 FT 20 SEP 93 / Blair rallies party modernisers LABOUR party modernisers today seek to intensify pressure on union leaders to fall into line behind Mr John Smith's party reforms, by emphasising how vital they are to Labour's electoral prospects. Writing in the Fabian Review, Mr Tony Blair, shadow home secretary, says that the party needs a 'bold and radical identity', and must resist both those who believe 'one more heave' will win the party the next election, and those on the left of the party whose solutions offer a return to Labour's past.
