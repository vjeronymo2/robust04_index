BFN [Report by Vicente Briceno from Miraflores Presidential Palace in Caracas from the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] Dr. Gustavo Roosen, who has been appointed special commissioner for reforming the financial system, said a few minutes ago that within the next 72 hours he intends to establish a solidarity pact between commercial banks and the Central Bank of Venezuela to preserve the country's financial network. Speaking at Miraflores Palace, Dr. Roosen broadly outlined the complex situation for reporters. He said this pact is intended to clarify the issuance of financial instruments, which can help ease the situation by decreasing the issuance of zero-coupon bonds and, in place of these bonds, issuing Treasury bills of exchange that would be purchased by domestic banks. [Begin Roosen recording] In accepting this important duty, we intend to coordinate between the Finance Ministry, the superintendency of banks, FOGADE [Deposit Guarantee and Banking Protection Fund], and the Central Bank of Venezuela in every way possible in an effort to establish a solidarity pact to restore confidence in the financial system. We will do this by implementing programs that lead to the recovery of subsidized banks, gradually reducing the current gap between passive and active interest rates in the national market, and seeking ways to use the system's financial surpluses in the placement of Treasury bills of exchange or financial instruments other than zero-coupon bonds, which has been the only available mechanism to remove surplus liquidity from circulation. [end recording]
