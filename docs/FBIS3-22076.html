Language: Urdu Article Type:CSO [News Report: "Arrested Drug Smugglers Given Jail Term, Fines"] [Text] Peshawar (Staff Reporter)--The Peshawar courts sentenced two persons to a total jail term of seven years, fines of 15,000 rupees, and 15 lashes while trying two cases of drug smuggling. Khan Akbar Khan, district and session judge of Peshawar, sentenced Amirzada of Bara Gate to three years hard labor, a 5,000 rupee fine, and five lashes for possession of five kilogram of heroin. It is important to mention here that this person was arrested by Paskhara police in 1988 for possession of drugs. Meanwhile, in another drug-related case, Shah Jehan Khan, additional district and session judge of Peshawar, has sentenced Ghulam Jaib of Mardan for four years of hard labor, ten lashes, and a 10,000 rupees fine for a similar crime. In case of failure to pay the fine the accused will serve an additional six months in jail. It should be known that the Narcotics Police had recovered 250 grams of opium and 200 grams of heroin from the accused's person last year during a bus search at Bara check post.
