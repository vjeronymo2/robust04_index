Language: Korean Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Concerning the recent public remarks made by U.S. Deputy Assistant Secretary Hubbard, the U.S. side's delegate to U.S.-North Korea New York working-level contact, on the abolition of the National Security Law in our country, a Foreign Ministry official said this issue was never officially raised between the ROK and U.S. Governments. [passage omitted] Meanwhile, Ha Sun-pong, a spokesman of the ruling Democratic Liberal Party, today made a statement pertaining to the remarks of U.S. Deputy Assistant Secretary Hubbard, who suggested the abolition of the National Security Law. In the statement, he said that logically his remarks are not appropriate, and if the report on his remarks is true, it is regretful. Spokesman Ha Sun-pong noted that he will further inquire into the truth of the remarks. Pak Chi-won, spokesman of the opposition Democratic Party, in a commentary pertaining to Deputy Assistant Secretary Hubbard's remarks, said he agrees with the remarks in principle. He maintained that Hubbard's remarks should be considered from the standpoint of the promotion of human rights and not of interference in our domestic affairs. Spokesman Pak Chi-won said that he understood that Deputy Assistant Secretary Hubbard also conveyed his position to our government, and urged the government to expedite reviewing whether the Security Law should be revised or abolished.
