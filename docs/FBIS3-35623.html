Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] The article "Battle in Crimea, Smoke Is Everywhere," published in the Moscow-based newspaper KOMSOMOLSKAYA PRAVDA on 25 January, contains a number of inaccuracies, probably of a deliberate nature, says a report circulated by the press service of the Ukrainian Defense Ministry. The newspaper announced that large-scale exercises of Armed Forces headquarters units and formations took place in certain southern oblasts of Ukraine and in the Crimea from 24-28 January. According to the newspaper, the exercises were connected with the results of the first round of the presidential election on the peninsula. The press service of the Ukrainian military department, the report indicates further on, had more than once reported about the upcoming strategic exercise that was held from 24-27 January this year under the leadership of the supreme commander of the Armed Forces of Ukraine. The plan of holding the exercise, aimed at solving the tasks of the country's territorial defense, was approved as far back as November 1993. As for the units and formations of the Armed Forces of Ukraine, they were not involved in the exercise at all.
