BFN [Text] In a pre-departure statement in Sanaa today, UN envoy Lakhdar Brahimi expressed disappointment at the collapse of yesterday's cease-fire agreement. He stressed that the conflict can be solved through dialogue between the concerned parties, but such a dialogue is still not possible as the south wants a complete halt to battles and full northern commitment to a cease-fire before anything else. As for the conditions of the north, here is 'Abd-al-Salam al-'Ansi, chairman of the General Committee of the General People's Congress, which is led by President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih, speaking to our correspondent Ahmad Birru in Sanaa. [Begin al-Anisi recording] To agree to dialogue, the following conditions should be met: 1. The Socialist Party should issue a decision abrogating the secession decision. 2. It should clearly and frankly announce its acceptance of the results of the parliamentary elections held on 27 April [1993]. 3. It should announce its commitment to the constitutional legitimacy and to dealing with the institutions accordingly. 4. The dialogue should take place in Sanaa. 5. The dialogue should not last more than five days. 6. The 16 Socialist Party members subpoenaed by the public prosecutor should not take part in the dialogue. [end recording]
