Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 26 March Morning Edition] [Text] Commenting on the issue of North Korea's suspected nuclear development on 25 March, a leader of the ruling Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ] said: "The SDPJ plans to decide what it thinks about the issue early next week." In this way, he indicated the SDPJ will make its official view on the issue clear early next week. He also stressed: "The SDPJ is the sole Japanese political party that has maintained a relationship with the Workers' Party of Korea in North Korea and the relationship has a long history. As one of the coalition partners, however, the SDPJ is in the position of shoring up the coalition government." He also suggested the SDPJ, in its official view, will press North Korea to accept all nuclear inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency.
