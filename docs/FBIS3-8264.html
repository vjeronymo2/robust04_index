Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Mirko Pejanovic, member of the state presidency, today held talks with Sukru Tufan, Turkish ambassador to Bosnia-Herzegovina, on the denouement of the situation in our country. After the talks, Ambassador Tufan briefly stated that they discussed the new, real denouement of the situation, especially after the signing of the federation agreement between the Bosnian and Croatian sides. Tufan stressed that attempts are being made to find a model according to which the Serbian side would also be included in the agreement on the federal organization of Bosnia-Herzegovina that has already been signed. Mirko Pejanovic, member of the state presidency, stressed the need for the further involvement of Turkey as a friendly country in the settlement of the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Pejanovic said the following: I believe that the (?talks) with the Turkish representatives will be of great importance for the implementation of the federation agreement in Bosnia-Herzegovina and confederation with the neighboring Republic of Croatia, as well as a possible participation of the Serbian people in the implementation of the agreement. This was stated by Mirko Pejanovic, member of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Presidency.
