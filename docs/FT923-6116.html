920826 FT 26 AUG 92 / International Company News: Bank of Montreal ahead 14% despite provisions BANK of Montreal, Canada's third largest bank, reported a 14 per cent gain in third-quarter profit despite higher loan loss provisions. Net income for the July 31 quarter was CDollars 173m (USDollars 89m), or CDollars 1.29 a share, up from CDollars 152m, or CDollars 1.17 a share, a year earlier. This reflected improving market share, strong treasury and international operations and tight cost control. Net interest income was up 16 per cent. Return on common equity was 15.1 per cent. Despite the recession, small and medium-sized business loans were up 15 per cent and consumer mortgages up 17 per cent. Growth in US corporate business continued good. Foreign exchange trading revenues gained 18 per cent and a CDollars 7m dividend was received from the Brazilian subsidiary. Expenses were held to a 5 per cent increase. Nine month earnings were CDollars 480m, or CDollars 3.59 a share, against CDollars 419m, or CDollars 3.28, a year earlier. Average assets were CDollars 103bn against CDollars 93bn. Non-performing loans totalled CDollars 2.1bn, or 3.2 per cent of total loans, up CDollars 318m from April 30.
