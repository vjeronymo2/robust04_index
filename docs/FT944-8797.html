941118 FT 18 NOV 94 / Nestle to close Norwich factory Nestle said yesterday that it will close its Norwich confectionery factory in two years with the loss of 900 jobs, Roderick Oram writes. Production will be transferred to several other plants. York, home of the Rowntree's business Nestle bought in 1988, is apparently the biggest winner. It is the third tranche of job losses announced by the Swiss multinational since it completed a review of its UK operations this summer. Job losses so far total 2,000 out of a UK workforce of 17,000. Mr David Harris, managing director of Nestle Rowntree, said: 'We need to reduce our capacity and costs to improve competitiveness so that we can protect and develop the business for the future.'
