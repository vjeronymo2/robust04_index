BFN [Text] Mass rallies organized by the association of defense workers' unions were held in a number of Russia's cities in protest at the destruction of the defense industry. The chairman of the association's central committee, Sergey Shuklin, told Interfax that only representatives of the aviation industry had failed to take part in the rallies. Shuklin said a number of St. Petersburg enterprises kept their sirens on and held shop-floor meetings. Their workers also picketed the administrative building. Similar actions were also held in Novosibirsk, Barnaul, Kirov, Perm and Moscow. In particular, defense workers demanded their salaries be paid, jobs preserved, a mechanism to reconcile mutual debts be set up and state control over fuel prices established.
