910430 FT 30 APR 91 / World News in Brief: Quakes in Georgia kill at least 30 Strong earthquakes hit the southern Soviet republic of Georgia, killing at least 30 people as it sent boulders crashing down mountains on to houses. The first tremor, measuring about 7 on the Richter scale, was as strong as the one which killed 25,000 Armenians in 1988. It was followed by more powerful quakes. Georgian officials reported many injuries and widespread damage to property.
