March 20, 1989, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: LEGAL FILE

A federal judge has set May 24 for a hearing on a lawsuit filed by a group of real estate investors who are suing talk-show host Morton Downey Jr. for allegedly selling them worthless plots of land in the California desert, advertised as being laden with gold. The class action suit, filed in December in U.S. District Court in San Francisco, charges that in 1982-83 Downey bilked investors out of $10 million to $20 million. Downey, 56, whose controversial TV show is taped in Secaucus, N.J., told a Hackensack newspaper (The Record) over the weekend that the complaint is unfounded. ALEENE MacMINN and CLAUDIA PUIG
