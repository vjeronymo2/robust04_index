930113 FT 13 JAN 93 / International Company News: Mellon Bank net profits soar 56% MELLON BANK of Pittsburgh reported a 56 per cent jump in its full-year 1992 net profits, to Dollars 437m. Earnings per share for 1992 were Dollars 6.96, against Dollars 4.66 in 1991. Fourth-quarter net income was 46 per cent higher at Dollars 105m, or Dollars 1.62 a share, against Dollars 1.11 in 1991. Mr Frank Cahouet, chairman and chief executive, said the bank's credit quality improved during 1992, a year that saw the acquisition of The Boston Company and 27 branches from Meritor, the former savings bank. The fourth quarter saw 17 per cent higher net interest income of Dollars 301m and a 21 per cent rise in fee income to Dollars 228m. Bad debt provisions were Dollars 35m, against Dollars 75m in the same quarter of 1991. For 1992, Mellon's bad debt provisions were Dollars 185m, against Dollars 250m.
