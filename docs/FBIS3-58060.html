Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Public Debt Tops 1,753 Trillion Lire"] [Text] Rome, 4 Jan (ANSA) -- Italy's public debt reached the staggering figure of 1,753.577 trillion lire (1.031 trillion dollars) in September, or close to 31 million lire (18,260 dollars) for every Italian man, woman and child, the Bank of Italy reported today. Over the 12 months from September 1992, the debt grew by 178 trillion lire (104.7 billion dollars), or about 500 billion lire (294 million dollars) a day. August was the worst month, with debt ballooning by around one trillion lire (588 million dollars) a day. In percentage terms, the debt was up 11.3 percent on a year previously, almost 25 percent on two years previously, and more than 42 percent on six years previously. It is almost double what it was six years ago. The total debt in September is split into domestic debt of 1,671.16 trillion lire, and foreign debts of 82.417 trillion lire. The overwhelming bulk of the public sector debt derives from market debts (state securities, post-office savings accounts, etc.): 1,492.808 trillion lire.
