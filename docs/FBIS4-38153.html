BFN [Text] A strike is being observed today in some parts of country at the call of the Federation of Pakistan Chambers of Commerce and Industry [FPCCI] against nonacceptance of their budget proposals by the government. In Karachi, industrial units and main shopping centers are closed. However, public transport and private vehicles are plying as usual. Most of the main shopping centers and markets in Islamabad, Rawalpindi, and Peshawar are closed. However, the minister for commerce speaking in the National Assembly said a number of factories in Sialkot, Naushahra, Gujrat, and Sindh are not observing the strike. The minister for commerce, Chaudhary Ahmad Mukhtar, has again asked the Federation of Pakistan Chambers of Commerce and Industry to call off the strike and resume talks with the government about their demands. Replying to a point of order by Haji Mohammad Nawaz Khokar in the National Assembly today, the commerce minister said that the government during talks with representatives of FPCCI wanted to evolve some package. However, they continued their threatening posture saying that if their demands were not accepted, they would go on strike. He said they wanted taxation rules and laws of their choice and were trying to dictate to the government. He said there were some politically motivated people who raised slogans and misbehaved during the talks.
