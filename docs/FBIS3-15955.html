Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Andrey Streletskiy report under the "Resonance" rubric: "Major Diplomatic Incident; This Is How American Press Assesses Russian Leaders' Refusal To Meet With Richard Nixon"] [Excerpts] The American press is commenting on Russian President Boris Yeltsin's harsh statement that neither he, nor anyone around him, nor members of the Russian Government will be meeting with Richard Nixon, the 37th U.S. president, who is in Moscow. As many people know, such a decision was made in retaliation for Mr. Nixon's contacts that had taken place with prominent figures of the Russian opposition. [passage omitted] In the meantime it is becoming clear that those close to the Russian president have different assessments of the harshness shown to the American visitor. For example, Andranik Migranyan, member of the Russian Federation Presidential Council, does not see anything objectionable in the desire of an "aging" Nixon to get an objective idea of the political processes in Russia with the aid, among other things, of talking with representatives of the radical opposition in Moscow. In this connection, Mr. Migranyan told an INTERFAX correspondent that "he is not quite clear" about the motives of Boris Yeltsin's refusal to receive Richard Nixon. "This is even less clear if there had been a previous agreement and promise from our side of having a meeting," he added. Yeltsin's adviser thinks the trips by USSR President Mikhail Gorbachev to the United States, during which he freely communicated with officials and political groupings hostile to Bill Clinton, as an analogy are appropriate.
