920507 FT 07 MAY 92 / UK Company News: Chelsea chief gives Fulham stern warning Mr Ken Bates, chairman of Chelsea Football Club which is planning to buy its west London ground from Cabra Estates, has warned Fulham Football Club that it should come to share the facilities by the start of next season - or not come at all. A move by Fulham to Stamford Bridge is a crucial part of Chelsea's agreement to buy the ground for Pounds 13m cash, plus the assumption of Cabra's liabilities to Fulham, worth Pounds 8.25m.
