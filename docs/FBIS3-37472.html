Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Oleg Velichko] [Text] Moscow February 17 TASS--Armenia, which faces an acute energy crisis, may restart its nuclear power plant with the Russian help this year, Russian First Deputy Prime Minister Oleg Soskovets and Armenian Prime Minister Hrant Bagratyan agreed on Thursday. They agreed that a corresponding agreement may be signed already next week. The power plant, which was shut down after a major earthquake shook Armenia late in 1988, may resume operations already this year if Russia supplies the necessary equipment and personnel. Armenia pledged to bear all expenses and Soskovets advised that Armenian diaspora abroad could render financial assistance as well. It is expected that the Russian personnel at the plant will number 560 experts and the plant will produce 800 megawatts of electricity. The restart of the Armenian nuclear power plant is opposed by Germany and Turkey, according to Bagratyan.
