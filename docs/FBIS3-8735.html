Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Representatives of the opposition parties that, in fact, represent the coalition already, together with Jozef Moravcik and Roman Kovac, returned from the talks with the president of the Republic shortly before 2200. He announced to the president the name of the candidate who is to form the new government. Our reporters have just returned from the parliament building. Hence, we can offer you the following interviews. [Begin recording] [Unidentified official] The parties that formed the opposition so far, presented [the president] with the name of a new prime minister to be authorized to form the new Slovak government. This proposed prime minister, approved by the president as well, is Jozef Moravcik. [Unidentified correspondent] Did the opposition parties propose candidates for ministerial posts as well? [Official] This was not the topic of the talks. According to the information given by the hitherto opposition parties, the names for individual posts are more or less known [to the opposition parties]. However, this [list of names] will be fine-tuned. There is a chance that the names for individual ministerial posts will be announced by Wednesday [16 March]. [passage omitted] [Unidentified official] The Party of the Democratic Left will propose seven names [for ministerial posts]. The Center Bloc, as I called it this afternoon--i.e., the Alliance of Democrats, the National Democratic Club, and another independent club, headed by Mr. Bajan--will also present seven names. The Christian Democratic Movement will present five names. [Correspondent] How many deputy prime ministers will there be? [Unidentified official] It is expected that there will be three deputy prime ministers. One deputy prime minister will be in charge of the economy. There will be another deputy prime minister for the so-called superstructure or ministries of the nonproductive sphere, and a deputy prime minister for legislative matters. [passage omitted] [end recording]
