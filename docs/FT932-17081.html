930402 FT 02 APR 93 / People: Cullimore to exploit Naafi challenge Colin Cullimore, who ran the Dewhurst chain of butchers for more than 20 years, has become chairman of Naafi, succeeding Sir Malcolm Field, group managing director of WH Smith, who has inhabited the voluntary post for the past seven years. 'Originally there was no thought of my becoming chairman,' says Cullimore, who is 61 and joined the board in 1984, 'as they would normally look for someone younger.' But with the impact of Options for Change, under which almost half the British soldiers stationed in Germany (Naafi's most profitable area) are to return to the UK, the need was felt for an experienced hand to guide the institution through one of the biggest changes since its founding in 1921. As well as his retailing experience, Cullimore, who served in the parachute regiment in Cyprus and the Canal Zone in the mid 1950s, has kept close contact with the Services. Naafi, the official trading organisation of the Forces, which sees an annual turnover of Pounds 430m, gets no money from the government - unlike the situation in other countries - and aims to return a surplus (Pounds 16m last year) to Service welfare funds. 'With a much more sophisticated customer base, there is actually no longer a need for the Naafi in much of the UK,' says Cullimore, 'but there is a desperate need for it in Benbecular or Northern Ireland.' One way to make money - and support the loss-making welfare operations such as Bosnia - will be to expand the nascent financial services division. 'Servicemen worried about anything from motor insurance to investing redundancy money feel they can trust us. This is an area with tremendous growth potential.'
