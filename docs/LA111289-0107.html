November 12, 1989, Sunday, Bulldog Edition

MILITARY PLANE CRASHES IN LESOTHO; 15 ON BOARD DIE

A military plane crashed Friday in a mountainous area in southern Lesotho, killing all 15 people on board, the government said.

The plane, which crashed shortly after takeoff, was en route to a military base in Maseru, the capital, from the town of Qachasnek, military headquarters said.

There were three crewmen and 12 other military personnel on board the twin-engine plane, a Spanish-built CASA-212.

An investigation was begun.

Lesotho is a small, mountainous kingdom surrounded by South Africa.
