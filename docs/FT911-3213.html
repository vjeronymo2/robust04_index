910425 FT 25 APR 91 / UK Company News: World of Leather loses Pounds 823,000 The downturn in consumer spending, plus sharply higher interest charges, pushed losses from Pounds 88,000 to Pounds 823,000 at World of Leather, the upholstered leather furniture retailer, in 1990. Operating profits of Pounds 70,000 achieved in the previous year reversed into losses of Pounds 187,000 in spite of increased turnover of Pounds 24.49m (Pounds 23.16m). Net interest charges jumped to Pounds 636,000 (Pounds 158,000). Group borrowings have risen to Pounds 3.25m since its net cash position turned into a net debt position after the acquisition of seven leather furniture stores in July 1989. Losses per share were 10.5p (0.8p). There was no dividend payment for the year (1.6p).
