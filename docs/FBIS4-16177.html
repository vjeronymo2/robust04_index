BFN [Text] In an interview with Interfax and RIA news agencies on Wednesday evening President Boris Yeltsin summed up the main results of his first official visit to Spain. "The main thing is that the visit was fruitful. The foundation for active cooperation between Russia and Spain was laid", the president said. He pointed to the significance of the friendship and cooperation treaty as well as several other key agreements signed in Madrid. He also attached great importance to personal relations with the Spanish leaders and politicians, which he described as very good. "I felt that Spaniards were as attracted to Russia as Russians to Spain. Nothing separates us, while Europe, European affairs, common security and concerns about economic stability in both Russia and Spain unite use", Yeltsin said. The president spoke of the importance of the Spanish experience of the transition from dictatorship to democracy. He said Russians should draw useful conclusions from this experience. Yeltsin again spoke for signing a cooperation agreement between Russia and the European Union in the nearest future. On Russia's membership in the NATO Partnership for Peace program Yeltsin said the country will gradually prepare for signing such an agreement, but will not hurry.
