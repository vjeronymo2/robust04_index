931202 FT 02 DEC 93 / Commodities and Agriculture: British Gas in Polish deal British Gas has been granted oil and gas exploration rights in a 9,000 sq km area 100km west of Warsaw. The British company is expected to spend USDollars 20m over the next three years on the project. Amoco was granted petroleum exploration rights in Poland just over a year ago and is also to drill for coalbed methane in Silesia in projects worth a total of USDollars 30m. The Polish government is also talking to a Shell-Exxon consortium on exploration rights in an area east of Poznan, while a new tender closing next April has just been opened for sites further east of Poznan and east of Gdansk.
