BFN [Text] On Friday the Crimean parliament adopted a resolution under which all residents of the republic of draft age should pass military service in the army on the territory of the peninsula. Deputies also decided to suspend sending draftees out of Crimea and to draft them for serving in Crimea and in Black Sea Fleet units. At the same time the Crimean parliament ordered the military commissar of Crimea, Volkov, to fulfill this resolution. However, Volkov said at the session that he would not be able to fulfill the resolution because he is subordinate directly to Commander-in-Chief, Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk. Volkov also said that "it is impossible to have all draftees stay in Crimea." In the meantime, Vice Commander of the Black Sea Fleet, deputy Aleksandr Frolov said at the session that "the commander is ready to receive all draftees from the Republic of Crimea." On the same day the republic's parliament formed a delegation which will leave for Kiev in a few days for talks on the return to the first version of the Crimean Constitution of May 6, 1992. The delegation is led by Chairman of the Crimean parliament Sergey Tsekov. The delegation includes, Refat Chubarov, one of the leaders of the Crimean Tatars and the faction Kurultay of the Crimean Tatars in the Crimean parliament.
