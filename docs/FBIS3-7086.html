Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN [Text] Second Prime Minister Samdech Hun Sen of the Kingdom of Cambodia has informed Mrs. Anne Bruzellius, UNICEF representative in Cambodia, that the Royal Government of Cambodia [RGC] has asked UNICEF to join hands with the International Committee of the Red Cross [ICRC] to help maintain the rights and well-being of the children and people forced by the Khmer Rouge into Thailand. This follows the capture of the Pailin and other areas of the group by government forces. Samdech Hun Sen disclosed that the RGC was in the process of negotiating for the repatriation of the Cambodian refugees with the Thai side. On the children's general welfare, the second prime minister has asked UNICEF to continue giving children and women health education through radio and television as well as other forms of mass media and contact with women's associations existing in every locality. Samdech Hun Sen also said that their majesties the king and queen planned to gather all orphans in a ceremony to show the enhancement of the children's value and to foster the upkeep of their rights and welfare. In her reply, Mrs. Anne Bruzellius pledged that UNICEF would strive to meet the RGC's request to save children from misery. She also disclosed that if security was sufficiently insured, UNICEF would travel to Pailin to help the children there in its capacity as an UN agency.
