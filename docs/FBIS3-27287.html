Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shanghai, February 21 (XINHUA) -- Computerized business using satellite communications have been carried out successfully in Shanghai's ten major exchanges. Open since 1990, these exchanges are involved in the fields of securities, metals, chemicals, grain and edible oil, motor vehicles, petroleum, building materials, technology, coal and farm capital. By updating its central computer system several times, the Shanghai stock market is able to handle 1,800 transactions per second at present, up from only six in the past. Officials with the Shanghai stock exchange said that nearly eight million U.S. dollars were spent to upgrade the whole system, which uses optical fibre communication lines to link up exchanges of other regions. At present, the trading of Shanghai's stocks can be carried out in other major cities via satellite communication channels. A nationwide securities market is in shape. High technology has made brisk business possible. In only three years, the daily transaction volume of the Shanghai stock market has jumped from the original 105,000 yuan to an average of 900 million yuan. Now more than five million people are holding Shanghai stocks and trading at 500 markets in different regions. With the help of the Shanghai and Shenzhen stock exchanges, all leading cities in the mainland, including Lhasa, capital of the Tibet Autonomous Region, have opened stock market. At present, the Shanghai grain and oil and metal exchanges are also releasing and receiving market information via satellite communications.
