BFN [Text] At the conclusion of his meeting with Nabil Sha'th at the Erez checkpoint a short while ago, Major General Amnon Shahaq said that Sha'th would apparently not visit Jerusalem tomorrow due to changes in his schedule. Our correspondent Ron Nesi'el reports that Maj. Gen. Shahaq said that Nabil Sha'th had asked him to increase the number of entry permits for Palestinian workers from the autonomous areas. Shahaq, for his part, raised the issue of the collaborators who have been arrested in the autonomous areas. The two men agreed that this matter, as well as the issue of the Palestinian prisoners, would be discussed at their meeting next week. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 1300 GMT on 19 June reports that "Maj. Gen. Shahaq and Nabil Sha'th this afternoon agreed that Israel and the PLO will next week launch negotiations on the beginning of the next stage: The gradual transfer of powers in Judaea and Samaria to the Palestinian authorities." The report notes that "next week, the two men will also discuss the full implementation of the Gaza-Jericho First agreement and the release of all the Palestinian prisoners, including those who were transferred to Jericho. Nabil Sha'th asserted that he intended to visit Jerusalem tomorrow; however, this also depended on how busy he would be in the Gaza Strip. At any rate, Sha'th said, his intention was to hold a private visit and to people he described as friends, such as Faysal al-Husayni, visit the churches, and pray at al-Aqsa mosque." The report adds that "Nabil Sha'th and Maj. Gen. Shahaq today agreed that at the beginning of next week, 30,000 permits to work inside Israel would be issued to the residents of the Gaza Strip. It was also agreed that the families of the Palestinian policemen would immediately be allowed to enter Israel. The Army will also ease the policy regarding entry and departure from the autonomous areas."] Our political correspondent Shlomo Raz reports that Nabil Sha'th's planned visit to Jerusalem and the intention to convene the autonomy council at the Orient House building were raised today at the weekly cabinet session. The speakers at the meeting expressed doubts as to whether the autonomy council would really be convened in Jerusalem. The cabinet was told that in general, the territories were more quiet, although the autonomy authorities had failed to meet the timetable for assuming responsibility over the residents' daily affairs. The cabinet revoked the ban against signing contracts with South Africa following the Security Council's decision to end the military embargo and other restrictions against South Africa. The cabinet also issued another 10,000 work permits for laborers from the territories. Approximately 55,000 work permits for Palestinians from the territories have been issued so far.
