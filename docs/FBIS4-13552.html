BFN [Text] Tehran, April 20, IRNA -- Deputy Foreign Minister of Sudan Yusuf Baradah in his meeting with the First Deputy President of Iran Hasan Habibi said today that most of the problems faced by the Muslim countries have been imposed by the enemies of Islam. "The enemies of Islam fear the awakening of Muslims in countries like Iran, Sudan, Algeria and Bosnia, and are therefore trying to suppress them by all possible means," Baradah pointed out. He lauded the accomplishments of Islamic Iran and said the country is indebted to the unity of its people, its leadership and dedicated efforts of responsible officials. Baradah who arrived in Tehran on Monday, said the purpose of his visit to Iran is become more familiar with the official views and positions for further developing of Tehran-Khartoum relations. Habibi said in response that one of the ways for fighting problems in the Muslim world after steadfastness, and struggle and proper programming, is development of relations and exchange of views between the officials of Muslim countries. He said the setting up of the Iran-Sudan joint economic commission has facilitated bilateral trade relations and suggested that the two sides should explore the grounds for private investments in the other country.
