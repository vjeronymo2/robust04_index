940912 FT 12 SEP 94 / Arts: Today's Television For some reason Channel 4 is staying very quiet about one aspect of its new series Quarrels. The billing says these programmes 'bring together disputing parties, whether individuals or groups', but in the first two episodes, at any rate, race is a factor. Today a Muslim resident of Leicester confronts a prostitute who works on the street outside his house (8.00pm). BBC2's series on technology, White Heat, which began well last week, moves on to the question of technological change: does it occur to supply a need, or do innovations create their own demand? Until somebody created a disposable nappy, nobody needed one. Fiction writers 'invented' death rays, and then along came the laser which can shoot down aircraft, restore sight and read bar codes - yet it was not invented to do any of those (8.00pm). BBC2 considers OJ Mania: The Media Trial Of OJ Simpson (11.15pm). Millions of Americans watched the scenes live on television when Simpson set off up the highway and was pursued by a phalanx of police cars. Some now doubt whether Simpson can ever get a fair trial.
