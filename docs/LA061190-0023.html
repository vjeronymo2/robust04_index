June 11, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

YOUNG SURFERS

In response to Linda Blandford's column "Golden Boys, Princes of Darkness," Commentary, May 14:

On April 14, a deputy was called to Salt Creek Beach because of juveniles throwing water balloons. The deputy contacted the proprietor of the concession stand who had called about the incident.

The deputy was told a group of juveniles were involved with throwing water balloons at beach-goers and yelling obscenities. The juveniles had dispersed and could not be identified or located by the proprietor.

The deputy remained at the concession stand and contacted two juveniles and questioned their possible involvement. Their information was documented for further reference. The deputy handled the situation properly and professionally.

Linda Blandford, a free-lance writer, was at the beach during the disturbance. She confronted the deputy and wanted him to contact some additional juveniles who were in the ocean surfing with numerous others. Blandford was not sure these juveniles were involved in the incident, but she "thought" they might have been.

The deputy had no reason, based on this reporter's information, to contact these two young men, nor was such contact possible. Subsequently, in a frenzy of literary misstatement of fact, Blandford's diatribe appeared in The Times.

It is unfortunate to have any disturbance of this type at our beaches. Linda Blandford has documented her experience based on emotion, not merit and fact.

DENNIS W. LaDUCER

Assistant Sheriff, Chief of Operations

Orange County
