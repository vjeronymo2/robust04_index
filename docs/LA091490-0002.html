September 14, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

MANDATORY STATE QUAKE INSURANCE

It is remarkable that the Legislature has made allowances for the provision of over $25,000 insurance coverage for every California homeowner against earthquake damage; all for $36 a year (Part A, Aug. 28).

Legislators are to be congratulated on the one hand and admonished on the other.

In a state of more than 28 million, approximately 5 million are uninsured for medical care. This includes a significant number of blue- and white-collar workers who either choose not to or cannot find affordable medical insurance.

It must be clearly understood that a significant part of the high cost of medical care for all Californians occurs as a result of the economic loss suffered by hospitals and health-care providers in caring for those patients who are uninsured.

This injustice to the health and well-being of our citizens, who predictably will require medical care, is underscored by the compassion shown toward the potential for damages from an earthquake that may never occur or cause serious or significant personal and economic injuries.

MICHAEL H. SUKOFF, MD

Santa Ana
