921105 FT 05 NOV 92 / International Company News: Air Canada registers CDollars 14m loss AIR CANADA, which has dropped its merger plan with rival Canadian Airlines International, reported a CDollars 14m (USDollars 11.6m) loss for the third quarter, its busiest period, writes Robert Gibbens. The latest loss, equivalent to 19 cents a share, compares with a profit of CDollars 4m, or 6 cents a share, a year earlier. Operating revenues were up 4.6 per cent to just over CDollars 1bn. Revenue passenger miles flown totalled 4.6bn, up from 4.2bn. Operating profit before financial charges for the latest quarter was CDollars 39m, against CDollars 47m, while asset sales increased cash by CDollars 407m to CDollars 511m. The nine-month loss was CDollars 307m, or CDollars 4.14 a share, against a deficit of CDollars 126m, or CDollars 1.70 a share, a year earlier, on revenues almost static at CDollars 2.7bn.
