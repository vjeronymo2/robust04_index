941018 FT 18 OCT 94 / Telecom networks in Africa A unit of Japan's Nippon Telegraph and Telephone is joining a project of California's Stanford University to set up telecommunications networks in Africa using optical fibre networks and satellites. Nippon Telegraph and Telephone International and Hughes Aircraft of the US agreed to join Afronetwork, a company set up in June by Stanford's Satellite Planning Centre. The company would provide consulting services for the construction and management of telecommunications networks in African countries. Separately, Tunisia and AT&T of the US are discussing a cable project around the African continent.
