Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Ivan Ivanov and Gennadiy Talalayev] [Text] Moscow January 14 TASS -- Twelve bilateral documents have been adopted during the Russian-American summit according to the latest official information received by ITAR-TASS. This morning Russian President Boris Yeltsin and U.S. President Bill Clinton signed in the Kremlin the Moscow declaration confirming the fundamental importance of Russian-American cooperation for global stability. Joint statements on human rights and on non-proliferation of weapons of mass destruction and the means of their delivery were adopted on behalf of the Presidents of the two countries. Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher signed intergovernment agreements on air links, on cooperation in health services and biomedical research, on cooperation in the study of radiation hazards for human health and the environment, as well as the memorandum of intent on cooperation in export controls. Joint statements on export controls and on the Middle East were adopted on behalf of the heads of the foreign policy departments of Russia and the United States. A package of documents on the implementation of the second stage of the 1989 Wyoming memorandum and the contract on the sale of low-grade uranium extracted from enriched uranium were also signed in the framework of the summit. The joint statement of intent was also announced to implement the Russo-American "partnership" programme of exchange of entrepreneurs and specialists ("Business for Russia").
