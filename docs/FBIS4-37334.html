BFN [Text] Cairo, 20 Jun (MENA)--President Husni Mubarak said that all the African leaders have praised Egypt's relations with all the countries of the world and the international organizations that helped to calm the situation in some areas and to begin a new era in relations through dialogue and understanding. In statements to the weekly newspaper MAYU today, President Mubarak added that these African leaders with whom he met in Tunis during the recent African summit also praised the decision to establish a mechanism to examine conflicts by peaceful means and the efforts to reduce African debts. He said that Egypt's status among the African states has greatly been enhanced, noting that all the African states with whom he met in Tunis thanked Egypt for the large role and sincere effort it undertook to solve the problems of the continent during Egypt's chairmanship of the OAU in the past year. President Mubarak added that all the African leaders have felt the genuineness of the efforts Egypt has made to resolve the continent's political and economic problems, particularly border problems, existing tensions, and internal problems in some states. Regarding the Nile water and the danger threatening it as a result of the corpses dumped in it from the Rwandan war, President Mubarak reassured the people of Egypt that there is no danger at all, noting that the Victoria Lake water represents no more than 10 percent of the water that reaches Egypt. He said that the water is purified and cleaned by natural and geographical factors in the course of its long journey. He affirmed that Egypt is keen to safeguard its people's health, and that this issue is being carefully monitored by the pertinent departments.
