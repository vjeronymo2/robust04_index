July 29, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

'PHILOSOPHICAL BAGGAGE' OF EVOLUTION

The Times' editorial "Evolution Only, Please" (July 21) failed to comprehend the educational, scholastic and political issues at hand in Sacramento. Forced teaching of creation is not the issue. The Times used a non-issue as a smoke screen to twist public opinion and cover up the real issues.

The issues are:

1) The proposed science framework states that evolution is a fact and places this as a foundation for the entire work. The state Board of Education twice asked for its removal but framework authors have refused to make the changes the board has the authority to require.

2) The theory of evolution is presented in a dogmatic way, without applying the scientific standards of testing, verifying, modifying and refuting. It thus is presented as a "compelling belief," putting it in the category of de facto religion, even if it is the religion of secular humanism.

Additionally, material should be included in the textbooks that gives students information by various credible scientists. Yes, there are scientists who do not accept evolution (Michael Denton, a renowned Australian biologist, is one), who say that evolution, particularly macro-evolution, cannot be substantiated.

3) In addition, this commission is suppressing the First Amendment rights of students and teachers. The Supreme Court in Edwards vs. Aguillard ruled that teachers have always had the right under the Constitution to discuss creation in their classes. Honig and this commission are trying to remove these rights.

REV. LOUIS P. SHELDON

Chairman, Traditional Values Coalition

Anaheim
