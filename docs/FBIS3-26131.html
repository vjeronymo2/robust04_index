Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Shenzhen, February 3 (XINHUA) -- Shenzhen, one of the earliest special economic zones in south China's Guangdong Province, will take measures to speed up the internationalization of its banking industry, according to a municipal banking working conference held here today. The measures include: -- More overseas financial organizations will be allowed to set up branches in Shenzhen and foreign investment in such sectors as banks, trust, foundations, securities and insurance companies will be encouraged. -- The business scope of foreign-funded financial organizations will be admitted into such areas as savings, loans and accounting in RMB [renminbi], the Chinese currency. -- Locally-funded financial organizations are encouraged to set up branches overseas and transnational banks. At present, there are 41 foreign-funded financial organizations in Shenzhen, including banks, securities companies and insurance companies. Over the past decade or so, they have introduced more than one billion U.S. dollars of foreign funds into Shenzhen. Shenzhen plans to increase its savings by 18 billion yuan this year, an increase of 36 percent over last year, according to today's conference. In 1993, the banking industry of Shenzhen maintained healthy momentum. At the end of last year, it reported a savings balance of 65.7 billion yuan, an increase of 10.2 billion yuan over the beginning of 1993, up 18.4 percent; a loan balance of 50.2 billion yuan, an increase of 12.6 billion yuan and up 33.6 percent; and a foreign currency loan balance of 3.66 billion U.S. dollars, an increase of 800 million U.S. dollars and up 16.1 percent.
