940125 FT 25 JAN 94 / Observer: Portcullis lowered Has John Major's counter-attack commenced? Michael Portillo could be forgiven for thinking so. Despite Downing Street's insistence that the chief secretary's recent speech condemning national cynicism had John Major's full support, someone in No 10 seems to think Portillo should adopt a lower profile. Portillo planned to deliver another hard-hitting address - this time on Britain's place in Europe - last weekend. But officials at No 10 scored red lines through several key passages of the draft version. Portillo concluded that there was nothing left worthy of releasing, so the speech went unreported. That's two defeats in a week; two days earlier Portillo's opposition to London's cross-rail transport project was publicly over-ruled by Major. The big guns are stirring . . .
