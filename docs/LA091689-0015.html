September 16, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

LOWERING CAPITAL GAINS TAX

Until Malcolm Forbes was taken aback by public resentment of his big bash in Morocco (re: Morris Philipson's "700 Guests Don't Add Up to Fun," Editorial Page, Sept. 2), he had expected to declare this as a business expense, as he acknowledged. He has a point.

It would be poetic justice if this silly saturnalia helped to kill George Bush's capital gains tax cut. In order to make the rich richer, Bush seems to be hoping to surpass Ronald Reagan's record of more than doubling our deca-millionaires in a scant five years.

The creed of greed is alive and well.

FRANCES LEVEILLE

Long Beach
