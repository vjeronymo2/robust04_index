Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Boris Sitnikov] [Text] United Nations February 17 TASS -- The U.N. secretary general met yesterday Russia's permanent representative to the United Nations Yuliy Vorontsov who handed over to him a message by the Russian foreign minister concerning the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina, the spokesman said adding that he could not give any details of the confidential letter. [Moscow ITAR-TASS World Service in Russian at 2338 GMT on 17 February adds the following: "After Yuliy Vorontsov handed over the message from the Russian president to the UN secretary general, he told journalists: ["`First of all, Russia has informed the UN secretary general that during our talks with the Serbs they promised to withdraw their artillery from present positions within two days. After that Russia expressed readiness, with the agreement of UN secretary general and the commander of the UN Protection Force (UNPROFOR) to send 400 Russian assault troops, who are UNPROFOR soldiers from the Russian battalion now stationed in Croatia, into Sarajevo to separate the warring sides. The Serbs have agreed to withdraw voluntarily artillery from present positions, but they have also asked for there to be Russians among the UN forces in Sarajevo.' ["Yuliy Vorontsov noted that the Russian peacekeeping forces were ready to enter Sarajevo within two days."]
