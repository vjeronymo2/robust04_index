BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Nikolay Morozov] [Text] Bucharest, 19 Apr -- "It is vital for Armenia and Romania to establish good relations," emphasized Vahan Papazyan, foreign minister of the Republic of Armenia, who is in Bucharest to discuss the opportunities for developing bilateral Armenian-Romanian ties and economic cooperation in particular. In the course of their talks, Armenia's and Romania's foreign ministers stressed the need for a speedy completion of the work on a political treaty between the two countries. Papazyan informed his counterpart on the situation in the Transcaucasus and the latest steps aimed at settling tensions in Nagorno-Karabakh. [Romanian Foreign Minister] Teodor Melescanu presented the main directions of Romania's foreign policy, described the current stage of its relations with NATO and the EC, as well as with neighboring countries. Agreement was reached at the talks to extend cooperation within the framework of international organizations, namely the CSCE and the UN, and to extend cooperation in the Black Sea area. The need to further relations was also emphasized at the 19 April reception for Armenia's foreign minister by Romanian President Ion Iliescu. The two sides discussed conflicts in Nagorno-Karabakh and in Bosnia-Herzegovina, to the settlement of which both Armenia and Romania are committed. "We tried to find common viewpoints on these problems," noted Papazyan, who passed to the Romanian president a message from the president of Armenia and an invitation to visit the country. On 18 April, the Armenian ambassador to Romania presented his credentials to the Romanian head of state. The official opening of the Armenian Embassy in Bucharest is to take place in the next few days.
