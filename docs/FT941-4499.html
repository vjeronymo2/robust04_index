940310 FT 10 MAR 94 / Economy beats bad weather in US The US economy grew modestly at the beginning of this year despite the effects of bad weather and showed little sign of rising prices, the Federal Reserve system said yesterday, Reuter reports from Washington. 'The economy expanded moderately in January and February despite unusually severe weather in the east and the Midwest,' the Fed said in its Beige Book report on national activity. The economic summary by the 12 Federal Reserve district banks covers the period from mid-January to Feb 28. It was prepared by the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston. 'Overall, only limited price pressures are reported - prices of building materials, selected metals and some chemicals are said to be rising, but prices of manufactured goods and consumer goods generally are not experiencing upward pressure, the Fed said. Manufacturing activity picked up in most industries across the country, notably for vehicle-makers and their suppliers and for producers of building products.
