Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Presidential Bulletin" No. 9 feature] [Text] Turkey will grant Georgia additional credit worth $50 Mn. The understanding was reached Thursday during the visit to Turkey by Georgian leader Eduard Shevardnadze, Interfax was told by the spokesman for the Georgian leader, Zaza Kandelaki. The visit has shown, he said, that Georgia and Turkey have common interests. At the joint news conference in Ankara, Shevardnadze, according to the spokesman, said that Turkey is taking a real part in stabilizing the Georgian economy. In an interview with reporters at the Tbilisi airport, Shevardnadze said that his visit to Turkey following Georgia's entry into the Commonwealth of Independent States was very important. He emphasized the package of economic problems discussed at the talks. The Georgian leader said that there are good prospects for the Georgian economy. "If the process of stabilization continues in Georgia, we shall receive more essential aid from Turkey," he said. The speaker of the parliament of the Ajarian Autonomous Republic, Aslan Abashidze, told Interfax that during the visit the two sides reached understanding with the Turkish government on Turkish energy supplies to Ajaria beginning January 25. Also signed were other agreements on cooperation.
