Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Zagreb, 30 Mar (TANJUG) -- Representatives of the Republic of Croatia and the republic of Serb Krajina [RSK] signed a cease-fire agreement in Zagreb early this morning. The agreement will come into effect on 4 April. The signing of the agreement was attended by special Russian envoy Vitaliy Churkin and by Charles Redman, the special envoy of the U.S. President. The cease-fire agreement was signed after 18 hours of difficult negotiations which became bogged down last night over agreement on the lines of the separation of forces on military maps.
