January 8, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

CALIFORNIA IN BRIEF;

SAN FRANCISCO;

3 SENT TO HOSPITAL AFTER CABLE CAR STOP

A cable car jolted to a stop to avoid a car while descending a hill, but there were no serious injuries, authorities said. Several passengers complained of minor pain after being knocked against poles and other inside parts of the cable car on Mason Street, police said.

Three women passengers went to San Francisco General Hospital, where they were in stable condition while being observed for possible injuries, a nursing supervisor said. The incident occurred when a car made a left-hand turn in front of the cable car at Union Street, said Police Officer Emmett Hickson. Because the trolley was headed downhill, the gripman already had the brake on and was forced to apply the emergency brake to stop, Hickson said.
