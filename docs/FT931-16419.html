930107 FT 07 JAN 93 / Keating and son guilty MR Charles Keating, one of the best-known figures in the US savings and loan crisis, was found guilty yesterday on 73 counts of racketeering and fraud, Reuter reports from Los Angeles. The charges against Mr Keating and his son, Charles, who was found guilty on 64 similar counts, stemmed from the collapse of American Continental Corp and its subsidiary, Lincoln Savings and Loan. Sentencing was set for March 15. Mr Keating senior is already serving a 10-year state sentence for bilking investors out of Dollars 268m (Pounds 176.3m).
