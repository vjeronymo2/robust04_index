BFN [Text] The amphibious Zafar-1 joint military exercise, which started in Persian Gulf waters and the strategic Strait of Hormoz on 30 Khordad [20 June], was concluded successfully today. Light and heavy vessels, fast assault boats, rangers, and commandoes of the Armed Forces and the Islamic Revolution Guard Corps, and a number of fighter-bombers of the Islamic Republic Air Force took part in the exercise. Operations were carried out in six phases, involving the control of communication routes and navy equipment; dispatching units of light vessels; air and surface bombardment of communication routes, vital positions, and installations of an imaginary enemy; reconnaissance and mine-sweeping operations by special teams; and capturing the positions and sea rescue operations of the vessels of the imaginary enemy. The Zafar-1 maneuver is one of a series of joint annual exercises of the Armed Forces and the Guards Corps, aimed at raising defensive capabilities and exchanging experience and military know-how.
