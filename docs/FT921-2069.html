920320 FT 20 MAR 92 / Election 1992: Heseltine launches London manifesto THE Conservatives yesterday summoned the genie of the defunct Greater London Council (GLC) in an attempt to link Labour's proposed strategic London authority with 'loony left' extremism, David Owen writes. Labour's tax and national insurance plans meant that 'people on top taxes would be paying those taxes at lower levels than even under (former Labour chancellor) Denis Healey,' said Mr Michael Heseltine, environment secretary. Mr Heseltine attacked Labour's proposed authority as 'a GLC, which we will remember very clearly is . . . a body to undermine the elected government of the day'. The aggressive tenor of his remarks appeared to confirm the view of many observers that Labour's proposed London government might prove attractive to the voters in the capital.
