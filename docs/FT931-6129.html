930303 FT 03 MAR 93 / Judge tells BSB to pay Philips damages A HIGH Court judge yesterday ruled that British Satellite Broadcasting was in breach of a contract with Philips Electronics when it merged with Sky Television in November 1990. Mr Justice Tuckey said the merger in effect destroyed the market for BSB-receiving equipment. The Dutch group had signed a contract in 1989 obliging Philips to develop, manufacture and deliver receivers for satellite television reception until the end of 1992. The judge held there was an implied term in the contract that BSB would do nothing to destroy the market for Philips' products. Damages are to be assessed later. BSkyB, in which Pearson, owner of the Financial Times, has a significant stake, said it would appeal.
