930120 FT 20 JAN 93 / Storms damage two rail bridges STORM damage to bridges in Perthshire, Scotland, could cost Pounds 400,000 to repair, with estimated revenue losses of up to Pounds 500,000 while the repairs are carried out. The bridges carry the main rail line from Inverness to the south, and the line linking Glasgow with Aberdeen. Buses are being used to take passengers across the missing parts of the route. Rules governing driving times and rest periods for drivers of goods and passenger vehicles have been suspended in Scotland for up to the next 14 days to help the transport industries cope with the severe weather.
