920716 FT 16 JUL 92 / International Company News: Buoyant Gannett climbs to Dollars 99m GANNETT, the US publisher of 81 daily newspapers, including USA Today, yesterday said a recovery in advertising revenues helped it to a 4 per cent rise in net profits for the second quarter of 1992. The Virginia-based company said its second-quarter net profit was Dollars 98.745m, while earnings per share were 13 per cent higher at 69 cents. Wall Street reacted favourably to the profit increase, marking the Gannett share price Dollars 1 3/8 higher to Dollars 45 3/8 . Gannett said its second-quarter operating revenues were 2 per cent higher at Dollars 892.1m. Operating income for the quarter was, meanwhile, 9 per cent improved at Dollars 176.7m. The company said its newspaper publishing earnings rose due to higher advertising and circulation revenues, along with significantly lower newsprint costs. At USA Today, the down-market national daily, paid advertising pages increased by 3 per cent during the quarter. Broadcasting revenues were unchanged at Dollars 97.5m as revenues from Gannett's 10 television stations grew by 2 per cent and those from its 15 radio stations declined. Revenues from Gannett's outdoor advertising division, the largest such company in North America, fell 4 per cent to Dollars 66.8m in the quarter. For the first six months of 1992, earnings per share were 16 per cent higher at Dollars 1.07. Net income was Dollars 154.2m, compared with Dollars 145.1m in the first half of 1991. Operating revenues for the first six months were 1 per cent better at Dollars 1.7bn.
