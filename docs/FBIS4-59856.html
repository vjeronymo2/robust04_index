BFN [Text] Tehran, May 16, IRNA -- Bandar-e Anzali on the Caspian Sea has been declared a free trade zone. The announcement, according to the Persian evening daily KAYHAN TODAY, was made by President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani last night, after his inspection tour of the port city in Gilan Province. The president on Sunday at the end of his four-day visit of Gilan, referred to the capabilities of Bandar-e Anzali and stated that a bright future awaits the port and the region. Bandar-e Anzali with its extensive facilities is expected to serve as a key link in Iran's trade connections with Caspian Sea states which include Azerbaijan, Kazakhstan, the Russian Federation and Turkmenistan. The announcement brings to five the number of free trade zones in Iran. The other four zones are the Qeshm and Kish Islands off the southern Iranian coast in the Persian Gulf, Chahbahar port on the Gulf of Oman in Sistan-Baluchestan Province and Sirjan in the mineral rich interior of Kerman Province in the southeast.
