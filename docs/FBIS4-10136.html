BFN [Text] Cuban Foreign Minister Roberto Robaina said the great efforts of the Cuban people are aimed at increasing production and exports. In a news conference held in Montevideo, Uruguay, where he is carrying out an official visit, Robaina stressed that the Cuban Government decided to associate itself with foreign capital as an alternative for development in determined spheres such as tourism, oil, and nickel. Robaina added that Cuba's economic problems will not be solved with foreign investments alone. In explaining the human rights issue, Robaina said that in Cuba there are no disappearances or tortured people. In contrast, Robaina highlighted that for some time now, every Cuban generation has participated in the government. Robaina also held meetings with members of the Latin American Integration Association and with members of solidarity organizations with Cuba. In the latter, Robaina stated that Havana hopes to establish normal relations with Cuban emigres residing in 15 countries, and that the upcoming forum The Nation and the Emigre Community will help achieve just that. Cuban Foreign Minister Robaina is carrying out a tour through Uruguay, Peru, Argentina and Paraguay aimed at expanding and strengthening the bilateral relations between Cuba and those South American states.
