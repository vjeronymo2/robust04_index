940826 FT 26 AUG 94 / Railtrack accused on retired workers The RMT transport union last night accused Railtrack of employing a 'Dad's Army' of retired signalling staff in its efforts to break the 11-week-old rail strike, Charles Batchelor writes. Mr Jimmy Knapp, RMT general secretary, said Railtrack planned to employ a retired inspector at Birmingham New Street next Tuesday and had approached other retired staff in a move which posed a threat to the safety of the railway network. Railtrack confirmed that it had signed up one recently retired signalling manager who had taken early retirement in his mid-50s. It was looking to re-employ retired staff in their fifties and not people over 65. Railtrack said: 'These are very senior people with very valuable experience who would be very offended to be described as a Dad's Army.'
