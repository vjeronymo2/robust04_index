BFN [Article by Manki Ponomarev: "Russia and NATO Building Bridges"] [Excerpts] For many years the European continent was split by a deep abyss which divided the countries of the East and the countries of the West. It used to seem simply impassable -- in the political, economic, ideological, and military respects. One side was personified by the Warsaw Pact, the other by the North Atlantic bloc. But times have changed. There is no longer a Soviet Union or a Warsaw Pact. Even the former abyss has disappeared. Russia and the Western countries have taken the path of developing cooperation. The embodiment of this new era in international life was the signing by Russian Federation Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev at NATO headquarters of the Partnership for Peace program, adopted by the alliance in January this year. [passage omitted] It should be noted that, generally speaking, the path toward Russia's entry into partnership relations with NATO essentially began at the meeting that Army General Pavel Grachev, the Russian defense minister, had in Brussels in May. Subsequently, however, the Foreign Ministry virtually took over all the work preparatory to the signing of the framework document and the final protocol. Defense Ministry representatives were effectively eliminated from this work, even though the implementation of all the military measures within the partnership framework will, naturally, be the responsibility of the Russian Armed Forces. Be that as it may, the most important thing is that the implementation of the Partnership for Peace program as far as Russia and NATO are concerned will be a bridge across that abyss which divided Europe until just a few years ago.
