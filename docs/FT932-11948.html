930430 FT 30 APR 93 / Arts: Today's Television You sometimes get the impression that British terrestrial television is deliberately trying to make life easy for the satellite and cable people. Glance across Britain's four main networks in peak viewing time tonight, nine o'clock, say, and what do you find? On BBC1 a party political broadcast followed by the news. On BBC2 snooker. On ITV The Chief and on Channel 4 Grow Your Greens. If you are hooked on events at the Crucible no doubt BBC2's offering will enthrall you. If not, and entertainment is your aim, then ITV's police series is the only choice. At least The Chief is a superior example, with Tim Pigott-Smith being succeeded today by Martin Shaw in the key role of Eastland's Chief Constable. Remember Shaw as Doyle in 'The Professionals'? Hasn't he done well? Kaleidoscope (9.15 Radio 4) considers the way that 'sculpture is colonising our town squares' (something new?) And in Michael Bakewell's play A Sorceress Of Her Time, Jane Lapotaire plays Alma Mahler, who managed to marry - successively - Gustav Mahler, Walter Gropius and Franz Werfel (10.30 Radio 3).
