May 5, 1990, Saturday, Ventura County Edition

VENTURA COUNTY NEWS ROUNDUP: SANTA PAULA;

15-YEAR-OLD HELD IN THEFT OF ICE CREAM

A 17-year-old ice cream vendor was beaten and robbed of 140 ice cream bars in Santa Paula, authorities said Friday.

Police took a 15-year-old girl into custody about 4:30 p.m. Thursday after discovering the vendor's cart turned over in her front yard and the bars stuffed in her freezer, Santa Paula Police Cmdr.

Bob Gonzales said.

The vendor, from La Tapatia Ice Cream of San Bernardino, was hawking the snacks from his pushcart on Pleasant Street when two males and a teen-age girl beat him and ran off with his cart, Gonzales said.

The vendor's name is not being released because he is a minor, a Police Department spokeswoman said.

The vendor, badly shaken by the experience, told police that he had been "hit all over," Gonzales said. But he did not require medical attention and was not taken to a hospital.

No money was taken. The robbers were after only the frozen goods, Gonzales said.

Police discovered the cart on its side in front of the girl's house about a block away on Walnut Street, Gonzales said.

Police are searching for the two males, believed to be juveniles, Gonzales said.
