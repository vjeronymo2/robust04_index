BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow, 31 May -- "For Russia the Balkans are not just a dot on the map, but it has a place in our hearts", Ivan Rybkin, the chairman of the State Duma, stated during today's meeting with Karolos Papoulias, the foreign minister of the Greek republic. "We want very much", the speaker of the lower chamber of the Russian parliament noted also, "that there should be no hot spots in Europe and even more so that the flame of war should not burn in the fraternal Yugoslavia". Karolos Papoulias assured Ivan Rybkin that on international issues the positions of Russia and Greece are similar or fully coincide and noted that his side is interested in "Russia again taking a worthy place in the Balkans, which it used to occupy earlier and which it is worthy of by right". Incidentally, he admitted that "the European Union countries have committed mistakes with regard to Yugoslavia and Greece bears responsibility for it as well". At the same time Karolos Papoulias stressed that "despite the fact that no-one likes this, Greece will follow the same path as Russia in the matter of a peaceful settlement in the Balkans". The sides also discussed issues of interparliamentary cooperation between the two countries and spoke about the need to expand them.
