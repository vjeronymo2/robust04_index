931001 FT 01 OCT 93 / Observer: Pulling strings Chinese accountants finding life too exciting should apply to Eagle Star, which has decided to put money into turning the first 20 of China's 1.2bn people into actuaries. Steve Melcher, chairman of Eagle Star Life, says that a group of English-speaking mathematical whizzes are preparing for a three-year course for a master's degree in actuarial science. Asked if they will be producing carbon copies of their British brethren, Melcher explains that the course will be adapted to reflect the characteristics of the Chinese economy - such as that 'interest rates are manipulated differently'.
