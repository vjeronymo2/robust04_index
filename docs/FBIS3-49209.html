Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, 5 Jan (TANJUG) -- Slobodan Milosevic, president of the Republic of Serbia, received Thorvald Stoltenberg and David Owen, cochairmen of the Geneva Conference, and their aides who arrived in Belgrade this afternoon, it was announced in Belgrade this evening. On the Serbian side, the meeting was also attended by Radovan Karadzic, president of the Srpska republic; Momcilo Krajisnik, president of the Srpska republic assembly; Srpska Vice President Nikola Koljevic; Srpska Foreign Minister Aleksa Buha; and Yugoslav Foreign Minister Vladislav Jovanovic. They exchanged views with Thorvald Stoltenberg, David Owen, and their aides in connection with efforts to achieve a peace agreement for former Bosnia-Herzegovina, as well as about issues linked with creating conditions for the successful outcome of the peace process. The Serbian side noted that it has fulfilled its part of the obligation toward the European Twelve by securing 33.3 percent of the territory for the Muslim republic and that the European Union is now expected to fulfill its part of the obligation which refers to the lifting of sanctions against the FRY [Federal Republic of Yugoslavia]. The view of the Serbian side is that the remaining issues -- which are of lesser importance -- can be successfully resolved in the course of further negotiations, the cabinet of the Serbian president announced.
