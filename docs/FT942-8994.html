940518 FT 18 MAY 94 / Spending falls on acquiring companies Expenditure on acquisitions fell from Pounds 2.8bn in the final quarter of last year to Pounds 2.7bn in the first three months of 1994, according to the Central Statistical Office, Andrew Jack writes. The number of UK acquisitions by industrial and commercial companies rose from 164 to 176. In the first quarter of last year, there were 103 acquisitions with a value of Pounds 1.6bn. Expenditure on acquiring independent companies was Pounds 2.3bn compared with Pounds 900m in the previous quarter. Cash expenditure on acquisitions accounted for 46 per cent of the total, down 87 per cent, with the remainder coming from issues of ordinary shares.
