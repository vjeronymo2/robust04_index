Language: Japanese Article Type:CSO [Complete text of article obtained through NIKKEI BIZ DATABASE] [Text] Mitsubishi Heavy Industries has added a new type to its line of turbo refrigerating machines for large capacity air conditioners by employing "HFC-134a," a CFC substitute, as the refrigerant. By adjusting the heat exchange cycle, the new machines can be used for heating up to 50°C and for cooling to -10°C. HFC-134a has less impact on the environment than "CFC-11" and does not deplete the ozone layer. The turbo refrigerating machines using HFC-134a are the third type to employ a CFC substitute, following those with "HCFC-123" and "HCFC-22." The new type consists of six models with different refrigeration capabilities ranging from 150 to 400 refrigerating tons and priced from ¥39.5 million to ¥56 million. Large capacity models of up to 7,000 refrigerating tons will be developed based on the sales conditions of the smaller models.
