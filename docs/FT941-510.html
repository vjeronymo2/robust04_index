940330 FT 30 MAR 94 / NZ offer to repay Nauru New Zealand said yesterday it would pay compensation to the Pacific island state of Nauru for environmental damage caused by phosphate mining, Reuter reports from Wellington. New Zealand, Australia and Britain jointly mined for phosphate until Nauru won independence in 1968. Mr Don McKinnon, foreign minister, said Wellington and London would each contribute ADollars 12m (Dollars 8.5m) towards a total compensation package of ADollars 107m.
