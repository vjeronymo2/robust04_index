940108 FT 08 JAN 94 / Exchange breaks eight records Eight records for capital raising, trading markets and indices were set on the London Stock Exchange in 1993. The exchange said that after record turnover in November, December was the second-busiest month ever, with business in London's domestic and international equity markets together totalling Pounds 109.38bn. The FT-SE-A All-Share Index reached a record high of 1698.75 on December 29, while the FT-SE 100 Index reached its historical peak on the same day at 3462.0. In 1993 a record 180 UK companies achieved exchange listings, up from the previous high of 155 set in 1987. Total equity turnover of Pounds 1,143.5bn was up 34.5 per cent from the previous record of 1992. Cash raised from UK equity rights issues was a record Pounds 11.38bn, up 12.3 per cent from the record set in 1991. The exchange said monthly turnover in UK equities had been the highest since July 1987 at Pounds 55.8bn, mostly reflecting investor enthusiasm following chancellor Mr Kenneth Clarke's Budget statement. Business in overseas equities reached Pounds 53.6bn, the fourth-highest ever, after the successful conclusion of the Uruguay round of Gatt trade talks.
