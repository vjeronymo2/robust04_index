Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] At a session held today under Speaker Shaykh 'Abdallah Bin-Husayn al-Ahmar, the House of Representatives endorsed the 3d Protocol of the Constitution of the Universal Postal Union [UPU] approved at the Hamburg Congress of 1984, as well as the documents of the UPU's 20th Congress held in Washington in 1989 with sufficient votes. The House had earlier discussed voting on Republican Decree No. 33 of 1992, which concerns the salaries and pensions of the Armed Forces and security personnel, in light of the provisions enshrined in Articles 83 and 130 of the bylaws of the House of Representatives. The House postponed voting on the aforementioned decree to its next session. At the outset of its session today, the House discussed the overall economic situation, and particularly the declining exchange rate of the Yemeni currency, the exorbitant cost of living, and price hikes of staples, foremost among which are food supplies, medicines, and other basic survival requirements throughout the Republic of Yemen. The House decided to hold a general debate on this issue in the presence of government ministers in the session it will hold on the morning of Monday [10 January], God willing, to make firm decisions to address the people's living conditions, remedy the situation of the Yemeni currency, and accord due attention to public issues that concern citizens throughout the country. The House asked the House committee assigned the task of finding the facts pertaining to the current political crisis to continue its duties in light of the accomplishments it has thus far made, and to inform the House of the results of the tasks it will undertake during the next phase without any delay. For the Ministry of Transportation, the session was attended by Ahmad Hasan Qa'id, director general of the Public Postal Organization, and 'Ali Muhammad al-Qasim, director general of the Postal Services Department.
