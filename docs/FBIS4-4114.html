BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, April 28 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Premier Li Peng arrived here this afternoon for a two-day official visit, the first by a Chinese head of government to Mongolia in over three decades. Prime Minister P. Jasray presided over a grand ceremony at the airport to welcome Li Peng, who ended a 10-day tour of Central Asia earlier today. A written statement was issued upon Li's arrival at the airport. "We are happy to see the gratifying expansion of Sino-Mongolian friendly cooperation in all fields in the past few years," Li said. The maintaining of a friendly and good-neighborly relationship between China and Mongolia conforms with the common wishes and fundamental interests of the two peoples, and will benefit peace, stability and development in the Asia-Pacific region as well, he said. During this visit, Li said, he expects to hold talks with Prime Minister P. Jasray and meet with president P. Ochirbat to exchange views on ways of furthering bilateral relations and on other issues of common concern. Also, the two sides will sign a treaty on Sino-Mongolian friendship and cooperation, he said. The document will further advance the friendly relations as well as exchanges and cooperation between the two countries in all fields, he said. China will work together with Mongolia towards that goal, he said.
