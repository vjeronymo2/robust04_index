Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following are reports on insurgent activities in Peru compiled from Peruvian papers monitored between 17 and 19 December. A group of 12 Shining Path "terrorists" stormed a bank in Lima and opened fire at point blank range against the seven employees and some 12 clients who were there, killing five people. A "terrorist" identified as Teodora Huamani Ramos also died during the incident. The "terrorists" escaped after scattering leaflets and planting two bombs on Riva Aguero Avenue, and one on Tranquilino Velarde Street. Another two groups of "terrorists" keeping watch outside the bank machine-gunned a police pickup truck that went past the bank, killing two plainclothesmen. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 17 Dec 93 p A18) On 16 December a group of approximately 60 Shining Path "terrorists" invaded the township of Chingal, in Sihuas Province, 270 km north of the city of Huaraz, burning public buildings and threatening to kill local authorities if they did not resign their posts within 15 days. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 18 Dec 93 p A17) On 14 December a group of nearly 60 Shining Path "terrorists" invaded Quinches District, in Sihuas Province, 310 km southeast of the city of Huaraz, where they first threatened to kill the local mayor and then later hung him from a tree in the Plaza de Armas. The "terrorists" stole money, food, animals, and medicines during the two days they stayed in the township. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 19 Dec 93 p A19)
