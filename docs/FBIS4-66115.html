BFN [Unattributed article: "Rwanda: `Utmost Care' in Assessing Any Mission, Martino Says"] [Text] (ANSA) Washington, May 24 -- The "extremely important military and diplomatic implications" of an Italian peacekeeping mission to Rwanda "must be studied with the utmost care" by the Italian government before any decision is taken, Italian Foreign Minister Antonio Martino said at a press conference here. Asked to comment on Defence Minister Cesare Previti's statement at a Brussels NATO meeting of "general willingness" for such a mission, Martino said "I think Previti wanted to stress the Italian government's concern for the orphans in Rwanda". He added that "nothing precludes" an Italian mission, but pointed out that there are "risks of various kinds" attached to it. "A decision can only be taken by the government, obviously," he said.
