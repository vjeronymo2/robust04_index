Language: English Article Type:BFN [From ELTA "NEWS BULLETIN" No. 408] [Text] Vilnius, Jan 5 -- The right-wing opposition leader Vytautas Landsbergis made a statement concerning the official request for the membership in NATO: "I hope that the West will notice the letter and request by Lithuanian president and Lithuania will stand next to Poland not only in the map, but in the list of candidates for NATO as well. Nobody can protest against that. We are striving for stability and security and this causes neither danger nor humiliation for anybody. I also see that Lithuania's wish to become a member of NATO and its request do not contradict the agreement on Partnership For Peace. But the agreement on partnership will not serve as a substitute for conditions which should be fulfilled by Lithuania in order to become a member of NATO. This complies with the agreement on European security initiated by France and maintained by European Union. It is very important that the process of peace and European consolidation in the Baltic region should not be disturbed by any non-democratic imperialistic forces".
