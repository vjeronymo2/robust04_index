940208 FT 08 FEB 94 / Business failures 'at four-year low' Business failures reached their lowest level last month since December 1989, a survey said yesterday. There were 163 appointments of receivers or administrators to troubled companies in January, 56 per cent down on the number reported in the same month last year, said accountants Touche Ross. Touche Ross said the January data showed that business failures were continuing to fall at a significant rate. From a peak of 5,734 appointments in 1991, the figure fell to 3,226 in 1993 and was expected to be as low as 2,000 this year. The firm said: 'This would seem to confirm (our) previously stated opinion that not only is the recession over but that companies are recovering steadily and cautiously and not being persuaded to overtrade.' The reduction in receivership appointments meant more attention could be given to the underlying financial and structural deficiencies of firms, it added.
