BFN [Text] The court of criminal appeal of Algiers Judicial Council today issued a ruling in the case of the deceased president, Mohamed Boudiaf, and decided to refer the defendant Boumaarafi Lembarek to the criminal court of Algiers Judicial Council. Boumaarafi is charged with committing a premeditated murder, causing deliberate injuries with firearms, and planning to topple the regime. These crimes are punishable in accordance with Articles 254, 255, 261, 256, 257, 77, and 266 of the penal code. It is worth noting that the court of criminal appeal had, on 1 March 1994, ruled in favor of further investigations into the case to examine all the elements of the inquiry. Afterwards the measures linked to these additional inquiries were implemented by the examining magistrate who was busy completing the investigation entrusted to him. At the end of his mission, the magistrate sent the file back to the court of criminal appeal of Algiers Judicial Council which, after examining it, ruled to refer the defendant Boumaarafi Lembarek to the criminal court of Algiers Judicial Council. Thus, the case is now ready to be examined at a trial. The trial is due to start in July 1994, in accordance with the provisions of the law.
