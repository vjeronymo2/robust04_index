Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 30/3/1994 -- "We are going to visit a country that we have specially good relations with," Romanian President Ion Iliescu told the press at the Otopeni airport on his departure for a two-day visit to Greece. Evoking the traditional relations between the two countries, President Ion Iliescu said that Romania and Greece were promoting the Balkan collaboration and understanding and reminded that the relations between the two countries had continuously developed since the December 1989 revolution in Romania, as Romania and Greece were tied not only through mutual interests in the zone. The agenda of the visit includes talks with Greek President Konstandinos Karamanlis, with Premier Andreas Papandreou, with representatives of political parties in the Greek Parliament, the first European parliament to have ratified Romania's association to the European Union, as well as with Greek businessmen.
