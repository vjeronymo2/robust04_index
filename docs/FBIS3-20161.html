Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] Willy Claes, Belgian vice prime minister and minister of foreign affairs, has arrived on an official visit in Ankara. At Esenboga Airport he was received by Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin. In an airport statement, Cetin said that Belgium and Turkey have maintained their relations for years as two friendly countries and allies and that the two countries have joint values such as democracy, the supremacy of the law, and a free market economy. He added that regional and international problems and primarily the issues of Bosnia-Herzegovina and Azerbaijan will be discussed during Claes' visit. Claes said that the two countries have, for years, maintained good relations as two NATO member countries. He added that there are currently some 100,000 Turkish citizens living in friendship and peace in Belgium. Claes said that Turkey's relations with the European Union as well as problems concerning Europe will be discussed during his visit.
