Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Akimov] [Text] Almaty March 16 TASS -- Kazakhstan's state delegation headed by Prime Minister Sergey Tereshchenko set off on Wednesday for a working visit to Moscow. The visit aims to pave the way for the proposed Kazakh-Russian summit. Under an earlier agreeement, the delegation is scheduled to meet for talks with Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin and cabinet members to discuss a wide range of issues of mutual concern. These issues can be grouped into four sets, which are of especial importance to further development of bilateral relations, a spokesman for the Kazakh prime minister reported. The two sides will consider military and military-technical cooperation, operation of the Baykonur space launching site, peaceful use of atomic energy, improvement of ties in the economic, scientific, cultural and law spheres. The same problems are to be discussed by Presidents Boris Yeltsin of Russia and Nursultan Nazarbayev of Kazakhstan during their summit scheduled for late March.
