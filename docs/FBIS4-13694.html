BFN [Text] Tehran, April 21, IRNA -- Thousands of pupils and high school students staged demonstrations outside the U.N. office in Tehran Thursday, condemning the silence of the U.N., its Security Council and other international bodies on the massacre of Muslims in Gorazde. Carrying placards and banners in condemnation of the crimes of Serbs, the demonstrators chanted slogans such as "Serbs are committing crimes, Washington is supporting them" and "Death to the Serbs." They also called for resignation of the U.N. Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali. The students denounced the biased policies and shameful silence of western governments as well as the Security Council's negligence towards Bosnia-Herzegovina. Similar demonstrations were held in front of the U.N. office by Iranian university students and in the holy city of Qom by ulama Wednesday in protest at the cold-blooded massacre of Muslims in Gorazde.
