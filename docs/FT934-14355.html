931016 FT 16 OCT 93 / Finance and the Family: FT Quarterly Review of Personal Finance - At a glance A recent survey by an insurance company found that two out of three workers want to retire before they are 60. But early retirement can bring financial headaches. Someone retiring at 55 could easily live for another 35 years - longer than the time they spent in work. The autumn issue of the FT Quarterly Review of Personal Finance, to be published with next Friday's paper and repeated with Saturday's, considers different aspects of 'going early'. The Review also includes a survey on Unit Trusts.
