920227 FT 27 FEB 92 / Nato worried by military pull-out MR Manfred Worner, Nato secretary-general, yesterday said he regretted that Canada had decided to bring home its troops from Europe, signalling alliance concern about the weakening of crucial transatlantic links, Reuter reports. Canada's government announced the decision on Tuesday as part of cuts to pull the country out of recession. 'While I recognise the financial pressures which led to this decision, I have noted it with considerable regret, given the political and military importance of the presence of Canadian forces in Europe,' Mr Worner said. Britain yesterday also expressed concern at the Canadian move. 'The decision to withdraw the remaining Canadian forces is a sad end to a long and valued Canadian deployment in West Europe,' the Foreign Office said. 'We note that the withdrawal will be phased over two to three years and we hope that this may give time (for Canada) to reconsider.'
