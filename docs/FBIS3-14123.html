Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Sana' al-Imam report from Damascus] [Text] U.S. coordinator Dennis Ross has relayed a message from U.S. President Bill Clinton to President Hafiz al-Asad on the peace process, and the talks and contacts he has recently held to activate the peace process. Ross also reviewed with President al-Asad the outcome of his current regional tour. U.S. Coordinator Dennis Ross has said that his discussions with President al-Asad today addressed the next steps on the Syrian-Israeli track. He added: A decision to resume the bilateral talks in April has been made. We will hold consultations with our Russian partners on a date for resuming them. Following almost four hours of talks with with President al-Asad, which he described as good, Ross said: We are committed to do whatever we can to achieve a breakthrough this year. The U.S. coordinator noted that he has been asked by U.S. President Bill Clinton and Secretary of State Christopher to visit Syria to brief President al-Asad on the outcome of Rabin's Washington talks. Ross added: I have also apprised President al-Asad of the Israeli-Palestinian discussions held in Tunis, and of the progress made in these discussions. U.S. coordinator Dennis Ross, who arrived in the Syrian capital from Jordan yesterday evening, made a statement upon arrival, saying that his discussions in the Arab capitals he has visited were fruitful, and that they are moving in the right direction. He added that his Syria visit is prompted by the fact that it is a major player in the peace process, as he put it.
