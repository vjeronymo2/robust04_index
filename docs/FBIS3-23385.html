Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Home Ministry's annual report for the year 1993-94 says that the unabated aiding and abetting of terrrorism from across the border in Jammu and Kashmir [J and K] is coupled with growing infiltration of foreign nationals and mercenaries into the state. It said efforts were made to destabilize and destroy the democratic institutions by the fear of the gun and to change the demographic profile in parts of the state. The report also regretted that new trend was observed in the form of efforts to spread violence in the Jammu region, particularly to Doda District. Referring to the government's efforts to expose the false propaganda and the role of Pakistan in fuelling terrorism in the state, the report gives details of several initiatives launched during the year for accelerating development and economic activity in J and K. On Punjab, the report observed that the situation there has shown a marked inprovement. The report also highlighted various steps taken by the government to curb communal violence in general and that which was related to the Ayodhya incident. Other steps taken by the government on the Ayodhya issue were also given in the report.
