Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The government's most important tasks concern the economy, declared Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak during this morning's conversation with listeners and journalists of Polish Radio's First Program. [passage omitted] One of the important subjects broached during the conversation with Prime Minister Pawlak was the possibility of Poland joining the economic and defense structures of the West. [Begin Pawlak recording] This is not just a case of our expectations and our choice -- it is also a case of the decision and the responsibility of European and other countries that make up the NATO structure. I think it is in our common interest that the changes that took place in 1989 should find a very definite expression in these joint interrelations -- both economic ones and those connected with external security -- in order that we might calmly be able to say that this was not just a short breath of freedom before having to cope with the same problems that we have had to deal with for such a long time. [end recording]
