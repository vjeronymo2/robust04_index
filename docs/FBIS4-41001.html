BFN [By an ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent] [Text] Moscow June 21 TASS -- UNESCO Director-General Federico Mayor began his visit to Moscow with talks with Russian minister of science and technology and head of the Russian commission of UNESCO Affairs Boris Saltykov. During his first working day, the UNESCO chief is also scheduled to meet patriarch of Moscow and All Russia Aleksiy II, mayor of Moscow Yuriy Luzhkov and Russian mass media officials. Federico Mayor is a leading Spanish scientist and biochemist. He has written over 50 scientific works, member of several academies, and a noted political worker. Mayor has been at the helm of the prestigious specialized organization in the U.N. system for the past more than six years. UNESCO helps strengthen peace, security and cooperation among the people in the field of education, science and culture. Before beginning his current visit to Russia, in an interview with ITAR-TASS, Federico Mayor emphasised that he would like to see closer interaction between the two sides in the reconstruction and modernization of the Hermitage museum in St. Petersburg, and the Russian state library and the Bolshoy theatre complex in Moscow. Favouring everyday contacts with Russia, Mayor intends to take part in the opening ceremony of a UNESCO bureau in the Russian capital. According to him, the international organization can make use of Russia's experience in its own activities. The UNESCO director-general is expected to be received by Russian President Boris Yeltsin on Wednesday.
