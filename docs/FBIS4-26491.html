BFN [Text] Canberra, June 23 (XINHUA) -- Li Tieying, state councilor of China, arrived in Melbourne this morning to start his nine-day visit to Australia. Li, who is also minister in charge of the State Commission for Restructuring the Economic Systems, is heading a delegation to have an in-depth investigation into Australia's economic and financial structures. During his trip, which will also bring him to Canberra and Sydney, Li will meet government ministers as well as industrial and financial leaders. It is understood that these meetings will focus mainly on bilateral economic relations and Australia's economic and fiscal polices. He will also make speeches at the Australia-China Business Council and the Asia-Australia Institute, briefing them on China's on-going economic and financial reforms and its foreign investment policy.
