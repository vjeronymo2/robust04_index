931006 FT 06 OCT 93 / International Company News: Hershey chairman to quit HERSHEY Foods, the US chocolate producer, said yesterday that Mr Richard Zimmerman would step down as chairman and chief executive at the end of the year, writes Frank McGurty. Mr Kenneth Wolfe, currently president and chief operating officer, was elected by the board of directors as his successor. Mr Joseph Viviano, president of Hershey Chocolate USA, will replace Mr Wolfe. 'These changes have been studied by the board over the past two years and are part of a planned, orderly succession,' said Mr Zimmerman, who has headed the Pennsylvania-based company since 1985. Hershey, which expects record earnings this year in spite of an overall slump in domestic candy sales, recently launched a white chocolate version of its Kisses candy. The new product, Hugs, is expected to generate annual sales of Dollars 100m.
