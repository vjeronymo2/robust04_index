Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 17 KYODO -- Japan's corporate pretax profits for the October-December quarter slid 6.2 percent from a year before, declining for the 14th straight quarter, the Finance Ministry said Thursday [17 March]. The pretax profits for manufacturing companies fell 16.5 percent, while those for nonmanufacturers increased 3.9 percent, the ministry said. Combined sales dropped 1.4 percent, suffering the seventh consecutive quarter of decline from year-earlier levels. Corporate capital investment during the reporting quarter shrank 16.8 percent, for the eighth straight quarterly decline from year-earlier levels.
