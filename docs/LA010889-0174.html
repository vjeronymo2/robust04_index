January 8, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

THE WAY OF COMMAS

I must acknowledge that those portions of my book, "The Way of the Hunter," that Schuyler Ingle (Book Review, Dec. 4) did not find too tiresome a chore to read, he did seem begrudgingly to enjoy; and I do appreciate that. It is regrettable, though, that he found so much of the rest of it such "convoluted slogging." It's a shame that the proper use of grammar forced me to "scatter" all those commas through the text, as he so coyly put it, "like thousands of little kisses." It's a pity I was unable to convey complex ideas in as simplistic a fashion as he would have liked.

It just might be, though, that there are other readers at large who would not find the task nearly so grueling.

I would also like to note that the book, rather than the 180 pages in length that Ingle indicated, is with bibliography and index actually 223 pages long, and contains more than 60 photo illustrations.

THOMAS McINTYRE

Hunting Editor
