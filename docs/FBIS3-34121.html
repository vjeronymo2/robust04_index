Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest, Feb. 3, IRNA -- Iranian and Romanian foreign ministers discussed issues relevant to the security and unity of the European countries as well as developments in central Asia in their second round of talks here today. A source close to the talks said that Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Viorel Melescanu had raised the issue of cooperation between the Economic Cooperation Organization (ECO) which grouped Iran and nine neighbours, and the Organization of the Black Sea Littoral States. The two decided the issue should be followed up by the secretariats of the two organizations, the source added. Iranian Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati and his Romanian counterpart inked two political and trade agreements as well as a plan on the scientific, educational, cultural and art exchanges at the end of the meeting. Talking to reporters after the meeting, Velayati said the agreements reached by the two countries showed that the two were keen to further expand their ties. Asked about the attempted assassination against President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani last Tuesday, Velayati condemned terrorism, and expressed pleasure that the terrorist act had failed. He also said that Islamic Iran would spare no efforts in the way of the establishment of a just and honorable peace in Bosnia-Herzegovina.
