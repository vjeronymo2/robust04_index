920410 FT 10 APR 92 / Election 1992: Fallon ousted from Darlington MR MICHAEL Fallon, the junior education minister, lost his Darlington seat to Labour. Mr Alan Milburn overturned Mr Fallon's 2,661 majority, polling 26,556. Mr Fallon polled 23,758. He had held the seat since winning a 1983 by-election. Mr Fallon was the only minister with a seat in the 30 constituencies between the Scottish border and North Yorkshire. Mr Fallon was a member of the No Turning Back Group, the self-styled guardian of Thatcherite doctrine. The group called for the dismantling of the welfare state, the privatisation of British Rail, and considered a voucher scheme for the NHS. He began his political career as political adviser to Lord Carrington, the former foreign secretary, and was appointed education minister in 1990.
