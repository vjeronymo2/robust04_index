BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 23 KYODO -- The Japanese manufacturers' trade terms index dipped 0.1 percentage point in April from March but remained 1.2 points higher than a year before, the Bank of Japan said Monday [23 May]. The index of the scale of price changes passed on from raw materials to finished goods stood at 111.1 against a base of 100 for 1985, the central bank said in a monthly report. Input prices declined 0.2 percent in April from March and were 3.2 percent below a year before, while output prices fell 0.3 percent and were down 2.1 percent from April 1993, it said. The input price index was 82.2, with domestic prices dipping 0.2 percent for the month and import prices down 1.8 percent. The output index was 91.3, with domestic prices down 0.2 percent and export prices 0.7 percent lower, the report said. The trade terms index rose for seven of the report's 14 industrial sectors, led by a 3.8 percentage point increase for oil and coal products, of which petroleum products were up 5.4 points. The report cited lower crude oil and other raw materials prices for the improvement. The index declined for textiles, ceramics, metal products, electrical machinery, transportation machinery and precision machinery, with the report noting higher overseas market prices for cotton but falling export prices for machinery.
