931116 FT 16 NOV 93 / World Commodities Prices: Tea The Tea Broker's Association reports, Improved general demand. Coloury North Indians sold well at around last levels but prices for lesser mediums tended lower following quality. Bright and good medium East Africans met more competition and prices often advanced 2 to 5 pence. Plainer types however lost 4 to 8 pence. Ceylons met fair demand at easier rates. Offshore good demand. Ceylons sold well but Kenyas moved easier. Quotations: quality 190p/kg, good medium 130p/kg, medium 107p/kg, low medium 97p/kg. The highest price realised this week was 200 for a Rwanda pf. 1.
