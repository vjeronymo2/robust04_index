940308 FT 08 MAR 94 / Names defeat agents' appeal for delay Lloyd's Names on the lossmaking Gooda Walker syndicates yesterday defeated a bid by the agents they are suing to adjourn a court case scheduled to begin next month. The case by 3,000 Names - the individuals whose assets support the insurance market - is due to come to court on April 26. Agents had applied to adjourn the case on the grounds that a key witness had refused to co-operate with them. Mr Justice Cresswell rejected the application, awarding costs of the hearing to the Names. Mr Michael Deeny, chairman of the Gooda Walker Action Group, said the courts were 'showing commendable and consistent determination to deal with all the Lloyd's litigation with despatch and efficiency'. He deplored agents' efforts to delay the action by 'every legalistic, pettifogging device'. A Lords ruling in a separate appeal by agents on an issue which has a bearing on the case will be held later this month. The Lords are to consider whether members' agents, which handle the affairs of Names, can be vicariously liable for the default of managing agents, which administer syndicates. The Association of Lloyd's Members, which represents about 9,000 Names, has urged Lloyd's to eliminate 'double counting' from its accounts. Double counting occurs because the same losses are registered by the syndicates that pay claims, as well as syndicates specialising in errors and omissions insurance and personal stop loss insurance. Errors and omissions insurers cover Lloyd's agents against legal awards for negligence, while personal stop loss insurers provide Names with a kind of personal reinsurance policy. Indemnity Insurance Services, a broker which administers the Integer Stop Loss scheme, estimates that the double count may amount to as much as Pounds 400m. The company said that given some additional information it would be able to calculate a special credit for all Names that would effectively remove the double count. Mr Valentine Powell, chief executive of the association, said: 'This is a step Lloyd's could and should take.'
