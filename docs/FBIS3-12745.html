Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali received at the Prime Ministry this morning Karolos Papoulias, Greek foreign minister and chairman of the European Community [EC] Council of Ministers, and the delegation accompanying him. Their discussion dealt with bilateral relations and the existing cooperation between Jordan and the EC countries, ways of enhancing them, as well as the most important issues in the international arena. In a press statement on the most important ideas he communicated to the Israeli leaders on behalf of the Palestinian president, Papoulias said: These ideas revolve around calming the atmosphere and creating a new atmosphere that would facilitate things, provide security for the Palestinians, and lead to disarming the settlers and resuming the dialogue between the two sides. He said that the Israeli side has promised to take these ideas into consideration and will reply to them later. He said: Efforts will be exerted in the future and the EC hopes that the peace efforts will produce good results and will not reach a dead end. The meeting was attended by Talal Sat'an al-Hasan, minister of state for foreign affairs; Dr. Fawwaz Abu-al-Ghanam, minister of state; the Jordanian ambassador to Greece; the Greek ambassador to Jordan; and the EC representative in Amman.
