Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Jakarta, Jan 22 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- Malaysian International Trade and Industry Minister Rafidah Aziz has urged ASEAN countries not to give in to pressures by big countries to change the forum of the Asia-Pacific Economic Co-operation (APEC) into a formal institution. Malaysia will not give in to pressures and we hope other ASEAN countries will do the same, she said during a dialogue session to expose Indonesian businessmen on trade and investment opportunities in Malaysia here Friday. Responding to a question on Malaysia's stand towards APEC which a dialogue participant said differed to that of other ASEAN countries, she said Malaysia had no objection to cooperation between Asia and Pacific as it was a loose forum and programme and project orientated. What we disagree is turning APEC into a structural and formal institution, holding summits for country leaders and, with its secretariat expanding and no longer programme and project orientated, she said. Rafidah, who is leading a 25-member delegation for a two-day visit to Indonesia, said ASEAN would lose out if APEC were to be a formal institution. Why we don't want it to be so because ASEAN, with its six countries, has 320 million people. We have long promoted ASEAN. We shouldn't be submerged in Asia-Pacific and losing our voice in a formal APEC, she said. It was for this reason, she said, that Malaysia proposed the East Asia Economic Caucas (EAEC) with ASEAN as the backbone. Rafidah also called on ASEAN businessmen to be more aggresive in establishing economic cooperation among them apart from intensifying efforts to set up joint venture companies. They should exploit the rapid economic growth in the region, like their non-ASEAN counterparts were doing, she said. She said such cooperation was vital in pooling together their experience, expertise, and financial resources in order to compete with non-ASEAN entrepreneurs not only in the region but also elsewhere. Earlier, Rafidah held discussions with Trade Minister Satrio Judono on bilateral trade as well as regional and global issues. She also met Research and Technology Minister B.J. Habibie where she discussed the possiblility of Malaysia participating in Indonesia's aviation industry.
