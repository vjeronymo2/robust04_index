Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ulaanbaatar, March 24 (OANA-MONTSAME) -- The newspaper "ARDIYN ERH" informs that the spring session of the Great State Hural of Mongolia has completed the consideration in first reading of the regulations of the research centre to be founded under the jurisdiction of the parliament. The draft regulations were drawn up by a special commission. The newspaper notes that the research centre under the parliament is intended to carry out research work promoting the improvement of the draft laws being elaborated and to provide the Great State Hural with information required. According to the newspaper, the session has charged the standing parliamentary committee on meetings and adminstration to work out within the April month for submitting for consideration to the Great State Hural the proposals on setting up a working group to provide making changes and am endments to the election law. The newspaper reports that the chairman of the great state Hural N. Bagabandi congratulated the population of the country of Kazah descent on the occasion of their conventional spring holiday "Nauryz". The "ARDIYN ERH" informs that on the invitation of the deputy prime minister of the state council, Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China Qian Qichen, the Minister of External Relations of Mongolia T. Gombosuren would pay an official visit to China. The visit is to last from 6 to 11 April. The newspaper carries an article by chairman of the subcommittee of the Great State Hural on struggle with bribery and abuses of official position M. Zenee. In the article it is stressed that it is the spread of corruption which is the major reason hampering the progressive development of any state. M. Zenee believes that over a few last years the tendency towards widespreading of the corruption and abuses of official position is being observed to be characteristic of a number of representatives of high-ranking state authority bodies including a member of the parliament R. Gonchigdorj. The capital newspaper "ULAANBAATAR" continues publishing materials devoted to the forthcoming 355th anniversary of the city. In this issue the newspaper writes about the creation at the end of the 30s a thermo power station in Ulaanbaatar. Such a power station was built under assistance of a German AEG firm with the participation of German and Swedish engineers. The newspaper underlines that the power station was installed with the most modern for that time equipment made in such countries as Britain, Germany and Sweden. The power station was put into operation in the autumn of 1931. In his article published in the newspaper an academician T. Dabaajamts puts forward his ideas aimed at improving the conventional Mongolian dwelling ger. [as received] According to the academician, the ger is of not so least importance for solving the housing problem of the population. The newspaper gives an inverview with the head of the veterinary service of the capital D. Baljinnyam who noted that according to the results of the census there are more than 266 thousand head of privately owned cattle. D. Baljinnyam said that the vaccination of the cattle population is started in the city. He expresses his anxiety about the growing number of stray dogs in the capital regarding them to be impeding a serious danger for both the residents of the city and the population of cattle as well. According to the newspaper, on the initiative of a group youth [as received] in the Mongolian capital there has been created the first fan club named after the well known rocksinger from Great Britain Elton John. Members of the club are planning to celebrate the birthday of the singer who will be 47 tomorrow.
