Language: English Article Type:BFN [By Lilian Wu] [Text] Taipei, Feb. 21 (CNA) -- Taiwan will lift its import restrictions on apples in preparation for entry into the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade, the Council of Agriculture (COA) said Sunday [20 February]. However, imported apples will face even stricter quarantine measures after the restrictions are lifted, especially those from the United States, Australia, New Zealand, South Africa and Chile, which have been declared codling moth regions, the COA added. COA officials said that Taiwan currently limits the purchase of apples from the US and allows limited imports from Japan, Australia and New Zealand. The officials said that after lifting the restrictions, apples from Japan and Korea will have the competitive edge here while apples from the US, Australia and New Zealand will not be allowed in if they do not meet the stricter quarantine measures. COA officials said that the enhanced quarantine measures are necessary to protect domestic agriculture in the face of an expected flood of imported apples. Australia and New Zealand have expressed concern about the new quarantine measures, Sun Ming-hsien, COA chairman, said upon his return home Sunday from a weeklong visit to New Zealand. Sun said he has agreed to a New Zealand request for a meeting on the measures. The time of the meeting has yet to be set, he noted. Australia is also expected to raise the issue of the new quarantine measures during a scheduled meeting with COA in March.
