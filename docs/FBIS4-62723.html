BFN [Text] Charles Freeman, assistant secretary on regional security at the U.S. Defense Department, is visiting Estonia today. The U.S. visitors had a meeting with Prime Minister Mart Laar, Defense Minister Indrek Kannik, Foreign Minister Juri Luik, and Defense Forces commander Major General Aleksandr Einseln. Freeman said at a news conference that during the day a substantial amount of work had been done in Estonia. He appreciated the structure of the Estonian defense system and noted that the United States supports comprehensively Estonia's integration with NATO. Indrek Kannik said at a news conference that issues to do with the Partnership for Peace program had formed the focus of their discussions as well as U.S. assistance towards the formation of the Baltic peacekeeping battalion. Kannik noted that Estonia is now able to obtain military equipment also from the United States, an option that did not exist previously. Tonight President Lennart Meri will also receive the U.S. visitors.
