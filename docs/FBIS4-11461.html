BFN [Excerpt] Buenos Aires, 25 Apr (NA) -- The Grand Front (FG)'s Constituent Assembly members confirmed today their rejection of the Olivos Pact Basic Agreement Nucleus [Nucleo de Coincidencias Basicas del Pacto de Olivos] and announced their decision to walk out of the Constituent Assembly at voting time if the "13-point `package'" agreed upon between the PJ [Justicialist Party] and the UCR [Radical Civic Union] on the constitutional reform issue "is not opened." The proposal was approved today by 29 of the 31 FG Constituent Assembly members elected on 10 April. This was the FG's first meeting after the elections. The meeting was held in the new FG headquarters at the intersection of Rivadavia Avenue and Ayacucho Street. The meeting was chaired by Deputy Carlos "Chacho" Alvarez, Deputy Graciela Fernandez Meijide, Deputy Fernando "Pino" Solanas, and Neuquen Bishop Monsignor Jaime De Nevares. Among the FG's main leaders also present were former Deputy Carlos Auyero, teachers union leader Mary Sanchez, Buenos Aires municipal councilman Anibal Ibarra, metalworkers union leader Alberto Piccinini, [Communist Party leader] Patricio Etchegaray, and Eduardo Barcesat. During the meeting, it was confirmed that Monsignor De Nevares will be the FG candidate for Constituent Assembly president. De Nevares' candidacy was raised by Solanas. The Constituent Assembly is scheduled to reform the Constitution. [passage omitted]
