February 4, 1990, Sunday, South Bay Edition

AROUND THE SOUTH BAY

Congressman Dana Rohrabacher is having a hard time selling his proposal to offer coastal communities a cut in future offshore oil royalties.

Last month city leaders of Palos Verdes Estates sent him a letter stating that, royalties or no royalties, they would continue to oppose offshore oil drilling under any circumstances. And on Tuesday, the Rancho Palos Verdes City Council decided unanimously to mail him a similar message.

"We don't think that money could compensate for the damage oil drilling could do," Rancho Palos Verdes City Councilwoman Jacki Bacharach said. "We didn't want him to think there was a scheme that could buy us off."

Rohrabacher (R-Lomita) has asked the U.S. Department of the Interior to draft a measure that would require the federal government to share its royalties from new offshore oil and gas production with coastal cities affected by the drilling.

He thinks the proposal could help Washington interest local communities in oil and gas development in the federal waters off California and other coastal states.

Rohrabacher wants to gauge support for the measure before deciding whether to introduce it in Congress. Though he received a letter in opposition last month from the city of Palos Verdes Estates -- and is about to receive another negative review from Rancho Palos Verdes -- Rohrabacher remains unfazed.

Once voters realize that city budgets could be bolstered significantly by oil royalties, he said Friday, they may support offshore drilling. Elected officials, he added, should be open to debate on the issue.

"No one is talking about steamrolling local cities and forcing offshore oil development on anybody," Rohrabacher said, promising he will oppose offshore drilling in the Santa Monica Bay area if it is unacceptable to coastal communities. "But there is no reason to shut off debate about what the citizens of Southern California may or may not want to accept."

Rohrabacher said his proposal needs more consideration.

"Right now we are in the initial exploration," he said. "It could be a dry hole or it could be a gusher." George Hatch
