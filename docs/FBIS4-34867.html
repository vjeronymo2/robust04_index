BFN [Text] The Jonassaint government is planning elections. The provisional government issued a press release following a ministerial council meeting on 22 June expressing its determination to make all the necessary arrangements for national elections as soon as possible and in compliance with the concerned institutions. No dates have been set for the elections or for the necessary steps to prepare for the elections. [Port-au-Prince Signal FM Radio in French at 1030 GMT on 24 June reports: "Government sources have reported that the Jonassaint government will have to summon Permanent Electoral Council members next week to discuss national elections. On 17 June, provisional President Emile Jonassaint said that holding elections is the only honorable way out of the crisis and that elections will prove democracy can come to Haiti. Attorney Mireille Durocher Bertin of the National Coalition agrees and has suggested that a team be organized to open the files necessary for holding elections in Haiti in the next few months."]
