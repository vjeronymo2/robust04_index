Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] India and the United Arab Emirates [UAE] have agreed to sign an extradition treaty to help contain terrorism. This was decided at a meeting in New Delhi today between the UAE foreign minister, Mr. Mohammad Khalifa Bin Yusuf al-Zubaidi, and the external affairs minister, Mr. Dinesh Singh. An External Affairs Ministry release later said that considerable headway has been made in this regard. Discussions at the levels of experts will be held in Abu Dhabi at an early date. The two countries also signed an agreement to control narcotics and drug trafficking by exchanging information and increasing cooperation. At the end of the three days of the joint commission meeting, the two countries also signed a cultural exchange program for 1994-96. At their meeting, both Mr. al-Zubaidi and Mr. Dinesh Singh expressed satisfaction at the recommencement of India-Pakistan dialogue. The two ministers hoped that the process will continue.
