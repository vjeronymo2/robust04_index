Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, Feb. 1, IRNA -- Iran and Kazakhstan Monday [31 January] discussed implementation of the agreements so far reached between the two countries in the field of transportation. The Iranian ambassador to Almaty, Rasul Eslami, met with Kazakh Minister of Roads and Transportation Nematjan Issin Kirim on the eve of the Kazakh official's trip to Iran. Referring to Iran's important strategic location, Issin Kirin expressed hope that mutual cooperation in the area of transportation would be further promoted. The Kazakh minister underlined the importance his country attached to cooperation between the two countries in road and railway transportation. Kazakhstan could also be connected to Turkey, Pakistan, and the Persian Gulf Arab states through Iran, he added. Issin Kirim is due to visit Iran this week at the invitation of his Iranian counterpart Akbar Torkan.
