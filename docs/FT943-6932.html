940826 FT 26 AUG 94 / Taiwan groups back Subic business zone Mr Richard Gordon, chairman of the Subic Bay Metropolitan Authority, said yesterday he had commitments from 135 Taiwanese companies to move into an industrial zone which bills itself as the largest military base conversion project in the world. A Dollars 24m (Pounds 16m) loan from Taiwan is financing the development of a 660-acre industrial estate on which the companies will be located. Eighty-five companies, about half of them foreign, have already set up businesses in Subic since the departure of the US military in 1992. Five US companies, including AT&T, Reebok and Federal Express, are the largest investors in Subic, which is being developed as a significant Asian trans-shipment freight hub. Mr Gordon was in Washington for final discussions about a World Bank loan totalling Dollars 40m for upgrading the Subic airport. Work is to begin next month and will be completed in March, so that runways can take six cargo shipments a day. The US company Westinghouse has won a Dollars 6.2m contract to supply a surveillance radar system so the airport can operate 24 hours a day. The authority has been able to move its conversion plans quickly, because of the efforts of 8,000 volunteers who protected the infrastructure and facilities left behind by the US from theft and damage. They worked for months without pay; their efforts have paid off in the creation of 11,000 jobs thus far. 'The volunteers slew the myth that the Filipino is dishonest,' Mr Gordon said. 'There were Dollars 8bn in facilities left by the Americans. Today, they are all intact.' President Ramos had set an example of unity by asking Mr Gordon, a former mayor, to stay on in his job, although he was from a different party. 'Thus, our people discovered a sense of community.'
