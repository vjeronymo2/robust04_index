Language: Urdu Article Type:CSO [News Report: ``Attempt To Smuggle Millions of Rupees Worth of Heroin Into Saudi Arabia Foiled; Ghani Mohammad, Resident of Char Sada Was Arrested; He Had Hidden 1600 Grams of Heroin in his Luggage.''] [Text] Islamabad (PPI): Customs agents at the airport foiled an attempt on Tuesday to smuggle heroin worth millions of rupees into Saudi Arabia. The accused smuggler who is a resident of Char Sada intended to fly to Riyadh (Saudi Arabia) by Saudi plane SV-724 carrying with him more than a kilogram of heroin. The accused is believed to be a member of a notorious gang of heroin smugglers and his arrest is expected to uncover many sensational facts. Reportedly, Customs superintendent Sayed Hussain Shah received a tip that a gang of smugglers was trying to smuggle a large quantity of heroin into Saudi Arabia by means of Saudi airlines. On receiving this information, Customs agents at Peshawar airport began a thorough inspection of the luggage of passengers and when [the luggage] of Ghani Mohammad son of Gul was searched, he was found to be carrying 1600 grams of heroin. The accused was arrested under the Customs Act and investigation has started.
