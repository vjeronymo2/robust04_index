Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] London, Damascus -- 'Abd-al-Wahhab Darawshah, Arab deputy in the Israeli Knesset and representative of the Arab Democratic Party, has stressed that a visit to Syria by a delegation headed by him is imminent. And that the aim is to condole Syrian President Hafiz al-Asad for the death of his son, Staff Major Basil al-Asad, who was tragically killed in an accident last month. He said that "contacts are under way to set a date for the visit and the place from which we are going to cross to Syria." Darawshah told AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT over the telephone from his office in al-Nasirah that the visit is expected to take place within the next few days. And that he prefers to cross into Syrian territory via the Golan Heights so that there would be no need to use passports. He expressed his confidence that "Syria will offer all capabilities to make this visit a success." [passage omitted] Syrian sources have not confirmed whether the delegation is going to discuss matters concerning the peace process. However, they said that it may propose some views vis-a-vis peace. As for Darawshah, he stressed that the visit is limited to offering condolences to President al-Asad, his family, and the Syrian people for Basil's death." [no opening quotemarks as published] He added: "We are not entrusted with any official tasks or with conveying any messages." He went on: "Raising any political issues depends on the host, who is the one to decide the meeting's program and contents." [passage omitted] On the makeup of the delegation, which will comprise 30 figures, Darawshah said that it will represent all Palestinian geographical, religious, literary, educational, political, and national sectors and groups in Israel." [no opening quotemarks as published] According to Darawshah, the delegation will include Knesset members 'Abd-al-Wahhab Darawshah and Talab al-Sani'. [passage omitted]
