Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, March 17 (YONHAP) -- Electronics exports surged more than 50 percent in the first two months of this year over 1993 due to better quality and a strong Japanese yen, industry sources said Thursday. Samsung Electronics Co. recorded exports of 1.35 billion U.S. dollars in January-February, up 50 percent from 900 million dollars last year. Exports of electronic equipment, including semiconductors, surged 58 percent to 630 million dollars from 400 million dollars. Goldstar Co. saw exports rise 32 percent to 500 million dollars in the two months. Exports to Asia soared 67 percent to 150 million dollars while new target areas -- the Middle East and Africa -- accounted for a 60-percent rise. Daewoo Electronics Co. enjoyed a leap of 57 percent in January-February exports to 340 million dollars. Shipments of washing machines and refrigerators skyrocketed 103 percent and 78 percent, respectively. Growth in exports to the Commonwealth of Independent States and Latin America was most evident, Daewoo officials said. Should the trend continue, Korean electronics exports may reach 30 billion dollars, up 3.5 billion dollars from the initial target of 26.5 billion dollars.
