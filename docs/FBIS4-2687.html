BFN [Text] Beijing, April 12 (XINHUA) -- Visiting Portuguese Prime Minister Anibal Cavaco Silva said here today that Portugal and China enjoy good political relations and their cooperation in economy, finance and trade has a bright future. Speaking at a press conference the prime minister said that he and Chinese leaders had discussions on bilateral ties, the Macao question as well as some international issues in a friendly atmosphere. Expressing satisfaction at the implementation of the joint declaration on Macao, Silva said that Portugal and China will continue their dialogue and do their best to resolve all problems that may crop up during Macao's transition period with a view to ensuring Macao's stability, prosperity and development and helping its residents maintain their confidence. Earlier today, Portuguese Foreign Minister Jose Durao Barroso and his Chinese counterpart Qian Qichen held talks on bilateral ties and international issues of common interest. Prime Minister Silva and his party are scheduled to leave Beijing tomorrow on a tour of southern China.
