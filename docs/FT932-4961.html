930607 FT 07 JUN 93 / Commuters face rail disruption COMMUTERS using London's Paddington station face cancellations or delays for the next two days after workers cut off power supplies to signals and damaged cables, British Rail said yesterday. The damage was caused early yesterday during routine maintenance work at Dolphin Junction near Slough, Berkshire. Almost 80,000 passengers from the West Country and Thames Valley pass through Paddington every day. The disruption will affect InterCity services from south Wales, Bristol and the West Country. Commuter routes affected are those from Oxford and the Thames Valley, Reading, Newbury and Hungerford in Berkshire.
