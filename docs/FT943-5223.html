940905 FT 05 SEP 94 / World Bond Markets: Frankfurt Since last week's Bundesbank meeting provided no new clues as to future German rates developments - the repo rate was again fixed at 4.85 per cent for the next two securities repurchase offerings - the mood in the domestic bond markets depends as usual this week on external influences. For domestic investors, the DAX stock market index, driven entirely by hopes of profits improvements, remains the focus of attention. Thursday's release of first-half west German gross domestic product data has already been widely forecast and discounted. According to some dealers, the bond market is at present characterised by an underlying negative sentiment, possibly exacerbated by post-holiday sloth among dealers. Last week's surprise rises in French banks' base rates prompted a flurry of selling, sharpened by Germany's higher than expected industrial production figures. US labour market data for August, released on Friday, stirred a brief bout of buying on the grounds that unchanged unemployment figures and lower than expected new job signings signalled less buoyant economic activity. But bund futures suffered later when US analysts claimed to have detected new grounds for fearing inflation in a slight increase in hourly wages. Today's US holiday will give German dealers a chance to make up their own minds.
