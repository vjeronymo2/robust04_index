Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Representatives of the Guadeloupe Islands who are in Cuba today visited the site of the Provincial Assembly of the People's Government in Havana. Mas Biset and Michel Basterd were received by Pedro Chavez, president of the Provincial Assembly in Havana, and by [name indistinct], director of the [words indistinct]. Cooperation agreements can be reached in the area of hydrography, especially concerning technical assistance, distribution of [water] reserves, and water treatment. Mr. Mas Biset also referred to cooperation in purchasing equipment for a home repairs project in the neighborhood of Podolutti. Concerning the results of this meeting, we can say that concrete progress has been achieved in the areas of medical services, computer science, and research in the use of solar energy in tourist installations. Mr. Basterd, who represents a union of enterprises in Guadeloupe, some 150 small- and medium-sized companies, said that upon returning to his country, in addition to speaking about the beauties of Cuba, he will have more elements for discussing its economic potential.
