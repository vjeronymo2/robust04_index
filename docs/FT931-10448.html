930209 FT 09 FEB 93 / International Company News: MacMillan Bloedel cuts losses MACMILLAN Bloedel, the Canadian forest products group, has reported a 1992 net loss of CDollars 57.7m (USDollars 48m) compared with a loss of CDollars 104.6m in 1991, and said it sees improved markets this year, Reuter reports from Vancouver. For the fourth quarter net losses narrowed to CDollars 18.9m against CDollars 59m in the year-ago period. Chief executive Mr Bob Findlay said that MacMillan has agreed sell its 50 per cent stake in UK Corrugated creating a profit of about CDollars 38m in 1993. MacMillan Bloedel said that it had turned around the operating side of its forestry business, moving from an operating loss of CDollars 117.5m in 1991 to an operating profit of CDollars 66.8m in 1992.
