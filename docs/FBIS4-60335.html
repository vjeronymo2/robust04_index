BFN [Text] President Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari has said that during his stay in the United States he will hold talks at various levels on political issues, foreign affairs, economic matters and national security. Talking to APP in London prior to his departure for the United States, he said that he will also exchange views with IMF authorities and hold talks with officials of Congress and Senate committees. The president refuted the impression that Pakistan-U.S. relations have suffered any jolt. He said that Pakistan's relations with the United States are not limited to only one dimension; rather, they are wide-ranging. The president said that some people view these relations only in the context of nuclear nonproliferation, which is not a correct approach. Pakistan has categorically said in this regard that on principle it favors restrictions on nuclear weapons, and to say that Pakistan should do this and India should not do this is not acceptable to Pakistan. The president was seen off at Heathrow Airport by a representative of the Queen of England, acting Pakistani High Commissioner (Naim Jan Naim), high commission officials, and Pakistani residents. Later, the president arrived in Rochester, beginning his private visit to the United States. He was received at the airport by Pakistani Ambassador Dr. Maliha Lodhi, senior officials of the Pakistani Embassy, and Pakistanis residing in the city. During this stay, the president will be given an honorary LLD degree by University of Rochester. The president will leave for Washington on 23 May, where he will have a very busy time and hold some important meetings.
