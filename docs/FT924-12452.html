921021 FT 21 OCT 92 / International Company News: Growth in volume helps boost GTE GTE, the largest local telephone company in the US, yesterday posted a 9 per cent advance in third-quarter earnings from continuing operations, reflecting volume growth and strong gains in the mobile-cellular business. For the three months to September 30, earnings rose to Dollars 500m, or 55 cents a share, from continuing businesses, compared with Dollars 457m, or 51 cents, a year earlier. Revenues and sales were 2 per cent higher at Dollars 4.97bn, against Dollars 4.85bn. The figures, which met analysts' expectations, do not include GTE's electrical products operations it sold in the third quarter in two separate deals valued at Dollars 1.1bn. For the first nine months, earnings from continuing operations advanced to Dollars 1.37bn, or Dollars 1.51, against Dollars 1.05bn, or Dollars 1.16, last year, when it took a net charge of Dollars 204m, or 12 cents a share, related to last year's Dollars 6bn merger with telecommunications group Contel. Stripping out last year's one-time charge, earnings per share from continuing operations rose 9 per cent in the first nine months. Revenues and sales were up at Dollars 14.85bn from Dollars 14.39bn.
