BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 9 KYODO -- Cambodia's second Prime Minister Hun Sen arrived in Japan on Monday [9 May] for an unofficial visit. Hun Sen is in Japan to attend a council meeting on Tuesday and Wednesday of the Socialist International. Organizers of the meeting said he is expected to leave on Wednesday. Hun Sen, 43, is vice chairman of the Cambodian People's Party and serves as co-premier of Cambodia's coalition government along with first Prime Minister Norodom Ranariddh of the royalist party FUNCINPEC. He is visiting Tokyo at the invitation of the Social Democratic Party and the Democratic Socialist Party. Hun Sen was last in Japan in March for the International Conference on the Reconstruction of Cambodia (ICORC). Foreign Ministry officials were unable to confirm if a meeting will be arranged between Hun Sen and Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata.
