BFN [Text] Budapest, 21 April (MTI) -- "If elected, the Socialists would choose the Alliance of Free Democrats and the Federation of Young Democrats as partners for coalition talks," Hungarian Socialist Party [MSZP] acting deputy chairman Imre Szekeres said in response to a reporter's question in Budapest on Thursday [21 April]. "Only after knowing the election results can the MSZP think about (always assuming it is even in a position to) with whom to form a coalition," he added and cited four criteria for such a decision: Compatibility of party programs and an agreement on concrete government tasks for the first six months; consensus on the new government's composition and style; agreement to conclude a social pact; and partners' ability to create a constructive relationship with the opposition.
