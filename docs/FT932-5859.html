930602 FT 02 JUN 93 / UN relief convoy attacked two dead Two Danish drivers and a local interpreter were killed, and four Danes wounded, yesterday when artillery fire hit their UN relief convoy in northern Bosnia, a UN spokesman said, Reuter reports from Geneva. The 12-truck convoy, carrying food to the northern Bosnian town of Maglaj, was unloading supplies when shells landed at the mouth of the tunnel on the road south of Maglaj, said a Geneva spokesman of the UN High Commissioner for Refugees. Meanwhile, the bodies of two Italians - reported to have been shot while delivering food aid in central Bosnia on Saturday - were found yesterday. A third Italian also thought to have been shot was still missing. In Sarajevo, two shells landed on a football match, killing at least 11 people and wounding about 100. The players thought the pitch was safe as it was surrounded by apartment buildings. One of the wounded said he was convinced the target was deliberately chosen.
