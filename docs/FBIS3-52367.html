Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Muscat, 2 Jan (WAKH) -- Sa'id Bin-Ahmad al-Shanfari, Oman's minister of petroleum and minerals, has received a letter from Malaysia's minister of law [title as received] dealing with developments in the oil market. The minister's letter, according to the OMANI NEWS AGENCY, stresses Malaysia's full support for Oman's effort to coordinate policies between OPEC and non-OPEC members to map out a new strategy to reflect the real value of the crude. The letter emphasizes that Malaysia will support the measures required to prop up oil prices, and contains an invitation for the Omani minister to visit Kuala Lumpur for a review of oil-related issues. The Omani minister, meanwhile, also received a letter from his Angolan counterpart addressing cooperation between OPEC and non-OPEC members and stressing the need for coordination to counter declining oil prices.
