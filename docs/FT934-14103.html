931019 FT 19 OCT 93 / Business and local strategy surveyed MORE action is needed to convince managers and financial institutions of the commercial benefits of corporate community involvement, a survey by Opinion Leader Research shows today. The survey results will be launched at tonight's annual meeting of Business in the Community's Per Cent Club - a group of business leaders pledged to regular support for community activities - which will be addressed by Mr John Major, the prime minister. It shows that while many companies believe community involvement can contribute to business performance through better competitive advantage, public image, employee relations and customer loyalty, opinions of its value differ among different sections of the business community. While chairmen and chief executives had a broad understanding and acceptance of the importance of the subject, says the survey, senior commercial managers had a 'less clear understanding of, and a reduced commitment to, community involvement.' There was a need for companies to involve a wider management team in community investment programmes. as a 'credible and essential part of the core business strategy.'
