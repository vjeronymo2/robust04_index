Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 11/01/1994 -- Petre Roman, the president of the Democratic Party (PD) and head of the Commission for Defense, Public Order and National Security of the Romanian Chamber of Deputies (lower house of the parliament), said on Tuesday [11 January] in a statement that the NATO summit in Brussels did not bring any surprise as the American proposal of Partnership for Peace was adopted. This result can be considered as disappointing for all the former members of the Warsaw Treaty including Romania, said Petre Roman. In the present context of the European security, Romania considers itself entitled to a larger and more assuring status with regard to its national security. Petre Roman said the Partnership for Peace represented a compromise between two recent tendencies in Europe: On one side, the direct and strong request for NATO membership by the former socialist countries as they do not feel safe because of the revival of imperialist tendencies in Russia where the democratic process is far from being a firmly established reality and, on the other side, the very insistent, even tough request of Russia that NATO extending should not take place. In front of the revival of certain hegemonic tendencies, of certain strategies of spheres of influence, Romania cannot feel secure as long as it has not found its natural place in the Western world which means its NATO membership, PD leader Petre Roman said. Petre Roman appreciated as very important the fact that the United States had warned that if militarist and expansionist tendencies continued in Russia, the NATO would proceed to its enlargement toward the countries in the Central and Eastern Europe, including Romania.
