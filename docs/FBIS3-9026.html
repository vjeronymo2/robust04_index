Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, should he visit the Czech Republic, should be treated at least like the Germans "who caused the death of 325,000 people during Nazi occupation of Czechoslovakia and are visiting the Czech Republic unpunished," former Communist Party high-ranking official Miroslav Stepan said today. Stepan, who is currently chairman of the People's Union of National and Social Salvation (LUNSZ), told journalists he considered it absurd for a "person from a friendly Slav country to come here with the handicap of being followed." He said that as LUNSZ chairman and as a Czech citizen he would watch Zhirinovskiy's views with interest and would "read between the lines." Jan Vik, spokesman of the Assembly for the Republic-Czechoslovak Republican Party (SPR-RSC) which invited Zhirinovskiy to visit the Czech Republic, said today that no matter whether the visit took place in several days or later it would be very welcomed. Zhirinovskiy is a Russian ultra-nationalist and chairman of the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) which emerged victorious in the Russian parliamentary elections recently. Stepan said that Zhirinovskiy had become a victim of an international information conspiracy. He said that a period of strengthening of pan-Slavic solidarity is approaching, so that "300 million Slavs not to find themselves in the position of nations subservient to overlord nations," the latter being the "advanced" states.
