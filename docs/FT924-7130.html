921118 FT 18 NOV 92 / Parliament and Politics: PPS posts are given to 'loyalists' FIVE Conservative back-benchers from the 1992 intake yesterday became parliamentary private secretaries - the unpaid 'eyes and ears' in the Commons for ministers. Downing Street described those promoted as 'loyalists' in an unspoken reminder that Euro-sceptics and other rebels must mend their ways if they seriously seek preferment. The five were Mr Stephen Milligan (Eastleigh), Mr George Kynoch (Kincardine and Deeside), Mr Hartley Booth (Finchley), Mr Jonathan Evans (Brecon) and Mr Michael Bates (Langbaurgh).
