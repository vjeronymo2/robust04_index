Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Official talks have been held in Ankara with a view to developing the relations between Turkey and Belgium. Belgian Foreign Minister Willy Claes who arrived in Ankara yesterday on an official visit met with Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin. Later the delegations of the two countries headed by the respective foreign ministers held official talks. In a statement after the talks, Cetin and Claes said that the political relations between the two countries were assessed. Cetin said that the talks were fruitful, and that he briefed the visiting foreign minister on the government's fight against terrorism, and its views on human rights and on the lifting of legislative immunity. Cetin added that they also discussed economic cooperation issues, Turkey's relations with the European Union and the Western European Union, and international questions. Willy Claes pointed out that besides Bosnia-Herzegovina, there are problems in the Balkans that await solutions. He stressed that Turkey and Belgium agree in their assessments of regional issues and bilateral relations. He added that Belgium believes in the necessity for Turkey's territorial integrity. President Suleyman Demirel received Claes at the Cankaya Mansion.
