920601 FT 01 JUN 92 / International Company News: Qantas merger considered THE Australian government is considering merging Qantas Airways, the state-owned international carrier, with Australian Airlines, the government's domestic airline, and might then sell part of the merged group, AP-DJ reports from Canberra. Prime Minister Mr Paul Keating said in a television interview yesterday that the proposal would be considered by the cabinet today and tomorrow. He added that the government might sell 70 per cent of the merged airline in an initial public offering, with international airlines allowed to acquire holdings. The government previously announced plans to sell all of Australian Airlines and 49 per cent of Qantas.
