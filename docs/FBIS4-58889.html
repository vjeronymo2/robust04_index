BFN [Text] Damascus, May 7 KYODO -- Japanese Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa on Saturday [7 May] asked Syrian President Hafiz Al-Asad to join multilateral talks in the Middle East peace process, Japanese officials said. During the 30-minute meeting, Kakizawa told Al-Asad that he is looking forward to a positive proposal to facilitate further Israeli steps in the peace talks, the officials said. But they did not disclose immediately how Al-Asad responded. On Friday, Syria's Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' told Kakizawa that Syria is negative about joining the multilateral talks at the present stage. Al-Asad expressed his support for Japan's bid to obtain a permanent seat in the U.N. Security Council, the officials said. Al-Asad said he backs Japan's move, adding, "I expect Japan to tackle (international) issues from a fair position," they said. Kakizawa also asked Al-Asad to pay an early visit to Japan. The Syrian president promised to make efforts to meet Japan's request, the officials said. The meeting with Al-Asad concluded Kakizawa's two-day visit to Syria on the final leg of a weeklong Middle East tour which took him to Egypt, Israel, Jordan and the Israeli-occupied territories . He will return to Tokyo on Sunday.
