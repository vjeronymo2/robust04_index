Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Zhengzhou, January 14 (XINHUA) -- Central China's Henan Province has become attractive to overseas businessmen, as the number of foreign-funded enterprises approved in 1993 surpassed the total in the previous 14 years. According to a local official, last year 1,653 foreign-funded enterprises were approved, with a promised investment of 1.55 billion dollars, up 73 percent and 77 percent over 1992, respectively. And ten projects financed with loans of foreign governments to the tune of a total of 519 million U.S. dollars started in 1993. As a result, a batch of medium and large state-owned enterprises have made great progress in their restructuring by utilizing foreign funds. For example, the newly established Jiaozuo Xinda Chemical Company, financed with 10 million U.S. dollars by a chemical industry factory in Jiaozuo City and a Hong Kong firm, has become the largest ink powder producer in the world. The official said the paid-in investment reached 376 million U.S. dollars in 1993, exceeding the total in the previous 14 years.
