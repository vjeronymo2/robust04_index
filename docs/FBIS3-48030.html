Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Kuala Lumpur, Wed -- The Cabinet has accepted Indonesia's offer of a new NAS-332 Super Puma helicopter to replace a similar aircraft used by the Royal Malaysian Air Force [RMAF] which crashed near Sungai Petani, Kedah, on December 31 last year, killing the crew of four. Defence Minister Datuk Sri Najib Tun Razak said the Cabinet at its weekly meeting today accepted the offer as it was a sign of goodwill from the Indonesian Government. [passage omitted] "However, the RMAF will evaluate the technical aspects of the Super Puma and its after sales support before taking delivery of it," Najib said. He said this to newsmen after receiving Brunei's Navy commander Colonel Pehin Datuk Paduka Kefli at his office in Jalan Padang Tembak. Earlier, Pehin met Royal Malaysian Navy chief Laksamana Madya [Rear Admiral] Datuk Sri Shariff Ishak. [passage omitted] On Pehin's visit, Najib offered to train more Brunei navy personnel here and build naval warships for them. [passage omitted]
