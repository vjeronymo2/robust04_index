Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, 12 Jan (ITAR-TASS) -- At their meeting today, Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Stanislav Shushkevich, chairman of the Belarus Supreme Soviet came to the conviction that it is necessary to draw up a new interstate Russian-Belarus treaty on friendship and cooperation, which would be in line with Russia's and Belarus' desire to build democratic states with a socially-oriented market economy, and with the historically special level of economic, political, and human relations between the two countries. This message is contained in the communique released by the Russian president's press service. A thorough analysis of the existing interstate relations was conducted at the meeting. Issues of economic cooperation were the focus of attention. Other questions of bilateral relations were also discussed, the document notes. The two sides have exchanged opinions in connection with the forthcoming visits to Moscow and Minsk by U.S. President Bill Clinton, the communique notes. It has been noted that in the process of building economic relations with the United States, it is time to go from aid programs to programs of assistance in trade and investments as the main proviso of economic development and market integration of CIS countries' economies in the world economy.
