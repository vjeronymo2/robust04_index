BFN [Text] The Mines and Energy Ministry signed an agreement with Electrobras [Brazilian Electric Power Company, Inc.] subsidiaries for the implementation of a system permitting rapid repairs of transmission lines in case of breakdowns anywhere in the country, thus preventing outages. The 37,600 km of energy transmission lines in the country will be linked to prevent outages and to permit rapid repairs in case of breakdowns in any single transmission line. Mines and Energy Minister Alexis Stepanenko on 22 June signed an agreement with Electrobras subsidiaries to put the National System for the Transmission of Electric Energy [Sistema Nacional de Transmisao de Energia Eletrica], Sintrel, into operation. According to the minister, the new system will permit reducing the cost of the energy reaching consumers: [Begin Stepanenko recording] The National System for the Transmission of Electric Energy in the near future will permit consumers to select their energy supplier and look for the most convenient or cheapest energy offer. [end recording] Minister Stepanenko also said the National System for the Transmission of Electric Energy is in accordance with an international trend seeking to rationalize and modernize energy systems.
