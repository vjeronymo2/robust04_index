BFN [Excerpts] A number of partisans from three people's organizations began a sit-in on 18 April in front of the U.S. Embassy in the Biscentenaire area. According to the authors of this action, the sit-in will continue until the embargo is lifted. The demonstrators, who intend to continue to the end, assert that they will be received on 19 April by Ambassador William Lacy Swing. They intend to obtain from the Clinton administration, through the U.S. diplomat, an end to the sanctions against Haiti. Jean-Max Blanc filed a report: [Begin Blanc recording, in Creole] The Convergence of Mass Organizations, COM; the Haitian People's Alliance [Alliance Populaire Haitienne], APH; and the Haitian Front for Democratic and Nationalist Organizations [Front Haitien des Organisations Democratiques et Nationalistes], FODNH, began a demonstration on 18 April in front of the U.S. Embassy in Port-au-Prince to protest the embargo on the country. Samuel Mirtil, APH leader; Roosevelt Frage, FODNH president; and Mondestin, Jr. for COM assert that their movement will continue until a solution is found to the embargo. According to them, this problem is ruining the Haitian people and Haiti. They assert that they are to meet with U.S. Ambassador William Lacy Swing regarding this issue. [passage omitted] Embassy officials have not reacted to the demonstration, which the demonstration's leaders say is to avoid an explosion in the country. These same leaders have criticized those among the population who are taking advantage of the sanctions to get rich or richer. [passage omitted] This is the first time Haitian citizens held a sit-in in front of the U.S. Embassy in Port-au-Prince to protest the embargo since the crisis began in Haiti when the sanctions were imposed. [end recording]
