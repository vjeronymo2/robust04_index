BFN [Report by Fabian Dubal] [Text] Cartagena de Indias, Colombia (NA) -- President Carlos Menem will participate in the Fourth Ibero-American Heads of State and Government Summit where "crucial issues of today's world" will be discussed. Menem arrived at the local airport at 1915 (2115 Argentine time) [0015 GMT 14 June]. After a brief contact with journalists, he headed for the Hilton Hotel where he will stay together with the other heads of state attending the meeting. Menem reiterated that he is willing to meet Cuban leader Fidel Castro "if he" asks for a meeting. Regarding the Cuban situation Menem said "for years Argentina respected the blockade" imposed by the United States. The Argentine president made clear, however, that Argentina "has good relations" with the island and is "constantly monitoring" the situation. "The possibilities for integration are enormous if Cuba becomes democratic," Menem stressed. The Argentine president was accompanied by Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella. Menem also mentioned the world soccer tournament and reiterated that the championship will be won by either Argentina, Germany, Brazil, Colombia, or Italy.
