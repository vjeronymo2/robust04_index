920829 FT 29 AUG 92 / Briefcase, Q&A: Avoiding double tax WHEN I last bought government stock the broker sent me a statement splitting the acquisition cost into the capital value purchased and the accrued interest in the price. The end result was that, for the accrued interest element, I had converted my capital into income and therefore had to include that as income in my tax return and on it, pay 40 per cent tax. Does it mean that I should only buy and sell gilts during the ex-dividend period if I want to avoid the double taxation I have described? It is a pity that you did not give us the precise figures and dates, because it looks as though you have paid too much tax - but we cannot be sure on the bare facts outlined. Ask your tax office for the free pamphlet 1R68 'Accrued income scheme: taxing securities on transfer.'
