931006 FT 06 OCT 93 / Plans to tax lottery proceeds attacked THE LOTTERY Promotion Company, backed by eight economists, will next week attack the government's national lottery plans. The aim is to 'improve' the Lottery Bill before it receives royal assent early next month and make sure more of the proceeds go to good causes. The company has been campaigning for the national lottery, which could be a Pounds 4bn to Pounds 5bn business, to be a charitable foundation. Mr Denis Vaughan, the company's executive director, yesterday said: 'Big business - and the Treasury - are going to make hundreds of millions profit from a lottery designed for the benefit of the country.' The company argues against the Treasury imposing a 12 per cent tax on the lottery's proceeds. 'The economists point out the only reason for a monopoly lottery is to maximise funding for good causes,' said Mr Vaughan. 'Therefore the lottery should be untaxed.'
