930730 FT 30 JUL 93 / International Company News: Placer Dome downturn PLACER DOME, one of Canada's two top gold producers, has reported lower second-quarter earnings because of a decline in gold production and lower realised prices. The June quarter's sales dipped 10 per cent to USDollars 234m, reflecting an 8 per cent fall in gold sales volume and lower realised prices for gold, copper and molybdenum. Net profit, including investment income, amounted to USDollars 21m, or 8 cents a share, down from Dollars 24m, or 10 cents, a year earlier. Gold output totalled 453,000 oz, against 505,000 oz, and the average cash cost of production was up slightly to Dollars 191 per ounce. First-half profit was USDollars 32m, or 13 cents a share, against Dollars 35m, or 15 cents, after special gains of Dollars 13m, against Dollars 7m. Sales revenues were Dollars 447m, against Dollars 507m. Gold output was 903,000 oz, a fall of 6 per cent, while cash cost of production was slightly lower.
