930326 FT 26 MAR 93 / International Company News: FEMSA may conclude negotiations with Coca-Cola soon FEMSA, Mexico's largest drinks company, could conclude negotiations with Coca-Cola over selling a minority stake in its soft-drinks division in two or three weeks, according to Mr Othon Ruiz, the company's director-general, writes Damian Fraser in Mexico City. Mr Ruiz said Coca-Cola was seeking to buy 30 to 49 per cent of the soft drinks division, which would then be spun off from Femsa and listed separately on the Mexican stock market through an initial public offering (IPO). Once an agreement is concluded the new company will be an anchor bottling operation for all Latin America, including Brazil. Femsa and Coca-Cola have reached agreement on the business plan of the new company, the nature of the joint agreement, and the franchise agreement the company would have with Coca-Cola. The only issue to be resolved was price, with the two sides 'close'. Baring Research estimates the soft drinks division is worth around Dollars 600m. Mr Ruiz said once the Coca-Cola deal was finalised he would study the possibility of similar joint venture for the beer division. There is no question, he said, that Miller is the most probable partner. 'We need to be ready in time for the North American Free Trade Agreement, which comes into force on January 1 1994.'
