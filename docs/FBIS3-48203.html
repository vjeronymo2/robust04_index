Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Jan. 18 (YONHAP) -- Former Prime Minister Chong Il-kwon died of lymph cancer at a hospital in Hawaii late Monday [17 January] (Tuesday morning in Korea). He was 77. Since he stepped down as president of the Korea Freedom League in April 1991, Chong had been undergoing cancer therapy in Washington and Hawaii. He was hospitalized late last year when his illness got worse. A graduate of the Japanese Military Academy, Chong was general commander during the Korean war (1950-53) and served as ambassador to Washington, foreign minister, prime minister (1964-1970), a two- term parliamentarian, and house speaker. He is survived by his 47-year-old wife, Pak Hye-su, and two children. His body is due to arrive in Seoul around Wednesday.
