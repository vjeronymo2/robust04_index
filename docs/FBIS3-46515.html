Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] On 22 December, Kadena town assembly members handed copies of a resolution protesting against noise caused by military training at Kadena Air Base [KAB] to officials of the same base, the prefectural government, and the Naha Defense Facilities Administration Bureau [DFAB], calling for a reduction in noise. Chairman Eiji Miyahira and eight members of the Special Committee for Base Affairs visited Ansei Higa, deputy chief of the prefectural governor's office, at the prefectural government office. They handed Higa a copy of the resolution, which calls for, among other things, restrictions on flight time and a ban on flights over residential areas in Kadena. The resolution is composed of five requests, including a ban on takeoffs and landing, and flights over residential areas between 1900 and 0700. Copies of the resolution will also be forwarded to Prime Minister Hosokawa, Foreign Minister Hata, Defense Agency Director General Aichi, Prefectural Governor Ota, the director of the DFAB, and the U.S. ambassador to Japan.
