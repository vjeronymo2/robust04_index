Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Feb. 5 KYODO -- The Japan External Trade Organization (JETRO) will start next Tuesday [8 February] providing free counters to importers in seven cities to help sell foreign products, JETRO officials said Saturday. The counters, called "JETRO Import Squares," are aimed at boosting the availability of imports outside Tokyo, where the majority of importers run shops. Importers can use the JETRO squares for a year free of charge, excluding utility costs. The squares will be provided in seven major local cities -- Sapporo, Nagoya, Sendai, Takamatsu, Fukuoka, Osaka and Hiroshima.
