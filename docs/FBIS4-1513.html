BFN [Text] Beijing, March 26 (CNS) -- Early spring drought in Beijing is seriously threatening agriculture and dwellers living in mountainous areas there. The municipal government has called for the entire city to take urgent measures to combat the drought. According to the municipal meteorological bureau, Beijing has had no soaking rain for fifteen months with an average rainfall of only 2.1 millimetres between last December until now, less than one- quarter of average rainfall in the same period in past years, making this fourth most serious drought year for the city this century. The two biggest reservoirs in Beijing, the Guangting and Miyun reservoirs have seen their water storage falling by 600 million cubic metres from that of the same period last year. Water storage in another 12 medium-scale reservoirs has also fallen by 45 million cubic metres. The city's underground water level has also dropped to a large extent. The drought is seriously threatening agriculture and the people's lives there with 2.42 million mu wheat and over 800,000 mu land area under spring sowing in urgent need of irrigation. The city's mountainous areas are now suffering insufficient water supply. The municipal government has called for the whole city to combat the drought.
