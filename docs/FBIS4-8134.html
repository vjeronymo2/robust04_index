BFN [Text] Four Romanian citizens who had been condemned in Iraq for entering that country illegally were released this morning from a prison near the Iraqi capital of Baghdad. The pardon decree was signed last night by Iraqi President Saddam Husayn, following a request by Romanian President Ion Iliescu. A press release received from the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs notes that the message addressed in this respect by the head of the Romanian state to his Iraqi counterpart was handed over yesterday to Iraqi Prime Minister Ahmad Husayn Khudayyir by Marcel Dinu, secretary of state with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and special envoy of Romania's president. The names of the four citizens are Danila Dumitru, Nicolae Aghachi, Florin Boiga, and Gabriel Encea. The formalities for bringing the four of them back to Romania with the assistance of the TAROM company are being completed by the Romanian Foreign Ministry through Romania's embassies in Baghdad and Amman. Their arrival in Bucharest is scheduled for 13 April at 1400 on a TAROM flight from Amman.
