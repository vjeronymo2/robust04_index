BFN [Report by UKRINFORM correspondent Lev Ryabchikov for TASS] [Text] Simferopol, 23 May (TASS) -- The Crimean militia has been ordered to stay in the barracks in connection with the latest developments on the peninsula, particularly the Ukrainian national guard's preparations for action. The number of the national guards in Simferopol has increased by more than five times lately to reach 2,000 men. According to the Crimean Minister of Internal Affairs Valeriy Kuznetsov, the situation in the Republic is under control and the law enforcement agencies are taking tough measures to maintain public order. Kuznetsov rejected the statement of the press service of the Ukrainian Interior Ministry, which alleged that the former Interior Ministry Aleksandr Plyuta had been unlawfully dismissed from his post and the criminal situation in the Crimea has aggravated as a result. Valeriy Kuznetsov stressed that Plyuta had tendered his resignation himself, asking not to be considered for a post in the new Crimean Government. The parliament met his request.
