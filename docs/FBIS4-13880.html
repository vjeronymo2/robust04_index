BFN [Text] Baghdad, 24 Apr (INA) -- Rolf Ekeus, head of the UN Security Council's Special Commission, arrived in Baghdad at noon today at the head of a delegation on a two-day visit to Iraq. In a statement to the IRAQI NEWS AGENCY, Rolf Ekeus said that he would resume the talks he began with Iraqi officials in New York and Baghdad in July, 1993, which resulted in tangible progress. Asked whether the visit was part of the dialogue between Iraq and the Special Commission, Ekeus said that the UN Security Council welcomed the visit, endorsed it, and asked for an early report on the results of these talks, and that the Special Commission promised to present the report to the Security Council next week. Ekeus also clarified that talks with the Iraqi side will deal with political and technical aspects. The Special Commission consists of Maurizio Zifferero, deputy chairman of the International Atomic Energy Agency; Tim Trigan [name as transliterated], spokesman for the UN Special Commission; and a number of technicians. The IRAQI NEWS AGENCY has learned that the talks between Iraq and the Special Commission will begin at the Foreign Ministry tomorrow evening.
