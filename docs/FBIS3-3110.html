Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington, March 15 (XINHUA) -- The United States announced today that it would extend for one more year its moratorium on nuclear testing when it expires in September. President Bill Clinton notified congressional leaders of the extension on Monday [14 March]. Any resumption of nuclear weapons testing would have to be approved by Congress. "The President will decide next year whether to extend the moratorium beyond September 1995," White House Spokeswoman Dee Dee Myers said. The U.S. Congress has set a September 1996 target for an end to nuclear tests.
