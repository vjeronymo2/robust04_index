October 19, 1989, Thursday, Orange County Edition

2 CAMP PENDLETON BRUSH FIRES BURN, ONE IN CLEVELAND FOREST

Two brush fires blackened at least 250 acres of Camp Pendleton Wednesday, a Marine Corps spokesman said.

The first fire was reported at 11:15 a.m. in San Mateo Canyon just south of Orange County and burned 200 acres in the northern part of the base and a portion of the Cleveland National Forest, the spokesman said.

The second fire broke out at about 4:30 p.m. and had burned about 50 acres near the Camp Horno area of Camp Pendleton close to San Clemente.

No one was injured in either blaze, and officials don't know how the fires were started. A Marine Coprs spokesman said late Wednesday night that firefighters were still battling both blazes.
