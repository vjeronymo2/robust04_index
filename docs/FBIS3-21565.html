Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS] [Text] Moscow March 2 TASS -- "Russia and Ukraine have finally begun real implementation of the tripartite Russo-U.S.-Ukrainian agreement "on conditions of the elimination of nuclear weapons in Ukraine" signed by Boris Yeltsin, Leonid Kravchuk and Bill Clinton in Moscow on January 14," the newspaper TODAY [SEGODNYA] reported with reference to "more than reliable sources." "In accordance with the agreement, 60 warheads removed with precautions from strategic missiles of the 46 missile troops division of the 43d missile army were placed on platform cars of a special letter-marked echelon and sent to Russia," the newspaper said. On Wednesday, the train loaded with the special cargo is expected to arrive at a plant located in one of closed compounds of the Ministry of Atomic Energy in the Urals, where the warheads had been once assembled and where they are currently being dismantled," the newspaper said. The newspaper described the move as the first tangible result of the Russo-U.S. partnership. "Ukraine still possesses around 2,000 nuclear warheads, but there is a hope that this problem will be finally resolved," the newspaper said.
