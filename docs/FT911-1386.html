910507 FT 07 MAY 91 / More than 1m face famine in Ethiopia MORE than a million people are facing food shortages in the inaccessible and drought-devastated eastern region of Ethiopia. A huge influx of refugees fleeing civil war in Somalia and impoverished Ethiopian refugees returning to the arid Ogaden and Harerge regions has created a new human crisis, adding another burden to the already over-stretched relief effort in the famine-endangered country. In the northern provinces of Eritrea, Tigray and Wollo, relief agencies are already trying to deliver 1m tons of food this year to an estimated 4m people at risk of starvation. But the size and the speed of the influx into the east and the logistical problems getting food there have placed additional burdens on aid agencies and pushed up the total food requirement for the country to 1.275m tonnes of food. 'Our priority is in the east at the moment,' said Mr Micheal Ellis, director of operations for the World Food Programme. 'We are desperately trying to spread what little food we have coming in but with a shortage of food there has been a serious decline in nutritional levels, especially among children in the refugee camps.' The WFP has said 240,000 tons of food aid was needed but that there were firm pledges for only half the amount. According to Mr Scott Fahia of the relief agency CARE, the problems of drought have been exacerbated by the disruption of cross-border trade with Somalia and a deterioration in the security situation. Normal problems of deteriorated roads, lack of trucks and fuel, and heavy rains have been compounded by the growing insecurity as rebels intensify their campaign to overthrow President Mengistu Haile Mariam. In the last three months three land-mine incidents have been reported and one aid agency lost a truck and a driver. 'It is going to be a major logistical operation to get food in there to keep people in a reasonable condition,' said Mr Ellis. 'Reduced access due to insecurity has made the situation worse.'
