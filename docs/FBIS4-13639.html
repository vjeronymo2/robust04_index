BFN [Editorial Report] Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 1200 GMT on 21 April reports that "the latest round of Israeli-PLO talks ended in Cairo a short while ago. The talks are slated to resume at the beginning of next week." Dr. Nabil Sha'th told correspondent Moti Amir that "several important understandings in the security and civil spheres were achieved this week," and that he expects that an agreement on the autonomy's legal powers will be achieved next week. "Brigadier General Gadi Zohar, head of the Civilian Affairs Committee in the Cairo talks, estimated that the Civil Administration would be able to transfer powers in Gaza to the Palestinians a few days after the agreement is signed." Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in English at 1500 GMT on 21 April, reporting on "progress" in the Cairo talks, says that "both sides were optimistic that an agreement could be wrapped up by next week's end. Major General Amnon Shahaq, head of the Israeli delegation to the talks, said Israel could not meet the PLO's demand for a timetable for the release of all Palestinian prisoners before an agreement is signed. He described the week's negotiations as extremely fruitful." PLO chief delegate, Nabil Sha'th, was also optimistic, and spoke to Qol Yisra'el's Moti Amir about the progress made in this week's talks, and his expectations for next week. He said the negotiations this week concluded "all the elements of the transfer of authority. So, all civilian authorities, or what's called civil authorities, including water, zoning, air transport, telecommunications, electricity, fuel and gas -- all of these issues were concluded during this week. We have been able to conclude also several remaining items of the security protocol or the security annex, but we remain with few of these issues, such as the modalities of joint patrols and crossing points, and the passage between Gaza and Jericho, and some elements that remain in the issue of visas and the renewal of visas. All of these will be, I hope, finished during next week. We have decided that next Monday we will be more ready to announce the exact date of the signing. We, in all probabilities and barring major disagreement or major events, we should be concluding everything during next week." Sha'th added: "We are going to do our best to conclude next week and sign the week after." Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 1505 GMT on 21 April cites a report by Moti Amir who points to the assessment that "a date will be set next week for the signing ceremony on the Gaza-Jericho accord which will take place in Cairo and will be attended by the prime minister, the PLO head, and President Mubarak, the main sponsor of the accord." Amir notes that "the conclusion of the negotiations on the Gaza-Jericho accord also depends on the negotiations on economic issues taking place in Paris." Also, besides the remaining dispute on the legal powers of the autonomy's authority, "the two sides are still divided on the Gaza Strip's fishing zones, the construction of a port there, and the stationing of Palestinian policemen at the border crossings in Jericho and Rafah. Handling of some of these questions will continue after the agreement is signed. "Brig. Gen. Zohar, head of the Civilian Affairs Committee, emphasized that upon signing the agreement with the Palestinians, a body will be established to coordinate matters between the Palestinian authority and the Israeli authorities." Amir adds that "the final draft of the agreement on civilian issues will be formulated in Cairo next week, except those few issues both sides decided to pass on to the two leaders who will sign the Gaza-Jericho accord." In an earlier talk with Moti Amir Maj. Gen. Shahaq stressed that about 5,000 prisoners would be released in stages, in accordance with criteria agreed upon by the sides. Still, "the release will not include Hamas [Islamic Resistance Movement] members, terrorists who murdered Jews, or terrorists who operated after the signing of the Declaration of Principles." Understandings will have to be achieved also regarding "Palestinians who are persecuted by their brethren for collaboration with Israel."
