BFN [Text] Beijing, April 28 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen met here this afternoon with Jean Matteoli, chairman of the Economic and Social Council of France. They expressed hopes that bilateral relations will experience better development. Matteoli and his party arrived here on April 26 on a goodwill visit to China at the invitation of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC). At the meeting, Matteoli said that he is deeply moved by the warm reception by the Chinese side after he arrived here, which has proved that bilateral relations are on a positive development track after the China visit by the French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur. He said that French personages from political and economic circles spoke highly of the reception of Balladur by China and they also hope to further bilateral relations. Matteoli said that his talks with Chinese leaders during the past days show that conditions are ready for the harmonious development of bilateral relations in the fields of politics, economy, culture, science and technology. Qian expressed his appreciation for the active evaluation of Sino-French relations, saying that this year is very important for their development. He recalled that he was warmly received when he visited France in January this year, adding that not long ago, Balladur's visit to China was successful. He announced that Gerard Longuet, French minister of industry, posts, telecommunications and foreign trade will soon visit China, claiming that Sino-French economic and trade relations have a new prospect. He pointed out that "we are very pleased to see that the development of Sino-French relations has returned to the time when the two countries established diplomatic relations." He noted that the tasks facing the leaders of the two countries are to carry forward the Sino-French friendly relations of cooperation initiated by General Charles de Gaulle, former Chairman Mao Zedong and former Premier Zhou Enlai.
