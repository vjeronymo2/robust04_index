930416 FT 16 APR 93 / Video cameras 'help to cut crime' THE USE of security video cameras has helped to cut crime in two towns, police said yesterday. In Airdrie, Lanarkshire, the equipment had helped to reduce crimes by more than 75 per cent in the first five months of operation. Detection rates had grown from 35.5 per cent to more than 70 per cent. And Mr Barrie Loftus, project officer for King's Lynn, Norfolk, said car thefts were down by 91 per cent and thefts from vehicles by 97 per cent.
