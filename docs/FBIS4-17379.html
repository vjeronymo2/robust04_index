BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Larisa Beloivan and Leonid Vinogradov] [Text] Vladivostok, 23 Apr -- Russia does not yet have the resources to build purification installations, but the problem of utilizing waste from nuclear submarines must be tackled immediately, said Yuriy Maslakov, the director of the Zvezda nuclear submarine repair yard, at a briefing devoted to the completion of Russo- Japanese consultations on utilizing nuclear waste. Nikolay Yegorov, Russian deputy minister of atomic energy, who attended the consultations, told journalists that there were three possible solutions to the problem: Build a purification complex on the territory of the yard itself; set up the same system in an isolated area of the Maritime Kray; or introduce advanced purification plants. As has already been reported, Russia and Japan have signed an agreement on an allocation by the Japanese side of $70 million toward converting Russia's arms enterprises. There are plans to use part of this money at the Zvezda yard.
