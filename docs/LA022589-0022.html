February 25, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

WITH RAVELING, USC BASKETBALL PROGRAM CONTINUES TO UNRAVEL

I like George Raveling. Always have. I admired his incredible success in that remote outpost, Washington State.

But when Raveling charges that criticisms of his USC program are racially motivated, well, it shows that George is losing more than just ballgames.

Within weeks of Raveling's arrival at USC, he forced three blue-chip players, two of whom are black, out of USC's basketball program. During his tenure, USC is 25-57, has finished in the cellar for two consecutive years, lost 13 consecutive games this season and is a lock for finishing in the basement for the third year in a row. Bob Boyd, coaching year after year against John Wooden, was 216-131 at USC.

One more thing. Despite John Thompson's Olympic setback, he has proved year in and year out that he can recruit and coach. But if Walt Hazzard is Raveling's idea of a great coach, he soon may join him on the sideline.

TOM LALLAS

Los Angeles
