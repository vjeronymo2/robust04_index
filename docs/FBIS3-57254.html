Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Jan 22, BNS -- The output of farm produce decreased 16 percent compared with last year, the Latvian State Statistics Committee reports. From this, plant growing dropped 5 percent and cattlebreeding declined 22 percent. The production of grain and sugar beet reduced 18 percent and 30 percent, respectively, over the last two years. The total yield of potatoes and vegetables, however, is on the rise. It increased for at least one-third because of the expanding areas sown with these crops. Farmers owned 90 percent (71 percent in 1991) potato planting areas and 95 percent (65 percent in 1991) vegetable sowing and planting areas. The total amount of meat produced in 1993 was 281,000 tons, or 76 percent, the total amount of milk was 1,219,000, or 82 percent, the total amount of eggs 387,000,000, or 65 percent, compared with 1992.
