920311 FT 11 MAR 92 / The Budget (News): Talk of a man going for broke IN THE sunlit conservatory the quiche and dry white wine beckoned, but next door 200 north-east business people were rooted to the spot for the live broadcast of Norman Lamont's budget speech. Amid the drapes and gilt of Wynyard Hall, former mansion of the coal-owning Londonderrys and now home of Sir John Hall, the MetroCentre developer and a Thatcherite miner's son, industrialists and finance specialists looked dazed by a PSBR of Pounds 28bn. 'This is called going for broke,' whispered Sir Ian Wrigglesworth, northern region CBI vice-chairman. At the mention of Uniform Business Rate the pens went into overdrive. Northern businessmen have long regretted that the original UBR plans were watered down to soften the blow down south. At 4.15 the first glass of wine was sipped. At 4.20 the afternoon's first chicken leg met an accountant's teeth. Just at this moment, Mr Karl Watkin, the outspoken Northern Businessman of the year watching the budget at his Gateshead company's headquarters, gave up on it. "There isn't anything in there for business," said Mr Watkin, chairman of Crabtree, a printing equipment maker. At Wynyard Hall the audience chuckled at Mr Lamont's 20p tax threshold pirouette. 'He's shot the Labour party's goose,' said Sir Ian.
