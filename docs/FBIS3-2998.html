Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 16 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen said here today that French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur's upcoming China visit is a major event in bilateral relations. At a press conference hosted by a spokesman for the ongoing session of China's parliament, Qian said French prime minister's visit to China in April will help push forward Sino-French relations including bilateral trade ties. Responding to a question, Qian said that in a joint communique issued on January 12 this year by the Chinese and French Governments, the French side has promised not to approve French enterprises' participatation in arming Taiwan, a province of China's. He said that a major task for his visit to France shortly after the issue of the communique is to prepare for French prime minister's scheduled visit to China.
