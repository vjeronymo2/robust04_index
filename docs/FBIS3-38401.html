Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Wuppertal (DDP/ADN) -- By noon today, about 17,000 workers in the metalworking and electrical engineering industry in North Rhine-Westphalia had joined token strikes. The regional executive of the Wuppertal branch of IG Metall (Engineering and Metalworkers' Union) said the strikes are focused on the towns of Aachen, Gevelsberg, Gummersbach, Hattingen, Leverkusen, Luedenscheid, Olpe, Remscheid, and Wuppertal. The largest rally was in Wuppertal, where 6,400 engineering workers marched through the streets to protest employers' demands for a workers' pay cut. The fourth round of negotiations ended last week without agreement on a date for their resumption.
