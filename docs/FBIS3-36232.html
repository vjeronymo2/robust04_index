Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Pavel Kuznetsov] [Text] Moscow, 3 Feb -- In the opinion of the members of the State Duma's Committee for CIS affairs and links with fellow countrymen, the Treaty of Friendship and Cooperation between Georgia and Russia, which was signed today in Tbilisi, contains a whole range of provisions destabilizing the situation in the Caucasus. This is noted in a statement from the committee made public by its chairman Konstantin Zatulin at a news conference in Moscow. The document notes: There are no grounds to trust the capability of the Georgian side to guarantee that the weapons will be handled carefully or that the treaty's stipulation on not transferring them to third parties will be strictly fulfilled.
