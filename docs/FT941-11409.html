940204 FT 04 FEB 94 / Technology (Worth Watching): Banks put IT on top of shopping list Banks in the US are spending heavily on information technology, according to a survey from Ernst and Young. According to the report, banks with assets of more than Dollars 1bn (Pounds 600m) increased their total spending on IT by 6.5 per cent to Dollars 15.3bn in 1993. This is the first time since 1990 that IT spending by the banks has been higher than the rate of inflation, according to the report. Big areas of spending include customer service centres and automatic teller machines. Ernst and Young: US, 212 773 2527.
