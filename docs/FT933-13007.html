930721 FT 21 JUL 93 / International Company News: Income from trading main factor behind rise at Chemical Bank RECORD income from trading was the main factor behind a 59 per cent rise in net income at Chemical Bank in the second quarter, to Dollars 381m, writes Richard Waters. In common with other big US banks, Chemical reported a sharp rise in income from trading in foreign exchange and other financial markets, up at Dollars 298m from Dollars 170m in the same period in 1992. The bank also recorded a 7 per cent rise in net interest income, to Dollars 1.175bn. Provisions for credit losses rose to Dollars 363m, from Dollars 345m a year ago, although provisions for the first half as a whole were down at Dollars 675m, from Dollars 720m in the corresponding period. Mr Walter Shipley, president, said that the bank had seen a 'sharp improvement in credit quality' during the quarter, with non-performing assets falling by Dollars 843m. Second quarter earnings per share were Dollars 1.39, against 83 cents in the same quarter of 1993. First half net income rose to Dollars 755m, or Dollars 2.70, from Dollars 500m, or Dollars 1.83, in the 1992 period. Chemical estimated that its Tier 1 capital ratio had risen to 7.6 per cent by the end of June, from 7.0 per cent at the end of the first quarter.
