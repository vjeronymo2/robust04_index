BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 7 KYODO -- Japan agreed Thursday [7 April] to import 7.15 million square meters of silk goods from China in fiscal 1994, the Trade Ministry said. The sum compares with 7.2 million square meters in the previous year, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) said, summing up two days of silk trade talks in Tokyo between Japan and China. The accord also calls for Japan to import 10,500 bales of Chinese-made silk thread for the year which started April 1, down from 11,500 bales in fiscal 1993. Raw silk shipments to Japan in the current year will total 19,000 bales, up from 13,500 bales, MITI said. One bale weighs 60 kilograms.
