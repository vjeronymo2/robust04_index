BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] Luis Humberto Guzman, president of the Nicaraguan Parliament, traveled today to the United States to investigate the background of a report [as heard] on property and its effects in Nicaragua. Before leaving for Washington, the head of parliament said that he will discuss the issue of Nicaragua's foreign debt, inasmuch as it is the National Assembly's duty to make some contribution to this effort. [Begin Guzman recording, in progress] ...having an effect in international financial circles, trying to obtain special treatment for the country. [end recording] Guzman did not say with whom he will meet in Washington, adding he will also discuss the issue of property and the background of a law promoted by Senators (?Martinez and Helms) concerning properties owned by U.S. citizens in several countries, including Nicaragua. [Begin Guzman recording] With regard to this measure, we want to see what the National Assembly can do to contribute to finding a solution to this problem. [end recording] Guzman said that it is urgent to solve the problem of the properties. A recent government report disclosed that 37 percent of the demands by 125 U.S. citizens with 412 properties had been resolved. Deputy Foreign Minister Jose Pallais has also said that an important aspect is that a procedure is functioning in Nicaragua and the U.S. Government has approved it. [Begin Pallais recording] This process is under way, seeking to solve all cases as soon as possible. [end recording]
