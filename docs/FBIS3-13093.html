Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [By Da'ud Kuttab] [Text] Jerusalem -- Al-QUDS has learned that in recent days the charter of the independent Palestinian citizen's rights society was approved and that Dr. Hanan 'Ashrawi was chosen as its secretary general. Sources have affirmed to AL-QUDS that the society's internal elections were held when most of its officials, some of whom had come from abroad, were present here. In addition to 'Ashrawi, Dr. Mamduh al-'Akir was chosen as deputy secretary general, Dr. As'ad 'Abd-al-Rahman as secretary, Lamis al-'Alami as treasurer, and Iyad Sarraj as deputy secretary. The society comprises 15 officials, including Professor Edward Said, poet Mahmud Darwish, judge Eugene Qatran, Dr. Nasir 'Aruri, Professor Ibrahim Abu-Lughod, Hanan Riyan al-Bakri, Fatih 'Azzam, and Anis Fawzi Qasim. The society is expected to release details of its aims and charter within the coming days.
