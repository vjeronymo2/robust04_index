Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Athens, Feb. 4, IRNA -- Iranian Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati left Athens for Istanbul Friday afternoon [4 February] after ending a brief but hectic 24-hour important working visit to Greece. Greek state-run televison channel ET-2 said Velayati's talks with Greek Government officials produced "positive results" in advancing bilateral realtions. Velayati met and discussed a wide range of issues including the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina and the Middle East with Greek Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou, Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias, and parliament President Apostolos Kaklamanis. Papoulias said his talks with Velayati were of "special significance" and "they achieved positive results in all sectors." Talking to IRNA parliament speaker Kakalmanis noted that "we had very friendly talks with the Iranian foreign minister which underscores the close ties between our two peoples and countries." "I believe that this visit and his talks in Athens will contribute in strengthening the excellent relations and cooperation," he added. Mass-selling afternoon Greek daily ELEVTHEROTIPIA said Velayati's visit "uinderlines the positive climate in Greek-Iranian relations, as well as Iran's interests in the developments in the Balkans and in promotion of Iran's relations with Europe." Greece currently holds the European Union (EU) presidency.
