941219 FT 19 DEC 94 / Workers say main worry is the sack Half of all employees feel their jobs are less secure than they were last Christmas, says a survey of workplace opinion by the white-collar union MSF. It says job security is the main concern in the workplace. The survey also shows that nearly half of respondents think that they are treated badly by their employer and stress at work is increasing. Compared with three months earlier, 36 per cent said jobs in their workplace were less secure. Significant increases in job fears were reported in electronics, health and financial services. Nearly half of sales staff, 40 per cent of health workers and one-third of managers reported jobs as being less secure. The quarterly survey said 43 per cent described the treatment of staff in their workplace as poor or very poor. Dissatisfaction with employers' treatment of workers has increased, with 19 per cent saying it has become worse since the last quarter. The survey covers 426 private and public sector workplaces employing 140,000 workers. Just over half are in manufacturing. Mr Roger Lyons, the union's general secretary, said: 'People are going into the Christmas period not knowing if they will have a job in the new year. More and more bosses seem to be using Ebeneezer Scrooge as a role model, treating their employees with complete disdain.'
