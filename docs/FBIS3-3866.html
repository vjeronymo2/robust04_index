Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [Text] Guangzhou, 14 Mar (ZHONGGUO TONGXUN SHE)--Last year, the Guangzhou City police seized 145.3 kg of heroin and over 3.9 kg of "ice" and investigated and punished over 6,500 people involving in drug trafficking and drug abuse. In reporting to the city people's congress, the Guangzhou City Public Security Bureau said that it also seized 1,425 guns of various kinds (of which 57 were designed for military use) and 3,237 bullets last year. Data has shown that, last year, more than 32,000 people in Guangzhou were involved in gambling and were punished, and more than 9,700 people were investigated for being involved in prostitution, patronizing prostitutes, procuring prostitutes, and harboring prostitution. Statistics have indicated that the total value of goods and money confiscated by the Guangzhou police in criminal cases amounts to more than 310 million yuan. A Guangzhou City Public Security Bureau report says that the social order situation in Guangzhou remains quite grim. The number of serious criminal cases is increasing steadily, the damage resulting from organized crime is increasing day by day, and some criminal gangs are becoming underworld societies. Manifestations of the "vices in seven aspects" sometimes rise and sometimes fall. Road and fire accidents occur frequently. Wang Long, deputy director of the Guangzhou City Public Security Bureau has revealed that the number of cases of missing cars is increasing day by day, and such cases have become an important part of criminal cases in Guangzhou. Last year, 965 cars and more than 6,600 motor cycles were stolen in Guangzhou. Deputy Bureau Director Wang Long said that, this year, the Guangzhou police will make great efforts to improve Guangzhou's social order and will curb the momentum in the rising number of major and serious cases, will send more policemen to patrol the streets, will arm traffic policemen so that they can take care of both traffic and social order, and will strengthen the management of people from other localities and mobilize the residents to set up "small security zones" to promote the comprehensive improvement of social order.
