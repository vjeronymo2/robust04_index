Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Outside of parliamentary business, there will be no contacts between Zhirinovskiy's party and ourselves, said Gavriil Popov, chairman of the Russian Movement of Democratic Reforms, commenting on the statement he made at the second congress of the movement about the inevitability of cooperation with the present Liberal-Democratic Party of Russia [LDPR] before it degenerates into a fascist structure. It is still premature to talk about the LDPR as a proper party, Gavriil Popov believes. The possibility that it will fall apart like Vasilyev's Pamyat cannot be ruled out.
