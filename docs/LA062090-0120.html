June 20, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Mattel Sued for Cleanup Costs: A $1.4-million lawsuit was filed against Mattel Inc. by H&H Paramount, a Los Angeles limited partnership, which claims that the toy maker "actively concealed" its use and disposal of hazardous waste at a plastic molding plant in Paramount.

The suit was filed in U.S. District Court in Los Angeles. At the time H&H bought the property in 1971, Mattel had been a tenant for one year, the suit says. It was not until after 1988 that H&H learned Mattel used the site as a plastic injection molding facility, where it had used "various dangerous, toxic and hazardous substances," according to the lawsuit. Hawthorne-based Mattel could not be reached for comment.
