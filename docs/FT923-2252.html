920918 FT 18 SEP 92 / International Company News: Fay, Richwhite's net profits in strong rise to NZDollars 14.3m FAY, Richwhite, the New Zealand investment bank, yesterday announced a strong rise in net profits to NZDollars 14.3m (USDollars 7.85m) for the 12 months to June 30 - a sharp improvement on the NZDollars 10.03m earned in the previous 15 months. The directors said the profits included a net contribution of NZDollars 2.2m from its 26 per cent holding in BNZ, the New Zealand bank it is selling to National Australia Bank. Fay, Richwhite also earned NZDollars 10.2m in equity accounted earnings from BNZ, down from NZDollars 20.1m in the previous 15 months. Fay, Richwhite's merchant banking operations contributed NZDollars 12.1m. The directors said that over the past 12 months this side had completed the sale of NZDollars 500m of mortgages purchased from the Housing Corporation. Sales and other gross operating revenues were NZDollars 105.8m compared with NZDollars 121.94m, while taxable profits came to NZDollars 14.3m against NZDollars 9.83m. No tax was payable. Profits after extraordinary items were NZDollars 24.32m compared with losses of NZDollars 20.88m. Shareholders' funds amoun-ted to NZDollars 182.49m against NZDollars 173.37m last time. The dir-ectors are recommending a fin-al dividend of 2 cents a share down from 10 cents. Net tangible assets per share emerged at 39 cents, against 37 cents. AP-DJ adds: Mr David Richwhite, joint chief executive, said: 'We look forward to completing the sale of our BNZ shares to National Australia Bank, which will enable us to retire all investment debt of NZDollars 281m and direct our attention to producing an increased level of equity earnings from merchant banking operations.'
