BFN [Text] Nouakchott, April 28 (XINHUA) -- Mauritanian President Maaouiya Ould Sid'Ahmed Taya and visiting Chinese State Councillor Chen Junsheng expressed here today their hope to enhance cooperation between the two nations in fisheries and agriculture. Chen, who is leading a Chinese delegation to pay a four-day visit to Mauritania, met with President Taya this afternoon. Chen said Mauritania is China's reliable friend, and he expressed thanks to Taya for support in international affairs such as China's return to GATT (General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade) and the issue of human rights. The Chinese state councillor, who just arrived here today, said that one of the goals of his visit was to promote all-round cooperation between the two countries, especially in agriculture and fisheries. President Taya said that the two countries have a long history of friendship and cooperation and he believed that such friendship and cooperation would be further developed. He highly appreciated China's achievements in agriculture and industry and said that his nation was willing to cooperate with China in fisheries and agriculture.
