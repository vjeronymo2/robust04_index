Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 30 Jan (MENA)--Informed diplomatic sources have stated that during their meeting in Davos, Switzerland, the Palestinian delegation led by Yasir 'Arafat and the Israeli delegation led by Shim'on Peres have agreed on 12 points that were sources of disagreement. In a statement to MENA, these sources said that among the points on which they agreed is allowing a Palestinian police presence at the crossing points between Gaza, Jericho, and Jordan, and the flying of the Palestinian flag at the crossing points that lead to Gaza and Jericho. They also agreed to jointly search goods passing through the crossing points, to establish Palestinian projects by the Dead Sea, on joint Palestinian-Israeli projects, and to set up a crossing in the al-Maghtas area [site where Jesus Christ is believed to have been baptized] and raise the Palestinian flag in the area on certain occasions. These sources also said that among the 12 points of agreement is that the security belt of Israeli settlements in the Gaza Strip should be 36 km long. They also agreed on the presence of joint Palestinian-Israeli security control on roads in Gaza and Jericho and to have joint patrols on the borders. These sources said that defining the size of Jericho's area was left to the Israeli side [words indistinct] a size of 54 kms. The sources expect the two sides to sign the points of agreement they reached in Cairo soon.
