Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "Voz Andes" newscast] [Text] Francisco Acosta, energy and mines minister, has announced that 30 companies have requested registration documents to participate in the seventh round of oil concession bidding. Acosta issued a communique stating that so far 24 companies have purchased documents for the Amazon region, and six have purchased packages for the coastal and offshore sites. There are a total of 13 drilling sites on the block, including 10 in the Amazon region. The other three are located alongside and off the Ecuadoran coastline. The concessions include strict environmental conservation requirements. National park areas and special reservations have not been included. The oil companies that are awarded drilling rights are expected to lay 7 km of [word indistinct] and drill 15 exploratory shafts. The investment is estimated at $150 million. The bidding will conclude on 31 May with the presentation and adjudication of contracts.
