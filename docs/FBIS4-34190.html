BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Venezuelan economic developments monitored through 15 June. The government has announced that the Aeropostal airline will be put on public bid once again within 90 days. If during this time it is not privatized, the company will be closed down, because currently it is producing a loss of about 20 million bolivars daily. (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 2200 GMT 7 Jun 94) Gustavo Roosen, presidential commissioner for financial reform, has reported that of the 750 billion bolivars granted as financial aid by FOGADE [Deposit Guarantee and Banking Protection Fund], only 30 percent will be recovered, because it would be unwise to sell the assets of the banks FOGADE is supporting. If the assets were to be sold, the market would be flooded with property, thereby hurting the construction sector. (Caracas Union Radio Network in Spanish 1100 GMT 9 Jun 94) Venezuelan Petroleum Inc. (PDVSA) has reported that the production of crude is growing at an annual rate of about 7 percent in 1994. PDVSA also indicated that between 1992 and 1994 annual investments of $3.5 billion have been made in this sector and that 1993 closed without a deficit. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 9 Jun 94 p E-3) Acting Finance Minister Luis Xavier Grisanti has reported that fiscal revenues for the 1994 budget have been reestimated at 1.55 trillion bolivars, representing a 322 billion increase from last year. (Caracas EL NACIONAL in Spanish 9 Jun 94 p E-3) The Finance Ministry has reported that 17.6 billion bolivars were collected as part of the new Bank Debit Tax debits in the first month of its application. The ministry also reported that the financial crisis unleashed by the Banco Latino intervention has caused uncertainly among the public, which has been reflected in the exchange rate and caused a demand for dollars that exceeds the needs of the nation's normal economic transactions. (Caracas ECONOMIA HOY in Spanish 10 Jun 94 p 8) Berend Roosen, president of the National Council of Commerce and Services, has said merchants remain very concerned about the devaluation of the bolivar and increasing interest rates. Both factors are prompting higher prices, inflation, a decline in sales, and a loss of purchasing power, thereby forcing many merchants to close their shops. (Caracas ECONOMIA HOY in Spanish 10 Jun 94 p 10) According to estimates diclosed Saturday, the recovery of petroleum prices in recent weeks will provide for additional revenues of $242 million for Venezuela. In addition, revenues in May increased by approximately $120 million. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1636 GMT 11 Jun 94) According to Inter-American Development Bank sources, Venezuela has been granted 50 loans for a total of $3.501 billion, registering disbursements of $1.694 billion to be paid out in 10 years. Part of this loan will used to construct a power station with an installed capacity of 2.160 megawatts, and in which environmental aspects have been taken into account. (Caracas EL UNIVERSAL in Spanish 11 Jun 94 p 2) An official report today indicated that between 2,500 and 3,000 small and medium-sized industries that have debts of more than $1 billion risk going bankrupt. The debts of the industries that provide jobs for approximately 100,000 people is unpayable by the debtors under the current conditions, according to a Development Ministry document. The report indicates that massive bankruptcies could be unleashed, which in turn would increase the current registered unemployment rate of 22 percent.
