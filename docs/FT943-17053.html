940701 FT 01 JUL 94 / Consumer credit demand strong Demand for consumer credit remained strong in May, in spite of the impact of tax increases, figures released today by Infolink, the credit information organisation, show. Infolink measures the number of consumer credit inquiries from finance houses and from companies offering retail, home and car loans. The number of inquiries in May in the retail sector was 9.8 per cent higher than in May last year, down from the 10.7 per cent annual increase in April. In the home loans field inquiries were up 8.3 per cent over May last year, compared with a 7.5 per cent annual rise in April.
