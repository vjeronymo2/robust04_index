931210 FT 10 DEC 93 / Drogoul jailed for 37 months Mr Christopher Drogoul, the former banker accused of funnelling around Dollars 5bn (Pounds 3.3bn) in illegal loans to help arm Iraq during the 1980s, was sentenced to 37 months' imprisonment yesterday. The former Atlanta branch manager of Italy's Banca Nazionale del Lavoro, Mr Drogoul has consistently claimed the Italian and US governments were fully aware the money was being channelled to Iraq. He claimed the BNL Atlanta branch had been 'a tool of the US government'. Mr Drogoul, 44, had pleaded guilty to two charges of filing fraudulent reports to the Federal Reserve and one of wire fraud, as part of a plea agreement under which more serious charges of bank fraud were dropped. Hehas already spent 20 months in jail, reducing his term to 17 months.
