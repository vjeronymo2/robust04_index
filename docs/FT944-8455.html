941119 FT 19 NOV 94 / Weekend Money (Companies): New issues BSkyB, the satellite broadcasting group is likely to raise around Pounds 810m, which represents 20 per cent of the enlarged share capital writes Christopher Price. The company intends to use the money to pay back debt of around Pounds 1.2bn. The offer price is expected to fall between 233p and 268p per share, giving the company a market value of between Pounds 4.0bn and 4.6bn. UK private investors are to be offered 36.4m shares out of a global share offer of 343m. US and UK institutions will be offered some 109m shares apiece, with 88m assigned to investors outside the two countries. Of the latter, 52m will be taken by Chargeurs, the French conglomerate, in order to maintain its 17.5 per cent stake in the broadcaster. BSkyB's other main shareholders, Pearson, publisher of the Financial Times, and Granada, will see their similarly-sized stakes reduced after the float. For small UK investors, a prospectus will be launched on November 25. Twelve share shops have been established at various high street banks and institutions at which interested investors can register to receive further information. Alternatively, there is an information service on 0345 400700 (see next page). Brightreasons, the owner of Pizzaland, became the latest new issue to abort. Brightreasons had planned to raise about Pounds 35m from investors in an offer that would have valued the group at between Pounds 60m and Pounds 80m. Shares in Hydro International, the maker of control systems for storm water control and sewage separation, were priced at 80p, valuing the company at about Pounds 10.5m. RAP, a distributor of industrial products such as hoses, gloves, gaskets and uniforms, is looking to raise about Pounds 4.6m in an institutional placing next month. Stega Pharmaceuticals, a biotechnology company, hopes to raise Pounds 6.7m net. It is making a subscription offer of 1.9m shares at 400p each, representing 52.8 per cent of the company's enlarged equity and valuing the company at Pounds 14.4m.
