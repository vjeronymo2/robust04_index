BFN [Excerpt] Negotiations between the delegations of the Croat republic of Herceg-Bosna and the Bosnia-Herzegovina Government have begun in Sarajevo. They are to discuss the implementation of the constitution of Bosnia-Herzegovina and the Washington agreement. Ankica Bosavljak reports: [Begin Bosavljak recording] A working meeting beteen the Croat and Bosnian Muslim sides in Bosnia-Herzegovina began in Sarajevo this morning. The meeting is intended to prepare deputies for the upcoming session of the Assembly of Bosnia-Herzegovina. The negotiating teams are headed by Kresimir Zubak, president of the presidential council of the Croat republic of Herceg-Bosna, and Bosnian Prime Minister Haris Silajdzic. The final result of today's talks should solve the dilemma about the election of the future president and vice president of the federation and the prime minister-designate of the federal government. Even if no names are made public, it will be known which nation will provide which officials. In a break at 1400, Kresimir Zubak told us that during the first part of the meeting they discussed humanitarian and economic issues, and that though the talks were successful, the more difficult, political part of the agreement was to follow. Kasim Trnka, minister of justice in the Bosnian Government, was also optimistic in his evaluation of today's talks. He expressed hope that a successful agreement will be reached.
