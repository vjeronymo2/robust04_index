Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Nicosia FILASTIN AL-THAWRAH in Arabic on 6 March publishes on page 11 what it claims to be a photocopy of a cable sent on 16 October 1993 by the chairman of the Supreme Islamic Committee in Jerusalem and the chairman of the Awqaf and Islamic Affairs Council to Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin complaining about the settlers' practices in the al-Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron. The "cable" reads: "Mr. Prime Minister and Defense Minister, Tel Aviv: "Subject: The Holy al-Ibrahimi Mosque "On the evening of Friday 8 October 1993, a number of Jewish settlers attacked six of the guards of the al-Ibrahimi Mosque and a 64-year-old worshipper during and after the call for evening prayers after a certain (Barukh) [parenthesis as published] attacked the muezzin. Incendiary chemical material was thrown on the mosque's carpets on the evening of Thursday 14 October 1993. But for the vigilance of the guards and worshippers, the worst could have happened. "These repeated daily attacks on the holy al-Ibrahimi Mosque cannot remain ignored. "Regrettably, we have never felt that the Israeli authorities have adopted any measures on the practices of these aggressive settlers. "We would like to make it clear that we did not approve in the past, do not approve now, and will not approve or condone any prayers by non-Muslims in the holy al-Ibrahimi Mosque. We hope the officials will put an end to these provocative and aggressive practices against the freedom of worship and against the Muslims' absolute right to their mosque." The cable is signed: "With due respect, "Chairman of the Awqaf and Islamic Affairs Council, "Chairman of the Supreme Islamic Committee/Jerusalem "Written on 16 October 1993"
