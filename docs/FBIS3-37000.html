Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Vitaliy Churkin, Russian deputy foreign minister and presidential special envoy, has called the last round of Geneva talks on Bosnia "the most unsuccessful" one, but the negotiating process will continue, and Russia diplomacy intends to play an active role in this, he stated in an interview with RIA. On Tuesday [15 February], the Bosnian problem will be discussed at talks in Moscow with British Prime Minister John Major, and on Friday Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev will have a meeting in Athens with his counterparts from Greece, Germany, and Belgium, and then possibly with representatives of the United States. Vitaliy Churkin himself had talks in Belgrade today with President of Serbia Slobodan Milosevic. He will have a meeting in Skopje tomorrow with President of Macedonia Kiro Gligorov, and will then leave for Sarajevo to assess the situation and talk with the Bosnian leadership.
