920206 FT 06 FEB 92 / UK Company News: Greencore counts the cost GREENCORE, the Irish sugar and foods group, yesterday spelt out in its 1990-91 annual report the high costs of the 1989 share deal affair which resulted in the resignation last september of Mr Chris Comerford, chief executive, and Mr Michael Tully, the company secretary. A IPounds 4m (Pounds 3.74m) exceptional provision was made in connection with High Court proceedings that have been initiated against the group by former executives. Further proceedings may be initiated by the group against other parties following completion of the current reviews. In addition, extraordinary charges of IPounds 1.1m were taken in respect of estimated aggregate costs and expenditure payable to various professional advisers retained by the group throughout the period.
