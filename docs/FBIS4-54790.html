BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 4/5/1994 -- Romanian President Ion Iliescu sent a message to the leader of the African National Congress (ANC) whereby he appreciated the ANC success in the first all-race elections in South Africa as "a natural result of its (ANC) long-time and consistent efforts as well as to the sacrifices assumed by its leaders in order to achieve a South-African society beneficial to all its members, irrespective of race, religion or political option." "At the same time, we appreciate the ANC victory," reads President Iliescu's message "as an expression of the confidence of a large share of the population in your political wisdom and in the ANC capacity of ensuring together with other political parties and forces a transition without violence, well-balanced, toward a society based on full equality of rights."
