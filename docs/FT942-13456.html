940425 FT 25 APR 94 / Observer in South Africa: And now, the news In the bad old days, the South African Broadcasting Corporation was utterly under the thumb of the ruling National party. Fortunately, the ANC is committed to an independent state broadcasting system. So the top three posts in the new SABC hierarchy are all occupied by ANC supporters, including Zwelakhe Sisulu, former editor of the pro-ANC newspaper The New Nation. Walter Sisulu, once of the most senior executive members of the party, is his dad. Maybe they should pin on the office walls some lines from William Blake: The iron hand crushed the tyrant's head, And became a tyrant in his stead.
