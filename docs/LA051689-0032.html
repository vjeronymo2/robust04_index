May 16, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

MUSIC REVIEWS ;

PIANIST HORACIO GUTIERREZ AT AMBASSADOR AUDITORIUM

In a light introduction to an otherwise demanding recital at Ambassador Auditorium Saturday evening, Horacio Gutierrez invited actress Mariette Hartley to join him in Satie's "Sports et divertissements" for piano and narrator. Hartley charmed and pantomimed her way through the narration (in Virgil Thomson's translation) prefacing the 20 satirical sketches, while Gutierrez gave the programmatic salon music a tongue-in-cheek seriousness.

But Gutierrez brought true seriousness and utmost integrity to his interpretation of Schumann's Fantasy in C. His approach was decidedly unromantic, allowing the work's own romanticism to speak instead through thoughtful clarity. He permitted himself few liberties with internal tempos. He pedaled for delineation and emphasis.

The final movement benefited most from a quiet and simple approach. Modulations worked their own subtle effect, imparted by a master of quiet shading without the warning flags of prior tempo changes.

The Fantasy is as demanding as it is romantic, but Gutierrez has a facile technique that leaves even the devilish coda of the second movement seeming naturally effortless. This technique served well for the agonized rumblings and frenzied nervousness of Prokofiev's 8th Sonata, which received the same attention to melodic delineation and shading that had illuminated earlier offerings. SUSAN BLISS
