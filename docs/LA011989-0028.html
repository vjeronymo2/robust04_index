January 19, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

HINTS FROM HELOISE: MAKING AN INSTANT LID FROM HOT PLASTIC WRAP

DEAR HELOISE: Last week I took some pork and beans in a glass bowl to a potluck dinner. I didn't have a lid for the bowl and I knew the beans would, no doubt, spill in my car on the way.

Then I remembered how plastic wrap clings to the sides of any glass bowl when heated in the microwave oven. I heated the beans on HIGH (100%) for a couple of minutes and the plastic wrap created a tight seal so the beans didn't make a mess.

Some of the lids on my casserole dishes aren't very tight, so this tip could also be used to make sure food doesn't spill while being transported even when it has a lid. Also, the plastic wrap seal keeps the food hot longer.

-- KELLY, Chandler, Ariz.

DEAR KEITH: There is an easy solution to this. Just mix one cup of rubbing alcohol to a quart of water and use this solution on the cloth when cleaning the freezer. Since rubbing alcohol will not freeze, the cloth will not stick.

-- HELOISE

DEAR HELOISE: My mother-in-law told me the best way to make poached eggs. First of all, spray the pan lightly with a non-stick vegetable spray, fry the eggs until the whites are barely set, then add about one tablespoon of water. Cover tightly and turn the heat down very low.

The result is a semi-poached egg that is not greasy and there is no turning needed.

-- RUTH, Honeoye, N.Y.
