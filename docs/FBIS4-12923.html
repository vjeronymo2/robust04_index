BFN [Unattributed report: "First Egyptian-Russian Summit in Five Years"] [Text] Cairo, AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT -- Cairo will in May witness the first Egyptian-Russian summit meeting in over five years. Russian President Boris Yeltsin will visit Egypt during a tour of the Middle East which will take him also to Syria, Jordan, and Israel. Reliable political sources in Cairo have told AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT that the peace process in the Middle East will be high on the agenda of the first summit meeting between Egyptian President Husni Mubarak and Russian President Yeltsin. A round of talks confined to the two leaders will be held before the two delegations join the talks. Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa and Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev will participate in the talks. The sources confirmed that the Egyptian-Russian talks will discuss in their political and economic aspects ways of reinforcing bilateral relations in the various spheres and the two countries' contribution to advancing the Arab-Israeli peace negotiations, in addition to reviewing two countries' efforts in this connection. The discussions will also include the possibility of settling some of the outstanding issues with regard to payment methods in the economic and trade exchange and agreeing on the preparation of a new trade exchange protocol guaranteeing an increase in the volume of trade exchange between them on the basis of new terms by closing the file concerning the settlement of payments related to the former Soviet Union. The sources pointed out that Yeltsin's Middle East tour reflects a Russian desire to regain the active role to achieve a peace based on justice and accentuate Russia's commitment to the need to guarantee the legitimate Arab rights to territory and peace. AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT has learned from political sources that Yeltsin and his foreign minister are expected to discuss with leaders of the states they are going to visit a number of Russian proposals and initiatives that will reflect positively on the Arab-Israeli peace negotiations. And that the Russian talks in Israel will deal primarily with settling the Arab-Israeli conflict in a comprehensive manner that is fair to all parties.
