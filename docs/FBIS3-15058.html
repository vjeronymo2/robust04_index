Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Palariya] [Text] Moscow March 1 TASS -- The Russian counter-intelligence service arrested a leading Russian official from an organisation of the military-industrial complex for spying for the British secret services. The Russian military prosecutor's office is carrying out the investigation. The arrested man is charged with treason in the form of espionage, TASS was told by a source in the Federal Counterintelligence Service on Tuesday who added that the spy had been arrested on January 15, and admitted to the charges on January 25. The service said that, according to preliminary investigation, the official had been supplied with materials for invisible writing amd means of secretly passing information to his control in the British embassy in Moscow. "On orders from the British special services and for monetary remuneration, the Russian citizen collected and passed (to a foreign power) classified information of military and economic character", the source said. The counter-intelligence service source said the spy had sold data about Russia's latest research in the field of new armaments and their technical characteristics, on financing military research and development, on the state and prospects of Russian military cooperation with other countries. Speaking to ITAR-TASS, a service spokesman, Aleksandr Mikhailov stressed that the arrest "is in no way connected with the Ames' case", but noted that while the American court has yet to prove aims' guilt, "the arrested spy was caught in the action with all the spying devices on him". A British embassy official, directly out of a working meeting with a London official in the embassy, declined to comment, saying the embassy would not normally issue comments for the press. She denied the meeting in the embassy had anything to do with the case.
