BFN [Text] Vilnius, March 31 -- Polish Minister of Environmental Protection, Natural Resources and Forestry Stanislaw Zelichowski and General Director of the Department for Environmental Protection of Lithuania Evaldas Vebra signed in Vilnius on Thursday a plan of cooperation between the two ministries for the years 1994-1995. The plan provides for cooperation in environment monitoring, environmental protection in border regions as well as control and counteracting illegal transborder transport of waste. During his two day official visit, the Polish minister was received by Lithuanian Prime Minister Adolfas Slezevicius, held talks at the Ministry of Forestry and met with the main geologist of the Republic of Lithuania. Asked during a press conference whether the two sides discussed the Ignalina nuclear power plant which posed threat to natural environment not only in Lithuania, the Polish minister said they spoke about possibilities of cooperation in all fields. Zelichowski said Poland had a modern monitoring system which would make it possible to detect any danger two hours after a break-down in the power plant. Vebra stressed the signed plan of cooperation provides for cooperation in radiation monitoring.
