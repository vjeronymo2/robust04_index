July 28, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

DEBATE OVER RAIL LINES

Offerings for sale of Southern Pacific and Santa Fe rail line rights of way is a timely opportunity that should not be missed by the Los Angeles Transportation Commission.

These rights of way can provide a nucleus for development of a modern rail mass transit system throughout the county that is critical to our area's future, moving large numbers of people efficiently, helping reduce air pollution and preventing gridlock.

The former Pacific Electric Red Car rail system utilized some of these same rights of way, and was excellent, fast and efficient and provided the needs for many commuters.

The dismantling of the rail service was initiated and completed by the shortsighted greed of special interests, which was later confirmed by congressional inquiry.

For those concerned with the noise factor, may I point out, modern rail technology offers a quieter mode of transportation than automobiles, buses, trucks and motorcycles.

JACK KRIEGER

Los Angeles
