940513 FT 13 MAY 94 / UK Company News: New auditor for Enterprise Enterprise Computer Holdings, the Berkshire-based computer services company, yesterday said it had asked KPMG Peat Marwick, its auditor, to resign over a fee dispute. The company will announce in the next few days the name of a replacement 'Big Six' firm of accountants which has agreed to carry out the audit for considerably less than KPMG's proposed fee. Mr Les McNeill, chief executive, said: 'We had a dispute over fees that became rather prolonged. I felt we were being significantly disadvantaged and it was time to part ways. We had a fiscal and a moral responsibility to our shareholders.' He said KPMG had originally agreed to an audit fee for the current financial year and then added a further 30 per cent. The firm also wanted to charge an additional Pounds 12,000 for the previous year's audit. The increases brought the proposed audit bill to about Pounds 80,000. KPMG's filing at Companies House states the reason for the resignation was: 'We have not been paid in full for agreed outstanding invoices nor have we been able to agree to audit fee for the year ended March 31 1994.' Mr McNeill said he believed the KPMG fee should have been lower, given the substantial restructuring of the company in the last few months which should have simplified the audit. He stressed that he had no criticism of the firm's skills or professionalism, but that 'right now this company watches cost, cost and cost.'
