BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jun 27, BNS -- Lithuanian President Algirdas Brazauskas' popularity rating increased by 2 percent in June after a sharp drop in May, according to the results of a public opinion poll conducted June 8-15 by the British-Lithuanian Baltic Surveys. But the survey indicates that Lithuanians continue to lose confidence in nearly all other political leaders, with the exception of the chairman of the opposition Conservative Party's board, Gediminas Vagnorius, whose rating jumped by 5 percent. Vagnorius is now ahead of Prime Minister Adolfas Slezevicius and the Conservative Party leader, Vytautas Landsbergis. Centrists Egidijus Bickauskas and Romualdas Ozolas retained second and third positions. Like earlier, the popularity lists ends with Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys, even though his popularity went up by 1 percent.
