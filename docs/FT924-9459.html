921105 FT 05 NOV 92 / Forgery used to get Welsh agency job A MAN forged the signature of a Home Office minister on headed notepaper to help him get a Pounds 40,000-a-year job with the Welsh Development Agency, Cardiff Crown Court heard yesterday. Mr Neil Smith, from Walsall, produced a glowing reference from Mr Peter Lloyd, a junior minister, when he applied for the post of marketing director. He also claimed to have held senior jobs with Laker Airways and Polly Peck, and said he had a Master of Business Administration degree from Madison Business School, Wisconsin. Mr Smith, who had served three previous sentences for deception, admitted forging and then using references and CVs to obtain pecuniary advantage and was jailed for a total of two years. Mr Martin Kelly, prosecuting, told the court: 'Mr Lloyd had no knowledge of this letter whatsoever. It is wholly false. He had no knowledge of the defendant at all.' WDA executives interviewed him on five occasions, but failed to uncover his real background as a bankrupt businessman with a recent jail term. Mr Smith spent four months in the WDA job before he was arrested. Mr Greg Bull, defending, said Mr Smith resorted to deception in his anxiety to find work after leaving prison. He added: 'It might be seen that he was a good marketing man because he sold himself to the WDA and took up employment.'
