Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Text] Israel's Chief Rabbi Baqshi Doron has decreed that the entry of Muslims to the Temple Mount should be prevented. The rabbi also ruled that Muslim tourism to the site should not be encouraged. Our correspondent Mikha'el Tuchfeld reports that the halakhic edict stated that Muslim tourists endangered the existence of the Jewish people in their country. Meanwhile, Waqf officials in Jerusalem said they would continue to bar Israelis and tourists from entering the Temple Mount. Israel Police, on the other hand, said that it was preventing the entry of Israelis and tourists out of operational considerations. Our correspondent Aviv Bushinsky reports that representatives from the police and the Jerusalem Municipality met yesterday with Waqf officials on the matter.
