Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Tomorrow in Jamaica, experts from 38 Caribbean Basin countries will begin forming an integration organization that intends to create a free-trade zone in the area. This will include Cuba, the organizers of the forum announced. The work group will discuss, in Kingston, Jamaica, the document structuring the planned association of Caribbean nations, the first of its kind and one that will include all the area's countries. The organization will bring together the nations of the Caribbean Basin or those with a Caribbean coast, to form a bloc of nations ranging from small, agriculture-exporting nations to countries such as Colombia, Mexico, and Venezuela. It will be a conglomerate of 205 million inhabitants, with a joint gross national product of $6 billion and a commercial flow of $2 billion a year.
