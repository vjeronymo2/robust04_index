BFN [Text] UN Special Representative Alioune Blondin Beye, who held a meeting with the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] leadership for more than four hours in the city of Huambo, addressed the following words to Huambo residents: [Begin Beye recording, in French fading into Portuguese translation] Dr. Savimbi, chairman of the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA], and residents of Huambo: on behalf of the team accompanying me, ambassadors of the United States, Portugal, and the Russian Federation, I thank you for welcoming us. We are trying to help solve all the difficulties you and all other Angolan populations are facing. We have been meeting in Lusaka for seven months now, trying to achieve a peaceful solution for the Angolan crisis. Quoting the words of Dr. Savimbi, we have already gone 90 percent of the way. Following the fruitful talks we held this morning, we believe there will be a way out of the current situation in Angola. We know that you have no food, that there is war in Angola, and that everyone wants peace. If you support your leaders, we believe we will soon achieve peace in the country. I thank you very much for your presence here this morning. I am aware that you have faced innumerous hardships, you have been brave, since you have been keeping us company the whole day. Thank you very much. [end recording]
