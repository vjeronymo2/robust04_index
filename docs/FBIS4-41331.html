BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent Boris Krivoshey] [Text] Moscow, 23 Jun -- The edict of President Askar Akayev, which is aimed at reducing the emigration of the Russian-speaking population from the Republic of Kirghizia [Kyrgyzstan], will undoubtedly further strengthen all-round ties between Russia and Kyrgyzstan. This was stated today at a briefing by Grigoriy Karasin, director of the Department of Information and Press of the Russian Federation Ministry of Foreign Affairs. "The measures envisaged in this edict," he noted, "meet the objective demands of an extensive part of the population of Kyrgyzstan half way, are a step in the development of democracy, boosting the main rights and freedoms of the country's citizens, they take into account the wishes expressed by the Russian side with regard to this issue, including on a summit level." The representative of Russia's foreign policy department has also expressed the hope that the drafts for the relevant laws that have been submitted to the Kyrgyz Supreme Council will be approved and the government will take effective practical measures to enforce out the president's edict.
