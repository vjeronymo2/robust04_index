BFN [Text] RIGA, May 16, BNS - The Bank of Latvia decided to finish the exchange of Latvian rubles for lats before July 1, its spokesman Edzus Vejins told BNS. The Latvian ruble was introduced in May, 1992, as a provisional legal tender. After the introduction of the national currency, the lat, on March 5, 1993, the Latvian rubles were circulated in parallel with the lats. The Bank of Latvia gradually withdrew the rubles from circulation and exchanged them for lats against the exchange rate 1:200. According to information at the central bank's disposal, residents hold around 0.2 percent of the totally issued Latvian rubles.
