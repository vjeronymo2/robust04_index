Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kunming, February 5 (XINHUA) -- China's first itaconic acid production line has been put into production in this capital of southwest China's Yunnan Province. With an annual production capacity of 2,000 tons, it is said to be the third-biggest in the world. Located in the Yunnan Tianli Bio-Fermentation Plant, the line's main equipment was designed, manufactured and installed by China, at a cost of over 60 million yuan (about seven million U.S. dollars). A high-tech product, itaconic acid is a non-saturation binary organic acid, widely used in the chemicals, pharmaceuticals, building materials, ion-exchanging and light industries. China started research into the acid in 1958, and the project was listed as a state key project of the Eighth Five-Year Plan period (1991-1995). During two years of trial production the plant produced over 300 tons of itaconic acid, which was exported to Japan, the United States, New Zealand and Australia. Sugar is the main raw material in itaconic acid, and Yunnan is one of the biggest sugar producers in China, with a total output of one million tons last year. Itaconic acid was first discovered in the United States in 1936, but because of complicated production technology, large-scale production only started, in the United States and Japan, in the early 1980s.
