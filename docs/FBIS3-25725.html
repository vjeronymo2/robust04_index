Language: Tigrinya Article Type:BFN [Text] General Muhammad Farah Aidid, the leader of a Somalian opposition group, met President Isayas Afewerki after arriving in Asmara today. The main objective of his visit to Eritrea is to discuss issues affecting Somalia and the Horn of Africa. He was met on arrival at Asmara International Airport by Foreign Minister Muhammad Sharifo and Mr. Girma Asmerom, the head of the Africa desk at the Foreign Ministry. General Farah Aidid extensively discussed with President Isayas Afewerki issues affecting Somalia and the Horn of Africa, noting that the security situation in Somalia was improving and stabilizing, and that the various political organizations were reconciling and engaging in dialogue. Having stated that the Somali people could solve their own problems without the intervention of foreign (?forces), he noted that the efforts of President Meles and other East African leaders to solve Somalia's problem were the best and most appropriate. The efforts of President Isayas and the state of Eritrea to solve Somalia's problem were highly appreciated by the Somali people. For his part, President Isayas noted that the Eritrean Government believed that Somalia's problem could only be solved by the Somalis themselves, and that it opposed foreign intervention.
