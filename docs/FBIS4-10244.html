BFN [Text] Buenos Aires, 10 Apr (EFE) -- The ruling Justicialist Party (Peronist) won in the elections held today to choose 305 legislators tasked with reforming the Constitution, according to private polls held "by word of mouth." The Great Front [Frente Grande] center-left coalition won in the federal capital and moved up to third place in Buenos Aires Province, the country's most populated district. It is reckoned that President Carlos Menem's Peronism won between 43 and 45 percent of the votes in the country's 24 districts. The Great Front won between 35-36.5 percent of the votes in the federal capital, followed by Peronism with 25.5 percent, and the Radical Civic Union (UCR) with 15 percent, according to polls conducted by Buenos Aires television stations. The voting results in the federal capital mean a hard defeat for radicalism led by former Argentine President Raul Alfonsin. Menem's Justicialist Party won 44.5 percent of the votes in Buenos Aires Province, followed by the UCR with 15.2 percent of the votes, the Great Front with 14.7 percent, and the right-wing Movement for Dignity and Independence [MODIN] with 11.7 percent.
