Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Lagos, 25 Jan (AFP)--The policy making body of the human rights group Campaign for Democracy (CD) has passed a vote of confidence in its national chairman, after he was accused of collaboration with the military. The vote of confidence was revealed in a communique issued here Tuesday [25 January] on the outcome of an emergency meeting of CD's National Coordination Council, held at the weekend in Lagos. Last Tuesday, CD secretary-general Chima Ubani accused chairman Beko Ransome-Kuti of "collaborating" with the military and "compromising" the CD, the umbrella group for pro-democracy and human rights associations in Nigeria. Ransome-Kuti has denied the charges. A two-day national convention of the CD is scheduled to begin February 4, during which issues related to the association's internal problems as well as its future approach to national matters are expected to be discussed.
