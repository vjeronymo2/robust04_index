December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

KIDS AT MOVIES (CONT.)

After reading Dr. Joseph Herman's Dec. 10 letter criticizing Barbara Marcus' desire to restrict children under the age of 5 from movie theaters, I strongly regret that he wasn't sitting next to me last Saturday evening as a 3-year-old boy in front of me talked throughout the entire funeral scene of "Steel Magnolias." Then he, too, could have had the climax scene of the film completely ruined.

As an early-childhood education teacher, I know the limits of young children. I think it's cruel for parents to expect their toddlers to sit quietly and still for two hours at a movie that holds no interest for them.

If we cannot restrict toddlers from movies, maybe the theaters could take a lesson from restaurants ("smoking" and "non-smoking") and offer seating in "talking" and "non-talking" sections.

SETH E. CUTLER

Glendale
