Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Report by Miriam Ortiz from the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] Charges leveled by a guerrilla from Arauca on the "24 Hours" newscast have confirmed that there is clandestine arms trafficking by the Venezuelan Guard to guerrillas of the ELN [Army of National Liberation] and the FARC [Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia] in the border area. On 5 January, the commander of the Army confirmed this information and said he had already given the Venezuelan authorities a list of names of Venezuelan soldiers involved in this illegal arms trade. [Begin Army Commander General Hernan Jose Guzman recording, identified by caption] The operation we carried out and the statements of a guerrilla member produced information that was turned over to the Prosecutor's Office. If you want names, I can tell you that I cannot reveal them because this case is now the responsibility of the Venezuelan authorities. [end recording] The "24 Hours" newscast, however, learned that the following Venezuelan soldiers are involved: Colonel Montes, Guard Escalante, and Inspectors Ramos and Nunez. We also learned that the Venezuelan Government is investigating certain reports that many Venezuelan soldiers and guards offer protection to Colombian guerrillas. Gen. Guzman also confirmed that there are constant exchanges between Venezuelan subversives and Colombia's ELN. [Begin Guzman recording] We know that certain guerrillas, especially those in the ELN, have crossed the border to give training on the Venezuelan side. [end recording] The Army commander added that although it is impossible to control the 2,219 km borderline, the necessary measures were implemented in coordination with the Venezuelan Army.
