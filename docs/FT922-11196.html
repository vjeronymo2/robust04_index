920427 FT 27 APR 92 / Executive pay rises attacked THE British Institute of Management has criticised executives after a survey found that directors of big businesses awarded themselves pay rises averaging 9.3 per cent last year. The institute survey, published today, found that employees received rises of about 8 per cent while middle managers were awarded about 6.3 per cent. Mr Roger Young, BIM director general, said directors of large companies should heed the example of managers. 'They must take a long hard look at their remuneration packages and ask themselves if their increases are justified.' He added: 'Now we are in recession they must be seen to be tightening their belts along with everyone else.' He warned that directors who continued to reward themselves far more than their employees would 'have to answer for it'. He said: 'The politics of envy will ensure that increases in taxation will greatly reduce their real earnings.'
