October 12, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

HINTS FROM HELOISE: HOW TO AVOID STIRRING UP TROUBLE IN THE MICROWAVE

DEAR HELOISE: I enjoy cooking with my microwave, but when a recipe says to stir, is it still necessary since the microwave has a turntable?

-- RENE, Houston

DEAR RENE: If a recipe says to stir, it is very important that you do so even though the microwave has a turntable. Stirring usually keeps dishes from cooking unevenly on the bottom and around the edges. The turntable has metal gears that may block out some of the microwaves and the food might cook unevenly.

-- HELOISE

DEAR HELOISE: My husband discovered a way to keep the contents of two-liter pop bottles from going flat after being opened. Put the cap back on loosely, then squeeze the plastic bottle until the remaining liquid comes right up to the neck or as close to it as possible. Then, while holding it there, tighten the cap.

With practically no air remaining in the bottle, the contents will retain most of their original qualities. This works except for the last few ounces.

-- MAYLO, Glendive, Mont.

DEAR HELOISE: I read in your column a hint about mixing and preparing frozen orange juice by using a potato masher to break down the frozen juice.

I know an easier way. The night before I want to serve the juice, I dump the frozen juice out of the can into my plastic container, then add the required amount of water. I place the container in the refrigerator and when I take it out in the morning, the frozen juice has thawed and I only need to stir or shake it.

-- JEAN, Concordia, Kan.

DEAR HELOISE: My daughter and I had a half-bushel of cherries to be pitted. It was taking us forever and I thought there had to be an easier way. A good thought came to me -- try a carrot peeler.

I put the end of the peeler through the top of each cherry and pulled the seed out. It really saved time and lots of trouble. Wanted to pass this along to other cherry pitters.

-- HAZEL, Henderson, Ky.

DEAR HELOISE: Coffee filters sure are versatile. I use them for so many things.

Our family is watching calories and fat intake, so I steam as many things as I can. One of our favorite steamed dishes is fish. The problem is in removing the fish, which crumbles into a zillion little pieces. What a mess.

I came up with this. Before placing the fish in the steamer, I place it in a coffee filter. When the fish is done I simply lift it out in the filter in one piece. It looks more appetizing this way too.

-- RONNIE, Scottsdale, Ariz.

DEAR HELOISE: I bought some new smoked glass pie plates, but they look smaller than my standard nine-inch pie plates.

I don't want to prepare pie filling for a nine-inch pie and find that it won't all fit into one of my new plates. How can I test them?

-- ANN, Casper, Wyo.

DEAR ANN: Our home economist suggested the following method: Pour four cups of water into the plate. If it holds it, then it is a nine-inch pie plate. A nine-inch pie plate should hold four cups of liquid exactly.

-- HELOISE

DEAR HELOISE: I just moved into an apartment and really have a problem with insects. Any tips sure will be appreciated.

-- CELIA, Colorado Springs, Colo.

DEAR CELIA: One of the most important things to do to keep insects away is to regularly clean the areas where they are most commonly found. Usually these areas are the kitchen, bathroom, basements or areas that are damp and dark.

To discourage these pests, clean under all appliances and heavy furniture. You may want to treat this area with an insecticide to deter any stubborn residents. Read all label directions on any insecticide and follow them exactly.

Don't forget to clean areas like air ducts, cracks, baseboards, floor registers, corners and other areas where insects can hide and breed. These areas can also be treated with an insecticide.

All foods should be stored properly with tight-fitting lids or they should be covered appropriately. When bringing grain products home, place the products in the oven at 130 degrees for 30 minutes or place them in the freezer at zero degrees for seven days. If the products have weevils, this procedure will keep them from spreading.

If there is a heavy infestation of unwanted insect tenants, call a professional exterminator. This is often the only way to totally rid a home of all unwanted pests.

-- HELOISE

DEAR HELOISE: When cooking pasta we all know the trick of using a drop of cooking oil in the water to keep the pot from boiling over, but here is another idea.

Spray a light film of vegetable-oil spray on the inside of the colander before you drain the pasta in it. You can now drain the pasta for as long as you want and it won't stick to the surface of the colander.

-- J .I., Washington
