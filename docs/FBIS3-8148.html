Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prishtina, February 28 (ATA) -- The building of the Academy of Arts and Sciences of Kosova, which this week was usurped and closed by the Serbian police, is given now to the Deaf-Mute People Association of Kosova, reports the Serbian means of information, referring to the installed Serbian governor of Kosova, Mr. Milosh Simovic, who says that "the academy has been tolerated enough because the decision for its abolishment has been taken since two years ago." The Academy of Arts and Sciences of Kosova has been established since sixteenth years ago according to a decision of the Kosova Assembly, during the time when Kosova was an autonomous region. "The academy is of Kosova and it will exist and act as the highest institution of science, art and culture in Kosova" is stressed in the many reactions done against the Serbian attacks over the academy. The Kosovar Academy of Helsinki, expressing his indignation to the closing of the building of the academy and appealing and asking help from the international institutions, stresses that "the usurpation of the building of the academy is a signal that shows that the Serbian authorities with premeditation want to abet the inter-ethnic conflict in Kosova, which will have unforeseen consequences not only for the Albanians of Kosova, but even for all the Balkan region.
