930505 FT 05 MAY 93 / Eddie George shuns moralising role MR EDDIE GEORGE, governor designate of the Bank of England, yesterday told a City congregation he had no intention of setting himself up as a moral 'soothsayer' for the British banking system, Gillian Tett writes. Mr George was speaking to a lunchtime meeting at St Mary le Bow on Cheapside at the invitation of the rector, Victor Stock. He said: 'I will endeavour to do my best to avoid becoming a soothsayer . . . it is terribly important that we should not pretend to be able to do more than we possibly can do.' Mr George faced tough questioning from Mr Stock on the Bank's role in the regulation of the collapsed Bank of Credit and Commerce International. He said: 'If we were to attempt to ensure that no institution failed we would have such tight regulations on all those financial institutions that they would find it extremely difficult to serve the community.'
