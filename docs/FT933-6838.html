930825 FT 25 AUG 93 / UK Company News: Acquisition helps Sunleigh to Pounds 208,000 SUNLEIGH, the USM-quoted leisure products group, lifted pre-tax profits from Pounds 11,000 to Pounds 208,000 in the first half of 1993 despite substantial losses by PJO, its last remaining industrial business, which was sold in June for Pounds 25,000. Operating profit from continuing operations rose from Pounds 77,000 to Pounds 487,000 on sales revenue ahead 39 per cent from Pounds 6.13m to Pounds 8.55m. Including Gavel - acquired in April 1992 - for a full six months in both periods the sales rise was Pounds 504,000 and operating profit advance Pounds 606,000. Earnings worked through at 0.05p (0.1p losses). Mr Robert Upsdell, chairman, said that the commitment to the organic development of the core businesses by investing in new products was 'already justified by the encouraging results.'
