Language: Azeri Article Type:BFN [Text] Afiyeddin Dzhelilov, deputy chairman of the Azerbaijan Supreme Soviet, has said that the CIS Interparliamentary Council meeting in Saint Petersburg will discuss the dispute between Armenia and Azerbaijan, in addition to other issues. Afiyeddin Dzhelilov is heading a delegation to Saint Petersburg. He disclosed at a briefing he held before he left Baku that the Interparliamentary Council will discuss the dispute between the two republics. Dzhelilov recalled that the Interparliamentary Council's previous session was briefed by the republic's delegation on the dispute and Azerbaijan's approach; and he stressed that the republic's representatives will reiterate Azerbaijan's position on the peaceful settlement of the dispute when the Council meets on 18 March. Azerbaijan's position has already been explained by the republic's leaders several times. The journalists were informed that the Interparliamentary Council's permanent committees deliberated on economic and financial issues, protection of the environment, social policies, and other matters a few days ago. They also set the agenda of the Council. Dzhelilov said that several representatives from the Azerbaijan National Assembly attended the permanent committee meetings. He also said that the republic's delegation has made plans to hold talks with the parliamentary representatives of the Russian Federation, Belarus, and Georgia.
