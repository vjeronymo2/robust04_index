December 31, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

TICKET SALES SLOW FOR FIRST COPPER BOWL

The newest postseason college football game, the Copper Bowl, is having trouble drawing attention and ticket buyers.

The Copper Bowl debuts today (5 p.m. PST) with North Carolina State playing Arizona. But the game between the 7-4 teams may be played before a half-empty house.

Only 30,000 tickets had been sold as of Saturday morning, though Arizona Stadium seats 57,000, and officials said with TBS' broadcast of the game being aired in Tucson and the chance of cold, rainy weather, even a turnout of 35,000 "would be hopeful."

North Carolina State lost its last three games.
