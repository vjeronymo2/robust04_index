Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Latvia's government refrained from a meeting with NATO officials planned to be held on January 7 to discuss the country's relations with the alliance. As Foreign Minister Georgs Andrejevs has explained to INTERFAX, the cabinet found such a discussion "premature" before January 10, when NATO will open its summit. Western leaders are to work out a common position toward the prospects of integration in the alliance of Central and East European states, the Baltic republics included. As has been reported, President Algirdas Brazauskas of Lithuania formally applied to NATO for membership on Tuesday [4 January].
