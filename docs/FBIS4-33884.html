BFN [Report by special correspondent Fabio Callejas from Cartagena de Indias; from the "Caracol's Quarter Hour" newscast; passages within quotation marks are recorded -- live] [Text] At this time, the tarmac of the Rafael Nunez Airport in Cartagena is completely full of aircraft. As the central studios have reported, the Spanish royal couple, King Carlos and Queen Sofia, arrived from Madrid aboard a Spanish Air Force flight at 1535 [2035 GMT] and at 1555 Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez arrived. At 1615 [2115 GMT] Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori arrived. Later, arriving from San Jose, Costa Rica, were Costa Rican President Jose Figueres, Salvadoran President Armando Calderon Sol, Guatemalan President Ramiro de Leon Carpio, Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina, Nicaraguan President Violeta Chamorro, Panamanian President Guillermo Endara, and Brazilian President Itamar Franco, all aboard the same Colombian Air Force [FAC] Fokker airplane. Costa Rican President Jose Maria Figueres, who was one of the first to emerge from the FAC aircraft, said: [Figueres] "We are all pleased to be here. May my first words be of solidarity with the Colombian people for last week's tragedy." Humanitarian economic development and consolidation of the Central American peace process are the main issues the presidents of these countries have outlined, upon arriving just moments ago at the Rafael Nunez International Airport. Nicaraguan President Violeta Chamorro also greets the Colombian people on Radio Caracol. [Chamorro] "Good afternoon, dear friends of Cartagena de Indias, dear journalists, and all those listening. This is Violeta Barrios de Chamorro, the president of Nicaragua." The leaders who arrived aboard the special FAC flight also expressed their feelings of sympathy for the Colombian people, following the catastrophe in Huila and Cauca Departments. Salvadoran President Armando Calderon Sol also offers his greetings: [Calderon Sol] "As president of El Salvador, it is a real pleasure to be in this generous nation, Colombia, which opens its arms to welcome all of the Ibero-American leaders. We all believe such summit meetings are very beneficial for our people." Honduran President Carlos Roberto Reina briefly addressed the Colombian people. [Reina] "I offer a fraternal salute to the Colombian people and express my sympathy for the trying moments you are experiencing." The Group of Three [G-3] nations will sign the G-3 trade agreement at 1930 at the guest house for illustrious visitors, here in Cartagena de Indias.
