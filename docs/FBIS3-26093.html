Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 2 (XINHUA) -- With the approval of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China (CPC), two books on veteran proletarian revolutionary Deng Yingchao (1904-1992) have been published here in commemoration of the 90th anniversary of her birth. The two books -- "The Collected Works of Deng Yingchao" and "In Memory of Sister Deng" -- were compiled by the Party Literature Research Center of the CPC Central Committee and published by the People's Publishing House. The 300,000-word "Collected Works of Deng Yingchao" contains 89 articles, letters, reports and records of talks she gave in the period 1938-1992. Deng took part in the May 4th movement of 1919 (an anti-imperialist, anti-feudal, political and cultural movement influenced by the Russian October revolution of 1917). She also served as chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference. "In Memory of Sister Deng" has 72 articles written by her colleagues, friends, relatives and people working around her. Chinese party and state leaders Jiang Zemin and Li Peng, as well as Chen Yun, Bo Yibo and Chen Muhua wrote inscriptions for the book.
