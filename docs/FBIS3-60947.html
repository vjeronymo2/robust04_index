Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Cairo, 5 Jan (MENA)--Minister of Tourism Dr. Mamduh al-Biltaji has expressed optimism about the future of tourism in Egypt. [passage omitted] At a meeting with a visiting U.S. Congressional delegation today, he reviewed the Tourism Ministry's future policy to revive tourism in Egypt, especially from the United States as an important tourism export market. Speaking about Egypt's losses due to terrorism, he said Egypt has incurred losses at all levels. He added that these incidents have sharply and directly deepened the tourism crisis in Egypt. He noted that the number of tourists decreased from 3.2 million in 1992 to 2.2 million as of October in 1993. Tourist nights decreased from about 20 million to around 13 million as of October 1993 and tourism revenues dropped from $2.3 billion in 1992 to $1.1 billion as of October 1993. He pointed out that these statistics are serious indications as to the severity of the crisis. [passage omitted]
