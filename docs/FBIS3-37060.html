Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Valentin Sergeyev, the head of the government press service, has described information saying that a group of economists, including Shatalin, Abalkin, Saburov, and Lvov, are working on alternative programs for restructuring the economy on the orders of the government as not corresponding to reality. The course of reforms and the plan of measures aimed at implementing them was adopted in August last year at a sitting of the Council of Ministers and endorsed by the president. This course remains unchanged, the statement says. A working group has been set up by the government to draw up a plan of urgent and high priority measures and actions for this year. It is headed by Sergey Vasilyev, deputy economics minister. It examines every proposal submitted by scientists and industrialists irrespective of their stance, the statement by Sergeyev notes. The group will submit a corresponding document to the head of the government having taken into account the proposals and a message from the Russian president to the Federal Assembly, which is to be made public on 18 February.
