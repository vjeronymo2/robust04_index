940201 FT 01 FEB 94 / New Glyndebourne Opera House unveiled The new Glyndebourne Opera House was unveiled yesterday amid claims that it is the first professional opera house built in the UK since Glyndebourne opened 60 years ago. The Pounds 33.5m auditorium, designed by architects Michael Hopkins & Partners, and constructed in less than 18 months. It occupies the site of the old theatre in Sussex. The new theatre on four levels has a total capacity of 1,200, with 370 more seats than the old one. The money was raised from corporate and private sources. The 1994 summer festival opens on May 28th with a performance of Mozart's Le nozze di Figaro. The top seat price has been raised from Pounds 90 to Pounds 100 this season but for the first time there will be seats at Pounds 30 and Pounds 15, plus the introduction of 42 standing places at Pounds 10 each, sold on the day of the performance. It is expected that the seats available to the general public will rise to almost 25 per cent of capacity.
