931006 FT 06 OCT 93 / Crisis in Russia: Russians cool to state sales FOUR fifths of Russians do not expect privatisation to make any difference to their standard of living, according to the findings of a survey from the Centre for the Study of Public Policy which presents some ominous findings for western reformers pushing for faster economic change in Russia. The survey, which questioned nearly 2,000 Russians across the country this summer, says most think privatisation is having a bad effect on the economy overall. Only half of those questioned saw the connection between privatisation and more goods in the shops. Although a majority of Russians have already laid plans to invest the privatisation vouchers issued to them, they remain uncertain about the implications of privatisation for their work place and how it might affect their economic behaviour. Professor Richard Rose of Strathclyde University, author of the study, says: 'The idea of privatisation appears to have penetrated the consciousness of the great majority of Russians in the way that a 'free gift offer' might. . . . Most Russians appear to be participating in privatisation without yet feeling confident that they know what difference it will make.'
