Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] The Cuban Government and Rio de Janeiro State University (UERJ) have signed an agreement to develop joint technological projects in the areas of bioceramics, industrial automation, oil technology, and informatics. The result of the programs will then be commercialized and the profits divided among the participants. The initiative to sign the agreement came from the Brazilians. The negotiations began last February with the visit of UERJ Rector Hesio Cordeiro and Paulo Jorge Paes Leme, director of the Polytechnic Institute, to Havana. Cuban Minister of Higher Education Fernando Vecino Alegret was in Rio de Janeiro yesterday to coordinate with Hesio Cordeiro the details for signing the agreement. Cordeiro will return to Cuba on 20 March to sign the final terms of the agreement.
