April 6, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

THE BUTCHER: BASIC MEAT SAUCE: NOW YOU CAN MAKE YOUR OWN CONVENIENCE FOODS

In my opinion we spend an unnecessary fortune on foods we perceive to be convenient.

In the market where I do much of my shopping, the three-tiered self-service meat case and the frozen food case are on either side of the same aisle. It is easy and -- for the seriously frugal shopper -- almost impossible not to compare the prices of frozen convenience entrees with the price of the fresh meat from which they are made.

On a recent trip to the market to pick up lean ground beef for 99 cents a pound, I stepped across the aisle to check on the prices of the stuff I would make with it: chili con carne, 8 3/4-ounce single serving, $1.95; spaghetti with meat sauce, 12 7/8-ounce single serving, $2.25; lasagna, 10 1/2-ounce single serving, $2.25. In my opinion, those prices border on the ridiculous when compared to my ground-beef buy.

What follows is a recipe for a simple Basic Meat Sauce that can be prepared ahead -- frozen in appropriate-sized portions and used to make all kinds of delicious dishes quickly. It's like making your own convenience foods. It takes a bit of time, but considering the savings to be made, it's well worth the time spent.

BASIC MEAT SAUCE

6 or 8 onions, chopped

3 or 4 carrots, chopped

1/4 cup olive or salad oil

2 or 3 tablespoons parsley or 1 fresh bunch, chopped

5 pounds lean ground beef

1 (15-ounce) can tomato puree

1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce

1 1/2 cups strong chicken stock (if stock is weak, add 1 to 2 chicken bouillon cubes)

Salt, pepper

Cook onions and carrots in oil in 8-quart stock pot until tender, but not browned. Stir in parsley and cook until tender. Remove vegetables from pan to large bowl and set aside.

Brown ground beef in same pot in 1 1/2-pound batches. As each batch is cooked, add to vegetables.

After all beef is browned and removed, add tomato puree, tomato sauce and chicken broth and stir to loosen bits on bottom of pot. Return beef and vegetables to pot and simmer, uncovered, about 30 minutes. Allow sauce to cool, package into 2- or 3-cup packages and freeze. Makes about 2 1/2 quarts.

Now what are you going to do with all that stuff? Let me suggest a few ideas to get you started.

CHILI

2 (15-ounce) cans pinto or kidney beans, undrained

3 cups Basic Meat Sauce

1 (6-ounce) can tomato paste

3 tablespoons chili powder, or 2 tablespoons chile powder and 1 tablespoon ground, dried California chile powder

1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds

Chopped onions

Shredded longhorn Cheddar cheese

Tortilla chips

Shredded lettuce

Sliced avocado or thawed frozen guacamole

Sour cream

Lime wedges

Combine pinto beans, Basic Meat Sauce, tomato paste, chili powder and cumin seeds. Bring to boil, then simmer, stirring as needed to prevent sticking, until reduced to about 6 cups, or thickness desired.

Serve in bowls topped with choice of chopped onions, cheese, tortilla chips, lettuce, avocado, sour cream and freshly squeezed lime wedges. Makes 4 or 5 servings.

Variation: Spoon chili and other ingredients into soft, warm corn tortillas.

Note: Serve with beer, sangria or orange juice.

CHICKEN CACCIATORE

4 whole chicken legs with thighs

2 tablespoons oil

2 cups Basic Meat Sauce

1 (1-pound) can stewed tomatoes, undrained

2 green peppers, cut into large chunks

1/2 cup pitted black olives

Sour cream

Frozen gnocchi, shell pasta or baked potatoes

Brown chicken legs well in skillet with oil over medium heat. Add Basic Meat Sauce and stewed tomatoes. Cover and simmer 30 minutes.

Uncover and add green peppers and olives, pushing down well into sauce. Simmer additional 10 minutes.

Skim fat from pan juices and serve chicken with sour cream and hot, boiled and drained frozen gnocchi. Makes 4 servings.

RAVIOLI

3 cups Basic Meat Sauce

2 tablespoons flour

1 cup beef or chicken broth

1 cup whipping cream

Dash ground nutmeg

1 (12- to 14-ounce) package fresh or frozen, thawed ravioli

Grated Parmesan cheese

Bring Basic Meat Sauce to simmer in wide skillet. Blend flour to smooth paste with small amount broth. Stir broth and flour paste into meat sauce and cook, stirring, until slightly thickened. Add whipping cream and nutmeg and bring to boiling, stirring.

Meanwhile, cook ravioli according to package directions in large amount of boiling water, or until ravioli pasta is tender to bite. Drain ravioli and mix with meat sauce. Serve topped with grated Parmesan cheese. Makes 4 to 6 servings.

SPANISH-STYLE MEAT AND RICE

1 cup short grain rice

1 tablespoon olive oil

3 to 4 cups Basic Meat Sauce

1 tablespoon paprika

2 cups water

2 chicken or beef bouillon cubes or 2 teaspoons powdered stock base

1 (10-ounce) package frozen peas

Saute rice in olive oil in wide skillet, stirring until rice turns opaque. Stir in Basic Meat Sauce, paprika, water and bouillon cubes. Bring to boil and simmer about 15 minutes, covered, or until rice is tender.

Pound peas against hard surface to loosen, then stir into meat mixture. Serve mixture when peas are thawed and hot. Makes 5 to 6 servings.

Note: Accompany with sliced cucumbers in cream dressing.
