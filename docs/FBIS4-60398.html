BFN [Excerpts] Tunis, 16 May (KUNA) -- The Embassy of the State of Kuwait in Tunis today denied that there are Algerian good offices for what is described as clearing the air between Kuwait and the Iraqi regime, on the occasion of a tour to the recent Maghreb countries by Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad, first deputy prime minister and foreign minister of Kuwait. In a statement, a copy of which was received by KUNA, the Embassy expresses "Kuwait's rejection of the attempt to link Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad's tour and the message which the head of the Iraqi regime sent to the Algerian president." [passage omitted] The Embassy statement affirms that relations between the State of Kuwait and the Iraqi regime are ruled by international legal resolutions which Iraq must observe, instead of making open attempts to contain them and evade implementing them.
