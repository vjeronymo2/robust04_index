Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 22 February (MTI) -- Hungarian President Arpad Goncz met with Russian Foreign Minister Andrei Kozyrev in the Parliament today. Presidential spokesman Andras Farago said the president congratulated Kozyrev on the joint Russian-Western success in dealing with the the situation in Sarajevo. In Hungary's view, NATO's Partnership for Peace is not an empty initiative but a practicable scheme giving attention to the human, legal, and social factors of security, in addition to its military side. Kozyrev conveyed greetings from President Boris Yeltsin to Goncz. He assured Goncz that Moscow always examines how its foreign policy moves contribute to Central-European stability. He said Moscow supports cooperation and peace partnership with NATO, but stressed the need to find concrete solutions to individual problems in the region. Russia also attaches great importance to the CSCE and its summit, to be held in Budapest at the end of the year.
