October 25, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

WHO'S HOT

* Jordan quarterback Johnny Nanson, who passed for more than 200 yards and two touchdowns in a 33-13 victory over Millikan.

* Paramount, which improved to 7-0 with a 28-17 victory over Lynwood.

The Pirates are ranked second in Division II.

* Poly, which won its fourth consecutive game, 21-7, over Wilson. It was the Jackrabbits' 14th win in a row over the Bruins.

* El Rancho, which beat Pioneer, 35-13, to improve to 4-0 in Whitmont League.

* Glenn, which defeated previously unbeaten La Mirada, 13-7, to move into first place in the Suburban League.

Who's Not

* Centennial, which lost, 49-12, to West Torrance for its 21st consecutive defeat. The Apaches are 0-7 this season.

* Bellflower, which had its losing streak extended to four games in a 37-7 loss to Artesia.

* Pius X, which lost its 26th consecutive game, 27-8, to St. Anthony.

* Whittier, which lost, 9-6, to Santa Fe, a team that had not won this season. The Cards dropped to 1-3 in the Whitmont League.

* Warren, which lost to cross-town rival Downey for the first time in five years, 12-0. The Bears dropped to 1-6.
