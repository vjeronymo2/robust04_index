930429 FT 29 APR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Spending on Aids outlined THE GOVERNMENT has spent nearly Pounds 900m on tackling Aids and HIV during the past seven years, equivalent to almost Pounds 40,000 for each case reported. Responding to written questions, Mr Thomas Sackville, health minister, said this total included money made available for medical treatment and care, training of health and social workers, infection control, blood supply protection and other purposes. Since 1986, 7,083 cases of Aids and 16,164 cases of HIV had been reported.
