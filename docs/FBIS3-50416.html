Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN ["ip"-attributed report: "Moderate Growth of Prices; Lasting Decline of Production"] [Excerpts] Bratislava -- The price growth slowed in the last three months of 1993, Rudolf Krc, chairman of the Slovak Statistical Office, told a news conference yesterday. Whereas in October prices rose 1.4 percent [compared with the preceding month], in November they rose 1 percent, and in December 0.6 percent. [passage omitted] Goods worth 314.4 billion korunas [Sk] were produced in industry in the first 11 months of the year, 13.9 percent less than in the same period the year before. The volume of building work reached the value of Sk39 billion, which was 23.4 percent less than in the first 11 months of 1992. The Slovak Statistical Office has also released some data for December 1993. In livestock production, the numbers of cattle, pigs, and poultry continued to decline. Milk production reached 1.2144 billion liters, 8.7 less than in December 1992, while the production of eggs declined more than 11 percent. Average industrial monthly wages in enterprises with 25 and more employees reached Sk6,942 in November, which was Sk1,034 (17.5 percent) more than in the same month the year before. Average monthly wages in construction stood at Sk6,573, in transportation at Sk6,206, and in retailing at Sk5,126. A month-to-month comparison shows a slight increase in the productivity of labor. A total of 368,095 job applicants were registered with labor offices at the end of the year. The number of unemployed increased 0.4 percent in the course of December (to 14.4 percent). An annual comparison shows an increase in employment by 4 percent.
