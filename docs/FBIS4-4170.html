BFN [Text] Phnom Penh, April 28 (XINHUA) -- Military conflict between Cambodian Government troops and Khmer Rouge guerrillas is spreading from Pailin to Poipet, a senior officer of the Defense Ministry said here today. He said that government troops were fighting Khmer Rouge guerrillas in Poipet, an important Cambodian-thai border town in the west of Battambang Province, in the past few days. The Khmer Rouge started an offensive against the government troops on April 26, he added, but disclosing no details. A representative of the Khmer Rouge said here yesterday that after the recapture of Pailin on April 19, their guerrillas have pushed the fighting toward an area about 30 kilometers from Battambang. Broadcasts of Khmer Rouge radio monitored here today also said that the guerrillas took over the headquarters of the 4th division of the government troops in Treng, along road No. 10 yesterday morning. There is no signs of a cease-fire from the two warring sides, although all parties have responded positively to the King Norodom Sihanouk in favor of a roundtable meeting on peace and national reconciliation, scheduled for May 2-7 in Phnom Penh.
