Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By reporter Wang Wencheng (3769 2429 2052)] [Text] Yamassoukro, 7 Feb (XINHUA) -- The funeral of the late former Ivorian President Felix Houphouet-Biogny, who died of illness on 7 December last year, was ceremoniously held in the Ivorian political capital of Yamassoukro today. The funeral rites were held in a combination of the traditional customs of the Baoule people and the ceremony of a modern state funeral. In the morning, the "Biogny Peace Foundation Building" was filled with solemn silence, with Biogny's bronze coffin well placed in the middle of the mourning hall where his portrait was hung. At 1100, the cortege left the "Peace Foundation Building" for the "Ave Maria Peace Cathedral" for a memorial mass. The remains were later buried at the Biogny Tomb within the compound of the Presidential Residence. Leaders and special envoys from over 70 countries, and representatives of nearly 40 international and regional organizations, attended Biogny's funeral. Chinese President Jiang Zemin sent a condolence cable. Liu Chengguo, vice agriculture minister and special envoy of the Chinese Government, was all the way here to attend Biogny's funeral. Born to the family of a tribal chief in Yamassoukro on 18 October 1905, Biogny practiced medicine in his early days and was president of the Ivory Coast since its independence on 7 August 1960. He was a pioneer of the African nationality independence movements and an influential senior statesman.
