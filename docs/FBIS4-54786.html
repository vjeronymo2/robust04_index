BFN [Text] Warsaw, May 5 -- President Lech Walesa received on Thursday U.S. Secretary of Commerce Ronald Brown in Warsaw for the East-West Economic Forum. Greeting the guest Walesa said that Poland is trying to reform its economy. The process is difficult and the road from communism to capitalism is long, but there is no danger that Poland will veer from this direction. Walesa also said that he was counting on the fact that the United States will monitor the equality of partners. We do not want any special treatment, we want to be treated as partners emphasised Walesa. Brown said that the dialogue between the east and west is of key importance to peace and development in the world. The United States is very supportive of the changes in Poland, said Brown adding that the U.S. and Poland are linked very closely and are co-dependent.
