November 28, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

RUELAS WINS STATE FEATHERWEIGHT TITLE

Rafael Ruelas of Arleta won the state featherweight championship Tuesday night by stopping Richard Abila of Los Angeles in the seventh round of a scheduled 12-round fight at the Country Club in Reseda.

Ruelas, 19, ran his record to 22-0 with his 19th knockout.

It was the first time he fought for a title.

Ruelas knocked Abila down midway into the seventh round with a straight left and the referee stopped the fight. Ruelas dominated the fight, winning each round easily as Abila (16-7) threw few punches and didn't land many.

Earlier, former middleweight contender Alex Ramos, a New Yorker living in Simi Valley, weathered a severe beating early in the fifth round and stopped Roberto Rosiles at the end of the round.

It was Ramos' second victory in two fights since a two-year layoff. A member of the 1980 U.S. Olympic team, he spent more than 20 months in state prison on an assault conviction.

He was taking a pounding in the fifth when he landed a right under Rosiles' left ear and followed it with a right to the body.

Rosiles went down from the second punch. After the bell, his handlers asked the ringside doctor to stop the fight.

Ramos is 30-8-2. Rosiles, of Mexicali, Mexico, is 21-12-1.

In another preliminary bout, heavyweight Rocky Pepeli of Burbank, knocked down by a left hook to the chin in the second round, came back to stop William Morris of Ontario in the third round.
