BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Aleksandrova] [Text] Moscow, 3 Jun (ITAR-TASS) -- The question of signing an agreement between Russia and the European Union [EU] on partnership and cooperation was a focal point at a meeting between Russian President Boris Yeltsin and Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias in Moscow today. At the start of the meeting the Russian president expressed amazement that "Russia occupies half of Europe and is still not in Europe." The Greek minister stressed that Greece, which is the current chairman in the EU's leading bodies, is among those countries which actively support the idea of close ties between Russia and the EU. Karolos Papoulias invited Boris Yeltsin to the Greek island of Kerkira [Corfu] 24-25 June for a meeting between the leaders of the EU countries to conclude an agreement between Russia and the EU. He gave an assurance that everything is ready for the signing of this important document.
