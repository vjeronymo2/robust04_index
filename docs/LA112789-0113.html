November 27, 1989, Monday, P.M. Final

P.M. BRIEFING;

JAKE BUTCHER GUILTY OF FRAUD

Former Tennessee gubernatorial candidate Jake Butcher pleaded guilty in federal court today to charges that he defrauded a Florida bank.

U.S. District Judge G. Kendall Sharp accepted the plea, part of a plea bargain with the prosecution, and scheduled a sentencing hearing for March 21, an official in his office said.

Under the terms of the plea bargain, the former banker admitted to two counts of defrauding the Florida Center Bank of Orlando and conspiracy charges against him were dropped, said Edward Garland, Butcher's attorney.

Butcher admitted using proceeds from a bank loan for purposes other than those stipulated by the loan agreement, Garland said. He was indicted on 19 counts of fraud by an Orlando grand jury Aug. 30.

The indictment charged that Butcher and several others defrauded the bank of about $3 million.
