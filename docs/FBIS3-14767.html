Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Lyudmila Yermakova] [Text] Moscow February 25 TASS -- The Russian Federation Council, upper house of the Russian Parliament, approved dispatching an additional military contingent, numbering 300, to join U.N. peace-keeping forces deployed in former Yugoslavia to ensure control over the unconditional observance by the warring sides of a ceasefire agreement in the area of Sarajevo. This problem was discussed by the upper chamber of the Russian Parliament in connection with the Russian president's address to the nation in response to the U.N. secretary general's appeal asking Russia to send an additional military contingent to former Yugoslavia. The session of the upper chamber of the Russian Parliament was attended by representatives of the Russian Defence and Foreign Ministries, federal departments, a counter-intelligence department and a department for emergency situations. In accordance with the Russian Constitution, "a decision on the possibility of the use of the Russian Armed Forces outside Russia's territory" is within the competence of the Russian Federation Council. A resolution passed by the Federation Council instructs the Russian Government to resolve problems connected with the formation of a corresponding military contingent on a voluntary basis and on terms envisaged by the resolution of the Russian Parliament of March 6, 1992. The Russian Government was also instructed to urgently resolve the problem of giving a pay rise to the personnel of the Russian contingent. Under the resolution, the Russian Defence Ministry, shall, jointly with the Russian Foreign Ministry, create a system of measures providing social guarantees to the Russian personnel taking part in peace-keeping operations in former Yugoslavia and coordinate these measures with the U.N. secretariat. The Russian Foreign Ministry was instructed to institute additional posts for Russian officers in the headquarters of the U.N. peace-keeping forces. The Federation Council drew attention to the fact that it is necessary to step up the work of corresponding departments for the ultimate solution of the Bosnian crisis by political means.
