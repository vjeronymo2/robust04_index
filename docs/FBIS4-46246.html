BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on insurgent activities in Peru compiled from Peruvian press published through 18 May. The Huallaga political military command issued an official communique stating that nine Shining Path, SL, "terrorists" who operated in Huanuco and San Martin Departments were arrested on 6-10 May. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 13 May 94 p A10) On 12 May, four repentant SL "subversives" were presented to the press. They were members of the main SL column. They carried out many attacks in Huacho, Huaura, Barranca, Huaral, Cajatambo, and Oyon. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 13 May 94 p A16) On 13 May, an unknown number of SL "terrorists" attacked the Tocache airport, south of San Martin Department. Air Force officer Juan Nestor Asto Alfaro died in the attack. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 14 May 94 p A12) Special Forces Division personnel on 13 May arrested 34 alleged "terrorists" during a dragnet in the Felix Raucana shantytown in Lima. According to intelligence agents, Oscar Alberto Ramirez Durand, aka Feliciano, ordered the terrorist cells to resume activities in Felix Raucana, where the forces found "subversive" propaganda and documents. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 14 May 94 p A11) On 13 May, heavily armed SL "terrorists" invaded the terminal of El Rapido Inc. company, where they overpowered 20 people. They hurled fire bombs at four buses but no one was injured. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 14 May 94 p A13) On 14 May, two "terrorists" were killed during a clash between Army forces and the SL "Red Sea" battalion in the township of Amarillo, Tingo Maria. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 15 May 94 p A4) A column of 50 SL "terrorists" from the dismantled "Red Sea" battalion headed by Jose Picon Hilario, aka "El Manco," invaded the township of Pucayacu, where they killed two employees of the National Compensation and Social Development Fund. Repentant SL "terrorists" said El Manco has enough weapons and ammunition to continue attacking townships and military patrols. They noted that automatic rifles, AKM submachine guns, grenades, and a large quantity of ammunition are buried in many parts of the jungle in Leoncio Prado Province. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 16 May 94 p A10) On 16 May, the Huallaga Front countersubversive battalion captured 10 members of a SL cell in Monzon Valley, Leoncio Prado Province, Huanuco Department. Military sources said the detainees belong to the "popular committees" that tried to support the "Red Sea" battalion, and the so-called "Local Force." They were planning to attack civilians and ambush military patrols. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 17 May 94 p A7) On 16 May, "terrorists" dynamited a segment of the Norperuano oil pipeline in Huarmaca zone, Huancabamba Province, Piura Department. The damage caused a loss of approximately 9,500 barrels of oil. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 17 May 94 p A1) SL "terrorists" shot to death Camila Arce in her stall at a market in San Juan de Miraflores. (Lima EFE in Spanish 0152 GMT 17 May 94) Army and National Police personnel on 17 May arrested four SL "terrorists" and seized weapons and explosives shortly after they exploded dynamite near the El Pueblo hotel, Santa Clara District, Lima. They were planning to attack schools and buses. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 18 May 94 p A12) On 16 May, Army personnel arrested a SL top leader who operated in Tingo Maria. The "subversive's" identity was not revealed, but it was learned that he was planning to attack the Mobile Rural Patrol Unit in charge of fighting drug trafficking in the area. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 18 May 94 p A10)
