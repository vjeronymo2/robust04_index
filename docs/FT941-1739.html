940324 FT 24 MAR 94 / World Trade News: Ford set to make parts in China Ford, the US vehicle-maker, has reached outline agreement with Shanghai Automotive Industry Corporation (SAIC) to set up a components joint venture in China. It would be Ford's first manufacturing venture in China, and the company said it hoped to go ahead 'as soon as possible'. The two companies expect to finalise the deal 'in a few weeks'. It will then be submitted to the authorities for final approval. The collaboration between Ford's plastic and trim products division and SAIC's Yan Feng division is aimed at producing a range of components including interior trim, seats, instrument panels and other plastic parts for the Chinese motor industry. Ford said that Yan Feng was currently the largest supplier of automotive trim components in China. The US company is also having discussions with several other Chinese manufacturers that could lead to components joint ventures in areas such as glass, electronics, engine management systems and air conditioning. It said it was also interested in vehicle assembly opportunities in China.
