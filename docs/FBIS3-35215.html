Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] 'Ali Salim al-Bid, vice chairman of the Presidential Council in the Republic of Yemen arrived in Damascus at noon today. Vice President 'Abd-al-Halim Khaddam led those who received al-Bid at the Damascus International Airport. Khaddam and al-Bid then headed in an official motorcade for the guest palace, al-Bid's residence. Al-Bid made a statement to the correspondent of the Syrian Arab News Agency in which he said: "We are glad, while visiting the sisterly Syrian Arab Republic, to meet with our brothers in this fraternal country, led by President Hafiz al-Asad, to discuss fraternal bilateral relations and ways of bolstering and developing them, in addition to the Arab and international situation. "It is an opportunity to acquaint our Syrian brothers with developments in Yemen and the results of national dialogue and efforts contained in the document of pledge and accord that will be signed in the Jordanian capital, Amman, on the 10th of the blessed Ramadan. "We believe that it is an opportunity to discuss with our brothers in the beloved Syria the means of healing the rift, restoring Arab cohesion, and organizing the single Arab house in a manner that will contribute to the restoration of Arab solidarity and the activation of the Arab nation's role among the nations. This will come in the wake of new international circumstances and changes, and in a manner that allows the Arab nation to confront the dangers and challenges facing it. "On this occasion, I am pleased to express my satisfaction with the development of fraternal relations between our country and the sisterly Syrian Arab Republic, and to emphasize the importance of developing these relations in a manner that serves the joint interests of the two fraternal Yemeni and Syrian peoples, and the issues that concern our Arab and Islamic nations, and strengthens the role and status of the two countries in the Arab and international arenas." Al-Bid is accompanied by Dr. Yasin Sa'id Nu'man, a Yemeni Socialist Party Political Bureau member and head of the secretariat of the party's Central Committee.
