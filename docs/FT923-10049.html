920801 FT 01 AUG 92 / World Stock Markets (Europe): Fears about Deutsche's results depress Frankfurt BOURSES ended lower in uncertain trading, writes Our Markets Staff. FRANKFURT came under renewed pressure from Allianz and Deutsche Bank. Forecasts that the latter's half-year results, due on Tuesday, may prove disappointing saw its shares fall DM11.50 or 1.8 per cent to DM637. Some analysts said that there were rumours that total operating profit would show a 5 per cent decline compared with the same period in 1991, instead of estimates of a rise of between 5 and 7 per cent. At midsession the FAZ was down 6.12 at 636.90, slightly lower on the week while the DAX shed 8.57 to 1,615.42, but rose 0.3 per cent over the week. Turnover fell to DM4.6bn from DM5.1bn. BMW, up DM10 at DM553, was lifted by Thursday's pleasing results while other car makers fell :Volkswagen off DM3 at DM356.50, Daimler down DM4.50 at DM685 and Porsche DM12 lower at DM515. News that the Swiss retailer Metro was to take a majority stake in Asko saw the latter's shares fall as low as DM762 before recovering to close up DM2 at DM792. Some analysts welcomed the deal, since it would enable Asko to consolidate its position in the retail sector. PARIS was unsettled by weakness in the bond market and worries that the French will vote against the Maastricht Treaty in the September referendum. The CAC-40 index fell 27.90 to 1,754.67, but was still up 1.2 per cent on the week thanks to an earlier but short-lived bear market rally. Volume was a modest FFr1.9bn and was mainly generated by arbitrage activity. Euro Disney continued its fall, losing FFr3 to FFr85 while BSN lost another FFr15 to FFr1,000. The stock has lost 4.1 per cent since last Friday when the food company said it planned to restrict voting rights. MILAN rose as dealers squared their positions ahead of the weekend and the start of August, when many companies close down for the summer holidays. The Comit index rose 4.0 to 401.72, but was 1.1 per cent lower on the week, in turnover estimated at slightly above Thursday's L80bn. The market was pulled higher at the start by the insurance sector, with Generali adding L345 to L26,345 and jumped to L27,850 after hours. Fondiaria rose L560 to L23,810 and Ras added L340 to L13,850. The market's rebound caused some 10 shares to be suspended temporarily for excessive gains, including the steel group Falk and the energy group Edison. AMSTERDAM saw selling in Philips after the consumer electronics group's announcement on Thursday that it was delaying the introduction of a new compact cassette product. Its shares shed 50 cents to Fl 24 while Polygram slipped 70 cents to Fl 47.80. The CBS Tendency index closed down 0.8 at 116.2 but rose 0.8 per cent on the week. Royal Dutch was lifted late in the session on good half year results from its Shell Oil division in the US. Its shares advanced Fl 2.30 to Fl 147.20. Fokker, rebuked by the stock market authorities for the way it kept its minority shareholders appraised of its takeover talks with Dasa of Germany, was 20 cents weaker at Fl 25.80. ZURICH weakened in late trading. The SMI index fell 8 to 1,798.1, but put on 2.4 per cent on the week. Union Bank bearers lost SFr5 to SFr701 and Swiss Bank bearers dropped SFr11 to SFr247 after announcing on Thursday a 7.7 per cent fall in half year operating profit. Nestle bearers lost SFr70 to SFr9,370. BRUSSELS was lifted by gains in Solvay and Petrofina. The Bel-20 index gained 4.08 to 1,145.26, adding 0.4 per cent on the week, in low turnover of BFr645m. Solvay was actively traded after Thursday's positive half-year results, advancing BFr200 to BFr12,475. Petrofina firmed BFr100 to BFr11,025. VIENNA eased in line with other markets. The ATX index lost 9.54 to 782.62, but was up 1.4 per cent on the week. Steyr Daimler Puch fell another Sch8 to Sch140 while Austrian Airlines recovered Sch5 to Sch1,575 after falling Thursday on news that it may have to cut its 1992 dividend. STOCKHOLM rose on foreign interest in Astra. The Affarsvarlden index gained 2.70 to 861.70, up 3.2 per cent on the week.
