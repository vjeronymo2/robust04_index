940519 FT 19 MAY 94 / International Company News: Chinese group plans HK offering Dongfang Electrical Machinery, the last of the first nine Chinese state companies to be listed in Hong Kong, yesterday said it would offer 170m H-shares at HKDollars 2.83 each in an initial capital raising of HKDollars 456.3m (USDollars 59.1m). Dongfang is China's second largest manufacturer of hydroelectric power equipment and is located in Sichuan province in China's south-west. It is coming to the market on a prospective price-earnings multiple of 11.94 times 1995 earnings. Given the recent performance of H-share companies - shares listed outside China and in which foreigners can invest - this may be considered expensive. Concerns about the direction of the Chinese economy have made investors wary of mainland companies about which they know little. Last week, Tianjin Bohai Chemical's HKDollars 408m offering was barely subscribed. On its debut on Tuesday the company's share price fell below the offer price. Dongfang said it planned to use the proceeds of its share offer to upgrade production capacity and production technology. This would enable it to meet the demand for power generating equipment required by plans to dam the Yangtze river. Mr Yuan Changhe, chairman, said: 'With the huge demand for power and power generation equipment in China, we believe the company is at a watershed in its development.'
