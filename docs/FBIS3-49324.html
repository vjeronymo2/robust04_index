Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Zagreb, Jan 7 (AFP) -- British UN troops have exhumed a mass grave containing the bodies of 27 Croats killed by Bosnian Government troops in a village near the central Bosnian town of Vitez, a UN spokesmans said Friday. Bosnian Croats forces (HVO) have accused the Moslem-dominated government army of massacring some 50 civilians in an attack on the village of Dubravica on December 22 and have demanded an international inquiry. British forces in the area began a search for graves and on Thursday exhumed 27 bodies from a trench in the presence of UN military and European Community observers, but said they had no concrete evidence of a massacre. Lindsey French, a spokeswoman for the British battalion contacted in Split, said the bodies were all dressed in HVO uniforms all carried "wounds received in a battle." "We have no indication of any form of massacre," she said.
