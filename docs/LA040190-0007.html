April 1, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

THE MOVIE CHART: FILMS GOING INTO PRODUCTION:

Love Field (Midge Sanford/Sarah Pillsbury). Shooting in North Carolina. Michelle Pfeiffer's a young Southern woman who travels to our nation's capital after JFK's assassination.

En route she meets and establishes a relationship with a black man. Producers Midge Sanford and Sarah Pillsbury. Director Jonathan Kaplan. Screenwriter Don Roos.

Sleeping With the Enemy (Fox). Shooting in North and South Carolina. Julia Roberts stars in this thriller as a woman who suffers much after falling for the wrong man. The Nancy Price novel comes to life under the direction of "The Stepfather's" Joseph Ruben. Executive producer Jeffrey Chernov. Producer Leonard Goldberg. Screenwriters Lloyd Fonvielle, Ron Bass and Bruce Joel Rubin. Also stars Patrick Bergin and Kevin Anderson.

The Taking of Beverly Hills (Nelson). Shooting in L.A. and Mexico City. "Wiseguy" heartthrob Ken Wahl stars as a high-profile pro quarterback who finds himself partnered with a crooked cop during the biggest heist Beverly Hills has ever witnessed. Producer David Giler. Director Sidney J. Furie. Screenwriters Rick Natkin, David Fuller and David Burke. Also stars Harley Kozak and Matt Frewer.

Until the End of the World (Road Movies/Argos Films). Shooting in Italy, France, U.S.S.R., China, etc. "Wings of Desire" director Wim Wenders' mammoth project (shot in 65mm) stars William Hurt and Max Von Sydow and purports to be a Hitchcockian love story set a decade hence. Producers Jonathan Taplin and Anatole Dauman. Screenwriters Wenders and Peter Carey. Late '90 release. David Pecchia
