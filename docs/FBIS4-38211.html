BFN [Text] The Golboddin-Dostam militia's attacks were aborted in Qala-e Ahmad Khan, and a large area was cleared up in Karta-e Naw [southeast of Kabul]. Reports received by the Bakhtar Information Agency [BIA] say that forces of the Islamic army were consolidating their positions in the course of the day, and they crushed two attacks by the Golboddin-Dostam militia in Qala-e Ahmad Khan. As a result a number of the militia were killed. According to a report by BIA, calm has resumed in Kabul, and the refugees of the city are returning to their homes in the old Microrayon and Shah-Shahid. The report says that a large number of Kabul's refugees who had gone to Jalalabad six months ago are returning to their places. Over the past three days the Islamic army's conquests have removed militia's heavy weapons positions from the city. They can hardly cause trouble to the people, as a result. However, the conquests have put urgent tasks on the shoulders of the state. The reconstruction of the conquered areas, most of which have become ruins, the reactivation of the police security zones, and the construction of educational and health institution in those areas rest at the top of the list of programs. The report adds that the danger has not completely been eliminated, but that the Golboddin-Dostam militia threaten residential areas through their stealthy attacks. According to reports, in a crowded area of the city 13 residents of Kabul were martyred as a result of three bombs dropped by a jet airplane. The raid took place at 0800 this morning. As a result, three shops were damaged. According to an authentic source, strong words were exchanged two days ago between Golboddin and Dostam in a radio conversation, and militia's scandalous defeat in Kabul has been considered its real reason. In the course of a face-to-face battle, six foreign citizens were killed and seven others have been captured. A BIA correspondent who had personally spoken to the prisoners reported to us that the prisoners were taken by mojahedin in the area of Kara-e Naw. Despite the fact that senior state authorities have spoken about the existence of foreign citizens, and the Islamic army reported their capture, this is the first time that reporters have been allowed to see the prisoners and to photograph them. The prisoners have given their names as follows: Naddem Shahzad, from Mianbagh in Punjab; Sayed Ahmad, from Punjab; Mohammad Aziz, from Sailkot in Punjab; Farhatnur, from Multan; Abed Saleem, from Karachi; Jawed Akhtar, from Peshawar; Bahram Khan, from Peshawar. Nadeem Shahzad says that he has lost six of his friends in the course of the battle whose bodies were left in the battlefield.
