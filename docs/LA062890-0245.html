June 28, 1990, Thursday, P.M. Final

LIZ SMITH: PAPARAZZI HOT FOR IVANA; DUNNE CASTING ABOUT

OF ALL the names attending Helen Gurley Brown's 25th anniversary party Monday, the one the paparazzi went mad over was Ivana Trump. They liked her even better than Don Johnson, once the sizzling hero of "Miami Vice."

As for those photos of Ivana embracing Don in such a warm way -- big deal. He and his wife, Melanie Griffith, getting ready to take a Mediterranean cruise together, have long been pals of both the Trumps.

Hot skinny yesterday had it that some of Ivana's admirers are saying "I told you so!" They wanted her to accept the money of the nuptial agreement and take it and run. Says one: "She'd have been better off. Now she may end up with half of nothing!" But Ivana is going ahead with her lawsuit to declare herself the equal partner in Donald's empire, and she'll be giving legal depositions early next month. Her plans haven't changed. She does not intend to accept the terms of the nuptial agreement nor to file for divorce.

AUTHOR DOMINICK Dunne has blanketed the United States with his promo tour for his new novel, "An Inconvenient Woman." (This is the sizzler about a powerful and rich California couple with friends at the top. The wife is Madame Perfection. The husband has a longtime mistress who makes trouble, etc.)

Now didn't Nick Dunne recently have a little "meet" with Farrah Fawcett, who is dying to play the "inconvenient woman"? And who does the writer envision in the film version as the all-too-perfect wife? Faye Dunaway!

HAVE A staggering statistic. Conservative columnist Patrick J. Buchanan's coming "From the Right" newsletter will tell us that if Trump wanted to stay rich, he should have gone to law school!

Buchanan reports that the Resolution Trust Corp. (set up to oversee the savings and loan bailout) has budgeted $500 million to be paid to consulting lawyers in 1990 alone. These fees will grow to billions before it's over, even if it is ever over in our lifetimes.

The General Accounting Office's low estimate now of the cost of the S&L bailout is $325 billion. (Their pessimistic estimate is $500 billion.) The Stanford Law and Policy Review, meanwhile, predicts that the bailout could reach -- wait for it -- $1.4 trillion. Write that as $1,400,000,000,000.

TO KNOW her is to love her! That's Tina Turner. And that's all it took for Britain's Lord and Lady Tavistock to reverse their ban on rock shows on the grounds of their Woburn Abbey. His lordship and her ladyship had a personal talk with Tina, and suddenly the regal estate near London will become the site for two very special performances -- July 28 and 29 -- for more than 130,000 concertgoers.
