BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 27 (KCNA) -- An endless stream of people is flowing to the revolutionary battlefields and historical sites of Mt. Paektu area in the northern part of Korea. In the last two months, they were visited by anti-Japanese revolutionary fighters, veterans of the fatherland liberation war, working people of various social strata and tends of thousands of students from more than 100 senior middle schools. Going round the battlefields and historical sites associated with the history of the anti-Japanese revolutionary struggle of the Korean people, the visitors get a deeper knowledge of the glorious and brilliant revolutionary history of the great leader President Kim Il-song and his undying feats. In Korea Mt. Paektu area is one of the main centres of education in the revolutionary traditions. This area where the Mt. Paektu base was established in the period of the anti-Japanese revoutionary struggle has secret camps, secret liaison spots, bivouacs, battlefields including those of the Pochonbo and Taehongdan battles. In June 1956, the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il led a group of excursionists to hew the path of march to the revolutionary battlefields in Mt. Paektu area and wisely guided the work of well building up the revolutionary battlefields and historical sites. As a result, statues of the great leader, towers and monuments were erected and revolutionary museums built in Hyesan City, the starting point of the excursion to Mt. Paektu area, Pochonbo, Chongbong and Lake Samji and other battlefels and hstorical sites. And preserved in their original state there are the historical relics and remains and slogan-bearing trees. A mauser, binoculars, a field bag, a map and other historical relics used by President Kim Il-song in the period of the anti- Japanese revolutionary struggle are on display in the log cabin of the headquarters in the Mt. Paektu secret camp. The log cabin is near Chong-il Peak and Sobaek Stream. The time-honored old home where dear Comrade Kim Chong-il was born preserve valuable historical relics he used in his childhood, as well as simple household utensils. On the shore of Lake Samji where the great leader took a rest after advancing into the homeland with the main force of the Korean People's Revolutionary Army (KPRA) in May 1939, there is a grand monument consisting of 6-metre high large group sculptures portraying members of the KPRA and people of various strata, 85 in all, and a 50-metre signal-fire tower in a spacious area 400 metres in length and 250 metres in width with the statue of President Kim Il-song in the centre. They are surrounded by the Samjiyon Revolutionary Museum, the marker of the historical relics, lodging houses and cultural and amusement facilities for the excursionists. The revolutionary battlefields and historical sites in Pochonbo, Chongbong and Konchang, too, have been built up well as monuments of eternal value. The Mt. Paektu area is famous also as a tourist resort.
