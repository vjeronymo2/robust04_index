BFN [From the "ACIR Radio News" newscast] [Text] Ignacio Pichardo Pagaza has been appointed the Institutional Revolutionary Party's [PRI] secretary general by the PRI's National Political Council. Ignacio Pichardo Pagaza was born in Toluca, Mexico State, on 13 November 1935. He is the son of Attorney Carlos Pichardo Cruz and Maria del Carmen Pagaza Varela. He is married to Julieta Lechuga, who is a voluntary social worker. In 1953 Pichardo Pagaza enrolled in National Autonomous University of Mexico's Law School, where he earned a bachelor's degree in law and graduated in 1958 with a thesis entitled "The Judicial Basis of Economic Planning in Mexico." He earned a bachelor's degree in industrial relations and a master's degree in administration and public finances at the London School of Economics, UK, and another master's in administration at Dartmouth College in the United States. Regarding his political activities, which is what interests us, Pichardo Pagaza has worked as IEPES [Institute for Political, Economic, and Social Studies] coordinator. He also was a member of the PRI's CEN [Central Executive Committee] and a consultant to the CNC [National Peasant Confederation]. Pichardo Pagaza served as a federal deputy for Toluca and was president of the Programming and Public Budget Commission. He was also Government Secretariat general secretary and Finance and Public Credit Secretariat income under secretary. As an academic, Ignacio Pichardo was a professor at the Mexico School and author of works such as "Ten Years of Planning and Public Administration" and "Our Nation's Fiscal Policy." He also published the books: "Public Administration and Urban Development" and "The Mexican State's Municipal Development Institute in Mexican Productivity and Public Administration." Pichardo Pagazo served as ambassador to Spain until 5 April [date as heard], when he was appointed PRI CEN secretary general.
