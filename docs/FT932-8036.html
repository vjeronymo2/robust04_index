930520 FT 20 MAY 93 / International Company News in Brief: General Motors General Motors said yesterday it had reached agreements with more than 100 banks on about Dollars 21bn of syndicated credit lines that together make up about half of the group's total worldwide bank credit facilities, writes Patrick Harverson. GM added that it was renegotiating about Dollars 4bn of additional credit lines with banks in Europe, Canada and Australia. The newly-negotiated syndications, which replace various individual bank facilities extended to GM and GMAC, the group's financial services subsidiary, represented a 'significant increase in the amount of committed, long-term credit available to the corporation,' said GM.
