BFN [Text] The Kazakh Foreign Ministry has issued a statement in connection with the underground nuclear test carried out by the PRC at Lop-Nor test site on 10 June. It says: Kazakhstan is persistent in its policy of banning nuclear tests and signing relevant agreements. President Nursultan Nazarbayev addressed heads of nuclear states in August 1993 with a proposal to extend a moratorium on tests of weapons of mass destruction till the year 2005. During his visit in October 1993 to the PRC he held talks with the Chinese leadership. He briefed the Chinese leadership on Kazakhstan's stance with regard to nuclear tests. Kazakhstan has approved a declaration made by the PRC's Foreign Ministry that said China understands concern of non-nuclear states regarding nuclear tests. The foreign ministry notes that the last test in China was carried out during [words indistinct] of moratorium on nuclear tests. The statement says: It worries us this test was carried out in the neigboring state and might cause serious problems in ecology and (?people's health). The Kazakh Foreign Ministry believes it is necessary to resume the discussion of the specific proposals by Kazakhstan on setting up a joint group of experts on the problems of Semipalatinsk and Lop-Nor test sites.
