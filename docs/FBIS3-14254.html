Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto has said Pakistan being a peace-loving nation wants peace with its neighbors on the basis of honor and equality. Addressing a darbar [meeting] of the Punjab Regiment in Mardan today, the prime minister said we do not cherish aggressive designs against anyone. However, we cannot remain oblivious to our defense which will be strengthened within our limited resources. Our neighbors must not dream to establish hegemony because we can neither be provoked nor submit, she said, adding the people of Jammu and Kashmir must be given the right of self-determination and the Kashmir issue should be solved in a manner that it leads to laying a solid foundation for peace in the region. A neighboring country is dreaming to bring us under its hegemony, she said, adding we will not be cowed down by anybody. Ms. Benazir Bhutto said the government is making every endeavor for the welfare of the people. The task is stupendous and the problems are many, which need cooperation from every segment of society. She said the prime objective before the political parties should be to ensure that the country is put on the road to progress and prosperity and its image enhanced in the community of nations. She, therefore, appealed to the people, including political leaders, ulema [religious scholars], and students to cooperate with the government in its efforts to solve socioeconomic problems. The prime minister commended the Punjab Regiment for its glorious services and said the martyrs of the regiment have carved out a prominent place in the nation's history by having laid down their lives in the defense of the motherland. On this occasion, the prime minister announced a grant of 8 million rupees for various welfare projects being carried out by the regiment. Governor of Punjab retired Lieutenant General Mohammad Iqbal Khan, Governor of NWFP [North-West Frontier Province] retired Major Gen. Khurshid Ali Khan, Defense Minister Mr. Aftab Shaban Mirani, Chief of Army Staff Gen. Abdul Waheed, and other high civil and military officials were present on the occasion. Earlier, the colonel commandant of the Punjab Regimental Center, Lt. Gen. Nasir Akhtar, and Commandant of the center Brigadier Qamar Zaman, in their speeches, gave a resume of the regiment's historic achievements and its role both in times of peace and war. The regiment founded in 1759 in Madras has the unique honor of winning of 440 military awards, including four Nishan-i-Haider. The colonel commandant presented the regiment's crest to Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto and Chief of the Army Staff Gen. Abdul Waheed.
