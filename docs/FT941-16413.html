940111 FT 11 JAN 94 / IG Farben assets case dismissed A German court said yesterday that IG Farben, a shell company handling the liquidation of the notorious second world war Nazi chemicals cartel, has no legal claim to dispossessed assets in eastern Germany, Reuter reports from Berlin. The federal administrative court in Berlin confirmed an earlier ruling by a lower court in Halle. IG Farben shareholders had hoped to gain millions of marks in windfall profits from the sale of the company's property in east Germany. IG Farben, which developed the gas used to kill millions in Nazi death camps, was broken up afterwards and formed the nucleus of Germany's three main chemical groups Hoechst, BASF and Bayer.
