BFN [Text] Warsaw, June 8 -- Some 48 per cent of respondents to a DEMOSKOP poll said they thought that President Lech Walesa was performing badly over the last few weeks, while 17 per cent voiced the opinion that he was doing "very badly". 21 per cent of respondents think the president is doing well, while 1 per cent say he is doing "very well". The remainder had no opinion on the matter. Thirty-nine per cent of respondents thought that Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak was performing well as of late. 36 per cent were of the opposite opinion, while 7 per cent thought that he was doing very poorly. 3 per cent thought the prime minister to be performing very well and 15 per cent answered no comment. The government has fallen out of favour with 47 per cent of respondents, while a further 8 per cent feel that it has been performing "very poorly". 28 per cent thought the government was doing "well", 1 per cent felt all was going very well. The poll was conducted between May 19 and 26.
