BFN [Text] Pyongyang, June 13 (KCNA) -- The National Council of Christian Churches of South Korea in a policy discussion of the reunification committee June 10 said that the U.S.-led move of "sanctions" against the North might get on its nerve and demanded that "the United States stop utterances and acts creating tensions on the Korean peninsula," a Seoul-based radio said. Noting that the authorities' inconsistent "policy" towards the North increases the confusion and uneasiness of the people, the organisation decided to strongly urge the puppet authorities to "shape and promote a reunification policy giving top priority to the existence and peace of the nation." The Federation of Christian Social Movement in a recent statement clarified its stand opposed to "`sanctions' against the North," that heightens the war crisis.
