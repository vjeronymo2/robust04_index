BFN [Excerpt] Belem, 9 Jun (EFE) -- Foreign ministers and delegates attending the 24th OAS General Assembly today approved a resolution benefiting indigenous groups, as it commits them to celebrating on this continent the International Decade of the World's Indigenous Peoples. The resolution, which was proposed by Bolivia and sponsored by Mexico and Guatemala, was unanimously approved today by the delegations of the 34 OAS member states on the fourth and penultimate day of the assembly. The resolution entrusts the OAS secretary general with the task of acting as regional coordinator for the International Decade of the World's Indigenous Peoples within the cooperation framework established with the United Nations. Six months ago the UN General Assembly designated the decade starting on 10 December 1994 and ending in the year 2004 as the International Decade of the World's Indigenous Peoples. [passage omitted]
