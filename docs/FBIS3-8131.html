Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 2/3/1994 -- Foreign Ministry Spokesman Mircea Geoana announced at the today's news conference President Ion Iliescu's unofficial visit to China on 11-14 March this year at the invitation of the Chinese Government. The Romanian president will be shown the special economic zones in the South of China, Shen Zhen and Zhu Hai, the Quandong Canton Province where, the Romanian delegation will meet with the local administration. The president will be accompanied by a group of Romanian businessmen, executives of state and private companies, and will examine the prospects of cooperation between the two countries. [passage omitted] The spokesman for the Foreign Ministry read a communique at a Wednesday, March 2, press conference, saying that the Romanian Foreign Ministry has taken note with satisfaction of the agreement on the creation of a Croatian-Muslim Federation in Bosnia- Herzegovina and of a confederation made up of the said federation and Croatia. The Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs considers that the putting in place of the provisions of this accord would represent an important step in finding a political solution to the crisis in Bosnia-Herzegovina, in parallel with solutions for the other problems under negotiation. The Foreign Ministry expresses hope that this favorable moment will be made good use of in the interest of all the parties, of stability and security in the region, reads the communique. The relations between Romania and Moldova are not function of the electoral cycle, which was already proved when elections were held in Romania, said Foreign Ministry Spokesman Mircea Geoana at his Wednesday, March 2, press conference, speaking about the elections held in the Republic of Moldova on February 27. The results of the polls reflect the state of mind of the population and they can be interpreted in terms of the economic and social difficulties confronting the Republic of Moldova, of the way in which the electorate took those aspects of the programmes of the parties running for seats in parliament that refer to the current situation of the country, mentioned the spokesman for the Foreign Ministry. An evaluation of the results has to take into account the conditions specific to the Republic of Moldova, the tensions appeared in consequence of the situation in Dniester and the districts inhabited by Gagauz, the general evolutions in the former Soviet space. "That is how the option for the left can be explained, the relative high percent of vote got by the bloc formed of the Socialist Party (the former Communist Party) and the "unity" party. It is interesting to mention, nevertheless, that the Democratic Agrarian Party had the biggest number of seats in the previous parliament and it has preserved its position, added Geoana. At the 2 March weekly news conference of the Romanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Spokesman Mircea Geoana referred to the statement of 28 February of the Council of Representatives of the Hungarian Democratic Union of Romania (UDMR), issued as a follow-up and in response to a letter sent to this body by Bishop Laszlo Tokes. The spokesman said that the letter confirms, once again, the extremist and anti-Romanian angle from which Pastor Tokes, encouraged by some UDMR leaders and political persons in Hungary, approaches his relations with the Romanian state and with UDMR itself. "We express the hope that the UDMR leaders will distance themselves from the positions of the honorary president of their party, in whose view at present in Romania Ceausescu's policies on minorities are being continued. It is only to be regretted that the declaration issued by the UDMR's Council of Representatives practically reiterates all the accusations listed in the instruction-letter of Pastor Tokes. UDMR's declaration is accusing the Romanian authorities of lacking the political will to implement the amendments of the Council of Europe. We think it is high time UDMR understood that the addressee of these amendments is the Romanian society as a whole and that the observance of the commitments made before the Council on Romania's admission to it is a matter of national interest," the spokesman said. He also stressed that the withdrawal of UDMR from the Council for Ethnic Minorities -- the place where it would be but natural that such problems were debated -- reveals the lack of both real interest and pragmatism in solving them, and lack of consideration for the interests and role of the other ethnic minorities in Romania. "The Ministry of Foreign Affairs expresses the confidence that, joining the competent institutions in the rapid finalization of this process, all the forces interested in proving the democratic propensity and realities of Romania will bring a constructive and sincere contribution," the Foreign Ministry spokesman concluded.
