January 10, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

HOLLYWOOD AND BLACK AMERICANS

I am amused, and somewhat bemused too, at one Benjamin Stein's plea for more roles for blacks in the movies. As a black man myself, I think Stein asks too much.

For example: I'm not a big TV fan, but it does seem to me that there are more black shows on the air than there are white. In every movie, blacks have leading roles, very often where they would not be found in everyday life.

About 10 years ago, several black movies were made, and apparently they lost money. As a black, I am not interested in paying to see other people who are my color: I need more motivation than that. I think we blacks now have more than our fair share on film and that it's time to give that plea a rest.

GABRIEL FURLONG

Los Angeles
