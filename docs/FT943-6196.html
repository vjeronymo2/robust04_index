940831 FT 31 AUG 94 / Ulster Unionist leader accepts Major's pledge Mr James Molyneaux, leader of the Ulster Unionist party, said yesterday he was confident that the government had no plans to change Northern Ireland's constitutional position - 'contrary to wild speculation over the weekend'. The measured tone of his remarks was echoed by Mr John Taylor, UUP MP for Strangford, who warned against premature reaction by unionists. But the hardline Democratic Unionist party warned of fresh trouble in the event of new government concessions. The Rev Ian Paisley, the DUP leader, angrily demanded a meeting with Mr John Major, saying a truce based on a British 'surrender' could lead to fresh strife. He said: 'Any government that subverts the democratic wish of the people and goes against all the pledges they have made is going to have a situation which is a recipe for civil war in Northern Ireland.' The Ulster MPs' comments came after a statement on Monday night by Mr Gerry Adams had raised mounting expectations of a ceasefire still higher. In his first public acknowledgement that he had advised IRA leaders to consider halting their 25-year armed campaign, Mr Adams said he had always argued that a 'changed political climate could break the . . . stalemate and create the potential to eradicate the underlying causes of conflict. 'In my updated assessment to the IRA leadership I expressed my belief that the current political climate provides that potential.'
