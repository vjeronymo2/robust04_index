BFN [Text] Budapest, 14 June (MTI) -- The Tuesday [14 June] round of the coalition talks ended with success as the Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP) and the Alliance of Free Democrats (SZDSZ) ended the first stage of talks and agreed on issues related to joint government. The agreement includes the creation of the post of coalition deputy prime minister to have the smaller partner in the government, the SZDSZ, represented. The agreement sets down that the sides will pass decisions by mutual agreement. If they do not agree on certain issues, the matter will go to a coalition coordination council, whose members will be the prime minister, the coalition deputy prime minister, the chairmen of the two parties, the two parties' parliamentary group leaders, plus one member each delegated by the two parties.
