940601 FT 01 JUN 94 / Wales climbs wealth league Greater London remains the wealthiest region of the UK, but Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland are catching up, figures issued by the Central Statistical Office showed yesterday. In 1992, personal income per head in Greater London was Pounds 11,577, which was 122 per cent of the average for the whole of the UK, while the comparable figure in Wales and Northern Ireland was just Pounds 7,980 or 84.1 per cent of the average. However, over the period from 1990 to 1992, incomes in Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland grew most strongly while growth in the south-east of England and Greater London was below average. Scotland climbed for the first time above the UK average in terms of personal disposable income per head, which is calculated after deducting factors such as taxes and the community charge. The county with the highest level of income per head was Surrey, with an average of Pounds 11,225 in 1991. The wealthiest county outside England was Lothian, with an average of Pounds 9,510. The poorest county in terms of income per head was Mid Glamorgan, with an average of Pounds 6,858. The poorest English county was Durham, with an average of Pounds 7,312. Economic Trends. HMSO.
