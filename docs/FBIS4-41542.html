BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Evald Kessariyskiy] [Text] Nizhniy Novgorod, 26 Jun -- Analyzing the political situation in the country, Vladimir Zhirinovskiy, leader of the LDPR (Liberal Democratic Party of Russia), has not ruled out the possibility of dissolution of the State Duma and introduction of direct presidential rule in the next few months. He arrived in Nizhniy Novgorod today together with members of the LDPR group of deputies in the State Duma. The city's residents showed particular interest in a rally held in its main square. At the rally, the party leader reiterated that "there has been no real change in the lives of the people of Russia following the elections." This is why, Vladimir Zhirinovskiy explained, "we are in opposition, and if we do not succeed in replacing this government, we do not rule out the possibility that we ourselves would cancel the Treaty on Social Accord we signed earlier."
