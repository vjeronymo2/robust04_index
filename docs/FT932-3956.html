930611 FT 11 JUN 93 / Observer: No handicap You might think that a hole-in-one would be every amateur golfer's dream. Not so in Japan, where the souvenirs and celebrations a lucky player is expected to pay for can leave him seriously out of pocket. But in other people's misfortune lies an insurance company's business opportunity: hole-in-one protection plans. For a few yen a week Japanese golfers can insure against the calamity of ever getting a hole-in-one.
