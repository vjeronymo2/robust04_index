Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Prague, March 16 (CTK) -- New Slovak Prime Minister Jozef Moravcik was born on March 19, 1945. After he completed law studies in Prague and Bratislava he lectured at the School of Economics and worked shortly at the Bratislava Chemapol chemical company. Since 1972 he lectured at the Faculty of Law of Komensky University in Bratislava. In the years 1990-91 he was its dean. In January 1991 Moravcik filled a vacant seat in the Slovak National Counil (parliament) representing the Public Against Violence (VPN) movement. When the VPN split in 1991 he joined the HZDS [Movement for a Democratic Slovakia]. From April to June 1992 he was a member of the Slovak National Council presidium. In the June 1992 elections Moravcik was elected an HZDS deputy to the House of the People of the Federal Assembly (parliament) and in July he was appointed federal foreign minister. He held the post until December 1992 when the Czechoslovak Federation ceased to exist. In 1993 he became a legislative adviser to Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar. He was appointed Slovak foreign minister on March 19, 1993, to replace Milan Knazko (then HZDS) who was fired after long-term disputes with Meciar. [passage omitted] Moravcik made clear his opinion that a political structure compatible with the political structure common in Western Europe should be created in Slovakia. He warned that the HZDS did not have a partner in the West and said this was "a certain handicap." He also claimed he sympathized with the social democratic orientation.
