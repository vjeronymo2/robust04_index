BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, June 28 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- The Malaysian Armed Forces is expected to herald a new era as studies are underway to equip the army with tanks to enhance the country's defence in facing future challenges and threats. Armoured Corps Chairman Brigadier General Mohamed Ariff Ali said Tuesday the study was important to gauge the effectiveness of deploying tanks to strengthen the country's defence as the armed forces did not have any at present. Tanks are now necessary in modern warfare. The armoured cars being used do not come under the category of armoured cars, he told reporters in east Malaysian Sarawak state. [sentence as received] He said the study involved evaluation of modern tanks made in Eastern Bloc countries like Russia in line with Malaysia's recent purchase of military equipment like the MiG-29 fighter jets.
