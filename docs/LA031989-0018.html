March 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

OUTTAKES: STRANGE BEDFELLOWS

Studios are reportedly bidding hard for the right to finance the filming of "Body Politic," based on a political satire published by St. Martin's Press in 1987. The authors, Lynne Cheney and Victor Gold, ought to know how to lampoon D.C. mores -- Cheney, chair of the National Endowment for the Humanities, is the wife of Wyoming Rep. Richard Cheney, the nominee for secretary of defense.

The plot: During a President's re-election campaign, his vice-president has a fatal heart attack while engaged in a "delicate act" with a female news anchorwoman. The press secretary's assignment: Keep the veep "alive" in as long as possible.

A screenplay has already been written by John Warren, with Frank Oz attached as director. The indie producers developing the project are John Davis, Katherine Knowlton and David Kennerly. From Leonard Klady
