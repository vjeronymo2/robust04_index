Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gennadiy Kulagin] [Text] Almaty, 13 Feb -- Today President Nursultan Nazarbayev left for an official visit to the United States at the invitation of that country's president, Bill Clinton. Speaking to journalists prior to his departure, Nazarbayev said this visit, his second to the United States, promises to be highly productive. He voiced the hope that the forthcoming summit talks and meetings with business circles will inject a new economic and political quality to diverse Kazakhstan-U.S. ties. Increased mutually beneficial cooperation with the United States, he said, will greatly help the republic to lift its economy. The leader of Kazakhstan also announced that on the return journey, he will stop over in Paris at the invitation of President Francois Mitterrand. Their meeting will look at ways of developing existing agreements on bilateral cooperation.
