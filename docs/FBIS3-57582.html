Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] In his interview with Lithuanian national radio Prime Minister Adolfas Slezevicius stated that relations between Lithuania and Belarus may deteriorate after two pro-Moscow communist leaders, who were among the organizers of the 1991 January crackdown in Vilnius, had been brought back from Minsk to Vilnius by the Lithuanian law enforcement officers. The Belarusian MPs [word indistinct] their top law and order officials of handing over Mykolas Burokevicius and Juozas Jermalavicius to the Lithuanian side on an illegal basis. On Tuesday the Belarusian Parliament accepted the resignation of the Interior Minister and [words indistinct] service chief. According to Prime Minister Slezevicius, the detention of the two communist hardliners was carried out successfully and had been coordinated with the law enforcement institutions in Belarus.. Meanwhile chairman of the Belarusian Parliamentary Committee on National Security, Mechislav Gryb, claims that the two men were detained in violation of the Belarusian and international law. Speaking at a news conference in Minsk on Tuesday Mr. Gryb stated that the cases nine other persons wanted by the Lithuanian law enforcement authorities would be reviewed on a separate basis and that any kind of, as he called, illegal handover, like that of Burokevicius and Jermalavicius, would not happen in the future.
