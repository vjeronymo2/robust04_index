BFN [Excerpt] Amman, 6 May (MENA)--Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi stated that Egypt's leadership and security organs are confronting terrorism and terrorists with the firmness and strength they deserve and that all their activities are confronted decisively. He said: We should not worry about Egypt. Speaking at a meeting with members of the Egyptian community in Jordan before leaving Amman this afternoon, the prime minister said that compared with other countries, terrorism in Egypt is decreasing in a big way. Answering a question, he said that attempts were made over a long period to hold a dialogue with these terrorists at times between 1980 and 1987 or 1988. He added: The dialogue continued in one way or another but they were not ready to listen to the voice of reason and their ideas rendered the dialogue useless. When they started to use bullets and kill, the security organs confronted them decisively and firmly. [passage omitted]
