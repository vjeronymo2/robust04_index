BFN [Report by ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Pshenichnikov] [Text] Oslo, 29 Apr -- Consultations held here today were devoted to a discussion of the initiative put forward the other day by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev to hold a meeting of the heads of the foreign policy departments of the United States, Russia, and other "key countries" in order to work out ways of settling the crisis in Bosnia. They were attended by Deputy foreign Minister Vitaliy Churkin, the Russian president's special envoy, and Thorvald Stoltenberg and Lord Owen, the cochairmen of the coordinating committee of the international conference on the former Yugoslavia. At a news conference on the results of the consultations, it was stated that the forthcoming meeting of foreign ministers of the United States, Russia, and other "key countries" was intended to coordinate the efforts of the world community to end the war in Bosnia. The parties to the Bosnian conflict will not be invited to the meeting.
