941019 FT 19 OCT 94 / Howard at International Police Exhibition and Conference Mr Michael Howard, the home secretary, yesterday tested a viewer for recording scenes of crime in 3-D at the International Police Exhibition and Conference at Olympia, London. At the conference Mr Howard announced a plan to invite crime-hit communities to bid for Pounds 2m from the government - complemented by private and public sponsorship - to install closed circuit television systems. Housing estates, shopping centres, hospitals and car parks will be eligible to submit bids. Mr Howard said the government would only be looking for applications from smaller communities and local neighbourhoods since large towns and cities had already received support for the systems. Bids have to be submitted by next month. The maximum that schemes will receive is Pounds 100,000. Mr Howard said: 'Given the success of CCTV in many parts of the country in preventing crime and increasing arrests for crimes committed, this is an important development.'
