940712 FT 12 JUL 94 / Job prospects for graduates improve Job opportunities for graduates are increasing, according to companies which recruit from universities and colleges. The Association of Graduate Recruiters said member companies had reported a 4.2 per cent increase in vacancies, mostly in the non-industrial sector - the first rise reported in its annual survey for four years. However the survey said the number of graduates continues to grow faster than the number of jobs. Almost a third of 300 companies said they had some difficulty in filling vacancies, with a small number reporting a serious shortage, mainly of science and engineering graduates. Mr Roly Cockman, executive secretary, said that employers were now looking for business awareness and communication, whereas three years ago employers had complained of a shortage of knowledge and competence among graduates in their subjects. Mr Cockman said: 'Increasing numbers of graduates mean there are more people around with the academic competence they are looking for. He added: 'What they are looking for are other skills as well, awareness of how business works, communication skills and proven capacities for teamwork and leadership.'
