BFN [Text] The Jammu and Kashmir governor, General Krishna Rao, met the prime minister and the home minister in New Delhi. He is understood to have discussed with them measures to improve the situation in the state. In Kashmir valley, militants shot dead a former MLA [member of legislative assembly], Mr. Ghulam Qadir Mir, last evening at his residence at village (Uran) in Pulwama District. An associate of late Sheikh Abdullah, Mir is the 11th former legislator to be killed by militants in the last four years. Meanwhile, two militants have been killed and 16 arrested during the last 24 hours. A large quantity of arms and ammunition was recovered from them. The Indian ambassador to the United States, Mr. Sidhartha Shanker Ray, has said the accession of Jammu and Kashmir to India is legal, final, and irrevocable. He said there can be no compromise on the matter. Addressing the members of the Jammu Bar Association, Mr. Ray said the seizure of over 10,000 weapons supplied by Pakistan and the capture of foreign mercenaries is clear evidence of Islamabad's continuing support to the militants. He said the U.S. Administration is fully aware of Islamabad's direct involvement in terrorist activities.
