BFN [Text] Brasilia, 7 Apr (EFE) -- Congressional sources today told EFE that the Brazilian Chamber of Deputies is investigating reports on the exchange of young prostitutes for cocaine on the Brazilian border with Bolivia. A delegation of three deputies today went to investigate the charges raised by residents of Brazilian townships located in border areas with Bolivia in Mato Grosso, Acre, and Rondonia. The deputies investigating the report are socialist Deputy Chico Vigilante and conservative Deputies Maria Valadin and Zelia Mendes, who serve on the Chamber special committee that investigates child prostitution. According to the reports received by the committee, the minors are in the 11-16 age bracket -- although one was eight years old. They are exchanged for cocaine and taken to coca growing and producing areas in Bolivia as prostitutes for the men working there. The delegation will hear testimonies of state and municipal officials and local residents. Some witnesses told the congressmen that a presumably Brazilian couple is recruiting minors in the Amazon region, ostensibly to work as household servants in Sao Paulo, but these minors are taken to the border with Bolivia to be exchanged for cocaine. The congressional committee, which started to work six months ago, will release its final report on 31 May. Social democratic Deputy Moroni Torgan, who is a police officer and the rapporteur of this committee, was rather cautious about this case but did not rule out this type of crime.
