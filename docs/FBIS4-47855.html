BFN [Text] With just over 66 percent of the provisional national votes now known, the ANC will be the dominating party in the National Assembly with 251 seats, compared with the NP [National Party] with 88, the IFP [Inkatha Freedom Party] with 34, and the Freedom Front [FF] with 11 seats. The DP [Democratic Party] should have seven seats, the PAC [Pan-Africanist Congress] six, the African Christian Democratic Party [ACDP] two, and the African Moderates Congress Party [AMCP] one seat. This means that eight parties could take their seats in the National Assembly. Until now none of the other smaller parties has obtained enough support for a seat in the assembly. According to the latest provisional results the ANC has 64.8 percent of the national vote, compared with the NP's 20.5 percent.
