920603 FT 03 JUN 92 / Arts: Today's Television This year's Prince Of Wales Award For Innovation is announced on Tomorrow's World (7.30 BBC1). Seven ideas in technology and medicine were selected as finalists in 1990. They included an 'environmentally friendly' aerosol, a miniature microscope, and a surgical laser. Today Prince Charles reveals which has best fulfilled its potential. Timewatch considers the assassination on May 27 1942 of SS Obergruppenfuhrer Reinhard Heydrich, architect of the Nazis' 'final solution', and the reprisals which followed (8.10 BBC2). Dispatches tries to establish whether the Queen uses her unique public position for her private benefit, to enable her to become the richest monarch since the advent of parliamentary democracy more than 300 years ago (9.00 C4). Bob Peck stars in the final episode of the repeat showing of the political thriller Edge Of Darkness on BBC2 (9.25). Inside Story goes to Westbourne Park bus depot to look at the operation of London's red double deckers. The capital's bus service is to be privatised within two years and not everybody is convinced this will result in a better service (9.30 BBC1).
