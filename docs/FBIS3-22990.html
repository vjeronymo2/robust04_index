Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Gunmen carried out an attack on three Israelis this afternoon near Ramallah. The injured Israelis, who are members of the General Security Service [Shin Bet], were transferred to hospital, while security forces have been searching for the attackers. More from Alan Ben-'Ami: The three Israelis were attacked near the entrance to the village of Baytuniya beside Ramallah and about 12 km north of Jerusalem. The indications are that there were two gunmen waiting in ambush. The Israelis drove by in a saloon car and came under a hail of automatic weapons fire. Dozens of bullets penetrated their vehicle. One of the men was mortally wounded in the head and chest, the other two were both injured by metal splinters in the head, and one was wounded in the shoulder, while his companion was injured in the hand. [passage omitted] A curfew has been placed on Baytuniya, the surrounding area, and parts of Ramallah as the security forces try to find the perpetrators of this latest attack. [Jerusalem Israel Television Network Channel 1 in Hebrew at 1800 GMT on 13 February adds that "Hamas has claimed responsibility for the attack." Later in the newsreel, the anchor announces: "It has been just released for publication that 28-year-old Shin Bet member No'am Kohen from Jerusalem, who was wounded in the Baytuniya attack today, has died of his wounds."]
