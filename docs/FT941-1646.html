940324 FT 24 MAR 94 / Observer: Call home In the coming era of telephone banking, Royal Bank of Scotland's choice of BT chairman Sir Iain Vallance as a vice-chairman has the right ring. With a father who once headed the Post Office north of the border, Vallance's Scots credentials also happen to be impeccable. Colin Brown, BT's departing head of corporate affairs, was moved to recall that Scots connection the other day apropos some advice he was given on joining the Post Office, of which BT was then a part. How fast could a graduate trainee rise to the next grade, he inquired gingerly of a golfing pal in the business. 'Three years minimum,' came the reply, 'unless your father runs the Post Office in Scotland - then it's five months.'
