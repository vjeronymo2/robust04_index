BFN [Text] Ankara, May 2 (A.A.) -- A delegation including the General Staff and Foreign Ministry officials returned to Turkey after completing official contacts with UN Protection Forces (UNPROFOR) officials. General Staff spokesman Colonel Dogu Silahcioglu said the delegation met Croatian officials on April 28 and UN officials in Bosnia-Herzegovina to set up a date for the deployment of Turkish troops to Bosnia. Silahcioglu said the Turkish delegation and UN officials reached an understanding on the structure of Turkish soldiers, their necessities and location where they will function. Turkish troops will include a mechanized infantry battalion and military engineering and logistics team including health and transport personnel to support this battalion. He said all necessary preparations were completed for the active functioning of Turkish soldiers. Following a request from the United Nations, Turkey has agreed to send a 2,700 military personnel. UN officials said the infantry comprising 1,000 people is expected to be set up between Bosnians and Croatian forces in the buffer zone.
