931009 FT 09 OCT 93 / International Company News: Metra finishes notes placement METRA, the Finnish industrial conglomerate, has completed a private placement of preferred capital notes worth Dollars 100m to help pay off debt in what the group said was the first bond issue of its kind by a Finnish company outside the banking sector, writes Hugh Carnegy in Stockholm. The notes, with a renewable maturity of 50 years, are subordinate to senior debt and carry no voting rights or equity options. But the interest paid has precedence over dividend payments. The issue was lead managed by Nomura International, the Japanese investment bank.
