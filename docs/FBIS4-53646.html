BFN ["South Korean People's Cause of Independence, Democracy and National Reunification Is Invincible" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, May 21 (KCNA) -- The people's struggle for independence, democracy and national reunification is going on unremittingly in South Korea in spite of brutal suppression and despicable intrigues by the fascist clique, and their desire will surely be realized, says NODONG SINMUN in a by-lined article today. The article charges that the Kim Yong-sam puppet clique under the patronage of the U.S. imperialists are intensifying fascist suppression of the South Korean students and other sections of people who rose in struggle, while resorting to wicked intrigues to disorganize their movement from within through its reformation. The article reads in part: The puppet clique are, above all, trying to decompose the fundamental nature of the South Korean people's movement as one for independence, democracy and national reunification. They let their stooges, venal scholars and pseudo activists argue that there is no need to put up the slogan of independence, democracy and national reunification in the South Korean people's movement vociferating about the "change of the times", and even contend that there cannot and should not be a fight under the "civilian" regime. Their argument that the slogan of independence, democracy and national reunification should not be put up in the South Korean people's movement is, in essence, an extremely wicked and cunning trick to deny and decompose the fundamental character of the movement as one for anti-U.S. independence, anti-fascist democracy and national reunification and to cut off the lifeline of the movement boasting of scores of years of their unremitting struggle. The South Korean puppet clique also preach compromise with the "civilian" regime, not head-on confrontation with it or a fight for its overthrow. Their talk that the Kim Yong-sam regime should not be overthrown, but a compromise should be made with it, is, in the final analysis, as good as saying the people should continue to meekly obey the U.S. imperialists' colonial domination, not complain about dictatorship and fascism and keep mum about whatever they do against national reunification. They preach "petition" and "nonviolence", encouraging "dialogue" and "compromise", but it is a nonsensical talk under such fascist system as that of South Korea where suppression is going unhindered. Their preach is an artifice to lull the anti-Kim Yong-sam sentiments of the South Korean people, keep them from rising in fierce struggles and, furthermore, to totally disarm their movement and make them submit to the principle of non-resistance and capitulationism. The article stresses: The South Korean people's desire cannot be realized so long as such sycophant traitors, fascist dictators as Kim Yong-sam are left alone. The South Korean people, therefore, should kindle fiercer flames of struggle to overthrow the Kim Yong-sam puppet regime and establish a genuinely independent democratic regime.
