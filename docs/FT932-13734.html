930422 FT 22 APR 93 / Parliament and Politics: Tory candidate pledge on VAT MR Julian Davidson, the Tory candidate in the Newbury by-election, promised yesterday to do 'everything I could' to prevent a further widening of Value Added Tax beyond its imposition on domestic fuel announced in last month's Budget. His comments, after he had been challenged over the Tories' election pledges not to raise taxes, were seized on by his opponents. The main challenger in the Tory seat, Mr David Rendel, the Liberal Democrat candidate, said: 'I think there is now a widespread feeling in this area that the government was re-elected on the basis of a whole series of false pledges . . . think now people are no longer going to trust the government's pledges.'
