BFN [Text] RIGA, Apr 13, BNS - Latvia's foreign debt equalled 242.98 million U.S. dollars by April 1, Finance Ministry's Foreign Economic Relations Department Director Guntis Urlovskis said at a briefing Wednesday [13 April]. The total sum of the received foreign credits (the paid back credits are not counted) also is included in Latvia's foreign debt. Urlovskis said that, at the moment, Latvia has signed credit agreements for 493 million U.S. dollars in total. Latvia also has drafted credit agreements with the European Union for 40 million ECU, with Norway for 6.3 million USD, with Switzerland for 8.6 million U.S. dollars, and with Austria for 32 million Austrian schillings. Latvia plans to receive credits from Denmark for 6.3 million U.S. dollars and from Iceland for 2.1 million U.S. dollars. Urlovskis said that the credits are issued in Latvia according to Latvian Cabinet's regulations on usage of foreign credits given to Latvian Government, adopted February 1994. A special governmental committee controls the issuing of foreign credits. Urlovskis said that the Finance Ministry has drafted regulations on the choosing of auditor firms which could control the usage of foreign credits, given to Latvian enterprises.
