Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Washington, Jan. 24 -- Foreign Minister Andrzej Olechowski met Deputy Secretary of State designate Strobe Talbott in Washington on Monday [24 January] for talks lasting 45 minutes. After the meeting Olechowski said that Poland would be the first country of Central and Eastern Europe to be visited by a western delegation of technical experts in order to prepare the ground for Polands participation in NATOs "Partnership for Peace" programme for cooperation with those countries. Olechowski said the mission would probably arrive in Poland next week. Olechowski had arrived in Washington on Sunday evening to hold a series of talks with representatives of the Clinton administration and congressmen on Monday and Tuesday. Meeting with U.S. politicians, Olechowski is expected to discuss such key issues as Poland's participation in the "Partnership for Peace" programme, security and Polish-U.S. bilateral relations. From Washington Olechowski is scheduled to go on a tour of four U.S. cities to meet with businessmen whom he will try to encourage to do business with Polish partners.
