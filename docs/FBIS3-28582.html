Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Text] With the 11 February Japan-U.S. summit near at hand, a senior Foreign Ministry official commented on the outlook for the Japan-U.S. economic framework talks, which will become the focal point of the upcoming summit. On 7 February, the official stated: "The atmosphere surrounding the ongoing economic framework talks will be greatly changed depending on whether Japan can indicate its macroeconomic policies during the upcoming summit meeting." Thus, he expressed concern over the situation in which the government and the ruling parties are facing difficulties in working out plans to implement income tax cuts. Moreover, in view of the worsening situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina and the deadlocked talks between the DPRK (North Korea) and the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) over the former's suspected nuclear program, the official indicated: "At the summit meeting, measures to deal with the former Yugoslavia and North Korea issues will be discussed in addition to the economic framework talks."
