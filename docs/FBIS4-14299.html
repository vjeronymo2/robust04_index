BFN [Text] Cairo, 28 Apr (MENA)--Rwanda's Trade, Industry, and Crafts Minister, Justin Mugenzi, arrived in Cairo early this morning on a visit to Egypt of several days. The Rwandan minister said he would meet President Husni Mubarak, in his capacity as chairman of the OAU, to discuss the current critical situation in Rwanda following the death of Rwandan President Juvenal Habyarimana and the attacks carried out by the rebel Rwandan National Front. The Rwandan minister said he would also explain to President Mubarak the failure to honor the Arusha Peace Agreement signed on 4 August 1993 and the violation of international accords and the principle of good-neighborliness by our neighboring state of Uganda. He noted that what matters now is for the international public and the OAU to realize the role the Ugandan president is playing toward Rwanda and the extent of his material and moral influence. The Rwandan president explained that he will brief President Mubarak on the UN role in supervising the implementation of the peace agreements in his country and will discuss the effects the UN Security Council resolutions will have on the UN mission in Rwanda. He said he will seek President Mubarak's help to stop the violence and massacres in his country and restore stability and security.
