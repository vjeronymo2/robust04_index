Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, February 21 (XINHUA) -- China's president, Jiang Zemin, met visiting Italian Senate President Giovanni Spatolini here this morning. During the meeting at Zhongnanhai, headquarters of the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party and the State Council, Jiang expressed his satisfaction with the smooth development of Sino-Italian relations. Jiang said that Sino-Italian ties are close, with frequent high- level contacts, adding that much progress has been made in bilateral cooperation in various fields including economy and trade. The Chinese president also extended his appreciation for the positive efforts the Italian Government has made in pushing forward the full normalization of relations between the European Economic Community and China. Spatolini said that Italy is very interested in China's economic development and Italian entrepreneurs have shown great confidence in investing in China. He said that one of the purposes of his current visit was to promote cultural exchanges between the two countries. Jiang said that cultural exchanges between China and Italy went back to ancient times and prospects for them were still promising. During his stay in Beijing, Spatolini received an honorary doctorate in history from Beijing University. Spatolini first visited China in 1985 when he was the Italian defense minister.
