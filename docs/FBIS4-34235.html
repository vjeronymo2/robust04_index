BFN [From the "First Thing" program] [Text] On 15 June, Cuba was officially appointed the seat of the Ninth Ibero-American Heads of State Summit in 1999 according to the closing document of the Fourth Ibero-American Summit just held in Cartagena de Indias. The next summits will be held in Argentina in 1995, Chile in 1996, Venezuela in 1997, Portugal in 1998, Panama in the year 2,000, and Peru in the year 2,001. Vice Foreign Minister Jorge Bolanos, who participated in the meeting of experts which analyzed the closing document, announced Cuba's formal request during a news conference on Saturday.
