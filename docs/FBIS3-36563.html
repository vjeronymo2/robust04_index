Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Nikolay Medvedev, head of the directorate in the Russian president's administration in charge of dealing with regions, believes that the task of establishing a presidential party is a topical issue. In his opinion, the party will play its part in the presidential elections in 1996 and help the voters to make up their minds. Medvedev said that President Boris Yeltsin is ready to form the party, and that the initiative has already brought about a response. An organizing committee of the Russian Presidential Party of Reformers has already been set up in the Siberian city of Irkutsk. The committee has proposed that the revival of the country, the integration with the international community, democracy, commitment to solving internal and external problems by peaceful means, as well as to private ownership and enterprise should be proclaimed as the new party's main principles and tasks.
