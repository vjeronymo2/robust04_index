940131 FT 31 JAN 94 / Malaysian dam project defended Malaysia has awarded a Dollars 5.6bn (Pounds 3.73bn) dam project in Sarawak state to a company headed by Mr Ting Pek Khiing, a local tycoon, despite environmentalists' warnings of ecological disaster, Reuter reports from Kuala Lumpur. Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad, launching the project, defended the government's decision and said it was vital for the country's energy needs. The project would generate 2,400MW of electricity a year, mostly for peninsular Malaysia, Mr Ting said.
