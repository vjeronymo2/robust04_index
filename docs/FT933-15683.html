930708 FT 08 JUL 93 / World News in Brief: 11 injured in Pamplona bull run Eleven people, including an American and an Englishman, were injured during the first bull running of the San Fermin fiesta in Pamplona, northern Spain. Six fighting bulls stormed through the streets of the city in what long-time Pamplona watchers called a particularly dangerous run in the nine-day festival, which has been held almost continuously since 1591 and was made famous by Ernest Hemingway's 1926 novel, The Sun Also Rises. The bulls that make the morning run fight and die in the evening bullfight. Tens of thousands of Spaniards and foreign visitors are expected at the eight bull runs.
