Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Colombian elections scheduled for 29 May. Conservative Party presidential hopeful Juan Diego Jaramillo has given up his presidential aspirations. Jaramillo has acknowledged most Conservative Party members support the candidacy of Andres Pastrana, though he has reiterated he will not endorse Pastrana. Jaramillo has concluded he recently declined an offer by candidate Miguel Maza Marquez to become his running mate. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 19 Mar 94 p 7A) Upon analyzing the results of the Liberal Party, PL, presidential primary election, opposition presidential candidate Andres Pastrana said on 14 March that one of his campaign's main objectives will be to lure 52 percent of PL members who did not vote for Samper. He said Samper was unable to garner 50 percent of the vote, because his program is incoherent. Pastrana concluded he will also concentrate on seeking the support of youths, who appear indifferent regarding the election. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 15 Mar 94 p 11A) Pastrana highlighted the differences on 20 March between his proposal and that of PL candidate Ernesto Samper to build a subway in Santa Fe de Bogota. On touring the capital, Pastrana said while he will build the subway by granting a concession to private companies, Samper will fund construction via new taxes and the privatization of public companies. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0130 GMT 21 Mar 94) Pastrana cited the need on 21 March to strengthen the Army and called on guerrillas to demonstrate a genuine desire for peace. Speaking at a rally in Florencia, capital of Caqueta Department, Pastrana said if guerrillas do not show their determination to attain peace, "the Armed Forces will be forced to enforce the state's obligation of guaranteeing Colombians' lives and property, as established by the Constitution." Pastrana pledged to offer credits to peasants to make them private entrepreneurs. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0130 GMT 22 Mar 94) Luis Fernando Ramirez, Conservative Party vice presidential candidate, has challenged his colleague, Humberto de La Calle, to a public debate to discuss ideas and the government program. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0130 GMT 21 Mar 94) Samuel Moreno Rojas, representative of the National People's Alliance, has said he will endorse Ernesto Samper in his bid for the presidency. Moreno has said Samper's political platform is fully committed to social investments. He has concluded Cusiana oil resources must be invested in the infrastructure to be able to better compete in international markets. Moreno Rojas called for the reactivation of the economy in the countryside. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 25 Mar 94) The 19 April Movement Democratic Alliance, AD/M-19, presidential candidate Antonio Navarro Wolff has chosen Indian Jesus Enrique Pinacue as his running mate. AD/M-19 suffered a setback on 18 March, however, after Representative Gustavo Petro resigned from the party, announcing he will not support Navarro in the presidential election. Petro said the results of the legislative election reflect the party's inability to differentiate itself from traditional parties. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 19 Mar 94 p 8A) Navarro Wolff said in Barranquilla on 20 March his running mate is the best alternative for Colombians. Meanwhile, vice presidential candidate Jesus Enrique Pinacue has said he favors the resumption of talks with the guerrillas. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 0130 GMT 21 Mar 94) Eighteen candidates have reportedly registered to run for president in the upcoming May elections. Among the most prominent candidates are: Ernesto Samper Pizano; Andres Pastrana; Enrique Parejo; Antonio Navarro Wolff; Miguel Maza Marquez; Gloria Gaytan; Regina Betancur de Liska; Oscar Rojas Mazo; Jose Antonio Cortez; and Guillermo Aleman. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Cadena 1 in Spanish 1800 GMT 19 Mar 94)
