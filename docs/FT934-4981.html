931201 FT 01 DEC 93 / UK Company News: West Trust to focus activities on food side West Trust yesterday announced an acquisition and a disposal to achieve its objective of transforming the company into a specialised, high-added-value food group and its virtual withdrawal from the textiles sector. At the same time West reported a jump in interim pre-tax profits to Pounds 172,000 (Pounds 25,000). The acquisition is of Drenning, whose Enco subsidiary is a wholesaler and distributor of Caribbean and other food and drink products. The consideration of Pounds 6.48m will be satisfied by the allotment of new ordinary shares and Pounds 1.52m in cash. The vendor will retain 638,736 shares with the remaining 14.8m being offered by way of rights to West shareholders on the basis of 11-for-20 at 32p. West also announced the sale of Ken Moore, its wholly owned lycra manufacturing subsidiary, to Ken Moore's managing director for Pounds 33,000 cash on completion. Ken Moore will also repay Pounds 117,000 of inter-company debt, the balance of which, expected to amount to Pounds 622,000, will be paid in instalments. For the six months to September 30 the pre-tax advance was achieved on turnover of Pounds 4.92m (Pounds 3.76m). Earnings per share amounted to 0.75p (0.19p) and, subject to the acquisition being completed, an interim dividend of 0.2p will be paid.
