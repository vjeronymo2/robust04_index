BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Viktor Shulman] [Text] St. Petersburg June 8 TASS -- "Peace is still too fragile and demands for the maximum results in the settlement of the six-year armed conflict are hardly acceptable," Russian Ambassador at Large Vladimir Kazimirov, who has been acting as the Russian president's special envoy to Karabakh, said at the session of the CIS inter-parliamentary assembly. He was speaking in the discussion of the Commonwealth's peace-keeping missions. Kazimirov spoke after chairman of Kyrgyzstan's parliament Medetkan Sherimkulov reported on the work done by the CIS interparliamentary group led by him to promote the peaceful settlement in Nagorno-Karabakh. Sherimkulov said in his report that since a cease-fire protocol was signed in Bishkek, the warring parties have observed its provisions in the zone of the Armenian-Azeri conflict in the past four weeks.
