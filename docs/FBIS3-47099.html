Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, 8 Jan. (OANA-BERNAMA) -- The first batch of 450 men of the Seventh Battalion of the Royal Rangers Regiment (RARD) left here Saturday morning for a six-month tour of duty under the United Nations Operations in Somalia (UNOSOM). The men, in full combat gear, were seen off by Army Chief Gen. Borhan Ahmad and other army top brass. Their families were also at the Royal Malaysian Air Force Base in Subang near here to bid them farewell. The Mentakab-based rangers will form the Malaysian Battalion (Malbatt) II in Mogadishu, relieving Malbatt I which comprises mainly members of the mechanised 19th Royal Malay Regiment (RAMD) based in Sungai Petani. While the members of the 19th RAMD are returning, the rangers are being sent to the Horn of Africa in Batcheas from today. The next flight on the UN-chartered civil aircraft will be on Jan. 10.
