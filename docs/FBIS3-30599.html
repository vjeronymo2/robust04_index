Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Belgrade, Feb 7 (AFP) -- European Community mediator Lord Owen was evasive Monday on the possibility of launching air strikes to retaliate against the weekend mortar attack in Sarajevo saying that only "a fool" would want such action. "Nobody but a fool wants air strikes but on the other hand nobody but a fool can go on tolerating the situation in which as many people can lose their lives as it happened Saturday in Sarajevo," Owen said following a meeting with Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic. The mortar attack Saturday occurred in a crowded Sarajevo market killing 68 people and injuring about 200. Owen said his meeting with Milosevic centered on what could be done to avoid a similar tragedy in the future. He added that the meeting to be held Thursday in Geneva between Bosnia's three warring parties -- Moslems, Serbs and Croats -- "is not a meeting to discuss global peace settlement but to discuss confidence building measures." He said these measures would include setting up a "hot line" between the Serbs and Moslems that would lead to a demilitarization of the Sarajevo region.
