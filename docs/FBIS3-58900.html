Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- The return to Germany of the 1,300 Bundeswehr servicemen still in Somalia is to be accomplished by a combined air-sea operation for security reasons, German Defense Minister Volker Ruehe decided today, a ministry spokesman announced. The operation is to start at the beginning of February and -- just like the return of the Americans -- be completed at the end of March. The Defense Ministry's plans currently envisage first of all, transporting German servicemen from the Somali capital of Mogadishu by German naval vessels, probably to Mombasa (Kenya). The return flight to Germany will then be from there. According to the ministry, Ruehe intends to avoid the possible risks of transporting them by air in the Mogadishu area, and thus to aim for a higher degree of security for the servicemen. The plans were closely connected with the U.S. Government decision to transport its personnel initially by sea.
