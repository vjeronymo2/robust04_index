December 3, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Canada Is in Recession, Government Says: After seven years of robust growth, Canada's economy has officially slipped into recession, the government said.

It reported that gross domestic product, a wide measure of economic activity, fell for the second consecutive quarter -- Canada's definition of a recession. In the July-to-September period, the economy contracted at an annualized rate of 1%. In the second quarter, output declined at a 1.2% rate. Economists said the downturn is mild, however, and, barring a Persian Gulf war and further oil price increases, should end sometime next year.
