920515 FT 15 MAY 92 / Mitterrand in Lithuania Visiting French President Francois Mitterrand yesterday called the continued presence of former Soviet troops in Lithuania 'abnormal and shocking', Reuter reports from Vilnius. Mr Mitterrand, the most senior western leader to visit the Baltic states since they regained independence from Moscow last August, told the Lithuanian parliament: 'Fix a timetable, organise discussion, gain the support of the other democratic states of Europe and the solidarity of international conferences of free countries so that your freedom of decision and action can be fulfilled.' He said Lithuania's desire to join the European Community eventually was legitimate but added: 'Let us not speed things up faster than is appropriate. Let us plan the (intervening) time.'
