Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Windhoek March 10 SAPA -- A Russian cargo aircraft has been grounded at Rundu in northern Namibia following Namibian and Angolan claims that the medical supplies on board are bound for UNITA [National Union for the Total Independence of Angola] rebels in Angola. Police have also confiscated radio equipment from the aircraft, saying there are no valid transportation permits for the equipment. The grounding was authorised by the district commissioner of Namibia's north-eastern region, Mr Ben Haingura, after a request from Angolan authorities. The Antonov-26's pilot, Mr Oleg Pantiuhia, denied the claims of supplying UNITA and said his employer, Ecomex, supplied medical equipment to "Angolan civilians".
