February 11, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

HERSHISER'S DEMANDS TURN THESE FANS OFF

I've lost all respect for the uppity Mr. Hershiser. His demand for a salary of more than $2 million a year for three years is bad enough, but he also wants the Dodgers to promise to pay him if a labor dispute suspends play during the 1990 season.

The Dodgers or some other club will give him what he demands, and he will be counting his blessings while laughing all the way to the bank.

CARLA ROMERO

Newhall
