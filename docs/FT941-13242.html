940126 FT 26 JAN 94 / International Company News: Capital Cities surges 21% in quarter Capital Cities/ABC, the US entertainment group, yesterday reported a 21 per cent increase in fourth-quarter net income, led by a better performance at its ABC television network. The group reported net income of Dollars 166.7m, or Dollars 10.35 a share, up from Dollars 137.9m, or Dollars 8.38, in the same period last year. Revenues rose 7 per cent to Dollars 1.75bn. Broadcasting revenues rose 9 per cent. Revenues from the ABC network and other television stations rose moderately. However, at radio operations and ESPN, the cable sports channel, there were substantial increases. Publishing revenues were up 4 per cent on a like-for-like basis. The group's operating income rose 27 per cent to Dollars 302.5m, with the television network reporting a big increase in operating earnings. ABC's television stations, radio operations and ESPN also reported significant increases. Publishing earnings, however, were up just 1 per cent. For the full year, the group reported net income of Dollars 467.4m, or Dollars 28.53 a share, compared with Dollars 389.3m, or Dollars 23.45, in the same period of 1992.
