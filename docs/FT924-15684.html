921002 FT 02 OCT 92 / Survey of Software at Work - Autumn 1992 (40): Market-Base - Product Profile Supplier: Kensington Marketing Systems, (KMS) was founded in the UK in 1990 and privately owned. It has six employees. Product: Market-Base version 2.0 was launched in March 1992. According to KMS, it is a relational database which co-ordinates marketing activities from daily sales planning, recording and reporting to market segmentation and campaign targeting, implementation and analysis. It incorporates a letter writer and a powerful report generator. Number of users: 450 users in 38 companies. Hardware and operating requirements: any IBM compatible PC with MS/DOS 3.3 or later; any NetBIOS compatible network. Price range: Pounds 595 to Pounds 10,000. Sales and support: sales are direct and through selected value added resellers. Support is free for the first 12 months and subsequently for 10-20 per cent of the initial charge per annum; support includes free upgrades. Other services: training and bespoke software development. User views: 'A small company, willing to please. Hierarchical database allows unlimited contacts within company. Flexible product, can be tailored to the user's needs. Easy to learn and to use.' The standard report formats were unsuitable for the users interviewed; they preferred to build their own using the report generator included; limited ability to work with word processors.
