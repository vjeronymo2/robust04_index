920421 FT 21 APR 92 / International Company News in Brief COLGATE-Palmolive, the US consumer products group, produced a 25 per cent increase in first-quarter net income, from Dollars 91.3m last year to Dollars 113.8m, on sales which rose from Dollars 1.46bn to Dollars Dollars 1.6bn, writes Martin Dickson. Earnings per share rose from 65 cents to 74 cents. The company attributed the profits advance to unit volume growth, manufacturing efficiencies and a more profitable business mix. ***** AMERICAN Airlines, the large US carrier which provoked an industry-wide response when it overhauled its domestic fare structure two weeks ago, announced yesterday it was reducing fares further in an effort to beat competitors, writes Nikki Tait. It is converting its 21-day advance fare to a 14-day advance purchase fare; reducing coach fares in 1,500 domestic markets and cutting some of the 14-day fares to match competitors' discounts. ***** UNITED Technologies, the US aerospace and engineering group, yesterday reported first-quarter net income of Dollars 109m, or 77 cents a share, up from Dollars 40m, or 25 cents, in the same period of last year, writes Martin Dickson. Revenues totalled Dollars 5.2bn, against Dollars 4.8bn. Contributory factors included the group's restructuring programme and higher deliveries of aircraft engine spares from its Pratt & Whitney subsidiary. ***** LOCKHEED, the US defence and aerospace group, made an after-tax profit of Dollars 66m in the first quarter, up from Dollars 54m a year ago, Nikki Tait reports. First-quarter sales were marginally lower at Dollars 2.23bn. ***** GILLETTE, the Boston-based shaving products group, reported after-tax profits of Dollars 129.4m in the first three months of 1992, compared with Dollars 107.2m a year earlier, Nikki Tait reports. Sales were Dollars 1.21bn, up from Dollars 1.11bn.
