Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pusan, Jan. 15 (YONHAP) -- The Pusan Waterworks Agency reported on Saturday that the water quality of its water treatment plants has been restored to pre-pollution normalcy. The report said the ammonia and nitrogen content stood at 0.45ppm in treated water at Toksan water treatment plant and 0.48ppm at Hwamyong plant, respectively, Saturday morning, below the tolerable level of 0.5ppm. A public uproar erupted on Jan. 8 after toxic smell was smelt and a high content of hazardous substances was detected at piped-water in the Pusan area. The waterworks agency also said no more benzene and toluene were detected at Mulkum and Maeri water intake yards or in piped-water in five separate areas in Pusan on Friday.
