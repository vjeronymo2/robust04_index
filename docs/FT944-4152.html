941209 FT 09 DEC 94 / UK Company News: Sep rises 81% and makes Dutch buy Sep Industrial Holdings, the engineering group, reported an 81 per cent rise in full-year pre-tax profits and announced the acquisition of Fastening Driebergen of the Netherlands for Pounds 1.28m in shares and cash. Sep estimates that Fastening Driebergen, which makes fasteners and fixings, will incur a trading loss of Pounds 146,000 on turnover of Pounds 2.9m for 1994. Net assets at end-1993 stood at Pounds 698,000. Exceptional profits of Pounds 689,000 from disposal of assets helped lift Sep's pre-tax outcome from Pounds 2.08m to Pounds 3.77m for the year to September. Sales rose to Pounds 43.3m (Pounds 37.7m). Earnings per share including exceptionals were 4.03p (2.87p). A final dividend of 0.6p (0.45p) gives a total of 1.05p (0.8p).
