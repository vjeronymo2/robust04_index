920925 FT 25 SEP 92 / Observer: Nouveau which? Naturally, if you happen to head a polytechnic apotheosising itself into a university, the critical issue is which title to award yourself. After all, while 'director' might have befitted a poly, it's far too plain a style for the newly grand. 'We had to have a vice-chancellor to show we were doing the whole thing seriously,' says a 'deputy vice-chancellor' at the University of Greenwich (alias Thames Polytechnic). In the event, most of Britain's former polys have likewise adorned themselves with V-Cs although a few have plumped for Euro-appeal with rectors. But neither such style is good enough for Chris Price, the former Labour MP who runs what is now Leeds Metropolitan University. 'Americans only understand presidents,' he contends. So he is about to become one.
