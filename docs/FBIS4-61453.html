BFN [Text] The state of emergency declared in Kazakhstan's capital Almaty on April 29 due to the shortage of fuel was lifted on 4 May. As the Deputy Mayor Yakov Zayts, who leads a commission on emergencies, has told INTERFAX, there was fuel rationing in the city on 29 April for all enterprises and organizations not involved in vital urban services. In his words, the city's administration were forced to take extreme measures because of poor oil supply, slashed because of the consumer debt. He reported that the debts had been partially repaid at the expense of credits. According to his information, a number of state oil refineries have already sent about 3000 tonnes of gasoline to Almaty. He noted that "a small fuel reserve" has been created in the city, and "the situation is returning to normal."
