BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 15 KYODO -- A delegation from Japan's No. 2 opposition party, the Social Democratic Party (SDP) [Social Democratic Party of Japan -- SPJ], flew to Beijing on Sunday [15 May] for an eight-day visit to mark the 10th anniversary of exchanges with China's Communist Party. SDP General Secretary Wataru Kubo is leading the mission. During their stay in China through May 22, Kubo and his party will meet with Chinese officials on Japan's political scene and North Korea's suspected nuclear weapons program, party officials said.
