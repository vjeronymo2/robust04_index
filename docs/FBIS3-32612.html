Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Quotation marks as published] [Excerpt] Assuming his new post on 1 February, Lieutenant General (Air Force) Raul Sampedro, the new commander in chief of the Air Force, urged his subordinates to "strictly obey, without exception, the constitutional precept that bans them from any public or private act of a political nature -- with the exception of the act of voting." The Armed Forces, and thus the Air Force, are traditionally one of the basic guarantors of the electoral process, giving the citizens the necessary security to vote freely," he said. He added: "This is the way it is and this is the way it will be." He stated: "An essential condition for this is the fact that all military personnel on active service in the Air Force, from the commander in chief to the lowliest soldier, must strictly obey without exception this constitutional concept."
