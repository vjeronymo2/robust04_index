Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The speaker of the Russian State Duma Ivan Rybkin had a meeting in Moscow on Tuesday [1 March] evening with the leader of Bosnian Serbs Radovan Karadzic visiting Moscow now for 1 hour and 15 minutes instead of 20 minutes scheduled beforehand. The meeting behind closed doors was attended by the chairman of the State Duma's committee for international affairs Vladimir Lukin. As INTERFAX was told at the press service of the State Duma, during the meeting Rybkin "confirmed the position of the chamber that without Russia's involvement the settlement in former Yugoslavia is impossible". Karadzic told journalists that he expects the arrival of a Russian parliamentary delegation. He also informed about his request to send an additional Russian peacekeeping contingent to the Bosnian city of Tuzla.
