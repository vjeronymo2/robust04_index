BFN [Text] Every possible effort to prevent a tragedy like the October events in Moscow should for any politician of Russia be the paramount aim, the main requirement both of himself and of his comrades-in-arms. Russian President Boris Yeltsin stated this in an interview to the INTERFAX agency. There should be only one road to achieving one's political aims -- elections, the president noted. In his view, we are assimilating the lesson of the October tragedy, albeit with difficulty. Boris Yeltsin named as evidence of this the drafting of an agreement on civil peace. which an ever broader circle of parties and organizations are voicing the wish to join. Yet another lesson of those dark days is the vital necessity for the strengthening of the state, the president declared. Nobody should be allowed to destroy state buildings, mount assaults upon television, and form armed detachments. In the words of Boris Yeltsin, there is now the necessary minimum for strengthening the state. The Constitution has been adopted by the vote of all the people. The president noted that the new basic law had put an end to dual power and opened up a peaceful, civilized road to the resolution of contradictions.
