BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Ivan Ivanov] [Text] Moscow, 27 Apr -- Viktor Yerin, minister of internal affairs, said at a government session today that he is ready to tender his resignation "if my resignation is necessary in the interests of Russia and in the interests of the position of the government." He stressed he is ready to resign only if this is in the interests of state "and not in the interests of certain factions." Viktor Yerin made this statement in connection with today's demands voiced in the Duma by deputies. Some Duma politicians have appraised the murder of their colleague Andrey Ayzderdzis as proof that the Ministry of Internal Affairs is unable to curb crime.
