920103 FT 03 JAN 92 / World Stock Markets (Asia Pacific): Region trades quietly after closing gains of 1991 TRADING in the region was thin yesterday, reflecting a pause after the closing gains of 1991. Tokyo, New Zealand, Seoul and Taiwan were closed. AUSTRALIA ended slightly lower after swinging wildly in morning trade. The All Ordinaries index was 18 points ahead at one stage but closed a net 3.5 off at 1,647.9. Volume at 54.4m shares was the lowest in almost 12 months. Timber group Bunnings was steady at ADollars 4.00 after Wesfarmers launched an ADollars 163m takeover bid. Wesfarmers fell 17 cents to ADollars 4.90. HONG KONG ended slightly higher in the absence of any clear direction. The Hang Seng index inched up 4.45 to 4,301.78. Most actively traded were Cheung Kong, up 10 cents at HKDollars 19.80, and HSBC Holdings, unchanged at HKDollars 35.75. Turnover rose to HKDollars 1bn from Tuesday's HKDollars 910m. SINGAPORE closed weaker on slight volume, the first decline after six consecutive gains. The Straits Times Industrial index finished at 1,479.35, down 11.35 from Tuesday. KUALA LUMPUR was affected by the absence of buying by the government investment fund, which has driven prices higher in recent weeks. The composite index receded 4.06 to 552.16 in volume of 23.3m shares, against 64.7m on Tuesday. MANILA gained ground in a mood of general market optimism. The composite index climbed 20.91 to finish at 1,172.78, the highest since it closed at 1,173.59 on May 31. BANGKOK was disrupted by a computer breakdown whichsharply reduced trading volume. The SET index closed 0.66 up at 712.02 after turnover of Bt 1.63bn. JAKARTA ended little changed in very slow trade. The official index eased 0.44 to 246.95. Turnover was a meagre 1.67m shares, against more than 16m on the last day of trading in 1991. BOMBAY scored its second successive high on expectations of low carry-forward charges. The BSE index rose as high as 1,973.59 before finishing at 1,969.16, up 11.83.
