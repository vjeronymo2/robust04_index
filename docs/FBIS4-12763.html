BFN [Text] Reacting to the Hadera attack, President 'Ezer Weizman calls for national unity, for keeping calm, and continuing the peace process. In his opinion, the government should consider several additional steps that can ensure security. Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin also calls for national unity and for finding the unifying factors in anticipation of the hard times ahead. He adds that despite the bloodshed, Israel will continue extending its hand in peace. Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres says the Hadera attack will not halt the peace process or deter Israel in the pursuit of its goals. He further said that 'Arafat is not omnipotent, and there is no point in continuously demanding that he denounce terrorist attacks. Likud Chairman Binyamin Netanyahu blames the government for the rising terror and demands that the prime minister either discontinue the negotiations with the PLO or announce elections. Tzomet head Refa'el Eytan demands that the government stop the peace talks until terror stops. This is also the demand of YI'UD [Independent and Democratic Israel] head Gonen Segev. Meretz faction chairman Ran Kohen calls on 'Arafat and the Palestinian leadership to immediately condemn the heinous murder. He calls on Israeli opposition leaders not to disrupt the national unity of Remembrance Day mourning and not to exploit the attack for political purposes. The National Religious Party calls on the prime minister to resign at once. Minister Yosi Sarid says the government will do all it can to pursue terrorists, but it will pursue peace at the same time. The Arab Democratic Party and Deputy Minister Walid Sadiq condemn the Hadera attack. The Moledet faction calls for an immediate halt of the negotiations with the Palestinians and for banning their policemen from Gaza and Jericho. Police Minister Moshe Shahal promises to capture the perpetrators. He says the government will not rest until it brings peace.
