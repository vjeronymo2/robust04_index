930119 FT 19 JAN 93 / Moslem-Croat clash puts pact at risk Fighting between Moslems and Croats, theoretically allies in the war against Serbs, intensified in Bosnia-Hercegovina yesterday, overshadowing a crucial Bosnian Serb vote due today on an internationally brokered peace plan for Bosnia, writes Robert Mauthner. The battle around Gornji Vakuf, a town in south-west Hercegovina near the Adriatic, appears to have been provoked by the Geneva peace process itself, particularly the map tabled by the international mediators, Mr Cyrus Vance and Lord Owen. Bosnian Croatian forces have attempted to consolidate their positions in areas designated as Croat-controlled on the map which divides the proposed future Bosnian state into 10 semi-autonomous provinces.
