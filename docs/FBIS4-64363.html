BFN [Text] UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali will submit his report to the UN Security Council on the confidence-building measures [CBM's] in 10 days. This period will be used as a new silent deadline for the Turkish side to accept the CBM's. According to information from the Cyprus Broadcasting Corporation correspondent in New York, the reason for the new deadline is the secretary general's heavy work schedule. In reality, though, the new deadline is due to the weakness of the Security Council permanent members to agree on a common line. The silent deadline was decided by the ambassadors of the five UN Security Council members in Nicosia. Joe Clark, the UN secretary general's special representative in Cyprus, agrees with this decision in principle but appears dissatisfied, mainly about the tolerant stance that the United States is showing toward Turkey. Clark links the renewal of his contract with the outcome of the efforts at the end of the 10-day deadline. A UN official said he hopes the CBM's will be accepted while the report is being written, otherwise the UN secretary general will proceed based on UN Resolution 889. Clark met Robert Lamb, the U.S. State Department special coordinator on the Cyprus issue, in San Francisco last night and discussed ways to utilize the 10-day deadline as well as what must be done if nothing happens by the end of the deadline. A UN statement said that Clark and Butrus-Ghali discussed the preparation of the report and the future direction of UN efforts. A UN source in New York said that despite the failure of the latest efforts of the UN envoys, the United Nations will not abandon its efforts to implement the CBM's. The same source said it is almost impossible for the United Nations to wash its hands of a problem to which it has devoted so much time and energy.
