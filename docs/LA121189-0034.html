December 11, 1989, Monday, San Diego County Edition

SAN DIEGO SPORTS ET CETERA: STANFORD WOMEN RING UP RECORD ROUT OF SDSU, 109-45

Without much size or experience, San Diego State's women's basketball team posed little trouble for Stanford, the No. 3-ranked team in the NCAA. The Cardinal (3-0), which set one Pac 10 record and three school records, defeated SDSU, 109-45, Sunday in Palo Alto.

Stanford's Katy Steding led six players in double-figure scoring with a career-high 27 points. Val Whiting had a career-high 19. The 64-point margin of victory was a Stanford record, topping a 57-point victory over Wisconsin last season.

Stanford's 11 three-pointers tied a Pacific-10 single-game record. Steding had five of those for another school record.

SDSU (0-6) had just player in double figures scoring -- senior guard Julie Evans had 16 points.

TENNIS

Jeff Miyamoto of San Diego defeated David Bain of Newport Beach, 6-4, 1-6, 6-3, to win the Boys' 16 junior championship at the J.P. Yamasaki tournament at Santiago Canyon Tennis Club in Orange. Deta Huber of La Jolla won the Girls' 16 singles and doubles.

Other winners in the second-largest junior tournament in California included, Jeff Young of Escondido in the Boys' 14, and San Diego's James Conda and Reggie Buenviaje in the Boys' 16 doubles.

MEN'S SOCCER

Trong Nguyen, a University of San Diego midfielder, was selected to the West Coast Conference first team for the second consecutive year. Two other Toreros -- Jason Pearson and Tom Crane were named on the second team.
