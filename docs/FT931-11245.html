930204 FT 04 FEB 93 / International Company News: Allied-Signal ahead 33% in fourth quarter ALLIED-SIGNAL, the US high-technology company, yesterday reported a 33 per cent increase in fourth-quarter earnings, excluding special items, and forecast a 12 per cent to 18 per cent rise in 1993 earnings per share. The company has been enjoying strong profits growth since the arrival as chairman in July 1991 of Mr Larry Bossidy, who has been carrying out a major restructuring. Allied-Signal reported fourth-quarter earnings of Dollars 153m, or Dollars 1.09 a share, compared with earnings of Dollars 115m, or 83 cents, in the same period of last year, excluding unusual items. Including unusual items, fourth-quarter net income totalled Dollars 138m, or 98 cents a share. Sales totalled Dollars 3.1bn, up 2 per cent. For the full year. Allied reported earnings of Dollars 566m, or Dollars 4.02 a share, up 65 per cent on 1991's earnings of Dollars 342m, or Dollars 2.51, before unusual items. After special changes in accounting for retiree health benefits and income tax, the company recorded a 1992 net loss of Dollars 712m, or Dollars 5.05 a share. In the fourth quarter, income from aerospace business rose 17 per cent to Dollars 75m, mainly due to productivity improvements. Automotive was up 20 per cent at Dollars 36m and engineered materials was up 58 per cent at Dollars 49m.
