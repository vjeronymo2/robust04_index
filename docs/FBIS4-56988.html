BFN [From the "Union Radio News" newscast] [Text] The public transportation strike called to take place on Monday was observed throughtout the entire Caracas metropolitan area, even though the president of the Single Union of Independent Vehicles, the labor organization that convened the strike, reached an agreement at noon with the Transport and Communications Ministry on the payment of student subsidies. Simon Ladera, president of the Single Union of Independent Vehicles, met with Transport Minister Cesar Quintini Rosales and reached an agreement, supposedly to end the strike, but the strike occurred anyway. Ladera, after signing an agreement with the transport and communications minister stating that the public transportation sector would begin to charge the subsidized student bus fare rate starting this week, called on all labor union affiliates to return to work, but his request was not complied with. Only buses operated on Monday afternoon. The subway operated with extra units. There were many pirate taxi cabs operating as well. The public transportation situation in Caracas and part of the interior of the country is expected to return to normal on Tuesday.
