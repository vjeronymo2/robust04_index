940625 FT 25 JUN 94 / Letters to the Editor: Benefit of employee ownership Sir, Those who think reserving 10 per cent of the shares in a privatised Post Office ('PO staff may get free shares in sell-off', June 20) is a waste of scarce equity might examine the evidence in the US. Employees own an average 12 per cent equity in more than a quarter of the Fortune 500 industrials. This is not altruism. An index of 265 public companies where employees own at least 10 per cent of the equity, compiled by a team at Rutgers University, outperformed the Standard and Poors 500 Index by 26 per cent over 11 quarters to September 1993. Nigel Mason, Capital Strategies, 59 Charlotte Road, London EC2A 3QT
