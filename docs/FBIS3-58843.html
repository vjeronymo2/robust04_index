Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report signed "Fz": "Wehner Case: CSU Does Not Rule Out Investigating Committee"] [Text] The Christian Social Union [CSU] group in the Bundestag has requested that the GDR contacts of Social Democratic Party of Germany [SPD] officials Herbert Wehner and Karl Wienand be clarified. The CSU does not rule out establishing an investigating committee in this connection. In Bonn yesterday, Michael Glos, head of the CSU group in the Bundestag, said one must clarify the extent to which foreign powers had an influence on activities in the old FRG. Brigitte Seebacher-Brandt, the widow of former SPD Chairman and Federal Chancellor Willy Brandt, who died in 1992, disclosed notes of her husband that create the impression Mr. Wehner, who died in 1990, was involved in secret cooperation with Moscow and East Berlin. With a view to the "confessions" of young Wehner in Moscow, which have now become public and in which he denounced comrades in Germany during the Stalin era as deviationists, Glos stated that it had to be clarified whether the former chairman of the SPD Bundestag group was liable to blackmailing. Glos added that clarification was also necessary in connection with the suspicion that Karl Wienand, Wehner's long-standing Bundestag group manager, might have spied for the GDR or the Soviet KGB. Guenter Verheugen, federal manager of the SPD, spoke of a "campaign" that, considering the treatment of hitherto unpublished files, provided a foretaste of the 1994 super election year. The SPD's attention was to be deviated from the election campaign and its party leadership was to be presented in a suspicious light. Former SPD Chairman Hans-Jochen Vogel told Cologne EXPRESS that Brandt never accused Wehner of espionage. SPD Deputy Klaus Lennartz accused Brigitte Seebacher-Brandt of suffering from an image neurosis. Wienand rejected the accusation that, regarding Willy Brandt's overthrow in 1974, Wehner served the cause of the former Socialist Unity Party of Germany [SED] rulers or intercepted Honecker letters to Brandt. Wienand said Wehner, who was a member of the German Communist Party until the 1940's, used his former contacts to achieve progress in inner-German relations.
