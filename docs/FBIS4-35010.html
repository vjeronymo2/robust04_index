BFN [Text] Bonivert Claude, governor of the Central Bank of Haiti, denied the allegation that General Raoul Cedras withdrew $500,000. THE WASHINGTON POST had asserted that the head of the Haitian Army, Gen. Cedras, had withdrawn $500,000 from whatever strong currency remains in the Haitian central bank. Quoting government officials and members of Haitian banking circles, the newspaper explained that the reasons for this withdrawal were not clear. Claude exclaims: This is impossible! The general does not have an account at the Central Bank. Claude further pointed out that the bank is concerned with the currency problem affecting the Haitian financial system, and looks for a quick solution to this problem.
