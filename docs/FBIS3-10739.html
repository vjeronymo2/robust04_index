Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 10 March. The "Peru Textile Fair" ended very successfully in Tokyo, Japan. Several Peruvian textile industries displayed cotton and alpaca clothes, thus increasing the chance of exporting to Japan. The Peruvian industrialists representing 16 companies negotiated with Japanese wholesale importers, who were surprised at the quality of the products. A seminar also was sponsored as part of the fair in order to report on the characteristics of the Japanese textile market and to properly train Peruvians for future negotiations. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 24 Feb 94 p 2) The Chinese Bank has agreed to a $40 million loan to support social projects being implemented by the government. President Fujimori noted that the Chinese credit is one of the most advantageous ever granted to Peru because the interest is much lower than that of international banks. He said China has granted to Peru $55 million in credits and $16 million in financial aid in the past 12 months. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 23 Feb 94 p A6) Several specialists told EXPRESO that the Peruvian foreign debt notes suddenly dropped from 72 percent to 65 percent last week because foreign investors reportedly are starting to evaluate the impact of the norms on the exchange of debt for privatization. Most believe that those who bought 70 percent of the debt notes made a bad investment because Peruvian debt notes will continue to drop in upcoming days. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 24 Feb 94 p A24) The National Institute of Statistics and Information Science reported that the February inflation rate in metropolitan Lima was 1.8 percent, raising the cumulative inflation rate for the year's first two months to 3.7 percent. The GDP rose 9.7 percent in January compared with the same month last year. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 2 March 94 p A1) On 3 March, the Lima stock exchange hit a record $63.3 million as a result of the successful privatization of the Peruvian Telephone Company, which sold 74 percent of its shares. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 3 Mar 94 p B1) The Special Privatization Committee reported that the government has decided to accept $60 million worth of Peruvian foreign debt notes in the privatization of the Central Peru Mining Enterprise. The base price for the sale of the company is $280 million. The name of the owner of 100 percent of the shares will be disclosed on 11 April. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 3 Mar 94 p A23) The Central Reserve Bank's net international reserves rose to $2.968 billion through 22 February, $182 million more than in late January. It has increased $267 million so far this year. (Lima EL COMERCIO in Spanish 28 Feb 94 p B16)
