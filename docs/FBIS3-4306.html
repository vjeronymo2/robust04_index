Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 29 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-President Rong Yiren met here this morning with visiting Libyan Foreign Minister 'Umar M. al-Muntasir. Rong said that since the two countries established diplomatic relations in 1978, bilateral friendly relations of cooperation in various fields have developed continuously; the two countries share similar views on some important international issues; and they have cooperated well in international organizations, supporting each other and coordinating their steps in handling some issues. Rong said that China is ready to make concerted efforts together with Libya to further strengthen such friendship and cooperation. He expressed the conviction that al-Muntasir's visit to China will help further the friendship and cooperation between the two countries. Al-Muntasir conveyed the compliments of the head of state of Libya Mu'ammar al-Qadhdhafi for the Chinese Government and people. Al-Muntasir said that Libyan leaders are committed to strengthening and developing friendship and cooperation with China, hoping that such relations would be furthered. Prior to the meeting, Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen held talks with al-Muntasir on bilateral relations and issues of common concern. Qian said that since the establishment of diplomatic relations between the two countries, China and Libya have conducted effective cooperation in various fields, and China is satisfied with the development. He noted that China is willing to continue to develop friendly relations of cooperation with Libya on the basis of the five principles of peaceful coexistence. On Lockerbie incident, Qian said that China has all along been concerned with the issue. China opposes terrorism of any description and holds that international disputes should be settled through peaceful means. He said that China advocates for the settlement of the Lockerbie crisis through consultation and negotiations by parties concerned. "We disapprove of imposing sanctions on Libya, let alone imposing severer sanctions," Qian said. He pointed out that sanctions against Libya have brought about pains to the Libyan people and caused difficulties for Libya's neighboring countries and other countries concerned, thus intensifying the already strained regional situation. He said that China understands and sympathizes with Libya concerning the current conditions and does not want to see the situation worsening. He said that China appreciates the stand of Libya and the flexibility exhibited by it in settling the Lockerbie incident through peaceful negotiations. Qian said that China appeals to parties concerned to take measures to prevent the situation from worsening and promised that the Chinese Government will make its due efforts to this end. Al-Muntasir praised China for its important role in establishing a new world order, saying that Libya is to strengthen bilateral relations, coordination and cooperation in international affairs with China. He also briefed Qian on the latest development concerning the Lockerbie incident and Libya's stand on the issue, adding that Libya will apply negotiations and peaceful means to resolve the international crisis. Muntasser arrived here on March 28 at the invitation of Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen.
