921120 FT 20 NOV 92 / German government and opposition hoping for agreement on immigrants The German government and opposition hope to reach agreement this year on new rules to curb the flood of would-be immigrants, party leaders said yesterday. They agreed to hold two days of talks to thrash out a compromise later this month. The move has been made possible by this week's agreement within the opposition Social Democrats (SPD) to accept constitutional amendments on the right to political asylum.
