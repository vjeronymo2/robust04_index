940726 FT 26 JUL 94 / Arts: Today's Television The 25th anniversary of man on the moon has enabled the historians to take a more considered view of what now seems like ancient history. In Network First (ITV at 10.40) James Burke examines Apollo 13, the craft that nearly floundered in space when an electrical fault left the crew with just fourteen minutes of oxygen. They were forced to cram into their Lunar Excursion Module, but as the world held its breath they returned safely, just, and now tell their uncensored tale. Rugby has always been a national totem among whites in South Africa. A Whole Different Ball Game looks at how will it fare in a multi-racial democracy. Already 'development clinics' are turning out skilled black players who seem certain to become the Springboks of the future. (BBC 2 7.40). No one has stirred up psychology in the UK more than Hans Eysenck whose controversial views on such matters as inherited intelligence and the spuriousness of psychiatry have raised hackles in his profession. That some of his ideas come with impressive data makes him even more of a Heretic (BBC 2 10.00).
