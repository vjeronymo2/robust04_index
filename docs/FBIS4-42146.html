BFN [Report by Michael Kerbler] [Text] For the past 11 years a war has been going on in Sudan between non-Muslim rebels and the Muslim government. Over 1.3 million people have died in this war, more than 3.3 million refugees have left the country. Some 300,000 have found refuge in Uganda. For [Ugandan] President Museveni this is a reason to work out a peace solution for Sudan together with Djibouti, Eritrea, Ethiopia, and Kenya. The bilateral talks in Muerzsteg [Austria] might have given a new impetus to the IGADD [Inter-Governmental Authority on Draught and Development] peace initiative. President Museveni did not release any details about the talks, probably so as not to endanger the dialogue with Sudan. [Begin Museveni recording, in English with superimposed German translation] We have worked out a method to clarify open questions, including a peace settlement for southern Sudan. [end recording] And Sudan's head of state 'Umar al-Bashir adds: [Begin al-Bashir recording, in Arabic with superimposed German translation] In our talks we have agreed to improve relations between Sudan and Uganda and to remove all the obstacles to a peace settlement for southern Sudan. [end recording] On 18 July in Nairobi, the African IGADD states, Sudan, and the warring factions of the southern Sudanese resistance want to meet for fresh negotiations. [Austrian] President Klestil expressed satisfaction with the talks and he said that this proves that in spite of concentrating on the European Union, Austria has maintained a global outlook.
