920713 FT 13 JUL 92 / Construction Contracts: Hospital refurbishment in Weymouth WICKENS CONSTRUCTION, based in Chertsey, Surrey, has been awarded Pounds 8m work in five contracts. The largest, at Pounds 3.5m, is a three-phase 70-week contract for Warden Housing Association to refurbish and convert Portwey Hospital, Weymouth, into self-contained dwellings and to design and build a further 57 flats and houses. At Worple Road, Isleworth in Middlesex, the company has begun work on a Pounds 2m 75-week contract to build a primary school for the London Borough of Hounslow. The work also involves demolition of the existing building. The third award is a design-and-build commission worth Pounds 1.25m for a community day hospital in Cullompton, Devon for Mediken. Also design-and-build is the phase 2 development contract valued at Pounds 938,000 for 30 houses for Knightstone Housing Association at Totton, Southampton. The last is a refurbishment project worth Pounds 355,000 for offices at Fratton Road, Portsmouth for Portsmouth Housing Association.
