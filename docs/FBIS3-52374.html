Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Sanaa, 1 Jan (INA)--Dr. Muhammad Sa'id al-'Attar, Yemeni deputy prime minister and industry minister, has said he is optimistic that the current political crisis in Yemen will end soon, and that the country's economic situation will improve. In press statements published in the Yemeni newspaper AL-THAWRAH today, al-'Attar said the political crisis has affected the economic situation in Yemen. However, detente will come, he added. Al-'Attar disclosed that Yemen's foreign debts total $1.5 billion. This sum includes $4 billion [as received] for former southern Yemen and $1.5 billion for northern Yemen prior to Yemeni unity. The Government of Yemen is currently holding negotiations with former East European and Soviet states in order to reschedule Yemen's debts to them. He pointed out that, apart from these debts, Yemen only has very limited loans to repay. Al-'Attar criticized the policies of the Arab countries that invest funds abroad. He indicated that many international firms are operating with Arab capital. Trade cooperation between the Arab states and Europe has increased to 95 percent, while it is only about 3 percent among the Arab countries, al-'Attar explained.
