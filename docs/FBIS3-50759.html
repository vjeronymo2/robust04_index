Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 6 January. The Central Bank reported that the balance of payments in the year's first nine months registered a $166.7 million profit, including a $313.3 million deficit in the current account and a $480 million surplus in the capital account. The Central Bank's net international reserves rose $197.4 million from January to September 1993. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 23 Dec 93 p 47) On 22 December 1993, the Central Bank reported that the GDP increased 1.2 percent from January to September 1993 compared with the same period in 1992. Agriculture and livestock increased 1.2 percent; construction, 16.3 percent; trade, restaurants, and hotels, 8.0 percent; transportation and communications, 9.0 percent; and others, 3.7 percent. Fishing dropped 2.8 percent; manufacturing industries, 8.6 percent; and electricity and water, 6.6 percent. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 23 Dec 93 p 47) On 27 December 1993, the Central Bank reported that the Uruguayan trade balance registered a $72.3 million deficit in November 1993 with exports at $134.7 million and imports at $207 million. The deficit totaled $686.9 million in the past 12 months, with exports at $1.593 billion and imports at $2.2798 billion. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 28 Dec 93 p 52) The National Statistics Institute reported that the unemployment rate in Montevideo was 7.87 percent between September and November 1993. This rate is four percentage points higher than the previous quarter. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 31 Dec 93 p 47) The Central Bank reported that the December devaluation rate was 2.0323 percent and the accrued devaluation rate for 1993 was 26.88 percent. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 3 Jan 94 p 28) The Central Bank on 3 January reported that the public sector's net foreign debt was $1.8614 billion through 31 October 1993, a drop of 6 percent in the year's first 10 months compared with 1992. The public sector's gross foreign debt was $4.2046 billion in the year's first 10 months, an increase of 1.7 percent. Gross international reserves were $2.1787 billion through October 1993. The gross foreign debt in September was $4.2047 billion, a drop of $100,000. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 4 Jan 94 p 48) The consumer price index was 3.67 percent in December compared with the same month in 1992 when it was 3.28 percent. The accumulated inflation rate was 52.86 percent in 1993; in 1992 it was 58.91 percent. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 4 Jan 94 p 4) Inter-American Development Bank President Enrique Iglesias and President Luis Alberto Lacalle on 27 December 1993 signed a $38 million loan agreement to implement health projects in the Rincon de la Bolsa area, San Jose Department. Later they signed a $28 million loan agreement to improve the Labor University of Uruguay's technical education. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 28 December 93 p 4)
