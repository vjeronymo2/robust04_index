Language: Slovak Article Type:BFN [Text] Ivo Hlavacek, the director of the legal and consular department in the Slovak Foreign Affairs Ministry, handed over to Jenoe Boros, the Hungarian charge d'affaires, in Bratislava today a written statement in which the ministry expresses its concern over the activity of the Hungarian governmental office for Hungarian minorities abroad. The specific reason for the ministry's concern is that this office drafted and disseminated in foreign diplomatic circles information on the so-called Komarno initiative that takes matters out of context and, according to our ministry, is nonobjective and misleading. The Foreign Ministry said Slovakia did all it could to meet specific obligations it had accepted on joining the Council of Europe.
