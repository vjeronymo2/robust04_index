Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] In the event the Baltic countries or Latvia join the Partnership for Peace program and a real danger from Russia threatens its security, NATO will not only have consultations with the Baltic countries but will try to influence Russia to cease its aggressive activity, the delegation of the Latvian Defense Ministry was told today in Brussels by (Dick Zandie), spokesmen for the political affairs department. (Zandie) added that there are virtually no such threats from Russia in reality, and that much would be done for Latvia's security by Russia's participation in the Partnership for Peace program. In (Zandie's) view, cooperation with Russia would also be good for NATO. At NATO headquarters today, the Latvian Defense Ministry delegation met with two other NATO spokesmen. During the meeting they mainly discussed European security problems and the involvement of NATO in solving them.
