Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "News at Ten" program] [Text] His Royal Highness Crown Prince Hasan met Croatian President Franjo Tudjman after he arrived in Zagreb on a brief visit. Besides his meeting with the Croatian leader, Prince Hasan will inspect the Jordanian United Nations peacekeeping force in the former Yugoslav Republic, which has recently been beefed up. The commander of the Jordanian UN contingent, who is also a head of the UN peacekeeping mission, Ghazi al-Tayyib, and senior officers received the crown prince. His royal highness is accompanied by His Royal Highness Prince 'Abdallah Bin-al-Husayn and Minister of State for Foreign Affairs Talal al-Hasan. The Crown Prince told reporters he was glad to have a second opportunity to discuss with Tudjman efforts aimed at peace in former Yugoslavia: [Begin Hasan recording, in progress]...discussed with President Tudjman at the gracious reception the ongoing developments in the region. I would like to say how gratified we are to see progress in the ongoing talks in Vienna. We are deeply interested in the concept of a bicommunal community as a first step leading to further cooperation between Croatia and the Bosnian Muslims. And I certainly would like to say that the containment of violence is very much the role to which, we, through the United Nations, have committed ourselves to. [end recording] Prince Hasan also met the UN secretary general's personal representative in former Yugoslavia, Yasushi Akashi, and discussed the UN peacekeeping operation. The crown prince left Amman earlier in the day on a two-day visit and was seen off by senior officials.
