BFN [Excerpt] The People's Party's [PP] executive committee is this evening analyzing the results it obtained, after having tasted victory. The PP has said that it will wait until the government reacts and draws conclusions for national policy after what has happened in the European elections. The PP does not intend at present to submit a censure motion. A news conference was scheduled to be held after the PP executive committee meeting. Antonio Criado is at PP headquarters in Madrid: [Criado] The executive committee has been meeting for two hours, discussing the 12 June elections. Javier Arenas, who was a candidate in yesterday's elections, has just emerged and expressed his satisfaction at the result in Andalusia because he said that a political era has finally ended in Andalusia. As we said, this news conference has just begun. According to what RNE has been able to learn, the PP insists that results in the European elections are a clear response from the people and have domestic political consequences despite the European nature of the elections. The PP awaits the government's decision. They call on Felipe Gonzalez to take actions. In statements to RNE, Mariano Rajoy, the PP's deputy secretary general, said that it would be silly for the government to try to carry on as normal not only by not taking on political responsibility but also without even knowing if it had sufficient support in the Congress of Deputies. He highlighted several scenarios ranging from a no-confidence motion to the calling of general elections and Felipe Gonzalez's resignation. Regarding the presentation of a censure motion, which it is up to the PP to submit, he said that he is not convinced by this. [Begin Rajoy recording] I think that we as the party of opposition have to give an alternative and facilitate a way out of the present situation. I think that it is very difficult to facilitate that way out through the presentation of a censure motion that could have a political effect but that later in practical terms would come to nothing. Therefore, I don't rule out the presentation of that censure motion, but personally I do not think that it is the most correct formula to use to solve Spain's problems at present. [end recording] [Criado] Mariano Rajoy also said that Felipe Gonzalez's continuation as prime minister is now no longer positive or good for Spain's general interests. Rajoy will give an official news conference tomorrow to discuss what was said during the executive committee meeting. [passage omitted]
