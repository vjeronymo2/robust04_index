Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Text] The Ministry of Internal Affairs and the General Staff have not confirmed the reports that the Turkish Armed Forces have carried out a cross-border operation into Syria. Stressing that the question of cross-border operations is always on Turkey's agenda, Prime Minister Tansu Ciller has said: "However, the latest operation must not be regarded as one specially planned for Syria." Meanwhile, Internal Affairs Minister Nahit Mentese has also denied the reports on a cross-border operation into Syria. Stressing that the security forces launched an operation the night before last in the Suruc District of Sanliurfa, which is on Turkey's border with Syria, Mentese said: "The operation in Suruc is continuing. Suruc and Syria may have been confused. Six terrorists have been killed or captured in the operation thus far." Mentese also said an operation was carried out in Tunceli at about the same time. Five terrorists were killed or captured. The officials affiliated with the General Staff have said the operation in Suruc may have been regarded as one aimed at Syria. Stressing that a cross-border operation into Syria has not taken place, the officials said the operation in Suruc is continuing.
