BFN [Text] The 21-member Indian business delegation that accompanied the prime minister, Mr. Narasimha Rao, on his visit to the United States has expressed satisfaction at the outcome of their visit. The delegation comprised representatives from the Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry, the Confederation of Indian Industry, and ASSOCHAM [Associated Chamber of Commerce]. [Begin recording] [Unidentified correspondent] Indian businessmen have termed their visit to the U.S. as highly successful. They feel that the meetings with the U.S. officials and businessmen has helped them to have a free and frank exchange of ideas that will give a major fillip to existing bilateral trade ties. American businessmen have shown special interest in investing in the energy, communications, banking, and financial sectors. Besides areas of infrastructure and consumer durables, a major attraction for the U.S. industry is the large untapped market of 200 million consumers and a growing industrial sector with an insatiable appetite for technology and infrastructure. India's highly skilled class of English-speaking professionals and scientists was another attraction for the U.S. investors. [Unidentified business leader] The Confederation of Indian Industry signed a memoranda of understanding [MOU] with the National Association of Manufacturers of USA, and this MOU would now really formalize our relationship, which would address the subject of increasing business cooperation involving small and medium-size enterprises in U.S. because large multinational organizations are present here and are reasonably well informed, but the small and medium where there is a tremendous opportunity and this MOU would enable us to focus on that task.
