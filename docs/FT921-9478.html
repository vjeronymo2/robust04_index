920212 FT 12 FEB 92 / Arts: Today's Television Public understanding of Aids has been thwarted by the determination of so many campaigners to pursue political correctness rather than truth. If it is thought the facts may offend homosexuals or black Africans then everything is done to suppress the facts. Most recently desperate efforts have been made to conceal the latest figures concerning heterosexual Aids in Britain because those figures do not support the apocalyptic predictions of 1990 and 1991. On the contrary, they show that heterosexuals with the disease are still limited almost exclusively to those who have had sexual relationships with drug abusers, homosexuals and sub-Saharan Africans. One public information source with a good record of thinking for itself on this is Dispatches where two previous programmes made by Joan Shenton have questioned the received wisdom. Today's edition (9.00 C4) queries the efficacy of AZT, the main drug so far prescribed for the treatment of Aids. BBC1 begins a new series of QED with an international competition among engineering students to build waste collecting machines from junk (9.40).
