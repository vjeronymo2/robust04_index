Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Lefkosa [Nicosia], Jan 14 (AA) -- Prime Minister Hakki Atun of the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus (TRNC) said on Friday [14 January] that his country will announce its views on confidence building measures after discussing the issue together with Turkish officials. Prime minister of the coalition government and Democratic Party leader Atun, speaking after a meeting with Gustave Feissel, the United Nations deputy special envoy to Cyprus, said Feissel conveyed the UN Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali's recent offers on the issue. Atun said Greek Cypriots had a negative approach to the confidence building measures, adding that their negative approach affected the process of talks. Feissel said their aim was to provide a peaceful and lasting solution to the Cyprus problem. Meanwhile TRNC foreign minister who is also acting as defense minister Atay Ahmet Rasit criticized the coming of Greek commandos to the island saying Greek Cypriots' military initiations hampered confidence measures.
