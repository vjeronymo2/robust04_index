CSO [Editorial Report] Managua BARRICADA in Spanish on page 6 of its 27 April 1994 edition reports that the Bank of Nicaragua (Banic), which is near its lending capacity, sold to the Bank of Central America part of its 60 million cordoba loan portfolio in order to increase its loans to the production sector. Banic General Manager Esteban Duquestrada pointed out that while banks are allowed to lend up to 16 times their capital, the private banks are "generally reluctant" to grant credit. The sale of the loan portfolios is being financed by AID, according to the article. Duqestrada said that one of the drawbacks of the program is that state banks are losing their best customers to the private banks, which will only purchase the portfolios of the best loan risks.
