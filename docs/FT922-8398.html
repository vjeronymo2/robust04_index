920513 FT 13 MAY 92 / SDLP outlines Ulster proposal THE nationalist Social Democratic and Labour party has proposed administrative machinery run by six commissioners for Northern Ireland. Three of the commissioners would be elected by the voters of Northern Ireland. One would be appointed by the British government, one by the Irish government and the other by the EC. They would preside over a system of power-sharing between the four main local political parties. All sides involved in the round-table talks refused to comment.
