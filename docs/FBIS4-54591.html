BFN [Text] Sofia, April 29 (BTA) -- Iraqi government special envoy Abd-al-Amir al-Anbari today met with Deputy Prime Minister Evgeni Matinchev, briefing him on Iraq's compliance with UN Security Council recommendations for the reparation of damage caused by the Gulf War. Mr. Anbari is here to prepare a session of the Bulgarian-Iraqi Committee on Trade and Economic Cooperation. The visitor said Iraq was strictly compliant with UN requirements, yet the United Nations has not yet lifted the embargo inflicting irreparable damage on the Iraqi economy and people. He said the embargo has had an adverse impact on countries directly or indirectly linked to that war, including Bulgaria. Anbari said that after lifting the sanctions, Bulgaria and Iraq can resume cooperation in trade, construction and industry. Deputy Prime Minister Matinchev recalled that as a result of the strict observance of the four-year long embargo, Iraq has run up a $3,250 million debt to Bulgaria.
