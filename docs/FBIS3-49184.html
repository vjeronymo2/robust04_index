Language: Bulgarian Article Type:BFN [Text] On 5 January Zhan Videnov, leader of the Bulgarian Socialist Party [BSP], came to 134 Rakovski Street [headquarters of the Union of Democratic Forces, SDS], where he met Filip Dimitrov, chairman of the SDS National Coordinating Council. The meeting took place at the insistence of the BSP. After the meeting the two leaders made statements on the more important aspects of their meeting. First the host, Filip Dimitrov: [Begin Dimitrov recording] The first topic we discussed was the issue of the parliamentary order and the risks involved in violating this order if the parliament will be paralyzed until new elections take place. This could be a period of time during which Bulgaria would have a caretaker cabinet. It is well-known that the SDS submitted a proposal on amending the Constitution in this aspect. We also discussed the issue of the Supreme Judicial Council. The position of the SDS on this matter is very clear. We think that the BSP position on this matter is anticonstitutional, and I explained this to Mr. Videnov. The third issue he invoked was related to the differences of the two major political forces in anything related to the matter of municipal by-elections. The possibility of eliminating the differences was discussed. We think that not allowing parties that are not represented in the parliament, to participate in those elections is an illegal act. However, we will think in the future about the specific form of resolving this problem. Generally, I would say that the visit was an indication of the problems that are discussed or that exist in the BSP, and I think that the SDS could not be expected to get involved in those problems. [end recording] BSP leader Zhan Videnov said that he is satisfied with the meeting because the conversation clarified the positions of the two most important political forces in Bulgarian public life, and this according to him means regulating political life, imbuing it with a predictable and normal character. This is what Zhan Videnov said: [Begin Videnov recording] We discussed the necessity of guaranteeing the functioning of the parliament and the developments of the political life of the country, regardless of the shortcomings of the constitutional and legal arrangements. We also discussed the necessity of eliminating the existing normative and judicial obstacles that prevent the main political forces from fully participating in municipal by-elections. We made our proposals in this sphere and clarified our positions. Relevant information was exchanged between the main political organs of the two parties. We do not plan new meetings, but perhaps specific future parliamentary and political actions will emerge as a result of our meeting today. [end recording]
