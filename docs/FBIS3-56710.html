Language: English Article Type:BFN [Report by diplomatic correspondents Dmitriy Voskoboinikov, Igor Porshnev, Vitaliy Trubetskoy, and others; from the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature] [Text] Russia continues to actively cooperate with India's National Space Agency and is prepared to supply it with seven contracts, the Director General of Russia's Space Agency, Yuri Koptev, told Interfax. According to him, the $220 Mn contract signed with the former USSR remains virtually the same amount, even though Moscow had to give up the supplies of booster technologies stipulated in the contract. According to Koptev, Russia agreed to shoulder some of the commitments connected with India's space program. For instance, it is prepared to supply cryogen boosters (i.e. the last stage of the booster rocket) for India's Geostationary Satellite Launch Vehicles. In addition, Russia has signed a contract worth $28 Mn for the production and supply of equipment to service cryogen booster systems.
