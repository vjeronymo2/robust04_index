920527 FT 27 MAY 92 / World Trade News: Satellite TV accord near A convention on copyright law and neighbouring rights in trans-frontier satellite broadcasting moved a step closer to finalisation yesterday when an informal ministerial meeting of the 27-nation Council of Europe backed the broad lines of its text, writes Karen Fossli in Oslo. Ms Aase Kleveland, Norway's culture minister, said that the amended text of the proposed convention provided a basis for finalisation at a meeting of the Committee of Ministers in November. The purpose of the convention is to clarify the legal position of authors, composers, performers and broadcasters in the face of uncertainties stemming from the proliferation of satellite television broadcasting, the Council of Europe explained.
