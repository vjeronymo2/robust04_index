Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Paraguay monitored through 3 February; source is in parentheses following each item. The Central Bank reported that the public internal debt increased 21 percent in 10 months. It went from 1.1 billion guaranies in December 1992 to 1.3 billion guaranies in October 1993. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 30 Jan 94 p 17) The Central Bank reported that the debt owed to multilateral organizations, the Paris Club, and international banks decreased 1.8 percent in 1993. It dropped from $1.249 billion in December 1992 to $1.227 billion in December 1993. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 30 Jan 94 p 17) On 2 February, the Central Bank reported that the consumer price index increased 3.1 percent in January, compared with December when it was 1.2 percent. The inflation rate in the last 12 months totals 20.5 percent. Food, beverage, and tobacco registered the highest increase with 2.3 percent; housing, 6.5 percent; clothing, 0.5 percent; and other expenses, 2.7 percent. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 3 Feb 94 p 13)
