920902 FT 02 SEP 92 / House prices fall for third month HOUSE prices fell 0.2 per cent in July according to figures published yesterday by Nationwide. It was the third month in succession that prices had fallen, the building society said. Mr Tim Melville-Ross, Nationwide chief executive, said concern about the state of the economy was continuing to depress the market. He said that uncertainty over interest rate movements and the re-introduction last month of stamp duty would 'act as a further brake on the market in the short term'. According to the Nationwide the average price of a home in July was Pounds 55,328.
