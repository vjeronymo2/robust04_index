April 25, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

WORLD IN BRIEF;

ZAIRE;

MOBUTU PLEDGES MULTI-PARTY STATE

President Mobutu Sese Seko lifted a 20-year ban on opposition parties in Zaire and announced that he will let a multi-party government replace his regime in a year.

Mobutu, 59, has been absolute ruler since taking power Nov. 24, 1965. His government has come under intense criticism recently for alleged corruption. In a nationally broadcast speech, Mobutu said that he is setting up a transitional government to rule until free elections can be held.
