Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Guatemala City, 30 Jan (ACAN-EFE) -- Voting in the referendum held today in Guatemala to reform the constitution and replace the Parliament and Supreme Court concluded nearly everywhere in the country. It was characterized by high abstentionism and an absence of major incidents. The presidents of several voting tables in the urban and rural areas estimated the turnout at 20-25 percent at closure time. One of the first tables to yield results in Totonicapan reported, according to local reporters, 71 yes votes, 18 no votes, and 35 void votes, and was marked by very high abstentionism that was not quantified.
