Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Feoktistov] [Text] Ankara December 30 TASS -- A protocol on economic cooperation between Turkey and Russia was signed here on Thursday [30 December]. The document envisages cooperation in the power industry, transport, communications, construction, and new technologies. As regards the expansion of contacts, much importance is being attached to the private sector. During the talks in Ankara, the two sides considered the state of affairs not only in traditional areas of cooperation but in new ones as well. These include satellite communication systems, advanced technologies, and defence industry conversion, Valeriy Mikhailov, Russian first deputy minister of economics, who leads a Russian delegation, told ITAR-TASS. In future, it is essential to develop cooperation at the level of Turkish and Russian firms and in third countries, Mikhailov emphasised.
