Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Libreville, 26 Mar (AFP) -- On 26 March, official sources disclosed that Mrs. Pascaline Mferri Bongo, President Omar Bongo's daughter and former Gabonese foreign minister, was appointed permanent undersecretary to the Presidency in charge of francophone affairs by presidential decree on 25 March. Mrs. Mferri Bongo joined the government in June 1991, when she took over the post held by Ali Bongo, another one of President Omar Bongo's children. In the cabinet formed by Prime Minister Casimir Oye-Mba on 25 March, former Mines Minister Jean Ping became the new foreign minister. However, the title of his post is simply "minister of foreign affairs and cooperation," while that of Mrs. Mferri Bongo used to be "minister of foreign affairs, cooperation, and francophone affairs."
