BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 12 [date as received] (KNS-KCNA) -- A Tokyo metropolitan meeting of Koreans in Japan took place on June 11 to protest the Japanese authorities' unjustifiable suppression of the General Association of Korean Residents in Japan (Chongnyon) and national persecution. In his report at the meeting Pae Pyong-tu, vice-chairman of the Chongnyon Central Standing Committee and concurrently chairman of the Tokyo metropolitan headquarters of Chongnyon, condemned the Japanese authorities' suppression of Chongnyon and said all the branches, chapters, organizations and enterprises of Chongnyon should put spurs on the "each-visit-100 family drive" with this meeting as an occasion, going deep among the compatriots and uniting them and arousing them to the struggle to smash the crackdown on Chongnyon. Nachiko Aoki, vice-chairman of the Tokyo metropolitan headquarters of the Japan Social-Democratic Party and member of the Tokyo metropolitan assembly, and other Japanese figures in their solidarity addresses to the meeting vowed to strive actively to eliminate discriminations against Korean residents in Japan and prevent a recurrence of human rights abuses in the Japan society. Koreans of various strata under the Tokyo metropolitan headquarters said in their indignation speeches that they would firmly unite with compatriots and turn out as one in the struggle to safeguard the socialist homeland of chuche and the Chongnyon organization and defend the rights of compatriots under the wise guidance of the dear leader Marshal Kim Chong-il. A protest message to the Japanese prime minister was adopted at the meeting. Earlier, on June 10, a Kyoto prefectural meeting of Koreans in Japan took place to denounce the Japanese authorities' political suppression of the Kyoto organization of Chongnyon and national persecution.
