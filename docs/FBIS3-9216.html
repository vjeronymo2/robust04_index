Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, 22 March (MTI) -- "Integration is the demand of our age, and any country failing to answer it will inevitably fall behind," Prime Minister Peter Boross said at the Tuesday [22 March] plenary session of parliament, arguing for submitting Hungary's bid for membership to the European Union [EU]. "Hungary is returning to Europe. Although the EU is primarily based on common economic interests, the Hungarian Government is expecting the membership status to increase the country's security," Boross said. "In the long run, EU membership will eliminate the reason for concerns over the situation of Hungarian communities beyond the border, too," he added. The prime minister said that borders would become passable in the Europe of regions, neighboring peoples would be connected more directly, and the common European law would guarantee minority rights. Asked whether a referendum was intended to precede the admission, Boross said it would be due only after the issue getting on the EU agenda. Negotiations are expected to start in 1997, and there is a chance for full membership in 1999 or the year 2000. The current situation is favorable for submitting the bid, Boross said, because the EU states have just ratified the Maastricht treaty and can now turn to the new applicants. "We have been assured of Hungary's favorable prospects. It would be a mistake to miss this historical opportunity," Boross said, asking Parliament to authorize the government to make this step.
