BFN [Text] Ankara, May 18 (A.A) -- Parliament Speaker Husamettin Cindoruk who was in Georgia to attend the Black Sea Economic Cooperation Parliamentary Assembly (KEIPA) returned to Ankara on Wednesday [18 May] after concluding talks. He told reporters at Ankara's Esenboga Airport that he held positive talks with Georgian Head of State Eduard Shevardnadze and other senior officials. "KEIPA has started to reach results. Specially in the Azeri-Armenian conflict, we hope our propositions will help in its solution," he said. He added they were very well received by Shevardnadze and present bilateral relations are steadily developing.
