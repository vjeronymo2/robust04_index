Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] In a news conference that was called following the publication of a news article in EL TIEMPO yesterday, Cuban Ambassador Jesus Martinez Beaton said that "it is not true that the Simon Bolivar National Guerrilla Coordinating Board participated in a guerrilla summit in Havana" or that the Cuban Government has set up an office for the Colombian guerrillas in the Cuban capital. The diplomat repeatedly said that reports on the alleged office are "pure fantasy, speculation." He confirmed that the summit mentioned by EL TIEMPO was the Fourth Latin American and Caribbean Meeting of Solidarity, Sovereignty, Self-Determination, and the Life of Our Peoples, which was convened by the government and which was held in Havana between 2 and 28 January 1994. He said 25 Colombian delegates attended the event, of which previous editions were held in Quito (Ecuador), Santa Fe de Bogota, and Sao Paulo (Brazil). Some of them were: Guillermo Munoz from the AD/M-19 [19 April Movement Democratic Alliance]; Beatriz Zuloaga from Patriotic Union; Jaime Cedano and Miguel Gonzalez from the Communist Party of Colombia; and Manuel Rodriguez Garcia, labor union leaders from Telecom [Colombian Telecommunications Enterprise]. According to the list released by Ambassador Martinez Beaton, others who attended the event were: Alberto Rivera, Alejandro Motato, Ana Maria de Villate, Antonio Jose Villate, Antonio Morro, Fidel Castro, Francisco Carbajal, Isabel de Guevara, Jairo Guerrero, Jorge Ortega, Luis A. Garcia, Luis Eduardo Vargas, Luz Ramirez, Nohora Espinal, Oscar Garcia, Rafael Espinosa, Rafael Ivan Forero, Fierbo (sic) Tulio Casas, Teddy Reyes, and Victor Manuel Bautista. The diplomat said all of them are persons linked to labor unions as well as social and peasant organizations that operate in Colombia. "It is totally false that it was a meeting of guerrilla representatives; this is an event that is not secret and that is why our embassy processed the visas normally," he said. He said Cuba is not currently involved with Colombian guerrilla groups because "we have honest and sincere plans to achieve maximum relations with Colombia, and it is not our policy to interfere in the domestic affairs of other countries." Ambassador Martinez said these persons arranged their trip through travel agencies, but admitted not knowing if there is a Colombian bishop among those mentioned on the list because "we do not investigate each person." He said he does not believe persons other than the ones mentioned on the list traveled to Cuba without the Cuban Embassy's knowledge because his government has very strict security and control measures to determine who participated in the event. The diplomat insisted that "no meeting by Colombian guerrilla representatives was held in Havana; there was no church participation nor any attempt to discuss the internal situation of Colombia in the framework of the event or outside the event." Finally he pointed out that his immediate reaction to the report was due to the fact that "we have excellent relations with the Colombian Government and what appears to us is that there may be attempts to spoil this harmonious relation."
