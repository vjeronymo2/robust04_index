940128 FT 28 JAN 94 / International Company News: Results push McDonald's stock to 52-week high Wall Street celebrated McDonald's Corporation's Dollars 1bn 1993 earnings by pushing the hamburger chain's stock to its highest price in a year, Dollars 60. The world's largest fast-food company reported fourth-quarter net income of Dollars 264.5m or 72 cents per share, on sales of Dollars 6.1bn. That compares to net income of Dollars 227.9m or 61 cents per share on sales of Dollars 5.7bn a year ago. For the year, McDonald's had net income of Dollars 1.08bn or Dollars 2.91 per share on sales of Dollars 24bn, up from fiscal 1992's earnings of Dollars 958m or Dollars 2.60 per share on sales of Dollars 21.8bn. The company added 900 restaurants to its 14,000-unit chain during 1993. McDonald's does not disclose comparative results excluding new restaurant additions. Operating income within the US grew at a moderate 4 per cent for the year and 5 per cent in the fourth quarter, while operating income in McDonald's markets outside the US grew by 9 per cent for the year and 10 per cent in the fourth quarter. A large portion of the company's restaurant expansion occurred outside the US in 1993, leading to the higher overseas growth rate.
