BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Vadim Byrkin and Yuriy Tyssovskiy] [Text] Moscow, 4 Apr -- "The positions of Russia's Defense Ministry and Foreign Ministry with regard to joining NATO's Partnership for Peace program coincide," the Russian defense minister told an ITAR-TASS correspondent today. Pavel Grachev met a small number of Russian and foreign journalists after his talks with UN Secretary General Butrus-Ghali. The minister revealed that he and Andrey Kozyrev had jointly sent appropriate documents to the Russian president, who had expressed support for this position in conversations. "We hope that Andrey Kozyrev will sign an outline agreement on Russian entry in the Partnership for Peace program in Brussels in April," Pavel Grachev said. "On the whole, I see no disagreements on this issue in the government and in the presidential structures."
