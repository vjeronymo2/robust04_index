BFN [Text] On Monday the Russian Deputy Minister for Foreign Economic Relations Georgiy Gabunia told INTERFAX that Russia and the European Union have reached agreement on a draft treaty for partnership and cooperation and on Tuesday the document is expected to be formally approved by the EU Council. President Boris Yeltsin and the leaders of EU countries plan to sign the document in Corfu, Greece on Friday. According to Gabunia, the package of documents related to the treaty, including a joint declaration and letters of exchange, consists of some 100 typewritten pages. Under the treaty the European Union will recognize Russia as a country with a transitional economy, not one with a state-run system of trade. Gabunia said that talks on the agreement have been going on for two years. The section on trade in commodities has caused the greatest difficulties. The sides decided to offer each other most- favored-nation status in trade. The treaty has sections regulating business and investment activities, in particular the formation and operation of companies, the flow of capital and payments. It describes cooperation in finance, economy, culture and crime- prevention. The implementation of the agreement will be controlled by a Cooperation Council formed of high-ranking government officials: ministers or deputy prime-ministers. Under the draft the council is to assemble onece a year. Routine control will be carried out by a Cooperation Committee consisting of officials of a lower rank and experts.
