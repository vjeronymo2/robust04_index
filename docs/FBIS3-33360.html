Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Response to Prosecutor General De Greiff's letter from the SEMANA editorial staff] [Text] With regard to the first information, related to the differences between the national government and the Prosecutor General's Office regarding the surrender of persons linked to the Cali cartel, those differences are part of the public domain and have been reported by various communications media. In the recent past, even high-level government sources from the Prosecutor General's Office itself have described in detail to this magazine discrepancies of this sort with the government regarding the case of the Cali cartel. In addition, the week before last this disagreement was confirmed once again to SEMANA by a high-level source from President Gaviria's cabinet. With respect to the reports on differences between the Colombian Prosecutor General and his counterpart in the United States, these reports have been confirmed both by sources in the U.S. Government and by Colombian cabinet ministers. Thus, Mr. Prosecutor General, it is not a question of either inventions, or feverish imaginations, or irresponsible journalism.
