BFN [Report by Danko Peric from Knin] [Text] Lieutenant Colonel Marinko Gajic, assistant commander of the General Staff of the Serbian Army of Krajina, has said that members of infantry units had withdrawn by 0900. There have been minor problems in western Slavonia and the assistant commander of the Serbian Army of Krajina expects them to be solved during the day. So far, nothing has been heard from UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces], whose units are going to be deployed between Krajina and Croatian infantry units who have moved apart by one kilometer each. The first meeting of the central joint commission, attended by Lieutenant General Bertrand De Lapresle, UNPROFOR commander for the former Yugoslavia, is under way in Turanj, near Karlovac. The second meeting of the first session of the republican assembly is under way in Knin at the moment. It has been learned that the report by the negotiating team to the peace negotiations held in the Russian Embassy in Zagreb is also going to be discussed at the session. It has also been learned that the proposal on prime minister designate and the new government had been taken off the agenda and that this is most probably going to be discussed on 20 April.
