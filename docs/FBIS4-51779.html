BFN [Text] Seoul, April 26 (YONHAP) -- The Korean agricultural team has gone on the offensive in trade negotiations. In recent talks, Seoul has bluntly told its trading partners to import Korean farm products or accept its export conditions, the Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Ministry said Tuesday. The ministry held separate quarterly beef talks with Australia, New Zealand and the United States this month and strongly urged them to carry forward this year's excess import volume to the 1995 quota. It reminded them of Seoul's concessions to beef exporters in a series of talks and the Uruguay Round negotiations and told them it was their turn to compromise. In a meeting with visiting Chinese Internal Trade Minister Zhang Haoruo on Monday, Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries Minister Choe In-ki pointed out Korea's growing trade deficit with China in the agricultural sector. Korea imported 1,055 million U.S. dollars worth of Chinese farm, fisheries and livestock products last year while exports only came to 35 million dollars, resulting in a 1.02-billion-dollar deficit. Seoul reportedly asked Beijing to import more Korean processed farm goods, vegetables and apples. Choe, meeting his Canadian counterpart Ralph Goodale last Saturday, strongly requested that the North American country lift a ban on Korean apples. Goodale, in turn, asked Korea to purchase 300,000 tons of feed barley and lower tariffs on rape imports. Seoul refused, pointing out that barley was inefficient as feed compared with other grains and because Korea would nevertheless have to import barley from next year under the UR agreement. It also explained that it could not lower the tariff on rape because rape farmers on Cheju island would be hurt. Canada, the world's largest barley grower, has been persistent in trying to sell its products to Korea.
