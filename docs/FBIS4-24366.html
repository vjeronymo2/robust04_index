BFN [Text] Beijing, May 27 (XINHUA) -- An International Academy of Management conference was held here this morning, focusing on economic reforms in China. Pedro Nueno, chancellor of the International Academy of Management, and Jing Shuping, managing director of the China International Trust and Investment Corporation and president of the All-China Federation of Industry and Commerce, made opening speeches. This is the academy's first conference in China. It has been sponsored by the International Academy of Management, China International Economic Consultants Ltd., and the China-Europe Management Institute. Representatives from 11 countries at the gathering are discussing such issues as China's current economic reform policy, securities, using overseas investments, and financial, taxation and state-owned enterprises reforms. The International Academy of Management, set up in 1958, has 232 members from 34 countries. The current conference is aimed to promote the academy's study of China's economic reforms and development.
