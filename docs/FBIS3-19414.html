Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Madrid, March, 16, IRNA -- Visiting Iranian Deputy Foreign Minister Mahmud Va'ezi and Deputy Minister of Transport and Environment Antonio Llardan have discussed ways of speeding up the establishment of Madrid-Tehran air link and finalizing a surface transport agreement between the two countries. In a meeting held here Tuesday night [15 March], the two officials described as "friendly" bilateral relations and explored new avenues for expansion of mutual relations in various fields. The Spanish official announced Madrid's readiness for participating in various Iranian economic projects. Outlining economic and commercial policies of Iran, Va'ezi, welcomed Madrid's willingness to help in implementing economic projects in Iran. Also on Tuesday, the Iranian official held talks with Spanish Foreign Ministry Director General Francisco Villar on issues of mutual interests as well as regional and international crises. Calling for promotion of Tehran-Madrid cooperation, Villar said the full implementation of agreements already reached between Iran and Spain could pave the way for expansion of bilateral cooperation, particularly in economic area. Expressing hope that the second session of Iran-Spain joint economic commission would be held in Tehran in a near future, he stressed the need for providing further facilities for Spanish investors to invest in Iran. Va'ezi announced Iran's readiness to upgrade ties with Spain, noting that there was no restriction on expansion of bilateral cooperation. The two also discussed the crises in Bosnia-Herzegovina, Russia, Iraq, Central Asia and the Middle East.
