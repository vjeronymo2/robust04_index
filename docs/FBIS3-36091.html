Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Nekrasova] [Text] Lvov February 3 TASS--The Ukrainian Procurator-General's Office accepted for examination a message by legislator Khmara and some of his colleagues where they demand that a criminal case be instituted against acting Premier Yukhym Zvyahilskyy who permitted 17 leading engineering factories to ship their products to countries of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) without preliminary payment. In legislators' opinion, this is "an illegal order inflicting material damage on Ukraine". The difficult economic situation in the CIS countries has brought about a situation when some enterprises and organisations went bankrupt and did not pay for delivered goods or paid with great delays. Former Ukrainian Premier Leonid Kuchma forbid to ship goods to former Soviet republics without preliminary payment or without guarantees by a foreign bank which could vouch for payments in time. But, six months later, Zvyahilskyy signed a decision permitting the Kiev Elektronmash, Kharkov bicycle factories and other leading engineering enterprises to deliver goods to the CIS countries without the confirmation of preliminary payments.
