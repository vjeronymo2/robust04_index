910429 FT 29 APR 91 / UK News (Employment): Most Tecs favour partnership for Careers Service MOST TRAINING and Enter-prise Councils are understood to have told the government that the Careers Service, which provides advice and guidance to young people, should be administered in partnership arrangements between Tecs and the local education authorities, which now run them. A consultation exercise between the Department of Employment, Tecs and local education authorities ended on Friday. The Labour-dominated Association of London Authorities has said the exercise was 'rushed and inadequate' and has rejected the proposed options. It said the service should remain under LEAs' control with local advisory committees of all interested parties. The Department of Employment has been considering the future of the service for some months. Mr Michael Howard, employment secretary, gave three options in his consultation document: Tecs should acquire full responsibility for providing the Careers Service Education-business partnerships, jointly operated by LEAs and Tecs, should be responsible The service should be contracted out through competitive tender. Mr Howard said the Careers Service, in place since 1974, had done well under the circumstances in which it had operated. 'Times, however, have changed,' he said. A majority of Tecs is understood to prefer partnership arrangements with LEAs. Some, though, are not sure that existing business-education partnership arrangements are the right mechanism. Mr Michael Hanson, chief executive of South Thames Tec, said: 'We favour a partnership with local education authorities but we are still agnostic over whether the education and business partnership is the best mechanism.' Mr Jon Power, chief executive of Sheffield Tec, said he was concerned about the lack of detail in the consultation exercise. 'My concern about a partnership arrangement is that it has to be robust enough to safeguard the service.'
