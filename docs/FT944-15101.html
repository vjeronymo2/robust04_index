941020 FT 20 OCT 94 / World Trade News: Thai price curb annoys Nestle Thailand has put instant coffee on a list of products subject to price control, prompting veiled suggestions from Nestle, the Swiss foods multinational, that foreign investors would take offence at such a backward step on the road to free enterprise. Nestle, whose Thai subsidiary Quality Coffee Products has more than 80 per cent of the local instant coffee market, angered the government by raising prices by 10 per cent in July and by refusing commerce ministry demands to reverse the price increase. This week the cabinet authorised the ministry to put instant coffee on the list of controlled consumer products. It had been removed two years ago along with 100 other products as part of an economic liberalisation drive.
