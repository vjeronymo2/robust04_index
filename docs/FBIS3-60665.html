Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pakistan has expressed the hope that a rewrite of the American Foreign Assistance Act to be undertaken by Congress will further the objectives of nuclear nonproliferation jointly shared by the United States and Pakistan. A Foreign Office spokesman said in Islamabad today that Pakistan views the Pressler Amendment as a discriminatory law. He said Senator Pressler is likely to visit Pakistan by the middle of this month, and Pakistan will try to put across its point of view on the discriminatory nature of this law. Replying to a question, the spokesman said Pakistan is continuously negotiating with different governments on different means to achieve the objectives of nonproliferation in South Asia,. He, however, said Pakistan has not received any specific proposal from the United States recently.
