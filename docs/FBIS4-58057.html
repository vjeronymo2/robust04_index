BFN [Report by 'Abdallah Hammudah, Lutfi Shatarah, Hammud Munsir, and Salwa al-Ustuwani: "Southern Forces Prepare To Launch Revenge Attack After 100 People Are Killed in 'Imran Fighting"] [Excerpts] London, Sanaa, Damascus -- Military confrontations between northern and southern forces in Yemen entered a serious stage yesterday after more than 100 people were killed from the two sides, among them 20 civilians, and 200 wounded, and after forces moved from northern and southern camps and set up posts in the areas adjacent to their camps. A state of maximum alert has been announced among southern forces in Aden, including the air force, in what has been described as preparation for launching a revenge attack within 24 hours. The security situation has become more serious following the attempt in Sanaa to assassinate Dr. Hasan Makki, first deputy prime minister and member of the General People Congress [GPC] General Secretariat (political bureau). [passage omitted] At the military level, Yemeni sources said that the continuing tank battles between northern and southern forces in 'Imran, north of the capital, Sanaa, presage an expansion of the fighting to all Yemeni governorates, especially as military reports confirmed to AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT yesterday that the forces present in all northern and southern camps in the former fringes area have moved from their barracks and set up posts in the areas adjacent to their camps. Authoritative sources in Aden have said that all camps in the city, including the air force, are on maximum alert. The sources expect southern forces to launch a revenge attack within the coming 24 hours on northern forces in some southern governorates. The sources accused Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih of "wanting to eliminate the southern brigades stationed in the north. So it is difficult to return the northern forces based in the south peacefully." [passage omitted] In a statement to AL-SHARQ AL-AWSAT yesterday former South Yemen President 'Ali Nasir Muhammad urged the political leadership and conflicting Yemeni parties to take steps to quickly contain the crisis and stop the explosion which could lead to disastrous consequences. He said: The political leadership must stop evading the crushing crisis Yemen is experiencing. He also said: The fighting that broke out among comrades in arms is unjustifiable, because there can be no victors in this fighting. President 'Ali Nasir appealed to the Armed Forces "not to aim their weapons against one another, in order to spare blood and keep the Armed Forces strong and coherent so they can protect and safeguard the homeland." He urged all parties to adhere to the Pledge and Accord Document. Former President 'Ali Nasir Muhammad stressed that the solution to this crisis should be Yemeni and that others' task should be to help only. President 'Ali Nasir Muhammad revealed an initiative he submitted recently to President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid, saying that this initiative can form a prelude to a dialogue and breakthrough in the Yemeni situation. The former president praised Arab efforts, especially those being made by Egypt and the United Arab Emirates and some other Arab brothers. He said: Everyone in Yemen is responsible for this conflict; no one can be cleared. And they must rescue Yemen and the Yemeni people.
