Language: Belarusian Article Type:BFN [Text] As BELAPAN has learned, U.S. President Bill Clinton will pay a visit to Belarus on 16 January 1994. During the visit, Belarusian and U.S. officials are expected to specify their positions on the issue of security guarantees for Belarus, which has implemented its constitutional law to become a neutral and nonnuclear state. Although it is unlikely that such guarantees will be announced on 16 January, in a conversation with BELAPAN, [Supreme Soviet Chairman] Stanislav Shushkevich said that he will present, within the framework of Bill Clinton's Partnership for Peace initiative, Belarus' strategy for establishing a belt of neutral countries in Central Europe. Problems connected with the protection of U.S. investments in Belarus will also be discussed during the negotiations.
