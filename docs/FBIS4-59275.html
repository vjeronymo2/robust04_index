BFN [Text] Ahmad Quray', alias Abu-'Ala, director of the PLO's economic affairs and planning department, has said that Jordanian and Palestinian delegations held intensive economic talks yesterday. The talks focused deeply and comprehensively on all the details of every aspect of Jordanian-Palestinian economic relations and the Palestinian-Israeli economic agreement, recently signed in Paris. In a statement after the talks, which lasted for five hours, Brother Abu-'Ala said that it was stressed that the Jordanian-Palestinian economic agreement signed in Amman early this year is the basis for the Jordanian-Palestinian relationship. He pointed out that discussions on bilateral issues are still under way and will be resumed today. Brother Abu-'Ala said that no changes will be made to the bases and principles of the Jordanian-Palestinian economic agreement. Discussions of the issues of banks, trade, currency, and customs will be continued.
