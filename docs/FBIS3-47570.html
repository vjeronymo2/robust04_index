Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 13 KYODO -- A major Vietnamese trade exposition, the first of its kind ever held in Japan, will open in Tokyo on January 19, officials of the Japan External Trade Organization (JETRO) said Thursday [13 January]. Officials said the exposition, aimed at enhancing trade ties between the two countries, will be sponsored by Jetro and the Vietnam Chamber of Commerce and Industry and supported by both the Japanese and Vietnamese Governments. It will last through January 27 and precede the holding of a Japanese industrial product trade fair in Hanoi next March. Representatives from 25 Vietnamese companies will be exhibiting such potential export items as marine products, coffee, handicrafts, furniture and clothing. According to Finance Ministry figures, Vietnamese exports to Japan have skyrocketed in recent years, from 196 million dollars in 1988 to 870 million dollars in 1992. Vietnam had a whopping trade surplus with Japan of 419 million dollars in 1992. Mineral fuels accounted for 62.2 percent of Japanese imports from Vietnam in that year, followed by foodstuffs and industrial products.
