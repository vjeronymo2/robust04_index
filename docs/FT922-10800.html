920429 FT 29 APR 92 / MPs urge investigation by MMC MPs from all parties yesterday called for a Monopolies and Mergers Commission investigation into the projected Lloyds Bank bid for Midland. The Labour party urged the commission to consider both the Lloyds bid and the agreed offer by Hongkong and Shanghai Bank with particular emphasis on the future of the workforce. Most Tories believe a Lloyds bid should be referred. The main banking union, the Banking, Insurance and Finance Union, promised to fight a Lloyds takeover. Large companies, however, welcomed the latest planned bid. The chief executive of a large textile company said: 'This should have happened years ago. Any reduction in competition will not be damaging.' Smaller companies were concerned about the reduction of choice if there were only three big banks instead of four. Mr Jonathan Fowler, chairman of Chinecrest Archives, a Pounds 1m-turnover company which stores documents for banks and insurance companies, said: 'The larger companies have the bargaining power to stand up to the banks but we have to go along with what they say.'
