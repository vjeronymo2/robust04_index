941027 FT 27 OCT 94 / Call to liberalise media rules Sir Frank Rogers, chairman of the European Publishers' Council, yesterday called for radical liberalisation of media rules in the UK, Raymond Snoddy writes. He warned that outdated rules were putting publishers at a disadvantage in investing in the multimedia revolution. He was speaking at the launch of a study which found that while newspaper publishers in the UK were prevented from owning more than 20 per cent of a television company there were no special restrictions on computer companies. The report on media regulation in the UK argues that rapid changes in technology have created anomalies. For example, the owner of a foreign newspaper controlled within the European Union can buy an ITV company but a UK newspaper publisher cannot.
