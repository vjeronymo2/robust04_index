Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] (DYN-NA) -- Former de facto president, General Jorge Rafael Videla, was acquitted yesterday on charges of promoting crime filed against the former head of the military dictatorship after he had said that the Armed Forces should have been paid, instead of having to pay the price for their actions during the Dirty War. According to the verdict issued by Judge Fernando Larrain, Videla did not intend to make his opinions public because they came in the context of a speech made at a dinner last year in front of some 50 retired military officers. The complaint had been filed last November by the government through the Human Rights Under-Secretary Alicia Pierini after Videla's statements were reproduced by Buenos Aires daily CLARIN. Government sources yesterday refused to comment on the verdict. [passage omitted]
