940307 FT 07 MAR 94 / International Company News: ICI and Amoco in Dollars 6m China polyester deal Imperial Chemical Industries of the UK and Amoco of the US have each taken strategic stakes of 2.5 per cent in the Yizheng Chemical Fibre Company, China's biggest polyester producer, which is being floated on the Hong Kong stock exchange. The stakes are worth USDollars 6m apiece. Yizheng is claimed to be the world's fifth biggest polyester producer. ICI, which got rid of its fibres operations in the 1980s, said it did not intend to re-enter the fibres market, However, it recently completed a Pounds 150m (Dollars 226m) plant in Taiwan making terephthalic acid, a raw material for making polyester.
