Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Tomorrow the Knesset Internal Affairs Committee will discuss the status of Orient House, the PLO's headquarters in East Jerusalem. The discussion will be held in the wake of a report aired here yesterday, in which the Jerusalem Police chief admitted that the building is outside the police responsibility. Internal Affairs Committee Chairman Yehoshu'a Matza has invited the police minister to tour the building. Our police correspondent Moshe Nussbaum reports: [Begin recording] [passage omitted] A senior Orient House element today told us off the record that in recent secret contacts with Israeli officials, a clear understanding was achieved under which the building was granted an unofficial status of an embassy or a consulate. He confirmed that the security personnel had been ordered to ban Israeli policemen from the building. [end recording] Police Minister Moshe Shahal said in reaction that Orient House has no special status, and that the police behave toward it as they do any other building.
