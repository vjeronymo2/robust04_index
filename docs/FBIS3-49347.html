Language: German Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Bonn (DDP/ADN) -- Germany's Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel this evening welcomed the presidents of Bosnia and Croatia, Alija Izetbegovic and Franjo Tudjman, at Petersberg near Bonn. [passage omitted] Immediately after the welcoming ceremony in the marble hall of the government's guest house, the presidents, the mediators, and Kinkel withdrew to the conference hall. Kinkel intends to leave the scene of the negotiations following brief preliminary talks. Kinkel previously disclosed that the talks are taking place at the initiative of himself and his French counterpart Alain Juppe. Kinkel voiced the hope that there would be "steps forward" at the talks. He added that he would leave this evening for advance talks prior to the NATO summit beginning in Brussels tomorrow. He wished the Bosnian-Croatian negotiations a "good outcome." The public will be advised of developments in the talks as soon as this is "worthwhile."
