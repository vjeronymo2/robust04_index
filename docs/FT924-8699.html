921110 FT 10 NOV 92 / Parliament and Politics: Peers urged to register interests PEERS should be required to register special interests in the same way as MPs in the Commons, the government was urged yesterday. Tory Lord Marlesford said that in recent years 'institutions have been undermined, in some cases by misbehaviour and real scandals, in others by innuendo, often badly based and malicious'. He told the Lords: 'There are two alternative defences: one, privacy and secrecy; and the other, transparency. Isn't transparency by far the better approach?' Lords Leader Lord Wakeham stressed that peers spoke 'on their personal honour' and voluntarily declared any interests before they addressed the House.
