931129 FT 29 NOV 93 / Survey of Software at Work - Winter 1993 (20): Supplier and product profiles Product: SICAD is a geographical information system owned and developed by Siemens Nixdorf. It is a modular system developed by Siemens Nixorf's German parent, designed for customisation and integration with existing business systems. Supplier: Siemens Nixdorf Information Systems, founded in October 1990, is owned by Siemens Nixdorf Informations Systeme AG, in turn owned by Siemens AG. The company supplies hardware of its own and third party manufacture, but in its role as systems integrator concentrates on the provision of complete software and hardware solutions. Turnover: DM13bn in 1992; DM12.1bn in 1991. Employees: 1,000. Number of users: over 600, worldwide. Price: Modular: cost depends on platform, contract, customisation. Entry level price about Pounds 40,000 inclusive of hardware. Hardware and operating systems: BS2000 mainframe and Unix workstation. Sales and support: dedicated GIS group and maintenance. Other services available: training, consultancy. User views: 'Flexible, easy inter-connectivity with other systems, good back-up and support.'
