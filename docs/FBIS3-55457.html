Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vilnius, Jan 07, BNS -- U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher expects the upcoming NATO summit in Brussels to make decisions on the establishment of essentially new cooperation between the alliance and East European countries. Lithuanian Foreign Minister Povilas Gylys received a letter from Christopher this week, saying the Brussels summit would be a favorable possibility to start closer security cooperation between the alliance and the newly emerging democracies in Eastern Europe, including Lithuania, the Lithuanian Foreign Ministry said. Christopher said the summit is likely announce that NATO will welcome new members in the future. The alliance's evolutionary expansion would preserve its strength, harmony among its members and its ability to act. The secretary of state said the Partnership for Peace program offers Lithuania close practical cooperation with both military and political structures of NATO. The initiative would require serious military and political steps -- to observe international obligations, to guarantee civil control of the armed forces and to properly arrange the military budget. Christopher expressed hope that Lithuania will support the Partnership for Peace program and will take an active part in it.
