Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Yuriy Grachev] [Text] Vladivostok, Feb 11 TASS -- A group of Japanese and South Korean experts in nuclear power arrived in Vladivostok. The group intends to inspect the recent dumping grounds of radioactive wastes of the Pacific Fleet and will study problems involved in the processing of radioactive wastes and their impact on the environment. Members of the group will report their findings to the Foreign Ministries of their countries which arranged for their trip to the Primorye territory. The group includes Amano Yukiya, chief of the Nuclear Power Department of the Japanese Foreign Ministry. This is not his first visit to Russia. Each time he contributes more to the strengthening of mutual understanding and goodneighbourliness with the Primorye territory.
