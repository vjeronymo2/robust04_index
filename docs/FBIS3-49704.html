Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 12/1/1994 -- Referring to the steps taken by the county council chairmen of Satu Mare and Maramures Counties for adhesion to the Euro-Carpathian Region and to Ukraine's initiative to include the Romanian counties of Botosani and Suceava in the same organization, Romanian Foreign Ministry Spokesman Mircea Geoana emphasized the following things at the news briefing of January 12: "In the first case, the issue has two aspects: A legal and a political one. As concerns the legal aspect, the things are clear: The two chairmen of county councils were not empowered to sign a possible adhesion. As for the political aspect concerning the future of Europe, the enhancement of integration and stability on the continent and in our region, this kind of cooperation at the level of localities or territorial administrative units is a viable formula. The problem consists only of the manner of delimitation of the area in which this organization is functioning. We want to be sure that such steps do not conceal also things contrary to the European organizations' generous and noble purpose of securing the states' cooperation and prosperity. Ukraine's efforts to include in the Euro-Carpathian Region two counties of Romania -- Botosani and Suceava --, where the numbers of the Ukrainian minority are rather inconsequential, means a stand taken by the Ukrainian Government precisely on this preoccupation of ours. The possibility of Romania's participation in the Euro-Carpathian Region or in other forms of similar cooperation has been under the foreign ministry's consideration," spokesman Mircea Geoana said.
