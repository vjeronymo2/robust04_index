941123 FT 23 NOV 94 / World Stock Markets: Brazil turns firmer Sao Paulo rebounded from losses to end 0.25 per cent up on the day after very volatile trade, on hopes that the central bank could ease severe reserve requirements to lend a hand to troubled small financial institutions. The Bovespa index gained 113 points at 44,948, recovering from a 3 per cent fall seen at midday which followed a drop of nearly 5 per cent on Monday, its steepest decline since the currency was overhauled in the summer. Hopes that the government would help small banks emerged after the president of Brazil's Bank Association (Febraban), Alcides Tapias, met president Itamar Franco earlier yesterday and agreed to meet central bank head Pedro Malan later in the day. On Monday the central bank liquidated Banco Atlantis, the third bank shut down in the last 10 days, because the institution failed to comply with reserve requirements and to settle previously set positions. Bear Stearns' Latin American research team commented that it did not expect a large-scale crisis to develop. 'Most banks will adjust to the loss of their inflationary 'float' income very quickly, due to the agility and high quality of the leading Brazilian private banks.'
