BFN [Text] Bonn, May 6 KYODO -- Japanese and German finance officials acknowledged that their economies are showing a similar trend with bright signs emerging in both countries, when they met for regular talks Thursday [5 May], a Japanese official said. The bilateral finance meeting, designed for exchanging views on fiscal and economic matters, was the first in two and a half years and only the fifth occasion of such a meeting between the two countries. The Japanese and German officials agreed to hold the next meeting in Tokyo next year, the official said. The Japanese delegation was led by Kosuke Nakahira, vice finance minister for international affairs. Nakahira is scheduled to fly to London on Friday and to Stockholm on Monday for bilateral financial talks.
