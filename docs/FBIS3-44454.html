Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hong Kong, December 30 (XINHUA) -- Overseas companies had set up 624 Asia-Pacific regional headquarters and 844 regional offices here in Hong Kong as of June 1 this year, according to the latest survey by the Industry Department. The survey, which was published here today, shows that the United States had the largest number of regional headquarters in the territory with 182 companies, followed by Japan (88) and the United Kingdom (81). The major business of the regional headquarters included wholesale, retail, and import and export activities, transport and related services, manufacturing and finance and banking. As regards regional offices, the survey showed that Japan had the largest number with a total of 185, followed by the United States (183), the United Kingdom (102), China (42) and Switzerland (38). Trading was the most common economic activity pursued by regional offices in Hong Kong, followed by finance and banking, and manufacturing. The survey showed that 94 percent of the respondents considered Hong Kong's banking and financial facilities as the most important and the most favorable factor for the regional operations. About 80 percent of the respondents considered that, compared with the year before, the overall favorability of Hong Kong as a regional headquarters or regional office had improved or had remained satisfactory. The value of overseas investment in Hong Kong's manufacturing industries at the end of 1992 totalled 37.3 billion HK dollars (4.78 billion U.S. dollars), according to the latest figures from the Industry Department. Japan continued to be the leading source of overseas investment in the manufacturing sectors, followed by the United States and China, the department said.
