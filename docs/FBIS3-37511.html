Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow February 17 ITAR-TASS -- In closing his official visit to Russia the British prime-minister, Mr John Major said, "We have been considering for some time the possibility of the queen visiting Russia. Her Majesty is keen to do it and I believe it is a very good idea. The Russians have now had democratic elections and have a democratically elected president. Now seems an appropriate time to proceed. It may happen as early as this autumn but final details are not yet arranged." Asked what Britain hoped to achieve with the visit by the queen, Major replied: "The relationship between the United Kingdom and Russia has been getting closer over some time. I think that will set the seal on the much closer relationship we now have." he added that it will be "the first visit for a very long time, it may even be the first visit ever." In response to a question about whether the queen will be seeing more than Moscow, Major said, "It is not yet clear how much she will see but I would think certainly more than Moscow. She is keen to make this visit." "Her Majesty is one of the finest ambassadors the United Kingdom has ever had. I think from that point of view it is highly desirable that she should visit Russia," John Major said.
