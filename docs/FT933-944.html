930927 FT 27 SEP 93 / World Bank/IMF Meeting: Polish election results to warn western nations that they must help eastern European countries Poland's election result should warn western industrialised nations that they must help eastern European countries to become fully integrated into the world economy, according to the chairman of this week's IMF/World Bank meetings, writes George Graham in Washington. Mr Ivan Szabo, Hungary's finance minister, is the first representative of a former communist country to preside over the meetings. 'It is a warning signal. I think this should warn the whole world,' Mr Szabo said of the Polish election results, in which a grouping of former communists emerged as the largest party in parliament. 'Unless the countries of our region are allowed to integrate further into the world economy things could get worse. It is impossible not to notice the signs of protectionism, and they have been growing in the last year.'
