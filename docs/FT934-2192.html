931215 FT 15 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television Does it matter that many of the programmes made under BBC2's Bookmark umbrella have been so good that the series has now won the right to make programmes in which the 'book' content may be a bit hard to find? No it doesn't. Excellence is always to be welcomed, and anyway there is invariably some bookish connection. For today's programme they took Israeli writer David Grossman into the Occupied Territories on the day after Israel recognised the PLO to discuss the moral aspects of the Arab/Israeli conflict (8.00). Crime Story has not been the greatest series of its sort, but today's 'real life' episode is a memorable one. When clothes belonging to Labour politician John Stonehouse were found on a Miami beach some assumed he had committed suicide, but others said 'Who's he trying to kid?' Sure enough, he had sloped off to Australia and was later brought back to face trial (9.00 ITV). In Stark Ben Elton, writer adaptor and star of this story about businessmen scheming to take over the world, is still trying to get the celibate Rachel into bed (9.25 BBC2).
