920928 FT 28 SEP 92 / Construction Contracts: Brechin bypass development scheme HENRY BOOT, based in Sheffield, has been awarded the Pounds 9.1m contract to dual the A94 Brechin bypass following a lump sum design and construct optional bid placed with the Scottish Office. The 78-week project involves the reconstruction of six kilometres of single carriageway and the construction of a second carriageway alongside using unjointed continuously reinforced concrete. It is understood that this is the first time concrete has been used on a road this far north. All bridges are to be completely reconstructed and an additional split level interchange is to be provided at the southern end of the scheme. Other work includes a Pounds 3.2m tower block housing refurbishment for Leeds City Council; a Pounds 2.4m fit-out of a Boots store in Norwich; a Pounds 1m fast-track contract for Boots; road improvements costing Pounds 760,000 for Northamptonshire County Council; a day unit worth Pounds 800,000 at Derby City Hospital for Trent Regional Health Authority and water reservoir construction work valued at Pounds 745,000 for North West Water.
