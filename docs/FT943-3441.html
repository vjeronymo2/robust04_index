940914 FT 14 SEP 94 / Arts: Today's Television The last in the Channel 4 series Witness seeks to discover how Sascha Anderson, known as a leading light in the dissident movement in East Germany before the fall of the Berlin Wall, came to be one of the top informers for the secret police. Among those he reported to the Stasi were his own wife and the Swedish journalist Bjorn Cederberg. Here Cederberg interviews Anderson's betrayed friends and tracks down the man himself (9.00). The first of a new series of Video Diaries is made by a schizophrenic named Sharon who was abandoned at birth, brought up in a foster home, took an overdose at 13, but is now married (to another schizophrenic) and living in a flat in Manchester (9.30 BBC2). Last week's opening episode of BBC1's drama series about dustmen, Common As Muck, showed distinct promise: drama, comedy, character and dialogue all well above average. The second episode today should show whether this was a flash in the pan (10.00). ITV screens a two-hour recording of the Donmar Warehouse production of Cabaret with Jane Horrocks as Sally Bowles (10.40).
