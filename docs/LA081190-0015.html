August 11, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

SAVINGS AND LOAN CRISIS AND FREE MARKET FORCES

So now our government technocrats are talking seriously about further deregulation of the banking system in this country. Just bear in mind that when the S&Ls were deregulated under Jimmy Carter, our Congress lifted the FDIC insurance from $40,000 on deposits to $100,000.

We didn't know they had decided to gamble with our money.

They say we must deregulate the banks if they are to remain competitive on an international level. So, OK, but let the banks buy their own insurance and pass the cost on to their big depositors. The majority of people in this country have less than $1,000 on deposit or in savings. Why, then, should we agree to back the big boys plays.

LAURELLA A. CROSS

Irvine
