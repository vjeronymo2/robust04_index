Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] According to the latest figures released by the Central Electoral Commission of the Republic of Moldova, after computing the votes cast in 82 percent of the polling stations, the Agrarian Democratic Party is heading the list with 43.27 percent, followed by [the Bloc of the Socialist Party and] the Edinstvo Unity Movement with 21.71 percent, the Bloc of Peasants and Intellectuals with 8.98 percent, and the Christian-Democratic Popular Front Alliance with 7.3 percent. The other political groupings did not reach the 4 percent threshold needed to be admitted to parliament. According to our Chisinau correspondent, Cristian Nita, in an interview granted [yesterday] to THE WASHINGTON POST, Moldovan President Mircea Snegur said that the Agrarian Democratic Party is a center political party and its election victory will not alter the political orientation of the Republic of Moldova.
