920120 FT 20 JAN 92 / UK News (Employment): Government may face ILO censure BRITAIN runs the risk of being the first western industrial country to be singled out for criticism at an annual conference of the International Labour Organisation, according to the Trades Union Congress. Special mention is given to governments which have been 'grave and persistent' violators of ILO conventions which they have ratified, said the TUC. The British government is said to have denied the fundamental human right to freedom of association, and to have continued - in defiance of ILO rulings - to ban independent unions at the GCHQ intelligence base at Cheltenham, Glos.
