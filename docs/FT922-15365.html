920401 FT 01 APR 92 / UK Company News: 39% downturn at John Jacobs John I Jacobs, the shipping and freight group, returned a pre-tax profit of Pounds 612,000 for 1991, a near 39 per cent reduction on the previous Pounds 999,000. Turnover improved from Pounds 4.21m to Pounds 4.43m. Profit from broking increased substantially to Pounds 827,000 (Pounds 441,000). But shipowning turned in losses of Pounds 175,000 (profit Pounds 33,000) and financial ran up a deficit of Pounds 40,000 (profit Pounds 525,000). The profit included Pounds 100,000 part release of a provision for broking department development (debit Pounds 150,000), and was struck after Pounds 273,000 provision for diminution in value of investments. Earnings per share came to 1.9p (3.63p). The final dividend is again 1.4p for a total of 1.9p, against 2.9p.
