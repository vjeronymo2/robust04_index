Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Afghan warring factions have agreed to declare Jalalabad a safe haven, at the instance of Pakistan. A Foreign Office spokesman told newsmen in Islamabad today, that the agreement came after meetings separately of Pakistan's charge d'affaires in Kabul with President Borhanoddin Rabbani and Prime Minister Golboddin Hekmatyar. He said there has also been a positive response from General 'Abdorrahim [as heard] Dostam, when the acting consul general of Pakistan contacted him at Mazar-e Sharif. The spokesman said Pakistan would continue to provide humanitarian relief to the displaced people at Jalalabad. He said Pakistan was pressing all parties to the conflict to accept a cease- fire. On the other hand, the 100-member Jalalabad mediation team, which arrived in Pol-e Charkhi near Kabul last night, has started informal contacts with leaders of the warring factions, to prepare the groundwork for negotiations. Meanwhile a rocket and heavy artillery exchange rocked the Afghan capital for more than an hour today. Forces opposing the government attacked Kabul's [word indistinct] Wazir Akbar Khan district with rockets. The pro-government batteries retaliated for more than an a hour.
