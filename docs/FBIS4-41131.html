BFN [Text] President Nursultan Nazarbayev received today Kyrgyzstan's Prime Minister Apas Dzhumagulov, who is on a working visit in Kazakhstan. Government delegations of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan, led by Prime Ministers Tereshchenko and Dzumagulov had a meeting later. They discussed implementing the agreement on trade and economic cooperation between the two countries. They also discussed the issue of paying our republic's debt of $55 million to the neighboring state for power supplies. The meeting discussed prospects for cooperation in the joint construction of the Kumbartinskiy GES and the possibility of power supplies to the southern parts of Kazakhstan, the volume of this year's supplies of Kazakhstan's chemical compounds with a trace of uranium to the Karabaltinskiy ore mining combine. The initiative for a joint Kazakhstan-Kyrgyzstan venture for processing and refining raw materials containing uranium was supported. It was decided that profits from selling uranium protoxide and oxide will be split in the following way: Kazakhstan's works will get 71 percent and Kyrgyzstan's 29 percent. An intergovernmental protocol on the implementation of agreements on trade and economic cooperation between the two republics for 1994 and an agreement on supplying Kyrgyzstan with raw materials containing uranium starting from this year up to the year 2,000 were signed. Tereshchenko and Dzhumagulov told journalists that the working visit has given a new impetus to trade and economic cooperation between the two countries.
