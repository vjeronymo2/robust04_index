Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Statements by Foreign Minister Faruq al-Shar' and Egyptian Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa to unidentified reporters "following the evening closed meeting held by the Damascus Declaration foreign ministers" in Damascus on 9 January; carried within the news reports following the newscast--recorded] [Text] [Al-Shar'] We studied various aspects of political and economic cooperation among the Arab states which signed the Damascus Declaration. We also discussed some details regarding the scopes of this cooperation in the coming phase. Tomorrow morning, we will focus on the peace process in particular, especially concerning the anticipated meeting between Presidents Hafiz al-Asad and Bill Clinton. This is because the ministers have expressed profound interest in both this summit and its results, which will be reflected on the overall regional situation. [Reporter] What issues will be discussed in the anticipated summit between Presidents Clinton and al-Asad? [Al-Shar'] Naturally, the peace process will be the leading issue. Also expected for discussion are bilateral relations and the overall situation in the region. [Reporter] What are your expectations about this summit? [Al-Shar'] We hope the summit will yield important results. [Reporter] Your Excellency the Minister: How effective will the first meeting, held today, be regarding the implementation of the Damascus Declaration? What articles will be highlighted in this session? [Al-Shar'] There will be emphasis on economic and political cooperation. As for security cooperation, it will be addressed on a bilateral basis between the Arab states which signed the Damascus Declaration. [Reporter] With regard to the Syrian-U.S. summit, do you expect a breakthrough in the peace talks? [Al-Shar'] We hope so. [Reporter, first part of question unheard] ...achieve tangible results? [Al-Shar'] We hope so. [Reporter] Mr. 'Amr Musa, what is the atmosphere of the meetings held? [Musa] The atmosphere is extremely good. [Reporter] Has any emphasis been placed on implementing the Damascus Declaration? [Musa] Yes, of course. There has been emphasis on implementing the Declaration and on continued consultations and joint action. [Reporter] What about implementing the military and economic articles? [Musa] We are now discussing several matters which will implement the Damascus Declaration and which may benefit the general situation in the Arab world. [Reporter] Is there a mechanism to implement this declaration? [Musa] We will discuss such a mechanism.
