BFN [Text] Croatian President Franjo Tudjman spoke on the telephone to his Bosnian counterpart Alija Izetbegovic today, and expressed satisfaction with an agreement reached by the Croatian and Bosnian delegations in Vienna on Wednesday evening, the presidential office said in a statement today. The two presidents agree that the implementation of the Washington agreement on establishing a Muslim-Croatian federation in Bosnia-Herzegovina should continue. They also talked about solving technical issues to enable President Tudjman to visit Sarajevo as soon as possible, and about their joint stand on the contact group and the upcoming meeting of foreign ministers in Geneva. Tudjman asked Izetbegovic to use his authority to step up the process of finding the missing Croats from Bugojno.
