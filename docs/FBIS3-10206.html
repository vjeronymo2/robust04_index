Article Type:CSO [Editorial Report] Managua LA PRENSA in Spanish of 5 February 1994 on pages 1 and 14 reports that Adolfo Calero Portocarrero, vice president of the National Conservative Party (PNC), announced the formation of the Blue and White Triangle, a political alliance joining the conservative and liberal parties and the Nicaraguan Resistance. Calero explained that the alliance responds to "the wishes of the rank and file." Calero stated: "The rank and file will follow the conservative/liberal party which shows the most leadership...." The strongest parties in the National Opposition Union are the Liberal Constitutionalist Party, the Liberal Independent Party, and the PNC, according to the article. PNC official Rina Cardenal announced that each party within the alliance will hold primaries and then "the rank and file will elect their true representatives."
