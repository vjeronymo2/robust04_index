April 6, 1990, Friday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

One-Year T-Bill Rates Fall: Interest rates on 52-week Treasury bills fell at the latest auction.

The Treasury sold $9.8 billion of new bills at an average discount rate of 7.72%, down from 7.76% at the last comparable auction on March 8. The rates were the lowest since Feb. 13, when the one-year bills sold for 7.42%. The new discount rate understates the actual return to investors -- 8.32%, with a $10,000 bill selling for $9,219.40.
