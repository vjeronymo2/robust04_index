940512 FT 12 MAY 94 / Water cut-offs fall by a third The number of homes in England and Wales that had water supplies disconnected for not paying bills fell by a third to 12,452 in the 12 months to the end of March 31, from 18,636 in the previous period, water watchdog Ofwat said. This compares with 21,282 in the peak period for disconnections, the 12 months to March 1992, which lead to widespread criticism of the privatised water companies. Mr Ian Byatt, Ofwat director-general, said: 'Companies have responded well to my guidelines on handling debt sensitively and avoiding disconnections. Companies are improving their approach.'
