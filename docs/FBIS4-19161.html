BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Text] Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and foreign minister, concluded his contacts in Ankara and left Turkey today. He was seen off at Ankara's Esenboga Airport by Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin. In an airport statement, Granic said that his visit was extremely beneficial for the solution of the Bosnia-Herzegovina problem and the reconstruction of the country. The measures to be adopted for the development of bilateral economic relations were assessed and a decision was adopted to expand cooperation. Granic declared. In turn, Cetin said that it was agreed during the talks to hold a meeting among the foreign ministers of Turkey, Bosnia-Herzegovina, and Croatia for the implementation and institutionalization of the Washington agreement.
