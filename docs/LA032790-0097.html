March 27, 1990, Tuesday, Home Edition

RICK DU BROW: NBC TINKERING WITH SATURDAY SCHEDULE

TV or not TV. . . .

SPRING FEVER: NBC is playing around with its important Saturday lineup, anchored by "The Golden Girls." Carol Burnett and Robert Mitchum are a couple of the prominent players.

Mitchum's new sitcom, "A Family for Joe," gets a crack at the important 8 p.m. leadoff slot this Saturday. He portrays a homeless man who poses as grandfather to four orphans to keep them together. There was a preview at 8:30 p.m. last Saturday.

As for Burnett, her new comedy series, "Carol & Company," has the first of two tryouts right after "The Golden Girls" Saturday at 9:30 p.m. It's a nice spot -- usually occupied by "Empty Nest."

Along with the Cosby-led Thursday lineup, Saturdays keep NBC No. 1 -- so any tinkering is watched closely.

The two nights are also great launching pads for new series -- to see if they have the stuff that hits are made of.

RERUN HEAVEN: "Moonlighting" followed by "The Days and Nights of Molly Dodd" on Lifetime cable last Saturday.

The perfect TV package.

And apologies to Bruce Willis -- I never really appreciated how good he was as "Moonlighting's" David Addison.

Just the right flair as David lets fly about his brother:

"Waiter, take this man back to the kitchen -- he's not completely cooked."

And Cybill Shepherd as Maddie Hayes:

"You see one guy in his socks, you've seen them all."

David to Maddie, at work: "You always this much fun early in the morning?" Maddie: "You're gonna die wondering."

On April 6, by the way, Lifetime airs a behind-the-scenes look at "Molly Dodd," followed by the first of the season's new episodes.

GOING IN STYLE: Really charming season finale for ABC's "Anything but Love" Wednesday as Hannah (Jamie Lee Curtis) marks her 30th birthday with bittersweet memories -- and the realization that she loves Marty (Richard Lewis). Will the series be back next fall? Well, let's put it this way: If ABC blows it and says no, it's another great candidate for Lifetime.

AND WHILE WE'RE AT IT. . . : Let's not hear even the slightest thought that ABC might not renew the Peabody Award-winning "China Beach" for next season. Still another great candidate for the female-oriented Lifetime, where, we should add, yet another ABC series (which got canceled) -- "Spenser: For Hire," with Robert Urich -- looks better and better as time goes by.

MATCHED SET: Nice job by Arsenio Hall Friday with guest Jessica Tandy ("Driving Miss Daisy"). He even looked touched when she said that he was better known than she. But we can sure live without his other side -- that tasteless expression he uttered on another show last week, which was reported by several offended readers. A Paramount spokeswoman said that Hall is allowed to be "free and clear," and studio executives declined to comment. In short, he's getting good ratings.

CORRALLED: Look, if CBS scored potently with "Lonesome Dove," its tale of a cattle drive, and a new movie version of "Gunsmoke" that ranked No. 7 in last week's ratings, shouldn't the network be thinking of a real, traditional Western series? CBS' "Paradise" was an amiable entry but not a genuine, bang-up frontier epic.

SILVER LINING: Amid the early ratings wreckage of its three-hour, prime-time, nightly news, KCAL Channel 9 should be taking a close look at the work of newcomer David Jackson, who's quick on his feet and agreeable. But the station's new sports report is so pathetic that the bonehead decision to fire Scott St. James seems more absurd every day.

STAR: If you tuned in Steve McQueen in "Bullitt" on superstation TBS Saturday night, you were watching him on what would have been his 60th birthday. He died 10 years ago. And, oh my, was he ever trim and tough and every inch a Hollywood prince in "Bullitt," backed up by Jacqueline Bisset and Robert Vaughn -- and seeming almost part of his car in that sensational chase through San Francisco.

IT'S MAGIC: Can't wait for next Tuesday's CBS movie "Laker Girls," about the cheerleaders for our favorite dribblers. Do you think they'll love it in Boston?

PERSPECTIVE: So there's Candice Bergen as the TV news hot-shot in "Murphy Brown," telling a secretary: "Please, don't feel you have to make a fuss or give me any special treatment because you see me on TV every week." Secretary: "I don't have a TV. I have a life."

MOMENTS IN TIME: Judy Holliday shuffling her cards in the gin rummy scene of "Born Yesterday" on the Comedy Channel. . . . Gary Cooper dancing with the exquisite Suzy Parker in "Ten North Frederick" on American Movie Classics. . . . Lucy and Desi doing a knockout "Cuban Pete" dance number on KTTV Channel 11 -- somehow making us understand what attracted them to each other in the first place.

DATEBOOK: "Tour of Duty" has been an honorable series for CBS, and the Vietnam drama keeps on reaching for something special -- on April 14, it deals with the spraying of Agent Orange.

BEAUTIFUL DOWNTOWN WARSAW: Wonder if the folks who make Fred Sands' TV real estate program saw this ad in Sunday's Times: "POLAND-WARSAW Studio apt. 380 sf. Nr dwntwn. $20K or make ofr." Well, as any broker will tell you -- location, location, location.

BEING THERE: "Sometimes I just wish I were Ozzie Nelson. He'd just say, 'Go to college' -- and that would be the end of that." Who said it? Howard Cunningham (Tom Bosley) to son Richie (Ron Howard) in "Happy Days."

Say good night, Gracie. . . .
