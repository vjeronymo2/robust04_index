BFN [ITAR-TASS report: "Care for Veterans"] [Text] On the recommendation of Ruslan Aushev, president of Ingushetia and chairman of the Council of the CIS Heads of Government Committee for Internationalist Troops, the Interparliamentary Assembly Council adopted at its session yesterday a decree in which the attention of the parliaments of the Interparliamentary Assembly's member states is drawn to the necessity of providing internationalist troops and their families with a full degree of social and legal protection. "The Interparliamentary Assembly Council adopted this decision affecting the future of more than 700,000 people with practically no discussion," Vladimir Shumeyko said in an exclusive interview for an ITAR-TASS correspondent. He emphasized that, unfortunately, questions of state social assistance and legal protection for veterans of wars and military operations in various countries are being resolved with difficulty in the CIS, and advocated improving the system to implement interstate legal acts on assistance to veterans adopted within the framework of the CIS, including the monitoring of their execution by the sovereign states' supreme legislative organs. Shumeyko expressed the hope that the programs for material and everyday and medical provision for war veterans agreed by the CIS countries will be backed by budgetary finances.
