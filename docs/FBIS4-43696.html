BFN [Unattributed article: "Industrial Production Climb Confirms Recovery"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, June 17 -- Industrial production in Italy rose 0.4 percent in the first four months of the current year which, together with an upswing in electricity consumption, has confirmed that the economy is on the path to recovery. The national statistics bureau ISTAT [Central Statistics Institute] said the 0.4 percent increase was coupled with a 1 percent decline in April but, adjusting the fact that this year there were two less working days than in April 1993, the daily production growth was 7.5 percent. The state electricity utility ENEL [National Electric Power Board] reported that the demand for electricity on its and other networks were by 4 percent gross and 2.9 percent after adjustment, and followed the 3.4 percent increase reported in April, and that both confirmed that industrial production was on the upswing. In the first five months of the year, ENEL added, the demand for electricity had risen by an overall 1.6 percent over the same period in 1993. In the industrial sector alone, ENEL said, the increase in demand for May was 3 percent and was evident in all sectors except construction. According to ISTAT, the industrial sectors which showed the greatest increases in production were: business machines, up 7.6 percent; clothing, 5 percent; automobiles, 4.5 percent; petroleum production, 3.7 percent; and rubber and plastic, up 3 percent. Running against this trend were the sectors of transport means other than automobiles, down 14.1 percent; precision instruments, off 9.2 percent; radio-television and telecommunications devices, 8.7 percent; metal products, 8.1 percent; and the working of non- ferrous minerals, down 5 percent. For the first four months of the year, ISTAT added, an increase of 1.9 percent was recorded for the intermediate goods sector and 0.8 percent for consumer goods, while investment good dropped by 5.8 percent. There was also a 10.7 increase in footwear production, a 4 percent climb in lumber production, rubber and plastics rose by 3.4 percent, while there was a 5.7 percent increase in the industrial production of mechanical machines and devices, 2.6 percent for textiles, 1.4 percent for paper and publishing, 1 percent for chemicals, and a 1.8 percent increase for various sectors. At the other end of the spectrum, industrial production declined by 5 percent for mining, 3.4 percent for foods, 2.6 percent for petroleum, 2.6 percent for metal products, 0.3 percent for finished minerals, 7.2 percent for transport means and 0.9 percent for electricity and gas.
