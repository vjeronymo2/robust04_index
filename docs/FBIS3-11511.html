Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Peru monitored through 17 March. Education Vice Minister Esther Gago Hurtado and PRC Ambassador to Peru Chen Jiuchang on 5 March signed an agreement to promote a broad program of cultural exchange and strengthen links between the two countries. The program will be implemented through 1996. (Lima LA REPUBLICA in Spanish 6 Mar 94 p 16) The Japanese Government has agreed to lend Peru, together with the Inter-American Development Bank, IDB, $21 million to help improve health services. The agreement was signed on 16 March by Prime and Foreign Minister Efrain Goldenberg and Japanese Deputy Foreign Minister (Shoko Atuma). Goldenberg said he hopes the loan will benefit at least 5 million Peruvians. (Lima Global de Television Network in Spanish 0100 GMT 16 Mar 94) Net profits for the Central Peru Mining Enterprise, Centromin, was $15 million in 1993. Sales domestically and abroad totaled $389 million. Although production increased $97.5 million in 1993, sales dropped 3 percent compared with 1992 because of a decline in international prices for the main minerals produced by Centromin. (Lima EL PERUANO in Spanish 9 Mar 94 p B1) On 9 March, the Chilean and Peruvian agriculture ministers signed an economic, technical, and scientific cooperation agreement designed to protect regional llamas and vicunas. (Lima EXPRESO in Spanish 10 Mar 94 p A23)
