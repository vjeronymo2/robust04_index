930713 FT 13 JUL 93 / International Company News: Peak profit at Motorola matches Wall St hopes MOTOROLA, the US electronics and semiconductor manufacturer, reported record second-quarter earnings, matching Wall Street expectations. Net income rose 57 per cent to Dollars 224m, or 81 cents a share, from Dollars 143m or 53 cents fully diluted. Revenues were Dollars 3.9bn, up 25 per cent from the Dollars 3.1bn of the same period last year. Net profit margins on sales were 5.7 per cent , up from 4.6 per cent a year ago. Semiconductor sales rose 28 per cent to Dollars 1.39bn, with orders up 30 per cent at an all time high, the company said. Orders increased most rapidly in chips for the communications market. Motorola's general systems group sales, which includes telecommunications equipment, recorded sales of Dollars 1.18bn, up 40 per cent from the second quarter of 1992. In the communications segment, including land mobile products and the paging and wireless data group, sales rose 23 per cent to Dollars 1.13bn. Demand for Motorola's semiconductors, communications products and electronic equipment continued to increase throughout most of the world, even in regions such as Europe, where economic conditions remain sluggish, said Mr George Fisher, chairman and chief executive. For the half year, sales reached Dollars 7.56bn, up from Dollars 6.2bn in the first half of 1992. Earnings were Dollars 428m, or Dollars 1.58 per share, against Dollars 268m or Dollars 1.01, before the cumulative effect of a 1992 change in accounting related to retirement health insurance benefits. Fully diluted earnings per share were Dollars 1.53 for the first half of 1993, up from 99 cents a year earlier.
