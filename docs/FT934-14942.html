931014 FT 14 OCT 93 / One in four reject M-way tolls plan A QUARTER of motorists would stop using motorways if they were charged Pounds 50 a year to use them, the Consumers' Association said yesterday. Even Pounds 25 a year would lead to one in six deserting motorways, its survey of 1,168 drivers interviewed early last month found. The government is considering introducing motorway charging and has suggested cash raised could be used to speed up road building. But 85 per cent of those surveyed said motorists should not have to pay extra to fund any improvements, and nine out of 10 motorists who said they were against funding any sort of improvement thought they already paid enough. 'Traffic congestion is the scourge of the 1990s but forcing drivers off motorways isn't going to help,' said Mr Derek Prentice, association assistant director. 'If road pricing is introduced only those motorists in the higher income brackets may be prepared to use motorways.'
