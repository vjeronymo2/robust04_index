Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, the deputy chairman of the Presidential Council, in Aden last night received a delegation from the Arab League comprising brothers Staff Major General Muhammad Sa'id Bayraqdar, the assistant secretary general of the Arab League and director of its Department of Military Affairs; Ambassador Mahyub, secretary general [as heard], director of the General Department for Arab Affairs; and Dr. Amin (?Fu'adi), official in charge of the Gulf and the Arabian Peninsula at the Arab League. During the meeting, the Arab League delegation delivered to brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, the deputy chairman of the Presidential Council, a letter from his brother, Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid, the Arab League secretary general, concerning relations between our country and the Arab League, the situation in Yemen, and the Arab League's efforts on behalf of Yemen. At the beginning of the meeting, brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid, welcomed the Arab League delegation and briefed it on developments affecting the national arena and on the dangerous military imbroglios it had experienced, particularly following the signing of the document of pledge and accord. He briefed them on the efforts made to contain and bring to an end those imbroglios. He also stressed the need to bring about conditions conducive to the implementation of the document of pledge and accord inasmuch as this was a document of national consensus and a new social charter to rectify the march towards unity and the construction of the modern state. Brother 'Ali Salim al-Bid expressed his appreciation for the attention the Arab League had paid to the state of affairs in our country. He also expressed gratitude for the efforts being made by Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid, which are an extension of the efforts being made by friendly and fraternal states to help our country overcome the present crisis and create conditions conducive towards restoring stability on the road that leads towards the building of a modern Yemen. The Arab League delegation expressed the league's concern over the developments affecting the Yemeni arena, its keenness that the crisis be resolved, and that an end be put to the imbroglios which brought it about. The delegation stressed that the league was prepared to do everything it could to participate in helping our country to resolve the problems it was undergoing. This would enable it to strengthen security and stability for itself and the peoples of the region thus preserving the unity of the people and their national and democratic options in building their modern democratic state.
