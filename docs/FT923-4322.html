920908 FT 08 SEP 92 / Russia presses for arms sale to India Russia hopes for an early agreement on arms sales to India, which have slumped after months of argument about deliveries and pricing, Itar-Tass news agency said yesterday, Reuter reports from Moscow. Tass quoted Mr Pavel Gravchev, the Russian defence minister, saying he and Mr Sharad Pawar, his Indian counterpart, hoped to restore military links between their countries. Mr Gravchev hoped agreement would be reached before President Boris Yeltsin of Russia visits India in January. Russia, India's main arms supplier, has faced economic turmoil since the Soviet Union collapsed. Indian officials say exports to India have fallen short of agreed figures.
