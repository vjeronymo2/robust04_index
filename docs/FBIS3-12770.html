Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Poland today assured India that it will not support Pakistan's resolution on Kashmir at the UN Human Rights Commission meeting in Geneva. The visiting Polish foreign minister, Mr. (Andrzej Olechowski), gave this assurance in his talks with the external affairs minister, Mr. Dinesh Singh, in New Delhi. The Polish foreign minister said his country gives special emphasis to its relations with India and wants to strengthen the economic ties. Mr. Dinesh Singh briefed him about Pakistan's attempt to internationalize the Kashmir issue under the guise of raising human rights issue. Pakistan's own record in this area is abysmal. Mr. Dinesh Singh said the political process in Jammu and Kashmir can easily begin if Pakistan stops aiding and abetting terrorism in the state. Both also discussed the ongoing reforms in the UN system and called for making it more democratic.
