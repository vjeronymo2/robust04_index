941021 FT 21 OCT 94 / UK Company News: OFT clears Healthcall on competition Healthcall Group, the UK's largest provider of out-of-hours doctors, has been exonerated by the Office of Fair Trading over claims of anti-competitive practices. In July, the OFT announced that it would make 'some preliminary enquiries' into charges regarding Healthcall's relationship with the British Medical Association and its treatment of potential competitors. Sir Bryan Carsberg, the director general of fair trading, said that the BMA/Healthcall agreement with respect to doctors' deputising services did not fall within the provisions of the Restrictive Trade Practices Act. However, after considering whether it had effects on competition which might merit investigation under the Fair Trading Act, Sir Bryan said he was satisfied 'the agreement is not operating in a way which significantly influences competition in this market.'
