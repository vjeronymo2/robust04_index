941209 FT 09 DEC 94 / International Company News: Nat Semi ahead in quarter National Semiconductor, the US chip maker, yesterday reported improved second-quarter net income, up at Dollars 67m from Dollars 60m on sales up slightly to Dollars 584m from Dollars 582m, Agencies report. For the half year, net climbed to Dollars 126m from Dollars 117m, which included a gain of Dollars 4.9m for the cumulative effect of an accounting change. Half-year sales were virtually flat. National Semiconductor said: 'New order rates are up 20 per cent to 25 per cent over last year, led by the analog and mixed signal product lines that support our moving and shaping markets.' The company said analog and mixed signal products represented 56 per cent of total sales in the second quarter, compared with 52 per cent a year ago, and orders for analog and mixed signal devices were up more than 35 per cent. The company also said its power management segment led bookings growth with additional strength shown by our Super I/O and local area network product lines. National Semiconductor said: 'Orders improved throughout the (latest) quarter, with November showing strong results.' Intel, the leading US microprocessor manufacturer, has announced plans to build a new chip manufacturing and development plant in Hillsboro, Oregon, spending Dollars 565m on the first phase, Reuter reports from Hillsboro. That phase, to begin immediately, will include a 500,000 sq ft wafer fabrication facility, and office and support buildings. Intel expects completion by 1997.
