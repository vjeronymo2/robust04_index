930727 FT 27 JUL 93 / Business and the Law: Supreme Court - Legal Briefs The US Supreme Court has failed to clarify when punitive damages awards are excessive. A divided court recently upheld by 6 votes to 3, the constitutionality of a Dollars 10m punitive damages award made by a West Virginia court that was more than 500 times greater than the Dollars 19,000 compensatory damages it awarded to TXO Production Corp, a subsidiary of the US steel, oil and gas giant USX Corp, for slander of title in a dispute over rights to oil and gas beneath a tract of land. The court reiterated its reasoning in a 1991 case that punitive damages must be reasonable within the context of the case, but failed to spell out what would be regarded as unreasonable.
