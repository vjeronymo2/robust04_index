920905 FT 05 SEP 92 / UK Company News: Pittencrieff beats low US oil prices Despite the recession and depressed oil and gas prices in the US, Pittencrieff increased its pre-tax profit by 42 per cent in the first half of 1992. The group, also engaged in the communications business, saw turnover jump from Pounds 4.19m to Pounds 7.09m. The profit was up from Pounds 1.55m to Pounds 2.21m, and included Pounds 401,000 exceptional net gain on the sale of the shareholding in AmBrit International. Gross profit rose almost 50 per cent to Pounds 2.7m despite margins being reduced from 42.4 to 37.5 per cent as a result of curtailed oil and gas volumes and low product prices, and the temporary impact of high operating costs inherited from recent communications acquisitions. In communications, turnover more than doubled to Dollars 9m (Pounds 4.5m) while gross profit doubled to Dollars 2.9m. Earnings were 9.4p (7.19p) and the interim dividend is 3p on increased capital (2.5p).
