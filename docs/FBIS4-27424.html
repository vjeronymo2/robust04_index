BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 2 June Morning Edition] [Text] Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa said on 1 June that when U.S. Forces enforce a sea blockade against the DPRK (North Korea) over the issue of its nuclear weapons development: "Generally speaking, Japan will offer possible cooperation within the constitutional limitation." He was also asked the government's attitude toward the U.S. proposal for an "acquisition and cross-servicing agreement (ACSA)," an accord aimed at the mutual provision of military supplies and services between Japan and the United States. He said the government was studying possibility of enacting such an agreement, saying: "To design the framework of the concept, the government is studying various aspects, including the need for the ACSA." Kakizawa made his remarks in reply to questions by Representative Taro Nakayama of the Liberal Democratic Party during the 1 June session of the lower house budget committee. Director General Atsushi Kanda of the Defense Agency said that as far as North Korea's preparations for the test launching of new-type missiles are concerned: "We are watching developments with great concern."
