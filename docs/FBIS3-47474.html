Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 13 KYODO -- Japan and Russia will indefinitely postpone planned working-level talks on a bilateral territorial dispute originally set for late January, a top Foreign Ministry official said Thursday [13 January]. The meeting, scheduled in Moscow as the first talks on the territorial row between the two sides since Russian President Boris Yeltsin visited Japan last October, is being postponed at Russia's request, the official said on condition of anonymity. The official quoted Russian officials as citing as an obstacle mobility of personnel in the wake of Russia's parliamentary elections in December. Japan claims four Russian-held islands off the northernmost main island of Hokkaido. The islands -- Shikotan, Kunashiri, Etorofu and the Habomai islets -- were seized by the former Soviet Union at the end of World War II.
