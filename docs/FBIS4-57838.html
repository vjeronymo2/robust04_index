BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 24 May. A Chinese mission headed by Wang Lian Ying, chairman of the Real Estate Development Corporation of Economic Technology Development of Yukou, arrived in Mexico City on 18 May. The purpose of this mission is to sign a final agreement for the start of a "Mexican Town" tourist project in China. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 19 May 94 p 18) The inflation rate in 66 percent of the country's major cities, not including Mexico City, was greater than the 7.2 percent registered nationwide from January to April. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 20 May 94 p 3A) Figures released by the Finance and Public Credit Secretariat indicate the GDP rose 0.5 percent during the first quarter of 1994, which is the smallest first-quarter increase within the past five years. (Mexico City UNOMASUNO in Spanish 20 May 94 p 19) Speaking in Guadalajara, Jaime Serra Puche, commerce and industrial development secretary, reported that Mexico has exported more than $4 billion to the United States since NAFTA went into effect, thereby setting an all-time record. Meanwhile, Pedro Aspe, finance and public credit secretary, reported that customs revenues are increasing at an annual rate of 5 billion new pesos. The Finance Secretariat also reported nonoil exports rose 15.7 percent in March from the same period a year earlier, while oil exports rose 23 percent. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 0430 GMT 21 May 94) Mexican exports increased 17 percent during the first quarter of 1994 when compared to the same period a year earlier. Exports of manufactured goods rose 26 percent. Figures show Mexican imports from the United States increased 22 percent. (Mexico City XEW Television Network in Spanish 1300 GMT 23 May 94) The Finance Secretariat has reported that, during the first quarter of 1994, the Mexican trade deficit hit $4.317 billion, with exports and imports amounting to $13.735 billion and $18.052 billion, respectively. Meanwhile, the Mexican GDP increased 0.5 percent during the same period. (Paris AFP in Spanish 1709 GMT 21 May 94) A quarterly report issued by the National Banking and Insurance Commission indicates that expired portfolios amounted to 38.431 billion new pesos, representing a 59.8 percent increase, in the first quarter of 1994. The report also shows that loans amounted to 471.975 billion new pesos, while bank reserves amounted to 15.369 billion new pesos, a 46.7 percent increase, during the same period. Bank net profits increased 18 percent. (Mexico City EL FINANCIERO in Spanish 23 May 94 p 4)
