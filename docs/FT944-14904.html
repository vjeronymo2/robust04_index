941020 FT 20 OCT 94 / World Stock Markets: Markets in perspective The listing this week in New York and locally of Indosat, the Indonesian telecommunications company, has given a boost to the Jakarta market, which has been languishing so far this year a long way behind its regional competitors. The global offering of shares in the group, the largest in Asia, raised about Dollars 1bn. Brokers say this should provide a much needed stimulus to the equity market and encourage the return of foreign investors. Yesterday Indosat made a successful debut on the local exchange, initially gaining nearly 30 per cent on its IPO price of Rp7,000 before falling back on profit-taking to close at Rp8,475. One difficulty facing the market's forward progress remains the limit on the foreign ownership of companies, currently at 49 per cent, which is not, at least in the short term, to be lifted.
