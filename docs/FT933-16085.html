930706 FT 06 JUL 93 / Parliament and Politics: DSS promise on mothers' benefits THE SOCIAL security department yesterday distanced itself from remarks by Mr John Redwood, Welsh secretary, apparently suggesting that state benefit for lone mothers should be withheld until the father is pursued. The department said Mr Redwood's comments were 'not government policy' while Mr Peter Lilley, social security secretary, said in the Commons that there was no question of penalising particular groups of benefit claimants. Ministers appeared happy, however, for a full debate in the Conservative party on the treatment of single parents. Mr Lilley told MPs that a government decision on equalisation of male and female pension ages was unlikely before the judgment in the Coloroll case, expected in September or October.
