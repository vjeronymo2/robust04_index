October 13, 1989, Friday, Valley Edition

HUNTERS PLAY KEY ROLE IN CONSERVATION

The letters you printed Sept. 30 in response to the article "Changing Stripes" show, unfortunately, how ill-informed the public really is. Our news media and the so-called "Friends of Animals" have no concept of what conservation is and the role hunting really plays. If it wasn't for the revenues generated and the donations made by the hunting community, there wouldn't be any "Bambis" left. The true conservationist understands that we must balance the land, the animals and the amount of space that man allows nature to be as she once was before man changed the ecological balance with concrete and steel. Conservation costs money, and, by allowing a certain number of animals to be harvested for a fee, we can afford to maintain their habitat and environment and promote the continuation and growth of the species.

The true big-game hunter doesn't just kill to kill but uses the animal he harvests for food and the trophy for the memory of the hunt. Nothing is wasted. Not everyone can be a true hunter. People eat fish, fowl and meat, and just because they didn't kill their food doesn't mean that it wasn't killed. Fish die by suffocation, fowl by mechanical strangulation and meat by wholesale annihilation. Is this compassionate? I think not!

JOHN HUTCHESON, San Gabriel
