930811 FT 11 AUG 93 / UK Company News: New Ireland jumps to IPounds 4m New Ireland Holdings, the Dublin-based composite insurance company controlled by UAP, the French insurance group, lifted pre-tax profits by 53 per cent from IPounds 2.63m to IPounds 4.05m (Pounds 3.79m) in the first half of 1993. Premium income jumped 62 per cent to IPounds 69.2m. Annual premium new business showed a substantial increase, while single premium business doubled. The Irish National Insurance offshoot turned in pre-tax profits 71 per cent higher at IPounds 3.15m after an exceptional IPounds 901,000 exchange credit. Underwriting losses, however, rose from IPounds 1.34m to IPounds 2.54m as a result of severe weather claims and increased large public liability claims. Group earnings per share were almost doubled at 18.5p (9.6p), and the interim dividend is raised to 3.5p (3.3p).
