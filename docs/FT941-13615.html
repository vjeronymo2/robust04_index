940125 FT 25 JAN 94 / People: Turner heads APCIMS The Association of Private Client Investment Managers & Stockbrokers (APCIMS) has brought in as its new chief executive Geoffrey Turner, 48. Until the new appointment, Turner has been director of public affairs at the Securities and Futures Authority, the self-regulatory organisation for the professional securities business. At APCIMS he succeeds Michael Baker, who has been in the post three years. Turner, above, has been at the SFA and its forerunner - The Securities Association - since 1988. Prior to that he had spent 15 years at the Stock Exchange and was latterly involved there in a variety of issues leading up to Big Bang. As head of membership there, he devised new rules to encompass the market's new international firms. APCIMS is the trade association promoting the interests of private client stockbrokers and investment managers.
