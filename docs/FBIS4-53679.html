BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 22 KYODO -- New party Sakigake leader Masayoshi Takemura left Tokyo on Sunday [22 May] on a weeklong visit to the United States for talks with government and congressional leaders on economic and trade issues, and North Korea's suspected nuclear weapons program. Sakigake officials said Takemura, 59, will also talk with U.S. officials about Japan's bid to become a permanent member of the U.N. Security Council. He will address the Asia-Pacific Center in San Francisco on Thursday before returning home Saturday, they said. Sakigake, set up by a splinter group of 15 Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) legislators, quit the ruling coalition in April along with the Social Democratic Party (SDP), leaving the cabinet of Prime Minister Tsutomu Hata a minority government.
