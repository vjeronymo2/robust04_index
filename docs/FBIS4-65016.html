BFN [Text] New Democracy Chairman Miltiadhis Evert returned to Ioannina at noon today folloowing a two-day tour to North Epirus, during which he visited villages and towns where the Greek minority lives. In a news conference at Ioannina, Evert called on the Albanian leadership to respect the human rights of North Epirotes. Good relations with Greece, Evert said, are to Albania's benefit. He said that it is inconceivable for relations between the two countries to be at the level of serious misunderstandings on the eve of the 21st century. Referring to Turkey's involvement in Greek-Albanian relations, Evert said that if something like this were to happen, Ankara would have chosen a wrong direction. Finally, Evert said that he will visit the Albanian capital only if the five Omonoia [organization of the Greek minority of Albania] officials of the Greek minority, currently in Tirana prisons, are freed.
