Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Port-of-Spain, Trinidad, 26 Jan, CANA -- Trinidad and Tobago has mobilised its army in the fight against crime on the street. National Security Minister Russell Huggins said 300 armed soldiers have been placed on foot patrol in crime-hit areas throughout the country. They are in the eastern part of Port of Spain, the southern San Fernando City and in some rural eastern and southern villages. The issue of putting soldiers on foot patrol was mentioned in a statement to the nation last Friday by Prime Minister Patrick Manning, who expressed concern over the increase in serious crimes. Last year, almost 20,000 serious crimes were reported to the police. There were 108 murders, including drug-related killings and the execution of key state witnesses. For this year, the country has so far witnessed 17 murders. Following Manning's statement to the nation, Huggins met with Commissioner of Police Jules Bernard and chief of defence force, Brigadier Ralph Brown, over the weekend to work out a plan to have the soldiers on foot patrol. Previously, soldiers had joined policemen in joint-patrols but this came to an end when there were no working vehicles to accommodate the special crime-fighting initiative. Huggins said that like ordinary citizens, the soldiers were allowed to make citizen's arrest. He also said that the soldiers' presence would act as a deterrent to criminal activites while bringing comfort to citizens. "Having soldiers unaccompanied by the police is designed to bring comfort to the citizens," he said. "It is not envisaged that bandits, seeing armed soldiers would say they don't have powers of arrest and decide to pull out their guns and commit robberies. "The comfort aspect is just as important."
