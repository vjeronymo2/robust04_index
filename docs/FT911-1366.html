910507 FT 07 MAY 91 / Construction Contracts: Pounds 8.8m four-storey hotel in Coventry Contracts worth over Pounds 19m have been won by companies in the construction division of TRAFALGAR HOUSE. Among contracts awarded to Willett and Cementation Construction, the largest is for a four-storey hotel in Coventry, worth Pounds 8.8m. In Leeds, Bramley Baths are being restored to their original Victorian splendour under a Pounds 1.5m design and build contract. In London's Fleet Street, Trollope & Colls (City) has a Pounds 2.2m order from The Royal Bank of Scotland for the second phase of refurbishment of Child & Co's Grade 1 listed bank property built in 1878. Childs claims to be the oldest bank in the UK, dating to 1584. Cementation companies have work valued at Pounds 5.72m, including sinking a 7.5 metre diameter underground bunker shaft at Welbeck Colliery, and piling for the second phase of the National Library of Scotland in Edinburgh.
