Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Colombo, March 1 (XINHUA) -- Chinese vice-premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen and Sri Lankan Foreign Minister A.C.S. Hameed exchanged views on bilateral, regional and international issues at their two-hour official talks here today. At talks, Hameed briefed his Chinese counterpart on the current situation in his country, saying that his country will stick to a stand of solving domestic problems through political dialogue. Qian spoke highly of the economic development in Sri Lanka and appreciated the efforts by the Sri Lankan Government in realizing national conciliation and safeguarding territorial integrity and unity of the country. On regional issues, Hameed said, "Although disputes are existing among the South Asian nations, the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC) has made much progress in recent years." Qian said, "China has attached importance to developing relations and cooperation with South Asian countries as South Asia is an important region with a large population and a potential large market as well". Qian expressed the hope that South Asian countries will remove external interference and find peaceful resolutions of the disputes so as to create a peaceful environment for developing economy in the region. About Indian Ocean Peace Zone, Hameed said, "As a sponsor country, Sri Lanka hopes this peace zone will make progress as the confrontation of the two big powers has ended." Qian said, "China has been supporting the proposal for establishing the Indian Ocean Peace Zone." He noted that "The big powers outside the zone should withdraw the military existence in the Indian Ocean region and respect the independence, sovereignty and security of the countries in the region". The two foreign ministers also had in-depth discussions on other regional and internal issues of common interest. Qian arrived here on February 28 on a two-day official visit to Sri Lanka.
