BFN ["Statement of the Spokesman for the Albanian Foreign Ministry" -- ATA headline] [Text] Tirana, May 17 (ATA) -- A report by the spokesman for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Albania issued on May 16 says that the Albanian Foreign Ministry expresses the satisfaction for the fact that the Albanian proposal for a forthcoming Albanian-Greek meeting in Tirana, has had a positive response from the Greek foreign minister, Papoulias. The report adds that the freedom and the rights of the Greek minority in Albania are and will be guaranteed and invulnerable. This reality of the Greek minority in Albania has been acknowledged and esteemed by relevant international organizations as well. The Albanian deputy foreign minister, Arjan Starova, had a meeting on May 13 with the ambassador of the Greek Republic in Tirana, Mr. Tsalikis. He forwarded the Albanian proposal for a meeting in Tirana between the foreign ministers of the two countries. Such an idea was expressed some days ago by the president of Albania, Sali Berisha, in an interview with the Albanian Radio and Television at the end of his visit to Romania last week.
