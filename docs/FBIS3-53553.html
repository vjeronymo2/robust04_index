Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Political sources in Yemen think there are two main reasons for Yemeni Vice President al-Bid's boycott of a meeting last Sunday with Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih in al-Janad Mosque in the Yemeni city of Ta'izz, which is surrounded by green hills. The sources told AL-SHURUQ that al-Bid was not sure about the security measures. In fact, people close to him said openly that al-Bid had great fears that he would be a victim of a carefully concocted assassination attempt. The second reason is that if al-Bid went to Ta'izz, he would find himself in a hostile atmosphere. The reason for this is not President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih but the ulema, who are known for their support for President Salih. Despite all the recent developments, the ulema still regard al-Bid as a socialist and communist. Whatever the reasons behind al-Bid's boycott of the meeting, the failure of the Ta'izz meeting has exacerbated the Yemeni crisis. President 'Ali Salih acknowledged this when he warned of "serious pitfalls facing Yemeni unity" which was realized on 22 May 1990. The Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP] has made a meeting between the Yemeni president and the vice president conditional on the implementation of the YSP's 18 points, despite the fact that the General People's Congress had earlier approved those 18 points. This means the YSP does not plan to make any concessions and that al-Bid is deliberately seeking to "humiliate" 'Ali Salih to corner him and drive him to adopt positions and measures which al-Bid probably would like to see happen without himself taking the responsibility of declaring or adopting such measures. Informed Yemeni sources told AL-SHURUQ that the telephone call between al-Bid and King Husayn of Jordan after canceling the Ta'izz meeting has left the door open to hold a meeting, but in a neutral venue outside Yemen and after complying with new conditions set by al-Bid.
