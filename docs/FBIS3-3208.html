Language: Chinese Article Type:BFN [By reporter Yu Yiguo (0205 1355 0948)] [Text] Tokyo, 10 Mar (XINHUA) -- This afternoon, Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa, who will soon visit China, met with Chinese Ambassador to Japan Xu Dunxin at the prime minister's official residence. Hosokawa said: The development of Japan-China relations is now very smooth in all fields. He said he was looking forward to exchanging opinions with Chinese leaders on issues of common concern. Touching on the question of economic cooperation between Japan and China, Hosokawa expressed his view that Japan and China each has its advantageous points, that China's development serves the interests of Japan, and that Japan's development is also beneficial to China. He hoped the two sides would make joint efforts to build a relationship of interdependence and make up for each other's deficiencies. During the meeting, Xu Dunxin indicated that the Chinese Government attached great importance to Prime Minister Hosokawa's forthcoming visit to China and hoped that the prime minister's visit would achieve complete success.
