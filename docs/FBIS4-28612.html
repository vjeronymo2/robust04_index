BFN ["KYODO and YONHAP report from Tokyo"] [Text] Japan's KYODO News Agency reported: North Korean President Kim Il-song said that North Korea can abandon its nuclear development program if it can receive economic support from the United States to convert its graphite-moderated reactor to a light-water reactor, along with the normalization of diplomatic relations with the United States through dialogue. Selig Harrison, senior associate of the Carnegie Endowment for Northeast Asian Security Affairs, said in his news conference with KYODO News Agency: President Kim expressed that he has the intention to stop the radiochemical laboratory and the ongoing construction of a 200,000 kilowatt nuclear reactor if the United States and other foreign countries promise to provide technology to North Korea for building the light-water reactor. Harrison, who paid a week-day visit to Pyongyang at the invitation of North Korea, met with President Kim on 9 June and held talks with other North Korean high-level leaders, including First Vice Foreign Minister Kang Sok-chu. After this, he arrived in Beijing on 10 June.
