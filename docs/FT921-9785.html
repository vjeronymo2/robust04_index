920210 FT 10 FEB 92 / UK News (Employment): TGWU plans higher fees to combat debt THE TGWU general workers' union plans to increase contributions by more than 18 per cent in an attempt to balance its budget this year after several years of losses. The increase, which is likely to be approved by a special delegate conference this week, should raise an extra Pounds 1m a month, which is roughly equivalent to the rate of the losses. Members will be asked to pay a further 21p a week. This will take the weekly contribution to Pounds 1.35, which is below the average level for union dues but slightly above the contribution to the GMB, the other main general union. The TGWU expects to make savings this year of about Pounds 5m. That, with the extra income from the contributions increase, should enable it to balance its budget. The union's structure and finances are being examined by Klein & Co, a US management consultancy which is expected to produce an interim report at the end of next month. Mr Ron Todd, the TGWU's retiring general secretary, said: 'This is an important increase. The union's leadership is determined to balance our budget this year after several years of running a deficit.'
