Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, Feb. 1, IRNA-- Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov Monday called for promotion of Tehran-Sofia ties. Speaking to IRNA, Daskalov termed his first round of talks with the visiting Iranian Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati as constructive and said Sofia was ready to sign trade agreement with Tehran. He said that the two countries have agreed to set up a joint commission for cultural cooperation. Daskalov stressed that his country would not allow its relations with Iran be influenced by pressure of foreign powers. He said that differences of views existed between the two countries with regards to settling the Bosnian crisis but added that this cannot hamper expansion of Tehran-Sofia relations. He said his country favoured lifting sanctions on former Yugoslavia adding that Iran has a different approach to the issue. He added that the U.S., Russia and the U.N. play a key role in settling the Bosnian crisis. Daskalov declined to give an explicit comment to the question on why Europe and the U.S. did not put an end to the Bosnian tragedy.
