BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on developments related to the Panamanian elections scheduled for 8 May. Presidential Candidate Ernesto Perez Balladares, of the Democratic Revolutionary Party, PRD, met with workers of the diplomatic corps and students on 13 April. He said: "The Foreign Ministry must start recovering our authority, our true dignity, our image of a free, independent, and sovereign country in the eyes of all the world's nations, beginning 1 September." He added the ministry will have the important task of upholding the Panama Canal Treaty and "promoting our geographic position and our potential as a nation." In doing so, "we will attract the foreign investment we need to create jobs." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 13 Apr 94) Presidential Candidate Ruben Dario Carles, of the Nationalist Liberal Republican Movement (Molirena)-Change '94 Alliance, attended the Cade (Annual Conference of Business Executives) 94 conference hosted by Apede (Panamaian Association of Business Executives) on 9 April. He strongly criticized the Endara administration for "being incapable of solving the country's social problems." He said as comptroller general he contributed to development "through strict fiscal control." Early polarization among ruling parties has resulted in "paralyzation of the government's development plans." (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 10 Apr 94 p 48) Carles toured the public market, accompanied by his running mates and Panama City mayoral candidate Omayra Correa on 13 April. Carles chatted with vendors and told them he, as president, will improve conditions at the market. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 13 Apr 94) Ruben Blades, Papa Egoro Movement, MPE, presidential candidate, spoke to the Jewish charity association B'Nai B'Rith on 7 April. He said hunger and poverty and not ideological positions are the real enemies of personal wealth. He said "the comfort we enjoy today does not free us from responsibility, because that comfort could suddenly disappear if the current precepts and poor examples of private and government corruption continue to prevail." (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 8 Apr 94 p 2A) Blades took his campaign to Chepo, a rural area east of Panama City, on 11 April. He walked with the people and went for a bicycle ride. Citing recent poll results and his campaign expenditures, Blades said: "That is an example of what good administration can do. We have spent only $50,000, and we are in second place." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 11 Apr 94) The National Popular Party, PNP, announced on 12 April it will endorse the MPE and the Blades ticket for the 8 May presidential elections. Party President Jorge Flores said Blades "is responsible and honest and will do a good job." (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 12 Apr 94) Presidential candidate Mireya Moscoso de Gruber, of the ruling Arnulfist Party, PA, while campaigning in Arraijan District on 9 April, affirmed the PRD and the PA will polarize the final race for the presidency. She said "there will be two options -- a government for all Panamanians or a return to the past." (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 10 Apr 94 p 1A) In Colon, Gruber outlined her plans for the recovery of the province's economy. "There is much work to be done in Colon, but, as I have said before, I have more than enough energy and courage," Gruber said during a rally in downtown Colon. (Panama City Circuito RPC Television in Spanish 2300 GMT 11 Apr 94) Presidential Candidate Eduardo Vallarino, of the Christian Democratic Party, PDC, told reporters on 7 April Panama's image abroad is poor, but the U.S. State Department report on drug trafficking and money laundering has worsened it. He called on all candidates "to file a detailed declaration of our assets" and, what he considered more important, "an explanation of from where the millions of dollars being spent on advertising campaigns" is coming. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 8 Apr 94 p 1) Presidential candidates Perez and Carles have addressed a meeting of the Panama Canal chapter of the AFL-CIO. Perez suggested creation of a "full time presidential commission to reinforce the efforts of canal operations." He said he will "prevent expertise from leaving the country, exclude U.S. laws from the Panama Canal, and fulfill the Torrijos Carter Treaty." Meanwhile, Carles said that finally, "after 17 years, the canal will be ours, and the future belongs to all Panamanians." (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 8 Apr 94 p 2A) Presidential candidates Perez, Carles, Vallarino, and Munoz were present for a special forum hosted by the Single National Union of Construction and Related Workers on 7 April. They agreed on the need for reforms to the labor code. Vallarino said he "would not change anything which the unions did not propose." Perez said "there is no way we can ask the workers to sacrifice what they have earned." Carles said, however, any changes "will depend on what would work and how it would work. We must speak to the workers first." (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 8 Apr 94 p 4A) Vice presidential candidates Guillermo Quijano and Tomas Herrera of the Molirena-Change '94 Alliance; Tomas Altamirano Duque of the PRD; Raul Ossa of the PDC; Raul Bernal of the PPD; and Fernando Manfredo and Ricardo Bermudez of the MPE participated in the Apede and Cade annual conference on 8 April. All candidates agreed there is a "moral crisis" in Panama. Quijano said the solution lies in giving the people "real alternatives, instead of false promises." Altamirano blamed the crisis on the "unemployment rate, extreme poverty, and unequal distribution of wealth." Ossa called for a "national concerted" effort to face the problem. Manfredo and Bermudez outlined their party's government plan for facing the issue, stating the MPE is the "only party to have presented a serious plan." (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 9 Apr 94 p 1A) On April 6 the Federico Guerra Corporation released the results of its latest poll, taken between 29 March and 3 April. Perez still leads with 33.2 percent. The new figures show Blades and the MPE taking second place with 15 percent over Carles, who totaled 14.5 percent. Gruber follows with a close 14.4 percent behind Carles; Vallarino, with 2.8 percent; Lewis, with 2.4 percent, and Munoz, with 0.5 percent follow. The report states "Blades gained 3.8 percent, Carles lost 2.3 points, and Gruber increased her support by 2.2 percent" over the previous poll taken from 12 to 15 March. (Panama City LA ESTRELLA DE PANAMA in Spanish 7 Apr 94 p 4A) A special Dichter and Neira poll on the 11 April television presidential debate indicates Blades won by 36.8 percent, followed by Perez, with 30.1 percent, Carles, with 15 percent, and Vallarino, with 9.4 percent. When asked if the debate had made them change their mind about their favorite candidate, 75.9 percent said no, 11 percent gave no answer, and 15 percent said yes. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 12 Apr 94 p 1A) On 12 April Dichter and Neira also released the results of a poll taken from 8 to 10 April. The results show Perez still leads with 34.5 percent, while Carles is second, with 18.6 percent. Blades follows with 17.3 points. Gruber is next, with 11.3 points, and Vallarino, who lost some support, totaled 2.7 percent. Perez slipped five percentage points; Carles gained 1.9 percentage points; Blades won 2.9 percent, and Gruber inched up .8 percent. (Panama City LA PRENSA in Spanish 13 Apr 94 p 1A)
