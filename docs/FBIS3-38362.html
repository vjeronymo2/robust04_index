Language: Slovene Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] An annual meeting of the World Trade Forum has started in Davos. Approximately 800 businessmen and politicians from over 80 countries all over the world are taking part in this politico-economic summit. A three-day visit will be divided into three topics. The participants will first assess the situation in the international economy at the beginning of 1994 and establish the priority spheres of action. A Slovene delegation, led by Prime Minister Dr. Janez Drnovsek is also attending the Word Trade Forum. Suzana Kos reports: [Begin recording] [Kos] [passage omitted] Slovene Prime Minister Janez Drnovsek, who is attending the World Economic Forum in Davos this year, too, and will meet with important financiers and politicians here, held talks with the GATT director general this morning: [Drnovsek] With Mr. Sutherland we have had a very thorough debate about all issues that are still open in the negotiations between Slovenia and GATT. We have established that, recently the negotiations have been continuing successfully and intensively, that some open issues remain, especially regarding the protection of agriculture and the service industry, but that there are realistic chances for the resolution of these problems. Despite a tight schedule, Slovenia could still become one of the founding members of GATT, on the condition that this organization be formed, that is to say, starts working at the beginning of 1995. [Kos] As Dr. Drnovsek also said, the talks also concerned Slovenia's latest proposal for the new system of protection in agriculture. Mr. Sutherland promised his assistance here so that the answer to our proposal would be such that Slovenia could accept. This afternoon, the Slovene prime minister will also talk with the new president of the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development, which has promised us assistance in reconstruction and the development of our infrastructure. [end recording]
