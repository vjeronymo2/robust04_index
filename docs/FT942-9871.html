940513 FT 13 MAY 94 / Arts: Today's Television Yet another Friday night when the best advice is to stay away from the television ..unless your idea of a good time is watching Danny Baker ask whether Arthur Lowe appeared in adverts for Trillions sweets or Hanky Panky popcorn. That, we are promised (all right, threatened) is the sort of question in which the new quiz Bygones will specialise. Is this part of the BBC's drive towards the 'Himalayan option', the domination of the high ground of broadcasting? (7.00 BBC1). You might have hoped that Europe Express would be television's version of Christopher Booker's hair-raising column in the Sunday Telegraph, detailing the demented bureaucratic taradiddle which, week by week, reduces the quality of life in Britain, in the name of a united Europe. But no. It is a series of tabloid interviews, this week including singer Mari Boine who is 'set to' take Europe by storm (8.00 Channel 4). The oddest billing of the night is on Radio 3 in the 'Friday Feature', L'Homme Arme, presented by Jeremy Beadle who traces the history of a famous medieval song (10.45).
