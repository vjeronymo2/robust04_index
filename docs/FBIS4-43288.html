BFN [Text] Ankara--Turkey's troubled balance of payments position worsened in the first quarter of the current year as Prime Minister Tansu Ciller continues to promise to produce a balanced account by the end of the year as part of her economic recovery program. The current account deficit soared from $925 million in the first quarter of 1993 to $1.135 billion in the corresponding period of this year, an increase of 22 percent, official figures revealed on Thursday [9 June]. The Central Bank said that the current account deficit widened despite a smaller foreign trade gap. It said Turkey's foreign trade deficit in the January-March period dropped from $2.207 billion last year to $1.814 billion this year, a decline of 17.8 percent. Net unrequited official transfers declined from $183 million to $89 million. Direct foreign investment went down from $94 million last year to $51 million this year, a fall of 45.7 percent. Turkey's foreign debt repayment amounted to $1.940 billion in the first quarter of this year, compared with $1.736 billion twelve months ago.
