Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Jerusalem, March 3 (AFP) -- Israeli troops shot and wounded more than 30 Palestinians in the West Bank and Gaza Strip on Thursday [3 March], but the level of violence was down from the previous days after the Hebron massacre. Palestinian sources said 27 people were shot in the West Bank, including 10 in a clash between troops and demonstrators in the village of Nahalian near Bethlehem. Seven Arabs were hit by army bullets in Gaza, they said. Also on the strip, an alleged collaborator with the Israelis was killed. PLO chief Yasir 'Arafat's mainstream Fatah group claimed responsibility for the killing of Salim Alibani, who was 40. [passage omitted] The army also sealed off the territories on Sunday preventing 70,000 Palestinians from reaching their jobs in Israel. The closure is to be enforced for another week, according to Israeli television. Troops were on alert for Friday, the day of Moslem prayers and a week after the Hebron killings.
