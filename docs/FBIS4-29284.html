BFN [Text] Kuala Lumpur, June 16 (OANA-BERNAMA) -- The paper on proposals to attract foreign and Malaysian scientists and engineers working abroad to the country is ready and awaiting approval from the relevant government agencies, Science, Technology and Environment Minister Law Hieng Ding said Thursday. He said the working committee, headed by his deputy, Peter Chin, had finalised the paper recently and was waiting for government agencies such as the Public Services Department and Treasury to endorse it before presenting it to the cabinet for final approval. The scheme was meant to meet the country's needs for scientists and engineers to fulfil its plan to be an industrialised nation by the year 2020, he told reporters after opening an exhibition on the beauty of nature at the National Museum here. Law said that the incentive package, among others, offered the target group salaries comparable to that offered by other countries with fringe benefits including permanent resident status, if necessary.
