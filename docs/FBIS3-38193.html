Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Davos, Jan 30 (A.A) -- Turkmenistan President Saparmurad Niyazov on Sunday [30 January] presented President Suleyman Demirel and Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin with the work on its oil and natural gas pipeline projects for evaluation and examination. Speaking to an A.A. reporter following a breakfast he was hosting in honour of the heads of state of the Central Asian republics and Pakistan's Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto, Demirel said there are two possible routes for the pipeline, one being through Iran and Turkey, the other through the Caspian Sea, both reaching the Mediterranean. Demirel said that Turkmenistan favours the first choice, adding that British, French, German and Turkish companies closely follow the developments concerning these project. He said Turkey, in the coming years, will become one of the most important energy terminals in the world. Kazakhstan President Nursultan Nazarbayev, Turkmenistan's Saparmurad Niyazov, Uzbekistan's Islam Karimov, and Pakistan's Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto attended the breakfast. Following the meeting, Demirel received Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres. [passage omitted] Demirel's scheduled meeting with Slovenia's Prime Minister Janez Drnovsek did not take place and was cancelled. [passage omitted]
