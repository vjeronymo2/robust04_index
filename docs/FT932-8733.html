930518 FT 18 MAY 93 / Gross national products grows by 10 per cent in Saudi Arabia The Saudi Monetary Agency, Saudi Arabia's central bank, said yesterday that gross national product grew by an average of more than 10 per cent during the two years of the Gulf crisis and would grow more than 5 per cent in both 1992 and 1993, Reuter reports from Dubai. The figures were published in a report handed to King Fahd yesterday. It said they represented real growth, with GNP calculated at constant prices. Exact economic indicators were withheld for 1990 and 1991, the years of crisis over Iraqi occupation of Kuwait.
