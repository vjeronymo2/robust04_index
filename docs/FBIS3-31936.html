Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Yesterday, Army spokesperson Berta Edith Vargas de Marroquin reported the National Army will carry out a "military control at a distance" operation in the capital's area given the upcoming referendum on Sunday. The Government Ministry is responsible for the citizens' safety in order for the people to be able to exercise their right to vote in an appropriate atmosphere, although the Army is providing its cooperation in a coordinated way. The actions will include patrols along roads used by people traveling from one place to another, and the number of Army forces will be increased accordingly in certain areas. Although she did not specify the number of soldiers that would directly participate in this operation, she said a special operation has become traditional for electoral events in recent years. She also said tranquillity and a peaceful electoral process should be expected on Sunday due to the work done by civilian security and military forces. Members of the Attorney General's Office reported certain people have plans to obstruct the referendum; they said it involves groups that are interested is preventing the event. They added: Investigations conducted into recent terrorist attacks indicate unfortunate events may occur on Sunday, 30 January. Therefore, a contingency plan was prepared to guarantee the Guatemalan people's safety. Carlos Alvarez, a member of the Public Prosecutor's Office, and Claudio Porres, investigations director at the Attorney General's Office, said the plan will be executed on a national level and will include all security forces. Public prosecutors and investigators will also be deployed in strategic areas so they may immediately proceed to arrest people or groups found perpetrating criminal actions. The officials said: Likewise, we have information that members of the government worker unions will carry out demonstrations, but they will be immediately arrested and arraigned if they violate the law. "We trust the groups that have called for demonstrations will reconsider their decision and allow the people to freely cast their ballots so everything may be carried out normally," Alvarez and Porres said.
