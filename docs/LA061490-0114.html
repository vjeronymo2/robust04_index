June 14, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

TOP AIR POLLUTION PENALTIES

The South Coast Air Quality Management District has responsibility to control air pollution. It can seek court-imposed fines against polluters of from $25 to $25,000 a day based on such factors as the extent that emissions exceed legal limits, potential danger to the public, whether the violation was intentional, accidental or due to negligence and the company's history of violations. These are the 10 top penalties in April:

COMPANY: Laidlaw Gas Recovery Systems, Inc., Newark

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Gas-recovery firm

PENALTY: $30,000

VIOLATION: Failure to maintain the temperature of a gas-burning flare; exceeding flow-rate limits; failure to submit a performance test; failure to keep adequate records at its gas-recovery facility in Irvine.

COMPANY: Cigna Healthplans of California, Glendale

TYPE OF BUSINESS: HMO

PENALTY: $25,000

VIOLATION: Failure to submit an AQMD-approved ride-sharing plan.

COMPANY: Pauly Inc., Commerce

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Office furniture manufacturer

PENALTY: $25,000

VIOLATION: Excessive hydrocarbons from paint usage and from solvent content in paints; using non-complying equipment contrary to permit conditions.

COMPANY: OHM Corp., Anaheim

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Landfill excavation management firm

PENALTY: $19,000

VIOLATION: Excavating contaminated soil in Long Beach without an AQMD-approved plan or a valid permit

COMPANY: Century Marble, Pomona

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Marble products manufacturer

PENALTY: $10,000

VIOLATION: Failure to keep adequate records.

COMPANY: Greater El Monte Community Hospital, South El Monte

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Hospital

PENALTY: $10,000

VIOLATION: Failure to submit an AQMD-approved ride-sharing plan.

COMPANY: Swift-Cor Engineering, Gardena

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Sheet metal manufacturer

PENALTY: $10,000

VIOLATION: Exceeding limits on the solvent content of primer applied to aircraft parts.

COMPANY: Ganahl Lumber, Anaheim

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Lumber company

PENALTY: $9,500

VIOLATION: Failure to submit an AQMD-approved ride-sharing plan.

COMPANY: Fernando's Foods Corp., Compton

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Food supplier

PENALTY: $8,000

VIOLATION: Failure to submit an AQMD-approved ride-sharing plan.

COMPANY: Southern California Auto Auction, Fontana

TYPE OF BUSINESS: Auto auction firm

PENALTY: $7,800

VIOLATION: Failure to submit an AQMD-approved ride-sharing plan.
