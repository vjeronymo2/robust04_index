Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 30 KYODO -- Japan's industrial production in February grew 0.2 percent from the preceding month, the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) said Wednesday [30 March]. The growth pace was slower that January's 1.0 percent increase. The seasonally adjusted production index for the mining and manufacturing sectors stood at 88.9 against a base of 100 for 1990, MITI announced in a preliminary report. The reading compares with projections of output increases for February ranging from 0.8 to 3.0 percent made by 20 major think tanks in a survey by KYODO NEWS SERVICE earlier. A production outlook index for the manufacturing sector indicates a rise in March and a downswing in April. The future course of final demand is uncertain, necessitating further monitoring of coming movements, MITI said. Shipments climbed 1.2 percent in February, with the index standing at 91.1, compared with a 0.7 percent rise in January. The inventory index dropped 1.0 percent to 106.8, following a 0.4 percent decrease in January. The February inventory-to-sales ratio fell 2.6 percent against a 2.9 percent slide in January, with the index standing at 119.1. Industrial output had swung up and down in the six months to January -- showing a 1.2 percent fall in August, a 2.2 percent gain in September, a 5.5 percent slide in October, a 2.2 percent rise in November, a 1.9 percent drop in December and a 1.0 percent increase in January. On an unadjusted basis, industrial production in February slid 4.2 percent from a year before, extending a record string of year-to-year declines to 29 months.
