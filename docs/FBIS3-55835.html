Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Russia is ready to reduce the term of the temporary operation of its early warning radar station in Skrunda, Latvia from 6 to 5 years. This was reported to Interfax on Tuesday [11 January] by the head of the Russian delegation to a Riga conference of Russian and Latvian officials Sergey Zotov. He said the Latvian side was offered a new Russian draft agreement on the temporary status of the strategic facility. He stressed that the indicates Russia is trying to meet the Latvian side halfway. As we have reported one of Russia's conditions for the withdrawal of its remaining 13,000 troops by August 31 is the right to control the radar station in Skrunda for several years. Latvia has agreed to that in principle, but finds it necessary to reduce the length of this period. The conference in Riga is expected to continue on Wednesday and the discussion of the draft agreement on the radar station to continue during the coming round of talks in Moscow later this month. Sergey Zotov described the Monday incident involving the Russian military and Russian army facilities as a crude provocation of extremist and nationalist forces aimed at torpedoing bilateral talks as well as a test of Russia's force, its ability to protect its facilities. Zotov thinks the incident should prompt the West to conclude that Latvia is an unreliable partner. Before the meeting Sergey Zotov protested against the incident to the head of the Latvian delegation Martins Virsis and demanded a full investigation of thee incident and punishment for those responsible.
