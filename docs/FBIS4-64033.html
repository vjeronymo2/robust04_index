BFN [Text] Kiev [no dateline as received] -- Today, as the country holding the European Union [EU] Presidency, the Greek embassy in Ukraine issued a statement on events in Crimea. The document reads: "The European Union expresses grave concern at the developments in Ukraine's Crimean region and backs further efforts on the settlement of the problems on the basis of law by way of negotiations, without confrontation or unilateral actions." The statement emphasizes: "The EU once again states its full support for the territorial integrity of Ukraine in accordance with the UN Charter and Helsinki Agreements and warns against any attempts to destabilize the situation in the region, as any escalation of the conflict will lead to grave consequences for the whole of the East European region."
