930724 FT 24 JUL 93 / Finance and The Family: Directors' transactions THE SHARE price performance of electronics group Vistec over the past 2 1/2 years has been strong. Robert Morton, the group's high-profile chairman, capitalised when he bought 1.8m at between 10-13p in 1991. Now, chief executive Adrian Wallis has sold 350,000 at 29p, having disposed of 650,000 for 26p at the start of the year. Having slumbered peacefully for most of the past five years, Norbain Electronics' share price burst into life at the turn of the year, rising from a low of 58p to the present level, a record high. John Nicol, chairman and managing director, has sold 350,000 shares at 155p. But perhaps this disposal should be viewed in the context of two previous sales - one of 1m shares at 37p, the other of 350,000 for the same price - in September 1991 and 1992 respectively. There was selling this week in another electronics company, Logica. Logica has had its fair share of problems in recent times but seems to have got over the worst. In September 1990, Leonard Taylor, a non-executive director, bought 100,000 shares at 155p and sold them for 236p a few months later.
