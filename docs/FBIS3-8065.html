Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Report by Anton Mrkonjic in Zepce] [Excerpt] At 0635 this morning three 82-mm mortars fell on the British UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] base in the village of Ljeskovica, between Zepce and Zavidovici [central Bosnia, around 100 miles northwest of Sarajevo]. British forces commander Major Darling informed the command of the 111th HVO [Croatian Defense Council] Brigade that their radar equipment detected the direction and even the location from where the mortars were fired, but declined to say who was responsible for the shelling of the British battalion. Fortunately, there were no casualties, but material damage is huge. Maj. Darling said that if the shelling happens again he would demand air strikes against those who fire shells on UNPROFOR.
