Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, March 26 (KCNA) -- A national tree-planting ceremony was held here Friday on the lapse of 47 years since the great leader President Kim Il-song planted trees on Munsu Hill in Pyongyang and on the lapse of 37 years since the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il planted trees on Chang Hill in the city. Present at the ceremony were Pak Song-chol, Politburo member of the C.C. [Central Committee], the Workers' Party of Korea [WPK] and vice-president; Kim Chung-nin, secretary of the WPK Central Committee; and Kim Pok-sin, vice-premier of the Administration Council. Chu Song-il, chairman of the C.C., the General Federation of Trade Unions of Korea, made a report there which was followed by speeches. The reporter and speakers recalled that President Kim Il-song opened up a broad vista for land development, indicating the orientation, ways and tasks for reforestation and wisely led the work to carry them into practice. They also noted that Comrade Kim Chong-il instituted tree- planting day and tree-planting months to convert afforestation into a nationwide work and called for a brisk movement of tree-planting on a mass scale. As a result, mountains and fields of the country are covered with green forests and the cities and countryside have changed their appearance, looking more beautiful, they added. They referred to the task to create in the next few years 200,000 hectares of forests of oil-bearing trees and 200,000 hectares of forests of trees for industrial use. After the ceremony the participants and working people planted hundreds of thousands of trees on Munsu and Chang hills and in Nungna islet, the Taesongsan holiday resort, Kwangbok street and other places to make the capital city of Pyongyang more beautiful and covered with more luxuriant verdure.
