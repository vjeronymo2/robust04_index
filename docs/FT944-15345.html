941019 FT 19 OCT 94 / Thatcher link claim in papers on Saudi arms Ministers are to study documents which Mr Tam Dalyell, Labour MP for Linlithgow, claims show that Mr Mark Thatcher was involved in the Al Yamamah arms deal between the UK and Saudia Arabia while his mother, Baroness Thatcher, was prime minister. Mr Dalyell handed the documents, based on a report from from US intelligence and an internal British Aircraft Corporation memorandum, to officials in the Commons during the two-day defence debate. Mr Roger Freeman, minister for defence procurement, said the documents would be examined, but insisted that the deal was conducted on a government-to-government basis. He said: 'No commissions were paid, and no agents or middle men were involved.' Mr Dalyell produced the documents after Mr John Major, the prime minister, challenged MPs who supported allegations that Mr Thatcher had received Pounds 12m in commissions to produce evidence that could be examined by the proper authorities. The prime minister reaffirmed that Lady Thatcher had 'acted with complete propriety throughout her long and distinguished period as prime minister of this country'. Mr Dalyell said the documents highlighted Mr Thatcher's role in the arms deal, and referred to the part he played in administering a 'sizeable payment' to the Conservative party. He challenged a government denial that Sir Clive Whitmore, former permanent undersecretary at the Ministry of Defence, had told Lady Thatcher of his disquiet over the involvement of her son in the negotiations on the arms deal. Mr Dalyell called for a specific assurance that 'there was no contact on this issue, official or unofficial, between the permanent secretary to the Ministry of Defence and the then prime minister'. Earlier, when Mr Paul Tyler, a Liberal Democrat MP, asked for an assurance 'that your administration is now a sleaze-free zone', Mr Major said: 'I am as concerned as any member in this House or any person in this country that we should have the highest standards in public life.'
