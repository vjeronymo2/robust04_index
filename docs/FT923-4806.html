920904 FT 04 SEP 92 / Observer: Footnote Everyone knows that 'man' is the answer to the Sphinx's ancient riddle of 'what moves on four legs in the morning, two in the day, and three in the evening?' But what, besides doing that, travels on six legs late at night? The answer is a drunken customer of the Red Lion inn at Liskeard in Cornwall, where boozers who've had one too many at closing time are issued with four-footed walking frames to help them home. Landlord Chris Warden, who found the 20 frames in the cellar when he took over the pub, has fitted them with reflectors and bells for good measure. 'Be sloshed, be safe, be seen - that's my motto,' he says.
