BFN [Text] Chengdu, May 30 (XINHUA) -- Southwest China's Sichuan Province has been engaged in popularizing agricultural science and technology and has consequently reaped bumper harvests over the past few years. According to statistics from the agricultural department of the province, grain output increased by 1.3 billion kg and the farmers obtained a total extra income of 588 million yuan last year by utilizing the new technologies and techniques. As the major pork production base of the country, the province has spread scientific pig-raising techniques. Animal husbandry in the province has been developing for 17 years running. The province produced 4.l49 million tons of pork last year, 3.33 percent more than in 1992. From the beginning of this year, the provincial committee of science and technology, the provincial agricultural academy, Southwest China Agriculture University in the province and Sichuan Agriculture University have sent 100 scientists and technicians to grassroots units to contract a total area of 10,000 mu (about 666 hectares) to set examples of high-yield farmlands for the local peasants. Since 1990, governmental organs also sent more than 2,200 officials to comparatively backward counties to help residents to shake off poverty. Thousands of technicians from large enterprises work in township enterprises to help them develop products and seek markets.
