Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, 28 Feb (ITAR-TASS) -- A statement by Russia's Foreign Affairs Ministry was circulated here today which said: "According to the information we have so far, aircraft guarding the no-fly zone in Bosnia-Herzegovina air space this moring shot down four planes that had violated the zone and bombed the area at the town of Travnik, that is, in the zone of armed clashes between Muslims and Croats. Furthermore, it is alleged that these planes could belong to Bosnian Serbs, though the latter's military command denies this. The circumstances surrounding the incident are now being clarified. Whichever side carried out the military sortie over Bosnia- Herzegovina, violating the resolutions of the UN Security Council relating to the no-fly zone, it carries all the responsiblity for what has happened.
