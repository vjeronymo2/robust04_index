Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Georgy Shmelev] [Text] Bishkek January 25 TASS -- Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev arrived in Bishkek on a working visit early on Tuesday [25 January]. At Bishkek airport Kozyrev was met by Kyrgyz President Askar Akayev, Prime Minister Tursunbek Chyngyshev and other officials. This is the first foreign trip by the Russian foreign minister in 1994. In an interview with ITAR-TASS Kozyrev said that he has delivered a personal message to Akayev from President Boris Yeltsin in which he confirmed Russia's readiness to provide assistance to Kyrgyzstan to further its reform. In Bishkek Kozyrev is expected to meet with Kyrgyz leaders to discuss a wide range of issues concerning bilateral relations between the two countries, prospects for the development of the Commonwealth of Independent states and some aspects of regional and international cooperation. From Bishkek Kozyrev will fly to Blagoveshchensk, administrative centre of the Amur region in the Russian Far East, and then further to China where he will arrive on an official visit later this week.
