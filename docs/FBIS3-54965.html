Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tiraspol BASAPRESS, 3/1/1994 -- From our special correspondent: Grigore Maracuta, speaker of the Dniester Republic's Supreme Soviet, and Viktor Sinyov [name as received], deputy premier of the Dniester Republic, have visited Moscow. They attempted to have an audience with Russian Minister of Defense Pavel Grachev. Reliable sources state that Tiraspol representatives intended to suggest Grachev to annul his visit to Moldova and Romania and sign some military agreements. Maracuta and Sinyov also intended to obtain the transfer of the 14th Army commander [General Aleksandr Lebed] to another military district. However, the audience was not granted.
