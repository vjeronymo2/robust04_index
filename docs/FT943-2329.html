940920 FT 20 SEP 94 / Lecturers quit pay talks with colleges Talks between lecturers at further education colleges and their employers over new contracts broke down again yesterday, with unions accusing college principals of 'contemptuous' gestures. The National Association of Teachers in Further and Higher Education said it had abandoned national discussions, and wanted lecturers to 'go on the offensive locally'. Its dispute started with the attempted introduction of new contracts last June. National talks restarted only last week after a gap of six months. The union said that the only offer made by the Colleges' Employers' Forum, which has represented most colleges since they were removed from local authority control in April last year, was to put a limit of 1,000 hours on teaching time each year. The current maximum is 756 hours. Mr John Akker, the union's general secretary, said: 'An enormous opportunity has been lost and I am bitterly disappointed. The Colleges' Employers' Forum does not appear to understand either the job of lecturers or the concept of negotiation.' Mr Roger Ward, forum chief executive, said lecturers offering higher education or A-level courses would not be required to work more than 800 hours. He added: 'Since the beginning of this dispute, regrettably, the lay leadership of Natfhe has never produced one sensible proposal. If the full-time officers cared to put forward a proposal I would willingly examine it.'
