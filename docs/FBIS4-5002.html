BFN [Text] Urasoe -- The Urasoe City Assembly (with Ichiro Akamine as chairman) held an emergency meeting on the morning of 6 April and unanimously adopted a statement and a resolution of protest against the incident in which an F-15 jet fighter crashed the previous day. In the statement and resolution, the city assembly demanded that "a thorough investigation be made to determine the cause of the accident, clarification be made to the people of Okinawa Prefecture, and flights and exercises by F-15's be completely suspended until the cause is determined." It also demanded that "the unit to which the crashed F-15 belongs be withdrawn from Okinawa." Copies of the statement are to be sent to the prime minister and the governor of Okinawa, while copies of the resolution are to be sent to the U.S. ambassador to Japan, the Kadena Air Base commander, and other officials.
