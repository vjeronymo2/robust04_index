BFN [Excerpts] There is no coldness on the part of the Israeli Government towards Italy, only friendship: these were the words of Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi, who recalled that a Jerusalem daily newspaper today said that the views of the Israeli deputy foreign minister, Yossi Beilin, on the Italian Government were irresponsible. [passage omitted] Avi Pazner, the Israeli ambassador to Rome, met with Foreign Minister Antonio Martino for the second time in three days. This is what he told our reporter at the end of the meeting: [Begin recording] [Pazner] We had a good meeting with Foreign Minister Martino. We spoke of the relations between Israel and Italy -- relations which we hope will continue to be good in the future. We spoke of the future meeting between Martino and Shim'on Peres in Luxembourg next Monday [13 June]. [Correspondent Paolo Borelli] Will the misunderstandings be overcome? Will this cultural protocol be signed? [Pazner] We hope that the misunderstandings will be overcome because there is a willingness on the part of Italy and Israel to overcome these difficulties. [Borelli] In general, does the Israeli Government remain concerned at the presence of members of the National Alliance in our government? [Pazner] There is a debate currently taking place in Israel. All that has happened in the last few days is a result of this debate. We must now find a way of continuing the good relations which exist between Israel and Italy. [end recording]
