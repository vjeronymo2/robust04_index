August 7, 1990, Tuesday, Valley Edition

REGIONAL EARNINGS: TRANSAMERICA INSURANCE GROUP PROFIT FALLS 27% IN QUARTER

Transamerica Insurance Group in Woodland Hills, citing lower investment gains, said its second-quarter profit fell 27% from a year earlier, despite a 13% increase in revenue.

In the quarter that ended June 30, Transamerica Insurance earned $20 million, down from $27.5 million, while its revenue climbed to $526.1 million from $465.4 million.

The earnings decline reflected a 54% drop in the company's gains from investment transactions, to $4.14 million from $8.95 million.

For the first half of 1990, Transamerica Insurance's net income fell 14% to $33.6 million from $39.2 million a year earlier, and its six-month revenue rose 10% to $1.05 billion from $931.5 million.

The company is the property/casualty insurance unit of Transamerica Corp., a financial services concern based in San Francisco.
