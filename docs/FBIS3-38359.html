Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Frankfurt/Main (DPA) -- According to IG Metall [metal workers' trade union], more than 107,000 employees in 482 companies had participated in the warning strikes in the west German metal industry by midday Tuesday [1 February]. According to the Frankfurt headquarters of the trade union, more metal workers have been called upon to participate in rallies in the afternoon, for instance in the Hesse towns of Giessen and Kassel, as well as in Kempten in Bavaria. The focus of the activities was North Rhine-Westphalia, where, according to IG Metall, about 57,000 workers from 351 companies went on strike for one to three hours. At a total of eight rallies --- in Dueren, Hattingen, Siegburg, and Luedenscheid, among other places -- the metal workers protested the employers' wage policy. The trade union counted about 20,000 participants in various protest rallies in Lower Saxony. In Bavaria, which was the focus of the protests yesterday, more than 13,000 metal workers participated in warning strikes. In Baden-Wuerttemberg 9,000 employees from 14 companies went on strike for up to four hours. Further protest actions took place in Hesse, Rhineland-Palatinate, Saarland, and Berlin.
