BFN [Text] Today a plenary session of the Saeima adopted at its second reading the Latvian citizenship bill. The bill determines that Latvian citizens are persons who were Latvian citizens on 17 June 1940, as well as those persons' descendants who have registered themselves in the manner laid down by law, excepting persons who, since 21 August 1991, have obtained citizenship of another country -- that is, those who have obtained another country's citizenship since 21 August 1991 [sentence as heard]. According to this bill, citizens are also persons who have been naturalized or who have otherwise obtained citizenship of the Republic of Latvia in the manner determined by law; foundlings and orphans living in Latvian children's homes or boarding schools, as well as children, both of whose parents were citizens of the Republic of Latvia at the moment of their birth, irrespective of the child's place of birth. The Saeima plenary session is still continuing.
