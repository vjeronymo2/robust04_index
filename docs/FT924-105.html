921231 FT 31 DEC 92 / Foreign investment in former communist states tripled Foreign investment in the former communist states of eastern Europe and the Soviet Union tripled in the year to end-September with 719 acquisitions, joint ventures and green field projects worth Dollars 28bn (Pounds 18.4bn), according to East European Investment magazine, a New York-based publication, writes Anthony Robinson. Joint ventures account for 351 of the 719 projects while 72 per cent of all deals were signed with only five host countries - Russia, Hungary, Poland, former east Germany and the Czech Republic. US companies emerge as the biggest investors in the 28 countries of the former Soviet bloc with 219 acquisitions, joint ventures and greenfield investments amounting to commitments of nearly Dollars 8bn. Russia, Poland and Hungary were the principal targets for US investment with over 40 per cent of US investment, or Dollars 3.2bn, pledged to Russia alone. European and Arab oil companies have also been active in the oil and energy sector, particularly in former Soviet republics like Kazakhstan, Azerbaijan and Turkmenistan.
