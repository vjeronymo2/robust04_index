September 30, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

SONGWRITER IRVING BERLIN DIES

Your editorial ("America's Music Master," Sept. 26) on the passing away of Irving Berlin brought to mind an episode that happened while I was an American military prisoner-of-war in Germany in February, 1945. Wounded and then captured in the Battle of the Bulge, my spirits were very low because of a lack of regular food and the depressing influences of the war. While walking toward the main gate, I suddenly heard the German loudspeaker system play a wonderful and marvelous recording of the late Bing Crosby singing Irving Berlin's "Blue Skies."

When I heard the song, my low spirits picked up; I determined then and there to overcome any obstacles and to make it through to the end. Forty-four years later, I still marvel at Berlin's wonderful songs and in particular that one significant song that carried me through that crisis and a safe return to this choice and free land.

KENNETH LLOYD LARSON

Los Angeles
