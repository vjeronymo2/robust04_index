920730 FT 30 JUL 92 / International Company News: Bethlehem Steel warning BETHLEHEM Steel, the second largest US steel manufacturer, yesterday reported increased second-quarter net losses and warned it expected to remain in the red in the third quarter. The company reported net losses of Dollars 64m, or 92 cents a share, compared with Dollars 29m, or 46 cents a share, in the same period of last year, on net sales of Dollars 1.0bn, down from Dollars 1.1bn. The results, which included a Dollars 25m litigation contingency charge, were in line with a previous warning by the company. Bethlehem said it expected a moderate improvement in steel demand for the balance of 1992. An expected improvement in the economy, higher levels of steel demand and remedies against unfair steel imports should provide opportunities for improved results later this year and in 1993, it added.
