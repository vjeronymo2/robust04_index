940919 FT 19 SEP 94 / World News in Brief: European Monetary System In a week which saw some D-Mark weakness against other currencies, the D-Mark managed to hang on to its third place in the EMS grid. Its spread against the peseta, the weakest currency in the system, fell to 4.89 per cent from just over 5 per cent. Currencies, Page 37 The chart shows the member currencies of the exchange rate mechanism measured against the weakest currency in the system. Most of the currencies are permitted to fluctuate within 15 per cent of agreed central rates against the other members of the mechanism. The exceptions are the D-Mark and the guilder which move in a 2.25 per cent band.
