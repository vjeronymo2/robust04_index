Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Text] Some of the documents prepared for signing at the Russian-Kazakhstan Summit have been withdrawn from the discussions, a POSTFACTUM correspondent learned on 25 March from informed sources in the Kazakhstani Cabinet of Ministers. Minister Sergey Tereshchenko. [Moscow 2x2 Television in Russian at 1330 GMT on 26 March 1994 repeats this report adding the following information at this point: "The treaties on customs union and dealing with the consequences of nuclear testing at the Semipalatinsk nuclear testing ground have been withdrawn from the talks at the initiative of the Russian side, which also declined a protocol on extending the agreement on retaining specialized defense industry enterprises and a protocol on implementing the agreement on cooperation on developing fuel and energy complexes."] As POSTFACTUM learned from the same sources, the fate of the agreement on the status of the Baykonur cosmodrome is also in doubt, despite the fact that the Kazakhstani side has made significant concessions on the amount of lease payments to be made.
