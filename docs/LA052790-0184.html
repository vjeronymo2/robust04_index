THE WEEK'S FILM PICKS, BY TIMES CRITICS: IT'S TOON TIME FOR DISNEY AND TMC; OTHER CHOICES INCLUDE THREE ATMOSPHERIC FILMS AND A WESTERN SPOOF

WHO FRAMED ROGER RABBIT

Dense, satisfying, feverishly inventive and a technical marvel, this 1988 live-action/animation blockbuster hit is a sort of inked-in film noir with outrageous sight gags. Bob Hoskins stars as a seedy gumshoe, and the film's mystery plot involves a last, great roundup of cartoon stars. Directed by Robert Zemeckis, with Oscar winner Richard Williams as director of animation.
