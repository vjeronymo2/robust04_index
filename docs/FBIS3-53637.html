Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] President Sardar Farooq Ahmad Leghari has said that Pakistan has no intention of interfering in Afghanistan's internal affairs, but it is ready to play a role for peace there with the consent of the Afghan leaders. He was talking to the Afghan president's special emissary, Engineer Ahmad Shah Ahmedzai, who apprised the president about the latest situation in Afghanistan. Expressing deep concern at the present crisis in Afghanistan, Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari said that the entire Islamic world, including Pakistan, is anguished at the outbreak of fighting in Afghanistan. He said that is why he and Prime Minister Ms. Benazir Bhutto have appealed to all the Afghan groups to observe a cease-fire to provide an opportunity to friendly countries to forge an agreement between the rival groups. The president urged the Afghan leaders to adopt the path of dialogue to end their differences peacefully for the sake of preserving Afghanistan's territorial integrity. Reports say fighting resumed in the Afghan capital Kabul this morning after an overnight lull. A spokesman of Prime Minister Golboddin Hekmatyar has reported further aerial attacks and artillery shelling.
