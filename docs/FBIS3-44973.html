Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] Beijing Central Television Program One Network in Mandarin at 0805 GMT on 5 January broadcasts the 13th and last in a series of 13 lectures on "Deng Xiaoping's Theory of Building Socialism With Chinese Characteristics." An editorial reporet describing the content of the 12th lecture in the series was published in the Political & Social section of the 7 January China DAILY REPORT, page 12. The 13th lecture, which lasts for 25 minutes, is entitled: "Further Strengthen Party Building According to the Requirements of the 14th CPC National Congress," and is given by Dai Zhou, director of the Propaganda Bureau under the Propaganda Department of the CPC Central Committee. The last lecture begins by underlining the important role of the CPC in leading the Chinese people to establish a socialist market economy and achieve the second-step strategic goal of quadrupling the gross national product by the end of this century. Therefore, Dai maintains, it is imperative to build the party into a strong core of leadership in the great undertaking of building socialism with Chinese characteristics. Discussing measures for strengthening party building, Dai stresses: Efforts must be made to persistently take Deng Xiaoping's theory of building socialism with Chinese characteristics as the basic guideline in strengthening the building of the party in accordance with its basic line. Moreover, it is necessary to strictly handle party affairs to consolidate a high degree of the party's unity organizationally and in action. Explaining the significance of party building under the new situation of reform and opening up, Dai points out that efforts should be made to improve the party's style of work, promote clean government, and fight corruption throughout the entire process of reform and opening the country to the outside world. At 0830 GMT the lecture concludes by calling for continuously strengthening and improving party building in the light of new circumstances and problems as well as newly-acquired experience.
