Language: Russian Article Type:CSO [Article by Andrey Novikov: "Quite Official; It Is Easy To Become a Citizen in Our Country"] [Text] In connection with making changes to the law of the Russian Federation "On RSFSR Citizenship," the president of Russia signed an edict that concretely defines the procedure for the examination of questions of citizenship in the Russian Federation. This document does not affect the interests of Russians who are residing permanently on the territory of the Russian Federation and who have the passport of a citizen of the USSR that indicates Russian Federation citizenship. No kinds of additional documents will have to be drawn up for them. It concerns only those who desire to become Russians or to acquire the citizenship of another state. Those who have Russian relatives will have to submit an application to the organs of Internal Affairs for acquiring citizenship, a copy of their birth certificate, and also confirmation of the relationship with the Russian citizens. To become Russians, foreigners and persons without citizenship must submit an appropriate petition addressed to the president of Russia. Parents who are citizens of different states, in selecting the citizenship of their child submit to the organs of Internal Affairs a copy of the birth certificate and written agreement on one or another citizenship of the child. Those who desire to drop their Russian citizenship can submit an appropriate petition addressed to the president and, in addition, a declaration that there are no property claims against them on the part of physical and juridical persons of Russia. Draft-age youths must also have a certificate from the military commissariat, which corroborates that a notice on callup into the Army was not sent to them. Verification of documents concerning a change of citizenship will be conducted by organs of Internal Affairs, and also by the Federal Counterintelligence Service of the Russian Federation. They will arrive at a reasoned conclusion and report their decision to the persons concerned.
