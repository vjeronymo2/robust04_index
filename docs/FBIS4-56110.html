BFN [Text] India and Bulgaria have signed six agreements for promoting cooperation in various fields. The agreements were signed in Sofia yesterday on the first day of the state visit of the president, Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma, to Bulgaria. An important agreement signed by the two countries relates to cooperation in combating international terrorism and illegal trafficking in narcotic drugs. The home minister, Mr. S.B. Chavan, signed the agreement on behalf of India. The other agreements relate to cooperation in tourism, avoidance of double taxation and for cooperation in plant protection measures and in veterinary and sanitation areas. Earlier, the president, Dr. Sharma, had talks with the Bulgarian president, Dr. Zhelyu Zhelev. The talks later continued at delegation levels. The Bulgarian president supported India's stand on Kashmir and urged New Delhi and Islamabad to resolve the issue through bilateral talks under the Simla Agreement. An official spokesman said Dr. Sharma appreciated Bulgaria's consistent support to issues of concern to India. The two leaders also discussed bilateral issues and matters of regional importance. Speaking at a banquet hosted in his honor by the Bulgarian president, Dr. Sharma underlined the need for closer economic cooperation between the two countries. He said the changes which have taken place in both the countries have opened up fresh avenues of cooperation and urged entrepreneurs to take full advantage of the emerging markets. He said the Indo-Bulgarian friendship is motivated by the desire to work for a peaceful world and harmonious humanity.
