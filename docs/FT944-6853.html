941128 FT 28 NOV 94 / Business Travel: Literary snapshot A galaxy of writers, including seven Nobel laureates, published a literary snapshot of the world last week with diaries of a day plucked at random: April 29 1994. The French magazine Le Nouvel Observateur compiled accounts by 240 writers and called them A Day in the World. Colombia's Gabriel Garcia Marquez says he was woken on April 29 by a telephone call to his hotel room and a woman's voice saying: 'Felipe expects you for dinner at eight.' A frequent traveller, the writer checked his location by consulting the sign he had posted by his bedside the night before: 'I am in Madrid.' His dinner host was Spanish prime minister Felipe Gonzalez.
