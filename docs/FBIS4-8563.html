BFN [Text] Prague, April 12 (CTK) -- Cooperation in fighting the drug mafia and drug terrorism was discussed by Czech Foreign Minister Josef Zieleniec and visiting Colombian Foreign Minister Nohemi Sanin Posada at their meeting today, the two ministers told journalists after the meeting. The integration structures of Europe and America such as, for example, the Partnership for Peace project, the Organization of American States, NATO and the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) were also discussed, Posada said, adding that Columbia was interested in joining NAFTA. The two ministers discussed bilateral Czech-Colombian relations. Posada mentioned the prospects of concluding mutual agreements, especially an agreement on investment protection. The necessity of strengthening concrete cooperation between the two countries' businessmen and cooperation in the cultural sphere was stressed. At the end of their meeting Posada presented Zieleniec with Order of St. Charles, the civic distinction which the Colombian Foreign Ministry awards to foreigners contributing to the development of relations with Columbia. The decision to award it Zieleniec was made by Colombian President Cesar Gaviria Trujillo in late March. In his Decree, Trujillo pointed to Zieleniec's contribution to pursuing the principles of international co-existence, mutual respect and solidarity between governments and nations and to the development of new relations of friendship and cooperation between the Czech Republic and Latin American countries, including Columbia.
