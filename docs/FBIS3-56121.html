Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] The capital of Belarus is preparing for the arrival of Bill Clinton. The U.S. president will spend no more than six hours in Minsk. Clinton's visit on 15 January will include talks wih the heads of the parliament and government of Belarus, a speech to Belarusian young people in the Academy of Sciences, and a visit to Kuropaty, the site of mass shootings in the 1930's. Belarusian Foreign Minister Petr Kravchenko said it is possible that several bilateral documents will be signed, including documents on protecting investments, on cooperation in science, and banking. The president's wife Hillary Clinton will visit medical establishments where Chernobyl victims are being treated. Petr Kravchenko said Bill Clinton's visit was an acknowledgement that Belarus is most logically and consistently conducting a policy of nuclear disarmanent. [passage omitted]
