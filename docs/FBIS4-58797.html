BFN [Text] The Joint Jordanian-Egyptian Higher Committee has held its final meeting at the Prime Ministry. The session was cochaired by Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali and Egyptian Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi. At the conclusion of the session, the minutes of the committee meetings were signed. Our reporter Jawad al-'Umari has the details: [Begin al-'Umari recording] During the final session, the Jordanian and Egyptian sides approved the minutes drafted by the preparatory committee of the Higher Jordanian-Egyptian Joint Committee, for its 11th session, held in Amman from 2-5 May under the chairmanship of Dr. Ziyad Fariz, the planning minister and acting minister of industry and trade, and Dr. Yusuf Butrus-Ghali, the Egyptian minister of state for international cooperation. The minutes were signed for Jordan by Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali and for fraternal Egypt by Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi. The minutes stressed the important role that can be played by Egypt and Jordan in restoring Arab solidarity and formulating a unified Arab position on various issues. This is in addition to finding a mechanism for coordination and consultation between the two countries and informing each other of their respective positions on various issues. Egypt will also support Jordan's request to join the GATT and help Jordan benefit from Egypt's experience since joining the GATT. The minutes further stated that the two sides welcome the launching of Palestinian self-rule in Gaza and Jericho as a step down the road to achieving a just, lasting, and comprehensive solution in the region. In the field of economic cooperation, the minutes reviewed the status of the holding company, the Egyptian-Jordanian Investment and Development Company, as well as the company's current and future plans being implemented in both countries. The minutes also highlighted cooperation between the two fraternal countries and ways of developing further cooperation in trade, customs affairs, planning, industry, specifications and standards, energy, mining, agriculture, transportation, communications, health and medicine, education, tourism, culture and information, sports and youth, and manpower. In addition, the minutes recommended holding the next round of talks in Cairo during the second half of November of this year. Prime Minister Dr. 'Abd-al-Salam al-Majali and Egyptian Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi are now holding a news conference to discuss the meetings and answer reporters' questions on various issues. [end recording]
