BFN [Text] Thailand and Japan have agreed to cooperate in the preservation of biodiversity in accordance with the Earth Summit resolution. Director of the National Science and Technology Development Agency Yongyut Yutthawong has signed a memorandum of understanding with his Japanese counterpart to jointly implement the project to preserve biodiversity. According the United Nations Convention of Biodiversity, signed by 156 countries on 29 December 1993, biodiversity will be strictly preserved at cooperative efforts among signatory countries -- Thailand and Japan included. Mr. Yongyut says the cooperation between Thailand and Japan will enhance the conservation and protection of biodiversity as well as reasonable utilization of flora and fauna, including equal sharing of benefit from the biodiversity. The two countries will exchange data, information, and researchers in the field of biodiversity.
