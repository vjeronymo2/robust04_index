August 9, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

KEEPING FRESH BASIL

While basil should ideally be used as soon as possible after purchase; it will keep well for several days if treated like a bouquet.

Strip leaves from lower ends of stems -- if leaves are immersed in water, they'll rot, and the fouled water will hasten the decay of the whole bunch.

If roots are attached, leave them on; otherwise, cut a slice from the end of each stem, then set them in water to come up 3 or 4 inches.

In hot weather, the bunch should be kept in the refrigerator, but if temperatures are moderate the basil bouquet can sit right on the kitchen counter where it will both look pretty and remind you to use it up. LESLIE LAND
