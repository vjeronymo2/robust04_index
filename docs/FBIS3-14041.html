Language: Urdu Article Type:BFN [Text] Nawabzada Nasrullah Khan, chairman of National Assembly Kashmir Committee, said that the Kashmiri people are determined to free themselves of Indian dominance. Speaking at a fast-breaking party in Muzaffargarh, he said the Kashmiris have risen against the repression of Indian troops and seek to achieve freedom. He said the Kashmir issue can only be solved through a plebiscite. Nawabzada Nasrullah Khan said that he will call a meeting of the Kashmir Committee on the 20th of this month, which will be constituted of delegations to be sent abroad to mobilize world public opinion against the grave human rights violations in occupied Kashmir. According to APP, he said that he was not happy with the deferment of the voting on the resolution on Kashmir at the Human Rights Commission in Geneva. Mohammad Nawaz Sharif, Pakistan Muslim League-Nawaz Group president, and Maulana Mohammad Ajmal Khan, acting chief of Jamiat-i-Ulema-i-Islam, have also criticized the deferment of the Pakistan resolution on Kashmir. Hamid Mufti, a member of Azad Jammu and Kashmir Council, has commended the efforts being made by Prime Minister Benazir Bhutto's government to raise the Kashmir issue at various international agencies. Commenting on the deferment of the voting on the Pakistan resolution on Kashmir at the UN Human Rights Commission, he said the objectives for moving the resolution have been achieved. He asked the political forces to commend the government's efforts due to which India has allowed a Muslim delegation to visit occupied Kashmir to investigate human rights violations. Pakistan Solidarity Front Chairman Nabid Malik has also commended the government's efforts due to which India has been forced to allow the ambassadors of Muslim countries, Amnesty International, and other human rights agencies to visit occupied Kashmir. *
