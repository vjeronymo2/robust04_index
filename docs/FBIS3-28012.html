Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Seoul, Feb. 2 (YONHAP) -- Kim Tae-chung, former opposition leader and chairman of the Peace Foundation for the Asia and pacific region, plans to visit China as early as March. One of his aides said Tuesday that Kim is slated to deliver a lecture at the Pacific Historical Society and receive an emeritus professorship from Nankai University. Kim is also pushing to meet with Chinese leaders during his visit, he said. One foundation official said a Kim aide visited China from Jan. 19-25 and discussed Kim's itinerary with the Korean Embassy in Beijing and Chinese officials. At that time, the aide received a proposal to grant an emeritus professorship to Kim from Nankai University, said the official, who will visit China to finalize Kim's itinerary. The official said he believed Kim Tae-chung would be able to meet with Chinese Government leaders.
