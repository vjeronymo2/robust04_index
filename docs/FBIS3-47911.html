Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 20 (KCNA) -- The North headquarters of the National Alliance for the Country's Reunification (Pomminnyon), the Committee for the Peaceful Reunification of the Fatherland (CPRF), the Central Committee of the Democratic Front for the Reunification of the Fatherland (DFRF), the North headquarters of the National Alliance of Youth and Students for the Country's Reunification (Pomchonghaknyon) and the Central Committee of the Korean Christians Federation (KCF) today sent messages of condolence to the funeral committee of Rev. Mun Ik-hwan, a South Korean patriotic champion of national reunification. The messages expressed deep condolences to the bereaved family and the members of the funeral committee over the death of Rev. Mun Ik-hwan, who devotedly struggled for independence, democracy and reunification. The CPRF said that though he passed away, the feats he performed for national reunification and his patriotic spirit would always remain in the memory of the Koreans in the North and the South struggling together for reunification in the `90s. The C.C., DFRF expressed the belief that the South Korean people and students desirous of national reunification would turn their grief at the loss of a standardbearer of the movement for reunification and democracy into the will for reunification and invigorate the patriotic struggle for independence, democracy and reunification. Former war correspondent of the Korean People's Army Yi In-mo, the incarnation of the faith and will, also sent a message of condolence to the bereaved family of Rev. Mun. Recalling that Rev. Mun visited him in sickbed at the hospital of the Pusan University last year and told him that they would meet each other again after the reunification of the country, he expressed the hope that the desire for reunification cherished by Rev. Mun in his lifetime would be realised.
