Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, 22 Mar--Parliamentary democracy and market economy are the foundations of the current government program, Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak said on 22 March when addressing about 200 participants in an international forum in Warsaw that had been organized by the FINANCIAL TIMES under the slogan "Doing business in Poland." The prime minister emphasized that his government would observe discipline in monetary and budget policies and that it would continue the privatization process, while also involving foreign capital. He pointed out that it was necessary to achieve public support for the economic reforms being implemented. "We want the reforms to affect not only the elite but also the whole of society," he said. Polish President Lech Walesa, in a letter sent to forum participants, pointed out that "Poland has a central position between the East and the West, the North and the South, and, consequently, it must be part of the integrated continent because this is in the interest of the whole of Europe. Poland is a country of enormous possibilities and a place where good business can be done. To place your stakes in Poland means to choose success," the president added.
