Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS diplomatic correspondent] [Text] Moscow, 12 Feb -- The telephone conversation between the Russian and U.S. presidents over the Bosnian problem took place on 11 February, rather than on 9 or 10 February as expected earlier. This delay caused speculations in the media over the state of repair of the Russian Federation communications system. On this matter, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev told ITAR-TASS today that "there are absolutely no technical communications problems on our part, it would be better to ask the other side about this." "All allegations that one cannot get a line to Moscow are absolutely unfounded," he emphasized. Andrey Kozyrev also said that Russian President Boris Yeltsin is in good shape and he has had a phone conversation with him. "There are no problems at all in this respect either," the minister remarked.
