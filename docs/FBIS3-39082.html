Language: French Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Audiovisual: European Union-U.S. Dialogue Reopened Soon"] [Text] After adopting a tougher tone at the beginning of January, the United States now seems to be ready for dialogue with the European Union (EU) on the audiovisual issue. "This is very important for our country," Joan Spero, U.S. undersecretary of state for the economy, said on Thursday [3 February] after discussions with her French and European Commission colleagues. "At the same time, we would like to find a means of establishing a somewhat calmer dialogue." Top U.S. and European officials met this week in the framework of the "transatlantic declaration" which, since the end of the 1980's, has enabled Washington and Brussels to discuss their differences every six months instead of launching trade wars. The audiovisual sphere is still a considerable bone of contention, and U.S. Trade Representative Mickey Kantor stepped up the pressure in January, announcing an offensive against European aid systems, which he regards as discriminatory. The end of the GATT negotiations in December did not make it possible to clear up the matter. The audiovisual sector is mentioned in the agreement, although the EU has not given any pledge to reduce its subsidies to the producers of programs or to dismantle legislation such as the quotas for the distribution of European works. For the Europeans, and especially France, this amounts to the "cultural exception" which had been demanded. "As far as we are concerned, the matter is settled," a Commission spokesman said. But Washington does not see things that way and has not given up trying to force the opening up of European borders to products of its audiovisual industry, which generate export revenue of over $3.5 billion per year.
