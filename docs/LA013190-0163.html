January 31, 1990, Wednesday, P.M. Final

BRETT STUNS ALL WITH SHARP DEMAND FOR MORE MONEY

Kansas City Royals superstar George Brett has stunned his employers, teammates and fans with a sharply worded demand for a heftier contract.

Brett's remarks, which became public after an interview with the Spokane (Wash.)

Spokesman-Review, included a suggestion that he should be traded if the Royals will not renegotiate his current deal of $1.5 million a year.

Brett also listed six teams -- Los Angeles, Boston, St. Louis, San Diego, Texas and the Chicago Cubs -- he would be willing to play for if the Royals want to trade him rather than pay him.

Brett signed a so-called lifetime deal in 1984, an arrangement that includes a $1.5-million salary through 1991 with options for two years beyond that. Real-estate deals and other considerations were included that would keep Brett on the Royals' payroll indefinitely.

But Brett, 36, apparently has been chafing over huge salaries being paid to players without records approaching his own. So last Friday, on a business trip to Spokane, Brett made his demands known to the Spokesman-Review, which ran the story Tuesday.

"I understand that, yeah, I did sign a contract," Brett was quoted as saying. "I've got to play out that contract, and I will honor that contract, but right now I'm not looking forward to going to spring training unless my attitude changes."

Brett characterized his relationship with the Royals as shaky and said the contract problems are disturbing his off-season conditioning routine.

Royals General Manager John Schuerholz confirmed that he had spoken with both Brett and his agent-brother Bobby in a frantic 24-hour period that began Monday.

"I talked to George very briefly," Schuerholz said. "At the moment, that's all I will say on the subject. I intend to talk to him again."
