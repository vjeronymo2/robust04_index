BFN [Text] Cairo, 30 May (INA) -- Foreign Minister Muhammad Sa'id al-Sahhaf, the head of Iraq's delegation to the Non-Aligned conference met with Norodom Sira, the foreign minister of Vietnam [name and title as received] here today. Mr. al-Sahhaf indicated to the Vietnamese minister a shift favoring Iraq in the UN Security Council. He also noted that more permanent and nonpermanent members of the council, as well as the international community, are demanding an end to the sanctions. Al-Sahhaf further noted that Iraq was complying with the obligations stipulated in Security Council resolutions. Al-Sahhaf impressed on the Vietnamese minister the need for the Council to honor its obligations to Iraq. He hoped that when the council next reviews Iraq's compliance with the Security Council resolutions, it will take a fair and clear-cut stand leading to the lifting of the sanctions. The two ministers also talked about relations between Iraq and Vietnam. The IRAQI NEWS AGENCY correspondent in Cairo reports that the Vietnamese minister renewed his country's support for removing the sanctions as a matter of urgency. The Vietnamese Government and people, he said, view Iraq's performance under more than three years of the sanctions with pride and admiration. The two ministers also reviewed the agenda of the Non-Aligned conference, which opens tomorrow, both agreeing on the need for the movement to be strengthened and its principles upheld. Iraq's permanent Arab League delegate attended the session.
