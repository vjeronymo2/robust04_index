BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on Guatemalan economic developments monitored through 28 June. The Guatemalan Energy and Mines Ministry reported on 21 June that crude oil production increased 13.2 percent during the first two months of 1994. During that same period, imports of petroleum and petroleum by-products increased 51 percent. (Mexico City NOTIMEX in Spanish 0031 GMT 22 Jun 94) Official sources confirmed on 22 June that the World Bank has granted Guatemala a loan for $7 million to be invested in a low-income housing project in El Mezquital. (Panama City ACAN in Spanish 1601 GMT 22 Jun 94) Economy Minister Eduardo Gonzalez has announced that private sugar producers in Guatemala and Mexico are seeking a formula to permit the entry of Guatemalan sugar into the Mexican market. Mexico recently implemented restrictions on the import of sugar from Guatemala to protect Mexican producers. (Guatemala City PRENSA LIBRE in Spanish 22 Jun 94 p 79) Bank of Guatemala President Willy Zapata said on 22 June that macroeconomic indicators for the past few days have been adequate, particularly the inflation rate. Inflation in March was 14.14 percent, 13.91 percent in April, and is currently 12.36 percent. He added that should this trend continue, the goal established for 1994, which is 12 percent, will be achieved. (Guatemala City SIGLO VEINTIUNO in Spanish 23 Jun 94 p 6)
