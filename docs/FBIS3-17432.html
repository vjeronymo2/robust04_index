Language: English Article Type:BFN [Unattributed article: "Russia/Italy: Andreatta Meets Yeltsin"] [Text] Moscow, 10 Mar (ANSA) -- Foreign Minister Beniamino Andreatta of Italy conferred today in the Kremlin with Russian President Boris Yeltsin who was quoted as telling his guest that Russian-Italian relations "are successfully developing in the political as well as the economic, humanitarian and technical and scientific fields." The Russian leader also said, "What stirs our admiration is that Italian authorities interpret partnership between Russia and Western countries in the true sense of the word. This makes it possible to construct equitable and productive relations," added Yeltsin. Also taking part in the meeting were Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and the president's advisor on international affairs, Dimitriy Ryurikov. At the end of this meeting, Andreatta went to the Kozyrev's residence where the two foreign ministers continued their talk. The Italian minister arrived in Moscow in the evening yesterday. In the afternoon today, after a meeting with journalists at the Italian Embassy here, Andreatta will leave Moscow to travel to Tallinn and Vilnius for talks with Estonian and Lithuanian leaders. Andreatta arrived here in his role as duty-president of the Conference on Security and Cooperation in Europe and the Group of Seven ministerial councils. His talks were expected to center on Bosnia, the CSCE activities and on greater participation by Russia in the context of the Group of Seven leading industrial nations, scheduled to hold their annual summit in Naples in early July.
