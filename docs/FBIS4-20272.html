BFN [Text] The Bolivian foreign affairs and finance ministers begin an official visit to Spain today in Madrid in search of business partners among Spanish firms for their state telecommunications, electricity, petrol, gas, air transport, and railway companies. Bolivian Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar Quiroga and Finance Minister Fernando Alvaro Cosio [name as received], have a full agenda including meetings with King Juan Carlos, Prime Minister Felipe Gonzalez, Foreign Minister Javier Solana, as well as Justice Minister Alberto Belloch, Interior Minister Antoni Asuncion, and Trade Minister Javier Gomez-Navarro. In addition, they will have a business breakfast with Spanish businessmen. Bolivia has started a program of state company privatizations in which the private partner will take total control of the management for an ongoing term and will be the sole owner of the profits. Spanish aid for Bolivia is another item on the agenda. Since 1989 Spain has granted credits to Bolivia worth $200 million, half of which have been used, while the rest is waiting for the Bolivian projects to be financed to be identified.
