BFN [Text] Pyongyang, April 22 (KCNA) -- A meeting marking the first anniversary of the publication of "10-point Programme of the Great Unity of the Whole Nation for the Reunification of the Country", an immortal classical work of the great leader President Kim Il-song, was held in Lima on April 8 under the co-sponsorship of the Peru-Korea Institute of Culture and Friendship and the Peruvian Committee for Supporting the Independent and Peaceful Reunification of Korea. Angel Castro Lavarello, president of the National Executive Committee of the Left Revolutionary Union of Peru and chairman of the Peru-Korea Institute of Culture and Friendship, Oscar Felipe Venture, secretary general of the Peruvian Committee for Supporting the Independent and Peaceful Reunification of Korea, and Juan Mariategui, director of the Peruvian Institute [words indistinct] and African Studies, made speeches at the meeting. The speakers said the "10-point Programme of the Great Unity of the Whole Nation for the Reunification of the Country" put forward by the great leader President Kim Il-song is the most fair and aboveboard programme of reunification which makes it possible to achieve the national reunification through the great unity of the whole nation in view of the situation prevailing on the Korean peninsula. They expressed full support to it. A Peruvian initiative committee for a signature campaign to support the 10-point programme of the great unity of the whole nation was organized at the meeting and a signature campaign was conducted. An enlarged meeting of the Executive Committee of the Ghana-DPRK Friendship Association was held in Accra on April 7 on the occasion of the first anniversary of the publication of the 10-point programme of the great unity of the whole nation for the reunification of the country. The meeting discussed the question of conducting a signature campaign nationwide in support of the 10-point programme. A committee for the promotion of the signature campaign was formed and it was decided to launch the signature campaign across the country. Participants in the meeting signed a signature paper in support of the 10-point programme.
