Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] Simferopol -- On 10 March, Crimean President Yuriy Meshkov signed a decree on conducting a poll of citizens of the Republic of Crimea on 27 March, at the same time as elections to the Supreme Councils of Ukraine and Crimea. The document says: with reference to the constitutionally consolidated principle of the sovereignty of the people, in accordance with which the bearer of sovereignty and the only source of state power are the people, which are made up of citizens of the Republic of Crimea, and with the aim of taking account more fully of the will of Crimean citizens when resolving important state issues directly affecting the interests of citizens, in accordance with articles 2, 34, 129, and [number indistinct] of the Constitution of Crimea, I resolve: 1. To conduct a poll of citizens of the Republic of Crimea on 27 March. 2. To present the following questions to citizens of the republic of Crimea in a poll: A. You are in favor of renewing the clause in the Constitution of the Republic of Crimea of 6 May 1992, which determined the regulation of mutual relations between the Republic of Crimea and Ukraine on the basis of a treaty and an agreement? (Yes or No). B. You are in favor of renewing the clause in the Constitution of the Republic of Crimea of 6 May 1992, which declares the right of citizens of the Republic of Crimea to dual citizenship? (Yes or No). C. You are in favor of granting the decrees by the president of the Republic of Crimea on issues that are temporarily unregulated by the legislation of the Republic of Crimea the force of law? (Yes or No). Serhiy Tsekov, head of the Republican Party of Crimea, informed a UNIAN correspondent that the Republican Party of Crimea intends to picket the Crimea's Supreme Council session on 11 March in support of the decree, which has been signed. On the same day [10 March] the president of the Republic of Crimea signed another decree abolishing the Council of Ministers of Crimea as a body of executive power. In the transitional period, which will last until a new government headed by Yevgeniy Saburov is formed, employees of the Council of Ministers will carry out their duties.
