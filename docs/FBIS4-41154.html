BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondent Dmitriy Gorokhov and Aleksandr Mineyev] [Text] Brussels, 22 Jun -- At a joint news conference with Andrey Kozyrev, Russian minister of foreign affairs, Sergio Balanzino, NATO deputy secretary general, made public a protocol on the fundamentals of interrelations between NATO and Russia. According to the document, "Russia and NATO have agreed to have wide-ranging, in-depth dialogue and cooperation in those spheres where Russia can make a unique and important contribution that corresponds to its importance and responsibility as a great European, world, and nuclear state." The forms of cooperation comprise exchange of information, holding political consultations as necessary, and interaction on the whole range of security issues, including those in the sphere of maintaining peace. The sides agreed to prepare a broad individual partnership program that would correspond to Russia's size, importance, and potential.
