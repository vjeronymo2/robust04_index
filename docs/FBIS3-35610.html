Language: Ukrainian Article Type:BFN [Text] The visit to Washington by the governmental delegation of Ukraine, led by Economic Minister Roman Shpek, has ended with the adoption of a joint statement on the outcome of the Ukrainian-U.S. economic negotiations. In particular, the statement says that the United States of America attributes primary importance to the success of the democratic and market reforms in Ukraine. Taking into account the fact that Ukraine plays a stabilizing role, its economic and political security is of very great significance for the security of the United States and Europe. In connection with this, the document points out, proceeding from the results of the negotiations, the United States submitted for consideration plans to expand aid to Ukraine in 1994. The United States of America is also ready to work with its G-7 partners and international financial organizations with the aim of immediately mobilizing additional aid to support the reform program worked out by Ukraine and the International Monetary Fund. The U.S. side, the statement further indicates, expressed concern about the current economic state of Ukraine and hailed the measures by the Ukrainian authorities geared to overcoming the economic crisis. Ukraine and the United States of America agreed on setting up a joint commission on issues of trade and investments and also completed negotiations on the agreement on avoiding double taxation and continued negotiations on bilateral protection of investments.
