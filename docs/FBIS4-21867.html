BFN [Text] Cairo, 6 Apr (MENA)--Unidentified men diverted a Sudanese airliner coming from Khartoum and forced its captain to proceed to the Luxor airport, where the airliner has landed. The Boeing 737 airliner with 93 passengers and six crew members on board took off from Khartoum en route for al-Danaqilah in south Sudan. The hijacker forced the captain to fly to Egypt. Defense Minister Field Marshal Husayn Tantawi gave permission to the airliner to land at the Luxor airport. The hijacker is a Sudanese national and is asking for political asylum in Egypt.
