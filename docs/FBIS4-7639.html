BFN [Report by Istvan Kulcsar] [Text] Hungarian Prime Minister Peter Boross and Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin, along with the Hungarian and Russian ministers concerned, signed six documents early this afternoon in the Cupola Hall of the parliament building here in Budapest. At their subsequent news conference, Peter Boross highlighted two agreements in particular: The first provides for the transportation to Russia of the spent heating elements of the Paks nuclear power station, and the second for the settling of the Russian debt. The modalities of the latter are to be services, deliveries of goods, and access for the Hungarian side to property in Russia in Russian or mixed enterprises. The latter was warmly welcomed by Prime Minister Chernomyrdin, who stated that the trade the two countries conducted with each other last year could be doubled or even tripled in the medium term. In answer to questions, the Russian prime minister said that his country welcomes Hungary's intention to join the European Union. As for Hungary's possibly joining NATO, that is something that will have to be decided by the Hungarian leadership and the Hungarian people. Peter Boross stated that the Hungarian-Russian discussions have been even more successful than expected.
