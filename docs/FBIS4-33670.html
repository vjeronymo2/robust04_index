BFN [Excerpt] Belem, Para (Brazil), 7 Jun (EFE) -- During a speech to the 24th OAS General Assembly on 7 June, Bolivian Foreign Minister Antonio Aranibar Quiroga stated that the Bolivian Government hopes that this year it will start holding talks with Chile on the problem of Bolivia's land-locked situation. Aranibar said: "We believe there are new conditions to tackle the issue of our aspirations to an outlet to the sea." He reported that the presidents of both countries will discuss the issue during the Fourth Ibero-American Summit of heads of government and state to be held in the Colombian city of Cartagena de Indias on 14-15 June. The Bolivian foreign minister asserted that "in the course of the meetings that (Bolivian) President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada held with Chilean presidential candidates before the elections, including then-candidate Eduardo Frei, he sensed that Chile was more willing to discuss that issue." Bolivia's land-locked situation was on the agenda of the 24th OAS General Assembly, which was inaugurated in Belem, a city in Para State in the Amazon, on 6 June. This issue was discussed by the foreign ministers of the two countries involved. This question has been included on the agenda of all OAS General Assemblies since 1979 at the request of Bolivia and despite the fact that Chile has always objected to it. The Bolivian claim has been endorsed in the last few years by 11 OAS resolutions. In his address to the foreign ministers and chiefs of delegations of the 34 OAS member countries, the Bolivian foreign minister asserted that his country has the right to ask the international community to discuss Bolivia's possibility of having an outlet to the sea and to give it the same treatment as the cases of Palestine, South Africa, and Haiti. Chilean Foreign Minister Carlos Figueroa Serrano stated that his country's stance is based on respect for the principle of nonintervention and faithful compliance with the treaties on that matter. He said: "We would like once again to insist at this meeting on the legal arguments that prove that our claim is valid." [passage omitted]
