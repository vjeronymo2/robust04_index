BFN [Text] Seoul, June 19 (XINHUA) -- South Korea began full preparations today for the inter-Korean summit meeting between South Korean President Kim Yong-sam and President Kim Il-song of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK). President Kim Yong-sam on Saturday [18 June] accepted the proposal made by President Kim Il-song to hold talks aimed at resolving the nuclear issue on the Korean peninsula. South Korea's National Unification Board (NUB) has formed a working-level task force headed by Vice-Minister song Yong-tae and including other high-ranking officials. The task force is making a variety of preparations for the summit, said an NUB official, who added that its members are now engaged in discussions of details, such as the date and venue of the summit. Meanwhile, the South Korean Government has decided to hold a high-level strategy meeting on Monday to coordinate its position on the inter-Korean summit and discuss measures for dealing with the DPRK's nuclear issue. South Korea and the DPRK are expected to hold working-level talks soon to discuss details relating to the summit, according to official sources here. In a related development, former U.S. President Jimmy Carter left here today for home, following his six-day visit to Pyongyang and Seoul. On Saturday, Carter conveyed DPRK President Kim Il-song's proposal for a summit to South Korean President Kim Yong-sam, who accepted immediately. During his stay on the Korean peninsula, Carter exchanged views with South Korean and DPRK leaders on the nuclear issue in a bid to break the deadlock.
