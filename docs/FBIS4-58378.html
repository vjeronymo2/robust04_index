BFN [Text] President Sardar Farooq Ahmad Khan Leghari has said that the government and the opposition have to play an important role by cooperating with each other to solve national problems and to promote democratic values. He was talking to reporters after presiding over a convocation of Jamia Khuddamuddin in Lahore this afternoon. The president stressed the need for a national consensus on important matters like Kashmir and nuclear issue. He expressed regret over the violent incidents in Karachi and asked people not to let the matter exceed the limit. He asked Muhajir Qaumi Movement leader Altaf Hussain to prevent his workers from being involved in violence and emphasized that peace is very essential to create integrity among various sections of the people. The president said that Karachi is a home of Muhajirs, Balochs, Sindhis, Pashtuns, and Punjabis. He urged all sections of the society to pursue the principles of peace, integrity, love, and brotherliness. Earlier, addressing the convocation function, the president asked people to seek guidance from the holy Koran. He regretted that the country, acquired in the name of Islam, is lagging behind in the education of science and technology.
