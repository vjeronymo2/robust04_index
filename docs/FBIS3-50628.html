Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Quito, 1 Jan (AFP) -- On Saturday evening, Ecuador's Supreme Electoral Tribunal [TSE] announced that legislative elections will be held on 1 May to choose deputies, provincial councillors, and minority councilmen [concejales de minoria]. In a resolution released via a radio and television network, the TSE stated that the call to elections abides by the constitution, electoral laws, and municipal regulations. "This call is based on the results of the fifth population census and the fourth housing census," the TSE explained, noting that 77 deputies, 54 councillors, and 607 councilmen will be elected. The TSE added that the elections will last 10 hours and will be free and popular. Those elected will hold office for two years.
