April 5, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

A review panel for The Athletics Congress, U.S. track and field's governing body, lifted the two-year suspension of Texas A&M sprinter Andre Cason.

TAC suspended Cason last week for failing to report for a drug test. Four-time Olympian steeplechaser Henry Marsh was suspended Tuesday for a similar offense.

Also, TAC suspended UCLA horizontal jumper McArthur Anderson for three months because he tested positive for a banned substance.

TAC officials said Anderson tested positive for psuedophedrine, a stimulant commonly found in over-the-counter medications, Jan. 19 in Los Angeles.
