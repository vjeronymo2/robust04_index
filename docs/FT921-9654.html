920211 FT 11 FEB 92 / International Bonds: Scandinavian Ecu offers meet reasonable demand TWO Ecu offerings for top-rated Scandinavian borrowers met reasonable retail demand in generally sluggish market conditions. Dealers said the unplaced inventory on underwriters' books, particularly in the dollar sector, shows little sign of shrinking. ing for Norway's Eksportfinans was considered fairly priced, but dealers said that demand is concentrated at the five to seven-year area of the yield curve, The deal, arranged by Nikko, was bid at 99.75, below its fixed reoffer price of 99.95. Meanwhile, the Swedish National Housing Finance Corporation (SBAB) made its debut offering in the Ecu market. The Ecu300m five-year deal via UBS Phillips & Drew was also considered reasonably priced, but had not broken syndicate at the end of trading. Despite signs of less than enthusiastic investor interest in recent deals, a number of sovereign and supranational borrowers are said to be set to raise funds in Ecu. As well as the European Community, which is expected to launch its next Ecu transaction later this week, Spain and Portugal are both said to be eyeing the market, while Denmark is expected to launch its Ecu1bn domestic bond issue in the next few weeks. In the dollar sector, Nomura International arranged a two-tranche transaction of senior floating-rate and subordinated fixed-rate bonds, backed by US adjustable rate mortgages. The transaction, issued through a special purpose vehicle, is one in a series of three offerings currently being prepared.
