940331 FT 31 MAR 94 / UK Company News: Watson & Philip pays Pounds 9.2m for convenience stores Watson & Philip, the retail and wholesale food group, is further expanding its convenience store business with the Pounds 9.2m acquisitions of Midlands Convenience Stores and Convenience Stores West. The company also announ-ced a Pounds 10.7m open offer and placing at 357p per share. Under the open offer, ordinary shareholders can apply for up to 3m new ordinary shares, on a 1-for-13 basis. The shares closed 5p lower at 379p yesterday. The Pounds 7.7m consideration for Midlands Convenience will be met by the allotment of 460,349 new ordinary shares, a Pounds 500,000 cash payment in respect of interest on shareholders' loans and 1.54m new ordinary shares, which are being offered to group shareholders as part of the open offer to raise Pounds 5.5m for the vendors. W&P is also issuing 1.46m additional new shares for cash to finance the Pounds 1.5m consideration for Convenience Stores West and the cash element for Midlands Convenience. In addition, the Pounds 5.2m cash placing will repay the external borrowings of the two acquisitions - currently Pounds 2.6m - and finance the expenses of the purchases. Midlands Convenience operates 33 stores - of which 9 are on petrol station sites - in the west Midlands. Convenience Stores West operates 13 stores in the south of England. Both companies - with turnover totalling Pounds 27m - currently operate under licence from one of W&P's subsidiaries, and will increase the number of convenience stores owned by the group to 289. W&P has an objective of opening 50 new stores this year, which will be financed from its own resources. The acquisitions are in addition to this expansion and accordingly, the group is using its shares to finance the acquisitions. The open offer has been underwritten by Flemings and brokers to the placing are SG Warburg Securities.
