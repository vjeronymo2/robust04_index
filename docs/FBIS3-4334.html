Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 28 (XINHUA) -- A new national company specializing in China's maize exports was set up here today. The China National Maize Company, the country's sole agent handling maize export business, was jointly established by the China National Cereals, Oils and Foodstuffs Import and Export Corporation, China Grains Trade Company and nine other companies in the cereals and oils trade of the main producing provinces. The company's president, Sun Zhenchi, said that the new company, set up in the light of the spirit of the country's new reform policy that major commodity items should be managed with joint efforts in a unified manner, will give full play to the strengths of each partner and play the role of a lever in adjusting the domestic supply according to the changes in the world market. He said that the setting-up of the new company would bring new vitality to the nation's foreign trade development. It will join all strands of maize export together to improve the quality and standard as well as to increase the competitiveness of China's maize in the world market. China's annual maize exports make up about 70 percent of the country's total export volume of grains.
