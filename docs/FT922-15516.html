920401 FT 01 APR 92 / Arts: Today's Television By today perhaps the election campaign will have moved from childish bickering to some of the important issues: Europe, for instance. Britain is facing its most significant constitutional change in centuries, yet scarcely a word has been said in this campaign, presumably because all three parties support the same pro-European policy. In any event, Dispatches is investigating the subject tonight (9.00 C4). Among those taking part is Lord Rees-Mogg, who says that we set out to get free-trade and co-operation in Europe but the process has been hijacked by a very powerful group which is trying to create a federal Europe. If you cannot face another word about politics, perhaps you will want to watch Goldfinger again (8.00 ITV). It is, after all, one of the best of the Bond movies, those big expensive spoofs which took the gimmicks of modern technology and, after trying them out, blew them up spectacularly. Sean Connery stars and Shirley Eaton is the one painted gold all over. Timewatch shows the first of two programmes about Elisabeth Nietzsche, sister of Friedrich. She founded an Aryan colony in Paraguay (8.10 BBC2).
