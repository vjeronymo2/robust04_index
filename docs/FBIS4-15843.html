BFN [Text] Riga, Apr 11, BNS -- Latvian President Guntis Ulmanis has announced a shake-up in his office appointing manufacturer Ivars Millers to head it instead of Janis Eihmanis who will continue working there but won't be office head any longer. The office employees did not give reasons for the office shake-up. Ulmanis would inform about the motives of his decision after he returns from his trip to Europe, said Sandra Dreimane, the deputy head of the office. Ivars Millers is a former deputy director for economic issues in the state-owned Riga Motor Plant. The plant's director Ceslav Raubisko told BNS that Millers worked in the plant for 19 years. He has a university degree, is married, has one child. Was a member of the Communist Party. Raubisko characterized Millers as efficient and talented employee.
