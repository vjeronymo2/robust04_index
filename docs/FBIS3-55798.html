Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The presidents of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan, Nursultan Nazarbayev and Islam Karimov, have spoken out against the idea of dual citizenship, which was introduced recently by Turkmenistan, they told reporters in Tashkent Monday. Karimov, for example, has said that Russians living in Uzbekistan should believe that the republic's constitution provides them equal conditions and rights alongside the local population and other nations living in the republics. For his part, Nazarbayev said that Kazakhstan's Constitution does not offer dual citizenship and he, as the president, must obey it.
