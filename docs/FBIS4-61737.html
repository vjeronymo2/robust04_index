BFN [Text] Over 15,000 attended the opposition march from the Belarusian Railway Terminal down Tverskaya Street in Moscow on May 9. The column was headed by war veterans. Sazhi Umalatova, chairman of the so-called Permanent Presidium of the USSR Congress of People's Deputies, Oleg Shenin, chairman of the Council of Communist Parties-Communist Parties of the Soviet Union, and Ilya Konstantinov and Viktor Aksyuchits, National Salvation Front leaders, were with them in the first row. Former vice-president Aleksandr Rutskoy. [sentence as received] He congratulated every veteran in the first row and headed the column with his bodyguards. The crowd shouted "Rutskoy for presidency." Flags of the USSR, St. Andrew-crossed flags, flags of USSR Armed Force Services [as received] and portraits of Stalin were displayed in front of the column. One of the posters said: "Presidents carried out Hitler's plan to destroy the USSR." There was not a single Russian flag but there were black-yellow-and-white flags of the Russian empire in the column. A separate column of the Communist Party of the Russian Federation was headed by its leader Gennadiy Zyuganov accompanied by Anatoliy Lukyanov, member of the State Duma and former chairman of the USSR Supreme Soviet, and Yuriy Voronin, former first deputy chairman of the Supreme Soviet of the Russian Federation. Working Russia and the Russian Communist Workers' Party was led by their leader Viktor Anpilov. They shouted "Victory or death" and "Victory banner to the Kremlin". Aleksandr Sterligov, leader of the Russian National Sobor (assembly) and Stanislav Terekhov, leader of the Officers Union took part in the march. The monarchist League of the Russian People made up a separate column. The police did not interfere.
