BFN [Text] Bonn (DPA) -- As compared to the year before, Germany's imports from Latin America decreased by 15.1 percent to 12.4 billion German marks [DM] in 1993. All big Latin American countries were affected by it, which is shown by statistics published by the FRG Economics Ministry in Bonn on Thursday [19 May]. Smaller countries like Bolivia and Paraguay registered gains. However, German exports to Latin America increased by 7.2 percent to DM14.8 billion. For the first time after 1991, this resulted in a trade surplus for Germany again.
