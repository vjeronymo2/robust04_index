May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

TENNIS ROUNDUP;

UNSEEDED ITALIAN STUNS MECIR; WILANDER STRUGGLES BUT WINS

Italy's Omar Camporese, a wild-card entry ranked 120th in the world, eliminated fourth-seeded Miloslav Mecir, 6-2, 7-5, Tuesday night in the first round of the Italian Open at Rome.

But another major upset was averted earlier in the day when top-seeded Mats Wilander came to life after a listless first set and outlasted Javier Frana of Argentina, 6-7 (8-6), 6-3, 6-2.

Mecir, who returned to action two weeks ago after injuring his back in March, had three set points at 5-4 in the second set before Camporese fired three serves that the Czech couldn't handle.

Camporese then got a service break to go ahead, 6-5, when Mecir double-faulted once and committed three unforced backhand errors.

The 21-year-old Italian, a doubles specialist who had beaten Mecir once before in Milan, served out the match in the next game. On the second match point, Mecir slapped a backhand into the net after an rally of more than 30 shots.

Meanwhile, second-seeded Andre Agassi survived a brief second-set lapse to beat fellow American Todd Witsken, 6-1, 6-4; and fourth-seeded Jimmy Connors, playing in Rome for the first time in 14 years, defeated French qualifier Cedric Pioline, 6-4, 6-2.

Other seeds advancing were No. 6 Emilio Sanchez of Spain, a 6-1, 6-2 winner over Eduardo Bengoechea of Argentina; No. 11 Alberto Mancini of Argentina, who defeated Francisco Clavet of Spain 6-3, 6-2, and No. 13 Jaime Yzaga of Peru, who beat Marian Vajda of Czechoslovakia, 7-5, 6-2.

Two other seeded players were eliminated. No. 9 Ronald Agenor of Haiti, who reached the semifinals here last year, was upset by Bruno Oresar of Yugoslavia, 7-6 (7-3), 3-6, 6-2. No. 12 Andres Gomez of Ecuador, winner of the Italian Open in 1982 and 1984, was ousted by Spain's Sergio Bruguera, 2-6, 6-2, 6-2.

Top-seeded Steffi Graf, ranked No. 1 in the world, overcame early deficits in both sets and went on to defeat Tine Scheuer-Larsen of Denmark, 7-5, 6-4, in the first round of a $300,000 women's tournament at Berlin.

The West German took 2 hours 8 minutes to dispose of her opponent, ranked 94th internationally, after falling behind 1-2 at the start of both sets.

Nathalie Tauziat of France, ranked 12th, also won but with difficulty, 4-6, 7-5, 7-6 (7-3), against Sabrina Goles of Yugoslavia.

Noelle Porter of Pepperdine, an unseeded freshman, and Stanford's Lisa Green advanced to the semifinals of the National Collegiate Athletic Assn. women's individual tournament at Gainesville, Fla.

Porter held off Danielle Jones of Oklahoma State, 6-4, 3-6, 6-4, and then defeated Shannan McCarthy of Georgia, 7-6 (7-4), 6-1.

Green pulled off two upsets, defeating No. 5 Stacey Martin of Tennessee, 6-4, 6-0, and then No. 2 Jessica Emmons of UCLA, 7-6 (8-6), 3-6, 6-2.

"When I was down 5-1 (in the first set against Emmons), I was just trying to win one game to maybe make her think about it some more," Green said. "After that, I just started playing better. I stoped rushing so much. It was a good match to win."

Green will next play her Stanford teammate, Sandra Birch, who is seeded third. Porter will meet No. 4 Jennifer Santrock of Southern Methodist.

Birch advanced by defeating Nicole Arendt of Florida 6-2, 7-6 (9-7), and then Ginger Helgeson of Pepperdine, 3-6, 6-2, 7-6 (7-4). Birch later teamed with Debbie Graham to upset the top-seeded doubles team, Mamie Ceniza and Stella Sampras of UCLA, 6-4, 6-1, in a second-round match.
