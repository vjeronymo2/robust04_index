CSO Editor's Note: The FBIS Terrorism Desk welcomes subscribers' comments and suggestions at (703) 733-5512. Highlights and major incidents in this edition of the FBIS Terrorism Report include:

Argentina
Buenos Aires police raided a right-wing military training camp on an island in Tigre District, seizing explosives and medium weapons. A police chief said that the camp probably belonged to the "Painted Faces" group, who were training to free former coup-plotter Seineldin from prison.
Colombia/U.S.
Just before the New York trial of Dandenys Munoz Mosquera, a.k.a. La Quica, a Colombian informant "with a great deal of credibility" has said that La Quica was a leading figure in the 1989 Avianca airlines bombing, taking orders from Carlos Mario Alzate Urquijo, a.k.a. El Arete.
Iran/International
London's THE SUNDAY TIMES publishes a report on a November 1993 "terrorist summit" in Tehran, which it says was held to plan operations in European capitals.
Lebanon/Germany
The Hammadi family told AL-HAYAH newspaper that press reports that 'Abbas Hammadi is in Germany trying to break his brother out of prison are "completely wrong."
Libya/Germany
Germany is investigating the mid-April death of a counterterrorism officer from the Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution while on vacation in Libya.
Peru
According to Madrid's EFE news service, the alias "Ricardo Z" has appeared as the new leader of the Shining Path. The Counterterrorism Department speculates that Ricardo Z could be a new figure, or merely a new alias for Oscar Alberto Ramirez, a.k.a "Feliciano."
Ukraine/Russia
Security officers in Mariupol arrested several "foreign citizens" who had built an explosive device, intending to use it against an unspecified embassy in Moscow.
