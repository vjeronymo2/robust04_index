BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA June 12 -- Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet and his party stopped over in Saint Petersburg, capital of the province of Leningrad and a brotherhood city of Ho Chi Minh City on June 10 and 11, on his way to visit Kazakhstan. He was given a banquet in the evening on June 10 by Leningrad Governor A.C. Belyakov, Russian Deputy Prime Minister Y. Yarov, Russian Ambassador to Vietnam R.L. Khamidoulin and Vietnamese Ambassador to Russian Ho Huan Nghiem were present at the banquet. Prime Minister Kiet and his party were received on June 11 by the Leningrad governor who said his province pays much attention to developing its cooperation with Vietnam, economically in particular, stressing that the experiences Vietnam got in its economic reform is very useful to Leningrad. He expressed his wish that the fine traditional friendship and cooperation between the two countries, and those between Saint Petersburg and Ho Chi Minh City will be further strengthened and promoted. Prime Minister Kiet also expressed his wish for further development and consolidation of the fraternal friendship and cooperation between Vietnam and Russia. Saint Petersburg Mayor A. Sobchak received Prime Minister Kiet and his party on the same day. Mr. Kiet and his party also visited a number of economic and cultural establishments in the city.
