BFN [Text] Ankara, April 22 (A.A.) -- Parliament Speaker Husamettin Cindoruk described today the crisis in Turkey, being a political one, rather than economic. `To eliminate the great diversity in the country's politics, people should be united around principles, not persons,' Cindoruk said. `If they call me to duty, because of my 40-year experience in politics, then I may resign from my position as parliament speaker, to unite them.' In an interview to be aired Saturday [23 April] on TRT-2, Cindoruk said that today's problems in Turkey were similar to a solar eclipse. The Parliament's public relations office said today that during the interview, Cindoruk said Turkey's future will `certainly be bright.' `I share the opinion of citizens that there is an excess of political parties and this prevents democracy from being genuine,' he said. `Mergers among right and left wing parties would prevent a political extravagance.' There are 25 political parties registered in Turkey. Of these, 11 have seats in parliament.
