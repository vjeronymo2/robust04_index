BFN [Text] Lagos, 28 Jun (AFP) -- Seven women demonstrators were wounded by gunfire and hundreds of people arrested during a rally in support of opposition leader Moshood Abiola, the committee for the defence of human rights said on Tuesday [28 June]. The women, many of them small-time traders from the Ikeja district of northern Lagos where Abiola lives, were holding a demonstration Monday when police intervened, firing in their direction and wounding seven, the committee said in a statement signed by its national secretary Segun Jegede. There was no independent confirmation of the incident. Abiola, a millionaire tycoon, was arrested on Thursday after declaring himself president, a year after the military annulled presidential elections he was widely held to have won.
