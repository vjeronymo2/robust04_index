Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [From NIHON KEIZAI SHIMBUN 26 Mar Morning Edition] [Text] South Korean President Kim Yong-sam met with former Japanese prime ministers and leaders of both ruling coalition parties, and the opposition Liberal Democratic Party at the State Guesthouse on the afternoon of 25 March. Referring to North Korea's suspected nuclear development during a meeting with former Prime Minister Yasuhiro Nakasone, the South Korean president said: "I think that North Korea does not have nuclear weapons." This is the first time the president has acknowledged the nonexistence of nuclear weapons in North Korea since he arrived in Japan. The president went on to say: "North Korea is eager to develop nuclear weapons, however, I intend to resolve the issue while closely cooperating with Japan."
