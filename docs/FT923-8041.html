920813 FT 13 AUG 92 / Ballots posted to BCCI creditors TOUCHE ROSS, the liquidator of Bank of Credit and Commerce International, started balloting creditors yesterday to sound out opinions on the compensation deal negotiated with the Abu Dhabi government, the majority shareholder in the bank. The ballot is being carried out at the request of the district court of Luxembourg, which must approve the agreement before it can go ahead. The liquidators are trying to contact about 50,000 depositors of BCCI SA, the Luxembourg-registered bank that formed one of the two main banking arms of BCCI. Most of BCCI's other banking operations were carried out in BCCI Overseas, based in Grand Cayman. The ballot paper, sent out yesterday, asks depositors whether they are in favour both of the contribution agreement, under which Abu Dhabi is expected to commit about Dollars 1.7bn, and the pooling agreement, which provides for a pooling of assets of BCCI Holdings and the two main banks, including branches in the UK and the Isle of Man. The ballot paper is accompanied by a letter from the liquidators. Creditors are 'strongly recommended' to accept the compensation deal. 'The rejection of this agreement, so as to create the possibility of renegotiation, would be a pure gamble,' says the letter. The results of the ballot are not binding on the court, which hopes to make a decision on the liquidation on October 1. The judge, however, wanted to gauge the strength of objections to the proposed scheme among creditors. Mr Ahmed Al Sayegh, a member of the majority shareholders' working group which has been carrying out the negotiations with the liquidators, said last night that the only alternative to the arrangement was 'hugely expensive' court action around the world. 'The liquidators believe that the arrangements are the best option . . . ,' he said. 'Without the arrangements the only people to gain will be lawyers.'
