Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Moscow, Jan 6 (AFP) -- Russian Prime Minister Viktor Chernomyrdin will seek a vote of confidence from the Duma, or lower house of parliament, when it meets next Tuesday [11 January], Deputy Prime Minister Sergey Shakhray said Thursday. He told reporters that the move "would help eliminate any misunderstanding between the government and the deputies." Shakhray added that the 24 deputies belonging to his party, the Russia Unity and Accord Party that ran against the government-backed Russia's Choice party during the recent legislative elections, would give Chernomyrdin their vote of confidence. He said that he expected a government reshuffle planned by President Boris Yeltsin to be a source of tension between the Kremlin and parliament, which has little say in the matter. Shakhray, who may be targeted by the reshuffle according to local press reports, said he would not react "hysterically" if that were the case. He said he plans to seek the presidency of the Duma despite the clear opposition of many of the political parties.
