Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 18 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese Government presented the country's highest annual national scientific and technological awards to 4,484 scientists and technical experts here today. President Jiang Zemin and Premier Li Peng met representatives of the award-winning scientists before the ceremony. The award winners, including nine scientists from Hong Kong and foreign countries, had between them completed 781 scientific and technological projects for the country in 1993. Congratulating the scientists, Jiang said that he hoped the scientists will spread the seed of science and technology throughout the country and make yet greater contributions to the country's economic development and social progress. He said Deng Xiaoping's strategic thinking of making science and technology the driving force behind production is the guiding principle for developing the country's science and technology and the national economy as a whole," the president said. Jiang asked the scientists to pay close attention to applied research and development research while keeping a necessary contingent in basic sciences. He said, "the practice of reform and development needs a correct theory and rich knowledge." He also urged officials at all levels to gain more knowledge of modern science and technology, and make new and realistic contribution to the country's modernization. The national science and technology awards include natural sciences awards, invention awards, science and technology progress awards and spark awards. According to professor Zhu Lilan, vice-minister in charge of the State Science and Technology Commission, the prize-winning projects of 1993 have produced an estimated economic result of 21.3 billion yuan (2.45 billion U.S. dollars). Projects associated with key industries in the nation's economic development plan outnumbered the others in the winners' list. The winning projects are concerned with industries and transport, agriculture, education, medicine and basic scientific research. One project was on research to make pandas give birth to twins, conducted by the animal institute of the Chinese Academy of Sciences and other research institutes in Chengdu, Sichuan Province. Another winner was research into Jurassic dinosaurs in Sichuan mountain regions, conducted by the Chinese Academy of Sciences and Chengdu Geology College.
