Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, March 21 (KCNA) -- Sowing of seed rice on cold-frame beds began on the cooperative fields of Korea as spring came. The cooperative farmers throughout the country have made full preparations for rice-seedling cold beds with a goal of harvesting another bumper crops this year marking the 30th anniversary of the publication of the socialist rural theses (February 1964). Doing farming on a scientific and technological basis on the principle of the right crop in the right time and the right crop on the right soil, they set down to the sowing of seed rice on cold-frame beds in good time. In Yonbaek, Jaeryong, Yoltusamcholli and other plain areas, the granaries on the west coast of the country, the first day sowing quotas were overfulfilled with the help of various kinds of highly efficient machines including seed rice sowing and manuring machines. The cooperative farms there have more than doubled their work speed daily by scrupulously organizing workforce according to different processes of the sowing. The rural communities in North Phyongan and North Hwanghae Provinces chose fast-growing and high-yielding strains and are strictly meeting the technical requirements in sowing. The sowing of seed rice on cold beds takes more than ten days. It is a very important farming process in Korea. Meanwhile, the preparation of humus-potted maize seedlings is in full swing in rural communities throughout the country. The work had been carried out at 57 percent nationwide as of March 18.
