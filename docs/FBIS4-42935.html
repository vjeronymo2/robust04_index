BFN [Text] Athens, June 9, IRNA -- Public order and security were among the issues which Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias discussed with his Iranian counterpart 'Ali Akbar Velayati in Tehran, Greek Government spokesman Evangelos Venizelos told a regular press briefing Thursday [9 June]. In reply to a Greek journalist's question if Papoulias raised the issue of "terrorism" during his talks, Venizelos replied that he discussed a number of topics with Velayati and other Iranian officials including the issues of public order and security. Papoulias Wednesday flew to Istanbul after a two-day visit to the Islamic Republic of Iran. Last month the british newspaper INDEPENDENT alleged that members of the greek guerrilla organisation `17 November' had met Iranian officials. The news was also reported in the Greek press. Tehran rejected the report as "baseless and false."
