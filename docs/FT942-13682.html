940423 FT 23 APR 94 / Economic Diary: Forward Events TODAY: Greece's opposition New Democracy party congress in Halkidiki. Taipei's nationalist central committee debates electoral reform. Yugoslavian president Zoran Lilic in Romania signs political friendship treaty with President Ion Iliescu. TOMORROW: Israeli prime minister Yitzhak Rabin visits Moscow. China's premier Li Peng on tour of Turkmenistan, Uzbekhistan, Kyrgyzstan and Mongolia. MONDAY: European Union finance ministers meet in Luxembourg. European Union farm ministers in Luxembourg discuss German request to ban imports of cattle, beef and veal from Britain on grounds of possible BSE link. Officials from Jordan, Lebanon and Syria at conference on Middle East travel and tourism in Beirut. UK balance of trade figures with countries outside EC. UK GDP first quarter (preliminary estimate). Anniversary celebrations in Rome of Italy's wartime liberation. TUESDAY: CBI industrial trends survey (April). UN biological weapons inspectors return from Iraq. Sixth international conference on infectious diseases in Prague. Thai Prime Minister Leekpai Chuan visits Japan. WEDNESDAY: UK mortgage possession actions (first quarter). Bosnia's Moslem president Alija Izetbegovic visits Romania. Europol meets in Athens to focus on action against terrorism and drug dealing. Home Affairs Committee inquiry into organised crime takes evidence from Home Office permanent secretary Sir Clive Whitmore. THURSDAY: Final day of South Africa's first all-race elections (results 48 hours after polls close). Opec secretary general Subroto speaks at conference on World Energy in Transition in Dallas. Britain's Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd opens conference in London on financial markets in Middle East. Energy trends (February). New vehicle registrations (March). FRIDAY: UK economic trends (April). Monthly digest of statistics (April). SPD state conference takes place in Mecklenburg, Germany.
