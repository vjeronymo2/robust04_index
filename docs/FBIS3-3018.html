Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 16 (XINHUA) -- China's top judge and top procurator reported the handling of several big corruption cases to the ongoing session of the National People's Congress. Ren Jianxin, president of the Supreme People's Court, reported that Li Xiaoshi, a former vice minister of the State Science and Technology Commission, abused his power to support a nationwide fraud scheme and accepted bribes of 52,000 yuan and embezzled 20,000 yuan, and that Li was sentenced to 20 years in prison on March 4. Shen Taifu, general manager of the former Beijing Changcheng (Great Wall) Machinery and Electronics Corporation and the main culprit of the fraud case, was sentenced to death on the same day. Shen and his wife embezzled over three million yuan. Both men have appealed to a higher court, he said. On a cigarette smuggling case in Rushan city, Shandong Province, Ren said, Liu Qishan, a former head of the city Administration for Industry and Commerce, Fan Zhanwu, a former political commissar of the frontier guard sub-bureau of Weihai city, and Liu Ning, a section chief of the sub-bureau, were sentenced to death. Wang Jianzhi, former secretary of the Rushan municipal party committee, was sentenced to death with two years' reprieve. The accused have appealed to a higher court. Zhang Siqing, procurator-general of the Supreme People's Procuratorate, reported several serious cases now under investigation. They include: -- Acceptance of more than three million yuan in briberies by five bureau-level officials and 10 section-level officials in Minjiang Construction Bureau in Fujian Province; -- Offences of embezzlement, corruption and tax evasion by 43 officials of the Changtu County Grain Bureau in Liaoning Province; 25 of them have been arrested and 32 million yuan have been withheld or confiscated; -- Acceptance of 100,000 yuan in briberies by Guo Zhengmin, director of the Guizhou provincial Bureau of Public Security; -- Acceptance of 20,000 yuan in briberies by Cheng Zhiwen, chief procurator of the Changping County Procuratorate; -- Acceptance of 10,000 yuan by Li Kaipu, a chief judge in Hubei Province.
