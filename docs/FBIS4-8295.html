BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 9/4/1994 -- According to a public opinion poll conducted over March 24-April 2, the Democratic Convention of Romania [CDR] holds the largest share in the political options expressed by the Romanian citizens with 24 percent, followed by the ruling party, Social Democracy Party of Romania [PDSR], with 21 percent. The major problems are believed to be inflation (78 percent,) corruption (67 percent,) unemployment (66 percent,) and salary size (64 percent.) The poll conducted by the Center for Urban and Regional Sociology together with Data Media ranked the other political parties in the public preferences as follows: Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania [UDMR] 5 percent, the National Romanian Unity Party [PUNR] and the Socialist Party of Labor [PSM] 4 percent each, the Democratic Party [PD] and the Greater Romania Party [PRM] 3 percent each, and the Democratic Agrarian Party of Romania [PDAR] 2 percent. The majority of the population considers that the following things worsened: The living standard, the observance of public order and law, and the people's trust in the future. The social problems that should immediately be solved are: Curbing inflation, creation of jobs, issuance and handing over of property title deeds on land, and speeded-up reform and social protection. A proportion of 35 percent of the subjects said early elections were necessary, while 42 percent pronounced themselves against such elections. The findings of the poll were published by dailies EVENIMENTUL ZILEI and ROMANIA LIBERA.
