BFN [Text] Beijing, April 13 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen met with his Portuguese counterpart Jose Durao Barroso here Tuesday [12 April]. They exchanged views on issues of common concern. Qian said the premiers of the two countries had held good talks and the Sino-Portuguese cooperation on successfully solving the Macao issue has set a fine example for settling problems left by history. He noted that there still exists potential in promoting economic and trade ties between China and the European Union, which is an important trade partner of China. The Chinese foreign minister stressed that no matter what happens in the world, China will play a role of peace and stability. Durao Barroso said that his country favors the development of relations between the European Union and China and support resumption of China's status in GATT.
