November 26, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

THE BLACKLIST

"Children of the Blacklist," by Jack Mathews (Oct. 15), gave me a jolt. I was one of the blacklisted, although not important enough in either "the party" or "the industry" to be called before HUAC. Needless to say, I avidly absorbed the photos and the text -- and then I came to Hugo Butler. I worked with Hugo for many, many months and became friends with him and with his wife, Jean. I can tell you that he was one of the most original, delightful, engaging personalities I ever knew. It is simply not possible that he could have been a monster in private.

Jack Mathews was irresponsible in using only the interview with Mary Hugo. He had to see that her input was not pertinent. He mentioned that Hugo had five daughters. Why didn't he interview any of the others?

ANNE F. TAYLOR

Venice
