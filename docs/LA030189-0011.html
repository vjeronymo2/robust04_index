March 1, 1989, Wednesday, Valley Edition

COLLEGE ROUNDUP

In other baseball games:

Valley 10, El Camino 9 -- Mike Caputi singled in Harold Ciccione with the winning run with two out in the ninth inning of the nonconference game at Valley, capping a dramatic Monarch comeback.

Trailing, 9-1, Valley (12-1-1) scored six runs in the eighth and three in the ninth.

Cal Lutheran 5, Chapman 4 -- Senior right-hander Jay Anderson allowed two runs in 7 1/3 innings and freshman Chris Matkin pitched out of jams in the eighth and ninth innings as Cal Lutheran won its sixth in a row in a nonconference game at Chapman.

Azusa Pacific 5, The Master's 1 -- First baseman Jeff Preston and pitcher Peter Wilson were both two for four for Master's in an NAIA District 3 loss at Azusa Pacific.

Pasadena 7, Pierce 5 -- Pierce erased a four-run deficit to tie the score, 5-5, but Pasadena hit solo home runs in the seventh and eighth innings in a nonconference game at Pierce. Damon Lantz hit a solo home run for Pierce (4-5).

SOFTBALL

Cal State Northridge 5-2, Chapman 0-1 -- Debbie Dickmann won both games as CSUN (10-6 overall, 2-0 in conference play) swept a California Collegiate Athletic Assn. doubleheader at Chapman.

Moorpark 1, Rancho Santiago 0 (9) -- De Dow pitched her first no-hitter and fifth shutout of the season in the nonconference game at Moorpark.

CS Bakersfield 4-10, Cal Lutheran 2-2 -- The defending NCAA Division II champion Roadrunners took advantage of nine Cal Lutheran errors in a nonconference doubleheader at Cal Lutheran.

MEN'S VOLLEYBALL

UCLA d. Cal State Northridge, 19-17, 15-7, 16-14 -- Northridge trailed, 7-1, in the opening game, rallied, and then blew three game-point opportunities before falling in a Western Intercollegiate Volleyball Assn. match at UCLA.
