920103 FT 03 JAN 92 / Saudi spending up in new budget KING Fahd of Saudi Arabia yesterday announced a 1992 budget, with expenditure 27 per cent higher than the last budget two years ago, but less than the swollen spending of last year, the Gulf crisis AP reports from Riyadh. The monarch said his kingdom this year was giving priority to military and economic development, as well as education. Budget figures later released by Mohammed Abalkhail, finance minister, showed expenditures in 1992 totalling 181bn riyals (Pounds 25.77bn). Projected revenue is SR151bn officials said. The king said the budget would be balanced by borrowing SR30bn riyals from domestic and foreign sources. The minister said spending in 1990 and 1991 combined had swelled to SR472.36bn. The military and security took the lion's share - SR54.2bn.
