Language: English Article Type:BFN [by ITAR-TASS correspondent Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Moscow January 26 TASS -- Russian President Boris Yeltsin appointed Yevgeniy Shaposhnikov, former CIS Armed Forces commander, his representative in the state company for arms exports-imports, Rosvooruzheniye. The company was set up on November 25 1993 to handle arms exports-imports, including weapons, military hardware, equipment, double-purpose technologies and strategic raw materials. Additionally, it uses its own funds and borrowed means to invest in the defence industry. The company has been established to protect Russia's state interests while determining contract prices for arms exports and control hard currency revenues from such deals. Rosvooruzheniye plans to set up maintenance bases and training centers for specialists to transfer relations with partners purchasing Russian arms and military hardware on a long-term basis. The company has been allowed to set up branches and offices inside and outside of Russia, including with the participation of foreign capital. At present it has branches in 38 countries and cooperates with 60 countries.
