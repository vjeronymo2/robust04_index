Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] After the night's lull, Azeri troops launched an offensive at 10 a.m. on January 18, in the north of the Martuni region of Nagorno-Karabakh, INTERFAX was told by a spokesman for the Karabakh Defense Ministry. Reports say fierce fighting continues at that section of the front. On Tuesday morning there was a relative lull at other sections of the front. In the morning of January 18 an Azeri war plane attempted to drop bombs on Stepanakert, the capital of Nagorno-Karabakh. The Karabakh air defenses forced the plane away from the city. Meanwhile, a source in the Azeri Defense Ministry spoke about attempts by the Armenian forces to attack the strategic railway station at Goradiz, but were recently beaten off by the Azeri army. The station is situated near the Azeri-Iranian border. Two attacks to re-capture the station were thwarted. A Karabakh tank was put out of action. The source also spoke about fighting on Monday evening to the east of the Azeri regional center of Agdam, which is under Armenian control, as well as at the approaches to the village of Novruzlu, where, the source says, a Karabakh armored personnel vehicle was put out of action. In the early hours of Tuesday, the fighting at this section of the front, as well as in the north part of the conflict zone, was restricted by sporadic artillery shelling. The source says four local inhabitants were wounded as a result of the shelling of Azeri settlements bordering on Armenia.
