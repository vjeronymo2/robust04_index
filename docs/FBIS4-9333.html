BFN [Text] Belgrade, April 22 (TANJUG) -- The Bosnian Serb Army investigative officials have secured the arrested eleven members of the French non-governmental humanitarian organizations Premiere Urgence a fair trial, Bosnian Serb Army Chief Command said Friday [22 April]. Premiere Urgence members, suspected of involvement in the smuggling arms for the Bosnian Moslems, have been given an interpreter and advised in French of their rights. They have also been allowed to hire a lawyer and to be visited by French authorities officials. The eleven Frenchmen, arrested at a Serb check-point in Sarajevo on April 8 charged with transporting arms and ammunition to Moslems. The Bosnian Serb news agency SRNA said they were to stay in prison in the Bosnian Serb republic territory until the investigation was completed. The Bosnian Serb Army Chief Command, recalling that illegal arms and ammunition transport was violating the U.N. Security Council ban on arms deliveries to the former Yugoslavia, on Thursday disclaimed that the French nationals were held hostage.
