Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Warsaw, Jan. 13 -- Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak termed the "Partnership for Peace" initiative as "a construction which will alllow for a future integration of central European countries with NATO" although it offers no membership guarantees. Speaking after his return from Prague late on Wednesday, Pawlak said that the supplementing of the "Partnership for Peace" by a "partnership for development" would give the initiative a "broader dimension." "We can not change geography," said Pawlak, stressing the significance of economic cooperation between the Visegrad Group states.
