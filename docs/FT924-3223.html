921209 FT 09 DEC 92 / Half of workers 'fear job losses' ALMOST HALF the population believes there will be job losses at their workplace in the new year and one in seven fear their own jobs are at risk if the recession continues, Emma Tucker and Daniel Green write. These results of a survey published yesterday by the British Market Research Bureau underline fears that people's anxiety about unemployment will hold back economic recovery. The survey, in which BMRB polled 1,028 people between last Friday and Sunday, was carried out on behalf of the Trades Union Congress and published to run ahead of today's TUC National Recovery Day. Two out of three people polled believed prospects for economic recovery had worsened over the past year and 77 per cent lacked confidence in the government to lead the country out of recession. The findings come when there are some indications that the economy could be showing signs of a slow recovery. Yesterday Infolink, the UK's biggest credit information organisation, said consumer credit inquiries rose 5 per cent in the first week of December compared with the same period a year ago. Mr Brian Bailey, Infolink's chairman, said the figures represented 'a very significant rise', although he emphasised that they reflected enquiries rather than sales.
