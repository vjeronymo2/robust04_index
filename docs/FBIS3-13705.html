Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] President Yasir 'Arafat has renewed the PLO's appeal to the co-sponsors of the peace conference and the members of the Security Council to respond to the Palestinian people's request for international protection in the occupied territories. 'Arafat renewed this appeal when he received Jean-Louis Tauran, official in charge of foreign affairs at the Vatican, in Tunis yesterday. The Palestinian president also insisted on the need for the Security Council to adopt a resolution condemning the massacre at the Tomb of the Patriarchs and providing international protection for the Palestinian people. In reply to a question on the Palestinian response to President Clinton's call for a resumption of talks, 'Arafat said that he wants a frank reply before making any answer on the Palestinian position on this matter. The Palestinian news agency reported yesterday evening that President 'Arafat and the Vatican envoy discussed ways of strengthening relations between the Palestinians and the Vatican and providing international protection for the Palestinian people from the attacks and massacres perpetrated by Israeli settlers. They also discussed the role of the Vatican in supporting the peace process on the basis of safeguarding Palestinian rights, particularly the issue of holy Jerusalem, which is important to Palestinians, Arabs, Muslims, and Christians alike.
