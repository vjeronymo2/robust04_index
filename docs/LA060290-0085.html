June 2, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

CHEVRON PLANS UNLEADED LOW-EMISSIONS GASOLINE

Chevron Corp. is test-marketing a lower-emissions gasoline in Florida and plans to introduce a version of it on the West Coast in the near future, Chevron dealers said Friday.

The new gasoline, to be called Chevron Unleaded Plus, is similar in composition to Atlantic Richfield Co.'s EC-1 gasoline, the first of several lower-emissions gasolines to hit the market in the last several months, dealers said.

Meanwhile, Arco confirmed that it may come out with another reformulated gasoline to accompany EC-1 as soon as this fall. EC-1, which was introduced in September, is sold to cars that previously used only leaded gasoline.

Arco's new lower-emissions gasoline, code-named EC-X, would be marketed to cars that use unleaded gas, possibly as a premium grade such as Shell's new SU 2000E.

Few other details concerning Chevron's new gasoline were available, and a Chevron spokeswoman in San Francisco declined to comment.

If Chevron proceeds with plans to market such a gasoline, it will join seven other major oil companies marketing some form of so-called reformulated gasoline in various parts of the country. Exxon Corp. began selling a reformulated gasoline in 20 Eastern and Gulf Coast states on Friday.

At least two more refiners are planning similar introductions.

In an interview, George H. Babikian, president of Arco's refining and marketing subsidiary, said the company had not made up its mind yet on how to proceed with its new gasoline.
