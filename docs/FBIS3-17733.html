Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Pacific Ocean" program] [Text] The Sakhalin Oblast department of the Russian Federal Counterintelligence Service summed up the results of its operations for last year. According to the department's data, workers' efforts resulted in the halting of the illegal export by ship of 24 tonnes of nonferrous metals, 110 tonnes of Alaskan pollock roe, and a large quantity of marine products valued at 722 million rubles [R]. The investigation of criminal matters by the department's investigators last year resulted in the confiscation of R11,915,000, $900,000 in U.S. currency, items and valuables worth R3.2 billion, 338 grams of marijuana, more than 15 kg of mercury, and a large quantity of ammunition. Counterintelligence operatives uncovered and prevented a number of attempts by using fake documents to defraud the oblast banks of an amount that altogether exceeded R4 billion. Federal Counterintelligence Service staffers estimate that as a result of their operations, they have prevented an economic loss to Russia totalling R5.8 billion. According to information from the service's staffers, they are currently tracking the development of contacts between Japanese yakuza [Japanese underworld] and Russian mafia structures aimed at smuggling fish and marine products and establishing channels for delivering firearms and drugs to Japan using fishing vessels and the merchant fleet.
