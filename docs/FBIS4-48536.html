BFN [Excerpt] The president, Flight Lieutenant Rawlings, has held discussions with a nine-member delegation from the World Bank at the Castle, Osu. Our Castle correspondent, Teye Kitcher, has the story: [Kitcher] The nine members of the delegation are all executive directors of the World Bank and are part of the 24-member board of the bank, its highest management body. The board of the World Bank is responsible for the approval of loans, credits, and other forms of assistance. The delegation, which is led by Mr. Nicholas Flanol, is on a familiarization tour of the country to see how the bank's assistance has been utilized so far, and to discuss future prospects with the government. It is expected that, after discussions with government officials on the economy, the delegation will appreciate Ghana's peculiar problems and be in a position to react favorably when requests for assistance come before the board of the World Bank. The delegation, which has been in Ghana for the past four days, has met the ministers of finance, education, food and agriculture, trade and industry, and mines and energy. [passage omitted]
