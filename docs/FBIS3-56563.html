Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [From the "Novosti" newscast] [Text] Yegor Gaydar said that one of the reasons for his resignation was the inter-bank agreement on unification of the monetary systems of Russia and Belarus. Concerning this, Stanislav Shushkevich, head of the Belarusian Parliament, expressed to INTERFAX the supposition that Gaydar had not been presented with an analysis of Belarus's expenditure on elimination of the aftermath of the Chernobyl accident, on which the republic annually spends 15 percent of its annual budget. Minsk believes that Russia should take into consideration the factor of the Chernobyl tragedy in the talks on creating a monetary union with Belarus.
