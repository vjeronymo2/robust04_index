BFN [Text] Istanbul, June 28 (A.A) -- President Suleyman Demirel on Tuesday [28 June] said that if the West sees democracy in Turkey as allowing the country to split apart, Turkey will not be a part of it, adding terror in Turkey aims at dividing and attacking its unity. Speaking at a conference at the military academy, he said that Turkey has a free press, elections and judgment. He said there are people from different origins in Turkey who live together in peace, adding the Turkish people of Kurdish origin have been living here in peace for the last 1000 years. "All have equal rights and all are first class citizen," he said. Demirel also said that the southeastern region is part of Turkey and the indivisible unity of Turkey will always be protected by its people, organizations, and its armed forces. He said that Turkey is facing problems but they will be overcome in time, adding the population increase must be taken down to 1 percent from the present 3 by the beginning of the next centruy and the agricultural population percentage must be lowered from its present 45 percent to 10 percent.
