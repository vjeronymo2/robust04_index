Article Type:CSO [Text]Translating material from the press of the Hungarian minorities in Romania, Slovakia, Serbia, and Ukraine presents several difficulties. In the original text, cities and other geographic areas are called by their traditional Hungarian names, which are usually different from the official names used in the majority language. In addition, parties and organizations are often designated by unexpanded acronyms, and it is not even always clear whether these are derived from the Hungarian or from the majority language. Moreover, some designations are politically sensitive due to territorial disputes or historical considerations. FBIS has adopted the following guidelines for handling such material. The guidelines assume that the translator has access to the bilingual list of place names published in Budapest: (MAGYAR NEVE? Hatarokon tuli helysegnev-szotar; Arany Lapok, 1990). Since the Ukrainian section of the book gives the Russian form and is therefore obsolete, a partial Hungarian/Ukrainian list of place names is included below (p.6). In addition, lists of party and organization acronyms are provided.

I. Use of Bilingual Place Names and Acronyms
Translations should give the majority-language form of place names and organizations that is approved by the Board of Geographic Names (BGN) gazetteer, because this is the internationally recognized form found on maps. At the same time, the source's use of the Hungarian should be indicated because it reflects the original material and may have political implications. The following guidelines address both requirements: 1. In the headline and subslug, use the majority language version of the place or organization (in the latter case, usually an acronym). 2. In the text, use whatever place name or acronym is used in the original. If this is in Hungarian, cross-reference the majority-language version in a bracketed editorial note on its first appearance in the item. 3. As is indicated by the above, the English acronym is not used. Example: Marko Interviewed at UDMR Conference in Oradea Bucharest ROMANIAI MAGYAR SZO in Hungarian 19 Jun 93 p 9 [Interview with Bela Marko, chairman of the Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania, by Janos Szabo in Oradea; date not given: "Marko on Future Role of UDMR"] [Text] Shortly after his arrival in Nagyvarad [Oradea], Bela Marko, president of the Democratic Union of Hungarians in Romania [RMDSZ--in Romanian, UDMR], gave an interview to our paper. [Szabo] What do you expect the RMDSZ to achieve here in Nagyvarad? [Marko] I hope that we can resolve some of the problems we encountered at last month's session in Kolozsvar [Cluj], where the Hungarian Smallholders Party of Romania [RMK--in Romanian, PMGMR]....
II. List of Acronyms
The following lists are incomplete but include most of the acronyms used in the Hungarian minority press of these countries in 1993. When possible, both the Hungarian and majority-language acronyms are listed and cross-referenced because the minority press sometimes uses them interchangeably. NOTE: The translation of "szovetseg" and several similar words--translated variously as union, alliance, federation, and association--could not be standardized because of previously established variations in common usage.

IV. Miscellaneous or Difficult Terms
Bacska = Backa in Serbo-Croatian (partially Hungarian-inhabited area of northern Serbia) Baranya = Baranja in Serbo-Croatian (partially Hungarian-inhabited area of northern Croatia) Bos = Gabcikovo (Slovak) In keeping with the guidelines on bilingual place names, references to the controversial dam project on the Danube should be rendered as Gabcikovo in headlines and subslugs. In the text, Bos should be retained, with [Gabcikovo] in brackets after the first occurrence. When the reference is to the original joint Slovak-Hungarian project, use Gabcikovo-Nagymaros in headlines and subslugs, retaining Bos-Nagymaros in the text. Treat the first occurrence in the text as follows: Bos [Gabcikovo]-Nagymaros. Csallokoz = Velky Zitny Ostrov (Slovak) (area between the main Danube and its northern branch) Csango = [Partly assimilated Hungarian minority in the Moldavian region of Romania] Untranslatable. Avoid in headlines. Unless the text provides a definition, put the above in brackets after the first occurrence in the text. Delvidek (or delvidek) = ["Southern country"--southern Hungary until 1920, now northern Serbia and Croatia]. Do not translate. Use the original, putting the above in brackets after the first occurrence in the text. Avoid in headlines. Dunantul = [Hungary west of the Danube river] Do not translate as Transdanubia, because it may not be clear whether the region east or west of the Danube is meant. Use the original, putting the above in brackets after the first occurrence in the text. Avoid in headlines. Note that the suffix "tul" (beyond) indicates west of the Danube but east of the the Tisza (see below). Felvidek (or felvidek) = ["Upper country"--northern Hungary until 1920, now Slovakia] Do not translate. Use the original, putting the above in brackets after the first occurrence in the text. Avoid in headlines. Karpatalja = The area of Ukraine on the Western side of the Carpathian mountains. In headlines and subslugs, translate as "Transcarpathia," which reflects the Ukrainian term for the region. However, in the text, translate as Subcarpathia (reflecting the Hungarian original). After the first occurrence of Subcarpathia in the text, put [Ukraine's Transcarpathian region] in brackets. NOTE: When rendering the official majority-language form of a place name, use the Ukrainian form (rather than the Russian as previously in Soviet times). A transliterated list of common place names is appended. Kiralyhagomelleki Reformatus Egyhaz = Kiralyhagomelleki [Pasul Craiului (Oradea)] Reformed Church District. NOTE: Put Romanian term in brackets after the first occurrence only. Magyar = Hungarian Always translate--do not use "Magyars," etc. When the text requires distinguishing between citizens of Hungary and Hungarians living in other countries, qualify as appropriate with a term in brackets: [minority] Hungarians; [ethnic] Hungarians; [emigre] Hungarians. Magyarsag (1) = all Hungarians everywhere. Example: A magyarsag erti Istvan kiralynak a. All Hungarians everywhere understand King Stephen's. Magyarsag (2) = being Hungarian; Hungarian identity. Example: Buszke a magyarsagara. He is proud of being Hungarian. Example: Amerikaban is megorizte a magyarsagat. Even in America, he kept his Hungarian identity. nemzet = nation; people nemzetiseg = ethnic minority; ethnic group; nationality (citizenship) nemzeti kisebbseg = national minority nemzetalkoto elemek = constituent elements of the nation tarsnemzet = co-nation Szekely = Szekler Szekler is the appropriate English translation, but it is obscure. Therefore, avoid in headlines, and put the following in brackets after the first occurrence in the text: [sub-group of the Hungarian minority in the Transylvanian region of Romania]. Tiszantul = [the region of Hungary east of the Tisza river] Do not translate. Use the original, putting the above in brackets after the first occurrence in the text. Avoid in headlines.
