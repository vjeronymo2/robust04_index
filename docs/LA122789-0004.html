December 27, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: MOVIES

Historic Movie House May Close: The Texas Theater, the Dallas movie house where Lee Harvey Oswald was arrested after the assassination of President John F. Kennedy, may be closing soon. The 60-year-old theater was built by Howard Hughes but its current owner, United Artists, wants to shut it down because it's losing money. The Texas Theater Historical Assn. is trying to declare the theater a historical landmark and has asked director Oliver Stone to consider allowing a special screening at the theater of his new film, "Born on the Fourth of July," which was filmed in the same neighborhood. Claudia Puig
