910511 FT 11 MAY 91 / Fines over water workers' deaths THAMES WATER Authority and Davy Mining, a tunnelling company, were fined a total of Pounds 30,000 yesterday after two workers died of lack of oxygen during an inspection of the London Water Ring Main works at Stoke Newington. Thames Water Authority and Davy, of Rotherham, South Yorks, admitted two counts each of failing to ensure the safety of the men in 1988. Thames Water Authority was fined Pounds 10,000 on each count and ordered to pay Pounds 4,200 costs. Davy Mining was fined Pounds 5,000 on each count with Pounds 2,078 costs.
