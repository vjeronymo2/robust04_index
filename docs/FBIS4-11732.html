BFN [Text] Ankara, March 29, Irna -- Issues of common interest were discussed here today between Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister For Commonwealth of Independant States (CIS) Morteza Bank And his Turkish counterpart Uzdom Sunberg. The two sides agreed on expansion of bilateral ties to promote stability and security in the region and to strengthen the position of the Caucasus and Central Asia republics both regionally and at the international level. Reffering to the good ties between the two countries, the Turkish official said Iran's presitige among the regional countries can play an effictive role to settle the Karabakh issue with Turkey's help. Bank said Tehran and Ankara could use their influence and coordinate efforts to settle the regional issues. The Iranian official arrived here last night on a 2-day visit.
