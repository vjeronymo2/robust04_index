Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] After meeting Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou at Maximos Palace at noon today, Hans Van Den Broek, the European Union [EU] Commissioner in charge of foreign affairs, said the Greek Government has some good ideas on Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM] so the dialogue will continue under his own mediation. Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias participated in the meeting. He said that the efforts continue and added that he will meet the U.S. President's envoy in Skopje Matthew Nimetz and international mediator Cyrus Vance. He said: There are coordinated efforts to resolve the issue of Skopje. I hope there will be a significant outcome. The EU commissioner said the EU is trying to find a formula to prepare the two sides to move closer. He said the EU is in a difficult position because of the Greek measures against Skopje, adding that these measures are against international law. The European Commission and the EU support Vance and Nimetz's efforts and will try to find a solution. Van Den Broek said the issue does not impact only Greece, but also peace and stability in the greater Balkan region. The commissioner said he is in contact with FYROM because Greece has some good ideas. The European Commission will discuss Skopje after the Easter of the Catholics, which will be celebrated next Sunday [3 April].
