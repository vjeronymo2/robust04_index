August 9, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

AMPCO-PITTSBURGH ACQUIRES STAKE IN MIDWAY AIRLINES

Ampco-Pittsburgh Corp., an engineering company, said Tuesday that it has acquired a 6.6% stake in Midway Airlines Inc. with the possible view toward acquiring it, a move that could spark a bidding war for the regional airline.

Pittsburgh-based Ampco, which has recently undergone a major streamlining, said it currently has no plans to acquire Chicago-based Midway, but that it may be interested in some type of merger.

Analysts said the announcement may spur other interested parties to come forward as well. "Midway is in play. Who knows what will happen if the right offer comes along?" Louis Marckesano, an analyst with Janney Montgomery Scott Inc., said.

The price of Midway stock rose $1.50 a share to close at $20.50 on the New York Stock Exchange.

Midway Airlines said in a statement that Ampco-Pittsburgh had not contacted it in connection with the investment.

But it said it was aware that Ampco had notified the Securities and Exchange Commission of the stock purchases, which were made between July 25 and Aug. 3 at prices ranging from $14.625 to $18 a share.
