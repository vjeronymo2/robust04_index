940226 FT 26 FEB 94 / Peacekeepers close in on Tuzla United Nations peacekeepers yesterday moved closer towards opening Tuzla airport in eastern Bosnia which has besieged by Bosnian Serb forces, writes Judy Dempsey. Nine German Leopard tanks, which can withstand mortar and artillery strikes, reached Tuzla as part of the UN's efforts to re-open the airport by March 7. The move coincided with an announcement by the Russian parliament to send an additional 300 peacekeeping troops. Mr Vitaly Churkin, Russia's deputy foreign minister and Moscow's special envoy to Bosnia, said the decision would 'symbolise Russia's growing central role in a Bosnian settlement.'
