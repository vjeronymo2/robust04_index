August 27, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

THE MOVIE CHART: FILMS NOW GOING INTO PRODUCTION:

Blood Oath (Village Roadshow). Shooting in Australia. Bryan Brown stars in this political thriller, which parallels the Nuremberg trials. Brown portrays Capt. John Williams, an Aussie who fearlessly defends a Japanese officer in a post-World War II trial of war criminals. Executive producers Graham Burke, Greg Coote and John Tarnoff. Producers Charles Waterstreet and Annie Bleakley. Director Steve Wallace. Screenwriters Dennis Whitburn and Brian Williams. Also stars George Takei, Nicholas Eadie, John Clarke and John Bach.

The Haunting Fear (American Independent Productions). Shooting in Southern California. Jan-Michael Vincent and Karen Black star in this psychological thriller concerning a woman whose intense fear of being buried alive is exploited by her devious spouse. Executive producer Fred Olen Ray. Producer Diana Jaffe. Director Ray. Screenwriter Sherman Scott. Also stars Robert Clarke, Brinke Stevens, Robert Quarry, Michael Berryman and Jay Richardson. Distributor AIP. Late '89 release.

The Reflecting Skin (Skyehost Canada). Shooting in Calgary. This Canadian drama examines a love story through the eyes of an 8-year-old boy. Executive producer Jim Beach. Producers Dominic Anciano and Ray Burgis. Director/screenwriter Philip Ridley. Stars Lindsay Duncan and Viggo Mortensen.

Strangers (Lost Cowgirl). Shooting in Dallas. Psychological drama set in a small eastern Texas town involving a young boy who's desperately trying to outrun various and dangerous situations. Producers Mike Dempsey and Kim David. Director/screenwriter Donna Roark. Stars Stephen Cummins, Stephanie Geyer, Keith Oncale and Duane Utech. David Pecchia
