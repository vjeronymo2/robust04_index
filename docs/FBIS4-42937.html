BFN [Text] Istanbul (AA) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin has said that Turkey is justified regarding its requests concerning the Karkuk-Yumurtalik pipeline. Cetin was speaking after his 50-minute meeting with British Foreign Secretary Douglas Hurd held during the North Atlantic Council meetings in Istanbul today. He said that he told his British counterpart that Turkey's sole aim is to save the pipeline. Cetin continued: I explained to him that this cannot be compared to other countries' relations with Iraq, that it is a very special problem, and that we are justified in trying to save the pipeline. Cetin noted that relations with the European Union [EU] were also discussed in detail at the meeting. Recalling that Germany and France will be chairing the EU meetings consecutively as of July, Cetin said: We view this period as a significant one in Turkey's relations with the Customs Union as well as the EU. Cetin stated that the situation in Iraq and the Azerbaijani-Armenian clashes were also discussed at the meeting. Pointing out that they also dwelt on the benefits of extending the tripartite meetings process among Turkey, Britain, and Germany to include France as well, Cetin concluded by saying that the tripartite meetings can be converted into quadripartite ones.
