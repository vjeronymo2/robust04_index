Language: Polish Article Type:BFN [Relayed by Warsaw TV Polonia Network] [Text] Foreign Affairs Minister Andrzej Olechowski has returned from the United States, where he held talks with representatives of the U.S. Government on the Partnership for Peace program, among other things. According to the minister, the results of the talks will bring Poland closer to participation in the implementation of the program. [Begin Olechowski recording] The primary objective of the Partnership for Peace concept is to create a Europe without borders and dividing lines. If, however, dividing lines were to exist, it is understood--and this view is shared--that Poland would find itself on the NATO side of the divide. Because the United States firmly emphasizes that its security depends on our security, it is in the interest of both sides, Poland and the United States--and NATO--that Poland should become an active partner in the Partnership for Peace. [end recording]
