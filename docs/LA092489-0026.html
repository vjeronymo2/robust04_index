September 24, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

ANTI-SAVINGS POLICIES

Concerning James Flanigan's column in August, as a baby boomer, I'm not going to rely on Social Security just because the government says it's "safe." That's like letting a drunk drive you home, just because he promises he will deliver you home "safely."

Once you realize that the so-called cash reserves are really just paper, you won't feel so secure. Here's how the government "invests" the Social Security surplus. They just give this extra money to other government agencies that need it. That agency then issues Social Security a bond (really an IOU).

Thus, the buildup of the Social Security "cash" reserves is really just a buildup of the national debt. The money isn't in a bank account, waiting to be withdrawn. When the baby boomers retire, the national debt must be converted to cash to pay benefits. How will this happen? Either the government must develop a surplus in other areas,or it must put a tremendous tax burden on our children. Both of these aren't very likely. Most likely, Social Security will cut our benefits.

I'm relying on my own savings for retirement.

STEVEN G. VERNON

Los Angeles
