Language: Indonesian Article Type:BFN [Text] President Suharto has said that the Group of 15 [G- 15] wants to offer ideas or initiatives not only for the Nonaligned Movement [NAM] but also for enhancing South-South cooperation. Speaking to reporters aboard a DC-10 airliner flying home with the prsidential delegation, President Suharto said: [Begin Suharto recording in progress] As I said earlier, the G-15 wants to offer ideas or initiatives on the selection of projects as a follow-up to NAM decisions. The G-15 is not only working for its own interests, but also wants to contribute ideas to NAM members through the NAM chairman as well as other South nations through the Group of 77. Thus, such ideas will serve the interests of both the NAM as well as enhance South-South cooperation. Various nations have made practical suggestions on the implementation of projects as a realization of South-South cooperation. I would like to say once again that not only will this serve the interests of the NAM itself, but it will also promote South-South cooperation. [end recording] President Suharto and his delegation arrived at Halim Perdanakusuma Airport in Jakarta at 0930 West Indonesian Standard Time this morning from New Delhi. The president, who attended the G-15 summit in India from 28 to 30 March, was greeted by Vice President Try Sutrisno, ministers, and other senior officials at Halim Perdanakusuma Airport.
