Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] During a session of the House of Representatives Budget Committee on 30 March, Taro Nakayama of the opposition Liberal Democratic Party asked Prime Minister Hosokawa how the government will deal with economic sanctions against North Korea, which is suspected of developing nuclear weapons, if the UN Security Council votes to impose sanctions on it. In response to the question, the prime minister said, "Each ministry and agency is now studying the concrete ways possible within its administrative jurisdiction to deal with the economic sanctions." The prime minister also stressed that "Japan will have to carry out complete preparations." In this way, the prime minister made it clear that the government is now studying a wide range of measures to deal with the economic sanctions and to maintain public peace and order in Japan. During the session, Sadao Yamahana, the minister in charge of political reform from the Social Democratic Party, was asked how he will deal with the issue of North Korea's nuclear development program. In response, Mr. Yamahana echoed the prime minister's statement, saying, "As a cabinet member, I will deal with the issue in a responsible manner within the limits of the Constitution." Testifying before the session, Shigetake Ogata, Public Security Investigation Agency director general, stated, "Of the ethnic Koreans residing in Japan, approximately 247,000 have some relationship with the General Association of Korean Residents in Japan, and approximately 56,000 of them are formally affiliated with the pro-North Korean organization in Japan." As for the amount of their remittances to North Korea, Mr. Ogata said, "We possess no accurate measure of the remittances," but he added, "rumors have it that the amount ranges from 60 billion yen to 80 billion yen a year."
