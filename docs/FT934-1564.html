931218 FT 18 DEC 93 / World News in Brief: Life terms for 'appalling' murder Four people were convicted at Manchester Crown Court of murdering Suzanne Capper, 16, who was doused in petrol and set on fire near Stockport, Cheshire, after being held captive for nearly a week. Jean Powell, 26, her estranged husband Glyn, 29, and Bernadette McNeilly, 24, were given life terms for what Mr Justice Potts, the trial judge, called 'as appalling a murder as it is possible to imagine'. Anthony Dudson, 17, was ordered to be detained during Her Majesty's pleasure.
