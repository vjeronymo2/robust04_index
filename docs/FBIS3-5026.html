Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Singapore and Vietnam have signed an avoidance of double taxation agreement to bring about more trade, investment, and exchange of expertise. The signing ceremony in Hanoi today was witnessed by Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong and Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. Minister of State for Finance and Communications Commodore Teo Chee Hean signed for Singapore, while Finance Minister Ho Te represented the Vietnamese Government. The tax agreement will be particularly welcomed by Singaporeans, who have investments of some 700 million dollars in Vietnam. It is the latest in a series of bilateral trades signed since 1992 which have further boosted economic ties. These include shipping, trading, and investment guarantee tax. [passage omitted]
