Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, March 17 (CTK) -- Slovak President Michal Kovac's Office today said it considered the accusation by the Petition Committee that it "has desecrated the sacred constitutional, human and natural right of citizens to referendum" deplorable. On Wednesday [16 March], the President's Office said that a referendum through which the Movement for a Democratic Slovakia (HZDS) wanted to solve the question of early elections, the validity of the mandates of the deputies who have switched parties and the "origin" of the money used in privatization, would not take place since the 350,000 signatures, which according to the Slovak constitution are needed for the president to call a referendum, were not collected. The Office said that out of more than 420,000 signatures collected only 233,000 were valid. This statement provoked anger on the part of the Petition Committee established by the HZDS of former Slovak Premier Vladimir Meciar. In a statement given to CTK today, the President's Office said that Kovac would violate the constitution if he called a referendum without 350,000 valid signatures. While checking the validity of the petition sheets the President's Office proceeded in harmony with the law on petitions and it considers the accusation of the illegal interpretation of some of its provisions untrue, deliberate and non-trustworthy, the statement says.
