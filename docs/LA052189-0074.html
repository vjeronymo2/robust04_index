May 21, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

RECORD RACK: THE GODFATHERS "MORE SONGS ABOUT LOVE AND HATE." EPIC **: ALBUMS ARE RATED ON A SCALE OF ONE STAR (POOR) TO FIVE STARS (A CLASSIC).

This second effort from the besuited British quintet best-known for the roiling, boiling single "Birth, School, Work, Death" (1988) is as bedrock-solid as it is ultimately unspectacular. Not a bum track in the bunch, but aside from "Walking, Talking Johnny Cash Blues" -- a definite cult classick -- and the current single "She Gives Me Love" -- which pretty much packs everything these wise guys know into one lashing, slashing hunk of pop aggression -- there's naught that memorable either. Still, fans of dour vocals, sour romance, kinda Kinks-y, semi-Stonesy, sixtieszoid riffslinging -- (some HAIR-raising guitar outros here) can lay their ample heads on this weary bosom and find that the heart of rock 'n' roll is still making an offer you can't refuse. DON WALLER
