Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yerevan, March 18 (SNARK) -- 15.7 per cent of Yerevan residents would vote once again in favor of the President Levon Ter-Petrosyan if the election were held in the nearest future. These are the results of the poll carried out in the first week of March by the "National Self-determination" Union (NSU)'s Public Opinion Studies Center. In 1991, when the President election was held, over 80 per cent of Armenian citizens voted for Levon Ter-Petrosyan. More than a quarter of the respondents, the total number of which is 600, claimed they wouldn't vote for any candidate. 13.2 per cent prefer the NSU leader Paruyr Hayrikyan. Other candidates' rating is far lower. According to the poll, published in Yerevan newspaper "Ankakhutyun" (Independence), about 9 per cent of Yerevan residents will vote for the ruling party - Armenian National (Pannational] Movement. Most chances have Armenian Revolutionary Federation "Dashnaktsutyun" (20.8 per cent), and NSU (11.5 per cent). A little more than 8 per cent of the respondents choose to vote for the Communist Party.
