Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Budapest, Jan 21 (AFP) -- Russian crude deliveries to Hungary on the Friendship pipeline resumed Friday [21 January] following a two-and-a-half day break since Tuesday, officials of the state-owned Hungarian Oil and Gas Company (MOL) said. The supply break was due to administrative problems at Nafta, the Russian exporter, MOL said. The break, which "was not the first example of a halt of supplies", did not affect work at Hungary's oil refineries and resulted only in rescheduling a 40,000-ton delivery to Hungary, the officials said. Last year, Hungary imported 5.1 million tonnes of crude from Russia and this year's imports were also planned to exceed five million tons, they said.
