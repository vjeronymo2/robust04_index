Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Video report by Metin Gokce in Davos] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] Speaking at a news conference today, President Suleyman Demirel said the following about his meetings with Israeli Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres and Palestinian head of state Yasir 'Arafat: [Begin Demirel recording] I said that this is a golden opportunity that should not be missed. An enmity of 100 years, of at least 50 years, can be eliminated. This opportunity must be used well. Turkey supports all sides to achieve this. If closer support from our side is needed, we are ready to give it. However, if we interfere without being asked, we might further complicate this very difficult and complicated issue. I said the same thing to 'Arafat. [end recording] Demirel said that at a meeting with Bulgarian Prime Minister Berov today, he stressed the importance of establishing regional peace and stability and building the east-west highway that will connect Istanbul to the Adriatic Sea over Bulgaria, Macedonia, and Albania. At the meeting, Berov categorically denied the reports that the PKK [Workers Party of Kurdistan] terrorists expelled from certain European countries are basing themselves in Bulgaria. Demirel also attended a meeting on Central Asia today. He ended his contacts in Davos today. After spending the night here, he will return to Turkey tomorrow.
