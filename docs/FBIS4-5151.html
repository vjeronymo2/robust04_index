BFN [Text] British Secretary of State for Education John Patten says he sensed a great desire on the part of Malaysia and Britain to bring business back on normal footing as soon as possible. He said this was his impression from meetings with various ministers and British businessmen during his three-day visit to Malaysia which ended today. He was anxious that the flow of investment from the United Kingdom into Malaysia continues because he thinks that it would be a benefit to both countries. Patten, the highest-ranking official to visit Malaysia since the Malaysian Government slapped a ban on awarding government projects to British companies in February. Patten said that once he returned home, he would try his best to give a picture that relations between the two countries were getting better again and that investment opportunities in Malaysia were too good for British investors to miss.
