Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, March 8 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Premier Li Peng met here this afternoon with visiting Dutch Minister of Foreign Affairs Pieter Kooijmans, saying that China attaches much importance to developing friendship and cooperation with the Netherlands. Li said that the Netherlands was one of the earliest Western European countries to acknowledge the independence of the People's Republic of China. He noted that there are no conflicts of fundamental interests between China and the Netherlands. He said that the Netherlands is China's fifth largest trade partner in Western Europe and the two countries are cooperating in a wide range of areas including electronics, shipbuilding, petrochemicals, water conservancy and agriculture. Li expressed the hope that the two sides will make fast progress in the talks on some large cooperation projects now underway. China is a big market, Li said, and it offers many new cooperation opportunities to Western European countries including the Netherlands and the momentum of development in trade and cooperation between the two countries should continue. Kooijmans said that Sino-Dutch economic and trade relations have been strengthened and the prospect for such cooperation is broad. He noted that while meeting with the Chinese foreign minister and foreign trade minister, he expressed the sincerity of the Dutch side to further develop bilateral relations. This afternoon, Minister of Justice Xiao Yang and Minister of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation Wu Yi met with Kooijmans on separate occasions. Kooijmans and his party will end their China visit tomorrow morning.
