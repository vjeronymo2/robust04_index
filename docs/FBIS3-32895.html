Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From "The Voz Andes" newscast] [Text] The UN General Assembly today confirmed Ecuadoran UN Ambassador Jose Ayala Lasso as the first UN high commissioner for human rights. His duties will involve the promotion and protection of human rights, including the right to development and the conception of suggestions to improve its implementation. Upon expressing gratitude for the appointment, Ayala Lasso requested the support of all governments and invited them to a sincere dialogue. He also pledged to be objective and impartial in all situations and with all states. The new high commissioner reaffirmed the universality, indivisibility, and interdependence of human rights, although he pledged to pay attention to national and regional characteristics and to historic, cultural, and religious traditions. Ayala Lasso also issued a warning to his own country, Ecuador. [Begin Ayala Lasso recording] In the performance of my duties, I will be objective and impartial in all situations and with all states, but as an irrepressible expression of love for my people, which I will soon cease to represent at the United Nations, I will be particularly harsh in the execution of my duties, to ensure that Ecuador's conduct with regard to respect for human rights will be flawless. [end recording] The Ecuadoran diplomat will have the rank of UN assistant secretary general. His mandate will last four years as of 28 February, and he will be based in Geneva.
