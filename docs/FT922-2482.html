920617 FT 17 JUN 92 / Parliament and Politics: Bottomley defends plans for dentistry HEALTH SECRETARY Mrs Virginia Bottomley last night defended government proposals to cut dentists' fees by 7 per cent and rejected privatisation claims in the Commons. Mr Peter Hain, Labour MP for Neath, told her at question time: 'By increasing charges to patients and cutting fees to dentists you are effectively privatising dental care, especially since many people cannot actually find an National Health dentist in their part of the country. 'Instead of playing Mary Poppins, you have become the Cruella de Ville of the National Health Service,' he protested. Mrs Bottomley replied: 'The proposals that we've brought forward in fact would result in a reduction in the charges to patients.'
