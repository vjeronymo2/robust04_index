BFN [Text] Beijing, June 8 (XINHUA) -- The Central Military Commission held a ceremony here today to promote 19 army officers to general. Chinese President Jiang Zemin, who is also chairman of the Central Military Commission, gave them their certificates of rank. The 19 new generals are: Xu Huizi and Li Jing, deputy chiefs of the General Staff of the People's Liberation Army (PLA); Yang Dezhong, director of the Security Bureau of the PLA General Staff; Wang Ruilin, deputy director of the PLA General Political Department; Zhou Keyu, political commissar of the PLA General Logistics Department; Ding Henggao, minister in charge of the Commission of Science, Technology and Industry for National Defense; Dai Xuejiang, political commissar of the commission; Li Wenqing, political commissar of the National Defense University; Wang Ke, commander of the Shenyang Military Area; Li Laizhu, commander of the Beijing Military Area; Gu Shanqing, political commissar of the Beijing Military Area; Liu Jingsong, commander of the Lanzhou Military Area; Cao Pengsheng, political commissar of the Lanzhou Military Area; Zhang Taiheng, commander of the Jinan Military Area; Song Qingwei, political commissar of the Jinan Military Area; Gu Hui, commander of the Nanjing Military Area; Li Xilin, commander of the Guangzhou Military Area; Shi Yuxiao, political commissar of the Guangzhou Military Area; and Li Jiulong, commander of the Chengdu Military Area. In 1988, 17 officers were promoted to the rank of general, and six more in 1993.
