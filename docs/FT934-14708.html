931015 FT 15 OCT 93 / Parliament and Politics: Chalfont attacks plan for Radio 5 LORD Chalfont, chairman of the Radio Authority, last night attacked the BBC for deciding to go ahead with a news and sports radio network in place of Radio 5 - and pre-empting next year's new national commercial channel. 'If the BBC is able to pre-empt a format which certainly could be provided by the commercial sector at no extra cost to the TV licence fee payer and without narrowing listener choice, how can we, in the independent sector, be expected to make rational plans for broadening listener choice in the future?' he asked. Lord Chalfont was speaking at a dinner in London to mark the 20th anniversary of UK commercial radio, which the Radio Authority regulates. Lord Chalfont said the BBC should have to stick to performance promises. 'No independent radio station can change its format or its promise of performance unless the change can be shown not to narrow the range of programmes available,' he added.
