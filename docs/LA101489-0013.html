October 14, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

COUP ATTEMPT AGAINST GEN. NORIEGA

Another day, another congressional outcry. On Oct. 4, the day after the failed military revolt against Noriega, senators and representative like Sen. David Boren (D-Okla.) and Rep. Henry Hyde (R-Ill.), respectfully blasted the Bush Administration for doing too little to help the insurgents (Part I). Boren, a usual opponent of covert action against Noriega, said Bush should have ordered U.S. military intervention while the rebels held the headquarters of the Panamanian Defense Forces. Likewise, Hyde thought the U.S. missed a perfect opportunity to resolve the Noriega problem.

While these senators and representatives talk about blown chances, they fail to see the most powerful weapon in front of them. This weapon is patience. By being patient, the United States will eventually capture Noriega. Noriega can escape bullets, rockets, and coups but he cannot escape time.

KEN HEMPY

San Clemente
