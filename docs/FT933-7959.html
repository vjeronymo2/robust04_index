930818 FT 18 AUG 93 / International Capital Markets: Japanese telecoms group to be listed on Tokyo SE DDI, the Japanese tele communication company, will list its shares on the second section of the Tokyo stock exchange early next month. The company is one of the two private long-distance telecom companies set up after the liberalisation of the long-distance carrier market. It is capitalised at Y10.8bn (Dollars 107m) and has 177,222 outstanding shares. DDI's shareholders include Kyocera and Sony. Ahead of the TSE listing, the company will float a total of 15,000 shares, and will accept bids on August 23 starting at Y2.27m per share for 7,500 shares for a competitive auction to be held the following day. DDI said the price was based on valuations of Nippon Telegraph and Telephone, the partially state-owned telecom company, and KDD, the international telecom company. The remaining 7,500 shares will be offered at a weighted average price from August 27 to August 30.
