BFN [Text] Moscow, March 30 (CTK) -- An intergovernmental protocol on the mutual supply of goods in 1994 was initialed today in Moscow by the Russian Ministry for Internal Economic Relations [as received] and the Czech Trade and Industry Ministry. Among other goods, the document calls for the purchase of 7 million tons of Russian oil and 6.9 billion cubic meters of natural gas. Both countries have agreed that instead of drawing up yearly protocols, medium or long-term agreements should be concluded in coming years, Deputy Trade and Industry Minister Vaclav Petricek said. The Czech delegation in Moscow agreed to cooperate further on the construction of natural gas works on the basis of 1982 agreements, according to which the Czech Republic should get Russian natural gas in return for Czech supplies and labor. Petricek in Moscow also discussed the possibility of Czech participation in the modernization of the natural gas industry in Russia and in the construction of a new natural gasline from Russia to Germany via Belarus and Poland. Czech firms could help build the gasline on Russian and Belarussian territory. Further negotiations concerned the possibility of hooking the gasline up to the Czech Republic's natural gas distribution network.
