940308 FT 08 MAR 94 / Arts: Today's Television In Assignment Julian Pettifer reports on the trade disputes between Japan and the US which are damaging relations between the two economic super powers, and on Japan's search for a more independent role in the world (7.45 BBC2). Suddenly the motor car is the most popular subject for television series. After Alexei Sayle's 'Drive' on BBC1, and before 'Tales Of Modern Motoring' which starts later this month on BBC2, the Channel 4 culture series Without Walls offers a three-part series called 'Carnography'. They begin today by comparing a Ferrari with the Dodge Viper (9.00). In the second half of WW Craig Raine defends the poet Rudyard Kipling from the politically correct lemmings who mindlessly attack him (9.30). Hidden under television's most forgettable umbrella title, Network First, is a documentary about the Central School Of Ballet in London (10.40 ITV). BBC1 screens the long and inferior version of Spielberg's 1971 television drama, Duel about a car driver (played by Dennis Weaver) who is pursued by an articulated tanker (11.45).
