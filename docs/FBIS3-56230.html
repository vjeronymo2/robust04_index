Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Galina Kuchina] [Text] Riga January 13 TASS -- "The whole package of agreements dealing with the troop withdrawal from Latvia may be signed as early as in February," ambassador at large Sergey Zotov, head of the Russian delegation, told ITAR-TASS. After the end of the talks that lasted two days, Zotov had a meeting with Latvian Prime minister Valdis Birkavas. Both sides pointed to perceptible progress, achieved on the key issues. A complex agreement on the legal status of a Russian anti-missile radar station in Skrunde has almost been completed. An agreement may be reached even on the most controversial issue -- the time- limit of the functioning of the radar station. The Latvian prime minister again offered apologies to the Russian side about the incident [as received], that took place in Latvia, and said the government was taking measures for preventing in the future the actions that could have a negative impact on bilateral relations.
