BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vladimir Khody] [Text] Irkutsk May 19 TASS -- The far-regional inspection for the protection of the freedom of the press and the mass media has suspended the newspaper RUSSKIY VOSTOK instituted by the local national patriotic movement Vernost. The newspaper managers were notified that the newspaper was suspended for going beyond its goals and tasks outlined in its programme, which the newspaper did several times. The inspection declared that a recent publication containing insults against lawmakers of the Irkutsk regional legislative assembly, which was entitled "Thieves Have Won or New Regional Committee in Action", was aimed at flaring up social tension and intolerance, which violates Article 4 of the Russian Law on the Mass Media. The newspaper, which is sold beyond the Far-Eastern region and practically has a nationwide status, will have to be re-registered in Moscow.
