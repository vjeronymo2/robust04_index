BFN [Text] Podgorica, 18 May (TANJUG) -- Talks were held today between Momir Bulatovic, Montenegrin president and member of the Supreme Defense Council, and top Yugoslav Army officers. At the talks, they concluded that officers and commands of the Yugoslav Army have displayed a high degree of responsibility for the overall combat readiness of their troops under very complex circumstances. As it has been announced, the commanders of the First, Second, and Third Armies -- Generals Jevrem Cokic, Bozidar Babic, and Dusan Samardzic respectively -- General Ljubisa Velickovic, commander of the Air Force and anti-aircraft defense, and Navy Commander Admiral Dojcilo Isakovic informed Bulatovic about the level of combat readiness of the strategic formations under their command, the implementation of measures set by the Supreme Defense Council relating to the security of the state territory under present conditions, further transformation of the Yugoslav Army, and other issues. Stable financing, in line with the capabilities of the state, will enable the speedier resolution of problems in supplying units with state-of-the-art equipment and in securing better living standards for the Yugoslav Army.
