Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Oslo, Jan 4 (AFP) -- Norway's Foreign Minister Johan Joergen Holst, who suffered a stroke last month, still has problems with his speech and walking, a medical report from his doctor said. Holst, 56, fell ill on his return from an official visit to Madrid on December 16. The first medical report, issued shortly after the incident, said Holst's intellectual capabilities were not affected. The doctor's statement said the foreign minister was improving and that he would be transferred to a special center outside Oslo for further rehabilitation. Holst's doctor, Professor Rolf Nyberg-Hansen, said the stroke had affected his speech and the coordination of his walking and that he would be off work for some time. Holst was first admitted to hospital in November, suffering from overwork and exhaustion. He had just resumed limited duties when he suffered the stroke. Holst last year helped broker the so-called "Oslo Treaty" on limited Palestinian self-rule in the Jericho area and the Gaza Strip through a series of secret meetings in Norway. A spokesman for Prime Minister Gro Harlem Brundtland said on Tuesday [4 January] that she was not planning to have Holst replaced. Both family members and Foreign Ministry officials often visit Holst at the National Hospital in Oslo. On Sunday the Israeli-PLO peace negotiators Uri Savir and Abu Ala, who were in Oslo to receive a Royal medal of merit for their peace efforts, paid him a visit.
