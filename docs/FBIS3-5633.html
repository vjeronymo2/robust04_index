Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, March 9 KYODO -- Mining industry leaders Wednesday [9 March] asked the Trade Ministry to take steps to spur demand for mineral products. Presidents of major mining companies belonging to the Japan Mining Industry Association made the request in a breakfast meeting with Hiroshi Kumagai, international trade and industry minister. Mining companies are struggling due to slack demand from the auto and precious metals industries, the presidents of Sumitomo Metal Mining Co., Mitsubishi Materials Corp., Dowa Mining Co. and other companies told Kumagai. They also requested the Ministry of International Trade and Industry (MITI) to promote a policy to ensure "smooth purchases" abroad of mineral ores. Kumagai told the executives the ministry will work hard to help the mining industry by improving fiscal and tax systems, MITI officials said.
