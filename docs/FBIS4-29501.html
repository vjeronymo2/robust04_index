BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA June 17 -- Japan's unchangeable policy is to develop its cooperation with Vietnam despite political changes in Tokyo, said Japanese Foreign Minister Kakizawa while receiving yesterday Vice Foreign Minister Vu Khoan who was attending an international meeting there. Both Mr Kakizawa and Mr Vu Khoan expressed their pleasure at the expansion and consolidation of the friendly and cooperative reactions between the countries, especially those following Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet's visit to Japan. They also exchanged views on measures to be taken to further those relations. On the same day, Mr Vu Khoan had a meeting with Mr. A. Nishigaki, chairman of the Overseas Economic Cooperation Fund (OECF) during which host and guest exchanged views on the implementation of Japan's aid programme for Vietnam and things to be done for their effective cooperation.
