May 17, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

FISH REPORT: YELLOWTAIL ARE HITTING SOUTH OF SAN DIEGO

Yellowtail have made a strong showing at the Coronado Islands south of San Diego and, according to Carol Stock, a reservations clerk at Fisherman's Landing, they're right on schedule.

On Monday, anglers aboard nine boats brought in 375 yellowtail, all in the 12- to 15-pound class.

On Tuesday the bite showed no signs of slowing. The Conquest, with 18 anglers aboard, reported that 82 of the popular jacks had already been landed, and the Prowler, with 45 fishermen aboard, reported catching 85 yellowtail.

Meanwhile, barracuda are cooperating in large numbers with fishermen at many areas along the coast, a further indication that this could be a successful summer.

WILLOW BEACH -- Trout scattered, biting best upriver. Striped bass biting three miles downriver.

LAKE MEAD -- Striped bass bite best in Overton Arm near meadows, and at Vegas Wash. Anchovies best bet. Catfish picking up, biting throughout lake in shallows. Largemouth bass slow, best above Temple Bar. Waterdogs working best.

BULLHEAD CITY -- Striped bass bite very good on river, averaging two-four pounds. Artificials working best near Davis Dam; drifting anchovies productive throughout river. Lake: Largemouth bass biting in mornings, with plastic worms and waterdogs working best. Catfish biting in coves "on anything that smells." Trout slow.

SALTON SEA -- Orangemouth corvina bite slow because of winds. Fish, averaging 10 pounds, biting best in mornings on live bait. Flathead catfish to 32 pounds biting near mouth of Alamo River. Croaker bite good. Sargo and tilapia slow.

LAKE CACHUMA -- Largemouth bass being caught mainly in shallow water along east shoreline and near the flats. Dan Deegan, Lompoc, two bass, 14 pounds. Catfish to 10 pounds, biting best in backs of bays and shallows. Mackerel best bet. Trout biting best at Cachuma Bay.

LAKE CASITAS -- Largemouth bass taking crawfish and dark-colored plastic worms to 10 feet. Station Canyon and Sunken Island best areas. John Bohanon, Ventura, 9 1/2-pound bass. Trout bite fair, best in mornings on surface. Catfish biting to 15 feet. Deadhorse Canyon and Deer Slope best areas.

PYRAMID LAKE -- Largemouth and smallmouth bass biting to 25 feet. Most artificials working. Catfish bite very good in coves, with fish averaging seven-10 pounds. Mackerel best bet. Crappie and bluegills scattered, but anglers' catching some. Striped bass slow. Glen Morgan, Chatsworth, 14-pound striper, caught at Yellow Bar.

CASTAIC LAKE -- Bluegills biting consistently in both lakes. Largemouth bass best in lower lake, averaging six pounds. Nightcrawlers and plastic worms best baits. Fish averaging three pounds in upper lake. Trout bite good in both lakes, averaging 1 1/2-2 pounds. Berkley Power Bait best bet.

IRVINE LAKE -- Trout bite consistent throughout lake. Paul Mintzer, Anaheim, 9 1/2-pound trout. Largemouth bass biting along shoreline on spinner baits and plastic worms. Crappie biting best under docks.

LAKE SKINNER -- Trout providing most action, biting best at dam. Largemouth bass slow. Catfish picking up.

BIG BEAR -- Trout slow; Power Bait best bet. Largemouth bass picking up, averaging one-two pounds. Plastic worms best bet. Catfish slow.

BISHOP AREA -- Owens River good for brown trout to three pounds. Crickets working best. Lower Owens: Wild-trout section good for fishermen using crickets, small spinners. Fly fishermen using nymphs doing well. Intake II: Mostly small brook trout and some browns. No crowds. Sabrina: Water level very low; only small boats permitted. Trollers doing best. South Lake: Level low, good for small brook and brown trout. Meal worms best bet. Rock Creek Lake: Good for pan-sized rainbows. Small spinners working best. Convict Lake: Excellent for rainbows and browns -- one measured at 5 3/4 pounds. Fly fishing good in creek; only small boats allowed. Crowley: Fishing slow in terms of numbers, but some big trout reported. Sacramento perch picking up, with small jig-and-worm combination most productive.

JUNE LAKE AREA -- Fishing good despite poor weather. Shore fishermen using Power Bait doing best, catching some limits. Trollers using black and gold Rapalas doing equally well. Gull Lake: Four-four rainbow taken on nightcrawler. Still-fishing in front of condos good for fish to five pounds. Rush Creek: Salmon eggs working best, with pan-sized rainbows the norm.

BRIDGEPORT -- Virginia Lakes: Little Virginia good for mostly pan-sized rainbows. Ice melting in Big Virginia and Trumble Lakes. Streams in runoff conditions, bite very good for pan-sized rainbows. Most baits working. Reservoir: Fishing spotty, water level still low. Bigger fish taking Rapalas or large Roostertails. Twin Lakes: Good for mostly pan-sized rainbows. Brown trout picking up. West Walker River: High and off-color, most fish coming out of deep holes. Carmen Lake good for one-three pound brook trout and an occasional cutthroat.
