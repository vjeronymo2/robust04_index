920730 FT 30 JUL 92 / UN plans airlift for Somalia The United Nations special envoy in Somalia said yesterday that the international airport in war-shattered Mogadishu would re-open for an emergency airlift and that 50 UN military observers would be deployed in the city soon, Julian Ozanne writes from Nairobi. Mr Mohamed Sahnoun, a UN ambassador, who on Tuesday met one of Somalia's feuding warlords, appears to have made little headway, however, with negotiations to send in 500 UN guards to protect humanitarian operations in Somalia, where up to 4.5m people are in need of food aid.
