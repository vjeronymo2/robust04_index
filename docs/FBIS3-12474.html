Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Tunis, 1 Mar (MENA)--The Palestinian news agency WAFA reported tonight that Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat received a written message from Russian President Boris Yeltsin. The agency said that 'Arafat received this message when he met this evening with Mr. Igor Ivanov, Russian President Yeltsin's envoy, and that 'Arafat and Ivanov held extended and detailed talks on the current stage of the Middle East peace process after the al-Ibrahimi Mosque massacre in Hebron. They discussed the results of that massacre when all Arab parties to the talks suspended negotiations with Israel. The news agency said that during his meeting, 'Arafat concentrated on the basic Palestinian demands at this stage, which are: First, sending an international or multinational force to the occupied Palestinian territories, the West Bank and Gaza Strip, by coordinating and consulting with the co-sponsors of the peace process, the United States and Russia, and the UN secretary general to guarantee the security and safety of the Palestinian people there. Second, disarming the settlers and preserving public peace. Third, starting to dismantle the settlements that have extremists in them.
