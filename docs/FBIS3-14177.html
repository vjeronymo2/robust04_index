Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] All the warring factions in Sudan today in Nairobi signed an agreement for safe passage of relief supplies and humanitarian assistance to civilians in the war affected areas. The representatives, who met under the chairmanship of Kenya's Foreign Minister, Mr. Stephen Kalonzo Musyoka, who is also the chairman of the IGADD [Intergovernmental Authority on Drought and Development] foreign affairs standing committee overseeing the Sudanese problem, agreed that the humanitarian and political dimensions of the Sudanese problem be treated separately but simultaneously. The Sudanese Government delegation was led by Mr. Muhammad Khalifah, Commander Salva [Kiir] mayar dit for SPLM/SPLA, while SPLM [Sudanese People's Liberation Movement]/SPLA [Sudanese People's Liberation Army]-United was represented by Dr. Richard Mola. Mr. Musyoka also said that the parties had also agreed to declare corridors of tranquillity to be set by the UN and the concerned parties from 15th to 30th April, May and June this year [as heard] to allow for vaccinations of Sudanese children under five years. The parties also said that the decision was necessary to alleviate unnecessary hunger and restore basic social services. They, however, maintained that humanitarian activities should be transparent and should never be channelled to warring parties. The occasion was also witnessed by all IGADD foreign affairs ministers.
