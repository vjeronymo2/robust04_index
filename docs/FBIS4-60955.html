BFN [Text] A visit to Budapest by an official Kazakh delegation led by Deputy Foreign Minister Konstantin Zhigalov was devoted to examination of the possibilities to deepen and broaden political and economic integration between Hungary and Kazakhstan. The guests from Almaty visited one of the largest Hungarian enterprises -- the Videoton joint stock production amalgamation, whose products are well known on the former USSR market. The Kazakh delegation was interested first of all in the progress of privatization of large Hungarian enterprises, in the level of involvement of working collectives in this process. According to one of the leaders of Videoton, Peter Lakotosz, his enterprise has begun to establish close links with Kazakhstan. Hungarian foreign policy pays increasing attention to the development of ties with the Asian republics of the former USSR which have gained their independence, said Andras Kelemen, political state secretary of the Hungarian Ministry of Foreign Affairs. He stressed that during this visit a framework for a joint agreement aimed at strengthening Kazakh-Hungarian ties in all fields will be discussed.
