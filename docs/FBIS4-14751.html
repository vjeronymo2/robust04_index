BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Belskiy] [Text] Moscow April 3 TASS -- Parties and movements of the opposition held a meeting and a mourning ceremony in Moscow on Sunday in remembrance of those killed during tragic events in Moscow in October, 1993. The column of thousands of mourners who marched along the Krasnaya Presnya to the Russian White House was led by relatives of those killed six months ago. The mourning ceremony continued for about half an hour. Before the ceremony began, photographs of the victims of the October 1993 tragedy in Moscow were placed on a wall of the Krasnaya Presnya stadium. Addressing all those present, speakers called for reconciliation in society. Participants in the remembrance ceremony adopted an address to the people and authorities, pointing out, in particular, the futility of attempts to resolve political problems in Russia by means of force. Participants demanded that October 4 be proclaimed a day of national mourning and that the parliamentary commission for the investigation into the circumstances of the October 1993 developments resume its work. Upon completion of the meeting, a religious service in remembrance of those killed was held.
