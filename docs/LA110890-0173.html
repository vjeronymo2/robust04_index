November 8, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

BRIEFLY

Black Journalists Won't Sell List to News: Striking New York Daily News workers have pressured a black journalists organization into rescinding its offer to provide management with a list of potential replacement workers. At one point, the National Assn. of Black Journalists had agreed to sell its list for $400. Association President Thomas Morgan announced the decision after striking workers held three news conferences, sent him a telegram and telephoned him.
