BFN [Text] Seoul, March 31 (YONHAP) -- A North Korean man who worked as a propaganda specialist defected to South Korea on Thursday by way of a third country, the Agency for National Security Planning (NSP) reported. The defector was identified as Kim Kwang-uk, age 26, a member of a mobile propaganda team of three revolutionary squads department, South Pyongan Province chapter of the Workers' (Communist) Party. "Kim arrived at Kimpo Airport through a third country and asked for asylum in South Korea," an NSP official said. He said that a preliminary questioning showed that Kim graduated from Pyongsong Engineering College and escaped to China toward the end of last year while working as a mobile propaganda team member.
