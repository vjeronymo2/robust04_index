February 25, 1989, Saturday, San Diego County Edition

BISHOP'S WINS 1-A SOCCER WITH 1-0 OVERTIME VICTORY

Josh Parks, a sophomore midfielder, scored from six yards with 5 minutes left in overtime to give The Bishop's School (15-4-3) a 1-0 victory over the Army-Navy Academy (11-4-3) Friday in the San Diego Section 1-A boys' soccer championship match at San Diego High School.

The schools had split two matches during the regular season and were scoreless after 85 minutes of play Friday until Phillip Button passed to Charlie Marshall, whose shot from 13 feet was deflected by the Army-Navy goalie.

Button took the deflection and, with his back to the goal, passed left to Parks, who scored the winning goal.

In other boys' games:

Division 2-A quarterfinals

Valhalla 3, Mission Bay 0 -- Derek Berry scored two goals as top-seeded Valhalla (23-0-0) scored all its goals in the second half. Valhalla recorded its 17th shutout. Mission Bay finished 12-5-5.

University City 2, Ramona 1 -- Mike O'Shea and Kevin Fay each scored for University City (16-3-3). Ramona finished 19-3-2.

San Marcos 3, Crawford 0 -- Joel Neri scored two goals for San Marcos (15-4-3). Victor Chavez added the third goal. Crawford finished 17-7-1.

San Diego 3, Carlsbad 0 -- Manny Rodriquez, Jose Pulido and Gaing Lai each scored for San Diego (17-1-2). Carlsbad finished 14-7-2.
