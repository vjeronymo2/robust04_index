Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Buenos Aires, 17 Mar (NA) -- President Carlos Menem and his Bolivian counterpart, Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada, today analyzed, among other subjects, the probable granting of Argentine credits for environmental sanitary projects on the common border area between the two countries for the purpose of preventing the spread of cholera, the situation of illegal Bolivian immigrants in Argentina, the struggle against drug trafficking, and the projections of Mercosur [Common Market of the South]. Sanchez de Lozada today arrived in Argentina on an official 48-hour visit with the objective of signing an agreement on plant and animal sanitary controls and an agreement on investment promotion and guarantees. President Menem and Foreign Minister Guido Di Tella received the Bolivian president at 1020 today in Metropolitan Airport's military section. After the welcoming protocol, City Council President Juan Trilla, from the Radical Civic Union, gave the Bolivian chief of state the keys to the city. During a brief news conference after the ceremony, Sanchez de Lozada said one of the subjects he would discuss with Menem is the situation of some of his fellow countrymen living illegally in Argentina. He added: "The control of drug trafficking is very important, and with the aid of Argentina and the international community we will be able to stop this problem." [passage omitted] Regarding drug trafficking control, Sanchez de Lozada said: "It is a very important subject, and we will speak about the Bolivian criteria to ask Argentina and the international community for help to put an end to this phenomenon, which is something we cannot stop." He added that the future of Bolivia "depends a lot on what is going on in Argentina, on the possibility of increasing exchange, and on a rapprochement" between the two countries.
