Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Article by Jorge Freitas in Rio de Janeiro] [Text] The Civil Aviation Department (DAC) on 3 March reported that Major Brigadier Renato Claudio Pereira, president of the Committee for Studies Related to International Air Navigation (CERNAI), on 1 March signed a bilateral air transportation agreement with Macau, the Chinese territory that will be administered by Portugal until 1999. Signing the agreement for Macau was Jorge Ferreira Guimaraes, president of the Civil Aviation Authority. The agreement now will be reviewed by the Sino-Portuguese Liaison Group. A DAC communique states that the agreement "is characterized by liberal operational provisions enabling the two parties to offer air services between Brazil and Macau to meet the needs for transportation of both passengers and cargo between the two parties, including those of third countries." Macau is located on the southeastern coast of Asia, 64 km west of Hong Kong. The agreement will give Brazilian companies greater access to the Asian market.
