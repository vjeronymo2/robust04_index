March 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

COOK IT LIGHT: A HAM LOAF WITH VEAL -- AND TURKEY

DEAR JEANNE: I have used this Ham Loaf recipe since the 1930s. It is hard to find veal in small markets, so the last time I made it I used turkey. That changed the taste for me very much. I also used liquid egg substitute.

Could you lower the cholesterol and fat, and still have a nice Ham Loaf for me? Thank you.

-- HELEN, Broadlands, Ill.

BAKED HAM LOAF

1 pound ground ham

1 1/2 pounds ground veal

2 eggs, lightly beaten

1 cup cracker crumbs

1 cup milk

1/2 teaspoon salt

1/8 teaspoon black pepper

6 slices pineapple

10 maraschino cherries

3/4 cup brown sugar, packed

1 teaspoon dry mustard

1/4 cup vinegar

Combine ham, veal, eggs, cracker crumbs, milk, salt and pepper.

Generously grease 10 1/2 x 6 1/2-inch loaf pan. Place 6 slices pineapple with maraschino cherry in center and between each slice in bottom of pan. Combine brown sugar, mustard and vinegar and spread 1/2 over fruit.

Pack meat mixture in pan over fruit and sauce. Spread remaining brown sugar mixture over top of loaf. Pack down well. Bake at 400 degrees 1 1/2 to 2 hours. Turn out on platter. Makes 6 servings.

DEAR HELEN: This is truly a delicious meat loaf. Unfortunately, there isn't much I can do to it that is going to make it significantly healthier than the variations you may have already tried.

Veal is naturally higher in cholesterol than almost any other meat. Since the turkey substitution didn't taste right to you, I have left the veal alone. Just be sure you use the leanest meats that you can get. You might try using turkey and veal in combination (such as 1 pound veal and 1/2 pound turkey). Turkey has about half the cholesterol of veal, and only about two-thirds of the calories.

I have used liquid egg substitute and non-fat milk in my revision, and I also specified pineapple packed in juice to be sure you aren't using the kind packed in heavy syrup, which is practically pure sugar. I don't recommend using maraschino cherries either, so I made them optional. I prefer to avoid the chemical additives they contain, especially the red dye.

By eliminating the salt and substituting whole-wheat bread crumbs for the cracker crumbs, I was able to reduce the sodium in your recipe. I like the flavor the bread crumbs give the loaf as well as the added fiber.

The sauce is another source of calories, so I reduced the amount of brown sugar and specified "dark brown sugar" to get maximum flavor for the calories consumed. I also based my nutritional analysis on both recipes making eight servings since I determined that the whole loaf yields about sixteen 1/2-inch slices.

You have been enjoying this Ham Loaf for more than 50 years, and I hope my revised version will enable you to enjoy it for another 50!

REVISED BAKED HAM LOAF

1 pound extra-lean baked ham, ground or very finely chopped

1 1/2 pounds extra-lean ground veal

1/2 cup liquid egg substitute

1 cup nonfat milk

1 cup soft whole-wheat bread crumbs

1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

1/2 cup dark brown sugar, packed

1 teaspoon dry mustard

1/4 cup white vinegar

6 slices canned pineapple, packed in juice

10 maraschino cherries, optional

Combine ham, veal, egg substitute, milk, bread crumbs and pepper. Combine brown sugar, dry mustard and vinegar in separate bowl.

Spray 10 1/2 x 6 1/2-inch loaf pan with non-stick vegetable coating. Place 6 slices pineapple on bottom of pan. Place cherry in center of -- and in between -- pineapple slices. Pour 1/2 sauce mixture over fruit.

Pack meat mixture in pan over fruit and sauce. Pour remaining sauce over top of loaf. Bake at 400 degrees 1 1/2 to 2 hours. Turn out onto platter. Makes 8 servings, or 16 (1/2-inch) slices.
