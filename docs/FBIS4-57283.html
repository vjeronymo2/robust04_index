BFN [From the "RPC Television News" newscast] [Text] Dr. Ernesto Perez Balladares will retain the Institutional Protection Service [SPI] and will promote its professionalization. As the president-elect sees it, it is important to insist on a coalition government. This is what he hinted when he learned of the Arnulfist's decision to reject two ministries he offered to them. [Begin Perez Balladares recording] My offer was not an intention to create political slots, but an intention to create agreements, affinity, and to have the best men and women in the country for national projects. [end recording] Perez Balladares said that the Legislative Assembly must approve the designation of the new comptroller general from a list of nominees he will submit when he takes over the government in September. The president-elect was also asked about the future of the SPI. [Begin Perez Balladares recording] I think that all presidential offices in all countries must have a protection service for people and institutions. I think this service must be professional, apolitical, and capable of fulfilling its functions. If I find these characteristics in the SPI, it will continue functioning exactly as it functions now. [end recording] Perez Balladares said that he has a special office to answer the grievances of public employees who have been unjustly dismissed. He flatly rejected the possibility of an Arnulfist becoming president of the Legislative Assembly in September. [Begin Perez Balladares recording] I believe that the legislative branch will have to make its own decisions, and I highly respect that. However, seeing how the Assembly is taking shape, I think that it will be presided over by the party that has obtained the most votes, and that is us. [end recording]
