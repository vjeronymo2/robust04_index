930430 FT 30 APR 93 / London Stock Exchange: Tiphook plunges Container leasing group Tiphook was the market's biggest casualty yesterday, the shares plunging more than 36 per cent at one point after a shock profits warning. At worst the shares touched 186p. They later stabilised to end a net 63 down at 230p after heavy turnover of 7.4m. Tiphook said its second-half profits would not match those of the first half but the final dividend would be not less than 14.4p, indicating that the dividend total would be 19.3p, against last year's 17.3p. Analysts had previously been expecting Tiphook to achieve profits of some Pounds 75m. After the warning estimates were chopped to around Pounds 58m.
