BFN [Text] Boris Yeltsin said it was premature to raise the question of whether he intends to be a candidate in the next presidential elections. In an interview for an INTERFAX correspondent, the president said that in the course of the two remaining years before the elections, he saw his main task as being the establishment of stable mutual relations between the executive, legislative, and judicial authorities. He said that new relations were already beginnig to take shape with the Federation Council and the State Duma. The president said the second task was to strengthen Russia's unity. Acknowledging that the process of the establishment of federal relations was at times painful and tense, Boris Yeltsin replied that this process was, as a whole, under control. The president regards the economy as the third priority area. He expressed the hope that in the coming two years, the numerous ills of our economy would start to vanish and that we would all feel better for that.
