Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] The Export-Import [Ex-Im] Bank of Korea has agreed with Romania's state-run telecommunications corporation to extend a $50 million loan from the Economic Development Cooperation Fund (EDCF), a spokesman said. The agreement was signed in Bucharest last Friday (local time) between Ex-Im Bank president Kim Yong-pin and Dumitru Anghelescu, president of the communications corporation Rom Telecom R.A. The EDCF loan carries an annual interest of 4.2 percent and has to be paid back over the next 20 years with five years' deferment of repayments of the principal. The state-run telecommunications company will use the loan for modernizing communications equipment in the Romanian state of Prahova. Signed prior to the state visit of Romanian President Ion Iliescu today, the agreement is expected to boost bilateral economic cooperation and to help Korean companies export machinery and equipment to East European countries, the spokesman said.
