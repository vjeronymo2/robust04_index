Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riyadh, Jan 1, (SPA) -- Three royal decrees have been issued endorsing the national state budget for the fiscal year 1414/1415 H. The first decree No. M/14, dated 19/7/1414 H issued in accordance with articles 72, 73, 76 and 78 of the government system and articles 25, 26 and 27 of the Council of Ministers' system, and according to the Council of Ministers Resolution No. 85, dated 19/7/1414 H, estimated the state's revenues and general expenditures for the fiscal year 1414/1415 H at SR [Saudi riyals] 160 billion. The royal decree also included the regulations for the implementation of the budget. The second decree No. M/15, dated 19/7/1414 H issued in accordance with the Council of Ministers resolutions No. 86 dated 19/7/1414 H estimated the municipalities and water departments' budget for the fiscal year 1414/1415 H at SR 5,269,800,000. The Third Decree No. 16/M dated 19/7/1414 H issued in accordance with the Council of Ministers Resolution No. 87 dated 19/7/1414 H endorsed the budgets of the general organisations whose budgets are attached to the state's general budget. The 22 general organisations include the general ports authority, the Saudi Arabian Airlines Company (Saudia), the general electricity organisation, the general organisation for flour mills and grain silos, the General Saline Water Conversion Corporation, the general railways organisation, Petromin, the Royal Commission for Jubail and Yanbu, Saudi Arabian Standards Organisation, King Sa'ud University, King 'Abd-al-'Aziz University, King Fahd University for Petroleum and Minerals, Imam Mohammed Bin Saud Islamic University, the Islamic University of Medina, King Faysal University, Umm al-Qura University, the general organisation for technical education and training, King 'Abd-al-'Aziz City for Science and Technology, the Institute of Public Administration, the Saudi Red Crescent Society, the general organisation for military industries, and the pensioners fund. The total budget estimated for these organisations for the fiscal year 1414/1415 H amounts to SR 28,387,493,000. Furthermore, the government specialized credit institutions shall extend easy-term, interest-free loans to citizens working in the fields of agriculture, industry and real estate. Total loans to be extended by these institutions are estimated to reach SR 7,870,000,000 during the new fiscal year.
