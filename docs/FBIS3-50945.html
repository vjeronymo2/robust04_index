Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Text] Cuba's cooperation with Bolivia has solved the serious health problems of about 5,000 [Bolivian] children. Furthermore, "many Bolivians are walking about with Cuban kidneys," Cuba's ambassador, Angel Gustavo Bruguez Perez, said yesterday. At the end of a formal farewell visit to Bolivian President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada, the Cuban diplomat completed his mission with a report on his 10 years in La Paz. He spoke of the health care programs his country's government has supported in Bolivia, including the establishment of three intensive care facilities for children in the cities of La Paz, Santa Cruz, and Sucre, where the lives of 5,000 children have been saved. He said that thousands of Bolivian citizens are now walking about with Cuban kidneys. These people went to Cuba for specialized medical treatment. Bruguez was decorated with the "Condor of the Andes" medal with the Great Cross, in recognition of his work to benefit Bolivia during the 10 years he headed his country's diplomatic legation. Certain that he will return to Bolivia, the diplomat said he will again stroll along Avenida Buenos Aires or the Rodriguez Market. He said he has established a home in La Paz and that his children are studying in Bolivia. He also spoke of Cuba's cooperation in sports, especially for Bolivian participation in the Bolivar Olympic Games. Minister of Foreign Relations Antonio Aranibar said: "Despite the enormous difficulties facing the Cuban people, their government has provided significant cooperation in the fields of education, health, and sports."
