940912 FT 12 SEP 94 / Chipper outlook for spud supremos The humble spud is making a comeback against the combined onslaught of rice and pasta, a conference in Harrogate, North Yorkshire, will be told today. Delegates to the World Potato Congress, which opened last night with a gala reception and a parade of flags of the 50 countries attending the two-day event, will be told how advertising and ideas for new recipes have reversed a drift away from the potato. Consumption in the UK fell from 105kg per head a year in the early 1980s to 99kg in 1991 as consumers were attracted to 'glamorous' pasta or rice dishes. But an advertising campaign by the Potato Marketing Board, in its third year of five, has helped push consumption up to 107kg, said Mr Bob Griffiths, the board's marketing services manager. The increase has been in processed potatoes but Mr Griffiths added: 'Our efforts have stopped the slide in consumption of fresh potatoes.' The campaign has focused on the nutritional value of potatoes and their versatility. Consumer surveys suggest potatoes are difficult to prepare compared with pasta, sales of which are rising 10 per cent a year. So far the potato board has spent Pounds 5.5m on the campaign although the industry might be seen as overreacting. Rice consumption, although 70 per cent higher than a decade ago, is just 3kg per head in dry weight and pasta consumption is just over 2kg. The retail potato market is worth Pounds 3.5bn compared with an estimated Pounds 185m for pasta. Potato prices have risen 50 per cent this year however, due to lower plantings and a poor harvest. But Mr Griffiths insisted there is no evidence of consumer resistance. 'People are prepared to pay more providing they get a good-quality product.'
