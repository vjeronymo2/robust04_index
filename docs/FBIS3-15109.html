Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Andrey Palaria] [Text] Moscow, March 1 TASS -- Former parliament speaker Ruslan Khasbulatov released on Saturday from Lefortovo jail in accordance with the Duma amnesty, is leaving tonight for the Chechen capital Groznyy. According to his spokesman Konstantin Zlobin, in Chechnya Khasbulatov will see his mother who resides there. After some recreation, the former speaker is going to resume working on a book on relations between legislative and executive powers which he started to write in prison. However, Zlobin denied reports that Khasbulatov intended to settle in Chechnya permanently. Khasbulatov, ethnic Chechen, had been elected to the previous Russian legislature from Russia's southern republic of Chechnya. He was deprived of the Chechen citizenship when refused to leave parliament after the independence-seeking republic called back all its deputies. The citizenship was returned to Khasbulatov after he was jailed as a ringleader of the October hard-line revolt in Moscow what intstantly made him "a national hero" in Chechnya.
