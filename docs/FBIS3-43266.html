Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] Jonas Savimbi's National Union for the Total Independence of Angola [UNITA] today plans to mount a large-scale attack on the town of (Libongo) in Bengo Province. This was disclosed by Colonel Manuel Cartucho, spokesman for the Northern Operational Front. Col. Cartucho says over the next few days UNITA troops also plan to occupy Caxito with the backing of foreign forces. [Begin Cartucho recording] Between 14 and 17 December our forces were involved in heavy clashes. Enemy troops were repulsed. Right now, there are large concentrations of UNITA troops -- estimated at four battalions -- including foreign elements. They are poised to attack the (Libongo) region between 31 December and 1 January. If they succeed in taking (Libongo), UNITA forces will have created the conditions for moving into Caxito. The morale of our forces is high. They are ready to repel the enemy. [end recording]
