Language: Georgian Article Type:BFN [Text] Peter Hancock, ambassador extraordinary and plenipotentiary of Canada to Turkey and Georgia, today handed his credentials to head of the Georgian state Eduard Shevardnadze. Prospects of future cooperation between Georgia and Canada were discussed at the meeting. The necessity for creating a legal basis for relations between the two countries was mentioned. The head of state familiarized the ambassador with the results of Russian President Boris Yeltsin's visit. He noted that the day of the meeting was a historic one in the relations of the two neighborly countries and that it gave momentum to establishing new and kind relations between Russia and Georgia. Eduard Shevardnadze thanked the Canadian Government for its support at international organizations for granting Georgia the status of a developing country.
