November 29, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

GRAMMAR

Jerry Colburn argues that low achievement in writing is the result of "junking the grammar lesson" and a failure to correct errors ("The Ostrich Method of English Grammar," California Commentary, Nov. 20). Research over several decades, however, has shown that direct teaching of grammar and error correction has little or no effect on student writing. The research does show that students who read more read better, write better, have larger vocabularies, and even do better on grammar tests. Our students need good literature programs and improved school libraries, not more sentence diagramming.

STEPHEN KRASHEN

Professor of Linguistics

USC
