Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] The removal of party representatives whose parties have fewer than six deputies from the activities of municipal war presidencies is an undemocratic act and constitutes the first tangible proof of the totalitarianization of the political space in Bosnia-Herzegovina, said Rasim Kadic, president of the Liberal Party of Bosnia-Herzegovina, at today's news conference. The decision was passed by the country's Presidency on the proposal of the Bosnia-Herzegovina Government and, in my opinion, it is politically conflicting. Rasim Kadic warned that this means that parliamentary parties that had a smaller number of voters will be thrown out of war presidencies, while representatives that had not even participated in the elections would remain in these executive bodies. This is a paradox. Moreover, a part of the text of the draft constitution for the federal organization of Bosnia-Herzegovina is also considered undemocratic. The part in question foresees a limit of 5 percent for entering the parliament. According to Kadic, this is the introduction of a single-party system. He also said that in the majority of European countries this limit was set at 2 percent, and that the Liberal Party would demand at the Bosnia-Herzegovina Assembly that this provision is changed, because it limits the development of democracy in these areas.
