BFN [Unattributed report: "Economy Shrunk by 0.3 Percent Last Year"] [Text] The recession in 1993 was comparatively moderate in Austria: The gross domestic product [GDP] only shrunk by 0.3 percent. The main reasons for last year's performance of the Austrian economy, which was better than expected, are to be found in industrial production and exports, which decreased less than predicted. Presently, the most important impetus for a new upswing is currently coming from both industry and exports.
