Language: French Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] [Passage omitted] To kill two birds with one stone, the U.S. ambassador went to the lower chamber to meet with the new president of the Chamber of Deputies. However, they did not reach the common position that was the case at the upper chamber, because William L. Swing and Frantz Robert Monde expressed differing points of view on the Haitian crisis and the measures adopted by the international community. President Monde, once more, called on the Americans for more humaneness toward Haiti and solicited the lifting of the embargo: [Begin recording] [Monde] It is the richest country in the world, the most prosperous, the most powerful that is imposing economic sanctions on the weakest, poorest one on earth. We asked him to ask the international community to view the crisis on a more humane angle -- the unfairness, the human aspect of the embargo -- the solution to the crisis in order to avoid a social explosion, according to critics of the policy of the international community which is adhering to the Governors Island accord. [Reporter] What was the ambassador's answer? [Monde] The same: It is not our fault; it is the military's fault. However, they did not realize that their approach to the crisis was greatly biased, which explains the fact that they have personalized the crisis as between two men, making a solution very difficult. [end recording] This visit by the U.S. ambassador comes at a time when the Chamber of Deputies has basically resumed normal operations, while the Senate of the Republic is heading for a new rupture that is becoming more and more imminent. [end recording]
