BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Gadzhi Shamov] [Text] Makhachkala May 10 TASS -- The Kizlyar board of the Terek Cossack community, affiliated to the Russian Union of Cossacks, has objected to the draft of the new constitution of the Republic of Dagestan (southern Russia), which was put to nation-wide discussion by the republic's Supreme Soviet. A regular meeting of the board has ruled its adoption impermissible. This is due to the fact that the draft does not reckon with the interests of smaller nationalities and envisages the possibility of Dagestan's future secession from Russia, which contradicts the Constitution of the Russian Federation and is fraught with tragic consequences. If the new constitution of the Republic of Dagestan is adopted in its present form, the Cossack board will rule it invalid on the left-hand bank of the Terek river and will demand the annulment of the decree of the RSFSR [Russian Soviet Federated Socialist Reoublic] Supreme Soviet of January 9, 1957, placing the Kizlyar, Tarum, and Nogaysk districts under Dagestan's jurisdiction. The Cossacks have informed the president, the parliament, and the government of Russia about their decision, as well as the leadership of the Dagestan republic and the local organs of power.
