940708 FT 08 JUL 94 / International Capital Markets: Vietnam to raise Dollars 3bn Vietnam plans to tap international capital markets for the first time to raise up to Dollars 3bn by the end of the century, a senior finance ministry official said, Reuter reports from Hanoi. Vietnam is seeking to raise a total of Dollars 50bn by the end of the century to upgrade infrastructure, hoping to double gross domestic product and achieve an annual growth rate of 8 per cent to 10 per cent. At least half would be raised domestically and half would come from overseas sources such as direct investment, foreign aid and by tapping capital markets, said Ms Le Thi Bang Tam, director of the ministry of finance's state Treasury department. The Treasury has been charged with planning the issue and marketing government bonds abroad.
