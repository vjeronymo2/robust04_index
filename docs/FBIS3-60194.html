Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] The headquarters for a campaign against narcotics, in a session today chaired by Mr. Hashemi-Rafsanjani, approved in general terms a draft memorandum of understanding for regional cooperation by Iran, Pakistan and Afghanistan. It provides for a more determined campaign against narcotics under UN supervision. At the session, the president expressed satisfaction and appreciation over efforts undertaken by those engaged in anti-narcotic operations in the country. He stressed the need for political and infrastructural measures including the closure of the eastern borders of the country, identification and destruction of internal narcotics production and distribution networks, and creation of employment and rehabilitation camps for addicts. The commander of the law enforcement forces and the secretary to the headquarters for anti-narcotic operations announced at the session that during the current year [beginning 21 March 1993] 66 tonnes of various types of narcotics have been uncovered and more than 53,000 production and distribution agents and narcotic addicts have been arrested. According to a report by our correspondent, during the session a proposal by the governor general of Sistan va Baluchestan Province concerning a plan for closing the eastern borders was raised and the required funds for the project approved.
