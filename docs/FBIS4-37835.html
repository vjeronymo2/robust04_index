BFN [Text] In the Kashmir Valley, the two Britishers -- Kim Housego and Mr. David Mackie -- kidnapped by the militants have been released after 17 days of captivity. The two were released unconditionally today in the jungles of Pahalgam. Later, they were brought to Srinagar where they were handed over to the first secretary of the British High Commission in India. The two Britishers were kidnapped on the 7th of this month from Kharo in Pahalgam by a militant outfit, Harkatul Ansar. Talking to AIR [All India Radio] correspondent in Srinagar after their release, Kim Housego and David Mackie said they did not know the exact location where they were kept. They were taken from place to place during their 17-day ordeal. The British foreign secretary, Mr. Douglas Hurd, has welcomed the release of the two Britons. In a statement in London, he thanked the Government of India and others who helped their release.
