Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [From the "24 Hours" newscast] [Text] Another incident has exacerbated the already tense situation on the Colombian-Venezuelan border. The Guerrilla Coordinating Board downed a civilian helicopter from Venezuela that was transporting Venezuelan soldiers. The subversives also dynamited a vehicle transporting children in Aguazul, Casanare. [Begin reporter Alvaro Ayala recording] The public order situation on the Colombian-Venezuelan border worsened today when ELN [Army of National Liberation] guerrillas opened fire on a civilian helicopter transporting Venezuelan soldiers. The incident occurred near the mouth of the Arauca River near Saravena. The two civilian pilots and four soldiers in the helicopter were seriously injured. They were subsequently rescued by the Venezuelan National Guard. Meanwhile, the Guerrilla Coordinating Board dynamited a jeep of tourists coming from Boyaca as they were passing through Aguazul Municipality, Casanare. Two adults were killed and eight people, including seven children, were seriously injured. [end recording] Regarding the case of the helicopter downed on the Colombian- Venezuelan border, we have just received another report from Arauca. According to that report, the Venezuelan helicopter landed in Colombian territory to pick up some peasants, and that is what led to the attack. The authorities are expected to issue a report on the events.
