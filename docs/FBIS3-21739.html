Language: Spanish Article Type:CSO [Article by Ignacio Gutierrez A.] [Text] Yesterday, in the town of Tijuana, Baja California, Federal Judicial Police [PJF] personnel captured three presumed drug dealers with 43 kg and 980 grams of cocaine base in their possession. The drugs were worth over 120 million new pesos and were to be shipped to the United States. Martin Torres Flores, Fausto Gonzalez Solis, and Jose Luis Rivas Rosas were arrested by federal agents on the Guadalajara-Tepic highway, eight km from the municipality of Magdalena. They were carrying the drugs in compartments concealed under the roof of a van with state of California plates. According to investigations conducted by the agent of the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office, a vehicle with certain characteristics was due to travel along that highway carrying the alkaloid shipment. Hence, at a checkpoint in the aforementioned locality, the PJF agents intercepted these individuals riding in a red Dodge Ram van with a detachable roof and license plates 30X100. When the vehicle was searched, the drugs were found, and those subjects, together with the cocaine, were taken into custody and placed at the disposal of the agent at the Federal Public Prosecutor's Office. In their preliminary statements, they claimed that the alkaloid had come from Tuxpan, Jalisco, and was bound for Tijuana, Baja California, to be smuggled into the United States later. It should be noted that, thus far in this administration, a total of 225 tons of cocaine has been seized in antidrug operations throughout the country. Moreover, 165 Federal Judicial Police agents have lost their lives in direct confrontations associated with the relentless battle against the drug traffic.
