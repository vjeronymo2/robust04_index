November 3, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

CHAMBERS SCORES 38 TO LEAD SUNS TO EASY VICTORY OVER JAZZ IN TOKYO

The Phoenix Suns, led by Tom Chambers, started the NBA season half a world away from home Friday night with a 119-96 victory over the Utah Jazz in the first regular-season NBA game held outside North America.

A sellout crowd of 10,111 at Tokyo Metropolitan Gymnasium, paying as much as $175 a seat, was predominantly young Japanese.

The game was close during the first half before the Suns pulled away with a 19-2 run late in the third quarter, giving Phoenix an 85-70 lead that was never threatened.

Chambers scored eight of his 38 points during the run. He scored 27 points in the second half when the Suns outscored the Jazz, 61-39, after leading only by one at halftime.

Karl Malone led the Jazz with 33 points, 26 in the first half.
