BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Nikolay Geronin and Anatoliy Krasnov] [Text] Moscow April 14 TASS -- Talks between Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and his South Korean counterpart Han Sung-chu began in Moscow today. The first item of the agenda is the preparation of a visit of President of the Republic of Korea Kim Yong-sam to Russia, which is scheduled for early June, Kozyrev said before the beginning of the talks. Speaking about the nuclear problem at the Korean Peninsula, Kozyrev pointed out that "unfortunately, bilateral efforts did not bring the desired results, and, I think, we should expand the range of consultations and consider some other approaches in order to get the problem out of a deadlock by joint efforts." Han Sung-chu said in his turn that it was the fifth meeting of the Russian and South Korean foreign ministers, which is an indication of good relations between the two countries. Han Sung-chu will tell Kozyrev about Kim Yong-sam's meetings with Japanese Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa and China's leader Jiang Zemin during his recent visits to those countries, Kim Sok-kyu, South Korea's ambassador to Russia, told ITAR-TASS. The Republic of Korea would like to establish close contacts with Russia on the nuclear problem of North Korea, the South Korean ambassador said. According to Kim Sok-kyu, the ministers will discuss Moscow's proposals on holding an international conference on ensuring the security and the nuclear-free status of the Korean Peninsula. The South Korean Government is highly appreciating the proposal and regarding it as a friendly step of Russia, aimed at settling problems of the Korean Peninsula. How, he continued, the issue is being examined by the U.N. Security Council, and it is worthwhile to wait and see how those problems will be settled within the U.N. framework.
