BFN [Text] Lieutenant General 'Umar Hasan Ahmad al-Bashir, the president of the Republic, has again denied the U.S. allegations about Sudan's violation of human rights and support for terrorism. The president made this denial yesterday in an interview with Russian television, in his office at the general command of the people's armed forces. During the interview Lt. Gen. al-Bashir also talked about political, economic and social developments in Sudan, as well as Russian-Sudanese relations. Russian television also conducted an interview with the minister of state at the Foreign Ministry, Archbishop Gabriel Rodge, during which he explained that his participation in government is for the sake of the country's unity and advancement. He said that there is no religious problem in Sudan as portrayed by the Western mass media. He explained that the Government of Sudan is serious in its efforts to solve the southern problem. He said that Sudan enjoys a normal life in which peaceful coexistence prevails. Regarding conditions in the south of the country, the minister of state at the Foreign Ministry noted that the situation is very stable, which suggests that the achievement of a comprehensive peace in a unified Sudan is getting closer.
