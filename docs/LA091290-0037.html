September 12, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: STAGE

Hip Hop: The ever-prolific director Reza Abdoh is replacing himself on Los Angeles Theatre Center's upcoming fall/winter roster of plays.

Abdoh's new "The Hip-Hop Waltz of Eurydice," which blends the Orpheus/Eurydice myth with science fiction and Japanese ritual, will open Dec. 13, with previews beginning Nov. 28. That had been the time slot planned for his "Bogeyman," co-written with Mira-Lanicq Oglesby. But the play has been postponed until the 1991-92 fall/winter season. BETH KLEID
