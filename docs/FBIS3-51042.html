Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Text] Posadas -- Official sources in Posadas have announced that a unique exchange will be established between Misiones Province and Cuba. The government headed by Fidel Castro will provide free care for patients who go to Cuba from Misiones Province to receive treatment for eye, spine, skin, respiratory, or other diseases, while the island will receive log cabin type housing, corrugated cardboard, and liner or Kraft paper manufactured by the Papel Misionero factory, owned by the Misiones Government in partnership with the Federal Government. To this end a delegation of government officials, headed by Governor Federico Ramon Puerta, will go to Havana in late January. During his visit the Misiones governor will sign an agreement with Castro.

With Other Provinces
The Foreign Ministry reported that this type of agreement is called a "counterbalanced trade" agreement, and that one agreement of this type has already been signed with Rio Negro Province. In addition, similar negotiations are currently under way between the Cuban Embassy in our country and Entre Rios Province. Argentine diplomatic sources reported that the governments from other provinces are also interested in these agreements. The Caribbean nation has recently signed a training agreement with Santa Fe Province to provide advanced training for Cuban experts on engine mechanics.
