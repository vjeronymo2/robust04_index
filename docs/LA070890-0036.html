July 8, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

POP EYE

ON THAT SUBJECT: Raffi and Midnight Oil aren't the only pop artists who don't want their new CDs released with a long box. According to manager Kenny Laguna, rock songstress Joan Jett manager has made a formal request to CBS Records asking that the label issue her next album without a CD long box.

"Joan isn't someone who gets involved in a lot of causes, but she feels strongly about this -- she doesn't want to be a party to destroying millions of trees for a box that everyone throws into the garbage," explained Laguna, who said Jett's album is tentatively scheduled for a Christmas release. "So we sent a letter to the top execs at CBS about a month ago, along with some recent Earth Day articles. Joan made it clear she's serious. She said if they felt they couldn't market her CD without a long box, then she'd rather them not put a CD out at all, even though that could cost her a lot of record sales." Laguna said the CBS brass is currently considering her request.
