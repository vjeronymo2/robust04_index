BFN [Text] Pyongyang, May 3 (KCNA) -- Foreign papers carried the famous work of the great leader President Kim Il-song "Answers to the Questions Raised by the U.S. `WASHINGTON TIMES' News Coverage Group." The Tanzanian paper UHURU carried the gist of President Kim Il-song's work with his portrait. The Norwegian paper AFTENPOSTEN under the title "North Korea Has No Nuclear Weapons" said: In his work President Kim Il-song said Korea had declared more than once that it had no need and no ability to make nuclear weapons. He said the introduction of Patriot missiles into South Korea by the United States was rendering the already strained situation on the Korean peninsula all the more acute. Referring to Japan's nuclear armament, he said this greatly threatened peace and security in the Asian region including the Korean peninsula. The work was carried by the German papers FRANKFURTER ALLGEMEINE ZEITUNG and DIE WELT, the Zimbabwean paper HERALD, the Malaysian paper SIN CHEW JIT POH, the Czech paper RUDE PRAVO and the Slovak paper PRAVDA. Foreign media reported the famous work of President Kim Il-song "Answers to the Questions Raised by the U.S. CNN News Coverage Group". The Egyptian paper AL GOUMHOURIA carried the same work with his portrait. The Vietnamese paper NHAN DAN reported the work under the title "President Kim Il-song Refutes Reports About `Development of Nuclear Weapons' by DPRK." The paper quoted the great president as saying there is no nuclear weapons in the DPRK and it has no intention or capacity to manufacture them. The work was reported by the Czech papers HALO NOVINY, RUDE PRAVO, LIDOVE NOVINY, MLADA FRONTA DNES, PRACE, and SVOBODNE SLOVO, the German paper NEUES DEUTSCHLAND, the Slovak paper PRAVDA, the Finnish paper HELSINKI SANOMAT, the Pakistani paper PAKISTAN TIMES, the Romanian papers CURIERUL NATIONAL, ROMANIA LIBERA, EVENIMENTUL ZILEI, VOCEA ROMANIEI, ROMPRES News Agency and radio and TV.
