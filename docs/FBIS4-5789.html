BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 14 KYODO -- Nissan Motor Co. said Thursday [14 April] that one of its U.S. subsidiaries will start production of Nissan electric forklifts in the second half of 1995. Nissan Forklift Corp., North America (NFC), based in Marengo, Illinois, made the decision in order to further advance localization as well as to help shorten delivery time and cut production costs, the second-largest Japanese automaker said. NFC now produces internal-combustion-powered forklifts mainly for the North American market. It also makes electric forklifts under the Barrett brand name. NFC is owned 2 percent by Nissan, 88 percent by a Nissan subsidiary, Nissan Industrial Equipment Co., and the remaining 10 percent by Marubeni Corp., a giant Japanese trading house.
