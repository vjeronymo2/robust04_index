BFN [Text] A team of Nigerian military personnel has arrived in Freetown to train and reorganize the Sierra Leonean Armed Forces. The 32-member team is led by Brigadier General (Seidu Zubiru). Addressing newsmen in Lagos, the director of defense information, Brigadier General Fred Chijuka, said the team will be in Sierra Leone for an initial period of two years. He said the exercise was at the instance of Sierra Leone Government, adding that more African countries were also requesting similar military assistance from Nigeria. Meanwhile, about 200 Nigerians residing in Sierra Leone were flown into Lagos last night, in a Nigerian Air Force plane, following renewed fighting by government and rebel forces in that country. They are mainly children and women. A radio Nigeria correspondent reports that some of them came with little or no luggage, saying that they had been dispossessed of their belongings.
