Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Text] Slovene Foreign Minister Lojze Peterle concluded his visit to Bucharest tonight. The program of his visit included a meeting with Romanian President Ion Iliescu at Cotroceni Palace. According to statements made by the guest to our correspondent, during the meeting aspects and common aspects of the transition process in both countries and issues referring to security and bilateral relations were discussed. Mr. Lojze Peterle was also received by Prime Minister Nicolae Vacaroiu. The meeting was also attended by Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu. During the meeting the sides assessed the stage of relations between the two countries in the regional, European, and world context. The sides also briefed each other about the development of the process of transition to market economy. The dialogue provided a good opportunity for the sides to emphasize the possibility of furthering and expanding relations between Romania and Slovenia on the basis of the summit meetings that are going to be held and of the bilateral accords that will be signed in the near future. Mr. Lojze Peterle also held talks with Romanian Foreign Minister Teodor Melescanu. An accord between the government of the two countries on cooperation in the areas of education, culture, science, and sports as well as the protocol on cooperation between the two ministries were signed. The guest was also received by Adrian Nastase, chairman of the Chamber of Deputies of the Romanian parliament.
