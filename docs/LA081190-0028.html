August 11, 1990, Saturday, Home Edition

SIGNS OF THE TIMES LOOK BAD FOR FANS

Last night, my 7-year-old son came home from the baseball game bursting with pride because he witnessed Jose Canseco's back-to-back home runs. However, this morning I had to explain to him that we could not go to the autograph signing of Jose and other sports figures because of the exorbitant sums of money being charged.

No, the money does not go to charity or to help any worthy organizations. It goes into the pockets of Jose Canseco, Rickey Henderson and Steve Garvey.

It used to be that an autograph was an act of kindness, a social responsibility toward kids who worshiped them. Now it is a commodity and might as well be listed with pork bellies on the exchange.

SARA HAZLEWOOD

Orange
