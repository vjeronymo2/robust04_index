BFN [From the "National Newscast"] [Text] Nicaragua will request $1.6 billion in loans from the Paris Club for the next three years. Before departing for Europe, Finance Minister Emilio Pereira and Central Bank President Evenor Taboada reported that the World Bank and IMF Boards of Directors will approve soft loans for Nicaragua next week. In Paris, a government delegation headed by the Presidency minister will present to the delegates of 25 countries the country's economic strategy for the next three years. [Begin recording] [Taboada] The agenda deals with Nicaragua's cooperation, reform, and modernization, the transformation of its social agenda, and its need for technical, economic, and social assistance. [Pereira] Approximately $310 million of the $550 to $560 million that we expect to borrow in 1994 has virtually been obtained. [end recording] The officials reported that they will request a total of $1.6 billion for the next three years. In addition, they reported that the IMF and the World Bank will approve loans soon. [Begin Taboada recording] The IMF director and the World Bank Board of Directors on 22 and 23 June will study Nicaragua's request, after which we expect to receive the first disbursements. [end recording] One of the subjects to be explained to the Paris Club is the progress attained in the solution of problems related to the confiscation of real estate owned by foreigners. [Begin Pereira recording] We are confident there will be no problems. I am taking a report of all the cases that have been settled and whose solutions meet other countries' demands with me in my portfolio. [end recording] The Presidency minister will fly to Paris tomorrow.
