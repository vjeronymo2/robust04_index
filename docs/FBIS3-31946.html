Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [News Conference by Guatemalan President Ramiro de Leon Carpio with unidentified reporters at the University Auditorium polling station in Guatemala City on 30 January--recorded] [Text] [De Leon] There is calm here and in the country's interior. I have just been informed that everything is normal, and the people are participating. [Reporter] But participation.... [De Leon, interrupting] We do not know that yet because participation.... [Reporter, interrupting] [Words indistinct] if the yes vote wins? [De Leon] This would demonstrate that the Guatemalan people have ratified what they demanded following the 25 May events: the purging of state organizations, Congress, and the Supreme Court, and the restructuring of [word indistinct]. This will be the first.... [Reporter, interrupting] [Words indistinct]. [De Leon] Political stability and calm that will allow us to govern and to face important social and economic problems through a government plan we have drafted. [Reporter] Mr. President, do you have any preliminary report from the interior? [De Leon] Of course. Fortunately everything is going normally, calmly. We do not know how many people are voting, but the truth is that despite all the terrorist problems, obstacles, pressure, and efforts to boycott the event, the referendum is taking place. The fact that the event is taking place strengthens the democratic system, because this is an example to the world. While in other countries domestic problems are resolved antidemocratically, through violence, here in Guatemala we are resolving this national political problem democratically, the most sacred way the people have as the expression of their sovereignty: through votes.
