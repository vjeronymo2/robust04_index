940202 FT 02 FEB 94 / UK Company News: Ex-Lands seeks Pounds 20m to fund property buy Ex-Lands, the property and leisure company, yesterday announced a placing and open offer to raise a net Pounds 19.85m for the acquisition of a substantial property portfolio. The group, whose largest shareholders are the Saatchi brothers, is topurchase Londinium Investments for Pounds 16.5m, providing it with a number of office and industrial properties with annual rental income of Pounds 1.9m. Ex-Landsis issuing 35.28m new shares at 36p, compared with yesterday's closing price of42 1/2 p, and Pounds 7.05m of 7.5 per cent convertible loan stock. The shares are beingoffered to existing shareholders on a 3-for-10 basis and Pounds 3 of loan stock for every 50 ordinary shares. This is the company's second share issue in sixmonths, as management reacts to what it describes as a highly favourable climate for property acquisitions. In July last year, the company launched a Pounds 14.3m open offer and placement to pay for several properties in southern England. Londinium owns 13 industrial/office properties around London, with a gross asset value of Pounds 12.5m. In addition, it has agreed to acquire several properties inthe Great Western Trading Estate, Brentford, for Pounds 4.75m. Ex-Lands will pay Pounds 16.5m for the combined assets, of which Pounds 10.4m will be utilised to pay off Londinium's existing debt. Following the acquisition, Ex-Land's gross rental income will be increased to Pounds 3.6m, based on current rental rates. The company is also involved in a number of golf course projects in continental Europe.
