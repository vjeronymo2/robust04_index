931206 FT 06 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television These days a new business guru with a miracle wheeze seems to turn up every month, promising that our entire world is about to be revolutionised by magnetic string or the power of diagonal thinking or some such malarkey. Today in Crazy Ways For Crazy Days BBC2 shows guru Tom Peters addressing a London seminar and declaring 'I'm not talking skill enhancement, I'm talking everyone in this room, everyone on this island, needing to learn totally new stuff in the next 60 months'. He is American, of course. Still, he certainly seems to have his own business skills sorted out: the BBC is promoting his video, price Pounds 395 plus VAT (7.20). Ken Livingstone claims that when asked to play himself, in tonight's episode of the embarrassingly naff Woman's Guide To Adultery on ITV, he specified 'No nude scenes with Amanda Donohoe'. How politically correct can you get? (9.00). Panorama reports on new government figures which suggest that racial attacks in Britain are more common than hitherto assumed (9.30 BBC1).
