BFN [Article by Oleg Cherkovets: "To the Front Outside the Pentagon...."] [Text] INTERFAX has reported that Russian Defense Minister P. Grachev in a telephone conversation with his U.S. counterpart, W. Perry, supported the holding of the scheduled joint Russian-U.S. maneuvers at Orenburg Oblast's Totskoye test ground. This idea, as we know, has given rise to sharp criticism in Russian society. The State Duma has urged President B. Yeltsin to abandon the maneuvers and the latter in turn has "suggested" to the Defense Ministry that it think about rescheduling them -- in the interests of "achieving national accord." But that prospect thoroughly displeased the Pentagon, whose high-ranking representatives issued a statement in favor of "rehearsing joint peacekeeping actions." Meanwhile very curious details became known. The Russian side has rejected the return exercise suggested by the Americans on their territory: Why inconvenience our host, it said! But inconveniencing its own citizens -- that is another matter, it can do that. As V. Semenov, commander in chief of the RUssian Ground Forces, said in an interview with OBSHCHAYA GAZETA, in response to proposals to hold joint exercises on FRG territory the Germans refused. Why did their country need such a "gift?" But Russia, through its defense minister, is giving the go-ahead to a dubious action and counter to assurances that they are seeking "accord" the authorities nonetheless intend to ignore the State Duma decree, the Orenburg Oblast local authorities' decision, and public opinion....
