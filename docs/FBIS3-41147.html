Language: English Article Type:CSO [Article by Lloyd Williams: "Three Jamaicans Die From Cocaine Ingestion"] [Text] Kingston (NICA)--Three Jamaicans have died here and overseas in the last few days from ingesting cocaine which they were smuggling in packets in their stomachs. Police sources have identified them as Sonia Whitley-Anderson, 38, of Kendal, Hanover; Ovel Washington Porter, 28, a seaman of Flankers, St. James and an unidentified male higgler. Mrs. Whitley-Anderson died in Montego Bay on Thursday on arriving on the SAM flight from San Andres, Colombia; Mr. Porter died in Bermuda on Sunday and the unidentified higgler died Thursday morning while he was preparing to take the COPA flight back to Kingston from Panama City. According to Narcotics Police Division sources, Mrs. Whitley-Anderson went to San Andres off the Colombian coast on SAM flight 506 which left from Montego Bay on Sunday and returned on Thursday. She fell ill while the plane was returning to Montego Bay and when it landed at 3 p.m., a physician who was on board, pronounced her dead. Five small packets of cocaine were found in her brassiere and she is believed to have swallowed several more to smuggle them in. An autopsy is to be done on her body tomorrow. The unidentified male higgler was found dead in the Aruba hotel in Panama City on Thursday morning.

Sources say he was getting ready to leave for Kingston on the COPA flight yesterday when he died from an overdose of cocaine which he swallowed to smuggle here. Mr. Porter worked as a seaman aboard the cruise ship Song of America but lost his job recently. He left Jamaica on November 23 and after travelling to Miami, Florida and Atlanta, Georgia, arrived in Hamilton, the capital of Bermuda, on December 4. He fell ill and died the following day and an autopsy done on him on December 6 revealed that he had in his stomach 24 packets with substance confirmed to be cocaine, weighing 191.99 grams. Half a gram of cocaine in Bermuda sells for US$125. Mr. Porter's body is to be flown to Jamaica today (Saturday). On Thursday agents from the Narcotics Police Division accosted four women who had arrived at the Norman Manley International Airport in Kingston aboard COPA flight 4l8 from Panama City. They were intercepted outside the airport after they had cleared immigration and customs. One, identified by the police as Joan Watson, 38, higgler, of 11 Olympic Way, Kingston, stripped herself naked and refused to be searched, but the police subdued her and when she was searched, a packet with 1 lb. of cocaine was found in her vagina. The others, Joan Watson's sister, Densetta Watson, 33, Maureen Patterson, 30, of 263 Amos Path, Bay Farm Villas, Kingston and Lavern Wilson, 38, of Chapelton Road, May Pen, Clarendon, were similarly searched and each was found with a packet with 3/4 lb of cocaine in the vagina. The cocaine was wrapped in masking tape. All four were charged with possession of cocaine, dealing in cocaine and importing cocaine. They are to appear before the Sutton Street RM court next week.
