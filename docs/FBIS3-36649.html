Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksandr Potyomkin] [Text] Warsaw February 7 TASS -- Russia and Poland agreed on Monday to boost cooperation in fighting organised crime. Russian Interior Minister Viktor Yerin, who is on a visit in Warsaw, told ITAR-TASS that "our cooperation has reached such a level that the time has come for joint activities of Russian and Polish Interior Ministry bodies to combat organised crime." He said this is the time for discussing concrete operations. Information exchange and coordinated activities have helped Russian and Polish law enforcement bodies solve a series of crimes, including car thefts. Polish Interior Minister Andrzej Milczanowski stressed the need for both countries to increase cooperation especially in combating organised crime and illegal drug trafficking. He said an agreement had been reached to exchange permanent representatives to maintain contacts between the Interior Ministries of the two countries. During the three-day visit that ended on Monday, Yerin was also received by Polish President Lech Walesa and Prime Minister Waldemar Pawlak. The Russian delegation visited several special police and Interior Ministry troops units.
