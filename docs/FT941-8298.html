940221 FT 21 FEB 94 / GMB urges police to 'feel collars' The GMB general union has urged the Serious Fraud Office to investigate companies that have profited by persuading people to transfer into private pension plans while disregarding a legal requirement to give best advice. Mr John Edmonds, general secretary, said he was writing to the SFO calling for an investigation. He said: 'They should start at the top. The fat-cat directors of the insurance companies knew what was going on. They set up the personal-pension plans, they organised the commission systems, they failed to train the salesmen properly, and they failed to operate proper supervision.' He told a weekend conference in Epsom, Surrey, that it was time for the police to become involved. He said that 'millions of people have been connned out of thousands of pounds each' by insurance company sales staff peddling 'second-rate' personal pensions. Police should enter the boardrooms of companies involved, he said, and 'feel a few collars'. He criticised the composition of the steering group set up by the Securities and Investments Board in December to try to establish how many people were worse off. No trade union or consumer representatives were included in the group. People who had been made redundant had proved easy targets for the insurance companies, he said. 'Almost no one can calculate how much their existing pension will be worth when they retire. So we are all easy prey to a salesman with a plausible line in patter.'
