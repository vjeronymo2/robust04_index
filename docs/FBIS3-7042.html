Language: English Article Type:BFN ["Pressure Will Only Make Situation on Korean Peninsula Tense" -- KCNA headline] [Text] Pyongyang, March 24 (KCNA) -- Chinese Premier Li Peng told Chinese and foreign reporters on March 22 that it has been China's consistent position to stand for negotiations and dialogues rather than pressure on the issue of nuclear inspection in the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, XINHUA reported. Answering a question by a reporter on China's stand if the issue is submitted to the Security Council of the United Nations, the premier said that if the method of pressure is used, it will only make things more complicated, and make the situation on the Korean peninsula tense, and that will not be good for the peace and stability of the region.
