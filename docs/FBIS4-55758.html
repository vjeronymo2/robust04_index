BFN [Text] Sofia, May 19 (BTA) -- A Foreign Ministry spokesman today denied a REUTER report from Ankara, concerning a stand taken by Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov on the commitment of Turkish troops to UNPROFOR [UN Protection Forces] in Bosnia-Herzegovina. At a news conference in Ankara, cited as a source by the authoritative news agency, Foreign Minister Daskalov confirmed Bulgaria's principled position against the involvement of neighbouring and Balkan countries in UNPROFOR in Bosnia-Herzegovina, says a press release received at BTA. It was also stressed that this position was directed neither against Turkey nor against any other country in the region, the spokesman said. On May 17 REUTER reported: "Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov said Bulgaria in principle opposed the involvement of Balkan countries in the conflict but did not object to Turkey sending troops to join a UN peacekeeping force in Bosnia."
