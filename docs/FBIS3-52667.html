Language: Hebrew Article Type:BFN [Report by Hemi Shalev and Washington-based correspondent Avino'am Bar-Yosef] [Excerpt] Sources in the prime minister's bureau yesterday confirmed that Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin is not enthusiastic about the idea of posting U.S. troops on the Golan Heights. In a closed-door discussion held in the last few days Rabin also referred to the Palestinian desire for deploying U.S. forces at the border crossing points: "I do not want any American mother to cry on our account." Rabin adduced the example of U.S. intervention in Somalia, recalling the grave impact that the death of only a handful of American soldiers had on U.S. public opinion. [passage omitted]
