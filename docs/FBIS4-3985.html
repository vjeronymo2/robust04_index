BFN [Text] Beijing, April 28 (XINHUA) -- The Chinese People's Association for Friendship with African Countries (CPAFAC) is considering sending a delegation to the inauguration ceremony of the new South African [RSA] president, said a Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesman at a weekly press conference here this afternoon. In response to a question, spokesman Wu Jianmin noted that the inauguration marks the end of apartheid rule in South Africa and the start of a new, non-racial South Africa. "The Chinese people have always staunchly supported the just struggle of the South African people and share the great joy at their historic victory," Wu said. As an expression of the congratulations of the Chinese people to the South African people, Wu said, the CPAFAC is expected to send a delegation to the inauguration.
