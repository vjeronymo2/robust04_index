Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] There will be no elections in Chechnya in the near future, Interfax was told by President Dzhokhar Dudayev, answering the question whether Chechnya intends to take part in the March by-elections to the Russian Federal Assembly. "Russia has had the elections and we congratulate it with their successful completion," said the president. "As to Chechnya, it held the elections in 1991 (the presidential elections after which the republic proclaimed its independence -- `IF')." "The Chechen people have made their choice," said Dudayev. He said that another presidential election in Chechnya would take place in 1995, while the parliamentary elections were planned for 1996. "If there is a need for early elections to some power structures of the republic, this will be announced by leaders of the independent Chechen Republic," said the president.
