BFN [Text] Som Chatusiphithak, general manager of Siam City Bank and chairman of Thai Banking Association, disclosed that the association has selected its officials and representatives from four commercial banks -- Bangkok Bank, Krung Thai Bank, Siam Commercial Bank, and Thai Farmer Bank -- to be appointed as members of the committee of the "Thailand-Singapore Business Council," an organization to be active in boosting trade and business cooperation between the two countries. The name list was submitted to the council on 17 June. Som said that the said organization was the result of the negotiations made during the latest official visit to Singapore of Prime Minister Chuan Likphai. At the initial stage, the council will concentrate on working out its framework before it can realy start functioning in promoting free trade. Som noted: "The Thailand-Singapore Business Council stresses on cooperation between the two countries in business and financial areas as well as industries concerned. More important is that the council will promote good understanding and cooperation between Thailand and Singapore as both countries are striving to become financial hubs in the region. It is believed that the council will help prevent conflict and competition."
