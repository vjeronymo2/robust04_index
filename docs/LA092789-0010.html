September 27, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

QUAYLE

Elizabeth Watson's statement that "maybe Bush deserves congratulations for choosing Quayle in spite of how foolish a move it seemed about a year ago," in response to Vice President Dan Quayle's public assessment of "Star Wars," deserves comment (letter, Sept. 21).

The job President Bush gave Quayle -- to pop President Reagan's "Star War" bubble -- could have just as easily been given to any vice president at hand, or to the secretary of state, for that matter. Quayle remains a disastrously ignorant next-in-command in case President Bush dies in office.

Seeing how responsibly Bush behaves in his job as President, I hope that at the end of his first term he will quietly replace Quayle with a fully competent and knowledgeable person, especially in view of the added danger to Bush from the Colombian drug cartels.

This country has always taken its luck for granted. We shouldn't have to wait to see what happens.

HILDA E. WENNER

Santa Barbara
