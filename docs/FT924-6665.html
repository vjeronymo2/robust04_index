921120 FT 20 NOV 92 / World Trade News: Computer engineering centre opens in Kiev UKRAINE'S first computer engineering centre was opened this week in the capital, Kiev. Officials hope the centre, supplied with more than Dollars 1m (Pounds 600,000) worth of IBM computers and software, will help Ukraine's collapsing manufacturing industries make the leap to modern technology. The Computer Integrated Manufacturing Centre (CIM), based at the Kiev polytechnic institute, will teach students and retrain professional engineers in computer industries, design and manufacturing. Ukraine is a world leader in cybernetics research and has a huge, and under-employed, scientific and engineering community. IBM, has supplied 14,000 personal computers to schools in the former Soviet Union and opens an office in Kiev this week.
