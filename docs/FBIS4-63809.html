BFN [Text] Each of the parties proposed its own version of the final document at the talks between the Russian and Ukrainian governmental delegations headed by Premiers Viktor Chernomyrdin and Yukhym Zvyahilskyy in Moscow. Earlier on the same day, the two premiers had held a private meeting. Interfax learned that Russian representatives insisted that an inter-governmental agreement on the basing of the Russian part of the Black Sea Fleet in Crimea [sentence as received]. The Ukrainian delegation wanted to sign a memorandum with the same title. Moscow has insisted that Sevastopol be the main base of the Russian Navy and that the Ukrainian fleet be based beyond the city limits. It can be stationed in Balaklava (Crimea) or in Odessa. Russia recognizes that Sevastopol is a Ukrainian city under jurisdiction of Ukraine. Sources in the Ukrainian delegation told Interfax that the joint expert group was trying to combine the versions and to create a single final document which would provide for the basing of the Russian Black Sea Fleet in Sevastopol and for the basing of the Ukrainian Navy in Balaklava and partially in Sevastopol. The final document is expected to be signed on May 24 evening. Earlier today, sources in the Russian delegation had told Interfax that Ukraine stood for division of the fleet ships in accordance with the March agreements between the Russian and Ukrainian presidents. However, Ukraine insisted that it keep coastal facilities and infrastructure, including dwelling places for marine servicemen which did not conform with the position of the Russian delegation.
