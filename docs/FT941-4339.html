940311 FT 11 MAR 94 / UK Company News: John I Jacobs loses Pounds 0.5m John I Jacobs, the shipowning and shipbroking group, yesterday reported a pre-tax loss of Pounds 498,000 for 1993, after a provision of Pounds 480,000. This represented a write-off of the group's investment in Queen's Moat Houses' convertible preference shares. The comparable figure of Pounds 3.49m for 1992 included a profit of Pounds 3.91m on the disposal of a discontinued operation. Mr John Jacobs, the group's 74-year-old chairman, yesterday also announced plans to retire. He said that this would 'definitely be my last report . . . as chairman.' Turnover advanced to Pounds 2.84m, compared with Pounds 2.25m. The deficit came through as 2.3p per share, against earnings of 11.5p last time. A final dividend of 1.4p has been proposed, making a total of 3.3p for 1993. This included an interim payment of 1.4p which was in lieu of a final dividend for 1992, when a total of 0.5p was paid.
