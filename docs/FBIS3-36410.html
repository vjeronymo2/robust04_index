Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Yuriy Meshkov, the first President of the Crimea (a self-proclaimed republic within Ukraine) elected last Sunday, "optimistically inclines to continue contacts with President Leonid Kravchuk of Ukraine". He said so in an interview with INTERFAX given upon his return to Simferopol from Kiev, where he conferred with President Kravchuk on Friday. According to Meshkov, his conference with the Ukrainian president passed in a "frank atmosphere" and gave "a new impetus" to relations between the Crimea and Ukraine. Nevertheless he noted that the problems faced by his republic remain the same: The lack of social security, rising crime, the Black Sea Fleet. For its part, the press service of the Crimean president told INTERFAX that Meshkov had returned from Kiev "satisfied with his talks there". Right after his visit to Kiev Meshkov went on vacation, the press service added. Earlier the leader of the domestic politics service of Ukraine's president, Nikolay Mikhalchenko, said that the meeting was "open and constructive, and the two sides reached agreement easily". Judging by the official communique released after the meeting by the press service of Ukraine's president, the two sides focused on economic issues. During the talks they agreed to form a mixed working group representing the Ukrainian and Crimean governments, and discussed a mechanism for maintaining working contacts between the two presidents. Meshkov outlined to Kravchuk a model for developing economic ties between the peninsula and its traditional partners - Russia and other CIS countries. In the meantime the journalists who saw the two presidents have said that after his meeting with Kravchuk Meshkov looked "depressed".
