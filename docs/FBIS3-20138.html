Language: Greek Article Type:BFN [Text] U.S. President Bill Clinton has sent a letter to Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou, asking for the earliest possible settlement of the issues pending between Athens and Skopje [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia--FYROM]. Mr. Clinton pointed out that a solution that is fair to both sides can be found. Referring to the Cyprus issue, the U.S. President expressed his intention to have personal contacts with both interested parties, particularly with regard to the confidence-building measures. Responding to a question from journalists on President Bill Clinton's letter to Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou, Government spokesman Evangelos Venizelos made the following statement: President Clinton's letter to Prime Minister Andreas Papandreou was been sent in connection with the upcoming visit by Mr. Andreas Papandreou to the United States and refers concisely to all the issues that will be discussed. It is a friendly and constructive letter that constitutes a serious basis for discussion, given the U.S. intentions to address all the problems and de-escalate tensions. Answering another relevant question, the Government spokesman made it clear that the question of Turkish troops being sent to Bosnia is not raised in the letter, while on the Skopje issue President Clinton outlined the well-known U.S. positions, which have also been expressed publicly.
