November 2, 1989, Thursday, Home Edition

STEEL FABRICATORS

Esther McCoy's article "Los Angeles Case-Study Houses" (Opinion, Oct. 8) revived memories by this retired steel fabricator. Houlberg Iron performed several such houses, the most notable being Craig Ellwood's "Case Study House Number One," on Schuyler Road in Beverly Hills (circa 1952). P.B. Philbrick was the general contractor and proclaimed he only used $200 worth of wood on the entire structure, and that was the forming material for the concrete floor and foundation!

Ellwood was a perfectionist who demanded that all welds be ground smooth, and hand-filed mitered corner joints were the rule. This was Houlberg Iron's first such project. I also recall examining the drawings of the late A. Ouincy Jones' "Below Grade" design (mentioned in McCoy's article) in his offices in West Los Angeles and was excited by the sheer simplicity of lightweight steel construction carrying the design to clean-cut perfection.

J. PETER HOULBERG

Montecito
