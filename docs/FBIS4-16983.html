BFN [POSTFACTUM report under the "In Brief" rubric] [Text] Konstantin Borovoy, chairman of the Economic Freedom Party [EFP], has advised that the EFP's attitude to the Agreement on Civil Peace will be discussed at the party's political council on 28 April. In his view, the signing of the agreement would signify a conservation of the status quo, whereas "putting today's situation, with all its negative sides considered, on ice is a renunciation of the democratic process of development." He appreciated the stance of the president who seeks accord in society; however, in his words, the EFP "cannot give up supporting citizens' interests," that is, waging struggle.
