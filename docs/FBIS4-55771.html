BFN [Text] Budapest, 19 May (MTI) -- "The Hungarian Democratic Forum [MDF] has quickly recovered after the shock which the May 8 election result caused to its voters and organizers," MDF acting chairman Sandor Lezsak told the party's news conference in Budapest on Thursday. He said he believes broad cooperation between conservative forces could obstruct a Socialist-Liberal majority in the new parliament. According to Lezsak, many people want to vote on May 29 for fear of an overwhelming ruling power leading to autocratic rule and a constitutional dictatorship. On June 4, the MDF's National Board will look into the reasons behind the party's poor election performance. It will also decide whether to discuss past and current affairs with delegates of the 8th National Convention, or to elect new delegates, and select a date for the next national meeting sometime before October.
