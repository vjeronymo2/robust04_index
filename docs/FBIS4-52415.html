BFN [Text] Tokyo, May 7 (KNS-KCNA) [dateline as received] -- Officials of the General Association of Korean Residents in Japan (Chongnyon) and Koreans in Japan continue their protest against suppression by the Japanese police. Officials and compatriots under the Osaka prefectural headquarters of Chongnyon on May 4 demanded that the Osaka prefectural police apologize for the crackdown on the Chongnyon organization at once and return the materials Lhich had been confiscated. On the same day, compatriots of the Shibugawa section of the South branch of Chongnyon in Higashiosaka joined in the protest, led by a streamer inscribed with a slogan denouncing the police. A symposium on the human rights of Koreans in Japan on May 2 released a letter of protest condemning the repression by the Japanese police as a crackdown on the Koreans in Japan and violation of their human rights. Branding the police search of Chongnyon as a political repression, the letter said such a repressive row itself is an expression of national discrimination and chauvinism.
