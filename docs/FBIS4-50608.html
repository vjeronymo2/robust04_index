BFN [From the "Hookup" program] [Text] Speaking yesterday [7 May] at a seminar on mainland-related economic and trade policy and cross-strait transportation sponsored by the [words indistinct] Junior Chamber in Taichung City, Huang Kun-hui, chairman of the Mainland Affairs Council under the Executive Yuan, said: As part of our plans to build Taiwan into a business center in the Asia-Pacific region, we do not exclude the possibility of establishing direct transportation links with Mainland China. However, we should not end up turning Shanghai into a business center following the establishment of such links. Now is not the time to foster wide-ranging transportation links. Huang Kun-hui said: Direct cross-strait transportation can indeed reduce the cost and time of shipping containers. However, since our country and Communist China are in a state of hostility, the unconditional establishment of such links will amount to an acknowledgement that any direct routes thus established are domestic routes in Communist China. This will make it difficult to clarify to other nations that our country is not a province of China. Also, direct transportation services will affect our country's alert status.
