Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Pyongyang, January 22 (KCNA) -- A memorial service for the late Rev. Mun Ik-hwan, a patriotic champion for reunification of South Korea, was held in Pyongyang today. Rev. Mun was a reputed patriotic champion for reunification who devoted his all to the struggle for the independence and democratisation of South Korean society and the reunification of the country. Seen on the platform of the hall of the memorial service were a portrait of the deceased wearing black ribbons and wreaths from the Committee for the Peaceful Reunification of the Fatherland, the Korean Christian Federation and other public organisations. Present at the memorial service were Yang Hyong-sop, alternate Politburo member of the C.C. [Central Committee], the Workers' Party of Korea, chairman of the Supreme People's Assembly [SPA] and vice-chairman of the Committee for the Peaceful Reunification of the Fatherland [CPRF], Chong Chun-ki, chairman of the Committee for Cultural Relations with Foreign Countries and vice-chairman of the CPRF, and other officials concerned. Memorial addresses were made by Chairman of the North side headquarters of the National Alliance for the Country's Reunification [Pomminnyon] Paek In-chun, director of the secretariat of the Committee for the Peaceful Reunification of the Fatherland Paek Nam-chun and member of the C.C., the North side headquarters of the National Alliance of Youth and Students for the Country's Reunification [Pomchonghangnyon] Choe Yong-hui. They said the life of the late Rev. Mun, the fallen patriot, was the life of an admirable paoriotic-minded person who devoted his all to the struggle for independence, democracy and reunification and the life of a reunification champion who made a breakthrough on the barrier of national division with the devotion of his whole body and who kindled a fierce flame of reunification in the hearts of the compatriots at home and abroad. His death is a big loss to our nation, they noted. They recalled that the pastor hailed and supported the proposal for convening a North-South political consultative conference to discusy the confederacy formula of reunification which was put forward by the great leader President Kim Il-song in his New Year address for 1989, visited Pyongyang in March that year and was received by the great leader, after which he renewed his patriotic faith and will for reunfication and, back in South Korea, he did not cease his struggle behind bars. By refusing a Seoul trip of our mourners' group on the death of the late Mun, the South Korean authorities stripped themselves naked to the world, revealing their true color as fascist dictators and a group of barbarians who have totally thrown away human morality and the "civilian" mask, they said. They stressed that, though the Rev. Mun passed away, his patriotic spirit for reunification will remain in the hearts of our people and the day of national reunification longed for by him will come certainly. A poet Pak Se-ok recited a poem "To the Memory of Rev. Mun Ik-hwan" and people's actor Yi Tan read a poem written by the late Rev. Mun at the memorial service.
