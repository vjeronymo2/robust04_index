Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA Jan 28 -- A diplomatic note on Japanese Government's soft loan of 52.3 billion yen (roughly U.S.$476 million) in 1993 fiscal year to Vietnam was signed here today by Deputy Foreign Minister Vu Khoan and Japanese Ambassador Hiroyuki Yushita. Loan agreement to this effect was also signed by Finance Minister Ho Te and Vice President of the Overseas Economic Cooperation Fund of Japan Hiroshi Kikumoto. This is the first soft loan of Japanese Government to Vietnam to help the later build a number of infrastructure constructions. The two diplomats also signed diplomatic note on Japanese Government's grand aid to water project in Hanoi suburban district of Gia Lam (second stage) valued at 65 million yen (about U.S.$590,000), and To Can Tho Agriculture University 788 million yen (some U.S.$7.2 million). Present at the signing ceremony were Deputy Prime Minister Phan Van Khai and other Vietnamese high-ranking officials. Speaking at the ceremony the signatories stressed on the significance of these events and expressed their wishes for further development of the relations between Vietnam and Japan. On the same day, Deputy Foreign Minister Vu Khoan met Japanese Ambassador H.Yushita. The two sides informed each other of the [words indistinct] country and exchanged views on international and regional issues, especially the situation in Asia and the Pacific. The two sides discussed steps to further promote the friendly and cooperative relations between the two countries.
