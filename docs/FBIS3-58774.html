Language: Turkish Article Type:BFN [Announcer-read report over video] [Excerpt] [passage omitted] Douglas Hurd, British secretary of state for foreign and Commonwealth affairs, visited Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin in Ankara today. Following the tete-a-tete meeting between the two ministers, official talks began between the Turkish and British delegations. In a statement before the talks, Hurd expressed pleasure over meeting with Cetin, with whom he is in direct contact or speaks to on the telephone almost once a month. Pointing out that bilateral relations, terrorism, human rights, and the recent developments in Iraq and Bosnia-Herzegovina were discussed with Cetin, Hurd noted that a tripartite meeting will be held tomorrow in which German Foreign Minister Klaus Kinkel will participate as well. Cetin declared in turn that relations between Turkey and Britain are extremely positive, and there are no problems between the two countries. During the talks with the visiting minister, agreement was reached to further develop bilateral relations, Cetin remarked. He noted that Turkey has always enjoyed British support in its relations with Europe, expressing the belief that this support will continue in the future as well. Hurd was then received by Prime Minister Tansu Ciller. Cetin and the two countries' ambassadors to London and Ankara respectively also attended the meeting. The foreign ministers of Turkey, Britain, and Germany will hold their tripartite consultative meeting in Ankara tomorrow. Kinkel will arrive in Ankara tonight to attend the meeting.
