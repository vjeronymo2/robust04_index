BFN [Text] Seoul, May 28 (YONHAP) -- Three of the five International Atomic Energy Agency [IAEA] officials currently in North Korea will leave the country Saturday after negotiations collapsed on the terms of the IAEA monitoring the North's unloading of spent fuel rods from the controversial Yongbyon nuclear reactor, diplomatic sources here said. "The negotiations broke down because the North proceeded with the replacement of spent fuel rods, ignoring the IAEA request to stop withdrawing them," said the sources. "Accordingly, three out of the five IAEA officials in North Korea will pull out of Pyongyang today." The sources, however, said two inspectors would remain in North Korea to witness the work of discharging spent fuel rods at the Yongbyon reactor.
