941124 FT 24 NOV 94 / Lack of overtime 'blocks feelgood' A key reason for the lack of a 'feelgood' factor in the economy is the low level of overtime, says a report by Kleinwort Benson, the broker. It argues that the new flexibility in the labour force has significantly eroded the level of overtime and left people increasingly insecure about job prospects. Overtime rose by 1m hours a week on average between August and September, to reach 9.84m hours, its highest level since last July, but below the levels of two years ago. A 37-hour basic working week has become the norm for Britain's manual workers, a study by Industrial Relations Services has found.
