930608 FT 08 JUN 93 / Arts: Today's Television You never know, television may yet grow up. Usually documentaries on social subjects reflect the politically correct view: Boo] to landowners and non-socialist governments, Hooray] for the little people. It makes better television. Today's Assignment considers projects for dams in India and Sri Lanka of a sort normally considered to be among the best ways of providing cheap electricity and water. We are told, however, that opponents say such dams destroy the habitat of people and animals while the extra power and water are often grabbed by rich farmers and industrialists. Usually this would ensure the hostility of the documentary maker, yet the billing for today's programme (7.45 BBC2) ends by asking 'But is the new orthodoxy as doctrinaire and blinkered as the one it replaces?' This suggests a quite abnormal maturity. BBC2 also puts together a fascinating sequence of 90-second opinion pieces shown some weeks ago in which all sorts of people enthused over their favourite works of art. Together they make a 35-minute programme, The Great Picture Trail (9.45).
