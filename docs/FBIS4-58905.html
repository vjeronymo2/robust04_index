BFN [Text] Cairo, 7 May (MENA)--Dr. Nabil Sha'th, political adviser to Palestinian President Yasir 'Arafat, has stated that Palestinian and Israeli negotiators will begin talks in Cairo next week on the points that are still the subject of disagreement, namely the stationing of a Palestinian policeman on the Allenby Bridge, the appointment of a Palestinian director for the Rafah crossing, the al-Mawasi area on the Khan Yunis beach, and the size of the Jericho area. This came at a news conference he held today to explain the details of the Gaza-Jericho agreement which was signed by President 'Arafat and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin in Cairo on 4 May. Dr. Sha'th said that parallel to the talks on the points that are still the subject of disagreement between the Palestinian and Israeli sides, talks will also start on extending the Palestinian authority to the West Bank at an early date so that the Palestinian police will deploy in the West Bank towns to prepare for the Palestinian elections. He refused to set a date for these elections, indicating that, in accordance with the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles, they were supposed to take place on 13 July. However, in view of the delay in signing the agreement from April to 4 May, holding these elections in Gaza and the West Bank might be delayed . Dr. Sha'th explained that President 'Arafat will deliver the list of the names of the members of the Palestinian authority to Israel on 11 May. Dr. Sha'th said that Palestinian-Israeli negotiations will also take place in Washington when the Arab-Israeli talks resume on the other tracks. The talks, which on the Palestinian side will be chaired by Dr. Sa'ib 'Urayqat, will focus on the Palestinian legislative elections and the withdrawal from the West Bank. He added that talks will be held in Gaza and Jericho on the return of displaced people who left their homes after 1967. He noted that the exact number of these displaced people is not known because of population growth over the past 27 years. Dr. Sha'th said the first group from the Palestinian police force consisting of 360 policemen and officers left for Rafah today. He added that 1,500 policemen will cross the Jordan River to Jericho tomorrow. He said more Palestinian policemen will continue to arrive in the Gaza Strip and Jericho, indicating that the PLO will adhere to the timetable mentioned in the agreement, namely one to three weeks to establish security control in the autonomous areas in Gaza and Jericho. Dr. Sha'th noted that the Palestinian forces in Yemen today began moving overland to Najran in Saudi Arabia in view of the circumstances in Yemen and the closure of airports. He said Saudi planes will fly them to Cairo. From there they will move to their assembly point in Rafah. The Palestinian forces in Libya have also moved overland to Cairo and from there to their assembly point in Rafah. The Palestinian forces in Algeria will also move to Egypt and then to the assembly point in Rafah. Dr. Sha'th said Maj. Gen. Nasr Yusuf has been appointed commander in chief of the Palestinian police force. Ziyad al-Atrash has been appointed representative of the Palestinian side on the Palestinian-Israeli Coordination Committee. Al-Atrash arrived in Gaza yesterday. Brig. Gen. Ghazi al-Jabali has been appointed police commander of the Gaza Strip. Dr. Sha'th said the interim period will begin on 4 May 1994, the date of the signing of the agreement. He indicated that a general amnesty will be issued for all prisoners and detainees when agreement on the interim period is reached. He added that no detainee will make a pledge to adhere to the accord. The pledge will be given to the Palestinian authority and not Israel; it will be a pledge to adhere to what the authority adheres to. Dr. Nabil Sha'th said the agreement signed in Cairo Wednesday was the best possible agreement under the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles signed in Washington on 13 September. He noted that the Declaration postponed discussion of main issues such as Israeli settlements, Jerusalem, Israeli withdrawal, border protection, and the return of the refugees. He said the Palestinian National Authority, as it was agreed to call it, will be responsible for implementing what has been agreed upon. This authority, he added, will not have the right to exercise international powers, and countries cannot, for example, open embassies in the self-rule areas, but they may have representation offices in Gaza and Jericho. Sha'th said the PLO outside will have the right to open embassies because the Palestine National Council had announced the establishment of a Palestinian state. Concerning the structure of the Palestinian National Authority, Dr. Sha'th explained at the end of his news conference that a 24-member council will assume legislative and executive powers. The judicial power, he added, will be exercised by appointed judges that cannot be dismissed, in accordance with the familiar norms in this regard. Sha'th said the Palestinian negotiator managed to regain an important part of his rights within the framework of the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles. He stressed that the Palestinians will continue the negotiations until they attain all their rights to self-determination, the establishment of the Palestinian state, and repatriation. He emphasized that the Palestinians will respect all their obligations and will work for a just and genuine peace.
