Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpt] Guatemala City, 6 Jan (ACAN-EFE) -- The Guatemalan Army today placed its troops along the Mexican border on alert after it confirmed the presence of insurgent Mexican columns within 800 meters of national territory. Army spokeswoman Major Edith Vargas told ACAN-EFE that the Military Command is coordinating surveillance along the border, which is divided into seven military zones, although it has not felt the need to send reinforcements. On 1 January, the Zapatista National Liberation Army (EZLN) declared war on the Mexican Government in the southeastern state of Chiapas, where clashes have left more than 200 dead, according to various sources. Since yesterday, EZLN rebels have been present in the community of Guadalupe Tepeya, about 800 km from the Guatemalan border north of Huehuetenango Department. Security and defense in this area is under Military Zone No. 19, but the border area is also being guarded by troops and detachments that are under commands [centrales] in the northern departments of Quiche and Alta Verapaz.
