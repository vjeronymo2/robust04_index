BFN [Text] Chisinau BASAPRESS, 26/4/1994 -- Yesterday the ambassador of the Republic of Moldova in Moscow, Anatol Taran, submitted his resignation. Taran declared to BASA that until President Mircea Snegur signs the order accepting his resignation, diplomatic ethics do not allow him to present details concerning the reasons of his request. Asked if he was sure that Snegur will accept his resignation, Taran said it was highly probable; in case of rejection he intends to insist upon acceptance. According to Taran, after the Easter holidays, during which he intends to visit to take his leave of the president, government and colleagues from the diplomatic corps accredited in the Russian Federation; he will then return to Chisinau. Informed sources from Chisinau announced to BASA that a possible candidate for the resigned post would be Grigore Eremei, current ambassador of the Republic of Moldova in Minsk.
