Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Aleksey Kuznetsov] [Text] Dehli, 14 Feb -- Feliks Gromov, Russian Navy commander in chief, arrived in India today on a visit. In an interview with ITAR-TASS he said that "specific issues on the development of military and technical cooperation are to be resolved, taking into account the accords reached during the visit by Russian President Boris Yeltsin to India in January 1993." Pointing out that the "Indian Ocean is important to Russia," F. Gromov said that the supply of spare parts and equipment for the republic's navy will be discussed at the talks with India's military leadership. The sides will also look into the prospects of holding joint naval exercises. According to the commander in chief of the Russian Navy, "there are no obstacles to this." The program of his stay also envisages visits to Indian naval bases in Bombay and Vishakhapatnam, a naval academy, and shipyards in Goa.
