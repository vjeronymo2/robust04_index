Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS-KAZTAG correspondents Amangeldiy Akhmetalimov and Boris Sitnikov] [Text] United Nations February 16 TASS -- U.N. Secretary General Butrus Butrus-Ghali met here on Wednesday with Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev. During the meeting, Nazarbayev moved an initiative to convene a special session of the U.N. General Assembly on the post-confrontation period. The participants in the meeting also discussed the Kazakh initiative for creating a collective security system in Asia by concluding a treaty on coordination and trust-building measures on the continent. This initiative was put forward by the Kazakh president at the 47th session of the U.N. General Assembly in 1992. The U.N. secretary general praised the initiatives moved by the Kazakh president. Butrus-Ghali and Nazarbayev also discussed the current situation in Central Asia, Kazakhstan, Russia and the entire world. After the meeting, the Kazakh president put his signature to the convention on child's rights. Kazakhstan became the 156th nation to join to this convention. In conclusion of his staying at the U.N. headquarters, Nazarbayev met administrator of the United Nations Development Programme James Spet.
