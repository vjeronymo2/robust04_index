Language: Spanish Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Mexico City, 27 Jan (EFE) -- Manuel Camacho Solis, presidential commissioner for peace and reconciliation, today unexpectedly postponed his reply to the demands of the Zapatist National Liberation Army (EZLN) for a possible dialogue to end the armed conflict in Chiapas State. Camacho said the postponement of the reply, which had been announced on 25 January for 1800 GMT today, is due to the fact that he had received in the last few hours "new information and elements that allow progress toward a dialogue." Camacho reiterated that the only objective of this entire effort is achieving an end to hostilities, and that is why he decided to postpone the reply. "This is a delicate moment, which we must handle with utmost responsibility," Camacho Solis said. [passage omitted] Camacho's announcement has created a great deal of anticipation in Mexico City, where there is conjecture about the origin of the new information received by the presidential peace commissioner. The peace commissioner and Bishop Samuel Ruiz of San Cristobal de las Casas (Chiapas) are in the Mexican capital. The bishop has been appointed the Mexican Government's mediator with the EZLN rebels.
