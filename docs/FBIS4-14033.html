BFN [Excerpts] The Syrian side led by Dr. Salim Yasin, deputy prime minister for economic affairs, and the Russian side led by Oleg Soskovets, first deputy prime minister of the Russian Federation, began official talks at the Prime Ministry this evening. [passage omitted] At the beginning of the meeting, Dr. Yasin welcomed Soskovets and his accompanying delegation and wished them a happy stay in Syria. He also stressed the profound friendly relations that have long linked the two countries. He then referred to the strong ties, which have developed in all areas, as well as the positive results they have produced and will continue to produce. He also pointed to the fact that both sides recognize that they have joint interests and seek to activate, expand, and diversify these ties. He also stressed the need to exert every effort and embark on every cooperative effort to reap the fruits within this framework. Soskovets then spoke, expressing his happiness over the warm reception accorded him and his delegation. He said: This visit has been planned for a long time, and we sought to make it successful and fruitful in the field of bilateral cooperation. He then referred to the political and economic developments in Russia over the past period, as well as the great changes it has seen. The two sides then reviewed several issues of common interest in various areas, as well as bilateral relations and ways of promoting them in the interest of both countries. At the end of the session, both sides agreed to resume the meetings tomorrow morning at the level of bilateral and specialized committees.
