BFN [Text] Budapest, 30 May (MTI) -- In the May 29 second round of the 1994 parliamentary elections, 55.12 percent of eligible voters, numbering 4,339,801, turned out for the vote, and 4,286,373 (98.77 percent) cast valid ballots, Chairman of the National Electoral Committee Janos Nemeth told an international news conference in the Parliament building today. According to an unofficial, computerized summary, the Hungarian Socialist Party (MSZP) won 209 mandates in the 386-member Parliament, the Alliance of Free Democrats (SZDSZ) 70, the Hungarian Democratic Forum (HDF) 37, the Independent Smallholders Party (FKGP) 26, the Christian Democratic People's Party (KDNP) 22, the Federation of Young Democrats (Fidesz) 20, and the Agrarian Association and the four-party liberal coalition one each. Accordingly, the MSZP won 54.15 percent of seats, the SZDSZ 18.14 percent, the HDF 9.59 percent, the FKGP 6.74 percent, the KDNP 5.7 percent, the Fidesz 5.18 percent, and the Agrarian Association and the four-party liberal coalition 0.26 percent each. The MSZP triumphed in 149 individual constituencies, the SZDSZ in 17, the HDF in four, the KDNP in three, and the FKGP, the Agrarian Association, and the four-party liberal coalition in one each. The MSZP won 53 mandates on regional lists, the SZDSZ 28, the HDF 18, the FKGP 14, the Fidesz seven and, the KDNP five. Seats from the national lists were distributed as follows: SZDSZ 25, HDF 15, KDNP 14, Fidesz 13, FKGP 11, MSZP seven.
