BFN [Dinh Khai's report from Phnom Penh, Cambodia] [Summary] "At the invitation of Samdech Norodom Ranariddh and Samdech Hun Sen, the first and second co-prime ministers of the royal government of the Kingdom of Cambodia, SRV Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet arrived in Cambodia today for an official friendship visit. "He is accompanied by Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam, Minister of Commerce Le Van Triet, Minister of Education and Training Tran Hong Quan, Customs General Department Head Truong Quang Duoc, and some other high-ranking cadres." The plane carrying the prime minister arrived at Pochentong International Airport at 0900 hours sharp. The airport was bedecked with banners and the national flags of the two countries. "Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet was greeted at arrival by Co-Prime Ministers Ranariddh and Hun Sen" and their deputy prime ministers and foreign minister. "Also on hand to welcome the prime minister was SRV Ambassador to Cambodia Tran Huy Chuong and many cadres and members of the Vietnamese embassy in Cambodia." Together with the Cambodian co-prime ministers, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet stepped on a raised dais to salute the colors as a military band played the national anthems of the two countries. The Cambodian co-prime miniters then invited Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet to review the Guard of Honor and to say hello to various well-wishers including high-ranking Cambodian Government officials and members of the diplomatic corps. After the welcoming ceremony at the airport, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet was whisked in a motorcade to the government guest house in Phnom Penh. "Upon his arrival in Phnom Penh, Prime Minister and his entourage laid a wreath at the independence monument in memory of fighters who sacrified their lives for their Cambodian fatherland." Later, "at the office of the Council of Ministers, a delegation of the royal government of the Kingdom of Cambodia led by First Prime Minister Ranariddh and Second Prime Minister Hun Sen held talks with our Vietnamese Government delegation led by Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet. At the talks, the two sides informed each other of the situation in each country and of matters of mutual concern with a view to strengthening, consolidating, and developing the traditional neighborly relations of friendship and cooperation." This afternoon, "Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet and his entourage visited the Cambodian National Museum, held a get-together with the representatives of Vietnamese nationals in Phnom Penh, and visited the Vietnamese embassy staff."
