Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ankara, Feb 14 (A.A) -- France will extend 302.2 million Francs (approximately 51-million dollar) credit to Turkey in a bid to finance regions which have priority for development, it was reported on Monday [14 February]. Turkey and France ratified a protocol on the issue foreseeing France's financial support to Turkey. Under the protocol which was published in today's OFFICIAL GAZETTE, the credit will be used in Balikesir and Mersin's third water purification systems, electrification of Divrigi-Iskenderun railway line, in Alanya's small churn water purification system and in Istanbul's organized leather industry projects. The credit will provide a financial support to purchase French-origin items.
