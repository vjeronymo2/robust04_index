BFN [Text] Prague, May 23 (CTK) -- For the first time in the past twelve months the popularity of the ODS (Civic Democratic Party) dropped under 30 percent, to 29 percent, a poll conducted by the Center of the Empiric Researches (STEM) in early May revealed. Last May 36 percent of those surveyed would have voted for the ODS. On the other hand, the polls of the ODA (Civic Democratic Alliance) are steadily increasing from 10 percent a year ago to the present 14.8 percent. The ODA is followed by the Czech Social Democratic Party (CSSD) with 14.2 percent, and the Left Bloc, the Communist Party of Bohemia and Moravia (KSCM) and Democratic Left Party (SDL) with a total of 11.5 percent. Parties under the 5-percent limit necessary to get to the parliament would receive 4.1 percent (Christian Democratic Union- Czechoslovak People's Party, KDU-CSL), 4 percent (Assembly for the Republic-Czechoslovak Republican Party, SPR-RSC) and one to two percent (Bohemian and Moravian Center Party, Green Party, Christian Democratic Party, and Liberal Social Union). The voter turn-out would be 64 percent, while 23 percent have not made up their mind, and 12 percent are decided not to take part, the poll found.
