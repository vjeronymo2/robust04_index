Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Bulantsev] [Text] New Delhi January 4 TASS -- "Our visit to India has been successful. We have fulfilled all our plans. The visit has been rather fruitful. India and Uzbekistan have big possibilities for developing cooperation," Uzbekistan's President Islam Karimov said. The announcement was made in an interview with ITAR-TASS on Tuesday on the second day of Karimov's official visit to India. "We have reached understanding with India in the sphere of economy and culture, as well as diplomacy and in determining our common positions in order to ensure stability in Central Asia and the entire region," Karimov stressed. "We are connected with India, this great Asian power with a great potential, by both similar mentality and common historic roots. Now new possibilities for cooperation are opening up before us," he said. On Tuesday, Karimov held talks with Indian Foreign Minister Dinesh Singh and Finance Minister Manmohan Singh. He also met with representatives of Indian business circles and attended a ceremony at the Jawaharlal Nehru University where he was awarded an honorary degree of doctor of law.
