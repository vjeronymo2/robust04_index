920305 FT 05 MAR 92 / Algerian court dissolves FIS An Algerian court yesterday ordered the dissolution of the Islamic Salvation Front (FIS), two months after the fundamentalist party was denied almost certain victory by the cancelling of parliamentary elections, Reuter reports from Algiers. The decision, which takes effect in one week but is subject to appeal, closed off any other legal outlet for FIS militants who wanted to make Algeria an Islamic state. Mr Larbi Belkheir, the interior minister, had asked the court on February 9 to order the dissolution or suspension of the FIS, accusing the party of multiple violations of the law. The same day, the authorities ordered a 12-month state of emergency, opening internment camps to hold detainees. Algeria yesterday reached agreement with a group of international banks on a Dollars 1.5bn (Pounds 850m) loan to refinance its principal commerical bank debt, writers Alice Rawsthorn in Paris.
