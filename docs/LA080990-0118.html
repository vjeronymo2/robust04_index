August 9, 1990, Thursday, Valley Edition

WOODLAND HILLS TEAM BEATEN IN U.S. OPEN

The Woodland Hills Astros surrendered two runs in the bottom of the ninth inning and fell to the Florida Braves, 3-2, Tuesday night in a National Division game of the U.S. Open Baseball tournament at Galvin Field in Ontario.

The Astros, 3-1 in the tournament, complete division play tonight against the Fairbanks (Alaska) Goldpanners (4-0).

First- and second-place finishers in the National and American divisions advance to another tournament.

Against the Braves, the Astros scored in the second inning on a single by Rio Mesa High senior-to-be Dmitri Young, then in the third on a two-out double by Billy Morris.
