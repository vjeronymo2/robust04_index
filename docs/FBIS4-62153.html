BFN [NEZAVISIMAYA GAZETA unheadlined report under the "Visit" rubric] [Text] Responding to a question from a NEZAVISIMAYA GAZETA correspondent, Russian Federation Culture Minister Mikhail Shvydkoy stated that "according to Culture Ministry information," the Russian president had set off for talks with Chancellor Kohl bringing along none of the books and other valuable objects in the so-called restitution category, or in other words, those taken to the USSR from the territory of Germany during the war and during the early post-war years. Hence the rumors about Boris Yeltsin's making a gift to the German side of the world-famous rarity -- the Gutenberg Bible -- are just rumors. But, alternative sources report, books from the Gotha Library may still be delivered to Germany toward the visit's end.
