940719 FT 19 JUL 94 / International Company News: American Barrick profits jump American Barrick Resources, the North American gold producer expanding internationally, has posted strong profit gains in the second quarter and first half. Gold output rose 24 per cent to 947,535 ounces in the first half, with most of the gain at the Betze-Post mine in Nevada. Average realised price was USDollars 404 per oz sold, against the average spot price of Dollars 383 and with higher output, operating costs dipped to Dollars 162 per oz, compared with Dollars 172 per oz. Net profit in the June quarter was Dollars 62.4m or 22 cents a share against Dollars 57.2m or 20 cents a year earlier, on gold sales of Dollars 207m, against Dollars 175m. Gold sales were 513,412 oz, up from 427,741 oz. First-half net profit improved to Dollars 122.8m, or 43 cents a share, from Dollars 103.2m, or 36 cents, an increase of 19 per cent, on gold sales of Dollars 395m, against Dollars 319m. At June 30, Barrick had Dollars 382m in cash and short-term securities and little debt. According to Goldman Sachs and Salomon, Barrick will pass Placer Dome, Newmont and Homestake to become North America's biggest gold producer with output of 1.84m oz. It also holds one of the largest gold reserves.
