Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tbilisi, January 19 (SNARK)--The General-Major Boris Dzhukov, the assistant commander of the Russian troops in Transcaucasia, called premature the assertions that the Russian troops would provide with the security of the refugees, returning to Abkhazia. "Everything will be clear only after the meeting of Boris Yeltsin and Eduard Shevardnadze", he has told SNARK today. As it has already been reported the Russia's president visit to Georgia is planned on the first fortnight of February. The general also refuted the rumors that the Russian special purpose detachments would be located in the Tbilisi suburbs to maintain order in the cities of Georgia. Moreover, he told some units of the Russian troops are dislocated from Tbilisi and Kutaisi to Adjaria.
