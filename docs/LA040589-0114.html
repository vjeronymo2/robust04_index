April 5, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST: LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF;

REWARD OFFERS IN KILLING TOTAL $40,000

The Los Angeles City Council offered a $25,000 reward Tuesday and a teachers' union added $5,000 for information leading to an arrest and conviction in the ambush slaying of Harold Arthur, a popular Grant High School teacher.

The rewards, combined with the $10,000 already offered by the Los Angeles Unified School District, bring the total reward to $40,000.

Arthur, the Van Nuys high school's senior class adviser and a teacher since 1961, was shot at least three times in the back as he got into his car in front of his Sherman Oaks home on the morning of March 24.
