BFN [Text] The recycling of Ukrainian ammunition which has exceeded its expiry date is now being conducted by the joint-stock company "Alliyant-Kiev," formed in 1993 by the Shostkinskiy scientific and research institute, the U.S. Alliyant-Tecsystems, and Repirbase Ltd of Great Britain. The Ukrainian partner has 51 per cent of the shares in the joint-stock company. In the first quarter, "Allyant- Kiev" already received 5,600 tonnes of ammunition for recycling. The 127 tonnes of extracted gunpowder were sent to the UkrVybukhBud explosives manufacturer and the 250 tonnes of brass cartidges to a nonferrous reprocessing factory to be reprocessed into rolled metal. According to unofficial sources at the Defense Ministry, there are 320,000 tonnes of ammunition stocks in line to be scrapped.
