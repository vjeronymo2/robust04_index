Language: Romanian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] The authorities of North Rhine-Westphalia postponed the deportation of several hundred refugees from Serbia and Montenegro via Romania, an action that should have started tomorrow, the Interior Ministry of that land, quoted by AFP, announced. [passage omitted] The Romanian General Directorate for Passports within the Ministry of Interior told our reporters tonight that there is no understanding regarding the arrival in transit of refugees from Serbia and Montenegro. Tonight, at 2100, our correspondent received information from the Romanian Embassy in Belgrade that the Romanian charge d'affaires, Constantin Garbea, was received today at the FRG Foreign Ministry upon his request, where he presented the official position of Romania in connection with the deportation of the Yugoslav refugees from Germany and their transit through Romania on their way to Yugoslavia. It was noted that no understanding or accord between the Governments of Romania and Germany regarding the transit through Timisoara of Yugoslav citizens who will be deported from Germany has been reached and rumors that were circulating are groundless.
