Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Speaker of the State Duma Ivan Rybkin said that cooperation between the Russian and Kazakh parliaments should contribute to preservation of a single economic space. "We can encourage this process by lessening legislative differences," he told journalists on Monday after a meeting with Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev in the Kremlin residence. "We parliamentarians should prevent the legislations of our two countries, Russia and Kazakhstan, from getting too different from each other, so that it would be difficult to unify them later," Rybkin pointed out. He expressed the opinion that "political approaches to closer cooperation, to cooperation on the basis of equal rights and mutual respect for the sovereignty of the two countries are not far away. But, let me repeat, the path to that lies through cooperation in the sphere of economy." This was the main issue of the conversation with Nursultan Nazarbayev, Rybkin said.
