Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Vadim Byrkin] [Text] Moscow March 9 TASS -- Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev left Moscow for Germany today for talks on military cooperation between the two countries. The minister is also scheduled to discuss the withdrawal of Russian troops from Germany. Grachev and his German opposite number Volker Ruehe are expected to focus on European security and Russia's role in the partnership for peace project, as well as on interactions of their peacekeepers in the former Yugoslavia. Grachev will lay a wreath to the monument to Soviet soldiers in Tiergarten, and visit the 31st Bundeswehr Paratrooper Brigade in Oldenburg and the Lehenin testing site to get first-hand knowledge of Bundeswehr combat training. The visit will last through March 11.
