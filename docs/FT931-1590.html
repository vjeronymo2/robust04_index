930324 FT 24 MAR 93 / Security dominates Belfast meeting SECURITY issues including the Warrington bombing dominated the meeting of the Anglo-Irish conference in Belfast yesterday, our Belfast Correspondent writes. The recent upsurge in terrorist violence by the IRA and loyalist paramilitaries was high on the agenda. However, Sir Patrick Mayhew, the Northern Ireland secretary, said of the relationship between the Royal Ulster Constabulary and Garda, the Irish police force: 'I think that co-operation is the best means by which the two police forces can bear down upon these evil people, and I am very glad to say that co-operation has never been closer.'
