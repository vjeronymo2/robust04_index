BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Anna Melnikova] [Text] Moscow April 20 TASS--"For the first time all CIS countries have agreed to (pursue) close integration and set up a single standing economic commission for economic integration between countries," Russian President Boris Yeltsin said at a meeting with the heads of Russian regional administrations in Moscow on Wednesday. Summing up the results of the CIS summit meeting, Yeltsin praised Ukraine's decision to join the economic union as an associated member. The CIS leaders adopted decisions on 26 political, economic, border, military and international issues. The president noted that they had also discussed the situation in trouble spots and ways to settle conflicts there. Speaking about the situation in Nagorno-Karabakh and the Georgian-Abkhazian conflict, Yeltsin said "there are signs of real influence on the settlement of these processes."
