BFN [Text] Geneva, June 6 (TANJUG) -- U.N. Secretary-General Butrus Butrus-Ghali, who arrived in Geneva late on Sunday, early on Monday conferred with his special envoy to former Yugoslavia Yasushi Akashi and Co-Chairman of the Conference on the Former Yugoslavia Thorvald Stoltenberg, it was said in the U.N. headquarters in Geneva. Akashi gave Butrus-Ghali a detailed account of the efforts made over the past four days in order to enable the beginning of a conference on a comprehensive cessation of hostilities in Bosnia-Herzegovina, said a spokesman at the U.N. headquarters in Geneva. Butrus-Ghali was pleased with the fact that Akashi's efforts were fruitful and expressed hope that this would pave the way to a comprehensive political settlement in Bosnia-Herzegovina. Speaking on behalf of the mediators and the two negotiating sides (the Bosnian Serb republic and the Moslem-Croat federation), the spokesman has officially confirmed that the talks began at 11.15 A.M. (0915 GMT) on Monday with a meeting between Akashi and Vice- President of the Moslem-Croat Federation Ejup Ganic. Butrus-Ghali especially thanked Akashi for providing a platform for the beginning of the talks and promised to give him his full support in the forthcoming negotiating process.
