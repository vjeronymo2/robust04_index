BFN [Report by Ekaterina Kazasova] [Text] Sofia, June 17 (BTA) -- "There is every evidence that the position of the incumbent Bulgarian Government on human rights and the rights of Muslims in Bulgaria in particular is the right one," Dr. Ahmad 'Ismat 'Abd al-Majid, secretary general of the League of Arab States, told a news conference today at the end of his official visit. The visit, paid at the invitation of Bulgarian Foreign Minister Stanislav Daskalov, is the first one by a leader of the collective body of the Arab states to Bulgaria. During his visit, the guest acquainted himself with the Bulgarian Government's positions on the Arab region and on a number of international problems. "The problem of Muslims in Bulgaria has been raised at all my meetings at different levels," Dr. Majid said. "Based on what I have been told, I can draw the conclusion that at the moment all Bulgarian citizens enjoy equal rights irrespective of their religion," he went on to say. Concerning Bulgaria's request for assistance in removing the question of Bulgarian Muslims from the agenda of the Organization of the Islamic Conference, the guest promised to inform the organization's leadership about everything he had learned in Bulgaria and expressed his hope that the request would be met. The possibility of Bulgarian companies taking part in building the infrastructure in the region of Jericho and the Gaza Strip was also discussed with Dr. Majid. "Bulgaria has been maintaining active relations with the Arab countries, it has taken part in the building of their infrastructure and I think it is perfectly natural for it to take part in the construction in that area," Dr. Majid said. Asked about the stand of the League of Arab States on the latest developments in Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM], Dr. Majid expressed his hope that Macedonia would continue to develop normally, avoiding any conflicts and bloodshed. "No doubt what is happening in Macedonia now has a negative effect on its neighbours, Bulgaria included," he stated. Earlier today Dr. Majid was received by President Zhelyu Zhelev and Prime Minister Lyuben Berov. He met National Assembly Chairman Aleksandur Yordanov too. It was stressed at the meeting with President Zhelev that Bulgarian-Arab cooperation should be restored and activated. "In a historical, geographical and economic aspect, Bulgaria has always maintained good contacts with the Arab states," Kamen Velichkov, the president's foreign political adviser, said after the meeting. The positions of Bulgaria and the Arab League on some major issues in the Balkans and the Middle East were clarified at Dr. Majid's meeting with the chairman of the Bulgarian Parliament. Both sides agreed that Bulgaria would only benefit from the quick settlement of the conflict in Bosnia and Herzegovina. Meeting with Prime Minister Berov, Dr. Majid showed interest in the difficulties Bulgaria has got to overcome in the implementation of economic reform and in its transition to a market economy; he was also interested in the progress of negotiations on Bulgaria's foreign debt with the international financial institutions, government spokesman Raycho Raykov said after the meeting. Prime Minister Berov stressed the fact that despite the difficulties, Bulgaria's transition to a market-based economy is peaceful. The guest wanted to know if foreign investments are protected in Bulgaria; he was assured that they are and that Bulgarian and foreign investors are treated on an equal footing. Dr. Majid visited St. Aleksandur Nevski Cathedral in the centre of Sofia. He took a walk round the open market-place in the square in front of the cathedral where Bulgarian artists and craftsmen sell their works. He seemed to be especially interested in Bulgarian crafts and crochetwork. Tomorrow Dr. Majid will be visiting the Rila Monastery. He is leaving the country on Sunday.
