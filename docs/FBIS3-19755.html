Language: German Article Type:BFN [Unattributed report: "Withdrawal of Russian Soldiers According to Schedule"] [Text] Berlin -- The Russian armed forces have so far kept to the official schedule for their withdrawal. The government's representative for the withdrawal, Major General Hartmut Foertsch, announced in Brandenburg on Thursday [17 March] that 90 percent of the personnel and 88 percent of the vehicles and equipment had returned to Russia by the end of March. About 55,000 members of the military are still deployed in the new laender. A total of 250 members of the armed forces have applied for asylum in Germany. No decision has yet been made. According to a spokesman for the Berlin Senat, Michael-Andreas Butz, Chancellor Helmut Kohl and Russian President Boris Yeltsin will participate in the farewell ceremony for the Russian troops in August. The Chancellor's Office announced that a farewell for the Western Allies will also take place in Berlin in September "with high-level participation." Butz stated that the talks among the four victorious powers have nearly been completed. Agreement has been reached on the basic issues. There are plans to organize an outstanding farewell ceremony with a tattoo by the Bundeswehr and with the participation of the armed forces of the Western Allies.
