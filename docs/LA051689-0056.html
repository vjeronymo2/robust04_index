May 16, 1989, Tuesday, Home Edition

YUGOSLAVS INSTALL YOUNGEST PRESIDENT

A 39-year-old economist was installed Monday as the youngest president in Yugoslavia's history, taking over as head of a collective presidency.

Janez Drnovsek, a virtually unknown lawmaker from northern Slovenia, won 56% of votes in his republic in balloting in April that gave Yugoslavia its first directly elected president.

Previously, presidents were chosen by regional parliaments and just one candidate was put forth by Communist authorities.

Under the country's rotating presidency, introduced in 1974, the country's six republics and two autonomous provinces take turns.
