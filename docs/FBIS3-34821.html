Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] In a meeting chaired by Prime Minister Dr. 'Atif Sidqi today, the Council of Ministers discussed a report presented by Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa on the developments of the Palestinian-Israeli negotiations and the efforts of President Muhammad Husni Mubarak and Egyptian diplomacy to bring the viewpoints of the two sides closer. These efforts ended with the initialing last Wednesday of a document on one of the phases of the implementation of the Gaza-Jericho agreement. President Mubarak attended the signing. This was stated by Information Minister Safwat al-Sharif. He said the Palestinian-Israeli talks brought the two sides' views on the size of Jericho and Gaza closer together. The Jericho area is 60 square kilometers in area and Gaza 300 square kilometers. The information minister said the Council of Ministers stressed the need for Egypt to continue its efforts until the Israeli withdrawal from Gaza and Jericho takes place in April. It also emphasized Egypt's continued role in advancing the peace process on all negotiating tracks with a view to achieving a lasting and comprehensive peace in the Middle East. The information minister said the foreign minister's report also dealt with the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. The Council of Ministers stressed Egypt's adherence to the Security Council resolution authorizing NATO to use force against the Serbs if they fail to withdraw their heavy weapons from around Sarajevo. The Council of Ministers condemned the Serbs' massacres and criminal acts against Muslims in Bosnia. It called on the world community to deal a decisive blow to Serbian aggression and to coordinate with the Islamic countries to lift the arms embargo against Bosnia and support the Bosnian people in attaining their legitimate rights. The information minister said the Council of Ministers approved the draft agreement between Egypt and the PLO on economic and technical cooperation. It also approved a number of draft agreements on exploration for oil in various regions in Egypt. The information minister said the Council of Ministers also discussed a report presented by Interior Minister Hasan al-Alfi on the efforts of the security agencies against criminal terrorists. In the report, the interior minister stressed that the security services are chasing the criminals who have perpetrated terrorist acts at home and abroad. He said the capture of these criminals and the details of their abortive schemes will be announced. The Council of Ministers stressed its support for the security services in their all-out confrontation with terrorists within the framework of the law and the Constitution. The information minister said the interior minister's report also explained many activities for maintaining security and pursuing outlaws.
