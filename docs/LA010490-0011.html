January 4, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

PHILIPPINE PLOTS

Thank you for the excellent article on the former Marcos crony Eduardo Murphy Cojuangco Jr. (Part A, Dec. 26).

One does not have to be a genius to conclude which Filipino businessmen and politicians were most likely to have been involved in plotting and financing the latest attempt to topple the democratically elected Corazon Aquino administration.

As your own newspaper reported, ordinary soldiers were openly paid $15 a day during the height of the stand-off in the Makati financial district. Reports from the islands also tell of payments in millions of pesos to the rebel armed forces officers.

But one thing that these businessmen and politicians seem to have forgotten are historical lessons, ranging from Adolf Hitler to Manuel Noriega. Once these types of people are in the saddle, among the first that they will eliminate are the very ones who helped them seize power.

The other disturbing development during the latest coup attempt was the rebels' breaking of the time-honored code that the physical safety of the country's president not be jeopardized. There is a saying in the Philippines which roughly translates thus: "There is no limit to revenge. . . ." Now that the code has been broken, there is no telling what the fanatical elements of the other side will attempt to do. That can only lead to more misery for ordinary Filipinos.

EUGENE V. CORAZO

Granada Hills
