Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tokyo, Jan. 14 KYODO -- Prime Minister Morihiro Hosokawa said Friday morning [14 January] that recent movements in the yen's rate and share prices have been stable, though he said the economic situation is still severe. Hosokawa made the comment in response to reporters' questions on market developments in connection with the government's monthly economic report, released earlier in the day. The report said the nation's economy remains at the worst stage of the recession. The dollar opened the day at 111.80 yen in Tokyo, down 0.50 yen from Thursday's close, and the 225-Nikkei stock average, a key barometer on the Tokyo Stock Exchange, fell 90.91 points to 18,486.35 in early trading.
