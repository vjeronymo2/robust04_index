Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 10 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-Premier and Foreign Minister Qian Qichen left here this evening for Africa. He will pay a goodwill official visit from January 11 to 21 to Ethiopia, Eritrea, Sudan, Kenya, Madagascar and Mauritius. During the visit, said officials accompanying Qian at the airport, the foreign minister will exchange views with leaders of the host countries on issues of mutual interest, such as international situation, African affairs and bilateral relations. Among those seeing Qian off at the airport were Vice-Foreign Minister Tian Zengpei and diplomatic envoys of the host countries in Beijing.
