Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Israeli sources in Washington have expressed disappointment with what they say was a lukewarm response by the State Department spokeswoman, Christine Shelly, on the Israel Government's decision to outlaw Kakh and Kahana Hay. She was quoted as saying the decision should be seen as part of the overall effort Israel is making to meet Palestinian security concerns. She also said the United States is considering whether to put Kakh and Kahana Hay on its list of terrorist sponsors. Christine Shelly told reporters yesterday that Kakh and Kahana Hay, on the basis of their words and actions, have advocated and engaged in terrorism. Shelly added that U.S. law had provisions for curtailing fund-raising for such groups in the United States. [Jerusalem Qol Yisra'el in Hebrew at 0500 GMT on 15 March, in a similar report, adds that "Israel's Ambassador in Washington Itamar Rabinovich told our correspondent Arye Golan that senior State Department officials had reacted very positively to the steps taken by the Israeli Government in the wake of the Hebron massacre."]
