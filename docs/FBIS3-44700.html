Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Singapore, January 6 (XINHUA) -- Wen Jiabao, alternate member of the Political Bureau of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China (CPC), today called on Singapore Deputy Prime Minister Lee Hsieng Loong. Wen, who is leading an eight-member CPC delegation, arrived here Wednesday [5 January] night for a two-day visit at the invitation of Lee Hsieng Loong. During the meeting, Lee told Wen that Singapore was happy to see that China had rapidly grown in economy due to its reforms and open-door policy. The Asia-Pacific region has always benefited from China's prosperity and stability. Wen Jiabao said that the relationship between China and Singapore is getting closer in recent years and both sides cooperated well in many areas and at higher levels. He said that the three visits paid by Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew in 1993 marked the new development in the relationship between the two countries. The Chinse delegation also visited the Port Authority of Singapore and the Jurong industrial township today. Wen Jiabao is scheduled to call on Prime Minister Goh Chok Tong and Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew Friday.
