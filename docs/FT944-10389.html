941110 FT 10 NOV 94 / International Company News: Stronger markets help Brascan back to black Brascan, a holding company controlled by Toronto's Bronfman family and their senior managers, has reported an improved third-quarter performance, due mainly to stronger commodity markets, writes Bernard Simon in Toronto. Earnings totalled CDollars 31m (USDollars 23m), or 31 cents a share, compared with a CDollars 19m loss, or 20 cents, a year earlier. Revenues climbed to CDollars 1.65bn from CDollars 1.26bn. Brascan forecast a further improvement for the rest of this year and 1995, based on expectations of continuing growth in the global economy
