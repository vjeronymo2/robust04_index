BFN [Text] This morning in Madrid, Spain's King Juan Carlos received Mate Granic, Croatian deputy prime minister and minister of foreign affairs. Our minister also met Apolonio Ruiz Ligero, the state secretary for foreign trade. We have a phone report from Madrid by Andjelko Perincic: The reception at the residence of the Spanish royal family Zarzuela, 20 kilometers from the center of Madrid, was also attended by Spanish Foreign Minister Javier Solana and Croatian Ambassador to Spain Sergey Morsan, and Spanish Ambassador to Croatia Felipe Luis de la Pena. Sources of the Croat delegation said that the atmosphere was very friendly and the meeting lasted twice as long as is customary. Minister Granic informed the Spanish king about attempts to reintegrate Croatia's occupied areas in a peaceful way, and noted problems and obstructions by the Serbs in the implementation of the Zagreb agreements. He also spoke about the problems relating to the implementation of the agreement on the Croat-Bosnian Federation in Bosnia-Herzegovina and praised the Spanish blue helmets. They also discussed cooperation between the two countries, in which respect the Spanish king lent support, particularly to the development of economic relations. The Spanish sovereign also sent greetings to Croatian President Dr. Franjo Tudjman and the Republic of Croatia and wished our country speedy admission into the European circle of countries. At the Ministry of Trade and Tourism the two sides discussed exclusively economic cooperation and Spanish support for Croatia to receive financial aid as soon as possible. The Spanish side lent its full support to our initiative to be admitted to GATT as soon as possible, to obtain credits from the European Union [EU] PHARE [Economic Reconstruction Aid for Poland and Hungary] program, and to be admitted, with the credits obtained, into the EBRD [European Bank for Reconstruction and Development], IMF, and the World Bank. Everything is ready for the signing of bilateral Croatian-Spanish treaties: On mutual protection of investments and avoidance of double taxation, while agreements on road traffic and tourism are to be signed today. While I am reporting this, talks between Croatian and Spanish ministers of foreign affairs, Mate Granic and Javier Solana respectively, are in progress.
