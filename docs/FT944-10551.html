941110 FT 10 NOV 94 / Sport body expects Pounds 1.6bn from lottery The Sports Council said yesterday that it expected Pounds 1.6bn to be available for sport between 1995 and 2000 from the proceeds of the National Lottery. Projects ranging from local community sports facilities to a possible new national stadium might be funded by the lottery, which is first drawn on November 19. Sport is one of the five 'good causes' that will receive an equal share from every Pounds 1 ticket sold. Mr Rodney Walker, chairman of the Sports Council, the body responsible for distributing lottery proceeds to sports projects in England, said yesterday: 'Every town and village in England could benefit from a new lottery-funded sports facility between now and the turn of the century.'
