921209 FT 09 DEC 92 / UK Company News: Chiltern Radio Pounds 392,000 in red For the period to September 30, Chiltern Radio reported its first full-year loss since it began broadcasting in 1981 after what Mr Peter Burton, chairman, described as another difficult year for the industry. He added that the company had been trading profitably in the present year. On turnover of Pounds 5.27m (Pounds 4.38m), which included a full-year contribution from the west of England acquisitions, the pre-tax loss was Pounds 392,000, compared with a profit of Pounds 114,000. There was a Pounds 77,000 exceptional provision against its investment in Independent Radio News. Losses per share were 4.6p (1.5p earnings) and the final, like the interim, is passed. Last year's total was 2p.
