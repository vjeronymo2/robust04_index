BFN [Text] Foreign Minister 'Ali Akbar Velayati, who had gone to Sarajevo in order to hold talks with the Bosnian officials and to open the embassy of the Islamic Republic of Iran, returned to Tehran this evening. In an interview with our correspondent, referring to the tripartite talks between Iran, Croatia, and Bosnia, Mr. Velayati said: Expansion of ties between Iran and Croatia will play a positive role to reduce the suffering of the Bosnian people. Stressing the importance of agreement between the Muslims and the Croats and the signing of new agreements between Croatia and Bosnia, he went on: This agreement has strengthened the position of the Muslims in the confrontation against the Serbs. Referring to the opening ceremony of the Iranian Embassy in Sarajevo attended by the president of Bosnia-Herzegovina, Mr. Velayati said: During the talks with the Bosnian president and other officials of that country, they thanked Iran for its constructive role and unstinting support for the people of Bosnia. Mr. Velayati pointed out that the Serbs continue their aggression in other regions of the Republic of Bosnia. He warned the international community about the Serbs' new waves of aggressions in some regions of Bosnia, including Brcko.
