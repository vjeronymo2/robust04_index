920227 FT 27 FEB 92 / National Institute Economic Review: Motor parts industry lags far behind Japan THE PRODUCTIVITY gap between the UK and German vehicle components industries narrowed in the 1980s according to the National Institute. The report says both countries still lag far behind the productivity of Japanese suppliers. In the 1980s, UK components suppliers 'finally dealt with blatant under-utilisation of capital equipment, but this has not proved to be enough'. The report highlights deficiencies in UK training and education as underlying causes for low productivity. Mr Christopher Carr, senior fellow in strategic management at Manchester Business School, the author of the report, says that if the remaining gap between the UK and German industries is to be bridged, 'the 1990s needs to become the decade in which Britain grapples with similarly blatant under-utilisation of people's full career potentials'. The report says that 'the critical conclusion, however, is that while Britain may still have some lessons to learn from Germany in respect to manufacturing productivity, the much larger and still continuing gap is against Japan'. According to one battery maker, 'while the most efficient battery plants in western Europe were now generally up to six or seven batteries per man hour,' the Japanese were producing more than 10.'
