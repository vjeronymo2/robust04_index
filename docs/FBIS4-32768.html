BFN [Text] Bucharest, ROMPRES, 28/6/1994 -- The Agrarian Democratic Party of Romania [PDAR], whose representation in Romania's Parliament is ensured by only five parliamentarians, has decided to withdraw its support given so far to the ruling Party of Social Democracy, told a news conference PDAR leader Victor Surdu, on 28 June. Also, he said the Agrarian parliamentarians "will take advantage" of the debate of the opposition's no-confidence motion in order to present their stand on their own, while shunning certain "opinions" of the opposition. The PDAR leader said that the no-confidence motion promoted by the Democratic Convention ignored certain achievements of the ruling party. The PDAR will back it though.
