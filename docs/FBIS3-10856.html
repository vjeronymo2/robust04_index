Language: French Article Type:BFN [Text] Senator Bernard Sansaricq has reacted to the position of the U.S. spokesman on the conflict in the Senate. Senator Sansaricq warned the American diplomat against interference in Haiti's affairs. Bernard Sansaricq accused the U.S. spokesman of trying to stir up the crisis in parliament for unknown reasons: [Begin Sansaricq recording] The office especially reminds Mr. Schrager of the formal provisions of Article 41 of the Vienna Convention and of Article 56 of the Haitian Constitution. Furthermore, the office makes a point of comparing the intentions of the nation with those of the international community, wrongly informed that in compliance with Article 108 of the Constitution, the Upper Chamber has definitively solved the problem of the 18 January elected senators who resumed their activities as full senators and whose powers have been validated. The assembly of senators has indeed overwhelmingly decided to contest that matter: 1. By denouncing the New York pact. 2. By dismissing the office [not further identified] irregularly acclaimed on 10 August 1993 under direct pressure by Dante Caputo and Leandro Despuy. As a consequence, the office would like the immediate suspension of all insinuations, statements, or provocations that worsen the situation of conflict for the benefit of unrevealed interests. [Signed] Bernard Sansaricq, president of the Senate. [end recording]
