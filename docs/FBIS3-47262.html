Language: English Article Type:BFN [Station commentary] [Text] Six delegations of the United State Congress and large American companies are expected in Vietnam during the first two weeks of January. Currently, a delegation of the U.S. Senate Energy and Natural Resources Commission [as heard] is in Hanoi. Here is our radio editor's opinion: From now to the middle of January, five United States delegations will come to Vietnam. They will represent large United States companies such as IBM and General Motors, and include the delegation of the Defense Subcommittee of the United States Senate, a delegation of the Asia-Pacific Subcommittee of the U.S. House of Representatives led by Russell Wilson, a delegation under Senator John Kerry, and a delegation led by Admiral Charles Larson, commander-in-chief of the U.S. Asia-Pacific command. According to Western news agencies, the delegations will visit Vietnam to find out possibilities of cooperation and for a precise assessment of the renovation process in Vietnam. Through their visits, they expect to make a recommendation to President Clinton to remove the 20-year-long economic embargo against Vietnam. United States Senator Bennett Johnston, head of a delegation from the U.S. Senate Energy and Natural Resources Commission, last Saturday [8 January] called on the United States President to remove the embargo. He said in Hanoi that it was time to close the door on the past and move toward normalizing of relations and cooperation. Speaking to a REUTERS correspondent in Hanoi, he hoped the U.S. President would quickly remove the sanction against Vietnam. Senator Don Nickles, a Republican Party member of the same delegation, also said he wants the United States President to lift the embargo. The voice of the United States politicians demanding the lifting of the embargo will become stronger in 1994. The American delegations to visit Vietnam in the coming days will surely have the same voice as the delegation of Senator Johnston. It is an issue President Clinton has to consider and solve.
