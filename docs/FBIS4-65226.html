BFN [Text] The talks on the confidence-building measures [CBM's] continue in Vienna. Gustave Feissel, the UN secretary general's alternate special representative, continued the talks with Turkish Cypriot leader Rauf Denktas' envoys in the presence of Robert Lamb, the U.S. State Department special coordinator on the Cyprus issue, and Tugay Ulucevik, the Turkish deputy foreign minister. Lamb told the Cyprus Broadcasting Corporation that he and Ulucevik were just observers in the talks. He added that today's talks focused on a detailed analysis of the 21 March UN document. He avoided evaluating the talks' results. The talks began at 1030 today, stopped at 1500 and resumed at 1600. Acting government spokesman Spiros Arotis said nothing happened in yesterday's seven-hour talks. He added that the government is being kept informed.
