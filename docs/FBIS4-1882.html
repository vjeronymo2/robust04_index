BFN [Text] Beijing, April 5 (XINHUA) -- Chinese Vice-President Rong Yiren met Peter Tapsell, speaker of the New Zealand Parliament, and his party in Beijing today. Rong said that China and New Zealand should enhance friendly relations, cooperation and exchanges so as to contribute to peace and stability in Asia and the Pacific region. He said he expected more entrepreneurs of the two countries to increase investments in the other country, to promote economic relations and trade. Rong Yiren visited New Zealand in 1991. Tapsell said that his country and China have long-term friendly relations. China was now in a period of reforms and opening to the outside world, and New Zealand, which is also observing changes, puts its hope in the Asian and Pacific region.
