941012 FT 12 OCT 94 / UK Company News: Inchcape sells M5 service area to management Inchcape, the international motors, services and marketing group, has sold the services area on the M5 motorway at Strensham, Worcestershire, to its management for about Pounds 12m, writes Andrew Bolger. Inchcape acquired the site with TKM, the motor dealer it bought for Pounds 383m in 1992. The Strensham management was backed by the Birmingham office of 3i, the investment capital group, and advised by Coopers & Lybrand and Edge and Ellison in Birmingham. Strensham, which will now trade under the TakeaBreak name, is situated on both sides of the M5 motorway, and employs 209 full-time staff and 65 part-timers, catering for 6m visitors annually. 3i led the equity element of the total finance requirement of Pounds 16.8m, providing Pounds 4m.
