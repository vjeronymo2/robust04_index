Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Ukrainian President Leonid Kravchuk is likely to arrive in Moscow on January 14 to meet with U.S. President Bill Clinton and Russian President Boris Yeltsin. According to a well-informed source, Bill Clinton had invited Leonid Kravchuk to attend the Russian-American summit. On January 3, tripartite talks began in Washington between U.S., Russian, and Ukrainian delegations. The talks will continue until January 8. The participants are discussing details of an agreement specifying the main aspects of nuclear disarmament, which is likely to be signed on January 14. In an interview with Interfax, Ukrainian Vice Premier Valeriy Shmarov, who is in charge of the military-industrial complex, expressed hope that "during the talks with the U.S. and Russia we shall pass over to initialing specific documents," above all the agreement to be signed by the three presidents. He pointed out that this document would enumerate guarantees given to Ukraine and specify which material, technical, and economic assistance Ukraine will be provided with during nuclear disarmament.
