BFN [Text] Beijing, April 11 (XINHUA) -- China's first labor law is now under formulation and is expected to be enacted this year. Xue Zhaoyun, vice-chairwoman of the All-China Federation of Trade Unions (ACFTU), told a press conference here today that the draft labor law prepared by the Labor Ministry, the State Council's Legislative Affairs Bureau and ACFTU, has been approved by the State Council and has been submitted to the National People's Congress Standing Committee for deliberation. If the law is passed, it will be China's first labor law, designed to standardize labor relations in an "all-round manner," Xue said. The union leader noted that "brand new" labor relations are emerging in China to meet the needs of building a socialist market economy. And the law is expected to effectively protect the legitimate rights and interests of both employees and employer.
