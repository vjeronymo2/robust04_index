February 12, 1990, Monday, Home Edition

MORNING REPORT: MOVIES

Violence on Film Evaluated: The National Coalition on Television Violence, which computes the violence and assesses the pro- and antisocial thematic content in movies and TV shows, has picked its best and worst movies for 1989.

"Romero," "Born on the Fourth of July" and "Dad" were rated the most pro-social pictures. Tom Cruise ("Born on the Fourth of July") and Sally Field ("Steel Magnolias") were cited for their work. The "most violent (and) socially destructive" films of 1989 were "Bloodfist," "Tango & Cash" and "Friday the 13th Part VIII: Jason Takes Manhattan." CLAUDIA PUIG
