October 29, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

EXPRESSING SOME ROQ-SOLID POSITIONS

It doesn't take a genius to figure out why KROQ has been slipping in the ratings. Reeb acknowledges the deejays' talkiness, but he neglects to mention problems such as a skewed format that sounds like "New Wave Oldies" on the weekend and "talk radio" during the week.

While in London recently, I heard some great music on the air and in the clubs -- the kind of music that used to find its way onto KROQ in its "hip" days. Of course, the present management would rather play the Rolling Stones than try something new. It is unfortunate that the accountants and time salesmen who make up most of L.A.'s radio station managements insist on copying each other.

BILL BAUER

Santa Monica
