940521 FT 21 MAY 94 / Old foes unite to lead South Africa An apartheid-era justice minister and an anti-apartheid veteran jailed for fighting the system were chosen to lead South Africa's new multi-racial Senate at its first meeting yesterday, AP reports from Cape Town. The 90-member chamber, comprising 10 representatives from each of the country's nine provinces, will work alongside the 400-member National Assembly in governing the country for the next five years. Senators unanimously chose Mr Kobie Coetsee to be Senate president. Mr Govan Mbeki, who was in prison with Mr Nelson Mandela while Mr Coetsee was rising through the ranks of the National party, was unanimously chosen deputy president of the Senate. The Senate will have the power to initiate legislation and to block bills introduced by the National Assembly.
