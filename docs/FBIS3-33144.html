Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Brazil through 17 February: The Sao Paulo State Federation of Industries reported that the unemployment rate in Sao Paulo rose for the fourth consecutive month in January with the dismissal of 11,939 workers, a drop of 0.51 percent compared with December 1993. The unemployment rate dropped 0.57 percent in the past 12 months, which represents 13,453 dismissed workers. (Sao Paulo FOLHA DE SAO PAULO in Portuguese 9 Feb 94 Section 2 p 1) Sugar exports registered a record increase of 42.7 percent in 1993 with revenues of $773 million, compared with $540 million in 1992. The volume of export increased from 2 million metric tons to nearly 3 million metric tons. (Sao Paulo GAZETA MERCANTIL in Portuguese 12 Feb 94 p 13)
