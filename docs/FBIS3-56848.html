Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] [First unidentified announcer] Talks on security and economic problems of the Baltic states are taking place in Jurmala between the prime ministers and government delegations of the Baltic states. The delegations are discussing issues related to national security, power engineering, border protection, customs, consular relations, trade and agriculture, as well as social protection. [Second unidentified announcer] The delegations must agree on several fundamental issues of the creation of the Council of Ministers of the Baltic States and information bureaus of the Baltic states. The prime ministers of the three countries are discussing the problems of national security and power engineering. Apart from that, bilateral talks are scheduled between Latvian and Lithuanian representatives on the construction of the Liepaja oil terminal as well as on the Latvian-Lithuanian sea border. [First announcer] As early as on 18 January, after a meeting with Estonian Prime Minister Mart Laar, Adolfas Slezevicius, head of the Lithuanian Government, stated that power engineering was a priority direction in cooperation between the Baltic states. Slezevicius and Laar were mainly speaking about the strengthening of cooperation in the sphere of power engineering, as well as the increasing of power engineering autonomy and the possibility of importing energy resources from the West.
