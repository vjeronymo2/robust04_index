September 27, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

REASONS FOR S&L CRISIS

If we lived in a true democracy, here is how the bill for the savings and loan mess might be distributed. Those in Washington who made it possible through legislation and those who owned/managed the S&Ls that failed, would be stripped of all they own to pay the bill.

Then those who had $100,000 or more on deposit would be taxed a certain percentage, going on down to lesser taxes for those who had only a few thousand on deposit. And the poor, urban dweller who never even heard of an S&L? His tax bill for this would be zero.

Unfortunately, we have something that interferes with such a democratic spreading of the bill -- they are called politicians!

FRANK W. HEISER

Riverside
