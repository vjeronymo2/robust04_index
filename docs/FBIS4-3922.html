BFN [Text] Yichang, Hubei Province, April 24 (XINHUA) -- China started construction on the Three Gorges Dam as ground was broken on a temporary set of ship locks here this morning in Yichang, central China's Hubei Province. So far, earth work on three big projects for the Three Gorges Dam has begun on one side of the dam, including permanent ship locks, a power station and the temporary ship lock and lift system. Earth and rock work for these three projects adds up to 39.24 million cubic meters, accounting for one-third of the dam's total. The first phase of the permanent ship locks work, which started on April 17, is 3,800 meters long and nearly 100 meters in depth. The two-way five-staged locking and lifting system will have an annual handling capacity of 50 million tons and is to be the biggest of its sort in the world. The one-way one-staged temporary ship locks with a handling capacity of 3,000 tons aims to ensure normal water traffic during the dam's construction.
