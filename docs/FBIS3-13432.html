Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, March 14, IRNA -- Iran's representative to the OPEC board of governors, Hoseyn Kazempur Ardebili, has been proposed for the post of the secretary general of the organization. The three-year term of the present OPEC secretary general, Ahmad Subroto, is to terminate in the next few months. The new secretary general will be elected in next OPEC session to be held in Vienna on March 25. According to the oil minister, Gholamreza Aqazadeh, if a rotating policy is adopted, it will be Iran's turn to chair the organization. However, he added, in case an alphabetic order is followed, the representative of Algeria would be elected. Kazempur Ardebili is presently Iran's ambassador in Tokyo and is an expert in international oil policy. He has cooperated with OPEC for nine years as Iran's representative. He has also served as deputy oil minister for international affairs and head of the National Iranian Oil Company (NIOC) for two years. Meanwhile, Venezuela and Algeria have also proposed their candidates for the post.
