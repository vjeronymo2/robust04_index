Language: Portuguese Article Type:BFN [Text] An African National Congress [ANC] delegation arrived in Luanda today on an official visit at the invitation of the MPLA [Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola]. The delegation is headed by Tokyo Sexwale, a member of the ANC Executive Committee. Sexwale was welcomed by MPLA Secretary General Lopo do Nascimento. Speaking to journalists, Sexwale said he was particularly optimistic about the future of the South African people. [Begin Sexwale recording, in English fading into Portuguese translation] We believe a democratic South Africa will play an important role in the region and will go a long way in helping the people of Angola themselves to solve their problems. [end recording] This afternoon, the ANC delegation called at the MPLA headquarters to learn about the activities carried out by the party that won the first multiparty elections in Angola. Lopo do Nascimento discussed the activities of his party, leading to the September 1992 victory at the polls. He blamed the National Union for the Total Independence of Angola for the deplorable state of the country. One of the main goals of the ANC delegation's visit to Angola is to learn about the Angolan elections because the first multiracial elections will be held in South Africa soon. Also today, the ANC delegation went to the Congressional Palace, where MPLA members of parliament told them about electoral systems. Tomorrow, the ANC delegation is scheduled to visit Benguela Province.
