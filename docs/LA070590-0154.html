July 5, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

PICK OF THE WEEK

A re-creation of famous events in American history, sculpted from 380,000 pounds of sand, is at the center court of Santa Anita Fashion Park through September.

The sand sculptures include a 20-foot-tall replica of the Statue of Liberty, and life-size figures of Thomas Edison holding a light bulb and Alexander Graham Bell making the first telephone call.

Abraham Lincoln is posed at a podium reading the Gettysburg Address.

Events to be held in conjunction with the display include a program by the Talent Towners, ages 8 to 18, who will sing and dance at 1 p.m. Saturday. From noon to 2 p.m. July 14, 20 children from the Foothill Academy of Gymnastics will perform routines on mats and the balance beam.

Fashion Park, at 400 S. Baldwin Ave., Arcadia, is open from 10 a.m. to 9 p.m. weekdays, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Saturdays and 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. Sundays.
