BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Mikhail Shevtsov] [Text] Moscow May 30 TASS -- Russia intends to join NATO's Partnership for Peace Programme in two to three weeks, Russian Defence Minister Pavel Grachev said. Speaking at a meeting with parliamentary state secretary of the German Defence Ministry Michaela Geiger in Moscow on Monday, Grachev said that the final date of signing will depend on how soon Russia's proposals are considered and approved by NATO. Grachev stressed that the results of his visit to Brussels and his speech at the NATO headquarters have been approved by Russian President Boris Yeltsin. He added that technical aspects of Russia's accession to the programme will be ironed out through diplomatic channels.
