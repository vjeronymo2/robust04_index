BFN [Text] Istanbul, June 9 (A.A) -- Foreign Minister Hikmet Cetin said after a meeting with his Canadian counterpart Andre Ouellet that both ministers agreed all efforts should be made to bring peace in Bosnia at the soonest. Speaking to reporters following his meeting, Cetin said that there are no problems in relations between the two countries, and Canada and Turkey have undertaken cooperation in military fields. He said a number of joint economic projects, and the situation in the former Yugoslav republic were discussed in detail during his meeting with Ouellet. Ouellet for his part said his meeting was very constructive, expressing hopes that the prime ministers of both countries will meet at the soonest.
