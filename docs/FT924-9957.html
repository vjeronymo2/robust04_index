921103 FT 03 NOV 92 / International Company News: Teleglobe doubles to CDollars 15m TELEGLOBE, Canada's international telecommunications group which has a minority held by BCE, the Canadian conglomerate, doubled profits to CDollars 15m (Dollars 12m), or 27 cents a share, in the third quarter, compared with CDollars 7.1m, or 13 cents, last time. The increase was achieved on revenues of CDollars 126m, against CDollars 105m. Telecommunications activities accounted for the strength, while communications products and insurance systems showed a small loss. Teleglobe has begun carrying all international traffic between Canada and Mexico formerly handled by Canadian telephone companies via US networks. It also has formed an international consortium to lay the CDollars 400m Cantat-3 fibre optic cable between North America and Europe, and will upgrade an earth station in the Ukraine. For the first nine months, the group reported a loss of CDollars 68.6m, or CDollars 1.56 a share, after special charges totalling CDollars 91m, against net profit of CDollars 13.7 or 28 cents a share. Revenues advanced to CDollars 245m from CDollars 203m. Power Corp of Canada has sold its 50 per cent share of the Celgar softwood pulp mill on the British Columbia coast to Venezolana de Pulpa of Venezuela. Power said the book value of the investment was CDollars 22.5m, and a small gain was made.
