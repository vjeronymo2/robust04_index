BFN [Text] Nouakchott, 28 Apr -- Mauritania has stressed today her "supportive stance vis-a-vis the independence of the State of Kuwait and her sovereignty over her internationally recognized boundaries according to the international Resolution No. 833" and demanded a settlement to the issue of the Kuwaiti POW's and detainees in the Iraqi prisons. A joint communique released here at the end of the visit by Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad, the Kuwaiti first deputy prime minister and foreign minister, to Nouakchott stressed "Mauritania's continued abidance by the resolutions of international legitimacy relating to the war of liberating Kuwait [no closing quotation mark as received]. The communique added that the two sides, the Kuwaiti and the Mauritanian, have discussed the overall developments at the Arab and Islamic arena. On the other hand, Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad hailed the results of his visit to Mauritania and described it as "successful and fruitful." This remark was made in an interview by Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad with the Mauritanian information media before his departure from Nouakchott for Rabat. Shaykh Sabah al-Ahmad said the visit has paved the way for building strong and solid relations between the two countries. The two sides called for serious efforts to cleanse the Arab air and overcome the obstacles that prevent the strengthening and consolidating Arab and Islamic solidarity. They expressed their commitment to the Arab League and UN charters, and their respect for the territorial integrity and regional security of states. The statement said that the two sides expressed their support for the Middle East peace process on the basis of land for peace and the international resolutions calling for the restoration of all Arab rights, particularly the Palestinian people's right to regain their land and self-determination. The statement added that the two sides expressed their hope that the brothers in Yemen would reach a solution that would please everyone and safeguard Yemen's territorial integrity and stability. Regarding the situation in Somalia, the two sides expressed their hope that a settlement would be reached that would safeguard Somalia's sovereignty and territorial safety. In their statement, the two sides called on the international community to find a quick solution to the problem of Bosnia-Herzegovina, and expressed their condemnation of the attacks by Serb gangs to which the people of Bosnia-Herzegovina were exposed. The statement pointed out that the Kuwaiti first deputy prime minister and foreign minister had extended and invitation to Mohamed Ould Lekhal, the Mauritanian foreign affairs and cooperation minister, to visit the State of Kuwait that was accepted by the Mauritanian minister, and that Shaykh Sabah had expressed his gratitude for the hospitality and honor he was accorded during his visit to Mauritania.
