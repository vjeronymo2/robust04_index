931019 FT 19 OCT 93 / International Company News: BNP shares open at 18% premium SHARES in Banque Nationale de Paris (BNP), the French government's first privatisation candidate, yesterday closed at FFr283.90, an 18 per cent premium over the original FFr240 offer price, at the end of the first day of official trading on the Paris stock market. BNP's shares opened at FFr277.20, immediately breaching the 15 per cent trading limit for the first day of dealings. The shares were suspended for 15 minutes, only to resume trading at FFr282.50. They then rose to a high of FFr285.90 before settling down to the closing price. The enthusiastic response to the BNP issue caused chaos on the Paris market as brokers were swamped with orders for shares. Dealers estimated that some 2.25m BNP shares changed hands in the first two hours of trading and that around 4m shares were traded in the course of the day, representing 3.4 per cent of the bank's equity. 'It's been crazy, just crazy,' said one Paris banking analyst. 'It's almost like London used to be during the Thatcherite privatisations back in the 1980s.' The BNP issue, which was priced far lower than analysts had expected, was heavily oversubscribed by both institutional and individual investors. The French government was forced to reduce dramatically the allocation of equity to institutions, to try to satisfy demand from the public. However, it still rationed the number of shares given to individual investors. Banking analysts attributed the strong demand for BNP shares to heavy buying from institutions that had been disappointed by their allocations in the original issue. Some individual investors seized their chance to make quick profits by selling their shares. 'The offer price was so low that subscribing for BNP shares almost looked like a licence to make money,' commented another analyst. 'But the French government wanted to make sure that the first privatisation was a success - and it's done it.'
