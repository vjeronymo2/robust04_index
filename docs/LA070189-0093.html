July 1, 1989, Saturday, Home Edition

NEWSWIRE

Several possible rules violations in the University of Minnesota's men's athletic department, mostly involving former university administrator Luther Darville and the football program, are outlined in a report the school submitted to the National Collegiate Athletic Assn.

Darville, former acting director of the Office of Minority and Special Student Affairs, allegedly disbursed $197,897 in cash and illegal benefits to football players in 1983-88.

Darville, a native of the Bahamas, returned home during the investigation. Minnesota is trying to have him extradited.
