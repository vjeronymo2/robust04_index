BFN [Text] Paris, June 23 (AFP) -- France called for political dialogue in Algeria and elections as soon as possible, as Algerian Prime Minister Mokdad Sifi held a second day of talks with French officials on Thursday [23 June]. Foreign Minister Alain Juppe said France considered a political dialogue with "all those who agree to put their knives and Kalashnikovs on one side" was "indispensable". Speaking after a meeting with Sifi, Juppe said France hoped the dialogue would culminate in legislative elections "at the earliest possible date." [passage omitted] On Thursday, Juppe said political stabilisation in Algeria would depend on the success of its economic recovery programme, notably in improving housing and cutting unemployment. [passage omitted] Sifi also met with National Assembly speaker Philippe Seguin on Thursday.
