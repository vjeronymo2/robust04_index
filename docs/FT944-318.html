941229 FT 29 DEC 94 / World Stock Markets: Mexican stocks recover Mexican stocks closed up 2.67 per cent after a late surge - spurred by news of discussions for a Dollars 10bn credit facility to stabilise the Mexican economy - reversed earlier downward trends. The bolsa had a highly volatile session with mixed trading until the market received the positive news of discussions between the US, Canada and the International Monetary Fund on an an emergency credit line. Volatility, traders said, was caused by the recovery of the Mexican peso against the dollar. Investors sold high-dollar revenue stocks and bought into dollar-indebted companies as the burden became lighter with the peso's gains. The key IPC index closed up 60.82 points at 2,337.72. Volume totalled a heavy 1.9bn new pesos on 134.9m traded.
