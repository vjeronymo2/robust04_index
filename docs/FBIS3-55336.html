Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "Diplomatic Panorama" feature] [Text] In view of the agreement reached in principle on some important bilateral issues, including those connected with nuclear arms, President Boris Yeltsin invited his Ukrainian counterpart, Leonid Kravchuk, to come to Moscow, the Russian president's press service said on Monday. The communique says that a working Russian-American-Ukrainian top-level meeting will take place in the Kremlin on January 14. According to an understanding reached between Boris Yeltsin and Bill Clinton, the American president may make a brief stop in Kiev's airport, Borispol, on January 12 on his way to Moscow. At 16:00 on January 10 U.S. diplomats in Moscow announced they knew nothing about any stopover at Kiev's Borispol Airport on his way to Moscow. Their message was received by Interfax.
