Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 23 Jan (MENA)--Dr. Usamah al-Baz, Foreign Ministry first under secretary and director of the President's Office for Political Affairs, said that President Husni Mubarak and President 'Arafat today reviewed the PLO-Israeli negotiations in Oslo, the progress made in the Taba talks, and ways to achieve further progress on a formula to implement the Palestinian-Israeli Declaration of Principles. Commenting on the Palestinian-Israeli talks, al-Baz said obviously the climate in which the talks are conducted has improved and become positive and that agreement between the two sides is likely in the next few weeks. For his part, Dr. Nabil Sha'th, head of the Palestinian side in the Taba talks, said after his meeting today with Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa and Dr. Usamah al-Baz that important talks to overcome obstacles delaying an agreement on Gaza and Jericho, especially differences on the crossings and Jericho, were held after President 'Arafat's visit to Oslo and his meeting with the Israeli foreign minister. Sha'th added that President 'Arafat briefed President Mubarak on developments in Oslo and discussed future steps with him. Sha'th said that President Mubarak invited the Palestinian delegation to a working breakfast with President 'Arafat. Presidents Mubarak and 'Arafat then asked the Palestinian delegation to meet with 'Amr Musa and Dr. al-Baz to continue discussion of a plan for a joint move to resolve the problems. Sha'th affirmed that Egypt is exerting efforts, as it has been since the start of the negotiations, to resolve outstanding problems. The head of the Palestinian side on the Gaza-Jericho Committee said that the latest talks in Taba saw a serious, objective attempt to break the deadlock. He added: We submitted a paper in Taba that was the subject of discussion in Oslo, and thus, the attempt to break the deadlock began in Taba and continued in Oslo and then in Cairo. Sha'th said he will return to Taba after tomorrow, Tuesday, to resume negotiations with General Shahaq, who will be briefed by then on Shim'on Peres' view of the talks that were held with 'Arafat in Oslo. He added that talks on the civilian authority will be resumed in Taba tomorrow. He said that President 'Arafat will meet Shim'on Peres in Davos, Switzerland, on Sunday [30 January] and expressed his belief that the Davos meeting will help lead to an agreement soon. Sha'th added that no time has yet been fixed for a meeting between 'Arafat and Rabin.
