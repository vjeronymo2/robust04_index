Language: English Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Sofia, Dec 18 (AFP) -- Countries bordering Macedonia [The Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia -- FYROM] agreed on Friday [18 February] to set up a supply corridor to the country, to help break a controversial trade embargo imposed by Greece. Transport officials from Turkey, Bulgaria, Albania and Italy and Macedonia told journalists they had agreed on a road and rail link to the former Yugoslav republic, which would be coordinated in the Bulgarian capital. The creation of such a corridor was an "urgent necessity" after Greece's decision Wednesday to bar Macedonia access to the Greek port of Salonika, halting its oil imports, the country's deputy Transport Minister Yorgo Sundovski said at a press conference. [passage omitted] Greece's EC partners sharply criticised the trade embargo on Friday. [passage omitted]
