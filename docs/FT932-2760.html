930617 FT 17 JUN 93 / Observer: Departridge His 10 years at the head of the Bank of England, said Robin Leigh-Pemberton at the Guildhall on Tuesday, had been a bit like the 12 days of Christmas: 'Twelve economies diverging. Eleven Lord Mayors presiding. Ten select committee MPs a-questioning. Nine ERM members a-realigning. Eight trade secretaries a-trading places. Seven wise men arguing. Six chief secretaries cutting. Five monetary aggregates. Four chancellors of the exchequer. Three deputy governors. Two prime ministers. And one Governor of the Bank of England.'
