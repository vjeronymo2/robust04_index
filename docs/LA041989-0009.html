April 19, 1989, Wednesday, Home Edition

RACISM ON CAMPUSES

I'm a Chinese-American parent of four college graduates. I'd like to comment on the editorial.

From the day my children entered kindergarten through university, I implored that they mix as much as possible with their fellow students. White, Afro-American, Latino or Asian. I inculcated into their young minds that by being friendly to all races during their formative years, it would make the transition to adult life and into the multiethnic American mainstream much easier.

I told them it was not only the right way, but the American way.

My early advice was well-taken. Today, my four grown kids are successfully active in their chosen professions and well integrated into the American Dream.

MONROE LEUNG

Beverly Hills
