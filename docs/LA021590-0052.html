February 15, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

ERMA BOMBECK: MOM 'DOESN'T WORK'? HA!

She described herself as a "rare breed who made a conscious decision to stop work, stay at home and raise our child."

She went on to say that she's discovered it was a heck of a lot simpler to manage an office of 20 employees (10 with PMS) than to be a full-time homemaker.

The only problem, she writes, is, "How do you explain to people who ask, 'What do you do with yourself all day?' "

It's the oldest question in the world. People never wait around for the answer. They probably won't read to the end of this column, but here are some of the answers shared by my readers through the years:

Thaw hamburger in the dry cycle of the dishwasher. Worm the dog, play cards with a sick child who cheats, dress naked dolls, take phone messages for people with a social life, make Christmas ornaments out of lint. Nurse a dead fern that cost $20.

* "I spent an entire morning at the hospital when one of my kids swallowed a rock," wrote one mother. "When I asked her why, she said she wasn't allowed to eat any food before dinner and she was hungry. Surgery is scheduled for Monday."

* "We looked everywhere for Marlene's sock. It turned up on the inside of her brother's shirt during a social studies test."

* "It took a whole day to clean the stove, but it was worth all the chipping, scrubbing and asphyxiation. My husband looked at it and said, 'You missed a spot.' "

Cook 2,000 meals a year. Find everything that is lost. Wait for repairmen. Get sitters who won't do for minimum what you do for free. Change toilet tissue spindle.

* "The policeman pulled me over and said, 'I'm giving you a ticket for no plate on your car.' I couldn't believe it until I looked at my son and his friend in back. They had been playing gas station with it and didn't put the screws on tight."

* "My sons belonged to Little League, and I spent my springs on a lawn chair in a field. We ate supper at 4 p.m. and again at 8. I did my Girl Scout work while I waited for my sons to be in left field for the one inning they played to miss all the balls."

* "I fed the dog a new dog food and he threw up on the new beige carpet and it left a hot-pink stain. It took a folder full of correspondence from me to get the product off the market."

Make a meat loaf shaped like a heart for Valentine's Day, keep children from killing one another, check food daily to see what expired as I slept. Teach morality, respect, cleanliness, goodness, and how to say thank you. Drive. Drive. Drive.

What do we do all day? Even if people stopped to listen, they wouldn't believe it.
