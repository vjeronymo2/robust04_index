BFN [Text] Russia wants the next "ministerial" meeting on settlement in Bosnia and Herzegovina to be held in a week or two, a high-ranking Russian diplomat said in an interview with INTERFAX on Thursday. He said that the exact time-frame for this meeting could be defined before the end of the week. The foreign ministers and secretaries of Russia, the U.S.A., Great Britain, France and the EU troika (Germany, Greece and Belgium) will discuss these issues in Istanbul where they will take part in the meeting of the North Atlantic Cooperation Council (NACC). Russia seeks to invite representatives of the parties to the conflict in Bosnia to the "ministerial" meeting, to tell them about the results of the work of the contact group on the map of the demarcation in Bosnia and Herzegovina. After this the parties must have two or three weeks to think everything over and to settle the disputed issues. Thereafter, a long-term agreement putting an end to hostilities must be signed, the same sources told INTERFAX.
