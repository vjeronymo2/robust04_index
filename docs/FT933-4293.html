930909 FT 09 SEP 93 / UK Company News: Exceptionals lift Singer & Friedlander PRE-TAX profits at Singer & Friedlander, the merchant bank and property group, rose from Pounds 3.3m to Pounds 11.7m in the six months to June 30, but were considerably affected by exceptionals. The latest figure benefited from an exceptional gain of Pounds 2.6m on the sale of an investment, while last's figure was depressed by an exceptional write-down of Pounds 5.3m in the value of investment properties. Profits from merchant banking and investment management rose by 19 per cent to Pounds 5.7m. The group said all divisions of its core business had improved significantly. Property profits increased by 31 per cent to Pounds 1.9m because of lower interest rates and increased rents. An internal valuation of the portfolio showed no significant change since December. Interest and investment income fell from Pounds 3.1m to Pounds 1.47m as a result of spending Pounds 45m on loan stock redemption and a share buy-in. The interim dividend rose to 1.25p (1p).
