Language: Pashto Article Type:BFN [Text] Esteemed Professor Borhanoddin Rabbani, president of the Islamic State of Afghanistan, this morning received Pakistan's foreign minister, Sadar Asif Ahmad Ali. In the meeting, they discussed current issues and the need for the ensuring peace and security. Conversation and the exchange of views were also conducted on relations and cooperation between the two countries of Afghanistan and Pakistan. President Rabbani expressed regret and sorrow at the incidents in the embassies of the two countries. The Pakistani foreign minister emphasized the expansion and consolidation of relations and friendly cooperation between the two countries. He also said that the incidents which occurred in the embassies of the two countries would not have any impact on the relations of Pakistan with Afghanistan. In the meeting, he expressed the readiness of that country to cooperate to restore peace and security in Afghanistan and said: We do not tell the Afghanis what to do, and we are ready, at any moment, to play any role we can to resolve the problems of Afghanistan. He also said: We envision taking some measures to send food to Kabul. The Pakistani minister of foreign affairs thanked Afghanistan's security officials for preventing embassy officials from being killed during the incident at Pakistan's embassy in Kabul. Asif Ahmad Ali, who arrived in Kabul yesterday afternoon, left for his country this morning. At the end of his visit, he expressed pleasure with the result of his meetings and visit and said: The incidents that have recently occurred in the embassies of the two countries will not cause any change in the previous status of Afghan refugees who live in Pakistan, and we welcome, as in the past, Afghan refugees as our guests as long as they do not return to their homeland.
