Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Cairo, 13 Jan (MENA)--Arab League Secretary General Dr. 'Ismat 'Abd-al-Majid met French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe and his delegation today. Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid said afterward that they discussed several important issues, including the Middle East peace process, the Libyan-Western problem, Bosnia, and the Euro-Arab dialogue. He added that they exchanged views on promoting ties between France and Arab countries and discussed cooperation among the Mediterranean basin countries in light of France's relations with European and Arab countries and with the Mediterranean countries in general. He said he explained the Arab view on the Lockerbie issue and expressed his appreciation of Egyptian and Tunisian efforts in this regard. He called for not escalating the situation and for treating Libya in a manner that will preserve its rights. On Middle East peace, he said they discussed France's support for the peace process. The French side, he added, asserted its concern that the timetable agreed by the Israeli and Palestinian sides should be respected and that peace should be comprehensive and just. As for Iraq, Dr. 'Abd-al-Majid said he and the French minister exchanged views on it. He affirmed that the Arab League wants to ensure Iraq's unity and territorial integrity and the need for Iraq to respect Security Council resolutions and Kuwait's sovereignty as an independent state. Asked about the crisis in Yemen, the Arab League secretary general said: We are in touch with the brothers in Yemen and the dialogue committee will pursue its task. He noted all the Yemeni parties' interest in unity as a primary principle and in finding a solution within the general framework of the agreement between President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and his deputy 'Ali Salim al-Bid. He said he would not hesitate if he were given the chance to go to Yemen to participate in the reconciliation efforts.
