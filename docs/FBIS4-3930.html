BFN [Text] Shanghai, April 26 (XINHUA) -- The Pudong New Area in Shanghai, the largest economic development zone in China, reported an import and export volume of 575 million U.S. dollars in the first quarter of this year, a 128.17 percent increase over the same period of last year, according to the latest information from Shanghai customs. The area registered an import value of 305 million U.S. dollars, an increase of 132.8 percent; and an export value of 270 million U.S. dollars, an increase of 123.1 percent, according to the customs office. The number of countries and regions having trade relations with the area has grown from 106 in the corresponding period of last year to 136. Some developed countries have become major trade partners of the area. In the first quarter of this year, the area had a trade value of 151 million U.S. dollars with Japan, an increase of 118 percent over the same period of last year; a trade value of 117 million U.S. dollars with European Community countries, an increase of 148 percent; a trade value of 75 million U.S. dollars with the United States, an increase of 188 percent; and a trade value of 52 million U.S. dollars with Hong Kong, an increase of 108 percent. Foreign-funded enterprises have become a major force behind the development of the area's foreign trade. In 1993, they reported an import and export value of more than one billion U.S. dollars, 41 percent of the area's total. In addition, entrepot trade in the area, which is a bonded zone, is booming.
