November 19, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

AN ACTIVE ALLIANCE OF CHEFS

Regarding the Nov. 5 articles that appeared under the headline "Women Chefs: Checking In on the Revolution": It was great to see such extensive pieces on women chefs. However, I was angered by Elka Gilmore's assertion that the Women's Culinary Alliance had "disintegrated into a catering coffee klatch."

As a documentary film maker currently working with the WCA, I can tell you that the alliance is an active, committed group of professionals working in all aspects of food service. The organization not only provides support to its members but is active in community affairs.

WCA members were involved in the American Wine and Food Festival benefiting Meals-on-Wheels. The group sponsors Dining for Dollars, which last year raised more than $30,000 for AIDS Project Los Angeles, and it is working with Avery Restaurant Supply to renovate the Union Rescue Mission's kitchen.

These are hardly the pursuits of a "catering coffee klatch."

WENDY LEVY

Santa Monica
