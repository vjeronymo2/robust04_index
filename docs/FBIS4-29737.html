BFN [Text] Tokyo, June 20 KYODO -- The Japan-U.S. Economic Council on Monday [20 June] formally elected Yotaro Kobayashi, chairman of Fuji Xerox Co., as the new council chief, council officials said Monday. Kobayashi replaces Akio Morita, the former chairman of Sony Corp. who is currently undergoing medical treatment after suffering a cerebral hemorrhage late last year, the officials said. The Council, which is made up of influential Japanese business leaders, is a preparatory organization for the annual Japan-U.S. business conference which holds debates on trade and economic problems between the two countries. The three-day conference will be held in Tokyo from July 10. Kobayashi told a press conference after the meeting that the Council will create objective standards by which to gauge the progress of market-opening measures, and will jointly establish a working group for discussions on deregulation by both countries at the next conference.
