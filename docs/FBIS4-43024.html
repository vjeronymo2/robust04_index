BFN [Text] Government spokesman Ioannis Kasoulidhis today said that yesterday, President Glavkos Kliridhis sent personal letters to U.S. President Bill Clinton and British Prime Minister John Major on the latest developments in the Cyprus issue and the continuing negotiations at the UN Security Council. Asked why the letters were sent to these two specific leaders, Kasoulidhis said the United States and Britain are the two powers that have recently moved toward the alternative of continuing the confidence-building measures [CBM's]. Kasoulidhis said: Kliridhis' letters aim to promote our side's positions. Answering another question, the spokesman said Kliridhis' letter to Russian President Boris Yeltsin, which was conveyed by Foreign Minister Alekos Mikhailidhis during his visit in Moscow, had the same goal. The spokesman said meetings have been held on the same issue with France and China. President Kliridhis and the foreign minister met the U.S. and British ambassadors in Nicosia.
