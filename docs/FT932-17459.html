930401 FT 01 APR 93 / UN halts Bosnia evacuation after nine more die THE United Nations has suspended the evacuation of Moslems from the besieged Bosnian town of Srebrenica after nine people, seven of them children, were killed yesterday in the crush to leave. The latest UN convoy, carrying more than 2,000 women, children and elderly men, reached the Moslem town of Tuzla after being delayed by soldiers. Before they left Srebrenica, the trucks were overrun by thousands of Moslems desperate to get out and two children died in the stampede. Five more children and two women were later found dead on the overcrowded trucks, Tuzla officials said. Mr Peter Kessler, spokesman in Sarajevo for the UN High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) said: 'We are putting this evacuation on hold until we get sufficient UN presence on the ground in Srebrenica, so we can have an evacuation in a dignified and organised manner.' Ms Lyndall Sachs of the Belgrade office of the UNHCR said: 'The level of panic is at an all-time high. The local community has lost all confidence in the ability of the United Nations to protect them.' Six Moslems died in a similar incident on Monday when UN lorries evacuated more than 1,300 people to Tuzla, a government stronghold about 70 miles to the north-west. Lieutenant-Colonel Tricia Purves, a British UN officer, said of the chaos: 'This is going to happen every time we send trucks in to evacuate people because so many are desperate to get out'. UN convoys to Srebrenica are allowed to take only light military escorts which lack the resources to maintain order among the refugees. When asked how many troops would be needed to control those among the 30,000 to 40,000 Moslems still trapped in Srebrenica who want to leave, Col Purves said: 'I don't think a brigade could control people this desperate.'
