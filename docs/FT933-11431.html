930729 FT 29 JUL 93 / International Company News: Ferfin's banking problems mount FERRUZZI Finanziaria (Ferfin), the Italian holding company staggering under total borrowings of about L31,000bn (Dollars 19.6bn), may be facing increasing difficulties in persuading banks to keep open lines of credit, in spite of expressions of support by foreign bank creditors earlier this week. A meeting yesterday of the five big Italian banks working on a rescue plan for the group, which also lies at the heart of a growing storm concerning alleged bribes to politicians over the Enimont chemicals venture, is believed to have proposed bringing in a third foreign bank as a 'consultant'. Citibank could join Societe Generale of France and UBS, which have already been asked to act as advisers to the Italian five-bank committee and to the Ferruzzi group, according to one banker present.
