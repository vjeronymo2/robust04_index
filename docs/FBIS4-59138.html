BFN [Text] Cairo, 10 May (MENA)--Dr. Usamah al-Baz, first Foreign Ministry under secretary and director of the President's Office for Political Affairs, has stated the need to broaden the Middle East peace process to involve every track, particularly the Syrian-Israeli track. Dr. al-Baz pointed out that comprehensive and just peace will be achieved only if all these tracks are included. Dr. al-Baz made these remarks during a meeting here today with a delegation of new heads of Israeli diplomatic missions to several countries, which is visiting Cairo. He stressed that Egypt is trying to overcome the obstacles obstructing the peace process, particularly along the Syrian-Israeli track. Dr. al-Baz explained to the delegation Egypt's position on various Arab, African, and international issues. Israeli Ambassador in Cairo David Sultan said the Israeli diplomats wanted, before starting their new jobs abroad, to learn Egypt's views on the peace process in the region and the negotiations relating to this process. The ambassador noted that the delegation's visit to Egypt will last three days.
