BFN [Text] Belgrade, April 23 (XINHUA) -- United Nations Special Envoy Yasushi Akashi, Serbian President Slobodan Milosevic, and leader of the Bosnian Serbs Radovan Karadzic today expressed satisfaction with their two days of talks. After their second round of talks here today on implementation of the cease-fire agreement in Gorazde agreed to on Friday [23 April] night, all three were satisfied with the outcome. Milosevic said that the agreement was "a big step toward peace", adding that the Republic of Serbia would continue to support a peaceful solution to the Yugoslav crisis through negotiation. Special Envoy Akashi said he believes that the agreement "created a possibility for avoiding a crisis". Karadzic said that if peace, de-militarization and the elimination of the possibility of Muslim attack were achieved in Gorazde, a real peace could be expected. At the end of 10 hours of talks on Friday, the Bosnian Serbs reportedly agreed to a cease-fire and the deployment of U.N. Protection Forces to within three kilometers around Gorazde.
