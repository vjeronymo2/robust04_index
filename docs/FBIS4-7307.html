BFN [Text] Tokyo, April 22 KYODO -- Yamaha Motor Co., Japan's leading motorcycle manufacturer, said Friday [22 April] it has established a new firm in Taipei to boost motorcycle shipping capacity from Taiwan. The new firm, called Yamaha Motor Taiwan Trading Co., is part of Yamaha Motor's strategy to make Taiwan a globally important production site of small motorcycles. Yamaha has produced 400,000 motorcycles annually at its local venture, Yamaha Motor Taiwan Co. of the total, 20,000 to 50,000 units have been shipped yearly to Europe and Asia, including Japan. The new firm was capitalized at 32 million yen, with 75 percent of equity shares owned by Yamaha Motor of Japan and the remainder by Yamaha Motor Taiwan, it said.
