BFN [Text] United Nations, April 23 (XINHUA) -- A cease-fire agreement has been reached today between the U.N. secretary- general's special representative for the former Yugoslavia and Bosnian Serb civilian and military authorities. According to the agreement, which is available here, both sides have agreed on "an immediate and total cease-fire in and around Gorazde from 1200 hours" on Saturday [23 April] and immediate start of negotiations on all military matters. They also agreed on, as a first step, urgent deployment of a U.N. Protection Force (Unprofor) battalion in an area of three kilometers from the center of Gorazde on both sides of the Drina River, from which Bosnian Serb Forces will be redeployed by midnight on Saturday, to closely monitor, supervise and report on the observance of the cease-fire by both parties. Not later than midnight on April 26, heavy weapons will be withdrawn out of a 20 kilometer radius of the center of Gorazde and out of range so as not to constitute a threat to the safe area, the agreement said. The two sides also agreed on the guarantee of secure conditions for immediate medical evacuation from Gorazde and complete freedom of movement for all personnel of Unprofor and humanitarian organizations.
