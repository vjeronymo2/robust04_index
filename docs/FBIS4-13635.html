BFN [Text] In their talks in Bucharest today Foreign Minister Shim'on Peres and PLO leader, Yasir 'Arafat, agreed to establish and man three committees which will operate during the overlap period between the IDF [Israel Defense Forces] withdrawal and the establishment of autonomy: a committee on routine security, a committee on joint patrols, and a committee on the transfer of administration powers. Our correspondent Gid'on Kouts reports that Peres and 'Arafat will meet again in an hour for the fourth time. They will then decide whether they will meet in Cairo next weekend. The two sides apparently set the first week of May as a target date for the signing of the agreement. In a speech before the economic forum in Bucharest Minister Peres said that the peace process is irreversible. In his speech, 'Arafat condemned acts of violence by both sides, saying the PLO is committed to democracy and a market economy. He again warned that if an agreement is not achieved, balkanization of the Middle East is expected. An agreement was achieved in the Paris economic talks between Israel and the PLO on free tourism traffic between Israel and the autonomy. At the same time, our correspondent Yo'av Tocker notes that the dispute on customs issues remains.
