BFN ["Carter-Kim Chong-il (replacement)" -- YONHAP headline] [Text] Seoul, June 18 (YONHAP) -- North Korean leader Kim Il-song stressed the importance of high-level dialogue between Pyongyang and Washington, saying that is where the two sides could discuss ways to make North Korean nuclear program transparent, Former U.S. President Jimmy Carter told South Korean officials Saturday. He delivered Kim's remarks in his talks with Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu after his arrival from Pyongyang, ministry spokesman Chang Ki-ho said. Han was quoted as telling him that the North Korea-U.S. talks should be consulted between Seoul and Washington after the two countries confirm North Korea's true intentions. Carter explained that reports about him telling Kim Il-song that the United States stopped discussing sanctions against North Korea was a result of misinterpretation, saying he meant the sanctions will be temporarily suspended on condition that the nuclear suspicion is resolved. The ex-president and his wife returned to Seoul after a four-day visit to North Korea where he met twice with the senior Kim for extensive talks on the nuclear problem. He came back with a message that Kim wants to directly meet South Korean President Kim Yong-sam "without any delay." Meanwhile, Carter said he tried to meet also with Kim Il-song's heir-apparent son Kim Chong-il during his trip but was denied. A South Korean official said Carter asked several times that he be able to meet junior Kim through North Korean officials but was refused, being told that Kim Chong-il did not have time.
