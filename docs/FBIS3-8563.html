Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Sofia, March 10 (BTA) -- The government adopted at its meeting today regulations for the Interdepartmental Committee on Environment and Development, set up last year. The document regulates issues concerning the committee's structure, operation and relations with other departments. The committee will help ministries and departments publicize opportunities to move and requirements for priority environmental protection projects. The ministers approved draft agreements on trade and economic cooperation between Bulgaria and Armenia, Azerbaijan and Uzbekistan as a basis for negotiations. They also approved a draft agreement on reciprocal investment protection and promotion between Bulgaria and Poland. At the suggestion of the Minister of Industry Rumen Bikov the cabinet gave consent to admit Ukraine, Moldova and Belarus as full members of the International Centre for Scientific and Technical Information in Moscow. The government decided to propose to President Zhelyu Zhelev to appoint Bulgaria's ambassador to Spain Mikhail Petkov also ambassador to Andorra. The Council of Ministers gave permission to the Embassy of Tunisia to open a polling station here during the presidential and parliamentary elections in that country due to be held on March 20, 1994. The cabinet returned a proposal to open new border checkpoints, one on the River Danube, and two on the Black Sea, for a reconsideration of its expediency and of ways to fund them. After the meeting the Cabinet held a lengthy closed-door discussion, said national radio tonight, quoting Acting Prime Minister Evgeni Matinchev. [passage omitted] In a televised interview tonight Deputy Prime Ministers Evgeni Matinchev and Valentin Karabashev dwelled on the problems of privatization and expressed their dissatisfaction with its progress. The privatization of major units such as hotels and banks is bound to provoke heated disputes in Bulgaria, Mr. Karabashev said. The Government should support those interests which are in defence of the national economy. In response to a question, Mr. Matinchev denied having pursued at government level the interests of certain circles in the privatization of tourism, interested in the preservation of the old structure of this economic branch. This year privatization in tourism may yield revenues exceeding 2 billion leva, Mr. Matinchev said. According to Mr. Karabashev, the government is to take measures for speeding up the process of privatization.
