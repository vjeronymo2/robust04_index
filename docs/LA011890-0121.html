January 18, 1990, Thursday, San Diego County Edition

HIGH SCHOOL BASKETBALL ROUNDUP;

MT. CARMEL NEARS RECORD WITH 132

Mt. Carmel came within four points of tying the section record for most points scored in a game by a boys' basketball team when it defeated Orange Glen, 132-58, at home Wednesday night.

Sweetwater holds the record, 136 in a 1988 victory over Coronado (48). Mt. Carmel passed the 130 scored by Sweetwater in a victory over Montgomery (51) in 1981.

The previous high for Mt. Carmel was 116 in 1979 against El Camino.

Seven players for Mt. Carmel had season highs and eight were in double figures.

Those in double figures were: Tom LaBuda, 23, Shane Knight, 17, Ryan Wootton, 17, Bill Landry, 16, Jason Beeman, 16, Mike Graves, 16, Matt Castillo, 12 and Jon Young, 11.

Mt. Carmel improved to 13-3, 5-0 in Palomar League play, and Orange Glen fell to 0-12, 0-5.

In other Palomar League games:

San Dieguito 68, Poway 66 -- Sean Howard scored 21 points and grabbed 11 rebounds and Brian Malewicz had 18 points and 11 rebounds for San Dieguito (7-8, 2-3) in its upset of fourth-ranked Poway (11-3, 2-3).

Fallbrook 54, Rancho Buena Vista 48 OT -- RBV (7-7, 3-2) led, 30-16, midway through the third quarter but Fallbrook came back to tie, 44-44, at the end of regulation. A three-pointer by Alan Chatman helped Fallbrook (13-4, 4-1) to the overtime victory. Chatman finished with 14 points, five in overtime. Chris Miller led Fallbrook with 16. Daryl Parker scored 20 for RBV.

Torrey Pines 84, Vista 75 -- Vista set a section record with 30 three-point attempts but made only seven.

The Panthers are 3-10, 0-5. Bill Scheibe led Torrey Pines (16-2, 4-1) with 23 points.
