Language: German Article Type:BFN [Report signed "stw": "Increasing Number of People Losing Their Jobs in Mines"] [Text] Duisburg -- Although the decline in the Ruhr economy -- which is particularly severely affected by the coal and steel crises -- seems to have been stopped, more and more people in that region are losing their jobs. Surveying 1,700 companies, the Chambers of Industry and Commerce in Bochum, Dortmund, Duisburg, Essen, and Muenster discovered that every second firm is thinking of reducing its staff this year. In industry, two out of three companies want to cut jobs. In 1993, more than 37,000 people in the Rhein and Ruhr areas lost their jobs; 13,000 of them had been employed in the mining industry. Since a large number of jobs have been destroyed by the escalating costs and structural changes, there will be no success stories from the labor market in the foreseeable future, the president of the Duisburg Chamber of Industry and Commerce, Hans Georg Willers, said. In the Ruhr area, 155,000 jobs have been created in the service sector over the past 15 years -- yet the same number was lost in manufacturing industry.
