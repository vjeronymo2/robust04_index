Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Text] Latif Nusayyif Jasim, chairman of the Iraqi Friendship, Peace, and Solidarity Organization, met today with Hugh Stevens, coordinator of the British commission for investigating international war crimes. During the meeting, Jasim reviewed the effects of the unjust blockade imposed on the Iraqi people in contravention of international and humanitarian laws and norms. He reviewed the Iraqi people's extensive efforts under the leadership of struggler President Saddam Husayn to reconstruct what the evil ones have destroyed and to launch the new construction campaign. Latif Nusayyif Jasim urged international organizations and honorable individuals in the world to continue their protests against the crime of the unjust blockade through activities that enhance the Iraqi people's steadfastness in the face of the unjust blockade. Praising Iraq's reconstruction of what the aggressors have destroyed, Hugh Stevens expressed great admiration for Iraq's achievements under the leadership of President Saddam Husayn in the face of the aggression. He indicated that such achievements would not have been realized had it not for the Iraqi people's rallying around their national leadership and love for their historic leader.
