931021 FT 21 OCT 93 / Costs of reshaping Border region GOVERNMENT proposals to reshape local government in a key area of Scotland will increase operating costs, produce high transitional costs and add Pounds 100 to the average D band council tax bill, the consultants Coopers & Lybrand estimate. In July a government white paper proposed creating four unitary authorities in Lothian and Borders regions, compared with the present two regions and eight districts. But the study commissioned by the regions says that this would cost Pounds 10.2m a year extra.
