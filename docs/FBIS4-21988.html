BFN [Text] Asyut, Upper Egypt, 16 Apr (MENA)--The centers and villages of Asyut Governorate have been the scene of intensified security sweeps to arrest escaped criminals and terrorists. Asyut Security Chief Major General Majdi al-Basyuni has said that the sweeps, which started early this morning and covered remote and shanty towns, resulted in catching 28 terrorists wanted in connection with various cases. Maj. Gen. al-Basyuni added that these terrorists included nine in the city of Asyut and surrounding villages and urban centers, eight in al-Qusiyah, six in the city of Manfalut, two in al-Badari, two in Abu Tij, and two in al-Ghana'im. The sweeps involved the State Security Detectives, Central Security, regular troops, the police, and Public Security forces. A total of 10 weapons -- two automatic rifles, five pistols, and three rifles of various types -- were seized during the sweeps. The teams involved in the sweeps arrested 197 persons on suspicion of illegal political activity, who are being interrogated, and 141 fugitive convicts. To facilitate full security control of the governorate, Asyut was recently divided into four security zones. Each zone is headed by an assistant security chief.
