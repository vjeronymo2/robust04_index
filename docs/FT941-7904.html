940223 FT 23 FEB 94 / Leak shuts down Russian N-plant One of four units at the Sosnovy Bor nuclear plant near Russia's second city, St Petersburg, was shut down yesterday after a 'small leakage of water', Interfax news agency said, Reuter reports from Moscow. A routine check revealed a hole in a welded section of pipe in the reactor's emergency cooling system, the report said. A leak at Sosnovy Bor in March 1992 triggered international concern, with radioactive iodine and inert gases escaping after a loss of pressure in a reactor channel. The Norwegian radiation protection authority said of the incident: 'The reactor is now in a stable, shut-down condition with normal cooling. There has been no emission exceeding the permitted limits.'
