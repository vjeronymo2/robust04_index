Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Tehran, Feb. 16, (IRNA) -- The visiting Georgian minister of foreign affairs, Aleksandre Chikvaidze, here Wednesday [16 February] delivered a written message from President Eduard Shevardnadze to his Iranian counterpart Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani. In a meeting with President Rafsanjani, the minister also underlined the significance of friendship and neighbourly relations between Georgia and the Islamic Republic of Iran. Chikvaizde said that in the light of the existing political undestanding between the two countries, Tbilisi is ready to expand bilateral relations in trade and economic areas. He briefed Rafsanjani on the latest developments in Georgia and voiced his country's interest to buy Iranian gas. President Rafsanjani referred to the Geographic situation of Georgia in the Caucasus and voiced satisfaction with restoration of peace and stability in the republic. He also welcomed consolidation of Tehran-Tbilisi relations and said that transfer of Iranian gas would be possible after expert studies in the area.
