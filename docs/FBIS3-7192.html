Language: Thai Article Type:BFN [Text] Finance Minister Tharin Nimmanhemin yesterday reported on the country's economic, capital, and financial markets to Prime Minister Chuan Likphai. During the meeting, he told the prime minister that the country's economy is progressing in a good direction. Some past problems have been tackled well. The Bank of Thailand is readjusting its economic growth forecast from 7.8 percent to 8 percent, an increase of 0.2 percent. The growth rate is considered quite high. The new adjustment is likely to be announced in April. Meanhwile, Tharin also said he has informed the prime minister that IMF officials would come to study and assess the Thai economy. The officials are specifically interested in the country's policy on wealth distribution to rural areas because they believe that Thailand has implemented such measures effectively. The IMF officials want clear a explanation of the policy so that it can be used as a guideline for conducting the study.
