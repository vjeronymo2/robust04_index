April 22, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

WOOD AND CRA PATRONAGE

Jim Wood has an obvious, king-sized conflict-of-interest problem, as your columnist Bill Boyarsky so aptly pointed out in his commentary on political patronage ("The Power of Patronage and the CRA," Metro, April 14). Wood should not have been confirmed for another term as a member of the Los Angeles Community Redevelopment Agency, in control of a $500-million annual budget, while he continues to serve as one of the top officers in the Los Angeles County Federation of Labor, AFL-CIO.

If Wood wishes to promote the full employment and economic well-being of the thousands of members of the AFL-CIO, fine, let him do so full-time, and not while he continues to hand out multimillion-dollar construction contracts while wearing his CRA hat.

There is another blatant case of conflict of interest involving Wood and the City Council. Wood, because of his high position, has a key voice on a special committee to determine AFL-CIO political endorsements. If it is perceived as improper for a council member's vote to appear to be influenced by a campaign contribution, how is a vote influenced by a campaign endorsement any different? An endorsement can prove even more critical to a campaign than a contribution.

After all these months of talk about a higher level of ethics and integrity being needed in local government, Wood's easy confirmation for another term on the CRA board indicates that the buddy-buddy political patronage train is still chugging along, right on track in City Hall, conflict-of-interest issues notwithstanding.

ERNANI BERNARDI

Councilman, 7th District

Los Angeles
