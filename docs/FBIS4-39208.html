BFN [Vitaliy Denisov report: "Will Bandits Now Have Difficulties in Georgia?"] [Text] Tbilisi -- A special-purpose paratroop brigade has appeared within Georgia's State Security Service. According to the service's chairman, Igor Giorgadze, brigade personnel have been trained at Russian special services training centers. The "special-purpose forces" include motorized rifle and combat engineer subunits and an antiterrorist squad. The "special forces" will cost the taxpayer around 5 billion Russian rubles a year. But, according to independent experts, the brigade will be capable of more than covering the expenditure on its upkeep. Provided of course that certain conditions are created for it to fight crime.
