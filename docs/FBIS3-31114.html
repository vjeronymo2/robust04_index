Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Zagreb, Feb 14 (AFP) -- Eleven battle tanks will make a new effort to reach United Nations troops in the enclave of Tuzla, northeast Bosnia, Tuesday [15 February], after being held up for nearly five months, the UN command said here. One hundred Danish troops will accompany the German-designed Leopards, the first heavy tanks to be used by the UN Protection Force (UNPROFOR) in Bosnia-Herzegovina, from Split in southern Croatia, the UN force said Monday. The tanks were moved to Split to try to reach Tuzla from the south three weeks ago, after being prevented for four months by the Serbian authorities from leaving Pancevo, near Belgrade, on the grounds that they were offensive weapons. Tuzla, held by the mainly-Moslem Bosnian armed forces, is being besieged by Bosnian Serb forces, who are resisting international pressure to reopen the airport there to humanitarian flights. The Leopards will first head for Tomislavgrad in southwest Bosnia, which is under the control of Bosnian Croat militia, pending further orders from UNPROFOR. They are destined for the 1,000-strong Nordic battalion of UNPROFOR in Tuzla.
