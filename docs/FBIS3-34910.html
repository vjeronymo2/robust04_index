Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Reports by Faysal Makram in Sanaa and Iqbal 'Ali 'Abdallah in Aden: "Aden: Socialist Party Talks About Foiling Attempt To Shoot Down al-Bid's Plane"] [Excerpts] Military sources in Aden have confirmed that the city's military intelligence (which is controlled by the Yemeni Socialist Party) [YSP] recently "uncovered a plot to shoot down the civilian aircraft scheduled to take Presidential Council Vice Chairman and YSP Secretary General 'Ali Salim al-Bid and his party's delegation from Aden to Amman in the next few days to sign the Pledge and Accord Document." They accused some central security elements attached to the northern governorates and working at the Radfan Camp overlooking Aden airport of preparing to carry out the mission of attacking al-Bid's plane with "shoulder-fired RPG shells" when it takes off from the airport. They said that "Brigadier General Salih Munsir al-Suyayli, governor of Aden and member of the YSP Political Bureau, ordered the expulsion of the northern members of central security from Aden to the north and the arrest of those who were planning to carry out the attack on al-Bid's aircraft." Furthermore, military sources in Aden say that "the forces deployed in al-'Anad camp (80 km north of Aden) arrested two Iraqis in the evening of the day before yesterday as they tried to infiltrate the camp, which has a military airfield and some installations." They added that "The Iraqis, who came from the northern governorates, confessed that their mission was to damage a number of military aircraft belonging to the army in the south and to hijack two planes to Iraq." These developments coincided with the republic decree issued by Presidential Council Chairman Lieutenant General 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih revoking Prime Minister and YSP Political Bureau Member Engineer Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas's decision the day before yesterday appointing Muhammad 'Ali Ahmad governor of the southern governorate of Abyan in succession to Colonel Yahya al-Ra'i, who was elected member of the House of Representatives. [passage omitted] The General Committee (Political Bureau) of the General People's Congress [GPC] issued a press statement yesterday citing an official source as saying that "At a time when our masses and all the sincere people who care about unity and democracy were looking forward to 10 Ramadan [20 February] for the signing of the Pledge and Accord Document to end the country's plight resulting from the political crisis, our people were surprised by the report carried on Yemeni television's channel two about the divisive decision signed by Prime Minister Haydar Abu-Bakr al-'Attas appointing Brother Muhammad 'Ali Ahmad as governor of Abyan, ignoring the constitution and the local administration law. That decision runs contrary to the principle of the state of law and order and undermines the homeland's higher interests." It appealed to all the political forces to "condemn that attitude" which it described as being "a violation of the Constitution and the law with negative and dangerous consequences harmful to the homeland's stability and democracy."
