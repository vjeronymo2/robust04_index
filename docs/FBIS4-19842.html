BFN [Text] The Prime Minister today paid tribute to the role that the Richard Nixon played in ending the Cold War between East and West. In a message to President Bill Clinton, John Major said he had heard "with sadness" the news of the former president's death. "I send to you and to the American people my sympathy and condolences," he said. "Richard Nixon will be remembered for his tireless work for a better understanding between East and West. He was a leading architect of the process which led to the end of the Cold War and his judgment on international affairs was widely respected. He will be greatly missed by his many friends and admirers on this side of the Atlantic." Contents of the message were released by the Foreign Office.
