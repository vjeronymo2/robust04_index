May 19, 1989, Friday, Home Edition

UAW FILES PETITION FOR ELECTION AT NISSAN'S PLANT IN TENNESEE

After 16 months of organizing, the United Auto Workers union filed a petition Thursday calling for an election at the Nissan automobile and truck plant here.

The filing with the National Labor Relations Board sets up a showdown between organized labor and the second-largest Japanese automobile plant in the nation.

Eleven union leaders and workers led by Jim Weaver, who has coordinated the drive since January, took a box of cards signed by Nissan employees to the NLRB office in Nashville Thursday morning.

Buddy Shonting, a technician in the trim and chassis plant, said, "I think we're going to whip the britches off of them."

Union officials had wanted to time the filing so that the election would occur on a favorable day for them either before or after the plant takes its regular annual production break July 3-17.

Company officials have said that it would probably take about 60 days after a petition is filed for the NLRB to hold an election.

Gail Neuman, vice president of human resources for the company, welcomed a conclusion to months of organizing that often left employees bitterly divided.

"We are confident that the great majority of Nissan employees will vote against the union," she said.
