Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Vladislav Ardzinba, chairman of the Abkhazian parliament, held a conference in Sukhumi on 30 March to report on the operation to eliminate Georgian saboteur groups active in the Kodor canyon, the parliament's press service reported. The conference was attended by Abkhazian Deputy Prime Minister Vladimir Mikanba and top officials of the Defense and Interior Ministries and the security service. An attack by Georgian gangs was said to have been beaten back and several armored vehicles and small arms and a large amount of ammunition captured. The village of Lata, Gulripsh District, was liberated. The press service also reported that remains had been found of passengers of a helicopter that had flown on a humanitarian mission from the besieged town of Tkvarcheli to Gudauta when it had been shot down by the Georgians in the vicinity of Lata on 14 December 1992. According to Abkhazian Deputy Defense Minister Nikolay Jonua, "the situation in the Kodor canyon has stabilized and no hostilities are underway other than local exchanges of fire with remnants of Georgian units that have escaped up the Kodor canyon."
