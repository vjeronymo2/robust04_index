Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Beijing, January 11 (XINHUA) -- Li Peiyao, vice-chairman of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress (NPC) of China, met with Senator John F. Kerry, member of the U.S. Senate Foreign Affairs Committee, and his party here this afternoon. The two sides exchanged views on topics of common interest. Li noted that as two influential countries on the Pacific Rim, China and the U.S. share many common interests and their economies are highly complementary. The two countries should keep their eyes on the future and intensify their cooperation on the principle of seeking common ground while laying aside differences, Li said, adding that this is in the fundamental interests of the two peoples and conducive to world peace and development. Kerry noted that he was pleased to be here and hoped to see bilateral ties improved. After the meeting, Zhu Qizhen, vice-chairman of the NPC's Foreign Affairs Committee, held working talks with Kerry and his party. Also this afternoon, Wu Yi, Chinese minister of foreign trade and economic cooperation, met with the U.S. visitors. Kerry and his party arrived here on Monday [10 January] as guests of the NPC's Foreign Affairs Committee.
