BFN [Text] President Fidel V. Ramos and [South] Korean Foreign Minister Han Sung-chu, on Monday, reaffirmed their commitment to work for peace and stability, which they said is a key to progress and prosperity for all countries. The two also vowed to further strengthen bilateral relations between Manila and Seoul through constant dialogue and enhance economic cooperation. In their 45-minute meeting, the president also reiterated to Minister Han the country's position on the North Korean issue which is for continued dialogue and search for peaceful approaches to the problem. The president told Minister Han, and I [newscaster] quote, we have taken a very strong supportive position, vis-a-vis the North Korean issue, [word indistinct] our region we are one of the most anti-communist here, unquote. As a member of the executive board of the International Atomic Energy Agency, the Philippines will support any initiative on the part of South Korea to diffuse tension between Seoul and Pyongyang. The president said, and I quote, again we are very much concerned that's why we are pushing for an encouraging maximum possible diplomatic and political courses of action that can bring about better convictions for peace and stability in the region, unquote. The president also thanked the Korean Government for the training and skills upgrading it offered to the Filipino workers there. Also present during the meeting were Korean Ambassador Chang-su Yi, Foreign Affairs Director General for Asia-Pacific (Yu Byung Woo) and Foreign Affairs Director for Southeast Asia and Pacific Division (Moon Tai Yang). They were accompanied to Malacanang by Foreign Affairs Secretary Roberto Romulo.
