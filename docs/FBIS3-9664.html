Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Croatian President Franjo Tudjman this evening received a film personality of world acclaim -- Branko Lustig, the producer of "Schindler's List" -- and awarded him with the Order of Prince Trpimir. [passage omitted] Tudjman said: As president of today's democratic state of Croatia, I take this opportunity to apologize to you and all members of the Jewish community on behalf of those who took part in the Holocaust and enforced the Nazi-fascist racist laws in the NDH [the Independent State of Croatia, a World War II Nazi puppet-state]. At the same time, it is with pleasure that I stress the historical truth that the large majority of the Croatian nation condemned all types of that criminal policy, and many Croats took an active part in the antifascist struggle, including myself, who, as a young man fought for four years. It is probably not a coincidence that it is a man like myself -- a man from the ranks of the Croatian antifascist movement -- who is leading the modern democratic Croatian state, but neither is the fact that your hometown Osijek came under threat in our time, and its surroundings suffered the crime of ethnic cleansing.
