930701 FT 01 JUL 93 / Fall in housing equity withdrawal THE AMOUNT of equity withdrawn from housing last year fell to its lowest level since 1981, the Housing Finance Review published by the Joseph Rowntree Foundation, the social research organisation, says. In 1988 consumer demand was boosted by Pounds 21.6bn by withdrawal of equity from private homes, with more than half the money raised through mortgages not spent on housing. Last year the figure fell to Pounds 3.5bn. The review estimates that 1.3m households were living in homes worth less than their mortgages at the end of last year. The average level of negative equity was Pounds 2,800. JRF Housing Finance Review 1993. Joseph Rowntree Foundation, 40 Water End, York YO3 6LP. Pounds 15.
