October 15, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

CURRENT PAPERBACKS: THE SACRED PIPE BLACK ELK'S ACCOUNT OF THE SEVEN RITES OF THE OGLALA SIOUX RECORDED AND EDITED BY JOSEPH BROWN (UNIVERSITY OF OKLAHOMA PRESS: $7.95, ILLUSTRATED)

Black Elk, the Sioux holy man, explains the enormous importance his people attached to what is commonly called "the peace pipe." The Sioux believed that their ritual pipe was a gift from Wakan-Tanka, the Great Spirit, presented by his intermediary, the White Buffalo Cow Woman. Its sacred origins made it an essential element in tribal ceremonies involving the purification of the soul, relations with the dead and preparations for entering womanhood. These rituals focused on the relationships that linked humanity, the Earth, its other inhabitants and the all-embracing Great Spirit. At a time when people are realizing that they may have irrevocably damaged the planet, the Sioux veneration of the Earth seems a worthwhile subject for contemplation.
