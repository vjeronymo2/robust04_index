BFN [Unattributed article: "GATT: Italian Farm Groups Criticize Accord"] [Text] (ANSA) Rome, April 19 -- A drop in exports, the compression of farm income -- with its consequent expulsion from the productive cycle of tens of thousands of farming outfits -- thereby leaving more and more farmland fallow and unproductive are the negative repercussions Italian agriculture must grapple with because of the recent accord forged within the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT). Italian farm organizations consider this accord insufficient and in a joint communique, Coldiretti [National Confederation of Small Farmers], CIPA [Italian Confederation of Agricultural Experts], and Confagricoltura [General Confederation of Italian Agriculture] today insisted that the new international regime for farm products would prove deleterious for this primary sector in Italy and especially for those particularly Mediterranean products such as wine, olive oil, olives, fruit and vegetables. These organizations are calling for a reform of the relevant joint market organizations within the European Union [EU] and revised guidelines which take into account the increase in EU resources that will be brought about by Community enlargement to include Sweden, Norway, Finland and Austria.
