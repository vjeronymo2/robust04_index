Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS parliamentary correspondent Ivan Novikov] [Text] Moscow February 2 TASS -- Russia can be admitted to the Council of Europe not earlier than the end of 1994, according to a delegation of the Russian Federal Assembly that recently returned from a session of the Council of Europe Inter-Parliamentary Assembly in France. The delegation included deputy chairman of the Council of the Federation, the upper house of the new parliament, Ramazan Abdulatipov, Deputies Yevgeniy Ambartsumov, Sergey Baburin, Sergey Glotov, Aleksandr Dzasokhov and Vladimir Podoprigora. Russia's admittance to the Council of Europe should be preceded by visits to Russia of the heads of some of its committees and council leaders, Ambartsumov said. He said the visit to France aimed at restoring ties between the Russian parliament and the Inter-Parliamentary Assembly which have been suspended after the 1993 October revolt in Moscow. "We think this goal was achieved," he added. Speaking at a news conference in Moscow on Wednesday, the members of the delegation said that Russia's admittance to the Council of Europe in being artificially delayed, which signifies that Western parliamentarians are concerned about the situation in Russia. Abdulatipov stressed that the visit "was a good start of coordinated activities of both houses of the Federal Assembly." He said the parliament should set up a permanent delegation of about 18 people in the interests of future visits. The delegation should include representatives of all factions of the parliament.
