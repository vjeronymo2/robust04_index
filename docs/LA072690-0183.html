July 26, 1990, Thursday, Home Edition

U.S. CEREAL MAKERS TO BUY BRITISH COMPANY

General Mills Inc. and Nestle SA announced an agreement to buy a British cereal company for about $168 million through their joint venture, Cereal Partners Worldwide.

A partnership between the two companies, to be called Cereal Partners UK, has agreed to buy London-based RHM Breakfast Cereals, a division of Ranks Hovis McDougall PLC.

The purchase has been approved by the RHM board of directors and the transaction is expected to be completed on or before Aug. 17.
