920428 FT 28 APR 92 / UK Company News: How dives to Pounds 1.3m as recession takes toll RECESSION IN the construction industry hit How Group, the Midlands-based building services contractor, which saw pre-tax profits for 1991 fall from Pounds 4.21m to Pounds 1.3m. The final dividend is cut to 1.5p to make an uncovered total of 2.5p (3.6p); earnings worked through at 1.82p (5.94p). The shares gained 1p to 32p, compared to a net asset value per share of 56p. Turnover fell to Pounds 223.9m (Pounds 236.6m). The group wrote off Pounds 1.5m for bad debts and made exceptional provisions of Pounds 1.02m for redundancies as the workforce was cut 18 per cent to 2,670. Engineering services reported a loss before tax of Pounds 2.16m (Pounds 2.42m profit). Mr David Summerfield, chief executive, said order books fell 30 per cent year on year. The maintenance division improved pre-tax profits from Pounds 519,000 to Pounds 1.27m. Fire services turned round to pre-tax profits of Pounds 504,000 (Pounds 67,000 loss) despite lower turnover. This followed the centralisation of the division to one site. Year-end net cash was Pounds 5.2m (Pounds 3.6m) while net interest receivable increased to Pounds 426,000 (Pounds 388,000).
