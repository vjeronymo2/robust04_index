BFN [Text] Hanoi VNA April 11 -- Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet held talks here this morning with Australian Prime Minister Paul Keating, now on an official friendship visit to Vietnam. Speaking at the talks, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet welcomed Australian Prime Minister Paul Keating and his wife's first official friendship visit to Vietnam as an important event, opening a new chapter in the relations of friendship and cooperation between the two countries. The Vietnamese leader informed the Australian prime minister of the achievements recorded by the Vietnamese people in the current renovation process and in the implementation of the foreign policy of independence, sovereignty and openess in the direction of diversification and multilateralization of international relations. He reaffirmed Vietnam's desire to strengthen the relations of friendship and all-sided cooperation with Australia on the basis of mutual respect, non-interference in each other's internal affairs, equality and mutual benefits. For his part, Mr.Paul Keating thanked Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet and his wife and the Vietnamese people for their hospitality. He spoke highly of the achievements recorded by the Vietnamese people in various fields, particularly economy in the renovation process. The two sides expressed their satisfaction at the rapid development of the friendship and effective cooperation in many fields since Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet's visit to Australia in May 1993. They also expressed their wishes to promote the multi-sided cooperation between the two countries, and held that the two countries could supplement each other in their economic development process for the prosperity of each country, as well as of the Asian-Pacific region. They exchanged views on measures to further strengthen the cooperative relations between the two countries. The Vietnamese side highly appreciated Australia's policy of attaching importance to the development of cooperation with Vietnam in trade, investment and other fields and its aid programme to Vietnam, especially Australia's experience sharing, technical assistance and personnel training to Vietnam as well as its cooperation in building the north-south 500 kv power line. The Australian side pledged to continue its aid to Vietnam, and said that Australia was ready to broaden its cooperation and assistance to Vietnam in the oil and gas industry, telecommunications, energy, water resources, infrastructure construction, healthcare, education, personnel training and preservation of Hanoi's ancient sector. The two sides also compared notes on international and regional issues of common concern. Early this morning a state ceremony was held at the presidential palace to welcome Prime Minister Paul Keating, his wife and their entourage who arrived here yesterday. Present at the ceremony were Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet, Foreign Minister Nguyen Manh Cam, Mayor of Hanoi Le At Hoi and other Vietnamese officials. This evening, Prime Minister Vo Van Kiet and his wife gave a banquet in honour of Prime Minister P.Keating, his wife and other distinguished Australian guests.
