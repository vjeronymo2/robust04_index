BFN [Text] Defense Minister Aftab Shaban Mirani said that if matters relating to the F-16 deal aree not settled with the United States, the world market is open for Pakistan to purchase other planes. Talking to newsmen at Larkana, he said Pakistan will not accept any condition in this deal that is against its national interests because there are other things more important than F-16's. Negotiations, however, are in progress. When asked about occupied Kashmir, the defense minister spoke of the heavy deployment of Indian forces there. He said, however, that our frontiers are well protected and we are capable of facing all eventualities. To other question, Aftab Shabaz Mirani replied that the prime minister has openly asked the opposition to end its boycott and return to the National Assembly. He said the government could still adopt many measures to end the confrontation and bring the opposition back to the negotiating table.
