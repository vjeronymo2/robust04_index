Language: Persian Article Type:BFN [Text] Reports from Afghanistan indicate that the clashes in Kabul have died down and that the conflicting sides are only firing at military areas. Our correspondent reports that the conflicting sides claim to have inflicted heavy damage on each other. A spokesman for General Dostam has said that yesterday a plane of the Supervisory Council attacked Bagram Airport, which is 60 km northeast of Kabul and is under the control of Ahmad Shah Mas'ud. However, a spokesman for Ahmad Shah Mas'ud has said that positions of General Dostam's forces and of the Hezb-e Eslami [Islamic Party] in the Bala Hesar Heights and Maranjan Hill were bombed several times. Another report from Afghanistan also states that our ambassador to Afghanistan handed a written message from Velayati to the Hezb-e Wahdat [Islamic Unity Party] secretary general yesterday. Also, the UN secretary general has announced his readiness to send a special political delegation to Afghanistan in a bid to establish peace. The UN secretary general's statement says that with the help of the Islamic Republic of Iran and other countries, a group known as the friends of the secretary general on Afghan affairs will be formed and will play a consultative role in the secretary general's decision.
