920822 FT 22 AUG 92 / Collor aides withdrew large sums Close aides to President Fernando Collor de Mello withdrew large sums just before the government froze most of the funds in Brazilian bank accounts in 1990, a congressional commission investigating government corruption charges said yesterday, Reuter reports from Brasilia. The commission said it unearthed documents showing that President Collor's election campaign treasurer, Mr Paulo Cesar Farias, withdrew a total of Dollars 303,000 (Pounds 159,000) from two accounts on the last day banks were open before the president's inauguration. Mr Farias is at the heart of a corruption scandal that threatens to bring down Mr Collor's government. Congressional investigators have heard testimony apparantly linking Mr Collor to a multi-million dollar extortion racket allegedly organised by Mr Farias.
