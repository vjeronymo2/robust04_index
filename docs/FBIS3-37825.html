Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Latvian Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs arrived in Minsk on Monday morning. On the same day he started talks with Belarusian Prime Minister Vyacheslav Kebich at the government residence. A bilateral agreement on creating a commission for the demilitarization and demarcation of the Belarusian-Latvian border and on a simplified procedure for border crossing by inhabitants of border areas is expected to be signed in Minsk. Birkavs and Kebich are expected to sign a treaty on legal assistance between Latvia and Belarus, a bilateral agreement on ecological problems, and a trade/economic agreement for 1994. A spokesman for the Latvian Government told Interfax, Birkavs is accompanied by a government delegation including railway and economics ministers, the commander of the border guard brigade and other officials.
