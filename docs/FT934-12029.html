931028 FT 28 OCT 93 / UK Company News: Lower costs help BMSS to Pounds 207,000 Reduced distribution costs and administrative expenses, together with lower interest charges, helped BMSS, the USM-quoted timber and builders' merchant, achieve pre-tax profits of Pounds 207,000 for the half year to July 31. There were losses of Pounds 16,000 last time. Sir Stephen Hastings, chairman, said trading conditions had remained depressed in the period, especially repairs, maintenance and improvements, the sector which accounted for the bulk of its business. The decline in turnover, from Pounds 8.28m to Pounds 7.52m, was entirely accounted for, he said, by a fall in low margin direct sales business. Looking ahead, Sir Stephen said the long-awaited recovery in the construction industry remained at best fragile and patchy. Sales volumes for the second half would be little better than those achieved in the latter part of 1992, he added. Operating profits improved to Pounds 304,000 (Pounds 157,000) after lower distribution costs of Pounds 1.66m (Pounds 1.91m) and administrative expenses of Pounds 498,000 (Pounds 2.16m). The pre-tax result was after net interest payable of Pounds 97,000 (Pounds 173,000). Earnings per share were 1.7p (0.1p losses) and the interim dividend is 1.5p (nil).
