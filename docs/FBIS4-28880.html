BFN [Text] Kim Yong-nam, vice premier and foreign minister, who was in Egypt, met with the Iranian foreign minister, the Guinean minister of foreign affairs and cooperation, the Tanzanian minister of foreign affairs and international cooperation, and the Indian state minister of external affairs on 2 and 3 June. On these occasions, the foreign minister extended greetings from the great leader Comrade Kim Il-song and the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il to Indian President Shankar Dayal Sharma, Indian Prime Minister P. V. Narasimha Rao, Iranian Islam leader Sayed 'Ali Khamene'i, Iranian President Akbar Hashemi-Rafsanjani, Guinean President Lansana Conte, and Tanzanian President Ali Hassan Mwinyi. The foreign ministers expressed deep appreciation for this and asked our country's foreign minister to extend greetings from the head of state and government of each country to the great leader Comrade Kim Il-song and the dear leader Comrade Kim Chong-il. Conversations took place in a friendly atmosphere. The foreign minister also met with the minister of foreign relations. Previously, on 1 June, the foreign minister met with Indonesian President Suharto's adviser in charge of the Nonaligned Movement and talked with him.
