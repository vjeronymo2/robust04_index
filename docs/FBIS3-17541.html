Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS special correspondents Vasiliy Belousov and Dmitriy Gorokhov] [Text] Murmansk March 26 TASS -- Russia is striving for full-scale cooperation with the European Union, Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev told correspondents here today upon the completion of his Murmansk negotiations with Norwegian Foreign Minister Bjorn Tore Godal. The minister said they are working on a major agreement with the European Union. Kozyrev welcomed the upcoming Norwegian joining of the European Union and expressed the hope this will help to attract European Union's investments in development of the Barents Sea area and the Murmansk region. He said Russia will benefit from Norwegian joining of the European Union, as Russian-Norwegian relations are perfect. "The better our neighbours live the better we will live," said Kozyrev. Norway welcomes Russian constructive contribution in solution of world problems, including its initiative on settlement around Sarajevo, Norwegian Foreign Minister Bjorn Tore Godal told correspondents in his turn. The minister was on a brief visit to the major city of the extreme north at the invitation of his Russian counterpart. Bjorn Tore Godal, recently nominated foreign minister, described his first meeting with Andrey Kozyrev as important and fruitful. He said they managed to discuss bilateral cooperation in the Barents Sea area in view of broader European prospect and dwell on many aspects of bilateral and regional interaction. Europe does not need new lines of delimitation, it is necessary to consolidate contacts in the security sphere, stressed the Norwegian minister. He welcomed the Russian intention to sign the NATO Partnership for Peace program in the near future. The Norwegian official said that the negotiations discussed Russian relations with the European Union, which the sides plan to regulate by a special agreement. On the same day Bjorn Tore Godal left Murmansk for Norway.
