920819 FT 19 AUG 92 / International Company News: IBM joins Sears in data scheme INTERNATIONAL Business Machines and Sears, Roebuck, the US retailer, yesterday announced a joint venture through which the companies will merge their voice-and-data networking businesses into a new service company, to be called Advantis, writes Karen Zagor in New York. The combined size and strength of Sears and IBM, make Advantis, from its inception, one of the biggest private data networks in the world. It will be able to draw upon Sears' huge network in North America, which includes Dean Witter Reynolds, Allstate Insurance and Coldwell Banker. Advantis will be jointly owned by IBM and Sears, with IBM holding a majority stake through its Integrated Systems Solutions Corporations unit.
