Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Bratislava, Feb 8 (CTK) -- German Defense Minister Volker Ruhe arrived on a two-day visit in the course of which he will sign a Slovak-German agreement on cooperation in the military sphere. Volker Ruhe is scheduled to meet Slovak President Michal Kovac and Slovak Prime Minister Vladimir Meciar. He will hold talks with his Slovak counterpart Imrich Andrejcak and Foreign Minister Jozef Moravcik. He will also meet Slovak Parliament Chairman Ivan Gasparovic and will see the training of Slovak soldiers in a military zone in Sucany, central Slovakia. Ruhe's first visit to Slovakia is also the first visit by a NATO representative to the country after the recent NATO summit in Brussels. The Slovak Defense Ministry reported that the primary goal of Ruhe's visit was discussion of a whole range of problems regarding cooperation of the two countries' armies. Under discussion will be Slovakia's joining the Partnership for Peace project of European security, and the current political and military situation in Europe, in the context of the latest developments in Bosnia. Prime Minister Meciar is scheduled to confirm Slovakia's interest in joining the U.S. partnership program in Brussels on Wednesday [9 February].
