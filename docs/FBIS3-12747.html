Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Amman, 3 Mar (PETRA)--Planning Minister Dr. Ziyad Fariz today received Karolos Papoulias, Greek foreign minister and chairman of the EC Council of Ministers, and his delegation. Dr. Fariz reviewed the challenges Jordan is facing in the current phase, the circumstances prevailing in the region, the economic measures Jordan has adopted, and the achievements it has realized. He also reviewed the repercussions of the Gulf crisis, including the decline in Jordanian exports to the Gulf states and the reduced demand for Jordanian workers in these states. [passage omitted] Dr. Fariz noted his appreciation for the EC aid to Jordan, and urged the EC to further support Jordan in the economic field in order to fill the 1994 financial gap and alleviate the burden of the general indebtedness. [passage omitted] Dr. Fariz also reviewed Jordanian-Greek relations in the economic, trade, technical, and cultural fields based on the bilateral cooperation agreement signed in 1976. He discussed with the guest minister ways to activate bilateral cooperation by establishing joint food and agricultural industries, and holding meetings between businessmen from both countries to explore new ideas and promote cooperation in various fields. The guest minister noted the extent of identity between the objectives of the Jordanian development plans and those which the EC is seeking to achieve in order to encourage competitiveness and fight both unemployment and poverty. He said he highly appreciated the great efficiency Jordan is enjoying in the field of economic and other policies. He noted that Jordan will be supported by the EC in various fields.
