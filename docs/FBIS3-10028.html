Language: Japanese Article Type:BFN [Report on interview with President Cesar Gaviria Trujillo by Kiyoshi Yoshikawa on 16 February; place not given] [Text] Samta Fe de Bogota, 16 Feb -- Colombian President Cesar Gaviria Trujillo granted an interview to Japanese reporters, including one from YOMIURI SHIMBUN, on 16 February. In the interview, the president revealed his plan to visit Japan from 26 February to 3 March. He also indicated his intention during his visit to urge Japan to invest in Colombia. President Gaviria said: "I have concrete aims for my visit, such as promotion of relations with Japan in the technical field." The president expressed his hope that Japan's loans will be increased to assist Colombia in setting up a research center designed to enhance productivity and competitive power in the manufacturing industry, as well as in improving production facilities in large oil fields. Moreover, he stated that the country had no rescheduling (deferring debt repayment) in the 1980's. The president requested Japan to increase loans, saying: "The international financial circles have highly evaluated that Colombia has completed repayment of foreign debts. I hope that the Japanese financial circle will deepen connections with Colombia."
