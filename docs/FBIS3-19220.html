Language: German Article Type:BFN [Text] Frankfurt/Main (DPA) -- In January, the money supply grew even more strongly than assumed: The monetary stock grew at an annual rate of 21.2 percent. By announcing this, the Bundesbank revised upward the preliminary value of 20.6 percent, which it had published at the beginning of March. For 1994 the Bundesbank is aiming for a money supply growth between 4 and 6 percent. In particular the strong expansion of loans as well as some "special factors" contributed to the unusually strong expansion of M3 -- this includes cash in circulation, sight deposits, time deposits with maturities of less than four years, and savings deposits -- in January.
