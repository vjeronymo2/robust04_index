BFN [Text] One of the defendants in the trial of members of the 1991 abortive coup in Moscow, Valentin Varennikov, (formerly, deputy Soviet defence minister) has said he did not want the amnesty declared by parliament in February to be applied to himself. Varennikov has appealed to Russia's High Court for further hearings into his own case, his lawyer Dmitriy Shteynberg told Interfax. Varennikov reasons that he wants those responsible for wrecking of the former Soviet Union to be found out, something that can only be done during court hearings, he believes.
