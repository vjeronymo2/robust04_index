BFN [Text] Seoul, April 22 (YONHAP) -- Prime Minister-designate Yi Yong-tok pledged Friday that he would do his utmost to serve the country and people if his appointment is legally approved by the National Assembly. "I will see to it that the entire government becomes one under the leadership of the president and devote myself to live up to the responsibility bestowed upon me," Yi told reporters at his office in the National Unification Board [NUB] after his appointment was announced. The NUB minister said the unification is possible once the people unite and continue with reforms to build a free country. "Picturing a unified country, I will do my best to ensure freedom and welfare of the 70 million people of the Korean peninsula," he said. He added that press reports about the intra-government confusion over unification and security-related policy were wrong.
