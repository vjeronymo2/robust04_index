BFN [Text] Aivars Vovers, under-secretary of state for Eastern Affairs at the foreign ministry, and leader of the Latvian group of experts at the upcoming joint talks with Russia, said today a group of experts from Latvia and Russia will have ten agreements on mutual economic cooperation ready for signing. The experts have mainly worked in preparation for the visit of Russian Prime Minister Chernomyrdin to Latvia, scheduled for late June. It is possible that the two countries' leaders will sign the prepared agreements on Latvian-Russian cooperation in transportation, on mutual protection of capital investments, on handing over to Russian ownership of the Amber Coast sanatorium, and on cooperation in the protection of industrial property. The experts are coordinating the text of a declaration on the trends in and prospects for the two countries' economic cooperation. During Chernomyrdin's visit they will also discuss questions relating to the two countries' prospective cooperation in educational, cultural and information areas. In addition, the leaders might discuss the issue of the construction of an international gas pipeline through Latvia.
