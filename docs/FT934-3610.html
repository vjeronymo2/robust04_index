931208 FT 08 DEC 93 / Arts: Today's Television There must be something pretty funny about Ben Elton because some of us continue to laugh even though his obsessive political correctness makes us groan. It will be interesting to see whether his own adaptation of his own book, Stark, is as funny as his stand-up act. Elton himself stars as an English writer in Australia at a time when the earth is being asphyxiated by industrial pollution (9.25 BBC2). Elsewhere the schedules are dominated by state terrorism. Salman Rushdie introduces Shooting The Writer. Six of Algeria's intelligentsia have been assassinated this year, and Islamic terrorists have now issued an ultimatum to the 100,000 foreigners in the country: leave or become targets (8.00 BBC2). In Human Rights, Human Wrongs Rushdie describes the way hundreds of thousands of families have been destroyed in Bosnia-Herzegovina in 'ethnic cleansing' (8.50 BBC2). In his BBC1 series States Of Terror, Peter Taylor investigates US reactions to the suicide attacks on Americans in Beirut in the early 1980s (9.30).
