CSO [Editorial Report] The Kuala Lumpur UTUSAN MALAYSIA in Malay on 20 March, p 2, reported that the government has allocated $5 million over a five-year period to establish the "Malaysian Science Academy." The academy is to be an "elite" organization for "outstanding Malaysian scientists." Science, Technology, and Environment Minister Datuk Law Hieng Ding said the objective of the organiziation is to foster scientific know-how, and to exchange ideas among scientists in analyzing national problems and in making recommendations to the government. Additionally, Minister Law said, the academy will ensure that outstanding scientific contributions, such as new inventions, receive due recognition. Scientists who make valuable contributions will be called "academicians," and will be eligible for a life pension.
