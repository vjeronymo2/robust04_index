BFN [Text] Cairo, 17 Apr (MENA)--Ambassador Badr Hamman, assistant foreign minister for Arab affairs, left Cairo for Sanaa as President Mubarak's special envoy this afternoon. He will deliver two messages to Yemeni President 'Ali 'Abdallah Salih and Vice President 'Ali Salim al-Bid, secretary general of the Yemeni Socialist Party [YSP]. Ambassador Hammam said before leaving that the two messages deal with Egyptian-Yemeni relations and the Egyptian view of the Yemeni situation and stress the need for cooperation among all the Yemeni parties to surmount the situation in the country. Hammam pointed out that his mission in Yemen falls within the context of the continuing Egyptian role of helping brother Yemenis overcome the situation the country has been encountering since last August. This role also seeks to preserve Yemeni unity and implement the pledge and accord document approved and signed by all the political parties in the Jordanian capital, Amman, on 20 February, he explained. The Egyptian official added: We are confident that the political leaders in Yemen know of their responsibility to foster and maintain unity. We will cooperate together to achieve the collective goal of all the Yemeni parties, he said, adding: We will try to provide favorable conditions and common ground for bringing the parties together to overcome the crisis.
