BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic developments monitored through 17 May. Colombian sugar exports increased 27.7 percent in 1993 while sugar production fell 0.33 percent. This drop meant that 1.833 million tons of sugar were produced in 1993 compared to 1.839 tons in 1992. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 7 May 94 p 3B) As of 12 May, 125 kg of coffee will be sold at a price of 132,000 pesos, which constitutes a 10-percent increase over the former price. The National Committee of Coffee-Growers agreed on this price readjustment on 11 May. The increase obtained will give the National Coffee Fund additional revenues of 70 billion pesos. (Santa Fe de Bogota Emisoras Caracol Network in Spanish 1200 GMT 12 May 94) The financial system continues to have liquidity problems. The interbanking rate dropped to 35-percent annually. Savings accounts are growing at an average rate of 44-percent. An increase in imports has helped increase the current dollar exchange price to 841 pesos. Interest rates are stable. The inflation rate for the first trimester of 1994 has been around 12 percent. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 15 May 94 p 2B)
