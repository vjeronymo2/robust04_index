Proposed Amendments: Form N&hyph;1A_File No. 270&hyph;21 Form N&hyph;2_File No. 270&hyph;21 Form N&hyph;3_File No. 270&hyph;281 Form N&hyph;4_File No. 270&hyph;282 Form N&hyph;5_File No. 270&hyph;172 Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;2_File No. 270&hyph;186 Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;3_File No. 270&hyph;179 Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;4_File No. 270&hyph;180 Notice is hereby given that, pursuant to the Paperwork Reduction Act of 1980 (44 U.S.C. 3501 et seq. ) (``the Act''), the Securities and Exchange Commission (the ``Commission'') has submitted for OMB approval amendments to Forms N&hyph;1A, N&hyph;2, N&hyph;3, N&hyph;4, N&hyph;5, N&hyph;8B&hyph;2, N&hyph;8B&hyph;3, and N&hyph;8B&hyph;4 (the ``Forms'') under the Securities Act of 1933 (``1933 Act'') and the Investment Company Act of 1940 (``1940 Act''). The Forms are used by management investment companies (``funds'') to register their securities under the 1993 Act and/or register as an investment company under the 1940 Act. The proposed amendments will add a requirement to the Forms to disclose legal proceedings involving directors, executive officers, and ``managerial persons'' ( i.e., person affiliated with the investment adviser to the fund who are engaged in the management, direction, supervision, or performance of any activities related to the fund). Form N&hyph;1A is the registration statement form used by open-end funds, other than small business investment companies and insurance company separate accounts, to register their securities under the 1933 Act and to register as an investment company under the 1940 Act. The average additional burden imposed by the proposed amendments to Form N&hyph;1A is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. The total annual burden for Form N&hyph;1A per registrant would be 1,063 burden hours per registrant and the total for all registrants would be 2,976,497 hours. Form N&hyph;2 is the registration statement form used by closed-end investment companies to register their securities under the 1933 Act and to register as an investment company under the 1940 Act. The average additional burden imposed by the proposed amendments to Form N&hyph;2 is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. The total annual burden for Form N&hyph;2 per registrant would be 1,632 burden hours per registrant and the total for all registrants would be 204,018 hours. Form N&hyph;3 is the registration statement form for insurance company separate accounts organized as management investment companies. The average additional burden imposed by the proposed amendments to Form N&hyph;3 is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. The total annual burden for Form N&hyph;3 would be 517 burden hours per registrant and the total for all registrants would be 27,425 hours. Form N&hyph;4 is the registration statement form for insurance company separate accounts organized as unit investment trusts. The average additional burden imposed by the proposed amendments to Form N&hyph;4 is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. The total annual burden for Form N&hyph;4 would be 140 burden hours per registrant and the total for all registrants would be 40,448 hours. Form N&hyph;5 is the registration statement form for small business investment companies. The average additional burden imposed by amendments to Form N&hyph;5 is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. The total annual burden for Form N&hyph;5 would be 351 burden hours per registrant and the total for all registrants would be 702 hours. Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;2 is the registration statement form used by unit investment trust currently issuing securities to register under the 1940 Act. The average additional burden imposed by the proposed amendments to Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;2 is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. An average of 22 filers file Form N&hyph;8b&hyph;2 and the total annual burden hours per registrant would be 1,627 hours. The total burden for all registrants filing Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;2 would be 35,794 hours. Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;3 is used by unincorporated management investment companies currently issuing periodic plan certificates to register under the 1940 Act. Approximately 2 filers file Form N&hyph;8b&hyph;3 annually. The average additional burden imposed by the proposed amendments to Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;3 is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. The total burden for all registrants filing Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;3 will be 342 hours. Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;4 is used by face-amount certificate companies to register under the 1940 Act. The average additional burden imposed by the proposed amendments to Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;4 is estimated to be 1 hour per registrant. Approximately 1 filer will file Form N&hyph;8B&hyph;4 annually. The total annual burden hours per registrant would be 171 hours. Direct general comments to the Clearance Officer for the Securities and Exchange Commission at the address below.
