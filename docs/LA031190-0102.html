March 11, 1990, Sunday, Home Edition

EQUITY SHARING ADVICE

The article by Robert J. Bruss, "Home Equity Sharing Benefits Owner, Renter" (Feb. 25), was excellent, with one notable exception: the negative statement about the problem arising when the resident co-owner stops making the monthly payments.

In the real estate investors program, we call the co-owners the "buyer" and "investor."

The buyers start with no equity and earn their 50% share of the equity increase over five years or 60 payments. If they default or have to move before five years, their share is prorated. Therefore, even in a default the buyers don't lose everything.

We establish an open holding escrow to collect the monthly payments including a requirement of depositing one month payment in advance. The holding escrow also retains a signed grant deed from the buyer to the investor. If a problem is developing, the escrow officer will know one month before that payment is due.

HERMAN W. PASS

Los Angeles

Pass is president of Real Equity Investors Inc.
