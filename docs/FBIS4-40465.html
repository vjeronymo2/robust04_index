BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Sergey Ostanin] [Text] Moscow, 17 June -- A naval detachment of the Black Sea Fleet has today begun practicing joint tasks with the Russian group of troops in western Georgia. It will take part in the peacekeeping operation to stabilize the situation in this region, ITAR-TASS was told at the Fleet's press center. Rear Admiral Yuriy Chichik, head of the Black Sea Fleet command center, will be in command of the operation. According to the press center's information, a naval detachment of the Black Sea Fleet, with the large antisubmarine vessel Kerch as flagship, has left for the Greek island of Corfu tasked with preparing for the Russian president's visit. The detachment is under command of Rear Admiral Anatoliy Manchenko, first deputy head of Fleet Headquarters.
