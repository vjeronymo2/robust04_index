940114 FT 14 JAN 94 / UK Company News: Rank at Pounds 277m in weak market - Upturn attributed to cost-cutting and improved cash management The Rank Organisation yesterday announced annual pre-tax profits of Pounds 276.6m, compared with a restated Pounds 125.8m, but said there had been little improvement in market conditions. Mr Michael Gifford, chief executive, said the upturn was the result of cost-cutting and improved cash management. The UK and US markets had remained weak in 1993 and conditions in Japan and some continental European countries had deteriorated. There were signs, however, that consumer spending was picking up in the UK and the US. The year to October 31 saw sales rise 5 per cent to Pounds 2.11bn (Pounds 2.01bn). Operating profit increased by 13 per cent to Pounds 200.4m (Pounds 176.6m). The shares rose 20p to Pounds 10.01p. All of Rank's main business sectors increased operating profits. Film and television's input rose to Pounds 35.7m (Pounds 22m). Mr Gifford said the advance was largely the result of improved profitability in film processing. Admissions to Odeon cinemas rose 10 per cent, largely because of the film Jurassic Park. Rank was unable to increase admission prices, however, because of strong competition. Operating profit from holidays rose to Pounds 51m (Pounds 42.5m). Holiday volumes grew by 8 per cent, although some of the improvement came in the off-peak period when prices are lower. Profits rose at the Butlin's holiday camps but the Shearings coach holiday business saw a small decline. Mr Gifford added that holiday bookings for the first half of 1994 were up 9 per cent. Bookings for the second half, which includes the summer season, had increased 7 per cent. The recreation businesses, which includes bingo, casino and social clubs, reported operating profits of Pounds 63m (Pounds 57.3m). Admissions were similar to 1992, but spending per head in social and bingo clubs increased by 6 per cent. Spending in the casinos rose 4 per cent on unchanged admissions. The leisure division, which takes in US resorts, Hard Rock Cafes and nightclubs, lifted profits to Pounds 50.4m (Pounds 33.6m), of which the US contributed Pounds 10m. UK nightclubs increased admissions by 13 per cent, but spending per head remained static. Mr Gifford said many of the new customers were coming to nightclubs during the week, when spending was usually lower. Rank's share of the pre-tax profits of Rank Xerox rose to Pounds 151.2m (Pounds 137.3m). The contribution from other associates, primarily Universal Studios in Florida, rose by more than 50 per cent to Pounds 11.5m. Earnings per share were 46.5p (6.1p). There was no final dividend as Rank announced last July that it was paying an accelerated total for the year of 31p, the same as in 1992. A Kershaw, whose main asset is its shareholding in Rank Precision Industries, had investment income after all charges of Pounds 10.1m (Pounds 9.9m).
