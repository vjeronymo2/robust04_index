Language: English Article Type:BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondents Vladimir Akimov and Georgiy Shmelyov] [Text] Almaty February 9 TASS -- Delegations of the Russian and Kazakh Foreign Ministries which met for talks in Almaty on Wednesday [9 February] expressed a unanimous opinion about an entire range of problems they discussed. In addition to agreements reached several hours ago by Russian Foreign Minister Andrey Kozyrev and Kazakh President Nursultan Nazarbayev and proposals expressed in a personal message by Russian President Boris Yeltsin addressed to the Kazakh president, a decision was adopted to form three working groups from each side to search for ways of resolving the most tense problems in the relations between the two countries. Under the patronage of the two foreign ministers the working groups shall, within a month or a month and a half, to prepare a package of agreements between Russia and Kazakhstan on the basis of earlier projects, pertaining to problems of citizenship, military cooperation and the joint use of the Baykonur cosmodrome to subsequently submit these agreements for approval to heads of state in order to give them a legal character. This is expected to take place during a forthcoming summit meeting scheduled to be held no later than at the end of March, 1994. Simultaneously, Russian foreign minister and his Kazakh counterpart Tuleutay Suleymenov agreed to step up preparations for signing other draft documents expected to promote further cooperation between the two countries. The matter primarily concerns the signing of a consular convention and a protocol on regular consultations between Russian and Kazakh Foreign Ministries. Mutual understanding was reached on the organisation of a conference of heads of Caspian states on ways of resolving problems of the Caspian Sea.
