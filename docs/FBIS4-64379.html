BFN [Text] Stuttgart -- PLO leader Yasir 'Arafat has expressed confidence in Stuttgart today regarding the signing of the autonomy agreement for Gaza and Jericho. At an international news conference at the Daimler-Benz headquarters, 'Arafat stated, referring to the meeting between him and Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhaq Rabin in Cairo on Tuesday [3 May], that "there are still a few problems to be settled." He hopes that it will be possible "to eliminate these obstacles" during the talks. Asked about the planned date of the signing of the agreement in Cairo on Wednesday, he pointed out: "Where there is a will, there is a way." He added: "It takes two to tango, one also depends on others." He declined to mention a date as to when he will return to Jerusalem, Jericho, and Gaza. It depends on when the Israelis will withdraw from these regions. The final departure of the Israelis was to have been completed by 13 April, 'Arafat stressed. "But you see." 'Arafat paid a one-day visit to Stuttgart today at the invitation of Daimler boss Edzard Reuter.
