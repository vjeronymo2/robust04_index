930910 FT 10 SEP 93 / Petrol stations in rural areas 'at risk' A NUMBER of rural petrol stations operated by large integrated oil companies could close if competition from hypermarkets continues unabated, Sir John Collins, chairman and chief executive of Shell UK, said yesterday. Sir John said in the company's annual energy review that competitive pressures were undermining the 'old public services undertakings' by which the big oil companies ensured countrywide access to fuel at uniform prices. Sir John said that hyper-markets accounted for 16 per cent of national sales of vehicle fuels. Their market share is growing rapidly because they can take advantage of cheap supplies from surplus capacity in British refineries. Hypermarkets can also concentrate on a few sites where they can incorporate the latest health, safety and environmental standards at relatively low costs. 'There is a growing differential between low volumes and high unit costs of rural sites and the converse for hyper-markets who operate only at prime sites,' Sir John said.
