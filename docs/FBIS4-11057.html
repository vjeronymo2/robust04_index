BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on guerrilla activities monitored through 20 April. Army major Luis Demetrio Yepes and Sergeant David Bawy Guerrero are still missing after being kidnapped by National Liberation Army (ELN) guerrillas in La Milagrosa, between El Santuario and Cocorna, Antioquia, on 10 April. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 12 April 94 p 8a) Cattleman Ricardo Blanco, kidnapped by FARC (Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia) in La Trinidad Hacienda, Oiba Municipality, Santander Department, was released by his captors on 11 April. A man known as Luis, reportedly FARC's chief of intelligence, and seven guerrillas were captured in Salamina, Caldas department. In addition, four police were wounded when reported FARC members attacked a patrol in Apartado, Antioquia. (Santa Fe de Bogota EL TIEMPO in Spanish 13 April 94 p 8a) In Flor del Monte, Sucre Department, an infantry patrol was apparently ambushed by a FARC group. Five men, soldiers and guerrillas, were killed. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Canal A in Spanish 1730 GMT 18 April 94) The authorities have dealt a heavy blow to the ELN, as military forces seized a large amount of equipment held by the clandestine radio station, Radio Patria Libre, which the guerrillas used to instigate its members to commit crimes. During the operation, three guerrillas were killed and two were captured. The 2d Mobile Brigade dismantled three camps that served as refuge for at least 240 guerrillas and seized communications equipment, booby trap mines, weapons, and ammunition. (Santa Fe de Bogota Inravision Television Canal A in Spanish 1730 GMT 19 April 94)
