BFN [From MAINICHI SHIMBUN 30 May Evening Edition] [Text] On the morning of 30 May, the Budget Committee of the House of Representatives continued its comprehensive interpellations on the fiscal 1994 draft budget. At this session, Yoshio Hachiro (of the Social Democratic Party of Japan [SDPJ]) raised the question of how the government intended to deal with the breach between the DPRK [Democratic Poeple's Republic of Korea] and the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) on consultaions on the nuclear inspection issue. In reply, Foreign Minister Koji Kakizawa stated: "They are engaged in extracting fuel rods from the five-megawatt experimental nuclear reactor. If this operation should continue, it would become -- within two or three days -- difficult to carry out further inspection. And we are concerned about this." His comments indicated that the government is seriously concerned about the situation. He added: "We have made proposals in various forms to North Korea, asking for its sincere handling of the issue. And to carry out further inspection, consultations are being held between the United States and the DPRK, between Japan, the United States, and the ROK, and also with China." His remarks disclosed the fact that consultations are currently being held on the issue among various countries.
