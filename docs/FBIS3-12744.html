Language: English Article Type:BFN [From the "News at Ten" program] [Text] Greek Foreign Minister Karolos Papoulias arrived in Jordan on a visit, which will include talks on bilateral relations and the Middle East peace process. The Greek minister is the current chairman of the European Union Council of Ministers. He arrived in Amman after visits to Israel and the PLO headquarters in Tunis. He told Jordan Television that he will discuss with Jordanian officials current conditions in the Middle East in the wake of the Hebron massacre. He said the most pressing issue now is to maintain the momentum of the peace process. The Greek foreign minister added that the European Union believes that a demand by the Palestinians in the occupied lands for international protection is just one.
