Language: Italian Article Type:BFN [Text] Gianfranco Fini is optimistic that a new government will be formed quickly. He has not put himself forward for the office of prime minister but is backing Silvio Berlusconi, and has ruled out Northern League politicians. He has pointed out that the League is only present in some areas of Italy. After stressing that he has not spoken to Umberto Bossi on the telephone, either yesterday or today, Fini asked the Northern League leader to be completely frank about the value of national unity and solidarity between the north and the south, which is fundamental to the National Alliance. He also pointed out that Berlusconi's bid to be prime minister is in keeping with the results of the election.
