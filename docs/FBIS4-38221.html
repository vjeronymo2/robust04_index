BFN [Text] Tehran, June 28, IRNA -- A new commission called the `Islamic Human Rights Commission' is to be established by the judiciary system to follow up issues related to the Islamic countries, said Head of the Judiciary Ayatollah Mohammad Yazdi here Tuesday [28 June]. Speaking at the closing session of the nationwide seminar on `Judiciary Week,' the Ayatollah noted that if faced with any case of violation of Islamic human rights inside or outside the country, the commission "will have the authority to use its capabiities to settle the problem." Addressing the participants, Yazdi described as "acceptable" the body's performance in the past five years, adding that the judiciary system was looking after people's affairs very actively in the said period. Further in his talks, the Ayatollah said that fewer cases were referred to the state Supreme Court in the past five years and added that some 33,279 inmates have been pardoned in the said period. He said his term in office is going to be over, adding that he would be happy if the burden were to be shouldered by someone else.
