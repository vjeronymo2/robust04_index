Language: Russian Article Type:BFN [Video report by correspondent T. Aldoshina; from the "Vesti" newscast] [Text] [Correspondent T. Aldoshina] Today, Oleg Soskovets held a news conference on the results of the government's extended session. He said that the main directions of the country's development have been indicated in the president's message. The first deputy prime minister named the following among priority areas in the economy: The fuel and power complex, industry, agriculture, conversion, and restructuring of the social sphere. Commenting on information by Yegor Gaydar that the draft budget provides for an increase in expenditure on the upkeep of the state apparatus and not for social needs, Oleg Soskovets said: [Begin recording] [Soskovets] It is the normal thing that when the state apparatus increases, the social sphere is reduced. [Aldoshina] But still, precisely this situation is set out in the draft budget, isn't it? [Soskovets] We have not yet put out the draft budget anywhere. [end recording] [Aldoshina] The draft budget, according to the first deputy prime minister, may be passed on to the State Duma this week. Commenting on the question of Gaydar's intention to return to the government, Oleg Soskovets said: That is his problem. [end recording]
