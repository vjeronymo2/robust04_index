Language: Latvian Article Type:BFN [Text] A delegation from the Danish Ministry of Defense, headed by Minister Hans Haekkerup, arrived in Latvia today for an official working visit. The Danish and Latvian defense ministers signed an agreement on cooperation in the military field. The agreement provides for mutual consultations on issues of defense planning and strategy, as well as security. The agreement also provides for exchange of experience and information and visits by officials and officers. Educational and practical training programs will be worked out together; other issues will be also tackled together. Denmark is the first NATO member state with which such an agreement has been signed. Pavlovskis [Latvian Defense Minister] values the agreement as one more step forward in the formation of good relations between both states. The Danish defense minister said that Denmark attaches great significance to this visit and to cooperation with Latvia. When meeting Prime Minister Valdis Birkavs, the Danish defense minister expressed unambiguous support for Latvia in the issue of the withdrawal of the Russian Army. He expressed hope that it would be possible to also agree on the future of the Skrunda radar. Haekkerup welcomed the possible participation of the Baltic states, including Latvia, in the UN peacekeeping forces. He said that this would be a good beginning in cooperation with the European security structures. The Danish defense minister promised that the issue of the Baltic states' involvement in the peacekeeping forces will be discussed at the next meeting of the Nordic states' defense ministers. He did not exclude the possibility that Denmark and other Nordic states could grant material assistance in the formation of the peace battalion of the Baltic states.
