BFN [Excerpt] At the opening of the ordinary session of the Socialist Forces Front [FFS], the acting secretary-general stated that his party rejected any dialogue which did not aim at establishing democracy and human rights or did not conform to international values. During the session there was also talk about the Amazighi [Berber] and the national identity. Nasima Kirat reports: [Kiratg] At FFS headquarters acting General Secretary Lounaoussi Hamid met veterans of the FFS. He directed his comments to the current situation the country is going through. [Begin Lounaoussi recording] The FFS rejects violence as political means. The FFS rejects any agreement or settlement that could be reached between parties which advocate violence at the expense of democracy and the Algerian people. The FFS rejects any dialogue which does not aim at building an Algeria of democracy, an Algeria of Human rights, an Algeria that conforms with international values. [end recording] [Kirat] He also spoke about the Amazighi cultural movement which continues, as he said, to advocate the Amazighi identity. [passage omitted]
