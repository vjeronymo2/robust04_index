Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Foreign Minister Prasong Sunsiri has concluded his official visit to New Zealand as a guest of Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister, Donald McKinnon. The visit during 24 to 27 March 1994 was aimed at enhancing cooperation, exchanging views on Asia-Pacific situation, and listening to opinions on ASEAN ministerial meeting hosted by Thailand in July this year. New Zealand has increased cooperation with Thailand in many fields such as trade, investment, and tourism. Foreign Minister Prasong asked New Zealand to open its market to Thai goods. Thailand's trade with New Zealand amounts to 1,700 million baht at present with Thailand suffering a trade deficit. On ASEAN ministerial meeting in Bangkok in July, New Zealand has pledged to attend the meeting as it views the meeting as a significant event to maintain peace and stability in Asia and the Pacific region. On the situation in Cambodia and Myanmar [Burma], the Thai foreign minister called for a political settlement in Cambodia. He informed the New Zealand minister about Thailand's intention to invite Myanmar to attend the ministerial meeting in Bangkok in July.
