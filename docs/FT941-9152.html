940216 FT 16 FEB 94 / Observer: Knight's turn Ruth Dudley Edwards' conclusion, in her history of The Economist, that 'nobody ever got the last word in a debate with Andrew Knight', is spot on. The executive chairman of News International had been asked, along with several other former editors of The Economist, to sign the frontispiece of a first edition of Edwards' history, one of several books, including an uncut first edition of Pope's Rape of the Lock from Mary Moore, which have been donated to a fund-raiser for St Anne's College, Oxford. Jeffrey Archer bangs the gavel at Middle Temple Hall on February 23. But whereas Sir Alastair Burnet, Rupert Pennant-Rea and Bill Emmott just signed their names, the self-made millionaire has glued copies of both his reviews of Edwards' book inside the front cover.
