July 4, 1990, Wednesday, Home Edition

METRO DIGEST / LOCAL NEWS IN BRIEF: IRWINDALE TO PAY FORMER CONSULTANT $1.28 MILLION

The Irwindale City Council has agreed to pay former redevelopment consultant Fred Lyte $1.28 million to end a lengthy legal battle stemming from his firing in December, 1988.

Lyte was let go when city officials decided that his contract, which gave Lyte 3% of the building permit fees garnered from the city's redevelopment zone, was too generous.

The city later sued Lyte for $2 million, claiming he had violated his contract by recommending projects in which he had a financial interest.

Lyte denied that and countersued for $1.4 million in back pay. The decision to settle out of court was, according to city council members, an attempt to save legal fees, for which the city already had spent $500,000, not an admission of guilt.

Lyte said that he was pleased and added: "They paid me what they owed me."
