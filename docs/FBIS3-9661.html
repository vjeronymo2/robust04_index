Language: Serbo-Croatian Article Type:BFN [Text] Cease-fire talks between the delegation of the Republic of Croatia and representatives of rebel Serbs from the UNPA [UN Protected Areas] zones resumed at 1100 in the Russian Embassy in Zagreb. These talks, which began 22 March, were adjourned because of additional consultations by both sides and consideration of the proposals which emerged during the talks. Today's talks are being attended for the first time by Charles Redman, the special envoy of the U.S. President. Vitaliy Churkin, special envoy of the Russian president, and U.S. Ambassador to Croatia Peter Galbraith were also taking part. The Croatian delegation comprises Engineer Hrvoje Sarinic, Slavko Degoricija, Gen Petar Stipetic, Naval Captain Davor Domazet, Smiljana Reljic and Budimir Pleskina. The Serbs from the UNPA zones are being represented at the talks by Dusan Rakic, Slobodan Jarcevic, Mile Novakovic, and Aco Draca. The meeting in the Russian Embassy is being chaired by representatives of the international conference on the former Yugoslavia, ambassadors Geert Ahrens and Kai Aide. The only subject of the talks, it has been announced, will be an agreement on a cease-fire and in this connection the separation of forces.
