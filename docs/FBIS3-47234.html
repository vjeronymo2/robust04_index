Language: Cambodian Article Type:BFN [Text] This morning, Samdech Chea Sim, acting head of state and chairman of the National Assembly, granted an audience to His Excellency [H.E.] Jacques Chirac, former French prime minister and mayor of Paris, and his colleagues at Chan Throne Hall in the Royal Palace. Samdech Chea Sim welcomed and regarded H.E. Jacques Chirac's visit to Cambodia as a sign of further consolidation and expansion of Cambodian-French relations and cooperation that have existed since time immemorial. Samdech Chea Sim thanked the French Government for its role in helping seek peace and national reconciliation in Cambodia. He also thanked the Paris administration for assistance to the city of Phnom Penh, which is facing difficulties. Samdech Chea Sim also informed the guests of His Majesty King Norodom Sihanouk Varaman's health, saying that his health is good, his medical treatment will be complete by the end of March, and he will return home at the beginning of April to attend the Cambodian traditional new year celebration with all Cambodian citizens. H.E. Jacques Chirac stressed the cooperation and assistance given by the French Government and the Paris administration to Phnom Penh in restoring this capital of the Kingdom of Cambodia, which has been ruined by war in the past over two decades. Through Samdech Chea Sim, H.E. Jacques Chirac wished His Majesty King Norodom Sihanouk Varaman a quick recovery from his illness so that he may return to serve his people. On the same day, H.E. Chhim Siekleng, delegate of the Royal Government of Cambodia to Phnom Penh City, also held cordial talks with H.E. Jacques Chirac. On that occasion, H.E. Chhim Siekleng thanked the French Government and Paris administration for helping to restore the city of Phnom Penh. He informed the guest of the difficulties facing Phnom Penh and asked the Paris administration to provide assistance in rearranging the traffic in Phnom Penh and construction equipment and machines for repairing the badly damaged roads in the capital. H.E. Chhim Siekleng also asked the Paris administration to provide experts for developing Phnom Penh and setting up a computer system for the government's administrative work. H.E. Jacques Chirac emphasized the goodwill of the French Government and Paris to help Cambodia restore the city of Phnom Penh. In response to Phnom Penh's requests, H.E. Jacques Chirac promised to provide additional equipment, machines, and garbage trucks for use in developing and beautifying Phnom Penh. In this regard, France will continue helping to establish a clean water system and send experts to Cambodia as requested within the framework of the association for assistance to the Francophone cities.
