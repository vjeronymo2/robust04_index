BFN [Text] San Salvador, 1 May (ACAN-EFE) -- Gloria Salguero Gross, a deputy for the ruling Nationalist Republican Alliance (ultra right-wing ARENA) was elected by a split vote the new president of El Salvador's Legislative Assembly today, Sunday. Salguero received the support of 70 of the 84 deputies that make up the assembly, including seven members of the former guerrilla Farabundo Marti National Liberation Movement (FMLN). The remaining 14 FMLN deputies abstained. The presidential election took place during installation of the Legislative Assembly ceremonies. The process was marked by chants, jeers, and shouting by FMLN militants, who were protesting because the previous legislature changed the institution's bylaws, allowing the new president to vote twice.
