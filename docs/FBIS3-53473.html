Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] Cairo, 17 Jan (MENA)--Foreign Minister 'Amr Musa returned to Cairo from Geneva this evening after heading the Egyptian delegation to the ministerial meeting of the Islamic Conference Organization's [ICO] liaison group bureau devoted to discussing the situation in Bosnia-Herzegovina. In an arrival statement, he said the conference discussed Bosnia and the latest European proposals, including the latest NATO Brussels summit resolutions to solve the Bosnian problem, and the Bosnian Government's stance on these proposals. [passage omitted] Musa described the Syrian-U.S. summit held in Geneva yesterday as successful, adding that it is considered to be an important step, a landmark, on the Syrian-Israeli track and on the peaceful (?course) toward a comprehensive solution. He noted that the Syrian-U.S. summit may give a strong boost to the peace process that began in Madrid on the basis of land for peace. He noted that the peace process is now moving on the four Lebanese, Syrian, Palestinian, and Jordanian tracks.
