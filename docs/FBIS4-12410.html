BFN [Excerpt] Lieutenant General 'Umar al-Bashir, the president of the Republic, has affirmed the cohesion of the internal front and its firm stand against all challenges facing the nation's march. During his meeting today with the leaders of information and press bodies at the general secretariat of the Council of Ministers, the president discussed the public's understanding of the revolution's programs and his perception of its achievements in various fields, particularly with regard to the security situation. His excellency said recent attempts to cast doubt on the revolution's leadership by some people was due to personal reasons or to agents from foreign quarters. Lt. Gen. 'Umar al-Bashir said any conspirator who tried to hinder the Sudanese people would not be able to achieve his aims, pointing out that the revolution is proceeding despite all challenges. The president of the Republic touched on the talks he held recently with U.S. envoy Madeleine Albright. He said President Clinton's envoy did not touch on anything new in the talks. He pointed out that she commented on the situation and the U.S. accusations against Sudan with regard to human rights, terrorism and relief. We submitted, during the talks, Sudan's stance towards the U.S. Administration, he said, adding that the contentious issue, in essence, was the issue of Islam. Lt. Gen. al-Bashir described the meeting as the start of a serious dialogue between the two countries. He confirmed Sudan's desire to continue the dialogue, so that relations between Sudan and the United States would be based on a clear foundation. [passage omitted]
