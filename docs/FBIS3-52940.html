Language: Arabic Article Type:BFN [Excerpts] French Prime Minister Edouard Balladur has stressed that the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia constitutes an element of stability, equilibrium, and security, and it is regarded as a key factor for strengthening stability in the region. He said this in a statement opening the news conference he held in Riyadh today at the conclusion of his visit to the Kingdom. [passage omitted]. The prime minister further said: We have agreed on coordination, consultation, and dialogue at all levels between state bodies as well as between companies, with a periodic exchange of views in order to assess bilateral relations and bolster the ties of cooperation in all fields, and to give fresh momentum to economic and trade cooperation. The French prime minister mentioned the domains of cooperation that had been discussed, such as energy and defense, and he said: We have decided to set up a team of experts to complete the projects for cooperation which have already been discussed, examined, and prepared in detail, and which we want to start carrying out. [passage omitted] Naturally, the French prime minister said, we discussed the situation in the region and recent events there, and we reaffirmed the need for the full implementation of the UN resolutions. Concerning the problems of the region at large, he said, we both stressed our full support for a just and comprehensive solution to the Middle East problem on the basis of the Security Council Resolutions 242, 338, and 425. [passage omitted]. The prime minister affirmed that his visit was aimed at giving a fresh impetus to relations between the two countries, and at laying down the principles that will clearly govern these relations. He denied that his visit was to conclude precise and defined contracts. He told journalists that France and the Kingdom had decided to set up a working team to discuss a number of projects of cooperation in the two friendly countries, complete what has been achieved, and secure progress in these matters. [passage omitted]. For his part, and to a question on the problem of Bosnia-Herzegovina, the French Foreign Minister Alain Juppe, who took part in the news conference, pointed out that he had discussed this matter with Saudi Foreign Minister Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal, and he had the chance to explain the French and European diplomacy on this matter. He said: I believe that I have given a useful and detailed explanation to Prince Sa'ud al-Faysal that permits the strengthening of common understanding aimed at a peaceful settlement of this issue. He expressed his regret at the appalling deterioration of the situation, and he blamed all the parties to the conflict for rejecting the peace plans. [passage omitted] On the other hand, with regard to the problem of Bosnia-Herzegovina and what he learned in the Kingdom concerning the Saudi position in this respect, the French prime minister expressed understanding for the concern of Saudi Arabia toward this part of the world. He said that at the talks, he had tried to explain the French position and that of the European Union with a view to breaking the vicious circle surrounding the problem. [passage omitted]
