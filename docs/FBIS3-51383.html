Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Uruguay monitored through 13 January. Source is cited in parentheses following each item. On 4 January, the Central Bank reported that the state registered a 357.2 million-peso deficit in the January-October 1993 period. This was the result of the public sector's nonfinancial surplus of 73.8 million pesos and a 431 million-peso deficit in the "parafiscal" sector. (Montevideo LA MANANA in Spanish 5 Jan 94 p 43) The Central Bank's net international reserves increased $213.3 million in 1993; total reserves now amount to $1.1603 billion, 22.5 percent more than in 1992. (Montevideo EL OBSERVADOR ECONOMICO in Spanish 6 Jan 94 p 9)
