930126 FT 26 JAN 93 / UK Company News: St Andrew shows 10.3% rise in net asset value ST ANDREW Trust, which is the subject of a bid from Ecclesiastical Insurance Office, reported a 10.3 per cent rise in net asset value, from 226.1p to 249.5p, in 1992. It also announced a second interim dividend of 4.85p per share in lieu of a final. This maintains the total at 7.55p from earnings of 7.42p (7.12p). The EIO offer is a technical one designed to meet EC regulations. Acceptances passed the 50 per cent mark last week but EIO does not want more than 75 per cent because that would jeopardise St Andrew's investment trust status; those who accept will receive the higher of 237p or 93 per cent of St Andrew's formula asset value. About 65 per cent of the portfolio is in the UK; the remainder is split between the US (14 per cent), the Pacific Basin (12 per cent) and continental Europe (8 per cent). Mr David Ross Stewart, chairman, said 'although it is still too early to assume a rapid acceleration towards global economic recovery, there are some encouraging signs.'
