BFN [Text] Bucharest ROMPRES, 31/3/1994 -- Romanian Premier Nicolae Vacaroiu received Thursday, March 31, Hans Blix, president of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), currently in Romania to make an inspection of the way in which works are being carried out at unit one of the atomic station of Cernavoda. Hans Blix, voiced, on this occasion, the preoccupations of the world body he works with for permanent surveillance of the atomic stations in Eastern Europe, for support to be granted to them to improve their protection systems. The IAEA president showed interest in the creation in Romania of a unique authority for surveillance and control of nuclear activities, under the premier's direct subordination. Premier Vacaroiu said that the government was interested that the Cernavoda atomic station start operation as soon as possible with respect of all international duties in the domain. The director general of the International Atomic Energy Agency also met on Thursday with the Romanian Minister of the Industries, Dumitru Popescu. Appreciating Mr. Hans Blix's visit in a very important moment when Romania prepares for the actuation of the first unit of the nuclear plant at Cernavoda, about 200 km east of Bucharest, the Romanian minister said after the meeting that the two parties agreed that the situation of the nuclear sector was vital for Romania's energy system as the conventional energy resources -- methane gas, oil -- were shrinking while the water power stations very expensive. "Mr. Hans Blix's findings during the visit at the Cernavoda plant, the second in four years, concerning the security and quality standards of the works are encouraging us in speeding-up the finalisation of the works and in trying to find, together with the other cabinet members, the funds necessary for the completion and actuation of the unit one during the current year," said the Romanian minister. The IAEA head and the Romanian minister approached also issues concerning the cooperation between the IAEA and the Romanian Ministry of Industries. The Vienna-based agency provides yearly the financial means for the training of about 30-40 Romanian experts following specialization stages in various countries, said Minister Popescu. Hans Blix, director-general of the International Atomic Energy Agency, IAEA, was conferred, during a specific ceremony on March 31, the title of Doctor Honoris Causa of the Bucharest University. During the laudatio, Professor Emil Constantinescu, chancellor of the Bucharest University, referred, alongside Blix's scientific merits, to his rich political and diplomatic activity, to the particular stress he lays on legal guarantees apt to prevent the use of atomic energy for purposes other than peaceful.
