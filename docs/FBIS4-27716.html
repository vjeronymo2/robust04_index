BFN [Text] A Myanmar [Burmese] delegation led by Foreign Affairs Minister U Ohn Gyaw returned to Yangon [Rangoon] tonight after having attended the 11th foreign ministers' meeting of the Nonaligned Movement held in Cairo, Egypt from 31 May to 3 June. The minister and the delegation were welcomed at Yangon airport by Lieutenant General Khin Nyunt, secretary-1 of the State Law and Order Restoration Council; Lt. Gen. Myo Nyunt, minister of religious affairs; Lt. Gen. Kyaw Ba, minister of hotels and tourism; U Nyunt Swe, deputy minister of foreign affairs; Muhammad Hasan Ghanim, Egyptian ambassador to Myanmar; Mohamed Sanusi, Indonesian ambassador to Myanmar; responsible personnel from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and family members. Major General Kyaw Than, shadow leader of the Myanmar delegation, commander of the Central Military Command, and chairman of the Mandalay Division Law and Order Restoration Council; U Khin Maung Win, deputy director general of the Ministry of Foreign Ministry; and U Thaung Tun, director of the Ministry of Foreign Ministry, also returned to Yangon with the minister. In Cairo, Egyptian President Husni Mubarak received U Ohn Gyaw, leader of the Myanmar delegation and minister of foreign affairs. Minister U Ohn Gyaw also had a friendly exchange of views on bilateral relationships, the Nonaligned Movement, and other mutual interests with foreign ministers from Egypt, Cuba, Thailand, South Africa, Brunei, Bangladesh, Vietnam, Philippines, and Malaysia and the Lao and Italian deputy foreign ministers, who were leaders of their delegations.
