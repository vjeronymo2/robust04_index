Article Type:BFN [Editorial Report] The following is a compilation of reports on economic activities in Paraguay monitored through 27 January. Source is listed in parentheses following each item: A General Customs Directorate report notes that export earnings in 1993 were $748,145,804.64 -- free-on-board value, a 15.3 percent rise compared with the previous year. Exports rose 33.5 percent, totaling 2,783,377.6 metric tons. Paraguay's chief exports were soybeans, uncarded cotton, wood, leather, furs, and frozen beef. Its main trading partners were Brazil, which accounted for 30.1 percent of Paraguayan export earnings, followed by the Netherlands, 25.4 percent; Argentina, 9 percent; the United States, 8.7 percent; Chile, 6.1 percent; and Switzerland, 3.7 percent. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 25 Jan 94 p 13) The Inter-American Development Bank and the Argentine-Paraguayan Yacyreta Company on 27 January signed a $130 million loan agreement in Washington. This agreement includes the implementation of projects to alleviate the environmental impact of the hydroelectric dam's construction and several infrastructure and housing development programs to resettle 10,000 families, industries, and shops. (Asuncion ABC COLOR in Spanish 27 Jan 94 p 30)
