Language: English Article Type:BFN [Text] Riga, Mar 12, BNS -- Latvia's population decreased 28,000 in 1993 on the account of interstate migration, according to figures from the State Statistics Committee. As many as 19,291 person resettled for permanent residence in Russia, 5,120 left for Belarus, 3,427 for Ukraine and for Israel. The population exodus to neighboring Lithuania and Estonia was 346 and 99, respectively. Another 744 people went to live in Germany, 559 in the United States, 34 in Canada and 32 to Australia. Ethnically speaking, emigrants included 17,762 Russians, 3,681 Belarusians, 3,676 Ukrainians, 1,161 Hebrews, 448 Poles, 236 Germans and 205 Lithuanians. As a result of migration processes, the number of ethnic Latvians among permanent residents increased 382 people last year.
