Language: Czech Article Type:BFN [Text] Public opinion polls are geared to the coming visit of U.S. President Bill Clinton. Ilona Dientsbierove has more details. [Dienstbierova] The DEMA joint-stock company has prepared a series of questions concerning President Clinton's visit, targeted at 300 respondents from all Prague districts. Some 63 percent of Prague citizens are looking forward to this visit, and 60 percent view it as an appreciation of current developments in our country. Approximately 50 percent of the respondents consider the visit by the U.S. President to be an expression of appreciation for President Vaclav Havel. Approximately the same percentage of the respondents consider this event to be an important step that should lead to the strengthening of trade and economic relations between the Czech Republic and the United States. It is interesting to note that approximately half of the respondents were unable to answer the questions. The DEMA company was also interested in President Clinton's popularity among Prague citizens. According to the survey, the pattern of his popularity is relatively the same in all age groups. However, women are looking forward to his visit to a greater extent than men, by 67 percent to 59 percent.
