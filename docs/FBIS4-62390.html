BFN [By ITAR-TASS correspondent Igor Tkachenko] [Text] Chelyabinsk May 16 -- Two researchers at the Russian Federal Nuclear Centre in Snezhinsk (former Chelyabinsk-70) were seriously injured in a chemical explosive blast on Friday, May 13, deputy director of the Technical Physics Research Institute Vladislav Nikitin told ITAR-TASS. The blast occurred at 10.45 on Friday at a safe distance from the centre's nuclear reactors and caused no change in the radiation background on the scene and around. The blast occurred on a testing site where a new device was being tested for the rehabilitation of old oil wells under a conversion programme. Ten grams of chemical explosive were used in the device with no radioactive components. The investigation of the accident is now under way. The institute chief, professor Vladimir Nechai has suspended all operations associated with risks. There is no threat to the lives of engineer Nikolay Kosorukov and researcher Andrey Stepanov who were injured in the blast.
