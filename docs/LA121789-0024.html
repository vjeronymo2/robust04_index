December 17, 1989, Sunday, Home Edition

ROMANCE IN THE '80S

While Rainer can rest assured that there is no conspiracy to portray the world in Hollywood's image, romance is, by definition, a bit removed from reality, and fills an important need.

Love, a deeper concept Ranier avoided entirely, was depicted in most of the films he criticized. Love works, and it's a lot more mature than romance.

Ranier believes optimism recalls a bygone era, but perhaps the author is stuck back in the bra-burning, sexual-war era and its immediate aftermath, which are both passe.

JAMES TUGEND

Los Angeles
