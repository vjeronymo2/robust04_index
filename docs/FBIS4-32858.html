BFN [Excerpts] The situation in the Armed Forces has been tense in the last few days as a result of the personnel reduction in the Armed Forces. Defense Minister Raul Tovar said that the comments made by General Moises Schiriqui Bejarano, Armed Forces chief of staff, were distorted. [Begin recording] [Reporter Mario Espinoza] This report does not seek to increase a disagreement between the Armed Forces and the constitutionally established civilian government. The idea is to reflect what is happening between the Armed Forces and the executive branch. Today President Gonzalo Sanchez de Lozada arrived 40 minutes late to a ceremony prepared by his escort batallion. Furthermore, the issue of the reduction of personnel and economic resources in the Armed Forces has created concern not only because of the reaction of the Armed Forces chief of staff, as reported by an editorial in LA RAZON, but also because of how it was done, as if it were an answer to an offense against the Armed Forces which in reality never existed. This is why the comments of the defense minister also created concern when he said that the declarations of General Moises Schiriqui Bejarano have been distorted. [Tovar] His comments were distorted. [Espinoza] Does this mean that there are no disagreements between the Armed Forces and the civilian power? [Tovar] No, there are no disagreements, absolutely none. There is complete respect for democracy. [Espinoza] As far as we know, General Schiriqui never questioned the government's policy, in this case democracy. What is indeed under discussion is the possibility of a reduction of personnel and economic resources in the Armed Forces within the framework of the economic crisis affecting the country for the last few years. [passage omitted] [end recording] After President Sanchez de Lozada delivered his speech the impasse with the Armed Forces was overcome. President Sanchez de Lozada was very clear in the speech he made during the ceremony on the anniversary of the Colorados Regiment. [Begin recording] [Espinoza] President Sanchez de Lozada, with the Constitution in his hands, reminded the Armed Forces of their constitutional role of guaranteeing the stability of the legally established government and of cooperating in the overall development of the country. [Sanchez de Lozada] The Armed Forces have the basic role of defending and maintaining national independence [words indistinct], and the honor and national sovereignty, insuring the reign of the Constitution, guaranteeing the stability of the legally established government, and cooperating in the overall development of the country. This is the government policy, this is the government guideline [words indistinct]. [Espinoza] President Sanchez de Lozada said there is not, nor will there be, any policy that does not benefit the nation and this includes the Armed Forces because when a country grows, its Armed Forces also grow. [Sanchez de Lozada in progress] a stronger institution, better prepared to fulfill its constitutional duties, and to represent, and defend our country. [words indistinct] [Espinoza] The president believes there are no poor countries with great Armed Forces and that the current effort is to strengthen the national economy and society, to educate the people, to watch over their health, and also to appropriate resources to have a strong Armed Forces. In the afternoon, the president summoned the high military command to explain the government policy regarding the Armed Forces. Then Armed Forces Commander General Fernando Sanjinez referred to the presidential speech. [Sanjinez] We are very pleased with the speech delivered by the president this morning. We are really very pleased with his speech. [Espinoza] Apparently the situation is now clearer. There was undoubtedly a moment of confrontation but following the meeting the situation is completely normal. [end recording]
