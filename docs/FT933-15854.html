930707 FT 07 JUL 93 / Social Chapter opt-out defended MR ALASTAIR Goodlad, Foreign Office minister, yesterday launched a fierce defence of Britain's opt-out from the Social Chapter of the Maastricht agreement. He insisted that future European success in the face of fierce competition from east Asia depended on flexibility for employers over wage rates, and over where to locate. 'We face a choice between a flexible and competitive life, or an accelerating economic death by drowning in a sea of regulation and rising costs,' Mr Goodlad said in a speech to the London Chamber of Commerce. The EC's annual labour report, due to be published today, is expected to attack Britain's claim that rising social welfare costs hurt competitiveness. Mr Goodlad's comments appear aimed at deflecting this criticism. 'Rising wages, unaccompanied by higher productivity, mean only one thing - unemployment,' Mr Goodlad said. He pinpointed 'three arresting trends': EC member states are selling more to each other, are selling less outside the EC, and are importing more. Rising labour costs are making European companies less competitive. Slow growth in Europe is a symptom of declining competitiveness. 'The last thing we need is new, extensive, and burdensome social harmonisation,' he added. 'That would wreck our competitive position.'
